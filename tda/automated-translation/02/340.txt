Writers' Trip to the Pioneer Lands
Heading Towards North Manchuria
-Becoming Envoys of Pioneer Culture-

Yu Ch'ijin
Recently, through the recommendation of the Chosŏn Writers' Association, I have been dispatched to observe the settlements in Manchukuo, sponsored by the Development Section of the Government-General (本府拓務課).
The purpose of this observation trip is to witness the state of Manchukuo, which has grown amidst the vigorous sounds of the construction of Greater East Asia during the 10 years since its founding, and the brave pioneering lives of our compatriots dedicating themselves to its construction, and to report accurately on this through writing to the Korean literary world and society. However, being unworthy, untalented, and shallowly learned, I worry whether I can fulfill this responsibility.

The itinerary is as above, but I will try my best to make time to sufficiently observe the lives of the native inhabitants of Manchuria and the lives of the dispersed Korean migrants as well.
I believe that the Manchukuo Settlement is not merely simple emigration like in the past, but a new way of life being built according to the blueprint for the construction of Greater East Asia. I worry whether I can see all of that grand ideology and dream during these three weeks. However, I will do my best and return.

Prelude to the Manchuria Trip
Chŏng In't'aek
As someone born and raised in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), I know nothing whatsoever about rural conditions or the lives of farmers. That someone like me was chosen to go to Manchuria this time to see the settlers seems, no matter how I think about it, like an ill fit. However, considering the recognition I've received from my seniors, I reconsidered, deciding it wasn't something to rashly decline, and with great determination, I am now diligently preparing.
Regarding Manchuria, regarding rural life, regarding agriculture, regarding the lives of settlers, etc.—after cramming about 10 books in roughly ten days, my head is spinning, and I cannot get my bearings. Still, without time to finish reading them, I stuffed five or six more books into my rucksack. I plan to read them on the train.
What I will see, what I will hear, and what I will do upon returning—it's all a complete blank slate. I thought approaching it with a blank slate attitude would be the best strategy. However, I am determined to capture everything that enters my field of vision without missing a single thing.
After I return, please do not hesitate to provide much guidance and encouragement.

**Summary**

This article presents preludes written by two Korean writers, Yu Ch'ijin and Chŏng In't'aek, before their departure on a three-week inspection tour of Korean pioneer settlements in Manchukuo in 1942. Sponsored by the Japanese Government-General and the Chosŏn Writers' Association, their stated purpose is to observe the progress of Manchukuo, the lives of Korean settlers contributing to the "construction of Greater East Asia," and report back to Korea. Yu expresses a sense of duty mixed with inadequacy, while Chŏng, a Seoul native unfamiliar with rural life, describes his hasty preparations and determination to observe everything objectively. The detailed itinerary highlights specific settlement locations.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it showcases the mobilization of Korean intellectuals to support and legitimize Japan's colonial and expansionist project in Manchukuo. It reflects the pervasive ideology of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere and frames emigration not as simple migration but as participation in a grand, state-led construction effort. The writers' expressions of duty and humility, alongside the official sponsorship, reveal the complex pressures and roles of cultural figures under colonial rule, serving essentially as cultural envoys for a state-sponsored settlement program often viewed critically by historians for its role in displacing local populations and serving Japanese imperial interests.

作家開拓地行
北滿으로向하면서
-開拓文化의使節이되어-

柳致眞
今般에 朝鮮文人協會의 추선으로 저는 本府拓務課主催 滿洲國開拓視察에 파견되게 되었읍니다.
滿洲國 건국 10년동안에 大東亞建設의 씩씩한 소리를 치고 성장된 滿洲國에 자태와 그 건설에 헌신하고 있는 동포의 용감한 개척생활을 보고 문필로써 정당히 조선문단 及 사회에 보고하려는게 그 시찰의 목적입니다. 그러나 不肖菲才淺學으로 이 책임을 다 할 수 있을는지 걱정입니다.
이번 일정은 3주일간이오, 시찰할 예정지는 다음과 같습니다.

開拓地視察日程

일정은 이상과 같습니다만 가급적으로 틈을 내어서 滿洲原住民의 생활과 朝鮮分散移住民의 생활 등도 충분히 시찰해 보겠습니다.
滿洲國開拓이란 종래의 단순한 이민이 아니오, 大東亞建設의 설계도뿐에서 건축되는 새로운 생활의 방식인 줄 나는 생각합니다. 그 장엄한 사상과 꿈을 이 3주일 동안에 다 볼 수 있을는지 걱정입니다. 그러나 최선을 다하고 돌아오겠습니다.

滿洲行前記
鄭人澤
서울 태생으로 서울서 자란 저는 지방사정이나 농민의 생활을 터럭만치도 몰릅니다. 그런 제가 뽑혀 이번에 滿洲로 開拓民을 보러가게 되었다는 것은 아무리 생각해도 구격이 맞지 않는 것 같습니다. 그러나 先輩諸氏의 知遇를 생각할 때 서뿔리 사퇴를 할 일이 아니라고 고쳐 맘먹고 저는 큰 각오아래 지금 준비를 게을리 아니하고 있습니다.
滿洲에 관한것, 지방생활에 관한것, 농업에 관한것, 開拓民生活에 관한것 등 한 열흘 동안에 10여권을 벼락 공부로 읽고 나니까 머리가 띙하고 갈피를 찾일 수 없읍니다. 그래도 채 읽을 틈이 없이 대 여섯권은 「류크새크」속에 처넣엇습니다. 차 속에서 읽을 작정입니다.
무엇을 보고 무엇을 듯고, 도라와서는 무엇을 할지 전연 백지입니다. 백지의 태도로 대하는 것이 제일 상책이라고 생각햇기 때문입니다. 그러나 내 시야에 들어 오는 것은 하나토 빼놓지 않고 다 사로잡아 가지고 올 결심입니다.
도라온 후에도 많은 지시와 편달을 아끼지 말아 주시기 바랍니다.
다음에 이번 滿洲行의 일정을 적어 두겠습니다.
