**Body Text Translation:**
India's Resources
						By Paek Tong-yŏn

India is a peninsula of the Asian continent demarcated by the Himalayan and Hindu Kush mountain ranges, but it possesses the character of a continent itself. Its area exceeds one million square miles, and its population reaches 350 million. Although its position within the British Empire accounts for only 13% of the total area, in terms of population, it represents over 70% of the whole, thus constituting the most important human resource zone for Britain.

Since 1600, after Britain made India its colony, Britain has, at every opportunity, mobilized India's vast human resources, securing its own safety with their blood. In the current Greater East Asia War as well, the fact that Indian soldiers were put on the front lines in Hong Kong and Malaya, while the British soldiers themselves stayed in rear positions, clearly proves this. Truly, it is no exaggeration to say that losing India means the loss of all organic functions for Britain's national defense. Herein lies an aspect that cannot be overlooked in accomplishing our world-historical great task of establishing the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

It is extremely natural that India, with the fertile alluvial soil of the Ganges, Indus, and Brahmaputra rivers, and possessing a vast area mostly with a tropical climate, stands before the world as an agricultural nation.

Despite the primitive and thus unscientific farming management methods, the fact that agricultural production volume ranks among the world's highest is due to the mobilization of the aforementioned inexhaustible human resources. Taking the examples of the 1938 and 1939 fiscal years, the figures are as follows:

Sugarcane (raw sugar) 5.3 million tons, World No. 1
Jute 8.65 million bales, World No. 1
Peanuts 3.34 million tons, World No. 1
Rice 26.73 million tons, World No. 2
Cotton 5.66 million tons, World No. 2
Tobacco 500,000 tons, World No. 2
Cottonseed 2.4 million tons, World No. 2
Rapeseed, Mustard 1.02 million tons, World No. 2
Sesame seeds 440,000 tons, World No. 2
Tea 403 million pounds, World No. 2

Besides these, linseed is World No. 3, wheat and barley are No. 4 and No. 5 respectively, and it produces all sorts of agricultural products such as rubber, beans, corn, millet, coffee, copra, opium, indigo, spices, etc. Also, livestock includes:

Cattle 160 million head, World No. 1
Goats 36 million head, World No. 1

In addition, there are 31 million water buffalo, 15 million sheep, 4 million horses, and 50 million camels, making India truly a world-class livestock country.

India, possessing abundant natural forests, has benefited from Britain's afforestation policy, producing large quantities of useful timber like teak and sandalwood, and it is said that harvesting 100 million tons of timber annually is easily achievable.

Mining resources have not yet been fully developed, but looking at the main ones from the statistics of the 1937 and 1938 fiscal years, they are as follows:

Manganese ore 960,000 tons, World No. 1
Mica 86,000 tons, World No. 1
Tungsten 3,000 tons, World No. 3
Lead ore 88,000 tons, World No. 3
Chromium ore 31,000 tons, World No. 4
Nickel 1,300 tons, World No. 4
Iron ore 2.7 million tons, World No. 7
Pig iron 1.57 million tons, World No. 8
Coal 28.3 million tons, World No. 9

Besides these, zinc, gold, silver, copper, salt, etc., are produced. Furthermore, coal reserves are estimated at 60 billion tons, and iron ore at 3 billion tons. As for petroleum, most of its resources have now been lost under the control of the Imperial Army [Japanese Army], and currently, 65 million gallons are produced annually in Assam, and 4.4 million gallons annually in Punjab.<117>

**Summary**

This 1942 article by Paek Tong-yŏn details India's vast human and natural resources, emphasizing its significance as Britain's largest source of manpower and a major global producer of agricultural goods (sugarcane, jute, peanuts, rice, cotton, tea, livestock) and mineral resources (manganese, mica, coal, iron). The author highlights Britain's exploitation of these resources, particularly Indian soldiers, for its own benefit. The article frames India's importance within the context of the Greater East Asia War, suggesting that depriving Britain of India is crucial for Japanese victory and the establishment of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is revealing. It shows a Korean author contributing to the Japanese wartime narrative, portraying the conflict as a liberation of Asia from Western imperialism, even while Korea itself was under Japanese rule. The focus on resource mobilization and critique of British colonialism aligns perfectly with Japanese propaganda aims of the era, making it an interesting artifact of intellectual collaboration or coercion during the colonial period. Historians might find the specific (and potentially inflated) statistics on resources, the explicit framing within the GEACPS ideology, and the authorship by a Korean figure particularly noteworthy.

印度의 資源
						白東然
印度는 히마라야, 힌쯔-·쿠시의 兩山脈에 의해서 區劃된 亞細亞大陸의 半島지만 그 자체가 한 개의 大陸으로서의 性格을 所有했다. 그 面積은 백만平方哩를 초과하며, 人口는 3억5천만에 달한다. 그리고 英帝國內에서 占한 地位는, 面積으로서는 1割3分에 지나지 않지만 人口에 있어서는 全體의 7割 이상에 當하기 때문에 英國에 있어서 가장 중요한 人的資源地帶를 構成하고 있다.
1600년, 英國이 印度를 그 屬領으로 한 후로 부터 英國은 모든 機會에 尨大한 印度의 人的資源을 動員하여, 그들의 피로써 自國의 安全을 保장해 왔던 것이다. 今次 大東亞戰에 있어서도 香港에서나 馬來에서 最前線에는 印度兵을 내여세우고 自己들 英國兵은 後方陣地에 있었다는 것이 이를 明確히 證明하는 바이다. 實로 印度를 잃은 것은 英國의 國防에 있어서 一切의 有機的 機能喪失을 意味하는 것이라해도 과언이 아니다. 이에 우리 大東亞共榮圈 確立의 世界的 大業을 完遂함에 있어 看過할 수 없는 一面이 介在한 것이다.
깬디스, 인따스, 푸라-마푸트라 諸河의 豊饒한 沖積土, 또한 廣大하며 그 大部分이 熱帶的 氣候를 가진 印度가 農業國으로서 世界에 臨한 것은 극히 自然한 일이다.
農業經營方式이 原始的이오 따라서 非科學的 임에도 不拘하고 農産物의 生産高가 世界掘指인 것은 前述한 無盡藏의 人的資源의 動員이 그렇게 한 것이다. 1938, 9 兩年度의 例를 보면 다음과 같다.
甘蔗(粗糖) 530만 톤 世界 弟 1位
黃麻 865만 俵 世界 弟 1位
落花生 334만 톤 世界 弟 1位
米 2,673만 톤 世界 弟 2位
棉花 566만 톤 世界 弟 2位
煙草 50만 톤 世界 弟 2位
棉實 240만 톤 世界 弟 2位
菜種, 辛子 102만 톤 世界 弟 2位
胡麻子 44만 톤 世界 弟 2位
茶 403,000만 封度 世界 弟 2位
이외에도 亞麻仁子가 世界 弟 3位, 小麥, 大麥이 각각 弟 4位, 5位이며, 고무, 豆類, 玉蜀黍, 粟, 커피, 코푸라, 阿片, 藍, 香料 등, 모든 農作物을 産出한다. 또 家畜은,
牛 1억6천만 頭 世界 弟 1位
山羊 3천6백만 頭 世界 弟 1位
그외에도 水牛가 3천백만 頭, 羊이 1천5백만 頭, 馬가 4백만 頭, 駱駝가 5천만 頭로서, 印度는 실로 世界的인 畜産國이라고 하지 않을 수 없다.
天然林을 豊富히 가진 印度는, 英國의 植林政策의 効果에 適中되어, 치이크, 白檀 등의 有用材를 多量이 産出하며, 木材 년 1억 噸의 採取는 容易하다고 稱한다.
鑛業資源은 아직 充分히 개발함에 이르지 못했으나, 1937, 8 兩年度의 統計로부터 주요한 것만 보면 如左하다.
망강鑛 96만 톤 世界 弟 1位
雲母 8만6천 톤 世界 弟 1位
탕구스텐 3천 톤 世界 弟 3位
鉛鑛 8만8천 톤 世界 弟 3位
쿠롬鑛 3만1천 톤 世界 弟 4位
닉켈 1천3백 톤 世界 弟 4位
鐵鑛 270만 톤 世界 弟 7位
銑鐵 157만 톤 世界 弟 8位
石炭 2,830만 톤 世界 弟 9位
其外에도 亞鉛, 金, 銀, 銅, 鹽 등이 産出된다. 더욱 石炭의 埋藏量은 6백억 噸, 鐵鑛이 30억 噸이라고 推算되고 있으며, 石油는 지금 皇軍의 制壓下에 그 資源의 大半을 잃게 되었고, 現在는 「앗삼」에서 年額 6천5백만 깨론, 「판쟈푸」에서 年 額 440만 까론이 산출된다.＜117＞
