The Great War and the Path for Women
						By Im Hyo-jŏng (林孝貞), Secretary-General of the Women's Corps, Wartime National Service Corps (臨戰報國團婦人隊幹事長)

The world is now divided into two factions.
One is Japan and the Axis powers Germany (獨逸) and Italy (伊太利), who seek to build a new order so that all peoples living on this earth can coexist and co-prosper, mutually sharing what they have. The other faction is the so-called status quo defenders, Great Britain (英國) and the United States (米國), who want to maintain the territories and interests that are the result of past aggressions.

Against our Japan's assertion, based on the founding ideal of *P'arhonga'il'u* (八紘一宇, Eight Corners of the World Under One Roof), that all peoples living on this globe should live well together, Britain and America are thoroughly selfish and aggressive.

Asia (亞細亞) began to suffer British aggression from the late 18th century. As for how Britain made India (印度), the Malay Federation (마레 연방), Burma (삘마), and Borneo (보루네오) its territories, there are only the four characters: Deceit (詐謀) and Intimidation (恫喝).

Before India suffered British aggression, it was the richest country in the world, fertile and abundant in treasures. However, today, 180 years after the aggression began, it is the poorest country in the world. It is a well-known fact that salt (소곰) is the dearest commodity in the world in India. Is it because there are no salt resources? That is not the case. Britain does not allow industry to develop in India. India is kept only as a source of raw materials; industry is never allowed to develop to enable self-sufficiency. Resources produced in India are sent to the British mainland, made into goods by British hands, then brought back to India and sold to Indians at expensive (빗싼) prices.

Britain, having thus exploited the lifeblood (膏血) of the Indian people, then extended its poisonous fangs (毒牙) to begin aggression even in China (支那), forcibly seizing Hong Kong (香港) from the Qing Dynasty (淸國) 100 years ago through the infamous Opium War (阿片戰爭).<58>

America is just like Britain. Stealing the Kingdom of Hawaii (하와이 王國), deceiving the Philippines (比律賓) by promising independence and then stealing it—all are the same crafty and sinister pretexts and schemes of trickery (弄奸).

America began its aggression in the East (東洋) recently, in the 20th century. At that time, Britain had already occupied India, Burma, the Malay Peninsula, Borneo, Singapore, New Guinea, Hong Kong, and Australia (濠洲); France (佛蘭西) had made French Indochina (佛領印度 支那) its own; and Holland (和蘭) had colonized the Dutch East Indies (蘭領 東印度 支那 諸島).

Seeing all the abundant natural resources in these various places, America's greed flared like fire. However, the time was already late, and there was nothing it could do. So, America devised a scheme and, under slogans like 'freedom of trade' and 'equal opportunity,' began a campaign to cut into (割込運動) existing rights and interests.

America's interference in China, politically and economically, is ultimately not based on justice and humanity (正義人道), but rather on the tungsten ore (당구스텐鑛) with an annual output of 18,000 tons produced in Jiangxi Province (江西省), and secondly, on securing new markets in Asia to sell their manufactured goods. However, there is just one obstacle to the domination of East Asia (東亞制覇), and that is the country called Japan.

They realized their ambitions could not be carried out, seeing that Japan, which already controlled all of China, was unlikely to give up the resources <59> they demanded, and furthermore, Japan had recently advanced peacefully into French Indochina (佛印), and Thailand (泰國) was also sympathizing with Japan's construction of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (東亞共榮圈).

Therefore, to block Japan's advance, they formed the so-called ABCD encirclement (ABCD의 包圍陣) to trouble Japan militarily, while simultaneously issuing an order to freeze assets, completely prohibiting Japan-US trade. These were all means to achieve economic aggression.

Due to the various circumstances described above, our Japan could not but go to war, resolutely rising up on December 8th of last year and unleashing the roar of cannons.

It was not just for the 100 million people of the Japanese Empire to live well, but for the 1 billion people of East Asia to all be happy together, that war was declared on Britain and America. In other words, the Japanese Empire, staking the 2,600-year national destiny of Japan and pouring in the total strength of its 100 million people, began a make-or-break great war (乾坤一擲의 큰 전쟁) against Britain and America, the obstacles to securing the immutable national policy of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

From the Manchurian-Soviet border (滿蘇國境) in the north where the cold winds of Siberia (西伯利亞) blow, to the scorching tropics of perpetual summer in the south, across a vast front stretching truly 7,000 kilometers, hundreds of thousands, millions of officers and soldiers are bearing the honor of the fatherland and fighting valiantly (奮戰) day and night.

Tens of thousands of officers and soldiers have already scattered as the flowers of Japan on the continent of China, and in the Pacific Naval Battle (太平津海戰), our peerlessly loyal and brave Japanese airmen, holding fiercely burning torpedoes to their chests, shouting "Long live His Majesty the Emperor!" (천황폐하만세) at the top of their lungs, rush towards enemy fleets and incinerate their bodies. Even at this moment, as I write this, countless amounts of the Imperial Army's (皇軍) blood and flesh must be scattering.

They are fine sons of others, and also husbands. Among them, there might be only sons for three generations, and men who are fathers of four or five children.

In any case, they are precious beings who should not die, and they are also fine individuals with sensibilities, who know how to cherish (앗낄줄) life.

Why do they die? Why, I ask, do they die fighting those British and American people?

We Peninsular people (半島人) must feel something here and realize (깨닷는) something.

In this Greater East Asia War (大東亞戰), we must know how to be grateful to the Imperial Army, and also feel apologetic.

Although the war entered its sixth year this year, the land and houses where we live have not once suffered an enemy air raid, and regarding our food and clothing, although we eat mixed grains and lack canvas shoes (廣木) and rubber shoes (고무신), it is not to the extent of great difficulty (汨難).

Let us now go back 30 years <60> and reflect on the lives of us Peninsular people. Under the tyranny of corrupt officials (탐관오리), what unjust lives we lived!

People starving to death (굴머 죽는 놈) due to famine, people dying without ever seeing a doctor due to malignant diseases (惡疾), their illness unknown; people chased (쫒겨다니는 백성) from countryside to Seoul, unable to sleep soundly due to thieves and ruffians (불한당); how many people were there who, even if they had money, were oppressed (눌니여) by the yangban and couldn't even build a decent house? But look now, although famine has continued for several years, are there people who have starved to death? Are there those who cannot sleep for fear of thieves? Are there those who cannot build modern houses (문화주택) even if they have money?

Our lives have certainly become peaceful (安穩) and affluent (富饒) compared to before the annexation.

This has all been brought about by the great ideal of *P'arhonga'il'u* of Emperor Jimmu (神武天皇) and the Great Benevolence (大御心) of 'Impartial Benevolence' (一視同仁) from Emperor Meiji (明治天皇).

In other words, it is thanks to the infinite grace (은덕) of His Majesty the Emperor (天皇陛下) above, and the blood shed (픠흘닌) by our loyal Imperial Army below, that each could settle peacefully in their occupations.

We must repay this kindness (報恩). We must repay the grace we have received. Of course, the people living on our Peninsula have also demonstrated patriotism from various angles, donating a portion of their assets, striving for production expansion, sending volunteer soldiers, shouting for the strengthening of 'Mainland-Korea Unity' (內鮮一體), and so on.

However, regrettably, it is said that there are still those who have not realized (깨닷지 못하고) the Imperial Grace (皇恩) and stand on the sidelines with a critical attitude. This class consists of the so-called intelligentsia who have received the most national grace (國恩), and as they belong to the leadership class in Korea (朝鮮), their self-righteousness and silentism inflict considerable harm.

Most of them are gray elements, neither white nor black. Their children are sent to schools that educate (식히는) under the ideology of Mainland-Korea Unity, and their lives may seem like those of Imperial Subjects (皇國臣民) at a glance, but mentally, their self-awareness is still lacking.

Therefore, our urgent task is to become Japanese people possessing the Japanese spirit (日本의 혼). The awareness and practice of being the true children (적자) of His Majesty the Emperor is what is most needed. The later Koreans are in realizing and striving for this, the more unfortunate Koreans will be.

Koreans' happiness lies only in becoming Japanese people. Therefore, anyone who truly loves Korea, and possesses sound judgment, will surely understand that the lifeblood of Korean prosperity exists only within becoming loyal subjects (신민) of His Majesty the Emperor. Consequently, there will be not the slightest hesitation (躊躇) in offering everything one possesses before His Majesty the Emperor.

Let us resolutely become Japanese people possessing the Japanese spirit (日本의 혼). <61> Let us become Japanese people possessing the Japanese spirit (日本精神).

Let us serve (뫼시고) His Majesty the Emperor as our sovereign, know the Japanese Empire as our fatherland, and offer loyalty for our fatherland.

With deepest reverence, if the thought that His Majesty the Emperor is our sovereign becomes thorough among us, the resolve and determination to step forward as desperate warriors in this Greater East Asia War, upon which the rise or fall of Japan depends (달녀있는), will surely arise.

Regrettably, we Peninsular people, except for a few volunteer soldiers, have not had the honor of shouldering guns and going to the front line.

However, even on the home front (銃後), we should consider ourselves soldiers serving at the front and obey whatever the nation tells us to do. When ordered to conserve materials and strive for savings, we will obey with "Yes!"; when told to go out as industrial warriors for production expansion through the National Total Labor Movement (國民皆勞運動), we will also just go out with "Yes!".

This may seem like a small thing, but it is by no means small. If we offer loyalty (盡忠) with all sincerity, this too becomes repayment of kindness (報恩).

Frequently, among Korean people, such words are spoken: "We want Mainland-Korea Unity. We truly want to become one with the Mainland."

That Koreans became subjects of Japan was already decided at the time of the Annexation. It is only that the training (수련) for Koreans themselves to become Imperial Subjects is still incomplete (不定).

Before we speak of complaints and dissatisfaction, I think we need to reflect upon ourselves first.

What contributions have we made that deserve the exact same treatment as Mainland people (內地人)? Have we shed blood for His Majesty the Emperor like Mainland people? Have we offered our sons or husbands as sacrifices (제물) to illuminate (빗내기) Japan's history?

Since ancient times, the nation that strives not to die, dies; the nation that casts aside life like straw for righteousness, lives forever.

If Koreans too offer their red blood unsparingly as a sacrifice in this Greater East Asia War, Koreans will live forever. If, in this Greater East Asia War, they do not shed blood for the glory of the fatherland and fail to devote their loyalty to His Majesty the Emperor, the Korean people (朝鮮民族) will not be able to enter the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere and enjoy endless blessings together with our Japan.

Here, we Peninsular people must awaken greatly. If we are to leap forward to become leaders of the world and masters of East Asia, there must be great training (대수련) and great奋발 (대분발 - great exertion/effort).

For the happiness of our descendants and the eternal happiness of the 1 billion East Asian peoples, I earnestly hope and pray (비러 마지않습니다) for great resolve and determination.<62>
<58-62>

**Summary**

This 1942 editorial by Im Hyo-jŏng, a leader in a pro-Japanese women's organization, forcefully argues for Korean loyalty and participation in Japan's "Greater East Asia War." It portrays the war as a righteous struggle led by Japan against selfish Anglo-American imperialism, aiming to create a "Co-Prosperity Sphere." The author urges Koreans to abandon any critical stance, embrace the Japanese spirit, become true Imperial subjects, and contribute fully to the war effort, including shedding blood, asserting this is the only path to Korean prosperity and eternal life within the Japanese Empire. She contrasts the supposed stability under Japanese rule with the alleged hardships before annexation and criticizes Korean intellectuals for their perceived lack of commitment.

This article is highly revealing from the perspective of Korean colonial history. It exemplifies the rhetoric used by Korean collaborators and pro-Japanese organizations to mobilize the Korean population for the Japanese war effort during World War II. Historians would find it valuable for understanding the internal dynamics of collaboration, the nature of wartime propaganda aimed at Koreans, the promotion of the *Naesŏn Ilch'e* (Mainland-Korea Unity) ideology, and the pressures faced by Koreans, particularly the elite, to demonstrate loyalty to the Japanese Empire.

大戰과 女性의 길
						臨戰報國團婦人隊幹事長 林孝貞
세계는 지금 두 가지 파로 난호였습니다.
신질서를 건설하여 이 땅우에 사는 모든 민족이 다같이 有無相通하면서 共存共榮하자는 日本과 樞軸國 獨逸과 伊太利가 그 하나이고 또 한 파는 과거 침략의 결과인 영토와 권익을 그대로 유지하자는 소위 현상유지파 英國과 米國이 그 하나입니다.
八紘一宇의 肇國의 이상을 가지고 이 지구에 사는 모든 민족이 다같이 잘 살자는 우리 日本의 주장에 대해서 英米는 어듸까지나 이기적이고 침략적입니다.
亞細亞가 英國의 침략을 받기 시작한 것은 18세기말 이래인대 英國이 어떻게 印度와 마레 연방과 삘마와 보루네오 등을 자기네의 영토로 삼었는가하면 거기에는 오직 詐謀와 恫喝의 네 글자가 있을 뿐입니다.
