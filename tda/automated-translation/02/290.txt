기사제목	市民劇場 (Citizens' Theater)
기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous Article)

市民劇場
大東亞戰爭에 全國力을 기우리고 있는 我國에서는 한 편으로 民衆을 즐겁게하고 참된 마음의 糧食이 될 健全娛樂을 切望하고 있다. 그리하여 早稻田演劇協會에서는 市民에게 健全한 演劇을 제공하여 慰安과 敎養을 주려고 東京市設市民劇場의 설립을 제창했다. 시민극장은은 獨逸, 未國, 佛蘭西, 蘇聯 등의 大都市에는 以前부터 있었고, 勤勞者의 醵出金이나, 國家나 或은 市의 補助로 經營되고 있다.＜117＞

Citizens' Theater
In Our Country (我國), which is pouring its entire national strength into the Greater East Asia War (大東亞戰爭), there is, on the other hand, a desperate desire for healthy entertainment (健全娛樂) that will entertain the populace and become true spiritual sustenance (糧食). Accordingly, the Waseda Theater Association (早稻田演劇協會) has proposed the establishment of a Tokyo Municipal Citizens' Theater (東京市設市民劇場) to provide citizens with healthy plays, offering comfort (慰安) and culture (敎養). Citizens' theaters have existed for some time in the major cities of countries such as Germany (獨逸), the United States (未國), France (佛蘭西), and the Soviet Union (蘇聯), and are managed through contributions from laborers (勤勞者), or subsidies from the state or city. <117>

**Summary**

This short article from July 1942 reports on a proposal by the Waseda Theater Association to establish a Municipal Citizens' Theater in Tokyo. The goal is to provide "healthy entertainment" and "spiritual sustenance" to the populace during the Greater East Asia War. It notes that such theaters, often funded by worker contributions or state/city subsidies, already exist in major cities in Germany, the USA, France, and the Soviet Union. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it appears in a Korean-language magazine ("Taedonga") but discusses a Japanese initiative, using "Our Country" (我國) to refer to Japan, reflecting wartime assimilation policies. The emphasis on "healthy entertainment" also points towards state control and mobilization of culture for wartime morale.
