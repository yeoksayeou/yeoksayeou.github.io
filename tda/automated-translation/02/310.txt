連載長篇과 作家, 두 連載物에 對하여
						李泰俊

Starting from the New Year issues, I began publishing "Stars at Every Window" (별은 窓마다) in "New Era" (新時代) and "White Hands Towards Happiness" (幸福에의 흰손들) in "Morning Light" (朝光). Today, having just sent off the May installments, a request came asking me to write something about these two serialized works. Explaining my intentions and direction might, first and foremost, solidify my own grasp and conviction, so I take up my pen.

However, beneficial tasks are often things one dislikes doing. Among various writings, there are a few kinds I absolutely hate writing, and chief among them are articles summarizing one's own work (自己作品의 梗槪類). I don't know about others, but that's how it is for me. Every editor asks the author for a summary, but rather than having a principle of not writing them, I truly dislike it, so I often just brush it off. This seems a bit easier than a summary, but my head is already itching.

The other day, a certain female poet remarked about "Stars at Every Window," "It would have been good to use it as a title for someone else's poem!" Indeed, the reason I gave it this title was because I wanted to infuse this novel generously with "poetry" (詩).

Must reality always be lived only as reality? Cannot dreams shape reality? No matter how clean and beautiful flowers (꽂) may be, can they compare to the pure and innocent maiden (處女) of humankind? A maiden does not become the decoration (粧飾) of a flower, but <118> aren't flowers picked and tied for the maiden? Such wonderful, sacred maidens, young people – seeing them so readily satisfied with the ready-made clothes (旣成服) that reality provides, saying, "This is just the environment!" "This is reality!" "Life is just like this!" – seeing an era where almost all (거이 全部) such women are like this, I sometimes felt melancholic and concerned. Love is degenerating into a kind of affair (情事). Marriage is becoming instrumentalized as a kind of employment (就職). Whether looking at the sky, flowers, or the opposite sex, they feel neither God nor poetry. They deride knowing how to feel such things as mere sentimentality (感傷) and pride themselves on treating such things like worn-out shoes, thinking it intellectual (知性).

Within this trend (風潮), I want to depict a fragrant woman who stands tall and aloof (올然히), preserving the divinity and poetry towards nature, life, and the opposite sex, to perhaps give a little shake to the "fragrance pouch of life" for all young people.

"White Hands Towards Happiness" is a cumbersome title, but my large, rough, red hands are hands I want to open my true heart (眞心) to, sympathize with, encourage (激勵), and grasp consultatively, one by one.

Everyone seeks happiness (幸福). But who thirsts for it like women of marriageable age (婚齡期)? Everyone has pitiful circumstances. But are they as subtle (微妙) as those of young women? Moreover, it is an era where the home can no longer be the sole domain (所在地) for women. Their sphere of happiness (幸福圈) is expanding day by day. There is happiness for women in wearing an embroidered apron (繡놓은 에프론) made during sewing class (裁縫時間) and planning the morning menu (菜單) for their new husband (新郞), but the proud banner of happiness for modern women also flutters when they close their notebooks, face the Great Reality (大現實) directly, and become heroines (히로인) of certain duties (任務) and stages (舞臺) for the people (民衆). The paths for women (女性行路) are spreading out like radial lines (放射線). Taking the paths of three women who have captured my interest (興味) amidst these countless radial lines is also the path this novel will follow. <119>
<118-119>

**Summary**

Yi T'aejun discusses his intentions behind two serialized novels, "Stars at Every Window" and "White Hands Towards Happiness," begun in early 1942. He expresses concern about a perceived trend among young people, especially women, of succumbing to materialism and cynicism, losing touch with ideals, poetry, and genuine feeling in love and life. "Stars at Every Window" aims to portray an idealistic woman who retains her purity and connection to higher sentiments, hoping to inspire readers. "White Hands Towards Happiness" explores the expanding roles and diverse paths to happiness for modern women beyond the traditional domestic sphere, focusing on three women navigating their way through the "Great Reality" and potential roles serving "the People." From a historical perspective, the article is revealing of societal anxieties and changing gender roles during the late colonial period. Yi's focus on female characters grappling with tradition versus modernity, idealism versus pragmatism, and the emerging concept of women contributing to the larger society ("Great Reality," "People") offers historians insight into the complex social dynamics and evolving consciousness under Japanese rule, potentially reflecting wartime pressures and nationalist undertones coded in discussions of "the People."

連載長篇과 作家, 두 連載物에 對하여
						李泰俊
新年號들부터 「新時代」에 「별은 窓마다」, 「朝光」에 「幸福에의 흰손들」을 싯기 시작하였다. 오늘 마침 그 5월호 분들을 보내고 낫는데 이 두 連載物에 관해 무슨 말을 쓰라는 부탁이 왔다. 그 意圖하고 나가는 바를 한 번 설명해 보는 것은, 쓰는 내 자신에게 우선 要領과 信念을 굳게함이 될 지 몰라 붓을 잡는다.
그러나 이로운 일은 흔히는 하기 싫은 일이였다. 같은 글에도 한사코 쓰기 싫은 글이 멧가지 있으니, 그 중에도 첫재 되는것이, 自己作品의 梗槪類의 글인것이다. 남들은 어떤지 모르나 나는 그렇다. 編輯子마다 梗槪를 作者에게 청하나 나는 안쓰는 主義라기보다 정말 쓰기 싫여서 흔히는 뭉때리고 만다. 이것은 梗槪보다도 좀 쉬울 듯 하나 별서 머리가 긁힌다.
日前에 어느 女詩人이 「별은 窓마다」를 「남 詩題로 썼음 좋을걸!」하였다. 아닌게 아니라 題를 이렇게 붙였음은, 이 소설에는 「詩」를 흠벅 넣어보고 싶어서였다.
現實은 반듯이 現實로만 살아야 하는가? 꿈은 現實을 비즐수 없는 것인가? 꽂이 아모리 깨끗하고 고흔들, 人間의 純眞無垢한 處女를 當하랴! 處女는 꽃의 粧飾이 아니되나,＜118＞ 꽃은 處女를 위해 꺾이고 묶임이 되지 않는가? 그런 훌륭한, 거룩한 處女들, 靑年들, 그네들이 너머나 헐하게, 「환경이 그런걸!」 「이게 현실인걸!」 「人生이란 그저 이런거지!」하고 하로 아침으로, 現實이 주는 旣成服에 滿足해버리는 것은, 그런 女性들이 너머나 거이 全部인 時代인 것이, 나는 가끔 우울하고 걱정스럽기도 했다. 사랑이란 一種 情事로 墮落하며 있다. 結婚이란 一種 就職으로 手段化하며 있다. 하눌을 보나, 꽃을 보나, 異性을 보나 神도 詩도 느끼지 못한다. 그런것을 느낄줄 아는것은 허턱 感傷이라 비웃고, 그런것을 헌신짝처럼 여김을 知性인체 自尊한다.
이런 風潮속에 올然히 높이 앉어 自然과 人生과 異性에의 神과 詩를 직혀 나가는, 한 향긔로운 女性을 그려, 모-든 젊은이들의 人生의 香氣 주머니」를 좀 툭 텃드려 주고 싶은 것이다.
「幸福에의 흰손들」 거치장스러운 題目이나, 내 크고 험하고 붉은 손은, 眞心을 열어 同情해주며, 激勵해주며, 의논성스럽게 한 번씩 잡어주고 싶은 손들이다.
幸福은 사람마다 求한다. 그러나 婚齡期의 女性처럼 목마르랴! 딱한 사정도 사람마다 있다. 그러나 젊은 女人의 것처럼 微妙하랴! 더구나 女性도 家庭만이 그들의 所在地일 수는 없는 時代이다. 그들의 幸福圈은 날로 넓어가며 잇는 것이다. 裁縫時間에 지은 繡놓은 에프론을 입고 新郞을 위해 아침 菜單을 構想하는데도 女性의 幸福은 있지만, 노트는 덮어버린채 大現實에 直面해서 民衆을 위해 어떤 任務와 어떤 舞臺의 히로인이 되는데도 現代女性의 당당한 幸福의 깃발은 펄럭이는 것이다. 女性行路는 放射線으로 퍼지며 있다. 이 무수한 放射線속에서 내가 興味를 갖인 세 女性의 線路를 取해 나가는 것이 역시 이 소설의 나아갈 線路인 것이다.＜119＞
