印度의 國民會議派
						編輯室
Recent foreign dispatches report that because Kkandi insisted to the last on continuing the Non-Cooperation Movement without arms, a conflict of opinion arose with the Working Committee of the National Congress Party, which had agreed to cooperate with Britain in prosecuting the war, and that Kkandi was asked to step down from his position of leadership.

Within the National Congress Party, the great political body of India which holds political power in 8 out of India's 11 provinces, there were originally broadly three factions, and Kkandi represents one of them. Although he is of the old age of 72 this year, his conviction in non-violence has not changed in the slightest, and he is still supported by countless worshippers.

In contrast to this, the leader of the second faction is Pandit Nehru (판띠이트·네일); he does not refuse the use of violence <116> and is an anti-British socialist. The third faction is a group that seeks to rally progressive elements who cooperate with Kkandi but do not belong to the National Congress Party, and its leader is Rajagopalachari (라지꼬파라챠리).

Britain's Black Market Dealings (英國의 闇取引)
In the British House of Commons, Food Minister Woolton stated that due to the sharp increase in black market dealings and profiteering within the country, severe punishments must be implemented for these. Furthermore, the Food Minister added that the current number of cases of black market dealings and unfair profits has reached 3,130.
<116-117>

**Summary**

The article reports on a significant split within the Indian National Congress Party in mid-1942. Gandhi's unwavering commitment to non-violent non-cooperation clashed with the party's Working Committee, which had decided to cooperate with Britain's war effort, leading to calls for Gandhi's resignation from leadership. It briefly outlines the three main factions: Gandhi's non-violent group, Nehru's socialist, anti-British faction open to violence, and Rajagopalachari's group seeking broader progressive alliances. Separately, it notes the British Food Minister's concern over rising black market activities and profiteering in Britain itself. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it shows a Japanese-controlled publication reporting on internal dissent within the Allied camp (India/Britain) and highlighting Britain's domestic problems (black market), potentially to undermine the image of the Allied powers and emphasize fissures in the anti-Axis front. It also reflects contemporary interest in Indian nationalism as another anti-colonial movement.

印度의 國民會議派
						編輯室
최근의 外電은, 깐디가 어디까지든지 非武裝 不服從 運動의 績行을 주장했기 때문에 英國이 戰爭遂行에 協力하기로 一致된 國民會議派 運用委員會와 意見의 對立을 보게 되였으며, 깐디는 指導者의 位置로부터의 退去를 要求당했다고 報하고 있다.
印度 11州 中 8州의 政權을 掌握한 印度의 大政治團體인 國民會議派에는 大別하여 元來 3分派가 있었으며, 그 하나를 代表하는 것이 깐디다. 그는 금년 72세의 老齡이지만 非暴力主義에 대한 그의 信念은 조금도 변함이 없이, 여전히 無數한 崇拜者들의 支持를 받고 있다.
이것에 대하여 제 2의 分派의 指導者는 「판띠이트·네일」이며, 그는 暴力使用을 拒否하지 않고,＜116＞ 反英國的인 主張을 가진 社會主義者다. 제 3의 分派는 깐디와 協調하면서도 國民會議派에 屬하지 않는 進步的인 分子를 糾合하려고 하는 一派로서, 그 指導者는 「라지꼬파라챠리」다.

英國의 闇取引
英國下院에서 食糧相 울톤은 國內에 闇取引과 暴利가 激增하기 때문에 이에 대한 峻烈한 刑罰이 實施되지 않으면 안되겠다고 述했다. 더욱 現在의 闇取引과 不當利得의 件數는 3천백3‹­件에 이르럿다고 食糧相은 加言했다.
＜116-117＞
