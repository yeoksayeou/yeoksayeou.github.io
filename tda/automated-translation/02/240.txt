The Mission of the Mobile Theater Troupe
						By Ch'oesangdŏk, Director of the Korean Theater Association and Head of the Mobile Theater Troupe

In this time of National Emergency, the existence of all things will only be permitted for the sake of the Nation.— According to this solemn national command, it is natural that theater troupes (acting troupes) also are needed only for the sake of National Policy. There is hardly any room to consider things like the profit of individuals or companies, or the enjoyment of the populace. However, in the current situation where theater is not yet managed by the state, the aforementioned National Policy, which should naturally be carried out, is in reality not being fully executed. The reason for this is extremely simple: the actions of theater troupes, unable to disregard profit, aim for greater income, advance into urban areas where transportation is convenient and many spectators can be obtained, and consequently, cater to the sense of pleasure of those petty citizens, providing entertainment for entertainment's sake. Therein lies the contradiction of the theater troupes themselves.

Born after suffering the pains of labor amidst the above contradictions is the Mobile Theater Troupe.

The mission of the Mobile Theater Troupe is to send healthy and cheerful entertainment to the agricultural, mountain, and fishing villages suffering from an entertainment famine, thereby contributing to the policy of Industrial Patriotism.

In the Mainland (Naeji), recognizing the gravity of this National Policy mission, large-scale movements of this kind, with the total cooperation of authoritative businesses, media organizations, and relevant authorities, have been practiced for a long time. However, in Korea (Chosŏn), under the direct management of the Korean Theater Association, it has only been over a month since the first, extremely small-scale Mobile Theater Troupe cried its first cry.

That the famine of entertainment in the agricultural, mountain, and fishing villages of Korea is incomparably more severe than that of the Mainland is something that any person with eyes would more than affirm. It is an undeniable fact that these brave industrial warriors, who go out to their workplaces before the morning star has even hidden and forget laughter besides the abuse of their muscles, seemingly spitting fire, until the evening stars vie to appear, have never even thought of comfort.

The mission of the Mobile Theater Troupe—to seek out these warriors of sweat in remote mountain regions, find the laughter they have lost, distribute rations for their starved spirits, and have them offer even more vigorous labor to the Nation the next day—is great, and its sacrifice will be limitless. These members of the Mobile Theater Troupe, who pay this great mission and limitless sacrifice, are truly the front line and sentinels of the theater troops. Before them, there are no modern, convenient means of transportation, no splendid theater stages, and sometimes, not even electric lighting. Instead of trains and automobiles, straw mats must be prepared; instead of theaters glittering with neon, canvas screens in the open air are needed; and instead of electric lights, pine knot torches stand waiting.

Considering its operational nature, which completely disregards profit, the Mobile Theater Troupe should naturally be managed directly by the State or some other public-interest corporation. Nevertheless, its current status as merely a parasitic or dependent existence within the meager Korean Theater Association is deeply lamentable in view of the necessity and importance of this enterprise. At the same time, I earnestly hope for the day when this enterprise is re-recognized and re-examined by various esteemed figures, so that Korea's Mobile Theater Troupe, still in its swaddling clothes, may grow into adulthood and fully accomplish its mission.<99>

**Summary**

This 1942 article by Ch'oesangdŏk, head of Korea's newly formed Mobile Theater Troupe, argues for the crucial role of such troupes during the "National Emergency" (wartime). He states their mission is to bring "healthy and cheerful" entertainment to rural Koreans, boosting morale for "Industrial Patriotism" (supporting the war effort through labor), contrasting this with profit-driven urban theater. He notes the Korean troupe is nascent and under-resourced compared to efforts in mainland Japan (Naeji) and calls for greater state or public support. For historians, the article starkly reveals how cultural activities like theater were explicitly co-opted for state propaganda and mobilization in colonial Korea during the Pacific War. It highlights the discourse of national duty, sacrifice, the urban-rural divide in cultural access, and the perceived backwardness of Korea compared to Japan, all framed within the context of total war.

移動劇團의 使命
						朝鮮演劇協會理事 移動劇團團長 崔象德
이 國家非常時에 있어서는 모든 存在는 國家를 위하여서만 그 存在가 許容될 것이다.- 라는 이 嚴肅한 國家命令에 의하여 劇團(演團)도 또한 國策을 위하여서만 그 存在가 필요할 것은 물론이다. 개인 혹은 會社의 營利나 民衆의 享樂같은 것은 거이 돌아볼 餘地가 없다. 그러나 演劇이 아직 國家 經營이 못된 今日의 現狀으로는 당연히 遂行되여야 할 前記國策이 萬全히 遂行되고 있지못한 實情에 있으니 그 理由는 극히 간단한 것이어서 營利를 度外視할 수 없는 劇團의 演劇 行動은 보담 더 많은 收入을 目標로 하여 될 수 있는대로 交通이 便利하고 觀衆을 많이 獲得할 수 있는 都會地로 進出하고 따라서 그들 小市民들의 享樂感에 迎合하여 娛樂을 위한 娛樂을 제공하지 않으면 안되는 데에 劇團自體의 矛盾이 있다.
이상의 矛盾속에 陣痛되다 남저지 誕生된 것이 移動劇團이다.
移動劇團의 使命은 娛樂에 飢饉된 農山漁村에 健全明朗한 오락을 보내여 産業報國의 一策에 助함에 있다.
內地에서는 이 國策的 使命의 重大性에 鑑하여 權威있는 業者 言論機關 내지 所管當局 등 總協力으로 대규모의 이 運動이 實踐된 지 日富하다. 그러나 朝鮮에서는 朝鮮演劇協會 直營下에 極히 소규모의 移動劇團 그 제 1隊가 呱呱의 聲을 발한지 月餘에 지나지 않는다.
朝鮮에 있어서의 農山漁村의 娛樂의 飢饉은 內地의 그것에는 全然 比較가  안되리만치 尤甚하다는 것은 具眼의 士 肯定하고도 남을 바이다. 샛별이 아직 숨기도 전에 일터로 나간 그들 勇敢한 産業戰士는 저녁별이 다투어 나올 때까지 불을 吐하는 듯한 筋肉의 虐使外에 웃음을 이저버리었으며 慰安을 생각해 본 적이 없는 것이 숨김없는 사실이다.
이들 땀의 勇士들을 山間僻地로 찾아 그들에게 잃어버린 웃음을 찾아주고 굼주린 精神의 糧食을 配給하여 밝는 날의 보담 더 旺盛한 勤勞를 國家에 밧치게 하는 移動劇團의 使命은 크고 그 犧牲은 無限일 것이다. 이 크나큰 使命과 無限의 犧牲을 拂하는 移動劇團員들 이야말로 演劇部隊의 제일선이며 步哨이다. 그들의 앞에는 현대적인 便利한 교통기관도 없고 葉麗한 劇團舞臺도 없고 甚하면 電氣의 照明조차 없다. 汽車, 自動車 대신으로 집석이를 準備하고 네온이 빗나는 劇場대신으로 露天에 布幕이 필요하고 電燈대신으로는 광술불이 待機되고 있다.
營利를 전연 도외시하는 그 事業的 本質로 보아 移動劇團은 당연히 國家直營이나 기타 公益的 法人으로 경영될 것임에 불구하고 빈약한 朝鮮演劇協會의 한낫 寄生虫的 또는 食客的 存在의 現狀임은 이 事業의 必要性 重大性에 鑑하여 慨歎하여 마지않는 동시에 이 사업의 再認識 再檢討가 大方諸家의 손으로 되여 아직 襁褓에 쌓인 朝鮮의 移動劇團이 成人이 되여 그 使命을 完遂할 날을 기대리어 마지 않는다.＜99＞
