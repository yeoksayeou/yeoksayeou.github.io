Popular Medicine - Occupational Cramps, The Harm of Fatigue Which Cannot Be Ignored

Cramps are a symptom that occurs because muscles contract pathologically, and among diseases specific to certain occupations, there is something called occupational cramp.

For example, there are cases called writer's cramp (書攣), where a person who only writes year-round experiences cramps in their hand, or a typist experiences cramps and becomes completely unable to use their fingers for a while.

This type of illness is particularly common among manual laborers, and moreover, the accumulation of such habitual fatigue in a specific part of the muscles spreads systemically, lowers resistance to disease, and easily creates a source for contracting tuberculosis, so one must be careful.

Things used for fatigue recovery until today include Vitamin B1, which promotes metabolism, or B2, which breaks down fatigue substances into non-toxic ones. However, supplementing these with glutathione, which aids in tissue oxidation-reduction or metabolic detoxification, or phosphate salts, which neutralize blood acidosis caused by lactic acid production, is more effective than administering vitamins alone. Therefore, Wakamoto (와까모도), a complex preparation of the powerful Wakamoto yeast's Aspergillus NK bacteria (아스페루기루스NK菌), is the most suitable formulation as it contains these components without omission, along with a highly assimilable, concentrated Vitamin B complex.<43>

**Summary**

This 1942 article discusses occupational cramps and fatigue as health issues arising from repetitive muscle use, particularly in writers, typists, and manual laborers. It warns that accumulated fatigue can lower disease resistance, increasing susceptibility to illnesses like tuberculosis. The article promotes Wakamoto, a Japanese product containing Aspergillus NK bacteria, B vitamins, glutathione, and phosphate salts, as a comprehensive remedy superior to single vitamin supplements for combating fatigue and its effects. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects health concerns potentially exacerbated by wartime labor demands in colonial Korea. It highlights the promotion of Japanese commercial products (Wakamoto) as scientific solutions to health problems within the colonial context, potentially linking worker productivity and health maintenance to Japanese economic interests.

通俗醫學-職業性의 痙攣, 輕視할수없는 疲勞의 害
痙攣은 筋肉이 病的으로 收縮하기때문에 일어나는 한개의 症狀으로서 一種職業에 딸으는 特殊한 病에 職業的 痙攣이라는것이 있습니다.
例를 들면 書攣이라하여 年中 글씨만 쓰는 사람이 손에 痙攣을 이르키다든지 또는 타이피스트가 痙攣을 이르키여서 한동안 전혀 손가락을 쓰지못하는 일이 있습니다.
筋肉勞働자에게는 特히 이와같은 病이 많고 더군다가 이러한 一部筋肉의 常習的 疲勞의 累積은 全身的으로 傳播되고 杭病力을 低下하여 結核에 걸리기 쉬운 根源을 맨들기쉬우니까 注意하지않으면 않됩니다.
오늘날까지 疲勞恢復에 쓰이고 있든것으로는 新陳代謝를 促進하는 비타민 B1이나 또는 疲勞物質을 無毒物로 分解하는 B2 等이 있습니다만 이것보담도 더욱 組織의 酸化還元이라든가 代謝解毒에 協力하는 구루타치온 或은 乳酸産出에 依하여 일어나는 血液酸毒症을 中和하는 燐酸鹽 등의 補給을 합쳐 行하는것이 비타민을 單一히 投與하기보담 效果가 많은 까닭에 强力한 와까모도酵母의 아스페루기루스NK菌의 複合劑인 와까모도는 同化性이 높은 濃厚한 비타민B複合體와 함께 이러한 成分을 빼놓지않고 含有하고 있음으로 가장 適合한 製劑입니다.＜43＞
