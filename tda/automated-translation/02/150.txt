<70-74>
Before General Kawagishi and Other Generals, the Tokyo International Student Representative Takes an Oath, A Celebration Meeting for the Conscription System in the Imperial Capital

The other day, following the announcement of the conscription system, young men from the Peninsula [Korea] studying abroad in Tokyo gathered together to hold a gratitude and celebration meeting. On that day, many students burning with enthusiasm gathered. Furthermore, specially attending on the day were:

Korea Scholarship Association (朝鮮獎學會) Director, Lieutenant General Kawagishi (川岸中將)
Prisoner of War Information Bureau (浮虜情報局) Director, Lieutenant General Uemura (上村中將)
Army Preparatory Officer School (陸軍豫科士官學校) Head Instructor, Major General Makino (牧野少將), Major General Hasegawa (長谷川少將)

They were present. The following text is the fervent oath given by the student representative, Mr. Sin (申君), on that day.

We Hereby Swear

Tokyo, Nihon Daigaku (日本大學) Sin Ryonggyun (申龍均)

**Summary**

This article reports on a meeting held in Tokyo by Korean students studying abroad to celebrate the implementation of the Japanese conscription system for Koreans in 1942. Several high-ranking Japanese military officials attended the event, where a student representative, Sin Ryonggyun from Nihon Daigaku, delivered a fervent oath on behalf of the students. The article highlights the enthusiastic participation of Korean students in celebrating a policy aimed at integrating them fully into the Japanese war effort. For historians, this piece is significant as it exemplifies the complex dynamics of collaboration and assimilation during the late colonial period. It showcases how some Korean elites or students publicly displayed loyalty and support (whether genuine or coerced) for Japanese imperial policies, providing valuable propaganda material for the Japanese regime.
