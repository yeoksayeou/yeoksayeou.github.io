**Summary**

This short news piece from the July 1942 issue of *Taitōa* (大東亞 - Greater East Asia) magazine lists prominent Korean film figures—actresses, actors, directors, and cinematographers—along with the Japanese names they adopted under the *sōshi-kaimei* (창씨개명 - *ch'angssigaemyŏng*) policy enforced by the Japanese colonial government. The article simply presents these names without commentary. For historians, this list is a stark record of the cultural assimilation policies during the late colonial period in Korea, documenting the specific Japanese names adopted by well-known personalities in the Korean film industry. It provides concrete evidence of the reach and impact of the *sōshi-kaimei* policy on individuals in the public eye.
