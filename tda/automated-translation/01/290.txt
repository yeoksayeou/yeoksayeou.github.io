The True State of Resource Warfare
Modern warfare requires many resources. To give a few examples:
In Britain, during World War I, 250,000 pairs of soldier's boots per year were sufficient in peacetime, but once the war broke out, truly 400 million pairs were needed over four years.
Regarding ammunition, at the beginning of the war, 23 million rounds of rifle bullets were manufactured, but as the front expanded, 120 million rounds were produced per month.
In Germany, during the general attack on Poland, the number of machine gun bullets used in just four days was said to be the same as the number used over four years in the Battle of Verdun during World War I. Thus, what was used over four years 25 years ago is now expended in four days.
According to a French military expert, to fully operate a two-man combat vehicle (戰軍?) at the front line requires the labor of 46 people behind the lines (銃后), and<146> for one airplane to operate fully, 70 laborers are needed behind the lines.

Hitler's Royalties Confiscated
German Chancellor Hitler's authored book, "Mein Kampf," is a world-renowned bestseller that sold widely even in the enemy country, Britain. Royalties from it amounted to $800 just in the latter half of this year alone. However, these were not given to the author but confiscated as enemy assets and put into the British Red Cross fund.

Rome's Air-Raid Shelters
In Italy's capital, Rome, there are currently 359 public air-raid shelters accommodating 250,000 people. Additionally, there are 20,000 private home shelters capable of holding 8,000 people. Furthermore, with 30 new air-raid sirens installed, the total number of sirens in the city now reaches one hundred.

German Film Screened in Britain
A film shot from the German side about the German-Soviet War has reportedly been publicly screened in London. The film, titled "Battle on the Eastern Front," vividly records the superiority of German military equipment and operations. The British Information Bureau is encouraging this film to urge national vigilance.

Sports Equipment Distributed to Own POWs
The British Red Cross Society and the St. John Association, in cooperation with the Football Association, have started a campaign to supply sports equipment for maintaining the physical fitness of British POWs. British soldiers currently held as prisoners in Germany and Italy will thus be able to engage in outdoor exercise. Distribution is being handled by the International Red Cross Society in Geneva (Switzerland).

America's Rubber Countermeasures
Because naval supremacy in the Southwest Pacific has fallen into the hands of our Japan, the US government, finding rubber imports impossible, is reportedly planning the establishment of large-scale rubber companies in Central and South American countries. According to an official announcement, 15 million Indian rubber trees have been transplanted to Central and South America.<147>
<146-147>

**Summary**

This article, compiled by the "Information Office" of the Samch'ŏlli Editorial Office, presents several short news items from early 1942. It highlights the massive resource demands of modern warfare using WWI and WWII examples, mentions the confiscation of Hitler's book royalties by Britain, details Rome's air-raid preparedness, reports on Britain showing a German war film to boost morale, describes efforts to send sports equipment to British POWs, and outlines the US response (seeking rubber in Latin America) to Japan's control over Southeast Asian rubber sources. For historians of colonial Korea, the article is interesting as it shows the type of international war-related information being disseminated to a Korean audience under Japanese rule. The explicit mention of "我 日本" (Our Japan) taking control of the Southwest Pacific clearly reflects the colonial power's perspective being promoted within the colony. The focus on resource warfare and enemy actions (Britain, US) aligns with wartime propaganda efforts.

資源戰爭의 眞相
근대전은 많은 자원을 要하는데 數例를 들면
英國에서는 제1차대전때에 平時에 兵丁구두가 1년에 25만 足이면 足하드니 전쟁이 나자 4個年 동안에 실로 4억만 足이 필요하였다고.
彈藥도 개전당시는 2천 3백만發의 小銃彈을 製造하였는데 戰線이 擴大됨에 따라 1개월에 1억 2천만發을 제조하였다.
獨逸에서는 波蘭總攻擊에 當하야 僅僅 4일 동안에 機關銃彈을 쓰기를 제 1차대전때 「벨단」전으로 4개년간 노흔 數와 同數였다고 하니, 25년 전에 4개년 쓰든 것을 금일은 4일간에 써버리는 터이다.
佛蘭西의 軍事專門家의 說에 의하면 戰線에서 2人乘 戰軍를 완전히 활동시키자면 銃后에서 46인의 勞力이 필요하며＜146＞ 飛行機 1臺가 완전히 활동하자면 銃后에서 70인의 勞務員이 필요하다고 한다.

힛틀러 印稅를 沒收
獨逸 힛틀러 總統이 著述한 「나의 鬪爭」은 가장 많이 팔린 세계적 명저인데 敵國인 英國에서도 많이 팔려 그 印稅만 금년 하반기만해도 8백불에 달하는데 이것을 본인에게 주지않고 敵産이라고 하여 沒收하여 英國 적십자 기금에 넣어버렸다고.

로마의 防空壕
伊太利의 수도  로마에는 현재 359의 공공 防空壕가 있으며 25만인을 수용한다. 그리고 自家用 防空壕는 2만이나 되며 8천인을 收容할 수 있다고 한다. 그 他 空襲 싸이렌이 30개가 新設되였으므로 全市에 싸이렌의 數가 백에 달한다고 한다.

英國에서 獨逸映畵上映
獨蘇戰을 獨逸側으로 부터 撮影한 映畵가 론돈에서 公開되였다고. 그것은 「東部戰線의 싸움」이라는 映畵인데, 獨逸軍의 裝備와 作戰의 優秀를 생생하게 기록했다한다. 英國 情報局에서는 국민의 긴장을 促하기 위해서 이 영화를 獎勵하고 있다.

自國捕虜兵에게 運動具를 配給
英國赤十字社와 聖 죤 協會에서는 蹴球協會와 共同으로 英國兵捕虜의 體位保持를 위한 運動用品 供給의 運動을 始作했다. 현재 獨逸과 伊太利에 捕虜로 가있는 英國兵丁들도 이로써 戶外運動을 하게 되는데, 이 배급에는 쥬네-브(瑞西)의 國際赤十字社가 하게된다.

米國의 고무對策
西南太平洋의 制海權이 我 日本의 손에 돌아온 때문에 米國政府에서는 고무 輸入이 不可能하게 됐으므로 中南米 諸國에 大規模의 고무회사 설립을 계획한다고 한다. 當局의 발표에는 1500만本의 印度産 고무樹를 中南米에 移植했다고 한다.＜147＞
