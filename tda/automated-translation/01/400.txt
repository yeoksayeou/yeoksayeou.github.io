Editorial Postscript

This journal, having changed its title, seeks once again your patronage and guidance starting from this March issue. Looking back, it was founded in April of Shōwa 4 (1929) and published for 14 years under the title "Samch'ŏlli." However, reflecting now, the world's steps are currently preparing for a great leap, and the future of us, the 1.3 billion people of East Asia, is truly filled with light and hope. This journal, after deep reflection, has changed its title to "Tae Tonga" (大東亞 - Greater East Asia) and pledges to stand at the forefront as a brave warrior in the ideological and cultural battles of this Holy War for the construction of Greater East Asia.

Within the company, from the president, chief editor, reporters, to staff members, there have been no changes whatsoever from before. Future new management policies will be gradually reported through these pages, but the main point is to focus further efforts on editing so that this journal may render great service (奉公) towards the completion of the Greater East Asia Holy War (聖戰). (Significant renewal of the journal's pages starting from the April issue)

The patriotic movement (애국운동) in Chosŏn is heightening extraordinarily day by day, and patriotic leaders in various regions are taking to the streets (가두) teaching the path of utmost loyalty and dying for the country (盡忠殉國). This issue of our journal features speeches by various women figures, and furthermore, at this time when everyone's eyes are on Southern (南方) resources, we have devoted our full efforts to reporting on the situation in that region.

Regarding our company's book publishing policy, at this time when paper is precious and printing materials are also gradually becoming expensive (漸貴한), our policy is to proceed by publishing only what is truly necessary for the nation, society, and the war effort. Thus, having carefully selected and re-selected, we have published a few books (數三書籍) as advertised separately; please patronize them widely by reading them. <189>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript announces the renaming of the magazine "Samch'ŏlli" to "Tae Tonga" (Greater East Asia) in March 1942. The change reflects the magazine's explicit alignment with Japan's wartime objectives and the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere ideology. The editors pledge to act as "ideological and cultural warriors" in the "Holy War," emphasizing propaganda efforts. The postscript mentions the intensification of pro-Japanese "patriotic movements" in Korea, including street speeches advocating loyalty and sacrifice, and notes the magazine's focus on reporting about resources in Southeast Asia ("Southern resources"). It also touches upon wartime austerity, explaining that book publishing will be limited to essential wartime materials due to paper shortages. From a historical perspective, the article is revealing as it explicitly documents a Korean publication's shift to overt collaboration and promotion of Japanese imperial ideology during the height of the Pacific War. It highlights the pressure and propaganda mechanisms used in colonial Korea, the co-option of nationalist rhetoric ("patriotic movement") for imperial purposes, and the economic realities (resource scarcity, focus on "Southern resources") of the period.

編輯後記
本誌는 題號를 改하여 今 3월호부터 다시 여러분의 愛顧와 지도를 입으려 하나이다. 회고하매 昭和 4년 4월에 창간하여 14개 星霜을 「三千里」의 題號 밑에서 발간하여 왔으나, 지금 도리켜 보건대 세계의 발걸음은 바야으로 큰 躍轉을 준비하고 있어 우리들 東亞 13억 민중의 전도는 실로 광명과 희망에 가득 차고 있습니다. 本誌 깊이 성찰한 바 있어 제호를 「大東亞」로 改하옵고 이 大東亞 건설의 聖戰에 思想戰 文化戰의 용감한 전사로서 진두에 서기를 기하나이다.

社內는 사장, 주간, 기자, 사원에 이르기까지 종전대로 하등 변경이 없아오며, 금후 제반 新經倫은 차츰 지면을 통하여 報하려 하옵거니와, 요는 편집에 일층 주력하여 본지로 하여금 大東亞 聖戰 완수에 큰 奉公이 되려 하옵나이다.(4월호부터 誌面 대쇄신)
조선의 애국운동은 날이 수록 비상히 고조되고 있어 각지에서 애국의 지도자들이 가두에 나와 盡忠殉國의 길을 가르치고 있습니다. 이번 本誌에는 부인층 諸氏의 그 연설을 게재하였으며 또 南方 자원에 만인의 눈이 달리는 이때 그 방면의 情況報導에도 전력을 다했습니다.

本社의 서적출판 방침은 紙物이 귀하고 인쇄물자 또한 漸貴한 이때이기 진실로 국가사회와 전시에 필요한 것이 아니면 아니 발간할 방침으로 나아가옵나이다. 그리하여 이제 정선하고 정선하여 數三書籍을 別記 광고와 如히 발간하였으니 많이 애독하여 주소서.＜189＞
