Historical Drama in 4 Acts, Ich'adon
						Pak Yŏngho (Original Work), Kŭktan Sŏnggun (Performance)
Silla, 14th year of King Pŏphŭng
Silla Capital, Sŏbŏl (Kyŏngju)
Prologue: Before the "Subduing Mara and Attaining Enlightenment" Painting (降魔成道圖)
Act 1: Morye's House (毛禮家) (Chadai 子代)
Act 2: Chach'ŏn Hall (紫宸殿)
Act 3: Isabu's Rear Garden (異斯夫의 後院)
Act 4: The Princess's Palace (公主宮)
Act 5: The Eastern Palace (東宮)

Story
"Behead the wicked monk Ich'adon!" A great clamor arose in Sŏrabŏl. Those holding dogs and spears (짱), those brandishing clubs, those screaming horribly—it was truly a scene to make one's liver tremble.
"Kill Ich'adon, the goblin (독개비) figure who has no hair on his head, wraps his body in rags (보재기), hangs a string of beads around his neck, and holds a staff with a door handle!"
"Execute Ich'adon, who audaciously invoked the King's command to cut down the Heavenly Mirror Forest (한올거울숲 - 天鏡林) and build a grand temple!"
"Behead Ich'adon, the great traitor who, claiming he went to cut off the head of King Anjang (安藏王) of Koguryŏ (高句麗), surrendered to the enemy nation, changed his loyalties (lit. intestines), and infiltrated his homeland Silla (新羅) as a spy!"

At this time, in the Princess's Palace within the royal court, Princess P'yŏngyang clung to Ich'adon.
"My lord, what will you do with this body? Will you kill me, or let me live? Once this body has set its heart with devotion, how could it change even if heaven and earth split in two? Take me (걷우시오). If you refuse, take this life with this knife."
While the princess was thus, outside the side gate stood Tallim, the daughter of the Minister of the Treasury Isabu (調府令 異斯夫), her eyes full of tears. She was the bride to whom he had pledged his troth, swearing by Mount T'oham (토함산) and the countless stars of heaven.
"My lord, please marry the Princess. Please become the Crown Prince and take charge of the country."
While Tallim was also thus, in the dimly lit Chach'ŏn Hall, King Pŏphŭng heaved many heavy sighs.
"Ich'adon. Take charge of this country. Become the royal son-in-law. Become the Crown Prince (東宮)."
Noticing this situation, Alkong (謁恭) and Kongmok (工目) tried to incite the King's younger brother, Sŏnmaro (立宗), to stir up trouble.
"Your Majesty. Please elevate the nation's power. Please calm the hearts of the angry populace. Buddhism is a wicked way. It is a heterodox way. It is a perverse way."

Tallim behind him, the Princess behind Tallim, King Pŏphŭng behind the Princess, the twelve Yeongmun soldiers behind the King, the Buddha (부처님) behind the soldiers—Ich'adon's heart struggled in the void.
The shouts of the soldiers and the people grew louder. The surprise attack by Kongmok and Alkong hit its mark without fail (낙출없이). Ich'adon ran out into the street.
"You people, you pitiful beings of Silla! Escape from the five defilements (五濁) and the Latter Day of the Law (末法). Learn the principle of cause and effect (因果應報). Even if this body <162> dies, aren't you afraid of the principle that my karma alone will live on for eternity (永劫) and be reborn in the three realms and six paths (三界六道)?"
"Buddhism is not a shaman's spell. It is not the Kosingyo (고신도 - indigenous faith) served by male or female shamans. It is about uniting the Five Precepts (五戒) of the Hwarang way (花郎道) with the compassion (慈悲) of the Buddha."
As these words fell, stones flew from nowhere and struck Ich'adon's forehead. Returning to the palace, Ich'adon prostrated himself before the King.
"Your Majesty, what use is there in keeping this life? The sins of sentient beings that cannot be saved (걷우지 못할) even after myriad eons (億萬劫)—I wish to die on the path to guide them. The five teachings also say 'No retreat in the face of battle' (臨戰無退). Buddhism is also a battle, and a monk is also a warrior (虎班). It is a battle of the mind, a battle between the Buddha and demons. If by my death, Buddhism ascends into the laws of Silla, and the temple bells (梵鍾) recorded in the six villages of Sŏbŏl (서벌여섯마을) ring out, then throwing away my life like this is the true duty (보내오) of a monk."
Ich'adon leaped among the soldiers. Finally, Ich'adon's head rolled onto the grand floor (大뜰). White milk spurted from his neck, and heavenly flowers rained down from the void. <163>

**Summary**

This text is a synopsis or excerpt from a four-act historical play titled "Ich'adon," originally written by Pak Yŏngho and performed by the Kŭktan Sŏnggun theater troupe, published in March 1942. It dramatizes the story of Ich'adon, a Silla court official whose martyrdom led to Buddhism's acceptance as the state religion under King Pŏphŭng. The play depicts the intense opposition Ich'adon faced, accusations of treason, and the personal conflicts involving the King, a princess, and his fiancée, Tallim. Ultimately, Ich'adon embraces martyrdom, sacrificing himself for the establishment of Buddhism in Silla, culminating in the miraculous signs accompanying his execution.

From a historical perspective, the publication of this play in 1942 under Japanese colonial rule is significant. The magazine "大東亞" (Greater East Asia) itself reflects the wartime Japanese imperial project. Presenting a story centered on a Korean historical hero and the establishment of Buddhism (a religion also prominent in Japan) could be interpreted in multiple ways: it might align with Japanese attempts to find common cultural ground or promote certain values, or it could be a subtle assertion of Korean national history and identity through the figure of a martyr who sacrificed himself for his beliefs and, arguably, his nation's spiritual future. The emphasis on sacrifice ("No retreat in the face of battle," "a monk is also a warrior") might also resonate with the militaristic atmosphere of the time, albeit framed within a religious context.

이야기
「요승 이차돈을 버히라」 서랏벌에는 크나큰 민요가 일어났다. 개와 짱을 든 자, 몽둥이를 휘두루르는 자, 그악을 쓰는자, 실로 간담이 떨리는 판국이었다.
「대가리 털이 없고 몸뚱이에 보재기를 두르고 믁아지에다 구슬껜 줄을 걸고 손에 문꼬리 달린 집행이를 집흔 독개비의 화상 이차돈을 즉이라」
「한올거울숲(天鏡林)을 비어 생여도가 같은 절을 지으랴고 무엄히 왕명을 빙자한 이차돈을 참하라」
「고구려 안장왕(安藏王)의 목을 비러갔다는 놈이 적국에 항복하고 창자를 바꾸어 조국 신라에 염탐군으로 새여들어온 대역사죄 이차돈을 버히라」

이때 궐내 공주궁에서는 평양공주가 이차돈의게 매달리어
「서방님 이몸을 어쩌시랴오. 죽이시랴오, 살리시랴오, 이 몸이 편심에 한번 먹은 마음 천지가 두동강이 나기로 변할 줄 아오. 이 몸을 걷우시오. 싫다시면 이칼로 이몸의 목슴을 걷우시오」하였다.
공주가 이런가하면 협문밖에는 조부령 이사부(調府令 異斯夫)의 딸 달님이 눈물이 가득한 눈으로 서있었다. 토함산과 한울의 무수한 별을 걸어서 맹서한 색씨다.
「서방님, 공주마마와 혼인 해주시겨오. 태자도 되시고 나라도 맡으시겨오.」
달님이 또한 이런가하면 초불이 침침한 자신전에서는 법흥왕의 무거운 한숨이 여러번이었다.
「이차돈아. 이 나라를 맡으라. 부마가 되라. 동궁이 되라.」
이 눈치를 알어낸 알공(謁恭)과 공목(工目)은 임금의 아오 선마로(立宗)를 꼬득이어 딴전을 채리랴하였다.
「상감마마. 국력을 높이하소서. 성난 백성의 맘을 가라앉치소서. 불도는 사도오이다. 외도오이다. 벽도오이다.」

달님뒤에 공주, 공주뒤에 법흥왕, 왕뒤에 십이 영문군사, 군사뒤에 부처님 - 이차돈의 맘은 허궁에서 버정거렸다.
군사와 백성들의 고함소리는 높아갔다. 공목과 알공의 기습은 낙출없이 명중되었다. 이차돈은 행길로 내다랐다.
「이 사람들아, 이 불상한 신라의 중생들아. 오탁과 말법을 버서나라. 인과응보의 이치를 배우라. 이 몸은＜162＞ 죽을지라도 이몸의 업보만은 영겁에 살아서 삼계육도(三界六道)에 태여나는 이치가 두렵지 않으냐.」
「불도는 귀신의 주문이 아니요. 박수나 무당이 섬기는 고신도가 아니오. 화랑도의 오게(五戒)와 부처님의 자비(慈悲)를 하나로 합치고자함이오.」
이 말이 떨어지자 난데없는 돌팔매가 날러들어 이차돈의 이마를 맞추었다. 궐내로 돌아온 이차돈은 왕 앞에 부복하였다.
「상감마마 목숨을 두었다가 어따가 쓰오리까. 억만겁을 지나도 걷우지 못할 중생의 죄악. 그것을 제도하옵는 길에 이 몸을 죽이랴오. 다섯가지 가르침에도 림전무퇴(臨戰無退)라 하였오. 불도도 싸움이오 중도 호반이오. 맘의 싸움 부처님과 악마와의 싸움이오. 이 몸이 죽어서 신라국법에 불법이 오르고 서벌여섯마을에 기록한 법종(梵鍾)이 울린다 하오면 이렇게 목슴을 내버리는 것이 중된 보내오.」
이차돈은 군사속에 뛰어들었다. 드디어 이차돈의 목이 대뜰 우에 딩굴었다. 목에서는 흰젖이 솟고 허궁에선 하눌꽃이 나렸다. ＜163＞
