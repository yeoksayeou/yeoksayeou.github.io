Poem on the Deep Night of February 15th
						Noritake Mitsuo

Singapore has fallen!
Singapore has finally fallen!
On this day, this evening
My child, that's not the Singer sewing machine ball
It's a far, far away island
An island where blue waves make white waves
An island where there are jungles, elephants, and tigers
There, the roundworm scum (蛔蟲共) had built a fortress and were nesting
Yes, those long pink worms
We have completely exterminated them

Friend, think of the glory!
Manila, Hong Kong, Singapore, Borneo, Burma, New Britain, <35>
All were fought for and taken
All have returned (歸) to our hands
This time (秋) of glory
On this day, this evening, I can hear your voice
On this day, this evening, our East Asia is coming into being

Ah, the sea is blue, the waves white
These East and West Seas, three thousand nautical miles east to west
One thousand nautical miles north to south
Some friends went off to fight
Some friends fought and offered their lives to the Emperor
East Asia is built upon noble sacrifice (碧血)
East Asia is constructed with the poured blood of the gods (神神)
Who would not pledge an oath anew?

Ah, the islands where the sea is blue and the waves white
My child, sleep
Sleep, and together with your father, become a sea bird and soar to those islands
My child, be strong (健)
Our country (邦) is a country of the sea
In my childhood, I sang "We are children of the sea"
Now, the sea is truly our country
Now, the sea is truly our country <36>
<35-36>

**Summary**

This poem by Noritake Mitsuo celebrates the Japanese capture of Singapore on February 15, 1942. It portrays the victory as a glorious step in creating "Our East Asia," listing other conquered territories. The poem uses dehumanizing language ("roundworm scum") for the defeated enemy and emphasizes themes of national destiny, sacrifice for the Emperor, divine blessing, and Japan's identity as a maritime power. For historians, this piece is a stark example of wartime propaganda, illustrating the imperialistic ideology, militaristic fervor, and justification for expansion promoted by Japan during the Pacific War, framing conquest as liberation and national destiny. It clearly reflects the mindset behind the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere concept.

						則武三雄
シシガポール陷(お)つと
シシガポールが遂に陷つと
この日このタ
わが子よ それはシンガーミシンヤボールではない
それは遠い遠い島なのな
蒼い波が自波をたてる島
ジアングルガあつて象や虎のゐる島
そこに城砦築を蛔蟲共が巢食つてゐた
さうな桃色をし長こ蟲
それをすつかり退治したのた

友よ想へ榮光を
マニラ、香港シンガポール、ポルネオ、ビルマ、ニユープリテン、＜35＞
すべてはたたかひとられ
すべてはわが手に歸(き)した
いの榮光の秋(とき)
いの日いの夕僕には君の聲が聽える
いの日いの夕 われらの東亞が成つてゆくと

ああ 海蒼く波しろき
これら東西三千カイリ
南北一千カイリの東西海
ある友は戰ひにつき
ある友は戰つて大君に身を捧けしと
碧血によりて東亞は成る
神神の血が注がれて東亞は築かる
誰かまた誓はさるべさ

ああ 海蒼く波しろき島島
吾子よ 眼れ
睡りて父と俱に海烏となりてかの島島に飛翔せよかし
わが子よ 健(すこ)かれ
われらが邦(くに)は海の國
われは海の子と幼なき日は詠ひしを
今こそ海がわれらの國
今こそ海がわれらの國＜36＞
