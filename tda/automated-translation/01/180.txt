Women's Armament
Kim Hwallan, President of Ewha Women's College

Since the China Incident, we women, as those guarding the home front, have contributed what little strength we had. Sending off deployed soldiers, comforting bereaved families, or making defense donations, etc., were countless examples, but since the Great Edict (大詔) was graciously promulgated (渙發) on December 8th last, the incident up to now became known as the Greater East Asia War, and from the day our loyal and brave army and navy courageously rose up to expel the tyrannical Britain and America from the East, we women too must make a new start with a different meaning than before.
That is, I believe our lives must move one step forward from the passive <94> notion of merely guarding the home front, as we have done until now, and possess the spirit of being warriors on the home front.

Speaking of this Greater East Asia War, it is a great battle unprecedented in human history. People say America's war for the liberation of Black slaves and the Crusades that once stirred the blood of Europeans were great, but this Greater East Asia War surpasses them, certainly not falling short. Why is that? Because it is a sacred war to save all Orientals from the infringement of the Anglo-Saxon race. For over 300 years, they advanced from West Asia to East Asia, taking advantage of their superior weaponry, gradually extending their power, swallowing up more than half of our East. How can we watch this happen? How long can we leave it in their wicked hands? The path for Righteous Japan to advance is decided. The battle for morality, for the one billion people of the East, has finally begun.

Comparing Eastern civilization and Western civilization, the East has a civilization of morality (道義), while the West has a so-called material civilization dominated by materials and skills. There were times when our eyes were dazzled by their external luxury, and envying their lives led us to almost worship them. Citing their inhuman actions, lacking morality: of the world's population of about 2 billion, we Orientals in Asia alone exceed half, over 1 billion. The treasures of the East, even if managed solely by the hands of Orientals, would still not feel abundant, yet the hordes of America and Britain invade, swagger before our faces, and deceive the ignorant residents of the Southern regions (南邦) to pursue profit, which is an act entirely departing from morality. <95>

Our country, by Heaven's will, sees its population increase and prosper daily. However, our land area is insufficient to accommodate us all. If our country, like those immoral nations, had disregarded human will and tried to seize anywhere as its own by force of arms, it would not have been impossible in the last 30 years. Truly, seeing our nation's prosperous destiny, if we were to challenge, there would be no fearsome enemy in the world. But our country is a nation of morality. This can be understood by considering the course of events leading up to this Sacred War. Our country, trying to resolve matters peacefully to the very end, repeated efforts with patience upon patience, truly to the point of tears. But how could our sincerity reach those whose eyes are blinded by greed and do not know morality?

Our country has resolutely risen up. If their political and military actions in the East thus far were artificial aggressions ignoring Heaven's will and human justice, how could we endure any further? The East cannot serve as their colony for pleasure. We possess nothing that falls to them to the point where we must submit and follow, leaving things to their freedom.

Thousands of years of history tell us. The spirit of morality from our ancestors has been silently, unknowingly transmitted into our bodies. When our nation rises with its true strength, what is there to fear? Victory always rests upon the head of justice. Look at the attitude those British and Americans took towards our ally Germany. After the last Great European War, they exhausted every means to take everything from Germany, making it impossible for them to ever stand up again. But Germany did not lose the German spirit, endured hardship for over 20 years, and as a result, built the strong Germany of today. What is there to fear? Those British and American people, based solely on appearances, harbored arrogant feelings of having completely dominated the world. But the unprecedented Sacred War to rescue the one billion people of the East from their hands has begun.

How can we express the truly incomparable glory of being able to fight on that home front? We can only weep with gratitude for being subjects of the Emperor's nation. How can we transform that state of mind into service to the country? I believe it lies primarily in spiritual armament. That is, offering our souls and burning sincerity. Some people say that we women are useless because we cannot stand on the front lines like men. However, the fact that we are behind the front lines gives immeasurable encouragement to the officers and soldiers on the front lines. The single phrase spoken by a mainland Japanese woman to her departing son or husband, "Please <96> offer your life in service for His Majesty. Don't worry about home at all..." is an irreplaceable word of comfort to the advancing soldiers. It is the source that inspires their hearts. "We will firmly guard things here," that spirit must become the spirit of us guarding the home front. As the song "Umi Yukaba" (海ゆかば) says, "大君のへにこそ死なめかへりみはせじ" (For our Great Lord we shall die, we shall not look back) – although there may be a difference in location between the front line and the home front, it is the red heart (赤心) that everyone, as subjects of the Emperor's nation, must possess.

Without distinction between rich and poor, pouring all one's sincerity and devoting oneself entirely is the armament of us women. Service that is merely external, like conserving materials, making donations, or offering money, without this spirit, feels like a doll that has the form of a human but lacks a soul. We must offer our entire lives, gladly accept any suffering, and proceed with enduring, persistent service.

In this Sacred War, a battle for morality and the liberation of the people of the East, and a Great War in which the 100 million become one and advance, surely victory will be obtained. Let us have confidence and offer our hearts and souls entirely to the nation. Shall we not strive further with the joy of being born in this sacred era and the glory of the privilege of being Imperial subjects? (Applause) <97>

Let's Awaken from Deluded Dreams
Im Hyojŏng

I believe that those who have come to this place today, being the most discerning and quickest in critical judgment, surely know well what kind of country our Japanese Empire is currently fighting against and how.

Reading the newspapers delivered twice daily and listening to the news broadcast several times a day, it is perhaps not unreasonable for you to pride yourselves on understanding this situation better than anyone. However, in my view, you still seem to be wandering in a hazy dream.

Outside, the weather changed overnight, thunder roared, lightning struck, rain poured down, sewers overflowed, and walls collapsed, causing chaos, but because your rooms had the shutters tightly closed and the black curtains drawn long, you just thought it was the middle of the night and slept soundly.

The wealthy are obscured by money, the ignorant by lack of knowledge, and the so-called intellectuals are conversely hindered, failing to properly recognize this situation and wandering in deluded dreams.

If you had awakened from your deluded dreams, why is there hoarding (買占)? Why are you not buying bonds? Why are you not cooperating with the 600 million [yen] savings goal? Why are you not sending your sons out as volunteer soldiers?

When I meet women, they complain that there are no rubber shoes, no cotton cloth, no oil.

Yet, hearing that taxes would be imposed on silk and cosmetics from December 1st, people formed long queues stretching out from department stores and fabric merchants (布木 商門), with stories of one person buying 100 won worth of cosmetics or cutting 2,000-3,000 won worth of silk – what kind of disgraceful behavior is this? Doesn't a person with the financial capacity to buy 100 won worth of cosmetics have the ability to buy 1 or 2 won worth after the tax is applied?

And in this time of war, why do you need such luxurious clothes that you buy thousands of won worth?

If you knew what wartime life <98> is like in other countries, you wouldn't stage such disgraceful scenes as mentioned above. Let's briefly look at the state of wartime life in other countries.

Germany does not have a life where one person can own as many clothes as they want like in our country; a regulation limiting ownership to a certain number of items per person has been implemented, and food rationing is also very strict.

They had prepared everything before the war began, and as soon as the war with Poland started, they immediately implemented rationing. Compared to before the war, the rations were reduced to 43% for meat per person, 40% for fats, and 20% for sugar. Notably, beer (삐루), which they drink constantly like our tea, is not distributed to homes at all, and one can only get a single glass (곱뿌) at a restaurant, and even that is said to be watered down. Despite receiving such considerably restrictive rations, the German people, who well recognize the gravity of the situation, have no complaints or dissatisfaction, do not engage in black market dealings (闇取引), and commit no acts violating controls, so the government is steadily storing food according to plan.

Britain, compared to Germany's strong wartime life, is quite miserable. Britain can originally only survive for three and a half months on the food produced within its own borders; the remaining eight and a half months' worth must be brought from abroad.

Britain also implemented rationing immediately upon the start of the war with Germany, but due to Germany's strict naval blockade, they couldn't bring in food from abroad and fell into truly difficult circumstances. To give examples, bacon (빼콘) decreased by 70%, butter (パタ) by 50%, eggs by 70% (one egg costs 50 chŏn), and paper (죵이) by 60%, representing a 50-60% reduction compared to before the war. Among these, meat is in an even more indescribable situation; they give three slices of beefsteak (삐푸테익) per person per week, very small and thin ones at that, and since three days a week are designated as meatless days, it's as if there's no meat at all.

And the misery of wartime life in France is beyond description. Due to lack of time, I cannot speak in detail, but the famine problem in France is not just a French problem but has become a global issue.

Looking just at this, what else could be the cause if not the failure to properly observe the various internal and external situations until now?

Also, among you, there seem to be many who still do not well understand the reason and significance of why we must save and why we must buy bonds.

If you understood the meaning, how can a person who doesn't even buy a single 1-won bond not feel regretful spending money wandering the streets aimlessly or watching movies?

Everyone! War truly requires a lot of money. <99>

Before the Greater East Asia War began, from the start of the Japan-China Incident up to the budget of Shōwa 16 (1941), the war expenses amounted to over 22.3 billion yen. During the Russo-Japanese War, 1.7 billion yen was spent. Just looking at the money spent so far, isn't it an enormous figure? However, now that the Greater East Asia War has unfolded, over 22.3 billion yen is not even an issue.

This war involves construction on one hand while fighting on the other, so expanding production also costs a tremendous amount of money.

Where will this vast sum of money come from, and who will provide it? This is covered by the absorption of national bonds (國債消化) that the 100 million citizens must buy and by savings. If the bonds are not absorbed as determined by the state, and savings do not meet the budget, what will be the result? Truly frightening consequences will follow. At that point, not only will the war be impossible to continue, but the people's livelihood will face ruin.

What does this mean? Let's assume there is a merchant engaged in weaving. If someone who used to weave 1,000 bolts of cotton cloth with 10,000 won and put them on the market now weaves with 5,000 won, they can only make half, 500 bolts. But the consumers remain the same.

With fewer goods and many consumers, it's a set principle that prices will jump.

This can be well understood by looking at the example of Germany during the European War.

One mark, corresponding to one Japanese yen, rose to 100 marks, 1,000 marks, and eventually reached 1 billion marks. So, someone who took out a life insurance policy before the war found that when it matured and they collected the payout, it was only enough for tram fare. This is called malignant inflation (악성임풀레) resulting from currency expansion (통화팽창).

If you are negligent in saving and absorbing bonds, your one million won or ten thousand won might become worth only ten thousand won or one thousand won – frightening consequences will result.

Everyone! Let us rightly awaken from our deluded dreams. Let's wake up from the sleep where we kept the shutters tightly closed, drew the black curtains long, unaware if time was passing or coming. Our Japan resolutely rose up at dawn on the 8th last to strike down the public enemies of mankind, America and Britain, and secure the peace of Greater East Asia.

Our army, navy, and air force, having opened fire, are braving the rough waves of the 10,000-ri Pacific and achieving brilliant military exploits.

The final victory of righteous Japan is already certain. But, but I say. If we, the 10 million women of the peninsula, do not awaken, victory will be delayed. If you do not mobilize as warriors in this Greater East Asia War, our victory will be delayed, I say.

Everyone! You long for the war to end quickly and desire peace, yet why do you not cooperate?

Peace never comes overnight or in an instant. Peace comes after many <100> sacrifices and great compromises.

Peace between nations is absolutely impossible without real power. In other words, it is absolutely impossible without military and economic power sufficient to break the enemy's will to fight. Our Japan fights Britain and America precisely to establish eternal peace in this Asia. Asia belongs to Asians. This is not a matter of race and geography, but a matter of politics and "morality." For the purification of Asia, a chemical arrangement is needed rather than just a physical one. The harmony of all Asian peoples – if such a compound is formed, what brilliant radiance it will emit!

Everyone! I cry out to the 12 million women of the peninsula. I especially cry out to women who are mothers. Let us resolve to courageously go forth into the war for the future of your sons and daughters.

Without the blood of our 24 million mingling in this Greater East Asia War, our happiness can absolutely not be guaranteed.

Let us shed our blood in this war. Let us offer our bodies as sacrifices.

As loyal subjects (赤子) of His Majesty the Emperor, let us offer everything we possess.

With the conviction that our Japan will surely win and the belief that the future of our descendants will surely be happy, let us advance under the decisive battle system. <101>

New Order of the Home
Im Sukchae (P'ungch'ŏn Sukchae), Professor at Sookmyung Women's College

Our Imperial nation is now truly faced with carrying out the unprecedented Great Sacred War for the eternal peace of the world, for the liberation of the Asian peoples, and for propagating the great spirit of our nation, Hakko Ichiu (八紘一宇 - Eight Corners of the World Under One Roof), throughout the four seas. The 100 million citizens, as people living within our national territory, have reached a time when they must risk their lives to face this crisis, exerting the full strength of their bodies and families to fulfill their sincere duty of martyrdom for the country.

No matter the objectives under which the state and society formulate plans, the place where they are executed is the home, and its operation depends on the work of the housewife; therefore, great power lies hidden in the driving force of women.

The housewife is the center of family life, so at this time, she must establish and execute a new order for family life. The new order aims to break through the national crisis through family life, mobilizing the entire family's total strength to achieve victory in the ideological and economic wars, with both spiritual and material aspects of life being sound. This requires strong determination and constant effort. What does the state demand from the family? First, it demands materials, money, and sound human resources. Therefore, let our families first become one bundle of mind and strength, further promoting the spirit of pure loyalty and patriotic service (純忠報國), rationalize consumption of even one item or one penny, save goods and conserve, and strive to offer materials needed for military supplies to the military state, even overcoming all difficulties (萬難), and contribute labor service to munitions factories. For example, let's strive not to use metal goods, brass products, woolen items, wooden thread items, etc., in civilian life, so that military supplies can be offered to the country as much as possible. Let's simplify our lives regarding clothing, food, and shelter. First, let's completely abolish white clothes. White clothes are dangerous for protection (防護的) and are also mourning clothes worn during sad times. They are also extremely inconvenient for activity and uneconomical. If our 24 million people thoroughly wear colored clothes, about 150 million won will be saved. With this money, <102> 3,000 airplanes costing 50,000 won each can be purchased. Let's redirect the effort spent on frequent washing and starching of white clothes to effective and productive activities. Let's abolish the chŏgori (jacket) ties (고름) and attach buttons, and change outdoor clothing to simpler tube-like garments (통의). In wartime life requiring urgency, fluttering a long trailing skirt (꼬리치마) is not good for etiquette or activity. Even for women's health and sound national life, the trailing skirt is not good. Furthermore, in this time when we must live a wartime life, wearing expensive clothes, getting permanents (파-마넨트), and applying heavy makeup should be considered shameful. We should strive to make good use of existing clothes stored in chests and avoid making new clothes as much as possible. When unavoidably making new clothes, let's learn skillful ways to make spun rayon fabric (스푸직물) sturdy and long-lasting.

Regarding food, let's focus on nutrition sufficient to maintain health and gain energy for activity, abandoning excessive fine dining (美食) and complex formalities. Let's deeply reflect on personal warm clothing and fine dining, get accustomed to saving rice (節米), substitute foods (代用食), and mixed grain rice (잡곡반), and encourage these practices. Also, let's reduce the number or quantity of side dishes and strive not to put amounts beyond necessity on the table. Let's consume inexpensive vegetable protein and other nutrients instead of expensive meat, and refrain from throwing wood scraps into the trash can. The soldiers fighting on the front lines endure sleepless, restless lives in extreme cold and intense heat, sometimes drinking rainwater dripping from tents when even a drop of water is unavailable, and sometimes eating only one piece of hardtack (건빵) every three days. Thinking of the fact that we live peaceful, uneventful lives thanks to their life-risking efforts in desperate battles, how regrettable it is, and how can we express our gratitude?

Now, in the war against America and Britain, the brilliant war results achieved moment by moment are by no means coincidental; I believe they are due to the navy's earnest tactical training repeated over the past 30 years. Considering this, feeling sorry for having lived a comfortable life, can we complain about slight material shortages or say things are inconvenient? Let's rouse ourselves and experience inconvenience. Let's share even two sets of clothes and share even one day's food. Let's completely stop hoarding driven by selfish desires.

Regarding housing, since the home is a place of relaxation and comfort (團樂慰安所) for the family, let's beautify it through cleaning and organizing. Let's rest from fatigue, gain new driving force (原動力), strive for universal labor (皆勞) to increase income, base rural life on self-sufficiency (自作自給), and even in cities, utilize small vacant lots <103>

National Defense Family
Pak Sunch'ŏn

The speakers before me have already said everything I prepared to say, so it will be a rehash (재탕). My apologies to you all. However, like herbal medicine, a rehash probably won't be too harmful, so please bear with me and listen.

The term "wartime" (림전) is a word that has only recently emerged, and I think this word will no longer be necessary when the war ends.

Thanks to the soldiers who have fought, risking their lives without regard for cold nights and hot days for the long period of five years, we have lived safely and peacefully until today, almost unaware of where the war is being fought.

However, the event that always weighed on our minds with unease has finally occurred. On the morning of December 8th, I saw many people as I came to school. Perhaps it was just my feeling, but clearly, the faces of many people showed a tense look (밧이), and their steps seemed a bit quicker than before.

In reality, it must be so. As citizens whose fate depends on the nation, how could we not be tense? However, anxiety exists before things happen; once things have happened, I think anxiety or worry is useless. At this time, I believe we need the courage and defense to discard useless worries, trust in the military power of the Imperial Army (皇軍), and cooperate, superiors and subordinates, government officials and citizens, to overcome this difficult situation smoothly.

Soldiers go to the battlefield to fight the enemy, and we must all fight against the economy in our respective homes.

Now, the concept of a "National Defense Family" (國防家庭) can be clearly recognized and perceived in anyone's home.

Not only in large cities but also in farming, mountain, and fishing villages, there will be no one, young or old, unaware of the war. The wartime atmosphere (림전氣分) manifested in goods, and everything seen in national defense facilities, I think, is more than enough to understand. Regarding goods, the rationing system (배급제도) has emerged. I admire this rationing system as an appropriate <104> system for a nation at war. Some might find the rationing system bothersome, but while it might not matter in peaceful times when one could live freely, if there were no rationing system, no fair price system in this state of emergency, our families would just lose money to opportunistic merchants and gain little, and anxiety and difficulties in life would have been greater. However, I am grateful that we can live with peace of mind thanks to this rationing system. Another thing is the air defense drills (防空訓練). Initially, although we knew air defense drills had to be done, everyone disliked them, and their appearance seemed comical. Women who suddenly took off their skirts were a bit awkward to look at; onlookers laughed, and those being looked at felt embarrassed. But now, our housewives wearing air defense uniforms (防空服) and protecting their homes and neighbors are proudly displaying themselves like soldiers deployed to the front. Also, the general public now understands the concept of "National Defense Family Training" (國防家庭訓練), so there is no awkwardness at all between people; it's convenient, and in case of emergency, thanks to the air defense drills practiced during this time, not only my family but also our neighbors will be safe. No matter when the enemy attacks, our housewives show the spirit (勢氣) and determination to bravely say, "Come if you dare!" When the air defense drills first started, I heard a funny story from a friend's wife who said, "Last night they told me to do 'ichi ni' (one two - Japanese), and today they told me to do 'kocchi ni' (this way - Japanese)." A few days ago (數日前), I watched the air defense drill of some schoolgirls. Seeing their agile and disciplined training, I truly felt reassured. I was impressed by how rapidly they had progressed in the meantime. The power of teaching, the power of all guidance – everything speaks of this emergency situation. Now let us make use of this time.

In the National Defense Family, let's discard old habits (루습) and bad customs (페풍) and bring about a revolution (흑명 - likely a typo for 혁명) in our traditional way of life.

First, let's fix the habit of sleeping late. Let's save the sleep time spent sleeping until ten or eleven o'clock and make it six in the morning. In my house, at ten o'clock, all twelve family members get up at once, bustling about, dusting, so even those who want to sleep more can't lie down and have to get up. And let's have meals together at the same time, regardless of status. Maids (식모), seamstresses (침모), everyone together, let's not discriminate between people even during meal times. This is the first thing to adopt in a National Defense Family. If meals are not finished, efficiency or orderliness in work cannot be achieved. Then other household chores can also be easily organized.

Another thing is, let all family members do their own work with their own hands.

Don't rely on the maid for room cleaning (방소제), or washing water (세수물); let's cultivate the habit of everyone doing their own work. In educating our children, <105> of course, we must cultivate the habit of doing their own work. For one's own health, moderate labor is necessary. Because people only make others work and comparatively eat rich foods, obesity occurs, indigestion occurs, and many illnesses requiring bed rest arise. Let's adopt a method where there's no time to lie down even if you want to, and no leisure to die even if you want to die. Work busily and quickly. And if the husband has a habit of sleeping late, let the wife walk around dusting with a duster (몬지털이). If he complains, tell him such habits are unacceptable in an emergency. If that doesn't work, tell the Patriotic Neighborhood Unit leader (愛國班長) so the leader can go and dust him with a duster. And husbands too, please get into the habit of finding your own clothes and making your own bed (이부자리).

If you don't fix these habits during this time, you'll never fix them until you die.

Another thing is clothing. Let's wear what we have as much as possible until things settle down. Let's make do with worn clothes (빠러 입은 옷) instead of the previous practice of making new ones (新造業). And I hope there will be prudent self-restraint in self-aware households regarding unusual makeup or splendid clothing.

Things have changed astonishingly between before the incident and after the incident. Simple and plain makeup, in many aspects, is changing day by day. However, occasionally one still sees regrettable attire. Such people will be criticized <106> by others as lacking common sense, like someone wailing at a feast or laughing and enjoying themselves at a funeral. I urge self-restraint as soon as possible. Also, please conserve food (식양 - likely 식량), and save the seaweed soup and rice superstitiously thrown away on the streets, and eat it instead. It's always a very unsightly and wasteful thing. If you knew how many drops of sweat from farmers are intertwined in a single grain of rice, if you thought of people who cannot eat in the severe cold, you would fear heaven and dare not do such a thing.

Now, to briefly summarize what I have said: first, simplify the entire lifestyle; second, conserve food; third, utilize all materials in each household. And always be vigilant and be mentally prepared. Even if you have to run out in the middle of the night, keep your wits about you to know where valuables (과물 - likely valuables/important items) and fire are. Don't act rashly or thoughtlessly. Even if fire falls, there is time before it catches, so calmly and composedly fulfill the mission of the National Defense Family. What is the fundamental spirit of the 5-year Sacred War? Its purpose is the construction of Greater East Asia, aiming to restore the human rights and national territory lost by neighboring countries and achieve co-existence and co-prosperity for peace in the East. Since it is for justice, defeat is impossible, right? Be calm and self-composed, diligently protect the National Defense Family, and let us pray and wait for eternal peace. Human life is war. The family is also war. All humans are soldiers. -End- <107>

Awakening of Home Front Women
Hŏ Habŏk

As you all know well, our country, while the China Incident that has continued for the past four years has not yet ended, has found itself having to fight against the two nations of America and Britain. The reason, as you also know well, is that Britain and America possess not only vast territories relative to their populations globally but also abundant resources. Therefore, even though they wouldn't need to extend their hands to the East, their policy of self-interest and personal gain (私利私腹), derived from their extreme individualistic ideology, has repeatedly inflicted severe oppression upon us, the yellow race.

Consequently, our Invincible Imperial Army, firmly making the grave decision that this situation could not continue, raised the banner of the Sacred War for the construction of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere and opened fire on the two nations of America and Britain on December 8th last.

Since then, as I believe you know well through newspapers or radio news, our Imperial Army is annihilating enemy forces wherever they go and achieving brilliant war results.

In this situation, believing that we women on the home front also have a great mission and cannot just sit idly by, even someone as inadequate as myself has come to stand here as one of the speakers.

Of course, we have made significant efforts on the home front in the past, but this time, it's not about such lukewarm thoughts; we need the resolve to produce the most active and practical results.

Now, I would like to briefly tell you what we should do.

I. Sound Ideology
First, we must possess sound ideology. In this regard, we must first strengthen our reverence for the gods and respect for the Emperor (敬神尊皇). Paying homage to the Imperial Palace from afar (宮城遙拜) and visiting shrines (神社參拜) are givens, as is bowing when passing a shrine, and practicing the Patriotic Day (愛國日), etc. Through these actions, <108> we must demonstrate the spirit of Japanese-Korean unity (內鮮一體).

We must discard the individualistic thinking of the past and grasp the statist ideology that everything is solely for the nation. To give two or three concrete examples: even when we eat, it should not be merely for sustaining our individual lives, but under the thought that by eating this, we gain strength to become people who work for the nation.

Also, when sending children to school, the devoted spirit (헌신적 정신) of striving to raise them well to become useful individuals for the nation is the Japanese spirit (日本精神). If one lacks this thought, one cannot be called a true subject of the Emperor's nation. This ideology of Nation First (國家第一主義) is the sound ideology, and only when each citizen possesses this ideology uniformly can we become one heart and brilliantly win this war.

II. Gratitude and Comfort
When we think of those who throw away their lives fighting on the front lines, should we just endure their hardships? We should not complain about receiving slightly smaller rations. Isn't the reason we live so happily today thanks to the brave soldiers fighting on the front lines without regard for life or death? When thinking about this, one truly cannot help but shed tears. Therefore, instead of complaining, we should always adopt an attitude of gratitude, and more gratitude.

However, gratitude alone is insufficient. ① Send gifts to deployed soldiers to console them. ② For soldiers honorably wounded in battle, provide material support so they can receive treatment as quickly as possible and walk the path of rehabilitation (更生). ③ For the bereaved families of soldiers killed in action, help them materially and spiritually (物心兩力) and share their joys and sorrows.

III. Resource Conservation (Consumption Saving)
Consumption saving must first start with the things extremely close at hand in our daily lives. Even a single radish or a single potato should not be treated carelessly. Without discarding even the smallest things, we must cherish all materials.

It is said that victory in modern warfare is achieved only through economic warfare (經濟戰) and total war (總力戰). Knowing this, no matter how insignificant an item may seem, its use must be carefully considered. If such an attitude is lacking, one could be seen as having lost the qualification to be a citizen of a modern nation.

What I particularly want to emphasize here is that despite the luxury ban ordinance (사치품금지령) being issued last year, seeing women still nonchalantly wearing rings (指環) and strolling through intersections <109> is truly pathetic.

I implore you, if you have the self-awareness of being a home front woman, please eradicate all such luxury at this time.

IV. Waste Utilization and Recycling
This point, I believe, requires particular attention in our Peninsula households. Speaking in connection with the previous issue, first, ensure that waste items (폐품) are not generated; prohibit the use of items related to military supplies; do not hoard by buying up large quantities of goods.

Since waste items are collected by each neighborhood association (各町會) on Patriotic Day every month, if waste items are generated, let's collect them well and strive to utilize them effectively on that occasion.

We must have the determination to endure, no matter how long this war continues, using only the materials produced within our country. I believe this depends on our determination. You might think that Japan is facing difficulties due to lack of materials just because our lives are somewhat inconvenient compared to before the Incident, but that is definitely not the case. Rather, production is said to have increased. The reason is that before the Incident, you lacked the thought of valuing goods, so many things were discarded. But now, you conserve them. Nails, empty cans (간즈메 - canned goods), scraps of paper, etc...
From this too, we can infer how great the power of "unity of heart and mind" (一心團結) is.

V. Diligent Labor Service (勤勞奉仕)
Shamefully, until now, we Peninsula women have taken pride in employing many servants. This deep-rooted traditional thinking was quite strong even among the so-called intellectual class. However, now, at a time when our country is mobilizing the strength of its people and materials nationwide to fight towards the goal of constructing the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere, such vanity cannot be permitted.

Creating a diligent family tradition (勤勞的 家風) lies entirely in the hands of us women, I believe. Putting aside cases where circumstances are inherently unavoidable, entrusting the kitchen to others is, firstly, dangerous for family health, and secondly, causes significant economic loss.

At this time, the housewife must go to the kitchen or the market, and not only that, but the housewife should take on side jobs (內職), and women too should not just be consumers of goods but voluntarily do work that can contribute to livelihood.

In the past, working after establishing a household might have been considered somewhat shameful <110>, but now such notions are unacceptable. As home front women, each bearing a grave mission, we must realize how shameful it is not to work. Therefore, let us take pride in working without idling even for a moment.

VI. Encouraging Savings
As mentioned above, we must prevent all waste in our daily lives and save even 1 chŏn or 10 chŏn every day. There is a proverb, "Even dust, gathered, becomes a mountain," but if only oneself practices this and the neighbors do not, there will be little effect. To give an example, if 10,000 people each save 10 chŏn daily, it becomes 1,000 won. But if one man goes to a restaurant (요리집) and wastes 2,000 won in one night, it amounts to nothing.

Therefore, first, we must practice it ourselves, and then, our neighbors together, so that all citizens nationwide practice it, otherwise, we cannot avoid national debt.

In this regard, let me give an example from our school. On December 8th last, the day war was declared on America and Britain, we donated 2,000 won to the army and navy. Although it was money contributed by both staff and students, it was a huge sum for a girls' school. However, it became a large amount because it was collected together; if each person had spent their share individually, it wouldn't have become a large sum. Students contribute 20 chŏn each month, and staff contribute 1 won deducted from their salaries. Our school has decided to donate 2,000 won like this every year as long as this war continues.

This is just one example. I believe that in each household, or in the patriotic neighborhood units, the largest amount of savings can be achieved depending on the determination of the women.

I sincerely hope that each of you will put this into practice. <111>

Women Too Are Warriors
Mo Yunsuk

This is the first time that Pumin Hall (府民館) in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) has been built and so many women of the Peninsula have gathered like this. Just seeing this shows that today is indeed a time when some unusual situation has arisen, not only for men but also for women. If women just raised children at home and respected their parents-in-law well, it might seem their duties were fulfilled, but now we gather in one place, saying "let's do this, let's do that," and new precepts for life, unseen before, are constantly emerging. What is happening?

The time has come when your quiet world must be shaken overnight. A time has come demanding a transformation of your thoughts, a time when we must shout that your external work has increased.

Our tradition, beautiful for its modesty, considerateness, and shyness, is being broken. Since we cannot accomplish the great task we are now trying to undertake without breaking this, we have no choice but to break down this ivory tower of the past, which was called a virtue. In a way, a great change has occurred for us Peninsula women. A great difficulty has arisen. However, if we think about it again, isn't it because of this change that we Peninsula women born in this era can display our living value? Because we are gathered before the main gate of a new world, no, because we must become the pillars to create a new century, we are happier than the Peninsula women who lived for the past several thousand years. Why is that? Because through human life, there is no joy where there is no suffering, and without undergoing difficult historical processes, the advancement of humankind cannot be seen.

War is the unavoidable labor pain in the process where a nation, trying to ascend the stairs of new life, sheds its old skin and tries to create a new world. It is the expression of humanity's will to live strongly. Our Great Japanese Empire is now <112> burning with this will. It has drawn its sword out of a sense of justice, intending to lead humanity into a righteous world. In a war with such meaning, the entire nation must join hands.

War is not sports. In sports, victory comes when the person who first crosses the finish line, the athlete themselves, reaches the goal. But in war, merely breaking through the enemy's fortress walls cannot predict the final victory. We must realize that if the mothers and wives behind do not pull themselves together and manage the nation's household (살님), the war results achieved through shedding blood and sweat will turn to bubbles (水泡). Our Imperial Army is now destroying (뭇지르고) various islands in the Pacific Ocean. We are a nation possessing excellent combatants fighting on the front lines, better than any other country. We have incomparably brave soldiers who drop their very lives as bombs. However, this bravery is not needed only by the soldiers on the front lines. Everyone who has the blood and bones of a citizen must fight this war with this spirit (氣魂).

War is definitely not waged solely with military power. The country called America is the world's warehouse, storing the best military supplies. It has excellent airplanes and powerful warships, unmatched in the world. Because America felt pride in possessing all the good military equipment materials that other countries couldn't use, decorating them lavishly, we thought its power must be immense. They boast well-developed skills in building warships of tens of thousands of tons and countless airplanes, yet why couldn't they possess even one soldier capable of defending a single small island rising in the Pacific Ocean?

If there had been just one righteous soldier aboard the Prince of Wales (푸린쓰으즈월쓰), that great warship which embodied Great Britain's pride would not have suffered the ridicule of the great waves it navigated. Their hollow military power, hollow wealth, powerless culture – none of it is enviable.

Inside those supposedly magnificent enemy warships, there must have been warehouses of hundred-year-old liquor, and in each soldier's bedroom, the dreams of weary romance must have remained entangled. While they were engineering their self-destruction based on individualism, hedonism, egoism – a biological view of life – in a corner (모통이) of Asia, we raised the torch of totalitarianism and set out to rescue the yellow races groaning under their ideology or their politics. The land of spices, the land of music, the land of movies, the land of youth, the land of spirit, the land of money – with all these conditions, how they tempted the young men and women of our East! No, how much did we Peninsula women yearn for a country equipped with all those conditions?

Hearing and learning about the crimes of Britain and America this time, there are truly many outrageous and infuriating things unbearable for the yellow race. <113> There is one woman in the East dancing together with this Satanic figure. She is none other than Chiang Kai-shek's (장개석) wife, Soong Mei-ling (송미령). This woman, somehow, despite being an Eastern woman, is a deviant female who feels happiness only by licking the soles of America's feet. Steeped in all of America's hedonism and individualistic notions, this woman's life is unspeakably pleasure-seeking. Would you be surprised if I told you that one pair of Soong Mei-ling's stockings costs 70 won? Traveling back and forth to America, she gathers all sorts of depraved ideas and whispers them into her husband Chiang Kai-shek's head. The foolish and dull Chiang Kai-shek, his ears perked (솔깃해서) by Soong Mei-ling's words, repeats his prolonged groans in a state of dilemma (進退維谷).

It is lamentable for fellow Eastern women that such a woman exists in the East, muddying the situation. However, we must look back at ourselves more than at others, and hold the formless sword of the Yamato spirit (大和魂) in our hearts.

The ladder of flawed culture – hedonism, individualism – that was spreading on this Earth is collapsing, one step, two steps at a time. The power to liquidate this flawed spirit must originate from Greater Asia, from the Great Japanese Empire, from the people behind the fight, from mothers, from wives. Men's strength alone is not enough. Mothers-in-law, stop playing your role; young mistresses and brides, stop playing yours; let's gladly carry even manure buckets (거름통) and go out. Victory does not depend on the army; the victory in this new East Asian war depends on us women. The reasons were stated by the speakers earlier (앗가).

The notion that men produce and women consume belongs to leisurely peacetime. But now, women, young mistresses, madams, nobles (양반), commoners (상인) – regardless of family, status, or lineage – are all equal citizens of the Great Japanese Empire, and that's enough. Let's become wives and daughters-in-law who are not expelled from the nation, even if expelled from the family. If factories are empty, let's go to the factories; if companies are empty, let's enter the companies and work. According to a report on wartime women by Gertrude Bäumer (켄트루-드바우멜), a German woman journalist during World War I and also famous, in one factory, a single woman produced thirty-five or six heavy bombs weighing 80 pounds (封度) each day.

If we try, what can't we do? Who can stop someone from sacrificing their life for righteousness (義)? If there is conviction, what is impossible? Let's all work. Let's study aviation and how to operate submarines (잠항정). It's not fathers but mothers who forbid studying aviation or submarines. Don't forbid it (말니지 마십시오). If they want to do it, leave them alone (내버려두십시오). For the national emergency, not out of curiosity or vanity, female aviators are also necessary. <114> If any mother prevents this, that mother is only the mother of her one daughter, not a mother of the nation. It amounts only to hindering the war that much. In ancient France, one maiden of Orléans (오루레안 - Joan of Arc) shed her blood (흘여) to save the country. During the Crusades, only one woman, Nightingale (나이징겔), went to the front lines (戰線) and showed merit (功). However, in our war today, one Joan of Arc, one Nightingale is far too insufficient. All of you sitting here (안진), no, all 12 million of the Peninsula, must weep with the fervent patriotism of the Maiden of Orléans, and all 12 million of the Peninsula must embody the passionate female spirit of Nightingale and go out to the battlefield.

As we build the tower of new Asia, let's bring even a handful of earth to add, draw even a pail of water to pour, and gather under the high-fluttering (펄넉이는) Rising Sun flag (日章旗). Let's save rice, wood, clothes – everything. Let's save them for the nation. But let us not save our lives for the nation. After offering the lives of sons, the lives of husbands, if even we women are called out, let's exchange our lives for bombs and throw them usefully onto the battlefield. From now on, let's not be slaves to happiness, but creators who create happiness. Let's thus escape the motto of Great Britain's women, "Woman, thy name is frailty," and leave behind for human society the new immortal phrase, "Strong women come from Asia." (Applause) <115>

Mothers of the Empire
Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi

I cannot speak well. Especially speeches, I'm even worse at them. So I won't give a speech, but I'll tell a short story and sit down. I am a tender-hearted woman who cannot even kill a fly. When loving flowers, I cannot love the large, fluttering (너불너불) ones, but love insignificant flowers like wild chrysanthemums that would be swept away (쓸어질) by a single harsh morning (아츰) frost. It's not that I hate large, fluttering flowers. It's because they are beyond my strength. Even looking at (보드래도) this proves that I am a possessor of a weak heart.

However, these days (요새와서) I am trying to become stronger and stronger. I am trying to become a woman who is not weak at all. I strive to possess the courage, confidence, and conviction to swallow fire and ably receive bullets shot at me.

The motive that made me strive to possess such courage, confidence, and conviction lies not in the China Incident, nor in the Japan-US-Britain War, nor in my mother's teaching, nor in any teacher's persuasion. It is my child. My ten-year-old child attending the third grade of National School (국민학교).

This child, even when reluctant to get up (이러나기) from bed in the morning, as soon as the siren for paying homage to the Imperial Palace from afar (宮城遙拜) sounds, jumps up like lightning, faces east, and stands solemnly bowing his head (허리를 궆이고 섬니다). Also, before newspapers carrying the revered portraits (尊影) of His Majesty the Emperor or other such high personages, he invariably adjusts (곤치고) his posture and bows his head. Also, when watching war news at the newsreel theater, if a scene appears where the Imperial Army shouts "Banzai!" (만세), he shouts "Banzai!" together (가치) with them. Also, if perhaps a high personage appears (나타니시면) on the screen, he not only takes off his hat but also stands up straight.

But at such times, the child doesn't just do it alone, but always asks, "Mom, why aren't you doing it? Let's do it together, Mom."

It's an honest confession, but for some time at first, I couldn't do as the child said. Suddenly, especially in front of many people <116>, it was difficult to do so. However, I never tried to stop the child's actions. Gradually, I came to follow what the child did. It was because I realized (깨달었기) that the child was finding the right path, the path of light.

One day, the child asked,
"Mom, if I go to war and die, will you cry (울테야)?"
I was bewildered by this sudden question. Even knowing it was the right path, the correct way, he is my only child! What mother doesn't love her child? But for me, he is the only child, irreplaceable even for the whole world. I couldn't help but be startled by the word "die," and my heart couldn't help but sink.
So, for a moment, I could only be dumbfounded.

Then the child, showing a disappointed face, said again, "Mom, you're hopeless (틀렸어)." Indeed, I was a hopeless mother. From the child's words, I reflected on myself again. So, holding the child who was dissatisfied with his hopeless mother, I replied,
"I won't cry. Why would I cry when you die doing a good thing?"
The child, perhaps excited (났은지) by mother's answer,
Said, "Alright (ヨ-シ)! Then Mom, sing me the song 'Wagako yo tsuyokatta' (吾が子よ 强かつた - My child, you were strong)!" and this time, made a gesture (신늉) of rolling up his sleeves as if he would run off to the battlefield right away.

At this moment, thinking of nothing, forgetting everything, I hugged the child even tighter,
And showed the child a truly radiant face, just like his, saying, "Just sing? Mom will dance if you go to war and die."

This story sounds entirely like boasting about my child, but I absolutely do not see it as ending (끈친다고) with just my child's story. I believe this story of my child, our child, is also the story of the children of all of you who came (오신) here. I believe all your sons hold such precious thoughts. I believe all your children possess such high spirit and mettle, capable of reaching the heavens.

In the past, we lived (살어왔읍니다) for a long time forgetting such high spirit and mettle. We lived for a long time burying (파몯고) the high and noble idea of living for others, of offering (밭인다는) one's body for the country. But now, our small lives, the second generation (제2세), are trying to nurture new sprouts. Should we break them off (꺽거버려서야)? Should we rub them out (문질러 버려야)? No (안얘요)! Day and night, we must encourage (북도아 주고) and nurture (길러줘야 함니다) them. For them who seek the right path, for them who seek the light, for them who possess a high and noble spirit capable of reaching the heavens, we must encourage (붓도아주고) and nurture them who live with the sole thought (一念) of offering their one body <117> to the country.

Doing so will not only uphold the will of our beloved sons but also, consequently (따러서), uphold the will of God (신), I believe.

Let us forget everything and become mothers who uphold the precious and high will of solely our sons. Let us become women who uphold the will of God. Only then will we fulfill our duty as women like others; only then will we fulfill our duty as mothers like others. When all women in the whole world, under the blue sky, rise up and offer their sons to the country, what is it (무엄니까) that prevents us from offering ours? What are we lacking that we cannot do as others do? Do we have no eyes? Is our nose flattened (문질러젔읍니까)? Possessing (가젔는데) the same eyes, ears, mouth, and nose, what deficiency prevents us? Please, I beg you, become strong mothers who do not hear the words "Mom, you're hopeless" from your sons. I pledge before you that I will do so too. I pledge before God. They say woman is weak, but isn't mother strong? (Applause) <118>

**Summary**

This collection of speeches and essays, sponsored by the Korean Wartime Patriotic Corps and published in March 1942, features prominent Korean women (Kim Hwallan, Im Hyojŏng, Im Sukchae, Pak Sunch'ŏn, Hŏ Habŏk, Mo Yunsuk, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi) fervently endorsing Japan's Greater East Asia War against the US and Britain. They frame the war as a righteous crusade to liberate Asia from Anglo-Saxon oppression and establish a moral "Eastern civilization." The women urge Korean women to move beyond passively guarding the home front to becoming active "warriors," embracing austerity (saving materials, simplifying life, abolishing white clothes), buying war bonds, increasing savings, engaging in labor, utilizing waste, practicing spiritual devotion (Emperor worship, shrine visits), providing comfort to soldiers and families, and raising children with unwavering loyalty, ready to sacrifice their lives for the Empire. They denounce Western materialism and individualism, contrasting it with Japanese/Eastern morality and spirit, and condemn figures like Soong Mei-ling for aligning with the West. The overall message is one of total mobilization, sacrifice, and spiritual unity with Japan for achieving victory.

From a historical perspective, this document is highly significant. It showcases the active collaboration of elite Korean women in promoting Japanese imperial ideology and war mobilization efforts during a critical phase of WWII. The speeches reveal the specific arguments and pressures used to enlist women's support, blending appeals to traditional maternal roles with calls for radical transformation into "warriors" and laborers, emphasizing austerity, spiritual devotion (Shinto/Emperor worship), and the concept of *Na선일체* (Japanese-Korean Unity). The explicit denunciation of Western influence and the framing of the war as Asian liberation provide insight into the propaganda narratives prevalent at the time. Historians would find the specific examples of wartime life adjustments (rationing, air drills, clothing changes), the emphasis on savings and bond purchases, the critique of figures like Soong Mei-ling, and the raw emotional appeals (especially Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi's piece about her son) valuable for understanding the complexities of colonial collaboration, gender roles under imperialism, and the lived experience and ideological landscape of wartime Korea.

女性의 武裝
梨花女專校長 金活蘭

支那事變이후 우리 여성은 銃後를 지키는 자로서 미력이나마 다하여왔습니다. 출정군인의 賤送이며, 유가족의 위문, 혹은 국방헌금 등등, 이로 헤일수 없는 정도였으나, 지난 12월 8일 황송하옵게로 大詔가 渙發되시와 지금까지의 사변은 大東亞戰爭이라는 이름으로 되여 우리 忠勇한 육해군은 저 暴戾한 英米를 동양에서 구축하려고 분연히 이러난 그날부터는 우리돌 여성도 지금까지와는 또다른 의미의 새로운 출발을 하지 않으면 안되게 되였습니다.
즉, 우리들 생활은 지금까지와 같이 銃後를 지키고 있다는 소극적＜94＞ 관념으로부터 한걸음 나아가서 銃後에 있어서의 전사라 하는 기분을 가지고 있어야 할 것이라 생각합니다.
이 大東亞戰爭을 말씀하면, 인류역사에 지금까지 없었던 위대한 싸움입니다. 世人들은 미국의 黑奴解放의 전쟁과, 옛날 歐羅巴人의 피를 끓게한 十字軍의 싸움을 위대했다고 하지만, 그것에 이기면 이겼지 결코 떨어지지 않는 것이 今次의 大東亞戰爭입니다. 웨 그런고 하니 동양인 전부를 저 앵글로 색손 인종의 침해로부터 구하려하는 거룩한 전쟁이기 때문입니다. 그들은 300여 년 간 서쪽 亞細亞로부터 동쪽 亞細亞에까지 진출하여 자기들의 무기의 우수함을 幸으로 여겨 점차 세력을 뻐치어서 우리 동양의 太半을 삼켜버리였습니다. 어찌 이를 보아 버릴 수가 있겠습니까. 어느 때까지든지 그들 악독한 손에 맡겨버리고 있겠습니까. 正義日本의 나아갈 길은 결정되여진 것입니다. 동양 10억 여 민중을 위해 道義의 싸움은 드디여 벌어진 것입니다.
동양의 문명과 서양의 문명과를 비교하여 보면, 동양은 道義의 문명이오 서양은 물질과 技能에 지배된 소위 물질문명입니다. 우리들은 그들의 그 외형적 華奢함에 눈이 현혹되고, 그들의 생활을 부러워한 남아지 그들 자체를 숭배하게 된 일도 없지 않아 있었습니다. 道義를 가지지 못한 그들의 비인간적인 행동을 들며는, 세계의 인구 약 20억 중 우리 동양은 亞細亞洲만도 그 절반의 10억을 넘습니다. 동양의 寶庫는 동양인의 손에 의해서만 경영되여도 아직 풍부를 느끼지 못할 정도임에도 불구하고 그들 米英의 무리들이 침입하여 우리들 얼굴 앞에서 꺼덕대고, 또 무지한 南邦주민을 속여서 이익을 기도함은 전혀 道義를 떠난 행위인 것입니다.＜95＞
우리나라는 天意에 의하여 인구가 매일 증가되고 번창하여 갑니다. 그렇지만 면적은 우리들을 포용하기에 충분치 못합니다. 우리나라가 그들 非道한 나라와 같이 人意를 무시하고 무기에 의해 어디든지 나의 것으로 만들려고 했다면 이 30년래 불가능했을 理는 없습니다. 참으로 융성한 我國運을 睹해서 덤벼든다면 세계에 두려울 적이 없을 것입니다. 그러나 우리나라는 道義의 나라입니다. 今次의 聖戰이 이리나기 까지의 경과를 생각해보아도 알 것입니다. 우리나라는 어디까지든지 平和裡에 해결하려고 그야말로 눈물 겨울만치 인내에 인내를 거듭하여 노력을 거듭했었습니다. 그러나 물욕에 눈이 현혹되여 道義를 아지 못하는 그들에게 어찌 우리의 성의가 통하겠습니까.
우리나라는 단호 분기하였습니다. 그들의 그간 동양에서의 정치, 군사적 행동이 天意와 人義를 무시한 인위적 침략이였다면 어찌 이 이상 참아오겠습니까. 동양은 그들 식민지로서 즐거움이 되어줄 수는 없습니다. 그들에게 굴하고 따라서 그들의 자유에 맡겨버리기까지 그들에게 떨어지는 아무것도 가지지 않았습니다.
수 천년의 역사가 말해주고 있습니다. 우리들의 祖先으로부터 道義의 혼은 말없이 아지 못하게 우리들의 체 내에 전하여졌습니다. 我國의 실력으로써 나서면 무엇을 두려워 하겠습니까. 승리는 항상 정의의 頭上에 있는 것입니다. 저 友邦獨逸에 대하여 저들 英米가 취한 태도를 보십시오. 지나간 歐洲大戰 후 가진 수단를 짜내여서 독일의 모든 것을 빼앗아서 금후 어떻게 해서든지 서지 못하게끔 하였습니다. 그러나 독일은 독일혼만은 빼앗기지 않고 20수년간이나 苦에 견디어온 결과, 지금의 굳센 독일을 만들었습니다. 무엇을 두려워 할 것이 있겠습니까. 저들 英米人은 외형만에 의해서 완전히 세계를 지배했다고 오만한 기분을 품고 있었습니다. 그러나 동양 10억의 인류를 그들의 손에서 건질려는 前古未曾有의 聖戰은 열려졌습니다.
그 銃後에서 싸울 수 있는, 실로 무엇에도 비할 수 없는 광영을 여하히 나타낼 수 있을까요. 황국신민된 고마움에는 感泣할 뿐입니다. 그 심경을 무엇으로 바꾸어서 나라에 奉公할 수 있을까요. 그것은 우선 정신적 무장에 있다고 생각합니다. 즉 우리들의 혼과 불타는 赤誠을 드리는 일입니다. 어떤 사람은 이렇게 말합니다. 우리 여성은 남성과 같이 제일선에 서지 못하기 때문에 쓸 데 없다고. 그러나 제일선의 뒤에 우리들이 있다고 하는 것은 제일선에서는 장병으로 하여금 如何히 원기를 갖게 하는 것인가를 측량할 수가 없습니다. 內地婦人이 출정하는 자기 아들과 자기 남편에게 『임금님을 위해서＜96＞ 一死奉公하여 주십시오. 집 걱정은 조금도 하지 마시고......』라고 말한 그 一言이 진격 중의 장병에게는 무엇으로도 바꿀수 없는 위안의 말입니다. 마음을 분기케 하는 근원입니다. 『이쪽 일은 굳게 지키렵니다.』하는 그 마음이 銃後를 지키는 우리들의 마음이 되어야 할 것입니다. 「海ゆかば」라는 노래에 있듯이 「大君のへにこそ死なめかへりみはせじ」는, 설혹 前線과 銃後의 장소의 차이가 있다고 할 망정 황국신민으로서는 누구나 가져야할 赤心입니다.
빈부의 차별이 없이 자기의 온갖 진심을 기우려서 盡瘁함이 즉, 우리여성의 무장입니다. 이러한 혼이 없이, 그저 물자절약, 헌품, 헌금 등의 외형적만인 봉공은 마치 인간의 형체는 가추었으되 혼이 없는 인형과 같은 감을 갖게 합니다. 우리들의 생활의 전부를 바처서 如何한 고통이든지 달게받으며 나아가려는 영구적인 根氣있는 봉공을 해야할 것입니다.
이 聖戰, 道義와 동양인류해방을 위한 싸움, 그리고 1억이 하나이 되여 나아간다면 반드시 승리를 얻을 이 大戰에 際하여 我等은 자신을 가지고 마음과 혼을 모두 국가에 바칩시다. 그리하여 이 聖代에 생을 받은 기쁨과 제국신민된 특권의 光榮에 더욱 분투하지 않으렵니까.(拍手)＜97＞

迷夢에서 깨자 林孝貞

오늘 이 자리에 나오신 분은 우리 日本帝國이 지금 어떠한 나라와 어떻게 싸호고 있다는 형편쯤은 가장 현명하시고 가장 비판력이 빠르다고 자처하시는 분들이매 잘 아실 줄 아옵니다.
매일 두 번씩 보도되는 신문을 읽으시고 또 하로에도 몇 번씩 방송되는 뉴-스를 들으시는 여러분으로 누구보다도 이 시국을 잘 인식하기고 계시다고 자처함도 결코 무리는 아닐 줄 압니다. 그러나 나 보기에는 아직도 여러분은 몽롱한 꿈속에서 헤매고 계신 것 갔습니다.
밖갓흔 밤사히 日氣가 돌변하야 雷聲霹靂의 치고 비가 쏘다저 하수도가 넘고 담장이 터저 야단이 났지만은 여러분 방에는 덧문을 꼭꼭 닿고 검은 門帳을 기리 기리 느리엿기 때문에 한밤중인줄만 알고 쿨쿨 자고 있습니다.
돈이 있는 부호는 돈에 가리워서, 무식한 사람은 무지해서, 소위 인테리란 지식층은 반들어저서 이 시국을 잘 인식지 못하고 迷夢에서 헤매고 있습니다.
여러분이 미몽에서 깨엿다면 왜 買占이 있고 왜 채권을 사지 않고, 왜 6억 저축에 협력을 하지 않고 왜 당신의 아들들을 지원병으로 내여놓지를 않습니까?
여인들을 만나면 고무신이 없다 광목이 없다, 기름이 없다, 불평을 말합니다.
그러면서도 12월 1일부터 비단과 화장품에 세금이 붙는다고 각 백화점과 포목 商門이 메여지게 장사진을 느리여 가지고 한 사람이 100원어치 화장품을 삿다는 둥 2,3천 원 어치씩 비단을 끊었다는 둥 하니 그 무슨 추태입니까? 100원어치씩 화장품을 살 재력이 있는 사람이 세금이 붙은 후에 1,2원어치씩 사다 쓸 능력은 없습니까?
그리고 이 전시에 무슨 화려한 옷이 그리 필요해서 수 천원 어치씩 삽니까?
여러분이 다른 나라의 戰時生活이＜98＞ 어떻다는 것을 안다면 이상과 같은 추태는 연출치 안을 것입니다. 그러면 暫間 다른 나라의 戰時생활 狀態를 봅시다.
獨逸은 우리나라와 같이 한 사람이 얼마든지 의복을 가질 수 있는 생활이 아니고 1인당 몇 점 수 이상은 같이 못하는 법령이 실시되였고 식량에 있어도 퍽 엄격합니다.
전쟁이 시작되기 전부터 일체의 준비를 해 놓았다가 폴낸드와 개전이 되자 즉시 배급제를 썻는데 그 배급은 전쟁 전에 비해서 육류가 일인당 4할 3분 지방이 4할, 사탕이 2할로 줄었는데 其中에도 주목되는 것은 그들이 우리네의 茶와 같이 노냥 마시는 삐루는 가정에는 한 合도 배급되지 않고 요리집에를 가야 겨우 한곱뿌 얻어먹게 되는데 그것 역시 물에 탄 것이라 합니다. 이와같이 상당히 부자유한 배급을 받으면서도 시국의 중대성을 잘 인식한 독일국민은 아모런 불만불평도 없고 闇取引 같은 것도 아니하고 統制違反行爲 같은 것도 없어서 정부로선 계획데로 식량을 착착 저장해 나간다고 합니다.
英國은 독일의 강력적인 전시생활에 비해서 퍽 비참합니다. 영국은 본래가 자국에서 생산되는 식량으로 3개월 반 받게는 먹을 수가 없고 나머지 8개월 반은 국외에서 가저 와야합니다.

銃後婦人의 覺悟
許河伯

여러분은 누구나 잘 아시는 바와 같이 我國은 과거 4년간 계속해 온 支那事變이 아직 끝마치지 않은 때에 또 米英양국과 싸우지 않으면 안되게 되였습니다. 그 이유는 여러분도 잘 아시는 바와 같이 英米는 인구에 비하여 세계적으로 넓은 영토를 가지고 있을 뿐 아니라 많은 자원을 가지고 있는 나라입니다. 그러므로 그들은 동양에까지 손을 뻐치지 않을지라도 좋을 것인데도 불구하고 그들의 극단의 개인주의사상으로부터 出來한 私利私腹 정책은 우리 황색인종에 대하여 심한 압박을 거듭하여 왔었습니다.
이에, 우리 無敵皇軍은 이리해서는 안되겠다고 중대한 결심을 굳게 한 결과, 이야말로 실로 大東亞共營圈 건설의 聖戰이라는 旗帳 밑에서 米英 양국에 대하여 砲門을 연 것이 지난 12월 8일이였습니다.
그 이래, 여러분도 신문에서, 혹은 라디오의 뉴스를 통하여서 잘 아시리라고 생각합니다마는, 우리 황군은 이르는 곳에서 적군을 격멸시켜 赫赫한 전과를 거두고 있습니다.
이에 있어서 우리들 銃後의 부인도 가만히 앉아만 있을 수 없는 큰 사명이 있다고 생각하여서 변변치 못한 저까지도 이 자리에 연사의 한 사람으로 서게 된 것입니다.
물론. 우리들은 과거에 있어서도 매우 銃後의 노력을 하여왔었지만 이번은 그러한 미지근한 생각으로써가 아니고 가장 적극적이오 실천적인 결과를 나타내지 않아서는 안될 각오가 필요합니다.
그러면 어떻게 할까하는 것을 이로부터 제가 간단하게 여러분께 말씀코저 합니다.

一,健全한 思想
첫째, 건전한 사상을 가져야 할 터인데, 이에 있어서 우선 우리는 敬神尊皇의 念을 강하게 하지 않으면 안됩니다. 宮城遙拜와 神社參拜는 물론, 신사 앞을 지날 때에는 경례할 일과, 愛國日의 실행 등, 이리하여＜108＞ 內鮮一體의 정신을 발휘할 일입니다.
과거에 가졌던 개인주의적인 생각을 버리고 모든 것을 나라를 위해서만이라는 국가주의의 사상을 파악하지 않아서는 안됩니다. 구체적인 예를 2, 3 들면 우리들이 식사를 하는 것일지도 자기 一個人의 생명 지지를 위해서가 아니고, 이를 먹고 힘을 내여 국가를 위해서 일하는 사람이 되겠다는 생각 밑에서 식사도 해야할 것입니다.
또 자녀를 학교에 보내는데 있어서도 훌륭히 양육하여 나라를 위해 유용한 인물이 되도록 힘쓰는 헌신적 정신이야말로 일본정신입니다. 이 생각을 가지지 못했다면 참된 황국신민이라고 할 수 없습니다. 이 國家第一主義의 사상이야말로 건전한 사상이며, 국민 각자가 통일적으로 이 사상을 가저서 비로소 일심이 되여 이 전쟁을 훌륭히 이길수 있을 것입니다.

二, 感謝慰問
第一線에서 생명을 내여던지고 싸워주는 것을 생각할 때 우리들은 그 고생을 감수하고만 있을 것입니까. 배급을 좀 적게 받는다고 불평을 말해서는 안되겠습니다. 우리들이 今日 이처럼 행복스럽게 생활하는 것은 제일선에서 생사를 상관하지 않고 싸워준 용감한 장병의 덕택이 아닙니까. 이것을 생각하는 때는 실로 눈물 없이는 생각할 수 없습니다. 그러므로 불평을 말할것이 아니고 항상 감사하고 또 감사하는 태도를 취해야 할 것입니다.
그러나 감사만으로는 부족합니다. ① 출정군인에게 선물을 보내여 위로할 일. ② 전쟁에서 명예의 부상을 받은 장병에게는 하루라도 속히 치료를 받게 하여 更生의 길을 걸도록 물질적으로 후원할 일. ③ 전사장병의 유가족에게는 물심양력면으로 도와서 고락을 함께 할 일.

三, 資源愛護(消費節約)
소비절약은 우선 우리들의 일상생활의 극히 손 가까운 것에서부터 하지 않어서는 안됩니다. 무 한 개나 감자 한 개라도 이를 소홀히 해서는 안됩니다. 매우 적은 것이라도 이를 버리는 일이 없이, 모든 물자를 소중히 해야합니다.
현대의 전쟁은 經濟戰과 總力戰에서 비로소 승리를 얻는다고 합니다. 이를 안다면 아무리 보잘것없는 물건일지라도 반드시 그 용도를 잘 생각해야 할 것이니, 만일 이러한 태도를 缺했다고 하면 현대국가의 국민된 자격을 상실했다고 볼 것입니다.
특히 여기에서 역설하여 줄 것은 去年 사치품금지령이 발령되였음에도 불구하고 아직도 태연하게 指環을 끼고 네거리로 활보하는 여성을＜109＞ 보게될 때 실로 한심하기 잖이 없습니다.
청컨대, 銃後부인이라는 자각을 가젔다면 이 시기에 一切로 이와 같은 사치를 격멸하시기를 바랍니다.
