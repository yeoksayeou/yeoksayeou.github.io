From "Samch'ŏlli" to "Taedong'a"

Our magazine "Samch'ŏlli" (三千里), which was first published in April of Shōwa 4 (1929) and has continued publication through fourteen years (星霜), now, taking the opportunity of the historic great turning point in Asia, changes its title (題號) to "Taedong'a" (大東亞) effective from today [Note: Announcement dated May 1st, 1942, published in the March 1st, 1942 issue], and further renewing its contents, intends to strive vigorously towards the noble national policy (國策) of the state (邦家). We humbly request even more guidance and encouragement (指導鞭撻).
<13>

May 1st, Shōwa 17 (1942)
Taedong'a Company (大東亞社)

**Summary**

This brief announcement details the renaming of the Korean magazine "Samch'ŏlli" (Three Thousand Ri, a common name for Korea) to "Taedong'a" (Great East Asia). The change, effective May 1, 1942, is explicitly justified by the "historic great turning point in Asia" (referring to the Pacific War) and a desire to "strive vigorously towards the noble national policy of the state" (aligning with Japan's Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere ideology). For historians, this text starkly illustrates the co-option and transformation of Korean media under late Japanese colonial rule, forced to directly promote imperial wartime objectives and ideology, even erasing traditional Korean symbolism ("Samch'ŏlli") in favor of Japanese imperial terms ("Taedong'a").
