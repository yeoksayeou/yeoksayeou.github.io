The Meaning of the Word "Kōchin" (轟沈)

The word "Kōchin" (轟沈) is specialized terminology of the Navy, and its meaning refers to a case where [a ship] sinks within one minute. In the recent naval battles, the three battleships West Virginia, Oklahoma, and Repulse all suffered a "kōchin." <143>

**Summary**

This short piece defines the Japanese naval term "轟沈" (gōchin, read here as "hoemchim" in Korean), explaining it means for a ship to sink within one minute. It cites the recent sinking of the American battleships USS West Virginia and USS Oklahoma (damaged/sunk at Pearl Harbor) and the British battleship HMS Repulse as examples of this. Published in early 1942 in a Korean magazine under Japanese rule, the article's focus on a Japanese military term and its application to Allied losses serves as wartime propaganda, highlighting Japanese naval successes. For historians, it illustrates the permeation of Japanese military language and worldview into colonial Korean publications during the Pacific War.
