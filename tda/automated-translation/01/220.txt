**Summary**

This 1942 article provides statistics justifying the concept of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere. It details the vast combined area (15.87 million sq km) and population (750 million) of Japan, Manchukuo, China, and occupied/aligned Southeast Asian territories, comparing them favorably to the US and UK/US combined. It particularly emphasizes the immense natural resources of the "Southern Regions" (Southeast Asia/Oceania), listing production figures for key commodities like rubber, tin, oil, rice, and minerals, and highlighting their significant share of world production to argue for the region's economic self-sufficiency under Japanese control. The article also lists foreign (presumably Western) investment figures in these southern territories. For historians studying Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it showcases the propaganda disseminated in Korea (published in a Korean-language section of a likely Japanese-controlled magazine, or a Korean magazine under strict censorship) to justify Japan's wartime expansion and resource acquisition. It reflects how the ideology of the Co-Prosperity Sphere, emphasizing regional bloc economics and self-sufficiency against Western powers, was promoted to colonial subjects.
