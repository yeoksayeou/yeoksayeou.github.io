**Summary**

This article from the September 1, 1929 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine announces a forthcoming series featuring travelogues about the "Eight Views of the Peninsula." It lists eight famous locations in Korea and assigns a prominent literary figure of the era to write about each one, including well-known writers like Yi Kwang-su, Kim Tong-in, Yŏm Sang-sŏp, and Hong Myŏng-hŭi. From a historical perspective, this announcement is interesting as it showcases the vibrant literary scene of late 1920s colonial Korea and the engagement of its leading intellectuals with themes of national landscape and identity, framing specific locations as canonical "Views of the Peninsula." It highlights which figures and places were considered culturally significant at the time.
