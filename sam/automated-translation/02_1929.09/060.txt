Article Title: 술과 女子는 죽을 때까지 남자를 웃긴다. (Alcohol and Women Make Men Laugh Until They Die.)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous writing)

Alcohol and Women make men laugh until they die.

Alcohol and Women make men laugh until they die.

Once the lamp is turned off, all women are the same.

Two things rule the world: women and money.

A woman's tears and a dog's limp are false.

A woman always leaning against a window is like a cluster of grapes hanging by the roadside.<8>

**Summary**

This short piece presents a series of cynical aphorisms about women, alcohol, and money, reflecting a misogynistic viewpoint common in certain circles. The sayings suggest women are deceptive, interchangeable, powerful alongside money, and likened to easily accessible fruit if they appear idle or available. From a historical perspective, this text offers insight into prevalent social attitudes and gender stereotypes in late 1920s Korea. Historians might find the stark expression of these views interesting for understanding male perspectives on gender relations, societal power dynamics (linking women and money), and common tropes during the colonial period.
