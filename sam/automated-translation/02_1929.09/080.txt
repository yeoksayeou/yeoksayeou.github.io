Physique
When Yŏ Un-hyŏng was in Shanghai, he boasted to a friend, "In Chosŏn, there are three men with fine physiques and handsome features: first is Yi Tong-hwi, second is An Ch'ang-ho, and third is Yŏ Un-hyŏng himself."

Silence
Tsar Nicholas of Russia invited a famous foreign musician and had him perform, while he himself engaged in loud conversation with his courtiers. The musician, finding this attitude disagreeable, abruptly stopped playing. The startled Emperor asked the reason, and the musician wittily replied, "Because when Your Majesty speaks with his subjects, everyone must observe silence!"

Bamboo
The famous house where bamboo was said to have sprouted after the death of Min Ch'ungjŏng (閔忠正) is located quite a ways in from the main road of Kyŏnji-dong (堅志洞), past the Chinese restaurant called Yŏnghae-ru (英海樓) on the west side. The spot where the bamboo allegedly emerged is the wooden floor on one side of a small room that looks like either an outer chamber (사랑채) or a pavilion (정자). However, that house was sold to a famous rich man named Cho Chin-t'ae (趙鎭泰), and is now mortgaged to a bank. Taking advantage of the Exposition, someone has rented it, saying they will operate an inn (려관), and is decorating the rooms. The house is so large, it's said to have a staggering three hundred *kan* (칸 - units of space). How could the house of the Min family become lodging for Exposition bundle-carriers?

Heine's Prayer
It was when the German poet Heine was gravely ill and near death. The nurses gathered around urged him to offer his final prayer to God, as it was his last moment. He replied:
"Is that so? But even if I don't pray, God will surely forgive me. Why is that, you ask? Because doing so is God's job," thus remaining witty until the very end. <10>

**Summary**

This article presents four short, unrelated anecdotes. The first recounts Yŏ Un-hyŏng's boast about the three most handsome men in Korea (including himself). The second tells of a musician silencing Tsar Nicholas with wit. The third describes the famous house associated with Min Ch'ungjŏng, noting its decline from a historical site to being rented out as lodging during an exposition. The fourth anecdote relates the German poet Heine's witty, perhaps blasphemous, remark about forgiveness on his deathbed. From a historical perspective, the mention of prominent Korean nationalist figures like Yŏ Un-hyŏng, Yi Tong-hwi, and An Ch'ang-ho, even in a lighthearted context, is notable for a 1929 publication under Japanese rule. The lament about the fate of Min Ch'ungjŏng's house reflects on changing times and perhaps a perceived loss of national heritage during the colonial period.

揷話
풍채
려운형이 상해 잇슬 때 어느 친구더러 호언하기를 「조선에 풍채 조코 인물 잘난 사람으론 셋이 잇는데 첫재 리동휘, 둘재 안창호, 셋재 려운형 자긔라고.」

沈黙
로서아의 니코라쓰 皇帝가 외국의 유명한 音樂家를 불너다 노코 演奏를 식히면서 저는 廷臣들과 高聲談話하엿다. 그 態度에 아니꼬운 생각을 가진 音樂家는 돌연 연주를 중지하엿다. 皇帝- 놀라 그 뜻올 무르니 그는 익살맛게 「페하가 신하와 말슴하실 때는 누구나 다 沈黙을 직혀야 하니까요!」

참대
민충정의 사후에 참대가 낫더란 그 유명한 집은 堅志洞 큰 길가에서 서쪽으로 英海樓란 中華料理店을 끼고 한참 드러가면 잇는데 사랑채 갓기도 하고 정자 갓기도 한 그 조고마한 방 한편의 널마루 바닥이 바로 그 참대 나온 구멍이라고. 그런데 그 집이 趙鎭泰라는 유명한 부자의 손에 팔니엇다가 지금은 다시 은행에 뎐당으로 잡혀 드러갓는데 박람회를 긔회로 엇든 사람이 려관을 하겟다고 세를 맛허 방을 꾸미고 잇는데 집이 엇더케 크던지 무려 삼백간이라고. 엇잿는 민씨 집이 박람희 봇다리꾼의 숙소되다니?

하이네의 긔도
독일 시인 「하니네」가 중병에 걸려 거지반 운명을 하게 된 때엇습니다. 둘러 섯든 간호부들이 마즈막이라고 어서 최후의 긔도를 하나님께 드리라 졸느니 그는
「그려냐. 그러나 긔도를 아니 하드래도 하나님이야 나를 용서하여 주겟지. 엇재 그런가고 그것이야 그리는 것이 하느님의 직업이니까」하며 마즈막까지 익살을 피우드람니다.＜10＞
