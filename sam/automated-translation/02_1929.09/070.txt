If They Die
Pastor, excited during his sermon: "Everyone, what kind of person do you think goes to heaven?"<8>
Poktong: "Well, dead people go, of course."

Reason for Breaking Off Engagement
Pak: "I hear you broke off your engagement with that woman. Why? Why did you do that?"
Yi: "Yeah. That woman is just so stubborn, you know. She made such a fuss just because I kissed her lips a bit."
Pak: "Haha. That's really too much. Does she think kissing one's fiancée isn't allowed?"
Yi: "No, I mean with another woman."

Mencius's Surname
"Hey. What's Mencius's (孟子) surname?"
After thinking for a while, Student: "It's Wang. Wang Maengja (王孟子)."

The Way to Ullŭngdo
During Geography class: "Alright, explain the way to Ullŭngdo."
Student: "First, you go to Pusan (부산)."
"And then?"
"You get on a ship. After that, you just leave it to the captain."

Who Gets Insulted
A child looking at monkeys at the zoo: "Mommy, that monkey's face looks exactly like Daddy's."
"Shh, what are you saying? If you say things like that—."
"What? They say monkeys don't understand words, so what does it matter if I insult it?"

Activity
"If all the prominent figures in Chosŏn (조선) society right now were to die, whose turn would it be to be active next?"
"Probably the pallbearers (상두꾼)."

Medicine Bill Debt
"If you're feeling so unwell, why don't you see a doctor?"
"No, no. I hate doctors."
"Why? Can't trust them?"
"I trust them, but they don't seem to trust me much, hmph!"

Mother Bragging
Child A: "My mother is really tall."
Child B: "They say tall people are good thieves. What's so great about height! My mother is really good at talking."
Child C: "They say good talkers are like sparrows. What's so great about talking! My mother is incredibly strong. When she fights with Father, she instantly picks him up and throws him out the gate."<9>

This article presents a collection of short jokes and humorous anecdotes reflecting everyday life and social commentary in late 1920s Korea. The jokes cover topics like religion, relationships, education, common knowledge (or lack thereof), family dynamics, social hierarchy, and interactions with professionals like doctors. The humor often relies on misunderstandings, puns (like the "activity" joke involving pallbearers), or cynical observations about society. For historians, this piece offers glimpses into popular humor, social norms (like engagements), educational settings, common perceptions (about doctors, prominent figures), and perhaps subtle critiques or reflections of the times, such as the reference to "Chosŏn society" and its figures, or the dynamic depicted between parents. The style of humor and the specific situations (like travel to Ullŭngdo or visits to the zoo) provide cultural context.

笑話
죽으면
목사   연설에 흥분이 되어 「여러분 엇더한 사람이 천당에 갈 줄 압니까.」＜8＞
福童   「그야 죽은 사람이 가지요.」

파혼 리유
朴   「자네는 약혼한 녀자와 파혼히엿다구. 웨? 그리헷나.」
李   「응. 그 녀자는 아조 완고당이란 말이야. 내가 입울 좀 마첫다고 그 야단이야.」
朴   「하하. 그건 너무 심한걸 그래. 약혼한 녀자와 입마초는 것이 안된다고 그러나.」
李   「아니, 다른 녀자와 말이야.」

孟子의 姓
「얘. 孟子의 성이 무에냐?」
한참 생각하다가 生徒   「王가 야요. 王孟子 올시다.」

울능도 가는 길
地理時間에 「어듸 울능도 가는 길을 설명해 봐라.」
生徒   「처음에 부산에 가지요.」
「그리고는?」
「배를 타지요.  그 다음에는 선장에게 맛겨 버리지요.」

누가 먹는 욕
動物園에서 원숭이를 구경하고 잇든 어린아히 「어머니, 저 원숭이의 얼골이 똑 아버지와 갓해요.」
「쉬, 그게 무슨 소리냐. 그런 소리하면-.」
「무얼 원숭이는 말을 모른다는데 흉보면 엇대요.」

活躍
「지금 조선사회에서 누구누구라는 인물들이 모다 죽으면 그 다음에 누가 활약할 차래일가?」 「상두꾼 일걸.」

약갑負債
「그러케 몸이 괴롭거든 의사에게 보이면 엇대?」
「실혀 실혀. 난 의사는 실혀.」
「웨 신용할 수 업나.」
「나는 신용하나 저쪽이 나를 덜 신용한다나 쳬!」

어머니 자랑
甲兒   「우리 어머니는 키가 아조 크단다.」
乙兒   「키 크면 도적질 잘 한다더라. 그까짓 키! 우리 어머니는 말울 정말 잘 한단다.」
丙兒   「말 잘하면 참새란다. 그까짓 말 우리 어머니는 기운이 상사란다. 아버지와 싸우다가는 젝각 드러서 대문 밧게 내던진단다.」＜9＞
