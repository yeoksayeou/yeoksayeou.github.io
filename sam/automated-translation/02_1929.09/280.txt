**Body Text Translation:**
Seeing the New Rising China
						Yi Kwan-yong (李灌鎔)

1. What is "New Rising China"?

Samch'ŏlli has asked me to address the somewhat vague topic, "Seeing the New Rising China." As someone who has returned from seeing China, it would be appropriate for me to revive the impressions I received there and describe them here. However, I am unsure if the China I saw is the true New Rising China, and indeed, I myself would like to know what exactly is meant by "New Rising China." Is it Nanjing? Beijing? Perhaps Shanghai? Guangdong? Is it the warlord states reminiscent of the *Lieh-kuo chih* (列國誌 - Annals of the Warring States)? The 19th-century style merchant-industrial bourgeoisie? Or the feudal landlords? The intellectuals (知識分子) who have absorbed the latest European and American thought? If not them, is it the peasants undergoing Sovietization (쏘뱃트化)? The workers whose class consciousness is being established? Or perhaps the crowds of beggars (乞人群) barely clinging to life? The China seen in Beijing is different from the China of Shanghai, and the China of Nanjing is different from the China seen in Guangdong. Everyone knows that modern China is not yet this or that, but rather a cloud of chaos becoming something—or rather, a muddy stream in the midst of pouring rain.

Even speaking of figures who might be said to reflect the New Rising China, should we first consider the Beiyang Warlord Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥)? Or Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石), the new warlord produced by the bourgeois (comprador - 賣辨) class? Perhaps Hu Hanmin (胡漢民), the spokesperson for the feudal landlords, or the intellectuals of the old and new schools like Wu Zhihui (吳稚暉), Dai Tianchou (戴天仇), and Cai Yuanpei (蔡元培)? Only Chiang Kai-shek could perhaps be called a figure produced by the New Rising China; that is, in the sense of renewing the China that had been partitioned and fought over by the Beiyang Warlords since the Xinhai Year (辛亥年 - 1911) and aiming to build a New China centered on the national bourgeoisie (民族資産階級), one might say a fragment of the New Rising China can be seen in Chiang Kai-shek. However, this movement for China's new rise is not satisfied with just this and aims for the satisfaction of broader mass demands, thus Chiang Kai-shek, who was produced by the New Rising China, can no longer represent it. It is true that the New Rising China still shines brightly in figures like Wang Jingwei (王精衛), Chen Gongbo (陳公博), and Gan Naiguang (甘乃光), who still possess the consciousness to participate in mass political movements, or Chen Duxiu (陳獨秀), who is said to be still active at the forefront of the worker-peasant movement. However, this New Rising China has not yet been concretely realized and is mostly unfolding only as a national force; moreover, much of it exists merely in the form of consciousness. Therefore, such a New Rising China is not easy to witness under the dictatorship of Chiang Kai-shek, and given our circumstances, it is also difficult to describe what we have witnessed!

Ultimately, the China I saw was one where various new forces were inevitably brewing under the overwhelming pressure of declining old powers. The New Rising China constructed in Nanjing has mostly not escaped existence on paper, and even that showed no sign of being realistically achievable, but in any case, it is not without a mood of newness (新興 氣分). The prohibition of opium, mahjong, and prostitution (昌妓) can certainly be called concrete expressions of this mood of newness. Women's bobbed hair (削髮) and the prevalence of vernacular writing (白話文)<36> can also be seen as phenomena of the new rise. However, since this mood cannot be seen as having spread throughout the entirety, the use of the term "New China" cannot avoid the criticism of being premature. What differs from the old warlord era is inevitably that among the various warlords, only one is overwhelmingly strong, increasing the possibility for the central government's authority to stand. **Summary**

The author, Yi Kwan-yong, expresses skepticism about the term "New Rising China" after visiting the country in 1929. He observes a fragmented nation grappling with diverse forces – old warlords, new bourgeois leaders like Chiang Kai-shek, feudal landlords, intellectuals, Sovietized peasants, and conscious workers – none of which fully represents a unified "New China." While acknowledging Chiang Kai-shek's initial role in consolidating power against the Beiyang Warlords, Yi argues Chiang no longer embodies the broader aspirations for change, pointing instead to figures associated with mass movements. He notes superficial signs of modernity like bans on opium/mahjong/prostitution, bobbed hair, and vernacular writing, but concludes these represent a mere "mood" rather than fundamental transformation, deeming the term "New China" premature. The primary change observed is the concentration of power under one dominant figure, Chiang Kai-shek. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it shows a Korean intellectual analyzing China's turbulent modernization process. Yi's critical assessment of claims of "newness" amidst political fragmentation and authoritarian consolidation under Chiang Kai-shek might reflect anxieties or observations relevant to Korea's own situation under Japanese rule, offering insights into how colonized intellectuals viewed regional power dynamics and national development narratives. Historians might find the contemporary Korean perspective on China's complex political scene, the specific figures mentioned (Chiang, Wang Jingwei, Chen Duxiu, etc.), and the cautious evaluation of superficial versus substantive change particularly valuable.

1, 「新興 中國」이란 무엇이냐
삼천리에서 「新興 中國을 보고」라는 좀 막연한 문제를 내게 부탁하엿다. 나는 新興 中國을 보고 온 사람으로서 그것으로부터 바든 인상을 다시 살리여 여긔 기술함이 맛당할 것이나 그러나 내가 본 중국이 진정한 新興 中國인지 아닌지도 모르거니와 대관절 新興 中國이란 무엇을 가리처 말함인지 나도 알고 십흔 것이다, 그것이 南京이냐 北京이냐 혹 上海냐 廣東이냐 列國誌 냄새나는 軍國이냐 19세기식 商工資産家냐 혹 봉건적 지주나 歐美의 최신 사상을 흡수한 知識分子이냐 그것이 아니라면 쏘뱃트化 되고 잇는 농민이냐 계급의식이 확립되고 잇는 노동자이냐 혹 생활에 간신히 껄리여 다니는 乞人群이냐 北京에서 본 中國이 上海의 中國과 다르고 南京의 中國이 廣東에서 본 中國과 달러서 현대 中國은 아직 이것도 아니고 저것도 아닌 장차 무엇으로든지 되고 잇는 혼돈의 구름―이라는 것보다 霖雨中의 濁流인 것은 누구든지 다 아는 바이다,
新興 中國이 빗최엿다고 할만한 인물로 말하드래도 爲先 北洋軍閥인 馮玉祥이겟는가, 資産(賣辨)계급이 산출한 新軍閥인 蔣介石이겟는가, 혹 봉건지주의 대변자인 胡漢民이나 新舊智識分子인 吳稚暉, 戴天仇, 蔡元培 등이겟는가, 오즉 蔣介石만은 新興 中國이 産出한 인물이라 하겟스니 즉 辛亥年 이후로 北洋軍閥이 割據相戰하든 中國을 쇄신하야 民族資産階級을 중심으로 한 新中國을 건설하랴는 意義에 잇서서 蔣介石에게 新興 中國의 一片을 볼 수 잇다 할 것이다. 그러나 이 中國의 新興連動은 이것만으로 만족히 녀기지 안코 더욱 광범한 대중 요구의 만족을 목적함으로 新興 中國이 産出하엿든 蔣介石은 新興 中國을 대표할 수 업게 되엿다. 대중적 정치운동에 아직도 참여할 의식을 가진 王精衛, 陳公博, 甘乃光 등이나 노농운동의 前衛에서 여전히 활약하고 잇다는 陳獨秀 등에는 아직도 新興 中國이 빗최고 잇는 것이 사실이다. 그러나 이 新興 中國은 아직도 구체적으로 실현되지 못하고 오즉 민족적 세력으로만 전개되고 잇슬 뿐만 아니라 그 대부분이 다만 의식의 형태로 존재함으로 이러한 新興 中國은 蔣介石 독재 하에서 구경하기도 容易치 안커니와 우리 형편으로 구경한 것을 記述하기도 곤란하다!
결국 내가 본 中國은 쇠퇴되고 잇는 舊세력의 압도 하에 여러 가지 新세력이 필연적으로 釀造되고 잇는 것이엿다. 南京에 건설듼 新興 中國은 아직 대부분이 紙上 존재를 면치 못하엿고 그것이나마 도저히 실현될 형세는 뵈이지 아넛스나 엇잿든 新興 氣分이 업는 것이 아니다. 아편, 마작, 昌妓의 금지 가튼 것은 확실히 이 新興 氣分의 구체적 표현이라고 할 수 잇다. 여자의 削髮과 白話文의 성행＜36＞ 가튼 것도 新興의 현상이라고 볼 수 잇다. 그러나 이 氣分도 전체에 보급되엿다고도 볼 수 업슴으로 「新中國」이란 말을 사용함은 시기상조라는 평을 밧지 안을 수 업다. 舊軍閥시대와 다른 것은 여러 군벌 중에 오즉 하나만이 압도적으로 강하야 중앙정부의 위신이 슬 가능정도가 놉하진 것에 잇다 아니할 수 업섯다.(以下 次號 繼續) ＜37＞
