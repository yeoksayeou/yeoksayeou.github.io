Untitled

A woman's beauty, a mountain's echo (反響), and a rainbow soon disappear.

A beauty's flirtatiousness makes a wallet cry.

A beauty without fidelity is like flat wine.

A woman crying is a pitiful thing, like a *bird crawling on the ground.

Good medicine (良藥) is bitter to the mouth; a man acts sweetly towards a woman.

To a crying child, milk; to a hungry stomach, rice; to a man, a woman <47>

**Summary**

This short piece consists of several aphorisms reflecting on transient beauty, the perceived nature of women (their perceived lack of fidelity, their cost, their place relative to men), and basic human needs or relationships. The sayings portray women through a traditional, somewhat cynical male lens, emphasizing ephemeral beauty, potential faithlessness, and their role as companions to men, juxtaposed with basic needs like food for the hungry. From a historical perspective, these aphorisms offer insight into common social attitudes and gender stereotypes prevalent in 1920s Korea, reflecting patriarchal views on women, beauty, and relationships during the colonial period.

무제
녀자의 아름다움과 山의 반향(反響)과 무지개는 곳 사라진다.

미인의 웃정조 돈지갑을 울닌다.

미인의 정조없는 것은 김나간 술

녀자의 우는 것은 *鳥가 땅에 기는 것처럼 가엽은 일이다.

량약(良藥)은 입에 쓰고 남자는 녀자에게 달게 군다.

우는 아해에 젓 배 곱흔데 밥 남자에게 녀자 ＜47＞
