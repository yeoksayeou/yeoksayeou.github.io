Cossack Lullaby
						Translated by Kim Tongjin
This song is the original work of Lermontov (네르몬로푸), a song a Cossack woman sings while watching her child. While rummaging through an old textbook (敎科書), my eyes happened to fall upon this song, so I have freely translated (意譯) it to the extent that the original work (原作) is not harmed.

(One)
Pretty baby sleeps so well
Hush, hush
The bright moon in mid-heaven (中天)
Our baby above the cradle
Shall I tell an old tale?
Shall I sing a good song?
Sleeps so well, pretty baby
Hush, hush

(Two)
You don't know the world ahead
What will you do in this harsh life?
Bravely shoulder your gun
Mount your horse with a smile
I'll get silk gauze (明紬) to make a saddle (鞍裝)
And put it on the horse's back for you
Sleeps so well, precious baby
Hush, hush

(Three)
You are a hero of the future
How could you forget the spirit of the fatherland (祖國)?
When seeing you off as you go to war (出征)
Don't wave your hand to stop me <49>
On the night I send you off
What shall I do with sorrowful tears?
Sleeps so well, my angel (天使)
Hush, hush

(Four)
My heart (尋思) is impatient (操急)
When will you ever grow up?
During the day (白日), I pray (祈禱)
At night, it is worry (憂愁)
After sending you to war (戰爭)
How shall I live, wanting to see you?
You don't know anything yet
Hush, hush

(Five)
On the road you take to war
I will give you a token (標的)
Pray to God (하나님)
And keep (看守) it deeply, deeply hidden
In times of emergency (危急), take it out and look
And think of your mother (母親)
Sleeps so well, pretty baby
Hush, hush <49>
<48-49>

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean translation by Kim Tongjin of Mikhail Lermontov's "Cossack Lullaby." The poem, rendered in five stanzas, depicts a mother singing to her infant son. She reflects on his peaceful sleep but anticipates the harsh future awaiting him as a Cossack, destined for a life of bravery, warfare, and separation from home. The mother expresses her own anxieties about his departure for war and gives him advice, urging him to remember his homeland and her through a token she will provide. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this piece is interesting as it presents themes of martial valor, sacrifice for a 'fatherland', and the sorrow of separation due to war, translated and published during a period of increasing Japanese militarism. Historians might find it notable how foreign literature expressing such themes was received or interpreted in colonial Korea, potentially resonating with, or perhaps offering a different perspective on, the prevailing atmosphere.

哥薩克 자장歌
						金東進 譯
이 노래는 「네르몬로푸」의 原作인 歌薩克女人의 아해보는 노래이다. 녯날 敎科書를 휘적거리다가 우연히 눈이 이 노래에 머물르기 때문에 原作을 傷치 안흘 정도에서 意譯한 것이다.

(一)
엡분 애기 잘도 잔다
자장 자장
중천에는 밝은 달님
요람우엔 우리 애기
옛말이나 하야볼가
조흔 노래불너볼가
잘도 잔다 엡분 애기
자장자장

(二)
압날 세상 너도 알지
험한 생애 어이하련
용감하게 총을 메고
웃으면서 말을 타라
명주나서 안장지어
말잔등에 치어주마
잘두 잔다 귀한 애기
자장 자장

(三)
너는 장래 영웅이다
조국 혼을 이즐소냐
너희 출정 전송할 때
손흔드러 막지마라 ＜49＞
너 떠보낸 그날 밤에
설흔 눈물 어히할고
잘도 잔다 내의 천사
자장자장

(四)
내의 심사 조급하다
너 언제나 자라겟니
낫이 되면 긔도하고
밤이 되면 근심일다
너를 전쟁 보낸 뒤에
보고 십허 어히 살겐
아직 너야 무엇알니
자장자장

(五)
네가 전쟁가는 길에
표적하나 줄 것이니
하나님께 긔도하고
깁히 깁히 간수햇다
위급할 때 내여 보고
네 어머니 생각해라
잘도 잔다 엡분 애기
자장자장 ＜49＞
