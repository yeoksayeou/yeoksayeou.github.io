**Body Text Translation**

Number of Koreans in Various Places like Shanghai, Peiping, etc., Reaching 10,000 Just Before the Incident

According to a survey by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (外務省), the numbers of Koreans scattered throughout various parts of China just before the Incident [Second Sino-Japanese War] broke out are as follows:

Shanghai    1,036
Peiping (北平)    1,854
Tientsin (天津)    1,962
T'angshan (唐山)     447
T'angku (塘沽)     155
Lan-hsien (란縣)     554
T'ungchou (通州)     182
Shanhaikwan (山海關)   416
Ch'inhuangtao (진*島)   307
Ch'angli (昌*)     247
Chifu (지부)     236
Weihaiwei (威海衛)   112
Ch'ingtao (靑島)   1,255
Chinan (濟南)    155
Nanking (南京)    240
Hank'ou (漢口)    105

Besides these, several tens of people each are scattered in various places such as Kuangchou (廣州), Hsia-men (厦門), Shashih (沙市), Chiuchiang (九江), Changtien (張店), Haotungchen (豪東鎭), Changchiak'ou (張家口), etc. The total number of Koreans in China shown in the statistics is 10,879, the total number of Mainland Japanese (内地人) is 61,700, and the number of Taiwanese (臺灣人) is 13,645, reportedly bringing the total number of Japanese subjects to a large number reaching approximately 86,000. <7>

**Summary**

This news article reports the population figures of Koreans residing in various major cities across China, as surveyed by the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs just before the outbreak of the Second Sino-Japanese War in 1937. It lists specific numbers for cities like Shanghai, Peiping, Tientsin, and others, totaling 10,879 Koreans. The article also provides figures for Mainland Japanese (61,700) and Taiwanese (13,645), culminating in a total Japanese subject population of roughly 86,000 in China at that time. For historians, this article provides valuable demographic data regarding the distribution of Japanese imperial subjects, including Koreans and Taiwanese, in China on the very eve of full-scale war. This reflects the extent of Japanese influence and colonial presence within China prior to the conflict and can be useful for studying pre-war conditions, colonial migration patterns, and the composition of the Japanese empire's population abroad.
