China's Anti-Japanese Organizations
						Ministry of Foreign Affairs Information Department

The first Foreign Goods Boycott Movement (外貨 排斥 運動) in China occurred 40 years ago in 1898. Since then, there have been 11 nationwide boycotts, namely:

Year / Target Country / Cause
1905 / USA / Chinese Immigration Exclusion Act Issue (掛支移民法)
1908 / Japan / Tatsu Maru Incident II (第2辰丸事件)
1909 / Japan / Antung–Mukden Line Reconstruction Issue (安奉線改築問題)
1915 / Japan / So-called Twenty-One Demands Issue (所謂 21個條問題)
1919 / Japan / Shandong Problem (山東問題)
1923 / Japan / Lushun-Dalian Recovery Issue (旅順 大連回收問題)
1925 / Japan / May 30th Incident (5.30事件)
1925 / UK / May 30th Incident (5.30事件)
1927 / Japan / Shandong Expedition (山東出兵)
1928 / Japan / Jinan Incident (濟南事件)
1931 / Japan / Wanbaoshan Incident (萬寶山事件 - text reads 下寶山事件) & Manchurian Incident (滿洲事變)

Of these 11 instances, 9 were boycotts against Japan. (omitted section)

Subsequently, by around June 1936, a broad-based Anti-Japanese People's Front (抗日人民戰線), encompassing various social strata, was established. The major anti-Japanese organizations included within the front, categorized by social stratum, are as follows:

1.  Student Stratum: Since the May Fourth Movement (5.4운동) (the incident on May 4, 1919, involving the attack on the residence of the pro-Japanese Cho Yŏrim (曺汝霖) by students in Beijing (北京)), the role of students in social movements has been extremely significant. Students were also the first to respond to the calls of the Comintern (콤민테룬) and the Chinese Communist Party (中國共産黨). Indeed, the large-scale student demonstration in Beiping (北平) in December 1935 served as the beacon for the formation of the front. Anti-Japanese National Salvation Associations (抗日救國會) were organized in various universities, middle schools, and even elementary schools. These federated regionally, forming bodies such as the Beiping Student National Salvation Federation (北平學生救國聯合會). Such organizations were also established in Shanghai (上海), Nanjing (南京), Hankou (漢口), and Chengdu (成都). Finally, as their central coordinating body, the All-China Student National Salvation Federation (全國學生救國聯合會) was organized in Shanghai (June 1936). Separate from the students, university professors, middle school teachers, elementary school teachers, etc., also have their own National Salvation Associations.

2.  Cultural Circles: Centered around university professors, lawyers, journalists, etc., Cultural Circles National Salvation Associations (文化界救國會) were established in various locations. Among these, the most famous is the Shanghai Cultural Circles National Salvation Association (上海文化界救國會); prominent figures of the People's Front such as Sim Kyun'yu (沈*儒 - likely 沈鈞儒), Chang Naegi (章乃器), etc., initially used this association as their starting point.

3.  Literary and Arts Circles: This includes groups established in Shanghai, such as the Chinese Writers' Association (中國文藝家協會), the Literary Workers Faction (文藝工作者 一派), the Authors' Association (著作人協會), etc. Writers, critics, journalists, new theater actors, and film personnel are all encompassed within this network.

4.  Commerce and Industry Circles: Organizations affiliated with the Workers' National Salvation Federation (工人救國聯合會), etc., belong here.

5.  Women's Circles: In major cities, starting with Shanghai, Women's National Salvation Associations (婦女救國會) are generally organized.

6.  Propaganda Organs: While each National Salvation Association generally possesses its own organ publication, beyond these, specialized anti-Japanese magazines run by anti-Japanese journalists sprang up like mushrooms after rain (雨後의 筍과 같이). Among the most famous are To Haengji's (陶行知) "National Crisis Education" (國難敎育), Ch'u Tobun's (**奮 - likely 鄒韜奮) "Mass Life" (大衆生活) and "Life Weekly" (生活週刊). The "National Salvation Times" (救國時報), published in Paris (巴里), serves as the Chinese Communist Party's guiding organ for anti-Japanese activities, with the party's foremost theorist, Chin So'u (陳紹禹), and others writing for every issue.

7.  Military Circles: The 19th Route Army (19路軍), 29th Army (29軍), Northeastern Army (東北軍), Guangxi Army (廣西軍), etc., all form one wing of the front.

8.  Political Circles: The Social Democratic Party (社會民主黨)-affiliated Chinese National Revolutionary League (中華民族革命同盟), the Chinese National Revolutionary Action Committee (中華民族革命行動委員會), etc., are all components of the front. <9>

**Summary**

This 1937 article, written by the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs Information Department, outlines the history of boycotts in China, noting that 9 out of 11 major boycotts since 1898 were directed against Japan. It then details the formation of the Anti-Japanese People's Front by June 1936, listing key participating organizations across various sectors: students, cultural figures, literary/arts circles, commerce/industry, women, propaganda outlets (magazines, newspapers), military units (like the 19th Route Army), and political groups. The article highlights the central role of students and mentions specific organizations, publications, and prominent individuals involved in the anti-Japanese movement. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is revealing as it shows the Japanese colonial administration's detailed surveillance and analysis of anti-Japanese sentiment and organization in neighboring China, published in a Korean-language magazine likely intended for administrators or collaborators within Korea. Historians might find the specific categorization of groups, the list of boycotts, and the identification of key figures and publications useful for understanding both the structure of Chinese resistance and the nature of Japanese intelligence gathering and dissemination within its empire during this period.

中國의 抗日團體
						外務省 情報部
中國에서 처음 外貨 排斥 運動이 이러난 것은 지금부터 40년전의 1898년이다. 그 뒤 11회의 全國 뽀이콧트가 있었는데 卽
年次 / 相對國 / 原因
1905 / 米國 / 掛支移民法
1908 / 日本 / 第2辰丸事件
1909 / 日本 / 安奉線改築問題
1915 / 日本 / 所謂 21個條問題
1919 / 日本 / 山東問題
1923 / 日本 / 旅順 大連回收問題
1925 / 日本 / 5.30事件
1925 / 英國 / 5.30事件
1927 / 日本 / 山東出兵
1928 / 日本 / 濟南事件
1931 / 日本 / 下寶山事件 及 滿洲事變
이상 11회 중 9회까지가 對日 뽀이콧트이다.(중략-원문)
그 뒤 1936년의 6월경까지에 광범한 계층을 含한 抗日人民戰線이 완성되었다. 이제 계층별로 戰線 內에 含하여 있는 주요한 抗日團體를 들어보면 如左.
1. 학생층 5.4운동 (1919년 5월 4일 北京의 학생에 의하여 이루어진 親日派 曺汝霖邸憢打事件) 이래 학생의 사회 운동에 있어서의 역할은 지극히 크다. 콤민테룬 及 中國共産黨의 發聲에 대하야 가장 몬저 호응한 것도 학생이다. 실로 1935년 12월의 北平 學生 大데모가 전선결성의 狼火엿다. 각 大學, 中學, 심하면 小學生에까지 抗日救國會의 조직이 있었다. 그것이 연합하여 지방적으로 예컨대 北平學生救國聯合會를 만든다. 上海에도 南京에도 漢口에도 成都에도 만든다. 최후에 그 總中椹機關으로서 上海에 全國學生救國聯合會가 조직 (1936년 6월) 학생과는 따로히 대학 교수, 중학 교원, 소학 교원 등도 구국회를 가지고 있다.
2. 文化界 대학 교수, 변호사, 기자 등이 중심이 되어 各地에 文化界救國會가 성립되였다. 其中에 가장 유명한 것은 上海文化界救國會로 沈*儒, 章乃器, 등 유명한 人民戰線 巨頭 들은 최초 이 會를 출발점으로 하였다.
3. 文藝界 上海에 된, 中國文藝家協會, 文藝工作者 一派, 著作人協會등이 여기 속한다. 문학자, 평론가, 기자, 新劇배우, 영화인들이 一槪 여기 網羅되여 있다.
4. 商工界 工人救國聯合會 등의 계통이 여기 속한다.
5. 婦女界 上海를 위시하야 각 대도시에는 대개 婦女救國會의 조직이 있다.
6. 선전기관 각 救國會는 대개 기관지를 가지고 있는데 이 以外에 抗日 쩌-나리스트의 경영하는 전문의 抗日雜誌가 雨後의 筍과 같이 발생되었다. 陶行知의 「國難敎育」 **奮의 「大衆生活」 「生活週刊」 등이 그 중에서도 유명하고 巴里에서 발행되는 「救國時報」는 中國共産黨의 抗日 指導 機關으로 黨 제일의 理論家 陳紹禹 등이 每號 집필하고 있다.
7. 軍界 19路軍, 29軍, 東北軍, 廣西軍 등이 모다 전선의 一翼을 형성하고 있다.
8. 政界 社會民主黨系의 中華民族革命同盟, 中華民族革命行動委員會 등 모다 전선의 一分子이다. ＜9＞
