Haedanghwa Flower Melee: Polydor and Columbia Records, Sŏnu Ilson and Ch'ae Kyuyŏp

Poetry and prose surrounding the *Haedanghwa* (海棠花 - Sweetbrier/Rugosa Rose) of Myŏngsa Simni have been abundant every summer until now, but this year, it has suddenly become a target for record companies looking to capitalize on the heightened mood for folk songs. A white-hot competition (白熱的 競爭) has erupted between the two companies, Polydor (포리돌) and Columbia (컬넘비아), creating a scene much like the "Sakura Ondo Battle" (사꾸라온다 - 櫻音頭合戰) that took place in past years (往年) in Tokyo (東京) between singers like Ichimaru (市丸) and Katsutarō (勝太郞). First, let's look at the lyrics:

Polydor's Sŏnu Ilson

When the Haedanghwa Blooms
1. When the budding haedanghwa flowers wear a smile,
The eighteen-year-old sea maiden's face also turns red.
It was when the haedanghwa bloomed that he left,
And it's said he'll return when the haedanghwa blooms.
2. When spring butterflies flutter about, seeking the flower's scent,
The eighteen-year-old sea maiden's heart also pounds.
3. When the flower petals flutter down, withered and falling,
The eighteen-year-old sea maiden's heart also grows sorrowful.

Columbia's Ch'ae Kyuyŏp

Myŏngsa Simni
An old story of spring's return to Myŏngsa Simni (明沙十里), never to come again.
Though my beloved who whispered among the haedanghwa is here,
O setting sun beyond the western hills! O flocking seagulls!
Were the harbor (浦口) and the white sandy beach (白沙場) just a dream?

As dusk falls on the blue mountains (一暮蒼山), the horizon (水平線) is distant.
Though my beloved gazed endlessly (하염없이),
Youth (青春) is futile (부즐없는), unable to return once gone.
I shall weep through this long night.

Though I resolve not to think of it again, how can I bear to forget?
Though my beloved pledged (맹서하든) to live a hundred years (百年) together,
Our fate (因緣), full of regrets (한번많은 우리신세 - *likely error for '한 많은 우리 신세', 'our fate full of regrets'*), is a broken connection.
Clinging (부여잡고) to the haedanghwa, I heave a sigh.

The song sung by Sŏnu Ilson, with her thin and delicate voice, overflows with a charming atmosphere (멋진 풍정), as if she were gathering haedanghwa blossoms in the folds of her skirt and scattering them with each step of her boat-sock-like feet. It beautifully expresses the sentiments of girlhood (少女 時代) fond of sorrow (哀想). Consequently, its sales performance was excellent, ranking among the top few best-selling records (레코-드) this year.

Ch'ae Kyuyŏp's voice carries a certain heartrending (처절한) quality, much like the voice of Fujiyama Ichirō (藤山一郞) in "Is it Sad Tears or a Sigh?" There's a sorrowful tone (哀調) like someone lamenting life while wandering alone through a dense forest (密林) path at twilight (黃昏) in late autumn (느진 가을), yet it never forgets to remain thoroughly masculine (男性的). This "Myŏngsa Simni" is also abundantly filled with that signature style of his. It is said to be another one of this summer's best-selling records. <45>

**Summary**

This article reports on a commercial rivalry between Polydor and Columbia record companies in 1937 Korea, centered on competing songs about the *haedanghwa* flower. Polydor released "When the Haedanghwa Blooms" sung by Sŏnu Ilson, noted for its delicate, feminine charm and appeal to sorrowful sentiments, achieving high sales. Columbia countered with "Myŏngsa Simni" sung by Ch'ae Kyuyŏp, characterized by a masculine, heartrending vocal style reminiscent of Japanese singer Fujiyama Ichirō, which also sold well. The article explicitly compares this Korean record rivalry to a similar "Sakura Ondo Battle" between popular singers in Tokyo, highlighting the influence of Japanese popular culture trends. For historians, the article reveals the commercialization of Korean folk themes (like the *haedanghwa* associated with specific locales like Myŏngsa Simni) by competing record labels during the colonial period, adopting marketing strategies and even aesthetic comparisons directly from Japan. It also documents the distinct vocal styles and popularity of key singers like Sŏnu Ilson and Ch'ae Kyuyŏp in the late 1930s Korean popular music scene.

明沙十里 海棠花를 싸고도는 詩文은 지금까지 해마다 여름철이 되면 만해지었거니와 이제 금년에 드러서는 민요 정조를 고조하는 레코-드 회사의 한목 보는 표적이 되어 俄然 「포리돌」과 「컬넘비아」의 두 회사에서 白熱的 경쟁이 이러나서 마치 왕년 東京에서 있든 市丸, 勝太郞 등의 「사꾸라온다」(櫻音頭合戰) 비슷한 광경을 이루었다. 이제 爲先 가사부터 보건대

1. 봉오리 볼깃볼깃 해당화 우슴띠면

해당화 부여잡고 한숨지노나

鮮于一扇이 부른 노래는 그 가늘고 연연한 목청에다가 치마 자락에 해당화 송이 가득 싸안고서 외씨 같은 발을 한자곡 한자곡 옴겨 드딜 때마다 송이송이 뿌리며 오드시 멋진 풍정이 전편을 싸고 넘처 애상을 좋와하는 소녀 시절의 기분을 잘 나타내여 보였다. 그러기에 올에 잘 팔닌 레코-드 중 몇 재번에 안 가게 그 판매 성적이 좋왔다.
蔡奎燁의 목청은 『슬은 눈물인가 한숨인가』의 藤山一郞의 목소리같이 어덴지 모르게 처절한 맛이 떠돈다. 느진 가을 황혼길 밀림 속을 혼자 거닐면서 인생을 탄식하는 그러한 애조로되 어디까지든지 남성적임을 잊지안는다. 이 『明沙十里』도 氏 일류의 그러한 맛이 떠돌고 남음이 있다. 역시 올 여름 드러서 잘 팔닌 레코-중의 하나이란다. ＜45＞
