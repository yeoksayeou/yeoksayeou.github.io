The Current Situation and the Lineup of Various Organizations

Sijunghoe (時中會)
1. Founded: November 6, Showa 8 (1933)
1. Members: Approx. 2,000

1. Construction of a new life

Taedong Minuhoe (大東民友會)
1. Founded: September 20, Showa 11 (1936)
1. Members: 140
1. Principles (綱領)
1. We emphasize the consciousness of great nationalism (大國家主義) and strive for the prosperity of the entire nation.
2. We aim for the improvement of the political and economic status of the Korean people.
3. We [promote/support] (*成) the establishment of state-controlled economy and plan (圖함) for the stability of the lives of the masses.
4. We [reject/exclude] (배*함) communism and all other anti-state ideological factions.
5. We establish morals adapted to the progress of the times and aim (기함) for the spiritual unity of the nation's people.

**Summary**

This article from the October 1937 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine provides basic information about two contemporary Korean organizations: Sijunghoe and Taedong Minuhoe. It lists their founding dates, membership numbers, core principles (綱領), and key officers (任員). The principles reveal the political leanings of these groups during the colonial period. For historians, this piece is valuable as it documents the existence and stated aims of specific organizations operating under Japanese rule. Sijunghoe's explicit goal of "Naeseon Ilga" (Korea and Japan as one family) highlights assimilationist collaboration, while Taedong Minuhoe's platform shows a blend of Korean status improvement within a framework supporting state control, anti-communism, and "great nationalism," reflecting complex responses to colonial modernity and Japanese wartime mobilization. The list of officers includes figures whose activities during this period are of significant historical interest.
