**Body Text Translation**
Patriotic Donations Reach 1.3 Million Won, Led by Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak's 40,000 Won

Once the Sino-Japanese Incident (日中事變) broke out, the utmost sincerity (至誠) for the nation (國家) surged in unity between the upper and lower classes (上下一致), and the fever for patriotic donations (愛國獻金熱) has become quite high on the Peninsula (半島).

Thus, about 40 days after the Incident (事變), donations from the Peninsula alone are said to have surpassed 1.3 million Won. Among these, starting with Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak's (崔昌學氏) 40,000 Won, followed by Mr. Kim Yŏnsu's (金년洙氏) 20,000 Won, Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl's (朴榮喆氏) 10,000 Won, Mr. Pak Hŭngsik's (朴興植氏) 5,000 Won, and so on, donations reportedly extended down to even 1 or 2 jeon. <11>

**Summary**

This news brief reports that following the outbreak of the Sino-Japanese Incident in 1937, patriotic donations collected within the Korean Peninsula reached 1.3 million Won in about 40 days. It highlights significant contributions from prominent Korean businessmen, including Ch'oe Ch'anghak (40,000 Won), Kim Yŏnsu (20,000 Won), Pak Yŏngch'ŏl (10,000 Won), and Pak Hŭngsik (5,000 Won), while noting that donations came from all levels of society, down to mere pennies (jeon). For historians, this article is interesting as it documents the mobilization of resources from colonial Korea for Japan's war effort. It showcases the participation, whether voluntary or coerced, of Korean elites in supporting the Japanese empire during wartime, reflecting the complex dynamics of collaboration and pressure under colonial rule.
