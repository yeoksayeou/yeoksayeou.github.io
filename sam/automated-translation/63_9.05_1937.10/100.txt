The Sino-Japanese Incident and Foreign Public Opinion

United States

New York Daily News: "We praise the prudent attitude of the Secretary of State regarding the current Incident. At that time, Secretary of State Stimson continued unilateral protests, facing the risk of war between Japan and the US. The United States must not become an agent for protecting British and French interests in China. While we have passive sympathy for the weak China (支那), the situation does not warrant actively siding with the Chinese side, so let us not lose our composure. Whatever war occurs is none of our concern."

Sacramento Bee:
"Considering it, the United States does not possess interests in China that need preservation to the extent of going to war with Japan. Interfering with Japan's actions in China would mean war."

New York Times:
"The current Incident will likely not escalate into such a major affair in the end. For Japan, having tested the Soviet's weakness in the Heilongjiang Incident (黑龍江事件), it will likely feel a greater sense of security in carrying out its North China policy."

United Kingdom

Daily Herald:
"The North China Incident clearly risks developing into a Sino-Japanese war. War in the Far East is a great misfortune not only for Japan and China but for the entire world. The best way to prevent a Sino-Japanese war would be swift joint action by Britain and the United States."

France

Paris Soir:
"The responsibility for the expansion of the Incident lies entirely with China. That is, because China (支那) moved a large force of 30 divisions northward, Japan decided to dispatch reinforcements to counter them. Under the current circumstances, an on-site resolution is not impossible. However, if the Nanjing Government obstructs this at all costs, the problem will likely be very difficult to resolve."

Germany

Berlin Börsen-Zeitung:
"The cause of the clash between Japan and China is the anti-Japanese sentiment among the Sino-Japanese populace and the Chinese Army. That is, the Hebei-Chahar Political Council (冀察政務委員會) broke established precedent by lodging objections to the Japanese army's drills, further fueling the anti-Japanese fervor."

Beyond this, the stances from the Soviet Union, Italy, etc., are omitted. <9>

**Summary**

This article compiles foreign press reactions to the early stages of the Second Sino-Japanese War (referred to as the "Sino-Japanese Incident") in 1937. American newspapers largely advocate for isolationism, arguing US interests in China aren't worth war and warning against becoming entangled in European powers' affairs. The British press expresses concern about escalation and calls for Anglo-American cooperation to prevent war. French and German papers tend to place responsibility on China, citing Chinese troop movements or anti-Japanese sentiment as provocative factors leading to Japanese military responses.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it reflects the international political climate that heavily influenced Korea's situation under Japanese rule. The varying foreign opinions, especially the American isolationist stance contrasting with British concerns and French/German views seemingly more sympathetic to Japan's stated reasons, would have been noted by both Japanese authorities and Korean independence activists. For historians, it showcases the global perception (or lack thereof) of Japanese expansionism just before it fully erupted into WWII, and how these differing international views (or their portrayal in colonial media) might have shaped public opinion or policy within colonized Korea.

日中事變과 外國의 輿論
米國
紐育 델리, 뉴-스紙 「우리들은 今番 事變에 대한 國務卿의 신중한 태도를 賞讚한다. 그때 스팀손 國務長官은 단독으로 抗議를 계속하여 日米 開戰의 위험에 직면하였다. 米國은 中國에 있서서 英佛의 利益 權護의 압잡이가 되여서는 안 된다. 吾人은 弱者 支那에는 소극적으로 동정하지만 적극적으로 中國 측에 부틀 情勢는 아니니 냉정을 일치마라. 무슨 전쟁이든 우리들이 關知할 바가 아니다」

사꾸라멘트, 비紙
「생각건대 米國은 日本과 전쟁하여서까지 보존하여 필요할 권익을 中國에 가지고 있지 안타. 그런데 중국에 있어서의 日本의 행동을 干涉한다면 그는 전쟁을 의미하게 된다」

紐育타임스紙
「今番 事變은 결국 그렇게 큰 일에는 이르지 안으리라. 黑龍江事件에서 소비엣의 약점을 시험한 후의 日本으로서는 北中정책 수행에 있어 더 만흔 安全感을 가지리라」

英國
델리, 헤랄드紙
「北中事變은 명백히 日支전쟁에 발전할 위험이 있다. 日支 뿐 아니라 전 세계에 대하여도 極東에서의 전쟁은 큰 불행이다. 日中 전쟁을 방지할 최선의 방법은 英米 양국의 신속한 공동 동작이리라」

佛國
巴里 쏘알紙
「事變확대의 責任은 전혀 中國에 있다. 즉 支那가 30簡師의 大軍을 北上시키였기 때문에 日本은 對抗上 增援軍 파견을 決하였다. 현재의 사태들로는 현지 해결은 불가능치 안타. 다만 南京政府가 어디까지든지 이를 방해한다면 문제는 도저히 해결되기 어려우리라」

獨逸
伯林, 벨젠즈아이씽紙
「日支 양국 충돌의 원인은 日中 民衆 及 中國 軍隊 중에 있는 抗日 氣分이다. 즉 冀察政務委員會가 종래의 慣例를 破하고 일본군의 연습에 異議를 申立하였다 하면 抗日 氣勢를 더 올닌 것이리라」
이밖게 쏘비엣, 伊大利 등 각지 論調는 略한다. ＜9＞
