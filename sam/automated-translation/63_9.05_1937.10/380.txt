My First Work
						Ch'unwŏn

It was 28 years ago, when I was eighteen. Looking at my diary, it says I completed something called "Ho" (虎 - Tiger) in the eleventh month of the third year of Yung-hŭi. And although I expressed great satisfaction, it does not remain today.

Around the same time, I wrote "Yŏn ka" (戀か - Is it Love?) in the national script [Korean] and published it in the Paekkŭm Hakpo (白金學報) of Meiji Gakuin (明治學院), where I was studying. I remember this as the first time a novel I had written was printed. Looking at my diary from that time, it seems I was quite happy about it.

Among the works published in our language [Korean], the one that could be called my novel is, I think, "Kyuwŏn" (閨怨 - Grievance of the Inner Chamber), published in the Taehan Hŭnghakpo (大韓興學報), although I cannot clearly remember if it was that year or the next. However, I have no memory of my feelings at that time. What I wrote after that was "Ŏrin Hŭisaeng" (어린 犠牲 - Young Sacrifice), which was serialized for three issues starting from the March issue of Sonyŏn (少年). This was titled "Koju Yŏk" (孤舟譯 - Translation by Koju), but that was because the editor, Kongnyuk (公六) (now Yuktang 六堂), probably thought it was a translation; in reality, it was my own creation. For me, the joy upon its completion was quite deep, and I also remember struggling while writing it.

However, the time when I can say I truly tasted the joy of a first work was when the manuscript for "Kasil" (가실), published some years ago in the Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報), was completed. When this was finished, I was truly happy. No matter what others may say about it, for me, this is my first work, my first son.

But I await the day when I will truly taste the joy of a first work in the future. When an unfinished product like myself writes such things, it feels like I've grown old, and I chuckled to myself. <47>

**Summary**

The author, Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su), reflects on his early writings, trying to pinpoint which one truly felt like his "first work" (處女作). He mentions several pieces published in various journals like "Paekkŭm Hakpo," "Taehan Hŭnghakpo," and "Sonyŏn," including "Ho," "Yŏn ka," "Kyuwŏn," and "Ŏrin Hŭisaeng." Despite these earlier publications, he declares that the feeling of completing "Kasil," published later in the "Tong-a Ilbo," gave him the genuine joy of a first creation, calling it his "first son." He ends on a self-deprecating note, acknowledging he still feels like an "unfinished product" hoping for a future true first work. For historians, the article offers insight into the early career of a major (and later controversial) figure in modern Korean literature, Yi Kwang-su. It names several important publications and figures (like Ch'oe Nam-sŏn/Yuktang) of the colonial period, mapping out part of the literary landscape of the time and reflecting the author's evolving self-perception of his work.

나의 處女作
						春園
열 여덜 살 적 즉 28년 전이다. 일기를 보건대 륭희 삼년 십일월에 「虎」라는 것을 완성하였다고 썻다. 그리고 대단히 만족한 뜻을 표하였으나 그것은 지금 남지 아니하였다.
그와 거진 동시에 국문으로 『戀か』라는 것을 써. 내가 다니던 明治學院의 白金學報에 내였다. 내가 지은 소설이 인쇄가 되기는 이것이 처음이라고 기억한다. 그때 일기를 보면 그것이 꽤 깃벗든 모양이다.
우리 글로 발표된 것 중에 나의 소설이라 할 만한 것은 역시 그 해인가 그 이듬해인가 분명히 기억은 못하여도 大韓興學報에 내인 『閨怨』이라고 생각한다. 그러나 그때에 감정은 기억이 없다. 그 담에 쓴 것은 少年 3월호서부터 인가 『어린 犧牲』이란 것을 세호 동안 련재한 것이 있다. 이것이 『孤舟譯』이라고 하였으나 그것은 편즙인인 公六(지금은 六堂)이 아마 번역인가 하여서 그리한 것이오 기실은 나의 창작이다. 내게는 그것이 완성되던 때의 깃쁨이 꽤 깊었고 또 쓰느라고 애쓰던 것도 기억된다.
그러나 내가 진실로 처녀작의 깃쁨을 맛보앗다 할 말한 것은 년전 東亞日報에 게재한 『가실』의 원고가 완편된 때다. 이것이 끗난 때에는 정말 깃뻤다. 남들은 이것을 아모렇게 말하더라도 내게 있어서는 이것은 처녀작이오 첫아들이다.
그러나 나는 이 앞날에 정말 처녀작의 깃쁨을 맛보기를 기다린다. 나 같은 미완성품이 이런 소리를 쓰게 되면 늙어진 듯 하야 혼자 웃섰다. ＜47＞
