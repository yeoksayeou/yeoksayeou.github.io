**Body Text Translation:**
An Early Autumn Boat Trip, Seeking Seoul's Famous Scenic Spots

Every year when the season returns, the number of people seeking mountains and seas increases. Those who live nestled in the bosom of nature might not know, but the people of the bustling, dusty city (紅塵萬丈), where the clamor reaches ten thousand leagues, greatly long for cool mountains and blue waves. Thus, on Sundays in Seoul, young men and women flock in pairs towards the outskirts, or to Inch'ŏn's Wŏlmido Island, or towards the Han River area for boating. The writer has also gone boating on the Han River several times, but below the Han River Pedestrian Bridge (漢江 人道橋), not only are there too many people, but for some reason, due to the air and other background elements, I could not feel great satisfaction. Therefore, this time, I went searching far and wide for the Kŭmgang Boat Pier (金剛뽀트장) located in Hasuil-chŏng (下水溢町) downstream on the Han River, across from Yulto Island (栗島), which is said to have been newly established last year through the efforts of several interested individuals.

Indeed, I inwardly exclaimed for the first time that this place alone was an idle spot where one could launch a single small boat (一葉片舟) and play with brimming poetic sentiment. Sitting in a boat here and looking around in all directions, one can see various beautiful things; from the text written below, you can well guess how rich the poetic charm (詩趣) is.

Kwanak Ch'ŏngnam (冠岳晴嵐)   Yongho Muwŏl (龍湖舞月)
Usan Mokchŏk (牛山牧笛)   Yulto Myŏngsa (栗島明沙)
Map'o *pŏm (麻浦*帆)   Noryang Ŏhwa (鷺梁漁火)
*Am Moyŏn (*巖暮煙)   Yangjin Nakcho (楊津落照)

Furthermore, not only are there few people, but the air is also good, and it is a very quiet place. Lying obliquely on the boat, looking up at the blue sky and white clouds, and playing a tune on the flute, the thought indeed arose that I had come to a fairyland (仙境).<44>

**Summary**

The article describes the author's search for a pleasant boating spot near Seoul in the early autumn of 1937. Dissatisfied with the crowdedness below the Han River Pedestrian Bridge, the author visits the newly established Kŭmgang Boat Pier near Yulto island, downstream on the Han River. The author praises the location's tranquility, clean air, and scenic beauty, listing eight traditional scenic views visible from the river. The experience is described as idyllic, akin to visiting a fairyland. For historians, the article offers insight into the developing leisure culture among Seoul's urban population during the colonial period, contrasting the desire for natural tranquility with the perceived noise and dust of the city. It also documents specific locations for recreation and provides a contemporary perspective on the Han River landscape, including traditional scenic viewpoints.

初秋의 船遊, 서울의 名勝을 차저
해마다 제철이 도라오면 산과 바다를 찾는 사람이 많어진다. 자연의 품속에 안기워 사는 사람들은 모르겠으나 紅塵萬丈 싯그러운 都會의 사람들은 시원한 산과 푸른 물결을 여간 그리워하지 아니한다. 그리하여 서울에서는 일요일이면 야외로 또는 仁川 月尾島로 또는 뽀트노리를 하기 위하여 漢江 방면으로 젊은 남녀들이 쌍을 지여 몰녀간다. 필자도 漢江으로 여러 번 뽀트를 타러 나가 본 적이 있었으나 漢江 人道橋 밑은 너무 사람이 않을 뿐 아니라 공기와 기타 배경에 었전 일인지 큰 만족을 느낄 수 없었다. 그리하여 이번에는 작년부터 멫 有志의 힘으로 신설되였다는 漢江 하류 栗島越便 下水溢町에 있는 金剛뽀트장을 밸느고 벨너서 한번 찾어가 보았다.
과연 이곳만은 一葉片舟 띄워 놓고 시흥이 도도하게 놀 수 있는 한가한 곳이라고 입 속으로 비로소 감탄하였다. 이곳에서 뽀트를 타고 앉어 사방을 둘너보면 아름다운 여러 가지를 볼 수 있나니 다음에는 쓰는 글에 의하야 얼마나 詩趣가 농후한가를 가히 짐작할 수가 있다.

더욱 사람이 많치 안을 뿐 外라 공기도 좋고 매우 고요한 곳이다. 뽀트 우에 비스듬이 누어 푸른 하늘 흰 구름을 처다보며 퉁소 한 곡조를 부러불 제 果然 仙境에 온 듯한 생각이 떠도랏다.＜44＞
