**Body Text Translation:**
The Reviving Tong-A Ilbo
Is Paek Kwansu the President, and has Kim Sŏngsu completely retired?

The *Tong-A Ilbo*, which had been suspended for nearly a year, was allowed to resume publication from July 1st. Shortly thereafter, the Lukouchiao Incident broke out, and subsequently, the Sino-Japanese war situation expanded comprehensively, causing the situation to change moment by moment, and newspaper extras began to flood the streets like pouring rain. The reappearance of the *Tong-A Ilbo* happened at just such an opportune time; to borrow the words of Tokutomi Sohō, war makes newspapers grow larger. Thus, the company was able to rapidly expand its influence without the huge investment of capital, the at least three years of time, and the significant intellectual effort that would have been required to restore its former state in peacetime.

Consequently, although only two or three months have passed, the *Tong-A Ilbo* is once again being distributed throughout all important locations domestically and overseas, and its influence is also flourishing. Looking now at the key figures in the "new cabinet" (新內閣), the core of the renewed company:
Director and President: Paek Kwansu (白寬洙)
Director and Manager: Im Chŏngyŏp (林正燁)
Director: Chang Tŏksu (張德秀)
Director: Yang Wŏnmo (梁源模)
Director: Kim Yongmu (金用茂)
Director: Hyŏn Chunho (玄俊鎬)
Auditor: Chang Hyŏnsik (張鉉植)
Auditor: Hyŏn Sangyun (玄相允)
Thus, Paek Kwansu took over from the former president, Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹). It is a new development that Chang Tŏksu, who was formerly vice president, has become just an ordinary director in the new organization. Even more noteworthy is that the name of the company's pillar, Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙), has completely vanished from the list of executives. Regarding this, I intend to write in detail in a separate article titled "*Tong-A Ilbo* and Kim Sŏngsu" next time, so I will omit it here. In this way, the company's leadership structure has undergone a qualitative change.

Recovering Circulation and Advertising Volume
Above, I mentioned that it seems to have recovered almost its former imposing stature. To elaborate slightly further, firstly, regarding the pages, despite the soaring price of paper, from the first day of resumed publication, it published 12 pages daily, not falling behind the *Chosŏn* [Ilbo] and *Maeil Sinbo* newspapers. According to President Paek, its circulation fully recovered about a month after the ban was lifted, and moreover, reaching today, perhaps due to war-related news, it is said to have increased by about 30 percent. Even taking this statement with a grain of salt (lit., removing the promotional aspect), while a 30 percent increase may be uncertain, it seems the restoration to its original state is complete. Also, it appears accurate that the 300 branch offices have reopened; this can be seen nowadays by observing the continuous stream of provincial branch managers coming and going at the entrance of the *Tong-A Ilbo*. So, what about the advertising base? How is the advertising performance of the company, which relies to a certain extent on advertisers in Tokyo and Osaka? For a while, it drew public attention that advertisers were banding together to form a boycott ('non-publication' alliance, 不揭載) because it was an "anti-national newspaper" (非國民的 신문), with rumors flying about whether it had materialized or not. However, for merchants swayed by interests, if the paper circulates widely, what problem would that be? Evidence for this can be seen in the gradual appearance of Tokyo and Osaka advertisements these days. However, perhaps combined with the summer season, the advertising lineup seems not yet back to its former state. Also, the long-standing plan for expanding the company building seems to be on hold for now, as it appears somewhat difficult to suddenly increase capital, solicit new payments, or take out loans at this time.

Editorial and Business Staff
What is the lineup in the Editorial and Business Bureaus? With newcomers in and old-timers out, former chief editorial writer Kim Chunyŏn (金俊淵) has stepped down and gone down to read at some farm associated with Posŏng College (普成專門); Editor-in-chief Sŏl Ŭisik (薛義植) has also withdrawn; following the successive resignations, taking responsibility, of figures like editorial writer Yi Yŏsŏng (李如星), the new organization seems to be a provisional lineup, with the Editor-in-chief position yet to be decided. Profile of the New President
What kind of person is the newly appointed President Paek Kwansu? His hometown is Tŏksan-ni, Sŏngnae-myŏn, Koch'ang-gun, North Chŏlla Province. In Taishō 4 (1915), he finished Chŏnsu School (專修學校), the predecessor of the Law College (法專學校), and in Taishō 6 (1917), he went to Tokyo and entered the Faculty of Law at Meiji University (明治大學). Around the time he was a third-year student there, he participated in a certain political movement that arose in the Korean society in Tokyo, was imprisoned for one year, was released, re-entered the same university in Taishō 9 (1920), graduated the following year, and in Taishō 12 (1923), he took the position of Business Bureau Chief at the *Chosŏn Ilbo*. Later, along with Messrs. Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊), Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹), and others, he attended the Pacific Conference held in Hawaii (布哇) as one of the envoys from this land. Then, in the summer of Shōwa 3 (1928), he resigned from the *Chosŏn Ilbo* and managed a printing house called Hongmunsa (弘文社) near Pagoda Park in Seoul. He also launched the monthly magazine *Tongbang P'yŏngnon* (『東方評論』, Eastern Review) together with the late Yi Kwanyong (李灌鎔) and others. This year, he is 48 years old (lit., his springs and autumns are 48). Currently facing the internal issues of restoring the company's finances and personnel matters in staff organization, and externally dealing with the numerous complex and difficult problems concerning the Police Affairs Bureau (警務局), society, and the current situation (時局), Mr. Paek's heavy responsibilities that must all be handled are indeed quite burdensome. <21>

**Summary**

This 1937 article reports on the revival of the *Tong-A Ilbo* newspaper after nearly a year's suspension by Japanese authorities. It notes the paper's rapid recovery in circulation and influence, paradoxically boosted by the high demand for news following the outbreak of the Second Sino-Japanese War (Lukouchiao Incident). The article details significant leadership changes: Paek Kwansu replaces Song Chin-u as President, former VP Chang Tŏksu becomes a regular director, and crucially, the influential founder Kim Sŏngsu is removed from the executive board entirely. Despite challenges like high paper costs, a temporary advertiser boycott labeling it an "anti-national" paper, and paused building expansion plans, the paper is regaining its footing. The piece lists the extensive new staff across departments and provides a biography of the new president, Paek Kwansu, highlighting his past nationalist activities and journalistic/business experience. From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the operational realities of Korean media under Japanese colonial rule, including suspensions and pressures like advertiser boycotts instigated due to perceived "anti-national" stances. It illuminates the impact of major geopolitical events (the war) on the media landscape and provides insight into the internal power dynamics and personnel shifts within a major Korean cultural and political institution like the *Tong-A Ilbo*, particularly the sidelining of its founder, Kim Sŏngsu, amidst the tightening wartime controls.

復興하는 東亞日報
白寬洙씨 社長에 金性洙씨는 아조 引退乎?
將近 1년이나 停刊 中에 있든 東亞日報는 7월 1일부터 解禁이 되야 다시 續刊이 되어 나왔다. 그러자 미구에 蘆橋溝事變이 돌발하고 게속하여 日中 戰局이 전면적으로 확대되야 시국이 시각을 다투어 轉變이 되자 거리거리에는 號外가 빗빨치듯 나오게 되였다. 東亞日報의 재등장은 때마츰 이러한 호기회이었으니 德富蘇峰의 말을 빌면 전쟁은 신문을 크게 만든다 하는지라 평시엇다면 원상을 회복하기에 거대한 자금과 적어도 3년의 세월과 많은 人智를 너허야 할 것을 그토록 큰 대가의 지불이 없이 신속히 社勢를 확장할 수 있었다.
그리하야 겨우 2, 3개월에 불과하였건만 東亞日報는 또다시 海內 海外 重要地에 깡그리 그 신문지가 배포되고 그 성세, 또한 隆隆한 바가 있다. 이제 更新한 該社의 근간인 新內閣의 인물들을 보건대

恢復되는 紙數과 廣告量
이 우에서 昔日의 威容을 거지반 회복한 드시 말하였는데 그를 좀더 상기한다면 첫재 지면에 있어 紙價 高騰한 때에 불구하고 續刊 初日부터 朝鮮, 每申 兩紙에 뒤떠러짐이 없이 1일 12頁紙를 내였고 그 부수도 白사장의 말을 드러서는 解禁約 月餘에 그 부수를 완전히 회복햇고 그 우에 금일에 及하여는 전시 뉴-쓰 관계인지 모르나 오히려 3할 가량 증가하였다 한다. 이 말을 선전성을 빼고 드러도 3할 증가는 몰나도 원상 회복은 완전히 되였는듯 또 300의 지국도 다시 開門하게 된 것만은 정확한 듯하니 요지음 東亞日報 문전에서면 시골 지국장이 連絡不絶 來往함을 보아 알 수 있다. 그러면 廣告 地盤은 엇떠한고. 東京, 大阪 廣告主에 機分 의존하여 지내는 該社 광고 업적은 어떠한고. 한동안 광고주가 연합하여 「非國民的 신문」인즉 「不揭載」동맹을 하자 하야 일설에는 그가 성립되었느니 었저느니 하야 시청을 끄을었는데 그러나 이해에 좌우되는 상인에게 紙數 만히 내여 보내인다면 그것이 무슨 문제되랴. 그 증거로 요지음 차츰 東京 大阪 廣告가 실니는 것으로 보아 알 수 있다. 다만 夏節을 겸병하여 광고진은 昔日과 가치 아직 되여 있지 못한 듯하다. 그리고 年來의 포부이든 社屋增築案도 지금에 있서 갑작히 증자도 新 拂入도 차입도 하기가 다소 어려운 사정이 있서 當今 보류하는 눈치였다.

新社長의 人物
신임 사장 白寬洙씨란 어떠한 인물인고. 全北 高敞郡 星內面 德山里가 그의 고향이라. 大正 4년에 法專學校 전신이든 專修學校를 마치고 大正 6년에 東京 드러가 明治大學 法科에 입학하였다. 그때 3학년이 되든 때 이든가 在東京 朝鮮人 사회에서 이러난 某種의 정치운동을 하다가 入獄 1년 다시 나와서 9년에 同대학에 復校 그 翌年에 졸업하고는 同 12년에 朝鮮日報 영업국장에 취임하였다. 그 뒤 尹致昊, 宋鎭禹 등 제씨와 함께 이 땅 使節의 1인으로 布哇서 열닌 太平洋會議에 參列하였다. 다시 昭和 3년 여름 朝鮮日報를 辭하고 서울 파고다 공원 부근에서 弘文社라는 인쇄소를 경영. 다시 월간 잡지 『東方評論』을 故 李灌鎔씨 등과 함께 발간하였다. 올에 춘추 48이라. 방금 대내적으로는 社의 재정 회복과 직원 조직의 인사 문제와 대외적으로는 對警務局 對社會 對時局의 幾多 複雜多難한 문제를 모도 다 처리하여 노치 않으면 안될 씨의 重務는 실로 자못 무거운 바 있다 할 것이다. ＜21＞
