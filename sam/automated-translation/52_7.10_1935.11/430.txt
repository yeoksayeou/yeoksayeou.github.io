Burying a Wild Chrysanthemum
						No Ch'ŏnmyŏng

If you hadn't been on the sloping hill of the field,
How lonely autumn would have been?
Though no one calls you Queen,
You decline the splendid garden of spring,
Blooming among nameless grasses,
Bride of the wilderness, guarding your lonely integrity alone.
Your heart, softer than reed flowers, was so lovely
I didn't want to leave you carelessly in the rough field.
I carefully picked an armful, held you close, and returned,
Moving you to the vase on my desk.
There I prayed you would bloom freely, <260>
But day by day, you lost the vitality I saw in the field,
Your smiling face drooped,
Your radiant form withered, one petal, two petals.
Is the clear water I pour abundantly each morning
Not even equal to a single drop of dew from the field?
You should have ultimately spread freely and grown wild
In the familiar scent of earth in the rough field...
A foolish hand trembling with regret
Holds you again, withered and dry,
And comes out to bury you
Beneath the high sky, under the cool hill, wild chrysanthemum!
There is your blue ceiling.
Here is your cozy autumn cushion.
- Oct. 14. - <261>

**Summary**

The poem expresses the speaker's deep regret after picking wild chrysanthemums from a field. Initially captivated by their resilient beauty in the wild, the speaker brings them home hoping to cherish them, only to watch them wither despite care. Realizing the flowers belonged in their natural environment, the speaker remorsefully takes the dying chrysanthemums back to bury them where they came from. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects a personal connection to nature and themes of unintended harm through misguided affection. During the colonial period, such focus on nature and personal sentiment, detached from overt political commentary, might have been a safer form of expression or represented a turn towards appreciating native landscapes and inherent Korean aesthetics.

들국화를 무드며
						盧天命
들역 경사진 언덕에 네가 업섯든들
가을은 얼마나 적적햇쓰랴?
아모도 너를 女王이라 부르지 안는데도
봄의 화려한 동산을 사양하고
이름조차 모룰 폴들 사이에 석겨 펴
외로운 절긔를 홀로 직히는 황야의 시약씨여
가-ㄹ꼿 보다 부드러운 네 마음 사랑스러워
거츠른 들역에 함부루 두고 십지안어
한아름 고히 꺽거 안고 도라와
책상 우 화병에 너를 옴겨 노앗다.
거기서 맘대로 화창하라 비럿드니＜260＞
들에서 보든 그 생긔 나날이 이러 버리고
우슴거든 네 얼골은 수그러저
빗나든 네 모양 한닙 두닙 병드러 가는구나
아츰마다 병이 넘게 부어 주는 맑은 물도
들역의 한 방울 이슬만 못하드냐?
너는 끗내 거츠른 들역 정든 흙냄새 속에서
맘대로 퍼지고 멋대로 자랏써야 할 것을...
뉘우침에 떨리는 미련한 손이
시들고 말른 너를 다시 안고
노픈 하늘 시언한 언덕 아레
무더 주려 나왓다 들국화야!
저긔 너의 푸른 천정이 잇다.
여긔 너의 포근한 가-ㄹ방석이 잇다.
-10. 14.-＜261＞
