Article Title: 버드나무 그림자에 (In the Willow's Shadow)
Author: 異河潤
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

In the Willow's Shadow
						Yi Ha-yun
Looking toward a distant dreamland
A face reflected in the willow's shadow

Why are those bygone days so sorrowful?

Old memories blossom on every branch
That shadow reflects again upon my face
Why are those past times so sad?

My path ahead is hazy like the fog
This heart gathered in the willow's shadow
Why are those past youthful days so longed for?<278>

When the Snow Melts
You promised you would come when the snow melts
Why does my love, who left for the north, not come?
That snow has melted and piled up again some three times now
The snow melts once more, but why do you not come?

Is it because the land you went to, the north, is so cold
That the piled snow does not melt, so you do not come?
Or is your suffering body so exhausted
That you forget the passing time and do not come?

The footprints left when you departed, treading on the snow,
Were they left as a sign you could never return?
If so, before those traces fade,
I should have followed, even that night, after you left.<279>

**Summary**

These two poems by Yi Ha-yun express themes of sorrowful nostalgia and longing. The first poem, "In the Willow's Shadow," reflects on past memories, sadness, and an uncertain future while observing a face mirrored in a willow's shadow. The second poem, "When the Snow Melts," poignantly captures the pain of waiting for a loved one who promised to return from the north when the snow melted, a promise unfulfilled through three winters, leading to despair and regret. For historians, these poems offer insight into personal expressions of melancholy and longing common during the colonial period. The second poem's reference to the "north," the "suffering body," and the inability to return could potentially be interpreted as a veiled commentary on exile, imprisonment, or separation caused by the political circumstances of the time, making it particularly interesting for studying indirect expressions of hardship under Japanese rule.

버드나무 그림자에
						異河潤
아득한 꿈나라를 바라보면서
버드나무 그림자에 어리운 얼골

지나온 그 녯날이 어이 그리 애닯하

녯 기억 가지마다 피어 오르네
그 그림자 내 얼골에 다시 비최니
지나 온 그 시절이 어이 그리 서러워

내 압길 몽롱하기 안개갓구려
버드나무 그림자에 어러운 이 맘
지나 온 젊은 날이 어이 그리 그리워＜278＞

눈이 녹으면
눈 녹으면 오신다고 언약하시고
북녁으로 떠나신 님 웨 안오시나
그 눈 녹고 또 싸힘이 어언간 세 번
눈은 다시 녹건마는 웨 안 오시나

가신 나라 북족이라 하도 치워서
싸힌 눈이 안 녹으니 아니 오시나
괴로우신 몸이시니 너무 지처서
가는 세월 이즈시고 아니 오시나

눈을 밟고 떠나실 제 지운 발자욱
다시 못 올 표적으로 남긴 것인가
그러타면 그 자최가 흐리기 전에
님 가신 뒤 그 밤도아 따라 갈 것을＜279＞
