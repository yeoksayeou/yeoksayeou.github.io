**Summary**

This short piece simply lists several prominent teahouses (dabang) located in various districts of Seoul (Keijō) in 1935. The list includes the district name (Insadong, Chongno, Hasegawachō, Meijichō, Namdaemun-t'ong, Kwanch'ŏl-tong) and the name of the establishment. It offers a glimpse into the burgeoning modern cafe culture in colonial Seoul, showcasing a mix of Western (Venus, Mexico, Eliza, Diana, Boston, Bon Ami) and Sino-Korean (Nangnang, Paegabwŏn) names for these spaces. Historians might find this list valuable for studying urban social spaces, cultural consumption, modernization, and the geography of leisure under Japanese rule, noting the mix of Korean and Japanese administrative district names and the adoption of foreign names for establishments.
