Magazine Title: 삼천리 제8권 제11호
Publication Date: 1936년 11월 01일
Article Title: 歡迎!! 淸秋十月에 孫選手歸來 (Welcome!! Athlete Sohn Returns in the Clear Autumn October)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Welcome!! Athlete Sohn Returns in the Clear Autumn October
1. Where, when, and how did you first hear the primary news of Sohn's great victory, and how happy were you?
2. If we were to undertake a good commemorative project on this occasion, what kind would be best?

Tongil Bank, Yŏngbo Hapmyŏng Hoesa, Min Kyu-sik

1. I heard the first news of Sohn's victory at home that morning through an extra edition (號外) and the radio (라디오). As for what kind of emotion I felt at that time, unless you were one of us eagerly awaiting each word from the radio announcer (안나운스), you couldn't imagine or describe the emotion of that moment.
2. It is truly a magnificent feat that a short (키적고), yellow-faced East Asian person stepped onto the world stage, competed fairly based on skill against athletes from various countries, and won first place in the most difficult athletic event, the marathon (마라손). There must certainly be some kind of commemorative project. On such an occasion, holding a Chosŏn-wide celebration would be good, but strengthening the foundation of an organization like the Chosŏn Sports Association (朝鮮體育會), which has contributed much to Chosŏn's sports institutions, to enable the Chosŏn sports world to operate more vigorously would also be good.

Tonguhoe
Yi Kwang-su

1. I heard it at Wŏnsan Songdowŏn. When I first heard it, my heart just pounded, but thinking about it over and over again, my eyes welled up many times.
2. A welcoming celebration upon his return, a commemorative gymnasium, a commemorative sports school, the founding of a commemorative library – I wish all four could be done. I also wish for a commemorative statue to be erected.<34>

P'yŏngyang, Cho Man-sik

1. I learned of Sohn's championship victory via the first report from a friend who came to my house around 7:30 AM on August 10th, and the emotion at that time was immense.
2. For a commemorative project, establishing a cooperative gymnasium seems most appropriate.

Anak, Kim Hong-nyang

1. I heard the first report at an inn (旅舍) in Kyŏngsŏng, and what I felt at that moment would be the same as everyone imagines.
2. Planning some kind of commemorative project for Sohn's heroic feat would be very meaningful, but I only hope for the selection of its type and other details by the senior figures (先輩諸氏).

Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo President, Yŏ Un-hyŏng

1. I learned the joyful news that Sohn had finished first through the radio. I cannot put into words how happy and moved I was at that time. So, I immediately had an extra edition published.
2. Everyone sincerely congratulates and is moved by Sohn Ki-chŏng's great victory. However, tastelessly (멋없이) making a fuss about Sohn's victory, with shops saying they'll send merchandise or someone offering to pay his school expenses (學資), is not something Sohn himself would be very pleased about, and I believe it holds no social significance either. If we are truly to undertake a project for the triumphant Sohn, founding a commemorative gymnasium to plan for (圖함) the improvement of Chosŏn youth's physical education henceforth would likely be best. <35>

Tong-A Ilbo Editorial Bureau Chief, Sŏl Ŭi-sik

I do not intend to say anything on this occasion. I heard the first report of the great victory via a telegram that arrived at the newspaper office.

Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun School President, Yu Ŏk-kyŏm

2. I heard the first report of "Our Sohn's" championship at my humble home (鄙家) shortly after 2 AM on August 11th via a special delivery phone call (寄別전화) from the Tong-A Ilbo office. The moment I heard the joyful news, feeling that the teachings of ancient sages (先哲) like "What cannot be achieved when the spirit is concentrated?" and "Patience is the mother of success" were indeed true, tears of overwhelming emotion flowed, I thanked the Heavenly Father, and sincerely prayed that "Our Sohn" would practice self-care and self-respect forever.
1. With large donations (願納) from those with material means and small contributions (分錢의 喜捨) from numerous enthusiastic individuals, commemorative gymnasiums and libraries could easily be built in various places. If the total sum of donations (願納) and contributions (喜捨金) exceeds 500,000 wŏn, one commemorative sports school could be founded. A celebratory gathering could easily be held with just the impassioned spirit (感奮心) from the very night the joyful news was received.

Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo Editorial Bureau Chief, Yun Hŭi-jung

1. I heard the joyous news of Sohn's first-place finish at the newspaper office. I believe it was exactly 2:20 AM.
2. A gymnasium.

Han Yong-un

I first learned of Mr. Sohn Ki-chŏng's first-place marathon victory upon seeing the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo extra edition, and it was invariably refreshing (未嘗不 통쾌하였다). (omitted section)
Mr. Sohn's success certainly lies in his own aptitude and effort, but more than that, he must have had many thoughts in his mind and exerted the strength of his entire body.
That is something deserving sympathy for him. In any case, the fact that East Asians also have the power to conquer the world is, above all, evidence, so all youth should practice self-respect (自愛하라).
Any commemorative project for Mr. Sohn is fine. However,<36> it seems better for us not to make an excessive fuss and to act calmly.

Chosŏn Ilbo Chief Editor, Sŏ Ch'un

2. I first heard the joyous news that Sohn Ki-chŏng had achieved a great victory from a police officer at the P'ot'ae-ri substation in Pohye-myŏn, Kapsan County, South Hamgyŏng Province (咸南甲山郡普惠面胞胎里 駐在所 警官) at 4:20 PM on August 15th, on the way back after completing the Paektu Mountain exploration. The joy at that time was indescribable. Our Paektu Mountain exploration team, including the laborers, numbering over 80 people, were overcome with tearful emotion, jumping around uncontrollably.
2. I believe building a commemorative gymnasium to encourage physical education among the youth would be good.

Chosŏn Christian Temperance Youth Association President, Yi Tae-wi

1. I saw this joyful news in a train somewhere in Manchuria (滿洲) and thought that Chosŏn people are inherently geniuses (天才家), so this was only natural, and no other particular thoughts (殘念) arose.
2. If there is to be a commemorative project for Sohn, building a sports school and establishing a gymnasium as an auxiliary facility would be good.

Ewha Girls' High School, Kim Ch'ang-che

1. I first saw it in the Chosŏn Ilbo on August 10th. Ah—now we too are advancing onto the world stage! I was so moved I was on the verge of tears. It seems it must have been the same kind of emotion as Sohn felt at the moment of his victory.
2. Constructing a commemorative gymnasium seems the most meaningful and enduring project.

Sin'gyŏng, Kim Kyŏng-chae

I will answer only your first question.
I don't know sports, but I am glad that in determining victory and defeat, an East Asian won in the world stadium. I learned this from seeing a mimeographed extra edition issued by the Tong-A Ilbo Sin'gyŏng branch office.<37>

Chosŏn Presbyterian Church General Assembly President, Chŏng In-kwa

1. I heard the joyous news of Sohn Ki-chŏng's world championship through the radio in front of our house. The whole family was overflowing with emotion.
2. I hope a commemorative gymnasium for Sohn Ki-chŏng is made (맨드러) so that those interested in physical education can gather to discuss and encourage (討議獎勵) the future of Chosŏn sports.

Lawyer, Yi In

2. When I found out at home through extra editions from various newspapers, I thought, "An East Asian became world number one in the marathon!" Yet, as the victory flag fluttered proudly, I felt both happy and, perhaps overflowing with joy, also sad. How many from our support group were there at that spot?
1. For us, who have nothing but our bodies (身外無物), a strong physique is needed above all, and only from a strong physique can a sound mind be found. Therefore, to build this robust body, constant exercise, meaning physical education, becomes necessary. Thus, I feel boundless joy when I see the figures of youths leaping about lively and valiantly (活潑勇壯) in schoolyards or on the streets (街路). Therefore, I held 100 percent anticipation and interest in the Berlin expedition of both Sohn and Nam. Fortunately, on this occasion that has made the whole world ecstatic (狂喜케 하고) and boasted of the superiority of East Asians to the world, while all commemorative events are good, within the scope of our growing strength, I hope a commemorative gymnasium might come into being. For the specific plan, 100,000 wŏn would suffice.

Chosŏn Products Promotion Association
Chŏng Se-kwŏn

1. I first learned of Sohn's marathon championship by seeing the Tong-A Ilbo extra edition on the morning of August 10th, and my joy at that time was truly indescribable. What made me even happier was demonstrating to the entire world that our short-statured East Asians possess the aptitude to achieve world-class victory in anything.
1. To commemorate Sohn eternally, I believe establishing a commemorative gymnasium is necessary.<38>

Chungang High School, Han Ki-ak

2. The next morning (翌朝) at 6:30, I found out by seeing the newspaper extra edition placed by my pillow (枕頭). Is this really true? I looked, looked, and looked again. However, it felt like what was naturally due had arrived.
1. Sohn's marathon victory is something anyone who is an East Asian should congratulate and be moved by. However, there is no need to make an excessive fuss about this. Is there not a need to think a bit calmly? Greater East Asian victories and more global contributions must lie ahead. Holding something like a joint welcome and congratulatory meeting (聯合 歡迎祝賀會) involving various sectors seems appropriate.

Chosŏn Christian Youth Association General Secretary, Ku Cha-ok

2. I awoke to the sound of "Extra, extra! (號外오)", was overcome with emotion, couldn't fall back asleep, and had many various thoughts.
1. The immediately feasible option is to hold a celebratory gathering; the enduring commemorative project would likely be a sports school.

Kim Hang-kyu

2. I don't remember the exact date I heard the news of Sohn's world championship, but I learned it from the Chosŏn Ilbo's first extra edition. What emotion could a mere wooden puppet (木偶人) who cannot speak (啞), cannot write (文盲), and cannot work (白痴) have? I simply offered three cheers for Sohn Ki-chŏng's health.
1. I know there are various discussions and efforts by many people regarding Sohn's heroic feat (壯與) this time. My opinion is that, above all, establishing a memorial hall (記念관) and preserving it permanently would be best.

Samch'ŏlli Company, Kim Tong-hwan

1. I happened to be traveling in the southern provinces (南鮮), and that day I first learned of the great victory by seeing a newspaper extra edition at Pusan pier, and tears just kept coming. Because they kept coming, I just kept crying. In the evening, I went out to the seaside again, and someone gave me an extra edition from the Osaka Asahi (大阪朝日) from the ferry (連絡船). Holding the extra, somehow I just kept crying. Perhaps they were tears of joy too overwhelming to contain.
2. I want to advocate for the establishment of a sports school. I intend to write about this separately.<39>

Returning to the Old Place = Berlin, dispatched Aug. 27th

Sohn Ki-chŏng

The Olympics are over. From now, I will take the Siberian (西伯利亞) railway and return once again to the longed-for old place (녯터) with a joyful heart.
I pray for everyone's health, and wish to convey all my words (말슴) after returning and seeing you. <35>

**Summary**

This 1936 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine surveys prominent Korean figures about Sohn Kee-chung's Berlin Olympics marathon victory. Respondents overwhelmingly express immense joy, pride, and emotional reactions upon hearing the news, often via radio or newspaper extras. When asked about commemorative projects, suggestions range from celebratory events to more permanent structures like statues, libraries, gymnasiums, and even sports schools or associations, reflecting a desire to foster national athletic development and honor Sohn's achievement. The responses highlight the deep impact of Sohn's victory on the Korean psyche during the colonial period. Historians might find valuable the outpouring of nationalistic sentiment channeled through sports, the specific suggestions for nation-building projects (gyms, schools) disguised as commemoration, and the cautious tones of some (like Han Yong-un or Han Ki-ak) advising against excessive public display, perhaps mindful of the colonial authorities. The inclusion of Sohn's brief message from Berlin adds a personal touch.

歡迎!! 淸秋十月에 孫選手歸來
1, 孫군의 쾌승의 제1報를 처음 어느 곳서, 어느 때, 어떠케 듯고, 어떠케 기뻣읍니까.
2, 이 기회에 좋은 기념사업을 하자면 어떤 것이 좋켔읍니까?

東一銀行, 永保合名會社 閔奎植

1, 孫군 우승의 제1보는 집에서 그날 아침에 號外와 「라디오」로 들었읍니다. 그 때의 如何한 감격을 받았느냐 하는 것은 「라디오」로 안나운스의 한마디 한마디를 고대하든 우리들이 아니면 그 때의 감격의 如何한 것은 상상할 수도 없고 형용키도 어렵겠지요.
2, 이제 키적고 얼굴빛 노란 동양 사람이 세계적 무대에 나서 각국 선수와 정정당당히 실력을 다투어 「마라손」이라는 제일 어려운 운동 종목에 제1위를 얻었다는 것은 참으로 장한 일입니다. 반드시 무슨 기념적 사업이 있어야지요, 이러한 기회에 全鮮的으로 축하회같은 것을 하는 것도 좋켔지마는 朝鮮의 운동기관으로 공헌이 많은 朝鮮체육회같은 것을 더 한층 기초를 튼튼히 하야 朝鮮의 운동계를 더 한층 힘있게 활동시키는 것도 좋켔지오.

同友會
李光洙

1, 元山松濤園에서 들었읍니다. 처음 들을 때에는, 가슴만 울넝거렀으나, 두고두고, 생각할 때에 여러 번 눈이 젖었읍니다.
