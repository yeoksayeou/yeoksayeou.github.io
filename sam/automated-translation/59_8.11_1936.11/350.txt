Samch'ŏlli Secret Chamber (三千里 機密室)
Rumor of Tong-a Ilbo's 100,000 Won Loss

It is generally speculated that the loss incurred by the Tong-a Ilbo company due to its fourth suspension will reach a huge amount. According to an investigation by a certain informed source in the newspaper district, if the suspension lasts less than 6 months, the direct loss from advertising fees, newspaper subscriptions, etc., will amount to approximately 100,000 wŏn. If it extends beyond that, the loss is expected to increase by about 10,000 wŏn per month. Of course, this excludes the indirect losses suffered. Attention is now focused on how Tong-a Ilbo will handle salary payments to its employees during this suspension (during the previous suspension, half salaries were paid).

Eight Reporters Dismissed

Released by Police Department Without Incident

As detailed elsewhere, Tong-a Ilbo Social Affairs Department Head Hyŏn Chin-gŏn and eight other reporters were arrested by the Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department in connection with this incident. However, as a result of the investigation, the case was deemed unworthy of being sent to court, and after 29 days of detention, they were all released in late September. The day after their release, Tong-a Ilbo reportedly dismissed all eight individuals, holding them responsible. Furthermore, four employees of the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo were also released after 29 days.

Follow-up Report

(Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo President Yŏ Un-hyŏng and Editor-in-Chief Yun Hŭi-jung attempted to resign taking responsibility, but the board of directors has currently withheld acceptance of their resignations. Similarly, at Tong-a Ilbo, President Song Chin-u is reportedly withholding acceptance of the resignations of Chief Editor Kim and Editor-in-Chief Sŏl.)

Survey of Incomes of Figures from Various Fields (Part 1)

In the case of various government offices, there is a fixed staff registry, which clearly shows monthly salaries, ranks, etc., at a glance. (201) Anyone can look up this staff registry and easily find out who holds what position, their monthly salary, who is at what rank, and their monthly pay. However, regarding the income or financial status of individuals working in general society, whether in banks, companies, schools, newspaper companies, magazine companies, or literary circles, we have had no way of knowing. Furthermore, even within the same place, positions are divided into top-level and section chief levels, indicating stratification. We cannot help but be somewhat curious about all these points. Moreover, the current world tends to rank people based on the amount of their salary or wealth, rather than evaluating them based on character, ability, or disposition. Introducing this to the general public might not be an idle matter, so we now introduce the results of our first secret investigation. As we plan to continue reporting on this in the future, one thing we must apologize for is that matters related to income or other financial affairs are customarily kept secret. People are reluctant to disclose them and avoid doing so, thus there might be some ambiguity, stating ranges like 'around that level' rather than exact figures **** up to **. Here, we will write them down sequentially.

Banking Sector

Regarding banks, leaving aside regional banks or branch banks, we will look at a few important banks headquartered in Kyŏngsŏng.
The Bank of Chosŏn is the central bank. This bank has key figures such as the Governor, Vice Governor, Directors, and Department Heads, and their salaries differ according to these classifications.
Katō Yūzaburō (Governor) has an annual salary of 18,000 wŏn, which is about 1,500 wŏn per month. Including entertainment expenses, executive bonuses (twice a year, upper and lower terms), and others, his annual income reaches 50,000 to 60,000 wŏn. Since expenses like banquets and travel are covered by the bank, his income is quite substantial. Vice Governor Matsubara has an annual salary of around 13,000 wŏn, and adding entertainment expenses, executive fees, executive bonuses, etc., his annual income is in the 50,000 wŏn range, comparable to Mr. Katō. Directors below them have an annual income of around 15,000 wŏn, including monthly salary and bonuses. Department Heads vary depending on seniority, but generally average 500 to 600 wŏn per month, meaning their annual income, including bonuses, is likely not less than around 10,000 wŏn.
The Chōsen Shokusan Ginkō (Industrial Bank of Korea) is a major kingdom; its influence is not only beyond the reach of the Bank of Chosŏn but it also wields the formidable power of a special bank. Here, positions are divided into President (頭取), Vice President (副頭取), Directors, and Department Heads.
President Ariga's official annual salary according to the articles of incorporation is 10,000 wŏn. However, including executive bonuses, his annual income is said to be no less than 50,000 wŏn. Adding allowances from serving as an executive in various other companies, his actual annual income exceeds 80,000 wŏn.
Vice President Watanabe's annual salary is around 8,000 wŏn. (202) Including executive bonuses and others, his annual income is reportedly not less than 50,000 wŏn. Directors and Department Heads are said to have salaries largely similar to those at the Bank of Chosŏn, but their bonuses seem decidedly higher than those at Chosŏn Bank.
The Chōsen Savings Bank (朝鮮貯蓄銀行) is suddenly a step down, yet still far superior to Korean-run banks.
President Itsumori (伊森頭取) has an annual salary of 12,000 wŏn, but including executive bonuses and others, his annual income is around 30,000 wŏn. Department Heads reportedly have an annual income of around 5,000 to 6,000 wŏn.
Commercial Bank (商銀) President Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl receives 3,600 wŏn annually but spends over 10,000 wŏn a year on entertainment expenses. Managing Director Hori is comparable to the President. Department Heads earn around 160 wŏn per month, up to about 200 wŏn.
Tongil Bank (東一銀行) Chairman of the Board & Representative Director Min Kyu-sik has an annual salary of 3,500 wŏn, Managing Director Ichikawa 3,000 wŏn annually. Department Heads earn around 150-160 wŏn.
Hansŏng Bank (漢城銀行) is largely the same as Tongil Bank. Haedong Bank (海東銀行), starting with Managing Director Mun Sang-u, has executives and department heads receiving allowances averaging around 120-130 wŏn, making it seem like the bank with the lowest income in the banking sector.

Company Sector

Regarding companies, their number runs into the thousands, and they vary greatly in size, making it impossible to list them all. Therefore, we will limit ourselves to representing 2 or 3 companies here.
Han Sang-yong, President of Chosŏn Life Insurance (朝鮮生命), combines his salary from the company, allowance from Chosŏn Trust (朝鮮信託), and allowance as a member of the Central Advisory Council (中樞院參議), reaching an annual income of around 13,000-14,000 wŏn.
President Tani (谷社長) of Chosŏn Trust has an annual salary of 12,000 wŏn; including bonuses and others, his annual income is said to be 50,000-60,000 wŏn. Department Heads have monthly salaries ranging from 120-130 wŏn to around 200 wŏn. The Branch Manager of Mitsui Bussan reportedly earns over 70,000 wŏn annually, don't be surprised. The Mitsubishi Branch Manager, Mr. Suzuki, earns around 40,000 wŏn annually. Excluding Hwasin Department Store President Pak Hŭng-sik, Managing Director I Ki-yŏn earns a monthly salary of around 200 wŏn. Hwasin Chain Store (連鎖會社) Managing Director Chu Yo-han earns around 150 wŏn, and other Department Heads earn between 100 and 120-130 wŏn.

Among the newspaper companies within Chosŏn, the three major papers, along with Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo (京日), Maeil Sinbo (毎申), Chosŏn Sinmun (朝新), Chosŏn Ilil Sinmun (朝鮮日日), and Sanggong Sinmun (商工新聞) are published in Kyŏngsŏng City. We will now list the incomes of the top figures at the three major papers, Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo, and Maeil Sinbo, based on our investigation.
Tong-a Ilbo President Song Chin-u has a monthly salary of around 250 wŏn, plus several thousand wŏn for entertainment and other expenses covered by the company. Business Manager Yang Wŏn-mo earns 180 wŏn, Chief Editor Kim Chun-yŏn 170 wŏn, Editor-in-Chief Sŏl Ŭi-sik 140 wŏn, Social Affairs Department Head Hyŏn Chin-gŏn around 100 wŏn, and other Department Heads around 80 to 90 wŏn.
Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo President Yŏ Un-hyŏng earns 200 wŏn. Other Bureau Chiefs earn around 120 wŏn, the Chief Editor also around 120 wŏn, and Department Heads around 70 wŏn.
Chosŏn Ilbo President Pang Ŭng-mo earns 200 wŏn. Bureau Chiefs earn around 120-130 wŏn, and Department Heads 80 to 100 wŏn. Among newspaper companies, Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo's salaries are the lowest. Besides these, for Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo and Maeil Sinbo:
Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo President Tokizane Akihō has an annual salary of 12,000 wŏn, but combining various incomes, his annual average is no less than 90,000 wŏn. Bureau Chiefs have monthly incomes of 300 to 400 wŏn, and Department Heads also earn over 150 wŏn, around 200 wŏn.
Maeil Sinbo Vice President I Sang-hyŏp has an annual salary of 3,000 wŏn; including travel expenses and others, his monthly income is around 400 wŏn. Bureau Chiefs and Chief Editors earn around 150 wŏn, and Department Heads around 100 to 120 wŏn.

Mr. Yi Joins Chosŏn Sanggong Sinmun

Mr. I Kŭn-jong, who gained newspaper experience at the Sidae Ilbo over ten years ago, subsequently engaged in various activities, and until recently honed his business acumen at Hwasin, seems to have had an epiphany. He recently resigned from Hwasin and re-entered the newspaper world. He reportedly acquired the Chosŏn Sanggong Sinmun (朝鮮商工新聞), which was run by a Japanese mainland person, Saitō Gōkichi. The motivation stems from his uncle, I Pŏm-sŭng, who, dissatisfied with his position as Industry Section Chief in Hwanghae Province, decisively quit. He then joined hands with a certain wealthy man from Hwanghae Province (rumored to be a millionaire from gold mining), and securing a loan from him, looked for an institution to serve as a guide for Chosŏn's commerce and industry. He secretly negotiated to take over the aforementioned newspaper organization. When their opinions matched, it was decided to purchase it for 100,000 wŏn. 50,000 wŏn in cash has already been handed over, and the remaining 50,000 wŏn is to be paid in full within a year. There is some back-and-forth regarding the name change; one view is that the name cannot be changed until the remaining 50,000 wŏn is fully transferred, while another says it's difficult to change the name from a Japanese mainland person to a Korean. This matter concerns the wise parties involved, and it's unnecessary for a third party to comment. In any case, the fact that another daily newspaper (albeit a specialized one) has come into Korean hands is something to celebrate, and we sincerely wish for the newspaper's future development with both hands raised. Mr. I Kŭn-jong is expected to take the position of President of the newspaper company and handle all affairs.

Ōsaka Mainichi's 1,000 Wŏn Prize-Winning Author and Station Porter

When the Ōsaka Mainichi Shimbun recently offered a 1,000 wŏn prize for a popular novel, the winning Korean author, Kim Sŏng-min, is reportedly an author facing material hardship and is currently working as a station porter (驛夫) at Nyŏngbyŏn Station in North P'yŏngan Province.

Ms. Na Hye-sŏk Writing a Novel

Female painter Na Hye-sŏk (writing under the name Na Yu-sŏk), who has settled in the ancient capital Suwŏn to wield her colorful brush, is said to be currently writing day and night for a novel competition sponsored by a certain newspaper company.

Poetess No Ch'ŏn-myŏng's Devotion

Poetess No Ch'ŏn-myŏng, a product of Ewha Women's College, recently resigned from the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo company. Henceforth, she intends to solitarily cultivate her poetic thoughts at a bright window desk, focusing her efforts on poetic works (205) and creative writing.

Sale Price of Old Chongno Police Station Building

The Western-style building located in the heart of the Chongno commercial district, which housed the Hansŏng Electric Company during the late Korean Empire and later the Chongno Police Station, was recently vacated by the Communications Bureau's Postal Life Insurance Section, which had been renting it. It was sold not long after they left, the building was demolished, and now some construction work, including stone cutting and lumber preparation, has been ongoing for several months. There are rumors of building a hotel or an office building, but it's still unclear what kind of structure is being built. The buyer is a Japanese mainland person, rumored to be a certain official from the Government-General. The rumored price is 600 wŏn per p'yŏng. Since the total area is about 750 p'yŏng (???), the total price would be around 500,000 wŏn.

Hwasin Chain's Rumored 3 Million Wŏn Sales

Hwasin Chain Store Co., Ltd., a one-million-wŏn company with over 300 chain stores across Chosŏn, reportedly found during its fiscal year-end closing at the end of August that its total sales for the year reached approximately 3 million wŏn. Since it mainly deals in general merchandise, if the intermediate profit margin is calculated at 10% of the sales price, the net profit would be 300,000 wŏn; if 5%, it would be 150,000 wŏn. The exact percentage is unclear.

200,000 Wŏn Department Store in Taegu

In Taegu, the key city of Southern Chosŏn, a large department store is set to open soon, funded with 200,000 wŏn by a young Korean businessman. Just as the five major department stores like Hwasin compete in Seoul, a battle for commercial supremacy is expected to erupt in Taegu against the existing *Nakai Department Store. The owner of this new department store is Mr. I Kŭn-mu, a young man in his 30s who has run Muyŏngdang, a store dealing in Western goods and books, for over ten years. He aims to use Southern Chosŏn as his base and eventually advance into Kyŏngsŏng. A 4-story building is currently under construction.
The store opened in Taishō 12 (1923). Current departments include Books and Magazines, Stationery, Sporting Goods, Picture Frames and Art Supplies, Western Goods and Sundries, and Musical Instruments. With construction finishing at the end of November, new departments opening from December will include Travel Goods, Western Furniture, Foodstuffs, Toys, Pottery and Porcelain, Arlnikoko Tableware, Photography Studio, and a Restaurant.

Bank of Chosŏn's Daily Gold Purchase Value Exceeds 130,000 Wŏn

Recently, Chosŏn's gold production has increased, showing a sharp upward trend. Furthermore, due to the strict prohibition of illicit gold exports, the amount of gold brought into the Bank of Chosŏn by general miners has surged, keeping the bank's Bullion Department extremely busy. The average daily purchase amount at the bank is said to exceed 10 kwan (貫). Calculated at the current purchase price in Kyŏngsŏng City of 13 wŏn 7 chŏn per monme (匁) (Tokyo market price is 13 wŏn 50 chŏn), this amounts to an average of over 130,000 wŏn daily. This calculates to about 4 million wŏn per month.

Mr. Pang Ŭng-mo, who already achieved success worth over one million wŏn from the Sakchu Gold Mine in North P'yŏngan Province and runs the Chosŏn Ilbo company, has now joined hands with a certain Japanese mainland entrepreneur to establish a gold mining company capitalized at one million wŏn. The details are that Mr. Pang contributed three gold mines he owns, including the Paekt'o Gold Mine near Chŏngp'yŏng in South Hamgyŏng Province, as investment in kind. The Japanese mainland partner invested 300,000 wŏn, or several hundred thousand wŏn, in cash. The rights are reportedly divided as Pang 4 or 5 parts, the partner 4 or 3 parts, and the remaining 1/10th considered as investment by various individuals working at the mines. This has become a hot topic in the mining industry.

Regarding Mitsubishi Mining (三菱鑛業), which is said to be making significant inroads into the Chosŏn mining industry, (207) an investigation into how many mines they actually purchased over the past year reveals the following: The number of applications received from various places requesting purchase of their mines reached over 200. **Summary**

This article from the November 1936 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine provides a snapshot of various news items and social observations in colonial Korea. It covers the financial fallout from the forced suspension of the Tong-a Ilbo newspaper, the dismissal of its journalists, and similar issues at the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo. A significant portion details the estimated incomes of prominent figures in banking, business, and media, contrasting high salaries in Japanese-led institutions like the Bank of Chosŏn and Shokusan Ginkō with generally lower incomes in Korean-run banks and newspapers. It also lists Korean officials who passed the Higher Civil Service Exam, reports on business ventures like a new department store in Taegu and Pang Ŭng-mo's gold mining company, mentions cultural figures, and notes urban development with Korean-owned buildings in Kyŏngsŏng. The article reflects the economic stratification, the dominance of Japanese capital, the precarious state of Korean media under censorship, and the growing interest in personal wealth as a marker of status during the colonial period. Historians might find the detailed (though estimated) income data particularly valuable for understanding the socio-economic hierarchy, the financial realities of different professions (especially journalism vs. banking), and the economic penetration of Japanese interests versus Korean enterprises in 1930s Korea. The list of civil service exam passers also offers insight into Korean participation (and limitations) within the colonial bureaucracy.

各界人士收入調査狀(一)

各官公廳으로 論하면 職員錄이라는 一定한 것이 있어 그의 月俸, 級數 등 一見明瞭하야(201) 누구나 그 職員錄을 떠드러 보면 누구는 무었인데 月俸이 얼마 누구는 몃 級인데 月給이 얼마, 이러케 容易히 알고 알 수 있게 되었지만, 一般社會에서 혹은 銀行 혹은 會社, 혹은 學校, 新聞社, 雜誌社 文人 등의 各界에서 奉職하는 人士들의 收入 내지 財産狀態에 대하야는 우리로서 알 길이 전혀 없었으며 더구나 同一한 곳에서라도, 首腦級, 課長級으로 區分된만큼 거기에도 層下가 있는 것이니 이런 모든 점에 대하야 우리는 다소 궁금症을 면할 길이 없고 더구나 現世란 그 사람을 人格이나 實力 내지 品性을 本位로 評하지 안코 月給의 다소 財産의 多過로 그 等級을 차리는 世態이라. 이것을 一般에게 紹介함도 閑事가 아닐 것 가터 이제 秘密裡에 第1次로 調査한 것부터 紹介하는 바다. 그리고 今後도 繼續하야 報導하려니와 이것을 보냄에 있어서 한가지 謝할 것은 取入이나 其他財政에 關한 것은 누구나 秘密에 붓처 알이고저 하지 안는 것이 한 慣例가 태서 公開되는 것을 끄리고 廻避함으로 혹은 ****까지 到** 못하고 그런 程度라는 다소 모호한 점이 없지 못함이다. 이에 順次로 써보기로 한다.

銀行側

銀行으로 論하면 地方銀行이나 支店銀行은 고만두고 京城에 本店을 둔 重要한 銀行의 몇가  지를 들어보기로 했다.
朝鮮銀行하면 中央銀行인데 이 銀行에는 總裁, 副總裁, 理事, 課長, 등의 主腦部가 있으니 이 區分에 따러 그 俸給의 差異도 있은 바이다.
加藤友三郞(總裁) 年俸 1만8천원 즉 月俸으로 1천5백원의 程度인데 이외에 交際費 重役賞與(上下2期) 其他를 合하면 5, 6만원의 年收入이 있고 宴會費, 旅費같은 것은 모다, 銀行에서 但當하니까 相當한 收入이겠으며 松原副總裁는 年俸 1만3천원 假量에다가 역시 交際費, 重役費 重役賞與 등을 合하면 年收入 5만원台이니 加藤氏와 伯仲이오 以下 理事級은 月俸 賞與 合하야 年收 만5천원 假量이오 課長級은 不一하야 年祖長短으로 각각 다르나 대체로 月平均 5, 6백원 程度이니 賞與를 合하야 年收 만원 近處는 不下할 것이다.
殖産銀行으로 論하면 一大王國이어서 그 勢範圍가 朝鮮銀行이 밋칠 바 못될 뿐 아니라 特殊銀行의 무시무시한 勢力을 發輝하는 곳이다. 여기는 頭取, 副頭取, 理事, 課長 등으로 區分되었으니
有賀頭取 定款에는 年俸 1만원이다. 그러나 重役賞與, 合하면 年收 5만원 不下한다하며 其他 各會社主役을 兼한 그 手當 등을 合하면 실로 年收 8만원 이상이오
渡邊副頭取 年俸 8천원 假量인데(202) 역시 重役賞與, 기타 合하면 年收 5만원은 不下한다고 하며 理事 課長級은 朝鮮銀行과 大差가 업다고 하나 賞與金만은 朝鮮보다 단연 많은 것 갔다고.
朝鮮貯蓄銀行은 홀연 格下되나 그래도 朝鮮人側 銀行보다는 越等하니
伊森頭取 伊森頭取 年俸 만2천원이나 重役賞與 기타를 合하면 年收 3만원 程度요 課長級은 年收 5, 6천원 程度라 한다.
商銀頭取 朴榮喆氏는 年 3천6백원이나 交際費 年 만여원을 쓰고 堀專務도 頭取와 伯仲하며 課長은 月 백60원 程度로 최고 2백여원 內外라고 한다.
東一銀行 閔奎植氏(取締役會長 及 代表取締役) 年俸 3천5백원, 市川專務 年俸 3천원 課長級 백5, 6십원 내외.
漢城銀行 역시 東一銀行과 大同小異할 뿐이며 海東銀行은 專務 文尙宇氏로 비롯하야, 重役, 課長級이 手當程度로 平均 백 2, 30원 내외라고 하니 銀行界로서는 第一 收入이 적은 곳이라고 보겠다.

會社界
