Impression of Chang Tŏksu, He is a Man of Pure Heart
						Yu Kwangnyŏl

The first time I met Mr. Chang Tŏksu was one day in February of the year the Tong-a Ilbo was founded. It was when the Tong-a Ilbo company was preparing for its launch in a small house below the Cheil Kobo (First High Common School) in Hwa-dong. Through the introduction of a certain Mr. X, I joined the company, and at the same time, Mr. X said,
“Well, let me introduce you,”
and introduced Mr. Chang. At that time, Mr. Chang had his head shaved, and though his round face was shaven, he had prominent sideburns (구레나룻). He was busy with some clerical work (書役), then quickly stood up,
“I (he had a habit of not attaching the particle ‘nŭn’) am Chang Tŏksu,”
he said, and without paying much attention to me, sat back down and continued his clerical work. Being short with a round face, he didn't seem particularly impressive in stature, but his small, sparkling eyes looked clever. And seeing a certain amount of spite (암상) in him, I thought if Mr. Chang had fighting spirit, it would be thanks to those eyes.

When I first met him, I thought he was quite an arrogant person, but as I got to know him, I came to think of him as a pure-hearted (순정스럽고) and unreserved (흉허물없는) person. The senior Mr. X who introduced me to Mr. Chang praised him highly (激讚), saying,
“Among the young men I’ve seen, he is one who stands out above the horizon (수평선상).”
But who can know that? Whether he is above the horizon or below it can only be judged (評定) after the coffin lid is closed. Only time or God can prove it.

Will He Return?

Unsuk'aek (雲水客)

Will Mr. Chang return? If he knows the situation within Chosŏn well, I think he would not board a ship from the docks of New York, but would just stay in America (亞米利加). What is there for him to do if he returns? What is it he would *want* to do, and what is it he *can* do? Even if he were to return now<74> and occupy a pivotal position in the newspaper world, as rumored, the times (時世) have already changed, and it would be difficult for him to unfold the statesmanship (經倫) he might hold in his heart; moreover, it would be difficult to regain the public trust (人望?) of old. Would he want to return at a time like this?

According to hearsay, he has already become economically self-sufficient in America (米國) and lives without worry. Having obtained his doctorate, being fluent in English, and also confident in his writing, it is said he can now earn a considerable income through translation or other clerical work (書役), thus living without receiving assistance (補助) from others. It is said he even refused the assistance sent by the Tong-a Ilbo company.
Is his age now forty-five, or forty-six? He has left nothing in Chosŏn but his old mother and distant relatives (遠戚), so his longing for home (望鄕之情) probably won't be that strong.
He is a Doctor of Economics. Given that his research thesis was said to be "The History of the Development of the British Labour Party," it seems he has studied the labor movement considerably.
Will he return? If he knows the intricate situation within Chosŏn, he will realize it is not the time to return and will not board the steamship (汽船) crossing the Pacific.

I wish a refreshing breeze (一陣淸風) would blow from somewhere. More than anything, I wish a new person, an honest and strong new person, would appear. At a time like this, who is this Chang Tŏksu, about whom there are rumors (所聞) of making a new appearance? (Photo is Mr. Chang Tŏksu)<75>

**Summary**

This article presents two perspectives on Chang Tŏksu in 1936. The first, by Yu Kwangnyŏl, recounts his initial meeting with Chang during the founding of the Tong-a Ilbo. He initially perceived Chang as arrogant but later found him pure-hearted and unreserved, acknowledging his intellect but reserving final judgment. The second part, by "Unsuk'aek," speculates on whether Chang, then in the US after earning his doctorate, would return to Japanese-occupied Chosŏn. The author doubts Chang would return, citing the changed political climate limiting his potential role, his economic self-sufficiency in the US, and his relative lack of close ties remaining in Korea besides his mother. The piece expresses a yearning for strong, new leadership in Korea. For historians, the article offers valuable insight into the contemporary perceptions of a prominent intellectual and nationalist figure, Chang Tŏksu, during his time abroad. It reflects the atmosphere of uncertainty and limited opportunities for Korean elites under colonial rule, the internal debates about returning versus staying abroad, and the cautious hope placed on figures like Chang, even amidst skepticism about their potential impact given the political realities.

張德秀 印象, 그는 純情之人
						柳光烈
필자가 張德秀氏와 처음 맛나기는 동아일보가 창간되든 해 이월 어느 날이었다. 동아일보사가 花洞 第一高普 알에편 조고마한 집에서 創刊 준비를 할 때이다. 某氏의 소개로 필자는 同社에 입사를 하는 동시에 某氏는
『참 인사하지요』
하면서 張氏를 소개하였다. 그때에 張氏는 머리도 깍고 동그란 얼골에 면도도 했는데 「구레나룻」이 많이 낫섯다. 그는 한참 무슨 書役을 하다가 얼른 이러서며,
『나(는 字는 안 부치는 버릇) 張德秀이올시다』
하고 필자를 별로 주의치 안는 태도로 도루 안저서 書役을 하고 있다. 키가 적고 얼골이 동글어서 별로 풍채는 조와 보히지 안으나 그 적고 반짝 반짝하는 눈이 재조있어 보이었다. 그리고 다소의 암상도 들어 보이여 張氏에게 투지가 있으면 그 눈값이라 생각되었다.
처음 맛낫을 때에는 퍽 거만스런 사람으로 알었으나 차차 지내보니 순정스럽고 흉허물없는 사람이라 생각하였다. 나를 張氏에게 소개하든 선배 某氏는
「내가 본 청년중에 수평선상에 뛰어난 사람이라」
고 激讚하나 그것은 누가 알랴. 그가 수평선 이상인지 수평선 이하인지 관을 덥흔 후에야 評定된다. 오직 세월이나 신이 증명할 뿐이다.

그가 도라올가

雲水客

張君이 도라올가, 내 생각 갔해서는 그가 朝鮮안 사정을 잘 안다면 뉴-욕부두에서 배를 타지안코, 亞米利加에 그냥 있슬 줄로 생각된다. 그가 도라와서 할 일이 무었일고? 하고십허 할 일은 무었이고, 할 수 있는 일은 무었일고? 그가 지금 도라와서<74> 세상 소문과 가치 신문사 방면의 樞要한 자리를 차지하였다, 할지라도 이미 時世는 달나, 그 심중에 있을 經倫을 폐기는 어려울 것이요, 또한 녯날의 人望?을 다시 수습하기는 어려우리라, 이러한 때에 그가 도라오려고 할가.

들니는 말에 그는 이미 米國서 경제적으로는 자립하여 생활은 염려없이 하여간다고 한다. 박사학위도 어덧고, 영어에 능하고, 또한 문필에 자신있으매 이제는 飜譯이나 기타 書役으로 상당한 수입을 어들 수 있어서 남의 補助를 밧지안코 지낸다고 한다. 그래서 동아일보사에서 보내든 補助도 사절하였다든가.
그의 나이는 지금 마흔 다섯일가, 여섯일가, 그는 朝鮮에 노모와 遠戚밧게 남긴 것이 없으니 望鄕之情도 그러케 끌치 안을 것이다.
그는 경제학 박사다. 「英國노동당 발달사」가 연구논문이랄 제는 노동운동을 상당히 연구한 모양이다.
그가 도라올가, 조선내의 요지간 사정을 알면 도라올 때 아닌 줄 알고 태평양건너는 汽船을 아니 타리라.

어데선가 一陣淸風이 부러 왓스면 조켓다. 무엇보다 먼저 새사람, 正直하고 힘잇는 새사람이 나왓스면 조켓다. 이럴 때에 새로 등장한다는 所聞잇는 張德秀란 었든 사람이든고 (사진은 張德秀氏)<75>
