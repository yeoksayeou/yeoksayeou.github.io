Repeated Unfortunate Incidents in the Peninsula's Press World, Dong-A Ilbo Suspended, Chungang Ilbo Ceases Publication

The Dong-A Ilbo was suspended on August 27th for its "unpatriotic" (비국민적) attitude. Not only was the newspaper company itself shocked, but the public also received a great jolt. Several days later, on September 4th, the Chungang Ilbo also voluntarily declared a cessation of publication. Following this, for similar reasons, there were mass confiscations and publication bans that shook the peninsula's press world, including the halting of sales of Shin Dong-A, censorship deletions in Shin Kajŏng (New Family), confiscation of Ahi Saenghwal (Children's Life), and confiscation of Kidok Shinbo (Christian News Report), causing clouds of sorrow to drift through people's hearts.
Here, without mixing in any personal views or emotions, we intend only to report the facts regarding the suspension of the Dong-A and the cessation of the Chungang. (Photo is of the Dong-A Ilbo building) <26>

**Athlete Sŏn's National Flag Erased, Ten Employees Detained by Police and Under Investigation**

**The Truth Behind the Dong-A Ilbo Suspension**

First, inquiring into the truth leading up to the Dong-A Ilbo's suspension, athlete Sŏn Ki-jŏng (孫基禎), who had gone to Berlin, won the championship amid worldwide acclaim, received the laurel wreath, and stood on the Olympic podium. Faced with this good news, the Dong-A Ilbo, in the same manner as its fellow papers Chosŏn Ilbo and Chungang Ilbo, rejoiced (雀躍) and published sensational and glamorous pages daily, both morning and evening. These were filled with articles saying Hitler shook hands with Sŏn, that there was a lantern procession (提灯行列) in his hometown in North P'yŏngan Province (平北), that there was a flag procession (旗行列) and speech meeting somewhere else, and that someone donated money. As such reports appeared in the newspapers, the Sŏn Ki-jŏng fervor gradually intensified. Thus, even children and street vendors (兒童走卒) came to know the name Sŏn Ki-jŏng, even if they did not know the names Sŏng Chin-u (宋鎭禹), Pang Eung-mo (方應謨), or Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨).
However, no matter how much the Seoul newspapers clamored, they could not clamor more than the Osaka newspapers, and no matter how much the Seoul newspapers praised Sŏn, they could not praise him more than the Tokyo Broadcasting Station's radio. This is because the Ōsaka Mainichi and Ōsaka Asahi (大每大朝), possessing direct photo transmissions and wireless telephones between Berlin and Tokyo, dedicated full pages daily, vividly depicting Sŏn's every move, making everyone weep with emotional photos and articles. How many times were articles repeated, like the one by the Ōsaka Mainichi's Berlin special correspondent stating that the entire stadium stamped their feet and cheered at the moment of athlete Sŏn's splendid victory, and that the Japanese cheering squad all wept? The pages themselves testify to this. Didn't the editor-in-chief of the Ōsaka Mainichi specifically call athlete Sŏn at the Berlin venue via wireless radio to convey all sorts of emotional news from the homeland and praise him? Yet, our newspapers in Seoul, lacking funds and influence, could not manage such splendid feats. They merely followed the lead of the Ōsaka Mainichi and Ōsaka Asahi, reprinting news from other sources, calling out "Athlete Sŏn, Athlete Sŏn," and repeating the words that tumbled from the mouths of the Tokyo Broadcasting Station announcers. In any case, regarding reporting, the Seoul papers could not keep up with the various newspapers in Osaka and Tokyo due to geographical distance.
Regardless, athlete Sŏn's victory was a thrilling and emotional event. Scenes of both the incoming Governor-General Nam (南) and the outgoing former Governor-General Ugaki (宇垣) joyfully raising congratulatory toasts <27> were published in the Ōsaka Mainichi, and the Tokyo Shimbun reported that during a cabinet meeting, the cabinet ministers also enjoyed a moment of "pride in athlete Sŏn." In this way, people high and low, officials and civilians, old and young, all rejoiced. However, a problem arose here. The "special characteristics of Korea" (朝鮮의 특수성) made people reluctant to raise this celebratory toast in a popular manner. The Police Affairs (警務) authorities, discerning that the emotion of Sŏn's victory could easily transform into a nationalistic (민족적) emotion, intervened midway, banning celebration meetings, prohibiting initiatives to establish a commemorative gymnasium, and banning speech meetings, thus forbidding praise of athlete Sŏn. Consequently, the Chief of the Police Affairs Bureau (경무국장) and the head of the Publications Section (도서과장) summoned newspaper presidents or editors-in-chief about every other day, urging special caution regarding articles about Sŏn. Around this time, on August 25th, the evening edition of the Dong-A Ilbo was confiscated. This was because a photograph was published in which the Japanese flag (日章旗), which should have been on athlete Sŏn's chest, had been erased. Soon after, officials from the High-Level Affairs Section (高等課員) of the Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department (京畿道警察部) were dispatched, arresting and detaining the following 10 individuals from the newspaper company for investigation:
Social Affairs Editor Hyŏn Chin-gŏn (玄鎭健), staff members Chang Yong-sŏ (張用西), Im Pyŏng-ch'ŏl (林炳哲)
Sports staff member Yi Kil-yong (李吉用)
Artist Yi Sang-bŏm (李象範)
Photography team members (寫眞班員) - 4 people
As a result of the investigation, the fact that the Japanese flag had been intentionally erased (抹消) was exposed, leading to the suspension (停刊) order being issued on the evening of the 27th. In addition to the aforementioned 10 employees, the company's chief editorial writer (主筆) Kim Chun-yŏn (金俊淵) was also temporarily arrested but soon released. The managing editor (編輯局長) Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植) was traveling in the provinces around the time of the incident and thus had no connection whatsoever.
According to what is heard, the photograph published on that day's page was reprinted by the company from the Ōsaka Asahi. Several people, including photography staff, sports staff, and social affairs staff, used a white brush to erase the Japanese flag on the chest, making it slightly noticeable (알러지게 한 것이었다고 한다 - likely meaning 'retouched' or 'altered').

**"Shin Dong-A" Chief Editor Taken In**

**Mr. Yang Wŏn-mo Also Summoned Temporarily**

While the parent paper, Dong-A Ilbo, was embroiled in this misfortune, the fire spread to the monthly magazine Shin Dong-A (新東亞), also managed by the company. The magazine's chief editor (主幹) Ch'oe Sŭng-man (崔承萬) is also under arrest and investigation at the Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department. Mr. Yang Wŏn-mo (梁源模), the magazine's editor and publisher as well as the Dong-A Ilbo's business manager (營業局長), was also temporarily arrested but soon released. The September issue of Shin Dong-A was confiscated, and subsequent issues from October onwards cannot be published until an order permitting it is received from the authorities. Shin Kajŏng (新家庭) suffered partial deletions. The reason for the action against Shin Dong-A was likewise that it took an unpatriotic <28> (非國民的) stance by similarly erasing the Japanese flag in the photograph of Nam featured as the frontispiece illustration (卷頭口繪).

**Reason for Dong-A Ilbo Suspension**

**Announced as Statement by Chief of Police Affairs Bureau**

The Dong-A Ilbo has hereby been subjected to an order suspending publication.
The victory laurel won by Mr. Sŏn Ki-jŏng, of Korean origin, in the marathon race at the World Olympics held recently in Berlin is an honor for all of Japan (日本全体), something to be celebrated together by both mainland Japan (内地) and Korea (朝鮮). It should also be used as material for harmony between mainland Japan and Korea; it must not be misused in reverse to induce even slightly an atmosphere of ethnic confrontation. However, it has been generally regarded as regrettable that articles in newspapers and the like have, in fact, tended to adopt a writing style (筆致) that seems liable to provoke (剌戟함과 如한) confrontational sentiments.
Despite repeated warnings from the authorities in the past, the Dong-A Ilbo published a photograph of Mr. Sŏn Ki-jŏng on its August 25th edition. As there were traces (形跡) indicating that the mark of the Japanese flag, which should have been clearly visible in the photograph, had been intentionally erased (抹消), the edition was immediately confiscated (差押處分에 附하고), and the facts were investigated. It was consequently determined (判明) that when reprinting the photograph of Mr. Sŏn Ki-jŏng published in the Ōsaka Asahi Shimbun dated August 23rd, techniques were intentionally used to erase (抹消) the Japanese flag, out of aversion to it appearing in the newspaper. Therefore, an order suspending the publication of said newspaper has finally been issued. Regarding such unpatriotic (非國民的) attitudes, a policy of strict control (取締) will be applied in the future as well, and the general public is also asked to be careful not to make mistakes. (Photo is of the man of the moment, Dong-A Ilbo President Sŏng)

**Chief Editor Kim, Managing Editor Sŏl Submit Resignations**

**President Sŏng and Staff Observing Period of Self-Restraint, Reporting to Work**

When this suspension incident occurred, the company's chief editorial writer Kim Chun-yŏn and managing editor Sŏl Ŭi-sik submitted letters of resignation to President Sŏng (宋), taking responsibility. However, as the police investigation has not yet concluded (一段落을 짓지안코 있음으로), the potential development (發展性) of the incident and its true nature cannot be clearly known, so it is reported that President Sŏng is holding onto the resignations without accepting them yet. Furthermore, it is reported that the president and the entire staff are showing remorse (謹愼의 意) and continue to report to the company daily as before, dedicating themselves to reading.<29>

**Losses Said to Exceed 100,000 Won**

**Restoration Will Require Great Effort**

How much will the Dong-A Ilbo's losses be due to this suspension? Calculating the direct financial losses, out of approximately 30,000 won in newspaper subscription fees for the month of August, assuming two-thirds are uncollected, that's about 20,000 won. Advertising revenue from Tokyo and Osaka was about 10,000 won, and other miscellaneous income means a total monthly income of 30,000-40,000 won has completely disappeared. On the other hand, it is estimated that there is a direct loss of at least several tens of thousands of won each month for employee living expenses and other costs. It is said that during the previous, third suspension (第3次 停刊), which lasted about 5 months from April 17th to the end of August, the Dong-A Ilbo's losses amounted to 150,000 won. When they resumed publication with a new edition on September 1st, they supposedly used a meager sum of only 37,000 won or so remaining as capital for restarting (續刊). Judging by this, since advertising revenue and readership have increased compared to that time, the losses are expected to be even greater now. The period of suspension is one thing, but resuming publication (續刊) will require enormous manpower and capital to restore the lost readership base. Furthermore, recovering the advertising base in Osaka and Tokyo will certainly not be achievable in a short period. Considering this and that, this incident is a severe blow (重瘡) close to a fatal injury for the Dong-A Ilbo. To recover, a major financial decisive action (大英斷) by the owner and major shareholder, Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙), will likely be necessary. Moreover, unless they gather talent from home and abroad to make the paper's content more vibrant (精彩있게) than other papers, it may be difficult to return to the Dong-A Ilbo of former days.

**Lifting the Ban on Dong-A Ilbo?**

**Various Speculations, Both Hardline and Lenient**

Since the suspension on August 27th, a month has already passed one way or another. Facing the livelihood of its 300 employees and their thousand or so family members, the Dong-A Ilbo anxiously awaits the lifting of the ban (解禁) to happen even one day sooner. However, according to informed observers, one view is pessimistic, suggesting it will be long-term. The reasoning is that the current times are different from before; in this time of national emergency, they displayed such an unpatriotic attitude. Moreover, considering their past attitude regarding articles about the imperial family and their lack of active cooperation with the Governor-General's administration, they have repeatedly incurred the authorities' displeasure. Therefore, the ban is unlikely to be lifted unless they show considerable remorse (근신). Another view suggests that even if the ban is lifted, strict undisclosed conditions (內諾條件) will be attached. Extremists speculate that if something like the Shanghai Incident were to break out again anywhere in East Asia, there would be absolutely no hope for several months. On the other hand, there is speculation that it might be resolved in the short term. The basis for this is that the upper echelons of the company were completely unaware of this incident, and it was merely an act conspired by a few individuals like photography staff members. Thus, imposing long-term sanctions on a large organization for this reason is harsh. Also, the new Governor-General Nam will likely pursue a policy blending benevolence and authority (恩威竝行). Since sufficient punishment (懲治) has already been administered through the suspension, the new Governor-General's warmth might soon be extended. Furthermore, since the Chief of the Police Affairs Bureau is also newly appointed, the policy of the new Chief Mitsuhashi (三橋) will likely be to guide influential press organizations to reflect quickly and not oppose the times, so after sufficient admonishment (戒飾), the ban might be lifted soon. Who knows? Everything rests in the minds (胸中) of Governor-General Nam, Superintendent-General Ōno (大野), Bureau Chief Mitsuhashi, and Section Head Yagyū (柳生).

**What is General Public Opinion?**

**The "Unpatriotic" Attitude Was Wrong**

The Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo (京城日報) most vehemently condemned (痛罵) the Dong-A Ilbo's recent attitude, attacking it daily in editorials and articles. "Shimbun no Shimbun" (新聞之新聞) and "Shimbun no Nippon" (新聞之日本), published in Tokyo, also criticized it sharply (筆銖를 가하였으며). Besides these, groups like the Kapsa Club (甲子俱樂部), the National Association (國民協會), and the Great East People's Friendship Association (大東民友會), either as organizations or individuals, issued critical documents and statements (文書言說). However, examining public opinion (輿論), the act of erasing the national flag is considered, without room for further debate, an unpatriotic act (비국민적 行事). Regarding this one act, it is said that any sanction imposed would still be insufficient (猶不足하다). Both Dong-A Ilbo and Chungang Ilbo must silently accept all sanctions regarding this one matter. However, was this truly the stance of the company (社)? Judging by the scope of arrests made by the police, it has become clear that this was the misconduct (失行) of a few employees. Having published photos of athlete Sŏn 7 or 8 times with the Japanese flag on his chest since starting to feature him, why would the same company, with what prior intention and what later intention (先何心後何心으로), <31> decide to erase the national flag just once? At the company's Olympics film screening held at the Public Hall (公會堂), all the photos showed the Japanese flag. Was there ever a time when the national flag was not hoisted in front of the Dong-A and Chungang Ilbo buildings on any national holiday (祝慶節)? Was there ever an occasion, such as a celebration for the Crown Prince's birth or a reception hosted by the Governor-General, where the newspaper presidents' figures (容姿) were not present? Therefore, this misconduct (失行) was clearly not the intention of the entire company, but solely the misconduct of one or two employees. Furthermore, the notion that an influential private newspaper like the Dong-A Ilbo still harbors anti-Japanese sentiments (排日色彩) is something that affects the Governor-General's administration as well. Therefore, the general public hopes that after sufficiently admonishing (戒飾) their future attitude, the ban will be lifted quickly.

**Why Did the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo Cease Publication?**

**Losses Also Reach Huge Sum of Several Tens of Thousands of Won**

About a week after the Dong-A Ilbo, on September 4th, the Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo suddenly published a brief company announcement (社告) in its pages and ceased publication (休刊) from that day. The truth is as follows: While investigating the Dong-A Ilbo incident, it was discovered that the Chungang Ilbo, about 10 days prior to the Dong-A Ilbo, on August 15th, had also slightly erased the Japanese flag on athlete Sŏn's chest before publishing it.
Following the same track (軌を一にする - *using a Japanese expression*), if things continued, the company would inevitably face a suspension order like the Dong-A Ilbo. Therefore, sensing no hope after gauging the intentions of the Police Affairs authorities (警務當局) on September 4th, they made a decision. That night, President Yŏ (呂), former executive director Sŏng (成), managing editor Yun (尹), the business manager, and other top executives held a meeting and decided to voluntarily cease publication (자진 休刊). However, although called voluntary cessation, they cannot resume publication without the understanding of the Police Affairs authorities, so it is effectively no different from a suspension (停刊), merely perhaps less severe. Even if the cessation lasts less than a month, the company's losses will likely not be less than 10,000 won (萬圓). Currently, all employees report to the office and are observing self-restraint (근신의 뜻). It is said that 4 employees are still detained at the Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department.<32>
Sports Department staff member Yu Hae-p'eng (柳海鵬)
Photography Department staff members (寫眞部員) - 3 people

**President Yŏ Resigns Taking Responsibility**

**Entire Staff Observing Self-Restraint**

Several days after declaring the cessation of publication, company president Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨) submitted his resignation to the board of directors, but it has not yet been accepted. The rest of the staff continue to report to the office daily at the regular time, and although not working, they are showing remorse (謹愼의 意).<33>

**Summary**

This 1936 article from Samch'ŏlli magazine details the suspension of the Dong-A Ilbo and the "voluntary" cessation of the Chungang Ilbo following the "Sŏn Ki-jŏng flag erasure incident." After Sŏn's Olympic marathon victory, Dong-A Ilbo staff erased the Japanese flag from a photo of him, leading to employee arrests and the paper's suspension by Japanese colonial authorities for being "unpatriotic." The Chungang Ilbo preemptively ceased publication after discovering it had committed a similar, earlier infraction. The article discusses the police investigation, the financial impact on the newspapers, the history of Dong-A Ilbo suspensions, speculation about reinstatement, and public opinion, which condemned the act itself but questioned if it represented the newspapers' overall stance.

For historians, this article is highly valuable as it provides a contemporary account of Japanese colonial censorship and control over the Korean press. It vividly illustrates the political tightrope Korean newspapers had to walk, the sensitivity surrounding expressions of Korean nationalism (even indirectly through celebrating an athlete), the mechanics of colonial suppression (investigation, arrests, suspension, financial pressure), and the nuanced public reaction. The mention of specific officials, previous suspensions, financial details, and the internal dynamics (resignations, staff self-restraint) offers concrete data points for understanding colonial governance and media history. The use of terms like "unpatriotic" (비국민적) and the discussion around "cooperation" starkly reveal the colonial power structure.

東亞日報가 8월 27일에 「비국민적」태도로 정간을 당하였다. 신문사 자체로도 놀났으려니와 세상도 큰 충동을 받었다. 그로부터 수일후인 9월 4일에는 또 中央日報마저 자발적으로 휴간선언을 하였고 뒤니어 가튼 이유로 新東亞의 발매정지, 新家庭의 삭제처분 「아히生活」의 압수 基督申報의 압수 등 반도 언론계를 震駭케하는 대량적 押收發禁등이 疊起하야 사람사람의 가슴에 愁雲을 왕래케 하였다.
이제 우리는 여기에 아모란 주관이나 감정을 석지말고서 오로지 東亞停刊, 中央休刊의 진상만을 보도하여 보기로 하건대. (사진은 東亞日報社)<26>

東亞日報 停刊 理由

警務局長談으로 發表

東亞日報는 今回 발행정지처분을 당하였다.
前日 伯林에서 개회된 세계올림픽 대회의 마라손 경기에 朝鮮출신의 孫基禎군이 우승의 월계관을 획득한 것은 日本전체의 명예로 日本내지와 朝鮮 共히 함께 축하할 것이며 또 日本내지와 朝鮮융화의 자료로 할 것이지 此를 逆用하야 조곰이라도 민족적 대립의 공기를 유치하는 일이 있어서는 않될 것이다. 그런대 사실온 신문지 등의 기사는 자칫하면 대립적 감정을 剌戟함과 如한 필치를 취하는 것이 있음은 일반으로 유감視하든 바이다.
東亞日報는 종래 누차 당국의 주의가 있었음에도 불구하고 8월 25일 紙上에 孫基禎군의 사진을 게재하였는데 其 사진에 명료히 나타나야 할 일장기의 마크가 고의로 抹消한 形跡이 있었음으로 즉시 차압처분에 附하고 其 실정을 조사하였는 바 右는 8월 23일부 大阪朝日新聞에 게재된 孫基禎군의 사진을 轉載함에 際하야 일장기가 신문지상에 나터남을 기피하야 고의로 기술을 사용하야 此를 抹消한 것이 판명되였음으로 마침내 其 신문지에 대하야 발행처분을 나리게 되였다. 如此한 비국민적 태도에 대하여는 장래에도 엄중 取締를 가할 방침인데 일반도 과오가 없도록 주의하기를 바란다.(사진은 당면의 인물 東亞日報 宋사장)

東亞日報의 解禁은

强柔양면의 觀測區區

8월 27일 停刊이래 이럭저럭 벌서 한 달이 경과했다. 3백의 사원과 천여의 그 가족생계를 앞에 둔 東亞日報의 초조는 解禁이 하로 急하여지기를 고대하고 있다. 그런데 소식통의 관측에 의하면 一은 長期되리란 비관설인데 그것은 시국이 예전과 달라 국가비상시의 此際에 이와 같은 비국민적 태도를 보였으며 더구나 과거에 황실기사에 대한 태도와 총독정치에 대한 적극적 협조가 없었든 점으로 당국의 미움, 屢屢하였으니만치 이번에는 여간 근신치 않고는 解禁이 되지 않을 것이란 설이 있고 또는 解禁이 된다 할지라도 엄중한 內諾조건이 붙을 터이며 극단으로 관측하는 이는 上海사변같은 것이 東亞정국 어느 곳에서든지 다시 터지는 날이면 아조 머리지지 수개월로는 가망이 없으리라고 한다. 또 한편 혹은 단기에 되리란 설이 있는데 그 근거는 이번 사건은 社의 상층부는 전연 몰났고 그 아래 寫眞班員 등 數人이 共謀하고 한 행사일 뿐이기에 이 때문에 큰 기관을 장기적 제재를 줌은 가혹하다 함이요 또 南新총독은 恩威병행의 정치를 할 터임으로 停刊으로서 이미 10분 懲治를 하였은즉 新총독의 온정이 不久하야 베풀어질 듯하며 경무국장 또한 신임즉전의 事라 三橋新국장의 방침이 아모조록 세력있는 언론기관으로 하여곰 하로 급히 반성하여 시세에 배반함이 없도록 인도함에 있을 것임으로 충분히 戒飾를 가한 뒤 속히 解禁이 되리라고 함이다. 아지 못게라 모든 것은 南총독, 大野총감, 三橋국장, 柳生과장의 胸中에 있슴인저.

一般輿論은 엇더한가

「非國民的」태도는 잘못의다

東亞日報의 今般태도를 가장 痛罵한 것은 京城日報가 사설로 기사로 연일 攻擊함이였고 東京서 발행하는 「新聞之新聞」「新聞之日本」도 모도 筆銖를 가하였으며 그 밖에 甲子俱樂部國民協會, 大東民友會등에서 혹은 단체로 혹은 개인으로 攻擊하는 文書言說이 있었다. 그런데 一 譽論을 삺이건대 國旗抹消한 행위는 더 논의할 여지없이 비국민적 行事이라 한다. 이 一事에 대하여는 여하한 제재를 가하여도 猶不足하다. 東亞日報 中央日報 모다 이 一事에 대하여는 무언으로 모든 제재를 받어야 한다 한다. 그러나 이것이 과연 社의 태도였을가. 경찰에서 검거범위로 보아 이것은 수개 사원의 失行인 것이 판명되었다. 같은 孫선수 사진을 지면에 내기 시작한 지 7,8차 늘 일장기가 胸間에 부튼 사진을 내든 同社가 先何心後何心으로<31> 단 한번을 國旗 抹消를 하자했으리요. 公會堂에서 열린 同社 올림픽 영화대회때에도 모다 일장기있는 사진이엇고 또 어느 祝慶節에 東亞, 中央 兩社門前에 국기 안달린 때 업었* 황태자 탄생축하회나 총독 초대회 등 어느 좌석에 신문사장 容姿가 나타나지 않을 때가 있었든가. 그럼으로 이번 失行은 社의 전체의사가 아니요 오직 1,2 사원의 失行일 것이 분명하며 또는 東亞日報와 같이 유력한 민간지가 아직도 排日색채를 띠고 있다함은 총독정치에도 영향있는 일인즉 今番은 금후의 태도를 十分戒飾한 뒤 속히 解禁하여 줌이 좋겠다고 일반은 희망하고 있다.

朝鮮中央日報는 엇재 休刊햇나

損害도 數萬圓의 巨額에 達

朝鮮中央日報는 東亞日報에 뒤지기 約 1주일후인 9월 4일에 돌연히 一片의 社告를 지상에 게재하고 그 날부터 休刊하여 버렸다. 그 진상은 이러하다. 東亞日報사건을 취조하다가 보니까 中央日報가 東亞日報에 先하기 약 10여일인 8월 15일 지면에 역시 孫선수 胸間 일장기를 약간 抹消하여 지상에 내였다.
軌를 一혀한다면 이대로 가다가는 당연히 東亞日報모양으로 停刊처분을 당하게 된 同社에서는 최후로 9월 4일 警務當局의 맥을 살펴보아도 餘望이 없음으로 이에 결심하고 同夜呂사장 成전무 尹편집국장 영업국장 등 최고 간부회의를 열고 자진 休刊하기로 하였다. 그러나 이름은 이와 가치 자진 休刊이나 警務當局의 양해가 없이는 발간치 못할 것임으로 사실상 停刊에 不外한데 다만 그 정도가 경미하다할 것이다. 同社 亦 가령 1개월이내의 휴간이라 할지라도 손해는 萬圓을 不下할 것이다. 지금 사원전부는 出社하야 亦 근신의 뜻을 표하고 있는데 이제 아직도 京畿道경찰부에 구금되어 있는 사원은 4氏라 한다.<32>
