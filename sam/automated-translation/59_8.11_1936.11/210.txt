Assessment of Banking Leaders: Mr. Min Kyu-sik, Chairman of the Board of Directors of Tongŭn [Tongil Bank]
						By Kangch'on Hagin

After Tongil Bank (東一銀行) repeatedly faced waves of turmoil and disputes due to complex incidents and circumstances, through the intervention of supervisory authorities and strict directives from lending banks, it finally reached a resolution, albeit a forced one, via the bank's general shareholders' meeting on July 3rd last. Consequently, the previous executive system of Head (頭取) was abolished, and the system of Chairman of the Board of Directors (取締役會長) was adopted. Following this, a major reshuffle of top executives occurred: Head Min Tae-sik (閔大植) and Executive Director Sŏng Yak-hŏn (成藥憲) were dismissed, and the person appointed as the first Chairman of the Board of Directors after the system reform is, as everyone anticipates, the son (令胤) of the late Min Yŏng-hwi (閔泳徽) and the actual younger brother of Min Tae-sik, Mr. Kyu-sik (奎植).

Let us examine the character of Mr. Kyu-sik, who has ascended to the highest leadership position at Tongŭn, succeeding his elder brother (令兄), though the job title is different.
Regarding Mr. Kyu-sik, he is now 49 years old, having passed the age of forty (不惑) and approaching fifty (知天命), truly a prime time of life. Originally a man of reason (理智) and possessor of a brilliant mind, Mr. Kyu-sik has extensive experience in numerous businesses, earning recognition from everyone in the business world. He himself is fully confident, giving the impression of being the right person in the right place as the de facto leader of the currently troubled Tongŭn, and he is certainly worthy of such trust.

Mr. Kyu-sik is a brilliant talent and prodigy who once carried his books (笈) abroad to study in London, England, graduating from the Economics Department of Cambridge University (劒橋大學). Upon returning to his homeland (故土), he immediately entered the business world and has since undertaken various ventures, achieving success through agile management (手腕) and thorough calculation (透徹한 打算), possessing the demeanor of a seasoned veteran (百戰老將).

He previously served as the Managing Director (常務取締役) of Hanil Bank (韓一銀行), assisting his elder brother Tae-sik. When Hanil merged with Hoseo Bank (湖西銀行),<128> he resigned and served as president or director of Chōsen Seishi (朝鮮製?) and Chosŏn Silk Weaving (朝鮮絹織), and president of Kyŏngsŏng Cotton Manufacturing (京城製綿) and Yŏngbo Gomei Company (永保合名), further demonstrating his achievements in the business world while securing an unshakable foundation (確乎不拔의 地盤). Furthermore, Yŏngbo Gomei Company bundles his assets and the important private wealth of his entire clan with the intention of perpetual security (永世確保), creating the Yŏngbo Foundation (永保財團). This foundation manages department stores (貨店?), building management (삘딍經營), land and other real estate investments. It already operates large buildings in Chongno such as the Hwasin Building (和信삘딍), Yŏngbo Department Store (永保貸店?), and Yŏngbo Building (永保삘딍), providing convenience to the general business community. In addition, he manages businesses too numerous to count on ten fingers; suffice it to say, Mr. Kyu-sik is indispensable to the Korean business world. While the advantageous environment of being the second generation of a multi-millionaire (千萬長者) exists, Mr. Kyu-sik possesses outstanding financial talent (理財的 天稟), and besides the wealth he inherited, the fortune he amassed himself has reached a truly enormous sum. It is acknowledged by himself and others that his wealth indeed exceeds the ten-million-won mark, from which one can easily infer Mr. Kyu-sik's capacity for wealth accumulation (殖富). Mr. Kyu-sik's accumulated influence, stretching vertically and horizontally across the Korean business world, is comparable (though differing in degree) to America's "Morgan" (몰칸) or Tokyo's Mitsubishi (三菱) and Mitsui (三井). In any case, as a businessman, his capabilities and skills have been proven (試驗濟) to a certain extent. Judging by his track record thus far, he has passed the test and received promotion, earning the title of businessman. However, dissecting him as a bare individual (赤裸한 인간), having studied in England, he retains, to some extent, the lingering scent (餘香) of the British gentleman's code (英國紳士道). While perhaps not a perfect archetype, one can discover more traces of the British gentleman's code in Mr. Kyu-sik than in anyone else. Accordingly, while pursuing his own rights to the end, he also recognizes the rights of others. He does not permit encroachment upon his own interests, nor does he seek to infringe upon the interests of others. He accepts obligations and demands them in return. He is absolutely not the sort of misguided person (錯覺의 人) who typically only exercises rights and demands duties, while failing to speak of rights or duties towards others.

Therefore, at first glance, he is easily subject to the negative reputation of being a cold-blooded man (冷鐵漢). It seems to be Mr. Kyu-sik's character and life motto (못토) to maintain a composure where, even if his inner heart sees some room for sympathy, if it does not accord with reason (理), he firmly shuts his eyes and pretends not to see (視而不見).

Mr. Kyu-sik is also an embodiment of thrift (節險) and miserliness (守錢), feeling attachment to every penny (分錢). Possessing a sharp mind for profit and loss calculations, he is also likely the type of person who would contest even a tiny profit (厘利). However, Mr. Kyu-sik's distinguishing feature, as mentioned before, is that he does not merely fight for his own tiny profits but also refrains from violating the tiny profits (129) of others in the same sense. This principle can be observed not only in his business dealings but also in his private life.

Furthermore, like his elder brother Tae-sik, Mr. Kyu-sik is astute (內明), meticulous (緻密), systematic, and possesses the virtue of thrift and saving (節險節約). He maintains scale (規模) consistently throughout, making him one of Korea's wealthiest individuals, a multi-millionaire. Although there are reports that he once owned a private car, he usually travels on foot, occasionally using a rickshaw or tram (in urgent cases). Beyond official duties, there are no accounts of him frequenting high-class restaurants (料亭) or even rumors of affairs or scandals (醜聞) with certain favored women, suggesting he is quite indifferent (疏淡) to worldly pleasures (風情). Perhaps one could say he is thoroughly faithful to the gentleman's code and adheres strictly (墨守) to the traditional way of the scholar-official (士大夫)? Given this disposition, his daily conduct is static (靜的) and elegant (淡雅), rather than dynamic (動的) and enterprising (進取的).

However, in business matters (事業?), unlike his elder brother Tae-sik who tends to be passive and stagnant (退嬰枯息的), he is a man of peculiar intensity (怪癖) who devotes his full strength (力) and sincerity (誠). The power he wields to control numerous business organizations is evident from the fact that, despite owning 70 or 80 companies, he personally oversees (總攬禦侮) every detail, proving he is certainly no ordinary man (凡骨). His vitality (精力) is also demonstrated by how he personally travels to various locations in the capital and provinces for land investments, investigating and deciding on everything himself.

Moreover, when employing people, he adheres strictly to a principle of practicality based on substance over ostentation (去華就實), ensuring they operate within his scope (自己範疇). The fact that he has appointed individuals like Yi Chŏng-jae (李定宰) at Yŏngbo, Paek Hong-gyun (白泓均) at Hansŏng Cotton Manufacturing (漢城製綿 is likely 京城製綿), Cho Han-byŏk (趙漢壁) at Chosŏn Silk Weaving, and Ku Yŏn-ch'ang (具然昶) at Tongbang Plantation (東邦殖産?) to handle the front lines amply conveys this message.

His pragmatism (務實主義), prioritizing suitability over personal connections (情實), is commendable, but he certainly does not trust others casually. Also, perhaps seeking quietude (閑居), meeting him and gaining his favor (知遇) requires immense effort, as if "thorns grow on the calling cards at his gate" (門前剌紙生荊毛). Does this not speak volumes about his character?

Finally, my humble words (蕪言) to Mr. Kyu-sik are these: The troubled Tongŭn has welcomed him not merely in the sense of continuing the tradition of the Min and Sŏng families, but largely relying on his practical energy (務實力行), capacity, and skill (手腕). Therefore, Mr. Kyu-sik must strive to place Tongŭn, currently in a state of disarray (亂麻?), onto a foundation as solid as Mount Tai (泰山盤石). Simultaneously, he must sweep away all the previous unpleasant currents within the bank and decisively sever the roots of conflict causing confrontation and friction (對立軋礫), thereby eradicating (艾除) Tongŭn's troubles and restoring its former reputation. The difficulty of creation is always less than the difficulty of restoration (中興); painting a colorful picture on blank paper (白紙에 彩畵) is easier than returning a colorful painting to blank paper (彩畵를 白紙로 還元).<130>

**Summary**

This article profiles Min Kyu-sik upon his appointment as Chairman of the Board for the troubled Tongil Bank in 1936, succeeding his dismissed elder brother. The author details Min's background: Cambridge-educated, extensive business experience (Hanil Bank, Chosŏn Silk Weaving, Yŏngbo Gomei, etc.), and immense personal wealth exceeding 10 million won. His personality is described as rational, pragmatic, meticulous, frugal, influenced by the "British gentleman's code," potentially appearing cold but fair, demanding yet respecting obligations. While seen as less dynamic personally, he is intensely dedicated in business. The author expresses hope that Min's proven business acumen and practical approach will stabilize the bank, urging him to resolve internal conflicts and restore its reputation, acknowledging the challenge of revival is greater than initial creation.

From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the Korean business elite during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights their transnational education (Cambridge), involvement in significant industrial and financial enterprises (banking, textiles, real estate), methods of wealth accumulation (including inherited wealth and personal business success), and management styles (pragmatism, control, reliance on trusted deputies). The mention of the "British gentleman's code" reflects complex cultural interactions and aspirations within this elite class. Furthermore, the discussion of internal bank politics, family succession (albeit through dismissal and replacement), and the role of supervisory authorities sheds light on the operating environment for Korean businesses under colonial rule. Historians might find the detailed portrayal of Min Kyu-sik's character, blending traditional Korean elite elements (Sadaebu comparison) with modern, Western-influenced business practices and ethics, particularly interesting.

銀行 主腦者 人物評 東銀取締役會長 閔奎植氏
						江村學人
東一銀行이 복잡다단한 사건과 사정으로 波瀾과 紛糾를 거듭하다가 감독당국의 간섭과 融資銀行의 엄중한 示達로 드듸여 去 7월 3일 仝銀株主總會를 經하야 억지로나마 일단락을 告하였고, 따러 종래의 職制인 頭取制를 廢하고 取締役會長制를 채용케 되였다. 그에 따러 최고간부의 大更迭이 일이나 頭取閔大植氏와 專務成藥憲氏가 해임하고 職制改革後 第一代取締役會長으로 취임케 된 분은 누구나 다- 짐작하는 故閔泳徽氏의 令胤이오 閔大植의 實弟인 奎植氏이다.
令兄을 代하야 직명은 비록 다르나 東銀最高頭領에 군림한 奎植氏의 爲人을 한번 살펴보기로 하자.
氏로 논하면 지금 不惑의 고개를 넘어 知天命에 갓가운 49세라는 한참조흔 때이다. 元來 理智의 人이오 명철한 두뇌의 소유자인 氏가 그간 幾多事業에 ?驗이 많어 실업계에서는 누구나 한목을 노아주는 바요. 氏 자신도 자신이 확연하여 多難한 現下東銀의 실권자로는 適材適所의 感을 갔게 하며 그만한 信賴를 주어도 족한 바이다.
씨는 일즉이 ?을 負하고 英京에 遊하야 劒橋大學經濟科를 맛친 英俊이오 수재이다. 故士에 歸하자 즉시 실업계로 진출하야 지금것 各 事業에 착수하여 敏活한 手腕과 透徹한 打算으로 성공이 實를 擧한 백전노장의 풍체를 갔춘 분이다.

그래서 一見 그는 冷鐵漢이라는 악평을 듯기 쉽다. 凡事에 理智的 이어서 內心으로는 多少同情할 餘地가 있다고 보더래도 理에 합하지 않으면 눈을 딱 깜고 視而不見하는 冷靜을 있지 안는 것이 氏의 성격이오, 생활의 「못토」인 것 갓다.
