Article Title: 나의 墓地銘 (My Epitaph)
Author: Survey

My Epitaph
Yi Kwangsu
There was a moment once when, after undergoing surgery in a room at Severance Hospital (세부란스病院), I even left a "Last Will" (遺言) and awaited death.
I said something to the effect of, "When I die, please place this sinful body on the autopsy table (解剖臺) at Severance Hospital to be used as research material (硏究材料) for students!! Then, cremate (火葬) it, and when it becomes ashes (灰가루), please don't bury it in the ground but scatter it in the air (空中)..." Even now, I have not the slightest thought that should this sinful and filth-filled body die, my family (家族) or anyone else should make a tomb (墓) and erect a monument (碑) for me.
However, if my children, family, or close friends (親友) insist on making a tomb and erecting a monument after I die, well, that's something I can't stop even from the underworld (地下). If that were to happen, my thought would be that I'd like the phrase (글句), "Yi Kwangsu was a person who worked for the Korean people," to be written, but that too is just a wish.

Kim Ansŏ
You ask me to write an epitaph when I want to live a little longer; there's no magazine as cruel as Samch'ŏlli. Roasting people this way and grilling them that way, how can one survive, I ask? (220) Anyway, here is mine:
"Building sandcastles by the sea,
Claiming 'this is mine,' 'that is yours,' they quarrel.
Then the tide creeps silently in,
Washing away both mine and yours as it flows."

Pang In'gŭn
When I die, I want to leave these words as my last testament (遺言) to my descendants (子孫), and I think I might have them engraved on my tombstone (墓碑). Of course (勿論), there might be revisions (添削) at that time. It's long, but I'll write it here.

I am going – my son, my daughter –
As I go, among the mountains and seas of words I wish to entrust to you at the last, I have chosen these few below.
As I bid farewell to this world and look back (도라보니) on my past life (過去 一生), I did no good deeds, failed to do what I wanted, and feel only regret (後悔) and shame in various ways.
Knowing full well that death is certain for human life (人生), I always thought I would live for a long, long time and wasted the years away (헛되히 내버리었다). Thinking now, I realize how precious and regrettable a day (하로), a month, a year is.
One day of life is shortened (減壽) by one drinking bout, three days (사을) are shortened by one fit of anger (憤怒), and one month is shortened by failing to endure sexual desire (性慾) once. If I had paid attention (注意) to my health (健康), I could have lived ten years longer and done the unfinished (未盡) work.
One lie (거즛말) can determine the fate (運命) of a lifetime, and a slight error (着誤) in thought can lead to a mistake of a thousand ri. My son, my daughter – even if you starve, even if you are called foolish, just be honest (正眞) and good. And lower (나추워) yourselves to serve others.
If you ask me, whose breath is now failing, what my wish is, it would be to let me live a little longer, or rather, to be reborn and live one life over again – that is my only wish. (221) But that is a dreamlike story, like asking the sun to rise in the west. Therefore, my daughter, my son – become my substitute (대신). Though I die, you live, so let your bodies be the extension (延長) of my life. Please accomplish all the things this pitiful father could not.
However, even in death, I have three joys (기뿜). The first (첫재) is that you exist to succeed me (내 뒤를 니어). The second joy (둘재 기뿜) is that I have not truly died. Though the body dies, my soul (魂) is alive. Thus, I will always watch over you and be connected to you eternally (永遠토록) through the soul.
The third joy (셋재 기뿜) is that I was born (낫다가) in this land and will be buried (묻치니) in this land again, so I am eternally (無窮토록) a Korean person.

Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi
Erecting a memorial monument (記念碑) while alive is something that won't happen for me, and after death, like others do, I suppose a sharpened stake (깍끈 말뚝) might be driven in front of my tomb (墓). However, I will firmly request beforehand that nothing be written on that stake—. Not even my name—. It's because I don't want to trouble myself when I wish to rest comfortably.

Sim Hun
To Messrs. P'ain (巴人), Kangch'on (江村)
You don't know enough to send summer greetings (御見舞) in this heat, yet you speak of thinking about corpses rotting in the midst of the three hottest days of summer (三伏中)? Do you want a bowl of corpse juice soup (屍汁湯) instead of draft beer (生ビ-ル)? Strange friends, indeed.
However, if you send me a copy (卷) of last month's Samch'ŏlli, I'll grant special forgiveness (容恕).
Life is not as good as death, but those who cling to life are already dead. (生不如死而生之者死矣)
Sim Hun

Han Int'aek
Like a sparrow struck by an arrow, trembling (222)
Man, do not mock, for the arrow of destruction is near your breast too.
Before the red blood cools, look upon the path ahead of you.
(From "Age of Whirlwind" (旋風時代), Verse 1)

Chŏn Yŏngt'aek
Alone (외로이)
Foolishly (어리석게)
Poorly (가난하게)

Ham Taehun
You ask me, who has just barely passed the threshold of 30, to look through my writings (著作) for an epitaph I have in mind (意中); I really don't know how I should respond (對答). If, perhaps at age (나히) 50 or 60, I produce a great work, maybe I can pick a phrase (句節) then. But if I must try to write one from my current works:
"People needlessly (공연히) hate others, curse others, and doubt others, but there is nothing dirtier than that in the world..."
From the full-length novel (長篇小說) *Sunjŏng* (純情) by Hae-rye (海崍 - likely Ham's pen name)

Ch'ae Mansik
As things stand now, my achievements (功績) and my life don't seem worthy of even a wooden stake (木말뚝).
And even if, by good fortune (多幸), a monument (碑) marking my death (죽엄) were to be erected, regardless of merit (功의 有無間),
I would have it end (끄치게 하겠읍니다) with just writing "Ch'ae Mansik's Tomb" (蔡萬植의 묻엄) and below that, the date of birth (生年月日) and date of death (死年月日).

Kim Tongin
I (余) do not yet think of death. (223)

No Chayŏng
I answer your esteemed question (貴問).
An epitaph is unfitting (不當) for someone like me, nor do I wish to write one. However, if I absolutely must, putting aside novels and poems,
Perhaps I would write just this one phrase (마데나): "In death, there is peace." (죽으면 평안하니라)

Yi Chongsu
I have no achievements (功蹟) worthy of a memorial monument (記念碑), and I think it wouldn't be too late to consider an epitaph (墓誌銘) 30 or 40 years from now. Still, if you insist I write one thing, I would like to be "A literary figure (文學家) who steadfastly strived (努力) in search of Truth (眞實) throughout his life (一生동안)."

Kim Munjip
In the distant future (먼 將來), tombs (墓) will disappear from human society (人間社會). The peak (絶頂) of sentimentalism (感傷主義) – that seems to be what a tomb is. When I die, I wish to have my head cut off and thrown into the Sacred Pond (聖池) of Mt. Paektu (白頭山), and my limbs (四肢) burned (燒) and scattered (散) across the mountains and rivers of the Eight Provinces (八道山川). How could there be an epitaph (墓誌銘)? Would one call that sorrowful?

Sin Pulch'ul
"Good riddance!" (잘죽었다!)

Kim Hyŏngwŏn
I am grateful for your esteemed question (貴問), but I have never thought about matters after death, and also, I think such things are not for me, the living, to know (알 바가 아니라고 생각함니다).

Im Hwa
"Oh Enemy (敵), you are my Courage (勇氣)"
From the poem "Enemy" (詩「敵」) (224)

Yu Wanhŭi
Without beginning, without end (無始無終) – having passed through life without accomplishment (보람), how could I hope for anything remaining (나믐) after death? But who would fault passing by silently? Silence (沈黙) is the highest and greatest philosophy (哲學) possessed today! A person's life (일생) is nothing more than a conscious connecting line (意識的 連鎖線) joining eternity (永遠) to eternity (永遠); in this short journey (旅路), how much can one shout, how much can one leap about? Yet, the dreams formed on the journey are all the fresher in memory (記憶). Though silence be silence, in life and in spirit, one must not lose the tension (緊張) of the moment (刹那) – the seriousness (深刻) of the instant (瞬間). Do you know the sleeping lion (獅子)?
A fragment of old memory (묵은 記憶의 한토막) (225)
<220-225>

**Summary**

This 1936 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine presents responses from various prominent Korean writers to the question: "What would you want written on your tombstone?" The answers display a wide range of attitudes, from Yi Kwangsu's solemn patriotism and Pang In'gŭn's lengthy moralistic testament, to Kim Ansŏ's poetic reflection, Sim Hun's dark humor, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi's desire for anonymity, Kim Tongin's curt dismissal, and Kim Munjip's intensely nationalistic rejection of traditional burial. For historians, the article offers a fascinating snapshot of the Korean literary scene under Japanese rule. The diverse responses reveal individual personalities, philosophical outlooks, literary styles (including quotes from their own works), and varying degrees of engagement with themes of life, death, legacy, and national identity during a difficult period. The use of older Korean orthography, Hanja, and even Japanese loanwords (like O-mimai, Nama-biiru) provides linguistic data, while the content reflects the complex psychological and social landscape of colonial Korea.

나의 墓地銘
李光洙
나는 전날 세부란스病院 一室에서 手術을 하고 나서 「遺言」까지 남기고 죽엄을 기대리든 순간이 있었다.
「내 죽거든 罪많은 이 몸둥아리를 세부란스病院의 解剖臺에 올녀 놓와 學生들의 硏究材料로나 써달나!! 그러고는 火葬을 해서 灰가루가 되거든 땅 속에 파묻지 말고 空中에 뿌려달나...」 이런 意味의 말을 했었다. 나는 지금도 罪많고 더러움이 가득한 이 몸이 죽어진다고, 家族이나 어느 누가 墓를 만드러 놓고 碑를 해 세워주웠으면 하는 생각은 조금치도 없다.
그러나 내 자식들이나 家族, 또는 親友들이, 내 죽어간 뒤에 구태여 墓를 만들어 주고 碑를 세워 준다면 그야, 地下에 가서까지 말닐 수야 없는 일이다. 만일 그렇게 되여진다면 내 생각으로는, 「리광수는 조선 사람을 위하야 일하든 사람이다」하는 글句가 씨여젔으면 하나, 그도 마음뿐이다.

金岸曙
좀더 살고 싶은데 벌서부터 墓誌銘을 쓰라고 하니, 三千里처럼 야속한 雜誌는 없음니다. 사람을 이리 삶고 저리 골리니, 어디 살아먹겠느냐 말이외다.(220) 그래 나의 것은 이렀음니다.
「바다까에 모래城 쌓아들놓고
내것이다 네게다 서로 다톨제
밀물은 사륵사륵 밀려들어와
내네것을 부시고 흘러가거니.」

方仁根
나는 죽을 때 내 子孫에게 이렇게 遺言을 하고 싶고 그것을 墓碑에 삭일까 하는 생각을 가지고 있다. 勿論 그때 添削이 있을지는 모른다. 길지만은 여기에 적어본다.

나는 간다- 내 아들아 딸아-
가면서 마지막 너희들에게 부탁하고 싶은 산과 바다와 같은 말 중에서 이 아래 몇 마디를 골랐다.
나는 이 세상을 하직하면서 과거 일생을 도라보니 좋은 일 못하고 하고 싶은 것 못하고 가지가지 후회요 부끄럽다.
인생에게 죽엄이 반드시 있다는 것을 알면서도 나는 늘 오래오래 살 것으로 생각하고 세월을 헛되히 내버리었다. 지금 생각하니 하로, 한달, 일년이란 것이 얼마나 귀하고 아까운 것인지 모르겠다.
한번 술 먹는데 하로가 감수(減壽)되고 한번 분노하는데 사을이 감수되고 한번 성욕을 못 참는데 한 달이 감수되는 것이다. 만일 내가 건강에 주의하였더면 10년은 더 살아서 미진한 일을 하였으리라.
한번 거즛말이 일생의 운명을 좌우하고 자칫 생각을 잘못한 것이 천리의 착오를 이르킨다. 내 아들아 딸아- 너희는 굶어도 어리석다해도 다만 정직하고 착해라. 그리고 너희 몸을 나추워 다른 사람을 섬기라.
지금 숨이 넘어가는 내게 소원이 무었이냐고 물으면 좀더 살게 하든지 아니 그보다도 다시 살어나서 한 평생을 다시 지내밧으면 하는 오직 그것 뿐이다.(221) 그러나 그것은 꿈같은 이야기오 해를 서편에서 뜨라는 말과 같다. 그러니 내 딸아 내 아들아- 너희가 곳 내 대신이 되어다구. 나는 죽어도 너희가 살었으니 너의 몸이 곳 내 삶의 연장(延長)이 되어다구. 그래서 이 가여운 아비의 못한 일을 다 하여다구.
그러나 나는 죽어도 세 가지 기뿜이 있다. 첫재는 내 뒤를 니어 너희가 있다는 것이다. 둘재 기뿜은 나는 죽지는 아니했다는 것이다. 몸은 죽었어도 내 혼(魂)은 살아있다는 것이다. 그래서 늘 너희를 지켜보고 너희와 영원토록 혼으로 연락되어 있다는 것이다.
셋재 기뿜은 나는 이 땅에서 낫다가 다시 이 땅에 묻치니 무궁토록 나는 조선사람이라는 것이다.
