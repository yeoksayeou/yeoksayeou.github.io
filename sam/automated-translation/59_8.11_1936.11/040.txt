Dong-A Ilbo Publication Suspension List

Firstly, the 1st instance was about nine months after the said newspaper was founded on January 6, Taishō 9 (1920). On September 25th, it faced indefinite suspension on the charge (廉) of having made irreverent remarks concerning the Three Sacred Treasures (三種의 神器) in an editorial.

Secondly, the 2nd instance occurred on March 5, Taishō 15 (1926), when it translated and published a telegram received from the representative of the International Peasants' Union headquarters in Moscow (在莫斯科). It was subjected to an indefinite suspension, and furthermore, the managing editor and the editor/publisher, Mr. Kim Ch'ŏlchung (金鐵中), received judicial punishment and served time in Sŏdaemun Prison (西大門刑務所).

Thirdly, the 3rd instance happened on April 2, Shōwa 5 (1930), when, for the 10th-anniversary commemorative issue, contributions titled "Writings Contributed to Korea by Famous Figures from Home and Abroad" were widely collected and published. Among the articles by contributors like Ireland's Bernard Shaw (빠나드, 쇼), Hu Shih (胡適), Russell (랏셀), and Tokyo's Abe Isoo (安部璣雄) and Murobuse Kōshin (室伏高信), an article by an American, Piera (삐라), contained a maxim meaning something like "The most urgent, sleepless and restless (不眠不休) effort required of the Korean people," which led to an indefinite suspension. According to the announcement by the Director of the Police Affairs Bureau Asari (淺利警務局長) at the time, during the 90 days from January to March of that year, the number of sales ban orders reached 15, and the number of deletion orders amounted to 24.

**Summary**

This article from the November 1936 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine lists three instances where the Dong-A Ilbo newspaper faced indefinite suspension by the Japanese colonial authorities. The first suspension in 1920 was for alleged irreverence towards the Japanese Imperial Regalia. The second in 1926 resulted from publishing a telegram from the International Peasants' Union in Moscow, leading to the imprisonment of the editor. The third in 1930 was triggered by an article in the 10th-anniversary issue, specifically a contribution from an American writer deemed problematic, alongside statistics showing frequent censorship (sales bans and deletions) in early 1930. For historians, this article starkly illustrates the severe press censorship and suppression faced by Korean media under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting specific incidents, the reasons cited by authorities (often related to perceived disrespect towards Japanese symbols or connections to international socialist movements), and the personal consequences for journalists. The mention of specific foreign contributors like Bernard Shaw and Russell also indicates the newspaper's attempt to connect with international discourse, which was itself viewed with suspicion.

기사형태	소식

東亞日報 發行停止表
즉 제1차는 同報가 大正9년 1월 6일에 창간된 뒤 약 9개월후인 9월 25일 사설에 3종의 神器에 대하야 불경한 언사를 농한 廉으로 무기정간,
제2차는 大正15년 3월 5일에 在莫斯科國際農民組合본부 대표자로부터 온 전보를 번역 게재한 때문에 무기정간까지 당하고 또 편집국장과 편집 겸 발행인 金鐵中씨는 사법처분을 受하야, 西大門형무소에서 受刑하다.
제3차는 昭和5년 4월 2일에 창간 10주년 기념호 지상에 「내외명사가 朝鮮에 寄하는 書」
를 널니 모아실기로 되야 愛蘭의 빠나드, 쇼을 위시하야, 胡適, 랏셀, 東京의 安部璣雄, 室伏高信등의 기고를 실든 중 米國인 삐라도 기고의 『朝鮮민족의 가장 긴급을 요할 不眼不休의 노력』이란 의미의 격언운운으로 무기정간되다, 이때 당시의 淺利警務局長 발표에 의하면 其年 1월부터 3월까지 90일간에 발매금지처분건수 15일, 삭제처분을 명한 건수 24회의 다수에 달하였다고.
