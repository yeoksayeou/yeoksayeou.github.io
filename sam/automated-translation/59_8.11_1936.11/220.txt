Mr. Cho Man-sik's Speech, Title: "Youth and Social Service," Lecture on the Higher Agricultural Academy Project at the Songsan Education Association on September 11, Eulhae year [1935]

An American scholar who once visited our Korea, after finishing his inspection tour of Chosŏn and returning to his country, once said, "Among the youth of the world, there are none as busy [with potential tasks] as the youth of Chosŏn."

Also, the magnate of the British newspaper world, Mr. "Field," after finishing his inspection of Chosŏn and returning, said to newspaper reporters, "I am leaving having seen that 'work material' in Chosŏn remains completely untouched."

The view of Chosŏn seen by these two foreigners is that Chosŏn's work remains untouched, and they quickly saw that the youth have much to do.

Indeed, I believe this fact is unmistakably true.

During the time of Jesus, as He traveled through cities and villages, He said, "They are like sheep without a shepherd (無牧之羊), the work is much, the workers are many [likely referencing 'the harvest is plentiful but the workers are few']."

If Jesus were to see Chosŏn today, I believe He would emphasize even more strongly the words He spoke in Judea at that time.

Looking at the current state of Chosŏn, there is truly much work to be done in politics, economy, religion, education, culture, and other various fields. It is just like uncultivated land or a newly discovered territory.

Jesus said, "You are the salt of the earth, the light of the world (世之鹽世之光云). Salt, after sacrificing itself, yields its effect (鹽은 自身犧牲後妃?果 - *note: 后妃?果 seems corrupt, likely intended effect/result*); light, if placed under a bushel (斗下), is not placed before people, but only after being offered (樹於人前 卽提供以後) does it have value (乃有價値也)."

He also said, "I came into the world not to be served, but to serve (非受奉事, 乃爲奉事也). Therefore, for Christian youth, service is their calling (天職), duty (責務), and responsibility (本分也)."

Looking at the same Chosŏn, people will see it differently, either as having nothing to do or having much to do.

Youth of weak conduct and weak will (薄行弱志) seem intoxicated by vanity, luxury, and modern trends (風湖 - *likely typo for 風潮, trends*), and their only task seems to be finding employment. Dreaming only of a salaried life in the city with fine clothes, sweet food (美衣甘食), pleasures for the eyes and ears (耳目之好), and delights for the mind and heart (心志之樂), they are absorbed in job hunting, lamenting that in this era overflowing with tasks (事業洪水時代), there is nothing to do (無事) in society.<132> Such people are unenlightened (暗昧) individuals who fail to see and understand the bigger picture (大局).

They could be called clever fools or shameless blockheads.

Such people, seeking only employment, have a habit of frequently saying there is no work in society.

Youth with profound ideals and great reasoned hearts (理心), facing this era and this region, seeing these people (百姓), should not lack a spirit of service (奉仕的).

Outstanding people are the spirit of the land (人傑은 地靈); heroes are products of their time (英雄은 時代的 産物). Seeing this situation in this region, feelings of inspiration (感奮) and reaction (反撥) will inevitably arise, and naturally, a change in thought will occur, just as Moses (*西 - *likely typo for 摩西*) cast off (軀棄) his life as a prince in the Egyptian palace (埃及宮中) like worn-out shoes (敝履).

Indeed, conscientious youth will naturally discover a Chosŏn full of tasks (多事多端).

In short, one should not lament the lack of work based on the standard of individual employment, but realize that there is plenty of work based on the standard of social service. (The hall erupts in applause)

In the current situation of Chosŏn, the vigorous rising (奮起) of Christian youth is truly necessary. The question then is, what kind of work should we Christian youth undertake?

Regarding this, since all sorts of tasks (諸般事業) in Chosŏn remain in an untouched state (숫版), there is work to be done in every field, but I feel that work directed towards the farming villages (農村) and targeting the farmers (農民) is the most urgent (緊切). There are various reasons for this. Namely, the vastness of the region (地域의 廣闊), the large number of farmers (農民의 多數), the high value of agricultural production (農産額의 多額), the ease of cultural dissemination (文化普及의 容宜 - *likely 容易, ease*), and the need for equality (均* - *likely 均等, equality*). For example, compared to the small number of cities and towns (府邑), there are 26,641 villages (里); in terms of population, there are 16,132,503 farmers; there are 2,523 townships (面) and 3,009,855 households (戶數). Also, looking at the figures - agricultural production value 1.08 billion Won, forest products 60 million Won, marine products 80 million Won, mining value 50 million Won, industrial production 380 million Won, and others 150 million Won - compared to the total annual production (年總産額) of 1.8 billion Won, we can see the astonishing figure that agricultural products account for 1.08 billion Won. One can readily understand not only the ease of spreading Chosŏn culture but also how necessary it is to make it equal (均*케).

To reiterate, farmers can be seen as almost the entirety of the Chosŏn people, and in terms of production value, it is clearly an agricultural land (農産鄕).

Therefore, rather than spending excessive cost (費) and labor (勞) on the small number of urban dwellers and obtaining little effect (效果), I believe it is necessary, based on all considerations, to strive (務圖) with the farming villages as the standard (本位).<133>

Therefore, in recent years (近年), everyone advocates for rural promotion (農촌진흥). In other words, the impoverishment (疲弊) of the farming villages means the decline/decay (*敗 - *likely 凋弊*) of the cities.

But then, what is the most necessary work among the projects for the farming villages? Regarding this, there is something to refer to: in the special rural issue of "Sin Tong-a" (新東亞) last February, in response to the question "What is the most important task among rural projects?", 15 out of 27 respondents mentioned the need for leadership training (지도자양성), and the other 10 or so also roughly expressed this sentiment. Judging (미*워 - *older 미루어*) from such facts, the public will keenly feel how necessary this Higher Agricultural Academy is.

However, I do not know in detail how many such rural leadership training institutions exist throughout Chosŏn, but I understand them to be Masan Gospel Agricultural Academy (馬山福音農事學院), T'ongyŏng Higher Agricultural Academy (統營高等農事學院), the Ki-ch'ŏng Leadership Training Association (基靑指導養成會 - *likely Christian Youth*), Sinch'ŏn Farmer Academy (信川農民學院), and the like.

At this time when training institutions for such necessary work are so scarce (稀少), the establishment of a Higher Agricultural Academy in your esteemed village (貴洞) is something I cannot help but congratulate (慶賀함을 不禁하는 바).

Hearing (得問컨대) that there are also assets worth 5,000 or 6,000 Won makes it even more joyful and fortunate (欣幸).

Speaking of the valuable points of this Higher Agricultural Academy, it is truly superior (優秀) even to the establishment of a Higher Common School (高等普通學校). I am not opposed to establishing a Higher Common School and studying up to the highest level (最高學部), but in terms of bringing practical benefit (實益) universally to the general public, especially given the current circumstances (形便) of Chosŏn, there is nothing more necessary than this.

To mention the valuable points (貴重點):

First (첫재), it lies in training leaders (지도자 양성). Normal schools (師範學校) are for guiding elementary students, higher normal schools (高等師範學校) are for educating middle school students, military academies (士官學校) are for training soldiers by nurturing officers, and seminaries (神學校) are for training pastors (牧師) to save people falling into pitfalls (陷* - *likely 陷穽*).

Likewise, this academy is intended to rescue (救濟) the farming masses (農民大衆) wandering in poverty (貧窮) and darkness (暗黑) in the impoverished farming villages.
(Applause)

Second (둘재), it lies in being an education based on character and economy (人物 及 經濟本位). Since it is called an agricultural academy, it might easily be mistaken for an agricultural school (農學校) aimed at employment, or, seeing it's rural, in this time when urban culture is considered superior and rural areas are looked down upon (輕蔑視), it could easily be slighted (輕視). If there is such a person, that is a great misconception (錯覺) and a lack of understanding (認識不足) due to misunderstanding (誤解).

This Academy is, at the very least, an ideal education (理想的 敎育) and character-building education (人格的 敎育) aimed at rescuing (此를 救濟키) the 1.7 million farming masses <134> as its subject. It will be an academy built with Gospel truth (福音眞理) as its foundation (基礎) and science (科學) and ideals (理想) as its materials (材料).

Therefore, those who learn at this academy will develop love for their hometown (愛鄕心) and must become servants (傭人 - *likely in the sense of 'serving') of the rural community.

Third (셋재), it lies in being a purely service-oriented education (純然한 奉仕的 敎育). It is different (不同) from schools like medical schools (醫學校), law schools (法學校), mining schools (鑛山學校), etc., which are for the education of oneself (一人). Although not originally intended as such for all education received (受함) at the various schools mentioned above (以上의 諸學校), it seems to be a fact that nowadays (現今) almost all (擧皆) are merely situated (노여저 - *older 놓여져*) in circumstances (形便) aimed at individual private gain (私腹). However, only (오즉 - *older 오직*) this Academy, being different (달너서 - *older 달라서*) from those, is understood to have a service-oriented nature (奉仕的 性質). It will be a purely sacrificial education (純全한 ?牲的 敎育 - *likely 犧牲的*).

To say it again, the path for Chosŏn people to live – the gospel movement (福音運動), economic movement (經濟運動), spiritual movement (精神運動), life improvement movement (生活運動), etc. – it is perhaps no exaggeration (過言) to say that it lies solely here (此 에). Consider the example (例) of Denmark's Folk High Schools (國民高等學校)! Then you will understand how necessary a service-oriented, practical (事業的) education for Chosŏn is.

Thus, as mentioned above, the establishment of such a valuable academy in this village (此洞) is an honor (榮光) for the village and a fortunate (幸福) event for the village. In this era when people try to attract (引置) Myŏn offices (面所) or common schools to their own Myŏn or village (自面自洞), how joyful and fortunate (欣幸) it is that such a valuable academy has come into being in this village!

Confucius's sayings, "Living in a humane neighborhood is beautiful (里仁爲美)" and "Choose a neighborhood to live in (擇里以居)," and Mencius's "teaching of moving three times (更三遷之敎)," have been extremely famous since ancient times (自古로). Bad youth (납분 少年 - *older 나쁜*) and vice (惡德) arise from bad neighborhoods (惡洞), and if there is a tavern (酒家) in a village, that village becomes ruined (敗洞). On the other hand (反面), the emergence of such an academy in this village is truly a fortunate thing.

This village (此 洞), having a church and now also this Academy, can indeed be called a village of much fortune (多福洞).

It is truly a joyous (깃거운 - *older 기꺼운*) thing that many valuable promoters/involved persons (事設者) for Chosŏn will be produced (輩出) through this Academy from now on (從前以後). Kapila[vastu] (가비) in India (印度) is famous as the birthplace (出生地) of Śākyamuni (釋伽), and Bethlehem (뻬들네헴) is famous as the birthplace of Jesus (耶蘇), but this village must strive to become widely (널니 - *older 널리*) known to the world through this Academy (此學院). To achieve this, this village and this Church must support (支持) it well, preserve (保全) it well, and at the same time (아울러) resolutely protect and advocate (奮然히 擁護) for it to achieve its initial purpose (最初의 目的).

There is <135> sufficient reason to support it even for the prosperity (繁昌) of the village (洞里). Furthermore, promising youth (有望한 靑年) from all over Chosŏn will gather (集中), and as the academy progresses (進步), productive enterprises (生産事業) will also flourish.

This is why we all support (支持) this Academy together here.

So, what is the essential point (要諦) of managing this Academy? It is to put forward the three great principles (主義): Evangelism (福音主義), Personalism (人格主義), and Practical Action-ism (實行主義).

Evangelism (福音主義) is the great truth (大眞理) that will save the Chosŏn people physically (肉的) and spiritually (靈的). As Dr. Ppideuwi (삐드위 - *likely 'Peter'*) once said, the goal of evangelism (傳道) in Chosŏn is not future spiritual salvation (救援) in heaven (天堂) but present physical salvation (肉的 救援), making that the essential point (要諦) of evangelism. Our Evangelism should conform (準하여야) to this method.

Personalism (人格主義) means cultivating individuals with lofty ideals (高尙한 理想), thorough spirit (徹底한 精神), and firm will (堅確한 意志), emphasizing quality (質) over quantity (量).

Contemporary Chosŏn people seem to be living not thinking of the afterlife (來世), but just passing day by day (그 날 그 날) in a state of living as if drunk and dying without purpose (醉生反死 - *likely typo for 醉生夢死*). There must be ideals for oneself as an individual and ideals for the entire Chosŏn people. In other words, people with great wishes (大願) and great hopes (大望) must emerge in large numbers. Regardless of location (何地를 막론하고), society is driven (運轉), progresses (向上), undergoes destruction (破壞), and construction (建築) only when great people (大人物) and individuals of great character (大人格者) are obtained. (Applause)

Contemporary youth, due to mental confusion (精神의 混亂) and weak will (意志의 薄弱), have become anesthetized (마취되고 말었읍니다) by their environment (環境).

Practical Action-ism (實行主義) means that contemporary people, especially Christians (基督敎人), are people who only know words. We must become people of the heart (마음의 人). Rather than literacy education (文字敎育), practical application (實踐躬行) is needed. It is a fact that contemporary people may have knowledge ("知") but lack action ("行"). Knowing good (好) and bad (否), goodness (善) and evil (惡), right and wrong (義不義), what should be done and what should not be done, yet "knowing but not acting" (知而不行) – such "clever fools" (똑똑한 바보) or "shameless blockheads" (뻔뻔한 멍텅구리) are believed to be extremely (심히) numerous in our society. (Applause)

Therefore, this rural work cannot succeed without practical action.
In Denmark's Folk High Schools (國民高等學校), the all-day curriculum (終日學課) is not literary education (文學敎育) but proceeds based on manual work classes (勞?課本位 - *likely 勞作課*) and diligence (勤勞本位).

Lastly, issues such as the Academy's curriculum (學課), management methods (經營方法), and staff (職員) are not my concern (關知할 바). However, I congratulate the establishment of this Academy and simultaneously bless its future (將來).
-End-<136>

**Summary**

In this 1936 speech delivered at the Songsan Education Association, Cho Man-sik argues passionately for the vital role of Christian youth in serving Korean society, particularly through rural development. He contrasts the perceived apathy and urban job-seeking focus of some youth with the vast, untouched potential for meaningful work in Chosŏn, especially in agriculture, which forms the backbone of the nation's population and economy. Cho strongly advocates for the establishment of the Higher Agricultural Academy as a crucial institution for training dedicated leaders equipped with Gospel principles, practical skills, and a spirit of self-sacrifice, modeled after Danish Folk High Schools. He emphasizes character-building (人格主義) and practical action (實行主義) over mere knowledge, urging youth to overcome societal inertia and contribute actively to the nation's upliftment.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for highlighting the focus on rural development (農村振興) as a key concern in late colonial Korea, reflecting the economic realities and social challenges of the time. It showcases the active role of Christian figures like Cho Man-sik in nationalist and social improvement movements, framing service within a religious context that emphasized both spiritual and 'physical' salvation (addressing poverty). The speech provides insight into contemporary critiques of urban youth culture versus the idealization of rural service, and the perceived urgency for practical, character-focused education distinct from purely academic or vocational tracks aimed at individual success within the colonial system.

米國의 어떤 학자 한 분이 일즉이 우리 조선에 나왔다가 朝鮮視察을 끝마치고 귀국하면서 한 말에 「세계 中 조선청년처럼 多事한 청년은 다시 없다」는 말을 한 일이 있읍니다.
또한 英國 신문계의 巨頭 「필드」氏는 朝鮮視察을 끝맛치고 돌아가면서 신문기자에게 대해서 하는 말이 「余는 朝鮮에 「일깜」이 그대로 숫으로 있는 것을 보고간다」하는 말을 한 일이 있음니다.
이 두사람의 외국인이 본 朝鮮觀은 朝鮮事業은 숫으로 그대로 있으며 청년들은 할 일이 많다는 것을 얼는 보고 한 말임니다.
과연 이 사실이 틀님없는 사실인가 함니다.
예수 당시 예수께서 도시와 촌락을 周廻하시고 曰, 『無牧之羊, 役多軍多』라 하셧든 것임니다.
예수가 만일 오늘날 朝鮮을 보신다면 猶太國其時에 하신 말슴을 일층 더 그대로 강조하시리라고 믿슴니다.
現下 朝鮮現象을 보면 과연 정치, 경제, 종교, 교육, 문화, 기타 각 방면으로 할 일이 참말 많슴니다. 맛치 미개척지와 신발견지와도 같음니다.

또는 「余來世는 非受奉事, 乃爲奉事也, 故로 基督靑年은 奉任가 天職, 責務, 本分也」라고 하시였음니다.
동일한 朝鮮을 보면서도 사람에 의하여서는 無事多事로 볼 것임니다.
薄行弱志의 청년들은 허영과 사치, 현대 風湖에 마취되여 그네들의 일은 오직 취직인줄만 아는 듯한 현상임니다. 도시에서 봉급생활로 美衣, 甘食과 耳目之好, 心志之樂만 꿈뀌고 취직에 몰두하야 此 事業洪水時代에 사회에는 無事 즉 할 일이 없음을<132> 탄식하고 있음니다. 이러한 사람은 大局을 未見, 不知한 暗昧한 자라고 할 것임니다.
이야말로 똑똑한 바보, 뻔뻔한 멍텅구리라고나 할 것임니다.
이와 같은 자는 취직만 구하매 사회에는 일감이 없다고 행용 말하는 버릇이 있읍니다.
심오한 이상, 위대한 理心의 청년들은 此 時代 此 地域에 처하야 이 百姓을 보매 奉仕的 정신이 없지 않어야 할 것임니다.
人傑은 地靈이요 영웅은 시대적 산물임니다. 此 地域에서 此 現狀을 보매 感奮과 反撥이 不無할 것이며 자연히 *西(모서)가 埃及宮中의 공자생활을 ?履와 如히 軀棄함과 같이 사상의 변동이 생길 것임니다.
