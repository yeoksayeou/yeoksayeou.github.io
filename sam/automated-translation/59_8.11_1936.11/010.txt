Photo Feature
Seeking Traces of Old Seoul

Namdaemun Gate in Meiji 30 (1897)

The temporary houses (假家) on the left and right sides of the road were permitted for temporary construction, with the condition that they would be demolished anytime upon order from the royal palace.

Detached Palace on Pukhansan Mountain

This is the detached palace used long ago when the king fled for refuge (蒙塵); now only the palace walls remain.

Kyŏngbokkung Palace Main Gate in Meiji 40 (1907)

Although it has now been relocated, this is Kwanghwamun Gate, the former main gate of Kyŏngbokkung Palace, and the Haet'ae statues.

Long Autumn Night

Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi

Gathering of Famous Actors

Famous actors and directors gathered for a roundtable discussion (座談會) hosted by our company (本社). Front row, right to left: Kim Yuyŏng, Kim Yŏnsil, Pok Hyesuk, Mun Yebong, Pak Kich'ae. Back row, right to left: Our company's Pak Sanghŭi, Director Yi Myŏng.

**Summary**

This photo feature from the November 1936 issue of Samch'ŏlli presents images juxtaposing historical scenes of Seoul with contemporary figures. It includes photos of Namdaemun Gate (1897), a detached palace on Pukhansan Mountain, and Kwanghwamun Gate (1907), accompanied by brief descriptions. Alongside these historical images are portraits of the famous dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi and a group photo of prominent film actors and directors attending a roundtable hosted by the magazine. For historians, the article is interesting for its visual documentation of Seoul's landmarks from the late Joseon/early colonial period, the use of Japanese Meiji era years to date Korean historical sites, and the snapshot of Korean film and dance celebrities active during the colonial era, reflecting the cultural landscape of the time.
