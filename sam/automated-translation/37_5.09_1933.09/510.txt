Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제9호
Publication Date: 1933년 09월 01일
Article Title: 文人의 新團體 (New Group of Literary Figures)
Author: N/A
Article Type: 소식 (News/Notice)

As the bleak autumn wind (簫苾한 秋風) begins to blow, good news has recently been heard in literary circles. With Yi Chongmyŏng (李種鳴), Kim Yuyŏng (金幽影), Yi T'aejun (李泰俊), Yi Hyosŏk (李孝石), Kim Kirim (金起林), Yi Muyŏng (李無影), Cho Yongman (趙容万), and others as promoters (發起), a new literary arts group has been formed and is expected to be very active. The main purpose (主旨) of the association (結社) is said to be fostering fellowship among literary figures and promoting the arts movement from a position of freedom. In any case, its future activities are anticipated. <53>

**Summary**

This news item announces the formation of a new literary group in Korea, initiated by prominent writers including Yi Chongmyŏng, Kim Yuyŏng, Yi T'aejun, Yi Hyosŏk, Kim Kirim, Yi Muyŏng, and Cho Yongman. The group's stated goals are to promote mutual friendship among writers and to advance the arts movement based on principles of freedom. For historians, this article is interesting as it documents the self-organization of Korean intellectuals and artists during the Japanese colonial period, highlighting their efforts to maintain cultural activity and foster solidarity under restrictive circumstances. The emphasis on "freedom" (자유스러운 입장) is particularly noteworthy within the colonial context.
