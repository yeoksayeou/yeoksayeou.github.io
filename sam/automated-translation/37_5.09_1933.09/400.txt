Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제9호
Publication Date: 1933년 09월 01일
Article Title: 流言蜚語 (Rumors)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing/Column)

Right now in Chosŏn, the four phonograph companies—Sieron, Bikt'oao-k'ei, K'ŏllŏmbia—are fiercely competing in record sales. Among these, 'Pŏsŏjin Taemŏri' (벗어진 대머리 - The Stripped Bald Head) by Mr. Sin Pulch'ul (申不出), released by Sieron, is one of the best-sellers, reaching about 10,000 copies or more. However, compared to the 400,000 copies of Shōtarō's (勝太郞) 'Shima no Musume' (島の娘 - Island Daughter), this is merely 1/40th.

It is said that the famous actor Mr. Na Un'gyu (羅雲奎) recently entered and is staying at an old people's home (양로원) in Ogin-dong (玉仁洞). The miserable reality of life that Chosŏn's stage and film artists are experiencing is astonishing. When will better times come? <43>

**Summary**

This short column reports two pieces of news or "rumors." First, it notes the fierce record sales competition in colonial Korea among four phonograph companies, highlighting that a popular Korean song by Sin Pulch'ul sold around 10,000 copies, significantly less than the 400,000 copies sold by a Japanese song by Shōtarō. Second, it mentions the famous Korean film actor Na Un'gyu is reportedly staying in a nursing home in Ogin-dong, reflecting the dire financial straits of Korean artists. The article implicitly contrasts the success of Japanese popular culture with the struggles of Korean artists and cultural products under colonial rule, lamenting the difficult reality for Korean performers. Historians might find the sales figures useful for understanding cultural consumption patterns and the market dominance of Japanese media, as well as the precarious economic situation of prominent Korean cultural figures like Na Un'gyu during this period.

流言蜚語
지금 조선서는 시에론·빅토아오-케이·컬넘비아의 네 축음기 회사에서 레코-트 판매전을 불이나게 전개하고 잇는데, 시에론에서 내인 申不出氏의 「버서진 대머리」가 가장 잘 팔닌 중의 하나으로 약 10,000枚 以上에 달하엿든가, 그러나 이것을 勝太郞의 「島の娘」의 40만 枚에 비하면 겨우 40分之1.

名優 羅雲奎 氏가 최근 玉仁洞의 양로원에 드러가서 起留한다고, 朝鮮의 무대 영화 예술가들이 격고 잇는 현실생활의 慘憺은 놀날 만하다. 언제나 세월이 오나. ＜43＞
