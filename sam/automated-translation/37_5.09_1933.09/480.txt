발행일	1933년 09월 01일
기사제목	松花江畔에서 (On the Banks of the Songhua River)
필자	春園
기사형태	시 (Poem)

松花江畔에서
						春園
松花江 여기로다.
그 무엇을 보앗는고
우에는 하늘이요
아래에는 벌이로다.
그 속에 힌 한줄기는
내맘인가 하노라.

弔忠魂 (Mourning Loyal Souls)
千萬番 죽사온들
변할 뉘 아니여든
그뜻 富貴야
내 안다 하오리까
찰하로 忠魂이 되어
울고 울까 하노라.

三學士 피 흘린 곳이
여기리까 저기리까.
瀋陽城 풀 욱어진 곳에
風雨만 재오처라.
忠魂을 부르는 손이
갈바 물라 하노라.

세 번 부르노라.
三學士의 가신 넉을
300년 지나기로
忠魂이 스오리까
오늘에 치는 風雨를
눈물 흘려 뵈노라.
癸酉夏瀋陽에서 ＜49＞

On the Banks of the Songhua River
						Ch'unwŏn
Here is the Songhua River.
What was it I saw?
Above is the sky,
Below are the fields.
That white streak within them,
Could it be my heart?

Mourning Loyal Souls
Even if they died ten million times,
Who would change?
Their noble cause,
How could I claim to know it?
Rather, becoming a loyal spirit,
I wish only to weep and weep.

Was it here, or was it there,
The place where the Three Scholars (三學士) shed their blood?
In Shenyang (瀋陽) Castle, where the grass is overgrown,
Only wind and rain (風雨) stir.
The hand that calls the loyal souls (忠魂)
Asks where it should go.

Three times I call.
The departed spirits of the Three Scholars (三學士),
Though 300 years have passed,
Will their loyal souls (忠魂) fade?
The wind and rain (風雨) striking today,
I show them, shedding tears.
Summer of Kye-yu (1933), in Shenyang (瀋陽) <49>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su), written during a visit to Shenyang in 1933, reflects on the landscape of the Songhua River and mourns the Three Scholars (Samhaksa) who were executed there nearly 300 years earlier for refusing to submit to the Qing invaders. The poet contrasts the natural scenery with the historical tragedy, expressing sorrow and questioning the passage of time's effect on the memory of loyalty and sacrifice. Historically, the poem is significant as it was written by a prominent Korean intellectual in Manchuria during Japanese rule. It touches upon Korean historical grievances against foreign invaders (Manchus/Qing), a theme resonant during the colonial period. Historians find this interesting given Yi Kwang-su's later, controversial shift towards collaboration with Japan, making this earlier expression of patriotic sentiment towards historical loyalists a point of complex analysis regarding his ideological evolution and the pressures faced by Korean intellectuals under colonialism.
