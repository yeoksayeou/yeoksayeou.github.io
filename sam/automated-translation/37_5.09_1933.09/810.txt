The Tragedy of a Female Writer, Miss Kim Myŏngsun Meets Misfortune in Tokyo

On the Musashino (武藏野) plains, the scorching sun beat down, and as if the great city of Tokyo were entirely submerged in a blazing furnace (紅爐) during the dog days of summer (三伏), around nine o'clock on the night of August 8th, a pitiful and sad event occurred in front of the Yanagikawa (柳川) Restaurant at 3, 1-chōme, Kanda-ku (神田區), Tokyo.
Two gentlemen struck a beautiful young woman selling peanuts (落花生) with their geta sandals (게다짝), severely injuring her and knocking her down. Who would have thought this woman was Miss Kim Myŏngsun (金明淳), the female writer who once reigned like a queen in the literary world of the Peninsula (半島), stirring the hearts of young men and women with hundreds of beautiful songs and dozens of elegant novels she had written?

Miss Kim Myŏngsun went over to Tokyo four years ago and was studying French and music at the Athena Francais (아테네, 후란쓰), a French language school in Kanda (神田). At Athena Francais, her extraordinary talent (才操) shone through, astonishing her teachers and earning the admiration (欽慕) of her peers.

However, Miss Kim lacked funds for her studies (學資). Her elderly mother (老母) lived back in her hometown, barely getting by, and neither her relatives nor friends had the means to support her. Yet, remembering her past efforts of diligent study (螢雪의 功) during her girlhood, Miss Kim, having thrown herself into the window of learning (學窓) for a second time, resolved to study while enduring hardship (苦學) with her frail body, without a shred of despair. Since then, she attended the language preparatory course at Athena Francais during the day and went out as a peddler (行商) at night. Thereafter, on the vast streets of Tokyo, she could be seen anxiously trying to collect meager sums of 5 or 10 sen, sometimes carrying a handbag packed with items like toothbrushes (칫솔), tooth powder (치분), and socks, and other times carrying a box of snacks like Korean taffy (엿) and peanuts (落花生), going from house to house, person to person, in unfamiliar and strange places.

The reason Miss Kim attended Athena Francais was to fulfill her long-cherished desire (宿望) to study abroad in France (佛蘭西留學). She yearned to go study literature and music in beautiful Southern Europe (南歐), France, the land of passion where the poetess Sappho (삿포-) was born and where "The Hunchback of Notre Dame" (노-돌담의 곱사등) originated, the land of song and dance where Parisians (巴里쟌누) laugh and enjoy the Fête de Paris (巴里祭). Amidst such hardship, her resolute figure, wholeheartedly majoring in French, suggested that if her long-held dream were realized soon, would not the literary world of the Peninsula gain a brilliant morning star radiating the passion of Southern Europe?

Miss Kim's hometown is P'yŏngyang City (平壤市) by the Taedong River (大洞江). As a girl of only 17 or 18 who had graduated from P'yŏngyang Girls' High School (平壤女子高等普通學校) with excellent grades, she submitted a short story titled "The Girl of Mystery" (의문의 소녀) to the magazine "Ch'ŏngch'un" (靑春), then presided over by Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn (崔南善). Her story was chosen for second place among over 70 manuscripts submitted for the prize contest (懸賞應募原稿). The judge (選者) at that time was Ch'unwŏn (春園) Yi Kwangsu (李光洙). Thus, getting her start ("스타-트") with the "Ch'ŏngch'un" magazine, Miss Kim went over to Tokyo to major in literature, which she enjoyed, and published many <89> beautiful poems and writings through newspapers and magazines like Kaebyŏk (開闢) at the time. Consequently, as her literary fame (文名) grew, there were various rumors circulating about her love affair with a Mr. K.

Then, in Taishō 14 (1925), after publishing a collection of poems and fiction titled "Fruits of Life" (生命의 果實) through the Hansŏng Book Company (漢城圖書會社), she distanced herself from writing (筆硯) for a time. Now, it is impossible not to feel saddened to encounter Miss Kim again through this sorrowful news of her injury (負傷).

The reason for her injury that night is said to be that her request to buy peanuts annoyed the drunken patrons (酒客), who were tipsy (酒氣), leading to the assault (구타). The severe injury is expected to require about a week of recovery.

As an aside, it is said that the character Zhu Ying (朱英) in Nakanishi Inosuke's (中西伊之助) masterpiece novel "From Behind You All" (汝等の背後より) was modeled after Miss Kim Myŏngsun.

Now, we shall include a couple of Miss Kim's poems.

**Summary**

The article reports the assault of the prominent Korean female writer Kim Myŏngsun in Tokyo in August 1933. Once celebrated in Korea's literary scene, Kim had moved to Tokyo to study French and music at Athena Francais, aspiring to study further in France. Lacking financial support, she resorted to peddling goods like peanuts at night to survive and fund her education. She was attacked and severely injured by two drunken men while selling peanuts. The article laments her tragic circumstances, contrasts them with her past achievements and future aspirations, mentions a past relationship scandal, notes her 1925 publication "Fruits of Life," and includes three of her poems reflecting themes of sorrow and hardship. It also mentions a claim that she was the model for a character in a Japanese novel. For historians, the article offers a poignant glimpse into the precarious existence of a talented Korean woman pursuing intellectual ambitions in colonial-era Japan, highlighting the economic struggles, societal vulnerabilities, and personal costs faced by colonial subjects, particularly female intellectuals, in the metropole. The mention of specific locations, institutions, publications, and literary figures provides valuable context for the cultural and social history of the period.

女流作家의 此悲慘, 東京서 金明淳孃 遭難
무사시노(武藏野)벌판에 뜨거운 폭양이 내리쪼이어 大東京市가 紅爐 속에 아주 잠긴 듯한 三伏에도 8월 8일 이날 밤 아홉시 경 東京神田區一丁目3番地 야나기까와 柳川飮食店 압헤는 가련한 슬푼 일이 한 가지 생기엇다.
엇든 신사 두 사람이 「落花生」을 팔려온 젊은 미모의 여성을 게다짝으로 따리어 중상을 식혀 너머트리엇다. 이 여성이란 한동안 半島의 여류문단에 여왕가치 군림하여 수백 편의 아름다운 노래와 수십 편의 유려한 소설로 써서 靑春士女의 심금을 울니어 놋튼 여류작가 金明淳 孃일 줄 엇지 뜻하엿스랴.
金明淳 孃은 4년 전에 東京으로 건너가서 간다(神田)의 「아테네, 후란쓰」란 佛語學院으로 다니면서 佛蘭西 말과 음악을 공부하고 잇섯다, 「아테네, 후란쓰」에선 그의 비상한 才操가 나타내어 선생들도 경탄하고 동모들도 欽慕하여 마지 안엇다.

그러나 孃에게는 學資가 업섯다, 그의 고향에는 老母가 게시어 근근히 지내시고, 그이 친척이나 친구들도 그를 돌보아 줄만한 힘이 업섯다, 그러나 녯날 소녀시대에 螢雪의 功을 닥든 녯일을 생각하야 두 번재 學窓에 몸을 던진 孃은 조곰도 낙심함이 업시 연약한 몸으로 苦學하기를 결심하고 以來 낫에는 「아테네, 후란쓰」로 어학준비를 다니면서 밤에는 行商을 나섯다, 그 뒤 너르나 너른 東京의 거리거리에는 엇든 때는 「칫솔, 치분, 양말」등을 싼 손 가방을 들고, 엇든 때는 조선 엿과 「落花生」가튼 과자상자를 들고 이 집 저 집, 이 사람 저 사람, 산 설고 낫서른 곳곳에서 5錢, 10錢씩의 零粹한 돈을 모으기에 노심초사하는 양자를 볼 수 잇섯다.
孃이 「아테네, 후란쓰」에 다니는 까닭은 그는 年來의 宿望이든 佛蘭西留學을 가기 위함이엇다, 일즉 女詩人  삿포-가 나고 「노-돌담의 곱사등」이 난 정열의 나라, 巴里祭로 巴里쟌누들이 웃고 즐기는 노래와 춤의 나라, 이 아름다운 南歐 佛蘭西로 문학과 음악 공부를 나서려 그리하엿다. 그 고난 속에서도 一心不亂히 佛蘭西語學을 전공하는 그 굿굿한 자태, 이제 얼마 아니잇서 孃의 宿望이 다 달려진다 할진대 半島文壇에는 南歐의 정열을 發하는 찬란한 明星一座를 가지게 되지 안으랴.
孃의 고향은 大洞江까의 平壤市다. 平壤女子高等普通學校를 우수한 성적으로 마친 17, 8밧게 아니되든 소녀의 몸으로 일즉 崔南善 氏가 主宰하든 「靑春」잡지에 「의문의 소녀」란 단편소설 1편을 내여, 懸賞應募原稿 70여편 중에서 2등에 뽑히엇다, 당시의 選者는 春園 李光洙氏라. 이리하야 「靑春」잡지에서 「스타-트」를 끈은 孃은 東京에 건너가서 즐기든 문학을 전공하면서 當時 開闢其他의 신문잡지을 통하야 ＜89＞ 아름다운 詩文을 만히 발표하엿다, 그래서 文名이 점점 놉하가든 중 K氏와의 연애사건이 잇서 여러 가지 소문도 전하여 왓섯다.
그리고 大正14년에 漢城圖書會社의 손을 통하야 詩文 小說集 「生命의 果實」이란 단행본을 내어 노코는 一時 筆硯을 멀니하고 잇더니 이제 이 슬푼 負傷의 소식으로 다시 孃을 맛나게 된 것을 슬프게 녁이지 안을 수 업다.
이날 밤에 부상당한 이유도 「落花生」을 사 달나고 한 것이 酒氣를 띈 그 酒客의 비위에 거슬니어, 구타를 당한 것으로 약 1주간 예정의 重傷이라한다.
딴말이나 中西伊之助의 名作 「汝等の背後より」란 小說에 나오는 주인공 朱英이라 함이 金明淳孃을 모델로 삼은 것이란 말도 잇다.
이제 孃의 詩文 두어 편을 실으리라.
