Gandhi as I Saw Him
						Bernard Shaw

I cannot understand at all the state of mind behind things like Mr. Kkanji (깐지君) recently getting fed up with Indians (印度人) or trying out fasting, saying he will show them he can die.

If I had the chance to meet Mr. Kkanji, I intend to advise him,
"That is not your job. Hey, I'm telling you to stop."

Generally, a figure who receives respect and love (敬愛) from the world should not die. On the contrary, they should kill people. It would be better if Mr. Kkanji also killed off those 6 million Indian masses (印度大衆) of his party (黨). If he did so, Mr. Kkanji would be publicized (宣傳) as a much, much greater figure and value, and his cause would surely prevail. That non-armament (非武裝) or non-resistance principle (無抵抗主義) or whatnot is all wrong. No matter how much their weapons (武裝) are taken away, when people resist, they resist. They manage it even with fistfights...

However, whatever the case, that person is a great figure of the kind who appears in the world maybe once every 500 or 600 years. Speaking of the India problem (印度問題), it's quite simple and clear (간단명료). Isn't India (印度) the most populous place among British territory (英吉利領土)? Therefore, the day will come before long when Britain (英國), finding it too much for its grasp (손아귀), will say, 'Go ahead and do as you please.' That's all there is to it. <71>

**Summary**

In this short piece attributed to Bernard Shaw, the author expresses incomprehension regarding Gandhi's fasting and suggests it's counterproductive for a respected leader. In a typically provocative Shaw style, he argues Gandhi should metaphorically "kill" his followers to enhance his stature and achieve his goals, dismissing non-violence as ineffective. Despite this critique, Shaw acknowledges Gandhi's historical significance and predicts Britain will inevitably grant India independence due to the practical difficulty of governing such a vast population. For historians, this article offers insight into how a major Western intellectual's views on Gandhi and anti-colonial resistance were presented to a Korean audience during the colonial period. Shaw's cynical realism regarding both Gandhi's methods and Britain's motivations provides a potentially influential counterpoint to purely idealistic views of independence movements.

내가 본 깐지
						빠너드 쇼-
「깐지君이 최근 印度人에게 실증을 내엿다든지 또는 나는 저를 죽여 보이겟노라고 단식을 하여보는 일 등의 심경을 나는 전혀 이해 할 수 업다.
나는 기회가 잇서 깐지-君을 맛날 수 잇다면.
「그것은 자네 할 일이 못되네. 여보게 그만 두라니까.」
하고 권고할 작정이다. 大體 世上으로부터 敬愛를 밧는 인물은 죽어서는 안된다. 반대로 사람을 죽이는 것이다. 깐지君도 그 黨 6백만의 印度大衆을 죽여 버리는 것이 조타. 그리하면 깐지君는 좀더 좀더 훌융한 인물과 가치 宣傳되며 그 주장이 통하여 지리라. 그 非武裝이라든지 無抵抗主義라든지 하는 것이 다 틀렷다. 아모리 武裝을 빼앗겨도 사람이란 반항 할 때에는 반항하는 것이야. 주먹 따짐으로라도 해여내니까...
그러치만 엇지 되엿든 그 사람은 훌융하게 5, 600년간에 한 사람이나 그 정도로 세상에 나올 종류의 큰 인물이야. 印度問題로 말하면 그야 간단명료하다. 印度는 英吉利領土中 가장 사람이 만히 사는 곳이 아닌가. 그러기에 이제 얼마 아니잇서 英國이 제 손아귀에 넘처서 어서 마음대로 하시구려 하고 말할 날이 올 것이야. 다만 그 뿐이야.」 ＜71＞
