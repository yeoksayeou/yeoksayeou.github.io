잡지명	삼천리 제5권 제9호
발행일	1933년 09월 01일
기사제목	山 (Mountain)
필자	素月
기사형태	시 (Poem)

山
						素月
산새도 오리나무
우에서 운다
산새는 왜 우노 시메 산골
嶺 넘어 갈나고 그래서 울지.

눈은 나리네 와서 덥피네
오늘도 하룻길
7, 80里
도라섯서 60리는 가기도 햇소.

不歸, 不歸, 다시 不歸
三水甲山에 다시 不歸
사나희 속이라 니즈련만
15년 정분을 못 닛겟네.

산에는 오는 눈, 들에는 녹는 눈
산새도 오리나무
우헤서 운다
三水甲山 가는 길은 고개의 길. <18>

**English Translation**

Mountain
						Sowŏl
A mountain bird cries
on the alder tree.
Why does the mountain bird cry? In the deep mountain valley (시메 산골)
It cries because it wants to go over the high pass (嶺).

Snow falls, comes and covers.
Today too, a day's journey
Seventy, eighty *ri*.
Turning back, I've gone sixty *ri* already.

No return (不歸), no return (不歸), again no return (不歸)
To Samsu Kapsan (三水甲山), again no return (不歸).
A man's heart (사나희 속) would forget, but
Fifteen years of affection (정분), I cannot forget (못 닛겟네).

Snow falls on the mountain, snow melts in the field.
A mountain bird cries
on the alder tree.
The road to Samsu Kapsan (三水甲山) is a road of mountain passes. <18>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl (Kim Sowŏl) uses the imagery of a harsh mountain journey in the snow to convey feelings of sorrow, hardship, and inescapable attachment. The speaker travels a long distance, lamenting the difficulty of the path and reflecting on the impossibility of returning ("不歸") from Samsu Kapsan, a place symbolizing exile. Despite the passage of fifteen years and the desire to forget, a deep emotional bond remains. The recurring image of the crying mountain bird enhances the melancholic mood. From a historical perspective, the reference to Samsu Kapsan is significant. Known as a remote area of banishment in Korean history, invoking its name evokes themes of extreme hardship, isolation, and exile, which could subtly reflect the suffering and displacement experienced by Koreans during the colonial period, even without explicit political statements.
