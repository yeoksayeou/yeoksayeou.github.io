彈正臺 (Danjeongdae)
Starting this month, we have newly established the "Dangeumdae" (彈琴臺 - Platform for Playing the Geum; a pun likely meaning 'Platform for Striking Accusations') column. For the public good of society, if you have matters for appeal or censure, please submit a manuscript of no more than four pages. Manuscripts are accepted until the 25th of each month.

Suppress the Evil-Type Songs! To Mr. Shimizu, Head of the Government-General's Books Section

When passing by the Chongno intersection in the evening, one sees crowds of dozens gathered in front of shops, listening to phonographs and radios. However, looking at the majority of these songs, they are deplorable, evil-type songs (惡種 歌謠) that heighten decadent, lewd, and nihilistic thoughts. These songs are eroding the minds of the healthy populace day and night with a power like opium. If Hitler were to pass by the streets of Chongno and see this spectacle, wouldn't he, for the sake of his motto – "For the Reinvigoration of the German Social Populace's Vitality" – have the Nazis smash the phonograph horns in one fell swoop and disperse the crowd?
Mr. Shimizu, you who oversee Books, Music, and Pictures (圖書音畵), the total accumulation of the Peninsula's spiritual culture, as the Head of the Government-General's Books Section, we heard you say in a lamenting tone shortly after your appointment:
"Why do Korean newspapers and magazines report only the dark aspects of society like murder, family ruin, starvation, natural disasters, and other signs of decline (沒落相)? Wouldn't it be better to report on the bright and constructive aspects (光明面) to invigorate the vitality of the social populace?"
These words are truth. We too will strive to do so. Regarding these songs, which are called the flower of culture and have the greatest influence, we ask you to issue a great suppression (大彈壓) greater than Hitler's, using your authority and sincerity, to eradicate these evil-type songs from Peninsula society. How much brighter and more vibrant would this society become if these decadent, sorrowful melodies (哀調) ceased to flow into our ears? (Submitted by Ch'unch'u Hagin - 春秋學人寄)

Regretting the 8,000 Won. To Mr. O Yunsŏn, Chairman of the Societal Funeral Committee for the late Ms. Paek Sŏnhaeng

No one objects to dressing the late Ms. Paek in fine clothes (錦衣) for her funeral procession. Also, no one would disagree with society mourning and tearfully sending her off under the name of a societal funeral (社會葬). However, the late Ms. Paek was a person of frugality (節儉); sending her off with excessive luxury would be an insult to her spirit. She was a noble-minded person (慷慨之士) who knew society before herself; if a few individuals involved carry out the ceremony according to their own desires, with sincerity that society deems improper, she would be heartbroken and weep in the afterlife.
According to the announcement by Mr. O Yunsŏn, Chairman of the Societal Funeral Committee for the late Ms. Paek Sŏnhaeng, the funeral expenses this time exceeded 8,000 won. 8,000 won is a huge sum, close to 10,000 won. Even if her corpse were adorned with such a large amount of gold and stone (金石), her life cannot be restored – what foolishness (愚擧) is this? We believe that with 8,000 won, the great shame of P'yŏngyang society and the decaying aspect of humanity, the slum outside the Ch'ilsŏng Gate (七星門), could be renovated (革破), leading tens of thousands of poor people onto the path of eternal life (永生). Furthermore, we believe doing so is the common sense of societal figures (社會人士) and would have been the true intention (本意) of the late Ms. Paek.
Ultimately, aren't the tens of thousands of living souls (生靈) more precious than the single corpse of Ms. Paek, which will turn into a piece of earth and stone (土石)? Appealing to the common sense of Chairman O and others, we hope for your reflection so that such actions (擧措) do not occur again in the future. (Ch'obyŏngjŏng - 草兵丁) <66>

**Summary**

This article consists of two letters submitted to the magazine's new "Danjeongdae" column. The first, addressed to Shimizu, the head of the Government-General's Books Section, vehemently criticizes the popular songs played on phonographs and radios in Chongno, calling them decadent, lewd, and nihilistic "evil-type songs" that corrupt the public mind like opium. The writer urges Shimizu to implement a harsh censorship ("great suppression"), even invoking Hitler's methods as a positive example, to purify Korean culture and promote vitality. The second letter, addressed to O Yunsŏn, the chairman of the recent societal funeral committee for philanthropist Paek Sŏnhaeng, criticizes the excessive funeral cost (8,000 won) as wasteful and contrary to the deceased's frugal nature. The writer argues the money would be better spent alleviating poverty, such as improving the slums near P'yŏngyang's Ch'ilsŏng Gate, thereby honouring Paek's spirit more appropriately than lavishly decorating her corpse.

From a historical perspective, the article reveals significant social anxieties and critiques within 1930s colonial Korea. The first letter's extreme reaction against popular music, its call for strong state censorship, and the shocking positive reference to Hitler highlight deep cultural insecurities and the influence of authoritarian ideologies of the time, possibly reflecting a desire for moral/social order amidst perceived decay. The second letter offers a sharp social critique, contrasting extravagant spending for the dead with the pressing needs of the living poor, implicitly criticizing the priorities of the social elite and touching upon issues of wealth disparity and social responsibility.

惡種歌謠를 彈壓하시오. 總督府 淸水 圖書課長에게

저녁 때 鍾路 네거리를 지나느라면 점포 압헤 수십 명식 몰녀 서서 축음기와 라듸오를 듯는 군중이 잇슴을 봄니다. 그런데 이 노래의 대부분을 보면 壓世, 淫逸, 虛無思想을 고조하는 痛恨할 惡種 歌謠들로 이 노래가 日夜 阿片과 가튼 세력으로 건전한 인민의 두뇌를 侵蝕하고 잇슴니다. 만일 힛틀러가 鍾路 길바닥을 지나다가 이 광경을 보앗던들 그의 못토-인 「獨逸社會民衆의 元氣의 振作을 위하야」 나치스를 식혀 一擧에 축음기 통을 깨트려 버리고 군중을 驅逐하여 버릴 것이 아니오리까.
일즉 半島의 정신문화의 總集積인 圖書音畵를 주관하는 總督府圖書課長인 淸水氏 당신은 부임 초에 개탄의 어조로
「朝鮮의 신문 잡지는 엇재서 사회의 암흑면인 살인, 敗家, 기아, 천재지변 등 沒落相만을 보도하느냐, 오히려 사회민중의 원기를 振作키 위하야 명랑하고도 건설적인 光明面을 보도함이 올치 안켓느냐」하는 말을 들엇슴니다.
이 말은 진리외다, 우리도 그리하기를 노력하겟스니, 문화의 꼿이라는, 가장 영향이 큰 이 가요에 대하야 당신은 그 권력과 성의를 가지고 힛틀러 이상의 大彈壓을 내리어 半島社會에서 이 惡種歌謠를 근절시켜 주옵소서, 퇴폐적인 이 哀調를 띈 멜로듸-가 우리의 귓가으로 흐르기를 끈처준다 할진대 얼마나 이 사회가 명랑하여지고 생기가 잇서지리까.(春秋學人寄)

八千圓을 앗기노라. 白善行女史 社會葬委員長 吳胤善氏에
白女史에게 錦衣를 입혀 葬送함에는 누구나 항의가 업다. 또 白女史를 社會葬의 명목으로 一社會가 哀悼裡에 泣送하는데도 아모도 이의할 사람이 업다, 그러나 女史는 節儉의 人이라 그를 送함에 過分의 사치로써 함은 그의 靈을 욕보이게 함일 것이며, 그는 己身보다 사회를 先히 아는 慷慨之士라 사회가 정당치 안타고 認하는 성의를 局에 당한 몃몃 개인의 의욕대로 거행한다 할진대 그는 마음이 압허, 지하에서 울 것이다.
그런데 지난번 白善行 女史 社會葬 委員長 吳胤善 氏의 발표에 의하건대 금번 장례비용이 8천원을 넘엇다고 한다. 8천원이면 만원 갓가운 거액이다. 이와가치 거액의 金石으로 그 시체를 장식한다 할지라도 그의 생명이 환생치 못할 것인 이상- 이 무슨 愚擧인고, 吾人은 8천원이면, 平壤社會의 큰 치욕이요, 인류의 腐敗面인 저 七星門 밧 빈민굴을 革破하야 수만의 빈민을 永生의 길노 잇끌 수 잇스며 또 그리함이 社會人士의 상식이요, 白女史의 本意이엇슬 줄 밋는다.
결국 一片의 土石으로 化할 白氏의 1개의 시체보다, 사라잇는 數萬生靈이 더 貴치 안을가 委員長 吳氏의 상식에 訴하야 諸氏의 반성이 잇서 今後 또다시 이러한 擧措업기를 바라노라. (草兵丁)  ＜66＞
