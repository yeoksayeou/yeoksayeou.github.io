高手集 (Collection of Masters)

Masters who play Go (바둑) well are Messrs. Pak Sŭngbin (朴勝彬), Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Chŏng Taehyŏn (鄭大鉉), Yi Chŏngsŏp (李晶燮), Chang Yŏrun (張烈雲), and Min Taesik (閔大植). (The photo is of Mr. Min)

Masters who play Janggi (장기) well are Messrs. Chang Chonggŏn (張倧健), etc.

Masters of horseback riding are Messrs. Pak Hŭido (朴熙道), Chŏng Sŏkt'ae (鄭錫泰), etc.

Masters who drink much alcohol (술) are Messrs. Wŏn Sehun (元世勳), Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿), Ch'a Sangch'an (車相瓚), Sim Taesŏp (沈大燮), Yi Kapsŏng (李甲基), Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯), etc.

Masters who speak well are Messrs. Kim Ch'angje (金昶濟), Yŏ Unhyŏng (呂運亨), Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), Ok Chun Chin (玉濬珍), and Chu Yohan (朱耀翰).<85>

**Summary**

This short news item from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* lists prominent Korean figures of the early 1930s who were considered "masters" (高手, kosu) in various fields: Go, Janggi (Korean chess), horseback riding, alcohol consumption, and oratory. It names several well-known individuals for each category, providing a glimpse into the social recognition of specific talents and leisure activities among the elite during that period. For historians, this piece is interesting as it identifies individuals recognized for cultural or social skills, including figures known in politics, literature, and other fields (like Yŏ Unhyŏng, Kim Pyŏngno, Chu Yohan), offering a perspective on social hierarchies and recognized talents beyond professional or political roles within colonial Korean society. The inclusion of drinking as a skill highlights certain social norms or admired traits of the time.
