Publication Date: 1933년 09월 01일
Article Title: [감쟝새 적다 하고 大鵬아 웃지마라…] (Do not laugh, Dapeng, saying the quail is small...)
Author: 李澤
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Yi T’aek (李澤)
Saying the quail (감쟝새) is small,
Dapeng (大鵬), do not laugh.
In the vast sky of ninety thousand li (9만리 長空),
You fly and it flies too.
Leave it be; we are both ordinary flying birds (一般飛鳥).
How could you and I be different? <66>

**Summary**

This short poem uses the allegory of the small quail (감쟝새) and the giant mythical bird Dapeng (大鵬) from Zhuangzi. The poet, Yi T’aek (李澤), admonishes the Dapeng not to look down upon the quail simply because it is small, pointing out that both fly in the same vast sky and are fundamentally just birds. The poem emphasizes equality despite differences in scale or apparent stature. From a colonial history perspective, the poem could be interpreted as a subtle assertion of dignity and equality against overwhelming power. It might reflect a quiet resistance or a philosophical consolation, suggesting that even a small nation or individual (like the quail/Korea) shares the same fundamental existence and potential as a larger power (like the Dapeng/Japan), implicitly rejecting inherent inferiority.
