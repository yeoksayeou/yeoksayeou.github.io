Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제9호
Publication Date: 1933년 09월 01일
Article Title: [秋江에 밤이 드니 물결이 자노매라…] ([Night falls on the autumn river, the waves are calm...])
Author: 月山大君 (Wŏlsan Taegun)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

月山大君 (Wŏlsan Taegun)
Night falls on the autumn river (秋江),
The waves are calm.
I cast my fishing line,
The fish do not bite.
Only the indifferent moonlight fills [the boat],
And I return with an empty boat. <31>

**Summary**

This short article presents a classic Korean *sijo* (poem) attributed to Prince Wolsan (Wŏlsan Taegun), a figure from the Joseon Dynasty. The poem depicts a quiet, perhaps melancholic scene of night fishing on an autumn river where the speaker catches no fish and returns with only moonlight filling the boat. For historians, the inclusion of this traditional Korean poem in a magazine published during the Japanese colonial period (1933) is notable. It represents the persistence of Korean cultural heritage and traditional aesthetics (nature, contemplation, subtle emotion) in print media, potentially serving as a quiet assertion of national identity amidst colonial pressures that often sought to suppress or marginalize Korean culture.
