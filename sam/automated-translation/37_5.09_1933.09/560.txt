Misjudgment (誤審)

"Wasn't that woman's husband highly rumored to be a great judge (名判事)?"
"Yes. Everyone believed so until he married that woman."

Silent Insult (無言辱)

"Hey, that house right next to the public toilet over there is mine. Come visit sometime."
"Okay. By the way, which one is the public toilet?"

Han River Bridge (漢江橋)

"Do you know how long the Han River Bridge (漢江鐵橋) is?"
"Yes. It's as long as the width of the Han River (漢江) water."
"And the width of the water?"
"Well, that's as long as the bridge." <61>

**Summary**

This short piece presents three brief jokes or witty exchanges from 1933. The first, "Misjudgment," humorously suggests a man reputed to be a wise judge lost that reputation after marrying a particular woman. The second, "Silent Insult," uses directions involving a public toilet to imply the speaker's house is indistinguishable from it. The third, "Han River Bridge," features circular logic about the bridge's length and the river's width. For historians, these jokes offer a glimpse into the everyday humor and social commentary of colonial Korea, reflecting perhaps on reputation versus reality, living conditions (proximity to public facilities), and simple wordplay common in the period.
