Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제9호
Publication Date: 1933년 09월 01일
Article Title: [내 마음 베혀내여 저 달을 만들과저…] (If I cut out my heart and made that moon...)
Author: 鄭澈
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

鄭澈
My heart I would cut out
And make that moon;
In the vast ninety-thousand *li* sky
I would hang it brightly,
Go to where my fair love resides,
And shine upon them. <96>

**Summary**

This short poem, attributed to the famous Joseon Dynasty poet Chŏng Ch'ŏl (Songgang), expresses an intense longing for a loved one (often interpreted as the king). The speaker wishes to transform their heart into the moon so it can hang high in the sky and constantly watch over and illuminate the place where their beloved is. For historians, the publication of this classic *sijo* in a popular 1930s magazine like *Samch'ŏlli* is interesting. It demonstrates the continued appreciation and dissemination of traditional Korean literature during the Japanese colonial period, potentially reflecting a form of cultural continuity or nationalist sentiment amidst colonial rule.
