A History of Terrifying International Terrorism
						By Mok Pyŏngjŏng

A single bullet fired by a Serbian youth became the fuse for the World War, sacrificing millions of precious lives and turning billions in wealth to ash. Even today, more than a decade later, its repercussions persist, threatening world peace.
This was the largest act of terror, and looking at the major incidents that have occurred continuously throughout world history:

While en route to attend the opening ceremony of the Exhibition of Works by Writers Who Served in the World War, French President Doumer passed away on May 6th of last year at 3 PM, struck by a bullet from the exiled White Russian, Paul Gorguloff. This too was a major incident.

Regardless of East or West, ancient or modern times, the figures who are assassinated are, without exception, great men and heroes. It is, of course, deeply regrettable for the victim, but when a capable figure vital to the nation falls prey to an assassin's blade, the loss to that nation is immense. In the approximately 70 years since the latter half of the 18th century, there have been roughly 40 incidents of assassination of great figures.

First, Lincoln, the emancipator of slaves who achieved a great deed that shines eternally in the history of world humanitarianism, the 16th President of the United States, completed the unification of North and South America. The day after, on the 14th (Friday) of 1865, he was invited to Ford's Theatre, arriving with his party at 9 PM. He sat in a guest box draped with the Stars and Stripes, the national anthem played, and the play began. Just then, the sound of a six-shooter suddenly erupted within the theater. A young man holding a dagger appeared in front of the President's box, shouted, "This is the end of tyrants!" tore the flag, caused the incident, and fled. He was the twenty-eight-year-old actor, Booth.

Turning our gaze again to Russia, Tsar Alexander II, after the failure at the Congress of Berlin, enforced autocratic rule in his later years and gradually lost popularity. Thus, in 1881, he was assassinated by a Jewish person.

A lucky survivor of assassination was the former Emperor of Spain, Alfonso XIII. Despite seven attempts on his life, resulting in deaths and injuries among his attendants, the king himself was never harmed.

A very recent incident occurred the year before last, in December, this also <104> in South America: President Yrigoyen of Argentina was shot at by an Italian anti-fascist. The police chief and driver tragically died, but the president was unharmed.

Finally, considering the changing relationship between assassins and their weapons, political assassinations were frequent in ancient China, but 80 to 90 percent of them were poisonings. It was common to persuade political enemies with flattering words, invite them over, and then assassinate them by putting poison in their food.
Assassinations in the West are almost entirely by pistol or bomb. The Austrian Archduke fell to a Serbian youth's bomb, the Russian Emperor Alexander II vanished from a bullet fired by a Jew, and the 16th President of the United States died from the gunshot fired by a young actor. In short, assassination may be transitioning from the era of poison and daggers, through the pistol era, to the heyday of the bomb. <105>

**Summary**

This 1933 article by "Mok Pyŏngjŏng" surveys major political assassinations, framing them as "international terrorism." It begins with the assassination that sparked WWI and lists notable victims like Paul Doumer, Abraham Lincoln, Alexander II, and others from the US, Russia, France, Germany, and China up to 1933. The author notes that victims are typically "great men" and laments the loss to their nations. The article concludes by observing a historical shift in assassination methods from poison and daggers, prevalent in China, to pistols and bombs, more common in the West, suggesting an evolving "era of the bomb." For historians of colonial Korea, this article is interesting as it discusses "terrorism" (assassination) in an international context, written by a Korean under Japanese rule. The selection of examples (heavy on Western/Russian figures, but including Chinese figures like Zhang Zuolin, killed by the Japanese) and the conspicuous absence of Korean nationalist actions often labeled "terrorism" by Japan (like the recent 1932 actions of Yi Pongch'ang and Yun Ponggil) might reflect censorship, self-censorship, or a strategic focus on less politically sensitive international events.

戰慄할 國際 테로리즘史
						木兵丁
셀비아 一靑年이 발사한 一彈이 世界大戰의 導火線이 되어 數百萬의 귀한 生靈을 희생하고 數百億의 富를 재로 날려 버리어서 10여 년을 경과한 오늘까지도 역시 그 여파가 끈치지 안어 세계평화를 脅威하고 잇다.
이것이 가장 큰 테로로 계속하여 이러난 세계 력사상 가장 큰 사건을 보도하여 보면.
世界大戰 出征作家 作品展覽會의 개회식에 參列途中 불란서의 대통령 즈-멜 氏는 작년 5월 6일 오후 3시 亡命中의 白系露人 폴의 탄환에 逝去하엿다, 이것도 또한 큰 사건이엇다.

洋의 東西와 때의 古今을 뭇지 안코 암살를 당하는 인물들은 예외 업시 偉人傑士들이다, 피살된 當者를 위하야 痛惜에 勿堪함은 물론, 國家有爲의 士가 兇刃에 희생이 되는 것은 그 국가에 대하야 손해가 막대하다, 18세기 후반 약70년간에 偉傑暗殺事件은 대략 40여건에 달한다.
爲先 奴隷解放의 은인으로 世界人道史上 영원히 빗나는 偉業을 이룬 린컨은 米國 16대 대통령으로 南米統一을 완성한 이튼날인 1865년 14일(금요일) 포-드 극장에 초대되어 오후 9시에 일행은 도착되엿다, 星條旗의 帷帳에 싸인 객석에 안저 國歌가 울니고 연극은 시작되엇다, 바로 그때 六穴砲 소리가 돌연히 場內에서 터지자 大統領席압헤 匕首를 든 엇던 청년이 壓制者의 末路는 이러니라 라고 웨치면서 국긔를 찟고 일을 내고 다라낫다, 그는 스물 여덜에 나는 청년 배우 부-스엿다.
다시 露西亞를 바라다보면 露帝아렉기산다-2세가 伯林會議에 失敗한 뒤 晩年專制政治를 강행하여 차츰 人望을 이러버리엇다. 그래서 1881년에 1猶太人 때문에 암살당하엇다.
암살의 幸運者는 西班牙의 前皇帝 알폰조 13세엇다, 前後 7次의 저격에 從者에게는 死傷이 잇섯스나 왕은 한번도 다치지 안엇다,
가장 최근의 일은 재작년 12월 이것도 ＜104＞ 南米의 알젠진 대통령 이리고-엔 박사가 伊太利 反파시스트派에 저격되어 경찰서장과 운전수가 慘死하엿스나 대통령은 무사하엿다.

최후에 剌客과 凶器의 변천관계를 고찰하여보면 古來 中國에는 정치적 암살이 頻頻히 행하엿스나 그 8할 내지 9할은 독살이엇다. 政敵을 巧言令色으로 說服하여 초대하여온 뒤 料理속에 독을 너허 암살하는 경우가 만헛던 것이다.
서양에서의 암살은 거이 전부가 삐스톨이나 폭탄이다, 오스토리아 황태자는 셀비아 청년의 폭탄에 너머젓고, 露國皇帝 아렉산다-2세는 유태인이 쏘는 一彈에 사라젓고 米國16대 대통령은 靑年俳優가 쏜 銃聲에 죽엇다, 요컨대 암살도 毒藥短刃時代로부터 피스톨 시대를 지나 폭탄 전성시대가 될는지 모른다.＜105＞
