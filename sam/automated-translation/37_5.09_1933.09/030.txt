Rejection of Defeatism, Our Creed for Survival from Today Onward

"It would be better if everything just perished!"

People holding such defeatist thoughts have become numerous lately. Even when someone dies, they say, "What more is there to live for? It's good they died." Even when a major flood occurs in some region and thousands suffer disaster, they say, "Only a few thousand were affected? Tens of thousands should have died."

In this way, people who regard perishing as insignificant are increasing. However, when this thought stops there, it is still acceptable. Recently, we see an increase in people taking it a step further, possessing the astonishing thought:

"Let's make everything perish."

When an association (結社) does well, they enter it, create two or three factions, instigate fights, and make it all perish. When public esteem (衆望) gathers around someone, they spread slander (*마를 노아) and undermine that person's foundation (大를 헌다). They obstruct the utmost efforts to form central organizations and central figures and the endeavors to break through the current situation.

This psychology might stem from the idea that "If we perish quickly, we reach the end quickly," but this is a dreadful, evil, and toxic trend that harms societal development. During the Great War in Europe (歐洲大戰), pacifists (非戰主義者) went to the battlefield, deliberately shouldered their rifles backward, and returned. This was the practice of defeatism (敗戰主義). They did this based on their particular philosophy that only by perishing quickly could they reach the end quickly.

However, that argument is not the right path (正道), but a side path (橫道). Being a side path, it might be useful once in a thousand years, once in ten thousand years, but it is not something to be used always. How much less so in today's Korea (朝鮮)? Today is a time for us to gather even a single strand of rotten rope (썩은 색기 한 오래기). It is a time to advance further, even if by only one ten-thousandth of a foot (尺). It is absolutely, absolutely not a time to give up on ourselves and retreat. Moreover, objectively, there are no conditions whatsoever for retreat! A total rejection of this defeatism, the opposite of the principle of progress (前進主義)! This must become our creed for survival from today onward. <13>

**Summary**

The editorial vehemently denounces a growing sentiment of "defeatism" among Koreans, characterized by indifference or even a desire for collective ruin ("It would be better if everything just perished," "Let's make everything perish"). The author criticizes those who actively sabotage successful organizations or individuals out of a twisted belief that hastening collapse is the quickest way to an endpoint, comparing it to pacifists during the Great War who practiced defeatism. The article argues this is a destructive "side path," not the "right path," especially for contemporary Korea, which desperately needs to gather every resource and strive for even minuscule progress. The author concludes by calling for a total rejection of defeatism and the adoption of a "creed for survival" based on the principle of progress.

Historically, this article is significant as it reflects the deep despair and internal conflict within Korean society under Japanese colonial rule in the 1930s. It highlights the psychological burden of oppression leading some towards nihilism and self-destruction, while simultaneously showing a conscious intellectual effort to combat this sentiment. Historians might find the explicit discussion of internal sabotage ("create two or three factions," "spread slander") and the comparison to European wartime defeatism particularly revealing about the complex responses to colonization and the struggle to maintain national morale and cohesion.

敗退主義의 拒否, 今日以後의 우리 生存信條
『모다 亡하엿스면 조켓다!』
이러한 敗退主義的 생각을 가진 사람이 근래에 만허젓다. 누가 죽엇다 하여도 『더 살어 무얼하겟늬. 잘 죽엇지』 어느 지방에 큰 수해가 잇서 수천 명이 재난을 보앗다 하여도 『겨우 수천 명이 당햇서. 수만 명 죽어주지』
이리하야 亡하여감을 대소롭지 안케 역이는 사람이 만허간다, 그러나 이 생각이 여기에 끈처잇슬 때는 아직도 괜찬타, 최근에는 한거름 더 나아가서
『모다 亡하게 만들자』
하는 놀나운 생각을 가진 사람이 늘어감을 본다. 어느 結社가 잘 되면 그 속에 드러가 두 패 세 패로 만들어 싸홈을 부처 모다 亡하게 하고 어느 누구에게 衆望이 모히면 *마를 노아 그 사람의 「大」를 헌다. 중심단체와 중심인물의 조성을 極力헐고 局面의 打開에 노력함을 방해한다.
이 심리는 『速히 亡하면 速히 종점에 닷는다』하는 생각으로 그리함일는지 모르나 이러케 두려운, 사회의 발전을 害하는 악하고도 독한 사조가 업겟다. 歐洲大戰때 非戰主義者들은 戰場에 나갓다가 일부러 총을 꺽구로 메고 도로 退하여 도라왓다. 敗戰主義의 실천이다. 이것은 속히 망하여야만 속히 종점에 닷는다는 그네 一流의 철학으로 그리함이엇다.
그러나 그 주장은 正道는 아니고 橫道다. 橫道인지라, 천년에 한번 만년에 한번은 쓸때가 잇슬지언정 눌 쓸것이 못된다, 하물며 오늘의 조선에랴 오늘의 우리는 썩은 색기 한 오래기라도 더 이어모을 때요. 一尺의 10만 분지 1이라도 더 나아갈 때다. 결코 결코 自抛하여 후퇴할 때가 아니다, 더구나 객관적으로 후퇴할 아모 조건이 업슴에랴 前進主義인 逆인 이 敗退主義에 전면적 거부! 이것이 今日以後의 우리의 생존신조가 되어야 할 것이다. ＜13＞
