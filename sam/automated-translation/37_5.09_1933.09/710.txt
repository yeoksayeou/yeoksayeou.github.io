An Era of Suffering for Beautiful Women

Female aviator Miss Pak Kyŏngwŏn (朴敬元), while on a visiting flight to Manchuria (滿洲) after also visiting her hometown Taegu (大邱), tragically crashed and died near Hakone (箱根) due to fierce winds. Remembering the previous death of Mr. An Ch’angnam (安昌男), we mourn the repeated misfortunes afflicting these "bird people" (鳥人) who fly in the sky.

Speaking of female aviators, it is said that Miss Yi Chŏnghŭi (李貞喜) also recently attempted suicide by poison but failed. It is said she did so out of pessimism about life (人世) due to some sad incident in her relationship with Mr. Yi O-yong (李O鎔), a Doctor of Medicine (醫學搏士) educated in Germany (獨逸) who is now in Shanghai (上海), but the truth or falsehood (眞僞) of this is unknown (未詳). <76>

**Summary**

This news article reports on the recent misfortunes of two prominent Korean women. The pioneering female aviator Pak Kyŏngwŏn died tragically in a plane crash near Hakone, Japan, while flying towards Manchuria. This event is linked to the earlier death of aviator An Ch’angnam. Additionally, another female aviator, Yi Chŏnghŭi, reportedly attempted suicide due to relationship problems with a German-educated doctor residing in Shanghai, although the report's veracity is uncertain. From a historical perspective, the article highlights the emergence and tragic struggles of early Korean female aviators during the colonial period, notable figures operating on an international stage (Japan, Manchuria, Shanghai, Germany), and the societal attention given to their public and private lives.
