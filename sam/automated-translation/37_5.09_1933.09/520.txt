Success of "Turgenev's Night"

As August 22nd marked the 50th death anniversary of the great Russian writer (露西亞文豪) Turgenev (「투르게넵흐」), various literary figures (文人諸氏) present in Sŏul gathered on this day at Nangnang P'ara (樂浪팔라) in Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町), reminiscing about the deceased old master (故翁) in various ways and spending the time in great success (盛況). Previously, there was "Goethe's Night" (「꾀-터의 밤」), and now "Turgenev's Night" was held; it must be called a beautiful thing. <54>

**Summary**

This short article reports on a successful gathering ("Turgenev's Night") held by Seoul's literary figures on August 22, 1933, at Nangnang P'ara in Hasegawa-chō to commemorate the 50th anniversary of Russian author Ivan Turgenev's death. The event is mentioned alongside a previous similar gathering for Goethe, portraying such cultural commemorations positively. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the cultural activities of Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, showing their engagement with world literature (specifically Russian and German) despite the political situation. The location name "Hasegawa-chō" (a Japanese name for a district in Seoul, likely present-day Sogong-dong area) and the mention of Nangnang P'ara also provide context about the urban landscape and cultural venues of colonial Seoul.

『투르게넵흐의 밤』盛況
露西亞文豪 「투르게넵흐」의 50年祭가 8월 22일임으로 이날 서울 잇는 文人諸氏는 長谷川町 樂浪팔라에 모히어, 여러 가지로 故翁을 추억하며 盛況으로 지내엿다 한다. 前에 「꾀-터의 밤」이 잇섯고, 이제 투르게넵흐의 밤이 열니엇다, 아름다운 일이라 할 것이다.＜54＞
