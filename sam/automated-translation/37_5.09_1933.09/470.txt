Article Title:	鴨綠江畔에서 (On the Banks of the Yalu River)

鴨綠江畔에서
						石松

늘힌메 나린 물이
오리강 되엿서라.
이천리 멀고 먼 길
꾸준히 흘너나려
사천 년 이 겨레의
새 넉슬 북도드네.
내 사랑 내 사랑
오리강 내 사랑.

뗏목에 실닌 노래
무엇을 말하느냐.
지금은 오리강이
조선의 끗이라나
아득한 저 녯날엔
여기가 복판일세.
내 사랑 내 사랑
오리강 내 사랑.

개화 후 이 강 건너
도난이 몃 만명가.
그들이 뿌린 눈물
네 품에 고엿스니
오리강 너 혼자서
속사정 알지안늬
내 사랑 내 사랑
오리강 내 사랑. ＜48＞

On the Banks of the Ori River
						Sŏk Song

The water descended from the long mountain ranges
Has become the Ori River (鴨綠江 - Amnokkang/Yalu River).
Down the long, distant path of two thousand *li*
Steadily it flows,
Inspiring the new spirit
Of this people of four thousand years.
My love, my love,
Ori River, my love.

The song carried on the raft,
What does it speak of?
They say the Ori River now
Is the edge of Chosŏn,
But in those distant old days,
This place was the center.
My love, my love,
Ori River, my love.

After the Enlightenment (開化), crossing this river,
How many tens of thousands fled (渡難)?
The tears they shed
Have pooled in your embrace,
Ori River, you alone
Know their inner circumstances, don't you?
My love, my love,
Ori River, my love. <48>

**Summary**

This poem by Sŏk Song personifies the Yalu River (referred to as "Ori River") as a constant witness to Korean history. It flows through two thousand *li*, embodying the resilience and spirit of the Korean people over four thousand years. The poem contrasts the river's past significance as a central part of the nation ("복판") with its present status as a border ("끗"), a poignant reference to lost territory or influence. It concludes by lamenting the countless Koreans who fled across the river ("도난") after the 'Enlightenment' (often signifying the period of forced opening and subsequent colonization), suggesting the river holds the sorrowful 'inner circumstances' of these displaced people. For historians, the poem offers insight into the national consciousness during the colonial period, reflecting on historical continuity, the pain of division and border imposition, the memory of a larger historical territory, and the suffering associated with migration and exile forced by political changes. The term "도난" (crossing in hardship/fleeing) is particularly evocative of the refugee experience.
