Collection of Floating Words

The 6-pyeong vacant lot between Hwasin Department Store (和信 百貨店) and Taech'ang Trading (大昌貿易) at Chongno Intersection in Sŏul greatly harms the scenery of the Great Kyŏngsŏng's bustling area, like a missing tooth. The owner is a certain Japanese person (某 日人) who reportedly asked for 3,000 won per pyeong (每平), so Hwasin and Taech'ang bargained but eventually gave up.

In Sŏul, the halls where artists—including writers (문인), painters (화가), and musicians (음악가)—gather most are Naknang (樂浪) in Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町) and "Chebi" (「제비」) in Chongno I-chōme (鍾路 2丁目). ＜46＞

**Summary**

This short piece collects two anecdotes about Sŏul (then Kyŏngsŏng) in 1933. The first describes a vacant lot on the prime Chongno Intersection, owned by a Japanese individual demanding a high price, highlighting urban development issues and potential ethnic tensions over land. The second lists popular gathering spots for the city's artists, specifically mentioning the Naknang tearoom/cafe in Hasegawa-chō and the "Chebi" cafe in Chongno I-chōme. For historians, the article offers glimpses into the economic landscape (high land prices, Japanese ownership in prime locations) and the cultural scene (specific venues frequented by Korean artists) of colonial Sŏul. The mention of the Japanese landowner and the specific price demanded provides concrete detail about economic conditions and Japanese influence in the capital.

떠다니는 말 集
서울 종로 네 거리의 和信 百貨店과 大昌貿易 사이에 잇는 6평의 空地는 大京城 繁華處에 마치 이(齒)가 빠진 듯 풍경을 害함이 만타, 소유자는 某 日人인데 每平 3천원을 달나고 하여서 和信과 大昌서 흥정하다가 그만두엇다고 한다.

서울서 문인과 화가와 음악가들 통트러 예술가들이 만히 모히는 홀은 長谷川町의 樂浪과 鍾路 2丁目의 「제비」. ＜46＞
