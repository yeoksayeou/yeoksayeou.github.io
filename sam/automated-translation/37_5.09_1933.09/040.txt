Beauty
						Yi Kwangsu

I discuss beauty in two ways. One is looking at it comprehensively, and the other is looking at it analytically.
That is to say, concerning beauty viewed comprehensively, one could list:
Physical Beauty (體格美)
Beauty of Movement (動作美)
Beauty of Appearance (容貌美)
Beauty of Voice (音聲美)
Beauty of Expression (表情美)
Beauty of Taste (趣味美)
Beauty of Spirit (精神美)
And concerning beauty viewed analytically, one could list things like the shape of the eyes, the shape of the ears, the shape of the nose, the mouth, teeth, and skin tone.

If the physique, the hands and feet, the four limbs, and the torso seem measured as if by a ruler, fitting squarely without being too long or too short; if all movements like posture and walking are graceful and do not appear the least bit offensive to others' eyes; if the voice is gentle, clear, yet soft; if expressions during times of sadness, joy, or distress are innocent and free; and furthermore, if taste and mental attitude are also refined—if there were such a person, she would indeed be the beauty I seek.

Alas! In enumerating so many conditions, I seem to have missed the crucial ones.
A face with a roundish oval contour, eyes that are exceedingly large and seemingly moist, a nose and ears that are attractively ample, and fair skin.
A personality that is cheerful and modest, and moreover, a woman of passionate fervor, full of compassion and quick to sympathize with others' affairs.
Are there too many conditions? However, shouldn't one meet at least this much to be called a beauty? <14>

**Summary**

Yi Kwangsu outlines his definition of female beauty (*miin*), dividing it into comprehensive aspects (physique, movement, appearance, voice, expression, taste, spirit) and analytical aspects (specific facial features, skin). He describes his ideal beauty as possessing balanced physical proportions, graceful movement, a pleasant voice, natural expressions, refined taste, noble spirit, a roundish-oval face, large eyes, shapely features, fair skin, a cheerful yet modest personality, passion, and compassion. For historians, this article offers insight into the aesthetic ideals held by a prominent Korean male intellectual during the colonial period. It reflects contemporary discussions on modernity, tradition, and gender roles, showcasing how physical appearance was intertwined with desired personality traits like modesty, passion, and compassion within the cultural context of 1930s Korea.

美人
						李光洙
나는 미인을 두 가지로 논아서 봄니다. 종합적으로 보는 것과 분석적으로 보는 것이 그것이외다.
즉 종합적으로 보는 美로 말하면
體格美
動作美
容貌美
音聲美
表情美
趣味美
精神美
等을 들 수 잇겟고 그리고 분석적으로 보는 美로 말하면 눈 생긴 것, 귀 생긴 것, 코 생긴 것, 입, 잇빨과 살빗가튼 것을 들 수 잇겟슴니다.

체격이, 수족이나 四肢나 胴體가 자로 재인 듯, 네 귀가 길지도 짜르지도 안게 바로 맛고, 몸 쓰는 것, 것는 것 등의 모든 동작이 날신하여 남의 눈에 조곰치나 거슬니게 뵈지 안코 그 말소리가 사근사근하고 시언하면서 부드럽고, 슬푼 때 깁분 때, 괴로운 때 등의 그 표정이 천진스럽고도 자유스럽고 그리고 또 취미와 그 정신 가짐이 아울너 고상하다면-그런 분이 잇다면 그야말노 내가 찻는 미인이 될 것임니다.

앗뿔사 너무 여러 조건을 주서 섬기느라고 주요한 조건을 노처버렷구려.
얼골은 둥글둥글한 타원형의 윤곽에다가 눈은 어듸까지든지 크고 저진 듯하며 코나 귀가 복성스럽게 엡부고 살결이 하얀 분.
그 성격은 쾌활하고 얌전하며, 더구나 뜨거운 정열적의 여성, 자비심이 만허 남의 일에 동정 잘 하는 분.
너무 조건이 만흘가, 그러나 이만하여야 미인이라 하지 안을가. ＜14＞
