Actors’ Monthly Salary: Two Thousand Won

In Chosŏn (朝鮮), even famous actors like Na Un-gyu (羅雲奎), Kim Yŏn-sil (金蓮實), Sŏk Kwa-bul (石臥佛), and Chŏn Kyŏng-hŭi (全景希) earn only around 2 Won on days when box office results are good, and on bad days, their income is said to be merely 70 or 80 *chŏn*. The misery of their lives is unspeakable, but in contrast, the times are truly good for Japanese actors.

At Nikkatsu (日活), Kataoka Chiezō's (片岡千惠藏) monthly salary is 2,000 Won, Ōkōchi Denjirō (大河內傳次郞) earns 1,500 Won, and Suzuki Denmei (鈴木傳明) and Takada Minoru (高田稔) earned 1,500 Won when they were with Funi Eiga (不二映畵). Okada Tokihiko (岡田時彦) earns 1,000 Won. At Shochiku (松竹), Hayashi Chōjirō (林長二郞) earns 1,200 Won. At Kamata (蒲田), Ōhinata Den (大日方傳) earns 220 Won. At Nikkatsu, Sawada Kiyoshi (澤田淸) earns 280 Won, and Tamura Dōbi (田村道美) earns 200 Won.

The above concerns male actors. Now, looking at female actors: at Shin-kō (新興), Irie Takako (入江たか子) earns 1,000 Won, Kurishima Sumiko (栗島すみ子) 800 Won, Suzuki Sumiko (鈴木澄子) 400 Won. Takatsu Keiko (高津慶子), who became a freelancer (후리-란사), earns 500 Won. Mizuhara Reiko (水原玲子) from "Waitress" (女給) earns 200 Won.

Moving to Nikkatsu, Natsukawa Shizue (夏川靜江) earns 600 Won, Date Satoko (伊達里子) 300 Won, Ichikawa Haruyo (市川春代) 100 Won, and Mine Ginko (峯吟子) 180 Won. At Kamata, Okada Yoshiko (岡田嘉子) and Sawa Ranko (澤蘭子) earn 200 Won each, Tanaka Kinuyo (田中絹代) 400 Won, Oikawa Michiko (及川道子) 200 Won, Kawasaki Yayoko (川崎彌子) 150 Won, Fushimi Nobuko (伏見信子) 200 Won, and so on.

Gentlemen actors of Chosŏn, what are your thoughts? <59>

**Summary**

This 1933 article highlights the vast disparity in monthly salaries between Korean (Chosŏn) and Japanese film actors. While top Korean actors struggled to earn even 2 Won (Korean Yen) per day, dependent on fluctuating box office success, leading Japanese actors commanded monthly salaries ranging from several hundred to as much as 2,000 Won. The piece lists specific salaries for numerous popular Japanese male and female actors across major studios like Nikkatsu, Shochiku, and Shin-kō Kinema, starkly contrasting their financial stability with the "miserable" situation of their Korean counterparts. From a historical perspective, the article vividly illustrates the economic stratification within the Japanese empire's film industry during the colonial period, reflecting broader colonial economic disparities and the marginal status of Korean cultural producers compared to their Japanese contemporaries. Historians might find the specific salary figures and the direct comparison valuable for understanding labor conditions, cultural economics, and colonial dynamics in 1930s Korea.

俳優 月給 二千圓
朝鮮서는 名優 羅雲奎, 金蓮實, 石臥佛, 全景希 가튼 이조차 그 수입이 흥행 성적이 조흔 날에야 2圓 內外, 그도 不良한 날이면 7, 80錢의 수입 밧게 아니된다 하니 그 생활의 비참은 말할 수 업지만 이와 반대로 일본 배우들의 歲月은 참말 조타.
日活의 片岡千惠藏의 월급이 2,000원, 大河內傳次郞이 1,500원, 그리고, 鈴木傳明과 高田稔이 不二映畵때에 1,500원, 岡田時彦이 1,000원, 松竹의 林長二郞이 1,200원, 蒲田의 大日方傳이 220원, 日活의 澤田淸이 280원, 田村道美가 200원.
이상은 男俳優편 이거니와, 이제 女俳優들을 보면 신흥의 入江たか子가 1,000원, 栗島すみ子가 800원, 鈴木澄子가 400원, 후리-란사가 된 高津慶子가 500원「女給」의 水原玲子가 200원이다.
日活에 가서 夏川靜江이가 600원, 伊達里子가 300원 市川春代가 100원 峯吟子가 180원이요, 蒲田의 岡田嘉子와 澤蘭子가 200원, 田中絹代의 400원, 及 川道子 200원, 川崎彌子가 150원 伏見信子가 200원 등등이다.
朝鮮俳優諸公, 所懷如何인고. ＜59＞
