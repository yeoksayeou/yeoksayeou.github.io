Samch'ŏlli Wall Newspaper
This and That
Mr. Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), who is in America (米國), keeps saying he'll come back but has not yet returned to the country. Meanwhile, his mother's 60th birthday celebration (回甲宴) was held recently at his home in Yejidong (禮智洞), and many dignitaries (貴紳) reportedly gathered as guests. Of the three sons, the eldest and youngest have passed away, and the second son, Mr. Tŏksu, has gone to America, leaving no children under her care (슬하에 자녀라고 업는 터에). Guests that day were reportedly all puzzled as to how such a grand feast (盛宴) could be held. It is heard that Mr. Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) helped with the expenses. In this world, thin as a sheet of paper (백지장 가치 열분 世上), this can be called a beautiful story of human kindness (可謂人情美談).

Regarding those who went away for summer vacation (피서갓든 이를 들건대): Ch'ŏndogyo's (천도교) Messrs. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Chŏng Manjo (鄭萬朝), and Yi Tonhwa (李敦化) went together to Pongson Temple (奉先寺) in Yangju (楊州) and stayed for about two weeks. Yonhŭi Professional School (延專) Professor Chŏng Inbo (鄭寅普) toured the Eight Scenic Views of Yŏngdong (嶺東八景) in Kangwŏn Province (江原道), like the *Chosŏn Ilbo's* (朝鮮日報) "Haesan紀行" (Coastal Mountain Travelogue). Ewha Girls' High School (梨花女高) teacher Kim Ch'angje (金昶濟) climbed Paektu Mountain (白頭山) with the Musan (茂山) garrison. Dr. Chŏng In'gwa (鄭仁果) of Christianity (기독교) spent the summer (消夏) at Kŭmgang Mountain (金剛山) as a Bible study meeting (査經會) happened to be held at Onjŏngni (溫井里). Mr. Hŏ Hŏn (許憲) toured the Northern Provinces (北道) via Sambang (三防). Messrs. Song Chinu (宋鎭禹), Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), and Yang Wŏnmo (梁源模) traveled around various places enjoying the cool breeze (凉風마지며), doubling up their travels with branch manager meetings (支局長會議). Mr. Sin Hŭngu (申興雨) took an ocean voyage to attend a conference in the land of eternal summer <32>, Manila (「마닐라」).

Legacy of Literary Figures (文士의 遺産)
In Korea (朝鮮), there hasn't yet been a great literary master (文豪) who has died, so the interesting question of how much legacy they leave behind hasn't arisen. However, great Western literary masters often leave behind large sums of money when they die.
First, the famous Irish novelist George Moore ("쪼-지무-어"), who died recently at the advanced age of 80, left behind 750,000 won (75만원). Dr. Benson (벤손) of England (英國) left 1,110,000 won (111만원), Mr. Eimann (에이만) 1,000,000 won (100만원), Igglesden (잇겟쓰) 800,000 won (80만원), Garvice (가비스) 710,000 won (71만원), Dorothy Storr (도로로-뿌) 700,000 won (70만원), Haggard (밧가-드) 610,000 won (61만원), and Conrad (컨랏드) 200,000 won (20만원).
However, even more than these, the great French literary master Victor Hugo (삑도아, 유-고) had a legacy of 3,000,000 won (300만원). (Dispatch from London (倫敦 發信))

11,000 Killed or Wounded in Battle (Casualties Since the Manchurian Incident) (戰死傷 1萬 1千(滿洲事變以來 被害))
On May 31st last, the Japan-China (日中) Armistice Agreement was formally established, ending the flames of war (戰火) after 1 year and 9 months since the outbreak (勃發) of the Manchurian Incident (滿州事變) on September 18, Showa 6 (1931). During that period, the total number of killed and wounded on the Japanese military side (日本軍側) due to the conflict was as follows (according to Army Ministry (육군성) investigation).
Battle Outside the Great Wall (長城外戰): Killed in Action 158, Wounded in Action 733
Rehe Pacification Campaign (熱河討伐戰): Killed in Action 309, Wounded in Action 1,114
Manchuria-Shanghai Battles (滿州上海戰): Killed in Action 2,430, Wounded in Action 6,536
Total Killed in Action (戰死合計): 2,897
Total Wounded in Action (戰傷合計): 8,483
Total Killed and Wounded (戰死傷 總計): 11,380 <33>

Recent Personnel News (最近의 人事異聞)
Musician An Kiyŏng (安基永) and Miss Kim Hyŏnsun (金顯順), who went on a love journey (戀愛行脚) to Harbin (哈爾賓), are said to have recently gone to Shanghai (上海). Mr. Kim Ŭrhan (金乙漢) married the granddaughter of Count Min (閔伯爵) on July 15th. Mr. Kim Tongin (金東仁) has left the Arts and Culture Department (學藝部) of the *Chosŏn Ilbo* (朝鮮日報). Painter An Sŏgyŏng (安夕影), after leaving the newspaper company, is busy wielding his brush (畵筆) at home. Kim Wŏnju (金源珠), who went to Shanghai, seems likely to go to Nanjing (南京) recently. Sin Ilsŏn (申一仙) is expected to easily return to the film world soon, which should make Miss Sin's fans (팬) happy. <34>

**Summary**

This "Wall Newspaper" section compiles various news tidbits from September 1933. It covers social events like a prominent figure's mother's 60th birthday party (funded by Kim Sŏngsu), summer vacation travels of notable intellectuals and religious figures (Ch'oe Rin, Chŏng Inbo, Yi Kwangsu, etc.) to places like Yangju, Kŭmgang Mountain, and even Manila. It contrasts the lack of wealthy literary legacies in Korea with large sums left by Western authors like George Moore and Victor Hugo. Starkly, it also reports official Japanese Army statistics on casualties (11,380 killed and wounded) from the recently concluded conflicts related to the Manchurian Incident. Finally, it shares personal updates on cultural figures: musicians An Kiyŏng and Kim Hyŏnsun moving to Shanghai, Kim Ŭrhan's marriage, Kim Tongin leaving the Chosŏn Ilbo, painter An Sŏgyŏng's focus on art, Kim Wŏnju's potential move to Nanjing, and actress Sin Ilsŏn's anticipated return to film.

Historians might find this article interesting for several reasons. It provides a snapshot of the social life and movements of the Korean elite (intellectuals, cultural figures, religious leaders) during the colonial period, indicating a degree of normalcy and continued activity despite the political situation. The mention of specific travels and affiliations (Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, Yonhŭi, Ewha, Chosŏn Ilbo) maps out networks and interests. Crucially, the inclusion of Japanese military casualty figures from the Manchurian Incident, reported via the Japanese Army Ministry, offers insight into the flow of official information regarding colonial expansion and its costs within Korea at the time. The gossip column format itself reflects a particular style of journalism prevalent then.

三千里 壁新聞
이일 저일
米國 가 잇는 張德秀 氏는 온다온다 하면서 아직 귀국치 안엇다, 그런데 張氏母堂의 回甲宴이 얼마 전에 禮智洞 그의 집에서 열니엇는데 하객으로 貴紳들이 만이 모엿더라고. 아들 3형제 중 마지와 셋재는 저 세상에 보내고 둘재 德秀氏는 米國에 去하엿기 슬하에 자녀라고 업는 터에 엇더케 저러틋 盛宴을 베풀엇는고 하고 당일 하객들이 모다 의아하엿다 한다, 듯건대 金性洙 氏가 비용을 도왓다든가 백지장 가치 열분 世上에 可謂人情美談.

여름에 피서갓든 이를 들건대 천도교 崔麟, 鄭萬朝, 李敦化 3氏가 함께 楊州 奉先寺에 가서 약 2주일 체류하엿고 延專敎授 鄭寅普 氏는 朝鮮日報의 海山紀行과 갓치 江原道 嶺東八景을 週遊하여 왓고 梨花女高敎諭 金昶濟 氏는 茂山守備隊와 갓치 白頭山에 올넛고 기독교의 鄭仁果博士는 마츰 査經會가 溫井里에 잇서 金剛山에서 消夏하엿고 許憲 氏는 三防을 거처 北道를 週遊하엿고 宋鎭禹, 李光洙, 梁源模의 3氏는 支局長會議를 겸사겸사 하여 각지로 凉風마지며 도라다녓고 申興雨 氏는 常夏의 나라 ＜32＞ 「마닐라」로 회의 출석차 해양여행을 하엿다.

文士의 遺産
朝鮮에는 아직까지 큰 文豪로 죽은 사람이 업서 그 유산이 얼마나 되는지?하는 흥미이르킬 문제도 업지만은 서양의 큰 文豪들은 대개 거액의 돈을 끼처두고 죽는 일이 만타.
爲先 얼마 전에 80세의 고령으로 죽은 愛蘭의 유명한 소설가「쪼-지무-어」는 75만원을 끼처 두엇섯고, 英國의 벤손 박사는 111만원, 에이만 氏는 100만원, 잇겟쓰는 80만원, 가비스는 71만원, 도로로-뿌 70만원, 밧가-드 61만원, 컨랏드 20만원.
그러치만 이 보다도 더 만흔 이에 佛蘭西의 大文豪 삑도아, 유-고에 300만원의 유산이 잇섯다.  (倫敦 發信)

戰死傷 1萬 1千(滿洲事變以來 被害)
去 5월 31일 日中停戰協定이 정식으로 성립되여 昭和 6년 9월 18일  滿州事變 勃發以來 1년 9개월만에 戰火를 거치게 되엇는데 그 사이의 전란 때문에 日本軍側의 全戰死傷者 數는 如左하엿다.(육군성 조사)

最近의 人事異聞
哈爾賓으로 戀愛行脚한 음악가 安基永 氏와 金顯順孃은 최근 上海로 갓다 하며 金乙漢君은 7월 15일 閔伯爵의 손녀와 결혼하엿고 金東仁 氏는 朝鮮日報 學藝部를 그만두엇고 화가 安夕影 氏는 신문사를 나온 뒤 집에서 畵筆 두루기에 분주하며 상해에 간 金源珠 氏는 최근 南京으로 갈 듯 하다하며 申一仙은 다시 영화계로 쉬 출마하리라는데 이리되면 申女史 팬들이 깁버하리라고. ＜34＞
