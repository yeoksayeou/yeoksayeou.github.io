Novel Titles
						Yi Chongmyŏng

The late To Hyang (稻香) is said to have thought of his novel titles only after finishing the writing. However, for some reason, I cannot pick up my pen unless I decide on the title first. While this could simply be called the writer's personal habit, viewed another way, I wonder if this doesn't also reveal an aspect of the writer's style (作風). Before writing a novel, only after the conception is complete—for instance, only after I have mentally rehearsed detailed dialogues or descriptions of nature in certain parts multiple times and gained complete confidence—can I possibly pick up the pen. I believe this is not just me, but something naturally done by any writer with a slightly meticulous tendency.

What determines the value of a work is its content; the title certainly doesn't dictate it, but still, if it's going to be the same price, one wants to attach a good and interesting one. In a way, the title might be considered not just an extension of the content, but a part of it.

Already 7 or 8 years ago, when I first wrote a novel and sent it to a magazine, the editor arbitrarily changed the novel's title before publishing it. At that time, I felt quite unpleasant, as if my work had been insulted. A few years after that, when I wrote a novel titled "A Small Revenge" (조고만 復讐) for a magazine called *Chosŏn Munye* (朝鮮文藝), it happened that Mr. Yŏm Sangsŏp was also writing a serialized novel with the same title in that issue. The editor inquired if it wouldn't be better to change my novel's title to "A Small Joy" (조고만 喜悅), as having two novels with the same title in the same issue wasn't very interesting. Since he was a great master (大家) of the literary world, and moreover, his was a serialized novel, I reluctantly conceded, like crying while eating mustard, but I thought such policies of these "journalists" (쩌너리스트) were quite unpleasant. <21>

**Summary**

The author, Yi Chongmyŏng, discusses his habit of needing a novel title before starting to write, contrasting it with another writer, To Hyang. He believes the title, while not determining a work's value, is an integral part and should be chosen carefully. He recounts two unpleasant experiences where editors changed his chosen titles, once arbitrarily and once due to a conflict with a more established author, Yŏm Sangsŏp, causing him frustration with editorial practices he terms "journalist policies." For historians, the article offers insight into the working relationship between writers and editors in colonial Korea, the perceived hierarchy within the literary scene (respect shown to Yŏm Sangsŏp), and a writer's sensitivity towards the integrity of their work, including the title, under the constraints of magazine publication. The use of the loanword "쩌너리스트" (journalist) reflects contemporary linguistic influences.

小說表題
						李鍾鳴
죽은 稻香은 소설을 다 쓴 후에야 제목을 생각햇다고 한다. 그러나 나는 먼저 제목을 결정하지 안코는 무슨 까닭인지 붓을 들 수가 업다. 이런 것은 요컨대 그 작가 개인의 관습이라고 하겟지만 또 한편으로 생각하면 이런 것들도 그 작가의 作風의 일단을 보혀주는 것이 아닌가 한다. 나는 소설을 쓰기 전에 먼저 구상이 완성된 후에야-가령 말하면 어느 부분에 세세한 대화라든저 자연묘사 갓흔 것을 멧 번이나 머리 속에서 반복하야 완전한 자신이 생기기 전에는 도저히 붓을 들지 못한다. 이것은 나뿐 아니라 누구든지 조금 치밀한 경향을 가진 작가로서는 의당 한 일이라고 밋는다.

한 개의 작품의 가치를 결정하는 것은 그 내용이지 결코 제목이 좌우하는 것이 아니겟지만 그래도 갓흔 갑시면 조흔 것으로 자미잇게 부치고 십다고 생각한다. 제목도 엇떠케 보면 일종 내용의 연장-이라느니 보다도 일부분이 아닌가한다.

벌서 7, 8년전 내가 처음으로 소설을 써서 어느 잡지사에 보냇드니 편집자가 임의로 그 소설의 제목을 곤처서 낸 일이 잇섯다. 그때 나는 자기의 작품이 모욕을 밧은 것 갓해서 퍽 불쾌하게 생각햇다. 그 뒤 멧해 만에 「朝鮮文藝」하는 잡지에다 「조고만 復讐」라는 소설을 썻드니 그 號에 마침 廉想涉氏도 갓흔 제목의 연재소설을 쓰게되야 편집자로부터 갓흔 호에 갓흔 제목의 소설이 잇는 것은 자미 적으니 나의 소설을 「조고만 喜悅」이라고 곤치면 읏떠켓느냐고 문의가 왓다. 한편은 문단의 大家요 더욱이 연재소설이니 나로서는 울면서 겨자 먹기로 양보는 햇지만 이런 「쩌너리스트」들의 정책은 여간 불쾌한 일이 아니라고 생각햇다.＜21＞
