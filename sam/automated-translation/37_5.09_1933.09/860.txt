**Wife Unnecessary**

Kim: "So, I heard you got married."
Yi: "Yes, I took a wife."
Kim: "Well, is the bride good at cooking (作食)?"
Yi: "No, she's hopeless at cooking."
Kim: "Then, is she good at needlework (針繕)?"
Yi: "No, she can't sew either. But her voice is really lovely."
Kim: "Is that all? Just her voice?"
Yi: "Yes. You could say I married her because her voice is lovely."
Kim: "Tsk, my friend, you made a mistake. If it's just her voice, you should have just bought a nightingale instead. Compared to a wife, don't you know how much more money a nightingale costs!"

**Life-Size**

Customer who came to the photo studio: "I heard that at your place, you can enlarge any photograph to be just like the real thing?"
Owner: "Yes, if you just pay the fee?"
Customer: "I'll pay whatever the fee is. Then, please print me one photo of Paektu Mountain (白頭山) in the actual size of Paektu Mountain."<97>

**The Newspaper's Fault**

Teacher: "It's truly exasperating. No matter how much I teach him, your son Pok-nam insists that Napoleon is still alive. Where could such a baffling thing come from?"
Student's father: "Huh, is that so? Well, anyway, in our house, we just don't read newspapers these days."

**Dilemma**

Successful Man: "If you want to rise in the world and achieve success, there are two things you must definitely keep in mind."
Youth: "Yes. And what are those two things to keep in mind?"
Successful Man: "If you've made a promise to someone, never break it."
Youth: "And the other one?"
Successful Man: "Never make promises carelessly to others in the first place."

**Joint Responsibility**

Pak: "Hey, didn't we say that whatever happens within our Trading Company (商會) is the joint responsibility of you and me?"
Yi: "Of course, since it's a joint venture, we certainly share joint responsibility."
Pak: "I was relieved to hear that. You know that female office worker, Suk-hŭi? Well, she's gotten pregnant, and it seems to be the product of our joint responsibility (共同責任物)." <98>
<97-98>

**Summary**

This article presents five short jokes typical of the humor sections in magazines of the era. The jokes touch upon marital expectations (focusing on practical skills vs. superficial qualities), literal interpretations leading to absurdity (the life-size photo), blaming external factors like media for ignorance, paradoxical advice on success (keeping promises by not making them), and workplace scandals framed with business jargon (joint responsibility). From a historical perspective, the jokes offer glimpses into social norms and anxieties of 1930s Korea under Japanese rule. They reflect attitudes towards marriage, the novelty of photography, awareness (or lack thereof) of world figures, business ethics, and potentially changing gender dynamics or power imbalances in the workplace.

笑話
帶妻必要無
金 「결혼하엿다데 그러.」
李 「응, 장가 드럿네.」
金 「그래, 신부는 作食을 잘 하나?」
李 「아니야, 作食은 틀렷서.」
金 「그럼, 針繕을 잘 하나.」
李 「아니 바느질도 못하네, 그러치만 목소리가 정말 조타네.」
金 「겨우 그 뿐이야, 목소리 뿐니야.」
李 「응. 이를터이면 목소리가 조와서 결혼하엿네.」
金 「에익, 여보게, 잘못 하엿네, 목소리뿐이라면 차라리 꾀꼬리 한 마리 사오지, 녀편네와 꾀고리를 비기면 꾀꼬리가 엇더케나 돈이 만히 드는 줄 모르나!」

實物大
사진관에 차저온 客 「당신 집에서는 무엇이든지 실물과 가치 사진을 크게 널필 수 잇다구요.」
主人 「네, 요금만 내시면?」
客 「요금은 얼마든지 내지요. 그러면 白頭山 寫眞을 白頭山 實物大로 한 장 박여 주서요.」＜97＞

新聞의 罪
선생 「참 성화가 낫슴니다, 당신 집 아들 福男이는 아모리 가르처도 나파륜이 사라 잇다고 하니 이런 긔막힌 일이 어듸잇서요.」
학생의 아버지 「허, 그럿슴니까, 아뭇튼 우리집에서는 요지간 신문이라고 아니 보니까요.」

兩難
成功者 「立身出世를 하려면 두 가지 일을 꼭 명심하여야 하는 법이야.」
靑年 「그래. 명심하여야 할 두 가지 일이라 함은?」
成功者 「남과 약속하엿거든 결코 어기지 말 일.」
靑年 「또 한가지는?」
成功者 「절대로 타인과 약속을 함부로 하여 두지 말난 말이야.」

共同責任
朴 「여보게, 우리 商會 內에서 이러난 일은 무엇이든지 자네와 나의 둘의 공동책임이라고 하엿지.」
李 「물론이야, 공동사업이니까 서로 공동책임이고 말고.」
朴 「그걸 듯고 안심하엿네, 저 여 사무원 淑姬말이야, 애히를 배엇는데 우리 둘 共同責任物인 모양이야.」 ＜98＞
