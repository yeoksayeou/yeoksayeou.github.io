Tram and Woman, Summer Musings
						Kim Tongin

There is one empty seat on the tram. At a certain stop, a female student boarded the tram. Since there was an empty seat, I naturally thought she would go and sit there.

The female student went towards the empty seat. I naturally thought she would sit. But contrary to this humble self's (小生) expectation, the female student stood firmly right in front of the empty seat.

The tram sped along through several stops. Despite there being a seat, the female student remained standing, swaying.

More people boarded the tram. It gradually approached being full. Even as the inside of the tram became increasingly cramped, the female student still stood firmly in front of the empty seat.

If she didn't want to sit in that seat herself, it would be common sense, in this humble self's (小生) opinion, to step aside and yield the seat to someone else, yet this female student neither sat nor moved aside, occupying the space for two people while standing there swaying with a cheerful expression.

If she wasn't going to sit, why on earth did she bother seeking out the front of the empty seat?

At this point, this humble self (小生) lamented—
"Is the reason heaven created women in this world so that men, in their idle moments, might have the amusement of studying and observing them, and trying to solve this bizarre riddle?" <17>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Tongin, recounts observing a female student on a crowded tram who stands directly in front of an empty seat without sitting down or moving to let someone else sit. He finds her behavior perplexing and concludes with a musing that perhaps women were created by heaven as a complex riddle for men to ponder and study during their leisure time. This brief essay captures a mundane scene from 1930s Seoul urban life, specifically interaction on public transport. For historians, it offers a glimpse into the social etiquette (or perceived lack thereof) and gender attitudes of the period, reflecting a somewhat patronizing male perspective on female behavior common at the time. It also showcases the *sup'il* (essay) form popular among intellectuals for personal reflection.

						金東仁
전차에 한 사람 분의 뷔인 자리가 잇다. 어떤 정류장에서 녀학생 하나이 전차에 올랏다. 뷔인 자리가 잇는지라. 무론 거기 가서 안즈려니 하엿다.
녀학생은 그 뷔인 자리의 아프로 갓다. 무론 안즈려니 하엿다. 그랫더니 이 小生의 예긔에 반하여 녀학생은 뷔인 자리 아페 딱 버틔고 서고 말엇다.
몃 정류장을 전차는 그냥 다라낫다. 자리가 잇슴에도 불구하고 녀학생은 여전히 서서 비츨거리고 잇다.
전차에 사람이 더 올랏다. 차차 만원에 가까웟다. 차차 전차 안이 좁아 오는데도 불구하고 녀학생은 여전히 뷔인 자리 아페 버틔고 서 잇다.
만약 자긔가 그 자리에 안끼가 실흐면 자리를 비켜서 다른 사람에게라도 사양하는 것이 小生의 상식뎍 의견으로는 當且當하거늘 이 녀학생은 안찌도 안코 그러타고 자리를 비키지도 안코, 두 사람 분의 자리를 차지한 채로, 명랑한 안색으로 그냥 비츨거리고 잇다.
안찌를 안흐려면 무얼하려 구타여 뷔인 자리 아페까지 차저 갓는지?
여기서 小生은 탄식하엿다-.
「하늘이 이 세상에 여인을 만들은 그 까닭은 남자로 하여금 심심한 때에 이를 연구하고 관찰하고 이 긔괴한 수수꺽기를 풀으려는 흥미를 갓게 하기 위하여서 인가」 ＜17＞
