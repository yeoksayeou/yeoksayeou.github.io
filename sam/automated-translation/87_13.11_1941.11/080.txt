Magazine Title: 삼천리 제13권 제11호
Publication Date: 1941년 11월 01일
Article Title: 臨戰愛國者의 大獅子吼!!, 愛國의 至誠과 此機會 (The Great Lion's Roar of a Wartime Patriot!!, The Utmost Sincerity of Patriotism and This Opportunity)
Author: 李晟煥(安興晟煥)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial)

**Body Text Translation:**
I wish first to speak the frank confession within my heart. The time has come when this confession of mine can resonate with your hearts, and I believe you are willing to hear such a confession.

Although one became a subject of the Japanese Empire (日本帝國臣民), I did not possess the true awareness to serve with utmost loyalty from the genuine heart, thinking, "I am a subject of the nation of Japan (日本), I am a child of the Emperor (天皇の赤子)." While I did the things the authorities instructed, there was never a time I exerted myself voluntarily with devotion. I held the Japanese flag (日章旗) and recited "Long Live His Majesty the Emperor (天皇陛下萬歲)," but it did not stem from an overflowing sense of the national polity (國體), a precise national consciousness (國家意識), or a firm grasp of the Way of the Subject (臣民道). Thus, opaque and unclear times passed by in reluctant obedience.

Even looking at the situation since the China Incident (支那事變), Koreans (朝鮮人) have merely followed along, barely keeping pace with reality as it changed from moment to moment, lacking the spiritual readiness to rise up fervently and advance voluntarily. What made us this way? It was because we were bound by erroneous considerations within the conventional world-historical perspective and could not stand firm.

In the past, we sometimes yearned for the establishment of independent states for small ethnic groups based on the principle of national self-determination (民族自決主義), and at other times, we schemed for the construction of a class-based state through Communism (共産主義). The psychology <39>, sentiment, and movement seeking separation from the Japanese Empire whenever an opportunity arose were latently fluctuating. This was because we learned from academia and facts that the colonial policies of the imperialist aggressor nations of Europe and America (歐米帝國主義的 侵略國家) under the old system were, without exception, based on exploitation and enslavement. Consequently, we failed to frankly understand the fundamental spirit of the Japan-Korea Annexation (日韓倂合), its world-historical mission, and its great life, unimaginable in human history.

This is our frank past reality. However, from now on, the isolated survival of small ethnic groups (群小民族) is difficult. Politically, the existence of large regional superstates (大地域的 大國家) is necessary, and economically, broad regional economies (廣域經濟) are required. In East Asia (東亞), the peoples of East Asia cannot survive without building the East Asian Co-Prosperity Sphere (東亞公營圈) centered on the Great Japanese Empire (大日本帝國). For instance, if one considers whether Chosŏn could survive in the future with a political or economic system like that of the Three Kingdoms period of Silla (新羅), Paekche (百濟), and Koguryŏ (高句麗) in the past, the answer becomes clear. From now on, small-nation nationalism (小民族主義) must be subsumed under large-state nationalism (大國家主義). In other words, it must be a supra-nationalist large-state nationalism (超民族主義的 大國家主義), and small-state nationalism (小國家主義) must become large-regional-state nationalism (大地域國家主義). Put differently, one cannot survive without leaping from a local Monroe Doctrine to a broad regional bloc-ism (뿔럭主義). Consequently, only the citizens of a nation that has built the most advanced national defense can sleep soundly with their pillows high. Here, I advocate: first, the State; second, the State (一曰國家, 二曰國家). However, it must not be a liberal or individualistic state, but a state of the new order under a system where all people support the throne (萬民翼體制). Japan has long had the fundamental spirit that the people have a path of public service (奉公) through practicing the Way of the Subject (臣道), and the state ensures there is not one person gorging themselves nor one person starving. Nowadays, the term "new system" (新體制) has become almost as common as the word for eating, but the new system is by no means something that falls from the sky. It is correcting the mental framework of each and every citizen, turning it into conviction, and putting it into practice—that is the new system.

All of humanity worldwide has risen up, grappling with how to survive, how to live better than others, facing off with guns and shedding blood. The youth of the world are fighting for the victory and honor of their homelands, pouring out the utmost sincerity of patriotism (愛國の至誠). A serious and solemn struggle continues, where victory in the decisive battle means life, and defeat means death. We must live as first-class citizens of the world <40>, having attained a high cultural standard. To do so, we must build a state with advanced national defense (高度國防國家). To build such a state, we must thoroughly expand productive capacity and strengthen national defense, so that Japan can dominate the world. And to achieve that, our one hundred million citizens (一億國民) must unite as one (一心) and strive with loyal service to the nation (盡忠報國) and desperate effort to win the war.

So, to achieve final victory in this global decisive war, what must we do at this time? That is, what kind of resolve should we have as wartime citizens, and what should wartime citizens decide? Above all else, the entire populace must transcend their individual interests, thoroughly grasp the wartime objectives, and possess the spirit to step forward, dedicating their bodies and families for the sake of the nation and people. The brave soldiers fighting on the front lines have completely forgotten their bodies and families, enduring hardships more difficult than death, unseen, fighting with the utmost limit of the human spirit. If the citizens on the home front (銃後國民) adopt this noble and fiercely fighting spirit, what could possibly be unachievable in this world? To completely break away from calculations of personal gain and loss and rise up with true national awareness and national spirit (國家的 氣魄) is the essence of the wartime spirit. Yet, at such a time, engaging in black market dealings (闇取引), hoarding, or speculative buying (買占·賣惜), thinking only of one's own benefit, or harboring cowardly thoughts and escapist delusions (姑息的 생각과 逃避的 妄動) about how to avoid personal danger—this cannot be called a wartime spirit at all.

To build a new order in East Asia (東亞の新秩序), establish eternal peace in the Orient (東洋永遠の平和), contribute further to the construction of a new world order, and reconstruct all cultures under the spirit of Hakkō Ichiu (八紘一宇)—if the first step of the first departure towards accomplishing this great ideal and undertaking lay in the Japan-Korea Annexation (日韓倂合), then the historical mission of *Naissen Ittai* (內鮮一體 - Japan and Korea as one body) must be acknowledged as great in its contribution to world history. We must realize that there lies a historical destiny and a demand of the era that requires Japan and Korea to become one body (*Naissen* ga *ittai* to nari), the one hundred million to become one mind (一億が一心と 되여서), and to take courageous steps forward as the core nation and leading state of East Asia.

The world-historical mission of *Naissen Ittai* has become transparent precisely at this juncture of complex world affairs and 급한 East Asian circumstances. A common destiny is forged, demanding that we pour out the highest and most sublime utmost sincerity of patriotism (至高至崇한 愛國至誠) for the completion of the Holy War (聖戰完遂). We feel a strong sense of urgent necessity (緊急切迫) welling up in our hearts to intensify the wartime spirit, arm the peninsula and its mountains and rivers (半島와 山河를 武裝化), and turn the populace of the peninsula into volunteers (半島의 民衆을 義勇化). <41> We must cast aside all complaints, dissatisfactions, and demands, and resolve only to fight, offering our lives just like the soldiers on the front lines.

Many young people are gathered here, so I especially want to appeal to you, the youth who possess such fervent passion and resolute executive power. What is there without a nation? After the homeland is ruined, there will be no literature, no art, no commerce, no industry, no agriculture, no hope for life—nothing at all. Currently, our nation's surrounding situation is extremely pressing (刻* 逼迫하여), and whether for good or ill, we face a crossroads determining the rise or fall of the nation and the life or death of its people (國家興亡 國民死活의 分岐點). At this time, the Orient must step forward holding high the great banner "The Orient for Orientals" (東洋은 東洋人의 東洋) and march resolutely forward; we face such a destiny. Who is the most vital force at this time? It is indeed us, the youth. Looking back through the pages of history confirms this, as do the facts before our eyes. Only this passionate youth has shaken the axis of the world and created history. Will the youth of Chosŏn, born in the same era and sharing the same epochal aspirations, not rise up? Let us rise up and advance! Let us not be confused by this path or that, but advance solely under the one path of Nationalism (國家主義). At this opportunity, the spirit to truly defend the homeland must ignite. This spirit! This mentality is truly great. To do something "because I was told to" is cowardly (卑怯한 짓이다). Following blindly and acting thoughtlessly (追隨와 妄動) is humiliating. There must be voluntary courage (自發的 勇猛). There is a world of difference (天壤의 差) in outcome between thinking, "Should I try it because they told me to?" and thinking, "Right, this is my duty; I must accomplish it even if I have to sacrifice my life." Like the great young figures in history who cast aside their lives at critical junctures (危急存亡之秋), <42> let us too, at this turning point of rise or fall, become warriors for the construction of the new East Asian order and the establishment of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (大東亞共榮圈の確立), becoming youth of national concern (憂國の青年) burning with the ideal convictions of patriotism, prepared for precious sacrifice.

There is the term "service through one's occupation" (職域奉公), which means dedicating oneself to one's duties. However, the sole form of public service (奉公) is not just businessmen earning money or scholars merely conducting research. If the state requires it, everyone, whether a university professor or a multimillionaire, must put themselves in the position of a common soldier (一兵卒), take up rifle and bayonet, and offer loyalty welling up from the heart to protect the homeland. It goes without saying that today, national defense cannot be left solely to the soldiers; everyone from top to bottom must truly have the mindset of fighting. Previously, there was the term "front line and home front" (銃前銃後), and while there is a great difference in circumstances between those who went to the front lines and those who remained in the country, there is currently no front line or home front when it comes to the spirit of fighting for the country. This is the reason why we have risen up calling for the armament of the Korean peninsula (半島江山의 武裝化) and the volunteering of its 24 million people (二四〇〇萬의 義勇化). Contribute money! Contribute strength! Supply materials! Offer even your last breath!—this is the Practical Outline (實踐要綱) of the Movement for Service to the Nation in Response to the Wartime Situation (臨戰對策報國運動).

We believe and do not doubt that the authorities, as well as all people from the mainland (內地人 - Japanese), understand and will cooperate with the practice and actions stemming from our beliefs and this movement. In Japan before the Meiji Restoration (明治維新), there was a time when, to hasten domestic unification, nationalism was heightened, emphasizing Japaneseness and the Yamato spirit (大和魂), leading to criticisms like "island nation mentality" (島國根性), "exclusivism" (排他的), or "narrow-mindedness" (雅量이 狹小). However, today, Japan's own situation has changed. It is no longer before the Manchurian Incident (滿洲事變), nor before the China Incident (支那事變); it has adopted a much broader stance, making it impossible to manage solely with a Japanese perspective. As recent national policies demonstrate, the construction of East Asian Co-Prosperity (東亞共榮建設) has become an extremely urgent task.

If Japan fails to make Koreans (朝鮮人) face their shared destiny, become one mind in one hundred million (一億一心), clearly recognize that the leader of East Asia (東亞의 盟主) is the entire populace of one hundred million united as *Naissen Ittai*, and promote a core system under the principle of guiding all peoples (諸民族을 指導), then Japan cannot avoid losing its qualification for managing the continent (大陸經營) and building the East Asian Co-Prosperity Sphere (東亞共榮圈建設). There is an absolute necessity and destiny that prevents this from happening. Therefore, I wish to conclude that this movement of ours is a historical demand, an epochal mission, and a national command. Who can possibly stop this? We await your resolute determination and courageous advance. (Applause) <43>

**Summary**

Yi Sŏng-hwan confesses his past lack of sincere loyalty to the Japanese Empire but now passionately advocates for complete devotion. He argues that Koreans must abandon outdated ideas of independence or communism and embrace their role within the Japanese-led Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere as a matter of survival and historical destiny. He calls for Koreans to internalize the *Naissen Ittai* (Japan-Korea Unity) principle, reject personal interests, and fully mobilize for Japan's war effort with utmost sincerity and willingness to sacrifice everything, just like soldiers at the front. He frames this total commitment as fulfilling a world-historical mission under the *Hakkō Ichiu* ideal and a national imperative for both Japan and Korea.

This article is highly revealing for historians studying the collaborationist discourse during the late Japanese colonial period. It demonstrates the adoption and internalization of Japanese imperial ideology (including concepts like *Kokutai*, *Naissen Ittai*, *Hakkō Ichiu*, and *Tōa Kōeiken*) by some Korean elites and their efforts to persuade the Korean populace to actively support the Japanese war effort, framing it not merely as compliance but as a path to a supposedly greater, shared destiny and historical mission. The fervent tone and justification of total mobilization highlight the intense pressures and ideological climate of wartime Korea.

기사형태	논설

臨戰愛國者의 大獅子吼!!, 愛國의 至誠과 此機會
						李晟煥(安興晟煥)
나는 먼저 나의 가슴속에 있는 솔직한 고백을 말하럅니다. 나의 이 고백은 여러분의 가슴속과 상통될 시기에 이르렀고, 여러분도 이런 고백을 듣고 싶어하는 용의가 있는 줄로 믿습니다.
사람이 日本帝國臣民이 되어 있었으나 진심으로 나는 日本나라의 臣民이다, 天皇의 赤子이다하는 참된 마음으로 충성을 다하겠다하는 자각을 가지지 못하였든 것입니다. 당국에서 하라는 일을 하기는 하면서도 정성을 다하여서 자발적으로 노력하여온 일은 없었습니다. 일장기를 들고, 天皇陛下萬歲도 낭독하고 있었으나, 그러나 감격에 넘치는 국체관념이라든지, 정확한 국가의식이라든지, 確乎한 臣民道의 파악에서 발로하는 것이 아니었습니다. 그저 부득이한 복종으로 불투명, 불명백한 세월이 흘러갔든 것입니다.
支那事變이래의 현상을 보드라도 朝鮮人은 현실이 변하여가는 그대로 그때 그때에 겨우겨우 追隨하였고 진심으로 奮然히 이러나서 자발적으로 나가려는 정신적 준비가 없었습니다. 이것은 무엇이 우리로 하여금 그렇게 만드렀느냐 하면 종래의 세계사적 관념에 있어서 착오된 고찰에 拘?되여 서지 못하였든 까닭입니다.
과거에 있어서 우리는 때로는 民族自決主義에 의한 群小民族의 독립국가건설을 동경하여 보기도 하였고, 때로는 共産主義에 의한 階級國家建設을 策動하기도 하였습니다. 기회만 있으면 日本帝國과 떠러저 보려는 심리와 ＜39＞, 감정과, 운동이 잠행적으로 浮沈되고 있었던 것입니다. 이것은 구체제하의 歐米帝國主義的 侵略國家군들의 植民地政策이 예외도 없이 모도가 착취화, 노예화식힘에 있다는 학문과 사실을 배우고 하였기 때문에 日韓倂合의 근본정신과 그 세계사적 사명과, 인류사상에서 상상조차 못한 위대한 생명을 솔직하게 이해하지 못하였든 까닭이외다.
이것은 솔직한 우리들의 과거한 사실입니다. 그러나 금후로는 群小民族의 고립적 생존은 어렵습니다. 정치적으로는 大地域的 大國家의 존재가 필요하고, 경제적으로는 廣域經濟가 필요하게 되었습니다. 東亞에 있어서는 大日本帝國을 중심으로 하는 東亞公營圈 建設을 하지 않고는 東亞諸民族이 사러 갈 수 없습니다. 예컨대 朝鮮의 과거에 있어서 新羅, 百濟, 高句麗의 三國倂立時代와 같은 정치나 경제체제를 가지고 今後 사러갈 수 있겠는가 없겠는가를 생각하여보면 잘 알일 입니다. 이제부터는 小民族主義는 大國家主義下에 攝理되게 되었습니다. 다시 말하면 超民族主義的 大國家主義라야 되고, 小國家主義는 大地域國家主義라야 되에 되었습니다. 다시 말하면 지방적 몬로主義로부터 광역적 뿔럭主義로 飛躍하지 아니하고는 살 수 없이 되었습니다. 그러하여 가장 고도한 국방을 건설한 국가의 국민만이 벼개를 높이 베고 잠을 잘 수 있게 되었습니다. 여기서 나는 一曰國家, 二曰國家를 주장합니다. 그러되 그는 自由主義的이나 個人主義的인 국가가 아니라 萬民翼體制의 신질서의 국가래야 됨니다. 日本은 종래부터 인민은 臣道실천의 奉公의 길이 있고 국가는 1인의 포식자를 없이하고 1인의 기아자도 없이 하라는 본정신이 있습니다, 요새이 신체제라는 말이 밥먹는다는 말의 다음쯤 가리만치 되어있으나, 그러나 신체제라는 것은 결코 하늘에서 뚝 떠러지는 것은 아입니다. 그것은 국민 한사람 한사람의 마음의 체제부터 고처 가지고 그것을 신념화하고 그것을 실천화하는 것이 신체제입니다.
