Grand Symposium Sponsored by Our Company: Wartime Countermeasures Council, 120 Personages Gather at the Pumin'gwan Hall

The "Wartime Countermeasures Cooperation Association (臨戰對策協力會)," which is the parent body of today's Korean Association for Assisting the Imperial Rule (朝鮮臨戰報國團), was formed after being unanimously approved as a permanent standing organization following lengthy discussions when 120 individuals from various sectors of society gathered at the Pumin'gwan Hall on August 25th at the invitation of this company's president, Mr. Kim Tonghwan (金東煥). An excerpt of that night's proceedings is recorded below; first, the original text of the invitation is as follows.

Invitation Letter to the "Wartime Countermeasures" Council Meeting

Dear Sirs, Our company, "Samch'ŏlli," would like to invite approximately two hundred distinguished individuals from various fields in Korea, including the intellectual world, financial circles, education, religion, press, and culture, including yourselves, to convene for a "Wartime Countermeasures" Council meeting during this critical time. We wish to broadly convey the substance of this meeting to the populace of the peninsula to urge great self-reflection and inspire them to rise to the occasion.
As you are already keenly aware, the current World War has expanded into its final and largest stage, and the nations of Europe are engaged in a life-or-death struggle. For our Japanese Empire as well, it is utterly impossible to predict when or what kind of unforeseen turn of events might occur in the Far East. We firmly believe that the rise or fall of the Imperial Nation of Japan truly depends on how we overcome this difficult crisis.

(Photo is of the council meeting hall; the person at the podium is Mr. Cho Pyŏngok) <46>

However, the populace of our peninsula has become too accustomed to the weakness of the feeble enemy Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石), mistakenly viewing the world situation too optimistically. Even now, in this autumn of the fifth year of the Holy War, it is truly lamentable that a portion [of the populace] has still not fully become militant citizens, showing tendencies of hesitation, evasion, and appeasement.
Chiang the rebel (蔣逆) was a weak enemy, but the coming enemy will likely not be as weak as him. If, by any chance, we were to engage in conflict with those enemy nations possessing vast resources and insatiable greed, this time, we, the one hundred million brethren of Japan proper and Korea (內鮮一億同胞), must truly become a single unit of iron and blood, literally throwing away our lives to fight the final decisive battle.
Now, as the entire nation rises as one to prepare for an even greater war and establish a wartime footing this autumn, we believe there are still many areas where we ourselves have not yet exerted our full strength. The poor performance in absorbing patriotic government bonds (愛國公債), the slowness of labor service (勞務奉仕) for production expansion projects, and furthermore, due to a lack of national loyalty, conviction, and spirit, violations of controls such as black market dealings (闇取引) and hoarding (買溜), and even spreading rumors and rebelling against the nation (御國に弓を引く)—these are truly deplorable circumstances.
Facing these facts, we earnestly appeal to you, the pioneers. We want you, with your influence, to make this ignorant and egoistic segment of the populace—who think only of escaping harm alone to somewhere like Mt. Kyeryong (鶏龍山) if enemy planes were to attack, who try to secretly hoard one or two years' worth of food fearing shortages in material distribution, and who plan to flee south if the enemy comes from the north, or flee north if they come from the south, rather than going out to repel them—engage in deep self-reflection. We want you to pour your passion into rousing the unwavering courage to live and die with the nation. And please, whip, encourage, and guide us so that all of us, the populace of the peninsula, may become individuals who deeply cultivate ourselves internally and, externally, become loyal and brave citizens who offer our lives to the Emperor and the Nation. Now is not the time for even a moment's hesitation; we believe we must make great strides towards the complete transformation of the peninsula into a supply base (兵站基地化) and towards the armament of the peninsula (武裝化).

(Photo caption: From right, Maeil Sinbo President [金川 is likely an error for 京城=Kyŏngsŏng or refers to the newspaper name's origin], Mr. Yu Ukkyŏm, one person skipped, Mr. Pak Yŏnghŭi) <47>

We sincerely hope you understand the humble intentions behind holding this council meeting and earnestly request your presence and participation in the discussions at the time and place indicated below.
Furthermore, on the day, we would like to proceed with the discussion within the scope of the agenda items listed below. Also, due to preparation needs, we apologize for the trouble but request that you indicate your attendance or absence on the separate sheet and return it.
Sincerely,

Agenda
1.  Regarding practical measures for the spontaneous movement towards becoming Imperial subjects (皇國臣民化運動) under the wartime system.
    a.  Measures for the thorough strengthening of material and labor contributions.
    b.  Policy for the movement to standardize national life at a minimum level.
    c.  Policy for making wartime service voluntary (義勇化).

That Night's Meeting <49>

Responding to the urgency of the international situation, the atmosphere of the night, from beginning to end, proceeded amidst palpable tension and solemnity. Starting before the scheduled time, figures from our society's intellectual circles, education, finance, press, religion, and other fields began to gather, numbering over 120 in total. Among them, the faces of Messrs. Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊), Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Sin Hŭngu (申興雨), and Yi Kwangsu (李光洙) were visible. From the financial world, the faces of Messrs. Han Sangnyong (韓相龍), Min Kyusik (閔奎植), Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植), etc., were also seen, as were the faces of former high officials like Yi Chinho (李軫鎬), Ko Wŏnhun (高元勳), and Kim Sigwŏn (金時權).
At precisely 6:30, after the "National Ceremony (國民儀禮)," Mr. Kim Tonghwan (金東煥) took the podium and gave opening remarks for about 10 minutes regarding the significance of the day's gathering. Subsequently, following a motion from various gentlemen entrusting the selection of the chairman to the host side, Mr. Kim recommended Mr. Sin Hŭngu (申興雨) as the chairman for the day and stepped down.

**Summary**

This 1941 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* reports on a large meeting of over 120 prominent Korean figures from various sectors (intellectual, financial, cultural, religious, etc.) held on August 25, 1941, at the Pumin'gwan Hall in Seoul (Keijō). Organized by the magazine's president, Kim Tonghwan, the "Wartime Countermeasures Council" aimed to discuss ways to mobilize the Korean populace for Japan's war effort. The meeting resulted in the formation of the permanent "Wartime Countermeasures Cooperation Association," a precursor to the collaborationist organization Chosŏn Imjŏn Poguktan. The article includes the full text of the Japanese invitation letter, which harshly criticizes Korean perceived complacency and lack of commitment to the "Holy War," urging attendees to lead the masses towards becoming loyal Imperial subjects prepared for sacrifice. For historians, the article is highly significant as it documents a key moment in the forced mobilization of Korean society during the late colonial period and provides a stark example of collaborationist rhetoric. The extensive list of attendees, including many famous intellectuals, artists, businessmen, and former officials (Yun Ch'iho, Ch'oe Rin, Yi Kwangsu, Kim Hwallan, Pak Hŭngsik, etc.), is particularly valuable, highlighting the complex pressures and choices faced by the Korean elite under Japanese rule.

本社主催大座談會 臨戰對策協議會, 百二十人士가 府民館서에 會合
금일 朝鮮臨戰報國團의 모체인「臨戰對策協力會」는 지난 8월 25일에 本社 사장 金東煥氏의 초청으로 사회각방면인사 120여氏가 부민관에 회합하여 장시간 토의를 거듭한 뒤 영구적인 상설기관으로 만장일치가결, 결성된 것이다. 이제 當夜의 의사를 抄錄하건대 먼저 초청의 원문은 如左하다.

「臨戰對策」協議會 案內狀
拜啓 弊社「三千里」は今度貴下を始め朝鮮に於ける思想界、財界、敎育、宗敎、言論、文化等各界有志凡そ二百人の方を一座へ御迎へしてこの時局重大な折柄「臨戰對策」協議會を催し、その內容を廣く半島民衆へ傳へ一大猛省と奮起を促し度う御座います。
旣に御明察のことと存ずるが今や世界大戰は最後的最大の場面に擴大され歐洲諸列國はそれこそ、生命を賭して死鬪して居り、我が日本帝國にしても何時如何なる不測の變局を極東の天地にて相見ゆるに至るや全く計り知れず皇國日本の興廢實にこの難關の擊破如何にあるものと深く信ずる次第であります。


(寫眞은、協議會場、登壇한이는 趙炳玉氏)＜46＞

然るに我が半島民衆は弱敵蔣介石の弱さに慣れ過ぎ天下の大勢を過りに甘く見、聖戰五個年のこの秋、今尙一部には戰鬪的國民の姿に完全に成り切れず、逡巡、逃避懷柔の傾向あるは誠に痛歎の至りて御座います。
蔣逆は弱敵たつたが來るべき敵は彼並には弱敵でなからん、廣大な資源と措くなき吞慾を持つて居る彼等敵性國家ともし萬が一、事を搆へる段になると今度は眞に我等内鮮一億同胞は鐵血の一團となり文字通り身命を拋つて最後の決戰を鬪はねばならぬてありましよう。
今や國を擧げてより大なる戰爭への搆への爲め一億國民總立ちとなり臨戰體制を整ふる秋、我等自身には未だ全力を出し切れずに居る部分が多々あるものと存じます。あの愛國公債消化力の貧弱、生産擴充事業への勞務奉仕の緩慢さ、しかも國民的忠誠と信念と氣魄の不足から闇取引、買溜等の統制違反、剩つさへ流言を放ち御國に弓を引く等々誠に歎はしい次第であります。
これ等の事實を前にして先覺者たる貴下に心から御訴へ致します。敵機でも襲來したら「鶏龍山」あたりへ自分一人難を避け、物資配給の窘迫を思つては一二個年分の食糧を窃かに買溜めむとし敵北から來れば出でて擊退せんとせず却つは反對の南に逃げ南から來れば北へ避げる算段をするこの愚昧にして利己主義的な一部民衆をあなたの力を以て猛省せしめて頂き度い。國と共に生き國と共に死ぬる不退轉の勇氣を奮ひ起さしむる様あなたの熱を注いて頂き度い。而して我等半島民衆凡てが內にあつては自らを深く修むる者たらしめ外にあつては身命を君國に捧げる忠勇たる國民になる樣何卒鞭打ち勵まし導きて頂き度う御座います。今や寸刻も躊躇するときでなく、我等は半島の完全なる兵站基地化へ、又半島の武裝化へ、大なる前進を致さなければならぬことと存じます。



(寫眞說明。右로부터金川每日社長、兪億兼氏、一人건너서、朴英熙氏)＜47＞

當夜의 會議＜49＞

국제정세의 긴박에 호응하여 當夜의 공기는 처음부터 끝까지 터질 듯이 긴장하고도 엄숙한 가운데 진행되었다. 정각 전부터 우리사회의 사상계, 교육, 재계, 언론, 종교 등 각계인사가 參集하기 시작하여 총 수백 20명의 다수를 算하였다. 그 중에는 尹致昊, 崔麟, 申興雨, 李光洙氏의 얼골도 보이고 재계로는 韓相龍, 閔奎植, 朴興植氏등의 얼굴도 보이고 李軫鎬, 高元勳, 金時權등 전일의 고관들의 얼굴도 보이였다.
