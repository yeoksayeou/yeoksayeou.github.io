One, P'yŏngyang Sŏng

-Continuation: Sŏdo Sentiment-

P'yŏngyang Sŏng seagulls
Play by the Taedonggang River
The taffy seller in Yŏtjŏnkkol alley
Plays with the song Susimga
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

The girls of P'yŏngyang Sŏng
Play with the boat song
On Tano Day in May
They play on the swing across the way
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

The girls of P'yŏngyang Sŏng
Go out to the rubber factory
Scholar Hwang's funeral bier
Goes out with willow ropes
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

The ladies of P'yŏngyang Sŏng
White handkerchiefs at the bus stop
Braided hair around the forehead
Dark crimson ribbon (taenggi) <142>
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

P'yŏngyang Sŏng's Solmoesan mountain
When the haze shimmers
A basket of azaleas
A round of Menari song
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

Waiting in P'yŏngyang Sŏng
On the blue stone roof
Hikari, Nozomi
The sound of the steam whistle
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

P'yŏngyang Sŏng's pine wind
Escapes through Hyŏnmumun Gate
Sŏmunt'ong's new road (sinjakro)
Leads out to Pot'ongbŏl field
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

Swallow, crossing over
P'yŏngyang Sŏng's Karugae
Pungmangsan mountain is torn down
And becomes a residential area
(Ŏlhwa chihwa
It's spring, it's spring)

Two, 'Autumn Wind Song of Farewell'

-Based on an Old Song-

Autumn wind, river wind
Do not blow <143>
The willow leaves of Nŭngnado island
Are all departing
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

On the stone pillar of Ŭlmiltae

Only branches remain
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Tinged, tinged
The autumn colors are tinged
On Kŭmsusan's trees where flowers fell
This year's autumn colors arrive

Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Tinged, tinged
The autumn colors are tinged

(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Yongaksan's twin rocks
Do not gaze upon them
When the sun sets
They become a sea of fire
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Maryŏul, Maryŏul (Horse Rapids)
What startled you?
A flock of sparrows whirls
And scatters away
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Having built Pot'ongmun Gate, why then
Was Songgaekchŏng Pavilion also built? <144>
Holding the donkey's reins
It's farewell, farewell
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Following the bell sound
Let's go find the temple

Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Climbing Pubyŏngnu Pavilion
The peaks are dots, dots

The blue water swirls, swirls

Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

The moon rises, the moon rises
The moon rises over Pubyŏngnu Pavilion
The moon rising over Pubyŏngnu
Is the Taedonggang's moon
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Aeryŏndang's lotus flowers
There is no trace of them
My love who left me too
I know not their whereabouts
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Chrysanthemum under the fence
Cricket under the wall <145>
When the two were together, it was joyful
Alone, it's tears of blood
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

In the autumn wind, river wind
The sound of the jade flute (okt'ongsu)
In the dawn moon, cold frost, thoughts of my love
(Ŏruna tiya
Unforgettable enemy/resentment)

Three, Truly Unbearable

On the ridge of Nosŏngnang
A small jujube tree
Because a girl was born
She tears her skirt hem
(Truly, truly unbearable)

On the ridge of Yanggojae
A willow tree
Because she's grown tall
She tears her clothes
(Truly, truly unbearable)

On the ridge of Sŏgisan
In the evening wind
Beyond the acacia grove
It's the melody of a martial trumpet (?)
(Truly, truly unbearable)

Ch'anggwangsan's pine trees
Always deep green
Pot'onggang's water scales
Shimmering in the sunlight
(Truly, truly unbearable)

Maiden of Sŏn'gyori
Let me ask you
Across the Taedonggang River <146>
Was life good?
(Truly, truly unbearable)

Sinni, Sinni it is
Sinni across the pedestrian bridge (Indogyo)
On the road to Chunghwa and Sangwŏn
A daily market opens
(Truly, truly unbearable)

Since the railway bridge was built
One cannot climb the pole (?)
Yanggakto, be sorrowful
How to go to Man'gyŏngdae?
(Truly, truly unbearable)

At Yungnomun Gate ferry landing
Choosing laundry stones
To the beat of the laundry bat
Spring is fleeting
(Truly, truly unbearable)

This bridge, what bridge?
A forked road
The tramway clatters
Come by the old road
(Truly, truly unbearable)

Taedonggang catfish, mullet
Outer fortress kimchi cabbage
Drinking water against the current
Become lads and maidens
(Truly, truly unbearable) <147>

**Summary**

This article presents three poems by Chu Yo-han, styled as folk songs or ballads centered around the city of P'yŏngyang. "P'yŏngyang Sŏng" depicts vignettes of life in the city during spring, mentioning specific locations, daily activities, and modern elements like factories and Japanese trains. "Autumn Wind Song of Farewell" adopts a more traditional tone of sorrow and longing, referencing historical sites around P'yŏngyang in autumn. "Truly Unbearable" portrays scenes suggesting hardship, social change, and the juxtaposition of nature and development in and around P'yŏngyang. From a colonial history perspective, the poems are interesting for their portrayal of P'yŏngyang during the Japanese occupation, blending traditional Korean imagery and sentiment with clear markers of modernization and Japanese influence, such as factories, new roads ('sinjakro'), residential development replacing traditional sites (Pungmangsan), and Japanese train names (Hikari, Nozomi). The refrain "Truly, truly unbearable" in the third poem might hint at underlying social commentary on the difficulties or alienation felt amidst these changes.
