**Body Text Translation:**
Actor's Notebook: My Impressions of Appearing in the Volunteer Soldier Film "Kimi to Boku (You and I)"
						Theater Troupe Kohyŏp, Sim Yŏng

The planning of the film "Kimi to Boku (You and I)," produced by the Chosŏn Army Information Department (朝鮮軍報道部), not only contributed greatly to the Chosŏn film world and general society in various senses, but also marked a new starting position for films under the current situation. Therefore, I first cannot help but be deeply pleased myself to have gained the opportunity to appear in it.

This spring, Mr. Higa (日夏氏) came to Chosŏn with the plan and, while negotiating views with the Army Information Department, created an opportunity to inspect the theater troupe "Kohyŏpch'on (高協村)" that I manage, introduced it to the Mainland (內地 - Japan), and furthermore requested my appearance.

Not only is my joy great for appearing in a work with such a sincere theme of "Naeseon Ilche" (內鮮一體 - Japan-Korea as One Body), but also, as a youth of the Peninsula (半島), I am able to help the work of the Army Information Department, even if my strength is meager. Furthermore, it was also a joyous thing to be able to work together with the staff of this film, including Director Den Hatao (田坡具隆), as well as Messrs. Kosugi Isamu (小杉勇), Ōhinata Den (大日方傳), and Kawazu Seizaburō (河津濟三郞), who have struggled for art for a long time, ten or fifteen years, in the Mainland.

I appear portraying a volunteer soldier named Kinoshita (木下). Before appearing in the film, I only knew about volunteer soldiers in a common-sense way. However, in order to fully play this volunteer soldier role, our staff entered a training center (訓練所) and stayed together for three weeks, receiving training with actual guns and swords. What surged up, nearly choking my chest, was the great military spirit (軍人精神).

From my standpoint, I felt that in the past, the Peninsula's theater troupes, film world, and indeed all institutions of general society lacked integrity, were powerless, disorganized, and disunited because they had not mastered the upright, sound, brave, and combative military spirit. Therefore, I have decided to have all members of my theater troupe enter the training center <138> to receive training, in order to disseminate the noble spirit I experienced, and I intend to surely implement this.

Furthermore, meeting the aforementioned Messrs. Kosugi Isamu, Ōhinata Den, and Kawazu Seizaburō, who are good seniors and friends, was also a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

During the location filming in Puyŏ (夫餘), the sacred ground of Naeseon Ilche, Mr. Kosugi and I floated a boat on the Paengma River (白馬江) on a night brightly lit by the full moon. We exchanged stories of my ten years of hardship in theater and the brief history of his struggles, and enjoyed that night talking about the future of theater and film for Chosŏn, the supply base (兵站基地).

To sublimate theater and film to be sounder than they are now depends on the self-awareness and healthy spirit of Chosŏn artists. While capital, strengthening weak structures, and the issue of cultural refinement are not unnecessary, I believe that artistic action established upon the national ideology (國家理念) is the urgent issue presented to us.

To accomplish this, it must be the product of the military spirit mentioned above; it must not be the art of the individual, but the art of the nation; it must be the art of the entire people. This is because no art can exist apart from the nation and the people. Although this has been my belief all along, taking the opportunity of appearing in this film, I felt it keenly, to my very bones, and I intend to strive for its accomplishment in the future, not just for my new theater troupe, but for the entirety of Chosŏn theater, and further, if my strength can reach, in all cultural sectors of Chosŏn.

A scene from the film "You and I". Top row from left: Sim Yŏng (沈影), Ch'oe Robong (崔露峰), Yi Kŭmnyong (李錦龍). Middle row, left: Kosugi Isamu (小杉勇), right: Miyake Kuniko (三宅邦子). Bottom row from right: Nagata (永田), Kosugi Isamu (小杉勇), Sim Yŏng (沈影).

I send my deep gratitude to Major Akutagawa (芥川少佐), Secretary Hoshide (星出事務官), Military Commissioned Officer Takai (高井軍囑託), as well as Messrs. Kosugi Isamu, Ōhinata Den, and Kawazu Seizaburō, who did not spare their considerable support and encouragement while I, who knew little, appeared [in the film] this time.
(End) <139>

**Summary**

This 1941 article by Korean actor Sim Yŏng describes his experience starring in the Japanese propaganda film "Kimi to Boku (You and I)," produced by the Chosŏn Army Information Department to promote the volunteer soldier system. Sim expresses gratitude for the opportunity, praises the theme of "Naeseon Ilche" (Japan-Korea Unity), and recounts his positive interactions with the Japanese director and actors. He details undergoing military training for the role, extolling the "great military spirit" which he felt was lacking in Korean society and arts, and vows to instill this spirit in his own theater troupe and promote art aligned with the "national ideology." For historians, this article is a significant primary source illustrating active collaboration by a Korean cultural figure during the late colonial period, the mechanisms of Japanese propaganda through film, and the explicit adoption and promotion of Japanese militarist ideology within the Korean arts scene under the banner of "Naeseon Ilche."

俳優手帖 志願兵映畵「君と僕(너와나)」에 出演한 나의 感想
						劇團高協 沈影
朝鮮軍報道部 製作인 映畵「그대와 나」의 企劃은 여러 가지 意味에서 朝鮮映畵界와 및 一般社會에 이바지한 影響이 클뿐만 아니라 時局下 映畵의 新發的地位를 劃한것으로서 爲先나는 出演의 機會를 얻게된것을 스스로 저윽히 기뻐마지 않는 바이다.
今年 봄 日夏氏가 企劃을 가지고 朝鮮에 나와 軍報道部와 憲見을 交涉하는 一方 내가 經營하는 劇壇「高協村」을 視察할 機會를 만들어 內地에 紹介해주었고 더구나 나에게 出演을 請하여 왔었다.
나는 이러한 眞實된 內鮮一體의「테-마」를 가진 作品에 出演한 기쁨도 크려니와 軍報道部의 일을 微力하나마 半島靑年으로서 도울수 있게되며 나아가서는 이 映畵의「스타푸」로된 田坡具隆監督을 비롯해 小杉勇, 大日方傳, 河津濟三郞 諸氏等 內地에서 十年 或은 十五年이란 긴 時間에 藝術을 爲하여 奮鬪해나온 그들과가치 일할 있다는것도 또한 기뿐일이였다.
나는 木下라는 志願兵으로, 扮하여 出演하고 있다. 나는 志願兵이란것을 出演전가지는 常識的으로 안것뿐이였으나 이 志願兵役을 充分히 할수있기 爲하여 우리「스타푸」는 訓練所에 入所하여 三週日동안이나 合宿하면서 實地로 銃과칼을 가지고 訓練을받을때 내가슴이 메여지도록 치미러올러온것은 偉大한 軍人精神인것이다.
나의 立場에서 말하면 過去 半島의 劇團이나 映畵界나 또한 一般社會 모든 機關이 剛直하고 健全하고 勇敢하고 戰鬪的인 軍人精神을 體得치 못하였기 때문에 志操없고 無力하고 非組織的이며 非團結的이였다는것을 느끼여 나는 내 劇團으로 하여금 내가 經驗한 高貴한 精神을 普及하기 爲하여 會員이 모두 訓練所＜138＞에 入所하여 訓練을 받을것도 決心하고 꼭 實行하려고 하고있다.
더구나 우에 말한 小杉勇氏 大日方傳氏 河津濟三郞氏等 좋은 先輩요 親舊를 마지하게 된것도 一生에 두 번없는 機會였든것이다.
內鮮一體의 聖地 夫餘에서「로케」를 하는 동안 나와 小杉氏와는 보름달이 皎皎히 밝은 밤 白馬江에 배를 띄우고 나의 演劇十年의 苦生된 이야기며 그의 奮鬪小史도 서로 交換하며 앞으로 兵站基地 朝鮮이 나아갈 演劇과 映畵에 대한 이야기로 그 밤을 즐겼다.
健全한 演劇을, 映畵를, 現在以上으로 止揚하려면 조선 藝術家의 自覺과 健康한 精神力에 있다는것이다. 資本도 必要하고 貧弱한 機構强化와 및 敎養問題도 所要되는바 아닌것은 아니나 國家理念에 確立한 藝術行動이 喫緊한 提出된 問題이라고 생각된다.
이것을 完遂하는데는 우에서 述한 軍人精神이 所産이여야 하며 個人의 藝術이 아니고 國家의 藝術이여야 하며 國民全體의 藝術이여야 하겠다는 것이다. 國家와 國民을 떠나서는 何等의 藝術이 存立할수없는 까닭이다. 이것은 내 平素에 뜻해온바이기는하나 이 映畵에 出演을 契機로 하여 나는 뼈에사뭇치게 切實히 느끼였고 今後의 새 劇團 아니 朝鮮演劇 全體나아가서는 조선의 文化各部門에 내힘이 밋칠수 있다면 그 完遂에 努力하려는 바이다.

모르는 내가이번 出演하는동안 많은 後援과 激勵를 아끼지않은 芥川少佐, 星出事務官, 高井軍囑託을비롯해 小杉勇, 大日方傳, 河津濟三郞諸氏에게 깊이 感謝의 뜻을 보내는 바이다.
