**Summary**

This document lists the speakers and topics from the "Record of Proceedings of the Wartime Countermeasures Council" (臨戰對策協議會 議事錄) published in the November 1941 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine. Prominent Korean figures like Yun Ch'i-ho, Ch'oe Rin, Yi Kwang-su, and Cho Pyŏng-ok presented statements or speeches covering resolutions, gratitude, the tense situation in the Far East, the role of Koreans within the Japanese Empire, patriotism, the "Spirit of the Imperial Way," labor conscription, declining living standards, and mobilizing voluntary service for the war effort. For historians, this list is significant as it showcases prominent Korean intellectuals and public figures publicly aligning with and promoting Japan's wartime agenda during a critical period (late 1941, just before Pearl Harbor). The topics discussed directly reflect the pressures and propaganda themes of the time, including total mobilization (總力), imperial ideology (皇道精神), and integrating Koreans into the Japanese war machine (勞務供出, 戰時奉公).
