Article Title: 國民皆勞運動에 各團體의 動員令!! (Mobilization Order for All Organizations in the National Labor Movement!!)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

Mobilization Order for All Organizations in the National Labor Movement!!

We must send soldiers to the front lines and industrial warriors to the home front (銃後) so that this Holy War (聖戰) may swiftly achieve the beauty of a successful conclusion (有終의 美). To this end, an Imperial Edict (勅令) establishing the National Labor Movement (國民皆勞運動) is to be issued. The Government-General has designated the period until the end of November as a Labor Emphasis Period for preliminary training (練成). Regarding this National Labor Movement unfolding across all of Chosŏn, what plans and preparations do the various organizations in our society have? Let us glimpse the overall picture.

**Ch'ŏndogyo: Cultivating the Farmland of Dispatched Soldiers**
Yi Chongnin, Leader of Ch'ŏndogyo

Among the tenets of Ch'ŏndogyo is "Laborious Diligence" (勞苦勤勉), making diligent service a tradition of our faith. Thus, our ministers have traditionally proclaimed this doctrine to the believers. Upon hearing the news that the state is now actively encouraging this National Labor Movement, we are truly overjoyed.
However, although our organization has traditionally promoted the spirit of diligence, it has been in a state of stagnation, and its activities lacked vigor. We intend to take this opportunity to cooperate with the authorities and actively strive to make all believers step onto the front lines of labor.
Since eighty to ninety percent of our believers are farming masses, there are few in the idle class; most are diligent believers who work sweating. Therefore, there might seem no need to newly encourage labor. However, previously, the purpose of their labor was limited to their own happiness and that of their families—in other words, merely individualistic activity focused on the self. We intend to overturn this perception and attitude, encouraging them to abandon the small self (小我) and embrace the greater self (大我), possessing a totalitarian ideology and engaging in activities accordingly. We aim to instill the understanding that the purpose of one's work now is not for oneself, but for one's society, one's nation, and more broadly, for the establishment of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (大東亞共榮圈), and further, for the construction of a new world order.
The Second Patriarch of Ch'ŏndogyo, Master Wŏr'wol (每月神師), truly made "Laborious Diligence" the primary principle of his life. Even while fleeing due to suspicion from the Korean government at the time, he would plaster walls and plant trees even at houses he would leave after only a day. When his wife protested, asking why he planted trees at a house they would leave in a day or two, he replied, "Even if we leave, others will come and live here, so we must fix what needs fixing and plant what needs planting," working diligently. When there was no work, he twisted straw ropes. When there was no material for ropes, he would unravel previously twisted ones and twist them again. This was an attitude of refusing idleness. We intend to further promote this spirit of Master Wŏr'wol among all believers, encouraging them to abandon the self and courageously strive forward in diligent labor for others.
Next, as a concrete plan, we intend to organize and dispatch study tour groups to advanced farming villages <73> in the mainland (內地 - Japan).
Since eighty percent of our believers are farmers, we aim to increase production based on the most ideal farming methods. This study tour group will select representatives from the thirteen provinces, who, after their tour, will travel around their respective provinces providing guidance.
Thirdly, we plan to have believers help cultivate the farmland of farmers who have been dispatched as soldiers or are wounded soldiers, engaging in communal farming.

**Christianity: Abolishing Sunday Rest**
Chŏng Ch'unsu, Superintendent of the Christian Methodist Church

Religion is not solely focused on spiritual life. Religion emphasizes that along with spiritual life, our physical bodies must bear fruit that contributes to society through diligence and service.
In this sense, the Bible also teaches, "The one who is unwilling to work shall not eat," as instructed by the Apostle Paul.
This National Labor Movement is an extremely crucial essential element of national life, both from a national and religious standpoint. Although our Methodist denomination has advocated for it previously, we have gained new understanding through the authorities' recent directive and intend to promote renewed reflection among all believers so they may take the lead and cooperate in this brilliant movement.
Now, as one concrete plan, we will strive to ensure there is no rest not only on regular weekdays but also on the Sabbath, that is, Sunday. Whether to work on the Lord's Day (Sunday) or not is a significant issue theologically within Christianity. Traditionally, it has been a ritual to absolutely forbid work on Sunday except for attending worship services, but I believe this is a mistaken notion. Why? Because Jesus also performed works of helping others besides worship on the Sabbath. He healed the sick and helped the unfortunate, actions criticized by the Pharisees of the time as violating the commandment. However, Jesus replied, "If one of your sheep falls into a pit on the Sabbath, will you not take hold of it and lift it out?" In this sense, our denomination will strive to have all believers participate primarily in physical labor from Monday to Saturday. At the same time, we will not rest on Sunday either. After worship, all believers will be actively encouraged to engage in labor related to relief <74> for the unfortunate, labor related to welfare and health for the sick, labor related to spiritual training and soul cultivation, labor related to religious education, and primarily labor related to spiritual mobilization in this National Labor Movement. Thus, we aim to eradicate the bad habit of resting or being idle and ensure thoroughness in patriotic labor (勤勞報國).

**Chosŏn Buddhism: Mobilizing Thirty Head Temples for Labor**
Yi Chonguk, Abbot of the Head Temple of Chosŏn Buddhism

The National Labor Movement is one of the most crucial movements, especially during wartime. However, this movement should not only be emphasized during wartime but should also be practiced during peacetime, so that the people become the strength of the nation and achieve the highest level of production expansion.
Furthermore, it is the duty of us religious figures to step onto the front lines of this movement, roll up our sleeves, strive ourselves, and encourage the populace.
Our Chosŏn Buddhist organization will fully mobilize the thirty head temples across Chosŏn, widely convey the purpose of this movement to each temple, and then encourage believers at each temple to implement it, fostering both the spirit and practice of diligence so that not a single person remains idle. Of course, we will implement this according to the authorities' directives, but among the believers belonging to our organization, we will ensure no one is idle, guiding all, regardless of gender or age, into this movement. Through this, we aim to increase production and consequently strive forward in the labor battle for the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

**Hwangdohakhoe: Mobilizing Idle Housewives and Young Ladies - Awakening the Intelligentsia and Encouraging Misogi**
Sin Pongjo, President of the Hwangdohakhoe (Imperial Way Study Society)

Since our organization is primarily composed of cultural figures, our membership is not as vast (尨大) as other religious organizations. Consequently, most members are engaged in cultural endeavors <75>, meaning the majority perform intellectual labor.
Of course, to manage the affairs of a nation, physical labor alone is insufficient; mental labor must accompany muscular labor. In this regard, our society aims to strengthen the thoroughness of intellectual labor among all members while placing them in leadership positions to maximize their service (奉公) to the nation.
The most immediate concrete plans we intend to undertake first are:
One, ensure there are no idle housewives or young ladies among urban women. That is, we will strive through writing and lectures to encourage middle-class and upper-class idle madams—who traditionally left tasks like cooking, laundry, and sewing entirely to maids or housekeepers, idly forming groups to go out, even developing bad tastes for harmful amusements like mahjong, spending days with tobacco and alcohol—and idle young ladies—who only frequent teahouses and theaters—to adopt a spirit of diligence and walk the path of self-reliance (自力更生).
Two, instill thoroughly the notion that any work is sacred in young men who, after graduating from specialized schools or universities, seek only high positions beyond their station and despise lowly occupations, remaining unemployed. At the same time, ensure not a single person is idle, making them take up any kind of work during this national time of crisis.
Three, popularize Misogi (禊 - purification ritual) among the general populace to cleanse both mind and body, refining the ancient, pure character of the people. Concurrently, in practical terms, have people wake up just thirty minutes earlier than their usual time to sweep the yard, the street outside the main gate, and even the village roads, thereby fostering closeness with neighbors, nurturing fine customs, and cultivating the spirit of diligence (涵養). If the entire nation wakes up just thirty minutes earlier each day and does thirty minutes more work, the cumulative result will indeed yield enormous effects over an enormous amount of time.

**Chosŏn Confucianism: Emphasizing the Sanctity of Labor through Lectures**
Pak Sangjun, President of the Confucian Scholars' Association and Head Scholar of the Kyŏnghagwŏn

If even one person among the populace eats idly (徒食) without working, it would not be an exaggeration to say they are a parasite (寄生虫) on the nation, someone who harms the nation <76> by sucking its blood. From this perspective, even in peacetime, all citizens, regardless of gender or age, must work; how much more so during wartime and a time of emergency?
Therefore, for the completion of a high-level national defense state, our entire populace must unite as one body (一億一心) and offer great service to the nation solely through diligence.
For this National Labor Movement, our Confucian organization will first hold round-table discussions for leaders who will stand on the guidance front lines to educate the general public about the movement.
Next, on the upcoming October 16th, the day of the All-Chosŏn Confucian Assembly, we will use this gathering to hold lectures on the National Labor Movement. To deepen the understanding of labor among the nearly one million Confucian adherents and sympathizers scattered throughout the thirteen provinces of Chosŏn, we will form provincial lecture tour teams to conduct large-scale propaganda (宣傳) and strive for practical implementation.

**National Training Support Association: Mobilizing the Idle Class to Construct Air-Raid Shelters**
Nomura Kōen (野村弘遠), President of the National Training Support Association (國民訓練後援會)

Regarding the National Labor Movement, the assertions and policies of our National Training Support Association are as follows:
One, to conscript (徵用) Chosŏn men not subject to the Conscription Law, Chosŏn women, and mainland Japanese women who have reached the age of nineteen, subject them to physical examinations, and enroll those who pass into camps for one year to engage in compulsory labor.
Two, to award a Labor Badge to those who complete this compulsory labor, granting them priority in education, employment, etc.
However, those who pass the examination for Army Volunteer Soldiers are exempted.
The organization will model itself after the military structure, and an independent bureau or department should be established within the main office.
Beyond this, the methods for the male and female labor movement will follow the plan of the National General Mobilization League (總力聯盟), but we will primarily focus on the following points:
① Make the idle housewives and young ladies of wealthy families reflect deeply, abolish the traditional system of housekeepers, maids, and seamstresses, and encourage these idle women—who were formerly absorbed in tobacco, alcohol, and entertainment—to become their own housekeepers out of a spirit of self-sufficiency (自作自業). Encourage the young ladies—who previously frequented teahouses and movie theaters simply because they had nothing else to do—to learn sewing at home and make their own clothes. <77>
② Next, address the bad habit among the wealthy, who only seek to employ others and do absolutely no work themselves. Their idle time represents a huge waste of time nationally. Encourage them also to do work they can manage themselves, such as sweeping the yard, planting trees, and undertaking other simple sideline productions.
③ Round up all the delinquents and vagrant youths (浮浪靑年) who wander the back alleys of Chongno, fighting, harassing women, and frequenting bars. Make them dig at least 10,000 air-raid shelters, each capable of holding 50 to 100 people or more, at the foot of the mountains surrounding Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) for several hours each day. Due to the labor shortage, even delinquent youths who act as 'beggar bosses,' parasitizing and exploiting actual beggars and urchins (those who only practice feats of strength under the Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn bridge) while not being disabled themselves, should participate. Furthermore, as far as the police permit, round up all pickpocket gangs (쓰리團) and extortion gangs (마루이찌團) and make them participate.
This is truly killing two birds with one stone, simultaneously cultivating the spirit of diligence and creating excellent air-Raid shelters.

**Chosŏn Salvation Army: Planning Increased Production through Communal Farming**
Sakamoto Raiji, Commander of the Chosŏn Salvation Army (朝鮮救世團)

Regarding the National Labor Movement, the Bible records the sacred words, "The one who is unwilling to work shall not eat." Therefore, we have always been concerned with this labor movement and have consistently preached about it to all our members. Now, realizing anew that this issue is an even more significant national movement, we intend to mobilize all our various agencies to widely implement and promote it.
As one concrete plan, since our organization is affiliated with the National General Mobilization League (國民總力聯盟), we will mobilize the Patriotic Neighborhood Associations (愛國班) belonging to our various branches to strive for diligent service starting with the tasks closest at hand, aiming for results rather than just words.
Another plan involves our rural members. We will encourage communal work (共同作業) to increase production, thereby thoroughly engaging in activities to make a great contribution to the nation.
Currently, our Salvation Army has invited an influential <78> officer from Tokyo. With him serving as the executive director, we are formulating new plans responsive to the current situation—that is, aimed at the public good of the nation. While reorganizing the Salvation Army's social work machinery, we also intend to achieve practical results in the future by adhering to the League's directives.

**Taedong Minuhoe: Striving for Production Expansion**
Yi Kakkchong, President of Taedong Minuhoe (大東民友會)

At this time, truly an unprecedented emergency in history, the entire nation must unite, and all citizens must offer great service to the nation on the labor front lines. Every citizen, without exception, must pour their entire heart and strength towards the supreme goal of the nation and strive forward (邁進) for production expansion.
Therefore, our organization has formulated the following two plans to ensure the thorough dissemination of this movement:
One, first, to establish a guiding spirit, we will hold round-table discussions. These discussions will bring together thinkers from various fields in the leadership class, representatives of edification organizations, and women from the leadership stratum.
Two, second, we will hold lecture meetings. In cooperation with the National General Mobilization Chosŏn League (國民總力朝鮮聯盟), these lectures will travel from Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) to the provinces, actively promoting (宣傳) the National Labor Movement to the general public and striving to cultivate (涵養) the spirit of diligent service. <79>

**Taedong Ilchinhoe: Breaking Down the Notion of Despising Labor**
Yun Kappyŏng, President of Taedong Ilchinhoe (大東一進會)

Humans originally living idly without working goes against the fundamental duty of being human, I believe. Therefore, completely liquidating the habit of idle consumption and living by working diligently is the duty of a citizen. Not just in times of emergency like this, but at all times, one must have the mindset of working to live; how much more so in an emergency, when immediate action is required without further words.
I understand there are many people idling about, whether in cities or the countryside. We must start by pulling these individuals into the workplace. For instance, suppose there are 100,000 unemployed people across Chosŏn. Even if we assume they could do work worth one Won per day, if they are idle for a day, it's a national loss of 100,000 Won. If they work for a day, there is production worth 100,000 Won that day, and the nation becomes stronger by that much. Thus, the effect of this labor movement will surely be immense.
We will eradicate the ingrained backward spirit of idle consumption (遊衣徒食) among members and the public and ensure they engage in patriotic labor (勤勞報國). We will take this opportunity to actively break down the traditional notion of despising labor and firmly instill the spirit of diligence. Although most of our association's 100,000 members are scattered in rural areas and thus there were fewer idlers, we will henceforth further emphasize the concept of patriotic labor to achieve significant results. Regarding implementation, the first step will be to arouse (喚起) public opinion through lectures, round-table discussions, and written propaganda (宣傳) in branch locations scattered across various regions and other important areas. Next comes practice, starting with encouraging (勸獎) our 100,000 members. <80>

**Summary**

This 1941 editorial from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine announces and promotes the Japanese colonial government's "National Labor Movement" (國民皆勞運動) in Korea. It features statements from leaders of prominent Korean religious and social organizations (Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, Buddhism, Confucianism, Hwangdohakhoe, Salvation Army, National Training Support Association, Taedong Minuhoe, Taedong Ilchinhoe). These leaders express enthusiastic support for the mobilization decree, framing labor as a patriotic duty essential for Japan's "Holy War" and the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere. They outline specific plans, including cultivating soldiers' land, abolishing Sunday rest, mobilizing temple resources, lecturing on labor's sanctity, targeting idle urban women and unemployed youth for work (including constructing air-raid shelters), promoting communal farming, and generally combating the "backward" notion of despising labor. The article vividly illustrates the mechanisms of colonial control, showing the co-option of Korean civil society groups to enforce Japanese wartime policies and mobilize the population for the war effort through propaganda, social pressure, and specific labor projects. Historians would find the explicit cooperation of these diverse Korean groups, the detailed mobilization plans targeting specific demographics (women, youth, unemployed), and the pervasive use of wartime rhetoric (Holy War, Co-Prosperity Sphere, national sacrifice) particularly revealing about the nature of late colonial rule and forced mobilization in Korea.

天道敎 出征將兵의 農土를 耕作
天道敎敎領 李鍾麟

天道敎의 信條中에는「勞苦勤勉」이라는것이 있어서 勤勞奉仕는 한 敎風으로 되어있다. 그리하여 從來에 敎役者들은 敎徒에게 이 敎義를 宣布하여왔었는데, 今番 國家에서 이 國民皆勞運動을 積極獎勵한다고 하는 消息을 接할 때 實로 欽快하기 짝이 없다.
그러나 我團體에서는 從來 勤勞精神을 鼓吹하여 왔었으나 不振狀態에 있어서 그 活動이 活潑치못했었던것을 이번 機會에 當局과 協力하여 積極的으로 全敎徒로 하여금 皆勞線上에 나서도록 힘쓰려한다.
그런데, 우리 敎徒의 八九割은 農民大衆이니만치 有閑階級에 이는 者가적고 大部分이 땀을 흘려서 일하는 勤勞信徒이기 때문에 새삼스럽게 勤勞를 勸獎할 必要가 없을것이나, 그러나 從來는 그 勞働의 目的이 自己一身과 自己家庭의 幸福에만 限한, 다시 말하면 自身만을 爲한 個人主義的인 活動에 不過한것을, 그 認識과 態度를 轉倒시켜서 小我를 버리고 大我에 歸依하는 全體主義의 思想과 活動을 가지도록 하려한다. 現在 내가 일하는 目的이 나를 爲함이 아니오 내 社會, 내 國家, 더 크게는 大東亞共榮圈確立을 爲해서, 한걸음 더 나아가서는 世界新秩序建設을 爲해서 일한다는 觀念을 把握케 하려한다.
天道敎의 第二世敎主인 每月神師께서는 實로 「勞苦勤勉」을 生活의 第一義로 삼으신 이로써, 그는 當時 韓國政府의 嫌疑로 避身해 다니면서도 하루만 살고 떠날 집이라도 흙바르고, 植樹했다. 그래서 그 婦人이 하루 이틀만 살고 떠날 집에 나무를 심어서 무엇하느냐 하고 抗議할 때, 그는 우리가 떠나도 뒤에 사람이 와서 살테니까 고칠데는 고치고 심을데는 심어야한다고 부즈런히 일했었으며, 일감이 없으면 노끈을 꼬는 일을 했고, 노끈을 꼴 資料가 없으면 꼬았던것을 풀어서라도 또 꼬았다. 그것은 쉬지않으려는 態度에서였다. 이 每月神師의 精神을 全敎徒에게 더욱 鼓吹하여 나를 버리고 남을 爲한 勤勞에 勇躍 邁進하도록 하려한다.
다음의 具體案은, 內地의 先進農＜73＞村見學團을 組織하여 派遣하려한다.

基督敎監理敎團統理者 鄭春洙

宗敎라고 하는것은 精神生活만 爲主하는것은 아니다. 精神生活과 함께 우리의 肉體는 勤勞와 奉仕를 通하여서 社會에 끼처지는 結實이 있어야 할것을 宗敎는 强調한다.

이제 그 具體案의 하나로는, 平常日에는 無論, 安息에도, 卽 日曜日에도 休養이 없도록 힘쓰려 한다. 主日(日曜日)에도 일하느냐? 아니하느냐?가 基督敎界에서는 敎理上으로 한 重大한 問題이여서 從來는 日曜日에 禮拜보는 外에는 絶對로 일을 못하게 한것이 한 儀式이 되어왔지만 이것은 그릇된 觀念이라고 생각한다. 웨 그런고하니 예수께서도 安息日에 禮拜外에 남을 救濟하는 事業을 하섰다. 病者를 고처주시고 불상한 사람을 도와주신것을 當時의 바리새 敎人들은 誡命을 어그러트리는 行動이라고 非難했었다. 그러나 예수의 對答은 「萬一 安息日에 한 羊이 잘못하여 우물에 빠지게 된다면 安息日이라고해서 도아주지않고 그저 보고만 있을것인가」하섰다. 이런 意味에서 我敎團에서는, 月曜日로부터 土曜日까지논 主로 肉體의 勤勞에 全敎徒가 參加하도록 힘쓰는 同時에 日曜日에도 쉬지않고, 禮拜後에는 全敎徒가 불상한 사람을 도와주는,＜74＞救濟에 關한 勤勞, 病者를 爲한 厚生及 保健에 關한 勤勞, 精神訓練及 心靈修養에 關한 勤勞, 宗敎敎育에 關한 勤勞等, 主로 精神動員에 關한 皆勞運動을 積極的으로 勸獎하려한다. 그리하여 쉬거나 논다는 惡習을 一掃케 하여 勤勞報國의 徹底를 期하려한다.

一, 첫째로는 指導精神을 確立하고 爲해서 座談會를 開催할것. 이 座談會에는 指導階級에 있는 各方面의 思想客과 敎化團體의 代表와 指導層의 婦人들을 會合케하려한다.
二, 둘째로는 講演會를 開催할것 이 講演會은 國民總力朝鮮聯盟과 提携하여 京城을 爲始해서 地方으로 行脚하며 一般民衆에게 皆勞運動을 積極的으로 宣傳하며, 勤勞奉仕의 精神을 涵養하도록 힘스려한다.＜79＞

大東一進會 勞働賤視의 觀念打破
大東一進會長 尹甲炳

사람이란 元來 놀고 먹는다는것은 사람의 本義에 어그러지는 일인줄 아오. 그런 까닭에 절대로 놀고 먹는 習貫을 淸算하고 부지런히 일하고 먹고사는것이 國民된 者의 本分일가하오. 이런 非常時뿐 아니라 어느때나 일하고 살겠다는 觀念을 가저야 하겠거든 하물며 非常時에 있어서는 再言을 不要하고 곧 實行하여야 할것입니다.
都市나 地方을 勿論하고 閒遊하는 사람이 많은줄 압니다. 그런 분들부터 일터로 끄집어 넣어야 할줄 압니다. 가령 全鮮에 無職者十萬이라하고 그들이 하로에 一圓짜리 일을 한다 심치드라도 그들이 하로 놀면 國家的으로 十萬圓이 缺損이요 하로 일하면 하로 十萬圓의 生産이 있는동시에 國家가 그만치 當하게 될것이니까 이 勤勞運動의 效果란 果然 偉大할줄 압니다.
會員及 大衆에게 從來의 遊衣徒食의 固陋한 精神을 一掃하고 勤勞報國하도록 하겠소. 이 機會에 積極的으로 從來의 勞働賤視의 觀念을 打破하고 勤勞의 精神을 굳게 갖도록 하겠소. 우리 會의 十萬會員은 大槪 地方에 多數散在한것만큼 遊衣徒食輩는 적었으나 앞으로 加一層 勤勞報國의 觀念을强調하야 多大한 效果를 얻도록 하되 이 實行에 있어서는 第一着으로 各地에 散在한 支會所在地와 其他重要地帶에서 講演會, 座談會, 文書宣傳으로 與論을 喚起하고 그다음은 實踐하되 우선 十萬會員에게 勸獎하겠소.＜80＞
