Urgent Notice

Recently, due to paper restrictions along with a sharp increase in readership, we have unavoidably decided to reduce the number of pages again starting from this month's issue.

However, we intend to refresh the editing and include only key articles, so please look forward to it greatly.

Samch'ŏlli Company Editorial Department <127>

**Summary**

This brief notice from the Samch'ŏlli Company Editorial Department informs readers that the magazine's page count is being reduced again, starting with the November 1941 issue. The reasons cited are paper restrictions and a significant increase in readership. Despite the reduction, the editors promise to improve the editing and focus solely on important articles. For historians, this announcement clearly reflects the material shortages (specifically paper) impacting publishing in late colonial Korea due to wartime conditions under Japanese rule. It also hints at the magazine's continued popularity or the demand for published materials among Koreans, even amidst increasing colonial pressures and restrictions.
