Magazine Title: 三千리 제13권 제11호
Publication Date: 1941년 11월 01일
Article Title: 山寺의 밤 (Night at a Mountain Temple)
Author: 盧天命
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I
Following the wild grape shoots, alone I enter the mountain depths

Searching for tales of my childhood
Among the *taengdaeng-i* vines and leopard flowers (범부채)
I walk the mountain path
Arriving at the temple in June daylight—
Hwee— like a graveyard, it is utterly silent

A place utterly unvisited before
Looking around, only unfamiliar things
Is this what the path to the afterlife is like?<150>
An uneasy thought arises again

The night at the mountain temple deepens
Buddha's heart (佛心) settles over the hermitage (庵子)
From some meditation hall (禪房), the sound of a wooden gong……

Closing my eyes, I quietly pray for the repose of souls (冥福)

II
Sleep is far, the moon is bright—
Opening the window, I step out into the courtyard

In the middle of the night, the mountain valley's water sounds are clamorous
Sitting on a dew-covered rock, listening to the water's sound
Even in the mountains, the cuckoo swallows its voice as it cries

The third watch has passed
A nun climbs toward the Dharma Hall (法堂)
On the collar of her ink-dyed robe, "Impermanence" (無常) shimmers

Beneath the moon, Kayasan mountain is bright and majestic
But I—
My heart attaches neither to the mountain nor the temple……
Like a night-heralding bird (귀곡새), I feel desolate<151>

**Summary**

This poem by No Ch'ŏnmyŏng describes a solitary visit to a mountain temple. The speaker feels a sense of unfamiliarity and unease upon arrival, comparing the silence to a graveyard. As night deepens, the sounds of the temple (wooden gong) and nature (water, cuckoo) blend with the speaker's introspection. Despite the serene Buddhist setting and the majestic moonlit mountain, the speaker feels detached and desolate, unable to connect fully with the surroundings. From a historical perspective, the poem, written in 1941 during a difficult colonial period, might reflect a personal sense of alienation, escapism into nature and tradition, or quiet introspection amidst broader societal turmoil. The blending of natural beauty with feelings of melancholy and detachment provides insight into the complex sentiments of an individual artist during that era.

山寺의 밤
						盧天命
一
머루순을 호올로 山속으로 들다

댕댕이 넝쿨이며 범부채에서
내어린날의 애기를 뒤지며
山ㅅ길을 걸어
절을 찾아드는 六月의 낮의
휘-하니 墓地처럼 寂寂하구나

도무지 와보지 못한곳
둘러보아도 낮선것뿐
저승을 가는길이 이런가싶어＜150＞
언짠은 생각이 또 든다

山寺의 밤이 깊어
庵子우에 佛心이 어리는데
어늬 禪房에선가 목탁소리……

눈감고 조용히 冥福을 비러준다

二
잠은 멀고 달은 밝고-
영창을 제치고 뜰로 나스다

한밤중 山ㅅ골 水聲이 요란한데
이슬나린 바위에 앉아 물소리 들노라면
山에서도 두견이 소리를 삼키며 우리

三更도 지났는데
法堂으로 올라가는 女僧하나
먹물드린 장삼깃에「無常」이 아롱진다

달아레 伽倻山이 밝고 으젓한데
나
山에도 절에도 붓지안는 마음……
귀곡새처럼 凄凉하다＜151＞
