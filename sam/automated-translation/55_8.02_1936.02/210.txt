Publication Date: 1936년 02월 01일
Article Title: 望향小曲 (A Short Song of Longing for Home)
Author: 尹克榮
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

望향小曲
						尹克榮
가도 가도 끗업는 저 하늘가에
구름 떼가 北으로 몰녀 가오니
님이여 山에 올나 마저 주소서
저 구름두 섧지요 울어나 주소

검프른 저 바다의 뱃전을 차고
갈맥이 떼 北으로 날너 가오니
님이여 치마폭에 안어 주소서
그 새두 섧지요 울어나 주소<97>

Even going on and on, towards that endless edge of the sky
A flock of clouds drifts northward (北),
My love, climb the mountain and greet them, please.
Those clouds too are sorrowful; please weep for them.

Kicking the side of the boat on that dark blue sea,
A flock of seagulls flies northward (北),
My love, embrace them in the folds of your skirt, please.
Those birds too are sorrowful; please weep for them.<97>

**Summary**

This short poem by Yun Kŭgyŏng expresses a deep yearning for home and a loved one, symbolized by the direction "North" (北). The speaker observes clouds and seagulls traveling north and asks their beloved ('님'), who is presumably there, to welcome and console these natural elements, projecting the speaker's own sorrow and homesickness onto them. From a historical perspective, the poem likely reflects the sentiments of Koreans displaced or separated from their homeland during the Japanese colonial period. The ambiguous '님' could represent a specific person, the homeland itself, or the Korean people collectively. Historians might find the poem's indirect expression of longing and sorrow (*han*), channeled through observations of nature heading towards Korea (North), indicative of the constrained emotional expression under colonial rule.
