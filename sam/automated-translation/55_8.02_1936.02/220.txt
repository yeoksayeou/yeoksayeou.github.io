(Light Essay) Scene from a Round-table Discussion of New and Old Women
						By Sin Puch'ul

Moderator: "Alright, let's begin the meeting now. Although the weather is unfortunately cold, I offer my sincere gratitude on behalf of the organizers for such a large turnout, making this event a great success.
As you know, the conflict between the new and the old is unavoidable in any era or society; it is an inevitable phenomenon in the process of social evolution.
In other words, from the perspective of the New Women, everything the Old Women do likely smells moldy. Conversely, from the perspective of the Old Women, much of what the New Women do is likely unsatisfactory. Therefore, this round-table discussion was organized in the hope that by exposing the rights and wrongs of each side under broad daylight, we can fortunately realize the strengths and weaknesses and contribute to our women's society. As it is also the beginning of the New Year, when we are all about to make a fresh start in our lives, although the issue may feel somewhat outdated, the 'women's question' as a social issue is always an old problem yet cannot help but be a new one, thus holding significant meaning. Now! Please speak frankly without any hesitation or formal constraints."<105>

Old Woman: "I am an Old Woman. Lately, observing the behavior of New Women, it seems they are gradually becoming more masculine.
I don't know if that's the latest fashion, but no matter how modern it is, a woman should fundamentally be womanly. Isn't it that a woman displays her human value as a woman by preserving her natural qualities (天品) and virtues?
Therefore, what is most needed for a woman is a somewhat beautiful and gentle demeanor, or a manner of blushing and showing embarrassment. If a woman lacks these things, she will not escape the mockery of being called a complete tomboy. My request to the New Women these days is, first and foremost, please don't develop such stick-like figures (장작개비 같은 體格)."

New Woman: "I am a New Woman. However, the issue just mentioned is surely a matter of degree, and possessing such coquetry (嬌態) is not an absolute condition for being a woman, is it? Moreover, for people of today's era, who prioritize healthy beauty over classical beauty, there's no need to exert all our energy on such trivial matters."

New [Women]: "Hear, hear! Hear, hear!"

Old [Woman]: "Hey, New Women, stop chattering and let's be a bit more composed."

New Woman: "I am a New Woman. When we New Women view the existence of Old Women, they are nothing more than feudal 'historical relics' (歷史的 遺物).
The Old Women of Chosŏn, regarding the morality of Confucius and Mencius (孔孟) – which has been the logical iron rule of the East for thousands of years – as heavenly decree, have lived without knowing how the world turns, seeing themselves merely as sexual appendages (性的 附隨物) to men, or in other words, merely as tools for bearing children.
In today's age, both men and women must be members of the household within the home and members of society within society. I want to urge the Old Women's society, which lacks initiative (進取性) and is consistently passive and retiring (退嬰一貫), to please quickly learn how the world is turning."

Old [Woman]: "Right, you spoke well! Yes, I am a completely aged Old Woman. Instead of just blaming us Old Women day and night for not knowing things, why don't you teach us? Just don't become those horrid 'modern girls' (모던껄)!"

Moderator: "Heh heh, please be quiet! This is not a place for speaking so recklessly. This isn't about arguing over personal feelings <106>, but about discussing how we can all live well together."

Old Woman: "I am an Old Woman. It seems one of the Old Women just now got overly excited, but I suspect that in her household too, there is an irresponsible New Woman, and the feelings of a mother who has always been resentful have erupted.
But in reality, we Old Women in Chosŏn, having been firmly dominated by outdated concepts like 'men are respected, women are lowly' (男尊女卑) and smelly ideas like the 'Three Obediences' (三從之道) or 'men and women should not sit together after the age of seven' (男女七歲不同席), have indeed spent regrettable and lamentable times in gloomy and desolate back rooms, living in a drunken dream (醉生夢死), ignorant of the true rise and fall (眞亡盛衰), prosperity, decline, joys, and sorrows (榮枯憂樂) of human society. Men and women should live together with united hearts and efforts for the sake of the family and society, but having reached this state, like a cart with one warped wheel (한쪽 박휘), the family or society could not help but become sick. When viewed in light of social development or the promotion of human happiness, how utterly heartbreaking (絶痛) this is, and from a woman's perspective, how resentful (怨痛) it is, enough to make one's back sore! However, in this era of reforming all worldly affairs, while knowing it is something that must naturally be corrected, we do not know how to correct it. We sincerely ask the New Women's side to provide much guidance."

Old [Woman]: "I am also an Old Woman. Now, as that person just mentioned, it is indeed pathetic that Old Women have reached a point of self-abandonment (自抛自棄), even denying their own existence. But conversely, New Women, no matter how much new learning (신학문) they have acquired and become literary masters (文章博士) who know everything about the world, really need to have self-respect (自重함). Yet, it seems to be the norm that they often pretend to know everything when they know nothing, cannot distinguish water from fire, are rash (輕率), full of vanity (虛榮心), and lack composure (鎭重치). This cannot but be called abnormal reckless behavior (變態的 妄動) stemming from a sense of pride in representing the era without the awakening necessary to digest sociality.
In other words, it is only because of the benevolent existence of the 'era' that these immature New Women receive the benefit of being defended and having their merits highlighted. But viewed as individuals, how can New Women possibly match the character (인격) of Old Women?"

Old [Women]: "(Applause) Hear! Hear! That feels so refreshing!"

New [Woman]: "Old Women, please be quiet. We didn't come here to argue about who is better or worse, but to study the world. <107>
The era of you Old Women, who believed you could gracefully end your lives simply by respectfully setting down your father-in-law's (시아버니) dining table, being careful not to talk back to your mother-in-law (시어머니), and not fighting with your sister-in-law (시누이), has all been blown away by the winds of automobiles, streetcars, and the sound of the radio. Know that this is a world where you will face embarrassment at every turn and disgrace wherever you go if you do not learn new behaviors and new etiquette! Those who suffer embarrassment and disgrace on the streets, in government offices (官廳), theaters, lecture halls, or at train stations and inside streetcars are all Old Women. Please understand that people must adapt their lives according to the times."

Moderator: "Oh, right! I am the Moderator, but indeed, it's true that there are many such people among the Old Women! Some lower their eyes and walk too demurely on crowded streets, meeting unexpected accidents (橫厄), but also, housewives (안악네들) untrained in this splendid city get bewildered, glancing left and right,
'Hey, look at that!'
'What is that!'
'Oh my, what a world!'
and end up bumping into streetcars or automobiles and falling, stepping on others' feet, or inside streetcars or buses,
'Ma'am, please come in!'
'Child, get off quickly!'
'Oh dear, please stop!'
'[muttering] some kind of ditty or tune...'
These are rather mild cases. What about going to places like theaters or crowded gatherings with infants and letting them cry, or laughing boisterously telling unrelated stories during a tragedy (悲劇)!"

Old [Woman]: "No, excuse me! As a man, and moreover, as the Moderator of this event, you should just moderate. Instead, you're rattling off criticisms (험담) of us Old Women like shelling peas (콩 복듯). Are you saying you're the representative of the New Women? What kind of attitude is this?"

Moderator: "Not at all! How could that be? But since I started talking, I'll just say one more thing and stop. Besides, isn't it all the same now that I've started speaking? It's pitiful enough that some Old Women are good at shamanic rituals (무당판수) and holding memorial services (고사), or proudly display things like chamber pots (요강) where people can see them, but when they mix seasoned vegetables (나물) and taste them for seasoning, they tilt their heads back towards the sky, stick their tongues out a foot long..."

Old [Women]: "Stop it already! (Laughter, laughter, laughter...)"

Old [Woman]: "Alright, let's stop laughing! I am an Old Woman. These days, New Women often become the root cause (張本) of household troubles, causing distress to their husbands because they try to live luxuriously (奢侈) according to their ideals without considering their standard of living or their husband's income. <108>
Also, it's said that New Women, who supposedly know better because they are educated, lead discordant lives – lives that don't fit. In a Korean household where there is a crock stand (장독대) and an old man smoking a long bamboo pipe (長竹), they should act accordingly. But the sight of them, in strange, fancy clothes and bobbed hair, ducking into a thatched-roof house, just doesn't seem right no matter how you look at it. No matter how good Western style is, isn't it absurd to pour milk into red bean porridge (팟죽) or eat diced radish kimchi (까뚝이 - 깍두기) speared with a trident (삼시창 - likely a metaphor for a fork?)?
No matter how much you build a New Style world, you cannot do it without building upon the foundation of the Old Style. Yet, these New Women blindly imitate (模倣) new trends, thus despising our own things and senselessly wasting (부즈럽시 - 부질없이) resources in life. In any case, it seems New Women are generally inept at housekeeping (살림)."

Old [Women]: "Hear! Hear!"

New [Woman]: "I am a New Woman. What? New Women are bad at housekeeping? My goodness, you're saying all sorts of things now. That must be targeting women from the vagrant class (浮浪層) on one side; it can't be generalized. If a New Woman sets her mind to housekeeping, she does it better than an Old Woman. With a stronger economic sense (經濟的 觀念) for managing the household budget (家計), a deep sense of hygiene (衛生觀念), and having received social cultivation (敎養), she can guide children's education culturally (文化的). In all matters (凡百事), how can you say a New Woman cannot manage a household as well as an Old Woman?"

New [Women]: "Hear! Hear!"

Old [Woman]: "What? You mean you know how to cook rice as deliciously as we do, and make clothes as modestly as we do? In short, you New Women may know in theory, but you don't know in practice."

New [Woman]: "I am also a New Woman. What the Old Woman just said is very impressive, but I believe Old Women often make mistakes in raising (敎養) children because they lack scientific knowledge."

Old [Woman]: "I am an Old Woman. What? What did you say? If we raised our children incorrectly, then you New Women, who are at most like second or third daughters in age, under whose hand do you think you grew up?"

New [Woman]: "You shouldn't speak like that. That's not what I meant. Raising children <109> isn't just about giving breast milk or food, or changing clothes according to the season. In some severe cases, overly stubborn mothers prevent children from practicing even what they learned at school – this happens in countless ways, which is truly regrettable."

New [Woman]: "Speaking as a New Woman, the Old Women of Chosŏn have lived in ancient dreams within prison-like inner quarters (閨中). How could their gait be as lively as that of New Women? They lack the bright manner of speech and intonation, or the clear and neat demeanor (淸楚한 태도) that further enhances feminine beauty. This is likely why their husbands' affection towards their wives (안해) cools, leading to mistreatment and eventually divorce, or it causes the husband to lose interest in the home and finally fall into extramarital affairs (외도)."

Old [Woman]: "I am an Old Woman. What the New Woman's side is saying now seems too narrow-minded (所見), knowing only one side of the story and not the other. Indeed, New Women with sociable clothing styles, bright eyes and white teeth (明眸皓齒), the charm of a smiling face, and coquetry (애교) capable of melting even a bronze head and iron body (銅頭鐵身), may know cunning ways (卑怯한 아첨 수단) to gain the favor (寵愛) and win the hearts (환심) of modern men. However, there are social disturbances caused by such temptation (誘惑). They become the root cause (張本) of corrupting (타락) promising young men (有爲한 청년). And the household discord mentioned by the New Women's side – it cannot be denied that storms that bring blizzards into homes enjoying the spring breeze are often caused by the seduction of women like you."

New [Woman]: "No! Where did such derailed remarks come from? And that is a fatal insult (致命的 侮辱) to all New Women! We do not submit to such nonsensical abusive language (沒常識한 倣言 - 방언)! Watch your words!"

Old [Woman]: "No, hey, New Woman! What I said was definitely not directed at all New Women. Similarly, I know that when you criticize the Old Women's side from the beginning, you are not referring to all Old Women. And it goes without saying that the points we are raising are based on extremely one-sided facts occurring on the New Women's side. For your future well-being, reviewing the shortcomings and weaknesses of the past and present for awakening (覺醒), liquidation (淸算), and correction and improvement (矯正改善) is absolutely appropriate. Even if you feel agitated <110> and insulted, please understand the deep affection with which we tell you these things, with hearts full of tears, to you who are like our young sisters or daughters! We are already dead bodies, but don't you young people have a heavy responsibility?"

Moderator: "Indeed, those are righteous words."
Being a New Woman is not just about following the latest fashion trends of Western actresses in makeup and clothing, roaming department stores or the drawing rooms of wealthy men, and chattering about modern personality, New Sensibility (新感覺派) art, or whether it's acceptable not to know about shallow literary theories, music, or social dancing.
And it is an extremely unfortunate fact that even among the so-called 'intellectual' (인테리) class women with considerable cultivation, while they advocate for women's rights expansion (女權擴張) and the women's movement with their mouths and pens, exemplary figures actively working on the practical stage are rare.
Wouldn't it be so appreciated if, before knowing the price of cosmetics (化粧品), they first knew how much a doe of rice costs; if, before reading romance novels (연애소설), they first read the third page news articles in the newspaper; if they woke from the dream of only playing the piano in a modern house (문화주택), grounded themselves in genuine real life, wiped their children's runny noses for their sake, and became gracious wives and mothers who raise them to see the world clearly? Then the problem would be solved.
I apologize profusely for saying so many disrespectful things. If only we had ample time today,
we could have discussed important issues like the Concubinage Problem (蓄妾問題) and Women's Awakening,
the Marriage Problem,
also the Divorce Problem (離婚問題) and Remarriage Problem (再婚問題), and the Working Women Problem (職業婦人問題). But inevitably, we shall try to hold another session at the next opportunity. Thank you very much for sharing so many beneficial and interesting stories today despite your fatigue. This concludes the meeting. Now! Shall we go down to the restaurant downstairs for some rice cake soup (떡국)?
All: "(Applause)" <111>

**Summary**

This 1936 article presents a fictional round-table discussion between "New Women" and "Old Women" in Korea, moderated by a man. The discussion vividly portrays the clash between traditional Confucian values (filial piety, domesticity, modesty) upheld by the Old Women and the modern, Western-influenced ideals (education, social participation, equality, modern appearance) embraced by the New Women. Arguments range from appearance and behavior, household management, child-rearing, knowledge of the world, to the causes of marital strife. The moderator ultimately sides somewhat with the Old Women's criticisms, urging New Women towards practicality and domestic responsibility over superficial modernity while acknowledging the need for change. From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects the social transformations and tensions occurring under Japanese rule, where Western modernity was being adopted alongside enduring traditional Korean norms. It highlights the complex negotiation of female identity, the perceived impacts of education and urbanization, and the debates surrounding women's roles in a rapidly changing society, all within the context of colonialism which itself influenced the nature and pace of these changes. Historians might find the specific criticisms (e.g., inept housekeeping, vanity vs. traditional virtues, views on education and public behavior) and the expressed anxieties from both sides revealing about social pressures and gender dynamics of the period.

(漫文) 新舊女性 座談會 風景
						申不出
司會者 「자-이제부터 開會하겟읍니다. 날세가 공교로히도 치운데 이러케 많이 오셔서 盛況을 일우워주시니 主催側을 대표해서 衷心으로 感謝를 올립니다.
