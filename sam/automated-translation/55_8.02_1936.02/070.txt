Magazine Title: 三千里 제8권 제2호
Publication Date: 1936-02-01
Article Title: 우리의 第一主義는? (What is Our First Principle?)
Author: Cho Man-sik, Sin Hŭng-u, An Chae-hong, Yu Ŏk-kyŏm, O Kŭng-sŏn, Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Chu Un-sŏng, Im Wŏn-gŭn, Yi In, Ku Cha-ok, Wŏn Se-hun, Yi Hun-gu, Kim T'ae-hyŏp, Ch'a Sang-ch'an, Yi Suk-chong, Kim Ch'ang-je, Pak Ch'ang-hun, Song Pong-u, Ko Yŏng-hwan, Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng, Hŏ Hŏn, Ch'ae P'il-gŭn, Hyŏn Sang-yun
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

What is Our First Principle?

Organizing a Central Body

Cho Man-sik

The most urgent thing we need is to have a central organization. If we organize this well and lead with unified control, everything else will surely become easy.

Organizing a Body is Urgent

Sin Hŭng-u

Regarding what your company asked, education, industry, self-cultivation, organizing bodies—is there anything among these that our society does not need? However, if I were to pick just one among them, I believe that the most urgently needed thing right now is the organization of a central body. This is because the Korean people are exceedingly lacking in organizational training.<17>

Education First Principle

An Chae-hong

① In our thinking, what should advance as our first principle must become the immediate standard for our internal and external life. All new principles and policies must, for the moment, depart from the common need for Koreans (朝鮮人) to overcome [challenges to] existence, and from the emotion and impulse thereof.
② In terms of practical policy, we must proceed with an education-first principle in the broad sense. While the expansion and dissemination of education in various fields for all levels of men and women is necessary in pure school education, practical and implementable education and lessons extending to politics, industry, and various other areas are extremely valuable. Adults and seniors require instruction in some sense. In years past (왕년), I once advocated a theory called the "Instructive Formation Movement."<19>

With an Education First Principle

Yu Ŏk-kyŏm

We must proceed with an education-first principle. As the old saying goes, "Even jade buried deep in blue mountains (靑山) shines only when polished," by what means can human intellectual development (인지계발) be achieved if not through education? This is because I declare with certainty that without the power of education, whether it be industrial development, methods for literacy promotion, training in organizational structure, or anything else, the intended goal cannot possibly be reached.

Strength First

O Kŭng-sŏn

We must proceed based on practical strength (實力本位).<21>

Perhaps Education

Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi

Education, industry, literacy promotion, organizational structure, etc.—there is nothing among these that we do not need, so determining the order of first and second is impossible, but I think if we make education the first principle, the others will become accompanying conditions.

With a Principle of Mutual Love

Chu Un-sŏng

What I always deeply feel is the Principle of Mutual Love (相愛主義) and the Principle of Credit First (信用第一主義). Upon these two principles, education, industry, unity, everything will go well.

Advancing Together is Good

Im Wŏn-gŭn

Since everything is urgent, it seems there's no choice but to proceed together, even if just a little bit at a time.<23>

Starting with a Research Institution

Yi In

In Chosŏn, which is like a wasteland (황무지), wouldn't everything be urgent? But above all, conscious living (의식적 생활) must be the most urgent matter of all (緊中之緊). One might say, "We ate and wore clothes, so haven't we lived?" but if you think about it carefully, these days, doubt truly arises whether one is alive or dead. Isn't there some (엇든) way to emphasize conscious living? I wish there were an institution where we could all research this point together. Only after that, wouldn't marvelous principles (妙理) emerge in all things?

Education First

Ku Cha-ok

Education First Principle<41>

Survival Maintenance First

Wŏn Se-hun

If the two characters "we" (우리) refer not to a specific individual but are a pronoun referring to "Korean people" (朝鮮사람), then what should be the first principle can be sufficiently guessed without waiting for my answer. What is the use of stating a subjective view that objective circumstances (객관적 정세) do not permit (不許)? For the Korean people, of whom there are over 430,000 destitute peasants (細窮民) in Hamgyŏng Namdo (咸鏡南道) alone, isn't maintaining survival the most urgent task? To maintain that, of course, one must plan for the livelihood of food, clothing, and shelter; one must also provide education to pitiful children; one must also spread literacy to the illiterate masses; and one must also engage in collective life based on division of labor (分工分作的). Be that as it may, I think it is proper (可하다) to establish the first principle only after contemplating anew the old idiom, "When food and clothing are sufficient, etiquette is known" (衣食足而知禮節).<43>

Education is First

Yi Hun-gu

I will speak for the education-first principle. This is because activities in religion, politics, industry, and society cannot be done without receiving education.

We, Through Education

Kim T'ae-hyŏp

We must proceed with an education-first principle. Learning is power, and knowing is power; without bringing forth this power, it is difficult to act like a proper human being.

Money First Principle

Ch'a Sang-ch'an

Above all else, we must proceed with a Money First Principle (金錢第一主義). Education, industry, and anything else, without money, are just empty illusions and fruitless toil (虛想忘勞) that will produce no effect whatsoever.<45>

Through Education Dissemination

Yi Suk-chong

As the first principle and urgent priority measure, I would say it is the dissemination of education that reality demands.

Industry is First

Kim Ch'ang-je

No matter what is said, since "The people regard food as heaven" (民은 以食爲天), shall we call it the industry-first principle? Calling it the "Principle of Diligence" (勤勞主義) would be good.

Both Industry and Education

Pak Ch'ang-hun

Advance Industry and Education together (並進).<47>

That Level Is...

Song Pong-u

Regarding the first principle your company asked about, none of them are the first principle for me, so I will not speak.

Through Education

Ko Yŏng-hwan

We must, above all, proceed with the "Education First Principle," I think. Why? Because the fundamental reason industry is underdeveloped (부진) is the fault (탓) of not knowing well the trends of the times (時勢) and the state of affairs (物情); that there are many (만흔) illiterate people is, in the end, synonymous (異語同義) with saying education has not been disseminated; and that organizational structures are not well-formed is also inevitably due to a lack of culture/refinement (교양), I believe.<49>

Through Literacy Promotion

Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng

This and that all need to be done, but first, literacy promotion.

From the Central Body

Hŏ Hŏn

Organization. The formation (組成) of a central body is the very mother and the very shortcut (近道) for all affairs in our society. Leaders must quickly establish a plan upon which all hearts (萬心) can unite for this purpose; I believe the time has come to move forward.

Industry is Most Necessary

Ch'ae P'il-gŭn

The several things (멫가지) you asked about are all necessary, but for the time being, industry seems to be the most urgent among them.<51>

If Only Education Goes Well

Hyŏn Sang-yun
I think education is first. Industry, business, activities—all are necessary and also urgent. However, if only education goes well, those things will inevitably and incidentally all turn out well on their own (저절로). Therefore, the urgent task for Chosŏn at present (現下) lies in education.<53>

**Summary**

This 1936 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine asked various prominent Korean figures what "First Principle" (第一主義) Korean society should prioritize. Responses varied widely, reflecting diverse perspectives on the most pressing issues facing colonial Korea. Major themes included the need for a unified central organization (Cho Man-sik, Sin Hŭng-u, Hŏ Hŏn), the paramount importance of education (An Chae-hong, Yu Ŏk-kyŏm, Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Ku Cha-ok, Yi Hun-gu, Kim T'ae-hyŏp, Yi Suk-chong, Ko Yŏng-hwan, Hyŏn Sang-yun), focusing on practical strength or economic means (O Kŭng-sŏn, Ch'a Sang-ch'an, Kim Ch'ang-je, Ch'ae P'il-gŭn), ensuring basic survival (Wŏn Se-hun), promoting literacy (Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng), and fostering mutual love and trust (Chu Un-sŏng). Some advocated for parallel progress (Im Wŏn-gŭn, Pak Ch'ang-hun) or preliminary research (Yi In). For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of elite Korean discourse during the mid-colonial period, revealing anxieties about disunity, underdevelopment, and basic survival, alongside competing visions for national regeneration centered on organization, education, or economic power under Japanese rule. The emphasis on "survival" (Wŏn Se-hun) and "money" (Ch'a Sang-ch'an) starkly highlights the perceived dire economic conditions.

團體組織이 急務

申興雨

貴社에서 물으신 바, 교육 산업, 수양, 단체조직 등이 모두가 어느 하나 우리 사회에서 필요치 안흔 것이 잇겟슴니까? 그러나 그 중에서도 하나를 말한다면, 그래도 現今에 안저서 제일 시급히 필요한 것은 중심단체조직일 줄로 암니다. 조선사람은 넘우나 단체적 훈련을 못바든 사람들이무로서임니다.<17>

敎育第一主義

安在鴻

① 우리의 사상에서 우리 제일주의로 나아가야 할 것이 당면에서 對內, 對外의 생활상의 標幟가 되어야 합니다. 모든 새로운 주의와 정책이 당면해서는 즉 朝鮮人으로서의 공통한 생존타개의 필요와 및 그 감격과 충동에서 출발하게 되는 것입니다.
② 실제 정책방면에서는 廣義인 교육제일주의로 나가야 합니다. 순수한 학교교육에서도 남녀각층, 各科敎育의 확충, 보급을 요하려니와 정치, 산업 其他各般에 뻗히어 실천실시적인 교육 교훈이 매우 귀중합니다. 성인, 선배붙어가 어느 의미의 교훈을 요합니다. 나는 왕년 『교훈적 결성운동』이라는 論을 주장한 일이 잇습니다.<19>

敎育第一主義로

兪億兼

교육제일주의로 나가야 하겟슴니다. 古語에 이른바와 갓치 『靑山 속에 뭇친 玉도 다드머야만 光彩가 난다』는 것 갓치 인지계발을 교육에 의치 아니하고 무엇으로 가히 하겟슴니까. 산업의 발달, 문자보급의 방도, 단체조직의 훈련, 어나 것이나 교육의 힘 업시난 所期의 목적을 능히 달할 수 업다고 확언하는 까닭에.

硏究機關부터

李仁

황무지같은 朝鮮에 무엇할 것 없이 다 긴급한 일뿐이겟지마는 무엇보다 앞서는 것은 의식적 생활이 가장 緊中之緊이겟다. 밥먹고 옷입엇스니 살지 아니햇나 하겟지마는 가마니 생각해보면 요새 같해서야 참말 살앗나 죽엇나 의심이 생긴다. 意識的 생활을 강조할 엇든 방법이 없슬가? 이 점에 대하야 다같이 연구할 기관 하나 잇섯스면 함니다. 그런 뒤에야 만가지에 다 妙理가 생길 것이 안일가.

우리는 두 字가 어느 특정적 개인을 갈으친 말이 아니고 『朝鮮사람』을 갈으친 대명사라면 무엇을 제일주의로 하여야 올흘 것은 나의 대답을 기달이지 아니하고도 넉넉히 짐작할 것이다. 객관적 정세가 不許하는 주관을 말하면 무엇하는가뇨? 咸鏡南道에만 細窮民이 43만명이상이라고 하는 朝鮮사람으로서는 생존유지가 제일 급무인가 하오. 그것을 유지하자면 물론 의식주의 생계를 도모하여야 할 것이오. 가련한 자녀에서 교육도 주어야 할 것이며 문맹대중에게 문자의 보급도 식혀야 할 것이며 分工分作的 단체생활도 하여야 할 것이지오. 이러나 저러나 『衣食足而知禮節』이라는 옛날 숙어를 다시곰 含味한 뒤에 제일주의를 세우는 것이 可하다고 생각하오.<43>
