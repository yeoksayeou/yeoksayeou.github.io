Impressions of Works by Domestic and International Writers Recently Read

Kim Tong-hwan-(From Samch'ŏlli)- I am delighted to have this opportunity to gather seven female writers active in the poetry and fiction scenes to discuss various issues in our literary world. As you can see, in the New Year's issues of newspapers and magazines, about 40 works by various writers have flooded the scene. Moreover, the activities of KAPF-affiliated writers who returned from Chŏnju continue to appear. In terms of both quality and quantity, I believe there hasn't been a time as vibrant as this New Year's literary scene in the past 4 or 5 years. Therefore, I believe you must have much to say about the works produced, the writers, and the literary scene at this opportunity. So, first, I hope you will speak frankly about your impressions of recently published works and the tendencies of writers. However, even if it's about works, since the purpose wasn't a joint review meeting, please just share your simple impressions, even if it's just a cursory glance (走馬看山格).

Pak Hwa-sŏng- Among the domestic and international works I've recently read, none have left a particularly strong impression on my mind, but even if there are one or two, each has different characteristics, so I can't make a definitive statement. As for *sijo*, I enjoyed reading Cho Un's *Wansan Ch'iryŏng* (完山七咏) (Chogwang No. 2) and Nosan's *Hyapchihaeng* (*Sijo* from Hyapchi).
But since the topic came up, let me say something about Sim Hun's *Sangnoksu* (常綠樹). I originally (though I collect my own humble works (拙作)) have never collected newspaper novels. However, for *Sangnoksu*, because the Tong-a Ilbo's requirements were such, I thought it would surely feature notable scenes from farming and fishing villages and profound living conditions, so hoping there might be important reference material, I collected it diligently. But to this day, I haven't been able to grasp any material worth referencing. Perhaps something will emerge in the future...
However, despite many unnatural passages, and although the basic direction of the work differs from my thinking, there are moments that occasionally move the heart. There were times I wanted to cultivate Mr. Sim's passion.

Paek Sin-ae- Perhaps because I haven't read widely, I haven't read any work with deep admiration.<215>
I haven't read many foreign works either, but my impression after reading is just that I, too, must study diligently without rest.

Kim Tong-hwan- Instead of speaking like this, let's proceed in order, focusing our critique on newspaper serial novels and the creative works published in the New Year's issues of various magazines. Generally, this time, Samch'ŏlli magazine exceptionally carried eight works, so let's start from there. First, what do you think about Yŏm Sang-sŏp's "Silchik" (失職)?

Chang Tŏk-cho- We've only encountered half of "Silchik," and since it's set to continue in the next issue, perhaps we can discuss the writer's recent tendencies, but it seems right to postpone the critique of the work itself until later.

Kim Tong-hwan- Then, what about Chang Hyŏk-chu's "Kyeyak" (契約)?

Mo Yun-suk- Regarding Chang Hyŏk-chu's work, it has traditionally been the subject of various criticisms. Looking at his recent work, setting aside whether it's good or bad, first, the descriptive passages (地文) have many awkward parts, as if they were direct translations (直譯) of foreign works.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Anyone would probably get that feeling. He's not yet accustomed to Korean writing, and the focal point (燃燒点) of his work isn't clear. He fails to grasp a single phenomenon concretely, merely resorting to narrative, flat description (平面描寫). Frankly speaking, I wish he would return to his regional (鄕土的) roots and start anew.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Compared to first-rate writers (一流作家), his conception (構想) and narration are immature. However, the awkwardness in his technique (手法) is immediately noticeable. It seems it would be good for him to study the Korean language more and strive to understand Korean sentiment (情調).

Kim Tong-hwan- Chŏn Yŏng-t'aek's "Omuni"?

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- The work has become rather rambling (散漫). It seems he hasn't shown much progress (進境) from his earlier works.

Chang Tŏk-cho- The scale (스켈-) is not large, and it feels like he skipped over events too lightly in advancing the plot. It can only be called a work outside the scope of discussion (問題外).

Kim Tong-hwan- Then, Yu Chin-o's "Hwangnyul" (黃栗)?

Mo Yun-suk- It's more of a clean short piece (小品) or sketch (스켓치) than a novel. The subject matter treated was too simple (單純) to become a novel.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- One always feels the charm (魅力) in his writing style (筆致), but with "Hwangnyul," no distinct impression remained after reading (讀后).

Kim Tong-hwan- Paek Sin-ae's "Haksa" (學士)?

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Without unnatural passages, it deals commonsensically (常識的) with subject matter (題材) often found in daily life, and no slight irrationality is felt. However, it lacks deep flavor, and the power to stir (衝動) the heart is weak (稀薄).<216>

Chang Tŏk-cho- Paek's work is shown after a long time since its appearance in "Kaebyŏk" (開闢), and it's good that the narrative style (筆致) isn't mixed with even the slightest hint of forcefulness. It shows the potential (素質) to grow wider and larger in the future, like a growing tree. Although this time it was merely a personal story (身邊小說).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Yi Hyo-sŏk's "San" (산) - although the theme (테-마) is treated primitively (原始的), its writing style is bright (明朗), fresh (新鮮), and gentle, making it a beautiful work. It's a cleanly executed short story (短篇) that leaves a favorable impression (好感).

Mo Yun-suk- Yes, Hyo-sŏk's works, whichever one you look at, are new, bright (明朗), and without fault (欠節). Recently, the profound (深刻) and gloomy (陰散) atmosphere (空氣) seen in works like "Noryŏng Kŭnhae" (露領近海) is gradually diminishing.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Wherever it was published, the latter half (下半部) of Hyo-sŏk's "Sŏnghwa" (聖火) is also very good.

Kim Tong-hwan- How about Chang Tŏk-cho's "Han Kyohun" (한 敎訓) in "Samch'ŏlli"?

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Her talent (才致) shines on the surface (表面). In whichever work one looks at, I admire her technique (手法) of crafting works smoothly and skillfully without strain. This one too pulls you along with tension (緊張) until the end.<217>

Mo Yun-suk- Her previous "Ŏmŏniwa Ttal" (어머니와 딸) was also good. The ending was well done. I thought the *kisaeng* (妓生) appearing in it was a married woman. Her knowledge (智識) of *kisaeng* seems considerable (相當). Yi T'ae-jun's "Kamagwi" (가마귀) is also good. The subject matter (題材) was ordinary (平凡)—his works are always easy to understand, and the description has a fragrance (香氣). It's refreshing (삿듯하지요 - older spelling for 산뜻하지요).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- The poignant (沈痛) flavor one might find in Nordic writers (北歐作家) cannot be found. I like works with a large scale (스켈), somewhat rough yet with a flash of light (빗) from some corner (모통이).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- There is one new writer. It's Kim Yu-jŏng (金裕貞). His works, whichever one, are flawless (欠잡을 것이 업습데다).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Yes, he is worthy of attention. Looking at his several pieces in the New Year's issues of various magazines, a fresh taste flows through them.

Mo Yun-suk- "Sangnoksu" (常綠樹), which incorporates the most regional fragrance (鄕土的 香氣) into the work, was the best among the recent newspaper novels.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- However, the contrivance (作爲) for the newspaper's literacy campaign was too conspicuous, making it unpleasant. It's unpleasant for literature (文學) to become some kind of tool (道具).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Well, since it comes with the condition (條件付) of matching the newspaper's intention, it can't be helped. As mentioned earlier, we too would like to know about the *kisaeng* society once.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Indeed. Their view of life (人生觀), their view of love (戀愛觀), etc., would likely be interesting (興味).

Mo Yun-suk- Looking at Kim Tong-in's "Yŏin" (女人), it seems he treated it quite profoundly (深刻). For Tong-in, works like the old "Paettaragi" (배따라기) were his peak. Nowadays, he's wrestling with low-toned (低調) popular tales (野談).

Chang Tŏk-cho- Generally, recent male writers don't know how to portray intellectual women (인테리女性).

Mo Yun-suk- Overall, their view of modern women (現代女性觀) is shallow (淺薄).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Especially Ham Tae-hun's view of women, it would be better if he just stopped. But despite everyone, Yi Kwang-su's work flows smoothly and is interesting. It's regrettable that his novels serialized in the newspapers are not treated as a significant issue (問題視) in the literary world.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Nowadays, if a long novel appears in the newspaper, it seems people tend not to read each other's work. Since they are inherently newspaper novels, they inevitably lean towards entertainment value (興味本位), so nothing particularly astonishing (驚歎) is expected (期待) from the start.

Kim Tong-hwan- Hong Myŏng-hŭi's "Imkkŏkjŏngjŏn" (林巨正傳)?

Mo Yun-suk- The language is truly abundant (豊富). And the Korean sentiment (情調) of the medieval period (中世紀時代) is revealed like a mirror. Therefore, in terms of studying language and sentiment,<218> there is truly much for us juniors (后輩) to learn.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- However, "Imkkŏkjŏngjŏn" is storytelling (講談), not a novel. How can you call it a novel when it just seeks out and weaves together a storyline (줄거리)?

Kim Tong-hwan- Are there no comments regarding *sijo* and new poetry?

Mo Yun-suk- Chŏng In-bo's *sijo* in Samch'ŏlli lacked energy, while Nosan's *Hyapchihaeng* was good.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- However, *sijo*, originally considered representative even among short poetic forms (短時型), doesn't fit the breath (呼吸) of modern people (現代人). I doubt (疑問) how much development (發展) can be shown with that fixed-form poetry (定型詩).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- By the way, I meant to say this earlier, but for instance, Yi T'ae-jun's works are elegant and clear, but passages that strike the heart are rare. Compared to that, Yi Ki-yŏng's "Kohyang" (故鄕), although rough and coarse (엉성하다), conversely allows one to receive the impact (衝擊) often seen in great writers. What impressed me further about Minch'on (Yi Ki-yŏng's pen name) is a passage in *Kohyang* where two widows (寡婦) fight over a cow; they exchange long streams of curses (辱) over 3 or 4 installments, yet it's not vulgar (野卑) at all, and he carries it through to the end. That technique (手法) was skillful.

Mo Yun-suk- Pak Hwa-sŏng's "Han'gwe" (旱魁) was good. "Hongsu" (洪水) is also a recent<219> masterpiece (傑作). However, she has a tendency to deliberately contrive (作爲) putting proletarian (푸로) consciousness (意識) into her work. Because of this, there seems a danger (危險) of damaging (傷) the work.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Her writing style (筆致) seems masculine (男性的). And "Hongsu" had the suspicion (嫌) of seeming formulaic (公式).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- It's good that Hwa-sŏng uses a certain consciousness (意識) as a weapon to press forward (肉迫). She also observes farming village life meticulously (삿삿치). However, we wish she would explore (開拓) realms that only women can portray, not just what men can portray.

Chang Tŏk-cho- I want to evaluate (評價) Kang Kyŏng-ae higher than Pak Hwa-sŏng. She is a striving writer.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Reading "In'ganmunje" (人間問題), it seems she truly dissects (解剖) and studies (硏究) various problems of human society profoundly (深刻).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Kim Mal-bong's novels seemed ordinary (平凡). I wish each writer possessed a unique realm (特異한 境地) that others cannot express.

Mo Yun-suk- It seems she's heading in the proletarian (푸로) direction. Her conception (構想) has a large scale. Generally, Korean women must first become writers who skillfully portray historical psychology (歷史的 心理).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Han Yong-un's "Heukp'ung" (黑風) is not novelistic at all, and its description seems detached from the atmosphere (空氣) and emotions (感情) of the era.

Mo Yun-suk- I had that feeling too.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Yi Mu-yŏng's writing style (筆致), in trying too hard to be cutting-edge (尖端), sometimes becomes too shallow (야잣한 데가 잇습데다 - interpretation: shallow/superficial).

Mo Yun-suk- Whatever one says, the great harvest (收獲) recently is Chŏng Chi-yong's Poetry Collection (鄭芝鎔詩集). There are truly many passages deserving of high praise (三唱三歎).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Yes. It's truly good. The language is abundant (豊富) and beautiful, and the sensibility (感覺) is sharp (銳敏).

Messages for the Promotion of the Female Literary Circle, Addressed to Newspaper and Magazine Companies, Ewha Womans College (梨專) Literature Department, and Male Society

Pak Hwa-sŏng- Well now. Regarding what I want to say to newspaper and magazine companies for the promotion (振興) of the female literary circle... there's so much to say generally (全般的) for our literary world (文壇),<220> so I'd rather leave it at that. But what I want to say to the Ewha Womans College (梨專) Literature Department is, given that the school is the only women's college in this land of Korea (朝鮮), I hope the school authorities (學校當局) will ensure that in the future, instead of just letting students get drunk (醉) on poetry and prose from a hazy (몽농한) dream world, they show them the living reality (現實) of Korea, so that powerful laughter and cries (외침) can emerge from them. Also, what I want to say to general society (一般社會) is, please stop saying things like, "Female writers should write feminine works," or "Write works that only women can write." Goodness, when writing, why make such strict distinctions based on gender (性別)? Why is it then that male writers write not only things that only men can supposedly write but also delve into all sorts of profound points that even women might write poorly? While they themselves "recklessly" (?) write things women might write, they tell women to write only what women should write – this really doesn't make sense (말句가 아니다) from the start. I can't speak at length. I'll try to express my feelings about this on another occasion, but anyway, when so-called literary figures (文人) who seem like they should have understanding (理解) say such things often, my mind reels. The statement, "After writing a work, because she is female, she indeed grasped something a male couldn't see," is valid, but the advice to strive<221> from the outset to write "feminine" works is truly nonsensical (意味不通). In any case, striving to write works that are truly works, valuable and rewarding, should be the common hope (希望) of all writers. And I absolutely detest it when people approach works by female writers with a preconceived sneer (빗죽이다), already discounting them. After all (大關節), what's the point of making such a fuss (그악스럽게 야단칠 게 뭐란 말입니까?), constantly bringing up the "female" (女流女流하며 게집 女子만) aspect?

Paek Sin-ae- This is what I think. Regarding this issue, rather than making demands (要望) externally (對外的), first, those engaged in literature themselves must not be blinded by the small honors (榮譽) visible before their eyes, but must devote all their energy (全力) to researching calmly (침중이) and steadily (꾸준이).

Mo Yun-suk- Nevertheless, I have heaps (수두룩하게) of issues I want to shout loudly about and protest (抗議) to the outside world (박갓해). Firstly (첫재), as Pak Hwa-sŏng said, please don't treat female writers with mere curiosity (好奇心). In male society, they readily apply a handicap (핸듸캡), saying "because she's a woman," and deliberately make an issue if a work is written by a woman. Even when newspapers and magazines give the treatment (待遇) they rightfully deserve (當然), troublemakers (말성조와하는 이들) claim they've given too much preferential treatment (優待) to a trivial work just because it's by a woman, or when Critic A passes by after giving a fair (正當) critique, Critic B pops up out of nowhere (생뚱가치) writing a rebuttal (駁論) to A, playing with words (文字를 戱弄) that insult (侮辱) female writers – there are truly many times I feel indignant (憤).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- And because they don't view women as whole personalities (人格視), they fabricate (僞造) non-existent gossip (까십) as they please and chatter about this and that. When I hear this, there are many times I truly want revenge (復讐) against men. It's solely the male side that provokes (挑發) the struggle (鬪爭) between the sexes (兩性).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Because they think of women as licorice in medicine (藥에 甘草로 아니까요 - i.e., just an accessory).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- In reality (실상), looking at this society, both the literary world and general society, while pretending to give preferential treatment (優待) to women, in truth, they neither give opportunities (機會) nor lend the stage (舞臺). Men monopolize (獨占) everything, whether occupations or anything else. Women are treated as if they were parasites (寄生虫).

Kim Tong-hwan- You seem to have many complaints (不平). Please don't speak abstractly (抽象的), but concretely (具體的). What you all say might be right, but wouldn't their status naturally rise if more capable (力量) female writers emerged (輩出) in large numbers? From that perspective, even if unsatisfactory, shouldn't Ewha Womans College (梨專) produce (輩出) many female writers?<222> Do you have any complaints or hopes regarding Ewha (梨專)?

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- There's much to say. Since Mo Yun-suk and No Ch'ŏn-myŏng are both Ewha graduates, I'm not saying Ewha's contribution (功勞) is small, but first, the Literature Department's educational policy (文科敎育方針) aimed at producing middle school (中學校) English teachers (英語敎師) needs to be fundamentally (根本的) changed. This doesn't mean, however, that all graduates (卒業生) should become female writers.

Mo Yun-suk- That's right. In our current societal circumstances (形便), since it's difficult for female writers to be nurtured (養成) by newspaper and magazine companies, there are still high expectations (期待) for Ewha, but Ewha's goal (目標) doesn't match our wishes. The fact that the English Literature professor (英文科敎授) is British (英國사람) is the primary mistake. With the poor language ability (貧弱한 語學力) of someone who just finished girls' high school (女子高普), learning English literature (英文學) from a British professor (英人敎授), how can there be time for the actual literature study that needs to be done, burdened as one is by language study (語學에 딸려서)? Ewha's Literature Department should be widely opened, inviting (招聘) Korean writers (朝鮮作家) to take the lectern (敎鞭). Creative writing courses (創作科目) should also be significantly increased.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- As an Ewha graduate myself, aiming arrows (활살) at my alma mater (母校) is very painful, but the Ewha authorities (梨專當局者), considering the Korean literary scene (朝鮮文壇), should, as Yun-suk suggested, open the teaching platform (敎壇) to renowned Korean writers (朝鮮作家), have students<223> actually produce (製作) works of poetry or fiction, and create opportunities for appreciation (鑑賞).

Chang Tŏk-cho- I also attended Ewha's Literature Department for a short while, but if one aims to become a writer, I see little to learn from the current Ewha. It is truly regrettable (痛恨) that Korea's only women's literature department is like this.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Besides Ewha, newspapers and magazines should increase manuscript fees (原稿料) for women and give preferential treatment (優待) in publishing their produced works. And when works are written with great effort (苦心), publishing houses (出版書肆) should willingly (즐기여) show the sincerity (誠力) to publish them.

Mo Yun-suk- And this is a separate issue, but please correct the male writers' view of women. The women depicted in the works recently presented by male writers show no step forward (進境) from the past. Women are invariably (依例히) weak (나약), crying, dragged along by men (사내), simple (單純), ignorant (無智)—they portray their heroines (女히로인) with a view of women that hasn't advanced one step beyond the feudalistic (封建的) concept of male superiority (男尊女卑). Seeing this, there are many parts that make us burst into laughter (抱腹絶倒) and many parts that make us indignant (憤慨). The environment (環境), knowledge (智識), and character (性格) of the New Woman (新女性) today are complex (複雜), autonomous (自主的), and her psychology (心理) moves quite rationally (理智的). They always view women at the level of Nora from "A Doll's House" (人形의 집), which is dumbfounding (긔막혀요).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Yes, men have a habit (버릇) of praising (讚美) women as "flowers (꼿) and moons," but just as flowers and moons seem to lack life (生命) and character (性格), they try to handle, soothe (달내고), and observe (觀察) today's women with that same notion (觀念).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Right. Among the heroines (女主人公) portrayed by male writers today, not one properly depicts (描寫) the New Woman. Even looking at the descriptive text, they are all ignorant (無識) about the fabrics (옷감), colors (빗갈) women like today, the poses (포-즈) they strike in front of men, the new language flowing between women—everything.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- So, the point (要) is that male writers become ignorant when portraying women. Beyond ignorance, comes slander (險口). What would happen if female writers also strived to portray the ugliness (醜) of men? Ho ho ho. (All laugh)

Chang Tŏk-cho- If the male side speaks of equality (平等) between men and women and gives a fair observation (正當한 觀察) regarding economic issues (經濟問題), the relationship between the two sexes (兩性社會) would become much brighter (明朗) and more mutually loving (相愛的).

Kim Tong-hwan- However, just as male writers fail to portray today's New Woman well, female writers probably also fail to fully understand (充分히 理解) the anguish (苦悶)<224> of today's men. In relationships between the sexes, too, is there any reason why writers who simply fall in love (戀愛), marry (結婚), and live an ordinary, uneventful life (平凡無事히 사라가는) should be able to depict the profound psychology (深刻한 愛憎의 心理) of love and hate? Looking at female writers abroad or in Tokyo, they sometimes get divorced (離婚), sometimes live separately (別居), always either fighting or loving, loving or fighting; they don't live the kind of wishy-washy (물에 물 탄 것 가튼) life like ours, where we neither love fervently (열열히) nor hate fervently.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- We see many examples where heartbreak (失戀) gives birth to great literature (偉大한 文學). In any case, the saying that one cannot become a great writer without experiencing (經驗) great human tragedy (人間의 큰 悲劇) seems correct.

Tales of Hardship in Conception and Writing

Kim Tong-hwan- Please tell us about your struggles (苦心談) when holding the brush. How many pages do you write per day (하로), what do you eat (자시며) while writing, or what do you do when your brush<225> gets stuck (막힐 때)?

Pak Hwa-sŏng- There are many tales of hardship during writing (執筆). First, for several days (멫일동안), I groan while conceiving (構想) the plot, and whenever I have a moment (틈), I close the door and invariably lie down (드러눕습니다). I have a habit of being able to conceive only when I lie down quietly, either looking at the ceiling (天井) or tightly closing my eyes and curling up (곱으리고 누어서), but since I lack the time for that, it's painful (苦痛). After conceiving like that for a few days, I then become afraid to put the brush to paper. After hesitating (망서리다가), once I gather the courage (勇氣) and start writing, it flows smoothly, but there are times when, even while writing well, I get completely stuck (탁 막히는 때). At such times, I again lie down facing the ceiling. It's a bad habit. That's why, for the ceiling paper (天井紙) at least, I choose nice colors and good paper and change it often. Enough to be soothing (慰安) to my eyes...
There's no set time; I pick up the brush whenever the children are away, but even when they follow me, I inevitably scold them while writing, so I just write whenever. I end up writing a lot at night when the children are asleep, but since I work so much, my body is tired, and I get sleepy at night and can't write.
While there are days I can't write a single character (한 字), on days when it goes well, I can write over 80 pages (80餘頁) in a day.
Usually, I eat drops (또톱쓰 - candy)... but since I pick them up and put them in my mouth out of habit (손버릇) when I get stuck while writing, I just move my mouth mechanically (기게적으로) while thinking, without even tasting them.

Paek Sin-ae- I am extremely envious of those who have the freedom to rummage through reference books (參考될 書籍) at will for creative work (創作), go out for field research (實地調査), or find a place they like and write quietly. Under a father who would immediately expel (逐出) me if I wrote, and being busy even while idle (여가 업는 터이라), whenever I try to write anything, it's late at night after everyone else is asleep, hiding the electric light (電燈불) under the blanket (이불), illuminating only the manuscript paper (원고지만 빛어놋코), writing quietly. When my back pressed against the pillow on my chest (가삼에 고은 벼게) and my ribs (肋骨) start to ache (재릴 때까지), I can usually write up to 20 pages (400字 원고지 20枚) a day. However, since I force myself (무리) to finish short stories in one go, the next day (잍은 날) I become like a sick person. I don't eat anything. But now, since my father passed away (별세) some time ago, I'm thinking of trying to write sitting at a desk (책상) like others, maybe putting drops (트롭프스) in my mouth and letting them melt slowly.

Chang Tŏk-cho- I don't particularly struggle (苦心) that much. I don't force the conception (構想) either; themes (테-마) that come to mind one by one, like a spring naturally welling up (스사로 소사<226>오르듯) in a mountain valley, are organized (整理) and kept until the thoughts mature, at which point I take up the brush. Perhaps because of this process, writing (씨워 집데다) comes quite easily. "Ŏmŏniwa Ttal" (어미와 딸), which appeared in "Samch'ŏlli" previously (녜전), was also completed (脫稿) in 30 minutes. A 15-installment short story for the Chosŏn Ilbo was also finished in one day; I started writing after breakfast that morning and completed all 15 installments by around 10 PM. After writing, I don't even reread (再讀) it; I just send it off to the newspaper or magazine company. Even so, I seem to memorize (웨워 지겟서요) every phrase of the works I've written. I mean, I can recite them from memory (暗誦). If my body were always healthy (健康), I feel I could produce works without agonizing too much over every single phrase (一句一節).

Mo Yun-suk- Me too. I never revise. I should be diligently refining (刻苦精勵) by revising and erasing, but letting what I write once get printed (活字化) as is makes my conscience quite troubled. When I get poetic inspiration (詩想) and write poetry, if it goes well, I produce three or four poems (篇) a day, but when it starts going badly, I might not get a single poem even after ten days (열흘). What do I eat when I take up the brush? Perhaps some fruit (果實). Maybe I should try eating drops like Pak Hwa-sŏng. Ho ho ho. And when my writing gets completely stuck, since there are many lush forests (蒼鬱한 山林) near my home by Yŏngdo Temple (永導寺 近傍), I stroll alone in such places. Otherwise, I close the door tightly and sit alone in a large room, sometimes just sitting vacantly (물끄럼히) as if without thought.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- I am terribly dull-witted (鈍才), so getting even one poem requires considerable effort (苦心). Grasping (붓잡고) a vague thought (想) passing through my head, I struggle to attach "flesh and fragrance" (살과 香氣) to it. I often find myself thinking about it every morning in the tram coming from the newspaper office. And once I start writing, it goes quite well, but I'm so afraid (겁이 나서) of picking up the brush that I rarely manage to grasp it. Also, I like times when I feel constantly stimulated (亢奮). When snow falls heavily, flinging open the window (窓을 와락 제처노코) and gazing at the distant, vast snowscape (까마아득한 雪景), a "Venus" (비-너스) passes through my mind. A rainbow appears.

Mo Yun-suk- Yes, snowy days are nice, but days with strong winds are also good. At such dramatic (悽壯) times, when the world seems utterly obscured (晦暝), the blood in my head boils (끌어저요).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- My debut work (處女作) was "Chŏngdanghan Sŭp'ai" (正當한 스파이). Since it was my first time then, my devotion (精誠) to the work was considerable. I wrote it, revised it once more, showed it to a senior (先輩) before publishing (發表), trying to be very careful (신중이). But afterwards, receiving requests (請) to write, I just pick up the brush, perhaps writing without great emotion (感激). Usually, I write one<227> piece per night. Consequently, sometimes, a while later, even the story (스토리) of a work I wrote doesn't remain in my memory. From now on, I want to try writing while putting in painstaking effort (心血을 쪼기는 苦心). Also, when thoughts don't flow easily, taking a bath (沐浴) or going into a street tea room (茶坊) and drinking coffee (고-히) sometimes spontaneously excites (흥분) me, and good ideas come to mind.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Generally, stories of hardship are similar. I'm not particularly different either. When I'm writing something and the brush doesn't move well, going into a bathhouse (沐浴湯), immersing my whole body in warm (따슷한) water, and sitting there helps me grasp the tail end of new thoughts. Besides that, I also enjoy wandering through unfamiliar (未知) streets and villages I've never visited. In Seoul, places like Hongp'a-dong (紅把洞) or Chŏng-dong (貞洞) that have a foreign atmosphere (異國情調) are nice too.

Impressions of Male Writers as Seen by Female Writers

Kim Tong-hwan- Having actually met (實際로 맛나보니) the male writers you've known through their works, what were your impressions (印象)? I imagine you must have been considerably disappointed (失望), but please speak frankly.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Yi Kwang-su, from his works (作品갓해서는), seemed like a very emotional person (多情多恨) who cries and laughs easily. His constitution (體質) seemed like a weeping willow (수양버들) by the riverside (강가), with an air (風情) of fluttering slenderly (열다라케 나붓기는). But lo and behold (웬걸), upon meeting him (맛나뵈니), he was blunt (무뚝뚝하고), large-bodied (體大), lacking the flavor of a sentimental lyric poet (感傷的 抒情詩人).

Mo Yun-suk- That's right. It's wrong to think of him as the delicate (纖纖한) person who depicted Sŏng-chun in "Kaecheokja" (開拓者) or Pak Yŏng-ch'ae in "Mujŏng" (無情). He's quite large-bodied (體大) and realistic (現實的).

Chang Tŏk-cho- And I thought he must be quite handsome (美貌), but he wasn't. However, he seemed like a transcendent elder (脫俗한 어룬), readily saying "Haha" to anything and moving on, and his lines (線) are soft for a man.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- I first met him (맛나뵛는데) in the reception room (應接室) during his time as the editor-in-chief (編輯局長) of the Tong-a Ilbo. Seeing a man with a large face, large build, and rather unkempt appearance (텁텁한 분), my imagined impression was shattered. He seemed like a high monk (高僧) who had come out after ascetic practice (修道) in the mountains, or like a disciple (弟子) of the Great Master Sŏsan (西山大師) who had transcended worldly affairs (世事를 超脫) and was about to enter the mountains (將入山中)—anyway,<228> my impression was that he was like a monk (중). And, though also a novelist, Kim Tong-in is someone who seems simpleminded (愚直한 듯) yet possesses formidable strength (魁力). He doesn't talk much, resembles an eccentric (奇人 비슷하면서), and impressed me as having a unique character (特異한 性格).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- When he laughs, forgive me for saying so (失禮지만), his face is long like a cow's (소), and the way he slowly (徐徐히) opens his mouth suggests a side of him that seems naive (純眞한 것 가튼). Once, listening to his speech (演說) at the Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoegwan (靑年會館), he spoke well too.

Mo Yun-suk- Yi T'ae-jun, as impressed from his works, seemed like a very clean and quiet person. He had the flavor (風味) of quietude (閑靜), like an oak tree (참나무) standing alone (외로히) in a deep mountain valley (깁흔 山 고요한 谷자구니).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- My first impression was that he resembled a musician (音樂家) more than a novelist. A slender body, soft voice, someone who would likely choose a small path over a main road—a humble (謙遜한) person.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Pak Yŏng-hŭi, known as a proletarian (푸로) writer who often takes themes (題材) from the conflict (葛藤) between rich and poor, murder (殺人), arson (放火), and other terrifying (무시무시한) subjects, I imagined to have a very rough (우락부락한) personality. But contrary to that, he appeared to be a tenderhearted (多情한) person with fine lines (線이 가늘고) and abundant human warmth (人情이 潤澤하게 흐르는).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Not only does his face look tenderhearted, but there's also a shadow (陰影) somewhere, giving the impression that he is always thinking profoundly (深刻하게) about human suffering (人間苦).

Chang Tŏk-cho- Speaking of proletarian (푸로) writers, Yi Ki-yŏng seemed much older (늙으섯더구만) than imagined. Since he's said to be over 40, that makes sense, but given the mature (老熟한) quality of his writing style (筆致), the impression of the work and the person seems consistent (一致).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Though old, he somehow seems to have depth (깁흔 곳이 잇는 듯하여), leaving a good impression. Not frivolous (輕하지 안코), not talkative (多辯하지 안코), seeming to ponder something deeply (깁히 파면서 생각하는 듯) with single-minded focus (외골수로)—just as his works are deep (깁고) and weighty (묵직하드시).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- Yŏm Sang-sŏp, on first impression, wasn't such a handsome man (美男子). He seemed weighty (묵직하고), and likely a bit ill-tempered (심술도 좀 잇슬 상 십고)—but he exuded an air (風情) similar to the toughness (질기고) and breadth (幅이 넓은) conveyed by his works.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Once, I saw him giving a literary lecture (文藝講演) from the podium (演壇) from afar (먼 발충에서), and he seemed very bold (豪活), and his voice was thunderous (우럭우럭 합데다) in a manly way.

Chang Tŏk-cho- Hyŏn Chin-gŏn, who wrote "B Sagamgwa Rŏburret'a" (B舍監과 러부레타) or "Pul" (불), gives the impression from his works of being a very finely lined (線이 가늘고) and impressive elder (印象的 어룬). He is someone who doesn't seem particularly ill-favored (어그러지지 안는) either. As a writer, he appears to be someone who has carved out his own<229> unique realm (獨特한 境地) and walks forward firmly (꽉 거러 나가는 분) with conviction (信念).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Recently, as the Social Affairs Department Head (社會部長) at the Tong-a Ilbo, busy (急하여) making the newspaper, it's deeply regrettable (못내 안타까워요) that he isn't producing works. His talented pen (才筆) is truly anticipated (期待되건만). Anyway, his gait (거름거리), demeanor (몸가짐), and voice (말소리) project the very impression one gets from his works.

Kim Tong-hwan- Please provide some critiques (人間批評) of other writers like An Sŏ (岸曙), P'aryang (八陽), Ch'uho (秋湖), Hyŏk-chu (赫宙), Myŏng-hŭi (命憙), Chi-yong (芝鎔), Ki-rim (起林), etc.

Mo Yun-suk- Do you want more trouble (말성)?

Chang Tŏk-cho- In fact, in our literary world, opportunities for male and female writers to meet together (會席) are rare, so although we know everyone by name through their works, we often haven't met them and don't know them well.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Even if we have met, it's usually just once or twice on the street, to the extent of knowing "that person is so-and-so," passing by hastily (총총히) like a traveler at sunset asking for directions (夕陽過客이... 길 뭇고 가드시), so we don't get to know them personally.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Even if we know them, expressing it in words can easily lead to trouble (말성).

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- They'll immediately say the women are being insolent (검방지다고). (All burst into laughter)

Which Writers Do You Admire (私淑)? What Kind of Reading (讀書) Do You Do?

Mo Yun-suk- In the past, I avidly read (耽讀햇담니다) Tolstoy. "Resurrection" (復活), "Anna Karenina," "War and Peace" (戰爭과 平和), "Confession" (宗敎論), etc. Then I switched (再轉) and came to like French writers, reading many works by Anatole France. Gide's "Strait is the Gate" (좁은 門) is also good. Though not French, Hardy's works too—Among Tokyo writers, Hayashi Fumiko's works are good. Ishikawa Takuboku's progressive *tanka* (前進的 短歌) and Itō Byakuren's passionate songs (情熱的 노래) were also good. Oh dear, I forgot one thing. Gorky's "Mother" (어머니) was read with true admiration (感激). As for things published in Korea, I generally read (大槪 보고 지냅니다) newspapers and magazines.<230>

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- I liked Turgenev, and I have memories (記憶) of turning pages late into the night with excitement (感激과 亢奮). Of his works, I've read almost half (거지 반 뒤저읽엇슴니다) including novels like "On the Eve" (그 전날밤), "Smoke" (연긔), "Home of the Gentry" (貴族의 집), "Fathers and Sons" (아버지와 아들), as well as his prose poems (散文詩) and collected letters (書翰集). Recently, I'm enjoying "Seijo" (聖處女 - Holy Woman) by Muro Saisei, serialized in the Osaka Mainichi (大阪每日). The writing style (筆致) is good, and its ideas (想) have a reverent (敬虔) flavor.

Pak Hwa-sŏng- How many books am I currently reading? Maybe a few volumes (멫 券)? I've selected some concerning the rural village problem (農村問題), but since I really have no time (틈이 업스니까), I don't read much, though I diligently (부즈런이) try to pick up books. For newspapers, I read all four major Korean papers (朝鮮의 四大신분). Two are delivered to my home, and two I borrow. For magazines, I carefully read (精讀함니다) all those kindly sent to me. I read almost all magazines for free (공으로 읽지오). The writers I have admired (私淑해 오든) are Yi Kwang-su, Thomas Hardy, and Charlotte Brontë. There are truly many works I used to read avidly (耽讀하든).

Chang Tŏk-cho- I admire (思慕) and also avidly read (耽讀함니다) Yi Kwang-su's works. Also, the works of Yokomitsu Riichi, who wrote "Tōjin Okichi" (唐人お吉), are good, and Uno Chiyo's "Otokomichi" (男子の道 - The Way of Men), published in the New Year's issue of "Kaizo" (改造), was good. Speaking of Kaizo, the work by Shimaki Kensaku, the Kaizo prize winner, is good. Also, because the critic Kim Hwan-t'ae told me to read Mansfield's (멘스필스) works, saying they were short and good, I read them, and indeed (果然) they were good (좃습데다). Nowadays, I'm rather enthusiastic (熱中) about that writer. Although I dislike proletarian (푸로) novels due to my personality (性格上), Gorky's "Mother" (어머니) is good. I've read it 3 or 4 times.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- I also like Yi Kwang-su's various works. They are easy to understand, the writing (行文) is fluent (流麗), and there are no vulgar (野卑) parts—. Besides that, I like Maupassant (모팟상) and novels of his school (一派). Such as bold depictions of carnal desire (大憺한 肉慾描寫)... Among Tokyo writers, Tanizaki Jun'ichirō's works, like "Chijin no Ai" (痴人の愛 - Naomi) and "Sorenari ni" (사랑하니까 - Because I Love You?), are good.

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- The ones I liked in the past (예전에) are Tolstoy, Jean Cocteau—. And recently, Gide is good, especially his "Strait is the Gate" (좁은 門), which is really good. Besides that, I've read most (大槪 읽엇서요) of Dostoevsky's (토스토엡스키) works, which deal profoundly (深刻하게) with life issues (人生問題). Hirabayashi Taiko in Tokyo also deals extensively with women's issues, and there's something compelling (肉迫하는 무엇) about her work, so I like it. As Chang Tŏk-cho mentioned earlier, Shimaki Kensaku's "Korotsuki no Kubi" (コロヅキノ首) is also good.

Kim Tong-hwan- I generally understand the tendencies of the female writers. Then, changing the subject, are there instances (例가 업습덱가) where young male or female literary aspirants (男女文學靑年) from the provinces (地方) or elsewhere send works or letters (便紙) asking to become disciples (弟子)?<231>

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Occasionally. They are female students (女學生) who enclose poems (詩) in letters, asking for corrections or guidance (指導). Whenever that happens, I wish there were a literary research group (文學硏究의 크룹) where we could learn from and teach each other.

Chang Tŏk-cho- They come to me occasionally too. Asking me to take them as disciples (弟子)—but how can I accept disciples when I'm currently in my apprenticeship (見習時代)? However, as Ch'ŏn-myŏng said, guiding (引導) and teaching what we know to each other would be a very good thing.

Mo Yun-suk- I had this experience. Receiving letters (便紙) from readers (讀者) happens occasionally, but once, after publishing my debut poetry collection (處女詩集) "Pinnanŭn Chi'yŏk" (빗나는 地域), someone sent a long critical letter and, in the end (내종에는), added admonishing words (훈계하는 말) like "Study masterpieces (傑作工夫) more."

Kim Tong-hwan- There must be many letters of praise (讚美).

Mo Yun-suk- More criticism (꾸지람) than praise. Ho ho ho.

Kim Tong-hwan- Are there any works you particularly want to write in the future, devoting all your energy (全心力)?

Paek Sin-ae- I intend to write a full-length novel. I want to portray a woman weaker and more pitiful than Kŭm-bong in "Kŭ Yŏjaŭi Ilsaeng" (그 女子의 一生). I don't particularly admire (私淑) any specific writer. Though I've avidly read (耽讀) many works, the memory (긔억) of fervently reading a novel called "Nodongjain Saerop'ŭ" (勞働者인 새롭프 - Worker Sharov?) by Altszybashev(?) (알쯔이, 파-셋프 作) in my childhood still hasn't faded. And what I'm reading now are the Nirvana Sutra (涅槃經) from Buddhist scriptures (佛經), "Kaizo" (改造), and the Osaka Mainichi newspaper (大阪每日新聞).

Pak Hwa-sŏng- If you ask about my future plans (압날의 푸랜), I'd just say I want to produce good works. My ambition is large, but since I lack talent (재주), it's the same old lament (밤낮 그 타령임니다).

Isn't Having an Occupation Painful for a Female Writer?

Paek Sin-ae- I think it's unreasonable (무리) for a writer to have an occupation (직업). This problem likely arises because, in Korea (조선), it's impossible to support (지탕해 나갈 수 업는) oneself with manuscript fees (원고료), but for us, who still have much room (여지) for improvement (향상), isn't it regrettable (안타가운 일) to have precious time tied down (잡어 메키우는) by an occupation?

Pak Hwa-sŏng- At first glance, having another occupation as a female writer seems like it would cause pain (苦痛) and anguish (煩悶), but in reality (事實), it might not be so. Why? Because aren't there so many chores to do at home? Starting from cleaning (掃除) the room and floor (마루) at dawn (새벽), cooking meals, laundry (빨내), starching (풀), ironing (다듬이), sewing (바느질), looking after the children (아이들 뒤 봐주기)—while struggling (헤매면서) amidst these miscellaneous tasks (잡동산이 일), we still find the courage (勇氣) to pick up a brush and try to write creatively (創作). Considering this, it seems much better for clearing the head to take on an occupation, dedicate oneself (熱中) to it, entrust housework (家事) to a suitable person (適當한 사람), and only oversee childcare (育兒) and general matters (대강만 보삷히는). Therefore, it seems that creative work (創作) could consequently become more powerful. So, from my perspective, having an occupation is acceptable (無妨).
However, if someone were to build me a small study (書齋) in a quiet place far away, free from both occupation and household chores (家庭雜務), and tell me to just focus on creative work with all my devotion (精誠), I truly feel confident (장담) I could produce at least one masterpiece (傑作).
When will such a world ever come for me?

Kim Tong-hwan- Regarding this, Ms. No Ch'ŏn-myŏng, who is currently working (勸務하고 게신) at a newspaper company, must have the most firsthand feeling (實感). Please give us an unreserved critique (批判).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- Since the stage (舞臺) in Korea is narrow, opportunities for writerly activities (作家的活動) are few, so the issue of writers' occupations seems premature (尙早). That is (卽), for those like us now, who occasionally toss out a poem or a short story, it's perfectly fine to have another occupation. Also, if the ideal (理想) is for women too to be economically independent (自立), then if living expenses (生活費) cannot be earned from manuscript fees (稿料), having an occupation should be irrelevant (無關). Of course (勿論), if one marries (結婚), becomes the mistress of a household (家庭의 主婦), and bears the responsibility (責任) of childcare (育兒), then it's a separate issue (別問題).

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Unless we were born daughters of very rich families (큰 부자집 따님), or married (出嫁) husbands (男便) with a stable income (一定한 收入), entering the occupational front (職業戰線) to an extent (程度) that doesn't destroy (破壞) family life (家庭生活) is seen as an unavoidable phenomenon (現象). When faced with it (닥치면), one must do it.

Mo Yun-suk- I generally agree (大體로 同感) too. One must fulfill the responsibility (責任) as a housewife (家庭主婦), and also attend to one's occupation; the fatigue (疲勞) and pain (苦痛) arising from playing two roles (一身兩役) cannot be avoided (免할 수 업겠지만),<233> but as modern women (現代의 女性), we must have that much resolve (覺悟).

Chang Tŏk-cho- Looking at examples from foreign countries, it seems customary for writers, once they achieve a certain standing (어느 程度의 자리가 잡히면), to leave other occupations and return to the home (家庭). It seems right that we should probably become like that too. For a writer to hold another occupation seems, no matter what, an exception (例外) and a detour (橫道).

How Much Manuscript Fee Do You Receive Per Month?

Kim Tong-hwan- Not just (非端) female writers, but male writers too, find the remuneration (報酬) for their works, namely manuscript fees (原稿料), extremely low (至極히 싸서), so everyone lives a writer's life practically embracing poverty (安貧樂道格). Nevertheless, how much income (收入) do you all receive from manuscript fees?

Chang Tŏk-cho- It's unspeakable (말이 못되지요). If one were to rely (萬一에... 바라고 生活하다가는) on manuscript fees for a living, both male and female writers would surely starve to death (굴머죽고 말게 된 것이) in our society. Over the past year (昨年 1년 동안), the total manuscript fees I received (밧어 본) amounted to 135 Wŏn (圓). That's 11 Wŏn per month. An astonishingly large sum (놀라운 多額), isn't it? Ho ho ho. We too need to reach a point where we have income from standalone books (單行本 收入) and various other sources (이길저길 收入), so we can occasionally travel (旅行) and buy the books (書籍) we want.

Pak Hwa-sŏng- The total manuscript fees I've received to this day (오날까지) are just a little over 1,000 Wŏn (1,000圓이 조곰 넘지요). My hope regarding manuscript fees? Goodness. What can I say? Having received, at most (기-껏), just over 1,000 Wŏn over these 4 years—does this even make sense? Female writers in Japan proper (일본 내지 女流作家) are said to earn a minimum (最少限度) of 200 Wŏn or more per month. Isn't mine less than their 5 months' earnings? It's truly pathetic (寒心하지요). But what can be done? The capabilities (實力) on both sides are just that.

Kim Tong-hwan- What is the standard (標準) for manuscript fees?

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- From what I hear (이약이를 듯건대), newspaper companies generally pay 2 to 3 Wŏn per installment (回) for serials (連載物). For magazines, "Sin Tonga" (新東亞) or "Sin Kajŏng" (新家庭) pay 1 Wŏn per printed page (印刷하여 1頁), "Chogwang" (朝光) pays 1 to 2 Wŏn per page (printed), "Chungang" (中央) is similar, and "Samch'ŏlli" apparently pays around 5 Wŏn per piece (篇), regardless of length (長短不拘), for creative works (創作).

Chang Tŏk-cho- "Wŏlgan Yadam" (月刊野談) once brought me 30 Wŏn all at once. I was quite grateful (감사하드군요).

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- There's a rumor (所聞) that "Samch'ŏlli" pays writers it gives to much more (훨신 주고) and doesn't give at all<234> to writers it doesn't. I have a complaint (不平) about that.

Kim Tong-hwan- Tonight, how can the guest attack (공격) the host? Shh, shh— Let's omit (다 뺍시다) all talk of Samch'ŏlli. Hahaha— But Ms. Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi, how much did you receive (밧으섯서요)?

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi- Last year (年前), I wrote a 45-installment mid-length piece (中篇 45回치) for M Newspaper, and they gave me 90 Wŏn, at 2 Wŏn per installment (1回 2圓式). That might be called my largest income (第一 컷든 收入). My hope (希望) is somehow to have a manuscript income (原稿收入) of about 40-50 Wŏn per month (每月 4,50圓 程度), have a clean study (깨긋한 書齋 한아쯤), and just write.

Paek Sin-ae- For me too, adding it all up, it's merely a few hundred Wŏn (불과 몃 百圓). I wish a way could be opened (티어 젓스면 조켓서요) to receive more manuscript fees.

Kim Tong-hwan- Ms. Mo Yun-suk, you must have received a considerable amount (相當히 밧으섯겠군) from the standalone publication (單行) of "Pinnanŭn Chi'yŏk," etc.

Mo Yun-suk- Do they give a lot anywhere? It wasn't much. And the money trickling in occasionally from newspapers and magazines isn't more than a few pennies (몃 푼 아니되여요). It's a disaster (야단 낫소이다), this lack of income. Ho ho. Can't newspaper and magazine companies provide us with a much larger stage (훨신 舞臺를 提供) and give us more money? It would be great if such a righteous person (義人) emerged.

Kim Tong-hwan- Ms. Yi Sŏn-hŭi, you occasionally write popular songs (流行歌) for records (레-코드), I hear. You must have income from that direction (方面) too.

Yi Sŏn-hŭi- I wrote exactly one piece (꼭 한 篇) intended as a popular song for a record (레코-드). For that, they gave me 5 Wŏn.

No Ch'ŏn-myŏng- I too haven't written many works, but I also haven't received large manuscript fees.

Kim Tong-hwan- Thank you all for sharing your valuable thoughts over several hours.<235>

**Summary**

This 1936 roundtable discussion features seven prominent Korean female writers (Pak Hwa-sŏng, Chang Tŏk-cho, Mo Yun-suk, Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi, No Ch'ŏn-myŏng, Paek Sin-ae, Yi Sŏn-hŭi) moderated by Kim Tong-hwan of Samch'ŏlli magazine. They critique recent literary works by both male and female authors, discussing style, theme, and perceived shortcomings. A significant portion is dedicated to the challenges faced by female writers: societal prejudice, the expectation to write only "feminine" topics, lack of opportunities compared to male counterparts, inadequate educational support (specifically criticizing Ewha Womans College's curriculum), and the biased portrayal of women in male writers' works. They also share personal struggles related to writing processes, the difficulty of balancing writing with domestic duties or other jobs, and extremely low manuscript fees, highlighting the precarious financial situation of writers in colonial Korea.

From a historical perspective, this article is highly revealing. It provides direct insight into the consciousness and frustrations of educated women intellectuals operating within the constraints of colonial modernity and patriarchal structures. Their critique of male writers' perspectives on women, demands for institutional change at Ewha, and frank discussion of economic hardship underscore the dual oppression (colonial and gender-based) they experienced. The writers' engagement with both Korean and international literature (Russian, French, Japanese) also reflects the complex cultural currents of the time. Historians would find valuable the specific critiques of contemporary writers and works, the articulated desire for artistic and economic independence, and the clear awareness of gender discrimination within the literary field itself.

最近에 읽은 海內海外 諸作家의 作品 印象

金東煥-(本社)-詩壇, 小說壇에서 活躍하시는 일곱분의 女流作家을 모시고 우리 文壇의 諸般問題를 討議할 이 機會를 엇게 되엿슴을 깁부게 생각합니다. 보시는 듯이 新年號의 新聞雜誌에는 여러 作家의 作品이 約 40篇이나 汎濫되고 섯슴니다, 또 그 우에 全州로부터 도라온 캅푸系 諸作家의 活動이 繼續하여 나타나고 잇슴니다, 質로나 量으로 보나 今年新春文壇만치 活氣를 띈 적이 過去 4,5年동안 업섯든 줄 암니다, 그러기에 이 機會에 그 制作된 作品과 作家와 文壇에 對하여 하시고 십흔 말슴이 만흘 줄 암니다, 그래서 爲先 最近에 나타난 作品의 印象과 作家의 傾向 等을 忌彈 업시 말슴하여 주시기를 바람니다, 다만 作品이라할지라도, 合評會가 目的이 아니엇든이만치 走馬看山格으로 簡單한 印象만이라도 말슴하여 주세요,
朴花城-最近에 읽은 內外作品 中에는 별노 머리에 남은 큰 인상이 없습니다만 두어개 잇다할지라도 各各 特徵이 다*니까 斷定해서 말할 수 없고요, 時調로는 曺雲氏의 完山七咏 (朝光2號)과 鷺山의 陜地行의 時調를 자미잇게 읽엇습니다.
그런대 이왕 말이 낫스니 沈薰氏의 『常綠樹』에 對해서 한 말하지요, 나는 본시(拙作만은 뫃아두지만) 신문 小說을 뫃아본 일이 없지오. 그러나 『常綠樹』만은 東亞日報社의 要求條件이 그랫스니까 반드시 農漁村의 特記할 場面과 深奧한 生活狀態가 나올 줄 알고 혹 緊한 參考거리가 잇슬까해서 꼭꼭 뫃았지오, 만은 오날까지 參考될 만한 거리는 붓들니지 않코 말엇습니다, 앞으로나 혹 생길는지....
그러나 不自然한 대목이 많음에도 不拘하고 또 그 作品의 基本方向이 내 생각과 틀님에도 不拘하고 각금가다가 가슴을 울니는 자리가 잇서요, 氏의 熱情만은 기르고 싶은 때가 잇든군요,
白信愛-저는 고루고루 읽지 못한 탓인지 아주 감복하야 읽은 작품이 없었음니다.<215>
外國작품도 그리 많이는 읽지 못했음니다마는 읽고 난 감상이란 나도 어서 쉬지 안코 많이많이 공부를 해야 겟고나 하는 것 뿐이였음니다.
金東煥-이러케 말슴을 하지 말고 어디 順序를 따라, 新聞社의 連載小說評과 新年號 各雜誌에 실닌 創作을 中心으로 批評키로 합시다. 大體 이번에는 三千里雜誌에 여덜篇의 作品이 稀久하게도 실니엇는데 여기서부터 出發키로 합시다. 처음 廉想涉氏의 「失職」에 對하여 엇더케 생각하심니까.
張德祚-「失職」은 절반 만나고, 來號에 아직 繼續한다 햇스니까 作家의 最近傾向을 말한다면 몰라도 作品評은 后機로 미루는 것이 올흘 상 십구만요.
金東煥-그러면 그 다음 張赫宙氏의 契約은?
毛允淑-張赫宙氏의 作品에 對하여는 從來에 여러 가지로 批評의 對象이 되여 왓는데, 이번 나온 作品을 보면 作의 佳不佳는 次置하고, 爲先 그 地文이 마치 外國作品을 直譯하여 노흔 것 가튼 어색한 대목이 만습데다.
崔貞熙-그러한 늣김을 누구나 밧을 것이지오. 아직 朝鮮글에 익숙치 못하고, 作에 燃燒点이 分明치 못해요. 한 개의 事象를 具體的으로 붓잡지 못하고, 그양 敍述的으로 平面描寫만 하여버린 欠이잇습데다. 率直하게 말하면 氏는 다시 鄕土的으로 고처 出發하여 주엇스
李善熙-一流作家에 比하여 搆想, 敍述은 다조요. 그러치만도 手法에 잇서서 어색한 것만은 곳 늣겨 지겟던군요. 氏는 좀더 조선말 공부를 하고 朝鮮의 情調를 理解하도록 힘씀이 조흘 것 갓습데다,
金東煥-田榮澤氏의 「오무니」는.
盧天命-作品이 그만 散漫하여 졋습데다. 氏는 예전 作品에서 그러케 進境을 보이지 안은 듯 하던구만요.
張德祚-스켈-이 크지 못하고, 事件을 運行식히는 데 너무 가볍게 넘겨버린 듯 하엿서요. 問題 外의 作品이랄밧게,
金東煥-그러면 兪鎭午氏의 黃栗은,
毛允淑-小說이라기보다, 한 篇의 깨긋한 小品이요, 스켓치입데다. 小說 되기에는 取扱한 事件이 너무 單純하엿섯요.
李善熙-氏의 筆致에선 언제던지 魅力을 늣겨지지만, 黃栗에서만은 讀后에, 뚜렷한 印象이 남어지지 안습데다.
金東煥-白信愛氏의 「學士」는?
盧天命-不自然스러운 대목이 업시, 日常生活에서 흔히 發見할 수 잇는 題材를 常識的으로 取扱한 것으로 거기에는 조고마한 無理도 늣겨지지 안습데다. 그러나 깁흔 맛은 업서, 가슴을 衝動하는 힘이 稀薄합데다.<216>
張德祚-白氏作品은 예전 「開闢」에 난 以后 오래간만에 보여 준 것인데, 敍述하는 筆致에 조고만한 억지라도 석기지 안은 것이 조와요, 압흐로 더 넓고 커질 素質이 보여집데다. 자라는 나무 모양으로요, 이번 것은 비록 身邊小說에 不過햇지만.
崔貞熙-나는 李孝石氏의 「산」은 「테-마」가 原始的으로 取扱되여 잇지만 그 筆致가 明朗하고 新鮮하고 順한 것이 아름다운 作品이여요. 깨긋하게 빠진 好感이 가는 短篇입데다.
毛允淑-그래요, 孝石氏 諸作은 어느 것을 노코 보아도 새롭고, 明朗하고 欠節이라고 업지요. 最近에는 「露領近海」에서 보여주든 것 가튼 深刻하고 陰散한 空氣는 차츰 줄어들어 가고 잇습데다.
李善熙-어디 낫든지요. 孝石氏의 「聖火」의 下半部도 무척 조와요.
金東煥-張德祚氏의 「三千里」에 난 「한 敎訓」은 엇대요.
崔貞熙-才致가 表面에 나끌어요. 어느 作品을 볼지라도 無理업게 才致잇게, 作品을 만드러내는 手法에는 歎服햇슴니다. 이번 것도 끝까지 緊張하게 끄을고 갑데다<217>
毛允淑-以前의 「어머니와 딸」도 조왓지오. 끝이 잘 되엇서요. 나는 그 속에 나오는 妓生을 婦人으로 알엇지오. 妓生에 對한 智識이 相當합데다 그려. 李泰俊氏의 「가마귀」도 조와요. 題材는 平凡하엿스나-그분의 것은 어느 것이나 알기 쉽고, 描寫에 香氣가 돌아요. 삿듯하지요.
崔貞熙-北歐作家에서 맛보는 듯한 沈痛한 맛은 차질 수 업지요. 나는 스켈이 크고, 어듼가 거칠면서도 한 모통이에서 빗이 번적 發하는 그런 作品이 조와요.
李善熙-새로운 作家 한 분이 잇습데다 그려. 金裕貞氏인데 氏의 作은 어느 것이나 欠잡을 것이 업습데다.
崔貞熙-그래요, 그이가 注目할만 합데다. 新年號 諸雜誌에 난 여러 篇을 보아도 새로운 맛이 흘너요.
毛允淑-鄕土的 香氣를 가장 만히 作中에 너흔 「常綠樹」는 요지간 나오는 新聞小說 中 第一優秀하던군요.
盧天命-그러나 新聞社의 文字普及을 爲한 作爲가 너무 눈에 띄어서 不快하더구만. 文學이 엇든 道具 되는 것은 실흔 일이지요.

搆想, 執筆할 때 苦心談

金東煥-붓 잡을 때의 苦心談을 말슴하여 주세요. 하로에 몃장이나 쓰고, 쓸 때에 무엇을 자시며, 또는 붓이<225> 막힐 때 엇더케 한단 말슴들을요.
朴花城-執筆할 때 苦心談이야 많지오. 첫재로 한 멫일동안은 搆想을 하누라고 꿍꿍대면서 틈만 잇스면 문 닷고 반드시 드러눕습니다. 천정을 처다보든지, 눈을 딱 감고 곱으리고 누어서 조용히 생각을 해야만 搆想을 할 수 잇는 버릇이 잇는대 그럴 틈이 없으니까 苦痛이지요. 한 멫일 그렇게 해서 搆想이 되면 인제 좋이에다가 붓을 대기가 무섭지오. 망서리다가 勇氣를 내여 쓰기 시작하면 인제 술술 나오지만 또 잘 써가다가도 탁 막히는 때가 잇습니다. 그런 때는 또 천정을 正面으로 하고 눕습니다. 못된 버릇이지오. 그러기에 天井紙만은 色도 좋고 조이도 좋은 것으로 골나서 자주 갈아 바릅니다. 내 눈의 慰安이 될 만큼 하게스리...
때는 定해잇지 않고 어느 때나 아이들이 없슬 때면 붓듭니다만 아이들이 쪼차와도 하는 수 없시 야단처가며 쓰니까 그저 아무때나 씁니다. 아이들 자는 밤에 많히 쓰게 됨니다만 워낙 일을 많이 하니까 몸이 고달퍼서 밤이면 잠이 와서 못씁니다.
한 字도 써지지 않는 날이 잇는 대신 잘 써지는 날은 하로에 80餘頁도 씁니다.
대개는 또톱쓰를 먹읍니다만...그저 쓰다가 막힐 때 손버릇으로 집어다 입에 넣으니까 맛도 몰으면서 기게적으로 입만 움직이며 생각하지오.
白信愛-창작을 하기 위하야, 참고될 서적을 맘대로 뒤질 자유와, 실지 조사를 간다든지, 맘에 드는 곧을 차저 가서 조용이 글을 쓸 수 잇는 분들을 나는 지극히 부러워 함니다. 글을 쓰면 당장에 축출을 하려는 아버지 아래엿고 놀며서도 여가 업는 터이라, 한가지 무엇이나 쓰려고 하면 밤중 남들이 다-잠든 후 이불 속에서 전등불을 감초아 원고지만 빛어놋코 가만히 씁니다. 가삼에 고은 벼게에 등줄이 맟이고 肋骨이 재릴 때까지 쓰면 400字 원고지로 하로 20枚까지는 예사임니다. 그러나 단편소설은 좀 무리를 해가며 단번에 끝을 내니까 그  잍은 날은 병든 사람이 되고 말지요, 먹는 거라고는 아모 것도 없읍니다. 그러나, 이제붙어는 얼마 전에 아버지가 별세하섰으니까 트롭프스나 입에 넛코 살살 녹이며 남같이 책상에 놋코 안저서 좀 써볼가고 생각함니다.
張德調-저는 별로 그러케 苦心치를 안어요, 搆想도 억지로 짜내지 안코, 마치 山谷間 샘이 스사로 소사<226> 오르듯 머리에 하낫둘, 떠오르는 테-마를 整理하여 두엇다가 생각이 익어진 때에 붓을 드는 關係인 지 퍽으나 쉽게 씨워 집데다, 녜전 「三千里」에 난 「어미와 딸」도 30分동안에 脫稿햇고, 朝鮮日報에 난 短篇 15回分도 그날 아츰 먹고 붓을 들어, 밤 열시쯤까지 하로 동안에 15回分을 다 끗냇지요, 써 노코는 再讀하여 보지도 안코 그양 新聞社나 雜誌社에 보내버리는데, 그러터래도 제가 쓴 作品은 구절구절 다 웨워 지겟서요, 暗誦이 된단 말슴이지요, 몸만 늘 健康하다면 一句一節에 몹시 苦心치 안으면서도 作品이 制作이 되여질 것 갓해요.
毛允淑-나도 그래요, 고치는 法이 업지요. 곳치며 지우며 刻苦精勵하여야 할 터인데 한번 쓴 대로 그냥 活字化하게 하니 여간 良心에 붓그럽지 안어요. 詩想을 어더 가지고 詩를 쓸 때에는 잘 되면 하로에 서너 篇을 나코, 안되기 시작하면 열흘이 가도 한 篇을 못 엇고 말지요, 붓을 들 때에 무엇을 먹는가고요, 혹 果實이나 먹지요. 저도 朴花城氏 모양으로 뜨롭푸나 먹어볼가요. 호호호, 그리고 쓰다가 붓이 꽉 막힐 때에는 우리집 永導寺 近傍에는 蒼鬱한 山林이 만흐니까 그런 속도 혼자 거닐지요. 그러치 안으면 門을 꼭꼭 닷고 크다란 방에 혼자 안저 생각하는 양 업시 그저 물끄럼히 안젓기도 하고요.
盧天命-저는 원악 鈍才가 되여서 詩 한 篇을 엇자해도 여간 苦心하는 것이 아니람니다, 어슴프레 머리를 지나가는 想을 붓잡고는 거기에 「살과 香氣」를 부치기에 努力하는 데 흔히 新聞社를 나오는 電車 속에서 아츰마다 생각하게 됨니다. 그리고 쓰기 시작하면 꽤 써지는데, 원악 붓을 들기가 겁이 나서 좀처럼 붓이 잡혀지지 안는 담니다, 그리고 恒常 亢奮이 되는 때가 조와요, 눈이 평평 쏘다질 때, 窓을 와락 제처노코 까마아득한 雪景을 바라보느라면 머리로 지나가는 「비-너스」가 잇서요. 무지개가 떠올나요.
毛允淑-그래요, 눈 온 때도 조치만 바람이 몹시 부는 때도 조와요. 天地가 웬통 晦暝하는 것 가튼 그런 悽壯한 때에 머리의 피가 끌어저요.
崔貞熙-저는 處女作이 「正當한 스파이」이엇는데 그때는 처음이니만치 作에 對한 精誠도 대단해서 쓰고서 다시 한번 고처써서, 엇던 先輩에게 보여드린 后 發表하여, 퍽으나 신중이 하느라고 하엿지만, 그 뒤는 써달난 請을 밧고 그저 붓을 드는 關係로, 큰 感激 업시 쓰는 글이 되여 그런지요, 大槪 하로 밤이면 한<227> 篇씩 써냄니다, 그러키에, 얼마 后에는 제 쓴 作이건만 스토리조차 남어잇지 안은 때가 잇서요. 이 압흐로는 그러지말고 心血을 쪼기는 苦心을 하면서 써보고 십허요. 그리고 생각이 늘 나오지 안을 때는 沐浴을 하든가, 거리의 茶坊에 드러가 고-히나 마시든가 하면 저절로 흥분되여지면서 조흔 생각이 떠오르기도 합데다.
李善熙-大槪 苦心하는 이약이야 어슷비슷하지요. 저라도 별로 다른 배 업서요. 무엇인가 쓰다가 붓이 잘 나가지 안으면 沐浴湯에 드러가 따슷한 물에 온 몸을 잠그고 안젓스면 새로운 생각의 꼬리가 쥐여저요. 그 밧게 저는 아직 한번도 가보지 못한 未知의 거리, 未知의 村을 헤매는 것도 조와해요. 서울이면 저 紅把洞이나 貞洞가치 異國情調가 도는 곳도 조쿠요.

女流作家가 본 男性作家의 印象

金東煥-作品으로 親하여오든 男性作家들을 정작 實際로 맛나보니 그 印象이 엇더합덱가. 相當히 失望들을 하섯슬 줄 암니다만은 어듸 툭 터러노코 말슴하여 주세요.
李善熙-李光洙氏는 作品갓해서는 퍽으나 잘 울고 잘 웃는-多情多恨한 분으로 그 體質도 江가의 수양버들인 듯, 열다라케 나붓기는 風情이 도는 분으로 알엇더니 웬걸 맛나뵈니 무뚝뚝하고, 體大하여, 感傷的 抒情詩人 味가 업습데다그려.
毛允淑-그러치요, 「開拓者」의 性淳을 그리고, 「無情」의 「朴英彩」를 그린, 纖纖한 분으로 알어서는 잘못이지요, 퍽으나 體大하고 現實的이고.
張德祚-그리고 퍽으나 美貌의 분인드시 생각햇지만, 그러치 안어요. 그러나 脫俗한 어룬인 듯, 무엇이든 허허하고 쉽사리 말슴하여 넘기고, 線은 부드럽은 便이지요. 男子치고-
崔貞熙-나는 그분을 처음 東亞日報 編輯局長時代에 應接室에서 맛나뵛는데 얼골도 크고, 몸집도 크고, 몸맵시도 별로 차리지 안은, 텁텁한 분을 發見하고 내 그리어 오든 印象을 깨트렷지요. 山 속에서 修道하다 나온 高僧갓기도 하고, 世事를 超脫하여 이제붙어 將入山中하려는 西山大師 弟子갓기도 하고-엇잿든 내<228> 印象은 중가튼 분이다 햇서요, 그리고 가튼 小說家면서 金東仁氏는 愚直한 듯 하면서 魁力이 잇는 분이지요. 말도 별로 아니하고, 奇人 비슷하면서 特異한 性格을 가진 분으로 印象되더군요.
李善熙-그이는 우슬 때 보니 失禮지만 「소」가치 얼골이 길고 徐徐히 입을 여는 품이 純眞한 것 가튼 一面이 잇서요. 언젠가 靑年會館에서 하는 演說을 드른 즉 말도 잘 하더군요.
毛允淑-李泰俊氏는 그 作品에서 印象되드시 퍽 깨긋하고 고요한 분이더군요. 깁흔 山 고요한 谷자구니에 외로히 선 참나무 가튼 閑靜의, 風味가 도는 이더구만.
崔貞熙-첫 印象이 小說家라기보다 音樂家 비슷하더군요. 가는 몸, 부드러운 말소리, 큰 길을 내어노코 적은 길을 가리어 갈 듯한 謙遜한 이로요.
李善熙-푸로 作家라 하여, 作品의 題材를 貧富의 葛藤과 殺人, 放火 等 무시무시한 곳에서만 잘 取하여오는 朴英熙氏는 퍽으나 우락부락한 性格을 가진 분으로 想像하엿더니, 그와 反對로, 線이 가늘고 人情이 潤澤하게 흐르는, 多情한 분으로 보입데다그려.
崔貞熙-그이는 얼골이 多情하여 보일뿐더러 어듸엔가 陰影이 잇서 人間苦를 늘 深刻하게 생각하고 잇는 듯한 印象을 주어요.
張德祚-푸로 作家라면 李箕永氏는 想像以上으로 퍽으나 늙으섯더구만, 40이 넘엇다 하니 그러켓지만, 그이는 筆致가 老熟한 點으로 보아서, 作品과 사람으로의 印象이 一致하는 듯해요.
崔貞熙-늙엇스나, 엇전지 깁흔 곳이 잇는 듯하여 印象이 조흔 분이지요. 輕하지 안코, 多辯하지 안코, 무엇을 외골수로 깁히 파면서 생각하는 듯 하여서-氏의 作品이 깁고 묵직하드시.
李善熙-廉想涉氏는 첫 印象으로 그러케 美男子는 아니더구만, 묵직하고, 심술도 좀 잇슬 상 십고-그러나 作品이 던지는 질기고 幅이 넓은 그러한 風情이 돌더군요.
盧天命-언젠가 演壇에서 文藝講演 하시는 걸 먼 발충에서 보앗는데 퍽으나 豪活하고 말소리도 男子답게 우럭우럭 합데다.
張德祚-「B舍監과 러부레타」라거나 「불」이라거나를 그린 玄鎭健氏는 作品으로 밧는 印象은 몹시 線이 가늘고 印象的 어룬으로 뵈여지는데, 人物도 별로 어그러지지 안는 늣김을 주는 이지요, 作家로서도 自己의<229> 獨特한 境地를 開拓하여 信念을 가지고 꽉 거러 나가는 분이라고 보여저요.
崔貞熙-요지간 東亞日報 社會部長으로, 新聞을 만들기에 急하여, 作品을 내지안는 것이 못내 안타까워요. 그 才筆은 정말 期待되건만, 아무튼 거름거리, 몸가짐, 말소리가 作品에게 밧는 그 印象 그대로 던저주는 이라 할가요.
金東煥-그 밧게도 岸曙, 八陽, 秋湖, 赫宙, 命憙, 芝鎔 起林 等 여러 作家의 人間批評을 좀 하여 주서요.
毛允淑-또 말성이 나게요.
張德祚-事實 우리 文壇에는 男女作家가 會席할 機會가 드무니까 作品으로는 누구누구 다 알고 잇지만 맛나뵌 적이라고 업서서 잘 몰나요.
盧天命-맛낫다 하여도 한 두번 길가에서 저 이가 아모개란 程度로, 마치 夕陽過客이 총총히 길 뭇고 가드시 지나가 버려서는 人間的으로 잘 알어지지를 안해요.
崔貞熙-안다해도 정말 말로 表現하여 노으면 말성이 생기기 쉽고.
李線熙-녀자들이 검방지다고 대번에 할 걸요. (一同大笑)

어느 作家를 私淑하는가, 讀書는 엇던 傾向의 것을 하는가

毛允淑-녯날에는 톨스토이 것을 耽讀햇담니다. 「復活」 「안나카레니야」 「戰爭과 平和」 「宗敎論」 等을, 그리다가 再轉하여 佛蘭西作家가 조와지는데 「아날드, 푸란스」의 것을 만히 읽엇지요. 지이드의 「좁은 門」도 조코요. 佛作家는 아니나 하-디 것도-東京 것으로는 林芙美子의 作品이 조와요. 石川탁木의 前進的 短歌와 伊藤白蓮의 情熱的 노래도 조왓고, 앗불사 한 가지를 이젓군요. 골-키의 「어머니」는 정말 感激으로서 읽엇담니다. 조선서 나오는 것으로는 新聞과 雜誌는 大槪 보고 지냅니다.<230>
盧天命-저는 「트르게넵프」가 조와서 感激과 亢奮으로 밤 늣게까지 책장을 번지든 記憶을 가지고 잇슴니다. 그분의 것은 「그 전날밤」 「연긔」 「貴族의 집」 「아버지와 아들」들의 小說과 散文詩, 書翰集까지도 거지 반 뒤저읽엇슴니다. 요지간은 大阪每日에 나는 「聖處女」라고 室生犀星이 쓴 것을 조케 보고 잇슴니다. 筆致가 조코, 그 想도 敬虔한 맛이 잇서요.
朴花城-現在 읽는 冊이야 멫 券되나요? 農村問題에 對한 것으로 좀 골나놓고도 워낙 틈이 업스니까 잘못 읽습니다만 그래도 부즈런이 冊을 들어는 봅니다. 신문은 朝鮮의 四大신분이라는 것은 다 봅니다. 집에 오는 게 두 장 빌녀다 보는 게 두 장, 雜誌는 그저 호의로 보내주시는건 다 정독함니다. 잡지는 거진 다-공으로 읽지오. 私淑해 오든 作家는 李光洙氏와 토마쓰 하-듸, 솨롯 뿌론테 女史입니다. 從來에 耽讀하든 作品이야 참 많지오.
張德祚-李光洙氏의 作品을 思慕하며 또한 耽讀함니다. 그리고 「唐人お吉」을 쓴 橫光利一의 作品이 조코 新年號에든지 「改造」에 난 宇野千代의 「男子의 길」이 조왓서요, 改造 말이 낫스니 말이지만 改造 當選作家인 島木健作의 作이 조와요, 그리고 評論家 金煥泰氏가 작고 조타고 읽으란 말슴을 하기에 「멘스필스」의 作을 읽엇더니 果然 좃습데다. 요지간은 그 作家에 熱中하는 셈이지요. 저는 性格上으로 푸로 小說이 실치만 골-키의 「어머니」만은 좃습데다. 3,4讀을 하엿서요.
李善熙-저도 亦是 李光洙氏 諸作品이 조와요, 알아보기 쉽고, 行文이 流麗하고, 野卑한 곳이 업고-, 그러고는 모팟상과 그 一派의 小說을 조와함니다. 大憺한 肉慾描寫라든지가..., 東京作家 것으로는 痴人의 愛 「사랑하니까」 等을 쓴 谷崎潤一郞의 作이 조쿠요.
崔貞熙-저는 예전에 조와한 이는 톨스토이, 짠, 곡토-, 그리고 요지간은 「지이드」가 조흔데 더구나 그분의 「좁은 門」은 정말 조와요. 그러구는 人生問題를 들어 몹시 深刻하게 取扱하는 「토스토엡스키」 것도 大槪 읽엇서요.東京 잇는 平林다이子의 것도 女性問題를 만히 取扱하는데 肉迫하는 무엇이 잇서 조와요. 앗가 張德祚氏 말슴합데다 만은 島木健作의 「コロヅキノ首」 가튼 것도 조터구만.

女流作家로써 職業을 가지는 것이 苦痛이 아닌가

白信愛-작가로서 직업을 가지는 것은 무리라고 생각함니다. 조선같이 원고료로는 생활을 지탕해 나갈<232> 수 업는 사정이니까 이런 문제도 생기는 것이겟지만 아즉 앞으로 향상해 나가야될 여지를 많히 가진 우리들로서는 앗가운 시간을 직업에 잡어 메키우는 것은 안타가운 일이 안이겠음니가.
朴花城-女流作家로서 딴 職業을 가지는 것이 언뜻 생각하면 苦痛과 煩悶이 될 것 같지만 事實은 그렇지도 않을 듯합니다. 웨 그러냐하면 집 안에서 하는 일이 오직이나 많나요. 새벽 방, 마루 掃除붙어 밥 지어먹고 빨내, 풀, 다듬이, 바느질, 아이들 뒤 봐주기, 요런 잡동산이 일에 빠저서 헤매면서도 붓을 들고 제법 創作해 보겠다고 勇氣를 내는걸 보면 차라리 한 職業을 가지고 나서서 거기에 熱中하면서 家事는 적당한 사람을 얻어 마끼고 育兒라거나, 대강만 보삷히는 것이 훨신 더 머리를 깨끗하게 해 줄 것 같어요. 그러니까 따라서 創作도 좀더 힘찬 것을 쓸 수 잇슬 것같이 생각되니까 나로서는 職業을 가지는 것도 無妨하다고 봅니다.
그러나 職業도, 家庭雜務도, 말고 자그마한 書齋나 하나 뚝 떠러진 고요한 곧에 지어주면서 精誠을 다해서 創作이나 하라고 한다면 정말 내 장담하고 傑作 하나쯤은 내놓고 말 것 같이 생각됨니다.
언제나 내게 이런 세상이 올는지요.
金東煥-여기 對하여서는 現在 新聞社에 勸務하고 게신 盧天命氏가 가장 實感을 가지고 게실 터이니 숨김업는 批判을 하여 주서요.

崔貞熙-우리들이 큰 부자집 따님으로 태어나지 못한 以上, 또 一定한 收入이 잇는 男便에게 出嫁하지 못한 以上, 家庭生活을 破壞하지 안는 程度로 職業戰線에 나서는 것도 엇절 수 업는 現象으로 봄니다. 닥치면 해야 하지요.
