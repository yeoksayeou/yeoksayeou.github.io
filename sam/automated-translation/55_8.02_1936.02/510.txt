Article Title: 웃는 自由帖 (Smiling Free Page/Jokes)
Author: Unknown
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writings)

Birthday

A: When were you born?
B: On my birthday.
A: When is your real birthday?
B: Do you remember everything from when you were little?<280>

Economy

Wife: "Every day it's laundry or cooking, and even after a whole year, you don't buy me even one pair of shoes. Are you saying I'm just a housemaid (행낭 어멈)?"
Husband: "Nonsense! What are you talking about? A housemaid (행낭어멈) would cost money."<281>

Typist and Secret Revealed

A wife, visiting her husband's company after a long time, quietly approached him from behind, covered his eyes, and altering her voice asked:
"Guess who?"
"You playful imp again! Hurry up and type the letter I gave you earlier on the typewriter (타이부라이다). Suk-hŭi Ssi (淑姬氏)!"<282>

A Good Horse

A young mother, whose due date was approaching, held her firstborn son and asked:
"Ŭl-nam-ah, this time, shall I give you a younger brother or a younger sister?"
The child thought for a moment and said, "I like a horse (馬)."<283>

**Summary**

This short article presents four humorous anecdotes reflecting everyday life and conversations in 1930s Korea. The jokes touch upon themes like birthdays and memory, marital dissatisfaction regarding domestic labor and finances, potential workplace infidelity revealed through mistaken identity involving a typist, and the innocent (or perhaps telling) preferences of a child regarding a new sibling. For historians, the jokes offer glimpses into social dynamics, gender roles (the wife's complaints, the husband's dismissive reply, the presence of female typists in offices), economic awareness (the cost of servants vs. wives), and common conversational tropes of the era. The mention of a typist (타이피스트) and typewriter (타이부라이다) reflects the modernization occurring in workplaces.

經濟속

안해 「날마다 빨내 아니면 밥짓기 그러고도 일년 가도 구두 한 커레 안사주고 대체 내가 행낭 어멈이란 말슴이요.」
남편 「천만에 그게 무슨 소리 행낭어멈이면 돈이 들지.」<281>

타이피스트와 祕密發覺

납편잇는 회사로 오랜간만에 차저간 안해가 뒤로 가만히 가서 자는 눈을 뒤로 막으며 목소리 변해서
「내가 누구?」
「『또 작난군이 어서 앗가 준 편지를 타이부라이다에 처 달나구. 淑姬氏」<282>

말이 조와

아이 나올 때가 갓가워 온 젊은 어머니가 맛아들을 안고
「乙男아 이번에는 네 동생을 줄가 네 누이를 줄가.」
아이 한참 생각하다가 「나는 말(馬)이 조와.」<283>
