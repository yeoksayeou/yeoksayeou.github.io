Gratitude and Apology

○We are grateful that you read the previous New Year's issue with such enthusiasm. Please provide good guidance for the development of "Samch'ŏlli" (三千里), like watering a growing tree.

○Starting this month, we have newly established an "Esperanto" (에스페란트) section. Upon announcing the establishment of this section, words of encouraging praise arrived in succession from various places overseas. Feeling how great the world's anticipation is, we will strive to ensure this section fulfills its great purpose of aiding culture both at home and abroad.

○The second installment of the Complete Works of Yi Kwangsu (李光洙全集), "That Woman's Life" (그 女子의 一生), is also now on sale, and we hope for many enthusiastic readers. <300>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the February 1936 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* expresses gratitude to readers for their enthusiastic reception of the New Year's issue and asks for continued guidance. It announces the launch of a new Esperanto section, highlighting the international encouragement received and the aim for it to contribute to cultural exchange. The notice concludes by promoting the second volume of Yi Kwangsu's collected works, currently on sale. From a historical perspective, the piece reveals the magazine's active engagement with its readership and its efforts to cultivate loyalty. The introduction of an Esperanto section is particularly interesting, reflecting a contemporary interest in internationalism and global communication movements, even under colonial constraints. Furthermore, the promotion of Yi Kwangsu, a major figure in modern Korean literature, underscores the magazine's role in disseminating Korean cultural works during this period.
