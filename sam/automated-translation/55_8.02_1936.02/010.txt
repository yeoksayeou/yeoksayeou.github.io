Meetings Drawing Attention in the New Year

On the evening of January 14th, at a certain location in the northern part of the city, under the name of Mr. Pak OO's New Year's banquet,
Messrs. Yun OO, Kim OO, Ch'oe O, XOO, Pak OO
these five gentlemen gathered. And at that gathering, a certain Mr. X from a certain side also attended. All the figures who gathered were either key figures in the financial world or influential figures in society. Moreover, the fact that Mr. X participated in this meeting, right at the start of the New Year, is certainly drawing considerable attention, and there are rumors in some quarters that something is maturing. In any case, we shall see what happens next time, and for now, this report ends here.

The Leap Forward of Tonghŭng Middle School

Tonghŭng Middle School (東興中學校) in Yongjŏng (龍井), Kando (間島), which has a deep history, has recently appointed critic Kim Kyŏngjae (金璟載) as chairman of the board. While establishing financial policies and devising plans for school development, it is said that they will soon establish a university department that everyone will look up to.

Newspaper Companies' Year-End Bonuses

In the past, newspaper companies were so financially weak that they couldn't even pay their employees' monthly salaries properly. Now, thanks to a sharp increase in readers and a convergence of advertisements from Tokyo and Osaka, they are showing profits at the year's end.
Thus, looking at this year's year-end bonuses, the Tong-A Ilbo (東亞日報) and the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) generally gave around 200% (20割), the Maeil Sinbo (每日申報) was similar, and the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報) is said to have given around 100% (10割).<2>
As an aside, Hwasin (和信) is also said to have distributed an average of 200% to its 600 employees.

Commands at the Military Academy

The situation in Inner Mongolia (內蒙古) is drawing much public attention. According to someone who recently returned, at the Mongolian Military Academy (蒙古軍官學校), all drill commands are given in Japanese, and in a peculiar way at that.
Instead of saying
"Hands up!" (「手ヲ 上ニ ノバセ!」)
they say
"May the spirit of Genghis Khan pierce the heavens!" (「チンキスカンノ意氣天ヲ 衝ケ」)
So it is said.

Eighty Thousand Yuan on Mao Tse-tung's Head

On the head of Mao Tse-tung (毛澤東), Chairman of the People's Committee of the Provisional Central Government of the Chinese Soviet Republic, who currently commands 200,000 troops in Shensi (陝西), there is a bounty of 80,000 yuan placed by Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石). Likewise, on the head of Military Committee member Chu Te (朱德), there is also an 80,000 yuan bounty from the Nationalist Government side.

Pae Kuja Musical Theatre Troupe's Return Visit, One Step Ahead of the New Spring Flower News

The Pae Kuja Musical Theatre Troupe (裴龜子樂劇團), walking the true path of musical theatre, the one and only that Korea possesses, stayed for many years in places like Tokyo and Osaka, honing their skills. Last November, upon returning triumphantly for the opening ceremony of the newly built Tongyang Theatre (東洋劇場) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), their polished performance was met with rave reviews from the capital's connoisseurs, presenting a scene of fervent popularity with sold-out shows daily. In terms of both quality and quantity, they set the highest record in Kyŏngsŏng's entertainment world. Although the people of their homeland pleaded for them to stay longer, due to a prior engagement for performances in Tokyo, they regrettably had to bid farewell after a brief run and cross the Hyŏnhae (玄海 - Genkai Sea) again, much to the great regret of us theatre lovers. Moved by this, the Tongyang Theatre has decided to invite the Pae Kuja Musical Theatre Troupe back, one step ahead of this spring's flower news. This time, to also meet the expectations of people in the provinces, they are expected to undertake a tour of major cities throughout Korea (全鮮). This will surely be a golden opportunity for provincial audiences to see the Pae Kuja troupe face-to-face! This is undoubtedly the first news the New Spring entertainment world brings us.

A Happy Event for Two Female Writers

It seems the second generation of female poet Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑) and female novelist Chang Tŏkcho (張德祚) will cry their first cries on earth before long, as the two mothers-to-be are busy preparing for childbirth.<3>

Japan's Richest Person? - Based on Personal Income -

According to a phone call from Osaka (大阪) some time ago, among Japan's three great plutocrats, Mitsui (三井), Mitsubishi (三菱), and Sumitomo (住友), the young head of the Sumitomo zaibatsu, Baron Sumitomo Kichizaemon (住友吉左衛門), has far surpassed the two great zaibatsu of Mitsui and Mitsubishi. According to figures based on personal income, he has decisively become Japan's number one wealthiest person.
The tax assessment for last year in the 2 prefectures and 10 counties under the jurisdiction of the Osaka Tax Supervision Bureau has been completed. Baron Sumitomo's personal income amount was assessed at a staggering 2.52 million yen. This compares to Mitsubishi's Iwasaki Hisaya's (岩崎久彌) 2.5 million yen, Iwasaki Koyata's (岩崎小彌太) 1.75 million yen, and the head of the Mitsui main family, Baron Mitsui Takakimi (三井高公), who only earned 1.4 million yen last year. He has decisively distinguished himself, surpassing the heads of both zaibatsu, making a name for himself as number one in Japan based on personal income. The reason is said to be that Sumitomo's related businesses are mostly in munitions industries, and thus have fully enjoyed the effects of the munitions inflation boom of the past 2-3 years, resulting in such astonishing progress in income.

Chinese Publisher - Eleven Periodicals Alone -

Which publishing house is the largest in China today, what is its name, and where is it located?
It is none other than the "Chŏngjung Sŏguk" (『正中書局』 - Zhengzhong Shuju), a large bookstore located on Hŏbuk-ro (河北路) in the capital city of Nanking (南京市).
This bookstore is the most prominent establishment in all of China. Counting just the monthly magazines published regularly, there are:
"Kyoyŏhak" (『敎與學』 - Jiao Yu Xue)
"Sisawŏlbo" (『時事月報』 - Shishi Yuebao)
"Munye Wŏl'gan" (『文藝月刊』 - Wenyi Yuekan)
"Ch'ŏngnyŏn Wŏl'gan" (『靑年月刊』 - Qingnian Yuekan)
"Kaebal Sŏbuk" (『開發西北』 - Kaifa Xibei)
"Chijŏng Wŏl'gan" (『地政月刊』 - Dizheng Yuekan)
"Ilbon P'yŏngnon" (『日本評論』 - Riben Pinglun)
amounting to 7 types.
In addition, there are also regular quarterly magazines:
"Hoegye Kyegan" (『會計季刊』 - Kuaiji Jikan)
"Chungguk Sahoe" (『中國社會』 - Zhongguo Shehui)
"Nodong Kyebo" (『勞働季報』 - Laodong Jibao)
"Chibang Chach'i" (『地方自治』 - Difang Zizhi)
amounting to 4 types.
This alone shows the large scale of publishing activities this bookstore undertakes.
Besides these, the number of individual books published from time to time is said to be countless.

Three Tidbits Heard Through the Grapevine

Han Yongun and Son Ch'ŏnbang

This gentleman, deeply versed in Buddhism, recently began writing the novel "Hŭkp'ung" (『黑風』 - Black Wind) in the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper, turning towards the literary world anew as he approaches the old age of 60. According to recent rumors, upon the occurrence of the Son Ch'ŏnbang (孫傳芳 - Sun Chuanfang) assassination incident in China, he keenly felt that while this incident was treated significantly and reported in detail through media outlets in mainland Japan and other countries, it was met with too much indifference uniquely within our society. Having collected detailed materials reported in China and various other countries for a long time, he became greatly interested in this issue. He exclaimed with great passion that the story of the female protagonist leading up to the assassination of Son Ch'ŏnbang holds a great lesson that can be thrown to the women's world of Korea today. He is already refining his ideas, intending to write a major novel based on this incident as soon as he has completely gathered the <5> materials-.

An Chaehong's Filial Piety

Minse An Chaehong (安在鴻) has recently been dedicating himself entirely to literary life. Especially towards the end of last year, while writing a manuscript requested by a certain newspaper company, he reportedly spent 6 or 7 consecutive days without leaving the company gates, staying up late until 1 or 2 AM, constantly holding his pen. Besides that, visitors from various magazine companies requesting manuscripts are said to be countless-. When I visited his home on December 28th last year, as he wrestled with manuscript paper at his desk all day long, he said,
"...Lately, because of manuscripts day and night, my health has suffered, and I've even developed indigestion... Now, at the beginning of the new year, I have to go down to the countryside to pay respects to my elderly mother... My mother is now seventy, so I make sure to go down once every two weeks, no matter what else is going on, but each time I go, I see her white hair turning even whiter and her face gradually becoming paler, so my mind isn't at ease, and I have to go down again this time..."
As he spoke, the light of utmost filial piety seemed to shadow his venerable face.

Novelist Yi Kiyŏng's Difficult Situation

Anyone, not just the youth of Korea, would know the writer Yi Kiyŏng (李箕永), author of the full-length novel "Kohyang" (『故鄕』 - Hometown), as he is the most prominent novelist in our literary scene. Like any Korean writer, he too lived a life of honorable poverty in a single-room thatched house until recently. No sooner had he returned from Chŏnju (全州) than he began writing a full-length novel titled "In'gan Suyŏp" (『人間修業』 - Human Training) for the Central Daily News' (中央) Arts and Culture page. It's said that leading up to writing that novel, there were countless worries and bitter circumstances entangled-. Firstly, he is still not a man of complete freedom. His initial intention was absolutely not to pick up his pen until "that matter" was fully resolved. However, looking out from his former self's home, his day-to-day life was uncertain, and moreover, the newspaper company earnestly requested him to write. Faced with this situation, it's said that he was deeply troubled.
However, one piece of good news is that from his trip to Chŏnju this time, he gained a most precious opportunity as a novelist. Rumor has it that from his life there of about a year, he came out having formed the concepts for 18 novels.<6>

**Summary**

This 1936 "Samch'ŏlli" article presents a collection of short news items covering diverse topics: a potentially significant meeting of Korean elites, the expansion of Tonghŭng Middle School in Kando, rising profits and bonuses at major Korean newspapers (Tong-A Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, etc.), the business affiliations of Hwasin department store, Japanese military commands in Inner Mongolia, bounties on Mao Tse-tung and Chu Te in China, the popular return of the Pae Kuja Musical Theatre Troupe, personal news about writers Mo Yunsuk and Chang Tŏkcho, the ranking of Japan's wealthiest individuals (with Sumitomo surpassing Mitsui and Mitsubishi), the scale of a major Chinese publishing house (Chŏngjung Sŏguk), a comparison of global air forces, world leaders' salaries, and personal updates on prominent Korean intellectuals Han Yongun, An Chaehong, and Yi Kiyŏng. The article offers glimpses into the social, economic, political, and cultural spheres of Korea and East Asia in the mid-1930s. Historians might find the details on Korean newspaper finances, the mention of specific banks used by Korean businesses (Hansŏng, Chigsan, Haedong), the cultural impact of the Pae Kuja troupe, the reported activities and concerns of major intellectuals like Han Yongun, An Chaehong, and Yi Kiyŏng (including Yi's difficulties related to his political status), and the casual reporting on Japanese military influence (Inner Mongolia commands, Sumitomo's wealth via munitions) particularly interesting for understanding colonial Korean society and its connections to broader regional dynamics.

韓龍雲氏와 孫傳芳

安在鴻氏의 孝誠

民世 安在鴻氏는 근자에 와서는 오로지 문필생활에 전심진력하시는데 특히 지난 연말에 잇서서는 모 신문사에서 부탁받은 원고를 쓰시누라고도 장장 연 6, 7일을 社門不出하시고 꼼박 밤 새로 1시, 2시까지 새워 가시면서 붓대를 늘 잡으시엿다는데 그박게 여러 잡지사에서 원고 부탁으로 차저오는 손들도 이루 헤아릴 수가 업다고-. 氏는 지난해 12월 28일. 하루 종일을 책상 앞에서 원고지와 씨름하시는 때에 宅으로 차젓드니
『... 원 요사히 갓해서는 밤낮 원고 때문에 부닥겨 몸에 건강을 상하여서 소화불량까지 어덧서요... 이제 새해를 잡어 초순 경에는 또 시골로 늙으신 노모님 문안하려도 내려갓다 와야 하겟구... 모친께서는 이제 칠순이 되시는 이고 해서 내가 2주일에 1차식은 꼭꼭 아무리 별일이 다-잇드래도 내려갓다 오고는 하는데 한번 갈 때마다 허-연 머리카락이 점점 더 백발로 변하시면서 얼골도 차츰 더 파리해지시는 것을 보니 원 마음도 잘 노이지 안코 해서 이번에도 또 내려갓다 와야 하겟구...』
하시는 氏의 거륵하신 안면에는 지극하신 효심의 빗이 어리여지는 듯 하더이다.

小說家 李箕永의 딱한 事情

장편소설 『故鄕』을 쓴 작가 李箕永이라면 조선의 청년들은 물론하고 누구나 몰을 사람이 없으리만큼 우리 문단에서 가장 뚜렷한 소설가이다. 조선의 문인치고 어느 누가 그러하지 않으리요 마는 유독히 氏는 얼마 전까지도 單間草屋에서 淸貧生活을 하든 이다. 氏가 全州에서 도라오기가 밧부게 中央 學藝面에 『人間修業』 이란 장편소설을 집필하기 시작하엿는데 그 소설을 집필하기까지에는 무수한 고민과 쓰라린 사정이 많이 엉크러저 잇섯다고-.  첫재, 氏는 아직 안전한 자유의 人이 못되여 잇는 몸으로 氏의 처음 내심으로는 「그일」이 완전히 귀결을 짓기까지는 아직 절대로 붓대를 안 잡기로 결심하엿섯스나 전작 자긔의 가정을 나와 살펴보니 그날그날의 생활이 막연하고 더구나 신문사에서는 집필하여 달나고 간곡한 청을 하고하니 일이 여긔에 이르러서는 氏로는 무던히 속을 쎅이엿다든가.
그러나 한가지 반가운 소식은 그가 이번 全州행에서 소설가로서에 가장 귀여운 계기를 지엇다고 하니 그것은 1년여의 그 생활에서 18개의 소설 구상을 하여가지고 나왓다고 어느 풍문에 들여오더라.<6>
