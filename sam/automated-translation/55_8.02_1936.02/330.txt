**Body Text Translation:**
Heavy Tone Sung Narrative: "The Heartbreak Song of Yŏngwŏl"
						Performed by Haeulhŏn Hyŏn Ch'ŏl

The first day of the First Month of the Chŏngch'uk year (丁丑年)! Even when nothing special happens, people's hearts feel strange as evening approaches today. How much more so for someone in my position, facing the end of the year in such a place – how could I not feel dismayed (脹然)? Even in private homes, when the New Year (설) comes, they hold memorial rites (祭祠) for their ancestors (祖上) and visit ancestral graves (先塋) for respects (省墓). What sins (罪惡) have I committed that are so numerous that for three years I haven't been able to pay respects (展拜) at the Royal Ancestral Shrine (宗廟) nor visit my parents' tombs (父母陵)? What is the duty (道理) of being someone's child (子息)? Moreover, who is there to offer even a bowl of cold water (冷水) for the lonely souls (孤魂) of the loyal subjects of the state (國家忠良) who died innocently (無辜) for me? If times were different...』

As he said this and wept, the court ladies (內人) attending him also cried until their throats choked up.

Court Lady: 『Your Majesty, Upper King (上監), please do not worry (軫念) too much. This situation has come about due to a temporary blockage of fortune (運數否塞); surely there will be a time when you see blue skies and a white sun (靑天白日) again. Today is the last day of the twelfth month, so although we have nothing, even cold water shows sincerity. If you just have the spirit tablets (紙榜), this lowly one (쇤, 小人) will offer cold water with utmost sincerity...』

Retired King (上王): 『Oh, alright then, I will write the spirit tablets (紙榜).』<152>

And so the Retired King personally began to write the spirit tablets.
He unfolded the paper and lifted the brush,
Tears blurred his vision, soaking his dragon robe (龍袍).
The first spirit tablet he wrote
Was for his grandfather,
King Sejong the Great (世宗大王), and his queen consort.
Next, for his father, King Munjong (文宗大王),
And his mother, Queen Hyŏndŏk (顯德王后). As he wrote,
What connection (因緣) from a past life or this life
Caused us to be born as mother and son (母子) in this world?
This body grew up without knowing her form or echo (形響),
Far from comforting her spirit (魂靈) through filial piety (孝道),
Because of this unworthy son (不肖),
Even her spirit tablet (位牌)
Was cast out from the Royal Ancestral Shrine (宗廟), making her a commoner (庶人).
This deep grievance (至寃) and extreme pain (極痛), this resentment (怨恨) reaching the heavens (徹天),
Under which sky can it be resolved?
He lamented, tears falling like rain.
Next, the spirit tablet he wrote
Was for the one from whom he received much nurturing grace (恩惠),
Whose small bones grew strong,
His grandmother, who was like a mother,
Lady Hyebin of the Yang clan (惠嬪楊氏), he wrote and placed it.
Next, Prince Anp'yŏng, his uncle (安平叔父), and his son,
And among his uncles, the eldest, Prince Hwaŭi (和義君), he also wrote.
After writing for all the royal relatives (宗室) in order (次例次例 順序),
He then turned the brush to the loyal ministers (忠臣) who died in the year Kyeyu (癸酉年),
From Hwangbo In (黃甫仁), Kim Chong-sŏ (金宗瑞), Chŏng Pon (鄭笨), Hŏ Hu (許詡),
He wrote them all according to their official rank (職品). Then he turned the brush again,
Seeking the souls of the Six Martyred Ministers (死六臣),
Whose loyal integrity (忠節) is revered throughout eternity (千古).
Sŏng Sŭng (成勝), Yu Ŭng-bu (兪應孚), Sŏng Sam-mun (成三問), Pak P'aengnyŏn (朴彭年), Yi Kae (李塏), Yu Sŏng-wŏn (柳誠源), Ha Wi-chi (河緯地), he wrote them.
After that, he wrote his maternal grandmother (外祖母), his maternal uncle (外叔) Mr. Kwŏn (權氏), and his parents-in-law (丈人丈母).
He even included his wet nurse (乳母), Yi O (李午), and her husband (夫妻), omitting none. After writing them all,
He composed a simple memorial address (祭文) and summoned the spirits (魂靈).
When the lady-in-waiting (女官) performed the rite (祭),
He personally poured the cup (杯)<153>
And consoled the loyal spirits (忠靈).
The tears flowing from his jade eyes (玉眼)
Overflowed the wine cup.
As days passed and months accumulated,
The pent-up resentment (怨恨) deepened.
Before long, spring arrived that year.
The sound of the cuckoo (杜鵑) crying sorrowfully from all directions (四方)
Further stirred the heart's feelings (心懷) of the grief-stricken man.
From time to time, he would ascend the pavilion (樓),
Gaze towards Hanyang (漢陽),
And sing this tearful song:
「The moon is setting,」
「The cuckoo spirit (蜀魄) cries sorrowfully,」
「A person wrapped in sorrowful thoughts (愁情)」
「Leans on the pavilion railing (樓頭).」
「Because you cry painfully,」
「It is even sadder for me to hear.」
「If only your voice did not exist,」
「My worries would also not exist.」
「To the troubled people of this world (人間),」
「I address my words:」
「Lest you happen, in the third month (三月) of spring when the cuckoo (子規) cries,」
「Do not climb the Bright Moon Pavilion (明月樓).」
The desolate (凄凉), sad sound
Reached far and near (遠近),
And the common people (百姓) heard it
And were moved to tears.
Even while spending these years (歲月) full of resentment,
Unable to forget the profound affection of the former king,
People came in disguise (變裝) to Yŏngwŏl (寧越)
To offer tearful greetings (承候) without end.
Among them were Cho Sang-ch'i (曹尙治), Ku In-mun (具人文), Wŏn Ho (元昊), Kwŏn Chŏl (權節), Song Kan (宋侃), Pak Kye-son (朴季孫), and Yu Cha-mi (柳自湄).
And Kim Si-sŭp (金時習), known as Maewŏltang (梅月堂), the righteous man (義人) of eternity (千古),
Though he had never been his subject (臣下),
Could not overcome his surging righteous indignation (義憤).
He wandered the streets, mad,
Shaved his head and became a monk,
Roaming the mountains, rivers, lands, and skies (江山天地).
He looked at the heavens and wept bitterly (痛哭),<154>
He looked at the earth and wept bitterly,
He looked at the green mountains (靑山) and wept bitterly,
He looked at the green waters (綠水) and wept bitterly.
In spring and autumn (春秋), he secretly came to Yŏngwŏl
To inquire after (問候) the pitiful king.
His affection (情意) was ever more sincere (慇懃).
Flowing time (歲月) knows no pause,
And on the 24th day of the 10th month of the Chŏngch'uk year (丁丑年),
The cruel schemes of the treacherous villains (姦凶) still remained.
Poison (死藥) was sent down from Sŏul,
But as a subject (人臣), one could not bear to administer it.
With the bowstring of Kim Yong-gan (金用干), the 通引各 (t'ong'ingak - an official title, likely related to palace communication or escort), whose crime deserved a thousand cuts and myriad executions (千斬萬戮),
An eternal resentment (寃恨) was left behind.
How could Heaven and Earth and the Gods (天地神明) be so indifferent (無心)?
That 통인 (t'ong'in) fellow, right on the spot,
Bled from his nine orifices and died instantly (卽死).
O Sun and Moon (日月), I ask you! In all the nations under heaven (天下萬邦),
Deposed emperors and kings (失位 帝王) are not just one or two,
But is there another like King Tanjong (端宗大王)?
And in fratricide (骨肉相殘),
Is there another like Grand Prince Suyang (首陽大君)?
And in betrayal of grace and forgetting virtue (背恩忘德),
Were there others like Chŏng In-ji (鄭麟趾) and Sin Suk-chu (申叔舟)?
When a sovereign (人君) is insulted (辱), it is natural (當然)
In the loyalty between ruler and subject (君臣之義) for the subject (臣下) to die,
But were there ever others like the Six Ministers (六臣) of King Tanjong?
The great loyalty and integrity (精忠大節) bound in every character (字字), the great righteousness and moral duty (大義名分) entwined in every phrase (句句),
How can the true feelings (眞情) be fully depicted
In this insufficient single chapter (一章) of Kadam (歌談)?
Later (後日), dividing the sections (句節) again,
The venerable deeds (行蹟) of our ancestors (先人)
Will be praised (贊揚) together with you all,
So that the points of pride in our history (歷史)
Are known by us and by others,
Hoping it may contribute to a meaningful life (生活).<155>
This is a section of the newly created Kadam,
Though inadequate, presented to you all...
-End-

Postscript (后言)

Those of you who know Hyŏn Ch'ŏl (玄哲) might vaguely think of him as Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, the man of theater (演劇). However, Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, the man of theater for the past 20 years, has ended in utter failure (失敗). Why did Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, while studying abroad (留學) in Tokyo (東京) 25 years ago, cast aside numerous fields of study (學問) and choose to learn at a theater school (演劇學校)? The motive (動機) and purpose (目的) are not the focus here, so I will omit (略) that. However, the work I have carried out in the past stemmed from the thought that to establish (樹立) genuine drama (劇) in our Chosŏn (朝鮮), we must first nurture actors (俳優) of character (人格的). I attempted (着手) actor training (養成) five times, but not once did I achieve (達) the goal (目的), nor did I produce a single actor. Instead, I only garnered empty ridicule (嘲笑), wasted time (虛 歲月), and depleted my already insufficient personal funds (私財).

However, since this was my original goal (本來 目的), I couldn't bear to completely abandon it. Last year, to understand the actual situation (實際), I even followed a certain theater troupe (某劇壇) on a regional tour (地方巡業) and observed the actual circumstances (實際形便). And so, alas (於時乎), I realized it wasn't the way. I learned that given the current situation in Chosŏn, neither the theory (理論) nor the practice (實際) was right. With tears in my eyes, I decided (作定) to part ways with the theater world (劇界), which I had intended to be my lifelong companion (平生). However, I cannot completely forget the original intention (本뜻) of giving something to our masses (大衆). Thus, for over 10 years, even while running the theater school, I always thought that if a collaborative work like theater, involving many elements, didn't work out, there should be something that could be done individually (單獨). What I considered was Samdam (三談). In other words, if collaborative work like theater doesn't synchronize, I wondered if there was something like theater that could be done alone. That was Samdam.

The term Samdam (三談) is a newly coined term (名詞). Samdam refers to "Sandam" (散談 - scattered talk/monologue), "Yŏndam" (演談 - performed talk/dramatic monologue), and "Kadam" (歌談 - sung narrative). I published one piece of Sandam last April in the magazine *Chungang* (中央), but I have never published Yŏndam. I plan to discuss Sandam and Yŏndam when I publish them respectively. Since this is the first time I am publishing Kadam, I would like to briefly talk about Kadam so that all readers under heaven (滿天下 讀者諸位) can get an idea of what it is. Nowadays, the words "interest/enjoyment" (趣味) and "practical benefit" (實益) are frequently used in work aimed at the masses (大衆). Here, we should consider further: Do we humans need art (藝術)? Do we need entertainment (娛樂)? Or do we need solace (慰安)? I do not intend to elaborate (張皇) on the necessity argument (必要論) here.<156> However, if they are indeed necessary, then for our masses, interest (趣味) and practical benefit (實益) must be united (合一) for it to contribute (於是乎) to culture (文化) and add to moral edification (敎化). The art, entertainment, and solace (慰安物) that have the most contact (接觸) with our masses are likely film (映畵), theater (演劇), and music (音樂). However, film has not yet established its footing (立脚) with Chosŏn productions, and the same can be said for theater. Among these, however, with the development (發達) of radio (라듸오) in recent years (近年) and the proliferation (旺盛) of phonographs (蓄音器), listening to music (聽樂) has, from a mass perspective, a greater potential (可能) to become closer to us than anything else. Film and theater, with their complex content (內容 複雜), cannot easily connect (接觸) with the masses. But something like music is, among these various forms, the easiest (簡便) and most convenient (輕便) to listen to. This point has forged a significant connection (因緣) with our masses. The saying "Transforming customs and traditions is truly best achieved through music" (移風易俗 眞善於樂) seems to hold its deep meaning here. Even within such music, what is generally easier for the masses to understand is vocal music (聲樂) rather than instrumental music (器樂). Instrumental music finds its subtle flavor (隱味) in symbolizing (象徵) certain events (事件) through rhythm (리쯤), whereas vocal music lies in directly expressing (直喩) the meaning, thus possessing the most easily understandable and simple (簡便的) mass appeal (大衆性). Chosŏn music possessing this mass appeal, considering our past living conditions (生活 狀態), cannot but be said to have failed to develop in a mass-oriented way in terms of quality (質), quantity (量), or style (方式). In terms of style, almost everything is designed for a small number of people (小數人) to listen quietly indoors (室內). In terms of quantity, Namdo (南道) songs, Kyŏnggi (京畿) (Central region) songs, and Sŏdo (西道) songs all lack commonality (共通性). In terms of quality, almost all are short pieces (短的), and their value for moral edification (敎化的 價値) is arguably minimal. The reason it's not mass-oriented in style is that art aimed at the masses requires power (힘) and passion (熱), but mostly, the static parts (靜的 部分) outweigh the dynamic parts (動的 部分), featuring subtle nuance (隱微妙) more than overwhelming power (威壓力). Thus, while it has exquisite taste (絶妙味) when listened to quietly in a room, holding a long pipe and closing one's eyes, it feels inadequate (不足) when hundreds or thousands gather in a large building (大建物), craning their necks and focusing their eyes to listen. This can be seen as a drawback (缺点) from a commercial performance (興行的) perspective. In terms of quantity, Namdo songs, Kyŏnggi songs, and Sŏdo songs each have certain barriers (障壁), making them cliquish (小親的) rather than broadly unifying (大融的). Consequently, people from Kyŏnggi turn their heads away upon hearing Southwestern (西南道) songs, people from Namdo dismiss Kyŏnggi-Sŏdo medley (京西通) songs as not being songs, and people from Sŏdo likewise pay no attention (何等 關心 掛念) if it's not their own region's (自道) song. That is, it remains among acquaintances (小親的), and when performing, the singer always targets those acquaintances (標準). Therefore, rather than trying to make the lyrics (詞說) clear (明瞭), they focus (用意) only on vocal technique. They concentrate on the voice rather than conveying the content (內容), resulting in a lack (缺乏) of mass appeal and extension power (延長力). Furthermore, lyrics (歌詞)<157> and *sijo* (時調) are also based on (依持) certain class foundations (階級的 根據), making it difficult for them to advance (進出) to the masses. In terms of quality, the content (內容) is mostly vulgar (俗流). While certain parts might evoke interest (趣味), they lack the power for coherent content transmission (一貫的 內容傳達). Among these, *Ch'unhyangga* (春香歌), *Simch'ŏngga* (沈淸歌), *Hŭngbo chŏn* (興甫傳), or *Hwayongdo* (華容道) do strive (用力) for content transmission, but these too aim for a small audience (小親). Thus, the pressure (壓力) and passionate force (熱勢力) to maintain the consistent flow (氣脈 一貫) of the story (事件) are weak. They insert unrelated diversions (딴청), namely the performer's specialty pieces (長技 딴목), thereby obliterating (抹殺) the main story. For example (假令), in *Ch'unhyang chŏn* (春香傳), inserting a bird song (새타령) when the Royal Inspector (御史) is going to Namwŏn (南原), or in the story of Blindman Sim (沈盲人), inserting a pounding song (방아타령) when he goes to the capital (皇城), making the digression (沿文) longer than the main text (本文), or inserting irrelevant chatter (餘談) unrelated (何等 關係) to the story, not only diminishes (縮小) the mood (氣分) of advancing (進行) the original story (原事件) but also, unlike theater where multiple people (多數人) can appear on stage (登壇), when one person advances a story (事件), explanation (說明) is necessary, and if characters appear, dialogue (對談) is necessary, and to capture interest (興味), song melodies (歌調) are also needed. Therefore, unless the explanation, dialogue, and song melody can be connected sequentially (連續), it seems difficult to achieve narrative coherence (事件卽內容統貫). In this sense, while the appeal (趣味) known to Namdo song connoisseurs can be helpful, utilizing it for both mass appeal (大衆的) interest (趣味) and practical benefit (實益) seems rather difficult. Next, among a portion of the intellectual class (智識階級), Western songs (西洋唱歌) play a role. Of course (勿論), since it's the music of civilized people (文明人), if one says it's good, the matter might be simply settled (簡單 處理) without further issues (病弊). However, one wonders how much it resonates with the heartstrings (心琴) of the Chosŏn masses (朝鮮大衆). While we might enjoy Western food (洋食) as a special treat (別味), for daily meals (常食), well, perhaps civilized (文明人) Koreans are different, but for this Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, still full of barbaric taste (野蠻味 津津), there are many times when it seems inferior to kimchi and kkakdugi. Furthermore, in solo singing (獨唱) done purely in Western languages, how many people truly (果然) understand? It is said that in some Western country (某西洋), the Japanese *kappore* (カツポレ - a lively dance/song) was mistaken for a sad melody (悲調) and even featured in an international broadcast (國際效送 - likely 放送) as an expression of condolence (吊意) during someone's mourning period (喪中). This shows us how different the feelings (늣김) of sorrow and joy (悲喜) can be between East and West (東西). However, if this too can be utilized (利用) by us, shouldn't we utilize it as much as possible? Simply importing something because civilized people do it, covering up (덥허 놓고) its lack of necessity (必要), is not something to be welcomed (歡迎). But if something is truly necessary for us, we should bring it over as much as possible and make it ours. If you make Chosŏn shoes (朝鮮신발) out of leather (가죽), you're called a lowly cobbler (갓바치), the lowest of the low (下之下賤), but if you make Western shoes (구두) out of leather, you're an industrialist (工業家) or businessman (實業家). Let's set aside such illogical (理由 닷지 안는) matters for a moment. If you sing Western songs, you're an artist (藝術家), a respectable person. Newspapers (新聞紙) should support you wholeheartedly (全面 應援). If you sing Chosŏn songs, you're a clown (광대); associating with you is shameful (昌皮). You can play the piano (피아노) in the lecture hall (講堂), but not the hourglass drum (長鼓). Chosŏn dance (朝鮮춤), covering even the back of the hands, is lowly (賤), but Western dance (西洋춤), exposing up to the groin, is noble (高尙). Truly admirable deeds! Didn't we learn "What is learned in youth is practiced in adulthood" (幼而學之 將而行之)?<158> When learning something good from others that we lack (내게 없는), shouldn't we make it our own (내것)? Shouldn't we learn good things from others and make them our own good things? No matter how good Western things are, if they are not necessary (必要) for me, isn't it like a chicken eating a jewel (寶石)? After spending 5 or 10 years in the West (西洋), is the only outcome smoking cigarettes face-to-face with one's father (애비) or wanting to shake hands (握手) with one's younger brother's wife (弟嫂)? Surely none of that is necessary for us, is it? Of course (勿論), Western civilization (西洋文明) is a dynamic civilization (動的文明), and our Eastern civilization (東洋文明) is a static civilization (靜的文明). Currently (現時), the dynamic is likely better than the static in whatever aspect (方面) one considers. However, just because dynamic is better, if one doesn't sit still but instead wanders around Chongno Intersection (鍾路 네거리) with a club (몽둥이), hitting passersby indiscriminately (함부로) and smashing houses here and there, wouldn't that be outrageous (言語道斷)? Dynamic is good if it's based on necessity (必要), and static is good if it's based on necessity. What is necessary and what is not depends on each individual's (個人) method (方法) and circumstances (形便). When we always look at the masses (大衆), what do our masses need? Of course, the masses need many things, but considering what is necessary for the masses within my own capacity (自身 力量), I have thought since 25 years ago that what our masses need is theater (演劇). That's why, despite the opposition (反對) of family (家族), relatives (親戚), and friends (友人), I enrolled (不拘 入學) in theater school. However, my efforts (努力) in this field (斯界) for the subsequent (爾後) 20 years yielded no results (何等 成果). Thus, even as I give up (斷念) on this field entirely, I am reluctant to leave the masses. My past failure (失敗) was in nurturing many people (多數人); this time, I wondered if there wasn't something that didn't require many people, something that could be done alone (獨自), and what I chose myself (自擇) is this Samdam. And among Samdam, Kadam aims to advance (進展) a story (事件) through explanation (說明), dialogue (對話), and lyrics (歌詞), presenting that story to the masses (大衆) to aid in moral edification (敎化) and be interesting (興味) to listen to. This is the main point (眼目) of Kadam. Now, one issue (問題) arises here: how should the lyrics (歌詞), that is, the song, be performed? This requires the convergence (符合) of at least three conditions (條件).
一. The singing style (歌調) should take a middle ground (中間) between Namdo songs, Kyŏnggi songs, Sŏdo songs, and Western solo singing (西洋 獨唱), striving to harmonize (調和) the tastes (趣味) of the currently walled-off (墻壁) song styles.
一. The song structure (歌制) must allow the narrative text (說明文), dialogue (對話), and lyrics (歌詞) to connect and flow continuously (接續連續) to advance (進展) the story (事件).
一. The vocal tone (歌音) must originate not just from the throat (項程) but from the *tanjŏn* (丹田), i.e., the lower abdomen (下腹) below the navel. The vocal color (聲色) should be produced by pressing the throat to bring out an inner voice/falsetto (속청), so that the voice has passion (熱) and power (力/힘), possessing an overwhelming force (威壓 勢力) capable of commanding (率制) the masses. Consequently, art should attain its life force (生命) which is passion and power.
It is under these three goals (目標) that I have researched (硏究) this Kadam.<159> Regardless of the art form (藝術), bringing its life force to fruition requires at least 10 years of dedicated study (專攻); otherwise, success cannot be expected (可期). I always thought that when dealing with our masses, if multiple people can do it, theater is good, but if one person has to do it, Samdam would be good. However, it seems difficult in our circumstances (形便) to create truly genuine works (物件) in theater, which involves many people. Therefore, starting from early December two years ago, I began practical research (實地 着手) into this Samdam. I built a dugout hut (움집) in Sŏngbuk-tong (城北洞), a mountain village (산골) not far from Kyŏngsŏng (京城), and stayed there in seclusion (杜門不出) until last October, for a full 10 months. Though 10 months is a short time (歲月), even humans (人間), considered the most precious (最貴) in the universe (宇宙間), emerge into the world (世上) after 10 months in the mother's womb (母胎). In this sense, this Samdam, after being incubated (抱胎) in the hut for 10 months, let out its first cry (呱呱) towards the Three Thousand Li (三千里 - Korea) from the delivery room (産室) of the Kyŏngsŏng Central Broadcasting Station (京城中央效送局) on October 21st last year, with Mr. Kim Un-jŏng (金雲汀) and Mr. Ch'oe Sŭng-il (崔承一) acting as midwives (産婆). Of course (勿論), this first birth cry (産聲) is not considered a success (成功). It is enough just to receive an assessment (鑑定) of whether this newborn baby can grow well, or whether, when grown in the future (將來), it will provide even a little benefit (有益) to us or not. Fortunately, according to the assessment of these two midwives, it was judged to have future potential (將來). However, how should we raise this newborn child now? Should we feed it human milk (人乳) or cow's milk (牛乳)? Various issues (問題) remain ahead. But I myself believe that if I raise it well until the age of ten, it will become something substantial (相當). For now, I do not shy away from changing its diapers (오줌 똥 자리). Now that it has already been born (出生), it must continually interact (接觸) with the masses. There are three types (三種) of this Kadam: one is Chungjogado (重調歌 - Heavy Tone Sung Narrative), one is P'yŏngjogadam (平調歌談 - Level Tone Sung Narrative), and another is Kyŏngjogadam (輕調歌談 - Light Tone Sung Narrative). Comparing these to our old modes (古調), Chungjogado is like the *kyemyŏnjo* (界面調) mode, corresponding to the *sang* pitch (商聲), in the spirit of: "A loyal soul (忠魂) sinks in the river (沉江), resentment (恨) fills the land of Ch'u (滿楚). Gazing west (西望) towards Tongjŏng Lake (洞庭), the water ends at the southern sky (水盡南天), no clouds are seen (不見雲). The sun sets over Changsha (日落長沙), autumn colors stretch far (秋色遠); I know not where (不知何處) to mourn Lord Xiang (吊相君)." (A collage evoking Du Fu's poem about Qu Yuan). P'yŏngjogadam is like the *yŏ* pitch (閭聲) of the *p'yŏngjo* (平調) mode, in the spirit of: "When the *hwangjong* pitch (黃鍾) sounds once, all things (萬物) herald spring (皆春). The moon reaches the heart of heaven (月到天心處), the wind arrives upon the water's surface (風來水面時). This common, clear meaning (一般淸意味), few people understand (料得少人知)." (Quote from Cheng Hao). Kyŏngjogado is like the *u* pitch (羽聲) of the *ujo* (羽調) mode, in the spirit of: "When the Jade Dipper (玉斗) is struck and shattered (撞破), the broken pieces clang sharply (碎層鏘鏘). Snow purifies the northern sky (雪淨胡天), grazing horses return (牧馬還). The moon shines brightly on the flute (月明差笛), the watchtower is quiet (戍樓閑). May I ask where the plum blossoms fell (借問梅花何處落)? The wind blew one night, filling the border mountains (風吹一夜滿關山)." (Quote from Li Bai). This is not meant to imply strict conformity (符合) to music theory (樂理的). It merely conveys a general mood (意趣). Of course, discordant (失調) or mixed modes (混調) will likely constitute the majority, and there is absolutely no need to be constrained (拘束) by these modes (律調) for the progression of the story (事件). Please do not even compare (對照) them; just understand that such an intention exists. The Heavy Tone Sung Narrative "The Heartbreak Song of Yŏngwŏl" (重調歌談 『寧越斷腸曲』) published here is the very first publication of Kadam. From now on, through further refinement (練磨), I aim for live performances (實演) before the masses throughout Korea (全鮮的) to receive nurturing guidance (薰育 指導). Simultaneously, I will continuously publish (繼續的 發表) new works (新作) of Samdam, aiming to contribute (보탬) to meaningful entertainment (有意 娛樂), interest (趣味), and practical benefit (實益) for our masses,<160> while also creating materials for social edification (社會 敎化物). Thus, I intend to forge a path forward under the banner (標榜) of "Samdam Kyohwa" (三談敎化 - Samdam Moral Edification). Lastly, this Kadam absolutely requires accompaniment (伴奏). For 발표 (p'alpyo - presentation/performance, likely meaning accompaniment here), among Western instruments (西洋樂器), the guitar (키타) is good, and among Chosŏn instruments (朝鮮樂器), the *hyŏn'gŭm* (玄琴) or *kayagŭm* (伽耶琴) would be suitable. Initially, I intended to experiment (試驗) with the guitar, but the accompanist did not arrive by the broadcast time (放送時間), so I unavoidably (不得已) performed without accompaniment. As a result (結果), there was no time to adjust breathing (呼吸) and vocal cords (聲帶轉換), leading to the failure (失敗) of not producing the intended vocal sound (所期 音聲 發聲). Another point is that the full text (全文) of the "Heartbreak Song" requires about 47 minutes, but since the broadcast time is typically 30 minutes (通例), it was shortened (주리게). Therefore, I mention that this version is shorter than the full piece (全曲). I append these notes (附記) to explain how Kadam, one type of Samdam – aiming to create a foundation for new mass entertainment (大衆娛樂) in our society (社會) – came into being and how it will proceed. Please excuse my presumptuous words (妄言) and lengthy explanation (張皇). Many thanks (多謝).<161>

**Summary**

This article presents "The Heartbreak Song of Yŏngwŏl," a narrative song (Kadam) about the exiled King Tanjong, performed by Hyŏn Ch'ŏl. The first part depicts Tanjong's grief during his exile, particularly his lamentations and performance of ancestral rites for deceased family and loyal retainers. The second part is a postscript by Hyŏn Ch'ŏl explaining his artistic shift from conventional theater, which he deems impractical in contemporary Chosŏn, to a new solo performance form called "Samdam" (Sandam, Yŏndam, Kadam). He critiques existing Korean performance traditions (like pansori) and the adoption of Western music for their inability to effectively engage and edify the masses, outlining Kadam's goal to blend narration, dialogue, and song in a way that is both entertaining and culturally meaningful, drawing from Korean heritage while adapting to modern needs and aiming for mass appeal and moral instruction ("Samdam Kyohwa"). For colonial Korean history, the piece is revealing in its discussion of cultural identity and the search for modern artistic forms suitable for the Korean masses under Japanese rule. Hyŏn Ch'ŏl's critique of both traditional Korean arts (seen as potentially stagnant or cliquish) and wholesale adoption of Western forms (seen as potentially alienating or inappropriate) reflects the complex cultural negotiations of the era. His ambition to create a "Chosŏn-specific" mass entertainment form that provides both "interest and practical benefit" (趣味와 實益) and serves a didactic, culturally affirming purpose ("Samdam Kyohwa") highlights the desire to foster national consciousness and cultural resilience through popular arts, even within the constraints of colonialism.

后言

玄哲 아시는 여러분은 얼풋 생각하시기를 演劇의 사람 玄哲이라고 아실 것입니다. 그러나 過去 20년동안 연극의 사람 현철은 여지없이 失敗에 도라가고 말앗음니다. 玄哲이 엇지하여 25년전 東京에 留學을 하면서 許多한 學問을 다 던저두고 배운 바이 演劇學校이던가. 그 動機와 그 目的은 구타여 여긔서 說明해 드릴 場面이 안이므로 그것은 略함니다. 그러나 過去에 해내려온 일은 우리 朝鮮에 잇서서 劇다운 劇을 樹立하는 대는 먼저 人格的 俳優를 養成하여야 하겟다는 생각으로 다섯 번이나 俳優養成에 着手하여 보왓으나 그 中에 한번도 目的을 達하지 못하엿고 또한 한 사람도 俳優를 만들지 못하고 空然한 嘲笑와 虛된 歲月과 애매한 넉넉지 못한 私財만 다없세고 말앗음니다.
그러나 本來에 目的해 오던 바이라 참아 이것을 아주 노치지 못하여 昨年에는 그 實際를 알기 위하여 某劇壇과 한가지 地方巡業에까지 딸아가서 實際形便도 살펴보왓음니다. 그리하여 於時乎 그 안임을 알게 되엿음니다. 現時 우리 朝鮮處地로는 그 理論도 안입이 잇고 그 實際에도 안임을 알게 되엿음니다. 나는 눈물을 먹음고 平生을 동무하자는 劇界와 손을 난호게 作定하엿음니다. 그러나 우리 大衆에게 무엇을 주자는 그 本뜻만은 아주 이저버릴 수가 없음니다. 그리하여 10여년 전붙어 演劇學校를 經營할 적에도 萬一 演劇과 같이 여러 가지를 綜合해서 하는 일이 안이되면 單獨으로 될 만한 것이 잇서야 하겟다는 生覺을 하고 늘 생각해 오던 것이 三談이올시다. 다시 말슴하면 演劇과 같이 여러시 하는 일이 손이 안 맞지 안으면 혼자서 할 수 잇는 演劇과 같은 것이 없나 생각한 것이 三談이올시다.
三談이란 말슴은 새로이 만든 名詞이올시다. 三談이라고 하는 것은 「散談」「演談」「歌談」을 말하여 三談이라고 하엿음니다. 散談의 하나를 昨年 4月에-雜誌 中央에 한번 發表한 것이 잇으나 演談은 아직 한번도 發表한 일이 없음니다. 散談이나 演談은 各各 그것을 發表할 때에 말슴할 양으로 하고 여긔에는 첫 번으로 歌談을 發表하는 자리이니 歌談에 대한 말슴을 暫間하여 滿天下 讀者諸位에게 歌談이 무엇인가 하는 것을 짐작하시게 해 볼가함니다. 요사이 행용 많이 스는 말이 大衆을 相對하여 하는 일에 趣味와 實益이라는 말을 많이 함니다. 여긔에서 한번더 생각해 볼 것은 우리 人間에게는 藝術이 必要한가? 娛樂이 必要한가? 또는 慰安이 必要한가? 그 必要論에 대해서는 구타여 여긔에<156> 張皇히 말슴하고자 안이함니다. 그러나 萬一 그것이 必要하다고 하면 우리 大衆에게는 趣味와 實益이 合一하여야 於是乎 文化에 도음이 되고 敎化에 보태미 잇을 것입니다. 우리의 大衆과 가장 많이 接觸되는 藝術, 娛樂, 慰安物은 映畵, 演劇, 音樂 等일 것입니다. 그러나 映畵도 아직 우리 朝鮮 것으로는 立脚을 이루지 못 하엿고 演劇도 亦是 그러타고 할 수 밖에 없음니다. 그러한데 이 中에도 近年에는 「라듸오」가 發達해가는 한편 蓄音器가 旺盛함으로 大衆的으로 보와서는 무엇보담도 이 聽樂이 우리에게 가까워질 可能을 많이 가지고 잇다고 볼 수가 잇음니다. 映畵나 演劇은 그 內容이 複雜한만치 簡單이 大衆과 接觸할 수는 없게 되엿음니다. 그러나 音樂같은 것은 이 여러 가지 中에도 가장 簡便하게 들을 수도 잇고 가장 輕便하게 들을 수도 잇는 것입니다. 이렇한 点이 우리 大衆과 많은 因緣을 맷게 된 것이니 移風易俗이 眞善於樂이라는 말도 그 깊은 意味가 여긔에 잇는 듯함니다. 이러한 音樂 가운데도 大衆이 一般으로 알게 쉽게는 器樂보담도 聲樂이라고 할 수 잇는 것이니 器樂은 어떠한 事件을 「리쯤」으로써 象徵하는 데에 그 隱味가 잇으나 聲樂은 그 意味를 直喩하는데 잇는 만치 가장 알게숩게 簡便的인 大衆性을 가젓다고 볼 수가 잇음니다. 이러한 大象性을 가진 朝鮮의 音樂은 過去의 우리 生活 狀態로 보와 質로써나 量으로써나 또한 그 方式으로써 大象的으로 發達이 되여오지 못하엿다고 할 수 밖에 없으니 方式으로써는 그 어너 것이나 小數人이 室內에서 고요이 聽樂하게 되여잇고 量으로써는 南道소리, 京畿소리(中部소리) 西道소리가 다 共通性이 없고 質로써는 모도가 短的이요 敎化的 價値가 가상 적은 것이라고 안이할 수가 없음니다. 方式으로써 大衆的이 안인 理由는 大衆을 相對하는 藝術이 힘과 熱이 안이면 안이될 것인대 大槪는 動的 部分보담도 靜的 部分이 많아서 威壓力보다 隱微妙가 많음으로 고요한 房 안에서 긴 담배때 물고 눈 지것이 감고 듯기는 絶妙味잇으나 大建物 안에서 數百名 數千名이 한듸 모이여 목을 길게 빼고 눈을 한데 모와 듯기는 不足한 늣김이 잇음으로 興行的으로 缺点이라고 볼 수가 잇고 量으로써 南道소리나 京畿소리나 西道소리가 各各 어떠한 障壁이 잇서 小親的이요 大融的이 안입으로 京畿사람은 西南道소리를 들으면 고개를 돌이게 되고 南道사람은 京西通소리를 들으면 소리 안이라고 하며 西道사람 亦是 自道소리가 안이면 何等의 關心을 하지 안이하고 掛念을 안이하게 하니 즉 小親的으로 아는 사람끼리로 되여서 소리할 때에 항상 아는 그 사람을 標準하는 만치 詞說을 明瞭이 할야고 하는 것보담 목소리 쓰는 대만 用意함으로 內容을 일우자는 것보다도 목소리에만 힘을 써서 大衆的 延長力이 缺乏되는 늣김이 잇고 그 다음 歌詞나<157> 時調같은 것도 一部 階級的 根據에 依持되여 잇음으로 大衆的으로 進出되기 어려운 것이고 質로써는 內容이 모도 俗流되여 어떠한 部分的으로 趣味를 늣길는지는 몰으나 一貫的 內容傳達에는 힘이 적게 되엿으니 이 中에 春香歌, 沈淸歌, 興甫傳, 혹은 華容道 같은 것은 內容傳達에도 用力은 한 것이나 이것 亦 小親을 目的 함으로 事件의 氣脈을 一貫하는 壓力과 熱勢力 적어 中間에 딴청, 卽者의 長技되는 딴목을 너어 그 事件을 抹殺하는 것이니 假令 春香傳이 御史가 南原가는데
