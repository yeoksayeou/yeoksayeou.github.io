**Summary**

This article from the February 1936 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine serves as a comprehensive guide to entrance procedures for various secondary and post-secondary schools in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul). It lists details for universities, colleges (chŏnmun hakkyo), higher common schools (kodŭng pot'ong hakkyo for boys, yŏja kodŭng pot'ong hakkyo for girls), vocational schools, and other specialized institutes. Information includes founding dates, key personnel (founders, principals, deans), duration of study, recruitment quotas, application deadlines, examination dates, and required subjects. For historians, this article provides a valuable snapshot of the educational landscape in colonial Korea's capital. It highlights the hierarchy of institutions (from Imperial University down to vocational schools), the mix of Japanese-established schools (like Kyŏngsŏng Imperial University, Kyŏngsŏng First Higher Common School) and Korean-founded or missionary schools (like Posŏng, Yŏnhŭi, Severance, Ewha, Chungang), the presence of both Japanese and Korean administrators, and the curriculum emphasis, notably the requirement of "National Language" (國語, likely Japanese) alongside Classical Chinese (漢文) and sometimes Korean (朝鮮語) or English, reflecting the linguistic policies and educational structure under Japanese rule. The distinct types of schools for boys and girls, and the emergence of specialized training like childcare and medicine for women, are also noteworthy.
