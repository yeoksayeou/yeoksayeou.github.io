Welcoming the New Year
						By Chŏkku (赤駒)

Between the old year
and the new year,
Fate allows us
the glory of dividing Happiness.
The past year, the old year,
most peacefully
lets us have hope for the future
and reflect upon the bygone days.

Ah, the time of Anguish (苦惱)
has already passed.
Troublesome Sincerity (信實) too—
Painful love too—
More valuable days
now gather us in one place<238>
With cheerful songs
they delight our hearts.

Remaining joys
and vanished Pains (苦痛),
we hold hands
and joyfully, joyfully reminisce.
Oh, the inscrutable Providence (攝理)
of Fate (運命)!
Old Bonds (結合)!
New Gifts!

For the Happiness
approaching ahead,
be thankful.
For all the Blessings (惠澤)
sent by Fate (運命),
be thankful.
And rejoice in Change.
Cheerful Instincts—
Honest Affection (愛情)—
Inner Passion (內心)—<239>

The curtain covering
the old year,
some may gaze upon it
sadly, fearfully,
but the earnestly trusted light (光明) of the new year
is now shining above our heads.
Behold! The new year
makes even us new.

Just as, in a Dance Hall (舞踏場),
a loving pair of man and woman
might vanish from sight
and then reappear,
through the endlessly tangled
Maze (迷路) of Life (人生),
Oh Love (愛情)!
Guide us
into this—the light (光明) of the New Year.
-From Goethe’s (꿰-테) Poetry Collection-<240>

**Summary**

This 1936 poem by "Chŏkku" (Red Horse) celebrates the transition to the New Year as a moment granted by Fate to embrace happiness and hope, leaving behind past anguish and pain. It calls for gratitude for future blessings and encourages rejoicing in change, cheerful instincts, honest affection, and inner passion. Using the metaphor of dancers reappearing and invoking Love, it asks for guidance through life's maze into the "light" of the new year, referencing Goethe as inspiration. From a historical perspective, the poem, published during Japanese colonial rule, focuses on universal themes of renewal and hope. While not overtly political, its emphasis on overcoming past suffering ("苦惱," "苦痛") and looking towards a bright future ("光明") might subtly reflect the underlying aspirations of Koreans during a difficult period, expressed through acceptable literary themes perhaps made safer by the reference to a Western classic like Goethe.

새해를 마즈며
						赤駒
묵은 해와
새해와의 사히에
幸福을 눗기는 光榮을
運命은 우리들에게 허락한다
지나간 해 묵은 해는
가장 平和롭게
우리들에게
前途의 希望을 갖게하고
往古를 回顧케 한다

아아 苦惱의 때는
이미 지나갓다
煩惱롭든 信實도-
쓰라리든 사랑도-
보다 갑어치 잇는 날은
이제 우리들을 한자리에 모아놋코<238>
快活한 노래로써
우리들의 마음을 즐겁게 한다

남은 깃붐과
사러즌 苦痛과를
우리들은 손에 손을 맛잡고
깃부게 깃부게 回想한다
오오 運命의
不可思議한 攝理!
묵은 結合이여
새로운 선물이여!

압흐로 닥처오는
幸福에 感謝하라
運命의 보내는
모-든 惠澤에 感謝하라
그리고 變化를 깃버하라
快活한 本能을-
率直한 愛情을-
內心의 熱을-<239>

묵은 해를
뒤덥는 幕을
슯흐게 두려웁게
바라보는 사람도 있으나
간절히 밋거운 새해의 光明은
이제 우리들의 머리 위에 빗나고 잇다
보라! 새로운 해는
우리들까지도 새롭게 한다

흡사히 舞踏場에서
사랑하는 한 쌍의 男女가
자최를 감추엇다가
다시 나타나 듯이
끗입시 얼크러진
人生의 迷路를 通하야
오오 愛情이여!
우리들을
이해-새해의 光明으로 引導하라
-「꿰-테」의 詩集에서-<240>
