발행일	1931년 06월 01일
기사제목	失業京城 (Unemployed Kyŏngsŏng/Seoul)
필자	李亮 (Yi Ryang)
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)

失業京城
						李亮
"Seoul," at least, seems like a place where Koreans live. This was the first greeting a friend from the countryside offered when he came one morning.
I have known this country friend through correspondence for a very long time. We were together when I went to the Hitachi Mine (日立鑛山) in Ibaraki Prefecture (茨城縣), and we were also together in Siberia (西伯利亞), chewing black rye bread (黑麥包) while wandering.
Like me, he has drifted here and there for nearly 20 years, aiming for this or that.
Now, both he and I, having passed 30 without accomplishing anything, merely show the traces of hardship etched on our foreheads and in our eyes.

But I know well why this friend says such things. Because of his family, he abandoned the Korean movement and returned to his hometown, starting a small general store (雜貨商舖). He didn't have any capital from the start; his father's sole inheritance, a thatched-roof house (草家집), was mortgaged to the Financial Cooperative (金融組合) for 300 or 350 *wŏn*, which he used to sell inexpensive <66> daily necessities like rubber shoes, cotton cloth (美英疋), and blank notebooks (空冊).
However, in this world of capital competition, especially for someone as plain (武骨) as him with not an inch of commercial knowledge, how could there be any knack for customer service or managing product supply? Furthermore, with capital gradually shrinking due to the hardships of life, there was absolutely no way for the business to prosper.
Just as the store he watched shrinks day by day, the lives of the country folk shrink day by day. Especially as late spring arrives and early summer comes, the barley harvest is still far off, and there is no food to eat; the state of rural farming villages is truly a tearful scene.
He saw this in the countryside. He heard stories of this hard life from his relatives. And he saw the rough yards of farmhouses, where dried grass stalks and pine branches lay scattered around thatched houses like turtle shells (겨딱지). After looking at this day after day, he came up to Seoul this morning.
Here in Seoul, at least, electric trams whiz by, streets are filled with women viewing flowers, and brick and concrete buildings stand colorful—the modern cultural city dazzles the eyes. Seeing this, he thinks back to the countryside. These words, though his first greeting, are a sigh of lament.

After catching up on old feelings and sharing accumulated thoughts over a drink, we set out to see Seoul.
Seoul! Ah, this is Seoul, we thought, entering Namdaemun Street (南大門通), looking around Chin’gogae (진고개), stopping by Myŏngch’ijŏng (明治町), passing through Hwanggŭmjŏng (黃金町), and arriving at Chongno (鍾路). Then we wandered through the alleys of An’guk-tong (安國洞), Kwanhun-dong (寬勳洞), and Kyedong (桂洞)—the most central areas for Koreans in the northern part—and came out again onto Chongno.
Although I have a habit of wandering Chongno Street and other alleys every few days, whether I have business or not, this morning, because of this friend, it felt like seeing Seoul anew once more.

We passed the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) office in Kyŏnji-dong (堅志洞) and sought out Mr. K at the XX Hall (XX會舘). He seemed to have discovered a fact that absolutely needed discovering during this trip to Seoul.
The respected Mr. K whom we admire is currently engaged in a great debate with several others, speaking most eloquently about the unemployment problem I am about to discuss. However, the other friends just listened quietly, making it solely Mr. B's stage. Indeed, the unemployment problem is a major global issue. It is an unavoidable sickness in this capitalist world. It is estimated that the number of unemployed in Korea (朝鮮) has now reached nearly 1 million. Recently, it seems the authorities (當局) are extremely anxious (焦燥) about this unemployment problem. Issues like investigation committees (調査委員會), unemployment relief societies (失業者救濟會), and public works projects (土木事業) for unemployment relief are being discussed. However, this is an utterly nonsensical issue. Expecting a solution to the unemployment problem from capitalists or politicians is like trying to catch fish on a mountain. The solution to the unemployment problem lies with those who should be responsible, the working class (勞働階級). The working class, suffering the pain of unemployment, must see this problem as a matter of life and death and find its own method of resolution.
To elaborate further, the wages our working class receives are merely a very small portion (賃銀) of the value of the goods we produce. Therefore, the consumption volume of the world's total consuming class, namely the vast majority of the proletariat (無産者), the masses, <67> the people (國民), due to the contraction of their livelihoods, only leaves a surplus for the owners of the numerous products, the capitalists. Naturally, unsold surplus production forces factories to close their doors, entrepreneurs to lock up their bank books, and austerity policies prevent people from buying goods, further delaying market recovery.
Thus, the resolution of this unemployment problem inevitably depends on the awakening (覺醒) of the working class and its solidarity (團結).

So, how large is the unemployed population in Korea, and how many jobless people are there in this Seoul? We must examine the statistics.
However, there are no accurate statistics in Korea. Korea's unemployed population, unlike the working classes in foreign countries, lacks any labor legislation (勞働立案) or supplementary institutions with social facilities (社會的 施設). Furthermore, the unemployment registration offices (失業者登記所) necessary for a modern state do not yet exist. Moreover, the Korean people (朝鮮民), due to their traditional habits (傳來의 習性), lacked the national character (國民性) to resolve their own suffering and anxieties in a X-like manner. (Following 9 lines deleted - original text)
This is true for rural villages, cities, scholars, and economic commentators.
Korea today is an era of unemployment. It was also an era of unemployment before. The honorable poverty (淸貧志操) of the late 500 years of the Yi Dynasty (李朝) has now become the mindset of beggars (乞人意圖). Therefore, unemployment in Korea transcends time and numbers, making it extremely difficult to provide precise examples of the unemployed populace. However, if we estimate the number of unemployed in one city and one rural village that we know, we can roughly grasp the unemployment figures for Korea's 13 provinces (道) and 348 counties and prefectures (郡府). For example:
City of Wŏnsan (元山)
1928	1929	1903 [sic]
43,27 people   4,327 people   4,613 people
(Over 25% of the total population)
Rural Village of Kŭmje (金堤)
1928	1929	1930
3,200 people   3,461 people   3,496 people
(Over 15% of the total population)
Looking at these unemployment statistics, if Korea's population is 20 million, the number of unemployed would be at least 2 million—20% of the total population. However, considering the hardships in the lives of Koreans today, the unemployment rate might be 30% or 40%; the number is likely immense.

So, what about the issue of Kyŏngsŏng's unemployed—that is, unemployed Kyŏngsŏng? This Seoul of lumpenproletariat (이룸펜), this Seoul of rags (襤褸), this Seoul of hunger and thirst (飢渴症)—that is, this feverishly unemployed city of Seoul—its unemployed population likely reaches a staggering 50,000.
While this applies to all of Korea, one must wonder how Koreans in Seoul, in particular, manage to live, how they manage to eat. For any lifestyle analyst (生活計學者), the living conditions of Koreans in Seoul today would indeed be a difficult puzzle (難題). With a population of over 400,000, if each person eats just 5 *hop* (合) of rice per day, that would amount to 2,000 *sŏk* (石) daily. However, according to rice merchants (米穀商), the daily average is merely 1,500-1,600 *sŏk*. Excluding Japanese and other foreigners with different economic conditions, we know very well who is going hungry due to this shortfall. This isn't just something to be aware of; it is truly a <68> tearful reality.
Look at the Koreans walking on those streets. Look at the porters standing by the Pongnae Bridge (蓬萊橋) with only empty A-frames (빈지게). Look at the laborers wandering idly (어슬넝어슬넝) on Hwanggŭmjŏng, Chongno Street, and other streets. Look at the groups of child beggars huddled in every alley. And go look in front of the Yongsan (龍山) Army Supply Depot (兵站部). Go to the kitchen yard (炊車場) of the Police Training Institute (巡査講習所). Look at the Korean women gathering to buy bones (뼉다귀), cigarette butts (단배꼭지), rotten kimchi radishes (썩은 짐치무), and foul-smelling leftover food (殘飯)—this is the life of Koreans in Kyŏngsŏng today.
What little wealth exists is shown only by the spectacle of the landlord class (地主層), few in number, coming to Seoul to spend, revealing the remnants (殘骸) of Korean wealth. If the brokers (부로가-) huddled in inn corners—the so-called "thousand *nyang* dealers" (千兩판) and "ten thousand *nyang* dealers" (萬兩판)—represent Seoul's middle class (中産階級), and if the cave dwellers (土窟民) near Ogansumun Bridge (五間水다리) who scavenge cow and horse bones from the muddy waters of Ch’ŏnggyech’ŏn Stream (淸溪川) represent the proletariat (無産階級), then Seoul's proletariat is not the proletariat in the universal global sense, but rather a beggar class (乞人階級), a slave class (奴隷階級), XX-ed populace (XX된 百姓)—this is the Korean proletariat, these are the poor of Seoul today.
Recently, election campaigning for the Prefectural Assembly (府會) following the revision of local autonomy (地方自治改正) has intensified, and the contest (逐鹿) is proceeding amidst quite fierce campaigning. However, looking at the political platforms (政見) being announced, they are the typical jargon (常套語) of common merchants (凡俗商輩): sanitation facilities (衛生施設), school association fees (學校組合費), municipalization of electricity (電氣官營), and so on. While no expectations were originally placed on them, the discussion of people's livelihoods related to this autonomy revision, whether seen as part of a reform process (改良過程) or a path towards some political shadow (政治的 暗影), is connected to the political issue of the livelihood movement (生活運動). Therefore, it cannot be viewed with indifference (對岸視) from a position of principled refusal. We had hoped that today's unemployment problem—especially Kyŏngsŏng's unemployment problem—would be mentioned in the platform of at least one assembly member. Yet, there is not a hint of it.
Thus, regarding the demands of the people or the aspirations of the assembly members related to this revised autonomy bill, there are no hopes; they proceed as they are, and we sketch out our separate lives.
The greatest suffering in Kyŏngsŏng today is the unemployment problem.
It is unknown what results the recently announced unemployment relief projects (失業救濟事業) will yield, but first, the period of investigation (調査云云) has been long. Also, the controversy over intermediary exploitation (中間搾取) by contractors (請負業者) has been ongoing. Furthermore, while the issue of the interrupted road between Ch’anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑) and Chongmyo (宗廟) is contentious, the start date for the public works (土木事業) remains vague (漠然). Even if this relief project is implemented and some wages are distributed, it will provide no real help to the citizens of Kyŏngsŏng (京城市民).
Why is that? Because the citizens of Kyŏngsŏng are not laborers (勞働者). Especially, they are not day laborers (日傭) suited for earthmoving work (土役) in public works projects. They are mostly from the landlord class (地主階級), merchant/industrial class (商工階級), salaried employees (給料雇傭人), students (學生), factory workers (職工), or young people from the provinces (地方靑年) who came up to Seoul vaguely yearning for the city.
Therefore, apart from being parasites (寄生虫) feeding on the surplus value (剩餘價値) of land or capital, they must all seek some profession (職業) or start some business (營業). Hence, the countermeasure for Kyŏngsŏng's unemployment problem today lies not in public works construction (土木工事), but in establishing people's bank institutions (庶民銀行機關) for merchants and industrialists, and in the construction and revival of factories (工場) as demanded by workers (職工). The Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural government (京城府政) should initiate these measures. <69>
However, while this is something progressive modern nations and their cities should do, in the true sense, there is no real unemployment relief or countermeasure under capitalism. Thus, politics, under the principle of "power" (힘), compels the unemployed masses (失業群) to demand, through the consciousness and actions of the working class, the awakening—that is, the solidarity (團結)—of workers once again.
Moreover, even among modern statesmen (近代爲政家), the wise ones, in order to see the proper growth of their people's livelihood, focus on trade union movements (組合運動) and labor movements (勞働運動) by recognizing workers' right to organize (勞働者의 團結權) and freedom (勞働者의 自由權). This is not the dogmatic opinion (獨斷意見) of communists; the achievements of the coming world increasingly point to the righteousness of this path.
Among world nations, the United States (米國) is the wealthiest and strongest, yet during the economic panic (經濟恐慌) of 1929-1930, both the government and the people realized the gravity of the unemployment problem. President Hoover's (후-파大統領) White House (白堊舘) temporarily became like an industrial headquarters (産業的 大本營). The public works projects announced by Secretary of Commerce Lamont (라돈드商務長官) now amount to 7 billion, even 10 billion dollars. This reflects the budget prepared for the unemployment problem and the vigorous opinions surrounding it.
However, even the US is not optimistic about countermeasures for this unemployment problem; inevitably, one must point out the errors of the current industrial system (産業制度) and capitalist system (資本制度).

So, what should be done about unemployment in Korea, especially in Kyŏngsŏng?
It is decidedly not public works relief projects (土木救濟事業), nor road reconstruction (道路改築) or grand Kyŏngsŏng city planning (大京城都市計劃). The Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (京城府) needs policies (府是) and administration (府政) that plan how to promote the businesses of Kyŏngsŏng's merchants and industrialists today, and how to integrate new workers flocking from rural areas into industrial factory jobs. This requires establishing unemployment countermeasures specific to Kyŏngsŏng, distinct from the actions of the central government, the colonial authorities (拓務當局), or the Government-General's administration (總督府施政). The key lies in the thorough increase or reform of financial institutions (金融機關).
This does not mean securing funds through free grants of state land (國有地無償拂下) or municipalizing Kyŏngsŏng Electric (京電官營). Rather, the operating methods of semi-governmental financial institutions (半官金融機關) like the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行), the Industrial Bank (殖産銀行), or the Oriental Development Company (東洋拓殖會社), and financial cooperatives (金融組合) should be revised to pursue national and civic interests (國家的, 公民的 利益) differently from private banks or other financial institutions, particularly regarding loans (貸付) and savings (貯金). Furthermore, truly dynamic people's banks (庶民銀行) should be established in necessary districts of each neighborhood (洞町).
Where will the capital for these people's banks come from? Of course, it should be requested from the central government, but this doesn't mean the central government allocating separate capital. Instead, based on national census data (國勢調査) of the residents in that prefecture's neighborhood, capital should be recognized—not just signifying home ownership, land ownership, or other real estate (不動産), but also recognizing household goods (日用家具), salary levels (給料對象), clothing (衣類), and all household assets (家産). Based on this assessed capital, provisional credit (假設定의 振出) could be issued; as nationally recognized capital (國家的 公認의 資本), valuable currency (有價貨幣) could be issued indefinitely.
Through the establishment of such popularly oriented financial institutions (庶民化한 金融機關), commerce and industry (商工業) could flourish, and a dampening effect (凋落的 抑揚) on large capital enterprises might be seen.
Thus, countermeasures for the unemployment problem, while looking towards a reformed occupational front (改良的 職業戰線), should find the appropriate path—towards lifestyle stability in some direction.
Therefore, perhaps Unemployed Kyŏngsŏng (失業의 京城), through the courage and aspirations (抱負) of the newly reformed Prefectural Assembly members, will first demonstrate the effects of autonomous fundamentals (自治的 基本의 効果), and become a Kyŏngsŏng of business (業務의 京城)? <70>

**Summary**

The author, Yi Ryang, laments the severe unemployment crisis in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul) in 1931, contrasting the superficial modernity of the city with the desperate poverty experienced by its Korean inhabitants and the stark conditions in the countryside, as recounted by a visiting friend. Yi critiques superficial government relief efforts like public works, arguing they don't address the needs of the largely non-laborer urban population (merchants, former students, white-collar workers, etc.). He advocates for fundamental solutions rooted in the working class's awakening and solidarity, and proposes practical measures like establishing accessible "people's banks" (庶民銀行) tailored to the needs of small businesses and ordinary citizens, suggesting this requires bold action from the newly reformed Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural Assembly rather than relying on ineffective top-down policies. From a historical perspective, the article vividly portrays the socio-economic distress in colonial Korea during the early Great Depression, highlighting the perceived inadequacy of Japanese colonial administration's responses and reflecting socialist-influenced ideas about class consciousness and worker empowerment as potential solutions. Historians might find the specific details about urban poverty (scavenging, unemployment statistics, types of unemployed people) and the critique of both colonial capitalism and proposed superficial remedies particularly interesting.

失業京城
						李亮
『서울은』 그래도 朝鮮사람들이 사-는 듯하네. 이말은 어느날 아츰에 시골 친구가 와서 첫 人事로 한말이다.
나는 이 시골친구와 文誼가 잇슨지 퍽도 오래다. 내가 茨城縣日立鑛山에 가서 잇슬때에도 가티 잇섯고 또 내가 西伯利亞에 잇슬때도 가티 黑麥包를 씹부면서 流浪하던 사람이다.
그는 나와가티 近20년을 두고 이뜻- 저뜻- 轉轉漂流, 무엇을 意圖하고 잇섯다.
지금 이 자리에 그나 내나 다가티 30이 넘도록 하여노은 일이 업시 그저 困難의 痕蹟이 그 이마에나 그 눈초리에 흐르고 잇슬 뿐이다.
그런데 이 친구가 웨이러한 말을 하는지 나는 잘안다. 그는 家族 때문에 朝鮮의 運動을 버리고 그만 故鄕에 도라가서 적으마한 雜貨商舖을 始作하엿다. 처음부터 무슨 資本이 잇슨 것이 아니라 그 아부지의 唯一遺産인 草家집 한채가 金融組合에 抵當이 되야 300圓인지 350圓인지 어더다가 고무신작이며 미영疋이며 空冊가튼, 갑업는＜66＞ 日用雜品을 팔고 잇섯다.
그러나 資本競爭의 이 世上, 더욱 商業智識이라고 寸餘의 頭腦가 업는 그 武骨에 顧客接待며 商品需通에 무슨 辨通이 잇슬 까닭이 업다. 더욱 生活難에 漸漸 쭉으러저 가는 資本ㅡ 繁昌할 길이 바이 업다.
그가 보고잇는 商舖가 날날이 쭉으러가드시 시골 사람들의 生活은 날날이 쭉으러저 간다. 더욱이 느즌 봄철이 되고 첫여름이 오면 보리(麥)때로 멀고 먹을 양식은 업는데 시골 農村의 生活狀은 참말 눈물겨운 情景이다.

다시 더 말하자면 우리 勞働階級이 밧는 給料은 우리가 生産하는 物質에서 極히 少部分인 賃銀이다. 그런 때문에 世界生産의 總消費階級인, 卽 大多數의 無産者, 大衆,＜67＞ 國民의 消費量은 其生活萎縮에서 多數의 生産- 其所有者인 資本家에 한 餘剩를 남기여 줄 뿐이다. 그러니 自然이 消費치 안니한 生産過剩는 그것이 處分된 때까지 工場은 문을 닷치고 企業家은 銀行通帳을 잠추고 緊縮政策은 物件을 못사게 함에 市場의 恢復은 더욱더욱 느저간다.
이에 이 失業問題의 解決은 아모래도 勞動階級의 覺醒如何에 잇스며 其團結如何에 依存하여 잇는 것이다.

우리는 저 거리에 단이는 朝鮮사람을 보라. 저 蓬萊橋다리겻테 빈지게만 지고 섯는 짐車을 보라. 黃金町 鍾路通 其他거리에 어슬넝어슬넝 도라단이는 人夫들을 보라. 골목마다 모아 안즌, 거지떼 아희들을 보라. 그러고 龍山兵站部압페 가보라. 巡査講習所炊車場에 가보라. 뼉다귀, 단배꼭지 썩은 짐치무, 내가 드력드력 나는 殘飯을 사러 모혀드는 朝鮮婦人들의 일을 보라- 이것이 今日京城의 朝鮮人生活이다.
富者가 잇대야 얼마아니되는 地主層이 이 서울에 와서 消費하는 光景만 朝鮮富의 殘骸를 보혀줄 뿐이다. 旅舘구석에 모혀안즌, 부로가- 所謂 千兩판 萬兩판 群이 서울 中産階級이라면 五間水다리 附近에서 淸溪川 흙물에 소뼉다귀와 말뼉귀를 파먹는 土窟民이 無産階級이라면 서울의 無産階級은 世界的 通語의 無産階級이 아니라, 乞人階級, 奴隷階級 또 XX된 百姓- 이것이 朝鮮無産階級이요 이것이 今日 서울의 가난뱅이일 것이다.
요새 地方自治改正에 따르는 府會議員選擧運動이 激甚하야 其逐鹿의 形勢은 자못 猛烈한 運動가운데에 잇스나 그 發表하는 政見을 보면 衛生施設이니, 學校組合費이니 電氣官營이니- 하는 一種 凡俗商輩의 常套語이다. 그들의게 元來 무슨 期待가 잇스리마는 그래도 이 自治改正에 따르는 人民의 生活論定은 그것이 改良過程에 잇서서나 또은 무슨 政治的 暗影의 道程을 보거나 한가지 生活運動인 政治問題에 關聯할 수 잇는 것이니 이것을 否라하야 原則的으로 拒否하는 意味에서 對岸視할 수 업는 바이니 우리는 今日의 失業問題- 더욱 京城의 失業問題는 어느 會議員의 한사람이라도 그입에 發表할 政見이라 하엿다. 그런데 이것이 寸毫도 업다.
이에 이 自治改正案에 따르는 人民의 要求이거나 會議員의 抱負에거나 아모 바람이 업시 그는 그대로 우리는 우리대로 別個의 生活을 그리고 잇슬 뿐이다.
今日의 京城의 一大苦痛은 失業問題이다.
昨今 累累히 發表하는 失業救濟事業은 그 結果가 엇더한 成果를 매즐런지 모르나 爲先 調査云云의 時節이 오랫고 또 請負業者의 中間搾取의 是非- 이것도 오랫고 또 昌慶苑 宗廟間의 中斷道路是非가 말성이 만으나 아직 土木事業의 着手는 그 期日이 漠然하다. 萬一에 이 救濟事業이 實行되여 多少 賃銀의 分布가 된다하여도 實狀 京城市民의게는 何等 도음이 업다.
웨그러냐하면 京城市民은 勞働者가 아니다. 더욱 무슨 土木事業에나 그 土役에 日傭할 人夫가 아니다. 그들은 多少 地主階級, 商工階級, 給料雇傭人, 學生, 職工, 또 漫然히 都市를 憧憬하야 上京한 地方靑年- 이것이 大部分이다.
때문에 그들은 土地나 資本에 剩餘價値의 寄生虫外에는 다 무슨 職業을 求하여아 되겟고 무슨 營業을 始作하여야 된 것이다. 故로 今日 京城의 失業問題對策은 土木工事보다 商工業者를 위하는 庶民銀行機關, 職工의 要求인 工場의 建設과 再興에 잇슬것이며 또 이 事業에 京城府政은 始作하여야 할 것이다. ＜69＞
그러나 이것은 進步된 近代國家가, 그 都市가 할 일이지마는 眞正한 意味에서 資本主義의 失業救濟라던지 그 對策이라는 것은 참말 업는 것이다. 이에 政治라는 것은, 『힘』이라는 原則下 失業群은 그 勞働階級의 意識과 行爲에서 또다시 勞働者의 覺醒 卽 團結을 要求하게 되는 것이다.
그러고 近代爲政家 가운데도 聰明한 者는 그 國民의 正當한 生活伸長을 보기 위하야 勞働者의 團結權, 勞働者의 自由權의 認容으로 組合運動이며 勞働運動에 着目하게 되는것이니 이는 콤문이스트의 獨斷意見이 아니라 將來할 世上의 業蹟은 바야흐로 이길의 正大을 말하여 온다.
世界가운데에 米國은 가장 富强의 國家로되 1929-1930년의 經濟恐慌에 上下官民은 그 失業問題의 重大性을 自覺하야 후-파大統領의 白堊舘은 一時 産業的 大本營이 되다십히 되여왓다. 卽 라돈드商務長官의 發表한 公共土木事業이 今日 70億, 乃至 백億弗에 達한다. 이 失業問題에 用意인 豫算이며 그 潑潑한 意見이다.
그러나 米國도 이 失業問題의 對策이 樂觀은 안이니 이것은 아모래도 只今의 産業制度 資本制度의 過誤를 말치 안을수 업슬 것이다.
그러면 朝鮮의 失業, 더욱 京城의 失業問題는 엇더케 할것인가.
이것은 土木救濟事業도 아니며 道路改築大京城都市計劃도 決코 아니다. 엇더케 하면 今日 京城의 商工業者를 營業增進에 農村에서 모혀드는 新勞働者의 産業的 工場職工에- 計劃하는 京城府의 府是가 잇고 府政이 잇서야 될것이니 이는 中央政府의 所使나 拓務當局이나 總督府施政의 如何보다 京城府는 京城府로의 失業對策을 樹立함에, 金融機關의 增加, 또는 改革- 이것을 徹底함에 잇슬 것이다.
그는 金融의 調達로 國有地無償拂下, 京電官營等, 이것이 아니라 半官金融機關인, 朝鮮銀行, 殖産銀行, 或은 東洋拓殖會社, 金融組合等의 營業方法을 私設銀行이나 其他金融機關보다 따로히 國家的, 公民的 利益의 意圖下, 貸付, 貯金의 길을 改定하여야 되겟고 또는 各洞町의 必要地帶에 의하야 가장 洞町의 能動的인 庶民銀行을 創設하여야 될 것이다.
이 庶民銀行은 其資本을 어데에 求할것이냐하면 勿論 中央政府에 要求할것이나 이것은 中央政府로도 別個資本을 割積함이 아니라 그 府면 그 府의 그 洞町이면 그 洞町의 在住하는 人民의 國勢調査에 의하야 表明되는 資本- 卽 家屋所有나, 무슨 土地의 有無나 其他 不動産만 意味하는 것이 아니라 日用家具며 給料對象이며 衣類며- 모든 家産을 認定하는 資本으로써 假設定의 振出을 볼것이니 그는 國家的 公認의 資本- 얼마던지 有價貨幣를 發行할 수 잇슬 것이다.
이와가티 庶民化한 金融機關의 創設로 因하야 發興할 수 잇는 商工業이며 또는 大資本事業에 대한 한가지 凋落的 抑揚을 볼 것이다.
이에 失業問題의 對策은 그 改良的 職業戰線을 보면서 應當의 길- 어느 쪽으로의 生活安定을 볼 것이다.
때문에 失業의 京城은 今後改正되는 府會議員의 勇氣와 其抱負에 爲先 自治的 基本의 効果를 낫타내며 失業의 京城도 業務의 京城이 될것이 안일가-.＜70＞
