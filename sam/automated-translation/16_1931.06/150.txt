**Summary**

This 1931 article, compiled by Kim Tong-hwan, details foreign investment in China, focusing on railways, general investments (particularly Japan's significant stake), shipping, and banking. It highlights Japan's dominant post-WWI economic penetration, especially in Manchuria and key coastal cities, totaling nearly 2 billion Yen. The article also briefly touches upon China's burgeoning efforts in education to combat illiteracy and lists major foreign banks operating in China with their capital. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is revealing because it quantifies Japan's massive investment in China (particularly Manchuria) compared to infrastructure like railways in colonized Korea, emphasizing the scale of Japanese imperial ambitions on the continent. The listing of the Bank of Chōsen alongside major international and Japanese banks underscores Korea's integration into the Japanese economic sphere and its role as a base for continental expansion.
