Publication Date: 1931-06-01
Article Title: 兄妹間 戀愛와 血族結婚 否可論 (Romance Between Siblings and the Debate on the Permissibility of Consanguineous Marriage)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Symposium)

**Romance Between Siblings and the Debate on the Permissibility of Consanguineous Marriage**

1.  Can romantic feelings arise even between siblings?
2.  What about marrying a blood relative?

It is said that the Kinship Law (親族法) is likely to be somewhat revised by the Chosŏn Civil Law Revision Committee (朝鮮民法改正委員會), currently under deliberation, and as a result, a certain degree of freedom regarding consanguineous marriage will be recognized. Following this, the issue we should consider is precisely the problem of romance between siblings. Is it truly a sin for an older brother (옵바) and younger sister (누이) to fall in love and marry, or not? Or is it also a sin or freedom for a mother and daughter to love the same man, as in (The Love of Three Generations, 三代의 사랑)? Now, the views of gentlemen from the legal, medical, and intellectual circles on this matter will surely offer many suggestions. (Editorial Department)

**The Problem of Incest (亂倫) and Freedom of Romance**
Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng

Speaking from the position of a socialist, I believe that romance and marriage between close relatives are not particularly good things.
In the world, seeing "The Love of Three Generations" (三代의 戀愛), written by the woman known as a new thinker of Russia, Ms. Kollontai, many seem to misunderstand that in a socialist society, it is acceptable for one man to have physical relationships (肉體的 關係) simultaneously with a mother and her daughter. <61> However, "The Love of Three Generations" was written in haste to advocate for sexual liberation, imagining extreme situations, and it is merely an aspect of abnormal love in a transitional period (過渡期變態的 戀愛의 樣相), definitely not a normal (正常的) attitude. This must be remembered. If there are even cases like loving a mother and her daughter simultaneously and having physical relations, how much more would siblings, born from the same parents' womb and sharing the same room (한 구들 안), fall in love and marry? I believe many people might consecutively think of such facts. While that's an extreme, among actual incidents, not only in Russia but also in nearby places, there are stories of siblings loving each other and even having children.
When they see each other morning and evening in a close household, and on top of that, find each other beautiful or affectionate, becoming the object of their longing (憧憬), there's no rule saying love cannot arise just because she is a biological sister. In some (엇든) cases, human nature is stronger than reason (理智), and emotions often dictate actions, so for this reason, there's no logic (리치) why romance between siblings couldn't be established. Looking at the customs among savage races (野蠻人종), aren't there even cases like incest between mother and son (母子相姦)?
However, no matter how I think about it, romance between siblings or the establishment of sensory relationships between mother and daughter like in "The Love of Three Generations" is an abnormal phenomenon of a transitional period, and I believe it is not the right (올혼) attitude. Why would a socialist society permit incest (亂倫)? On this point, I even think it might be stricter (嚴格하지) than bourgeois society (뿔조아사회).

However, looking at our Chosŏn situation, as a result of confining marriageable young women (色시) or widows who lost their husbands in deep inner quarters (閨房), women ended up having affairs (情을 通하게 되어서) with the closest relatives who could enter the inner quarters. Because of this, children were born, called "seeds of sin" (罪의 씨), and secretly smothered (눌녀 죽이거나) or buried (뭇어 버리엇다가), only to be discovered, leading to prison sentences (懲役살이). How many (엇더케나 만히) such tragedies were there? Infanticide (嬰兒殺害) was largely the sinful result of incest between close relatives (近親間의 亂倫) produced by Chosŏn's non-open (非開放主義的) large family system (大家族制度).
Were such evils (쥐악) only committed by widows or maidens? There were indeed sordid (醜惡한) incidents sometimes (각금) between the wife of the inner chambers (內房의 婦人) and her paternal uncle (三寸) or her husband's younger brothers (媤族하들). Although these were not openly practiced consanguineous marriages (血族相婚), we can see how prevalent consanguineous marriage was in past society (過去社會) in its consequences.
But now, social conditions are becoming very different (달너지게 되어), homes are generally becoming more open (開放的), education is spreading, and the large family system is gradually collapsing. Therefore, I believe the holes where the tragedy of consanguineous marriage could hide have disappeared (업시된). This is a fortunate thing.

However, I think we should forgive this kind of situation. That is, suppose a man lives with his wife, and she falls ill (병드러) and dies (죽엇다). In such a case (境遇), if he has a wife's younger sister (妻弟), marrying her, raising the children from the previous wife well, and living together as a couple – this cannot be seen as incest (亂倫).
Such actual examples seem less common in Chosŏn, but I believe they are quite prevalent in Japanese society.

Even if a new law permitting consanguineous marriage (血族相婚) is enacted, our unique morality (固有한 道德) – although any morality will naturally change (變遷할) with the times – will not welcome this.

**Opposed Due to Racial Degeneration (種族低下) and Public Morals (風紀上)**
Hŏ Yŏngsuk

We Chosŏn people have strictly avoided consanguineous marriage between relatives of the same clan for thousands of years (數千年來). In other words, we have avoided it not only between siblings (男妹) and uncles/nephews (叔姪) within the fourth and fifth degrees of kinship (四寸五寸) on the father's side (父系), but even among distant relatives (遠戚) reaching the tenth degree and beyond (十數寸). <62> We have also maintained the custom (風習) of not marrying relatives from the mother's side (母系), the maternal family (外家집).
Is that all? Even if the clan origin (本) is the same, or even if the surname (姓) is the same, we have tried to avoid marriage as much as possible. It was the rule (례엇슴니다) that in eight or nine cases out of ten (십중팔구), people of the same Chŏnju Ri clan, or Ham'an Kim clan, would not pair up (짝을 짓지 안는 것이).
This is a social unwritten law (無形의 律法) that is still strictly observed (嚴然히 직혀오는) today.

Then, does love not arise between relatives? That is not the case. Rather, in the East (東洋), where social interaction channels are few, especially in a place like Chosŏn, relatives can visit each other freely without distinction (內外업시 通來할 수 잇스니까), so there are frequent opportunities for contact, which has the advantage (利點) of making it easy to become close (親近하여지기).
For that reason, for instance (가령), affection (愛情) tends to become deeper (濃厚하여 지는 페가 잇슴니다) between cousins of the fourth, sixth, or eighth degree (六八寸사이의 오랍누이 간에).
To speak in extremes, is there any law preventing "romance" from arising even between biological siblings (친누이동생 간엔들) born from the same parents?
However, Chosŏn's morality and tradition strictly prohibit (準絶히 禁止하고) this. Far back in the ancient times of primitive promiscuity (太古亂婚), we don't know, but at least since the patriarchal system (父權制) was established (確立되고) and monogamy (一夫一婦制) began to emerge, Chosŏn people have avoided marriage between blood relatives like a viper (사갈가치).

However, Japan is different from our situation. There, they do not particularly hesitate (꺼리지 안슴니다) to marry starting from sixth-degree cousins on the mother's side (母系). On the father's side too, if the relation is a bit more distant (먼-다리에 잇스면), they also intermarry (通婚하고 잇슴니다).
But I firmly oppose this consanguineous marriage. Why? Firstly, from the perspective of eugenics (優生學), it must be absolutely rejected (絕對的 배척하여야 할 것이니). Generally, from a medical standpoint (醫學上으로 보건대), when cells (細胞) from different types (類型) meet in the man and woman procreating humanity (人類創造하는 남녀간), the offspring (種族) becomes superior (優良하여짐니다). If the same cells meet, the degree of stimulus (刺戟) and activity is weak (微弱하야), resulting in the production (産出하게 되는 것이외다) of children who are short, have insufficient intelligence – an inferior race (劣等人種).
We can see this by looking at the races of uncivilized people in Africa (亞弗利加未開人), who marry their own sisters and even have relations with their own daughters, producing children. Because they constantly (밤낫) marry blood relatives, their race shows no physical (體格上) progress (進步) even after decades or centuries, and their intellect always remains at the level of uncivilized people.
On the other hand, aren't we always seeing and hearing (듯지 안슴니까) the fact that geniuses (天才) are often found among children born from distant relations (緣이 먼-) – for example, between an American man and a French woman?
Therefore, firstly, for the development of the race's physique and intellect, I oppose consanguineous marriage.

Secondly, for the sake of family morals (家庭의 風紀), that is, to prevent the harm (弊害) of lewdness (亂淫), I oppose it. If Chosŏn, like the West, had a family system where the breakdown (分解作用) is severe and the family unit (單位) is extremely simple (單純하고) and convenient (輕便하다면), maybe not, but as long as that is not the case (그러치안는 한에서는), I cannot but oppose it.

Even if Chosŏn's civil law (民事) is revised (改定되어) to permit (許諾한다) consanguineous marriage (血族相婚), meaning the law opens the way to freedom (法律로는 자유의 길을 터놋는다 할지라도), I think Chosŏn people will not change the morality and customs (道德과 風習) that have absolutely prohibited (絕對禁止하여 오든) consanguineous marriage.

**Law and Romance Between Siblings**
Lawyer Kim Pyŏngno

From a legal perspective (法律上으로 보아서), can an older brother and younger sister fall in love? Also, beyond just siblings, can blood relatives, i.e., family members, fall in love and marry? To understand this, let us first (爲先) look at the articles (條文) of the current Civil Code (現行民法).

Marriage is not permitted (婚姻함을 不得함) between lineal blood relatives (直系血族) or collateral blood relatives (傍系血族) within the third degree of kinship (三親等內) (Article 769). <63>
Marriage is not permitted (婚姻함을 不得함) between lineal relatives by marriage (直系姻族) (Article 770).
Marriage is not permitted (婚姻함을 不得함) between an adopted child (養子), their spouse (其配偶者), lineal ascendants (直系尊屬), or their spouse, and the adoptive parent (養親) or their lineal ascendants (Article 771).
Thus it is written. That is, even if a marriage occurs, it has no legal effect (效力) in the above three cases. One cannot marry a biological sibling on the father's side, nor within the third degree of kinship on the mother's side (maternal relatives, 傍傍血族 - *likely typo for 傍系血族*), nor between adoptive parent and adopted child (養父養女).

What were the actual circumstances (實際形便) in Chosŏn? In Chosŏn society, the doctrines (學說) of the School of Zhu Xi (朱子學派), which emphasized (高調하는) the Three Bonds and Five Relationships (三綱五倫), loyalty and filial piety (忠孝), etc., flourished (全盛하여서), even advocating for not marrying someone of the same surname and clan origin (不聚同性). Marrying someone with the same surname and same clan origin – for example, between Kyŏngju Ri (慶州李氏) and Kyŏngju Ri, or Kangnŭng Kim (江陵金氏) and Kangnŭng Kim – or more broadly (너르게 잡으면), between people of the same Kim family or Pak family, has been prevented (막어왓다).
In reality (實相), this morality has reigned (君臨하여) over our society with the weight of the nine tripods (九鼎의 무게), compelling (強要하여) obedience (服從) from everyone.
However, we must remember (긔억하여야 한다) that there were many exceptions (例外) to this. What about Zhu Xi himself, who advocated such theories? He too accepted (마저드리엇다) a younger relative (nephew level, 족하사위) as his son-in-law, a man from the same clan. Even in the families of Chosŏn's moral scholars (道學者), examples (례는) of intermarriage (相婚하는) between distant and close relatives (遠近親族間) occurred sometimes (각금 잇섯다).

No one can definitively say (斷言하지 못할 것이다) that romance will not arise (일지 안으리라고는) between an older brother and younger sister. Actual examples (實例) can be seen in places of extreme civilization (極度의 文明) and extreme primitiveness (極度의 未開한 地方). In uncivilized (未開國) or semi-uncivilized countries (半未開國) like those in Africa, where emotions and thought are not highly developed, we can see that marriage between biological siblings (親兄妹의 結婚) is prevalent (盛行하고 잇는 것을). Also, in certain places that completely deny (一切 否認하는) morality (道德), etiquette (禮儀), tradition (傳統), etc., and are free-spirited (自由奔放한), there seems to be a tendency (傾向) to approve (是認하려 하는) it.
Romance follows the senses (官能). When admiration (感歎) arises for something beautiful, soft, or graceful (優美), the mere barrier of bloodline (血統) often loses its power (力) to restrain (制裁할).
Therefore, since morality has only temporarily (臨時 臨時) blocked it like a shield (防牌), there will likely be no way (재조가 업슬 것이다) to stop love, which flows like water, just because they are blood relatives.

The modern large capitalist (大資本主義的) economic system (經濟制度) is inevitably confronting (마스고 잇다) the previously feudal (封建적이든) traditional family system (從來의 家族制度). At this time (이때에 잇서), it will be interesting (興味잇는 일리리라) to see to what extent (程度까지) the trend of avoiding (回避의 思潮) marriage between relatives will continue (繼續되어 갈 것인지). However, this one final word (마즈막에 이 한마듸) can be said: Even if the Civil Code is revised and marriage between blood relatives becomes somewhat freer (다소 자유된다), it is a big question (큰 疑問이다) how much it will be applied (適用하려 할가) in Chosŏn society, which has been soaked in (저진) convention (因習) for so long (오랫동안). It remains to be seen.

**Opposed from a Eugenic Standpoint (優生學上으로 反對)**
Medical Doctor Pak Ch'anghun

We Chosŏn people, while refusing (마다하면서) even marriage between those of the same surname/clan (不娶同姓), if you look into the actual situation (實際事情에 드러가보면), the Namin (南人) married among Namin, the Pugin (北人) among Pugin, and the Noron (노론) and Soron (소론) among themselves, each according to their factional distinctions (班族區別). In a broad sense (너른 意味로 보아서), what is this if not consanguineous marriage?
However, extreme close-relative marriage like that of the Japanese, who marry starting from sixth-degree cousins on the father's side, has not existed (업시 왓슴니다).

Since I am a medical scholar (醫學者), from a medical scholar's standpoint (醫學자의 見地에 서서), it is necessary to consider (相考하여 볼 필요가 잇는데) what kind of impact (利害) consanguineous marriage has on our human race from the perspective of eugenics (優生學). Modern medicine judges (判斷하고 잇슴니다) consanguineous marriage as bad (낫부다고는). <64>
First, children born from consanguineous marriage are often short, slender, and congenitally (先天的으로) weak in vigor (氣力).
Second, their comprehension (理解力) and memory (記憶力) abilities are often weak (薄弱하여), leading to many mentally handicapped children (低能兒가 만혼 것).
However, taking one step further (한거름 더 나아가서), medicine cannot explain (說明하지 못함니다) why (엇지 하여서) consanguineous marriage results in so many (그러케) physically or mentally (身體나 두노상에) inferior (劣等한) children. This must be because the development (發達) of modern medicine is still that primitive (幼稚한 까닭이겟지요). It only barely gets by (간신히 너머갈 뿐임니다) by proving (證明하고) the harm of consanguineous marriage through counter-evidence (反證) – that is, seeing (봄니다) that special geniuses (特別한 天才) are often born (나는 것을) from non-consanguineous marriages, such as between British and American men and women, or between a Russian father and a Chosŏn mother.
In Japan, there is a scholar named Nagai Hisomu (永井潛), an authority (權威) in the field of serology (血淸學界). He has a research work (研究著書) on consanguineous marriage, but even in that book, he ended up not establishing (내어 세우지 못하고 말엇습데다) a definitive (斷定的) conclusion (結論) regarding the relationship between consanguineous marriage and low intelligence.
Besides this (이밧게), looking at the opinions (所論) of various scholars (諸學者), there has been no thorough (徹底한) scientific proof (科學的 證明).
Then, what did Eastern medicine (東洋醫學) say about this? Since the School of Zhu Xi (朱子學派) dominated Chosŏn's morality, marriage between relatives was strictly forbidden (嚴格하게 금하여 왓슴니다). However, if you ask if this was based on any scientific grounds (科學的 根據가 잇서 그랫느냐하면), that wasn't the case. I believe it was because, from an ethical standpoint (倫理學的 見地), the views of the moral scholars (道學者) of that time dominated even the minds of medical practitioners.
Why (엇재서)? In the old days (녯날의) of Chosŏn, the family was much more (훨신) collective (集團的) than today; it was the era of the large family system (大家族制 時代). So, if restraints (制裁를) in the area of sexual morality (性道德方面) regarding interactions between male and female relatives were relaxed (緩和시켜 놋는다면) even slightly, shocking consequences of incest (亂倫의 結果) would surely arise (이러나고 말터이니까). I believe they acted so (그리한 줄로 암니다) to avoid that harm (페해와 비극).
We see in anthropology (人類學) the relationships between men and women in primitive times (原始時代). Let alone (고사하고) fourth or sixth cousins, they even had relations (關係하든) with the mother who bore them, or their grandmother. Also, in the French (불란서) royal court of Louis (루이), the crown prince (皇太子), while living together (同極하면서 - *likely typo for 同居*), took the favored consort (寵妃) who had received the love (寵愛를 밧든) of his royal father (父王) during the emperor's reign (皇帝時代) as his queen consort (王后) and enjoyed her.
Also, the tragedy among blood relatives (肉親間의 悲劇) depicted by the pen (붓으로) of Shakespeare (쎅스피어-) in "Hamlet" (함렛).
All vile (지독한) incestuous relations (亂倫들올) among blood relatives, such as father-daughter incest (父女相姦). From our perspective (우리로 안저서는), we must strictly avoid (準絶히 피하여야 할 줄 암니다) such things.
Consanguineous marriage! What else could it be but something that reverses (逆轉식히는 것밧게) human civilization?
In this respect (이점에서), the traditional (在來의) ethics and morality (倫理道德) of Chosŏn, which have avoided consanguineous marriage, were right (올햇든).
Then, the question of whether older brother and younger sister should not fall in love will naturally be settled (락착을 지을 줄 암니다). Since it has already become clear (判明된 以上) that marrying is unseemly (不美한) from eugenic and moral standpoints, why would romance, its initial phenomenon (初期現象인), be good (조흘 까닭이 잇겟슴니까요)?
This statement is completely different (아조 달늠니다) from the question of whether romance cannot be established between an older brother and younger sister. As long as both are human (사람인 以上에), there might be cases (경우도 잇겟지요) where affection that can only exist between opposite sexes (異性間에만 가질 수 잇는 애정) arises. If you look at novels (小切이나 - *likely 소설이나*) or confessions written by Japanese people, records (긔록가튼) of longing (思慕하는) for a fourth cousin (四寸누이를) are actually numerous (실상 만흐니까). <65>

**Summary**

This 1931 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine presents a symposium on the controversial topics of sibling romance and consanguineous marriage in colonial Korea, prompted by proposed revisions to the Civil Code's Kinship Law. Four commentators—Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng (socialist perspective), Hŏ Yŏngsuk (traditionalist/eugenicist), Kim Pyŏngno (lawyer), and Pak Ch'anghun (medical doctor/eugenicist)—weigh in. The consensus leans heavily against such relationships, citing concerns ranging from socialist ethics and traditional Korean morality (emphasizing the custom of 불취동성 - not marrying within the same surname/clan) to fears of racial degeneration based on contemporary eugenics (優生學), and potential social disorder (亂倫). While acknowledging the legal framework and the power of emotion, the commentators generally believe that deeply ingrained Korean customs opposing close-kin marriage will persist despite potential legal liberalization, though they also recognize historical exceptions and the tragic consequences (like infanticide) of past clandestine relationships within the restrictive traditional family structure.

For historians, the article is revealing of the intellectual currents in 1930s colonial Korea. It showcases the intersection of traditional Confucian values, emerging socialist thought, Western scientific ideas like eugenics (used here to reinforce traditional prohibitions), and legal modernization influenced by Japanese colonial administration. The discussion reflects societal anxieties surrounding changing family structures, the influence of foreign ideas (Kollontai, eugenics, Japanese legal/social practices), and the ongoing negotiation between tradition and modernity under colonial rule. The explicit mention of past infanticide linked to incest within the traditional large family system also provides a stark commentary on the perceived failings of older social structures.

兄妹間 戀愛와 血族結婚 否可論
1. 兄妹間에도 戀愛感情이 생기는가?
2. 血族結婚을 하는 것이 엇더한가?

요지음 審議中의 朝鮮民法改正委員會의 손으로 親族法이 多少 變改될 듯 하다는데 그 結果로 血族結婚에 대한 어느 程度의 自由가 認定되게 되리라 함니다. 여기에 따라서 우리가 問題삼어 조흘 것이 卽 兄妹間의 戀愛問題일 것이외다. 옵바와 누이 사이에 연애를 하고 결혼하는 것이 과연 죄악일가 아닐가. 또는 母女가 가튼 한 사나히와 사랑하는 것도 죄악일가 자유일가. (三代의 사랑) 모양으로, 이제
법조게, 의학게, 사상계 제씨의 여기 대한 견해는 만흔 暗示를 던저줄 것이다.(編輯局)

亂倫과 戀愛自由問題
丁七星
사회주의자의 처지에 서서 말할지라도 가튼 친척끼리 연애하고 결혼하는 것은 그리 조흔 일이 아닌 줄로 암니다.
새상에서는 로서아의 새로운 사상가라 하는 「코론타이」녀사가 쓴 「삼대의 사랑」(三代의 戀愛)를 보고서 흔히들 사회주의의 사회에서는 한 사나히가 「어머니와 그 딸」을 동시에 육체적 관게를 매저도 조흔 줄＜61＞ 잘못 알고 잇는 모양이지만 「삼대의 사랑」이라 함은 성적해방을 주장하기에 너무 급하여 극단의 사실을 상상하고 쓴 것이며 또 그는 과도긔의 한 변태적 연애의 양상(過渡期變態的 戀愛의 樣相)일 따름이고 결코 정상적(正常的)태도가 아닌 것을 긔억하여 주서야 하겟슴니다. 이러케 어머니와 그 딸을 동시에 사랑하여 육체젹 관게까지 맷는 일조차 잇다거든 하물며 가튼 부모의 배를 갈느고 나온 한 구들 안의 옵바 누이 사이에야 얼마나 만히 연애를 하고 결혼을 할 것인고? 하고 연달아 이런 사실까지 생각하실 분도 만흐실 줄 암니다. 그야 극단이지만 실제에 이러난 사실 가운데는 로서아 뿐 아니고 갓가운 곳곳에서도 형매끼리 사랑하여 자식까지 나은 일이 잇다고들 함니다.
갓가운 한 가정에서 아츰 저녁 서로 대하게 되고 그우에 또 서로 아름답다거나 정답거나 하야 그가 동경(憧憬)하는 대상이 되엇슬 때에 친누이라고 사랑이 이러나지 말난 범이 업겟지요. 엇든 경우에는 인간성이란 분마와 가치 리지(理智)보다 더 강렬하여 감정이 명하는대로 움지기는 일이 만흐니까 이러한 까닭에 형매간의 연애가 성립이 되지 안을 리치가 업겟슴니다. 야만인종 간의 풍속을 보면 심지어 모자상간(母子相姦)가튼 일까지 잇지 안슴니까.
그러나 아모리 생각하여도 형매간에 연애한다거나 「삼대의 사랑」모양으로 모녀간에 감각관게가 성립된다 함은 과도긔의 한 변태적 현상이고 그것이 올혼 태도가 아닌 줄 암니다. 사회주의의 사회라고 란륜(亂倫)이야 허락할 까닭이 잇겟슴니까. 이점은 오히려 뿔조아사회보다도 일층 더 엄격하지 안을가고도 생각이 됨니다.

그런데 우리네 조선사정을 보면 깁프나 깁픈 규방(閨房) 속에 과년한 색시나 남편 일흔 과부를 봉쇄하여 두엇든 결과로 녀자는 규방출입을 할 수 잇섯든 가장 갓가운 친척과 정을 통하게 되어서 그 때문에 아해가 나게 되어 그를 「죄의 씨」라 하야 남몰내 눌녀 죽이거나 땅에 뭇어 버리엇다가 발각되어 징역사리하는 비극이 엇더케나 만히 잇섯슴니까. 영아살해(嬰兒殺害)라는 것은 조선의 비개방주의적 대가족제도가 나은 근친간의 란륜이 준 죄악의 결과엇든 것이 대부분임니다.
엇지 과부나 처녀에게만 이러한 쥐악이 잇섯겟슴니까. 내방의 부인과 그 삼촌 또는 시족하들 사이라든지 실로 추악한 사실이 각금 잇섯슴니다. 이리한 것이 물론 공공연한 헐족상혼은 아니지만 결과에 잇서서 혈족상혼이 과거사희에 얼마나 만히 류행되엇든가 함을 알 수가 잇슴니다.
그러나 지금은 사회사정이 매우 달너지게 되어 가정도 대개 개방적이 되고 교육이 보급되고 그러고 대가족제도가 한족으로 작고 붕괴되어가고 잇슴으로 헐족상혼적 비극이 숨어 드러갈 구멍이라고 업시된 줄로 암니다. 이것은 다행한 일이외다.

다만 이러한 점은 우리는 용서할 것인가 함니다. 즉 엇던 사내가 자긔 처와 가치 살다가 그처가 병드러 죽엇다 합시다. 그러한 경우에 그의 처제(妻弟)가 잇다면 그에게 장가드러 전처의 자녀들까지 잘 길너주며 부생활하여 가는 것을 이것을 란륜이라고 볼 수는 업슬 것이외다.
이러한 실제의 레는 조선에서는 그러케 만치 못한 듯하나 일본 사희에서는 만히 류행하고 잇는 줄로 암니다.
