A Street Without Sun
						By Tokunaga Sunao, Translated by Pak Myŏnghwan

Street

1. Flyers (삐라)

The streetcar stopped. The automobile stopped. Bicycles, cargo trucks, sidecars, carts – they came rushing like arrows and stopped, backing up.
- What happened!
- What is it, what’s going on!
The extremely simple faces of the many people gathered were roughly pulled out from amid the harsh dust of the pale yellow October sun (十月의 太陽).
The wave of people, like a swarm of tadpoles in a puddle, pushed backward and milled about restlessly.
- It’s an imperial procession. His Highness Prince XX is visiting the Higher Normal School (高師)!!.
The whisper from the front line spread backward in the blink of an eye. Cars silenced their roaring engines (爆音), and people took off their hats.

After about fifteen minutes had passed, those standing in the front line saw five cars, like fleeting shadows, silently running off between the gold-braided police inspector and the saluting policemen lined up all around. A jet-black XXXXXX flashed once, striking the crowd's eyes amid the dusty rays of light. But those in the back rows could only see the policemen's hats.
The blockade line (遮斷線) was released.
The wave of people burst through like a broken dam and flowed out.
Just then, - Ouch! You damn brat, watch where you're going!
A man dressed in shabby clothes, who had been jostled in the crowd, jumped up and shouted. It was because a man in a yellowish raincoat had suddenly bumped into his chest.
- What's this about? - Two or three others who had also collided shouted at once. The shabbily dressed man stretched out his sturdy (屈强) arm and grabbed the hem of this violent Western-suited man's raincoat.
- Catch that guy!
The man in the raincoat, while being held, thrust his right arm over the crowd's shoulders and shouted like this: <78>
- Catch that guy! He shouted, pushing through the crowd, trying to break through to the front. At that moment (瞬間) - flutter, flutter, something shot up about three feet high, dancing violently. The crowd saw pure white scraps of paper fluttering down onto their heads.
- That's him! Catch the guy in the work jacket!
A man who looked like a detective shouted. The shabbily dressed man, whose foot had been stepped on, let go in surprise. But this time, a uniformed policeman who had popped out in front of him kicked him aside without warning.
He shouted as if he understood.
- It's a thief!
The wave of people descended into chaotic confusion. A traveling bag lay overturned on a fallen bicycle.
- It's XX!
- That’s not right. He's a socialist!
Uniformed and plainclothes police kicked the crowd aside, running around trying to catch the culprit. But the man in the work jacket had already vanished, who knows where he slipped away to.
- Did you collect the flyers (삐라)? The ones that guy just scattered... The man in the raincoat asked the uniformed officer, catching his breath.
- I don't see any...
- That can't be right, you careless -
He shook his head unpleasantly and was about to turn back.
- Ah! There it is!
An old woman who had been pushed over was trying to wipe her dirt-covered skirt hem with a piece of paper that had fallen on the ground.
- Isn't this it! This is it!
The crowd gathered around the bewildered old woman. The plainclothes officer snatched the flyer from the old woman's hand.

To our dear citizens of Koishikawa Ward (小石川區市民) and fellow citizens of Tokyo.
We, the 3,000 families and 15,000 members of the strike committee of the Daidō Printing Company employees, have been fighting for over fifty days against the tyrannical large capitalist, President Ōkawa (대천사장), and his cunning schemes. Starting with the firing (馘首) of thirty-eight workers in the casting department, he aimed to fundamentally destroy our union-organized labor and cast our 15,000 mouths into starvation (飢餓). We resist his vicious (惡辣) claws. We have received ardent support from our affiliated All-Japan Labour Union Council (전일본노동조합평의회) and labor organizations across the country. Fighting against the unyielding large capitalist Ōkawa, positioned at the forefront of the entire Japanese proletarian class, we are determined to achieve victory, not yielding even one step from our stronghold (牙城).
Dear citizens of Koishikawa Ward
And fellow citizens of Tokyo!!
We believe that you, the wise citizens, will surely side with the justice (正義) of our strike committee.

**Summary**

The article, a translated excerpt from a Japanese novel, depicts a chaotic street scene in Tokyo. A crowd gathers due to a traffic stoppage for an imperial procession. Amid the confusion following the procession, a man distributes flyers supporting a labor strike at the Daidō Printing Company before escaping police. The flyer details the plight of 3,000 striking workers (representing 15,000 people) fighting against layoffs and union-busting efforts by the company president, appealing to citizens for support. Historically, this piece is interesting as it represents the translation and consumption of Japanese proletarian literature in colonial Korea, reflecting shared class concerns across the empire despite the colonial hierarchy. It vividly portrays social unrest, police control, and the methods used by labor movements (flyer distribution) in early 20th-century urban Japan, themes that would resonate with Korean intellectuals and activists experiencing similar industrialization and labor issues under Japanese rule.

1. 삐라
전차가 멈추엇다. 자동차가 멈추엇다. - 자전거도, 짐자동차, 싸에드, 카드, 쏜살가치 달녀와서는 뒤로 뒤로 멈추어젓다.
- 엇전 일이야!
- 무에야, 무슨 일이 일어낫느냐!
깟득 모혀섯든 만혼 사람들의, 몸시 단순한 그 얼골과 얼골을, 싯누-런 시월의 태양(十月의 太陽)의 지독한 몬지 가운데로부터 거칠게 끄집어내고 잇다.
사람의 물결은, 웅뎅속에 올챙에떼처럼 뒤로 뒤로 밀녀와서는 웅글웅글 움지기고 잇다.
- 어행차이시다. XX궁전하의 고사(高師행게시다!!.
멘압줄의 속색임이 눈깜작할둥안에 뒤로 퍼저갓다. 자동차는 폭음(爆音)을 굿치고, 사람은 모자를 버섯다.
십오분이 좀 지난 뒤, 맨압줄에 선 사람은 금줄의 경부와 쫙둘너슨 경관의 거수의 사이로 다섯대의 자동차가 필님의 그림자처럼 소리업시 다러나는 것을 보앗다. 샛깜헌 XXXXXX한번 번적이고 몬지투성이의 광선가운데로 번적 군중의 눈을 쏘앗다. 그러나 뒤줄에 선 사람에게는 경관의 모자만 보혓슬 분이다.
차단선(遮斷線)이 풀엿다.
사람의 물결이 뚝을 텃치고 흘너젓다.
그때, -아야-ㅅ, 이놈의 자식, 정신을 차려라!
몰켜끼워가든 헌옷을 입은 사나히가, 펄적 뛰면서 소리를 질넛다. 누런 레인코트를 입은 남자가 별안간 그의 가삼에 부듸친 까닭이다.
- 엇지 이래. - 가치 부듸친 이, 삼인이 한거번에 소리첫다. 헌옷은 굴강(屈强)한 팔을 뻣어서 이 난폭한 양복장이이, 레인, 코트자락을 움켜 쥐엇다.
- 저놈을 붓잡어라!
레인, 코트는 붓들니우면서, 군중의 억개넘어로 올은 팔을 내밀면서 이러케 소리첫다.＜78＞
- 저놈을 붓잡어라! 그는 소리를 질으면서 군중의 사이를 헤이면서, 압프로 뚤코 나갈녀고 하엿다. 그순간(瞬間) - 펄넝펄넝, 석자 남짓한 놉히에, 팍 춤추며 소삿다. 쌕하얀 조희쪼각이, 펄넝펄넝 머리위에 떠러지는 것을 군중은 보앗다.
- 저놈이다! 저등거리 입은 놈을 붓드러라!
형사가튼 사나히가 소리첫다. 발을 밟힌 헌옷은, 깜작 놀나서 손을 노앗다. 그러나 이번에는 눈압페 튀여나온 정복순사가, 댓자곳자 그를 차던젓다.
그는 알어챈것가치 소리질넛다.
- 도적놈이다!
사람의 물결은 뒤죽박죽으로 혼란되엿다. 넘어진 자전거우에는 합비가 객켜 언젓다.

정복과 사복이, 군중을 거더차면서, 범인을 잡을녀고 뛰여단인다. 그러나 어듸로 새엿난지 정작등거리 입은 자 그림자는 벌서 업서젓다.
- 삐라를 거두웟나. 악가 그놈이 뿌리고 간... 레인, 코트는, 숨을 죄이면서, 정복에게 물엇다.
- 뵈이지 안는데요...
- 그럴 리가 업서 정신업시 -
그는 불쾌하게 머리를 혼들고, 뒤로도라 슬녀고 하얏다.
-아-ㅅ, 저것이다!
채워 넘어진 노파가, 땅바닥에 떠러진 조희쪽으로, 흙투성이가 된 압자락을 흠칠녀고 하고 잇섯다.
- 이것이 안닌가! 이것이다!
어리둥절하고 잇는 노파의 주위에, 군중이 몰켜왓다. 사복은, 삐라를 노파의 손에서 빼앗엇다.

친애하는 소석천구시민(小石川區市民) 제군 아울너 동경시민 제군에게 호소한다.
