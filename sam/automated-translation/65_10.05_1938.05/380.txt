Pak Yŏl's Ideological Conversion

- After 13 Years of Prison Life -

In March of Taishō 15 (1926), under the charge of high treason, Pak Chun-sik (this year he is 42), who went by the alias Pak Yŏl (朴烈), was sentenced to death along with his lover Kim Cha-mun (金子文子, who was 24 at the time). Later, through an act of Imperial clemency (恩命), he received a special commutation to life imprisonment. Since then, after serving time in Chiba Prison (千葉刑務所), he has spent the not-short duration of 13 years gazing only at the prison window as the fleeting years passed.
Pak Yŏl, the high treason criminal who had spent 13 years in silent seated meditation (端坐無言), repeatedly engaging in quiet contemplation (靜思), must have had something weighing on his mind that he kept hidden from others. Not long ago, he submitted a "Statement of Allegiance" (恭順上申書) before Prison Warden Andō (安藤), declaring, "I will offer my life and death for Japan," thus firmly expressing his ideological conversion (思想的 轉向). This is a recent development.
There is a saying that in ten years, even mountains and rivers change, and mulberry fields turn into blue seas (桑田碧海). During the 13 years Pak Yŏl, who once shook the world, spent in prison, the outside world changed tremendously. While he was devotedly pursuing a life of allegiance (恭順) in his solitary cell, the powerful tide of the times (時潮) flowing relentlessly outside the iron bars eventually triggered the China Incident. The wind of suppression and conversion swept not only through the camps that once cried for left-wing ideology but even through the ranks of liberals. Realizing that the era of national general mobilization had arrived, from his solitary cell in Katsushika Kosuge (葛飾小管), he wrote to an old friend outside the bars about donations for patriotic aircraft, expressing his Japanist sentiments and deeply moving the hearts of his old friends.

Pak Yŏl had called for the liberation of the Korean people and organized the "Society of Disaffected Koreans" (不逞鮮人社). When the high treason case was exposed, he was arrested along with his beloved mistress Kim Cha-mun and subjected to a long and harsh interrogation (慘烈한 取調). Being inherently strong-willed, he remained completely silent (緘口不言) about the entire substance and details of the case, astonishing the interrogating police officers at the time. Consequently, the preliminary judge of the Tokyo District Court (東京地方裁判所) at the time, Mr. Tachibana Kaisē (立松懷淸), finding considerable difficulty in uncovering the truth of the case,<206> attempted to soften Pak Yŏl's firm disposition by showing him kindness. This led to the notorious photo incident between Pak and Mun-ja, making "Pak Yŏl and Kim Cha-mun" a name that stirred the world in various senses.
Upon being incarcerated in Chiba Prison in Taishō 15 (1926), he cut off all contact with the outside world. Since then, even reading newspapers and magazines was completely forbidden, and he spent 10 years solely in introspective quiet contemplation (靜思). Then, in the summer of Shōwa 10 (1935), he suddenly sent out from prison a "Statement of Allegiance," expressing a 180-degree ideological turnaround, finally marking a day of heartfelt rebirth (赤心蘇生) in the life of the high treason convict.
Since then, he again ceased communication from prison, and now three years have passed! The former preliminary judge, lawyer Tachibana, who had a deep connection with him in the past, has already passed into another world, and his beloved wife Kim Cha-mun also committed suicide in the women's section of Bōmoku Prison (榜木刑務所). Now, only a few friends remain in the world. Truly, it is a transient world of constant change (有爲變轉)!
During these three years, when even his few remaining friends had lost contact with him, suddenly, a long letter flew out from Pak Yŏl in prison to a certain old friend, detailing his current state of mind.
"...For the past 10 years, from the standpoint of the Korean people, from the standpoint of the oppressed class, I intended to fight against Japan to the very end. However! Over these 10 years, as I sat quietly reflecting repeatedly on the changing conditions of the world day by day, and the reality of Japan, now my only thought is a sincere desire to become a loyal Japanese subject myself..."
The letter was filled with passages like this, each phrase deeply moving the reader's heart. At the end of the letter, he included a recently composed haiku (俳句): "濡れ衣をいまぬぎすて*日本晴れ" (Now casting off the false accusation / A clear Japanese sky) and also recited a poem (詩歌) like this: "門出れぼ雨が降つても晴天ぞ 濡れてもかまはぬ傘置いて行け" (When setting out, even if it rains, it's clear weather / Don't worry about getting wet, leave the umbrella behind), expressing a fragment of his state of mind.

Pak Yŏl was transferred to Kosuge Prison (小菅刑務所) at the end of October last year. In his solitary cell on the second floor of North Building 2, the fierceness (凶暴性) he once displayed has now vanished without a trace, and he works diligently in the printing department's bookbinding section*****.<207>

Three Figures of Japan's Anti-Communist Camp

- Home Minister Suetsugu, Superintendent-General Abe, Police Affairs Bureau Director Tomita -
On January 12th, newspapers across the country simultaneously issued extras reading "People's Front Faction Roundup (人民 戰線派 總檢擧)." According to the reports in these extras, individuals who had hitherto boldly put their names before the entire nation – Diet members, prefectural assembly members, city, town, and village assembly members, as well as figures who had long gained fame in the forums of magazines and newspapers – were listed one after another.
Subsequently, the authorities announced the following:
"...In light of this incident and the general situation of communist movements in our country recently, we wish to make a particular point at this juncture. Since the Comintern adopted new movement policies such as establishing an anti-fascist popular front and disguising or utilizing legal movements, as previously mentioned, all communists have been striving to infiltrate extreme social democratic groups or liberal groups, or to utilize their movements. Consequently, the scope of police control (取締) and vigilance must necessarily extend to such groups. Thus, we have reached a situation where democratic and liberal ideologies carry a significant risk of becoming breeding grounds (溫床) for communist ideology. At this time, we keenly feel (痛感) the increasing necessity to thoroughly fortify the national spirit and overcome these ideologies."
The announcement concluded with these words, delivering a rebuke (通*) to the series of liberals (リベラリスト).
Although people might say that the storm began after Mr. Suetsugu (末次) came in, it can be said that the direction Japan was to take had already been firmly decided before that.
Starting with the Luokouch'iao Incident (盧溝橋事件) on July 7th last year, the Incident has progressed further, and today we have reached the point of discussing a long-term war.<209>
Japan is currently facing the greatest national crisis (國難) since the Russo-Japanese War. This is the current situation. It is precisely here that true national unity – a wartime footing – is demanded.

Moreover, in the current "China Incident," the issue is not merely the simple chastisement of violent China (暴支 膺懲) or the overthrow of the Nationalist government. A major ideological defense, "defense against communization" (赤化 防衛), has newly emerged as a historical mission.
Japan, due to its national polity (國體), has long fought against the evil hand (魔手) of communization. Today, it has concluded an anti-Comintern pact with Germany and Italy, clearly defining its position to the world and participating in the anti-communist crusade.
This fact represents the great mission that Japan is shouldering. Consequently, while conducting military warfare against China, it has become unavoidable to simultaneously practice the even more difficult ideological warfare.
Thus, these two wars are closely intertwined (密接不離) and are fated to be carried out concurrently. The fact that this ideological war plays a more important role in the current conflict than in any past war makes it unique.
That is how multifaceted and complex today's warfare is, and the problems on the home front (銃後) are also extremely difficult.

Therefore, the most crucial matter in wartime is the ideological issue on the home front. The longer the current war becomes a long-term conflict, the more importance this issue gains. Thus, the government has, in fact, been pouring all its efforts (傾注) into this area since the Incident began.
This means the completion (完備) of the domestic anti-communist front. Of course, countermeasures include economic issues and various other problems, but there is a reason why Admiral Suetsugu came to occupy the chair of Home Minister.
Because of this, the Home Ministry has been devising various countermeasures since the Incident, including the decisive action (處置) regarding Professor Yanaibara (矢內原). However, the first proactive measure taken was the dissolution and subsequent arrests related to the Japanese Proletarian Party (日本無産黨) and the National Council of Trade Unions (勞動組合評議會).
However, in examining our country's anti-communist front in this time of emergency, one cannot overlook the existence of the then-*ho (*保) Bureau Director, current <210> Metropolitan Police Superintendent-General Abe Genki (安倍源基), and the then-Security Section Chief, current Police Affairs Bureau (警保局長) Director Tomita Kenji (富田健治).
Mr. Abe (*安保 - likely a typo for 安倍) is a prominent figure (傑物) who has left a significant mark on the current ideological world. It was no coincidence that he suddenly became the Superintendent-General of the Metropolitan Police at the young age of 44 amidst the current wartime situation.
The path he has walked until today precisely matches the path Japan is currently walking.
In his late twenties, the young Mr. Abe dedicated himself solely to studies in turbulent China. Later, based in Peking (北京), he traveled back and forth to Shanghai (上海) and Canton (廣東), actively engaging on the international stage.
His book, "The Nationalist Party and the Chinese Revolution" (國民黨과 支那革命), speaks to a part of Mr. Abe's image at that time. From then on, he possessed a bold character (剛膽) belying his physique, which laid the foundation (礎石) for his present success. He is an extremely bold individual. His handling of the grave national crisis during the February 26 Incident, shuttling between the military and the Metropolitan Police Department (警視廳) by himself, is exceedingly famous. Since then, the military authorities have also placed great trust in his capabilities.
In July of Shōwa 7 (1932), he returned from China, and coinciding with the establishment of the Special Higher Police Department (特高部) in the Metropolitan Police Department, he was selected as its first Director.
This was Mr. Abe's debut onto the stage of full-fledged activity. At that time, following the Manchurian Incident and the May 15 Incident, domestic reform movements were burgeoning, while on the other hand, the communist movement reached its peak intensity. The Ōmori *kyang* (컁) Incident and the Atami Incident occurred consecutively, and the atmosphere in the Imperial Capital was tumultuous.
During that period, as the right-hand man (片腕) of former Special Higher Police Section Chief Mōri *Gen* (毛利元 - likely 毛利基 Mōri Motoi), he handled the aforementioned situations. Simultaneously, he displayed formidable skill (竦腕) in dealing with the subsequent Emergency Communist Party, Lynch Communist Party, Shinpeitai Incident (神兵隊事件), and Black *kyang* (흑색컁) Incident (*possibly referring to Kokuryūkai or similar groups*). Furthermore, in the two instances of the Municipal Tramway disputes in Shōwa 8 (1933) and Shōwa 10 (1935), he demonstrated his unique prowess, earning the reputation of a renowned Special Higher Police Director. Consequently, it was natural that he became feared like a demon in the realm of ideological movements.
However, on the other hand, he is a person with great compassion for his subordinates, and it is said that a considerable Abe faction remains within the agency. Especially in the Special Higher Police circles, he was deeply revered (崇敬).
Afterward, taking responsibility for the February 26 Incident,<211>

Simultaneously with the Konoe cabinet, he advanced to Police Affairs Bureau Director, and this time, under Home Minister Suetsugu, he ascended to the position of Metropolitan Police Superintendent-General, one of the top three posts in the Home Ministry and the highest peak for regional governors. Although the status of the Metropolitan Police Superintendent-General is said to have somewhat declined recently, it is still a position equal to or higher than the Vice Minister among the three Home Ministry posts, and the coveted * post for regional governors.
To advance from Director-General of General Affairs to Superintendent-General within a year is, in various aspects, more remarkable than Futabayama (*a famous sumo wrestler*).
Such a miraculous advancement must have been unexpected even for Mr. Abe himself. At the young age of 45, without experience as a governor, his future path is truly brilliant.

Superintendent-General Abe and Home Minister Suetsugu are comrades brought together by the times. While comrades generally lack the warm friendship of close friends, they possess a fervent fighting spirit, pledged by blood oath, to overcome any obstacle that arises between them and charge forward.
It is a natural course of events that the era which produced Home Minister Suetsugu also produced Superintendent-General Abe.
Furthermore, that same era placed Mr. Tomita Kenji (富田健治) in the position of Police Affairs Bureau Director at the young age of 41.
Mr. Tomita is an innovative figure who embodies the hopes (輿望) of the young officials (少壯官吏) in the Home Ministry. During his time as *wari* (*) Department Director in Osaka Prefecture, he even lectured police officers on Yoshida Shōin's (吉田松陰) "Seven Rules for Samurai" (士規七則).
In cooperation with Abe **, he is someone who, during his time as Police Department Director, boldly presented his arguments (論陣) at directors' meetings. He will steadily implement the convictions (信念) of police officers facing the emergency situation.
The Home Ministry, under *Naishō* (內將 - likely 内相 Naishō, Home Minister) Suetsugu, although the presence of Vice Minister Habu (羽生) cannot be ignored, will henceforth take powerful strides with Abe and Tomita as its central axis (樞軸).
Entering the second year under war, China's long-term resistance (長期抵抗) against Japan is intensifying daily, and its reliance on the Soviet Union is becoming increasingly blatant (露骨化).
What Japan needs most going forward, to consolidate its military gains more effectively thus far, is to strengthen the home front and establish a long-term wartime system.<212>
In this regard, while the newly established Ministry of Health and Welfare (厚生省) will equip various facilities for the home front, it is believed that the police network, centered on the Police Affairs Bureau (警保局), will be strengthened to further secure the national ideological policy.
In fact, it would not be an exaggeration to say that the majority of the police force's role henceforth will be ideological policing. This must be the duty assigned (附加) to Messrs. Abe and Tomita, and the reason they have risen to their current positions.

British, American, Soviet, Italian Fleet Strengths in the Pacific Ocean

Today, the winds and clouds over the Far East become ever more tense as the Sino-Japanese Incident transforms into a long-term war. At this time, what is the naval power of the Western powers arrayed in the seas of Asia? Let us first look at Great Britain's Far East Fleet.
The naval force Britain permanently stations (常置) on the surface of the Far East is currently weak. Compared to the imposing majesty (威容) of the Japanese Combined Fleet, it is truly negligible, liable to be swept aside with a single brush of armored sleeves (鎧袖一觸).
To show it in numbers, its entire strength consists of: 5 heavy cruisers (大巡) of 10,000 tons, 2 light cruisers (輕巡) of 7,500 tons, 1 aircraft carrier (航空母艦) of 11,000 tons, 10 destroyers (驅逐艦), 15 submarines (潛水艦), 1 submarine tender (潛水母艦), 18 river gunboats (河用砲艦), 9 sloops (スルー-プ), and several auxiliary vessels (特務艦).
Among these, the main force is the China Fleet, always based in Hong Kong (香港), comprising 4 new County-class heavy cruisers of the "Cumberland" (캄파-란드) type, and 1 light cruiser of 4,300 tons assigned (配) here. Additionally, an aircraft carrier, submarine tender, destroyers, and gunboats are all attached here. With just the remaining 1 heavy cruiser, 2 light cruisers, 4 sloops, and several auxiliary vessels stationed (기터잇는) there, the East Indies Fleet (동인도함대) is formed (編成).
Besides this, thousands of miles away from Hong Kong, on the southern seas, exist the Australian Navy and the New Zealand Navy. The former possesses 2 heavy cruisers, 1 light cruiser, 1 seaplane tender (수상기모함) of 5,000 tons, and 10 destroyers. The latter holds 2 older light cruisers and 1 training ship of 3,000 tons. These <213> forces, even if the home country's Far East Fleet were pursued by a strong enemy and on the verge of annihilation (瀕) in the Pacific, would likely not leave the protection (*) of their respective territorial coasts (沿岸).
Britain's Far East Fleet is thus this weak (劣弱) fleet. Even if the dominion navies are included, it barely matches a fraction of the Japanese Navy. The only forces capable of resistance are the 7 new heavy cruisers, the aircraft carrier, and the 15 submarines.

As bases (根據地) for Britain's Far East Fleet, Hong Kong exists, and also the new Singapore (新嘉坡) naval base, nearing completion, as everyone knows.
Try unfolding a map of the Pacific. Singapore is the small island attached to the tip (尖端) of the Malay Peninsula (馬來半島), which hangs off the end of India.
4,539 *ri* east of Suez (スエズ), controlling (扼する) the mouth (口) of the Makassar Strait (마까랏해협), in this vast jungle region of swamps (泥沼) and dense forests (叢林), Britain has spent 20 years of time (星霜) and thrown in a huge sum (巨資) of 200 million to construct a formidable sea fortress (堡), intended as its base for Far Eastern operations.
Twenty years have passed, encountering various obstacles along the way, and construction was halted several times. Yet, with the admirable perseverance (堅忍不拔) characteristic of the British, this great fortress (要塞) has finally reached completion.
The scale (規模) of the Singapore fortress, now presenting an imposing (儼然) majesty over the vast waves (浩波) of the Indian Ocean, can truly be said to be unprecedented (未曾有).
It has heavy oil storage facilities with a capacity (貯藏量) reaching 1,250,000 tons. One must remember that this figure (數字) is enough to sustain (支撑) the entire Grand Fleet of Great Britain for the long period (長時日) of half a year (半個年).

The Strength of White Russians in Manchuria
Their Numbers, Occupations, and Distribution
Triggered (契機) by the great revolution of 1917, during the subsequent civil war and unprecedented famine (饑饉), hundreds of thousands, perhaps even more, Russians abandoned their homeland and flooded (汎濫) like waves to various parts of the world.<215>
Needless to say Paris (파리) or Berlin (伯林), where nearly 100,000 Russians were said to reside in the 1920s, they wandered seeking refuge (安住地) throughout the globe, excluding the Soviet Union: from Northern European countries (北歐), Near Eastern nations (近東諸國), British and American territories (英米領土), various colonial territories (各國植民地), French Indochina (佛領印度), Java (쨔-바), Borneo (포루네오), to the plains of South America and Mexico (墨西哥), and even to the wilderness of Alaska (알라쓰까). Of course, they also flocked (殺到) to the Far East – to China, Korea, and even mainland Japan.
Among those we once called "White Russian émigrés," there were those with unique pasts of "glorious" (光輝) careers – countesses, families of landowners, daughters of nobility, former artists of the old Imperial (舊帝室) theaters. On the other hand, the countless tragic stories (悲史) and sad tales (哀話) presented to our eyes and ears at the time involved the tears of the elderly, couples, young people, and orphans who lacked even sufficient travel expenses (旅費), let alone money to present upon landing. The narrow Japanese mainland, cultivated even to the mountaintops, could not become their place of settlement (安住地). At some point, they invariably left mainland Japan for China or America. Currently, apart from a very small number of remaining individuals (殘留人) in parts of Hokkaido, Kobe, and Nagasaki, only a group of Muslim peddlers (行商) of the *Torukodamaru* (도루고다마루) type, selling woolen cloth (羅紗), have settled (安住).
In the Far East, their places of settlement were Manchuria (滿洲) and the Chinese mainland. One can see them living diligently (孜孜) everywhere. Particularly, Harbin (哈爾賓) served as the center in Manchuria, with various locations in the North Manchurian region (北滿地方), Tianjin (天津) in North China (北支), and Shanghai in South China (南支) being the centers of their largest collective lives (集團生活).
The lives of Russians residing in the three centers of Harbin, Shanghai, and Tianjin have, of course, seen various ebbs and flows (消長) due to changes in the political situation up to the present day. However, among them, Harbin can be said to be the place where the old Russian culture and lifestyle were consistently preserved the longest.
Subsequently, with the implementation (*) of the North Manchuria Railway takeover some years ago, it is true that the total Russian population residing in Harbin drastically decreased (激減) due to the departure (退去) of Soviet-affiliated Russians. (According to statistics from a December Shōwa 9 [1934] survey, the Russians in Harbin at that time consisted of 20,824 Soviets and 34,169 White Russians [白系露人].)
Now, I will try to write about their individual lives.
Although referred to as White Russians (白系露西亞人), the fact that they consist of various ethnic groups (種族) has been mentioned above. Among them, the Great Russians possess the highest level (程度) of culture, which remains unchanged<216> from the past. Also, while there are many good people, there are also many unsavory individuals. Consequently, among them, there are those considerably superior in politics, arts, and academics, but at the same time, many who are less than admirable. Besides those who worked honestly (實直) for a long time as employees of the Chinese Eastern Railway, using their retirement pay (退職金) to spend a comfortable later life in the suburbs or near Nakhalovka (나하로푸가 - *Нахаловка*), there are also many who struggle with daily life and live day-to-day. Many hold occupations such as doctors, pharmacists, clerks, guards, laborers, porters, drivers, policemen, etc. Pure Russians lack business acumen (手腕), while those of Jewish descent are skilled in business, with many successful professionals among them. Those of Polish descent are often technicians (技術者). Caucasians (고-가사스인) seem to have decreased considerably in recent years, but most are wealthy, such as landowners, jewelers (貴金屬商), and owners of Caucasian restaurants. Armenians (알메니야인) often work as peddlers or dealers in miscellaneous goods (難貨商), and those of Greek descent (희랍계) often own grocery stores. Women are generally active. Many manage butcher shops or run boarding houses (下宿業) competently. During the era when opera and theater flourished, there were also many famous female artists (女流藝術家) and dancers. However, most of these people moved to the Shanghai area in recent years, and some have achieved considerable success in America. Many young women are active as clerks or typists, or as dancers in cabarets (캬바례), and naturally, some fall (轉落) into the dark streets.
As time flows, one can imagine that even among the émigré Russians, such human feelings of homesickness (望郷の懐抱) exist.
It is said that in recent years, starting with representative figures in Paris, the traditional political center of White Russians, the so-called "Defense Movement" (방위운동 - *Oborontsy* [оборонцы] movement - 요보론쯔이운동) has been advocated in various quarters.
This is a new movement asserting that, should the Soviet Union go to war with a foreign country, despite differences in ideology (主義の相違), they would participate in the defense of their homeland (祖國防衛). It specifically instructed China to organize the masses centered around anti-Japanism, utilizing the strong anti-Japanese sentiment among the populace. The first to respond (呼應) were the students in the Beiping (北平) area. 大正 15년 3월, 대역죄란 죄명으로 그의 애인 金子文子(당시 24세엿다)와 함께 극형(사형)의 언도를 밧엇다가, 그 뒤 恩命에 의해서 특히 무기징역의 감형을 바든 朴烈이란 별명을 가진 朴準植(금년이 42세이다)은 그 뒤 千葉형무소에 복역한 뒤로 오늘에 이르기까지 짧지 안은 13년 동안을 덧업는 세월을 옥창만 바라보고 지내왓든 것이다.
端坐無言 13년간 靜思를 거듭하든 대역 범인 朴烈도 흉중에 남몰내 생각하는 바가 잇섯든지, 「일본을 위해서 생, 사를 밧치겟노라.」하는 「恭順上申書」를 安藤형무소장 압헤 제출하며 단연 사상적 전향을 표명한 것은 얼마 전의 새로운 사실이엿다.
10년이면 강산이 변하고 桑田도 碧海가 된다는 말이 잇다. 일세를 진동식히든 朴烈이 옥중 생활 13년을 보내는 동안 박갓 세상은 엄청나게 변하엿다. 恭順한 독방 생활에 정진하고 잇든 그도, 철창 박게 거세게 흐르는 도도한 時潮는 맛츰내 支那사변을 야기식혓고, 일즉이 좌익사상을 부르짓든 진영은 말할 것도 업고 자유주의자의 일련에까지 탄압 전향의 바람은 석권되며, 국가총동원의 시기를 마지한 줄을 알고, 지금 葛飾小管의 독방에서 애국기 헌금에 대하야 철창 박 一舊友의게로 일본주의적 심경을 적어 보내여, 舊友들의 가슴에 감개가 무량케 하엿다.

朴烈은 조선 민족의 해방을 부르짓고 「不逞鮮人社」를 조직한 뒤, 대역사건이 폭로되자. 그의 애첩 金子文子와 함께 체포되여 오랫동안 慘烈한 취조를 바덧스니, 본래부터 성격이 강정한 그는 사건의 본체 전말에 대해서 일절 함구불언하여서 당시의 취조 경관들을 아연케 하엿다. 그래서 좀체로 사건의 진상을 찻기에 적지 안흔 곤란을 늣끼든 당시의<206> 東京 지방재판소 예심판사 立松懷淸씨는 朴烈의 굳은 성정은 풀어볼 내심으로, 그의게 친절을 보이는 나머지, 朴과 文子와의 사이에 예의 괴사진 문제까지 이르켜서, 「朴烈과 金子文子」라면 여러 가지 의미에서 일세를 경도케 하엿든 것이다.

그 뒤 先年 실시* 北鐵 접수에 딸아 소련계 로시아인의 퇴거로, 哈爾賓 在住 全 로시아인종이 격감하엿슬 것은 사실이다. (昭和 9년 12월 조사 통계에 의하면 당시 在 哈爾賓 로시아인은 소련 20,824인, 白系 露人 34,169인으로 되여잇다.)
이제 그들의 개개의 생활에 대하야 적어보려고 한다.
白系 露西亞인이라 하나 여러 종족으로 된 말은 이상에 말하엿스나 그 중에 가장 문화 정독 놉흘 것은 大로시아인으로 녯이나 지금이나 달을 것<216> 업다. 또는 好 인물이 만흔 반면에 不善한 사람도 만타. 딸아서 그들 중에는 정치, 예술, 학문 방면에 상당이 우월한 자 잇스나, 동시에 훌늉하게 지나지 못하는 자도 만타. 東支 철도의 종업원으로 오래 實直하게 근속하며 그 퇴직금으로 교외나 나하로푸가 邊에 안락한 여생을 보내는 자 외에는 일상 생활에 곤란하여 그날 그날를 보내는 자도 만타. 의사, 약제사, 점원, 수위, 노동자, 역부, 운전수, 순경 등의 직업이 만타. 純 로시아인은 직업적 재능은 업고 유대계의 그들은 ** 수완이 능하야 직업자로 성공자가 만흐며, 포- 라도 계는 기술자가 만타. 고- 가사스인은 근년에 대단이 적어진 듯 하나 대개는 부유자로 지주, 귀금속상, 고- 가사스 요리점 등으로 알메니야인은 행상, 難貨商, 희랍계는 식료품상 등으로, 일반적으로 부인은 활동적이다. 고기집 여주인 노릇 하숙업을 당당하게 경여하는 자 등이 만타. 가극과 연극이 성하던 시대는 유명한 여류 예술가, 무용가도 만이 잇섯다. 그러나 이런 사람들은 대부분 근년 上海 방면으로 가기도 하엿스며 다시 미국 방면에서 상당이 성공한 자도 잇다. 처녀들도 점원이나 타이피스트로 또는 딴사-, 캬바례에서 춤추는 여자로 활동하는 자도 만코 물론 어둔 거리로 전락하는 자도 만타.
세월이 흘음은 딸어 망명 露人 중에도 이런 인간적 망향의 회포가 잇슬 것을 상상할 수 잇다.
종래 白系 露人의 정치적 중심지인 파리의 대표적 인물을 필두로 하여 근년 소위 「방위운동」(요보론쯔이운동)이 각 방면에 제창되여 잇다고 한다.
만약 소련과 외국이 開戰하는 경우라면 주의의 相違는 잇서도, 조국 방위에 참가한다는 신 운동이다. 上海의 청년露西亞당(무라도롯츠이)은 帝政 復辟派로 분리하며, 이 신운동에 참가하엿다고 전한다. 그러타고 보면 일방으로 北滿지방에서는 로자에에푸스키를 당수로 하는 全露파시스트동맹이 당당이 반공운동을 개시한 모양이다.
만일 금후 세계가 두 개로 대립하는 사상 전선에 갈나지리라는 것이 사실로 된다면 금일까지 막연하게 「白系 露人」이라고만 알든 그들간에도 광의의 두 확연한 한계가 인식하게 되는 시대가 오지 안을가 한다.<217>

支那의 人民 戰線派

얼마나한 세력을 가지고 잇는가.
원래 인민 전선이라 함은 최초 불란서에서 시작한 것으로 1934년 즉 昭和 9년 6월에 불란서 사회당과 공산당의 공동 전선이 翌 1935년 즉 昭和 10년에 인민 전선이 되엿다.
그것을 본 컴민테룬 즉 모스코에 본부를 둔 국제공산당은, 1935년 夏 제7회 대회에서 새 전술로 이 인민 전선의 결성을 지령하고, 특히 支那에는 국민간에 항일 의식이 성함을 이용하여 항일이란 것을 중심으로 한 민중을 조직할녀고 하게되엿다. 그래서 「컴민테룬」 支那 지부인 중국 공산당은 그 지령대로 1935년 8월 1일로 유명한 항일 선언을 발표하고 민중 각 계급에 호소하게 되엿는데 최초에 호응하여 이러선 것이 北平 지방 학생이엿다. 北平에 학생은 이 해 12월 9월과 16일 양일에 北平 天橋란 곳에서, 민중 대회를 개최하여 시위 운동을 하여 경찰과 충돌이 생겨 다수에 부상자를 낸 소동을 연출하엿다. 그 보도가 각지에 전하자, 상하를 비롯하야 각지 학생이 이것에 호응하며 또는 노동자, 농민 등까지 파급하여 이들은 각각 항일 구국회를 맨들어 항일 운동에 몰두하기 시작하엿다. 이상과 갓치 컴민테른과 중국 공산당의 작전은 유리로 1935년 8월 1일로 약 1년이 지난 1936년 6월 경에는 支那 각 계급을 망라한 광범한 항일 인민 전선이 성립되엿다. 이럼으로 인민 전선파라는 말은 광의로서는 전술과 갓치 각 계급를 망라하엿다는 의미에 사용할 것이다. 我國 신문잡지에 인민 전선파라는 것은 협의로서 此 전선 전체를 칭함은 아니다.

그러면 엇던 계급이며 엇던 단체냐고 하면 작년 6월에 성립한 전국 각계 구국 연합회의 일파를 말함이다. 그리고 그 간부는 上海변호사회장 沈釣儒, 元浙江실업은행의 부 지배인으로 그 후 光華 대학의 교수된 章乃器, 교육가로 국난교육사소장인 陶行知, 항일 잡지 대중생활의<218> 주간이며 항일 전선의 총 대장인 鄒韜奮, 유명한 국가주의자의 변호사 겸 대학 교수의 王造時, 量才학교 교장인 李公樸, 변호사 沙千里, 여 변호사 史良 등이다. 이 외에도 잇스나 제일 활약하고, 제일 유명하게 된 것이 이 8인이다.
其 중에도 沈, 章, 鄒, 玉, 陶 5인은 항일 구국 운동의 5대 지도자라고 말하나 이 사람들은 최초 爲先 上海문화계구국회라는 단체를 조직하며 학자, 대학교수, 변호사, 신문 잡지 기자, 작가, 영화인, 배우 등을 망라하고 그 다음 이것을 기초로 上海 각 계 구국연합회를 조직하고 최후에 전국 각 단체 60여를 규합하며 전국 각계 구국연합회를 조직하엿다.
이것이 바로 인민 전선 본거라 할 만한 것이여서 沈釣儒, 章乃器 등은 동분서주 하엿다. 南京 정부에 대하여 내전 정지(즉 공산당 토벌 중지)를 청원하는 등 장문에 의견서를 발표하는 등 奈終에는 심지어 작년 11월에는 上海 我 방적회사 직공에 대하야 파업을 선동까지 하엿다. 南京 정부에서 당시는 아작 공산당과 타협하기 전임으로 그들 활동 배후에 공산당이 잠재한 듯 하다고 일망타진 후 상술한 8인을 포박한 것이다.
그러나 예에 西安 사건으로 흐지부지되고, 蔣介石과 공산당간 타협이 성립하여 첫재 조건으로 此等 8인 석방이 최근 실행되엿다. 蔣介石이 西安에 감금 중에 張學良과 揚虎城 두 사람으로부터 8개조의 요구 조건이 제시되엿다는 것은 당시 보도한 것과 갓거니와 그 중 일조가 「上海에서 포박당한 애국 운동의 수령을 석방하라」는 의미에 것이다. 그것을 蔣介石이 실행한 모양이다.

「人民 戰線」이란 무엇

그 語原과 各 國의 狀態

『인민 전선』의 어원은 1934년에 불란서 공산당과 사회당이 제휴하야 공동의 적인 팟쇼주의를 방위키 위하여 단일 전선을 결성한 것을 일너 인민 전선이라 하였다.
이래 모스크바에 본부를 둔, 국제<219> 공산당에서는 공산주의의 국제 선전 내지 실천의 일 전술로 이 인민 전선 운동을 취하게 되며 爲先 西班牙와 支那에 着目하며 전자에는 푸랑코 정권에 대립하는 인민 전선파 내각을 원조하고 후자에는 항일 인민 전선파 결성에 분주하였다.
支那 항일 인민 전선은 1935년 8월 1일에 中國共産黨 中央執行委員會와 중국, 소련 정부가 連名으로 항일 선언을 발표하며 그 선언을 지도 원리로, 국내 각종 항일 단체가 결성되여 1936년 6-7월경에 이르러 그 派 전선에 통일이 성립한 것으로 그 주류는 支那 공산당 (極左), 우익, 及 자유주의를 망라하고 또 예의 藍衣社 鐵血除奸團*의 우익도 가입한 극단의 복잡한 것으로 되였다.
我 일본에서 현재 인민 전선이라고 하는 것은 대개로 舊勞農派의 뒤를 니은 일파로 전번 결사 금지를 명한 일본 無産黨 及 전국 評議令 등 특수의 관계를 가진 일파도 亦 인민 전선파라 한다.

게리라 戰術
支那가 最後 手段으로 쓴다는

이 게리라 전술이라 함은 西班牙 말인데 교란 전투라, 기습 전법이라 말하는 것갓흔 의미로 往時 일본군이 西伯利亞 출병 당시에 露軍이 盛이 쓴 팔치산 전법과 비슷한 의의를 가젓다.
약소 군대가, 강대한 군대에 정면으로 대항한다면 승산이 업슴으로 의외에 토벌한다거나, 속여서 토벌하는 방법을 취하게 된다. 또는 惱重 기타 防護力이 적은 것을 급히 습격하며 양식, 탄약을 탈취하는 것으로 이것을 「게리라」전술이란 것이다. 옛날 나파륜 一世가 西班牙를 진격할 때 西班牙 軍이 그 전술를 채용하며 상당히 佛軍을 괴롭게 한 일이 잇다. 그때부터 이 「게리라」전술이란 말이 만이 유행하였다. 최근 이태리가 「에지오피아」를 공략할 때에도 「에지오피아」군에 이 전술를 사용하엿는데 신문에도 이 말이 盛히 쓰게 되였다.<220>
