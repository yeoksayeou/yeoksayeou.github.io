Millionaire Ch'oe Ch'anghak, the State of His Wealth Distribution, Rumors of Donating Two Million Won to Society with Close Friends
						By Ch'obaewang

People who saw the various capital city newspapers on April 8th all gasped, saying:
"Could that really be true?"
"Indeed, a man blessed by heaven."
Everyone was so astonished they didn't know what to do. And understandably so, because the news broke that Ch'oe Ch'anghak's mines were sold to Nihon Kōgyō (Japan Mining) for 6 million won.

Ch'oe Ch'anghak had recently gone to Tokyo, met with the executive director and executives of Nihon Kōgyō, gathered over 70 mines in Pyŏktong and Sakchu, North P'yŏngan Province, and signed a sales contract for 6 million won. He departed Tokyo on the 6th and returned to Seoul via the express train arriving at 3 PM on the 8th. While still in Tokyo, he had already handed over the Ch'osan and Pyŏktong mines to Nihon Kōgyō via telegram on the 5th, and handed over several more on the 7th. The handover of the majority of the remaining sold mining districts was also completed by mid-April, and he collected the payment of 6 million won (The Tong'a Ilbo reported 4 million won, the Chosŏn Ilbo 7 million won, and the Maeil Sinbo and Keijō Nippō 8 million won; although the reported prices varied, this publication has reliably ascertained it to be 6 million won) in one go.

Thus, his wealth, combined with his previous assets, reached over 10 million won, estimated to be around 15 million won. Becoming such a magnate through mining <330> is rare; in Tokyo, there is the former Minister of Communications, Kuhara Fusanosuke (久原邦之助), and from the old Korean era, the famous politician and Minister of Finance, Yi Yongik (李容翊). Although in recent years, the peninsula has seen the emergence of millionaires like Yi Chongman (李鍾萬), Wŏn Yunsu (元胤秀), Pang Ŭngmo (方應謨), Pak Yongun (朴龍雲), and Pak Kihyo (朴基孝), who each acquired several million won, Ch'oe Ch'anghak is still the only one to have become a great tycoon with assets currently exceeding ten million won. A ten-million-won fortune today might even surpass the wealth of the Min (閔) clan, and would look down upon figures like Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊) and Han Sangnyong (韓相龍) from afar. Even looking among the Japanese residents (내지인), excluding someone like Noguchi (野口) of Hamnam Nitrogen, there would likely be no individual property owner comparable to him.

How much is in Bank Deposits?

As word spread that Ch'oe Ch'anghak had become a wealthy man with astronomical figures, all sorts of talk arose about where his fortune was kept.
Some say there are 2 million won worth of gold bars (延金棒) stored in the underground vault of the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行), which the bank desperately wants him to sell, but he refuses, only requesting storage. Others claim that a frequent visitor to his house once saw him open a safe in a corner of a room, and inside, 100-won bills were stacked to the height of a hand span; he casually pulled out ten or so bills without even counting them and put them in his wallet for spending money... Besides these, there are many other stories. Based on my investigation, looking at the distribution, the story about storing gold bars at the Bank of Chosŏn seems plausible, and the story about stacking large sums of cash in an iron chest (鐵櫃) at his home also seems plausible. However, what I know is as follows.

2,000 Sŏk Paddy Fields in Kusŏng

In his hometown areas of Kusŏng (龜城) and Sakchu (朔州) in North P'yŏngan Province, when he acquired a large sum of money a few years ago, he cut off a chunk and bought paddy fields (水田) all at once, which are said to yield 2,000 sŏk (석, a traditional unit of volume) of harvest, valued at about 300,000 won.

50,000 Won House in Kusŏng

Also, when both his parents were alive, he built a splendid, palace-like house in Kusŏng, reminiscent of the luxurious palaces Qin Shi Huang might have enjoyed, for their filial care. Now, since his first wife has also passed away, the house is almost empty, with servants kept there to watch over it. In any case, building that house cost about 50,000 to 60,000 won. <331>

120,000 Won New House in Seoul

He began building a magnificent new house in Takezoe-chō 1-chōme (竹添町 1丁目), Seoul, last fall, which seems likely to be completed around May this year. This house, blending Western and Korean styles, is built on a fine plot of land on high ground and cost 120,000 won.

500,000 Won Land at the Former Andong Detached Palace Site

The front part of the former Andong Detached Palace (安洞 別宮) site, 230 p'yŏng (평, a unit of area), adjacent to where Mr. Min Taeju (閔大柱) bought the rear section to build the Hwimun Private Normal School (휘문사립보통학교) (now an elementary school), was purchased from the Office of the Yi Royal Family (李王職) at auction for 200 won per p'yŏng, meaning about 500,000 won is invested there.

3 Million Won Capital for Taech'ang Industrial Company (大昌産業)

This is the company run by Mr. Ch'oe, with its headquarters, a two-story Western-style building, right in front of his house in Takezoe-chō. This company builds buildings for rent or sale, buys and sells land, and also engages in finance using good collateral, with a total capital of 3 million won. Although it's called a joint-stock company, 99.9% of the investment is Ch'oe Ch'anghak's. The nearby Manpo Ford Western-style building was also bought by Taech'ang, and it owns many other plots and buildings within Seoul city.

4,000 Won for Dining Ware (飯床器), Etc.

While building the new 120,000 won luxury residence, he seems to be spending many tens of thousands of won on all sorts of furniture befitting it. Just the dining ware cost over 4,000 won, and the folding screens (병풍), mattresses (보료), blankets (담요), garden ornaments (정원의 주석), calligraphy, and paintings (서화) all seem to be worth hundreds or thousands of won each. Even so, adding all the above amounts to only 3 or 4 million won. It is speculated that there must be a place where a significant sum is stashed away, but apart from Ch'oe himself, even his secretaries don't know well. Presumably, it might be deposited in banks. His main banks in Seoul are:
Bank of Chosŏn Industrial Bank (조선식산은행) Head Office
Bank of Chosŏn (조선은행) Head Office
Hansŏng Bank (한성은행) Head and Branch Offices
Among these, he primarily deals with the Industrial Bank. There were rumors at one time that he had deposited 500,000 won in cash at the Shinŭiju branch of the Industrial Bank, or that he had deposited the entire 3 million won from the Sakchu gold mine sale at the Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) head office of the Industrial Bank, but since I haven't seen the passbook with my own eyes, I cannot be sure. However, it can be surmised that several million won are likely recorded in bank accounts. <332> His other assets are probably investments currently active in various gold mines.

Romance with His Current Wife – He is Blessed with Charm Too

People gossip endlessly, saying there can't be another person in the world as blessed by heaven (천복) as Ch'oe Ch'anghak. Indeed, in terms of wealth, he is doubly blessed by heaven, but what about his fortune with wives? After his former wife passed away years ago, there were rumors he took up with famous kisaengs (기생) of the capital, but none seemed to last long. There was no news of any woman officially registered in the family register (民籍). Then, this year, another splendid romance blossomed for him. In early January this year, at a grand banquet hall in the Chosun Hotel in Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町), Seoul, with about 300 distinguished guests, wealthy merchants and gentlemen of the capital, gathered, he held a wedding ceremony with an intelligent woman, beautiful as a flower and barely twenty-four years old. This woman who became the wife of the multimillionaire is Kim Chŏngsuk (金貞淑). She is a talented woman who graduated from Ewha Women's High School (梨花女子高普) and then attended the music department of Ewha Women's College (梨花女子專門) for two or three years. Her family home is in Sup'yo-dong (水標町), Seoul, and she has brothers who all received higher education. There is a romance story with this wife: Last summer, at Sinhŭngsa Temple (新興寺) just outside the East Little Gate (東小門), where Mr. Ch'oe was building a retreat, unable to resist the morning flowers and evening moon (朝花月夕) of the first summer, he drove his car out for a stroll to the temple, driven by poetic inspiration. That day happened to be when Marquis Yun Tŏgyŏng's (尹德榮) family was holding a Buddhist memorial service (齊). The ladies of the noble house, who usually never left the gate (門外不出), along with many attendants, had come out, prepared a lavish feast, and were holding a grand service, so grand the spacious dharma hall seemed to shake. At that time, many monks in long robes (長衫) were chanting sutras with hands clasped before the Buddha, and the ladies of this noble family, draped head to toe in silk, were bowing countless times, imagining lotus pedestals (蓮花臺) on their heads, praying for blessings.
Standing in a corner, leaning on his cane and intently watching this luxurious spectacle of a memorial service, Ch'oe Ch'anghak's eyes caught sight of a beautiful woman mingling in the crowd. Dressed from head to toe in snow-white mourning clothes (소복), looking shy and bashful, seeming as if she might be blown away by the wind, so fresh and graceful, she stood in a corner with her head slightly <334> bowed. The moment he saw her, his mind became intensely drawn to her. To say he was captivated (황홀하엿다) would describe the scene. Mr. Ch'oe couldn't take his eyes off her every move until sunset, when he returned, having accomplished nothing. In the car on the way back, he made a decision.
"She came from Marquis Yun's household, so perhaps she is the Marquis's daughter? If so, her family background is good, so I shall propose." Thus, upon arriving home, he immediately sent someone to find out about the woman. It turned out she had no connection to Marquis Yun's household but was the daughter of a certain rice merchant family in Sup'yo-dong, Seoul. It happened that the Kim (金) family, the rice merchants, had gone to Sinhŭngsa that day to hold their own service, which coincided with Marquis Yun's family's service. And so, a teacher named Yi (李) from X-dong Normal School (지금은 소학교 - now an elementary school) in Seoul acted as the matchmaker, and she entered Mr. Ch'oe's household as his legal wife. Thus concludes the tale of Ch'oe's amorous fortune (艶福史) in marrying this beauty, a woman educated at the highest institutions for women in Chosŏn, skilled in music, and beautiful of face.

Who is in His Family?

Here, we will present the family register transcript obtained from the Kyŏngsŏng-bu (Seoul City) Family Register Section exactly as it is, without altering a single character, at a later date, so readers may examine it closely as they wish.
His eldest son is an intellectual in his early 30s (31 or 32) who attended and left Chungang University (中央大學) in Tokyo. There seems to have been a newspaper report once that he was arrested by the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department (東京 警視廳) for harboring socialist ideas and engaging in some movement. He is said to be a young man with considerable insight into society who often advises his father, according to rumors. He is currently said to be in Kusŏng. The second son attends Kyŏngsŏng Middle School (for Japanese residents) and is 15 years old this year, supposedly in the 3rd grade.

Who are His Intimate Friends (莫逆한 親舊)?

Who is Ch'oe Ch'anghak close to? He doesn't have many friends. Among them, his closest friends are probably Pak Kihyo (朴基孝), who became rich through mining in Hamnam (咸南) and now lives in Shindang-chō (新堂町), Seoul, and a Mr. Chang (張) from P'yŏngbuk (平北). Next would be Pang Ŭngmo (方應謨), then Pak Yŏngch'ŏl (朴榮喆), Kim Kidŏk (金基德), Cho Pyŏngsang (曺秉相), Song Chinu (宋鎭禹), and Han Sangnyong (韓相龍), who seem to be drinking companions (酒朋) or acquaintances from entertainment gatherings (노릿좌석의 지기). He seems to have few exceptionally close friends. Hwang Taebyŏk (黃大壁) was his secretary but left last month due to a disagreement (수가<334> 틀니여 나와버렷고). Kim Sŭngbok (金昇福) has been his long-time secretary.
In other words, it seems Ch'oe has too much money, and thus, when he makes friends, they constantly ask for money, which becomes tiresome, so he avoids making many friends from the start. Furthermore, it's said he doesn't easily meet first-time visitors, regardless of their status. He also doesn't attend gatherings often. This spring, when Kim Kidŏk's second son's wedding reception was held at Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月館), Ch'oe's face was among the guests (빈속 - likely a typo for 빈객, guests). Such an appearance is probably rare (稀久).
Moreover, now that he is a ten-million-won tycoon, will he perhaps start going out with a police escort?

2 Million Won for Social Projects – Rumors of Intent to Manage a School on this Occasion

Until today, Ch'oe Ch'anghak's money has been, for Chosŏn society, "no better than a roadside stone" (不如 路傍의 一片石), meaning despite its vast amount, not a single penny (分) had been given away. No, wait. There were just two instances. Once, the former president of the Tong'a Ilbo, Yi Sŭnghun (李昇薰), persuaded him to donate 50,000 won for the endowment fund (基本金) of Osan School (五山學校) in Chŏngju (定州), P'yŏngbuk. And then, recently, when the Maeil Sinbo (每日新報) became a joint-stock company with one million won capital, he invested 20,050 won, purchasing 1,000 shares. Only these two times.

However, he surely knows he cannot carry his money with him to the afterlife when he dies. His descendants, no matter how much they spend, are like sparrows that peck their whole lives but cannot finish a single mal (말, a unit of grain) of rice before dying – at most, they might spend tens or hundreds of thousands of won. Therefore, it's unlikely that he, now past 50, hasn't thought about spending this vast fortune meaningfully before he dies. Years ago, when traveling to Paekch'ŏn Hot Springs (백천온천) by car with Song Chinu (宋鎭禹), Yi Sanghyŏp (李相協), Cho Pyŏngsang (曺秉相), and others, they fell from a thousand-fathom cliff (千仞 絶壁) over the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River (淸川江). Some suffered broken ribs, others had their facial skin torn off – having experienced such a catastrophe, hovering between life and death ("生死間 一髮"), he deeply realized the ephemerality of human life (人命이 덧업슴을 大悟하고). It was heard that he vowed to a friend to spend his money meaningfully before dying. However, that resolution soon faded, leading to the present situation.

But now, having acquired a large sum of 6 million won and become a magnate worth over 10 million won, someone close to him reports that he has made a significant decision. It is said that he intends to undertake a substantial social project – not a newspaper company, but managing a school – and has decided to donate 2 million won for this purpose. <335> It is also said that this announcement will likely be made at the housewarming ceremony (낙성식) for his house being built in Takezoe-chō, in front of all the top gentlemen of the capital gathered there.
If he does so, he might finally quell the heaps of insults (욕설을 산뗌이가치 어더먹든) he received from all sorts of people, calling him stingy (인색하단 말) or selfish (사리 사욕 밧게 모른다고), vent his frustrations (분푸리), and leave his esteemed name (芳名) in the annals of history (천추의 竹帛).
But who knows? The mind of a rich man is more complex and changeable than autumn clouds (가을 하늘 구름짱이 갈니기보다도 더 복잡다단한). Only on the day when 2 million won, or even just 100,000 won, actually leaves his hands and is dedicated to society, will society truly rejoice and welcome him with smiles.

From 70,000 Won to 6 Million Won – His Miraculous Luck with Money This Time

They say luck is good, luck is good, but few are as lucky as Ch'oe Ch'anghak. Just eight or nine years ago, his situation was such that he couldn't even secure a bowl of coarse millet gruel (조밥 대가리) for one meal a day, carrying a mining pick (마치) over his shoulder, crossing mountains and valleys, wearing straw sandals (황화 감발치고), humming tunes (콧장단 치며), wandering around searching for mines (광산차저 도라다니든 德大 - Tŏktae likely refers to his earlier, struggling self). Then he hit upon a good gold mine in Sakchu (朔州), sold it for 3 million won, and instantly climbed onto the golden chariot (황금의 수뢰). From then on, he succeeded in everything. Take the mines sold this time to Nihon Kōgyō for 6 million won – they were mines bought just last year for a mere 70,000 won! How many times the price is that? It sold for almost 100 times more, at 6 million won.
The mining areas he sold to Nihon Kōgyō (capital 300 million yen) this time comprise about 76 out of over 110 districts in Sakchu, Pyŏktong (碧潼), and Ch'osan (楚山), and it's said the remaining 40-odd districts might be added later. It's unknown how much more money heaven intends to lend this man.
Furthermore, it's heard he distributed 70,000 won among the roughly one thousand miners he employed, so it seems he has certainly started spending money.
It's also heard that he plans to build a very large building on the Andong Detached Palace site in Seoul this summer.
We earnestly hope that a great man like Ch'oe Ch'anghak will use his money splendidly, coming forward with an attitude of sharing joys and hardships with the people of the world. If he lives in a tiled-roof house (기와집), shouldn't it be good for others to live in tiled-roof houses too? Selfishness (독선) and personal gain (사리) are things to be most rejected.<336><329-336>

**Summary**

This 1938 article from the magazine Samch'ŏlli details the recent massive fortune amassed by Ch'oe Ch'anghak, a Korean mining magnate, after selling his North P'yŏngan Province mines to Japan Mining (Nihon Kōgyō) for 6 million won, bringing his total estimated wealth to 10-15 million won. It discusses the distribution of his known assets (real estate, company capital, personal possessions), recounts his recent marriage to an educated young woman, lists his few close associates, and speculates heavily on rumors that he intends to donate 2 million won to establish a school, contrasting this with his past reputation for stinginess. The article marvels at his rapid rise from poverty and expresses hope he will use his wealth for societal benefit. From a historical perspective, the article offers insights into the scale of wealth accumulation possible for some Koreans under Japanese colonial rule through resource exploitation, the social dynamics surrounding extreme wealth (envy, gossip, expectation of philanthropy), the interaction between Korean elites and Japanese corporations, and contemporary views on social responsibility among the wealthy Korean class. The mention of specific figures, banks, companies, and properties provides concrete details about the economic landscape of colonial Korea.

잡지명	삼천리 제10권 제5호
발행일	1938년 05월 01일
기사제목	千萬長者 崔昌學, 돈의 分布 狀態, 親한 親舊들과 二百萬圓을 社會에 내놋는단 所聞
필자	草貝王
기사형태	소식

千萬長者 崔昌學, 돈의 分布 狀態, 親한 親舊들과 二百萬圓을 社會에 내놋는단 所聞
						草貝王
지난 4월 8일의 都下 각 신문을 본 사람들은 모다 입을 헤 - 버리고
「그거 정말일가」
「과연 천복을 타고난 사람이군.」
하고 모다 놀나서 엇절 줄을 몰낫다. 그도 그럴 것이 崔昌學의 광산이 6백만원으로 일본 광업에 팔녓단 기사가 난 때문이다.
崔昌學은 그 동안 동경 드러가 일본광업 전무와 간부 등을 맛나 平北 碧潼, 朔州의 광산 70여 개소를 모라서 6백만원으로 매매 계약을 하고, 6일 동경 출발, 8일 千后 3시 급행으로 서울에 도라왓다. 광산은 벌서 楚山, 碧憧치를 동경 잇슬 때 전보로써 5일날 日鑛에 인도햇고 7일에 또 數三處를 인도햇스며, 남어지 매도 鑛區 대부분도 4월 중순까지 전부 인계를 了하고 대금 6백만원(東亞日報는 4백만원, 朝鮮日報는 7백만원, 每日申報 及 京城日報는 8백만원으로, 보도되여 가격이 區區不一햇스나 본사가 확실히 탐문한 바에는 6백만원이라 한다)을 한 손에 거두워 쥐엿다.
이리하야, 그의 재산은 과거의 녯 재산까지 합처서 1천만원 이상 천5백만원 정도에 달하엿다. 광산으로<330> 이런 거부 되기는 동경에 녯 체신 대신 久原邦之助가 잇고 그리고 舊 한국 시대에 유명한 정객이자 大藏院卿이든 李容翊씨가 잇슬 뿐, 근년에 반도에도 李鍾萬, 元胤秀, 方應謨, 朴龍雲, 朴基孝 등 수백 만원 정도를 바더 쥔 성금이 속출하기는 햇스나, 현금 천만원 이상의 거대한 재산가가 되어본 이는 아직 崔昌學 1인 뿐으로 현재의 천만원 재산가라면 閔가 일족의 재산도 능가할 지경이요, 尹致昊, 韓亮鎬 등 모다 멀니 足下에 내려다보게 되엿스며 내지인들을 둘너 보아도 咸南질소의 野口가튼 사람을 제하고는 그에 따를 개인 재산가가 업슬 것이다.

銀行 預金은 얼마마 잇는가?

崔昌學이 천문학적 수자를 가진 큰 부자되엿다는 소리가 터지자 그의 재산 둔 곳에 대하야, 별에 별 말이 다 만타.
엇든 사람은 조선은행 지하 금고에 보관하여 둔 延金棒이 2백만원어치 잇는데 은행서 아모리 팔나해도 아니 팔고 보관만 의뢰하고 잇다는 등, 또 엇든 이는 그 집으로 자조 출입하는 문객이 그 언젠가 방 한구석에 노힌 금고를 여는 것을 보니까, 100원짜리 한 뼘 기리로 그득이 싸여잇는 속에서 열 아문 장을 세지도 안코, 와락 끄내어 제 돈지갑에 용돈으로 너트라고 하기도 하고... 그 박게 별에 별 말이 다 만흔데, 내가 조사한 범위로 그 분포 상태를 보건대 조선 은행에 금방망이로 금을 보관해 두엇단 말도 그럴듯한 말이요 제 집 철궤속에 거금을 싸여두고 잇드란 말도 그럴듯한 말이다. 그러나 내가 아는 범위로는 이러하다.

龜城에 2千石 직이

자기 고향인 평안북도 龜城과 朔州 지방에 몃해 전에 큰 돈이 생겻슬 때에 한목 뚝 잘나 한 퇴에 노힌 水田을 산 것이 잇는데 그것이 추수 2천석 짜리라 하니 약 30만원으로 칠 것이요.

龜城 住宅에 5만원

그리고 부모 양친이 다 생존하엿슬 때에 孝養을 하기 위해서 龜城에다가 그야말로 녯날 진시왕이나 놀든 것 가튼 호화 장려한 아방궁가튼 집을 지엇는데, 지금은 자기의 전처까지 죽엇슴으로 그 집에 텡 - 비다십히 하고 하인들을 두어 집 직히고 잇스나 엇잿든 그 집짓기에 약 五六만원이<331> 들엇고

서울 新宅에 12만원

서울 竹添町 1丁目에 작년 가을부터 시작하여 짓는 훌륭한 新宅이 잇는데 아마 금년 5월경에는 낙성될 것 갓다. 이 집이 양식 조선식을 어울너 高臺의 地所 조흔 곳에 지은 집으로 12만원이 들엇다.

녯 安洞 別宮 土地에 50만원

閔大柱씨가 뒷쪽을 사서 휘문사립보통학교(지금은 소학교)을 지은 그 잇대인 전면 - 녯날 안동 별궁 자리 230평을 그때 李王職에서 낙찰할 적에 매 평에 200원씩 주엇다니 여기에 약 50만원이 들어잇다.

大昌産業의 資金 3백만원

崔씨가 하는 회사로서 본사는 바로 竹添町 자기집 압헤 잇는데 양식 2층집이다. 이 회사는 건물을 지어 집세 노커나 팔거나 하며, 또 토지도 매매하고, 그리고 조흔 담보물을 잡고 금융업도 하는 회사로서 총자본금 3백만원이다. 일홈은 주식회사지만 9할 9분까지가 崔昌學의 출자이다. 그 이웃 만포-드의 洋舘도 大昌서 삿고 그박게 서울 시내에 지소와 건물을 만히 사 가지고 잇다.

飯床器에 4천원 其他

그리고는 새 주택을 지으면서 12만원 짜리 호화로운 저택에 어울니게 온갓 가구를 아마 여러 만원을 드려 사노고 잇는 양으로 반상기에만 4천원을 너헛고, 병풍, 보료, 담요, 정원의 주석, 서화 등도 모다 수 백원 수 천원 짜리들인 듯. 그러터래도 이상을 합친 때야 3, 4백만원에 불과하다. 이밧게 어디엔가 한 곳에 흠씬 싸어둔 곳이 잇스리라고 추측되지만 그것은 崔 자신 이외에는 그 집 비서들도 잘 모른다. 추측컨대 아마 은행에 예금이 되여 잇슬 것으로 서을서 그의 取引 은행은

現 夫人과의 로-맨스-그는 艶福도 兼하엿다
