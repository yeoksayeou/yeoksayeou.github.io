Society's Inner Workings

Chronicle of Capital Figures' Comings and Goings

Marquis Pak Yŏnghyo = (Vice-Chairman, Central Advisory Council) Tōyama Mitsuru's son recently suffered a stroke and visited the Marquis, who is also bedridden, reportedly weeping at his bedside out of deep emotion. The Marquis's illness is not minor; he has already been confined to bed for several months.
Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl = (Honorary Consul General of Manchukuo) He visited Beijing and the Mengjiang government for about two weeks. It is said this trip was funded by confidential funds from Manchukuo.
Mr. Han Sangnyong = (Supreme Advisor to the Kwantung Army) When he went to Beijing with Marquis Yun Tŏgyŏng, he met all the key political figures, but it's heard he couldn't meet Wu Peifu, who is rumored to be a presidential candidate. Wu is reportedly famous for not residing in foreign concessions and not meeting foreigners.
Mr. Yun Ch'iho = In his youth, he avidly studied history, but these days he doesn't read much. He occasionally strolls with a cane at Ch'wiunjŏng (翠雲亭), enjoying the spring before returning.
Mr. Hŏ Hŏn = (Managing Director, Taedong Publishing Company) Unable to have his rights restored even in the recent special amnesty, Mr. Hŏ remains silent, diligently attending to his duties morning and evening as part of the Taedong concern. There is still no news of his daughter, Miss Chŏngsuk.
Mr. Kim Myŏngsik = An impoverished elder polemicist, he is hard of hearing and conducts affairs through written conversation. His solitary figure, marked by hardship and lack of success, is occasionally seen coming and going on the streets of Chongno<13>.
Mr. Ch'oe Rin = (Leader of Sijunghoe) Rumors suggest he might take up the presidency of a certain newspaper company, forming a major organization worth one million won. With public expectation and favorable timing converging, the world watches his movements closely.
Mr. Kim Sŏngsu = (President of Posŏng College) Having completely cut ties with the Tonga Ilbo, he now devotes all his energy day and night to the completion of a great Posŏng, much like Count Ōkuma who built the great Waseda. The public is watching closely to see who will be the first to achieve the status of a "private university"—Kim Sŏngsu, Yi Chongman, or Wŏn Han'gyŏng.
Mr. Ch'oe Kyudong = (Principal of Chungdong School) He suffers from chronic asthma. Having nurtured tens of thousands of youths over 30 long years, his mind and body seem utterly exhausted. For the construction of a great Chungdong, we hope Heaven grants him long life (壽).
Mr. Yu Ŏkkyŏm = (Vice-President of Yŏnhŭi College) Around 6 PM, as the evening train from Sinch'on is about to arrive, one can encounter Mr. Yu, looking healthy and ruddy, on the platform of Namdaemun Station. Sometimes mingling with students, sometimes among the female faculty of Ewha College (梨專 女敎員團), he never loses his youthful spirit.
Mr. Yi Chongman = (President of Taedong Mining Company) Mr. Yi, who shone brightly during the historic moment of Sungsil College's (崇專) demise and the opening of Taedong Technical College (大同 工專), is now said to be marching straight towards the completion of various projects promised to society. Even recently, he often visits the Changjin Mine dressed in miner's clothes.
Mr. An Chaehong = (Former President of the Chosŏn Ilbo) One early spring evening at twilight, I saw his lonely figure standing absently in front of the Bank of Chosŏn plaza. While Minse (An Chaehong's pen name) might not be the only one whose heart is filled with myriad emotions, it seemed particularly so for him. He spends his days quietly in his study, meditating and reading.
Ms. Pak Indŏk = After returning from America (米洲), there were talks of her doing rural work, becoming a professor at some school, or starting a magazine company, but soon she submerged again, and there's no news. Perhaps as the spring light ripens, she will press forward with the business plan she contemplated while crossing the Pacific.
Mr. Ku Chaok = (General Secretary of the Central Youth Association) Though small in stature (小軀), he displays his vigorous (精悍한) self at various social gatherings. In this time of crisis for Christianity, his activities are anticipated even more.
Mr. Sin Hŭng'u = (Director of Paejae High Common School) Having lost his position (失脚) many years ago, he recently became a director at Paejae School. He sometimes appears in places like the Asia Cafe (亞細亞 다방)<14> on Chongno, displaying a cheerful, refined, Westernized appearance, adding a touch of uniqueness (이채). Mr. Sin is also a star figure (花形) in our society.
Mr. Chang Tŏksu = (Lecturer at Posŏng College) Having taken up a lectureship at Posŏng College, he is diligently handling over 20 hours of English classes per week, morning and evening. After a long time, he published an article on "Political Affairs in Europe and America" in the New Year's edition of the Tonga Ilbo.
Mr. O Kyŏngsŏn = (Former President of Severance Medical College) After retiring from the presidency at the age of 60, he is now quietly reading in his study. On the day when Severance College (세專), which he cherished and nurtured (愛之育之), becomes a comprehensive university, Mr. O may perhaps rise again despite his aged body (老軀).
Mr. Pang Ŭngmo = (President of the Chosŏn Ilbo) He spent 15,000 won to hold the Chosŏn Products Exhibition. The exhibition is good, but if possible, we hope he will use the large-scale newspaper company as a background to host a grand event like a Chosŏn Exposition around the year after next, when the Olympics are held in Tokyo.
Mr. Pak Hŭngsik = (President of Hwasin Company) He recently invited prominent businessmen of the capital to his residence in Ch'wiunjŏng for an evening of fellowship. Rumor has it he is currently channeling his energy into realizing a major 10 million won company.
Mr. Paek Kwansu = (President of the Tonga Ilbo) As secretary of the Ch'unch'uhoe (春秋會), he has recently been active in aligning with the lines of press control. He seems to have many dreams of rebuilding the Greater East Asia era of old. Judging by the fact that the office building expansion hasn't happened yet, financial recovery still seems to require time (尙要時日).
Mr. Kwŏn Tongjin = (Leader of Ch'ŏndogyo) He is striving to lead (領率) the troubled Old Faction of Ch'ŏndogyo. However, being elderly now, he is often ill, and otherwise, he can only manage walks in his garden. One might say his condition makes one lament, "Where is the spirit of those years now?" (當年 意氣 今安哉).
Mr. Cho Mansik = He came up to Seoul from P'yŏngyang for Tosan (An Ch'angho)'s funeral and returned immediately the next day. What various sentiments must he have felt (늣기는) looking out the train window?
Mr. Yŏ Unhyŏng = (Former President of the Chungang Ilbo) His cheerful and striking (爽英한) figure appears during the day (나제는) at places like Paekhabwŏn (百合園) or Yŏngbo Grill (永保 그릴). Around 3 or 4 PM, he sometimes shows up beside the desks (机겻헤) at Maruzen Western Books (丸善洋書) in Chin'gogae, standing and reading new Western books and magazines before leaving. He says because various people visit him constantly at home, he often takes such strolls on the street.

The Inside Story of Dr. Yi Becoming Chief Editor

Dr. Yi Hun'gu, former Vice-President of Sungsil College (崇專), who had been active as a major pillar of Yi Chongman's Taedong concern and the top executive of the Taedong Rural Company (大同農村社),<16> suddenly changed direction and took office as the chief editor of the Chosŏn Ilbo, succeeding Mr. Sŏ Ch'un. There are various inside stories to this. Specifically, Dr. Yi's hidden efforts were significant in getting Mr. Yi Chongman to take over P'yŏngyang Sungsil College with a huge sum of 1.2 million won. Moreover, considering the nature of the matter and the sequence of events, it was publicly acknowledged (자타 공인하엿다) that Dr. Yi would naturally assume the presidency after P'yŏngyang Sungsil College was reorganized into an agricultural college. However, due to circumstances related to the larger picture, Sungsil College was transformed into a technical college (工業專門). Even though there was a plan for a second stage to establish an agricultural college in Taegu within a few years, Dr. Yi's position forced him to consider advancing or retreating. Knowing this, the Taedong side apparently suggested he might as well study abroad for several years, and one story even claims they offered to provide a monthly scholarship of 200 won. However, at that time, with Mr. Cho Mansik's recommendation also playing a part, he received an invitation from President Pang of the Chosŏn Ilbo Company and thus suddenly entered the world of journalism.

Koreans Flocking to Beijing

Recently, famous and unknown figures, including Lieutenant Colonel Yi Ryang and Mr. Yun Paengnam, have been flocking to Beijing like waves. Before the Incident (Sino-Japanese War), the number of Koreans and Japanese mainlanders was only 5,000, but recently it has doubled to about 10,000. At Zhengyangmen Station (正陽門驛) alone, daily passenger arrivals and departures exceed 1,000. According to observations by the Japanese Embassy, about 1,000 people are flowing into North China (北支) and Central China (中支) daily.
1,000 in Tianjin: The number of Koreans in Tianjin is also increasing daily, recently surpassing approximately 1,000.

Thirty Percent of Seoulites Understand the National Language (Japanese)

According to a survey at the end of Showa 12 (1937), among the 572,774 Koreans residing in Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture, those who know a little of the national language (Japanese) numbered 28,396 men and 15,948 women. Those capable of ordinary conversation numbered 105,145 men and 28,940 women. The proportion relative to the total population was 47.3% for men and 15.4% for women, with the total proportion being merely 31.5%.

Three Hundred Thousand Won for Tŏksu Palace Art Museum

For the 400,000 citizens of the capital Kyŏngsŏng, Tŏksu Palace across from the Prefectural Office serves as a pleasure ground offering a day's pure enjoyment. Now, cheerful news arrives that a new art museum within the palace, facing the cherry blossoms burgeoning this spring, is nearing completion and will soon be revealed to the citizens. Construction on this museum, located directly in front of the old Stone Hall (Sŏkchojŏn), began around July last year with a total construction cost<19> of 300,000 won. Recently, its exterior has been completed, showcasing its magnificent and beautiful Renaissance-style form, shining brilliantly under the sunny sky.
This art museum is a three-story building, with each floor covering over 300 pyeong (approx. 990 sq meters), totaling 1,029 pyeong (approx. 3,400 sq meters), significantly larger than the Stone Hall. By the end of April at the latest, the interior decoration will be finished (끗맛치고), and the exquisite art and craft items of the Yi Dynasty (李朝), currently displayed at the Ch'anggyŏng Garden (昌慶苑) museum, will be moved (옴겨다가) here for public viewing.
Furthermore, in its front garden, a large water basin is being created to raise carp, crucian carp, goldfish, etc., and a beautiful fountain is also being made, with work proceeding rapidly.
This summer, amidst the deepening green foliage of Tŏksu Palace, the Stone Hall and the newly adorned art museum will stand subtly, offering a refreshing sight (一景) to the eyes of Seoul's people.

Financial World's Inner Workings

Rumor of Hwasin's Four Million Won Public Offering

There was talk that Hwasin, managed by President Pak Hŭngsik, would consolidate all its direct and affiliated branches into one large company and increase its capital to 10 million won. However, due to the outbreak of the Sino-Japanese Incident in July last year, everything was put on hold, affected (걸니어) by the Temporary Funds Adjustment Law. Recently, however, one rumor suggests that the plan is to create the 10 million won company within this year by some means. Whether it happens or not will be decided by the future progress of the economic situation, but for now, let us report on the contents of the preparations being made.
First, regarding capital: currently, Hwasin Department Store is a joint-stock company with 1 million won capital, Sŏn'il Chimul (鮮一紙物) is a company with 500,000 won, Hwasin Chain Store (和信連鎖店) joint-stock company has 2 million won capital, and Taedong Hŭngŏp (大同興業) has 2 million won. Summing these up (though not all fully paid-in), the nominal capital reaches 5.5 million won. To reach 10 million won, 4.5 million won is needed. This amount is planned to be raised through public offering widely in Chosŏn, Tokyo, Osaka, etc. If that<20> happens, it is anticipated that large shareholders might emerge from unexpected quarters, and the color of Hwasin's capital structure might change somewhat.
Consequently, since the capital color might not be as monolithic as in the past, some speculate that the presidency might change. However, according to our investigation, such a thing will not happen, and the Pak Hŭngsik empire will continue uninterruptedly (업시).

One Thousand Employees

Even in these recessionary times, Hwasin maintains an imposing appearance and attracts a large customer base. It is heard that the current number of employees in the four direct and affiliated companies and the main store exceeds 1,000. The department store restaurant alone has 60 female service girls (써-비스 껄). Furthermore, the department store's daily sales are reported to be around 10,050 won, totaling over 400,000 won per month.

How Much Does Pŏkbomaru Sell?

With full-page advertisements appearing daily in newspapers and large posters hung on streets, the name "Pŏkbomaru" (百補丸) has become known even to children and common folk. How much does it sell, and how profitable is it? Since Sinsŏngdang made big profits in recent years, general entrepreneurs have shown great interest in the patent medicine business, and many have invested in it. However, looking at the overall results, public opinion suggests that P'yŏnghwadang (平和堂) with "Pŏkbomaru" and Kŭmgang Pharmaceutical Company (金剛製藥所) with "Salvarsan" (사루발산) have been the most successful. We intend to investigate and report on the entire patent medicine industry another day. For now, let's look into "Pŏkbomaru," which holds the greatest public interest. How much is it selling? According to our investigation, it seems to be selling quite a lot. Its sales network covers not only all of Chosŏn but also extends to Manchukuo, China, Europe, and various other countries. It appears that monthly sales range from 5,000-6,000 to 10,000 packs, large and small combined (어울너). Sales figures vary depending on good and bad months, but the annual total seems to reach several hundred thousand won.
It is said that 200 employees, including pharmacists, are engaged in making Pŏkbomaru. Besides raw material costs, monthly expenses for advertising and personnel are estimated (헤인다니) at several tens of thousands of won, so it seems they have indeed established (벌려노흔) a considerable operation.<21>

A Three Hundred Thousand Won Department Store

At a time when large department stores built by Korean hands are emerging across Chosŏn—starting with Pak Hŭngsik's 500,000 won Hwasin in Seoul and Yi Kŭnmu's 200,000-plus won Muyŏngdang (茂英堂) in Taegu, speaking to the vitality of the financial world—in the major port of Ch'ŏngjin, which handles the goods of the vast East Manchuria (東滿) region and the Northern Hamgyŏng area (北關), O Kyusŏp's 100,000-plus won Marumi Department Store (丸見百貨店) appeared. Now, again, the Taedong Department Store (大同百貨店) is being established with a capital of 300,000 won. The president is Mr. Kim Choho (金凋鎬) (holding 1,600 out of 6,000 subscribed shares), a young merchant in his mid-30s. Besides him, Messrs. Pak Kŭnsil (朴根實), Chŏn Ch'ungyŏng (全春景), Yi Ŭngsil (李應實), Nam Yun'gu (南潤九), Kim Ponggu (金鳳九), and others are all expected to play active roles as major shareholders. Much activity is anticipated from them in the North Chosŏn (北鮮) and East Manchuria regions going forward.

Bank Presidents' Retirement Allowances: Four Hundred Thousand Won

How much severance pay did the Bank of Chosŏn give to former Governor Katō, who served for 10 years, and the Chosŏn Industrial Bank to former President Ariga, who served for 19 years? This has recently become a hot topic buzzed about (짓까리는) by the sparrows of Seoul's financial circles.
It has reportedly been decided: 250,000 won for Katō Keizaburō (加藤敬三郞) and 400,000 won for Ariga Mitsutoyo (有賀光豊).
A retirement allowance for one person being 400,000 won or 250,000 won is indeed an astronomical figure that makes our eyes pop. Consequently, the wallets of these two magnates must have undoubtedly swelled like rubber balloons.
However, it's not as if all this vast sum goes into their coffers. The sharp-eyed tax authorities are standing by with rolled-up sleeves. Retirement allowances are considered splendid income, and it's said they will levy at least a 20% tax on it.
Mr. Ariga is expected to send 80,000 won flying to the heavens (날너 보내리라), and Mr. Katō, 50,000 won.<22>
Notably, Mr. Ariga is said to have coolly donated about 100,000 won to various company projects under the pretext of commemorating his time in Chosŏn. Ultimately, the net amount entering his coffers is expected to be around two hundred-odd thousand won.

Rumor of Mr. Ch'oe X's Several Hundred Thousand Won Profit
- Cotton Cloth and Iron Goods Ride the Market Trend -

War inevitably brings great prosperity to some and great depression to others. As the Sino-Japanese Incident has now lasted for over half a year, the waves of the wartime economy have brought much rise and fall to businesses not only in Seoul but also in provincial cities.
Among these, the biggest fluctuations occurred in the stock market, as is commonly known. Generally speaking, outside the stock market, the cotton cloth and iron goods sectors saw significant profits (大利). Since prices for nearly all iron goods doubled, someone with 100,000 won worth of stock became wealthy with 200,000 won. Many ironmongers around Chongno made substantial money during this opportunity. Those in the cotton cloth business also saw many who profited greatly. Among them, the Chinese merchant "X-dŏk" (X德) specializing in cotton fabrics in Sup'yojŏng (水標町) reportedly made several hundred thousand won. One rumor says Mr. Ch'oe X of "Tong X-dŏk" (東X德) in Chongno 4-chōme also made huge profits. Besides (밧게) these, the Chōjiya-affiliated (丁子屋系) Sŏnman Chikmul (鮮滿織物) and many cotton cloth merchants in the Chongno market area profited significantly.

Palatial Bando Hotel

Anyone setting foot (듸려놋는) in Seoul should take a walk down Hwanggeumjŏng (黃金町) street. Walking a little from the Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural Office towards Hwanggeumjŏng street, one's eyes will be dazzled by the imposing 8-story white building soaring high. This building is the "Chosŏn Building" (朝鮮 삘딍). The entire 6th, 7th, and 8th floors of this building constitute the "Bando Hotel" (半島 호텔). At the 1st-floor entrance, the grand hall boasts marble pillars topped with blue tiles exuding an Oriental atmosphere, showcasing architectural beauty. With the lobby (롯비-) in the center, the cloakroom and front office are neatly arranged on either side, and a dedicated elevator provides direct access to the guest rooms on the 6th floor.
The 6th floor has 50 Western-style rooms. The 7th and 8th floors each have 50 Chinese-style dining rooms suitable for small gatherings, wedding halls, etc., now completed.<23>
The staff consists entirely of female 'boys' (女 뽀-이), and a female graduate from the "Tokyo Hotel School" (東京호텔학교) has been brought in as a supervisor, pouring all efforts into providing 100% service (써-비스).
Indoor heating and cooling systems are naturally provided, and dressing rooms, bathrooms, and all other amenities are fully equipped, ensuring not the slightest inconvenience (업도록).
In the summer, a "Soda Fountain" will open on the rooftop, making the hotel's rooftop the premier cool-down spot (納凉園) in Kyŏngsŏng.
The new life of shining tourist Chosŏn can truly be said to be starting its dynamic pulse with the emergence of the "Bando Hotel."
Furthermore, ahead of its opening, it has been fully booked for the wealthy guests of the "National Rotary Convention" in May, and applications have also come in from a group of over 100 members of the Japan-Manchuria Business Association, sounding a truly magnificent prelude.

Rumor of Historian Ch'oe Namsŏn Becoming University Professor

Historian Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn has recently resigned from his position as Councilor (參議) in the Central Advisory Council and is set to become a professor at Manchukuo's Kenkoku University (建國大學). His field is likely to be related to East Asian history.

Theater and Film World Inner Workings

Tonga Ilbo Spends 1,200 Won on Drama Contest

The drama contest hosted by the Tonga Ilbo Company last February, featuring the mobilization (출동식혀) of four troupes—the Dramatic Arts Research Society (劇藝術硏究會), Chungang Mudae (中央舞台), Hwarangwŏn (花郞苑), etc.—at the Kyŏngsŏng Pumingwan (京城府民館) for a drama competition, was the grandest spectacle in Chosŏn theater history and received universal acclaim (절찬을 삿거니와). Regarding the income at that time, it's heard that, first, the Tonga Ilbo Company gave each of the four troupes 300 won as preparation funds when mobilizing them (식힐적에), totaling 1,200 won. Later, an additional 200 won was provided (주엇슴으로)<24>, making the total expenditure 1,400 won. However, the admission fees collected over the two days amounted to roughly the same, so the newspaper company itself essentially broke even. The audience count at that time reportedly reached 3,000-4,000 people.

Literary Masterpiece "Mujŏng" Made into Film

"Mujŏng" (無情), penned by the masterful brush of the great writer Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu), which drew tears from the talented young men and women of a generation, is now set to be filmed as the first production by Chosŏn Film Co., Ltd. (朝鮮映畵株式會社). The protagonist Yŏngch'ae will be played by Han Ŭnjin, and Yi Hyŏngsik by Mr. Kim Ch'igŭn. Notably, female writer Ms. Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi will also appear. With a total production cost of 20,000 won for a talkie (토-키), this rare literary epic, directed by Mr. Pak Kich'ae, is expected to be released (封切될 모양이라고) in May.

"The Good Earth" Draws 50,000 Viewers in Seoul

Pearl Buck's (바알·팍) novel adapted into the film "The Good Earth" is truly a world-class masterpiece, its excellent content needing no further mention. Moreover, the timing was during the Incident (事變), and the place was Seoul, filled with Koreans interested in the China issue. Consequently, when it was screened at Seoul's Meijiza (明治座), despite running three times daily (morning, afternoon, night) for two consecutive weeks, it was always packed beyond capacity (立錐의 여지 업시), and the total cumulative (延) audience truly surpassed 50,000.

The theater rented it from the distributor for 3,000 won for one week.
Even more expensive was Chaplin's "City Lights" (거리의 등불), screened some years ago, which cost 4,500 won. The famous Japan-Germany friendship film "Atarashiki Tsuchi" (新らしき土 - The New Earth), shown at Wakakusa Theater, cost 4,200 won.
More recently, "Nagŭnae" (나그내 - The Wanderer), starring Mun Yebong, was also in the 4,000 won range.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의 少女) was 2,500 won.
"The Road to Peace in the East" (東洋 平和의 길) was 4,000 won.
"Donzoko" (とん底 - The Lower Depths)
"Wakakusa Monogatari" (若草物語 - Little Women)
"Taikongōzanfu" (大金剛山譜 - Great Diamond Mountain Suite), starring Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, were all in the 3,000 to 4,000 won class.
Considering these are just the rental fees for a one-week screening, one can easily imagine how large the income and audience numbers must have been (만헛슬가) for the theaters showing these films.

Hwarangwŏn's Sunday Income: 400 Won

The Hwarangwŏn (花郞苑) New Drama Troupe, currently performing at Tansŏngsa Theater in the city, led by playwright Mr. Song Yŏng and featuring several excellent actors, is attracting (끄을고) popularity in the capital. 長安人物 集散記

朴泳孝 候 = (中樞院 副議長) 頭山滿 아들이 요전 중풍을 알어 臥席중의 候를 차저와 감개 무량하여 枕頭에서 울엇다 한다. 候의 병환은 不輕하야 벌서 數朔 臥席중.
朴榮喆씨 = (滿洲國 名譽 總領事) 北京을 비롯하야 蒙疆 정부를 약 2주간 방문하고 왓는데 이번 여행은 滿洲國으로온 機密費 속으로 가섯다 오섯다고.
韓相龍씨 = (關東軍 最高 顧問) 尹德榮 후작과 함께 北京 갓슬때에 要路 政客은 다 맛낫스나 大統領說 잇는 吳佩孚만은 못 맛낫다고 드른 즉 吳씨는 외국 租界에 이주치 안키와 外人 면접 안는 점으로 유명하다고.
尹致昊씨 = 청년 시대에는 史學을 만히 탐독하시더니 요지음은 별로 독서도 안 하시고 翠雲亭에 각금 曳杖, 賞春而歸 하신다.
許憲씨 = (大同出版社 專務) 이번 特赦에도 복권되지 못하신 氏는 黙黙 不言而 오직 大同 컨체른의 일원으로서 朝夕 사무에 精勵하신다. 따님 貞淑 여사의 소식 아직도 알 길 업다고.
金明植씨 = 赤貧의 老論客, 귀가 어두어 필담으로 事를 辦한다. 輱軻不遇의 孤影肅然한 그 몸을 각금 鍾路<13> 바닥에 나타내여 왕래하심 보인다.
崔麟씨 = (時中會 首領) 1백만원의 큰 조직을 이루는 모 신문사장으로서 취임설이 들닌다. 物望과 時勢 - 아울너 絶好의 秋기로 一世는 氏의 동향을 주시한다.
金性洙씨 = (普專 校長) 東亞日報에는 일절 발을 끈코, 이제는 大早稻田을 만들든 大隈伯 모양으로 일심정력을 傾注하야 大普成의 완성에 주야 勞心한다. 요는 「사립 대학」소리를 먼저 칠 자 金性洙냐, 李鍾萬이냐, 또는 元漢慶이냐 하고 세인은 주시하고 있다.
崔奎東씨 = (中東 學校長) 積年의 喘息으로 苦狀하신다. 장장 30년 그 사이에 수만 청년을 길너 내시기에 심신 極疲 하신 양. 大中東 建設을 위하여 天은 氏에게 긴 壽를 내리기를 바란다.
兪億兼씨 = (延喜 專門 副校長) 저녁 6시 쯤 新村서 오는 석양 차가 드러올 임박하여 南大門 역 푸랫트폼에 나서면 건강한 혈색 조흐신 氏를 마질수 잇다. 엇든 때는 학생 틈에 어울니어 엇든 때는 梨專 女敎員團 속에 끼어 늘 청년다운 기개를 일치안코 게시다.
李鍾萬씨 = (大同 鑛業 會社長) 崇專의 終焉과 大同 工專의 개막이란 역사적 순간에 잇서 光芒을 발하든 氏, 이제는 사회에 공약한 諸 사업의 완성을 위하야 一路邁進한다고- 요지음도 광부복 입고 長津鑛山으로 자조 내왕.
安在鴻씨 = (前 朝鮮日報社長) 初春의 어느 날 황혼, 朝鮮銀行 광장 압헤서 망연히 서 잇는 孤影을 보다. 胸裡에 만감이 交集할 분이 엇지 民世 한 분 뿐이리요만은, 氏에게는 일층 그러할 듯, 고요히 서재에 안저 묵상과 看書로 渡日 중.
朴仁德 여사 = 米洲 갓다온 뒤 농촌사업한단 말도 들니고, 모 학교 교수된단 말도 들니고, 잡지사 한다는 말도 들니더니 이내 다시 沈潛, 소식업다. 아마 春光 무르녹을 임박에는 태평양 건너올 때 생각햇든 사업 풀랜에 매진 하실 듯.

和信 四百萬圓 公募說

朴興植씨가 사장이 되여 경영하는 和信이 그동안 직계 방계를 모다 합처 한 덩어리의 큰 회사로 개조하고 자본금도 1천만원으로 증자한다는 설이 잇더니 작년 7월의 日支事變 돌발로 모든 것이 臨時資金調停法에 걸니어 보류되여 왓다 한다. 그러든 것이 최근 일설에 의하면 무슨 방법으로든지 금년 중으로는 예정대로 1천만원 회사를 만들 작정이라 한다, 되고 안 되고는 금후 경제계의 진전 여하에 따라 결정될 일이나 爲先 우리는 준비하고 잇다는 그 내용이나 보도키로 하자.
첫재 자본에 잇서 현재 和信百貨店이 1백만원의 주식회사요, 鮮一紙物이 50만원의 회사요, 和信連鎖店주식회사가 2백만원 자본이요, 大同興業이 2백만원으로 총괄하면 (물론 全額拂込은 아니지만) 公稱 자본 550만원에 달하는 바, 1천만원을 만들자면 450만원이나 부족한데 이것은 널니 조선과 東京, 大阪 등지에서 공모하리라 한다. 그러케<20> 되는 날이면 큰 株主가 의외의 방면에서 튀어나올 것은 예상되야 금후 和信의 자본 계열의 색채가 다소 달너질 것 갓다 한다.
그래서 자본 색채가 과거와 가치 단일치 못하니만치 혹은 일부에서는 사장도 변경되리란 말이 잇으나, 본사 探報에 의하면 그런 일은 업시 어디까지든지 朴興植 천하가 계속될 것이라 한다.

百補丸은 얼마나 팔니는가

날마다 신문지상에 全面廣告가 나고 길가에 나서면 포스타가 크게 걸녀, 엇잿든 그 일홈을 兒童走卒이라도 다 알게 된 「百補丸」은 얼마나 팔려, 얼마나한 자미를 보고잇는가, 최근수년이래 神聖堂이 큰 數를 본뒤부터 일반기업가들이 賣藥業에 대하여 만흔 흥미를 가지고 거기 투자하는 사람들이 만햇스나 총결산하여 보면 一般世評에는「百補丸」의 平和堂과 「사루발산」의 金剛製藥所가 가장 성공하엿다고 하는데 賣藥業 전체에 대하여는 다른날 일괄하여 조사보도코저 하거니와, 그러면 爲先 세인이 가장 크게 흥미를 갓고잇는 「百補丸」은 얼마나 팔니고 잇는가. 탐사한 바에 의하면 무척 만히 팔니는 모양으로 그 판매망은 全鮮은 물론이요, 滿洲國, 支那, 歐洲, 여러 나라에 수출되는 형편이며, 1개월에 대소 어울너 5,6천에서 1만 포 정도는 나가는 눈치며 판매고도 잘 팔니는 달과 못 팔니는 달에 의하여, 다르지만 1년 여러 십만원이 되는 듯하다.
百補丸 만들기에 종사하는 약제사 이하 종업원이 200명 이라든가, 그리고 原料代 外에 선전비 인건비 등 한 달 잡고 수3만원을 헤인다니, 상당히 벌려노흔 것은 사실인 듯.<21>

三十萬圓의 百貨店

서울에 朴興植씨의 50만원의 和信, 大邱에 李根茂씨의 20여만원의 茂英堂을 비롯하야 조선 각지에 조선 사람 손으로 큰 백화점이 여러 개가 이러나고 잇서 재계의 활기를 말하고 잇는 이 지금, 東滿의 광대한 지역을 끼고 北關의 물화를 呑吐하는 巨港 淸津에 10여만원의 吳圭燮씨 경영의 丸見百貨店이 나타나더니 또 다시 이제 자본금 30만원의 大同百貨店이 창설되는데 사장은 金凋鎬씨(引受株 6천株 중 1천6백주)로 년 34,5의 청년 상업가요, 그 외에 朴根實, 全春景, 李應實, 南潤九, 金鳳九 등 諸氏가 모다 중요 株主로 활약하리라는데 압으로 北鮮과 東滿 일대에서 만흔 활약이 기대된다고 한다.

銀行長 退職金 四十萬圓

朝鮮銀行은 재직 10년의 加藤 前총재에게, 또 殖産銀行은 재직 19년의 有賀 前頭取에게 과연 얼마나한 위로금을 주엇느냐? 함이 요사히 서울 안 재계 참새떼들이 한갈갓치 짓까리는 好話題로 되여잇다.
그런데 加藤敬三郞씨 25만원 有賀光豊씨 40만원으로 결정 되엿다고-.
