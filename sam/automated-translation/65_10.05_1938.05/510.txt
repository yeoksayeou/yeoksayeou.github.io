The Tomb of Wang Zhaojun

The tomb of Wang Zhaojun (王昭君), familiar to our ears, this tragic grave has recently emerged in North China (北支). Sacrificed in the conflict between the Han and the Mongols (漢蒙 항쟁), she was sent far away to a foreign land of endless yellow sands (黃沙 만리). Quietly shedding red tears (紅淚), she entrusted her long song of lament (長恨歌) solely to the pipa she cherished, fading away like dew in the land of the Hu (胡國). The tomb stands where the beautiful lady Wang Zhaojun is said to have taken her own life.

Outside Guihua City (歸化城) (present-day Hohhot (豪和厚特)), through how many years (幾星霜) of bitter winds and sorrowful rains (慘風悲雨), the tragic tomb stands merely in the middle of a desolate plain, still holding resentment amidst the bleak northern wind (蕭條한 삭풍). The Tang dynasty poet Chang Chien (常建) composed a poem about this tomb, lamenting the "dark wind of the North Sea (北海의 陰風)," and his verses, sorrowful and touching, draw tears from those who see it.<274>

**Summary**

This short article from 1938 reports on the location of the tomb of Wang Zhaojun, a famed Han dynasty beauty sent to marry a Xiongnu ruler, situated near Hohhot (Guihua City) in North China. It briefly recounts her tragic story as a victim of Han-Xiongnu relations, highlighting her sorrow and connection to the pipa, and mentions a mournful poem by the Tang poet Chang Jian about the site. For historians, the article's focus on a historical site in North China ("北支") reflects contemporary Korean awareness and interest, likely shaped by Japan's expansionism in the region during that period. The depiction of Wang Zhaojun as a tragic figure sent to a foreign land might have implicitly resonated with Korean readers experiencing colonial rule and displacement.

王昭君의 墓
우리들 귀에 익든 王昭君의 무덤, 이 비극의 무덤이 이번 北支에서 나젓다. 저 漢蒙 항쟁의 희생이 되어 멀니 黃沙 만리의 이역에 보내지어 紅淚 잠잠하게도 한갓 愛玩하는 비파에 긴 長恨歌를 托하여, 胡國의 이슬로 사라지든 미희 王昭君이 자살한 곳에 지터잇다. 歸化城(지금의 豪和厚特) 외 慘風悲雨 幾星霜 단지 적막한 평원가운데 지터잇는 비극의 무덤, 아직도 蕭條한 삭풍에 한을 품고 잇다. 당나라 시인 常建이 이 묘에 賦하어 「北海의 陰風」운운하여 그 詩歌 애절하여 보는 사람의 눈물 자아낸다.<274>
