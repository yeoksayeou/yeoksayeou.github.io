Magazine Title	삼천리 제10권 제5호
Publication Date	1938년 05월 01일
Article Title	艶書事件 (Love Letter Incident)
Author	李瑞求
Article Type	소설 (Novel/Fiction)

Love Letter Incident
						Yi Sŏgu
1. Morning Fog on the Han River

It was late spring, with willow branches swaying lightly in the gentle breeze by the Nodol riverside, and peach blossom petals fluttering like butterflies from somewhere. The Han River, shrouded in morning fog, was as soft and beautiful as a painting seen in a dream. Fishermen (漁翁), who had been casting their lines since dawn to catch sea fish coming up with the morning tide, were scattered sparsely like milestones. Indeed, it was a peaceful morning that made one want to hum a song of peace. Past Sŏbinggo, there is a tributary stream, and along its bank, willows droop, planted by someone unknown. It's a small piece of land rarely visited by people, except for engineer corps (工兵) soldiers from the Yongsan barracks who occasionally come for training on an iron boat (鐵船). On the branches of these drowsily lined willows, someone had tied a rope, and following the rope down to the riverbank, an elegant pleasure boat (遊船) was moored. Judging by the oars laid across it, there seemed to be no boatman. Peeking inside the boat, the windows on both sides were covered only by green curtains (가-덴) in the glass panes, making it hushed like a forbidden secret chamber. Judging by the boat's adornment,<187> it was a luxurious vessel worthy of being called the queen of the Han River. Who on earth would leave such a fine boat tied up here as if abandoned? The tributary stream's current is strong, making it difficult even for a top-class boatman to navigate such a boat safely down to Nodol. Why, then, was this fine boat left moored here overnight? Indeed, who was inside the green curtains? If a boatman wouldn't sleep in such a fine vessel, then who could be inside? A man? A woman? Or perhaps a man and a woman together? In any case, if someone was inside this boat, it was clear they must possess the status and wealth to afford such a luxurious vessel.
So, with spring not yet over and the night and dawn still chilly, what possessed them to spend an untimely night on the river in such a secluded boat? The curiosity was endless, the suspicion boundless.

2. A Rare Scene Inside the Boat

There's a proverb: "A green silk wrapper on dog dung." It means dog dung should just be thrown into the manure pit; wrapping it in blue silk serves no purpose and doesn't suit its station. Similarly, a private car for a day laborer would be like a green silk wrapper on dog dung, and imported cigars or fine cigarettes for a farmer driving an ox and plowing a field would also be like a green silk wrapper on dog dung. Likewise, the rare scene unfolding inside this boat is presented here as one of countless examples of a green silk wrapper on dog dung. His appearance—a worn-out Western suit with the cuffs of his time-stained white shirt (와이샤쓰) torn and flapping outside the jacket sleeves, and patches sewn onto the seat of his trousers—marked him as nothing more than a bum (룸폔) fit for picking up cigarette butts in Pagoda Park. Yet, wasn't he sitting wrapped in a silk quilt with feather stuffing on a silk mattress (보료)? Was that all? In his hand, he held ten ten-wŏn banknotes. Even stranger was the sight of the man himself, sitting on the silk mat holding a hundred wŏn, looking bewildered, as if he couldn't comprehend how his fate had taken such a sharp turn. He released the banknotes (지전) from his left hand and unfolded the letter he held in his right. It was a letter typed in Korean script (언문) on a typewriter (타이프).
"I am grateful for the happiness of spending an unexpected night pleasantly with you. Now,<188> I only wish for you to kindly erase last night's scene from your history. It is not much money, but I enclose it. Please accept this money without offense, as a fine for not being able to tell you my address, name, and identity. I am just a woman like the wind, who came from beyond the sky. Please consider this letter delivered from an observatory (천문대) and cheerfully forget the dreamlike night's history." Naturally, there was no signature. It was the first time he had read a letter delivered from an observatory. A dreamlike night spent playing joyfully with a beautiful woman who came from the sky and returned to the sky—and now, a hundred wŏn in cash held in his hand? It was too absurd to be real, yet too realistic to be a dream. Last night, he had clearly ridden in a car towards Tongdaemun, but when he opened his eyes this morning, he found himself in a boat floating on the Han River. What on earth had happened? The more he thought, the dizzier his head became. In any case, in his impoverished state where even seeing a one-wŏn bill was cause for bowing, a hundred wŏn was an incredible sum. He raised his hand to his nose. From the hand that had caressed her in bed last night, her fragrance still seemed to emanate faintly.
"Ah, 참, last night was good. Really good!"
A sigh mixed with a soliloquy escaped him.
"It can't be. I must be dreaming right now. Sometimes, even when dreaming, you think it's not a dream."
As if to ascertain his own existence clearly, he opened the small window and looked out at the world. The antenna rising high behind the Government-General's Residence (총독관저)! Log rafts flowing down through the night! Schools of fish swimming in the water. Across the river, in the fishing village by the dark rocks, morning smoke was already rising like fog from every chimney.
"You pretended you were the only one, but who was that you came to the theater with? How could you feign ignorance like that?"
Feeling utterly 답답 (frustrated/stifled), he sang the song he habitually hummed whenever he sat on a park bench. It was definitely reality. Ultimately, accepting it as reality, the events he experienced from last night to this morning seemed too much like a dream. It was because he had experienced something utterly impossible. As he stared blankly at the river,<189> a small boat approached from the direction of the dark rocks, rowing upstream at full speed. Its target was clearly the luxurious boat Kŭnsik was on.
"Oh—now maybe I'll get to solve the riddle."
Kŭnsik leaned out, waiting eagerly for the boat to arrive.

3. The Luxurious Boat Without a Boatman

The boatman had a reddish-dark face with a fluttering white beard, and his two eyes, seemingly deeper than the water itself, shone like stars. It was the face one could only see on a boatman born of the water, raised by the water, and grown old on the water. The oar held in his two hands swung leisurely from side to side, yet the boat approached like a swift arrow.
"Is this the boat without a boatman?"
A booming voice that had battled the sounds of water and wind. Before the words even finished, the boat had already entered the shade of the willows.
"Yes, it is. What on earth is going on?"
Hoping for some miraculous answer, Kŭnsik waited for the boatman's tightly sealed lips to open.
Old Man: "Let's just go down to Nodol."
He urged Kŭnsik to stop chatting and transfer to the small boat.
Kŭnsik: "Well, I'll go, but who are you, old man? And why am I here?"
The boatman smiled faintly.
Old Man: "Why are you acting like this? You know everything and yet you mock me. Anyway, I have a request I received, so I will just do as requested. Now—please get in quickly."
Kŭnsik: "Whose boat is this anyway?"
Old Man: "It belongs to Master Sŏbangnim from Minister Min's household. It's not yet time for pleasure boating, so it was entrusted to the boatmen, and we occasionally lend it to guests."
Kŭnsik: "Whose instructions did you follow to come and get me?"
Old Man: "Ah—you really don't know?"
Kŭnsik: "I told you I don't know."
Old Man: "Ah—is that really true?"
Kŭnsik: "Just answer my questions quickly."
Old Man: "Oh, what is this? Have I been tricked by a goblin (독갑이)?"
Kŭnsik: "What—a goblin?"
Indeed, Kŭnsik too began to wonder if he had been<190> tricked by a goblin, a chill running down his spine. So, this boatman, like him, probably didn't know the situation either and had just come for the money. Anyway, Kŭnsik transferred to the small boat.
Old Man: "It's too elaborate for a goblin's prank. Ah, the strange things that happen in this world."
Kŭnsik: "Ah—I'm so frustrated. Please tell me the story of how you got here."
Old Man: "Yes. Then I shall tell you."
The boatman began his story as the boat drifted downstream with the current.
"It was this morning. I was fishing by the riverbank when a woman, perhaps in her forties, approached me. She immediately asked if I wanted to earn some money. Wasn't that like hearing talk of an exorcism (굿) when you're a shaman? My ears perked up right away. She told me that once the sun rose, I should immediately take my boat to the willow grove by the tributary stream, where I would find a boat without a boatman and a gentleman inside alone. She asked if I would row him to the main road at Nodol. Hearing it, it didn't seem like a difficult task, and she offered ten wŏn as payment, so who would refuse? That's why I came."
A woman in her forties—it must have been the woman who took him last night. What kind of prank was this? Who was that woman, and what was the identity of the beautiful lady? Where did all the money they spent like water come from? Why, of all people, were they playing this kind of prank on him, a prank he couldn't even get angry about? They fed him well, entertained him well, let him sleep well, and then gave him a hundred wŏn in cash. Human desire knows no bounds; now, Kŭnsik longed desperately for the beautiful woman from that one night's pleasant dream—the one with fair skin (살빗), captivating eyes, and red lips.
Kŭnsik: "So, besides that woman, did another, younger woman come with her?"
Old Man: "No. I don't know who she was, but someone was looking down from the sacred tree grove by the Han River."
Kŭnsik: "Was it a woman?"
Old Man: "She wore a veil over her face."
Kŭnsik: "Right. That must have been her. Western women's hats often have veils."
Old Man: "Anyway, even from afar, the face visible through the veil looked very pale."
Kŭnsik: "So where did she go?"
Old Man: "Who knows? After firmly giving me the instructions, she just went up the bank."<191>
Before long, the boat had drifted down to the front of the Indogyo Bridge.

4. The Woman in Mourning Clothes

Recalling the fleeting sweet dream of one night, Kŭnsik stepped onto the Han River's Indogyo Bridge. He took out the banknotes (지전) stuffed in his pocket once more—they were definitely Bank of Chosŏn notes. His mind and body were extremely fatigued from playing and drinking in the bedroom draped with pink curtains all night.
"Ah, I feel dizzy."
Leaning against the railing of the Indogyo Bridge, he looked up at the yellowish, shimmering sky. His whole body felt like a deflated ball, as if it might blow away in the wind.
His throat was dry, his head was spinning, his joints ached, and he felt unbearably nauseous.
The beautiful woman who came from the sky and went back to the sky, the letter supposedly delivered from an observatory, the ten crisp ten-wŏn banknotes.
Unforgettable memories. A joyful night unlikely to happen twice in Kŭnsik's entire life—he looked up high into the sky, searching for traces of the person who had returned to the heavens. A childish hope that she might wave from beyond the sky overwhelmed Kŭnsik's heart.
Ah—I need to lie down somewhere and think slowly.
He had a hundred wŏn now. Kŭnsik immediately boarded a streetcar, got off at T'aep'yŏngt'ong, and went into a Chinese bathhouse. This seemed like the only place where he could relax his body in hot water, soothe his insides with hot food, and take a nap on a bed. Compared to his bum's life until yesterday, the bathhouse bed, however shabby, felt incredibly luxurious. The taste of sipping the hot soup (훈탕 국물) sprinkled with pepper and chili powder felt wonderfully satisfying. Wrapping his body, which had experienced luxury for the first time since birth, in a white towel, he jumped into the grimy and concave cement bathtub. He submerged his body, which held a scent too precious to wash away, into the water from which hot steam rose like smoke. Closing his eyes slowly, he sat there as if dead, systematically replaying the events of the previous night.
Last night, he unexpectedly met a friend from the countryside and enjoyed a proper dinner for the first time in ages at a department store restaurant. His stomach was full. The weather was lovely.<192> Spring songs everywhere.
"Spring tunes," swept along by the thronging citizens, he had wandered as far as Changch'ungdan Park. While strolling here and there, his body became extremely tired.
"I'll just sleep here today."
He lay down on a secluded bench (뻰취) and fell asleep right there. Before he knew it, the world fell silent, with only the occasional sound of car horns heading towards the Han River audible. Although he had no watch, it must have been past one in the morning.
"Excuse me. Excuse me."
Shaken awake, he opened his eyes to see a woman neatly dressed in Korean clothes. It was dark, and she wore dark glasses, so he couldn't make out her face, but her figure in mourning clothes (素服) was elegant.
Woman in Mourning: "Don't sleep here; let's go to my house."
Kŭnsik: "Go to your house? Who are you to take me?"
Woman in Mourning: "Look, don't be so startled, just follow me."
Kŭnsik: "But where are we going and why? You have to tell me the reason, don't you?"
The woman sat down on the bench and began her persuasion.
Woman in Mourning: "Do you have any money?"
Kŭnsik: "What money?"
Woman in Mourning: "Do you have any enemies?"
Kŭnsik: "No."
Woman in Mourning: "Then what worries do you have? No money means no fear of thieves, no enemies means no fear for your life. What's stopping you from going anywhere? You're just making a fuss for no reason."
Thinking about it, it was true. Though he had a large frame and strong fists, he had never once gotten into a fight, so he had no grudges against anyone. As for possessions, he had nothing but the worn-out suit on his back. Oh well, what was there to hesitate about or be suspicious of?
Kŭnsik: "What great fortune will come my way if I follow you?"
Woman in Mourning: "Ho ho... Of course, something great! There's a lovely young lady, delicious food, and on top of that, if you listen well, you'll surely get a handful of ten-wŏn notes as a reward."
Kŭnsik: "That sounds good. Alright. Let's go then. I have nothing to lose."<193>
As Kŭnsik tightened his belt, his physique, radiating concrete-like health and leopard-like dignity, appeared quite manly, even for a bum. The woman, watching him in the faint electric light filtering from afar, smiled contentedly.
Woman in Mourning: "Oh my, how old are you?"
Kŭnsik: "About thirty."
Woman in Mourning: "My, you have such a fine build."
Kŭnsik: "Ah—did you come here to buy cattle?"
Woman in Mourning: "Ho ho... Now then, you must do exactly as I say."
Kŭnsik: "Anything. Just don't kill me, do as you please. Even if I'm bewitched by a goblin, it's better than the fate of sleeping curled up like a shrimp in the cold dew on a park bench."
Woman in Mourning: "Alright—let's get up then."
Kŭnsik: "Let's go. I have nothing to lose. What trouble could I possibly get into?"
The two walked, one ahead, one behind, to the streetcar track in front of Pakmunsa Temple. Kŭnsik became curious about the face of the woman in mourning clothes.
Kŭnsik: "Ah—please take off your glasses. Aren't they uncomfortable?"
Woman in Mourning: "My lady values secrecy, so when escorting a guest, I must wear these glasses."
Kŭnsik: "This is just like a spy novel."
Woman in Mourning: "Isn't it fun? Ho ho ho ho."
Kŭnsik: "It's so much fun I'm starting to tremble."
Woman in Mourning: "Don't worry. A real man should have courage."
She grabbed Kŭnsik's wrist and hailed a passing car.
Woman in Mourning: "Let's go outside Tongdaemun."
The car silently drove along the Ch'ŏngnyangni line. Passing Ch'ŏngnyangni Station, they reached a secluded road near Chegiri.
Woman in Mourning: "Stop here."
A command to halt. The car stopped instantly. The woman paid the fare.
Woman in Mourning: "Alright—let's get out."
Kŭnsik had no choice but to follow her out. While the car backed up (빡크), they walked forward about ten steps.
Kŭnsik: "What are we doing getting out of the car in the dark?"
Woman in Mourning: "Look, don't worry, just follow me."
The woman in mourning clothes walked ahead. Kŭnsik had no choice but to trail behind.<194> Turning around a small pine grove, they found a motorcycle with a sidecar (자동자 행거) parked by the roadside. A young man in a black stand-up collar (쓰메에리) chauffeur's uniform stood up from the sidecar (폭쓰) beside the motorcycle.
Chauffeur: "Welcome."
He too wore glasses with dark lenses.
Woman in Mourning: "Alright—let's take him quickly."
Chauffeur: "Okay, please get in here."
Kŭnsik: "We're riding again?"
Woman in Mourning: "Look, stop talking nonsense and get in. There are three of us going, what's there to worry about?"
Kŭnsik: "Whatever you say."
Kŭnsik settled into the attached sidecar (폭쓰). The chauffeur took a black cloth from his pocket.
Chauffeur: "Now—please cover your eyes with this cloth."
Kŭnsik: "Why, are you going to execute me by firing squad?"
Chauffeur: "Look, don't worry about anything."
After Kŭnsik's eyes were securely blindfolded with the black cloth, the motorcycle carrying the party of three drove around for about 30 minutes, turning this way and that, bending here and there, seemingly wandering aimlessly. Perhaps they were deliberately trying to confuse his sense of direction by meandering all over. Finally, the motorcycle stopped. A woman's voice from inside:
Beautiful Woman: "You're quite late today."
Woman in Mourning: "My apologies. Instead, I've brought a very fine guest."
Beautiful Woman: "Alright—please come up here."
A warm hand grasped the arm of the blindfolded Kŭnsik. Inside the gate, the lights were bright, illuminating even the inside of the black cloth. Ascending the steps, passing through a corridor, and opening a door:
Beautiful Woman: "Please sit here."
Kŭnsik sat down heavily where guided. The plush spring of the chair sank deeply, startling Kŭnsik.
Kŭnsik: "Aigumeoni!"
Beautiful Woman: "Oh, I'm sorry. We tied the blindfold so tightly." An sleeve exuding a faint fragrance brushed against his face (낫), and a soft hand untied the cloth.

5. From Hell to Heaven

As soon as Kŭnsik opened his eyes, he instantly lost his senses. This must be the expression of someone ascended from hell to heaven. Mouth agape, eyes wide open and unblinking, he looked around. The bedroom was surrounded by pale pink silk curtains (카-덴). On the ceiling,<195> there was a painting of nude goddesses (라톄의 녀신) frolicking in the clouds. To the left was a double bed (따불뻿트), with a table at the head, upon which red and yellow flowers were abundantly arranged. Seated on the sofa (쏘프아) he occupied, a lovely young lady, appearing to be in her early twenties, dressed in a jade-green Western silk negligee, sat closely beside him, gazing at him. On the table were two or three bottles of Western liquor and simple snacks to accompany the drinks. Was someone else about to enter? A cautious knock sounded.
Beautiful Woman: "Who is it? Come in."
A young girl, looking about fifteen or sixteen, entered. She wore a yellow top (저고리), a pink skirt (치마), and a white apron (행주치마). She carried two cups of tea on a silver tray.
Girl: "I brought the tea."
Beautiful Woman: "Oh, leave it there and go."
The girl respectfully placed the teacups on the table and withdrew like vanishing mist.
Kŭnsik: "Who on earth are you?"
Beautiful Woman: "Guests who come to this house are absolutely forbidden to ask questions. Now—please have some tea."
A delicate hand, adorned with sparkling jeweled rings, lifted a cup and offered it to him.
Kŭnsik: "Yes, yes, thank you."
Accepting the teacup, Kŭnsik finally looked directly at her face. Her skin was like milk, her eyes held a subtle charm, and her lips were stained with rouge. Kŭnsik concluded that this must surely be the prank of someone with deviant sexual desires. The prank of a wealthy madam. A prankster who picks up young men wandering the streets and enjoys them for a night. Whatever it was, fine. Having ascended from hell to heaven, let things happen as they may. Kŭnsik abandoned his hesitant attitude and drank the tea readily.
Kŭnsik: "The tea tastes excellent."
Beautiful Woman: "Ho ho... Perhaps I should have made it stronger—shall I add some whiskey?"
Kŭnsik: "No—it's fine as it is."
Beautiful Woman: "Wouldn't you like to bathe and change your clothes?"
Kŭnsik: "Whatever you wish."
Once a man makes up his mind, there's no room for hesitation.
Beautiful Woman: "Then let's go to the bathroom. A negligee (침의) should be prepared there."
Kŭnsik: "Do as you please. I'm like a bride taken to a temple; what choice do I have?"
Beautiful Woman: "My, how refreshing you are."
Kŭnsik: "Just don't kill me."
Beautiful Woman: "I'll tease you all night long."<196>
(Seductive charm)
Kŭnsik: "Do as you wish."
(A night of glory)
Beautiful Woman: "Alright—let's go then."
The beautiful woman led the way. Kŭnsik immediately followed. The bathroom was just to the left after exiting the corridor. Clear water was already emitting white steam in the porcelain bathtub (사긔 욕조), and under the bright light, a towel and soap were neatly placed beside the tub. Kŭnsik undressed and entered the bath. The beautiful woman folded his discarded old clothes neatly, wrapped them in a cloth, took them away, and brought back a bright red silk man's gown (싸운) instead.
Beautiful Woman: "There's a safety razor (안전 면도) here, please shave."
Kŭnsik: "My beard hasn't grown much, do I need to shave again?"
Beautiful Woman: "I don't like it. Please shave."
Kŭnsik: "Whatever you say."
Kŭnsik experienced domestic bliss for the first time in his life. It felt like the joy of being a millionaire returning home to a waiting, beloved, beautiful wife and taking a bath. After finishing his bath, he immediately went into the bedroom, drank four or five cups (사오배) of the Western liquor offered by the beautiful woman, and clearly fell asleep in the bed with her—and yet—when he opened his eyes, somehow, he found himself wrapped in a quilt, lying in a boat on the Han River.
Kŭnsik: "Hmm. There must have been some anesthetic or sleeping drug in that liquor..."
Lying immersed in the dirty public bath (청탕), incomparable to last night's bathroom, Kŭnsik sighed. It was the sigh of someone cast out from heaven back into hell.

6. Two People, One Role in a Secret Room

After this incident, Kŭnsik wandered the main road outside Tongdaemun almost like a man who had lost his mind. The unforgettable sweet dream of one night—he was searching for the place where that dream occurred. He went to inquire about the owner/captain (선즉이) of the luxurious boat he had been on at the Han River, but couldn't find any leads. He searched thoroughly on both sides of the road from outside Tongdaemun all the way to Manguri Pass, but there was no trace of her. He only heard from an innkeeper that a motorcycle with a sidecar (자동자 행거) carrying a woman sometimes passed by, but being no detective (명정탕 - likely typo for 명탐정), he couldn't make much of it. Before long, the news of flowers subsided, and the season for water activities was in full swing. It was the night of a day when the scorching sun beat down like murderous rays, and horses pulling carts collapsed on the road. Suddenly:
"Extra! Extra!" (호외... 호외)
The sound of bells startled the citizens strolling<192> the streets. Kŭnsik, coming out of the restroom at Hwasin Department Store, also obtained a copy of the extra.
Murder Drama of a Beautiful Face
Under this headline, the following article was printed:
An attempted murder occurred at a newly built Western-style house behind Anjŏngsa Temple in district OO within the city (부내). The assailant is Kim Milye (41), a wealthy widow recently returned from America, and the victim is Kim Okkyŏng (22), a modern beauty who served as her secretary. The motive for the attempted murder lies in a love triangle conflict, as Kim Okkyŏng adored Kim Milye's lover. What intrigues listeners is that when Kim Milye returned from America, she commissioned a famous doll maker to create a beautiful rubber mask/face (고무 인면) tailored to her own face. Upon returning to Kyŏngsŏng, she searched widely through department stores and gisaeng houses (권번) for a woman whose features exactly matched the rubber face. The beauty who met this requirement was Kim Okkyŏng, who was nearly killed by Kim Milye's gunshot. After hiring Okkyŏng for a 1,000-wŏn deposit and 600 wŏn per month, Milye forced her into bizarre activities each night. Kim Milye, who enjoyed debauched nightly pleasures, would go into the city, lure robust young men, and have Kim Okkyŏng entertain them. When the men became infatuated with Okkyŏng's beauty and were enjoying themselves, Milye would ply them with strong liquor to cloud their minds. Then, Milye, wearing the rubber face, would take Okkyŏng's place and indulge herself with the young men. Recently, however, Okkyŏng fell for a certain young man and was suspected of revealing Milye's secrets, ultimately leading Milye to fire the pistol she had hidden, nearly killing her.
Holding the extra edition, Kŭnsik smiled faintly, as if he finally understood.
Everyone, don't you wish you could receive a letter from an observatory?<193>

**Summary**

A destitute man named Kŭnsik is mysteriously picked up from a park bench, blindfolded, and taken to a luxurious secret location. He spends a night of unexpected pleasure with a beautiful young woman, only to wake up alone in a fancy boat on the Han River with 100 wŏn and a cryptic note. Later, he reads a newspaper extra detailing an attempted murder involving a wealthy widow, Kim Milye, who used a rubber mask resembling her beautiful secretary, Kim Okkyŏng, to secretly entertain men lured by Okkyŏng. Kŭnsik realizes he was one of these men, entertained by Okkyŏng and then unknowingly by the masked Milye.

From a historical perspective, the story offers glimpses into colonial Seoul (Kyŏngsŏng) society in 1938. It highlights stark class contrasts (the "lumpen" Kŭnsik versus the wealthy Milye), the presence of Western influences (Western suits, cars, liquors, mentions of America, rubber masks), modern urban settings (department stores, bathhouses, specific locations like Tongdaemun, Ch'ŏngnyangni, Indogyo Bridge), and social phenomena like gisaeng houses. The use of a rubber mask and the elaborate deception points to themes of modernity, hidden identities, and perhaps anxieties surrounding changing social norms and female agency (albeit in a criminal context) during that period.

1. 漢江의 아침 안개

노돌 강변에 버들가지가 산들 바람에 가븨엽게 날니고 복사꽃닢이 어대서인지 나븨갓치 날나드는 느진 봄이다. 아츰 안개에 싸힌 한강은 마치 꿈속에 보는 그림갓치도 브드럽고 아름다웟다. 아츰 밀물을 따라 올나온 바다 고기를 낙그랴고 새벽부터 낙시를 드리고 잇는 어옹(漁翁)들이 드믄드믄 망두석 갓치 느러잇슬 뿐 그야말노 태평가라도 을프고 십흔 평화한 아츰이엇다. 서빙고를 지나면 샛강이 잇고, 그 샛강가로는 누가 심엇는지 버드나무가 느러저 잇다. 용산 군대에서 철선(鐵船)을 탄 공병(工兵)들이 연습차로 각금 차저올 뿐이지, 사람의 긔척이라고는 평생 업는 조그마한 육디이다. 이 조는드시 느러슨 버드나무가지에 누가 매어 노왓는지 동아줄이 느러젓고 그 동아줄을 따라 강가를 나려다보니 아담한 유선(遊船) 한 척이 매어잇다. 상아대가 가로질려 노힌 것을 보와 사공도 업는 모양갓고 배속을 들려다보랴니 좌우로 달닌 문에는 유리창만으로 초록빗 가- 덴이 가려잇서 맛치 금단의 밀실과도 갓치 숨을 죽이고 잇다. 배를 꿈인 픔으로 보아서는<187> 한강의 여왕이라도 할만한 호화선이다. 이런 아담한 배를 대관절 어느 누가 이런데다가 내다바리듯이 매어놋코 갓을까. 샛강물은 여울이 심해서 일등 사공이 안이면 이만한 배는 무사히 노들까지 흘너 나려가기가 힘이 들터인데- 대관절 엇저자고 이런데다가 이 조흔 배를 매어둔 채 밤을 재윗는가. 과연 초록빗 가- 덴 속에는 누가 잇는가. 이런 조흔 배에서 사공이 잘 가십지는 안으니- 그러면 들어잇는 사람은 누고일까. 남자인가 녀자일가. 또는 남자와 녀자의 합숙이나 안일가. 엇잿든 이 배에 사람이 드러잇다면 반다시 이러한 호화로운 배를 작만할만한 지위와 재산이 잇는 사람일 것은 분명하다.
그러면 아즉 봄도 다가지 안은 이 때 밤과 새벽은 아즉도 치울 지경인데 무슨 맘이 내켜 이런 호젓한 배에서 때안이 강상의 밤을 지내고 잇는가. 궁금은 한이 업고 의심은 끗이 업다.

2. 배 속의 珍景

속담에 개똥에 청보이란 말이 잇다. 개똥은 거름 구덩이로나 쓰러넛고 말 것이지 프른빗 비단 보잭이에 싸서든들 그게 무슨 소용 잇스며 무슨 격에 드러맛느냐, 하는 뜻이다. 동시에 막버리꾼에게 자가용 자동차가 개똥에 청보이겟고 소 몰고 밧 매는 농군의게 여송연이나 청지연이 역시 개똥에 청보 격일 것이다. 동시에 이 배 안에 전개된 진풍경도 두수업는 개똥에 청보격인 것을 소개하랴고 한다. 때가 색까맛케 무든 와이샤쓰 소매 속이 떠러저서 소매 밧그로 너펄거리는 양복 저고리 바지 궁둥이에다가 쪼각을 대고 꾀매입은 그의 형용은 두수업는 빠고다공원에서 꽁초나 주어먹을 룸폔으로 밧게는 볼 수 업는데 그는 비단 보료에 새털 안을 둔 비단 이불을 둘느고 안저 잇지 안는가. 그뿐일가. 손에는 십원짜리 지페를 열장이나 쥐고 잇다. 더욱 긔괴한 일은, 비단 자리에 안저 백원 돈이나 손에 들고 안저잇는 본인조차 자긔의 운명이 엇재서 이러케 급회전이 되야 바렷는지 아지 못하겟다는드시 경이의 눈을 뜨고 잇는 광경이다. 그는 왼편 손에 드럿든 지전을 놋코 바른손에 드럿든 편지를 펴 드럿다. 조선 언문 타이프로 찍은 편지이다.
〃뜻밧게 하로밤을 겁즐게 뫼신 저의 행복을 감사함니다. 이제는<188> 다만 당신의 력사에서 어제밤 한 장면을 선선히 삭제해 주시기를 복원할 뿐입니다. 만치 안은 돈이오나 동봉함니다. 이 돈은 제가 주소와 성명과 신분을 가릇켜 드리지 못하는 벌금으로 드리는 바이오니 허물치 마시고 밧아 주십시요. 저는 하늘 저 편에서 왓다가는 바람가튼 게집일 뿐입니다. 이 편지도 천문대에서 배달되얏거니 하시고 꿈가튼 하로밤 력사를 쾌히 이저주십시요. 물론 서명은 업다. 천문대에서 배달된 편지는 처음 읽는다. 하늘에서 왓다 하늘노 도라간 바람가튼 미인과 맛나 즐겁게 놀든 꿈가튼 일야- 당장 손에 쥐어잇는 백원의 현금? 생시의 일이라기는 너무나 허황하고 꿈이라기는 또 너무나 현실적이나 어제밤에는 분명이 자동차를 타고 동대문쪽으로 갓섯는데 오늘 아츰에 눈을 떠보니 어느 결에 한강에 떠 잇는 배속에 잇지 안은가. 이것이 도대체 엇지 된 일인가. 생각할사록 머리만 앗질앗질해 갈 뿐이다. 엇잿든 일원 짜리만 보와도 인사를 하게된 궁한 판에 백원 돈이 얼마나 대단한지 모른다. 손을 드러 코에다 대니 어제 밤 침대에서 그를 어로만즈든 손에서는, 아즉도 그의 향긔가 무더 풍기는 것 갓햇다.
"어 참 어제 밤은 조왓서. 참 조왓서!
한숨을 석근 혼자 소리까지 나왓다.
〃그럴니가 잇나. 내가 지금 그저 꿈을 꾸고 잇는게지. 꿈을 꾸면서도 꿈이 안이거니 하는 때도 잇스니까”
자긔의 존재를 분명이 알냐는 듯이 선창을 열고 세상을 바라보앗다. 총독관저 뒤로 놉히 소슨 안테나 밤을 도와 흘너 나려오는 떼목! 물 속에서 몰녀 다니는 고기떼들. 마즌편 감은 돌 어촌에서는 발서 아츰 연긔가 굴둑마다 안개갓치 피어오른다.
〃너 하나 뿐이라고 시침을 떼더니 극장에 갓치 온 건 누구랍듸가. 엇쩌면 고럿케도 시침을 떼요.
하도 답답해서 공원 벤취에 안기만 하면 일삼아 을프든 노래를 불너 보왓다. 분명이 생시이다. 결국 생시로 처 놋코 보니 어제밤부터 오늘 아츰까지에 자긔가 당한 일이 또 너무나 꿈갓다. 도모지 잇을 수 업는 일을 격근 까닭이다. 정신없이 강물을<189> 바라보고 잇스랴니, 검은 돌편에서 적은 배 한 척이 전속력을 내서 강을 거슬녀 오고 잇다. 분명이 목표는 근식이가 타고 잇는 호화선이다.
〃오- 냐 인제야 수수꺽기를 풀게 되나보다.
근식이는 고개를 내밀고 배가 닷기만 기다렷다.

〃이 배가 사공업는 배입니가.
