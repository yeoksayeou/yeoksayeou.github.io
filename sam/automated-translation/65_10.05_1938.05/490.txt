Magazine Title: 삼천리 제10권 제5호
Publication Date: 1938년 05월 01일
Article Title: 따니 꽃은 시들고 (Plucked Flowers Wilt)
Author: 岸曙 (An Sŏ)
Article Type: 시 (Poetry)

When my pockets are empty and I cannot seek out drinking companions, when I alone cannot fall asleep on a night when others are sleeping, when there is no way to soothe my lonely heart amidst the confusing hardships of the world, when I am bored with myself on long summer days because there is no topic of conversation – at such times, leafing through dust-covered collections of Han poems (漢詩集) is my comfort. While doing so, my interest was drawn to unexpected places; whenever I encountered so-called poignant poems close to the common style (巴調), not only did I jot them down in a notebook, but I also developed a habit of trying my hand at translating them, however unskilled I may be.

These songs have such origins; being originally songs of womenfolk close to the common style (巴調), they will naturally not suit the tastes of lofty individuals and will surely receive sharp criticism. However, what is good for person A is not necessarily good for person B, and what person B dislikes is not necessarily something person A must also like, so I cannot simply discard them. Amidst this, having unexpectedly received a request from Samch'ŏlli-sa<258>, I am publishing these, calling them songs without even knowing shame. Although I try to justify the reason with a word, having translated and written them down, I am simply startled myself at how unbecoming they are.

The Lotus Flower is My Heart

The lotus flower you sent, so cherished when held in hand.
Unable to contain my delight at its red color, I touch it,
But how many days has it been since it was plucked?
It has all withered, only the fragrance remains,
Like this heart of mine.

Sigh (嘆息)

How many years since you left! This sorrowful body of mine.
Why should I adorn myself?<259>
The sun and moon are my heart,
Unable to forget my beloved,
Rising in the day and then again rising in the night.

Indifferent is This River Water (無心타 이 江水야)

Everyone says this river water is good,
But I hate the boat floating on the river even more.
Since it left once, carrying my beloved,
Though years and months pass, it does not return.

Parting by the Riverbank (江까의 離別)

My beloved's boat going out to the wide, wide sea,
If only this body could become water, I would follow.<260>

Tears (눈물)

These tears soaking my collar, drop by drop,
O autumn wind, do not swirl and dry them.
On the wings of the wild geese flying west in pairs,
Let them be sent to my heartless beloved.

Autumn Night (가을 밤)

Autumn, when leaves have sparsely fallen from every branch.
Sitting alone on the railing of the pavilion,
This lonely state of a body
Hoping for the moonlight on a long night,
Who would know?

If the Moon Were a Mirror (달이 거울이라면)<261>

Drunkenly looking up, that round moon
Must be shining upon my person tonight as well.
Yet there is no way for that beloved to see it,
And only clouds drift in the sky, you say?

Turning Heart (돌아서는 마음)

Why do you say the road is long?
Could the heart, having turned away, not be far?
My heart is a cartwheel,
Turning and turning again,
Traveling a thousand, ten thousand *li* every day.

Playing Cards (골패짝)

Fondly touching the humble playing cards (골패짝),<262>
People lose themselves completely in games.
But when they become stained by hand marks after not long,
People discard them, pretending not to know.

I Do Not Know (난 몰으노라)

The willows are green, the river water is calm.
When my beloved sings on the riverbank,
The moon is in the east, and a light rain falls in the west.
Could my beloved’s heart be like this too? I do not know.

Candlelight (燭火)

Through the long night, the candlelight melts away with light,
Quietly forming tears, drop by drop.
I see a person's whole life—
In glory, empty worries tend to multiply.<263>
<258-263>

**Summary**

The author, An Sŏ, introduces a collection of poems translated from Chinese (Han poems), explaining that this translation effort serves as a personal solace during times of loneliness, boredom, or hardship. An Sŏ acknowledges these poems, described as being in a "common" or "folksy" style (巴調) and often reflecting female perspectives, might not appeal to refined tastes and expects criticism. Nevertheless, An Sŏ publishes them at the request of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*, despite feeling they are inadequate. The poems themselves are short lyrics primarily expressing themes of longing, separation, sorrow, the passage of time, and the fickleness of love or fortune, often using natural imagery like flowers, the moon, rivers, and seasons. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it showcases the personal engagement with classical Chinese poetry by a Korean intellectual during the colonial period, specifically focusing on translating less canonical, perhaps more emotionally direct or "feminine" verses. It reflects an effort to find personal meaning and perhaps subtle cultural expression through translation, even while acknowledging the perceived "lowbrow" nature of the chosen source material within the contemporary literary hierarchy.

따니 꽃은 시들고
						岸曙
주머니가 비여 酒朋을 찻지 못할 때, 남 다 자는 아닌 밤에 혼자로서 잠 못 이를 때, 뒤숭숭한 世苦남에 쓸쓸한 심회를 풀을 길 없을 때, 기나긴 녀름 날에 화제 거리가 업서 스스로서 심심할 때 -이러한 때에 이것저것 몬지싸인 漢詩集들을 둘추어 보는 것이 나의 위로외다. 그러는 동안 나의 흥미는 뜻하지 아니한 곳으로 쏠녓스니, 그것은 巴調에 가까운 소위 아릿아릿한 시들을 만날 때에는 공책에다 적어둘 뿐아니라, 되지도 못한 솜씨로 번역이랍시고 이것들을 옴겨 노하보는 버릇이외다.
이 노래들은 그러한 이유를 가진 것으로 본래가 巴調에 가까운 아낙네들의 노래이매, 놉흔 이들의 비위에 맛지 아니할 것은 물론, 톡톡이 꾸지람을 바들 것이외다. 그러나 갑에게 조흔 것이라고 을에게도 조흔 것이 아니요, 을이 실허하는 것이라고 반듯이 갑이 조화하는 것도 아니고 보니 새삼스러이 내여버릴 수도 업는 일이외다. 이러든 차에 우연이 三千里사의<258> 청을 바다 부끄러운 줄도 몰으고 이것들을 노래랍시고 발표케 되니, 한마듸로써 무어라고 그 所以를 변명해 두거니와, 무어라고 옴겨놋고 보니 넘우도 답지못한 데 혼자서 놀낼 뿐이외다.

蓮꽃은 나의 맘

손에 드니 살틀타 보내신 蓮꽃.
붉은 빗 못내 반겨 내가 만지나
꺽기운 지 멧츨고?
모다 시들어 향내만 남앗고야
이내 맘처렴.

嘆息

떠나신 지 멧 핸고! 설은 이내 몸.
무어라고 단장은 내가 할건가.<259>
해와 달은 나의 맘
님을 못니저
낫에 떳다 다시금 밤에 뜨거니.

無心타 이 江水야

이 강물을 모도들 조타고 하나
강 우에 떠가는 배 더욱 미워라.
한 번 님을 실고서 떠나간 뒤론
해가 가고 달 가도 아니 들오네.

江까의 離別

넓고 넓은 저 바다 가는 님의 배
이 몸이 물이드면 딸아나 갈 걸.<260>

눈물

방울방울 옷깃을 적신 이 눈물
갈바람아 휘돌아 말니지 말라.
쌍지어 서녁가는 기럭이 편에
모심한 그 내님끠 보내나지고.

가을 밤

가지마다 엉성이 입떨닌 가을.
다락이라 난간에 외로이 안자
기나긴 밤 달빗을 바라는 몸의
쓸쓸한 이 신세를
뉘가 알으리.

달이 거울이라면<261>

취하야 처다보니 둥글은 저 달
내 사람을 이 밤도 비최여 주리.
그런 것을 그 님은 볼 길이 업고
하늘에는 구름만 떠돈단 말가.

돌아서는 마음

무어라 길이 멀다 말슴하시노.
마음이 돌아서서 아니 멀으랴.
수레바퀸 나의 맘
돌고 또 돌아
날마다 천리 만리 다니는 것을.

골패짝

미천한 골패짝을 조와 만지며<262>
사람들은 놀음에 얼일코 노나,
얼마 안해 손때가 지는 그 날엔
몰으는 체 사람은 내버리거니.

난 몰으노라

버들은 프르르고 강물은 고요.
우리 님 江上에서 노래 불을 제
동엔 달이 서편엔 실비 오나니
님의 맘도 이러랴, 난 몰으노라.

燭火

기나긴 밤 燭뿔은 빗으로 새며
고요이 방울방울 눈물 짓나니,
사람의 한 세상을 나는 보노라-
영화엔 뜬 근심이 되려 만타고.<263>
