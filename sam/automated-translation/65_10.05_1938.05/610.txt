**Collection of Newspaper Telegrams from the Time of the Sino-Japanese War**

This is a rare document compiling war telegrams that appeared in various Tōkyō newspapers during the Sino-Japanese War, 40 years ago. It vividly depicts the stormy situation in East Asia at that time, which arose centered on the Korean peninsula.

**Sino-Japanese Incident, War Clouds Gather Urgently, Imperial Council Increasingly Hardline**
(July 14, Meiji 27 [1894], Tōkyō Kokumin Shimbun Extra)

At a meeting held yesterday from 1 PM at Prime Minister Itō Hirobumi's residence in Nagatachō, attended not only by all ministers but also by General Yamagata and Vice Chief of Staff Kawakami, after careful deliberation, it is said that a final decision was made regarding the measures to be taken towards the Qing Dynasty.

**Naval Battle off Pungdo (Hōtō Oki)**
(July 28, Meiji 27 [1894], Kokumin Shimbun Extra)

*Our Navy Sinks 1,500 Qing Soldiers*

Our fleet, consisting of the Akitsushima and Takachiho, encountered a Qing transport ship carrying 1,500 Qing soldiers from Tianjin towards Asan in the vicinity of Namyang Bay at 7 AM on the 25th, and sank it. The escorting gunboat Caojiang (操江號) was captured, while the [Ji]yuan ([濟]遠號 - Note: likely Jiyuan, original text uses 灣遠號 Manwŏn Ho, possibly a typo or variant name) fled towards Qing, and the Guangyi (廣乙號) fled towards Asan.
The Caojiang was a gunboat with a displacement of 950 tons (啍), armed with one 13-pounder steel cannon, two 16-pounder steel cannons, and four Gatling guns (갓도링 포).

**Declaration of War and Foreign Powers**
(August 4, Meiji 27 [1894], Kokumin Shimbun Extra)

Our country declared the start of war with the Qing Dynasty the day before yesterday. Hostilities between Japan and Qing are now open. Regarding the stance of various foreign powers towards the belligerents, it is heard that Great Britain will proclaim neutrality outside the conflict. It is said that other foreign powers will follow suit and stand in a position of strict neutrality.<345>

**Report of Victory at Asan**
(August 17, Meiji 27 [1894] Jiji Shinpō Extra)
(Note: Date seems wrong, battle was July 29. Maybe reporting date?)

On the evening of the 29th, our troops defeated Sŏnghwan and, with the fierce momentum of splitting bamboo, reached the Qing barracks at Asan. The Qing army formed a large, dense formation (團隊) attempting to charge our forces. Our army deployed in formations on both the left and right wings, dispersing soldiers onto small hills (小丘) on either side to break the Qing formation. With each shot from the 00-style 00 shells, dozens were killed, and rifle fire rained down. After about an hour, the number of Qing casualties was countless.
*Note - Admiral Ding (丁) refers to Ding Ruchang (丁汝昌), Admiral of the Beiyang Fleet, who later committed suicide by taking poison. Left Wing Commander (左翼 總兵官) Liu Buchan (劉步*), captain of the Dingyuan, followed him in death.* (Note: Original uses 劉步* likely for 劉步蟾 Liu Buchan).

**Fall of P'yŏngyang**
(September 17, Meiji 27 [1894] Jiji Shinpō Extra)

Since the 15th, our division surrounded P'yŏngyang and, after fierce fighting, achieved a great victory, completely capturing P'yŏngyang at dawn this morning. Enemy casualties are extremely high; our officer and enlisted casualties are about 300.

*September 16th, 8:00 AM, from Division Commander*

Despite great difficulties in transporting provisions, our division advanced towards P'yŏngyang from various routes. As of yesterday, we had surrounded the city on all four sides, and after fierce combat, achieved a great victory, completely capturing it at dawn this morning. The enemy commander Zuo Baogui (左翼貴 - likely 左寶貴 Zuo Baogui) and below suffered extremely numerous casualties, prisoners, weapons, and rice falling into our hands. The enemy strength was calculated at 20,000, but since yesterday, they have been fleeing our picket lines in groups of one or two; the rest were all killed, wounded, or captured. Our officer and enlisted casualties number 300. This great victory is due to the divine spirit of His Majesty the Emperor and the loyal diligence of the officers and men.

**Detailed Report on the Great Battle of P'yŏngyang**
(September 28, Meiji 27 [1894] Jiji Shinpō Article)

The attack on P'yŏngyang was said to be a great battle, rare in recent times. It goes without saying that the security of both Japan and Qing hinged on the fall of P'yŏngyang Castle. P'yŏngyang Castle was not only strongly fortified as a strategic point but also possessed naturally advantageous terrain for strategy. Furthermore, after the defeat at Sŏnghwan, defenses were further strengthened, making its capture undoubtedly a very difficult task. Among these<346> defenders were the elite Shengzi Army (盛字軍) from Fengtian Prefecture (奉天府) under Li Hongzhang, combined with the Fengtian Army Cavalry (奉天軍 馬隊), and further augmented by the defeated troops from Sŏnghwan. Their total number could be counted at over 10,000, and some people at the time even spoke of troop numbers far exceeding 40,000.
Their weapons were all sharp, cannons were Krupp new-style mountain and field guns (크르프 신식 山野雨砲), and rifles were Mauser repeaters (모-젤 연발) and Spencers (쓰펜젤), etc., not inferior to those of any civilized nation's modern army. Moreover, the people of P'yŏngyang city were all on the Qing side, making everything convenient for them. However, our army bravely invaded enemy territory. Not only was the weather extremely unfavorable, but provisions consisted only of millet and sorghum, causing great hardship as they advanced on the enemy; the difficulties were beyond description. Perhaps if there had been enough time to slowly consider strategy, our army's toil might have been less, but shortly after the arrival of the 5th Division commander in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), the mixed brigade was dispatched within a day or two, so what more needs to be said?
Prior to this, the mixed brigade, starting from the day of its triumphant return from Sŏnghwan, immediately headed for the P'yŏngyang attack, occupying the Imjin River area and Kaesŏng. Preparations, such as Major Ichinohe's (一戶) departure and the deployment of the quartermaster department, were proceeding steadily.
Specifically, the Wŏnsan detachment landed at Songgyo on the 13th, moved to Sunan on the 14th, and from Sunan headed straight for P'yŏngyang Castle on the 15th. The Sangnyŏng detachment stayed at Maekchŏn Tong along the Taedong River on the 13th, went to Taeji Tong on the 14th, and headed for P'yŏngyang Castle on the 15th. The division split into two groups: one crossed at Sap'o below Wŏlpong Mountain, and the other crossed at Pingp'o, reached Kangsŏ County, dispatched a left flank detachment, and advanced towards P'yŏngyang from the Chŭngsan County road, with the left and right sides converging. The mixed brigade arrived at Suwan Bridge, about 2 *ri* south of the Taedong River, on the 12th, planning to challenge and attack the Qing troops with the objective of reconnaissance. In short, the division's objective was to break through via five routes and immediately begin the siege of P'yŏngyang.

**Occupation of Weihaiwei**
(February 2, Meiji 28 [1895], Hōchi Shimbun Article)

The attack on Weihaiwei began today; three forts were captured by the army, and one was destroyed by the fleet.

**Battle Situation at Weihaiwei**
(February 3, Meiji 28 [1895], Hōchi Shimbun Article)
-Received January 30th by Ōyama, Commander of the 2nd Army- <347>

Our army attacked the enemy positions at Baichi Cliff (百尺崖) and the highlands stretching southwest on the 30th. The 6th Division advanced towards Wenquantang (溫泉湯), the [unknown unit, likely typo for 2nd Division?] towards Gushan (孤山) vicinity, and the 2nd Division towards Fenglin (鳳林) vicinity, occupying them. Our fleet bombarded and captured the fort at Baichi Cliff. Enemy ships are still in the bay, bombarding us. Casualties on both sides and captured items are currently under investigation.

**Beiyang Fleet Surrenders**
(February 19, Meiji 28 [1895], Hōchi Shimbun Article)
-Sent 8:28 PM, 17th from Imperial Headquarters, Received 11:10 PM by Navy Ministry-

Yesterday at noon, the Qing gunboat Zhenbei (鎭北) hoisted a white flag, and its captain came as Admiral Ding's emissary, carrying a letter from the Admiral. It stated: Although he initially intended to fight until the ships were destroyed and the men exhausted, he now wishes to preserve lives. Therefore, he will offer all warships, cannons, ammunition, and weapons. In return, he requests that the lives of officials, soldiers, other personnel, both Qing subjects and foreigners, be spared, and that each be allowed to return to their hometown. If this is granted, he asks for the commander of the United States fleet to act as a witness. Therefore, I replied, accepting the requested terms. Accordingly, the warships and other items will be received tomorrow, the 13th. The time and method will be discussed further. The personnel will be transported to a convenient location by our country's ships. Using the commander of the United States fleet as a witness will be unnecessary. (Remainder omitted - original text)
February 13th, Itō, Commander-in-Chief of the Combined Fleet

**Peace Negotiation Reports**
(March 17, Meiji 28 [1895], Hōchi Shimbun Article)

O Negotiation Venue: Shunpanrō (春帆樓) - Located beside the shrine of Emperor Antoku below Hongdŏk Mountain (紅石山) in Shimonoseki (馬關), Shunpanrō has been selected as the location for the peace negotiations. Since the 12th, the city mayor, assistants, city council members, councillors, and other prefectural officials have been diligently supervising 70-80 carpenters, decorators, etc., in its refurbishment.
O Li Hongzhang's Lodging - The renovation work at Innōji Temple (引樓寺), designated as Li Hongzhang's lodging this time, is currently being carried out by 80 carpenters.

**Attack on Li Hongzhang**
(March 28, Meiji 28 [1895], Hōchi Shimbun Article)

Human affairs are unpredictable; there is no way to know in advance what terrible incident may occur later. Today was the third meeting (with Our Plenipotentiaries). From 3 PM, Li Hongzhang rode in a palanquin (輿), while Li Jingfang, Luo Fenglu, Ma Jianzhong, Wu Tingfang, etc., rode in rickshaws, departing from Innōji Temple (引接寺 - Note: earlier called 引樓寺 Innōji?) and entering Shunpanrō. The meeting lasted over an hour. After 4:30 PM, Li Hongzhang started his return journey. Li's party proceeded as they had come, observing the crowds gathered on both sides. When they reached the front of the Kawamura residence (강촌저?) at No. 20 Sotohamachō (外濱町), a madman suddenly appeared and shot Li Hongzhang.<349>

**Summary**

This article compiles Japanese newspaper telegram reports from the First Sino-Japanese War (1894-1895), covering major events from the lead-up and initial naval clashes near Korea to land battles at Asan and P'yŏngyang, the capture of Weihaiwei, the surrender of the Beiyang Fleet, and the Shimonoseki peace negotiations, including the assassination attempt on Li Hongzhang. For Korean colonial history, the article is significant as it presents, through Japanese primary sources reprinted in a Korean magazine in 1938, the narrative of a war pivotal to Japan's rise and Korea's subsequent colonization. Historians might find value in how this conflict, fought partly on Korean soil and directly determining Korea's fate (shifting from Qing influence to Japanese), was portrayed to a Korean audience decades later under Japanese rule, showcasing the Japanese perspective on key battles and diplomatic events. The selection and framing implicitly reinforce the narrative of Japanese military success and decisiveness.

日淸戰爭 때 新聞 電報集

日淸事變, 戰雲急, 廟議愈强硬
(明治 27년 7월 14일 동경 국민신문 호외)

작일 千后 1시부터 永田町, 伊藤博文 수상의 관저 회의에는 각 대신은 물론, 山縣 대장, 川上 참모차장이 출석, 숙의한 뒤 淸國에 향하야, 최후의 처치를 하기로 一決하엿다 한다.

豊島沖의 海戰
(明治 27년 7월 28일 국민신문 호외)

我 해군 淸兵 1,500인을 격침하다

我 秋 津洲號, 高千穗號의 함대는 25일 천전 7시 南陽灣 근해에서 淸兵 1,500인을 탑재하고 天津에서부터 牙山에 향하려는 淸國의 운송선을 격침하엿는데 호위군선 操江號를 포획하고, 灣遠號는 淸國으로, 廣乙號는 牙山으로 향하여 도주하엿다.
操江은 포함으로서 배수 啍 수 950啍, 무기는 13근 鋼砲 1, 16근 鋼砲 2, 갓도링 포 4를 備하다.

(明治 27년 8월 4일 국민신문 호외)

我國은 一昨日부터 淸國과의 개전을 선포하엿다. 日淸간의 교전은 지금에는 공연하게 되나, 차제 諸 외국의 교전국간에 처할 길이 여하, 듯건대 영국은 局外 중립을 포고하리라 한다. 기타의 諸 외국도 여기에 따라 엄연한 중립의 지위에 서리라 한다.<345>

牙山 戰勝의 報
(明治 27년 17일 時事 新報 호외)

29일 夕, 我兵은 成歡을 파하고 파죽의 猛勢로서 牙山의 淸兵營에 다닥첫다. 淸軍은 일대 밀집 團隊을 형세지어 아군에 돌격하여 오려함으로 아군은 다 좌우의 兩翼에 포진하고 兵을 좌우의 小丘에 散兵식혀, 淸兵의 단대를 파격케 하다. 00식의 00탄 1발 할 때마다 수십인을 격살하고, 소총을 난발하여 약 1시간 여에 급하여 淸兵이 사상하는 자, 부지기수다.
註 - 丁 제독이라 함은 북양함대의 水師 제독 丁汝昌의 일로, 뒤에 독배를 바더 자살하다. 좌익 총 兵官 定遠 선장 劉步*도 이에 따라 순사하다.

平壤 陷落
(明治 27년 9월 17일 時事新報 호외)

15일 이래 我 사단 평양을 圍하고 격전의 후, 대승리를 得하고, 今朝 미명에 완전히 평양을 약취하다. 적의 사상 극히 多하고 아군 장교 이하 사상 약 300명.

16일 오전 8시 中私에서 師團 長官

我 사단은 양식 운수에 大 곤란하엿슴에도 불구하고 각 道로부터 평양에 향하여 전진하여, 昨日로서 성의 사면을 포위하고, 격렬한 전투 끗에 대승리를 得, 今朝 미명으로서 완전히 此를 약취하다. 적의 대장 左翼貴 이하 사상 포로 병기, 미곡의 我手에 落한 것이 극히 다수, 병의 병력은 2만이라고 算하엿스나 昨日來 1-2 群을 지어서 我 哨兵線을 逃去하엿스나, 他는 모다 사상 及 포로가 되다. 我의 장교 이하 사상 300인, 此의 대승리는 천황 폐하의 威靈과 장교 이하의 忠勤에 의한다.

平壤의 大戰鬪 詳報
(明治 27년 9월 28일 時事新報 所載)

평양 공격은 근래에 드믄 大戰이라 했다. 말하자면 日淸 양국의 안위가 이 평양성 함락에 달였다고 했으니까 거기 더 말할 것이 있으랴. 평양성은 要害地로 强築될 뿐 아니라 그 우에 천연적으로 전략상 유리한 지형이였는데 거기다가 成歡 패배 후엔 더 한층 방어에 힘을 다했으니 이것을 함락하기엔 무척 어려운 일이 아닐 수 없었다. 그 중에<346> 또 수비군병은 淸軍 중 정예라고 이르는 奉天府의 盛字軍 李鴻章 부하의 盛字軍과 또 奉天軍 馬隊가 합한 우에 다시 成歡의 패병을 겸했으니 그 총 兵數가 1만을 헤일 수 있었고 그때 사람들은 군병 수가 4만에 遠遠했다는 이얘기까지 했다.
무기는 죄다 예리하고 대포는 크르프 신식 山野雨砲에 총은 모-젤 연발과 쓰펜젤 등으로 어느 문명국 신식병에 비해서도 뒤지지 않었다. 더구나 평양성 사람들은 다 淸國 편이였음으로 만사가 편리했다. 그러나 아군은 용감히 적지에 침입하야 기후가 몹시 불순할 뿐 아니라 양식이라군 수수 조밖게 없는 형편이여서 매우 고생하며 적을 침입했는데 그 곤란은 이르 말할 수가 없었다. 혹 일수가 넉넉해서 전략을 천천히 생각할 여유가 있었다면 아군의 苦勞가 적었을지 모르지만, 제5사단장 着京 후 하로 이틀 사이에 곳 혼성 여단을 발했으니 더 말할 것이 있으랴.
이보다 먼저 혼성 여단은 成歡 개선일부터 곳 평양 공격에 향하여 臨津에 개성부에 점령, 一戶 소좌의 출발 及 병참부의 배치 등 모든 준비에 착착 진행 중이였다.
즉 元山枝隊는 13일 松橋에 착륙하야 다시 14일엔 順安으로, 順安서 15일에 곳 평양성을 향하고, 朔寧 枝隊는 13일 대동강을 따라 麥田洞에 머믈다가 14일은 大地洞에 가서 15일에 平壤城을 향하고, 사단은 2團으로 나누워 한 團은 月峯山 下綠少 沙浦를 건너고, 또 한 團은 氷浦를 건너, 江西縣에 이르러 左側隊를 내고 甑山縣 路부터 좌우가 합해서 평양을 향하고 혼성 여단은 12일 대동강 南 2리나 되는 水灣橋에 와서 적군 견제의 목적을 가지고 도전 淸兵을 공격할 계획이었다. 요컨대 사단의 목적은 五道를 뚫고 곳 평양 攻圍를 시작하려는 것이였다.

李鴻章의 遭難
(明治 28년 3월 28일 報知新聞 所載)

인사는 불측이와, 뒤에 엇더한 흉변 잇는 즐은 미리 알 길이 업다. 本日은 제 3회의 회견 (我의 全權과)임으로 千后 3시부터 李鴻章은 輿에 乘하여 李經芳, 羅豊錄, 馬建忠, 伍廷芳 등은 인력거를 타고 引接寺를 出하여 春帆樓에 入하다. 그 뒤 회견은 1시간 여, 4시 반을 지나서 李鴻章은 歸途에 올는다. 李의 일행은 오든 때와 가치 좌우에 모아든 군중을 바라보면서 外濱町 20번지 강촌저 압까지 왓슬 때에 一狂漢이 돌현하여 李鴻章을 저격하엿다.<349>
