Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Commentary)

Regarding the Rumor of the Office of the Yi Royal Household's Total Assets Being 200 Million Won, Explanation of What Kind of Place the Office of the Yi Royal Household Is, Etc.

What does the Office of the Yi Royal Household (李王職) do, and what kind of place is it? As not much literature about it has been published, many people are quite curious. Therefore, although extremely insufficient, I intend to convey at least its general outline here. (I will add a more detailed postscript on another day). That is, upon the establishment of the annexation in March of Meiji 43 [1910], the former Korean Imperial Household and its close relatives were granted the courtesy equivalent to that of the Japanese Imperial Family, their noble ranks were made hereditary, and it was decided that they would receive an annual stipend (歲費) equal to the amount before the annexation. Simultaneously, the regulations for the Office of the Yi Royal Household were established, creating the Office of the Yi Royal Household to manage various affairs and assets of the royal family, continuing to this day. Abstracting the regulations:

Abstract of the Office of the Yi Royal Household Regulations

(Imperial Household Ordinance No. 34, February 30, Meiji 43 [Note: Feb 30 is likely a typo in the original, should be April 30 based on historical context, but translated as written])
Article 1: The Office of the Yi Royal Household falls under the administration of the Minister of the Imperial Household (宮內 大臣) and manages the household affairs of the royal family (王族) and public family (公族).
Article 2: The following personnel are appointed to the Office of the Yi Royal Household.<323>
Director-General (長官) Physician (典醫) Assistant Ritual Officer (典祀補)
Vice-Minister (次官) Engineer (技師) Assistant Physician (典醫補)
Administrative Official (事務官) Interpreter (通譯官) Engineer (技師) [Duplicate listing in original]
Ritual Officer (典祀) Clerk (屬)
Article 3: The Director-General, one person appointed by imperial decree (勅任), supervises all affairs of the Office of the Yi Royal Household and directs and oversees subordinate officials.
Article 4: The Vice-Minister, one person appointed by imperial decree, assists the Director-General, manages the affairs of the Office of the Yi Royal Household, and acts on his behalf when the Director-General is incapacitated.
Article 7: Four full-time Ritual Officers (典祀) are appointed by the Emperor upon recommendation (奏任). One may be appointed by imperial decree (勅任). They manage affairs related to rituals (祭祀) and tombs (墳塋). Ritual Officers may be appointed as honorary officials.
Article 8: Three full-time Physicians (典醫) are appointed by recommendation (奏任). They manage diagnosis (診候) and medicine preparation (調藥).
Article 15: Two administrative officials, one physician, clerks, and assistant physicians are assigned to the Late Yi Queen (故 李王妃); one administrative official and clerks are assigned to each public family (公族).
The above is a rough outline. Looking at the current staff, Dr. Shinoda (蓧田), an official appointed directly by the Emperor (親任官), is the Director-General. He transferred from being the Governor of South P'yŏngan Province, has deep knowledge of the Kando problem, and holds a doctorate in law. Counting the civil officials appointed directly by the Emperor in Chosŏn, there are only the Director-General of Political Affairs, the University President, and this Director-General Shinoda. The Vice-Minister, Mr. Yi Hang-gu (李恒九), is the son of Marquis Yi Wan-yong (李完用) and is currently a Baron. Below is a list of other important officials:

What kind of work do they do?

Work done by the Ceremonies and Palace Management Sections

The Office of the Yi Royal Household is located at 2 Waryong-dong, Seoul, within the grounds of Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮). Just inside the Tonhwa Gate (敦化門), the elegant semi-Western style two-story building on the right is it. The various duties performed by the Office of the Yi Royal Household<325> are divided among four sections: General Affairs, Accounting, Ceremonies, and Palace Management.
The Ceremonies Section (禮式課) handles:
1) Celebrations and rituals (祝祭)
2) Management of shrines (廟), ritual halls (祭殿), ritual palaces (祭宮), and tombs (墳塋)
3) Ceremonies (儀式) and banquets (餐宴)
4) Reception of guests (賓客)
5) Matters concerning the Court Music Department (雅樂部)

The Palace Management Section (主殿課) handles:
1) Protection of the inner halls (內殿) and Injŏngjŏn Hall (仁政殿) and their attached buildings
2) Storage of furnishings and utensils (供用 什器) for the inner halls and Injŏngjŏn Hall
3) Preparation of meals (供膳)
4) Diagnosis (診候) and medicine preparation (調藥)
5) Assigned court ladies (內人)
6) Preservation of the royal family's genealogy (譜牒), documents (詞章), ancient seals (古印), etc.

A noteworthy matter is that His Highness the Yi King, concerned that the traditional Korean court music (雅樂) passed down solely through oral tradition lacked any musical notation, ordered composer Mr. Ishikawa Giichi (石川義一) from Shōwa 2 [1927] onwards to transcribe the 283 pieces currently housed in the Ceremonies Section. This resulted in 18,427 pages of recorded musical scores, which were presented to the Yi Royal Family on March 27th of last year. The Ceremonies Section is responsible for storing these. Also, purchasing artworks (御 買上) from exhibitions, such as the annual art exhibition, and storing them is also the work of the Ceremonies Section. For example, at the 16th Chosŏn Art Exhibition, the following three Ch'angdŏk Palace Prizes were announced and purchased on May 14th of last year:
Eastern Painting: *Old Story* (古談) by Kim Ki-ch'ang (金基昶)
Western Painting: *Nude* (裸婦) by Kim In-sŭng (金仁承)
Crafts: *Scene of Boating on the Willow Capital Stream* (柳京舟遊圖) by Yamada Katsuji (山田勝二)
These are all stored by the Ceremonies Section. Besides these, the Office of the Yi Royal Household performs various other tasks.

Palaces, Shrines, and Royal Residences
Eight Shrines/Palaces, Ninety-Eight Tombs Exist

Let's look at where the current Palaces (宮), Shrines (廟), and Royal Tombs (御陵) are located:
Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮): 2 Waryong-dong, Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (京城府)
Chongmyo Shrine (宗廟): 1 Hunjŏng-dong, Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture<326>
Sŏnwŏnjŏn Hall (璿源殿): 1 Hwa-dong, Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture
Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮): 5 Chŏng-dong, Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture
Chogyŏngmyo Shrine (肇慶廟): Chŏnju-myŏn, Chŏnju-gun, North Chŏlla Province
(Shrine for the Yi Royal Family founder, Sagonggong (司空公) and his consort, Lady Kim of Kyŏngju)
Hamhŭng Pon'gung (咸興 本宮): Sunnyŏng-myŏn, Hamju-gun, South Hamgyŏng Province
(Birthplace of King T'aejo (太祖 御 發祥地))
Furthermore, regarding Royal Tombs (御陵), there are 52 tombs starting from King T'aejo's Kŏnwŏllŭng (健元陵), located in Suwŏn and other various regions.
For the Shrines and Tombs (殿陵), ancestral rites (享祀) are performed according to traditional Korean ritual protocols (儀制) from the Koryŏ dynasty and earlier, following established standards. The term "Chŏn" (殿, Shrine/Hall) refers to sanctuaries where the spirits (靈璽) of dynastic founders from ancient times up to the Koryŏ dynasty, as well as previous kings with special merits, are enshrined (勸請) to express reverence and filial piety (追遠報本); there are 8 such places.
"Nŭng" (陵, Tomb) refers to the burial mounds containing the aforementioned remains. There are 98 whose locations are clearly known. Among these, for the 6 tombs of founding ancestors (始祖陵), rites involving offerings of silk and food (奠幣供饌) are performed twice a year, in spring and autumn.
However, there are a couple of special cases: for the founder's tomb of the Karak Kingdom (駕洛國, Imna (任那)), it has been customary since ancient times to perform the rites concurrently with the shrine rites (魂殿享祀), so a separate tomb ritual (陵墓祭) is not performed.
Also, the location of the founder's tomb of the Paekche King (百濟王) is unknown.
Furthermore, to conduct the regular services (常時 奉仕) for the 8 Shrines and 8 Tombs [Note: Seems to contradict the earlier counts of 8 shrines and 98/52 tombs, possibly referring to a specific subset or a typo], one Ritual Attendant (參奉) is appointed for each. These attendants are recruited from the descendants of meritorious subjects (各姓의 後裔者).
Strict supervision is maintained over the management of each tomb (陵 奉行), and regulations prohibiting logging (放牧), cultivation (耕墾), and gathering firewood (採樵) are established to maintain dignity (尊嚴). Guardians (守護人) are assigned based on the size and distance of the tomb.

How much wealth do they manage? - The saying goes, 200 million Won -

It is not easy to assess the value of the assets managed by the Office of the Yi Royal Household. Even setting aside things beyond imagination like palace buildings, treasures such as calligraphy and paintings from successive generations, or the forests attached to the tomb sites (陵所), the scope is truly too vast and complex to easily determine. Therefore, here I will list just a few materials based on what ordinary city dwellers can assess.<327>

How many stocks are there?

The primary way assets are held is through investments in stocks (株券) of reliable and sound banks and companies.
1. 150,000 Won in the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行)
(That is, 1,500 shares at 100 Won per share are held in the name of the Director-General of the Office of the Yi Royal Household)
2. 50,500 Won in the Chosŏn Commercial Bank (朝鮮商業銀行)
(1,100 shares in the Chosŏn Siksan Bank [Note: The text says 조선상업은행 but then mentions 조선식산은행, likely referring to Chosŏn Industrial Bank/殖産銀行 where the Commercial Bank shares might be held or it's a confusion] are owned in the name of the Director-General of the Office of the Yi Royal Household)
3. Oriental Development Company (東洋拓殖株式會社)
It is presumed that the largest number of shares are held in this Oriental Development Company, capitalized at 50 million Won, but the exact amount cannot be ascertained. Originally, after the Russo-Japanese War, as economic relations between Japan and Korea became extremely close, Japan aimed for industrial development (殖産興業). The Japanese government provided funds (給金), and the former Korean government contributed land for business purposes. At that time, the Yi Royal Family contributed much of its owned land and forests, which were converted into stocks (換價) and held. Furthermore, it is said that the aforementioned shares in the Bank of Chosŏn and the Commercial Bank also largely originated from in-kind investments (現物 投資) like land, and very few were purchased with cash (現金).
Besides these, there might be stocks held in other banks and companies, but since the numbers are not clearly known yet, they are omitted.

How much land and buildings are there?

This article will not cover the direct palace buildings of the Yi Royal Family but will note only those parts visible to the general public.
Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑) - A part of the garden opened by the Yi Royal Family for the citizens of Kyŏngsŏng, it contains a museum, zoo, and botanical garden. Originally called Suganggung (壽康宮), several old palace buildings (殿址) near the Myŏngjŏngjŏn Hall (明政殿) display ancient weapons, Buddhist statues, calligraphy, and paintings from the Silla period onwards. Anyone who has seen the buildings, flora and fauna, various facilities, pavilions, and grounds within the garden can roughly estimate its value.
Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮) - Originally called Kyŏngungung (慶運宮), a new museum has been built next to the Sŏkchojŏn Hall (石造殿) at a cost of 1.5 million Won. It houses artifacts excavated from Nangnang (樂浪) and Samhan (三韓), as well as Buddhist statues, stone artifacts, calligraphy, and paintings from the Silla period.<328>
Besides these, there is Kyŏngbok Palace (景福宮). It was a very large old palace with grounds covering 130,000 p'yŏng and a wall perimeter of about 30 chō (町步) [approx. 3.27 km], but this was transferred to the jurisdiction of the Government-General at the time of annexation. Other places have also been removed from the management of the Office of the Yi Royal Household.
The palaces mentioned above are all large-scale, ranging from at least 30,000-40,000 p'yŏng to over 100,000 p'yŏng, located in important areas of Kyŏngsŏng city. Additionally, as mentioned earlier, there are 52 Royal Tombs starting from King T'aejo's Kŏnwŏllŭng, each with attached dense forests covering many tens of thousands, or even hundreds of thousands or millions of p'yŏng.

So, what is the total wealth of the Yi Royal Family? Before that, how should the wealth even be calculated?
Based on common sense, one informed source (소식통) says this:
If the land (基地) of the various palaces within Kyŏngsŏng city and other owned land totals, say, 500,000 p'yŏng, and is valued at 100 Won per p'yŏng, that alone is 50 million Won. Adding the buildings, various facilities, equipment, calligraphy, paintings, and treasures would amount to another huge sum. Then there are the forests attached to nearly 100 tombs in various regions, covering millions of chō [Note: this seems exaggerated; likely meant millions of p'yŏng or a smaller number of chō], and the vast ranch in Nangok (蘭谷), Kangwŏn Province. Thus, the total wealth of the royal family is said to be 200 million Won. Since there is no way to record this more accurately, this author can only wonder if the rumor (風說) of 200 million Won is true.
Furthermore, the annual stipend (歲費) for the Yi Royal Family is currently 1.8 million Won per year. With occasional sales of timber from the forests and other miscellaneous income (諸 雜收入) amounting to 500,000 Won annually, the total available for annual expenses is 2.3 million Won. Of this, about one-third is said to be for the salaries of the Office of the Yi Royal Household staff, and another one-third for various miscellaneous expenses (諸 雜費).
One informed source mentioned that when His Highness the Yi King traveled abroad some years ago, the travel expenses and other total costs amounted to about one million Won.
As a postscript, even now, there are several impoverished nobles (貴族) who receive monthly stipends ranging from two or three hundred Won to seven or eight hundred Won, all paid from the budget of the Office of the Yi Royal Household.
With this, I will conclude this article for now, hoping for an opportunity to write in more detail another day.<329>

**Summary**

This 1938 article describes the Office of the Yi Royal Household (Yiwangjik), established by Japan after the 1910 annexation to manage the affairs and assets of the former Korean imperial family. It details the office's structure, regulations, key personnel (a mix of Japanese and Korean officials, headed by a Japanese Director-General), and functions, including managing palaces like Ch'angdŏk Palace and Tŏksu Palace, royal tombs, performing rituals, and preserving archives and artifacts. The article attempts to estimate the Yi Royal Family's total wealth, citing rumors of 200 million Won based on vast land holdings (palace grounds, tomb forests, ranches) and stock investments, while noting the difficulty of accurate valuation. It contrasts this rumored wealth with the family's actual annual budget of 2.3 million Won (1.8 million stipend plus income), detailing expenditures like staff salaries and support for impoverished nobles.

For historians, this article offers insight into the colonial administration's handling of the former ruling dynasty. The structure of the Yiwangjik, under a Japanese Minister and headed by a high-ranking Japanese official, clearly demonstrates the subordination of the Yi house. The listing of personnel reveals the integration of Korean elites (like Yi Hang-gu, son of Yi Wan-yong) into the colonial apparatus managing the symbols of the old monarchy. The discussion of assets and budget highlights the colonial state's attempt to quantify, control, and manage the former dynasty's wealth, transforming it from sovereign patrimony into administered property, while also using it for symbolic gestures like art patronage and support for loyal nobles. The speculation about the 200 million Won figure itself reflects public curiosity and perhaps the perceived scale of the wealth appropriated or controlled by the colonial regime through the Yi family.

李王職 總財産 2億萬圓說, 李王職이란 엇든 곳인가의 解說 等
李王職이란 무엇하는 곳이요, 또 엇더한 곳인가. 세상에는 아직 여기 대한 문헌이 발표된 것이 만치 못하기에 퍽으나 궁금하게 아는 분들이 만타. 그러기에 지극히 불충분하나마 여기에 그 대체의 윤곽만이라도 전하여 보려 한다. (다른 날 더 자세한 追記를 補하련다) 즉 明治 43년 3월에 병합이 성립됨과 동시에 구 한국 황실 及 그 근친에 대하야 일본 제국 황족의 예우을 賜하시고 隆錫을 세습식히시며 병합 전과 동액의 歲費를 주시기로 되엇다. 이와 동시에 李王職 관제를 정하여 李王職을 창설하여써 왕가의 각종 사무와 재산 등의 관리를 하게 하여 금일에 이르럿다. 관제를 초록하건데

예식, 主殿課에서 하는 일들

그런데 李王職은 서울 臥龍町 2번지에 잇는데 昌德宮 구내요, 바로 敦化門으로부터 드러가면 우편에 고아한 반양식 2층집이 바로 그것이다. 그래서 李王職에서<325> 하는 諸 사무로는 李王職에는 서무, 회계, 예식, 主殿의 4課가 잇는데
禮式課에서는
1) 祝祭
2) 廟, 祭殿, 祭宮, 墳塋의 管守
3) 의식 及 餐宴
4) 빈객 접대
5) 雅樂部 가튼 데 관한 사무
主殿課에서는
1) 내전 及 仁政殿과 부속 건물의 수호
2) 내전 及 仁政殿의 供用 什器의 보관
3) 供膳
4) 診候 及 調藥
5) 소속 內人
6) 왕가의 譜牒, 詞章, 古印 등의 간수
들인데 특기할 일은 李王 전하께옵서 고래로부터 전래하는 조선 아악이 다만 구전에 의한 것으로 하등의 악보가 업는 것을 御 우려하시여서 작곡가 石川義一 씨에 명하여 昭和 2년 이래 현재 禮式課에 기터잇는 283곡을 採譜하여 모다 기록 音譜 18,427頁을 완성하여 작년 3월 27일 李王家에 詞譜 상납하엿는데 이런 것도 모다 禮式課에서 보관하는 것이요, 또 해마다 전람회 가튼데서 미술품을 御 買上하시여, 보관하는 것도 亦 禮式課의 일이니, 즉 제 16회 조선 미술 전람회에서 昌德宮賞으로 작년 5월 14일, 아래의 3賞을 발표 御 買上 하시엿다.
동양화         古談           金基昶 작
서양화         裸婦           金仁承
공예           柳京舟遊圖     山田勝二
이것도 모다 禮式課의 보관이다. 이 박게 여러 가지 일을 李王職에서는 하고 잇다.

그러고 御陵은 태조의 健元陵 이하 陵이 52가 수원, 기타 각지에 잇다.
殿陵은 조선 종래의 祭祀속에 고려조 이전의 국가의 儀制로 하여 종래의 규격에 의하여 享祀의 전례를 행하고 잇고, 殿이라 함은 上古로부터 고려조에 至하기까지의 역대 시조와 특수한 공덕이 잇는 선왕 등의 靈璽을 勸請하여 追遠報本의 誠敬을 다하는 齋場으로 그 수가 8이 잇다.
陵은 同上의 유해를 매장한 분묘로서 소재 명료한 것이 98이요, 그 가운데서 始祖陵 6처에 대하여는 年年히 춘추로 2차의 奠幣供饌의 예로서 享祀를 행한다.
단 특례 두어 가지는 駕洛國(任那)의 始祖陵 종전으로부터 魂殿享祀할 때 병합하여 此를 행함으로 陵墓祭는 따로 행치 안는 습관이다.
또 백제왕의 始祖陵은 소재불명
또 8전8능의 봉행 常時 奉仕 때문에 參奉 각 1인 설치. 참봉은 각성의 후예자로서 등용.
각 陵 奉行 감시를 엄히하고 존엄 保持 때문에 방목, 耕墾, 採樵 금기의 制禮 설치, 陵의 대소 及 원근에 의하여 수호인을 둔다.

얼마나한 재산을 관리하는가-世稱 2억만원이라 하는 說-

李王職에서 관리하는 왕가의 재산은 평가할 길이 용이치 안타. 궁전 누각가튼 것은 생각박게 제한다 할지라도 역대의 서화가튼 보물이든지, 陵所에 부튼 산림이라든지 진실로 너무 범위가 크고, 복잡하야, 아러낼 길이 용이치 안타. 그러기에 여기에서는 시정인으로 안저 누구나 평가할 수 잇는 정도의 것으로 몃가지 재료를 나열하여 보려한다.<327>

株券은 얼마나 잇는가

재산이 보관되여 잇는 길로 첫재, 유력 확실한 은행 회사에 주권을 가진 投資가 잇다.
1. 조선은행에 15만원
(즉 1주 100원인데 李王職 長官名으로 1,500주가 잇다)
2. 조선상업은행 50,500원
(조선식산은행에 1,100주를 李王職 장관 명의로 소유)
3. 동양척식주식회사
자본금 5천만원의 이 동양척식주식회사에 가장 만히 주를 가지고 잇는 것가치 예상되지만 그 금액은 정확히 알 수 업다. 원래 日露전쟁 후 日韓 양국간 관계가 경제적으로 몹시 밀접하게 되자, 그때 식산흥업을 목표 삼고 일본 정부는 給金을 下附하고, 구 한국 정부는 사업용 토지를 내엿다. 그때 李王家에서 소유 토지와 산림을 만히 내엇는데 그 토지을 주식으로 換價 소유한 것이다. 더욱 상기의 조선은행과 상업은행 分도 지물 등의 현물 투자가 대부분이요, 현금을 내고 주권을 산 것은 극히 적다고 한다.
이 밧게 은행 회사 등에 주권을 가진 것이 잇슬 줄 아나, 아직 숫자를 명확히 알지 못하엿기에 略한다.

토지 건물은 얼마나 잇는가

본고에서는 직접의 李王家 궁전 누각에는 미치지 안코 일반 세인의 이목에 비치는 부분만 摘記하기로 한다.
昌慶苑-李王家에서 경성 시민을 위하야 정원의 일부를 개방하엿는데, 원내에는 박물관, 동물원, 식물원 등이 잇다. 원래는 壽康宮이라 하며, 그 안의 明政殿 부근의 수 棟의 녯 殿址에는 新羅 이래의 古 무기, 불상, 서화 등이 진열되여 잇다. 苑內의 건물과 동식물과, 제반 시설과, 그리고 여러 누대와 기지는 그를 한 번 본 사람으로 대략은 허가케 한다.
德壽宮-원래 慶雲宮이라 칭하엿는데, 지금은 거기 새로 석조전 엽헤 박물관이 150만원을 投하야 신축되엿다. 여기에는 樂浪, 三韓의 발굴물, 新羅 시대의 불상, 석기, 서화 등이 잇다.<328>
또 이 밧게 景福宮이 잇다. 부지 면적 13만평에 달하며, 성벽 주위 약 30町步에 及하는 매우 큰 舊宮이엇스나, 이것은 병합 당시 총독부의 所轄로 옴기엿고, 그 박게 여러 곳이가 李王職의 관리를 떠난 것이 잇다.
이상의 궁전은 모다 적어서 3, 4만평 크면 10여 만평식에 달하는 대규모의 것으로, 소재지가 경성 시가의 중요지에 잇스며 이 박게 御陵이 앗가 말한 모양으로 太祖의 健元陵 이하 52처가 잇는데, 거기에는 부속으로 그 주위에 울창한 산림이 여러 만평, 여러 수십 수백만 평씩 잇다.

그러면 李王家의 총 재산은 얼마나 되는가, 그것보다도 위선 재산은 엇더케 산출할 것인가.
상식으로 판단하야 모 소식통은 이러케 말한다.
경성 시내에 잇는 여러 궁전의 基地와 그 박게의 소유 토지를 합처 가령 50만평이라 하면 매 평당 100원으로 치기로 한다면 그가 벌서 5천만원이요, 건물과 제반 설비의 기구, 서화, 보물을 치면 그 또한 거대한 금액일 것이요, 그러고 각지 근 백 處나 되는 陵에 부터잇는 산림, 수백만 町步와 강원도 蘭谷에 잇는 광대한 목장 등 이리하야 모다 왕가의 총재산을 2억만원이라 한다. 이보다 더 자세하게 記할 길 업기에, 필자 또한 2억만원이란 풍설이 그런가 할 뿐이다.
그리고 李王家의 歲費는 지금 1년 180만원인데 산림에 잇는 수목을 간간 放賣하든지, 기타 諸 잡수입이 매년 50만 잇서, 모다 230만원을 가지고서 1년간의 총경비로 쓰는데 그 중에 李王職 직원의 봉급이 약 3분지 1의 額이요, 남저지 3분지 1 額이 諸 잡비라고 한다.
年前 李王 전하께서 洋行하실 적의 여비 기타 총액이 약 백만원이 들엇다고 모 소식통은 말한다.
附記할 것은 지금도 빈한한 귀족들에게 每朔 혹은 二三백원에서부터 七八백원식 지출하는 분이 여럿이 잇는데 그 亦 모다 李王職의 예산 중에서 한다.
