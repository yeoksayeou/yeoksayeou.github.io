Magazine Title: 삼천리
Publication Date: 1938년 05월 01일
Article Title: 水仙花 -謝嘉藍 贈 水仙花 漢詩 譯- (Narcissus - Translation of Sa Karam's Gifted Narcissus Chinese Poem -)
Author: 月灘
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

In pebbles, a white porcelain jar,
The Narcissus (水仙) came smiling.
Quietly parting the December blizzard,
Daintily, as if shy, it came seeking, head bowed.

At the scholar's place by the cold window (寒窓),
What is this meaning of spring?
As if drunk, as if seeing a beauty,
Like a bachelor, so bashful!

Gently, gently holding <256>
The green stem, the white cheek,
The clear, pure fragrance
Brushes the nose and whispers.

The meaning of spring—living and smiling spring—
Even within this jade-like child,
Lives on without fading.
Why must it be when the moon is dim at dusk
That the subtle fragrance (暗香) rises?
The meaning behind my friend sending this
I now understand again.
-(Chŏngch'uk, Old Lunar December, Tae Maeil) -<257>

**Summary**

This poem by Wŏlt'an is a translation of a Chinese poem about a narcissus flower, gifted by someone named Sa Karam. The poet contemplates the flower, which arrived blooming despite the winter cold ("December blizzard"). Its delicate beauty and fragrance evoke thoughts of spring's resilience, even in hardship, reminding the poet of the sender's thoughtful intention. Historians might find this poem interesting as it reflects the continuation of traditional East Asian poetic exchange (gift poems, translation of Hanmun) and aesthetic sensibilities (appreciating nature, scholar's symbols like 寒窓) among Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. The reference to Sa Karam and the specific dating (late 1937 lunar calendar) provide concrete context.

水仙花 -謝嘉藍 贈 水仙花 漢詩 譯-
						月灘
조약돌, 백자기에
水仙이 웃고 왓네.
섯달 눈보라를 가만이 헤치고서
아리잠직, 붓그런 듯 고개숙여 차저왓네.

寒窓 선비 자리
봄 뜻이 웬일이오.
취한 듯 미인보듯
총각가티 수집구야.

푸른 줄기 하연 뺌을
고히고히 안어 보니<256>
바자운 맑은 향기
코를 스처 속삭인다.

봄-살고 웃는 봄의 뜻은
옥동가튼 이 속에도
슬지안코 살어 있네.
하필시 월황혼이
暗香도든 때라드냐.
벗있서 보내신 뜻
이제 다시 깨달엇네.
-(丁丑 舊臘 大 每日) -<257>
