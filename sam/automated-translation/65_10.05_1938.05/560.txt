Magazine Title: 삼천리 제10권 제5호
Publication Date: 1938-05-01
Article Title: 女流 文士의 『戀愛 問題』會議 (Meeting of Female Writers on the ‘Love Problem’)
Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Panel Discussion / Roundtable Talk)

**Meeting of Female Writers on the ‘Love Problem’**
**Attendees**

No Ch'ŏnmyŏng
Yi Sŏnhŭi
Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi
Mo Yunsuk

**Discussion Topics Summary**

1) Critique of Mrs. Simpson’s Love Affair
2) How to View Love Suicides and Suicide
3) Is the Path of New Love Spiritual or Physical?
4) Love Triangles and the Handling of Children
5) Why is it said that ‘Beauty Fades Quickly’ (美人薄命)?
6) How Should the New Love Be Designed?

**Critique of Mrs. Simpson’s Love Affair**

O Kim Tonghwan - Tonight, let’s boldly critique various real-life issues, such as the Na Hyesŏk affair that actually happened in our society, or the Yun Simdŏk, Pae Kuja, Kang Hyangnan incidents, the Arishima Takeo and Akiko incident in Tokyo, the affair involving the exchange of a wife between Tanizaki and Satō in Osaka, and even more recently, the Mrs. Simpson affair that caused a worldwide stir.
First, regarding Mrs. Simpson, the world calls her the most foolish woman who engaged in love for fame (賣名的), while others see her as a brave woman who followed the path of love most justly. Which opinion do you side with?
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - She finally obtained the lover she desired as she wished, so I see her as someone who was most faithful to her own passion, and also as a woman who walked the right path. It only became an issue because, by chance, her lover was a man wearing a crown who had to deeply respect the traditions of the Imperial Family and the sentiments of the people. From the start, conditions like the title of king, money, and honor <309> likely held no calculation in Mrs. Simpson's eyes; she desired only the single, bare man named Edward, and succeeded in that love. What path of love could be more pure and passionate than this? Mrs. Simpson was a splendid victor on the ‘path of love.’ Since she is a victor, her judgment and actions are ultimately correct.
O Mo Yunsuk - However, I don’t see it that way. While love should certainly be sacred, Mrs. Simpson’s actions seem very selfish, calculating, and impure. Why is that? Let me list a few reasons, please listen.
First, Mrs. Simpson is a woman who has already changed husbands twice. Physically, she had already lost her purity and likely lacked the noble sentiment of a chaste virgin protecting her body. Consequently, she probably didn't value the sanctity of the body that highly and likely treated Edward similarly to her previous husband or the husband before that. Therefore, I cannot see her spirit of love as having started from a high and pure place.
Second, she seems like a woman who loves calculatingly. In anyone’s life, how can one expect never to make a single mistake? But if she had already failed in her first marriage, she should have planned meticulously for her second marriage to remarry and ensure she wouldn't change husbands again. Yet, even while living with man A, if she discovered partially better aspects in man B, she would immediately move there, and then move again... In this way, like a butterfly flitting from flower to flower, always searching for a slightly better one, she constantly moves from one man's embrace to another. She has the nature of a prostitute (娼婦性). That's why I don't trust Mrs. Simpson. If a man superior to Edward appears before her, she will surely divorce again and marry another man.
Third, although love should be supreme (至上), that supremacy, as long as we are members of society and citizens of a nation, must exist above the laws of that nation and the rules of that society. I believe the path of love that completely ignores the interests of the state or society, just doing what one pleases, is not a path we should take. <310>
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - If that’s the case, considering that the state and society always prioritize tradition and practical interests, how can we young men and women walk the path of new love according to our unrestrained individuality without disturbing the order of that state and society in the slightest?
O Mo Yunsuk - That's not what I meant. My point is this. Mrs. Simpson's lover was not an ordinary man in the street but the monarch (軍主) ruling a nation. The general public sentiment was that they wished for this emperor, having ascended the throne, to rule the country for a long, long time. To achieve this, they simply wished he would give up this foreign (異邦) American woman who had remarried two or three times. That is, for the sake of the British state and its people, Mrs. Simpson should have quickly stepped back. But Simpson trampled on the prosperity of the British Imperial Family (英 帝室) and the feelings of the entire British populace, ultimately satisfying her own selfish desires. Can love supremacism stand even at the sacrifice of the interests of the state and society? I don't think so.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - Is that really Simpson's fault? Isn't it the fault of the man, Edward? In any situation, isn't the active party the man? Therefore, if there's anyone to blame in this love affair, it would surely be Edward himself.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - Ms. Yunsuk, then in such a case – when two people are deeply entangled in a love they cannot break – what path could Simpson have taken? Without harming (損치) the interests of the state and society...
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - Are you saying she should kill herself? That Simpson should commit suicide?
O Mo Yunsuk - Even without going as far as suicide, there might have been other ways.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - For example?
O Mo Yunsuk - Temporarily retreating, perhaps going to a convent... Until those agitated feelings subside...
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - That’s a love of religious endurance (忍從). To love for others while killing oneself... Trying to put a mask of hypocrisy even on individual love, which should be the most conscientious, the most pure...
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - In short, you're ordering self-sacrifice. If that love wasn't a temporary agitation but something of a nature that wouldn't break (awaken) beyond that forever – that is, if it was a love that stepped forward <311> from the very beginning based on the most intelligent and rational life plan, how could there be a hesitation (猶豫) that kills oneself in the process? That would be opportunism in love; wouldn't that be toying with love emotions rather than love itself?
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - One party is a man well over 40, whose mind is as mature as can be; the other is a woman who well understands the experiences of real society and the subtleties of human feelings. How could there possibly be room for flawed judgment based on agitated emotions between them?
O Kim Tonghwan - This is becoming quite theoretical. Let's not do that, how about Ms. Yi Sŏnhŭi critiques?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - In my opinion, Ms. Mo Yunsuk likely thinks, what individual victory in love can there be when the happiness of the state or society is breaking apart? Strive to harmonize so that the happiness of the greater self (大我) and the smaller self (小我) can proceed side by side, sometimes necessitating the abandonment of the smaller self temporarily or partially. Applying this to Simpson's case, if only she weren't there, if only she weren't seen, wouldn't the tragedy of Edward's abdication have been avoided? At such a time, Simpson's heart would surely ache unbearably, feeling a void as if the whole world were lost, but she should endure it as a citizen's duty. The argument of Ms. No Ch'ŏnmyŏng and Ms. Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi seems to be that the emperor, money, status, fame, etc., are all external conditions. Since they are not absolute, one must sometimes bravely proceed in the manner of 'The world is not important, only I am important' (天下不重, 我惟重) to save oneself and preserve one's love.
So, in my view, in our current society where freedom of sex and economic freedom are hard to fully obtain, there will be many cases where one cannot go so far as to trample on the laws of the state or society. If one tries to tread the path of love in such an absolute, uncompromising way, saying there is nothing but love, knowing nothing but the happiness of the two individuals, eventually, the only remaining methods will be a love suicide (情死) or suicide. I think it's all a matter of degree.

**How to View Love Suicides and Suicide?**

O Kim Tonghwan - Then let's change the subject. Is it a sin for people in love <312> to commit a love suicide (情死)? Is it a sin to commit suicide, loving and loving, in order not to lose that love forever? Were Yun Simdŏk or Kang Hyangnan (the famous kisaeng who committed love suicide with Chang Pyŏngt'ien) people who committed sins? Should the ‘Hymn of Death’ not exist? To prepare the design for the new path of new love we are to establish from now on, let's discuss this issue once.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - I affirm death in cases like D'Annunzio's ‘Triumph of Death’. I also affirm a death like that of Sŏngsun in Ch'unwŏn's *Kaecheokcha* (The Pioneer).
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - I disagree, strongly disagree. Why die? Why end it with death? Why are people born and live in the world? It is solely to obtain happiness. Why do people pursue love while fighting against surrounding opposition? That too is all for the sake of obtaining happiness while alive on this earth. But happiness must be something real, something we can feel with our five senses. So what is dying? It is disposing of happiness oneself. Is there any reason for a land of blessings (福地) like Canaan, flowing with the milk of happiness, to exist beyond this world? One must live. Isn't it only while living that love exists and happiness can be obtained?
O Mo Yunsuk - That may be the ideal everyone desires. However, in reality, if there is absolutely no way for that love to be achieved, meaning if there is no way to attain a complete love of spiritual and physical unity (靈肉一致) with lasting continuity, then perhaps people flee to the path of love suicide or suicide to seek at least spiritual or ethereal affection, even without physical pleasure.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - That's right. Whether love suicide or suicide is right or wrong is a separate issue, but one can fully understand the psychology of the men and women who choose the path of death.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - I don't think so. If worst comes to worst and there are many obstacles, one can simply leave that environment. One should take the man's hand and go live even on a deserted island, a solitary island in the distant sea. Live and overcome.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - To go to a deserted island, one would have to abandon one's beloved first child, abandon pitiful parents, abandon all modern culture, and abandon the homeland where one was born and raised. Could one endure that suffering, that tragedy, not for a day or two, but for 10 or 20 years? <313> Since it's unbearable, they end up drowning themselves in the Hyŏnhae Strait (Genkai Nada) from the start or hanging themselves with a sash from the rafters of someone else's villa in Karuizawa. And when a person's life is burning with some great emotion, it seems they can transcend life and death. It seems they might even reach the state of ‘Life and Death are One’ (生死一如) that high monks speak of.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - This Ch'oe has a considerable risk of suicide... (All laugh)
O Kim Tonghwan - Let’s take the love of ‘Ch'unhyang’. Ch'unhyang did not say ‘I will not serve two husbands (不更二夫) – kill me, new magistrate!’ because of the outdated morals learned from elementary school texts or the Four Books and Five Classics, but rather, I see her as a woman of passion who resolved to die purely out of innocent love for Yi Mongnyong. If Yi Mongnyong's passing the state examination, like a dream in the ‘Legend of Ch'unhyang’, hadn't happened, wouldn't suicide have been the best path for Ch'unhyang in the end? And wouldn't it have been right for Mongnyong, who visited her in prison, to commit a love suicide with Ch'unhyang then? If they hadn't, wouldn't Ch'unhyang and Yi Toryŏng both just be a common prostitute and libertine?
O Mo Yunsuk - That's right, they should have died.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - The path of death would likely be the happiest path for the two of them.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - When the sanctity and dignity of the self are utterly trampled, there must be an elevation of the spirit (靈). That elevation could be said to be nothing but a leap towards Death (死).

**Is the Path of New Love Spiritual or Physical?**

O Kim Tonghwan - Observing the process of love among women of the new era these days, perhaps it's unavoidable as the whole world (一世 滔滔) leans towards materialism, but aren't most cases driven not by a longing for a higher spiritual life leading them to the path of love, but simply by 'sex'? That is, unable to overcome carnal desire (春情), they rush towards the opposite sex? Is it due to longing for an extremely realistic, extremely pleasure-seeking sensory life?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - The primary characteristic of modern love seems to be speedism. Hesitating, thinking, being shy, dithering – they quickly pass through <314> that ‘bashful stage (羞態 時機)’ and discard it, then immediately take action. It's thoroughgoing actionism. If a man who attracts them appears, they rather create opportunities for self-expression, become proactive in approaching him, reply immediately if a letter arrives, become bolder if a rival appears, make it routine to check the family register at the Kyŏngsŏng prefectural office – isn't it all like this? Therefore, observing from the side, they appear lively, and one praises their courage. On the other hand, there's a constant, precarious anxiety, making one feel dizzy and worried. It's like entrusting a long knife (長刀) to a small child. And the second characteristic is hedonism.
O Kim Tonghwan - How so?
O Mo Yunsuk - Well, there's a tendency to place too much importance on modern conditions. Lamenting from personal experience that their real lives as female students were not affluent because their older brothers or fathers were poor, they come to realize they must marry into a wealthy family. Therefore, while in the past, men's looks and style were considered, now money seems to be the top priority. It's the heyday of ‘Americanism’. Having lots of money for a nice house, good food, nice clothes, and traveling to scenic mountains and rivers when bored – they try to find happiness within this.
If there's anything they look for besides money, it seems to be only the man's health status. As long as he's a person unlikely to fall ill and die, that seems sufficient. <315>
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - While such characteristics of the times exist among women of marriageable age, there is also a group unable to escape the aestheticist trend. Romance over money, dreams over money – like the love in the back alleys depicted in ‘Quatorze Juillet’ (巴里祭), or like in ‘The Lower Depths’ (トン底), where the fragrance of love blooms amidst extremely miserable real life – there is also a group pursuing this sort of thing. These are the factions who understand what spiritual life is and have already realized that money and life's happiness often do not go hand in hand.
O Kim Tonghwan - Generally speaking, aren't female students of this era physically maturing faster?
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - Remarkably fast. It seems due to the enthusiasm for physical education in schools. Compared to when we were students, the speed of female students' maturity in the last 5-6 years has been astonishingly rapid.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - Their emotional lives, too, refined by theater and film, tend to preemptively avoid deep anguish, seeking the freedom to live as they please. Thus, their physical and emotional development seems leagues apart (天壤之差), quite speedy compared to our student days.
O Kim Tonghwan - Are there still many instances of experiencing the profound feeling once called ‘unrequited love (짝사랑)’?
O Mo Yunsuk - ‘Unrequited love’ is when one person feels affection and gets worked up all alone, even though the other person doesn't love them back. Isn't all that a relic of the past century? Can one sustain a love emotion for long if there's no response from the other side? Women of this era would probably settle it immediately. By their own willpower.

**Love Triangles and the Handling of Children**

O Kim Tonghwan - Works like ‘Anna Karenina’ or ‘The Sorrows of Young Werther’ are all tragedies arising from the collapse (붕괴) of love triangles. To overcome this tragedy, isn't economic independence required for both parties? How do you view this point?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - If both sides had economic independence, I don't believe the tragedy of the love triangle would be completely eradicated, <316> but it would surely diminish significantly. The frequency would decrease, and the pain suffered would be considerably less, I think.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - In relationships between the sexes, I don't think the emergence of a love triangle necessarily demands the sacrifice of one party by another. If there isn't an aspect forcing sacrifice, couldn't it end without tragedy? For instance, between a couple whose affection is cooling, like in the case of ‘My Husband’s Chastity (良人の貞操)’ reported in the Osaka Mainichi. In such cases, I mean. If issues of livelihood and money aren't involved, wouldn't everyone become independent individuals capable of taking free action according to their own judgment <317>? In that case, wouldn't cheap tragedy be excluded, and instead, wouldn't life deepen and become purified?
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - However, when there are children, sometimes money alone cannot resolve the situation. Affection for the children – the tragedy arising from maternal love – this is a matter of human essence, so it's a more absolute, more unavoidable, fated tragedy that cannot be glossed over (マギラワスル - Japanese 'magirawasu') by the amount of money.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - Therefore, when the tragedy of divorce occurs between a couple, the mother should always take responsibility for the children, raise and educate them.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - But reflecting on the reality of our society, since mothers lack economic power, even though their maternal love is strong, for the future upbringing and education of their children, they end up enduring with tears (含淚而忍從) and entrusting their children to the father, who has already become a stranger. Isn't this necessary so that the tragedy the mother experienced is not extended to the children?
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - If so, wouldn't the tragedy of 'being torn apart while alive' (生さゐ仲 - nama-sai-naka? Possibly 生き裂き仲 - ikisaki-naka, meaning a relationship torn apart while both are alive) occur more often? Entrusting the fate of one's own child entirely to another woman, a stranger, moreover one who is essentially an enemy (仇敵) in the context of the love relationship – isn't that rather creating a double tragedy?
O Mo Yunsuk - If not, the woman would have to pioneer a path to economic self-reliance. But in the current society, that's a standard (規準) applicable only to a few percent of women; it probably won't become a theory with universality.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - I know quite a few women who passively continue a impure, castrated marital relationship for the sake of their children's future.
O Kim Tonghwan - Love triangles – in other words, if your husband developed another lover, what attitude would you take?
O Mo Yunsuk - I would withdraw. Because I love my husband deeply, wishing for greater happiness to come to him than I can offer, I would quietly remove myself. I would resign myself.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - Self-sacrifice, I see. The pain must be immense.
O Mo Yunsuk - Of course, the pain, agony, and bitterness would be incomparable to any other suffering, <318> but one must be prepared beforehand to bear a cross of that magnitude.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - But will there ever be a day when that suffering is resolved?
O Mo Yunsuk - Perhaps the day of resolution will never come.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - Then eventually, you'll just wither away alone...
O Kim Tonghwan - In that case, Ms. Sŏnhŭi, what would you do?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - I'd kill the husband. Because I truly love him, I'd kill him.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - Fierce! But it seems that might be the only way for a woman. Giving up (アキラメ - Japanese 'akirame') doesn't seem likely; withdrawing oneself or sacrificing oneself silently is utterly unthinkable.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - They say love and hate are one. Extreme love would turn into extreme hatred. Having reached the extreme, there would likely be no room to consider the destruction or otherwise of happiness designed by involving another woman of the person one loves. Whatever the theory, human emotion is...
O Mo Yunsuk - One can withdraw. Just as a saint avoids all evil by leaving the place, one can quietly withdraw. And then, isn't it enough to shift to feelings of Platonic love? It seems these ideas arise because everyone is excessively drawn to carnal desire (肉慾). Isn't there a poem by Kunikida Doppo (國木田獨步):
"Middle of the night, three AM
I sit up alone
Kneel and pray.
That happiness may come
To the body of him who left me - -"
Shouldn't we, as cultured women, naturally possess this level of noble sentiment?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - You have many dreams-. The boundary between reality and illusion (夢幻) is always touching. Are you planning to live by eating rainbows? Haha. (All laugh)

**Why is it said that ‘Beauty Fades Quickly’ (美人 薄命)?**

O Kim Tonghwan - The saying ‘Beauty Fades Quickly’ (美人 薄命 - miin pangmyŏng) is not just a poetic fantasy but can be found in various facts we see and hear <319> daily. How do you ladies view this?
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - Like a single peony blooming amidst wildflowers, a beautiful woman's existence stands out most prominently. Therefore, many naturally become interested in the fate of that flower, and consequently, many try to pick that flower, to touch that flower, so her lifespan naturally shortens.
O Kim Tonghwan - More than that point, just as the deeper the sea, the larger the waves in its heart (海心), and the higher the mountain range, the more volcanic areas there are, isn't it that those who are outstandingly beautiful inherently (先天的) possess a passionate and intense aspect in their hearts not found in other women? Like a moth flying into a lamp flame and burning itself to death, because that burning emotion hastens its sublimation, don't they naturally meet an early demise (薄命)? Just as flowers that bloom too vividly fall quickly, and the full moon that shines too brightly wanes quickly -.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - Because she is beautiful, she would feel pride (プ라우ド - Japanese 'puraido' from English 'pride'). Because she is beautiful, her self-confidence (自信力) would be strong, but I don't think that just because beauty is abundant, other passions, blood, and the amount of tears would increase in direct proportion. I don't see beautiful women as having such pathologically abnormal characteristics.
O Kim Tonghwan - Isn't their desire to conquer men stronger than that of ordinary women? Like Cleopatra wanting to conquer heroes like Caesar and Antony, or Catherine (카자린) or Empress Dowager Cixi (西太后) wanting to suppress all the talents of the court and country? Because they consciously possess more of this desire for conquest than others, the opportunities for physiological depletion naturally increase, and also, due to the anxiety, unease, and anguish that conquerors possess mentally, don't they shorten their own lives (스스로 단명)?
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - What every woman sits and thinks about is, ‘Will I live long?’ or ‘Even if short-lived, should I live fervently (灼熱的)?’ It seems that beautiful people often choose the latter path.

**How Should the New Love Be Designed?** <320>

O Kim Tonghwan - Now, to establish new morals and standards (規準) for new love in the new era, what prerequisite conditions do you think should be cultivated first?
O Mo Yunsuk - Just as all human desires wish for everyone without exception to become rich and live well, in this matter of sexual morality too, cutting off the various constrained unnaturalnesses first seems to be the prerequisite issue. For example, opening the path to economic self-reliance for women, recognizing property rights, enabling them to resolve matters like child-rearing solely with the mother's power using money, and also enabling them to completely escape the status of economic slaves to their husbands upon divorce -.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - And also, the civil register (民籍) should be amended so that child dependency can be attached to the mother's side as well. Rather, it should be left to the child's free will, allowing them to go to the father if they wish or to the mother if they wish.
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - In any case, the path of new love must be based on gender equality. For that, equality in practice – sexual equality, economic equality, various equalities in the social system – must all be established first, I believe.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - That's right. And based on mutual understanding and tolerant feelings, love morals suited to the new society should be established, I think.
O Kim Tonghwan - Why are there no spectacular love romances that startle the world in our society lately?
O Yi Sŏnhŭi - Perhaps it's because life has deepened, and love mixed with playful emotions, as if done to show off to the world, has disappeared.
O Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi - As the breath of the era becomes rapid and the social situation becomes critical (深切), issues like love problems are relegated to secondary or tertiary importance, and everyone has become engrossed in various practical difficulties...
O Mo Yunsuk - Viewed broadly, it might be a symbol that the general mood (風情) of the times is purifying.
O No Ch'ŏnmyŏng - That's right. Because our new life design must be solemn and grounded in reality, wherever it may lead.
O Kim Tonghwan - Thank you for speaking for such a long time. I would like to create another opportunity in the future to discuss this issue further. <321>

**Summary**

Four prominent Korean female writers in 1938 discuss contemporary issues of love, marriage, and women's societal roles. They debate the controversial Wallis Simpson affair, the morality of love suicides (like Yun Simdŏk's), whether modern love prioritizes spirit or flesh (often driven by materialism or hedonism), the challenges of love triangles and child custody (highlighting women's economic dependence), the trope of "beauty fades quickly," and the prerequisites for a "new love" in a new era, emphasizing the need for women's economic independence and legal equality. The conversation reveals a tension between individual desire/passion (No Ch'ŏnmyŏng, sometimes Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi), social/moral obligations (Mo Yunsuk), and pragmatic realities (Yi Sŏnhŭi, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi).

Historians might find this article particularly interesting for its candid portrayal of educated women's perspectives on evolving notions of love and relationships in colonial Korea, navigating traditional constraints, modern influences (Western affairs, Japanese literature/events), and emerging feminist consciousness. The explicit discussion of economic dependence as a core obstacle to women's freedom in love and marriage, the differing views on self-sacrifice versus self-assertion, and the references to specific contemporary scandals (Na Hyesŏk, Yun Simdŏk) provide valuable insight into the social and intellectual climate for women during this period. The mix of Korean traditional thought, modern aspirations, and awareness of international affairs is also notable.

심푸손 夫人의 戀愛 사건 批判

O 金東煥 - 오늘 저녁은 우리 사회에 실제로 잇섯든 저 羅蕙錫 사건이라든지, 尹心悳, 裵龜子, 姜香蘭 사건이라든지, 또 동경 大阪 등지에서 이러난 有島武郞 對 秋子 사건, 谷崎佐藤間의 처녀 양도 사건, 더욱 갓가히는 세계를 떠들썩하든 저 심푸손 부인 사건 등 현실적 여러 문제를 모조리 대담하게 비판하여 보기로 합세다.
첫재로 심푸손을 세상에서는 제일 못난 賣名的 연애를 한 여자라고도 하고 다른 한 편으로는 가장 정당하게 사랑의 길을 밟어간 용감한 여성이라고도 하는데 여러분은 어느 의견에 가담하서요?
O 盧天命 - 엇고 십든 애인을 제 마음대로 끗끗내 엇고 마럿스니 그는 자기 정열에 가장 충실하엿슬뿐더러 또한 한 여성으로서 바른 길을 밟은 이라고 보아저요. 다만 우연히도 그 애인된 남성이 帝室의 전통과 국민의 감정을 몹시 존중하여야 할 왕관을 쓴 어른이매 새삼스럽게도 문제되엿슬 따름으로 처음부터 심푸손 부인의 눈 압헤 왕이란 지위와 돈과 명예가튼 모든 부수<309> 조건이 조곰치도 계산에 들지 안코서 오직 단 하나 赤裸한 에드워- 드란 남성을 그리워하야 그 연애에 성공한 것일 것이니 이러케 순정적이요, 정열적인 정당한 연애의 길이 어디 잇겟서요. 심푸손 부인은 「연애의 길」에서 보는 한 훌용한 승리자엿지요. 승리자이니까 그의 한 판단과 행위는 결국 다 올해저요.
O 毛允淑 - 그러나 나는 그러케 보지를 안어요. 연애가 신성하여야 할 것임은 물론이나 심푸손 부인의 한 행위는 몹시 이기적이요, 타산적이요, 또 불순하게 보여요. 웨 그런가하면 내가 몃 가지 이유를 들어볼 터이니 자아 들어보서요.
첫재 심푸손 부인은 두 번이나 이미 남편을 갈어대인 여성이매 그는 육체적으로도 벌서 순결을 일어서 깨끗한 처녀가 제 육체를 앗기고 옹호하는 것 가튼 고급한 감정이 업섯슬 것이며, 따라서 육체의 신성을 그러케 놉히 평가하지 안엇슬 것이길내, 에드워- 드도 前夫나, 그 前前에 가치 살든 남편 정도로 취급하고서 대하엿슬 것이니, 그의 연애 정신이 놉고 깨끗한 데서 출발하엿다고 볼 수가 업고,
둘재는 그는 타산적으로 연애하는 여성갓해요. 사람의 생활치고, 엇더케 한번의 失行조차 업기를 바라겟어요만은 이미 첫 결혼에 실패하엿다면 제 2차의 결혼 때에는 周到한 설계로써 改嫁하여, 다시 두 번 그 남편을 밧구지 말도록 하여야 할 터인데, 그는 갑이란 남자와 함께 살다도 부분적으로 을이란 남자에게서 더 조흔 점을 발견한다면 곳 그리로 옴기고 또 옴기고... 이리하야 나비가 이 꽂 저 꽂, 조곰 더 나은 꽂을 차저 작고 헤매이듯 남성에게서 남성의 품으로 작고 이동하지요. 娼婦性을 띄엇지요. 그러길내 심푸손 부인은 미더지지 안어요. 그는 이제 에드워- 드 이상의 남자가 자기 압헤 나타나는 날, 반드시 또 다시 이혼하고 다른 남성과 결혼을 할 것임니다.
셋재는 연애는 비록 至上하여야 할 것이로되 그 至上이란 우리가 사회인이요, 한 나라의 국민인 이상 그 나라 국법과 그 사회의 율법 우에서만 하여야 할 것이겟는데 즉 국가나 사회의 이해를 전연 무시하고서 저만 조흐면 한다하는 연애의 길은 우리로써 취할 길이 아닌 줄 아러요.<310>
O 崔貞熙 - 그러다면 국가나 사회는 언제든 전통과 현실상의 이익만을 존중하는 것인데 그 국가와 사회의 질서를 조곰치도 허트러노치 안고서 放奔한 개성대로의 신 연애의 길을 우리들 청년 남녀가 엇더케 거러갈 수 잇슬가요.
O 毛允淑 - 그런 의미인 것이 안니라 내 말한 뜻은 이러해요. 심푸손 부인의 애인이 보통 시정에 잇는 사람이 아니고 일국을 통솔하는 군주엿섯는데, 일반 국민의 감정은 이 황제가등극한대로 오래오래 그 국가를 통치하여 주기를 바랫는데, 다만 그러기 위하연 異邦의 아메리카의 재혼 삼혼하든 이런 여자를 단념하여 주기를 바랫슬 뿐이엇지요, 즉 英吉利 국가와 국민을 위하야 심푸손 여사가 어서 급히 물너가야 올햇겟는데 심푸손은 英 帝室의 번영과 전 영국민의 감정을 유린하고, 기어나 제 사욕을 채엇지요. 국가와 사회의 이익을 희생하고까지, 연애 지상주의란 설 것일가요? 나는 그러케는 생각되지 안어요.
O 盧天命 - 그야 어듸 심푸손의 잘못인가요. 에드워- 드란 남성의 잘못이지요. 엇든 경우에든지 능동적임은 남성편이 아닌가요? 그러기에 이번 연애 사건에서 責할 사람이 잇다면 그는 에드워- 드 그 사람 뿐이겟지요.
O 崔貞熙 - 允淑 군은 그러면 이런 경우에 - 즉 떼랴도 뗄 수 업는 애욕의 속에 두 남녀가 무처 잇슬 때에, 심푸손으로서 취할 수 잇슬 길이란 엇든 길인가요. 국가와 사회의 이익을 損치 안으면서...
O 盧天命 - 저를 죽이란 말인가요. 심푸손더러 자살하란 말인가요?
O 毛允淑 - 자살까지 안 가더래도 다른 길이 잇겟지요.
O 盧天命 - 가령?
O 毛允淑 - 일시 피하여 수도원으로 제가 가든지... 亢奮된 그 감정이 가라안즐 때까지...
O 盧天命 - 그는 종교적 忍從의 연애지요. 저를 죽이면서 남을 위해 사랑하라고... 가장 양심적이여야 할, 가장 純一하여야 할 개인의 연애에까지 위선의 탈을 씨우려하는...
O 崔貞熙 - 요컨대 자기 희생을 명령하는 것이구려. 만일에 그 연애가 일시적의 亢奮이 아니어서 영원히 그 이상 더 깨여질(覺) 성질의 것이 아니라면, 즉 가장 총명하고 이지적인 생활의 설계 우에서 첫거름부터 드듸어<311> 나오든 연애라면 엇더케 그 사이에 저를 죽이는 猶豫가 잇슬 수  잇겟서요. 그는 연애의 기회주의요, 그는 연애가 아니라 연애 감정의 농락이 아닐가요.
O 盧天命 - 한쪽은 40이 다 넘어 머리가 성숙될대로 성숙된 남성, 한쪽은 실사회의 경험과 인정의 기미를 잘 아는 여성, 그 사이에 엇더케 亢奮된 감정으로 하는 잘못된 판단이 끼울 여지가 잇섯겟서요?
O 金東煥 - 대단히 이론화하여 가지는데 그러지 말고 어디 李善熙씨 비판하시구려.
O 李善熙 - 내 생각에는 아마 毛允淑 군은 국가나 사회의 행복이 깨여지는 판에, 무삼 개인의 연애의 승리인들 잇스랴, 되도록 큰 大我의 행복과 적은 小我의 행복이 병행되어 가도록 조화되기를 힘써서, 일시적 부분적으로 小我을 버릴 경우도 잇슬 것이니 심푸손 경우에 비처보면 저만 업스면, 저만 보이지 말면, 에드워- 드께서 퇴위라는 비극에까지 아니가고 끗날 것이 아니냐, 그럴 때에 무론 심푸손의 가슴은 못견대게 앞으고 이 세상을 다 일는듯한 공허를 늣끼겟지만 그는 국민된 의무로써 참아야 올타함이고, 盧天命, 崔貞熙 두 분의 說은 황제, 돈, 지위, 명망 등은 다 외부적 조건이니 그가 이미 절대적인 것이 아닌 이상 저를 살니기 위하여 때로는 天下不重, 我惟重이란 식으로 용감하게 나가 자기 사랑을 살려야 한다는 것으로 주장하는 것 갓구만요.
그러니 내 생각으로는 성의 자유, 경제의 자유가 마음대로 다 엇기 어려운 우리의 현실 사회에서는 국가나 사회의 율법을 즛밟고까지 나갈 수 업는 경우가 만흘줄 아러요. 그러케 연애 이외에는 아모것도 업다, 두 남녀의 제 행복 이외에는 아모것도 모른다하는 식으로 절대  비타협적으로 연애의 길을 발버 나가자면 결국은 그는 情死나 자살의 방법밧게 남지 안을걸요. 모도 다 정도 문제인 줄 알어요.

情死와 自殺을 엇더케 보는가?

O 金東煥 - 그러면 문제를 밧구어서, 사랑하는 사람끼리<312> 情死하는 것이 죄일까요? 사랑하다가 사랑하다가 그 사랑을 기리 일치 말여고 자살하는 것이 죄일가요? 尹心悳이나 姜香蘭 (張炳天이와 정사한 명기)이나 다 죄를 저질는 사람들일가요. 「死의 讚美」란 잇서서는 안될 것인가요. 이제부터 새로 세워나갈 신 연애의 신 道의 그 설계를 만련하기 위하여, 이 문제를 우리 한 번 토의하여 봅시다.
O 李善熙 - 딴눈치오가 그린 「死의 勝利」와 가튼 경우의 死는 저는 긍정하여요. 春園의 開拓者에 나오는 性涥이 가튼 死도 저는 긍정하여요.
