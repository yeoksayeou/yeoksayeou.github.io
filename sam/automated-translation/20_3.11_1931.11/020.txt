Urgent Notice: Let's Save Our Compatriots in Manchuria!

According to reports dated October 28th in the Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, and Ōsaka Asahi Shinbun, around 100,000 Koreans have fled from various parts of North Manchuria (北滿) and gathered: 50,000 in Pongch'ŏn (奉天), 20,000 in Changch'un (長春), 3,000 in Taeryŏn (大連), and several thousand each in Musun (撫順) and Andong (安東). Besides these, about one million Koreans remain scattered throughout Manchuria and Mongolia (滿蒙), reportedly weeping from hunger and cold (飢寒) in this chilly season as the snow blows.

Ah, blood is thicker than water! O fellow Koreans, let us send one set of clothes, one *doe* of rice for these disaster-stricken (罹災) brothers and sisters in a foreign land. Your single teardrop, your single coin, can save the lives of our compatriots on the brink of starvation. Please, quickly! Please, regardless of the amount, donate money and goods right away.

November 1st
Kyŏngsŏng (京城) Samch'ŏlli Sa (三千里社), Paek (白)

**Summary**

This article is an urgent appeal published by the magazine *Samch'ŏlli Sa* in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul) on November 1, 1931. Citing major newspapers, it reports that around 100,000 Korean refugees have fled North Manchuria to major cities like Pongch'ŏn (Fengtian/Shenyang) and Changch'un following recent events (implicitly, the Manchurian Incident). It highlights the plight of these refugees and the approximately one million Koreans remaining in Manchuria, facing hunger and cold as winter approaches. The author makes an emotional plea based on ethnic solidarity ("Blood is thicker than water") for readers in Korea to donate clothing, rice, and money to save their compatriots abroad. For historians, this piece is significant as it provides a contemporary Korean perspective on the immediate humanitarian crisis affecting Korean settlers in Manchuria following the Japanese invasion. It underscores the scale of displacement, the reliance on Korean newspapers for information, the role of civic organizations like *Samch'ŏlli Sa* in mobilizing aid, and the strong sense of ethnic kinship invoked during a crisis in the colonial era.

急告, 在滿同胞을 救합시다
10월 28일 附 東亞, 朝鮮日報 及 大阪朝日新聞所報에 의하면 奉天에 5만명, 長春에 2만명, 大連에 3천명, 撫順, 安東에 각각 수천명식 모다 십만여의 조선인이 北滿 각처로부터 피난하여 와 잇고 그 밧게도 滿蒙 각지에 약 백만의 조선인이 그냥 남어 잇서 눈바래 날니는 이 치운 철에 飢寒에 울고 잇다 함니다.
아하, 피는 물보다 더 검니다. 동족인 당신이시어 한 벌 옷, 한 되 쌀을 외지의 이 罹災 형제를 위하야 보내 드립시다. 당신의 한방울의 눈물 한 푼의 동전이 기아선상에 선 동족의 생명을 구할 수 잇는 것이외다. 어서 하로 급히! 어서 다소를 불구하고 금품를 던저주시압.
11월 1일
京城 三千里社 白
