A Study of "Japanese Attitudes Towards the Korean Problem"
						Kim Tong-hwan

What kind of attitude, what kind of perspective do the leaders of the Government-General of Korea (朝鮮總督府), the governing authorities (爲政當路者) of the (omitted-original text) government, and politicians out of office hold regarding the Korean problem? (3 lines omitted-original text) It would not be a useless task to pay attention from time to time to the representative views of the Japanese.
This is precisely the intention behind establishing this column starting this month and introducing their opinions each month.

The gist of the main text draws attention by focusing on the proposal to establish a large investigative body composed of Japanese and Koreans to jointly research and discuss the Korean problem, similar to the relationship between Britain and India.

Maruyama Tsurukichi's Argument: 『Set the Goal of Korean Governance』
Introduction = Maruyama Tsurukichi (丸山鶴吉), shortly after the March 1st Movement (己未), served for four and a half years as the Director of the Police Affairs Bureau under Governor-General Saitō (齋藤 總督) and Administrative Superintendent Mizuno (水野 政務總監). Afterwards, he went to Tokyo, worked as a secretary for the Korea Association (조선협회), also served as the Superintendent-General of the Metropolitan Police (경시총감), and is currently a member of the House of Peers (貴族院).

This piece, "Set the Goal of Korean Governance," is quite lengthy, so summarizing the relatively unimportant first half, he (Maruyama) begins with the confession that during his time as Director of the Police Affairs Bureau in Korea, his own opinions (主見) changed four times. That is, his initial opinion upon appointment was:
"Whether the Japan-Korea Annexation (日韓合倂) was right or wrong, there could be arguments of right and wrong if considered from each side's position, but annexation is already a fact now, so the Japanese, down to the last person, will not let go of Korea." Thus, he held a hardline view completely denying Korean independence and proclaimed it at every opportunity. However, he soon realized that this view would not truly capture the hearts of Koreans. That is, the actual situation in Korea at the time was that a provisional government was established in Shanghai (上海), and with the expectation that an independent government would soon be established in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), various "conspiracies" were taking place throughout Korea (鮮內), showing an "extremely unstable situation (非常に不安なる形情)." Therefore, his own view inevitably had to be "dealt with appropriately" (善處). Etcetera.

Thus, having reached this point, he again, "as a result of repeated research," stated, "I understand what the Korean people desire. Thinking as a people who have lost their homeland, it seems plausible to aim for independence as a final hope. However, with the current level of culture, it is impossible in terms of actual capability for Korea to manage a country independently, so the urgent task is to strive for cultural promotion and the cultivation of real strength." He is said to have emphasized this point.
In other words, simply saying independence is impossible wouldn't enter the minds of Koreans, so it was decided instead to focus all attention on cultivating real strength and promoting culture, "to make them strive in practical work, improve customs and habits, or work towards cultural improvement. In this way," he frankly stated, "the intention was to quell 'conspiracies' or 'disturbances'."

Agreeing with this opinion, the National Association Movement (國民協會運動) of Min Wŏn-sik (閔元植) and others emerged. Meanwhile, this kind of opinion gradually became influential in Korea's "vernacular newspapers" (諺文新聞), and the momentum for cultural promotion and cultivation of real strength greatly increased. Youth associations sprang up (簇生) all over Korea, the passion for education rose extraordinarily, leading to movements for school expansion, and in the business sector, many industrial companies and banks managed by Koreans were established. If things went this way, 『結局 不知不識の 間に 所謂 民族思想, 獨立思想が 磨滅して 來るだらう 獨立運動に 正面から 反對すれば 却つて 反撥して來るに 相違ない, それよりは 寧ろ 斯の如く 落着いた 運動に 赴けば 長い期間を經て 次第に民族意識を 消磨し, 獨立運動も 鎭靜するだろうと云ふ考へてあつた』 (Eventually, before one knows it, the so-called national thought, independence thought will wear away. If we directly oppose the independence movement, it will surely provoke a backlash. Rather, if we proceed with such settled movements, over a long period, national consciousness will gradually fade, and the independence movement will also calm down – that was the thinking), he also said.

However, Maruyama continues his opinion, saying, "My thinking had to change for a third time." —
"While expecting the erosion of national consciousness this way, Korea's ideological leaders already noticed this point and greatly feared the development of this movement. So, instead of violent movements, they tried to awaken national consciousness through speech and writing. Because of this, various organizations 「獨立思想を 鼓吹する 機關でもあるが 如く變化し」 (changed as if they were also organizations for instilling independence thought) and 「內地人の 缺陷, 內地人の 橫暴を 力說 高調」 (forcefully emphasized and highlighted the flaws and tyranny of mainlanders (內地人)). Thus, all writing and speech in Korea and the mainland took on an anti-Japanese, anti-Japanese-person tendency. Therefore," he said, "the expectation was completely betrayed."
After that, when Japan's position became unshakable due to the Pacific Conference (太平洋會議), taking that opportunity, from then on, he adopted the opinion that Koreans had no choice but to comply with Japanese rule and proceeded with that policy... he finally confessed. After the above narrative, he concluded thus:

The institutional reforms carried out after the disturbances of Taishō 8 (1919) were, in any case, epoch-making. For the five years until Taishō 13 (1924), not only was almost nothing added to this, but also nothing was indicated to the Koreans regarding their future hopes.<13> And thinking that we could simply control and guide the thoughts of Koreans through speech and writing was, I believe, a grave mistake. Even if we are to guide the thoughts of Koreans in the future, we must decide fundamentally how best to handle Korea's future and proceed.
Considering from this point, can we really smoothly lead the Koreans by following the policy advocated until today, the *Naichi Enchō Shugi* (內地延長主義 - Policy of Extending the Mainland System), gradually developing it like the prefectures of the mainland, just like Hokkaidō or Okinawa Prefecture (沖繩縣), implementing the same system as the mainland? Or, based on Korea's 4,000-year history and the united nation of 20 million people, should we approach it with politics centered on Koreans, in other words, making Korea a dominion (自治領)? Whichever of the two is chosen, secrecy or speculation (*測) must be eliminated, and without hesitation towards others, the fundamental policy for governing Korea must be confirmed, and the direction to proceed sequentially must be clearly indicated. Judging from the situation in Europe after the Great War, the problem of minority peoples is always difficult. There is considerable doubt whether making Korea, which currently has a population of 20 million and encompasses a single nation with its history and culture, follow the same path as Japan's prefectures is necessarily appropriate, or whether it can actually be realized. That being said, even if Koreans were to become independent immediately, it is not something that can be easily realized. (remainder omitted-original text) Etcetera.

Also stated... Deciding the fundamental policy regarding Japan's governance of Korea is originally an extremely difficult task. If possible at this time, wouldn't gathering influential figures from government and private sectors, both Japanese and Korean (日鮮), to establish a large investigative body, and through this, researching a satisfactory resolution plan for both Japan and Korea and a fundamental future policy, be one method for resolving this problem?
Critics who oppose this proposal argue that in Britain, although there have been numerous meetings regarding the India problem, and while they have shown effectiveness each time, on the other hand, it always becomes the subject of troublesome debate. Therefore, they criticize, questioning how much achievement the establishment of such an investigative body would yield. However, simply standing by idly (公手傍観) in the current situation will never lead to a resolution. Therefore, although it might be imperfect, adopting this method might also be one possible plan (一案). If establishing an official investigative body is difficult, then as a preliminary step, gathering influential "leaders" (리-다) who truly care about Korea (思念) into one group, discussing frankly without reservation (忌憚) in "private" (푸라이베-트), and considering and researching Korea's future might naturally lead to a plan emerging from there. In this way, if this plan becomes the general foundation, there would be no problem submitting it to an official investigative body afterwards. In short, deciding our country's policy regarding the governance of Korea (治鮮) is, I think, the urgent task of the moment (刻下). Furthermore, although Korea's current situation appears extremely calm on the surface, if you look beneath, not only is the evil hand (魔手) of Leftist ideology (左傾主義) gradually spreading, but the sentiment of Koreans towards Japan and the Japanese is also gradually worsening. If left unattended, it might reach a state that is difficult to manage later. Therefore, we earnestly hope that knowledgeable people in both Korea and the mainland (內鮮朝野識者) will quickly devise an appropriate way to show a concrete goal for Korean governance... Etcetera.
(Published in Gaikō Jihō (外交時報), October 1, Shōwa 6 [1931])<14>

Japanese Economic Research Association's Argument: 『The Importance of Korea』
The gist of this argument is that in trade, Korea is Japan's exclusive territory, but the purchasing power is declining due to the impoverishment of the masses, mentioning some effect on capitalism.

1. Trade
The text begins with the opening statement, "Korea is an exclusive market for Japan, where the influence of other great powers is completely denied entry." It lists facts such as Korea's exports to foreign countries in Shōwa 5 (1930) being nearly 25 million yen, only 10% of the value of goods shipped (移出) to Japan. Particularly, rice exports amounted to 65 million yen, exceeding half of the total export value to Japan. Soybeans accounted for 7 million yen, fish products 1.4 million yen, raw silk 9.6 million yen, and minerals like coal, gold, and silver 2.6 million yen, with the majority being agricultural products. Due to Japan's food self-sufficiency plan in the age of imperialism XX, Korea has the mission of being a food supplier. Also in imports, Korea's imports of machinery from Britain, the US, Germany, etc., in Shōwa 5 were 3.8 million yen, corresponding to only 30% of machinery imports from Japan. After listing the numbers, it states:
"In short, Korea is an exclusive sales territory for Japanese goods. The import value from Japan is almost comparable to Japan's export value to China (對支), and Korea's role as an export destination for Japan's heavy industry sector, such as machinery, automobiles, iron products, fertilizers, and cement, is even greater. However, since the beginning of this year, imports from Japan have decreased sharply. Comparing Shōwa 5 with the previous year shows a 12% decrease, and comparing the cumulative total up to May of Shōwa 6 (1931) with the cumulative total up to May of the previous year shows a decrease of truly 27%. This signifies the worsening impoverishment of the XX masses, the decline in purchasing power... and is one cause that deepens the... of capitalism." (To be continued in the next issue)

Argument by Doctor of Economics, Hideo Sakurō (英雄策郞): 『Do Not Abolish the Ministry of Colonial Affairs』
The gist of this text argues for the continued existence of the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務省) to control overseas emigration, national defense, and other colonial development projects (拓植事業) in relation to solving Japan's population and food problems.

Summary: "Through the Russo-Japanese War (日露戰爭), Japan acquired the southern half of Sakhalin (樺太), the leasehold rights to the Kwantung Leased Territory (關東州), and rights related to the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵). Also, around the same time, Japan came to possess Taiwan (臺灣), Sakhalin (樺太), Korea (朝鮮), etc., and 'to ensure perfect governance over the newly attached territories (新附の領土), colonial administration (拓務行政)' has been carried out."
"We do not intend to cite further examples from foreign countries, but among the colonial empires other than the United States, there are almost none that do not have an independent ministry for colonies in the home country. Germany, in particular, after first occupying South-West Africa in 1884, proceeded with irresistible force (破竹の勢) to possess German East Africa, Togo, Cameroon, the South Seas Mandate islands (南洋諸島), Qingdao (靑島), etc. From 1907, it transferred responsibilities previously under the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and established an independent ministry. The first minister appointed was the renowned 'Hindenburg' (힌덴부루히), and it is known to everyone that German colonial policy regained its brilliance with this time as a new commemoration... Etcetera."
Published in Gaikō Jihō (外交時報), October 1<15>
<12-15>

**Summary**

This article from the November 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* compiles and analyzes contemporary Japanese perspectives on governing Korea. It highlights the evolving views of Maruyama Tsurukichi, a former high-ranking police official in Korea, who initially advocated hardline assimilation but later recognized its failure to win Korean hearts and proposed cultural/economic development as a means to pacify independence sentiment, only to see that also fail against rising nationalism. He ultimately suggests establishing a joint Japanese-Korean body to research and determine a fundamental policy for Korea, weighing assimilation (*Naichi Enchō Shugi*) against granting dominion status (*Jichiryō*). The article also includes perspectives viewing Korea primarily as an exclusive economic market vital for Japanese exports and resource supply, noting concern over declining Korean purchasing power, and an argument defending the necessity of Japan's Ministry of Colonial Affairs (*Takumushō*) for managing its empire, drawing parallels with European colonial powers. For historians, this article is valuable as it reveals the internal Japanese debate and shifting strategies regarding Korean colonial rule in the early 1930s. It shows a pragmatic recognition by some Japanese officials of the persistence of Korean nationalism and the failure of simple suppression or assimilation, leading to considerations of more nuanced, albeit still colonial, approaches like limited autonomy or joint consultation, alongside the starkly utilitarian view of Korea as an economic asset and the bureaucratic justification for colonial administration.

「朝鮮問題에 對한 日本人 態度」 硏究
						金東煥
朝鮮總督府의 수뇌자와 (중략-원문) 정부의 爲政當路者와 在野한 정객들은 조선문제에 대하야 엇더한 태도, 엇더한 견해를 가지고 잇는가? (3行 략-원문) 일본인의 대표적 견해를 시시로 주의하여 둠이 또한 무용한 일이 아니겟다.
이달부터 본란을 設하고 매월 그네들의 의견을 소개하려는 본의가 오로지 여기에 잇는 바이다.

본문의 요지는 일본인과 조선인으로 조직한 일대 조사기관을 설치하야 英印관계모양으로 조선문제를 공동연구협의하자는데 중점을 둔 것이 주의를 끄은다 할 것이다.

丸山鶴吉所論 『朝鮮統治의 目標를 定하라』
소개=丸山鶴吉은 己未 직후에 齋藤 總督, 水野 政務總監 아래에 警務局長으로 4년 반을 잇섯다가 그 뒤 東京에 드러가 조선협회 간사로 잇스며 또 경시총감을 經하야 현재 貴族院 의원이 되어 잇다.

「조선통치의 목표를 정하라」하는 이 一文은 상당히 長文임으로 비교적 중요치 아니한 전반부의 요지를 일괄하여 抄記 하건대 그(丸山)는 조선에 나와서 경무국장으로 잇는 동안에 네 번채나 자기의 主見이 달너 젓더란 고백부터 시작하엿다. 즉 부임 초초의 자기 主見에는
「日韓合倂이 올코 그른 것은 양자 각각의 처지에서 생각한다면 시비의 論도 잇겟스나 현재 벌서 합병은 한 개의 사실로 되어 잇스니 일본인은 최후의 1인까지 조선을 노치 안으리라」하야 전연 조선독립을 부인하는 강경론을 가지고 그를 기회마다 부리지저 왓다. 그러나 이 主見은 조선인의 마음을 정말 붓잡지 못할 것임을 얼마 뒤에 알어내엇다. 즉 당시의 조선 실정은 上海에 임시가정부가 설치되어서 不遠에 독립정부가 京城에 서려니 하는 기대를 가지고서 각종의 「음모」가 鮮內 각지에 행하고 잇서서 「非常に不安なる形情」를 보이엿슴으로 자기의 主見도 스사로 이에 「善處」 아니할 수 업게 되엇다. 운운.

그래서 이에 이른 그는 다시 『연구를 거듭한 결과』로 「조선사람들이 희망하는 바는 알엇노라. 조국을 이른 민족이 되어서 생각한다면 최종의 희망으로 독립을 도모함도 그럴 듯한 일이다. 그러나 현재의 문화 정도를 가지고 조선이 독립하여서 一國을 경영하기에 그 실력에 잇서 불가능하니 몬저 문화의 촉진과 실력의 양성에 힘쓰는 것이 급무이다」 라고 하고 이 뜻를 역설하엿다 한다.
즉 독립은 안되고 엇저고 한 대야 그 말이 조선인의 머리에 드러가지 안을 것은 정한 일임으로 차라리 실력양성, 문화촉진에 全注意를 집중케 하여 「실업에 힘쓰고 풍속 습관의 개량이라거나 문화향상에 힘을 쓰도록 하려함이엇다. 이리하야 「음모」라거나 「소요」를 진정하려 하엿다고 그는 솔직하게 말하엿다.

이 의견에 동의하야 閔元植 등의 國民協會運動이 나왓다. 한편 조선의 「諺文新聞」에도 점차 이러한 의견이 유력하게 되어서 문화촉진, 실력양성의 기운이 크게 떨치면서 全鮮 각지에 청년회가 簇生하고, 더욱 교육열이 비상히 발흥하야 학교증설운동이 잇섯고, 실업 방면에는 조선인 경영의 실업회사, 은행이 만히 이러낫다. 이러케 되면 『結局 不知不識の 間に 所謂 民族思想, 獨立思想が 磨滅して 來るだらう 獨立運動に 正面から 反對すれば 却つて 反撥して來るに 相違ない, それよりは 寧ろ 斯の如く 落着いた 運動に 赴けば 長い期間を經て 次第に民族意識を 消磨し, 獨立運動も 鎭靜するだろうと云ふ考へてあつた』라고 그는 또 말하엿다.

그런데 세 번재 予의 생각은 변하지 안을 수 업섯다 하고 丸山은 자기의 의견을 계속한다. ―
「이리하야 민족의식의 마멸을 기대하엿든 중, 조선의 사상적 지도자들은 벌서 이 점에 착안하고 이 운동의 발달을 몹시 두려 하엿다. 그래서 폭력운동 대신에 언론 문장에 의하야 민족의식을 환기하려 하엿다. 그 따닭에 각 단체는 「獨立思想を 鼓吹する 機關でもあるが 如く變化し」 「內地人の 缺陷, 內地人の 橫暴を 力說 高調」 하야 조선 內地의 모든 문장과 언론이 反日的 反日人的 경향을 띄게 되엇다. 그래서 「期待は 全く裏切られる結果」가 되엇다고 한다.
그런 뒤 太平洋會議로 日本의 지위가 움지길 수 업는 것이 되자 그를 기회로 그제부터는 日本의 통치에 순응하지 안으면 안된다 라는 의견을 갓고 그 방침으로 나왓노라...고 마즈막 고백하엿다. 如上의 서술이 잇슨 뒤 그는 결론으로 또한 이러케 말하엿다.

大正 8년 소요후에 행한 제도의 개혁은 엇재든 획기적의 것이엇다. 同 13년까지 5개년 간에 거지 반 여기 대하여 하등 부가한 바가 업섯슬 뿐더러 조선인에게 그 장래의 희망에 대하야＜13＞ 하등 지시하는 바가 업섯다. 그러고 우리들은 다만 언론과 문장으로 조선인의 사상을 좌우하고 이를 선도할 수 잇는 것인 것 가치 생각하여온 바는 중대한 착오이엇다고 생각한다. 今後 조선인의 사상을 선도한다 하여도 조선의 장래를 근본적으로 엇더케하면 조흘가 하는 점을 결정하고 나아가지 안으면 안되겟다.
