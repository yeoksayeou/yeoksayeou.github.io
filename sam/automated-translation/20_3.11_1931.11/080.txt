Samch'ŏlli Wall Newspaper No. 2

World Gold
Currently, every country in the world is crying because it has no money. However, saying there is no money ultimately means that the vast majority of poor people do not have it; it never means that the rich or the banks do not have it. Now, looking at the gold reserves of each country's central bank:

USA = In the great vault of the New York Federal Reserve Bank, 6.858 billion wŏn worth of gold coins lie dormant.

France = In the basement vault of the Bank of France in Paris, a great sum of 4.8 billion wŏn in gold is also held within.

UK = In the great vault of the Bank of England in London, 1.349 billion wŏn is piled up.

Germany = In the vault of the Reichsbank in Berlin, 683 million wŏn worth of gold is held.

Japan = In the great vault of the Bank of Japan in Nihonbashi, Tokyo, 810 million wŏn worth of brilliant gold coins are also held. <24>

**Summary**

This news brief from late 1931 reports on the vast gold reserves held by the central banks of major world powers (USA, France, UK, Germany, Japan) despite widespread reports of economic hardship ("no money") affecting the general populace globally. It lists the specific amounts held in each bank's vault, highlighting the concentration of wealth. For historians studying colonial Korea, this piece is interesting as it reflects the global economic context of the Great Depression era, shows awareness in Korea of international finance, and notably includes Japan among the major economic powers holding significant gold reserves, implicitly positioning the colonizer within this global elite structure while Korea itself faced economic difficulties.

三千里 壁新聞 제2호
世界의 黃金
지금 세계 각국에는 어느 나라이나 돈이 업서 울고 잇다. 그러나 돈이 업다는 것도 결국 대다수의 가난한 인민들에게 업다는 말이지, 부자나, 은행에 업다는 뿌는 결코 아니다, 이제 각국 중앙은행의 황금 보유량을 보면
米國=紐育聯邦 준비은행 대금고에는 68억5800만원의 금화가 묵어 잇고.
佛蘭西=巴里푸랑스은행 지하실 대금고에는 역시 48억원의 대금이 잡드러 잇고.
英國=倫敦의 잉글란드은행 대금고에도 13억4900만원이 싸여 잇고.
獨逸=伯林에 잇는 제국은행 금고에도 6억8300만원의 황금이 드러 잇고.
日本=東京日本橋의 일본은행 대금고에도 8억1천만원의 광채 나는 금화가 드러 잇다.＜24＞
