Gratitude and Apology

Lately, you folks on the peninsula (半島) must also be having uneasy dreams, not knowing which direction that dark cloud floating in the Asian sky will eventually scatter. Perhaps because of this situation, this month's issue of our magazine has regrettably become meager like this, as truly important articles – starting with the editorial foreword (卷頭言), Mr. Yi Sŏnghwan's (李晟煥氏) "Chosŏn Agriculture and Foreign Capital (朝鮮農村과 外來資本)," the responses from over 50 individuals (氏) on a certain important issue (某 重要 問題), the open letter addressed to Mr. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟氏), Mr. Hong Hyomin's (洪曉民氏) "Russia's November 7th (露西亞의 11월 7일)," and about 7 or 8 others – could not be included due to unavoidable circumstances. We feel very sorry about this.

Regarding the contents of the compiled "Women of All Nations (萬國婦人)" section, although we initially had various plans and felt a certain degree of pride (自衿), it too has become similarly sparse due to various circumstances. However, starting next month, we intend to devote our full efforts to make it substantial so that the mothers and sisters (母妹님) of the peninsula can enjoy reading it. Please look upon us broadly just this once.

Last October's Third Anniversary issue was completely sold out, including a second printing (再版), amidst fervent cheers. We were unavoidably unable to fulfill requests received after October 18th and have instead sent this month's issue as a replacement. We sincerely ask for the broad understanding of our readers (讀者 諸氏).<116>

**Summary**

The editorial expresses gratitude to readers and apologizes for the thinness of the November 1931 issue. It explains that several important articles, including pieces on Korean agriculture and foreign capital, responses to a significant question, an open letter to Ch'oe Rin, and an article on the Russian Revolution's anniversary, had to be omitted due to "unavoidable circumstances." The editors also apologize for the meager "Women of All Nations" section, promising improvements next month. Finally, they note the success of the previous anniversary issue, which sold out completely, requiring them to send the current issue as a substitute for later orders. The most interesting aspect for historians is the explicit mention of several politically sensitive articles being removed due to "unavoidable circumstances," strongly implying censorship by the Japanese colonial authorities. The specific topics mentioned (rural economy, foreign capital, Russia, responses on an "important issue") highlight contemporary concerns and areas deemed sensitive by the regime.

感謝와 謝罪
요지간 아세아의 하늘 우에 떠도는 저 거문 구름장이 어느 방향으로 장차 흐터질넌지 몰라서 반도의 여려분께서도 자못 꿈자리가 사나울 것이외다. 이러한 관게인지 이달 호의 본지 또한 卷頭言을 위시하야
李晟煥씨의 「朝鮮農村과 外來資本」 及
某 중요 문제에 대한 50여 氏의 회답문과
崔麟씨에 與하는 공개장 洪曉民씨의 「露西亞의 11월 7일」 등 실로 중요한 논문 7,8가지가 그만 부득이한 사정으로 전부 실니지 못하게 되어 11월호 잡지가 이 모양으로 빈약하게 되엇슴을 죄송스럽게 아나이다.
合輯하온 「萬國婦人」의 내용도 처옴에는 여러 가지로 계획한 바도 잇서서 어느 정도까지 自衿을 늣기엇스나 역시 여러 가지 사정으로 이가치 빈약하게 되엇습니다. 그러나 이 다음달부터는 전력을 다하여 반도의 母妹님께서 반가히 보서줄 수 잇도록 충실하게 할가 함니다. 이번만은 널니 보아주시압.
지난 10월의 三週年호는 열열한 환호리에 再版까지 전부 매진이 되어 부득이 10월 18일 이후의 청구에는 수응치 못하고 그 대신 이달 호로 대송하기로 하엿슴니다. 널니 독자 諸氏의 양해를 바라나이다.＜116＞
