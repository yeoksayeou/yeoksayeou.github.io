Magazine Title: 삼천리 제10권 제10호
Publication Date: 1938년 10월 01일
Article Title: 受難의 記錄(三) (Record of Suffering (3))
Author: 兪鎭午 (Yu Chin-o)
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Fiction)

Record of Suffering
						Yu Chin-o

After a while, Sae-min, not knowing what to do, finally spread out the half-folded bedding again and carefully lifted Ae-ra onto it. Ae-ra remained deathly pale, her lips tightly shut, shivering uncontrollably. He didn't know what kind of seizure it was, but he felt he had to warm her up somehow. Putting his hand under the bedding, he found it was as cold as ice. Sae-min went outside to make a fire, gathering the remaining bedding from the corner of the room to cover her first.

As he went to the front of the inner kitchen and looked around for firewood, the old landlady, who was cooking dinner, asked gruffly,
"What is it?"
"Where's the wood? I need to build a fire in the room over there."
At this, the old woman looked Sae-min up and down as if seeing some strange creature, and then <246>
"If you're going to light it, use this."
pointed with a fire poker to a bundle of firewood, barely a handful, that seemed utterly devoid of kindling.
Thinking what a gruff person she was, Sae-min picked up the firewood and turned to leave, when
"What is it? Is the young lady in that room shivering again?"
the old woman asked from behind. Her voice was still gruff, but within that gruffness, he could sense a warm humanity.
Sae-min turned back.
"Yes, she's shivering badly. Has this happened before?"
"Yes, it happens sometimes. It happened just a little while ago."
Then, as if talking to herself,
"She won't eat anything, surviving on nothing but white rice gruel, so wouldn't you expect it? Today too, she hasn't had a sip of water yet."
She turned her head back towards the stove.

While he lit the fire, faint groans leaked out from the room. Sae-min's heart clenched as he thought about what would happen if she were all alone and collapsed shivering like today.
- What Ae-ra needs more than anything right now is someone to protect her. -
Watching the flames flare up effortlessly in a single breath, he quietly resolved that from today on, he would be closer to Ae-ra and look after her. While he had been pointlessly maintaining appearances, the precious Ae-ra's body was weakening day by day, wasn't she enduring terrible suffering alone?

The firewood brought from the inner kitchen didn't seem enough to warm the room, so Sae-min went to the store, bought 10 jeon worth of firewood, added it to the fire, and went back into the room. At the sound of the door opening, Ae-ra opened her eyes.
"I'm sorry,"
she uttered just one phrase and closed her eyes again. She seemed a bit calmer than before, but still appeared to be in great distress. He put his hand under the bedding; the floor was still ice-cold. <247>
Watching Ae-ra lying there like a dead person, eyes closed and lips tightly shut, Sae-min crouched down and waited for the room to warm up. After about 30 minutes, as warmth gradually began to fill the room, he suddenly thought of the brazier, went outside, filled it with glowing embers, and brought it in. Ae-ra seemed much calmer.
"I'm sorry,"
she said, even managing a smile, but then, as if heavy sleep was pressing down on her eyes,
"Oh, I'm sleepy,"
she murmured, trying hard to keep her eyes open.
"If you're sleepy, try to get some sleep. After chills, a warm sleep is good."
Still, perhaps thinking it improper for a woman to fall asleep in front of a man, Ae-ra struggled against sleep, muttering defensively under her breath,
"I haven't slept well for several days."
"Don't worry about that, just try to sleep."
Sae-min pulled the blanket up, covering her snugly up to the chin, turned towards the brazier, and picked up an old magazine. Only then did Ae-ra seem to relax, and soon, she fell asleep, breathing softly.

Before long, the last rays of sunlight, which had clung precariously to the top of the west window, disappeared completely, the room grew dark, and then the electric light came on.
Ae-ra continued to sleep peacefully. Her bloodless face looked cold, like a marble sculpture. As Sae-min gazed at Ae-ra's face, he thought that she truly fit the type of "beauty destined for a short life" (佳人薄命), spoken of since ancient times.
A person who suppresses passion with reason, who refines feminine beauty with decorum and dignity - yet who could guarantee that behind that reason, decorum, and dignity, there wasn't a passion burning silently like a tidal flat fire (겟불)? Thinking about this, and considering his own personality, Sae-min felt the situation between himself and Ae-ra was deeply frustrating. Why couldn't the two of them <248> frankly express the affection they held for each other - even if it couldn't yet accurately be called love - and instead just hesitate and talk about other things? This strange distance - meaningless if you thought about it, yet a distance they couldn't easily overcome by their own strength alone - couldn't there be some external force to eliminate it? But the reality was that such a force was currently absent for them.

He had meaninglessly read through three or four popular novels in the old magazine, so perhaps two hours had passed since Ae-ra first fell asleep. Ae-ra, who had been sleeping as if dead, stirred for the first time with a rustle and sighed, "Hoo-". Sae-min briefly turned his head, saw Ae-ra was still asleep, and turned his eyes back to the magazine, but immediately, as if struck by a strange thought, he turned his head back to Ae-ra's sleeping face. Indeed, his brief impression had not been mistaken. In Ae-ra's sleeping face, as if by some miracle, a great change had occurred during that time. As if mesmerized, Sae-min looked intently at Ae-ra's face and gradually moved closer to her. The face that had been deathly pale earlier now had a rosy flush, beautifully coloring her cheeks, which, though thin, traced soft lines. Tiny beads of sweat even glistened on her forehead.

Ae-ra, now seemingly feverish, pushed the tightly pulled blanket down to her chest and put one arm out over the blanket. A hand, white and fine as jade. Sae-min tucked her hand back under the blanket and pulled the pushed-down blanket back up. The scent of sweat mixed with a woman's fragrance wafted under his nose. It seemed to possess some mysterious charm, tickling Sae-min's senses and making his blood race. Still, Ae-ra slept on soundly, breathing softly. Eyes closed in a half-moon shape. Naturally long, beautifully curved eyelashes. Lips painted with rouge. It was the first time he had looked at Ae-ra's face for so long and so clearly. As he did, Sae-min's heart began to pound strangely. An intense emotion he had never felt towards Ae-ra before blossomed vigorously within his veins.

As if trying to conceal his own new impulse, he took out his handkerchief and gently wiped the beads of sweat from Ae-ra's forehead. Strands of bangs, fallen onto her forehead, stuck there, damp with sweat. This action, intended to relieve the tension of his emotions, had the opposite effect on Sae-min. No matter how he looked, in Ae-ra's sleeping face before him now, <249> the dignity and cool reason he had seen before were nowhere to be found. He felt the illusion of looking at a painting by Cézanne, no, by Renoir. Something that had felt impossible just minutes before had now miraculously appeared before his eyes.

Sae-min gently lifted the corner of the blanket and, with a trembling hand, reached out and took Ae-ra's feverishly hot hand, the one he had tucked in earlier. A thrilling impulse swept through his entire body.
"Ae-ra-ssi, Ae-ra-ssi."
He gradually tightened his grip on Ae-ra's hand and softly called her name. But Ae-ra was truly fast asleep and didn't seem to hear him. Of course, if he spoke just a little louder, she would open her eyes instantly. He didn't want that. His true feeling was that he wanted to prolong that moment of beauty and emotion for as long as possible, if he could. Though he had called her name aloud, he was actually afraid she might wake up at the sound.

Suddenly, Sae-min felt his own cowardice. What kind of cowardice was it to touch her body like a thief while she slept, unable to express his love when facing her awake? Thinking this, Sae-min couldn't maintain that posture any longer and returned to the side of the brazier, picking up the magazine again. Within his heart, Satan and an angel began a terrible struggle. But the torrent of emotion, once unleashed, was not easily dammed. Especially when, as in this case, it hadn't formed accidentally in a day or two but had been simmering internally for many months, it was like new buds bursting forth across mountains and fields after a gentle spring rain overnight; there was no way to stop it by human effort.
- I love Ae-ra. If that feeling is true, what should I hesitate about in my actions?
Sae-min moved closer to Ae-ra again. She was still sleeping soundly. He put his hand under the blanket and took Ae-ra's hand as before. This time, he trembled even more than before. After staying in that position for a while, Sae-min gradually brought his pale face closer to Ae-ra's. Her two eyes, adorned with neat eyelashes, came into close-up. Her lips, slightly parted from the heat, exhaled warm breath. Trembling uncontrollably, Sae-min closed his eyes. For a moment, a thought rebuking his action flashed through his <250> mind like lightning, and like someone startled awake, he snapped his eyes open and lifted his head. Oh, what a foolish act! Sweat broke out on his back.

However, such reflection did not last long. His urgent impulse now did not allow him the leisure to continue cool reflection indefinitely. His head felt filled with emptiness, and all he could grasp was the single phrase, "I don't know!" Like a person under a magician's hypnosis, holding his breath, he once again gradually brought his pale face closer to Ae-ra's.

Startled, Ae-ra's eyes flew wide open. Sae-min recoiled like a thief caught in the act and hung his head. Ah! Ah! What a cowardly man this Kim Sae-min was. He lashed himself countless times in his own mind. If the sentence "A guy like you should die" came from Ae-ra's lips, he felt he could die on the spot.
"Please forgive me."
Only these words managed to escape his stammering lips. He thought it right to take this opportunity to confess that he had harbored feelings of love for Ae-ra for some time now and seek her understanding, but his face burned, and his mouth was dry; the words wouldn't come out.

Too surprised by the unexpected event to know what to do, Ae-ra stared at Sae-min with wide eyes. But Ae-ra too had felt a love for Sae-min that went beyond friendship for quite some time, and the only reason it hadn't turned into love until now was the invisible barrier between them and their lack of strength to break it down. If that barrier had now miraculously been broken—
Ae-ra gently squeezed the hand Sae-min was holding.
Sae-min lifted his face.
"Then, then, Ae-ra-ssi, do you forgive me?"
He was breathless as he spoke.
Instead of answering, Ae-ra briefly let a mysterious smile, like the Mona Lisa's, flicker across her face. As soon as the smile vanished like a shadow, <251> she slowly closed her eyes.

In that instant, Sae-min was swept away by a profound emotion. A hot lump surged up from his chest, through his throat, and burst into his eyes with a searing heat. It was a sacred, pure emotion he had never experienced in his nearly 30 years of life.
"Ae-ra-ssi!"
he cried out, collapsing onto Ae-ra's chest. Hot tears followed, pouring out.

The next day, Sae-min went to his research office, but his mind was so full of Ae-ra that he couldn't grasp the meaning of the books he tried to read, and he certainly had no capacity to pick up a pen and write. He just wanted to see Ae-ra, only Ae-ra, and worried only about her affairs. Can a person's heart become so uncontrollable once it starts to open up? He was astonished at the extreme change between the him of yesterday and the him of today.

Suddenly, he thought of Yi Hae-rim in prison. Thinking back, it seemed like a miracle that he had completely forgotten about Yi Hae-rim since the previous evening. Wasn't it primarily because of Hae-rim that he had restrained his love for Ae-ra until now? Hae-rim was Ae-ra's lover and, at the same time, Sae-min's respected friend. Because of that, hadn't Sae-min felt a sense of duty that, no matter what, he must not harbor anything more than friendship for Ae-ra?
"I am -"
As his mind gradually calmed down, Sae-min began to feel the pangs of conscience regarding Hae-rim. No matter what reason he tried to use to uphold his loyalty to Hae-rim and reassure his own conscience, it was impossible.
"- A man who stole the lover of a faraway friend!"
A terrible judgment weighed down on his entire body. Sae-min put down his pen, stood up, and paced around the room, but he couldn't find peace of mind.

Returning to his desk, he considered whether he shouldn't let his love for Ae-ra develop any further, but that seemed even more unthinkable in his current state than committing a wrong against Hae-rim.
Just then, the professor, having finished his morning lecture, entered the room with a very cheerful expression, saw Sae-min sitting blankly, clutching his head, and came over to his side. <252>
"How's the thesis coming along? Almost done?"
he asked, glancing at the scattered papers on the desk.
"Yes, I'm working on it, I am-"
Sae-min answered flusteredly.
"Why, you don't look well."
"Yes, my head hurts a bit."
After the professor went to his own desk, Sae-min picked up his pen and spread out the manuscript paper, but his thoughts still flew only to the problem of Ae-ra and Hae-rim. It was clearly a terrible dilemma from which there was no escape, but he couldn't just close his eyes and give up on finding a solution either.

Then it occurred to him that regarding the relationship between Ae-ra and Hae-rim, he had only been speculating on his own until now; he hadn't heard a single word directly from Ae-ra's lips. Thinking this, it seemed like a glimmer of hope for resolving the dilemma appeared. The more desperate a person is, the more they try to interpret everything in their own favor. Sae-min was a case in point now. And thinking about it, who knew if, unexpectedly, there had been no relationship at all between Ae-ra and Hae-rim—
Sae-min excused himself from the research office, claiming his headache was too severe to stay any longer, and went to Ae-ra's boarding house.

Ae-ra seemed to be in a much lighter mood, completely different from the previous day. She had cleaned the room neatly, applied makeup tastefully, and had manuscript paper spread out on her desk.
"Oh, I'm sorry about yesterday. I troubled you too much."
Ae-ra greeted Sae-min with a bright, smiling face.
"Not at all. It was I who-"
Sae-min replied, glancing up at the wall. It was the wall where the yellowed photograph of Hae-rim hung. If he could have his way, <253> he wanted to immediately ask about her relationship with Hae-rim, but he held back firmly and started with polite greetings.
"How can you work already like this? Are you trying to get sick again?"
"Oh, what work is this little bit?"
Ae-ra smiled and pushed the manuscript paper on the desk to one side.
Sae-min looked back and forth between Yi Hae-rim's picture on the wall and Ae-ra's face, then finally brought up Hae-rim's name.
"Do you still hear news from Mr. Yi sometimes?"
His voice was awkward.
"Mr. Yi Hae-rim? Yes. He's doing well."
Ae-ra answered calmly.
"Mr. Yi is-"
Sae-min started again, but the rest didn't follow easily. Ae-ra
"Eh?"
looked up at Sae-min's face, seemingly puzzled. Her expression asked him to quickly ask whatever his question was.
"Since when did you know Mr. Yi?"
"Let's see. Since I was in school, so maybe five or six years already?"
"Five or six years-"
Sae-min repeated the words "five or six years" inwardly, but no answer emerged from them.
As he hesitated, wondering what else to ask, Ae-ra suddenly, as if struck by a thought,
"Teacher!"
looked up at Sae-min, smiling coquettishly, almost like a spoiled child.
"Yes?"
"Let's have a Christmas celebration this Christmas. Yes?" <254>
"A Christmas celebration?"
The words were so unexpected that Sae-min could only repeat them back. Ae-ra, still beaming, said,
"Does Christmas have to be celebrated only by Christians? Anyone can pick that day to celebrate when they have something happy in their heart, isn't that a Christmas celebration? This Christmas, I'll prepare dinner, so you must come, okay?"
Gradually, Sae-min began to grasp the intention behind Ae-ra's words, but he couldn't immediately get into the same mood himself. Ae-ra said that after Sae-min left the previous evening, she couldn't sleep well because she was planning the Christmas celebration.
"We'll set up a small Christmas tree, hang a Santa Claus ornament, and turn on colored lights, okay?"
Ae-ra still possessed the beautiful dream world of an innocent seventeen or eighteen-year-old girl. Listening to Ae-ra, Sae-min gradually began to feel moved. In what corner of Ae-ra, known to the world only as a mature woman, had such an innocent girl been hiding? How could one connect the Ae-ra who depicted men of steel-like will and explored the profound paths of love and desire in the world of novels, with the Ae-ra who wanted to turn on colored lights on Christmas Eve?
"Well, that sounds nice too, but first, you need to get well by then, don't you?"
"My health? What about it? I already feel so light today. By Christmas, I'll be completely recovered, just you wait and see."
Ae-ra seemed truly confident about her health.
"Of course, you must. You must."
Sae-min, moved, repeated "You must" and looked at Ae-ra's joyful face. His eyes inexplicably grew hot. Ae-ra, perhaps embarrassed by having acted too much like a spoiled child while Sae-min watched, quickly flushed and lowered her head.
- Ae-ra has no experience whatsoever with romance. -
Sae-min gazed at the loose strands of hair on Ae-ra's bowed forehead and muttered inwardly. He thought that Ae-ra's words and actions today were something only a virgin with absolutely no romantic experience, or else someone whose skills in affection had been polished through countless experiences with men, <255> could do. And thinking about it, hadn't Sae-min himself, who immediately assumed a romantic relationship between Ae-ra and Hae-rim just by seeing Hae-rim's picture in her room, fallen prey to worldly gossip (꼬싶) about Ae-ra? No, even if it wasn't gossip or Sae-min's mistaken assumption, even if there truly had been a romantic relationship between Ae-ra and Hae-rim, what of it? Since the reality is that he loves Ae-ra and Ae-ra loves him, isn't that enough?
Sae-min resolved not to ask Ae-ra about Hae-rim again. And as he did, his affection for Ae-ra surged anew.
"Ae-ra-ssi! Why do I... like you so much..."
Sae-min gently took Ae-ra's wrist.

Ae-ra remained perfectly still. The quiet afternoon, as sunlight began to stream onto the western paper sliding door again, felt to him now like a world of eternity, transcending sorrowful reality.
Ae-ra's shoulders began to tremble slightly. It was immediately clear that a storm of emotion had swept over her.
"Ae-ra-ssi, look at me."
Sae-min asked Ae-ra to lift her face and look at him. Her face, as if forced to look up. In her beautiful pupils, teardrops glistened like crystals. With tears welling in her eyes, Ae-ra gave her habitual faint, bitter smile.

From the next day onward, Sae-min began skipping the university research office, claiming illness. Even if he went to the office, he didn't have the mental capacity to continue writing his depressing thesis, and more than that, it was because he just wanted to be by Ae-ra's side. Not being able to write the thesis due by the end of the year wasn't entirely without concern, but there was also a thought that it was okay not to be able to write such a thesis. Watching his own calculating reason, which had moved according to profit and loss until now, gradually break down, as if watching someone else's affair, he even felt a sense of exhilaration.
Ae-ra's health truly recovered day by day, as if by some miracle. Although she only sent manuscripts to the magazine company and hadn't returned to work yet, she had recovered in just a few days to a state where she could easily have gone back to work, unlike before. <256>

On Christmas Eve, Sae-min and Ae-ra went out together to the streets and bought things to make a Christmas tree: a small fir tree, groceries, colored lights, toys, and such.
"Alright then, Ae-ra-ssi, you prepare the food. I'll decorate the room."
While Ae-ra was in the kitchen, chopping sounds echoing from the cutting board as she prepared dinner, Sae-min set up the Christmas tree in the room. He placed cotton batting sparsely on the fir branches like snow and draped gold and silver threads in shreds. He tied toys onto threads and hung them, and finally, as he was turning the sockets to light the five-colored electric lights, Ae-ra opened the door.
"Are you done?"
she asked, wiping her hands on her apron (마에 가께).
"Almost done now."
As soon as he answered, brilliant light flared from the round, five-colored bulbs hanging on the fir branches.
"Oh, how pretty! Amazing!"
Ae-ra gazed, mesmerized. Thinking about it, it was childish play, and perhaps the shabbiest Christmas decoration in the world. But even if it was childish play, and even if it was incomparably shabby, if it brought even a little moisture to our lives, dry as a desert, wasn't it also something to be thankful for?

Ae-ra brought in the dinner table, and the Christmas Eve feast began in the room. Though it was a chipped hexagonal tray table, tonight it was laden full with dishes. The main dish was an unidentifiable stir-fry made by throwing together beef, fish cake (가마보꼬), radish, green onion, fried tofu, and the like. Besides that, simmered beans, shredded squid, simmered dried anchovies (메루치 조림), sliced fish cake (가마보꼬), and other items bought from a department store sat on their respective plates.
Sitting facing each other across the dinner table, the hearts of the two people felt constricted with the emotion of joy.
"Teacher, would you like some wine?"
Ae-ra asked, smiling meaningfully.
"Why? Yes, I'll have a little." <257>
"Hohoho. A banquet must have wine, mustn't it?"
Ae-ra stood up, reached under the desk, removed a wrapping cloth, and brought out a wine bottle she had bought beforehand. It was Akadama port wine.
There was wine but no cups, so after pondering for a moment, they brought out porcelain soy sauce dishes and poured the wine into them.
"Well then, this will do-"
Sae-min raised his cup first and offered one to Ae-ra as well. Ae-ra said she couldn't drink but held the cup anyway.
"Well then, please eat. It's not much, but..."
"Yes, Ae-ra-ssi, you eat too."
Sae-min urged her in return and picked up his chopsticks.

Not long after the two had started eating dinner, the sound of the outer gate opening was heard outside, someone strode across the yard, and right outside the paper sliding door,
"Is Teacher Kim here?"
a thick voice asked. The two inside stopped their chopsticks and looked at each other. The voice continued,
"Ms. Kim Ae-ra!"
it called a second time. The voice was unfamiliar to Sae-min, but for some reason, Ae-ra's face instantly turned deathly pale. The room was silent as death.
"Ms. Kim Ae-ra! It's me. An Il-su. May I come in?"
An Il-su! Hearing the name, Sae-min's face changed too. Wasn't this the very man who, just a few days ago, had written such baseless slander about Ae-ra that it had even caused her to cough up blood?
Putting down her chopsticks, Ae-ra stood up and went out onto the wooden porch.
"Hahaha. Do you have a guest? Well, it's alright if I come in, right? I came because I have something to tell you too-"
The sound of shoes being taken off and someone stepping heavily onto the porch was heard. <258>
Sae-min sat frozen like a statue. To have a scene like this, being together with Ae-ra, especially celebrating with a Christmas tree set up and even drinking wine, seen by someone like An Il-su, who already couldn't stand Ae-ra, was undoubtedly a huge blow. But now that it had happened, there was nothing he could do about it.
An Il-su opened the door, his eyes widening.
"Oh- my, my apologies. You were having a nice party... Oh dear, I truly apologize."
Slightly staggering, he entered the room and plopped down right in the middle. His face was flushed red with drink.
Ae-ra silently followed him in, shut the door tightly, and sat down quietly right there. More than being annoyed that Il-su had visited, her face showed she didn't know what to do because this unexpected scene had been discovered.
Il-su looked around the room.
"Oh, oh, I came at the wrong time today,"
he muttered as if to himself. The unexpected sight seemed to have sobered him up considerably.
The air in the room was chillingly cold.
Finally, Ae-ra seemed to regain her composure.
"Please introduce yourselves,"
she said, introducing Sae-min and Il-su.
"My name is An Il-su,"
Il-su bowed his head with surprising politeness.
"I am Kim Sae-min."
"Please excuse my rudeness on our first meeting. I'm a bit drunk - please forgive me."
Il-su greeted him with wide-open eyes. He had come looking for Ae-ra this evening with something specific in mind, but given what he had written in the magazine last time, even with his considerable nerve, he couldn't face Ae-ra sober, so he had deliberately had a few drinks before coming. However, encountering such an unexpected scene in Ae-ra's room had left him rather bewildered. <259>
"Actually, I came specifically because I really had something to tell Ae-ra-ssi, but it turned out like this. I'm terribly sorry for interrupting your enjoyable time together,"
Il-su said, trying in his own way to ease the atmosphere in the room, but Sae-min and Ae-ra offered no reply.
In such an atmosphere, Il-su must have thought he couldn't stay longer, so he picked up his hat and stood up.
"Well then, I'll be leaving first. I apologize for barging in and being rude,"
he said, but he lingered near the door, not leaving immediately.
Sae-min considered letting him go just like that, but sending Il-su away felt unsettling. Wanting to keep him seated a bit longer and, if possible, explain the evening's scene, he tried to detain him.
"Since you're already here, we have wine, why don't you stay and chat for a while before you go?"
At that, Il-su replied,
"Well, shall we do that then?"
as if he had been waiting for those words, and promptly sat back down. "I've made a mistake," Sae-min regretted, but it was already too late. Il-su slowly took off his overcoat and, smiling broadly, moved closer to the table.
"Oh- now that I think about it, tonight is Christmas Eve, isn't it? Yes-"
he said, nodding to himself.
As Sae-min watched Il-su's behavior, he felt an instinctive hatred. Even his appearance repelled Sae-min. His dark yet lustrous complexion spoke of unusual vitality, and his lips, which curved downwards at the corners whenever closed, plainly revealed a bold nature that always looked down on others and asserted itself.
"Ho- Port wine-"
Il-su picked up the port wine bottle, sneered once as if in admiration, but then immediately poured a full... cup and drank it down in one gulp.
"Sweet, so sweet, this isn't our kind of drink,"
he said, smirking and twisting his lips. Then, looking back and forth between Sae-min and Ae-ra, <260>
"Why are you two just sitting there? Are you telling me to drink alone? Come on, let's drink together."
Sae-min didn't want to sit opposite Il-su any longer, but he had no choice but to approach the table and pick up his cup.
Ae-ra sat motionless like a stone Buddha, her face expressionless. Il-su, perhaps thinking something, stared intently at Ae-ra for a while, then turned to Sae-min.
"Kim, Teacher Kim, since you're not involved in the literary world, you might not know, but the novel Ae-ra-ssi wrote this time is truly a masterpiece. I stopped by the Chungang P'yongnonsa (Central Review Company) today and read a copy fresh off the press-"
Then, turning to Ae-ra,
"This is the first time that issue has been treated so seriously in a Chosŏn novel, Ae-ra-ssi. It's definitely a masterpiece. Hahaha."
He laughed, watching Ae-ra's expression for any change.
"So, I came today partly to tell you that, and also because I felt bad about writing some unnecessary things about Ae-ra-ssi last time, so I came to apologize for that too, for both reasons."
Ae-ra didn't reply, but seemed to nod slightly. Il-su believed his tactic had succeeded and smiled inwardly, but Sae-min felt extremely frustrated. Why couldn't Ae-ra confront this man more sharply? Wasn't it obvious that words like "masterpiece" and "apology" were just lip service, and there wasn't a speck of sincerity in Il-su's words and attitude? If she were to point out, item by item, the despicable motive behind writing slander about Ae-ra before, and his despicable behavior in visiting today, wouldn't Il-su be unable to utter a single word, no matter what insults he received? He thought Ae-ra's weak personality was actually encouraging the nerve of a man like Il-su.

In truth, the reason Il-su visited Ae-ra that day wasn't because of things like "masterpiece" or "apology." After Ae-ra firmly rejected his advances, he had struck her down in a fit of anger, but his longing for Ae-ra had only intensified because of it. However, lacking the courage, he couldn't bring himself to meet Ae-ra. Then, that afternoon, after reading Ae-ra's novel "The Value of Love and Desire," his longing for her was further stirred, so he deliberately drank alcohol and came visiting, pretending to be drunk. <261>

But anyway, Il-su was a man of strong nerve. Whether Ae-ra answered or not, whether the unfamiliar man named Sae-min – though he knew the name – sat beside him or not, whether the atmosphere in the room was cold or warm towards him, he held his ground and kept chattering. Sae-min initially felt simple displeasure, but eventually, he began to feel an almost irresistible sense of being overwhelmed by Il-su. In the end, he didn't even want to be in the same place as long as Il-su was sitting there.

After the dinner table was cleared and a short while passed, Sae-min announced he was leaving and stood up.
Il-su still seemed hesitant, wanting to stay longer, but he had no choice but to get up with Sae-min.
The two men parted ways right in front of the boarding house, but Sae-min was so filled with unpleasant feelings that thoughts of Il-su occupied his mind all the way home. It was upsetting that the carefully planned evening feast had turned into a mess, but Il-su's attitude, treating people disrespectfully, was even more unpleasant. This feeling of displeasure towards Il-su went beyond mere displeasure, becoming an intense feeling akin to hostility.

The next day, Sae-min finished breakfast and went straight to Ae-ra's place. He felt he needed to vent about Il-su with Ae-ra to feel better.
Ae-ra seemed unwell and was lying down.
"I have a bit of a fever again, but it's not serious,"
she said, sitting up. The room was untidily scattered with orange peels, apple peels, and leftovers (끄트레기) from the previous evening.
"Well, there are all sorts of people in the world. How can there be such a shameless fellow?"
Sae-min recalled the previous evening and looked across at Ae-ra.
"Oh, it's so ridiculous!"
Ae-ra clicked her tongue too.
"To ruin a party we planned so carefully."
"How can he be so brazen?"
Just saying that much seemed to relieve some of Sae-min's pent-up frustration.
Ae-ra, perhaps recalling another annoying incident, <262>
"It's really upsetting!"
clicked her tongue once more, but then smiled faintly and looked up at Sae-min.
"- That rascal came again last night."
"What? Who? An Il-su?"
Sae-min's surprise at the completely unexpected news was immense.
"Yes, really-"
Ae-ra began to recount the story of the previous night. After seeing Sae-min and Il-su off, while she was tidying up the room, An Il-su had returned and barged straight into her room. Then he rambled on, asking how she became close to Kim Sae-min, suggesting they must be romantically involved, saying he would retract the article he wrote about her next time so she shouldn't hold it against him, promising to guide her sincerely from now on so she should know that and socialize with him as before, and so on, lingering nonsensically until one in the morning.
"My, what a persistent fellow,"
Sae-min marveled. Ae-ra, smiling playfully, said,
"Later, perhaps feeling jealous, he started asking if I was going to marry Teacher Kim, saying Teacher Kim must surely have a wife and children, saying all sorts of things. Really, for a person to be so shameless-"
Ae-ra might have been relaying Il-su's words casually, but the issues of "marriage" and "wife and children" did not sound like passing remarks to Sae-min. However, regarding that matter, Sae-min had already made up his mind.
"So he even said things like that. Hmm-"
Sae-min's face turned serious as he looked at Ae-ra, who lowered her head. Since the topic had come up, Sae-min decided to resolve this issue completely.
"Actually, I've been wanting to talk about this for a while, but I haven't had the chance. You might not know, Ae-ra-ssi, but as Il-su said, I am a man with a wife and child. However, if you feel the same way, Ae-ra-ssi, that issue <263> will absolutely not be an obstacle for us. I have already made up my mind."
Ae-ra turned her face, glanced briefly at Sae-min, and then lowered her head again.
"I cannot explain at length about my wife and child right now, but in any case, I am an unhappy man, so to speak, who has never once felt marital affection for my wife. The reason I've lived like this until now is simply because I lacked the blind courage others have. Ae-ra-ssi, you are, in a way, my benefactor. Because I came to know you, I found the courage to resolve the problem that had been weighing on my mind for years. Ae-ra-ssi, will you marry me? Of course, you'll consent, won't you? I will absolutely not let the matter of my previous wife and child trouble you. You can trust me on that, right?"
He spoke breathlessly up to this point, but Ae-ra kept her head down, fiddling with the hem of her skirt. Sae-min took her hand.
"Please consent. Yes? You consent, don't you?"
He fixed his gaze on Ae-ra's lips and swallowed.
Sae-min had thought that if he proposed marriage formally like this, Ae-ra would, nine times out of ten, immediately rejoice and consent. But for some reason, Ae-ra remained silent with her head bowed for a long while, then gently withdrew her hand from Sae-min's grasp.
"That kind of talk-"
she began, but didn't finish the sentence, instead smiling faintly and looking up at Sae-min. Ae-ra's attitude was quite unexpected to Sae-min.
"Why do you say that? Yes? If only you consent, Ae-ra-ssi, I intend to resolve the issue of my wife and child immediately. No, whether you consent or not, I have already decided to settle the matter of my wife and child once and for all."
"I understand what you're saying very well, and it's not that I'm making an issue of such things, but-"
Ae-ra again left her sentence unfinished and turned her head away. Then why was she acting like this? Sae-min was extremely anxious. Yet, he couldn't imagine that Ae-ra had some kind of promise with another person-
"Then why are you acting like this? Yes?"
Ae-ra lifted her head. <264>
"First - my body needs to get well - shouldn't I think about such things then?"
she said slowly, in a thin voice. The dark shadow of despair, of having given up hope in life, lingered deeply on her face.
A hot wave of emotion surged anew in Sae-min's chest. Ae-ra's face, looking up at him helplessly, seemed like the sacred countenance of a saint who had attained enlightenment.
"Your body will gradually recover,"
he managed to say, his voice choked with emotion.
"Well, if it recovers-"
Ae-ra smiled faintly.
"- Ah, if it recovers."
A desperate hope simultaneously constricted both their hearts.
"Taking it easy, by spring you'll be completely well, and then let's choose a good day-"
Sae-min tried to give Ae-ra strength by whispering happy plans for their marriage, but before he knew it, his own heart grew increasingly gloomy, and his words trailed off.
- Will Ae-ra's illness really be cured by spring?
He had heard stories that consumption, especially when contracted before the age of twenty, worsens in the spring, but he had never heard stories of it getting better. Sae-min's heart felt like it had crashed against a wall (담베락) of despair. (To be continued in the next issue) <265>

**Summary**

The story depicts the developing relationship between Sae-min, an intellectual, and Ae-ra, a writer suffering from a serious illness (likely tuberculosis). Sae-min cares for Ae-ra during a severe attack of chills and fever, leading to a mutual, unspoken confession of feelings. Their fragile intimacy culminates in a plan for a simple Christmas Eve celebration, symbolizing hope. However, their evening is disrupted by the intrusion of An Il-su, a crass literary figure who had previously slandered Ae-ra and seems possessively interested in her. The next day, Sae-min, wrestling with guilt over his existing family but determined, formally proposes marriage. Ae-ra hesitates, not because of his family, but citing her poor health and uncertain future, leaving Sae-min facing the grim reality of her potentially fatal illness.

From a historical perspective, the story offers insights into the emotional lives and social constraints faced by intellectuals in late 1930s colonial Korea. It portrays the prevalence and fear surrounding tuberculosis ("consumption"), the complex interplay between modern ideas of romantic love and traditional obligations (Sae-min's existing marriage), and the potentially predatory dynamics within the nascent literary circles (represented by An Il-su). The characters' internal conflicts and Ae-ra's vulnerability highlight personal struggles against societal pressures and physical frailty during a difficult period.

「자 그럼 나는 먼저 가지오. 공연히 튀어드러와 실례많엇읍니다」
하엿스나 곳 나가랴고는 하지 안코 문 앞에서 어름거렷다.
세민은 그대로 보내버릴까 하고 생각도 하엿스나 일수를 그대로 보내는 것이 도무지 마음에 두려웟다. 좀 더 안처놓고 될 수 잇다면 그 날 저녁의 장면을 변명해 주고 싶어서
「이왕 오섯는데 여기 술두 잇구허니 가치 이야기나 좀 허다 가시죠」
하고 부뜨러 보앗다. 그랫드니 일수는
「그럼 그러케 헐까요」
그 말을 기다렷다는 듯이 금시로 도로 주저 앉엇다. 「잘몯햇구나」하고 세민은 후회하엿스나 벌서 어떠케 할 수 없는 노릇이엇다. 일수는 천천히 외투를 벗고 빙글빙글 웃으면서 상 앞으로 닥어앉었다.
「오- 인제 생각하니 오늘 저녁이 크리스마쓰 이브로구먼. 녜- 」
하며 혼자 고개를 끄덱끄덱 하기도 햇다.
세민은 일수의 행동을 보고 잇는 동안에 본능적인 증오를 느꼇다. 그의 생김생김붙어 세민에게는 실혔다. 까므스름하면서도 윤태가 나는 살빛은 남달는 정력을 말하고 잇고 다물 때 마다 양편 끝이 아래로 휘는 입술은 어떤 때에나 남을 만만히 보고 자기를 주장하는 대담한 성질을 그대로 나타내고 잇섯다.
「호- 포도주- 」
일수는 포도주 병을 들고서 감탄하는 양 한 번 비웃고서 그러나 곧 저…주에 가뜩 따려 한 목음에 마시고서 「달데 단 것이 우리네 술은 아니로군」
하고 빙긋빙긋 웃으면서 입가를 삐두러트렷다. 그리고는 세민과 애라를 번가러보며＜260＞
「웨들 그리구 게십니까. 나 혼자만 먹으라시는 검니까. 자 가치들 허십시다」
세민은 그 이상 일수와 같이 대해 잇기도 실혓스나 하는 수 없이 상으로 닥어앉어 저를 들었다.
애라는 돌부처 모양으로 표정없는 얼골로 꼼짝도 안하고 잇다. 일수는 무엇을 생각하엿는지 애라를 한참이나 드려다 ³´드니 세민을 향해
「김 김선생은 문단과는 관게가 없으시니까 몰으시겟지만 이번 애라씨가 쓰신 소설은 단연 걸작입니다. 오늘 중앙 평론사엘 들였다가 갖 인쇄해 온 것을 읽었는데- 」
하고 이번에는 애라를 향해
「조선서 그 문제를 그러케 심각하게 취급허기는 이번 애라씨 소설이 처음입니다. 단연 걸작이드군요. 허허허」
웃으면서 애라의 표정 움지기는 것을 살펴보고
「그래 난 그 말심두 할 겸 또 저번에 애라씨헌테 좀 쓸데없는 말까지 쓴 것이 마음에 걸니든 판이라 그 사과두 헐 겸 겸두겸두 해 오늘 온 것입니다」
애라는 아모 대답도 하지 않었으나 약간 고개를 끗덱 하는 듯 하엿다. 일수는 그것으로 자기의 전술이 성공한 것으로 믿고 뱃속으로 실며시 웃어스나 세민은 안타깝기 짝이 없었다. 웨 애라는 좀 앵두라지게 이자를 대해주지 몯하는 가ƒ인가. 「걸작」이니 「사과」니 하는 말은 입에 발닌 소리고 일수의 말과 태도에 티끌만치도 성의가 없는 것은 뻔히 보이는 일이 아닌가. 요전 애라의 욕을 쓴 비열한 동기 또 오늘 찾어와 하는 비열한 행동을 조목조목 들어 닥거댄다면 일수는 아무런 모욕을 받어도 한 마디도 할 말이 없슬 것이 아닌가. 애라의 그런 약한 성격이 도리혀 일수같은 사내의 뱃장을 점점 더 북도다 주는 것이라 생각하엿다.
사실 일수가 그 날 애라를 찾어간 것은 그런 「걸작」이니 「사과」니 하는 것 때문은 아니였다. 애라가 자기의 구애를 끝끝내 거절하자 홧김에 그를 때려눞이기는 햇스나 애라를 그리는 마음은 그 때문에 도리혀 심각해갓다. 그러나 참아 용기가 나지 않어 애라를 맛나지 맛나지 몯하고 잇든 중 그 날 낮에 애라의 소설 「애욕의 가치」를 읽고 한층 그에 대한 연모의 마음을 충동받어 일부러 술을 마시고 취한 체 하고 찾어온 것이다.＜261＞
그러나 어쨋든 일수는 뱃심이 센 사람이였다. 애라가 대답을 하든 안 하든 세민이라는 낯몰으는 사내가 - 이름은 알고 잇섯지만 - 옆에 앉엇든 말든 그 방안의 공기가 자기에게 차든 더웁든 그는 버티고 앉어 작고 짓거렷다. 세민은 처음에는 단순한 불쾌를 느낄 뿐이엇스나 나종에는 차차로 저항치 몯할 것 같은 압도를 일수에게서 느꼇다. 끝끝내는 일수가 앉어잇는 한 그 곧에 더 잇기도 실혓다.
저녁상을 치우고 잠간 잇다가 세민은 간다고 일어섯다.
일수는 그래도 문짓문짓하며 더 잇고 싶은 모양이엇스나 하는 수 없이 세민과 함께 자리를 이러섯다.
두 사람은 하숙 앞에서 바로 갈너저스나 세민은 불쾌한 감정이 뱃속에 차서 자기 집에 오도록 일수 생각이 머리를 떠나지 않었다. 모처럼 게획한 저녁 잔치가 똥친 망태가 된 것도 분햇으나 사람을 사람으로 안 알고 덤비는 일수의 태도가 한층 더 불쾌햇다. 일수에 대한 이  불쾌의 감정은 불쾌의 정도를 지나 거의 적의라고 할만치 강렬한 것이다.
이튼 날 세민은 아츰을 먹고 곧 애라한테를 갓다. 애라하고 일수의 욕이나 해야 속이 시원할 것 같엇다.
애라는 기분이 나뿐지 누어잇다가
「열이 또 좀 납니다만 대단치는 않어요」
하며 이러나 안는다. 방안에는 귤 껍데기 사과 껍데기 어쩌녁의 끄트레기가 난잡하게 흐트러진 채다.
「원 세상에는 별 자두 다 잇군. 그런 뻔뻔헌 치가 잇담」
세민은 어쩌녁 생각을 새로히 하며 애라를 건너다 보앗다.
「아이 아이상해서!」
애라도 혀끝을 찻다.
「남 모처럼 게획한 파- 티를 그 꼴을 맨든담」
「어찌 그러케 유둘유둘헌지」
그만침만 해도 세민은 가슴이 좀 후련한 것 같했다.
애라는 또 무슨 화나는 일이 잇는지＜262＞
「참 속이 상해서!」
또 한번 혀끝을 차고 그러나 빙긋이 웃으면서 세민을 치어다본다.
「 - 그 녀석이 어쩌녁에 또 왓드랍니다.」
「녜? 누가 안일수가요?」
너무나 의외의 말에 세민의 놀남은 컷다.
「참 정말- 」
하며 애라는 지난 밤 이야기를 하엿다. 세민과 일수를 보낸 후 주섬주섬 방안을 치고 잇는데 안일수가 도루 찾어와 덮어놓고 방으로 틀어왓다는 것이다. 그래고는 김세민을 어떠케 해 친햇느냐는 둥 세민과의 새에 연애 관게가 잇는 것이라는 둥 전번 자기가 애라에게 대해 쓴 글은 다음 기회에는 취소라도 하겟스니 그 일에 대해 자기를 더 어떠케 생각하지 말어달나는 둥 앞으로는 충심으로 잘 지도 해줄테니 그러케 알고 전과 같이 교제해 달나는 둥 횡설수설하며 밤 한시가 되도록 문짓대다는 것이다.
「거 참 지독한 친구로군」
세민이 감탄하노라니까 애라는 작난스레 방긋이 웃으며
「내중에는 무슨 강짜를 허는 것인지 김선생허구 혼인을 헐테냐는 둥 김선생헌테는 처자가 없을리 없다는 둥 별별 소리를 다 허겟지요. 원 사람이 뻔뻔허다기루선- 」
애라는 무심코 일수의 말을 전하는 것이겟스나 「결혼」과 「처자」의 문제는 세민에게는 지나가는 말로는 들니지 않었다. 그러나 그 문제라면 세민으로서는 이미 결심한 바가 잇는 것이었다.
「그런 말까지 허구 갓으니까. 흠- 」
세민이 참된 얼굴이 되며 애라를 보니까 애라는 고개를 숙인다. 세민은 말이 난 김에 아조 이 문제를 해결하리라 하엿다.
「그러지 않어도 벌서붙어 나는 그 말슴을 허랴든 것인데 기회가 없었든 것입니다. 허기는 애라씨가 몰으시는지 난 몰으지만 일수 말대로 나는 처자가 잇는 몸입니다. 그러치만 애라씨만 그 마음이 되어 주신다면 그런 문제는＜263＞ 우리 둘에게는 절대로 장해가 되지 않을 것입니다. 나는 벌서붙어 결심한 바가 잇읍니다」
애라는 얼골을 틀어 잠간 세민을 처다보고는 도루 고개를 숙인다.
「처자에 대해서는 지금 길게 설명헐 가수 없읍니다만은 어쨋든 나는 지금까지 단 한번을 여편네헌테 부부의 애정을 느껴본 일이 없는 말허자면 불행헌 사람입니다. 여태것 그대루 지내온 것은 단지 나헌테 남같은 맹목적 용기가 없었기 때문입니다. 애라씨는 말허자면 내 은인이기도 하시죠. 애라씨를 알게 되엇기 때문에 여러 해를 꿍꿍거리구 알키만 허든 문제를 해결헐 용기가 난 것이니까요. 애라씨 나와 결혼해 주시죠? 물논 승낙해 주시겟죠? 그전 처자의 문제로 애라씨를 괴롭게 허지는 절대로 안켓읍니다. 그것쯤은 나를 믿어 주시겟죠?」
여기까지 한숨에 말햇스나 애라는 고개를 숙이고 치마자락을 만적어릴 뿐이다. 세민은 애라의 손을 잡으며
「승낙해 주세요. 녜. 승낙허시죠?」
애라의 입으로 시선을 모으고 춤을 삼켓다.
세민의 생각에는 이러케 정식으로 결혼을 청하면 십중팔구는 애라는 곧 기뻐하며 승낙하리라 하엿다. 그런데 웬일인지 애라는 여전히 고개를 숙인채 한참이나 말없이 잇드니 세민에게 쥐인 손을 가만히 빼며
「그런 이야기는- 」
한 채로 말끝을 맺지 않고 빙긋이 웃으며 세민을 치어다 보앗다. 애라의 태도는 세민에게는 몹시 의외엿다.
「웨 그러십니까. 네. 애라씨만 승낙허신다면 처자 문제는 당장에라도 해결지을 작정입니다. 아니 승낙허시든 안튼 처자 문제는 아주 결정을 지어버리랴구 벌서붙어 결심한 것입니다」
「그 말슴야 잘 알어듯겟구 저도 무어 그런 것을 문제삼고 잇는 것은 아닙니다만은- 」
애라는 또 말끝을 맺지 않고 고개를 도리켓다. 그러면 무엇 때문에 그리는 것일까. 세민은 초조하기 짝이 없었다. 그러타고 애라가 어떤 다른 사람과 무슨 약속이 잇는 것이라고는 상상할 수도 없고-
「그럼 웨 그러세요. 네.」
애라는 고개를 들며＜264＞
「우선 - 몸이나 성해야 - 그런 생각을 해 보는게 아니에요.」
가느단 소리로 뜨문뜨문 말한다. 얼골에는 인생의 히망을 단념한 절망의 검은빛이 짙게 떠돈다.
세민의 가슴에는 뜨거운 감격이 새삼스레 치밀어 올나왓다. 힘없이 자기를 처다보는 애라의 얼골이 도를 통한 성자의 거룩한 자태와도 같이 보엿다.
「몸이야 차차 회복되지오」
그는 감격에 목메여 겨우 이 말만을 하엿다.
「글세요. 회복이 되면야- 」
애라는 빙긋이 웃는다.
「- 아 회복이 되면야」
안타까운 히망이 동시에 동시에 두 사람의 가슴을 옹조렷다.
「넉넉잡어 봄까지에는 완전헌 몸이 되실게니 그러거든 우리 조흔날을 골나- 」
세민은 기꺼운 결혼의 플랜을 속삭여 애라에게 힘을 주랴 어느 결에 자기도 마음이 점점 암담해가 말끝이 맺어지지 않엇다.
- 애라의 병이 봄에논 나을 것인가?
가슴에 든 병 더군다나 이십 안짝에 가슴에 든 병은 봄이 되면 도진다는 이야기는 들은 일 잇스나 낳는다는 이야기는 들은 일이 없었다. 세민의 마음은 절망의 담베락에 부디트린 것 같엇다. (次輯續)＜265＞
