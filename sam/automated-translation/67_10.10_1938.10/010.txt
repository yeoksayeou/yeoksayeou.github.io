Magazine Title: 삼천리 제10권 제10호
Publication Date: 1938년 10월 01일
Article Title: 娥眉山月 (Moon over Mount Emei)
Author: 李白 (Li Bai)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

娥眉山月
						李白
娥眉山月半輪秋
影入平羌江水流
夜發淸溪向三峽
思君不見下鄱州

The autumn wind is already blowing chilly, making one long for the autumn night's mountain moon. At this time, we come to think of the mountain moon of Mount Emei and the Sage Poet Li Bai who sang of it. Mount Emei is 800 *li* from Chungking (重慶), in the famous land of Pach'ok (巴蜀). When gazing upon this mountain on a bright autumn night, anyone would become immersed in the poetic world of Li Bai and feel a sense of mystery and inspiration. <9>

**Summary**

The article presents Li Bai's famous poem "Moon over Mount Emei" and follows it with a short commentary. The commentary evokes the feeling of autumn and connects it to the imagery in Li Bai's poem, specifically mentioning Mount Emei's location relative to Chungking in the Szechwan (Pach'ok) region. It encourages readers to appreciate the poetic sentiment and feel the mystery and inspiration associated with the mountain and the poem on an autumn night. From a historical perspective, the article demonstrates the continued appreciation and integration of classical Chinese literature and culture within Korean intellectual life during the Japanese colonial period. The reference to specific Chinese locations like Chungking and Szechwan also provides geographical context understood by the readership of the time.
