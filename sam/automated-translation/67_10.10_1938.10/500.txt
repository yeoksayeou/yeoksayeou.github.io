Article Title: 編輯 後書 (Editor's Postscript)
Author: N/A
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Notice/Editorial Postscript)

Now that the autumn wind arises, we realize that all spirits are renewed. In early September, the Government-General invited numerous figures from within and without the territory to hold a conference on measures for the current situation, determining the path for the peninsula under the long-term war. Henceforth, all affairs on the peninsula will move forward even more actively.

Externally, the Imperial Army's offensive against the three towns of Wuhan (武漢) is currently developing fiercely. The day will come when the Chiang (蔣) regime, just as it abandoned Shanghai (上海) or Nanjing (南京), will now abandon Hanyang (漢陽) and flee far away in defeat. In this way, the great tide of East Asia is hastening historical transformation with bold steps.

The flood damage throughout the Hamgyŏng North and South provinces (咸南北) has swallowed up many lives and assets. We must not be stingy in sharing a warm piece of clothing and a bowl of rice with these flood-stricken compatriots (罫災 동포), and furthermore, we hope that the hand of relief reaches them with utmost urgency.

Once again, as the autumn wind arises, we realize that spirits are renewed. Going out into the streets, one still sees flyers for "some collected works" or "some lecture meeting," and the energetic procession of youths is seen on the thoroughfares, making one realize that spirits are refreshed by the autumn wind.

As we are now in a long-term war, paper is scarce and its price has risen, so all printed materials cannot avoid being affected by this; newspapers and magazines are all reducing pages and circulation. This journal, too, complying with this national policy (국책), announces that it cannot expand for the time being.

We had promised in last month's issue, which featured the Youth Digest (청춘 抄), to serialize the "Digest of Newspapers and Magazines" (신문 잡지 抄), but due to page count constraints, it was omitted in this issue. We will resume it starting from next month's issue.<304>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from October 1938 reflects on the changing season and connects it to the intensifying wartime atmosphere under Japanese rule. It mentions a Government-General meeting to decide the peninsula's direction during the ongoing Sino-Japanese War, confidently predicts Japanese victory (specifically the fall of Wuhan), calls for aid for flood victims in the Hamgyŏng provinces, notes the continued cultural activities despite the times, and announces page/circulation cuts for the magazine due to wartime paper shortages, framing this as compliance with national policy. For historians, the article starkly reveals the colonial administration's wartime mobilization efforts, the mandated conformity of the press ("complying with this national policy"), the optimistic pro-Japanese portrayal of the war effort, and the juxtaposition of everyday concerns like flood relief and cultural events with the overarching reality of war and colonial control.

編輯 後書
이제 추풍이 起하매 모든 의기 갱신하여짐을 깨닷나이다. 총독부에선 9월 초순에 內外地의 다수한 인사를 초청하여 시국 대책 조사회를 여러 장기전 하에 있는 반도의 진로를 결정하고 있읍니다. 앞으로는 반도의 모든 정세가 더 일층 활발하게 움지겨 나갈 것이외다.

밖으로는 지금 황군의 武漢 3鎭의 공략전이 맹렬하게 전개되고 있나이다. 蔣 정권은 上海나 南京을 버리드시 이제 漢陽을 버리고 멀니 패주할 날이 올 것이외다. 이리하야 동양의 대세는 대담한 거름으로 역사의 변혁을 재촉하고 있읍니다.

咸南北 일대의 수해는 많은 인명과 재화를 삼켜버리고 말었읍니다. 우리는 이 罫災 동포에 따듯한 옷 한 벌과 밥 한 그릇 나노 주기를 앳기지 말어야 할 것이며 더욱 그 구제의 손이 한 시급히 미처지기를 바라나이다.

또 한번 추풍이 起하매 의기 갱신하여짐을 깨닷나이다. 거리에 나서면 그래도 「무슨 전집」「무슨 연설회」하는 삐라가 보이고 청소년의 긔운 찬 행진이 街路에 보이니 추풍에 心氣一新하여 짐을 깨닷나이다.

지금은 長期戰 하인 것 만치 紙物이 귀하고 또 값이 높아젔기에 모든 인쇄물이 이 영향을 밧지 않을 수 없서 신문 잡지는 모다 減 頁과 減 部數를 하고 있나이다. 본지 또한 이 국책에 順하야 당분간은 확장 못할 것을 고하여 둡니다.

去月號 본지를 청춘 抄로 하고 게속하여 각 「신문 잡지 抄」를 연재키로 약속하였든 바 이번 호는 百數 관계로 缺하였고 다시 來月號부터 계속하껐나이다.＜304＞
