Organization and Activities of the Xinminhui
						By Huang He Scholar in Beijing

Just as Manchukuo has the Concordia Association (協和會), focusing on the harmony of the five ethnicities—Manchu (滿洲), Yamato (大和), Korean (朝鮮), Han (漢), and Mongol (蒙古)—and proceeding according to the spirit of the nation's founding, the Beijing Provisional Government (維新政府), which now controls the five provinces of North China (北支), likewise has the Xinminhui (New People's Association - 新民會) under its wing, marching towards ethnic harmony and the line of new governance. This reporter visited Mr. Miao Bin (繆斌), the head of the Central Guidance Department (effectively, the Chairman of the Central Executive Committee) of the Xinminhui, at the Xinmin headquarters in the judicial district of Beijing to exchange frank opinions, seeking to understand what this "Xinminhui and Xinminzhuuyi (New People's Doctrine - 新民主義)" are. Mr. Miao Bin is considered Wang Kemin's (王克敏) right-hand man in New China, and the Xinminhui will henceforth be entirely led by him. He previously served as an instructor at the Huangpu Military Academy (黃埔 軍官 學校), Director of the Civil Affairs Department of Jiangsu Province (江蘇省 民政廳長), and a member of the Kuomintang (國民黨) Central Executive Committee, and has also studied in Japan. His writings include "Theory of the Unity of Civil and Military Virtue" (文武一德論), which explains the theory of the New People's Doctrine.

Foundation of the New People's Doctrine
Reporter - What is the foundation of the New People's Doctrine? What is it based on?
Mr. Miao - It is based on the teachings of Confucius and Mencius (孔孟), meaning the New People's Doctrine is not new, but it is by no means a return to ancient Confucianism either. It is the adaptation and advancement of Confucianism, the inherent ideal of the East, to be applicable to the modern era.
Reporter - In that case, can an ancient ideology serve as the guiding principle for modern China (支那)?
Mr. Miao - Ancient Chinese people looked at Confucianism and thought it lacked dynamism due to its moralistic nature and austere lifestyle.<174> That is, Confucian scholars could read texts and write essays, but their thoughts and actions were disconnected from the modern world. Therefore, young people consider Confucianism outdated, but that is incorrect. I believe that morality must possess the power to act. Strength arises only when put into practice. That generates daily creation and development. The philosophy of the New People's Doctrine is dynamic. China, unaware of this for the past 2,000 years, was in a dark age. Now, we move towards a new Eastern ideal. Therein lies the mission of the New People's Doctrine.

The Sole, Absolute Organization
Reporter - Then, what kind of relationship will the Xinminhui and the new state build in the future? For example, will it be like Manchukuo's Concordia Association or Nazi Germany?
Mr. Miao - I don't know the details about Manchukuo's Concordia Association, but I think it will take a form similar to Nazi Germany. It will become completely one with the new state. If the government is the exterior, the Xinminhui is the interior (裏). For instance, the position of Xinminhui Chairman is currently vacant, but in the future, when the new state is formed, its head of state will concurrently serve as Chairman. Locally, the governor of each province will also serve as the head of the guidance department for that province, making the organization structurally unified. That is, the provincial, prefectural, and county offices are governing bodies that make the populace carry out matters necessary for nation-building, but they do not carry them out themselves. Conversely, the Xinminhui, as the governed, cooperates to resolutely implement social reforms necessary for nation-building in their own lives. Of course, since the ideal of the new state is unified, the Xinminhui is the sole, absolute organization.

Immediate Priority Issues
Reporter - Concretely, what kind of work does the Xinminhui do?
Mr. Miao - The current Guidance Outline of the Xinminhui is a system we intend to implement over the next three to four years. For the time being, we plan to undertake economic projects for the farmers who constitute 90% of the population. This is not Western-style capitalism but starts from the standpoint of Eastern familism; farmers <175> unite around the head of the household (戶主) to save the family. We will develop this from family units to villages and then to counties. We want to pursue the benefit of the whole through the cooperation of each family. For this reason, we have spent hundreds of thousands of gold [units] to distribute seeds to farmers. Also, improving the economic conditions of farmers by undertaking the repair of the Yellow River (黃河) and Yongding River (永定河), which caused the poverty of Chinese peasants, is a priority issue. As a second step, we plan to establish commercial branches, administrative branches, and industrial branches in each region, considering the improvement of the whole from a familistic standpoint.

Dissemination of New People's Education
Reporter - Then, how will you train the populace?
Mr. Miao - The New People's Doctrine is by no means a principle only for the new government. Through it, East Asia (東亞) can also achieve healthy development for the first time. First, we intend to win over influential figures and the intellectual class in each region and use them as points for dissemination. We also plan to have young people from various regions enter the central training center to receive New People's education, while simultaneously providing military training. When these youths return to their regions, holding both the brush and the sword, they will carry out the most necessary public security work, suppressing bandits and also conducting welfare movements. China's 400 million people are indeed numerous, but if organized into one, they cannot be called too many.
Reporter - What about relations with foreign countries?
Mr. Miao - In the future, we intend to launch an international movement for the New People's Doctrine. Concretely, we will shake hands firmly with Germany (獨逸) and Italy (伊太利) from the standpoint of anti-communism (防共) and consequently spread the doctrine's propaganda east and west, aiming to traverse Asia. Directing the eyes of the Chinese people, who are internationally oriented towards the Soviet Union (蘇聯), towards Germany, Italy, etc., is also a guiding goal of the Xinminhui.

Pivot of World Morality, Winning Over the Masses
Reporter - Will the New People's Doctrine be welcomed by the Chinese intelligentsia, who are steeped in the glamorous atmosphere of Communism or the Three People's Principles (三民主義)?
Mr. Miao - I can assert that the Communist Party lacks the elements to grow in China. Originally, the Communist Party operated under the banner of the Kuomintang. The Kuomintang has become a nursery for the Communist Party. They (彼等) do not know the excellent foundation of Confucianism. <176> Their faces are Chinese, but in reality, they are not Chinese. What can be said to them? We can only concentrate fully on winning over the new masses.
Reporter - What has the Xinminhui been doing so far?
Mr. Miao - We have already held rallies to annihilate communism (滅共) and have carried out movements necessary for the development of the new government. We have also worked on the enlightenment of the masses and agricultural relief. Along with that, we have put effort into labor service movements. What China has in abundance is labor power. By contributing this labor power, we can soon build a society good to live in. Students and members of society are doing work like this (如斯히) that we can pass down to our descendants. Through such service, for example, if we build a road heading west, even just one step at a time from the Beijing city gate, it will eventually cross Central Asia (中央 亞洲). Through that route (루-트), the New People's Doctrine will permeate each ethnicity. It will become the pivot of world morality—this can hardly be dismissed as just a dream. Under a lofty ideal, we intend to achieve steady development and add new creations daily.
The Zhonghua Xinminhui (中華 新民會), which held a splendid inauguration ceremony on December 23rd last year at Huai Ren Tang (懷仁堂) in Beijing, attended by Provisional Government Administrative Committee Chairman Wang Kemin (王克敏) and others, takes as its ideology the ancient political morality of the East, clarifying the Xinmin theory from the "Great Learning" (大學)—"在于新民" [It rests in renovating the people]. Its mission is the political enlightenment and training of the masses of reborn China. The association's supreme governing (至統) structure is broadly divided into an Inspection Department and a Guidance Department. The Guidance Department is the mainstream, with its core placed in the Central Guidance Department, to which the Capital Guidance Department (Beijing) and regional Provincial Guidance Departments are directly subordinate. Under the provinces, County Guidance Departments and City Guidance Departments are established. Aiming for cooperative movements in China's rural economy, it strives for rural construction work. Along with political enlightenment movements, it directly enters the villages, which account for 80-90% of the Chinese population, to strive for the improvement and reconstruction of the lives of ignorant farmers wandering on the edge of starvation, rural revitalization, and economic construction. To achieve its objectives, it utilizes the autonomous organizations that form the traditional centers of Chinese villages. As a first step, it focuses on building rural cooperative societies, thoroughly implementing the Xinmin political movement in the regions, establishing experimental areas for rural cooperative societies, and disseminating a public health network nationwide. <177> Furthermore, it has steadily implemented various measures, such as opening New People's Schools (新民塾) to train mid-level leaders, fostering technical instructors for rural cooperative societies, and establishing job placement services for laborers. On May 28th and 29th, the first plenary meeting of the Central Guidance Department was held, attended by Vice Chairman Zhang Yanqing (張燕卿), Central Guidance Department Head Miao Bin (繆斌 - referred to as 繆武 here, likely a typo), and newly appointed Vice Head, former Metropolitan Police Commissioner Hayakawa Saburō (早川三郞), along with various department heads and staff. They reviewed the achievements since its establishment and discussed future policies, including the expansion of the youth training center and the large-scale formation of Xinmin Youth and Juvenile Corps. This involves training young people at the Central Youth Training Center in Beijing since February this year, using them as leaders to form corps unit by unit, centered around each [location - 犸� seems corrupt, perhaps 區?], down to the village level, and having them directly guide the youth and juvenile corps. The Youth Corps comprises those aged 18 to 35, engaging in Youth Corps-style enlightenment and self-cultivation movements, while also serving as armed self-defense groups (自警團) for the villages. The Juvenile Corps consists of boys and girls aged 12 to 17, undertaking broad activities like social service and labor service. This bears some resemblance to the Nazi youth movement. The Xinminhui, with great ambition and enthusiasm, is striving to rally 5 million youths and juveniles under a three-year plan. <178>

**Summary**

This 1938 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* features an interview with Miao Bin, a key figure in the Japanese-backed Provisional Government in Beijing, about the Xinminhui (New People's Association). Miao Bin explains the Xinminhui's ideology ("New People's Doctrine") as a modernized, dynamic application of Confucianism aimed at revitalizing China. He describes the organization's structure as parallel to the state, similar to the Concordia Association in Manchukuo or the Nazi Party in Germany, intending to become the sole political force. Key activities focus on rural reform through familistic cooperative models, infrastructure projects (river repair), mass mobilization, youth training (modeled partly on Nazi youth groups), and anti-communist propaganda. Internationally, the goal is to align China with Germany and Italy against the Soviet Union. The latter part of the article details the Xinminhui's structure and activities, including youth corps formation and rural development projects.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it presents a clear, albeit propagandistic, view of the ideology and functioning of a major collaborationist organization in Japanese-occupied China, as reported for a Korean audience under Japanese rule. It reveals the attempt to legitimize the puppet regime by co-opting traditional Confucian values, the explicit admiration for fascist models (Nazi Germany), the focus on anti-communism, and the ambition to reshape Chinese society through top-down mobilization and indoctrination, particularly targeting youth and rural populations. Historians would find the direct articulation of the Xinminhui's goals, its comparison to fascist organizations, and the description of its structure and programs (like the youth corps and rural cooperatives) valuable for understanding the nature of collaboration and Japanese imperial ideology in East Asia. The framing of the Xinminhui by a Korean author (or at least published in a Korean magazine) also offers insight into the complex dynamics within the Japanese empire.

新民會의 組織과 活動
						在北京 黃河學人
만주국에 協和會가 있어 滿洲, 大和, 朝鮮, 漢, 蒙蒙古의 5민족의 協和를 위주하야, 건국 정신에 沿하야 진행하여 가드시 이제 北支 5省을 치하에 둔 北京 維新政府에서는 역시 그 配下에 新民會가 있어 민족 協和와 신정치의 線에 향하야 행진하고 있다. 이제 기자는 이 「新民會 及 新民主義」란 어떤 것이냐 하는 것을 알고저 新民會 중앙 지도 부장(중앙 집행 위원장) 繆斌씨를 北京 사법가의 신민 본부에 방문하여 기탄 없는 의견을 교환하였다. 繆斌씨는 新支那에서는 王克敏 씨의 한 팔이며 금후 新民會는 一切가 氏에 의하야 인솔되어 갈 것이다. 氏는 이전에 黃埔 군관 학교 교관 江蘇省 民政廳長 國民黨 중앙집행위원을 역임하였고 일본에도 遊한 일이 있다. 저서로는 「文武一德論」이 있어 신민주의의 이론을 說한 바 있다.

新民主義의 基礎
기자 - 신민주의의 기초 신민주의는 무었에 기초를 두었음니까.
繆씨 - 孔孟의 敎에 基한 것으로 즉 신민주의는 새로운 것은 아니나 그러타고 결코 옛 유교에의 복고도 아님니다. 동양 본래의 이상인 유교를 현대에 즉 應케하고 진보시킨 것이외다.
기자 - 그러타면 옛날의 이데으로기가 현대 支那의 지도 원리 될 수 있을가요.
繆씨 - 옛 支那인은 유교를 보고 그것이 도덕적이며 생활이 근엄한 점에서 활동력이 없는 것으로 생각하었지요.＜174＞ 즉 유학자는 글도 읽고 문장도 쓰나 그 생각이나 행동은 현대와는 인연이 멀지요. 그럼으로 젊은 사람은 유교는 낡은 것으로 알고 있는데 그것은 틀인 일로 나는 도덕은 행동력을 가지지 않으면 안된다고 생각하오. 실행에 옴기는 때야말로 힘이 生하지요. 그것이 날마다. 창조와 발전을 生하는 것이지요. 신민주의의 철학은 동력적인 것이지요. 이것을 알지 못하든 과거 2천년의 支那는 암흑 시대이었지요. 이제부터 새로운 동양의 이상에 향하지요. 이에 신민주의의 사명이 있음니다.

唯一 絶對의 團體
기자 - 그러면 新民會와 신국가는 엇더한 관계로 장래 결연되여 갈가요. 가령 만주의 協和會 나치스의 독일에서와 같은 것임니까.
繆씨 - 만주의 協和會에 대하여는 상세한 것을 모르기니와 나치스 독일에서 하는 형태로 되리라 생각하오. 완전히 신국가와 일체가 되는 것으로 정부를 표할진대 新民會는 속(裏)이 되오. 例하컨대 新民會 회장은 지금 空位이나 장래 신국가가 되면 그 원수가 회장을 겸하고 지방에 있어는 각 省의 省長이 그 省의 지도 부장을 겸하는 식으로 조직상 합체가 되어 있지오. 즉 省과 道*縣役所는 통치 기관으로 민중에게 건국에 필요한 사항을 행하게 하지만 자기가 행하는 것은 아니지요. 이와 반대로 新民會는 被治者로서 자기 생활상에 건국에 필요한 사회 개혁을 협동하여 단행하는 것이지오. 물론 신국가의 이상은 통일된 것임으로 新民會는 유일 절대의 단체임니다.

當面의 先決 問題
기자 - 新民會는 구체적으로 여하한 일을 하는 단체임니까.
繆씨 - 지금 新民會의 指導 要項으로 되어있는 것은 금후 3,4년 동안에 하여 가려는 시스템이오. 당분간 9할을 점령하고 있는 농민의 경제 工作같은 것을 하려하오. 그것은 서양류의 자본주의가 아니고 동양의 가족주의 입장에서 출발하여 농민은 戶主를 중심으로하여＜175＞ 일가를 구하는 것이지오. 가족 단위로 一村에서 縣으로 발전 시키지오. 각 가족의 합작으로 전체의 이익을 圖하도록 하고 십슴니다. 그 때문에 수십만 金을 드려서 농민에게 종자를 배급하였지오. 또 支那 빈농민의 원인을 지은 黃河와 永定河의 改修를 圖하여 농민의 경제적 조건을 향상시키는 것이 선결 문제이오. 제 2단계로는 각 지방에 상업의 分會 관리의 分會 공업의 分會를 만드러 가족주의적 입장에서 전체의 향상을 생각하는 바이오.
