Magazine Title: 三千里 제11호
Publication Date: 1931년 01월 01일
Article Title: 旗前進·人不進, 送年辭 (The Flag Advances, The People Do Not: A Year-End Address)
Author: 파인 (P'ain)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial)

A single candle flame, by itself, is nothing. But when that candlelight lights a thousand, ten thousand other candles like it, there arises a great light and heat (렬) that does not envy the sun and moon. Do not say that the number of people holding the flag (긔든 사람) today is small. Who can deny that one day, they will all become people holding lit candles, emitting light and heat?

In an Indian song (인도의 놀애), there is a passage: "Oh traveler on the road, choose the perilous path where evil (惡) is piled up." This means to choose and walk towards the place where there are people to fight and things to fight for. What is the use of setting up camp where there is no enemy? Those holding the flag must always lead the many onto the battlefield.

Now, we send off the Gyeong'o Year (庚午年 - 1930). Did those holding the flag truly go where they ought to have gone? Were candles numbering ten thousand, a hundred thousand, newly lit? When looking back at the camps that were said to have concentrated all efforts on chasing away evil and poverty, the lament "The flag advances, the people do not" (旗前進, 人不進) is not absent.
-P'ain-<1>

**Summary**

This editorial, written as a year-end reflection for 1930, uses the metaphors of candlelight and battlefields to critique the state of a movement (likely nationalist or social reform). The author, P'ain (likely Kim Tong-hwan), expresses concern that while the ideals or leadership ('the flag') may be advancing, the general populace ('the people') are not following or being mobilized effectively. He urges leaders ('those holding the flag') to actively seek out struggle ('the perilous path where evil is piled up') and engage the masses, lamenting the past year's failure to bring the people along in the fight against 'evil and poverty.' For historians, this piece offers insight into the frustrations and strategic thinking within Korean intellectual or activist circles during the colonial period. The call to action, the critique of leadership failing to mobilize the populace ("Flag advances, People do not"), and the coded language of fighting 'evil and poverty' reflect the challenges and internal debates of movements operating under Japanese rule.

旗前進·人不進, 送年辭
촛불 한대, 그것만 가지고는 아모것도 아니지만 그 촛불이 가튼 초 천자루 만자루에 불을 켜노을때 거기에는 일월을 부러워 안할 큰 빗과 렬이 이러난다. 오늘날 긔든 사람 수효가 적다고 마러라. 어느날은 모다 불을 켜쥔 촛불든 사람이 되어 빗과 렬을 발할 것을 누가 부인하랴.

인도의 놀애에 「길가는 행인아 惡이 싸여 잇는 험한 길을 골라가소」하는 구절이 잇다. 싸울 사람과 싸울거리 잇는 곳으로 골라서 거러가란 말이다. 적이 업는 곳에 가서 진을 처 무얼하느냐? 긔든 사람은 항상 싸움터에 여럿을 잇글어 가야만 한다.

이제 庚午年을 보낸다. 과연 긔든 사람은 제 갈 곳으로 갓든가, 과연 만자루 십만자루의 초에는 새로히 불이 켜젓든가 惡과 가난을 쫏기에 전력을 집중하엿다든 진영을 회고할 때 旗前進, 人不進의 탄식이 업지 안타.
-파인-＜1＞
