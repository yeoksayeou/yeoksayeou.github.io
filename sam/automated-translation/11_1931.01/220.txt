**Body Text Translation:**
The Undying Queen of the Music World, Miss Yun Simdŏk: The Truth Investigated by a Newspaper Reporter
						Kim Ŭlhan

Sudden Rumors of Survival in Italy

1. December 7th
A rumor is spreading that Miss Yun Simdŏk (尹心德), thought to have passed away five years ago, is still alive in this world. A dead person is alive? Could such a thing really happen? No matter how much I think about it, it's utterly unbelievable.

However, my colleague Mr. K, who visits the Government-General (總督府) daily, says he personally witnessed Mr. Kim Ikchin (金益鎭)—the younger brother of Mr. Kim Ujin (金祐鎭), who committed love suicide (情死) with Miss Yun Simdŏk—appear directly at the Foreign Affairs Section (外事課) of the Government-General to submit a search request (搜索願) for both Yun and Kim. He added that while it's still unclear whether they are alive or dead, the mere fact that the close relatives of the deceased submitted a search request to the authorities<50> is enough to become article material with 100 percent interest.

A search request for dead people! Even without borrowing Mr. K's words, it's certainly an issue sufficient to draw public attention.

But is it really true that they are alive? According to those who deny the love suicide theory, the story that Yun and Kim threw themselves into the blue waters of the Hyŏnhae-t'an (玄海灘) from the Kwanbu Ferry (關釜連絡船) Toksu Maru (德壽丸) in the pre-dawn hours of August 2nd, Taishō 15 (1926), was merely a staged act (一幕 演劇). In reality, they supposedly bribed a boy in the first-class cabin of the Toksu Maru to make it appear as though they had committed love suicide. Then, they themselves went via Nagasaki (長崎) to Shanghai (上海), crossed over to Italy under Chinese names, and are now said to be running a musical instrument shop (樂器店) in Rome (로-마), living a very harmonious family life. The direct reason Mr. Kim Ikchin suddenly submitted a search request for his elder brother to the authorities recently is reportedly because the president of a certain Japanese newspaper company (某 日本新聞 社長) in Fukuoka (福岡) included in his travelogue, published in the newspaper, an account of a young couple resembling Kim Ujin and Yun Simdŏk running a small musical instrument shop in Rome, Italy, where the husband was studying dramatic literature and the wife was studying vocal music. This prompted him, thinking "Could it be?", to frantically submit the search request to the authorities.

But what is the truth? The more I think about it, the more it seems like an unsolvable riddle (수수꺽기).

The Price for a Living Corpse: 30,000 Won

2. December 10th
A certain well-informed source, famous for knowing others' rumors well, denies the love suicide of Yun and Kim based on the following three points, affirming the still hazy (雲霧가치 朦朧한) survival theory (生存說).

First, Miss Yun Simdŏk, who earned the honorable title "Queen of the Music World," had a naturally chic and cheerful (洒落快活) personality, making it unthinkable for her to commit suicide. Furthermore, in August of Taishō 15 (1926), when she supposedly committed love suicide, she told her younger sister, the pianist Miss Yun Sŏngdŏk (尹聖德), who was going to the United States (米國) to study piano:
"Sister Sŏngdŏk! Until I achieve great success, I will absolutely not send any news, so understand this and never look for me..." leaving behind these meaningful words.

Second, since their love suicide incident occurred, both families, especially the Kim family, endeavored to find at least the bodies (屍體) of the deceased, even placing advertisements offering rewards (懸賞) in various newspapers in Pusan (釜山), Shimonoseki (下關), etc., and striving to find the bodies. However, even now, five years after the incident, not a single trace resembling a body has been discovered.

Third, Miss Yun Simdŏk's family, perhaps also due to her 70-year-old mother still being alive, has denied the death rumors from the beginning and has not held a formal funeral ceremony (發喪) to this day, and so on...

If that is the case, considerable travel expenses would be needed just for the two of them to get to Italy. How much more so to be running a musical instrument shop in Rome, a foreign land thousands of miles away (萬里異域), where the mountains are unfamiliar and the waters strange? With what money could they possibly have carried out such an unprecedented, strange drama (前代未聞의 珍劇)?

"The act where Miss Yun Simdŏk appeared to have died must have been preceded by the establishment of a major secret pact (密約) with some influential musical instrument shop (樂器店). That is, before staging the love suicide drama with Mr. Kim Ujin, she first recorded a song implying death, titled 'In Praise of Death' (死의 讚美), onto a record (레코-드). If she then immediately carried out the love suicide drama, the general public, feeling more than 100 percent interest in the romantic love suicide drama of the so-called Queen of the Music World, would rush, competing with each other, to buy the record, wondering what kind of song 'In Praise of Death' is. Then, the record company would naturally profit greatly, and in return, Miss Yun would also receive considerable compensation. The reason those two were able to go to Rome, Italy, is indeed because of the 30,000 Won reward (報酬金) received from a certain record company that recorded 'In Praise of Death,' on the condition of becoming 'living corpses' (산송장)."
So says a friend, speaking as if he truly knows the inside story. But even if Miss Yun was a popular musician in Chosŏn (朝鮮), could a record company really have given such an enormous compensation of 30,000 Won just for recording one song?......
If it is true that the record company paid 30,000 Won on the condition of becoming 'living corpses,' and if it is also true that the two secretly went (潛行) to Italy using the 30,000 Won as travel funds, what a fact more bizarre than a novel this would be!<51>
In any case, I am now halfway inclined to doubt (半信半疑) their deaths.

Survival Hinted at by a Sibling (同氣)

3. December 15th
To find out the truth about the Yun Simdŏk affair, which stands at the crossroads (岐路) of whether she is dead or alive, I visited Miss Yun Sŏngdŏk at Ewha Specialized School (梨花專門學校) today. This is because Miss Yun Simdŏk is Miss Sŏngdŏk's older sister. As I stepped onto the grounds of Ewha, not a single student could be seen in the desolate schoolyard, but the cheerful rhythm of a piano playing "dung-dang! dung-dang!" coming from the red two-story school building already seemed to make my heart solemn. When I pressed the doorbell attached to the entrance hall (玄關), an office worker came out and guided me to the music teachers' private room. Presently, a lady dressed entirely in Western clothes in a pure Yankee-style, very 'modern' (모-던), entered and said,
"Why are you here again today? What brings you here?"
Looking closely, it was Teacher Yun Sŏngdŏk.

Me: "Aren't you overjoyed?"
Yun: "What is there to be overjoyed about?"
Me: "Isn't it true that your older sister, Simdŏk, didn't die and is still alive? How overjoyed must you be, I mean."
Yun: "Well..."
Me: "What do you mean, 'Well...'? Mr. Kim Ikchin, the younger brother of Mr. Kim Ujin, even submitted a search request to the Government-General. Don't you know about that?!"
Yun: "Why wouldn't I know? Mr. Kim Ch'ŏlchin (金哲鎭), who is Mr. Kim Ikchin's other older brother, came and said there was a rumor that both are still alive in Italy and that he would submit a search request to the Government-General. I told him there would be no harm anyway, so he should go ahead and try it."
Me: "So isn't that rather joyful news? Although the truth is not yet known, the fact that there's talk of someone thought dead five years ago being vividly alive, leading to a search request being submitted to the authorities, isn't this good news for anyone who hears it?"
Yun: "It's good that someone said to be dead is not dead but alive. But I have nothing to be particularly happy about."
Me: "Why is that?"
Yun: "Why, you ask? It's because, from the very beginning, I absolutely did not believe in my sister's death. No, even now, I absolutely deny that death. Why should I be newly overjoyed just because people are making noise saying that someone I knew from the start wasn't dead, isn't dead?"
Me: "You denied the death from the beginning? Do you perhaps have any compelling evidence for that?"
Yun: "Well... When I was leaving for America, my sister (Simdŏk) told me that she would go to Italy immediately, and might cut off contact for quite a long time, but even so, I should never worry. So, I just believe my sister must be alive, that's all."
Me: "But the world considers them dead for five years already, yet you alone deny it, you mean?"
Yun: "I don't know about that. Neither I nor anyone in my family has ever said such a thing. The ones who made my sister into a dead person are the people of the world who always love to talk about others; that's their sin (罪)."
Me: "Then, is the story that Miss Simdŏk and Mr. Kim Ujin are currently living in Rome, Italy, true?"
Yun: "I cannot say. However, I still absolutely deny their deaths."
Me: "If what you say is true, they must surely be alive. Hasn't there been any news at all from Italy or perhaps elsewhere during this time?"
Yun: "Even if there had been some news, do you think I would talk about it now? All I can say is that I have denied my sister's death from the beginning. Beyond that, I absolutely cannot say anything else. Regarding that, I am a mute person (벙어리) who knows nothing."
Me: "If I were to honestly state what I am thinking right now, it seems there is some great secret hidden within Miss Yun Simdŏk's death, and the only person in the whole world who might know that secret is Miss Sŏngdŏk. What do you think?"
Yun: "A secret? Well. Anyway, just understand it that way, please."<52>
Me: "What is there to hide like that? In 40 days at the latest, a detailed reply will come from the Japanese Consulate (日本領事館) in Italy, and the whole hidden story (黑幕) will be revealed (判明). If their survival theory turns out to be true, how about announcing it fully right now?"
Yun: "N-no, even if it's true that they are living in Italy now, where is the law that says they must definitely be discovered by the Japanese Consulate staff? What's more, what if they aren't in Italy but somewhere else..."

Thus, Miss Yun Sŏngdŏk, generally keeping her lips sealed and not telling the detailed story from beginning to end, yet showing a meaningful demeanor as if there was something indeed, seemed to lament that people were overly sensitive (神經이 過敏한) about her sister, saying,
"Whether others are alive or dead, what concern is it? If they're dead, they're dead; if they're alive, they're alive. Anyway, Chosŏn society (朝鮮社會) is good at praising others and also good at criticizing others."
And she lightly shook her head from side to side.

4. December 17th
The Yun Simdŏk and Kim Ujin love suicide incident has merely unfolded the new fact that their relatives submitted a search request to the authorities, but the unsolvable riddle remains an unsolvable riddle.
The dead (?) have no mouths, so the truth of their life or death cannot yet be known, but anyway, as one who knew them well, I earnestly hope that the joyful news "Survival is a fact" will arrive and all doubts (의혹) will soon melt away like ice (氷解). The search request for Miss Yun Simdŏk, which has caused ripples on calm waters (平地에 波瀾)! What the result will be, of course, cannot be predicted. But the more I think about it, the more it is an unsolvable riddle. In any case, they say a definite reply will come from the Italian consulate within about 40 days, so let's wait until then! <53>

**Summary**

This 1931 article reports on persistent rumors that Yun Simdŏk, a famous Korean soprano, and her lover Kim Ujin, believed to have committed suicide together in 1926, might actually be alive and living in Italy. The reporter, Kim Ŭlhan, explores the basis for these rumors, including a search request filed by Kim Ujin's brother with the Government-General, alleged sightings reported in a Japanese newspaper, and the lack of discovered bodies. He interviews Yun Simdŏk's sister, Yun Sŏngdŏk, who enigmatically claims she never believed her sister died and hints at a secret departure for Italy. The article also mentions a sensational theory involving a 30,000 Won payment from a record company for Yun's hit song "In Praise of Death" in exchange for staging the suicide. The reporter concludes that the truth remains a mystery, awaiting official word from the Japanese consulate in Italy.

For historians, this article is interesting as it reflects the intense public fascination with celebrity figures and dramatic narratives in colonial Korea. The circulation of rumors, the involvement of colonial authorities (Government-General), the role of the press (both Korean and Japanese), and the speculation surrounding the couple's alleged escape (possibly funded by a record deal) highlight aspects of media culture, social anxieties, and perhaps a desire for narratives where individuals could defy tragic fates under colonial constraints. The sister's ambivalent responses and the reporter's own shifting perspective underscore the difficulty of discerning fact from fiction in a highly charged atmosphere.

						金乙漢
突然한 伊太利 生存說
1. 12월 7일
벌서 5년 전에 세상을 떠난 줄 알엇든 尹心德씨가 아즉도 세상에 사러 잇다는 소문이 들닌다. 죽은 사람이 사러 잇다고?  정말 그러한 일도 잇슬가? 암만 생각해 보아도 도모지 미들 수 업는 일이다.
그러나 날마다 總督府 출입을 하는 同人 K씨는 尹心德양과 함께 情死를 한 金祐鎭씨의 舍弟인 金益鎭이라는 분이 직접 總督府 外事課에 出頭하야 尹金 兩人에 대한 搜索願을 제출한 것을 이 눈으로 똑똑히 보앗다고 하며 사럿는지 죽엇는지는 물론 아즉 잘 모르지마는 어잿던 죽은 사람의 至親들이 당국에 搜索願을 제출한＜50＞ 사실만 가지고도 넉넉히 興味 100퍼-센트의 記事材料가 되겟다는 것을 보태여 말한다.
죽은 사람에게 搜索願! K씨의 말을 빌리지 안트래도 물론 남의 이목을 이끌기에 충분한 문제이다.
