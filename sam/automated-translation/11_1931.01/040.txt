Article Title: 新幹會全體大會, 代行中央執行委員會 光景 (Scene of the Singanhoe Plenary Session, Acting Central Executive Committee Meeting)
Author: 金起林
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

Scene of the Singanhoe Plenary Session, Acting Central Executive Committee Meeting
						Kim Kirim (金起林)

The Singanhoe, as a national unified party which "introduced XX elements from all layers and factions of the nation into its organization and strove to represent the popular XX desires," and which "sensed broadly and keenly the immediate national interests, defined the maximum possible X tactics based on the capabilities of each stage, and played the role of advocating cooperative XX," has been the object of the greatest interest among the Chosŏn populace because the scope of its organization is broad and the bond of interests that is its cohesive force is common to all classes within the nation.

It is one of the tragedies born from the extremely low-spirited objective circumstances surrounding the Singanhoe, past and present, that such an organization cannot hold a plenary session to look back on its past X achievements and, through popular will, discuss and define the most effective and scientific X tactics to constantly break through its future path.

Thus, the 3rd Central Executive Committee meeting held this time was a very significant gathering in the sense that it acted in place of the function of the Plenary Session, which is utterly impossible to hold under the current circumstances.

Although this Central Executive Committee meeting was deeply regrettable in terms of quantity [attendance], it can be asserted that in terms of quality, it did not disappoint the populace in the slightest. Why? Because this was truly a meeting of the fierce leaders of the Singan movement scattered throughout Chosŏn, and thus a meeting capable of truly representing the interests of the Singanhoe.

Not only this, but the past year was a period in which the Association suffered a severe blow to its ranks, starting with sending its Chairman and three Department Heads to prison, and inevitably had to endure the most painful trials under the rapidly pressing situation. For this reason too, this Central Executive Committee meeting drew our attention immensely.

Internally, because "various theories regarding its guiding principles and organizational form" led to "a strong tendency to doubt its value for the national movement, and most branches falling into stagnation, which characterized our Association's internal situation this time," this Central Executive Committee meeting, which had to look back and settle accounts for this critical year, also held very significant meaning internally.

These various circumstances made this Central Executive Committee meeting an intense focus of attention both internally and externally. To faithfully report the noteworthy proceedings daily to the interested populace, I occupied a back seat in the reporters' section during the two days of the meeting.

The 3rd Central Executive Committee meeting of the Singanhoe, acting for the Plenary Session, opened its first day's curtain a little later than scheduled, at 11:20 AM on October 9th, at the Association's headquarters inside the Tŏgwŏn Building (德元『삘딩』) on Chongno (鍾路) 2-ch'ŏm.

Numerous police officers from the Provincial Police Department, the Military Police Unit (憲兵隊), and the jurisdictional Chongno Station were present, and the gallery was filled with dozens of observers from various social organizations. Amidst thunderous applause, Mr. Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯) from the headquarters took the podium and delivered a profound and heartfelt opening address. Recalling the Association's painful past year,<8> his every word, along with the Association's two major slogans hanging on the left and right walls of the hall—
"National Total Strength into the Singanhoe!"
"Let's Boldlyを展開 (Develop/Unfold) the Struggle for Immediate Interests!"
—made the atmosphere in the hall even more tense.

As the provisional executive board, consisting of Chairman Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯), Vice-Chairman Kim Hangggyu (金恒奎), and Secretaries Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵) and An Ch'ŏlsu (安喆洙), took their seats and proceeded with the agenda, when Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵) attempted to report on the Chŏnju Ilbo (全州日報) insult article incident during the progress report, the attending police officers suddenly prohibited the reporting of the aforementioned item and seven subsequent items. An uneasy current began to flow through the hall.

Next, important progress matters were approved. According to the schedule, the revision of the regulations was next, but due to time constraints, preparations for the officer election were made first.
Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯), Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛), Kim Yŏnjin (金淵鎭), Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵), Chu Ik (朱翼), Hwang Ŭijun (黃義準), Kim Changhwan (金章煥), Chŏng Chongmyŏng (鄭鍾鳴), Pak Munhŭi (朴文熺)
were elected as the selection committee members, and the meeting recessed at 2:30 PM.

At 4:30 PM on the same day, the Chairman declared the meeting resumed. Right at the start, through a general vote, Mr. Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯) was elected as the Central Executive Committee Chairman, the highest responsible position in the Association, by an absolute majority. Although he repeatedly expressed humble intentions to decline with great earnestness, the committee ultimately did not accept his refusal. Thus, he finally gave a solemn and meaningful acceptance speech, and the entire committee, as well as the gallery, expressed their welcome with applause that seemed to shake the hall—one of the most beautiful dramatic scenes of this meeting.

The selection committee announced the results of the election for the new Central Executive Committee members as follows:
Central Executive Committee Members: Kim Ho (金湖), Sim Sangmin (沈相玟), Kim Ch'angyong (金昌容), Kim Ch'ŏl (金哲), Yi Wŏnsik (李源植), Kim Sijung (金時中), Ku Yŏndal (具然達), Yi Hanbong (李漢鳳), Ch'oe Sangjik (崔相稷), Kang Kidŏk (康基德), Kim Ch'anggwŏn (金昌權), Song Tuhwan (宋斗煥), Yi Chŏnghong (李廷鴻), Song Pyŏnghyŏk (宋秉赫), Yi Tŏgyong (李德容), Yi Tongsu (李東壽), Kim Wŏnho (金元浩), Kim Hyŏnggyu (金亨奎), Yun Chu (尹柱), Chu Ch'aehŭi (朱埰熙), Song Sugyŏk (宋洙格), Kim Saik (金士翼), Pak T'aehong (朴台弘), Sin Sangt'ae (申相泰), Hwang Sanggyu (黃尙圭), Yi Kwan'gu (李寬求), Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛), Kwak Sanghun (郭尙勳), Ch'oe Yunok (崔允鈺), Kim Hangjun (金恒俊), Kim Yonggi (金容起), Paek Kwansu (白寬洙), Pak Munhŭi (朴文熺), Yi Chŏng<9> (李正), Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵), Kim Hangggyu (金恒奎), Kang Sanghŭi (姜相熙), Yi Hangbal (李恒發), Sŏ Chŏnghŭi (徐廷禧)
Alternate Members: Pyŏn Hŭiyong (卞熙鎔), Kang Insu (姜仁洙), Yo Yŏngguk (姚永國), No Sŏkchŏng (盧錫正), Kim Kwidong (金貴東)
Central Inspection Committee Members: Yu Chint'ae (兪鎭泰), Yang Ponggŭn (楊奉根), Hyŏn Tongwan (玄東完), No Paegyong (盧百容), Pak Ŭiyang (朴儀陽)

Meanwhile, a congratulatory telegram addressed to the Central Executive Committee arrived from the Association's former Central Executive Committee Chairman, Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), who is in prison. While the secretary read this aloud, the hall became quiet as if water had been poured. With reverent applause, the committee members received this and resumed the proceedings.

The Chairman presented the draft amendment to the regulations and simultaneously declared that the Kyŏngsŏng (京城) Branch's proposal regarding the regulation amendment should also be discussed. At this, Mr. Hong Kimun (洪起文) from the Kyŏngsŏng Branch stood up sharply and "denounced the provisional executive board's lack of sincerity for delaying the discussion of the aforementioned proposal, which was submitted in the morning, until late, and at the same time questioned whether delaying the discussion of several other Kyŏngsŏng Branch proposals, mostly concerning personnel issues and officer elections, until after the officer elections were already completed, wasn't a planned action by the central leadership." He fiercely posed the question and withdrew the aforementioned proposal.

At this, provisional executive board Secretary An Ch'ŏlsu (安喆洙) offered his resignation, taking responsibility for this incident. Attacks and responses regarding this were exchanged between the headquarters and the branch for a long time. Eventually, the regulation amendment draft submitted by the headquarters was discussed. As a result, it was decided to:
Amend Article 14 of the Association's regulations, changing '過半數' (majority) to '從多數' (plurality).
Reduce the three departments in Article 22, '聯絡宣傳敎育' (Liaison, Propaganda, Education).
Insert the phrase '部長은 常務執行委員中에서 선거' (Department heads are elected from among the Standing Executive Committee members) into Article 24.
Delete entirely the clauses related to Articles 54 and 61 concerning '班' (groups/squads).

Next, the issue regarding the so-called words and actions contradicting the Association's guiding spirit by a certain central committee member from headquarters, which had been brewing low pressure between headquarters and the Kyŏngsŏng Branch for a long time, was submitted by the Kyŏngsŏng Branch in the form of a re-proposal and brought to the floor. The atmosphere in the hall instantly reached its peak tension. As the committee members, considering it a grave issue related even to the guiding spirit, requested the Kyŏngsŏng Branch to hear the facts, the Kyŏngsŏng Branch deferred this to the headquarters. However, committee member Chŏng Chongmyŏng (鄭鍾鳴) and several others insisted, "This is a major issue that could permanently bury a comrade from the movement line, so how can you not reveal the truth?" They persistently demanded an explanation from the Kyŏngsŏng Branch, but the Kyŏngsŏng Branch again evasively avoided explaining the facts, stating they could explain personally anytime but could not announce it officially.

While the hall was momentarily chaotic, suddenly Mr. Hong Kimun (洪起文) of the Kyŏngsŏng Branch, with firm resolve, clearly declared he would exemplify the aforementioned certain member's words and actions that contradicted the guiding spirit. The meeting thus reached its highest climax.

As his words repeatedly touched upon natural XX issues, etc., the attending police officers again prohibited discussion, stating the issue pertained to current affairs. Thus, the issue that had become the focus of interest fell back into a maze.

The committee selected Hwang Ŭijun (黃義準), Chu Ik (朱翼), and Kim Yŏnjin (金淵鎭) as an investigation committee, ordering them to urgently investigate the facts of the aforementioned incident and report to the next Central Executive Committee meeting. The meeting recessed again at 8:00 PM, and the committee members headed to the banquet hosted by the Kyŏngsŏng Branch at Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月館).

The meeting resumed the next day, the 10th, at 3:40 PM. Before entering into various discussions, Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛), An Ch'ŏlsu (安喆洙), and Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵) were selected as a review committee for the proposals submitted by headquarters and the Kyŏngsŏng Branch proposal. A 19-minute recess was declared to allow them to complete the review during that time.

As a result of the review, among the proposals submitted by the Standing Executive Committee, the item concerning stagnant branches was decided to be discussed together with the item concerning branch tours. Among the Kyŏngsŏng Branch proposals, only the item concerning the Kwangju (光州) Branch and the item concerning the lifting of the ban on the Three Associations (三總解禁) were adopted, but discussion of both was prohibited by the attending police officers.

During the discussion on organizational methods and communication methods, the central topics that drew the most interest were the issue of group admission and the question of whether cooperative struggle could be waged even with groups or individuals whose guiding spirit differed. The former, in particular, was a new and noteworthy proposal.

Centering on these two issues, the proposer Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵), Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛), Pak Munhŭi (朴文熺), and <10> Yi Hangbal (李恒發) engaged in fervent debate.

Hereafter, the main points of their arguments are presented.

Pak Munhŭi (朴文熺): Since the Singanhoe is not an organization formed through struggle—in other words, it's not an organization based on the struggle for the urgent daily interests of workers and peasants, but a diplomatic union of the floating petty bourgeoisie (小『뿌르죠아』)—its struggle is consequently not vigorous.

Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛): Is this statement merely acknowledging the fact that the Singanhoe's composition includes many petty bourgeois, or does it mean that the petty bourgeois should therefore withdraw from the Singanhoe? It's unclear.

Pak: Looking at the occupational statistics of the constituent members, it says peasants are numerous, but most of these are actually unemployed people who wrote 'agriculture' for convenience.

Han: That is a cognitive error. If past struggles were inactive, the question is how to effectively develop future struggles. The fundamental issue of the Singanhoe's organization is already a settled matter.

Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵): The Singanhoe is not a permanent union of the various classes within the nation, but only a temporary union for common immediate interests. The existence of such unified disunity is evidence that society has not evolved that much. Why? Because class differentiation is clear proof of social evolution. To expand and strengthen our current capabilities, I propose group admission.

Han: The term 'unified disunity' is not an appropriate expression for the Singanhoe's organizational form; it is a cooperative front (協同戰線). Even if class differentiation develops to an extreme, a cooperative front is possible and necessary. For cooperative struggle, I agree to group admission, provided that individual admission is also permitted for those deemed necessary.

Yi Hangbal (李恒發): Given that attitudes of doubt, neutrality, and secession towards the Singanhoe currently coexist, these various attitudes must also be mixed within any given group. Permitting admission of such groups may be an expansion in quantity, but it is a lowering of quality.

Pak: Chosŏn society is characterized by the remnants of feudal forms. Therefore, the bond of religious sentiment is stronger than class interests. Thus, Christian groups (基督敎단체) and other religious groups should also be brought into the organization.

After such debate, which was the most noteworthy discussion during this meeting, the group admission issue was ultimately deferred by Mr. Chu Ik's (朱翼氏) motion, and the second issue [cooperative struggle with differing groups], after being initially approved, was then cancelled. All other three proposals were deferred to the next Central Committee meeting. Finally, the issue concerning the words and actions of the certain member [某氏] came up again awaiting resolution. Investigation committee member Hwang Ŭijun (黃義準) reported the full details of the facts he investigated. After some discussion on this, the matter was ultimately handed over to the Central Inspection Committee. The individual at the center of the issue, the certain member [某氏], finally stood up, denied all the scandals attributed to him, clearly stated his intention to withdraw from the organizational sphere for the time being, and declared that he would henceforth settle his past only through actual struggle.

During the momentary commotion in the hall caused by this issue, someone from the gallery shouted towards the leadership, who had maintained a stance of protecting the certain member [某氏] until now, "The leaders are people just like X [某]!" At this, headquarters executive Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵) abruptly stood up and roared angrily, "Who said we are like X [某]? In what way are we like X [某]? For the Singanhoe movement, I would not avoid having my neck cut! I only regret losing even one fighter forever."

The strength of the Singanhoe certainly lies in its mass organization, but the existence of passionate individuals like Yi Chuyŏn (李周淵), sharp-minded theorists like Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛) who always clearly analyze presented issues to grasp and present their focal points, and other gentlemen, surely constitutes a significant part of its power. Adjourned at 8:00 PM with three cheers of "Manse!" (終) <11>

**Summary**

This article reports on the third meeting of the Singanhoe's Central Executive Committee in October 1930, which served as a substitute for a full plenary session due to difficult political circumstances. Written by Kim Kirim, it details the tense atmosphere marked by police surveillance and intervention, the election of Kim Pyŏngno as chairman, and internal friction. Key debates included a proposal from the Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) branch, challenges regarding a controversial member accused of actions contrary to the organization's spirit, and strategic discussions on whether to allow entire groups (including religious ones) to join the Singanhoe and whether to cooperate with ideologically different entities. The meeting highlighted deep divisions and the immense pressure the nationalist organization faced.

From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for its candid portrayal of the internal dynamics and ideological conflicts within the Singanhoe shortly before its dissolution in 1931. It reveals the tension between broad nationalist unity and emerging class-based perspectives (articulated by figures like Pak Munhŭi and debated by Han Pyŏngnak and Yi Chuyŏn), the practical difficulties of political organizing under Japanese colonial rule (censorship, arrests like Hŏ Hŏn's), and the critical debates about the organization's structure and strategy (group vs. individual membership, united front tactics) that ultimately contributed to its demise.

『민족의 各層 各派의 XX要素를 그 조직 내에서 引入하엿고 힘써 대중적 XX的 慾求를 대표하고』 『民族的 當面利害를 광범하게 예민하게 감지하고 各 階段의 역량에 基하야 가능한 최대한도의 X術을 規定하고 協同XX을 提唱하는 역할을 담당』한 민족적 單一黨으로서의 新幹會는 그 조직의 범위가 광범하고 단결력의 紐帶인 利害關係가 민족 내의 各 階級에 共通하엿슴으로 따라서 朝鮮 民衆의 최대의 관심의 대상이다.
이러한 新幹會가 全體大會에서 그 과거의 X績을 回顧하며 대중적 의지에 의하야 가장 효과적이요 과학적엔 X術을 討議規定하야 부단히 그 前路를 打開하지 못한다 함은 新幹會를 에워싸고 도는 과거와 또는 현재의 至極히 低調를 띈 객관적 情勢가 나흔 悲哀의 하나다.
그리하야 今番에 열리엇든 제3회 中央執行委員會는 目下의 形勢로는 도저히 열기 不能한 全體大會의 職能을 代行한다는 의미에서 매우 중대성을 띤 회합이엿다.
今番의 中央執行委員會는 量에 잇서서 甚히 遺憾이엿스나 그 質에 잇서서는 秋毫도 민중을 실망식히지 안엇다는 것을 단언할 수가 잇다. 왜 그러냐하면 이는 실로 全朝鮮에 散在한 新幹運動의 猛將連의 회합이며 그만치 新幹會의 利害를 여실히 대표할 수 잇는 회합이엿든 까닭이다.
이 뿐 아니라 과거 一週年間은 동會는 그 委員長과 三部長을 獄舍에 보낸 것을 비롯하야 그 陳容에 深刻한 타격을 바든 기간이요 급박해 가는 정세 아래서 가장 괴로운 試練을 당하지 안흐면 아니 되엿든 까닭에 今番의 中央執行委員會는 이러한 의미에서도 우리들의 注意를 환기하여 마지 안엇다.
그러고 대내적으로는 그 『指導理論及組織形態에 대한 種種의 이론으로』『그 민족운동에 대한 價値를 懷疑하는 傾向이 濃厚하고 대부분의 支會가 沈滯하여지는 것이 今番 우리 會內  정세의 특징』이엿든 까닭에 이 危機的인 一週年을 回顧 淸算하지 안흐면 아니 될 이변 中央執行委員會는 내적으로도 매우 중대한 意義를 가지고 잇는 것이다.

朴, 朝鮮社會는 봉건적 諸 형태가 殘存하고 잇는 것이 그 특징이다. 그럼으로 계급적 이해관계보다도 종교적 감정의 紐帶가 더 强靭하다. 그럼으로 基督敎단체 등 諸 종교단체도 조직 내에 引入할 것이다.
이와가티 이번 회의 중 가장 주목할 만한 議論이 싸워진 후에 畢竟 단체 가입 문제는 朱翼氏의 動議로 保留하기로 되고 제2의 문제는 일단 可決되엿다가 다시 취소하엿다. 其他 3種의 議案은 모다 次期 中央委員會에 일임하기로 하고 최후로 또다시 某氏의 언동에 관한 문제가 해결을 기다리며 俎上에 올러 왓다. 조사위원 黃義準씨가 그 조사한 사실의 전말을 보고하자 이에 대한 議論이 잇다가 畢竟 中央檢査委員會에 문제를 引渡하엿다. 문제의 주인공인 某씨는 최후로 起立하야 자기의 추문을 모다 부인하고 당분간 조직 권내에서 탈퇴할 뜻을 宣明하고 압흐로 오직 현실의 투쟁으로만 그 과거를 청산하겟다고 표명하엿다.
이 문제로 因하야 場內가 일시 소란할 때에 방청석으로부터 于今까지도 某씨를 비호하는 태도를 지속하는 간부 측에 향하야 간부들은 某와 갓흔 者들이다 하고야지 하엿슬 때 本部 幹部 李周淵씨는 猛然히 이러나서 『우리를 某와 갓다고 말한 자가 누구냐? 어느 곳이 某와 갓다는 말이냐? 新幹運動을 위하야는 목을 베허도 피하지 아니하련다. 다만 한사람의 투사를 영영 일허버리는 것을 앗겨 할 뿐이다.』하고 怒呼하엿다.
新幹會의 역량은 물론 그 대중의 조직우에 근거가 잇슬것이나 열정의 人 李周淵씨나 항상 제기된 문제를 刻明하게 분석하야 그 초점을 파악하야 제시하는 예리한 明眼을 가진 理論家 韓炳洛씨와 기타 諸씨 등의 존재는 적지 안흔 힘의 일부분일 것이다. 오후 8시 萬歲三唱으로 폐회. (終)＜11＞
