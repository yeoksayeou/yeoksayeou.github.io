Publication Date: 1941년 01월 01일
Article Title: 志願兵일기, 그네의 生活 一日 (A Volunteer Soldier's Diary, A Day in Their Life)
Author: 陸軍 上等兵 兒島義英 (Army Private First Class Kojima Giei)
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

At the border garrison OO (국경OO), autumn has already arrived, and the wind is chilly in the mornings and evenings. It is already the second autumn since I parted ways with the instructors at the training center.

Because I have diligently cultivated and trained myself as a soldier of His Majesty (폐하), I have never once been sick since enlistment, nor have I missed any training duties, thus earning the trust of the company commander and other officers.

I have been entrusted with the education of new soldiers and the duties of a messenger for the officers, and during training, I am always part of the command group, handling various important tasks.

I know this is all thanks to the teachings I received from my instructors when I was at the training center, and I am deeply grateful. Furthermore, as one of the first volunteer soldiers from the Peninsula (반도) youth, I am working diligently so as not to betray the great expectations of the people on the home front (총후국민), so please rest assured.<151>

**Summary**

This article, presented as a diary entry or letter from a Korean volunteer soldier in the Imperial Japanese Army named Kojima Giei, describes his positive experience serving at a border garrison in 1941. He reports being healthy, trusted by superiors, and actively involved in training new recruits and important duties. He expresses gratitude for his training and emphasizes his commitment to fulfilling the expectations placed upon him as one of the first Korean volunteers, assuring those reading (likely instructors or the home front) of his dedication. From a historical perspective, this piece serves as propaganda promoting the relatively new Korean volunteer system within the Japanese military. It aims to depict service as honorable and rewarding, portraying the volunteer as loyal to the Emperor and integrated successfully, likely to encourage further Korean participation in the Japanese war effort and reinforce assimilation policies.

						陸軍 上等兵  兒島義英
국경OO에도 벌서 가을이 차저들어서 아츰 저녁으로 바람이 찹니다. 훈련소의 선생님들과 리별한 지도 벌서 이년재 맞는 가을입니다.
폐하의 군인으로서 수양과 단련을 부지런이한 까닭에 저는 입영후에 한번도 병난 일도 없고 연습근무에 빠진 일도 없어서 중대장과 다른 간부들의 신임을 받고 있읍니다. 초년병의 교육과 간부들의 전령의 일을 맡었으며 연습할 때는 항상 지휘반이 되여서 중요한 여리 가지 일을 보고 있읍니다. 이런 것도 모다 훈련소에 있을 때에 선생님의 교훈을 받은 까닭으로 알고 깊이 감사하옵니다. 그러하옵고 반도 청년의 첫번 지원병으로서 총후국민의 크나큰 기대를 저버리지 않으려고 열심으로 일을 보고 있아오니 안심하옵시기를 바랍니다.＜151＞
