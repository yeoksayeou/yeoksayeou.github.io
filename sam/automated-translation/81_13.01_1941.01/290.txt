Publication Date: 1941년 01월 01일
Article Title: 八紘一宇 (Eight Crown Cords, One Roof / All the world under one roof)
Author: 주요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

八紘一宇
						Chu Yohan
The fire is one
But the brightness fills all.
A single thought
Will envelop the hearts of tens of millions
And bring salvation.

The bell rings within the house
But spreads across the whole land.
A single melody
Will harmonize the steps
Of ten thousand nations. (260)

A single thought
Has existed since ancient times,
And a single ideal
Will surely come.
Its depth is unknown.
Its breadth is unknown,
Its height is unknown,
Its farness is unknown.
So unknown, its appearance
Is overwhelming.

Right now we hold guns,
Right now we grasp swords,
Right now we fight,
Right now we hate.
But in the end, the guns will be put away. (261)
The swords will be sheathed.
Love shall reign.

Some will laugh,
Some will hate,
Some will obstruct.
But the day the laughers will be surprised,
The day the haters will rejoice,
The day the obstructors will fall down,
Is not far off.

The fire is one
But lights tens of millions of lamps.
The thought is one
But achieves tens of millions of salvations. (262)

A single thought
Has existed since ancient times
And will surely come.
We cannot yet fathom its sound.
We cannot yet decipher its meaning.
Still, rather than knowing,
We can only hope.
Our sons will know.
Our grandsons will enjoy it.

Still, we can only hope—
For the single thought.
For the single light.
-End- (263)
<260-263>

**Summary**

This poem by Chu Yohan, titled "八紘一宇" (Hakko Ichiu), uses metaphors of a single fire spreading light and a single bell's sound filling the land to represent a powerful, unifying idea. This idea, described as ancient and inevitable, will ultimately bring salvation and harmony, overcoming current conflict (symbolized by guns and swords) and resistance (laughing, hating, obstructing). Though its full meaning is not yet understood by the current generation, the poet expresses hope that future generations ("our sons," "our grandsons") will fully realize and benefit from this unifying "single thought" and "single light." From a historical perspective, the poem is significant as it directly employs the Japanese imperial slogan "Hakko Ichiu," promoting the ideology of Japanese expansionism and assimilation under the guise of universal harmony and salvation. Written by a prominent Korean intellectual in 1941, it exemplifies the phenomenon of collaborationist literature during the late colonial period, aligning Korean cultural production with Japanese wartime propaganda.

八紘一宇
						주요한
불은 하나나
밝음은 가득하외다.
한 낱의 생각이
억만의 마음을 감기고
구원할것입니다.

종은 집속에 우나
온 따에 퍼집니다.
한 낱의 곡조가
만 나라의 거름을
어우를 것입니다.(260)

한낱의 생각이
예부터 있고
한낱의 리상이
기엏고 올것입니다.
그 깊이를 모릅니다.
그 넓이를 모릅니다,
그 높이를 모릅니다,
그 멀기를 모릅니다.
모르도록 그 나타남이
어수선하외다.

시방 우리는 총을 들고
시방 우리는 칼을 잡고
시방 우리는 싸우고
시방 우리는 문흡니다.
그러나 나종은 총을 거두리라.(261)
칼을 꽂으리라.
사랑이 다스리리라.

혹은 웃을것이고
혹은 뮐것이고
혹은 막을 것입니다.
마는 웃는이는 놀랄날이,
뮈는이는 반길날이,
막는이는 업델날이
머지안하외다.

불은 하나나
억만 등을 켭니다.
생각은 하나나
억만 구원을 이룹니다.(262)

한낱의 생각이
예부터 있고
기엏고 올것이외다.
음을 새기지 못합니다.
뜻을 풀지 못합니다.
아직 우리는 알기보다도
바랄 뿐이외다.
우리의 아들들은 알것이외다.
우리 손자들은 누릴것이외다.

아직 우리는 바랄 뿐이외다-
한낱의 생각을.
한낱의 광명을.
-끝- (263)
