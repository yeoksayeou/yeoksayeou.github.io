Editorial Postscript

As we bid farewell to the significant and auspicious Imperial Year 2600 and greet the New Spring of the 5th year of the Holy War, we are endlessly grateful for the Imperial favor from above and the efforts of the Imperial Army officers and soldiers below, and we respectfully wish for the health of all our esteemed readers.

With this New Year issue, "Samch'ŏlli" now counts its 13th year of publication, becoming the magazine with the longest history in Chosŏn. We deeply appreciate that this is solely due to the constant patronage of our dear readers, and we hope for your continued love, support, and encouragement.

We believe the "Current Status and Development Plans for Koreans at Home and Abroad," sent from locations such as Hsinching, Shanghai, Tōkyō, and Ōsaka for this issue, will be a special feature article. Furthermore, we are confident that Baron Yi Han-gu and other distinguished figures' "A Respectful Record of Deep Emotion before the Nijūbashi Bridge," the "Real Combat Stories of Volunteer Soldiers Returned from the Battlefield," Mr. In Chŏng-sik's "The Economic Character of the East Asian Sphere and Korea's Position," and Mr. Chŏng Kyo-wŏn's "The Ethical Conclusion of Nai-Sen Ittai (Japan-Korea Unity)" are highlights of this month's issue.

Moreover, Ms. Yi Kyun-mo's "Account of Escape from Singapore under Turbulent Skies" and the Kyŏngsŏng Prominent Ladies' Unit's "Account of a One-Day Visit to the Volunteer Soldier Training Center" are also good reads this month. In arts and culture, Mr. Yi Kwang-su's "The New Order and Literature/Film" and Mr. Ch'ae Man-sik's "Literature and Totalitarianism," these two major essays, along with Mr. Han Sŏr-ya's enjoyable work "Son" (아들) and Ms. Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi's powerful piece "Ch'ŏnmaek" (天脈), we believe are all major bombshells for our literary world. <300>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the January 1941 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine celebrates the start of the new year (marked as Imperial Year 2601, the 5th year of the "Holy War") and the magazine's 13th anniversary. It expresses gratitude towards the Japanese Emperor and military, as well as the readers. The postscript highlights key articles in the issue, emphasizing themes supportive of the Japanese colonial project, including the status of Koreans within the empire, Japan-Korea unity (Nai-Sen Ittai), the war effort (volunteer soldier stories, experiences in Singapore), the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere's economic structure, and cultural discussions on the "New Order" and totalitarianism by prominent Korean literary figures. For historians, this text is revealing of the magazine's explicit alignment with Japanese imperial ideology during wartime, showcasing how Korean cultural production was mobilized to support colonial policies and the war effort, featuring contributions from well-known Korean intellectuals justifying or promoting the colonial framework.

編輯後記
뜻깊은 佳年 皇紀 2600年을 보내고, 聖戰 第5年의 新春을 마지하여, 우으로는 皇恩과 아래로는 皇軍將兵의 努力을 感荷不己하는 바이며, 및 讀者諸彦의 健康을 仰祝하나이다.

「三千里」는 新年號로써 이미 誌齡 第13年을 算하게 되여써 朝鮮에서 가장 長久한 歷史를 가지게 된 것은 오로지 愛讀者諸位의 平素의 庇援임을 深謝하오며 앞으로도 끊임없는 愛護와 鞭撻이 있기를 바라나이다.

今號에 新京, 上海, 東京 大阪 等 現地에서 보낸 「海內海外의 朝鮮人 現狀과 發展策」은 本誌特種記事일줄, 알며, 더욱이 李恒九男爵 以下 斯界 名士諸氏의 「二重橋前感激謹記」와 「戰地에서 歸還한 志願兵實戰談과」 印貞植氏의 「東亞圈의 經濟的 性格과 朝鮮의 地位」, 鄭僑源氏의 「內鮮一體의 倫理的 歸結」은 이 달의 자랑으로 自信하나이다.

더욱이 李均茂女史의 「風雲下의 新乫坡脫出記」와 京城名流婦人部隊의 「志願兵訓鍊所一日入營記」도 이 달의 好讀物이오며, 學藝에 있어서 李光洙氏의 「新體制와 文學·映畵」와 蔡萬植氏의 「文學과 全體主義」, 2大論文을 비롯하여 韓雪野氏의 快作「아들」, 崔貞熙氏의 力作 「天脈은 다 我文壇의 巨彈일 줄 믿나이다.＜300＞
