필자	毛允淑
기사형태	시 (Poem)

To the Volunteer Soldiers
						Mo Yun-suk

At the dazzling mountain corner,
In the bright forest depths,
Beneath the sky where vigorous energy rises,
I entered, pushing through the autumn thickets.

Strong footsteps
Striking the hillside as they pass,
The fervent movements of body against body,
Sword-light engraves lightning under the sun—
My heart plays within the garden of strength and zeal.(264)

Eyes pierce the heavens, chests repel bullets,
Embracing tightly the grand ideal of Greater East Asia (大東洋) in their arms,
Running, breathing heavily, warriors of justice,
You are the light (光明) of this land.
You who will illuminate the strong future of the Great Harmony Spirit (大和魂) for eternity (永却),
Vanguards, guides of this nation,
Ready to offer up blood and flesh without reservation,
You are the sons (男兒) of the Peninsula (半島), the flower crown (花冠) of hope.

What can this poor body offer?
Neither dazzling swords and spears nor ornaments of gold and silver
Do I present before you; I simply pass by.
I only moisten and add a draught of my boiling blood.

The darkness that once lingered at the edge of my eyes,
Today, before your thunderous shouts,
I wash it all away cleanly and behold a new season (新季節).
I wash it all away cleanly and sing a new song.(265)

**Summary**

This poem by Mo Yun-suk fervently praises Korean men who volunteered for the Japanese Imperial Army. It portrays them as heroic figures embodying strength, justice, and the spirit of sacrifice for the "grand ideal of Greater East Asia" and the "Great Harmony Spirit" (Yamato Spirit). The speaker expresses deep admiration and offers symbolic support ("a draught of my boiling blood"), claiming personal renewal and hope inspired by the soldiers' commitment. For historians, this poem is a significant example of pro-Japanese propaganda authored by a prominent Korean intellectual during the late colonial period. It starkly illustrates the pressures and complexities of collaboration, the promotion of Japanese militarism and ideology (like the Yamato Spirit and Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere) within Korea, and the mobilization of Koreans for Japan's war effort through the volunteer system.

志願兵에게
						毛允淑
눈부신 山모통이
밝은 숲속
힘찬 기운 떠오는 하늘밑으로
가을 떨기를 헷치며 들어갔노라.

기슭을 후리고 지나가는
억센 발자욱
몸과 몸의 뜨거운 움직임들
칼빛은 太陽아래 번개를 아로삭여
힘과 열의 동산안에 내맘은 뛰놉니다.(264)

눈은 하늘을 쏘고 그가슴은 탄환을 물리처
大東洋의 큰理想 두팔안에 꽉 품고
달리여 큰숨 뿜는 正義의勇士
그대들은 이땅의 光明입니다.
大和魂 억센 앞날 永却으로 빛내일
그대들 이나라의 앞잽이 길손
피와 살 아낌없이 내여바칠
半島의男兒 希望의 花冠입니다.

가난헌 이몸이 무엇을 바치리까?
황홀한 창검이나 금은의 장식도
그대앞에 디림없이 그저 지냅니다
오로지 끓는피 한 목음을 축여보태옵니다.

지난날 이눈ㅅ가에 기뜨렸던 어둠을
내 오늘 그대들의 우렁찬 웨침앞에
다- 맑게 씿고 새季節 뵈옵니다
다- 맑게 씿고 새노래 부릅니다.(265)
