Magazine Title: 삼천리 제13권 제1호
Publication Date: 1941-01-01
Article Title: 志願兵일기, 그네의 生活 一日 記錄 (Volunteer Soldier's Diary, A Record of Their Daily Life)
Author: 第1區 第1訓練班 大山正隆 (1st District, 1st Training Squad, Ōyama Masataka)
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellany)

We, the thousand trainees, look up to Major General Kaida (가이다소장) as a father, think of the various instructors as mothers, help each other, and share both hardships and joys together, just like a paradise.

We must believe from the bottom of our hearts that we are the Japanese race.

Mainlanders (내지인) and Peninsulars (반도인) are merely peoples who lived separately for a short while. Now, they have been reunited.

Considering this, how regrettable it is for those subjects who cannot become soldiers.

Respected fellow countrymen! If you truly love your brothers and children, send them as volunteer soldiers.

Beloved youth! Let us overcome all difficulties and become volunteer soldiers. Let us show manly spirit for the sake of the nation.

Before long, when the results of our efforts bear fruit and the conscription system is implemented, that will truly be when Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e (Japan-Korea Unity, 내선일체) is completed.

Is it not time for us all to rise up together, bearing in mind the glorious responsibility of participating in the East Asian Holy War (동아 전설)? <147>

**Summary**

The author, Ōyama Masataka, a trainee in the Korean volunteer soldier program, portrays the training camp as a harmonious "paradise" where trainees view their Japanese superiors as parental figures. He strongly asserts that Koreans must believe they are part of the Japanese race, recently reunited after a brief separation. He urges Koreans to prove their loyalty and achieve true Japan-Korea Unity (Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e) by sending their sons to volunteer for the military, framing it as a patriotic duty essential for participation in the "East Asian Holy War" and a precursor to mandatory conscription. This article serves as potent pro-Japanese propaganda, illustrating the ideological justification for Korean assimilation and military mobilization under colonial rule. Historians would find its explicit promotion of Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e, the framing of military service as a path to unity and imperial belonging, and the call for participation in Japan's war effort particularly indicative of late-stage colonial policy and rhetoric aimed at Koreans.

志願兵일기, 그네의 生活 一日 記錄
						第1區 第1訓練班 大山正隆
우리들 일천명이나 되는 훈련생은 가이다소장을 아버지처럼 뫼시고 여러 교관선생을  어머니처럼 생각하고 서로 도읍고, 괴로움이나 즐거움을 한가지로 난호고 있는 모양은 꼭 락원과 같습니다. 우리들은 마음 속으로부터 일본민족인 것은 믿어야 합니다. 내지인과 반도인은 잠깐동안 서로 분가해서 산데 불과합니다. 그것이 지금 다시 합해진 것입니다. 그렇게 생각하면 군인이 되지 못하는 국민은 얼마나 섭섭한 일입니까. 존경하는 동포 여러분이여! 형제와 자제를 참으로 사랑하시거든 지원병으로 보내시요.
사랑하는 청년 여러분이여! 모든 어려운 것을 깨트리고 지원병이 됩시다. 나라를 위하야 남자다운 의기를 보입시다. 얼마 아니 되여서 우리들의 애쓴 결과가 잘 되여서 의무병의 제도가 실시되는 그때야말노 내선일체가 완성될 것이겠읍니다. 동아 전설에 참가할 영광 있는 책임을 명심하고 다 같이 이러날 때가 아닙니까.＜147＞
