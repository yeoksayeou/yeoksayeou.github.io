東邦科學興業會社 社長 金秉業씨, 靑年社長 訪問記
						石兵丁

Though *Usu* (Rain Water) and *Kyŏngch'ip* (Insects Awaken) have passed, and the spring equinox is not far off, the snow left unmelted from the harsh winter still remains. On March 10th, when the chilly and biting north wind of this northern land tugged at my sleeves and reproached me, the sun was slowly dipping behind the western mountains, and around 5 PM, as the gray curtain began to silently descend upon Changan (the capital), the reporter, weary from a whole day of hurried steps and struggles with the pen, forced his tired body onward, embracing the cold wind, and hurried towards the intended destination.

The symphony of the great metropolis roared ever louder as it delivered tens of thousands of people returning to their nests. Passing through Chongno and Namdaemun streets, I set foot at the main entrance of a strikingly tall, three-story modern Western-style building in a corner of Kosich'ŏng (古市町), clearly marked with a brilliant, arch-shaped gold sign reading 東邦科學興業株式會社 (Tongbang Kwahak Hŭngŏp Chusik Hoesa). As I had arranged the meeting through several phone calls and had set a specific time in advance, I was immediately able to meet him in the president's office.

He greeted me warmly, making it impossible not to feel a deep sense of cordiality. After the initial greetings, I sat down opposite him in the chair he indicated. If I were to describe the appearance of this young president, Mr. Kim Pyŏngŏp, as reflected in my eyes:

He is said to be 32 years old, but he looked about 35 or 36 to me, possessing a rather mature air. His height seemed around 5 *ch'ŏk* 5 *ch'on* (approx. 167 cm). While not exactly fat, his build was robust, with a rather prominent belly, which indeed seemed to equip him with the qualifications of a company president and the dignity of an executive. Although his voice has a somewhat cheerful quality, his speech is deliberate and affectionate. His broad forehead is refreshing, warmth distinctly flows from his eyes, and the shape of his mouth when speaking holds a charming <172> appeal for anyone. His movements are graceful and cheerful, revealing his Kyŏngsŏng origins. However, the keen glint that momentarily appears in his eyes hints at an impatient nature. Seated in the president's chair, his composed and earnest demeanor fully displays the inviolable dignity befitting an executive. Yet, he is not cold or harsh, but rather natural and full of human kindness, appearing at first glance to be a very warm and sensitive person. His articulate and logical manner of speaking suggests that Mr. Kim's mind is meticulous, clear, and rational.

I was greatly relieved to find that he was not the kind of dissolute, contemptuous, base commoner (凡骨) one often finds among the sons and nephews of the wealthy. Furthermore, he is no dullard (鈍物) characterized by indecisiveness (愚柔不斷), looking left and right (左顧右貶). Instead, he appeared to possess the decisiveness and courage to forge ahead (勇往邁進) and persevere through a hundred setbacks (百折不撓) once he sets his mind to something. At the same time, he has the demeanor of a magnanimous elder (溫厚長者). Our young president, Mr. Kim Pyŏngŏp, certainly passes muster for the qualifications of a president.

Mr. Kim Pyŏngŏp, a young man of 32, currently has his permanent domicile in Sin-dong, Chich'ŏn-myŏn, Ch'ilgok-kun, North Kyŏngsang Province, but he is originally from Kyŏngsŏng. He graduated from Taegu Higher Common School (대구고보) and is said to be a man of great wealth, with assets of about 800,000 *wŏn*. In his hometown, he engaged in agriculture and dealt with various tenant farmers, striving for their advancement, and was early recognized as a good landlord, earning deep trust and esteem. Because of this, it's said that there was no one in the Ch'ilgok-kun area who didn't know him. But how could a jade remain buried in the dirt? He had always heard about the misfortunes of Chosŏn's genius inventors through newspapers and magazines, constantly lamenting this situation. Not only that, but he had resolved to dedicate his life to the advancement of genius inventors and the growth of a "Scientific Chosŏn" (과학 朝鮮). Just then, he received cooperation from influential figures in Kyŏngsŏng, made a firm decision, moved his stage to Kyŏngsŏng, and realized his long-cherished dream. Our question-and-answer session went like this:

Me: What motivated you, sir, to establish the Tongbang Kwahak Hŭngŏp Chusik Hoesa?
Kim: While living in the countryside, I often saw and heard, through various channels, about the pitiful state and reality of Chosŏn's invention scene. It struck my heart, and my motivation, if you can call it that, was to somehow contribute to this tragic world of Chosŏn invention, encourage unfortunate inventors, and help the growth of a Scientific Chosŏn. That is, aiming for the Chosŏn invention scene and the construction of a Scientific Chosŏn, I wanted to ring the morning bell for its dawn. But being originally weak, how could I become a vanguard figure in such a great enterprise?

Our young president Kim Pyŏngŏp's first words were thus. A sign of firm determination was evident on his brow (眉字), and his eyes shone brightly.

Me: What invention <173> is your company tackling first?
Kim: The fundamental purpose of this company is to select promising inventions made by Chosŏn people and progressively manufacture and sell them. However, regarding what we are currently working on, there are some points that need to remain confidential, so it's a bit difficult to speak about it yet...

Me: How do you view the current state of Chosŏn's invention scene, and what do you think its future holds?
Kim: Even now, Chosŏn's invention scene produces inventions that are second to none, and I believe such genius inventors will continue to emerge in the future, so I see the prospects as very bright. The only problem is that there are no institutions to nurture and encourage these inventors. So, even if someone makes an outstanding invention, they lack the personal financial resources to turn it into a product, and it ends up falling into the hands of others, which is truly lamentable. You can't find inventors in any other country who struggle with misfortune like Chosŏn inventors do.

Every word and phrase, stemming from passion and sincerity, in our young president's well-reasoned speech, was more than enough to stir the heartstrings of this listener. The joy of welcoming Mr. Kim to Chosŏn, no, to Chosŏn's invention scene and blessing the growth of Scientific Chosŏn, is greater than any other joy.

Me: Hearing your words, sir, it seems your company cannot proceed purely on a profit basis. Then, will you be able to achieve a 'balance' (빠란쓰)?
Kim: From the time I first focused (着目) on this, profit was not my primary goal. If profit were the only aim, there are plenty of other ventures besides this, aren't there? Therefore, our company plans to set aside 13/15ths of the net profit as capital for building Scientific Chosŏn and nurturing genius inventors. In the future, we plan to establish a research institute within the company to cultivate talented individuals, and if we see potential for global contribution, we intend to send them abroad for further study.

Mr. Kim expressed his firm resolve to selflessly dedicate himself to the genius inventors of this land, transcending self-interest. His fiery passion seemed capable of melting even hearts of iron and stone.

Me: There must be a secret to running the company. If you could share it within permissible limits, it would be a valuable reference for those who follow.
Kim: What would a beginner know? (Humble posture, serious) But past entrepreneurs failed because their words and actions did not match. To avoid repeating that mistake (전철), our company devises unique business policies. To be more specific, there are two trends in modern enterprise:
One is the method of trying to embellish the exterior, presenting things as larger than reality to the outside world to capture public popularity all at once. <174>
The second is to prioritize substance, disregard external appearances, and adopt a straightforward management approach based purely on actual capability and creditworthiness. However, the first approach can easily lean towards emptiness (浮虛) and fall into mere tactics, so I am reluctant to adopt it. My ideal is to walk steadily, cultivating our own strength internally with solid steps. So, I put forward 50,000 *wŏn* to take the first step. As much as possible, I intend to abandon the old method of immobilizing capital and adopt rational methods and means. In personnel administration too, I aim to prioritize quality over quantity, avoid hiring many low-level employees, and adopt a policy where generally hired employees can stabilize their livelihoods and fully demonstrate their efficiency.

Me: What standards will you use then for selecting employees?
1. Calmness. 2. Soundness, but rational. 3. Someone who approaches everything with confidence, whose actions do not deviate from common sense (someone who doesn't pretend to have external influence (外界取引力) they don't possess, doesn't engage in empty publicity, but presents actual credit and capability as they are – essentially, someone capable of strong diplomacy). 4. Drinking is not prohibited up to a certain extent. 5. A little romance is acceptable. 6. Just not a deviant person. 7. A sociable person would be good. (But vanity is strictly forbidden).

Now, this reveals our president's open-minded personality, clear ambition, and the demeanor of a versatile and magnanimous leader (能小能大한 觀仁長子). Especially from the criteria for selecting personnel, one can glimpse what a sociable and graceful personality he possesses. Furthermore, although he is young (少壯), one can see he has tasted the sweet and bitter of life, and especially when glimpsing the background of his rural life, one can understand how much human warmth flows from him. It is fortunate for our invention community to welcome such a person, and it gives confidence in the construction of a Scientific Chosŏn.

Me: What are your hobbies, sir?
Kim: I am a man of no particular hobby (無趣味漢). When I have time, I find some solace in reading, calligraphy, and painting.

Me: What about recreation?
Kim: I like music. I particularly favor pieces that carry the Chosŏn sentiment and local color. Haha... (Bursts into laughter...) (According to a certain employee, his appreciation for the arts is quite deep.)

Me: What approach do you take towards your children at home?
Kim: I have one son and one daughter. The older one's name is Changsŏp (章燮), and he is now 8 years old. I just take the approach of letting them grow freely, but I am not negligent in supervision.

Me: My questions are out of order, but considering your company's business, a publicity department seems necessary. Do you plan to establish one in the future? <175>
Kim: Of course, we plan to. Since we follow the principle of diligent practical effort (務實力行主義), perhaps we will promote just that.

Me: Looking at the Chosŏn business world overall, sir, what are the prospects for the future?
Kim: I am absolutely not pessimistic. It has decayed to the point where it couldn't possibly decay any further, so from now on, only improvement remains.

Me: What actual phenomena do you see as symbols of this?
Kim: Chosŏn received the influence of Chinese characters after the Jin (晉) dynasty. The so-called ideology of honorable poverty (청빈주의) of the Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove (죽림칠현) became the foundation of Chosŏn people's thought. Even the ruling class considered having money shameful, hiding and deceiving about it, thus naturally refraining from undertaking business ventures, and consequently, their skills (수완) were not refined (洗鍊). Then, with the development of capitalism and the convenience of transportation, during the half-century after enlightenment (개화), they suffered the bitterness and pain from this tradition of honorable poverty.
Also, because recent educational policies are shifting from civics (公民) to vocational training (실업), the main current (主潮) of all these things is breaking the old traditions. It's starting to take root in the minds of Chosŏn people, and the psychological drive to engage in business is becoming stronger.

Me: Rushing in recklessly leads to many failures. Can this trend be considered only positive?
Kim: Of course, there will be many failures. But for every failure, there will be progress on the other side, so in the future, there will only be advancement and improvement. Generally, there are only two principles in the world: Advance (進) or Retreat (退). Since we have retreated much, there is no more room to step back, so now only Advance remains, doesn't it? Ho ho...

He speaks with the firm conviction of someone who understands the iron laws of the universe and has faced the path of life head-on, revealing that he embarked on this business with such belief. What a precious birth, and what a distinct presence Mr. Kim is!

Having been granted the time to hear his aspirations, I somehow felt a surge of courage. So, I bid him farewell, turned my steps, and came back, praying that this valuable company would grow steadily and greatly within the embrace of this insightful president.

Finally, a glance at the executives of our Tongbang company:
President Mr. Kim Pyŏngŏp, Executive Director Mr. Sin Kyŏngsun, Managing Director Mr. Kim Pyŏng* (秉*), Standing Auditor Mr. Kim Pyŏngdong. The company was established with a fully paid-up capital of 50,000 *wŏn*. (End) <176>

**Summary**

This 1936 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine interviews Kim Pyŏngŏp, the 32-year-old president of the newly established Tongbang Kwahak Hŭngŏp Chusik Hoesa (East Asian Science Promotion Company). Kim, a wealthy landowner originally from Kyŏngsŏng with experience in rural Ch'ilgok-kun, explains his motivation for founding the company: to support struggling Chosŏn inventors and foster a "Scientific Chosŏn," lamenting the lack of institutional support that forces inventors to lose control of their creations. He emphasizes that his goal transcends mere profit, planning to reinvest most earnings into research and inventor development. Kim outlines his business philosophy, favoring steady, substance-based growth over superficial appearances, and details his criteria for hiring practical, sensible employees. He expresses optimism for the future of Chosŏn business, believing that after hitting rock bottom, the only way is up, spurred by changing attitudes towards wealth and a shift towards practical education.

From a historical perspective, the article offers insights into the nascent Korean scientific and industrial aspirations under Japanese colonial rule. Kim Pyŏngŏp represents a new type of Korean entrepreneur emerging in the 1930s, blending traditional land-based wealth with modern corporate ambitions and a nationalistic desire to uplift Korean capabilities, specifically in science and invention. His critique of the lack of infrastructure for inventors and his explicit goal of fostering a "Scientific Chosŏn" highlight the challenges and aspirations of Koreans seeking modernization and self-reliance within the colonial context. Historians might find Kim's blend of nationalism, modern business strategy, and critique of traditional Korean attitudes towards commerce particularly interesting.

나-. 朝鮮 발명계의 현황을 어떠케 보시며, 장래는 어떠케 되겟습니까?
