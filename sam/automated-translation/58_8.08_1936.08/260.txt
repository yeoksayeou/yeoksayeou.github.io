Number of Visitors Per Day at Major Department Stores in Seoul
In the city of Seoul, there are 5 or 6 grand department stores housed in imposing 4- or 5-story buildings, such as Mitsukoshi, Hwasin, Minakai, Chōjiya, Hirata, and Kanebō.
About how many customers pass through these major department stores in a single day? Of course, the number is highest on sunny Sundays, and fewer customers visit on rainy days or days other than Sunday. Below are the recorded numbers of people entering these six major department stores in one day:
Mitsukoshi ......... 126,000 people
Minakai ......... 119,000 people
Hwasin ......... 117,000 people
Chōjiya ......... 95,000 people
Hirata ......... 62,500 people
Kanebō ......... 18,000 people <229>

Good Fortune Comes to Boxer Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn: Marriage to a Lady with a 30,000 Won Dowry
Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn, the third son of Mr. Sŏ Pyŏng-kyu, who gained the honor of ranking 6th as a world-class boxing champion and crossed the Pacific last year, recently became engaged to Miss Kim Sunbok (age 20), the second younger sister of the wealthy Mr. Kim In-ch'ŏl of Chŏnju, through the diligent arrangement of Mr. Yi Kang-wŏn, a former councilor of the Central Council (中樞院叅議) from Chŏnju. The splendid wedding ceremony was held at the Chŏnju Public Hall from 5 PM on the 15th, followed by a reception banquet at the restaurant "Hutaba" (二葉) starting at 6:30 PM.<230>
Miss Sunbok is a talented woman who graduated from Chŏnju Girls' High School and is currently attending Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School. As the daughter of a wealthy man, she is said to be marrying with a dowry of 30,000 Won. Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn, receiving this good fortune, has become quite the topic of conversation among the people of Chŏnju.

Which Restaurant's Business is Thriving?
There might be days when the sun doesn't rise over the city of Seoul, but could there ever be a day when the sounds of singing and strings from male entertainers (花郞) are missing from the high-class restaurants (料亭)? Indeed, every night from these pleasure quarters hidden among the willows (柳暗), the song tune "Let's play, let's play, let's play while young; half my life is spent in old age, not much time left to play" flows clearly. So, at which restaurant do the most kisaeng and male entertainers gather? There's no easy way to know for sure, and even if one knew, it's awkward to say. However, to provide just one piece of information regarding this area, looking at the business tax amounts collected by the Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture from seven city restaurants last year:
Myŏngwŏlgwan 921 Won
Kugilgwan 718 Won
Chosŏngwan 503 Won
Ch'ŏnhyangwŏn 469 Won
Sikto'wŏn 430 Won
Songjukwŏn 387 Won
T'aesŏgwan 379 Won

Industrial Bank's Net Profit: 90,000 Won
There's no business that accumulates money as well as a bank. Among them, the Bank of Chosŏn and the Industrial Bank (殖銀) likely earn the most. Looking at the business results for the last half-year:
Chosŏn Industrial Bank Net Profit: 1,760,000 Won
Bank of Chosŏn: 930,000 Won
Looking at the money spent from this net profit on entertainment expenses and executive bonuses:
Bank of Chosŏn: 65,000 Won
Industrial Bank: 90,000 Won
As for shareholder dividends, the Bank of Chosŏn is said to pay 4% (4分) and the Industrial Bank 9% (9分).<231>

Record Singers Who Are Famous Kisaeng Born in P'yŏngyang
Currently, the majority of record singers in Chosŏn are from P'yŏngyang. This trend is even more pronounced among female singers.
Of course, if counted by singers who have recorded only once or twice, Kyŏngsŏng, being the premier city in Chosŏn and the center of culture, would naturally have the highest number. However, it is a fact that Kyŏngsŏng cannot possibly match P'yŏngyang in terms of exclusive contract singers.
If we list the representative figures currently shining as exclusive female singers:
Wang Su-bok, Sŏnu Il-sŏn, Ch'oe Yŏn-yŏn, Kim Yŏn-wŏl, Ch'oe Ch'ang-sŏn, Han Chŏng-ok, Kim Pok-hŭi, Ch'oe Myŏng-ju, etc., were all born in P'yŏngyang, and furthermore, they are all kisaeng.
P'yŏngyang of the Entertainment World, P'yŏngyang of Singers
Why on earth does P'yŏngyang have so many singers like this? It is truly an interesting question. In short, the 'River Fortress City' (浿城) has long been a land where so-called connoisseurs of arts and pleasures were born and gathered.
This is likely because the climate, customs, and traditions of P'yŏngyang naturally nurtured artistic talent among the residents of this land.
Today, the place first associated with exceptionally beautiful scenery is P'yŏngyang. That's how scenic the land of the 'River Fortress City' is.
Therefore, from ancient times, the mountains and waters of P'yŏngyang <232> have endowed its women with innate beauty, and characteristically, both men and women are blessed with musical talent.
Though the story briefly digressed into praise for P'yŏngyang, let's look here for the profiles of kisaeng born in P'yŏngyang who are famous kisaeng renowned in the Kyŏngsŏng entertainment world and also hold popularity as record singers.
Besides the aforementioned Sŏnu Il-sŏn, Ch'oe Ch'ang-sŏn, etc.:
Belonging to the Chosŏn Kwŏnbŏn are:
O P'i-ch'wi, Kim Yŏ-ran, Im Myŏng-wŏl, Kim Ok-sŏn, Kim Ok-chin, Chang Hyang-nan, Ko Pi-yŏn, Paek Un-sŏn, Yi Chin-hong, Yi So-hyang, Kwak Hyang-nan, Chang Ok-hwa, Cho So-ok, and others.
And in the Hansŏng Kwŏnbŏn, one can count:
Kim Ok-yŏp, Yi Chuk-yŏp, Cho Mok-tan, Paek Mok-tan, and others.
Among them, Sŏnu Il-sŏn, Wang Su-bok, Kim Pok-hŭi, Ch'oe Ch'ang-sŏn, and Kim Ŭn-ok can truly be called representative figures of the Chosŏn female record singer world.

Dance Pilgrimage to Europe
Mr. Cho T'aek-wŏn, an outstanding talent from the school of Ishii Baku (石井漠), whose name rose alongside Ms. Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi, established a Dance Research Institute in Kyŏngsŏng's Yeongnak-chŏng several years ago and has been pioneering the virgin territory of the Peninsula's dance world. According to recent news, Mr. Cho, full of ambition to advance into the world dance scene, had been waiting for an opportunity. Feeling that the pioneering of the Peninsula's dance world has now reached a certain stage, the time has finally come to realize his long-cherished desire. Soon, he will go to Europe (渡歐), staying in European countries like France (佛蘭西) and Germany (獨逸) to devote all his efforts to本格的な (authentic) dance research.
Now, facing his hopeful departure, filled with joy and tension, Mr. Cho speaks as follows:
"Going to Europe this time fulfills my long-held hope. It had been postponed until today due to various circumstances, but now I can finally leave. I cannot yet say the departure date, but the preparations are all done, and I plan to go to Europe for about one year. Before departing, I might hold a farewell dance performance."

Twenty Years of Korean Restaurants in Seoul City
There are currently about 50 high-class restaurants (料亭) scattered throughout the city and suburbs of Seoul, catering to the enjoyment of wealthy people. As reported previously, the amount of money squandered day and night at these restaurants, calling for famous kisaeng and singers and making merry, is astonishing even for just one day. However, here we will specifically take a brief look only at the restaurants in Seoul operated by Koreans primarily serving Koreans.
Of course, there are more restaurants in Seoul operated by Japanese (內地人) primarily serving Japanese, with at least fifteen or sixteen prominent ones, but these will be omitted here, and we will only write about the Korean restaurants.
It has now been twenty years since so-called Korean restaurants have existed in Kyŏngsŏng. Listing the important ones among them:

Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月舘)
The first Myŏngwŏlgwan appeared in Seoul around Taishō 6 or 7 (1917-1918) when Mr. An Sun-hwan first established a purely Korean restaurant on the empty lot beside the current Tonga Ilbo Company building; this was the predecessor of Myŏngwŏlgwan. After it suffered a fire, Mr. An newly built the current Sikto'wŏn. One might consider Mr. An the originator of the Korean restaurant business in Kyŏngsŏng.
Afterward, on the current site of Myŏngwŏlgwan, Mr. Im Hak-no built a Korean restaurant called Changch'ungwan. After Mr. Im passed away, Mr. Yi Chong-gu took it over, changed the name to Myŏngwŏlgwan, and began operating it. Although it's a joint-stock company, Mr. Yi Chong-gu invested most of the capital, and Mr. Ch'oe Ton handles the actual management of all affairs. He is a successful man who has now built a fortune of about 100,000 Won.

Sikto'wŏn (食道園)
Operated by Mr. Yun Hŭi-t'ae, owner of the Tonga Leather Company (東亞皮革商會). Until last year, it was managed by Mr. An Sun-hwan, but Mr. Yun has been managing it since. This restaurant has long received high praise for its Korean cuisine and has recently been frequently used as a venue for wedding ceremonies and wedding banquets.

Kugilgwan (國一舘)
Founded by Mr. Pak Hŭi-wŏn, who had the financial backing of Mr. Kim Kap-sun, a wealthy man from Ch'ungnam. Three years ago, the current owner, Mr. Pak Hŏn-yong, took it over from Pak Hŭi-wŏn <234> and has been managing it ever since. Thanks to his devoted efforts, it is said that the business is now generally doing quite well and prospects are improving.

Ch'ŏnhyangwŏn (天香園)
Managed by the strong-willed woman Ms. Kim Ok-kyo, who once gained fame for her glamour in the Kyŏngsŏng entertainment world. This summer, she even newly built a villa in *nŭng-ni outside the prefecture, and Ch'ŏnhyangwŏn is reportedly achieving good results now. The main building of Ch'ŏnhyangwŏn was established 7 or 8 years ago. Ms. Kim's control over her strong-boned male employees is said to be truly well-disciplined, surpassing that of men. Burning with extraordinary business ambition, she reportedly plans to build a purely Korean-style hotel, her long-cherished dream, in the near future. She is truly a representative figure among female entrepreneurs.

T'aesŏgwan (太西舘)
The owner of T'aesŏgwan, which operates on such a large scale and enjoys favorable conditions, even having set up an annex in Noryangjin's Han'gang-chŏng, is Mr. Ko Rye-un. Mr. Ko was someone who, until not long ago, ran a small restaurant supply shop and has achieved great success.

Tongmyŏnggwan (東明舘)
Mr. Cho Nam-jin originally ran a small restaurant called Ch'ilsŏnggwan in Sŏrin-chŏng within the city, which gradually succeeded and became Tongmyŏnggwan.

Chosŏngwan (朝鮮舘)
Mr. Kim Kyŏng-bae, who spent 17 or 18 years diligently working as a bookkeeper at the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch, started this restaurant last December. Still under forty, Mr. Kim is an active individual said to have considerable experience in the restaurant business over many years.

Ŭmbyŏkchŏng (飮碧亭)
The owner, Mr. Han T'aek-su, is the beloved grandson of Mr. Han Chu-go from a prestigious Korean family, a young man under thirty this year. Having stepped into the different field of restaurant management, Mr. Han entrusts all management to his confidant, Mr. Cho Man-gu. Among the restaurants in Seoul, its location in a villa area offers an truly excellent scenic view.

**Summary**

This "Samch'ŏlli Apricot Blossom Village" column from August 1936 presents a series of short, miscellaneous reports on contemporary life and culture in Seoul (Kyŏngsŏng) and beyond. Topics include daily visitor numbers at major department stores (Japanese and Korean-owned), the marriage of a famous boxer with a significant dowry, business conditions (tax revenue) of prominent Korean restaurants (yojŏng), bank profits and dividends, high-value items at a London auction, the dominance of P'yŏngyang-born kisaeng among popular recording artists, a profile of dancer Cho T'aek-wŏn's planned European study tour, a brief history and overview of major Korean-run restaurants in Seoul, a listing of prominent kisaeng from the three main Kyŏngsŏng kisaeng associations (kwŏnbŏn) categorized by artistic skill (dance, music, languages, etc.), telephone line counts in Seoul, and the construction costs of the Government-General building and Kyŏngsŏng Station.

From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable glimpses into the urban landscape and social dynamics of colonial Seoul. The coexistence and competition between Japanese and Korean businesses (department stores, restaurants), the prominence of kisaeng in popular culture (recording industry, high-class entertainment), the discussion of dowries, the emergence of modern cultural figures like dancers seeking international training, and the sheer scale of colonial infrastructure projects (Government-General building cost) provide concrete details about modernization, cultural shifts, economic activity, and social stratification under Japanese rule. The listing of kisaeng names and skills is particularly interesting for social and cultural history, highlighting the specific arts cultivated and the perceived importance of different talents, including foreign languages.

石井漠 門下의 逸材로서 崔承喜여사와 함께 그 일홈이 높아진 趙澤元군은 수년 전부터 京城永樂町에 舞踊硏究所를 설치하여 놋고 半島舞踊界의 處女地를 開拓하고 잇더니 최근 들니는 소식에 의하면 조군은 세계무용계로의 진출에 雄心勃勃 기회만을 엿보다가 半島舞踊界의 개척도 인제 일단락을 지운 듯하여 마츰내 君의 多年의 宿望도 이루워 볼 때는 다다러 오게 되엿스니 머지 안허 渡歐, 佛蘭西, 獨逸 등 歐洲各國에 滯留하여 잇스면서 본격적인 무용연구에 모든 힘을 기우리겟다는 것이다.
이제 희망에 찬 牡途를 앞에 두고 환희와 긴장 속에서 조군은 다음과 갓치 말한다.
이번 歐羅巴로 가게 되는 것은 나의 다년간의 희망을 이루게 된 것이외다. 그 동안 여러 가지 사정으로 해서 오늘날까지 연기되엿든 것인데 이제야 겨우 떠나게 되엿슴니다. 아직 출발할 일자는 말치 못하겟스나 인제 준비는 다- 되엿스며 약 일個年 예정으로 渡歐할 작정이외다. 출발하기 전에는 고별 무용회도 열어볼가 하외다.

서울 長安 朝鮮人料亭二十年記
서울에는 현재 약 50處의 料亭이 시내 교외에 버러저 있어 돈 있는 사람들의 흥을 도우고 있다. 이 요정에서 밤낮을 가리지 않고 名妓, 名唱을 불너 놓고 뚱땅뚱땅하면서 蕩財하는 돈이 하루만 치드라도 可驁할 만하다 함은 앞서서 보도한 바 있지마는 이 자리에서는 특히 서울에서 조선인 經營으로, 주로 조선인을 상대하는 요정만을 대강대강 살펴보려 한다.
물론 서울에는 內地人 經營으로 內地人을 주로 상대하는 요정이 더 많어 가장 有數한 곳만 치드라도 十五,六處가 되나 이것은 여긔에서 省略하고 다만 조선인 요정만을 적어 가기로 한다.

明月舘
서울에 처음으로 明月舘이 생겨나기는 大正6, 7年경 安淳煥씨가 지금 東亞日報社側의 空地에 純朝鮮料理屋을 비로서 처음 세우게 된 것이 明月舘의 前身이다. 그 뒤 火災를 맛나 安씨는 새로히 현재의 食道園을 세웟다. 짐작컨대 안씨는 경성에 있어서 조선요리업자의 원조라고 할 만하다.
그뒤 현재의 明月舘基地에 任學魯씨가 長春舘이란 조선요리옥을 세웟으며 임씨가 死去한 다음에는 李鍾九씨가 그것을 引受經營함으로부터 名稱을 고처 明月舘으로 하엿다. 合資組織이지마는 李鐘九씨가 대부분의 자본을 出資하엿스며 실제의 경영은 崔燉씨가 모든 일을 처리하고 있다. 지금 약 십만원의 當財를 이룬 성공한 사람이다.

食道園
東亞皮革商會主 尹熙泰씨가 경영하고 있다. 작년까지에는 安淳煥씨가 경영하엿섯스나 尹씨가 경영하여 온다. 이 요리옥은 오래 전부터 조선요리의 好評을 받어오며 근래에 와서는 結婚式場 또는 婚禮宴會場으로써 많이 이용하고 있다.

國一舘
忠南의 富豪 金甲順씨를 物的 背景으로 가지고 있는 朴熙源씨의 창립으로 이루워 젓는데 3년 전부터는 현재의 주인 朴憲鎔씨가 朴熙源으로부터 인수하여＜234＞ 爾來 경영하여 오는데 씨의 揮身的 노력으로 지금은 일반에게 상당히 好調를 띄워 業路가 나어 간다고 한다.

天香園
일즉이 경성화류계에서 그 艶名을 날니든 여장부 金玉橋여사의 경영이다. 금년 여름 府外 *陵里에 別莊까지 新築하엿고 天香園은 지금 好成績을 내이고 있다는 것이다. 天香園 본관의 창립은 벌서 7, 8년전 김여사의 剛骨의 男使用人들에 대한 操縱은 실로 整制된 바 있어 남성에 앞스는 바가 있다한다. 비범한 事業慾에 불타는 그는 年來의 宿望인 順朝鮮式 호텔도 不遠에 세운다고-. 실로 여류사업가로서의 대표적인 인물이다.

太西舘
鷺梁津 漢江町에 別館까지 設置하고 그러틋 大規模로 好調를 밟는 太西舘의 주인은 高麗云氏. 氏는 얼마 전까지도 조고만 요리品店을 경영하든 분으로 큰 성공을 이룬 분이다.

東明舘
趙南鎭씨가 본래 市內 瑞麟町에서 七星舘이란 소요리점을 하여가든 것이 점차 성공하여 東明舘을 이루게 된 것이다.

朝鮮舘
明月舘支店에서 과거 17, 8년간 帳簿係에 있으면서 勤勉의 날을 보내든 金畊堷씨가 작년 12월부터 처음 시작하엿다. 아직 사십 미만의 활동가인 씨는 다년간 요리업 체험이 상당한 분이라고 한다.

飮碧亭
경영주 韓澤洙씨는 조선의 명문가인 韓主高씨의 愛孫으로 本年 삼십 미만의 弱冠의 몸이다. 방면이 다른 요리업 경영에 발을 듸려 놓은 씨는 心腹의 人 趙萬九씨에게 모든 경영을 맷기고 있다. 서울에 있는 요리옥으로서는 그 위치가 別莊地帶에 처하여 있어 展望의 風光은 실로 絶好하다.
