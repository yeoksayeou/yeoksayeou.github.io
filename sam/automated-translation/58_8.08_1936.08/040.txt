Seeking Contemporary Hermit-Scholars

Who is this Mr. Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭氏) whom the writer is now seeking? Since everyone well knows the stories about Mr. Kwŏn, whose many deeds have left a deep impression on our memories, I will omit the details and trace only the outlines in a cursory manner (走馬山格). Related to the Kimi Yukwŏl Incident (己未六月之變), that is, the Queen Min Incident (閔妣事件), which once shook this land, he was unable to remain here and went into exile on the Japanese mainland. He spent ten years, not a short period, unable to live openly, wrapped in sorrow and anxiety, living a life of much regret and many tears. He returned to this land in the Pyŏngo year (丙午年, 1906) along with Messrs. Son Pyŏng-hŭi (孫秉熙) and O Se-ch'ang (吳世昌). Not only that, but he also served for many years as the Head of the Business Department of the Taehan Hyŏphoe (大韓協會), forgetting sleep and meals while rushing about (東奔西走). As a pivotal figure in Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎), he led many people and has continued thus until today<62>; furthermore, his significant activities as an important executive in the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) still remain fresh in our memories.

Last month, I wondered what this gentleman, with such a complex history, now 76 years old by Korean count, is doing. What views on life and society does he hold? Although his friends nearby say this and that, seeing once is better than hearing a hundred times (百聞不如一見). I decided to visit him and stir the strings of his heart (心琴).

Last 29th, at nine in the morning, I knocked on the gate of his house in the Kye-dong alley. Soon, a child came out, and I gave my name card (名刺), which was delivered to Mr. Kwŏn. He guided me into a room that serves as his study and reception room. Whenever I see him, his face, possessing the integrity of pine and bamboo (松竹), shows a stern light of indomitable spirit (百折不屈). Though nearing eighty, what is this? He wears a bright blue jeogori jacket paired with grey trousers, sitting upright. Although his face is wrinkled and his hair is white, I feel as if I am meeting a young man. If he still had living parents, one might imagine the ancient Lao Laizi (老來子), who wore colorful clothes at 70 to hide his age from his parents, but that is not the case for Mr. Kwŏn. If asked the reason for his blue clothes, he would likely smile without answering, his heart at ease (笑面不答心自閑). Looking around, on top of a chest are stacked Ch'ŏndogyo books, including *Sijŏnju Cho Hwa Chŏng, Yŏngse Pulmang Man Sa Chi* (侍天主造化定, 永世不忘萬事知), alongside classics like *Annotated Romance of the Western Chamber* (註解 西廂記), *Tongguk Pyŏnggam* (東國兵鑑), *Chunggyŏngji* (中京誌), *Samguk Sagi* (三國史記), *Collected Works of Yŏnam* (燕岩文集), and *Yŏrha Ilgi* (熱河日記). On his desk lie various newspapers and magazines.

Me: How have you been spending your time recently?
Him: Just passing time without anything special. Now that I've grown old, it seems there isn't much for me to do.

Me: Looking back on all the past events now, you must feel deeply moved (感懷無量).
Him: Well, perhaps it's because my body has weakened, and my spirit along with it, but even just a few years ago, whenever thoughts of the time I was exiled to the Japanese mainland due to the so-called Kimi Yukwŏl Incident (己未六月之變) came to mind, uncontrollable feelings from the past would evoke countless emotions in me. Now, it seems even those intense recollections are fading.

Me: How many years were you with the Taehan Hyŏphoe (大韓協會)?
Him: I think it was probably 5 or 6 years. Thinking back on how vigorously I bustled about then, with spirits soaring to the heavens (意氣衝天) like you young people now, just makes me laugh.

Me: After that, you were always involved with Ch'ŏndogyo, and for a while, you were also in the Sin'ganhoe?
Him: That's right. Matters like the Sin'ganhoe are recent events, so you all probably know them well.

Hobbies and Reading

Me: What do you do to pass the time these days? <63>
Him: There's nothing special I do. I play with the young children, and when I have time and am not tired, I browse through those old books stacked over there.

Me: When you read now, do the words still enter your head as they used to?
Him: Not really. The purpose of reading is not like that of young people, who read specifically for research. I just read to relieve boredom, so it only provides a slight sense of satisfaction at the moment of reading.

Me: (Mixing in a laugh) Sir, why do you wear blue clothes like a young person?
Him: Haha, you ask all sorts of things. If an old person like me wears old-looking clothes on top of that, what would that be like? Sitting here wearing these blue clothes, my heart feels a bit younger too. (He laughs)

Me: (Pointing to a framed picture of peony flowers on the wall) Sir, you also seem to greatly enjoy looking at flowers. Seeing that you have hung up a picture of bright red peonies...
Him: My granddaughter brought that and hung it up, but the pleasure of looking at flowers is something I haven't disliked, then or now. (He laughs)

Me: Please excuse my bluntness, but you don't have any worries about your livelihood, do you?
Him: Well, it's regrettable that I can't do everything as I wish because I don't have money like others, but for daily living expenses, thanks to my son who previously attended medical school (醫學校) and is now practicing as a doctor, we manage somehow.

Me: Sir, exactly how old are you this year?
Him: It seems the day I die is not far off now. They say I am seventy-three. Thinking quietly while sitting about how one passes through life, it seems quite amusing, if one can call it that.

How Will You Spend Your Remaining Life?

Me: Now that you are advanced in age, how do you plan to spend your future days?
Him: Since I have been involved with Ch'ŏndogyo from before, I intend to continue on this path. Other than that, there's nothing specific I plan to do.

Me: Even now, if circumstances demanded, as in the past, that you not sit idly by, would you consider being active again like in former days?
Him: Well, one would have to face the situation to know, but now, the kind of ambition I had in my youth, when my energy was overflowing, seems to have all disappeared.

After exchanging these words, as I was about to leave, he bid me farewell, saying, "Please go carefully." A peaceful look lingered on his face. <64>

**Summary**

This 1936 interview profiles Kwŏn Tong-jin, a 73-year-old Korean nationalist figure, reflecting on his past activism and current life. Kwŏn discusses his exile after the "Kimi Yukwŏl Incident / Queen Min Incident," his roles in the Taehan Hyŏphoe, Ch'ŏndogyo, and Sin'ganhoe, and his present quiet life reading classics and enjoying simple pleasures. He expresses a sense of detachment from past struggles due to age but reaffirms his lifelong commitment to Ch'ŏndogyo. The interview captures his enduring spirit despite his advanced age, symbolized by his choice of youthful blue clothing. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the mindset of an aging first-generation nationalist leader during the mid-colonial period, his reflections on key historical events (though with some apparent chronological confusion by the author regarding the exile trigger), his connection to Ch'ŏndogyo and past organizations like Sin'ganhoe, and the personal circumstances of such figures under Japanese rule. His comments on diminished youthful ambition versus continued spiritual commitment provide insight into the evolution of nationalist sentiment across a lifetime.

기사형태	소식

當代 處士 찾어
지금 필자가 차즈려는 權東鎭씨는 엇더한 분이든가, 너무나 우리의 긔억에 感銘깊흔 일이 만엇든 氏에게 대한 이약이는 누구나 잘 아는 바임으로 자서한 것은 그만두고 走馬 山格으로 대강만 더듬어 보기로 하겟다. 한때 잇땅을 뒤끌케 하든 己未六月之變 즉 閔妣事件에 관련되야 부득이 이땅에 잇지 못하고 일본내지에 망명하야 10년이란 쩔지 안흔 세월을 버젓하게 보내지 못하고 憂愁와 思慮 속에 싸여서 多恨多淚의 생활을 하시다가, 孫秉熙, 吳世昌씨 등과 갓치 丙午年에 이 땅에 다시 도라오섯다. 그뿐 아니라, 大韓協會實業部長으로 여러 해 동안 잇스면서 寢食을 잇고 東奔西走한 일도 잇섯고, 天道敎의 重鎭으로 만흔 민중을 잇끌고 오날까지＜62＞ 나려왓섯고 또한 新幹會에 重要幹部로 잇스면서 활동이 만엇든 것은 아직도 우리의 긔억에 새롭고도 남움이 잇다.
지난달에 이러케 단순치 안흔 역사를 가진 氏는 春秋 76을 算하는 잇대 무얼하고 게시는가, 또는 엇더한 인생관, 사회관을 가지고 게시는가 주위에 잇는 氏의 친우 여러분들은 이러니 저러니 하지만은 百聞이 不如一見이어든, 氏를 한번 차저 心琴을 흔들어 보기로 하고,
지난 29일 아참 아홉시, 桂洞골목 氏의 문을 두라렷다. 이윽고, 아해가 나오기로 名刺을 내어 주엇더니 氏의게로 전한다. 氏는 자기의 書齋요, 應接室인 방으로 안내한다. 언제보든지 松竹의 절개를 가진 氏의 얼골에는 百折不屈의 엄격한 빗이 흐르고 잇다. 나히 팔순을 잡어들엇것만 웬일인가. 샛파란 저고리에다가 회색바지를 밧처 입고, 正坐하신 것이 비록 얼골에 주름쌀이 잡혓고 백발이 성성하것만 오히려 젊은이를 대하는 듯한 늣김이 든다. 아직도 부모를 모시고 게시다면 부모에게 늙은 것을 감추기 위하야 70에 色옷 입은 옛날 老來子를 연상하렷만은 그럿치도 안을 氏의 파랑옷은 그 이유를 물어본대야 笑面不答心自閑하실 듯 四面을 흘러보니 궤짝 우에다가, 侍天主造化定, 永世不忘萬事知의 天道敎書籍을 위시하야, 註解 西廂記, 東國兵鑑, 中京誌, 三國史記, 燕岩文集, 熱河日記 등 古書를 가득이 싸여 두웟다. 그러고 책상우에는 각종 신문잡지가 노여 잇다.
나, 최근 엇더케 지나심니가.
그, 그저 별일 업시 지남니다. 이제는 나먹고보니 별로 할 일도 업는가 봄니다.
나, 지나간 모든 일을 지금 회고하여 보신다면 감개무량 하시지요.
그, 글세올시다, 지금은 육체가 쇠잔하자, 정신까지 쇠잔하여 그러지는 몰으지만은 몃 해 전만 하여도 所謂 己未六月之變으로 말미암아, 일본내지에 망명하든 때 일이 머리 속에 떠올을 때마다, 것잡을 수 업시 지난날 회포가 나로 하여곰 만감을 자아내드니 지금은 그럿케 심히 추억되는 것도 업서지는가 봄니다.
