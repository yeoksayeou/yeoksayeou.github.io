The Paradise Lancers

What a big deal
"What a downpour! The rain that suddenly started shows no sign of stopping. My wife went out without an umbrella, this is a big problem!..."
"Why are you worrying so much? She'll just go into some house and rest until the rain stops. What could happen?"
"Ha- You don't understand, *that's* the big problem!"

A Thoughtful Gift
"Mother, for your birthday this year,<229> I'll give you a really splendid vase (花甁) as a present."
"Oh dear, but when it comes to vases, don't I already have a splendid one?"

Son's Rebellion
Son: Father, is it okay if I go over to Tolswae's place to play?<230>
Father: Are you hanging out with the son of a lowborn fellow like that!
Son: Father, then, is it not okay even if I just go over to beat Tolswae up?

The Greedy Person
"The guy who moved in next door this time seems terribly poor!"
"How did you figure out he was poor already?"
"Of course, I know! Is there any doubt? He doesn't have a single thing I want to borrow!"

A Fair Legal Fee
Client: Ah- what are you saying? The legal fee is three-quarters of the settlement money?
Lawyer: Ha- But nevertheless,<231> isn't it true that the three thousand Won appeared out of thin air thanks to my skill alone?
Client: But look here, wasn't *I* the one who got run over by the car (轢傷)?
Lawyer: Oh, that sort of thing is something anyone could do-

Medicine Cost
Doctor: Regarding your illness, you should know that you recovered not so much from my medicine, but more from your wife's kind nursing.
Patient: Yes- is that so? In that case, I'll pay my medicine bill to my wife, so don't worry.
Doctor: "......"

Just the Same Temperament
Woman: I really dislike men.<232> That's why I intend to spend my whole life single.
Man: Yes- is that so? How very strange. How could our temperaments be so exactly alike? I greatly dislike women. Since our temperaments are so alike, how about we spend time together?<233>

**Summary**

This article is a collection of short, humorous anecdotes or jokes typical of the period. The jokes rely on situational irony, misunderstandings, wordplay, and cynical observations about human nature, covering topics like marital worries, gift-giving, social class, neighborly relations, professional fees (lawyer, doctor), and courtship. From a historical perspective, the text offers insights into the social norms, language use (including older orthography and colloquialisms), and common humor of 1930s Korea under Japanese rule. The mention of social class ("lowborn fellow") and the dynamics between professionals and clients reflect contemporary societal structures and concerns.
