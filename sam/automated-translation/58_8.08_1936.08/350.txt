Bee-Butterfly
						Yun Kŭg-yŏng
Bee-Butterfly
It is April, in the bee-butterfly season
The buckwheat field has opened wide
The buckwheat flowers have ripened

It is July, the sound of cicadas
Waves across the whole mountain
The forest grove has ripened

It is July, the setting sun
Runs along the new road (新裝路)
The swallow's tail has ripened <275>

Cow
Where the millstone turns
The cow circles
The farmhand circles

If this road stretched three thousand ri
To Pukkando where my beloved is
Going would have been better

All day long, steamed barley
Will it even become half a sŏm?
The sun has set

Moon, moon, bright moon
Oh dear, the cow cries
The farmhand cries too <276>

Ninaninaya
On the very peak of that mountain, my beloved's song
Crossing this valley, turning around that mountain, endlessly unfolding
Scattering on the river water, crying at the boat's side
The moon is bright too, boatman, ninaninaya

Beneath the tree branches, a blade of grass
Tossing and turning, blown by the wind
Will it enter my beloved's skirt fold, or not?
The moon is bright too, white gull, ninaninaya

Let's Unleash Love
Pair by pair, pigeons
Holding green peas (녹두알), hid in the green pea field
My love, come quickly, be embraced in these arms
Let's find the woods of Pukak and unleash our love <277>

Pair by pair, wild geese
Playing with the moon afloat, entered the clouds
My love, come quickly, be embraced in these arms
Let's spend the night on the Han River and unleash our love

My Beloved's Photograph
Holding the photograph you sent in my bosom
I looked for the moon in the scattering clouds
That affectionate night from ten years ago
As soon as I found it in a dream, the rooster crowed

While my beloved's photograph rests comfortably in this bosom
In the dawn wind, fallen leaves drop
On this long autumn night, in my rusted heart
The owl hoots, hoot-hoot, the paper screen cries <278>

Shall I go, Shall I not go
Shall I go, shall I not go
Shall I follow my beloved and go?
To go, it's a distant thousand ri
Not to go, it's my youth
Boatman, bring the boat
Shall I follow the waterway and go?

Shall I go, shall I not go
Shall I follow my beloved and go?
To go, I don't know where to go
Not to go, the path of life is distant
Raising the sail on that boat
Shall I go with the wind's path? <279>
<275-279>

**Summary**

This collection consists of six lyrical poems by Yun Kŭg-yŏng, employing vivid natural imagery and themes of rural life, longing, and separation. The poems depict scenes from different seasons ("Bee-Butterfly"), the toil of a farmhand ("Cow"), wistful folk-song like verses ("Ninaninaya"), passionate yearning ("Let's Unleash Love"), reminiscing over a photograph ("My Beloved's Photograph"), and the painful indecision of whether to embark on a long journey to reunite with a loved one ("Shall I go, Shall I not go"). From a historical perspective, the poems offer glimpses into the emotional landscape of 1930s Korea. The mention of Pukkando in "Cow" directly references a key destination for Korean migrants during the colonial period, hinting at the separations and hardships faced by many. The general tone of longing, melancholy, and the descriptions of rural labor could reflect the broader social and economic conditions under Japanese rule.
