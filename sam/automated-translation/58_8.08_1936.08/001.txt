How much salary do the Central Advisory Council Councilors (中樞院參議) receive? Upon investigation, there are two ranks among the Councilors: Ch'ŏgim (勅任) treatment and also Ch'inim (秦任) treatment. This distinction is based on the individual's official rank and qualifications, and they are appointed by the Government-General. Previously, the highest annual salary for Councilors receiving Ch'ŏgim treatment was 3,000 wŏn, with several grades below that. However, after the revision of the Central Advisory Council regulations, the average annual salary is said to be 2,800 wŏn, which corresponds to a monthly salary of about 220-odd wŏn. Councilors receiving Ch'inim treatment also previously received around 1,200 wŏn annually (average 110 wŏn monthly), but this has now been lowered to an overall average of 1,000 wŏn, meaning they receive a little over 80 wŏn per month on average. Is it that the number of Councilors, which was small before, has gradually increased, and in inverse proportion to the increase in personnel, their salaries have slightly decreased?
However, it is said that among them, there are many non-remunerated, i.e., honorary councilors (名譽參議). This means many people hold the official position as an honor without receiving a salary. These honorary councilors are said to be numerous among those receiving Ch'ŏgim treatment.
In the 2,800 wŏn category are Messrs. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Han Sang-yong (韓相龍), Chang Hŏn-sik (張憲植), etc.
In the 1,000 wŏn category are Messrs. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善), Kim Sa-yŏn (金思演), etc.
There are many others besides these, and we will report sequentially as the investigation progresses. <17>

Rumors of Top Executive Changes at the Chungang Ilbo

According to recent rumors, Mr. Sŏng Nak-hŏn (成樂憲), the current Managing Director of Tongil Bank (東一銀行), is expected to bravely retire from his position as Managing Director following the general meeting this July. It is said that understandings have already been reached with the financial authorities and the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行), so this can be seen as a likely event. His replacement as Managing Director is expected to be Mr. Sŏng Nak-hŏn's eldest nephew, Wŏn-gyŏng (元慶). If this materializes, the repercussions are expected to reach the *Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo* (朝鮮中央日報), one of our Korean private newspapers.
That is, Mr. Sŏng Wŏn-gyŏng (成元慶), who brought in a huge sum of 200,000 wŏn from his own circle when the said newspaper carried out a capital increase previously, serves as a director of the paper. If he joins Tongil Bank, he is expected to resign from his directorship, and Mr. Sŏng Nak-hŏn will reportedly take up a directorship at the *Chungang Ilbo* in his place. In other words, the two men will swap positions. If this becomes a reality, general observers are paying close attention, anticipating a major shift among the top executives of the *Chungang Ilbo*. Furthermore, Mr. Kim Sŏng-gwŏn (金聲權) is also said to be resigning his directorship at Tongil Bank, sharing the same fate as Mr. Sŏng Nak-hŏn. The impact of the changes among the top executives of the *Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo* is expected to be quite profound, but these are still just rumors. How it concludes, only time will tell...

From Yŏ Un-hyŏng's Tongue to His Pen

Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨), who, while serving as a newspaper company president, has been ceaselessly busy in the Korean press world, appearing everywhere from officiating at private wedding ceremonies to giving congratulatory addresses at graduation and new student welcoming events—a great lion of lectures, speeches, and eloquence, always showing his imposing figure on the peninsula's speaking platforms that seem extremely stagnant—is recently rumored to have made remarks to the following effect:
Henceforth, regardless of the type of gathering, he will rarely appear on the speaker's platform. Instead, while taking a break for a while, he will change direction from before and focus his efforts on writing for publication. Thus, starting last month, he has been wielding his powerful pen, publishing installments under the title "My Reminiscences" (나의 回想記) in a central magazine. As for Mr. Yŏ, there will likely be frequent opportunities to encounter him in print in the future, but it is said it will be difficult to find his former presence on the speaking platform. <22>
Upon hearing this rumor, the intellectual circles of the capital, Seoul, feel regretful on one hand, while also wondering quite anxiously if a major change might not be coming to the peninsula's speech front. Indeed, for the past several months, Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng's resonant and powerful words have been completely absent, making it a topic of conversation on the streets of Seoul lately.
Truly, amid the global trend of turning and turning again, Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng has also turned from the lion's roar on the stage to writing on paper; this too is a noteworthy matter.

One P'yŏng of Land in Seoul for 10,000 Wŏn

Land prices are rising and rising in both cities and provinces, showing a rate of increase that decidedly stands out even compared to the period immediately after the Great War [WWI]. This might be the first time such land prices have been seen in Korean history. But here is something astonishing.
It is the offer to pay 10,000 wŏn (一金萬圓) for one p'yŏng (坪) of land. This land is located in the very heart of the Pukch'on commercial district of Kyŏngsŏng city, specifically on Chongno 4-ga-ri (鍾路四街里), and its owner is Mr. Cho Hyo-sun (趙孝淳). A Japanese cosmetics dealer wants to buy the site where the Cho Hyo-sun Cloth Store (趙孝淳布木店) is located to erect an advertising tower for "Rate Cream" (レートクリーム), offering 10,000 wŏn per p'yŏng. The total area of the Cho Hyo-sun store site is 20 p'yŏng, meaning they offered 200,000 wŏn. However, Mr. Cho resolutely refused, believing he could sell it for more than 10,000 wŏn if he waited a bit longer. In any case, 10,000 wŏn per p'yŏng is said to be a land price heard for the first time recently, becoming a topic of conversation in the Chongno commercial district.

It details the salaries of Korean councilors in the Japanese colonial Central Advisory Council, rumors of executive shuffles involving prominent Korean figures at Tongil Bank and the *Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo*, lists foreign correspondents in Tokyo active during the February 26 Incident, and provides extensive lists of major Korean shareholders in various banks and companies across different sectors (banking, insurance, industry, media, etc.). It also notes Yŏ Un-hyŏng's shift from public speaking to writing, mentions the wealth of the young head of Japan's Sumitomo group, compares the income taxes of Japanese zaibatsu, presents a ranking of historical "great men," reports an unprecedentedly high land price offer in Seoul's Chongno district, and recounts an anecdote about author Kang Yong-hŭl's early recognition.
For historians, the article is particularly valuable for its detailed snapshot of the Korean economic elite in 1936, listing major shareholders in key Korean-run enterprises, including banks, newspapers (*Tong-A Ilbo*, *Chosŏn Ilbo*, *Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo*), and industrial firms like Kyŏngsŏng Spinning and Weaving. The information on Central Advisory Council salaries and the discussion surrounding figures like Yŏ Un-hyŏng, Sŏng Nak-hŏn, and Kim Sŏng-su provide insights into the activities and status of prominent Koreans under colonial rule. The list of foreign correspondents covering Japan highlights the international attention on events like the February 26 Incident, while the report on Seoul land prices reflects economic conditions.

中樞院參議ㆍ月給調査狀

中樞院參議는 月給을얼마나밧나? 이것을 調査하여보건대 參議에는 兩階級이잇스니, 其一은 勅任待遇와  秦任待遇이다. 이것은 그 當者의 官職高下와 資格如何로 區分되여 總督府에서 任命하는것인데 從前에는 勅任待遇를하는 參議에게는 年俸三千圓이 最高로 그 以下 여러 等級이 잇섯는데 中樞院法規가 改正된 後론 平均年俸二千八百圓이라니 月俸 約二百二十餘圓의 比例오 秦任官待遇를 밧는 參議도 亦從前에는 千二百圓(月平均百十圓)假量이든 것이 지금은 통트리 平均千圓으로 引下되엿다하니 月平均八十圓남짓하게밧는세음이된다. 前에는 參議數가적든 것이 漸次로 任命人員數가 만해젓고 또 人員이만해지는 反比例로 그 給料는 조곰씩적어진다고할가?
그런데 그中에는 無報酬 卽 各譽參議가만타하니 그職를 名譽로삼어서, 俸給을아니밧고 官職만 띄고잇는사람이 만타는인녜 이 名譽參議는 勅任待遇를밧는中에 多數라고한다
二千八百圓級에는 崔麟, 韓相龍, 張憲植氏等이오
千圓級에는, 崔南善, 金思演氏等等이다.

呂運亨氏舌에서 筆로

新聞社社長으로 게시면서 朝鮮言論界에서 쉴사이없이 奔忙하신 呂運亨氏는 個人의 結婚式主禮에서부터 卒業生, 新入學生大會의 祝辭에이르기까지 講談, 演說雄辯의 大獅子吼로, 極度로 沈滯한듯한 半島의 演壇에 늘 그 夾英한 風姿를나타내드니 最近에 들니는 風聞에 依하면 氏는 다음과같은 意味의 말슴을 하신다고-.
氏는 今後로는 좀체로 어떤 集會를 莫論하고 演壇우에 나스지않고, 한동안 機口하시는 한편, 前과는 方向을 달니하여 紙上을 通하여 執筆에 注力 하시겟다는 것이다. 그래서 月前부터 中央誌에다 『나의 回想記』란 題下에 回를 거듭하면서 健筆을 휘날리는데, 氏로서는 今後도 자주 紙上에서는 對할 機會를 얻을것이나, 演壇에서는 전날의 그 風貌를 찻기가 어려우리라고 한다.＜22＞
이 所聞을 듯게되는 서울長安의 인테리層에서는 한편 섭섭하게 녁이는 同時에 半島演說戰線에 一大異狀이 招來되지나 않을가하고 퍽이나 궁굼하게 녁인다는데, 事實 最近數朔동안은 도모지 呂運亨氏의 그우렁찬 힘있는 말슴을 들어볼수가 없어서, 요사히 서울거리의 한 話題꺼리가 되었다는 것이다
實로 世界的으로 轉向,에서 轉向하는 風潮속에서, 呂運亨氏도 壇上의 獅子吼에서 紙上執筆로 돌아젓스니 이도 注目 일이다.

二十三歲에 一億八千萬圓富豪
