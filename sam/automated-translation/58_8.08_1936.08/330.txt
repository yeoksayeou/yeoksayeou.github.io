Publication Date: 1936년 08월 01일
Article Title: 靑春譜 (A Song of Youth)
Author: 赤駒
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

靑春譜
						赤駒

Beneath the fierce June sun, the deepening green shade day by day,
The waves quietly rolling in, laden with the south wind,
Youths! Do you see? Seeing, what do you feel?
Do you not feel the shining nature—nature's tranquility?

Amidst the forests, the chaotic chirping of myriad birds,
Following from shade to shade, the breath of all creatures seeking companions,
Do you hear? Hearing, what do you feel?
Do you not feel the pulsation of life—the symbol of vitality?

Youths! Your name is Youth!
The heart of youth is like a cloud patch running across the empty sky,
Joining together they become rain, yet knowing not where they go next,<271>
The path ahead for youth is like a lone boat setting sail on the great ocean,
Hoping for the distant shore (彼岸), continuing the arduous voyage.

Oh, youths! Oh, youth!
Now, the youth in nature—the youth in all life,
Does it not call to your youth?
From the mountains, from the fields, from the water
—From the heart of the entire universe, from your chests?

Youths! Vigorous life!
Kick away all decaying notions,
Come out onto the stage of the youthful universe!
Come out and dance! Sing!
And love with all your heart!
Love with all your life!

And so, not to fail the distant voyage
That will bring the hope and light of the future,
Possess a shining youth—possess a strong youth! <272>
<271-272>

**Summary**

This poem, "A Song of Youth," by "Red Colt" (赤駒), is an impassioned call to young people. Using vivid natural imagery—the summer sun, deepening green, waves, birds, and animals—it urges youth to perceive and feel the vibrant pulse of life around them. It compares youth to unpredictable clouds and a determined ship on a vast ocean, emphasizing both its boundless potential and the challenging journey towards future hopes. The poet exhorts young people to cast off old, restrictive ideas, embrace life fully through dance, song, and love, and maintain a strong, shining spirit for the voyage ahead. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the emphasis on vitality, action, and hope often found in youth-oriented literature during the colonial period. While not explicitly political, its call to reject "decaying notions" and embrace a vigorous future could resonate with aspirations for change and progress within the constraints of the era. Historians might find its focus on inner strength and future hope, rather than direct resistance, noteworthy for understanding cultural expressions of the time.

靑春譜
						赤駒
강열한 六月의 太陽아래 나날이 지터가는 綠陰을
南쪽 바람을 가득 시ㅅ고 고요히 밀리는 물결을
젊으니 들이여! 그대들은 보는가-보고 무엇을 늣기는가
빗나는 自然을-自然의 정숙을 늣기지 안는가

숩과 숩사히에 어즈럽게 우지지는 뭇새의 소리를
그늘과 그늘을 따르며 동무를 구하는 온갓 즘생들의 숨결을
그대들은 듯는가-듯고 무엇을 늣기는가
生命의 躍動을-躍動의 表徵을 늣기지 안는가

젊으니 들이여! 그대들의 일홈은 靑春!
靑春의 마음은 虛空을 달니는 구름장과도 가타여
합치면 비되면서도 가서 다음을 모르고＜271＞
靑春의 압길은 大洋에 돗다른 한조각 배와도 가타여
彼岸의 希望을 바라고 艱艱한 航程을 繼續하는 것이다

오오 젊으니 들이여! 靑春이여!
지금 自然에온 靑春은-모-든 生命에온 靑春은
그대들의 靑春을 부르지 안는가
山에서 들에서 물에서
-오-ㄴ 宇宙의 心臟에서 그대들의 가슴에서

젊으니 들이여! 씩씩한 生命이여!
모-든 좀먹은 觀念을 박차버리고
靑春宇宙의 舞臺로 나오라!
나와서 춤추라! 노래하라!
그리고 마음것 사랑하라!
목슴을 다하야 사랑하라!

그리하야 압날의 希望과 光明을 가저올
遙遠한 航海를 그릇치지 안키위하야
빗나는 靑春을 가즈라-굿세인 靑春을 가즈라!＜272＞
