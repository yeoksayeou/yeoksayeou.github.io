西部戰線 別탈업다 (No Trouble on the Western Front)
						By Germany's Remarque (獨逸 루마루케 作)

1
The platform at "P'ŭremel" station was bustling with a chaotic wave of people. It was a wave of numerous soldiers heading towards the battlefield under the name "brave warriors defending the glorious fatherland," and the parents, wives, children, and siblings bidding them farewell. The sound of kisses was heard like popping beans. White handkerchiefs fluttered ceaselessly. The whistle signaling the train's departure was heard.

"Pa'ul! Ppa-rett'! Rek'uru? Kuruppu! Are you well? Do well and come back! Everything is for the fatherland. Kick the Frenchmen (불난서놈들) in the chest. Cut off the Englishmen's (영길리놈들) heads and come back!"

Shouting this was Germany's loyal dog (忠犬), the schoolteacher K'ant'ŭ Retk'ŭ. It was his final request, encouraging young students barely nineteen years old. However, the young men's faces showed no particular sign of emotion.

The soldiers were crammed into military freight cars, like pigs or cattle, without benches or even crude window openings.

The train began to depart. Parents and siblings, despite their choked hearts, forced courage and shouted in gilded voices, "It's for our country. Do well and come back!"

"Yeah! We'll do what we can and come back!"

Such replies flowed out frequently from within the train cars. The inside of the train was packed so tightly one couldn't move freely. There were youths chatting energetically. There were also laborer-like fellows with frown lines creasing their brows, looking resentful. There were optimists saying they'd grab any French bastards they encountered. The chubby "Ppŏm" was pale-faced and silent. He had been dragged before the conscription commander against his will. But surely "Ppŏm" was not the only one with such a complexion. Many, many more people must have been thinking this way. And it seemed no one was thinking about what their future held.

However, the simple poor people could be the most cool-headed and composed. This was because they could not help but feel the foreboding sense that war meant misfortune!

"Hey Kuruppu, isn't your head feeling strange? I can't think about anything anymore. Am I just going to become a complete fool like this?"

Pa'ul lowered his voice and spoke.

"Don't talk nonsense. Aren't you the best at mathematics (數學) in our class? It's alright, something will happen once we get to the battlefield."

Indeed, once they got to the battlefield, something would happen. An arm might be lost, a leg broken, or if fate was generous, one's entire body could be pulverized and die.

Not long after that, they arrived, carrying their rifles, at a place about ten *majang* (마쟝 - unit of distance, approx. 10 km?) from the battlefield. Pa'ul and his classmates were all scattered. However, Kuruppu, Ppa-rett', Kemmerihi, and Pa'ul, the four of them, ended up together in the Ninth Squad. For these young friends, whose taste of warm life from school and home was deeply ingrained in their bones, life in this chaotic warzone was truly like a hell mixed with lies.<68> Blisters constantly formed on their feet, weary from boots as hard as stones. But being docile as cattle was the absolute rule here.

In the meantime, Pa'ul, "Ch'yaden," and "Esŭdoŭsŭ?" gained a comrade named "Kajinsŭk'i." This man Kajinsŭk'i, about forty years old, was a stubborn and tough fellow who acted like the leader even among his peers. Because of him, Pa'ul and his group sometimes went to places on the battlefield where bullets didn't often reach, and occasionally got to eat delicious, high-quality food that soldiers couldn't even dream of.

As one month, then two, passed, the young men became animals nearly devoid of feeling due to the grueling military drills. They realized the inescapable suffering. But instead, like humans who hadn't seen a grain of rice for a year, their hearts burned with the desire for delicious food. Just the thought of eating their fill and sleeping soundly—

It was a beautifully clear day. Everyone had received two or three letters and wore happy faces. Pa'ul and the others went behind the cowshed and sprawled out in the grassy field. To the right, a sturdy communal latrine with a roof was visible. Pa'ul thought back to when he first became a soldier and experienced military life.

"Hey Kuruppu. Our communal latrine didn't even have doors, did it? We had to squat like in a train car, all visible at a glance. Why do soldiers have to be watched even when taking a dump?"

"Yeah. Watching each other even while relieving ourselves. It was truly unbearable from embarrassment! At first." But now, the young men had completely changed. Such things became routine, as easy as eating cold porridge. Moreover, going to the battlefield, relieving oneself was a kind of pleasure. It was as natural as eating and drinking. Only then was it a truly innocent time without any thoughts. The infinitely high blue sky above. On the horizon, yellow observation balloons floated. Shrapnel (榴散彈) fragments flew about, and flowers bloomed all around.

From near the cowshed, the sound of an organ drifted over.

From the bottom of Pa'ul and the others' hearts, there was a strong but suppressed emotion that couldn't be explained. Therefore, the red poppies blooming in the grass, the tasty food, the tobacco, and this summer wind—everything felt fresh and intense.

Kuruppu, as if suddenly remembering, asked,
"Has anyone among you seen Kemmerihi?"

"That fellow is in the field hospital,"
Pa'ul answered quietly.

"They say he's dying from a through-and-through gunshot wound to the upper thigh (上腿部貫通銃創). Did he get his discharge certificate (歸還免狀)?"
The one speaking was "Pparets'ŭ." They decided to visit the man that afternoon.

Kuruppu, who had been reading a letter, said,
"K'ant'ŭ Retk'ŭ (that teacher from before) sends his regards to everyone." Everyone laughed at his words.

"And what else did he write?"
Pparets'ŭ deliberately asked. Kuruppu laughed and said,
"He says we are iron youths defending the fatherland!"

"What!"
Pa'ul felt his head throb and stamped his foot irritably. Yes, Germany was full of K'ant'ŭ Retk'ŭs with such sentiments. Iron youths, iron youth! But youth, youthfulness, and submission abandon us, tossing away our twenty-year-old green youth, he thought. (Continued in next issue) <69>

**Summary**

This article is a Korean translation of a portion of Erich Maria Remarque's "All Quiet on the Western Front," published in the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* in 1930. It depicts young German soldiers, including Paul Bäumer (Pa'ul) and his school friends, being sent to the Western Front during WWI. It contrasts the jingoistic encouragement of their teacher, Kantorek (K'ant'ŭ Retk'ŭ), with the soldiers' growing disillusionment, fear, and focus on basic survival needs amidst the harsh realities of training and the impending horrors of war. The narrative highlights the dehumanizing effects of military life and foreshadows the physical and emotional toll of the conflict, mentioning the severe injury of their comrade Kemmerich (Kemmerihi).

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the publication of this translation is notable. Presenting a German anti-war novel, critical of nationalism and depicting the grimness of war, in a Korean magazine under Japanese rule suggests a potential space for subtle critiques of militarism or reflections on suffering, even if focused on a European context. It might interest historians studying intellectual currents, censorship, and the reception of foreign literature in colonial Korea, possibly revealing how international themes were adapted or resonated with local concerns about forced mobilization and the cost of imperial conflicts.

「프레멜」정거장의 뿌랏트폼에는 사람들로 어수선한 물걸을 치고 잇섯다. 영광스러운 조국을 직히는 용사」라는 일홈 아래에 싸홈터로 향하여 가는 만흔 병정들과 또 그네들을 전별하는 부모 처자 형제들의 물결이다. 입마추는 소치가 콩복는 드시 들니엇다. 힌수건이 런해 날니엇다. 차가 떠나는 호가소리가 들니엇다.
「바울」「빠렐」「렉루」빠 「구룹뿌」! 조흐냐 잘하고덜 오너라! 모든 것이 조국 때문이다. 불난서놈들의 가슴을 차바리는거야. 영길리놈들의 모가지를 따고 온단말이야」
이러케 부르짓는 것은 독일의 忠犬인 학교교사 칸트 렛크이엇다. 겨우 열아홉에 나는 어린 생도를 격려하는 마즈막 부탁을 하는 터이다. 그러나 젊은이들의 얼골에는 별노 감격하는 빗도 보이지 안엇다.
병정들은 마치 도야지나 마소 모양깐으로 걸상도 퇴챵가튼 퇴창도 업는 군용절차에 드리채웟다.
긔차는 떠나기 시작하엿다. 부모와 형제들은 긔막히는 가슴에도 거짓 용긔를 내어 도금칠한 소리로 「우리나라 때문이다. 잘하고 오너라!」하고 소리첫다.
「응 할 때로 하고 오지!」
이러한 댓구도 렬차 속으로 만히 흘너나왓다. 긔차 속은 모다 용신할 수 업시 가득찻다. 긔운조케 떠드는 청년들도 잇다. 양미간에 내천자를 세우고 분해하는 로동자가튼 놈팽이도 잇다. 불란서의 색기들을 닥치는대로 집어세우겟다는 락천자도 잇다. 뚱뚱보인「뻠」은 얼골이 쌧팔해서 잠잠하고 잇다. 실타고 하는 것을 억지로 징병사령관 압헤 끄을니어 나온 것이다. 그러나 이러한 낫빗을 하고 잇는 것은 결코 「베-ㅁ」뿐이 안일 것 이리라. 더 더 만흔 사람들이 이러케 생각하고 잇슬 터이다. 그러고 누구든지 이 압날이 엇지 될 것을 생각하지 안는 모양이다.
그러나 단순한 가난한 사람들은 가장 냉정하고 침착하게 될 수 잇섯다. 그네의게는 전쟁은 즉 불행한 것이다!하는 짐작을 늣길 수 엇든 까닭이다.
「여보게 구룹부 머리가 엇더케 되지 안나? 나는 이제는 아모것도 생각할 수 업게 되엇네. 이대로 아조 바버가 되고 말지 안나」
「바울」은 소리를 나추어 말한다.
「멍텅구리 소리 말게. 자네는 우리 학급에서 제일 수학(數學)을 잘하지 안나. 괜찬어 싸홈터에나가면 엇더케 되겟지」
실로 싸홈터에 나가기만 하면 엇더케던지 될 것이다. 팔을 떨구던지 다리를 분질느든지 또는 팔자가 신통하면 전신을 가루를 만들어 죽어갈 수도 잇기 때문이다.

그 뒤 얼마 잇지 안어서 그들은 싸홈터로부터 십여마쟝되는 곳까지 총를 메고 왓다. 「바울」과 가튼 학급학생들은 모다 뿔뿔히 갈나지게 되엇다. 그러나 구롭부 빠-렛트 겜매리나 바울의 네사람은 아홉재 반에 모이게 되엇다. 학교와 가정에서 따뜻한 생활을 하여오든 맛이 뻬속에까지 사무친 이 젊은 친구들의 게대하야 이곳 난리판의 생활은 실로 거짓말 셕거 지옥살림과＜68＞ 갓하엿다. 돌뎅이와 가치 굿은 구두에 지친 두 발에서는 종긔가 작고 나섯다. 그러나 마소가치 온순한 것이 여기의 절대규측이엇다.
그리하는 사이에 「바울」에게나 「챠덴」에게나 「에스도흐스」에게는 「카진스키니라」는 동무가 생기엇다. 가진스키-라는 이 사내는 나히 한 사십이나 되는 동무들 팻장에서도 대장질하는 줏대 세고 억센 놈팽이엇다. 그 때문에 파울들에게는 싸홈판에 나가도 벌로 탄환이 잘 오지 안는 곳에 가서 병정들은 꿈에도 생각못할 맛난 상등 음식을 엇어먹는 수가 각금 잇섯다.
한달 두달 지나감에 따라 젊은 청년들은 지독한 군대조련 때문에 거지반 감각이 업는 동물이 되어버렷다. 엇더케 하야서라도 버서날 수 업는 괴로온 일을 깨달엇다. 그러나 그 대신에 마치 한 일년 밥알이라고 구경못한 인간들과 가치 맛난 음식을 먹고 십흔 생각에 가슴이 타는 듯하엿다. 그저 배부르게 먹고 한잠 푹 자다 이러나고 십흔 생각만이―
