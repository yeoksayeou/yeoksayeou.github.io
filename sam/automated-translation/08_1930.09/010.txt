Alas, A Horrible Disaster!
						Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥)

To our ears, which only hear sad news whenever we wake up, now comes the report that 2,000 souls have tragically died due to flood damage (水災), and it is said that hundreds of thousands of victims (罹災民) are likely to wander abroad (海外) this autumn due to starvation (飢餓). Having inherited (引繼밧은) the decisive, lamentable fate (決定的宿命) of the previous generation (先代), we can only tremble (戰慓) upon discovering that we now have nothing left to be taken from us besides our thin bones (骨節) and faded skin (皮). Truly, if there is a natural disaster (天災) (Two lines omitted here - original text), there should at least not be an X disaster (X災), but for us, whatever karmic fire (業火) there may be, at this time when terrifying X calamities (X禍) are reported everywhere (處處), heaven has again burdened us with a great catastrophe (大慘害) amounting to approximately 20 million won, encompassing 130,000 *chō* (町步) of paddies and fields (田畓), 50,000 houses (家屋), and 2,000 lives (人命). Burdened (질머진) with all these disasters (災禍), where shall we find a place of safe dwelling (安住) in the future?

We, who know well that land (土地) is the foundation (基礎) of national collective life (民族的集團生活), possessed the intelligence (聰明) and courage (勇氣) to protect this land, even if barren (瘠土), but now oppression (逼迫) of this intensity (强度) forces us to abandon even the noble emotion (高貴한 感情) of love for the homeland (鄕土愛) and compels us to go abroad (海外). Even if we go, where is there a guarantee (保障) of livelihood (生活)? Our advance abroad is not one possessing financial (金融的) or cultural (文化的) talent (才能) and effort (努力); we are going merely as unavoidable (不可避), unskilled (未熟練) agricultural laborers (農業勞働者) or industrial laborers (工業勞僮者) for the sake of subsistence (口腹), so we cannot help but be overwhelmed (壓倒) by the native laborers and farmers (元住勞働者農民) in the host country (寄寓國) who already sit receiving political (政治的) and economic (經濟的) protection (保護). Does the miserable state (慘狀) of our compatriots (同胞) who have already gone abroad not speak to this? Then what should we do?

Long ago, when the people (民衆) of Judea (『유다야』) were driven as slaves (奴隷) to the land of Babylon (『빠빌냀¼』), one of their nation's (民族) poets lamented, saying, "By the rivers (江邊) of Babylon we sat down and wept when we remembered Zion (시온), our homeland's (祖國) capital. We hung our harps on the willows by the riverbank and merely reminisced and wept." Should we just sit and sigh (歎息) like this? Faced (當面) with this totalizing, accelerating suffering (全軆的加速度的의 受難), what is the right thing for us to do? Truly, contemplating (展望) this great catastrophe (大慘害) this time, we feel that we must make a more active (積極的), more constructive (建設的) effort. (Kim Tong-hwan)

**Summary**

The editorial by Kim Tong-hwan laments the recent devastating floods in Korea, which killed 2,000 people and caused immense property damage, adding to the existing suffering of the Korean people. He questions where Koreans can find refuge when faced with both natural disasters and other unnamed (likely political or social) calamities (referred to as 'X disaster'/'X calamity'). The author notes the intense pressure forcing Koreans to consider emigrating, even though prospects abroad are bleak, as they would likely face exploitation as unskilled laborers competing with protected native workers. Drawing a parallel with the Jewish exile in Babylon, he questions whether Koreans should passively lament their fate or adopt a more active and constructive approach to overcome their accelerating suffering. For historians, this article powerfully illustrates the deep despair and sense of crisis felt by Koreans under Japanese rule in 1930, compounded by natural disasters. The mention of redacted calamities ('X災', 'X禍') hints at censorship and the unspoken political dimension of Korean suffering, while the discussion of emigration reflects the real pressures and difficult choices faced by many Koreans during this period. The final call for proactive effort, despite the bleakness, suggests a struggle for resilience.

鳴呼慘禍
						金東煥
자고 이러나면 슬푼 긔별만 들니는 우리의 귀에 이제 또 水災로 2,000의 生靈이 慘死하고 올 가을 안으로는 그 수 십만의 罹災民들이 飢餓 때문에 海外에 流離할 듯하다고 傳한다. 先代의 痛恨할 決定的宿命을 引繼밧은 我等은 이리하야 이제는 여윈 骨節과 褪色한 皮*밧게 더 빼앗길 것이 업시된 몸임을 發見하고 戰慓할 따름이다. 實로 天災가 잇스면(此間二行略-원문) X災라도 업거나 하여야할거시로되 우리에겐 무슨 業火가 잇섯든지 處處에 戰慓할 X禍를 傳하는 이 때에 하늘은 또 13萬町步의 田畓과 5萬의 家屋과 2,000의 人命等 約 2,000萬圓에 達하는 大慘害를 負擔식히니 이 모든 災禍를 질머진 우리는 장차 어대로 安住의 곳을 차즐것이냐.
土地가 民族的集團生活의 基礎인줄을 잘 아는 우리는 瘠土라도 이 땅을 직힐 聰明과 勇氣를 가젓건만 이제 이와가흔 强度의 逼迫은 高貴한 鄕土愛의 感情조차 버리게 하고 海外에 나가기를 强要한다. 나간들 어대에 生活이 保障될 것이 잇스랴. 우리는 金融的 乃至 文化的의 才能과 努力을 가진 海外進出이 아니고 오직 口腹을 爲한 不可避의 未熟練한 農業勞働者 或은 工業勞僮者로서의 나가는 것이매 寄寓國의 이미 政治的, 經濟的 保護를 밧고 안젓는 元住勞働者農民에게 壓倒를 밧지 아니할 수 업다. 이미 밧게 나간 同胞의 慘狀이 이를 말하고 잇지 안는가. 그러면 우리는 엇더케 할 것인가.
녯날 『유다야』의 民衆들은 『빠빌냀¼』나라에 奴隷로 쫏기여 가면서 그 民族의 一詩人이 『우리는 빠빌논의 江邊에 안자서 祖國의 서울 시온을 回想하고 눈물 흘니리로다. 거문고는 江邊버들가지에 걸고 한갓 回想하고는 울니로다.』하는드시 歎息만 하고 안젓슬것인가. 이 全軆的加速度的의 受難에 當面한 우리들은 엇더케 하여야 올흘 것인가. 실로 今番의 大慘害를 展望하면서 우리들은 보담 더 積極的인 보담 더 건설적인 努力이 잇지안으면 안될 것인가 하노라.(金東煥)
