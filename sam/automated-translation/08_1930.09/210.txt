An Incompetent Doctor's Surgery Means Certain Death

An Incompetent Doctor's Surgery Means Certain Death
At a certain hospital in M-dong, a doctor asked a newly arrived nurse,
"Do you know how to handle a patient after surgery?"
"Yes, I do. You transport them to the morgue!"

Noah's Flood
Pastor: "Noah's flood killed all the living creatures that existed in the world at that time."
Beggar: "So, did it kill all the fish like pollock and trout in the floodwaters too?"
Pastor: "????"

The Right Reason
Pak: "Why did you break off your engagement with Miss Ŭnsuk?"
I: "What else could I do? Just yesterday, when I hugged that woman, she whined at me, saying, 'Kwangsik, you can't do this. If you do, I'll tell Mother!'"
Pak: "Isn't that even cuter?"
I: "Hmph, but my name is Kiyŏng."

A Beautiful Woman
A beautiful woman is heaven for the eyes, hell for the soul, and purgatory for the wallet.

A Nagging Wife and Fine Writing
A certain "pessimist," completely worn out by a nagging wife, [said]:
A filial wife is not as good as a nagging wife; a nagging wife is not as good as earth and stone (土石).<54>

**Summary**

This article is a collection of short jokes, anecdotes, and aphorisms from 1930. The pieces include dark humor about medical incompetence, a literal questioning of the Noah's flood story, a humorous reason for a broken engagement based on mistaken identity, a cynical view of beauty's cost, and a pessimistic comparison involving nagging wives. From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into the humor, social views (on medicine, relationships, women), and everyday language (including older spellings and Hanja usage) of colonial Korea in 1930. The mix of traditional sayings, modern jokes, and references to Western concepts (like pastors and pessimists) reflects the cultural transitions of the period.
