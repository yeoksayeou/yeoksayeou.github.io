Roundtable Conference of Concerned Individuals on the Current Situation
Over 20 individuals concerned about the current situation gathered in one hall
Regarding
1. The Problem of Realizing Japan-Korea Unity (Naeseon Ilche)
2. The Problem of Constructing the East Asian Cooperative Body
3. Various Problems of Domestic Reform
There was an evening of frank opinion exchange. This manuscript is the transcript from that evening.

Date and Time: December 14, Showa 13 (1938), 6:00 PM
Location: Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall Auditorium
Attendees <36>
Yi Kwangsu (Former member of Susyang Tonguhoe)

The Problem of Realizing Japan-Korea Unity (Naeseon Ilche)

An Chun - In this time when the situation is truly wartime, there are many issues before us to discuss, and many things we must do from the standpoint of the people behind the lines (銃後國民). I am thankful that we can have such a gathering at this time to discuss various internal and external problems. From now on, Mr. Cha Chaejŏng will serve as the moderator and proceed with the discussion.

Cha Chaejŏng - Then, let us begin now. All of you gathered here today are people who stood at the forefront of social or national movements in the past and engaged in them actively for many years, so I know you have come to this place with deep study and a thorough attitude. Therefore, I hope you will open your hearts with a truly sincere attitude and present your opinions regarding the path forward for us, the Korean populace, faced with this emergency situation. The order of topics for discussion has been roughly divided into four items:
1. The Theory of Japan-Korea Unity (Naeseon Ilche)
2. The Problem of Domestic Reform
3. The Future Path for Koreans (What should we do going forward?)
4. Our Duty Regarding the East Asian Cooperative Body
However, let us begin with the first issue. Since Governor-General Minami came to his post, the theory of Japan-Korea Unity has become quite serious and is currently in the process of its realization. But from our own standpoints, is this truly possible politically, culturally, economically, and in various other aspects? Secondly, if Japan-Korea Unity is concretized, can both Japanese and Koreans exist in a state of happiness? Regarding this, first, please let us hear Mr. In Chŏngsik's opinion.

In Chŏngsik - As with all problems, I believe that in considering this issue, we must make the happiness and prosperity of the Korean people the central standpoint. Secondly, I believe that the consideration of political problems is always constrained by objective reality. Therefore, I believe the attitude that political thinkers must adopt at each stage is to grasp reality coolly, affirm it, and seek the greatest possible happiness under this reality.
From this viewpoint, I have these thoughts regarding Japan-Korea Unity. <38>
First, I think that Japan-Korea Unity, seen from today's realistic conditions and regardless of Governor-General Minami's policies, is operating with a certain historical necessity. Therefore, I believe Governor-General Minami's policies merely stimulate and shorten the "process" of this necessity.
Now, I see three factors contributing to this necessity. The first factor stems from the geographical and historical relationship of Korea as a supply base (兵站基地) in the execution of the Japanese Empire's continental policy.
This reason, that Korea is a supply base, cannot but bring about, first, the vigorous development of metal and chemical industries. Also, such development requires the large-scale organization of a modern labor force, while simultaneously absorbing surplus rural population and urban unemployed masses, making a significant contribution to solving or alleviating the population problem. Second, industrial development promotes the trend of increasing the weight of Industrial Korea relative to Agricultural Korea, thereby alleviating the fundamental contradictions of Korea's agricultural problem. The previous agricultural policy heavily focused on rice cultivation is being improved, and the production of agricultural products for industrial raw materials like wool, hops, tubers, and hemp is being encouraged. This alleviates or resolves the contradictions of agriculture and the crisis of rural villages in various ways. Third, the fact that the industries required of Korea due to its status as a supply base are large-scale metal or chemical industries not only does not hinder the existence or development of Korea's existing small and medium-sized industries but rather serves as a spur, stimulating and developing the latter. Furthermore, Korean small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists have no reason to fear this point, just as seen in advanced countries.
Fourth, what is required along with industrial development must be a large-scale, organized, and modernly primary-educated labor force. The Government-General has already announced plans to increase the number of elementary schools with a capacity for 2 million children by Showa 22 (1947), along with the revision of the Education Ordinance. This, of course, reflects these circumstances and makes a huge foundational contribution to the cultural advancement of the Korean populace.
Fifth, due to its status as a supply base, Korea's position increasingly gains importance in continental policy. Therefore, it is necessary not only to secure the peace and security <39> of the Korean populace but also to have them actively cooperate with the Empire's national policy. In other words, making Koreans genuinely love the Japanese Empire becomes a necessary requirement from the perspective of national policy. To achieve this, while imposing duties equal to those of mainland Japanese politically, it will also be necessary to guarantee equal civic rights. Whatever political form emerges, I am confident that in various fields related to national duties and rights, such as elections, family registers, education, and customs duties, Japan and Korea will sooner or later become completely unified.
In short, all these trends will lead the Korean populace to a happier position economically, politically, and culturally.
From here arises the second catalyst for Japan-Korea Unity: namely, the populace's fervent desire for it. At the same time that the "courses" of nationalism and communism are completely crushed, I currently clearly perceive a tendency for the ideology of Japan-Korea Unity to overwhelmingly dominate Korea's intellectual world in the nearest future. Also, as these various happier trends progress, the degree of the populace's trust in the Empire is increasingly strengthening, as we well know.
In other words, I am confident in its inevitability that the hand of Japan-Korea unification extended by the Empire will be firmly grasped by the hand of the Korean populace.
What further strengthens this confidence in inevitability is the domestic reformist force (革新勢力). As you know, the characteristic of reformism (革新主義) is being anti-communist and simultaneously anti-capitalist. Its ultimate ideal is to uphold only the Emperor and affirm discrimination and inequality only between oneself and the Emperor. The fundamental ideal of Japanism (日本主義) is to eradicate capitalist exploitation and the capitalist concept of colonies, and reconstruct a society based on co-existence and co-prosperity centered around the Imperial House. Because this reformist force is anti-capitalist and anti-exploitative, I see the inevitability of Japan-Korea Unity in the inevitability of the rise of this reformist force. Why? Because it allows for the complete transcendence of Korea as a colony, uniting the Korean nation and the Yamato nation into a single, more <40> advanced concept of a New Japanese Nation (新日本民族).
What should be noted here is that unification into a New Japanese Nation absolutely does not mean that Koreans must lose their entire ethnic distinctiveness. Korea's unique language, cultural traditions, national spirit, etc., must be preserved to the end as one aspect of the life of the newly formed New Japanese Nation and must also be developed. Those ignorant fellows who think that Japan-Korea Unity immediately means the abolition of the Korean language or the prohibition of Korean clothing are truly worthless human beings.
In short, I am confident in the inevitability of Japan-Korea Unity based on these three catalysts: the necessity of Korean industrialization from the standpoint of national policy, the inevitable desire of the populace, and the inevitability of the rise of the reformist force. Also, because it is inevitable, it is possible. Only that which possesses inevitability is possible.

Cha Chaejŏng - Thank you. Mr. In Chŏngsik spoke of the inevitability of Japan-Korea Unity from an economic perspective. But regarding the cultural aspect, especially in relation to the intellectual class, how should the idea of Japan-Korea Unity be viewed? Mr. Hyŏn Yŏngsŏp, please speak.

Hyŏn Yŏngsŏp - Regarding the feasibility of realizing Japan-Korea Unity, I am one who is confident in its possibility. Early this year, my small writing concerning Japan-Korea Unity incurred the displeasure of various members of the intellectual class, but whether due to my personality seeking purity or the influence of my previous ideology, it did not stem from impure motives.
Before delving into the theory of Japan-Korea Unity, I first think, isn't it necessary for us to resonate with Japan's ideals? I believe even capitalist Britain conquered the world by propagating ideals of freedom or respect for the individual, that is, democracy, to the world. Just as one should not view Britain's world policy solely through politics and economics but also see the ideals Britain possessed, I intend to see Japan's ideals and support them. The fact that Gandhi, Gandhi's successor Nehru, or Tagore are currently siding with Britain means British ideals have won over the Indian people. Nehru has interest in the ideals of Britain's Labour Party or socialist parties and shows a tendency to cooperate with them.
I read the Meiji Emperor's poems (御製) and was deeply <41> moved. I believe that where the people think of the Emperor as a parent, and the Emperor thinks of the people as children, we can build the ideal utopia of freedom we once dreamed of. That is, I believe we can build an ideal society of co-existence and shared joy centered on the Emperor. That is, by confirming Japan's ideal of "Eight Cords, One Roof" (八紘一宇), the ideal of universal love, and contributing our everything to the Japanese state, can we not obtain all freedoms? I dream of the freedom and independence that can be enjoyed by becoming influential members of the Japanese state and a superior people of the world, and I am confident in the realization of that ideal. I believe the ideal of Japan-Korea Unity will be realized by realizing the ideals Japan possesses, and by us being supporters of those ideals. Therefore, in settling our past, we must be pure and untainted, and a religious attitude of offering everything, not a compromising attitude, is necessary. Even the Jews, with their fervent national sentiments, abandoned their national languages in various countries and manifested their individuality. I think we do not have much individuality to defend to the death, and also, as a result of adhering too strongly to individuality, we must not obstruct the ideal of Japan-Korea Unity, so I think fusion and unity, even down to language and customs, are necessary.
Since Korea lacks Shinto experience, I think it is necessary for us to gain Shinto experience henceforth and strive for the spiritual deepening of Japan-Korea Unity.
Though brief, from the viewpoints above, that is, by grasping Japan from an idealistic standpoint and emphasizing the realization of its ideals, I believe the ideal of Japan-Korea Unity will be realized where we adopt a self-sacrificing stance.

Cha Chaejŏng - Regarding Japan-Korea Unity and the culture problem, more specifically, concerning the question of whether Japan-Korea Unity might become an obstacle to Korean culture, it would be good if Mr. Yi Kwangsu could speak a bit.

Yi Kwangsu - While in prison, I thought about this. Japan-Korea Unity has been a fact since the time of the Japan-Korea Annexation, but I think the responsibility for making a fuss about Japan-Korea Unity anew now lies with both sides, Japanese and Korean. That is, on the Korean side, there was the obstinacy and <42> national consciousness of not being Japanese internally, even though legally Japanese citizens. On the mainland Japanese side, ignoring the Imperial Will of "Impartial Benevolence" (一視同仁) and treating Korea and Koreans as a colony is the responsibility for now bringing up Japan-Korea Unity anew, I think.
In prison, people asked various things about the war. At that time, I said this. If I became God and needed to choose only one nation among the various peoples of the East as the leader, I thought I would, from an impartial stance, elect the Japanese nation to that position. No matter how you look at it, the Chinese nation is a somewhat inferior nation. This is a nation that possesses the karma (因果) to be defeated. When thinking about the superiority of the Japanese nation, I thought that the happiness of Koreans also culminates in becoming completely Japanese.
So, I resolved from my conscience to discard the stubborn identity of being Korean and become Japanese, possessing the Japanese spirit. However, obtaining this Japanese national sentiment was by no means an easy task. At first, after leaving prison, I tried to cultivate this sentiment starting with raising the national flag. Initially, my conscience and the act of raising the flag were somehow not completely unified. The old obstinacy of raising it reluctantly because I was told to was still alive, I discovered.
However, while raising it many times, I gradually felt a national sentiment welling up from my conscience. Now, not only with the national flag but also in shrine worship and all other matters, I have become able to act with feelings truly close to the Japanese spirit. In short, in my view, the path to Japan-Korea Unity lies solely in training daily actions to thoroughly cultivate this national sentiment.
And returning to the question asked, if Japan-Korea Unity were to result in the obliteration of Korean culture, I think that would be a very unfortunate thing.
Some quarters clamor about abolishing the Korean language, but I worry whether such a policy might actually worsen Korean sentiments and produce the opposite effect. I think Korea's language, culture, etc., must be preserved to the very end. While preserving Korea's culture <43> and language to the end, I believe Koreans can become Japanese subjects who love Japan sincerely from the heart and revere His Majesty the Emperor sincerely as their own "King" (임검 - archaic Korean for king/ruler). Also, I believe that only doing this is the true path of Japan-Korea Unity.
Also, because I believe in causality (因果論), I believe in Japan-Korea Unity. The Japan-Korea Annexation is also explained by causality. A nation that is annexed has the karma deserving annexation, and the nation that annexed also had the karma deserving annexation. I think Japan-Korea Unity also possesses such causality.

Yi Kakkchong - The realization of Japan-Korea Unity, in a word, ultimately lies in the abolition of the Governor-General's rule. With this as a premise, all institutions starting from the Constitution will be applied to Korea exactly the same as in mainland Japan. And regarding Korean administration as a regional system, if things like a Kyushu Agency, Shikoku Agency, Tohoku Agency, Hokkaido Agency are realized in the future of reformed Japan, perhaps such an administrative structure might arise in Korea as well.
In short, not treating it as a colony becomes the premise and content of Japan-Korea Unity. So-called colonial politics is a mistaken imitation of recent Western thought; such things are not permitted by the true Japanese spirit. Some suspect that Japan-Korea Unity might mean the obliteration of Korean culture, but that is not so. At a time when even buried Lelang culture or Silla culture is being unearthed and utilized in modern national life, I believe that rejecting Korean culture simply because it is Korean is not the path Japan should take.
Also, regarding the special skills of Koreans, I have had several discussions with Governor-General Minami, and Governor-General Minami affirmed that the characteristics of Koreans would be respected to the very end.
I think it is important that we not stop at just being Japanese subjects, but cultivate a sense of fellowship (同胞感) as siblings (赤子) under the same Emperor, between the 70 million and the 20 million. That is, not only sharing the national ideals, living conditions, and interests, but also sharing the fraternal emotions of joy, anger, sorrow, and pleasure on the foundation of shared blood and cultural origins. Only when this becomes the core can we talk about the construction of the future New East Asia. <44>

Discussion on Domestic Reform Problems

Cha Chaejŏng - From everyone's words – Mr. In Chŏngsik's economic interpretation, Mr. Yi Kakkchong's political view, and Mr. Hyŏn Yŏngsŏp's and Mr. Yi Kwangsu's cultural interpretations – we can grasp the main substance.
To summarize what everyone has said, what Mr. In Chŏngsik mentioned seems to be the fundamental principle of the inevitability of Japan-Korea Unity. And the cultural issues, the issue of national sentiment, etc., mentioned by others, are all thought to be important conditions for achieving this fundamental principle.
Let us conclude the Japan-Korea Unity issue here and move on to the problem of Reformism (革新主義). First, it would be good if Mr. Cho Pyŏngok could speak.

Cho Pyŏngok - From the perspective of Japan's national polity (國體) concept, striving for internal political reform centered on the Imperial House is most inevitable and can also produce significant results for national happiness. In Japanese history, there was the Taika Reform where the assets of some privileged classes were returned to the Imperial House and then redistributed to the people by the Imperial House. Also, looking at the Meiji Restoration, illuminated by Japan's unique spirit, I am confident that reform will surely succeed. Therefore, we can have great expectations for the Japan of tomorrow in all matters.

Cha Chaejŏng - Next, please let us hear Mr. Chu Yohan's words.

Chu Yohan - Whatever ideology it may be, if there is truth in that ideology, no matter what opposition it receives, it will eventually become the guiding principle for the world. For example, the revolutionary tide of liberty and equality from the time of the French Revolution still guides humanity in Europe today. And as Koreans, putting aside all books concerning various issues, in this time of emergency, we must stand from the position that Japan must achieve victory and devote our full efforts with utmost sincerity (赤誠) behind the lines (銃後) for the certain victory of the Imperial Army.

What Shall We Do?

Yun Hyŏngsik - Since I haven't conducted deep research yet, I cannot speak definitively, but in short, without clearly seeing the future trends or developments <45> of Japanese political thought, I think it is still difficult to speak clearly about Reformism. Seeing all the recent movements in domestic and international affairs, I am convinced that we must discard everything from the past and grasp some new guiding principle.

Kwŏn Chungil - I believe Japan-Korea Unity can only be realized through Reform (革新). Therefore, Reform and Japan-Korea Unity have an inseparable relationship. Not only that, but for the future of Reformed Japan, we must also self-sacrificingly serve (奉任) for Japan's victory. When I went to Tokyo for the National Conference for Responding to the Current Situation some time ago, I spoke about this issue, and the attention of activists in the mainland is also focused on this point. I emphasized that especially at times like this, through Reformism, Koreans could discuss everything on a completely equal level with mainland Japanese.

Cha Chaejŏng - From the principles of social science, can terms like 'Naeseon Ilche' or 'Reformism' be permissible? In other words, can they be established as concepts? I hope Mr. Cho Tuwŏn will speak.

Cho Tuwŏn - I attended the Communist University in Russia, and because the foundations of scholarship and the handling of concepts are entirely different, it is difficult for me to say anything specific. In other words, it is difficult to judge whether those concepts would be suitable for the actual situation in Korea. However, what I always think is that we must always establish a new guiding principle adapted to the times, based on the national particularities of Korea or mainland Japan.

Cha Chaejŏng - In short, Reformism is simultaneously anti-communist and anti-capitalist. Because it is anti-capitalist, it ideals a society centered solely on the Emperor and opposes all capitalist exploitation, harsh labor for profit, and also colonial exploitation and oppression. Therefore, my conclusion is that Korea's happiness is only possible through Japan-Korea Unity based on Reformism.
There is still the East Asian Cooperative Body, etc., remaining, but because time is limited to 9 PM, we cannot stay longer. I think the harvest from this meeting has been very great. I hope there will be another fervent discussion at the next opportunity, and with this, I declare the meeting closed.

An Chun - Thank you very much, everyone. <46>

**Summary**

This 1939 roundtable discussion features prominent Korean intellectuals and former activists debating the concept of "Naeseon Ilche" (Japan-Korea Unity) and "Domestic Reform" (革新). Participants like In Chŏngsik argue for the economic and historical inevitability of assimilation due to Korea's role as a supply base for Japan's continental expansion, predicting cultural and political convergence leading to greater Korean prosperity within the Empire. Others like Hyŏn Yŏngsŏp and Yi Kwangsu emphasize embracing Japanese ideals (Emperor worship, 八紘一宇) and the Japanese spirit, even suggesting cultural assimilation in language and customs, while Yi Kwangsu hopes core Korean culture can persist. Reformism is presented as an anti-capitalist, anti-communist, Emperor-centered ideology necessary for achieving true equality and eliminating colonial exploitation within the unified structure. The overall tone strongly supports collaboration and assimilation into the Japanese Empire as the only viable path for Koreans during wartime. Historians would find this article highly valuable as a primary source documenting the collaborationist ideology articulated by influential Korean figures during the peak of Japanese imperialism. It reveals their justifications for assimilation, blending economic determinism, idealistic interpretations of Japanese nationalism, personal conversion narratives, and critiques of Western colonial models, offering insight into the complex intellectual landscape of colonial Korea under wartime mobilization.

內鮮一體의 具現化問題

安浚 - 時局이 바로 戰時下에 있어 우리들의 앞에는 토의할 문제도 많고 또 우리가 銃後國民의 입장에서 하여할 事項도 많은 이때에 이런 자리를 가지게 되야 內外諸般問題를 말슴하게 되였으니 감사하게 생각함니다. 이제부터 車載貞氏가 進行係가 되여 말슴을 진행케 하겠읍니다.
車載貞 - 그러면 지금부터 시작하겠읍니다. 이 一堂에 모힌 여러분은 모도다 前日 사회운동이나 民族운동의 진두에 서서 多年活躍하시든 분이매 스사로 깊은 연구와 철저한 태도로써 이 자리에 오신 줄 암니다. 그러느니만치 아조 眞摯한 태도로 胸襟을 터러 이 非常時局에 처한 我等 朝鮮民衆의 진로에 대하여 의견을 開陣하여 주시기를 바람니다. 말슴할 순서를 大槪  네 항목에 分했음니다. 즉
1. 內鮮一體論
2. 國內革新問題
3. 朝鮮人의 今後進路(우리는 今後 무엇을 할가)
4. 東亞協同體에 대한 我等의 임무
그런데 첬재 문제부터 말슴키로 하겠음니다. 南總督이 赴任하여 온 이래 內鮮一體論은 상당히 심각하게되어 현재 그의 具現化과정에 있는 바이지만 우리는 자기 입장에 서서 이것이 정치적으로 혹은 문화적 경제적 등 諸方面에 있어 과연 가능한 것이냐. 둘재는 內鮮一體가 구체화化되면 內鮮人이 모다 행복된 지위에 있을 수가 있는가를 말슴하여 주시요. 이에 관해서 爲先 印貞植氏의 의견을 말해 주십시요.
印貞植 - 나는 모든 문제에 있어서와 마천가지로 이 문제의 고찰에 있어서도 朝鮮人의 행복과 繁榮을 위한다는 것을 중심적인 立脚點으로 삼지 않으면 안된다고 생각함니다. 또 둘재로는 정치문제의 고찰이란 것은 每樣 객관적 현실에 의해서 제약된다는 것 따라서 현실을 냉정히 把握하고 또 긍정하고 이 현실 밑에서 가능한 최대의 행복을 구하려는 것이 정치적 思想人의 各個의 階段에 있어서 取하지 않으면 안되는 태도라고 믿슴니다.
이러한 見地에서 나는 內鮮一體에 대해서 이러한 생각을 가지고 있음니다. <38>
첬재로 內鮮一體란 것은 今日의 현실적 조건으로 보아 南總督의 정책 여하를 떠나서 한 個의 역사적인 필연성을 가지고 작용하고 있다고 생각함니다. 따라서 南總督의 정책이란 것은 다만 이 필연성의 「푸로세스」를 刺戟하고 短縮하는데 불과한다고 생각함니다.
그런데 나는 이 필연성의 요인을 세 가지로 봄니다. 첬재의 요인은 日本帝國의 대륙정책의 수행에 있어서 朝鮮이 점한 바 兵站基地로서의 지리적 역사적 관계에서 출발함니다.
朝鮮이 兵站基地란 이 이유는 첬재로 금속공업 화학공업 등의 왕성한 발전을 齎來하지 않을 수 없음니다. 또 이러한 발전은 근대적인 노동력의 대량적인 조직화를 요청하는 동시에 농촌의 過乘人口, 도시의 失業群衆을 흡수하야 인구문제의 해결 혹은 緩和에 多大한 공헌을 하고 있음니다. 둘재로 공업의 발전은 農業朝鮮에 대한 工業朝鮮의 비중을 무겁게하는 경향을 促成하야 朝鮮의 농업문제의 기본적 모순을 緩和함니다. 從來의 米穀偏重의 농업정책이 개량되여 羊毛, 홋부, 芋類 麻 등 工業原料用 농산물의 생산이 獎勵되고 있는데 이것은 농업의 모순과 농촌의 위기를 여러 가지 방면으로 緩和 내지 해결하고 있음니다. 셋재로 兵站基地란 이유에서 朝鮮에 요구되는 공업이 대규모의 금속공업 혹은 화학공업이란 것은 從來의 朝鮮의 중소공업의 존립 내지 발전에 대한 장해가 되지 않을 뿐 아니라 도리혀 後者를 자극하고 발전케 하는 拍車가 되고 있음니다. 더욱이 朝鮮人의 中小商工業者들은 이 점에 있어서 先進諸國에서 보는 바와 맛찬가지의 恐怖를 가질 이유가 없음니다.
넷재로 공업발전에 따라서 요구되는 것은 대량적인 조직화된 또 근대적으로 초등교육을 받은 노동력이 아니면 안될 것이다. 總督府에서는 이미 敎育令의 改正과 아울러 昭和 22년도까지에 있어서 200만 兒童의 收容力을 가진 小學校의 增設計畫을 발표하고 있는데 이것은 물론 이러한 事情을 반영하는 것으로 朝鮮民衆의 문화향상을 위해서 막대한 기초적 공헌이 됨니다.
다섯재로 兵站基地란 이유에서 朝鮮의 지위가 大陸政策上 더욱 더욱 중요성을 加하게 됨니다. 따라서 朝鮮民衆의 <39> 治安을 確保할 뿐 아니라 그들로 하여금 帝國의 국책에 적극적으로 협동케하지 않으면 안될 것임니다. 다시 말하면 朝鮮사람으로 하여금 日本帝國을 진심으로 사랑케 하지 않으면 안될 것이 國策上의 필연적 요구가 될 것임니다. 이러하기 위해서는 정치적으로 內地人과 동등한 의무를 부담케하는 동시에 또 公民的 권리에 있어서도 동등한 것을 보장하여야 할 것임니다. 정치형태에 있어서는 어떤 것이 출현하던지 左右間 選擧間題 戶籍問題 敎育問題 關稅問題 등 국민적 의무 及 권리에 관한 각 분야에 있어서 內鮮이 조만간 완전히 一元化 될 것이라고 確信함니다.
요컨대 이러한 모든 경향은 朝鮮民衆으로 하여금 경제적으로 정치적으로 또 문화적으로 보담 행복된 지위에 達하게 할 것임니다.
여기에서 內鮮一體의 第2의 계기가 출발함니다. 즉 民衆의 이에 대한 熱烈한 要望이 생기게 됨니다. 民族主義와 共産主義의 「코-스」가 완전히 분쇄되는 동시에 內鮮一體의 사상이 가장 갓가운 장래에 있어서 朝鮮의 思想界를 壓到的으로 지배하게 되리라는 것을 저는 지금 傾向的으로 명백히 看取하고 있음니다. 또 이상의 여러 가지 보담 행복된 경향의 진행에 따라서 帝國에 대한 민중의 신뢰의 度가 더욱 더욱 강화되고 있는 것은 우리가 잘 알고 있는 바 임니다.
다시 말하면 帝國이 삐치는 內鮮一體化의 손은 朝鮮民衆의 손에서 굿게 잡혀지리라는 것을 저는 그 필연성에 있어서 確信하고 있음니다.
이 필연성의 確信을 더욱 굿게하는 것은 국내의 革新勢力임니다. 아시는 바와 같이 革新主義의 특징은 反共産임과 동시에 反資本的임니다. 그의 궁극적인 이상은 오직 天皇만을 推戴하고 天皇과의 사이에서만 차별과 불평등을 긍정하자는 것임니다. 資本家的 搾取와 資本家的 植民地 觀念을 근절하고 共存共榮을 기조로 하는 사회를 皇室中心으로 재건하자는 것이 日本主義의 根本理想임니다. 이처럼 革新勢力은 反資本的이고 또 反搾取的이기 때문에 이 革新勢力의 擡頭의 필연성에 있어서 內鮮一體의 필연성을 보는 것임니다. 웨 그러냐하면 殖民地로서의 朝鮮을 완전히 止揚하고 朝鮮民族과 大和民族을 합하야 한 개의 보담 <40> 高級의 개념을 가진 新日本民族에로 통일할 수가 있기 때문임니다.
여기에 주의할 것은 新日本民族에로 통일된다는 것은 결코 朝鮮人이 그의 民族的인 고유성전반을 상실하야 한다는 것은 절대로 안임니다. 조선民族의 고유한 언어 문화전통 民族정신 등 이러한 것은 새로히 형성되는 新日本民族의 생활의 일부면으로서 끝까지 보존되고 또 발달되야 할 것임니다. 내선일체라 하면 곧 조선어의 폐지 조선衣服의 禁用 등을 의미하는 것으로 생각하는 그런 무지한 徒輩야 말로 가이없는 인간들임니다.
要컨대 나는 이러한 세 가지 契機 즉 국책의 見地에서의 朝鮮工業化의 必然性 民衆의 필연적 要望 革新勢力의 대두의 필연성에서 內鮮一體의 필연성을 確信함니다. 또 필연적인 까닭에 그것은 가능함니다. 필연성을 가진 것만이 가능함니다.
車載貞 - 고맙슴니다. 경제적 관점에서 印貞植氏가 內鮮一體의 필연성을 말했읍니다. 그런데 文化方面에 있어서 더구나 知識階級과의 관련에 있어서 內鮮一體의 사상을 어떻게 보아야 할 것임니까. 玄永變씨 말슴해 주십시요.
玄永變 - 內鮮一體의 실현가능성에 대하여는 저는 그 가능성을 確信하는 한 사람임니다. 今年初 內鮮一體에 관한 小著로 말미암아 知識階級 諸君의 불평을 삿스나 그러나 純粹性을 求하는 나의 성격 내지 例前思想의 영향인지 불순한 動機에서 나온 것은 아님니다.
나는 內鮮一體論에 드러가기 전에 爲先 우리들은 日本의 理想에 대하야 共鳴함이 필요하지 아니한가고 생각참니다. 資本主義의 英國도 자유라든가 개인의 존중 즉 民主主義의 이상을 세계에 선전함으로서 세계정복을 하였다고 나는 봄니다. 정치와 경제만으로 英國의 세계정책을 보지말고 英國이 가진 理想을 봄과 같이 日本의 理想을 보고 그것을 支持하랴고 함니다. 只今 깐디나 깐디의 후계자인 네-르 혹은 타고-르翁이 現在 英國便을 들고 있는 것은 英國의 理想이 印度人을 이긴 것임니다. 네-르는 英國의 노동당 내지 사회주의정당의 理想에 흥미를 갓고 그이들과 협동하랴는 경향이 있음니다.
나는 明治天皇의 御製을 拜誦하고 매우 거륵한 감격을 <41> 어덧슴니다. 百姓이 天皇을 어버이같이 생각하고 天皇께서 百姓을 아들같이 생각하시는 그곳에 우리들이 옛날에 꿈구든 자유의 理想鄕을 건설할 수 있다고 생각함니다. 즉 天皇中心의 共存共樂의 이상사회를 건설할 수 있다고 밋슴니다. 즉 日本의 八絃一字의 理想, 人類愛의 理想을 확신하야 우리들의 一切을 日本國家에 공헌함으로써 一切의 자유를 어들 수 있지 아니한가고 나는 생각함니다. 日本國家의 유력한 分子로서 세계의 우수한 국민이 되야 享有할 수 있는 자유와 독립을 夢想하고 그 理想의 실현을 確信함니다. 日本이 가지고 있는 理想을 실현함으로서 또 우리들은 그 理想支持者로서 內鮮一體의 理想은 실현된다고 생각함니다. 그런 까닭에 우리들은 과거의 청산에 있어서 純潔無汚하야야만 할 것이요 妥協的 態度가 아니라 一切을 밧치는 宗敎的 態度가 필요하다고 생각함니다. 저 民族的 感情이 熱烈한 猶太人도 각 국에서 자기民族語를 버리고 자기의 개성을 발휘하였음니다. 死守할 개성이 우리들에게는 만치 않다고 나는 생각함으로 또 개성을 넘우 堅守하는 결과 內鮮一體의 理想을 障害함이 있으면 아니됨으로 言語風俗에까지도, 融合一體가 필요하다고 생각함니다.
조선에는 神道的 경험이 없음으로 우리들은 신도적 경험을 今後함으로서 내선일체의 정신적 심화를 기도할 필요가 있다고 생각함니다.
