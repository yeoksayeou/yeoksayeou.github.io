**Body Text Translation**
Chronicle of the Glory and Decline of Seoul's 'Talented Men and Beautiful Women'
Yi Sŏ-gu
Pyŏn Hye-suk, [Samch'ŏlli's] Kim P’al-lyŏn

① Who are the Popular Men?

Kim P’al-lyŏn (Samch'ŏlli) - Whose era shall we call it now? Who is the 'Hero of Chang’an' (長安豪傑), monopolizing popularity in Seoul? A hero, I say. If the word 'hero'<106> has negative connotations (語弊), we could call them 'talented scholars' (才士)...
Yi Sŏ-gu - It's the era of Yi Chong-man (李鍾萬), of course.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - There's hardly a day when the newspapers don't talk about Yi Chong-man, and there isn't a drawing-room or main hall where the story of Chang’an's millionaire Yi Chong-man isn't discussed. It truly is the Yi Chong-man Era now.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Did Yi Chong-man's popularity come from having money?
Yi Sŏ-gu - It seems it's not just any amount of money. According to mining experts (鑛山專門家) and various other people, the Changjin Gold Mine (長津金鑛) is expected to yield 3 million won annually. Since 60% is said to be net profit (純利益), isn't that 1.8 million won in net profit per year? The iron mine (鐵鑛) in P’yŏngbuk (平北) is also estimated to have reserves of 100 million tons and be valued at several tens of millions of won. For someone who was once dirt poor (赤貧如洗), hasn't he made quite a leap (一躍)? One could say there's Cecil Rhodes (쎄실卿), the diamond king of South Africa (南阿弗利加), in the West (later Prime Minister of Britain), and Yi Chong-man in the East.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - He's the successor to Yi Yong-ik (李容翊), isn't he? Having dug out the Kapsan and Changjin gold mines. And despite being such a magnate (巨富), he wears worn-out Western suits, rides the 5-chŏn tram, and sometimes appears in the poor neighborhoods (貧民窟) outside Tongdaemun (東大門), giving away 50 won or 100 won, without the slightest hint of seeking fame (賣名). He's truly a giant (巨人) like Chang P’al-ch’an from *Ah, Mujŏng* (噫無情). He has a somewhat mysterious air (面影), like the Count of Monte Cristo from *The Sea King* (海王星). That's why he naturally attracts the popularity of Chang’an.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Will Yi Chong-man's popularity continue?
Yi Sŏ-gu - I think so. Unless someone even greater emerges to overwhelm (壓倒) him, he'll likely dominate the scene.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who was it before Yi Chong-man?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That would be Pak Hŭng-sik (朴興植), wouldn't it? He opened the first department store, became the president of a company worth 5-6 million won as a young man, commands over a thousand plausible subordinates who are former county magistrates, former bank managers, or former police inspectors, and on top of that, he's handsome, so he's popular in the entertainment world (花柳界) too.
Yi Sŏ-gu - We talked about this at another gathering recently; Pak Hŭng-sik is the foremost industrialist (實業家) in Chosŏn. People like Min Tae-sik (閔大植) or Han Sang-nyong (韓相龍) are merely bankers who just protect their ancestral wealth (父祖 財産). But the lifeblood of a modern industrialist lies in skillfully gathering others' money and undertaking new, creative work. Seen from that perspective, Pak is certainly a remarkable figure (傑物). Though a young, burgeoning industrialist, he built Hwasin (和信) and other things... Although he lacks having spent money for the world yet.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - The 7-story Hwasin building at the Chongno (鍾路) intersection is the face of Pak Hŭng-sik. Therefore, as tens<107> of thousands of people pass by it daily, they think of Pak and talk about him, so Pak Hŭng-sik's popularity will continue as long as that building stands.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who was before Pak Hŭng-sik?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Before him, or perhaps after him, I'm not sure, but it might be Son Ki-jŏng (孫基禎). Recently, the newspapers made a fuss about his romance with a gymnastics teacher at Tongdŏk High Girls' School (同德高女), calling it a world-class romance. Perhaps it's the first since the romance of the British Duke of Windsor (英國윈자公)? Haha.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Well, I heard an interesting story about Son Ki-jŏng. When athletes from various countries gathered in Berlin (伯林) for marathon practice (豫習), he consistently came in first even during practice. So everyone said that if he placed first in the preliminaries (豫選), he would surely collapse during the actual competition. But guess what? In the competition, he won first place with a record-breaking time, and still had energy (긔운) left over to run a full lap around that wide stadium. They say even bull-like strongmen collapse upon reaching the finish line, but seeing this, the Westerners all said, "Son Ki-jŏn is not human."
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Will there be a day when Son Ki-jŏng dominates (制覇) the Olympics again?
Yi Sŏ-gu - If it had been held in Tokyo (東京) next year, who knows.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Now that he's getting married, won't his stamina decrease? Anyway, an ordinary person who used to eat kimchi and kkakdugi, then ate buttered toast, and traveled tens of thousands of ri by land and sea on ships and trains would surely collapse, but he's truly amazing.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Was the popular man before Son Ki-jŏng Pang Ŭng-mo (方應謨)?
Yi Sŏ-gu - That's right. Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak (崔昌學) and Pang Ŭng-mo. But Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak just lives as a rich man with a wife and concubines in a nice house. Trivial town gossip (市井消息) is heard, like him falling out of a car on the way to Paekch’ŏn Hot Springs (白川溫泉), but he hasn't undertaken any activities to capture the attention of society figures, so he only holds low-level popularity. He's out of the question (問題外). Compared to him, Pang Ŭng-mo was a hero of his time because he ran the *Chosŏn Ilbo* (朝鮮日報).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That's right. At a time when it seemed like it would instantly fall into the hands of swindlers or disappear like the Hwaryongdo affair (華龍道판), if he hadn't appeared holding a bundle of 500,000 won banknotes, that newspaper company might have vanished.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who was before Pang Ŭng-mo? Kim Ki-dŏk (金基德)?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Kim Ki-dŏk's popularity was also considerable for a time. With the emergence of Manchukuo (滿洲國), Najin (羅津) in Northern Chosŏn<108> became a major naval port, and he caused a stir as the 'king' who owned islands (島) and also possessed millions of p’yŏng of land worth tens or hundreds of won per p’yŏng. According to some investigation at the time, his assets were said to be 7 million won, or 3 million won.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That's right. Kim Ki-dŏk also seems to be quite a remarkable figure (傑物). I don't know if they are as big as Cheju Island (濟州島) or the Han River's (漢江) Yŏŭido (汝矣島), but imagine owning two islands! And until things worked out well in Najin, he was said to be one or two steps away from being the 'debt king' (負債王) in all of Chosŏn, owing 500,000 or 1 million won to the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行).
Yi Sŏ-gu - For a while, there were rumors he would take over (맛는단) the Tongil Bank (東一銀行). There were also rumors he would take over Paejae High School (培材高普). We'll have to wait and see about this person in the future.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who was before Kim Ki-dŏk?
Yi Sŏ-gu - It must have been Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨). Didn't people make quite a fuss, chanting "Yŏ Un-hyŏng, Yŏ Un-hyŏng"? When he went to officiate a wedding in some town in Hwanghae Province (黃海道), the church (禮拜堂) was reportedly packed like a human sea (人山人海) with people wanting to see Yŏ Un-hyŏng's face.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Speaking of weddings, they say he officiated 80 or 90 weddings a year. No wonder he earned the nickname 'Officiating President' (主禮社長) during his time as a newspaper president. Apparently, he took charge of such social duties even when he was in Shanghai (上海). Anyway, he's a bright and refreshing (明朗하고 爽快한) elder.
Yi Sŏ-gu - Always youthful – there are three 'eternal youths' (四時靑年): Yun Ch’i-ho (尹致昊), Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨), and Yŏ Un-hyŏng. Whenever you see these three, they are always aged youths.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who was before Yŏ Un-hyŏng?
Yi Sŏ-gu - It must have been Ch’oe Rin (崔麟). Leading the 1 million followers of Ch’ŏndogyo (天道敎), didn't he have quite the influence (聲勢) for a time? Like Song Pong-u's (宋奉瑀) description, he walked slowly as if mountains were moving (山이 움지겨가듯), took 5 minutes to stop when called, 5 minutes to turn around, and 5 minutes to answer. Because he was said to be so weighty, the followers hailed him as the second coming of Son Ŭi-am (孫義庵), and society paid close attention to his every move (一動一靜).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - He was also deeply involved in scandalous rumors (艶聞) due to the Mo-yŏ-sa incident (모여사사건).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Before Ch’oe Rin?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Then it becomes ancient history (太古史), but we could say there was the Sin Hŭng-u era, the Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) era, the Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙) era, the Chang Tŏk-su (張德秀) era, the Ch’oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善) era, the Son Pyŏng-hŭi (孫秉熙) era, and the Yun Ch’i-ho era. Ch’unwŏn (春園 - Yi Kwang-su's pen name) hasn't lost much popularity even today and is renowned as a literary figure. Kim Sŏng-su reigned like a prince during the heyday of the *Tong’a Ilbo* (東亞日報), his reputation (聲譽) covering the nation, and even now, as the president of Posŏng College (普專校長 - Pojŏn), his fame spreads far and wide (名振四海格). Chang Tŏk-su's prime (花形時節) was also during his *Tong’a Ilbo* days, but since returning from America (米國),<109> he hasn't appeared much on the glamorous stage. Sin Hŭng-u's good times were when he busily traveled (奔走) to America every year as some representative. Ch’oe Nam-sŏn had good times from his youth during the Sinmun’gwan (新文舘) and Kwangmunhoe (光文會) eras, and recently began to shine again as a professor at Kenkoku University (建國大學).
Son Pyŏng-hŭi - There's no need to elaborate further on giants (거물) like Son Pyŏng-hŭi and Yun Ch’i-ho.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Son Pyŏng-hŭi was also famous for unfolding quite an Oriental-style romance (東洋式 로-맨스) fitting the "hero loves beauty" (英雄好色) trope with the famous kisaeng (名妓) Chu San-wŏl (朱山月), wasn't he?
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Then, if we trace the lineage of popular men of each era in Chosŏn society over the last 20 years or so, it would look like this:
Son Pyŏng-hŭi (孫秉熙) Era
Yun Ch’i-ho (尹致昊) Era
Ch’oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善) Era
Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙) Era
Chang Tŏk-su (張德秀) Era
Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) Era
Ch’oe Rin (崔麟) Era
Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨) Era
Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨) Era
Kim Ki-dŏk (金基德) Era
Pang Ŭng-mo (方應謨) Era
Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak (崔昌學) Era
Son Ki-jŏng (孫基禎) Era
Pak Hŭng-sik (朴興植) Era
Yi Chong-man (李鍾萬) Era
So, the present is indeed the Yi Chong-man Era.
Yi Sŏ-gu - Generally, yes. Of course, there were many other figures who appeared, but generally, these individuals passed the baton of prominence among themselves, creating the history of the last 20 years.

② Who are the Popular Beautiful Women?

Kim P’al-lyŏn - Then, shall we now list the beautiful women (佳人), the females, in historical order? Let's start with Pak In-dŏk (朴仁德) as the starting point - *although there were figures like Kim Maria (金마리아) before, let's exclude them from discussion for a moment.* From Pak In-dŏk.
Yi Sŏ-gu - Pak In-dŏk's popularity came from being an orator (演說客). Her lively attitude, like a bachelor dressed in a skirt and blouse, and her sturdy physique might have been focal points attracting popularity, but anyway, when I first joined the *Tong’a Ilbo* as a reporter, Pak In-dŏk's popularity was astonishing. Fathers and mothers would say that if they were to have a daughter, they should have one like Pak In-dŏk.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - At that time, I was at a mission school (밋숀스쿨) in Yokohama, but there wasn't a Korean man or woman<110> who didn't know Pak In-dŏk's name, so it was quite magnificent (宏壯). Furthermore, with stories about her having a love affair, getting divorced, and whatnot, she gathered the popularity of the era all to herself, much like the New Women group of the Seitō-kai (靑踏會), such as Hiratsuka Raichō (平塚雷子) and Okumura Fusako (奧ムメオ).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - After Pak In-dŏk?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Kim Il-lyŏp (金一葉) and Kim Myŏng-sun (金明淳), perhaps. At that time, both wrote for *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) and newspapers. Sometimes, when there was a concert at the Youth Hall (靑年會館), the female poet (女詩人) Kim Il-lyŏp, wrapped head to toe in bright blue silk skirt and blouse (샛파란 비단치마 저고리), would float out ethereally to accompany on the piano. All the men and women of Chang’an were captivated (惱殺). Later, she published a magazine called *Yŏjage* (女子界), and contemporary poets and literati like Min U-bo (閔牛步) gathered around Il-lyŏp's love. She was someone who fully savored the atmosphere of this land's dawning period (黎明期) amidst the envy of all people.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Kim Il-lyŏp later wrote sijo poems too, and now I hear she has completely become a Buddhist nun and entered a temple deep in some mountain, chanting "Namu Amitabul."
Kim P’al-lyŏn - After Kim Il-lyŏp and Kim Myŏng-sun?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Yun Sim-dŏk (尹心悳), perhaps. When she abandoned her teaching position at Ewha Hakdang (梨花學堂) and appeared on the T’owŏrhoe (土月會) stage, Yun Sim-dŏk received much criticism, but on the other hand, new people highly regarded the spirit of this pioneer (先驅者) for her bold debut.
Yi Sŏ-gu - Yun Sim-dŏk - her beauty, her talent, and her frenzied state, unable to control her passion like the Greek poetess Sappho (希臘女詩人 삼포-) - she was a pitiful and regrettable person. If the times had been more favorable, she might have followed a more glamorous path like that of Itō Byakuren (伊藤白蓮). "Hymn of Death" (死의 讚美) and the love suicide (情死)! How much impact did that love suicide incident have on society at the time?
Kim P’al-lyŏn - And then who? After Yun Sim-dŏk?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Yi Wŏl-hwa (李月華). An intellectual who attended Ewha Hakdang but quit, she was perhaps the epitome of a woman full of affection and sorrow (多情多恨), like La Dame aux Camélias (椿姬). She was adept at hopping on and off trams (도비노리도비오리). Hopping on and off lightly like a butterfly (胡蝶).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That's right. Yi Wŏl-hwa was also a regrettable person. She went to Shanghai and worked as a dancer (딴사-). If her passion had been less intense, she might have spent her life as a girls' school teacher.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - After Yi Wŏl-hwa?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Kwŏn Ae-ra (權愛羅). Her hometown is Kaesŏng (開城), where Hwang Chin-i (黃眞伊) was born. While lecturing at the Seoul Youth Hall, she famously sang a verse of the "Kaesŏng Nanbong-ga" (開城難棒歌), asking, "Why discard Korean things? Is only Western culture civilized?" She was a type of women's movement activist (女流運動者). When walking around during the three hottest days of summer (三伏), she would wrap herself loosely in a hemp skirt and blouse (열새배 치마저고리).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Kwŏn Ae-ra was also famous. And then?<111>
Pyŏn Hye-suk - From then on, many women emerged, and romances and rumors poured out from every corner. The figures who formed an era at that time might be the Kŭn’uhoe (槿友會) faction. Chŏng Ch’il-sŏng (丁七星), Chŏng Chong-myŏng (鄭鍾鳴), Hwang Sin-dŏk (黃信德), Hŏ Chŏng-suk (許貞淑), Sim Ŭn-suk (沈恩淑), Kang Sŏk-cha (姜石子), Kang Agŭnnia (姜아근니아) - they were all formidable figures (錚錚한 패들).
Yi Sŏ-gu - That's likely true. It was the golden age of women activists, and the trends of the time offered them stages and opportunities. After this wave of the Kŭn’uhoe faction's contending heroes (群雄割據的) swept through, the next figure to emerge was Sin Il-sŏn (申一仙). There probably hasn't been anyone in modern history who played the role of 'Chosŏn's sweetheart' like Sin Il-sŏn.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That's right. After the master director Na Un-gyu (羅雲奎) discovered her and made the movie *Arirang* (아리랑), Mt. Kŭmgang (金剛山) and Sin Il-sŏn became the famous attractions of Chosŏn. If there was a concert at the Ch’ŏndogyo Hall (天徒敎堂) or the Youth Hall, it was always a full house (滿員) if Sin Il-sŏn was performing. It's truly regrettable (可惜) that such a young lady later became a kisaeng, and rumors now say she treads a different path, trampling on her past fame.
Yi Sŏ-gu - This isn't just about Sin Il-sŏn; generally, women can maintain their fame through beauty and graceful appearance when young, but as they age, it's impossible to maintain popularity without the power of cultivation (教養). But Sin Il-sŏn attended Tongdŏk High Girls' School (同德高女) or somewhere similar but dropped out and didn't cultivate herself afterwards, so she had no choice but to decline. It's the fate (運命) of the vast majority of Chosŏn women.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - After Sin Il-sŏn?
Yi Sŏ-gu - Another era of turmoil (混難時代), an era of contending heroes (群雄割據時代), arrived. At that time, new theatre (新劇) also flourished in Chosŏn, and with movies – especially the advent of records (레코-드) – many figures emerged. First, Ms. Pyŏn Hye-suk sitting here. This lady, whose father was a pastor, attended the Yokohama Mission School, jumped into the T’owŏrhoe theater troupe (土月會劇團) of Tokyo exchange students, began treading the stage as an actress, and her activities (行狀) were diverse. And from records, figures like Sŏnu Il-sŏn (鮮于一扇), Wang Su-bok (王壽福), and many others poured out.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Then, appearing like a comet (慧星的) to overwhelm the world once again was Ch’oe Sŭng-hŭi (崔承喜), I believe.
Yi Sŏ-gu - That's right. From the Pak In-dŏk era to the present, the person who appeared receiving more applause than anyone else was Ch’oe Sŭng-hŭi.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - When she returns to Chosŏn after touring London (倫敦) and Paris (巴里), she'll surely make a name for herself once again.<112>
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Then whose era is it now?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰), perhaps.
Yi Sŏ-gu - Starting from the movies *The Wanderer* (나그내) and *Ch’unhyangjŏn* (춘향전), it's correct to see Mun Ye-bong reigning decisively as the queen. Han Ŭn-jin (韓銀珍) is said to be good too, but she hasn't appeared on screen yet, so she's an unknown quantity (未知數).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Whatever anyone says, after Ch’oe Sŭng-hŭi, it must be Mun Ye-bong. I like her Oriental, willow-leaf type (東洋的 버들입 같흔 타입).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Then, based on what you've said, listing the popular women of successive eras in order would look like this:
Pak In-dŏk (朴仁德) Era
Kim Il-lyŏp (金一葉), Kim Myŏng-sun (金明淳) Era
Yun Sim-dŏk (尹心悳) Era
Yi Wŏl-hwa (李月華) Era
Kwŏn Ae-ra (權愛羅) Era
Chŏng Ch’il-sŏng (丁七星), Chŏng Chong-myŏng (鄭鍾鳴) Era
Sin Il-sŏn (申一仙) Era
Sŏnu Il-sŏn (鮮于一扇), Wang Su-bok (王壽福), Pyŏn Hye-suk (卜惠淑) Era
Ch’oe Sŭng-hŭi (崔承喜) Era
Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰) Era

③ Seoul's Famous Kisaeng

Kim P’al-lyŏn - Who were Seoul's famous kisaeng (名妓)? Let's start first with Pae Chŏng-ja (裵貞子). Regarding Pae Chŏng-ja, the world generally assumes... how about the next person?
Yi Sŏ-gu - After Pae Chŏng-ja, they say it was Haedanghwa (海棠花). She was a P’yŏngyang kisaeng who was quite famous during the Kyŏngsŏng Exposition (共進會) in Taishō 4 (1915).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - And there was also a famous kisaeng named Chebyŏng (제병). That's a nickname; there was a type of expensive Chinese silk called 'chebyŏng' (제병). They say this kisaeng lived so extravagantly – wrapping herself head to toe in this chebyŏng silk, and even her bedding and the cushions spread on the floor were all made of silk – that's why she was called Chebyŏng. Recently, I heard she went to find some evangelist (傳士) who is practicing asceticism (修道) in Mt. Kŭmgang.
Yi Sŏ-gu - And then, it must be "Tadong Ch’ongch’ae" (茶洞총채).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - What does "Ch’ongch’ae" mean?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - "Tadong" is because she lived in Tabang-gol (茶房골 - Teahouse Alley) in Seoul. "Ch’ongch’ae" (whisk/duster) refers to how talkative and chatty (수다스러운지) that woman (녀편네) was, so they called her that.
Yi Sŏ-gu - In other words, Tadong Ch’ongch’ae was said to socialize (交際) very well with figures from high society (上流社會).<113> That's how she became famous. After Ch’ongch’ae would be the famous Chu San-wŏl (朱山月).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Didn't Chu San-wŏl become famous because she became the concubine (愛妾) of Son Pyŏng-hŭi?
Yi Sŏ-gu - No. She reigned over Chang’an as the top kisaeng (名妓) of her time. Because Son Ŭi-am (孫義庵 - Son Pyŏng-hŭi's courtesy name) took the number one kisaeng, she became even more famous.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - And then?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - After her? Paek Un-sŏn (白雲仙), perhaps. Someone gave her a good name, Paek Un-sŏn (White Cloud Immortal). This person sang well, had outstanding looks, and made the heroic men (長安豪俠男兒) of Chang’an weep quite a bit for a while. She played the kŏmun'go (거문고) as well as Zhuo Wenjun (卓文君).
Yi Sŏ-gu - I've heard middle-aged and elderly men sighing in their drawing rooms (舍廊), still unable to forget Paek Un-sŏn. After Paek Un-sŏn came Hyŏn Kye-ok (玄桂玉), Chŏng Kŭm-juk (丁琴竹), and Kang Hyang-nan (姜香蘭).
That time was right around the 8th year of Taishō (1919). Hyŏn Kye-ok ran off to Shanghai with a man named Hyŏn. Chŏng Kŭm-juk changed her name to Chŏng Ch’il-sŏng (丁七星) and became a women's activist. Kang Hyang-nan also changed her name to Kang Sŏk-cha (姜石子), cut off her silk-like hair, became Chosŏn's first bobbed-hair girl (斷髮娘), and threw herself into the women's movement. It was truly a glamorous era.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - That's right. That period was probably the most stylish, when women's popularity peaked. It could be called a time when women, especially kisaeng, broke free from being treated like playthings and awakened to self-awareness, like Nora leaving the doll's house.
Yi Sŏ-gu - After this special type of kisaeng swept through, the next to emerge was O So-hong (吳素紅). She studied in Tokyo, returned, and worked as a kisaeng. She paid tuition fees (學費) for needy students (苦學生) and seemed like a woman with quite a compassionate heart. I hear she's now in Shanghai, running a dance hall (딴스홀) or a bar (빠-).
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Next would be Kang Myŏng-hwa (康明花). Just as she was a woman who drank poison (독약마시는 여지), her temperament must have been quite strong, but hearing stories from her friends of that time, she was apparently a very passionate woman.
Yi Sŏ-gu - With her beautiful face and passionate nature, plus committing love suicide with Chang Pyŏng-ch’ŏn (張炳天), the son of a millionaire (백만장자), her name became even more elevated. For a while, didn't the story of Kang Myŏng-hwa dominate the Seoul scene?
Kim P’al-lyŏn - That's right. Kang Myŏng-hwa and Chu San-wŏl were both women whose stories spread widely even to the countryside. And then?<114>

Kim P’al-lyŏn - Now, let's change the subject. Have you ever visited the residence (邸宅) of a famous millionaire or a very high-ranking person in Chang’an? Tell us about that.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - I haven't been to the homes of powerful and noble families (權門貴家), but one evening several years ago when I was a kisaeng, I was called to Namsan Chang (南山莊). It turned out to be the day Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak (崔昌學) agreed to sell the Samsŏng Gold Mine (三成金鑛) for 1.3 million won or some amount, and the contract was being finalized. At that time, a young man, supposedly a manager from a mining company in Tokyo and a university graduate, sat on one side, and Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak sat on the other, signing the contract. Even though he was a university graduate, the pen held in the young man's hand trembled (후둘 후둘 떨니더구만요) as he signed the contract worth over a million won. But Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak sat there calmly and quite dashingly scribbled his three-character name well. Others gossip that he's uneducated (無識), but when I saw him then, he seemed like a very bold person.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - That's an interesting story. Have you had lunch with a millionaire or visited their house?
Yi Sŏ-gu - I once visited the house of Im Chong-sang (林宗相) outside Tongdaemun. I remember it was during my time as a reporter for the *Tong’a Ilbo*. As I recall then, the residence was large, tall, and incredibly spacious, but all the furnishings (調度品) were not placed where they should be, making it seem a bit disorganized (엉성하더군요). Im Chong-sang - at that time, he was truly one of the few great millionaires.
Pyŏn Hye-suk - I also visited Marquis Pak Yŏng-hyo's (朴泳孝후작) house when I was very young. It was either the Marquis's granddaughter or some relative; she attended the same girls' school as me. I sometimes went over to play following her. The palace-like large house even had a rainbow bridge (虹橋), and it was overwhelmingly impressive (어머어마 하드군요).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Then let's change the topic once more. Who had the most extravagant wedding ceremony in Seoul?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Pan Pok-ch’ang (潘福昌), perhaps. He's always called Pan Ch’arang (潘次郞). He made a fortune of about 300,000 won dealing in rice and beans (米豆), and after becoming rich, his first major undertaking was building a large house and marrying a fine bride. So, he built a magnificent, palace-like (阿房宮) huge house on the Inch’ŏn (仁川) coast, and then chose a bride. He came up to Seoul and searched through all the girls' schools. From among them, he reportedly chose a young lady named Hu Tong (後童) attending Kyŏngsŏng Women's High School (京城女子高普). Who is this Hu Tong? She was the elder sister of the famous Wŏndong Tchaget'ŭ (苑洞 짜겟트 - Won-dong Jacket, likely a nickname). I saw her too; her face was just like a flower. Truly a beauty (美人).
Yi Sŏ-gu - That's right. I saw her too; she looked like a celestial maiden (선녀). That woman was the first in Chosŏn<116> to wear embroidery (수) on the hem of her skirt. She started it. Hu Tong also played the violin (바요링) well. At Pan Pok-ch’ang's wedding, many gentlemen (紳士) from Inch’ŏn flocked, it was held at the Chosŏn Hotel (朝鮮호텔), the Mayor of Inch’ŏn (仁川府尹) gave a congratulatory speech, and it was quite bustling (떠들석).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - And then?
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Sin Yong-uk's (愼容郁 - Yong-in renamed) wedding ceremony held in an airplane (비행기 우) was also extravagant. And when Ch’oe Ch’ang-hak had his wedding last year or so at the Kyŏngsŏng Hotel (京城호텔), Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl (朴榮喆) officiated, high officials and wealthy men (大官富豪) gave congratulatory speeches, and that too was a topic of conversation (話題꺼리) for a time.
Yi Sŏ-gu - I think Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙) had the most extravagant wedding. At that time, Pak Yŏng-hyo was the president, and Kim Sŏng-su was the owner (社主) of the *Tong’a Ilbo*. A wedding preparation committee was formed with various prominent figures from society, the ceremony was held at the Public Hall (公會堂), the venue was filled with flower wreaths (화환), bureau chiefs (各局長) from the Government-General (總督府) also attended, and hundreds of Chang’an notables (長安名士) were present. It was truly splendid (버젓하였지요).
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Now, the discussion is getting too long. Finally, could you two please name about ten families in Seoul who represent the happiest love match marriages (戀愛結婚)?
Yi Sŏ-gu - That's difficult. Unhappiness and turmoil often follow love match marriage families, so it's hard to name them. But let me try to list a few.
1. The family of Chang Tŏk-su (張德秀) and Pak Ŭn-hye (朴恩惠)
2. The family of Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙) and Yi Ae-ju (李愛主)
3. The family of Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰) and Im Sŏn-gyu (林仙圭)
4. Although from Tokyo, the family of modern Japanese company president Ma Hae-song (馬海松) and dancer Pak Oe-sŏn (朴外仙)
Pyŏn Hye-suk - Let me list a few families too.
1. Despite this and that, the family of Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) and Hŏ Yŏng-suk (許英肅)
2. The family of An Ki-yŏng (安基永) and Kim Hyŏn-sun (金顯順)
3. The family of Kye Chŏng-sik (桂貞植)
4. The family of Yu Ch’i-jin (柳致眞) and Sim Chae-sun (沈在淳). These are generally the ones.
Kim P’al-lyŏn - Alright, let's end it here then. Please receive many blessings in the New Year. (End)<117>

**Summary**

This 1939 roundtable discussion from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features Yi Sŏ-gu, Pyŏn Hye-suk, and Kim P’al-lyŏn chronicling the "rise and fall" of popular figures—"talented men" (才子) and "beautiful women" (佳人)—in Seoul (Chang’an) over the preceding twenty years. They trace the succession of popular men, starting from historical figures like Son Pyŏng-hŭi and Yun Ch’i-ho, moving through cultural and political leaders like Yi Kwang-su and Yŏ Un-hyŏng, industrialists Pak Hŭng-sik and Pang Ŭng-mo, athlete Son Ki-jŏng, and culminating in the then-current "Yi Chong-man Era," defined by immense mining wealth. Similarly, they chronicle popular women, beginning with early activists/intellectuals like Pak In-dŏk and Kim Il-lyŏp, artists like Yun Sim-dŏk, kisaeng-turned-activists like Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng, film star Sin Il-sŏn, dancer Ch’oe Sŭng-hŭi, and concluding with film star Mun Ye-bong as the reigning popular woman. The discussion also touches upon famous kisaeng, extravagant lifestyles and weddings of the rich, and speculates on happy love-match marriages. For historians, this article offers valuable insights into contemporary perceptions of fame, wealth, and influence in colonial Seoul. It reveals the shifting criteria for popularity—from traditional leadership and cultural renown to modern industrial wealth, athletic achievement, artistic performance, and female activism/stardom—and provides a snapshot of prominent personalities, social commentary on gender roles (especially the perceived link between female fame, beauty, and lack of 'cultivation'), and the mingling of traditional and modern values during the late colonial period.

卜惠淑 - 그러치요. 巨匠 羅雲奎가, 끄러내어 영화「아리랑」을 만든 뒤로는 朝鮮에 金剛山과 申一仙이 명물이 되었지요. 天徒敎堂이나 靑年會館에 음악회가 있으면 申一仙이 출연한다면 언제든 滿員이었지요. 그러든 색시가 어쩌면 妓生으로 또 지금 소문에는, 저 딴 길를 발버 옛날의 名聲을 즛밟고마다니 참 可惜한 일이여요.
李瑞求 - 비단 申一仙이에만 하는 말이 아니라 대체로 여자란 젊었을 때는 그저 미모와 아름다운 자태로 그 명성을 유지할 수 있지만은 그러나 나이 들면 그때는 교양의 힘이 아니고는 도저히 人氣를 유지해 갈 수가 없거든. 그런데 申一仙이야 同德高女인가 어덴가 다니다가 말고 그 뒤도 수양을 아니하니 전락할 밖에 별수 있어야지. 朝鮮 女子 大多數의 運命이야.
金八蓮 -申一仙이 다음에는.
李瑞求 - 또 한 번 混難時代 群雄割據時代가 왓지. 그때는 朝鮮에 新劇도 勃興하고 영화와- 더구나 레코-드가 티여나와 많은 인물이 쏘다저 나왔지요. 위선 여기 안즌 卜惠淑씨. 이 여사는 아버지가 목사요 저는 橫濱밋숀스쿨에 다니면서 東京留學生의 土月會劇團에 뛰여들어 女優로서 무대를 밥기 시작한 뒤 그 行狀이 多彩하였고 그러고 레코-드에는 鮮于一扇
王壽福 등등 수두룩하게 쏘다저 나왔지요.
卜惠淑 - 그러다가 다시 한번 慧星的으로 세상을 壓倒하면서 나타난 이가 저 崔承喜일걸요.
李瑞求 - 그러치 朴仁德時代로부터 현대에 이르기까지 누구보다도 더 만흔 갈채를 받으면서 등장한 이가 崔承喜였지요.
卜惠淑 - 이제 倫敦巴里를 휘-돌아 다시 조선에 오면 또 다시 한 번 일흠을 날닐걸.<112>
金八蓮 - 그러면 현대는 누구의 시대일가요.
卜惠淑 - 文藝峰일걸.
李瑞求 - 「나그내」, 「춘향전」의 영화에서부터 단연 文藝峰이 여왕으로 君臨하고 있다고 보아서 옳겠지. 韓銀珍도 조타고 하나 아직은 스크린에 올은 적 없어 未知數이고.
卜惠淑 - 누가 무에라든지 崔承喜 以後에는 文藝峰이겠지요. 그 동양적 버들입 같흔 타입이 조와요.
金八蓮 - 그러면 지금 말슴한 것으로 歷代의 人氣女性을 순서대로 처들면 이렇하겠구만.

③서울 名妓들은

金八蓮 - 서울 名妓는 누구일가. 맨 처음 출발을 裵貞子로부터 시작이하지. 裵貞子에 대해선 세상이 대개 짐작하기 그 다음 분으로요.
李瑞求 - 裵貞子 다음엔 海棠花라더구만. 平壤妓生인데 大正 4년 共進會 당철에 아조 일홈이 있었다데.
卜惠淑 - 그러고저 제병이란 妓生도 유명하였대요. 제병이란게, 벌명인데 청국비단에 아조갑*는 것으로 「제병」이란 가음이 있었대요. 이 妓生이 어떻게 호사를 하였는지 그 제병이란 비단으로 아래위를 칭칭 감고 이부자리도 방에 깐 방석도, 모다 비단으로만 하였대서 제병이라고 한다더군요. 요지간 드르니 金剛山에 드러가 修道하는 어느 傳士를 차저가 있다더군요.
李瑞求 - 그러고는 「茶洞총채」일걸요.
金八蓮 - 「총채」라니 무슨 뜻인가요.
卜惠淑 - 茶洞이란 것은 그가 서울서 茶房골에 살았기에 茶洞이요 총채라니 그 녀편네가 어떻게나 말을 잘하고 수다스러운지 그래서 그렇게 총제라 부첬다는구만.
李瑞求 - 말하자면 茶洞총채는 上流社會<113> 人士와 아조 交際를 잘 하였다는구만. 그런데서 유명하여젔지. 총채 다음이 저 유명한 朱山月이겠지.
金八蓮 - 朱山月이야 孫秉熙 그 분의 愛妾이 되었으니깐 유명하여지지 않었는가요.
李瑞求 - 아니지요. 그 당시 名妓로 長安 안을 눌넜지. 그러기에 孫義庵이 一等名妓를 떼여드려 갓기에 더 一層 유명하여젔지요.
金八蓮 - 그러고는.
卜惠淑 - 그 다음에는? 白雲仙일걸. 일홈을 누가 잘 지었지. 白雲仙이라고, 이 분은 소리 잘하고 인물이 뛰여나고 한동안 長安豪俠男兒를 꽤 울렀지. 거문고는 卓文君만치나 잘 하였고.
李 - 白雲仙이를 지금도 이저버리지 못해서 中老들이 각금 舍廊에서 탄식하는 소리를 드렀어. 白雲仙의 뒤가 玄桂玉, 丁琴竹, 姜香蘭이겟지.
그대가 바로 大正 8년 前後지. 玄桂玉이는 玄이란 사내따라 上海로 다라나섯고 丁琴竹은 丁七星이로서 일홈을 고치고서 女性運動者가 되엇고 姜香蘭도 姜石子라고 일홈을 고치고 삼단같은 머리를 잘나버리고서 朝鮮最初의 斷髮娘이 되어 女性運動에 뛰여들었지요. 참으로 호화로운 시철이었지요.
卜惠淑 - 그러치요. 그 한철이 아마 가장 멋지고 여자의 人氣가 가장 끌던 때일걸. 말하자면 여자가 더구나 妓生이 노리개같은 시절에서 뛰여나와 人形의 집을 나온 노라 같이 自覺하든 시절이라 할걸요.
李瑞求 - 이러한 특별한 타입의 妓生이 한바퀴-휘 지나간 뒤 그 다음에 등장한 이가 吳素紅이라고 있었지. 東京留學하고 도라와서 妓生 노릇하였는데 苦學生들 學費를 대여주고 퍽으나 감촌 뜻이 있든 여자 갓했어요. 지금도, 上海가서 딴스홀인가 빠-를 경영한다 하더군요.
卜惠淑 - 다음이 康明花일걸. 독약마시는 여지인 것만치 성질도 퍽 강하기도 하였겠으나 그 시절 동무들 이야기를 들으면 퍽으나 열정적 여자라더군요.
李瑞求 - 얼골곱고 열정적인데다가 백만장자의 아들 張炳天이와, 情死까지하고 났으니까 더욱 그 일홈이 높젖지요. 한동안은, 康明花 이야기가 서울판 안을 덮지 안었든가.
金八蓮 - 그러치. 康明花와 朱山月 이 둘이 모다 시골에까지 널니 소문을 낸 여성이었지. 그러고는?<114>
金八蓮 - 이번에는 화제를 돌려서 長安에 유명한 백만장자라거나 아조 높은 사람의 邸宅을 구경한 일이 있어요. 그 이야기나 하구려.
卜惠淑 - 나는 權門貴家에 가 본적은 없지만 몇 해전 妓生으로 있을 적에 하로 저녁은 南山莊에서 불느기에 갓더니 그날이 바로 崔昌學이가 三成金鑛을 130만원엔가 얼마에 팔기로 되야 계약을 성립하든 날이더군요. 그때 東京서 鑛山會社 支配人이라고 하는 무슨 대학나온 청년이 한편에 안고 한쪽에는 崔昌學이 안저서 서로 계약서에 署名하는 광경을 보았는데 암만 대학 나온 이라도 그 청년은 백여만원짜리 계약서에 署名할 적에 손에 쥔 붓자루가 후둘 후둘 떨니더구만요. 그런데 崔昌學이는 태연하게 안저 제법 제 일홈 석자만은 흭흭 갈겨 잘 써넘기더군요. 남들은 無識하다고 숭을 보아도 난 그때 보니까 사람이 아조 대담하더군요.
金八蓮 - 그거 자미있는 이야기구만요. 또 백만장자와 점심을 같이 먹엇거나 그 집 구경한 일이 없어요.
李瑞求 - 동대문박 林宗相 집을 한번 차저가 본적 있었지. 아마 東亞日報 記者시대인줄 기억하는데 그때 내 기억으로는 邸宅은 크고 높고 굉장히 너르지만 모든 調度品이 놓일데 노여있지 안어서 좀 엉성하더군요. 林宗相-이라면 그때는 참으로 멫재 안가는 큰 부자엿지.
卜惠淑 - 나도 아조 어릴때에 朴泳孝후작 집으로 가본 일이 있어요. 후작의 손녀든지 일가든지요. 나와 같은 女學校에 다닌 일이 있엇지. 그애 따라 각금 놀너갓는데 宮闕같은 큰 집에 구름다리(虹橋)까지 하여있고 어머어마 하드군요.
金八蓮 - 그러면 한 번 話題를 돌려서 서울서 결혼식을 호화롭게 한 이가 누구일가.
卜惠淑 - 潘福昌일걸. 恒常 潘次郞이라고 하지요. 米豆을 하여 30만원인가 하는 부자가 되었는데 부자되고나서 첬 정치가 큰 집짓고 좋은 색시에 장가 든다는 것이 엇시요. 그래서 仁川海岸에다가 阿房宮같은 굉장한 큰 집을 짓고 그러고는 신부를 골났는데 서울 올너와서 各女學校를 죄-다 뒤젖거든. 그 중에서 京城女子高普에 다니는 後童이란 처녀를 골났다는구만. 이 後童이가 누군가 하니 저 유명한 苑洞 짜겟트의 바로 兄이엇지요. 나도 보았는데 얼골이 그냥 꽃이야요. 참말 미인이거든.
李瑞求 - 그러치. 나도 보았는데 선녀갓했어요. 그 여자가 朝鮮서 처음으로<116> 치마 끄트머리에 수를 노아 입었지. 그 여자가 시작이엇서. 後童이는 바요링도 잘했지. 潘福昌의 결혼식에는 仁川서 紳士多數가 몰려오고 朝鮮호텔에서 하였는데 仁川府尹이 祝辭도 하고 떠들석 하였지.
金八蓮 - 그러고는.
卜惠淑 - 愼容郁(容인을 改名)이가, 비행기 우에서 결혼식 한 것도 호화로웠고 崔昌學이 작년엔가, 京城호텔서 결혼식을 하였는데 朴榮喆이 주례하고 大官富豪들이 축사를 하고 그도 한때 話題꺼리였지요.
李瑞求 - 나는 호화로운 결혼식을 한 이로는 金性洙가 第一인 줄 알어. 그때는 朴泳孝가 사장이고 金性洙는 東亞日報의 社主로 있든 때인데 사회의 여러 명사로 결혼식준비위원을 내고 式은 公會堂에서 하였는데 場內가 화환으로 가득 차섯고 總督府로부터도 各局長들이 나왔었고 長安名士도 수백이 참석했고 참으로 버젖하였지요.
金八蓮 - 이제는 너무 話題가 길어지니 마즈막으로 두 분이 서을서 가장 행복스러운 戀愛結婚의 가정 열 家庭씩만 드러주서요.
李瑞求 - 그거 어렵구만. 戀愛結婚家庭에는 흔히 불행과 풍파가 따렀기에 지명키 곤란하구만. 그러나 몇 가지 드러볼가요.
1. 張德秀와 朴恩惠의 家庭
2. 金性洙와 李愛主의 家庭
3. 文藝峰과 林仙圭의 家庭
4. 東京 것이나 모던 일본사장 馬海松과 무용가 朴外仙의 家庭
卜惠淑 - 나도 몇 家庭 드러보지.
1. 이러쿵 저러쿵 하여도 李光洙와 許英肅의 家庭
2. 安基永이와 金顯順의 家庭
3. 桂貞植의 家庭
4. 柳致眞이와 沈在淳의 家庭. 두루 이러할 걸요.
