The Advance of the Korean People onto the Continent, The Phenomenon of Their Activities in Manchukuo
						Sin'gyŏng Hŭng Yangmyŏng

I. The Current Situation of Koreans in Manchuria

Brother Kim Tonghwan! Knowing well what you expect from me, it was certainly not my initial intention when picking up the pen to use your valuable pages to speak of other things to other people. Therefore, I trust you, who understand well this mental process of mine, will recognize that I am still not in a state to write any constructive piece. Furthermore, having been here for merely two months, and although I have set foot on this land three or four times in recent years, I have never ventured even a single step into the rural areas beyond the two cities of Xinjing and Harbin. Thus, I think it is surely presumptuous for me to discuss the lives of Korean agricultural immigrants in the agricultural state of Manchukuo. I fear it might become the kind of nonsense performed by the recently fashionable "China experts" made in two or three weeks, who go near the front lines, hear a few cannon shots, and then come back talking loudly about how China is, or how the future of the Incident (事變) looks. However, since I have already taken up the pen, I will now humbly attempt to summarize and glance over, in a "viewing flowers while riding a horse on paper" (紙上走馬看山格) manner, some aspects of the current situation of Koreans in Manchuria, using recent statistical literature. This is something I myself needed to know from before, and I believe there might be points that are generally thought to be known but are not well understood in concrete terms.

A. Total Number of Koreans in Manchuria

Regarding the question of how many Koreans generally live in Manchuria, it has long been said in Korea, "called one million" (呼曰百萬). However, the currently available statistics show a figure below 1 million due to difficulties in investigation arising from the frequent<87> movement of Koreans in Manchuria, their scattered presence in remote areas (僻地散在), and other reasons. But it goes without saying that this statistic differs significantly from the actual number. This point can also be understood from the fact that Staff Officer Katakura (片倉參謀) of the Kwantung Army announced the number of Koreans in Manchuria as 1.2 million at the Time Policy Investigation Committee (時局對策調査會) held in Kyŏngsŏng in early September this year. Seeing that Chientao Province accounts for about half, it is well known that Chientao* is practically an extension of Korea, a densely populated Korean area; about 80% of the total population of Chientao Province are Koreans. Even using this figure as a convenient baseline, Koreans occupy about 1/30th of the total Manchukuo population of approximately 30 million, showing that they are widely distributed, to the extent that one could say there is no place in the valleys (谷谷) of Manchukuo without Koreans. Considering such a rapid increase in recent years, if the Government-General's immigration policy continues unchanged, through Manchuria-Korea Development (滿鮮拓殖), adding the annual group immigration of 10,000 households (approx. 50,000 people) to the free immigration of no less than 30,000-40,000 people annually, and roughly calculating the natural growth rate of resident Koreans and incoming Koreans at 1% per year (年 1分), it can be sufficiently estimated that the population will increase to 2 million or 2.2-2.3 million after 10 years. Not just North China (北支) or Central China (中支), but the importance of the Korean issue in Manchukuo, the main destination for overseas Koreans, is destined to further increase. Since the abolition of extraterritoriality (治外法權撤廢) on December 1st last year, Koreans in Manchukuo, as important members among the "Five Races Under One Union" (五族協和), bear the duties of citizens of the Manchukuo Empire. At the same time, considerable effort can be observed from the Manchukuo government and the Concordia Association (協和會) to guide them towards becoming settled residents, leaving behind the notion of being mere immigrants, and cooperating in coexistence and co-prosperity with other ethnic groups having different cultures and customs. Of course, I also have my own opinions about the limits and strategies for the cultural and economic development of Koreans as an ethnic unit within the multi-ethnic state of Manchukuo, which advocates the Five Races Under One Union, but I will omit them this time. <89>

B. Livelihoods of Koreans in Manchuria

The absolute majority of livelihoods for Koreans in Manchuria is occupied by agriculture, with 154,10 households as of Showa 13 [1938], corresponding to about 80% of the total 192,897 households. Looking at this regionally, the order is Chientao, South Manchuria, and North Manchuria. Others include day laborers (日傭人) approx. 10,000 households, commerce approx. 7,000 households, government/public officials (官公吏) 2,300 households, bank/company employees 2,000 households, restaurant business 1,500 households, teachers (敎員) 1,300 households, other occupations approx. 5,000 households, and unemployed 3,000 households. Among the approximately 10,000 households of day laborers mentioned above, the majority are people employed by farming households during the busy agricultural season and engaged in farming. The approximately 7,000 households in commerce, second only to agriculture, are concentrated in cities such as Chientao, Harbin, Xinjing, Andong, and Fengtian, engaged in rice milling (精米業) and other small-scale commerce with small capital, a rather insignificant state. It is noteworthy that a considerable number of Koreans have become Manchukuo government and public officials. This is due to the fact that following the abolition of extraterritoriality on December 1st last year, 2,127 personnel related to the Government-General of Korea and Civil Association (民會) staff were directly transferred and appointed as Manchukuo officials. Also, during the administrative structure reform last July, about 25 mid-level Korean officials of Kan'nin (簡任 - Chokuninkan), Sōnin (奏任 - Senninkan), and Chokuninkan (勅任 - Kanninkan) rank were appointed. Following this, 6 more mid-level officials advanced from Korea in August. Adding those trained at the Great Harmony Academy (大同學院), Manchukuo's training institution for mid-level officials, and others appointed locally, plus the mid-level Koreans working as staff in the Concordia Association, which serves as the spiritual backbone of the Manchukuo Empire's formation, one can see that over 3,000 Koreans are working within the Manchukuo administrative structure. Among them, there are about 50 officials of Ch'ŏnim (薦任 - Sōnin) rank, and as Kan'nin (簡任 - Chokuninkan), there are five individuals: Ch'oe Namsŏn (Professor at Kenkoku University), Pak Sŏgyun (Director of Investigation Bureau, Foreign Affairs Office), Chin Hakmun (Councilor, Internal Affairs Bureau), Yi Pŏmik (Governor of Chientao Province), Yu Hongsun (Director of People's Welfare Bureau, Chientao Province). Additionally, there are dozens of individuals in important positions at the Section Chief (科長) level, including Yun Sangp'il (Chief of the 2nd Guidance Section, Development Bureau), who are showing great vigor (氣熖), which is a joyous matter. In Xinjing alone, nearly 10,000 Koreans reside, among whom pale-faced intellectuals (蒼白한 인테리) such as government officials and company employees number close to 1,000.

C. Korean Farmers and Their Production

It goes without saying that Korean farmers in Manchuria are rice farmers (米作農民), so their main product is naturally "rice" (쌀). In a short essay by an official here praising the innate talent (天禀) of Koreans for rice cultivation, one can find such a passage:
"The Korean farmer's perception of water utilization (水利) is genius-like. Their accuracy in judging how to guide water by observing the terrain's highs and lows, and the ingenuity (丐妙 - likely 巧妙) of their water guidance, are astonishing (警歎). When these free immigrants wander in search of land and see a stream, they cultivate paddy fields there. It is not uncommon (드믄 일이 아니다) to encounter scenes where two or three<90> Korean farming households, completely separate from the Manchus in overlooked mountainous remote areas (山間僻地), protect and live off small paddy fields (小畓)."
Indeed, imagining the sighs and sweat of our fellow Korean farmers who lost their livelihoods in mainland Korea (內地), crossed the Yalu and Tumen Rivers carrying burdens on their backs (男負女載), and, under the exploitation of Manchu landlords (滿人地主), carried out the great task of paddy field development step by step (一步一步) through painstaking efforts (苦心慘擔), it can be called a great elegy of the world. Thus, even before the Manchurian Incident, the production of one million koku (石) of rice was accomplished in the Manchurian plains by the hands of Koreans. Following this, Manchu farmers, who were originally dry-field farmers (田作農民), gradually realized the advantages of paddy cultivation (畓耕作), and in recent years, Manchu farmers (滿農) have also begun to engage in this. However, as seen in the following table, there is no comparison.

D. Manchukuo Government's Policy Towards Korean Immigrants (朝鮮度民 - likely 移民)

Passing through the era of free immigration that began decades ago with Koreans adventurously crossing the river (冒險渡江), we are now in the stage of planned immigration where immigration is systematically guided. It is well known (周知) that the Manchukuo government established the Manchuria-Korea Development Company (滿鮮拓殖株式會社) by Imperial Ordinance (勅令) in Showa 11 [1936] and is cooperating with the Government-General authorities to protect and control Manchurian immigration. However, this planned immigration policy had restricted the distribution area of Korean immigrants to Chientao Province and the 33 counties of the former Eastern Frontier Provinces (舊東邊道), while designating 16 counties within the provinces of Outer Manchuria (表滿洲) as control and concentration areas (統制 集結地) for already resident Koreans. But based on the immigration results over the past two years, the conclusion was reached that there was no need for regional concentration or settlement restrictions (入植制限). Consequently, since July 27th this year, the regional restrictions have been abolished. This can be called a groundbreaking improvement (劃期的 改善) concerning Koreans. Furthermore, regarding the education of Koreans, following the abolition of extraterritoriality on December 1st last year, only the 14 elementary schools within the former South Manchuria Railway Zone (舊滿鐵附屬地內) continue to be managed by the Japanese side (under the jurisdiction of the Embassy Education Department - 大使館敎務部管轄). All other public and private schools were transferred (移讓) to Manchukuo. This point can be considered a major issue in terms of cultural exchange (文化交流上). The number of Korean educational institutions currently transferred to Manchukuo is as follows:
Elementary Schools (including village schools - 서당 포함) 492 schools, student count 69,209 people
Middle Schools 8 schools, 1,651 people
Kindergartens 27 schools, 1,136 people
Special Schools 19 schools, 850 people

There are various issues concerning Koreans in Manchukuo to point out and discuss, but I intend to write more as I observe the situation further. For now, I conclude this rough sketch (濶筆). (December 15th, in Xinjing)<92>

**Summary**

This 1939 article by Hŭng Yangmyŏng reports on the status of Korean immigrants in Manchukuo. It provides statistics showing nearly one million Koreans residing there (officially, though likely more), with Chientao Province having the highest concentration. The author notes a dramatic five-fold increase in Korean immigration after the 1931 Manchurian Incident compared to before. Agriculture, particularly rice cultivation where Koreans excel, is the dominant occupation (80% of households). A significant number of Koreans (over 3,000) also work as officials within the Manchukuo administration, facilitated by the recent abolition of Japanese extraterritoriality. The article mentions the Manchukuo government's shift from geographically restricting Korean settlement to abolishing these limits and the transfer of Korean schools (except former SMR zone ones) to Manchukuo control.

From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for its contemporary statistics on Korean migration patterns and settlement density in Manchukuo under Japanese influence. It highlights the state-sponsored nature of migration post-Manchurian Incident (Manchuria-Korea Development Company) and the integration (or co-option) of Koreans into the Manchukuo administrative structure as part of the "Five Races Under One Union" ideology following the end of extraterritoriality. The discussion of Korean agricultural prowess, particularly in rice farming compared to Manchu farmers, and the shift in educational administration offer insights into the socio-economic roles and evolving status of Koreans within the Japanese puppet state.

						新京 洪陽明
一. 在滿朝鮮人의 現勢

金東煥兄! 형이 나에게 바라는 것이 무었인지 잘 아는 나로서 형의 귀중한 지면을 비러 딴 사람에게 딴소리를 하는 것은 확실히 붓을 들때의 豫定은 아니있다. 따라서 이러토록의 나의 심적 과정을 잘 이해하여 주실 형은 내가 아즉도 엇더한 건설적인 글을 쓸 수 있는 狀態에 있지 못한 것을 認定하여 줄 것은 더욱 이곳에 온지 겨우 두달. 최근 수년간 전후 3, 4차 이땅에 발을 드디엇섯으나 언제나 新京 哈爾賓의 두 都會以外에 농촌에는 한발자곡도 못 발버 본 나로서 농업국 만주국의 농업이민 조선인의 생활을 云云하는 것은 확실히 猥濫한 일이라고 생각된다. 요새 유행하는 2, 3週間 急製의 支那通들이 戰線附近에 가서 대포소리 몇 방쯤 듯고 와서는 支那가 어떻다는 등 事變의 前途가 어떻다는 둥 큰 소리를 하는 것과 같은 넌센쓰를 演하는 것이 되지 않을가 저어한다. 그러나 旣往 잡은 붓이니 이제 삼가 이전날 나로서도 아러불 필요있는 일이오 一般으로도 다 아는 듯하면서도 구체적으로 잘 모르는 점이 있으리라고 생각되는 재만 조선인의 若干의 현세를 최근의 統計文獻을 통하야 紙上 주마간산격으로 集約一瞥하여 보기로 한다.

A. 在滿朝鮮人의 總數

大體 滿洲에 朝鮮人이 얼마나 사는가 하는 점에 대하야는 朝鮮內에서 呼曰百萬이라고 하여온지 이미 오래다. 그러치만 현재 통계상으로 나타난 것은 在滿 朝鮮人의 移動의<87> 頻繁僻地散在 등의 理由 其他로 인한 조사곤란으로 100만이하의 數字로 추출되어있다. 그러나 이 통계가 實數와 훨신 相異한 것임은 두말 할 것도 없는 것이니 이 점으 本年 9월 상순 京城에서 開催되었든 時局對策調査會에서 關東軍片倉參謀가 재만 朝鮮人數를 120만이라고 발표한 사실로써도 알 수 있는 것이다. 이제 기록에 낱아난 省別 在滿朝鮮人數를 摘記한면 다음과 같다.

B. 在滿朝鮮人의 生業

在滿朝鮮人의 생업의 絶對 大部分을 占有한 것은 농업으로 昭和 13년 현재 154,10戶로써 全戶數 192,897의 約 8割에 해당하며 이것을 地方別로 보면 間島 南滿 北滿의 순위이다. 기타는 日傭人 약 10,000戶 상업 約 7,000戶 官公吏 2,300戶 은행회사원 2,000戶 음식점업 1,500戶 敎員 1,300戶 기타직업 約 5,000戶 無職 3,000戶로 되어 있는데 右中 日傭人 約 10,000戶중의 大半은 大槪 農繁期에 농가에 屈傭되어 農耕에 종사하는 사람으로 농업에 다음 가는 상업의 約 7,000戶는 間島哈爾賓 新京 安東 奉天 등의 都會에 집중하야 소액자본으로 정미업 기타 소규모의 상업에 종사하는 것으로 그리 보잘 것 없는 狀態이다. 朝鮮人의 滿洲國官公吏가 상당한 다수에 달한 것은 주목할 점이니 이것은 작년 12월 1일 治外法權撤廢에 따라서 朝鮮總督府關係職員 及 民會職員 등 2,127名이 滿洲國 官公吏로 그대로 引繼任命된 것과 또 작년 7월 行政機構改革時에 朝鮮人 中堅官吏 簡任 勅任 薦任 奏任 約 25名 任命 또 이어서 8월에 6名의 中堅官吏가 朝鮮에서 진출된 것 밋 이곳에서 滿洲國中堅官吏養成機關인 大同學院出身 其他 現地任用 등을 합하고 滿洲帝國構成의 精神的 母體의 역할을 하고 있는 協和會의 직원되고 있는 朝鮮人中堅까지를 가산하면 3,000餘名이상의 朝鮮人이 滿洲國行政機構中에서 일하고 있는 것을 알 수 있다. 이 중에는 薦任(奏任)官이 50여명 簡任(勅任)으로는 崔南善(建國大學敎授) 朴錫胤(外務局調査處長) 秦學文(內務局參事官) 李範益(間島省長) 劉鴻洵(間島省民生廳長)의 5氏가 있고 薦任으로는 尹相弼(拓政司第2指導科長)씨 외 수십인의 科長級의 중요지위에 있는 이들이 있어 氣熖을 토하고 있는 것은 기쁜 일이라 할 수 잇다. 新京에만 해도 近 10,000名의 朝鮮人이 사는데 官公吏 會社員 등 蒼白한 인테리가 近 1,000명이나 된다.

C. 朝鮮農民과 그 生産

在滿 朝鮮農民은 말할 것도 없이 米作農民임으로 주요 생산물이 『쌀』일 것은 물론이다. 朝鮮人의 米作에 대한 天禀을 讚歎한 이곳의 當局者의 小論에 이러한 구절을 볼 수가 있다.
「朝鮮농민의 水利에 대한 인식은 천재적으로 地의 高低를 보고 水를 엇더케 誘導할가 하는 판단의 정확과 導水의 丐妙는 警歎할만 한 것이다. 彼等 自由移民이 토지를 차저 거러다니다가 水流를 보기만 하면 그곳에 水田을 開拓한다. 처다보지도 안는 山間僻地에 滿人과는 뚝떠러저 2, 3호의<90> 朝鮮農民이 小畓을 직히고 사러가는 광경을 접하는 것은 드믄 일이 아니다.」
실로 朝鮮內地에서 살길을 잃고 男負女載하야 鴨綠江 豆滿을 건너와서 苦心慘擔 滿人地主의 착취하에서 一步一步 畓開發의 大事 등을 遂行한 朝鮮農民同胞들의 한숨과 땀을 상상하면 一個의 위대한 세계의 엘레지라고 할 것이다. 이리하야 滿洲事變前 이미 백만石의 米의 産出이 滿洲平野에서 朝鮮人 손에 의하야 수행된 것이다. 이에 따러서 田作農民인 滿人들도 漸次 畓耕作의 유리한 점을 깨닷게되야 近年에는 滿農들도 이쪽에 손을 대기 시작하였다. 그러나 次表에서 보는 바와 같이 비교가 못된다.

D. 朝鮮度民에 대한 滿洲國政府의 方策

朝鮮人의 冒險渡江으로부터 開始된 過去 수십년전의 自由移民時代를 지나 統制的으로 移民을 지도하는 計劃移民段階에 처하야 滿洲國政府가 昭和 11년에 勅令에 의한 滿鮮拓殖株式會社를 설립하야 總督府當局과 협력하야 滿洲移民의 보호통제를 하고 있는 것은 周知하는 바이다. 그런데 이 計劃移民方針은 朝鮮移民의 분포지역을 間島省 及 舊東邊道의 33縣으로 제한하고 一方 旣住 朝鮮人에 대하여는 表滿洲 各省中의 16縣을 統制 集結地로 지정하였든 것인데 爾來 2개년간의 移民實績으로 보아 地域的 集結 乃至 入植制限을 할 必었 없다는 결론을 엇게 되야 今年 7월 27일 爾來 地域的 制限을 철폐하게 되였다. 朝鮮人에 대한 획기적인 結集改善이라 할 것이다. 또한 朝鮮人의 교육은 작년 12월 1일 治外法權撤廢에 따러 舊滿鐵附屬地內의 보통학교 14校만은 그대로 日本側(大使館敎務部管轄)이 經營하나 其外의 公私立學校는 全部 滿洲國에 移讓되였는데 이 점이 文化交流上 重大問題라 할 것이다. 現在 滿洲國에 移讓된 朝鮮人敎育機關의 數는 다음과 갓다.
