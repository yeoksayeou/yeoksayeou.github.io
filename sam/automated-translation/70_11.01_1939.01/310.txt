**Body Text Translation:**

Buddhist Invocation
						Ch'unwŏn
My Lord is here

My Lord is here, always before me,
But my eyes are dim, I cannot see the Lord beside me.
How I wish my eyes, clouded for kalpas (천겁), would brighten just once.

If these eyes were to open now, I would see my graceful Lord,
If my ears too were unblocked, I would hear that voice.
Clearly beside me, yet I neither see nor hear.<242>

Is that shape, flashing into sight, my Lord?
Is that sound, seeming to be heard, the voice of my Lord?
It feels like a body brushing past, but cannot be grasped.<243>

A face glimpsed

Thinking the face glimpsed was my Lord's,
I hurriedly adorned myself and waited for the sound of the gate.
Perhaps it was just a hallucination (헷뵈온 듯) born of my longing heart.

Was the sound heard by my ear not the Lord's voice?
I strained my ears, but there was only the sound of the wind.
Know that I sat up all through this night.

How long has it been since I started following the Lord's footsteps?
Though I believe I will meet Him at the end of my searching,
I wish to see Him before this day darkens.<244>

When I saw Him then, why could I not fall into His embrace?
Letting Him go, following behind, grieving with a breaking heart.
If He appears even fleetingly this time, could I ever let Him go?<245>

Longing for the Lord

Though longing for the Lord brings sickness or death, would I grieve?
But forgetting Him entirely, that is far more sorrowful.
This longing and forgetting, even I do not understand it.

When I forget the Lord, is it I who leaves the Lord?
Could I possibly leave the Lord whom I long and yearn for?
Perhaps it is the Lord who is abandoning me.

Please do not leave, please do not disappear.
Where can I live, seeing the Lord always, always?
If that place is beyond death, I would grieve dying too soon.<246>

Thinking of the Lord, the more I think, the more graceful He is.
Thinking and longing, the more I long, the deeper the yearning.
Believing in a promise of sometime, unknown, I shall long for Him.

When the flowers bloom, the Lord will seek me.
Before the buds have even formed, thoughts of the Lord bloom first.
In every gust of wind, every drop of rain, I wonder, is it the Lord?<247>

The Lord's Voice

Block one sound, and another comes, then another.
Ah, the clamor! Idle sounds, floating sounds.
There is no way to hear the Lord's voice I long for.<248>

Violent Storm, Rain, Thunder, and Lightning

On a night when thunder roars loudly and lightning flashes brightly,
I raise my sitting body and kneel before the Lord,
Quietly examining the depths of my own heart.

Though my blocked and darkened heart cannot grasp the Lord's will,
The great light and great sound are different from usual times,
I wonder if carefully looking up in reverence is my duty.

These ears that do not open even hearing the thunder,
These eyes that do not open even when lightning strikes,
I feel ever more keenly what a worthless body this is.<249>

Wherever I Go

Wherever could I go that is not the Lord's land?
Wherever this body is, is it not within the Lord's embrace?
Each time I die and am reborn, I see a new world.<250>

Reading Chuang Tzu (장자를 읽고)

As the Lord plays, I attend and listen,
Overcome with joy, how could I not dance?
Dancing, I see the Lord, and the Lord too rejoices.

As the Lord builds, I follow and build too,
When He tears down again, I follow the Lord and tear down,
Learning that tearing down is also building, I rejoice.

When joyful, as joyful; when sorrowful, as sorrowful,
In old age and sickness, keeping tune with the Lord's rhythm,
Finally, to the measure of death, I will dance my heart out and go.<251>

The Lord's Face

Lying on my sickbed with a fevered throat, the Lord's face appears. Is it the face of the Buddha? Yet, my childish spiritual heart (道心) can only express true, fond affection after creating a face that can be seen by eyes and touched by hands! My heart pounds against Heaven and Earth to sculpt one Lord, then longs for Him, rejoices in Him, fears Him, pleads with Him, acts like a spoiled child. My ignorance (無明), wanting to smash even this idol (優像), yet finding it too precious to bear losing.

Though I see Him daily, the Lord's face is ever new.
Because it is so, after seeing Him, I long for Him again.
Even seeing Him for ten million years, there is no end to it.<252>

The beauty of the Lord's face must be ten thousand times ten thousandfold.
Even if I attend Him closely my whole life, seeing Him always,
Would I see even half His beauty? Would I see even one ten-thousandth?

With these clumsy eyes, with this narrow and childish heart,
His beauty, immeasurable even if pondered my whole life.
Better to feign ignorance and enter the Lord's embrace.<253>

The Small Spring

In my rough garden, I have found a spring.
Could the water be plentiful? Could the taste be fine?
But Lord, please come, please drink the spring water.

Though called a garden, it has no walls or gate.
Could there be a path? Tread anywhere you please,
Come as you will, day or night.

Though the garden is rough, could there be no grass or flowers?
However poor, is there not sunlight by day, moonlight by night?
Especially on a summer night, could there be no dew?<254>

Placing a gourd dipper by the small spring,
When you drink from my spring on your way coming or going,
This body hides nearby, watches, and rejoices.<255>

Lotus Flower

Placing the lotus bud given by the Lord in a jade vase,
Night after night, I wondered, "Will it bloom tomorrow, will it bloom?"
But dawn after dawn passed, showing no sign of blooming.

Its roots severed, even if it blooms, could I hope for fruit?
But if the bud, formed with such effort, goes without opening,
Though the bud itself may be unconcerned, how can I bear my sorrow?

A flower destined not to bloom might as well be discarded,
But the reason I keep watching it as it withers and dries
Is that I cannot help but cherish its former intention, striving to bloom.<256>

The Cicada

A cicada comes to my window each morning and cries.
It must be the Lord's will, intending to awaken me,
Me, so dull, who cannot understand no matter how much told.

Though I don't fully grasp the cicada's words,
Its desperate cries, from a body that won't live even half a month,
Seem to be a song of "Fleeting, fleeting!"

As if having much to do in its short fourteen-day life,
It flies from tree to tree, crying as it goes, crying as it goes,
Crying and crying until it perishes, completing its life.<257>

Though they say the cicada cries calling for its mate (임),
A moment of love cannot be the whole meaning of a life.
Perhaps it tries to convey the meaning left behind, unresolved.<258>

To Mr. Pak Inbae (朴仁培君께)

Live long, don't die, take your medicine and get well soon.
Please, please don't die, your earnest words,
Receiving them, I weep, feel fear, and shame.

Though sixty-seven years cannot be called long life,
Looking back on the path traveled, even that life seems excessive.
Even if I recover and live longer, who would find it agreeable?

If life remains, I will not cut it short myself.
I will use the remaining strength as worthily as I can,
But I cannot trust my own heart, so I cannot make a firm promise.<259>

Expressing My Thoughts (述懷)

Like my weeping and laughing in dreams my own mind conjures,
So I am born and die in the world of my own making.
Both dreaming and waking are but dreams, I know.

Though I know weeping and laughter are dreams,
When I see sentient beings (중생), not yet awakened, grieving,
To awaken them from their dreams, I too dream another dream.

What does it matter if one knows a dream is a dream?
Even a dream soon to end seems real before waking.
Seeing beings pressed by nightmares, I shed tears.<260>

Adoration (思慕)

To what can I compare this heart that adores the Lord?
Neither tongue nor brush can depict it,
So I gather my hands, hold them to my chest, and close my eyes.

Since seeing the Lord once, I have cut ties with the world.
Hiding deep in my room, not going out,
Is because, having the Lord, whom else could I meet? There's no desire to meet anyone.

When the world, not knowing, speaks ill of the Lord,
Though I know He is not a Lord to be harmed by slander,
My heart aches for Him, held so precious.<261>

He is the Lord of all sentient beings, so I cannot serve Him alone,
Though I know this clearly, is it my foolish heart?
I desperately wish He would be my Lord alone.

He will come when the time is right, but I cannot bear the wait.
Crying "It's late!" I let the good seasons pass,
And when He comes, I haven't even touched the gifts to offer Him.<262>

The Lord There (임 거긔)

I know by word that the Lord is there,
But the time is different, and my steps are slow.
I fear the sun will set before I reach the end of the path.

When night falls on the way, my body weary, my legs aching,
Folding my hands, I kneel on the roadside,
And call, "O Lord!" Please come out and meet me.

The path to find the Lord, the more I walk and walk,
The hardship and weariness are difficult for this body.
Please take my hand, let me lean on you.<263>

On the dark wilderness path, when wind and rain strike,
It is hard even to shield my fragile candle flame.
Unable to go forward or back, I call the Lord and weep.

My prayer, conveying the sorrowful depths of calling the Lord,
The storm shatters and scatters it utterly.
Please gather the scattered fragments.<264>

This day too has passed on the journey begun to find the Lord.
Though the path walked under sun and moon was long,
Contemplating the endless road ahead, I feel dizzy and lost.<265>

What else is there for this body to do but this?
Walk, just walk; when morning comes, walk, when morning comes, walk.
Until life expires, walk the path seeking the Lord.

They say the place where the Lord resides is ten trillion lands away.
Even if ten trillion lands, if I go, there will be a day I arrive.
It is a path I must take, so how can I speak of far or near?

When I see the Lord before me in the original Pure Land (本願 正土),
Ah, that joy! Even the thought is precious.
How can I call this mere trudging a hardship?<265>

**Summary**

This extensive poem, "Yeombul" (Buddhist Invocation), by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu), explores themes of deep spiritual longing, devotion, and the struggle for enlightenment, framed as a search for "Im" (the Lord/Beloved, likely representing the Buddha or ultimate reality). The speaker laments their spiritual blindness and deafness, yearns for direct experience of the divine presence, reflects on the nature of attachment and detachment, finds resonance with Buddhist concepts like impermanence (through the cicada) and the illusory nature of reality (dream metaphor, Chuang Tzu), and ultimately expresses unwavering determination to continue the arduous journey towards the "Pure Land," finding solace even in the thought of eventual union. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1939, reflects Yi Kwangsu's later turn towards Buddhism and potentially offers insights into the spiritual landscape of intellectuals during the late colonial period. His personal reflections on mortality (in the section dedicated to Pak Inbae) and the blending of personal yearning with Buddhist doctrine provide historians with material on the intersection of individual experience, religion, and literature under Japanese rule.

임 여기 겨시다네

임 여기 겨시다네 내 앞에 늘 겨셔라만
내사 눈 어두어 곁에 둔 님 못 뵌다네

귀마자 열릴진댄 그 음성도 들을 것이

언뜻 뵈온 얼골

언뜻 뵈온 얼굴 임이신가 여기고서
밧비 단장하고 문ㅅ소리를 기다렸네
아마도 그리운 마음에 헷뵈온 듯 하여라

귓결에 들린 소리 임의 음성 아니신가
귀 기우리니 바람소리 뿐이로다
이 밤이 다 지내도록 앉어샌줄 아소서

임의 발자욱을 따라난지 얼마런고
찾고 찾는 끝에 만날 줄을 믿사와도
이 날이 저물기 전에 뵙고 싶어 합니다<244>

그 때 뵈왔을 때 품에 어이 못 들고서
보내고 뒤를 따라 애를 끊고 그리는고
이번에 번뜻만 한소서 놓을 줄이 있으랴<245>

임그려

임 그려 병드온들 죽사으들 설우라만
하다가 잊사옵기 그이 더욱 설은지고
그립고 잊히는 일은 나도 몰라 하노라

임을 내 잊사올 때 내, 임을 떠남인가
그립고 그린 님을 내 떠날 줄 있오릿가
아마도 날 버리시는 임이신가 합니다

떠나지 마옵고저 엊삽지도 마옵고저
어디서 길이 길이 임 뵈옵고 사옵고저
그것이 죽음 너머서라면 고대 죽다 설우리<246>

임을 헤어하니 헤일사록 고우서라
헤여 그리오매 그릴사록 더 그리워
언젠지 모를 기약을 믿고 그려 하오리다

꽃이 핀 적에야 임이 나를 찾을 것이
봉올도 안 진 것이 임생각만 먼저 피어

한 소리 막아내면 또 한 소리 또 한 소리
아아 시끄러라 허튼 소리 뜬 소리여
바라는 임의 음성을 들을 길이 없어라<248>

우레 크게 울고 번개 크게 빛나는 밤

우레 듣잡고도 못 열리는 이 귀옵고

임의 얼골

열 올은 목이 病床에 누었노라면 떠오르는 임의 얼골. 그것은 부처님의 얼골인가. 그러면서도 눈에 보여지고 손으로 만저지는 얼골을 만드러 놓고야 비로소 살틀한 그리운 정을 발할 수 있는 나의 어린 道心! 天地를 두둘겨 부셔서 한 분 임을 비저놓고 그리워하고 반가워하고 두려워하고 빌고 어리광 하는 나의 마음이어. 이 優像마자 두둘겨 부시고 싶우면서도 한긋 차마 아깝기도 한 내 無明이어.

날마다 뵈옵것만 늘 새로신 임의 얼골
그러 하옵길래 뵙고나면 또 그리워
千萬年 두고 뵈와도 그만인줄 없어라<252>

임 얼골 고우심이 천만 되리 만만 되리
一生에 곁에 뫼셔 두고두고 뵙더라도
고우신 반이나 뵈오리 만일이나 뵈오리

어설핀 이 눈으로 좁고 어린 이 맘으로
生前 헤아려도 못 헤아릴 그 고우심
차라로 모른척하고 임의 품에 들리라<253>

적온 샘

거츨은 내 동산에 샘 하나를 찾았어라
물인들 많사오리 웬 맛인들 좋으리만
임이어 오시옵소서 샘물 마시옵소서

동산이라 해도 담도 문도 없아옵고
길인들 있아오리 아모레나 밟으시고
낮이나 밤이 낫간에 마음대로 오소서

동산이 거츨기로 풀 꽃이야 없오리까
아모리 가난해도 낮에 했볕 밤에 달빛
대마처 여름 밤일진댄 이슬이야 없오리까<254>

이 몸은 숨어 앉사와 뵙고 깃버합니다.<255>

연꽃

임 주신 연꽃봉을 옥화병에 꽂아놓고
밤마다 내일이나 필까필까 하였더니
새벽이 가고 또 가도 필뜻아니 보여라

뿌리 끊였으니 핀들 열매 바라리만
모처롬 맺힌 봉을 못펴보고 가량이면

매암이
