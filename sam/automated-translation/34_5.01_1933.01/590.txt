Stage
On a mountainside, one side of which has been cut away to make a new road, shack-like houses of people who have been pushed out are visible. A single bare Poplar tree—it is a desolate, waning autumn sunset. Upstage, there is a fence gate, and in front of the gate, a wooden board for selling fruit is placed low. Downstage, the corner of a tavern with a sign indicating a restaurant is visible. From afar, the single shouts of Chinese people can be heard, and the sound of clothes being beaten smooth occasionally echoes.

Curtain Rises

Old Man Yang is sitting properly inside the fence gate, having laid out fruits, and neighborhood children with runny noses have gathered around, looking at the fruits.
The neighborhood Elder (Mr. Ch'oe) and his Wife enter from Downstage.
Elder   『Have you been well?』
Old Man Yang 『Please come in. It’s been a long time. Madam, what brings you here?』
Wife   『What brings me here? It feels entirely like a dream.』
Elder   『You should tell the story a bit more slowly, dear.』
Wife   『Some things can’t be told slowly! What is this? Why do you people make it so difficult for us to live? Huh?』
Old Man Yang 『Ah—no. This is the first I’m hearing of this. It’s like thrusting a rolling pin in the dark night, <117> isn't there a limit?』
Wife   『What did you just say? Good heavens, you utterly wicked—Honey, say something! Where in the world could such a thing happen?』
Old Man Yang 『What exactly happened?』
Elder   『It’s nothing else.』
Wife   『Oh, it's so frustrating! Why can't you just tell him quickly?』
Old Man Yang 『Madam, please be quiet for a moment. So, you say we made it difficult for you to live? This story sounds quite unusual. How did we make it difficult for you?』
Elder   『It wasn’t just making it difficult, what else could it be? If he was going to do that damn strike (동맹파업), your son should have done it alone. Why did he have to incite other people’s children and cause them to starve?』
Old Man Yang 『That can’t be right. My son…』
Wife   『"That can’t be right?" Then, would people as foolish as our son know anything to jump into that? It’s all because of your son, Elder.』
Elder   『That boy, your son, where on earth did he go? He went around rattling the neighborhood as if he alone knew everything in the world, and in the end, is this all he has accomplished? He’s just a quick-witted brat (반지 빠른 자식).』
Old Man Yang 『So, my son was the ringleader (앞잽이)?』
Wife   『Of course. We had nothing to eat, so I went to the factory owner to plead, but they said some other people had already been hired, so those who went on strike with your son are out of work from now on. It's like the shrimp's back breaking in a whale fight, but there should be a limit! Because of your son, what kind of ordeal is this? We're going to die! Now, you people must decide whether to let us live or die.』
Elder   『Yes, that brat who deserves to have his legs broken! Always being rude to the neighborhood elders—inciting other people’s children—and finally, even making them starve to death! If it were the old days, would such a rascal be left alone? It’s entirely “When the top is murky, the bottom cannot be clear (上濁下不淸)”; the parents who produced such worthless offspring (씨밧지 못할 자식) are at fault, what else?』
Old Man Yang 『Please calm down. The world isn't as simple as you think. The thoughts of the old and the thoughts of the young differ with the times. Please, all of you, go back now.』
Wife   『Hey, look here! Since he's your son, Elder Brother, you're taking his side! You're biased!』
Elder   『Those who have nothing should live accordingly. Do they stick to the fat ones? What's the point of all this fuss when you just live one life and die? Does he think he's the only one who knows?』
Wife   『Does one follow the dying to die? If a sparrow tries to follow a stork, its legs will tear. Save us, save us! Why did you make it impossible for us to live? (Throws a tantrum)』
Old Man Yang manages to send the couple away.
Elder   『Just you wait. It’s not just us... (Omitted)... they said they’d run him through.』
Wife   『Hmph, like things will turn out well! Let’s just wait and see how well things go.』

The Elder and his Wife exit Downstage. Twilight deepens.
The lights in the restaurant turn on. Ok-hŭi returns from the factory carrying a lunch box (벤도). Enters Downstage.

Ok-hŭi   『Father.』
Old Man Yang 『Oh, Ok-hŭi. You’re quite late today.』
Ok-hŭi   『(Staring intently at her father’s worried face) Father, is something worrying you? Why are you like this, Father?』
Old Man Yang 『…』
Ok-hŭi   『Older brother hasn’t come either…』
Old Man Yang 『Well. It’s because of your brother. I don’t know what we should do in the future.』
Ok-hŭi   『Why? Please tell me clearly.』
Old Man Yang 『Just now, the parents of Sŭng-ho from the village below <118> came and said your brother was the ringleader (압재비) and led a strike. So now the aftermath is messy. In our dire situation where we earn daily to eat daily, what should we do?』
Ok-hŭi   『Then, is everything ruined for me?』
Old Man Yang 『Ruined?』
Ok-hŭi   『Since older brother caused such trouble at the factory, will Mr. Yun-gi like it?』
Old Man Yang 『Yun-gi? You mean the factory owner’s son?』
Ok-hŭi   『So, will Mr. Yun-gi even think of me? Everything’s ruined for me, isn't it?』
Old Man Yang 『You pitiful child. Why are you saying such foolish things? Even if this incident hadn’t happened, you and Yun-gi are in circumstances where you cannot be close to each other—how much less likely is it now?』
Ok-hŭi   『Why? What’s wrong with Mr. Yun-gi?』
Old Man Yang 『You thoughtless child. As for Yun-gi, he’s the descendant of a wealthy family and can do whatever he wants in the world. Why would he promise his life to you when there are plenty of girls elsewhere?』
Ok-hŭi   『But Mr. Yun-gi told me he is definitely not lying. And he swore by heaven and earth that he would never change. That's what he said.』
Old Man Yang 『No, my child. The words that sound like honey to your ears now will become a sorrowful matter that makes you vomit X in the near future.』
Ok-hŭi   『Then, Father, you’re saying you don’t want your daughter to marry into a rich family and do well?』
Old Man Yang 『No. How could that be? What parent would stop their child from succeeding? But in this world today—』
Ok-hŭi   『I don’t know, I don’t know. I’m going to do as I think now. Isn't it a person's freedom to try and carry out what they believe is right?』

Ok-hŭi exits into the house. From inside the restaurant, boisterous noises are heard, and then three drunkards appear, staggering.

Drunkard 1  『(Looking into the restaurant) So, you think we can't eat anywhere else but your place? I've never seen such a disagreeable woman.』
Drunkard 2  『This whole neighborhood is full of taverns, are you the only one selling?』
Drunkard 3  『Let's go. That woman, after changing husbands for the seventh time, seems to think she's above everyone else. Tsk, tsk. What a loose woman (환양년).』

The drunkards borrow a match from Old Man Yang and light their cigarettes.

Drunkard 1  『I'm drunk.』
Drunkard 2  『Drunk already? Come on.』
Drunkard 3  『How many rounds (巡盃) did we have in total?』
Drunkard 1  『Fifteen.』
Drunkard 2  『And you get drunk after just that much? Man, what's wrong with you? Let's go again. Shall we go to that "Aengmu House (앵무집)" this time?』
Drunkard 1  『Hmm.』
Drunkard 2  『Hey!』
Drunkard 3  『Yeah?』
Drunkard 2  『Let's go to Aengmu House.』
Drunkard 3  『Right. Where was Aengmu House again? I heard she moved again.』
Drunkard 1  『She went up.』
Drunkard 3  『Up?』
Drunkard 2  『Went up? You mean she rode a dragon?』
Drunkard 1  『She went up that mountain. Hey—over there, can't you see that house with the board fence? That's the one.』
Drunkard 3  『Well, damn that rascal's place. It sure went up high. That’s the very materialization of a castle in the air (空中樓閣), isn't it?』
Drunkard 1  『Right—let's pay respects to the castle in the air. Right. Hey, the other day I saw in the newspaper that those prankster Yankees equipped an airship with a cafe and are doing business while flying around. If we go up there, it’ll probably be like that. Hahahaha.』
Drunkard 2  『Be that as it may, it’s nice while eating, but if you happen to make a mistake coming down drunk, it’s a guaranteed trip straight down to hell (飛流直下地獄行), isn’t it? Hahahaha.』 <119>
Drunkard 3  『By the way, when did Aengmu House move up there?』
Drunkard 1  『Probably about a week (週餘) ago. She had no choice but to give up that precious house and leave. Anyway, it’s a pathetic situation. If they keep getting chased up like that, what will happen in the end?』
Drunkard 2  『Maybe that too became the civilized Western style, moving up the mountain. Good air, no worries about water pollution, isn't that killing two birds with one stone (一擧兩得)?』
Drunkard 3  『Man. Even in the old curiosity-seeking era (獵奇時代), people built houses on mountain tops and lived there. Who knows if it's a civilized Western style or a barbaric style (野蠻風) regressing historically?』
Drunkard 2  『Whatever it is, it’s an inevitable retreat (退程).』
Drunkard 1  『Hmph. You all talk as if it’s someone else’s story. Right.』

(Ok-hŭi enters from the house)

Ok-hŭi   『Father, please have your dinner.』
Old Man Yang 『Alright. Let's wait a little longer and eat together when your brother comes.』
Drunkard 3  『Oh—isn't that Ok-hŭi?』
Ok-hŭi   『Sir. How have you been?』
Drunkard 2  『Wow. That girl has grown up quite a bit. Huh. She looks completely like a young lady now.』
Drunkard 3  『Do you know who this girl is?』
Drunkard 1  『Who?』
Drunkard 2  『Why, she's Ok-hŭi who used to attend the XX night school (夜學). Hey, Ok-hŭi. Say hello. That gentleman is Mr. Yi Sŭng-ch'un from the newspaper branch office. Go on.』
Ok-hŭi   『(Bows her head shyly.)』
Drunkard 1  『Haha. She really is quite cute. Huh. How old are you?』
Ok-hŭi   『Seventeen.』
Drunkard 2  『Just the right age, huh? Hahahaha.』

They tease her; one grabs Ok-hŭi's wrist. She pulls away. They tease her again. A bit rudely.

Old Man Yang 『(Unable to bear it anymore) Hey! What is this rude behavior? You look like respectable gentlemen.』
Drunkard 2  『What?』
Old Man Yang 『What do you mean, "what?" What kind of talk is that?』
Drunkard 2  『That old man doesn't know his place. Really.』
Drunkard 3  『Hey, stop it. Stop it (Tries to stop him).』
Old Man Yang 『That rascal speaks like someone raised without manners (밴데 업시). Calling an old man "that old man." Is there such a scoundrel who deserves to die?』
Drunkard 2  『What did I do? Huh?』
Old Man Yang 『You brat! How dare you grab a grown girl's wrist from someone else's house and act rudely? You nasty-looking scoundrel!』
Drunkard 3  『Hey, sir. That's not it. Let's just stop.』
Drunkard 1  『Hey, hey, stop it. Stop it. Elder, we apologize. Let's—go.』
Drunkard 2  『No, so what if I grabbed her wrist for a moment because I thought she was cute? Is it right for an old man (늙은 자) to talk like that?』
Old Man Yang 『You brat! "An old man's words"—so what about an old man's words? I hear you scoundrels claim to be the so-called influential gentlemen (유지 신사) in this neighborhood. Are you guys, who claim with your mouths to work for the world, only capable of getting drunk on the main road and grabbing girls' wrists? You scoundrels deserve to be cut into ten pieces and still have guilt remaining!』
Ok-hŭi   『Father, please stop.』

The drunkards hesitate and are about to exit Downstage when Min Yun-gi, the factory owner's son, enters from Downstage. Seeing Min Yun-gi, the drunkards cover their faces and quickly exit Upstage.

Old Man Yang and Ok-hŭi compose themselves.
Min Yun-gi 『Ah, Miss Ok-hŭi. You're already back?』
Ok-hŭi   『(Looks at him, shy expression) Yes, I'm already back.』
Min Yun-gi 『Haha, work ended quite early today. Right—(As if noticing Old Man Yang for the first time) Ah, how do you do?』
Old Man Yang 『Yes, please come in.』
Min Yun-gi 『Have you been well?』
Old Man Yang 『Yes, it’s just the same as always.』
Min Yun-gi 『I meant to come and see you sooner, but naturally, I became busy. (Gives a slight smile.)』
Old Man Yang 『By the way, my son is said to have caused <120> a big disturbance at your factory. What happened?』
Min Yun-gi 『Yes, yes. That sort of thing is of no concern to me at all, but originally, my father is someone who would trample on the lives of many people to satisfy his personal desires. I am different from my father, but anyway, I see Mr. Yangsan's actions, resisting such injustice, as certainly more than justified. I rather feel ashamed to face you all. (Makes eye contact with Ok-hŭi and smiles.)』
Old Man Yang 『Even just your words are generous.』
Min Yun-gi 『The world is in such a murderous recession that you must have many difficult times making a living, I imagine.』
Old Man Yang 『It’s just "life is worse than death" (生不如死). Hahaha (A lonely laugh).』
Min Yun-gi 『(Occasionally exchanges smiles with Ok-hŭi.) (Takes out a wad of 300 won from his pocket) Elder, this money is a small amount, but please use it for drinks and tobacco.』
Old Man Yang 『No. What is this?』
Min Yun-gi 『Please accept it. I am giving this money out of respect (정리), so please don't think anything of it.』
Old Man Yang 『Yes. Thank you very much, but I cannot accept money without reason out of the blue.』
Min Yun-gi 『It's disappointing that you refuse my sincerity.』
Old Man Yang 『No matter if we eat only nine meals in thirty days (三旬九食), I do not want to live wretchedly relying on others' charity. I would rather move my own body and live off the compensation for that.』
Min Yun-gi 『Hahaha. I feel rather sorry—Then let’s do this. You can sell me that fruit, right?』
Old Man Yang 『Yes, you may do that.』
Min Yun-gi 『(Picks up one fruit) Here, please take this. It's the price of the fruit. (Offers the 300 won wad again.)』
Old Man Yang 『(Feels a sort of insult, stares, then) This is 1 chŏn each, so please give me only 1 chŏn.』
Min Yun-gi 『(Slight smile) No, Elder. Why do you stipulate that one fruit must necessarily be 1 chŏn? In short, the compensation for the labor of receiving, displaying, and selling that fruit is infinitely precious, so I think even this 300 won is rather a small amount. Here, please take it.』
Old Man Yang 『(Speechless, just laughs.)』
Min Yun-gi 『Isn't compensation something one has the right to demand?』
Min Yun-gi places the 300 won wad on the fruit board.
Min Yun-gi 『By the way, as I mentioned before, I am definitely not lying (stands up and goes towards Ok-hŭi) Miss Ok-hŭi and I, for a lifetime… hahaha.』

Min Yun-gi takes Ok-hŭi's wrist. Ok-hŭi does not try to refuse and remains held, smiling faintly and turning away with an expression of feeling a certain physical sensation (肉感).
Just then—Yangsan enters from Downstage, extremely agitated.

Ok-hŭi   『Older brother!』
Min Yun-gi 『(Startled, turns around) Ah, Mr. Yangsan!』
San     『(Stares fixedly at Min Yun-gi.)』
Min Yun-gi 『Mr. Yangsan, where have you been... ... (His voice trails off mid-sentence because Yangsan has such a terrifying expression.)』
San     『Why did you come to my house? What did you come here for? Aren't you ashamed to meet us, you people who mercilessly tear apart even our final pleas and use those dog-like scabs (개색기들)?』
Min Yun-gi 『I am not unaware of Mr. Yangsan's words. However, that is my father's sin, not my fault. I, too, keenly feel my father's errors.』
San      『I don't want to hear it. Go tell such lies to fools or madmen. If you knew your father's actions were improper, why would you be wasting time here at this urgent moment when tens of thousands of lives are fading away? While your gold watch chains glitter and you suck on a Manila cigar, do you know how much blood and tears pour from people like us? (Sees the money. Picks it up) Father, what is this money? <121> Where did this money come from?』
Min Yun-gi 『N-no, Mr. Yangsan. Th-th-that money is the price for this fruit.』
San     『What? The price of fruit? Hey! You snake-like bastard! If the owner of that fruit were someone other than Ok-hŭi's father, would you be the kind of guy to pay 300 won for one piece? If your sympathy were genuine, rather than showing this superficial (껍떼이) sympathy to one person, why can't you say even one word of advice to your beast-like (즘생같은) father for the sake of those countless suffering people crying out? (Turns to his father) Father, why did you accept this money? Why did you accept this unjust money? Do you covet money so much? I cannot sell my integrity for money. I cannot let my comrades starve just so I alone can live well. (Turns to Min Yun-gi) Disgusting! Take it and use it, you bastards who view my spirit as a commodity!』

Yangsan throws the money down. Min Yun-gi picks up the money and runs off Downstage.
Ok-hŭi cries.

San      『You wench, why are you crying? Are you not this older brother's sister? Are you crying because you're sad to be my sister? If you want to follow him, go! The body of a rotten-hearted wench is worse than a doll. Why are you crying? Is it unfair? Are this older brother's words unjust? If you must cry, if you must cry, then just die!』

Yangsan hits Ok-hŭi. The Old Man intervenes. The stage goes dark. Yangsan's friend Chang enters hastily. Another person follows him.

Chang     『Hey, Yangsan! Big trouble!』
San     『What is it?』
Chang     『How could things turn out so heartbreakingly with such people? The comrades who worked with you until now have betrayed you. They've changed sides because of that guy's money. And those guys have sided with the owner and agreed to start work from tomorrow.』
San      『So, they finally betrayed me. And they turned traitor because of poison (독 - implies money). Ahh. There's no one to trust. Why isn't there anyone in this neighborhood willing to lay down their life for loyalty (신의)? Ahh, loyalty, loyalty, I long for loyalty! I long for comrades who uphold loyalty!』

Yangsan starts sharpening a knife on a whetstone.

Old Man Yang  『Son, Yangsan. What are you trying to do? Son, huh?』
Ok-hŭi    『Older brother. Why are you sharpening the knife?』
San     『(While sharpening the knife) Money is necessary, but if these morally fallen people have money, there's no way that money will be used well. It's because they don't know how to uphold loyalty. It's all because they are ignorant (무식한). Our enemy, more than anything, is ignorance. For thousands of years, for tens of thousands of years, until the day this shell of ignorance is shed, the world's sorrowful tears will never dry.』

Yangsan takes the knife and is about to leave.

Ok-hŭi    『Older brother, where are you going?』
Old Man Yang  『Son, really, what are you going to do with that knife?』
Ok-hŭi    『Older brother, please endure it. (Grabs Yangsan's wrist) And put down this knife.』
San     『Hey! Let go! I can't put it down. Yangsan's knife, the Yangsan Knife (梁山刀), can never be put down. Father, I am your son, and also a son of this world. Please believe that only after I first become a worthy son of this world can you truly have a son worthy of the name.』
Ok-hŭi    『Older brother, just put down this knife. Put down this knife.』
San      『Hey! Let go! Even if this body were dismembered (릉지) a hundred times, Yangsan's knife, the knife, cannot be put down!』

(a) E-hei-ye—
Even if the sun, moon, and stars shatter to pieces, Yangsan's knife cannot be put down. E-hei-ra, let go! I cannot put it down. Even if dismembered, I cannot put it down.』
(b) E-hei-ye—
Though the rivers, mountains, grass, and trees weep pitifully, the human dream knows not how to wake. E-hei-ra, let go! I cannot put it down. Yangsan's knife, I cannot put it down.』

**Summary**

This 1933 Korean play, "Yangsan's Knife," depicts the struggles of a poor family caught in a labor dispute during the colonial period. Yangsan, the son, leads a factory strike but faces betrayal from fellow workers bribed by the owner. His sister, Ok-hŭi, is tempted by the factory owner's son, Min Yun-gi, who offers money and affection, creating conflict with her idealistic brother and proud father. The play culminates with Yangsan, disillusioned by the betrayal and perceived moral decay driven by poverty and ignorance, sharpening his knife ("Yangsan's Knife") in a desperate, symbolic act of resistance against the injustices he perceives, declaring his commitment to a larger cause beyond his family.

For historians, the play offers a valuable glimpse into the social and economic hardships of 1930s colonial Korea. It vividly portrays class conflict, the nascent labor movement (strikes and scab labor - "개색기들"), the desperation caused by poverty ("生不如死"), the displacement of marginalized communities ("쪼끼는 사람들"), and the differing responses to oppression – principled resistance (Yangsan), traditional pride (Old Man Yang), opportunistic temptation (Ok-hŭi, striking workers), and the casual cruelty of the privileged (Min Yun-gi, drunkards). The theme of ignorance ("무식") as the root enemy reflects contemporary intellectual discourse on societal problems.

무대
신작로가 나느라고 한 쪽이 깍겨진 산 비탈에 쪼끼는 사람들의 괴딱지 갓흔 집들이 보인다. 앙상한 白楊木 하나―늘스년스럽게 점으는 가을 석양이다. 上手로 울타리 문이 잇고 그 문 압헤다가 菓物파는 목판을 나케 한다. 下手에도 음식점 간판을 붓친 술집 못텡이가 보인다. 멀니서 中國人들의 외마듸 소리가 들니고 옷 다듬는 마치소리가 때로 울녀온다.

玉姬   『(마조보고 붓그러워하는 표정) 녜 벌서 왓세요.』
閔允基 『하하 오늘은 퍽 일즉 파햇슴니다. 그려―(비로소 梁老人을 븐 드시) 아 안녕하섯슴니가.』
梁老人 『녜 어서오십쇼.』
閔允基 『그간 재미조셋서요.』
梁老人 『녜 그저 늘 한 모양이외다.』
閔允基 『진작 좀 차저와서 봐오려면 자연히 奔忙한 몸이 되여서요.(얄분 우슴을 웃는다.)』
梁老人 『그련데 참. 내 자식놈이 댁 공장에서 ＜120＞ 큰 소란을 이르켯다니 엇더케 된 일임니까.』
閔允基 『녜녜. 그까짓 것이야 제에게는 하등의 관심할 배도 업지만 원래 나의 아버지께서는 여러 사람의 목숨을 짓발버서라도 아버지 개인의 욕망을 만족 하시겟다는 분이니까요. 나는 아버지와는 달은 터이지만 엇잿든 그러한 不正當에 반항하는 梁山씨의 행동이야말노 물론 정당이상의 정당이라고 봄니다. 나는 도리혀 여러분을 봐을 면목이 없음니다.(玉姬와 눈을 마조치며 눈 우슴을 짓는다.)』
梁老人 『말삼만이라도 관대하시외다.』
閔允基 『세상이 너무 살인적 불경기가 되여서 지내시기에 곤란하실 때가 퍽 만으시겟슴니다 그려.』
梁老人 『그저 生不如死지요. 하하하하(쓸쓸한 우슴)』
閔允基 『(때때로 玉姬와 눈우슴을 맛친다.)(주머니에서 3백원 뭉텡이를 끄내며) 영감 이 돈이 약소함니다만 酒草에나 보태쓰시기 바람니다.』
梁老人 『아니. 이게 왼 일이심니까.』
閔允基 『받으십쇼. 이 돈은 제가 정리로 듸리는 것이니 엇지 아지 마십쇼.』
梁老人 『녜. 많이 감사하오만은 까닭 없는 돈을 턱없이 받을 수 없소이다.』
閔允基 『정성을 막으시니 섭섭함니다 그려.』
梁老人 『아모리 우리가 三旬九食을 하든 한이라도 남의 의뢰를 받어서 구차히 살고 싶지는 안소. 차라리 우리의 육신을 움직여서 그 보수로 살지로.』
閔允基 『하하하. 오히려 퍽 미안하게 되엿슴니다―그러면 이러케 하지요. 그 과일을 제게다 파실 수는 게시겟지요.』
梁老人 『녜 그것은 그럿케 하십쇼.』
閔允基 『(과일 한 개를 집어들고) 자 받으십쇼. 과일 갑시올시다.(3백원 뭉치를 또 준다.)』
梁老人 『(일종 모욕을 늣기며 바라보다가) 이것은 한 개에 1전식이니 1전만 주시요.』
閔允基 『(얄분 우슴) 아니 영감. 왜 과일 한 개의 必曰 1전이라는 규정을 내리심니까. 요컨대 그 과일을 바더다 노코 파시게 되는 노동의 보수라는 것은 무한히 고귀한 것인즉 나는 이 돈 3백원도 오히려 적은 돈이라고 생각함니다. 자 어서 받오십쇼.』
梁老人 『(기맥혀 웃기만 한다.)』
閔允基 『보수란 맛당히 차즐 권리가 잇는 것이 아님니까.』
閔允基는 과일 목판 우에다가 3백원 뭉치를 놋는다.
閔允基 『그련데 일전에도 말슴햇지만 나는 결코 거짓이 없이 (이러서서 玉姬게로 가며) 玉姬씨와 한 평생을 하하하하.』

閔允基는 玉姬의 손목을 잡는다. 玉姬는 거절하려고도 아니하고 그대로 잡혀잇서 일종 肉感을 늣기는 표정으로 방그레 웃고 도라슨다.
때―梁山이가 대단히 흥분이 되여서 下手로부터 등장.

玉姬   『오라버니』
閔允基 『(놀나 도라스면서) 아 梁山氏.』
山     『(閔允基를 노리고 바라븐다.)』
閔允基 『梁山씨 어데를 갓다오... ...(梁山이가 너무 무서운 표정을 하고 잇슴으로 말이 중도에 희미해진다.)』
山     『당신이 엇재서 내 집엘 왓소. 무엇 때문에 왓느냔 말이요. 무자비하게도 우리의 최후애원까지를 찌저바리고 개색기들을 이용해다가 쓰는 그대들이 우리를 맛나기가 붓그럽지 안소.』
閔允基 『梁山氏의 말슴을 모르는 것도 아니올시다. 그럿치만 그것은 우리 아버지의 죄이지요. 나의 허물은 아님니다. 나 역시 우리 아버지의 과실을 절실하게 늣기고 잇슴니다.』
山      『듯기 실소. 그련 거짓말은 천치나 밋친놈 앞에 가서 하시요. 당신 아버지의 행동이 不溫當한 줄 알면 엇재서 數萬生靈이 숨어가는 이 급한 시간을 이곳서 허비하고 잇슬게가요. 너의들의 금시계줄이 뻔적어리고 여송연 한목음을 빠는 그 순간에 우리 가튼 사람들의 숨찬 피눈물이 얼마나 쏘다지는 지를 아느냐? (돈을 본다. 집어들고) 아버지, 이 돈이 왼 돈임니까? ＜121＞ 이것이 어데서 난 돈임니까.』
閔允基 『야야 양산씨. 그 그 그 돈은 이 과일 값이람니다.』
山     『뭐. 과일 값이라고. 예기 이 뱀의 색기 같은 놈 같으니. 만일 그 과일의 임자가 玉姬의 아버지가 아닌 딴 사람의 과일이라도 한 깨의 3백원 식을 주고 사는 놈이냐? 네의 동정이 진정한 동정이라고 할 것 같으면 이러케 한 사람에게다 껍떼이 동정을 베푸는 것보다 아우성을 치는 저 수많은 사람들을 위하야 즘생같은 네 애비에게 왜 충고의 말 한 마듼들 못한단 말이야.(아버지를 도라 보며) 아버지 이 돈은 왜 받으섯소. 이 불의의 돈을 왜 받으섯소. 돈이 이러케 탐이 나심니까. 나는 돈으로 나의 절개를 팔어 버릴 수는 없음니다. 나는 나 한 몸만 잘살기 위해서 동지들의 굶어 죽일 수는 없소. (閔允基를 도라 보며) 더럽다. 나의 정신을 상품으로 보는 너의 놈들이나 갓다 써라.』

梁山이는 돈을 던진다. 閔允基는 그 돈을 집가지고 下手로 다러난다.
玉姬는 운다.

山      『이년아, 너는 왜 우니. 너는 이 오래비의 동생이 아니란 말이냐. 일건 오래비의 동생이 되기가 설어서 우느냐. 따러 가려거든 가거라. 맘 썩은 년의 몸둥아리는 차라리 인형만도 못하다. 왜 우느냐. 원통하냐. 이 오래비의 말이 억울하냐. 울녀거든 울녀거든 차라리 죽어라.』

梁山이는 玉姬를 때린다. 老人은 말닌다. 무대는 캄캄해진다. 梁山의 친구 張이 황급한 거동으로 등장. 또 한 사람이 그 뒤를 따러왓다.

張     『여보게 梁山이 큰일 낫네.』
山     『뭐냐』
張     『그래 이련 분하고 절통할 데가 어데 잇단 말인가. 지금까지 자네 같이 일하든 同侔들은 이제 자네를 배반하고 말엇네. 그놈들의 돈으로 해서 그만 변절을 하엿다네. 그리고 그놈들은 주인과 한 편이 되여서 래일부터 일을 시작하기로 되엿다네.』
山      『맟음내 저들은 나를 배반 하엿구나. 그리고 독 때문에 변절을 하엿구나. 아아. 믿을 놈이 없구나. 엇재서 이 洞里에는 신의를 위해서 목슴이라도 바칠 사람이 없느냐. 아아 신의, 신의, 신의가 그립구나. 신의를 직히는 동무가 그립구나.』

梁山이는 숫돌에다가 칼을 갈기 시작한다.

梁老人  『얘야, 梁山아.  너 엇지려고 이러고 얘야 응.』
玉姬    『오라버니. 칼은 왜 가심니까?』
山     『(칼을 갈면서)돈은 필요한 것이지만 인간적으로 타락한 이 사람들의게 만일 돈이 잇다기로 그 돈의 활용이 잘될 일리가 없을 것이다. 신의를 직힐줄 모르는 때문이다. 모도가 무식한 때문이다. 우리의 원수는 무엇보다도 무식이다. 멫 천년을 가도, 멫 만년을 가도  이 무식한 껍덱이가 벗겨지지 안는 날에는 세상의 슬픈 눈물은 언제고 말으지 안을 것이다.』

梁山은 칼을 들고나가려 한다.

玉姬    『오라버니, 그래 어데로 가심니까.』
梁老人  『얘야, 글세 그 칼은 가지고 엇더케 하려느냐.』
玉姬    『오라버니, 제발 참으십쇼. (梁山의 손목을 잡고) 그리고 이 칼을 노십쇼.』
山     『에라 노아라. 못노켓다. 梁山이의 칼 梁山刀만은 언제라도 놀 수가 없다. 아버지 나는 아버지의 아들이요, 또 이 세상의 아들이외다. 내가 먼저 이 세상의 훌륭한 아들이 되고 난 연후랴야 비로소 아버지는 아들다운 아들을 가질 수 잇게 되는 것임만 믿어 주십쇼.』
玉姬    『오라버니 이 칼만은 노세요. 이 칼만은 노세요.』
山      『여라 노와라. 가사 이 한 몸을 열 백번 릉지를 하기로 梁山의 칼 칼만은 놀 수가 없다.』

(노래)
(가) 에헤이에―
日月星辰이 조각이 나드래도 梁山의 칼만은 놀 수가 업구나. 에헤라 노와라 못노켓구나. 룽지를 하여도 내가 못노리로구나나.』
(나) 에헤이에―
江山 草木이 구술피 울어도 인간의 꿈만은 깰 줄을 모른다. 에헤라 노와라 못노켓나. 梁山의 칼만은 내가 못노리로구나.』
