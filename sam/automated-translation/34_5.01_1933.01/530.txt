Article Title: 와잇트챠플, 現代英詩壇 (Whitechapel, Modern English Poetry Scene)
Author: 리챠-드·앨딍튼
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Whitechapel, Modern English Poetry Scene
						Richard Aldington

Noise—
Iron hooves, iron wheels, passing
Cart, tram, feet's clanging iron sound.
Iron resounding in the harsh, frenzied, discordant sounds.

The swallow's passing,
Sharp, distant cry is also in vain,
The April meadow's
Quiet blue light is also in vain,
The clear white rain is also in vain.

Soot and mud,
People drained of spirit by labor,
Limitless collision of effort—
Iron resounding on iron.
Wordlessly, powerlessly,
Smoke circles and rises.

Where the sea waves dance blue,
The flitting seagull's
Sharp, distant cry is also in vain,
The April meadow too—

Noise, iron, smoke.
Iron, iron, iron.
-End- <107>

**Summary**

This poem, translated from Richard Aldington's work, depicts the harsh, noisy, and polluted industrial environment, likely Whitechapel in London. It contrasts the overwhelming presence of iron—sounds of transport, machinery, and the general cacophony—and the smoke-filled air with the futility of nature's beauty, represented by swallows, April meadows, and seagulls. The poem laments the draining effect of labor on people and the dominance of the industrial over the natural. For historians studying Korean colonial history, this piece is interesting as it shows Korean intellectuals engaging with contemporary Western modernist literature and its themes of industrial alienation and despair during the 1930s. Its publication in *Samch'ŏlli*, a major popular magazine, suggests an attempt to broaden cultural horizons and perhaps implicitly comment on the societal changes and hardships, whether industrial or colonial, being experienced or anticipated in Korea.
