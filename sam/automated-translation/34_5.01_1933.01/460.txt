Love Cannot Be Auctioned (競賣), A Small Piece (小品)
						Snead Ogburn? (Original Author), Ki Rim (起林) (Translator)

They borrowed money to build a house.
They steadily paid it back, thinking the house would soon be theirs.
Then came—what they hadn't even dreamed of.
All their things were taken away because of what they couldn't pay back to the bank.
Despair became like all they had left.
Leaving only him, his wife, and their five young children.

But the wife—the wife said it wasn't so bad.
How wonderful it was that they were all alive.
"It's nothing. A house in the back alley isn't nothing, either."
"Let's start over like a groom and bride again."
"They can't auction off my love for you, can they?"
"Think of the love of our five children on top of that."
"Those folks took our house, but they couldn't make us shed tears."
"Perhaps this is how we bless each other."
"It's a thankful thing. We gained that over many years."
"It's a thankful thing. We lost it through no fault of our own."

So they roused themselves, worked, and faced their business.
And friends were warm-hearted, as friends should be.

Today I stopped before their gate—to soak in the warm spring sun and fragrant breeze—. <103>

**Summary**

This translated poem tells the story of a family losing their home and possessions due to inability to repay a bank loan. Despite this devastating material loss, the wife encourages her husband, emphasizing that their love for each other and their children cannot be taken away or "auctioned." They resolve to start anew, finding strength in their family bonds and the support of friends. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the economic hardships potentially faced by families in colonial Korea during the 1930s, a period marked by economic depression and colonial exploitation. The theme of resilience and prioritizing intangible values like love and family over material wealth offers a poignant commentary. Furthermore, the translation by Kim Ki-rim (Ki Rim), a prominent figure in Korean modernist poetry, highlights the engagement of Korean intellectuals with Western literature and themes relevant to their own societal context.

사랑은 競賣 못함니다, 小品
						스니-드오그번(原作), 起林(譯)
그들은 집을 짓기 위하야 돈을 꾸엇다.
그들은 이윽고 집은 내 것이 되겟지니하걀  착착 무러갓다.
왓다-그들이 꿈꾸지도 안턴 일이.
모-든 물건이 그들이 은행에 미처 무지 못한 것 때문에 가 버렷다.
落望이 그들의 가지고 잇는 전부와 가티 되엿다.
그와 그의 안해와 어린 아해들과 다섯만 남기고.
그러고 안해는-그러나 안해는 그러케 낫부지 안타고 말햇다.
그들이 모다 사러 잇다는 것은 얼마나 훌륭한 일이냐.
「아모 일도 업어요. 뒤ㅅ골목의 집도 일 업지 안어요.」
「우리는 다시 신랑과 신부로 출발하지요.
「그들은 당신에게 대한 나의 사랑까지 경매할 수는 업지 안어요.」
「그 우에 다섯 아이들의 사랑을 생각하게」
「그놈들은 우리 집을 빼이지만 눈물은 흘리게 못햇지요.」
「아마도 이러케 저이끼리 축복한 세음이지요.」
「고마운 일이얘요. 몃 해를 두고 그것을 어덧지요.」
「고마운 일이지요. 우리는 아모 잘못한 일 업시 그것을 버렷지.」

그래서 그들은 분발해서 일하면서 그들의 사업에 직면햇다.
그리고 동무들은 동무들답게 정다웟다.

오늘 나는 그들의 門前에 멈처섯다. 따스한 봄볏과 향그러운 바람을 쏘이려-. ＜103＞
