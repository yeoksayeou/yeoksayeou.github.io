Before the American Liberty Bell
						Li Hongzhang

In 1892, the old prime minister of China, Mr. Li Hongzhang, who had visited Washington, USA, saw the Liberty Bell Pavilion in Philadelphia and captured his boundless feelings before it in poetry. Now, on the first day of the new year, we recite that song. <6>

In my eyes, it seems to teach the symbol of freedom,
In my ears, it seems to designate the direction.
Only the ding-ding ringing sound is the sound of cast iron ringing.
And though it makes no sound now, still, thinking of that sound fills my heart.

Though the bell made of cast iron does not ring now,
It teaches me for the sake of freedom.
Sons of America, do not call the Liberty Bell an old thing.

From among all nations,
I came from the oldest nation,
And as a disciple of old philosophy,
I know well what this bell says.

Before this Earth was formed, many myriad centuries ago,
This bell spoke the wisdom of heaven.
It spoke what is in the word of God.
It rings and rings again.
Oh, ring until the very end. <7>
<6-7>

**Summary**

This article presents a poem attributed to the Chinese statesman Li Hongzhang, supposedly written during a visit to the Liberty Bell in Philadelphia in 1892 (though his visit was actually in 1896). The poem reflects on the bell as a symbol of freedom, whose message, though silent now, resonates with timeless, divine wisdom. For Korean colonial history, publishing this poem in 1933 is significant. It subtly introduces the powerful symbolism of American freedom, potentially encouraging readers under Japanese rule to contemplate liberty, using a respected historical figure from a neighboring country as a conduit. Historians might find it interesting as an example of indirect expression and the circulation of ideas about freedom and nationhood within the constraints of colonial censorship.

米國自由鍾아페서
						李鴻章
1892년, 米國 華盛頓을 방문하엿든 支那의 老宰相 李鴻章氏는 다시 필라델피아의 自由鍾閣을 보고 그 압헤서 無量한 感慨를 詩歌로 담엇다. 이제 새해 첫날에 그 노래를 읇기로한다.＜6＞

내 눈에는 자유의 符號를 가라처주는 것 갓고
내 귀에는 방향을 지정 해 주는 것 갓다.
오직 뗑뗑 울니는 소리만은 무쇠가 울리는 소리나 .
그리고 只今은 그 소리조차 나지 안으나 그래도 그 소리 생각고 맘이 꽉 찬다.

무쇠로 맨든 종이 지금은 울지안는다만은
자유를 위해서 나에게 가라처준다.
美國의 아들들아 自由鐘을 녯 것이라고 부르지말나.

나는 모-든 나라 가운데서
제일 오랜 나라에서부터 왓고
또 오랜 哲學의 弟子로서
이 鐘이 무엇이라고 말하는 것을 잘 안다.

이 地球가 생기기 前, 여러 萬世紀 前
하늘의 知惠를 이 종이 말햇다.
神의 말에 잇는 것을 말햇다.
울니고 또 울린다.
오, 끗까지 울어라.＜7＞
＜6-7＞
