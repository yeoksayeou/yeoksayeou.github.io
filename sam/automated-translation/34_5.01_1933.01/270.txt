Strategy for the Current Economic Movement, Proposal for Organizing a Self-Sufficiency Company
						By Sŏl T'aehŭi

1. The Necessity of Organizing a Self-Sufficiency Company (自給社)
It is something we all feel that the economy of the Korean people (朝鮮人) is flowing into recession moment by moment. And yet, it is also a responsibility we all share that we do not know what measures to take.
Recently, consumer cooperatives have been discussed from various quarters, and the cooperative movement has been advocated. Thus, although this movement has seen some degree of realization, it is regrettable that there is much dissatisfaction compared to what was hoped for and expected. Rather than gathering in the center [Seoul] and merely guiding the provinces through pen and tongue, I believe it is exceedingly necessary for each of us to initiate practical movements through local initiative. However, initiating a practical movement solely through pen and tongue is a very roundabout affair. Therefore, the organization of a financial foundation (財團組織) is discussed as a prerequisite issue. To this end, by organizing a company, to the best of our ability, with North and South Hamgyŏng Provinces (咸鏡南北道) as its area, so that this company may become the source of measures to retrieve the economic ruin of the Koreans living in North and South Hamgyŏng Provinces, the organization of the Hamgyŏng Self-Sufficiency Company (咸鏡自給社) is first necessary.

2. The Proposed Method and Its Rationale
The Self-Sufficiency Company intends to establish institutions (the location of the head office and branches is a separate discussion) in key locations such as Kyŏngsŏng, Wŏnsan, Hamhŭng, Sŏngjin, and Ch'ŏngjin to carry out the company's objectives, becoming an agency that handles consignment for the export and import of all kinds of goods consumed and produced within the entire province. Even when unavoidably purchasing goods from outside the region, it aims to transact directly with the manufacturers of products produced within the region, naturally, and avoid passing through intermediary merchants. Instead, transactions should go solely through the hands of the Self-Sufficiency Company to be directly supplied to local consumer cooperatives or cooperative associations. While fostering existing economic and production-promoting organizations is a matter of course, it intends to focus all efforts on organizing new rural stores (농촌상점), making villages more like cities to refuse and avoid urban exploitation. (Understanding regarding the establishment of rural stores will be discussed again in a separate section.) In this way, the entire region is to be fully organized through economic or production assistance, so that its every move breathes in unison, making the entire region function like a single physiological individual entity.

3. Method and Benefits of Organizing Rural Stores
Consumer cooperatives, by their nature and methods, are easy to establish among urban laborers, factory workers, employees of various companies, government and public officials, and others, regardless of type, who all live in occupational groups. While there's no reason they can't exist in rural areas, the living conditions, working conditions, and all other environmental factors make the activities of consumer cooperatives somewhat more difficult in rural areas compared to cities. Moreover,<80> in the illiterate Korean countryside, this new type of business, never tried before, uses terminology that is difficult even for the completely ignorant classes to understand. However, since even ignorant men and women (愚夫愚婦) commonly know that a store is inherently a place for making money and it is also something they aspire to, establishing a store in any village will likely be easily understood and agreed upon. Whether organized by *myŏn* (면, township) or by several *tong* (동, villages), this should be done appropriately according to the prosperity of the locality. The organization of these stores must absolutely be based on equal shares (平等服分), precisely because it is the originating (嚆矢) project for achieving the grand ideal of a balanced livelihood. For poor households unable to contribute the full amount, methods such as advance loans from the wealthy or other means like joint borrowing should be used to enable their investment, and the repayment (償却) should be covered by methods like allocating their due share of profit dividends. Once these stores are established, they become the consumer cooperative, the cooperative association, the joint purchasing cooperative, and the joint selling cooperative all at once, potentially achieving all strategies for rural revival in one go. Consequently, various cultural enlightenments and production promotions can be induced laterally:
(a) The influence of engaging closely with trade has the benefit of fostering a spirit of production promotion.
(b) The influence of engaging closely with trade has the benefit of fostering a spirit of providing economic common sense to the residents of that area.
(c) By unifying the channels for purchasing and selling, unnecessary purchases in daily life are controlled, and at the same time, the influence of valuing local products (土産輕重) has the benefit of becoming the key to preventing foreign products (外産) that cause us to perish.
(d) Seeing as trade in goods often introduces cultural exchange, rural stores certainly have a high probability (成算が確有) of promoting rural cultural enlightenment.
(e) Using these stores as a base, continuously conducting various kinds of training within the area to avoid having to go to the police substation or the *myŏn* office (remainder omitted in original source) would also be a significant achievement.
(f) If these stores are sustained, stores in each district can mutually connect, consolidating several districts to organize village finance cooperatives (촌락금융조합). By planning for financial convenience with urban banks or semi-governmental financial associations (半官營金組), achieving the smoothness (圓滿) of the rural economy, organizing production cooperatives, and initiating various other rural revival measures becomes entirely possible.

4. Outline of Business Operations
The business of the Self-Sufficiency Company is, of course, consignment of export/import goods from land and sea (物商客主 - goods merchant/commission agent). Although this consignment business is primarily an institution intended to engage in export/import consignment for economic and production-related organizations, since it is engaged in this field, it will also engage in other general consignment business as capabilities allow. The special characteristics of this company are explained below.
(a) The commission fees of this company will generally be comparatively low. For organized economic entities, a rebate based on their transaction volume cannot be given [TN: Text seems slightly corrupted or missing context, likely meant 'will be given' or has a negative structure like 'cannot fail to be given'] annually.
(b) Since the main purpose of this company is to prevent the economic ruin of Koreans in North and South Hamgyŏng Provinces and to plan for production promotion, it will naturally seek connections with various economic and production organizations within the province. Furthermore, it will eagerly promote (銳意促成) the opportune organization of rural stores. The methods are:
1. Establish facilities like training centers and preemptively train personnel to be employed by local economic or production institutions. Dispatch these personnel to the provinces to take charge of various organizational tasks, and also have them engage in respective duties as requested by existing organizations.
2. Especially for the organization of rural stores, provide financing for establishment funds, etc., according to the locality.
3. Encourage vocational education within the province, plan for the promotion of agriculture, fishing, forestry, and mining industries, and concurrently provide eager support (銳意應援) for acquiring the rights related to these.
4. Compile annual aggregate statistics for the province to serve as reference material for planning implementation.

5. Company's Capital Amount and Fundraising Method
For the company's capital, the more the better (多多益善), but given the current situation (以今事勢), it is regrettable that raising a large amount at once is difficult to achieve (難能). However, as the undertaking is grand, too small an amount <81> will make it utterly difficult to achieve even a fraction of the objective. Since the area spans both North and South provinces, if only the sincere effort (誠力) of the promoters exists, raising 40,000-50,000 shares might be possible. If it's 50,000 shares, the nominal capital amount is 2.5 million Won, and the first paid-in amount (辨納額) would be 625,000 Won. Although this is a small amount, it should be acceptable for the initial, foundational (張本的) business startup. Fundraising, of course, is based on ideology, but since it involves money, approaching those with funds is unavoidable. The wealthy should be well persuaded (慫慂) to participate favorably, as it is a public interest project for the province.

If this objective is carried out and rural stores proliferate in every county, township, and village within the province, the merchants traditionally engaged in commerce in each town and market will suffer a major blow. Since they are also our provincial people, the miserable state of their unemployment will surely be a problem. However, one cannot refrain from operating trains or freight trucks just to prevent the unemployment of oxcart and horse-cart drivers. It is an inevitable trend of human societal evolution that no one can deny. <82>

**Summary**

Sŏl T'aehŭi proposes the creation of a "Hamgyŏng Self-Sufficiency Company" (Hamgyŏng Chagŭpsa) covering North and South Hamgyŏng provinces to combat the deepening economic crisis faced by Koreans under Japanese rule. He argues that existing cooperative movements have been insufficient and advocates for a practical, locally-driven approach centered on this company. The company would act as a central agency for export/import consignment, dealing directly with producers and bypassing intermediary merchants to supply goods to local cooperatives and newly proposed "rural stores." These rural stores, organized on principles of equality, are envisioned as multi-functional hubs combining consumption, sales, finance, and cultural development to empower rural communities and counter urban exploitation. The author outlines fundraising strategies, acknowledging difficulties but suggesting a target capital, and addresses the potential negative impact on existing traditional merchants as an unavoidable consequence of progress.

From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects Korean efforts towards economic self-reliance and grassroots organization within the constraints of the colonial period. It highlights the awareness of economic marginalization ("economic ruin," "urban exploitation"), the desire to modernize rural areas ("making villages more like cities"), and the promotion of local products against imports ("preventing foreign products that cause us to perish"). The proposal for the "Chagŭpsa" and "rural stores" demonstrates practical strategies combining capitalist structures (company, shares, capital) with cooperative ideals (equal shares, community benefit, financial support for the poor) to address specific socio-economic problems faced by Koreans, particularly in the Hamgyŏng region. The mention of needing to persuade the wealthy and the acknowledgment of displacing traditional merchants provide insights into the social dynamics and challenges of such an initiative.

1. 自給社 組織의 必要
朝鮮人 경제가 刻一刻으로 불황에 흘너감은 人我共感하는 바이다. 그러면서도 所措를 罔知함도 또 人我共負할 책임이다.
저즘께 각 방면으로부터 소비조합을 운위하며 협동운동을 說道해왓다. 그리하야 이 운동의 多少實現도 업지 안엇스나 所期 所待와는 너무도 불만이 만흔 것이 유감이다. 우리가 중앙에 모혀와서 한갓 筆舌로써 지방을 지도하랴는 것보다는 각히 自지방적 솔선으로 실제운동을 이르키는 것이 못처럼 필요한 일이라고 생각된다. 그러자면 역시 단순한 筆舌로써 실제운동을 이리키기는 매우 迂遠한 일임으로 이에 財團組織이 선결문제라고 議及되는 바 이것을 咸鏡南北道를 구역으로 力所及대로 一會社를 조직하야 그 회사가 卽 咸鏡南北道에 사는 朝鮮人의 경제파멸 挽回의 策源이 되랴 함에는 咸鏡自給社의 조직이 먼저 필요한 것이다.

2. 策線되는 方法과 그 理由
自給社는 京城, 元山, 咸興, 城津, 淸津 등 각 요지에 그 회사의 목적을 실행하는 기관을 (本支部의 위치론은 別論)두어 全道內의 소비생산의 萬種물품의 수출수입하는 委托을 業하는 기관이되랴는 것이다. 不得己로써 域外물품을 구입하는 것이라도 域內 생산품의 製造先直接取引는 물론 중간상인을 경유치 말고 오직 자급사의 손을 거처서 지방 소비조합 又는 협동조합 등에 직접 출납이 되게 하랴는 것이다. 경제생산조장 旣成團體의 조장은 물론이려니와 새로운 농촌상점조직을 전력하야 농촌이 도시화하야 도시착취를 拒免케 하랴는 것이다. (농촌상점이 설치에 관한 양해는 別項에다 다시 논함)이리하야 전 지방을 경제 又는 생산의 조력으로 총 조직하야 그 一動一靜이 모다 일률적 호흡으로 一 지방체가 生理的 個人體計를 되게 하랴는 것이다.

3. 農村商店組織 方法 及 効益
소비조합은 그 성질 及 방편상 , 도시노동자, 공장직공 각종회사원, 관공직원, 기타, 某某종을 물론하고 모다 직업적 집단생활을 하는 자에게 하기 용이하다. 농촌이라고 못될 이유는 업지마는 그 생활상태라던지 작업상태 등 모든 환경이 농촌에서는 소비조합활동이 도시에서의 그것보다는 좀 하기 어려운 점이 만타. 더욱이＜80＞ 문맹인 조선농촌에서 그리고 해보지 못하던 신사업이 캄캄한 무식계급에서 알어들끼조차 어려운 術語임에랴. 상점이라면 의례 돈버는 곳인줄 아는 것은 愚夫愚婦라도 다 아는 상식인 동시에 또  선망하는 일이기 때문에 어떤 농촌이던지 상점설치는 容易히 理解贊成할 것이다. 一面을 단위로 하던지 數洞을 단위로 하던지 이것은 지방의 盛否를 따라 適宜히 할 것이다. 이 상점의 조직은 절대로 平等服分으로 하여야 되는 것은 다름아니라 원대한 이상인 平衡생활을 營하는 嚆矢사업으로 되는 까닭이다. 그 출자에 미흡한 貧戶의 것은 適宜한 방법으로 有産者先貸 又는 기타 共同債入과 갓흔 방편을 써서 출자케 하고 그 償却은 所當服分의 이익배당 등으로써 충당케하는 방법을 써야 된다. 이 상점이 한번 일어나고 보면 소비조합이 곳 이것이며 협동조합이 곳 이것이며 공동구매조합이 곳 이것이며 공동판매조합이 곳 이것이어서 모든 농촌부흥의 策이 가히 一氣畢得인 것이다. 따라서 측면으로 種種의 문화계발과 생산발흥을 유도할 수 잇스니

4. 營業의 槪要
自給社의 영업은 물론 海陸産 수출입물품 위탁업이다.(物商客主) 이 위탁업은 주로 경제 及 생산적 단체의 수출입위탁에 종사하랴는 기관이지마는 旣斯道에 종사하는 바에는 능력대로 기타 일반적 위탁업에도 종사할 것이다. 본회사의 특색으로는 아래 여러 가지 설명을 附함.
(가) 본 회사의 수수료는 일반적으로 비교적 廉케 할 것이오. 조직된 경제적 단체에 그 取引高에 대하야 연도마다  割反을 못함
(나) 본 회사는 주목적이 咸鏡南北道의 조선인 경제파멸을 방지하며 생산발흥을 企圖하랴   는 것임으로 도내 諸種 경제적 단체와 생산적 단체와 연락을 圖함은 물론이오 나아가       서 농촌상점조직의 機宜를 銳意促成함  그 방법은
1. 강습소 갓흔 것을 설치하고 지방의 경제기관 又는 생산기관에 수용될 인원 양성을 선행하야 그 업무 그 인원을 지방에 派送하야 각종 조직의 임무에 當케하며 또 기성단체의 요구가 잇는대로 그 각기 임무에 종사케 함
2. 특히 농촌상점조직에는 設立資金融資 갓흔 것을 지방을 따라서 행함
3. 道內에 실업교육을 장려하고 농,어,림,광업 諸産業의 진흥을 策하며 아울너 此의 이권 획득에 銳意應援함
4. 연도마다 도내총계표를 작성하야 수행계획에 참고재료를 作함

5. 會社의 資金數量 及 募集方法
회사자금은 多多益善이지마는 以今事勢로 일시에 거액 모집은 難能함이 유감이다. 그러나 事가 거창한 만콤 너무 소액으로는＜81＞ 도저히 목적수행의 만일에도 이르기 어려웁다. 구역이 남북도에 亙햇스니 발기인의 誠力만 잇스면 즉 4,5만株의 모집은 가능할가 싶으다. 5만주라면 공칭자금액이 250만원이오.  제1회 辨納額이 625,000원이니 이것이 소액이지만 張本的으로 創初사업이 이만하기로 괜찬을 것이다. 모집은 물론 사상이 토대이지마는 금전인 관계상 有金者를 접근치 안을 수 업스니 유산자에게 도내공익사업이니 찬성적으로 응모토록 잘 慫慂하여야 될 것이다.
