愛人
						張德祚
Saturday afternoon—
Receiving the chilly wind of the waning year all over her body, Insuk was swaying in the tram heading towards Tongdaemun.
—I'm always the one who gets there first; today, I should make him wait a bit— A ripple of a smile pulled her pointed lips even more pointedly.
"La la— la la la, la..."
After pulling up the collar of her overcoat once, she rehearsed Chopin's No. 30, which she had started practicing today, with her right fingertips.
Among the days of the week, Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday afternoons were days for piano practice. On each of those days, <94> it had been a long-standing custom for Insuk to stop by the house of the Western teacher R near the entrance of Chŏngdong on her way from school.
However, about two months ago, the repertoire suddenly became difficult, and she had begun to complain, with sighs, to her older brother, who was now at university, that she couldn't manage unless the practice time was extended by more than two hours.
"Hmph, really—" And each time, as if understanding the content of her seemingly pitiful sigh, her brother would dismiss it with a wry laugh.

After getting off the tram in front of Ch'anggyŏngwŏn, Insuk suddenly noticed the sound of her shoes quickening without her realizing it.
"Aigo, gosh, I hate this!" Meaningless laughter welled up, and she shifted the book bag containing sheet music and a couple of textbooks, which she had been holding in her left arm, to her right hand. Several K University students, wearing their intentionally tilted caps askew, passed by her, chattering loudly.
Entering through the side gate of Ch'anggyŏngwŏn and keeping the zoo to her left, after descending and ascending a gentle slope for a while, she arrived in front of the botanical garden.
The familiar old pine tree between the botanical garden and the greenhouse— she was now walking towards it as her landmark.
"Aigo, he's not here yet!"
It was a little past 3:40.
—The appointment was for 3:30...
In the sorrow of having her trusting feelings unexpectedly trampled, she let out a long sigh.
—Still, that can't be right— Having been disappointed once, she regained hope and sat down leaning against the tree roots.
5 minutes, 10 minutes, the wristwatch pointed to 4 o'clock.
"Gosh, what could be the matter!" Insuk nervously strained her ears towards the space between the bare trees. But only the distant shouts of middle school students skating could be heard! Only the sound of the wind sharply struck her earlobes.
"Hoo—" She sighed a second time and cast her gaze to the sky. The winter sky, clouded over in ash-gray, held its breath as if asleep, filled with cotton-like clouds.
When they met on Thursday— Chŏnggyu's melancholy expression, as if about to cry, suddenly floated up onto that sky.
—Could he have some worry? That can't be right. Then? Still, he wouldn't just break a promise like this— Among her brother's friends who visited Insuk's house, Chŏnggyu, the most diligent and cheerful one— for some reason, she was drawn to him.
A few months later, they had become companions who met there without fail on every piano practice day.
—Something must have happened, something must have happened— Her heart pounding, she scanned the bare trees with damp eyes. Standing on tiptoe, she listened intently towards the bottom of the hill. She sat there until her back grew numb from the cold wind.
But the figure of the tall young man wearing a university cap pulled down to his eyebrows did not appear, even one hour, even two hours after the promised time.
When the frowning sky darkened, Insuk finally turned her heavy steps away, carrying a heart as tumultuous as a field battered by hail.

"Hey, that must be mine, is it a Christmas present?"
"No! I told you it's not!" Insuk, flustered to an amusing degree, clutched the jacket she was knitting into her arms and buried her face over it.
A ball of brilliant "blue" yarn rolled off her lap.
"This New Year, I'll have to show off wearing a jacket hand-knitted by a schoolgirl. Anyway, thanks." That day, her older brother Chinsik teased Insuk, who had returned late from school, holding her fast. <95>
"Seriously, Oppa! I told you this isn't yours!"
"Then, if it's not mine, what kind of man's item are you knitting so diligently? Huh?"
"I don't know!" She finally got annoyed.
"Hmph, I wondered what had gotten into our lazybones... Then whose is it? Confess. Huh?"
"Confess... ..." Insuk's long eyelashes avoided her brother's gaze.
"Uh-huh, I knew it. It's for that fellow Chŏnggyu... ..."
After a moment, Chinsik looked at his sister as if convinced. And his expression gradually took on a shadow of sympathy, of pity.
"Graduation isn't far off, but he's an unlucky fellow! We already knew he was involved with the XX Party (XX당), but the night before last, he was finally dragged away—"
He quietly recounted how Chŏnggyu had already gotten quite deeply involved in "that movement" (그 운동).
"And you too, look towards your own path, okay? Having a lover with whom you cannot only share pleasures..."
Brother Chinsik finished his words like this.
"Yes." Clutching the knitting needles tightly in both hands, Insuk felt her heart calm down in a way that surprised even herself.
At the same time, it felt as if she had clearly seen the whole picture of Chŏnggyu, which she hadn't known before.
His melancholy, his anguish, and the reason he broke the promise the day before yesterday—
"Mr. Chŏnggyu! Don't worry. When Insuk sees you again, she too will have cultivated herself no less." Suppressing the urge to cry out, she began to move the needles quickly again for her lover who must be trembling in a cold place right now. <96>

**Summary**

Insuk, a young woman, eagerly anticipates her regular meeting with Chŏnggyu at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn after her piano lesson. When he fails to appear even hours after the agreed time, she grows worried and disappointed. Later, her older brother, Chinsik, reveals the reason for Chŏnggyu's absence: he was arrested two nights prior due to his involvement with the "XX Party" and "that movement." Understanding dawns on Insuk, explaining Chŏnggyu's recent melancholy and the broken appointment. Instead of despairing, she resolves to better herself while he is away, continuing to knit a jacket for him with renewed determination.

This story offers a glimpse into the lives of educated urban youth during the Japanese colonial period in Korea. Historians might find interesting the portrayal of seemingly ordinary activities like piano lessons and courtship intertwined with the underlying political tension and risks associated with nationalist or socialist activism (obliquely referred to as "that movement" and the "XX Party," likely due to censorship). The story subtly highlights the personal sacrifices and anxieties stemming from political involvement under colonial rule, showing how it impacted personal relationships and future prospects (like Chŏnggyu's impending graduation).
