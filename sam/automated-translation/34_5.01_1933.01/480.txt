Snowflakes on the Tree Branch, Poem
						Hwang Sŏk-u

Snowflakes covering the tree branches
They are flowers with beautiful gazes and glances
Of white pupils—.

Snowflakes covering the tree branches
They are flowers with beautiful lips (입설) and smiles (우슴)
Of white teeth (니ㅅ톨)—.

Snowflakes covering the tree branches
They are flowers with quiet breath and harmony.
Of white breasts (젓 가슴)—

Their blood-filled (담킨) hearts are also white
Their slender (가느른) bone marrow (骨髓) is also white
Their souls are also white.

Snowflakes on the tree branch
They are sacred flowers of water blooming (픠는) amid the cold wind
They are flowers that eat the bright (밝안) sunlight (해ㅅ빛) and beautifully (고흡게) lose their lives (목슴). <105>

**Summary**

This poem by Hwang Sŏk-u portrays snowflakes on tree branches as pure, white flowers. It uses imagery associated with human beauty—eyes, lips, smile, breath, breasts—to describe the snowflakes' delicate appearance. The poem emphasizes the pervasive whiteness, extending it to the snowflakes' core (heart, marrow, soul), symbolizing ultimate purity. It concludes by highlighting their ephemeral nature as "sacred flowers of water" that bloom in the cold wind but vanish beautifully under sunlight. From a historical perspective, the poem's intense focus on the purity and transient beauty of nature (snow), presented in lyrical Korean despite the colonial context, might subtly reflect an inward turn towards appreciating native aesthetics or perhaps an escapist longing for purity amidst challenging times. The poem's older Korean orthography is also valuable for linguistic historians studying language evolution during this period.

나무가지 우의 눈송이, 詩歌
						黃錫禹
나무가지 우에 덮인 눈송이
그는 눈찌와 시선이 고흔 꽃들이다
하얀 눈동자의-.

나무가지 우에 덮인 눈송이
그는 입설과 우슴이 고흔 꽃들이다
하얀 니ㅅ톨의-.

나무가지 우에 덮인 눈송이
그는 숨결이 고요하고 화기로운 꽃들이다.
하얀 젓 가슴의-

그들은 그 피 담킨 심장도 희고
그 가느른 骨髓도 희고
그 혼도 희다.

나무가지 우의 눈송이
그들은 찬바람 가운데 픠는 물의 성스런 꽃
그들은 밝안 해ㅅ빛을 먹고 목슴 고흡게 사라지는 꽃. ＜105＞
