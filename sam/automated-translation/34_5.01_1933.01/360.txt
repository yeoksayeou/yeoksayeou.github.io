Wind Sounds
						Kim Chahye

It is a cold night, and from outside, only the sound of the wind can be heard. *Swaa!* It blows, then rustles (*sareureuk*), then falls silent as if cut off, then the *sareureuk* sound is heard again along with the sound of tree leaves.

The sound of the wind, weeping alone through the quiet night. How desolate a sound it must be! How heart-wrenching a sound!

The wind that blows, cruelly plucking off the withered leaves clinging tightly to the gaunt, dry tree branches! The wind that runs along, fiercely scratching with hands cold as ice at the last cosmos blooming in the corner of the yard! The wind that rushes in, freezing the teardrops of the pitiful beggars huddled at the street corner! Just thinking of it makes my heart shudder.

When that wind sound and the dry leaves rolling in the corner of the yard rustle (*sagak*) together, I dislike hearing that sound so much that I sometimes cover my ears tightly with both hands.

However, depending on how one listens, there are also strange times when that sound is heard very affectionately. The sound as it passes by, making the paper door coverings flap (*paldak paldak*), seems as if it's delivering the whispers of a dear person, so I sometimes listen closely. Thinking of it roaming about, its wide coattails fluttering endlessly, it seems perhaps as if it has gathered up the beloved breaths of those far away, and I feel like rushing out into the *swaa*-blowing gust of wind and breathing it deep into my heart. Perhaps because autumn is a time when one misses those who have departed... ...

The *sagak sagak* blowing wind sounds like the footsteps of a missed loved one, the *paldak paldak* blowing wind sounds like the rustling hem of someone with no appointment, and the *hododok* blowing wind is like the sound of a whistle heard as if in a dream. As I sit intoxicated by these various sounds, this sound and that sound, the wind inevitably makes my heart weep.

While listening to the wind and sighing, the autumn night just keeps growing deeper. <94>

**Summary**

This lyrical essay by Kim Chahye describes the sounds of the wind on a cold night, likely in late autumn or early winter. The author personifies the wind, initially hearing its sounds as desolate and harsh, associated with dying nature and the suffering of the poor. However, the wind's sounds also evoke feelings of affection and longing, reminding the author of absent loved ones and sounding like whispers, footsteps, or whistles. The essay captures a melancholic mood, reflecting on loneliness and memory as the night deepens. For historians, the piece offers a glimpse into personal, introspective writing by a Korean woman during the colonial period. Its focus on sensory experience and emotion, tinged with sadness and longing, might reflect a subtle turning inward or a form of quiet expression amidst the political realities of the time, contrasting with more overtly nationalistic or political literature.

바람소리
						金慈惠
찬 밤인데 박게서는 바람소리만 들녀 온다. 쏴! 불다가는 사르륵대고 끈친 듯이 고요하다가는 나무닙 소리와 함께 또다시 사르륵 소리가 들닌다.
고요한 밤을 혼자 울녀 놋는 바람소리. 얼마나 쓸쓸한 소리랴! 얼마나 안타가운 소리랴!
앙상하게 말른 나무가지를 꼭 붓늘고 스러지는 초다한 나무닙들을 매정스럽게 똑똑 떠러트리며 부러오는 바람! 마당귀에 마주막 핀 코쓰모쓰를 어름가치 찬 손으로 매섭게 갈리며 다라오는 바람!  길모퉁이에 웅성그리고 안젓는 가엽슨 거지애들의 눈물 방울을 얼니며 달겨드는 바람! 생각만 해도 가슴이 옷삭해지는 소리다.
그 바람소리와 마당귀에 구는 마른 나무닙들이 가치 사각땔 때에는 그 소리가 듯기 실혀 두 손으로 귀를 꼭 막기도 한다.
그러나 어떠케 들으면 그 소리가 몹시도 다정하게 들니는 야릇한 때도 잇다. 문풍지를 팔닥팔닥 떨면서 지나가는 그 소리는 맛치도 정다운 이의 귀속말을 전해다 주는 듯도 십허 가만히 귀를 기우리기도 한다. 그 넓은 옷자락을 한 업시 날니고 도라단이는 것을 생각하면 혹시 멀니 떠나 잇는 이들의 그립은 숨결이나 폭 싸가지고 온 듯 십허 쏴-부는 바람결 속으로 뚜처나가 가슴속 깁히 그 바람을 듸려쉬고도 십허진다. 가을은 떠난 이들이 그리운 때라 그런지... ...
사각사각. 부는 바람은 그리운 이의 발자최 소리 갓기도 하고 팔닥팔닥 부는 바람은 약속도 업는 이의 옷자락 소리 갓기도 하며 호도독 부는 바람은 꿈결가치 들니는 휫바람 소리와도 가터서 이 소리 저 소리 가지각색 소리에 취해 안젓느라면 기여고 그 바람은 내 마음을 울녀 눗코야 만다.
바람소리 들어가며 한숨쉬는 동안 가을밤은 작고 기퍼만 간다. ＜94＞
