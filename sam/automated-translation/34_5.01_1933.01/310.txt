Our Fond Emotion, the Willow Flute

The willow flute—when the willow trees draw water, when spring comes, new sprouts emerge, and a white haze drifts over the distant mountains, we play this willow flute.

When spring comes, we children
Make flutes from willow branches
Put them to our lips and play
La la la, it's so fun
When spring comes, we
Break off royal azalea flowers
Build Mt. Kŭmgang with them
Playing house is fun
When spring comes, we
Butterflies, butterflies fly
When the bluebird sings
La la la, it's so fun

Thus we sing and play.<86>

**Summary**

The article describes the traditional Korean children's activity of making and playing willow flutes (버들피리) in the springtime. It includes the lyrics of a simple song children sing while playing, mentioning activities like gathering azaleas to build a miniature Mt. Kŭmgang and enjoying the sights and sounds of spring like butterflies and bluebirds. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it highlights a simple, idyllic aspect of Korean folk culture and childhood during the Japanese colonial period. It evokes a sense of nostalgia and attachment to traditional Korean sentiment (情調) and nature, potentially serving as a subtle assertion of cultural identity amidst colonial pressures.
