Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제1호
Publication Date: 1933년 01월 01일
Article Title: 숩속에 심근 꼿, 시 (A Flower Planted in the Forest, Poem)
Author: 石友
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

숩속에 심근 꼿, 시
						石友

1.
香花豈植叢林中   향긔러운 꼿을 엇지하야 말은 숩풀 가운데 심엇는고
種者非耶折者非   심은자 그르냐? 꺽는자 그르냐?

Why was the fragrant flower (香花) planted amidst the withered thicket (叢林中)?
Is the planter (種者) wrong? Or is the picker (折者) wrong?

2.
關山楊柳錄如絲   관산에 푸른버들은 실 갓흔데
願以佳連甘結情   원컨대 가연을 달게 정으로 맺고자 하노라.

The green willows (楊柳) at the border pass (關山) are like silk threads (絲);
I wish to sweetly bind this fine connection (佳連) with affection (情).

3.
石工放心不啄石   석공아 마암노아 돌을 쫏지마러라
我謙其中破順玉   내 그 가운데 순한 옥이 바서질가 염녀한다.

Stonemason (石工), carelessly do not strike the stone;
I fear that the gentle jade (順玉) within might break (破).

4.
秋家庭前霜葉落   가을 집압헤 서리닙이 떠러지니
是於君疑曳履聲   이 그대의 신 끄으는 소랜가 의심하노라.＜102＞

Frosty leaves (霜葉) fall before the autumn house (秋家庭前);
I wonder if this is the sound of your dragging shoes (曳履聲).<102>

**Summary**

This poem, attributed to the pen name Sŏgu (石友), consists of four quatrains expressing themes of misplaced beauty, longing for connection, cautiousness towards fragility, and anticipation of a loved one. The verses use natural imagery (flower in the woods, willows, stone with jade, falling autumn leaves) to convey subtle emotions, possibly related to love or a cherished but precarious situation. For historians, the poem offers insight into literary expression during the colonial period, showcasing the continued use of Hanja alongside vernacular Korean (with older orthography) in poetic forms. The themes of fragility, misplaced value, and longing might subtly reflect the anxieties or sentiments of the era, though expressed in a personal, lyrical style rather than overt political commentary.
