Magazine Title: 三千里 제5권 제1호
Publication Date: 1933년 01월 01일
Article Title: 새해의 맹세 (New Year's Pledge)
Author: 許憲, 權東鎭, 金若水, 韓龍雲
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing/Essay)

새해의 맹세
許憲

萬事皆以誠爲之則 何謨不遂何事不成
(If one does all things with sincerity, what plan will not succeed, what task will not be accomplished?)

權東鎭

새 精神으로 살자. ＜8＞

(Let's live with a new Spirit. <8>)

金若水

今年에는 最小限度이나마 우리의 運動方面에 잇서서 實行의 깃붐을 보고십슴니다

(This year, I wish to see, at least to a minimum degree, the joy of execution (實行) in the aspect of our Movement (運動方面).)

韓龍雲

닥처오는대로 일 합시다. ＜9＞

(Let's work as things come upon us. <9>)
＜8-9＞

**Summary**

This short article presents New Year's resolutions for 1933 from four prominent Korean figures: Hŏ Hŏn, Kwon Tong-jin, Kim Yak-su, and Han Yong-un. Their pledges emphasize sincerity, renewal, practical action, and a desire for tangible results, particularly concerning "our movement," reflecting the aspirations and perhaps cautious optimism of Korean intellectuals and activists during the Japanese colonial period. Historians might find Kim Yak-su's specific mention of seeing "the joy of execution in the aspect of our Movement" of particular interest, as it hints at ongoing efforts and hopes for progress within nationalist or social movements despite the restrictive colonial context.
