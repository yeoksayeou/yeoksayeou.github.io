新年文藝 (New Year Literary News)

The 1932 Nobel Prize in Literature (文學賞), which had been predicted to go to Germany's Stefan George (게오르그스테판), France's Valéry (발레라), Noailles (노아이유), or Jammes (쟘므), ultimately went (떠러젓다) to England's John Galsworthy (존·골스워디). <103>

**Summary**

This short news item reports that the 1932 Nobel Prize in Literature was awarded to John Galsworthy of England, mentioning other contenders from Germany and France (Stefan George, Paul Valéry, Anna de Noailles, Francis Jammes) who were expected to win. From a historical perspective, this piece highlights the awareness and interest within colonized Korea regarding international cultural events like the Nobel Prize during the 1930s. It demonstrates a connection to global literary trends despite the political situation under Japanese rule.
