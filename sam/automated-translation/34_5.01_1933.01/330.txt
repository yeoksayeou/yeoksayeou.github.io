黃金行進曲
						片石村

Let's take the Hamgyŏng Line train for a moment.
Then you hear all sorts of grand stories, like someone bought land in Najin and made several hundred thousand won at once, or someone else whose land left neglected by the Sŏngjin seaside suddenly saw its price soar, so they've already torn down their shack and started constructing a three-story Western-style house. It is indeed true that the nerves of all the residents in this region are certainly in a state of excitement.

However, while such welcome news is heard on one side, on the other side, there is an excessively painful contrast.
A certain Kim, who lives in Ch'ŏngjin, believing only that Ch'ŏngjin would become the XX Line Terminal Port, scattered a huge fortune (累巨萬) of gold across the Susŏng Plain buying up millions of p'yŏng of land, dreaming his own "Rothschild" dream. But then, reportedly, upon receiving a telegram stating that Najin had become the terminal port instead, he took to his sickbed for three days, continuously calling out "Terminal Port, Terminal Port," <90> and then passed away to the other world.

Is that all? A sand dune left abandoned on the Najin coast suddenly commanded a price of 400,000 or 500,000 wŏn. Because of this, a pitiable employee of the X Bureau (X廳 雇員), earning a monthly salary of merely 30 wŏn, agonized intensely for a whole week over how to handle this huge sum of money, which so vastly transcended the limits of his daily abacus calculations (日常算盤), and eventually went mad.

At this point, the mischief of "Mammon" (만모쓰), in its cruelty and splendor, rivals the Geneva League Play (제네빠의 聯盟劇) in entertainment value, now exceeding 100 percent.

I recall being surprised once hearing the story of a strange cyclist who snatched a gold hairpin (금비녀) from the head of a noblewoman walking down the main street of Chongno in broad daylight. More recently, a Japanese newspaper carried a story about a fake dentist (假者 치과의사) who took an innocent housewife's gold tooth (金齒), promising to fix it, and then vanished without a trace.

It has reached a point where one must insure even the gold tooth hidden beyond the lips, which falls within the complete control domain of one's own brain (腦髓), and cannot walk safely concerning the hairpin stuck in one's hair without extending a security perimeter (경비선) even a few minutes' distance around the head. The world cannot but be called extremely unhealthy.

This story is by no means confined only to the East. <91> It is said that young people, flocking from the East in search of the Seven Cities of "Cibola" (기볼라) and "Gran Quivira" (그란퀴벨라) – once thought by scientists to belong only to myth – are currently raising the price of meals in Mexico (墨西哥).

Long ago, "Coronado" (코로나도) too was deceived by an Indian guide and failed while wandering in search of "Cibola" and "Gran Quivira." But anyway, according to legend, in that country, even the food bowls are all made of pure gold. This was enough to provoke the ambition of young people in "America" (아메리카) who were struggling at the bottom of the extreme depression of 1932.

Just like "In search of Mecca" (멕카를 차저서) or "In search of Jerusalem" (예루살렘을 차저서), "In search of Gold" (黃金을 차저서) is the slogan of the disciples of "Mammon" (만모쓰). Tragedy already began together with human history. Keeping time with the gold march resounding chaotically here and there across the land, what kind of new Age of Gold Rhapsody/Frenzy (黃金狂詩代) is 1933 about to unfold? <92>
<90-92>

**Summary**

The article describes a "gold fever" sweeping parts of northern Korea, particularly around Najin and Ch'ŏngjin, fueled by speculation related to Japanese development plans for ports and railways (the Hamgyŏng Line). It contrasts stories of sudden wealth with tragic tales of ruin and madness caused by the volatile speculation. The author laments this "gold march" as a sign of moral decay, citing examples of theft (gold hairpins, gold teeth) in Korea and Japan, and linking it to a global phenomenon of greed, referencing historical searches for legendary cities of gold like Cibola and Gran Quivira. The piece ends on a foreboding note, questioning what new "Age of Gold Rhapsody/Frenzy" 1933 will bring. For historians, the article vividly illustrates the socio-economic impact of Japanese colonial development projects on Korean society, highlighting the intense speculation, psychological stress, and social disruption experienced by Koreans caught up in the rapid, externally driven changes. It also reflects a contemporary critique of materialism and moral decline perceived during the period.

그러면 누구는 羅津에 땅을 삿가가 몃 십만원을 한꺼번에 버럿다는 둥 누구는 城津 바다가에 내버려 두엇든 땅이 갑작이 시세가 조와저서 벌서 오막사리를 허러 버리고 3층 양옥을 起工한다는 둥 엇전지 이야기가 모다 큼직큼직하다. 의 지방의 모-든 주민의 신경이 확실이 흥분상태에 잇다는 것은 사실이다.

여기에 이르러 「만모쓰」의 작난은 그 잔인성과 華麗에 잇어서 「제네빠」의 聯盟劇에 지지안케 흥행가치 바야흐로 超 100「퍼-센트」다.
언젠가 白晝의 종로 대로를 거러가는 귀부인의 머리에서 금비녀를 빼서 가지고 다라난 괴상한 자전차수의 이야기를 듯고 놀란 일이 잇더니 近者의 日本新聞에는 순진한 엇던 주부의 금이(金齒)를 고처 주마고 가지고 간 후 일거에 소식이 頓絶한 假者 치과의사의 이야기가 실려 잇엇다.
자신의 腦髓의 완전한 통율범위에 속한 입술기의 저편에 감춘 금이(金齒)에까지 보험을 부처야 하며 머리의 近處數分의 공간에까지 경비선을 느리지 안코는 머리에 꼬친 비녀에 대하야 안심하고 보행할 수 없슬 지경이니 세상은 지극히 건전치 못하다고 아니할 수 없다.

오래인 녜전에 「코로나도」도 印度人의 안내자에게 속혀서 「기볼라」와 「그란퀴벨라」를 차저헤매다가 실패한 일이 잇지만 엇재든 전설에 의하면 그 나라에서는 음식그릇까지가 모다 純金으로 되여 잇다니 1932년의 극도의 불황의 저층에서 헐덕이고 잇던 「아메리카」의 젊은이들의 야심을 도발하기에 넉넉한 것이엇다.
