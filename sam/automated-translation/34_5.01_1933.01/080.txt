Our Nostalgic Sentiment, Tug-of-War

This was a New Year's game traditionally popular in various regions south of Kyŏnggi Province since ancient times, but it was mostly played on the 15th or 16th day of the first lunar month, the day of the Great Full Moon (Taeborŭm).

A village divides into East and West teams, and each household gathers straw (집) to twist a thick rope (바). Then, all the villagers, regardless of gender, come out to pull this rope. This rope has a male (雄) rope and a female (雌) rope; the East is called male, and the West is called female. It is said that the winning side will not get sick for a year and will also have an abundant harvest, so everyone puts forth all their strength, truly deciding the match desperately.

Also, in some regions, one county (面) or district (郡) divides into East and West to compete, but at such times, some injuries occur.

Although many such customs are disappearing, this tug-of-war (줄대리) is still prevalent. This tug-of-war is quite a rural and popular (대중뎍) game with significance. To begin it, like a hundred thousand troops setting up camp, they divide into East and West, arrange their lines, and at the head, they erect large and small flags (긔) with big characters written on them, such as "Certain Victory" (전필승) or "Victory Flag" (승전긔). They ring gongs (묵증), sing, dance, and display their morale to each other, truly overflowing with a brave spirit.

In the coastal regions of Kyŏngsang Province and near Taegu, they sing a song called "Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane" (쾨지나 칭칭나네) during the tug-of-war. It is said to be a song sung by both children and adults, passed down from the time of the Imjin War in the old days. The song is recorded below. From "Chosŏn's Annual Events" by Mr. O Ch'ŏng (吳晴氏).

There are many stars in the sky
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
There are many pebbles on the beach
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
There are many pinecones in the forest
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
There are many verses in the song
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
Where the farmer played, isn't a straw shoe (집신짝) dropped?
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
Where the young bride (시악씨) played, isn't a ribbon (당기) dropped?
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
Where grandfather rested, isn't a tobacco pipe (담배통) dropped?
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane
Where grandmother rested, isn't a pair of spectacles (안경짝) dropped?
Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane <21>

**Summary**

This 1933 article describes the traditional Korean folk game of *chuldarigi* (tug-of-war), typically played around the first full moon (Taeborŭm). It details how villages divide into East (male) and West (female) teams, the belief that winning brings good fortune and health for the year, and the large scale and spirited atmosphere of the event, complete with flags and music. The article notes the game's persistence despite other customs fading and mentions a specific song, "Koaejina ch'ingch'ing nane," sung during *chuldarigi* in the Kyŏngsang region, believed to originate from the Imjin War. For historians, the article is interesting as it documents a specific folk tradition during the Japanese colonial period, highlighting its communal and regional aspects, perceived connection to historical events (Imjin War), and the use of older Korean orthography, reflecting cultural practices and language of the time. The emphasis on its "rural" and "popular" nature suggests an interest in preserving or documenting native Korean folk culture.

그리운 우리 情調, 줄다리기
이것은 정월노리로써 고래로부터 경기이남 각지에서 성행하는 것이엿스나 모다들 정월 십오륙일 즉 대보름날 한 것이다.
한 부락이 동서로 난누어저 각 집에서 집흘모아 굴근 바를 꼰다. 그리하야 그 마을의 사람들은 남녀 물론하고 모다 나와서 이 줄을  잡아 다니는 것이다. 이 줄에는 수(雄)줄 암(雌)줄이 잇서 동은 수, 서는 암이라고 불으며 이긴 쪽은 일년동안 병에도 안걸니며, 또 한해 농사도 풍작이라는 말이 잇슴으로 모다 자기의 힘을 다하야 그야말로 필사적으로 승부를 결한다.
또 어느 지방에는 일면 또는 일군군이 동서로 갈나저 싸호는 것도 잇스나 이때는 약간에 부상자가 난다.
모든 이런 풍속이 업서지지만은 이 줄대리만은 아직도 성행한다. 이 줄대리기는 퍽 농천뎍이며 대중뎍의 의의를 가진 노리의 하나다. 이것을 시작하려면 십만대병이 진을 치듯이 동서로 갈나 열을 정리하고 진두에는 전필승이라든가, 승전긔라든가 크다란 글자를 쓴 대소의 긔를 세우고 묵증을 울니며 노래하며 춤추며 서로의 의기를 보히느라고 실로 용감한 기분이 넘처 흘은다.
경상도 해안지방 及 대구부근에는 줄대리기할 때 쾨지나 칭칭나네라는 노래를 한다. 아이도 어른도 모다 부르는 노래로 전하는 말에는 그 옛날 임진란리때에 일어난 노래라한다. 이 아래 그 노래를 적기로 한다. 吳晴氏著「朝鮮의 年中行事에서」
