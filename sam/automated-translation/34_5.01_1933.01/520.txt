Magazine Title: 삼천리 (三千里)
Publication Date: 1933년 01월 01일
Article Title: 燦爛, 現代英詩壇 (Brilliant, The Modern English Poetry Scene)
Author: J. G. 프렛처
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

1.
Blue light color light blue light- sky sand sea-
I grow infinitely like its edge.
I shall run to the endless water's edge.
I shall shout at the crashing foam.
I shall try to touch the sky with my fingers.
Happiness is like this sand-.
I let the sand flow from my hand.

2.
Clouds pass by.
Over the sky like a polished mirror-
Clouds pass by. Like grey cotton.
There are no stars. <106>

Clouds pass quietly―
Suddenly a solitary star twinkles.
The night is cold and the clouds quietly
Ripple across the sky. <107>
<106-107>

**Summary**

The article presents two short poems by the American Imagist poet J. G. Fletcher. The first poem uses imagery of the sea, sand, and sky to convey feelings of vastness, infinity, and the transient nature of happiness. The second poem depicts clouds moving across the night sky, initially obscuring the stars, but eventually revealing a single, twinkling star, evoking a sense of quiet solitude and coldness. From a historical perspective, the publication of contemporary American poetry, specifically by an Imagist poet like Fletcher, in a major Korean magazine like *Samch'ŏlli* in 1933 is significant. It demonstrates the engagement of Korean intellectuals with international literary movements, even under colonial rule, and their efforts to introduce modern Western culture to a Korean audience.

푸른 빗  갈 빗  푸른 빗- 하늘 모래바다-

별은 업다. ＜106＞

구름은 고요히 지나간다―
어느듯 한가로운 별 하나 반짝인다.

하늘 우으로 물결처 간다. ＜107＞
