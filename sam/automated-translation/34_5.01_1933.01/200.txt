Our Nostalgic Sentiment, Torch Play

Torch fighting (홰ㅅ불 싸홈) is one of the quite manly games. The young men of the village prepare for this torch fight from that morning, and when evening comes, each holding their torch, they gather in groups and go up the mountain. When the moon comes out, one side looks at the other side and shouts loudly, "Come on!" Then the other side also responds, "Come on!" shouting loudly, chasing and being chased, throwing their torches freely at each other, knocking them down and capturing them. While fighting like this, one person, two people, three people... gradually, people who surrender appear. The side with many surrendered people loses. Likewise, on this mountain and that mountain, children among children and young men among young men divide into teams and set up camp. Thus, this day is like a magnificent battle of brave heavenly soldiers and myriad horses.

Although they strike each other with torches in this torch fight, there are not many injuries, and it looks quite lively and spectacular to onlookers.

This play (노리) was once extremely popular throughout the country (전도), regardless of region, but now it is gradually disappearing, and such play is only occasionally done in mountainous areas.

From O Ch'ŏng's (吳晴氏) "Chosŏn's Annual Customs (朝鮮의 年中行事)" <65>

**Summary**

The article describes "Hwaetbul Ssaum" (Torch Fighting), a traditional Korean folk game primarily played by young men in villages. Teams gather on mountainsides at night, challenge each other, and engage in mock battles by throwing lit torches, with victory determined by which side has fewer members 'surrender'. The author emphasizes the game's manliness, spectacle, and relative safety despite its combative nature. The piece concludes with a nostalgic tone, lamenting that this once widespread custom was declining by 1933, surviving mainly in remote mountain regions. For historians, this article provides valuable ethnographic detail on Korean folk traditions during the colonial period. The expressed nostalgia and observation of the game's decline might hint at underlying concerns about cultural preservation amidst modernization and colonial influence.

그리운 우리 情調, 횃불노리
홰ㅅ불 싸홈이라는 것으로 퍽 사내다운 작난의 하나이다. 마을의 장정들은 그날 아침부터 이 홰ㅅ불 싸홈의 준비를 하야서 저녁때가 되면 각각 그 홰ㅅ불을 들고 한떼가 뭉치여 산우로 올나가 달이 나오면 이쪽 한패가 저쪽패를 보고 『자-오너라』하고 크다란 소래를 지르면 저쪽에서도 『자-오너라』하고 응하야 크다란 함성을 지르며 몰니고 쫏고 쪼기고 서로들 그 홰ㅅ불을 마음대로 던지여 넘어틀이고 잡바틀인다. 이러케 서로 싸호는 중 한 사람 두 사람 세 사람... 차츰 항복하는 사람들이 생긴다. 이 항복한 사람이 만흔 쪽이 지는 것이다. 이와 갓치 이쪽이쪽 산우에는 아이는 아이들끼리 장정은 장정끼리 서로들 편이 갈너저 진을 친다. 그리하야 이날은 마치 용감한 천병만마의 웅장한 접전갓다.
이 홰ㅅ불 싸홈은 서로 홰ㅅ불로 따리기는하나 그다지 부상자는 아니나며 남보기에 퍽으나 활발하며 장관이다.
이 노리는 전도를 물논하고 한참은 굉장이 성행하얏섯스나 지금은 차차 업서저서 산간에서 때때나 이런 노리를 한다.
吳晴氏 著 「朝鮮의 年中行事에서」＜65＞
