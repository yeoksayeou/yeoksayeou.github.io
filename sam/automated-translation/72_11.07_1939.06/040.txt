Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Interview/Discussion)

Record of a Visit to a Company President
						By Ch'angnang'aek

Late spring, April. In the old castle of Changan (Seoul), azaleas and forsythia bloom profusely, reminding one of the yearly green of spring grasses, as my excited steps pass the bustling Chongno intersection and head up towards Andong. It is to seek Mr. Yi Chong-man, president of Taedong Mining.

Some call Mr. Yi Chong-man the Rothschild or Carnegie of Chosŏn. Others say that despite being a multimillionaire, he wears worn-out Western clothes with leggings, enters the mine shafts himself, works alongside the miners (坑夫) grasping a pickaxe, and sometimes takes the 5-chŏn streetcar to appear in the slums outside Tongdaemun, giving away 100 wŏn or 1,000 wŏn before returning, imagining him like the Count of Monte Cristo appearing from Neptune (海王星). In any case, who exactly is this Mr. Yi Chong-man who draws the attention of hundreds of thousands in the world, and what are his views on business and wealth? This reporter is also endlessly curious.

Before long, my steps reached the threshold of a two-story brick building. Four or five green pine trees in the front garden adorned the entrance elegantly, and on the hanging plaque (懸板), the eight characters for Taedong Mining Company (大同鑛業株式會社) were engraved in gold letters on white marble.

It was already 4 PM, precisely the time to leave the office (退廳) for government and public offices working an 8-hour day. Knocking on the president's office door, I expected a magnanimous and prosperous <40> elder-like giant (巨人) to greet me, which brought on melancholic thoughts. Weren't this building and this president's office the very place where the old traces of those who decorated Chosŏn's cultural history in various colors lingered? Indeed, when the Chosun Ilbo was located here, starting with old Yi Sang-jae, giants like An Chae-hong, Sin Sŏk-u, and Yu Chin-t'ae frequented the place, wielding influence (采配) over social affairs both inside and out. Later, when it became the Chungang Ilbo, Yŏ Un-hyŏng and Ch'oe Sŏn-ik also sat in this chair, discussing worldly affairs.

How could the affairs of the world (世事) be so tumultuous? They say great men live obscurely (*한다더니 -> 隱한다더니) in the marketplace; are those gentlemen of that time still in Seoul today? Now, there is no way even to hear of their well-being.

A Few of His Beliefs (氏의 信條 몇 가지)

Reporter: Sir, how much do you yourself estimate your assets to be?

Yi: Well now. What should I say? People in the world probably know better.

Reporter: If you absolutely must say it in your own words?

Yi: Last year's production value (産金額) from the Changjin Mine reached about 2 million wŏn. Of that, the money actually turned into cash from selling ore was about 1,054,000 wŏn, and the remaining roughly 900,000 wŏn worth of ore has been mined but couldn't be sold yet because the electrification facilities (電化施設) in the Changjin region are delayed, so it's piled up at the site.

Reporter: In that case, what is the planned amount for this year and the future outlook?

Yi: This year's production amount has already been precisely calculated scientifically by the Government-General's Gold Production Section (總督府 産金課) to be 2 million wŏn worth, and they instructed us to mine that designated amount. It is proceeding exactly according to plan, so about 2 million wŏn worth will likely come out this year. And I am confident that once the mining facilities (採鑛施設) are completed in a few years, we can definitely mine 10 million wŏn worth every year.

Reporter: Yes. I understand well. Then, how much debt (負債) do you have?

Yi: I borrowed about 1 million wŏn to set up the mine facilities, school projects, and business. The largest debt among that is 700,000 wŏn financed (융통) from a gold mining company (産金會社).

Reporter: I heard that besides the Changjin Mine, you also have gold mines, special mineral mines, and iron mines elsewhere?

Yi: Yes. There are still several dozen gold mines throughout Chŏnson (All Korea), and there is one iron mine in P'yŏngan Pukto with reserves (매장량) of 100 million tons. <41>

Reporter: If the Changjin Mine brings in 10 million wŏn annually, even estimating conservatively compared to other peaceful industries (平和産業), assuming a 30% profit, the valuation of that gold mine would be about 300 million wŏn. Is that right?

Yi: Whether it's 300 million or 500 million wŏn, there's no way to evaluate it, but I anticipate it will reach an enormous sum we haven't heard of before.

Reporter: Would you sell it if companies like Mitsubishi or Mitsui offered 100 or 200 million wŏn?

Yi: Under no circumstances will I let it leave my hands. This is also a great gift heaven has given us. How could I possibly sell it?

What is His View on Business? (氏의 事業觀은 如何한가)

Reporter: You have already succeeded in mining, but considering Chosŏn's industries, besides this, there is the sardine fishing industry worth 40 million wŏn annually, and the timber industry centered on railroad ties (枕木) worth tens of millions. Do you not intend to venture into (投足) other businesses?

Yi: Absolutely not. I once tried fishing for about 3 years in my hometown, Kyŏngsang Province, but with my disposition, fishing is something I really cannot do.

Reporter: Because the profit is low?

Yi: No, profit-wise, if the nets catch well overnight, tens of thousands of wŏn can be made. But that involves ending the lives of tens of thousands of living creatures right before one's eyes. Aren't fish also living beings that cling to their lives? The act of forcefully snatching away (*아서 -> 앗아서) the lives of fish struggling desperately not to die truly pained my heart, so I thought, 'This is work I cannot do, even if tens of thousands of gold coins could be made overnight.' I gave it up completely, sold the fishing nets, handed over the fishing boats to others, and washed my hands of it. Besides this, I have tried farming, been a school teacher, and there are probably few occupations in the world I haven't tried—.

Reporter: —And among them, you chose this mining business as the most suitable work?

Yi: Yes, that's right. This involves digging up treasures buried underground, that is, treasures given by God. With skillful means, meaning if the system is well-organized, it can be extracted without exploiting (搾取) the miners and without undue struggle. What is extracted becomes an unparalleled treasure for the nation and society, and if used well (善用), it can be dedicated to the <42> happiness of all humankind. So, it fits my ideals and personality best.

Reporter: However, according to common thinking, it's the way of the world (人之常性) that the rich get richer (富益富), a millionaire wants to become a multimillionaire, and a multimillionaire wants to become a billionaire. Do you not think of becoming even richer than you are now?

Yi: No. I declare firmly. I will not pursue more than this for the sake of money. My aspiration is not to become a Rothschild or a Carnegie.

His Philosophy of Life (氏의 人生哲學)

Reporter: Excuse my rudeness, but among the wealthy of Changan (Seoul), it's rare to find one who doesn't keep a wife and concubines (妻妾). Also, those known as rich typically build luxurious mansions like Afang Palaces (阿房宮) and indulge in fine clothes and food (美衣美食). But you reside in a small house worth only a few thousand wŏn, I haven't heard talk of you taking a beloved concubine (寵妾), nor do you indulge in fine wine and clothes like others. Where does your life's pleasure lie?

Yi: (Silence)

Reporter: There are many who find pleasure in guarding their bankbooks and opening and closing their safes, but you have already donated most of your wealth to the Taedong Technical Specialized School, and also to Taedong Rural Village and Taedong Mining, so it doesn't seem like that either—.

Yi: Thank you for asking. My pleasure lies in cultivating the Way (道).

Reporter: The Way?

Yi: While reading good classics (經典) ten times, a hundred times, the teachings of the ancient sages (聖賢) naturally become known. It is the aspect of thinking to try and cultivate even one ten-thousandth of the path they followed.

Reporter: Could you speak a little more deeply?

Yi: Classics (經書) are placed on my bookshelf. While looking at these whenever I have the chance, with the diligence of 'reflecting on myself three times daily' (吾日三省吾身), I think of spending my time as someone helpful to this world in any way possible.

Reporter: Śākyamuni (釋迦)?

Yi: Yes. Didn't Śākyamuni abandon the joyful royal palace and the noble status of a prince, seeking to resolve the suffering of human life and save sentient beings (중생), personally undertaking that difficult ascetic practice (艱難의 修道)? I, too, stand on the path of wishing to become someone who alleviates, even just a little, the sorrow and bitterness of this world. <43>

Reporter: Only Śākyamuni?

Yi: I revere all saints. Christ (기독), even while being nailed to the cross by his own hands, spoke of forgiving his enemies. I, too, constantly reflecting on my own lowliness, strive to become an apostle who forgives others in any circumstance and seeks a path for everyone to live well together.

Reporter: Since you have deep learning in Chinese classics (漢學), then Confucius (孔夫子) too?

Yi: Yes. I also read the Analects deeply. I strive to emulate the spirit and effort of Confucius, who worked hard to spread the Way (道) in the world, traveling throughout the realm (轍環天下) until his old age.

Reporter: Besides such spiritual cultivation, is there anything else?

Yi: There is nothing other than seeking a way for everyone to live well together.

The Path of His Future Dedication (氏의 將來 獻身할 길은)

Reporter: Then, if you say you will not pursue vast wealth further, nor seek worldly pleasures beyond necessity, and will follow the path of the sages, what becomes the relationship between your current business and the hundreds of millions of wŏn laid out before you?

Yi: This position of president that I currently occupy is actually not my true intention. It is more comfortable for my heart, and it is my wish, to leave such a position, go into the mines with those miners (坑夫), work alongside them, and teach them.

Reporter: What circumstances prevent you from doing that now?

Yi: Anytime. If just one person emerges who can reliably take charge of the various societies and schools I currently manage, I will step down anytime.

Reporter: Are you looking for one or two individuals of character?

Yi: More than that, I want to have a steel-like organization (鐵과 같이 다저진 조직체). Therefore, I am thinking of completing the organizational structures for all the business institutions I am involved with, so that they can operate well without any hindrance even after I am gone someday.

Reporter: On the day that organizational structure is completed?

Yi: I intend to completely depart from managing or earning money and devote all my life's energy to nurturing the talented individuals (英材) of this world, such as through educational work. <44>

Reporter: Do you have any plan (案) formulated? Do you have a plan roughly laid out in your mind (흉중에 저윽이 짜놓은 풀댄)?

Yi: Since it's before realization, I don't want to speak of it, but if you promise not to announce it too widely, although it's not yet a concrete plan, I will tell you the intention in my heart.
I truly want to build one university with my own hands. Since one can be established with 20 or 30 million wŏn, I am thinking of creating this university composed of several colleges. If things go as I wish, I want to create these three suitable for Chosŏn's reality:
College of Engineering (工科大學)
College of Agriculture (農科大學)
College of Mining (鑛山科大學)
...and make a comprehensive university (綜合大學).

Reporter: The money?

Yi: Even just the Changjin Mine will produce 10 million wŏn annually within a few years (不出*年 -> 不出幾年), so I think possessing that alone is sufficient.

Reporter: Is the current Taedong Technical College (大同工專) in P'yŏngyang also preparation for that?

Yi: You could interpret it that way.

As the conversation reached this point, I wept inwardly. "Why did we meet so late!" (何相見而晩也), I wanted to grasp his wrist and weep for a long time.
The evening sunset glowed red on the excited faces of the host and guest. <45>

**Summary**

This 1939 interview profiles Yi Chong-man, president of Taedong Mining and founder of Taedong Technical College. Yi discusses his vast wealth derived primarily from the Changjin Mine, but expresses disinterest in accumulating more riches or pursuing typical luxuries. He details his business philosophy, emphasizing mining as ethically preferable to fishing (due to the killing involved) and as a way to utilize God-given resources for societal benefit without exploiting labor. Yi outlines his personal philosophy, drawing inspiration from Śākyamuni, Christ, and Confucius, focusing on self-reflection, alleviating suffering, forgiveness, and finding ways for all to prosper. His ultimate goal is to establish robust organizations to run his enterprises so he can dedicate himself entirely to education, aspiring to found a comprehensive university in Korea focused on practical fields like engineering, agriculture, and mining, funded by his mining profits.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it portrays a highly successful Korean industrialist during the Japanese colonial period expressing strong philanthropic and nationalistic (albeit subtly) aspirations. Yi's desire to build Korean institutions (schools, university) and his ethical framework, contrasting sharply with purely profit-driven motives often associated with large capital, offer insight into the complex motivations and strategies of some Korean elites under colonial rule. His conscious blending of Eastern and Western philosophical ideals (Buddhism, Confucianism, Christianity) to shape his life and work is also noteworthy.

社長 訪問記
						滄浪客
暮春 4월. 長安 古城에는 진달내 개나리가 어지럽게 피어 春草의 年年綠을 생각게 하는데 나의 흥분한 거름은 雜踏한 鍾路 네거리를 지나 安洞으로 향하여 올너간다. 大同鑛業社長 李鍾萬씨를 찻기 위함이라.
남들은 李鍾萬씨를 마치 朝鮮의 로스촤일드요, 카-네기라고 부르며 엇든 이는 천만장자의 몸이면서 다 찌저진 양복에 각반을 치고 손소 굴 속에 드러가 坑夫들과 괭이 잡고 일도 하며 어떤 때는 5전 짜리 전차를 타고 동대문 밧 貧民窟에 나타나 100원도 주고 1,000원도 주고 도라온다 하여 「海王星」에 나타나는 몬테 크리스트 백작 모양으로 상상하는 이도 있다. 어째든 세상 여러 10만명의 주목을 끄을고 있는 李鍾萬씨란 대체 어떠한 인물이며 그의 사업관 황금관은 어떠한고 필자 또한 궁금하기 짝이 없는 일이다.
幾未에 나의 발거름은 벽돌 2층집 문턱 앞에 다달었다. 4, 5樹의 庭前 靑松이 입구를 아담하게 장식하였고 懸板에는 백대리석 우에다가 金字로 大同鑛業株式會社의 8字를 刻入하여 노았다.
벌서 오후 4시, 8시간 勞役하는 官公廳이면 正히 退廳할 시각이다. 사장실 문을 叩한 즉 寬厚裕福한＜40＞ 長者風의 巨人이 마저 준다 생각하면 滄然한 생각이 든다. 이 건물과 이 사장실이야말로 朝鮮 문화사를 여러 빛갈로 장식하든 녯 자최 머물든 곳 아니든가. 실로 朝鮮日報 앉었을 적에는 李商在 翁을 爲始하여 安在鴻 申錫雨 兪鎭泰 등 한다하는 巨人들이 드나들며 안팍으로 사회 일의 采配를 잡든 자리요 또 後日央日報가 되면서부터 呂運亨 崔善益 등이 또한 이 의자에 걸터 앉어 천하를 논하든 곳이 아니런가.
世事 愴惶함이 어찌 이러틋 심하든고. 大人은 市井에 *한다더니 當年 諸氏 지금도 서울에 잇는가 而今엔 안부조차 드를 길 없다.

氏의 信條 몇 가지
기자-선생의 재산은 선생 스사로가 얼마라고 평가하심니까.
李-글세요. 무에라 할는지요. 세상 人士들이 더 잘 아라주실걸요.
기자-기어히 선생 입으로 말슴하여야만 되신다면?
李-長津鑛山의 작년 産金額이 약 2백만원에 달하는데 그 중에 실지로 鑛石을 팔아서 현금화된 돈이 1,054,000원쯤 되고 남어지 약 90만원어치는 鑛石을 파내 노코도 長津 지방의 電化施設이 느저서 미처 팔치 못하여 현지에 싸여 노코 있어요.
기자-그런다면 금년의 예정액과 장래는요.
李-금년 産額은 이미 總督府 産金課에서 정밀하게 과학적으로 계산하여 2백만원어치야 나올 터이니 그 지정액대로 파내라 하여 꼭 그 예정대로 지금 진행 중에 있으니 금년은 2백만 원어치는 나오겠지요. 그리고 수년만 지나서 採鑛施設이 완성되면 꼭 1천만 원은 매년 파 낼 수 있다고 자신해요.
기자-녜. 잘 알겠음니다. 그러면 선생에게 負債는 얼마나 있어요.
李-광산시설과 학교사업과 사업을 작만하느라고 약 1백만원쯤 어더썻는데 그 중 큰 빗은 産金會社서 융통해 온 돈 70만원이지요.
