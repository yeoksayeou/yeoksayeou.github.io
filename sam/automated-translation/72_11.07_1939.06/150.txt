기사제목	朴錫胤氏의 印象 -新任한 波蘭總領事를 보내며- (Impressions of Mr. Pak Sŏgyun - Seeing Off the Newly Appointed Consul General to Poland -)

기사형태	문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literary Piece)

Impressions of Mr. Pak Sŏgyun - Seeing Off the Newly Appointed Consul General to Poland -
						Sin'gyŏng Kim Kyŏngjae

One

Brother Pain (巴人), I apologize for not replying sooner despite receiving your letters several times, as I've been constantly busy. I am once again impressed by your steady dedication (誠力) as Samch'ŏlli greets its 10th anniversary.
You asked me to write a character sketch of Mr. Pak Sŏgyun (朴錫胤), but I am currently preparing for a trip to the Central and South China (中南支) region, making it difficult to meet your request. Therefore, I offer these few words to fulfill my obligation (塞責).

Two

My acquaintance with him began when I came to Sin'gyŏng (新京), so it hasn't been that long. However, he has a strong determination to become a faithful leader for the Koreans residing in Manchuria (在滿). He holds the firm conviction that through the Concordia Association (協和會) movement, Koreans must self-reflect (自省), and those of us with new consciousness and resolve must unite under a new banner (旗幟). Resonating with this point, I agreed, through unspoken understanding (無言中), that we, like-minded individuals, should become pioneers for our compatriots in Manchuria, with me assisting him. I was at the Concordia Association Capital Headquarters (協和會首都本部), and under its jurisdiction was the Sin'gyŏng Korean Branch Association (新京朝鮮人分會). Given that Sin'gyŏng is the national capital (國都), the president of that branch association effectively stands in the position of a representative for all Koreans. Last year, Mr. Pak was appointed President of the Concordia Association Sin'gyŏng Korean Branch Association by popular acclaim (衆望). Since then, my relationship with him has deepened further. We met almost daily through work. Afterwards, we discussed both good and bad matters together, and I helped him (帮助) so that his ideas could be realized as conceived. <98>

Three

Introducing his résumé (履歷) here would actually be laughable. I will just mention a fragment (片貌) of what I have seen and heard while associating with him, sufficient to know him.
Although he is going to Europe (歐洲) as a diplomat, he is a person who doesn't know social graces (社交). He can't even distinguish whether what he says will please or anger others. He lays out what he wants to say right in front of others without any embellishment, making it easy for those who first associate (相從) with him to misunderstand. Thus, a person who doesn't know how to flatter others, a person who doesn't know how to read others' moods, that person is going to Poland (波蘭) as Consul General. He is the exact opposite type of the conventional diplomat who thinks a diplomat must first know how to eat Western food, know how to dance, be quick-witted in reading others' expressions, and lie as easily as drinking cold water. However, thinking that the things listed above are the special qualities (特質) of a diplomat is a big mistake. The fact that Mr. Pak is going as a diplomat, especially to Poland, an important area concerning relations with the Soviet Union (對蘇) and famously ** [word missing] in the world, shows that the definition of a diplomat is separate from the conditions listed above, and herein also lies the unique characteristic (特色) of Manchukuo (滿洲國).

Four

Next, he is a broad-minded person. He is entirely indifferent to small matters. When he goes out, he doesn't try to find out how his wife is doing at home or how his young children (兒孩) are growing, nor does he ask anyone.
One might think this makes him very neglectful (等閑) of household affairs, but he leaves all household matters entirely to his wife, neither seeking to know nor asking about them; instead, if it's about societal matters, he spends his own money chasing after them. He is also fortunate domestically. His wise and virtuous (賢淑) wife, who has ** [word missing], never causes her husband worry over family matters (家事). His broad-minded personality can easily lack meticulousness (緻密). However, his strength is
that he readily listens to the words of friends who are wise and have organizational minds. Even if his own personality is broad-minded and rough (거*다 - word partially illegible), the fact that he always readily accepts the words of meticulous and planning-oriented friends means we must *say* that he will *have few* failures (*겠다고 *아야 함니다 - words partially illegible/missing) in whatever he does.

Five

Although he cannot drink alcohol, he sings quite well and likes to have fun. I ******* [word missing/illegible] saying, "He would have succeeded if he became an entertainer (風角쟁이)," such is his disposition of a *free spirit* (*활량 - word partially illegible/missing, possibly 풍월량?). When he goes to a restaurant (料理* - last char missing, likely 집 or 점), he only knows to get up when his pocket is empty. Following around people who drink and get drunk when you can't drink yourself is very difficult. I know this well, as I often face difficulties bumping into drinking friends since I can't drink. Even though he can't drink a single drop, he accompanies drinking friends until dawn breaks, singing, playing, and cheerfully making noise. Does he only socialize well like that with Koreans? Not at all. He socializes well with Japanese people from the mainland (內地) and also gets along well with Manchurians (滿人). Especially among young Manchurian officials (滿人의 靑年官吏), there are many who respect (敬慕) and have expectations for Mr. Pak. <99>

Six

He is a genius in languages (語學). During his stay in North China (北支), he learned Chinese (中語) well enough not only to communicate his intentions fully but also to give simple speeches.
As for other languages like English, German, or French (獨逸 佛語), he is very proficient (能), as he has been to Europe (歐洲) several times (* several빈 갓든이만큼 - formatting issue, likely '여러 번 갔던이만큼'). His proficient linguistic skills (素養), cheerful personality, and polished international experience raise great expectations for his activities in Poland. Although he is a Consul General, he is effectively going at the Minister (公使) level, and the pressing situation (逼迫한 情勢) in Europe provides a stage (舞臺) for his activities. It was just yesterday. I met a certain young Manchurian administrative official (滿人의 靑年事務官 某氏) working at the Foreign Affairs Bureau (外務局) at a Russian café near the station. Seeing me, he asked, "『朴樣何時頃コチラへ着キマスカ』" (Around what time will Mr. Pak arrive here?). I asked why he, from the Foreign Affairs Bureau, didn't know and was instead asking me. He replied, "『朴樣ノコト*スカラ 役所ニハ何ンノ聯絡モナイ*ス* ソノ內ニ波蘭ハ亡*マスト 赴任モ出來ナイ*スネ』" (Because it's Mr. Pak (朴樣ノコトですから), there's been no contact whatsoever with the office (役所). In the meantime, if Poland collapses (波蘭ハ亡びますと), he won't even be able to take up his post, right (赴任モ出来ないですね)?). This gives a glimpse into one aspect of Mr. Pak's personality: he doesn't even maintain detailed contact with his own workplace, the Foreign Affairs Bureau. Next, another point is that because his personality is such, when he trusts someone, he trusts them unconditionally, but once he starts distrusting someone, he doesn't trust them easily. For those who understand Mr. Pak, substituting for his personality, they know his character (人格), know his capabilities (力量), and place their entire expectations on him. However, those who don't understand him only know him as an impudent person, a difficult person to associate with, someone completely ignorant of others' difficult situations (딱한 사정).

Seven

I know him well. I know his capabilities (力量), know his dedication (誠力), know what he thinks, and *know* the path he intends to take (*럼니다 - likely 압니다). Therefore, I have great expectations for him. However, I, who know Mr. Pak well, sometimes laugh to myself. What is it? It's that he calmly expresses passionate opinions (熱氣滿滿한 소리) quite unbecoming of the usual Mr. Pak. Even if others object, saying <100> what foolish talk that is, he explains it sincerely (衷心). Here, I come to expect even more from him. Reading biographies of great men (偉人), I find that retaining a touch of childishness (穉氣) is characteristic of great men. People who are overly clever (怜悧), whose words are always measured, and whose actions are consistent front-to-back, are smart people. Though they may leave no room for criticism, they cannot accomplish great things. I have great expectations for Consul General Pak, who is disorganized (非組織的), unsociable (無社交的), just broad-minded, covers things up, trusts his comrades (同志), knows absolutely nothing of petty tricks (細刀小工), and at times calmly insists on childlike ideas.
I know. Since his appointment as Consul General, he has been treating an eye ailment (眼疾) in Tokyo (東京), so I haven't met him even once yet nor heard his plans (經倫), but I know he will return to Manchuria before long. We in Manchuria are also very sad to see him go. This is not just polite talk stemming from human sentiment (情的); there are many things he needs to do in Manchuria. Not yet done, and in that respect, Mr. Pak himself will likely miss Sin'gyŏng's makgeolli (막걸니) more than Warsaw's (왈쇼) Western food, and the crimson skirts (다홍치마) of Manchuria will be more unforgettable than the beauties of Poland.
Brother Pain, in haste, I have jotted down thoughts as they came to mind. Of course, this isn't polished writing, nor does it have a coherent structure even as speech. However, I believe one can glimpse at least a partial aspect (片貌) of the man Pak Sŏgyun here. With this, I will *lay down my pen* (*筆 - likely 擱筆, Gakpil). <101>
<98-101>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Kyŏngjae, writing from Sin'gyŏng (Xinjing), offers his impressions of Pak Sŏgyun upon Pak's appointment as Manchukuo's Consul General to Poland in 1939. Kim portrays Pak as a dedicated leader for Koreans in Manchuria, heavily involved in the Concordia Association (Hyŏphwahoe), but also unconventional. Pak is described as socially inept, blunt, broad-minded yet sometimes lacking meticulousness, indifferent to domestic life but passionate about public affairs, trusting of friends, a talented linguist, and someone who holds strong, occasionally naive-sounding convictions. Despite these quirks, Kim expresses high hopes for Pak's diplomatic mission, seeing his unconventional nature as a potential strength.

From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it provides a contemporary, personal view of a Korean figure collaborating with the Manchukuo state and its Japanese-sponsored Concordia Association. It reveals the complex dynamics among Koreans in Manchuria, their aspirations for leadership within the Manchukuo framework, and the personal characteristics valued (or criticized) in that context. The mention of Pak's mission to Poland just before WWII highlights Manchukuo's diplomatic activities and the precarious international situation. The casual mention of Manchurian officials and interactions provides a glimpse into the multiethnic environment of Manchukuo's bureaucracy.

						新京 金璟載
一

巴人 형 여러 차레의 형의 私信을 받고 향상 바뿌게 지내기에 修信못하야 죄송함니다. 三千里가 10주년을 맛게 되었다고 하니 형의 꾸준하신 誠力에 다시금 感服함니다.
朴錫胤씨의 인물평을 쓰시라고 하였으나 나는 방금 中南支 방면에 여행하려고 준비 중이여서 下托하신 요구에 應키가 困難함니다. 그래서 두어 字로 塞責함니다.

二

그와 내가 알게된 것은 내가 新京에 와서이니 아즉 年이 淺하외다. 그러나 그는 在滿의 朝鮮人을 위하야 충실한 지도자가 되고저 결심이 강하외다. 協和會運動을 하야 朝鮮人은 自省하고 새로운 의식과 각오가 있는 우리는 새로운 旗幟하에 뭉키여야 한다는 굿센 신념을 갓고 있음니다. 이 점에서 共鳴한 나는 그를 도와서 서로 의사가 맛는 사람끼리 滿洲의 동포의 선구자가 되여야 한다는 無言中에 意思가 일치되였었음니다. 내가 協和會首都本部에 있고 首都本部 管下에 新京朝鮮人分會가 있는 바 新京이 國都*라는 점에서 그 分會의 회장은 사실상에 있어 全 朝鮮人의 대표자와 같은 처지에 스게 됨니다. 작년에 와서 朴씨는 衆望에 의하야 協和會新京朝鮮人分會長에 就載되였음니다. 그 후부터 그와 나는 일층 관계가 깁허젔음니다. 일을 통하야 매일같이 만나게 됨니다. 그 후는 조흔 일이나 구즌 일이나 議論을 같이 하게 되고 나는 그를 도와서 그가 생각하는 바가 그대로 실현되도록 帮助도 했음니다.＜98＞
