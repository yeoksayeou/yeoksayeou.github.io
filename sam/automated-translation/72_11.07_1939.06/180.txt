Hanja Newspapers of the Chiang Regime

One reaches Chunggyŏng (重慶), the final base for resistance of the Chang (蔣) regime, by traveling 1,500 *li* up the Yangjagang (揚子江). Let us take a glance (一瞥) at the current situation of the Hanja newspapers published here. Before the Incident (事變), there were 6 daily newspapers and 4 evening papers. However, because various newspapers from Pukchi (北支), Sanghae (上海), Nanking (南京), and Han'gu (漢口) have relocated (移轉) here, the number of newspapers published in Chunggyŏng has become the most (最多) numerous compared to any other city. Currently, 11 types of Hanja papers (漢字紙) are being published, including the *Ta Kung Pao* (大公報) from Ch'ŏnjin (天津). Although each newspaper differs (달니하고) in its respective interests and viewpoints, <107> they maintain a united front in supporting the Kungmin Chŏngbu (國民政府 - Nationalist Government) and Chang Kaesŏk (蔣介石) on the anti-Japanese war front. Let's briefly overview (槪觀하여보기로) the newspapers in Chunggyŏng below.

The *Chungang Ilbo* (中央日報 - Central Daily News) is the mouthpiece of the Kuomintang (國民黨) and likely the most prominent supporter of the current regime. When Nanking fell, it temporarily relocated (移轉) to Changsa (長沙). It returned to Chunggyŏng in September of last year, but still maintains a branch office in Changsa.

The *Ta Kung Pao* (大公報) is the most influential newspaper in China (支那). It was first published in Ch'ŏnjin in Meiji 35 (1902, *Translator's Note: 1902, not 1920 as stated in the text*), and later published in Sanghae, and has long been considered (해왔다) one of China's four major newspapers. This paper is published in Chunggyŏng and is currently also reproduced and published in Hong K'ong (香港). The refugee newspaper *Saotang Pao* (掃蕩報), which came from Han'gu, and the *Sŏnam Ilbo* (西南日報 - Southwest Daily News), which was originally (본래부터) in Chunggyŏng, are considered sister newspapers because they are related to the Whampoa Clique (黃浦系) within the Kungmin Chŏngbu.

The *Saotang Pao* is famous for publishing (設載) a lot of military information, while the *Sŏnam Ilbo* holds special interest in the economic development (開發) of the Southwest region (西南地方), and it appears to receive subsidies (補助金) <108> from the Provisional Headquarters (臨時大本營).

The *Shishi Shinpo* (時事新報 - *Translator's Note: Corrected from 時人時報*) and *Shinmin Pao* (新民報) are both similar (同樣). The *Shishi Shinpo* was a local newspaper in Sanghae but relocated to Chunggyŏng early last year. The owner of this *Shishi Shinpo* is the Kungmin Chŏngbu's Finance Minister, Kong Sanghŭi (孔祥熙).

The *Shinmin Pao* is affiliated with Chang Kaesŏk; founded in September of Shōwa 4 (1929), it is an anti-Japanese newspaper that prominently featured (特筆大書) anti-Japanese incidents (對日設事). After the fall of the lower Yangjagang region following the Incident, it was the very first to relocate to Chunggyŏng.

The *Shinhwa Ilbo* (新華日報 - Xinhua Ribao) began publishing in Han'gu in January 1937 and relocated to Chunggyŏng just before the withdrawal of Chinese troops from Wuhan. This paper is the organ paper (機關紙) of the Chinese Communist Party (支那共産黨) and is actively engaged (活發히 動하는). A characteristic feature of this paper is publishing reports from the Soviet Union's (蘇聯) TASS News Agency (타스 通信社), carrying long essays and news related to mass movements. Nearly half a page (半頁) each day is filled (파묻히고) with TASS dispatches, and the editorial department operates under a committee system (委員會制), with committee members writing in turns (委員들이 교대로).

According to a reliable (確實한) investigation, the circulation figures for daily newspapers in Chunggyŏng are as follows (다은과 갓다): <109>
*Chungang Ilbo* 5,000 copies / *Ta Kung Pao* 7,000 copies / *Kuomin Kung Pao* (國民公報) 6,000 copies / *Shinhwa Ilbo* (*Correction: Likely 新華報, not 新罨報*) 7,000 copies / *Shishi Shinpo* 6,000 copies
*Shinhwa Ilbo* 6,000 copies / *Shinmin Pao* 8,000 copies / *Saotang Pao* 6,000 copies / *Sŏnam Ilbo* 2,000 copies / *Chech'ŏn Kongbo* (濟川公報) 500 copies
*Sangmu Ilbo* (商務日報) 500 copies / *Taejang Ilbo* (大江日報) 500 copies / *Shang wu Shih Pao* (Shang wu 時報) 500 copies Total above <110>

**Summary**

This 1939 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* describes the newspaper landscape in Chunggyŏng (Chongqing), the wartime capital of Chiang Kai-shek's Nationalist government. It notes the influx of newspapers from fallen cities like Nanking, Shanghai, and Hankow, making Chunggyŏng a major publishing center. Despite differing affiliations (Kuomintang, Whampoa Clique, Communist Party, independents like *Ta Kung Pao*), all listed papers are united in supporting Chiang and the anti-Japanese war effort. The article details specific papers like *Chungang Ilbo*, *Ta Kung Pao*, *Saotang Pao*, *Shishi Shinpo*, *Shinmin Pao*, and the Communist *Shinhwa Ilbo*, mentioning their backgrounds, affiliations (like H.H. Kung owning *Shishi Shinpo*), editorial stances, and circulation figures. For Korean colonial history, this piece is interesting as it shows a Korean perspective (published in Seoul under Japanese rule) on the internal workings and propaganda apparatus of the opposing Chinese government during the Sino-Japanese War. It provides contemporary details on Chinese media, political factions (Nationalist vs. Communist cooperation), and specific circulation numbers, offering insight into information flow and political dynamics as understood or presented to a Korean audience at the time.
