**Body Text Translation:**
Samch'ŏlli and I
						An Sŏ

Although rice is precious life that we cannot cultivate without eating, we often regard this life-giving rice dismissively, saying, "Oh, it's just rice." Humans are creatures who enjoy being ungrateful like this. Samch'ŏlli is rice. It is the rice of life that we must eat. At the same time, it is also the "Oh, it's just rice" kind of rice. However, this certainly does not mean there is even the slightest stain on its distinct existence over the long ten years. Even as a Pa In (巴人), regarding this point, I can rest assured and freely dream blue dreams on a high pillow.

Ten years—it's easy to say, but ten years is by no means a short time. There's a saying that even the sea changes into a mulberry field [a metaphor for drastic change] <0xE3><0x80><0xB2><0xC6><0x92> during ten years, so how much must this world have changed, not to mention the countless transformations of human life within it? In such an incredibly changing world, the fact that Samch'ŏlli has maintained its distinct existence as rice that we must eat is, in any sense, something we cannot help but be grateful for.

However, here, as one of the contributors <62> during that time, I cannot help but feel ashamed looking back, because I often sullied Samch'ŏlli, which is rice for precious life, by treating it as "just rice." Despite this, perhaps because the connection was already forged, I did not sever it and had my manuscripts, such as they were, scribbled out and put into print. It goes without saying that this is embarrassing, and looking back on the ten years of Samch'ŏlli consequently makes me reflect on my own insignificant traces. Thus, if I may say so, I can find all my merits and demerits within Samch'ŏlli.

Regretting past mistakes will certainly not make up [for?] [*ch'ungdoel*] them. Just as wailing and struggling by the grave at dusk will never bring back someone who cannot return, I* [*na*] today, rather than trying to find fault [?] [***cha orya neun*] with everything from the past [******], will simply do what I can for myself in the tomorrow to come, and if I can help the precious rice that is Samch'ŏlli even just a little, I will do so. <63>
<62-63>

**Summary**

The author, An Sŏ, reflects on the 10th anniversary of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*. He compares the magazine to essential "rice" for life, acknowledging its vital importance to its readers, yet admits to sometimes taking it for granted ("Oh, it's just rice"). He expresses shame over his past contributions, feeling they were inadequate ("scribbled"), but sees his own literary journey intertwined with the magazine's history. He resolves to move past regrets and contribute more meaningfully in the future. For historians, the article offers insight into the perceived cultural significance of *Samch'ŏlli* during the colonial era, framed through the vital metaphor of "rice." The author's self-deprecating tone and expression of past shortcomings might also reflect the difficult position of Korean intellectuals and writers under Japanese rule, perhaps hinting at limitations or pressures alongside personal humility.
