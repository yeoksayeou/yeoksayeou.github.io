**Body Text Translation**
From a Famous Detective's Notebook, The Secret Story of Yang Kŭn-hwan's Swift Arrest, Memoir of Detective Ōsawa, Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department

It was February 16th, Taishō 12 (1923) - the Imperial Diet (帝國議會) was in session in Hibiya, a time when politicians frequently came and went. That day, Patrolman Fujiki of the Tōkyō Station front police box (東京驛前派出所) finished his shift change at 10:30 AM and came out into the station square. The winter sun, just before the start of spring (立春), shone down warmly.
At that moment, without any particular thought, as the patrolman's eyes drifted towards the brick building of Tōkyō Station, he saw a young man wearing a spotted Kokura hakama (小倉 「하까마」) jump swiftly from a second-floor sealed window near the octagonal tower. The man jumped onto a pine branch near the window, then slid down the 7 or 8-*ch'ŏk* (approx. 2.1-2.4 meters) branch and hid in the bushes. The young man, with an impressive flash of the soles of his feet as if flustered by something, disappeared from near the Seiyōken Restaurant (精養軒料理店) into the crowd near the Central Post Office (中央郵便局).
"Quite an agile fellow,"
he thought admiringly, when suddenly,
"Murder! Murder!" <90>
a startled cry rang out from the station hotel window. Only then did Patrolman Fujiki come to his senses, suspecting the young man, and followed after him, but he had already vanished without a trace.

The Murdered Man was Min Wŏn-sik

In room 14 on the 3rd floor of the Station Hotel, Mr. Min Wŏn-sik (閔元植, 35), president of the Chosŏn People's Association (朝鮮國民協會), who had come to Tokyo several days prior for the movement to acquire political suffrage for Koreans, lay collapsed in his morning coat near the round reception table, drenched in blood. His shoulder and abdomen had been stabbed with a sharp object, and his forehead (前額部) was also pierced.
The green rug was stained dark red with pools of blood; amidst the blood lay a yellow pencil near his right hand. In the middle of the blood-spattered sofa was a mysterious memo paper – something was written in Min Wŏn-sik's fine handwriting.
Beside the body, five or six Korean gentlemen were weeping. These men were executives of the People's Association who had come to Tokyo with Mr. Min and were staying separately at an inn called Seikōkan (淸光舘) in Sakuradamokugōchō (櫻田木鄕町). Just before the incident – perhaps around 9:30 AM – they had received a phone call from Mr. Min Wŏn-sik.
"Why aren't you here early today?"
he had supposedly scolded them, summoning them. When they rushed over, the hotel boy was shouting "Murder! Murder!" in the corridor.
This was just five minutes before Mr. Min died.
When lifted up, Mr. Min seemed to regain slight consciousness at the sound of several people calling "Teacher, Teacher."
"Comrade, comrade,"
"Doctor, doctor,"
"Metropolitan Police, Metropolitan Police,"
he cried out clearly twice in Korean, and then he breathed his last.
From the Metropolitan Police Department (警視廳), the head of the criminal investigation department and about 30 skilled detectives rushed over. Judge Nishida (西田), Prosecutor Matsuda (松田), and other court officials also arrived at the scene and immediately began the examination.
The bellboy, Takemura Tomekichi (竹村留吉), who first discovered the scene, was called before Investigation Section Chief Koizumi (小泉). According to his statement, a young man resembling a student, wearing an indigo kasuri (곤가스리) kimono, bumped straight into him at a bend in the 3rd-floor corridor. In the collision, the man dropped the short sword (短刀) tucked at his side. The 6-*ch'on* (approx. 18cm) blade in its plain wooden sheath (白鞘) had dark bloodstains on it. Realizing something terrible had happened, the man, startled, picked the short sword back up and fled down <91> to the 2nd floor. He immediately chased after him, but couldn't see him, so he returned to the 3rd floor and found the door to room 14 pushed open.
The culprit was hatless and wore hotel slippers. He looked like a student, possibly a Japanese mainlander (內地人), according to the bellboy Takemura.

Let's talk about Mr. Min Wŏn-sik's background here. He was the son of Min Yŏng-ŏk (閔泳憶) from Yangp'yŏng County (楊平郡), Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道), said to be a relative of Queen Min (閔妃). During the Tonghak Peasant Rebellion (東學黨亂) in Meiji 26 (1893), his parents were assassinated, so he fled to Shanghai. At age 11, he returned to Korea (歸鮮) but had nowhere to turn, wandering various regions, making a living by writing calligraphy (書畵) which he was good at. In Meiji 31 (1898), he went to Tokyo and gained the acquaintance (知遇) of Soejima Taneomi (副島種臣), Hijikata Hisamoto (土方久元), and Itō Hirobumi (伊藤博文), after which he began advocating Pan-Asianism (大亞西亞主義). Later, he returned to Korea and served as the Head of the Sanitation Section under Minister of the Interior Min Yŏng-gi (閔泳綺) and also became the Director of Kwangjewŏn Hospital (廣濟院長). After the Japan-Korea annexation (日韓合倂), he was in the Central Council (中樞院). He became President of the People's Association (國民協會長) in Taishō 8 (1919) and dedicated his life entirely to the petition movement for granting suffrage (參政權) to the people of the peninsula.
This trip to Tokyo was for the third petition campaign. He arrived in the capital on the 9th, and on the 15th, he visited the House of Representatives (衆議院), obtaining informal consent from about 26 Diet members (代議士).
That morning, around 9:15 AM, after finishing breakfast, he was waiting for his subordinates to arrive from the Seikōkan Inn (淸光舘旅舘) to visit Diet member Ichinomiya (一宮), who was providing enthusiastic support for the petition movement.
The crime was carried out during the approximately 50 minutes he was waiting for his subordinates. The culprit must have been a young man dressed like a student. However, it wasn't a robbery. Was it someone opposed to the movement for peninsula suffrage? Even so, was he a mainlander (內地人) or a Korean (朝鮮人)? The Metropolitan Police authorities couldn't decide.
The Memo Dropped in the Sofa
Detective Ōsawa (大澤) took one look at the corpse and then stared intently at the memo paper dropped in the middle of the sofa.
"Nihon University (日本大學), Korean Christian Youth Association (朝鮮基督敎靑年會), Tonguhoe (同友會), 500 people, Paek Nam-hun (白南薰) (Youth Association President), Hongō Yushima Tenjinchō 62 (本鄕湯島天神町62), c/o Nishimura (西村方), Yi Ki-nyŏng (李基寧) (28)"
was written in Min's distinctive, fine handwriting. Detective Ōsawa slipped out of the crowded room 14 and appeared at the hotel reception.
"Did Mr. Min have any visitors this morning?"
"Nobody at all. Except one young man in Kokura [hakama] around 8 o'clock, who went straight up to the 3rd floor without asking for directions at the desk."
"Where did he come in from?" <92>
"From the entrance over there."
"Hmm—"
Detective Ōsawa silently went towards the geta storage box near the entrance.
Amidst the scattered geta (집신짝), one worn-out pair lay there. One of the geta had a broken strap (鼻緖 - hanao), replaced with a string made from old indigo kasuri (곤가스리) cloth.
The detective carefully picked up the geta and returned.
At the Metropolitan Police Department, an investigation meeting convened, centered around Criminal Investigation Section Chief Shōriki (正力). This incident might be part of a major organized crime. It was imagined the culprit might still be hiding in the city.
An emergency alert put all stations under its jurisdiction on high tension, and plainclothes detectives' eyes shone on men in indigo kasuri in stations, trains, and streetcars. From the Metropolitan Police investigation headquarters, over 50 detectives scattered in all directions.
They began investigating peninsula students and masterless samurai (浪人) types on one hand.
By the 17th, dozens of suspects (嫌異者) and persons of interest (參考人) had already been brought into the investigation headquarters, but the case remained shrouded in darkness (暗中模索).
The remains of the assassinated (暗殺) Mr. Min were autopsied at the Imperial University (帝大) that morning. At 1 PM, a farewell ceremony was held in the Tōkyō Station waiting room, attended by Minister of Education Nakahashi (中橋文相), Inspector Oka (岡*監), and other prominent figures from government and society. Afterward, escorted by his brother-in-law, Second Lieutenant Ŏm (嚴少尉), the body began its journey back to Korea (歸鮮).

The Yi of Number Sixty-Two and the Sixth Sense
It was around 5 PM that evening, just as the coffin was about to leave Tōkyō Station. Detective Ōsawa, along with detectives Yamamoto (山本) and Masuda (增田) from the Nishikanda Station (西神田署), was walking through Tsurumakichō, Waseda, in Ushigome Ward (牛込區早稻田 鶴卷町). They had gone into a certain lodging house at number 42 to investigate Koreans, and on the way, on a streetlamp (街路燈?) in Gokenchō (?)
the nameplate "Yi Ŭn-jong (李殷宗), 62 Tsurumakichō, Waseda (早稻田鶴卷町 62番地)" was attached.
He stopped dead in his tracks.
The name "Yi Ki-nyŏng (李基寧)" from the paper left at the crime scene sprang to mind. The address for Yi Ki-nyŏng was given as Hongō Yushima Tenjinchō 62, c/o Nishimura (本鄕湯島天神町62西村方), but no such name existed at that location.
Even if the name Yi Ki-nyŏng wasn't the name of the culprit witnessed by the bellboy or the police box patrolman, since Min wrote it just before being murdered, it couldn't be completely unrelated to the case.
People can freely use aliases (僞名), but in sudden situations, characters completely unrelated to oneself rarely come out.
Even within written characters, one unconsciously <93> uses characters that somehow represent the person. As long as the habits of human life exist, the knowledge of the individual imposes such patterns on complex human behavior.—This is Lesson One of the ABCs of detective life.
Upon simultaneously discovering the "62" of Waseda Tsurumakichō and the character "李" (Yi) of Yi Ŭn-jong, Detective Ōsawa compared them with the number 62 of Hongō Yushima Tenjinchō and the "李" (Yi) of Yi Ki-nyŏng written on the memo paper. It was purely a sixth-sense investigation (六感搜査), trusting in coincidence.
The surname Yi (李) is so common among Koreans it could be called *the* Korean name, but there was something about the Yi at number 62 that was hard to dismiss.
He urged the two accompanying detectives forward and had them open the dilapidated glass window.
"Is anyone home?"
"Who is it?"
A soft female voice. A young woman emerged, opening the broken sealed window. Her clothes were shabby, but she had a round, cute face. And surprisingly, she was a Japanese mainlander (內地人) woman.
"Is the master not in?"
"He's not here today."
"Where did he go?"
"He went out last night and hasn't come back yet. Excuse me, but who are you?"
"Ah, well— What business is the master currently engaged in?"
"He sells taffy (엿장사), but hardly any sells, so things are like this..."
It seemed to be a considerably poor household (살님). On the floor of the room they entered, four pairs of men's geta (게다) were scattered about. It seemed there was a housemate.
"Actually, I'm from the police. I came because there's something small I need to investigate."
At these words, the woman's hands trembled faintly, and her furrowed brow bent forward.
"Are the housemates upstairs now?"
"Mr. Sin (申상) and Mr. Yi (李상) are both out."
"What? Isn't Mr. Yi your husband?"
"No. My husband (박갓 어룬 - outside elder) is Yang Kŭn-hwan (梁槿煥). Mr. Yi also went out yesterday afternoon, saying he had something to do, and hasn't come back yet."
"Mr. Sin?"
"Well... I think he'll be back tonight."
"Setting him aside, was Mr. Yi at this house yesterday?"
"He was preparing for a trip since yesterday morning."
"Did he not go out?"
Then the detective told her that when the housemate Mr. Sin came in, he should come to the Nishikanda Station (西神田署). The matter was nothing to worry about, just that he had something to ask about a friend's affairs. <94>

Culprit Identified Through Housemate's Neutrality
The investigation bureau banned newspaper publication (新聞 載揭 禁止) and interrogated over 100 suspects day and night, but found no leads.
Detective Ōsawa was also extremely fatigued, having rushed to the investigation headquarters to interrogate promising suspects reported from various stations. It had been exactly four days since he went out to Waseda Tsurumakichō. Just as he was thinking of investigating the Yi family at number 62 once more—around 8 PM on the 21st, a call came from Nishikanda Station.
It was a report that Yi's housemate, Sin Hyŏn-sŏng (申鉉聲, 25), a student at Seisoku English School (正則英語學校), had arrived, so he immediately drove over.
Sin Hyŏn-sŏng was a smart-looking student. He had a clean and seemingly honest face. From his mouth came the information:
Yi was a peddler (行商人) who had never even been to school. The house was rented by Yi, but now Yang Kŭn-hwan (梁槿煥, 28) acted as the head of the household, and his wife Katsu (가쓰, 22) managed everything. Katsu was the daughter of a construction foreman (土工長) named Ishikawa (石川) who had followed Yang, charmed by his diligent studying despite poverty (苦學), and they even had two children, Chŏng-ja (貞子, 6) and Ai[-ko?] (*, 2). Both Yang and Yi were members of the Peninsula People's Friendship Association (半島人同友會 - Tonguhoe) and had been hauled in together before. Yang had returned strangely by rickshaw (人力車) around 11 AM on the 16th, gave Chŏng-ja a dahlia flower (따리아꽃), and the women in the tenement house (長屋) had gossiped about it. He answered everything he was asked.
Detective Ōsawa, who had just been responding with "Uh-huh, uh-huh," asked in a soft voice unsuited to his face,
"So where might Yang have gone?"
"Mr. Yang (梁君) borrowed Yi's clothes and black fedora after dinner (夕陽 食後) on the 16th and went off somewhere."
"Ha! Didn't Yang own any clothes for going out?"
"He's poor, even his indigo kasuri clothes are stitched up (꿰젔어요). Lately, it seems he doesn't earn anything even when he goes out."
"When people are poor, they tend to do bad things (나쁜 즛), so humans must work. Here! Have some tea and snacks."
Detective Ōsawa was anxious. That night, Sin was taken to the investigation headquarters. Although technically as a person of interest, the Metropolitan Police, led by Section Chief Koizumi, quickly deployed an active investigation team. Avoiding newspaper reporters, five or six detectives waited for a car in the darkness near Hibiya Park. <95>
They had left for a house search (家宅搜査) at Yang's home in Waseda Tsurumakichō. That night, at Yang's house, his wife, perhaps waiting for Sin to return, hadn't even locked the entrance. Startled awake by the sound of the car pulling up late at night, the detectives ignored the children and the trembling Katsu and began rummaging through the house haphazardly. From an old box in the closet, they found worn-out indigo kasuri hakama and Kokura hakama with seams coming apart.
"Are these what Yang changed out of?"
Detective Ōsawa brought his face close to Katsu's tear-stained one and demanded. Red bloodstains were soaked into every seam. Moreover, the indigo cloth (紺) was the same old fabric as the strap on the geta discarded in front of the Station Hotel entrance.
If Yang was the culprit, they first needed to find out his whereabouts. A strict interrogation (追求) of Katsu, holding her young child, began on the spot. Only at dawn did she confess that Yang had said he would write a letter from Dairen (大連). The house search team, celebrating their victory (凱歌), took Katsu and her young children into protective custody at Waseda Station (早稻田署) and, while returning to headquarters, immediately ordered surveillance (取締) for Yang at all ports in the Kansai region. Steamers bound for Dairen were subjected to strict inspections by water police (水上署員) everywhere.

Culprit Caught During Departure from Nagasaki

Nine days after the incident occurred, on the evening of the 24th, a sudden telegram arrived at the investigation headquarters.
"Have arrested wanted fugitive Yang Kŭn-hwan."
The culprit was finally caught. At the investigation headquarters, buzzing like sparrows, Inspectors Arimatsu (有松) and Tsuchiya (土屋) immediately rushed to Kyūshū (九州). Simultaneously, the wanted alert was lifted. At headquarters, the investigation teams declared it was finally time for a drink and raised their glasses repeatedly. Only Detective Ōsawa couldn't drink the alcohol he loved, besieged by newspaper reporters.
The culprit was transported on the 28th. Wearing a black fedora (黑中折) and a short coat (半*), the culprit was disembarked at Shinbashi Station (新*驛) in extreme secrecy, but the passengers somehow found out, causing a huge commotion.
Yang stated that his crime was an independent act, carried out because he believed Min's suffrage movement (參政權獲得運動) hindered the independence movement (獨立運動). However, the Tonguhoe (同友會) they organized was a secret society (神秘結社) connected with rebellious Koreans (不逞의 鮮人) in Shanghai, Kando (間島), and San Francisco (산푸란씨스코).
Below is his confession from the crime to his arrest.
On the 14th, before the crime, using the alias Yi Ki-nyŏng (李基寧), a Nihon University student, he visited Mr. Min at the Station Hotel <96> and discussed the pros and cons of the suffrage movement. The next day, he said,
"I admire your opinion, teacher, but there are assassins (刺客) around you, so please change rooms,"
and kindly advised him, leading Min to move to room 14.
He committed the crime the morning after that. First, he received 54 yen from a friend, a certain *benshi* (辨士 - silent film narrator) at the Asakusa San'yūkan (淺草三友舘), bought a short sword (短刀) for 2 yen 30 sen with that money, and entered the hotel. He didn't ask for directions at the hotel, knocked on the door of the room he already knew well, and had his third meeting.
Initially, he asked about the situation, saying the Tonguhoe was thinking of welcoming him, but eventually, it turned into another heated argument (激論). On the spot, he drew the short sword he had hidden, stabbed Min, lodging it in his right shoulder, then blocked the fleeing Min's path and stabbed him in the chest (胸中).
After that, he fled from Tōkyō Station to Shinagawa (品*), discarded the hotel slippers he was wearing in front of the station, and bought other footwear. At that time, seeing an artificial red dahlia flower (따리야꽃 造花) lying on the road in front of the station, he suddenly felt an urge to see his wife and child once. He picked up the dahlia flower and, unaware of anything else, boarded a JNR (省線) train. Some time later, he got off at Takadanobaba (高田馬場), took a rickshaw, and returned home. Having decided to flee to his comrades in Manchuria (滿洲), he intended to part with his wife and child. After eating dinner (前後の夕食?), he fled to Yokohama (橫濱) as a light rain fell. The next day, the 17th, disguised in a worker's short coat (印半*), he boarded the Takasagomaru (高砂丸) bound for Dairen and headed for Nagasaki (長崎).
In Nagasaki, spending his last night in Japan, he went ashore and slept at a mainland-style inn (本*旅宿). However, the image of his beloved child Chŏng-ja (貞子), happily playing with the artificial flower he had given her, kept appearing before his eyes, and he couldn't bear to sleep.
The next morning, hearing that the mail steamer Yahatamaru (八幡丸) bound for Dairen was departing, he gave the alias of a carpenter from Ibaraki Prefecture (茨城縣) and boarded the ship. However, shortly after entering his cabin, he was exposed during an inspection (檢索) by water police (水上署員). <97>

**Summary**

This article, a memoir by Japanese detective Ōsawa, recounts the investigation into the assassination of Min Wŏn-sik, a prominent Korean figure advocating for suffrage within the Japanese empire, at the Tōkyō Station Hotel in February 1923. It details the discovery of the crime, the initial confusion, the methodical police work focusing on clues like a dropped memo and a pair of geta, the identification of the suspect Yang Kŭn-hwan through interviewing his housemate, the house search yielding bloodstained clothes, and Yang's eventual arrest in Nagasaki while attempting to flee to Dairen. Yang confessed he killed Min because he believed Min's pro-suffrage activities undermined the Korean independence movement.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a Japanese police viewpoint on a politically charged assassination involving Korean activists. It highlights the divisions within the Korean community regarding strategies towards Japanese rule (collaboration/reform vs. independence), depicts the surveillance and suspicion under which Koreans lived in Japan, and provides details about the networks (like the Tonguhoe) connecting activists across different locations. The casual mention of Min's connections to high-ranking Japanese figures like Itō Hirobumi contrasts sharply with Yang's motivation rooted in anti-colonial sentiment, illustrating the complex political landscape of the time.

名刑事 手帖에서, 梁槿煥 捷捕 秘話, 東京警視廳 大澤刑事 手記
그것이 大正 12년 2월 16일-日比谷에서는 帝國議會가 개최되여 政客들이 왕래* 빈번한 때 였다. 그날 東京驛前派出所의 藤木 巡査는 오전 10시반의 교대를 마치고 驛前廣場에 나왔다. 입춘을 압둔 겨을해는 포근히 내려 쪼인다.
그때 별로 딴 생각도 없이 눈이 가는대로 東京驛 벽돌건물을 바라보는 순사의 눈이 팔각탑측의 2층 밀창으로부터 점박인 小倉 「하까마」를 입은 청년 하나가 휙 뛰여 나리더니 창까에 있는 소나무 가지에 뛰여붓자 7, 8척이나 있을 듯한 그 나무 가지에서 슬슬 뛰여나려 수풀 속에 감춰버린다. 그 청년은 무엇엔가 랑패하고 있는 듯한 비선바닥을 인상적으로 번득이면서 精養軒料理店 앞허로부터 中央郵便局 부근의 사람물결속에 잠겨버린다.
「퍽도 날샌 사내로구나」
하고 感心하고 있을 적에 돌연히
「살인이야! 살인이야!」＜90＞
하고 驚愕하여 부르짓는 소리가 停車場호텔늘창으로서 들려왔다. 그제야 비로소 정신차린 藤木 순사는 그 청년이 수상타고 그 뒤를 따랐으나 벌서 어디로 갔는지 흔적조차 없었다.

被殺되기는 閔元植
스테-숀, 호텔 3층 14호실에서는 수일 전부터 朝鮮人參政權獲得運動때문에 上京하여 있는 朝鮮國民協會長 閔元植씨(35)가 端鹿안 모-닝 입은 채 응접실의 원탁 겻해* 피에 저저 쓰러젔다. 銳利한 인物로 억개와 腹部를 찔너였고 前額部(이마)에도 찔니워있었다.
綠色빗 깐자리는 꺼멋케 피덩이로 물드리그 피 속에 右手에 黃色鉛筆이 구을너 있었다. 血沬를 바든 소파 가운데 수수걱기* *은 메모용지 한 장이-閔元植의 達筆로 무엇인가 직히여 있었다.
死體 엽의 헤는 5,6의 朝鮮紳士들이 울고 있었다. 이 사람들은 閔씨와 함께 上京한 國民協會 幹部들로 櫻田木鄕町의 淸光舘이라는 여관에 分宿하고 있었다가 사건의 직전-아마 9시 반쯤 하여 閔元植씨로부터의 전화로
「오늘따라 웨 빨니 오지 안나」
하고 꾸짓는 듯 호출을 당하고 급히 와본 즉 호텔 뽀-이가 「살인이야! 살이이야」하면서 廊下에서 떠들고 있었다 한다.
閔씨 絶命하기 전 바로 5분 전 일이다.
안어 이르킨 閔씨는 여렀의 선생님 선생님하고 부르는 소리에 의식을 약간 차린 모양으로
「동무, 동무」
「의사, 의사」
「警視廳, 警視廳」
하고 두 번씩이나 분명히 국어로 외치고는 그는 숨이 끈어저버렀다.
警視廳으로부터 형사부장 이하 약 30명의 敏腕의 형사가 달려오고 西田 豫審判事, 松田 檢事 등 재판소의 일행도 현장에 도착, 이내 檢視를 시작이 되였다.
맨 처음 이 현장을 발견한 뽀-이 竹村留吉이 小泉 수사과장 앞헤 불니워 나왔다. 그의 말을 드르면 3층 廊下의 구부러지는 모퉁이에서 와락와락 달여오는(곤가스리) 감봉을 입은 학생 비슷한 청년이 정면으로 맛부드첬는데 그 서슬에 엽헤 끼였든 短刀를 떨그었다. 刀渡 6寸의 白鞘에는 꺼먼 피선지가 부텃었다. 큰일 낫다고 생각할 때에는 벌서 그 사네는 놀나서 短刀를 도로 차저들고 2층에 도망하며＜91＞ 내려다렀다. 곳 뒤로 따랏스나 모양이 보이지 안키에 다시 3층에 도라와본즉 14호실의 방문이 열여 제친다고 있었다.
범인은 無帽요 호텔의 집세기를 신었다. 학생인 듯, 日本 內地人인 듯한 얼골이라고 竹村 뽀-이는 말했다.

여기에서 閔元植씨의 경력을 이약기하자. 京畿道 楊平郡, 閔泳憶의 아들로 閔妃의 족하라 한다. 明治26년 東學黨亂에 兩親이 암살되였음으로 上海에 도망하였으나 11세 때에 歸鮮하여 의지할 데도 없어 잘 쓰는 書畵를 揮毫하면서 各地를 漂泊하였다. 同31년 東京에 와서 副島*臣, 土方久元, 伊藤博文 등의 知遇* 어든 뒤부터 大亞西亞主義를 주장함에 이르렀다. 그 뒤 歸國하여 韓國 內部內臣 閔泳綺 아래서 衛生課長이 되고 廣濟院長도 되였으나 日韓합병 후는 中樞院에 있었다. 國民協會長은 大正8년부터로 전혀 半島民의 參政權附與請願運動에 一身을 바처왔다.
이번 上京은 셋재번의 請願運動으로 同9일에 入京, 15일에는 衆議院을 방문, 약 26명의 代議士로부터 略名快諾을 어더었다.
이날 아츰 9시 15분경 朝飯을 먹고난 뒤 淸光舘旅舘으로부터 부하들이 오기를 기다려, 請願運動에 열심한 원조를 하여주는 一宮 代議士를 방문키로 되여 있었다.
범행은 부하들을 기다리고 있는 약 50분 동안에 수행된 것이다. 범인은 學生風의 사내에 틀님 없으리라. 그러나 盜賊은 아니다. 半島民의 參政權獲得運動에 반대하는 자인가? 그러터레도 內地人인가 朝鮮人인가. 警視廳 當局의 머리에는 판단이 서지 안었다.
쏘파 속에 떠러진 「메모」
大澤 형사는 死骸를 한번 보자 쏘파 가운데 떠러진 메모용지를 물끄럼히 드려다 보았다.
「日本大學, 朝鮮基督敎靑年會, 同友會, 500인 白南薰(청년회장), 本鄕湯島天神町62, 西村方 李基寧(28)」
이라고 閔씨 독특의 名筆로 적혀 있었다. 大澤 형사는 그 분잡한 14호실을 빠저 나와 호텔 受付에 나타났다.
「누가 오늘 아츰 閔씨에 방문자가 있앀ˆ어요?」
「아모개도 없어요. 다만 단 한 사람이 여들點쯤 小倉의 젊은 사내가 帖場의 안내도 없이 급한 거름으로 3층으로 올나갔어요」
「어듸로부터 드러 왔어?」＜92＞
「거기 현관으로서요」
「응-」
大澤 형사는 묵묵히 입구편인 집신 버서놋는 상자 겻흐로 갓다.
산란한 집신짝속에 달아 떨어진 게다가 한엽에 노여 있었다. 게다 한쪽은 하나오(鼻緖)가 끈어저서 그 대신 곤가스리(감봉)의 헌겁 끈으로 동이여 있었다.
형사는 그 게다를 소중한 드시 들고 도라왔다.
警視廳에서는 正力 형사과장을 중심으로 수사회의가 열니였다. 이 사건은 중대한 조직적 범죄의 일부분일넌지 모른다. 범인은 아직 시내에 잠복하여 있을는지 모른나고 상상되였다.
긴급 경계의 지령이 管下 全署을 긴장케 하고 역이나 기차, 전차 속에서도 감봉의 남자에게 私服刑事의 눈이 빛낫다. 警視廳의 수사본부로서도 50여명의 형사가 四面八方에 날녓다.
半島人의 學生浪人을 한쪽으로 조사하기 시작하였다.
수사본부에는 벌서 수십명의 嫌異者와 參考人이 연행되어 사건은 暗中模索인대로 17일로 넘기였다.

六十二番地의 李와 第六感

인간은 자유로 僞名을 쓰는 것이나 돌연한 경우는 자기와 전연 무관계한 문자는 좀처럼 나오지 않는 것이다.

同居人의 中立오로 犯人 判明
수사국은 신문 載揭를 금지하고 100명도 더 넘는 嫌疑者를 連日連夜 取調하였으나 아모 잡히는 종직이 없었다.
大澤형사도 各 署로부터의 보고하여 으는 有力 嫌疑者의 訊問調査에 수사본부에 달녀 와서 몹시 피로하였다. 早稻田鶴卷町에 나선 뒤부터 꼭 4일만이다. 다시 한번 62번지의 李 一家를 조사하여 보려고 생각하든 지음-同 21일 밤 8시경, 西神田署로부터 전화가 왔다.
李의 동거인으로 正則英語學校 生徒 申鉉聲(25)의 왔다는 보고이기에 곳 자동차를 모랐다.
申鉉聲은 스마-트한 학생이었다. 깨끗하며 정직할 것 가튼 얼골을 하고 있었다. 그 입으로
李는 행상인인데 학교에도 가 본 일이 없다. 그 집은 李가 빌닌 집이나 지금은 梁槿煥(28년)이 主人役으로 그 여편네 가쓰(22년)가 죄다 마터 가지고 있다. 가쯔는 石川이라는 土工長의 딸로 梁의 苦學함에 惑心하여 따라왔는데 貞子(6년) 愛*(2년) 두 아이까지 나았다. 梁과 李는 半島人同友會에 入會하여서 나노 끌니어 간 일이 있다. 梁은 16일 오전 11시였다. 이상하게 人力車를 타고 도라와서 貞子에게 따리아꽃이랑 주군해서 『長屋』의 여편네들이 떠들어 대였다고 물어 보는대로 대답하는 것이다.
「응응」하는 대답만 하든 大澤형사는 그 얼골에 얼니지 않는 부드러운 소리로
「그러면 梁은 어디 갔을가」
하고 물었다.
「梁君은 16일 夕陽 食後 李의 옷과 검정 중절모를 어더 가지고 어디론가 갔읍니다」
「하! 梁은 외출복을 갖지 않었든가?」
「가난해서 감봉의 옷도 그거나마 꿰젔어요. 요새는 외출하드래도 벌진 않는가 봐요」

大澤형사는 초조하였다. 그날 밤 申은 수사본부에 연행되었다. 물론 참고인으로서였으나 警視廳에서는 小泉과장 이하 급히 활발한 搜査陣을 전개하고 신문기자를 피한 형사들 5, 6명이 日比谷公園측 어두운데서 자동차를 기다리고 있었다.＜95＞
早稻田鶴卷町 梁의 집 家宅*査를 떠났든 것이다. 그날 밤 梁의 집에선 그 안해가 申이 도라오길 기다렸든지 출입구를 잠그지도 않고 있었다. 깊은 밤 달여든 자동차 소리에 깨여난 아이들과 벌벌 떠는 가쓰를 보는 체도 않고 형사들은 함부로 집을 뒤지기 시작했다. 벽장 속 헌 상자에서 다 해진 감봉의 하까마와 솔기가 다 해여진 고구라 하까마.
「이것은 梁이 갈어 입은 것인가」
大澤형사는 눈물에 저는 가쓰의 얼골 갓가히 밧삭 디레대고 물었다. 솔기마다 빨간 피가 배였다. 더구나 그 紺변은 스테손... 호텔 현관 앞에 버서버린 게다 끈과 똑같은 헌겁이다.
범인이 梁이라면 위선 범인의 행방을 알어두지 않어선 안된다. 어린 것을 안고 있는 가쯔에게 그 자리에서 엄중한 追求가 개시되였다. 새벽이 되여서야 梁이 大連에서 편지 하겠다드란 말을 고백하였다. 凱歌를 부른 家宅搜査 일행은 가쯔와 그 幼兒들을 早稻田署에 보호하고 본부에 도라가면서 곳 關西 방면 각 항구에 梁의 取締를 命하었다. 大連行 汽船은 곳곳에서 水上署員의 엄중한 검사를 받게 되였다.

長崎出帆에서 犯人이 잪이다

사건 발생 9일 되든 24일 夕陽에 揭査본부에 돌연 전보가 들어 왔다.
