Financial and Social Circles

4,000 Koreans in Shanghai
According to a recent account from someone who returned from Shanghai, the number of Koreans in Shanghai is increasing daily, reaching about 4,000. Furthermore, there are reportedly 3,000 in Nanjing and other areas of Central China. Koreans are said to be proficient in English and Chinese, thus many are engaged in jobs like interpretation.

If the Conscription Law is Implemented Next Year, Many Koreans in the Manchukuo Army
It has been repeatedly reported that 30 Koreans are currently among the national representatives of the Concordia Association (協和會). Now, as the conscription law is expected to be implemented in Manchukuo starting next year, although there are already many Korean officers and soldiers like Lieutenant Colonel Yi Yangjung, it is drawing significant attention that from next year, all young men aged 20 will undergo conscription physicals, and among the 1.2 million Koreans, several tens of thousands of eligible individuals are likely to be enlisted.

Frequent Tokyo Visits by Notables - Messrs. Song Chinu, Yŏ Unhyŏng, Sin T'aewak, etc.
Recently, one sees that many notables, carrying various errands, are traveling back and forth to Tokyo with increasing frequency. Here are a few of them:
Mr. Song Chinu = Recently packed his bags and left his Wŏn* Study (苑*書齋) for Tokyo. The purpose is said to be perhaps for sightseeing or perhaps related to newspaper business, but the details are unknown.
Mr. Yi Chongman = Attended meetings at the Government-General as the Gold Production Council (産金協議會) was held, and recently went to Tokyo for about a two-week trip and returned.
Mr. Sin T'aewak = Busy in the legal circles, traveled to Tokyo, Osaka, etc., for about two weeks and returned.
Mr. Yŏ Unhyŏng = Also went to Tokyo and returned regarding matters related to his children's education.
Mr. Yi Kagyŏng* (李覺*) = Stayed in Tokyo for several days during the early summer season and returned recently.

Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl's Estate: 1 Million Yen - Life Insurance Also Around 30,000 Yen
Regarding Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl, the president (頭取) of the Commercial Bank (商業銀行) and Honorary Consul General of Manchukuo＜2＞, who collapsed from cerebral anemia in the Government-General's Education Bureau Director's office and passed away, an investigation into his estate revealed the following: although rumors circulated that he had several million yen, in reality, land in Chŏlla Province and other assets entrusted by his father to the Chosŏn Trust (朝鮮信託) amounted to about 1 million yen. Besides that, he had about 300,000 yen in stocks in the Commercial Bank where he was president. Other than that, there was his residence at Sogyŏk-chong 144 and suburban villa land. From this total, several hundred thousand yen had already been distributed to his younger siblings and his children and nephews, so the actual amount was around 1 million yen. Also, regarding life insurance, although there were rumors he had taken out the largest amount in Chosŏn, investigation showed it was only about 30,000 yen.

Mr. Pak's Share Count in the Commercial Bank
Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl reportedly held 10,470 shares in the Commercial Bank.

Korean Policewomen in Nanjing; Already 30 Chinese Women
Recently, new rumors are heard from Nanjing that Korean women are applying for recruitment as policewomen. Although their numbers are not yet large, they are expected to be hired in the future, and Korean policewomen will be patrolling the streets of Nanjing. Currently, there are about 30 Chinese policewomen in Nanjing, primarily assigned to duties such as body searches of Chinese women coming and going at various city gates like Kwanghwamun (光華明 - likely 光化門), Siaomun (小西門), T'ungwanmun (通灣門), and train stations, with 5 bobbed-hair policewomen stationed at each location.
The exam includes composition, arithmetic, and language. Working hours are in two shifts, from 8 AM to 9 PM.

Yŏngch'ang Sŏgwan Buys Land for 120,000 Yen
Mr. Kang Hŭisu of Yŏngch'ang Sŏgwan (永昌書舘) recently purchased the land next to the Hanch'ŏng Building (韓靑삘딩), a prime location at the Chongno intersection in Seoul, for 120,000 yen. As the current company building (社屋) will soon be demolished due to the opening of Sinchang Road, they plan to build a magnificent new company building here and relocate.

Taeryuk Building to be Completed This Time
Mr. Ha Chunsŏk, president of the Chosŏn Engineering Society (朝鮮工作會), had planned to construct the Taeryuk Building (大陸빌딍) on the former site of the Chongno Police Station in Seoul. However, due to the current situation, construction had been delayed. Recently, it was decided to proceed with a temporary structure (假建築) for now, building a two-story brick building. Construction is underway, with costs estimated at around 200,000 yen and completion expected by this autumn, around August.

Propaganda Leaflet Text
To induce enemy soldiers on the front lines to surrender, our forces drop many propaganda leaflets from airplanes. The text of a leaflet recently dropped over a certain battlefield in Central China was as follows:
"What has Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) given you? Is it not just defeat and the destitution of the people? Quickly throw down your guns, discard your swords, surrender to the Japanese army, and strive under the flag of the new government for the construction of a new prosperous Asian China (興亞新支那建設)!"

Mourners for Historian Mun Ilp'yŏng
The funeral for the late historian Mun Ilp'yŏng, who devoted his 50-year life to historical research before passing away, included a farewell ceremony held the other day in the plaza in front of the former Chosŏn Ilbo building. Numerous figures attended, including Yun Ch'iho, Hŏ Hŏn, Pang Ŭngmo, Ch'oe Rin, Hyŏn Sangyun, Yi Hun'gu, Yi Yŏsŏng, Ch'a Sangch'an, Kim Tongin, Hong Myŏnghŭi, Ham Sanghun, Kim Hyŏngwŏn, Yi Kŭngno, and Kim Tonghwan.

Night School at Posŏng Middle School
As a way to alleviate the difficulty of school entrance, two-shift middle schools, i.e., night middle schools, are being actively established in places like Osaka. Therefore, Posŏng Middle School (普成中學校) in Seoul also applied to the educational authorities (學務當局) to establish a night middle school, but it was recently disapproved due to insufficient facilities.＜4＞

Memorial Auditorium at Chungang Middle School
Chungang Middle School (中央中學校), located in Kyesan-ildong, Seoul, which has produced numerous talented individuals (수재) on the peninsula, celebrated its 30th anniversary last year. As one of its commemorative projects, it plans to build a memorial auditorium by collecting donations (淨財) from parents and current students. The budget is 30,000 yen, and completion is expected by this autumn.

Congratulating Mr. Pak Hŭngsik
On the evening of April 21st, Messrs. Yun Ch'iho, Kim Kidŭk, Ch'oe Rin, Pak Hŭido, Kim Chuik, and about 10 others gathered at the Chosŏn Hotel and hosted a reception for Mr. Pak Hŭngsik, who recently donated a large sum (巨財) to manage the Hyŏpsŏng Industrial School (協成實業學校) (formerly Osŏng School).

Mr. Yŏ Unhong Takes Over Management of Paegyangsa for 15,000 Yen
The Paegyangsa Garden Restaurant (白合園料亭) on Chongno 2-chome, Seoul, renowned as a meeting place for the social elite, had been managed by Mr. Kim Tongsong until now. Recently, Mr. Yŏ Unhong took it over, paying a premium (권리금) of 15,000 yen, and will manage it.

Mr. Han Inbo Donates 30,000 Yen
Mr. Han Inbo, who once became a major topic in our society by offering to donate several hundred thousand yen (巨財) to take over and manage P'yŏngyang's Sungshil College (崇實專門) when its lifeline seemed to be hanging by a thread (명맥이 風前에 있을 지음에), later caused great trouble (말성) by withdrawing his donation, as previously reported (旣報). Subsequently, several P'yŏngyang figures, including lawyer Chu Kanch'in, deeming this unacceptable (不可라 하여), filed a lawsuit. Recently, a settlement was reached where Mr. Han proposed two conditions:
1. He will make an appropriate (응분의) donation to Taedong Engineering College (大同工專), the successor institution (後身인) to P'yŏngyang Sungshil, to the best of his ability (誠力껏).
2. He will contribute 30,000 yen to P'yŏngyang society for useful purposes.
As a result, the lawsuit was reportedly withdrawn (取下되였다고 한다).

Ch'anggyŏngwŏn Night Cherry Blossoms: 60,000 Yen
This year's night cherry blossom viewing (夜櫻) at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn was held for 10 days starting April 17th. During that period, the average nightly attendance reached 30,000 people, totaling 300,000 visitors. With an admission fee of 20 chŏn each, the exact revenue (正額) reached 60,000 yen. Although some of this money was spent on facility and personnel costs (施設人費) for electric decorations (電飾) etc., for the night viewing, most of it＜5＞ was income. The Yi Royal Household Office (李王職) is said to use this money to supplement expenses for animal feed (飼料) at the zoo and the management of Ch'anggyŏngwŏn.

Literary, Film, and Theater Circles

Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi to Perform in Twelve Countries - Scheduled for Berlin in May
According to a recent letter from Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, who has left the United States (米國) and is now in Paris (巴里), contracts have already been signed for her to perform (入演키로) in top theaters in 12 countries, including England (英), France (佛), and Germany (獨), by December of this year. Performances in Berlin (伯林) are scheduled to start in May. During her tour of Europe and America, Ms. Ch'oe has also devised (創案한) more Korean dances. To prepare these more thoroughly (충실히), there was news requesting more costumes and musical instruments to be sent from Chosŏn. It is reported that a woman named [*]kawa Hisa (*川ヒサ), a graduate of Kyŏngsŏng Girls' High School (京城高女) who worked as a secretary at Ms. Ch'oe's dance institute, is set to depart for Paris soon.

Mr. Ch'oe Yonghwan's Estate: 150,000 Yen
It was previously reported (旣報) that Mr. Ch'oe Yonghwan, a former employee of the Tong-A Ilbo, passed away during a trip to North China where he intended to flee (飛流코저). After his death, his estate amounted to 150,000 yen.＜6＞ As a result of discussions between his widow, Ms. Im Hyojŏng (former professor at the Women's Commercial School), and relatives, it was decided to allocate 50,000 yen for the education of his descendants, 50,000 yen for social public works, and another 50,000 yen as family public property.

Kim Tongin Becomes Nouveau Riche (卒富)
Novelist Kim Tongin purchased 7,000-8,000 pyeong of land in front of West P'yŏngyang Station 15 or 16 years ago for about 200 yen. Due to the recent economic conditions, land prices soared (騰貴하여), and it was sold (팔니어) for 4 yen 20 chŏn per pyeong, allowing him to pocket about 40,000 yen in cash. The sickly (病弱한) Mr. Kim is now expected to pursue his literary path without worrying about living expenses, which is considered good news (朗報) in the literary world, and his friends rejoice (깁버한다).

Yun Sŏkchung Studies Abroad (留學)
It was previously reported (旣報) that Mr. Pang Ŭngmo, president of the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), had employee Mr. Kim Kirim enroll at Tohoku Imperial University (東北帝大) and study there for three years. Mr. Kim already graduated this spring and returned to the company (歸社하였으며). Furthermore, Mr. Yun Sŏkchung, who was the chief editor (편집주임) of the company's "Sonyŏn" (少年) magazine and a children's song writer (동요작가), has been sent to study abroad (留學) in Tokyo starting this spring, studying at Rikkyō University (立敎大).

The names of the delegation members are as follows (如左):

Mr. Yi Sŏkhun Joins Chosŏn Ilbo (朝報)
Writer Mr. Yi Sŏkhun, who had been working at the Hamhŭng Broadcasting Station (咸興放送局), resigned (辭하고) from there and joined the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝報 - likely referring to 朝鮮日報), taking charge (맛게 되었다고) of editing the "Sonyŏn" (少年) section.＜7＞

**Summary**

This 1939 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine provides a snapshot of Korean society under Japanese rule, focusing on the elite. It details the significant wealth held by some Koreans, compares them to Japanese counterparts, and notes the growing Korean population in China and Manchukuo, including their roles in interpretation and potential future military service in Manchukuo. The piece chronicles the comings and goings of prominent figures (intellectuals, businessmen, lawyers) between Korea and Japan, social events, business dealings (like Pak Hŭngsik's ventures and property transactions), and cultural news involving writers (Kim Tongin's windfall, Yi Kwangsu's move), artists (Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's international tour), and journalists (staff changes at major newspapers). It also touches upon educational initiatives and civic matters like donations and building projects. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the economic status and activities of the Korean elite, their mobility within the Japanese empire, the presence and roles of Koreans abroad (Shanghai, Manchukuo), the cultural landscape (literature, dance, film), and the social dynamics involving Japanese interactions (like the visiting literary group and propaganda). The mention of conscription in Manchukuo and Korean involvement in its administration (Hyŏphwahoe) is particularly noteworthy.

財界, 社會方面
一千萬圓 以上가는 富豪 百三十名-崔昌學,閔大植,金秊洙씨 등은 어떻게 되나-
崔昌學,閔大植 등 千萬長者가 朝鮮에도 많치만은 日本 전국을 통터러 처보면 놀납게도 多數하야 一非 손꼬락으로 곱을 길이 없다.
이제 昭和13度의 소득세 上에 나타난 전국의 富豪數를 보면 이러하다. 물론 이 속에는 崔昌學, 閔大植, 金鴻亮,閔奎植, 韓亮鎬, 元胤洙, 金秊洙 등 한다하는 朝鮮 부자들도 많이 끼여 있는 터이다. 그리고 이제 이러케 소득액은 알 수 있겠지만 그 재산은 얼마나 될가함을 알려하거든 재산에 1割이 그 소득이라 가정한다면 4백만원 年收者의 재산은 4천만원 이상이요 백만원 이상은 亦 천만원 정도의 재산가로 보면 틀님이 없다.

上海에 朝鮮人 四千名
上海서 도라온 최근 某씨의 談에 의하면 현재 上海에는 朝鮮人이 날로 증가하여 약 4천 명의 多數에 달하며 다시 南京과 기타 中支 일대에도 3천 명이나 있다 하는데 朝鮮사람은 영어와 支那말을 잘함으로 통역같은 일에 많이 종사하고 있다한다.

明年度부터 兵役法 實施하면 滿州軍人에 朝鮮人 多數
協和會의 전국 대표 중 현재 30인이 朝鮮人이 석기여 있다 함은 屢報한 바 이제 滿州國에는 明年度부터 병역법이 실시되리라한즉 이리 되면 현재에도 李亮中佐 등 多數한 朝鮮人 장교와 병정들이 있지만은 아마 명년부터는 滿 20세된 청년들은 모다 징병검사를 시행할 터인 즉 120만의 朝鮮人 속으로서도 여러 만명의 적격자가 入營될 듯 하다하야 크게 주목을 끄을고 있다.

名士의 東京往來頻繁 - 宋鎭禹·呂運亨·辛泰嶽氏 等이-
최근에 名士 다수* 種種의 용무를 帶하고 東京으로 왕래함이 심히 滋甚한 것을 본다, 이제 그 중의 數三氏를 기하면
宋鎭禹씨=최근에 旅裝을 차리고 苑*書齋로부터 나와 東京으로 갓는데 용무는 혹은 遊覽次라고도 하고 혹은 신문사 일인 듯 하다고도 하나 仔細는 未詳하다.
李鍾萬씨=産金協議會가 열니어 總督府 회의에 각금 출입하다가 日前에 약 2주일 여정으로 東京으로 다녀오다.
辛泰嶽씨=법조계에 있어 多事한 氏는 약 2주일 예정으로 東京 大阪 등지로 往還하다.
呂運亨씨=子弟의 교육관계 일도 東京에 단녀오다.
