First Published Book
"Mujŏng (Heartlessness)"

Yi Kwangsu

My first published book is "Mujŏng" (無情). I still haven't forgotten the time when I was writing "Mujŏng". It seems it's because it was a time when I was enduring great hardship.
At that time, there were more than one or two instances when I lost consciousness from hunger, and not being able to buy textbooks was secondary; it was a period when I frequently couldn't even go to school because I couldn't pay the tuition fees right away.
Even while suffering like this, I steadily completed this novel. From the first installment to the last, it was serialized in the *Maeil Sinbo* (每日申報) for about 300 installments. As for the manuscript fee, at first, they sent 5 wŏn per month, but later they sent 10 wŏn per month.

"Piripin ŭi Kwagŏ wa Hyŏnjae (The Past and Present of the Philippines)"
Yi Yŏsŏng

My first published book was "Piripin ŭi Kwagŏ wa Hyŏnjae" (比律賓의 과거와 현재); 1,500 copies were printed, and there was no manuscript fee (別無).

"Na ŭi Ch'amhoe (My Confession)"
Kim Ansŏ

My first published work was a translation of Tolstoy's "My Confession" (나의 籤悔), published by Hansŏng Tosŏ Hoesa (漢城圖書會社). I think I received 70 or 80 wŏn for the manuscript fee back then, but I don't remember clearly now.
Looking back, it's a dimly bygone past. Of course, looking at it now, the style and terminology are nonsensical and incredibly childish. But regardless, it was unexpected that parts were even deleted (削除) from it. I worked hard, comparing the English translation and the Japanese translation as I translated.
I was twenty-six or seven years old then. Although it was just a translation, it was my first stand-alone book, so the joy I felt then is unforgettable now.

"Paektusan Tŭngjŏkki (Record of Climbing Paektu Mountain)"
An Chaehong

The first of my writings to become a stand-alone book is "Paektusan Tŭngjŏkki" (白頭山 登陟記), but since I entrusted it to the publisher, I still don't know the amount of royalty (印稅) income.

"Arŭmdaun Saebyŏk (Beautiful Dawn)"
Chu Yohan

The poetry collection called "Arŭmdaun Saebyŏk" (아름다운 새벽) is the first work I put out into the world.
Now, I think it's only worthy of being burned, but back then, it seemed quite something to be proud of. Though I suppose it's customary to feel that way about almost any work!
It was published in 1924. I didn't receive a single penny for the manuscript fee.
To date, I've only received manuscript fees exactly twice. Once was when Mr. Chŏn Mugil was publishing the magazine "Taejo" (大潮), I received a total of 2 wŏn for five *sijo* (詩調) poems. Another time was when Mr. Pang In'gŭn was running *Chosŏn Mundan* (朝鮮文壇), I received 10 wŏn for a "Mundan Wŏlp'yŏng" (文壇月評 - Monthly Literary Review) (about ten pages). So, if you add it up, it's quite a large sum, isn't it? Haha.

"Kukkyŏng ŭi Pam (Night on the Border)"
P'ain

My first published book is the poetry collection "Kukkyŏng ŭi Pam" (國境의 밤). I made it after receiving the impulse to publish a poetry collection through Ansŏ's (岸曙) recommendation (勸).
However, I want to burn all that too and publish just one poetry collection into which I've poured my true capabilities (역량), making it my first and also my final (최후) poetry collection.<58>

**Summary**

This article presents responses from several prominent Korean writers of the early 1930s (Yi Kwangsu, Yi Yŏsŏng, Kim Ansŏ, An Chaehong, Chu Yohan, P'ain) about their first published books. They recount the circumstances of publication, often highlighting financial hardships, low or non-existent pay, and the personal feelings associated with these early works, ranging from pride at the time to later embarrassment or a desire to create something better. Historians might find interest in the specific details about manuscript fees (or lack thereof), publication channels (newspapers like *Maeil Sinbo*, specific publishing houses and magazines like *Taejo* and *Chosŏn Mundan*), and the authors' reflections on their early careers and the literary environment during the Japanese colonial period. The candid remarks about poverty and the desire for artistic integrity offer valuable insights into the lives and motivations of intellectuals under colonial rule.

最初의 著書
「無情」

李光洙

나의 최초의 저서는 「無情」임니다. 「無情」을 쓰든 때의 일은 지금도 이처지지 안슴니다. 그것은 아마 내가 몹시 고생하든 때 일이 되어 그러한 듯 함니다.
그때에 나는 배곱하서 정신을 이른 적도 한두번이 아니엇고 교과서를 못 사는 것은 둘재로 당장 수업료를 바치지 못해서 학교에도 못가든 때가 頻頻하엿슬 그 시기엇슴니다.
이러케 고생하면서도 이 소설만은 꾸준히 완결식혓슴니다. 첫 회부터 끗회까지 약 300회 동안 每日申報에 연재하엿는데 원고료는 처음엔 한달에 5원식 보내주더니 나종에 10원식 보내주더이다.

「比律賓의 過去와 現在」
李如星

나의 최초의 저서는 「比律賓의 과거와 현재」엇는데 1,500부를 인쇄하엿스며 원고료는 別無하엿슴니다.

「나의 籤悔」
金岸曙

나의 최초의 저작은 漢城圖書會社에서 발행한 「톨스토이」의 「나의 籤悔」를 번역한 것이외다. 그때에 원고료는 7, 80원 밧엇든지 지금 자세이 기억이 나지 아니함니다.
돌아보면 그것은 깜한 지내긴 옛날이외다. 물론 지금으로 보면 문체니 용어니 하는 것도 말이 아니 되거이와 유치하기가 짝이 업슴니다. 그러나 저러나 그 중에서 삭제까지 당한 것은의외엿슴니다. 에쓰어역본과 일본역을 대조해가면서 번역이라고 하엿슴니다.
나의 나희가 스물 여섯인가 일곱 때엿슴니다. 번역이나마 단행본으로는 첨인 것 만치 깃벗든 것은 지금으로 이즐 수가 업슴니다.

「白頭山 登陟記」
安在鴻

나의 저작물로 단행본 되기는 「白頭山 登陟記」인데 출판자에게 맛긴 채로 잇스니 아직은 印稅수입 정도를 알 수 업슴니다.

「아름다운 새벽」
朱耀翰

「아름다운 새벽」이란 시집이 내가 처음으로 세상에 내여노흔 저작물임니다.
지금에 잇서는 불에 태여버릴 것 밧게 못된다고 생각되지만은 그때에는 퍽 자랑스러운 것 갓햇슴니다. 그야 엇던 작품을 물론하고 대개 그러한 늣김을 가지게 되는 것이 常例이겟지만!
출판은 1924년에 하엿슴니다. 원고료는 일전 한푼도 밧지 못햇슴니다.
지금까지 원고료라고 밧어본 적이 꼭 두 번 밧게 업는데 한번은 全武吉 씨가 「大潮」라는 잡지를 할 적에 「詩調」 다섯 수에 모다 2원을 밧엇고 또 한번은 方仁根 씨가 朝鮮文壇을 할적에 「文壇月評」(열 페지 가량)에 10원을 밧엇지요. 그래서 합계하면 대단한 거액이라 할 것이지요. 하하.

「國境의 밤」
파인

최초의 저서는 「國境의 밤」이란 시집인데 岸曙의 勸으로 시집을 내고 십흔 충동을 밧어서 만들엇든 것임니다.
그러나 그것도 다 불살나버리고 정말 내 역량을 다한 시집 한 권만 발행하여 최초요, 또 최후의 시집을 삼고 십슴니다.＜58＞
