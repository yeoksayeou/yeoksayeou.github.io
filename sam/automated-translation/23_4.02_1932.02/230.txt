Article Title: 新婦의 3日間 (Three Days of a New Bride)
Author: 金貴德―柳光烈 씨 부인 (Kim Kwi-dŏk - Mrs. Yu Kwang-nyŏl)
Article Type: 회고·수기 (Reminiscence/Memoir)

新婦의 3日間
						金貴德―柳光烈 씨 부인

First Day: Returning from the Ceremony Hall

From morning, my heart was restless. When I went over to the room across the way, my little sister Kyu-hŭi clung to me, calling, "Older sister!" It was pitiful that my little sister didn't know what was happening to me today and just followed me like before.

I barely ate breakfast. In the morning, my father smilingly had me practice reading the vows I was to read at the wedding hall. I didn't read them because I was embarrassed, and he scolded me, then just laughed.

While things were going on like that, 3 o'clock in the afternoon came, and the ceremony was completed at the Youth Association (청년회) hall. It was cold, and I trembled with embarrassment trying to read the vows in front of 500 or 600 guests. At the reception, I was too embarrassed to even touch the food.

When I returned home, the room across the way had become the bridal chamber. My older sister, who had gone to her husband's home, came and made a fuss, saying she would guard the bridal chamber. It seemed like everyone was tormenting me today. He, without any word, took off his ceremonial clothes and changed into the prepared Korean clothes. The night deepened. The red candlelight lit on the table burned quietly.

Several hours passed after the candle was put out. I was terribly restless. All the events since childhood wandered through my head. I couldn't fall asleep for a long time. I suffered alone in the dark room. He slept peacefully. The anxious night brightened.

The Next Day

Following his words, we took a car to his family home, the *shidek*. I performed the *paerye*, meeting and bowing formally to the various elders who were his relatives. The woman who was my sister-in-law kindly came, took my wrist, and comforted me sincerely, asking, "How much trouble you must have gone through in the cold yesterday." I felt grateful for these words but was too shy to dare lift my face. In the evening, we went back to my family home again, and while doing this and that, the sun set. At night, it seemed my older brother's friends and his friends gathered together, drinking and enjoying themselves.

I went to the inner room (*anpang*) to pass the time. <62>
Among the several guests enjoying themselves, they asked him to sing. He declined but eventually gave in, but his voice was so soft that both Mother and I laughed. Only after night fell did we go to sleep.

Day 3

This was the day I finally bid farewell to my little sister and Mother and left. As I lifted the rice spoon at breakfast, seeing the faces of my mother and little sister, I felt sad and almost burst into tears, but I held them back firmly because of Mother's words, "You shouldn't shed tears on a happy occasion." When I arrived at the *shidek*, there were still many guests. After most had left, I faced the first night since coming to my husband's home from the main house. With everything being unfamiliar, the faces of my mother and siblings kept appearing before my eyes, and I cried. He comforted me with kind words. His comforting words were not ordinary; he spoke very "sentimental" (쎈틔멘탈) words, as if plucking things from inside my heart.

It felt like my lonely and weary spirit (영) could rest within the broad wings of his words. I couldn't fall asleep until late at night, finally sleeping near dawn, and I dreamed of going to school from my family home, carrying my book bundle (책보).

Day 4

I woke early in the morning, combed my hair, and was about to go to the kitchen (부억) when my sister-in-law stopped me. I had no choice but to stay in the room. He told me various stories. Among his stories, there were interesting ones, but also many difficult words and things I couldn't understand.

Then he opened the bookcase and showed me books stamped with gold letters (금자). He seemed to be showing off his books proudly. In the afternoon, he went out somewhere and came back, bringing 5 congratulatory telegrams from the wedding hall. There were over 50 telegrams in total.
I could tell his circle of acquaintances was wide. At night, again, I couldn't sleep peacefully. I've never slept over at someone else's house since I was young, so perhaps it was because I was sleeping in a different place. In my dream, it was raining, and I wanted to go to school wearing new clothes, but my mother told me to wear old clothes. I cried, saying I didn't want to, and then woke up abruptly, embarrassing myself in front of him. <63>
<62-63>

**Summary**

This memoir by Kim Kwi-dŏk recounts her first four days as a bride in 1932 Korea. She describes the wedding ceremony, her emotional state (shyness, anxiety, sadness at leaving home), interactions with her new husband and in-laws, and traditional customs like the *paerye*. The account details her transition from her parents' home to her husband's (*shidek*), her difficulty sleeping in the new environment, and her husband's attempts to comfort her. The piece provides a personal glimpse into the blend of tradition and emerging modernity in colonial Korean marriage practices, reflecting the emotional experience of a young woman navigating this significant life change.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into female experiences, marriage customs (including the temporary return to the bride's home), and the emotional landscape of educated women in 1930s Korea. The mention of the Youth Association hall (Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoe), the husband's pride in his books and social connections (telegrams), and the bride's dreams of school highlight the interplay of tradition, modernity, social status, and perhaps subtle aspirations beyond domestic roles during the colonial period. The use of the loanword "sentimental" (쎈틔멘탈) is also noteworthy.

첫날 式場에서 도라와서
아츰부터 마음이 살난하엿다. 건넌방에 건너가니 어린 동생 奎姬가 「언니」 하면서 매어달닌다. 그 어린 동생은 오늘 나의 신상에 무슨 일이 잇는지 몰으고 전과 가치 따르는 것이 안타가웟다.
아츰밥도 먹는둥 마는둥 하엿다. 오전 중에 부친이 웃으며 결혼식장에 가서 읽을 선서문을 읽혀본다. 붓그러워서 아니 읽엇더니 꾸지람을 하시다가 웃고 마신다.
그럭저럭 하는 동안에 오후 3시가 되어 청년회에 가서 식을 마치엇다. 날도 춥고 5, 600명의 내빈 중에서 선서문을 읽으려니까 붓그러워서 떨니엿다. 피로연에서는 붓그러 음식에 손도 못대이엇다.
집에 돌아가니 건너방이 신방이 되엇다. 싀집에 갓던 언니가 와서 신방을 직힌다고 야단이다. 이날은 모든 사람이 나를 괴롭게 구는 것 갓다. 그이는 아모 말도 업시 예복을 벗고 준비하엿든 조선복으로 가려입는다. 밤이 깁헛다. 상 우에 켜노흔 붉은 燭불이 고요히 탄다.
燭불을 끈 후 몃시간이 지낫다. 나는 몹시 마음이 산란하다. 어릴 때 이래의 모든 일이 머리에 배회한다. 오래 잠들지 못하엿다. 어두운 방에 혼자 고생고생 하엿다. 그이는 평온히 잔다. 불안스런 일야는 밝엇다.
그 이튼날
그이의 말대로 자동차를 타고 그이의 댁인 싀댁으로 갓다. 그이의 친척인 여러 어룬들과 새로 맛나는 배례를 하엿다. 싀누이 되는 분이 친절히 와서 나의 손목을 잡고 「어적게 추운대 얼마나 애썻느냐」고 친절히 위로한다. 이 말에 고맙게 생각하엿스나 붓그러워 감히 얼골을 들지 못하엿다. 저녁때 다시 친정으로 가서 이럭저럭하는 동안에 해가 저무럿다. 밤에는 옵바 친구요, 그이 친구가 한태 모히어 술을 먹고 노는 모양이다.
나는 안방에 가서 놀앗다.＜62＞
그 여러 손님이 노는 중에 그이에게 소리를 식히니 그이는 사양하다가 못하여 하는데 목소리가 어찌 적은지 어머니도 웃고 나도 웃엇다. 밤든 후에야 자게 되엇다.

第3日
이날은 마지맑으로 어린 동생과 어머니를 작별하고 떠나게 되엿다. 아츰 밥숫갈을 떠먹다가 어머니와 어린 동생의 얼골을 보니 섭섭하야 눈물이 날번 하엿스나 어머니 말슴이 「조흔 때 눈물을 흘니면 안된단다고」 하시는 말슴에 꾹 참엇다. 싀댁에 오니 손님은 여전히 만타. 대강 도라가시고 큰집에서 싀집 온 후 첫밤을 마젓다. 모든 것이 생소한 데다가 어머니 얼골과 동생들 얼골이 눈에 발피어 울엇다. 그이는 나를 친절한 말로 위로한다. 그이의 위로하는 말은 보통말이 아니오, 퍽 「쎈틔멘탈」한 말로 내속을 집어내는 듯이 말한다.
그 말의 넓은 날개 속의 나의 외롭고 고달푼 영이 쉬일 수 잇슬 것 가탓다. 밤들도록 잠들지 못하다가 새벽녘에야 자는데 친정에서 책보끼고 학교에 가든 꿈을 꾸엿다.

第4日
아츰 일즉 깨어 머리 빗고 부억에 가려 하엿더니 싀누이 되는 분이 말린다. 부득이 방에 잇섯다. 그이는 나에게 여러 가지 이약이를 하여준다. 그 이약이 속에는 자미잇는 것도 잇스나 어러운 말, 못아라 드를 말도 만타.
그러고 책장을 열고 금자 박힌 책들을 보힌다. 그이는 자기의 책을 자랑하듯이 보인다. 오후에 그이는 어대 나갓다 오더니 결혼식장에서 축전 5통을 가저왓다. 축전만 50여 통이다.
그이의 교제범위가 넓은 것을 알 수 잇다. 밤에는 역시 잠이 편안히 들지 못한다. 나는 어려서부터 남의 집에 가서 자본 일이 업는데 자리를 떠나서 자니까 그런가보다. 꿈에는 비가 오는데 내가 새옷을 입고 학교에 가려니까 어머니가 헌옷을 입고 가라는 것을 실타고 울다가 내처 깨어 그이에게 망신을 당하엿다.＜63＞
