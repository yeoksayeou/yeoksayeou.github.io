**Impromptu Verse**
By Wŏn Sehun

In sleeplessness during the recent dawn moon over sleeping Sŏul,
The sound of wheels rolling on steel legs from yonder sky.

Waking sleeping Sŏul, where is it headed?
Loaded full with guns and swords, they say it crosses the Amnokkang.

Now the backyard is coldest, hard to endure.
When the coming spring warms, slowly prepare the way.

In groups of threes and fives, hand in hand, singing,
Shouldering hoes and spades, let's go till the fields and paddies.

The wide land in the backyard has much old grime,
If we plant millet and hemp, there's no worry about making a living.

Cut trees and dig stones, build houses and light fires,
Read books, eat three meals a day, wouldn't know hunger.

Moreover, the grassland there is plump and fertile,
Never mind raising dogs, if we raise sheep and cows.

Sheep's milk for the children, beef for the elderly,
Even after eating so, much remains to give to others.<98>

Everyone gives their own effort, eats by their own strength.
No need to borrow others' strength, clothe oneself by one's own strength.

When backs are warm and bellies full, singing and dancing,
What is there to fight about? What is there to dispute?

Airplanes fly, submarines (潛航艇) swim.
Their skill is marvelous, but what is the reason for flying and swimming?

Dropping bombs kills people, firing cannons kills people.
Is there no skill to save lives, that they only practice skills to kill?

Digging the earth with a hoe (흐미), cutting trees with an axe (독기),
Though the skill is crude, what is the reason for digging and cutting?

Plant beans, people live; cut pines, people live.
There may be no skill to kill, but the skill to save lives is ample.

Clever skills kill others, crude skills let others live.
Neighboring people (리운누 사람들), which will you choose?

If striving to save lives, why blame the skill?
Don't make Gandhi-sounds (깐듸소리), Marx-sounds (맑쓰소리) are over there.<99>

**Snow** (and 1 other)
By Ha Yun

**Snow**

It is too quiet.
It is too white.
It comes down too much.
It is too chilly.
It is too vast.

**Fallen Leaves**

Rustle...
Rustle... and

Every year when late autumn comes
And yellow leaves scatter in the autumn wind.

Saying they have lost their vigor now,
When they gather, rolling on the ground (따우에).

That sound in the garden (뜰우에) stops,
On the morning when cold frost descends white.

The path to the moonlit hometown (향월의 길) left long ago
Is needlessly missed, like a leaf (입새).<100>

**Summary**

The first poem, "Impromptu Verse" by Wŏn Sehun, contrasts the ominous signs of militarization (implied Japanese expansion across the Amnok River) with an idealized vision of peaceful, self-sufficient agricultural life. It critiques modern technology when used for destruction (airplanes, bombs) and champions simple, life-sustaining labor (farming), urging readers to choose life-saving actions over life-taking ones, seemingly dismissing complex ideologies like Gandhism or Marxism as peripheral. The second part, by Ha Yun, presents two short, imagistic poems: "Snow" evokes a sense of quiet, cold desolation, while "Fallen Leaves" uses autumn imagery to convey melancholy, nostalgia, and the passage of time. From a historical perspective, Wŏn Sehun's poem is particularly interesting for its veiled criticism of Japanese military actions in 1932 (leading up to Manchukuo) and its articulation of a desire for autonomy and peace rooted in traditional agrarian life, reflecting anxieties and aspirations common in colonial Korea. Ha Yun's poems offer a glimpse into the lyrical sensibilities and potential escapism found in literature of the period.

偶吟
元世勳

잠든 서울 새벽달에 잠못들어 근일적에
저 한울의 쇠다리로 굴러오는 수레소리.

잠든 서울 깨우는 듯 가는 곳이 어데메냐
총과 칼을 가득 실고 압록강을 건넌다나.

이때 뒤뜰 가장 춥어 단이기가 어럽어라
오는 봄 따듯할제 천천히 길을 차려.

삼삼오오 짝을 지어 손목 잡고 노래하며
호미광이 둘너메고 논밧 가리 갈염으나.

뒤뜰의 널은 따이 묵은 때이 흠썩만어
베와 조를 심어내면 먹고살기 걱정 업고.

나무 베고 돌숫 파서 집도 짓고 불도 때면
글 읽고 삼삼끼에 춤은 줄을 몰을이다.

하물며 그곳 풀밧 살지고도 기름저서
개돗침은 말을 말고 양과 소를 길너내면.

양젓을 난 어린이게 소고기는 늙은이게
그리먹다 남은 것이 남줄 것도 만은이라.＜98＞

모다 제힘 제드려서 제힘으로 제가 먹고
남의 힘을 빌 것 업시 제힘으로 제 입어라.

등 덥고 배 불을 제 노래하며 춤출 곳에
싸홀 것이 무엇이며 다툴 것이 무엇이냐.

비행기는 날음 날고 潛航艇은 혀음 혀다
그 재조는 묘타만은 날고 험이 무슨 까닭.

폭탄 던저 사람 죽고 대포 노하 사람 죽네
살일 재조 어데 업서 죽일 재조 일삼는냐.

흐미로 따를 파고 독기로 나무베니
그 재조는 못 생겨도 파고 벰이 무슨 까닭.

콩 심어 사람살고 솔 베어 사람사네
죽일 재조 업다만은 살일 재조 넉넉하네.

잘난 재조 남 죽이고 못난 재조 남 살이네
리운누 사람들아 어느 것을 가지려나.

살일 내기 힘쓴다면 재조탓을 어이하리
깐듸소리 하지말 맑쓰소리 저게 잇네.＜99＞
