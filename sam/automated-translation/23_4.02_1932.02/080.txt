**Samch'ŏlli Wall Newspaper**

The Prosperity of Tokyo's Myŏngwŏlgwan
According to the story of someone recently arrived from Tokyo, a Korean restaurant called Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月舘) has opened in Tokyo. Its building is purely Korean style with red railings and painted walls (朱欄畵壁), the food includes sinsŏllo, kimchi, and kkakdugi, the music is the "e-i-" sound of the samhyŏn yukkak ensemble, the songs are Susimga and yukchabagi, and those providing service are about 10 kisaeng wearing ch'ima chŏgori, all possessing flower-like faces and moon-like forms (花容月態). The majority of customers are Japanese, and it's said that on busy days recently, daily sales exceeded 5,000 won, and even on slower days, 2,000 or 3,000 won is considered normal. As for why Myŏngwŏlgwan became so famous and developed like this, it seems to be because the alluring figures of the Korean kisaeng, combined with unique Korean food, attracted their curiosity.

Regarding Emperor Sunjong and "nŏ"
The late Emperor Sunjong (純宗; Yunghŭi Emperor 隆熙帝), even after his abdication, always referred to the late Yi Wanyong (李完用) as
"nŏ" (너; informal "you").
It is said that Yi Wanyong, even though he was a subject, complained to a close friend (知己) in his later years that this was too severe.

**Summary**

This article compiles three short news items: the success of a traditional Korean restaurant, Myŏngwŏlgwan, in Tokyo, popular among Japanese for its authentic food, architecture, music, and kisaeng entertainers; an anecdote about the demoted Emperor Sunjong still addressing the pro-Japanese minister Yi Wanyong with the informal "nŏ"; and a list of editorial members and advisors for the "World Proletarian XX Writers' League" and its publication, featuring writers from the Soviet Union, Europe, the US, China, and Japan. Historically, the Myŏngwŏlgwan story reveals the dynamics of cultural consumption and exoticization of Korea in Imperial Japan. The Sunjong anecdote subtly touches on lingering sentiments about deposed royalty and the controversial legacy of collaborators like Yi Wanyong. The list of international leftist writers indicates Korean intellectual engagement with global socialist movements during the colonial period, even under likely censorship (implied by "XX").

東京明月舘의 번창
최근 東京서 온 사람의 이약이를 듯건대 東京에 明月舘이란 조선요리점이 생기엇는데 그것은 건물도 純조선식의 朱欄畵壁이요, 음식도 신선로에 김치깍둑이요, 음악도 에-이- 하는 3絃 6角이요, 노래도 愁心歌요, 육자박이며 써-비스 하는 이도 전부 花容月態의 치마저고리 입은 기생 10여명이라는데 손님의 대부분은 일본인들로 요지음 만흔 날의 하로 매상고가 5천원을 초과하엿고 그러치 못한 날도 2천원, 3천원을 보통 헤인다는데 엇재서 이러케 明月舘이 유명하게 발달하는가 하면 조선 기생의 요염한 자태에다가 조선 독특한 음식이 그네의 호기심을 끄은 까닭인 듯 하다고.

純宗께서와 너
도라가신 純宗(隆熙帝)께서는 퇴위하신 뒤까지도 故 李完用을 불늘 때에 늘
「너」
라고 하섯다. 李完用은 아모리 신하라도 너무 심하시더라고 만년에 知己에게 고백하더라고.
