March of the Five Daughters from Noble Families

Like five plum blossoms quietly blooming on the spring breeze, daughters from noble families, sharing the blood of the same parents, born into the world and tenderly raised together under one roof, find themselves walking surprisingly different paths as they grow year by year, some attending school, others marrying husbands and attending to in-laws, as we often see and hear in this world. Like scattering east and west according to the direction of the blowing wind, the fate of women diverges into a thousand layers and ten thousand differences (千層萬差).

So, where and to what places have the five daughters from the three families introduced here scattered? Observing their paths from girlhood to maidenhood, and then to their current lives as housewives – could this merely be a repetition of Bai Juyi's (白樂天) phrase, "Woman, do not make yourself another's body; a hundred years of joy and sorrow depend on others"?

**The Five Daughters of Mr. Son Pyŏnghŭi**

The second daughter is the wife of the current Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) leader, Mr. Chŏng Kwangjo; the third daughter was the wife of the late Mr. Pang Chŏnghwan, now widowed.

**The Girlhood of the Five Sisters & Son Yonghwa**

Five daughters! That is not a small number. If they were boys, lining up five brothers would have easily formed a platoon of soldiers right then and there.
The late teacher Son Pyŏnghŭi always smiled watching them grow up cozily together, from the eldest to the youngest daughter, under the same roof. He was always delighted. Although Teacher Son, as the second leader of Ch'ŏndogyo, commanded a million followers and sometimes issued thunderous orders, when he entered his home, he never raised his voice before these growing daughters but always cherished them dearly. Among them, he especially loved the third daughter (who later became Mrs. Pang Chŏnghwan), the most intelligent and prettiest among the sisters, always saving and giving her food he was eating.
Three of the five daughters attended Tongdŏk Girls' School (同德女學校), founded by their father, and all excelled academically.
As the daughters matured, the father discreetly sought out sons-in-law. And so, they have all married out now. Did they truly find happiness as envisioned in their rosy dreams as young brides? On a cold winter day not long after the solar New Year, the reporter, braving the falling snow, sought news of these five sisters, deciding first (爲先) to find the third daughter, Mrs. Yonghwa (溶嬅), the widow of the talented late Mr. Pang Chŏnghwan.

Ms. Yonghwa (溶嬅) lives in a small thatched house in a narrow alley in Sogyŏk-tong, spending sorrowful days missing her departed husband, raising four pitiful fatherless children.<51>
With no inheritance left by her husband, she manages a poor household (살님) on the small sum sent from Kaebyŏk Sa (開闢社) addressed to her mother. Except for the youngest daughter, she sends the three children to school like others, so her hardship must be considerable.
If there is any solace for her, it would be the hope for the future of her children, who grow day by day, unaware of their mother's suffering. She graduated from Tongdŏk Girls' School (同德女學校). She is still young, an age where living alone feels lonely.

**The Eldest Sister - Kwanhwa (寬嬅)씨**

Ms. Kwanhwa is 51 this year, an age where she could enter the ranks of the elderly without feeling out of place.
Her husband passed away when their beloved son was just nine years old.
That young son has grown considerably and has now reached 34, busy being a father to his own children rather than just acting as a son to his parents.
Ms. Kwanhwa, too, is said to find her daily joy in the antics of her grandchildren, having little time to think of her long-departed husband.
Living nearby, she often visits the grieving Ms. Yonghwa to offer comfort, so their lonely conversations likely often turn to days gone by.

Ms. Yonghwa's second older sister, Ms. Kwanghwa (廣嬅), lives in Chae-tong. Her husband is Mr. Chŏng Kwangjo, the leader (대령) of Ch'ŏndogyo. It is probably not an overstatement to say she leads the happiest life among the five sisters.
At the relatively young age of 46, she already has a daughter-in-law (머느리). With two sons, two daughters, and material comfort, her happiness can be surmised in many ways.

**The Fourth Sister—**

Ms. Yonghwa's next younger sister is said to be living an enjoyable (자미스러운) life in Puyŏ, South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠南 扶餘). However, lately, she seems worried due to the illness of her husband, who was commuting to Posŏng Chŏnmun (普專). She is only twenty-five this year! Still very young.
As a mother of daughters, she reportedly makes good use of the childcare methods learned (뵈운) at school.
Like Ms. Yonghwa, she also graduated from Tongdŏk Girls' School (同德女學校).

**The Fifth Sister—**

The youngest sister is even younger. Perhaps it's better to say she is still a child. Just twenty-two years old!
Seemingly willing to follow her beloved husband anywhere, she left her mother and four sisters to set up house in the foreign land of Tokyo (東京).
With no children yet, she is free and busy helping her husband with his business, a life one cannot help but call enjoyable (자미스러운).
The five sisters, raised identically under the same father's hand, have changed in such diverse ways over the passing years.
The parents' wish and desire for them all to achieve equally happy lives has come to naught, suggesting human desires are perhaps never fully realized.<52>

**The Five Sisters of Hwang Aesidŏk and Hwang Sindŏk**

The older sister is an American graduate; the younger sister is a Women's University graduate.
**The Five Sisters in Full Bloom**

It is P'yŏngyang Castle (平壤城), renowned for its clear mountains, beautiful waters (山明水麗), and superb scenery (景慨絶勝). In the Outer Castle (外城) Hwang clan family, known as yangban, at the home of Inspector Hwang Sŏkch'ŏng (黃錫淸 監察令監任), five young ladies were blossoming beautifully – they were the inspector's granddaughters, the five sisters including Hwang Aesidŏk (黃愛施德). Seeing that their grandfather was an inspector, anyone can guess they lacked neither honor, status, nor material wealth.
However, invisible chains bound them. Thus, even as young children, they feared going outside the gate, and as they grew older, they only knew that a woman's duty was to learn cooking, sewing, and household chores well, and then get married.
But as times changed (밧귀임에), these intelligent young women struggled to escape their restrictive environment. However, Ms. Aesidŏk's two older sisters, unable to seize the moment, could only become obedient (?) daughters-in-law to their mothers-in-law and beloved wives to their husbands. The next sister, Hwang Aesidŏk, bravely escaped the restrictive environment and entered school at the age of fourteen. But her path to schooling was by no means smooth. However, her two younger sisters likely attended school without any hindrance and probably played actively like modern young ladies.<53>
Thus, among the five sisters, three fully sought to find their individuality and realized they had some mission beyond living for themselves alone. Under the motto "We must strive to learn (뵈워야)," they departed to seek education – some to Japan (日本), some to Europe and America (歐米), some to Seoul (서울). As a result, Hwang Aesidŏk graduated from Columbia University (컬넘비아대학) in New York, America (米國); the next younger sister, Hwang Indŏk (黃仁德), completed two years at Ewha Women's College (梨花女子專門學校) in Seoul; and the youngest sister, Hwang Sindŏk (黃信德), graduated from Japan Women's University (日本女子大學).
Now, I will briefly introduce where these five sisters are scattered and how they are living.
The eldest sister, now 60, lives in Wŏnsan (元山), engrossed in raising her two motherless grandchildren. Her husband reportedly went to America (米國) shortly after their marriage and has yet to return. After her husband left, she became a devout Christian, trying to forget all worldly sorrows while raising her only daughter, but even that beloved daughter passed away after marrying and leaving behind two children. How could she, who only believes in God, not want to resent God's spitefulness (심술구짐)!?
The second older sister lives in Tangju-dong, Seoul (서울 唐珠洞), managing somehow with her grown son who attends Posŏng Chŏnmun (普成專門). Her husband apparently took a new wife in P'yŏngyang (平壤) and, absorbed in new pleasures, seems to neglect both his first wife (本마느라) and precious son.
Next is Hwang Aesidŏk. She lives with her esteemed mother (慈堂님) in a sweet home with her husband, Mr. Pak Sun (朴順), in a small two-story Western-style house on a high hill in the very quiet and airy Sajik-tong, Seoul (서울 社稷洞). Mr. Pak Sun currently runs the Manbo Building (맨폿삘딍) outside Seodaemun (서대문), and Ms. Hwang works tirelessly as an executive of the Korean YWCA (朝鮮女子基督青年會).
Then there is Hwang Indŏk (黃仁德) – a name familiar to our ears. She reportedly had a love affair with her husband, Mr. Pang T'aeyŏng (方台榮), while attending Ewha Women's College (梨花女專) back in the day, and married him without even finishing her graduation. She is now busy playing mother to four lovely sons next to the Dongdaemun Women's Hospital (東大門婦人病院) in Seoul, and her husband, Mr. Pang, is reportedly the president (頭取) of a printing company in Yongsan (龍山). Until a few years ago, her name could often be seen in magazines and newspapers, but recently (니르러서는), for some reason, she cannot be encountered, which is considered rather regrettable.
Now, the youngest sister, Hwang Sindŏk (黃信德). After marrying Mr. Im Pongsun (林鳳淳), she became the mother of two lovely children but unfortunately lost her elder daughter. Now she seems quite devoted to being the mother of her beloved young son and working as a teacher at Myŏngsŏng Girls' Commercial School (明星女子實業學校), where she currently serves.
Her residence is in Sinsŏl-ri, outside Dongdaemun (東大門 밧 新設里) – a quiet suburb. Isn't she also the Hwang Sindŏk we know well? However, it is regrettable that she doesn't often bring us cheerful news these days.
The five daughters raised by one mother live through the world in these various ways.
According to Ms. Hwang Aesidŏk, her two older sisters are already old and feel like parents, while she feels her two younger sisters share her views and affections, making them feel closer to comrades than mere siblings.<54>

**Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk and Her Four Sisters**

Four skylarks perched on one branch, born into a great house where hundreds of sons and thousands of grandsons stretched forth in lines – what became of them afterwards?
**Hŏ Yŏngsuk and the 4 Sisters**

The time is about 30 years ago from now. In a corner of Seoul's Sae Munan (새 문안) (present-day Sŏdaemun-jŏng, 西大門町), there was a large-gated house that seemed to have abundantly received material blessings. Inside, four beautiful young ladies were growing up gracefully.
Born to parents who were Seoul yangban – and possessors of material wealth beyond what others imagined – the four sisters knew neither the bitter nor the sweet tastes of the world. They simply ate during the day, slept at night, wore new clothes according to the season, and received their parents' love; they knew nothing more.
Unlike the young ladies of today carrying book bundles to school, from the age of seven, they feared stepping outside (밧갓헤), observing strict separation of men and women (내위, 內外) within the confines of the large house with its tightly shut main gate. Since this was already 30 years ago, there must have been even more conventional morals restricting women that we can hardly imagine today.
But these four young ladies, growing steadily (모록모록) without any complaint, who were they really? Now, the writer intends to introduce who they are and where and how they are traversing the world, based on what was heard.
Among the four young ladies, the eldest was called "K'ŭn-aegi" (Big Baby/Eldest Girl), the second "Tuljjae-aegi" (Second Baby/Girl), the third "Setchae-aegi" (Third Baby/Girl), and the fourth is Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk (許英蕭), whom we think of with familiarity. To trace their lives from childhood <55> to the present, it seemed most advantageous to seek out Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk first. So, one day, with only a few days left before the New Year, I visited (방향햇슴니다) her at the large-gated house in Sungsam-dong (숭삼동).
It wasn't my first time visiting (방향함) her; I had stepped (듸려놋키도) inside the large gate several times before. But on this day, the gate to her house seemed even larger, making my small self feel (늣겨젓슴을) even smaller, which I thought pitifully.
Crossing the wide wooden floor (maru), I arrived before her room door and knocked. From inside, her soft voice saying, "Please come in," was heard, and I stepped into the room without hesitation. At that moment, what caught my eye was her bobbed hair. Ms. Hŏ, who originally looked much younger than her age, appeared even younger than before, looking easily around 20 years old.
Even seeing her holding and breastfeeding (젓먹이는) a baby felt somewhat out of place (격에 맛지 안는 것 갓치), only (밧게) because she looked incredibly young.
Rather than hearing other stories, I requested to hear the story I had intended to hear, so she recounted in detail the facts about her four sisters, from their old stories to the present.
Her eldest sister, "K'ŭn-aegi," is already 50 years old and lives in Ch'ŏnan, North Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠北 天安) with her three sons. Just like in her childhood, she lives comfortably with all five blessings, never knowing material want.
The second sister, "Tuljjae-aegi," is in Chongno 3-ch'ŏngme, Seoul (서울 종로 三丁目), a middle-aged woman of 45, mother to one son and two daughters. Her husband runs the Hŭngmun Tang Printing House (興文堂印刷所).
The third sister, "Setchae-aegi," lives in the house where their parents gave birth to and lovingly raised the four sisters – the Saemunan house (새문안집) (11 Sŏdaemun-jŏng 1-ch'ŏngme, 西大門町一丁目 11번지). There, reminiscing about various past events, she has reached the advanced age of 40, acting as mother to two sons and two daughters. Her husband reportedly runs a cloth store, and they too possess enough material wealth not to envy anyone.
Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk's three older sisters, who grew up under their parents' care (슬하, 膝下) without any lack of material goods or freedom in other aspects, were bound (억매여서) by the misguided customs then considered "beautiful traditions" (미풍, 美風), and all three married before the age of 15.
However, just as apples hanging on the same tree can be bigger and tastier, Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk alone, as the youngest daughter (막뚱딸), took a different path from her sisters. She entered Chinmyŏng School (進明學校) at age nine, graduated from Hansŏng School (漢城學校), a middle school in Seoul, at 15, and immediately went to Japan (渡日), where she graduated from a medical college (의학전문학교). Graduating from a medical college as a Korean woman at that time must have been considered a rare event.
Her husband is Mr. Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), whom many of you know well.
Perhaps weary (실증) of her sisters' early marriages, she married remarkably late, at the age of twenty-six.
She has already become the mother of two lovely sons and reached the not-insignificant age of 36.
Several memories from the past—she still seemed to deeply miss the old days, speaking of receiving her parents' love and the times growing up with her three sisters, showing expressions deeply moved by the old stories.<56>
What she remembers most vividly: when she was seven, she went to her eldest uncle's house with her third sister. After playing there, the head covering (쓰개치마) she had worn was missing. There were no women at the uncle's house except the eldest aunt, who had gone out somewhere. They needed to go home but had no head covering, so she shared her third sister's 쓰개치마. On the way home, people on the street mocked them, saying, "One person has four legs!" Also, when moving from the Saemunan house to "Tabang-kkol" (다방꼴), riding together with her third sister in one palanquin (가마). When her second sister got married, being sent away to another house with her third sister due to the custom of keeping unmarried girls away (내위). And when her third sister started menstruating, if she threatened to tell others, her sister would take out embroidered pouches she had made for her trousseau from the three-tiered chest and plead, "Don't say such things." Also, being poor at arithmetic (산술), her sister would open seven or eight matchboxes and teach (가라처주든) her using the matches. Her sister (third) only knew Korean script (국문) and not Chinese characters (한문), so when Hŏ Yŏngsuk had an exam and asked her sister to check her recitation (강을 밧어 달나면), the sister would look only at the grammatical particles (토) marked, and despite feeling sleepy (졸음이 오는 것도 불구하고), would listen until Hŏ Yŏngsuk had memorized everything, correcting her in a sleepy voice, "It's not 'hamyŏ' (하며), it's 'hago' (하고)," when the particle was wrong. All these, she said, are memories firmly embedded in her hardened mind from the old days.
Her first and second sisters, due to the large age gap and their early marriages, feel more like parents than sisters. But her third sister, from childhood until now, is still thought of as an affectionate older sister. Judging by her expression, Ms. Hŏ seemed to deeply miss the enjoyable romance (로-맨쓰) of the past. In that moment, I felt that perhaps humans are never satisfied with the present, enjoying reminiscing about the past while yearning (갈망하는) for something unseen in the future, as I stepped out of the large gate of her house. (End) <57>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine profiles the adult lives of daughters from three prominent Korean families: the five daughters of Ch'ŏndogyo leader Son Pyŏnghŭi, the five sisters including educators/activists Hwang Aesidŏk and Hwang Sindŏk, and the four sisters including writer Hŏ Yŏngsuk (wife of Yi Kwangsu). It contrasts their shared upbringing with their diverse fates – some finding happiness and stability, others facing widowhood (Son Yonghwa, Pang Chŏnghwan's widow), abandonment (Hwang Aesidŏk's second sister), or the sorrow of losing children (Hwang Aesidŏk's eldest sister, Hwang Sindŏk). The article highlights the changing opportunities and enduring constraints for women, contrasting those who received higher education (even abroad) and pursued public roles (Hwang Aesidŏk, Hwang Sindŏk, Hŏ Yŏngsuk) with those who followed more traditional paths, often marrying young. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the social realities of elite Korean women during the colonial period, showcasing the tensions between tradition (early marriage, domesticity, *naewoe*) and modernity (education, careers, delayed marriage), differing family fortunes, and the personal struggles behind public personas. The mention of specific schools, organizations (like the YWCA), and social customs provides concrete details about life in the 1930s.

둘재형임 되시는 분은 서울 唐珠洞에서 普成專門에 단이는 크다-란 아들 한아를 대리고 그럭저럭 지내심니다. 그 부군은 平壤에서 새 안해를 어더서 새로운 쟈미에 本마느라와 귀중한 아들까지도 도라보지 안은 모양임니다.
그다음 黃愛施德 씨는 서울에서도 몹시 한적하고 공기 조흔 社稷洞 놉흔 언덕 조고만한 이층 양옥에 慈堂님을 뫼시고 씨의 부군되는 朴順 씨와 스윗트홈을 니르고 지냄니다. 朴順 씨는 현재 서대문 외에서 맨폿삘딍을 경영하시고 씨는 朝鮮女子基督靑年會 간부로서 부절의 노력을 하고 게심니다.
그 다음에 黃仁德 씨―우리들 귀에 익숙하게 들니든 黃仁德 씨 임니다. 부군 方台榮 씨와는 녯날 梨花女專에 단일 때에 연애관계가 잇섯서 학교 졸업도 그만 제처놋코 결혼햇담니다. 현재 東大門婦人病院 엽집에서 벌서 사랑스러운 네 아들의 어머님 노릇 하시기에 분주하게 지낸다 하며, 부군 되시는 方씨는 龍山 어느 인쇄회사 頭取로 게시담니다. 몃해 전까지도 씨의 성함을 잡지나 신문을 통해서 종종 볼 수 잇드니 최근에 니르러서는 웬일인지 대면할 수 업스니 한갓 섭섭한 일이라고 생각됨니다.
인제 긋헤동생 黃信德 씨―林鳳淳 씨와 결혼하신 후 사랑스러운 두 자녀의 어머님 노릇을 할변 하엿스나 불행히도 큰딸 하나를 니저버리고 지금은 사랑하는 아들 어린애기의 어머님 노릇 하시기와 현재 근무하시는 明星女子實業學校 선생님 노릇에 퍽 노력하시는 모양임니다.
주택은 東大門 밧 新設里―조용한 교외임니다. 씨도 역시 우리들이 잘 아는 黃信德 씨가 아님닛가? 그러나 요새 와서는 깃거운 소식을 종종 들여주지 안음을 유감으로 생각할 뿐임니다.
한 어머님의 손에서 길너난 다섯 따님들은 이럿케 여러 가지로 세상을 지내감니다.
黃愛施德 씨의 말삼을 듯건댄 큰형임 두분은 별서 늙어서 부모와 갓흔 늣김이 잇다는데 아래로 동생 두분과는 듯이 맛고 정이 합하기 때문에 형제라는 것 보담 동지에 갓갑다고요.＜54＞

許英蕭 씨와 네분 兄任
百子千孫이 줄줄히 버더나가는 大家에 태어난 한 가지에 안젓든 네 종달새는 그 후에 엇더케
허영숙과 4형제
때는 지금으로 30여년 전임니다. 서울의 새 문안(現 西大門町) 한모통이에 물질의 혜택을 풍족하게 차지한 듯 십든 큰대문집이 이섯스니 그 속에는 아름다운 아가씨 네분이 곱게 자라고 이섯슴니다.
서울양반―더구나 남들이 상상하는 이상의 풍부한 물질의 소유자인 부모님에게 태여난 네 형제는 세상에 쓴맛이나 단맛을 분별할 줄도 몰낫슴니다. 그들은 낫이면 먹고 밤이면 잠자고 때를 따라서 새옷 입고 부모님의 사랑을 밧는 것 외에는 더 알 길이 업섯슴니다.
지금 시대의 아가씨들 모양으로 책보를 끼고 학교에 가기는 고사하고 일곱 살 되는 때 부터는 큰대문이 꼭 닷친 큰집속에서 「내위」를 하느라고 밧갓헤 발을 내놋키를 무서하엿슴니다. 벌서 30년 전 때의 녯일이닛깐 더 한층 우리들이 상상도 못할 만한 여성제재의 인습도덕이 만헛슬 것임니다.
그러나 아모 불평업시 모록모록 자라가기만 하는 이 네 아가씨는 과연 엇더한 아가씨들일가요. 인제 필자가 그들이 누구이며 지금은 어듸서 엇더한 모양으로 세상올 건너고 잇슴을 드른대로 소개할여고 함니다.
네 아가씨 중에 제일 큰아가씨는 「큰애기」, 둘재아가씨는 「둘재애기」, 셋재아가씨는 「셋재애기」, 넷재아가씨는 우리가 익숙하게 생각되는 許英蕭 씨람니다. 그들의 어렷슬＜55＞ 때부터 現今까지를 •Œ녀면 누구보담 許英蕭 씨를 찻는 것이 유리할 줄 알고 새해가 몃츨 안 남은 어느 하롯날 숭삼동 큰대문집에 씨를 방향햇슴니다.
씨를 방향함이 처음이 안이엿스니 큰 대문에 발을 듸려놋키도 몃 번이엿슴니다만은 이날의 씨의 집 대문은 더 한층 크다-란 것 갓허서 적은 내 몸덩이가 한층 더 적게 늣겨젓슴을 가엽게 생각하엿담니다.
널다란 마루를 것처 씨의 방문 압헤 가서 「녹크」 햇드니 안에서 「들어오십시요」하는 씨의 부드러운 목소리가 들니자 나는 꺼리낌 업시 방안에 발을 듸려노앗슴니다. 그와 동시에 내 눈에 띄운 것은 씨의 단발이엿담니다. 본래 나희보담 퍽 어리게 보이든 씨는 전보담 더 어리게 보혀서 20좌우라고 해도 조흘이만치 되엿슴니다.
어린애를 끼고 안저서 젓먹이는 것 좃차 격에 맛지 안는 것 갓치 늣겨젓스니 엄청나게 어리게 보이기 때문이라고 밧게 할 수 업지요.
나는 다른 이약이를 듯는 것보담 임이 뜻햇든 이약이 듯기를 요구하엿슴으로 씨는 자기들 4형제의 녯날 이약이부터 現今까지의 사실을 자세히 들녀주엇슴니다.
씨의 큰형임 되시는 「큰애기」는 벌서 50세가 되여서 아들 삼형제를 다리고 忠北 天安에서 어릴 때와 맛찬가지로 세상의 물질이 그리운 줄 몰으고 오복이 가즌 살님사리를 하고 잇다고 함니다.
또 둘재형임―「둘재애기」는 서울 종로 三丁目에서 아들 하나와 두 딸의 어머님―연세는 45才의 중노인으로 부군은 興文堂印刷所를 경영하고 잇담니다.
셋재형임―「셋재애기」는 녯날의 어버님, 아머님이 자긔들 4형제를 나으시고 또한 곱게 길너주시든 집―새문안집―(西大門町一丁目 11번지)에서 녯날의 가지가지 일을 추억하면서 어느듯 40세라는 고령에까지 이르러 아들 둘과 딸 둘의 어머님 노릇을 한담니다. 부군은 포목점 한다는데 이도 역시 누구를 부러워하지 안을만한 물질의 여유를 가젓다고 함니다.
부모님 슬하에서 자라날 때부터 물질에나 또는 다른 엇더한데든지 조곰도 부자유함이 업시 자라난 英蕭씨의 세분 형임은 당시에 미풍이라고 일으는 참 되지 못한 인습에 억매여서 세 분 다―15才 미만에 결혼을 하엿담니다.
그러나 한나무에 달닌 능금도 더 크고 더 맛잇서 보이는 것이 잇는 것과 맛찬가지로 英蕭씨만은 부모님의 막뚱딸노 그 형임들과는 딴길을 밟게 되엿스니 씨는 아홉 살에 進明學校에 입학하게 되엿스며 15才 때에 서울 漢城學校라는 중학교를 졸업하고 그 즉시로 渡日하여서 씨는 의학전문학교까지 졸업하게 되엿슴니다. 아마도 그때 그 당시의 朝鮮여자로서 醫學專門學校를 졸업한다는 것은 진기한 일이라고 생각햇슬 것임니다.
씨의 부군은 여러분도 잘 아시는 李光洙 씨람니다.
씨는 형님들의 조혼에 실증이 낫든지 엄청나게도 스물여섯이나 되여서 결혼을 햇슴니다.
벌서 사랑스러운 두 아들의 어머님오로 36才라는 적지 안은 연령에 니르럿슴니다.
녯날의 몃가지 추억―아직끗 녯날이 몹시 그리운 듯이 어머님, 아버님의 사랑 밧든 이약이와 세분 형임과 함께 자라나든 때에 녯날 이약이에 몹시 감흥을 이르키는 듯한 표정이 보엿슴니다.＜56＞
씨가 제일 니처지지 안는 것은 일곱살 적에 씨의 셋재형임과 함께 큰댁에 가섯는데 큰댁에 가서 놀다가 나니 쓰고 갓든 쓰개치마가 어듸를 갓는지 업섯담니다. 큰댁에는 큰어머님 밧게는 여자라고 업섯는데 큰어머님이 어듸로 나가섯기 때문에 집에 도라가기는 해야 하겟스나 쓸 치마가 업고 해서 셋재형임의 쓰개치마를 함께 쓰고서 집으로 도라올 적에 길가에서 「사람 한아에 다리가 넷이다」 하고 비웃든 일,― 또 새문안 집에서 「다방꼴」노 이사갈 적에 「가마」 한나에 셋재형임과 함께 타고 가든 일―, 둘재형님이 싀집갈 때에 「내위」 하느라고 셋재형님과 갓치 다른 집에 쫏겨가든 일―, 그리고 셋재형임이 월경이 잇다는 것을 다른 사람에게 광포하겟다고만 하면 싀집갈여고 수주머니 햇든 것이라도 삼층 장속에서 끄집어 내주면서 「그런 말은 하지말어」 하고 달내든 일―, 산술을 잘 몰나서 「성냥」통―일곱여들통―을 터처놋코 가라처주든 일―, 형님(셋재)이 국문만 알고 한문을 몰낫기 때문에 자기가 시험볼 적에 강을 밧어 달나면 「토」를 달어노흔 것만 보고 졸음이 오는 것도 불구하고 다- 외우기까지 강을 밧어 주면서 「토」를 달어논 「하고」가 「하며」로 될 때에는 졸니는 목소리로 「하며」가 안이다, 「하고」란다 하면서 가라처주든 일―어느 것이나 굿어진 머리속에 꼭 박힌 녯날의 기억이라고 함니다.
첫재와 둘재형님은 나희의 차가 만코 또 일즉이 싀집들 갓기 때문에 형임이라기 보담 부모와 갓흔 늣김을 가지게 되여지나 셋재형임만은 어럴 때부터 지금까지도 다정한 형임이라고 생각되여진다고요, 씨의 표정을 보아서 씨가 녯날의 자미스럽든 로-맨쓰를 몹시 그리워하시는 뜻 십헛슴니다. 나는 그-순간만은―사람은 현재에 만족을 늣기지 못하는 동시에 녯일을 추억하기 조화하며 보이지 안는 압날의 무엇을 갈망하는 것인갑다는 감을 가지면서 씨의 집 큰 대문 밧그로 나섯슴니다.(끗)＜57＞
