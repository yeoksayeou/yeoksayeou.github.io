**A Parade of Talents from Five Major Institutions of Higher Learning**

**Posŏng Chŏnmun Hakkyo (Bosung College)**

Perhaps when counting the private schools that have produced the most talent for Peninsular society, one cannot help but count Posŏng Chŏnmun Hakkyo, located in Songhyŏn-dong, Seoul, on the first finger.

Looking back now to what seems like a distant past, in the 8th year of Kwangmu (1904), the late Duke Ch'ungsuk, Mr. Yi Yongik (李容翊), who in many respects left a great mark on modern Korean history, founded it for the purpose of cultivating the future pillars (棟梁) of peninsular society. For the past 26 years, despite weathering the spring winds and autumn rains, it has sent out approximately 1,000 brilliant talents—some into the legal world to protect and expand civil rights, some into the business world, some into education, and some into social and national movements.

Isn't the role of this school comparable to the existence of Waseda University under Ōkuma Shigenobu, which brought the Meiji Restoration to completion?

During this time, the principal changed 8 times, and graduates were produced 26 times—the management also varied, sometimes being Yi Yongik personally, sometimes Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎), sometimes a joint venture of supporters, and currently, it has reached the status of an incorporated foundation.

Looking ahead to the vast and eventful future of peninsular society, let's look back at the talents this school has produced—.

Counting the graduates in chronological order, on April 15th of the 11th year of Kwangmu (1907), among the 16 students of the 1st graduating class of the Law Department who received their diplomas that day, there were many outstanding figures. First,

Hŏ Hŏn (許憲): Born in Myŏngch'ŏn, North Hamgyŏng Province, after finishing Posŏng College, he went to Japan, completed the Law Department at Meiji University, and returned to open a law practice. Meanwhile, for the peninsula entering its dawn period, he also created youth associations and engaged in youth enlightenment movements. As a conciliatory measure against oppressive power, he also became a Christian. Thus, he continued vigorously navigating the turbulent currents of social thought. When the March 1st Movement occurred, he bustled about defending the 33 signatories, eventually raising the Issue of Non-acceptance of Appeal (控訴不受理問題) and delivering a lion's roar (獅子吼) in court, capturing the attention of the era.

After that, he assumed the position of the 7th principal at his alma mater, Posŏng College, dedicating himself to education. After returning from a world tour, he served as the chairman of the central executive committee of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會), leading 40,000 members in activities. Then, after the Kwangju Student Incident, due to the "Mass Rally Incident," he is currently weaving nets behind the iron bars of Sŏdaemun Prison along with Hong Myŏnghŭi (洪命憙), Yi Kwan'yong (李灌鎔), and 7 or 8 other comrades <13>. The path he will take in the future also draws the attention of myriad eyes.

Mr. Yun Ikson (尹益善): Born in Kyŏngsŏng, North Hamgyŏng Province, he is one of the respected seniors in Korean education. After graduation, he assumed the position of the 5th principal and brought the difficult founding period of his alma mater to completion. During the March 1st Movement, he undertook important responsibilities and engaged in political activities multiple times, resulting in several years confined behind iron bars. After his release, he again took up the principalship of Tonghŭng Middle School in Yongjŏng (龍井), North Kando (北間道), dedicating his mind and body to cultivating spirited young men in the desolate northern continent. As a brilliant talent among the 1,000 graduates of this school (斯校), his character further elevated the standing of Posŏng College.

Mr. Yi Hangjong (李恒鍾): Currently the manager of the Hongsŏng Branch of Tongil Bank, he was an exceptionally talented student since his school days. Born in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), he is currently active in the business world and is reputed by the world as someone with the talent to make great leaps forward.

Besides these, among those who went into government administration is Mr. Yi Sŏngmuk (李聖黙), born in Kongju, South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, whose current official title is Secretary in the Office of the Yi Royal Household (李王職事務官).

Among the 1st class graduates, many later became lawyers. Besides Hŏ Hŏn, there were four others: Chang T'aekhwan (張宅煥), Yu Wanyŏng (柳完永), Kye Myŏnggwi (桂命夔), and Yi Chŏng (李炡), all actively taking the lead (牛耳 lit. 'holding the cow's ear') in the legal field.

Law Department 1st Class (Night School) February 15th, 11th Year of Kwangmu (1907)
At this time, there were 18 graduates.

Mr. Chu Chŏnggyun (朱定均), born in Kyŏngsŏng, is currently striving for the completion of his alma mater as a director of its incorporated foundation. In the financial world, the late Yu Okkyŏm (兪鈺兼) made a name for himself. Mr. Han Kwangho (韓光鎬) is the current manager of the Suwŏn branch of Hansŏng Bank. Lawyers include Yi Han'gil (李漢吉) and Wang Tonggyu (王東奎). In education, there is Mr. Kang Taljun (姜達駿), currently a teacher at Kyesan Posŏng School.

Economics Department 1st Class April 15th, 11th Year of Kwangmu (1907)
At this time, there was an economics department, and its 18 graduates mostly spread into the business world.

Law Department 2nd Class (Day School) January 29th, 2nd Year of Yunghŭi (1909)
There were 28 graduates in the 2nd class. Among them, one person distinctly stood out later:

Mr. Nam Hyŏng'u (南亨祐): Born in Kyŏngsŏng, now in his 50s, he spent a long time wandering abroad, gaining renown as a leader of the national movement. He is still in China, guiding the national movement. Years ago, he served as the Dean (學監) of his alma mater, rendering unforgettable service to the school.
Others include lawyer Chŏng Chunmo (鄭竣謨), and in the government, County Magistrate Cho Chongha (趙鍾夏), among others.

Law Department 2nd Class (Night School) January 29th, 2nd Year of Yunghŭi (1909)
There were 28 graduates from this night school division. Among them are lawyer Pyŏn Yŏngman (卞榮晩) and Christian evangelist Yi Sŏkchin (李錫璡).

Law Department 3rd Class February 15th, 4th Year of Yunghŭi (1910)
By this time, the night school had been abolished. From this class, Yun Hae (尹海), born in Yŏnghŭng, South Hamgyŏng Province, traveled abroad and made a mark. He is said to be currently undertaking an important role in the social movement field in Russia.

Law Department 4th Class January 28th, 44th Year of Meiji (1911)
This class had 45 graduates. Many are active in religious, educational, governmental, and other fields, but notably, Mr. Chang Tobin (張道斌), who later gained fame as a writer, was among them. Born in Chunghwa, South P'yŏngan Province, his historical writings and critiques once stirred the blood of the youth.

Economics Department 2nd Class: This class had 22 graduates. Mr. Hwang T'aeyŏn (黃泰淵), currently working diligently for his alma mater as the secretary of its incorporated foundation, stands out. <14>

Law Department 6th Class: This class had 28 graduates. Among them is Mr. Ch'a Sangch'an (車相讚), a senior figure in the Korean magazine world and the current Chief Editor (主幹) of Kaebyŏk Publishing Company (開闢社). As the head of Kaebyŏk Publishing today, he also puts much effort into his alma mater.

Law Department 8th Class: This class had 20 graduates. It is worth noting Mr. Kim Yongmu (金用茂), currently active as a prominent figure in the Kyŏngsŏng legal circle. Also, Mr. Yi Chongsong (李宗聖) is a lawyer from this class.

Law Department 9th Class: Additionally, there is Mr. Yi Ch'unsuk (李春塾). Born in Chŏngp'yŏng, South Hamgyŏng Province, he was active in the Shanghai XX movement for many years and later was a stalwart fighter in the Sin'ganhoe.

Law Department 10th Class: Among the 19 graduates of the 10th class, several deserve special mention:
Mr. Han Kiak (韓基岳): Born in Kyŏngsŏng, he is one of the pioneers of the Korean textile (操觸界 lit. 'reeling world') industry and the current managing editor of the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), a seasoned veteran of the Korean newspaper world.
Mr. Kim Kit'ae (金起田 - *Note: 기전 likely transcription of 起田*): A native of Kusŏng, North P'yŏngan Province, he was a past leader of the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Party and is currently an advisor to the Ch'ŏndogyo Ch'ŏng'u Party (靑友黨), one of the key figures in Ch'ŏndogyo.

Law Department 11th Class: Among the 27 graduates of this class:
Mr. Son Chaegi (孫在基): Born in Kyŏngsŏng, he is a former representative of the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth League and the current leader of the Ch'ŏndogyo Ch'ŏng'u Party, a key figure in that religion. Additionally, there is Chu Ik (朱翼), a social activist born in Pukch'ŏng, South Hamgyŏng Province, and a pioneer of the Hamgyŏng Province social movement. Mr. Yi Yunjong (李允鍾), former political editor at the Chung'oe Ilbo (中外日報), is also from this class.

Law Department 12th Class: Graduates from this class include:
Mr. Chŏng Ŭngbong (鄭應琫): Currently the deputy leader of the Ch'ŏndogyo Ch'ŏng'u Party, a key figure in Ch'ŏndogyo.
Kang Kidŏk (康基德): This person is the Wŏnsan Branch Manager of the Chosŏn Ilbo, a leader of the Wŏnsan social movement, a key figure in the Wŏnsan Labor Federation (元山勞聯), and a man full of fighting spirit who was elected chairman after the dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe. This graduate is currently imprisoned (囹圄). Having likely been in and out of prison 7 or 8 times, he holds the highest record in that regard. Besides him, O Ilch'ŏl (吳一徹), who is a secretary at the alma mater, Yi Pyŏnghŭi (李丙羲), who is a correspondent for the Osaka Asahi Shimbun, and in education, Yi Ilsŏng (李一星), the current vice-principal of T'aehwa Women's School, are all shining graduates.

Commerce Department 7th Class: Among the 9 graduates of this class, most are active in the financial sector. Among them, Mr. Ŏm Hangsŏp (嚴恒燮) is currently in Shanghai, active in the national movement, making a distinct mark.

Commerce Department 10th Class:
Mr. Kim Yŏnhŭi (金演羲): Born in Kimp'o, Kyŏnggi Province, he spent many years in prison due to the first Chosŏn Communist Party incident and is currently a reporter for the Chung'ang Ilbo (中央日報).

Law Department 1st Class (Professional School under the Chosŏn Education Association):
Mr. Han P'oyong (韓普容): Born in Chŏngp'yŏng, South Hamgyŏng Province, he is currently studying political science and economics in the United States. Among the Korean students in the US, he is a senior figure whose future is highly anticipated.
Mr. Yi Pyŏnghŏn (李炳憲): Born in Chinwi, Kyŏnggi Province, he is currently active as the Kyŏngsŏng city deputy representative of the Ch'ŏndogyo Ch'ŏng'u Party and was very active in the former Sin'ganhoe.

Law Department 2nd Class:
Mr. Yi Ch'anghwi (李昌輝): Born on Cheju Island, he is a young lawyer showing remarkable progress. Besides him, graduates are scattered throughout Chosŏn, mostly concentrated in the financial world. There are many talented individuals in business and other fields, but due to space limitations, we will stop here for now and promise to record more details later as we lay down this brush. <15>

**Ewha Yŏja Chŏnmun Hakkyo (Ewha Women's College)**

Boasting a long history of over 20 years, the highest institution of learning for Korean women—Ewha Yŏja Chŏnmun Hakkyo—has nurtured over one hundred talents through no fewer than 15 graduation ceremonies. In Korean society, which lacks workers, especially in women's society, how much joy must there be in seeing the young ladies from this place take step after step forward, and how much expectation must be placed on their abilities?

However, looking back, what kind of individuals has Ewha Women's College produced until today? Has it indeed sent out the workers widely anticipated by society? This is a question that the reporter—and perhaps even more so, you readers—are curious to know.

If asked to name the most exceptional individuals from Ewha Women's College, anyone would mention Ms. Kim Hwallan (金活蘭), Ms. Pak Indŏk (朴仁德), and Ms. Hwang Aesidŏk (黃愛施德). Besides them, countless talented women have emerged and are now active in some corner of society—or perhaps at home, wearing an apron, living vigorously.

However, what feels somewhat regrettable is that perhaps because the school's spirit is originally aimed at spreading the way of Christianity, those nurtured here tend to revolve specifically around Christian society.

In any case, let's count the talented individuals worth noting. (Due to space limitations, only a few first)

Ms. Pak Ŭnhye (朴恩惠)! She is the mother of Korea's children.
The Christian Building—a house rising prominently in the middle of Chongno—enter the main gate, go up quite a way in the elevator, and to the west, you'll see a brightly lit glass window labeled "Sunday School Association" (主日學校聯合會).

Pass through that door and pierce through another, and there you will find a person suited to being a true mother to children, an affectionate individual, sitting with a table in front of her, either reading something or busy writing.

Born in P'yŏngan Province, after graduating from Ewha College the spring before last, she resolved to work for the children of Korea who are in the most pitiful circumstances. Perhaps this is because she possesses an unusually keen mind and a benevolent nature. You all probably know well that "Children's Life" (아희생활), one of Korea's children's magazines, is steadily published through her hands.—

Whichever way you look at her, talent seems to flow from her. Someone praised her as "a fortunate child possessing genius capable of doing anything." <16> Anyone who knows her would likely agree.
Since her student days, she not only had good grades but also possessed a writing style that few could match.

Is that all? Sometimes, she became the protagonist in plays performed within the school, excelling at making people laugh and cry! I recall a photo of her dressed as Joan of Arc (짠따-크) being published in the Tonga Ilbo (東亞紙). Although the modest and unassuming Ms. Pak might not seem to possess the qualities of a theatrical lead, looking closely, one feels anew that she seems truly capable of anything.

In any case, she is a multifaceted talent (才士). Finally, what I wish for her is that she expands her activities beyond the Christian sphere to work broadly for the proletarian children of all Korea.

Ms. Sin Ŭigyŏng (辛義卿): She was born in Seoul. Tall and slender with a longish face (얼이골 길숙하게 생긴 - *archaic phrasing*), she is said to have been so lively that among her peers (동모들), she was even called a "tomboy" (말괄양). Yet, because she appears very pleasant, she gains goodwill from everyone.

She was the first graduate from Ewha Women's College. After finishing the Literature Department, she entered Japan's Tōhoku University, studied diligently for many years, completed the history course, and has now returned to her alma mater that nurtured her, holding a teaching position. During her student days, she earned immense trust from teachers and peers, showing she was a person of special talent. She is the first Korean woman to graduate from an Imperial University. Wouldn't it be not too much of an exaggeration to call her the pride of Korea?

Go up the alley in Kye-dong for a while, and you'll see a house that, even glimpsed from the outside, appears to be a respectable home.
That is her residence.
Her husband is currently said to be a professor at the Higher Industrial School (高等工業學校).

There's a bit of young romance concerning Ms. Sin: while she was studying at Tōhoku University, she met a certain student in the Mining Department (鑛山科) of the same class. From then on, the two young people sympathized with and loved each other.
Thus, the two lonely souls in a foreign land helped and comforted each other, spending the long time until graduation day without loneliness!
It goes without saying that her current husband was that certain student attending the Mining Department at the time.

Ms. Yi Kŭmjŏn (李今田 - *potentially Kŭmdan?*): Although over 30, she is still unmarried. She, too, is one of Ewha's first graduates.
Although she studied in the Literature Department, perhaps because she deeply loves cleanliness above all else, she developed a desire to study hygiene and subtly changed her path.
She graduated from the nursing school within Severance Medical College (세부란쓰醫學專門學校). Still unsatisfied, with great ambition and much hope, <17> she moved her feet to England; that was already four years ago.
There, she graduated from the Public Health Department and returned home the year before last.
Since returning, she is said to be working very hard for public health initiatives at the Public Health Department of the T'aehwa Women's Center (泰和女子舘).

Ms. Yi Soran (李少蘭)! At the end of Sŏngbuk-dong, a quiet suburb with good air, stands a moderately sized Western-style house, neat like a colored box. Anyone seeing the house would think it's a foreigner's residence, as it's built in that style. However, I want to say that this Western house is not a foreigner's home but a Korean's residence. No, rather, I want to mention right away that it is Ms. Yi Soran's sweet home.

Although in the Literature Department, she also had musical talent. Born in Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, she is still young at 23. Possessing artistic genius, she is quite affectionate and warm-hearted, having had many peers and special friends during her student days. Among them, she was said to be particularly close with a peer named Ch'ae Sŏnyŏp (蔡선엽). Ch'ae Sŏnyŏp is, needless to say, the younger sister of Mr. Ch'ae Tongson (蔡東鮮), who went to Germany, studied violin for 7 years, and returned.

Being a close friend of Sŏnyŏp, Ms. Yi also got to know her older brother. As a result of this, she came to know Sŏnyŏp's brother, Mr. Tongson, more intimately than Sŏnyŏp herself, leading to their marriage in the spring she graduated. When Mr. Ch'ae performs, Ms. Yi accompanies him on the piano. The piano melodies flowing from the fingertips of the lovely Ms. Soran in their elegant Western house, harmonizing with the finely drawn violin melodies, are said to be sufficient to comfort the hearts of people walking the quiet suburban paths.

Ms. Yun Sŏngdŏk (尹聖德)! Sister of the vocalist Ms. Yun Simdŏk (尹心德), whose memory is still fresh in people's minds, she completed the music department at that school early on, then went to America to study for several years before returning. Now teaching at her alma mater, she is beloved at the school for her lively personality, rare beauty, and naturally gifted voice (美聲). Still under 30 by Western age, she can be called a promising worker in the Ewha flower garden.

Ms. Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑)! This person has talent (才分) in English literature. A Literature Department graduate, she is now in desolate North Kando, where harsh winds blow over, teaching at a girls' middle school. She occasionally sends beautifully written prose and poems to the homeland's literary circles to adorn them. A flower quietly blooming in the lonely literary world of women! Isn't she perhaps the first gift presented to the peninsular literary world from Ewha's Literature Department in several years?

Ms. Kim Hyŏnsun (金顯順)! Having studied voice for three years under the peninsula's renowned vocalist Mr. An Kiyŏng (安基永), she is now a leading female vocalist, vying for the top spot. Having appeared on performance stages in various places like Kyŏngsŏng, P'yŏngyang, and Taegu, captivating audiences of thousands, was it solely due to her dove-like, graceful demeanor?
Even according to her teacher, Mr. An Kiyŏng, her future is said to be most brilliant. Soon, she will become a most respected female artist blooming in the peninsular music scene.

Besides these, there are too many famous butterflies who strolled through Ewha's flower garden. However, unable to record them all, we end here. There are also those who went to prison for participating in ideological movements during their student days, and others too famous to need our pen, like Ms. Pak Indŏk and Ms. Kim Hwallan, but we have decided to omit them all. <18>

**P'yŏngyang Sungsil Chŏnmun Hakkyo (Pyongyang Soongsil College)**

In the wide fields of Sinyang-ri in Sŏdo P'yŏngyang, stands a grand Western-style academy, soaring as if to pierce the sky—this is Sungsil Chŏnmun Hakkyo. That it has sent out many talents in literature and agriculture is widely known (詳知) by the world, needing no elaboration from our pen.

Originally, P'yŏngyang was a place where culture flourished most brilliantly on the peninsula. The women of Sŏdo, reminiscent of Spartan mothers, and their children! That's why, during the Kwangmu and Yunghŭi eras, when the enlightenment movement arose in Seoul, this land, sooner than anywhere else, saw the vigorous establishment of Taesŏng School (大成學校) and other renowned academies. Also, various political, economic, and social organizations, like such-and-such associations, followed one after another, giving significant impetus to the cultural movement of the entire peninsula.

Founded in the autumn of 1898 by the American Dr. William M. Baird (裵良 - Paeryang), it was initially called Sungsil University (崇實大學), then changed to a Chŏnmun Hakkyo (專門學校 - Professional School/College) in the 14th year of Taishō (1925), continuing to this day. The total number of graduates from this school is 229: 158 from 16 classes during the university era, and 71 from 6 classes during the professional school era. Looking at the occupations of the 229 graduates:

Pastors (牧師)           25
Professors (敎授)         7
Theology Graduates (神學士) 15
Teachers (敎員)           58
Church Workers (敎會役者) 11
Doctors (醫師)            6
Bank/Company Clerks (銀行會社事務員) 48

One can see how evenly they are distributed and active in various fields.
Furthermore, what this academy can be most proud of is that among these 200-plus graduates, those who obtained doctoral degrees number one, and those with bachelor's degrees reach the large number of 40.
It can be predicted that many more talents will emerge from Sungsil's highest institution in the future, something to be deeply glad about for the sake of the peninsula's civilization.
Now, let's pick out a few talents from this school:

Mr. Chŏng In'gwa (鄭仁果), currently the General Secretary of the Chosŏn Sunday School Association, is one who has made the name of his alma mater resoundingly famous (嚇嚇하게). After finishing Sungsil University (崇大) in 1911, he went to America, finished Princeton University, and obtained a Bachelor of Divinity degree. Finding that insufficient, he plunged into Columbia University <19> to earn a Master of Arts degree as well, then returned to be active in the religious sphere. He holds key positions in various organizations related to religious education, children's life, and peasant life. Last year, he attended the International Missionary Council meeting held in Jerusalem as the Korean delegate.
He is a prominent figure in the Presbyterian Church and an eloquent speaker. Among the 4th class graduates of the university era, there is also

Mr. Pyŏn Sŏngok (邊成玉) who shines brightly. He also holds Bachelor of Divinity and Master of Arts degrees. Currently, he is a committee member of the Chosŏn Sunday School Association, Dean (學監) and Professor at Kyŏngsŏng Hyŏpsŏng Theological School, and a Methodist pastor.

And then there is someone who showed prominence in a field completely different from literature and agriculture: Mr. Cho Ŭngch'ŏn (曺應天), a Doctor of Science (理學博士), rare indeed even in Chosŏn. Graduating from Sungsil University (崇大) in 1916, he went to America, studied physics and mathematics at the doctoral program of Indiana State University, obtained his degree, and is a renowned diligent scholar (篤學者). He has authored two books on wireless telephony, wireless cinematography (無線電影), and vacuum tubes (無空官). He will surely make a name for himself in this field in the future.

Among those who returned to their alma mater to teach is

Mr. Pak Pongjo (朴鳳祚). A genius in languages, in his graduation year, he already astonished the world by interpreting specialized lectures on the Great European War and military strategy. Afterwards, through pure self-study, he mastered English typing and English shorthand (英語記術), reaching the inner sanctum (堂奧) of proficiency.

If one mentions Mr. Pak T'aejun (朴泰俊), gentlemen of the cultural world (江湖) interested in arts will nod their heads, saying, "Ah, was he also from Sungsil University (崇大)?" A 1921 graduate and composer, he established his own style, particularly excelling in children's songs and short pieces (小曲). Currently serving as a teacher at Kyesŏng School in Taegu. Speaking of artists, Sungsil Academy produced quite a few composers and musicians.

One year after Mr. Pak T'aejun came

Mr. Ch'a Chaeil (車在鎰). He teaches at Sungsil College (崇專) and is as famous a vocalist as An Kiyŏng in the South, hence the saying "An Kiyŏng in the South, Ch'a Chaeil in the North."
His fame is resounding (嘖嘖) from recording "Moranbong" (牧丹峯) and others on records, a fact known to all experts in that field. Furthermore, he is renowned as a master coach (명코-취) of soccer. Years ago, when a major national soccer tournament of Japan was held in Taegu, he led the Sungsil School team. Seeing their exceptionally superior teamwork, Japanese soccer critics highly respected him as a "master coach."

While introducing musical talents, let's mention one more. Mr. Hyŏn Chemyŏng (玄濟明), currently a professor at Yŏnhŭi College (延禧專門). He is also a graduate of the university's 15th class in 1924. He went to America and obtained a "Doctor of Music" degree, his specialty being voice. The fact that he broadcasted his singing in America before returning home and was greatly welcomed shows that his talent is also astonishing.

Mr. Yi Kuhwa (李龜化) is a 1925 graduate who finished Tōhoku University, obtained a Bachelor of Science (理學士) degree, and is now teaching at his alma mater. He possesses amazing talent (才華) especially in mathematics (數理), having astonished his peers (濟俳) since his student days.

Finally, let's introduce one more person, Mr. U Hoik (禹浩翊). After finishing Sungsil College (崇專), he went to Tokyo and graduated from Waseda University. A Bachelor of Arts, he is currently a professor at his alma mater and also the president of the alumni association. He is also known as an eloquent speaker. He has also written works on history.

Besides these, Pastor Kim Ch'angjun (金昌俊) of Seoul Central Church,
Pastor Pak Yoch'ang (朴料昌) of Sŏnch'ŏn or Kaesŏng,
Pastor Chŏng Sang'in (鄭尙仁) and others are all graduates of this school. Additionally, pastors, doctors, students studying in Europe and America are truly as numerous as the stars on an early summer evening (星羅).
The great mother mountain that is Sungsil Academy will surely give birth to many more geniuses in the future. How could the wish for the health of the mother body be limited to just one person? <20>

**Severance Ŭihak Chŏnmun Hakkyo (Severance Union Medical College)**

Carrying needles (針) and medicine pouches (藥封), traveling through the thirteen provinces, protecting the health of the peninsular people—the traditional doctors (한방의사) are being succeeded by graduates from modern scientific medical professional schools and this Severance Ŭihak Chŏnmun Hakkyo. The former (referring to Kyŏngsŏng Medical College, implied) is a government school, the latter a private school, but as it is invested in and managed by American missions, the grandeur of Severance Medical College's facilities and scale is sufficient to surprise both locals and foreigners.

Thinking back, in 1884 (Meiji 17), Dr. Horace N. Allen (아-렌), the first Christian missionary to Chosŏn, saved the life of the late Minister Min Yŏngik (閔泳翊). On that occasion, the reigning Kwangmu Emperor, also expressing gratitude, established the Royal Hospital (王室病院) in Tonghyŏn, Southern Hansŏng, which was the predecessor of Severance Hospital. At that time, of course, a small Korean-style house constituted the entire hospital building, but by the 8th year of Kwangmu (1904), through the activities of foreign missionaries, a large sum of money (巨資) was raised to construct a grand-scale building at the current Namdaemun site and even establish a school alongside it.

The first graduates emerged in the 2nd year of Yunghŭi (1908), numbering 7. Since then, for about 30 years, every spring, it has produced talented individuals for the medical field, sending them into the peninsular world of scalpels and medicine (刀圭界). Their number has truly reached the large figure of:
Main Course (本科) graduates: 244
Special Course (別科) graduates: 11.
Looking at graduates by province, Kyŏnggi Province had the most with 80, followed by North P'yŏngan with 38, South P'yŏngan with 33, Hwanghae with 31, and South Hamgyŏng with 16.

**Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun Hakkyo (Yonhi College)**

Since we still don't have even one People's University (民立大學), what else is there to say? Just as Sungsil College, Severance Medical College, and Ewha Women's College mentioned above were all established through the surplus grace (餘澤) of foreign missionary work, this Yŏnhŭi College, which we are about to discuss, was also founded by the hands of the various denominations of the North American United States Christian missionary bodies residing in Chosŏn.

However, let us be satisfied with the results and pull our pen into the main topic.

Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun Hakkyo is located in Yŏnhŭi Township, Koryang County, outside the city. A short distance (幾 丁) north from Sinch'on Station, about ten Western-style school buildings (校舍) soaring into the clouds (雲宵) surprise people.

The principal of this school is Dr. O. R. Avison (오.알 어비슨). Initially called the University Department of Kyŏngsin School (儆新學校 大學部), that was the beginning of Yŏn-jŏn (延專 - Yonhi College). That was in April of the 4th year of Taishō (1915).

A characteristic feature of this school's graduates is that most go to America to study. Generally, there are two types of academic backgrounds in Chosŏn. One consists of students who studied in Japan, attending schools like Waseda (早稻田), Keiō (慶應), Meiji (明治). The other consists of students who studied in Europe and America, primarily the United States. Their actual abilities and activities after returning to society will be discussed on another occasion, but in any case, Yŏnhŭi College accounts for the majority of students studying in America. Therefore, among the 300-plus graduates to date, those who studied in America likely constitute almost half, or more than half. Also, as the training of clergy (敎役者) seems to have been an important purpose for establishing the school, a large number of graduates dedicate themselves to religious fields. **Summary**

This 1932 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine profiles prominent graduates from five major private higher education institutions in colonial Korea: Posŏng College, Ewha Women's College, Sungsil College (P'yŏngyang), Severance Union Medical College, and Yŏnhŭi College. It highlights the diverse career paths of these elites, including law, business, education, journalism, religious work, social activism, medicine, arts, and sciences, often mentioning studies abroad (Japan, USA, Europe). The article celebrates their achievements and contributions to Korean society while occasionally noting involvement in nationalist activities, imprisonment, or specific social movements like the Sin'ganhoe or related to the Kwangju Student Incident.

From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for mapping the early elite networks emerging from these key institutions under Japanese rule. It showcases the significant role of Christian missions in establishing higher education (Ewha, Sungsil, Severance, Yŏnhŭi) and the perceived importance of these schools in producing leaders for various sectors. The mention of graduates involved in nationalist movements, like Hŏ Hŏn or Kang Kidŏk, alongside those pursuing professional careers or religious work, reflects the complex landscape of Korean society navigating colonialism. The detailed lists of names, affiliations, and activities provide rich data for studying the social, political, and cultural history of the period, including the early development of specific professions and intellectual fields.

잡지명	삼천리 제4권 제2호
발행일	1932년 02월 01일
기사제목	五大學府 出의 人材 언.파렛드
기사형태	소식

五大學府 出의 人材 언.파렛드
普成專門學校
아마 半島사회에 인재를 가장 만히 내보낸 私學을 치자면 서울 松峴洞에 잇는 普成專門學校를 첫 손꼬락에 꼽지 안을 수 업스리라.
지금으로 도라다 보면 깜아아득한 녯날 가튼 光武 8년에, 당시 어느 점으로 보든지 조선 근세사상에 위대한 족적을 끼치든 故 忠肅公 李容翊 씨가 장래 반도사회의 棟梁을 양성할 목적으로 창립하엿든 것이다. 이래 26년 동안 춘풍추우에 부닥길대로 부닥기면서도 약 1,000명의 俊聰을, 혹은 민권을 옹호, 신장하는 법조계에, 혹은 실업계에, 혹은 교육방면에, 혹은 사회운동, 민족운동 방면으로 내어보내엇든 것이다.
이 학교의 역할은 흡사히 維新을 완성케 하든 大隈重信의 早稻田大學의 존재와 갓다 하지 안을는지.

세부란쓰醫學專門學校
針대와 藥봉을 가지고 13도 방방곡곡으로 도라다니며 반도민족의 보건을 직히여 준다든 한방의사를 新銳한 과학의 의학전문학교졸업생과 이 「세부란스」醫學專門學校 출신들이 잇다. 전자는 官學이요, 후자는 私學이지만 米國선교회의 투자경영인 것 만치 세부란스醫專의 설비와 규모의 웅대에는 내외국인을 놀나게 하기에 충분하엿다.
생각건대 1884년(明治 17년) 조선 최초의 기독교선교사 아-렌 씨가 故判書 閔泳翊 씨 생명을 구하여준 기회에 그때의 光武帝는 謝意를 겸하야 漢城 南部銅峴에 王室병원을 설치하엿든 것이 세부란쓰병원의 전신이엇다. 그때는 물론 조고마한 조선식 가옥이 병원건물의 전부엇스나 光武 8년에 至하여 외국인선교사의 활동으로 巨資을 취하야 현재의 南大門에다가 규모 웅대한 건물을 신축하고 학교까지 병설하엿든 것이다.
졸업생은 隆熙 2년에 처음 내엇스니 그때의 수는 7인이라. 그 뒤로는 약 30년 동안 해마다 봄이면 의학계의 영재를 배출하여 반도 刀圭界에 보내엇스니 그 수 실로
本科 출신 244명, 別科 11명의 다수에 及하엿다. 졸업생은 京畿의 80명이 가장 만헛고 平北의 38인, 平南의 33, 黃海의 31, 咸南의 16명이 그 버금이엇다.
이제 이 「세부란쓰」학원에서 일홈을 날닌 출신의 몃몃분을 드러보면
隆熙 2년의 수석졸업생 중에
洪錫厚 씨
가 잇다. 뒷날 米國 건너가서 M, D학위까지 밧고 도라온 뒤 모교에서 교편을 잡고 잇는 한편 耳鼻科 과장으로 그 전문부문에 잇서서는 一世의 권위로 존재한다. 최근은 종로 네거리에 사설병원까지 내고서 일반환자까지 취급하고 게신다 하니 그 絶倫한 정력에 凡庸 의사는 오직 愕然할 뿐이다. 그때 洪錫厚 씨와 가치 두뇌의 명민을 자랑하든 청년의학자가 또 한 분이 잇섯스니 그는
朴瑞陽 씨
다년 北間島 局子街라는 萬里胡地에 이르러 救世病院에 주무를 보고 잇다. 그밧게 朱賢則 씨가 잇서 平北 宣川에 이르러 그곳서 同濟醫院을 경영하는 한편 고아원을＜21＞ 설립하여 天地無住의 고아를 위하야 심혈을 다하고 잇다.
또 제3회 때에 학자 한분이 잇섯스니 그이는 현재 모교에서 교편을 들고 잇는
高明宇 씨
라 米國 가서 M, D의 학위를 엇고 도라왓섯다.
제4회 때에
張震燮 씨
란 분이 잇섯다. 아즉 米國서 공부하고 잇는 중으로 씨도 졸업만 하면 모교에 도라와서 교편을 드는 한편 일생을 연구실에서 헌신할 듯 하다하며, 동기의 출신으로 申弼浩 씨가 잇서 지금 서울 인사동에서 개업하고 잇고 또 사회사업인 京城中央實費治療院에서 빈곤한 소시민들의 건강 때문에 애쓰고 잇는 元在 씨와 哈爾賓扶蘇病院에 잇는
羅聖鎬 씨
도 亦 동기이다.
제5회 출신으로 멀니 中國 天津에 가서 博仁醫院을 경영하고 잇는
金基貞 씨
가 잇스며

金顯國 씨
와 北間島 龍井村의

M, D학위를 갓고 모교 교수로 잇는
吳漢泳 씨
며 모교 조수로 잇는데 不遠에 M, D학위를 밧을
金鳴善 씨
등이 유망한 청년의학자이며 기외 조수급으로는 부속병원 의원으로 잇는 분들노 刀胃界를 위하야, 모교를 위하야 애쓰는 분이 훨신 만타.
세부란쓰醫專은 30년의 긴 역사를 가젓거니와 압흐로도 만흔 인재들 배출하여 반도에 잇서 유일하게 의학을 위하야 만장의 氣焰를 토할 것이다.
더구나 그 학교 교장 어비슨 박사는 모교를 더욱 일층 완비식히기 위하야 거액의 자금을 엇고저 米國에 드러가 활동 중이고 또 피부학계의 권위로 학원의 敬慕를 일신에 모으고 잇는 吳競善 박사의 釆配가 우수하야 학원 「세專」의 압날은 광명에 찻다 할 것이다.

延禧專門學校
아직 民立大學 하나를 가지지 못한 우리의 일이니 무에라고 남으리랴만은 이 우에 쓴 崇實專門이나 세부란쓰醫專이나 梨花女子專門이나 모다 외국선교사업의 餘澤으로 된 것인 모양으로 지금 말하려 하는 이 延禧專門 역시 朝鮮在駐 북미합중기독교 각파 선교사단의 손으로 창립되엿든 것이다.
그러나 우리는 결과만 가지고 만족하기로 하고 본론에 붓을 끄으러넛차.
延禧專門學校는 시외 高陽郡 延禧面에 두엇스니 신촌역에서 북편으로 幾 丁 쯤 가면 雲宵에 소슨 10여 棟의 洋風 校舍가 사람을 놀내게 한다.
이 학교 교長은 오.알 어비슨 박사로 처음에는 儆新學校 大學部라고 한 것이 延專의 시초엇다. 그것이 大正 4년 4월달의 일이다.

이 학교 출신은 대개 亞米利加로 유학가는 것이 특색이다. 대체 조선에는 학벌이 두 개가 잇다. 하나는 早稻田, 慶應, 明治 하는 日本系 유학생이요, 다른 한편은 米國을 주로한 歐米系 유학생들이다. 그 실력의 여하와 귀국 후 사회에 나와서 활약하는 여부는 다른 기회에 언급하려 하거니와 엇잿든 米國유학생의 다대수를 점령함이 이 延禧專門인데 그러기에 오늘까지 300여 명 졸업생 중 亞米利加 出이 거지 반, 반수 이상은 차지할 것이다. 또 敎役者 양성이 학교 세운 중요한 취지인 것 갓치 출신들은 대개 종교방면에 헌신하는 이가 다수하다. 취업별로 보면 (昭和 6년 8월 조)
