That Guy is Also That Guy

A certain country fellow came looking for a post office in Sŏul and asked, "Hey there. Hasn't a package arrived from P’yŏngyang for a fellow named Kim Kyŏngsik?"

Clerk: Yes. It has arrived.

That fellow: Egad, that's great. I am that very person, so please give me the package.

Clerk: However, I have no way of knowing that you are Mr. Kim Kyŏngsik, so I cannot give out the package.

That fellow thought for a moment, then took his photograph out of his pocket and showed it.

That fellow: Then look at this. Isn't this photograph undoubtedly me?

Clerk: (Looking at the photograph, relieved) That's right! Please take it quickly. (He handed over the package.)<37>

**Summary**

This short anecdote recounts a humorous interaction at a Sŏul post office in 1932. A rural man arrives to pick up a package addressed to Kim Kyŏngsik. When the clerk asks for proof of identity, the man simply presents his own photograph. The clerk, surprisingly, accepts this as sufficient proof and hands over the package. From a historical perspective, the piece offers a lighthearted glimpse into everyday interactions and bureaucratic processes (or lack thereof) in colonial Korea. It might subtly comment on perceived naiveté (either of the rural man or the urban clerk), or simply serve as a simple joke reflecting common occurrences or social observations of the time.

그놈도 그놈이야
서울 모 우편국에 엇던 시골사내가 차저와서 『이보소. 金京植이라고 하는 사내에게 平壤서 소포가 안 왓서요?』
局員―네. 와 잇서요.
그 사내―에그, 잘 되엿구만. 내가 바로 그 본인이니 소포를 이리 내주오.
局員―그리치만 당신이 金京植 씨 인줄을 내가 알 수 업스니 소포는 내어줄 수 업소.
그 사내는 한참 생각하더니 호주머니에서 제 사진을 끄집어 내서 보인다.
그 사내―그러면 이것을 보세요. 틀님업시 이 사진이 내가 아니오.
局員―(사진을 들고 보더니 안심하며) 올쿠만, 어서 가저가시오.(소포를 내어주엇다)＜37＞
