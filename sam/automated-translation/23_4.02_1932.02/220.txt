"Our Baby Day"
						Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi

1. Yesterday, a good day, our baby
On the day's journey of ninety ri, not a speck of wind
Tonight, passing Surijae Pass
Our baby rests peacefully in Hanyang.

2. Spring wind blows where our child goes
When our baby sleeps on the journey, the waves sleep too
Each of our blessed babies
Gives peace and joy to the world.

I wrote this poem thinking of the young child I can never meet again. The gloomy falling snow and rain have cleared, and the sky has become vividly blue. And my illness has also completely healed like this. This poem, "Our Baby Day," was set to music by Mr. An Kiyŏng and has also been recorded onto a "record" (레-코트). I bought the "record" and brought it home, and gathering my wife and young child, I put this song on.

As various things from days past came to mind, we realized, without knowing it ourselves, that tears were streaming down. The "record" turned round and round, the "song" continued, and our tears flowed without knowing how to stop.

—On the day of the first full moon, January 1932—(Written by Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi) <61>

**Summary**

The author, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi, presents a short, tender poem titled "Our Baby Day," imagining a baby's peaceful journey. He reveals that he wrote it while mourning a child he lost ("the young child I can never meet again") and recovering from an illness. The poem was set to music by composer An Kiyŏng and released on a phonograph record. The author recounts the poignant experience of listening to this record with his wife and surviving child, bringing tears to their eyes as they remembered the past. For historians, this article offers a personal window into grief, memory, and family life in colonial Korea. It also highlights the intersection of traditional poetic sentiment with modern technology (phonograph records) and the collaboration between literary and musical figures of the era (Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi and An Kiyŏng).

「우리 아기날」
						崔貞熙
1. 어제날 조흔날 우리 아기
날나무리 구십리 일점풍 업네
오날밤 수리재치나
우리 아기 한양에 평안히 쉬네.

2. 우리 아이 가는데 봄바람 불고
우리 아기 나무리 잠잘 때 물결도 자네
복 만흔 우리 애기마다
세상에 화평과 깃븜 주네.

나는 다시 못 맛날 어린 애기를 생각하고 이 시를 썻슴니다. 음산하게 나리든 눈비도 거두고 샛파-랏케 하늘이 맑어젓섯슴니다. 그리고 내 병도 이럿케 완쾌해 젓슴니다. 「우리 아기날」 이 시는 安基永 씨의 작곡으로 노래가 되여 「레-코트」에도 취입하엿슴니다. 나는 「레-코트」를 사가지고 와서 안해와 어린 애기를 대리고 이 노래를 걸어노앗슴니다.
지나간 날에 가지가지가 머리에 떠올으게 되엿슴으로 우리는 자신도 몰으게 눈물이 흘너내림을 깨달엇슴니다. 「레-코트」는 작고 돌아서 「노래」는 게속되고 우리의 눈물도 끈흘줄 몰으고 흘넛슴니다.
―1932, 정월 보름날에―(崔貞熙 筆記)＜61＞
