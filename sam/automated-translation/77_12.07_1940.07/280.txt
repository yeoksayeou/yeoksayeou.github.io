Popular Lecture on Physiognomy, Continued Study of the Features of Wealth and Nobility, Those Who Will Become 'High Officials and Great Merchants'
						Pae Sangch’ŏl

Four Spiritual Methods (四靈術法)

The Four Spiritual Methods (四靈術法) do not require special spiritual communication; they are simply methods to judge fortune (吉凶) by memorizing four laws—① Body Fortune (體運), ② Disaster/Misfortune (災厄), ③ Depletion/Loss (耗損), ④ Fixed Misfortune Year (定厄年)—and comparing them with the person's facial features. However, for Disaster/Misfortune (災厄), if it falls in a good area according to the facial year chart (面部流年) for that year, it can instead bring auspicious events like family celebrations or honor. For example, a person born in the year of the Rat (子) encounters Disaster/Misfortune at ages 27, 28, and 29. If their Ch’ongmyo (塚墓, tomb area, associated with age 27) is good, then age 27 will instead bring good events. If their Indang (印堂, area between eyebrows, associated with age 28) is sunken, has lines or moles, or is narrow, that year will be unlucky. If their Chwa Sanrim (左山林, left forest area, associated with age 29) is good, even though it is a Disaster/Misfortune year, auspicious events will occur. The Four Spiritual Methods are recorded below.

(A) Body Fortune (體運). To understand Body Fortune, one must first know the Five Elements Body Framework (五行의 體局). Since the framework (局) is not necessary here, I will omit it and explain only the body type (體), which must be understood first.
① Metal Type (全體 is likely a typo for 金體): The face is square and upright, the complexion is clear and white, the facial flesh is<202> not excessive, yet the bones are not thin.
② Wood Type (木體): Tall and straight, joints are firm, complexion seems bluish, body is slender.
③ Water Type (水體): Round and thick, blackish complexion, stomach and back are prominent.
④ Fire Type (火體): Forehead is receding or the top of the head is pointed, yet the face is full and sharp, and reddish.
⑤ Earth Type (土體): Face resembles the character 田 (field), stout and thick, complexion is yellow, back is prominent.
However, to determine the Five Elements body type without error, one must consult the palm (手掌).
① Metal Type Person's Palm (金體人의 手掌): Round and thick, finger joints are round.
② Wood Type Person's Palm (木體人의 手掌): Thin, long fingers, many lines (紋).
③ Water Type Person's Palm (水體人의 手掌): Much flesh, appearing puffy (浮服), soft and smooth (軟滑), finger joints not protruding.
④ Fire Type Person's Palm (火體人의 手掌): Palm is thin, fingers are pointed (尖).
⑤ Earth Type Person's Palm (土體人의 手掌): Square and thick, fingers are angular (모지고) and short.
When assessing Body Fortune, for a Metal Type person, the years ending in four and nine, and three and eight are unfavorable. For example, years like 14, 24, 34 and 19, 29, 39 are unfavorable. However, one should judge by also consulting the auspiciousness or inauspiciousness of the facial feature corresponding to the year (流年部位). This is recorded concisely below.
Metal Type Person (金體人): Years 4, 9, 3, 8 are unlucky (凶).
Wood Type Person (木體人): Years 1, 3, 8, 9 are unlucky (凶) (Pure Wood type finds year 1 unlucky; if mixed with Metal framework (金局), year 1 is conversely lucky).
Water Type Person (水體人): Years 1, 2, 10, 4 are unlucky (凶).
Fire Type Person (火體人): Years 2, 7, 9, 5 are unlucky (凶).
Earth Type Person (土體人): Years 4, 9, 5, 10 are unlucky (凶).

(B) Disaster/Misfortune (災厄). Disaster/Misfortune is determined by calculating from the person's birth year, and there is much to consider.
People born in Rat (子), Horse (午), Rabbit (卯), Rooster (酉) years: Ages 3, 4, 5; 15, 16, 17; 27, 28, 29; 39, 40, 41; 51, 52, 53; 63, 64, 65; 75, 76, 77; 87, 88, 89.
People born in Tiger (寅), Monkey (申), Snake (巳), Pig (亥) years: Ages 7, 8, 9; 19, 20, 21; 31, 32, 33; 43, 44, 45; 55, 56, 57; 67, 68, 69; 79, 80, 81.
People born in Dragon (辰), Dog (戌), Ox (丑), Sheep (未) years: Ages 11, 12, 13; 23, 24, 25; 35, 36, 37; 47, 48, 49; 59, 60, 61; 71, 72, 73; 83, 84, 85.
(The Disaster/Misfortune years listed above may involve marriage (嫁娶), personal illness, financial loss (損財), gossip/disputes (口舌), mourning (重服), or the birth of sons or daughters. If the year corresponds to an auspicious facial feature (面部의 吉處), auspicious events occur. If it corresponds to a feature with lines, moles (紋痣), depressions (凹), or protrusions (凸), it is an unlucky year. Disaster/Misfortune comes in three-year cycles (三年式 닿는 三災).)

(C) Depletion/Loss (耗損). In years of Depletion/Loss, one may suffer financial loss, fail in endeavors, <203> or experience other misfortunes (厄) (sometimes official trouble (官厄)).
For those born in Rat (子), Tiger (寅), Dragon (辰), Horse (午), Monkey (申), Dog (戌) years: Ages 8, 20, 32, 44, 56, 68, 80 are Depletion (耗) years.
For those born in Sheep (未), Snake (巳), Rabbit (卯), Ox (丑), Pig (亥), Rooster (酉) years: Ages 6, 18, 30, 42, 54, 66, 78, 90 are Depletion (耗) years.
For those born in Rat (子), Tiger (寅), Snake (巳) years: Ages 7, 19, 31, 43, 55, 67 are Loss (損) years.
For those born in Ox (丑), Rabbit (卯) years: Ages 9, 21, 33, 45, 57, 69 are Loss (損) years.
For those born in Horse (午), Monkey (申), Dog (戌) years: Ages 8, 20, 32, 44, 56, 68 are Loss (損) years.
For those born in Sheep (未), Rooster (酉), Pig (亥) years: Ages 18, 30, 42, 54, 66 are Loss (損) years.
For those born in Dragon (辰) year: Ages 4, 16, 28, 40, 52, 64 are Loss (損) years.

(D) Fixed Misfortune (定厄). Fixed Misfortune Year refers to two types of misfortune combined: one based on the specific year of birth according to the sexagenary cycle (生甲) indicating which year is unfavorable, and the other being Misfortune Years in Physiognomy (相厄年) which apply to everyone at certain ages regardless of birth year.
① Fixed Misfortune by Birth Year (生甲定厄). Gengyin (庚寅) born: Age 31, financial loss, family worries, or other misfortune. Wushen (戊申) born: Age 29. Renzǐ (壬子) born: Age 26. Dingyou (丁酉) born, Bingwu (丙午) born, Renchen (壬辰) born: All at age 29. Dingwei (丁未) born: Age 30. Jiawu (甲午) born: Age 39. Yiwei (乙未) born: Age 43. Xinchou (辛丑) born: Age 29, 30. Jichou (己丑) born: Ages 43, 45, 46, 47, 48 difficult (困). Guisi (癸巳) born: Age 40. Xinmao (辛卯) born: Age 46. Gengzi (庚子) born: Ages 37, 39.
Among women, unfavorable birth years (生甲) are Jichou (己丑), Bingwu (丙午), Wuzi (戊子), Gengshen (庚申); all are unfavorable for the husband's palace (夫宮), meaning frequent separation (離別數) during their lifetime. Men born in Bingwu (丙午) and Gengshen (庚申) years also frequently experience separation from their wives (別妻) or remarriage (再妻數). Also, women born in the Ox (丑) year often lose their husbands (喪夫), those born in Rat (子) or Sheep (未) years often separate from husbands (別夫), and those born in Tiger (寅) year also have a high chance of separation from husbands. (The above applies to the entire life (一平生論)).
② Misfortune Years in Physiognomy (相厄年). Misfortune Years are ages 15, 19, 25, 33-34, 35-36, 41-42-43, 51, 60, 73, 77. These are misfortune years for everyone, male or female. Among these, for women, ages 19, 43, 45 bring severe misfortune (厄), and for men, ages 25, 35, 41, 43, 61 bring more misfortune. However, consult the facial year chart (面部流年).

Chapter Four

First, How to Know the Poverty or Wealth of the Maternal Family (外家)
If the head of the eyebrow, near the Indang (印堂), has eyebrow hairs that are pointed (尖) or stick downwards (揷下), forming an angle (稜角) and standing stiffly, the person has discord with siblings and also comes from a poor maternal family (外家). Those with both eyebrows short and fine (短細) often have wealthy maternal families.<204> Those with thick and murky (濃濁) eyebrows often have discordant relationships with their maternal family.

Second, How to Know About Leaving One's Hometown (故鄕)
People who leave their hometown (敎鄭 is likely a typo for 故鄉) to live elsewhere, or who will leave in the future, have ears that are darkish (검으충충하여) with little clear white color, and the ear skin is coarse (粗). People with inverted ears (耳反, ears flipped outward) or those lacking the helix ridge (弦根, rim and structure of the ear) also leave their hometown. People whose Sangŭn (山根, root of the nose between eyes) is low and sunken (低陷) or who have a protruding Adam's apple (結喉) also leave their hometown. People whose Pyŏnji (邊地, border land area of face) and Yŏngma (驛馬, post horse area, related to travel) are overly raised (過起) or overly sunken (過陷) also constantly travel to other places or leave their hometown entirely.

Third, Profession (職業)
A person's profession changes according to their will, making it the most difficult thing to determine. However, those who can be seen, through physiognomy (相法), to possess the aptitude for a certain suitable profession (適業) generally engage in that profession or achieve success through it. However, if a person changes their field of work too drastically before age 40—for instance, a government official (官吏) switching to commerce (商業), then from commerce to industry (工業)—without considering their aptitude and capacity, their chances of great success (大機) are often reduced. This is because they lack the time to accumulate sufficient experience and foundation (근사, likely 根基). Now, let's discuss suitable professions.
The physiognomy of an Industrialist/Craftsman (工業家) includes few lines (紋) on the palm, stiff and spread-out fingers; this is often seen in woodworkers (木工), ironworkers (鐵工), etc.
A person who works in service to others (使役) (like errand boys (小使), laborers (人夫), servants (僕從), shop clerks (店員), etc.) has a small nose, rough and spread-out fingers, lean facial flesh, a weak voice (聲音), and a proper spirit (神).
The physiognomy of an Artist (藝術家) includes shining eyes, fine and smooth eyebrows (眉毛), a long nose and face, soft hands, and prominent cheekbones (顴骨). Also, those with large eyes where the eyeballs do not protrude (浮露) often have artistic talent. High-level technicians (高等한 技術家) also have similar features. Those with a large mouth (口) and a small face (面) have features indicating a love for singing and playing instruments (歌吹), thus suited to being vocalists (聲樂家).
A person suited to farming (農夫) or specializing in agriculture (農業) has a short upper section of the face (上停, forehead), a long lower section (下停, chin), a coarse bone structure (粗한 골격), thick and murky eyebrows and eyes (濃濁한 눈섭과 눈). Additionally, their face often looks dusty (塵埃), the light in their eyes is yellowish (黃), and their hands are rough (粗大).
Those whose Nyŏnsang (年上) and Susang (壽上) areas (parts of the nose bridge) are low (大商은 反高, great merchants are the opposite, high) have large hands, and a shallow navel (大商反深, great merchants are the opposite, deep) are often sedentary merchants (坐商) or traveling traders (行賈); traveling traders often have long legs. Those with a large nose showing stagnant energy (滯氣) work as traveling merchants (商旅), while those with a small nose showing stagnation work as shop clerks (店員), errand boys (小使), attendants (給仕), etc. <205>
Those whose height (身長) is barely 5 ch'ŏk (尺, approx. 150cm), have a small and low head, and messy hair (頭髮이 亂散된 者) often become craftsmen (職工).
Those with a white complexion (面色), red or spread-out fingertips, a shallow navel, and a nose lacking energy (無氣) (not firm, or the Nyŏnsang/Sangŭn area is sunken) often become photographers (寫眞師), watch repairmen (時計修繕業), seal engravers (印章彫刻), or similar technicians (技工).
Those with a white complexion, pointed fingers with many flaws (痕跡), and few palm lines (紋) are often suited for ironwork (鐵工).
Teachers (敎員) and Doctors (醫師) are often found among those of the Wood Type (木體) with clear and refined eyebrows and eyes (眉目이 淸秀), but this is not universally true. Those who become teachers of others (人의 師) have cheekbones (顴骨) higher than the eye level, reaching the Myŏngmun (命門) and Oktang (獄堂) (both in front of the ear). That is, the area in front of the ear gate (耳門 前) is called the Haktang (學堂, study hall). Those whose Haktang is fleshy and full (肉豊), have beautiful eyebrows (眉美), and a flat Indang (印堂平), possessing both these latter features, will become teachers of learning (學問의 師).
The face of a person suited for government service (官吏) or other salaried positions in society is primarily found in those of the Metal Type (金體) with clarity (淸), or Wood Type (木體) with clarity. Firstly, the Ch'ŏnjŏng (天庭), Chungjŏng (中正), Indang (印堂) areas (parts of the forehead) should be flat. Generally, the entire forehead should be square and flat (方平), round and raised (圓聳), or reclining like a silkworm (偃臥), but taut (팽팽한). Next, the Sangŭn (山根) and Nyŏnsang (年上) should not be sunken, the Ch'ŏnch'ang (天倉) and Chŏnjŏng (天井) (temple areas) should not be sunken, the fingers should be fine, the back of the head should not be sunken or shaved-looking (陷削), the ears should be white or reddish, the nose not short or small, the teeth not short or pointed (短尖), the navel deep (深), the face should show energy (氣), appearing well-structured (結曲), and the eyes clear and eyebrows refined (目淸眉秀). If someone lacking these features becomes a government official, they will merely be a very minor and insignificant errand boy (小使).
Furthermore, people possessing the Eight Greatnesses (八大相) – large eyes (眼大), large nose (鼻大), large mouth (口大), large ears (耳大), large head (頭大), loud voice (聲大), large face (面大), large body (身大) – or the Eight Smallnesses (八小相) – small eyes (眼小), small nose (鼻小), small mouth (口小), small ears (耳小), small forehead (額小), soft voice (聲小), small face (面小), small body (身小) – or the Five Longnesses (五長) – long head (頭長), long face (面長), long body (身長), long hands (手長), long feet (足長) – or the Five Shortnesses (五短) – short head (頭短), short face (面短), short body (身長), short hands (手短), short legs (脚短) – will surely receive significant government stipends (官祿). Among the Eight Smallnesses and Five Shortnesses, those who are also Wood Type may look plain, with eyes and nose seeming clustered together (다닥다닥 붙든 것 같에여) as if lowly (賤할 듯 하되), but in reality, great officials (大官) are more numerous among such people. However, all these faces must have good eyes, facial energy (氣), and clarity (淸). Those with a black face and white body (面黑身白) are also often found among noble people (貴人) who receive stipends (食祿).
Currently employed government officials or other salaried individuals have a lustrous sheen (潤擇한 빛) on their Ch'ŏnjŏng (天庭). Retired individuals have less of this sheen. Those who will soon be dismissed (免職) have a reddish energy (赤氣) in the Kandae (諫臺) and Chŏngwi (廷尉) areas (parts of the chin/jaw), and dust-like marks (塵埃) appear faintly in the center of their Indang (印堂).
Those who frequently change their profession often have a sunken Chungjŏng (中正, middle part of forehead), a pointed Chigak (地閣, chin), inverted ears (耳反), or lines on the forehead (額紋).
Those with large, protruding eyes (眼이 大하고 露出한 者), those with deeply sunken eyes, those lacking flesh in the Ch'ŏnch'ang (天倉) and Chijŏng (地庭, likely 地閣 - chin area), those with a narrow space between the eyebrows (兩眉間이 狹窄한 者), those with a sharply protruding Adam's apple (結喉), those with a small nose and a pointed Chundu (準頭, tip of the nose), those with soft and thin ears (耳가 軟하고 薄한 者), those with an excessively small head and a thin waist, and those whose hand flesh is thin and rough (荒한 者) – all frequently change professions and do not know how to be content in their position (安分). (End of Volume One) <206>

**Summary**

This article by Pae Sangch'ŏl is a popular guide to Korean physiognomy (face reading), continuing a series on identifying features associated with wealth and high status ("High Officials and Great Merchants"). It details the "Four Spiritual Methods" for predicting fortune based on body type (linked to the five elements), calculating unlucky years (Disaster, Depletion/Loss, Fixed Misfortune) based on birth year and age, and reading specific facial features to determine maternal family background, likelihood of leaving one's hometown, and suitable professions ranging from artisans and farmers to officials and artists. The text provides specific physical characteristics thought to correspond to different occupations and social roles.

From a historical perspective, this 1940 article is interesting as it reflects the enduring popularity of traditional divination practices like physiognomy in colonial Korea, offering people perceived ways to understand and perhaps navigate their fate during a period of significant social change and uncertainty under Japanese rule. The detailed categorization of professions and the features associated with them provides a glimpse into the social hierarchy and occupational landscape as perceived at the time. The emphasis on identifying potential "great officials" and "great merchants" might also reflect aspirations for success and social mobility within the constraints of the colonial system.

四靈術法
四靈術法이라 함은 별로 通靈을 하여야 됨이 아니오, 다만 ①은 體運, ②는 災厄, ③은 耗損, ④는 定厄年이니, 이 四法을 暗誦하여, 그 사람의 얼굴부위와 대조하여 吉凶을 판단하는 법이라. 그런데 다만 災厄은 그 해의 面部流年의 좋은 자리에 놓이면 도로혀, 家慶이나 명예같은 吉事가 도래하는 것이다. 比컨대, 子生은 27,8,9歲에 災厄이 닿는 바, 그 사람의 27歲에 流年이 닿는 塚墓가 좋으면 27歲는 도로혀 좋은 일이있고, 28印堂이 陷커나, 紋痣가 있거나 狹하면 그 해에 不吉하며, 29歲 左山林이 좋으면 비록 災厄年이지만 吉慶의 事가 있는 것이다. 左에 四靈法을 記한다.
(甲) 體運. 體運은 먼저, 五行의 體局을 알어야 하는 바 局은 여게 불필요함으로 畧하고, 體만 설명하는 바이니 이것을 먼저 알어야 한다.
①全體는 얼굴이, 方正하고 色이 潔白하고, 面肉이＜202＞ 不盈하고도 骨이 不薄하며.
②木體는 瘦直하고 骨節이 堅하며, 色이 靑한 듯하고 身體-빼냈으며.
③水體는 圓厚하고, 黑色이며, 배와 등이 두드럭하고
④火體는 이마가 빠르거나, 頭上이 尖커나 하고도, 얼굴이 豊銳코 赤色이며.
⑤土體는 얼굴이 田字와 近하고 敦厚하고, 색이 黃하며 등이 두드럭하니라.
그러나 五行의 體를 定하려면 手掌을 참고하여야 틀림이 없나리라.
①全體人의 手掌은 圓厚하고 指節이 圓하며.
②木體人의 手掌은 파리하고 指長, 紋多하며.
③水體人의 手掌은 肉이 多하여 浮服하고 軟滑하며 指節이 露치않고.
④火體人의 手掌은 손바닥이 파리하고 손가락이 尖하며.
⑤土體人의 手掌은 方厚하고 손가락이 모지고도 短하다.
體運을 볼 때에는 金體人은 四九年과 三八年이 不利하니 比컨대 十四, 二十四, 三十四의 年이 닿는 해와. 十九, 二十九, 三十九 등의 해가 불리하다. 그러나 流年部位의 길흉을 참고하여 판단할 것이다. 左에 그것을 簡明케 記한다.
金體人. 四, 九, 三, 八年凶

第四章

第一, 外家의 貧富를 아는 法
眉의 頭, 즉 印堂附近의 眉毛가 尖하거나 揷下하여 稜角처럼 되고 빳빳하게 일어슨 이는 형제간에 不睦하고 兼하여 外家가 빈한하며. 兩眉가 短細한 이는 外家가 富하게＜204＞ 사는 이가 많고, 兩眉가 濃濁하면 외가와 不和케 지내는 이가 퍽 많다.

第二, 故鄕 떠남을 아는 法
자기의 敎鄭을 떠나 타향에 가서 사는 이나 혹은 장차 떠날 사람은 두 귀가 검으충충하여 潔白色이 적고 귀가죽이 粗하니라. 귀가 뒤집힌(耳反)사람이나 귀에 弦根(귀, 갓테와 권밥이 없읍)이 없는 사람도 고향을 떠나고 山根이 低陷커나 結喉(목아지뼈가 툭 나온 것)가 된 사람도 고향을 떠나며 邊地와 驛馬가 過起, 혹 過陷된 사람도 늘 客地로 다니거나 아조 離鄕을 하거나 한다.

第三, 職業
사람의 직업이란 마음대로 變함으로 第一 알기가 어렵다. 그러나 適業으로 볼 수 있는 것, 즉 그러한 소질을 타고 났다고 相法에 볼 수 있는 이는 대개, 그런 직업에 從事코 또는 그것으로 성공케 된다. 다만 사람이 40이전에 직업의 방면을 너무 엉뚱하게 변하면, 즉 官吏가 商業으로 商業에서 다시 工業으로 이같이 소질과 역량을 생각지 않고 자조 변하는 이는 大機가 성공이 적게 된다. 그것은 그만한 경험과 근사를 모딜 여가가 없는 까닭이다. 이제 그 適業을 말하자.
工業家의 相은 손바닥에 금(紋)이 적고, 손가락이 빳빳하고도 버서지고 하는 바, 대개는 木工, 鐵工 등에 많다.
남의 使役(小使, 人夫, 僕從, 店員 등)노릇을 하는 사람은 코가 적고 손가락이 거칠고 버서지며 얼굴의 살이 홀족하니 되고, 聲音이 약하고 神이 端하니라.
藝術家의 相은 눈이 빛나고 眉毛가 細하고도 고흐며 코와 얼굴이 길고 손이 연하고 顴骨이 나왔으며. 또 눈이 크고도, 눈알이 浮露되지 않은 이가 흔히 예술가로서의 소질이 있다. 高等한 技術家도 또한 이상과 近似하니라. 口가 大하고 面이 小한 이는 歌吹를 좋아하는 相인즉, 聲樂家에 適當하니라.
農夫 노릇이나 農業을 專業할 사람은 上停(이만)이 短하고, 下停(턱)이 長하며 粗한 골격에 濃濁한 눈섭과 눈을 가진 者이며 겸하여 面上에 塵埃가 많고 眼中의 빛히 黃하며 손이 거치니라.(粗大)
年上壽上이 低하고(大商은 反高) 손이 크며 배꼽이 얕은 者(大商反深)는 대개 坐商이나 行賈를 하는 바 行賈는 흔이 다리가 진이라. 코가 크고도 滯氣가 있는 이는 商旅노릇을 하고 적고도 滯한 이는 店員이나, 小使, 給仕 등의 役을 한다.＜205＞
身長이 불과 5尺될 만치 短하고 머리가 小低하고 頭髮이 亂散된 者는 흔히 職工이 되고.
面色이 白하고 손끗치 빨고 또는 버서지며 배꼽이 얕고 코가 無氣(딱딱하지 못하거나, 年上, 山根이 陷한 것)한 이는 寫眞師, 時計修繕業, 印章彫刻 등의 技工을 하고.
面色이 白하고 손가락이 尖하고 痕跡(흠)이 많고 손바닥 금이(紋) 없는 이는 鐵工에 近하니라.
敎員과 醫師는 木體중의 人으로서 眉目이 淸秀한 이 중에 흔이 많으나 전체로 그런 것은 아니다. 人의 師가 되는 이는 顴骨이 眼際보다 高하여 命門, 獄堂(모다 耳 前)에 達한다, 즉 耳門 前을 學堂이라고 하는 바 學堂이 肉豊하고 眉美, 印堂平의 兩者를 겸한 이는 學問의 師가 되나리라.
官吏나 혹 다른 사회에 봉급생활을 할 사람의 얼굴은 첫째, 金體로 되고 淸하거나, 木體로 되고 淸한 이에게 많으며 첫째로 天庭, 中正, 印堂 등이 판판하고 대체로는 이마 전체가 方平하거나 圓聳하거나 偃臥하되 팽팽한 사람에 있으며. 그 다음 山根, 年上이 陷치않고 天倉, 天井이 陷치 않고 손가락이 곱고 뒤통수가 陷削치 않고 귀가 희거나 紅色이 있거나 하고 코가 短小치 않고 이가 短尖치 않고 배꼽이 深하고 얼굴에 氣, 즉 結曲해 보이고 目淸眉秀한 사람 중에 있나니 만일 그렇지 못한 이로서 官吏가 된다면 極히 작고 薄한 小使가 될 뿐이니라.
그리고 眼大, 鼻大, 口大, 耳大, 頭大, 聲大, 面大, 身大의 八大相을 가진 사람이나. 또는
眼小, 鼻小, 口小, 耳小, 額小, 聲小, 面小, 身小의 八小相을 가진 사람이나. 또는 頭長, 面長, 身長, 手長, 足長의 五長을 가진 사람이나. 또는
頭短, 面短, 身短, 手短, 脚短의 五短을 가진 사람은 반드시 官祿을 重케 먹는 바 八小, 五短에 木體를 겸한 이는 보기에 못생겨 보이고 눈 코가 다닥다닥 붙든 것 같에여 賤할 듯 하되 실상 그런 이로서의 大官이 더 많은이라. 그러나 이런 얼굴들은 모다 눈이 좋아야 하고 얼굴에 氣가 있고 淸하여서. 面黑身白한 이도 또한 食祿할 貴人에게 많으니라.
현재 官吏나 다른 봉급받는 이의 얼굴은 그 天庭이 潤擇한 빛이 나고 退職한 이는 그런 빛이 적으며 免職을 未久에 당할 사람은 諫臺廷尉에 赤氣가 있고 印堂 중심에 塵埃가 隱現하나니라.
자조 그 직업을 고치는 이는 中正이 陷커나 地閣이 尖커나 耳反額紋이 된 이에 많다.
眼이 大하고 露出한 者와 눈이 크게 들어간 者와 天倉地庭에 無肉한 者와 兩眉間이 狹窄한 者와 목에 結喉가 뾰족히 된 이와 코가 적고 準頭가 尖커나 귀가 軟하고 薄한 者와 머리가 過小하고 허리가 가는 者와 手의 肉이 薄하고도 荒한 者는 모다 자조 職業을 고치고 安分할줄을 모르나니라.(一卷終)＜206＞
