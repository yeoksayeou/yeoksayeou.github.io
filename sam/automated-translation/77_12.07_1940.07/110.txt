Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제7호
Publication Date: 1940년 07월 01일
Article Title: 自己를 罪주는 精神, 還都記念論文發表 (The Spirit of Self-Reproach, Paper Published Commemorating the Return of the Capital)
Author: 汪精衛
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Argumentative Essay)

Upon the occasion of the ceremony marking the Return of the Capital (還都式典) on April 26th, Mr. Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛), Acting Chairman of the National Government (國民政府), published a commemorative paper titled "The Spirit of Self-Reproach." In it, he expressed his firm resolve to advance toward the glorious future of Peace, Anti-Communism, and Nation-Building (和平反共建國).

How can the responsibility of Peace, Anti-Communism, and Nation-Building be fulfilled? Regarding this, it merely comes down to one phrase: possessing the spirit of self-reproach. The spirit of self-reproach is different from arrogance, and even more different from servility. Why is it different from arrogance? Because in the Chungking (重慶) sphere, there are still those who say, "In the current war, Japan is the aggressor and China is the victim of aggression, so if Japan withdraws its troops (撤兵), everything will be resolved." This is purely an expression of an arrogant spirit. Why is the spirit of self-reproach different from servility? Because there are those who say, "China has lost the military advantage, sees no hope for ultimate victory in the current resistance, and thus, having no choice but to protect the life of the nation and its people, bends its will and seeks peace talks (講和)." That is a servile spirit.<100>

Then, what is this spirit of self-reproach? I intend to raise the following two points:
One. To possess deep self-reflection.
Two. To possess a deep sense of responsibility.
On this day when my dear compatriots celebrate the Return of the Capital of the National Government and advocate for peace, the reason we cry out the slogan "Reproach yourselves!" is none other than to spur ourselves on and mutually encourage one another. Since ancient times, when two countries fight, the defeated nation bears the duty of reparations and cession of territory. As a result, they lose morale and merely sustain their remaining life, or they grit their teeth and resolve to clench their fists for revenge (抱腕復仇). However, what we must shoulder now is not such a duty, but a different one. This duty is no ordinary duty. That is, it demands that China (中國) and Japan (日本) jointly shoulder the responsibility for the Revival of East Asia (東亞復興).

Currently, the National Government has returned its capital to Nanking (南京), published its declaration, clarified its policies, and envoys from the Japanese government and national envoys have arrived. From this single aspect, it can be known that the realization of peace is not necessarily impossible, and thus the first step towards peace has already become manifest as reality. People of the Chungking side, come! You should no longer be obstacles to peace. Do not delay comprehensive peace through your opposition and skepticism. The reality of peace awaits the efforts of the entire nation's populace. As we celebrate the Return of the Capital of the National Government and advocate for peace, we especially think of you, hoping to strive together with you and bravely march forward towards the glorious future of Peace, Anti-Communism, and Nation-Building.<101>

**Summary**

This article is an editorial by Wang Ching-wei, head of the Japanese-sponsored Reorganized National Government of China in Nanking, published in a Korean magazine in 1940. Wang argues for his policy of "Peace, Anti-Communism, and Nation-Building" through collaboration with Japan, framing it as a necessary "spirit of self-reproach." He contrasts this with the "arrogance" of Chiang Kai-shek's Chungking government (which demands Japanese withdrawal) and the "servility" of simply surrendering due to military defeat. He urges all Chinese, including those aligned with Chungking, to join his government in Nanking to achieve peace and the "Revival of East Asia" alongside Japan. For Korean colonial history, the article is significant as it showcases pro-Japanese collaborationist rhetoric being disseminated in Korea. It reveals the ideological justifications used by collaborators like Wang Ching-wei, framing cooperation with Japan not as surrender but as a responsible path towards regional revival, an argument likely intended to resonate with or persuade colonized Koreans facing similar pressures and narratives.

自己를 罪주는 精神, 還都記念論文發表
						汪精衛
4월 26일의 還都式典을 마지함에 際하여 國民政府 代理主席 汪精衛氏는 『自己를 罪주는 精神』이라고 題한 기념논문을 발표하고, 和平反共建國의 光輝있는 前途를 향해 매진할 굳은 결심을 표명하였다.

어떻게 하여야 和平反共建國의 책임을 완성할 수 있을가. 이것에 대해서는 그저 자기를 罪주는 정신을 가질 것이라는 한마디에 그친다. 자기를 罪주는 精神이라함은 오만과 다르며, 비굴과는 더욱 다르다. 웨 오만과 다른고 하니 重慶방면에는 아직 『今回의 전쟁에 있어서 日本은 침략자요 中國은 피침략자이기 때문에 일본이 撤兵하며는 一切가 해결된다』고 말하는 자가 있으나 이것은 철두철미 오만한 정신의 표현이다. 자기를 罪주는 정신은 웨 卑屈과 다른고 하니 『中國은 군사상 利를 잃고 목전의 抗戰, 도저히 최후의 승리의 희망이 없음을 보고 할 수 없이 국가민족의 생명을 보호하기 위해서 뜻을 굴하고 講和를 구한다』고 말하는 자가 있으나 그것은 비굴한 정신이다.＜100＞

그러면 자기를 罪주는 정신이만 여하한 것이냐. 나는 다음의 두 점을 들려고 한다.
一. 깊은 반성을 가질 것.
二. 깊은 책임감을 가질 것.
나의 친애하는 동포가 國民政府의 還都를 경축하고 和平을 옹호하는 날에 있어서 『자기를 罪주라』는 슬로-간을 절규함은 자기를 편달하고 상호 편달하기 위함에서 다른 것이 아니다. 古來로 양국이 항전한 때에는 戰敗國은 賠償과 영토할양의 의무를 지게되며, 그 결과 의기를 상실하고 餘命을 이어가거나 혹은 이를 갈며 抱腕復仇의 뜻을 決하는 것이다. 그러나 지금이야말로 우리들이 질머질 것은 그러한 의무가 아니고 다른 의무인 것이다. 이 의무는 보통의무가 아니다. 즉, 中國과 日本이 공동하여 東亞復興의 책임을 질머질 것을 求하는 것이다.

현재 國民政府는 南京에 還都, 선언을 발표하고 정책을 闡明하여 日本정부의 사절과 및 國民사절은 到來하게 되였다. 이 一斑을 가지고서 和平실현이 반드시 불가능한 것이 아니고, 따라서 화평의 第一步가 이미 현실이 되여 나타난 것이 알려질 것이다. 重慶측의 인민들아, 오라. 그대들은 벌써 화평의 장애가 될 것이 아니다. 그대들의 反對와 懷疑에 의해서 전면적 화평을 지체하게 하지 말라. 화평의 현실은 전국 민중의 노력을 기다린다. 우리들은 國民政府의 還都를 경축하고 화평을 옹호함에 당하여 특히 그대들을 생각하고, 그대들과 함께 노력하여 和平反共建國의 光輝있는 前途를 향하여 勇往邁進하기를 바라는 바이다.＜101＞
