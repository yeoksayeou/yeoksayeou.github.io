Magazine Title: 삼천리
Publication Date: 1940년 07월 01일
Article Title: 孤獨(외 10편) (Solitude (and 10 others))
Author: 파인 (P'ain)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Solitude
						P'ain
Solitude
The poplar tree at old Samgŏri crossroads
Stands alone in the wide-open, empty field,
In its prime, wicked branches held a swing; on Tano and Ch'usŏk holidays,
Village ladies would gather in crowds, chattering and playing happily.
Now, only a single worn straw shoe lies buried in the sunset leaves;
Who is there to remember how good those prime days were?
When youth passes, everything becomes like this.

Fate
Through fields, through forests, through streets<134>
For many days and nights, like one mad, I search and cry out,
Hoping perhaps this heart, with no way to be filled, might become full,
Hoping perhaps this uneven soul might also be set right,
Just as a wave that hasn't found its level
Wanders here and there, searching and crying out until it finds its level,
Before I could meet you, becoming like this – isn't this my Fate?

A Fine Heart
Having drunk the spring water from which valley did you grow,
That your touch, your voice should be so gentle?
Having modeled yourself on the cleanly bloomed flowers of which mound,
That your face, your form should shine so brightly?
Still, the spring water and the flowers are all things of the Earth,
But that fine heart surely has no place to come from if not Heaven.<135>

Receiving Everything Back
If sending a bouquet of flowers to the one I long for could express my affection,
I would quickly gather an armful of flowers and present them.
When others offer a single leaf, a single stem,
I would bring a whole plant, roots and leaves attached, entirely.
The meaning of giving everything like this,
Is to receive everything back in return. That heart, that voice, that youth – all of it.

Your Touch
Pressing two strings, it cries two notes; pressing three strings, it cries three notes;
The *seureureung* kŏmun'go, when you think about it, is just strings and wood,
But because the plucking hand is affectionate, even wood and stone weep.
Though I am an unremarkable man, could I be wood or stone unable to feel affection?<136>
When will that longed-for touch ever brush against me?

Between Us
In a stony gravel field with not a handful of soil,
The rugosa rose that bloomed with effort
Does not wither in ordinary winds,
Only after bearing a cluster of fruit does it finally scatter.
How could it not be so, between us who met through hardship?

Even a Seed
The poplar tree standing tall in the wide-open field,
Even if tied twice or thrice with a bull's halter, it wouldn't flinch.
That poplar, seeming like it would stand for a hundred, then another hundred years,
Dig less than three fathoms underground, and its roots will be lifted.<137>
How can one trust outward appearance? In this heart with nothing to rely on,
Let's trust even a tiny seed, unseen, scattered and blown by the wind.

The Force of Life
Lest darkness come again,
Burning its body with fire,
The candle stands tall!
Knowing it will be extinguished after this evening passes,
It stands tall, surges, clings, and burns – hating the darkness.

The Heart's Hometown
If I were a boatman, I'd steer my boat to a new sea no one has visited.
If I were a cowherd, I'd lead my cow to a mountain valley no one has gone to.<138>
My inherent nature likes new things in everything,
But only you, for my whole life, I wish to keep just as you were, with the old heart, the old appearance.

My Happiness
Even if told to pass through the main street of the capital,
Leading a thousand men before me,
And commanding a thousand women behind me,
I would quietly slip out of that procession
And hide on a side path, as my Happiness does not lie there... ...

Emptiness
Whether Heaven gives me Reward or Punishment,
Whether you Love me or Hate me,
These frustrating days with no response whatsoever,
Are called today... ...

-From the Lyrical Poetry Collection 「Susimga (愁心歌)」- <139>

**Summary**

This collection of eleven short lyrical poems by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan) explores themes of solitude, fate, longing, enduring love born of hardship, the deceptiveness of appearances, the fleeting nature of youth and life, the resilience of the spirit (like a candle burning against darkness), and a preference for simple, personal happiness over public grandeur. The poems often use imagery from nature (trees, flowers, fields, seas) contrasted with inner emotional states. From a historical perspective, the publication in 1940, a period of intense Japanese colonial rule and assimilation pressure, makes the focus on personal emotion, memory, Korean natural imagery (poplar tree, rugosa rose), traditional instruments (kŏmun'go), and a sense of enduring hardship or quiet resistance potentially significant. Historians might find the expression of individual melancholy, longing, and the search for personal meaning and happiness noteworthy within the context of suppressed national identity and cultural expression.

孤獨
						파인
孤獨
舊길 三巨里의 황철나무
텡 빈 너른벌판에 외로히 섰네,
한창철은 저 모진가지에 추친 매고 단오, 가위마다
마을각씨 한상뒤나 몰려와 재질거리며 잘도 놀더러니
이제는 외 짝재기 갓신이 夕陽나무입헤 무처갈뿐
한창철 조왓슴을 누가잇서 긔역하리.
젊음이 가는날 모도다 이리 될 것을,

宿命
들이고, 숲이고, 거리로＜134＞
여러날 여러밤을 미칠듯, 찾아 외침은
채울길 바이없는 이가슴 행여 그득하여지올가고,
고루지못한 이영혼 또한 바로잡혀 지올가고,
마치 水平 못얻은 물결이
水平 어들철까지 이리저리 헤매고 찾고, 부르짖듯
그대 뵈옵기전 이리됨이 이몸의 宿命 아니올가.

고흔마음
어느골재기 샘물을 마시고 자라섯길래
그 손길 그 음성이 이리 부드러우신고,
어느 두던 깨긋히 핀 꼿 본바드섯길래
그얼골, 그 모습이 저려틋 윤채나는고,
그래도 샘물도 꼿도 모다 地上의 것이언만
그 고흔마음씨야 분명 하늘 아니면 올곳이 업스리.＜135＞

모도다 밧고서
그리운이에 꼿다발보내어 정이 표해진다면
내 얼는 꼿 한아름 안어다 드리우리다,
남들이 한입사귀 한떨기 바치올적에
나는 뿌리채, 입 달닌채 통채로 한그루 갓다드리오리다.
이러케 모도 드리는 뜻은,
도로모도 밧기위해서. 그 마음씨, 그 음성 그 젊음을 모도다-.

그대손길
두줄 눌너 두가락 울고, 세줄눌너 세가락 우는
스르룽 거문고도 알고보면 실이요 나무뿐인데
뜻는 손길 情다우니 木石조차 울어주데.
내 변변치 못한 사내이나, 情 못늣길 木石일이야 잇는고.＜136＞
그리운 손길은 어느제나 부드처 주려는고.

우리사이매
흙 한줌 업는 짜작 돌밧헤
힘 들에 핀 해당화는
예삿 바름엔 지드를 안타가
한타래의 열매 맷고사 겨우 흐터를지데
엇지 아니 그럴라디야, 힘들에맛난 우리사이매

씨갓이나
휘덩그레 너른벌판 웃둑선 황철나무
황소곱비 두세벌 곱처매어도 움찔 안하리,
그렇게 百年 또 百年 설것같은 황철나무도
땅믿 세길 채 못파서 뿌리 들릴걸.＜137＞
겉 모양을 어찌믿으리, 지접할 것 업는 이심사엔
뵈지 않드래도 바람에 날려흐터지는 조고만 씨갓이나 믿자구나.

生命의 힘
어둠이 또다시 올가봐
몸을 불로 태워
도두고 선 촛불(燭火)!
이 저녁이 지나면 꺼질줄 알면서
도두고 솟구며 작고 붙어오르네, 어둠이 싫여서.

마음의 故鄕
내 사공이라면 아무도 안가본 새 바다로 내 배를 몰겠고
내 소치는 아이라면 아무도 안가본 산골로 내 소를 이끌리라.＜138＞
내 본대 성미 모든것에 새것을 좋와하건만
그대만은 일생두고 예대로, 옛맘, 옛양자대로 꼭 지키고저.

내 幸福
千名 男性을 앞에 세우고
千名 女性을 뒤에 거느리고
장안 큰거리로 지나라해도
나는 그 행렬로 가만히 빠저서
오솔길에 숨으리, 내 幸福이 그런데 있지않으매... ...

空寞
하늘은 나를 賞주든지 罰주든지
그대는 나를 사랑하든지 미워하든지
아모란 응긔업는 이 답답한 날을
오늘이라 하데... ...
