From the Old City of Dresden, Recent Correspondence from Mr. Pak Sŏgyun

From war-torn Europe (戰亂의 歐洲), I pray once again for my elder brother's (吾兄) health. Your younger brother (弟), since then (其後), has greatly (만히) harmed his health and is now indebted to a sanatorium in the old city of Dresden. Indeed, to whom should I direct this lament? It is enough to make one laugh hollowly. I intend to return to Korea around August. I only pray that Heaven's blessing (天祐) will be abundant for the future of East Asia (東亞).

How are the Polish Beauties (波蘭 美人)? (Because this picture postcard (繪葉書) was a photo of a Polish Beauty). This is the place that gave birth to (나흔) "Walewska," who captured Napoleon (奈巴崙) alive (生).

From the old city of Dresden, Your younger brother Pak Sŏgyun (朴錫胤 弟)
To Kim Tonghwan Hyŏng (金東煥 兄) <95>

**Summary**

This short article is a personal letter from Pak Sŏgyun in Dresden, Germany, to Kim Tonghwan in Korea, dated July 1940. Pak reports being ill and staying in a sanatorium in Dresden ("the old city"). He expresses a desire to return to Korea in August and offers prayers for East Asia's future amidst the war in Europe ("war-torn Europe"). He also makes a lighthearted comment about a postcard featuring a Polish beauty, referencing Maria Walewska and Napoleon. For historians, the letter provides a glimpse into the life of a Korean intellectual abroad during World War II, revealing his health struggles, connection to home, awareness of European events (war, historical figures like Napoleon/Walewska), and concerns for East Asia under Japanese colonial influence.
