**To the Volunteer Soldiers, A Letter Encouraging All Korean Youths Upon Hearing the News of Surpassing 100,000 Applicants**

**The Volunteer Soldier and the Person of Blood, Sweat, and Love**
By Yi Chong-man, President of Taedong Mining Co., Ltd.

It is truly a matter for endless congratulations that the youth of Korea are now able to participate, unified with the sons of the Yamato race, in the sacred task of constructing the new order in Greater East Asia.
Asia must be built by the hands of Asians. Truly <65> thinking upon it, I keenly feel that now is the time when all races—our Japan, Manchuria, and China—must cast aside ethnic prejudice, join hands together, and work for the peace of the Orient.
Therefore, how can we restrain our feelings of gratitude at the current sight of 100,000 Korean youths, a truly large number, sincerely desiring to become volunteer soldiers out of their fervent love for the nation and their understanding of the current situation, wishing to dedicate their bodies to the cause?
To become such warriors, I urge you, the youth of Korea, to first become people of Blood, Sweat, and Love. Blood and Sweat signify production-first-ism. In other words, it means working. Just as the Bible says, "He who does not work, neither shall he eat!", the New East Asia seeks working warriors.
Next, Love means uniting and becoming one under the banner of co-existence and co-prosperity economically, and mutual aid socially, casting aside all prejudices between each other. Whether one is an Inner Land person (Naichi-jin), a Korean, a Manchurian, or a Chinese—no, all peoples of Asia must become people of Love and spur the advance towards the completion of Asian unity, so that the construction of the New Asian peace may swiftly be realized on this earth. This is what I sincerely hope for from you all.

**Grasp the Yamato Spirit!**
By Kim Tae-u, Director of Internal Affairs, South Chŏlla Province

[Prefatory Letter in Japanese]
Respectfully Submitted,
I hope this letter finds you well in the current season.
Regarding the matter at hand, I understand that your esteemed company is planning to publish a special issue of Samch'ŏlli to commemorate the volunteer soldier applicants surpassing one hundred thousand. I believe this is a truly splendid undertaking. Therefore, in accordance with your request, I am sending the attached manuscript. I ask that you handle it appropriately.
June 19th, Shōwa 15 (1940)
Kim Tae-u
Director of Internal Affairs, South Chŏlla Province
To Mr. Kim Tong-hwan
President, Samch'ŏlli Company

[Main Text in Japanese]
Since the outbreak of the present Incident, the crimson <66> sincerity of patriotism overflowing throughout the upper and lower strata of our Peninsula has, through its outpouring, ultimately moved heaven and earth, fostering the momentum for the establishment of the volunteer soldier system. Bathed in the vast and boundless Imperial benevolence of "impartiality and universal benevolence" (isshi dōjin 一視同仁), this system finally came into being in April of Shōwa 13 (1938), amidst the deep emotion of the entire Peninsula, both official and private sectors.
Since then, the recognition of this system among the 23 million populace of the Peninsula has gradually deepened. Especially, the spirit among the youth strata shows truly soaring vigor. The number of applicants desiring to enter the volunteer soldier training centers has surged dramatically year by year. That this year saw such a prosperous situation with applicants actually surpassing one hundred thousand is truly a matter for shared celebration, and it strengthens the prestige of the nation.
In our South Chŏlla Province as well, since the implementation of this system, we have been exerting efforts to encourage increased applications as one of the most important campaign points for the National Spiritual Mobilization. This year, we have secured a superior position in all of Korea by producing over ten thousand applicants and nearly five thousand qualified candidates. Moreover, among the volunteer soldiers hailing from this province, we have already produced glorious war dead like Mr. Yi Hyŏng-su (李亨洙), making the blood of the youth boil. Last August, he scattered like a flower on the fields of Shanxi while establishing brilliant military merit. Along with the late Private First Class Yi In-sŏk (李仁錫) from North Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, he is now enshrined as a guardian god of the nation at Yasukuni Shrine. I do not doubt that these noble spirits teach the future direction of the Peninsula, serve as living models of Japan-Korea unity (naisen ittai 內鮮一體), and will surely spur the rise of the youth strata on the Peninsula henceforth.
Here is what I hope for from all our youths:
One, never forget that among your predecessors, there have emerged brave warriors who firmly grasped the pure blood of the Japanese people and the sublime Yamato spirit (大和魂) and performed noble deeds. Also, strongly bear in mind the great responsibility you have to harvest, with your own hands, the results of the many noble sacrifices of the Imperial Army, to the greatest and highest extent.
One, consider that the Empire is now amidst a great global transformation, boldly yet calmly advancing the construction of a New East Asia, and holding a leading position in the world. Remember that the grave responsibility of achieving the far-reaching mission of the Imperial Nation, having been born as subjects of the Imperial Nation (皇國の民) and nurtured within limitless benevolence, rests upon your shoulders, you who bear the future.
One, think that the fulfillment of this responsibility is precisely your duty to the Nation, to your ancestors, and to your parents. You must not neglect cultivation and training, but must discipline yourselves through mutual improvement (砌磋琢磨) to possess both a sound mind and body, storing up the ability to perform great deeds as pillars (御栒) of His Majesty. And what is most crucial is to shed the old husk of formalism, into which it has often been easy to fall in the past, and take a further step forward to manifest the Imperial spirit (皇國精神) passed down together for 2,600 years, returning to a pure spirit, vigor, and samurai ethos (士風) flowing truly from the heart, thereby constructing our new world.
Although brief, I have stated my thoughts above and hope for the vigorous rise of all the youth of the Peninsula. <67>

**The National Total War and Your Duty**
By Ha Chun-sŏk, President of Korean Industrial Management Co., Ltd.

Today, as the celebration of the 2,600th year of the Kōki era (皇紀) of the Empire, whose Imperial line stretching unbroken crowns the world, draws near, the direction of the Holy War (聖師) already faces no enemy. Seeing the Chiang regime's death throes, the establishment of the new [Wang Jingwei] regime, and the securing of the New Order in East Asia being achieved through the unparalleled loyalty and bravery of the Imperial Army and the discipline of the people on the home front (銃後國民), with the Imperial majesty shining ever more brightly across the eight corners of the universe (八宏), one cannot help but offer a silent prayer of sincerity for the many heroic souls who have become the soil of the continent. At this time, the third year's results of the volunteer soldier system, long desired by the entire populace of the Peninsula, show truly 100,000 applicants, with the quota jumping to 3,000. That it has become possible for even more sons of the Peninsula to courageously strive and be active on the front lines of continental national defense is indeed an extremely prominent achievement in the materialization of Japan-Korea unity (內鮮一體), and truly a matter for endless celebration for the sake of the Empire and the Peninsula.
Now, the flames of war in Europe, with Italy's impending entry, seem poised to initiate a hundred-year world war. The national total war (國家總力戰), where the entire world strives under the banner of justice, both at the front and on the home front, to carry out the national policy, has truly spread from the East to the whole world. And you, volunteer soldiers, are taking the first step towards becoming warriors of the Imperial Army who vow loyalty unto death (殉忠之誠). At the same time, you entrust yourselves to military families that transcend life and death, dedicating yourselves to the dōjō (道場) of national physical and mental training. For young Imperial subjects (皇民) born on the Peninsula, there can be no greater fulfillment of their true desire.
It is said, "A sound mind dwells in a strong body." The Japanese spirit being cultivated in the youth of the Peninsula is truly perfected through these volunteer soldiers who advance to become members of the Imperial Army, warriors at the front. By serving in the military, they become loyal and brave soldiers, and upon returning to their hometowns, they become good subjects, thus becoming the pillars of their local communities (鄕黨).
I hope that you, volunteer soldiers, will cast aside self-interest (私) and proceed towards righteousness (義), respond to the encouragement of your hometown elders and brothers, practice self-restraint and self-discipline (自肅自戒), cultivate the military spirit, not betray the expectations of His Excellency the Governor-General (總督閣下) in implementing this system, and furthermore, fulfill your utmost sincerity and loyalty (盡忠之誠) in carrying out the Holy War (聖戰). My hopes are boundless.

**Absolutely Different from Foreign Native Troops: Our Country's Volunteer Soldier System**
By Amado Michio (玄永燮 Hyŏn Yŏng-sŏp), Secretary of the National Spiritual Mobilization Korea Alliance

1. According to newspapers, among the soldiers captured as prisoners in the British and French armies, there are many native soldiers (土人兵) from various colonies. Even in the colonies of various countries, which are nothing more than products of capitalism and imperialism, there are native soldiers who go forth to fight for the home country, namely Indian soldiers (Muslims), Annamese soldiers, and Black soldiers from places like Tunis, Algeria, and Morocco. Although there are still people among both Inner Landers and Koreans who view Korea as a colony, it is a solemn fact that no one can deny that a new concept—that Korea is not a colony of Japan—has arisen and is gradually becoming common sense among the 100 million compatriots.
Currently, Korea is treated as an external territory (外地), but it will not remain an external territory forever. We anticipate that at some point, it will attain a status equal to the Inner Land (內地). On the day the volunteer soldier system develops into a conscription system, elementary schools become compulsory education, and suffrage expands (though people often think of universal suffrage only in terms of the Diet representative system, Koreans becoming Prefectural Governors (知事), Councilors (叅議) of the Central Advisory Council (中樞院), or Councilors (協議員) of Provincial or Municipal councils is also suffrage (叅政) in a broad sense), there will be absolutely no distinction between Inner Land and External Territory, other than geographical differences. If we imagine Inner Landers and Koreans going to live in Manchuria, then Korea, the Inner Land, and Hokkaido would inevitably be collectively called the Inner Land. For that reason, I think of Korea becoming the second Inner Land, and I believe that only when Korea becomes the second Inner Land will the Inner Land and Korea merge and harmonize (合體融和) to build a New Japan as one indivisible entity (渾然一體).
If our Korea is something different from India or Algeria, <69> that is, if the proposition that Korea is not a colony of Japan is proven ideologically, realistically, scientifically, and artistically, then we must think that we have a duty to naturally apply for the volunteer soldier system, which is appropriate for the provisional status of being an External Territory.
It is because it is an External Territory that the volunteer soldier system exists; if it were the Inner Land, it would naturally be a mandatory military service system. We, affirming that Japan-Korea unity (內鮮一體), the supreme goal of Korean governance, is the highest ideal of our people, are voluntarily mobilizing and participating fully in the Japan-Korea unity movement. By fulfilling this sacred mission of becoming complete subjects of Japan, Koreans are fulfilling a momentous mission for achieving human peace, more splendid and glorious than the Belgians, Dutch, Czechs, Poles, or Irish. From today onwards, as we strive for Japan-Korea unity, we will mentally express a role similar to the art of Silla. That is, by daring to perform courageous actions that no small nation could, we create the historical character of the Korean people.
Japan-Korea unity is absolutely not a path for Koreans to become arrogant (增上慢), but neither is it a servile action. By all Koreans, without exception, becoming part of the Japanese nation (日本民族化), we become equals. For that reason, if we assume we possess things like a warped disposition (ひがみ根性) or a slave mentality (奴隷根性), we need to thoroughly eradicate them.

2. It is an undeniable fact that in the past, Koreans were more effeminate/fond of literary pursuits (文弱) than Inner Landers. If we are now to display fervent loyalty and patriotism (忠君愛國) on equal footing with Inner Landers, we must liquidate the cultural poisoning (文化中毒) of the past and cultivate the Bushido spirit (武士道精神). If 70,000 Inner Landers died for the country, then 30,000 of us must also die. If they have heroic deeds that would make ghosts weep, then we too must establish military merits (武勳) that will last for ten thousand generations. If not, I declare that Japan-Korea unity, that is, the Governor-General Nam's (南總督) "Japan-Korea non-discriminatory equality" (內鮮無差別平等), will absolutely never be realized. With what face can we expect equal treatment if we lack the same fervent patriotism (熱熱な愛國心) as Inner Landers?
If one understands the fundamental principle of Japan-Korea unity, one will understand that Korea's volunteer soldier system is absolutely different from native troops (土人部隊). The latter is a system of employment; the former stems from the essence of the national polity (國體の精華). <70>
Since the volunteer soldier system uses the National Language (國語 - Japanese) as its language, incorporates soldiers into a part of the Imperial Army, and centers on the customs and practices of the Inner Land, which constitute the majority of Japanese customs, we, the youth who must dutifully apply for the volunteer soldier system, should pay attention to acquiring the National Language and cultivating Japanese tastes.
Furthermore, the fathers, mothers, older brothers, and sisters of the young sons should awaken to the sacred world-historical mission of Japan-Korea unity and Korea's role, and campaign so that the 100,000 applicants turn into several hundreds of thousands. Even something like the name-changing policy (創氏 - sōshi) might not be felt as necessary by some in Korean society, but I believe name-changing is necessary as long as we interact and live with Inner Landers. Moreover, for volunteer soldiers, name-changing has become a realistic, urgent matter (急務). Since the 100,000 volunteer applicants will become several hundreds of thousands in the future, we should rapidly implement name-changing even in consideration of that time.
That there are few volunteer soldiers among middle school and professional/college graduates might be an overstatement if attributed to the lack of awareness (無自覺) of the Peninsula's intellectual class, but this is, in any case, our shame. The intellectual class needs to reflect more deeply and thoroughly on the fate of the world's small nations and realize how sacred and pure the path of Japan-Korea unity is for the people of the Peninsula.
We, regardless of whether we are young, middle-aged, or old, must follow the example of the late Private First Class Yi In-sŏk's (故李仁錫上兵等) last instructions. I consider the late Mr. Yi, younger than myself, to be my great senior and my daily teacher.

**Let's Inspire the Martial Spirit!**
By Han Yang-ho, Founder of Kyŏngsŏng Women's Commercial School

It is a matter of utmost gratitude and congratulation that only three years after the implementation of the volunteer soldier system, the number of applicants has surpassed 100,000.
That the martial spirit (尙武の精神) has risen so high among Koreans, who were wandering in effeminacy (文弱), stems from the sound education received since the beginning of the Governor-General's administration and a thorough recognition of the dawn of the construction of the New East Asian order. Therefore, I hope that the youth of Korea will possess an even stronger and more vigorous spirit in the future.

**Just Raising Both Hands in Praise**
By Nam Paek-u, Councilor of the Central Advisory Council

Respectfully Submitted, I offer congratulations on the increasing prosperity of your esteemed company. **Martial Spirit and Patriotism**
By Kim Chung-gŭn (Created Name: 和義重根 Kazuyoshi Shigenori?), Member of the South Hamgyŏng Provincial Council

In a constitutional state, the rights and duties of the people are clearly defined by the constitution.
Generally, the State fully recognizes the rights of its citizens and guarantees their peace and freedom. In return, it is natural for the citizens to fulfill their duties, and those who are citizens must gladly fulfill these duties.
We, the subjects of Japan (日本臣民), have three major duties as prescribed by law: the duty of military service (兵役の義務), the duty of tax payment (納稅の義務), and the duty to receive the education mandated by the state. <72>
Military service, in particular, is the fulfillment of the duty as a citizen to protect one's country, so one must serve in it with honor. Therefore, if a citizen receives the guarantee of peace and freedom but neglects their duties, they cannot be called a complete citizen.
In this sense, we Peninsula people (半島人) could be said to lack the qualifications of complete Imperial subjects.
However, among us Peninsula people too, the emotion of being Japanese citizens, which exploded following the Manchurian Incident and the China Incident, combined with Governor-General Minami's (南總督閣下) policy of Japan-Korea unity (內鮮一體), finally led to the implementation of the Army Special Volunteer Soldier System (陸軍特別志願兵制) and the Revised Education Ordinance (改正敎育令), as prerequisites to the previously lacking duties of military service and compulsory education.
Therefore, to see the volunteer soldier system achieve unexpectedly great results in its second year of implementation, with applicants surpassing 100,000, is truly beyond 감격 (deep emotion).
Thus, I believe and do not doubt that the timing for the implementation of our greatest concerns—compulsory military service, compulsory education, and suffrage (叅政權)—will be hastened in proportion to the progress of the results of this volunteer soldier system. Truly, the participation of volunteer soldiers in military service not only fulfills your duty to the state but is also a sacred task (聖業) that makes us Peninsula people complete Imperial subjects. At the same time, from the perspective of self-cultivation, it promotes health and fosters discipline and communal living, thereby contributing to one's own progress and development.
Finally, what I want to say to our fellow countrymen on the Peninsula home front (半島銃後同胞) is that every citizen (國民, 公民) must possess patriotism (愛國心) and serve the state and society. No, having patriotism is not so much a duty of a citizen as it is a natural feeling.
Just as we love ourselves and our families, we possess the feeling of loving our hometown and our country. This feeling is stimulated even more strongly in situations like today, where the international situation is tense and every action of our citizens directly impacts related countries.
However, our need to fulfill the duty of national defense, namely military service, as citizens is, of course, not merely based on abstract emotional patriotism.
I still remember the words spoken by the late Dr. Takata Sanae (高田早苗), renowned as the president of Waseda University, in an address to students during the tragic Great Kantō Earthquake of Taishō 12 (1923).
He said that although the Great Kantō Earthquake caused the loss of 200,000 lives and 5 billion yen worth of property, making the disaster beyond words, compared to suffering defeat in a war against a foreign country and facing the invasion of enemy forces, that disaster was still relatively minor. This serves as a great warning to us regarding national defense <73>.
Therefore, we must deeply recognize that in times like the present, when military power completely dominates international relations, the maintenance of the people's livelihood can only be expected through its sufficiency.
Furthermore, our individual lives last merely 60 or 70 years, but the life of the nation is eternal. Therefore, our efforts dedicated to the development of the nation will reside within the life of the nation and live on forever.
From this perspective as well, we must deepen our understanding of military service, which is most crucial for enhancing national development and the sentiment of patriotism.

As someone who has long hoped for the Military Service Law to be implemented on the Peninsula, hearing that the number of applicants for the Army Special Volunteer Soldier program this year has reached over 100,000 makes me feel the necessity of its implementation even more strongly. Volunteer soldiers, without needing many words, must deeply realize the grave responsibility of a soldier and exert their utmost sincerity in courageous service and public duty (義勇奉公). <75>

**Young Warriors of Greater East Asian Construction, Today and Tomorrow are Yours!**
By Yi Sŏng-hwan, Executive Director of Taedong Mining Company

Considering the current various situations, I believe tomorrow's battle will not be a struggle where Orientals fight each other based on ethnic concepts, but a battle between the East and the West. In other words, it will be a war between the Yellow Race and the White Race. If that is the case, we as Orientals must discard narrow-minded ethnic struggles like Japan, Korea, Manchuria, or China, and the entire Orient must become a single entity to face the defense of Greater East Asia. It is not the defense of just Japan, nor the defense of just China, but the defense of the entire Orient for the sake of Oriental peace.
That Koreans cannot participate, strive, and endeavor in this defense of Greater East Asia is, I believe, a question of whether Koreans are dead or alive. In other words, the inability of Koreans to participate in the defense of Greater East Asia means that Koreans are dead.
However, the emergence of volunteer soldiers from among Koreans for the defense of East Asia is truly a matter for congratulation.
If a conscription order were issued to Korean youth, I believe they would provide 1 million soldiers. But the currently implemented volunteer soldier system is not conscription; it is literally a volunteer system. That the number of volunteers, who applied willingly because they wanted to, surpassed nearly 100,000 (86,000 in the second recruitment drive) is truly something to be recorded in large letters (大書特記). The state must nurture and promote this momentum, ultimately leading to conscription.
Looking back at the history Korea has walked for the past several decades, it is diverse and colorful. Because of this, within current Korean society, there are politicians from the late Korean Empire (韓末) era, figures who engaged in nationalist movements <76> thereafter, and also figures who engaged in socialist and communist movements after that. Not only Koreans, but also Inner Landers who have lived in Korea for decades, and Inner Landers in the Inner Land, cannot deny that in the past, they thought of Korea as a colony, and undesirable phenomena existed economically or spiritually. However, now, under the Imperial will of "impartiality and universal benevolence" (一視同仁の聖旨), it is truly joyous that both sides are discarding all these undesirable concepts. Yet, for those steeped in such thoughts, I suspect that at least a small trace of that ingrained nature remains somewhere in the back of their minds. Therefore, I believe true Japan-Korea unity lies more with the youth of both Inner Land and Korea, who possess purer spirits than the people of the past.
Therefore, bury the past as the past; I want to cry out that Today and Tomorrow belong to the youth! Therefore, you youths must shout, "People of the past, step aside! Today and tomorrow are ours!" and bravely advance in the construction of the new order in Greater East Asia. I sincerely hope for redoubled efforts for the sake of Oriental peace.

**Urging Reflection from Upper and Middle-Class Families**
By Kim Ch'ŏl-chin, Member of the South Chŏlla Provincial Council

The volunteer soldier system is a system that can be seen as having brought about a major, revolutionary, and significant relationship for the Korean populace. How could one, born in this sacred era (聖代), not be deeply moved? However, one must not forget that the essential significance of this system lies solely in its role as a precursor to the conscription system, and that the conscription system is impossible without the implementation of compulsory education – this causal relationship must not be forgotten.
That volunteer soldier applicants surpassed 100,000 truly speaks volumes about the improvement of Koreans' national consciousness and the expression of their loyalty. As the Meiji Emperor (明治天皇) stated in his Imperial Rescript (御製), "If the strength of ten million people is combined, what cannot be achieved in this world?" For Koreans to bear a part of the national defense is, considering the profound meaning (深意) of this Imperial Rescript and also from the perspective of Korea fulfilling its mission as a logistical base (兵站基地), a natural duty and thus an honor for the people. <77>
One regrettable aspect regarding this year's applicant recruitment is the still insufficient understanding of this system among the populace. I myself have experience in recruiting applicants—persuasion (lectures, roundtable discussions, tours, individual home visits, etc.). Based on that experience, firstly, the fact that the number of applicants from upper and middle-class families is not large is extremely shameful. Also, it is the height of regret (遺憾の極) that applicants are sporadically seen applying out of vanity—thinking there might be advantages in employment or better treatment in society—without a thorough awakening as loyal Imperial subjects (皇國臣民). Furthermore, there is some suspicion that the quality of the applicants might tend towards crudeness (粗雜). While I applaud and rejoice with both hands at the surpassing of 100,000, a decline in quality absolutely must not occur. Considering the number of eligible Koreans, I pray that the number reaches 200,000 or 300,000 without a decline in quality.
Also, my hope for the authorities—the military (軍部) or administrative organs (行政機關)—is that they thoroughly and sufficiently publicize this system. For example, filming the daily life at the volunteer soldier training centers, making films and picture-story shows (紙芝居) about the loyal deaths of the first volunteer soldiers, and touring villages far and wide with them would surely have a great effect.

The youth of Korea must fully realize and recognize this system, strive and train themselves to bring about the implementation of compulsory education and conscription systems in the near future. They must not forget that the speed of this depends solely on the youths themselves.
Stagnation is regression, and regression is ruin. Our youth must embrace high ideals step by step, advance, only advance.
With the awakening and consciousness of loyal Imperial subjects, never losing hope, keeping eyes wide open and looking high, I pray and wish wholeheartedly for their advancement. <78>

**Summary**

This collection of opinion pieces from the July 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine celebrates the fact that over 100,000 Koreans applied for the Japanese Army's Special Volunteer Soldier system. Written by prominent Korean figures (businessmen, officials, educators, council members) and one Japanese official (or Korean using a Japanese name), the articles uniformly praise this milestone as proof of Korean loyalty to the Emperor and the Japanese Empire, and a crucial step towards *Naiseon Ilche* (Japan-Korea Unity). The authors exhort Korean youth to embrace the "Yamato Spirit," discard past "weaknesses," shed ethnic particularism for a pan-Asian identity under Japanese leadership, contribute to the "Holy War" for a "New East Asian Order," and see military service as an honorable duty and path to becoming "complete" Imperial subjects. Several authors explicitly link the success of the volunteer system to the future implementation of conscription and compulsory education for Koreans, framing it as progress towards equality within the Empire, while sharply distinguishing Korean volunteers from the "native troops" of Western colonial powers. One author specifically criticizes the lack of participation from upper-class and educated Koreans, urging them to overcome past resentments or perceived injustices and fully commit to the Imperial cause.

For historians, these articles are valuable primary sources illustrating the intense pressure and propaganda surrounding assimilation policies (*Naiseon Ilche*, *Sōshi-kaimei*) and wartime mobilization in colonial Korea. They reveal the rhetoric used by collaborating elites to encourage participation in the Japanese war effort, framing it as an opportunity for Koreans to prove their loyalty, achieve equality, and fulfill a "sacred" historical mission. The emphasis on shedding "effeminate" pasts for a "martial spirit," the explicit linkage of military service to rights like education and suffrage, and the condemnation of perceived reluctance among the educated class offer insights into the complex dynamics of collaboration, coercion, and ideological justification during this period. The comparison with Western colonial troops is also noteworthy, reflecting Japan's attempt to position its empire differently.

지금 大亞細亞의 신질서건설의 聖業에 朝鮮靑小年도 大和族男兒와 일체되어 참가하게 된다는 것은 참으로 경하하여 마지않는 바이다.
亞細亞는 亞細亞人의 손으로 건설해야만 된다. 진실로＜65＞ 생각건대 我日本과 滿洲, 支那 모다 민족적 편견을 떠나서, 여러 종족이 모두 손을 맞잡고 東洋平和를 위하여 일 하여야할 때인줄 절실히 느낀다.
그런데 이 거룩한 사업에 우리 조선 청소년이 진심으로 나라를 사랑하는 정열과 시국을 인식하는 理解밋헤서 10만이란 만흔 數가 일체로 지원병을 희망하야 몸을 우에 바치고서 나아가려 하는 오늘의 모양에 어찌 감사의 念을 금하랴.
이러한 용사가 되기위해선 우리 조선 청소년 제군은 爲先 血과 汗과 愛의 人이 되라. 血과 汗은 생산 제일주의를 의미하는 것이다. 다시 말하면 일하는 것을 의미하는 것이다.『일하지않는 자는 먹지도 말라!』는 성경의 말씀과 같이, 新東亞는 일하는 용사를 찾는 것이다.
다음으로 愛라하는 것은 피차 모든 편견을 떠나서 경제적으로는 共存共榮, 사회적으로는 상부상조의 旗幟밑에서 결합하고 일체가 되는 것을 의미하는 것이다. 內地人이나 朝鮮人이나 滿洲人이나 支那人이나, 아니 全亞細亞 인류가 다 愛의 人이 되여 亞細亞 일체에의 완성에 拍車邁進하여써 新亞細亞 평화건설이 속히 이 지구 우에 이루어지기를 諸君에 희망하여 마지않는 바이다.

만일 조선인 청소년에게 징병령이 내린다면 100만의 병정을 내리라고 봅니다. 그런데 지금 시행되고 있는 지원병은 징병이 아니고 문자 그대로 지원병이외다. 하구 싶어서 자기가 자진해서 지망하는 지원병이 제2기 모집에 8만6천이라는 근 10만을 돌파한 것은 실로 大書特記할 일이오, 이 기세를 국가는 培養하고 助長해서 최후에는 징병에까지 이르러야 할 것이외다.

지원병제도는 조선민중에게는 일대 혁신적 중대한 관계를 주었다고 볼 수 있는 제도이다. 聖代에 生을 受한자로써 어찌 감격지 않으리오. 그러나 이 제„는, 오로지 징병제에 前行한다는 데에 그 본질적 의의가 내재하는 것이요, 또 징병제는 의무교육의 실시가 없이는 불가능하다하는 인과관계가 있음을 잊어서는 아니 된다.
지원병지망자 10만 돌파는 실로 조선인의 국민의식의 향상과 충성의 표현을 여실히 말하는 바이니 明治天皇御製에 「천만의 백성의 힘을 합한다하면 이 세상에 무엇이 아니되겠느냐」라고 말슴하셨거니와, 조선인이 국방의 一端을 부담한다함은 이 御製의 深意로 보아 또한 조선이 兵站基地로서의 사명수행상으로 보아 국민 당연의 의무이요 따라서 영광이다.＜77＞
금년도 지망자모집에 한갓 遺憾으로 생각함은 아직도 이 제도에 대한 민중의 인식이 부족하다는 점이다. 필자자신이 이 지망자모집-勸誘의 경험(강연회, 좌담회, 순회, 개인가정방문 등)이 있으니 그 경험상으로 보아 첫째로 上流, 中流계급 가정에서의 지망자 수가 많치 못하다 함은 대단히 부끄러운 일이요, 또 지망자가 철저한 황국신민의 자각이 없이 漫然히 或을 就職의 편의가 있다든가, 세상 대우가 좋다든가 하는 허영심에서 지망하는 자가 散見됨은 遺憾의 極이다. 또 그 지망자의 질로 보아 조잡한 경향이 있지 않은가 하는 의심이 다소 있으니 나는 10만 돌파를 쌍수로 찬양하고 환희하는 者이나 질의 저하는 절대로 있어서는 아니 된다. 조선인의 適齡者 수로 보아서 그 질의 저하 없이 20만, 30만의 數가 되기를 기원하는 바이다.
또 當局-軍部나 行政機關-에 희망함은 이 제도의 철저한 선전을 충분히 하여 달라는 바이다. 예컨대 지원병훈련소의 일상생활을 영화화하고 지원병 최초의 충성된 戰死를 영화화 紙芝居를 작성하여 村落 방방곡곡 순회하면 그 효과가 대단할 줄 믿는다.

조선의 청소년제군은 이 제도를 충분히 자각 인식하여 가까운 장래에 의무교육제와 징병제의 실시를 초래하도록 각자 노력하고 수련하여야 한다. 그 遲速은 오로지 청소년 제군의 자신에 있음을 잊어서는 아니 된다.
