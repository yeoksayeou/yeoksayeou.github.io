Editorial Postscript

This coming July 7th marks the 3rd anniversary of the Holy War. To commemorate this significant day, this month's issue has been compiled as a special feature issue with articles related to the current situation, and in particular, we have collected and included commemorative writings from various literary figures for the 3rd anniversary of the Holy War.

Marking the 3rd anniversary of the Holy War, we must push forward with the renewed recognition and renewed determination that "The war starts now!" and achieve substantial results as citizens behind the guns, both materially and spiritually. Especially in this war situation, the number of applicants for the volunteer soldiers, our pride, has already surpassed 100,000. To encourage these loyal sons devoted to protecting the country, to spur on their bright future, and to console the mothers and sisters of the volunteer soldiers, we have gathered and published messages of encouragement from high officials of the military and the Government-General authorities, as well as prominent figures from the private sector. We believe this is the most appropriate mission in this current situation, and we are confident that this literary movement will yield great results in serving the nation.

In Europe, it is impossible for us not to be astonished that our ally Germany has captured the capital, Paris, with the force of splitting bamboo. Furthermore, with Italy now also joining the war, checking and frustrating the power of the cunning Britain and France, we sincerely hope that we too will completely drive out the British and French forces from the lands of the Orient, bringing new light to the construction of the New Order in Greater Asia. Accordingly, writings commemorating Italy's entry into the war are also included in this issue.

Publishing Department News

The long-announced "Collection of Great Patriotic Speeches" (愛國大演說集) began sales in mid-May and has received unexpectedly great acclaim. Orders have flooded in not only from government offices and public organizations but also widely from the general public, nearly selling out, and we are preparing to start a second printing. Also, "Peninsula Mountains and Rivers" (半島山河) and "Collection of Modern Korean Poetry and Songs" (現代朝鮮詩歌英\*集) are currently being printed, and we are making every effort to publish them within this month as a gift to the reading world during the three hottest days of summer (三伏). Following these, as previously announced, we plan to continuously publish "Selection of Famous Chinese Poems" (支那名詩選), "Collection of Doctoral Dissertations" (博士論文集), "Ten Great Figures of the Korean Financial World" (朝鮮財界十傑), "Ten Great Educators of Korea" (朝鮮十大敎育家), "Representative Collection of Korean Children's Stories" (朝鮮代表童話集), and other fiction collections, thereby striving to achieve substantial results in serving the nation through writing (文章報國). <250>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the July 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine commemorates the third anniversary of the Sino-Japanese War ("Holy War"). It emphasizes the need for Koreans to redouble their support for the Japanese war effort, materially and spiritually. The piece celebrates the large number of Korean volunteer soldiers for the Japanese army and includes messages encouraging them. It also hails the recent victories of Axis powers Germany (capturing Paris) and Italy (entering the war) and expresses hope for the expulsion of British and French influence from East Asia to establish a "New Order in Greater Asia." The postscript concludes with updates on the magazine's publishing activities, listing several books aimed at promoting patriotism and Japanese wartime goals. For historians, this text is a stark example of pro-Japanese propaganda disseminated through Korean media during the colonial period. It vividly illustrates the mobilization of Korean society for the war, the co-option of intellectuals ("literary figures," "prominent figures"), the promotion of the "volunteer" soldier system (often coercive in reality), and the explicit alignment with Axis ideology, framing the war in Asia as part of a global conflict against Allied powers.

編輯後記
今 7월 7일로써 聖戰 第3周年을 마지하게 되였나이다. 이 뜻깊은 날을 기념하기 위하여 今月 號를 時局記事 特輯號로 엮었사오며 특히 文士諸氏로부터는 聖戰 第3周年 記念文을 蒐集하여 실렸나이다.

聖戰 第3周年을 미지함에 際하여 우리는『전쟁은 이제부터다!』하는 재인식과 再覺悟로 邁進하여써 物心 兩面으로 銃後 國民의 實을 거두어야 하겠나이다. 특히 이 戰局에 있어서 우리의 자랑인 志願兵 志望者는 벌써 10萬을 돌파하였으며, 이 護國 忠烈의 男兒들을 격려하고, 그 빛나는 前途에 박차를 加하기 위하여, 그리고 志願兵 母妹를 위로하기 위하여 軍部와 總督府 당국의 高官으로부터 民間 有志에 이르기까지 이에 관한 격려문을 모아서 게재한 것은 이 時局에 있어서 가장 적절한 사명인줄 아오며, 이 文書運動으로 인하여 나라에 報하는 큰 成果가 있을 것을 자신하나이다.

歐洲에서는 我盟邦 獨逸이 破竹之勢로 수도 巴里를 陷落한 것은 우리로서도 놀나지 않을 수 없으며, 이에 다시 伊太利까지 參戰하여, 老獪한 英佛의 세력을 牽制, 挫折케 됨과 동시에 我等도 東洋 天地에서 英佛세력을 완전히 몰아내여 大亞細亞新秩序建設에 새로운 광명이 있기를 빌어 마지않는 바이외다. 이에, 伊太利 參戰을 기념하는 글도 本誌에 함께 실리나이다.

出版部通信
오랫동안 豫告하였던 『愛國大演說集』은 지난 5월 중순부터 發賣를 개시하였는데 예상 이상의 大好評을 博하여 官公 단체는 물론, 널리 一般으로부터 주문이 殺到하여 거의 賣盡되여가오며, 다시 再版에 着手하려 하나이다. 그리고 『半島山河』와 『現代朝鮮詩歌英*集』은 현재 인쇄 중이며, 今月 內로 上梓하여 三伏의 讀書界에 膳物코저 가진 努力을 다하고 있나이다. 뒤를 이어 이미 豫告하였던 「支那名詩選」, 「博士論文集」, 「朝鮮財界十傑」, 「朝鮮十大敎育家」, 「朝鮮代表童話集」, 기타 소설집 등을 續續 발간하여 文章報國의 實을 꾀하는 바이외다. ＜250＞
