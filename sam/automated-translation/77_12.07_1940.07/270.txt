A Collection of Prefaces by Various Authors
						Ch'uho Chŏn Yŏngt'aek et al.

On Yi Kwangsu's "Essays and Poems"
Ch'uho Chŏn Yŏngt'aek

I went to visit you, my elder brother, at your detached house in Hongji-chŏng where the azaleas were in full bloom below Pukhan Mountain, intending to unburden my heart, weary from the dusty streets, and talk to my heart's content. But before I knew it, the flowers had all fallen, and the season of green shade had arrived; now, crossing the pass at Ch'angŭi Gate makes one sweat!

When I briefly visited with the little ones then, I saw you planting trees barefoot in the yard. Regardless of the practicalities, I partly envied your life and partly rejoiced in your health.
To my words, "You have good fortune! But with bare trees all around, why plant them right in the yard?"
You replied, "Young friends keep bringing them, asking me to plant them. Here, come look over there."
And you pointed towards the spot visible from the warmest part of the inner room where many flowers bloomed, boasting like a child. I remember that childlike aspect of yours. <192> You definitely have a childlike side, brother. I feel strangely grateful to this mundane world for allowing such an innocent, childlike brother like you to live even this much.

I believe that your vow, pledged over 30 years ago while wandering through Siberia (西伯利亞), for "serving the country through literature (文學報國)," has now been substantially fulfilled. Although the fruits of cultural efforts and spiritual service do not quickly become apparent, and although shortcomings are often more visible than achievements, nonetheless, setting aside the establishment of the foundation for language and writing (語文) in this land, your steadfast provision of moral nourishment (道德的 糧食) to the youth, to those who can read widely—telling them to "love one another," "live for others," "live righteously"—is a great deed that others cannot emulate.

It is shameful that I myself, who vowed to guide lost sheep onto the path of salvation, have yet to enter the realm of "holiness (聖潔)" and still dwell amidst worldly afflictions (煩惱), often pointlessly blaming the world. Isn't it frustrating that I have accomplished so little? Of course, it might be alright to just set the goal straight and die while heading towards it. We have no choice but to proceed knowing that the suffering you endure is a human burden and God's whip, and to live thinking of my own suffering in the same way. Work for truth has always faced much persecution (逼迫) and obstacles (障害), and it is not something easily achieved. I know that even a single line of writing, a single word spoken for truth, if it is for truth, will fall like a seed and have its time to sprout (삯). Making it sprout, grow, and bear fruit is not our work, but the work of God (神).

As the white hairs on my head increase, impatient and sorrowful thoughts arise, but on the other hand, I feel I am coming to understand Heaven's way and the human heart (天道人心). In any case, please take good care of your health. Let this letter serve as the preface to your "Essays and Poems."

On Sim Hun's "Evergreen Tree"
Pyŏkch'o Hong Myŏnghŭi

Sim Hun is a friend like a younger brother to me, so my eyes are accustomed to looking down on him slightly, and my demanding heart anticipates much from him. Therefore, I have never once praised his works satisfactorily. When others praise him, I feel as happy as if I were receiving the praise myself, yet I do not wish to praise him; this is because the affection (情分) between us is <193> distinct from others (與他自別). When Sim Hun previously published "Eternal Smile," he asked me for a preface. I thought to myself that I would be a groom who whips an already running horse rather than a peddler who sets up shop on mere lip service, and began to take up my brush. However, at that time, my mind was disturbed (心神이 紛擾하야) due to family matters (家間事), and the brush did not flow well. Thus, I inevitably wrote hastily (草草히). After the book was published, I looked at it again, and the so-called preface was not even proper writing from the start, nor did it reveal any of our affection, making it extremely unpleasant. Therefore, having failed with the preface for "Eternal Smile," I resolved to compensate for it with the preface for this "Evergreen Tree." I picked up and put down the brush several times, mulling over my thoughts.

Others might shower it with clichéd praises (恒用語套) like "the high peak (高峯) of the full-length novel" or "the most outstanding (白眉) of rural novels," but our affection does not permit (許諾) this. While pondering this and that,
It suddenly occurred to me that when "Evergreen Tree" was published in the newspaper, the author's note said:
"I (小生) will grow as a literary youth (文學靑年)."
Although Sim Hun is by no means a minor figure among the established writers of the Chosŏn (朝鮮) literary world today, he refers to himself as a "literary youth." Is this humility that avoids arrogance (自大), or pride in not aging prematurely (早老)?
No, it is not! It is a declaration of his self-expectation for progress and great achievement (長進大成) and a public pledge (公約) of his strenuous efforts (發奮努力). Even looking only at the fact that among Sim Hun's diverse works, the novel "The Weaver Star (織女星)" showed progress (進境) compared to the earlier "Eternal Smile," and this "Evergreen Tree" shows remarkable progress compared to the previous "The Weaver Star," one can sufficiently see the traces of his effort towards great achievement. However, what new progress will there be in the future, causing us to look at him with new eyes (刮目相對)? I am curious and anxious. In my anxious curiosity, I pondered deeply on my own. The rural problem (농촌문제) is the biggest issue in Chosŏn, both now and in the future. Since Sim Hun has already embarked on this, if he depicts peasant life not as some makeshift (苟且) ideal but strictly according to reality, digs deep into it instead of skimming the surface, and expresses as realistically as possible the phenomena of peasants collapsing and stumbling under the weight of contradictions (矛盾) they cannot resolve themselves (스사로), then that work is destined to become a precious legacy (遺業) of the Chosŏn literary world, transmitted far into the future.
Following the spirit of mutual encouragement (勉勵) of the ancients (古人), I write these thoughts for Sim Hun, hoping they might be of some help (有助), even if they are ill-formed. Yet, I cannot help but feel ashamed of merely having the seniority of one day (一日之長).
Autumn, 7th Month, Pyŏngja year <194>

On Pak Yŏnghŭi's "Hwoewŏl's Poetry Collection"
Wŏlt'an Pak Chonghwa

The beautiful songs of my old friend Hwoewŏl (懷月) have finally become a single volume (單行本) to appear before fellow enthusiasts (同好者) in the world. Thinking back, time flies. It has already been 20 years since Hwoewŏl, then a handsome youth with a rosy face and black hair (紅顔黑髮), immersed himself in the realm of poetic trance, murmuring these songs. He fondly wore a tall, black Mexico hat (墨西哥 모자) and whistled his way through the broad daylight (白晝) of the capital (長安 - Seoul), singing these songs. Looking back, Hwoewŏl and I, I and Hwoewŏl, were then two naked young bodies burning with passion (情熱). How those two young souls embraced each other, relying on each other through long, long nights!
Today, after passing through 20 years of unusual wind and rain, the way of poetry (詩道) has become rough, and elegant song (雅唱) has long grown rusty. Quiet breath (呼吸), serene contemplation (靜謚한 思索)—I wish to call back the old soul (魂) and cry 'Let's go home!' (歸去來兮) to the land of beautiful songs.
Just in time, someone wished for its publication (上梓), and Hwoewŏl himself handpicked only the gems (珠玉). The beautiful musical rhythm (韻律), the exquisitely beautiful (玲瓏華麗) poetic thought (詩思)—reading it again and again, I am reluctant to close the pages, as if caressing a precious jewel (寶玉). Why? Because it is art imbued with Hwoewŏl's entire spirit (渾身의 넋). Good grape wine (葡萄술) grows more fragrant as it ages (묵을쑤록). This small collection of poems will surely raise the price of paper in the capital (長安의 紙價).
Early Winter, Pyŏngja year, at Chosu Pavilion

On Pak T'aewŏn's "Novelist Mr. Kubo's Day"
Yi T'aejun

It is autumn, a good season for reading as the fragrance of print (活字香氣) freshens. How delightful a fruit of this autumn it is that the works of our long-distance runner (長距離선수) Kubo (仇甫) are now compiled into one volume (一卷書) and placed by the lamplit desk (冊床머리)!
For me, nothing has been more enjoyable to read than Kubo's works. I enjoyed how distinctly different he was from me; I enjoyed that he wrote because he himself enjoyed it, before putting forth notions of "era" (時代) or "the masses" (民衆); I enjoyed that, while seeming playful (弄調) at first glance, <195> that is a simplistic view of literature—rather, works as consistently truthful (眞實一路) as Kubo's are rare among other writers.
Moreover, his characters' sensibilities towards human nature and social conditions (人情世態) are always Kubo's own. Their taste of culture (敎養맛) and refined manner inevitably draw us intellectuals into his affable sociability (사귐성), as if meeting Kubo himself.
Furthermore, Kubo is a stylist (스타일리스트) more pioneering (先覺한) than anyone. His unique, tenacious, long and flowing (치렁치렁한) long-distance sentences (長距離문장)—once psychology (心理), events (事件), or anything else gets caught in this style, it is exposed nakedly (赤裸하게) without concealing a single thread (一絲). The futility of trying to live for art in this land, yet the sorrow of it being fate; the spirit (魂膽) of the pale group of intellectual ghosts (蒼白한 知識幽靈群); truly (可히) laughable, truly sad, truly making one bow one's head in deep thought (低頭沈思)—a display (陳列) of ourselves unfolds in every work.
Moreover, the perfection of his writing style (文體) deserves more than respect. It is not necessary to elaborate (區區하지 않아) with examples how widely the long-sentence style has become fashionable since "Novelist Mr. Kubo's Day (小說家 仇甫氏의 一日)" was published; Kubo's style now fully exists as one style of Korean prose (朝鮮文章).
Thus, this collection of creative works, "Novelist Mr. Kubo's Day," so distinct in content and style, splendidly rises as a new peak in Korean literature (朝鮮文學). It can be called a newly discovered peak and valley of our Literary Kŭmgang Mountains (文學金剛). May the virtuous ladies and gentlemen of the literary world (江湖 淑人君子) who highly enjoy literature savor the happiness of ascending and descending (나리는) this New Kŭmgang (新金剛) at will (隨意로).
Showa, Muchin year, a few days before the Double Ninth Festival

On Yi Pyŏnggi's "Karam's Sijo Collection"
Chiyong Chŏng Chiyong

Looking again at this precious Sijo Collection (時調集) now compiled, it is so refined (精하고), elegant (조찰하고), and high in quality (品) that it seems to emanate fragrance, making me feel almost apprehensive (悚懼하기까지) about adding (덛붙이기가) any words. When any form of art reaches perfection (완벽), no words of explanation (辯解) seem fitting (맞득지 않다). Even considering poetry (詩歌), rather than comparing (고누기보다는) whether it is good or bad, isn't the decisive factor whether it was truly born as poetry (詩歌로 태어나온 것이냐) or just carelessly (히지 부지) and trivially (조잔히) crafted? A person with discerning eyes recognizes (알어 낸다) the genuine article. Although understanding has levels (층층 이지마는), those who can understand (알만한) will know, and those who cannot will remain ignorant. What else can be done but entrust it to those who know poetry and buy joy? Knowing is also an innate blessing (福). Perhaps this is why Karam (嘉藍) just handed over the Sijo manuscript without a single word (말슴). As for me, feeling awkward (궁거워), I cannot help but add a few words.
If we consider the age of our literary circle to be 30 years, then Karam's Sijo are comparably mature (年富) in age.
The one who historically (史的으로) pursued (追求한 이) sijo, the one who theoretically (理論으로) analyzed (分柝한 이 - likely typo for 分析), the careful (叮寧한) commentator (註釋家) who established criteria for criticism, and the one who popularized it in an enlightening manner (啓蒙的으로) is none other than Karam. If Sijo studies (時調學) can stand, it is only from Karam onwards.
In Sijo composition (時調製作), there is still (아즉) no one who can sit to the right of Karam in terms of quantity and quality. That he was born a natural poet (天成의 시인) possessing overflowing refined skill (精功) is perhaps a point that makes it even harder to rival Karam. At this time (이지음), when the whereabouts of the once flourishing (드날리던) Sijo poets are completely unknown, Karam's steps cannot but be the gait (步法) of an elephant emerging from a dense forest (密林). Comparing him to the elders of the past (예전 어른) might be commonplace, but I wonder if Karam has not risen after Songgang (松江). While none, past or present, may match Songgang's vigor (覇氣), it is unknown if anyone else yet possesses Karam's meticulous and delicate (緻密纖細한) quality. Songgang composed quite a number of sijo poems (首), and while each piece is genius enough to shine for eternity (千古), many seem to have been composed in one breath (一氣呵成) as a sideline (副業) to Chinese classics studies (漢學), perhaps while intoxicated (醉餘에) (though it is impossible to view Songgang's *kasa* this way). Since only 70-80 poems (首) are transmitted, it is not thought that Songgang particularly (구타여) devoted his heart and soul to refining (精進하셨다고는) sijo. If Songgang, the most outstanding (最高秀逸) in Sijo literature, was like this, then among the hundreds of others throughout history (역대로) who dabbled in this path (斯道) and then stopped, who truly approached sijo with literary spirit (意氣) and artistic soul (魂膽)? Most likely expressed their feelings (托意叙懷) in the three-stanza song form (歌形三章) upon feeling life's spirit (義氣) and vanity (虛妄). However, apart from uniformly adding grammatical particles (吐를 단 것) to Chinese poetic style (漢詩調), or the absurd (唐荒한) habit of lamentation/praise (咏嘆癖) for nature (江湖風月) or a taste for reminiscing (懷古趣味) about distant figures like Yao, Shun, Wen, and Wu (堯舜文武), there is actually little worth seeing. Occasionally, the threads of delicate human sorrow and regret (情恨) were wound and unwound in sijo, creating a Korean lyricism (朝鮮的 리리시슴) that later generations (後世) can indeed draw upon; the songs left by elders like Myŏnangjŏng (俛仰亭) or female poets (閨秀歌人), famous and nameless, besides Chin'i (眞伊), are examples. But even collected together, they are too insignificant (하잔하다) to form a grand compilation (集大成). In short (요컨대), it is undeniable that the elders of the past, being preoccupied with other forms of poetry (詩), did not particularly exert themselves in composing sijo as poetry. <197> However, there was no poetic form (詩形) other than the three stanzas of sijo that was most suitable (맞가롭고) for containing pure Korean poetry (朝鮮的 포에지), could be recited and sung, and possessed the marvelous (神妙한) quality of a fixed-form poem (整形詩), like a short bamboo flute (短簫) among musical instruments.
In the 30 years since new literature began to arise vigorously (勃興되기) in the literary circle, several motivated individuals recognized the new value of this poetic form and attempted writing (試作하여) it, but ultimately, shouldn't new poetry be contained within it? There was nothing that could be called poetry (詩랄 것). They were merely preoccupied with filling in letters awkwardly (窘塞한) within clichés (진부한) and conventions (상투적인), producing contrived poetic ideas rather than expressing natural disposition (天然한 性情). The value of sijo lies not in any prescriptive (章程的인) worth derived from limitations (제한) on syllable or stanza count, but its characteristic is possessing an eternal (悠久한) instrumental capability (器樂的 성능) that allows the pursuit of poetry's rhythmic freedom (節調的 自由) through the form's restrictive lack of freedom (제약적 不自由). All the virtues (美德) of fixed-form poetry manifested (顯揚된) in Korea (朝鮮) through the three-stanza sijo form. Thus, apart from sijo poetry, no other appropriate (타당한) Korean fixed-form poetry has yet been invented. Reverently following (追尊하여) the traditional poetic form and breathing the spirit (氣息) of poetry into it should originally (월래) have been the great work (大業) of poets. Entrusting it (맡는댓자) to mere writers (文筆家) who were not poets could not produce its true sound. That vigorous (勃勃한) poetic aspirants kept a respectful distance (敬遠하고) from sijo and turned away was undeniably one reason they could not find poetry in sijo. Doesn't the new generation have no obligation to practice asceticism (고행할) under the imposing name (尊大한 名目下에) of "classic," especially amidst staleness (陳腐) and convention (常套)? Thus, sijo continued its extremely anemic (貧血的) existence and finally faced a crisis (위기), just as calligraphy (書道) fell into a dim (奄庵한) state before and after Ch'usa (秋史).
Although a single minister (各相?) cannot prop up (붓돋아) an entirely collapsed nation (社稷), the lifeline (명맥) of the way of art (藝道) can sustain its blood circulation (血行) through just one genius. Now, we need not be stingy (인색히) in corroborating (助證하기에) Karam's position in the history of Sijo literature.
Finally, sijo met (맞나서) a poet and returned to the poet. By meeting a natural-born poet equipped with sensitivity's (感性) delicacy (纖細), nerve's (神經) sharpness (銳利), and contemplation's (觀照) intelligence (聰慧), sijo came to produce its own sound (제 소리 낳게 된). Karam's sijo succeeded because Karam the poet succeeded. To conclude quickly, had he not been born a poet, he could never have had the undue confidence of "...oh, just one sijo poem..."
Moreover, his firm (確乎한) linguistic foundation (語學的 토대) and profound knowledge (造詣) of old songs (古歌謠) enabled Karam to gain both strength and brilliance in sijo composition. His sijo are so pious (경건하고) and sincere that readers can take them as a lifelong textbook (平生敎科). In their thoroughness (철저한) regarding nature and reality (리알리티), which is hard to bear in traditional sijo (傳來時調), <198> one rather comes to admire his passionate intention for the reconstruction of sijo (時調再建) through a modern poetic spirit (근대적 詩精神). Thus, Karam, starting from tradition, broke away from it, and again, transcending the trends of the time (시류에 초월한), stands in the honorable (영예로운) position of the revival of sijo (時調中興).
Showa, Kimyo year, 7th Month

On Pak Kyeju's "Record of Martyred Love"
Ch'unwŏn Yi Kwangsu

Pak Kyeju, under the pen name (펜네임) Pak Chin (朴進), serialized (連載하였다) "Record of Martyred Love (殉愛譜)" in the Maeil Sinbo (每日新報). It was a prize-winning entry (懸賞當選), but for a prize novel (懸賞小說), its spirit and theme (테마) were far too grand.
The author says the origin (由來) of the title "Record of Martyred Love" is martyring (殉한다) oneself for the highest and purest love. Love is giving, not possessing. Love is giving limitlessly (限量없이), giving and giving until finally giving up one's own life. The author, a Christian (크리스치안), is a believer (信奉者) in the principle of love taught by Christ (그리스도)—"Give!" He wrote the novel "Record of Martyred Love" to sing of this spirit he learned through experience (체득한) and let his fellow mankind (인류동포) hear it, to make them share this same spirit.
In this modern age where everyone is self-centered, aspiring to a world without "self" and living a life of self-negation (自己否定) is truly like "living bearing the Cross (十字架)." However, just as the sun is undoubtedly the source of life on Earth, the principle of love remains unchangingly the one and only principle that can save (구제할) humanity. As the saying goes, "Those who seek the Buddha's way are inexhaustible through countless eons (求佛道者 窮劫不盡)," so even in these degenerate latter days (淆薄한 말세), the seed of those who seek truth and the true path (眞道) will not cease (끊어짐이 없을) as long as humanity exists (존속하는). If so, the readers of this novel "Record of Martyred Love" will also not cease.
I do not intend to speak of the artistic value (藝術的 價值 如何) of this "Record of Martyred Love." Readers will judge that for themselves. I only wish to recommend (推獎하고 싶다) to the readers the great motive (動機) the author tried to express in this novel. There may be countless (암만이라도) entertaining novels, ingenious novels, novels with fluent prose, but novels attempting to capture (捕捉하려는) the fundamental problem of all humanity—of individual life, family life, national life, world peace—are not so common. Pak's "Record of Martyred Love" is one of this category (부류).
The author is still young. He is certainly not a person of the past, <199> and rather than a person of the present, he is a person of the future. I hope the author dedicates his selfless life to this holy (聖한) goal, becoming both a doer and preacher (行爲요 說者) of love.
It is said that when one sentient being resolves to seek enlightenment (發心하는 날에), all ten directions of the world (十方世界) tremble. This means that the sprouting (싻트는 것) of a loving heart within one sentient being is the foundation of human salvation (人類救濟). This is the reason (所以다) I believe the publication of the novel "Record of Martyred Love" is by no means a small matter.
Showa, Kimyo year, Cold Dew Festival, at the Paegak Mountain Foothills (白岳山麓)

On Kim Tonghwan's "Night on the Border"
Ansŏ Kim Ŏk

Having been born and quietly spent his childhood in a mountain country (山國) of the north, where white snow piles high (힌 눈이 가득 싸히고) and sandstorms (모래바람) rage, Mr. P'ain (巴人), with his unique sentiment (정서), took material from his homeland, the "Border Region (國境方面)," full of sorrow (설음) and feeling. With a profoundly sorrowful and heroic (沈痛悲壯한) brush, he produced a "romantic" (로맨틱) epic poem (敍事詩) and several other lyric poems (抒情詩) singing of youth. Now, he sends them out into the world under the name "Night on the Border (國境의 밤)." Generally, such poetic creation (詩作) can only manifest its true life when it passes through (것처서야) the hands of such an author. Moreover, choosing the long epic poem (長篇敍事詩) as the form of expression (표현형식) is the first instance in our poetry circle (시단). Seen from various perspectives, this is a precious (貴여운) harvest (수확) for our poetry circle. Mr. P'ain's poetry possesses solemn (엄숙한) strength and soft (보드러운) "beauty" (美). Thus, its solemn strength fervently dissects (메쓰하여 마지않며) reality, while its soft beauty, tinged with warm (다사한) "human" (휴-맨) tones, sings loftily of life. In a word, Mr. P'ain is a poet with strong "humanist" (휴맨이스트) colors. It is admirable (거륵하다) that the author, as a knight (騎士) of humanity (人道), takes the lead with a torch (炬火), and spits phlegm (가래침을 배앝는) unforgivingly at anything that displeases him. Furthermore, his scolding (꾸짖음) and correction (바로잡음) directed towards collapsing (문허저 가는) modern civilization, while praising (찬미하는) the innocent life of the pastoral (田園), is a very beautiful thing. Is this the author's fault, or the fault of modern materialistic civilization? Perhaps you, the readers, will surely feel sorrow and fight alongside him.
I am greatly pleased to send such a brave warrior (勇士) into our poetry circle, and I hope that this "Night on the Border" resonates (울어지어다) in people's hearts.
1924, 12, 13 <200>

**Summary**

This article from the July 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine compiles seven prefaces written by prominent Korean literary figures of the time for books by their contemporaries. The authors (Chŏn Yŏngt'aek, Hong Myŏnghŭi, Pak Chonghwa, Yi T'aejun, Chŏng Chiyong, Yi Kwangsu, Kim Ŏk) offer praise, personal reflections, encouragement, and critical perspectives on works by Yi Kwangsu, Sim Hun, Pak Yŏnghŭi, Pak T'aewŏn, Yi Pyŏnggi, Pak Kyeju, and Kim Tonghwan. The prefaces touch upon themes like the role of literature, artistic dedication, rural issues, Christian love, the revival of the sijo form, and critiques of modernity, showcasing the close-knit and dynamic literary scene of late colonial Korea. For historians, this collection is valuable as it reveals personal relationships and intellectual exchanges among key writers, reflects contemporary literary debates (e.g., modernism vs. traditional forms like sijo, the purpose of literature), and provides insight into the cultural and ideological currents (national consciousness, social critique, religious thought) present in Korea under Japanese rule, just before the intensification of wartime suppression. The dates on some prefaces (Pyŏngja/1936, Muchin/1938, Kimyo/1939, 1924) also help contextualize the specific works and literary moments being discussed.

더구나 仇甫는 누구보다도 先覺한 스타일리스트다. 그의 독특한 끈기있는 치렁치렁한 長距離문장, 心理고 事件이고 무어던 한번 이 문장에 걸리기만 하면 一絲를 가리지 못하고 赤裸하게 노출이 된다. 이 땅에서 예술에 살려는 부질없음, 그러나 운명임에 슬픔, 蒼白한 知識幽靈群의 魂膽, 可히 웃고, 可히 슬프고, 可히 低頭沈思케 하는 우리 자신들의 陳列이 작품마다 전개되는 것이다.
더구나 문체의 완성에는 敬意를 표하고도 남는다. 『小說家 仇甫氏의 一日』이 발표된 후에 장거리문장이 얼마나 널리 유행하며 있는가는 예를 들기까지 區區하지 않아, 仇甫의 문장은 이제 온전히 朝鮮文章의 한 문체로 존재하는 것이다.

귀한 時調集을 꾸미어 놓고 다시 보니 하도 精하고 조찰하고 品이 높기를 향기가 풍기는 듯하여 무슨 말이고 덛붙이기가 悚懼하기까지 하다. 어늬 부문의 예술이고 그것이 완벽에까지 이른 것이고 보면 조금도 辯解다운 말이 맞득지 않다. 詩歌를 들어 볼지라도 그것이 잘되었고 못되었고를 고누기보다는 그것이 진정 詩歌로 태어나온 것이냐 히지 부지 조잔히 만들어진 것이냐라는 것이 결정적으로 들어날 것이 아닌가. 눈을 바로 가춘 사람은 진짜를 알어 낸다. 안다고 하는 것도 층층 이지마는 알만한 이는 알고 모르는 사람은 모르고 말 것이니＜196＞ 詩歌를 아는 이께 맡기고 기쁨을 사는 외에 무슨 도리가 있겠는가. 아는 것도 타고난 福이라 이래서 嘉藍이 時調原稿만 내맡기고 말슴 한마디 없는 것인지 나로서는 궁거워 몇마디 아니 붙일 수 없는 노릇이다.
우리 문단의 나이가 30년이라고 보면 嘉藍時調 나이도 이와 못지 않게 年富한 편이다.
시조를 史的으로 追求한 이 理論으로 分柝한 이 비평에 기준을 세운 叮寧한 註釋家요 啓蒙的으로 보급시킨 이가 바로 嘉藍이다. 時調學이 설수가 있는 것이고 보면 嘉藍으로서부터 비로소다.
時調製作에 있어서 量과 質로써 嘉藍의 오른편에 앉을 이가 아즉 없다. 天成의 시인으로서 넘치는 精功을 타고난 것이 더욱이 嘉藍과 맞서기 어려울 점인가 하노니 한참 드날리던 時調人들의 행방조차 알 길이 아득한 이지음 嘉藍의 걸음은 바야흐로 密林을 헤처나온 코끼리의 步法이 아닐 수 없다. 예전 어른을 들어 비교할 것은 흔한 노릇일지 모르겠으나 松江이후에 嘉藍이 솟아오른 것이 아닌가 한다. 松江의 覇氣를 당할 이 古今에 없겠으나 嘉藍의 緻密纖細한 점이 아즉 어떤 이가 그만한지를 모를 일이다. 松江은 얼마쯤 지으신 時調 首도 많으신 편이시요 首首마다 千古에 빛날 만한 천재적인 것이기는 하나 혹은 漢學의 副業으로 醉餘에(松江歌詞를 그렇게 뵈일 수는 도저히 없는 일이나) 一氣阿成으로 된 것이 多分인 것으로 살필 수 있고 傳하는 것이 7,80首에 지나지 않고 보니 松江께서도 時調에 구타여 심혈을 다하여 精進하셨다고는 생각되지 않는다. 時調文學의 最高秀逸이신 松江이 이러하셨거니 그 외에 역대로 斯道에 손을 대다가 말은 數百을 헤일 수 있는 분들이야 그야말로 문학의 意氣와 예술의 魂膽으로써 時調에 대하였다고 할 분이 누구 누구실고! 인생의 義氣와 虛妄을 느낀 나머지에 이를 歌形三章에 托意叙懷한 것이 대부분이겠으나 一律로 漢詩調에 吐를 단 것이 아니면 거리가 당치도 않은 堯舜文武의 懷古趣味나 江湖風月의 唐荒한 咏嘆癖 이외에 보잘 것이 실상 없다. 間或 아기자기한 인생 情恨의 실마리를 時調로 감고 풀고하여 朝鮮的 리리시슴을 後世 사람으로 따서 쓸 것이 과연 없지 아니하니 俛仰亭같으신 어른이나 眞伊 외에 有名無名의 閨秀歌人들의 끼치고 간 노래가 이것이다. 그러나 모조리 읊어 놓아야 集大成되기에 너무도 하잔하다. 요컨대 예전 어른들은 詩를 달리하느라고 詩를 時調로 하기에 별로 성의를 베풀지 않었던 것이 사실이 아닐 수 없었던 것이다.＜197＞ 그러나 순수 朝鮮的 포에지를 담기에 가장 맞가롭고 읊을 수 있고 불을 수 있는 整形詩로서 악기로 치면 短簫와 같이 神妙한 詩形이 時調 3章 외에 없었던 것이다.
문단에 새로운 문학이 勃興되기 비롯한 30년 來로 몇몇 有志한 분들이 다시 이 詩形의 새로운 가치를 알어 試作하여 보았으나 마침내 새로운 詩가 담기어야 할 말이 아닌가. 詩랄 것이 없었다. 진부한 상투적인 것 天然한 性情의 流露가 아닌 무리한 詩想의 허구에다 窘塞한 글자 채움에 급급하였을 뿐이다. 時調가 字數 章數에 제한이 있어서 무슨 章程的인 가치가 있는 것이 아니라 詩形의 제약적 不自由를 통하여 詩의 節調的 自由를 추구할 수 있는 悠久한 器樂的 성능을 가춘 것이 특색일 것이다. 모든 整形詩의 美德이 朝鮮에서는 3章 時調形으로 顯揚된 것이니 朝鮮的 整形詩는 아직까지 時調詩 이외에 타당한 詩形이 발명되지 않은 것이니 전통적 詩形을 追尊하여 이에 詩의 氣息을 불어 넣기란 월래 詩人의 大業이 아닐 수 없었던 것을 詩人이 아닌 文筆家가 맡는댓자 제 소리가 날 리가 없었다. 勃勃한 詩的 志願者들이 時調를 敬遠하고 돌아서는 것은 時調에서 詩를 얻을 수 없었던 것이 한가지 이유가 아닐수 없었던 것이다. 새로운 세대가 陳腐와 常套에서 더욱이 고전이란 尊大한 名目下에 고행할 의무가 없는 것이 아닌가. 이리하여 時調가 극도의 貧血的 존재를 계속하던 것이 마침내 위기에 직면한 것이니 마치 書道가 秋史전후에 아조 奄庵한 상태에 빠젔던 것과 달을게 없던 것이다.
온전히 기우러진 社稷을 一個 各相으로서 붓돋아 일으킬 수야 없지마는 藝道의 명맥은 一個天才만으로서 血行을 이을 수 있는 것이니 이제 時調文學史上의 嘉藍의 위치를 助證하기에 우리는 인색히 굴 필요가 없이 되었다.
마침내 時調들이 詩人을 맞나서 시인한테로 돌아오게 되었다. 비로소 感性의 纖細와 神經의 銳利와 觀照의 聰慧를 가춘 天成의 시인을 만나서 詩調가 제 소리를 낳게 된 것이니 嘉藍시조가 성공한 것은 시인 嘉藍으로서 성공한 것이라 결론을 빨리하면 시인으로 태어나지 않었던들 아이예 시조 한 首쯤이야...하는 부당한 자신을 가질 수 없었던 것이다.
더욱이 確乎한 語學的 토대와 古歌謠의 造詣가 嘉藍으로 하여금 時調製作에 힘과 빛을 아울러 얻게 한 것이니 그의 시조는 경건하고 진실함이 이를 읽는 이가 平生敎科로 삼을만한 것이요 傳來時調에서 참기 어려운＜198＞ 자연과 리알리티에 철저한 점으로서는 차라리 근대적 詩精神으로써 時調再建의 열렬한 의도에 敬服케 하는 바가 있다. 이리하여 嘉藍이 전통에서 출발하여 그와 결별하고 다시 시류에 초월한 時調中興의 영예로운 위치에 선 것이다.
昭和 己卯 7月

朴啓周 著 「殉愛譜」에
春園 李光洙
朴啓周군은 朴進이라는 펜네임으로 每日新報에 『殉愛譜』를 連載하였다. 그것은 懸賞當選으로였으나 懸賞小說로서는 너무도 정신과 테마가 컸었다.
가장 높고 가장 깨끗한 사랑에 저를 殉한다는 것이 『殉愛譜』라는 題號의 由來라고 작자는 말한다. 사랑은 주는 것이오, 가지는 것이 아니다. 限量없이 주고 주어 마침내 제 목숨까지 주어버리는 것이 사랑이다. 크리스치안인 작자는 그리스도의 『주라!』하신 사랑의 원리의 信奉者요, 그가 소설 『殉愛譜』를 쓴 것은 자기가 체득한 이 정신을 노래하여서 인류동포에게 들리자는 것이다. 인류동포로 하여금 이와 같은 정신을 나누게 하자는 것이다.
모두들 자기 중심인 현대에 처하여서 『나』없는 세계를 동경하여 自己否定의 생애를 보내자는 것부텀이 진실로 『十字架를 지고 사는』일 것이다. 그렇지마는 인류를 구제할 하나요 오직 하나인 원리가 사랑이 원리임에는 변함이 없는 것이 마치 지구상의 생명의 원천이 태양임에 틀림이 없음과 마찬가지다. 『求佛道者 窮劫不盡』이라는 말씀과 같이 아무리 淆薄한 말세라 하여도 진리와 眞道를 구하는 者의 씨는 인류가 존속하는 동안은 끊어짐이 없을 것이다. 그렇다하면 이 소설 『殉愛譜』의 독자도 끊어짐이 없을 것이다.
나는 이 『殉愛譜』의 예술적 가치 如何를 말하려 하지 아니한다. 그것은 독자 스스로 판단하실 것이나 나는 오직 작자가 이 소설에서 표현하려고 한 큰 動機만을 독자에게 推獎하고 싶다. 재미있는 소설, 妙한 소설, 문장이 流麗한 소설은 암만이라도 있을 것이어니와 全인류의 근본문제-개인생활의, 가정생활의, 국가생활의, 세계평화의 근본문제를 捕捉하려는 소설은 그리 흔한 것이 아니다. 그런데 朴군의 『殉愛譜』는 이러한 부류의 소설의 하나다.
작자는 아직 젊은이다. 과거의 人은＜199＞ 물론 아니오, 현재의 人이라기보다도 미래의 人이다. 나는 작자가 이 聖한 목표를 향하여서 『나』없는 일생을 바쳐 사랑의 행위요 說者가 되기를 원한다.
한 중생이 發心하는 날에 十方世界가 다 진동한다고 하니, 사랑의 마음이 한 중생의 속에 싻트는 것이 人類救濟의 근본임을 이른 것이다. 소설 『殉愛譜』의 발행이 결코 적은 일이 아니라고 믿는 所以다.

金東煥 著 「國境의 밤」에
岸曙 金億
힌 눈이 가득 싸히고 모래바람 심한 북쪽나라 山國에서 生을 받아 고요히 어린 때를 보낸 巴人군이 그 독특한 정서로써 설음이 가득하고 느낌 많은 고향인 『國境方面』서 재료를 取하여 沈痛悲壯한 붓끝으로 『로맨틱』한 敍事詩와 그 밖에 청춘을 노래한 抒情詩 몇 편을 제작하여 『國境의 밤』이라는 이름으로 지금 세상에 보내게 되었으니, 대개 이러한 詩作은 오직 이러한 작자의 손을 것처서야 비로소 참생명을 발현할 것인 줄 압니다. 더구나 이 표현형식을 長篇敍事詩에 取하게 되었음은 아직 우리 시단에 처음있는 일이매 여러 가지 의미로 보아 우리 시단에는 貴여운 수확이라 할 것입니다. 그런데 巴人군의 시에는 엄숙한 힘과 보드러운 『美』가 있습니다. 그래서 그 엄숙한 힘은 열렬하게 현실을 『메쓰』하여 마지않며, 보드러운 美는 다사한 『휴-맨』의 색조를 띠어, 높이 인생을 노래합니다. 한마디로 말하면 巴人군은 『휴맨이스트』적 색채를 많이 가진 시인입니다. 작자가 人道을 騎士로 앞장서서 炬火를 쥐고 앞장서서 나가며, 맘에 맞지 않는 것이 있으면 용서없이 가래침을 배앝는 것이 한끗 거륵하다 하겠고, 더구나 문허저 가는 근대의 문명에 대하여 꾸짖음과 『바로잡음』을 보내며 田園의 순진한 생활을 찬미하는 점에 있어서는 매우 아름다운 일인가 합니다. 이것은 작자가 잘못입니까 근대의 물질화한 문명이 잘못입니까, 아마 여러분은 반드시 슬퍼할 줄 알며 함께 싸울 줄 압니다.
나는 우리 시단에 이러한 勇士 하나를 보내게 됨을 몹시 기뻐하며, 아울러 이 『國境의 밤』이 사람의 가슴에 울어지어다 하고 바랍니다.
