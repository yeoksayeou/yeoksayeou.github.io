No Chŏngae

I. Literature

"Soil and Soldiers"
What is war like? Also, what are the psychological changes of soldiers during war?
This work focuses on these points, and I truly marveled at how clearly it depicted what the Yamato spirit (大和心) is.
I especially think the scene where the Imperial Army (皇軍) charges, having entered a state of selflessness (無心), is truly magnificent.

"The Good Earth"
I think this is the greatest masterpiece among the works written by the female author.
Her observations regarding human beings are truly profound.
I also feel enjoyment (甘味) in the way she expressed the lives, thoughts, and customs of the Chinese people.

"Young People"
Among novels, this one is particularly good-hearted.
I truly wonder if the personality of the female protagonist, Eba, isn't representative of women.
It seems female students would feel much enrichment (滋味) from reading it.

"Winter Lodging"
No matter what environment we are in, we shouldn't think of it with discontent.
However, we also shouldn't feel satisfied.
Simply trying to improve from one's own environment, that is, the current position, allows one to recognize the proper value as a human being, and the work makes one feel this point particularly.

Chŏng Kyusun

I. Literature

"The Good Earth" <187>
Reading The Good Earth, I could learn about Chinese customs, and I realized that these customs are very similar (恰似) to those of Korea.
The love for the soil shown by Wang Lung and O-lan in Part One gave me a sense of realism, as I was born in a rural village.
In Part Three, one could see the clash between the old era and the new era.
As the author, Ms. Pearl Buck, even received the Nobel Prize, I cannot help but be happy as a fellow woman.

"Gone with the Wind"
The personalities of the four main characters, Scarlett, Ashley, Melanie, and Rhett, are contrasted and meticulously described against the backdrop of the American Civil War.
The beautiful cotton fields of the American South flicker before my eyes, imbued with the Southern sentiment.
Scarlett's love for her homeland (鄕土愛) gives a deep impression.
Reading this filled me with an indescribable, delicate (떼리게-트한) feeling.

"Young People"
Because the heroine is a student, it drew my interest. I don't know if the author wrote it using a real incident as a model, but it seems too unrealistic.

II. Music

"Chŏng Hunmo"
The lady is a humble and simple person, both on stage and at home.
Her physique is small, but her vocal volume is large.
One cannot help but admire her effort in dedicating herself to art as a housewife (家庭의 한 주부).

III. Film

"Tuition"
I see it as having well captured the realistic appearance of Korean peasants.
However, Yŏngdal lacks the innocent feel of a young child and seems too contrived.
Also, in the final scene when he meets his mother, it seems it would have been better if he showed a more joyful expression.
It's a bland expression, as if meeting a mother he's always with.

Pak Hoae

I. Literature

"Gone with the Wind"
It expressed nature and the characters' personalities so well that it felt like watching a movie or a play.
There wasn't a single point I thought was unnatural, and the Mississippi River (미시십피河), which I had wanted to see just once, was drawn before my eyes <188>, and it even seemed like the fragrance of the nameless flowers blooming on its banks (河畔) was wafting by.
Anyway, it's simply an enriching (滋味있는) novel.

"The Good Earth"
Perhaps because it depicts the romanticized life of Chinese peasants, there is undeniably a romantic feeling throughout the entire volume of The Good Earth. I sincerely hope that Korea will have a great writer (文豪) capable of writing such a novel.

II. Music

"Mr. Fujiwara Yoshie"
The gentleman, who abundantly possesses the type of a musician, seems even better when singing folk songs rather than famous art songs (名曲).

"Ms. Chŏng Hunmo"
As a vocalist of the Peninsula (半島), her voice never tires no matter how many times one hears it; isn't her future, more than anything, greatly anticipated?

III. Film

"Tuition"
Should we call it a characteristic of Koreans? In scenes of reunion after a long time, is that all the joy there is between mother and child, father and child? What if they clung to each other, overcome with joy, and cried their eyes out? The whole thing felt too unnatural.

Yang Chaewŏn

I. Literature

"Biography of Madame Curie"
One can see that behind a great man, there is always an intelligent woman. I hope that among us Korean women too, there will be many possessors of a self-sacrificing spirit like Madame Curie.

"Poetry Collection of the Fallen"
The free world of men, and the bitterness of women dominated beneath it. The heart of a woman who, having suffered a romantic breakdown due to a heartless man, still holds lingering affection for him.

II. Music

"Kye Chŏngsik"
There is a noble feel somewhere, and his type, appearing on stage, reminds one of the musical sage (樂聖) Schubert, whom one might imagine.

"Fujiwara Yoshie" (Note: original uses 藏江, likely typo for 義江)
Although he received repeated encores, he doesn't seem like a person to be respected.

III. Film

"Tuition"
It frankly captured the wandering state of life in poverty (貧寒) <189> that can be witnessed here.
Listening to someone's impressions, they say various things like it lacks expressive technique or is poor, but rather than saying this movie is poor, I want to say that the lives of Koreans are poor.
The expressive technique doesn't seem particularly inferior compared to other famous films. That is because the fundamental purpose of film should not be viewed merely as an entertainment medium, but as something that reflects the national life of that era and country, in other words, their view of life or worldview. Therefore, rather than discussing the film itself, I think it is appropriate for us to discuss our lives.
The fact that Eastern films are lower in level than Western films is nothing more than the fact that the lives of Eastern people are lower in level than the lives of Western people.
This time, "Tuition" portrays impoverished rural life, and the pitiable boys who suffer in poor homes, away from the period when they should be engrossed in studies—it would not be an exaggeration to say that the majority of boys currently in rural areas are in this situation.
However, there is not a bit of the innocent aspect aimed at children, and the value and lack of value of money should be represented more accurately; it failed to provide a strong stimulus suggesting something therein.

Kim Okyŏp

I. Literature

"Love"
I felt that one absolutely cannot live in the world without love. Yet, I thought that on the flip side of love, there is much suffering, jealousy (妖嫉), and misunderstanding.

II. Music

"Beethoven," "Schubert"
Whenever I listen to the compositions of these two, my head naturally bows, yet overcome by excitement, my heart becomes restless and agitated.
Even looking at their photographs, they already looked like composers.

"Chŏng Hunmo"
That noble type, and even the sound coming from that throat, sounded beautiful and noble.

III. Film

"Stone by the Roadside"
For a boy, he appeared to have remarkably strong willpower. I think it's admirable how he endured such terrible suffering, contempt, and oppression without losing heart <190> at all, establishing his will to the very end.
I felt that success follows diligence.

"The Nightingale"
It's already been 3 or 4 years since I saw this movie, perhaps a long time if you say long, or a short time if you say short, so it's become a thing of the past and I don't remember it well, but I will try to write down what vaguely comes to mind.
Hearing the title "The Nightingale" (鶯), I thought it was truly strange, why would they make it with the name of a bird, what meaning could be inside it that people rave about it being good? When I actually watched it, I think it truly depicted the opposite of devils dancing—the world of paradise (樂園), that is, paradise itself.
That is, it meticulously expressed the period from before a human is born into the world to after birth, and within it, the immensely large issue of human happiness was satisfactorily well represented, I think.

"Tuition"
The life of a poor, destitute (가없은) rural village in some district was well expressed, but it felt so pitiable, regrettable, and sad.
For a young boy, he was thought to possess an unusual maternal love (母性愛), rarely seen. It seems to have ended too anticlimactically (시시하게) at the end. <191>

**Summary**

This article presents the cultural reviews of female students from the Central Childcare School in colonial Korea, 1940. Divided into literature, philosophy/religion, music, and film sections, the students (No Chŏngae, Chŏng Kyusun, Pak Hoae, Yang Chaewŏn, Kim Okyŏp) share their impressions of various works, primarily Japanese and Western, but also including some Korean pieces like Yi Kwangsu's "Love," Yi Hyosŏk's "Blue Sky," Yu Chin'o's "Flower Fantasy Score," and the films "Arirang," "Sŏnghwangdang," and "Tuition." The reviews reflect the students' engagement with themes of war ("Soil and Soldiers"), social conditions ("The Good Earth," "Tuition"), personal growth ("Young People," "Stone by the Roadside"), love, and the role of women ("Gone with the Wind," "Biography of Madame Curie"). The discussions often draw parallels to Korean life or express aspirations for Korean culture. The article offers a valuable glimpse into the cultural consumption patterns and intellectual concerns of educated young Korean women during the late colonial period. Historians might find the mix of Japanese, Western, and Korean cultural products consumed, the students' interpretations (e.g., linking "The Good Earth" to Korean rural life, discussing the Yamato spirit in "Soil and Soldiers"), and the commentary on Korean films like "Tuition" (debating its realism versus artistic merit and linking its perceived flaws to the state of Korean society) particularly revealing about cultural life, national consciousness, and the pervasive influence of Japanese and Western culture under colonial rule.

「授業料」
이곳에서 구경할 수 있는 貧寒에서＜189＞ 彷徨하는 생활상을 솔직하게 낱아내었다.
어느 분의 感想을 들으니 표현기술이 없다느니 빈약하다느니 여러 가지 말을 하는데 나는 이 영화가 빈약하다는 것보다 朝鮮人의 생활이 빈약하다는 것을 말하고 싶다.
표현기술은 다른 名映에 비해도 떨어진 점은 별로 없을 것 같다. 그것은, 영화의 근본 목적은 다만 오락기관으로만 간파하는 것보다 그 시대 그 국가의 국민생활, 다시 말하면 그네들의 인생관이라든지 세계관을 그대로 반영해 놓은 것이다. 그러므로 우리는 영화 그 자체를 논하는 것보다 우리들의 생활을 논하는 것이 마땅하리라고 생각한다.
東洋영화가 西洋영화보다 레벨이 낮다는 것이 즉 東洋人의 생활이 西洋人의 생활보다 레벨이 났다는 것에 지나지 않는다.
이번 授業料는 貧窮한 농촌생활이라든지 한참 공부에 열중할 시기를 떠나 貧家에서 고뇌하는 가련한 소년들 현재 농촌에 있는 소년들은 대다수가 이러한 입장에 있다 해도 과언이 아닐 것이다.
그러나 아동을 상대로 한 無邪氣한 점은 조금도 없으며 금전의 有價値와 無價値를 좀더 정확하게 나타낼 것이며 거기에 그 무엇을 암시하는 强烈한 자극은 주지 못하였다.
