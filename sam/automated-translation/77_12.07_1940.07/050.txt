Article Title: 世界大戰은 어떻게 되나, 名家諸氏의 興味있는 諸觀測(6월 10일 회답 전부 도착분) (What Will Become of the World War? Interesting Observations from Various Notables (All responses received by June 10th))
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

What Will Become of the World War? Interesting Observations from Various Notables
Current Affairs Q&A
1. Do you think the European War will be won by the German side or the British-French Allied Forces side? Also, what is your prediction for the future of the Great War?
2. Do you think Italy, the United States, and the Soviet Union will enter the war? Also, when might that be?
3. Do you have any plans to travel to the "New China" under the Nanjing National Government, which is steadily being built under the banner of peaceful nation-building? If you go, what facilities would you mainly see, and what kind of people would you primarily wish to meet?

Ha Chunsŏk, President of Chosŏn Kongsak Corporation (朝鮮工作株式會社)
1. The question is too large, so for someone like me, who is not a military commentator but a complete layman (門外漢), giving a clear answer to such a difficult question might rather be rash (妄擧). However, since you've asked, thinking extremely commonsensically within the scope of my shallow knowledge (淺識), it seems Germany's victory in the European War is decisive as things stand now, but I don't think it will end in such a short period as generally thought. Even if Britain and France are said to have declined (衰頹), they have been recognized by themselves and others (自他共認) as world-class nations. Especially Britain, possessing territories across the globe to the extent that the sun never sets (日沒不知) on its empire, will not easily abandon the pride of the Great British Empire.<79> Moreover, although its army is no match for Germany's, its naval power is considerably strong, making it impossible to predict the outcome suddenly. In short, compared to France, Britain is superior in economic power, military strength, and other various aspects; it's no comparison with France.
From this perspective, it seems highly possible that it will become a long-term war rather than a short decisive one.
2. Italy's entry into the war is a matter of time; its eventual participation is undoubtedly certain.
I think the United States will not enter the war so hastily, but spiritually and economically, it has sided with Britain and France, which is like participating in the war. If the war extends over a long period as mentioned in item 1, I believe the US will eventually join in at the end, but perhaps its timing will be unexpectedly soon? Why? Because the US President has already declared spiritual support for Britain and France, securing the US-UK communication line is the primary measure the US must take. Due to this, and with Germany's attack on the British mainland imminent in today's war situation, hasn't the timing for US entry been significantly hastened?
3. I don't have plans to travel yet, but if I were to travel, I would like to meet Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛) and other key figures of the new government, as well as promising individuals from the younger generation.

Kim Kapsun, President of Chosŏn Sinmun (朝鮮新聞)
Reply: This is an extremely difficult question. In my humble opinion (淺見):
1. Although it's difficult to predict the future of the European War, Germany's considerably strengthened and solid economic preparation and blitzkrieg tactics have been effective (奏効), and I think eventually a new order of life will be established in Europe with the downfall (落魄) of Britain and France.
2. The US seems likely to enter the war mainly due to ethnic prejudice, while Italy and the Soviet Union due to interests (利權關係). However, due to Italy and the Soviet Union's ambition (野望) for unearned gains (不勞自得的), the timing seems likely to be quite delayed. But Italy, because of Mediterranean hegemony and Africa, will likely be the first among the three to enter the war. Perhaps within two months?
3. If public order is secured, I will definitely go to China (支那) to see the reality of the construction of the New East Asian Order (東亞新秩序). If I go, I would mainly see cultural facilities and meet not only Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛氏) and other important figures in and out of government but also the leaders of misguided youth groups (思想靑年群). <80>

Chŏng Sŏkt'ae, Doctor of German Medicine (獨逸醫學搏士)
We must understand that the current European War is primarily between Germany (獨逸) and Britain (英國). As for which side will win, unless one is God, it's impossible to give a definite answer. However, predicting based on past and present history, Britain must inevitably be defeated. Also, if God exists and there is so-called justice (正義) and humanity (人道), Britain should rightfully receive heavenly punishment this time. Briefly listing the reasons:
1. Britain's history of exploiting (착취) colonies for over 400 years cannot be called righteous in its methods; it has employed all sorts of evil ways deviating from humanity. While the British population is merely 30-40 million, they hold a quarter of the world's territory, yet they show not an inch of compromise to the German nation of nearly 100 million, snatching away their livelihood with merciless treaties. While letting natural resources rot in their own territories due to lack of population, they strictly prohibit (嚴禁) immigration from other ethnicities. They inflict suffering even on their own colonies by preventing the import of goods from other countries through unimaginably high tariffs (高率의 重稅), and extract the lifeblood (膏血) of 300 million people in India (印度) to maintain the luxurious facade (體面) of so-called gentlemen (紳士). Even a merciful God could surely not help but be angered by this. History since ancient times suggests to us a fate that cannot continue indefinitely.
2. Taking an example from individuals, just as the thoughts and lifestyle of a rich young master (도련님) are weak, while the will and physique of a child from poverty are strong and sturdy, wealthy Britain, having lived a sweet dream for 400 years without rough storms, is destined always for maintaining the status quo (現像保管). The German nation, having tasted the bitter fate of hunger, advances bravely (勇進) with a burning determination to survive, awake or asleep. Even an unseen God, one can guess which side's fate they would favor.
3. Just as the histories of ancient Greece or the Roman Empire disappearing show, rise and fall (一興一亡) is the natural order; there seems no reason why God would uniquely help Britain forever.
4. Fate aside, of course, Germany is by no means inferior to Britain in terms of ethnicity, Germanic spirit, quality (質), or quantity (數). Even if, historically, Britain's time to fall <81> has not yet come and it achieves victory this time, I firmly declare that Britain will inevitably fall before long. In history, several decades are but a few minutes in an individual's life.
5. This is not based on the war results between the two sides to date. From the beginning of the war, I have clearly predicted this based on the above reasoning, and there has been no deviation whatsoever. The results in Northern Europe (北歐) and Flanders (푸란돌) show an unprecedented defeat for the Allied forces, Paris (巴里) will return to Germany within a few days (by the time the reader sees this text, the fall of Paris will surely be in the past), and Prime Minister Churchill is begging God for a miracle; I already consider the fate decided.
It should be common sense since the start of the war that Italy (伊太利) will, of course, enter the war on Germany's side. Italy's fate is exactly the same as Germany's; if Germany were defeated, Italy's fate would inevitably gradually decline. The reason why it hasn't entered the war yet is that delaying Italy's entry is extremely advantageous for Germany, both politically and practically (reasons omitted), and there's also the delicate situation with the Soviet Union (蘇聯). Perhaps the time when Germany lands on the British mainland will be the time of Italy's entry into the war.
The United States (米國) is a strange country advocating for so-called international policing power. It internally hopes to join the British side, but it has largely missed its chance for war, and the intangible power of the Japanese Empire can be seen as prohibiting US entry. That is, the impetus for Germany's current swift campaign can also be attributed to the power of the Soviet Union, Italy, and Japan.
Soviet participation also seems likely towards the end. Perhaps the location will be Iran (Near East, 近東歐) or the India region.

Kim Tongjin, Councilor (叅事) at Sŏnman T'akchik Corporation (鮮滿拓殖株式會社)
1. It's still uncertain which side will win the European War, but if Germany wins, all order in the world will change, which is quite interesting.
2. Italy and the US show a high possibility of entering the war, but the Soviet Union seems unlikely to step in readily, as it waits for the capitalist countries to collapse through internal strife (自相賤踏).
3. For me personally, it seems too early to travel to New China (新支那). Therefore, I don't have any particular person in mind.

Yu Chongha, Hamgyŏng North Province Assembly Member (咸鏡北道會議員)
1. The German side will definitely win.<82> As for the future of the Great War, I assert that the United States will join the British-French Allied forces. The Soviet Union shakes hands with Germany now but will fight Germany in the end.
2. Anyone can guess that Italy will join Germany and declare war this month.
3. I do not wish to travel. The reason is that all causes of the current European War and the China Incident (支那事變) are the schemes (策動) of the Jewish people (猶太民族), so future developments will be ever-changing (千變萬化); ultimately, there is no need to see it.

Han Yangho, Principal of Kyŏngsŏng Women's Commercial School (京城女子商業學校)
1. Even the belligerent nations themselves probably don't know the outcome, so how can we predict it? However, I estimate it will drag on for a long time.
2. I believe they will eventually enter the war; the timing will be when the flames of war erupt in the Mediterranean (地中海) and the Balkans (빨칸).
3. If I travel, I intend to see the past and present progress related to education. Because I am an educator.

Hong Yangmyŏng, Editor-in-Chief of Mansŏn Ilbo (滿鮮日報), Xinjing (新京)
1. Germany seems likely to win the military battle, but won't the result be a stalemate? As for the future of the Great War, well, won't it make the United States even richer and turn Europe into an arena (鬪技場) for Soviet Bolshevism and Italian Fascism? No one has the courage to give a definite answer, so shall we leave it at that?
2. I'm not a fortune teller, so I can't say for sure, but the possibility of Italian entry is 70%, US entry 50%, and Soviet entry 0. The timing would be when German ground forces set foot on the British mainland.
3. The need to restrict travelers aiming for sightseeing visits amidst the current complicated transportation situation between Japan and China (日支間) is increasingly urgent (益緊切). That is all.

Yi Sŏn'gŭn, Director of the Anshan(?) Branch (安家所長) of Manmong Industrial Corporation (滿蒙産業株式會社), Harbin (哈爾賓)
1. It's quite difficult to assert how the Great War will turn out; it seems likely to become a long-term war. Regardless of who wins or loses the Great War, the world seems poised for a change.
2. Italy already entered the war, according to an extra edition that circulated before I started writing this. Regarding whether the Soviet Union or the US will enter, I think the US is more likely to enter before the Soviet Union.<83>
3. Wanting to see New China (新支那) has been a long-cherished wish (宿望) for years, yet it remains an unfulfilled pending issue. If the harvest season ends this year, I'd like to go even before the year's end, but it's undecided. Assuming I go, I would mainly want to see historical sites (史蹟) - especially the harsh traces (자최) of Western power's eastward advance (西力東漸). As for people... well, if he'd meet me, perhaps Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛)?

So Wan'gyu, Lawyer (辯護士)
1. I am somewhat concerned about the German side. I think eventually both sides will be exhausted, allowing the Soviet Union to reap the fisherman's profit (漁夫의 利).
2. I think all will enter the war, perhaps Italy first, then the US, and finally the Soviet Union.
3. I am too busy earning a living to go. If I were to go, I'd head north to Mansu Mountain (萬壽山) and south to Suzhou and Hangzhou (蘇杭) to sightsee to my heart's content.

Ham Sanghun, Editor-in-Chief of Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報社)
1. It's difficult to predict at this stage. Just because the German army is currently winning doesn't necessarily mean it will win until the end. France might be attacked and forced to surrender. However, occupying Britain or forcing the British Empire to surrender will be difficult.
2. Italy might enter the war around the time of Paris's fall. The US will enter by sending relief forces when Britain and France fall into difficulty after Italy's entry. The Soviet Union will engage in Near East (近東) and Balkan (빨칸) maneuvers within limits that avoid conflict with Germany and Italy.
3. I intended to go last year but couldn't, and planned to go this spring but couldn't. I definitely intend to go. If I go, I want to observe the state of public order and public sentiment, and hear the aspirations (포부) of current National Government leaders like Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛) and Wang K'emin (王克敏).

Kim Irin, Editor-in-Chief of Maeil Sinbo (每日新報社)
1. Looking at the current war situation in the European War, it seems Germany will win. The German army's preparation and new weapons are superior, leading to continuous success in blitzkriegs, so the future war situation also looks very advantageous for the German side.
2. Italy's entry can be said to be decisive. Even if the US enters, it seems likely to take considerable time, and eventually, it probably won't enter beyond supplying munitions. The Soviet Union's attitude <84> is unpredictable (不可測), but if Germany and Italy reach a good compromise with the Soviet Union regarding the Balkan issue, it seems unlikely to participate in the war. However, towards the end of the Great War, it might enter with the aim of global communization (世界赤化).
3. If I get the chance, I will definitely visit New China (新支那) once. I want to see the scenery of North and Central China, of course, the culture of the newly born China, talk with Chou Fo-hai (周佛海) and Lin Po-sheng (林伯生), and if possible, meet Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛) as well.

Yi Chŏngsŏp, Kyŏngsŏng Central Broadcasting Station (京城中央放送局)
1. You ask a truly difficult question in a difficult way. Well, looking at the current situation, it's true that it's advantageous for Germany, but we can't view this as decisive. The other day, I met Mr. "Ma-t'el" (마-텔) on the street, and he said the war would continue even if Paris falls.
2. It seems best to ask Italy, the US, and the Soviet Union directly. Italy and the Soviet Union are untrustworthy countries. Only the US's direction can be somewhat guessed, but well, I wonder if it will enter the war that easily.
3. I would like to visit New China (新支那), but I have neither money nor time. I went as far as Wuhan (武漢) in Showa 2 (1927), so I intend to be satisfied with that.

Yi Sŏnghwan, Director (重役) at Taedong Mining Corporation (大同鑛業株式會社)
1. In the current situation, Germany's victory is certain. However, who besides God (神明) could know the final victory? My prediction for the future of the Great War is as follows:
1. Organization of a European Federation (歐洲聯盟) = Redistribution of colonies. Renegotiation of maritime and aerial supremacy, etc.
2. Reappearance of Disarmament Conferences (軍縮會議).
2. Italy's entry is decisive. The entry of the US and the Soviet Union depends on how the general situation develops, but the Soviet Union's attitude needs to be considered separately.
3. I plan to travel around next spring. I intend to investigate and research the treatment issue (대우문제) of Koreans residing in China (在支朝鮮人) and meet all key figures promoting Japan-China relations.

Yi Kŭngno, Doctor of German Philosophy (獨逸哲學博士)
1. I do not know which side will win the European War.
2. I do not know whether Italy, the US, or the Soviet Union will enter the war.
3. I have no plans to travel to New China (新支那). <85>

**Summary**

This 1940 survey captures the perspectives of various Korean elites on the unfolding World War II. Most respondents predicted an initial German victory, though opinions diverged on the war's ultimate duration and outcome, with several anticipating a long conflict and potential US involvement later. Italy's entry on Germany's side was widely expected, while the Soviet Union's role remained uncertain, with some predicting late entry for opportunistic gains or ideological reasons. Views on visiting Wang Jingwei's Nanjing regime varied; some expressed interest in seeing the "New China" and meeting its leaders, particularly Wang, while others cited lack of time, safety concerns, or disinterest. Historically, the article is significant as it reflects the complex attitudes of colonial Koreans towards the global conflict, influenced by Japanese propaganda (evident in pro-Axis leanings and terms like "New East Asian Order"), but also showing diverse individual assessments, including skepticism about a quick German win, concerns about Soviet expansion, and specific interests like the welfare of Koreans in China or the cultural/political landscape under the collaborationist Nanjing government. The range of opinions, from pro-German sentiment (sometimes justified with anti-British colonial arguments) to cautious neutrality or pro-Allied leanings (like anticipating US entry against Germany), offers valuable insight into the varied intellectual currents among prominent Koreans under Japanese rule during a pivotal historical moment.

時局問答
一. 歐洲大戰은 獨逸側이 勝戰할 것 같습니까, 英佛聯合軍側이 勝戰할 것 같습니까, 또 大戰 장래의 예측은 如何?

朝鮮工作株式會社長 河駿錫
一. 문제가 넘우 큼으로 나와 같은 군사평론가가 아닌 전연 門外漢이 이와 같은 難問題에 대하야 明答을 하는 것은 도로혀 妄擧가 아닌가 합니다. 然이나 못처름 문의하섰음으로 나의 淺識의 범위 내에서 극히 상식적으로 생각하건대 歐洲大戰은 지금같애서는 獨逸의 승전은 결정적이나 然이나 세상 일반에서 생각하는 것과 같이 그렇게 단기간에 종료될 줄은 생각치 안습니다. 英佛은 衰頹하였다할지라도 세계의 일류국가로서 自他共認하여왔을 뿐 아니라 특히 영국은 세계에서 日沒을, 不知할 만큼 세계 각지에 영토를 가지고 있음으로 英大帝國의 자존심을 그렇게 용이하게 버리지는 못할 것＜79＞입니다. 그 우에 육군은 獨逸의 적이 못되지마는 해군력은 상당히 강대함으로 其勝敗를 卒地에 예단할 수는 불가능합니다. 요컨대 英國은 佛蘭西에 비하야 경제력, 무력 기타 諸點에 있어서 佛蘭西와의 비교는 아닙니다.
이상과 같은 관점으로 보며는 對戰보다는 長期戰이 될 가능성이 충분한 줄로 생각이 됩니다.
ºŒ. 伊太利의 참전은 시기의 문제로 결국 참전은 확정적임은 틀님없습니다.
米國은 그렇게 조급히 참전치 않으리라고 생각은 되나 정신적으로나 경제적으로 英佛에 가담하야 참전한 것과 같습니다. 전쟁이 제1項에서 述한 바와 같이 長期에 亙하면 米國도 결국 끝을 니어 드러갈 줄 思합니다만은 其 시기는 도리혀 예상外 速하지않을가. 何故냐하면은 旣히 米大統領이 英佛을 정신적으로 원조한다는 것을 言明한 바와 같이 米英의 連絡線의 확보는 무엇보다 米國의 취할 제1의 방도이고 이것으로 인하야 獨伊의 英본토 공격이 목전에 迫하여 온 今日의 戰况으로 보아서 米國의 참전은 현저히 其 시기를 速하게 한 것이 되지 않을가.
三. 아즉 여행할 예정은 없으나 만일 여행하게 되면 王精衛을 비롯하야 新政府의 요인과 청년층의 유리한 인물을 맛나 보았으면은 합니다.

朝鮮新聞社長 金甲淳
拜復 大端 어려운 下問입니다. 淺見으로는
一. 歐洲大戰의 장래를 豫斷하기는 곤란하지만 獨의 상당히 强化堅實한 경제적 用意와 전격작전이 奏効하야 결국 歐洲에도 英佛의 落魄으로 신생활 질서가 건설되리라고 생각합니다.
二. 米는 주로 민족적 편견으로, 伊蘇는 利權關係로 참전할 것만 사실인데 伊蘇의 不勞自得的 야망으로 시기는 퍽 느저질 듯 합니다. 그러나 伊는 地中海 패권과 亞弗利加 때문에 3국중 제일 먼저 참전하겠지요. 其 시기는 2개월 이내라고나 할가요.

獨逸醫學搏士 鄭錫泰
今般 歐洲大戰은 즉 獨逸과 英國 양자간의 주력됨을 알어야하겠고 양자간의 어떤 측이 승전할가함에 있어서는 神이 안인 이상 확연한 明答을 못할 것은 물론이러니와, 역사상 과거 현재를 들어 예측을 하여본다면 필연적으로 英國측이 패배를 하여야 할 것이요, 또 神이 있어 소위 정의와 人道가 있다면 응당 英國은 今般 필히 천벌을 받으야만 정당타고 할 것이다. 其 이유를 간략히 열거하여 본다면
一. 英國이 과거 4백여 년 동안 식민지 착취하여온 역사로서는 其 방법이 정당하다고도 못할 것이요 人道를 버서난 가진 惡道를 발버왔으며, 英國민족이 겨우 3, 4천만에 지나지 못하면서 세계영토 4분지 1을 가지고도, 1억에 가까운 獨逸민족에게는 一步의 양보도 없이 여지없는 조약으로 생활력을 빼아스며, 英國 자기영토로서는 인종이 없어 천연과 자연의 財源를 썩여가면서까지 타민족의 입국을 엄금하며, 想像치 못할만한 高率의 重稅로서 타국의 물품까지 식민지에 수입치 않어 자기 殖民까지 고통을 주며, 印度 3억의 膏血을 뽑아다가, 소위 紳士(젠틀맨)의 호화스러운 體面를 갖게 하는 것은 도저히 인자한 神으로서도 필연코 노하지 않을 수 없을 것이요, 自古의 역사로서도 언제든지 續할 수 없는 운명은 吾人에게 암시를 주고서 있다.
二. 개인에게 예를 들어도 부유한 도런님으로서는 其 사상과 생활방법이 연약하고 貧歎한 자식으로서는 의지와 체격이 굳세고 튼튼한 것과 같이 부유한 英國으로서는 4백 여 년 단꿈에 거츠러운 풍파가 없이 언제든지 現像保管에 운명을 가지게 하고 쓰라린 운명의 배고푼 맛을 본 獨逸민족으로서는 자나깨나, 살어야만 하겠다는 불타는 결심으로 勇進을 하니 아모리 보이지 안는 신이라도 其 운명의 어느 측을 도둔하여야 겠는냐는 것을 가히 짐작을 할 것이요.
三. 昔日 기리시야나, 로마제국이 없어진 역사와 같이 一興一亡은 자연의 순서인즉 유독 영국만 언제든지 神이 助力할 理는 없어 보인다.
四. 운명론은 물론이나  獨逸은 절실히 민족으로나, 겔만정신으로나, 質노나, 數로나 英國에게 결코 부족되는 바가 없다. 설사 역사상으로 英國이 망할 期運이＜81＞ 未及이 되어 今般 승리를 본다하여도 나는 얼마 안 있어 필연적으로 영국이 망할 것을 확언한다. 역사상 수십년간이란 개인생활에 數分에 지나지 안는다.
五. 금일까지의 양자간 戰績으로 언명하는 바는 안이다. 나는 開戰시초로부터 이상의 이론으로 분명히 예측한 바와 틀림이 조금도 없다. 北歐, 푸란돌 戰績으로서도 역사상 전례가 없은 연합국이 敗北을 당하고, 불과 수일 내로 巴里도 독일에게로 도라가게 되였고, (독자가 본문을 볼 때는 巴里의 함락도 물론 과거로 될 것) 체칠수상이 神에게 기적을 애걸하게 되었은즉 발서 나는 운명의 좌우가 分岐로 생각된다.
伊太利는 물론 獨逸측으로 참전됨은 開戰부터 상식으로 판명될 것이다. 伊國운명이 독일과 꼭 같으며 만일에 독일 敗北을 한다면 伊國의 운명은 필연코 점차 敗運될 것이 물론인대 가령 참전 안된 이유는 伊國이 참전을 연기하는 것이 정치상으로나 實戰으로나 독일 측이 극히 유리한 (이유는 略)점이 많으며 미묘한 蘇聯간의 정세도 있으나 독일이 영국 본토 상륙시가 伊國 叅戰時가 안일가 한다.
米國은 소위 국제간 경찰권을 주창하려는, 괴상스러운 國이다. 영국 측의 참전을 내심으로는 희망하나 其 戰時를 노친 측이 다분히 많으며 日本제국의 무형의 위력이 米國 참전를 禁止하고 있다고 볼 수 있다. 즉 독일이 今般 快戰을 감행케 된 동기가 蘇聯, 伊 日本의 위력이라고도 할 수 있다.
