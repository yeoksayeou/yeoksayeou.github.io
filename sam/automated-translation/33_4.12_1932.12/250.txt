Article Title: 中國飛行學校 志願하는 故國靑年에게 (To the Youth of the Homeland Aspiring to Chinese Aviation Schools)

Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

To the Youth of the Homeland Aspiring to Chinese Aviation Schools
						Nanjing, Kim Ryŏn-gi

I reverently wish for the ever-increasing development of your esteemed Samch'ŏlli Company.

The reason I came to write this is that there are very many Korean youths who hold a great interest in the world of aviation and want to learn to fly airplanes, and occasionally letters come to me. Therefore, because there are many youths who want to learn to fly but don't know how best to go about it, I will tell the story of my experiences up to now, from becoming a second-class pilot in Japan to becoming a Lieutenant Colonel in Chinese aviation. This photo is of me, and this airplane is my cherished plane; it is a <64> fighter plane with a 575 horsepower engine.

For a Korean youth to become an aviator, one must be between the ages of 17 and 23, be in good health, pass the physical examination, and have an educational background equivalent to middle school graduation.

For a Korean youth to enter a Chinese aviation school, it might be a bit difficult due to the language barrier, but compared to Japan, it seems easier to enter. Chinese aviation schools are not civilian flight schools; they are Army Aviation Schools managed by the government.

Once admitted, you are a student whose expenses are covered by the school until graduation; the school pays and educates you. The graduation term is 2 years, and after graduation, you are sent out to the air corps.

Currently, there are Central Air Force Squadrons No. 1 through No. 7, so upon graduation, one gets assigned to various squadrons. Initially, as an Aviation Captain, the monthly salary is about 120 yuan. However, to enter a Chinese aviation school, one must first know Chinese. If you know Chinese and have a sponsor, you should be able to enter. There is the Hangzhou Central Aviation School, and there are also aviation schools in the Guangdong and Yunnan regions. Also, to obtain a second-class pilot's license from a Japanese civilian flight school, if you have 4,000 to 5,000 won for tuition and personal miscellaneous expenses, you can obtain the second-class pilot's license certificate. To graduate in Japan, you need your own assets, but if you enter a Chinese aviation school, you are a student with covered expenses and don't need money, provided you know Chinese. I entered the Japan Aviation School in Tachikawa-machi, Tokyo Prefecture, in 1926, obtained my second-class pilot's license certificate in 1929, and in 1930, I joined the Chinese Nationalist Government's Ministry of Military Affairs, Aviation First Squadron, as a Major Pilot. Around mid-April of this year, I was promoted to Lieutenant Colonel of the Aviation First Squadron. I also spent over 5,000 won to graduate as a second-class pilot in Japan. There are many mistakes in this writing, so please read with forgiveness. I am not a man of literature, just know that it was written by Kim Ryŏn-gi, a person of technical success, and please forgive the mistakes as you read. I will end here.

—(October 3rd)—
Dongchang Street, Nanjing, China
Aviation First Squadron, Kim Ryŏn-gi, Respectfully <65>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Ryŏn-gi, a Korean Lieutenant Colonel in the Chinese Air Force based in Nanjing, writes to encourage young Koreans interested in aviation. He contrasts the paths available: Japanese civilian schools require significant personal funds (4-5,000 won) but result in a second-class license, while Chinese military aviation schools (like the one in Hangzhou) cover all expenses but require knowledge of Chinese and offer a direct path to becoming an officer (Captain, 120 yuan/month salary) in the Chinese Air Force upon graduation after two years. He shares his own journey of studying in Japan (spending 5,000 won) before joining the Chinese forces and achieving his current rank. The article is interesting for historians as it highlights an alternative path for ambitious Koreans during the colonial period, bypassing the limitations within the Japanese system by seeking opportunities in China, specifically within its military structure. It reveals the transnational aspirations and networks some Koreans utilized, as well as the practical considerations (language, cost, connections) involved.

中國飛行學校 志願하는 故國靑年에게
						南京 金鍊器
貴三千里社 益益 발전하옵심을 仰祝하나이다.
이것을 쓰게된 것은 항상 조선청년으로서 航空界에 만흔 취미를 두고 비행기를 배우고 십다는 청년이 아주 만어 잇다금 마다 나에게로 편지가 오는데 그래서 나는 비행기를 배우고 십흔데 엇더케 하엿스면 조흘지 모르는 청년이 만키때문에 여지끗 지금까지의 나의 경험과 日本서 이등비행사가 되여가지고 中國航空 中佐가 될 때까지의 이약이를 하겟슴니다. 이 사진은 나의 것이며 이 비행기는 나의 愛用하는 비행기인데 發動機가 575馬力＜64＞ 전투기올시다.
조선청년으로서 飛行家가 될나면 年齡 17세로 23세까지 된 者 신체건강한 者로 체격검사에 합격될 者 입학정도는 중학졸업한 者외다.
조선청년으로서 중국항공학교에 입학할나면 言語不通이무로 좀 곤란이겟지만은 日本과 비하여서는 입학하기가 쉬울 뜻함니다. 중국항공학교는 민간비행학교라는 것이 업고 정부에서 경영하는 陸軍飛行學校임니다.
입학만하면 졸업할 때까지 校費生으로 학교에서 돈내여주며 공부시킴니다. 졸업기한은 2년이며 졸업하고나면 항공대로 내여보내지요.
지금 中央航空隊 第1隊에서 第7隊까지 잇스니가 졸업만 하고나면 各隊로 나오게 되지요. 처음에는 항공大尉로 1개월에 월급이 120여원이올시다. 그런데 중국항공학교에 입학할나면 먼저 中語를 알어야만 됨니다. 中語만 알으면 紹介人이나 잇고하면 입학할 수가 잇겟지요. 비행학교는 杭州中央航空學校가 잇스며 廣東雲南지방에도 비행학교가 잇지요. 그리고 日本民間飛行學校에서 이등비행사 免許狀을 得할나면 學費와 自己雜費 합하여 4,5千元만 잇스면 이등비행사 免許狀을 得할 수가 잇지요. 日本서 졸업할냐면 자기의 자산이 잇서야만되고 중국항공학교에 입학하면 校費生으로 金錢은 들지 안치만 中語만 알으면 입학할 수가 잇슴니다. 나는 1926년 東京府下立天町 日本飛行學校에 입학하여 1929년에 이등비행사 免許狀을 得하여가지고 1930년도에 中國國民政府 軍政部 航空 第一隊 少佐飛行機師로 入隊하엿슴니다. 今年 去 4월 중순경에 항공 제1대 中佐로 昇格되엿지요. 나 역시 日本서 이등비행사까지 5천여원을 들려 졸업하엿슴니다. 이 글 가운데 잘못된 말도 만흐니 용서하시고 보시요. 나는 문학가도 안이고 단지 기술적 성공인 金鍊器가 쓴 것인 줄만 알고 잘못된 말을 용서하시고 보시요. 이만으로 끗침니다.
―(10월 3일)―
中國南京 東廠街
航空第一隊 金鍊器 拜＜65＞
