Company Announcement
						Samch'ŏlli Company Editor

Regarding a certain issue (某문제) - a long treatise (長篇論文) by Cho Man-sik (曹晩植)
Same topic - by Sŏ Sang-il (徐相日)
Same topic - by Wŏn Se-hun (元世勳)
A critical biography (評傳) of Colonel Pak Il-liya (朴일니야大佐) of Siberia - by Ch'o Sa (草士)
Impressions (印象) of Kaizō (改造) President Yamamoto (山本) - by Yun Paeng-nam (尹白南)
My Shanghai (上海) Days - by Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨)
A Discourse on Actresses of the New Era - by Sin Pul-ch'ul (申不出)
The First Prize Substituted by Blood - by Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求)
Two Poems (詩歌二篇) - by Sin Pul-ch'ul (申不出)
Other 10-odd topics omitted (其他 10餘題 省略)
etc.

We announce with apologies (謝告하오며) to our various writers (筆者諸氏) and readers that approximately 30 articles, amounting to 150 pages of manuscript, including those listed above, unavoidably could not be published due to certain circumstances (모종의 사정). Furthermore, because of these circumstances, we had to suddenly (truly, in the two days from October 26th) prepare replacement manuscripts to submit for censorship (검열), resulting in this month's issue's contents becoming meager (빈약하게 되엇슴과), and the publication date unavoidably becoming late like this (이가치 늣게 되엇슴을). We deeply apologize (深謝하나이다). The coming December issue will be prepared well in advance and put on sale around November 25th. Esteemed readers (江湖諸賢), please understand deeply (深諒하여주옵소서).
Respectfully, The Editor of Samch'ŏlli Company (三千里社 主幹拜)＜25＞

**Summary**

The editor of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine apologizes to writers and readers for the unavoidable removal of approximately 30 articles (150 pages) intended for the current issue (Vol. 4, No. 12, Dec 1932) due to "certain circumstances." This necessitated the hasty compilation of replacement content over two days (Oct 26-28) for censorship, leading to a meager issue and publication delays. The editor promises the next issue will be prepared earlier and released around November 25th. For historians, this announcement is significant as it explicitly mentions censorship ("검열") and the euphemism "certain circumstances" (모종의 사정) commonly used to refer to pressure or censorship by the Japanese colonial authorities. It highlights the difficult publishing environment faced by Korean intellectuals and media during this period, the last-minute scrambles to fill pages after censorship, and the direct impact on content quality and publication schedules. The list of censored authors and topics (including prominent figures like Cho Man-sik and Yŏ Un-hyŏng) provides insight into potentially sensitive subjects at the time.
