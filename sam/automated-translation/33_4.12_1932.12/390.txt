A Desolate Evening
						Kim Tongin (金東仁)

He was of medium height. Though not large, his body looked sturdy. His eyes were calm, but when opened wide, they seemed capable of showing a sharp glint.
By any measure, he looked like a bold man.
As Tu-sik stood blankly trembling before this intruder, the intruder bowed his waist once more.
"Excuse me. In the middle of the night..."
At this attitude devoid of any hostility, Tu-sik, for the first time, slightly regained his senses. Then his eyes fell upon the name card the intruder held in his hand. And from that name card, Tu-sik learned that this intruder was Detective Kim Ch'an-su of the T Police Station.
"A detective..."
After seeing the name card, Tu-sik muttered softly to himself. However, as he identified the intruder from the name card and knew that the intruder was certainly not someone who would commit an unlawful act, anger towards the man gradually began to rise in Tu-sik's heart.
"A detective..."
Muttering softly once more, Tu-sik lifted his eyes sharply. And he cast his eyes upon the detective's face as if reprimanding him.
"A detective committing unlawful entry—"
"Yes, I am not unaware that it is an inappropriate act. But—"
"Unlawful entry— So it's right to recklessly enter a law-abiding citizen's house?"
"Yes, I am not unaware, but—" <91>
Tu-sik repeatedly cut off the words intending to offer continuous excuses.
"I will call the station chief about your conduct, so leave this house at once."
"Yes, just one word—"
"Leave quickly..."
"Just a moment."
"Leave! If you don't leave—"
Tu-sik stepped back a couple of paces. And he raised his arm to press the doorbell.
However, before Tu-sik's hand could reach the doorbell, the detective's arm first grabbed Tu-sik's hand.
"Wait a moment."
It was more of a stern command than a request. The detective's gleaming eyes met Tu-sik's directly.
"Look here. I admit that I have overstepped my authority. Wouldn't it suffice if you call the station chief even tomorrow morning and have me punished? As for me, having come this far, even committing an act beyond my authority, isn't it clear I won't just turn back? Earlier, I heard strange pistol shots several times near the detached house (별당) of this residence. After that, I saw lights moving back and forth here. Clearly, some incident has occurred. However— well— well— for matters of little consequence, such households often just resolve them internally (색여버리다), and there are many cases where they don't bother reporting them to the police. I don't know about this incident, but perhaps it's something the household intended to resolve internally and not report to the police. But from our position as police officers, if any incident occurs, we must investigate it fully so that if another incident arises later, it will be easier to figure out the connections and systems. Therefore, we must investigate every single minor incident without overlooking any. That's why I came in to investigate, even though it might be an incident the household intended to resolve internally. Also, seeking an interview through ordinary means in the middle of the night would certainly not succeed, which is why I climbed over the wall and came in stealthily. And—"
The detective released Tu-sik's hand, which he had been holding until now. And he lowered his voice slightly.
"Also— let me say. I cannot yet guess what kind of incident this is, but if this incident is indeed one to be resolved internally by the household, then perhaps— couldn't this household make use of me? That is, if I were to return to the station and report that such-and-such happened at a certain residence last night, this incident would inevitably become public. But if you and I reach an agreement here, I won't report it to the station and can investigate the incident secretly. If it seems like a dangerous matter, there might also be a way to secretly prevent it, or if it's an undesirable matter, couldn't restraints be applied? Well, how about it?"
In other words, Detective Kim Ch'an-su was suggesting that if Tu-sik <92> overlooked the unlawful entry and allowed him to investigate quietly, they could have a second negotiation based on the results of the investigation; he was offering to play the role of a secret detective for this incident.
Tu-sik interpreted the undertone of the detective's words as implying a demand for monetary reward. He stared at the detective's face for a moment, then, still looking displeased, said,
"Still, in the middle of the night—"
As he was about to scold further, the detective bowed his waist again.
"Regarding entering in the middle of the night, I apologize ten million times."

An agreement was finally reached between Tu-sik and Ch'an-su. As Ch'an-su bowed several more times requesting an agreement, Tu-sik finally consented. No, rather, Tu-sik himself, who had been struggling with how to handle this incident, considered the appearance of this figure named Ch'an-su as fortunate and agreed to the compromise. This incident was clearly a serious one. Tu-sik could not possibly handle it alone. However, reporting it to the police also had points he was reluctant about. He couldn't know what kind of person Kim Tŏk-sam's son was, but revealing everything might uncover his own father's entire history of tyranny (暴虐史) from the first half of his life. To avoid revealing such things, he would have to pretend not to know about the person claiming to be Kim Tŏk-sam's son and cover it up. If he covered it up, who knew what kind of incident might occur later.
It was at this point, where Tu-sik could neither turn right nor left, that the figure named Kim Ch'an-su rushed in. Kim Ch'an-su proposed to investigate this incident secretly.
Having reached an agreement with Ch'an-su here, Tu-sik explained the entire sequence of the strange events that occurred last night to Ch'an-su. Furthermore, he even told him about the causal relationship between Kim Tŏk-sam and the old master, as heard from Wangbo.
After hearing Tu-sik's full explanation, jotting down the key points in his notebook, and bowing his head in thought for a moment, Ch'an-su looked up.
"Please tell me one more thing."
"What is it?"
"That—"
Ch'an-su exhaled a long breath through his nose.
"That— this household sold a lot of land to buy foreign bonds, didn't you?"
"?!"
Tu-sik was startled. It was an exceedingly strange question. It was undeniably true that they had sold land to buy foreign bonds. However, only two or three people knew about it, and there was no reason Ch'an-su should know.
Ch'an-su seemed to understand why Tu-sik was surprised. <93>
"My profession being what it is, I know all such things. How much have you bought so far?"
"..."
"Is it around 100,000 won?"
"What connection does that have with this incident?"
"Well, I don't know. It might be related. I cannot assert that it is unrelated either. In any case, since you trust me and entrust this matter to me, don't I need to know all the circumstances of this household?"
"..."
"Up to what amount do you plan to convert land into bonds?"
"Gradually, the plan is to sell all the land."
"And entirely into bonds or stocks?"
"Yes."
"Why? For what reason?"
"—I must decline to answer that."
Tu-sik chuckled softly. Ch'an-su also smiled slightly.
"How much have you converted now?"
"Up to recently, about 150,000 won. In about 4 or 5 days, it looks like it will be double that amount."
"Did you keep those bonds or stock certificates in the detached house, or did you keep them here?"
"They are kept here in this Western-style building, but I cannot tell you exactly where they are kept."
"4 or 5 days later... 4 or 5 days later..."
Ch'an-su bowed his head for a while, seemingly thinking about something.
"Perhaps, don't you think the strange man who came earlier came to steal those?"
"Well. If he intended to steal those, he should have come to me, why would he go to the detached house where my father is? Besides, there would be no need for him to reveal his own name, calling himself Kim Tŏk-sam's son."
At this, Ch'an-su folded his notebook, put it in his pocket, and readily stood up.
"Excuse my intrusion in the middle of the night. By the way, could you lend me a servant? I need to go to the detached house and investigate."
Tu-sik called a servant and had him guide Ch'an-su to the detached house and sent him off.

After sending Ch'an-su off, Tu-sik locked the door, returned to his bed, and threw himself down. Extreme fatigue overwhelmed his body.
Tu-sik, who had lit a cigarette, couldn't even finish smoking it and simply dropped it, falling asleep without even taking off his clothes.

Deeply immersed in a heavy sleep, Tu-sik heard a tapping sound in his sleep. Focusing his drowsy mind on the sound, he realized it was someone knocking on his bedroom door.
"Who is it, in the middle of the night?"
Pulling the watch from his pocket as if tired, he saw it was no longer night. The watch indicated 8:20. <94>
Tu-sik got out of bed. Rubbing his eyes, he went to the door, turned the lock, and opened it. Outside, a servant stood holding a name card on a wooden tray.
"Someone like this has come asking to see you."
Detective Kim Ch'an-su, T Police Station— The name card was the same as the one received last night.
"Tell him I haven't woken up yet, tell him to come back in about two hours."
Extremely tired, Tu-sik said only this, closed the door again, returned to bed, and lay down. The moment he lay down, he was about to fall back asleep.
However, just as he was about to drift off, he heard the sound of knocking again.
"Who is it?"
The door opened. The same servant entered again.
"He says there's an urgent matter and he'll leave after seeing you for just 3 minutes, he won't leave."
"What a bother—"
But at this moment, Tu-sik suddenly sat bolt upright. When Ch'an-su, who had left three hours ago, had already returned and insisted on meeting him urgently, there must truly be some urgent matter. Perhaps he had already found some clue.
"Alright, go down and tell him to wait in the drawing room. I'll be right down."
Having sent the servant back down, Tu-sik changed out of the clothes he had slept in, which were now wrinkled, into new ones and went downstairs.
Tu-sik went to the drawing room. And with a hand trembling from fatigue, he opened the drawing room door.
"?"
Inside the drawing room, an unfamiliar young man was sitting slumped in a chair, who then stood up and bowed. Ch'an-su was nowhere to be seen.
Tu-sik, who had brought down the name card received from the servant earlier, quickly turned around towards the servant, showed him the card, and asked,
"Where is this gentleman?"
The servant bowed his head.—
"That gentleman over there is him."
"That gentleman?"
In an instant, all the blood in his body rushed to his head. Tu-sik slammed shut the drawing room door, which he had opened halfway. Then, putting his mouth close to the servant's ear, he said,
"Listen, stand guard here until I finish making a phone call, and if that person inside tries to run, beat him and catch him without mercy."
After giving the instruction, he quickly went to the telephone on the other side.
He called the T Police Station. However, the station chief had not yet arrived. So he asked for the duty inspector (경부) to come to the phone.
"This is U Tu-sik. I have something to ask."
Tu-sik spoke into the phone towards the police station with a trembling voice. The inspector answered.—
"Yes, what can I do for you?"
"Is there a detective named Kim Ch'an-su at the station?"
"There is."
"Could you please describe his appearance? <95> It's nothing else, but the Detective Kim Ch'an-su who came yesterday and the Kim Ch'an-su who came this morning are different people."
"Kim Ch'an-su is lanky and tall."
"Tall?"
"Yes, his complexion is dark; he wears glasses; he has a Chaplin-style mustache below his nose; and also—"
Tu-sik hung up the phone without fully listening to the inspector's continuing description.
The person currently sitting in the drawing room undoubtedly matched the inspector's description of Kim Ch'an-su. Though seen only briefly, he was the owner of glasses, a Chaplin mustache, and a dark complexion.
Then who was the person who came last night?
The mysterious man from last night, who had no mustache, wore no glasses, was only of medium height, and had a relatively fair complexion— who indeed was he? At the very least, he was not Detective Kim Ch'an-su of the T Police Station. Yet, carrying Kim Ch'an-su's name card, that mysterious man who had persistently pried into the details of the incident— who was he? Standing blankly for a moment, Tu-sik slammed his fist on the desk. A servant ran up.
"Go upstairs, on top of my cabinet, a name card—"
Tu-sik held out the name card in his hand.—
"There's a name card exactly like this one, bring it down at once." And before the servant returned, he quickly went up to the staircase to meet the servant halfway. Then, holding the two name cards, he rushed to the drawing room.
"Are you Mr. Kim Ch'an-su?"
He asked, even panting for breath.
"I am Kim Ch'an-su."
"This is the name card you brought, right?"
Tu-sik placed one name card on the table.
"Yes."
"And this one?"
Tu-sik placed the other name card on the table.
The detective's eyes widened. The detective looked back and forth between the name cards and Tu-sik's face several times.
"Look here, at 4:30 this morning, a person carrying this name card came looking for me."
When Tu-sik followed up with this explanation, Ch'an-su also seemed quite surprised.
"What? That's—"
He half-rose from his seat.
"Yes, at 4:30 in the morning, unlawfully entering the premises without permission—" Ch'an-su picked up both name cards. He looked at them intently for a long time. When he put the name cards back down and raised his eyes, his face had returned to composure.
"I know him. The person who brought the name card—"
"You know him? Is he your friend, a detective?"
"No, sir."
"Then? An ordinary robber?"
"No, sir."
"Then what?"
"An important figure with an ideological background (사상적 배경을 가진 증대한 인물)— it's a man named Sŏ Wŏn-dŏk (徐元德), who arrived in the capital by the night train the day before yesterday. Medium height, gleaming eyes— isn't that right?"
As Tu-sik nodded there, Ch'an-su calmly closed his eyes as if deep in thought. (Continued) <96>

**Summary**

A man named Tu-sik is visited late at night by someone claiming to be Detective Kim Ch'an-su, investigating gunshots heard near Tu-sik's family home. This imposter extracts information about the incident, the family's history (involving someone named Kim Tŏk-sam), and their recent conversion of land assets into foreign bonds. The next morning, the real Detective Kim Ch'an-su arrives. Comparing name cards reveals the previous night's visitor was an imposter, identified by the real detective as Sŏ Wŏn-dŏk, a recently arrived "important figure with an ideological background." The story ends on this cliffhanger, leaving the motives of Sŏ Wŏn-dŏk and the nature of the "incident" unresolved.

For historians, the story offers glimpses into colonial Korean society: the anxiety surrounding wealth management (shifting from land to potentially more portable/liquid foreign bonds), the power and potential menace of the colonial police force (even when impersonated), and the underlying tension suggested by the term "ideological background," likely hinting at anti-colonial or socialist movements perceived as dangerous by the authorities. The mention of a father's "history of tyranny" also points to lingering social conflicts and historical grievances.
