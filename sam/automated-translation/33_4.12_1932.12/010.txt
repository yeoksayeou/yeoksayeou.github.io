Publication Date: 1932년 12월 01일
Article Title: 이땅우에 둥주리치라 (Build Your Nest Upon This Land)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Build Your Nest Upon This Land
						P'ain

Until this island sinks into the sea,
These descendants will protect it and protect it again.
Oh, eagle, build your nest upon this land,
Build your nest with peace of mind.

Who knows not how to bow its head to others,
Who knows not how to be chased by others,
When your strong seed spreads to every nook and cranny,
Oh, that cloud covering the sky shall be lifted.

Outside the Castle Gate (城門), dung, dung, dung, that booming drum sound,
That sound is the sound that tells us we are alive.
Though there may be days the sun does not rise, there will be no day that drum sound ceases.
Oh, eagle, build your nest upon this land with peace of mind.
—1932. 12. 1 P'ain—<5>

**Summary**

This poem is a powerful call for the Korean spirit, symbolized by the resilient eagle ("수리개"), to remain steadfast and establish itself firmly upon its land ("이땅우에 둥주리치라"). It expresses unwavering determination to protect the land and its lineage ("이자손들은 직히고 또 직히오리니") and foresees a future liberation when the oppressive 'cloud' lifts ("하늘에 덥힌 저구름 거더지오리다"). The persistent drumbeat symbolizes the enduring life force and resistance of the people. From a historical perspective, the poem is significant for its overt expression of nationalistic sentiment and hope during the Japanese colonial period. Published in a popular magazine, its use of potent symbols like the eagle and the unceasing drumbeat to articulate defiance and the longing for freedom, despite censorship, would be of great interest to historians studying cultural resistance and national consciousness under colonial rule.

이땅우에 둥주리치라
						파인
이섬이 바다물속에 잠길때까지
이자손들은 직히고 또 직히오리니
오, 수리개여 이땅우에 둥주리 칠지어다
마음노코 둥주리 칠지어다

남에게 머리숙일 줄 모르는
남에게 쫏김을 밧을 줄 모르는
굿센 당신의 씨가 방방곡곡에 펴질때
오, 하늘에 덥힌 저구름 거더지오리다

城門박게 둥, 둥, 둥 울니는 저 북소리
저소리는 우리들이 사랏단 소리라
해는 솟지안는 날 잇서도 저북소리 끗는 날 업스리니
오, 수리개여 마음노코 이땅우에 둥주리 치라
―1932. 12. 1 파인―＜5＞
