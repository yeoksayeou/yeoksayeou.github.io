Notice Regarding Sending Money to Samch'ŏllisa

As the Samch'ŏllisa (三千里社) has this time published a new magazine called "Man'guk Puin" (萬國婦人), we are currently in the process of revising the company's transfer account name (振替名義) with the Communications Bureau (遞信局). Therefore, for the time being, everyone sending transfer funds (振替送金) to either Samch'ŏllisa or the Man'guk Puin Department should please make the payment (拂込) to "Transfer Kyŏngsŏng 4284, Man'guk Puin Sa" (振替京城四二八四番 萬國婦人社). <특별8>

**Summary**

This short notice informs readers that due to the launch of a new magazine, "Man'guk Puin," by Samch'ŏllisa, the company is updating its postal transfer account details with the Communications Bureau. Readers wishing to send money to either Samch'ŏllisa or the new magazine are instructed to use the temporary account name "Transfer Kyŏngsŏng 4284, Man'guk Puin Sa" for the time being. This article provides a small window into the practical business operations of a Korean publishing house during the colonial period, including interactions with the colonial administrative body (Communications Bureau) and the specific financial mechanisms like the postal transfer system (振替) used for subscriptions or payments. The launch of a women's magazine ("Man'guk Puin") also reflects the expanding scope of the publisher.
