Our Cherished Sentiment
The Princess's Notdari

In the land of Andong (安東), North Kyŏngsang Province, on the night of the first full moon of the year, they play a game called "Notdari." Groups of beautifully dressed young brides and maidens gather, singing various songs with refined voices as they roam the streets. Then, when the night deepens and becomes quiet, the maidens all assemble in one place. They stand in single file, bending their waists to form the shape of a bridge. Above them, an old woman stands astride facing two directions, and has one woman walk across the bridge of people. While doing this, all the maidens sing with beautiful voices and play until dawn approaches. This is the Notdari game. During this game, a song called the "Notdari Song" is invariably sung. The origin of this song dates back about five hundred and sixty years ago, during the Koryŏ Dynasty (高麗朝), when the thirty-first king, King Kongmin (恭愍王), came fleeing to Andong in Kyŏngsang Province with a princess (王女). At that time, the people of that village, regardless of age or gender, all went out to respectfully welcome them. As a sign of their devotion (赤誠), they made a human bridge out of flower-like young maidens and had the princess walk across it. It is said that ever since then, the women of this village have always performed this game as an annual event in commemoration of the princess's visit. Now, let us write down the Notdari song:

What bridge is this bridge?
It's the bronze bridge of Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn.

How long is the bridge?
I've come across fifty-five spans.

Whose land is this land?
It is the King's land.

What roof tile is this tile?
It's the jade tile of Upper Stream Valley (上溪谷).

Where did the guest come from?
I came from Kyŏngsang Province.

For what reason did you come?
I came here while fighting.

What clothes did you come wearing?
I came wearing a fine ramie jacket.

What shoes did you come wearing?
I came wearing *nobare* shoes.

What horse did you come riding?
I came riding a white horse.<52>

**Summary**

The article describes the "Notdari" folk game played by women in Andong, North Kyŏngsang Province, on the first full moon of the lunar year. It details how women form a human bridge for another woman (originally a princess) to walk across, accompanied by singing the "Notdari Song." The tradition is traced back to the Koryŏ Dynasty, specifically when King Kongmin and a princess sought refuge in Andong, and villagers created this human bridge as a gesture of welcome and respect. The lyrics of the accompanying song are also provided. For historians, this piece offers valuable documentation of a specific Korean folk tradition, including song lyrics, during the Japanese colonial period. It showcases the persistence of cultural practices tied to pre-colonial Korean history (Koryŏ Dynasty) and reflects an effort to preserve and cherish native Korean sentiment ("우리 情調") amidst external pressures. The specific details of the game and its origin story provide insight into local history and folklore transmission.

그리운 우리 情調
王女의 놋다리
경상북도 안동(安東)땅에 가면 정월보름날밤에 「놋다리」라는 노름을 한다. 아름답게 차린 각씨와 색시들이 무리를 지어 여러 가지 노래를 목청을 가다듬어 가며 부르면서 거리거리를 도라다닌다. 그러다가 밤이 깁허 고요할 때에 그 색시들은 모다 한 곳에 집합하여 색시들이 일렬종대로 서서 마치 다리모양으로 허리를 굽힌 뒤 그 우에 노파가 두쪽으로 마주서서 녀자 한아를 식혀 그사람 다리우로 건너게 한다. 이리하면서 모든 색시들은 아름다운 목성으로 새벽이 갓갑도록 노래부르며 노는 것이다. 이것이 놋다리노리다. 이 노리 때에는 반드시 「놋다리 노래」라는 노래가 불니운다. 이 노래의 유래는 지금부터 오백륙십여년전 고려조(高麗朝) 제삼십일대의 공민왕(恭愍王)이 왕녀와 함께 경상도 안동으로 피난왓섯슬 때 그 마을 백성들이 남녀노소할 것 업시 모다 나아가서 봉영하면서 적성(赤誠)을 표하는 이미로 꼿가튼 절문 처녀들로 사람다리를 만들고 그우로 왕녀를 거러 지나시게 하엿다 하는데 그 뒤에도 늘 이마을 녀자들은 왕녀왓든 긔념으로 년중행사 모양으로 늘 이 노리를 한다고 한다. 이제 놋다리노리를 적어보면

淸溪川의 구리다리

다리 기리는 얼마인고
쉰다섯간 밟고 왓네

이땅은 늬 땅이신고
그것은 임금임 땅이라

이 기와 무슨 기와
上溪谷의 옥기와라

경상도에서 왓노라

싸홈하면서 여기 왓네

고이적삼 입고 왓소

무슨 신을 신고 왓노
노바레를 신고 왓소

무슨 말을 타고 왓노
