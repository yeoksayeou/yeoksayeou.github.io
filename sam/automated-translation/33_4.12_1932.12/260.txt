Nostalgic Korean Sentiment
Bridge-Treading on the Day of the Great Full Moon

The custom of treading bridges (dari pomnŭn nori) on the day of the first full moon! This is a custom that can be seen in various places in Chosŏn. On this night, it is said that if one treads on a bridge, one's legs will be healthy throughout the year, one will not suffer from leg ailments, and furthermore, misfortune will be averted, so men and women, young and old, eagerly engage in bridge-treading.

Although it originated from superstition, the sight of throngs of men and women, young and old, treading bridges, coming and going under the brilliant moonlight, must also be one of the beautiful things that bring Chosŏn sentiment (朝鮮情調) to life.

It is said that bridge-treading is still most prevalent in Kaesŏng (開城). <65>

**Summary**

The article describes the traditional Korean custom of "Dari Bopgi" (다리봅기, bridge-treading), practiced on the Daeboreum (the first full moon of the lunar year). Participants believed walking on bridges this night ensured healthy legs and prevented misfortune for the coming year. The author acknowledges the custom's superstitious roots but emphasizes its beauty as an expression of Korean sentiment (Chosŏn chŏngjo), particularly the scene of people under the moonlight. It notes that the custom was especially popular in Kaesŏng at the time of writing (1932). For historians, this piece documents a specific folk tradition during the Japanese colonial period, reflecting both the persistence of customs and a contemporary interest in defining and appreciating uniquely Korean cultural expressions ("Chosŏn chŏngjo") amidst colonial pressures.

그리운 우리 情調
대보름날의 다리봅기
정월 보름날에 다리봅는 노리! 이것은 조선의 처처에서 볼 수 잇는 풍속이다. 이날밤에 다리를 밟으면 일년 중에 다리가 건강하여 다릿병에 걸니는 일이 업고 또 흉사가 업다하여 남녀노소업시 다리밟기를 몹시 한다.
미신에서 출발한 일이나 밝은 달밤 다리밟기하는 남녀노소의 일대가 교교한 월색아래로 오고가고하는 그 광경도 조선정조를 살니는 아름다운 일 중의 하나일 것이다.
다리봅기는 지금도 개성(開城)이 가장 성하다든가.＜65＞
