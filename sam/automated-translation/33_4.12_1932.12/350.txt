Magazine Title: 三千里 제4권 제12호
Publication Date: 1932년 12월 01일
Article Title: 文壇雜話 (Literary Miscellany/Gossip)
Author: Unstated
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Professor Yang Chu-dong of Sungsil College recently led Sungsil students on a trip to Japan, where they visited the Fuji Spinning mill and returned. He reportedly shuddered upon witnessing the sight of the giant machinery, the sole sacred object of capitalism, pounding away, and beneath it, some 3,000 female workers with pale faces, as if in the third stage of tuberculosis, working silently all day long, recalling the "Sad History of Female Factory Workers" (女工哀史).

Ch'unwŏn went to Tokyo on business for the Tong-a Ilbo Company and returned to Chosŏn the other day. While in Tokyo, through the arrangement of President Yamamoto of Kaizōsha, he spent an evening banqueting with literary figures such as Fujimori Seikichi, Kikuchi Kan, Yoshida Genjirō, and Yamamoto Yūzō.

Mr. Chang Hyŏk-chu, who has recently<84> entered the Japanese-language literary scene via "Kaizō," had his third published piece, a short story titled "The Pursued Herd" (追はれて行く群れ), included in Kaizō. However, that piece touched upon taboo subjects, and its sale was banned within Chosŏn.
On another note, self-proclaimed genius writers who lament that Chosŏn is too small and there's no way to make a living through writing should stop merely sighing in their heated ondol rooms and advance into the Japanese literary scene instead. Even advancing to the extent of Mr. Chang Hyŏk-chu guarantees living expenses.
It's because there's a lack of truly talented people; if one genuinely possesses talent, rest assured that even in this harsh modern world that fails to recognize people, a way to live will be provided.

Regarding the sculptor Kim Pok-chin, who is currently imprisoned, his release date was thought to be this autumn, but it is still far off, apparently set for the spring two years from now.<85>
<84-85>

**Summary**

This article compiles several news items from the Korean literary scene in late 1932. It reports on Professor Yang Chu-dong's disturbing observations of female factory workers at Fuji Spinning in Japan, Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su)'s networking trip to Tokyo where he met prominent Japanese writers, and the censorship faced by Chang Hyŏk-chu whose Japanese-language story published in "Kaizō" was banned in Korea. The article encourages struggling Korean writers to seek opportunities in the Japanese literary market, suggesting talent will eventually be recognized, and concludes with an update on the delayed prison release of sculptor Kim Pok-chin. From a historical perspective, the piece offers insights into the colonial reality: the stark contrast between Japanese industrial might and the exploitation of (likely Korean and Japanese) female labor, the complex cultural interactions and power dynamics between Korean and Japanese intellectuals, the tangible impact of Japanese censorship extending even to works published in Japan if deemed problematic for Korea, and the economic pressures forcing Korean artists and writers to look towards the colonizer's market for survival.

文壇雜話
崇專敎授 梁柱東氏가 지난번에 崇專학생을 引率하고 日本드러가서 富士紡績을 구경하고 도라나왓다. 그 공장에서 자본주의 유일 神主인 거대한 기계가 쿵쿵쿵 굴너가는 光景과 그 기계 밋헤서 3천여명 女工이 肺病 第三期나 된 듯히 蒼色한 얼골을 하여가지고 終日 黙黙히 일하고 잇는 그 모양을 보고나서 실로 慄然하면서 「女工哀史」를 회상하엿다고 한다.

春園은 東亞日報社의 일을 띄고 東京 드러갓다가 日前에 조선 나왓다. 東京서 山本 改造社長의 斡旋으로 藤森成吉, 菊池寬, 吉田絃次郞 ,山本有三 등 文人과 一夕의 宴을 가치하엿다고.

「改造」를 통하야 日本語 文壇으로 최근에＜84＞ 진출하고 잇는 張赫宙씨는 이번에 第三次의 발표로 「追はれて行く群れ」라는 단편을 改造에 실엇든 중 그 一篇이 忌諱에 觸하야 조선안에서는 發賣禁止가 되엿다.
딴말이나 조선이 좁아서 글을 가지고는 사라갈 길이 업다고 탄식을 吐하는 自稱 천재작가들은 溫突房에서 한갓 탄식만 말고 日本文壇으로라도 진출하여라. 張赫宙씨 정도로 진출하여도 생활비는 保障밧는다.
才人이 업서 그러치 정말 自己에게 才分이 잇다면 아무리 사람을 몰나보는 刻薄한 現世라도 사라갈 갈을 주리라 안심하라.

在獄중의 各 조각가 金復鎭의 出獄期를 今秋인 줄 알엇더니 아직도 前途 遠하야 明後年 봄철이라고.＜85＞
＜84-85＞
