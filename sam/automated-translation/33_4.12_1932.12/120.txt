Our Cherished Sentiment
Young Women Dancing the "Kanggangsuwŏllae"

This is a custom prevalent in Chŏlla Province (전라도)—particularly in the coastal regions of South Chŏlla Province (전라남도). When the bright moon shines like daylight, or on the full moon, groups of twenty, thirty, or sometimes forty or fifty young women gather to dance while singing the song "Kanggangsuwŏllae" (强羌水越來). The phrase Kanggangsuwŏllae dates back about 330 years to the time of the Imjin Disturbance (임진란리), when Naval Commander Admiral Yi Sunsin (李舜臣), Duke of Loyalty and Chivalry, led sailors in naval battles (海戰) against the forces of Toyotomi Hideyoshi (豊臣秀吉). To instill hostility towards the enemy in the common people and simultaneously support the departing soldiers, women from near the battlefields gathered in groups of several dozen, went up into the mountains, lit fires in various places, and danced in circles while singing the song of Kanggangsuwŏllae, it is said.

Originating from this, young women from the vicinity of those former battle sites, to commemorate and remember that time, seek out the seasons when the five grains ripen and, under the bright moonlit nights, perform the Kanggangsuwŏllae song and dance, they say.

Even now, around the time of the eighth lunar month's full moon, if you go to the South Chŏlla Province (전라남도) area, you can find and see this beautiful custom in various places. <40>

**Summary**

This article describes the traditional Korean folk dance and song "Kanggangsuwŏllae," primarily performed by young women in the coastal areas of South Chŏlla Province under the full moon. It attributes the origin of the custom to the Imjin War (1592-1598), suggesting it began as a tactic devised under Admiral Yi Sunsin where women gathered on hillsides, lit fires, and sang to make Korean forces appear larger and to boost morale against the invading Japanese armies of Toyotomi Hideyoshi. The custom is presented as a living tradition commemorating this historical resistance. From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its explicit celebration of a Korean tradition rooted in national defense against a past Japanese invasion, published during the Japanese colonial period (1932). It highlights the persistence of Korean cultural identity and historical memory, focusing on a figure (Yi Sunsin) symbolizing resistance against Japan, which could be seen as a subtle form of cultural nationalism tolerated or overlooked by censors.

그리운 우리 情調
색씨들이 춤추는 「강강수월래」
이것은 전라도―그 중에도 전라남도 해안지방에서 만히 성행되는 풍속인데 밝은 달이 대낫과 갓치 또는 보름이 되면 이십명, 삼십명, 혹은 사오십명씩의 색시를이 무리를 지어 춤추면서 강강수월래(强羌水越來)라는 노래를 부른다. 강강수월래라함은 지금부터 약 삼백삼심년전 임진란리때에 수군통제사 충무공 리순신(李舜臣)이 수병을 잇글고 풍신수길(豊臣秀吉) 군사와 해전(海戰)을 할 때에 일반백성들에게 적개심을 너허줌과 동시에 출정인을 응원하기 위하야 그 싸홈터 근방의 녀자들이 수십명씩 무리를 지어 산으로 올나가서 곳곳에 불을 피우고 빙빙 춤을 추면서 강강수월래의 노래를 불넛다 한다.
이것이 원인이 되어 그뒤로는 그 전란지 부근의 색시들이 그 당시를 긔념하여 추억하기 위하여 오곡이 익어가는 철긔철긔를 차저 달박은 밤을 타서는 강강수월래 노래춤을 춘다고 한다.
지금도 팔월 보름끼에 전라남도 일대에 가보면 아름다운 이 풍속을 처처에서 치저 볼 수 잇다.＜40＞
