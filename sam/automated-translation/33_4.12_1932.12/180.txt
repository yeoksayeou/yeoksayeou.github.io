**Summary**

This article is a company announcement from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* detailing the establishment of three new branch offices: one in Bongcheon (Mukden, Manchuria), one in Wonsan (Korea), and a general branch office covering the Hokuriku Line area in Fukui, Japan. It lists the locations, key personnel (branch managers, general affairs staff, reporters, advisors), and contact information (telephone for Wonsan). This piece is interesting for historians as it demonstrates the expanding reach and organizational structure of a significant Korean cultural magazine during the Japanese colonial period. The establishment of branches not only within Korea but also in Manchuria (a major destination for Korean migration and activity) and Japan itself highlights the transnational scope of Korean media and business networks, even under colonial constraints.
