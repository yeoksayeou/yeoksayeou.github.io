**Body Text Translation**

Chongno Intersection

Mr. Nakano Seigō (中野正剛), who came from Tōkyō, recently gave a lecture at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall (京城公會堂). Prior to this, Mr. Yŏ Unhong (呂運弘) met him at the Chosŏn Hotel and, regarding Nakano's personally held principle of Greater Asianism (大亞細亞主義), asked him in English, "Asianism is fine, but isn't that an Asianism centered on [Japan]?"
He then critically questioned the theory of a Japan-Korea-Manchuria Economic Bloc (日鮮滿經濟뿌록說), but Mr. Nakano just trailed off his words.

Only the Tong-A Ilbo (東亞日報) welcomed Kim Ŭnbae (金恩培), who returned from the Olympic Games, and only the Tong-A Ilbo made a fuss.
Regarding his departure and return, what deep meaning was there that the Tong-A [Ilbo] poured such passion into this? Concerning that, a certain social movement organization sent a scathing letter of inquiry, and the reply stated:
"We only did so based on the spirit of sport."
We must seriously critique the relationship between this 'spirit of sport' and ourselves (吾人) before moving on.

In the mid-October pages of the Keijō Nippō (京城日報), there was this phrase:
"Previously, during Governor-General Saitō's (齋藤總督) era, there was an intention to establish a Korean Assembly (朝鮮議會) and grant it the power to deliberate on ordinances (制令) issued by the Governor-General and the right of assent (協贊權) regarding various administrative expenses, but this policy will not be followed exactly as it was.<41> Governor-General Ugaki (宇垣總督) plans to greatly reform the Central Council (中樞院) and establish an organization similar to it," etc., etc.

The dilapidation of the Octagonal Pavilion (八角堂) in T'apdong Park (塔洞공원) is regrettable in many ways. If the Government-General (總督府) lacks the surplus capacity to repair it, it should be transferred to the Kyŏngsŏng Municipal Office (京城府), and if the Kyŏngsŏng Municipal Office also lacks the strength to repair it, then it should be opened up to the private sector. No matter how lacking in people and money we are, surely there isn't a lack of sincerity (誠意) to repair just one Octagonal Pavilion?

A year has already passed researching how to spend the one million Yen from Kyŏngjŏn (京電 - Kyŏngsŏng Electric Company). Let me just shout this: Build a thousand houses with that one million Yen (at 1,000 Yen per household) and house the homeless, poor citizens. Isn't the memory of seeing the sorrowful tears (悲淚) of the dugout dwellers (土幕民) when the eviction order was issued for the slums in Songwŏl-dong (松月洞) and inside Tongsomun (東小門) like yesterday?

According to a survey by the Ministry of Education (文部省), last year, 800 middle school students became convicts (受刑者), involving 145 schools. Of course, all were violators of the Peace Preservation Law (治安維持犯).

It is said that there has never been a time recently when the Peninsula (半島) was so rich in talented people (人材). Indeed, those involved in major incidents *** related to public order are few (though there are several, many who were overseas have come in). What comes after such an abundance of figures (人物)?

In the previous issue's column (本欄), it was mentioned that the Products Promotion Association (물산장려회) split into the Myŏng Chese (明濟世) faction and the Chŏng Sekwŏn (鄭世權) faction, etc., etc., but as the content is somewhat different, that passage is hereby retracted. <42>
<41-42>

**Summary**

This article presents several news snippets from Chongno, Seoul in late 1932. It covers a critical exchange about Japanese expansionism (Greater Asianism, Economic Bloc), questions the Tong-A Ilbo's singular focus on an Olympic athlete, reports on proposed changes to colonial governance (replacing a potential Korean Assembly with a reformed Central Council), laments the neglect of T'apdong Park's Octagonal Pavilion, suggests using funds from the electric company for public housing, notes the alarming number of middle school students arrested under the Peace Preservation Law, comments on the perceived abundance of 'talented figures' despite the political climate, and retracts a previous statement about factions within the Products Promotion Association. From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its direct (though brief) critique of Japanese ideology, its commentary on colonial administrative plans, the stark mention of student arrests under repressive laws, and its reflection on social issues like housing and the state of national monuments, offering glimpses into everyday concerns and political undercurrents in 1930s Korea.

鐘路네거리
東京서 나온 中野正剛씨가 日前 京城公會堂에서 講演을 하엿는데 先是하야 朝鮮호텔에서 呂運弘씨가 맛나보고 中野의 持說인 大亞細亞주의에 대하야 英語로서 「亞細亞주의는 조흐나 그것은 OO을 중심으으로 하는 亞細亞주의가 아니냐」
그래서 日鮮滿經濟뿌록說을 비판 질문하엿는데 中野씨는 그만 語尾를 흐리어 버리더라고.

올림픽대회에 갓다 나온 金恩培君을 唯獨 東亞日報만 환영하고 唯獨 東亞日報만이 떠들엇다.
갈 때 올 때에 무슨 뜻이 그리 만하여 東亞子 이러케 열정을 다 붓는고 그에 대하야 사회의 某운동단체에서 痛熱한 질문狀을 보냇더니 그 회답에 曰
「우리는 오직 운동정신에 의하여서 그리하엿술 뿐이라고」
우리는 이 운동정신과 吾人의 관계를 嚴肅하게 한번 비판하여 보고 지내야 하겟다.

京城日報의 10월중순 紙面에 이런 一句가 잇섯다.
「先에 齋藤總督시대에 朝鮮議會를 設하야 總督이 發하는 制令의 審議와 行政上 諸經費에 대한 協贊權을 줄 意思를 가지고 잇섯거니와 그 方針을 그대로 踏襲함은＜41＞ 아니다. 宇垣總督은 中樞院을 大改革하야 그와 近似한 조직을 設하리라고」 云云

塔洞공원 八角堂이 荒廢함은 여러 가지 의미로 애닯다. 總督府에서 重修할 餘力이 업거든 京城府에 移管할 일이고 京城府에서 또 重修할 힘이 업다면 民間에 내어 노흘 일이라. 아모리 사람과 돈이 업다기로 八角堂 하낫쯤이야 重修할 誠意가 업스랴.

京電에서 나온 百萬圓을 엇더케 쓸는고 하고 연구하기에 벌서 一年의 세월을 보내엇다. 내조고만치 소리치노니 百萬圓으로 주택千戶를 지어서(每戶 千圓式) 그래서 집업는 貧寒시민을 收容하여라. 松月洞과 東小門안 貧民窟에 撤退令을 내려 土幕民들의 悲淚를 본 기억이 어제갓지 아느냐

文部省 조사에 의하면 작년 중학생으로 受刑者 800명 그 학교수 145에 達하엿다 한다. 물론 全部가 治安維持犯

최근에 半島갓치 人材 풍부한 때가 업다고들 전한다. 과연 큼직큼직한 사건의 관계*** **치안은 이가 별로 업고(몃몃분 잇지만은 海外에 잇든 분으로 드려와 잇는 이도 만타 人物登豊後에 오는 것은 무엇일고.
