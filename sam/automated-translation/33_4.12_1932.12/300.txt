**Jokes**

**Oh, Beautiful You**
Painter: "Ah, beautiful you! Hands like white jade, skin like snow, cheeks like apples, you are the most beautiful person I have ever used as a model. For you, I would not hesitate to give my life."
Model: "Oh, wonderful! Am I that beautiful? Even among all the models you've had..."
Painter: "Yes! That's right."
Model: "Oh, wonderful! So then, how many models have you used until now..."
Painter: "Eight pigs, three cows, two dogs."

**Naturally?**
Young Lady: "This road is lonely and quite scary. Let's go by the bright moonlit road."
Young Master: "What? Why be scared? Aren't I here?"
Young Lady: "That's why I say it's scary." <69>

**Different Times**
"You wench, yesterday in the backyard, you kissed some man, didn't you? I saw it clearly with my own eyes. Who was it!"
"Mother, even if you saw it, was it before eight o'clock? Or was it 9:15?"

**Age of Remarriage**
Movie Star A: "I heard you got married recently. Who is it this time..."
B (Searching handbag): "Uh— I should have his business card..."

**Secret to Success**
Magazine Reporter: "Please tell us one story about your success, sir."
Wealthy Man A: "Well, the reason I achieved the success I have today is, back when I was still at the company, you know... Anyone who came to the company to collect money, I always beat them and threw them out, saying the manager wasn't in— Also, when the manager paired up with the typist and went to an inn or somewhere, if the manager's wife called, I always said he went on a business trip with the president for work— Because I always did that—"

**You Rascal!**
Politician (代議士): "When I get up on the podium, the audience will surely listen to my speech with mouths agape, spellbound."
His Friend (友): "Hey! Stop lying. How can people possibly yawn all at once like that?" <70>

**It's a Fixed Principle**
A: "How do you know that she is my wife (처)?"
B: "You can tell, can't you? Didn't that pawnshop clerk bow very politely?"

**Kindness Unnecessary**
Robber (强盜): "Don't move. If you move even a little, your life is forfeit—"
Patient (病人): "Oh, thank you. The doctor also tells me every day not to move."

**That's a Big Problem**
Owner: "You've punctured the tire (다이야) again. Good grief. What was it this time—"
Driver (運轉手): "It was a nail."
Owner: "But how can a nail cause punctures in ten places like this? Good grief, what are your eyes for?"
Driver (運轉手): "There's nothing I could do about that. I hit some guy, and he had that many in his pocket..."

**What is This?**
Old Man: "Uh— Aren't you the section chief (課長) at the company where my son works?..."
Section Chief (課長): "Oh— How nice to meet you! Are you Mr. A's father?..."
Old Man: "Yes. My son causes a lot of trouble."
Section Chief (課長): "Well, that's alright one way or another, but when did you come back to life? Mr. A definitely said he went to your funeral some time ago."
Old Man: "No, Section Chief, how did *you* come back to life? In fact, yesterday my son said you had passed away, so he stayed up all night [mourning] and hasn't come in yet today." <71>
<69-71>

**Summary**

This article presents a series of short jokes (笑話) typical of the humor sections in magazines of the era. The jokes cover various themes: misunderstandings in conversation (painter/model, young couple, mother/daughter), cynical views on marriage (movie stars), questionable paths to success (wealthy man), political satire (politician), social observations (pawnshop, robber/patient), mishaps (driver/tire), and absurd situations (father/section chief). The humor often relies on puns, irony, unexpected twists, and commentary on social stereotypes or situations. From a historical perspective, these jokes offer insights into popular humor, social norms (regarding relationships, marriage, business ethics, authority), and everyday concerns (like road safety, interactions with professionals) in colonial Korea during the 1930s. The language itself, with its older spellings and grammar, is also a valuable artifact reflecting the Korean language during that period.

아름다운 그대여
화가 「아- 아름다운 그대여! 백어갓흔 손, 눈갓흔 살결, 능금갓흔 뺨, 나는 지금까지 모델하든 중에서 당신갓치 아름다운 분은 처음이요. 나는 당신이라면 목숨도 사양하지 안겟슴니다.」
모뎰 「아 조화라! 그러케 제가 아름다와요. 지금까지 모뎰한 중에서도...」
화가 「네! 그럿슴니다.」
모뎰 「아 조화라! 그러면 지금까지 모뎰을 얼마나 쓰섯길네...」
화가 「도야지가 여덜 마리, 소가 세 마리, 개가 두 마리.」

當然?
그 아가씨 「이길은 적적하고 엿재 무섭군요 달은 밝은 길노 가세요」
그 도령 「뭬요, 무섭기는 내가 잇지안어요.」
그 아가씨 「그러니ㅅ까 무섭단 말에요」 ＜69＞

「저- 그이 명함을 가저슬탠대...」

성공의 비결
잡지기자 「선생의 성공담을 한가지 들녀주십소」
甲富 「저 대체 내가 오늘갓흔 성공를 하게 된 것은 내가 아직 회사에 잇슬 때 말야. 회사로 돈을 밧을어 오는 사람은 모다 두드러 내좃치고 지배인 업다고 하고- 또 지배인이 타이피스트하고 작지어 여관이나 어대로 갈 때 지배인 부인에게서 전화가 오면 사무로 사장하고 출장갓다고- 어느 때나 하엿기 때문에-」

이놈아!

代議士 「내가 연단에 올나서니 청중은 입을 딱버리고 넉을 일코 내 연설을 듯겟지」
그의 友 「야! 그짓말마러 사람이 엇더케 그러케 일제히 하품을 할 수가 잇서-」 ＜70＞

친절 無用
