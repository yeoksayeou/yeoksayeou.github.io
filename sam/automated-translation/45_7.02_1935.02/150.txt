The Governor-General and the Prisoner
Duke Osuna, the Governor-General of Naples (나폴리), Italy (伊太利), one day inspected a prison ship. A prison ship is a floating prison.

The Governor-General called the prisoners one by one and asked, "Why are you here?"

The prisoners, wishing to receive pardon, each spoke of their innocence.

"Sir, I have committed no crime but unfortunately fell victim to another's framing (몰님)."

"Your Excellency the Governor-General. I was wrongly arrested and came here, taking this form instead of the real culprit (眞犯人)."

Thus, only good people pleading their innocence seemed to be housed (*앗다) in that prison ship.

Among them, only one man came before the Governor-General and said, "I encountered an untimely (不時) disaster, had no way to support (供養할) my 70-year-old parents, and no way to feed my wife and children. Although I knew it was wrong, I was caught trying to steal (흠치려다가) food from someone's restaurant and have been here for three years. Of course, I ended up like this (이러케) because of the crime I committed."

Hearing those words, the Governor-General was startled and exclaimed, "Ha! You mean there is a criminal amidst these good people!" and, striking his back lightly with the staff (집행이) he held (들엇든) in his hand, said, "I have just discovered that everyone on this prison ship is innocent. But you, one fellow, are a true criminal! You should not be together with these good people. You might exert a bad influence on others. Get off this ship at once!" He issued the order (呼令을 나리엇다).

And so, he immediately had the guards (看守) expel (放逐하엿다) that criminal from the prison ship (to the land of freedom). <113>

**Summary**

This short article tells a parable about the Duke of Osuna, Governor-General of Naples, inspecting a prison ship. Every prisoner claims innocence due to being framed or wrongly accused, except for one man who frankly admits he stole food out of desperation to feed his family and support his elderly parents. The Governor-General, feigning surprise at finding a self-admitted criminal among so many "good people," ironically orders the honest prisoner released immediately, stating that such a "bad influence" should not contaminate the others. For historians of colonial Korea, this piece is interesting as a potential allegory. Published under Japanese rule, it could subtly critique the hypocrisy and absurdity of authority (perhaps hinting at the Japanese Government-General), where outward declarations don't match reality and justice operates paradoxically. The unexpected "freedom" granted to the one honest person highlights the arbitrary nature of power or perhaps offers a veiled commentary on societal values.

總督과 罪囚
伊太利 나폴리總督 오스나公이 어떤 날 囚徒船을 巡視하엿다. 囚徒船이란 水上監獄이다.
總督은 罪人들을 하나식 불러 『너는 웨 여기와 잇느냐.』고 물엇다.
罪人들은 赦함을 밧고저 하여 저마다 자기의 無罪함을 말하엿다.
『네 저는 아모 罪도 업는데 不幸히 남의 몰님에 빠젓습니다.』
『總督閣下. 저는 잘못 잡히어 와어 眞犯人 대신에 이 모양이 되엇습니다.』
이리하야 저마다 無罪함을 변명하는 善人들 뿐이 그 囚徒船에 收容된 것 *앗다.
그 가운데 한 사람만이 總督 앞에 나아와 『저는 不時의 災難을 당하고 70되신 老父母를 供養할 길이 없으며 妻子를 먹일 길 없사와 잘못된 일인 줄은 알면서도 남의 飮食店에서 먹을 것을 흠치려다가 잡히어 이리로 온 지가 3년이올시다. 물론 제가 지은 罪로 인하여 이러케 된 것입니다.』고 말하엿다.
그 말을 들은 總督은 깜짝 놀라 『하-이 善人들의 틈에 罪人이 잇단 말이냐!』하고 손에 들엇든 집행이로 그의 잔등을 툭 치며 『지금 들은 즉 이 囚徒船에 잇는 이가 모다 無罪한 사람들인 것이 發見되엇다. 그런데 너 한 놈은 참 罪人이란 말이지!』 이 善人들 틈에 같이 잇어서는 아니 될 것이다. 남에게 좋지 못한 영향을 끼칠런지도 모르니까 썩썩 이 배에서 나아가라.』 呼令을 나리엇다.
그리하여 곳 看守를 시켜 그 罪人을 囚徒船 밖으로 (自由의 天地로) 放逐하엿다.<113>
