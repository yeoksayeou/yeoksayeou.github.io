Account of the Accident Involving Mr. Song Chinu, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak, and Party

On January 6th, the beginning of the New Year, at precisely 2:00 PM, a luxury automobile sped like the wind and arrived in front of the Tonga Ilbo Company building. Inside the car were Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學), the gold mine king worth 3 million Won, Yi Sanghyŏp (李相協), Vice President of the Maeil Sinbo, and Cho Pyŏngsang (曹秉相). A little later, President Song Chinu (宋鎭禹) came down from the Tonga Ilbo Company and joined them in the car. Thus, the four men drove the car towards the Sŏdaemun area and disappeared like swallows. They were on their way to Sinch'ŏn Hot Springs (信川溫泉) in Manhaedo (萬海道) for a New Year's outing.
The car belonged to Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學); it was a new one bought recently for either 18,000 or 16,000 Won. Having spent nearly 20,000 Won, the entire car body shone brilliantly as if studded with luminous pearls, and the glass installed was especially fine, said to be impenetrable even by pistol shots. It's said that American banking magnates can remain calm even when encountering gangsters on the street because their luxury, high-priced cars have glass as good as Mr. Ch'oe's.
Eventually, the car passed the Independence Gate (獨立門) and raced along the Ŭiju Road (義州街道) faster than an express train, as if saying "Look at me fly!" Passing Susæk (水色) and Changdan (長湍) in a flash, it soon passed the 500-year-old capital Kaesŏng (開城) and then sped along the wide, newly built road (垣垣大路) towards Sinch'ŏn (信川).
3:30 PM! They reached the ferry crossing of the meandering Yesŏng River (禮成江). The various blue waves flowed calmly, much like the Nile where Cleopatra enjoyed a boat trip with Antony. It was a superb view.
At that moment, ah! The car wheels, just like in Li T'aipaek's (李太白) line "Mountains beyond mountains fall halfway from the blue sky," began to roll down, down a cliff hundreds of feet high towards the riverbank.
Once!
Twice!
Three times!
Hearing President Song's direct account after the incident, when the car first plunged, he thought, "Now I'm dead." When it rolled the second time, "Is this what death is like?" When it rolled the third time, "Am I surely dead now?" As he tightly closed his eyes, the feelings of the other gentlemen riding with him must have been the same.
If the car rolled just one more time, it would fall into the river hundreds of *kilri* deep. At that moment, whatever Heaven intended, it had an acacia tree growing on the riverbank stop the car wheel that was about to make its final roll. Thus, the four men escaped disaster by a hair's breadth.
The aftermath: President Song suffered a fractured femur and required three weeks of hospital treatment. Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學) required two weeks of home treatment. Mr. Yi Sanghyŏp (李相協) required one week of home treatment. Mr. Cho Pyŏngsang (曹秉相) sustained minor injuries requiring one week of treatment. The biggest loss was the 18,000 Won automobile, which required four weeks of repairs costing 800 Won. Finis.<11>

Copy of the Special Pardon Certificate

The Governor-General Terauchi assassination attempt incident (寺內總督暗殺事件), which shocked the world, led to arrests centered around Sŏnch'ŏn (宣川), Sinŭiju (新義州), and P'yŏngyang (平壤) in February of Meiji 44 (1911). The preliminary examination concluded on June 28, Meiji 45 (1912), and the public trial opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court (京城 地方法院). At that time, the sentences for the 105 people involved were generally between 5 and 10 years. Among them, the heaviest sentences were:
Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊)
Yi Sŭnghun (李昇薰)
Yang Kit'ak (梁起鐸)
Im Kijŏng (林豈正)
Ok Kwanbin (玉觀彬)
An T'aeguk (安泰國)

They appealed to the Kyŏngsŏng Court of Appeals (京城 覆審法院), and on March 21, Taishō 2 (1913), 96 people were acquitted and released entirely, while the aforementioned six were sentenced to 6 years imprisonment. They appealed again to the High Court (高等法院), but it was dismissed.
Therefore, they all served time in Seoul's Kongdŏk-ri Prison (孔德里監獄) and Taegu Prison (大邱監獄). Mr. Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊), who was identified as the ringleader, received a special pardon on February 13, Taishō 4 (1915) (Lunar New Year's Day). *** is as follows.

Queen Min Who Became a Soldier's Older Sister

During the military mutiny (兵亂), the soldiers who stormed in were lawless. They were looking for Queen Min (閔妃), as hinted by the Taewŏn'gun. Soldiers rushed into the six inner court ladies' quarters (內人廳). Shouts erupted where previously no one dared raise their head. The court ladies trembled. Queen Min (閔妃) had already changed into court lady attire and blended in among them, showing a plausible composure, but it was a time when soldiers were searching for her and she was about to fall into violent hands.
At this moment, a sturdy soldier named Kim Chunghyŏn (金重顯) rushed in with the crowd, shouting loudly and causing chaos. When he saw the disguised Queen Min (閔妃) standing there, about to face disaster, his mood instantly changed. He couldn't bear the thought of violent hands touching Queen Min (閔妃). Kim Chunghyŏn (金重鉉) charged forward, shouting,
"Hey, you scoundrels! This is my older sister. What Queen?"
"Indeed, brother. They say I'm the Queen. Please carry me on your back and let's get out of here."
Kim Chunghyŏn (金重鉉) carried Queen Min (閔妃) on his back, passed through the menacing crowd, reached Honghyŏn (紅峴), set Queen Min (閔妃) down, and then hid himself. In this way, Her Majesty fortunately escaped the tiger's mouth.

The Military Mutiny and the Palace Scene

Although military system reforms were undertaken by the Enlightenment Party figures like Kim Okkyun (金玉均), Pak Yŏnghyo (朴泳孝), and Kim Hongjip (金弘集), the military mutiny (兵亂) soon broke out, leading to utter defeat. An anecdote from the history of the Kapsin Coup (甲申政變) occurred then. When the tide turned and mutinous soldiers stormed the palace, Yun Yŏnggwan (尹領官) (father of Mr. Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊)), one of the officials responsible for the new military system, appeared before the Emperor. Emperor Kojong (高宗) was startled and urged him,
"Hurry and hide somewhere."
"I will lay down my life before Your Majesty."<12>
"No. This relates to the dismissal of the old-style troops, so there will surely be trouble. You must quickly hide somewhere."
As Yun Yŏnggwan (尹領官) hesitated, Emperor Kojong (高宗) raised his voice again,
"Go quickly! This is not the time for you to be here."
"If that is Your Majesty's wish, I shall offer this seal."
He untied the Magistrate's (府使) seal from his waist, but Emperor Kojong (高宗) bestowed upon him a white paper envelope containing two *nyang* worth of gold, instructing,
"Take this with you. You might need it."
As soon as these words ended, the sound of the Honghwamun Gate (弘化門) breaking open rang out chaotically, and countless soldiers poured in. Yun Yŏnggwan (尹領官) bowed his head, left the royal presence, and hid himself.

Old Place Names in Seoul

Among Seoul's (서울) place names, the area where the Chosŏn Hotel (朝鮮호텔) is located was called Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町) because the Commander of the Japanese Army stationed in Korea (朝鮮駐箚 軍司令官), Hasegawa Yoshimichi (長谷川好道), resided there. The area outside Sŏdaemun Gate (西大門外) was called Takezoe-chō (竹添町) because the Japanese Minister (日本公使) at the time, Mr. Takezoe (竹添), resided there. The vicinity of Kyŏngsŏng Station (京城停車場 附近) is called Furuichi-chō (古市町) because a railway engineer named Furuichi Kōi (古市道威), who was present when construction began on the Kyŏngbu Line Railway (京釜線鐵通工事), had resided there, hence the naming.<16>

宋鎭禹, 崔昌學씨 一行 遭難記

新年 正初인 1월 6일 오후 2시 正刻에 東亞日報社 압헤는 高級自働車 한 대가 疾風가치 달려와 다엇다. 차 중에는 300만원의 金鑛王
崔昌學, 그리고 每日申報 副社長 李相協, 또 曹秉相의 3氏가 타섯다. 조곰 잇다가 東亞日報社로부터는 社長 宋鎭禹씨가 내려와 차에 同乘하엿다. 그리하야 모다 네 사람은 차를 모라 西大門 方面으로 제비나름해 사라저 버렷다. 萬海道 信川溫泉으로 설노리 가는 길이다.
차는 崔昌學씨 所有라 18,000원인가 6,000원인가 주고 산지 얼마 되지 안는 새것으로 近 20,000원 돈을 드리니만치 車體全部가 夜光珠가 달닌 듯 光彩가 자못 揮煌하거니와 끼여 박은 유리는 더욱 조흔 것으로 피스톨을 쏘아도 께둘느지 못한다는 것이다. 米國 大銀行家들도 市街에서「깽」를 만나도 泰然自若할 수 잇슴이 崔씨 차와 가치 그 유리가 조흔 高級高價車인 까닭이라 한다.
及其也 차는 獨立門을 지나 義州街道로 수리개여 날 보아라 하드시 特急列車보다도 더 발느게 疾驅하엿다. 칠패팔패 휙휙 모라 지나듯 水色 지나 長湍지나 어느 사이에 500년 古都 開城까지 지나 그제는 垣垣大路 新作路를 信川을 향하여 다름질 첫다.
오후 3시반! 구비처 흐르는 禮成江나루까지 왓다. 樣樣한 푸른 물결이 마치 구레오파도라가 안토니와 船遊하든 나일江가치 고요히 흐른다. 絶景이다.
이리할 때 앗! 자동차 박휘는 三山半落靑天外라는 李太白 文句 그대로 놉히 수백尺되는 낭떠러지에서 밋흐로 밋흐로 강가에 구을 시기작하엿다.
한 번!
두 번!
세 번!
事變后 宋社長의 그 때 直話를 드르면 첫 번 차가 곤두박질 할 때「인제 죽엇구나.」 두 번 넘어갈 때「죽음이란 이런 건가.」 세 번 너머 갈 제「이제는 내가 확실이 죽엇나.」 그리면서 두 눈을 딱 감엇다고 할 제는 同乘한 다른 紳士의 心境도 아마 同一하엿스리라.
이제 차가 한 번만 더 구을면 수백기리의 강물 속에 떠러진다. 그 때에 天은 무엇을 생각하엿든지 강가에 난「아가시야」 나무를 식혀 마즈막 구을려는 차박휘를 막엇다. 이리하야 네 분은 危機一髮에서 無事함을 어덧다.
그 뒤 經過는 宋社長은 股骨이 불너저서 3주간 入院治療 崔昌學씨는 2주간 自宅治療, 李相協씨는 1주간 自宅治療, 曹秉相씨는 1주간 治療의 輕傷을 입고 가장 損害는 18,000원 짜리의 自動車로 4주간 修繕에 이 費用 800원也 以上<11>

兵亂 때 몰켜 드는 兵士들은 無法天地엿다. 찻는 것은 大院君이 暗示한 閔妃이엇다. 여섯 內人廳으로 兵士들이 몰켜 들어섯다. 감히 머리를 들지 못하든 곳에서 高喊이 엇섯다. 나인들은 떨고 잇섯다. 閔妃는 이미 內人衣裝을 하고 內人에 석기어 그럴 듯한 泰然을 보히시엇다만은 兵士들은 차저 내이고 暴力의 손에 대여질 때이엇다.
이 때에 壯士의 兵卒 金重顯이가 무리에 몰키어 들어와 몹떠들고 亂場을 하다가 及其也 變服하고 섯다가 곳 逢變하는 閔妃 光景을 보자 氣分이 금시로 변하여젓섯다. 閔妃에게 暴力의 손까지 감이야 참을 수 업섯다. 金重鉉은 달겨 들면서
「여 이 놈들아. 이것은 내 누이다. 마마가 무어야.」

金重鉉은 閔妃를 업고 凶凶한 무리들 사이를 나와 紅峴까지 와서 閔妃를 나리고는 몸을 가리엇다. 이리하야 閔妃께서는 虎口를 요행 脫하섯다.
