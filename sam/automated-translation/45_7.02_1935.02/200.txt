Tragic History of an Ill-fated Beauty, Ch'oe Hyanghwa, a Flower That Fell Amid Tears
						Hongŭi Tongja
Popular Songstress Ch'oe Hyanghwa

A songstress of beautiful voice! A famous flower of Loyang (洛陽)! Like a flower petal bloomed on the wind, she scattered as soon as she bloomed. A flower blooming in her nineteenth spring, her young spirit, fluttering alongside the fading spring flowers, must still be murmuring under the moonlight of the port (浦口). Born as the third daughter among six siblings, her pure character and bright intelligence stood out among her peers from a young age. Until her father's business failed, her life consisted of attending school and singing songs she loved. But when her father failed and lost the means to move forward, she had no choice but to become a kisaeng (妓生). Together with her eldest and second eldest sisters, the three siblings stayed up for several nights, holding each other.
"You two continue going to school. I alone will become a kisaeng and cover the household expenses and school fees."<130>
The tearful voice of the eldest sister was heard.
"Sister, I will go out. You shouldn't go, sister, and Sundeok (順德 - Hyanghwa's real name) is too young, so I will go."
It was the choked voice of the second eldest sister.
"There's no need for that. Let's all three siblings go out. We can't act all high and mighty alone while having a kisaeng older sister, and could we possibly marry well having a kisaeng younger sister? Let's all three siblings make up our minds firmly, step forward, raise our collapsing family fortune, and find a way for our father to recover."
The words of the young Sundeok carried the weight of a thousand pounds (千斤).
Thus, the three siblings joined hands and became kisaeng. However, times had changed; the era favored typical kisaeng who were boisterous and didn't discriminate among clients, rather than demure and selective ones. For these newly debuted, innocent kisaeng, earning money as they wished was harder than a dream. Those with money seemed unpleasant, those wearing perfume seemed arrogant, and those who chased after them saying they were smitten seemed dirty. How could they possibly perform the role of a proper kisaeng? Less than a year after the three siblings debuted as kisaeng, the eldest and second eldest sisters successively chose reliable partners and started their own households, leaving Hyanghwa alone to take care of her mother, father, and three younger siblings. The five family members resting on the shoulders of the 17-year-old young kisaeng were a heavy burden (重壓).
"Alright, since I've taken this on, I won't give my married sisters any cause for lingering worry (後慮)."
Under the tragic determination (悲壯한 決心) to earn "Money! Money!", Hyanghwa wouldn't hesitate even if the engagement was in a deadly place (死地), traveling between the capital (京) and the provinces (鄕) to earn, yet she never showed the slightest sign of weariness. Her characteristic trait of never turning back once she made a decision made her endure unpleasantness. It made her hold back tears and become deaf to others' ridicule (비우슴). Her traveling around places like Kanghwa (江華) and Suwŏn (水原) was due to this level of determination. Then, in the spring of her 18th year, she thought,
"Whatever it takes, I'll try to establish a foothold in Kyŏngsŏng (京城)."
With this thought, she newly renovated a house in Kwanch'ŏl-dong (貫鐵洞) and came to press closely upon the ranks of all the kisaeng, such as the Eight Beauties (8美人) or the Four-Member Group (4人組), in the Chosŏn Kwŏnbŏn (朝鮮券番). She gathered budding beauties (솔봉이 美人) of the same 17 years of age, like Mun Kŭmja (文錦子), Pak Sanok (朴山玉), and O Yŏngja (吳英子), forming a Four-Member Group. The first thing they focused on was social dancing, imported following the trend of the times.
"Hey girls, let's learn social dancing. These days, young clients, finding the floor mats of the restaurants (料理집) slippery, only want to do social dancing."
Thus, the entire Four-Member Group learned social dancing day and night. Who would have known (뉘 아랏스리요) that these sniffling baby kisaeng would become the famous flowers of Seoul and reign (君臨) over the entertainment world (花柳界)?
Rumors spread from mouth to mouth that the kisaeng named Hyanghwa was not only beautiful and refined but also danced social dances well. Following this, her reputation for having a beautiful voice also grew. At this time, there was someone who paid attention <131> to Ch'oe Hyanghwa's beautiful voice (美聲); it was Kim Sŏjŏng (金曙汀), who had been an exclusive composer for Columbia (콜님비아) and had recently moved to Sierra Records (시에론 레코-드 會社).
Hyanghwa, knowing that Wang Subok (王壽福) of P'yŏngyang (平壤) was making a name for herself as a singing kisaeng, felt her heart stir and asked quietly,
"Mr. Kim (金先生님), I'd like to try learning songs, what do you think?"
Sŏjŏng, captivated by her beautiful voice, readily consented (快諾) on the spot, telling her to come to his house to learn singing. Whether it rained or she felt unwell, as soon as she opened her eyes in the morning, she would head out beyond Tongdaemun (東大門).
"Hyanghwa will definitely succeed."
This was Kim Sŏjŏng's assertion (斷言). Thus, in the late spring (느진 봄) of her 18th year, May of Shōwa 8 (1933), an exclusive contract for recording popular songs was concluded (締結) with Sierra Records under the most favorable conditions. And so, she leaped forward to reveal her beautiful figure to the world as—
A famous partner in social dancing—
A famous songstress of popular songs—.
She received praise as "Kyŏngsŏng's finest," along with Mun Kŭmja, from renowned social dance masters in Kyŏngsŏng such as Paek Myŏnggon (白命坤), Yi Kappyŏng (李甲寧), Kim In'gyu (金寅奎), and Yi Haesŏn (李海善). Consequently, everyone sought Hyanghwa. Adding flowers to embroidery (錦上添花), on top of her burgeoning popularity, her first recorded popular song, "P'ogu ŭi Talpit" (浦口의 달빗 - Moonlight on the Port), began to sell like hotcakes (lit. 'sprouted wings and sold'). Singing Ch'oe Hyanghwa! Dancing Ch'oe Hyanghwa! Beautiful Ch'oe Hyanghwa! Graceful Ch'oe Hyanghwa! It seemed as though Kyŏngsŏng's entertainment world in Shōwa 8 (1933) was virtually her spotlight stage.
Arirang! Arirang Arariyo! Going over the Arirang pass.
The moonlight on the port fades, but the boat carrying my love doesn't come.
This "Moonlight on the Port" played every night from every musical instrument store, and Hyanghwa's photograph hung in the window of every record store. Newspapers, magazines, the Sierra Company's publicity department—it seemed all their promotion was solely about Ch'oe Hyanghwa. Her beautiful voice, beautiful figure, beautiful physique—as the world unanimously praised her for possessing these three, her popularity seemed destined only to advance with irresistible force (破竹의 形勢).
A rumor began to circulate that if you wanted to meet Hyanghwa, you had to book her three days in advance, otherwise, you couldn't meet her even if you went to the restaurant. There's even a story that once, when the Sierra employees were having a party at the Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月館) main branch, a certain famous exclusive dealer from P'yŏngyang, who was visiting Kyŏngsŏng, wanted to see Hyanghwa before leaving. He sat waiting at T'aesŏgwan (太西館) but couldn't find her, so he inevitably consulted Myŏngwŏlgwan and managed to borrow 30 minutes to meet her. During this time, she achieved first-rate income. First, she moved the family to a new house in Kwanch'ŏl-dong. She decorated her room as she had dreamed in her young heart. Her success was enough to make even onlookers feel excited.
The flower blooming in the heart, do you know its name?
Struck by parting, its form is dim (암암), a smiling flower, delicate (야외네).<132>

When it must wither, only tears flow
Fluttering down, it's trampled underfoot (발밋에 채네). The young spirit is sorrowful (설다네).
As her second hit, "Kasŭme P'inŭn Kkot" (강슴에 피는 꼿 - The Flower Blooming in the Heart), flowed out onto the streets, her popularity reached its peak (絶頂). Love letters flew into the company, supporters called her to restaurants, writers, musicians, connoisseurs of arts (風流郞), young students, businessmen, sons of the wealthy (富豪) swirled around her offering songs and lyrics!
"Mother, these days I feel strangely scared for no reason."
Mother: "Why do you say you're scared? Everything is going as you wished."
Hyanghwa: "Everyone I see only says nice things, perhaps because I'm too happy, I feel scared."
For an 18-year-old maiden who hadn't experienced the hardships of the world, perhaps it was too terrifying a popularity.
Hyanghwa: "Kŭmja-ya. Let's not seek lovers until we're past twenty."
She said this lying under the same blanket with Mun Kŭmja, one of her closest companions (동모).
Kŭmja: "Is there any doubt (여부가 잇나)? If we get entangled with the men chasing us around these days, saying they'll die for us, we'll just ruin ourselves pointlessly and face utter disgrace."
Hyanghwa: "Ah, scary!"
Hyanghwa even shuddered.<133>
Hyanghwa: "That's right. If we are to love, let's wait until we're over 30, after we've learned to see the world and men, and then seek someone reliable."
The modern boys (모던 뽀이) chasing her to dance, the wealthy men (財産家) pestering her, offering to buy her a house! These could never be the object of love Hyanghwa sought today. As long as she was playing the role of a kisaeng who didn't sell her smiles (우슴), her counterpart also had to be more manly and gentlemanly. To find such a person, there was no other way (道理) but to look far away from the people who frequented the restaurants every night, every evening as a kisaeng.
Before long, Shōwa 9 (1934) arrived. It was time for the company's New Year recordings. As she began practicing the songs, she caught a light cold (가뵈아운 감긔) and started to have a nagging cough. She worried that her body temperature (身熱) rose slightly in the afternoon. However, no one thought it could possibly be tuberculosis (肺病). After returning from a trip to Tokyo, less than a month later, she became sick again and took to her bed. Knowing how joyful and good the spring of her 19th year should be, she was anxious (조바심), wanting to get better quickly. But as the days passed, her condition began to worsen (기우러지기 시작하야), making the sickbed visitors who gathered every evening unable to lift their faces (낫을 페지 못하게 하얏다). Then, finally, after March passed, she received the diagnosis: tuberculosis. Hearing the word tuberculosis, Hyanghwa cried for several days (몃칠).
"Mother, please somehow let me live. Isn't it unfair to die at nineteen?"
With a pale face (햇슥한 얼골) and listless pupils (힘업는 눈동子), she stares at the ceiling (天井) and mutters to herself. The three great doctors, An Chŏng (安井), Im Myŏngjae (任明宰), and Sim Hosŏp (沈浩燮), visited continually and did not leave her sickbed, but what could be done (엇지 하리요)? Time had already run out (때는 벌서 기울고 마랏다).
"So, even with so many doctors visiting, they can't save just one person like me?" She truly regretted having to die. There were too many unforgettable things (못 잇치는게 너모 만핫섯다). When spring comes—what clothes to wear, how to play, where to go—she had counted the days with her only close friend, Mun Kŭmja. Eventually, the flowers bloomed and the days grew warm, but the disease demon lodged in her body ate away at her chest (가슴을 좀먹으니), making even going out the door (門 밧 出入) impossible according to her wishes.
"Hey, this flower was picked from the mountain pavilion in Sŏngbuk-dong (城北洞 山亭)."
Holding the news of spring brought by a companion (동모), she cried uncontrollably (소김업시 우럿다).
"What if I die like this?"
Who would enjoy (질겨) dying? But for Ch'oe Hyanghwa now, the path of good fortune stretched endlessly before her eyes (幸運의 길이 눈 압헤 끗업시 열녓거늘). To abandon this path and turn back—how heartbreaking (애달고) and regrettable (원통하얏겟느냐) it must have been. Her charm (어엿븜), her youth! Her beautiful voice! Her gentle personality (성걱)! Her pure attitude (개결한 태도)! Those who knew her and met her almost raised an uproar (아우성 소리), saying,
"Hyanghwa must not die."
"Let's at least hospitalize her so she has no final regrets (마즈막 恨)."
Hyanghwa herself said she felt she might get better if hospitalized. Thus, one morning in May, held in the arms of her family and company staff, she was admitted to Dr. An Chŏng's internal medicine department (安井內科) at the University Hospital (大學病院). However, her condition worsened progressively,<134> and Dr. An Chŏng, licking his lips (입맛을 다시며 - implying regret/difficulty), said,
"It is too late (때가 느젓소이다). One lung is completely damaged (餘地업시 傷햇스니); it's only a matter of time (時間問題). You should rather (숫제) take her home and let her pass away peacefully in bed (臥席終身이나 식히시요)."
The young assistants who knew Hyanghwa whispered quietly (나즉나즉 수군거렷다),
"We must at least ensure her life is preserved (保全케 해야지) until she gets home."
Thus, several injections (몃 대의 注射液) were gradually administered (좀좀 드러갓다), piercing (뚤코) her skin, white as wax (白납갓치 힌 피부), stretched over bone. The 20-odd family members and related people standing crying outside the window (窓 밧게서 울고 섯든) couldn't even voice their cries, only wetting their collars (옷깃만 적시엇다), lest the patient perhaps notice (或是나 病人이 눈치를 채일 갑아). Hearing the words about being discharged, Hyanghwa's closed eyes became round (둥구래 젓다).
"Why are they telling me to leave when I'm dying?"
This one phrase ruthlessly pierced the hearts of those close to her.
"No. That's not it. They said it's fine to go home and get treated there," her mother soothed her pitiful daughter with a choked voice. But the悲愴한 空氣 (tragic atmosphere) all around (四面에) was too thick (가득하얏섯다) to believe those words readily (고지 듯기에는).
"Well, I must be going off to die."
Speaking as if about someone else, Hyanghwa closed her eyes again. On the night of her discharge, she stayed up beautifully, and on the morning of May 9th, at 8 AM, as if pushed away by the morning sunlight (아츰 해빗에 밀녀가듯이), she closed her eyes for the last time. The lover she intended to seek after turning 20 had now become a lover she could never seek. In the young Hyanghwa's chest (가츰에는), lying silently (말업시 누어잇는) in the Miari (미야里) Public Cemetery, only the record of her debut work, "P'ogu ŭi Talpit" (『浦口의 달빗』), rests in place of a lover (愛人을 代身하야 안겨잇슬 뿐이다). The half-life (半生) of a beautiful person (佳人) who came early and returned too early! How could one say she was not ill-fated (薄命치 안엇다 할소냐)?

Pale face like a pear blossom (배꼿갓치 햇슥한낫), burning lips (불타는입살)
Breath like waves (물결치듯 급한숨결), echoing in the chest (가슴에울녀)
Sleepless dawns, groping for (더드머찻는)
The bedside companion (벼개머리 말부엇이), who was it (누고이엇나)?

After the funeral was completed (葬禮를 맛치고) on May 11th, a grand memorial service (追悼會) was held subsequently on the 13th at Kakhwangsa Temple (覺皇寺) in Susong-dong (壽松洞).

Youth and joy, too (청춘도 깃븜도), are like a dream (꿈결갓고나)
Calling the departed person (가버린 사람 불너본들) is only frustrating (답답만 하고나)

Traces of where you played (노시든 자최여), please disappear (사라저 다오)
Unable to endure the burning thoughts (불붓는 생각 참도 못해), only the heart burns (가슴만 타노니)<135>

**Summary**

This article from Samch'ŏlli magazine recounts the short, tragic life of Ch'oe Hyanghwa (real name Sundeok), a popular Korean singer and kisaeng in the early 1930s. Born into a family that faced financial ruin, she and her two older sisters became kisaeng to support the family. Hyanghwa quickly rose to fame in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) due to her beauty, dancing skills (especially social dancing), and singing voice. Discovered by composer Kim Sŏjŏng, she signed with Sierra Records and achieved immense popularity with hits like "P'ogu ŭi Talpit" (Moonlight on the Port). Despite her success and resolve to remain independent, she contracted tuberculosis at age 18 and passed away at 19 in May 1934, cutting short a promising career.

From a historian's perspective, the article offers insights into the social and cultural milieu of colonial Korea in the 1930s. It highlights the precarious economic conditions forcing women into the kisaeng profession, the changing tastes in entertainment (rise of popular music records, social dancing), the mechanisms of the burgeoning recording industry (talent scouting, promotion, contracts), and the public fascination with celebrity culture, even under colonial rule. The blend of modern aspirations (social dancing, recording career) with traditional constraints and tragic tropes (美人薄命 - beauty and misfortune) is particularly noteworthy.

美人薄命哀史, 눈물 속에 진 꼿 崔香花
						紅衣童子
流行歌姬 崔香花

美聲의 歌姬! 洛陽의 名花! 바람결에 피어난 꼿닙과 갓치 그는 피자마자 落花되얏다. 열아홉 靑春에 피어나는 꼿 봄을 지는 꼿과 함께 나붓긴 그의 절문 넉은 浦口의 달빗을 아즉도 음죽어리고 잇스리라. 6남매 중에 셋재 딸노 태나서 개결한 性品과 영농난 총명은 어려서부터 무리에 뛰어낫섯다. 아버지가 營業을 할 때까지는 學校에 다니기와 조화하는 노래 부르기로 일을 삼든 그이엇스나 一朝에 아버지가 失敗를 하야 움즉일 資力을 일케 되니 하는 수 업시 妓生이 되고 마랏다. 큰 언니 둘재 언니와 함께 3兄弟가 마조 잡고 몃칠 밤을 새웟다.
너의들은 學校에를 그대로 다녀라. 내 나혼자 妓生이 되야 집일과 學費를 대이지.<130>
큰 언니의 눈물겨운 목소리가 들녓다.
언니 내가 나가리다. 언니는 나가 만코 順德(香花의 本名)이는 나가 어리니 내가 나가리다.
둘재 언니의 목메인 목소리이다.
그럴 것 업시 3兄弟 다 나갑시다. 妓生언니 두고 혼자 잘난 체 할 수도 업고 妓生동생 두고 오른 데로 싀집이나 가겟소. 3兄弟 마음만 올케 먹고 나서서 쓰러저 가는 家運을 이릇켜 아버님의 更生하실 길을 어더 드립시다.
나 어린 順德의 一言에는 千斤의 무게가 잇섯다.
이리하야 3兄弟는 손길을 마조 잡고 妓生이 되고 마랏다. 그러나 時勢는 뒤집혀서 얌잔하고 조나 빼는 妓生보다는 넝느레 좃코 손님 가리지 안는 典型的 妓生들의 歲月이 조화저서 갓 나온 숫북김이 妓生들에게 마음먹은 대로 돈버리가 되기는 꿈에도 어려운 일이엇다. 돈잇는 이는 안이꼽고 향수바른 이는 건방저 보히며 반햇다고 쫏처 다니는 이는 더러워 보히니 무슨 수로 妓生다온 妓生노릇이 되얏스랴. 3兄弟가 妓生 나온지 1년이 다 못가서 큰 언니 자근 언니는 次例론 堅實한 일군을 선택하야 살님을 드러가게 되고 홀노 남은 香花만이 엄마 압바와 삼남매 동생을 맛게 되고 마랏다. 17세 어린 妓生의 엇개에 실닌 다섯分 家族은 重壓이 深한 負擔이엇다.
오-냐 이왕 내가 맛흔 以上 싀집간 어니들에게 後慮의 거릿김을 주지 안으리라.
香花는 『돈』! 『돈』!을 벌자는 悲壯한 決心아래 노름이라면 死地에라도 사양치 안으리 만치 京鄕으로 벌어 다니되 조금도 시린 氣色을 내지 안앗섯다. 한 번 作定하면 도릇키지를 안는 그의 特性은 그로 하야곰 아니꼬음도 참게 하얏다. 눈물도 참게 하고 남의 비우슴에도 귀먹은 사람이 되게 하얏다. 그가 江華이니 水原이니 地方으로 도라다니게 된 것도 이만한 決心이 잇는 까닭이엿다. 이리하다가 18세 되든 해 봄에 그는
엇잿든 京城에 터를 닥가 보겟다
는 생각을 가지고 貫鐵洞에 집을 새로 修裝하고 朝鮮券番에 8美人이니 4人組이니 하는 모든 妓生들 축에 肉迫을 하게 되얏다. 文錦子, 朴山玉, 吳英子 등 가튼 17세의 솔봉이 美人들을 叫合하야 4人組를 結合해 가지고 爲先 着目한 점이 時代의 潮流를 따라 輸入된 社交춤이엇다.
이 애들아. 社交춤을 배자. 요사히의 절문 손님들은 料理집 장판방이 밋그러우니가 社交춤만 추자드라.
이리하야 4人組 一同은 밤과 낫을 不계하고 社交춤을 배웟다. 뉘 아랏스리요. 코물 흘니는 이 아기妓生들이 서울의 名花가 되야 花柳界에 君臨할 줄을.
香花라는 妓生이 人物도 곱고 사람도 점잔하고 社交춤도 잘 춘다는 所聞이 입에서 입으로 옴겨 傳하자 뒤를 이워 목소리도 곱다는 評判이 놉하젓다. 이 때에 崔香花의 美聲에 귀를 기우린<131> 사람이 잇스니 그는 콜님비아 專屬 作曲家로 잇다가 시에론 레코-드 會社로 자리를 옴긴 金曙汀이엇다.
香花도 平壤의 王壽福이가 노래 부르는 妓生으로 일홈을 날니는 줄은 아는지라 마음이 움즉이어
金先生님 노래를 배워보고 십흔데 엇덜가요.
조용히 무럿다. 그의 美聲에 취하다 십히한 曙汀은 直席에서 자기 집으로 노래를 배흐러 오라고 快諾을 하엿다. 비가 나리나 몸이 不便하거나 그는 아츰에 눈만 뜨면 東大門 밧그로 나섯다.
香花는 반다시 成功하리라.
이것이 金曙汀의 斷言이엇다. 이리하야 그가 18세 되든 해느진 봄! 昭和 8년 5월에 그는 가장 有利한 條件으로 시에론 레코-드와 流行歌 吹込의 專屬契約이 締結되고 마랏다. 그리하야 그는 一躍
社交춤의 名파트나로-
流行歌의 名歌姬로-
世上에 아름다온 자태를 드러내게 되기에 이른 것이다. 京城에 社交춤의 名手로 손꼽는 白命坤, 李甲寧, 金寅奎, 李海善 등 諸氏에게 文錦子와 아울너 京城一流라는 讚辭를 듯고 보니 너나 할 것 업시 찻는 이 香花 뿐이엇다. 그 우에 錦上添花로 한참 피어나는 人氣 우에다가 그의 第 1回 吹込 發賣된 流行歌와「浦口의 달빗」이 날개가 돗처 팔니기를 시작하니 노래 잘하는 崔香花! 춤 잘 추는 崔香花 人物 고흔 崔香花 몸곱게 갓는 崔香花 맛치 昭和 8年度 京城의 花柳界는 그의 燭点舞臺이나 다름업는 風이 잇섯다.
아리랑! 아리랑 아라리요 아리랑 고개로 너머 간다.
浦口에 달빗은 자자드는데 우리 님 탄 배는 안오네.
어는 樂器店에서든지 밤마다 트는 이 浦口의 달빗이요 어는 레코-드가 유리窓에든지 거러 놋는 것은 香花의 寫眞이엇다. 新聞으로 雜誌로 시에론會社 宣傳部 맛치 崔香花밧게는 모른다는 다ㅅ 그의 宣傳 뿐이엇다. 아름다온 목소리 아름다온 자태 아름다온 몸맴시 이 세가지를 아울너 가즌 그를 世上이 입을 모화 내세우기로만 하니 그의 人氣야 말마다 破竹의 形勢로 뻐더 나아갈 줄만 알게 되고 마랏다.
香花를 맛나보랴면 3일 前에 미리 맛치지 안으면 料理집에는 가도 맛날 수는 업다논 소문이 들니기 시작하얏다. 어느 때인가 시에론社員들의 노리가 明月館 本店에 잇섯는데 平壤 어는 著名한 特約店 主人이 京城에 온 김에 香花를 보고 가겟다고 太西館에 안저 찻다 못하야 하는 수 업시 明月館에 相議를 하야 30분을 비러다 맛나 보왓다는 이약이까지 남게 되고 마랏다. 이리하는 동안에 一流의 收入을 엇게 되니 爲先 집을 貫鐵洞 새집으로 옴긴다. 어린 가슴에 그려오든 房치장을 한다. 그의 得意는 엽헤서 보는 사람들조차 신바람이 날만 하얏다.
가슴에 피는 꼿 그 일홈 알소냐.
이별에 부닥겨서 형용도 암암 웃는 꼿 야외네.<132>

시드러야 되면 눈물만 흐르고
나붓겨 떠러지면 발밋에 채네. 절문 넉 설다네.
