Bizarre Travels Through Ports of Various Countries, South America's "Rio Port"
						Hong Unbong

Whether fortunate or unfortunate, I, a Korean person who still does not know Korea's three famous sceneries, have become a sailor (마드러스), and for this reason, I came to personally see the watery landscape of "Rio" Port, called the foremost of the world's three great sceneries.

Even an elementary school (普通學校) child knows well that Rio Port is located in the Western Hemisphere and the Southern Hemisphere. Located at 22 degrees 54 minutes South Latitude and 43 degrees 3 minutes West Longitude, it is in the tropics and is at the antipode (對蹠點) to Korea.

Over 400 years ago from now, on January 1st, 1502, a single sailor (마드로쓰) first found this port, like a piece of oil painting. The discoverer, Amerigo Vespucci (아메리고·베스밧슈), named it Riosejineiro ("River of January") after the flowing river (江) and January (正月).

The expansive port scenery is a beautiful port (美港) embellished with three-dimensional lines (線), colors (色彩), and feeling (感), featuring Italian Renaissance-style buildings (建築物) and roadside trees (街路樹) of betel palms (檳榔樹).

Anyway (左右間), many people write about the city's (都市) outward appearance (外觀), but few write about its inner side (裏面). Looking only at the surface (表面), one cannot discuss the fine scenery (風光), the international trade port (貿易港), and the luxurious consumer life (消費生活) of its citizens (市民). It is known that there is some great connection (關聯) with the dark society (暗黑社會) of the inner side. <146>

Economics (經濟學) is researched (硏究), but the poor (貧困者) increase; medicine (醫學) develops (發達) and drugs (藥物) are invented (發明), but the sick (疾病者) do not decrease. For over 1,000 years, religion (宗敎) has not increased the happiness (幸福) of the people (人民) and has not saved (救願) criminals (犯罪者).

Anyway, Rio, if glanced at (一瞥) from the surface, is a beautiful port, but step one foot into its inner side, and it is a demonic city (魔都) filled with murderers, the sick (殺人病者), ruffians (無賴漢), and prostitutes (淫賣婦). In that aspect (方面), it is now said to be attempting to surpass (凌駕) Mexico, which has a world-renowned (世界有數) name as a crime city (犯罪都市).

When a traveler (旅行者) is resting their fatigued (疲困) body on a hotel bed (호텔벳드), there is a young beauty who knocks on the door and enters. She makes meaningful (意味) facial expressions (表情) with her eyes and locks the door (門鎖) from the inside. Perhaps because it is a tropical region (熱帶地方), she wears clothes (衣服) that clearly show her developed body lines (肉體線), and being undressed, of course (노-츠로스는 물론이다).

"Sir, you can't sleep because you are lonely, right? Shall I keep you company for a bit?" By the time she finishes speaking, her elastic (彈力) body is already on the guest's (客) lap, her delicate jade-like hands (纖纖玉手) somehow embrace his neck, and her lips burning with passion (情熱) moisten. When homesickness (鄕愁) blocks the heart in a faraway foreign land (萬里異域) and one cannot sleep, there is no one who would be displeased (不快) to receive comfort (慰安) at the beauty's whim.

After waking from an instant's (瞬時間) dream, the Southern Country (南國) beauty is gone, and the travel expenses (旅費) have been stolen without a penny left.

If one is unlucky (運不吉), catching gonorrhea (淋病) or syphilis (梅毒) is just a common occurrence (例事).

Rio's hotels, regardless of (不拘) first-class (一流) or second-class (二流), are the base areas (根據地帶) for these kinds of (種類) groups of women (女子群).

If a guest should refuse (拒絶) an unjust (不當) service fee (써비스료), the woman's patron (バトロンー) – in reality, her lover (情夫) – this ruffian does not consent (承諾). The final (最後) act (幕) when that fellow makes his appearance (登場) is to take off and throw down his glove (掌甲). (Meaning to unleash fists (拳銳)). Here, a gentlemanly (紳士的) attitude (態度) does not pass (通用). Victory or defeat (勝負) is decided by the method (方法) where the one who draws and fires a pistol (비스틀) first wins. That is what Rio is like.

The prevalence rate (蔓延率) of venereal disease (性病) is truly extremely tragic (甚凄).

About midway up the mountainside (山中腹) is a slum (貧民窟) called the "Bird's Nest."

Rather than calling it a slum, it would be more appropriate (適當) to call it a settlement (部落) of the diseased (病疾者). It goes without saying that the residents (住民) are poor, but the majority (大半) are in a condition (形便) that passersby (行人) cannot bear to look at due to syphilis (梅毒) and lupus (狼瘡).

Their whole bodies (全身) are swollen, pus (濃汁) flows here and there, flesh is exposed (露出), and they suffer the attacks (襲擊) of swarms of flies.

The place with many flies is the toilet (便所) here. Even calling it a toilet, it's no different than a communal toilet (共回便所) where they dig around the houses (家屋周圍) and everyone relieves themselves (大小便) without distinction (分別). The facilities are incomplete (設備不完全) and open-air (露天), and moreover, under the southern sun (南國太陽), the filth (汚物) ferments (醱酵), so the uncleanliness (不潔) is beyond words, and the bad smell (惡臭) makes passersby cover their noses.

Due to malnutrition (榮養不良), even the children (兒孩) seem unable to grow (發育) sufficiently (滿足). Their complexions (血色) are pale (蒼白), and they are skinny and bluish like Korean dried pollack (明太魚).

Because life (生活) here is so miserable (悲慘), many cannot eat and suffer (苦) from diseases, becoming madmen (狂人). From time to time, these madmen take weapons (武器) and indiscriminately stab everyone without distinction (區別), creating gruesome (凄慘) sights (光景). <147> (Following 5 lines illegible - National Institute of Korean History compilation)

Then, are there no social charity hospitals (社會의 施療病院) when there are so many sick people? It's not that there aren't any (업는 것은 안이다). They do exist (잇기는 잇으나), but not only is the treatment (待遇) of patients poor, but the actions (行動) of the doctors (醫師) are said to be very suspicious (疑甚). For example (例), many die after being recklessly injected with what might be Salvarsan (606號) or mercury (水銀) for syphilis, and many barely survive after nearly dying. If one is going to die after injections and *eating and **doing things, it doesn't matter (無妨) if one dies from a disease received from a prostitute (娼婦), they say, so it's not strange (異常치 안타) that venereal diseases (花柳病) are rampant.

The way of kissing (기쓰하는 方法) is different with Negro (네구러) prostitutes (淫賣婦) and courtesans (娼妓) – the kiss of the black (黑) angel Negro involves pressing noses together and smelling the scent (내음새 맛듯 한다).

The pleasures (歡樂) of Negro prostitutes are of a kind (類) unparalleled in the world (世界).

Readers (讀者), you may have read in movies (映畵), novels (小說), especially detective novels (探偵小說), about bizarre large opium (阿片) smoking dens (大吸引場). In a large room (房), numerous (數多) men and women (男女) are gathered in groups on sofas (소퐈) and beds (벳드) here and there (處處), rapturously (陶然) inhaling (吸引) opium. As their mutual excitement (興奮性) reaches its peak (極度), they are not satisfied (滿足) with mere oral intoxication (陶醉) and seek (探求) a dreamland (夢境).

There is no opium in the Negro brothel (娼妓집).

The exterior of the brothel (娼妓家) is not a particularly strange (奇異) building (建物), but a single white light (白燈) on the front (正面) serves as the sign (目標).

When a guest enters, a beautiful prostitute (娼妓할 미女) guides (案內) him to the waiting room (控房). On the waiting room (控室) table (테블), photographs (寫眞) of the prostitutes are laid out (노여 잇다). They are not proper photographs taken in full dress (盛裝) like those in Tokyo or Osaka's red-light districts (遊廓). They are photos of nude women (裸體婦) not even covering what should be covered. If one makes a wrong choice (選擇) after seeing these, one must resign oneself (觀念) to the fact that it's one's own fault. A cross (츠로쓰) covers the photograph of a prostitute currently (現在) with a guest upstairs.

Seeing without buying (素見) is absolutely (絶代) prohibited (禁制). The prostitute's fee (娼妓花代) is expensive (高價), but during that time (時間), it is said to be the guest's freedom (自由) to do whatever (如何한 方法) he wants. There are definitely (斷然) plentiful (多多的有) special features (特點) that cannot be experienced in other countries (他國). Anyway, it's different from the ordinary (平凡), formalistic (形式的) desire (欲求) one gets for money, so one can enjoy pleasure (亨樂) as much as one wants. If you buy a Black prostitute (黑人娼婦), be prepared (覺悟) for your insides (五臟) to melt away.

It is captivating (魅惑的) or overwhelming (壓倒的). A mixture of yellow, black, and white people (黃黑白雜性) gather together (一堂의 會合), wandering amidst primitive (原始的) embraces (抱擁), melting away (溶化) into that midst.

Amidst this liberated (解放的) sexual conduct (性行), occasionally a black angel (黑人의 天使) goes around pouring fragrant liquor (色酒) with a mellow and rich aroma (芳醇한 香氣) from a wooden cup (木製酒杯) by each bed (寢臺).

Even in such a dreamlike place (夢境가튼 곳), be prepared (覺悟) for the baptism (洗禮) of the dagger (短刀) and pistol (비스톨) if things go wrong.

**Summary**

The author, Hong Unbong, a Korean sailor, describes his visit to Rio de Janeiro in 1935. While acknowledging its surface beauty as one of the world's great scenic ports, he focuses almost entirely on its dark underside: rampant crime, poverty, disease (especially syphilis), and dangerous prostitution. He details encounters with seductive thieves in hotels, the squalor of slums filled with diseased people, ineffective and suspicious medical care, and the unique, unrestrained, yet perilous world of Negro brothels, contrasting the surface allure with hidden dangers like theft and violence. For historians, this article offers a rare glimpse into how a Korean traveler viewed and represented a major non-Asian, non-Western city during the colonial period, reflecting anxieties about modernity, social decay, disease, and racial encounters, potentially mirroring or contrasting with conditions and perspectives within colonized Korea itself. 朝鮮사람으로 아직 朝鮮 3景을 모르는 내가 幸인지 不幸인지「마드러스」가 된 因故로 世界 3大景의 隨一이라고 부르는「리오」港의 水鄕을 親見하게 되엿다.
「리오」港은 西半珠이고 南半球의 位置하여 잇는 줄은 普通學校 兒童이라도 잘 안다. 南緯 22度 54分 西經 43度 3分의 位한 熱帶이고 朝鮮과는 對*點이 되여잇다.
至今부터 400여년 前 1502년 正月 1일의 一介「마드로쓰」가 처음으로 一幅의 油畵가튼 이 港口를 찻게 되다. 發見者「아메리고·베스밧슈」가 흐르는 江과 正月의 인하야 즉 (Riosejineiro「正月의 江」이라고 命名하다.
寬潤한 港口의 景色은 立體的의「線」과「色彩」와「感」을 加工하야 伊太利 루녯선스式의 建築物과 檳榔樹의 街路樹로 된 美港이다.
左右間 都市의 外觀마는 쓰는 사람이 만으나 裏面을 쓰는 사람은 적다. 表面만을 보고는 風光조코 國際的 貿易港이고 市民의 消費生活도 豪奢 등을 論할 수 업다. 裏面의 暗黑社會와는 무슨 큰 關聯이 이슬 줄 안다.<146>
經濟學은 硏究되나 貧困者는 늘어가고 醫學은 發達되고 藥物은 發明되나 疾病者는 줄지 안은다. 1,000여 년부터 宗敎가 人民의게 幸福을 늘이지 못하고 犯罪者를 救願치 못한다.
左右間 리오도 表面을 一瞥하면 美港이나 裏面의 一步를 드러서면 殺人病者 無賴漢 淫賣婦가 가득찬 魔都로다. 그 方面의 말이라면 至今은 犯罪都市로서 世界有數한 일음잇는 멕시카를 凌駕할나고 한다.
旅行者가 疲困한 몸을「호텔벳드」의서 쉬일 때의 門을 녹크하고 드러오는 절문 美人이 잇다. 意味잇게 눈으로 表情을 하며 안으로 門鎖를 채워 버린다. 熱帶地方이 되여 그러치만 발달된 肉體線를 確實이 비치워 주는 衣服을 입고 또한 노-츠로스는 물론이다.
「나리 적적하여 잠 못 일우지오. 동모 좀 하여 드릴나고요」 그말이 끗날 때에는 벌서 彈力잇는 몸이 客이 무릅 우에 와잇고 纖纖玉手는 어는 듯 목을 얼매고 情熱의 타는 입술은 저저오른다. 萬里異域에서 鄕愁가 가삼을 막아 잠 못 일우는 때의 美人의 마음대로에 慰安을 밧으면 不快할 者업다.
瞬時間의 꿈을 께고 나면 南國美人은 간 곳 업고 旅費는 1分도 업시 빼앗겨 버린다.
運不吉하면 淋病梅毒밧기 例事로다.
「리오」의 호텔은 一流 二流를 不拘하고 其種類들의 女子群의 根據地帶다.
만일의 客이 不當한 써비스料를 拒絶한다면 게집의 バトロンー實은 情夫인대- 이 無賴漢이 承諾지 안는다. 그 놈이 登場한 最後의 幕은 掌甲을 버서 던진다. (즉 拳銳을 放射한다는 말) 이곳의서는 紳士的 態度는 通用되지 안는다. 勝負는 비스틀을 만저 放射하는 者가 이기는 方法이다. 그거시「리오」라는 데다.
性病 蔓延率도 實로 甚凄하다.

貧民窟이라 함보다 病疾者의 部落이라 함이 適當할 줄 안다. 住民이 貧困者임은 물론이나 大半이 梅毒과 狼瘡으로 行人들의 눈으로 보지 못할 形便에 되여 잇다.
