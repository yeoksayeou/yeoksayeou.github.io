名作 流行歌謠選 (Selection of Famous Popular Songs)

綾羅島 (Nŭngnado Island)

岸曙 (An Sŏ)

As autumn deepens on the willow trees of Nŭngnado Island,
Wild geese fly freely high in the clear sky.
The boat floating on the river follows the current,
Circling the silk island, it may be called joy,
But how can one know the past dreams that have gone by?

The water falling down Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk cliff has been the same for four thousand years,
As the autumn wind comes and goes, only fallen leaves fly freely in the sky.
The boat floating on the river follows the current,
Circling the silk island, it may be called joy,
But how can one know the future dreams? (Note: Text says '지낼 옛꿈야', likely meaning 'future old dreams', implying recurring cycles or perhaps a typo for '지낸 옛꿈야' - past dreams, as in other stanzas. Translation follows the latter for consistency.)

When the evening bell of Yŏngmyŏngsa Temple sighs for old affections,
Croaking, croaking, crows fly freely, circling the pine forest.
The boat floating on the river follows the current,
Circling the silk island, though it is called joy,
But how can one know the past dreams that have gone by?

紅淚怨 (Red Tear Resentment)

Mountains are unfamiliar, waters are unfamiliar, whom did I come here searching for?
The one called lover, the place to rely on, is all in vain.
Far away, at the end of the clouds, is my distant hometown.
Will even one day my heart be at ease?

Even floating grass circling the sky has a time to settle,
But this body, a thousand leagues away in a foreign land, follows the lover, wandering.
My endless sorrow, the more I think, the lonelier I become.
On nights when the cuckoo cries, I cannot fall asleep.<222>

Will crying solve my situation?
The indifferent, cruel dream replays the bygone days.
Waking up, on my pillowing arm, there are only shimmering tears.
Whether I cry or lament, what use is there?

꼿을 잡고 (Holding a Flower)

When flowers bloom in the gentle spring breeze,
That unforgettable bygone old day.

Though I try to forget the past years like clouds,
My sad heart cannot forget.

It seems like only yesterday we played holding flowers,
But that lover is gone, and I am alone.

The more I think, the sadder my heart, should I not cry?
Unable not to cry, I hold a flower.<223>

비나리는 밤 (A Rainy Night)

異河潤 (I Ha-yun)

Since you are the lover who visited like a dream,
Why would I not miss you?
On this night, retracing old memories,
Only the dreary rain falls.

Leaning blankly against the window pane,
This sorrowful heart of mine.
Only endless darkness, on this lonely night,
Only the dreary rain falls.

The indifferent whistle sounds desolate,
Whom should I resent?
On this night, lost in memories, sighing,
Only the dreary rain falls.

Tears flow down like rain,
They know no end.
On this night, crying for the lover, even sadder,
Only the dreary rain falls.

눈물어린 그림자 (Tear-stained Shadow)

The boat about to disappear dimly into the fog,
The night I cried watching from the port.
If only the dreary rain hadn't fallen that night,
This piercing pain in my heart wouldn't have set in.

Two or three times, I only resolved not to go,
But when evening comes, I go out to the port.
Looking at the sea that meets the edge of the sky,
I spend another anxious day like this.

What can be done? That time is unforgettable.
Buried in the fog, drenched by the rain,
The lover who departed for a path unknown,
Your tear-stained shadow is missed.

애닯흔 피리소리 (Sorrowful Flute Sound)

A lost, lonely boat,
Floating gently on the lake.
All night long, endlessly,
The sorrowful sound of the flute.<224>

In the thick night fog,
A gently floating, troubled heart.
The sound drifting desolately,
How sorrowful that sound is.

O homeless wanderer,
Riding the gently floating boat,
Where are you heading,
That you only keep playing the flute?<225>

갈대꼿 (Reed Flower)

李瑞求 (I Sŏ-gu)

On the withered reed stem by the riverside,
Spring came, time passed, and the flower bloomed,
But saying there is no one to see, shedding tears,
Sadly, the reed flower withers alone.

A fate to bloom and wither unknown to others,
A sad spring hue whose flower color cannot last long.
Do not discard it saying it has no color or fragrance,
The love hidden in the heart burns like fire.

How many times has it withered and bloomed again?
Even welcoming spring is now soaked in tears.
The sad pleas fluttering on every stem,
Saying there is no one to hear, it cries again.

노래야 바람타고 (Song, Ride the Wind)

A single paulownia leaf flutters in the wind,
One whose heart aches seeing the moon embraces it.
On this autumn night, already filled with tears,
How can I sleep alone on a cold pillow?

Who said only autumn nights are lonely?
There are also people who sigh looking at flowers.
If even a flower garden without a lover is sad,
How much more so an autumn night forsaken by a lover?

Embracing the paulownia leaf that longs for the old branch,
I sing alone the song I hummed long ago.
Song, ride the wind and fly, fly away.
Others may not know, but the lover will surely know.

님마저 가네 (Even the Lover is Leaving)

Where shall I go to catch the departing spring?
Following the wind, I will cross the mountain.

When the wind blows, flower petals fly,
When fallen flowers fall, spring also leaves.

It's not me who is sad that spring is leaving,
But they say when spring goes, even the lover leaves.<226>

My heart wants to catch the departing lover,
But the silent lover makes me sad.

If even the colors of a flowerless spring are sad,
How much more so a love that has lost its heart?<227>

진달내 (Azalea)

劉道順 (Yu To-sun)

The lover who said they would come when the azaleas bloom,
Even when the azaleas fall, they do not come.
Three springs have passed in waiting,
Every time the azaleas bloom, I cry sadly.

Seeing the azaleas bloom every year,
The waiting each spring feels new in my heart.
As that spring of azaleas passes by,
I intend to live embracing a lonely dream.

님의 무덤 (The Lover's Grave)

Climbing up to find the collapsed old fortress wall,
It is the lover's grave where crows fly back and forth.
Love is futile, like a dream.

Wandering mist, let out a sigh,
Shedding tears in the setting evening,
The heart that found the lover's grave is desolate.

The grave by the old fortress wall is my lover's grave,
On a moonlit, deep night, the cuckoo cries,
The futility of love is even sadder.

朝鮮打令 (Chosŏn T'aryŏng - Ballad of Korea)

Ah - Mt. Paektu rises, spreading its vital energy,
The fields of the three thousand leagues are fertile.
Era jowa eolssu era jowara (Exclamations of joy)
In the place where the five great rivers and ten great mountains spread,
The twenty million people live well.
Let's joyfully bless Chosŏn.

Ah - The five grains in the fields form golden waves,
The fields of the three thousand leagues leap in dance.
Era jowa eolssu era jowara
Mt. Kŭmgang, Mt. Myohyang, mountains upon mountains,
Spread silk with rare flowers and wondrous herbs.
Let's joyfully bless Chosŏn.

Ah - Culture shines brilliantly like blooming flowers,
The fields of the three thousand leagues are filled with song.
Era jowa eolssu era jowara
Amnok River, Tuman River, rivers upon rivers,<228>
Create jade streams with golden fish and silver fish.
Let's joyfully bless Chosŏn.

Ah - Outstanding figures are born in great numbers,
The fields of the three thousand leagues live and leap.
Era jowa eolssu era jowara
Heroes, great men, and men of letters appear,
Their words and deeds live powerfully.
Let's joyfully bless Chosŏn.<229>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of twelve popular song lyrics (가요) from 1935, formatted as poems. The poems cover themes common in popular songs of the era: longing for absent lovers ("Red Tear Resentment," "Holding a Flower," "A Rainy Night," "Tear-stained Shadow," "Azalea"), sorrow over separation and loss ("Sorrowful Flute Sound," "The Lover's Grave," "Even the Lover is Leaving"), melancholy reflections on nature and time ("Nŭngnado Island," "Reed Flower," "Song, Ride the Wind"), and finally, a starkly contrasting patriotic ode ("Chosŏn T'aryŏng"). The majority express personal sadness and yearning, often using natural imagery, characteristic of sentimental popular music. The final piece, "Chosŏn T'aryŏng," stands out with its overt celebration of Korea's landscape, resources, culture, and people, using celebratory refrains. For historians, this collection offers insight into popular sentiment and cultural expression during the Japanese colonial period. The juxtaposition of personal laments with the final, almost defiantly optimistic, patriotic song is particularly noteworthy, perhaps reflecting complex emotional undercurrents and articulations of national identity within the constraints of the time.
