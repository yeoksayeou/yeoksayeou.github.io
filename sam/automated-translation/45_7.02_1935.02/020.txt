朝鮮 基督靑年運動 振興策 -아울너 聯合會 及 靑年會의 改造案-
(Tension in the Two Great Camps of Christianity and Ch'ŏndogyo, Trends Among the 30 Million Believers and the New Leadership
Revival Plan for the Korean Christian Youth Movement - Including Reorganization Proposals for the Federation and the Youth Association -)

Recently, Mr. Shin Hŭng-u (申興雨), General Secretary of the Korean Christian Youth Association (朝鮮 基靑總務), and Mr. Hyŏn Tong-wan (玄東完), Associate General Secretary, have resigned. On this occasion, voices calling for a major revitalization of the Christian Youth Movement are rising throughout the entire peninsula society. Considering that just 10 years ago, it had 3,000 members and received a huge subsidy of 300,000 won from the United States, and under the elder Yi Sang-jae (李商在), outstanding talents gathered like clouds, fulfilling the role of a great reservoir of culture in this land, how has it come to pass today that, despite having a huge corporate body worth 1 million won, the number of members has drastically decreased, the US subsidy has drastically dropped to around 40,000-50,000 won, and there is a tendency for it to gradually drift away from the actual situation of the vibrant Korean society? Therefore, isn't it feasible to return the Christian Youth Movement to its former heyday?<18> Thus, our company has gathered and is publishing esteemed opinions from various social figures based on the following key points. We hope this serves as a catalyst for the great reform and revitalization of the peninsula's Christian Youth Movement. (Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥))

(Key Points) Compared to the time when Mr. Yi Sang-jae (李商在) was in office, the Christian Youth Movement of the last 10 years has certainly been stagnant, withered, and on a downward trend. Weren't the main reasons for this:
A. The tendency towards dictatorship, negligence, and inactivity among the current leadership?
B. Factional strife in general, such as the introduction of overseas factional disputes like Northwest vs. Kiho (畿湖) factions = US-educated vs. Tokyo-educated = Yi Sŭng-man (李承晩) faction vs. An Ch'ang-ho (安昌浩) faction?
C. Depletion of funds due to the cutoff of US subsidies?
D. Changes in popular thought = namely, the existence of an anti-religious movement, and also the temporary decline of the nationalist movement and the spread of socialist ideology?
Therefore, to establish a revitalization plan now:
A. What kind of personality, ideology, and skills should the new leadership, succeeding the resigned General Secretaries Shin and Hyŏn, possess to be organized?
B. By what method should the separated local Christian Youth Associations be brought back under the Federation (聯合會), and how should the system and organization of the Kyŏngsŏng (京城) City Youth Association be improved? What are the key points for reform?
C. Methods to expand operational funds, increase membership, and greatly invigorate the association's influence?
D. How to resolve the factional strife?
E. Methods to revive US subsidies, issues concerning communication with the US?
F. What kind of projects would be good to undertake in the future?
Regarding the points above, we requested the esteemed opinions of the following individuals.<19>

Open Doors, Gather Talent Urgently
Hŏ Hŏn (許憲)

1. I express sufficient gratitude for the meritorious service of Mr. Shin Hŭng-u (申興雨) and Mr. Hyŏn Tong-wan (玄東完), who have diligently worked for the Youth Association for 10 years. Let bygones be bygones. Now, in organizing the new executive board, I hope it will be composed, as much as possible, of individuals who have frequent contact with society. Also, I hope the Youth Association will open its doors wide to allow many influential figures from the intellectual and financial worlds to join, and that it will become a stage for them.
2. It is a sad affair that the money coming from the US, which used to be 300,000 won, has dwindled to around 50,000-60,000 won. In our Korea, which has many projects that need to be done, there is no reason to refuse money coming from outside. I wish even a little more would come in to develop the intellect and industry of this land. I cannot readily think of concrete measures for the revival of the subsidy at this moment.

Do Not Rely on US Subsidies
Yi Kwang-su (李光洙)

I have little knowledge about the Christian Youth Association. Only whenever I pass through Chongno (鍾路), I always think, couldn't this fine building be used more effectively? I have neither heard nor seen the factional strife of Northwest vs. Kiho (西北幾湖), nor the Yi Sŭng-man (李承晩) faction vs. An Ch'ang-ho (安昌浩) faction strife. I have heard talk that a few individuals treat the Youth Association as their private possession in the leadership, but I do not know about that either. However, judging from the current stagnation, I generally think it is a proper course of action for the leadership to take responsibility and resign, allowing new people's skills to be tested. Who the suitable person is, of course, is not something an outsider should know or say.
I think it might be the best policy to resolutely refuse the US subsidy at this time. There is a concern that the Youth Association might become like a concession<20> because of the US subsidy. And I believe reliance should always be rejected.
Then, how can the Christian Youth Association be revitalized? On this occasion, the leadership should be completely renewed, but temporarily, as an interim executive board. The doors should be opened wide to recruit members broadly, and after a year or two, a new executive board should be organized by the public election of the newly recruited several thousand members (currently said to be only about 300). I believe this is the fundamental strategy to display the true character of a fair, democratic, constitutional Christian Youth Association.
Then, there is no need to discuss again what kind of organization and projects the future Christian Youth Association should have. Because that is determined by the constitution of the Christian Youth Association. Based on the spirit of Christ, it is to provide youth with moral, physical, and intellectual education (德, 體, 知 三育). The effective implementation of projects like training courses, lectures, social gatherings, and various sports can only be expected from the character and skill of the central figures.
On this occasion when the long-serving leadership is changing, I pray that the Board of Directors makes a great, courageous decision for major reform, bringing a fresh and lively atmosphere into the Christian Youth Association.

Focus on Rural Projects
Kim Ch'ang-je (金昶濟)

1. The position of General Secretary of the Christian Youth Association is indeed a very important one. It is natural to appoint someone with sound character, ideology, and skill to the post. However, the Christian Youth Association has two types of members: full members and associate members. Full members are those with faith in pure evangelicalism. Associate members can be members of society, but only associate members lack the right to be elected or vote. Therefore, while it would be good to open the doors on this occasion to select new executives from among those outstanding in all fields, according to the Christian Youth Association constitution, the scope seems narrow, but they will inevitably be selected from among the full members.
2. It is true that there was a subsidy from the US in former years. Of course, even now, if it were possible to receive subsidies again, it would be good to receive them and allocate them to all project expenses. However, in the US currently, many of their own Youth Associations have closed due to financial difficulties, and even those that haven't closed are feeling a lack of funds, so it seems unlikely they can afford to subsidize Korea.
3. Thinking about future projects, I haven't researched specifics. However, looking back at all past achievements, during the time when the passion for education was most intense in Korea, Christianity also considered education an urgent priority. In this sense, lecture halls, night schools, etc., were established even in remote villages, focusing efforts on enlightenment movements for a while. However, with the gradual implementation of government educational controls,<21> they were abolished or suspended, but I think they actually had many positive effects. Currently, since education has spread to a certain extent, there is more interest in rural projects than education. The reason is that just as no one can neglect food, clothing, and shelter, in agricultural Korea, one cannot help but focus on rural projects, which can be called the lifeline. I believe the revival of the ruined economy and the relief strategy for the collapsed insecurity of life lie in rural revitalization. However, one thing to add is that this should be limited to the nationally *unified* Youth Association projects, and city Youth Associations should not focus all their efforts on rural projects. In Kyŏngsŏng (京城), which is a central hub culturally, politically, and economically, there should be projects that enable numerous youths and students to gain upward momentum in academics*, social interaction, and morality. This, in fact, is the meaningful mission of the Youth Association. In other words, the mission of the city Youth Association is to illuminate the path to regeneration for those naive youths who waste time, energy, and money in mahjong clubs, tea rooms, or cafes. Once again, I leave the following slogan for the emerging youth of today: "Keep faith aristocratic, live life like a commoner." While we hold faith loftily, our lives should be lived at the most basic level. Even while holding faith loftily, the will to enter among the masses, whether starving or eating, laughing or crying together, should stir in the minds of the youth.

門戶開放, 人材聚集이 急
許憲

1. 申興雨, 玄東完 兩氏가 10년 동안이나 靑年會 일을 위하야 꾸준한 努力을 하여주신 功勞에 대하여는 十分 謝意를 表함니다. 往事는 往事고, 이제 새 幹部組織을 함에 잇서서는 될 수 잇는 대로 社會와 接觸이 만흔 人士로 되어지기를 希望함니다. 그러고 靑年會의 門戶를 크게 開放하여 思想界 財界의 有*人士를 多數 加入케 하고 또한 그 분네들의 舞臺가 되어 지게 하기를 希望함니다.
2. 米國서 30만원 씩이나 오든 돈이 5,6만원 程度로 줄어젓다는 것은 슬푼 일이외다. 하여야 할 事業을 만히 가진 우리 朝鮮에서 박갓헤서 드러오는 돈을 拒絶할 까닭도 업거니와 조곰이라도 더 드러오게 하여서 이 땅 人智와 産業을 開發케 하엿스면 조켓슴니다. 補助金의 復活에 대한 具體的 方策은 蒼卒間 잘 생각나지 안슴니다.

米國補助을 밋지 말나
李光洙

나는 基督敎靑年會에 대하여서는 별로 智識이 없습니다. 다만 鍾路를 지날 때마다 이 좋은 建物을 가지고 좀 더 소리나게 利用할 수가 없을까, 하는 생각을 늘 하엿습니다. 西北幾湖의 派爭이라든가, 李承晩派 對 安昌浩派의 派爭이라든가 하는 것은 나는 듣지도 보지고 못하엿습니다. 首腦部에 대하야 幾個人이 靑年會를 專有勿視한다는 말은 들엇스나 그것도 나는 모릅니다. 다만 現狀의 不振으로 보아서 首惱部가 引責辭職하고 새 사람의 手腕을 試驗케 하는 것은 대체로 보아서 正當한 일이라고 생각합니다. 누가 適任者인지는 물론 局外人이 알 바도 아니오 말할 바도 아닙니다.
米國의 補助金은 이 때에 斷然 拒絶하는 것이 上策인가 합니다. 米國補助金이란 것 때문에 靑年會가 一利權처럼<20> 될 근심이 없지 아니합니다. 그리고 依賴心은 언제나 唾棄할 것이라고 생각합니다.
그러면 엇지하면 基靑을 振興할 수가 잇을까. 이 機會에 首腦部를 一新하되 一時的으로 즉 臨時幹部로 하고 크게 門戶를 열어 會員을 널리 募集하야 1년이나 2년 후에 新募된 수 천 (지금은 300여명에 不過한다고 합니다.)의 會員의 公選으로 新幹部를 組織하야 公正한 民主立憲의 基督敎靑年會의 本色을 發揮하게 하는 것이 根本策인가 합니다.
그러면 今後의 基靑은 엇더한 組織과 事業을 할 것인가에 대하야는 다시 論議할 필요가 없습니다. 그것은 基靑의 憲法에 確定되어 잇는 것이니까. 基督의 精神에 基하야 靑年에게 德, 體, 知 三育을 주는 것이지오. 講習, 講演, 社交, 各種 體育 등 이 事業의 實行은 効果的으로 하고 못하는 것은 中心人物의 人格과 手腕에서 기달릴 수 밖에 없을 것입니다.
多年 心勞하던 首腦部가 갈리는 이 機會에 理事部가 大革新의 大勇斷에 出하야 淸新活潑한 긔운에 基靑이 들어오기를 빔니다.

農村事業에 置重하라
金昶濟

1. 基督敎靑年會 總務외 任이란 實로 重要한 地位올시다. 人格, 思想, 手腕이 健實한 분으로 그 任에 當케하는 것은 當然한 일이겟지요. 그런데 基督敎靑年會에는 正會員 準會員 2種別이 잇는 바 正會員은 純實한 福音主義에 信仰 가진 이요. 準會員은 社會人도 될 수 잇는 바 準會員에 限하야 被選擧權 投票權이 업슴니다. 그런 까닭에 이런 機會에 모든 方面에 卓越한 분으로 新幹部를 選出하기 위하야 門戶를 開放한다면 좃켓지만 基督敎靑年會 憲則에 의하야 範圍는 좁은 듯 하지만 不得已 正會員 中으로 選出될 것입니다.
