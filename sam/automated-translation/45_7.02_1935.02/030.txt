기사제목	戰敗國家의 失地恢復運動, 西藏과 新疆과 蒙古 (Movement for Recovery of Lost Territories in Defeated Nations: Tibet, Xinjiang, and Mongolia)
필자	金璟載
기사형태	소식 (News/Report)

戰敗國家의 失地恢復運動, 西藏과 新疆과 蒙古
						金璟載

Recent international relations are seeing the emergence of many new situations. Let us now list the important ones. First, Great Britain possesses the largest colonies, and as the birthplace of free trade principles, it had become the world's central market. Consequently, like the wealthy aristocrat who knows no hardship, it continued a life of pleasure. However, around Showa 5 [1930] [illegible text], even Britain began to see increasing deficits in its national budget, and the lives of its people gradually felt hardship. In response, the British government attempted to implement trade promotion policies as a solution, only to find that Japanese goods already dominated the world market. From this arose the Anglo-Japanese economic war.

Next, after the Manchurian Incident, the world's attention was focused on the Far East problem, due to the complicated relationships involving Japan, the Soviet Union, China, and the United States surrounding Manchuria. Because of this, the League of Nations dispatched an investigation team,<36> Japan withdrew from the League, Manchukuo continued steadfastly on its established course, the Huang Fu (黃郛) regime was established in North China according to the North China Ceasefire Agreement, and the Nationalist Government under the Chiang-Wang (蔣汪) collaboration, under the new slogan of "Resistance on one hand, negotiation on the other," tried to muddle through the situation. Consequently, the specter of a Japan-Soviet war, which had once so stimulated our nerves, gradually faded. While the entire world was engrossed in the Manchurian problem, the Soviet Union's efforts to advance into Central Asia were being completed in Xinjiang and Mongolia. Meanwhile, in Tibet, Britain, which already held special rights and interests there, was sharpening its momentum further, seeking to detach it from China.

The Anglo-Japanese economic war was Britain's flustered attempt to formulate countermeasures after its setbacks. While China, having stumbled over the Manchurian issue, was seeking support from various powers, running east and west, it had forgotten that its borderlands like Mongolia, Xinjiang, and Tibet were being encroached upon by others. Thus, the complex relationships involving Britain, the Soviet Union, Japan, and China in Xinjiang, Tibet, and Mongolia have turned the Far East into a new powder keg.

Third is the Soviet Union's entry into the League of Nations and its accompanying diplomatic shift. After the Manchurian Incident, Japan's assertive foreign policy instilled a fear of Japan in Russia. In Europe, Hitler's rise in Germany worsened relations between Russia and Germany. Furthermore, Hitler's attack on the Versailles system caused France and the Little Entente to fear Germany. These circumstances led the Soviet Union to abandon its previous stance and move towards international cooperation.

With this background, let us examine the situation in China and its border regions.
Currently, China faces two major difficulties. One is the Chinese Communist Party problem, that is, the Sinification movement (赤化運動 - Redification movement). The other is the separatist movement in the borderlands. The Southwest faction is also an issue, but it will inevitably be suppressed by the central government someday, along with the decline of the Guangdong financiers. Among these, Mongolia and Xinjiang are, on one hand, undergoing Sinification (Redification), and on the other, seeking to break free from China's control (羈絆).
Thus, the advance of the Sinification (Redification) movement and the separation of the borderlands are the two major dark clouds hanging over China.

Xinjiang lies west of Jiayuguan (嘉峪關), the western terminus of the Great Wall, east of the Pamir Mountains (葱嶺), and north of the Kunlun Mountains (崑崙山). The Kunlun Mountains were considered the ancestor of mountains, known in ancient times as the highest and most rugged range. Accordingly, Xinjiang is often referred to as the Western Regions (西域) in Chinese poetry and songs.
Transportation is inconvenient: it takes 10 days from Nanjing (南京) to Xi'an (西安) in Shaanxi Province (陝西省), and about two months from Xi'an to Dihua (迪化, modern Urumqi). Therefore, although it is Chinese territory, it is located at such a vast distance that its inhabitants are only 30% Han Chinese, 10% Manchus, Mongols, and Tibetans, while the Hui people (回民族) constitute 60% of the total population. As the races differ, so do religion, language, customs, and habits<37> entirely. Herein lies the uniqueness of Xinjiang Province, and consequently, the central Chinese government did not pay much attention even when minor political changes occurred there. Japan, holding a leading position in the Far East, also viewed it as a fire on the other side of the shore. However, for the Soviet Union, Xinjiang is one of its most valuable assets. Through Xinjiang, a "Comintern Route" (콘민테룬.루스) connecting various Soviet areas like [...], [...], [...] West, [...] Hunan [...] Central, [...] North, traversing China north to south and reaching Fujian, is formed. Especially today, after the eastward advance of [...] was blocked by the Manchurian Incident, Xin[...]jiang's importance has further increased.

Despite being such an undeveloped [...] area with not a single mile of railway track, and with roads barely amounting to three routes - 1) from Yumen (玉門) and Anxi (安西) in Gansu (甘肅) to Hami (哈密), where it splits, one branch going north of the Tianshan (天山) Mountains via Sangsŏ Kidae (相西奇臺?) to Dihua, the other going south of the Tianshan via Turpan (吐魯番) to Dihua. From Dihua, the two routes merge and reach Yili (伊犁) on the southern Russian border; 2) heading southwest from Turpan, passing through Yanqi (焉耆), Luntai (輪臺), Kucha (庫車), and Kashgar (河疏勒? 喀什噶爾), reaching Soviet Central Asia; and 3) from Kashgar, passing through Hotan (和闐) and Khotan (于闐) to near [...]. Route 1 is the planned line for the Yili Railway; route 2 is the traditional main road between China and Central Asia; and route 3 is the path taken by Xuanzang Sanzang (玄奘三藏) during the Tang Dynasty. Although these three road lines exist, from a transportation perspective, it is more integrated into the Russian sphere than Chinese territory. Using the Turksib Railway (터크.쉽鐵道) starting from Semipalatinsk, one can reach Dihua in a few days, showing the Soviet Union's geographical advantage. Therefore, the increasing Soviet influence in Xinjiang is a natural development, but Britain cannot afford to simply leave Xinjiang entirely in Soviet hands and feign ignorance. Britain needs Xinjiang to protect India and fully secure Tibet, while the Soviet Union also absolutely needs Xinjiang to hold Central Asia and secure Outer Mongolia. One side shouts opposition to Redification, the other shouts "Down with Imperialism." These irreconcilable poles are vying for influence in Xinjiang. And Japan, which until now had regarded Xinjiang as a foreign land and turned a blind eye, cannot remain indifferent if the Soviet Union takes control of Xinjiang, leading to its Redification and the completion of an "international route" (國際路線) connecting North and South China.

Xinjiang evokes images of deserts and barren land. However, in reality, Xinjiang is the treasure trove of Northwest China, and deserts cover only about one-fifth of the total area. While the climate in desert areas reaches 100 degrees Fahrenheit, elsewhere, mornings and evenings are around 70 degrees, nights 50 degrees. The coldest region is the northern Tianshan route, but days below -20 degrees Celsius number only a few throughout the year, making it much better than Heilongjiang Province in Manchuria. Why, then, has China failed to develop<38> this treasure trove until now? One reason might be the incessant internal strife since the Republic's founding, preventing resources from reaching Northwest development. Another reason might be that Yang Zengxin (楊增新) established himself in Xinjiang and resisted central interference.
Yang Zengxin initially served as the Daoyin (道尹) of Aksu (阿克蘇), then became the Zhendi Dao (鎮迪道) and concurrently Tifashi (提法師) during the Xinhai Revolution. Later, when revolutionary forces rose in Yili (伊犁), Governor Yuan Dahua (袁大化) entrusted affairs to Yang and fled. Thus, Yang became the Military Governor (都督) of Xinjiang. He later merged with Altai (阿爾泰) to become the Military Governor and Civil Governor (民政省長) of Greater Xinjiang, establishing a dictatorship based on a Xinjiang Monroe Doctrine, unrelated to the conflicts in Central China. However, in 1928, coinciding with the success of the Northern Expedition of the Nationalist Revolutionary Army, unrest arose within the province. Fan Yaonan (樊耀南), who had secret ties with the Soviet Union, collaborated with Jin Shuren (金樹仁) to assassinate Yang on July 7, 1928. This is the so-called July 7th Incident (三七之變). Jin Shuren, having conspired with Fan Yaonan to kill his benefactor Yang Zengxin, then collaborated with Jiang Songling (蔣松齡) to eliminate Fan, declaring himself Chairman of the Provincial Government and Border Defense Commissioner (邊防督辨). Then, the Manchurian Incident occurred, leading to the birth of Manchukuo. Seizing the opportunity while China was preoccupied with border issues, the Soviet Union instigated Jin Shuren to launch an independence movement. Britain, in opposition, supported Ma Zhongying (馬仲英), commander of the 36th Division in Gansu, aiming to establish a rival independent regime. Last April [1934], Jin Shuren was ousted, and Sheng Shicai (盛世才) emerged as the new figure. Sheng Shicai, Commander-in-Chief for Bandit Suppression in the Southern Circuit (南路剿匪 總指揮), became the provisional Military Governor (臨時督辨), and Liu Wenlong (劉文龍), the Education Commissioner (敎育總長), became the provisional Chairman (臨時主席). Sheng Shicai is a young man, just past 30, and one of the pro-Soviet figures. The Soviet Union helped him defeat the pro-British Ma Zhongying. Consequently, 80% of Xinjiang's foreign trade is now with the Soviet Union. As already mentioned regarding transportation, reaching Xinjiang from mainland China requires either taking the Trans-Siberian Railway to the Turksib Railway or passing through Outer Mongolia. Furthermore, numerous automobile roads are planned centering on the terminus of the Turksib Railway, and an air route connecting Kazalinsk (카질니스크?) in Soviet territory with Dihua, Tacheng (塔城), and Urga (庫倫) in Outer Mongolia is reportedly under planning. If realized, Outer Mongolia and Xinjiang will be fully incorporated into the Soviet sphere of influence. Countries that cannot simply let this happen include Britain, which fears the southward spread of the Red threat impacting India and Tibet, and must focus on preventing it. On the other hand, penetrating through India, Xinjiang, and Tibet into Sichuan (泗川) connects to British interests along the Yangtze River (長江) in southern China, making it an indispensable condition. Therefore, Britain cannot tolerate leaving Xinjiang in Soviet hands. Similarly, Japan, having succeeded in blocking the Soviet Union's major eastern advance route in Manchuria, cannot remain indifferent seeing the Comintern route being completed through Central Asia via Xinjiang, linking North and South China. Moreover, Japan's national defense after the emergence of Manchukuo parallels its Far East Monroe Doctrine, and the complex web of interests surrounding China<39> means that China's Redification cannot but be a premise for global [...]. Accordingly, Japan's presence in the Far East today serves as a global bulwark against it. Such a Japan cannot indefinitely ignore the burgeoning Soviet influence in Xinjiang. Next, China's movement to recover its rights cannot simply ignore this either. This is why Xinjiang is considered a newly established powder keg.

Similarly, [...] China [...] [...] independence movement occurred. However, compared to [...] of [...], it did not make a significant impression on observers because Tibet had long been virtually a British dependency, and from China's perspective, although Tibet fell within the Chinese sphere, it was merely an autonomous territory. Even with this feudal suzerain-vassal relationship, Tibet sought to avoid even that connection with China, leading to constant disputes. As a British special interest zone, the hand of Britain was always behind such movements, manipulating them.
Tibet is the world's highest plateau, located in western China, bordering Xinjiang Province to the north, India to the southwest, and reaching Qinghai (靑海) and Sichuan (泗川) to the southeast. Its inhabitants are the Tibetan people (西藏族), said to be descendants of the San Miao (三苗) exiled by the ancient Emperor Shun. Consequently, Han Chinese regarded them as barbarians (弔狄); they were called Huishi (回氏) during the Han Dynasty, Tubo (吐蕃) during the Tang, Xixia (西夏) during the Song, Wusizang (烏斯藏) during the Yuan, and Xifan (西番) during the Ming. Their base was always the Tibet-Qinghai region, and successive Chinese rulers treated them with disdain. Therefore, unless assimilated by the Han, they were constantly in conflict with border defense forces. So, who holds real power in Tibet? Religion dominates Tibet. Due to its proximity to India, Buddhism became the state religion. Buddhism in Tibet split into four sects: Red (紅敎), Yellow (黃敎), White (白敎), and Black (黑敎). Among these, the Red Hat sect initially gained power, but during its peak, the corruption and decadence of its monks reached extremes, causing them to lose the trust of the Tibetan people. In its place, the Yellow Hat sect rose, suppressed the Red Hat sect, and came to rule all of Tibet. The founder of the Yellow Hat sect was Tsongkhapa (宗喀巴), and the current Dalai Lama (達賴喇嘛) and Panchen Lama (班禪喇嘛) are his 13th-generation disciples.
Although the suzerain-vassal relationship between Tibet and China is said to have begun in the Tang Dynasty, the purpose here is not to clarify historical relations. During the ascendancy of the Yellow Hat sect, the Zangba Khan (藏巴汗), protector of the Red Hat sect, was killed by the Yellow Hats, which offended the Qing court. Thus, the Qing sought to conquer Tibet by exploiting the conflict between the Yellow and Red sects. After several subsequent clashes, the Amban (駐藏大臣 - Resident Minister in Tibet) appointed by the Qing court became a mere figurehead, with real power in Tibet always held by the Dalai and Panchen Lamas. Later, by skillfully exploiting the secret strife between the Dalai and Panchen Lamas, reforms were made to diplomacy, trade, taxation, etc., <40> expanding the Amban's authority. In Tibetan administration, administrative officials were appointed by the Amban, while autonomous officials were appointed from the Lamas. However, since the Tibetan army (藏兵) remained under Lama command, whenever an uprising occurred, the central government had no choice but to suppress it. Even this situation led to a direct confrontation with the maneuvering hand of Britain, which had already occupied India and was looking across. Observing Britain's attitude, the Qing court incorporated Xikang (西康) into its administrative district; Xikang was originally one of the four parts of Tibet. In the 30th year of Guangxu [1904], Assistant Amban Feng Quan (鳳全) was killed by locals. Seizing this opportunity, the Qing court began implementing provincial administration in Xikang, which led to Britain's attitude towards Tibet becoming increasingly overt. Instigated by Britain, the Dalai Lama clashed with border defense forces everywhere but was ultimately defeated and fled to British India. Although the Qing Dynasty formally achieved victory, after the Dalai Lama's exile to India, Tibet knew no peace due to disturbances caused by elements cooperating with him internally. Consequently, the Qing court was forced to send envoys requesting the Dalai Lama's return. However, the Qing court could not accept the Dalai Lama's demands: 1. Restoration of religious titles, 2. Withdrawal of the resident Chinese army, 3. Dismissal of the Amban. Negotiations broke down, and the Qing court invited the Panchen Lama from Rear Tibet (後藏) and entrusted him with the governance of Tibet.
In 1912, the Qing Dynasty collapsed, and the Republic was established. As the tide of revolution swept across China, it also affected the Chinese army stationed in Tibet. They raised the banner of revolt against the Qing-appointed Amban, Lian Yu (聯豫). Britain, waiting for the opportune moment, incited the Tibetans to rebel against China. The Chinese army stationed in Tibet was defeated, and the Dalai Lama, who had been in exile in India, returned and declared Tibet's independence, expelling all Chinese officials. The pro-China Panchen Lama fled to Beijing (北京). As a result, Xikang Province once again fell under the control of the Tibetan army. Subsequently, the Republic of China resolutely intended to dispatch troops to suppress it by force, but had to halt under the threat of non-recognition from Britain. Thus, Tibet, backed by the British Empire, dreamt of building a Greater Tibet by seizing half of Xikang Province. The Sino-Tibetan peace treaty in the 6th year of the Republic [1917] virtually recognized Tibet's independence legally. In 1930, Tibet once again ignited conflict – the Yala Monastery Incident (亞拉寺事件). It originated from border defense forces intervening in a clash between Tibetans within their defense zone. This led the Dalai Lama to dispatch troops to the border, and Xikang was formally declared as Tibetan Xikang. Thus, the so-called Kang-Zang dispute (康藏紛糾) reignited.

Tibet was famous as a land of seclusion and xenophobia. Thus, it earned the title of the world's secret country. However, after Britain occupied India, Tibet's policy of seclusion could not naturally avoid destruction. Why then did Britain covet Tibet?<41>
1. Since Tibet borders India, if Imperial Russia's influence reached this region, [...] security would be compromised.
2. Tibet is rich in resources like [...] [...], [...], making it absolutely necessary as a source of raw materials.
3. From the perspective of Britain's policy of aggression against China, the upper reaches of China's Yangtze and Yellow Rivers originate in Tibet. Therefore, if Britain established influence in Tibet, it could connect through Gansu and Sichuan to Central China, i.e., the Yangtze River basin.
Thus, the 1890 Tibet-India Convention and the 1893 Supplementary Regulations Regarding Trade between Tibet and India were concluded. In 1904, another treaty of 5 articles was signed, granting Britain exclusive rights over various interests in Tibet, including mines, railways, electricity, and businesses related to gold, silver, goods, and currency trade. Furthermore, land sales in Tibet required British approval, and Tibet promised to refuse entry to representatives of all foreign countries. Through subsequent treaties, Tibet was completely turned into a British colony, and China lost Tibet entirely. However, Tibet, now in British hands, did not stop at Tibet itself but sought to annex Xikang and occupy Qinghai. According to a report by Sang Zeshun (桑澤順), a Nationalist Government envoy dispatched earlier to investigate the Tibet issue:
"The Tibetan army, commanded by British officers, has occupied Xikang and is continuing its advance. Automobile roads run from Tibet through India and Burma, and through these routes, British goods are imported duty-free, dominating the economy. Education uses both English and Tibetan, while Chinese has been abolished. Thus, Tibet has effectively become British territory."
After the pro-British Dalai Lama returned from India and regained power, and the Panchen Lama fled to Beijing, Chinese influence in Tibet was completely expelled. Britain, having secured confirmation even through treaties, monopolized all rights and interests in Tibet. It even prohibited the exchange of representatives with foreign countries.
Thus, taking advantage of China's panic after the Manchurian Incident, Britain had the Tibetan army occupy Xikang, advance into Qinghai, and further aimed to recover former territories spanning Kangding (康定) in Sichuan, Shaanxi (陝西), and southward to central Yunnan (雲南) to build a Greater Tibet. Simultaneously, it sought to block the Soviet advance into Xinjiang and connect with its interests along the Yangtze River. The Nanjing Government could not simply overlook this situation. Therefore, seizing the opportunity of the pro-British Dalai Lama's death, the Nanjing Government is attempting to restore its influence by sending back the Panchen Lama, who was in Beiping (北平, Beijing). However, British influence is deeply entrenched, making confrontation extremely difficult.
Furthermore, in Inner Mongolia, demands for autonomy grew, leading to the permission for the establishment of the Autonomous Political Affairs Committee (自治政務委員會) in April last year [1934]. Its headquarters were set up in Bailingmiao (白靈廟), with Prince Yun (雲王) as Chairman and Prince De (德王) as Secretary-General, under the command of He Yingqin (何應欽). However, Soviet influence was already consolidated there. Moreover, after the emergence of Manchukuo and especially after the North China Ceasefire Agreement,<42> Japan cannot remain indifferent to the new developments occurring in that region. This is necessary both to protect its existing rights and interests and for national defense purposes.
As these border issues become increasingly troublesome, it is naturally a major headache for China, the most directly involved party. However, the complex interests of Britain, the Soviet Union, and Japan related to these borderlands are also becoming increasingly complicated. Viewing Manchuria as the Far East's tinderbox and powder keg is already part of bygone history. Thus, the new powder keg is sprouting in these border regions with inconvenient transportation and underdeveloped civilization. (December 15, Showa 9 [1934])<43>

**Summary**

This 1935 article by Kim Kyŏngjae analyzes the volatile situation in China's border regions—Tibet, Xinjiang, and Inner Mongolia. It portrays China as weakened ("defeated nation") and unable to control these territories, which are becoming focal points of international rivalry. Britain is consolidating control over Tibet and competing with the Soviet Union in Xinjiang to protect India and expand influence towards the Yangtze. The Soviet Union is solidifying its grip on Outer Mongolia and Xinjiang, aiming to create a "Comintern Route" through Central Asia into China proper. Japan, having secured Manchuria, is watching these developments, particularly Soviet expansion and Inner Mongolian autonomy, with concern for its own interests and regional stability ("Far East Monroe Doctrine"). The author concludes that these remote borderlands, not Manchuria, constitute the "new powder keg" of the Far East.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it reflects a Korean intellectual's view (writing under Japanese rule) on the broader geopolitical shifts impacting East Asia. It implicitly highlights China's decline, a narrative often used by Japan to justify its own expansionism. However, it also provides a detailed, seemingly objective analysis of the complex power dynamics involving multiple empires (Britain, USSR, Japan) vying for influence in China's periphery, showcasing an awareness of international relations beyond the immediate Korea-Japan-China triangle. Historians might find the framing, the detailed account of regional politics (especially Xinjiang and Tibet), and the perspective on Soviet and British actions valuable for understanding contemporary East Asian viewpoints during a period of intense imperial competition.

최근의 國際關係는 幾多의 新事態가 發見되고 잇다. 이제 그의 중요한 者를 擧하* 英國이 最大의 殖民地를 갓고 잇으며 自由貿易主義의 原産地인 만큼 世界의 中心市場이 되여 이섯고 따라서 富者의 貴公子가 군색한 것을 모루고 지낼 수 잇듯이 歡樂의 生活을 持續하고 잇는 중에 昭和 5* *** **** 英國에도 國家豫算에 赤字가 增大되고 잇고 國民의 生活은 점차로 군색을 늣기게 되엿다. 이에 英國政府는 그의 打開策으로 貿易振興策을 實行하려고 드니 世界의 市場은 어느듯 日本商品이 支配하고 이섯다. 여기에서 나온 것이 日英의 經濟戰이다.
다음에는 滿州事變 이후 世界의 注目이 極東問題에 集注되여 이섯으니 滿州를 싸고 도는 日蘇中米의 錯雜한 關係가 잇는 까닭이엿다. 그로 하야 國際聯盟에서는 調査團을<36> 파견하게 되고 日本은 脫退하게 되고 滿州國은 그저 旣定의 코-스를 그대로 발고 잇으며 北中 停戰協定에 의하야 北中에는 黃郛政權이 樹立되고 잇고 蔣汪合作의 國民政府는 이른바 一面抵抗 一面交涉이라는 새로운 口號 밋헤서 그저 局面을 히지부지하여 버리려 하니 한 때는 그럿케도 우리의 神經을 刺극 하고 잇든 日蘇開戰도 점차로 影子가 稀薄하게 되엿다. 이럿케 全世界가 滿州問題에만 熱中하야 잇는 때에 新彊과 蒙古에서는 蘇聯의 中央亞細亞의 進出工作이 完成되고 잇섯으며 西藏에서는 그 전부터 特殊權益을 갓고 잇든 英國이 一層 氣勢를 날카롭게 하야 中國에서 離脫식키고저 하고 이섯다.
日本經濟戰은 英國의 狼敗에서 唐慌히 그 對策을 세우려는 것이고 滿州問題에 失脚한 中國은 列國의 後援을 구하고저 之東之西하는 동안에 蒙古 新彊 西藏 등의 邊境이 남의 손에 侵犯되고 잇는 것을 잇(忘)고 잇섯다. 그리하야 新彊, 西藏, 蒙古에서의 英蘇, 日, 中의 錯雜한 關係는 그가 極東에 잇서 새로운 火藥庫가 되엿다.
3에는 蘇聯의 國聯 加入과 아울너 그의 外交轉換이다. 滿州事變 이후 日本의 對外 積極政策은 露西亞로 하야금 日本에 대한 恐佈心을 갓게 하엿으며 歐羅巴에서는 獨逸의 힛트러의 躍動은 露西亞와 獨逸의 關係를 惡化케 하엿으며 더욱이 힛트러의 벨사유體制 파괴의 突擊은 佛蘭西와 小協商國으로 하야금 獨逸에 대한 恐佈心을 이르키게 되엿다. 이러한 事情은 蘇聯으로 하야금 從來의 態度를 버리고 國際協調에 進出케 하얏다는 것이다.



우리는 이것을 前提하고 中國 及 그의 邊彊의 事情을 살피여 보자.
방금 中國에는 2大 難關에 當着되여 잇다. 그 하나는 中國 共産黨問題이니 中國의 赤化運動이요. 다른 하나는 邊彊의 離脫運動이 그것이다. 西南派도 問題이나 그는 廣東財閥의 沒落과 共히 점차로 中央政府의 손에 鎭壓되는 날이 반듯이 오고야 말 것이다. 그 중에도 蒙古 新彊은 一面은 赤化이요. 다른 一面으로는 中國의 羈絆에서 離脫코저 하고 잇다.
이럿케 中國에 잇서 赤化運動의 進展, 邊疆의 離脫은 그가 兩大 暗*이다.
新彊은 萬里長城의 西便盡頭인 嘉峪關 外 葱嶺의 東이 그 崑崙山의 北域이다. 山之祖宗은 崑崙山이라 하야 옛날에는 가장 놉흔 산이고 준험한 산으로 아럿으며 따라서 新彊은 中國의 詩歌에는 西域이라고 表現되여 잇다.
交通은 不便하야 南京에서 陝西省 西安까지가 10일, 西安에서 迪化까지는 약 2개월의 旅程이다. 그럼으로 中國의 領地라고 하나 넘우도 遙遠한 거리에 位置하야 잇서 그곳의 住民은 漢人은 100分之 30이요. 滿人 蒙人 藏人이 100分之 10이고 回民族이 全人口의 60퍼센트를 차지하고 잇음으로 人種이 다른이 만큼 宗敎도 言語도 風俗 習慣이<37> 全然이 다르다. 여기에 新疆省의 特異性이 잇고 따라서 거긔에 약간의 政變이 잇다기로니 中國의 中央政府는 그리 關心을 갓지 안엇섯다. 極東의 盟主的 地位에 잇는 日本도 彼岸의 火災와 갓치 보아왓든 것이다. 그러나 新疆은 蘇聯에 잇서서는 가장 貴重한 存在의 하나가 되여 잇으니 新疆을 通하야 **, **, **西, *湘 *中央, **北 등의 각 쏘벳트 *를 連絡하면서 中國의 南北를 縱斷하야 福建에 달하는 『콘민테룬.루스』가 結成된다. 더욱이 滿州事變 이후 **의 東* 進出의 第 1**이 阻止된 오날에 잇서 新***는 一層 重要性을 倍加케 된 것이다.

그리하야 1890년의 藏印條約, 1893년의 藏印通商에 관한 追加條約을 맷기에 이르럿고 1904년에는 다시 5條約을 締結하야서 西藏의 鑛山, 鐵路, 電氣 등의 각 項 利權, 金銀, 貨物, 錢幣貿易 등의 諸事業을 英國이 獨占하얏고 그리고 西藏의 土地買賣도 英國의 承諾이 잇서야 하고 西藏에는 諸外國代表의 入藏을 拒絶할 것을 約束하엿다. 그 후도 여러 차레의 條約에 依하야 西藏을 완전히 英國의 植民地化햇고 中國은 西藏을 喪失하고 마럿다. 그러나 英國의 掌 중에 든 西藏은 西藏에서만 끈치는 것이 아니요 西康을 倂呑하고 靑海를 占據하려고 한다. 일즉이 西藏問題調査次로 갓든 國民政府의 派遣員 桑澤順의 報告에 依하면
『英國將校의 指揮를 밧는 西藏軍은 西康을 占領하고 다시 前進을 게속 중이다. 自働車路는 西藏으로부터 印度와 緬甸에 貫通하야 잇고 이 通路를 통하야 英國의 商品은 無稅로 輸入되여 經濟界는 英國에게 獨占되여 잇고 敎育은 英文과 藏文을 幷用하며 漢文은 廢止되엿다. 그리하야 西藏은 事實上 英國의 領土化하엿다.』
親英派인 達賴喇嘛가 印度에서 도라와 勢力을 恢복하고 班禪喇嘛가 北京에 亡命한 후부터는 西藏에서의 中國의 勢力은 완전히 驅逐되엿고 그리고 條約上에 잇서서까지 確認을 엇은 英國은 西藏의 諸權益을 獨占하여 버럿다. 外國과 代表交換까지 禁制하야 버렷다.
그리하야 滿州問題 發生 이후 中國이 唐慌해 하는 틈을 타서 英國은 西藏軍으로 하야금 西康을 占領식키고 다시 靑海를 進격하며 나아가서는 四川의 康定, 陝西, 南으로는 雲南의 中部에 亘하는 舊領土를 回收하야 大西藏의 建設을 圖謀하며 한편으로는 蘇聯의 新彊進出을 阻止하려 하고 長江一帶의 利權과 接續식키고저 하엿다. 이 形勢를 그저 黙過만 할 수 업는 것이 南京政府이다. 그래서 南京政府는 親英派의 達賴가 죽은 것을 期會로 하야 北平에 잇든 班禪喇嘛를 보내서 勢力의 回復을 策하고 잇으나 英國의 勢力이 뿌리 깁히 扶植되여 잇음으로 그와 對抗이 결코 쉬운 일이 아니다.
그 외에 內蒙古에서는 自治要求가 커서 작년 4월에는 自治政務委員會의 成立을 許容하야 白靈廟에 本部를 두고 委員長에 雲王 祕書長에 德王이 就任하얏고 何應欽이 그의 指揮長官이 되엿다. 그러나 거긔에는 蘇聯의 勢力이 임이 鞏因한 바 잇고 滿州國 出現 이후 더욱이 北中停戰協定 이후<42> 그 地域에 잇서 發生되고 잇는 新事態에 대하야 無關心할 수 업는 것이 日本의 처지이다. 그는 旣存의 權益을 擁護하기 위하야서 그러하고 國防上 그러하다.
이럿케 邊疆問題가 식그러워즈니 가장 直接 當事者인 中國의 頭痛이 클 것은 물론이나 그 邊境에 關聯되여 잇는 英, 蘇, 日 등의 錯雜한 利害關係도 가면 갈사록 複雜性을 倍加하고 잇다. 滿州를 極東의 빨간이라 하고 火藥庫라고 보든 것은 벌서 한 개의 묵은 歷史로 넘어가고 마럿다. 그리하야 新火藥庫는 交通도 不便하고 人文도 未開한 이들 邊境에서 싹트고 잇다. (昭和 9. 12. 15일)<43>
