私慾보다도
						米國大富豪 록펠라

사회에 나가서 활약하려 하는 청년 제군에게 나는 이 말을 전하고 십다.- 제군은 살라리만이 되든지 또는 제 손으로 사업을 경영하든지 간에 단지 일신상의 사리사욕에만 잡혀서는 안된다.

자기의 이해를 생각하기 전에 몬저 사회때문에 활동하여라. 그러면 저절로 성공의 榮冠이 오리라. <161>

More Than Private Desire
						American Tycoon Rockefeller

To you young gentlemen who intend to go out into society and be active, I want to convey these words.— Whether you become salarymen or manage a business with your own hands, you must not simply be caught up in personal private interests and desires.

Before thinking of your own benefit, first be active for the sake of society. Then, the crown of glory (榮冠) of success will come automatically. <161>

**Summary**

Attributed to the American tycoon Rockefeller, this short piece advises young people entering the workforce or business to prioritize contributing to society over pursuing personal gain. Success, the author claims, will naturally follow from dedicating oneself to societal well-being rather than selfish interests. From a historical perspective, this article published in colonial Korea (1934) is interesting. It presents an idealized capitalist work ethic via a famous American figure, potentially serving as a model for diligence and societal contribution, though the definition of 'society' (Korean society vs. the Japanese Empire) remains ambiguous in the colonial context. Historians might examine how such messages of self-improvement and societal focus were framed and received during a period of limited Korean agency.
