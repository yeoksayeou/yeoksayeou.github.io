A Day in the Life of a Newspaper President - Mr. Pang Ŭngmo, President of the Chosŏn Ilbo
In the suburbs, in Chukt'am-jŏng (竹添町), beyond Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun School, is a Western-style house amidst the woods where many Westerners live, where the Dutch Legation (和欄公使館) is located, and where there are many modern "culture houses." This is Mr. Pang's home. He wakes up around eight in the morning, meets visitors related to the newspaper company and his mining businesses, and then leaves for the company by car around ten in the morning.

Then, in the executive office, he discusses, decides, and directs matters with Vice President Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), Editorial Bureau Chief Kim Hyŏngwŏn (金炯元), Business Bureau Chief Kim Kibŏm (金箕範), and General Affairs Bureau Chief Mun Sŏkchun (文錫俊氏), such as the groundbreaking for the new company building construction, the installation of a high-speed rotary press, and matters related to the Police Affairs Bureau (警務局). In all these plans, even if the newspaper's pages are not decorated splendidly for the time being, that is fine; he focuses all his efforts on establishing long-term, sustainable policies. During this time, he meets branch managers coming up from the provinces, greets visiting guests, and, unlike other newspaper presidents, gets involved in every detail of cash flow, personally inspecting the payment slips coming up from the Business Bureau. Sometimes he clicks away smartly on the abacus (수판질), gives directives on expenditures, noting whether too much was spent or too little received. In the evening, for social engagements, he goes around places like Myŏngwŏlgwan (명월관) and Siktobŏl (食道閥), visits the homes of financial magnates (財閥) and high officials (大官), invites guests from home and abroad, and is invited himself. He also attended an evening invitation from Army Commander Kawashima (川島軍司令), returned, and donated anti-aircraft guns (高射砲). Furthermore, when mine owners (鑛山王) from his home P'yŏngan region come up, he meets and talks with them. Occasionally, when a big mine owner like Mr. Pak Yongun (朴龍雲氏) comes, he introduces them to financiers in Japan until late at night and also plans land reclamation work in Suwŏn (水原).

When colleagues like Mr. Ko Ilch'ŏng (高一淸氏) come up to the capital, he becomes even busier with various matters. Moreover, as Mr. Pang still has several successful mines (鑛山) in the Hamgyŏng and P'yŏngan provinces, he secretly worries and puts effort into the mining business. And recently, he has been even busier because of the plan to start construction on a grand (宏壯한) 5-story company building on Kwanghwamun Street (光化門通), spending over 100,000 won. He usually goes to bed (就寢) around eleven or twelve at night. - In the next issue, we will publish "A Day in the Life of a Bank President." - <46>

**Summary**

This 1934 article details a typical day for Pang Ŭngmo, the president of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper. It portrays him as a hands-on manager involved in both editorial/business strategy (new building, printing press, dealing with police censorship) and financial details, even using an abacus. His day involves meeting visitors, managing staff, extensive socializing with officials, financiers, and fellow mine owners (reflecting his own background as a "mining king"), and handling his ongoing mining interests. He is depicted as focusing on the long-term sustainability of the newspaper and engaging actively in the colonial era's business and social elite circles, including interactions with Japanese military figures like Commander Kawashima, to whom he even donated weaponry.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is revealing. It shows a prominent Korean businessman navigating the complexities of the colonial system: managing a major Korean newspaper (likely under censorship from the Police Affairs Bureau mentioned), engaging with Japanese military leaders (attending dinners, making donations), leveraging connections between Korean and Japanese business circles (introducing mine owners to Japanese financiers), and balancing media operations with personal business interests (mining). The mention of donating anti-aircraft guns to the Japanese military commander is particularly striking, highlighting the collaborative aspects and pressures faced by Korean elites during this period. Historians might find the specific names, the description of business practices, the social interactions, and the casual mention of dealing with colonial authorities (like the Kyŏngmuguk) of interest.

新聞社長의 하로-方應模氏, 朝鮮日報社長
교외 竹添町 延禧專門學校 너머가는 洋人 만히살고 和欄公使館잇고 文化住宅이 만흔 그 수풀사이의 洋屋. 이것이 方氏의 집이다. 아츰 여덜시쯤 이러나서 新聞社 관계와 鑛山 관계의 來客을 맛나고 그리고나서는 自働車로 아츰 열시쯤 社로 出勤한다.
그래서는 重役室에서 副社長 李光洙 編輯局長 金炯元 營業局長 金箕範 總務局長 文錫俊氏들로 더부러 사옥신축기공에 대한 이약이와 초고속도 윤전기노을 일과 警務局 關係일들을 의론하고 결정하고 지휘한다. 이 모든 作定이란 것은 비록 新聞紙面은 일시적으로 화려하게 꾸미지 안어도 조흐니 오직 오래오래 하도록 持久方策을 세우는데 전력한다. 이러는 동안에 地方에서 올나오는 支分局長들을 맛나고 인사오는 손님을 맛나고 그리고 다른 新聞社長들과는 달느게 금전출납에 일일이 간여하야 營業局에서 올나오는 전표검사를 親히하고 엇든 때 는 수판질도 똑똑 띄우기도 하고 만히 지출하엇느니 적게 밧엇느니 하는 出納指揮도 하고 저녁이면 사교관계로 명월관 食道閥으로 도라 아니며  財閥과 大官집을 찻기도 하고 내외지로부터 오는 賓客을 招待하기도 하며 招待밧기도 하고 또는 川島軍司令의 저녁招待를 밧고 갓다가 도라와서 高射砲도 寄附하고 더욱 故鄕인 平安地方서 鑛山王들이 올너오면 맛나 이약이도 하며 각금 朴龍雲氏가튼 큰鑛山王이 오면 밤늦도록 日本 잇는 재벌들에게 소개도 하여 주고 水原開懇일을 꾸미기고 하고.
僚友 高一淸氏등이 상경하면 이일 저일로 더욱 분주하고 더구나 氏에게는 아직도 咸鏡道와 平安道방면에 成績조흔 鑛山이 數個所잇슴으로 鑛山일로 남몰래 惱心도 하며 애도 쓴다. 그리고 요지간은 10여 만원 드리어 光化門通에다가 宏壯한 5층社屋을 起工하기로 되어 그 일때문에 더욱 奔走하다. 밤은 대개 열 한시 열두시頃 就寢 - 이 다음 號에는 『銀行頭取의 하로』를 싯겟슴니다-」<46>
