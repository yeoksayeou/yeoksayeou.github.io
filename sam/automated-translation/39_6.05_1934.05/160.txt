Magazine Title: 삼천리 제6권 제5호
Publication Date: 1934년 05월 01일
Article Title: 新聞社長의 하로 宋鎭禹氏, 東亞日報社長 (A Day in the Life of a Newspaper President: Mr. Song Chin-u, President of the Dong-A Ilbo)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

Since he doesn't drink much alcohol lately, he usually wakes up around 7 AM, strolls through the fields, quietly contemplates and strategizes for the prosperity of the Dong-A Ilbo (東亞日報), then reads a bit from newly published books on democracy, which he always enjoys. After finishing breakfast, he departs from his Wŏn-dong (苑洞) residence, travels via the An'guk-dong (安國洞) road to the Kwanghwamun (光化門) headquarters, riding atop his private rickshaw, arms folded, beaming with a smile. It's said that if people in Chang'an (長安 - Seoul) see someone riding a rickshaw, they should know it's either OOO [name missing/illegible in original source] or Mr. Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹氏), so much does he favor riding this era-lagging rickshaw.

Perhaps this is a habit remaining from 5 or 6 years ago when socialists were rampant and cherry-wood clubs (사꾸라 몽둥이 - likely referring to police batons) would sometimes unexpectedly fly out; as a personal protection measure (身邊保護策), he would put the rickshaw puller ahead of him, and it seems he continues to ride the rickshaw even now.

Upon arriving at the company, he summons Business Bureau Chief Yang Wŏn-mo (梁源模氏) and Assistant Editor Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植氏) and others to the President's office. There, he directs, commands, and consults on matters related to editing, business, external relations, policy, and relations with the Government-General (總督府). Meanwhile, he receives greetings from Japanese advertisers and transferring government officials, and meets and talks with various visitors who come for this or that reason. He has just one principle: he refuses all meetings with female visitors.

Late in the evening, before the final proofs go to print, he personally reviews the layout (臺帳) of all eight pages of that day's newspaper. He usually has dinner out due to social obligations, either at a restaurant or a friend's house, and then spends the night socializing. For example, though he's not there now, he once visited the residence of Military Police Commander Iwasa (岩佐憲兵隊司令官) at Waesŏngdae (倭城臺), led by reporter Kim Tong-jin (金東進君), and shared drinks (酒盃) (furthermore, it was the beginning of the New Year then, and Army Chief of Staff Kodama (兒玉軍參謀長) was also present at that gathering). When Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙氏) hosted Hamnam (咸南) Police Inspector Ch'oe T'ak (崔鐸警視), who had come up to the capital, for dinner at his home, Song attended along with Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯氏). He is thus busy associating with high officials of the Government-General and influential private citizens, usually going to bed only as it nears eleven or twelve o'clock at night.<44>

**Summary**

The article details the daily routine of Song Chin-u, the president of the Dong-A Ilbo newspaper in 1934. It describes his morning habits, his commute by private rickshaw (a notable preference possibly stemming from safety concerns during earlier political unrest), his management activities at the newspaper involving editorial, business, and government relations, his strict policy of not meeting female visitors, and his evenings spent socializing with prominent Korean figures and high-ranking Japanese officials, including military police and government figures. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the daily life and networking strategies of a prominent Korean media figure under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting his navigation between Korean nationalism (running a major Korean newspaper, reading about democracy) and the realities of colonial power (socializing with Japanese military and government officials, concerns about political violence). His use of a rickshaw for perceived safety and his interactions with figures like the Kempeitai commander are particularly revealing of the era's tensions and power dynamics.

新聞社長의 하로 宋鎭禹氏, 東亞日報社長
요지간은 술을 만히 자시지 안키 때문에 아츰이면 대개 7시경에 이러나서 들로 거닐면서 東亞日報의 繁榮에 대하야 고요히 생각도 하고 計策도 하고 난 뒤에 늘 愛讀하는 민주주의에 대한 新刊書籍을 좀 보고 朝飯을 마치고 苑洞自宅으로부터 安國洞 길을 거처 光化門本社까지 專用人力車 우에 올나타고 팔장을 끼고 벙글벙글 笑顔을 띄면서 出動한다. 입납분이들이 長安안에 인력거 탄 이를 보거든 OOO 아니면 宋鎭禹氏인줄 알나고 하리만치 氏는 時代遲의 이 인력거를 愛乘한다.
아마 이것은 사회주의자가 全盛하야 사꾸라 몽둥이가 불의에 각금 튀어나오든 5, 6년전에 身邊保護策으로 인력거꾼을 압세우고 다니든 습관이 그저 남어잇서 지금까지도 인력거를 타고 단임인 듯.
社에 나와서는 營業局長 梁源模氏 編輯次長 薛義植氏 등을 連해 社長室로 招請하여 編輯 營業 對外關係 주장 總督府關係-일을 지휘하고 명령하고-謀議하고-이리하는 한편 日本廣告主와 轉勤오는 官公吏의 인사밧기와 이일저일로 차저오는 訪客을 접하고 이약이하고 단 한 가지 信條가 잇다. 여성의 來客은 一切 면회를 사절한다.
저녁 늦게 인쇄에 너머가기 전의 그날 新聞8면의 臺帳을 일일히 보고 저녁은 夕飯을 대개 交際關係로 요리점이나 友人집에서 마치고 그러고 사교로 밤을 보내게 된다. 一例를 들면 지금은 업지만은 기자 金東進君을 압세우고 倭城臺의 岩佐憲兵隊司令官집에 방문하여 酒盃도 나누고(더욱 그때는 新年正初로 그 席座는 兒玉軍參謀長도 同席하엿더라고) 金性洙氏가 상경한 咸南崔鐸警視를 자택에서 夕飯을 가치나눌 때 金炳魯氏와 함께 陪席도 하고 그러고 總督府大官이나 民間有力人士와 交遊에 奔走하야 밤은 늘 열한시 열두시가 갓가워야 就寢한다.<44>
