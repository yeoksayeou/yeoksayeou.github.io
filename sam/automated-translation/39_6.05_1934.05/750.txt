苦難을 맛나거든
						佛蘭西 文豪 에밀, 졸라

필경 인생에서 困苦艱難은 제거하기 어려운 것이다. 그 困苦艱難에 맛나슬 때 덤베고 낭패하지 말어라. 그럴 때에는 자기를 떠나서 友人의 입장이 되어 그 고난을 바라보는 이 조타. 그리하여 하로 밤 자면서 잘 생각하여 보라. 반다시 그 이튼날 아츰에는 빗나는 태양이 차저와 줄 것이다. <164>

When You Encounter Hardship
						French Great Writer Émile Zola

Ultimately, hardship and difficulty (困苦艱難) are unavoidable in life. When you encounter such hardship and difficulty, do not rush in recklessly and be dismayed. At such times, it is good (조타) to step away from yourself, take the perspective of a friend (友人), and observe that hardship. Then, sleep one night (하로 밤) while thinking it over carefully. Surely (반다시), the next morning (이튼날 아츰), the shining sun will come find you. <164>

**Summary**

This short piece, attributed to the French author Émile Zola, offers simple advice for dealing with hardship. It suggests that instead of panicking when faced with difficulties, one should detach emotionally, view the situation objectively as an outsider or friend would, contemplate it overnight, and trust that a brighter perspective or solution will emerge the next day. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it shows the popularization of Western literary figures and their (simplified) ideas in Korea through widely read magazines. It presents a stoic, perhaps universally comforting message during a period of national difficulty, though it avoids any direct political commentary, possibly reflecting the constraints or focus of such publications. Historians might note it as an example of cultural transmission and the type of accessible philosophical advice consumed by the Korean public or intelligentsia at the time.
