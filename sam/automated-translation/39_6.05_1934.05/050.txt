Magazine Title: 삼천리 제6권 제5호
Publication Date: 1934년 05월 01일
Article Title: 楊柳와 府尹 (Willows and the Prefect)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 세태비평 (Social Commentary)

A. When I went to P'yŏngyang, I saw that along the Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk riverside path leading up to Pubyŏngnu, they had planted coarse acacia trees all in a single row (一列字). It seems to be the handiwork of the P'yŏngyang Prefect (平壤府尹氏), but for the same cost, why couldn't they plant lush green weeping willows, fitting for the foremost landscape (第一江山)?

B. Does the Prefect (府尹氏) even know the difference between acacia and willow? Such matters should rightly be handled by a newspaper company. If they threw down a thousand won, they could plant willows, which beautifully express the island's sentiment (島情調), not just along Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk, but also on the road to Manwŏldae, or ascending Panwŏlsŏng, or even, if nothing else, as roadside trees on Seoul's Chongno. Didn't the Osaka Asahi plant willows on Tokyo's Ginza street? How much goodwill the 'Ginza willows' (銀座の柳ぎ) garnered from the five million citizens of Tokyo! It's regrettable that our newspaper companies, usually so good at imitation, haven't conceived of this one thing.

A. Well, if one newspaper company starts doing it now, perhaps other newspaper companies will rush (우-) to follow suit someday.

B. Truly infuriating (憤)! Instead of leaving such matters to the Prefects and County Magistrates (府尹郡守氏), people like Mr. Song (宋某氏), Mr. Pang (方某氏), and Mr. Yŏ (呂某氏) should get their act together and step up.<34>

**Summary**

This short dialogue criticizes the P'yŏngyang Prefect for planting unsuitable acacia trees instead of aesthetically pleasing willows along a scenic riverbank. The speakers lament the official's lack of taste and suggest that Korean newspaper companies should take the initiative to fund willow planting, citing a successful example by the Osaka Asahi newspaper in Tokyo. They express frustration that prominent Korean figures (likely newspaper owners) have not undertaken such culturally significant projects. From a historical perspective, the article reveals dissatisfaction with the aesthetic choices made by Japanese-appointed colonial officials (Puyun) and a longing for culturally appropriate landscaping reflecting Korean tastes. It highlights the role newspapers were expected to play in public life and points to a nascent sense of Korean cultural nationalism, even while referencing Japanese models, expressing frustration that Korean leaders weren't taking similar civic actions.

楊柳와 府尹
A. 平壤가보니 浮碧樓 올라가는 淸流壁 江邊에 가로수를 심는다고 왁살마진 아까시야를 一列字로 쭉-늘어노앗데. 平壤府尹氏의 솜씨인 모양인데 가튼갑이면 푸르靑靑 수양버들로 웨 심으지 못하나 第一江山에 어울니게서리.
B. 府尹氏가 아까시야와 버들의 구별을 아나 그런 일은 맛당이 新聞社에서 할 일이야 돈 천원 내어던지면 淸流壁뿐 아니라 滿月臺가는 길이라든지 半月城 오르는데 라든지 하다못해 서울 鐘路의 가로수에든지 島情調를 잘 나타내는 楊柳를 심을 수 잇스련만 大阪朝日에서 東京銀座通에다 버들을 심으지 안엇는가『銀座の柳ぎ』라고 얼마나 5백만 東京시민에게 호감을 주엇든가. 모방할 줄 잘 아는 新聞社에서 엇재 이 한가지만은 착상치 안는지 애달부이.
A. 이제 한 新聞社에서 하기시작하면 다른 新聞社에서 또 우-하고 따라할 날이 잇겟지.
B. 참말 憤하이 府尹郡守氏에게 맛기지 말고 이런 일은 宋某氏, 方某氏, 呂某氏가 정신차려 나설 일이야.<34>
