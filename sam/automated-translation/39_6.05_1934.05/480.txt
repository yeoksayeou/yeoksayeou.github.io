Don't Just Dream
						British Plutocrat Rothschild

Enterprise is not fantasy. One who merely embraces dreams and spends their days idly is like one fasting and awaiting death. There is no reason why income should ever arise in life where there is no toil. The God of Hunger (飢餓의 神) peeps into every house, but can never crawl through the doors and windows of a house where the sentry of labor stands guard. Work exists for the diligent person, and only then will all the hardships of life disperse like clouds and mist.<95>

**Summary**

This short piece, attributed to Rothschild, strongly advocates for diligence and hard work over passive dreaming. It argues that success and sustenance come only through labor, which acts as a guard against hardship ("the God of Hunger"). The article uses metaphors to emphasize that mere wishing or fantasy leads nowhere, while industriousness overcomes life's difficulties. For historians studying colonial Korea, this article reflects the promotion of self-help and industriousness narratives common during the period. Attributing such advice to a famous Western capitalist like Rothschild likely aimed to lend it authority and potentially align it with modernization ideals, albeit within the constraints of the colonial context.

꿈만 꾸지말나
						英國大富豪 로스촤일드
企業은 空想이 아니다. 한갓 꿈을 안고 그저 지내는 자는 斷食하고 죽엄을 기다리고 잇는 것과 갓다. 인생에 勞苦업는 곳에 결코 所得이 생길 까닭이 업다. 飢餓의 神은 집집마다 엿보고 다니나, 노동의 把守兵이 잇는 집 門窓으로는 결코 기어드러가지 못한다. 근면한 자를 위하야 일은 잇는 것이요, 그래야 모든 생활의 괴로움은 雲散霧消하리라.<95>
