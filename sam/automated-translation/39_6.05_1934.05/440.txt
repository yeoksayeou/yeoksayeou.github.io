戰爭과 文明
						獨逸哲學者 니체-

戰爭은 非難하여 말하자면 말할 수 잇다. 그것은 승리자를 甘醉케 하고 敗北者를 울게 하는 것이라고.

또 戰爭을 찬송하여 말하자면, 말하잘 수가 잇다. 그 단계을 지나서 新陳代謝가 행하여, 인간이 좀더 힘굿세게 更生하는 것이라고, 엇잿거나 戰爭온 문명에게는 睡眠시간으로 그의 성장상에 반드시 나타나는 현상이다.<85>

War and Civilization
                        German Philosopher Nietzsche-

War can certainly be spoken of in terms of criticism. It is said that it intoxicates the victor and makes the vanquished weep.

Also, one can speak in praise of war. It is said that after passing through that stage, renewal (新陳代謝) occurs, and humans are reborn (更生) somewhat more powerfully (힘굿세게). In any case (엇잿거나), war is like sleep time for civilization, a phenomenon that inevitably appears in its growth.<85>

**Summary**

The article, attributed to Nietzsche, presents a dual perspective on war: it can be criticized for causing suffering (making victors drunk and the vanquished weep), but also praised as a necessary stage for societal renewal (新陳代謝) and human regeneration (更生), making humanity stronger. Ultimately, it frames war as an inevitable "sleep time" essential for civilization's growth. Published in 1934 colonial Korea, this piece likely reflects or serves to bolster the increasingly militaristic ideology promoted by Imperial Japan. Historians might find it interesting as an example of how Western philosophy (specifically a potentially selective interpretation of Nietzsche) was employed in colonial contexts to legitimize conflict and aggression as forces of progress and revitalization, aligning with Japanese expansionist narratives of the era.
