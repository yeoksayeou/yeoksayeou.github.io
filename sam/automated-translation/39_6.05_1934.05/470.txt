劣等人間
						印度詩聖 다골!

Humans who live by eating human flesh (人肉) think, upon seeing a person, "This is my food (糧食)." They do not know the noble value of human flesh. They are detestable (可憎한) multitudes. Therefore, we call them barbarians (野蠻人) and regard them lowly. However, even in civilized countries (文明國), there are also groups (徒輩) who make the human flesh of their compatriots (동포) their food. That is, those who engage in the act of gathering money by buying and selling weak women based merely on the value of their flesh (肉의 가치). They, indeed, should be called detestable (可憎할) humans, even more inferior (劣等한) than barbarians (野蠻人).<94>

**Summary**

The article, attributed to Rabindranath Tagore, condemns those in "civilized countries" who traffic women, comparing them unfavorably to cannibals ("barbarians"). While cannibals consume flesh out of ignorance of its higher value, these traffickers exploit women solely for monetary gain based on their physical bodies ("flesh value"), making them morally inferior. For historians, this piece reflects the importation of international intellectual thought (Tagore) into colonial Korea to critique social ills, specifically the commodification and exploitation of women, framing it as a problem even within supposedly "civilized" societies, potentially including colonial Korea itself or its colonizer, Japan. The use of terms like "compatriots" (동포) and the stark moral contrast could also subtly resonate with nationalist sentiments against perceived societal degradation under colonial rule.
