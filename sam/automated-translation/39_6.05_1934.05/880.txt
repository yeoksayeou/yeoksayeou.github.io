Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Sailor's Wife (水夫의 안해)

Why does my beloved not come?
If I wait daily at the port (浦口),
Only the blue water of the sea endlessly turns,
And there's no way to know news of my beloved.<237>

The white sail that appears and disappears daily,
Hoping perhaps he might be aboard, if I look,
It just goes over the distant edge of the sea,
And there's no way to see my beloved's boat.

When I call for my love with this cruel heart (心思),
A seagull cries alone, circling the sky,
And only waves splash, splash against the shore;
My beloved gives no answer (對答).

Lovesickness (相思苦)

Oh wind, brushing past my temple hair,
From where have you come here?
Did you come from blowing on my beloved's face?
Please convey this thought of mine to my beloved.

Oh bright moon at the window on this Mid-Autumn Eighth Month (中秋 八月),
From your high place, you see this world (世上),
How is my beloved's heart (맘) now?
Please shine this haggard heart (맘) upon my beloved.

How many days will this one life (生) be, like blue water?
Leaves (닙) sprout and flowers (꼿) bloom, then the wind blows,<238>
In autumn, even those wither;
My body grows old like this because of my beloved.

Rainbow (무지개)

Is this youth (靑春) a rainbow?
My colorful, cherished dream
Sets its light (빗) across the whole sky, but
As the day breaks, there's not even a trace (자최).

Is this youth (靑春) a rainbow?
This light pink flower blossom (꼿송이) of mine
Flutters in the spring garden, but
When the wind blows, they all fall (떨니네).

Is this youth (靑春) a rainbow?
The beautiful heart (맘) that wandered the sky
Dances as if it's its time, but
As it fades (슬어지니), only sighs remain.

- On a spring day, 1934 -<239>

**Summary**

This article presents three poems by Kim Ansŏ: "Sailor's Wife," "Lovesickness," and "Rainbow." The poems explore themes of longing for an absent beloved, the pain of separation and waiting, and the ephemeral nature of youth and dreams, comparing them to a fleeting rainbow. From a historical perspective, these poems, written during the Japanese colonial period, express personal sentiments of yearning and transience. While not explicitly political, the themes of waiting, separation, and the fading of beautiful things (like youth or dreams) might implicitly reflect the broader atmosphere of loss, constrained lives, or uncertain futures felt by Koreans under colonial rule. The adoption of folk song style (俗謠) also points to an engagement with traditional Korean forms.

水夫의 안해

그립은 우리 님은 웨 안오시노
날마다 浦口에서 기두노라면
바다인 프론 물뿐 限업시 돌고
그립은 님 消息은 알 길이 업네.<237>

날마다 감실감실 도는 흰돗대,
幸혀나 님 타신가 바라노라면
저 멀리 바다 끗만 넘어가고요,
그립은 님의 배는 볼 길도 업네.

야속한 이 心思에 님을 부르면
갈매기 하늘 돌며 혼자 웨치고
기슭엔 찰삭찰삭 물결뿐이요,
그립은 우리님은 對答도 업네.

相思苦

귀밋털을 스치며 도는 바람아
어듸로서 여긔를 네가 왓느냐,
님의 얼골 불다가 네가 와드냐,
이 내 생각 님에게 傳해주렴아.

中秋 八月 窓까에 밝은 이 달아
놉혼 곳서 이 世上 네가 보거니,
님의 맘이 只今은 엇더 하드냐,
예윈 이 맘 님에게 비쳐주렴아.

이 한 生은 프른 물 메츨될는고,
닙이 나고 꼿 피자 바람이 불고<238>
가을철엔 그나마 말으는 것을,
이 내 몸은 님탓에 그러늙노라.

무지개

이 靑春은 무지개든가
을굿불긋 어린 내 꿈이
왼 하늘에 빗을 놋컨만
날이 드니 자최도업네.

이 靑春은 무지개든가
연분홍은 이 내 꼿송이
봄동산에 나붓기건만
바람 부니 모다 떨니네.

이 靑春은 무지개든가
하늘 돌든 아름답은 맘
제 때라고 춤을 추건만
슬어지니 한숨만 남네

-1934, 봄날에-<239>
