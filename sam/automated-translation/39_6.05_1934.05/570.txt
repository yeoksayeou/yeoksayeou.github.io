Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Does Woman Look Like an Angel or a Devil in Your Eyes?
Sŏ Kwang-je (徐光霽)

Woman is a devil. Truly a devil. Why? Because she possesses the 'weapon' (무기) capable of tempting even the firmest man? <118>

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求)

I call her a lovely devil. She might be cuter than an angel, but dealing with her, requiring money and emotional effort, is too heavy a duty (책무), and requires sacrifices of blood and tears, so I want to call her a devil. If she were hateful, I wish she were utterly hateful, or if lovely, just utterly lovely, but her appearance is beautiful and her heart is spiteful, so I can neither do this nor that, and have lived half my life swayed by her. <120>

Yi Chong-myŏng (李鍾鳴)

Woman is neither an angel nor a devil. She is simply a human being. However, it seems that when men are in love, they see their object as an angel, and when they suffer a broken heart, they see her as a devil. (However, as I am currently in love, I am serving one angel.) <136>

Pak Ch’ang-hun (朴昌薰)

One cannot equate all women.
1. There are those who look like angels.— When she is the housewife (主婦) type— Especially if her appearance and figure (容貌姿態) are outstanding, it would be adding brocade to silk (錦上添花).
2. There are also those who look like devils.— When she is the singing entertainer (唱妓) type— Especially if she is an unscrupulous, venomous woman (無良心的 毒婦), one might even get goosebumps.
3. There are also intermediate types between the above. <137>

Kim Ki-rim (金起林)

At the very beginning, she looked like an angel, then she looked like a devil, and now she just looks like a human being. Why? At the very beginning, I worshipped her, then I hated her, and after that, I realized that both her beauty and her ugliness all stemmed from her being human, and so all of it became lovable. <138>

**Summary**

This 1934 survey in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine asks several prominent Korean men whether they view women as angels or devils. The responses vary significantly: Sŏ Kwang-je sees women unequivocally as devils due to their power to tempt men. Yi Sŏ-gu calls them "lovely devils," acknowledging their charm but lamenting the emotional and financial cost of relationships. Yi Chong-myŏng offers a more balanced view, stating women are simply human, though men perceive them as angels during love and devils after heartbreak (he personally sees an angel currently). Pak Ch'ang-hun differentiates based on type, viewing housewife types as potential angels and entertainer types (especially malicious ones) as devils. Kim Ki-rim describes an evolution in his perception, from angel (worship) to devil (hate) and finally to human (acceptance and love of all aspects). From a historical perspective, the article provides a fascinating glimpse into the diverse, often polarized, and sometimes stereotypical views of women held by educated Korean men during the colonial period. It reflects contemporary social norms, gender dynamics, and male anxieties surrounding relationships, love, and female archetypes (the homemaker vs. the entertainer), making it interesting for historians studying gender roles and social attitudes in 1930s Korea.

당신 눈에는 女性이 天使로 보임니까? 惡魔로 보임니까?
徐光霽

여성은 惡魔다. 정말 惡魔다. 웨 그러야 하면 엇더한 굿센 남자이라도 유혹할 만한『무기』를 갓고 잇스니가?<118>

李瑞求

나는 어엿분 악마라 부름니다. 귀엽기야 천사 이상 일는지 모르나 돈을 드려야 하고 마음을 쏘아야 하는 그네와의 교섭은 너무나 과중한 책무이요 피와 눈물나는 희생이 필요해지니 악마라고 부르고도 십슴니다. 밉거든 아조 밉거나 어엿부건 아조 어엿부기만 햇스면 조켓는데 모습은 곱고 마음씨는 얄미우니 이럴 수도 업고 저절 수도 업서 半生을 그네에게 훠둘녀 사라왓슴니다. <120>

李鍾鳴

여성은 天使도 아니요 惡魔도 아님니다. 단지 사람입니다. 그러나 남성이 연애를 할 때에는 대상을 天使로 보고 실연을 당할 때는 惡魔로 보는 모임니다. (但 나는 요사히 연애를 하기때문의 한 분의 天使를 뫼시고 잇슴니다.)<136>

朴昌薰

여성 전체를 동일시 할 수는 업슴니다.
1. 天使로 보이는 이도 잇슴니다.-
主婦타입을 가진 여성인 때는-
특히 容貌姿態가 특출하면 錦上添花겟슴니다.
2. 惡魔갓치 보이는 이도 잇슴니다.-
唱妓타입의 여성인 때에-특히 無良心的 毒婦인 때는 소름까지 낏칠 수 잇겟지오.
3. 以上의 中間型도 잇슴니다. <137>

金起林

맨 처음에는 天使로 보엿고 다음에는 惡魔로 보이더니 지금은 그저 인간으로 보임니다. 웨그러냐고? 맨 처음에는 나는 그를 숭배하엿고 다음에는 그를 미워햇고
그 다음에는 그의 美도 醜도 모다 그가 인간인데서 오는 것을 아럿슴으로 그것이 모다 사랑스러웟소.<138>
