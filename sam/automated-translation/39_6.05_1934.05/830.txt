Magazine Title: 삼천리 第六卷 第五號
Publication Date: 1934년 05월 01일
Article Title: 어느 新聞의 連載小說을 繼續하여 보심니까 또 보신 部分에 대한 所感 (Which newspaper's serialized novel do you continually read, and your impressions of the parts you have read?)
Author: [Multiple respondents: Chang Hyŏkchu, Kim Kijin, Han Sŏrya]
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Chang Hyŏkchu

Because I only read the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), I only read Mr. Yun Paengnam's "Ponghwa" (烽火 - Beacon Fire). It is interesting. I wish it had more of a novelistic form, but I think this might actually be better for lower-level readers.

Kim Kijin

I was continuously reading Mr. Chang Hyŏkchu's "Mujigae" (무지개 - Rainbow) but haven't been able to read it for about the last 10 days. The tempo of the plot development is unusually slow, but the effort to observe today's social reality comprehensively and the solid realistic technique (寫實的 手法) are acknowledged. I cannot say more than this before reading it to the end.<224>

Han Sŏrya

Regarding Pyŏkch'o's "Im Kkŏkchŏng chŏn" (林巨正傳 - The Tale of Im Kkŏkchŏng) - There are no long novels that I read absolutely continuously. I pick and choose, read a few installments, then get bored and stop, and then maybe I wonder how it's going and read a few more installments. If this is due to my artistic appreciation (藝術的 鑑賞力) being loose (루-즈한), it would be fortunate for the masters of long-form novels—however, I do read Pyŏkch'o Hong Myŏnghŭi's "Im Kkŏkchŏng chŏn" with interest. Overall, the life of that time period is revealed in it. It is not a so-called farm village novel (農村小說) inserting urbanite-like characters dressed in farmer's clothes from that time, but rather the farmers and their lives as they really existed are depicted truthfully (如實히); one might say it is the best (白眉) in Chosŏn.<225>

**Summary**

Three prominent Korean writers of the 1930s (Chang Hyŏkchu, Kim Kijin, Han Sŏrya) respond to a survey about which serialized newspaper novels they are reading and their impressions. Chang reads Yun Paengnam's "Ponghwa" in the Tonga Ilbo, finding it interesting but perhaps simplified for a general audience. Kim Kijin comments on Chang Hyŏkchu's "Mujigae," acknowledging its realism and observation of social reality despite a slow pace. Han Sŏrya admits to not consistently following any single serial but praises Hong Myŏnghŭi's (Pyŏkch'o) "Im Kkŏkchŏng chŏn" for its realistic portrayal of historical peasant life, contrasting it favorably with other "farm village novels." The article reveals contemporary literary tastes, the importance of newspaper serialization for novel consumption, and differing views on realism, pacing, and audience engagement in colonial Korean literature. Historians might find the comments on social realism (especially Han Sŏrya's distinction regarding authentic rural depiction) and the discussion of reader engagement (Chang's comment on "lower-level readers," Han's boredom with serials) particularly interesting for understanding the literary and social dynamics of the period.

張赫宙

東亞日報밖에 보지 안키 때문에 尹白南氏의 「烽火」만을 봄니다. 재미잇슴니다. 좀 더 소설형식을 가추엇스면 하나 低級한 독자에게는 오히려 이것이 좋으리라 생각합니다.

金基鎭

張赫宙氏의 「무지개」를 게속하야 보다가 요지음 10여일 못 보앗슴니다. 사건의 전개에는 템포가 비상하느리나 今日의 사회현실을 전면적으로 관찰하는 노력과 견실한 寫實的 手法만은 인정됨니다. 끗까지 보기 전에 이 이상 더 말할 수 업슴니다.<224>

韓雪野

碧初作 「林巨正傳」에 대하야 -꼭꼭 계속하야는 장편은 업습니다. 벨느고 벨너서 몃 회式 읽다가는 그만 실증이 나서 그만 두엇다가 또 혹 엇덜고 하고 멧 회式 읽어봅니다. 나의 예술적 鑑賞力이 루-즈한 탓이라면 長篇大家들올 위하야서는 행복된 일이언만-그러나 碧初 洪命憙氏 作 「林巨正傳」은 자미잇게 읽습니다. 전체로 그 당시의 생활이 낫하나 잇습니다. 농부 옷 입힌 그 때의 都會人가튼 인물을 너은 소위 農村小說이 아니고 현실에 잇는 그 농부 그 생활이 여실히 그려진 것이 朝鮮서는 白眉라고 하겟지요.<225>
