女子硏究室
女子의 向方 (The Direction of Women)

Sukcha: "Look here, Pyŏngsik, you fool! When I asked whether you'd rather have 1,000,000 wŏn or me, you know what he said? He said he'd take the 1,000,000 wŏn!"
Ch'unhŭi: "Well, isn't Pyŏngsik right? He probably figured if he just had 1,000,000 wŏn, you'd just follow along anyway."

虛榮心 (Vanity)

A certain well-dressed noblewoman came to the Chongno Police Substation and said, "It's fine even if it's lost, but I just came to file a report anyway. A little while ago, I dropped a gold hairpin that I bought for 150 wŏn in a taxi, but I forgot the taxi number. Since it's 150 wŏn, if it's lost, I'll just buy another one.<54> It doesn't matter to me, but I'm just filing a report."
However, the morning of the next day, the found item (拾得物) was discovered, and the taxi driver brought it to the police box (交番所). So, the noblewoman gave 15 wŏn, which is ten percent (一割) of the value.
Ah, everyone, do you know? That gold hairpin was gold-plated (渡金), which the lady had bought the day before for 2 wŏn at the night market (夜市). The sin of vanity!

美德 (Virtue)

Student: "Professor (先生님), what should I major in?"
Doctor (博士): "You want to make money, don't you?"
Student: "I've suffered terribly from poverty."
Doctor: "In that case, do Gynecology (婦人科). Patients in gynecology customarily come to male doctors, and no matter how sloppy (껄넝껄넝한) the doctor is, they tend to keep coming back to the doctor they saw once."<55>

**Summary**

This article presents three short, unrelated anecdotes under the heading "Women's Study Room." The first is a conversation between two women where one complains her suitor would choose 1 million wŏn over her, and her friend cynically agrees money attracts women. The second tells of a woman feigning nonchalance about losing an expensive hairpin, which turns out to be cheap imitation gold, exposing her vanity. The third features a doctor advising a student to study gynecology purely for financial gain, suggesting female patients are loyal even to mediocre male doctors. From a historical perspective, the article offers glimpses into social attitudes in 1930s Korea: the perceived relationship between money and romance, social pretense and economic disparity revealed through the hairpin story, and cynical career advice reflecting both economic hardship and gender dynamics in the medical profession. The use of Japanese loanwords like '交番所' (kōban-sho) also points to the linguistic environment of the colonial era.

淑子「글세 이바 丙植이 바버야 네가 100만원 가지는 것과 나와 어느 것을 차지 하겟느냐고 무르니까 글세말야 100만원 가지는 편을 하겟다나」
春姬「그럼 丙植이 말이 올치안코-100만원만 잇스면 너는 그저 따라올 줄 알엇거든」

虛榮心

鍾路派出所에 엇든 잘 차린 귀부인이 와서「업서저도 조치만은 그저 한번 게출은 할려고 왓서요. 저요 150원주고 산 금비녀 한 개를 앗가 自働車에 떨거두고 왓는데 그 택시-번호를 그만 이저버렷서요. 150원이니까 업서지면 또 사겟<54>지요 나는 상관업지만 그저 屆出이나 함니다.」
그런데 그 이 날 아츰에 그 拾得物이 발견되어 自働車 운전수가 交番所이 가저 왓다. 그래서 그 귀부인은 가격의 一割인 15원을 주엇다.
아 여러분은 아시는가 그 금비녀는 渡金한 것으로 그 전날 2원을 주고 夜市에서 그 부인이 산 것이다. 虛榮心의 罪여!
