Magazine Title: 삼천리 第六卷 第五號
Publication Date: 1934년 05월 01일
Article Title: 1. 十年갈 名作 2. 百年갈 傑作 (Famous Works That Will Last 10 Years / Masterpieces That Will Last 100 Years)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

1. Famous Works That Will Last 10 Years 2. Masterpieces That Will Last 100 Years
Yi Kwangsu

I believe works that move the mainstream and undercurrents of human emotion (人情) will endure for a long time. I believe those based on transient waves of emotion or the theories of temporary contemporary ideologies (時代思潮) will be short-lived.

Yi Kapsu [Seems to be a typo in the original for Yi Kapsŏng (李甲成) or similar, but listed as 李甲基]

One. Yi Kwangsu's "Mujŏng" (無情)
One. Kim Tonghwan's "Kuktong ŭi Pam" (國境의 밤 - Night on the Border)
One. Ch'oe Sŏhae's "T'alch'ulgi" (脫出記 - Escape Log)
One. Cho Myŏnghŭi's "Naktonggang" (洛東江 - Nakdong River)
One. Kim Tongin's "Kamja" (감자 - Potato)
One. Yŏm Sangsŏp's "P'yobonsil ŭi Ch'ŏnggaeguri" (標本室의 靑개구리 - Green Frog in the Specimen Room)
One. Yi Kiyŏng's "Sŏhwa" (鼠火 - Rat Fire)
One. Im Hwa's "Uri Oppawa Hwaro" (우리 옵바와 火爐 - My Brother and the Brazier)
One. Yi Sanghwa's "Ppaeakk'in Tŭl e Pom ŭn Onuna" (악긴 들에 봄은 오누나 - Does Spring Come to Stolen Fields) (Poem)
(The above for 10 years)
One. Yi Kwangsu's "Mujŏng" (無情)
One. Yi Kiyŏng's "Sŏhwa" (鼠火)
(Among others for 100 years)
The works listed above are not necessarily meant in the sense that they are absolute masterpieces.

Pak Yŏnghŭi

One. In the Chosŏn literary world where works often disappear after appearing once in newspapers or magazines, it's impossible to read everything, making it difficult to evaluate rashly. We will only know clearly once publishing becomes organized and developed. Something like "Imkkŏkjŏng Chŏn" (林巨正傳), which is occasionally seen in newspapers, I refrain from evaluating its artistic value. However, this novel will likely be read by later generations due to its rich Chosŏn vocabulary (朝鮮語彙) and its realistic depiction (描出) of the human feelings and customs (人情風俗) of the Chosŏn people.<217> This is also a matter for after it's published.
One. We cannot know yet about 100 years from now. When such a great writer emerges, the literary world (文藝界) will likely become properly organized.

No Chayong

One. Mr. Yi Ŭnsang's "Nosan Sijojip" (鷺山詩調集)
Ch'unwŏn [Yi Kwangsu]'s "Hŭk" (흙 - Soil), "Chaesaeng" (再生 - Rebirth), "Maŭi T'aeja" (麻衣太子 - Prince Maŭi)
Mr. Kim Ŏk's Ansŏ Poetry Collection (岸曙詩集), the Samch'ŏlli Edition "Sam In Siga Chip" (三人詩歌集 - Three Poets' Poetry Collection)
Mr. Ch'oe Sangdŏk's "Sŭngbang Pigok" (僧房悲曲 - Tragic Song of a Monk's Cell)
Two. It is difficult to say. However, if I must force myself to speak, I would mention Mr. Han Yongun's "Nim ŭi Ch'immuk" (님의 沈黙 - The Silence of Love). (End)

Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi

It's difficult to pinpoint exactly 10 years or 100 years, but I think works by Mr. Yi Kwangsu like "Hŭk" (흙) or "Yujŏng" (有情 - Sentiment) will likely have quite a long lifespan. I don't know yet about poetry.

Im Hwa

This is a very awkward question. Especially for someone like me who reads excessively [or perhaps shallowly (寡讀)], judging the literary historical fate (文學史적 운명) of any author's work is likely not an easy task. However, if you insist on a definite answer, I suppose I can only say the following.
One. It sounds like self-praise, but Yi Kiyŏng's full-length novels "Sŏhwa" (鼠火) and "Kohyang" (故鄕 - Hometown) — though the latter is still being serialized — and other works will certainly not lose their artistic lifespan even after 10 years. As for poetry, it's still regrettable; it's difficult to find the seeds of genius anywhere.
Two. If a work is faithful, even to some extent, to the reality of its time, it will serve as material for reflection 100 years later, historically if not artistically. This applies, of course, to works produced up to the present.

Kim Kijin

Both your questions 1 and 2 are too difficult for this person to know the answer to. The only person who could possibly know about 10 years or 100 years hence would likely be a prophet from legend.

Hong Hyomin

Asking which famous works of poetry and fiction from our literary world (文壇) will last for 10 years makes it feel as if Chosŏn already has famous works.<218>
However, in my opinion, Chosŏn does not yet have any famous works (名作). If anything, there are perhaps fine works (佳作) and strenuous works (力作).
Some may ask by what standard I judge this, but in reality, Chosŏn lacks even someone like Russia's Pushkin from its very early days, nor does it have such poetry. Furthermore, in fiction, Chosŏn doesn't even have poets and critics comparable to England's Dryden from the 16th-17th centuries, so what can be done?
Therefore, I believe there are no works from Chosŏn that will carry the name "famous work" 10 years or 100 years from now. If they last 10 or 100 years, they will likely survive merely as relics of our predecessors (先人의 遺物). If the works produced today were transmitted to posterity as famous works, wouldn't the Chosŏn literary scene 10 or 100 years later be truly pathetic? I would be satisfied if the works currently available were recognized merely as something like the "Sketch Book" (스켓취뿍) in English literary history. I am one who hopes for great works like Tolstoy's "War and Peace" (戰爭과 平和) or Shakespeare's "Hamlet" (함으렛트) to emerge in the future. Indeed, only something that thoroughly contains and expresses the ideological basis (思想的根據) that Chosŏn possesses today can allow future generations to discern (窺知) the social conditions and level of consciousness (意識) of this era.
Therefore, I believe that among the works produced to date, there are strenuous works (力作) and fine works (佳作) resulting from the steady efforts (努力) of a few individuals.

Kim Tongin

I believe that as long as libraries exist, all works possess an existential lifespan.

Yang Chudong

One. Can a work that lasts merely 10 years be called a famous work (名作)?
Two. Yi Kwangsu's "Mujŏng" (無情), "Kaech'ŏkcha" (開拓者 - Pioneer), some say "Ch'unhyang Chŏn" (春香傳), "Maŭi T'aeja" (麻衣太子), "Tanjong Aesa" (端宗哀史).
However, his recent contemporary works (現代物) seem unlikely to last even 10 years.
Yi Ŭnsang's "Nosan Sijojip" (鷺山時調集)
Yang Chudong's "Chosŏn ŭi Maekpak" (朝鮮의 脈搏 - The Pulse of Chosŏn)
As I write this last line, the demons may laugh under the lamplight (書燈下에 귀신이 웃는다), but let those who laugh, laugh. Others.

Kim Kwangsŏp

One. There have been poems published in magazines or newspapers that left a deep impression at the time, but I don't recall any that I would say will last for 10 years. However, something like Pa'in [Kim Tonghwan]'s "Kuktong ŭi Pam" (國境의 밤) <219> — even if the primitive smugglers crossing that river disappear and the nights on the border brighten — the life contained within its content and its rough but substantial rhythm will always strike us as genuine (眞迫). Why only 10 years?
As for novels, perhaps Ch'unwŏn [Yi Kwangsu]'s historical novels—like "Maŭi T'aeja" (麻衣太子) or "Yi Sunsin" (李舜臣)—might shine more in later days because he faithfully accomplished what others couldn't?
Two. Who, 100 years from now, will say that Chosŏn today had a literary scene (文壇) and literature? It is a chilling temporal distance.

Kim Ansŏ [Kim Ŏk]

Regarding the lifespan of the works you asked about, I am not entirely without conviction, but life is such that we don't know what tomorrow brings. Moreover, there's a Japanese saying that if you talk about next year, the demons will laugh. Depending on one's perspective, one might predict a lifespan of 10 years, 30 years, or 5 years. But can that truly be trusted? Not being a prophet, I do not wish to become the demons' laughingstock.
Therefore, I intend to avoid answering this question.

Chu Yohan

Since the lifespan in the market is based on popular taste, I know there's no reason for a publisher to consider a work that might sell for 10 years. It seems like a slightly unconventional question. Moreover, readers 10 or 100 years from now might see a hasty answer and ridicule (鼻笑) my ignorance, so I shall follow the golden saying, "Silence is golden (沈黙이 黃金也)."

Yun Paengnam

One. "Imkkŏkjŏng Chŏn" (林巨正傳) (by Hong Pyŏkch'o [Hong Myŏnghŭi])
I expect it will likely continue for another two years even from now, and seeing it as a major work, because it is Oriental [in character].
Two. As for lasting 100 years, one truly cannot know unless 100 years have passed. Don't we see many examples in history where something considered worthless in its time only began to have its value discussed 100 years later? If I were forced to find something close to fitting the condition of lasting 100 years, perhaps it would be something like "Ch'unhyang Chŏn" (春香傳)? Not in the sense that its value is only recognized then, but in the sense that it will still be read...

Paek Ch'ŏl

Regarding the development of current Chosŏn literature and the value of its works, this is what I think. Of course, it is true that prominent Chosŏn novelists and poets up to now—for example, novelists like Yi Kwangsu, Yŏm Sangsŏp, Kim Tongin, Hyŏn Chin'gŏn, Yi Kiyŏng, etc., <220> and poets like Chu Yohan, Kim Tonghwan, Kim Ŏk, Chŏng Chiyong, Im Hwa, etc.—have each played a certain role. However, I feel that unfortunately, not a single masterpiece (傑作) worthy of preservation as a true literary heritage for 100 years or more has yet been produced. I hope this is merely due to my own limited reading (寡讀).

Han Sŏrya

If a creative work reveals the historical aspect (史的 相貌) of the past, it might hold indirect value even today. If it reproduces the reality of the present, immediate process (its essence), it would hold direct value right now. In this sense, it is truly regrettable that I cannot find concrete examples for your questions 1 and 2.
Perhaps the day will come when this great regret can be resolved, if authors and critics (or readers) exert more sincerity and training.

Kim Chinsŏp

One. Unfortunately, I do not know Chosŏn literature well. However, I am merely someone who knows the single fact that I cannot expect to enhance my own reputation even slightly by living another 10 years. It is only natural for someone as worthless as me to die even now; rather, my dying even one day sooner would be my supreme honor.
Two. I am convinced that there are truly many famous works that will last 100 years. That is, precisely because we have astonishingly dared to manufacture such clumsy literature, we may be remembered in the minds of our descendants 100 years hence for that sole reason. In this regard, all Chosŏn writers should rest assured.

P'ain [Kim Tonghwan]

One. We remember that around the time of the March First Movement (己未 - Kimi year, 1919), numerous poems and novels appeared based in various literary magazines like *Paekcho* (白潮), *P'yeho* (廢墟), *Ch'ŏngch'un* (靑春), etc. But we know well how many short-lived works, having barely passed 10 years since then, have already been completely erased (掃去) from public memory today. Likewise, if asked how many works by the numerous writers in the current literary scene (現文壇) will endure for the next 10 years, the vast majority will disappear, with perhaps only 1 in 100 or 1 in 1000 barely surviving.
So, what kind of works will remain?
Works forcibly produced under the influence of foreign literature will vanish. Also, excluding the eight or nine out of ten works in the current literary scene that are immature (生硬 未熟) and incomplete as artistic pieces, the remainder will last.
Two. Our literary scene (文壇) has many talented individuals (才士), but truly few writers revered as geniuses (天才). The works of the talented may last 10 years, but the work of a genius will flow through 100 generations (百代). Who are these geniuses? I can count several in my mind (心中에 곱을 수 잇스나), but I will refrain from naming them yet.<221>

**Summary**

This 1934 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine surveys prominent Korean writers and intellectuals, asking them to name contemporary literary works they believe will endure for 10 years (as "famous works") or 100 years (as "masterpieces"). The responses vary widely: some offer specific lists (Yi Kapsu, No Chayong, Yang Chudong), often mentioning Yi Kwangsu's works; others express deep skepticism about the quality of contemporary literature and its chances of survival (Hong Hyomin, Kim Kwangsŏp, Paek Ch'ŏl, Han Sŏrya, P'ain); some decline to answer, citing the difficulty of prediction or humility (Kim Kijin, Kim Ansŏ, Chu Yohan); and a few offer cynical or self-deprecating remarks (Kim Chinsŏp, Kim Tongin). The overall tone reflects anxiety about the state and future of Korean literature under colonial rule.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the self-perception of the Korean literary elite in the mid-1930s. Their engagement with questions of literary longevity highlights concerns about cultural identity, the influence of foreign literature (Japanese and Western), the perceived immaturity of their national literature, and the aspiration to create works of lasting value that could represent the Korean experience, despite the constraints of colonialism. The frequent mentions of specific authors and works provide insight into the contemporary canon and critical reception, while the widespread pessimism underscores the challenging cultural environment and perhaps a burgeoning national consciousness seeking more profound artistic expression.

林和

대단히 거북스러운 질문임니다. 덕우나 과독한 저와 갓흔 사람에게 대해서는 엇던 작가의 작품의 文學史적 운명을 판단한다는 것은 용리치 안은 일일까함니다. 허나 굿태여 확실한 대답이라도 하시라면 대개 다음의 정도로 말할 수 박게 업슬까 함니다.
一. 自畵自천 갓흐나 李箕永의 長篇小說 「鼠火」 「故鄕」둘이다. 연재중이나-등 諸作은 확실히 10년 후까지 그 예술적 수명을 일치 안흘 것임니다. 詩에 잇서서는 아즉 유감이니 엇던 곳에서도 그 천재의 萌芽를 찻기가 어렵습니다.
二. 그 시대의 현실의 얼마망큼이래도 충실한 작품이면 100년 후로도 예술적으로는 몰라도 역사적으로는 반성의 자료가 될 것임니다. 물론 현재까지의 작품으로.

金基鎭

貴問 1, 2가 모다 이 사람으로는 알 수 없시 어려운 질문임니다. 10년 후 100년 후까지 云云하는 일을 알 사람은 아마 전설에 있는 예언자박게 없을 것임니다.

洪曉民

우리 文壇의 詩歌 小說의 명작으로 10년 후까지 그 수명이 끼처갈 만한 것을 무르신 것을 보니 朝鮮에도 벌서 명작이 잇는 듯한 느낌이 납니다.<218>
그러나 나의 생각으로는 朝鮮에는 아즉까지 명작은 없다고 보아집니다. 그저 잇다고 하면 佳作과 力作정도의 것은 잇다고 봅니다.
그것은 어떠한 표준을 가지고 그러케 보느냐 하실 분이 게실는지 몰으나 그 實 朝鮮에는 극히 草創시대에 잇든 露西亞이 『푸쉬킨』같은 사람도 없고요 또한 그러한 詩歌도 없습니다. 그 다음 소설로는 朝鮮에서는 英國 16,7세기경의 『드라이든』만한 시인 비평가들도 없는데야 어찌합니까.
따라서 朝鮮에서 10년 후까지 또는 100년 후까지 명작이라는 이름으로 갈 작품은 없을 것이라고 봅니다. 10년 후 100년 후까지 간다면 그저 선인의 유물로 가겟지오. 지금의 나온 작품들이 명작이라고 후세에 전해진다면 그야말로 10년 후 100년 후 朝鮮文壇은 한심할 노릇이 아닌가요. 나는 지금 잇는 작품들은 녯날 英文學上에 『스켓취뿍』정도로 인정이 되엇으면 만족입니다. 앞으로 톨스토이』의 『戰爭과 平和』라든지 또는 『쉑스피어』의 『함으렛트」같은 대작이 나오기를 바라는 자의 하나이외다. 그야 오늘 朝鮮이 가지고 잇는 사상적 근거를 철저히 내포할 뿐 아니라 표백한 것이야말로 후세에도 이 시대의 사회상태 또는 의식의 정도를 窺知할 수 잇으리라고 보여지는 까닭이외다.
따라서 오늘까지 나온 작품은 몇몇 분의 꾸준한 노력에 의한 力作 佳作은 잇다고 봅니다.

一. 나는 불행히 朝鮮의 문학을 잘 알지 못합니다. 그러나 나는 다만 내가 이후 10년을 더 삶으로 의해 내 자신의 명예를 조곰이라도 느릴 수 잇다고는 생각할 수 업는 하나의 사실을 알고 잇는 자입니다. 나같이 무가치한 자는 지금이라도 죽는 것이 당연할 뿐 아니라 내가 하로라도 얼는 죽는다는 것은 차라리 나의 無上의 명예가 되는 것입니다.
二. 100년 후까지 끼처갈만한 명작은 참으로 만타고 나는 확신합니다. 즉 그것은 일즉이 우리가 놀나웁게도 拙劣한 문학을 감히 제조하며 잇섯다는 오즉 하나의 이유로서 100년 후의 우리 자손의 머리에 오히려 기억될 수 잇는 까닭입니다. 이 점 모든 朝鮮의 문인은 안심함이 좃습니다.

파인

一. 우리는 已未前後에 白潮, 廢墟, 靑春 등 여러 文藝雜誌에 據하야 數多한 詩歌, 小說이 나왓던 것을 기억하나 그로부터 겨우 10년을 지냇슬가 말가한 오늘에 이르러 벌서 세인의 기억에서 완전히 掃去된 短命작품이 엇더케나 만헛던 것을 잘 안다. 마천가지로 오늘 現文壇에 잇는 多數작가의 작품으로서 今后 10년을 지탱하여 나갈 것이 얼마나 될가 뭇는다면 대다수는 자최를 감추고 100에 1篇 1000에 1篇이 겨우 남어 갈 줄 안다.
그러면 엇더한 작품이 남어갈는고.
外來작품의 영향밋헤 억지로 제작되엇든 제작품은 업서치겟고 또 生硬 未熟하야 예술품으로서 완성되지 안은 現文壇의 십중팔구 諸작품을 제한 그 남어지가 기터 갈 것이다.
二. 우리 文壇에 才士는 만흐나 천재로 우러러 볼 작가는 퍽으나 적다. 才士의 작품은 10년에 긋칠 것이나 천재의 作은 100代를 흐를 것이다. 그 천재란 누구뇨 몃 사람을 心中에 곱을 수 잇스나 아직 지적만은 피하겟다.<221>
