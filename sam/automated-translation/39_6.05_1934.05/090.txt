Seven Women Lacking in Seoul

If you ask how many people (백성) live in Seoul, there are 79,519 households and a population (人口) of 382,491 people (人). Looking at this by gender, there are 191,249 males and 191,242 females, meaning there is a shortage (不足) of exactly 7 women. Isn't there some benefactor (特志家) somewhere who will arrange for just 7 young women (색시) to move (移住) to Seoul?<38>

**Summary**

This short piece from 1934 reports the population statistics for Seoul: 79,519 households and 382,491 people. It breaks this down by gender, noting 191,249 men and 191,242 women, highlighting a deficit of precisely seven women. The article concludes with a lighthearted question asking if a benefactor exists who could arrange for seven young women to relocate to Seoul. For historians, this snippet provides specific demographic data for the colonial capital during this period. The slightly humorous tone concerning the gender imbalance, framing it almost as a matchmaking issue ("색시"), offers a glimpse into contemporary social commentary, albeit brief, on population figures.
