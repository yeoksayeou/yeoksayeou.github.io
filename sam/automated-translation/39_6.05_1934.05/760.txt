Anecdotes of Famous Kisaeng, The Song of Kyehyang of Sŏnch'ŏn

While being skilled in dance, song, and having firm integrity are paths to becoming a renowned *kisaeng* (명긔) of an era, a truly immortal *kisaeng* whose fame reaches later generations must possess literary talent as lofty and brilliant as T'ak Mun-gun (卓文君) or Yang Ch'ang-gok (梁昌谷). Perhaps the name of the famous Sŏnch'ŏn *kisaeng* (平安道 宣川名妓), Kyehyang (桂香), whom this story is about, has been passed down precisely for this reason.

At that time, whenever there was a joyous event at the royal court (조정), a grand banquet (대연) was held, and exceptionally talented famous *kisaeng* from the eight provinces (팔도) were specially summoned to further grace the delightful occasion. One summer, due to a significant state affair, renowned *kisaeng* from Sŏnch'ŏn were also selected, and it was arranged for the King (임군) to bestow his viewing (賜覽) upon their talents. Kyehyang, outstanding in song, also went forth to that event. The King, merry with excitement, specifically called the Sŏnch'ŏn *kisaeng* before him and commanded them to sing a *chapka* (삽가, miscellaneous song). However, in that era which esteemed elegance (긔품), there were no *kisaeng* skilled in such popular songs.

At that moment, Kyehyang stepped forward and, with a clear and melodious voice, sang through one verse, then another, until she reached the part titled "Spring grasses are green year after year (春草는 年年綠)." As she was about to sing the following line, "When will the royal descendant return? (王孫은 歸不歸)," all the court officials turned pale with astonishment and were bewildered (소조망지). But Kyehyang, steadying her voice even more, brilliantly altered it to "Long live the royal descendant! (王孫은 萬萬歲)." At this, both His Majesty (상) and all the ministers rejoiced, acclaiming it as an unparalleled masterpiece for the ages (천고의 절창). Kyehyang may be gone, but these words, truly, will never perish through eternity.

**Summary**

This article recounts an anecdote about Kyehyang, a famous *kisaeng* from Sŏnch'ŏn in P'yŏngan Province, known for her singing ability. During a royal banquet, when asked to sing a popular song, she cleverly altered a potentially problematic traditional lyric ("When will the royal descendant return?") into a declaration of loyalty ("Long live the royal descendant!"), thereby delighting the King and court officials and securing her fame as not just talented, but also witty and loyal. From a historical perspective, the article, published during the Japanese colonial period (1934), highlights traditional Korean culture (the role of *kisaeng*, royal court proceedings) and celebrates Korean figures known for intelligence and adherence to traditional virtues. This focus on pre-colonial Korean history and cultural figures might have served as a subtle affirmation of Korean identity and heritage for readers during that time.

名妓揷話, 宣川 桂香의 노래
춤 잘 추고 소리 잘하며 절개 굿은 것도 일대 명긔되는 길이지만 그러나 진실로 후세에까지 미치는 천고의 명긔로 말하면 그 문장이 탁문군을 벗하든 양창곡 만치나 놉고 빗나야 한다. 여기 이약이하려는 선천 명긔(平安道 宣川名妓) 계향(桂香)의 일홈가튼 것은 실로 이리하야 전하여진 것이 아닐가.
그 당시 조정에서도 경사가 잇슬 때마다 대연을 ***고 그 자리예는 일부러 팔도로부터 뛰여난 명긔들을 불러 즐거운 자리를 더욱 빗내계 하엿다. 어느 한 해 여름 국가에 큰 정사잇서 유명한 선천긔생도 뽑히어 그 긔예를 임군께서 사람(賜覽)하시가로 되엿는데 소리로 뛰어난 계향이도 그 자리에 나아가게 되엇다. 흥에 취하신 임군께*는 그때 특히 선천긔생울 유연의 압헤 불너노코 삽가 한마듸 하라 말슴하엿는데 긔품을 숭상하든 그때에 잡가가튼 노래를 잘하는 긔생이 업섯다.
그럴지음 계향이 나와서 청아한 목소리로 한 곡조 두 곡조 불너 넘기다가 「春草는 年年綠」이라는 제목에 이르럿다. 그는 그 아래의 「王孫은 歸不歸」라는 구절을 불느는가 하며 만조백관들은 대경실색하야 소조망지할 때 계향이는 더 한층 목소리 가다듬어 「王孫은 萬萬歲」라고 멋지게 넘겨 버렷다. 이에 상께서나 모든 신하들이 깁부어 천고의 절창이라고 화창하엿다. 계향은 갓스나 이 말은 만고에 썩지 안을 진저.
