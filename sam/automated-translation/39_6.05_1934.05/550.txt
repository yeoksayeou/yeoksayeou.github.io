Article Title	出頭巨頭의 其後 ,第一·二次 共産黨事件의 首腦者, 民衆運動者大會事件의 首腦者 (The Later Circumstances of Prominent Leaders Who Appeared, Leaders of the First and Second Communist Party Incidents, Leaders of the Mass Movement Activists' Conference Incident)
Article Type	소식 (News/Report)

The Later Circumstances of Prominent Leaders Who Appeared, Leaders of the First and Second Communist Party Incidents, Leaders of the Mass Movement Activists' Conference Incident

As spring breezes and autumn rains repeatedly alternate, time flows on and on. Those significant incidents that once intensely stirred the world have, before we knew it, quietly hidden away in the tombstone called history. And the contemporary figures who plotted or led those incidents are also, one by one, vanishing from the public's memory—this is the reality.
However, even if they hide forever behind the silver screen as historical incidents and historical figures (omitted)... among the major incidents relatively fresh in memory are 1) The Korean Communist Party Incident and 2) The Mass Meeting Incident. Now, as my brush leads, I take it up to reveal under which star, dreaming what dreams, the figures related to these two incidents are now spending the long, long twenty-four hours of a day.

In your minds, the First Communist Party Incident likely brings forth scenes of all the derivative events like the June 10th Manse Incident. And the scenes of cadre-level figures being taken to Sinŭiju Police Station, the scenes of them being interrogated at the courthouse, the scenes of newspaper extras and the murmuring citizens coming and going on the main intersections.
At that time, when there were still no other organizations called the Communist Party elsewhere, the sudden news that a large number of 101 thought movement activists were arrested from all 13 provinces by the hands of the authorities—the memory of that terrifying news is as vivid as if it happened just yesterday, but counting the years, it has already become an event of 10 years past.
At that time, the cadres of the First Communist Party were the seven members: Responsible Secretary Kim Chaebong, Central Committee members Kim Yaksudae, Yu Chinhŭi, Chu Chonggŏn, Kim Ch'an, Cho Tongu, and Chŏng Unhae. In the Communist Youth League, there were Hong Chŭngsik, Pak Hŏnyŏng, Im Wŏngŭn, Kim Oya (Kim Dan-ya), and others.<112>

Kim Chaebong's Beekeeping

Under the old Public Order Maintenance Law, he received a sentence of 6 years. That was the maximum sentence. Besides this, he spent 3 years in pre-trial detention, so counting the years, it means he was in prison for 9 years. He was released from prison in September 1931. After recuperating from illness in Seoul for a time, he is now back at his home in P'ungsan Township, Andong County, Kyŏngsang Province, raising bees. It is not done on such a large scale as to aid his livelihood significantly. Rather, it is said he does it as a pastime because beekeeping allows him to follow the bees, breathe fresh air, and receive warm sunlight.

Kim Yaksudae's Magazine

Having served 4 years plus 3 years of pre-trial detention, he spent 7 years incarcerated. After his release, he observed the situation quietly for some time, then resolutely prepared to stand at the forefront of journalism. He returned to the streets and published several issues of a monthly magazine called "Taejung" (大衆), targeting the proletarian masses as its readership. Although it missed several issues due to midway setbacks, it is said to be revived starting this spring, with him possibly serving as the editor-in-chief.

Kim Ch'an is Incarcerated

Having fled far away to Moscow on the day of the roundup, he was later arrested again in Manchuria and brought back. He received a 10-year sentence and is currently serving time in Kyŏngsŏng Prison. It is heard that his sentence was reduced by one-fourth due to the recent amnesty, so he should re-enter society in another 7 or 8 years.

Chu Chonggŏn's Exile

When the whirlwind of arrests occurred at that time, he sensed it and vanished. Afterwards, it was said he went to Soviet Moscow, or that he was plotting domestic movements from Shanghai, but it seems no one knows his definite whereabouts. Even his former lover, Yi Tŏkyo, died as a visitor in Peking (北京), so by whose lips will his shadow be sought again? It is only desolate.

Yu Chinhŭi's Sin'gyedan (New Stage)

He too served 4 years plus 3 years of pre-trial detention, thus wearing prison garb ('bean rice and paulownia jacket') for a total of 7 years. After his release, he, along with Mr. Kim Yŏnŭi and others, founded the monthly magazine "Sin'gyedan" (新階段) and continued it for some time before closing it down. There must have been various external and internal reasons for this, but word is that they are reorganizing their lineup to revive it this spring.

Cho Tongu's Re-arrest

He also served 7 years, was released, and after some time recuperating from illness, joined the Chungang Ilbo. He continuously published political essays, drawing public attention, until just last winter when he was arrested by the Sinŭiju Police Station and is again undergoing preliminary examination.

Chŏng Unhae's Passing the Time

He too suffered in prison for 7 years (4 years sentence, 3 years pre-trial), then upon release, went down to his hometown in Taegu. There, he sometimes takes Laozi (老子) and the flowing clouds and water (行雲流水) as companions, sometimes immerses himself in the narrow alleys and red lights (狹斜紅燈) pretending to be a prodigal son, and sometimes spends entire days in the library, passing the time悠悠自若 (calmly and leisurely).

Hong Chŭngsik's Business Manager

He too groaned for 7 years in the sunless street (prison) before coming out. Before his arrest, he was the Business Manager at the Chosŏn Ilbo, and after his release<113> he also became the Business Manager at the Chungang Ilbo, taking charge of the difficult finances of the media organization and wielding his skills extensively. Some time ago, he was detained in Sinŭiju along with Cho Tongu, but was soon released as suspicions were cleared. He was a cadre of the Communist Youth League.

Pak Hŏnyŏng's Disappearance

He received a sentence and while serving time, became critically ill and was released on parole. Thus, he spent some time under medical care in Seoul, then went to Hamhŭng in Hamgyŏng Province, the hometown of his lover, Ms. Chu Sejuk, known for its beautiful mountains and waters, to recuperate. One morning, he vanished like the wind. Rumor has it that he went to Moscow with Ms. Chu, but no one knows where he might be now.

Im Wŏngŭn's Entry into the Business World

He also served 6 years and was released. In Seoul, he met a new lover, got married, and already has a second-generation Im Wŏngŭn. His older brother is a prominent merchant in the Osaka business world, so the couple moved to Osaka, becoming merchants who handle the abacus (珠盤) and record numbers in ledgers (帳薄). Previously, he had worked as the Osaka special correspondent for the Chungang Ilbo, but now he reportedly has no ties to any media organization.

Kim Tanya's Flight Abroad

He also received a 6-year sentence in absentia. Having disappeared beforehand amidst the whirlwind of arrests, he traveled around Russia, Shanghai, and Peking (北京), directing domestic movements. During the 1929 exposition, he slipped through the security cordon and appeared in Tohwadong, Seoul, organizing the Nth Party with his lover Ko Myŏngja, and engaging in activities. As official pursuit intensified, he vanished abroad again.
Besides these, Pak Ilbyŏng, though not a cadre, was renowned as a great orator of his time. After serving his full sentence, he lost his sight and is now undergoing treatment in his hometown Onsŏng, North Hamgyŏng Province.

Second Communist Party Incident

As the First Party was thus annihilated by arrests, it was immediately rebuilt, organizing the Second Party. The cadres at that time were:
Responsible Secretary Kang Taryŏng, Hong Tŏkyu, Kwŏn Osŏl, Yi Pongsu, Kim Kyŏngjae, and others. Among them,

Kang Taryŏng is Bedridden

After serving a 6-year sentence, he was released extremely weakened, and his mental state was also rumored to have temporarily deviated from the norm, his nerves being so weak. Thus, he is now quietly recuperating from his illness at his home in Chinju, Kyŏngsang Province. It is already a well-known fact that he led the June 10th Manse Incident and that, while operating underground as Responsible Secretary, he disguised his movements by posing as a banana seller.

Hong Tŏkyu, the Newspaperman

He too served 4 years, came out,<114> recuperated at home for a while, then joined the Chungang Ilbo the year before last. He is now active as the Sales Manager in the Business Bureau. Perhaps among those associated with the party who re-entered media organizations, there are only Mr. Hong Chŭngsik and Mr. Hong Tŏkyu.

Kwŏn Osŏl's Death

He received a 5-year sentence and died in prison while serving time. His younger brother, Kwŏn Ojik, was also imprisoned for thought-related matters and was reportedly released some time ago. There is no way to know what he is doing now.

Kim Kyŏngjae, the Polemicist

He too served a 4-year sentence, came out, recuperated, and after recovering, even married a new wife, setting up a newlywed home near Segŏmjŏng outside the city gate. While dedicating himself to reading and contemplation, he occasionally wields his brush for outspoken (侃侃愕愕) writings that warn the world, as everyone knows. He is the son-in-law of Mr. Sŏ Chŏnghŭi and the brother-in-law of Mr. Sŏ Pŏmsŏk.

Yi Pongsu's Re-imprisonment

After serving a 4-year sentence, he went to his hometown in Hamgyŏng Province to recover from illness. While there, he attended a friend's wedding and got into trouble for remarks made during a congratulatory speech (舌禍). He received a 2-year sentence and is currently serving time in Hamhŭng Prison.
Next are the incidents involving the Third Party's Kim Chun'yŏn, Han Wigŏn, An Kwangch'ŏn, Yi Sŏngt'ae, Pak Nakchun, Ha P'irwŏn, Kim Pokchin, Pak Ch'ŏn, etc., but this will be recorded in the next issue.

Mass Movement Activists' Conference Incident

Next is the Mass Movement Activists' Conference Incident. This occurred on December 11, 1929, when arrests were made by the Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department, and indictments soon followed. Without need for lengthy explanation, this incident was triggered by the Kwangju Student Incident, where leaders of the then Sin'ganhoe attempted to hold a mass meeting but were arrested due to prior detection.
Those convicted and imprisoned at that time were the six individuals:
Hŏ Hŏn, Hong Myŏnghŭi, Yi Kwan'yong, Cho Pyŏngok, Yi Wŏnhyŏk, Kim Musam.
And those who went as far as prison but were released without indictment were Messrs.:
Kwŏn Tongjin, Han Yongun, Chu Yohan, Kim Hangkkyu.

Hŏ Hŏn Passing Time Reading

He received a sentence of 1 year and 6 months, which combined with 1 year and a half of pre-trial detention, meant he spent 3 years in prison before being released on February 15, 1932. His health was weak, so he recuperated at his home in Samch'ŏngdong, Seoul, but has since been attempting various activities again. However, as it hasn't materialized into anything specific that can be pointed out in terms of field or work, let's just say he is passing the time reading (讀書消日). Since the restoration of his lawyer's license is imminent, there is speculation that he might return to the legal field.

Hong Myŏnghŭi's Novel

He too served 1 year and 6 months plus 1 year and a half of pre-trial detention, thus groaning in prison for 3 years. After his release<115> and recovering his health, he immediately took up his brush for the monumental work "Im Kkŏkjŏng chŏn" (林巨正傳), a novel into which he was pouring his life's effort, and it is currently being serialized in the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper.
Observing the state of the world, he passes his days writing novels, like an old steed lying in the stable (老棋伏櫪式).

Yi Kwan'yong's Long Journey [Death]

He too endured 3 years of hardship (1 year 6 months sentence, 1 year and a half pre-trial). After release, he was based at the Chosŏn Ilbo, wielding his principled pen (侃侃의 筆陣). Alas, he unexpectedly drowned at the Ch'ŏngjin seaside resort in North Hamgyŏng Province. There were talks of publishing his posthumous manuscripts, but there has been no news yet.

Cho Pyŏngok's Mining Venture

He endured nearly 3 years of prison hardship (1 year 4 months sentence plus 1 year and a half pre-trial). After release, he served as the Business Manager at the Chosŏn Ilbo, but has now withdrawn from the media organization and is engaged in mining operations, traveling between Chungch'ŏng Province and Hamgyŏng Province. It is said that Mr. Min Pyŏngju and others are involved in his mining business. Word is that the Yŏnghŭng gold mine is very promising.

Yi Wŏnhyŏk Passing Time Reading

He too spent 3 years in prison (1 year 4 months sentence plus 1 year and a half pre-trial). After his release, he has been recuperating from illness and is currently spending his time quietly at home with reading and contemplation (讀書와 思索).

Kim Musam's Construction Business

He is the only perpetrator in this incident who appeared at the Chosŏn Theater immediately after the event, distributed propaganda leaflets, and gave an agitation speech. After enduring hardship (1 year 4 months sentence and 1 year and a half pre-trial), he was released. He then worked at the Chosŏn Ilbo with Mr. Cho Pyŏngok and others but later withdrew from the journalism front. Recently, he has entered the construction industry and is reportedly active with indirect involvement in the establishment of a 500,000 won stock company called Kyŏngsŏng Kŏnmul Chusik Hoesa (京城建物株式會社) along with several others.
Among the several individuals released without indictment, to mention each briefly: Mr. Kwŏn Tongjin is based in Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) as the highest authority of the old faction, issuing directives to tens of thousands of followers; Mr. Han Yongun is based at Pulgyosa (佛敎社), a Buddhist media organization, expounding deep philosophy to the masses; Mr. Chu Yohan resigned as the Executive Director (專務取締役) of the Chosŏn Ilbo and has now moved to the Hwasin Sanghoe (和信商會); Mr. Kim Hangkkyu, after his release without indictment at that time, was later imprisoned for 1 year in relation to Sin'ganhoe, and after release, is now passing his time at his home in Sogyeokdong, Seoul, with contemplation, reading, and quiet observation of the times (思索과 讀量와 時世靜觀).<116>

**Summary**

This 1934 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* details the subsequent lives of prominent Korean figures involved in major political incidents under Japanese rule: the First and Second Korean Communist Party Incidents (mid-1920s) and the Mass Movement Activists' Conference Incident (1929, linked to the Kwangju Student Movement). It tracks the whereabouts and activities of key leaders after their imprisonment or escape, noting some continued activism (journalism, underground movements), others shifting to different fields (business, beekeeping, mining, construction), some remaining ill or having died, and others disappearing into exile (Moscow, Shanghai). The article provides a snapshot of the varied fates of Korean activists and intellectuals a decade after the initial communist crackdowns and several years after the mass movement suppression, illustrating the pressures, dangers, and adaptations faced by those involved in nationalist and socialist movements during the colonial period. Historians might find the specific details about individuals' post-prison lives, their career shifts, ongoing surveillance/re-arrests (like Cho Tongu), connections (like Kim Kyŏngjae's marriage), and the hints of continued underground or international networks particularly valuable for understanding the landscape of Korean resistance and its suppression in the 1930s. The mention of specific sentences, releases, and even an amnesty provides concrete data points.

出頭巨頭의 其後 ,第一·二次 共産黨事件의 首腦者, 民衆運動者大會事件의 首腦者
봄바람 가을비 번가라 거듭하는 사이에 세월은 흘너흘너 그러케도 몹시 세상을 격동식혀 놋튼 큼직큼직한 사건들이 어느 사이에 역사라는 墓標 속에 고요히 숨어버리고, 그러고 그 사건올 꾸미든 한다하든 當代 人物들도 한 사람 두 사람식 세인의 머리가운데서 자최를 거두어 버리고 잇는 것이 현실이다.
그러컨만 비록 史上의 사건, 史上의 인물로 영원히 銀幕 뒤에 숨어버린다 할지라도(略) 큰 사건으로 비교적 기억에 새로운 것이 1曰 朝鮮共産黨事件이요, 2曰 民衆大會事件이다. 이제 붓가는 대로 이 두 사건의 관계인물들로서 지금은 어느 별밋헤 무슨 꿈을 꾸면서 하로 24시간이라는 長長 긴 세월을 보내고 잇는가 그를 밝혀보려 이 붓을 잡엇노라.

여러분의 머리 가운데는 제일차 공산당사건이라 하면 저 6·10만세 사건 둥 派生的인 모든 사건의 광경이 떠오르리라. 그리고 간부급 인물이 新義州署로 잡혀가든 광경, 裁判所에서 審理밧든 때 광경, 新聞社號外와 중로 네거리 가고 오는 시민들이 수군거리든 광경.
그때는 아직 다른 데에도 공산당이란 조직체가 업는 때 돌연 官憲의 손으로 101명이란 多數한 사상운동자가 13道에서 檢擧되든 그 무시무시한 소식을 발 어적게 잇든 일 가치 기억이 선명하건만 해수로 따지면 벌서 10년 전의 녯날 일이 되엇다.
그때 제1차 공산당의 간부는 責任秘書 金在鳳, 中央委員 金若水, 兪鎭熙, 朱鍾建, 金燦, 趙東祐, 鄭雲海의 7인이요, 공산청년회에 洪璔植, 朴憲永, 林元根, 金O冶 등이 잇섯다.<112>

金在鳳의 養蜂

舊治安維持法에 의하야 그는 6년의 刑을 밧엇다. 그가 최고의 刑이엇다. 이박게 未決로 3년을 잇섯스니, 해수로 따지면 監房에 잇기를 9년 동안을 한 세음이 된다. 그가 出獄한 것이 1931년 9월이엇다. 一時 서울에서 병올 養하고 잇다가 지금은 고향인 慶尙道 安東郡 豐山面 제집에 이르러 버리를 치고 잇다. 生計에 도음이 되도록 그러케 크게 함은 아니요. 양봉하면 버리를 따라다니느라고 신선한 공기를 마일 수 잇고 땃듯한 해볏을 바들 수 잇스니까 消日삼아 하는 중이라 한다.

金若水의 雜誌

4년의 服役에 未決 3년까지 하고 나왓스니 7년을 在監한 세음이 된다. 그는 出獄하야 얼마동안 시세를 靜觀하고 잇더니, 결연히 언론의 陳頭에 나설 준비를 마련하여 가지고, 다시 街頭에 나와 無産大衆을 독자층으로 하는「大衆」이란 월간잡지를 刊行하야 여러 號를 거듭하다가 中途의 蹉跌로 몃 號를 缺하기는 하엿스나 今春부터 다시 復活하여 자기가 主幹하리다든가.

金燦은 在獄

檢擧일자 멀니 莫斯科에 走하엿다가 滿洲에서 도로 잡혀 드러와서 10년의 刑의 言渡를 밧고 지금 京城刑務所에 服役중이다. 듯건대 이번 恩赦로 4分 1이 減刑되엿다 하니 今后 7, 8년 후면 다시 세상에 나오리라.

朱鍾建의 亡命

그때 檢擧의 旋風이 일자 이 눈치를 차이고, 자최를 멈추어 버린 뒤 그는 혹은 쏘비엣의 모스꾸바로 갓다고도 하고 혹은 上海에서 國內운동을 획책하고 잇다고도 하나 확실한 所在는 아모도 모르는 모양이다. 그의 녯애인이든 李德耀 마저 北京가서 客死하엿스니, 그의 구름자는 누구의 입으로 다시 차저지랴 오직 막막할 뿐.

兪鎭熙의 新階段

그도 4년服役에 3년 동안을 未決로까지 잇섯스니 전후 7년을 콩밥에 오동저고리입엇다. 나와서 金演義氏 등으로 더부러 월간잡지「新階段」을 創刊하야 얼마 계속하다가 문을 닷고 잇다. 이리됨에는 외부와 내부에 諸原因이 잇섯겟지만, 들니는 말에 다시 陳容을 새로 조직하야 올 봄부터 復活한다는가.

趙東祐의 再檢擧

그도 7년을 치르고 나와서 얼마를 병치료하다가 中央日報에 입사하야 늘 政治論文을 발표하야 세인의 주목을 끄을고 잇든 중 바로 작년 겨을에 新義州署에 檢擧되야 또 豫審에 너머 가 잇다.

鄭雲海의 消日

그도 4년 징역에 산년 미결로 전후 7년을 철창에서 신음하다가 출옥하여 大邱의 고향에 나려가서 혹은 老子와 가치 行雲流水로 벗을 삼기도 하고 혹은 蕩子인 체 狹斜紅燈으로 몸을 잠그고 혹은 도서관에 盡日을 보내기도 하야 悠悠自若 세월을 보내고 잇다.

洪璔植의 營業局長

그도 7년을 태양업는 거리에서 呻吟하고 나온 이다. 그는 檢擧 전에도 朝鮮日報 營業局長으로 잇섯거니와 나온 뒤에도<113> 亦 中央日報의 營業局長으로 艱難만흔 言論機關의 살님사리를 마터 縱橫으로 手腕을 휘둘느고 잇다. 얼마전에 趙東祐와 함께 新義州로 拘禁되어 간 적이 잇섯스나 곳 嫌疑가 벗기어 도라 왓다. 그는 共靑의 간부엇다.

朴憲永의 潛跡

그는 刑을 밧고 服役 중에 병이 危篤하야 假出獄되어 나왓다. 그래서 서울에서 얼마동안 의약에 親하다가 山水 조혼 愛人 朱世竹 女史의 고향인 咸鏡道 咸興으로 가서 靜養하든 중 하로 아츰 바람갓치 사라젓다. 들니는 風聞에 朱女史와 함께 모스꾸바에 갓다 하나 지금줌 어듸 잇는지 아모도 아는 이가 업다.

林元根의 商界投身

그도 6년을 服役하고 나와서, 서울에서 새로운 애인을 맛나 결혼까지 하고 벌서 第2世 林元根까지 엇게 되엇다. 그의 兄이 大阪商界紳商이라, 그를 따라 두 내외는 大阪에 이주하야 아조 珠盤들고 帳薄에 數子記入하는 상인이 되엇다. 이전에도 中央日報 大阪特派員으로 활약한 일이 잇섯스나 지금은 아모 言論機關에도 관계를 맷지안코 잇다고 한다.

金丹治의 遠走

그도 6년의 缺席判決을 밧엇다. 檢擧旋風 속에서 미리 종적올 감추어 露西亞로 上海로 北京으로 도라다니며 國內運動을 지시하엿고, 1929년 박람회 때에는 警戒綱을 뜰고 서울 桃花洞에 나타나 第몃次 黨을 그의 애인 高明子와 함께 조직하여 노코 활약하다가 官憲의 追及이甚하여 다시 해외로 잠적하여 버리엇다.
이밧게 간부는 아니엇스나 一世의 雄辯家로 일홈을 날니든 朴一秉은 刑을 다 치르고 나오자失明하야 지금은 鄕里 咸北 穩域에 잇서 治療중다.

姜達永은 臥病

그는 6년의 刑을 치르고 나오자 極度로 衰弱한 몸에다가 정신상태까지 一時는 常軌를 버서젓다는 소문이 나도록 신경이 약하엿다. 그래서 지금은 慶尙道 晋州 自宅에서 고요히 身病을 養하고 잇다. 그가 六十萬歲事件을 지도하엿고, 그리고 責任秘書로 지하에 잠행할 때 빠나나장수로 행적을 감추고 다녓다 함은 이미 다 아는 사실이리라.

洪悳裕의 新聞人

그도 4년의 役을 치르고 나와서<114> 一時는 자택에서 병을 養하고 잇다가 재작년부터 中央日報에 드러가 지금은 營業局 販賣部長으로 활약하고 잇다. 아마 당의 관게자로 다시 言論機關에 드러간 이로는 洪璔植氏와 洪眞裕의 兩氏가 잇슬 뿐이리라.

權五卨의 死亡

5년의 刑을 밧고 服役 중에 監獄에서 사망하엿다. 그의 舍弟 權五稷도 思想關係로 投獄되어 잇더니 얼마 전에 出獄하엿다고 한다. 지금은 무얼 하고 잇는지 알 길이 업다.

金璟載의 論客

그도 4년의 刑을 치르고 나와서 몸을 養하고 잇더니 그 뒤 快差하야 새로 令夫人까지 마저 드리어 새문밧 洗劍亭附近에 新接살림을 꾸미고 잇다. 독서와 思索으로 일삼는 한편 각금 侃侃愕愕의 警世의 붓대를 잡고 잇슴은 世人이 다 아는 바와 갓다. 徐廷禧氏의 婿郞이요, 徐範錫군의 妹夫다.

李鳳洙의 再入獄

4년의 刑을 치르고 身病을 養하기 위하야 鄕里 咸鏡道에 가잇든 중 엇든 友人의 결혼식에 가서 祝辭하다가 舌禍에 걸니어, 2년의 判決을 박고 지금 咸興刑務所에서 服役 중이다.
