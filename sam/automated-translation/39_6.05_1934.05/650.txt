Article Title: 朝鮮에 태어난 것이 幸福하냐 (Is Being Born in Chosŏn Fortunate?)

Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Opinion Piece)

Is Being Born in Chosŏn Fortunate?
						Yi In

Countless fields of overgrown grass (蓬蓬草野) have long gone without tasting the hoe and spade (鍬鋤). For many years, our Chosŏn, which gives viewers a sense of utter desolation (滿目荒凉), has things to do.

Years ago, when professors and students from the University of Chicago (市俄古大學) in America came here on their tour of the East, at a reception held for them, I addressed the guest of honor with a bold greeting, which caused the whole room to roar with laughter (哄笑). I said, "You are in an admirable position where material civilization has reached its utmost limit (窮極), so your ancestors became historical figures (史上人物), but you yourselves have nothing more to do and no room to become historical figures. However, our Chosŏn is nothing but wasteland to be pioneered; the things to do and strive for are piled up like a great mountain (積如岳山). We are great figures who will rebuild history, so—though it may be rude to the guest of honor—we are greater and more fortunate than you." In other words, we have the happiness of youth, while they likely have the sorrow of old age and decay (老杇). <139>

**Summary**

Yi In argues paradoxically that being born in underdeveloped Chosŏn is fortunate compared to being in materially advanced America. He recounts telling visitors from the University of Chicago that while Americans inherit a completed civilization with no room left for great deeds, Koreans face a vast "wasteland" offering immense opportunity to become "great figures who will rebuild history." Korea possesses the "happiness of youth" and potential, contrasting with America's "sorrow of old age." For historians, this piece is interesting as it reflects a specific colonial-era Korean intellectual's attempt to frame national underdevelopment positively, emphasizing potential and future agency ("rebuilding history") despite the political realities. It showcases a narrative of optimistic nationalism rooted in the very challenges faced under colonial rule.

朝鮮에 태어난 것이 幸福하냐
						李仁
蓬蓬草野幾萬頃이 鍬鋤의 맛을 못본 지 오래오래이다. 보는 사람 滿目荒凉한 감이 잇는 우리 朝鮮은 할 일
年前 亞米利加 市俄古大學 교수와 학생이 東洋하는 길에 우리 곳에 왓을 때 그네들은 초대한 席上 나는 그 主賓을 대하야 대담한 인사 한마디를 하여 一場이 哄笑한 일이 잇다. 당신네들은 그 중에도 물질문명이 窮極에 달한 자랑할 만한 처지에 잇슴으로 당신들의 조상은 史上人物이 되엿지마는 당신 자신들은 더 할 일이 업는 史上人物될 여지가 업다. 그러니 우리 朝鮮은 개척할 황무지 뿐임을 할 일 힘쓸 일이 積如岳山이라. 우리는 역사의 재건할 위대한 인물임으로 主賓의게 실례는 되지마는 우리가 당신들보다 위대하고 행복된다고 한 일이 잇음니다. 말하자면 우리는 청춘의 행복이 잇고 그내들은 老杇의 비애가 잇을 것이오 이다. <139>
