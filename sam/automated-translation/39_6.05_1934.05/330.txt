The Path of Life
						British Thinker Smiles

Along the path of life, the skulls (頭蓋骨) of countless defeated stragglers (敗殘者) are scattered. These are the ones who lacked the strength and fell during their march (進軍). However, we need not look upon this and become pessimistic (悲觀).

<69> Moreover, besides these, many people have marched the same path, did not fall, and finally received the prize (賞) of life's victory (人生勝利). We must follow the example of these victors and advance.

**Summary**

This short piece, attributed to the British thinker Smiles (likely Samuel Smiles), uses the metaphor of life as a path strewn with the skulls of those who failed. It urges the reader not to despair but to take inspiration from the many who persevered and achieved victory in life, advocating for emulating these successful individuals. From a historical perspective, the promotion of Smiles' philosophy of self-help and individual striving in a 1934 Korean magazine is significant. Historians might find it interesting as it reflects an emphasis on individual perseverance and success during a period of colonial rule, potentially serving as either a form of apolitical encouragement or a message subtly aligning with colonial narratives that favoured individual diligence over collective resistance.
