Article Type: 설문 (Survey Response)

Is being born in Korea fortunate or unfortunate?
						Hyŏn Sangyun

Well... First, if I speak the truth that is in my heart, I can answer that being born in Korea (朝鮮) is unfortunate. This is because of the lack of conveniences that foster ability and the opportunities to display it, as well as the inability to enjoy pleasure.

However, I also want to willfully answer that being born in this land is fortunate. This is because, although I may not have been entirely endowed with the ability to improve the various political (政治的) and economic (經濟的) phenomena (諸現象), I want to possess at least a part of that ability.<80>

**Summary**

Hyŏn Sangyun expresses ambivalence about being born in colonial Korea. He initially states it feels unfortunate due to the lack of personal enjoyment and, more significantly, the absence of opportunities and means to develop and exercise one's abilities. However, he counters this by expressing a desire ('willfully') to see it as fortunate, stemming from a wish to possess even a partial capacity to improve Korea's political and economic situation. This juxtaposition reveals the constrained circumstances under Japanese rule and the author's sense of duty or longing for agency despite the perceived lack of opportunity. Historians might find this response interesting for its candid admission of misfortune alongside a conscious, almost defiant, assertion of wanting to contribute to positive change, reflecting intellectual attitudes during the colonial period.

朝鮮에 태여난 것이 幸福한가 不幸한가
						玄相允
글세올시다. 爲先 마음 가운데 잇는 실상을 말슴하면 朝鮮에 태여난 것이 不幸이라고 대답할 수 잇습니니다. 享樂을 누리지 못하는 것과 能力을 助長하여 주는 便宜와 또 그것을 發揮하도록 하여주는 機會가 없는 때문에 외다. 그러나 의지적으로 이 땅에 출생한 것이 행복이라고도 대답하고 십습니다. 그것은 政治的 經濟的 諸現象을 改善하는 能을 全部稟受한 것은 없으나 일부분이라도 가지고 십흔 까닭이외다.<80>
