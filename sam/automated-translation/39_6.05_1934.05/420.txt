Is Being Born in Chosŏn Fortunate or Unfortunate?
						Na Hyesŏk

Reason

Happiness is not when one has obtained wealth (富), acquired status (地位), or pursued learning (學問), but when one achieves a state of single-minded focus (一念) where the divine spirit (神) moves between things (事物).

However, this state of single-minded focus comes more often, both in quality (質) and quantity (量), during times of sorrow and suffering than during times of joy and pleasure. The bodies and minds of the Chosŏn people are a lump of anguish (煩憫). In other words, it is a time to attain single-minded focus.

Whether we rise (興) or fall (亡) will be decided within this single-minded focus, so how can now not be a time of happiness?<81>

**Summary**

Na Hyesŏk argues that true happiness isn't found in material gain or status but in a state of intense focus ("single-mindedness") where the divine is present. She paradoxically claims that because Koreans live in a time of great sorrow and suffering ("a lump of anguish"), they are constantly in a state conducive to achieving this profound focus. This focus, she suggests, is essential for deciding the nation's fate (whether it rises or falls), making the current suffering a necessary, and therefore potentially "happy," condition. From a historical perspective, this article offers a fascinating, if unsettling, glimpse into how an intellectual like Na Hyesŏk grappled with the harsh realities of colonial rule. Her attempt to reframe suffering as a catalyst for national determination or spiritual awakening might interest historians studying psychological responses to oppression and the diverse, sometimes contradictory, ways Koreans sought meaning and agency during this period.

朝鮮에 태여 난 것이 幸福한가 不幸한가
						羅蕙錫
理由
幸福은 富를 得하엿슬 때나 地位를 구하엿슬 때나 學問을 취하엿을 때가 아니라 事物과 事物사이에 神이 왕래하는 一念이 되엿슬 때입니다. 그런데 이 一念이 될 때는 깃부고 즐거울 때보다 슬흐고 고통스러울 때가 質노나 量으로나 만습니다. 朝鮮사람의 몸과 마음은 煩憫의 뭉텡입니다. 즉 一念으로 가질 때입니다. 이 一念 중에서 興하든지 亡하든지 決斷이 나는 것이니 지금이 엇지 幸福되지 아니릿가<81>
