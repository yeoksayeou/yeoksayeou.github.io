三千里機密室(The Korean Black-chember)
Political and Thought Circles Insider Report

Hŭngsa tan's Mainland-Centric Approach?
Mr. Song Chong-ik (宋鍾翊氏), the supreme leader of the Hŭngsa tan (興士團) based in America (米州), is said to be coming to Korea (朝鮮) within this year for the first time in over ten years. It is unclear whether this is a permanent return or a temporary visit, but his arrival seems certain. According to a certain informed source, the Hŭngsa tan possesses funds amounting to about $20,000 (나) in America and Shanghai (上海方面), equivalent to roughly 60,000 yen (日貨 約六萬圓), and there's speculation that Mr. Song might bring this money with him.
In any case, the supreme leader Mr. Song Chong-ik will soon enter the country, 60,000 yen from overseas will arrive, and with Tosan's (島山) prison term (刑期) having only one or two years left, he should be released (出獄) within a few years. Furthermore, the activities of the Suyang Tonguhoe (修養同友會) are expected to become even more persistent.
Synthesizing all these facts, a certain informed source suggests that the Hŭngsa tan is now shifting towards a mainland-centric approach. Is this truly the case? We must wait and see, but it is certainly the first major political news (政治的 뉴-스) heard in this New Year.

The Non-Dissolution Faction of the Sin'ganhoe
Many prominent figures who had gone far away have returned, and those imprisoned have served their sentences and mostly been released. There has never been a time like the present when the non-dissolution faction (非解消派) of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) is gathered together in Seoul. Indeed, the giants (巨頭) of the non-dissolution faction include Messrs. Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯), Cho Man-sik (曺晩植), An Chae-hong (安在鴻), and Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭), all of whom are now gathered in Seoul. Looking back at the situation following the dissolution, which led to the complete annihilation of representative organizations (表現團體), what are these individuals thinking now? As the spring breeze blows and the weather warms, it seems likely new topics surrounding the Sin'ganhoe will emerge. So it is told. <18>

War and the Army Commander's Opinion
Recently, Russian airplanes flew over the skies of Unggi (雄基) and Chŏljin (絶津), creating tension among the populace. At this time, the Commander of the Korean Army, General Kawashima (朝鮮司令官川島大將), hastily set out for a border inspection. Subsequently, at the 19th Division Headquarters (in Nanam, 羅南), he expressed the following opinions:
1. Regarding the establishment (段置) of an air squadron in Northern Korea, the military authorities recognize its necessity. This is especially true given that neighboring Russia (隣邦路西亞) has recently adopted an active posture towards the border region.
2. Judging from Foreign Minister Hirota's (廣田外相) diplomatic speech in the Diet, neither Russia's eastward red invasion (露國의 極東赤化侵入) nor the 1935-36 international crisis seems to be a cause for such great concern, and I do not think war will break out rapidly. However, the Great European War also started with a single shot from a six-shooter by a Serbian youth. War can break out anytime, anywhere, and in any manner, so the military authorities always approach this with readiness (覺悟).

Personal Allowance (親用金)
Upon inquiring (探聞) about the personal allowance (親用金) of His Majesty King Yi (李王殿下서), it has been budgeted at exactly 90,000 yen (九萬圓) annually since the annexation (合倂以來) to this day. This amount is divided into twelve months, with 5,000 yen (五千圓) given to His Majesty the King and 2,500 yen (二千五百圓) to Her Majesty the Queen each month, delivered through the hands of a chamberlain (■侍) on the 15th of every month.

Rumor of Emperor Kojong's Vladivostok Trip
When the Russo-Japanese War suddenly broke out, causing an unexpected storm in the political circles of Hansŏng (漢城), court officials, fearing the peninsula's mountains and rivers would become a battlefield for the two nations, turned pale. They planned where Emperor Kojong (高宗), then at Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮), should seek refuge (蒙塵). Initially, there was a plan to move the imperial carriage (御駕) to P'yŏngyang (平壤), but considering that place also perilous, some advocated moving him to Vladivostok (海蔘威) instead. The vanguard of this proposal was Mr. Yi Yong-ik (李容翊氏), then a giant of the pro-Russian faction and holding the prominent position of Minister of Finance (大藏院卿). However, due to Japan's swift victory, this plan ultimately failed to materialize. This story has recently been circulating in some circles.

300,000 Yen for the Southwest Tour
On January 7th, the third year of Yung Hŭi (隆熙三年, 1909), Emperor Sunjong (純宗서), leading various imperial family members and ministers of the former Korean Empire (舊韓國) at the time, embarked on a tour (巡行), accompanied by Resident-General Itō (伊藤銃監陪從). In South Chosŏn, they stayed overnight (御一泊) in Taegu (大邱) and another night in Pusan (釜山) before returning. In West Chosŏn, they stayed overnight each in P'yŏngyang (平壤) and Sinŭiju (新義州). Secret histories (秘史) record that the expenses (經資) for this trip amounted to 300,000 yen (三十萬圓).

Number of Indicted Persons by Religious Affiliation (敎徒別起訴者數)
Although it happened long ago, the full picture (全貌) has only recently become clear, so we report it here. According to the most accurate statistics from the time of the March First Movement (己未事變): <19>
The number of religious adherents found guilty (有罪) at that time was: Christians (基督敎徒) 2,072, Ch'ŏndogyo followers (天道敎徒) 2,268, Confucianists (儒敎徒) 346, Buddhists (佛敎徒) 222, Catholics (天主敎徒) 54, Sich'ŏn'gyo followers (侍天敎徒) 14, Ch'ŏndogyo leaders (天道敎導師) 27. Furthermore, the total number of people indicted (總起訴人員) in the incident was 9,441. Those found guilty in the first trial numbered 8,471 (including 153 women, 內媂女). Those found guilty in the third trial numbered 7,816.
Since we're on the topic, let's also record that group movements (團體運動) occurred in 618 locations, with 500,000 participants (參加人員). There were 1,409 people seriously or lightly injured (重輕傷者). On the government authorities' side (官憲側), there were 8 deaths (死亡) and 158 injuries (仝負傷).

10,000 Yen Each

Regarding the Enlightenment Party (開化黨) activists, starting with Kim Ok-kyun (金玉均), who rushed to introduce Japanese civilization (日本의 文明) and advance the nation's opening but met an untimely death (非命) at the hands of Hong Chong-u (洪種宇) on the Shanghai (上海) docks, and including figures like Sŏ Kwang-bŏm (徐光範) et al., there were Imperial Grants (恩賜金) given at the time of annexation. Upon investigation, it is said their surviving families received the following amounts:
10,000 Yen Each (一萬圓式)
Kim Ok-kyun (故 戶曹參判 - Late Vice Minister of Finance), Hong Yŏng-gyun (故 吏曹參判 - Late Vice Minister of Personnel), Ŏ Yun-jung (故 度支部大臣 - Late Minister of Finance), Kim Hong-jip (故 首相 - Late Prime Minister), An Hyŏng-su (故 軍部大臣 - Late Minister of Military Affairs).

Subsidy for the Kukmin Hyŏphoe

Everyone knows that the Kukmin Hyŏphoe (國民協會) was a Japan-Korea harmonization organization (日鮮融和機關) formed by individuals like Min Wŏn-sik (閔元植), who was later assassinated by Yang Kwŏn-hwan (梁權煥) in Tōkyō (東京). During its heyday (全盛時代), the association frequently dispatched lecture teams (講演隊) everywhere and published a daily newspaper called Minjung Sinmun (民衆新聞), greatly raising its profile (聲勢). However, since the Manchurian Incident, it has become quiet (寂寂). We hear the newspaper is still published, perhaps a few times a month.
Regarding the reason for this decline, someone remarked:
"Because there's no gasoline (까소링)..."
"Gasoline," of course, means money. Indeed, a car cannot run without gasoline. So, how much subsidy does it receive from the Government-General (總督府)? Investigating this, we found that recently, the amount has been drastically reduced, and the Kukmin Hyŏphoe and Minjung Sinmun together receive only 300 yen (月 300圓) per month in subsidies.

Mr. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn's Salary
How much salary does Mr. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善氏), pen name Yuktang (六堂), a commissioned member (囑託) of the Central Council (中樞院) and a committee member of the Korean History Compilation Committee (朝鮮史編纂會委員), receive from the Government-General?
It is an "annual salary of 2,160 yen (年俸2,160圓)."
Calculated monthly, he receives a salary (月給) of 180 yen (180圓) per month. It has already been 5 years since he became a commissioned member of the Central Council.

Media and Literary World Insider Report

Chosŏn vs. Tong-a Building Competition

The Chosŏn Ilbo's (朝鮮日報) five-story company building (社屋) contract has been awarded, with construction (起工) set to begin in early April and completion (竣工) scheduled for the end of October. It is planned to include facilities found in advanced newspaper organizations (先進新聞機關), such as a VIP room (貴賓室), small meeting rooms (小集會場), and large assembly halls (大集會場). Furthermore, the entire fifth floor will be made into a large hall open to the public, expected to accommodate at least 2,000 people, making it <20> the largest meeting venue in Seoul. As they invest 200,000 yen (20만원) to construct this grand building, the Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報), not to be outdone, is also planning to build a new company building twice the size of its current one, set to begin construction (起工) in the New Year. It is also reported that they plan to add another story above the current building and the future new one to create a large hall on the fourth floor. Thus, the two grand (宏大) buildings of both companies are expected to soar into the clouds (白雲間) soon. Which building will prevail? The competition for readers' popularity, both in print and in architecture, grows ever fiercer.

Sacrifice by Central Daily News Executives

Central Daily News (中央日報) President Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨), Vice President Ch'oe Sŏn-ik (崔善益), Chief Editor Yi Kwan-gu (李寬求), Editorial Director Kim Tong-sŏng (金東成), and Business Director Hong Hŭi-sik (洪璯植) – these five gentlemen are currently working without pay (無報酬) until the newspaper's finances become more stable (潤澤). While this might be expected given the poor finances, one wonders if the tens of thousands of readers appreciate the sacrifice (犧牲) of these gentlemen.

Newspaper Circulation Numbers (新聞部數)

Chosŏn Ilbo 380,000 copies, Tong-a Ilbo 380,000 copies, Chungang Ilbo 260,000 copies, Maeil Sinbo 190,000 copies – these are the current circulation figures (發行 部數) for each newspaper recently. However, for those who doubt whether they truly sell this many, dividing by a certain common number might make things clearer. That number is a secret not to be divulged (天機不可洩), so please guess as you see fit.

(Assistant Head level) Tōkyō Asahi 200-300 yen, Tōkyō Nichi Nichi 200-300 yen, Yomiuri 130-150 yen, Hōchi No Assistant Head System, Jiji 100-150 yen, Chūgai No Assistant Head System, Miyako Same, Kokumin 90-130 yen. (Regular Reporter level - 平記者) Tōkyō Asahi 70-250 yen, Tōkyō Nichi Nichi 70-270 yen, Yomiuri 50-150 yen, Hōchi 50 yen, Jiji 50-150 yen, Chūgai 55 yen, Miyako 55 yen, Kokumin 50-80 yen. Writers' Manuscript Fees (文人稿料)

The highest manuscript fees (稿料) in Chosŏn are likely for Hong Myŏng-hŭi's (洪命熹氏) *Im Kkŏk-chŏng* (林巨正) and Yun Paek-nam's (尹白南氏) *Ponghwa* (烽火). The fee for Pyŏkch'o's (碧初 - Hong Myŏng-hŭi's pen name) work currently being serialized (連載) in the Chosŏn Ilbo is 100 yen per month, and likewise, Yun Paek-nam's *Ponghwa* also earns 100 yen per month. Since Mr. Yun Paek-nam is a commissioned writer (囑託) for the Tong-a Ilbo, some compensation for this status is likely included in this amount.
Furthermore, for Yŏm Sang-sŏp's (廉想涉) *Morankkot P'il Ttae* (모란꼿 필 때 - When the Peonies Bloom) published in the Maeil Sinbo, or other short stories up to 30-40 installments, the rate is 2 yen (2원) per installment. For Chang Hyŏk-chu's (張赫宙氏) *Mujigae* (무지개 - Rainbow) and Hyŏn Chin-gŏn's (玄憑盧氏 - Hyŏn Chin-gŏn's name appears differently here) *Chŏkto* (赤道 - Equator) published in the Tong-a Ilbo, the rate is around 2 to 3 yen. Kim Tong-in's (東仁氏) *Unhyŏn'gung-ŭi Pom* (雲晛宮의 봄 - Spring of Unhyŏn Palace) and Yi Ki-yŏng's (李箕永氏) *Kohyang* (故鄕 - Hometown) in the Chosŏn Ilbo both fetch 2 yen per installment. Pak P'ar-yang's (朴八陽氏) *Chŏngnyŏr-ŭi Tosi* (情熱의 都市 - City of Passion) and Sim Hun's (沈熏氏) *Kyŏnu Sŏng* (牽牛星 - Altair) <21> in the Chungang Ilbo were both around 2 yen per installment.
Mr. Chang Hyŏk-chu, who publishes many fine creative works in Japanese in magazines like *Kaizō* (改造) and *Bungei Shuto* (文藝首都), receives a manuscript fee from Kaizōsha (改造社) of 7 yen per manuscript page (원고지 10자 24행 1매). There is also a rumor that Professor Paek Nam-un (白南雲氏) of Yŏnhŭi College (延專敎授), who authored and published *Chosŏn Kyŏngje Sa* (朝鮮經濟史 - History of the Korean Economy) as part of Kaizōsha's "Complete Collection of Economics" (經濟學全集), received a manuscript fee of 2,000 yen from Kaizōsha for that single book.
Monthly magazines affiliated with newspaper companies, such as *Sin Tong-a* (新東亞) or *Chungang* (中央), also pay manuscript fees, typically 1 yen per printed page (頁). Filling one page of the 4x6 format (46배版) requires about 4 sheets of manuscript paper (20행 24자). Broken down, this is 25 chŏn (25錢) per sheet. In the arts and culture sections (學藝欄) of various newspapers, payment is usually around 2 yen per column (일단).

Marquis Pak Yŏng-hyo's Reading Habits

Marquis Pak Yŏng-hyo's (朴泳孝侯) recent reading consists mostly of ancient Chinese classics (古代漢書). For newspapers, he reads four types: Chosŏn Sinmun (朝鮮新聞), Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報), Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), and Maeil Sinbo (每日申報).

Maeil Sinbo's Advertising Revenue: 70,000 Yen

It is common knowledge (萬人共知) that the Maeil Sinbo is an organ paper (機關紙) and receives subsidies from the Government-General. Nevertheless, some doubt how it manages (經營) with its small circulation. However, this doubt will be permanently resolved (永解) upon learning that the Maeil Sinbo earns 70,000 yen (년 7만원) annually just from "Public Notices" (公告) from registry offices (登記所) across the entire country (全鮮各地) and government advertisements (官廳 廣告). 70,000 yen in advertising revenue that private newspapers could never obtain! It must be said, this is large.

Mr. Yi Kwang-su's Manuscript Fee
It is well-established (정평) that Mr. Yi Kwang-su's (李光洙氏) manuscript fee is the highest. For *Kŭ Yŏja-ŭi Ilsaeng* (그女子의 一生 - That Woman's Life), currently running in the Chosŏn Ilbo, he reportedly receives 20 yen (20원) per month as a novel allowance (小說手當) in addition to his salary (月給). So, what is his salary? 180 yen (180원). Combined with the novel allowance, it totals 200 yen (2백원) (At Tong-a Ilbo, it was 150 yen).

Advancing Literary Front

The newspaper novel scene is currently experiencing a golden age (黃金時代). The Chosŏn Ilbo is running Hong Myŏng-hŭi's *Im Kkŏk-chŏng Chŏn* (林巨正傳), Yi Kwang-su's *Kŭ Yŏja-ŭi Ilsaeng*, Yi Ki-yŏng's *Kohyang*, and the prize-winning work by Yi Pa-ch'on (李巴村), *P'okp'ungu Chinagandwi* (暴風雨 지나간뒤 - After the Storm Passes). All of these are long novels (長篇). The Tong-a Ilbo is running Chang Hyŏk-chu's *Mujigae*, Hyŏn Chin-gŏn's *Chŏkto*, and Yun Paek-nam's *Ponghwa*, and it is said another new long novel by a certain author will be published soon. The Chungang Ilbo is running Pak P'ar-yang's *Chŏngnyŏr-ŭi Tosi* and Sim Hun's *Kyŏnu Sŏng*. The Maeil Sinbo is running Yŏm Sang-sŏp's *Morankkot P'il Ttae* and other long novels by Ub'o (牛步 - Yŏm Sang-sŏp's pen name) and others.
Such a grand spectacle (盛觀) of the great masters of the creative world (創作界) all taking up their pens simultaneously is likely the first such grand event (盛事) since the literary world (文壇) emerged on the peninsula.

Founding of a New Theater

Just as the Tōkyō theater world (東京劇界) sees the emergence of Kobayashi Ichizō's (小林13 - likely 小林一三) Takarazuka Theater and the Nippon Gekijo, wielding enormous capital and drawing huge crowds, similar good news is heard from Seoul.
It concerns Mr. Hong (洪氏), the lover (愛人) of dancer Pae Ku-ja (裵龜子女史), who is currently planning (計劇) to build a large theater in Seoul <22> and has already been making preparations. According to the most reliable sources, land has already been acquired: 530 p'yŏng of land facing the main road near the Federation of Financial Associations (金融組合聯合會) outside Seodaemun (西大門박), purchased at 57 yen per p'yŏng. The plan is to spend about 60,000 yen (약 6만원) to construct a Western-style building (洋式建物).
There's talk the theater might be named Shōchiku-za (松竹座), but nothing is confirmed yet.
The stage will feature Ms. Pae Ku-ja's dance (舞踊), as well as revues (례뷰-), plays (演劇), and operas (歌劇). Motion pictures (活動寫眞) will also be screened. Positioned against the Chosŏn Theater and Tansŏngsa, it seems a beautiful competition is about to erupt in the theater world.

Financial and Gold Mining World Insider Report

1.2 Million Yen Gold Mine

In North P'yŏngan Province (平北), which produced the two great gold mine kings (金鑛王) Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak (崔昌學) and Pang Ŭng-mo (方應模氏), another millionaire from a major mine (鑛山) has emerged. He is Mr. Pak Yong-un (朴龍雲氏) of the Sinyŏn Gold Mine (新延金鑛), which had long been rumored to be for sale. It was sold (賣渡) to Mitsui (三井) for 1.2 million yen (120만원). He received 500,000 yen (50만원) in cash (現金), with the remaining 700,000 yen (70만원) to be received this coming May.
The Sinyŏn Gold Mine is famous enough that anyone involved in the mining industry (鑛業界) would know it. Mr. Pak Yong-un had already invested over 300,000 yen (30여 만원) in machinery and facilities. Its annual production value surpassed 400,000 yen (40만원), yielding substantial yearly profits for the mine owner.
However, according to speculation from some quarters (某邊), while the mine is good, excavation has already reached 200 ch'ŏk (尺 - feet) underground. There are doubts whether the ore vein (鑛脈) continues to be as rich. Furthermore, continuing excavation would require investing hundreds of thousands of yen (數十萬圓) for large-scale machinery (大規模의 기계설비). Since this might be impossible, some view selling it off cleanly, albeit with mixed feelings, as the safe (安全之策) move.
Moreover, after selling the Sinyŏn Gold Mine, Mr. Pak Yong-un has started a large ironworks (製鐵所) called Sinyŏn Ironworks (新延鐵工所) in Sinŭiju (新義州). It is intended to manufacture and sell all kinds of machinery used in various mines (鑛山) throughout Korea (朝鮮全道). In addition to this factory, Mr. Pak Yong-un is also operating another large gold mine nearby, which is reportedly performing very well (成績이 대단히 良好).

Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak's Mining Department (鑛山科) Donation

On January 26th, a gentleman visited Governor-General Ugaki (宇垣總督) at the Government-General building and donated (寄附) a large sum of 35,000 yen before leaving. He was the Gold Mine King, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak (崔昌學氏).
This donation was for the establishment of a new Mining Department (鑛山科) at the Kyŏngsŏng Industrial School (京城工業學校), requiring 35,000 yen in expenses. Mr. Ch'oe, who started empty-handed (赤手空拳) in the mining world and became a multimillionaire (300만원의 거부), reportedly did this to foster future generations.
It seems Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak's money is starting to be released, which is a congratulatory matter.

Expansion of Mr. Kim Ki-dŭk's Lumber Mill

Mr. Kim Ki-dŭk's (金基德氏) Musan Lumber Mill (茂山製材所), the most renowned business (實業家) in the North Chosŏn region, unfortunately burned down completely (全燒) due to an accidental fire last winter. <23> However, as it was insured (保險) for tens of thousands of yen (數萬圓), the loss was reportedly not too severe. With construction work booming (大振) in Manchukuo (滿洲國) leading to increased demand for lumber (木材需要) within Chosŏn, the lumber business (木材景氣) in the North Chosŏn area is very strong. Therefore, Mr. Kim Ki-dŭk decided to further expand (擴張) his lumber business, investing an additional 50,000 yen (5만여 원) to enlarge the Musan Lumber Mill. So it is reported.

Rapid Increase in Great Fortunes

In North P'yŏngan Province (平安北道), large gold mines are appearing one after another, being sold off profitably to conglomerates (資閥) like Ōsei (王井), Mitsubishi (三菱), and Koga (苦河). Millionaires (百萬長者) like Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak and Mr. Pang Ŭng-mo are emerging successively (疊出). With the establishment of Manchukuo (滿洲國家) and the designation of terminal ports (終端港), land speculators (土地卒富) worth 3 million yen (300만원), 10 million yen (1,000만원), etc., such as Kim Ki-dŭk, Hong Chong-hwa (洪鍾華), and Kim Yŏng-gŭn (金永根), are appearing. Furthermore, with the second phase of construction (第2期工事) for the hydroelectric power (水力電氣) and nitrogen company (窒素會社) starting in Changjin (長進) at a cost of 18 million yen (1,800만원), barren land (不毛의 땅) is being sold for factory sites (工場用地), causing dozens of wealthy individuals (수십만의 富者) to emerge rapidly. The recent economic boom (景氣) sounds prosperous (興盛興盛) just hearing about it. However, it remains an unknown (未知數) how many people will truly enter the palace of gold (黃金의 殿堂).

**Summary**

This 1934 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine, titled "The Korean Black Chamber," offers insider news across various sectors of colonial Korean society. It covers political rumors (Hŭngsa tan's return, Sin'ganhoe faction), military tensions (border incidents, commander's view on war), royal finances, historical anecdotes (Kojong's potential Vladivostok trip, March 1st Movement statistics), financial dealings (gold mine sales creating millionaires like Pak Yong-un, donations like Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak's, subsidies for pro-Japanese groups), media competition (Chosŏn vs. Tong-a building race, reporter salaries, circulation figures, manuscript fees for famous authors like Yi Kwang-su and Hong Myŏng-hŭi), business developments (department store openings in P'yŏngyang), and updates on religious/educational institutions (Ch'ŏndogyo finances, Christian school funding cuts, Songdo High School foundation efforts). It concludes with brief notes on prominent individuals' activities and health.

For historians, this article provides a rich snapshot of elite and institutional life under Japanese rule. Interesting aspects include the detailed (though perhaps inflated) newspaper circulation figures and salary comparisons between Korean and Japanese papers, the explicit mention of manuscript fees revealing the economics of the literary scene, the rise of new Korean capitalists through ventures like gold mining, the financial struggles of institutions dependent on foreign (American) aid versus self-sufficient ones like Ch'ŏndogyo, and the continued activities and networking of nationalist figures (Hŭngsa tan, Sin'ganhoe remnants) alongside collaborators and businessmen navigating the colonial system. The juxtaposition of immense wealth creation (gold mines, land speculation) with widespread poverty (implied by the tiny number of millionaires) and institutional financial precarity is also revealing.

興士團의 海內中心主義?
米州에잇는 興士團의 最高首惱者 宋鍾翊氏가 今年안으로 十數年만에 朝鮮에 드러오리라는데 아직 永永歸國함인지 又는 一時歸國함인지는 不明하나 宋氏가 드러오는것만은 事實이라한다 그런데 某消息通이 語하는 바에 依하면 興士團에는 米州와 上海方面에 約二萬나의 돈이 잇서서(日貨로 푸리하여 約六萬圓) 그 돈을 宋氏가 지니고 드러올듯하다는 說이잇다
아무튼 未久에 最高首惱者 宋鍾翊氏가 入國하고 海外에 무처잇든 돈 六萬圓이 드러오고 그러고 島山도 刑期이제 一二年이 남엇슨즉 不出數年하야 出獄할터이며  修養同友會의 活躍도더욱 준하여갈터이고

天道敎의 財政

근래 말조와하는 이 잇서 가로되 天道敎는 재정이 몹시 缺乏하여 잇느라고. 그래서 내부에서 有給인원을 작고 陶汰하고 잇는 中이라고.
그러나 우리가 아러 본 바에 의하면 아직까지도 天道敎의 재정은 다른 단체 다른 종교에 비하야 越等 優秀하다. 외국의 힘만 바라고 안젓든 基督敎는 米國의 돈이 잘드러오지 안차 사업을 縮少하고 교세가 부진하는 중에 잇고 그러타고 2,000만원, 3,000만원 云하는 佛敎로 말하면 寺刹令때문에 財産을 한푼도 임의로 쓰지 못하는 중이다. 이러한 사이에 잇서 天道敎만은 년예산 6, 7만원을 세워가지고 各般사업을 하고 잇다.
六正 7, 8년 全盛時代 天道敎에는 100만원의 대금이 잇잇다고 한다. 그러기에 30만원드려 교당을 建築하고 普成專門, 普成中學, 出版書肆普成館 等을 경영하엿다.
이러케 거액이 모인 財源의 근본을 따지면 孫義庵의 誠米制度의 완성에 잇다. 아츰마다 한숫깔式 떠놋는 誠米 이것이 每人前 1년에 돈으로 환산하면 3원 75전에 該當하다고 한다.
3원 75전式 내는 분이 10만명 잇스면 37만 5천원이 되지 안으냐.
그런데 원칙적으로 말하면 天道敎도 100만원이든지 50만원이든지 하는 財團法人으로 완성하여 노아야 누가 보든지 百年基礎가 잡혓다고 할 것이다.
