Magazine Title: 삼천리 제6권 제5호
Publication Date: 1934년 05월 01일
Article Title: 勸告三則 (Three Pieces of Advice)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 세태비평 (Social Commentary)

Those individuals who once forgot food and sleep, busying themselves with the common affairs of the nation and society, have recently often degenerated into pursuing only the accumulation of money. As times become more turbulent, devotion to serving the nation and society should intensify, yet it is deplorable that one by one, they break away and head down the path of personal gain (個人의 리해길). From now on, let us all become righteous individuals, patriots, and politicians absorbed in the rise and fall of the nation and society, just as in a past era.

Just when it seemed the evil customs (악풍) of the late Chosŏn period—the Kiho faction (긔호파), Sŏbuk faction (서북파), Honam faction (호남파)—had finally been swept away, now again the tendency toward forming cliques and factions (偏黨比周) centered around newspaper companies grows strong. Do we not well remember how our ancestors engaged in factional strife that ruined the nation (派爭亡族)? Before the holy crusade for the construction of national culture, there should only be one faction. Let us not form cliques (派閥).

Messrs. Kim Kidŏk (金基德), Hong Chonghwa (洪鍾華), Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學), Kim T'aewŏn (金台原), Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植), Pak Yongun (朴龍雲), Kim Ŭngsu (金應銖), Pang Ŭisŏk (方義錫), Yŏm Kyŏnghun (廉璟勳), Pak Yŏngch'ŏl (朴榮喆), Sŏ Pyŏngjo (徐丙朝), the great capitalists of this land—we hope that from now on, you will dedicate the majority of your private fortunes (사재) to meaningful cultural enterprises. Schools (學校), media organizations (言論機關), medical facilities (醫療機關), vocational training centers (授産場), etc.—feel that the time for capitalists to exert themselves has become more urgent, and we earnestly advise this.<27>

**Summary**

This article offers three pieces of advice to Koreans in 1934. First, it laments that former patriots are now prioritizing personal wealth over national service and urges a return to dedication. Second, it warns against rising factionalism, especially around newspapers, recalling the destructive factionalism of the past and calling for unity in building national culture. Third, it directly appeals to prominent wealthy Koreans by name, urging them to donate substantially to cultural infrastructure like schools, media, hospitals, and vocational centers, stressing the urgency of their contribution. For historians, the article is revealing of the socio-political anxieties and aspirations within colonial Korea. It highlights concerns about the perceived moral decline among the elite, the persistent problem of factionalism hindering national unity efforts, and the strong desire for cultural development and self-strengthening through education and infrastructure, placing the onus significantly on the Korean capitalist class. The specific naming of prominent capitalists is particularly notable.

勸告三則
민족과 사회의 공변된 일에 침식을 잇고 분주하든 분들이 근래에 흔히들 돈 모으는 일에 타락하고 만다. 시세가 간란할사록 더욱 민족 사회에 봉사하는 정성이 끌어야 할터인데 한둘씩 작고 제 個人의 리해길로만 버서저나가게 되니 개탄할 일이다. 이제부터는 지나간 어느 한 시절 치 누구나 민족과 사회의 흥망성쇠에 몰두하는 의인, 지사, 정치인이 되자.
긔호파, 서북파, 호남파하든 한말의 악풍이 겨우 일소되엇는가 할 때에 이제 또다시 신문사를 중심으로 偏黨比周하는 경향이 농후하여저 간다. 우리는 우리 조상의 派爭亡族하든 일을 잘 긔억하지 안는가. 민족문화 건설의 성십자군압헤 오직 일파가 잇슬 뿐이리라. 派閥을 짓지 말자.

金基德, 洪鍾華, 崔昌學, 金台原, 朴興植, 朴龍雲, 金應銖, 方義錫, 廉璟勳, 朴榮喆, 徐丙朝等氏 이땅의 큰 재산가들은 이제부터 빗잇는 文化事業에 그의 사재의 대부분을 기우려 주기를 바란다. 學校, 言論機關, 醫療機關, 授産場 등, 지금은 財産家의 發憤이 더욱 급하여 질때임을 늣기고 이를 간절히 勸告한다.<27>
