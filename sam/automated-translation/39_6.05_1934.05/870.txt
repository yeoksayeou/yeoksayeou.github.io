Article Title: 어머니의 무릅 (Mother's Lap)
Author: 春園
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Mother's Lap
						Ch'unwŏn
Mother!
Your lap was soft.
Softer than the grass on the spring mountainside,
Softer than the clouds floating in the summer sky,
Softer than sheep's wool, softer than a silk cushion.
Mother!
It was on that soft lap that I sat!

Mother!
Your bosom was vast.
Vaster than all the plains upon the wide earth,
Vaster than all the seas upon the wider earth,
Perhaps, vaster than the boundless blue sky.<231>
Mother!
It was upon that vast bosom that I rested!

Mother!
Your bosom was my world.
When my belly was full, it was my playground,
When I was tired, it was my bed,
When I was scared, it was my refuge.
Buried deep in your bosom,
With only my eyes peeking out, twinkling,
Was I scared even if a tiger came?
Mother!
It was upon that safe bosom that I rested!

Mother!
Your bosom was fertile land!
As soon as I suckled
Your soft nipple, sweet milk flowed.
Was there ever even once a time
You said, "Child, I dislike you, don't nurse"?
Until my belly was full, until my body was warm,
I suckled and suckled until I grew tired of it.<232>

Mother!
After eating my fill,
When I still didn't want to let go of the nipple,
With my tiny teeth, only two of them,
I would bite down hard or chew gently.
At that time, Mother, you would say,
"Child, that hurts!" and
Spank my bare bottom, didn't you?
Really, did you spank me because you disliked me?

Mother!
I had the skill
To call you in from the kitchen.
Whether I called "Mommy!" or cried "Waah!",
If still there was no answer,
I'd kick my feet and thrash about,
And after crying my fill for a good while,
Suddenly, you'd be wiping your hands on your skirt hem,
And onto that lap, onto that bosom,
You'd lift me up as I threw a tantrum!

Mother!
In your embrace, there were no seasons.<233>
Clinging tightly to your nipple, closing my eyes tight,
Night became day, day became night!
At that time, as I kicked my legs about,
Babbling sounds I didn't understand all day long,
Would you call that night, would you call that day?
Whether snow covered the ground or not,
Whether ice formed and cold winds blew,
Buried deep in your bosom,
Fumbling with your nipple,
Would you call that winter, would you call that summer?

Mother!
Your lap was my playground.
When I crawled up, it was a mountain,
When I sat upon it, it was a grand palace,
When I lay down upon it, it was a bed.
When I crawled up, crying and flailing,
In my eyes, there was only that lap.
No matter how much I cried,
Once I just sat up there, I'd beam with a smile.

Mother!<234>
Your lap was my school.
That the creature that goes "Meow!" is a cat,
That the creature that goes "Woof!" is a puppy,
That the creature that goes "Choo-choo!" is a train (火輪車),
That when the sun's shadow reaches there,
Father will come home,
I learned it all at that school.

Mother!
The story of pitiful K'ongjwi and P'at'chwi,
Of the rabbit's liver and Master Tortoise,
Of the deeply interesting story of Shim Ch'ŏng (沈娘子),
Who fell into the water and became a lotus flower,
I learned it all at that school.
"Ka-gya, kŏ-gyŏ, ko-gyo, ku-gyu,"
Didn't you teach me, Mother?
"Sky ch'ŏn (天), earth chi (地), dark hyŏn (玄)," this too,
You taught me, didn't you, Mother?

Mother!
You must remember, right?
"What's this?" "Eye!"
"What's this?" "Nose!"<235>
"And this?" "I don't know that!"
"That's an ear!" you'd say,
And spank my bottom.

Mother!
You often spanked my bottom.
But did you do it out of dislike?
I also pulled your hair.
But did I do it to hurt you?
Your words, "Ouch!",
The slap striking my bottom,
I did it because I enjoyed that.

Mother!
But now I am in a distant land!
Everyone, they are people I couldn't see from Mother's lap.
Mother's songs and interesting stories,
No matter how I try to listen, I cannot hear them.
Mother, when did I come to such a place?
Mother!
Where am I now?
Why did you send me here!
Mother, where are you?<236>
Why did you leave me and go somewhere?
Don't you think of me?

Mother!
The words you taught me,
Written in the letters you taught me,
With the melody that flowed from your lips,
I sing this song thinking of you.
Your bosom, vast and fertile!
Your lap, softer than grass, than clouds!
I weep. In a distant land,
Missing it, longing to see it.<237>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su) is a deeply nostalgic and emotional ode to the poet's mother. It reminisces about the complete safety, comfort, nourishment, and education received within the mother's embrace, symbolized by her lap and bosom. The poet contrasts this idyllic past with the present reality of being alone in a "distant land," filled with unfamiliar faces and an aching longing for the mother's presence and the world she represented. The poem captures a universal feeling of homesickness but, given the author and publication date (1934), it strongly reflects the displacement and cultural loss experienced by Koreans during the Japanese colonial period. Historians might find the poem's expression of longing for a lost world (the mother's embrace as a metaphor for the homeland) and the sense of disorientation in a foreign environment particularly illustrative of the psychological impact of colonialism and migration on individuals during that era. The emphasis on learning Korean language, stories (K'ongjwi and P'at'chwi, Shim Ch'ŏng), and basic Hanja from the mother highlights the role of maternal figures in cultural transmission, especially poignant when that culture was under pressure.

어머니의 무릅

어머니!
당신의 무릅은 부드러웁데다.
봄철 메기슭의 잔디보다도
녀름 하늘에 뜨는 구름보다도
羊의 털보다도 비단房席보다도.
어머니!
그 부드러운 무릅헤 제가 안젓셧지오!

어머니!
당신의 가슴은 廣闊합데다.
넓다는 땅우희 모든 벌판보다도
더 넓다는 땅우희 모든 바다보다도
아마, 한정업나는 푸른 하늘보다도.<231>
어머니!
그 廣闊한 가슴에 제가 안졋섯지오!

어머니!
당신의 가슴이 제 世界엿서요.
배부른 때에는 노리터엿고
疲困할 때에는 寢台엿섯고
무서운 때에는 避難處엿서요.
당신의 가슴에 푹 파무처서
눈만 반작반작 내어 노핫슬적에
虎狼이가 온들 무서웟습닛가.
어머니!
그 安全한 가슴에 제가 안졋섯지오!

어머니!
당신의 가슴은 沃土엿서요!
당신의 부드러운 젓꼭지를
빨기만 하면 단젓이 흘럿습니다.
일즉 한번이나
「얘야 밉다 젓 먹지 말아」
하선 적 잇서요?
배가 부르도록, 몸이 훈훈하도록
빨다가 빨다가 제가 실혀야 말앗지오.<232>

어머니!
배껏 먹고나서,
그래도 젓꼭지를 노키가 실히면
저는 두개밧게 아니난 조고만 닛발로
꼭 물기도하고 잘근잘근 씹기도햇지오.
그때에 어머니께서,
「얘야 아프다!」하시고
벌거벗은 제 엉댕이를 때리섯지오?
그래, 당신께서 미워서 때리셧서요?

어머니!
저는 부억에 잇는 당신을
불러 들이는 재조가 잇섯서요.
「엄마!」하든지, 「앵이」하든지
그래도 아모런 대답이 업스시면
발버둥치고 몸부림하며
한바탕 실커정 울고 나면
어느덧 당신은 치마귀로 손을 씨스며
그 무릅헤, 그 가슴에
쓰는 저를 올려노흐셧지오!

어머니!
당신의 품속에는 歲月이 업섯습닌다.<233>
젓꼭지에 척 매어달려서 척 눈올 감으면
밤도 나지오, 낫도 밤이엿서요!
그때 제가 다리를 버둥버둥하면서
중일 저도 모르는 소리를 짓거릴때에
그것을 밤이라겟서요, 나지라겟서요?
땅에야 눈이 덥히거나 말거나
얼음이 얼고 찬바람이 불거나,
당신의 가슴에 푹 파무쳐서
졋꼭지를 쥐물적 쥐물적할 때에
그것을 겨울이라겟서요, 녀름이라겟서요?

어머니!
당신의 무릅혼 제 놀이터엿서요.
긔어올흘 때에는 山이엇섯고
올라안즌 때에는 大闕이엇섯고
두러누을 때에는 寢帶엿서요.
제가 울면서 벌네벌네 긔어올 때에
제 눈에는 오직 그 무릅히 잇섯서요.
아모리 울다가도
거긔만 올라 안즈면 방끗 우섯지오.

어머니!<234>
당신의 무릅혼 제 學校엿서요.
「냥!」 하는 짐생은 고양이란 말도,
「왕!」 하는 짐생은 강아지란 말도,
「붕!」 하는 짐생은 火輪車란 말도,
해 그럼자가 저긔까지 오면
아버지께서 들어오신단 말도,
다 그 學校에서 배홧서요.

어머니!
불상한 콩주와 팟주도,
토끼 肝과 자라主簿도,
물에 빠저서 蓮꼿이 되여난
沈娘子의 재미잇는 니야기도,
다 그 學校에서 배왓서요.
「가갸, 거겨, 고교, 구규」하고
어머니께서 아니가르치셧서요?
「하늘天, 地, 감을玄」이것도
어머니께서 가르쳐 주셧지오?

어머니!
생각나시겟지오?
「요것은 무엇?」 「눈!」
「요것은 무엇?」 「꼬!」<235>
「또 요것은?」 「그것은 몰라!」
「그것은 귀지!」하고
제 볼기작올 따리신것을.

어머니!
당신은 제 엉덩이를 잘 따리셧습닌다.
그러나 미워서 그리셧서요?
제가 당신의 머리카락도 뜨덧습닌다.
그러나 아프라고 그랫겟서요?
「아야!」 하는 당신의 말슴,
철석하고 내 엉덩이를 따림,
그것이 조와서 그랫지오.

어머니!
그러나 只今 나는 먼 나라에 잇서요!
모도 다, 어머니 무릅헤서 못 보던 사람.
어머니의 노래와 재미잇는 니야기는
아모리 들으랴도 들을 수가 업습니다.
어머니, 언제 제가 이런 고장에 왓서요?
어머니!
只今 제가 어듸 잇서요?
웨 저를 이리로 보내셧서요!
어머니 당신온 어대 게서요?<236>
웨 저를 두고 어듸로 가셧서요?
당신은 제 생각이 아니 나십닛가.

어머니!
당신께서 가라처 주신 말을,
당신께서 가라처 주신 글字로 써써
당신의 입설에서 흐르던 曲調로
당신을 생각하는 노래를 부릅니다
廣闊하고 沃土이던 당신의 가슴!
잔디보다, 구름보다 부드럽던 무릅!
저는 웁니다. 먼 나라에서,
그것이 그리워서, 보고십허서<237>
