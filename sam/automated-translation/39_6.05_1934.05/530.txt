Enlightenment (開明)

Hyŏngsik: "Didn't your father scold you afterwards? After seeing just the two of you sitting in the pitch black with even the electric lights turned off!"
Sukhui: "No. He was actually impressed (感心). Said I was an economist (經濟家) for not having unnecessary electric lights turned on."

Short-Term Marriage (短期結婚)

Ch'unsam: "Hey there. Won't you marry me?"
Myŏngsuk: "I don't think I'd live long with a gentleman like you."
Ch'unsam: "No, the contract (契約) can be extremely short, that's fine. A short-term marriage!"

Psychology of an Unmarried Woman (未婚女의 心理)

Mother: "Why have you been so melancholy (愁心) lately? Is it Ch'unsik (春植) from across the street?"
Daughter: "..."
Mother: "Then is it Poknam (福男) next door?"
Daughter: "No, no, Mother, isn't there anyone else?"

Courage of a Schoolgirl (女學生의 勇敢)

Excerpt from the Diary (日記抄) of a young woman named K traveling by boat during summer vacation<105>
Day 1 - Leaving my parents behind in my hometown and embarking on a long journey alone, I can't describe how lonely (寂寞) it feels. The sea is blue and the sunlight is clear-
Day 2 - I met the young ship's captain on the deck. He's the handsome type (美男子型). Shall I say he resembles Chevalier?
Day 3 - The captain came to visit my cabin. He seems like a very nice person. We talked for a couple of hours.
Day 4 - The captain came and said, "If you do not love me, I have nothing left to hope for in this world, so I will sink (沈沒) this ship. Then you will die, I will die, and the passengers (船客) will die too-"
Day 5 - I resolutely decided to sacrifice (犧牲) myself (一身) to save (構造) the 300 passengers on our ship, and in the evening, I knocked on the captain's cabin door.<106>

**Summary**

This article is a collection of four short, humorous pieces titled "Miscellaneous Writings." They include jokes about a daughter praised for saving electricity by sitting in the dark with a boy, a man proposing a "short-term marriage," a mother trying to guess which neighborhood boy her gloomy daughter likes, and a darkly humorous fictional diary entry of a schoolgirl who "sacrifices" herself to a ship's captain threatening to sink the vessel. The pieces reflect social interactions and humor of the time, touching on themes of frugality, marriage anxieties, young love, and a satirical, albeit dark, take on female "sacrifice." For historians, the snippets offer glimpses into colloquial language, humor styles, and perhaps underlying social commentary or anxieties (like the transactional view of marriage or the disturbing "schoolgirl courage" story) present in 1930s colonial Korea.

잡저
開明

亨植「그 뒤 아버님께서 꾸지람 아니하서요? 전기등까지 꺼버린 까만 안에 둘만 안젓는 것을 보시고서!」
淑姬「아녀요. 도 혀 感心하고 게섯서요. 쓸데업는 전등불을 커지 안코잇다고 經濟家라고요」

短期結婚

春三「네 이보세요. 나와 결혼하여 주지 안켓슴니까.」
明淑「나 당신가튼 양반과는 오래 살 것 갓지 안케 생각되여요.」
春三「아니 契約은 극히 짤너도 조흐니 短期結婚으로요.」

未婚女의 心理

어머니「엇제서 요지간 이러케 愁心끼니? 어듸 건너집 春植이냐」
딸「...」
어머니「그럼 이웃집 福男이냐」
딸「아녀 아녀 어머니 어듸 업수?」

女學生의 勇敢

녀름방학을 이용하야 배를 타고 여행하든 K라는 젊은 여자의 日記抄<105>
第1일-고향에다가 부모를 두고 혼자 먼 여행을 뎍난다는 것이 엇더케 寂寞한지 모르겟다. 바다는 푸르고 햇빗은 맑고-
第2일-갑판에서 젊은 선장을 맛낫다. 美男子型이다. 슈-바리에와 비슷하다 할가.
第3일-관장이 나의 침실로 놀나왓다. 퍽 조흔 사람갓다. 우리 둘은 두어 시간 약이하엿다.
第4일-관장이 와서 만일 당신이 나를 사랑하여 주지 안으면 나는 이 세상에 아모것도 바라볼 것이 업슴으로 이 배를 沈沒식히겟소이다. 그래서 당신도 죽고, 나도 죽고 船客도 죽이고-」 라고 한다.
第5일-나는 단연 一身을 희생하여 우리 배에 탄 300명 船客을 구조하기로 결심하고 저녁에 선장실 또이를 두드리엇다.<106>
