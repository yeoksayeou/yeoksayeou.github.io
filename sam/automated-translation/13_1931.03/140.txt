Legend of Bosingak Pavilion

As you set foot in Sŏul, the building that first deeply strikes your heart will likely be Posin'gak Pavilion at the Chongno crossroads.

However, a pitiful legend flows around this Posin'gak. About 550 years ago from now, Yi T'aejo <30> established the royal capital in Sŏul and decided to build a pavilion (閣) and hang a large bell inside it to announce the time to the citizens. To make this bell, one *kil* (approx. 2-3 meters) high and two and a half *kil* in circumference, not only within the capital but also from far away in the eight provinces, countless amounts of cast iron were requisitioned. However, for some reason, whenever the bell was made, it would immediately develop cracks and break. So, they asked a Taoist sage (道士), who said to boil a living young child together with the molten iron when melting it.

Military runners and messengers (軍奴使令) spread throughout the capital. It was to find a child breathing with vivid life. But no matter how beast-like the parents, none would offer their son or daughter to be boiled. Then, just as a government clerk (衙前) was passing through Sadong kolmok,

"Selling a child?"

he shouted, and from inside a certain house, a mother holding the young child she loved, finding it so adorable, jokingly laughed,

"Shall we sell our baby?"

However, the messenger (使者) who heard these words jumped for joy. Once the words were uttered, under the pressure of the royal command, there was no way to retract them, and threats were received. The child was finally snatched away by the messenger, and that day, together with the boiled molten iron, its bones, flesh, and life turned into molten iron. As its whole body melted, the young child, meaning "I die because of mother," cried out three times,

"Ŏmilnae! Ŏmilnae! Ŏmilnae!"

and died. Afterwards, it is said that when the bell is struck, the bell's sound goes "Ŏmilnae, Ŏmilnae".

But now, there is no way even to hear this sound.<31>
<30-31>

**Summary**

This article recounts the well-known Korean legend associated with the casting of a great bell, specifically linked here to the Posin'gak Pavilion in Sŏul during the time of King Yi T'aejo. The legend explains that repeated failures in casting the bell led to the horrific decision, based on a Taoist sage's advice, to sacrifice a living child in the molten metal. A child was obtained after its mother jokingly offered it for sale, and its dying cry, "Ŏmilnae!" (Mother!), is said to resonate in the bell's toll. The article concludes with a lament that this legendary sound is no longer audible. For historians, this piece is interesting as it documents the popular retelling of a traditional, albeit gruesome, Korean folk tale during the Japanese colonial period (1931). The final sentence, "But now, there is no way even to hear this sound," could be interpreted as a poignant commentary on the perceived loss of tradition or cultural voice under colonial rule, making it a subtle artifact of cultural sentiment during that time.

普信閣의 傳說
여러분이 서울에 발을 드러노면서 몬저 마음 깁히 부드치는 건물은 종로 네거리에 잇는 보신각일 것이다.
그런데 이 보신각에는 애처러운 전설이 흐르고 잇다. 지금부터 약 550년전 리태조가＜30＞ 서울에 王都를 정하고 시민에게 시각을 알니우고저 閣을 짓고 그  안에 큰 종을 만들어 걸기로 하엿다. 무쇠를 촬촬 녹여 놉히 한길, 두리 두길반이나 되는 이 종을 만들기에 장안안은 물론 멀니 팔도로부터 무쇠는 수업시 증발되엇다. 그러나 엇전일인지 종을 만들어만 노흐면 즉시 금이 슬어 깨어지고 께어지고 한다. 그래서 道士에게 무르니 쇠물을 녹일 때 살아잇는 어린 아해를 함께 너허 끌이라 하엿다.
軍奴使令들은 장안 안에 쪽펴지엇다. 숨이 퍼럿케 살아잇는 아해를 구하기 위함이라. 그러나 아모리 즘생가튼 부모라도 제아들딸을 끌이자고 하는 이는 업섯다. 그러자 마츰 한 아전이 사동골목을 지내가는데
「어린애 팔으시우」
하고 소리를 치자 엇든 집 안에서 어머니가 사랑하는 어린애를 안고 너무도 귀여워서 히롱삼아
「우리 애기를 팔어볼가」
하고 웃섯다. 그러나 이 말을 드른 使者는 깃부어서 뛰엇다. 한번 내인 말은 왕명압헤 엇절수 업시 위협을 밧엇고. 어린애는 마츰내 使者에게 빼앗겨 그날 끌은 쇠물과 가치 뼈와 고기와 목숨이 쇠물로 화하고 말엇다. 一身이 녹아질 때 그 어린 아해는 어미때문에 나는 죽는다는 뜻으로
「어밀내! 어밀내! 어밀내!」
하고 세 번 웨우고 죽엇다. 그 뒤 종을 치면 종소리가 어밀내 어밀내하고 난다고 한다.
그러나 지금은 이 소리조차 드를길 업구나.＜31＞
