百萬長者의 葬式
Millionaire's Funeral

「여보게, 盧叅奉. 宅은 웨 작고 우오. 엇더케 도라가신 이집 어룬과 족하벌이나 되시우」
"Hey, Mr. No Ch'ambong. Why are you crying so much? Are you perhaps a nephew or something to the deceased elder of this house?"

「족하벌이 되면 웨 울겟소. 八字사나워 이런 부자집과는 아모 인연업게 태어낫스니 울밧게」
"Why would I cry if I were his nephew? It's because my fate (八字) is so wretched that I was born without any connection to such a rich family that I can do nothing but cry."

女學生의 對話
A Female Student's Conversation

「얘 乙順아. 나는 엇저면 조흐냐. 글세 京植씨는 부자지만 나는 그이에게 사랑이 아니가고 風船씨는 가난하지만 사랑이 가니 누구와 결혼하면 조탄 말이냐」
"Hey, Ŭlsun. What should I do? You see, Mr. Kyŏngsik is rich, but I don't feel any love for him, while Mr. P'ungsŏn is poor, but I do feel love for him. Who am I supposed to marry?"

「그야 쉽지. 너 세상에 戀愛가 第一이란다. 어서 風船씨헌테 가고 京植씨라는 이를 나에게 紹介하여 주럼.＜51＞
"Well, that's easy. Love (戀愛) is the most important thing in the world, you know. Hurry up and go to Mr. P'ungsŏn, and introduce that Mr. Kyŏngsik fellow to me." <51>

**Summary**

This article consists of two short, unrelated satirical dialogues. The first depicts a man crying at a millionaire's funeral, not out of grief, but out of sorrow for his own misfortune of not being related to the wealthy deceased. The second features a female student agonizing over choosing between a rich suitor she doesn't love and a poor one she does; her friend advises her to choose love and humorously asks to be introduced to the rich man. These vignettes offer a glimpse into the social attitudes of 1930s colonial Korea, highlighting the stark awareness of class differences and the desire for wealth, juxtaposed with emerging modern ideas about romantic love ('yŏnae') challenging traditional marriage considerations, especially among the younger generation.
