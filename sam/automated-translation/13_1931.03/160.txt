Article Title: 植民地的 朝鮮의 財政論 (A Treatise on the Finances of Colonial Chosŏn)
Author: 北岳山學人
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Essay)

A Treatise on the Finances of Colonial Chosŏn
						Pugaksan Hagin (Scholar of Mt. Pukak)

The Advance of Imperialist Policy
Colonial finance, in its fundamental characteristics, is no different from central finance. It inevitably develops due to the progression of [illegible/censored]ism. However, simply because the colony itself is the [illegible/censored] and the [illegible/censored] of Japan's [illegible/censored]ist policy, its finances reveal, in the most blatant form, the underlying basis of [illegible/censored]ist Japan's finances. That is:
1. The expansion of the market for [illegible/censored]ist Japan's commodities
2. The acquisition of its raw material resources
3. Furthermore, the investment of capital accumulated within mainland Japan
It goes without saying that Japan obtained these things for these purposes. Of course, Taiwan (臺灣) was not acquired from the beginning with such clear intentions, nor had Japan's capitalism developed to that extent at the time. However, it is certain that Taiwan, after its annexation, has been governed under these intentions. To state such policies more simply, it would not be an overstatement to call them policies for the capitalization of the colony. The expansion of the market for mainland Japanese goods, the development of raw material resources, and the investment of accumulated capital all achieve their goals by capitalizing the colony.

The goal is the capitalization of the colony – therefore, to state the basis of colonial finance in one word, it could be said that it aims for the capitalization of the colony. In such cases, it is natural that mainland capital is treated advantageously. Consequently, colonial expenditures make extraordinary efforts towards capitalist enterprise management. Also, from the perspective of developing raw material resources, massive civil engineering projects are vigorously planned. The purpose of this text is to explain these points.

Supplementary Funds from Central Finance – Regarding the relationship between colonial finance and central finance, the first point to note is the issue of supplementary funds for colonial finance. Supplementary funds refer to cases where the central government treasury provides assistance when colonial finances, no matter what, cannot independently cover all expenditures. Namely:
Shōwa 4 (1929) Fiscal Year Actual Budget, Colonial Special Account Supplementary Funds
Chosŏn (朝鮮)      15,473,000 Wŏn

Kwantōshū (關東州)      5,000,000 Wŏn

Karafuto (樺太)      3,600,000 Wŏn

Nan'yō (南洋)      1,800,000 Wŏn

Total        25,873,000 Wŏn

Currently, the central government provides approximately 26 million wŏn in supplementary funds to the colonial finances of colonies other than Taiwan. It is natural that the amount of supplementary funds will decrease as the colony advances its capitalist development and becomes financially independent. Among the colonies, the only one that has succeeded in this sense is the colony of Taiwan. Taiwan received a total of 30,408,691 wŏn in supplementary funds from Meiji 29 (1896) to Meiji 37 (1904), but thereafter became completely independent, not only covering its expenditures but now even being able to make various contributions, to some extent, to the finances of mainland Japan.<32> However, the other colonies, far from discontinuing their supplementary funds, are actually seeing increases. For example, Chosŏn started with a supplementary fund of 12.35 million wŏn at the time of annexation in Meiji 44 (1911), decreased it to 3 million wŏn in Taishō 7 (1918), and even temporarily suspended it in Taishō 8 (1919). However, upon the outbreak of the Manse Incident (萬歲事件) in Taishō 8, it became necessary to require supplementary funds again starting from Taishō 9 (1920). Kwantōshū and Karafuto also show similar trends.

The Awakening of the Masses and the Increase in Supplementary Funds – Looking at the trend of these supplementary funds, the lowest amounts were around Taishō 7-8 (1918-1919), and thereafter, they have all increased instead. This undoubtedly speaks to the fact that the development of Japanese capitalism during the World War spurred the capitalist leap of the colonies, allowing them financial leeway. However, it holds a more profound meaning elsewhere. It concerns Japan's [illegible/censored] policy in response to the post-war instability of the world economy, the establishment of the Soviet Union, and the rise of national movements spurred by Wilsonian self-determination. That is, colonial finances were compelled by the need to implement various facilities to [illegible/censored] the colonial masses. Consequently, despite the halt in revenue growth due to the general post-war recession, expenditures had to expand. This is a major reason for the outcome.

Great Profits for Mainland Japanese Capitalists
So, what relationship do supplementary funds generally have with the interests of the colonial masses? As already discussed, the aim of colonial expenditures is the thorough capitalization of the colony. If so, those who benefit from this will be the capitalists, especially the capitalists of mainland Japan. Ultimately, the increase in supplementary funds merely shows the result of mainland capitalists' colonial development, funded by the burden on the mainland masses. Thus, as mainland capitalists benefit from colonial development, the colonial masses, according to the logic of capitalist development, gradually have the degree of their exploitation (被搾取率) intensified. This is a clear fact, evident even from the point that despite the increase in supplementary funds, the burden of national and local taxes on the colonial masses is gradually increasing.

The relationship between colonial finance and central finance does not end merely with supplementary funds. The expenditures of the central finance also include many expenses related to colonial development and the capitalist development of the colonies. Examples include colonial armaments (軍備), subsidies for various industries, and various expenses related to colonial administration. For instance, looking at the expenses incurred by the government for the development of Chosŏn according to the "Chōsen Tokuhon" (朝鮮讀本) co-authored by Ikeda (池田, Former Director of the Government-General's Industrial Bureau, Current Governor of Hokkaidō) and Hirai (平井, Former Head of the Government-General's Educational Affairs Section, Current Prefectural Governor):

Government Expenses Required for Chosŏn Development
(From Meiji 40 Fiscal Year to Shōwa 3 Fiscal Year)
Military Expenses    292,050 thousand wŏn

Administrative Expenses     85,988 thousand wŏn

Financial Supplementary Funds    206,185 thousand wŏn

Total      584,223 thousand wŏn

Although there is no clear explanation regarding the calculation method, this 584 million wŏn was necessary because of the possession of Chosŏn, and the majority of it was planned for the [illegible/censored] of mainland capitalists.

Ultimately, the Masses Lose... Thus, regarding the relationship between colonial finance and central finance, whether the former benefits from the latter or not, those sitting comfortably and profiting are the mainland capitalists [illegible/censored], while the proletarian masses of the mainland and the masses of the colony have no policy [illegible/censored] whatsoever to alleviate their ever-increasing living costs.

Various Characteristics of Colonial Expenditures
Classified Civil Engineering Costs – In colonial expenditures, particularly in the sense of promoting the capitalization of the colony, enormous civil engineering projects, expenditures for industrial promotion (勸業費), and operating expenses for projects like railways (鐵道) and harbor construction (築港) reach large amounts. Let's look at the Shōwa 4 (1929) fiscal year actual budget.

Chosŏn's Railway-Centricism – Chosŏn's expenditures total 174,395,000 wŏn in the regular section and 62,036,000 wŏn in the temporary section, summing to 236,431,000 wŏn. Extracting the major expenses,<33> the largest item in the regular expenses is Railway Operating Expenses (鐵道作業費), calculated at 57 million wŏn. Following this are Local Government Office Expenses (地方廳費) at 31 million wŏn, Monopoly Bureau (專賣局) expenses at 23 million wŏn, Chosŏn's burden for the Debt Reduction Fund (減債基金) at 19 million wŏn, and Communications Expenses (遞信費) at 13 million wŏn, all exceeding 10 million wŏn. The meaning behind the Monopoly Bureau expenses will be discussed in the explanation of the Monopoly System (專賣制度). The crucial significance railways hold for colonial development is also clear. As of November, Shōwa 3 (1928), the length of Chosŏn's government-run railways reached 1,588 miles, whereas at the time of annexation, it was merely 768 miles. Additionally, private railways account for 486 miles of operating lines and 911 miles of unopened lines, with subsidies for them calculated at 4.13 million wŏn in the temporary budget. Currently, the Government-General is planning a 12-year project starting from Shōwa 2 (1927) to construct 860 miles of new lines and purchase 210 miles of private railways, with a total estimated cost of a massive 320 million wŏn. The 13 million wŏn in Communications Expenses covers telegraph, telephone, and postal services in Chosŏn, which are also self-evidently expenses aimed at [illegible/censored] the capitalization of the colony.

Plan for Increased Production of Chosŏn Rice – Besides general business operations aimed at [illegible/censored] capitalization as mentioned above, projects for developing raw material resources for mainland industries are also extraordinarily vast. The Plan for Increased Production of Chosŏn Rice (朝鮮米增殖計劃事業), which Japan is [illegible/censored] pursuing to solve the food problem for the Second World War, can also be considered to belong to this category. That is, starting from Taishō 15 (1926), a 14-year plan involves improving 350,000 chŏngbo (정보, unit of land area) of land, for which the Government-General will disburse 65 million wŏn as subsidies for construction costs. Furthermore, to cover the agricultural improvement costs of this project, a way has been secured for the Oriental Development Company (東拓, Tōtaku) and the Industrial Bank (殖産銀行, Shokusan Ginkō) to provide over 240 million wŏn in low-interest loans. Through this, the aim is to increase rice production by 8.2 million sŏk (석, unit of volume) upon the project's completion.

The Colonial Monopoly System
Monopoly System and Its Types – In the colonies, being in the midst of capitalist development, it is not possible to fully implement a tax system. Therefore, since it is difficult to obtain revenue through direct taxes (直接稅), they inevitably adopt the monopoly system, a type of indirect tax (間接稅), and derive profits from it to supplement revenue. Not only that, but the monopoly system also provides significant direct and indirect benefits to capitalist enterprises, especially those of mainland Japanese capitalists.
Japan's colonial monopoly systems are as listed below, and the revenue earned from them constitutes a considerable amount of the total revenue.
I. Chosŏn's Monopoly System
1. Ginseng Monopoly (人參專賣)
2. Salt Monopoly (鹽專賣)
3. Tobacco Monopoly (煙草專賣)
II. Taiwan's Monopoly System
1. Opium Monopoly (阿片專賣)
2. Salt Monopoly (食鹽專賣)
3. Camphor Monopoly (橦腦專賣)
4. Tobacco Monopoly (煙草專賣)
5. Liquor Monopoly (酒專賣)
III. Kwantōshū's Monopoly System
1. Opium Monopoly (阿片專賣)

Monopoly Profits as a Burden on the Masses – To see how significant monopoly profits (專賣益金) are in colonial finance, looking at the Shōwa 4 (1929) Fiscal Year Actual Budget:
Chosŏn   38,917,000 wŏn

Taiwan   44,846,000 wŏn

Kwantōshū   2,657,000 wŏn

These all function as a type of indirect tax, borne indirectly by the colonial masses. The characteristic of the colonial monopoly system is not merely adopting the monopoly system to [collect?] indirect [taxes?] but actively [benefiting?] mainland capitalists.
Monopoly Profits from Chosŏn Ginseng – The ginseng monopoly in Chosŏn is a legacy system from the Korean Empire era, generally adopting the following structure. Special cultivation zones are designated in Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道) and Hwanghae Province (黃海道地方), and white ginseng (白蔘) produced outside these zones is prohibited from being processed into red ginseng (紅蔘). (Continued on page 21)<34> Only white ginseng from within the zones that the government accepts as qualified is made into red ginseng, which is sold. This red ginseng is the so-called Koryŏ Insam (高麗人蔘, Korean Ginseng) and is extremely expensive. The government has designated Mitsui Bussan Kaisha (三井物産會社) for the exclusive sale of this red ginseng since Taishō 3 (1914). Each year, 40,000 to 50,000 kŭn (斤, unit of weight) are sold to Mitsui Bussan, which pays 2.5 to 3 million wŏn and mainly exports it to China (中國).
Originally, ginseng cultivation requires considerable capital as it takes 6 years to harvest, so it is limited to the large landowner class in Chosŏn, and this cultivation provides no benefit whatsoever to the poor peasants of Chosŏn. Furthermore, when selling it, the government does not use Chosŏn capitalists but instead has mainland capitalists, specifically Mitsui Bussan Kaisha, the core of the Mitsui Zaibatsu (三井財闕), handle it.

**Summary**

This 1931 article by "Pugaksan Hagin" (translated from a piece by Japanese scholar Kubo) critiques the financial system of colonial Chosŏn under Japanese rule. It argues that colonial finance is fundamentally an extension of Japanese imperialist policy, aimed at capitalizing the colony to benefit mainland Japan by expanding markets, securing raw materials, and investing surplus capital. The author points out that while subsidies (supplementary funds) from Japan initially existed, they ultimately serve Japanese capitalist interests, not the Korean populace. Expenditures heavily favor infrastructure like railways and ports, and industrial promotion schemes like the rice production increase plan, all designed to facilitate exploitation. The monopoly system (ginseng, salt, tobacco) is presented as another tool for revenue extraction and favoring Japanese corporations like Mitsui, imposing a heavy indirect tax burden on Koreans. The article concludes that despite increasing financial activity, the burden on the Korean masses (both national and local taxes) continually grows, enriching Japanese capitalists while impoverishing Koreans.

From a historical perspective, the article is highly revealing. It provides a contemporary critical analysis of Japanese colonial economic policy, explicitly linking financial mechanisms like subsidies, infrastructure spending, and monopolies to imperialist goals and capitalist exploitation. The use of specific figures (budget allocations, supplementary fund amounts, railway mileage, tax burden per household) offers concrete data points for historians studying the colonial economy. The mention of the "Plan for Increased Production of Chosŏn Rice" and its connection to Japanese war preparations, the critique of the Mitsui conglomerate's role in the ginseng monopoly, and the observation that colonial unrest (Manse Incident) led to increased spending (interpreted as control measures) are particularly interesting insights into the dynamics and justifications of colonial rule and the burdens it imposed. The editor's note explicitly framing the article as a means to encourage Korean research and discussion on these issues is also significant.

帝國主義政策의 進出
식민지 재정이라하면 그 근본적 특질에 잇서서는 중앙재정과 하등 다른 것이 업다. 그것은 어듸까지든지 XX主義의 발전때문에 되여가는 것에 틀림이 업다. 단지 식민지 그것이 日本의 XX主義 政策의 XX이며 그 XX임으로 그 재정에 잇서서는 가장 노골스러운 자태를 가지고 XX主義 日本의 재정의 기조가 노출되여 잇는 것이다. 즉
1, XX主義 日本의 상품판로의 확장
2, 그 원료자원의 획득
3, 다시 또 日本內地에 축적한 자본의 투자
등을 목적하고 日本이 이것을 어든 것은 더 말할 것까지도 업다. 그야 臺灣과 如함은 최초로부터 이와가튼 명료한 의도하에 된 것은 아니고 또한 당시의 日本 자본주의 발전도 거기까지 발전하여 잇지 아녓다. 그러나 領有後의 台灣이 이러한 의도하에 통치되여 온 것은 물론이다. 이와가튼 정책을 일층 단순하게 말한다면 식민지의 자본주의화 정책이라고 하여도 과언이 아닐 것이다. 日本內地商品의 판로의 확장도 원료자원의 개발도 축적자본의 투자도 모다 식민지를 자본주의화시키는데서 목적을 달하는 것이다.
목적은 식민지의 자본주의화 - 그래서 식민지 재정의 기조를 一言으로 말하건대 식민지 자본주의화를 목표로 함이라하여도 가하다. 이와가튼 경우에 內地 자본이 유리하게 취급되는 것은 당연할 것이다. 거기에서 식민지 재정의 세출은 자본가적 기업경영에 비상한 노력을 한다. 또 원료자원의 개발이라는 점에서 盛히 거대한 토목사업을 기한다. 이 점 등을 설명하기 위함이 본문의 목적이다.

중앙재정으로부터 오는 보충금 - 식민지 재정과 중앙 재정과의 관계에 잇서서 몬저 주의할 것은 식민지 재정에 대한 보충금의 문제이다. 보충금이라함은 식민지 재정이 아모리 하여도 독립하여서 그 歲出을 다 마련못할 때에 중앙정부의 국고금으로부터 此에 보조하는 경우를 말함이다. 즉

이다. 현재 중앙정부은 臺灣이외의 식민지 재정에 대하야 약 2천6백만원의 보충금을 與하고 잇다. 보충금의 額은 식민지가 그 자본주의적 발전을 진전시킴에 딸아서 재정적으로도 독립함에 의하야 감소할 것은 당연하다. 식민지 중 이 의미에 잇서서 성공한 것은 아직 台灣 식민지뿐이다. 台灣은 明治 29년부터 明治 37년까지에 합계 3천4십만8천6백9십1원의 보충금을 어덧스나 기후 전혀 독립하여 그 歲出을 마칠뿐더러 이제는 日本內地財政에 種種의 의미에 잇서서 다소의 공헌을 할 수 잇게까지＜32＞ 되엿다. 그런데 기타의 식민지는 그 보충금을 중지하기는 고사하고 도로혀 증가가 되고잇다. 즉 朝鮮은 明治 44년 합병 당초의 보충금 천2백3십5만원에서 출발하야 大正 7년도에는 3백만원에 감하고 大正 8년도에는 일시 중지하기까지 이르럿스나 大正 8년에 이르러 萬歲事件의 발생을 보고 이 때문에 大正 9년도부터 보충금을 필요로함에 止하엿다. 또한 關東州나 樺太도 同樣의 추이를 示하고 잇다.
대중의 자각과 보충금의 증가 - 이제 此種 보충금의 추이를 보건대 大正 7, 8년경이 최저금액으로 기후는 모다 도로혀 증가하고 잇다. 이것은 더 말할 것 업시 세계대전 당시의 일본자본주의의 발전이 식민지의 자본주의적 비약을 시키여 재정적으로 식민지가 여유을 어든 사실을 말하는 것이나 더욱 다른 곳에 중대한 의미를 가지고 잇다. 그것은 대전후 세계경제의 불안정과, 소비엣트로서아의, 건설과, 윌손流의 민족자결 등이 촉진한 민족운동의 발생에 대한 日本의 XX策이다. 즉 식민지 재정이 그 식민지 대중을 XX하기 위하야 각종의 시설을 행할 필요에 捉迫되여 그때문에 전후의 일반적 불황에 의한 歲入 증가의 정지에도 불구하고 歲出의 팽창을 하지 아늘 수 업는 결과에 의한 것이 큰 이유이다.

日本內地資本家의 큰 利益
그러면 대체 보충금은 식민지 대중과 여하한 이해를 가지고 잇는가. 이미 논술한 바와가티 식민지의 歲出이 기도하는 바는 철저한 식민지의 자본주의화이다. 그럴진대 이때문에 이익을 밧는 자는 자본가 특히 日本內地의 자본가일 것이라. 결국 보충금의 증가는 다만 內地의 대중의 부담에 의하야 內地 자본가의 식민지 발전이란 결과를 보일 뿐이다. 그래서 內地 자본가가 식민지의 발전에 딸아서 식민지 대중은 자본주의발전의 理法에 의하야 점차 그 被搾取率의 정도를 굿세게 하여간다. 그것은 보충금의 증가에도 불구하고 식민지 대중의 국세, 지방세 부담이 점차로 증가하고 잇는 점으로 보아도 명백한 사실이다.
식민지 재정과 중앙 재정의 관계는 보충금에만 끗치지 안는다. 중앙 재정의 歲出에도 만흔 식민지 개발 식민지의 자본주의 발전에 관한 비용을 포함하고 잇다. 식민지 軍備와 如한, 각종의 산업에 대한 보조비와 如한, 식민지 행정에 관한 제경비와 如한 것이 모다 그러하다. 예컨대 朝鮮 개발에 대하야 정부에서 쓴 비용을 池田(前總督府殖産局長, 現北海道長官) 平井(前總督府學務課長, 現知事) 共著 「朝鮮讀本」에 의하여 보건대

植民地歲出의 諸特徵
類別한 土木事業費 - 식민지의 歲出에 잇서서 특히 식민지의 자본주의화를 촉진시키는 의미에서 尨大한 토목사업의 경영, 勸業費의 支出, 鐵道, 築港 등의 사업 경영비가 다액에 달하고 잇다. 즉 昭和4년도 실행예산을 들고보자.
朝鮮의 鐵道中心主義 - 朝鮮의 歲出은 經常部 합계 1억7천4백3십9만5천원 臨時部6천2백3만6천원 合計2억3천6백4십3만1천원인바 그중 큼직큼직한 경비를 摘出하면＜33＞ 經常費 중 가장 다액의 경비를 계상하고 잇는 것은 鐵道作業費로 5천7백만원을 算하며 그 버금가는 것이 地方廳費 3천백만원, 專賣局의 경비 2천3백만원, 減債基金의 朝鮮負擔金 천9백만원, 遞信費의 1천3백만원 등이 모다 1천만원 이상의 경비다. 專賣局 경비가 어떤 의미를 가지고 잇는가함은 專賣制度의 설명에 말하기로 하자. 鐵道가 식민지개발상 여하한 중대한 의의를 가젓는가함도 명료하다. 昭和 3년11월 현재의 朝鮮官設鐵道延長은 1,588哩에 달하엿는데 병합당시는 근근 768哩이란 수자에 불과하엿다. 또한 이바께 私設鐵道의 開業線 486哩 未開業線 911哩를 算하고 그 보조비도 臨時部에서 413만원을 算하고 잇다. 현재 總督府는 昭和 2년도 以降 12년 계획으로 860哩의 신설, 210哩의 私設鐵道買收을 예산하고 總經費 3억2천만원의 거액인 계획을 하고잇는 터이다. 遞信費 천3백만원은 朝鮮에 잇는 電信, 電話, 郵便事業費인바 이 또한 식민지 자본주의화를 XX하기 위한 경비임은 자명한 일이다.
朝鮮米의 增殖計劃 - 이상과 如히 일반적 자본주의화를 XX하기 위한 사업경영 이외 內地  산업의 원료자원개발의 사업도 또한 비상히 거대한 바가 잇다. 日本이 세계 제2차의 대전에 대한 식량문제의 해결을 기하기 위하여 XX하고 잇는 朝鮮米增殖計劃事業과 如함도 이 類에 속한다하여 가할 것이다. 즉 大正 15년부터 14년 계획으로 35만정보의 토지개량을 행하여 그 공사비에 대하야 6천5백만원을 總督府에서 보조금이라하여 지출한다 함이다. 더구나 이 사업의 농사개량비에 충당하기 위하여 東拓 及 殖産銀行으로 하여금 2억4천만원 이상의 저리자금 융통의 길을 어더노앗다. 이리하여 사업완성된 날에는 米820만석을 증식하자 함이다.
