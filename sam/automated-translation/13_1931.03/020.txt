Film Synopsis

During the Great European War, as the gunfire raged fiercely on the Western Front, large troop units marching towards the battlefield could be seen below the windows of schools in a certain German city.
Inside one such school, the old teacher "K'andŭrek" (칸드렉) is fervently instilling patriotism in the students. Stirred by the clamor of the great march and K'andŭrek's speech, the students' youthful heroism burns red, and they immediately volunteer for the army and depart for the war.
Young men Pol (보울), Alba-t'ŭ (알바-트), K'emmerit (켐메릿), Myu-ra (뮤-라), Ham (함), P'a-da (파-다) are among them.

On the very first night, the unit saw Hinmelsŭt'osŭ (힌멜스토스), who had previously been a postman in this village, standing before them as a strict sergeant major. Thus, after harsh training, they were quickly formed and sent to the battlefield. Their first experience at the transport post behind the front lines was sheer chaos. Pol and the others were shocked seeing their first shrapnel there.
Pol and his comrades try to get food from the veteran soldiers but instead receive ridicule. An adept old soldier, "K'ajinsŭk'i-" (카진스키-), steals a pig and offers it to them as food. They make their debut on the battlefield. It is a mission led by K'ajinsŭk'i- to set up barbed wire. While fierce artillery fire rampages like madmen in the sky.
"K'emmerit" also runs out, having become like a madman.
Thus, they had no choice but to spend many miserable days in the trenches, waiting for the attack of the French army.

In front of the field kitchen wagon, soldiers await distribution. The cook (콕구) has prepared rations for 150<2> men, but with only 80 men now remaining, he makes excuses, not wanting to give them all the food. Pol and others visit "K'emmerit" in the barracks. "Myu-ra-" (뮤-라-) asks for "K'emmerit's" military boots, which instead drives "K'emmerit" into a frenzy. His boots are already ruined, and one of his legs is already gone. And Pol watches with tears as "K'emmerit" dies.
During fierce fighting near a church, "Pol" is wounded in the arm. And as he tries to take cover from artillery fire in a shell hole created by an explosion, he stabs and kills a French soldier who jumps in.

"Pol" and the others bathe in the stream of a canal. Seeing young women passing by on the opposite bank, they feel the presence of the opposite sex after a long time.
Later, "Pol" is wounded in the right [*] part (右*部). And he returns home to the countryside for recuperation.
In his hometown, old men are vigorously discussing tactics. In the classroom, the old teacher "K'andŭrek" is still promoting patriotism as before.
Parting from his sick old mother, "Pol" returns to the battlefield and joins a squad with 16-year-old boy soldiers and the old soldier "Chya-*" (쟈-*). He visits "K'ajinsŭk'i-". That was the last time he saw him off.
"Pol" saw a butterfly on the battlefield. That was when "Pol" himself fell. —(End)—

**Summary**

This article provides a detailed synopsis of the American film "All Quiet on the Western Front," based on Erich Maria Remarque's novel, ahead of its March 1931 screening at the Dansungsa theater in Seoul. It recounts the story of young German students, including Paul Bäumer (Pol), who enthusiastically enlist during World War I after being inspired by their teacher's patriotic speeches. The narrative follows their harrowing experiences: brutal training, the shock of the front lines, the camaraderie forged through hardship (like stealing food with Katczinsky), the trauma of witnessing friends like Kemmerich die, the dehumanizing nature of trench warfare, a brief respite during leave, and Paul's eventual death symbolized by reaching for a butterfly. The article concludes by reflecting on the film's portrayal of the grim reality of war and questioning what lessons humanity should draw from such bloodshed for a better future.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it shows the active consumption and discussion of major Western cultural products, specifically American cinema, in 1931 Seoul. The review highlights the film's anti-war themes, even while recounting the patriotic fervor that led the protagonists to enlist, offering a complex reflection on war, youth, and ideology potentially resonant in a colonized nation under militaristic Japanese rule. The promotion of a film depicting the horrors of war experienced by German soldiers, screened in a colonial capital, offers a glimpse into the cultural currents and possibly subtle critiques permissible or overlooked by censors at the time.

映畵槪要

歐羅巴大戰當時 西部戰線에 銃火가 바야흐로 猛烈할 때에 獨逸 엇던 都市의 學校들 창아래로는 戰場을 향하야 進軍하는 大部隊가 보인다.
그 學校안에서는 老先生「칸드렉」이 學生들에게 愛國主義를 盛히 鼓吹하고 잇다. 大進軍의 雜音과 「칸드렉」의 演說에 學生들의 젊은 英雄主義는 빨가케 불타서 즉시 軍隊에 出願하여 出征한다.
보울, 알바-트, 켐메릿, 뮤-라, 함, 파-다 등 靑年이 그중에 석기엇다.

그 一隊는 첫날밤 已往 이 村落에서 郵便配達夫엿던 힌멜스토스가 嚴格한 曹長이 되어 그네들 압헤 선 것을 보앗다. 그래서 苛酷한 訓練끗헤 速成된 그들은 戰場으로 보내임이 되엇다. 처음 戰線後方의 輸送所 그것은 混難 그것이엇다. 볼等은 거기에서 最初의 彈片을 보고 놀랫다.
볼 等은 老兵士들에게서 食物을 구하다가 도로혀 嘲笑를 밧는다. 古參兵의 敏腕者 「카진스키-」는 도야지를 도적하야다가 그들의 압헤 食物로 提供한다. 그들은 처음으로 戰場에 登場한다. 그것은 카진스키-에 引率된 鐵條網치는 任務엿다. 天空에는 激烈한 砲火가 狂人과 가치 날뛰고 잇는 속을.
「켄메릿」도 또한 狂人이 되여 뛰어나왓다.
이러케 慘憺한 여러 날을 塹塚속에 보내면서 佛蘭西軍隊의 來襲을 기다리지 안으면 아니 되엿다.

糧秣車압헤서 兵士들은 分配를 기다리고 잇다. 콕구는 150＜2＞인분을 用意하고 잇스면서 지금 겨우 80명으로 줄어든 그들에게 食料를 모다 주지 안고 핑게하고 잇다. 볼들은 「켄메릿」을 宿舍에 訪問한다. 「뮤-라-」는 「켄메릿히」에 軍靴를 所望하다가 도로혀 「켄메릿히」을 狂亂식힌다. 그들의 구두는 벌서 破損되고 그의 한쪽 다리는 벌서 업서 젓슴으로. 그리고 볼은 「켄메릿히」의 죽어가는 姿態를 눈물로써 바라보고 잇다.
敎會附近의 激戰에서 「볼」은 팔에 負傷을 입는다. 그리고 爆破때문에 생긴 땅구멍의 砲火를 피하려 할 때 뛰어드는 佛兵을 그 자리에서 찔너 죽엿다.

「보울」들은 運河의 水流에서 몸을 씻는다. 저쪽 江邊을 지나가든 색시들을 보고 오래간만에 異性을 늣긴다.
나종에 「보울」은 右*部에 負傷을 당한다. 그리고 시골로 休養때문에 歸鄕한다.
故鄕에서는 老人들이 戰術論을 盛히 討論하고 잇다. 敎室에서는 老敎師「칸드레그」가 如前히 愛國論을 鼓吹하고 잇다.
病든 老母와 나뉘어 다시 戰場에 나온 「보울」은 16세의 少年兵과 老兵「쟈-*」의 分隊에 드러간다. 「카진스키-」를 訪問한다. 그것은 그를 마즈막 보낸 때엿다.
「보울」은 戰場에서 蝴蝶을 보앗다. 그것은 「보울」 自身이 너머지는 때엿다. ―(끗)―
