Magazine Title: 三千里
Publication Date: 1932년 10월 01일
Article Title: 따리겟다 (Will Take Off / Take It Off)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writings/Jottings)

Oksun: "No matter how much Napoleon (나파륜) boasts throughout the world (세게), even he had no choice but to take off his hat in front of that person."

Ch'aesun: "My goodness, Napoleon took off his hat? Then that person must be incredibly grand. Who on earth is it?"

Oksun: "Hmph, a barber (리발쟁이야)."<71>

**Summary**

This short article presents a simple joke in the form of a dialogue between two characters, Oksun and Ch'aesun. Oksun builds suspense by stating that even the world-famous Napoleon had to respectfully remove his hat for a certain person, leading Ch'aesun to imagine someone of immense importance. The punchline reveals this person is merely a barber, before whom anyone must remove their hat for a haircut. For historians, this piece provides a small window into the popular humor and everyday conversational style of 1930s Korea. The use of older spellings and colloquialisms like '리발쟁이' (barber) is also indicative of the language usage during the colonial period.
