Article Title	扶餘行 (Journey to Puyŏ)
Author	金雪崗
Article Type	시 (Poem)

扶餘行
						金雪崗

반월성 옛 성터에 남은 자취 그 무엇인고
부소산 저문 비는 궁녀들의 눈물인가
낙화암 저 숙아들아 너는 알까 하노라.

낙화암 그늘 아래 사비수 물걸친다
꽃 같은 삼천궁녀 그 어디메 갔는고야
어즈버 우는 새야가 그 넋인가 하노라.

고란사 종소리에 갈가마귀 울고 간다
삼충비 찾아가서 옛일을 묵상하고
천정대 송림 속에서 가슴 치고 우니라.

수북정 청풍이나 구룡평 낙안이나
규암진 배나드리 운치 있고 한가롭다
석조에 고기 낚는 이 날과 동모 어떠리.

백마강 흘러흘러 열두 구비 더도 흘러
이 땅에 물 주어라 새론 생명 부어주라
무너진 옛 성터 위에 새로운 집 짓고자.
―(1931, 舊稿에서)―＜68＞

Journey to Puyŏ
						Kim Sŏl-gang

On the old site of Panwŏlsŏng Fortress, what traces remain?
Is the evening rain on Pusosan Mountain the tears of court ladies?
Oh, roosting crows of Nakhwaam Rock, I wonder if you know.

Beneath the shade of Nakhwaam Rock, the Sabisu River flows by.
The three thousand court ladies, like flowers, where have they gone?
Alas! I wonder if the crying crows are their spirits.

To the sound of Koransa Temple's bell, crows cry and fly away.
Visiting the Samch'ungbi Monument, I silently ponder past events,
And in the pine forest of Ch'ŏnjŏngdae Pavilion, I beat my chest and weep.

The clear breeze at Subukchŏng Pavilion, the descending geese at Kuryongp'yŏng Plain,
The boat crossing at Kyuamjin Ferry – all tasteful and leisurely.
Fisherman casting lines in the evening glow, how about being my companion?

Paengmagang River, flow on, flow on through twelve more bends,
Water this land, breathe new life into it.
Upon the crumbled ruins of the old fortress, I wish to build a new house.
—(From a 1931 old manuscript)—<68>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏl-gang is a lament composed during a visit to Puyŏ, the last capital of the ancient Paekche kingdom. The poet reflects on historical sites like Panwŏlsŏng Fortress, Nakhwaam Rock (associated with the legend of court ladies leaping to their deaths), Koransa Temple, and Ch'ŏnjŏngdae Pavilion, evoking the sorrow of the kingdom's fall. The poem contrasts the tragic past and scenic beauty with a final stanza expressing a strong desire for renewal and rebuilding ("breathe new life into it," "build a new house") on the ruins of the old. From a colonial history perspective, the poem is interesting as it expresses deep sorrow for a lost Korean kingdom and a yearning for national revitalization. While ostensibly about ancient history, the call to "build a new house" on "crumbled ruins" in 1932 could be interpreted by historians as a veiled expression of hope for Korea's future resurgence during a period of Japanese occupation.

半月城 녯城터에 남은자최 그무언고
扶蘇山 저믄비는 宮女들의 눈물인가
落花岩 저宿鴉들아 너는알까 하노라.

落花岩 그늘아래 泗泚水 물걸친다
꼿까튼 三千宮女 그어드매 갓는고야
어즈버 우는塞鴉가 그넉인가 하노라.

皐蘭寺 종소리에 갈가마귀 울고간다
三忠碑 차자가서 예ㅅ일을 黙想하고
天政臺 松林속에서 가슴치고 울니라.

水北亭 淸風이나 九龍坪 落雁이나
窺岩津 배나드리 운치잇고 한가롭다
夕照에 고기낙는이 날과동모 어떠리.

白馬江 흘러흘러 열두구비 더도흘러
이땅에 물주어라 새론생명 부어주라
문허진 옛城터우에 새로운집 짓과저.
―(1931, 舊稿에서)―＜68＞
