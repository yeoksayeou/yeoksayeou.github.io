露西亞詩壇展望
						데미얀, 베드늬(원작), 蘇民(번역)

神의 죽엄
(1932년 4월 7일 露紙 「이즈뻬스지야」 轉載)

조용하라! 조용하라! 神의 죽엄에
未曾有의 神의 죽엄에
잇는 것이 아니다. 그러나 살어잇다.
아래에도 잇고 우에도 잇다.
美麗한 想像속에서
數업는 多量으로
驚怯의 열매와 어두운 空想
지금 그는 最終의 날을 당하엿다.
죽는다 나간다 그들은
나가는 것이 造物主가 아니오 製造品이다.
사람의 열매가 夢魔냐
如斯한 病的은 發散되엿다.
우리들은 空虛의 沼로부터
그 무엇이 自己欺瞞이엇든가
-어둠-
그는 구름과 갓치 갓다-. (下略-원문)＜93＞

The Death of God
(Reprinted from the Russian paper "Izvestia," April 7, 1932)

Be quiet! Be quiet! For the death of God
For the unprecedented (未曾有) death of God
He does not exist. Yet he lives.
He is below and he is above.
In beautiful imagination
In countless quantities
The fruit of fear (驚怯) and dark fantasy (空想)
Now he has met his final (最終) day.
They die, they depart
What departs is not the Creator (造物主) but the manufactured product (製造品).
Is the fruit of man a nightmare (夢魔)?
Such (如斯) sickness has dissipated.
We are from the swamp of emptiness (空虛)
Was that something self-deception (自己欺瞞)?
-Darkness-
He went away like a cloud—. (Remainder omitted - original text) <93>

相反
(1932년 2월 27일 露紙 「뿌라부다」 所載)

즐거운 머리로 滋味잇는 제목에
나는 쓴다. 2월 27일
바로 오후 7시에
多血的으로 모스크바는 산다.
내맘 夕刊신문에는
모스크바 議會報道가 실엿다.
議會집 圓柱大廣間에는
구레나르 회의를 진행한다.
그런데 그의 課業은-
자세한 계획적 指導의 討議이다.
얼마나한 資金과 엇던 尺度로
모스크바 도시
건설의 資金
건설은 切迫하엿다.
할 수 잇슬만한 性質엣것-
住宅문제를 爲始하야
歸結은 地下電車 施設까지!
신문에 소식은 엇더한가?
주택시설의 預算으로
하나... 셋... 다섯... 여섯 零으로
1億 3千萬 留에- ＜94＞
힘잇는 소리로 이 주택建坪에는
거이 30萬 住民이 살 수 잇다.
납부지 안타! 다만 한해에-
납뿌지안타-
우리는 暴風雲갓치 건설한다.
우리의게는 아모 狼狽도 업다.
도리여 그와 반대로
우리가 사는 곳은 아메리까가 아니오
破滅의 도시가 아니다.
치까고의 기록을 보라!
그들의 신문에서-
치까고 도시는 破滅하엿다. 아메리까에 2등 도시인 이 도시의 기관은 모-다 문을 닷친다. 最近日 內에 도시은행의 半은 문을 닷첫스며 그리고 14,000명 敎員과 학교의 7割은 일하지 안는다. 그리고 市自治費에 4千萬弗의 缺乏이 生하엿다고 報道되엿다. (下略-원문)

Contrast
(Published in the Russian paper "Pravda," February 27, 1932)

With a cheerful head, on a savory (滋味) topic
I write. February 27th
Precisely at 7 PM
Sanguinely (多血的), Moscow lives.
In my heart, the evening newspaper
Carried the Moscow Assembly (議會) report.
In the Assembly building's Colonnaded Great Hall (圓柱大廣間)
A plenary session is proceeding.
And its task (課業) is—
A discussion (討議) of detailed, planned guidance.
With how much funding and by what measure (尺度)
The city of Moscow
Funding for construction (建設)
Construction is urgent (切迫).
Things of a nature that can be done—
Beginning with (爲始) the housing problem
Concluding with (歸結) the subway (地下電車) facilities!
What is the news in the paper?
With the budget (預算) for housing facilities
One... three... five... six zeros
At 130 million Rubles (留)— <94>
With a powerful voice, in this housing floor space (建坪)
Nearly 300,000 residents (住民) can live.
Not bad! In just one year—
Not bad—
We build like a storm cloud (暴風雲).
We have no confusion (狼狽).
Rather, the opposite is true
The place where we live is not America
It is not a city of ruin (破滅).
Look at the record of Chicago!
From their newspapers—
The city of Chicago has collapsed. In America's second city, the institutions of this city are all closing their doors. Within recent days, half of the city's banks have closed their doors, and 14,000 teachers (敎員) and 70% of the schools are not working. And it was reported that a shortfall (缺乏) of 40 million dollars has occurred in the city's self-governing expenses (自治費). (Remainder omitted - original text)

볼가江 中流의 建設
昨日 쏘벳트 人民위원회와 黨中央委員會의 決議를 공개하엿다. 그것은 뽈가江 電氣시설에 대한 것이다.

새 使命에 如斯히 當着하야
엇더한 構想을 要하는가!
―뽈가江 中流에 3大 水力電氣의
건설이 切迫한 것을 是認한다-.
벌서 波汶은 이러난다. 제목자체로부터-
「뽈가江 電氣시설에 대하야」 萬歲! (下略-원문) ＜95＞

Construction on the Middle Volga River
Yesterday, the resolution (決議) of the Soviet People's Commissariat (쏘벳트 人民위원회) and the Party Central Committee (黨中央委員會) was made public. It concerns the Volga River (뽈가江) electricity facilities (電氣시설).

Facing a new mission (使命) like this (如斯)
What kind of plan (構想) is needed!
—We recognize (是認) the urgency (切迫) of constructing
three major hydroelectric power (水力電氣) facilities on the Middle Volga River (뽈가江 中流)—.
Already ripples (波汶) are spreading. From the title itself—
"Regarding the Volga River Electricity Facilities" Long live! (萬歲!) (Remainder omitted - original text) <95>

**Summary**

This article presents three poems by Soviet poet Demyan Bedny, translated into Korean in 1932. The first poem, "The Death of God," proclaims the end of religious belief, framing God as a human creation born of fear. The second, "Contrast," juxtaposes the dynamic progress of Moscow, detailing ambitious construction plans (housing, subway) funded by large budgets, with the reported collapse of Chicago during the Great Depression, citing bank closures and teacher unemployment. The third poem celebrates the Soviet plan to build major hydroelectric facilities on the Volga River, portraying it as a vital new mission. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the publication of these poems in a mainstream Korean magazine like *Samch'ŏlli* is significant. It reflects an interest among some Koreans in the Soviet Union's ideology and rapid industrialization as a potential alternative model to Japanese colonial rule and struggling Western capitalism, even if presented through the lens of Soviet propaganda.
