Osaka Performance Report
						From Osaka, Bae Gu-ja

For various reasons, we should not be forgotten by you all.
However, it has already been almost a year since we left home last autumn and came over.
Among our group, there are many who have come to this Japan for the first time.
When we first arrived, we were clumsy with everything from staying at the inn (려관), table manners (식사범절), to calling a maid, so we had a hard time for a while. The types of performances we are doing here can be broadly divided as follows:
"Korean Folk Dances"
"Korean Children's Song Dances"
"New Korean Expressive Dances"
"Western Dances"
and sketches, etc.

After finishing performances in each place, we listen closely to the critiques from various quarters.
And we always pay attention with interest to what kind of impression our performances are giving to the people of this country.
Looking comprehensively at the reviews heard in various places, they are generally as follows. The folk song *Arirang* is said to sound melancholic yet interesting to their ears, and we see it invariably become popular after the performance ends.
Also, the dances performed to the tunes of the children's song *Pakkot Agassi* (박꼿 아가씨 - Gourd Flower Maiden) and *Yangsan-do* (양산도), *Toraji T'aryŏng* (도라지타령 - Bellflower Ballad), and *Chantotori* (잔도토리 - Fine Acorns) and other ballads are said to reveal a new flavor suggestive of Korean sentiment (정조), and are very welcomed among young people who like new things.
The other day, while performing at the Kyōto Shōchiku-za (경도송죽좌), I saw the following review of us in an entertainment article in a newspaper called the *Kyōto Nichinichi* (경도일일): "今松竹にかっつている 朝鮮の 舞踊團の 公演は いろいろな 意味で話題になつている 中にも 朝鮮の 童謠舞踊が 一等吾吾に いろいろなものを 感じさせる―朝鮮らしい 味, リズムが 吾吾の心持を 捉へるのだらう." [Translation: The performance of the Korean dance troupe currently appearing at Shōchiku has become a topic of discussion in various ways. Among these, the Korean children's song dances particularly make us (吾吾) feel various things—perhaps the Korean-like flavor (味), rhythm (リズム) capture our feelings (心持).]
However, people who do not understand modern Korea sometimes look down on us, calling us just a dance troupe from Chosŏn. But those who see the stage even once say, "朝鮮人もバカにならないね." ["Koreans aren't fools, are they."]. Every time I hear such words, my stomach turns, and I feel like packing my bags right away and going back. (To page 93) <66>

(From page 66) However, thinking it over, this journey is the first test of introducing our things abroad through our own efforts, so perhaps we have no choice but to be satisfied solely with the experience gained on this path.
And although we intended to travel more widely abroad on this trip, too much time has already passed in Japan alone.
We will leave it for the next opportunity and plan to return soon to research more diligently the folk songs and children's songs embodying our flavor (맛) and rhythm (리즘). <86>

**Summary**

Writing from Osaka in 1932, Korean dancer Pae Ku-ja recounts her troupe's performance tour in Japan. She details their repertoire, including folk, children's, and modern Korean dances, noting the positive reception, particularly for *Arirang* and children's song dances which Japanese audiences found novel and representative of Korean sentiment. However, she also expresses deep frustration encountering prejudice and condescending remarks from some Japanese who viewed Koreans as inferior, despite being impressed after seeing the performance. Ultimately, she frames the tour as a valuable first attempt at cultural diplomacy, resolving to return to Korea to further develop works based on Korean folk traditions. For historians, the article offers a first-hand account of Korean artists navigating the complexities of cultural exchange and colonial prejudice in Imperial Japan, highlighting both the appreciation for Korean arts and the underlying discrimination faced by Koreans during this period. The specific mention of audience reactions and the quote reflecting Japanese condescension are particularly revealing.

大阪公演記
						大阪서 배구자
여러 가지 의미로 여러분의게 이저버림을 바더서는 아니될 저이들이올시다.
그런데 작년가을 집을 떠나 건너온후 발서 일년이 갓가워옴니다.
저이의 일행가운대는 이 일본에 처음 와보는 이들이 여럿 잇슴니다.
드러온 당시에는 려관에 기*와 식사범절 하녀를 부리는 것까지 모두가 서투러서 얼마동안은 괴로히 지냇슴니다. 이곳에서 공연하는 종목은 대별하면 아래아 갓슴니다.
「조선민요무용」
「조선동요무용」
「조선표현의 신무용」
「서양무용」
외 스켓취 등이 올시다.
저어들은 각지에서 공연을 맛치고는 각 방면의 비평을 엿듯슴니다.
그러고 저이들의 공연이 이 나라 사람들의게 엇더한 감상을 주고 잇는가 하는 것을 자미잇게 생각하야 늘 주이하야 보고 잇슴니다.
각지에서 듯는 평을 총활적으로 보면 다음과 갓슴니다. 민요아리랑은 그 사람들 귀에 멜로듸가 애연스럽고 자미잇게 들린다고 하야 공연을 마치고 나면 반다시 류행이 되는 것을 봄니다.
그러고 동요 박꼿 아가씨와 양산도, 도라지타령과 잔도토리와 타령 등 곡조에 맛추어 추는 춤은 조선의 정조일 뜻한 새맛시 드러난다고 하야 새것을 조와하는 절문 사람들 사이에서는 매우 환영을 함니다.
일전에도 경도송죽좌에서 공연하고 잇슬 때에 경도일일이란 신문연예기사 가운대서 다음과 갓흔 저이의 평을 보앗슴니다. 今松竹にかっつている 朝鮮の 舞踊團の 公演は いろいろな 意味で話題になつている 中にも 朝鮮の 童謠舞踊が 一等吾吾に いろいろなものを 感じさせる―朝鮮らしい 味, リズムが 吾吾の心持を 捉へるのだらう.」
그러나 현대의 조선을 리해하지 못하는 사람들은 조선서 온 무용단이라 하야 업세 여녁이기도 함니다. 그러나 한번 무대를 본 사람은 朝鮮人もバカにならないね. 라고 함니다. 그런 소리를 드를 때마다 비위가 뒤집혀서 곳 짐을 묵지가지고 도라가고 십흔 생각이 남니다. (第93頁에)＜66＞
(第66頁에서) 그러나 도리켜 생각을 하야보면 이번 길은 우리의 것을 저이의 힘으로 해외에 소개하야 보는 쳣시험의 길이오니 이 길에서는 엇는 경험 다만 그것으로 만족할 수 밧게 업슬가 하나이다.
그러고 이번길에 좀더 널리 해외까지 단녀올가 하얏싸오나 임이 일본에서만 너무긴 시일을 지난지가 오래되엿슴니다.
다음 기회로 하고 쉬이도라가서 우리의 맛과 리즘을 담은 민요와 동요에 더욱 힘서 연구하여 보겟습니다.＜86＞
