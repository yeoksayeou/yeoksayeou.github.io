Morning at the Factory
						Hwang Sŏk-u

The morning factory whistle (汽笛) blows like the long trumpet of a battle line. Heaven and earth grow tense—clouds, wind too; above in the sky, the red-faced giant sun has risen, and on the earth below, young laborers (勞人) going from house to house to the workplace, lightly armed (武裝) in work clothes, mobilization! Mobilization! They run out onto the streets. From the West (西方), the sound of hammers (마치소리 함머-소리), the sound of machinery moving, or the shouts of people, the sounds of automobiles, electric trams (電車), horse carriages (馬車) rumbling, rattling—thus begins the earth's solemn march of labor for the day.

All these sounds are the grand orchestral music (管絃樂)—a symphony (綜合樂)—of steel instruments (鋼鐵器樂).

Amidst this orchestral music echoing, the sacred and brave [march] of wealth and culture.<69>

**Summary**

This short prose piece by Hwang Sŏk-u poetically describes the sounds and atmosphere of an early morning in an industrial setting. The factory whistle acts as a military call, mobilizing workers who rush to their posts like soldiers. The combined noises of machinery, vehicles, and human activity are portrayed as a powerful, grand "symphony" or "march of labor," signifying the start of the day's contribution to wealth and culture. From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its romanticized and somewhat militaristic depiction of industrial labor ("mobilization," "armed"). It reflects an embrace of modernity and industrial power, presenting the factory environment not necessarily critically, but as a site of dynamic, almost sacred activity, which historians might contrast with the actual labor conditions of the time.

工場의 아츰
						黃錫禹
공장의 아츰汽笛은 戰陣의 長喇叭가치 분다. 天地는 긴장해진다―구름도 바람도 한울우에는 紅顔의 巨人 태양이 나섯고 따우에는 집집에서 일터로 나가는 젊은 勞人들이 작업복으로 가든이 武裝하고 動員! 動員! 거리로 달녀 나간다. 西方에서 마치소리 함머-소리 기계움직이는 소리 또는 사람들의 외치는 소리 자동차, 電車, 馬車의 박휘ㅅ소리 덜덜 들들들 地上의 한날의 壯嚴한 노동행진곡은 이러케 시작되여 간다.
이 모든 소리는 커다런 鋼鐵器樂의 管絃樂―綜合樂이다.
이 管絃樂이 울니는 가운데서 富와 문화의 聖스러운 용감한.＜69＞
