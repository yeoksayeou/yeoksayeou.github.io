**Body Text Translation**
Literary Scene Gossip

Ch’unwŏn (春園) was in his youthful period of 24 or 25 when he wrote "Mujŏng" (無情) and "Kaech'ŏkcha" (開拓者). Sangsŏp (想涉) wrote "Mansejŏn" (萬歲前) and "Ch'ŏnggaegori P'yobonsil" (靑개고리 標本室) when he was 25 or 26. Tongin's (東仁) "Moksum" (목숨), Pinghŏ's (憑虛) "Chisaenŭn angae" (지새는 안개), Sŏhae's (曙海) "T'alch'ulgi" (脫出記), and Yohan's (요한) "Arŭmdaun saebyŏk" (아름다운 새벽) were all works completed when they were 23 or 24. Judging from this, it seems one shines brightly around 23 or 24, and perhaps intends to shine brightly once again in old age. In any case, it appears masterpieces are not often produced in middle age.

Poet Han Yong-un (韓龍雲) laments the times, crying out, "There are no geniuses! Let a genius appear!" Truly, as Mr. Han says, lately one cannot find a single heart-stirring novel or poem. They are all works like sleep-talking. Without exception – if I say this, literary figures might lament, perhaps, that they are hard-pressed by the difficulties of living (생활難), but even if pressed by difficulties of living, while they might not be able to produce a large quantity, why can they not put forth works of superior quality?
The point is, there are no geniuses. Because there are no geniuses, like this <93> all four sides are utterly silent (四面이 俱寂)!

Mr. Pang In-gŭn (方仁根), pen name Ch’unhae (春海), recently went to P'yŏngyang for a literary lecture tour. A lecture meeting by a literary figure! The literary world is so desolate (簫條) that even hearing this phrase feels like it's been a long time.

Ch’unwŏn's "Hŭk" (흙) in the Tonga Ilbo (東亞일보) has finished, and word has it that following it, a medium-length novel by Mr. Pang In-gŭn will be serialized. Also, following Mr. Yi Ik-sang's (李益相) "Kŭdŭrŭi muri" (그들의 무리) in the Maeil Sinbo (每日申報, 每申), a full-length novel by Mr. Kim Tong-in (金東仁) is set to be serialized. Since the topic came up, investigating the Maeil Sinbo's manuscript fees reveals that novels are paid 3 wŏn per installment, amounting to 90 wŏn per month.

Miss Mo Yun-suk (毛允淑), a graduate of Ewha Womans College English Department (梨專英門科), who once gained great fame as a female poet, had gone to Kando (間島) for a time and held a teaching position at a girls' school, but recently came up to Seoul (上京). Now that she says she will stay in Seoul, it's like a single flower blooming (一朶花發) in the desolate poetry scene.

Investigating the foreign language abilities of literary figures, Mr. Sŏ Hang-sŏk (徐恒錫) is proficient in German, Mr. Yang Chu-dong (梁柱東) is proficient in French, Mr. Kim Ŏk (金億) in Esperanto, and Messrs. Ch’unwŏn (春園), Yohan (요한), Suju (樹州), and Hoewŏl (懷月) would rank first in English.

It was already over 10 years ago. When Shimamura Hōgetsu (島村抱月) and Matsui Sumako (松井順麿子) were performing for the Arts Association (예술협회), Mr. Yŏm Sang-sŏp (廉想涉), who was a boy attending middle school in Tokyo (東京) at the time,<94> still occasionally talks about how he cried intensely after seeing Sumako's (順磨子) Katyusha. <95>

**Summary**

This article from 1932 presents miscellaneous news and commentary on the Korean literary scene (文壇). It notes that many famous writers produced major works in their early twenties and questions the lack of masterpieces in middle age. The piece laments the absence of "genius" and high-quality works, attributing it partly to writers' financial struggles but ultimately concluding a lack of talent is the main issue. It reports on recent activities like Pang In-gŭn's lecture tour, upcoming novel serializations by Pang In-gŭn and Kim Tong-in in major newspapers (Tonga Ilbo, Maeil Sinbo) along with payment details, the return of poet Mo Yun-suk to Seoul, and a brief survey of prominent writers' foreign language skills. It concludes with an anecdote about Yŏm Sang-sŏp being moved by a Japanese play in his youth. For historians, the article offers insights into the perceived state of colonial Korean literature, the economic realities for writers (mentioning specific payment rates), the perceived hierarchy and specialties of prominent authors, and cultural interactions with Japan (Yŏm Sang-sŏp's Tokyo experience). The mention of specific newspapers and writers' movements provides concrete data points for cultural history.

文壇雜話
春園의 「無情」과 「開拓者」를 쓸 때가 24, 5의 청년시대엇다. 想涉의 「萬歲前」이나 「靑개고리 標本室」를 쓴 것도 25, 6 東仁이 「목숨」 憑虛의 「지새는 안개」 曙海의 「脫出記」 요한의 「아름다운 새벽」이 모다 23, 4 때에 된 작품이다. 이로 미루어보면 대개 23, 4에 하번 번적하엇다가 老後에 또 한번 번쩍하려는가. 엇재든 中年에는 傑作이 잘 생기지 안는 모양

詩人 韓龍雲씨는 時世를 慨嘆하되 「天才가 업다. 天才가 나오라」고 부르짓는다. 사실 氏의 말슴과 갓치 近來에는 가슴을 치는 소설 하나, 詩 한 篇 어더 볼 수 업다. 모다 잠고대갓흔 작품들이다. 내남이 업시- 이렷케 말하면 文人들은 생활難에 쪼들여서-라고 慨嘆할는지 모르지만 생활難에 쪼들니면 多量은 내일수야 업겟지만 質노야 웨 優秀한 것을 내어놋치 못하랴.
要는 天才가 업다. 天才가 업서서 이럿케＜93＞ 四面이 俱寂하구나!

春海 方仁根씨는 日前에 平壤으로 文藝講演을 단여왓다. 文人講演會! 엇더케 文壇이 簫條하든지 이말조차 오래간만에 들어본다.

春園의 東亞일보 「흙」은 끗낫는데 듯기는 말에 그 버금에는 方仁根씨의 중편소설이 실니니라하며 每申의 李益相씨 「그들의 무리」 버금에는 金東仁씨의 장편소설이 실니기로 되엿다 한다. 말이나든 김이니 每申의 원고료를 조사하면 소설은 每回에 3원, 월 90원이라고.

女流詩人으로 一代에 盛名을 날니든 梨專英門科 출신의 毛允淑孃은 그동안 間島에 드러가 女학교에 敎鞭을 잡고 잇다가 日前에 上京하엿다. 인제부터 서울에 머무리라니 簫條한 詩壇에 一朶花發.

文人들의 외국어에 대한 능력을 조사하여 보면 徐恒錫씨는 獨逸語에 能하고 梁柱東씨 佛蘭語에 能하고 에쓰페란토엔 金億씨요 英語에는春園, 요한, 樹州, 懷月씨 등이 第一指에 들 것이다.

벌서 10數年 前 일이다. 島村抱月과 松井順麿子가 예술협회의 出演을 할 때에 그때 東京서 중학교에 다니든 소년인 廉想涉씨는＜94＞ 順磨子의 가쥬-샤를 보고 몹시 울엇섯다고 지금도 각금 이약이한다.＜95＞
