Magazine Title: 삼천리 제4권 제10호
Publication Date: 1932년 10월 01일
Article Title: 上京道中 (On the Way to the Capital)
Author: N/A
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

In the seventh month, passing Ssangnyŏng (雙嶺), cicadas chirp—early rice autumn;
Setting sun on the Han River (漢江) road, fragrant grasses—old palace (舊宮) sorrow. <70>

**Summary**

This short Hanja poem describes a journey to Seoul (the capital) in late summer/early autumn. The poet observes the natural scenery—cicadas chirping, early rice indicating autumn, the setting sun over the Han River road—but concludes with a feeling of melancholy inspired by the sight of fragrant grasses around an old palace, symbolizing decay and sorrow. For historians, the poem captures a sense of subdued national sentiment during the colonial period, contrasting the beauty of nature with the sorrow associated with neglected symbols of Korea's past sovereignty (the old palace) upon approaching the colonial capital.
