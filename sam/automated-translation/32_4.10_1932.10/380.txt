기사형태	문예평론 (Literary Criticism)

朝鮮의 流行歌
						李瑞求
Korea! Give birth quickly, even for a moment, to a genius popular song composer!

In the true sense, are there really popular songs (流行歌) in Korea (朝鮮)? If asked, I would answer immediately that there are none. Why is that? Because most are translations of others' songs, or reworkings and alterations of others' melodies—that is why, at the very beginning of writing this piece, I cry out like this.

Still, it cannot be that there are absolutely no original creations. Like grains of white rice mixed in with millet rice,<84> they are scattered here and there, breathing lonely and desolate sighs.

In the blue sky's Milky Way, in a white crescent boat
One cinnamon tree, one rabbit
Without a mast, without a pole
It goes along so well, to the western land

This is the masterpiece of the daytime genius composer Mr. Yun Kŭgyŏng (尹克榮). It is the most widely popularized song. Although composed for children, it became popular even among adults (成人) and in the entertainment districts (花柳界).

The next popular song is the theme song (映畵主題歌) for the movie *Nakhwa Yusu* (落花流水 - Falling Flowers, Flowing Water), with lyrics and music composed by Mr. Kim Yŏnghwan (金永煥) (a different person from the pianist Kim).

The Kangnam moon is bright where my beloved used to play
His face is hidden within the clouds
Standing alone on the hill where forget-me-nots bloom
Shall I stay up alone all night on this water-borne night?

There were quite a few movie theme songs, but apart from "Arirang" (아리랑), which I will discuss shortly, none became as massively (大大的) popular as this "Nakhwa Yusu." Following this were:

*Se Tongmu* (세동무 - Three Friends), *Amno* (暗路 - Dark Road), *P'ungun'a* (風雲兒 - Child of Fortune), *Chŏlmun'iŭi Norae* (절문이의 노래 - Song of the Young One), *Ch'unhŭi* (椿姬 - Camellia Princess/La Dame aux Camélias), *Ongnyŏ* (玉女), *Hon'gae* (昏街 - Dim Street), *Sŭngbang Pigok* (僧房悲曲 - Sad Song of the Monk's Cell)

And so on, there were various others, but perhaps because the melodies were difficult and the words tricky to memorize, they ultimately failed to become widely popular.

The song of the young one is a heartfelt plea
Carrying youth on a flowing dream path
Seeking love that greets at every port
Singing a song of vain farewells

This is the first verse of "Chŏlmun'iŭi Norae" which I wrote. It was the theme song for a film directed by Mr. Kim Yŏnghwan, and the composition borrowed the efforts of Mr. Yi Kwangjun (李光俊), but perhaps because the melody was too refined (高雅), it failed to quickly become popular among the masses. Aside from movie theme songs, popular songs (excluding, of course, those first popular in Japan and then translated or plagiarized in Korea) included the one popular long, long ago:

Having encountered this turbulent world (이 풍진 세상), what is my hope?
Having enjoyed wealth and glory, is hope satisfied?
Sitting quietly under the blue sky and bright moon, thinking deeply
My deeds are so many, my mind is dizzy.

Starting with this song called "I P'ungjin Sesang" (This Turbulent World), followed by songs children sang on the streets for a time whose composers and lyricists are unknown, "Arirang" became powerful in songs, movies, plays, dance (舞踊), and even dance music (땐쓰曲).

The song was first sung in Korea by Miss Kim Yŏnsil (金蓮實).
The movie was starred in and produced by Mr. Na Un'gyu (羅雲奎).
The play was written and directed by Mr. Pak Sŭnghŭi (朴承喜).
The dance was choreographed and presented by Miss Pae Kuja (襄龜子女史 - assumed mistake for 裵龜子).
The dance music version was arranged and recorded by Sieron Record (시에론 레코-드).
The "New Arirang" (新아리랑) was written by myself (筆者) and sung by Miss Yi Aerisŭ (李애리스).

When the fever for the Arirang tune was gradually cooling, famous beauties of the stage like Yi Kyŏngsŏl (李景雪) and Yi Aerisŭ (李애리스) sang the children's song:

Ŏŭŏra tŏŭŏra
Kŏktŭlnami kŏktŭlnami

There was that children's song, and after that, *Changhanmong* (長恨夢 - Dream of Eternal Sorrow), *Sidŭrŭn Pangch'o* (시드른 芳草 - Withered Fragrant Grass), *K'achyusya* (카츄샤 - Katyusha), *P'yobagŭi Norae* (漂泊의 노래 - Song of Wandering), *Tŭiabodo* (듸아보도 - Diabolo?), *Arabiya* (아라비야 - Arabia), *Penŭsŭŭi Norae* (베느스의 노래 - Song of Venus), etc. There was no time to rest from directly translating popular Japanese songs. It was only in recent years that songs worthy of being called popular songs began to be sung in Korea, reaching a peak with "Arirang," which was even exported to Japan. Consequently, it is generally popular in regions where theatre troupes (劇團) have visited.

The moon has risen, the stars have risen too, the jar in the clouds
Smiles brightly, Arirang Arirang
Slanted moonlight, rest at the gate where my love will come, then go.

Next would likely be the folk song (民謠) "Odong Namu" (오동나무 - Paulownia Tree). Although said to have existed before, it became popular anyway around the time Miss Pae Kuja's (裵龜子女史) folk dance was presented. Two verses among them existed previously.

Baby, let's go, don't cry, the sun is setting over Paektu Mountain's ridge.<85>
And another verse was written by myself (筆者) for Miss Pae Kuja. Looking at popular songs in a more refined sense, there is *Kŭriun Kangnam* (그리운 江南 - Longing for Kangnam).

January and February have passed, it's March
When the swallows that went to Kangnam return
Spring will come to this land again
Arirang Arirang Arariyo
Arirang, let's hurry to Kangnam

Lyrics by Mr. Kim Sŏksong (金石松), composed by Mr. An Kiyŏng (安基永). This much might be the best lyrics and composition one could ask for in Korea, but perhaps due to its refinement, it didn't quite match the vulgar tastes of the masses. There is a *Pangnangga* (放浪歌 - Song of Wandering) with lyrics by Mr. Yi Kyusong (李圭松) and arrangement (編曲) by Mr. Kang Yunsŏk (姜潤錫).

A young man's cold blood soaked in tears
With a body sick from despair and sorrow
Endlessly heading towards the northern snows of Pukkuk (북국한설 오라라 - possibly referring to Siberia/northern lands)
Who could know this sorrowful heart of mine?

There is no one who doesn't know this song; it was extremely popular. Miss Yi Kyŏngsŏl (李景雪) sang it best. Although it has now drifted far from the popular trendline, it was frightfully popular for a while. There probably isn't any other song made in Korea that became popular enough for even children on the street to sing. This shows just how few Korean songs there are. As new songs, new popular songs, flood in from Japan every month, we have no time but to sing them as they are or translated. In theatre troupes (劇壇), the songs sung during intermissions are mostly made by taking others' melodies and twisting them this way and that, patching them together somehow. If only an excellent composer were to emerge here, one cannot imagine how vibrant it would be. Currently, among musicians involved with theatre troupes, the only ones who can produce something resembling an arrangement are Chŏn Surin (全壽麟) and Kim Kyosŏng (金敎聲). Sieron (시에혼), Pathé (타 - Pathé is likely), Columbia (콜롬비야) record, press (吹込), and sell (發賣) popular songs every month. The people who record them are mostly actresses from the theatre troupes. Therefore, popular songs in Korea primarily become popular through records. Singing only the dregs that others sang until one's mouth is dry is by no means a pleasant affair. Because Victor (삑타-) and Columbia (콜름비아) focus on actors for recording, there are many songs sung in theatres. However, Sieron Record (시에론) focuses on recording newcomers studying vocal music (聲樂) in Tokyo, so there are many new songs we haven't heard well. The songs sung by Mr. Kim Taegŭn (金大根), Miss Kim Alla (金安羅), Miss Pak Yŏngsin (朴永信), etc., have a flavour of opening a new path for us. Miss Kim Alla's *Mokhaŭi Sŏnyu* (目下의 船遊 - Boat Ride Under the Eyes) and Mr. Kim Taegŭn's "Chŏmŭrŭn Padakka" (저므른 바다까 - Evening Seashore) are thought to have the potential to become popular in the future.

Record companies, in any case, aim for guaranteed sales (販賣率) by first recording already popular songs. Therefore, the records sold in Korea are mostly filled with fake popular songs that crossed over from Japan. When will popular songs overflowing with interest and aesthetic taste (雅趣) in composition and lyrics emerge for us? The thought makes the future seem distant. There are composers who act respectable, but there is no master for popular songs that resonate with the hearts of the masses. Therefore,
Korea! Give birth quickly, even for a moment, to a genius popular song composer.
-End-<86>

**Summary**

The author, Yi Sŏgu, writing in 1932, laments the state of popular music in colonial Korea. He states there are virtually no truly original Korean popular songs, with most being translations or adaptations of Japanese hits. While acknowledging a few original works like Yun Kŭgyŏng's "Blue Sky" and Kim Yŏnghwan's "Nakhwa Yusu," and the massive success of "Arirang" (in song, film, theatre, dance), he emphasizes that the market is flooded with Japanese imports promoted via records and theatre troupes. He criticizes the lack of skilled Korean composers capable of creating original works that resonate with the masses and concludes with an impassioned plea for Korea to produce a "genius" popular song composer. From a historical perspective, the article vividly illustrates the cultural landscape of colonial Korea, highlighting the overwhelming influence of Japanese popular culture, the nascent efforts towards creating indigenous modern cultural forms (like popular songs and film theme music), the role of new media like records, and the underlying nationalist sentiment yearning for cultural self-reliance and originality. Historians might find the specific names of artists, songs, films, and record companies valuable for reconstructing the early popular culture scene, alongside the author's critical perspective on cultural dependency.

이 노래는 누가 모를 사람업시 가장 만히 유행되엇다. 李景雪孃이 가장 잘 불넛섯다. 이제 발서 유행線上에서 멀니 떠러진 편이나 한참동안 무서읍게 유행햇섯다. 조선서 된 노래로 거리에 가는 아해들까지라도 브를만치 유행된 것은 이 以上 더 잇지못할 것이다. 이만큼 朝鮮의 노래는 量이 적다는 것이다. 다달이 새 노래 새 流行歌가 日本서 밀녀드러오면 우리는 그것을 그대로 또는 飜譯해 브르기에 결흘이 업는 것이다. 劇壇에서 幕間에 브르는 노래는 全部 남의 曲調를 갓다놋코 이럿케 저럿케 돌녀 꾸며서 그럭저럭 맨드러놋는 편이 만흐니 여긔에 훌늉한 作曲家만 생겨낫스면 얼마나 生光스러울지 모를 것이다. 지금 劇壇에 관계하는 樂師도 編曲답게 해내는 사람이라고는 全壽麟, 金敎聲 두사람이 잇슬 뿐이다. 시에혼, 타, 콜롬비야에서 每朔 流行歌를 吹込 發賣한다. 그 吹込하는 사람이 대개 劇壇에 잇는 女俳優들이다. 그러함으로 朝鮮의 流行歌는 무엇보다도 레코-드를 통해 유행되게 되는 것이다. 어느 때까지나 남의 곳에서 브르든 찌걱이만 갓다가 입에 침을 말니우고 불느는 것은 결코 유쾌한 일은 못될 것이다. 「삑타-」 「콜름비아」에서는 俳優가 중심이 되야 吹込하는 故로 극장에서 브르는 노래가 만타. 그러나 「시에론」 레코-드에서는 東京에서 聲樂공부를 하는 新人을 중심 吹込함으로 괘들이 잘듯지 못하든 새 노래가 만타. 金大根씨, 金安羅, 朴永信孃 等이 브르는 노래는 우리에게 새길을 여려주는 맛이 잇다. 金安羅孃의 目下의 船遊와 金大根씨의 「저므른 바다까」라는 노래는 將次 유행할 가능성이 잇다고 생각하고 잇다.

레코-드 회사에서는 엇쨋든 몬저 유행된 노래를 吹込하야 販賣率의 정확을 期하고 잇다. 그러함으로 朝鮮에서 팔니는 레코-드에는 日本서 건너온 가짜 流行歌가 대부분을 차지하는 터이다. 어느 때이나 우리에게도 作曲 作詞에 흥미와 雅趣가 넘치는 流行歌가 나아올넌지 생각하면 창창하다. 점자는 체하는 作曲家는 게시나 대중의 마음을 울녀주는 流行歌에는 임자가 업다. 그러함으로
朝鮮아! 너는 一時라도 速히 天才잇는 유행作曲家를 나어라.
