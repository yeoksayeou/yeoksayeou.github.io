My Shanghai Era, Autobiography Part 2
						Lyuh Woon-hyung

Before heading to Shanghai like this, I had set foot on Chinese soil once before. That was the previous year, 1913. At that time, my thought was to decide whether Manchuria or Southern China would be better if I were to go to China. After visiting, I reached this conclusion: Regarding the Sŏkando region, the land is vast and borders Russia on one side, making it suitable as a stage for initiating some great undertaking, but transportation is extremely inconvenient. Therefore, considering the global situation, I concluded that for someone interested in the future state of affairs, places like Shanghai and Nanjing, where culture is advanced, humanities are developed, and transportation is convenient, making them most valuable as a base of operations (策源地), would be best.

Thus, while my younger brother went to America, I packed my bags and headed straight for Nanjing. However, I didn't know the languages. I thought, "First, I need to learn English and Chinese..." After much consideration, I entered Jinling University in Nanjing, run by Americans. I was 28 years old at the time. The general age of students at Jinling University was mostly between 20 and 22 or 23. Originally, it was quite awkward for an older student like me to be among the younger students, and I didn't fit in well, but I managed to get along with them through sports. The sports were primarily track and field and baseball. Since my time in Korea<20>, I had always paid close attention (着念) to sports, so I was confident in various competitions. Therefore, I joined the representative teams and competed with the young students. Thinking back to that time, it was truly delightful. Meanwhile, I also endeavored to absorb (吸取) the thoughts and feelings of China's newly emerging youth.

The three years I spent attending Jinling University, ages 28, 29, and 30, were the most pleasant period. While mingling with the youth of this country like this, I studied desperately day and night.
Consequently, I became able to speak English and Chinese, which I had regarded as so difficult, freely.

However, even after three years, I couldn't receive a graduation certificate. The academic system at Nanjing Jinling University required completing all the prescribed courses of the school to receive a graduation certificate. Even subjects like theology had to be completed. But my goal at that time was not to get a graduation certificate, so I simply enrolled in the English department and focused my efforts on studying English literature and philosophy.

As for tuition, I had left home with a little money, so I entered the university dormitory and spent the first and second years without much hardship. However, in the third year, I ran out of funds and inevitably had to borrow (貸用) money from the school to finish my studies. I paid back this borrowed money soon after coming to Shanghai and finding employment.

By the way, there is one unforgettable incident from my time at Jinling University. In 1915, the Twenty-One Demands treaty was concluded between the Ōkuma Cabinet and Yuan Shikai. It was during the World War, and in the East, Japan and Germany were exchanging fire (兵火를 밧귀고 잇섯다) in Qingdao. At this time, Jinling University lit the first beacon fire (봉화) against this treaty XX. This instantly spread nationwide. (Omitted from original text) At that time, the Jinling University students all prepared, saying they would go out as volunteer soldiers if XX and war broke out. I also resolved to become a volunteer soldier together with my fellow students. That period was quite turbulent, but I will mostly conclude it here.

After finishing Jinling University, I immediately came to Shanghai. At that time, there was an institution in Shanghai run by Americans called the Union Bookstore (協和書局), and I entered employment there for my livelihood.
This Union Bookstore (協和書局) was a kind of mediation agency (周旋 기관) that negotiated with American steamship companies and relevant authorities for people intending to go to America without travel permits or those planning to cross to America through picture marriages. There were hundreds of applicants each year, so the work was extremely busy.

**Summary**

In this second part of his autobiography concerning his time in China, Lyuh Woon-hyung recounts his decision-making process in 1913, choosing southern China (Nanjing, Shanghai) over Manchuria as a base due to better infrastructure and cultural development, despite Manchuria's potential for large-scale actions. He details his experiences entering Jinling University in Nanjing at age 28 (1914), learning English and Chinese, connecting with younger Chinese students through sports, and witnessing nascent Chinese nationalism during student protests against Japan's Twenty-One Demands in 1915, where he resolved to join a volunteer corps. Lacking funds, he borrowed money to finish his studies (without graduating) before moving to Shanghai and working at the American-run Union Bookstore, an agency helping Chinese emigrate to the US.

This excerpt is interesting for historians as it shows Lyuh's early strategic thinking regarding the best location for overseas Korean activities, favoring developed areas for networking and influence over remote regions. It also provides a first-hand account of his immersion in Chinese university life and early exposure to, and participation alongside, Chinese anti-Japanese sentiment, formative experiences for his later political career involving Sino-Korean cooperation. His practical approach to education (language acquisition over diplomas) and early employment reveal his resourcefulness.

나의 上海時代, 自叙傳 第二
						呂運亨
나는 이와 가치 上海로 향하기전에 中國 땅을 한번 발버본 일이 잇섯다. 그것은 바로 그 前年인 1913년이라 그 때의 생각으로는 중국으로 간다면 滿洲가 조흘가 南支那가 조흘가함을 결정하기 위함이엇다. 가 본 결과 나는 이러한 결론을 어덧다. 西間島 일대로 말하면 토지가 광막하고 한편으로 露西亞를 끼고 잇서서 무슨 큰 일이든지 이르킬 무대로는 조치만은 교통이 심히 불편하다. 그러기에 세계 대세를 따저볼 때에는 장차 시국에 관심을 가진 者일진대 문화가 압서고 인문이 개발되엇고 또 교통이 편하야 策源地로써 가장 갑이 잇는 上海 南京 等地가 조흐리라고 단정하엿든 것이다.
이리하야 내 동생은 亞米利加로 갈 때에 나는 행장을 꾸리어 가지고 一路 南京으로 향하엿든 것이다. 그러치만 나는 어학을 몰은다. 위선 英語와 中國語를 배워야 할터인데...하고 오래 생각한 결과 米國사람이 경영하는 南京 金陵대학에 입학하엿스니 그 때 내 나이 28세엇다. 金陵대학의 일반 학생의 연령은 대개 20세로부터 22, 3에 達 하엿는데 원래 나만흔 학생은 나어린 학생축에 끼어 지내기가 퍽 거북하고 잘 어울니지도 안치만은 나는 운동을 통하야 그네들과 잘 어울니엇다. 운동이라면 주장 육상경기와 야구엇다. 朝鮮에 잇슬 때부터＜20＞ 운동에만은 늘 着念하야왓든 까닭으로 각종 경기에 자신이 잇섯다. 그러기에 나는 대표틈에 드러가서 어린 학생들과 경기를 하엿든 것이다. 그 때 생각을 하면 실로 유쾌하다. 그리는 한편 中國의 새로 자라나는 신진청년들의 사상과 감정을 吸取하기에 힘썻다.
내가 金陵대학을 다니든 28, 29, 30살의 3년간은 가장 유쾌한 시일이엇스니 이러케 이 나라 청년들과 어울너 노는 한편 주야로 필사적으로 공부를 하엿다.
그래서 그러케 난해로 역엿든 英語와 中國語을 자유로 말할 수 잇게 되엿다.
그러치만 3년이 지내도 나는 졸업증서는 못밧엇다. 南京金陵대학의 學制는 그 학교 소정의 모든 학과를 다 마추어야 졸업증을 준다. 심지어 신학가튼 학과도 맞추어야 된다. 그러치만 나는 그 때 생각이 졸업증서 밧는 것이 목적이 아니엇스니까 그저 입학은 영문과에 하여가지고 영문학과 철학을 힘써 공부하엿다.
학비는 내가 집 떠나갈 때에 돈을 조곰 쥐고 나갓기 때문에 대학기숙사에 드러 첫 해와 둘재 해는 과히 고상이 업시 지내다가 제3년에 이르러는 학비가 업서서 부득이 학교에서 학자를 貸用하여가지고 학교를 마첫다. 이 貸用하엿든 돈은 그 뒤 上海에 와서 취직하여서 곳 갑하버렷다.
그런데 金陵대학에 다닐 때 한 가지 이처지지 안는 일이 잇다. 1915년에 大隈內閣과 袁世凱사이에 21개조약체결이 잇섯다. 그 때는 세계대전란중이라 東洋에서도 靑島에서 日獨兩國이 兵火를 밧귀고 잇섯다. 이 때 金陵대학에서는 이 조약 XX의 첫 봉화를 들엇다. 이것이 삽시간에 전국적으로 확대되엇다.(中略-원문) 그 때 金陵대학생들은 XX과 戰端이 이러나면 의용군으로 나가겟다고 모다 준비하엿다. 나도 학우들과 가치 의용군이 되려고 결심하엿다. 그 때 일은 퍽으나 파란이 만햇지만은 대개 여기에서 끈치기로 한다.
金陵대학을 마춘 나는 곳 上海로 왓다. 그 때 上海에 米國사람이 경영하는 協和書局이란 기관이 잇섯는데 나는 생활을 위하야 그 곳의 고용으로 드러갓다.
이 協和書局이라함은 대개 여행권 업시 米國으로 가려는 사람이나 또는 사진결혼으로 米國을 건너가려하는 사람들을 米國汽船회사와 관계당국에 교섭하여주는 일종 周旋 기관이엇는데 매년 수백명식 지원자가 잇서 일이 몹시 분주하엿다.
