Argument for Preparation for the Second World War
						By Sŏ Pyŏngha
Aiming for the division of the world, the imperialist great powers, in the war of 1914-1918, inflicted immense damage of a substantial nature, directly and indirectly, upon hundreds of millions of people across the entire world in an unceasing period, and ultimately made惨惨 (ch'amch'am -惨惨 -惨惨) sacrifices of millions of people in Europe, Asia, Africa, etc., on land and sea, to satisfy their wild ambitions.

The great powers, concerning the surrounding world market network, began an imperialistic struggle for spoils, which eventually brought about the First World War that shook the entire world. Now, 15 years after the end of this Great War, the economic and political crisis of world capitalism has become extremely acute, inevitably bringing the danger of confrontation among imperialist powers for a new market struggle and re-division of the globe ever closer. Due to the intensification of their own contradictions, they are inducing a war of plunder moment by moment. Consequently, each of the great powers, facing the Second Great War, is making a complete leap forward in war preparations with extraordinary activity.

In its nature, compared to the First Great War, the application of technology and chemistry is expanding to an unprecedentedly wide scope. On the one hand, for the millions of troops equipped with the latest weapons, for munitions and wartime provisions of fodder, "national economy" is required to reach extreme tension and adopt a military character based on wartime standards. Furthermore, all industrial locations are being armed, and the imperialist bourgeoisie of each country is pouring all its efforts, by all means, into the total tension of a militarized "national economy." For example, as tangible proof of war preparations, a key point of post-war French law is listed below:

One. Preparations must be made for a long-term war.
Two. War involves all aspects of "national activity," including military, political, and economic aspects.<41>
Three. Preparation and use of domestic materials [missing text] will be carried out in two phases.
   a. During [missing text] time, extremely complete technical wartime goods must be manufactured, necessitating the storage for [missing text] equipment, distribution, and [missing text], and expanding production in economic [missing text] sectors that are crucial for war [missing text].
   b. During [missing text] time, weapons, munitions, and all [missing text] wartime goods will operate automatically.
Four. [missing text] temporary "All French Nationals" and the entire national body's [missing text] are obligated to participate in [missing text] national defense in economic and moral aspects [missing text].
Five. Material means necessary for [missing text] war are obtained through the consent of the owner or by requisition.

This clearly indicates the militarization of the entire French national organization, as clear as observing fire (明若觀火).

Furthermore, the United States, starting with the promulgation of the Army General Mobilization Act in 1923, has advanced active methods, establishing a central organization for "National General Mobilization," founding the Army War College, and conducting special research and education regarding this, while diligently conducting national military exercises under the name "National Defense Day." Accordingly, Great Britain, Italy, XX, etc., are also undertaking intense, unified activities with the same pace to further complete various preparations for armed intervention against the common enemy XXX Alliance and attacks among imperialist powers.

Next, as another proof of war preparations, the increase in the military budgets of the great powers can be clearly seen. Even excluding budgets indirectly spent on industry, transportation, etc., and those spent according to the baseline of the aforementioned "national general mobilization" plans, the military budget shows an overwhelming growth rate compared to the "total national budget." This demonstrates truthfully that these imperialists are engaged in fanatically enthusiastic activities for war preparations.

One. Military Budget
Looking at the total military budgets and growth rates of the major imperialist nations as above, compared to $2,001,000,000 in 1923-24, it shows a gradual numerical increase to $2,164,000,000 in '27, and $2,217,000,000 in '28. If the growth rate of 1923-24 is taken as 100, it becomes 107.8 in '27 and 110.3 in '28.
We must bear in mind that this ever-increasing heavy burden is imposed entirely on the working masses.

Two. Armaments
Comparison of Armies by Country
The number of army personnel in the above five imperialist powers shows a gradual increase: from 1,827,000 in 1913 to 1,917,000 in '23,<42> 1,978,000 in '27, and 2,066,000 in '28. However, this does not include 66,000 Gendarmes, 50,000 colonial troops, and 300,000 Fascist militia.
Furthermore, the number of personnel is only for active-duty army soldiers. Each country has shortened the compulsory service period, reducing the general service term to 2 or 3 years, or 1 year and 8 months. Compared to the previous service period of 5 or 4 years, this is a significant reduction in years, but the capacity of the military and the military education and training of the populace have advanced severalfold compared to before the Great War. That is, in the same amount of time as before the war, more than twice the number of people receive military education.
Also, in addition to military training, mass groups such as schools, youth groups, and sports groups are being rapidly militarized.
Moreover, today's military possesses automatic firing weapons several times more effective, accuracy unimaginable by humans, smokeless long-range artillery unprecedented before, tanks, and other advancements in war technology such as airplanes and poison gas, whose scope of action has expanded, making it utterly incomparable to the pre-war era.

Three. Naval Power
Judging by the figures shown above, the general increase in the naval power of the great powers is a confirmed fact. To elaborate more concretely, Great Britain began the construction of 2 battleships, 2 cruisers, 2 torpedo boats, and 2 submarines, completed the construction of 2 new aircraft carriers (each capable of carrying several dozen airplanes), and built 1 minelayer. Japan also built 4 cruisers, 1 aircraft carrier, and numerous torpedo boats and submarines. France manufactured 3 cruisers, 1 aircraft carrier, 7 dispatch vessels, and various types of submarines. Italy built 2 cruisers, 16 torpedo boats, and numerous submarines. Thus, while a fierce shipbuilding competition was conspicuously beginning among these imperialist nations, the 1927 Geneva "Naval Disarmament" conference of Japan, Britain, and the US, which possessed the greatest naval power, ultimately failed to reach an agreement on the issue of naval parity between the US and Britain. Consequently, the US quickly began warship construction, embarking on a five-year plan to build 25 cruisers of 10,000 tons, 5 aircraft carriers of 33,000 tons, and 32 submarines, allocating $725 million to $1 billion for this shipbuilding plan, gradually focusing on preparations in anticipation of a great war.

Four. Number of Land and Sea Planes Built
The expansion of the air forces of the five major imperialist powers, compared to 1918, the final year of the imperialist war, shows a rapid expansion of combat technology. The performance of military aircraft in each country has dramatically changed compared to before: speed increased by 50-60%, the operational range of reconnaissance and fighter aircraft by 30-70%, bombers by 2.5 to 3 times, bomb dropping speed by 3 to 4 times, and machine gun firing rate by 6 to 7 times. To cite an example of current air power capability, first, the German air force (on page 29)<43>(from page 43) dropped 280 tons of bombs on British territory, causing immense damage with 1,413 deaths and 3,408 injuries. The fact that the French air force could also attack London once and drop the same amount of bombs with ease demonstrates the truly terrifying combat means of air power.
Furthermore, the chemical warfare methods and means of air forces pose an even greater danger to large cities, the centers of major industries. The range and volume of the battlefield for developed air forces have generally increased, and for future wars, destructive power and combat techniques utilizing XX, XX are clearly being fully prepared.

Conclusion
Due to the XX policies of imperialism, the struggle for hegemony among the great powers is decisively approaching a future Great War, and they are frantically preparing for war in lockstep. However, these imperialist bourgeoisies superficially hold empty, hypocritical meetings like "Pacifism," "Arms Reduction," "War Abolition" in Washington, London, Geneva, etc., as "annual events." For world peace, they monopolize the "advocacy of peace," seemingly making infinite efforts, shouting themselves hoarse with fervent speeches championing peace. But as the intensification of their own contradictions and the grave danger of confrontation sharpen, their fundamental intentions are not the "Abolition" of war or "world peace." These are, in fact, nothing more than illusions. Regarding their sensitive self-interest issues, "pacifism and abstract propaganda about peace are merely one of the forms used to stupefy the XXX class." These imperialist bourgeoisies can expand their commerce to the maximum by newly XXing the XXX, XX of the entire world on a large scale, and they can achieve satisfaction by carrying out war to fill their pockets (뽀케트). Therefore, the imperialists, under the guise of "pacifism," are preparing for war with ever more cunning means. That is, the XXX, XXX emotions, having experienced the XX nature of the past Great War, are only becoming more agitated.<29>

**Summary**

This 1932 article by Sŏ Pyŏngha argues that despite superficial peace talks and disarmament conferences, the major imperialist powers (specifically mentioning France, the US, Britain, Italy, and Japan, with a note on Germany's past air capability) are inevitably heading towards a Second World War due to inherent capitalist contradictions and the desire for new markets and global re-division. Sŏ details the escalating war preparations, including massive increases in military budgets, army sizes (utilizing shorter conscription periods for faster training), naval expansion (highlighting the US-UK shipbuilding race after the failed Geneva conference), and the development of air forces with vastly improved technological capabilities (speed, range, bombing capacity, chemical warfare potential). He dismisses pacifist rhetoric as a hypocritical facade used by the bourgeoisie to mask their true intentions while imposing the heavy burden of militarization onto the working masses. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is significant as it reflects the growing global tensions and militarism of the early 1930s, a trend that heavily impacted colonized Korea under Japanese rule. It highlights the perceived inevitability of large-scale conflict and the arms race among the powers, including Japan, providing context for the increasing militarization and resource extraction experienced within Korea during this period, framed within a critical, likely left-leaning, anti-imperialist perspective.

또 현재의 군대는 有用性이 數倍强大한 자동發砲器와 인간으로써는 예상할 수 업는 命中確寶, 無煙火出의 未曾有의 長距離砲 탕크, 其他 비행기, 毒瓦斯 등의 작용범위가 확대된 전쟁기술의 발전이 도저히 戰前에 比치 못할 바이다.
三. 海軍力
이 우에 표시된 數字로 보아 列强諸國家의 海軍力의 일반적 增大은 確證的 사실인데 이것을 좀 더 구체적으로 詳述하자면 英國에서 戰鬪艦 2隻과 巡洋艦 2隻 水雷艇 2隻及 잠수함 2隻의 建造를 開始한 것을 비롯하야 新式航空母艦(各 數十臺의 비행기를 搭載할 수 잇는) 2臺의 建造를 맛치고 水雷布設艦 1隻를 建造하엿다. 日本에서도 巡洋艦 4隻 航空母艦 1隻과 多數의 水雷艇及 잠수함을 建造하엿스며 佛蘭西에서는 巡洋艦 3隻, 航空母艦 1隻, 通報艦 7隻及 各種 潛水艇을 조작하엿다. 또 伊太利는 巡洋艦 2隻 水雷艇 16隻及 多數의 잠수함을 建造하엿다. 이럿케 彼等 帝國主義 諸國家간에는 격렬한 製艦競爭이 現著하게 시작되고 잇슬때 1927년 海軍力의 최대 强力를 소유한 日, 英, 米,의 제네바「海軍軍縮」 회의은 彼等 米, 英, 兩國의 海軍力 평등의 문제에 일르러 결국 일치점에 도달치 못한 까닭으로 그 회의는 실패에 돌아가자 米國은 敏速하게 軍艦製造에 直時 着手하야 5個年間 계획으로 一萬噸의 巡洋艦 25隻 33,000噸級 航空母艦 5隻及 잠수함 32隻을 建造할 造艦계획의 실현을 하기 위하야 7억2천5백만弗 乃至 10億弗을 計上하고 점차 大戰을 豫機한 준비에 몰두하고 잇다.

四. 水陸飛行機의 建造數
以上 5大帝國空軍의 확대은 1918년 즉 帝國主義전쟁의 최후 연도에 비교하야 급속도로 전투적 기술의 확대을 보게 되엿다. 各國의 軍用비행기는 從來보다 그 성능이 급격히 변화하야 그 속력이 5割乃至 6割 搜索機及 戰鬪機의 행동범위는 3割乃至 7割 爆擊機는 2倍半乃至 3倍 폭탄 投下속도는 3倍乃至 4倍 機關銃의 발사속도는 6倍乃至 7倍로 增大되엿다. 현재 空軍실력에 대하야 一例를 열거하면 爲先 獨逸共軍이(제29頁에)＜43＞(제43頁에서) 280톤의 폭탄를 英國領土에 투하하되 그 피해로써는 死者 1,413명 부상자3,408명의 거대한 손해를 줄 수 잇는 것과 佛蘭西 空軍이 또 1회에 倫敦으로 공격하되 同量의 폭탄으로 여반장으로 투하할 수 잇다는 것은 과연 전율할 空軍의 전투적 수단이다.
그리고 空軍의 화학적 전투방법과 수단은 大공업의 중심지의 大도시에 대하야 더욱히 일대위험을 주며 발달된 空軍의 전장의 범위와 용적은 일반적으로 증대되여 장래의 戰爭에 잇서서는 파괴력 及 XX, XX으로 발휘할 전투기술이 확연히 완비되고 잇다.

結論
XX적 제국주의의 諸정책에 의하야 彼等 열강제국간에 패권 쟁탈은 결정적으로 가까운 미래의 大전쟁을 압두고 똑갓흔 보조로 전쟁준비에 광분하고 잇다. 그러나 彼等 제국주의 뿌르조아-지는 표면으로 「평화주의」 「군비축소」 「전쟁絶滅」 등 허공한 위선적 회합으로 華盛頓倫敦 제네바 등지에서 「연중행사로 개최하고, 세계평화을 위해서 「평화주장」를 독차지하고 각기 열변적으로 「평화주창」에 목구멍이 터지도록 무한히 노력을 하는 모양이다. 그러나 彼等자체의 諸모순의 격화와 심각한 대립적 위험이 첨예화하야 그들의 기본적 의도가 아인 전쟁의 「絶滅」이며 「세계평화」라는 것은 사실상 한갓 환상에 지나지 못하고 민감한 자기들의 이해문제에 잇서서 『XXX계급을 우둔화하는 諸형식의 하나로써 평화주의 及 평화에 대한 추상적 푸로파칸타를 하는 것이다』 彼等 제국주의 뿌르조아-지는 全세계의 XXX, XX를 새로히 大규모적으로 XX함에 인하야 최대의 商買를 확대식힐 수 잇스며 彼等의 뽀케트를 충만키 위해서는 전쟁를 수행함으로써 만족를 어둘 수 잇는 것이다. 그럼으로써 제국주의자들은 일층 교묘한 수단으로 「평화주의」의 가면 밋테서 전쟁을 준비하고 잇다. 즉 과거의 大戰에서 XX性을 체험한 XXX, XXX的 감정은 격앙되고 잇슬 뿐 이다.＜29＞
