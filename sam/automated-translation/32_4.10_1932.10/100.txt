Article Title: 靑年學徒에 與하는 十戒銘 (Ten Commandments Given to Young Students)
Author: Multiple (吳兢善, 蔡弼近, 玄相允, 金昶濟, 朴熙秉, 朱鍾宜, 金允經, 金萬壽, 張錫倫, 趙東植, 安鍾元, 辛鳳祚, 金美理士, 朴準鎬)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Piece)

Ten Commandments Given to Young Students
Become a Servant Rather Than a Leader of Society
O Kyŏngsŏn, Vice Principal of Severance Union Medical College

1st Commandment: Think even more gratefully of the benevolent efforts of the parents who support my education.
2nd Commandment: Know how to respect elders rich in experience, as I am lacking in experience.
3rd Commandment: Do not forget that while ideals (現想) are necessary, practical implementation (實地履行) is urgent.
4th Commandment: Remember that acquiring abundant common sense is better than clumsy specialized knowledge.
5th Commandment: Re-examine the virtues of ethics and morals traditionally handed down in Chosŏn.
6th Commandment: Work with your brain, not just with your eyes.
7th Commandment: When doing something that does not shame your conscience, do not fear others' ridicule (誹笑).
8th Commandment: Do not wish to become a leader of society, but strive to become a servant (奉仕者) to society.
9th Commandment: Fulfill the duties (職務) entrusted to you with a sense of responsibility, sincerity, and devotion (誠心誠意).
10th Commandment: Do not lose heart (落心) if your endeavors do not quickly succeed.

Do Not Despair, Live in Reality

Ch'ae P'ilgŭn, Dean of P'yŏngyang Sungsil College

1st Commandment: Do not be swayed by emotion, but stand firmly on an intellectual (理知的) foundation.
2nd Commandment: Do not live only in ideals close to fantasy (空想), but live in reality, bringing ideals closer to reality (現實).
3rd Commandment: Do not despair (悲觀) about the future, but exert your utmost effort until you unfold a world of optimism (樂觀).
4th Commandment: Do not seek truth in language and writing, but find truth in practical morality.
5th Commandment: Treat personality, whether others' or your own, not as a means but as an end (Kant).
6th Commandment: Before being loyal to social service, be loyal to self-cultivation (自己修養) (Tolstoy).
7th Commandment: Do not seek others' trust until you can trust yourself.
8th Commandment: While imitating the good things of others, also create new good things.
9th Commandment: Do not deliberately choose suffering, but do not seek comfort (安樂) only for its own sake.
10th Commandment: Even if you fail to obtain wealth (金), wisdom (智), fame (名), or position (位), do not depart from the path of justice (正義).<30>

Be Brave, Avoid Literary Weakness

Hyŏn Sangyun, Principal of Chungang High Common School

1st Commandment: Be true and honest. This is the foundation of character.
2nd Commandment: Be fair in all matters (萬事).
3rd Commandment: Be diligent (勤勉). Laziness (惰怠) is a failing of our people (민족).
4th Commandment: Be brave. Literary weakness (文弱) is also our failing.
5th Commandment: Possess the virtue of harmony (協和). Envy (猜忌) and jealousy (질투) are a great affliction (病痛) of the Chosŏn people.
6th Commandment: Have sincerity (誠意). Loyalty (忠信) and reverence (敬虔) are its methods.
7th Commandment: Have love for society (社會愛). This is currently a major point concerning our public virtue (公德).
8th Commandment: Have high ideals.
9th Commandment: Be frugal (儉素). Thus strive for substance (內實) rather than outward show (華).
10th Commandment: Be active. If you do this, failure is not impossible (不無하나), but success is certain (必有하니). Try things out in every direction (좌우 開試驗하고 해보아라).

Avoid Bad Friends, Be Careful with the Opposite Sex

Kim Ch'angje, Dean of Ewha Girls' High Common School

1st Commandment: Live each day as a lifetime, do not waste even a moment (寸時), and whatever you think is good, do not postpone it until tomorrow but carry it out (斷行) today.
2nd Commandment: Keep relations with the opposite sex (異性) as distant as possible.
3rd Commandment: Avoid alcohol and tobacco (酒草) from the beginning.
4th Commandment: Be extremely careful in choosing friends (交友) and do not associate with bad friends (惡友) even for a moment. Absolutely avoid places where you are easily tempted, such as cafes (카페) and theaters (극장).
5th Commandment: Be careful in reading; read good books (良書) thoroughly (精讀) and avoid reading many miscellaneous books (雜書). Keep away from light literature (軟文學書類) like novels and prose (散文) as much as possible.
Since my youth, I haven't had a particular motto (좌우명). I just strive to carry out whatever I think is good—what satisfies my conscience—and avoid the opposite as much as possible. For decades, my self-cultivation (修養)—my daily routine (日課)—has consisted only of these three: reading the Bible (聖經) every morning, indoor exercises (실내체조), and keeping a diary (日誌).<31>

Display My Individuality

Pak Hŭibyŏng, Principal of Osan High Common School

1st Commandment: Keep your body healthy (健全).
2nd Commandment: Devote yourself (精勵) to foundational studies.
3rd Commandment: Discard the spirit of dependence (依他的) and be independent and self-reliant (自主自立).
4th Commandment: Display "my" individuality (獨自性).
5th Commandment: Overcome all adversities (逆境) with righteousness (義) and courage (勇).
6th Commandment: Transform your surroundings with faith (信), love (愛), and respect (敬).
7th Commandment: Be loyal in all things.
8th Commandment: Sweat. Become a worker (일꾼).
9th Commandment: Firmly uphold your responsibilities (책임) and duties (의무).
10th Commandment: Immerse yourself (自我를 몰입하라) for the sake of society.

Sweat, Persevere

Chu Chong'ŭi, Dean of Posŏng High Common School

1st Commandment: Always strive to know, and sweat at your work.
2nd Commandment: Esteem (崇尙) frugality and restrain (抑止) luxury.
3rd Commandment: Observe time strictly (嚴守) and lead (營爲) a life with rules.
4th Commandment: Keep ideals high but start practice from a low place.
5th Commandment: Discard self-interest (私利) and submit (服從) to the group's public rules (公律).
6th Commandment: Take responsibility yourself to the end (끗까지) for your words and actions (言行).
7th Commandment: Endure (忍耐) hardship (苦行) and do not indulge (耽) in comfort (安逸).
8th Commandment: Train your body to be strong like steel (鋼鐵) and pay attention (着念) to hygiene (衛生).
9th Commandment: Strive to build a versatile character (多工的 인격).
10th Commandment: Respect your own character and at the same time respect the character of others.

Make the Body Strong, Love Your Neighbor

Kim Yun'gyŏng, Principal of Paehwa Girls' High Common School

1st Commandment: Clearly recognize (認識) that the primary purpose of education is "character completion" (인격완성).
2nd Commandment: To achieve this first purpose, cultivate (修養) yourself physically so as not to contradict (背馳) health and longevity (健康長壽).
3rd Commandment: Also, to achieve this first purpose, strive mentally to pursue Truth (眞; intellectual requirement), Goodness (善; requirement of will), and Beauty (美; emotional requirement).
4th Commandment: Clearly recognize that the second purpose of education is "loving your neighbor" (이웃사랑).
5th Commandment: This "neighbor" refers to those society members with whom my relationship is closest, sharing life and death, joy and sorrow (死生休戚), narrowly the nation, broadly humanity (人類). Loving them is loving myself. Therefore, one must cultivate oneself in this regard.
6th Commandment: For this second purpose, first discard the delusion (妄想) that I can exist or be happy apart from my neighbor, and cultivate a spirit of communal unity (공동단결).
7th Commandment: Also, for this second purpose, avoid even the slightest falsehood (虛僞) like snakes and scorpions (蛇蝎) and keep faith (信義).
8th Commandment: Also, for this purpose, one must regard sincere effort and faith (信義) as life itself. One must always be bright/clear.
9th Commandment: Also, for this purpose, one must have courage (용기). Courage is a combination of boldness (膽大함) and perseverance (忍耐).
10th Commandment: Always discern what that "neighbor" requires, do not be lazy in responding (應答) to it, and prepare the skills (技能) or knowledge (지식) to do so.
There would be no limit (限) if I listed more items, but if one clearly recognizes the above two major purposes (二大目的), all else is included therein.

Be Loyal, Be Devoted

Kim Mansu, Principal of Taedong Commercial Vocational School

1st Commandment: Justice (정의)
2nd Commandment: Loyalty (충성)<33>
3rd Commandment: Discipline (규율)
4th Commandment: Devotion (헌신)
5th Commandment: Honesty (정직)
6th Commandment: Diligence and Frugality (근검)
7th Commandment: Demureness/Quiet Virtue (정숙)
8th Commandment: Kindness (친절)
9th Commandment: Faith/Trustworthiness (신의)
10th Commandment: Cleanliness (청결)

Cultivate the Courage of Self-Determination

Chang Sŏngnyun, Dean of Kyŏngsin School

1st Commandment: Be careful not to perform righteousness (義) just to be seen by others (他人).
2nd Commandment: Always act (起居行動) appropriately for the time (時機), place (장소), and status (身分).
3rd Commandment: Never (何時든지) decline the labor (勞) of sweating (流汗).
4th Commandment: Be frugal and simple (검소 質薄).
5th Commandment: Cultivate (養成) individuality (個性) and the courage of self-determination (自決).
6th Commandment: Absolutely trust (信任) and love your own school.
7th Commandment: Write home (上書) to your family at least twice a month.
8th Commandment: Choose your school friends (學友) wisely.
9th Commandment: When you have troubles (煩惱) concerning yourself, consult (相議) with teachers or close friends (知己).
10th Commandment: Always wear your school cap (校帽) when going out; this will make you mindful (삼가게) of yourself.

Remember There Are Storms

Cho Tongsik, Principal of Tongdŏk Girls' High Common School

1st Commandment: Remember that in one's life (人生 一世), there are many disasters (災變) like storms.<34>
2nd Commandment: There is no success without labor (勞) and suffering (苦).
3rd Commandment: There is much construction (건설) for those abundant in self-control (克己) and perseverance (忍耐).
4th Commandment: People with money (金錢) are not necessarily happy.
5th Commandment: The failure of an honest person (정직한 者) will surely be recovered.
6th Commandment: If only one's character (인격) is noble (高尙), any occupation is acceptable (可하니라).
7th Commandment: Protect the spirit more than the body.
8th Commandment: A servant (奉仕者) of a living society (活사회) requires living knowledge (活지식).
9th Commandment: The future of a person without ideals (理想) holds only wandering (방황).
10th Commandment: If you judge right and wrong (是非), good and evil (善惡) too quickly (速), there will be regret (후회).

Discard Luxury, Be Diligent and Frugal

An Chong'wŏn, Principal of Yangjŏng High Common School

1st Commandment: Be truthful, without falsehood (虛僞) and pretense (假飾).
2nd Commandment: Be diligent (근면) in all things (凡事).
3rd Commandment: Discard luxury (奢華) and make diligence and frugality (勤儉) your virtue (美德).
4th Commandment: Make reverence (恭謹) and modesty (遜讓) my good conduct (美行).
5th Commandment: Cultivate (涵養) your character (품성) by being deliberate (鎭重) in action and thorough (周密) in thought (思索).
6th Commandment: For the sake of what is right (올은 일), advance bravely without looking back (前膽後顧치말고).
7th Commandment: Reflect upon yourself (自身에 反求) regarding praise and blame (毁譽), do not resent others (他人을 怨尤치 말어라).
8th Commandment: Do not hope for luck (僥倖), but strive to fulfill the duties (本務) you ought to perform (當行).
9th Commandment: Be severe (심각히) in blaming yourself, and be generous (寬厚) in blaming others.
10th Commandment: Do not harm (害) others to gain benefit (利) for yourself.<35>

Cultivate Strength, Cooperate

Sin Pongjo, Dean of Paejae High Common School

1st Commandment: Cultivate "Strength!" (힘!). Strength is our life. The final solution to all (一切) problems lies only in strength.
2nd Commandment: "Originality!" (獨創!). Imitation cannot develop the life of self-uniqueness. Imitation is subservience (追從) and submission (屈服). Have an original, spontaneous (自發的) spirit.
3rd Commandment: "Service!" (奉仕!). Depart from egoism (이기주의) and individualism (개인本位), and devote yourself to working for society.
4th Commandment: "Effort!" (노력!). Become a person who strives (奮鬪) and makes effort. For youth without effort, ideals and hopes are nothing more than empty dreams.
5th Commandment: "Faith/Trustworthiness!" (信義!). A person who cannot be trusted (미들 수 업는 사람), a person without loyalty (의리가 업는 사람), may survive individually but is not qualified for social life. Keep faith (信義을 직히라).
6th Commandment: "Cooperation!" (협동!). Great power arises where strength is bound and combined (묵거서 합하는 곳). Even small strengths can produce great results. Have a spirit of unity and cooperation (一致협력), of mutual help (공동협조).
7th Commandment: "Ideals!" (理想!). The characteristic of youth is having great ideals. The scale of ideals is the measure that determines a person's future. Cherish far-reaching (遠大) ideals.
8th Commandment: "Spirit/Vigor!" (意氣!). Numerous difficulties (難關) lie across (橫在) the path ahead for our youth. Without superhuman (超人間的) bravery (용맹) and spirit (氣槪), we cannot break through (突破) these difficulties.
9th Commandment: "Responsibility!" (책임!). Take responsibility for the success or failure of all matters, large and small (大小一切), that you undertake. Great tasks cannot be entrusted (막길 수 업다) to irresponsible individuals.
10th Commandment: "Execution Power!" (실행력!). Whatever the task, think carefully about its organization and method, and once decided, execute (단행) its realization (실현) with firm conviction (신념) and responsibility. Youth without execution power, no matter how good their ideals and aspirations (抱負) may be, are no more than sleepwalkers (夢遊病者).

Cultivate a Group Spirit

Kim Mirisa, Principal of Kŭnhwa Girls' School

1st Commandment: Cultivate an attitude of inquiry (연구적 태도).<36>
2nd Commandment: Focus on reality (현실에 着眼하라).
3rd Commandment: Adapt (順應) to the general trend (大勢).
4th Commandment: Do not easily bend your beliefs (信條).
5th Commandment: Cultivate a group spirit (단체정신).
6th Commandment: Do not needlessly (空然히) suspect others.
7th Commandment: Be diligent, sincere, and act (勤實力行).
8th Commandment: Be faithful to your responsibilities.
9th Commandment: Let action precede words (말보담 실행이 압서라).
10th Commandment: Cultivate the perseverance (根氣) to rise up again after falling seven times (七顚八起).

Have a Correct View of Life

Pak Chunho, Principal of Tongsong Commercial School

1st Commandment: Do not forget that the purpose of students (學徒) is to acquire perfect character (완전한 인격).
2nd Commandment: Perfect character is based on self-cultivation (修養), and perfect self-cultivation is based on true religion (종교).
3rd Commandment: True religion gives a correct view of life (인생관) and a correct view of how to conduct oneself in the world (處世觀), thereby elevating all our thoughts, words, and actions (一切思言行爲) towards morality (도덕).
4th Commandment: All knowledge (智識) and all research/study (硏鑽) lacking a correct view of life and conduct are meaningless and worthless (無價値).
5th Commandment: Seek (차즈라) truth... Establish (樹立) the foundation of self-cultivation... Only then will you obtain (엇으리라) a valuable character.
6th Commandment: Obey (순종하라)... Respect and love (敬愛하라)... Disobedience and defiance (불효와 반항) towards parents and superiors (長上) are the root of evil (禍根) that throws family and society into disorder (紊亂).
7th Commandment: Be diligent (근면하라)... Be assiduous (精勵)... Laziness (怠慢) is a poison (毒藥) that causes people to atrophy (萎縮) and become numb (痲醉).
8th Commandment: Exercise self-control (節制하라)... Cherish yourself (自愛하라)... Lewdness and dissipation (淫逸放蕩) are pitfalls (陷穽) that lead the mind and body (心身) to ruin (파멸).
9th Commandment: Be frugal (勤儉하라)... Save (저축하라)... Indolence/greed (貪逸) and waste (낭비) invite (招致) economic ruin.
10th Commandment: Serve (奉仕하라)... Be devoted (獻身하라)... A life without altruistic (他愛的) sacrifice (희생) is empty (空虛).<37>

**Summary**

This 1932 article compiles "Ten Commandments" for young students from fourteen principals and deans of prominent Korean educational institutions (medical, college, high common, commercial, and girls' schools). The advice covers a wide range of themes: personal character development (honesty, diligence, frugality, self-control, courage, perseverance), intellectual pursuits (balancing common sense and specialized knowledge, seeking truth in action), social responsibility (serving society, cooperation, respecting others, avoiding bad influences), and practical life skills (hygiene, time management, responsibility). Several authors emphasize navigating reality, balancing ideals with practicality, and cultivating inner strength and morality, often drawing upon traditional Confucian virtues alongside modern concepts and occasional references to Western thinkers like Kant and Tolstoy.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing of the values emphasized for Korean youth during the Japanese colonial period. The calls for diligence, frugality, strength (contrasted with "literary weakness"), cooperation, and social service could be interpreted through the lens of fostering a capable populace, potentially aligning with colonial modernization goals or subtly promoting national consciousness and self-strengthening amidst occupation. The frequent mentions of "Chosŏn," "our people," societal harmony (countering "envy and jealousy"), and self-reliance (獨自性, 自主自立) suggest an undercurrent of national identity preservation and aspiration, despite the colonial context. The specific advice given by principals of girls' schools (emphasizing virtue, guarding against temptations like cafes/theaters, careful reading choices, neighborly love) also offers insights into gendered expectations and moral concerns of the era.

육체를 튼튼하게, 이웃사랑하라
培花女子高等普通學校長 金允經

第1銘 첫째 「인격완성」이 교육의 목적임을 확실히 認識하여라
第2銘 이 첫째 목적을 일우기 위하여 육체적으로는 健康長壽에 背馳되지 아니하도록 修養하여라
第3銘 또한 이 첫째 목적을 일우기 위하여 정신적으로는 眞(知的 要求) 善(의지의 요구) 美(情的 요구)를 추구하기에 노력하여라
第4銘 둘째 『이웃사랑』이 교육의 목적임을 확실히 認識하여라
第5銘 이 『이웃』이라 함은 나의 가장 관계가 밀접하여 死生休戚을 한가지로 하게 된 이들 사회 적게는 민족 넓게는 人類을 이름이니 그들을 사랑함이 나를 사랑함이라 그러한고로 이에 대한 修養이 잇지안니하면 아니된다.
第6銘 이 둘째 목적을 위하여는 첫째 이옷을 떠난 나만을 잇게 하러거나 행복되게 할 수 잇다는 妄想을 버리고 공동단결의 정신을 기르어야 한다.
第7銘 또 이 둘째 목적을 위하야 털억만치의 虛僞라도 蛇蝎가치 피하고 信義을 지커야 한다.
第8銘 또한 이 목적을 위하여는 성실한 노력과 信義를 목숨으로 알아야 한다. 언제든지 밝히어야 한다.
第9銘 또한 이 목적을 위하야는 용기를 가지어야 한다. 용기라 함은 膽大함과 忍耐를 합친 말이다.

힘을 기르라 협력하라
培材高等普通學校學監 辛鳳祚

第1銘 『힘!』을 기르라. 힘이 곳 우리 생명이다. 一切의 문제의 최후적 해결은 오직 힘에 잇다.
第2銘 『獨創!』 모방은 自我獨特의 생명을 발전식힐 수 업다. 모방은 追從과 屈服이다. 獨創 自發的 정신을 가지라.
第3銘 『奉仕!』 이기주의, 개인本位를 떠나서, 사회를 위하야 몸을 바처 일하라
第4銘 『노력!』 奮鬪 노력하는 사람이 되라. 노력이 업는 청년에게는 理想과 희망은 한갓 헛된 꿈에 지나지 못하나니라.
第5銘 『信義!』 「미들 수 업는 사람」 「의리가 업는 사람」은 개인적 생존은 가능하나 사회적 생활은 할 자격이 업다. 信義을 직히라.
第6銘 『협동!』 힘을 묵거서 합하는 곳에 큰 세력이 생긴다. 적은 힘으로도 큰 결과를 낼 수 잇다. 一致협력하는 공동협조하는 정신을 가지라.
第7銘 『理想!』 청년의 특색은 큰 理想을 갓는 것이다. 理想의 크고 적은 것이 그 인물의 장래을 결정할 척도이다. 遠大한 理想을 품으라
第8銘 『意氣!』 우리 청년의 압길에는 幾多의 難關이 橫在해 잇다. 超人間的 용맹과 氣槪가 업스면 이 難關을 突破하지 못하리라
第9銘 『책임!』 제가 맛흔 大小一切의 일에는 그 成敗에 대하야 책임을 取하라. 無책임한 인물에게는 큰일을 막길 수 업다.
第10銘 『실행력!』 무슨 일이던지 그 조직과 방법을 잘 생각해서 一且 결정하거든 굿은 신념과 책임을 가지고 그 실현을 단행하라. 실행력이 업는 청년은 아모리 조흔 理想과 抱負가 잇슬지라도 그는 夢遊病者에 지나지 안느니라
