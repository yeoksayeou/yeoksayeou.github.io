Article Title: 名文의 香味, 산새 (Fragrance of Fine Writing, Mountain Bird)
Author: 金素月
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Mountain Bird
						Kim Sowŏl (金素月)

Mountain
A mountain bird too, on the alder tree
Above, cries.

Mountain bird, why do you cry? In the deep mountain valley
To cross the pass, is that why you cry?

Snow falls, comes and covers.
Today's journey too is

70 or 80 *ri*.
Turning back, 60 *ri* have already been traveled.

The road ahead,
I long for it.
Shall I speak?
I long for it so

Shall I just go?
But still, once more again

On that mountain too, crows; in the field, crows
On the western mountain, the sun is setting, they say
Chirping.

Front river water, back river water
The flowing water

Quickly follow, let's go together, they say
Flowing, it flows continuously.<71>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sowŏl depicts a lonely traveler on a long, arduous journey through a snowy, mountainous landscape. The crying mountain bird, the falling snow covering the path, the vast distance yet to cover, the setting sun announced by crows, and the relentlessly flowing river all contribute to a sense of weariness, longing, and perhaps gentle urging forward. The poem captures a melancholic mood often found in Sowŏl's work. For historians, the poem offers insight into the common themes of wandering, hardship, and unspoken sorrow that might reflect the emotional landscape of Koreans during the colonial period. The use of older Korean spellings and grammar is also of linguistic interest.
