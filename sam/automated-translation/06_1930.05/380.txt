Editor's Monologue

Running a magazine is not something to be ashamed of as a great undertaking for a man of his generation. Just as the scholars of the South Seas (南洋士人) exert their full effort even to catch a single mouse, I intend to pour my blood and bones into this magazine. When others use money, I will use sincerity, and when others use talent (才操), I will use effort. Therefore, even if 99 out of 100 copies go unsold, and 99 out of 100 pages are deleted, I will hold onto the pen until the very end.

There was a time when I thought that, in our situation, anyone wanting to read a magazine is crazy, anyone wanting to make a magazine is crazy, and anyone asking someone to write for a magazine is also crazy. However, I realized that this was the self-mockery of an escapist. Now, even if someone spits on me instead of writing, I will gladly receive it, and if they slap my left cheek saying the magazine is worthless, I will offer my right cheek as well. Because I believe that my small effort will still be helpful in some corner somewhere—I have already braced myself for suffering.

Finally, I will make one announcement. The June issue will feature Chu Yohan's travelogue of Paekche's ancient capital, Puyŏ (百濟古都扶餘), and a short story by Pinghŏ Hyŏn Chinjŏn (憑虛 玄鎭健). If I say too much more, my commercial nature will show, so I will stop here. (P'ain)<80>

**Summary**

The editor expresses a fierce determination to continue publishing the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* despite acknowledging the immense difficulties and the seeming absurdity of such an endeavor in the current circumstances. He vows to dedicate himself fully, substituting sincerity and effort for money and talent, and is prepared to endure criticism and failure. He concludes by announcing upcoming articles by prominent writers Chu Yohan and Hyŏn Chinjŏn for the next issue. For historians, this piece offers a glimpse into the challenging environment for Korean cultural production under colonial rule, highlighting the resilience, sense of mission, and perhaps desperation felt by intellectuals trying to maintain a Korean voice. The editor's initial self-deprecating view of publishing reflects the harsh realities, while his eventual resolve underscores the importance placed on cultural activities as a form of resistance or self-preservation.

편집자의 독백
잡지경영이란 男兒一代의 快事業됨에 붓그럽지 안타. 나는 南洋士人들이 쥐 한 마리 잡는데도 全心力을 다하드시 이 잡지를 위하야 피와 뼈을 다하려 한다. 남들이 돈으로 할 때에 나는 정성으로써 하고 남들이 才操로 할 때에 나는 노력으로써 하련다. 그래서 百冊中99冊이 아니 팔니고 100頁中 99頁이 삭제된대도 끗까지 붓잡어 나가려 한다.

나는 우리 처지로 잡지를 보자는 놈도 미친 놈이요. 잡지를 만들겟다는 놈도 미친놈이요. 잡지에 글써달나고 부탁하는 놈도 미친 놈이라고 생각한 적이 잇섯다. 그러나 그것은 도피자의 自嘲인 것을 깨달엇다. 이졔는 글써주는 대신에 침을 배앗터준대도 나는 달게 밧겟고 잡지가 보잘 것 업다고 왼뺨을 치면 오른뺨까지 내어밀겟다. 나의 조고마한 노력이 그래도 어느 한모통이 가선 도음이 될 것임을 밋기 때문에-나는 이미 수난을 각오하고 낫다.

마즈막에 豫告 한마듸 하겟다. 6월호에는 朱耀翰씨의 百濟古都扶餘의 기행이 실니겟고 憑虛 玄鎭健씨의 단편이 실니겟다. 그 밧겟 것을 너무 말하면 商人本色이 나서 이만치만 한다.(파인)＜80＞
