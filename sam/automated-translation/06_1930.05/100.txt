**Body Text Translation:**
Our Concern and the Three Laws
						Yi In, Han Kukchong, Kwŏn Sŭngnyŏl

Regarding the Peace Preservation Law
Lawyer Yi In

1. What kind of law is the Peace Preservation Law?
Reporter=Recently, among us, I understand there are frequent instances of receiving unexpected punishments due to not understanding the "law" well or misunderstanding its provisions. Therefore, we recognize the need to provide detailed and easy explanations of all the laws and ordinances already promulgated for everyone to know, and we first ask about the Peace Preservation Law. Article 1 of the Peace Preservation Law states, "Persons who have organized an association with the object of altering the Kokutai (國體)..." etc. What does the "Kokutai" specified there refer to?
Yi=Let me explain "Kokutai." Kokutai refers to the form of the nation's establishment; for instance, countries like the United States, Germany, and France have a democratic republican Kokutai, meaning the people are the rulers of the state while also being the governed. Besides the democratic republican Kokutai, a country like Russia has a Soviet federal structure. However, Japan's Kokutai, as specified in Article 3 of the Constitution, is the rule of the Emperor of an unbroken lineage for ages eternal. That is, there is no one with power above the Emperor, and the general populace is all subordinate to the Emperor. Isn't such a Kokutai called a constitutional monarchy?
Reporter=So, if one were to alter that Kokutai?
Yi=It refers to altering the current constitutional monarchy Kokutai, for example, into a democratic republic like the US, Germany, or France, or changing it into the Russian model.
Reporter=Then, does this law apply only to the alteration of the Kokutai and have no relation to the alteration of the Seita (政體, governmental system)?<27>
Yi="Alteration of the Seita" has a completely different meaning. Isn't the current governmental system of Japan a bicameral system? That is, the legislature that enacts the laws used in the country has two bodies, the House of Peers and the House of Representatives. Movements proposing to change this to a unicameral system or a tricameral system are of no concern as long as their actions are legal. Aren't there various discussions about the bicameral system among Japan's various political parties and scholars currently? However, the Peace Preservation Law cannot impose any punishment for such changes in the governmental system. Besides that, changes in the administrative system, such as movements proposing to eliminate the ministers of state for each ministry and place them under the unified control of the cabinet, are also not problematic.
Reporter=Next, it says, "Persons who have organized an association with the object of denying the private property system, or persons who have joined the association with knowledge of the circumstances, or persons who have committed acts for the purpose of achieving the object of the association..." etc. What is the meaning of denying the private property system?
Yi=Denying the private property system means denying the current "capitalist system and its ideology," but it must be a total denial, not a partial one. That is, one must deny the ownership rights of the people to land and other property entirely to fall under this law.
Reporter=Don't states, not just people, also possess private property?
Yi=They do. They possess railways, postal services like telegraph and telephone, roads, bridges, tobacco, and so on. Therefore, even advocating for prohibiting private ownership of things like land and making it state-owned is not a total denial, so it is not relevant.
Reporter=The Tenant Farmer Creation Plan (自作農創定案) recently advocated by political parties like the Minseitō (民政黨) seems similar to that. Right.
Yi=That's right. Isn't the Tenant Farmer Creation Plan about having the state establish special institutions to lend money so that tenant farmers can also own the cultivated land they work? Also, aren't there discussions, due to uneven land ownership, proposing that for agricultural or social policy reasons, the state should first purchase the people's land for about 5 billion won and then redistribute it per capita for cultivation? This constitutes only a partial denial of land ownership rights, so such discussions are also fine.
Reporter=Besides that, wouldn't things like the nationalization of electricity, currently strongly advocated by people like Abe Isoo (安部磯雄) of the Social Democratic Party (社會民主黨), also fall under this? Right.
Yi=That too. Since electricity, like water, is essential for human life, the argument is that it would be better for the state to manage it directly for the benefit of the people rather than having one or two individuals manage it for profit. This also is not fundamentally and totally denying the private property system, so it is irrelevant. Furthermore, regarding this, when the Peace Preservation Law was revised by emergency Imperial Ordinance on April 29, Showa 3 (1928), the Minister of State at the time specifically declared that it must be a fundamental and total denial, so looking at it from that perspective, the matter should be clear.

2. Relationship between Ideological Research and Execution
Reporter=The provisions state, "Persons who have organized an association with the object of denying the private property system (omitted-original text) or attempted to organize..." etc. Then, if one holds such thoughts but does not organize an association, is it of no consequence?
Yi=Thought is free, so one cannot be punished just for holding such thoughts. One must take a step further to organize an association to execute those thoughts. Therefore, even if one has such an objective, just talking about it alone causes no trouble. For example, even if someone ran out onto Chongno Street and gave a speech about such ideas, they might be caught by another law, perhaps the Security Law, but they would not be caught by the Peace Preservation Law.
Reporter=So, even if one only holds thoughts of denying private property, it's useless, and even if one goes a step further and has the objective, if one hasn't organized or attempted to organize an association on top of that, one wouldn't fall under violation of the Peace Preservation Law. Right.<28>
Yi=That's correct. That point has the same nature as "Decree Violation" (制令違反). Decree Violation also requires the objective of changing the political system, but it's useless unless done collectively by many, and even if done collectively by many, it's of no consequence unless it disturbs public peace and order. It's the same principle. Incitement is a separate issue.
Reporter=However, how does the alteration of Kokutai mentioned in the Peace Preservation Law differ from the alteration of the political system mentioned in Decree Violation?
Yi=They are different, of course. The alteration of the political system in Decree Violation must be a partial denial concerning the ruling power under that Kokutai. For example, if Taiwan (臺潯 - likely intended 臺灣) or Sakhalin (樺太) were to secede from Japanese rule, wouldn't that constitute a regional and partial denial of that ruling power? For instance, even if sunlight is supposed shine everywhere, if there is a room that doesn't receive the sunlight, the sun itself doesn't change, does it? Similarly, the Kokutai remains as it is, but actions related to the slight reduction of Japanese territory are what fall under Decree Violation.
Reporter=In that case, would the current movement to change the so-called advisory organs into decision-making organs also be considered an alteration of the political system?
Yi=Of course. However, traditionally, that has been legal.

3. Other
Reporter=Who was the first person in Korea to be caught under the Peace Preservation Law? Was it figures related to the first Communist Party incident, like Kim Yaksu (金若水) and Kang Talyŏng (姜達永)?
Yi=No. Those people were before the revision of the Peace Preservation Law, so... let's see... I recall that the first person to whom the Peace Preservation Law was applied was probably in the Chŏng Chaedal (鄭在達) case.
Reporter=Approximately how many people have been caught under the Peace Preservation Law to date?
Yi=I haven't investigated in detail, so I don't know for sure, but I believe it's around 3,000 people.

4. Overview of Provisions
From Law No. 46 of Taisho 14 (1925), the Peace Preservation Law
As revised by Emergency Imperial Ordinance on April 29, Showa 3 (1928)
Article 1: Persons who have organized an association with the object of altering the Kokutai, or persons who have engaged in the duties of officers or other leading members of the association, shall be liable to death penalty, or imprisonment or confinement for life or for a definite term of not less than 5 years; persons who have joined the association with knowledge of the circumstances, or persons who have committed acts for the purpose of achieving the object of the association, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term of not less than 2 years.
Persons who have organized an association with the object of denying the private property system, or persons who have joined the association with knowledge of the circumstances, or persons who have committed acts for the purpose of achieving the object of the association, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term of not less than 10 years.
Attempts to commit the offenses mentioned in the preceding two paragraphs shall be punished.
Article 2: Persons who have consulted with others concerning the execution of matters which are the object provided for in paragraph 1 or paragraph 2 of the preceding Article, with such object, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term not exceeding 7 years.
Article 3: Persons who have instigated others to execute matters which are the object provided for in paragraph 1 or paragraph 2 of Article 1, with such object, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term not exceeding 7 years.
Article 4: Persons who have instigated riot, violence or other crimes calculated to cause injury to life, person or property, with the object provided for in paragraph 1 or paragraph 2 of Article 1, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term not exceeding 10 years.
Article 5: Persons who, with the object of causing others to commit any of the offenses mentioned in paragraph 2 of Article 1 or the preceding three Articles, have provided money or goods or any other material advantage, or have offered or promised the same, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term not exceeding 5 years.
Persons who have received such provision with knowledge of the circumstances, or have demanded or promised the same, shall likewise be punished.
Article 6: Persons who have committed any of the offenses mentioned in the preceding five Articles and have surrendered themselves shall have their punishment reduced or remitted.
Article 7: The present Law shall apply to any person who commits an offense under this Law outside the area where this Law is in force.<29>

Regarding Decree Violation (制令違反)
Lawyer Han Kukchong

Difference between Decree Violation and the Crime of Insurrection (內亂罪)
Reporter=How does Decree Violation differ from the Crime of Insurrection?
Han=As stated in the provisions, Decree Violation punishes those who aim to alter the political system, and in doing so, act collectively in large numbers, and in acting collectively, disturb or attempt to disturb public peace and order. The Crime of Insurrection, on the other hand, punishes "persons who commit riot with the object of overthrowing the government, usurping the territory of the State (邦土), or otherwise subverting the Constitution (朝憲)."
Reporter=Then, what is the meaning of altering the political system in Decree Violation?
Han=There are various types of politics, aren't there? Such as autocratic politics, constitutional politics, republican politics, etc. It refers to attempting to change the existing political system. For example, isn't there a Governor-General's administration in Korea? If some activist emerges and tries to abolish it, then a new, different political system would be established, so those engaging in such movements are punished under Decree Violation.
Reporter=According to your words, if one were to alter the Governor-General's administration, wouldn't that already imply the usurpation of state territory (邦土頉竊)? From my perspective, it seems more likely to fall under the Crime of Insurrection rather than Decree Violation.
Han=No. It does not correspond to the Crime of Insurrection. Why is that? Because Korea is a part of Japan's national territory (邦土). Altering the territory of just one part does not constitute the Crime of Insurrection. The reason is, for example, even if lands like Korea or Sakhalin were to disappear, Japan's national territory would only shrink somewhat, not disappear entirely. If shrinking were punished, then conversely, annexation, which increases the territory, would also have to be punished, leading to a contradiction, wouldn't it?
Reporter=Was the case of Son Pyŏnghŭi (孫秉熙) and the other 33 individuals, where the Crime of Insurrection was discussed but ultimately handled under the Security Law, also based on such an interpretation?
Han= That's right. The case involving the 33 individuals at that time was considered neither an overthrow of the government nor a subversion of the Constitution, nor a usurpation of state territory. Instead, Article 7 of the Security Law, which mentions "disquieting language or actions concerning politics..." etc., was applied.
Reporter= Yes, I understand. Then, Decree Violation was created to punish certain types of activists specific to Korea. Right. So, is its emphasis on individuals? Or is it focused on punishing groups or associations (結社)?
Han=The main purpose of this law lies in punishing collective bodies (多數體). That is, unless many people work together (excluding those who incite), one is not caught by this law. Meaning, even if someone goes deep into an uninhabited mountain valley and shouts that Korea must do such-and-such, they won't be caught here.
Reporter=Below that, it says "disturbing public peace and order or..." etc. What does that public peace and order refer to?
Han=Public peace and order (안녕질서) refers to the state of safety that comes into contact with the sight and hearing of the general populace. Therefore, causing disruption <30> to that state of safety is all called "disturbance of public peace (치안의 방해)." Any act that catches the eye or ear of the crowd is forbidden. It means punishing all situations like throwing a stone into water and causing ripples in the previously calm stream.
Reporter=This law seems to punish actions committed even abroad. What do you think?
Han=That's right. Actions committed in China or Russia are also punished, so this has been an issue in legal circles. Originally, a Decree (制令) is something the Governor-General, who is only entrusted with the administration of Korea, has the authority to issue. Since the Governor-General is merely a regional administrator, it would seem correct that the decree should be limited to within the governing area. For example, the Governor of Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道) can issue Provincial Ordinances (道令), but that ordinance should only apply to the residents of Kyŏnggi Province, not to the residents of Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸道) or Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙道). (Omitted-original text)
Reporter=Are there many people in Korea caught for Decree Violation?
Han=Perhaps fewer than under the Security Law or the Peace Preservation Law, but I still believe it amounts to several thousand people.
Reporter=Are there similar laws in other countries, especially in other places similar to Korea?
Han= It seems unlikely that there are legal forces as strict as this. In Korea, besides this law, aren't there just so many others?
Decree No. 7 (制令第七號)
Regarding the Punishment of Crimes Concerning Politics Promulgated Herewith After Obtaining Imperial Sanction Pursuant to Article 1 and Article 2 of Law No. 30 of Meiji 44 (1911)
Article 1: Persons who, with the object of altering the political system, act collectively in large numbers and disturb or attempt to disturb public peace and order, shall be liable to imprisonment or confinement for a term not exceeding 10 years. However, when the provisions of Chapter 2, Part 2 of the Penal Code apply, this Decree shall not apply.
Persons who incite others with the object of committing the acts mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be punished likewise as in the preceding paragraph.
Article 2: If persons who have committed the offenses mentioned in the preceding Article surrender themselves before discovery, their punishment shall be reduced or remitted.
Article 3: This Decree shall also apply to Imperial subjects who commit the offenses mentioned in Article 1 outside the country.

Regarding the Security Law (保安法)
Lawyer Kwŏn Sŭngnyŏl

Legal and Illegal
Reporter=For what meaning or need was the Security Law enacted?
Kwŏn=Judging by the fact that the law was made in July of Kwangmu 11 (1907), one can roughly guess, but in any case, it has a similar nature to Japan's Public Order and Police Law (治安警察法). Wasn't Article 17 of the Public Order and Police Law deemed a bad law and deleted by the Diet the year before last? Anyway, it is said to be a law created to promote tranquility (靜謚) within Korea for political reasons.
Reporter=What does tranquility (靜謚) mean?
Kwŏn=It refers to remaining still and quiet, maintaining the status quo. That is, it's because changes to the system and other things are not welcomed.
Reporter=Article 7 mentions disquieting language or actions concerning politics (政治에 관하야 불온한 언어동작) etc. For example, if someone exposes and denounces the misconduct of a county magistrate (郡守), would that also fall under this?
Kwŏn=That's right. Since the people in Korea do not yet have such rights,<31> criticizing will get one caught. Hasn't there already been such a precedent somewhere in Southern Korea (南朝鮮)? There is often something noteworthy like this: for instance, if a county magistrate's actions are morally reprehensible but acceptable under the law, meaning they do not violate laws or ordinances, and one denounces the magistrate citing these moral issues, one is liable to be caught under this law.
Reporter=Then, what if township residents (面民) denounce the township head (面長) of the township (面), which is a public entity, for mismanagement?
Kwŏn=That should be fine. If township residents do it for the benefit of their own township, that is, for the benefit of a public entity, it should probably be of no consequence. In any case, know that doing anything contrary to what the current laws command is all thoroughly, completely, meticulously, and carefully considered a disturbance of public peace and is liable to fall under this law. Isn't that why the abolition of Article 7 of the Security Law is called for every year at the Korean Bar Association conference?
Reporter=So, if all actions beyond the status quo in politics are dealt with by this law, then speaking of the so-called autonomy movement (自治運動), which is much talked about recently, wouldn't that also likely fall under this law?
Kwŏn=However, the law cannot punish a person's principles (主義). Objectives are also irrelevant; only actions are punished. Therefore, if the actions taken are legal, there would be no reason to be caught.
Reporter=Even if an activist initiates a major movement, going a step further than just the autonomy movement?
Kwŏn=That's right. As long as it is legal, it's fine.
Reporter=If it's legal?
Kwŏn=For example, it would refer to methods such as submitting a petition to the Diet according to the Petition Ordinance (請願令) or making representations (陳情) to the Governor-General, etc.
Reporter=It can be interpreted this way and that way.
Kwŏn=Of course. Since it's a law for the state. Anyway, in Korea, if the Peace Preservation Law is the father, Decree Violation is the elder brother, and the Security Law is the younger brother; these three inseparable laws exist, allowing for great flexibility in application regarding severity, scope, and duration.
Reporter=Is the prohibition of assemblies by the Three Headquarters (三總 - likely refers to a specific organization or group) also under the Security Law?
Kwŏn=That's right. It was done under Article 2.
Security Law (保安法)
Article 1: The Minister of Home Affairs may, when necessary for maintaining public peace and order, order the dissolution of an association.
Article 2: Police officers may, when necessary for maintaining public peace and order, restrict, prohibit, or disperse assemblies, mass movements, or crowds.
Article 3: Police officers may, when deemed necessary in the cases mentioned in the preceding Article 2, prohibit the carrying of weapons, explosives, and other dangerous objects.
Article 4: Police officers may, when recognizing the risk of disturbing public peace and order through the posting or distribution of documents or drawings, public reading, or language, gestures, or other acts in streets or other public places, order their prohibition.
Article 5: The Minister of Home Affairs may, with respect to persons deemed likely to engage in disquieting actions concerning politics, order their removal from their place of residence and prohibit their entry into a specified area for a period specified within one year.
Article 6: Persons who violate orders issued under the preceding five Articles shall be liable to flogging (笞刑) of 40 strokes or more, or imprisonment (禁獄) for a term not exceeding 10 months.
When the objects mentioned in Article 3 belong to the offender, they may be confiscated depending on the circumstances.
Article 7: Persons who engage in disquieting speech or actions concerning politics, incite, instruct, or employ others, or interfere with the actions of others, thereby disturbing public peace, shall be liable to flogging of 50 strokes or more, imprisonment for a term not exceeding 10 months, or penal servitude (懲役) for a term not exceeding 2 years.
Article 8: The statute of limitations for prosecutions under this law shall be six months.
Article 9: Crimes under this law, regardless of status, shall fall under the jurisdiction of the district court or port city court.
Addendum
Article 10: This Ordinance shall come into effect from the date of promulgation.

Assembly Control Ordinance (集會取締令) Reporter=What constitutes an assembly? How many people or more?<32>
Kwŏn=An assembly refers to an indefinite number of people gathering at the same place at the same time. As for how many constitute an indefinite number, it is set at two or more people.
Reporter=When it was enacted in August Meiji 43 (1910), it said "for the time being" (當分間), but doesn't "for the time being" extending to 20 years seem too long?
Kwŏn=That's right. Originally, this was promulgated as a Police Affairs Headquarters Ordinance (警務總監部令) at the time of annexation. It's a measure of a provisional nature that has lasted too long.
Reporter=Sometimes, in the provinces, people are punished for gathering for informal talks (懇談) after a youth association general meeting (大會). How does an assembly differ from an informal talk?
Kwŏn=If it's not discussing political issues informally, it should be fine, but such things depend on the circumstances at the time.
Assembly Control Ordinance (集會取締令) (Meiji 43 [1910] August, Police Ordinance No. 3; Revised September Meiji 43, Police Ordinance No. 8)
For the time being, political assemblies or outdoor mass gatherings are prohibited. However, this limitation does not apply to outdoor sermons or gatherings for physical exercise by school students, etc., which have received permission from the competent police station.
Violators of this Ordinance shall be liable to detention (拘留) or a minor fine (科料).
This Ordinance shall be implemented from the date of issuance.

Publication Law (出版法)
Reporter=Does news about sports meets also fall under the Publication Law?
Kwŏn=Article 10 of the Publication Law states that letters (書簡), correspondence (通信), reports (報告), etc., are not subject to the Publication Law. Therefore, as long as the content is not disquieting (不穩), it can be considered correspondence and should not be relevant.
Reporter=Is printing using a mimeograph (瞻寫版) also considered printing?
Kwŏn=It's the same. Furthermore, Son Pyŏnghŭi (孫秉熙) and his group were also caught under this Publication Law.
Publication Law (隆熙3年 [1909] February, Law No. 6)
Article 1: Printing documents and drawings for the purpose of sale or distribution, regardless of the method, whether by machine or any other means, is called publication. The person who writes, translates, or compiles the document or creates the drawing is the author. The person responsible for sale or distribution is the publisher. The person responsible for printing is the printer.
Article 2: When intending to publish documents or drawings, the author or their heir and the publisher shall jointly affix their seals and apply for permission to the Minister of Home Affairs via the Provincial Governor, attaching the manuscript.
Article 3: When intending to publish official documents or drawings, or transcripts of speeches or lectures by others, or when intending to publish copyrighted works of others, it is necessary to attach the permit from the relevant government office or the consent form from the speaker, lecturer, or copyright holder to the application mentioned in the preceding Article.
Article 4: For documents and drawings published by private schools, companies, or other organizations, the representative of said school, company, or organization and the publisher shall jointly affix their seals and follow the procedures in Article 2. The representative mentioned in the preceding paragraph is considered the author.
Article 5: When documents and drawings are published after obtaining the permission mentioned in Article 2, two bound copies shall be immediately submitted to the (Ministry of Home Affairs).
Article 6: When documents are published by a government office, that office shall send two bound copies to the (Ministry of Home Affairs).
Article 7: Publishers of documents and drawings are limited to those engaged in the business of selling documents and drawings. However, the author or their heir may concurrently serve as the publisher.
Article 8: The publisher and printer of documents and drawings shall state their names, addresses, place of publication, printing office, and the year and month of publication and printing at the end of said documents/drawings. If the printing office has a commonly used business name, that name shall also be stated. If several persons jointly manage publishing or printing, the business representative shall be regarded as the publisher or printer.
Article 9: When reprinting documents and drawings, the author or their heir and the publisher shall jointly affix their seals and report to the Minister of (Home Affairs) via the Provincial Governor, attaching two bound copies. However, if intending to add revisions, expansions, reductions, annotations, appendices, illustrations, etc., the procedures of Article 2 shall be followed.
Article 10: Persons publishing letters, correspondence, reports, company regulations (社則), handbills (引札), advertisements (廣告), programs for various arts (諸藝의 次第書), various types of forms (諸種의 用紙類), and photographs are not required to follow Articles 2, 6, and 7. However, in cases corresponding to Article 11, Items 1 and 3, they shall be dealt with according to this law. (Remainder omitted)<33>

**Summary**

This article from the May 1930 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features interviews with three Korean lawyers—Yi In, Han Kukchong, and Kwŏn Sŭngnyŏl—explaining three major Japanese colonial laws used to control Koreans: the Peace Preservation Law, Decree No. 7, and the Security Law. The lawyers detail the specific targets of each law: altering the Japanese Emperor system (Kokutai) or promoting communism (Peace Preservation Law), seeking political change like Korean autonomy or independence (Decree No. 7), and suppressing any speech or action deemed "disquieting" or critical of the authorities (Security Law). They clarify legal nuances, such as the difference between thought and action, total versus partial denial of private property, and the definition of "public peace and order," while also touching upon related regulations like the Assembly Control Ordinance and the Publication Law.

The article is historically significant as it provides insight into how these repressive colonial laws were presented and interpreted for the Korean public during that period. It clearly shows the legal framework designed to suppress both nationalist and socialist/communist movements by differentiating between fundamental challenges to the Japanese Empire (Kokutai) and demands for changes within the colonial political structure (Seita). Historians would find valuable the lawyers' careful explanations distinguishing legal boundaries (like permitted petitions vs. illegal assemblies), the explicit mention of the laws' extraterritorial reach, the comparison of the three laws as an interconnected system of control ("father, elder brother, younger brother"), and the discussion of specific cases and figures affected by these laws, offering a contemporary perspective on legal control and resistance in colonial Korea.

治安維持法에 대하야
辯護士 李仁

1. 治維法이란 엇든 것인가?
기자=근래에 우리들 사이에 「법」을 잘 이해하지 못하고 또는 그 條文을 오해하는 까닭에 뜻하지 안는 형벌을 밧는 일이 頻頻한 줄 압니다. 그러기에 이제 우리는 이미 발포되어 잇는 온갓 법령에 대하야 누구든지 알아두도록 자세하고 또 쉬운 해석을 들어둘 필요가 잇는 줄을 알고 爲先 치안유지법에 대하야 뭇슴니다. 치안유지법의 제1조에 「國體을 變革함을 목적하고 結社를 조직한 자나...」云云한 것이 잇는 바 거기에 明記된 「國體」라는 것은 엇더한 것을 가르치는 것임니까?
李=그 「國體」에 대하야 설명하지요. 國體라는 것은 그 나라의 성립형태로 가령 美國이나 獨逸 佛蘭西 등은 民主共和國體인데 그 뜻은 인민이 국가의 통치자가 되는 한편 또 被治者가 되어 잇슴니다. 민주공화국체 이외에도 로서가튼 나라는 쏘비엣연방조직이지요. 그러나 일본의 國體는 헌법 제3조에 명시된 바와가치 萬世一系의 천황의 통치로 되어잇슴니다. 즉 천황이상의 권력잡은 이가 업서서 일반인민은 천황에 모다 예속이 되어 잇슴니다. 이러한 국체를 입헌군주체라 하지 안슴니까?
기자=그러니 그 국체를 변혁한다 하면?
李=현재 잇는 입헌군주국체를 가령 美, 獨, 佛 등가치 민주공화제로 변혁한다든지 또는 노서아모양으로 변혁하는 것을 가르치는 것임니다.
기자=그러면 이 법은 국체의 변혁에만 적용되엇지 政體의 변혁에는 관계가 업슴니까?＜27＞
李 「政體의 변혁」이라하면 아조 뜻이 달너지지요. 현재 日本의 政體는 二院制가 아님니까 즉 그 나라에서 쓰는 법을 제정하는 입법부는 貴族院과 衆議院의 두곳이 잇는데 그것을 一院조직으로 곳친다든지 또는 三院조직으로 고치자는 운동가튼 것은 그 행동이 합법적인 이상 아모 관게가 업슴니다. 현재 일본의 여러 정당이나 학자들 사이에 이원제에 대하야 여러 가지 의논이 잇지 안슴니까? 그러치만는 그러한 政體의 변경에 대하여는 치안유지법은 아모 죄도 주지 못함니다. 그밧게 행정제도의 변경가튼 것도 예컨대 各省의 국무대신을 업새고 내각 안에 統轄식힌다 하는 것가튼 운동도 아모 일이 업슴니다.
기자=그다음 「사유재산제도를 부인함을 목적하고 結社를 조직한 자나 情을 알고 결사에 가입한 자 혹은 결사의 목적을 수행하려고 행위한 자는...」云云하엿는데 그 사유재산제도를 부인한다는 뜻은?
李=사유재산제도를 부인한다는 말은 현재의 「자본주의제도와 그 주의」를 부인한다는 말인데 그것도 부분적 부인이 아니고 전체적 부인이 되어야 함니다. 즉 인민의 토지 기타재산의 소유권을 전체적으로 부인하여야 이 법에 걸님니다.
기자=인민뿐 아니라 국가도 사유재산?을 가지고 잇지 안슴니까?
李=가지고 잇지요. 위선 철도라든지 전신전화 등의 우편이라든지 도로, 교량, 煙草가튼 것을 가지고 잇고 말고요. 그러기에 토지가튼 것의 민유를 금하고 국유로 하자하여도 그는 전체적 부인이 아니되니까 관계치 안슴니다.
기자=최근 民政黨가튼 정당에서 부르짓는 自作農創定案가튼 것이 그와 유사하겟슴니다. 그려.
李=그러치요. 自作農創定案이라는 것이 경지를 소작인도 다 가지도록 국가에서 특수기관을 식혀 돈을 빌너 주어 사게 하는 것이 아님이까. 또 토지소유가 고르지 못하야 농업정책상 또는 사회정책상 일단 국가에서 인민의 토지를 약 50억만원으로 매수한 뒤에 다시 그것을 인구별로 논아 갈아 먹도록 하자는 그런 의논도 잇지 안슴니까? 이것은 토지의 소유권만의 부분적 부인이 되니 이런 의론도 괜참슴니다.
기자=그밧게도 社會民主黨의 安部磯雄가튼 사람이 현재 力說하고 잇는 전기의 국영론가튼 것도 그것이겟슴니다그려.
李=그도 그러치요. 전기도 물과가치 인간생활에 필수품인데 그것을 한 두 사람이 영리적으로 경영한다기보다 국가에서 인민을 위하야 직접경영하는 것이 조흘 듯하니까 주창하는 것인데 그 亦 근본적 전체적으로 사유재산제도을 부인하는 것이 아닌즉 관계업는 것임니다. 더욱 이에 대하야는 치안유지법이 昭和3년 4월29일에 긴급勅令으로 개정될 때에 당시의 국무대신이 근본적 전체적 부인이어야 한다고 특히 언명한 바 잇스니깐 그에 비추어 보아도 분명한 일일줄 암이다.

2. 사상의 연구와 실행관계
기자=條文 속에 사유재산제도를 부인함을 목적하고 결사를 조직한 자나(略-원문) 또 조직하려한 자라 云云하엿는데 그러면 그러한 사상을 가지고도 결사만 조직을 아니하면 상관업슴니까?
李=사상은 자유니까 그러한 사상을 가젓다고 벌은 못함니다. 그 사상을 실행하기 위하야 한거름 나가서 결사를 조직하여야하는 것인즉 그러기에 그러한 목적까지 가젓더래도 혼자 떠들어는 또한 아모 탈이 업슴니다. 가령 누가 種路바닥에 뛰어나가 그런 사상에 대한 연설을 하엿다고 하여도 그는 혹 다른 죄 가령 보안법가튼 것에 걸닐는지 모르지만 치안유지법에는 아니 걸님니다.
기자=그러니까 비록 私財否認의 사상만 가젓더래도 소용업고 한거름 더 나아가 목적까지 하엿더래도 그 우에 결사를 조직하며 또는 조직하려한 것이 아니엇스면 治維法違反에는 들지 안켓슴니다그려.＜28＞
李=그럿숨니다. 그 점은 「制令違反」과 가튼 성질을 가젓섯스니 制令違反도 정치의 변혁을 목적하되 多數共同하지 안으면 소용업고 多數共同하되 안녕질서를 방해하지 안으면 상관업는 것과 갓슴니다. 선동은 별문제이나.
기자=그런데 앗가 치안유지법에 말한 國體의 변혁과 制令違反에 말하는 정치의 변혁은 엇더케 다름니까?
李=그야 다르지요. 制令違反의 정치의 변혁이라 함은 그 국체 밋헤서 통치권에 대한 부분적 부인이라야 하겟지요. 가령 臺潯이나 樺太가 日本의 통치권을 이탈한다하면 그는 지역적으로 또 부분적으로 그 통치권을 부인하는 것이 아니되겟슴니까? 예를 들면 태양광선이 어듸든지 다 비칠 것이로되 그 광선을 밧지 안는 어느 방이 잇다고 태양 그 물건이야 변치안는 것이 아님니까? 그 모양으로 國體는 그냥 잇되 다만 日本의 영토가 다소 축소되는 일에 대한 행동이 制令違反에 걸니는 것이외다.
기자=그러타면 현재와 가치 소위 諮詢機關을 決議機關으로 고치자하는 운동도 정치의 변혁이 되겟슴니까?
李=물론이지요. 그러나 종래의 그것은 합법적이엇지요.

4. 條文一覽
大正14년 법률 제46호 治安維持法中
昭和3년 4월 29일 긴급勅令에 의하야 개정한 것

사유재산제도를 부인함을 목적하고 結社를 조직한 자나 情을 알고 결사에 가입한 자 혹은 결사의 목적을 수행하랴고 행위를 한 자는 10년이상의 징역이나 금고에 처함.
前2項의 미수죄는 此를 罰함

제3조 제1조 제1항이나 제2항의 목적으로써 그 목적인 事項의 실행을 선동한 자는 7년이하의 징역이나 금고에 처함.
제4조 제1조 제1항이나 제2항의 목적으로써 소요 폭행 기타 생명 신체나 재산에 해를 가할 범죄를 선동한 자는 10년이하의 징역이나 금고에 처함.
제5조 제1조 제2항 及 前3條의 죄를 범하게 함을 목적하고 금품 기타의 재산상 이익을 제공하거나 그 申込(請願) 혹 약속을 한 자 5년이하 징역이나 금고에 처함

제6조 前5條의 죄를 범한 자가 자수한 내에는 형을 경감 혹은 면제함.
제7조 본법은 何人을 물론하고 본법실행구역 외에셔 죄를 범한 자에게도 또한 此를 적용함.＜29＞

制令違反에 對하야
辯護士 韓國鍾

制令違反과 內亂罪의 差違
기자=制令違反과 內亂罪와는 엇더케 다름니까?
韓= 條文에 잇는 바와가치 制令違反은 정치의 변혁을 목적하고 또 목적하되 다수가 共同하여하고 또 共同하여하되 안녕질서를 방해한 자 또는 하려하는 자를 벌하는 것이고 내란죄란 것은 「정부를 顚覆하고 又는 邦土를 頉竊하고 기타 朝憲을 문란할 것을 목적으로하고서 폭동을 한 자」를 벌하기로 된 것임니다.
기자=그러면 制令違反에 잇는 정치의 변혁이란 뜻은?
韓=정치에는 여러 가지가 잇지 안슴니까? 즉 專制政治 입헌정치 공화정치 등 그런데 현재잇는 그 정치제도를 변경하려 하는 것을 가르치는 것이외다. 가령 예들 들면 조선에 총독정치가 잇지안슴니까? 그런데 가령 엇든 운동자가 나서서 그것을 업새어 버린다하면 그때는 새로운 다른 정치가 설 터이니까 이러한 운동을 하는 자를 제령위반으로 벌하게 되엇슴니다.
기자=말슴대로 가령 총독정치의 변혁을 한다하면 그는 벌서 邦土의 頉竊을 의미하는 것이 되는 터인즉 제 생각갓해서는 制令違反보다도 내란죄에 더 만히 걸닐 듯한데요.

韓=그럿슴넨다. 中國이나 露西亞에 가서 한 일이라도 벌한게 되엇슴으로 이것이 법조계에 문제가 된 적도 잇섯슴니다. 원래 制令이란 것은 조선에 대한 통치의 任만 마튼 총독이 발할 권한을 가진 것으로 이미 총독이 一지방행정관인 이상 그 명령도 통치구역 안에 한할 것이 올흘 줄 압니다. 예를 들면 京畿道知事는 道令을 발할 수 잇스되 그 令은 오직 京畿道民에만 한할 것이요. 忠淸道나 慶尙道道民에게는 잇지 못할 것과 마천가지외다.(略-원문)
기자=조선에서 制令違反예 걸닌 자가 만슴니까?
韓=아마 보안법이나 치안유지법보다는 적겟스나 그래도 수천명이 될 줄을 압니다.
기자=타국 그중에도 조선과 류사한 다른 곳곳예 이와 유사한 법률이 잇슴니까?

보안법
제1조 內部大臣은 안녕질서를 保持하기 위하야 필요한 경우에는 結社의 해산을 命함을 得함.
제2조 경찰관은 안녕질서를 保持하기 위하야 필요한 경우에는 집회나 대중의 운동 혹은 군집을 제한 금지하거나 해산함을 得함
제3조 경찰관은 前 제2조의 경우에 필요로 인정할 시에는 戒器 及 폭발물 기타 위험한 물건의 휴대를 금지함을 得함
제4조 경찰관은 街路 기타 공개의 장소에서 文書 圖畵의 揭示 及 분포, 낭독 또는 언어, 형용 기타의 행위를 하야 안녕질서를 문란하는 우려가 잇다고 인정하는 시에는 그 금지를 命함을 得함
제5조 內部大臣은 정치에 관하야 불온한 동작을 행할 우려가 잇다고 인정하는 자에게 대하야 그 거주장소에서 퇴거를 命하고 또 일개년 이내의 기간을 特定하야 일정한 지역내에 犯入함을 금지함울 得함
제6조 前5조에 의한 명령에 위반한 자는 40이상의 笞刑이나 10개월이하의 禁獄에 처함.
제3조의 물건이 범인의 소유에 관한 時는 情狀에 의하야 此를 몰수함.
제7조 정치에 관하야 불온한 언론동작을 하거나 타인을 煽動 敎晙 혹은 사용하거나 또는 타인의 행동에 관섭함으로 인하야 치안을 방해하는 자는 50이상의 笞刑 10개월이하의 禁獄이나 2개년이하의 징역에 처함.
제8조 본법의 공소시효는 6개월간으로 함.
條9條 본법의 범죄는 신분여하를 불문하고 지방재판소이나 港市재판소의 관할로 함.

출판법(隆熙3년 2월 법률제6호)
제1조 기계나 기타 如何한 방법을 막론하고 發賣나 반포를 목적으로 하는 文書 及 도화를 인쇄함을 출판이라하고 그 문서를 저술하거나 번역, 편찬 혹은 도화를 作爲하는 자는 저작자라 하고 發賣나 반포를 담당하는 자를 발행자라하고 인쇄를 담당하는 자를 인쇄자라 함.
제2조 文書 圖畵를 출판하랴고 하는 時는 저작자나 그 相續者 及 발행자가 連印하고 稿本을 添附하야 地方長官을 經由하야 內部大臣에게 허가를 신청할 것.
제3조 관청의 文書 圖畵 혹은 타인의 연설이나 강의의 필기를 출판하랴고 하는 時 또는 저작권을 가진 타인의 저작물을 출판하랴는 時는 前條의 신청서에 該官廳의 허가서나 연설자, 강의자, 저작권자의 승낙서를 첨부함을 要함.
제4조 사립학교, 사회 기타단체에서 출판하는 文書 圖畵는 該학교, 회사 기타단체를 대표하는자 及 발행자가 연인하야 제2조의 수속을 행할 것 前項의 대표자는 저작자로 간주함.
제5조 제2조의 허가를 得하야 文書 及 圖畵를 출판한 시에는 즉시 제본 2부를 (內部)에 납부할 것.
제6조 관청에서 문서를 출판할 시에는 其관청에서 제본 2부(내부)에 송부할 것.
제7조 文書 圖畵의 발행자는 文書 圖畵를 판매함을 영업삼는 자에 한함. 단 저작자나 그 相續者는 발행자를 겸함을 得함.
제8조 文書 圖畵의 發行者 及 인쇄자는 그 성명, 주소, 발행소, 印刷所 及 발행인쇄의 연월일을 該문서도화의 말미에 기재할 것이오 인쇄소가 영업상 관용하는 명칭을 가진 경우에는 該명칭도 기재할 것이오 數人이 협동하야 발행이나 인쇄를 경영하는 경우에는 업무상의 대표자를 발행나 인쇄자로 간주함.
제9조 文書 圖畵를 再版하는 경우에는 저작자나 그 相續者 及 발행자가 連印하여 제본 2부를 첨부하야 지방장관을 경유하야 (내부)大臣에게 신고할 것. 단 改增減은 註解부록, 繪畫 등을 첨가하랴고 할 시는 제2조의 수속에 의할 것.
제10조 서간, 통신, 보고, 社則, 引札, 광고, 諸藝의 次第書, 諸種의 用紙類 及 사진을 출판하는자는 제2조 제6조 제7조에 의함 要치 아니함 但 제11조 제1호 제3호에 해당한 경우에는 본법에 의하야 처분함.(하략)＜33＞
