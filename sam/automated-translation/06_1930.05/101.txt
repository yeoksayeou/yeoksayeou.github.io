Welcoming Public Opinion

Previously, the fixed price for the magazine "Samch'ŏlli" was set at 15 *chŏn*. Due to the low price, the number of pages could not be large, and it was somewhat meager. We wish to ask for your esteemed opinions on whether it would be better, from now on, to make the number of pages abundant like this month's issue and then receive 30 *chŏn*, or, like before, keep the number of pages small and just receive 15 *chŏn* each. We would be grateful if you tell us what you hope for.<29>

**Summary**

The editorial staff of "Samch'ŏlli" magazine asks readers for their opinion on a potential change. They propose either increasing the page count significantly and raising the price from 15 *chŏn* to 30 *chŏn*, or maintaining the current smaller page count and the 15 *chŏn* price. They request reader feedback to guide their decision. This notice provides insight into the economic challenges faced by Korean publishers during the colonial period, balancing content volume with affordability for readers. It also highlights the magazine's attempt to engage directly with its readership regarding its future direction.
