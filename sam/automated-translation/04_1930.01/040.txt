Social Activities of Modern Women
Working Late at Night

Chosŏn Ilbo Sa Reporter, Yun Sŏngsang

December 1st, Cloudy. The weather has turned cold. I opened my eyes at half past seven. It was ridiculously late to wake up, but perhaps because I went to bed late last night, my head was pounding. Since it was Sunday, there wouldn't be much happening at the newspaper office anyway, so I went to the library for the first time in a while. It being the end of the term and especially a Sunday, students from various schools were coming and going. Even the Women's Reading Room, usually empty, was full. I skimmed through a few books on child psychology, but finding nothing particularly insightful, and my head still pounding as before, I couldn't remain seated. I left and went straight to S's house. In a dim side room, on one side lay a bloody new life and the mother. "The nose is a Westerner's nose," I said,<21> and looking at the bright red new life squirming, still alive, a strange feeling arose in my head. "In this reality full of turmoil, what will its future be..." Though it was the birth of a new life that should rightly be celebrated, instead, its very existence seemed cursed. Feeling my mood sink terribly due to useless thoughts, I quickly left.

At night, hoping to perhaps gather some material, I visited the house of L, with whom I wasn't particularly close. L's wife, one cheek swollen dark red, appealed to me, whom she was meeting for the second time, about her suppressed situation, saying, "Oh, it's suffocating. When we were first planning to get engaged, he was so kind, but now he has changed like this," showing me her cheek, and then a handful of her hair and her head where it had been violently pulled out. Her tone was filled with so much resentment; words of comfort wouldn't even come out, and my heart, too, ached terribly. And then, I hated her for saying, "Even so, I can't just leave." It was hatred born of sympathy. After bidding her farewell and leaving, the useless anxiety "What if she gets hit again?" arose in my head, and even the indifferent footsteps on the Andong crossroads seemed noisy.

December 2nd
A stubborn rain. The commotion of life chattering before the gate, the complex sounds of Chongno street; it was extremely chaotic. This city street is always complex, but the bad weather seemed to sharpen my emotions even more. I visited Messrs. P and C, but they said they couldn't speak due to responsibility related to their positions. If they possess such a thorough sense of responsibility, it's impossible that they lack firm convictions. Calling it responsibility—what is it ultimately, if not evasion? It's something I encounter from time to time, but today too, I felt a distinctly slight displeasure.

Female Writer

Mending Socks

Kim Illyŏp

I tried very hard to prepare something like a diary entry, but as ever, apart from commonplace things like making meals, laughing and talking with my husband, flipping through the newspaper, or mending socks—there's nothing else I do. This is the truth. Therefore, there's nothing worth recording as the diary of a female writer, so with a blushing face, I end this with a postcard.

Female Teacher

Female Students' Thoughts

N Girls' School Teacher, Yi Myŏngsuk

November 27th
To understand the psychology of female students, I asked the third-year high school girls, without writing their names,<22>
I. What kind of man do you consider the ideal husband?
II. Have you recently cried over any social matter?
I posed these two questions. The first was to understand the view of men held by modern women, and the second was to know what social facts, besides school lessons, they were concerned about. As for the result of the first question, among the 30 who answered:
1. A person with good thoughts (思想). 7 students
2. A passionate rather than rational man. 4 students
3. A man with money. 6 students
4. A second son who doesn't live with his parents. 3 students
Besides these, there were many minor conditions like 'dislike a doctor,' 'dislike a merchant,' etc. And for all the above answers, the basic condition was a man between 20 and, at the latest, 30 years old, with suitable looks and education. However, the meaning of "a man with good thoughts," which was the most common answer, probably refers to a man with firm resolve, who isn't easily swayed by others, and who isn't absorbed in personal pleasure—someone whose guts are properly set (창자가 바로 박인 사람). Furthermore, it seems to implicitly indicate men whose love does not change frequently. Should one call this commonplace? If the true meaning of education lies in the commonplace, then our school's education can be considered a success. Regarding the second question:
1. 11 students said they cried over an OO scene in a newspaper article.
2. 7 students said they cried seeing a Korean woman carrying someone else's child on her back at Chŭn'gogae.
From this fact, one could see that they too hold much interest in social matters.

November 28th
According to what English teacher Ms. M said with wide eyes, apparently, it's fashionable these days for upper-level female students not to wear inner trousers (속바지). She was making a fuss, saying "These girls have become debauched." If the dean finds out, there will probably be an inspection again. Anyway, let's see the results of tomorrow's staff meeting.<23>

**Summary**

This article presents three vignettes capturing aspects of modern women's lives in 1930 Korea. Yun Sŏngsang, a journalist, recounts observations of difficult childbirth and domestic violence, contrasting these harsh realities with the intellectual pursuits of female students. Kim Illyŏp, a writer, claims a mundane domestic life devoid of diary-worthy events. Yi Myŏngsuk, a teacher, shares results from a survey of her students, revealing their ideals for husbands (prioritizing "good thoughts" and passion over rationality, alongside practical concerns like wealth and independence from in-laws) and their emotional engagement with social issues reported in the news or observed directly, like the plight of other Korean women. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable glimpses into the varied experiences of women during the colonial period: the persistence of patriarchal issues like domestic violence, the emerging consciousness and social awareness among educated young women, their specific criteria for life partners reflecting both modern and traditional considerations, and even anxieties about changing social norms like fashion (not wearing inner trousers).

近代 女性의 社會活動
밤즁에 外勤

朝鮮日報社 記者, 尹聖相

12월 1일 曇, 날세는 추워젓다. 눈을 뜨니 닐곱時 半. 엉터리업는 늣잠이엿다만 엇저녁 늣게 잔 탓인지 머리는 뗑헷다. 日曜日이니 新聞社에는 들어가도 별일 업슬 터임으로 오래간만에 圖書館 出入을 햇다. 學期末 더구나 日曜日이여서 各 學敎 學生들이 들락날락. 언제나 텅 뷔어 잇든 女子閱覽室까지도 가득 차 잇섯다. 兒童心理에 關한 것을 몃 冊 뒤적어리다가 그다지 신통한 것도 업슬 뿐 아니라
如前히 머리가 뗑해서, 안젓슬 수 업섯다. 나와서 바로 S의 집을 차저 갓다. 컴컴한 단間房에 한편에는 피ㅅ뎡어리 새 生命 그리고 産母가 누어 잇섯다. 「코가 양코야」하고<21> 그래도 살엇다고 움즉이는 샛밝안 새 生命을 듸려다 본 내 머리엔 야릇한 感情이 떠 올랏다. 「波瀾 만흔 이 現實에 저것의 將來는...」하는. 맛당히 깃버해야 할 새 生命의 誕生이것만 도리혀 그 生이 咀呪스러운 듯 햇다. 쓸데업는 생각에 몹시 氣分이 沈査해지는 듯해서 곳 나와 버렷다.
밤에는 或 무슨 材料나 어들가 하야 그다지 親치도 안은 L의 집을 차젓다. 한편 빰이 검붉게 부어 오른 L의 안해, 그는 두번재 對하는 내게 抑査한 事情을 呼訴하며 「아이구 기가 막혀서요, 처음 約婚하려고 할 때 그는 그러케도 親切하드니 只今은 이러케도 變하엿담니다.」하며 그의 뺨, 그리고 한 아름되는 머리카락과 散散히 뜻긴 머리를 뵈엿다. 넘우나 怨痛해 하는 그의 語調, 慰勞의 말조차 나오지 안는 내 가슴 亦是 몹시 압핫다. 그러고 「이러타고 가 버릴 수도 업고-」하는 그가 미웟다. 同情함으로서의 미움이엿다. 그를 하직하고 나선 내 머리엔 「그가 또 마즈면」하는 쓸데업는 不安이 떠올나 安洞 네 거리의 無心한 人跡조차 식그러웟다.

12월 2일
철 구즌 비, 문압헤 짓거리는 삶의 動搖 鍾路거리의 複雜한 音響, 몹시 어수선햇다. 언제나 複雜한 이 市街련만 날 굿다는 것이 한층 더 내 感情을 銳利하게 한 모양이엇다. P, C 兩氏를 訪問햇드니 責任上 關係로 말할 수 업다고. 그만콤 責任感을 徹底히 가진 이라면 確固한 主張이 업술理 萬無하건만. 責任云謂하는 것은 結局回避가 아니고 무엇이랴. 각금 當하는 事實이지만 오늘도 적히 輕微한 不快를 感햇다.

女流作家

양말짝을 뀌매어

金一葉

저는 日記가음을 작만해 보려고 몹시 애를 썻지만은 여전히 요지간은 밥 해먹고 남편과 웃고 니야기하고 新聞장을 뒤지거나 양말짝을 뀌매거나 하는 시시부시한 일 밧게-다른 하는 일이라고는 업슴니다. 이것은 참말입니다. 그러기에 女流作家의 日記라고 記錄할 만한 거리가 못되어 붉은 얼골을 만지면서 葉書로 이에 끗침니다.

女敎員

女學生의 思想

N女學校敎師 李明淑

11월 27일
女學生들 心理를 아라보려고 高等科 3年級 아해들에게 자긔 일홈 쓰지말고서<22>
一. 엇든 男子를 理想的 男便으로 생각하느냐.
二. 요지간에 社會的의 엇든 일에 울어 본 일이 잇느냐.
하는 두 가지 물음을 發햇다. 첫재는 近代 女性의 男性觀을 알자는 것이요 둘재는 學課 以外에 엇든 社會的 事實에 關心을 가지고 잇는가 함을 알기 위함이엇다. 그 結果 첫재 무름에 잇서서, 30명이 答案한 가운데
1. 思想이 조흔 사람.         7名
2. 理智的보다 情熱的 男子    4名
3. 돈잇는 사내               6名
4. 父母와 合居아니하는 次子  3名
其外 醫師는 실타, 商人도 실타하는 등 무엇무엇하는 少少한 條件이 만하엿다. 그리고 以上의 對答도 全部 20歲로부터 느저도 30歲까지의 남자로 容貌와 學識이 相當한 이라는 것이 基本條件이 되어 잇섯다. 그런데 가장 만흔 「思想이 조흔 男性」이란 뜻은 아마 確乎한 意志가 서서 남에게 잘 건드리지 안코 또 個人의 亨樂에 沒頭하지 아니하는 창자가 바로 박인 사람을 指稱한 것일 것이며 그 우에 隱然中 사랑이 자조 變하지 안는 男性들을 가르치는 모양이다. 可謂 平凡타 할가. 平凡에 敎育의 本意가 잇다면 우리 學敎 敎育도 成功한 셈이다. 둘재 무름에 對하여는
1. 新聞記事 中 OO光景에 울엇다는 이 11名
2. 즌고개에서 朝鮮 女子가 남의 아해 업고 다니는 것을 보고 울엇다는 이 7名
들엇다. 그네들도 社會의 일에 만히 關心을 가지고 잇는 것만은 이 事實로 보아서 알 수 잇섯다.

11월 28일
英語敎師 M女史가 눈을 뚱그리고 하는 말을 드르면 요지간 上級 女學生들은 속바지을 아니 입는 것이 流行이라나, 이년들이 난봉낫다고 야단이엇다. 學監이 알면 또 檢査가 이러날 걸. 아무튼 來日의 職員會議 結果를 보자.<23>
