Life That Will Grow in the Next Ten Years!!, The Press, Education Circles, Etc.
Life Grown Over Ten Years

For someone suffering from a serious illness, what is essential are the developments since the onset and the treatment methods until recovery. What need is there to discuss in a thousand ways their past health or the things they will do after recovering while they are still sick? The problem lies in the present salvation. Being a sick body, the entire purpose lies in the recovery of health.

Likewise, what we need is not a long history of four thousand or five thousand years, nor does it lie in establishing plans for the next hundred or thousand years. Truly, everything depends on the past ten years since the stirrings that can be called the movement of self-awakening (긔미 이후), and the march forward in the next ten years based on those ten years. Thinking about it, since the sprouts have already emerged, they will surely grow; since the flower buds have formed, they will inevitably bloom. If we say ten years to sprout and ten years to bloom, this timing is unlikely to be greatly mistaken in measuring the years of history's march.

Already, the arrow released from the bow (離弓之矢) speeds towards the target. The press circles are like this, education is like this, and science and art are like this. Who can deny that after some years and months pass, a hundred flowers will vie in beauty (百花競姸) in their wake? Let us sit down at the dawn of the new year and examine with joyous hearts the footsteps of the past ten years we have walked and the path ahead for the next ten years we will walk.
In the first dawn of 1930—

The Press Circles

Upon investigating the confidential readership numbers of daily newspapers like Chosŏn (朝鮮), Tonga (東亞), and Chungoe (中外), each possessed thirty thousand to fifty or sixty thousand readers, totaling 150,000. Also, monthly magazines like Chosŏn chi Kwang (朝鮮之光), Sinhŭng (新興), Chosŏn Kangdan (朝鮮講壇), P'yŏngnon (評論), Pyŏlgŏn'gon (別乾坤), Sinsaeng (新生), Haksaeng (學生), Sinsosŏl (新小說), and Samch'ŏlli (三千里) each count one or two thousand to two or three thousand readers, so their total readership is not less than 100,000. Furthermore, the readership for standalone books on politics, economics, and literature such as Chosŏn Kyŏngje Ron (朝鮮經濟論), Chosŏn ŭi Hyŏnjae wa Changnae (朝鮮의 現在와 將來), Manse Chŏn (萬歲前), Hyŏrhŭn (血痕), Moksum (목숨) also numbers fifty or sixty thousand. Thus, the unique readership of Chosŏn's progressive publications reaches the surprising number of 300,000 [Author's calculation seems to imply 300,000, though the text states 30 million (3000만)], and the total annual readership reaches 56 million (신문 365일에 5475만 잡지 12월에 125만 단행본 년5만의 計 - Newspapers 54.75 million for 365 days, Magazines 1.25 million for 12 months, Books 50,000 annually). Now, if we divide the 300,000 unique readers into schools accommodating 300 people each, it means about 1,000 schools have been established in every nook and cranny of the 13 provinces (13道 坊坊谷谷), continuously providing social education to workers, peasants, students, and youth. 300,000 people educated in 1,000 schools! If we consider this as the total figure for the past 10 years, it means 10,000 schools have cultivated (「칼트」한) 3 million people. If the results from the founding era (草創時代) are already like this, what indeed will the effects be in the future?

Newspapers, magazines, and other publications are the face of their society. For example, suppose melancholy clouds (愁雲) gathered on the pages of the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) one day. That is because a dark cloud passed over the face of Chosŏn that day. Suppose laughter spread across the pages of Tonga (東亞) or Chungoe (中外) another day. That too is because there was an aspect of laughter spreading across the faces <2> of our people. However, while being a mirror like this, we must not forget that newspapers and magazines also become the "brain" (頭腦) of the populace.

Therefore, the articles in newspapers and magazines serve as injection fluid for our popular spirit and fulfill the role of a reader for the masses. What could surpass publications in transmitting thoughts and emotions? Newspapers and magazines are signs that we are still moving and alive. They are markers of existence (存在의 標幟). Of course, other countries have parliaments, assemblies, and speeches in addition to publications, so "speech" (言) and "discourse" (論) truly coexist, but since we lack "speech" (言), we inevitably have to manage this task with only "discourse" (論).

Let's not say we have no gathering places other than theaters, churches, and schools. Let's not say we can only recite things like "Sim Sun'ae, I Suil" (沈順愛 李守一), "Amen, Tamen" (아―멘 다―멘), and "Kwangje ch'angsaeng p'odŏk ch'ŏnha" (廣濟蒼生布德天下). Furthermore, let's say even the Posin'gak (보신각) bell has sealed its lips and all crying (鳴) has ceased. Even so, friends, don't we have newspapers, magazines, and publications? Aren't all these publications spreading like a flood through Tongdaemun (東大門), Sŏdaemun (西大門), and Namdaemun (南大門) to every valley of this land, day by day, month by month, year by year? This is living movement! Let's say it is faint (微微). But it will grow. Chosŏn's press circles will grow ever more steadily forward, and thinking that people with the dedication to nurture this will increase day by day, month by month, how joyous an affair this is! <3>

Education Circles

If the dark side of Chosŏn (朝鮮) is said to be the prisons, the bright side (光明面) is truly the schools. That is, education itself is the entirety of light (光明). Like anyone would, when I see the young masters and misses commuting to school, blowing on their tiny fists to warm them even in the cold winter, I feel so happy I could bow to them. When I look at those schoolchildren (學童), I am always reminded of the seeds of flowering plants crushed beneath a collapsed stone pagoda. Let's say that on a fine garden where flowers once bloomed and leaves were green, a stone pagoda crumbled, crushing the flowering plants. Even so, the seeds, truly just tiny seeds, are buried in some corner of the earth, secretly, secretly growing. When these schoolchildren become fine citizens (市民) and members of the public (公民) in the future, our arms and legs will be able to stretch freely. Thinking about it, nothing has shown as much rapid progress (長足의 진보) as education in the past 10 years. Now, <4> if shown numerically,

According to a recent survey (調査), whereas at the time of the Kimi Movement (己未 당시) there were only 570 common schools (普通學校) with about 90,000 total students (全生徒), today, after 10 years have passed, the number of schools has reached 1,419 and students number 450,000, showing a roughly 3-fold increase in schools and a roughly 5-fold surge (激增) in students. Besides this, middle schools, women's education, universities (大學), and professional schools (專門學校) all show a world of difference (雲泥의 差). What else could this be but a sign that new life is growing moment by moment?

Among these various schools, there are schools established and maintained by private citizens (民間 有志) pooling small amounts of money, schools founded by Westerners, such as Christian (基督敎) religious schools, and public schools established by the Government-General (總督府). Thus, the types (種類) of textbooks used in these various schools number over 120, requiring about 3 million volumes (冊) annually. Also, the education expenses poured just into public common schools (公立普通學校) annually are 11 million won, and those into public higher common schools (公立高等普通學校) are 1.5 million won. Therefore, if we count the total public and private expenses for universities and professional schools, it would likely exceed the large sum of 30 million won. Besides this, youth associations (青年會) and labor-peasant federations (勞農總同盟) in various regions gather rural women or village elders during the agricultural off-season for education, so there are such institutions in every nook and cranny (坊坊谷谷). Truly, education in present-day Chosŏn (朝鮮) can be said to be developing with astonishing results. Furthermore, regarding teaching materials (敎科材), in recent years, many are being compiled (編制) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城) by our Chosŏn Kyoyuk Hyŏphoe (朝鮮敎育協會), and many history books emphasizing Chosŏn sentiment (朝鮮情趣) are being produced by figures like Mr. So-and-so (某某氏). Also, the Chosŏn Ilbosa (朝鮮日報社) has been mobilizing students for Hangul education to eliminate illiteracy (文盲打破). In addition, wherever compatriots in white clothes reside, such as Sanghae (上海 - Shanghai), P'owa (布哇 - Hawaii), or Haesamwi (海參威 - Vladivostok), there are several large and small schools striving to teach reading whenever possible. Thus, young ladies and gentlemen with such enthusiasm for learning increase daily, and conscientious adults striving eagerly to teach appear everywhere on this street. Where else could there be such a joyous thing?

Since the achievements of the past 10 years, which suffered particular hardships like floods (水災), droughts (旱害), and insect damage (虫災), are already like this, if a day comes when there are sufficient crops to eat and fish catches and industrial products (工産) to sell to others, or even if we just reach the level of adequate food and clothing (足食足衣), the people of this land will have the dedication to take off the clothes they wear and set aside the rice they eat for the sake of education. How beautiful and splendid will be the flowers and fruits that bloom upon this hall of education in the coming 10 years! Truly, it must be called joyous. <5>

Arts Circles

Literature (文藝)=Just as population, press, and education have all grown astonishingly during the past 10 years, Chosŏn literature, even with its environment, has shown exceptional (異數) development. That is, after Ch'unwŏn (春園) produced long and short novels like Mujŏng (無情) and Yun Kwangho (尹光浩) 13 or 14 years ago, beginning to lay the cornerstone of modern novels in description and composition, pure literary writers emerged in Chosŏn. In novels: Yŏm Sangsŏp (廉想涉), Kim Tongin (金東仁), Ch'oe Sŏhae (崔曙海), Hyŏn Pinghŏ (玄憑虛), Yi Kiyŏng (李箕永), Na Tohyang (羅稻香), Ch'oe Tokkyŏn (崔獨鵑), Pak Yŏnghŭi (朴英熙), Kim Kijin (金基鎭), Yi Sŏnghae (李星海), Chu Yosŏp (朱요섭), Song Yŏng (宋影), Ch'oe Sŭngil (崔承一), Kim Yŏngp'al (金永八), Yi Chongmyŏng (李鍾鳴), Hong Pyŏkch'o (洪碧初), Cho P'osŏk (趙抱石), and others. In poetry: Chu Yohan (朱耀翰), Kim Ansŏ (金岸曙), Yi Ŭnsang (李殷相), Kim Sŏksong (金石松), Pak P'aryang (朴八陽), Yu Chŏkku (柳赤駒), Im Hwa (林和), Yi Sanghwa (李相和), P'osŏk (抱石 - Cho P'osŏk?), Chŏng Nop'ung (鄭盧風), Pak Chonghwa (朴鍾和), Kim Sowŏl (金素月), Kim Ch'angsul (金昌述), Yang Muae (梁無涯), Han Yongun (韓龍雲), O Sangsun (吳相殉), Pyŏn Suju (卞樹州), Kim Haegang (金海剛), P'ain (파인), and others. In Sijo (時調): Yuktang (六堂), Chŏng Injin (鄭寅晋), Yohan (요한 - Chu Yohan?), Yi Pyŏnggi (李秉歧), Kwŏn Kuhyŏn (權九玄), Choun (曹雲), and others. Playwrights (劇作家): Kim Unjŏng (金運汀), Yun Paengnam (尹白南), Kim Yŏngbo (金泳甫), Pak Sŭnghŭi (朴勝喜), and others. And literary critics (文藝評論): Ch'unwŏn (春園), P'albong (八峯), Hoewŏl (懷月), Muae (無涯 - Yang Muae?), Yi Pungman (李北滿), Yun Kijŏng (尹基鼎), Chŏng Nop'ung (鄭盧風), and others have emerged brilliantly like the constellations in the dawn sky, showing much construction. Thus, there were pure literary magazines like Sinsosŏl (新小說), Munye Kongnon (文藝公論), and Chosŏn Munye (朝鮮文藝), and dozens of novels and poems were published as standalone books. Writers' organizations like the Chosŏn Munyega Hyŏphoe (朝鮮文藝家協會), Chosŏn P'ŭroret'aria Yesul Tongmaeng (朝鮮프로레타리아藝術同盟 - KAPF), and Chosŏn Kayo Hyŏphoe (朝鮮歌謠協會) were founded and showed their respective activities. In essence, the past 19 years [likely typo, should be '10 years'] merely involved destroying the oral-tradition old literature (口碑式 舊文學) and laying the first cornerstone of construction. Great activity is expected in the next 10 years.
Film (映畵)=The development of film doesn't even have a 10-year history. Until 5 or 6 years ago, there was nothing that could be called a Chosŏn film. Only in 1925 [text says 1915, but context and Arirang (1926) suggest 1925/26 is more likely time Na Un'gyu prod. started] was the Na Un'gyu Production (羅雲奎푸로턱손) founded in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), producing the single film Arirang (아리랑). Because it received favorable reviews from the populace, the film business showed rapid progress, and films like P'ungun'a (風雲兒), Nak'wa Yusu (落花流水), Mŏn Tongi T'ŭl Ttae (먼동이 틀 때), Kŭmpung'ŏ (금붕어), as well as film adaptations of Ch'unwŏn's (春園) Kaech'ŏkcha (開拓者) and P'albong's (八峰) Yakhon (約婚) were made. Now, several specialized film production companies for shooting and distribution, such as Chosŏn P'ŭrodŏksyun (朝鮮푸로덕슌) and K'inema (키네마), have been established. Although it is unknown what path film will take in the future, it seems likely they will strive to produce works unique to Chosŏn (朝鮮獨特) while still receiving influence from the West (券西 - likely typo for 拳西 or just 西 - West) and Japan (日本). <6>
Theater (演劇)=In 1924 [T'owŏrhoe founded 1922/23, active period started then], the T'owŏrhoe (土月會), organized by students studying in Tokyo (東京遊學生), performed a epoch-making (劃時期的) important role in Chosŏn's theater history. Although it might have been merely imitation of Western plays like Resurrection (復活) and Alt-Heidelberg (하이델 배루히), it helped sweep away the old stench (舊臭) of the past in terms of directing methods and techniques (技倆). Since then, with the founding of the Paekchohoe (白鳥會) and the revival of the T'owŏrhoe, sound Chosŏn theater is in the process of being established. Although it can be said that there are still no remarkable figures among playwrights or actors, important gains are believed to be forthcoming as those who have been studying theater theoretically or practically abroad return. Furthermore, according to what is heard, various people are striving to create something like Japan's Tsukiji Little Theater (日本築地小劇場), an experimental theater system based on the experiences of the past 10 years. It is known that excellent theater, which will become nourishment for the populace, will eventually be constructed even on this land with its unique circumstances.
Music (音樂)=Traditional Chosŏn music, conventionally mistreated (虐待), began to display its true value after research commenced at Ihwa Chŏnmun Hakkyo (梨花專門學校) and Kyŏngsŏng Chedae (京城帝大), drawing public attention. Also, prominent vocalists (聲樂家), instrumentalists (器樂家), and composers (作曲者) such as Kim Yŏnghwan (金永煥), An Kiyŏng (安基永), Yi Sangjun (李尙俊), Chŏng Sunch'ŏl (鄭順哲) and others have emerged. While incorporating the strengths of Western music (西樂), they are striving to form a unique modern music that does not lose the characteristics (特徵) of Chosŏn music. In the future, as the public's ability to appreciate music develops with the spread of radio (라지오) and phonographs (畜音器 - gramophones), excellent music will inevitably be born (誕出) in this area too.
Originally, there is no weapon like music for infecting a wide populace with its emotions. This can be fully explained by citing historical examples (史例), but it is omitted here. In any case, as people gradually realize the role music plays in popular life, a true music movement will arise in this land as well.
Dance (舞踊)=Traditional dances such as the Monk's Dance (僧舞) and Spring Nightingale Dance (春鶯舞), which had the flaw of being crude (粗放) and disorderly (蕪雜) to the point of being called primitive (원시적), have been stimulated (刺戟) by new-generation dancers like Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi (崔承喜) and Pae Kuja (裵龜子). They are in the process of transforming into arts with faster tempo (템포) and complex colors. While doing so, the aforementioned accomplished female dancers (閨秀 舞踊家), who had honed their skills (技藝) somewhat in the West (西洋) and Japan (日本), have each established Dance Research Institutes (舞踊硏究所) and are training successors. Therefore, it can be said that the true emergence of modern dance in Chosŏn will occur within the next few years. <7>

**Summary**

This 1930 editorial from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine expresses strong optimism for Korea's development over the next decade (1930-1940), building on the progress perceived since the March 1st Movement (1919-1929). The author highlights growth in the press (newspapers, magazines, books reaching hundreds of thousands), education (a five-fold increase in common school students, development of higher education, textbook creation), and the arts (emergence of modern literature, film, theater, music, and dance). Despite hardships and colonial limitations ("lack of speech," reliance on "discourse" via publications), the article sees these fields as burgeoning "life" and sources of national strength and identity, fueled by popular enthusiasm and dedicated individuals.

From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for its contemporary assessment of cultural and social developments in colonial Korea during a period often characterized solely by oppression. It provides specific statistics (though some seem inflated or possibly mistyped, the calculations reveal intended figures) on readership and school enrollment, lists prominent cultural figures and organizations across various fields (literature, film, theatre, music, dance), and reflects a palpable sense of national consciousness and aspiration for progress, particularly focused on education and cultural expression as vital forces. The distinction made between "speech" (言 - political agency, assembly) and "discourse" (論 - publications) starkly highlights the constrained public sphere under Japanese rule.

十年에 자란 生命

중병 알는 사람에게 요긴한 것은 발병 이후의 그 경과와 또 병 나을 때까지의 치료 방법 등에 잇다. 병석에 잇는 그에게 넷날의 건강과 몸 회복한 뒤의 할 일들을 천백 가지로의 론하는 것이 무에가 필요하랴. 문제는 현재의 구제에 잇다. 병든 몸이매 건강의 회복에 젼 목적이 잇는 것이다.

마천가지로 우리에게 필요한 것은 사 천년 오 천 년하는 긴 력사가 아니요. 또한 금후의 백년지계 천년지계의 수립에 잇지 안타. 실로 자각의 운동이라 할 긔미 이후의 과거 십년동안과 그 십 년을 로대로 한 금후 십 년의 행진에 모든 것이 달닌 터이다. 생각건대 이미 돗은 싹이니 소슬 것이요. 여무러진 꼿봉오리니 피고야 박이리라. 돗기 십 년 피기 십 년이라 하면 력사 행진의 년대 측정에 과히 틀님이 엇슬진저.

이미 離弓之矢는 관역을 향하고 닷는다. 언론계가 그러하고 교육이 그러하고 과학과 예술이 그러하다. 이 뒤 얼마의 년 월이 지난 뒤에 그 결에에는 百花가 競姸할 것을 누가 잇서 부인하랴. 우리는 깁분 마음으로 거러온 지난 십 년의 발자최와 거러 잘 장래 십 년의 압날의 발자취를 새해 벽두에 안저 살펴보기로 하자.
1930, 첫 새벽에―

言論界

비밀에 부치는 朝鮮, 東亞, 中外 등, 일간신문의 독자 수를 알어본 결과 모다 삼만 내지 오육천만 식을 가지고 잇섯슨즉 그 총 수 15만을 헤일 것이요. 또 朝鮮之光, 新興, 朝鮮講壇, 評論, 別乾坤, 新生, 學生, 新小說, 三千里 등 월간잡지 亦 일이천 내지 이삼천만 식을 헤이고 잇슨즉 그 수 10만은 不下할 것이요. 또한 朝鮮經濟論, 朝鮮의 現在와 將來, 萬歲前, 血痕, 목숨 등 정치 경제 문예에 관한 단행본 독자 수 亦 오육만을 헤이고 잇는 터인즉 朝鮮의 진취적 간행물 독자가 單數 3000만 延數 5600만(신문 365일에 5475만 잡지 12월에 125만 단행본 년5만의 計)명의 놀라운 수에 달한다. 이제 單數 30만 명을 300명 식 수용하는 학교로 쪼기어 보면 약 1000校가 13道 坊坊谷谷에 설립되어 사회적 교양을 勞働者 農民 學生 靑年들에게 향하야  不絶히 실시하고 잇는 세음이 된다. 1000校의 30만 명 교양! 이 것을 과거 10년 간의 총 수로 따저 본다면 1만 校가 300만 명을 「칼트」한 것이 된다. 草創時代의 성적이 이미 이러하거든 금후의 효과는 과연 엇더할 것일고.

신문 잡지 등 출판물은 그 사회의 얼골이다. 가령 어느 날의 朝鮮日報 지면에 愁雲이 어리엇더라 하자. 그는 그 날의 조선의 얼골에 거문 구름 짱이 지나간 까닭이며 東亞中外의 지면에 또 어느 날엔 우슴이 떠돌앗다 하자. 그 亦 우리 민중의 <2> 얼골 우에 우슴이  떠도는 一面이 잇섯기에 그러한 것이다. 그러나 이가치 거울이 되는 반면에 신문 잡지는 또한 민중의 「頭腦」가 되는 것을 이저서는 아니 된다.
그러기에 신문 잡지의 기사는 우리 민중정신상의 주사액이요 민중 讀本이 임무를 맛허 히고잇다. 사상과 감정을 전염식힘에 잇서 출판물에 勝함이 또 잇스랴. 신문 잡지 등은 우리들이 그래도 움지기고 살어잇다는 표다. 존재의 標幟이다. 물론 다른 나라에는 출판물 이외에도 의회 등 집회와 연설 등이 잇서서 그야말로 언론이 俱存하나 우리에게는 「言」이란 것이 업슴으로 부득이 「論」 한나만 가지고 이 일을 맛허 보게 되는 것이다.

우리에게 극장과 교회당과 학교 이외에 모이는 장소가 업다 말자. 「沈順愛 李守一」과 「아―멘 다―멘」과 「廣濟蒼生布德天下」 밧게 念할 수 잇는 말이 업다 하자. 더욱 보신각 종도 입을 담을고  모든 우름(鳴)도 끗첫다 하자. 그러터래도 동무야 우리에게는 신문과 잡지와 출판물이 잇지 안으냐. 날로 달로 해로 이 모든 간행물이 東大門 西大門 南大門을 통하야 홍수와 가치 이 땅의 골골에 펴지고 잇지 안느냐. 이 것이 사라 잇는 움지김이다! 微微하다 하자. 그러나 자랄 것이다. 朝鮮의 언론계는 압흐로 압흐로 더욱 꾸준히 자라갈 것이며 이를 북도들 정성 가진 사람이 날로 달로 느러갈 것을 생각할 때 이 얼마나 깁분 일인고. <3>

교육계

朝鮮에서 암흑면을 감옥이라 하면 光明面은 실로 학교이다. 즉 교육 그것이 光明의 全體다. 누구든지 그러켓지만 나는 추운 겨울에도 조고마한 주먹들을 혹혹 불어 녹이면서 통학하는 도련님 아가써들을 바라보면 절하고 십흐리만치 반갑다. 그 學童을 바라볼 때에 항상 쓰러진 돌탑 밋헤 깔니운 화초의 씨를 생각하게 하나니. 녯날에는 꼿피고 닙히 푸르럿든 조흔 동산 우에 그만 돌탑이 문허지면서 화초가 깔니엇다 하자. 그러터라도 그 씨만은 실로 조고마한 씨만은 땅 어느 모통이에 뭇기어 잇다가 남몰래 남몰래 자라나고 잇는 것과 갓다. 이 學童들이 뒷날 훌용한 시민이 되고 공민이 되는 날에 우리의 팔과 다리는 마음대로 퍼칠 수 잇슬 것이라. 생각건대 과거 10년 간에 교육과 가치 長足의 진보를 보인 것이 업다. 이제 <4> 이 것을 교자로 보일 것 가트면

즉 최근의 調査에 의하건대 己未 당시에는 普通學校가 570校 全生徒 약 9만 명에 불과하든 것이 10년의 년월을 지난 오늘에 이르러는 校數 1419校 生徒 45만 명을 算하게 되어 校數에 잇서 약 3배 生徒 수에 잇서 약 5배의 激增을 보이고 잇다. 이 밧게 중학교나 여자교육이나 대학 及 전문학교나 모다 雲泥 의 差를 보이고 잇스니 이 것이 새 생명이 시시각각으로 자라 가는 표가 아니고 무엇이랴.

이 여러 학교 속에는 民間 有志들이 조고마한 돈을 모아서 설립하고 또 유지하여 나가는 학교도 잇고 서양사람들이 설립한 基督敎등 종교측 학교도 잇고 總督府에서 설립한 공립학교도 잇다. 그래서 이 여러 학교에서 쓰는 교과서의 種類는 120여 種 매년 약 300만 冊이 필요하다 하며 또 교육비가 公立普通學校에만 매년 던지는 돈이 1100만원 公立高等普通學校에 던지는 것이 150만 원이라 한즉 기타 대학 전문학교의 공사립 총경비를 헤이면 약 3000만원의 거액을 초과하리라. 이 밧게 각지의 청년회와 勞農總同盟에서 농촌부녀나 농한기의 村老들을 모아서 교육하는 기관이 坊坊谷谷에 또 잇논 형편이니 실로 현재의 朝鮮의 교육은 놀라운 성적으로 발전하고 잇다 할 것이다. 더욱 敎科材에 대하여도 근년에는 京城의 우리 朝鮮敎育協會 손으로 編制되어 가는 것이 만코 또 某某씨 등의 손으로 朝鮮情趣를 강조하는 역사책 등이 만히 나오고 또한 朝鮮日報社의 손으로 文盲打破의 한글 교육 학생동원이 잇서 오는 중이다. 이밧게 上海나 布哇나 海參威 등 힌 옷 입은 동포가 잇는 곳곳마다 대소학교가 몃개 씩은 잇서서 기회 잇는대로 글을 가르치기를 힘쓴다 하니 이러틋 글을 배우려는 열성 잇는 아가씨 도련님이 날로 늘어가고 또 글 가르키고 십퍼서 애를 쓰는 뜻잇는 어룬이 이 걸에의 處處에 이러나니 이러케 깁분 일이 또다시 어듸 잇슬가.

이미 水災 旱害 虫災 등으로 각별한 수난을 격겨오든 과거 10년 간의 성적이 이미 이러한지라. 만약 먹을만한 농작이 잇고 남에게 팔만한 漁獲과 工産이 잇는 날이 온다면 아니 足食足衣의 정도만에 다 닥처 온다 할지라도 이 땅의 인사들은 교육을 위하야 입은 옷을 벗고 먹든 밥을 덜어 노흘 정성이 잇슬 것이라. 압흐로 오는 10년 사이에 얼마나 아름답고 화려한 꼿과 열매가 이 교육의 전당 우에 피어날 것인가. 실로 깁부다 할 것이다. <5>
