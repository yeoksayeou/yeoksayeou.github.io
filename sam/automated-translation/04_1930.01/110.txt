雅號의 由來
						安在鴻, 金基鎭, 金億
Minse An Chaehong (民世 安在鴻)

In the year I turned twenty-one—perhaps due to youthful vigor (少年血氣), there was within my heart an extremely grand spirit (氣槪) and ambition (抱負). To express that, nothing seemed better than the two characters "Minse!" (民世!). So, from that time, I started calling myself Minse. I will skip the true meaning (眞義) as there will be a chance to speak of it later, but for now (爲先), it wouldn't hurt to interpret it today as meaning "The People's World" (民衆의 世上).

P'albong Kim Kijin (八峯 金基鎭)

Seven or eight years ago, I wrote a thesis (論文) titled "The Edification (敎化) of the Ruling Class (支配階級) and the Edification (敎化) of the Ruled Class (被支配階級)" in *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) magazine. At that time, Mr. Kim Kijŏn (金起田氏) read the manuscript first and advised me to use a different name (變名), saying the content was a bit radical (過激) and it would be difficult for it to pass under my real name. So, sitting right there (當席), I thought of P'albongsan (八峯山) mountain in Ch'ŏngju (淸州), North Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠北), where I was born, and took the pen name "P'albong" (八峯). I have continued to use it since. Therefore, the meaning of my pen name (雅號) is purely that I am a person born below P'albongsan mountain.

Ansŏ Kim Ŏk (岸曙 金億)

Originally, I used "Sŏkch'ŏn" (石泉), but I abandoned it because it had a Chinese flavor (中國臭). When I was twenty-two and studying abroad (遊學) in Tokyo (東京), I started calling myself "Ansŏ" (岸曙). The reason was that the atmosphere (情趣) of a seashore dense with morning mist endlessly appealed to my heart. That is (卽), it was a true expression of my state of mind (心境). That state of mind has not changed even to this day. Since it's a dawn seashore, even if you hear the sound of waves, what comes to mind is not a roaring crash—thump, thump—but rather a light, beautiful lapping.<35>

**Summary**

Three prominent Korean intellectuals of the colonial era—An Chaehong, Kim Kijin, and Kim Ŏk—explain the origins of their respective pen names (雅號, aho). An Chaehong chose "Minse" (People's World) to express youthful ambition. Kim Kijin adopted "P'albong" (Eight Peaks) after his hometown mountain, P'albongsan, when advised to use an alias for a potentially controversial article in *Kaebyŏk* magazine. Kim Ŏk switched from "Sŏkch'ŏn" (Stone Spring) to "Ansŏ" (Shore Dawn) while studying in Tokyo, finding the latter better captured his affinity for the atmosphere of a misty dawn coast. This article offers insight into personal motivations (aspirations, aesthetics, origins) behind choosing pen names, while Kim Kijin's reason highlights the political caution required by writers dealing with potentially sensitive topics like class under Japanese colonial rule, making *Kaebyŏk* magazine and its editorial practices points of interest for historians.

雅號의 由來
						安在鴻, 金基鎭, 金億
民世 安在鴻

내가 스물한살 되든 해-그 때의 나의 가슴 속엔 少年血氣의 關係인지 甚히 壯大한 氣槪와 抱負가 잇섯다. 그것을 表現식힘에는 아모래도 「民世!」 二字밧게 업섯다, 그래서 그 때부터 스사로 民世라 號하엿스니 그 眞義는 뒷날 말할 機會가 잇겟기로 略하고 爲先 「民衆의 世上」이라는 뜻으로 오늘은 解釋하더래도 無妨할진저.

八峯 金基鎭

7, 8年 前 開闢 雜誌에 支配階級敎化 被支配階級敎化라는 論文을 쓴 일이 잇는데 原稿를 그 때 金起田氏가 몬저 보고 內容이 좀 過激한즉 본 일홈으로는 通過하기 어럽다하야 變名하라고 하는 勸告를 드럿다, 그래서 나는 그 當席에 안저서 내가 난 忠北 淸州의 八峯山을 聯想하고 「八峯」이라고 號하엿는데 그 뒤 繼續하여 그를 쓰고 잇다. 그런 까닭에 나의 雅號의 뜻은 純全히 八峯山아래에서 난 사람이란 것이다.

岸曙 金億

나는 元來는 「石泉」이라고 하엿는데 그것이 中國臭가 잇슴으로 버리고 東京遊學을 하든 스물두살때에 「岸曙」라고 스사로 불넛는데 그 뜻은 새벽 안개 자옥한 바다까의 情趣가 無限히 마음에 끄을니든 까닭이외다. 卽 나의 心境은 진실한 표현이엇지요. 그 心境은 오늘에 이르기까지 變하지 안슴니다. 새벽바다까이니 파도 소리가 들닌다 하여도 우렁차게 탕-탕 친다기 보다 가비엽게 아름답게 치는 것이 聯想되는 터임니다.<35>
