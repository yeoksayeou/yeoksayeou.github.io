雜談室 (Chat Room / Miscellany)

**Eight Sights and the Archipelago Sea**

Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙)
This time, I want to see India in detail. So, on my first step towards Europe and America, I will briefly pass through Shanghai, reach India, stay there for a considerably long time traveling around, and then go to America via Europe.

Why was the Tadohae (多島海, Archipelago Sea) left out of Samch'ŏlli's "Peninsula's Eight Sights" (半島八景)? Looking at those who voted, only Ch'unwŏn (春園) and Pyŏkch'o (碧初) pointed it out. I have never seen a place with scenery as superb as Tadohae. In Japan, the Seto Inland Sea (瀨戶內海) is famous for the beauty of its ocean and islands, but Tadohae's scenery type is like the Seto Inland Sea, yet it is many times, tens of times more beautiful and also larger. Truly, every island was beautiful, and the winding shores and bays were like incomparable jade. I was full of admiration for a long time while passing through there by boat.
Perhaps the reason it is not well known is that Tadohae is located in such a remote place in the South Sea. But isn't Kŭmgangsan (金剛山) also a remote place? It's the way of the world that once it starts to become known,<15> it becomes a convenient place, so I hope the scenery of Tadohae quickly becomes widely known to the world.

**"Kunsang" (群像) and Heavy Duties**

Ch'unwŏn (春園)
Because Chu Yohan (朱耀翰) entered, although I was a sort of reserve force, I was caught again and came out to the newspaper company (Dong-A Ilbo Editor-in-Chief - Reporter's Note). However, having come out, there are too many things to think about and people to meet, making it incredibly difficult to handle these heavy duties (劇務). If only my body were healthy, it wouldn't be like this.

From the New Year, I even announced in the newspaper that I would write the novel "Kunsang" (群像), but now I haven't even properly formed the concept, which is pitiful when I think about it myself.

In the future, I want to go out onto the streets for some direct movement (運動), but due to my weak body and perhaps because my personality is originally not suited for it, I keep hesitating.

**Nursing a Sick Child**

Na Hyesŏk (羅蕙錫)
As someone greeting the passing seasons in the mountain valley of Pokch'ŏndong (福泉洞), Tongnae (東萊), there should be opportunities to take up the brush, but nowadays, my young child has fallen ill, and sitting by their side administering medicine and care, I cannot collect my thoughts at all. I shall end here, promising to send a longer letter once the little one recovers.

**Others' Practical Movements**

Chŏng Chongmyŏng (鄭鍾鳴)
I want to rest my body. I desperately want to rest. Thinking back, for the past seven or eight years, I have busied myself working in various organizations (團體), and during that time, I haven't been able to sit down and read even one book slowly. I resigned from my duties at Kŭnuhoe (槿友會) with the thought of resting my body a bit, but afterward, I still had no time due to this and that, and couldn't study. The thought irresistibly arises that I want to leave Chosŏn (朝鮮) and visit places like So-and-so (某某處 等) to restore my health, study theory (學理) a bit more, and learn about the practical movements (實際運動) others are doing.

**Photograph of the Post Office**

Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊)
The famous Post Office (郵政局) where Mr. Kim Okkyun (金玉均) launched the coup (政變) is now located across from the Chosun Ilbo Company (朝鮮日報社), opposite the Chinese restaurant "Pokhaehŏn" (福海軒). Sometimes, with nostalgic feelings (感舊之懷), I go to that site, and although it has begun to crumble, the large building (大廈) stands tall (兀然). This is truly an invaluable historical treasure (寶). Many people know the story of the Post Office Coup, but I believe few know that the actual Post Office building in question is still there. Anyway, before it deteriorates further, I always think it would be good<17> if newspaper companies like the Chosun Ilbo, Dong-A Ilbo (東亞日報), or Chungoe Ilbo (中外日報) took a proper photograph of it...

**The Aim is Singular**

Matchmaker: "Isn't the bride truly splendid? Since she graduated from girls' school, she plays music well, and on top of that, her face is exceptionally beautiful (絕色). Moreover, her father is the president of the Chongno Commerce and Industry Company (鍾路商工會社)."
Prospective Groom: "If that's the case, please show me a picture."
Matchmaker: "Of the bride?"
Prospective Groom: "No, no, more than that, the picture of the company her father manages."

**Inevitable Fact**

Yi: "Hey, how did that idiot Ch'oe become such a famous detective?"
Pak: "Well, wasn't he already chasing only the backsides of female students during his student days?"

**A Glimmer of Hope**

"Why are you so disheartened?"
"I filed a claim with the Railway Bureau (鐵道局) for compensation for my lost bag, and..."
"So, you were rejected?"
"No, that darn bag just turned up at the station."

**Wu Tingfang's Revenge**

When China's Wu Tingfang (伍廷芳) was serving as Minister Plenipotentiary in America,<18> one day he went to a reception banquet with his hair in a queue (編髮). Just then, a certain arrogant American gentleman came up beside Minister Wu, tapped his head once, and said,
"Minister Wu, I hear there's a movement in your country these days to get rid of these dog-tail-like queues, which is very pleasing news. But why do you still keep yours?"
Minister Wu looked sideways at the gentleman, seemingly displeased. Below his nose, there was a mustache.
"Why do you go around wearing such a mustache?"
"Me? I have a reason. To hide my chapped lips."
"I thought so. If you lack manners, your lips tend to get chapped."

**Ouyang Xun**

The world-famous calligrapher Ouyang Xun (歐陽詢) was riding a horse through a certain village when he saw beautifully written characters on a roadside stone marker (牌石). Dismounting, he asked a village farmer (村 農夫), who told him it was the brushwork of "Suo Jing" (索靖).
The more Ouyang Xun looked, the more he saw it as masterful calligraphy. He simply sat down right in front of it, looking at the writing, and only left the spot after four days. Is this like Boya understanding the zither?

**The Last Judgment**

At a certain church (敎會堂) in Seoul, a pastor (牧師) delivered a great sermon (大說敎) on the Second Coming of Jesus and the Last Judgment (最後審判), sweating profusely. Tŏkpo (德甫),<19> who was listening from the back, began to ask questions.
"Pastor, if that day truly comes, will every single person without exception be called before God to receive judgment?"
"Absolutely."
"Then, will Cain and Abel be there too?"
"Of course. It is written so in the Bible (聖經)."
"Will Ch'unhyang (春香) and Yi Toryŏng (李道令) also be there?"
"Yes."
"What about Sim Ch'ŏng (沈淸) and Sim Pongsa (沈奉事)?"
"If they were real people, they will certainly come too."
"Aigoo, then our turn will be ages away. I'm going to stop believing in Jesus."

**The Horse's Sin**

In Anju (安州), P'yŏngan Province (平安道), there was a Catholic believer named Father Chang (張神父). Before becoming a believer, one day he rode his horse to P'yŏngyang (平壤), and a certain beauty passed before him on the road. While he was admiring her inwardly, his mind captivated, the horse, for some reason, also just followed along right behind the woman's rear.
After going some distance, the woman disappeared through the main gate of a certain house, and looking, he saw the nameplate of a kisaeng (妓生) named Pak Kyehwa (朴桂花). The scholar, longing for her for a while from atop his horse, returned, sold his fields (田土), sought out the kisaeng's house, and spent dream-like days there. However, the money poured out for the woman was like a flood, and soon he was penniless (無一分). Then, having no choice, he sold the horse he had ridden<20> and continued living, but when that money also ran out, he heard that Catholicism (天主敎) offered good times, entered the faith, cultivated the way, and even reached the status of a priest (神父), yet he could not sever his ties with the kisaeng. At that time, the Taewŏn'gun's (大院大監) persecution of Catholics began, and Father Chang was also sentenced to execution by being run over by a cart (轢刑). As he went to the execution ground, he said,
"It wasn't me who was bad, the horse was bad."

**Taking the Pulse**

A young doctor, Mr. Kim (金氏), who graduated from medical school this spring, went to the house of the famous wealthy man Mr. M (M氏) in Anguk-dong (安國洞). It was to diagnose his daughter's sudden illness. However, being his first diagnosis, his heart was pounding (울넝울넝하며). Grabbing the patient's arm to take the pulse, he said,
"It looks like malaria symptoms (마라리아 症勢). But don't worry too much."
"Hey, you! This is my wrist,"
Pulling his arm away abruptly was not the patient, but her older brother who was nursing her nearby. "Oops (앗불사), I've made a huge mistake," thought the doctor, but quickly recovered,
"No, I always check [someone else] first."

**Meeting a Novelist**

The famous French (佛蘭西) novelist Émile Zola (에밀, 조라) was famous for not meeting newspaper reporters. However, a reporter from the "Paris Times" (巴里타임스) needed to meet Zola urgently. The reporter knew that if he simply visited and asked to meet, he would undoubtedly be refused. After thinking for a while, he appeared at Zola's gate, breathing heavily as if a great disaster<21> had occurred, and said to the servant,
"Coppée (곱뻬-) has passed away. If the obituary notice hasn't arrived yet, I wish to inform the Master, so please let me meet him briefly."
"Coppée" was a famous poet of the time. Startled, Zola rushed out barefoot, exclaiming,
"Oh dear, where could there be such sad news? Please, please tell me the details quickly."
"No, he hasn't passed away yet, but he has caught a cold (感氣)."
"What, you scoundrel!"
Furious (怒髮衡天), Zola raged for a while, but finally, having no other choice, he answered the reporter's questions one by one, and thus the reporter succeeded in "that interview."<22>

**Summary**

This "Chat Room" article from the January 11, 1930 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine presents a collection of short pieces by prominent Korean intellectuals and several anecdotes or jokes. Contributors like Kim Sŏngsu discuss travel and critique the popular "Eight Sights" list for omitting Tadohae. Yi Kwangsu (Ch'unwŏn) reflects on his demanding job at the Dong-A Ilbo and writing difficulties. Na Hyesŏk excuses herself due to nursing a sick child. Chŏng Chongmyŏng expresses burnout and a desire to travel abroad for rest and study. Yun Ch'iho laments the neglect of the historic Post Office building related to the Kapsin Coup and urges its documentation. The section concludes with jokes and anecdotes touching on marriage motives, detective work, historical figures (Wu Tingfang, Ouyang Xun, Father Chang), religious themes (Last Judgment, Catholicism), and a clever reporter tricking Émile Zola.

For historians, this piece offers valuable glimpses into the personal lives, concerns (health, workload, creative struggles), and opinions of leading figures during the colonial period. It reflects contemporary cultural discussions (ranking landscapes), awareness of historical sites and their preservation (or lack thereof), social commentary through humor, and references to organizations like Kŭnuhoe and newspapers like Dong-A Ilbo and Chosun Ilbo. The blend of personal updates, cultural critique, and humor provides a multifaceted view of the era's intellectual and social atmosphere.

雜談室
八景과 多島海

金性洙
나는 이번에 印度를 자세히 보고 십슴니다. 그래서 歐美로 가든 첫 거름에 上海를 暫時 거처서 印度에 이르러 相當히 긴 시일을 그 곳에 머물너 잇스며 다니다가 그 다음에 美國을 거치어 歐羅巴로 가겟슴니다.

三千里의 「半島八景」속에 웨 多島海가 빠젓서요? 投票한 분을 차저보니까 多島海는 겨우 春園과 碧初 兩君이 指摘하엿슬 뿐이더군요. 나는 多島海가치 景致가 絶勝한 곳을 보지 못하엿슴니다. 日本서는 海洋과 島嶼의 美로 「瀨戶內海」가 일홈이 놉지만은 多島海의 景致타입은 마치 瀨戶內海類이면서 그의 몃 倍 몃十 倍나 더 아름다고 또 크지요. 실로 섬마다 아름답고 요리조리 屈曲한 汀과 灣이 無比玉石이드군요. 나는 배를 타고 그 곳을 지나면서 讚歎長久하엿소이다.
아마 이것이 잘 알니지 못한 까닭은 多島海가 너무도 南海 외僻地에 잇기 때문이겟지요. 그러나 金剛山은 僻地가 아닌가요? 다 世上이 알기 시작하면<15> 便利한 곳이 되는 法이니까 多島海의 景致도 어서 世上에 널니 알니어졋스면 조흘 줄 압니다.
