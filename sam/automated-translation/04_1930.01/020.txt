Article Title: 過去 十年에 한 일 將來 十年에 할 일 (Things Done in the Past Ten Years, Things to Do in the Next Ten Years)
Author: Multiple respondents (Survey)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Things Done in the Past Ten Years, Things to Do in the Next Ten Years
2, Who is a person I like? (In terms of character, ideology)
3, How do I foresee my death? (Dying in bed, Love suicide, Suicide, Starvation, Execution, etc.)
4, If the Nobel Prize came to Chosŏn, who would receive it?
5, If becoming "More Youthful," becoming a "Millionaire," or having a "Love Affair with a Peerless Beauty" were possible, which would I choose?

An Chaehong, Chosŏn Ilbo Company

1, I have done nothing worth mentioning in the past 10 years. Looking at the time, I spent over 40 months in prison (囹圄), and over 15 months confined (幽閉) to my hometown. After that is my current life at the newspaper company. It seems my life, based on my work as a reporter already <8> reaching its seventh year, will see no particular change.
For the next 10 years, it seems unavoidable that I will continue this life until that unknowable time. One thing is that I must have achievements as a reporter, that is, I must leave behind sediments, dregs, through reading and research. For anything else, I will not commit the error of speaking ahead of myself.

2, This answer is difficult. I most like someone who lives with sincerity (誠意) towards the self, the nation, and life. That is, someone who, based most firmly on the solid reality within the historical process of present-day Chosŏn, explores, establishes, and promotes a rigorous policy, and who possesses a sharp sense of responsibility and high insight to carry out the work of salvation (匡救) to the maximum extent—such a person is best. If not perfect, someone close is also good. If lacking in talent (材幹), someone with at least sincerity is good.

3, Suicide is absolutely out of the question. I guarantee there will be no love suicide either. For the rest, let's leave it entirely to fate.

4, Who the Nobel Prize money will go to requires some consideration, but I don't have the time to consider it now.

5, If I were to act the greedy fool, girding my waist with 100,000 coins and riding a crane down to Yangzhou, my dearest wish would be to become "More Youthful," then become a "Millionaire," and then have a love affair with a "Peerless Beauty." But if I did that, Yama, King of Hell, would throw away his throne and persecute me to take that position instead. Ah, I'll just choose to become a "Millionaire." This is because the completion of one's principles is the completion of one's character, and the completion of character is the management of funds.

Yi Kwangsu, Tong-A Ilbo Company

1, Unfortunately, I have a frail body. It seems most of what I did in the past 10 years was being ill. If my body hadn't been so weak, wouldn't I have steadily engaged in some political or social activity? Writing novels and poems like "Tanjong Aesa" (端宗哀史) during that time was mostly done out of necessity to earn funds for medicine, as I was sick. When the Suyang Tonguhoe (修養同友會) was first founded, I intended to strive to nurture many healthy young people here, but due to illness, I couldn't achieve my goals as intended.
Also, among the things worth remembering during the 10 years I held a pen is writing "Minjokchŏk Kyŏngnyun" (民族的經綸 - National Management) and "Minjok Kaejoron" (民族改造論 - Theory of National Reconstruction) and suffering censure for my writing (筆禍). "Minjokchŏk Kyŏngnyun" was written 7 or 8 years ago in the Tong-A Ilbo in the format of an editorial over five or six days. The reason it met opposition back then was perhaps because the timing was too early? The platforms and projects of certain current organizations seem to be interpreted similarly.
As for what I will do in the next 10 years, I will also take up the pen. I will work as a newspaper reporter, and on the other hand, I plan to steadily write novels.

2, Regardless of whether they are inside or outside the country, among our people, I respect Tosan An Ch'angho-sŏnsaeng (島山 安昌浩先生). Truly, I have yet to meet a person of such noble character. The fact that this elder was so astonishingly consistent in his "honesty" further evokes feelings of reverence.
Among foreigners, Tolstoy is good. He is not only good as an artist, but I know him as an endlessly good "human being." Besides him, I know Shakyamuni (釋迦) and Jesus (耶蘇) are also good people. Originally, I have ties from receiving baptism in Christianity, but whenever I have time, I like reading the Bible. Buddhist scriptures are also good.

3, Currently—because I am ill, I will have no opportunity for a demise other than dying in bed (臥席終身).

4, Shall I say myself? Haha. I wish it would come a bit later...

5, I want all three. If I had to choose among them, since I am ill, I would like to try becoming "More Youthful" once.

Min T'aewŏn, Chungoe Ilbo

I wish to bathe in the glory of answering your company's ingenious five questions. <9>
1, I have spent the past 10 years as a newspaper reporter, and during that time, I thought I would do my utmost to contribute to the cultural advancement of our society and to guide our nation (abbreviated). However, whether the results of my efforts align with my hopes, I can only await the criticism of third parties with a sense of trepidation (悚懼). As for what I want to do in the next 10 years, I hope to become an even more faithful and excellent soldier on the path I have trodden.

2, The historical figure I admire for his character is Yi Ch'ungmugong (李忠武公); the person I like is Chŏng Sudong (鄭壽銅), who is free-spirited and unconventional (踈放不羇) and holds not a speck of worldly cares (塵累). I admire Duke Yi for being upright, magnanimous, sincere, and capable—a complete person.

3, My personality is too rational (理智的) to commit love suicide or suicide. Whether I will die away from home (客死) or not, no one can foresee except a fortune-teller. Starvation (饑死) is an opportunity that a person with sound limbs cannot easily hope to attain. Whether one will face execution (刑死) or not, it seems rash for someone in our circumstances to declare either way. If nothing special happens, dying in bed (臥席終身) would be the expectation of an ordinary person.

4, If there is someone in Chosŏn who would win the Nobel Prize, it would surely be someone from Samch'ŏlli Company who conceived these five questions. Why, you ask? Because someone who asks such ingenious questions should be called a genius. And if indeed a genius, there is ample possibility of winning the Nobel Prize. And I am confident they possess the generosity to forgive even this joke of mine.

5, Without a second thought, I would choose the love affair with a peerless beauty. You ask why? Because in the modern age, the one qualified to have a love affair with a peerless beauty would certainly be a young gentleman millionaire. Therefore, I am confident that choosing this ensures achieving three things at once without worry.

Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng, Chairperson of Kŭnuhoe

1, I have spent the past 10 years striving to rescue the women of Chosŏn, who are severely oppressed socially, economically, and domestically. Thus, since the formation of the Kŭnuhoe (槿友會), although my strength is lacking, I have devoted myself wholeheartedly and diligently to it. Of course, I leave the evaluation of the results to the critique of others.
Also, for the next 10 years, there are so many things I want to do. Based on the practical experience gained during that time, for the next 10 years, I intend to strive in my work so that life truly has meaning. Therefore, however small, I intend to firmly guard and brilliantly adorn the sector of the women's movement that I am responsible for.

2, Whether male or female, I like people with a strong sense of responsibility who carry out their assigned tasks completely.

3, How can I predict in advance where I will die? Especially in the prime of youth. However, I believe I can answer like this: Love suicide and suicide are things I will absolutely avoid based on my ideology and consciousness. Starvation (饑死) should not be a concern unless a great famine like the one in pre-revolutionary Russia occurs. Also, I don't think I have the fate to die in bed (臥席終身). Looking ahead at our future, where clouds gather and winds blow fiercely, I refrain from stating my death clearly, but in any case, I know it will be some death other than love suicide, suicide, starvation, or dying in bed.

4. Whether it's the Nobel Prize for Literature or Science, I believe it should rightfully be given to a talented person who benefits the people of Chosŏn. For something like the Literature Prize, perhaps someone like Pak Yŏnghŭi might be close.

5, Perhaps because I am greedy, I like becoming "More Virginal" (更處子), becoming a "Millionaire," and having a "Love Affair with a Peerless Handsome Man." Among them, the very best... are still all three: More Virginal, Millionaire, and Love with a Peerless Man.

Kim Tongin

1, What I did in the past 10 years: socially, literary work; <10> personally, the first half was debauchery, the second half was starting an irrigation project and its failure. For the next 10 years: socially, literary work; personally, raising my two children—that's it.

2, A person without secrets.

3, I have never thought about "death." Previously, when I was lying sick in bed and received a misdiagnosis of peritonitis from the doctor, and my friends and relatives had gloomy faces, I inwardly scoffed.

4, If there is a Chosŏn person to receive the Nobel Prize for Literature, it is naturally me.

5, If it were me before bankruptcy, among the three choices of "More Youthful," "Millionaire," and "Love with a Beauty," I would naturally choose the third. Just before and just after bankruptcy, I would choose "Millionaire." Now that I am over 30, I most want to choose "More Youthful." However, having lived a single life for over two years, my entire nervous system has become sharp as a needle's point due to sexual frustration, so never mind a "peerless beauty," I am not without the thought of wanting to try a love life even with an unattractive woman. Taking all three is probably best.

Pae Sŏngnyong

1, I am still in the preparatory stage, anticipating future activities. My past efforts, through books and practical training, merely amounted to accumulating self-cultivation. I believe this situation will continue for a considerable period going forward.

2, I like people who govern their lives, especially their social lives, more with reason (理智) than with emotion (情). Therefore, even if they are not affectionate or personable, I want to associate with people who are trustworthy and have a sense of responsibility.

3, The final death is inevitably dependent on circumstances. I believe it's not something one should have special premonitions or expectations about. Therefore, from a passive viewpoint, I believe I will absolutely not take my own life.

4, I do not know if there is anyone from Chosŏn who has shown outstanding achievements in this field (斯界). Accordingly, I cannot judge who deserves to receive it.

5, I will answer after comparing their merits more closely.

Pak Sŭngbin, Principal of Posŏng Chŏnmun School

1, My life for the past 23 years has been research into the Chosŏn language (朝鮮語學硏究). The next 10 years? No, my life's work is also decided to be that.

2, I like people strong in trustworthiness (信義). And I bow to renowned patriots (志士).

3, Life and death are outside the scope of human intervention; they are solely entrusted to God—this is my view. Therefore, I have neither premonition nor expectation. As a fanciful hope, I most yearn for death in battle (戰死), but it seems distant from me.

4, The Nobel Prize—I lack the knowledge to assess its recipients.

5, This amusing question (please forgive my rudeness)—I will try to score the three desires comparatively.
1, More Youthful: 80 points. 2, Love Affair with a Peerless Beauty: 65 points (worthless rationally). 3, Millionaire: 60 points.

Yi Chŏngsŏp, Chungoe Ilbo

1, What I did in the past 10 years? If forced to answer, about two things: I spent 7 years in France doing my own work, and for the 3 years since returning to Chosŏn, for my part, I spared no effort, to some extent, to steer Chosŏn towards the light. However, it is regrettable that I realize both were rather insignificant. What I will do in the next 10 years? If you state future plans definitively, the ghosts will laugh! Still, there is one thing I definitely intend to do. Time will tell this. For now, it is under a gag order (緘口令).

2, I like creators of epoch-making, ingenious deeds and ideas that ordinary people cannot easily accomplish. To give a few examples: among military men, Napoleon (奈破崙); among politicians, Cromwell, Lenin, Mussolini (뭇소리―니), etc. Among scholars, there are too many figures to enumerate <11>: Kant, Marx, Einstein, Voltaire, Rousseau, etc., etc.

3, I expect to die in bed (臥席終身), but I cannot deny a premonition of dying away from home (客死). Love suicide (情死) is almost absolutely impossible given my personality, I believe. However, being not yet 40, I cannot say absolutely impossible, so I merely softened the tone with the adverb "almost." Starvation (飢死) – I cannot say I won't die of it. Why? Because even someone who could live another 40 years by eating well might not live the full 40 because they eat poorly; that too is a kind of starvation. Execution (刑死) – how could one dare hope for that just by writing newspaper editorials? The possibility of suicide (自殺) is certainly there. Among suicides, suicide by six-shooter (六穴砲 自殺) seems the most ideal.

4. If the Nobel Prize came to Chosŏn, who should rightfully receive it? Are you talking about roughly a hundred years from now, or perhaps a thousand years from now? How could I possibly know? However, at present, I don't see anyone who could receive it.

5, I don't hope to become "More Youthful." Needless to ask why, what would be particularly remarkable about me, originally no genius, becoming younger? If I were a millionaire and a peerlessly handsome man, I would want to have a love affair with a peerless beauty, but lacking these two conditions, it's easy to look pathetic, and who would suffer such embarrassment? Safety first.

Yi Tonhwa, Ch'ŏndogyo

1, Both in the past and for the future, I have decided to dedicate my life to the work of Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎).

2, I like people who are honest, cheerful, and devoted to social work.

3, It seems likely I will die away from home (客死).

4, It has not yet been decided.

5, I have no choice but to select "More Youthful."

Sŏl Ŭisik, Tong-A Ilbo

I reply briefly as follows to each of your questions. I feel very sorry for having to give answers without any preface or elaboration, but as I have not yet risen from my sickbed, I cannot write at length and hope this will suffice to fulfill my obligation.
1, If lacking one year still counts as ten, the past 10 years have been consistently spent in newspaper life. For the next 10 years, unless some setback occurs, I intend to just continue like this, reading books, cultivating myself, and living as a newspaperman.

2, Character-wise, I like people who are affectionate, clean, and honest. Ideologically, I like idealists who accurately grasp reality.

3, It is difficult to answer. As someone who serves parents who feel endlessly sorry for their children growing older year by year, I lack the courage to publicly state premonitions about my own "death."

4, I refrain from naming names. However, for the Literature Prize, rather than setting a standard based on any past or present "Work" (作品), I would like to give it to someone particularly meritorious among the pioneers and builders of our new literature.

5, I choose "More Youthful." This is because I want to retrace the path I have trodden and correct it. (December 7th, from my sickbed at Kyesan)

Hong Myŏnghŭi, Sin'ganhoe

1, The past 10 years were spent wandering in the South Seas (南洋), Shanghai (上海), and Peiping (北平). After returning home, I did some work for the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) and the newspaper company. I refrain from speaking about the next 10 years yet.

2, A talented person with great capacity.

3, I anticipate a certain death, but I don't want to talk about it.

4, I don't know.

5, Shall I gird my waist with 100,000 strings of cash and ride a crane down to Yangzhou?

Sin Sŏgu

1, For the past 10 years, I strove, in my own way, for the Chosŏn Ilbo and for the success of all great things. For the next 10 years, I have many thoughts<12>, but I cannot speak of them yet.

2, A person of great capacity.

3, I don't know.

4, I don't know.

5, All three are good, but among them, the best would seem to be the "Love Affair with a Peerless Beauty."

Hŏ Hŏn, Chairperson of Sin'ganhoe

1, The past 10 years involved the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會), the Chosŏn Bar Association (朝鮮辯護士協會), and Posŏng Chŏnmun School (普成專門學校). For the next 10 years, I want to work centered around the Sin'ganhoe.

2, A politician-type figure.

3, I probably won't die away from home (客死).

4, Seems too early yet.

5, All three are good if you say they're good, and all bad if you say they're bad.

Kim Kijin

1, I have nothing to say in response to the question about the past 10 years. What I will do in the future, only I know. I cannot say it so readily.

2, I like people who are Prudent (愼重), Strict (嚴格), Diligent (勤實), Energetic in Action (力行), Magnanimous (寬大), and Strong-willed (意志). Even if they have the weakness of a weak will, I like people who are trustworthy (信義). I believe your company can imagine the kind of person I like ideologically.

2[sic], Dying away from home (客死)? Execution (刑死)?

4, I don't know.

5, None of the three are my wish.

Chŏng Chongmyŏng, Kŭnuhoe

1, In terms of the number of things done over 10 years, there are quite a few. After the March First Movement (己未運動) occurred, the following year I thought I couldn't remain idle, so first I founded the Women Students' Mutual Aid Society (女子苦學生相助會). Then, I became a founding member of the Pukp'unghoe (北風會), which played an important role in the history of the Chosŏn social movement, and worked there. After that came the founding of the Kŭnuhoe (槿友會), the founding of the Chŏnguhoe (正友會), and I also tried my best, in my own way, for the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會). Besides these, I initiated and organized the first Nurses' Association (看護婦協會) in Chosŏn, lectured throughout the 13 provinces, and often went to prison (lit. 'eat bean rice').
For the next 10 years, I currently have a definite plan (腹案) formulated. I intend to start this probably within a year or two, and personally, I consider it quite a bold (勇斷) undertaking. However, for certain reasons, I will postpone the announcement for the time being, but in any case, it will surely be an act of throwing myself into the Chosŏn movement.

2, I like anyone who sincerely dedicates themselves to the Chosŏn Movement. I like figures who possess solid communist ideology and move with passion and strength on the front lines of the social movement. What use is vast knowledge (宏壯)? Look at all the PhDs (學博士) in Chosŏn. Merely possessing profound knowledge does not make someone I like. Definitely not.

3, Frankly speaking, the impulse of sexual desire hasn't been that strong in recent years, and I don't particularly desire to have children anymore, so a romantic love suicide (情死) probably won't happen. However, when my time comes to die, it certainly won't be dying in bed (臥席終身). And if I am to die, I'll die within Chosŏn.

4, I don't know.

5, I want to become a virgin once.

Kim Ch'angje, Ewha Womans Professional School

1, For the past 10 years, I was engaged in women's education and Christian evangelism (through writing and speaking). In the future, it seems I will continue likewise. (What I want to do is free education and free evangelism).

2, Socrates, Chŏng P'oŭn (鄭圃隱), Yi Yulgok (李栗谷), Kant, George Washington, Abraham Lincoln, Tolstoy, etc.

3, Suicide or love suicide is disallowed by my principles (主義) and faith (信仰), and regarding other <13> deaths, I have no particular thoughts.

4, I wish to leave this question as homework for now.

5, More Youthful.

Chu Yohan

Question 1 (Abridged above) I wish to hold it grandly.
Question 2 A person whose inside and outside are the same.

Yŏm Sangsŏp

1, For the past 10 years, I drank alcohol and wrote novels. Thinking of spending the next 10 years the same way, even I find it pitiful.

2, A vigorous person. An honest person. A passionate person. A person clear about circumstances. (These are character-based)
Whether left or right, a person whose principles and assertions are consistent and clear. (Ideology-based)

3, It seems I'll end up only dying in bed (臥席終身), but that's mundane and boring. Judging by my current situation, I'll surely end up starving to death (飢死). Since I'm too broke (空囊) to take even one step out of Seoul, dying away from home (客死) is impossible. Not being an ideologue, execution (刑死) is also unbecoming of my station. If I try to live and live but cannot, suicide (自殺) is the only option, but being a coward (怯쟁이), I dare not entertain the thought (敢不生心). So, ideally (though calling it fantasy is more accurate), it's love suicide (情死), but women don't even glance sideways at such an ugly man (醜男), so that too is just talk. Therefore, inevitably, I must live forever (長生不死).

4. Playing the hero in my bedsheets—pre-selecting for a Nobel Prize I wasn't even offered seems embarrassing, so I'll stop. But first, let's strive to win the science prize.

5. Since I must inevitably live forever (長生不死), more than money or love, I must first become "More Youthful." If I become "More Youthful," will I be able to earn money? Perhaps, with the taste of youth, at least one woman might fall for me?

Yang Chudong, P'yŏngyang Sungsil Chŏnmun School

1, In the past 10 years, I have done nothing worth mentioning, either personally or socially. As for important things I want to do in the future, first, I want to devote my humble efforts (微力) to the construction of Chosŏn Literature, and second, to the field of education. It's presumptuous, but in short, my thought is to exert even an arm's strength (一臂) for this nation as much as my power allows. Personally, I have never managed anything specific.

2, I like people who are exactly like me, or those who are the complete opposite. However, needless to say, finding people with personalities and ideologies matching these conditions in reality would be very difficult. Generally, I like people with high ideals and integrity, who are truthful and brave, whose intentions are always grand, and who are not hindered by minor details (小節). Historically, I like Zhuge Liang (諸葛亮), who "chanted poems while embracing his knees" (抱膝長吟).

3, Perhaps because I am young, I have not yet thought about death. If I must die unavoidably, any one will do.

4, Asking "If the Nobel Prize came to Chosŏn" makes it very awkward to answer. I hope for the day when I can be asked, "It has been decided that it will come." At that time, I will answer gladly.

5, "Girding my waist with 100,000 strings of cash, Riding a crane down to Yangzhou." Haha.

Yi In, Lawyer

1, For the past 10 years, there is nothing noteworthy to present, but it can be said that suppressing the strong and opposing the weak (抑强排弱) according to the dictates of conscience (良心所使) was the primary stage of my past 10 years. For the next 10 years, if the current situation persists, I intend to devote my humble efforts (微力) to public discourse and education.

2, Sun Yat-sen (孫中山)

3, I anticipate a solemn and dignified suicide (自殺).

4, Dr. Kim Chungse (金重世博士) and Yi Sangch'un (李常春) of Kaesŏng (開城).

5, All three options are certainly tempting, but they are likely beyond my allotted fortune (分福), so shall I just hope for "More Youthful"?<14>

**Summary**

This 1930 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine captures the reflections and aspirations of prominent Korean intellectuals, writers, activists, and educators during the Japanese colonial period. Respondents discuss their past decade's activities (often involving writing, journalism, education, activism, and sometimes imprisonment or illness) and future plans, typically centered on national consciousness, cultural development (especially literature and language), social movements (particularly women's rights), or personal cultivation. The questions delve into personal values (admired figures, desired qualities), hypothetical choices about youth, wealth, and love, and poignant reflections on mortality, revealing a mix of idealism, pragmatism, nationalistic sentiment, and personal anxieties under colonial constraints. For historians, this article offers valuable insights into the diverse mindsets of the Korean elite in 1930: their engagement with modernity, nationalism (both cultural and political), perceptions of global figures and events (like the Nobel Prize), personal struggles, views on social issues, and their varied strategies for navigating life and contributing to society under Japanese rule. The candidness about personal desires, fears (like death predictions), and even internal conflicts (e.g., Yi Kwangsu's illness, Kim Tongin's phases, Yŏm Sangsŏp's self-deprecation) provides a rich tapestry of individual experiences within a shared historical context.

1, 과거 10년 간에 이러타할 일을 한 바가 업습니다. 시간으로 보아 40개월 넘어 囹圄중에, 15개월 넘어 鄕中에 幽閉한 생활을 하엿고 그 다음이 현재하고 잇는 신문사 생활입니다. 벌서 <8> 제7년을 맛는 기자 생활을 근거로 한 생활이 아모 전환될 바 업서 보입니다.
장래에 10년 간은 알 수 업는 그 때까지 이 생활할 것은 어긤 업슬 것이오. 기자로서 收獲이 잇서야 할 일 즉 독서연구로서 沈澱物 건대기를 남기어야 할 일이 하나이오. 그 외의 것은 말부터 압세우는 과오를 나는 아니 범하겟습니다.

3, 自殺은 절대로 업습니다. 情死도 업슬 것을 보장하지오. 그 외에는 전혀 운명에 부처 두십시다.
