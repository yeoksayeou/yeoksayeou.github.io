Activities (Accomplishments) of Modern Women
Yearning for Mary Pickford

By Actress O O-yŏp of S Theatre Troupe

December 17th
Looking at the newspaper, it says the famous American movie stars Douglas Fairbanks and Mary Pickford, on their way to Japan, arrived in Shanghai and are receiving a tremendous welcome. When he went to the welcome reception hosted by fans, seeing there were so many people that he couldn't swim through them, he supposedly lifted his wife lightly with his left hand and ascended the stage...<32>

December 18th
Starting tomorrow, our theatre troupe's third public performance will be held at A Theatre. On the streets of Sŏul, numerous posters bearing my face were put up, perhaps indicating I am the lead actress. People passing by would gather in front of them, whispering something and talking amongst themselves, and somehow my heart started pounding.
In the evening, we had the final rehearsal at that theatre, and I broke out in a sweat because I kept forgetting my lines (쎄리프). On the way back home with the dancers (딴사-) Yŏng-wŏl and Kwi-sun, we went to a café (캅페) and had tea.
Coming home and lying alone in my desolate room, I thought a lot about our theatre movement. But ultimately, it came down to money, actors, and the emergence of excellent writers who can create scripts.

Automobile Conductress

Bus Girl... O Ok-hŭi

December 6th, Clear
In the early morning ride, an old man of about fifty, who said he came up from Ch’unch’ŏn, sat with his legs sprawled out and suddenly asked if I was married. It wasn't the first or second time I'd heard such a thing, so I didn't particularly blush, but it seems the general public still has considerable curiosity about female conductors. I <33> was about to retort, "Why do you ask?" but thinking he was old enough to be my grandfather, I swallowed it down. Without any expression of embarrassment, he remarked,
"My, my—showing her lower legs like that."
He clicked his tongue and turned his head away.
It seems he doesn't know that female beauty is shifting from the face to the bosom, and from the bosom down to the lower legs.
After the old man got off near Hwangŭmjŏng, the driver, Mr. Pak, and I had a good laugh. On the streets, extras are being distributed busily, and students can be seen gathering and moving about. Has something else happened again?

December 7th, Clear
At the Chungno intersection.
"An automobile is a small moving building, a lovely building."
I can't forget that tall Kyŏngsŏng Imperial University student who said this while chatting with the student next to him as he got off. Seeing that student gets off at Chungno every day, maybe his house is near Kongp’yŏng-dong or the hardware bridge? Oh dear, why am I trying to figure out where a man I don't know lives? Ahem, ahem? Shh! Shh.
Speaking of which, looking at examples from places like America, it's said that female conductors on automobiles or streetcars mostly end up marrying the male drivers. In Japan too, supposedly, if a female conductor and male driver who get along well aren't put on the same automobile, many accidents occur?!
However, thinking it over twelve times, I don't feel like falling in <34> love with the current driver (데부 運轉手) of our No. XX bus. Perhaps I'll find such a person among the clingy student types trying to pursue a five-jeon romance?

December 8th, Late Afternoon
Today is a holiday, so the Ch’anggyŏngwŏn line is busy. There are quite a lot of young couples, dressed up nicely, taking their young daughters out.
But for us, there is only a life of labor amidst the dust. Isn't this vigorous? During the Great War in Europe, women put on uniforms, shouldered guns, and went out to the battlefield. Should Korean women spend their days idly like cats always sitting in sunny spots?
Sun-bok, the conductress of bus No. XX, is crying today, saying she got scolded by the supervisor. The supervisor is also a man. Do those sorts know anything about the physiological matters of a woman's body? I comforted the crying girl behind the garage (車庫) with kind words for a while.
We are working women. Working women don't live for emotions like love, as married women do, but work purely for money. So, shouldn't setting aside sentimentalism (센티멘탈리즘) and demanding improvement in our employment contracts (雇傭契約) be immensely more urgent and pressing for us than crying?<35>

**Summary**

The article presents diary entries from two modern Korean working women in 1930. The first, an actress named O O-yŏp, reflects on her admiration for Western movie stars like Mary Pickford, the pressures of her upcoming theatre performance, and the challenges facing the Korean theatre movement, namely the need for funding, talent, and playwrights. The second, a bus conductor named O Ok-hŭi, recounts daily interactions with passengers, including curious and critical remarks about her appearance and profession, contemplates the nature of modern female beauty, muses on workplace romance (contrasting Korean reality with supposed American/Japanese trends), and expresses solidarity with a fellow conductress while advocating for practical improvements in working conditions over emotional responses, asserting a distinct identity as a professional woman working for money. The entries offer glimpses into the lives, thoughts, and aspirations of women entering new public roles in colonial Korea. Historians might find valuable insights into changing gender roles, the perception of modernity (associated with Western figures and new jobs like bus conducting), class interactions (students, older passengers), the conditions and consciousness of early female laborers, and the nascent development of feminist thought focused on economic independence and workplace rights within the context of colonialism.

近代 女性의 活動(績)
憧惊하는 메리, 삑포-드

S劇團女優 吳O葉

12월 17일
신문을 보니 미국의 유명한 영화배우 「따그라스」와 「메리-픽포-드」가 일본오는 길로 上海에 도착하여 굉장한 환영을 밧고 잇는다 한다. 그가 팬들이 부르는 환영회장에 갓슬 때에 너무나 사람이 만허서 헤염처 나갈 수 업는 것을 보고 자긔 안해를 왼편손에 건 듯 치키들고 무대로 오르는 모양을다...<32>

12월 18일
래일부터 A劇場에서 우리의 演劇이 第三次로 公演이 되게 되엇다. 서운의 거리거리에는 主演女優라는 뜻인지 나의 얼골을 박은 포스타-多數가 부치어 잇섯다. 길 가고오든 사람들도 그 압헤 몰려서서 무에라고 수군거리며 저의끼리 이약이할 때 엇전지 가삼이 두군두군하엿다.
저녁에 最后의 연습을 그 극장에서 하엿는대 「쎄리프」가 각금 이저저서 땀을 빼엇다. 「딴사-」인 영월이와 귀순이와 가치 도마 오는 길로 캅페에 가서 차를 마섯다.
집에 와서 쓸쓸한 방에 혼자 드러누으면서 우리의 연극운동에 대하여 만히 생각하여 보앗다. 그러나 결국은 돈과 배우와 각본 쓰는 훌융한 작가의 출현에 잇섯다.

自動車 女車掌

빠스, 껄...吳玉姬

12월 6일 晴,
아츰 차깐에는 春川서 올러왓다는 한 오십된 늙은이가 다리를 버더버리고 안젓다가 불쑥 시집간느냐고 뭇는다. 한두 번 듯는 소리가 아니어서 별로 얼골도 붉히지 안엇스나 女車掌에 對한 好奇心은 아직도 일반이 상당한 모양이다, 나는<33> 엇재 그리소? 하고 反問하려다가 그래도 할아버지벌 되는 이거니 하고 꿀꺽 참엇더니 그는 무안한 表情도 업시
「저런-아랫다리를 저러케 내 놋는담.」
하고 헷가름을 채며 골을 돌닌다.
女性의 美가 얼골에서 가삼으로, 다시 가슴에서 아랫다리로 올마오는 줄 모르시는 모양.
그 老人이 黃金町 附近에서 나린 뒤 運轉手 朴氏와 한참 우섯다. 거리에는 호외가 분주히 돌고 학생들이 몰려가고 오는 것이 보인다. 또 무슨 일이 생기엇나?

12월 7일, 晴
鍾路 네거리에서
「自動車란 움지기는 조고만 「삘딩」이야, 사랑스러운 삘딩이야.」
하며 것헤 學生과 짓거리며 내리는 키다리 그 京城帝大 學生이 이저지지 안는다. 그 學生은 每日 鍾路에서 내리는 것을 보면 公平洞이나 철물교 부근이 집인가. 앗불사 내가 웨 남의 사내 住居를 알려고 이러나. 아예 아예? 쉬! 쉬.
말이 낫스니 말이지만은 美國가튼 곳외 例를 보면 自動車나 電車의 女車掌은 男子 運轉手와 結婚하는 것이 대부분이라 한다. 또 일본서도 그리는데 사이가 조흔 女車掌과 男運轉手를 한 自動車에 태우지 아니하면 事故가 만히 생긴다나?!
그러나 열두번 생각하여 봐도 지금 우리의 第XX號 데부 運轉手와는 러브할<34> 이 나지 안는다. 亦是 五錢짜리 戀愛하려 드는 치근치근한 學生層에 그런 분을 발견하게 되나?

12월 8일 晏
오늘은 공일이 되어 昌慶苑線이 번창하다. 곱게 차리고 어린 딸들을 다리고  가는 젊은 夫婦떼가 퍽으나 만타.
그러나 우리에게는 몬지 속의 勤勞의 生活밧게 업다, 이것이 썩씩하지 아느냐. 歐羅巴 大戰때에는 女子들이 군복입고 총메고 싸움터로 다 나갓다는데 조선 녀자들이라고 밤낫 양지 쪽에 줄고 잇는 고양이 모양으로 못나게 지낼 것일가.
XX號 女車掌 順福이가 오늘 監督에게 꾸지람을 드럿다고 운다. 감독도 사내라. 그까짓 것들이 女子身上의 生理關係를 아나, 車厙 뒤에서 우는 그 애를 한참 조흔 말로 달래 주엇다.
우리는 職業女性인데, 職業女性은 시집간 녀자가치 사랑이라는 감정에 사는 것이 아니라 순전히 돈 때문에 일하는 것이니 우리 압헤서 「센티멘탈리즘」을 떼버리고 雇傭契約의 改善을 바라는 것이 울기보다 멋배나 더 급하고 더 긴급한 일일가.<35>
