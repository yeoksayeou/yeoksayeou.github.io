Shrines of Loyal Vassals and Great Scholars

Where do the spirits of loyal vassals of late Koryŏ like Chŏng P'oŭn (鄭圃隱), scholars renowned in their time like Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn (崔致遠) or Yi Hangbok (李恒福), or famous generals like Yi Sunsin (李舜臣) and Yu Sŏngnyong (柳成龍) wander? Besides these, the traces of many other ancestors who made great contributions to the nation, the people, or culture since the beginning of this land's history are hard to find.

However, fortunately for a few among them, the government of their time officially recognized memorial rites, established shrines (祠院) in the sense of honoring their meritorious service and scholarly virtue and repaying their virtuous influence, and holds memorial services.

Moreover, among these, the academies known as *sŏwŏn* (書院), before the educational system was reformed, served as institutions for educating disciples in the provinces and greatly aided the development of this land's culture.

Now, in this fine autumn season, how about learned scholars, dragging their walking sticks here, paying tribute to these ancestors? We will now record the shrines scattered throughout all of Korea (全鮮).

**Summary**

This 1934 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* lists numerous memorial shrines (*sawŏn* and *sadang*) throughout Korea dedicated to prominent historical figures, including loyal Koryŏ and Chosŏn officials, renowned scholars, and military heroes. It reflects on the difficulty of finding traces of many ancestors but highlights these shrines as places where their legacies are officially honored. The article notes the past role of *sŏwŏn* as educational institutions and encourages readers to visit these sites in autumn to remember these figures. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it was published during the Japanese colonial period. While promoting Korean heritage by listing these traditional sites and figures, it focuses on individuals largely compatible with Confucian values shared across East Asia, rather than figures known for direct resistance against Japan (though some fought Japanese invasions in the past). This careful selection and the act of cataloging national heritage sites could be seen by historians as a subtle form of cultural preservation and potentially quiet nationalism within the constraints of colonial rule.

忠臣, 碩儒의 祠院
高麗末의 忠臣 鄭圃隱이나 學者로 一世에 일홈이 놉든 崔致遠이나 李恒福이나 名將 李舜臣
柳成龍가튼 모든 어룬의 靈魂은 어느 하늘에 뜨고 잇는가. 그밧게도 이 땅의 역사 잇슨 以后, 國家나 民族에나 文化에 貢獻이 큰 先人들의 자최는 차즐 길이 업다.
그러나 그 중에 多幸히 몃몃 분은 그때 政府에서 亨祀를 公認하고 그 勳功과 學德을 追慕하며 德化를 報謝하는 意味로 祠院을 設하며 亨祀하고 잇다.
그리고 이 중에 書院으로 말하면 敎育制度가 改革되기 以前하여는 地方에 잇서서의 弟子敎育機關으로 이 땅 文化의 發展에 도음됨이 만헛섯다.
지금은 情秋好節季, 뜻잇는 선비, 여기에 집행이를 끄으러 先人을 慕追함이 엇더할가. 이제 全鮮各地 흐터저 잇는 祠院을 記하겟노라.
