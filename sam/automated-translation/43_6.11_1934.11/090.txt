Military Horse Supply Department Unggi Branch
In Unggi, North Hamgyŏng Province, there is a branch of the Military Horse Supply Department, established in Taishō 10 (1921). The branch is located in Kwan'gok-dong, its sub-branch is in Sinan-dong, Sinan-myŏn, and its field offices are in Kyŏnghŭng-myŏn, P'unghae-myŏn, Kyŏnghŭng-gun, and Anyang-myŏn, Kyŏngwŏn-gun. It purchases military horses from civilians or raises horses produced by the branch. The grazing land covers 10,000 hectares in Kyŏnghŭng and Kyŏngwŏn. Recently,
the number of horses kept is 531 (as of the end of June, Shōwa 9 [1934]). Approximately 100 horses are produced annually.

Taking this opportunity to outline the Chinhae Naval Station: During the Russo-Japanese War, a temporary naval base defense unit was established at Songjin on Kŏje Island, South Kyŏngsang Province. This was later renamed the Chinhae Defense Unit. Also, the temporary defense unit established in Wŏnsan during the same conflict was later moved to Songjŏn Bay within Yŏnghŭng Bay and renamed the Yŏnghŭng Defense Unit.
In April of Meiji 44 (1911), the coasts of Tsushima and Korea were designated as the 5th Naval District, and Chinhae was made a naval port. However, a Naval Station Headquarters (鎮守府) was not established; instead, the Sasebo Naval Station was tasked with its administration. In April of Meiji 45 (1912), the defense unit at Songjin was relocated to Chinhae.
In April of Taishō 5 (1916), a Naval Port Department (要港部) was established at Chinhae Naval Port, and the Yŏnghŭng Defense Unit was abolished.
The Chinhae Naval Port Department is responsible for the defense and security of the entire Korean coast and the Tsushima Strait, as well as the supply of munitions.
There are fuel storage facilities belonging to the Naval Port Department in Inch'ŏn, Chinnamp'o, and Yŏnghŭng.

Navy Fuel Depot P'yŏngyang Mining Department
Located in Sadong, Taedong County, South P'yŏngan Province, it produces anthracite coal. The coal reserves are abundant, and the quality is also excellent. Currently, the mining area is divided into three coal sections, operating 12 mine entrances, and possessing three briquette machines. Most of the mined coal serves as raw material for briquettes and is transported via the P'yŏngnam Line through Chinnamp'o by sea to the Tokuyama Naval Fuel Depot (德山海軍燃料廠) to meet naval requirements.

An Overview of Police Strength
While we are on the subject, let's look at the police strength of the peninsula.
The total number of police offices in Korea is 2,958:
Police Departments: 13
Police Stations: 251
Police Substations (駐在所): 2,334
Police Boxes (派出所): 197
Police Detachments (出張所): 196
The total number of police officers is 18,065, broken down by rank:
Police Department Directors: 13
Police Superintendents (警視): 57 (including 8 Koreans)
Police Inspectors (警部): 403 (including 70 Koreans)
Assistant Police Inspectors (警部補): 652 (including 108 Koreans)
Police Constables (巡査): 16,943 (including 7,222 Koreans)
Under the current system, the Police Affairs Bureau (警務局) within the Government-General has a Director, administrative officers, engineers, interpreters, clerks, technicians, and interpreter trainees, handling police and sanitation affairs. Provincially, the Provincial Governor oversees this, appointing a provincial administrative officer as the Police Department Director. Below them are Police Superintendents, engineers, port medical officers, Police Inspectors, and Police Constables. Simultaneously, police stations and other agencies are established in each city (府), county (郡), and island (島). The total cost for all this policing amounts to approximately 20 million Yen per year.<48>

**Summary**

This 1934 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* details the Japanese military (Army and Navy) and police presence in colonial Korea. It lists the locations and composition of two army divisions (Najin and Yongsan), naval forces based at Chinhae (including three destroyers), border guards, military police units, and support facilities like a horse supply depot and a naval fuel mine. It also provides a detailed timeline of Korean Army unit mobilizations related to the Manchurian Incident (1931-1933) and statistics on the colonial police force, including the number of stations, personnel by rank (noting the number of Koreans in higher ranks), and the annual budget. For historians, this article provides a contemporary snapshot of the Japanese colonial military and police apparatus in Korea just a few years after the Manchurian Incident, revealing the scale of deployment, specific unit locations, Korean participation levels in the police force, and the direct involvement of troops stationed in Korea in military actions in Manchuria. The detailed list of unit movements during 1931-1933 is particularly valuable for understanding the operational tempo and geographic scope of the Korea Army's role beyond the peninsula.

明治44年 4月 對馬 及 朝鮮의 海岸面을 第5海軍區로 하고 鎭海를 軍港으로 하엿는데 鎭守府를 置하지 안코 佐世保鎭守府를 식혀 此를 管轄케 하고 明治45年 4月, 松眞에 잇는 防備隊를 鎭海에 移轉하엿다.
大正5年 4月 鎭海軍港에 要港部를 置하고 永興防備隊를 廢止하엿다.
鎭海要港部는 朝鮮全海岸 及 對馬海峽의 防禦 及 警備를 掌하고 아울너 軍需品의 配給을 任務로 한다.
仁川, 鎭南浦 及 永興에는 要港部에 屬하는 燃料貯藏場이 잇다.

海軍燃料廠 平壤鑛業部
平南大同郡 寺洞에 잇는데 無煙炭을 産하는 바 炭量 豊饒, 品質 亦 優良하여 現今 鑛區을 三彩炭區에 分하여 坑口12個所를 核行하며 煉炭機三基를 有하고 잇다. 採掘炭의 대부분은 煉炭의 原料로서 平南線에 依하여 鎭南浦를 經하여 海路德山海軍燃料廠에 移送하여 海軍用에 充한다.
