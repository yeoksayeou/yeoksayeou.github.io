Rumors about An To-san (安島山)
Sometime ago, the Ōsaka Mainichi Shinbun reported a rumor that An To-san (安島山), currently incarcerated at Taejŏn Prison, might be released easily before his sentence is up and head straight to America. Regarding this report, a certain close associate of his stated:
"The talk of him going to America is entirely unexpected and something I hear for the first time now. Also, the claim that his thoughts and feelings have changed from before is something I hear for the first time, and I cannot judge it well. Originally, didn't the Korean edition of the Ōsaka Mainichi write erroneous articles even back during the days of the Singanhoe (新幹會), which became an issue at the executive committee meeting, leading to the dispatch of an inquiry committee to have the erroneous article corrected? It's difficult to guarantee that newspaper articles always carry only accurate information, so perhaps the article about To-san is also of that kind. Furthermore, To-san's release date is November of next year." <14>

Property Ownership of Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎)
The magnificent Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) church soaring into the clouds in Kyŏngun-dong, Seoul, is an exceptionally outstanding building that never fails to amaze all foreigners visiting Korea for the first time, such as British and Americans. This was all built during the era of the third patriarch, Son Pyŏnghŭi (孫秉熙). However, there are rumors circulating that a portion of the church building and land has been pledged to a certain bank. But according to what our Secret Room has reliably investigated, such a thing absolutely did not happen, and both the land and building are completely preserved. The land is registered under the name of the old faction leader, Pak Yŏnho (朴演浩), and the building is said to be under the joint names of Messrs. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Kwŏn Tongjin (權東鎭), and others.
Furthermore, the market value of the land and building is said to correspond to approximately 100,000 wŏn.

Rapidly Increasing Large Buildings
High-rise buildings of reinforced concrete, brick, etc., proudly show off day by day, stacking up their magnificent forms, creating "streets of steel" everywhere in Seoul. Among those being constructed by Korean hands, the Chōsen Ilbo's 5-story edifice on T'aep'yŏng-t'ong is progressing steadily with a budget of 200,000 wŏn and is expected to be completed this December; the steel frame has already reached the second floor above ground. Mr. Min Kyusik's (閔奎植氏) Yŏngbo Building, a 5-story large construction next to the YMCA building on Chongno 2-chōme, already has its exterior completed and is busy with interior decoration, so it should open its doors within this year. Moving a little further west towards Sŏdaemun, across from the Hwasin Department Store, the Han Ch'ŏng Building construction, costing 80,000 wŏn, seems about to finish digging the basement foundation, and the steel frame for the above-ground floors will soon rise. Besides these, the Savings Bank building being constructed in front of the Bank of Chōsen plaza at a great cost of 680,000 wŏn has already raised its steel frame up to the 7th floor and is now busy adding the flesh [walls/cladding]; with a total floor area of 2,700 p'yŏng, completion is set for June next year. Right behind it, the Central Telephone Exchange, being built as a 4-story reinforced concrete structure, also has its exterior mostly finished but will be fully completed in June next year. Simultaneously, the old "Hello, hello" style telephones will be eliminated, and 5,000 automatic telephones will be installed throughout the city, it is said.
Entering Honmachi-dōri (本町通), the 4-story "Honmachi Hotel" building is set for completion in November, and the 4-story Ōzawa Clock Shop is also expected to be finished in November. Besides these, the Pumin'gwan (府民舘 - Civic Hall) on T'aep'yŏng-t'ong is slated for completion in December next year, the Chōsen Fire and Marine Insurance Company building in September next year, and the <17> Christian Young Men's Association Hall in Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町) now has its exterior completed and is expected to be finished this November.
In addition to these, a large apartment building with several hundred rooms is said to be under construction in Naeja-dong, so it seems the streets of Seoul will suddenly be filled brightly with modern large buildings soaring into the clouds.

200 Million Tons Kimhae Iron Mine
A few days ago, the Government-General Department Heads' meeting made a very important decision. It concerns the recent discovery of an exceptionally large iron mine in Kimhae, South Kyŏngsang Province, with estimated reserves of about 200 million tons. As a result of dispatching engineers from the Mining Affairs Section for an on-site investigation, it was indeed found to be extremely promising. Therefore, it was decided to conduct a major prospecting operation over two months, employing 4,000 laborers at 180 locations. If indeed 200 million [Error: likely meant 200 million, not 20 thousand] tons are buried there, along with Mitsubishi's iron mine in Musan, North Hamgyŏng, it will become not only famous in Korea but also one of the world's foremost iron mines.

Commencement of Large Iron Mine Development
The discovery of a major iron mine in Musan, North Hamgyŏng, to be excavated by the Mitsubishi Company <19> has already been reported multiple times. With the impending state of emergency [Likely referring to rising international tensions], meaning the mine should be opened quickly day by day, engineers Dr. Fukuda Ren (福田連), Doctor of Engineering, and Mr. Nabeshima Tomotoshi (鍋島朝俊) from the Kenjiho (兼二浦) Smelter recently arrived from Mitsubishi Company's Tokyo headquarters, bringing all necessary surveying equipment. They have begun on-site investigations across the Musan area. In any case, with reserves of 500 million tons and world-class ore quality, the excavation of this iron mine seems likely to be realized in the very near future.
