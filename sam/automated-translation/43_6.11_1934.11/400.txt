Magazine Title: 삼천리 제6권 제11호
Publication Date: 1934년 11월 01일
Article Title: 祈願(외 7편) (Prayer (and 7 other poems))
Author: 素月 (Sowŏl)
Article Type: 시 (Poetry)

Prayer
						Sowŏl
Prayer
Along that main road, a person slowly walks towards me, leisurely
White shirt, white trousers. A stiffly starched towel hanging around the neck
Just seeing them approach, I used to wonder who, who could it be
But that 'perhaps' is gone now, ah, why have I become like this!

Tennis matches on coming holidays, Debate Club on half-holiday nights
Swimming in summer saying sweat drips because it's hot, mountain climbing in the freezing cold winter,
Nights of ghost story gatherings (試膽會), returning home terrified after listening only to scary stories!
Curiosity and courage are gone now. Ah, why have I become like this!

Wanting to follow the neon signs that whisper well in the night of Ginza in Tōkyō, the Orient,
Thinking I must buy a pair of comfortable summer shoes to wear somehow
Money left after working, sweating, saving it for a friend or letting it go
Amusement and willingness are gone now. Ah, why have I become like this! <202>

With a brush and Raiong (라이옹) beside me, carrying soap (사봉) in a basin
Stepping out to the backyard, look! The morning moon over the well-house roof. Washing the body clean, clean,
Kneeling properly, closing eyes, praying and praying, until the sun rises
Take back all those wishes into my heart! Ah, why have I become like this!

Healthy Sleep
The fresh sun, with a face as if washed,
Shines upon the quiet street in the mountains.
On a body straightened from the spring morning bed
Dressing in light clothes and strolling in the field
The refreshing wind that penetrates the skin feels so good.
Afraid of stepping
On the pointy grass sprouts
When placing the feet down gently,
Memories of the past 10 years are vivid in my head, and today's rewarding plans are clearly set.
O, the sleep of last night, where mind and body were both refreshed.

Refreshing Morning
This house, as if placed out on a vast field
Or this rain, unsure where it came from or how it arrived overnight. <203>
Spring has come even to the newly opened land, the fine raindrops
Moisten the faint young sprouts of the willow on the bank,
And sprinkle the green onion field of a humble house on the wild plain.
Magpie flocks nesting in the thorny bushes behind chatter noisily
Ducks and chickens sit facing each other by the stream, preening their feathers.
This vast field, with no cultivated flowers growing, no bindweed,
Will perhaps nameless wildflowers bloom in this rain?
Without the magnificent ocean waves, or the subtle undulations of hills
Just a vast field, being as it is, existing as it is!
But I do not despair, that this land is desolate now,
I think, once again, as the cool raindrops strike my face,
Of the future that will only be here, after many transformations
This land will become beautiful in our hands! Will become beautiful!

Change of Mood
Sweat, sweat, sweating under the summer sun
Even while riding the furrows with a hoe in hand,
From somewhere, only the lark sings,
The vast sky is visible, visible.

Love, love, love, for the lover met in the twilight <204>
Waiting, wondering if they'll come, deeply anxious through the young night,
The river water flows beneath the bridge I stand upon!
The dawn light pools in the river water, pools.

Opportunity

The bridge was laid over the river!
While I hesitated, not crossing,
The rough waves of "Time" without a moment's notice
Demolished the bridge and flowed away.

When you, who crossed first, called so much
To come quickly, why could I not go!
You and I are just on this side and that side.
Only weeping and longing from time to time.

Hometown
Animals may not know it as hometown
Humans cannot forget it, the hometown
Something not even thought of when awake
In sleep, it is suddenly the hometown <205>

The place where ancestors' bones are buried
The place where I played with calf-friends
Perhaps because of that, I don't know, but
Ah, in dreams, it is always the hometown

2
In spring, everywhere the sound of mountain birds
Azaleas and wildflowers in full bloom
In autumn, the valleys dyed with crimson maples
Floating down on the flowing spring water

Looking out, the sky and the sea water
Where they gradually meet, touch
The shadow of a fishing boat's sail
The sound of 'eogiyeotcha diyeotcha' seems audible

3
If I am a wandering body
The hometown becomes the reason
Memories of parents, thoughts of siblings
Even in dreams, I always see them there <206>

Is the hometown in the heart?
The hometown is also in the heart
Is my soul in the hometown?
My soul is also in the hometown

Because it's in the heart, I see it in dreams
I long for the hometown seen in dreams
Because my soul is there, I go even in dreams
I long for the hometown I go to in dreams

4
A stem of duckweed drifted away on the waves
No time to settle down
Will there ever be a day to return to its place!
In this painful sea, this world, being human, I shall return

Those who say they have forgotten their hometown
Those who say it's the hometown that abandoned me
At least when you die, don't wander the ends of the earth
If you have a soul, go back to your hometown <207>

Suffering and Joy
The traveler carrying a heavy burden
Should not look only at the rugged path underfoot
But sometimes lift their head to the mountains and rivers all around
And gaze upon the refreshing scenery of the world

The sweetness and bitterness of food depend on the mouth
The suffering and joy of honor and disgrace also depend on the mind
Look, even when the sun sets, the moon rises
If the moonless night brings bad weather, rest and go

The traveler carrying a heavy burden
Should not lament the steep pass, saying it's breathless
But sometimes relax the mind and think
Of the smooth, wide road that might also exist

Comfort can be the seed of hardship
Bitterness becomes the seed of joy <203>
Look, even slash-and-burn fields, if plowed and planted
Yield heaps of golden grain in autumn

Life is like dancing on a knife's edge, they say
That wicked girl who threw herself into the water
Perhaps it seems plausible in a way, but
Why did she not know it isn't so?

Life is like dancing on a knife's edge, they say
She herself danced on the knife's edge
Who told her to dance that mad dance?
How twisted a girl she was

Truly buying her own hardship
Having nowhere else to hold onto, she complains
The traveler carrying a heavy burden
Should rest in the green grass field by the roadside

The traveler carrying a heavy burden
Should not look only at the rugged path underfoot <209>
But sometimes look at the changing world too
The spring, summer, autumn, winter, mountains and rivers all around

Should I cast off this heavy burden?
Should I not walk this painful road?
The traveler carrying a heavy burden
Look, see a single drop of water in the stream

Though just a single drop of water, if gathered and flowing
It flows on to become the waves of the sea
It rises to the sky to become clouds
It falls to earth again to become rain

When the water fallen as rain gathers together
In the mountains, it's a waterfall, hydroelectric power
In the fields, it's irrigation, ten thousand bushels of grain (萬鍾石)
For the parched, burning land, it is oil

When a lovely flower branch begins to wither
It becomes cool night dew and moistens it <210>
When a lonely traveler's heart is anxious
It becomes a cool spring by the roadside and relieves them

Even an insignificant drop of water in the stream
If its flowing purpose is like this
How could any single human life, just being itself
Complain that this road is too rugged?

There is meaning in this burden's weight
There is meaning in this burden's pain
The person carrying a heavy burden
Is the truly human person in this world

Righteousness and Sense of Justice
What is mere money, and what is position? (地位, 爵祿)
Whether they exist after death, one cannot know, but
It is said there is something definitely right and wrong in this world

It is not greed, nor is it for vanity <211>
That feeling of needing to do something, even after death!
Along with what is definitively wrong, I know there is also something that must be done

Whether it succeeds or fails is not the concern
From the start, I knew not what would happen to this body
Doing as that heart commands is what it means to be human, they say

2
How could that soul, if it exists, possibly emerge (出)?
Who would know that such conduct, said not to exist, could arise?
Surely, when others didn't know, I weep for that heart I also didn't know

Oh peach and plum blossoms visible in the fine spring of ninety days
Do not laugh at that pine tree in the weeds for not knowing the season
How could you know the great tree, unchanging (如一) for a thousand years? <212>
<202-212>

**Summary**

This collection of eight poems by Sowŏl explores themes of loss, nostalgia, endurance, and hope. "Prayer" laments a lost sense of wonder and vitality. "Healthy Sleep" and "Refreshing Morning" find solace and hope in nature and the potential for future renewal, even amidst desolation. "Change of Mood" juxtaposes hardship with natural beauty and anxious love with enduring nature. "Opportunity" reflects on missed chances and separation. "Hometown" is a powerful expression of deep longing and connection to one's origins, inescapable even in dreams. "Suffering and Joy" advocates for resilience, finding meaning in hardship, and perspective, comparing life's struggles to the continuous cycle of nature. "Righteousness and Sense of Justice" asserts the importance of inner conviction and acting according to principle, regardless of outcome or recognition. From a colonial history perspective, the poems are significant for their poignant expression of loss and longing ("Hometown," "Opportunity," "Prayer"), which resonates with the experience of cultural and national displacement. The emphasis on endurance ("Suffering and Joy"), inner strength ("Righteousness"), and hope for future renewal ("Refreshing Morning") could be interpreted as subtle forms of resistance or coping mechanisms under colonial rule. The mention of Tōkyō ("Prayer") also grounds the poems in the reality of Japanese influence.

祈願
						素月
祈願
져 행길을 사람하나 차츰 걸어온다, 너풋너풋
흰 적삼 흰 바지다. 빩한 줄센 하올 목에 걸고
오는 것만 보고라도 누군고 누군고 관심하든
그 행여나 인저는 업다, 아아 내가 왜 이럿케 되엿노!

오는 공일날 테니쓰 시아이, 반공일날 밤은 雄辯會
떠워서 땀이 쫄쫄난다고 녀름날 水泳 춥지추운 겨울登山,
그 무섭은 니야기만 골나가면 듯고는 위야 집으로 도라오는 試膽會의 밤!
好奇도 勇氣도 인저는 업다. 아아 내가 왜 이럿케 되엿노!

東洋 도-교-의 긴자는 밤의 귀ㅅ속잘하는 네온싸인 눈띄 좃차 가고 십퍼,
아무럿케라도 해서 발편하고 볼씨잇는 녀름신 한 커레 사야만 된다
벌어서 땀 흘니고 남은 돈, 그만이나, 친구 위해 앗기우고 말든
우끼끼도 선뜻도 인저는 업다, 아아 내가 왜 이럿케 되엿노!＜202＞

켭에는 부랏슈와 라이옹, 대야에 사봉 담아 들고
뒷뜰을 나서면, 져봐! 움물집옹에 새벽달. 몸 깨끗시 깨끗시 씻고,
端正히 꾸러안자 눈감고 빌고 빌든, 해뜨도록
그 비난수를 내 마음에 다 도로 줍소사! 아아 내가 왜 이럿케 되엿노!

健康한 잠
삭냥한 太陽이 씨슨 듯한 얼굴로
山 속 고요한 거리우흘 쓴다.
봄아츰 자리에서 가주닐은 다는 몸에
흣것을 걸치고 들에 나가 거닐면
산뜨시 살에 숨는 바람이 좃키도 하다.
뾰쥭뾰쥭한 풀엄을
밟는가바 저어
발도 사분히 가려 노흘 때,
過去의 10年 記憶은 머리속에 鮮明하고 오늘날의 보람 만흔 計劃이 확실히 선다.
마음과 몸이 아울너 유쾌한 간밤의 잠이어.

爽快한 아츰
무연한 벌우헤 드러다 노흔 듯한 이 집
또는 밤새에 어듸서 어뜨케 왓는지 아지 못할 이 비.＜203＞
新開地에도 봄은 와서, 간열픈 빗줄은
뚝까의 아슴푸러한 개버들 어린엄도 축이고,
난벌에 파릇한 누집 파밧테도 뿌린다.
뒷가시나무밧테 깃드린 까치떼 죠화 짓거리고
개굴까에서 오리와 닭이 마주 안자 깃을 다듬는다.
무연한 이 벌, 심거서 자라는 꼿도 업고 멧꼿도 업고
이 비에 장차 일홈몰을 들꼿치나 필는지?
壯快한 바닷물결, 또는 丘陵의 微妙한 起伏도 업시
