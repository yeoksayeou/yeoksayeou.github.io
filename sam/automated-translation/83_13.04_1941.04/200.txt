Article Title: 上海의 朝鮮人女子大學敎授, 滬江大學에 계신 趙東善孃 訪問記 (An Interview with Miss Cho Tong-sŏn, a Korean Female University Professor in Shanghai at Hujiang University)
Author: 上海特派員 朴巨影 (Shanghai Special Correspondent Pak Kŏ-yŏng)
Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Interview/Discussion)

An Interview with Miss Cho Tong-sŏn, a Korean Female University Professor in Shanghai at Hujiang University
Shanghai Special Correspondent Pak Kŏ-yŏng

To find Miss Cho Tong-sŏn, I knew I had to cross a river from where I was, but I wasn't sure which direction to head, so I got into a yellow rickshaw (人力車) holding the card with her address.

After a 40-minute rickshaw ride, the house I finally found was not as luxurious as I had imagined. (This is because I had heard previously that her father had possessed enormous wealth, and that Miss Tong-sŏn was managing the inheritance after her father passed away some time ago.)

After knocking twice, Miss Tong-sŏn herself came out to greet me, not the housemaid (阿馬). I was immediately guided into the reception room, where several foreign children seemed to be in the middle of a piano lesson; a child holding sheet music, a child just standing by the piano, a child with hands on the piano keys – all had stopped due to the unexpected intruder, looking just like figures in a painting on the wall.

After moistening my throat with cocoa the young lady made herself, I apologized for interrupting her work, and then began the conversation to fulfill the purpose of my visit.

"Hearing you speak, you must be busy with the inherited business (元昌公司) since your father passed away..."

"Well, there's nothing particularly busy about it."

"So, how many family members do you have?" <128>

"Just my mother."

"It must be very lonely. He was someone who should have stayed longer..."

The young lady's father is Mr. Cho Sang-hyŏp. Everyone among Shanghai's prominent figures knows that he left a great legacy in Shanghai society through his multifaceted work over 20 years. Not only through his work, but as a pastor (牧師), his character and spirit had many points that surpassed others, earning him the reverence of young men.

The young lady, seemingly reminiscing about her father, wore a sad expression and remained silent. Far from speaking, it seemed tears might fall at any moment, and even the air in the room grew heavy, making it hard to breathe.

At that moment, the child who had placed her hands on the piano began to play. It was an unconscious act, but I was immensely grateful to that child for breaking the heavy atmosphere. As if rescued, I quickly changed the subject.

"Miss Tong-sŏn, which schools did you attend?"

"I studied at Insŏng Elementary School (仁成學校), and then Ch'ŏngsan Middle School (靑山中學) and Hujiang University (滬江大學), graduating from the Education and Music departments."

"And were you immediately invited to be a professor at Hujiang University?"

"At first, I was a lecturer in the Music Department, but starting this semester, I received the official position of teacher."

"I heard that upon graduation, you were invited by an American women's organization and performed as the graduation representative, receiving much praise (讚辭) from foreigners. Please tell us about the emotions of that time."

"It just feels like a dream, because it's in the past." <129>

"This may be very rude, but as a Korean woman, how do you find the experience of being a teacher at a foreign school?"

"It's interesting, but the thought of wanting to work once in the Korean mainland (朝鮮內地) arises from time to time."

She turns her bright eyes, which look like they could spark fire at any moment, towards the window. She seems to be around 28 or 29 years old. She is still unmarried.

"Do you have any thoughts of establishing a music school in the future? In Shanghai, I mean."

"Actually, I am always thinking about that. Since Shanghai is such an important zone (重要地帶) in the East (東洋), I believe it would be very good. It would also be wonderful if famous musicians traveling between East and West performed whenever they pass through Shanghai. It might seem like a daydream, but if we could establish a music school with our own strength! Besides Western music, of course, I would especially like to include a department for Eastern music, and among that, ancient Korean music. If that happens, I would be the first one to learn it, before anyone else."

"You have a lot of interest in Korean music?"

"Well, yes. I want to learn something about that field in the future."

"The topic has changed. Please tell us your views on marriage?"

"How should I put it?"

She smiles faintly.

"Do you favor companionate marriage (友愛結婚), love marriage (戀愛結婚), or perhaps arranged marriage (見合結婚)?"

"Well, in a way, all three you mentioned seem justifiable, but then again, I think they all have their pros and cons (一長一短)."

"Forgive my bluntness, but regarding your own marriage problem, Miss, which side would you lean towards?"

"It's difficult to say anything suddenly. I'll tell you when the right opportunity arises."

As she deftly avoided the question, I changed the subject again.

"Wouldn't you like to hold a solo recital?"

"If I had the ability, it would be nice, but..."

"Why? Everyone was waiting expectantly when it was said you'd make a special appearance at Ms. Kim Sŏng-hŭi's recent recital, but we were greatly disappointed when you couldn't make it." <130>

"The expectations for me are too high; my shoulders feel heavy."

"Changing the subject again, but how is it? The world of Korean women in Shanghai... speaking generally, about their attitude towards life (生活態度) and the issue of culture/refinement (敎養問題)."

"Although I've lived in Shanghai for nearly 20 years, the path I've walked has all been student life (學窓生活), and right after that, I started working at the school, so I'm a complete blank slate (백지입니다) in that area. I know a few people, but can they represent the entire world of Korean women?"

"Generally speaking, aren't the Korean women in Shanghai qualitatively lacking?"

"Well... that's rather unpleasant to hear. In my opinion, women everywhere tend to have quite a bit of vanity (虛榮心), so if they're not careful, they easily forget their circumstances (處地) or status (身分) and indulge in excessive luxury (사치). If the Korean women in Shanghai belong to that type (類), it would be very unfortunate (不幸하겠습니다). I hope there are many who strive for a spiritual life as much as possible."

"Just one last word. I heard from a certain woman that the Korean men in Shanghai are 'worthless fellows.' Do you really think so?"

"Well... I'm not defending the Korean men in Shanghai, but aren't they rather purer/more naive (純眞하지) than young men elsewhere? They may have many other special points (特點), but isn't this purity essential for character cultivation (人格涵養)?"

The clock struck five, and the sunlight that had lingered on the window tilted away, so I bid the young lady farewell and left. <131>

**Summary**

This article is an interview conducted in Shanghai in 1941 by Pak Kŏ-yŏng, a correspondent for the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*, with Cho Tong-sŏn, a young, unmarried Korean woman teaching music at Hujiang University. Cho discusses her background, her teaching position, her memories of her recently deceased father (a prominent Korean pastor and businessman in Shanghai), her aspirations to establish a music school featuring ancient Korean music, and her views on marriage, the Korean community (both women and men) in Shanghai, and her desire to eventually work in Korea. Historically, the article offers a valuable glimpse into the life and thoughts of an educated, elite Korean woman living abroad in Shanghai during the late colonial period. Her comments on establishing a Korean music school, her nuanced views on marriage types, and her defense of Korean men in Shanghai against criticism provide insights into cultural identity, social norms, and community dynamics among Koreans in diaspora at that time.

上海의 朝鮮人女子大學敎授, 滬江大學에 계신 趙東善孃 訪問記
						上海特派員 朴巨影
趙東善孃을 찾으려면 나 있는데서 江 하나를 건너야 된다는 것까지는 알지만 어느 방향을 찾어야 할지 잘 몰라서 주소 적은 카-드를 든 채 黃色車(人力車)에 올라탔다.
黃色車로 40분이 걸려 겨우 찾어 낸 孃의 집은 내가 상상하든 것같이 화려하지 못 했다. (나는 일즉이 그 父親이 巨萬의 財産을 갖었든 분이라는 것, 그런데 그 父親이 얼마 전에 別世하셔서 그 遺財를 東善孃이 정리한다는 소문을 들엇기 때문이다.)
두 번 노크에 阿馬(下女)가 안나오고 東善孃이 親히 나와 맞어 준다. 곧 응접실에 안내되였는데 거기엔 異人아이들의 몇이 피아노 敎授받는 中이였든 모양으로 樂譜든 아이, 피아노 옆에 거저 서있는 아이, 피아노건반에 손을 얹은 아이, 모도가 不意의 侵入者로 해서 정지를 하고 있는 터라 똑 壁에 붙은 그림같았다.
孃의 손수 맨든 코코아로 목을 적신 후 나는 業務妨害를 해서 罪悚하단 인사말을 하고, 그리고 나선 내가 띠고 간 用務를 達하고저 이야기를 시작했다.
「참 말슴드르니 아버님이 도라가신 후 遺業(元昌公司)에 바쁘시다든데...」
「뭐 별루 바쁠 것까진 없습니다.」
「그래 가족은 몇 분이나 되시지요?」＜128＞
「한 분 어머님뿐입니다」
「매우 적적하시겠습니다. 더 오래 계서야 할 분이신데...」
孃의 父親은 趙尙燮氏다. 벌서 20여년을 上海에서 多角的으로 上海社會에 남겨놓은 업적이 크시였다는 것을 上海의 人士는 누구나 다 아는 바이다. 업적으로 뿐 아니라 牧師로서 性格과 그 정신이 뭇사람을 뛰여난 점이 많아서 젊은 청년들의 崇拜를 받어 왔든 것이다.
孃은 아버님을 追憶하는 양으로 얼굴에 슬픔을 띠우고 말이 없다. 말은 커녕, 금방 눈물이래도 흘릴 듯 해서 室內의 공기조차 숨이 갑부도록 가라앉았다.
이럴 때 피아노에 손을 얹었든 아이가 피아노를 울리기 시작한다. 그는 無意識하게 한 일이지만 묵어운 공기를 깨트려 준 그 아이가 한없이 고마웠다. 나는 救援이나 받은 듯 얼른 화제를 돌렸다.
「東善氏께선 어디어디 學校를 나오셨습니까」
「小學校는 仁成學校에서 배왔고 그 다음엔 靑山中學과 滬江大學 敎育科와 音樂科를 나왔습니다.」
「그리군 곧 滬江大學敎授로 초빙되였습니까?」
「처음엔 音樂科 講師로 있었는데 이번 學期부터 아주 敎師란 직책을 받게 됐죠」
「卒業 때 米國婦人團體에 招待받어 卒業代表루 出演하서셔 外人들께 많은 讚辭를 받으셨다는 데 그때의 감격을 말슴해 주십시요.」
「거저 꿈같습니다. 지나간 일이기 때문에」＜129＞
「대단 실례올시다마는 朝鮮女性으로서 外國人學校에 선생 노릇 하시는 재미가 어떴습니까」
「재미있어요, 하지만 朝鮮內地에 가서 한번 일해 봤으면 하는 생각이 때때로 남니다.」
그는 불이래도 금방 퉈여나올 듯이 총명한 눈을 창가에 말린다. 나이는 28, 9歲가량 될 듯하다. 아직 未婚이다.
「앞으로 音樂學校 하나 세워보실 생각없습니까. 上海에 말슴입니다」
「그렇지 않어도 늘 그런 생각을 하구 있는 데요. 上海가 東洋에서는 重要地帶이니만큼 퍽 좋으리라 믿습니다. 東西洋으로 來往하는 유명한 音樂家들도 上海를 통과할 때마다 연주하면 훌륭할 것입니다. 夢想같지만 우리 힘으로! 音樂學校를 설립케 된다면 洋樂도 물론이려니와 특히 東洋音樂, 그 중에도 朝鮮古代音樂科를 뒀으면 합니다. 그렇게 된다면 위선 누구보다도 제가 제일 먼저 배우겠어요,」
