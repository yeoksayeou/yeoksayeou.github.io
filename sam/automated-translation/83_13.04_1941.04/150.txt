**Body Text Translation:**
Watching Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi's Tokyo Performance
						Kondō Kōtarō

If the established practice for returning travelers until now has been to spend half a year or a year peeking at the West, then make a big show of their homecoming souvenirs and blow an air of being over there, then Ch'oe, who went abroad and yet showed works with a higher degree of Eastern ethnic character (東洋民族性) than before, is certainly intelligent and a woman in tune with the times (時勢). Moreover, while these things are easy to say but hard to do, Ch'oe executes them well. Since her return from abroad, the mainland (內地 - i.e., Japan) has welcomed the Korean Music and Drama Troupe (朝鮮樂劇團) and Han Sŏng-jun, and its appreciation for the peninsula's folk dance has rapidly advanced. Therefore, had it been mere lip service, the superficiality of Ch'oe's folk dance would likely have been quickly seen through. That she did not betray our expectations is worthy of being called great. Particularly praiseworthy is how she eliminated the flaw of rambling redundancy that clings to Korean music and utilized it well, and how she took folk dance and turned it into a brilliant stage art capable of being presented on the grand stage of the Kabuki-za.

While equally displaying a strong ethnic character, her works show three tendencies. First are the remnants of the Ishii Baku-style Western dance in which she grew up. This appeared only in "Sword Dance" and in the *taryŏng* within "Three Rhythms." And this, though only one instance, is the greatest failure. Korean dance, when forced into that format, only makes one keenly feel the lack of artistic intent. Second are works that incorporate the existing ethnic rhythms of Korean dance as they are. These include "Two Folk Dances," "Dance of the Hwarang," "Three Traditional Rhythms," "Boy Bridegroom," and "Impromptu Dance." Many adopt folk songs (俗曲) and are truly delightful, overflowing with a unique Korean humor, and are the most successful.

All skillfully select accents and syncopation (シンロペイシヨン), and "Three Rhythms" and "Impromptu Dance," which made particularly effective use of pianissimo (ビアニシモ), were incomparably charming. The third category includes works like "Okcho karyakcho (玉苗調)," "Body is Old, Spirit is Not Old (身老心不老)," "Form of the Long Sleeve (長袖の形式)," "Pohyŏn (普賢)," and "Kwanŭm (觀音)," where Ch'oe seems to have applied her own choreography to old Korean music (古曲) rather than relying on the existing ethnic rhythms found in the second group; these, as might be expected, do not achieve the results of the second group. Among these, "Form of the Long Sleeve" was the most beautiful, achieving a kinetic rhythm (動律) similar to the old Korean "Monk's Dance" (僧舞). However, in many cases, Ch'oe relies too much on pursuing movement and form alone, thereby losing the rhythm and forfeiting the lingering resonance (余韻). Especially, "Spirit is Not Old (心不老)" is merely an explanation of forms, while "Pohyŏn" and "Kwanŭm" move too much, smell of butter (パタ臭く - i.e., are too Westernized), and lack Eastern emotional appeal (東洋的情味). However, the very incompleteness (未完成) of this group represents Ch'oe's future prospects (前途), her future, and indeed, her hope. Besides these three types, the successful "Mask Dance" best fused Korean dance and percussion instruments (打樂器). During the brief change of rhythm (變調子) within the dance, I wished she had further discarded the movement aspect. Also, "Oriental Melody" incorporated techniques from Indian dance into Korean delicacy (朝鮮的纖細) and utilized the delicately beautiful (纖麗) sound of the *haikin* (拜琴), making it exceedingly beautiful.

**Summary**

The author, Kondō Kōtarō, reviews Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi's Tokyo performance, praising her for returning from the West with works emphasizing "Eastern ethnic character," specifically Korean elements, which he deems intelligent and timely. He contrasts this positively with performers who merely mimic Western styles. Kondō categorizes her dances into three groups: residual Western influences (criticized as failures), successful adaptations of traditional Korean folk rhythms (highly praised for charm and humor), and less successful attempts to choreograph new works to old Korean music (seen as lacking rhythm and emotional depth but holding future potential). He particularly commends pieces like "Mask Dance" and "Oriental Melody" for effectively blending Korean elements with other influences (percussion, Indian technique). For historians, the article is revealing in its explicit framing of Ch'oe's work within the context of "Eastern ethnic character" and being "in tune with the times," reflecting the wartime Japanese state's promotion of a pan-Asian identity (under Japanese leadership). The critique of Westernization ("bata-kusaku") and the specific evaluation criteria applied to Korean dance by a Japanese critic offer insights into cultural politics and aesthetic judgments within the empire.

崔承喜の東京公演を見る
						近藤孝太郞
半年か一年西洋をのぞいて來ても、歸朝土產を呼び物に出して、あちら風を吹きまくるのが、まづ今までの歸朝者の定石とすれば、洋行して來て却つて前より東洋民族性の高い作品を見せた崔は、確かに聰明であり、かつ時勢にかなふ女性だ。そしてそれらのこともいふは易く行ふは難いのに、崔はそれをよく實行してゐる。彼女が洋行後、內地は朝鮮樂劇團を迎へ韓成俊を迎へて、半島の民族舞踊に對してはその味覺を急速に進めてゐるから，それが單なる呼聲だけであつたなら崔の民族舞踊は忽ち底を見すかされるであらうところを、よくわれ等の期待を裏切らなかつたのは、偉とするに足る。特に賞し得ることは、冗漫性のつき纒ふ朝鮮音樂の缺陷を去つてよく活かしたことと民族舞踊を提げてよく歌舞伎座の大舞に乘せ得る華やかな舞臺藝術としたことである。
齊しく民族性を濃く出してゐながら、その作品の中には三つの傾向を見せてゐる。一は彼女が生ひたつた石井漠的洋舞の殘滓である。それは唯一つ「劒の舞」および「三つのリズム」中のタリオンに現はれてゐた。そしてこれは唯一つといはなくも最大の失敗作である。
朝鮮舞踊はあの形式に盛つては意欲の不足を痛感させるだけだ。第二は旣存の朝鮮舞踊の持つ民族的律動をそのまま持ち込んだ作品だ。「二つの俗舞」「花郎の踊り」「三つの傳統的リズム」「少年花婿」「即興舞」である。多く俗曲を採り、獨得な朝鮮的ユーモアの溢れた實に樂しいものであり、最も成功したものだ。
いづれもアクセントとシンロペイシヨンを巧に撰み、殊にビアニシモを有効に利かせた「三つのリズム」や「即興舞」は比類なく魅力的であつた。第三は朝鮮古曲に對して第二群種に旣存民族的律動によらず、崔自身が振を與へたらしい作品「玉苗調」「身老心不老」「長袖の形式」「普賢」「觀音」等の作品は、さすがに第二群種の成果は擧げてゐない。この中では「長袖の形式」が最も美しく、朝鮮古踊の「僧舞」に似た動律を得てゐた。しかし多くの場合崔は動きと形のみで追求することにたより過ぎて、却つてリズムを逸し余韻を失くしてゐる。殊に「心不老」は形の說明に過ぎ「普賢」と「觀音」は動き過ぎてパタ臭く東洋的情味かない。しかしこの一群の未完成こそ崔の前途であり、未來でありかつ希望でもある。その三種のほか成功したのは「假面舞」は朝鮮舞踊と打樂器とを最もよく融合させてゐた。舞中でちよつと入る變調子の時、動きの方をさらに棄てて欲しかつた。また「東洋的旋律」は朝鮮的纖細の中にインド舞踊の技術を盛り込み、纖麗な拜琴の音を活用した頗る美しいものであつた。＜106＞
