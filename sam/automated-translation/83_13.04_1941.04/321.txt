**Body Text Translation:**

Why Do We Get Sick?

Everyone experiences that maintaining peace of mind is, above all, important for preserving health.
Therefore, since ancient times, it has been said that "illness arises from the mind" and "illness enters through the mouth."
These words certainly grasp one aspect of the truth, and in times past, when disciplines like pathology or etiology were not developed, they were believable words for anyone, but they are not insignificant even from the perspective of modern medicine.
Looking briefly at the history of Western medicine here, Hippocrates of Greece (希臘), known as the father of modern medicine from 2,400 years ago, posited that there are four main fluids in the human body: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile [■ - likely 膽, meaning bile, completing 黑膽, black bile]. He established a kind of humoral pathology, stating that each fluid has its unique properties, and the proper mixture of these fluids creates a state of health, while abnormalities in the fluid combinations cause illness.

Cells Seen Through a Microscope

In contrast to this, Asclepiades of Rome (羅馬) stated that the cause of disease lies in changes in the size, number, position, and movement of atoms. Various other theories were vigorously proposed, such as it being due to an unhealthy soul or deviations from mechanical laws, but the one who made the final determination was the famous Dr. Rudolf Virchow (루드루푸ㆍ우일효) of Germany (獨逸). According to the Doctor's theory, "Any illness, whatever it may be, arises from the weakening of cells." So, what exactly are cells? <190>

Symptoms and the Root of Illness

Whether it's muscle, stomach, intestine, bone, or brain, if you take a piece and view it under a microscope, you can see it is organized into small bodies like the illustration on the right [Note: Illustration not provided in text].
These small bodies are the cells (細胞), and all organs of the body are organized by various kinds of cells in various forms. The blood that nourishes these is also composed of red and white blood cells and other cells.
Next, explaining the relationship between cells and disease using the example of gastrointestinal illness, conditions like hyperacidity syndrome—where the pit of the stomach hurts on an empty stomach, one feels heartburn, and sour fluid fills the mouth—occur because the stomach acid-secreting cells of the stomach wall have become abnormal, and their function is pathologically overactive.
If left untreated, because the stomach acid is excessive, the mucosal cells become rough, eventually breaking down in part, leading to a stomach ulcer (胃潰瘍) with bleeding. Conversely, if the function of the stomach acid-secreting cells atrophies, it causes hypoacidity syndrome, the symptoms of which closely resemble hyperacidity syndrome, requiring caution.
Gastroptosis and gastric atony—where ingested food doesn't digest well and appetite drops sharply—are conditions, like gastric dilation, where the muscle cells of the stomach have relaxed and lost their power of contraction. If the muscle cells of the intestine enter a similar state as the stomach, it results in habitual constipation.
Indigestion or gastrointestinal catarrh may have direct causes like overeating or chilling the stomach, but the stomach and intestines themselves invariably weaken starting from their tissue cells, leading to pathological changes.

The Harm of Symptomatic Drugs

Now, let's consider the effect of folk remedies commonly used—that is, taking baking soda (重曹) if the stomach is upset, bitter agents (苦味劑) if there's no appetite, laxatives (下劑) for constipation, or anti-diarrheals (下痢止) for diarrhea. Baking soda only has the effect of neutralizing stomach acid, so while taking it makes the stomach feel comfortable temporarily, it has no effect on the cells undergoing pathological changes, so once the drug's effect wears off, the condition returns to how it was before taking it.
Similarly, bitter agents or laxatives provide stimulation, and anti-diarrheals absorb moisture or reduce inflammation, merely suppressing symptoms temporarily. Not only is their effect temporary in every case, but using baking soda for hypoacidity can actually worsen the condition. Such adverse reactions or side effects, including habituation, highlight the considerable drawbacks of symptomatic chemical preparations that must be used differently according to the symptoms.
Completely different in effect, providing revitalizing energy to the protoplasm of the cells and fundamentally restoring the stomach and intestines themselves, is the Cell Activation Therapy (細胞賦活療法) newly pioneered by the famous Hefe (헤-풰) bacteria preparation. Among cell activation therapy drugs, the one acknowledged for its powerful and superior effect is the most advanced complex Hefe bacteria preparation, "Wakamoto" (와까모드).
Taking this always maintains the appropriate secretion of digestive fluids, activates the peristaltic movement of the stomach and intestines, and ensures the healthy operation of food digestion, nutrient absorption, and waste excretion. During this process, there is absolutely no strain, and importantly, because the main effect of the drug reaches the foundation of the illness, it is remarkably effective for the various aforementioned gastrointestinal diseases with just this one preparation, and there are absolutely no dangerous side effects or habituation.

Comprehensive Nutritional Supplementation

Furthermore, "Wakamoto" performs astonishing nutritional therapy concurrently with cell activation therapy.
Specifically, as nutrients, it is rich in the Vitamin B complex (비타민B複合體)—recently recognized for its significant effects on beriberi, appetite stimulation, fatigue recovery, growth promotion, etc.—as well as amino acids (아미노酸) for preventing weakness, the blood-building element nuclein (누구레인) and iron (鐵分), calcium (카류슘) necessary for deposition at sites of tuberculosis onset, Vitamin A and D complex (비타민AD體) to enhance resistance to disease, and glycogen (구리고-겐), the source of physical energy. Therefore, "Wakamoto" (available at all pharmacies in Korea [全鮮藥店有]) is widely used for chronic debilitating diseases such as kidney disease, diabetes, pneumonia, pulmonary tuberculosis, pleurisy, beriberi, neurasthenia, etc. There are many actual cases where patients have recovered from chronic conditions to become even healthier than before their illness. <191>

**Summary**

This 1941 article by Dr. Okabe Shō argues that illness fundamentally stems from cellular weakness, critiquing temporary symptomatic treatments like baking soda or laxatives. It promotes "Cell Activation Therapy," specifically advocating for the Japanese product "Wakamoto." Wakamoto is presented as a complex Hefe (yeast) bacteria preparation that revitalizes cells, regulates digestion, and provides comprehensive nutritional support with Vitamin B complex, amino acids, minerals, and glycogen, thus treating the root cause of chronic gastrointestinal and debilitating diseases without side effects. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it showcases the promotion of a Japanese commercial product within colonized Korea, using modern scientific language (cellular pathology, vitamins) attributed to a Japanese medical authority. It reflects the intersection of Japanese commercial interests, the dissemination of Western medical concepts adapted through Japan, and health marketing strategies during the colonial period.
