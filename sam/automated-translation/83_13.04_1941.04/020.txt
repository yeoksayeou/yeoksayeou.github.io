A Message from High Officials of the Government-General to the People of Chōsen under Wartime Conditions: The Path Forward for Merchants and Industrialists and the Low Price Policy
						Asakawa Tōshaku (Former Name: Han Tong-sŏk), Administrative Official, Commerce and Industry Section, Government-General of Chōsen

I. The Path Forward for Merchants and Industrialists

Reporter - Please tell us about the future path for merchants and industrialists.

Administrative Official Asakawa, Commerce and Industry Section - In terms of industrial distribution, commerce has a greater number and influence than industry. However, looking at the future situation, restructuring the system from being commerce-based to production-based is seen as the most meaningful path, both from a national policy perspective and for the survival of the merchants and industrialists themselves. That is, to take a step forward from the commercial spirit of buying and selling goods manufactured by others, to manufacturing goods oneself.

To make this new start from a commerce-based system to a production-based one, the major issue that follows is, above all, materials. Although materials may decrease due to the emphasis on production (生産重點主義), and although general businesses might worry about not seeing smooth distribution of materials (材料配給), it has not reached the point where one should abandon hope for production. Looking at the commercial and industrial world of Chōsen, there seem to be some business owners who are discouraged by the material problem, even fearing they might have to close down. However, the government adopts the production-first policy to guarantee the people's livelihood, and since this emphasis means not manufacturing anything other than daily necessities (生活必需品), the impact is considered small in Chōsen, where the manufacture of luxury goods (奢侈品等屬) and the like is limited. Of course, in the Mainland (內地), there are many varied industries, and consequently, a considerable number of manufacturers of luxury goods other than daily necessities. Under the emphasis on livelihood, those unable to receive material allocations are facing circumstances where they must consider changing businesses (轉業) or closing down. However, compared to the Mainland, Chōsen has relatively few industries, and even those that exist, as mentioned earlier, are almost entirely manufacturers of daily necessities, <35> so apart from seeing the closure of a very small number of luxury goods manufacturers, we likely won't see many sacrifices.

For that reason, if one focuses on and strives to manufacture daily necessities, materials can be secured. Furthermore, due to railway conditions and other various factors, Mainland goods will not be able to enter Chōsen in large quantities as before, so the movement to expand production capacity (生産力擴充運動) is seen as even more advantageous. In this respect, I urge the merchants and industrialists within Chōsen to reconsider.

II. Countermeasures Regarding Material Distribution

Reporter - Small and medium-sized producers are facing extreme difficulty in receiving material distributions. Please tell us about the countermeasures for this.

Administrative Official Han, Commerce and Industry Section - Given the current various circumstances, it seems very impossible for small-scale producers (弱少生産業者) to proceed independently. Thus, the issue of enterprise consolidation (企業合同) arises. This enterprise consolidation movement can be seen as a relief measure for small and medium-sized producers in receiving material allocations. Why is that? Because it is very difficult for individual producers to receive material distributions, but if 5 people group together, or 10 people group together, to form a limited liability company (有限會社), or organize an industrial cooperative (工業小組合) and join the association as a group, there should be no difficulty at all in receiving material distributions. For example, obtaining distributions for one or two sewing machines or sock knitting machines is difficult, but it becomes possible for a cooperative or company. Therefore, the consolidation of enterprises is, above all, one of the most urgent matters (喫緊事) for small-scale producers.

III. Regarding Relief Measures for Small and Medium-Sized Merchants and Industrialists

Reporter - Please share your thoughts on relief measures for small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists.

Administrative Official Han, Commerce and Industry Section - The difficulties faced by small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists under the current situation (時局下) are one of the very big problems, and the authorities (當局) are also striving for countermeasures. As I mentioned earlier, the only way to rescue small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists within Chōsen is thought to be organizing legal associations (組合). There seems to be no other way than to organize commercial associations (商業組合), industrial associations (工業組合), or small commercial cooperatives (商業小組合), small industrial cooperatives (工業小組合), and encompass the small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists within those associations. Business owners themselves should also adopt "prioritizing the public good" (公益優先) as the slogan for commerce, rather than solely pursuing profit (利潤追求).

IV. Regarding the Low Price Policy

Reporter - Next, please tell us about the low price policy (低物價政策).

Administrative Official Han, Commerce and Cndustry Section - Everyone knows about the low price policy, so I will avoid lengthy words, but <36> although there are some concerns that production capacity might decrease due to the low price policy, this low price policy, being the national policy, will be firmly maintained. In my opinion, I do not think that production will stagnate due to the low price policy. I believe the low price policy will be achieved through, first, the reduction of production costs (生産費의 減下), second, the rationalization of management methods (經營方法의 合理化), and third, striving for the improvement of technology (技術의 向上).

V. Regarding the Issue of Business Conversion

Reporter - Recently, the issue of business conversion (轉業問題) has been causing significant ripples in the commercial and industrial world. Please tell us about the countermeasures for this issue.

Administrative Official Han, Commerce and Industry Section - Discontinuing a business one has been running, especially one passed down for two or three generations, is very unfortunate for the business owner themselves, and even from the nation's standpoint, one cannot help but feel sympathy. However, compared to the Mainland (內地), those in Chōsen who absolutely must convert their businesses are extremely few in number, so this issue of business conversion is not considered something to be made a major issue of within Chōsen. As mentioned above, compared to the Mainland, there are few manufacturers of luxury goods other than daily necessities, and consequently, the number of people needing to convert businesses will naturally be small. I believe the most appropriate course of action is to put more research into the business one currently runs, make further improvements where needed, and strive to continue the business one already has. <37>

**Summary**

This 1941 interview features Asakawa Tōshaku (Han Tong-sŏk), a Korean official in the Government-General's Commerce and Industry Section, outlining the colonial government's economic policy for Korean merchants and industrialists under wartime conditions. He urges a shift from commerce to manufacturing, focusing on daily necessities due to material shortages caused by the "production-first policy." He advises small producers facing difficulties obtaining materials to form cooperatives or companies (enterprise consolidation) to facilitate allocation. The official reassures that the low price policy will be maintained through cost reduction, management rationalization, and technological improvement, and downplays the need for widespread business conversion in Korea compared to Japan, arguing most Korean businesses already produce essential goods. The article reflects the increasing state control over the economy, the push for industrial mobilization aligned with Japan's war effort, and the strategies proposed (or imposed) for businesses to survive under rationing and regulation.

Historians might find the article particularly interesting for several reasons. It explicitly shows a Korean official articulating and justifying Japanese colonial economic policy during wartime, including the potentially unpopular low price policy and material rationing. The mention of the author's dual name (Asakawa/Han) directly reflects the Sōshi-kaimei policy. Furthermore, the comparison drawn between the economic situation in Korea (Chōsen) and the Japanese mainland (Naichi) highlights the colonial hierarchy and the differing industrial structures, while simultaneously attempting to minimize the negative impact of wartime policies on Korean businesses compared to their Japanese counterparts. The emphasis on forming associations (組合) reveals the state's preference for managing the economy through controlled, collective bodies rather than individual enterprises.

一. 商工業者의 今後의 活路
記者 - 商工業者의 今後의 進路에 대해서 말씀해 주시기를 바랍니다.
朝川商工課事務官 - 産業配分上으로 보면, 商業과 工業에 있어서 상업은 공업보다 그 수도 많고, 세력도 많습니다. 그러나 今後의 정세로 보아서는 商業本位에서 生産本位로 그 體制  를 새로이 함이 國策的 見地로서나 혹은 商工業者 自體의 活路를 위해서 가장 有意義한     일이라고 봅니다. 즉, 從來 남이 제조한 상품을 매매하던 商業精神에서 일보 更進하여 자    신이 상품을 제조하자는 것입니다.
이렇게 商業本位에서 生産本位로 新發足을 갖기 위해서는 무엇보다 재료가 뒤따르는 큰     문제인데, 生産重點主義에 의해서 재료가 감소된다고 해도, 그리고 一般業者들은 材料配給  의 圓滑을 볼 수 없지 않을까 해도, 생산에 대한 희망을 버릴 정도에 이른 것은 아닙니다.  朝鮮의 商工界를 살펴보면 왕왕 材料問題로 落心하고 심지어 폐업하게까지 되지 않을까     미리부터 공포를 품는 업자도 없지 않은 모양이나 정부는 국민생활을 보장하기 위해서 生  産重點主義를 취하는 것이며, 重點主義이란 生活必需品 以外의 것은 제조하지 않는다는    것이기 때문에, 奢侈品等屬의 商品製造가 적은 朝鮮에서는 그 타격이 적다고 봅니다. 물론   內地에는 여러 가지 공업이 많아서, 그에 따라 生活必需品 以外의 奢侈品等屬製造業者도    상당히 많은 관계로 生活重點主義 밑에서 材料配給을 받지 못하는 그들은 轉業이나 폐업    을 각오해야 할 형편에 이르게 되나, 朝鮮에는 內地에 비하여 비교적 공업이 적고, 또 있    다해도 아까 말한 바와 같이 擧皆가 生活必需品製造業者이여서,＜35＞ 극히 소수의 奢侈品     製造業者의 폐업을 보는 외엔 희생자를 볼 수 없을 것입니다.
그러한 관계로, 生活必需品製造에 유의하여 노력한다면 재료도 확보할 수 있으며, 따라서    今後로는 鐵道關係, 其他 諸條件으로 內地商品이 전과 같이 朝鮮內로 많이 나올 수 없기    때문에 生産力擴充運動은 더욱 유리하다고 봅니다. 이 점에 있어서 朝鮮內의 商工業者의    再反省을 促하는 바입니다.

二. 材料配給에 關한 對策
記者 - 中小生産業者들이 무엇보다 材料配給을 받기가 극히 곤란한 형편에 있는데 그 대책  에 대해서 말씀해 주시기를 바랍니다.
韓商工課事務官 - 현재의 諸情勢로 보아 弱少生産業者는 각각 독립해서 나아가기는 매우   불가능하다고 보는데, 이에 企業의 合同問題가 대두케 됩니다. 이 企業合同運動은 中小生    産業者들이 材料配合받는데 있어서 救濟策이라고도 할 수 있다고 봅니다. 그것은 웨 그런  고 하니 生産業者 개인개인으로써는 材料의 배급을 받기가 매우 곤란하지만,  5人이면 5    人이, 10人이면 10人이 各各 합동해서 有限會社를 조직한다든가, 혹은 工業小組合을 조직    하여 조합에 단체적 가입을 한다면 재료배급을 받기에 도모지 곤란이 없을 것입니다. 例    컨대, 裁縫機械 양말기계같은 것도 1, 2대의 배급은 어려우나 조합이나 회사일 때는 배급    받게 됩니다. 그러므로 기업의 합동화가 무엇보다 弱少生産業者들에게 있어서 喫緊事의     하나일 것입니다.

三. 中小商工業者의 救濟策에 對하여
記者 - 中小商工業者의 救濟策에 대해서 선생께서 평소에 생각하시던 바를 들려주시기를   바랍니다.
韓商工課事務官 - 時局下의 中小商工業者의 곤란이란 매우 큰 문제의 하나로, 當局도 그 대  책에 腐心하고 있는데, 中小商工業者를 救濟하는 방책은 역시 아까도 말씀 드렸지만, 朝鮮   안에서는 법적 조합을 조직하는 것이 유일의 방안이라 생각합니다. 商業組合, 工業組合,     혹은 商業小組合, 工業小組合을 조직하여서 그 조합에 中小商工業者를 포용하는 외에는     별도리가 없다고 보는데, 업자 자신도 利潤追求만을 목적하지 말고 公益優先이라는 것을    商業의 슬로간으로 삼아야 할 것입니다.

四. 低物價政策에 對하여
記者 - 다음으로 低物價政策에 대해서 말씀해 주십시오.

記者 - 近者에 轉業問題가 商工界에 매우 큰 파문을 던지고 있는데, 이 문제의 대책에 대해 서 말씀해 주시기를 바랍니다.
