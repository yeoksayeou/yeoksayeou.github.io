**Body Text Translation:**
Ren's Lament, Wind and Waves
						Mo Yun-suk

A salty wind seeps in from some shore. Thin clothes flutter with the wind. Ren spent yesterday's dusk in Simŏn's arms. Simŏn trembled with a terrible fever, like a laborer standing in Hell. Ren immersed herself in Simŏn's eyes and was happy as a child. On the lonely path, over the pass curved like a bow, the two walked evenly along the evening road, drinking in the starlight facing each other. Simŏn did not show Ren the fear of a teacher. He did not recite the Bible. Ren held onto Simŏn's sleeve like a young girl, waiting for a fairy tale. Until they crossed the pass, Ren thought the village of sky, stars, and wind would continue all night. They were shadows walking eternity, stepping on wet snow through gaps in the half-melted slush. To Ren, human dwellings are the enemy. Among human dwellings, there are greedy ones who covet Simŏn's scent. Simŏn long ago became a master among human dwellings, a man of custom. Ren sometimes feels suffocated by the customs Simŏn possesses. Last night, around eight o'clock, Simŏn and Ren could not send their bright and kind hearts further away and crashed against the waves. It was when they went near a certain window from which a red light bloomed that a woman's voice calling Simŏn was heard. As soon as Simŏn heard the woman's voice, he raised his hand in a glad greeting and went into that house. Ren fell away like the wind and walked alone.

Night of March 6th!
Simŏn! Where does this scent of fresh greens permeate from so strongly? The sound of the lukewarm spring brings tears.
Simŏn! Did you get home safely? As I sent you off into the warm light and clean affection, I was endlessly happy. I was blissful. The world unfolding for you as I left you <235> was a scene of life I had despised until now, but I couldn't deny a moment of extreme longing that surged last night. Simŏn! The paper door cries in the gentle wind, and the wind flits around the lamp.
I lie still, thrown down, looking up at the ceiling, not knowing what I am thinking or what the content of my spirit is.
Simŏn! It is now nine-thirty. Your children must be asleep. It is the time when your endlessly gentle breathing drifts over the sleep of the children and your beloved wife. Doesn't your breath seep even this far, becoming wings of comfort?
Although I sometimes blur your face visible in my field of vision with tears, when I see your smiling face in your warm home, my heart too pounds with joy. But Simŏn! The fact that another woman besides me is by your side, being your comforter—though a fact I have known for a long time—presses upon my turbulent psyche like a newly discovered matter, and I cannot control it. Why can't this heart be calm? As you seem split into terrifying phantoms or some inscrutable jester, I have lost the Simŏn I enshrined in my heart. And all I see is my own pitiful face, my weak life.
Simŏn! When you abandon me and sit under the same roof with your wife, exchanging stories, Ren becomes your worthless object of ridicule.
Simŏn! Knowing all this, when I turn over, tired, drowsiness creeps in softly. The portrait of Ave Maria hanging on the wall looks farther away, and below it flows a white river. And beside that river, isn't Simŏn, who is always seen, standing there? I abruptly get up and bang my body against the wall. And then I follow you, standing beside that white river, walking somewhere, endlessly.
Simŏn! You wipe away my tears. And you hold me in your two arms. Tears pour out even more, soaking your hands and clothes. <236>
On the path, shepherd's purse, wild chives; in the rock crevices, azalea buds are bursting open.
Simŏn! Holding onto you, I told you I had a nightmare, that you had an extremely kind and lovely partner who was mine, that I was left alone, and so I returned to my empty room and cried in bed.
Simŏn touches my tear-streaked cheek and says—"There is no one in this world I love more than Ren. There is no one in this universe who can comfort me more than Ren." Walking somewhere, endlessly walking with Simŏn, I buried my head deeper into Simŏn's chest and cried.
And I pleaded with Simŏn to go on a road where there are no human dwellings, no villages visible, and moreover, where that lovely wife who shatters my heart completely does not exist. Simŏn stroked my hair, saying he would do so. Let's walk a path no one has walked before, leaving only Ren's and Simŏn's footprints, footprints that will never be erased, I said. Hot tears constantly flowed from me because Simŏn's arms wrapped more forcefully around my waist, and his eyes shone more intensely, enduring great sorrow. I didn't want to let Simŏn go again; I was afraid someone might recognize Simŏn's voice. Even if Simŏn fell ill, I intended not to ask anyone for a prescription but to use my blood and my flesh lovingly as medicine. If Simŏn were to leave my side without reason again like last evening—if such a thing were to happen one more time—I felt I would surely collapse on the road, so I grasped Simŏn's clothes even more tightly with my hands. I don't know where it was, but there was a stream, and jade-colored birds flew back and forth. The thought that Simŏn's face seemed entirely mystical was because the time I had lost him, even for a moment, was so heartbreakingly sorrowful that I captured every bit of the skin of longing, which is the expression of that spirit. The fact that Simŏn had a lovely wife was a heartbreaking worry that wouldn't easily leave my mind, no matter how far we walked. The fact that Simŏn had children was acceptable to some extent, but occasionally, <237> when, without realizing it, a phrase like "my wife is such and such" appeared in his conversation, my heart ached. Somewhere there, without any restraint, I could talk to Simŏn and speak out the anguish I couldn't resolve while keeping it inside. I pulled Simŏn, saying let's not go back the way we came, but sit here and wait for someone who might come down to the village to sell boiled water chestnuts or acorns, and have a bite to eat. Simŏn hesitated, then looked back again at the distant mountain behind us, said the baby at home was sick and he had to go see, and turned back. I remained alone on the rock.

Simŏn! Waking up from sleep, the window is bright. The white river flowing beneath the Maria portrait was also an illusion, and the path I walked weeping with you was all a road not of this world.
The sound of water carriers is heard, and the old woman in the room across makes sounds of washing dishes in the kitchen.
Simŏn! I feel I can no longer meet you now. Because it doesn't seem likely that you, who are thousands of miles away, will meet me again with a new countenance. In the dream, I could freely tell you everything in my heart, but in this hour, which is not a dream, I cannot dare to think you are affectionate or trustworthy.
Simŏn! I am a reality standing alone in an empty world. If only there were a place to stand, even under a seemingly collapsing dead tree, I would want to trade this agonizing, tearful moment.
Noon has passed. Mr. Hyemyŏng (慧明氏), who often visits, came again today. Why did his voice, which usually sounded meaningless following his tone, sound welcome today? I was pleased to often hear Simŏn's theories from him and discussions close to Simŏn's view of life, but besides that, there were times when he considered me a valuable companion. Hyemyŏng is a young man with a slender physique who is said to have taken a liking to Western philosophy (西洋道學) and studied it for a long time. When he speaks or laughs, he doesn't show elastic expressions, which <238> I thought was because he lost his youth due to the energy spent reading books (冊읽는). Hyemyŏng's work is to rest his body and study (工夫) in a quiet inn just over the back hill here. He used to visit several times, talk about the subject he was studying for about 30 minutes, and then leave, but today he talked for an hour and suggested we go for a walk as the evening sun was nice.
Simŏn! Hyemyŏng's world of research slightly attracted my heart. This was because it had much in common with the ideals of life mentioned in your words, Simŏn. From Hyemyŏng, I gained the opportunity to repeatedly hear interpretations of your difficult words. Since Hyemyŏng is a person without constraints, no matter how long we sat and talked, no one interrupted. However, what saddened me even during the walk was that once the theory ended, he felt no interest or inspiration, and occasionally when he asked about my life or my plans for the future, anger flared up, turning my face pale. Today again, I abandoned etiquette, said "Please don't ask such things," and sent him away disappointed. Because I dislike conversing with him outside of scholarly discussions. When he tries to touch upon some psychological world within me, fierce anger surges. I myself don't know why. I only need him as an interlocutor for Simŏn's sake. Once before, he sent a potted mountain chrysanthemum he had painstakingly raised indoors all winter, but I returned it without even looking at the note written there. At that time too, my psychology was such that I disliked keeping anything in this room other than things meant for Simŏn. Yet, he acted as if such a return was something to be expected, visiting again plainly, unfolding his stories. He is a young man equipped with both theory and dignity, but it seems he lacks the vitality (生命性) that you, Simŏn, possess. Returning after parting from him, I felt empty, as if I had just touched a cold corpse. Still, I don't know why I was able to walk with him for so long. -Continued- <239>

**Summary**

This excerpt from Mo Yun-suk's novel "Ren's Lament, Wind and Waves" portrays the inner turmoil of the narrator, Ren, entangled in a painful love affair with Simŏn, a married man. Through a mix of recounting recent events and a dream sequence presented as a letter or diary entry to Simŏn, Ren expresses her conflicting feelings of love, intense jealousy towards Simŏn's wife, despair over her situation, and a desperate longing for an exclusive, idealized relationship free from societal constraints ("human dwellings"). Her interaction with another intellectual, Hyemyŏng, highlights her fixation on Simŏn, as she only values Hyemyŏng as a conduit to understanding Simŏn's ideas, rejecting any personal intrusion.

From a historical perspective, the story offers insight into the emotional landscape and societal pressures faced by modern, educated women in colonial Korea. Ren's struggle reflects the tension between traditional expectations (Simŏn as a "man of custom," married with children) and modern desires for individual romantic fulfillment, albeit outside societal norms. The intellectual discussions and Ren's complex psychological state provide a window into the concerns and expressions of the Korean intelligentsia during this period, navigating personal lives amidst broader colonial realities.

『어느 기슭에선가 짠 바람이 슴여 온다. 엷은 옷이 바람과 같이 動謠한다. 렌은 시몬의 가슴에서 어제 黃昏을 지냈다. 시몬은 地獄에 선 人夫같이 무서운 열에 떨고 있었다. 렌은 시몬의 눈속에 몸을 잠그고 애기처럼 즐거웠다. 외따른 길 활등같이 굽은 고개에 둘이는 맞오뵈는 별빛을 마시며 저녁 길을 가즈런히 걸었다. 시몬은 렌에게 스승의 두려움을 보이지 않었다. 聖書를 외이지도 않었다. 렌은 시몬의 옷자락을 붓들고 少女처럼 童話를 기다렸다. 렌은 고개를 넘을 때까지 그저 하늘과 별과 바람의 마을이 밤새껏 게속되는 줄 알었다. 녹다 버린 눈석이 틈으로 저즌 눈을 발등에 업으며 永遠을 것는 그림자들이였다. 렌에게 人家는 敵이다. 人家 中에는 시몬의 내음새를 탐내는 욕심쟁이들이 있다. 시몬은 오래전에 벌서 人家 中에 한 主人이 되여버린 風俗人이다. 렌은 시몬이 갖인 풍속에서 이따금 질식을 느긴다. 어제밤 여덜시쯤 되었을 때 시몬과 렌은 그 명랑하고 善良한 마음씨를 더-멀니 노아 보내지 못하고 파도에 부딋첬다. 그것은 불이 연붉에 픠여 나오는 어느 창문 갔가히 갔을 때 시몬을 부르는 女子의 목소리가 들였다. 시몬은 그 여자의 목소리를 듯자 손을 드러 반가운 인사를 보낸 후 그 집으로 드러갔다. 렌은 바람처럼 떠러저 혼자 걸었다.』

3월 6일밤!
시몬! 어데서 푸성귀 내음새가 이처럼 배여듬니까? 微溫의 봄소리가 눈물을 자아냅니다.
시몬! 안녕히 들어가섰습니까? 따뜻한 불빛과 깨끗한 情 겨움속으로 나는 당신을 보내면서 끗없이 즐거웠나이다. 幸福하였나이다. 내가 당신을 떠남으로＜235＞ 당신에게 展開되는 世界는 지금까지 내가 경멸하던 人生의 한폭 畵面이었으나 어제밤에 極度로 솟는 동경의 한 순간을 否定할 수는 없었나이다. 시몬! 문풍지가 가는 바람에 울고 등불 주위로 바람이 산듯 거립니다.
나는 무엇을 생각고 있는지 내 精神의 內容이 무엔지도 모르고 그저 天井을 처다보고 고요한 채 던저 있습니다.
시몬! 지금은 아홉시 반 당신의 애기들은 잠이 들었을 것입니다. 한없이 부드러운 당신의 숨소리가 애기와 그리고 당신의 사랑하는 안해의 睡眠 우에 떠돌 때입니다. 이렇게 머-ㄹ니까지 당신의 숨소리는 安慰의 나래가 되어 슴여들지 않음니까?
나는 눈물로 내 視野에 보이는 당신의 얼굴을 흐리게도 합니다만은 따뜻한 家庭에서 있는 당신의 우슴진 얼굴이 보일 때는 제 가슴도 기꺼움에 뛰고 있습니다. 그러나 시몬! 나 아닌 다른 女子가 당신의 곁에서 당신의 慰安者가 되어 있다는 사실이- 비록 오래전부터 알고 있은 사실이나-새로 발견된 일처럼 저는 울렁거리는 심리를 누를 길이 없습니다. 이 마음이 웨 이리 평정치를 못합니까? 당신이 分裂되어 무서운 幻影으로 또는 해석할 수 없는 어떤 道化役者로도 생각되는 바람에 제 마음에 모시던 시몬을 잃어버리고 말었습니다. 그리고 보이는 것은 내 자신의 가련한 얼굴, 약한 生命이 보일 뿐입니다.
시몬! 저를 버리고 당신의 안해와 함께 한 집웅 밑에 앉어 이야기를 주고 받을 때는 렌은 하잘 것 없는 당신의 비우슴이 되고 맙니다.
시몬! 이런 것을 다-알면서도 피곤해서 도라 누었을 때는 조름이 소르르 듭니다. 벽에 걸린 아베마리아의 肖像이 더 멀리 보이고 그 아래로 힌 江이 흐릅니다. 그 江 옆에는 언제나 보이던 시몬이 서 있지 않읍니까? 벌덕 일어나 벽에 가서 몸을 부딋처 봅니다. 그리고 그 힌 江 옆에 서있는 당신을 따라 어댄지 작고 걸어갑니다.
시몬! 당신은 내 눈물을 싯처주십니다. 그리고 당신의 두 팔안에 나를 안어주십니다. 나는 더욱 더 눈물이 쏘다저 당신의 손등과 옷은 젖었습니다.＜236＞
길에는 냉이, 달내, 바위틈에는 진달래 봉오리가 터지고 있습니다.
