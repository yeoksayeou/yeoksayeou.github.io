Article Title: 朝鮮音樂學校 設置論 (Discourse on the Establishment of a Chosŏn Music School)
Author: Various contributors (see translated text)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

**Discourse on the Establishment of a Chosŏn Music School**

It is truly a matter for congratulation that the Chosŏn Music Association has been formed within the peninsular music world under the current New Structure (新體制), striving for the creation of healthy National Music (國民音樂). Taking a step further, establishing a "Music School" in Chosŏn would be a grand plan for the nation's future. This matter has previously received attention from the educational authorities (學務當局) on several occasions. To stimulate public opinion among private citizens and promote this cause at this juncture, we have collected the opinions of various musicians.

**Survey Questions**

1.  What message would you like to convey to Governor-General's Director of Educational Affairs Shiodome (鹽原 學務局長) and National Mobilization League (總力聯盟) Cultural Director Yanabe (矢鍋 文化部長) regarding the establishment of a music school?
2.  How can a music school be established quickly? Your opinions on promotion (Finance, Location, Other).
3.  If established, the school should greatly promote the national spirit through music. What academic departments and types of music would be appropriate? (i.e., Music subjects suited to Chosŏn's reality, instruction, etc.) Your views on this. <50>

**Establish a Chosŏn Aak Department Too - Difficulties are Capital and Personnel**
Kim Yŏnghwan (金永煥)
Kyŏngsŏng Sukmyŏng Higher Girls' School

While the need for a complete Music School to be founded in Chosŏn, alongside an Art School, as institutions specializing in the arts is not something I feel acutely only now, this issue of founding a music school is not to be treated lightly; I believe it is one of the urgent tasks assigned to us. As is true for any field of art, literature and music, in particular, have the closest connection to human beings. Especially in wartime, where tension and effort are paramount in life, it is necessary to soothe that tension and fatigue, enrich the people's emotions with the art of music, and thereby brighten the lives of the people on the home front. In this respect too, the necessity of founding a music school is urgently demanded. I myself have repeatedly researched and reviewed this movement from various angles in the past, but ultimately, the biggest difficult issues that followed were capital and personnel.

Director of Educational Affairs Shiodome once asked about establishing a music school and an art school for about 1 million yen (圓), telling us just to secure the funds. However, later discussions revealed that personnel was a bigger problem than anything else. No matter how well the school's structure is organized, the school's authority is paramount, and for that authority, authoritative professors must be appointed.

Even during my time commuting to the Tōkyō Ueno Music School (東京 上野音樂學校), to uphold the school's authority, they invited six famous German music professors from Germany (獨逸) to teach. Each person's monthly salary was 1,000 yen, and including rent, it was 1,075 yen. This was exactly 30 years ago, and 1,000 yen 30 years ago was a huge sum, comparable to perhaps 3,000 or even 5,000 yen today. Consequently, the annual expenses of the music school reached the enormous amount of 380,000 yen. The number of students nurtured and graduated while spending 380,000 yen annually was merely 150. Ultimately, this immense undertaking where 380,000 yen disappears <51> for just 150 people is one of the very difficult things to achieve in Chosŏn. Furthermore, the primary reasons authoritative professors might not come to Kyŏngsŏng (京城) are the narrowing of their sphere of activity and the lack of significant supplementary income. In Tōkyō (東京), income from private lessons (though private lessons are not impossible in Kyŏngsŏng, the number is small, and those wishing to learn tend towards low prices), solo recitals, school-sponsored concerts (even if sponsored by the school, they receive payment), etc., is substantial, making it very difficult to bring renowned professors to Kyŏngsŏng. Besides, it is a well-known fact that a music school requires more expenses than other schools because, for instrumental music, each individual needs their own piano to practice.

That said, I am not very much in favor of establishing a second-rate or third-rate music school that disregards capital or personnel. If we are to establish one at all, even if it means not being able to establish one otherwise, I wish to see the birth of such an authoritative school that even people from mainland Japan (內地人) or foreigners would aspire to come and learn. Of course, in this regard, I am someone who hopes for a public or government-established school rather than a private one. This is because I graduated from a private school called Tōyō Music School (東洋音樂學校) during my Tōkyō days and then graduated from a government-established school, the Ueno Music School. Based on my experience tasting both private and government-established systems, I feel that a music school in Chosŏn should indeed be government-established.

Moreover, even if we try to invite authoritative music professors from the Ueno Music School as part-time lecturers, it might work within the same Tōkyō city, but coming and going by airplane from Kyŏngsŏng is not feasible. So, the problem remains personnel and capital.

If the aforementioned issues can be resolved, the location should definitely be Kyŏngsŏng, and the subjects should follow the standard of the Tōkyō Ueno Music School, but I would particularly like to establish a Department of Chosŏn Music (朝鮮音樂科). Traditional Chosŏn music mostly signifies idleness, like "Let's play, let's play, let's play while we're young," or is largely erotic. It is difficult to uplift the sound emotions of the nation with music of this content, and thus, considering the current situation (現時局), it cannot be approved. However, Chosŏn's unique music should not be discarded. Just as bread and butter suit the palate of Westerners, and kimchi and soybean paste soup suit the palate of Koreans, our musical sentiment (or sensibility - 情操) cannot be ignored. In this respect, I advocate for the establishment of a Chosŏn Music Department that improves traditional Chosŏn music into an artistic form and teaches it. Of course, the authorities (當局) are already actively promoting the succession and encouragement of Chosŏn's classical music through the Yi Royal Household Aak Department (李王職雅樂部), so I believe the authorities will support this more proactively than us. <52>

**Music is Indispensable for Emotional Education; Orchestra and Chorus, German Professor Issue**
Musician Yi Insŏn (李寅善)

Dear Sirs, I am truly grateful for your company's noble intention to gather public opinion regarding the establishment of a music school to contribute to its founding and promotion. Regarding this issue, I too, rather than just my personal desire, have long hoped for this to become the general wish of society and for a path to be opened for society's wish to be expressed and realized. Receiving your inquiry now, I am immensely thankful and overjoyed. I hope that your company's initiative this time will become a noble step upon which a healthy bud sprouts, blossoms, and bears fruit.

Notes:
1.  The educational authorities (學務當局) have already keenly felt the necessity of music for emotional education (情緖敎育) and have incorporated music courses into the middle school curriculum as a required subject. Since there has already been talk of founding a music school, there is no need to debate its necessity anew now.
Looking at the current state of Chosŏn's education sector, one can say that specialized educational institutions in various fields like literature, commerce, law, medicine, engineering, etc., are almost completely established. However, it is a well-known and regrettable fact that there is not a single complete educational institution solely for music.
Music is, of course, indispensable for emotional education. Furthermore, this music can sometimes be a sharp sword of justice and humanity, and it can also be the best culture medium to inspire and cultivate the spirit of unity and patriotism capable of repelling thousands of soldiers and myriad schemes. If this is acknowledged, one cannot but say that the establishment of an institution for training musicians suitable for the urgent times is necessary. Now is truly the time when, setting aside the music schools in mainland Japan (內地), at least one school should be established in Chosŏn.

2.  As the times are what they are, rather than just pleading with the authorities for a non-existent budget, it would be more appropriate for us to resolve the financial issue through our own fervent dedication. I believe the issue is, above all, a matter of "heart" (마음). <53> On this land where many noble individuals generously donate their private wealth for educational projects without reservation, if there is just the "heart," why wouldn't we be able to establish one music school?
I believe the financial problem is ultimately a matter of "heart."
Regarding its establishment, the location would appropriately be Kyŏngsŏng, the center of Chosŏn.

3.  First, concerning departments, how about broadly dividing them into a Normal Course (師範科) for training personnel needed for general school music education and a Professional Course (專門科) for cultivating specialized skills? Then, within the Professional Course, various departments such as piano, violin, voice, cello, wind instruments, composition, etc., could be established.
While polishing individual skills to the highest level in each specialized department is a given, placing particular emphasis on collective training like orchestra (管絃樂), band (뺀드), and chorus (코러스) within the school seems more necessary for the current times. Here, the issue of selecting professors is seen as greatly determining the future destiny. I believe it is appropriate to select at least one professor for each department who is an authority in that field (斯道). We should not limit the search for professors only within Chosŏn but should extend our reach widely to mainland Japan or even allied nations (同盟國) to find suitable candidates, which would be beneficial for the school's future and results. To give an example based on my personal opinion, wouldn't it be good to invite at least one professor each for instrumental music and voice from the European system (歐羅巴系統)? Specifically, perhaps inviting German professors (獨人敎授) for piano and violin, and Italian professors (伊太利人敎授) for voice? Of course, it goes without saying that many suitable professors among Koreans and Japanese (內鮮人間) are also needed.
Eligibility for admission should be limited to men and women with at least a middle school graduation level of education, possessing basic musical aptitude and talent, and having upright conduct. That is all.

**Promoting National Spirit Through Musical Art - The Absence of a Complete Music School is Chosŏn's Shame**
Director of Chosŏn Music Ham Hwajin (咸和鎭)

Upon the formation of the Chosŏn Music Association this time, what deeply impresses me is that this marks a new beginning for our music world within the East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (東亞共榮圈) and can be seen as an expression <54> of the spirit of the New Structure (新體制). The fact that all institutions of art and culture have achieved concrete grand unity today has become a fundamental goal for great cooperation under the implementation of national policy (國策遂行), and the hope that the nation under the current situation will make a healthy artistic start is definitively believed.

However, I wish to speak only somewhat about Chosŏn Music (朝鮮音樂); regarding other matters, I am a complete blank slate. Therefore, while heartily congratulating the formation of the Chosŏn Music Association this time, I intend to briefly express my humble opinion on the current state and future of Chosŏn Music.
That music has had a close relationship with politics since ancient times can be sufficiently understood from historical records of the East.
However, in the current state of Chosŏn Music, whether high-class or low-class, it has merely become individual entertainment. Setting aside its relationship with politics, it is utterly impossible to find any trace of what influence it exerts on customs or national sentiment.
Music is capable of evoking the so-called seven emotions (七情) of humans at will; this can be seen as the true value of art.
However, looking back at the recent state of the Chosŏn music scene, there is only the Aak Department (雅樂部) cultivated by the Yi Royal Household (李王職), but this is solely for performances within the royal family. Its distance from general society is extremely far, and issues like dissemination to the general populace have not even been conceived of yet. It simply ends there. In the private social sphere, there are several small, weak groups, but they are rather stagnant and exist in name only (有名無實). Therefore, one can easily gauge how impoverished Chosŏn music has become today.
In such circumstances, it is indeed a great dawn for our music world that a healthy Music Association has been born through the guidance of the authorities and the cooperation of the people in the spirit of the New Structure.
However, now that musicians from various fields have united, let us not vainly end up existing only in name. I deeply trust that we will cooperate and develop with one heart and mind to newly pioneer the stagnant music and exert a significant influence on customs and national sentiment.
Thus, in transforming the national spirit through music, both the authorities and the populace must first have a concept (觀念) about this. Not just a concept, but there must be sincerity (誠意). Not just sincerity, but there must also be material resources (物質).
If even one of these three is lacking, fruition will be utterly impossible.
Where does this fruition lie? It lies in establishing levels for low-grade and high-grade music respectively, culminating in the founding of a completely sound specialized music school – this is something everyone is crying out for. Therefore, at this time when so-called art and culture are on the rise, the absence of a music school or art school in the great city of Kyŏngsŏng with its million inhabitants is, so to speak, <55> a shame for this city of a million.
However, the reason such facilities have not existed until today is, as mentioned above, firstly the lack of concept regarding this, consequently the lack of sincerity, and also the lack of material resources.
But the fact that the Music Association has been born, crying its first cry today, can be seen as the impetus of a concept regarding this.
As this impetus of concept arises, I believe that corresponding sincerity will also follow, and consequently, material resources will also become readily available.
Therefore, although the authorities have long paid attention to this matter, now is truly the time when, through the sincere guidance of the authorities and the sound cooperation of private citizens, realization can be achieved, and the shame of the city of a million can be thoroughly washed away. I keenly believe this time has come.
Furthermore, at the lower level, there seems to be no greater shortcut than music for invigorating the spirit of children and youth. Why? Because for children and students, according to their respective levels and following their environment and psychology, by composing and disseminating numerous songs based on certain lyrical themes imbued with Chosŏn's color (朝鮮의 色彩), without needing strict supervision like school education or adherence to fixed times, they can spontaneously grasp the charm of the songs anytime, anywhere, without any guidance, naturally singing them with their mouths while simultaneously associating the lyrical meaning, thereby transforming their thoughts by themselves.
Therefore, if implemented in this manner, I believe great effectiveness will arise.
Regarding the concrete plans for the above, I firmly believe that the President of the Music Association and the Cultural Director of the National Mobilization League have profound plans in mind, so I think there is no need for further tedious talk.

**Advocating for Chosŏn's Sentiment - Hope for Cooperation from the Wealthy**
Yun Kŭgyŏng (尹克榮)
Kando Ryūsei National Higher School (間島龍井國民高等學校)

Having returned home last night after running around east and west without accomplishing much, I finally saw your letter. The deadline has already passed, and from my remote perspective in a distant land, I have no way of knowing the recent <56> situation in Kyŏngsŏng. Consequently, regarding the issue of establishing a Chosŏn Music School, no effective opinion on how to proceed immediately comes to mind. However, I sincerely hope that such a school specializing in artistic subjects will definitely come into existence. Through this, I wish to further enrich the expression of "Art Chosŏn" (藝術朝鮮). Especially in wartime, various fields of art should be further promoted to enable the populace to have gentle and bright emotions and lives, but I think the mission of music, along with literature, might be even greater.

Considering the various points of the current situation (現下 時局), especially as someone in Manchuria (滿洲), under the significance of Korea-Manchuria as One Body (鮮滿一如), I keenly feel the specialty and immense value of Chosŏn's sentimental culture (朝鮮的 情緖文化). In that respect, it requires no further mention that the academy of Music Chosŏn should be born in Kyŏngsŏng, the capital of Chosŏn. However, if its realization cannot be seen, we can only blame the lack of sincerity and effort from gentlemen who pay attention to this field, and the ignorance of the newly rich (成金) who do not know how to spend their money.

**Music is an Organic Body for Character Cultivation - If a Single Music School, then a National Music School**
Pak Kyŏnghŭi (朴景嬉)

One. The Great Japanese Empire (大日本帝國), as the leader of East Asia, is forging ahead for the construction of a New Order in East Asia and furthermore, for the construction of a New World Order. As great citizens bearing the responsibility of such great leadership, it is natural that we should receive all necessary education without omission, and moreover, we should stand at the pinnacle compared to the citizens of any country in the world.
Observing the current Chosŏn education sector, what I keenly feel is the establishment of a Music School.
Music is solemnly an organic entity (一有機體) for character cultivation, not an appendage to any subject, but a foundational subject with its own independence. Therefore, music education too should receive the benefit of equal opportunity, and just as specialized schools for other subjects exist, it should be enabled to display the highest skills. Because there is no music school in Chosŏn, the number of students intending to specialize in music who tread the path of studying abroad increases year by year. <57> Furthermore, considering the training of music education leaders under the New Structure or the cultivation of high-level music scholars, the establishment of a Music School is felt as an urgent task.

Two. To establish a music school, there might be circumstances requiring gradual steps, but in terms of its significance, it should be a complete music school aiming for the highest authority. As mentioned above, as great citizens of the world, we require facilities befitting great citizens in everything. While the encouragement of private schools may occur in the future, in pioneering the establishment of a single music school now, a National Music School directly under the National Mobilization League (總力聯盟) is desired. As the saying goes, "Well begun is half done." Since the Cultural Department already exists within the National Mobilization League, and its members comprise authoritative figures from various artistic circles, I speculate that the idea of a music school might already be conceived (胚胎) in their plans. If so, I hope their conceived consciousness is not left latent to decay, but that there will be further effort. Regarding the financial resources, while donations from interested individuals are hoped for, even without the fortune of supplementary support, I believe realization is possible if only there is the determined sincerity from the Cultural Department, where the total will of the nation is focused.

Three. Music education must be education based on the fundamental principles of our national polity (我國體本義). To realize this, the first issue is excellent leaders, the second is creation (創作), and the third is the perfection of facilities.
1.  Following the advocacy for teacher re-education under the current New Structure, even existing music scholars feel the need for a fresh start. It is inappropriate for those who studied abroad to treat what they learned as their own music and transmit it as is, nor is it reasonable for domestic historical musicians to approach contemporary people with the music of ancient people as is. Therefore, we hope for the emergence of individuals equipped with the spiritual capacity to modernize music and adapt it to the training of great citizens.
2.  Regarding the issue of creation, it must be creation with the soul of music and permanence. However, rather than seeking this creation purely from a blank slate, relying on Western styles (洋式) is also acceptable. That Western styles cannot be entirely excluded is an undeniable fact, as seen in the harmonization of Western styles across various aspects of our Empire's culture, even extending to lifestyles. However, if it is not dominated by foreign cultural influences, does not tend towards individualism, becomes education for nurturing character as Japanese citizens, and is refined and transformed into national training, even if imitative (模倣的), it can be remodeled into original (獨創的) education.
3.  Regarding facilities, even for music suited to Chosŏn's reality, the focus of education must consider its potential influence on the broader East Asian, or even global stage, and as an educational institution for great citizens, <58> high-level facilities are desired.

**National Music and Creative Talent - May a Second Mr. Noguchi Appear!**
Yi Hŭngnyŏl (李興烈)
Music Teacher, Paejae Middle School (培材中學校)

One. Music does not merely stop at entertainment or amusement (道樂); moving a step further, it is an indispensable art for the cultured person required by the new era. Especially in times of emergency, music plays a significant role in guiding the national spirit – this has become acknowledged by everyone through various examples since the outbreak of the current Incident (事變).
However, the current situation on the Peninsula (半島), where educational institutions for nurturing talent, especially in the field of music among cultural sectors, are virtually non-existent, is deeply regrettable.
Therefore, the issue of establishing a music school is something the authorities themselves likely feel more keenly than those of us who are interested and demanding it.
At this time, when the establishment of Volunteer Training Centers (志願兵訓練所), the expansion of music education in middle schools, etc., have been decisively undertaken, and preceding the implementation of compulsory education, the extension of school grades and the establishment of elementary, middle, and vocational schools are steadily progressing, this issue regarding the establishment of a music school is also considered an urgent matter that cannot be overlooked.

Two. The problem ultimately seems to be finance. The simplest and easiest way would be if an industrialist like Mr. Noguchi (野口氏), who readily donated the huge sum of 20 million yen to society this time, takes an interest in this field, considers it a new cultural project, and shows courage. That would be the best solution. If not, the authorities must strive somehow to pass the relevant budget.

Three. Departments should be divided into Eastern and Western Music (東西音樂科). By selecting the strengths and weaknesses of both, emphasis should be placed on nurturing creative talent capable of producing new national music, and music that, in the future, can possess new musical ideas and forms.
To achieve this, the faculty must comprise authorities in this field (斯界) who are thorough in technique and theory. Still, most will inevitably have to be invited from mainland Japan (內地) or abroad (外地). <59>

**A Fund of 500,000 Yen is the Prerequisite - If It Cannot Be Established, Actively Support Existing Music Education Institutions**
Yi Sŭnghak (李升學)
Kyŏngsŏng Music Academy (京城音樂專門學院)

One. While establishing several schools in other fields this year, why are you not establishing a music school, which is most important for emotional education (情緖敎育)? I earnestly plead for its realization as soon as possible. If even that proves difficult, please actively support the music education institutions currently run by private citizens so they can be upgraded to music schools.

Two. Unless interested private individuals contribute capital sufficient to establish a music school, it seems extremely difficult. At a minimum, 500,000 yen (圓) would suffice. Unlike schools in other fields, the school building does not require a very large structure, so about 100,000 yen would be enough, and 400,000 yen could serve as the fixed capital to operate the school. However, unless this large sum of 500,000 yen is available, this is just armchair theorizing. The most feasible method is that there are already institutions on the Peninsula (半島), albeit small-scale, that are not very different from music schools. If interested individuals cooperate with such institutions, I believe realization is certain.

Three. In addition to the subjects included in music schools in mainland Japan or foreign countries, I hope Aak (雅樂), filled with Chosŏn's sentiment (朝鮮의 情緖), will be made a regular course (正科), and simultaneously, general East Asian music (東洋音樂), at least as an introductory course (槪論), will be included as a subject.

**Summary**

This 1941 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine compiles opinions from prominent Korean musicians and educators on the urgent need to establish a formal music school in Chosŏn (Korea). Respondents unanimously support the idea, emphasizing music's role in emotional education, national spirit cultivation, and cultural development under the Japanese "New Structure." Key challenges identified are securing adequate funding (estimated at 500,000 yen) and attracting highly qualified professors, potentially including foreign instructors (German, Italian) alongside Japanese and Korean staff. There's a consensus on needing both Western music disciplines and a dedicated department for traditional/reformed Chosŏn music (including Aak). Suggestions range from seeking government/public funding (potentially via the National Mobilization League) to appealing to wealthy private donors, perhaps a "second Noguchi." The Tōkyō Ueno Music School is frequently cited as a model.

From a colonial history perspective, the article reveals Korean cultural elites navigating Japanese colonial rule, attempting to advance Korean arts education within the constraints and ideological framework imposed by Japan (New Structure, National Music aligned with state goals). It highlights the tension between adopting Western/Japanese models and preserving/reforming indigenous Korean musical traditions (Chosŏn Music/Aak), reflecting broader debates about cultural identity and modernization under colonial pressure. The discussion about funding difficulties and the need for authoritative (often meaning Japanese or foreign) faculty underscores the limited resources and autonomy available for cultural development in colonial Chosŏn.

朝鮮音樂學校 設置論
今番 新體制下의 半島樂壇에 있어서 朝鮮音樂協會가 결성되여 건전한 國民音樂의 創生에 노력하게 되었음은 심히 慶賀할 일인 바 일보를 更히 進하여 朝鮮에도 「音樂學校」를 創設함이 國家百年의 大計가 될 줄 아는 바이다. 여기에 대해서는 종래에도 學務當局에서 屢次 留意하여 오던 일인데 이 기회에 民間人士의 여론을 喚起하여 그를 促進시키기 위해 音樂家 諸氏의 의견을 集載하다.

設問
1. 音樂學校設置에 대하여 總督府 鹽原 學務局長과 總力聯盟 矢鍋 文化部長에게 呈하고 싶은 말씀.
2. 어떻게 하면 速히 音樂學校를 設置할 수 있겠습니까. 그 促進意見(財政, 位置, 其他)
3. 設置된다면 音樂을 통하여 國民精神을 크게 진작하여야 할터인 바 學科와 音樂의 種目에 어떻하면 可하겠습니까. (즉 朝鮮 現實에 卽한 音樂科目 敎授 等 其他) 그에 대한 貴見.＜50＞

朝鮮雅樂科도 設置, -難點은 資本과 人材-
京城淑明高等女學校
金永煥

藝術을 전공하는 機關으로서 美術學校와 함께 朝鮮에도 완전한 音樂學校가 創建되여야 할 必要를 지금 새삼스러이 痛感하는 바는 아니지만 여하튼 이 音樂學校 創設問題는 등한시할 것이 아니고 吾人에게 부여된 시급한 課題의 하나라고 생각한다. 藝術의 어느 부문이나 다 그러하겠지만 특히 文學과 音樂은 藝術 중에서도 가장 人間과의 밀접한 관련을 맺고 있기 때문에, 그리고 緊張과 努力을 무엇보다 생활의 第一義로 하는 戰時 下일쑤록 그 긴장과 피로를 慰癒하고 民家의 감정을 音樂藝術로 潤澤케 하여써 銃後國民生活을 명랑케 해야 할 것이다. 이러한 점에서도 音樂學校의 創立 必要를 더욱 喫緊히 要望하는 바이오 나도 이 運動의 실현을 위해서 과거에 여러 각도에서 연구와 검토를 거듭했었으나 결국 뒤따르는 가장 큰 難問題는 資本과 人材問題였었다.
塩原學務局長도 100萬圓 정도로 音樂學校와 美術學校를 설립함이 여하하냐고 하며 자금만을 얻어 놓으라 한 일이 있다. 그러나 그 뒤 이 문제는 다시 논의되여서 인재가 무엇보다 더 큰 문제로 되였었다. 아무리 學校의 기구가 정비되였다 할지라도 그 學校의 權威라는 것이 무엇보다 긴요한데 그 權威를 위해서는 權威있는 敎授를 두어야 할 것이다.
내가 東京 上野音樂學校에 통학하던 시대만 해도 學校의 權威라는 것을 위해서 獨逸서 유명한 獨逸人 音樂敎授 6명을 초빙해서 敎授시컸는데 每人이 각각 1개월 봉급이 1,000圓이였고 집세까지 합하면 1,075원이였었다. 이것이 꼭 30년 전 일이니 30년 전의 1,000원이란 금액은 지금 돈의 3,000원이나 5,000원에 해당하다 해도 可할 정도의 巨金이였었다. 그리하여 音樂學校의 1년간 경비가 무려 38만원이라는 거액에 달했었는데 1년간 38만원을 消毫하면서 길러내여 졸원시키는 학생의 수는 겨우 150명에 불과하다. 그러니까 결국 150명을 위해서 38만원이＜51＞ 없어지는 이 엄청난 경영이 朝鮮서 이루어지기가 매우 難事 中의 하나요, 그리고 權威있는 敎授가 경성에 오지 않을 첫 조건은 자기의 활동무대가 좁아지는 것과 및 부수입이 별로 없는 것이다. 東京만 해도 個人敎授(京城서도 개인敎授할 수 없는 것은 아니로되 그 수가 적고 또한 배우려는 사람이 廉價主義를 쓰기 때문에)와 독창회, 學校主催音樂會(學校主催라고 해도 보수를 받는다)등으로 그 수입이 많기 때문에 이름을 드날리는 敎授를 京城에 모셔오기가 매우 곤란하다. 그 외에도 音樂學校는 機樂이면 개인개인이 다 한 피아노를 차지하고 배우기 때문에 다른 學校보다 경비가 더 한 것은 周知의 사실일 것이다.
그렇다고 자본이나 인재를 무시한 2流, 3流의 音樂學校를 설립한다는 것은 나는 그리 찬성하고 싶지 않다. 차라리 못세울 망정 세울 바엔 內地人이나 외국인까지도 와서 배우려고 지망하게 되는 그러한 權威있는 學校의 탄생을 보고 싶은 것이다. 無論 이 점에서 나는 私立보다 公立이나 官立을 희망하는 자이다. 그것은 내가 東京時代에 東洋音樂學校라는 私立學校를 졸업했고 다시 上野音樂學校라는 官立學校를 졸업했기 때문에 사립, 官立의 두 맛을 다 맛 본 나의 체험으로는 역시 音樂學校는 朝鮮에 있어서는 官立이여야겠다고 느낀다.
그리고 上野音樂學校의 權威있는 音樂敎授를 시간敎授로 모시자해도 같은 東京市內라면 몰라도 京城서는 비행기로 왔다갔다 할 수도 없고 그러니까 역시 문제는 人材요 資本이다.
만일 이상의 諸問題가 해결될 수가 있다면 그 위치는 역시 京城이오 科目은 東京上野音樂學校를 準하되 특히 朝鮮音樂科라는 것을 설치하고 싶다. 재래 朝鮮의 音樂이라는 것은 「노자 노자 젊어서 놀자」는 식으로 태만을 의미한다든가 혹은 에로틱한 것이 태반이여서 이러한 내용의 音樂으로서는 건전한 國民의 감정을 진작시키기가 어렵고 따라서 現時局에 鑑해서도 찬성할 수 없는데 그렇다고 朝鮮의 固有音樂을 버려서는 안 될 것이다. 西洋사람의 입에는 빵과 빠다가 맛들고 朝鮮사람의 입에는 김치와 된장국이 맛들 듯이 우리의 音樂的 情緖(혹은 情操)를 무시할 수가 없다. 이 점에서 재래의 朝鮮音樂을 藝術的인 것으로 개량해서 敎授시키는 朝鮮音樂科의 設置를 주장한다. 無論 이 점은 當局에서도 李王職雅樂部라는 것을 두어서 朝鮮의 古典音樂의 계승과 및 장려를 적극적으로 하고 있기 때문에 우리보다도 當局이 더 率先해서 支持할 것이라 믿는 바이다.＜52＞

音樂은 情緖敎育上不可缺한 것 , 管絃樂과 코러스, 獨伊人敎授問題-
音樂家 李寅善

拜啓 今番 특히 音樂學校設立에 대한 民間의 輿論을 모아 同校壯立提進에 資하려 하시는 貴社의 社한 意圖는 실로 감사합니다. 이 문제에 대하야 小生도 1個人의 의도를 願望보다도 이것이 사회일반의 願望이 되여지고 사회의 願望이 표현 내지 實現化되는 길이 열녀지기를 다년 苦待하던 중 이번 貴社의 下問을 받자오니 무엇보다도 감사히 생각하고 기뻐하기 마지 않습니다. 원켄댄 이번 貴社의 발족이 거륵한 거름이 되여 그 우에 건실한 싹이 트고 꽃이 피여 결실되기 바랍니다.
記
1. 學務當局에서도 이미 音樂의 情緖敎育上 必要를 통감하여 中等學校에 音樂科程을 必修   科目의 一로 編入시켯고 또 音樂學校創立說이 이미 있었든 만큼 同校創設의 필요를 이제    새삼스럽게 論할 것은 없을 것입니다.
現下 朝鮮敎育界를 전망컨댄 文, 商, 法, 醫, 工 등 각 방면의 專門敎育機關은 거의 다 완    비되여 있다 할 수 있으나 오로지 音樂만은 완전한 敎育機關이 1개도 없음은 熟知하는 바  요 유감으로 역이는 바입니다.
音樂이 情緖敎育上 불가결한 것은 물론이려니와 일보 더 나아가서 이 음악이야말노 때로    는 正義와 人道의 銳利한 칼도 될 수 있고 밀려오는 千兵과 萬機를 능히 뭇지를 수 있는    단결과 애국의 정신을 鼓舞 乃至 培養시키는 最良의 培養基도 될 수 있다고 보는데 이것    을 是認한다면 하로 밧비 시국에 적합한 音樂人의 養成을 위한 機關의 설립이 필요하다     아니 할 수 없습니다. 이때야 말로 內地에 있는 音樂學校는 별개로 치고 朝鮮에도 1校쯤    은 설립하여야 할 시기가 아닌가 합니다.
2. 時局이 時局이니만큼 없는 豫算을 當局에만 조를 것이 아니라 그 보다도 오히려 우리    의 熱誠으로 財政問題 쯤은 해결함이 지당치 않을지오. 문제는 무엇보다도 첫째 『마음』의  문제라고 봅니다. 敎育事業을＜53＞ 위해서는 아까움이 없이 私財를 던저주는 거륵한 人士가   많은 이 땅 우에 「마음」만 있다면 音樂學校 하나쯤 설립 못 할 리 있겠습니까.
나는 財政問題는 결국 『마음』의 문제라고 믿습니다.
