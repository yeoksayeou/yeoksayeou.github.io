**Body Text Translation:**
Miscellaneous Views on the War, Recent News from Berlin and Paris
						By Parija

It has already been one month since the start of the Great European War. According to the British announcement, during this period, German aircraft losses amounted to 3,945 planes, while their own losses were 1,012. However, according to the German announcement, their own aircraft losses during the same period were 1,050 planes, while the total losses for Poland, Norway, Belgium, Holland, France, and Britain amounted to 6,950 planes. Furthermore, German military losses since the start of the war are 39,000 killed in action, 143,000 wounded, and 24,000 missing in action. This number is markedly lower compared to the First World War, indicating that the technological advancements in modern weaponry have actually reduced the number of casualties. (1st)

London's theaters were clearly in a slump last year. This wasn't so much hesitation about going to see plays, but rather the fear of having to wait until dawn<200> after encountering an air raid during the performance. Therefore, daytime performances are proceeding as usual. (8th)

On the 9th, the harvest labor finally began in German rural villages. Thousands upon thousands of students from Berlin University and higher professional schools were dispatched throughout the country on specially prepared trains for labor service. Male and female students from the Berlin State High School of Music headed to the Ostprignitz region. Violinists operate threshing machines, cellists load barley onto trucks. Pianists lead horses. During break times, vocalists sing songs. On Sundays, they gather the villagers and hold folk song concerts. In this way, they experience rural life for several weeks. Naturally, the students also participate in the nationwide Harvest Thanksgiving Festival holiday. (12th)

The United States currently holds 21 billion dollars in gold, three-quarters of the world's gold reserves. Dr. James, Vice-Chancellor of McGill University, stated that this gold is in extraordinary danger because of the war. If Britain wins, the gold standard will likely be maintained. However, if Germany is victorious, a regional self-sufficiency system will be established, and gold<201> will become worthless except as an economic ornament. (15th)

Three months have passed since the German army occupied Paris, and the city has recovered two-thirds of its population. According to recent statistics, 3.1 million people currently live in the Paris district, compared to the pre-war population of 4 million. The streets are once again crowded with pedestrians, and most of the famous restaurants and some theaters and entertainment venues have reopened. (17th)

Shortly after entering Paris, the German army appointed an 18-year-old boy, who had until then been an elevator boy at the company, as the new leader of the famous local newspaper, "Paris-Soir" (巴里·소와-르). It is said that this boy received this unusual promotion due to his distinguished service in safely handing over the newspaper and its printing machinery to the German army. (18th)

London has already endured intense bombing by German aircraft for over two weeks, yet 778,000 children still remain. Especially in the East End, children whose elementary schools have been destroyed<202> by bombing are playing around, while children whose schools remain attend half-day classes. At night, most of the children sleep in subway stations, park shelters, and brick huts in the city. In Piccadilly Station, small pre-school-age children can be seen sleeping in opened suitcases. (22nd)

News of the death in battle of Maurice Jaubert (모-리쓰·죠-베르), famous for composing works such as "Paris Festival" ("Quatorze Juillet") and "Carnet de Bal" ("舞踏會手帖"), has been reported. With the outbreak of this Great War, he went to the front as a Captain of Engineers. In the battle on the banks of the Meuse River (므-즈 河畔), he remained until the very end as part of a suicide squad and performed a great feat by carrying out the demolition of an iron bridge. His final work, published just after the war began, is a collection of small songs titled "Book of Psalms" ("聖詩集"). (29th)<203>

**Summary**

This article compiles news snippets from Europe during WWII, focusing on Berlin, Paris, and London in late 1940/early 1941. It contrasts British and German reports on aircraft losses, describes German students participating in harvest labor, notes concerns about the future of the gold standard depending on the war's outcome, details the partial return to normalcy in occupied Paris (including a strange anecdote about a newspaper takeover), depicts the hardships of children living under bombardment in London, and reports the death of French composer Maurice Jaubert. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this piece is interesting as it shows how international war news reached the Korean public, likely filtered through Japanese censors. It presents a mix of factual reports, human interest stories (students, children), and potentially pro-Axis leaning details (German efficiency, Paris returning to life under occupation, the Paris-Soir anecdote), offering insight into the information environment of colonial Korea during the war.

戰爭雜觀, 伯林·巴里의 最近
						巴里子
歐洲大戰은 開戰以來 벌써 1개월을 지났다. 英國側의 발표에 의하면, 이 기간에, 獨逸機의 損失은 3,945臺, 自國側은 1,012臺라고 하나 一方 獨逸側의 발표에 의하면 동기간에 있는 自國機의 손실은 1,050臺 플-랜드, 노르웨 白耳義, 오란다, 佛蘭西 英國의 손실 總計는 6,950臺로 되여있다. 그리고 開戰以來의 獨逸軍의 손실은 戰死 39,000명 負傷 143,000명 行衛不明 24,000명이나 이 수자는 第1次 世界大戰 當時에 비교하면 현저하게 적고, 近代兵器의 技術的進步가 되려 犧牲者의 수를 감한 것을 지시하고 있다.(1日)

倫敦의 劇場은 작년 분명히 불경기인데, 그것은 演劇을 보러가기를 躊躇하기라보다, 觀劇中의 空襲에 밝을 때까지들＜200＞ 기다리는 것을 무서워 함에 서다. 그러므로 낮의 흥행에는 종전대로다.(8日)

9日에 들어서 獨逸農村에선 이윽고 收穫勞働이 시작되였다. 伯林大學과 高等專門生徒들이 몇千 몇萬으로 特別製造의 열차로 전국각지에 勞働奉仕에 派遺되었다. 伯林國立高等音樂學校의 남여 학생들은 오스트푸리크니치地方으로 향했다. 바오리스트가 打穀機를 잡고 체리스트가 튜력에 보리를 실른다. 피아니스트는 말을 끄은다. 휴식시간이 되면, 聲樂家가 노래를 부른다. 일요일이 되면 村人들을 모혀놓고, 民謠콘싸-트를 연다. 이래서 수주간을, 그들은 農村生活을 경험한다. 이제 全國的 收穫感謝祭祝日에는 學生들도 물론 참가한다.(12日)

合衆國에는 현재 世界 金保有高의 4분의 3되는 210億弗의 金貨가 모혀 있는데, 마크길大學의 副總長 제임즈博士는 戰爭때문이 이 金貨는 비상한 위기에 있다. 만약 英國이 이기면 金本位制는 유지되리라, 만 獨逸이 승리하면 地域的 自給自足制가 수립되고 金貨는 經濟的＜201＞ 裝飾이외에는 무가치하게 될 것이라고 말했다.(15日)

獨逸軍이 점거해서 3개월을 경과한 巴里는 그 인구의 3분지 2를 회복했다. 최근의 통계에 의하면 전쟁전의 人口 4百萬에 대해서 현재 巴里地區에는 310萬人이 살고 있다. 街頭는 다시 通行人으로 雜沓하고, 유명한 레스트람의 대부분과 劇場과 娛樂場의 약간이 다시 開場하였다.(17日)

獨逸軍은 巴里入城해서 얼마 안되여, 이 땅의 유명한 新聞 「巴里·소와-르」의 새 指導者로서 이때까지 同社에 엘레베-타·뽀이로 있든 18세의 소년을 그 重職에 임명했다. 이 소년은 同新聞과 그 印刷機械를 무사히 獨逸軍 손에 준 殊勳에 의해서 이 異類의 拔*을 받았다 한다.(18日)

倫敦은 임이 2주간 이상 獨逸機의 激烈한 爆擊을 당했으나 아직 77만8천명의 아이들이 남어있다. 특히 이-스트·엔드에선 爆擊되어 小學校가 없어진＜202＞ 아이들은 놀고 있으나, 學校가 남어있는 아이들은, 반일의 수업을 한다. 밤이 되면 아이들의 대부분은 地下鐵의 停車場과 공원의 避難所와 市街의 煉瓦小屋에서 잔다. 피카데리停車場에선 學齡前의 적은 아이들이 開幕한 스-츠케이스에서 자는 것이 보인다.(22日)

「巴里祭」 「舞踏會手帖」 등의 작곡으로 유명한 모-리쓰·죠-베르의 戰死의 報가 전하였다. 그는 이번 大戰勃發과 함께 工兵大尉로서 出征, 므-즈 河畔戰에는 최후까지 決死隊로서 남었었고, 鐵橋爆破를, 감행하여 偉効를 세웠다. 戰爭開始直後에 발표된 최후의 作品은 「聖詩集」이라는 小歌曲集이다.(29日)＜203＞
