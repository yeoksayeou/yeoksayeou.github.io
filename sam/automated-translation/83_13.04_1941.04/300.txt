Publication Date: 1941년 04월 01일
Article Title: 李香蘭, 金信哉 會見記 (Record of the Meeting Between Li Hyangran and Kim Sinjae)
Author: Not specified (Interview format)
Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Interview/Discussion)

**Li Hyangran, Kim Sinjae Meeting Record**
Time: Early March
Place: Chosŏn Hotel in Kyŏngsŏng
People: Li Hyangran · Kim Sinjae

**Li Hyangran's Hometown, Parents, Siblings**

Kim: Ms. Hyangran, there are rumors saying you are Korean, or Japanese [Mainlander], or Chinese. Where are you really from?
Li: Is that so? Well, where could I be from? (Hyangran looks over at Sinjae and blinks.)
Kim: Please tell me straight. Why don’t you clarify your identity and relieve the fans who are suffering from curiosity and thirst?
Li: It's because people imagine things on their own and say this and that. <180> If you look at my real name, Yamaguchi Yoshiko (山口俊子), you know where I'm from, don't you?
Kim: Then you are definitely Japanese [Mainlander]?
Li: ... (Hyangran tilts her head and nods slightly at Sinjae.)
Kim: Then where were you born?
Li: My father and mother were born in Saga Prefecture (佐賀縣) in the Mainland [Japan]. And I was apparently born in Fushun (撫順).
Kim: And where do you currently reside?
Li: It's Beijing (北京).
Kim: How did you come to have the name Li Hyangran (李香蘭)?
Li: A long time ago, when I applied as a singer at the Shinkyō (新京) Broadcasting Station, I used Li Hyangran. It's a stage name, so to speak. Since the name sounds Chinese or Korean, it seems people think whatever they want.
Kim: Then, is that why parents are supposedly appearing here and there?
Li: I guess so. My mother, my father, my younger siblings are all alive and well in Beijing right now.
Kim: But why did you tell a newspaper reporter you were looking for your parents?
Li: I never said such a thing. Why would I say that when my parents and siblings are alive and well? I am the eldest daughter of my parents, and I have six younger siblings. Including me, there are seven of us. The sibling right after me is studying in Tōkyō now. Because she is studying there, I've also set up a household in Tōkyō. So even if I go often, it doesn't feel like visiting a strange place. Ms. Sinjae, you should come visit too. (Hyangran seemed very fond of and cherished Sinjae. Her attitude was just like towards her own younger sister.)
Kim: Thank you. I will come visit... (Sinjae smiles, revealing her charming snaggletooth in a face full of affection. Then she continues speaking.) Then are all the things reported in the newspaper entirely false rumors?
Li: What kinds of things were reported?
Kim: The people who appeared in Pusan insisted you were definitely their daughter or sister. As proof, they correctly pointed out where you had scars or moles, so now people here seem to believe Ms. Hyangran is undoubtedly a <181> Korean woman.
Li: Well, people claiming to be my father, mother, younger sister, or older brother pop up here and there, and since it's happened more than once or twice, I don't even pay attention anymore.
Kim: How many times has something like that happened?
Li: In Tōkyō, a Taiwanese person came looking for me, claiming I was his younger sister; in Pusan, as reported in the newspaper, there was someone claiming to be my mother, someone claiming to be my older sister, and someone claiming to be my older brother; in Taegu, a woman claiming to be my younger sister came looking for me; and in Seoul (at the Chosŏn Hotel), a man who said he attends music school in Tōkyō came looking for me, calling me his older sister. But the most troublesome was in Pusan. The person claiming to be my mother was crying hysterically, insisting I was her daughter, I really didn't know what I should do.
Kim: They say they brought an album from their missing daughter's graduation from Tŏksu Elementary School (德壽小學校) to prove you were definitely their daughter and showed it to you, Ms. Hyangran. Did you really see it?
Li: Nothing like that happened...
Kim: But the rumor is that Ms. Hyangran saw the album and replied that although the faces looked alike, it definitely wasn't you. And they say the expression you had when denying it seemed somehow strange.
Li: What a scary world. Nothing like that ever happened.

**Performances Across Korea and Related Stories**

Kim: So, where did you perform on this Korea tour?
Li: Pusan, Taegu, Kyŏngsŏng, Wŏnsan, Hamhŭng, P'yŏngyang, and Chinnamp'o. Now, Northwestern Korea remains.
Kim: There must have been many interesting and funny stories from your performances so far, right? Everyone says Ms. Hyangran's popularity is unmatched by any actor from the Mainland [Japan]... I saw in the newspaper that a pickpocket followed you from Tōkyō, stole over 2,000 yen, and was caught?
Li: Well, apparently so. Anyway, many people came to see the shows. Still, in Pusan or Taegu, I didn't feel such great emotion. But at the Myŏngch'ijwa (明治座) in Seoul, my heart was pounding. It was the first time since Tōkyō. And it was probably the first time I made a mistake, also at Myŏngch'ijwa. Perhaps because I've been singing for a long time now, I usually don't feel anything when I go on stage, but this time at Myŏngch'ijwa, while I was singing, people sitting right at the front were acting funny in some way, so I just burst out laughing. What could I do? So I started singing again from the beginning. Also, because there were so many people, I didn't look around much but focused on one spot while singing. Then suddenly, someone took out a mirror and comb and started combing their hair and fixing their appearance. Perhaps that person thought I was only looking at them.
Kim: So what did you do?
Li: I quickly turned my face to another direction <182> and that was that.
Kim: Why couldn't you look a bit longer? (The two bend over laughing, thoroughly mocking the foolish fans. Indeed, they deserved to be ridiculed. The performances ran continuously for 4 days, day and night, and crowds of men and women gathered from early morning, so much so that several mounted police officers had to be deployed. The young man who took out a mirror and comb to preen himself must have been one of those who jostled, pushed, and squeezed his way into the crowd.)

**Hyangran's Love and Marriage**

Kim: Aren't you getting married?
Li: There has to be a partner. Ms. Sinjae, please find one for me. If it's someone Ms. Sinjae recommends, I'd be happy to marry him.
Kim: What nationality should he be?
Li: Well, what nationality would be good?
Kim: Korean?
Li: ... (Just looks at Sinjae and smiles.)
Kim: Chinese?
Li: Wherever he's from, as long as he's someone who will love me, someone who will love only me, that's all that matters.
Kim: Ah, but everyone loves you. How much must they love you to rush to see you even if their heads might get smashed?
Li: Is that so... Anyway, I think it's better for me not to get married.
Kim: Why?
Li: Because if I marry, I become tied down to one person.
Kim: Then will you never marry?
Li: I think it's better not to marry and have many lovers. Perhaps 'many lovers' sounds inappropriate, but I mean lovers who can be partners for discussing my art.

**Favorite Actors**

Kim: Who are your favorite actresses?
Li: Among Western actresses, Ann Sheridan, Greta Garbo, Danielle Darrieux. Among Japanese actresses, well, they are all colleagues, so it's hard to say, but Takamine Mieko (高峰三技子), Todoroki Yukiko (轟夕起子). And in Korea? In Korea, there's just one person. Do you know who? (Hyangran blinks her eyes mischievously.)
Kim: Who is it? (Sinjae smiles like a child, revealing her cute snaggletooth.)
Li: This smiling person who looks like a baby. They say her body is weak and she's often sick, but I don't know how she can be so plump. (She seems to feel she cannot compete with Sinjae's beauty.) But she doesn't come out as well in photographs. Those intricate, delicate charms just don't show up.

**Tuition Fee (授業料)**

While watching *Tuition Fee* (授業料), I thought she was a very pretty woman, but I didn't realize she was such a beautiful person.
Kim: I'm embarrassed. (Her ears really turn red.) I came out looking quite bad there...
Li: No. It's really good. The movie is good too. <183> I don't know how much I cried watching *Tuition Fee*.

**Hyangran's Hobbies**

Li: Let's stop talking about us and go listen to some good music somewhere. Like a tea house...
Kim: That sounds good too, but if we go out, people will mob us...
Li: I really love music. The times I enjoy most are when I listen to good music.
Kim: So your hobbies are music?
Li: Yes, music and movies. I did try to study music, though. I learned vocal techniques from a Russian person.

**Meeting Between the Korean Li Hyangran and Li Hyangran**

Kim: I heard that the Korean Li Hyangran came looking for you here (at the Chosŏn Hotel)?
Li: Yes, she came looking.
Kim: What did she say when she came?
Li: She said that because of me, she, also Li Hyangran, couldn't become successful.
Kim: What does she do?
Li: She said she was at the Moulin Rouge (무란루-주)?
Kim: Did she look like you, Ms. Hyangran?
Li: Well, anyway, she was certainly pretty.
Kim: No, they said her eyes were large like yours, Ms. Hyangran?
Li: No. She didn't say that... Anyway, she was certainly pretty. -End- <184>

**Summary**

This 1941 interview features the famous singer and actress Li Hyangran (Ri Kōran / Yamaguchi Yoshiko) speaking with Korean actress Kim Sinjae. Li Hyangran addresses persistent rumors about her identity, firmly stating she is Japanese (born Yamaguchi Yoshiko in Fushun to Japanese parents from Saga) despite her Chinese stage name and the frequent claims by Koreans that she is a long-lost relative. She recounts anecdotes from her recent, highly popular concert tour in Korea, discusses her preference for staying single to focus on her art rather than being tied to one person, expresses admiration for Kim Sinjae and the Korean film *Tuition Fee*, mentions her love for music and movies, and relates an encounter with another woman named Li Hyangran who blamed her lack of success on the famous star. From a historical perspective, the interview is revealing about the complex identities and pressures faced by individuals during Japan's colonial rule and expansionism. Li Hyangran's carefully worded assertions of her Japanese identity, despite being born in Manchuria and using a Chinese name, alongside the intense public speculation and attempts by Koreans to claim her as one of their own, highlight the blurred cultural and national lines, propaganda efforts (as she was a star in Japanese-sponsored Manchukuo films), and the public's fascination with her ambiguous persona in the context of the Japanese empire.

李香蘭, 金信哉 會見記
때 3월 初旬
장소 京城의 朝鮮호텔에서
사람 李香蘭·金信哉

李香蘭의 故鄕 父母兄弟
金 香蘭씰, 朝鮮사람이라구두 하구, 內地사람이라구두 하구, 中國人이라구두 하는 말이 도는데 대체 어디 사람입니까?
李 그래요? 글세 어디 사람일까요.(香蘭은 信哉를 전너다보며 눈을 깜박해 보인다.)
金 똑 바루 좀 말슴하세요. 정체를 분명히 하서서 궁금답답증에 조갈까지 나는 팬들 속 좀 씨원케 해줌 안좋와요.
李 괜이 자기 멋대루들 상상해 가지구 이러니 저러니 해서 그렇죠.＜180＞ 山口俊子라는 本名을 보드래두 어디 사람인걸 알잖아요.
金 그럼 內地人이 분명하군요.
李 ... (香蘭은 고개를 개웃이 信哉에게 까닥까닥해 봏인다.)
金 그럼 출생지는 어디세요?
李 아버지 어머님은 內地 佐賀縣 殆生이죠. 그리구 전 撫順서 났대요.
金 현재 거주하는덴 어디구요?
李 北京입니다.
金 李香蘭이란 이름을 가진 동기는 어디있죠?
李 저언에 新京放送局에서 가수로 應募했을 때 李香蘭이라구 했어요. 말하자면 藝名이죠. 이름이 中國人이나 朝鮮人같으니까 각각 제몃대루들 생각하는 모양이드군요.
金 그럼 父母가 예서 저서 나타나는 것두 그래서 그럴까요?
李 아마 그런가봐요. 우리 어머니 우리 아버지 동생들 지금 시퍼렇게 北京서 살구 있는데요.
金 그런데 웨 香蘭氏가 新聞記者에게 아버지 어머닐 찾겠노라 하섰어요.
李 그런 말 한 일이 없어요. 부모동생이 퍼렇게 살어있는데 웨 그런 말함니까. 제가 우리 부모의 맛딸루 제 동생이 여섯이얘요. 저까지 일곱이죠. 바루 다음 동생이 지금 東京가서 공부하구 있어요. 걔가 가서 공부하구 있길래 東京에두 한 살림사리 채려놨어요. 그래서 종종 가드래두 객지가 안얘요. 信哉씨두 놀러오세요.(香蘭은 信哉를 몹시 좋와하구 귀해하는 눈치었다. 똑 자기의 동생같은 태도다.)
金 고맙습니다. 놀러가겠어요... (信哉가 愛嬌가 담북 담긴 얼굴에 덧니를 방긋이 내놓며 웃는다. 그리고 나선 다시 말을 있는다.) 그럼 新聞에 난 말들이 죄다 虛說일까요?
李 어떠 어떠한 말이 났습니까?
金 釜山서 나타났단 사람들은 꼭 자기 딸이요 동생인 것이 분명하다구. 그 증거루 어디 어디 흠집있는 거라든지 사마구 있는 것까지 마쳐냈기 때문에 지금 여기선 香蘭씰 틀림없는＜181＞ 조선여자라구들 하나봐요.
李 글세 아버지라구 하는 사람 어머니라구 하는 사람 동생이라 옵바라 하는 사람이 여기서 저기서 툭툭 튀어나타나는데 인제 그게 한두 번이 아니기 때문에 심쪽두 안해저요.
金 그런 일이 몇번이나 됩니까?
李 東京서 臺灣사람두 절 누이동생이라면서 찾어왔구요 釜山선 新聞에 난대루 어머니라는 者 언니라는 者 오빠라는 者가 있었구요. 大邱선 동생이라는 여자가 찾어오구요 서울선(朝鮮호텔) 東京가서 音樂學校다닌다는 남자가 찾어와서 절더러 누님이라구 하겠죠. 그런데 젤 골란한 건 釜山서 였어요. 아니 어머니라구 하는 者가 막우 울며 자기 딸이라는데 참 어떡했음 졸지 모르겠두군요.
金 그이들이 자기 딸이 분명하다구 하면서 없어진 자기딸의 德壽小學校 卒業때 알범을 가지구 와서 香蘭씰 뵈여드렸다는데 정말 보셨어요.
李 그런 일 없었는데...
金 그래두 소문엔 香蘭씨가 그 앨범을 보시구 얼굴이 같긴 하지만 절대루 아니라구 대답했다구요. 그러구 그 아니라구 대답하는 표정이 아무래두 이상스런 데가 있드라는데요.
李 참 무선 세상이군요. 그런 일 절대루 없어요.

全鮮 各地公演과 거기 딸린 얘기
金 참 이번 朝鮮公演은 어디 어디 하셨죠?
