Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5권 제10호
Publication Date: 1933년 10월 01일
Article Title: 갈바람은 산들산들 (The Autumn Wind Blows Gently)
Author: 岸曙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The autumn wind blows gently, whirling around the wide field
This day too, the sun sets, a red glow on the West Mountain (西山)
Yellowed leaves flutter, brushing against the East Mountain.
If past times are a dream, shall I leave just like this?

The autumn wind blows gently, brushing against the sea
Where have the boats that came and went gone? Only waves lap, lap
Because the departing body feels desolate, the seagull cries out<18>
If everything is a dream, shall we too leave?

The autumn wind blows gently, whirling sorrowfully
It crosses mountains, circles fields, and crosses the sea
The autumn wind blows gently, is everything a dream?
Oh, my love wandering a thousand ri in a foreign land (他關千里), is love too a dream?<19>
<18-19>

**Summary**

This poem by An Sŏ, published in 1933, uses the imagery of the gently blowing autumn wind to evoke feelings of transience, melancholy, and longing. The wind touches fields, mountains, and the sea, witnessing the passage of time ("지낸 세월 꿈이라니") and the departure of things ("오가든 배 어데가고"). The speaker questions whether life and even love are fleeting dreams, culminating in a direct address to a loved one far away ("他關千里 도는 님아"). From a historical perspective, the poem's themes of wandering, sorrow, and the dreamlike nature of existence, while personal, could resonate with the sense of displacement and loss experienced by Koreans during the colonial period. The reference to being far from home ("他關千里") might subtly reflect the experiences of Koreans migrating or feeling alienated within their own colonized homeland.
