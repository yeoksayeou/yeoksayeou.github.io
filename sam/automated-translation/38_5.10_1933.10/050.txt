Publication Date: 1933년 10월 01일
Article Title: 秋宵 (Autumn Night)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Autumn Night
						P'ain

Entering a deep Mountain Fortress (山城)
Lighting incense
I wish to pass this whole night with the stone Buddha.

This young person's autumn night
without dear friends,
I miss the stone Buddha, truly miss it. <17>

**Summary**

The poem expresses a deep sense of loneliness and yearning on an autumn night. The speaker wishes to retreat to a secluded mountain fortress and find companionship or solace with a stone Buddha, emphasizing the absence of human friends and a longing for connection, albeit with an inanimate, spiritual figure. For historians, this short poem by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan) might reflect the feelings of isolation or spiritual searching experienced by some Koreans during the colonial period, potentially turning towards traditional symbols like Buddhism and mountain fortresses as sources of comfort or escape from the contemporary reality.
