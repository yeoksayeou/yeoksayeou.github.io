方今 搜査中
鍾路에서 잔뜩 취하여 엄벙덤벙 거러가는 주정방이 신사더러 구세군의 여사관이
「오, 불상한 하느님의 아들이여 당신은 악직 예수 그리스도를 발견치 못하섯군요.」

그 사내 「호, 지금 차저 다니는 중이야.」＜32＞

***

Currently Under Investigation

On Chongno, a female officer of the Salvation Army said to a drunken man who was staggering along completely intoxicated,
"Oh, you pitiful son of God! You have not yet discovered Jesus Christ."

The man replied, "Hoh, I'm looking for him now." <32>

**Summary**

This short anecdote recounts an exchange on Chongno street between a female Salvation Army officer and a very drunk Korean man. The officer laments that the man hasn't "found" Jesus Christ, and the man humorously retorts that he is currently "looking for" him. The piece uses wordplay on the verb "찾다" (to find/look for). For historians, this brief item offers a glimpse into everyday life in colonial Seoul (Keijō/Kyŏngsŏng), showcasing the public presence and street evangelism activities of Western missionary groups like the Salvation Army and reflecting local interactions, even humorous or dismissive ones, with these efforts. It also casually depicts public drunkenness.
