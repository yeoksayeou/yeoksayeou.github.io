失意 (Disappointment)
						P'ain

When leaving, with the lips
Cut the ring finger (無名指)
Sprinkle red blood in the sky.

Blood gushing from a young body
There is no one to receive it
Vainly sprinkle it in the sky.<16>

**Summary**

This short poem by P'ain expresses a sense of deep disappointment and wasted potential. The speaker performs a symbolic act of self-harm, cutting their ring finger and scattering their blood into the sky because there is no one to receive it. It conveys a powerful feeling of youthful energy or passion being spent in vain. For historians, the poem reflects the potential despair and frustration felt by individuals, possibly intellectuals or artists, during the colonial period, where avenues for meaningful action or reception might have felt severely limited, leading to feelings of futility.
