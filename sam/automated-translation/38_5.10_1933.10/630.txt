Self-proclaimed Beauty

Young female students gathered at some restaurant and after eating well, one of the students said,
"Tonight, let's have the most beautiful among us pay the bill."
"Good, good. I agree!"
"No objections. No objections!"
At that moment, one female student who had been quiet off to one side said,
"Hey everyone, this is too much. How am I supposed to pay for all of that?" <111>

**Summary**

This short article recounts a joke where a group of female students decides the "most beautiful" among them should pay the restaurant bill. One student, initially quiet, implicitly claims the title by complaining about having to pay the entire amount. For historians, this piece offers a small glimpse into the social interactions and humor involving young, modern female students ("여학생") in colonial Korea during the 1930s. It reflects contemporary perceptions or stereotypes about female vanity and wit, presented in a lighthearted manner within a popular magazine.
