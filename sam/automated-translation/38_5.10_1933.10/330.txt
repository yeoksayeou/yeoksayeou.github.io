Magazine Title: 三千理 제5권 제10호
Publication Date: 1933년 10월 01일
Article Title: 時調選 (Sijo Selection)
Article Type: 시 (Poetry)

Why do you intend to leave?
For what reason do you go?
Do you leave without cause because you tire of me?
Or have you heard the words of others?
Oh my love, how sorrowful, how sorrowful I am,
Seeing you seem to depart.

The bird crying in Shu (촉) longs for the Han (한) nation,
The flower smiling in the spring rain does so as it meets the season.
Let it be, each has its own feelings, laughing and crying.

Moon, bright moon, moon shining before my beloved's window,
Is my beloved there alone?
Whose love do they cherish? Oh moon, tell me what you see.
Life and death are decided.

On the night I parted from my beloved,
Why could I not die?
I should have plunged into the deep waters of the Han River (한강수),
But that I live now is to see my beloved again.

Emperor Shun (순), virtuous, passed away on Changwuya (蒼梧野),
To whom did he pass the 'Southern Wind' of his five-string zither (오현금) before passing?
Now, I cannot but grieve deeply for Zhao Zilong (뎡 호룡비).

Feed the Red Hare horse (적토마) well until it's plump, let it drink deep from the Tumen River (두만강수), <54>
Take the Longquan sword (룡천검), the sharp blade, and sharpen it again, hold it ready,
When will I serve my lord (성주) and see a reign of great peace?

The moon is bright, the moon is bright,
Boarding a boat, I descend to Jinling (금릉).
Below the water is the sky,
And in the midst of the sky is the bright moon.
Oh, boat boy, fetch the submerged moon; let's enjoy the moon fully and get drunk.

Lying down, does sleep come? Waiting, will my beloved come?
Now, even if I lie down, how could sleep possibly come?
Shall I pass this long night just sitting in one place?

My one regret in life
Is that I was not born in the age of Fuxi (희황시절); though I might wear plain clothes (초의)
And struggle planting trees and harvesting fruits,
I endlessly admire how people's hearts were pure then.

White heron, do not go where crows fight.
The angry crows might envy your white color.
Will they soil the body cleansed white in the clear river (청강)?

Don't laugh, great phoenix, that the swiftlet (감장 새) is small.
In the vast sky of ninety thousand li, both you and I fly.
Let it be, why speak of birds of the same kind?

Do not laugh, white heron, that the crow is black.
Though its outside is black, is its inside also black?
Is it only you whose outside is white but inside is black?

Casting a line in the blue waves, loaded onto a small boat,
The sound of rain on the clear river at sunset is even better.
Luring the golden carp on the willow bank,
Heading towards Xinghuacun (행회촌). <55>

Though Mount Tai (태산) is said to be high, it is but a mountain beneath the heavens.
If one climbs and climbs again, there is nothing that cannot be climbed,
But people do not climb and only say the mountain is high.

Shennong (신농씨) tasted a hundred herbs and cured ten thousand diseases,
But for the lovesickness of yearning for a beloved, all medicine is useless.
Oh my beloved, it is a sickness caused by you, please save me.

A boatman startled by wind and waves sold his boat and bought a horse,
But the winding mountain paths are harder than the water route.
Henceforth, neither boat nor horse, just plowing the fields.

"Return home, return home," they say, but it's only words, I cannot go.
The fields and gardens are overgrown, how can I not go?
In my thatched cottage, the clear wind and bright moon await me.

They say a star has appeared in the East; little child, go see.
It's not the Three Ministers or Six Generals stars, nor the Big Dipper's usual form,
Perhaps news has come from my beloved.
If truly news has come from my beloved,
Don't you go look, I shall go look myself.

If a great man is born and cannot establish himself and gain fame,
It would be better to cast all aside and grow old without accomplishment.
Beyond this, how could I be entangled in lowly fame and fortune?

Youths of spring green age, do not laugh at the white-haired old man.
Under the impartial heavens, will you remain young forever?
Our youthful pleasures seem like only yesterday. <56>

Even an empress fair as jade turned to dust on the barbarian frontier,
And Yang Guifei (양귀비), the flower that understood words, was discarded on the Byŏngno road.
Ladies, cherish carefully your fleeting beauty.

Wearing a straw coat on the mountain edge, holding a hoe in the light rain,
Weeding the mountain field, then lying down amidst the green shade,
A shepherd boy drives his cattle and sheep, waking me from sleep.

The moon is round and hangs in the azure sky.
Through ages of wind and frost, it seems likely to fall, but
Now, for the sake of the drunken guest, it hangs long over the golden cup.

His strength plucked mountains, his spirit overwhelmed the world, second only to the Hegemon-King of Chu (초패왕),
His loyalty that pierced the autumn frost was above Wu Zixu (오자서).
Ah, surely the awe-inspiring hero is the Marquis of Sujŏng (수정후).

Carve a horse out of jade, let it drink, flowing in the waters of the Ying River (영천수),
Tie it with a bridle on the green grassy bank,
May we be without parting until that horse eats all that grass.

In the twenty days of spring light, how many days are there to see flowers?
If a person's life is a hundred years, how many days are there for youthful pleasures?
Let it be, life is like a dream, shall we not enjoy it?

The mountains are the mountains of old, but the water is not the water of old.
Flowing day and night, how could the old water remain?
People too are like water, they go and do not <57>

**Summary**

This article presents a selection of traditional Korean Sijo poems. The poems cover a wide range of classic Sijo themes: longing for a lost love or homeland, reflections on nature's beauty and indifference, lamentations on the passage of time and fleeting youth, philosophical musings on ambition and contentment, historical allusions, and observations on human nature. The poems vary in tone from melancholic and nostalgic to stoic and reflective. For historians studying colonial Korea, this selection is interesting as it showcases the persistence of traditional Korean literary forms and themes (like loyalty, nature, historical figures such as Yi Sun-sin implicitly via '수정후') during a period of Japanese rule. The publication of such works in a popular magazine like Samch'ŏlli suggests a continued engagement with and valuing of Korean cultural heritage, potentially serving as a subtle form of cultural resistance or identity maintenance amidst imposed assimilation policies.
