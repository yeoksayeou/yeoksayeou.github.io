Handling of Guns by High Officials and Great Generals

On September 16th, at the Yongsan Army Firing Range (龍山陸軍射擊場), Governor-General Ugaki (宇垣總督), Army Commander Kawashima (川島軍司令官), Chief of Staff Ōgushi (大串叅謀長), Military Police Commander Iwasa (岩佐憲兵司令官), and others came out for a shooting competition to see who shoots a gun best. On that day, Governor-General Ugaki, holding a gun handle for the first time in 15 years, scored 15 points, Army Commander Kawashima scored 5 points, and Military Police Commander Iwasa hit 27 points. That is, out of one hundred shots, this many hits were made. It is said that on this day, someone of patrolman rank (巡査級) showed very excellent results, so in actual shooting, even the Governor-General and the Army Commander could not help but lose. Anyway, it's a world where even such high officials and great generals (大官巨將) try handling guns, so one could say, "Truly, wherever humans are, there are gunshots" (可謂 人間到處에 皆銃聲).

50,000 Won for a Love Letter

Recently in London, Britain (英國倫敦), the eighth love letter sent by Napoleon (나파륜), called a hero who covered the world (盖世의 영웅), to his lover Josephine (조세힌) was sold, and the transaction price (取引價格) reportedly reached about 50,000 Won.

Confucius's Surname

During a second-grade Chinese classics class at an ordinary school (普通學校), the teacher suddenly asked,
"What is Confucius's (孔子) surname?"
It's a true story that seems like a lie that none of the children answered. Well then, Gentlemen (諸君), do you know Confucius's surname? A very easy and very difficult question.

150,000 Won Manuscript Fee

Charlie Chaplin (차-리찻푸린), the genius child of Hollywood (하리웃드) and an eternal wanderer from the world to the world, recently published an autobiography titled "Impressions of the World" (世界印象記). Various bookstores (各冊肆) in New York (紐育) competed so fiercely that he eventually received 150,000 Won as a manuscript fee (原稿料). The face of the era's popular child (一代의 人氣兒) and King of Movies (映畵王) shines forth! <23>

**Summary**

This article compiles several short, unrelated news items from October 1933. The main piece reports on a shooting competition held at the Yongsan Firing Range involving high-ranking Japanese colonial officials, including Governor-General Ugaki Kazushige, where they were reportedly outperformed by a lower-ranking policeman. Other snippets mention the high price fetched for one of Napoleon's love letters to Josephine, a classroom anecdote about students not knowing Confucius's surname, and the large manuscript fee received by Charlie Chaplin for his autobiography. From a historical perspective, the lead story is particularly interesting as it depicts top Japanese colonial administrators engaging in a leisurely activity (shooting) on Korean soil, subtly highlighting the militarized nature of the colonial regime ("Truly, wherever humans are, there are gunshots") while also noting their lack of practical skill compared to subordinates. Historians might find the juxtaposition of colonial power displays with anecdotes of international celebrity culture and classical knowledge gaps revealing of the diverse information environment and underlying social commentary present even in seemingly light miscellaneous articles of the period.

大官巨將의 執銃
大官巨將의 執銃

9월 16일에 龍山陸軍射擊場에서는 宇垣總督 川島軍司令官 大串叅謀長 岩佐憲兵司令官 등이 나와서 누가 총을 잘 쏘느냐 하는 사격대회를 하엿는데 그날 15년 만에 총자루를 쥐어 본 宇垣總督이 15점, 川島軍司令官이 5점, 岩佐憲兵司令官이 27점을 마첫다. 즉 백발 중 이만한 적중이 잇섯다는데 이날 巡査級에는 매우 우수한 성적을 보인 이가 잇서 실지사격에는 총독, 군사령관도 지지 안을 수 업섯다 한다. 아무튼 이러케 大官巨將들까지 총 잡아 보는 세상이니 可謂 人間到處에 皆銃聲이로군.

戀愛便紙에 5만원
최근 英國倫敦에서 盖世의 영웅이라든 나파륜이 그이 애인 「조세힌」에게 보낸 여덜재 번의 러브, 레타-가 매매되엇는데 取引價格이 약 5만원에 달하엿다고 한다.

孔子의 姓
普通學校 2年級 한문시간에 교사가 돌연히
「孔子의 姓이 무엇이냐?」
하엿더니 아모개도 대답하는 아히 업더라고 함은 거짓말 가튼 참말이다. 그러면 諸君은 孔子의 姓을 아는가. 아조 쉽고도 아조 어러운 문제.

原稿料 15만원
세계에서 세계에로 영원한 방랑자인 하리웃드의 천재아 차-리찻푸린이 얼마 전에 「世界印象記」라는 자서를 출판하엿는데 紐育잇는 各冊肆가 엇더케고 경쟁하엿는지 결국 원고료로 15만원을 밧엇다고. 一代의 人氣兒 映畵王의 面目躍如! ＜23＞
