On a Night When Graupel Falls
						An Sŏ (岸曙)

As if dead, the village slept deeply,
On a night when rustling graupel (쌀악눈) fell alone.
With graupel white upon your head,
Crossing the mountain, my love came seeking me.

The frequent rooster crowed its first cry,
And faint dawn broke through the dark window.
Seeing my love off over the mountain,
We whispered again of vague promises.

Ten years have passed since parting; now it's all a dream.
When nights come with rustling graupel falling,
Pointlessly, the past cannot be forgotten.
In this unsettled heart, I cannot find sleep.<19>

**Summary**

This poem by An Sŏ (岸曙) reminisces about a secret nighttime visit from a lover during a snowfall of graupel. The speaker recalls the lover's arrival, their parting at dawn with whispered promises, and the lingering, sleepless longing ten years later whenever similar snow falls. The poem captures a sense of nostalgia, loss, and enduring memory. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1933 under Japanese colonial rule, offers a glimpse into personal sentiment and lyrical expression during a politically charged era. While not overtly political, its focus on personal emotion, memory, and longing, using evocative Korean natural imagery (graupel, mountains, dawn), could be seen by historians as a subtle assertion of Korean sensibility and experience within the constraints of the time.

쌀악눈 오는 밤에
						岸曙
죽은 듯시 마을은 잠이 깁헛고
바슬바슬 쌀악눈 혼자 오는 밤
쌀악눈을 하얏케 머리에 쓰고
산을 넘어 우리님 나 차자 왓소.

자즌 닭이 꼬꾸요 첫홰를 치고
어둔 창에 먼동이 훤하게 들제
산을 넘어 우리 님 바라다 주며
뜬 기약을 다시금 소군거렷소.

떠나 10년 지금은 모다 꿈이라
바슬바슬 쌀악눈 오는 밤 되면
애꾸지도 지낸 일 아니니치어
뜬 심사에 이 몸을 잠 못이루오.＜19＞
