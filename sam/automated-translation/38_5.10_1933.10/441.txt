U.S. Marine Recruitment Poster!

In America (米國), posters recruiting Marines (海兵) are posted everywhere (處處). The text (文句) reads:

"U.S. Navy – Defend our homeland (祖國), and in summer (녀름) you can enjoy the cool (시언한) sea breeze (海風). Let's go! Receive high pay (高給) and enjoy pleasant sea travel (旅行)."<75>

**Summary**

This short piece reports on the text found on a U.S. Marine recruitment poster seen in America. The poster emphasizes patriotism ("Defend our homeland"), the enjoyable aspects of naval life ("cool sea breeze," "pleasant sea travel"), and financial benefits ("high pay"). From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it presents a glimpse of military recruitment practices in the United States during a period when Koreans faced conscription or pressure to serve in the Japanese military under vastly different conditions. It might implicitly contrast the perceived volunteerism, appeal to personal benefit (pay, travel, comfort), and patriotism in the US context with the coerced and often discriminatory nature of military service within the Japanese Empire for colonial subjects.
