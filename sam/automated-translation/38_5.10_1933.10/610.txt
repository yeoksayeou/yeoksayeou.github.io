A Kisaeng's Sad Story, Nan-hwa is a Pitiful Woman
						By Yi Pong-hŭi

On an autumn night when a dreary rain falls ceaselessly outside the window, Nan-hwa (蘭花) couldn't fall asleep, tossing and turning, caught in agonizing thoughts.

"So what am I supposed to do? It hasn't even been half a year since I pulled you out of the entertainment world (화류게), and asking you to suffer like this again makes me feel sorry, but saying such words makes my heart ache as if it's being torn apart. However, I am a man who will do great things in the future. I am your husband. You are my wife. As a wife, shouldn't you help your husband in what he does as much as your strength allows? If you consider me your husband and truly love me, please calm your angry <110> father's heart, deceive his eyes for a while, and for the sake of our future, please, please continue that work for just a few more years as I say."

These were the words Nan-hwa's husband had used to persuade her earlier in the day.

However, Nan-hwa hated the thought of throwing herself back into the entertainment world now. The constant pestering of all the men, fussing over her, made her break out in a cold sweat and gave her splitting headaches. Just thinking about the past made Nan-hwa feel utterly disgusted.

"I don't want to. I don't want to. If you don't want to see me anymore, even if you can't just send me away, how can you bear to tell me to do that work again? Even if I die, as long as I am your wife, whether you hate me or love me, I want to stay cherished in your arms forever and ever until the day I leave this world."

Nan-hwa had pleaded like this, mixing coquetry with a stubborn attitude.

"What should I do..."

Nan-hwa thought it over and over again until dawn. As her husband said, if it was out of true love for him and for the sake of his future, Nan-hwa couldn't refuse any word from her husband, but thinking about the disgusting life she had lived, and thinking that her husband was now using tricks to deceive her, she hated the thought of doing that work again, even if it meant dying. <111>

"That's right. Let's just die! Let's solve all problems cleanly with death!"

After the rain had stopped falling, Nan-hwa, still holding onto her troubled and chaotic thoughts, ran out to the Chaeryŏnggang (載寧江) River, where blue waves rippled, flowing towards the sea.

But was Nan-hwa still too weak to tread the path of death? Sitting by the riverbank, she cried and cried, and before her feet, which wandered aimlessly, she saw nothing.

When Nan-hwa's husband opened his eyes, they widened.

"Ah, Nan-hwa! What happened? Nan-hwa, Nan-hwa, what on earth happened?"

Nan-hwa's husband shook Nan-hwa awake. Nan-hwa's husband was frantic, like a madman.

Nan-hwa slowly opened her eyes. Then, looking as if she were smiling faintly, she said,
"There's no need to be so surprised. You abandoned me, didn't you? Abandoned by you, I turned my back on this world. So last night, I went as far as the bank of the Chaeryŏnggang River, but for some reason, my heart was too weak for me to commit suicide. Instead of throwing this dirty body into the river water, which I couldn't bear to do, I turned back, and as a sign of rejecting this whole world, I cut my hair like this.
When people see me in this state, they probably won't be able to hold back their laughter and will turn away holding their noses. But I rather wish for that. Anyway, I will leave your house. And even if I starve to death, I am determined not to work as a *kisaeng* again."

Nan-hwa looked at her husband and sat up in her spot.

The chilly autumn wind blowing after the rain makes the leaves of the autumn-tinted trees fall one by one.
The morning sunlight shines affectionately through the east window.

Was it the unjust mindset of so-called untrustworthy men to drag a woman from the entertainment world into their home, professing undying love, only to coax her with sweet talk and sell her back into the entertainment world once their love cooled?

Nan-hwa was a brave woman. To show her firm will, how courageous was her heart to cut off her hair, precious as life itself to a woman, like three strands of hemp rope, without regret?

But perhaps it shows that women are indeed weak.
Nan-hwa, who couldn't bring herself to die after trying to throw herself into the Chaeryŏnggang River, returned, cut her hair, and ran out of the house—what truly became of her afterward?

Ah! Pitiful. Nan-hwa is said to be doing the *kisaeng* work she swore she would die rather than do again.
The blooming flower Nan-hwa, eighteen years old, has become a laughingstock, her body sold to Sinch'ŏn Hot Springs (信川溫泉), greeting the spring winds and autumn rains, flowery mornings and moonlit nights with sighs, and sending them off with tears?... (End) <112>

**Summary**

The article tells the tragic story of Nan-hwa, a former *kisaeng* (female entertainer). Her husband, after taking her out of that life, pressures her to return temporarily to appease his father and support his ambitions. Distraught but initially refusing, Nan-hwa contemplates suicide by the Chaeryŏnggang River but cannot go through with it. As an act of defiance and rejection of her past and her husband, she cuts off her long hair. However, despite her resolve, the story ends sorrowfully, revealing that Nan-hwa ultimately returned to the *kisaeng* life, sold to Sinch'ŏn Hot Springs. For historians, this story offers a poignant glimpse into the limited agency and social vulnerability of women, particularly those in the *kisaeng* profession, during the Japanese colonial period in Korea. It highlights themes of male exploitation, economic pressures forcing women into undesirable situations, and the societal constraints that could override personal determination.
