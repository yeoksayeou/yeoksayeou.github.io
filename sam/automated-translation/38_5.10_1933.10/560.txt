A Gathering for the 『Novels and Criticism Collection』
Hwaewŏl (懷月)

My 「Novels and Criticism」 was published by Samch'ŏllisa on September 8, 1930, and the publication party was held on the night of the 9th at Tabangwŏn. Actually, at that time, I didn't want to present past papers to the world again, nor did I even think of having a commemorative party for such a mundane booklet. However, my friends and close companions worked hard with excessive sincerity towards me, so I was just apologetic and grateful, simply following along with what they were doing. Later, I found out they intended to even enjoy some drinks with a 50 *chŏn* membership fee, which was quite unreasonable if you think about it. Having even one heavy drinker would be a concern, but almost everyone gathered was the type who might not bring drinks but could certainly drink their fill. Before long, there wasn't a single dish left on the plates, and although the liquor was strong *paegal* (白알), it was meant to lift the spirits, and people came fully prepared for that, but quietly observing, it seemed they hadn't even properly whetted their appetites. However, one or two glasses of alcohol can reverse a person's clear reason with emotion, so anyway, let's drink as much as we can. How could we part ways without excitement on such a rare opportunity? And so, it seems people got drunk, but seeing that everyone was fine afterwards, it appears there were some determined individuals with strong will. The reason for the party, a commemorative event for a拙劣 (humble/poorly written) small collection of works, was something I myself felt embarrassed about, but the result – spending an enjoyable night together in one place with close friends and especially with many people I was newly introduced to – is something I will not forget.
This 「pamphlet」 (팜푸례트) was not written recently, but 4-5 or 5-6 years ago, when there were many flaws in the author's research ability, so even now when I look at this book, I find myself unwittingly adding notes in the margins. There were many errors in the theory of literary policy (文藝政策論). I want to correct this properly someday; it also seems overly filled with just mood (기분). Mood is lovable and precious like youth, but it's hard to say it necessarily becomes something valuable. I just try to console myself by thinking that, whether good or bad, it was an expression of passion.
The congratulatory speeches by several people that night taught me many lessons. Even now, I remember those words. However, I don't know the whereabouts of the speakers of those words; there's no news.

Recollections of a Children's Song Collection
Yun Sŏk-chung (尹石重)

It happened four months ago, too fresh in memory to be called a recollection. It might be better called a belated report.

Publication Party for the Children's Poetry Collection 「The Lost Ribbon」
Date: Night of May 19, 1933
Place: T'aesŏgwan, Insadong, Seoul City
Organizers: Yi Kwang-su (李光洙)
Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰)
Yun Paek-nam (尹白南) and 10 others

Master Ch'unwŏn [Yi Kwang-su] gave the opening remarks. And on my behalf, he spoke of my personal circumstances. <100>
「Speaking of Mr. Yun, he lost his mother at age three and grew up an orphan under the care of his maternal grandmother. When he was still young, his father was involved in some incident and spent seven years imprisoned (囹圄). The lonely smile that occasionally appears on Mr. Yun's face or the sorrowful tone (哀調) that appears in his works are all traces of this solitary life. And Kannani, Kaettongi, the worn-out rubber shoes, the clogs with detached heels that appear in his children's songs—these were all sourced from the area around Sungidong (崇二洞), where Mr. Yun lives, beyond Paksŏkkogae pass.
Mr. Yun completed all his graduation exams in the third term of the fifth year at Yangjŏng High School (養正高普), but due to some displeasure, he dropped out without receiving his diploma (卒業狀). I opposed it at the time, but after that, he went to Tokyo (東京) and stayed for about two years (잇햇 동안). After returning, I met him on the street every day as he frequented the library. What he studied while going to the library, even I don't know. Last summer, he published one volume of children's songs, and this time he published the children's poetry collection 「The Lost Ribbon」. This 「The Lost Ribbon」 can be said to have opened a new path in its subject matter and form.
Currently, he is presiding over 『Ŏrini』 (어린이 - Children's magazine) at Kaebyŏksa (開闢社). He is twenty-three years old and still a bachelor. That's enough for the introduction; let's pick up our spoons before the soup gets cold. Please share many good words while you eat.」
The clinking of chopsticks was heard here and there. Like a borrowed sack of barley, I sat dumbfounded in a corner, moving my spoon absentmindedly.
The people who attended that day were Masters Yi Kwang-su (李光洙), Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥), Pak P'ar-yang (朴八陽), Yi T'ae-jun (李泰俊), I Ha-yun (異河潤), Sim Ŭi-rin (沈宜麟), Ch'a Sa-baek (車士百), Hong Nan-p'a (洪蘭坡), Chŏng In-sŏp (鄭寅爕), and several other teachers, along with friends Chŏng Chin-sŏk (鄭鎭石), Sŭng Ŭng-sun (昇應順), Im Tong-hyŏk (任東爀), Yu Ŭi-t'ak (柳義卓), and several others. The gathering that day, with teachers who were like fathers, uncles, and older brothers, and boys of my age kneeling face to face, felt like a special general meeting of some school support association where the principal, parents, alumni, and students were all mixed together.
Anyway, that one night was an exceedingly joyful and overwhelming night for me. My joy that day did not lie in the grand feast or the excessive praise. When the publication of children's literature, which is boycotted (보이고트 당하고) by the literary establishment and treated poorly by society, received such treatment, my heart was joyful and also overwhelmed.

Cho Myŏng-hŭi's 「Naktong River」 & Yi Ki-yŏng's 「Minch'on」
Pukak Hagin (北岳學人)

Posŏk [Cho Myŏng-hŭi]'s 「Naktong River」 (洛東江) and Yi Ki-yŏng's 「Minch'on」 (民村), two collections of short stories, were published simultaneously in the summer of 1930, when the proletarian literary movement (푸로레타리아 문예운동) in this land was at its most splendid. At the initiative of his friends P'al-bong (八峰), Hwaewŏl (懷月), Yun Ki-jŏng (尹基鼎), Kim Pok-chin (金復鎭), Ch'oe Sŏ-hae (崔曙海), etc., a publication party was held one evening at Ch'ŏngnyangsa Temple (淸凉寺) outside East Gate (東大門) in the outskirts of the city.
It was too long ago, so I don't remember well who gathered there, but the main guests, authors Cho Myŏng-hŭi (趙明熙) and Yi Ki-yŏng (李箕永), were present, and attendees included Kim Pok-chin (金復鎭), Pang In-gŭn (方仁根), Hyŏn P'yŏng-hŏ (玄憑虛), Yang Paek-hwa (梁白華), Im Hwa (林和), Yun Ki-jŏng (尹基鼎), Pak P'ar-yang (朴八陽), Kim Ki-jin (金基鎭), the late Ch'oe Hak-song (崔鶴松), Yi Ik-sang (李益相), Paek Ki-man (白基萬), Pak Yŏng-hŭi (朴英熙), Kim Un-jŏng (金雲汀), Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥), etc., about 30 people in total.
The fee for that day was 50 *chŏn* or 1 *wŏn*.
After the ceremony (式) was finished, the group went near the stream, which possessed the utmost beauty of springs and rocks (泉石), below the temple and took a commemorative photograph. Looking at that photograph now, Ch'oe Sŏ-hae (崔曙海), who was captured in it, has passed away, and the author Cho Posŏk (趙抱石) also went to Siberia (西伯利亞). Besides them, many others have also disappeared from the literary scene according to the vicissitudes of life (人世의 유전).
Posŏk's *Naktong River* received much high praise. When it appeared in 『Chosŏnjigwang』 (朝鮮之光 - Light of Korea magazine), the critic P'al-bong called it an epoch-making work (劃時代的 作品). That evaluation was supported by any connoisseur (識者). Afterwards, he tackled many works of this tendency, but none were as excellent as this work in terms of description or theme. In other words, it was his representative work and simultaneously reached the highest level of the Korean literary world. <105>

**Summary**

This article compiles three separate recollections by different authors (Hwaewŏl, Yun Sŏk-chung, Pukak Hagin) about publication parties held between 1930 and 1933 for their own or colleagues' literary works. Hwaewŏl reflects on the modest but lively party for his *Novels and Criticism Collection*, expressing mixed feelings about his early work. Yun Sŏk-chung describes the emotionally significant party for his children's poetry collection *The Lost Ribbon*, highlighting Yi Kwang-su's supportive speech and the rare recognition given to children's literature. Pukak Hagin recalls the joint party for Cho Myŏng-hŭi's *Naktong River* and Yi Ki-yŏng's *Minch'on*, emphasizing the context of the proletarian literary movement and noting the subsequent divergent paths of attendees, including Cho's exile to Siberia. For historians, these accounts offer valuable glimpses into the social dynamics of the colonial Korean literary world, including camaraderie, financial constraints (50 *chŏn* party fees), the perceived marginalization of genres like children's literature, the prominence and subsequent suppression of proletarian literature (KAPF implicitly referenced), and the personal consequences faced by writers under Japanese rule, such as imprisonment and exile. The lists of attendees also serve as a snapshot of key literary figures active during this period.

『小說及評論集』의 會合
懷月
나의 「小說及評論」은 三千里社의 발행으로 1930년 9월 8일에 출판되엿섯고 출판기념회는 同9일 밤에 茶芳園에서 개최되엿섯다. 사실 그때의 내 생각에는 過去의 논문 등을 또다시 세상에 내여 놋코 십지도 안엇스며 또한 이러한 凡俗한 小冊子에 관해서 더욱이 기념회 가튼 것은 생각지도 안엇섯다. 그러나 知友 親友들이 나에 도는 과대한 성의로서 노력하여주니 미안하고도 감사하엿슬 뿐이며 그저 그들의 하든 데로 따러만 갓섯다. 나종에 알고 보니 50錢 회비로서 酒興까지 맛보려 하엿스니 무리라면 큰 무리다. 酒大將이 한 사람만이라도 걱정일 텐데 거지반 모힌 사람들이 술을 가지고는 못 가도 먹고는 갈 만한 사람이며 얼마 안이 되여서 그릇에 음식이 한아도 남지 안코 술은 아모리 독한 백알이라기는 하나 기분을 유쾌하게 할 것을 밀이부터 단단히 차리고 드러온 노릇인데 가만히 보니 입맛도 충분이 다시지 못한 모양이엿다. 그러나 한 두 잔의 술은 사람의 맑은 理智를 감정으로 逆轉시키여서 여하간 먹을만치 먹어보자. 또 다시 엇기 어려운 好機에 無興으로 헤여질 수가 잇나. 그리해서 취하엿든 모앙인데 후에 무사한 것을 보니 단단한 有志가 잇섯든 모양이엿다. 拙劣한 著作小集의 기념회란 원인은 내 스스로 붓그러히 생각하는 바이엿스나 친한 친구들과 더욱이 새로 인사를 드리게 되는 여러분과 함끠 一席에서 자미잇게 一夜를 지냇다는 결과는 이저버리지 안을 것이다.
이 「팜푸례트」는 近年에 쓴 것이 못되고 4, 5년 5, 6년 전에 필자의 연구력에 만흔 결함이 잇섯슬 때의 것이라 지금도 이 책을 볼 때에는 나도 모르게 페-지 우에다가 加筆을 달리고 잇게 된다. 文藝政策論에는 만흔 과오가 잇섯다. 어느 때고 정당히 이것을 정정하고 십흐며 또 기분만이 橫溢한 듯하다. 기분은 사랑스러운 것이며 청춘과 갓치 귀여운 것이나 결코 가치잇는 것이 된다고는 말하기 어려웁다. 나는 다만 이것은 납부든 조튼 정열의 표현이엿다고 自慰하려 한다.
當夜의 몃 분의 축사는 나에게 만흔 교훈을 주엇다. 지금도 그 말만은 생각난다. 그러나 그 말의 주인공들은 다 어디로 간는지 소식을 알 수 업다.

「尹君으로 말하면 세 살 때 어머님을 여히고 고아로 외할머님 슬하에서 자랏습니다. 君이 아직 어렷슬 때 아버님이 엇든 사건에 관련되여 7년 동안 囹圄의 생활을 보내섯습니다. 잇다금 君의 얼골에 떠도는 쓸쓸한 우슴이나 君의 작품에 나타나는 哀調는 다 이 고독한 생애의 자최라 하겟습니다. 그리고 君의 동요에 나오는 간난이 개똥이 고무신 굽 떠러진 나막신 이것들은 다 박석고개 넘어 君이 살고 잇는 崇2洞 一帶에서 取材한 것들입니다.
君은 養正高普 5학년 3학기에 졸업시험까지 다 치르고 엇던 비위 틀리는 일이 잇서 卒業狀을 밧지 아니하고 학교를 자퇴햇습니다. 나는 그때 반대하엿습니다 마는 그 뒤 東京에 건너가 잇햇 동안 잇다가 돌아온 후 도서관에 다니는 그를 날마다 길에서 만낫습니다. 도서관에 다니며 무엇을 공부하엿는지 그건 나도 모릅니다. 작년 여름에 동요집 한 권을 내고 이번에 동시집 「일어버린 댕기」를 냇습니다. 이 「일어버린 댕기」는 그 取材나 형식에 새로운 길을 열엇다고 하겟습니다.
현재 開闢社에서 「어린이」를 主宰하고 잇습니다. 나이는 스물 셋 아직 총각입니다. 소개는 이만침 하고 국이 식기 전에 어서 숫갈을 드십시요. 잡수어 가면서 조흔 말슴 만이 들려주시기 바랍니다」
