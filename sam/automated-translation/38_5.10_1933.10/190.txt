Flower Selling Bonus Prize

A flower-selling woman wrote and posted an advertisement saying, "To those who buy flowers, I will give not only flowers but also a little bit of soil (土地)." <31>

**Summary**

This brief article recounts a humorous advertisement put up by a woman selling flowers. She promised customers not only flowers but also a "little bit of land (토지)" as a "bonus prize (副賞)". This likely refers tongue-in-cheek to the soil the flowers come with. From a historical perspective, this snippet offers a glimpse into everyday commercial wit during the colonial period and highlights the use of older Korean orthography (꼿, 조곰, 드림니다). Historians might find it interesting as a small example of everyday life, humor, and language use in 1930s Korea.
