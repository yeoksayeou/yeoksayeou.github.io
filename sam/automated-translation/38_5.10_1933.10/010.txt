Article Title: 秋天 (Autumn Sky)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Autumn Sky
						P'ain

That clear sky, ninety thousand *ri* high
There I send my dream.

Tomorrow, whether it rains or the wind blows
Who can take away this one night?

**Summary**

This short poem by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan) evokes a sense of vastness and personal reflection under the autumn sky. The speaker sends their dream into the immense, clear sky, finding solace in the present moment ("this one night") regardless of what the uncertain future ("Tomorrow") may bring. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, subtly conveys a desire for boundless freedom (symbolized by the vast sky and the dream) and emphasizes personal resilience or finding refuge in the present moment amidst challenging external circumstances, even if not overtly political.
