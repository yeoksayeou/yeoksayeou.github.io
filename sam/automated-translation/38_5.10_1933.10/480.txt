失手集
Collection of Blunders

During the funeral procession for the late Mr. Kim Ki-jung (金琪中), whoever the sensible person was who served as the funeral director placed a floral wreath from the sukiyaki restaurant T'aesŏgwan (太西舘) at the very front of the dozens of wreaths, and also placed one from some Rice Polishing Cooperative (精米組合) at the very front of the dozens of memorial banners (挽章). Even someone who had only graduated from elementary school (普通學校) wouldn't make such a senseless and insulting arrangement for the deceased. Those who served as the funeral directors that day should rightly be ashamed to show their faces in the streets of the capital.

Around lunchtime, they allow deliverymen from restaurants, carrying earthenware bowls (똑백이) of sŏllangt'ang (설낭탕), to enter through the main gate of a certain Western-style large building housing a newspaper company. Has the newspaper company forgotten about maintaining appearances? It's embarrassing even in the eyes of foreigners (外人所視), so it ought to be improved (宜當改善).

Rationalization
Modern Girl 1 — How on earth can you go see a moving picture with a fellow you don't even like?
Modern Girl 2 — What does it matter? I enjoy the moving picture, and he enjoys me...

Suit Measurement
A tailor measuring a very fat man for a Western suit asked, "Shall I make it to your pre-meal measurements or your post-meal measurements?"

Thrift
He was an extremely stingy person. During wartime, he strictly instructed his subordinates not to let anyone requesting donations <80> inside at all.
However, one time, a very beautiful woman came requesting a donation for the Red Cross. Because she was so beautiful, they ended up letting her through to where the master was. And so, in the end, the woman managed to secure a donation of 500 won from the master of this house, famous for his stinginess.
As the woman was leaving, she came back shortly after in a hurry.
Producing the cheque (小切手) she had just received, she said,
"Ah, look here. There's no signature on this."
"I make all my donations anonymously. I never sign them." <81>

**Summary**

This article, titled "Collection of Blunders," critiques lapses in social decorum and common sense in 1930s Korea, citing examples like the improper arrangement of funeral wreaths and banners, and allowing food deliverymen into the front entrance of a major newspaper office. It then presents three short, humorous anecdotes: one about a "modern girl's" pragmatic view of dating, another about a tailor measuring a fat man, and a third about a notoriously stingy man who donates generously to a beautiful Red Cross volunteer but refuses to sign the cheque, claiming he always donates anonymously. From a historical perspective, the article offers glimpses into social anxieties surrounding modernization and Western influence (Western buildings, suits, modern girls, moving pictures) in colonial Korea. It reveals contemporary concerns about maintaining public appearances, changing social norms, and traditional customs clashing with modern life, alongside humor reflecting societal types like the stingy rich man and the "modern girl."

節約
그는 대단히 인색한 자엿다. 戰爭詩에 寄附를 ＜80＞ 청하는 사람이 잇스면 일체 안으로 드려노치 말나고 부하에게 엄격히 말하여 두엇다.
그런데 한 번은 아조 어엽분 여자가 적십자의 기부를 청하려 왓다. 너무 어엽버서 그만 주인 잇는 데로 通하며 버리엇다. 그래서 끗끗내 그 여자는 인색하기로 유명한 이 집 주인으로부터 500원의 기부를 엇기로 되엇다.
