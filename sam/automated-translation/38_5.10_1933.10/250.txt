大帝의 妻觀 (View on Wives of a Great Emperor)

The famous Western Great Emperor Akparu (악바루 大帝) said, "A man must have four wives. That is, to bear children (兒孩), an Indian woman (印度女子); as a companion to talk with, a Persian woman (波斯女子); for use as a housekeeper (家政婦用), an Afghan woman (아후가니스단 여자); and as a source of pride to take around, a Turkish woman (土耳其女子) should be taken as wives."

Self-proclaimed fine men (好男兒) of the world, what are your thoughts (感慨如何)?<39>

**Summary**

This short piece quotes the Mughal Emperor Akbar (referred to as "Akparu" from the West) on his view that a man needs four wives, each of specific ethnicity suited for a different purpose: an Indian for children, a Persian for conversation, an Afghan for housekeeping, and a Turkish woman for show. It ends by asking self-proclaimed "fine men" for their reaction. For historians, this article reflects the type of exotic or historical information presented in popular magazines of colonial Korea. It showcases contemporary interest in foreign customs, polygamy, and contains explicit ethnic stereotyping tied to gender roles, which might offer insights into how such ideas were consumed or perceived in 1930s Korea.
