Article Title: 『華盛頓에서 돌을 더지면 大佐에게 맛는다.』 (If you throw a stone in Washington, you'll hit a Colonel.)
Author: Not Specified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing/Essay)

"If you throw a stone in Washington (華盛頓), you'll hit a Colonel (大佐)."

This is, of course, a joke (笑話), but it is true that there are many Army Colonels in America (米國). President Roosevelt (루-즈벨트) was also a Colonel. The famous Mr. House (하우쓰氏), who received Wilson's (윌손) trust, was also a Colonel. Lindbergh (닌린드바-크), who made a name for himself by conquering the Atlantic by air (大西洋 征空), is also a Colonel. Anyway, there are many Colonels. If one traces the origin (기원) of these Colonels, when the War of Independence (독립전쟁) broke out in April 1775, the army's preparations were incomplete, so as a hastily devised (急造) incentive measure (장려수단), a person who organized one company (中隊) (59 men) was appointed (任) Captain (大尉), and a person who organized 10 companies was given the rank (官等) of Colonel in one leap. The government, needing troops, frequently used this method (방법) to form (편성) the army, and in the end, looking at the total figures for the 8-year Revolutionary War (革命戰爭), it is said that a total of 400,000 Army soldiers (陸軍兵) were mobilized, and among them, over 230,000 were soldiers recruited (應募된) by Colonels. Even after that, Colonels kept increasing (증가), and it is said that today (今日) there are still many Colonels in various places (各地) across America (亞米利加). <108>

**Summary**

The article humorously notes the abundance of US Army Colonels, citing famous examples like Roosevelt, House, and Lindbergh. It traces this phenomenon back to the American Revolutionary War (1775), explaining that due to the urgent need for troops, individuals who raised ten companies were rapidly promoted to Colonel. This method led to over 230,000 soldiers being recruited by Colonels during the war, out of a total mobilization of around 400,000, and the number of Colonels continued to grow thereafter. For historians of colonial Korea, this article offers a small glimpse into how Koreans perceived the United States military structure and history during the 1930s. It reflects an awareness of American political figures and historical events, potentially indicating sources of information available beyond the Japanese empire's official narratives, even if presented as a light, anecdotal piece.

『華盛頓에서 돌을 더지면 大佐에게 맛는다.』
이것은 물론 笑話나 그러치만 사실 米國에는 陸軍大佐가 만키는 하다. 대통령 루-즈벨트도 大佐엿다. 윌손의 신임을 밧든 유명한 「하우쓰」氏도 大佐 大西洋을 征空하여 일홈을 날 「닌린드바-크」도 大佐다. 엇잿든 大佐가 만타. 이 大佐의 기원을 따저 본다면 즉 1775년 4월 독립전쟁이 발발하자 육군의 준비가 미완성하엿기 때문에 急造의 장려수단으로 1중대(59명)을 편성한 사람을 대위로 任하고 또 10중대을 편성한 사람에는 일약 大佐의 官等을 주엇다. 군대가 필요한 정부에서는 작고 이 방법으로 군대를 편성하여 결국 8년간의 革命戰爭을 통산하여 보건대 陸軍兵은 總數40下人 동원되어 잇는데 그중 23만명 이상이 실로 大佐의 손에 應募된 병정들 이엇다고 한다. 그 뒤에도 大佐는 작고 증가하여 今日 亞米利加 各地에는 아직도 大佐가 만히 잇는 것이라고. ＜108＞
