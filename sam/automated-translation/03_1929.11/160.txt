Article Title: 雜談室(續) (Conversation Room (Continued))
Author: 崔麟 (Choe Rin), 金東赫 (Kim Tong-hyŏk), 洪秉旋 (Hong Pyŏng-sŏn)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing/Essay)

Choe Rin says:

To see Chosŏn (Korea), going far away to a foreign country (外國) is the most accurate way. When I went to Europe (歐羅巴), comparing it with various other countries, I looked back at Chosŏn. At that time, I keenly felt how much value and brilliance this land has. If anyone truly wants to understand (理解) Chosŏn, it would be good to visit a foreign country once. Just like how one's own house, rather than being viewed from inside, is seen more clearly in its entirety (全體) and position (位置) when viewed from far outside the village entrance (洞口 밧갓헤).

Others say they travel the world (世界) to see foreign countries from Chosŏn, but I did the opposite; I went overseas (海外) several years ago (數年 前) to see Chosŏn.

Kim Tong-hyŏk says:

Mr. Yi's (李君) illness is very serious (重態). Nevertheless, he himself doesn't think of dying at all and is desperately striving (애를 바득바득 씁데다) to somehow survive (긔어히 살어나려고). Truly, it's the first time I've seen someone with such an "attachment to life" (生에 愛着). However, according to his attending physician (主治醫), his condition is almost hopeless (거지반 絶望狀態), so truly, where else could there be such a tearful situation?

Hong Pyŏng-sŏn says:

How about trying to sing the hymns (讚美歌) currently being sung in church (敎會) in a "six-beat" (6字박이) style, or in the style of "Arirang" (아리랑) or Susimga (愁心歌調), so that a truly Korean sentiment (朝鮮情調) emerges?<25>

**Summary**

This article presents three short conversational pieces from prominent individuals. Ch'oe Rin argues that one gains the clearest perspective on Korea by viewing it from abroad, referencing his own experience in Europe. Kim Tong-hyŏk recounts the poignant story of a gravely ill man ("Mr. Yi") clinging fiercely to life despite a hopeless prognosis. Hong Pyŏng-sŏn suggests adapting Western church hymns into traditional Korean musical forms like the six-beat rhythm, Arirang, or Susimga styles to better express Korean sentiment. Historically, the piece offers glimpses into the mindset of Korean elites during the colonial period, such as the value of international travel for national self-reflection (Ch'oe Rin) and the ongoing debate about cultural adaptation and syncretism, particularly regarding Western religious practices like hymns (Hong Pyŏng-sŏn).

雜談室(續)
崔麟氏 曰

조선을 보자면 멀니 外國에 나가 보는 것이 가장 正確하여요. 나는 歐羅巴에 갓슬 때 다른 여러 나라와 比하여 가면서 조선을 드려다 보앗는데 그 때에 엇더케나 이 땅이 갑이 만코 빗 잇는 곳인가 함을 切實하 늣기엇슴니다. 누구든지 정말 조선을 理解하자면 外國을 한 번 들너오는 것이 조켓드군요. 마치 자긔 집을 그 집 안에서 보기 보다도 멀니 洞口 밧갓헤 나가서 드려다 볼 때에 그 집 全體와 또 位置등이 분명히 보여지는 모양으로

남들은 조선에서 외국을 보려고 世界를 도라다닌다 합데다만은 나는 그 反對로 조선을 볼려고 數年 前에 海外에 나갓든 것이엇슴니다.

金東赫氏 曰

李君의 病은 아조 重態입데다. 그러컨만 自己는 조곰도 죽을 생각을 아니하고 긔어히 살어나려고 애를 바득바득 씁데다. 실로 그러케 「生에 愛着」을 가지는 이를 처음 보앗슴니다. 그러나 本人은 그래도 主治醫의 말을 드르면 거지반 絶望狀態라 하니 실로 이러케 눈물 나는 일이 어대잇습니까.

洪秉旋氏 曰

只今 敎會에서 부르고 잇는 讚美歌를 아조 朝鮮情調가 나타나게 「6字박이」調로 부르든지 「아리랑」이나 愁心歌調로 불러보면 엇덜넌지요?<25>
