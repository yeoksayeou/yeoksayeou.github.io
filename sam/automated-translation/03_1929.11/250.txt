Article Title: 北國點景 (Scenes from the North Country)
Author: 李孝石
Article Type: 소설 (Fiction/Short Story)

林檎 (Apple)

Apple tree grove, beautiful old grove, that grove which cannot be found now... Whether it was the Fall or not, the apple has been favored since Adam's time. The sharp sensation piercing the tongue, the fragrant flower that makes one dream, and the nostalgic old fragrance...

In this place long ago
Do you know, my dear?
Where flowers bloomed and fruit grew heavy
The fragrant apple orchard!

Starting on the hill, sloping down to the stream stretched the apple orchard. When the northern country's cold snow melted and water rose in the willow branches by the stream, sunlight flowed onto the purplish apple tree branches, east for ten *ri*, south for ten *ri*, the flat apple orchard shone beautifully, patch by fertile patch. When the barley in the fields grew ears and the sound of the pear blossoms outside the village was quiet, apple blossoms abundantly enveloped the hill, and the thick fragrance of flowers crossed the hill, crossed the field, crossed the stream, crossed the plain, and gently flowed into the village. Without needing to dream of the lemon scent of southern lands, the apple blossoms here were the villagers' dream. When the village maiden, wearing a *tahŭng* skirt, went to be married, the apple blossoms had fallen, and in late autumn when golden waves rolled through the fields, the frost-touched apples hung heavy, bunch by ruddy bunch. When the warm evening sun slanted onto the hill, carts laden with apples headed toward the village road. A maiden and a bachelor sitting on the cart, eating apples, their hair dyed by the golden sunlight... Who knew if it was the beginning of the Fall or the first step of decline... One bite for you, one bite for me, there were beautiful stories and innocent pastorals overflowing.<35>

In this place long ago
Do you know, my dear?
Where flowers bloomed and fruit grew heavy
The fragrant apple orchard!

However, that too is an old story, an old picture.
The sun scatters, the moon scatters, the position of the Big Dipper changes, and this beautiful scenery also crumbled away, and the quiet northern country stirred of its own accord.
The mountains moved, the stream at the foot of the hill turned its course, and the thirsty apple orchard gradually withered.
The sound of lamps flickered chaotically at the mountain bend, rice paddies were carved out, a new road stretched out, fields were dug up, and telegraph poles stood.
Straw sandals changed into "kollosin" (rubber shoes), pine knot torches changed into electric lights, bellows sheds changed into ironworks, and water mills changed into rice mills.
The fragrant apple orchard where flowers bloomed and fruit grew heavy! That was crushed, and upon it, a station stood.
On the village road of evening where apple carts rolled, two lines of railway tracks stretched endlessly, carrying unfamiliar dreams.
And the angular buildings of warehouses and assembly halls stood unevenly, surpassing the ridge.
Sooty smoke snatched away the beautiful stories, and the smell of paint swallowed the fragrance of flowers.
The railway carried goods from deep within Manchuria (만주) and carried away migrants. Maidens were carried off to the red-light district, and young men were carried off to prison.
Smoke, paint, railway, station, factory, warehouse, assembly hall...
This is now the new scenery of this village in the northern country. A distorted picture.
The poisonous air of the changing times completely killed the village consciousness.
However, what remains unchangingly beautiful are the sunlight and the moonlight. The sunlight that ripened the heavy apple clusters, the moonlight that shone on the apple blossoms at night, still shine with the same light upon the railway, the factory, the assembly hall...

마우자 (Russian)

Deep village, dense mountains. Soaring rocks. Hot springs. Clear streams. The cooing of pigeons filtering through the forest mixes with the flowing clear stream. Following the stream up, on a quiet, secluded green hill stands a stark, refreshing villa. A garden like Rakwonji (락턴지). That is the villa of the "Mauja" (마우자 - Russian). It is the villa of the family of Yangkowski (양코스키), an elderly White Russian who fled the capital when the revolution erupted and escaped far east.
As trains arrived and the world changed, even this remote village, which used to fear the sight of a mere shovel and pickaxe, now saw the arrival of high-nosed, different-looking "Mauja." On the site of an old fortress where Jurchen people once entered in ancient times, now different-looking "Mauja" entered and spread their limbs among the white-clad folk.
The Russian women strolling the streets with arms and napes exposed, the Russian women splashing in the hot spring water, are a spectacle of the village.
The hot water that melts the frozen flesh of the cold country, the crowd of beauties floating within it. Like a school of mermaids in a fog-deep sea, deeply submerged in the water, then coming out before the spring, languidly stretching their long waists—this elegance seems far more profoundly charming than that of Yang Guifei in olden times. Reflected in the sunlight filtering through the window, the gleaming flesh*—breasts, waist, belly, thighs—all dazzling like white snow. A school of smooth creatures. A school of white creatures.
The summer villa, on the green lawn, wrapped in crimson and green shimmering clothes, gathered around a white table, drinking and laughing, children running and playing along the edge of the lawn. Driving nimble automobiles along the village road, driving to the streets, to the beach. It is a desirable scene. A fragrant scene of the village.
Even those who fled their country, unable to bring anything, escaping in an instant, are like this. Even coming to this deep mountain village, their luxury remains unchanged. It seems the bourgeoisie (불조와), wherever they go, know how to enrich their lives and how to enjoy pleasure.
Anyway, the "Mauja" at the hot springs are a desirable scene, and a displeasing<36> scene.

C 驛風景 (Scene at C Station)

The two lines of stretching railway tracks give a chilling sensation to the spine, and the cosmos flowers on the embankment sway gently in the faint breeze—a quiet village station.
Perhaps because the train time was near, the front yard was noisy with the chatter of elementary school students, and inside the waiting room, a couple of "ankkan" (안깐 - women) carrying baskets of "kaenggae" (갱개 - potatoes) jingled coins bought at the market.
Suddenly, the platform became noisy.
The clamor of "travelers" (나그내) chatting and station workers shouting was heard loudly.
Bursts of laughter erupted intermittently, blowing like the wind, and mixed within it, the sound of someone shouting something loudly could be heard.
People gathered one by one, two by two, crowding around something on the platform outside the ticket gate, causing a commotion.
Visible through the moving crowd was a shabbily dressed, ragged old woman. She wore only one "kollosi" (골로시 - rubber shoe), and her tattered skirt hem fluttered in the wind along with her tousled, unkempt hair.
Looking around at this person and that person with glazed eyes, the woman cried out as if in vain.
"Where has my son gone?"
"You travelers, quickly, give me back my son!"
Kik-kik, laughter burst out, and people avoided the woman's eyes, murmuring amongst themselves.
The station staff, seemingly annoyed, pulled at her arm.
"Hurry up and leave."
"They say the 'Mauja' in bright red uniforms took your son away."
But the woman showed no intention of moving from her spot and cried pitifully.
"My son, who left on the night the snow fell heavily, crossing the pass, why has he still not returned?"
"My son who went away to study to become a provincial governor (도장관), where has he gone, leaving this mother behind?"
Every time a train departed, every time a train arrived, this woman would come to the station platform and pitifully cry out "My son"—she was the pitiful woman of this village, pointed at as the "mad woman."

Her only son, sent off to Seoul to study, did not return even after seven years, even after eight years, even after "getting his provincial governorship." Even if he had gone to pluck stars from the sky, he should have returned by now, but the "son who went to get his governorship" did not come. Yet, believing he was not the kind of "unfilial wretch" who would betray his poor, lonely mother, she leaned against the brushwood gate day after day, her body wasting away, gazing at the village entrance, dreaming of her son's triumphant return in glory, heralded by blue and red flags. She forcefully wiped away the tears that blurred her vision with imagined scenes. But even after ten years passed and railway tracks were laid in front of the village, the hoped-for figure did not appear, only disturbing rumors frequently reached her ears. That he became a socialist (주의자) and was in the newspapers, that he went to prison, that he went to Russia (아라사) with the red-flag carrying "Mauja" (마우자)—following these chaotic rumors, the mother fretted even at the cawing of a crow, and felt distressed even when the moonlight dimmed.
More than ten years passed, then two more years, then three, and finally, in the spring of the thirteenth year. One morning, when the village dogs barked and the crows in the trees were unusually noisy, the postman (전부) brought a small package to this tear-soaked hut. A strange box made of wood, square-shaped—ah, how could she have known that it was her son? How could she have known it was her son who made her dream of a "provincial governorship" and aged her with uncertain waiting? Her long-awaited son had returned after thirteen years, curled up inside a small wooden box. Pulling out the cotton wrapping, a couple of thick, bundled bones scattered gruesomely onto the mother's lap. It was his body, who had died abroad while fighting, whose comrade had gathered his bones and sent them far away to his homeland for burial.
"What is this!"
The words were merely a thought in her mouth; overcome by too great a shock,<37> she fainted on the spot. Only when that day dawned did the sound of wailing finally flow from this house. From then on, the mother gradually lost her sanity. She was no longer the sane mother of the old days. Day after day, night after night, she wandered the village, weeping mournfully, roaming the station crying out "My son." Whether it rained or snowed, whenever a train departed, whenever a train arrived, the sound of her searching for "My son" flowed pitifully on the station platform.
"Where has my son gone?"
"My son who left on the night the snow fell, crossing the pass, where have you gone, leaving this mother alone?"
The sound of a whistle came from the mountain bend, and the train arrived.
The station staff, the passengers boarding the train, the people gathered on the platform, all dispersed.
The woman peered into the passenger car windows one by one, beating the window frames with both arms, crying out wretchedly.
"Where is my son?"
"Where is Ilsuni (일순이)?"
People leaning out the windows spat phlegm and shut the windows.
Mixed among the white clothes and Western suits, seemingly heading for the hot springs, two "Mauja" gentlemen got off the train.
The woman, clenching her fists, rushed over to them frantically, grabbed hold of the two gentlemen, and demanded,
"Give me back my son!"
"You 'Mauja' who took my son, quickly find my son and give him back!"
"..."
The two gentlemen, not understanding the situation, stood blankly for a moment, then chattered something in barking voices, pushed her away, and headed for the ticket gate.
But the woman grabbed onto the gentlemen's clothes with both arms, followed them, still shouting loudly.
"You travelers, give me back my son!"
"Mauja, quickly find my son!"

飛行機 (Airplane)

Like dye squeezed from bellflower blossoms spilling onto a smooth surface, airplanes came flying in pairs across the deep blue sky of the northern country.
Wings shining white in the sunlight, the light, nimble sound crossed the overlapping mountain ranges thousands of feet above sea level, vibrating the heavy air.
"Airplane!"
"Airplane!"
People coming over hills and crossing waters to see the airplanes crowded the streets from dawn.
On the sandy hill of the training ground (련병장), waves of white clothes surged.
"Wow! Truly!"
"Isn't it terribly high?"
"What skill allows them to do that?"
On the large field of the training ground, yellow-uniformed soldiers, their sword hilts glittering, strutting about (웃줄하며), formed ranks like a swarm of bees in the center, and the drill began.
Suddenly, the sound of rifle fire, the sound of rapid-fire guns, crackled like popping beans, and the roar of cannons, seemingly swallowing heaven and earth, shook the mountains and fields.
Airplanes swooped low over the white clothes in pairs, and bombs followed immediately, carving into the sandy hill.
Smoke and the smell of gunpowder enveloped the hill.
The waves of white clothes scattered chaotically with a roar.
The people who had come to see the airplanes scrambled away over hills and across waters as fast as their legs could carry them.
"Airplane!"
"Airplane!"
"War is breaking out!"
"Where should we go?"
Troubled by the unwelcome airplanes, the villagers could not sleep that night due to anxiety.

모던 껄·메론 (Modern Girl · Melon)

While possessing the harsh winter with fierce winds blowing from Siberia (서백니아) and blizzards sweeping down the banks of the Tuman River (두만강), Hoeryŏng (회녕), on the other hand, possesses the fresh summer of beautiful women and melons (참외). Perhaps because it lies along the clear-watered Tuman River, Hoeryŏng has many beauties with fine complexions.<38> Although one doesn't know what correlation exists between beautiful women and melons, in Hoeryŏng, where beauties are many, melons are also plentiful. Centered on Hoeryŏng, the areas along the railway line heading north are mostly famous production sites for melons.
Hoeryŏng melons piled up like mountains on the roadside. Small, fist-sized yellow melons crunching between the teeth—the fresh melon (메론) of the northern country, the fragrance of summer.
The beauty glimpsed enticingly through the doorway of every house in a cool ramie jacket, a cherry cluster over the wall one wants to pluck—also the fragrance of summer.
The scent of flesh in the houses, the scent of melon in the streets—summer Hoeryŏng is a town of fragrance.
Hoeryŏng, summer, melon, beauty—.
Here is one scene of Hoeryŏng, also a fresh scene of beauty and melon.
It was a stuffy afternoon.
As the whistle of the train returning after skirting the border line sounded loudly, the eyes of the border guards in front of the station sharpened.
As the train let off steam, passengers poured out into the wide yard.
White clothes, Western suits, geta sandals, Chinese soldiers, coolies, "Mauja"—in the border city, now a crowd of people of different nationalities poured out.
Amidst them, there was one spot of red that uniquely caught the eye. A modern beauty with bobbed hair and Western clothes. A straight nose expressing strong will, proud eyes, firmly closed lips, a stern face difficult to approach—an extraordinary, educated modern girl (모던껄). On top of that, an eye-catching bright red blouse, a slender neck curving below the bobbed hair, smooth legs below a silver-green skirt, the primary condition of a modern beauty, beautiful legs—a fragrant Hoeryŏng beauty.
Whether they were merchants (장괘) or coolies (쿠리), anyway, men wearing Chinese clothes (호복) or perhaps young men with shaggy hair wearing rubashkas (루파시카) were all subjected to investigation (취됴) or body searches, which seemed plausible. But for a beautiful woman, an educated modern girl, there should be no suspicion warranting a body search or investigation. However, being experts, their perception, unlike that of ordinary people like us, was sharp enough that, for whatever reason or however it appeared to them, they detained this solemn modern girl.
After thoroughly rummaging through the contents of bags and shabby bedding bundles in the wide yard, completing the investigation of several suspicious individuals, their turn came to the anxiously waiting beauty.
Writing something in a small black notebook, after questioning her briefly, they finally ordered her to open the small basket she carried.
Inside the beauty's box! Could there possibly be opium inside? Could there be a bomb? Perhaps lamenting that the secret box of a lovely young woman was about to be carelessly opened in the middle of this main road, like the naked body of a woman on an operating table, the woman's face momentarily clouded over. But this being the border, and it being an official order, it couldn't be helped.
The beauty carefully opened the basket and showed the inside. The guard constable, with slender eyes that were either lecherous or wary, insisted she take out the contents.
The beauty, wordlessly, whether from shame or anxiety, took out one item after another.
A handkerchief, cosmetics, an opera bag.
The beauty hesitated for a moment, then continued, taking out silk stockings and a bright red slip (드로스).
The constable shook out the bright red slip, tempting just to look at, and the flesh-scented silk stockings right there on the main road.
The beauty, blushing with shame, hurriedly tried to stuff them back into the box, but the constable's hand stopped her, pointing inside the box.
"What is this?"
"..."
Wordlessly, she took out melons in a net bag.
"Melons (참외)?"
When and where she bought them was unknown; the melons, having lost their vitality and freshness, lay shriveled, two or three of them, inside the net. Small<39> and fist-sized, yellow, they were undoubtedly of the Hoeryŏng melon variety.
"Melons!"
The policeman, who had perhaps expected something different from melons, tilted his head and exclaimed once more.
Having shown all there was to show, the beauty gathered her belongings piece by piece and stood up from that spot.
The ambiguous modern girl—what could possibly have been inside her box? Seeming indignant at having only suffered the humiliation of having her stockings shaken and undergarment exposed publicly on the main road for nothing, the beauty crossed the wide station yard, heading toward the street with overflowing dignity.
The policemen put their heads together, looking into the black notebook, discussing something animatedly. Being experts, their intuition differed from that of ordinary people. They possessed eyes sharper than we could imagine.
As the beauty finished crossing the wide yard, their sharp gaze fell upon her retreating figure, and they called her back again.
Whether ten times or a hundred times, one must do as ordered without a second word; that is the border. That is the law.
But even if opened ten times, even if opened a hundred times, it was the same basket; had some miraculous event occurred in the meantime? Could something else have gotten inside?
As before, the handkerchief, cosmetics, opera bag, stockings, slip, and melon net bag spilled out onto the roadside. No miracle had occurred.
"Melon."
Perhaps even in Hoeryŏng, famous for melons, the melon was still tempting; the policeman picked up a melon from the net bag. Was that withered, shriveled melon so tempting? Well, perhaps compared to the fresh ones on the street, even a withered one stored in the beauty's possession might be tempting. As if about to put it straight into his mouth, they savored the scent of the withered melon.
"You can't have that."
The beauty suddenly tried to snatch the melon from their hands.
"It's for the little one at home."
"The little one at home?"
The policeman smiled meaningfully.
"You can't."
In the midst of this struggle, alas, the melon held in the hand fell to the ground. The melon fell and broke—and from inside it, a single jet-black weapon emerged. Whether it was a Browning (뿌로닝) type, a Colt (콜트) type, or perhaps a Mauser (모젤) type, it was quite a lovely weapon.
Subsequently, another dropped melon—from within it, jet-black beans scattered profusely.
In the wide station yard, the blue gun barrel, imbued with modern mechanical beauty (근대뎍 력학미), and the black beans, reflecting the border's evening sun, glittered menacingly.
Also reflecting the evening sun, the policeman's glinting gaze sharply pierced the face of the extraordinary modern girl.<40>

**Summary**

This work by Yi Hyosŏk consists of five vignettes depicting scenes ("chŏmgyŏng") from Korea's northern border region in the late 1920s. It contrasts the lost pastoral beauty of the past (symbolized by an apple orchard) with the disruptive forces of modernization (railways, factories, military presence) and social upheaval. We see the arrival of White Russian refugees ("Mauja"), the tragic story of a mother driven mad waiting for her politically active son (who likely died fighting abroad after associating with Bolsheviks), the fear induced by Japanese military exercises involving airplanes, and the tense atmosphere of a border crossing where a "modern girl" smuggles a pistol and bullets hidden inside melons. The story vividly portrays the jarring changes, anxieties, and socio-political complexities of colonial Korea, particularly in a region adjacent to Manchuria and Russia, highlighting themes of loss, modernization's impact, displacement, and underlying resistance or conflict. For historians, the piece offers rich social commentary on the era: the visible presence and varied interactions with Russians (both White émigrés and mentions of Bolsheviks), the psychological toll of colonial modernization and political suppression on ordinary people (the mad mother), the militarization of the landscape (airplanes, drills), and the subtle depiction of anti-colonial activity or smuggling under the guise of modernity (the "modern girl").

그러나 그것도 옛 이약이 옛 그림.
해가 흩으고 달이 흩으고 북두칠성의 위치 변하니 아름다운 이 풍경도 이즈러저 버리고 고요하든 북국도 스스로 움즉엿다.
산이 움즉이고 언덕밋 물줄기 도라버리니 목먹른 능금밧 점점 말너갓다.

연긔 펭키 철로 뎡거쟝 공장 창고 회관...
이것이 이제 북국의 이 마을의 새로운 풍경이다. 이즈레진 그림이다.
산문의 독긔 온전히 마을의식을 죽여버렷다.

마우자

깁흔 마을 욱어진 산. 솟은 바위. 더운 온천. 맑은 시내 숩 속에서 새여오는 비달기 소래와 엄처서 흘으는 맑은 시내. 시내를 따라 올나가 고요하고 오식한 푸른 언덕 우에 웃둑솟은 쳥쇄한 별장. 락턴지 가튼 동산. 그 것이 「마우자」(로서아인)의 별장이다. 혁명이 푹발되자 도웁을 좃겨나 멀니 동으로 다라온 백계로인 양코스키 일족의 별장이다.
긔차가 되고 세상이 변하니 사포와 사밸만 보아도 겁내든 이 벽촌에 지금에는 코놉고 빗다른 「마우자」까지 들어오게 도엿다. 옛적에 女眞人의 들어왓든 옛성터 남은 이 마을에 이제 빗다른 「마우자」 들어와 흰읏 사이에 네활개를 폇다.
팔과 목덜미를 드러내노코 거리를 그니는 아라사미인 온천물에 철벅으리는 아라사미인 마을의 긔관이다.
찬나라의 언 살을 녹이는 뜨거운 물 그 속에 헤이는 미인의 무리. 안개 깁흔 바다의 인어의 무리 가티 집숙히 물에 잠겻다가 샘전에 나와 느릿한 허리를 척척 누이는 풍류 옛적 양귀비의 그 것보다도 휠신 정취가 깁흘 것갓다. 창으로 새여드는 햇빗에 빗처 김르는 살* 젓가슴 허리 베 두다리 할 것 업시 백설가티 현란하다. 밋근밋근한 즘생의 무리. 하아얀 즘생의 무리.
여름의 별장 푸른 잔디 우에 진홍빗 초록빗 빗나는 옷에 싸여 흰테붙을 둘너싸고 마시고 웃고 아해들 잔디밧 가를 뛰고 노는 광경. 경쾌한 자동차를 마을길로 모라 거리로 해수욕장으로 드라이브하는 광경. 탐나는 정경이다. 마을의 양긔로운 풍경이다.
나라를 좃겨 가릴 것도 못가지고 삽시간에 도망해 온 그들로써 오히러 이러한다. 산 깁흔 이 벽촌에 와서도 그들의 호화는 오히려 다름업다. 불조와는 어데를 가든지 간에 생활을 윤잇게 할 줄 알고 향락할 줄 안다.
엇더튼 온천의 「마우자」 탐나는 정경이오 아니꼬운<36> 풍경이다.

C 驛風景

두 줄로 뻐친 철로 등날 싸늘한 촉감을 주고 단 우의 코스모스 간들바람에 회촌회촌 흔들니는 고요한 촌뎡거장.

동안울 두고 터지는 우슴소래 바람결가티 불어오고 그속에 석겨 무엇인지 고함놉히 부르짓는 소래 들녓다.

십 년이 넘어 두 해가 가고 세 해가 가고 마참 열 세 해 되는 봄이엿다. 마을에 개짓고 나무의 갈가마귀 떼 유심히 요란한 하로 아참 눈물에 감긴 이 오막살 안에 조고만 소포 한 개를 전부가 가저 왓다. 나무로 네모지게 짠 괴상한 통-아 그 것이 그의 아들일 줄이야 엇지 아럿스랴. 「도장관」을 꿈꾸게 하고 의문지망에 그를 늙게 한 그의 아들일 줄야 바라고 바라든 그의 아들 조고만 나무통 속에 쪼그리고 저서 열 세 해 만에 도라왓다. 무명보재 집어내니 굵게 추린 뼈 두어개 어머니 무릅 우에 앙크럿케 흐터젓다. 해외에 나가 싸우다 객사한 그의 시쳬 동모가 뼈추려서 고국에 멀니 장사지낸 것이엿다.

모던 껄·메론

서백니아를 불어오는 억센 바람과 두만강 기슭을 스처내리는 눈보라의 모진 겨울을 가진 반면에 회녕은 미인과 참외의 신선한 여름을 가젓다. 물맑은 두만강을 끼고 난 곳이기 때문인지 회녕에는 살빗<38> 고운 미인이 만타. 미인과 참외 그 사이에 엇더한 인관관계가 잇는지 몰으나 미인 만흔 회녕에 참외 또한 만타. 회녕을 중심으로 북으로 드러가는 철도연변이 거개 다 참외의 명산지이다.
행길바닥에 산덤이가티 싸인 회녕참외. 닛사이에 살강살강 같니는 조막만콤식한 노랑참외 신선한 북구의 메론 여름의 향긔.
집집마다 문 박그로 탐스럽게 보이는 서늘한 적삼 속의 미인 한가지 꺽고 십흔 울넘의 앵도송이-또한 여름의 향긔이다.
집에서는 살향긔 거리에는 메론향긔 여름의 회녕은 향긔의 고을이다.
회녕 여름 참외 미인-.
여긔에 한 폭의 회녕 풍경 잇스니 역시 미인과 참외의 산뜻한 풍경이다.
무더운 오후엿다.
국경선을 스치고 도러오는 렬차의 긔적이 요란히 울니자 뎡거장 압마당에 국경정비의 눈이 날카랍게 빗낫다.
긔차가 김을 뿜으니 넓은 마당에 승객이 쏘다저 나왓다.
흰옷 양복 게다 중국군인 쿠리마우자 국경의 도시에는 이제 국적을 달니한 뭇사람들이 쏘다저 나왓다.
그가 온데에 색달니 눈을 끄는 일점홍이 잇다. 단발하고 양장한 현대뎍 미인. 강한 의지의 표현인 반듯한 콧날 자랑놉흔 눈맵시 꼭 담은 입 범하기 어려운 엄숙한 얼골-평범치 안은 교양잇는 모던껄이다. 그 우에 눈을 끄는 샛밝안 웃저고리 단발 밋흐로 가늘게 휘인 목덜미 은초록색 스카트 밋흐로 밋밋한 다리 현대 미인의 뎨일 됴건인 고은 다리-향긔놉흔 회녕 미인이다.
장괘가 되든지 쿠리가 되든지 간에 엇더튼 호복 닙은 사나희나 그려치 안으면 루파시카 닙고 더벙머리 한 청년이면 모조리 취됴를 하거나 몸울 뒤지거나 그럴 듯한 일이지만 아름다운 미인 교양잇는 모던껄에게야 몸두지고 취됴할 아무 혐의도 업슬 것이다. 그러나 그것이 전문가이라 평범한 우리로는 도저히 생각지 못할 만큼 날카라운 그들에게는 무슨 의미로 엇더케 빗취엇든지 간에 그들은 거록한 이 모던껄을 붓들엇다.
가방 속과 람루한 이불 봇다리 속을 넓은 마당에 속속으로 풀어 헤처면서 몃사람의 호인의 취됴를 맛친 후 그들의 순서는 초조히 기대리는 미인에게 도라왓다.
샛감안 수첩에 무엇인지 긔록하면서 잠시간 심문을 하드니 나종에는 그들은 미인의 휴대한 조고만 바스켓을 열라고 명령하얏다.
미인의 상자 속! 그 속에 설마 아편이 들엇술 것인가 폭탄이 들엇을 것인가 귀여운 졂은 여자의 비밀상자가 이제 이 대로상에서 마치 수술대에 올은 녀인의 라체가티 함부로 열리랴함을 슯허하야선지 여자의 얼골은 일순간에 흐렷다. 그러나 그 것도 국경이니 관의 명령이니 엇절 수 업는 일이다.
미인은 바스켓을 조심스럽게 열고 속을 드려다 보엿다. 경비순사는 호색뎍인지 경계뎍인지 가느다란 눈초리로 속의 것 집어내기를 강청하얏다.
미인은 붓그럼인 불안스럼인지 말업시 한가지 속의 것을 집어냇다.
수건 화장품 오페라빽.
미인은 한참 저주하다가 계속해서 비단양말 샛밝안 드료스를 집어냇다.
보기만하야도 유혹뎍인 샛밝안 드로스 살냄새나는 비단양말을 큰 행길 우에서 순사는 휙휙 털어 보앗다.
붓그러워서 얼골 붉어진 미인은 황겁학데 그 것을 되로 상자 속에 쑤서너으랴 하얏스나 순사의 손이 그 것을 막으면서 상자 속을 가르첫다.
「무언가 이 것은?」
「...」
말업시 집어낸 것은 실망태에 너은 참외엿다.
「참외?」
언졔 어데서 사너은 것인지 생긔와 신선미를 일흔 참외 그물 속에 두어개 쪼글쪼글 시드럿다. 조막만<39> 큼직하고 노란 역시 회녕참외의 종자임에는 틀님업섯다.
「참외!」
참외보다 류 다른 그 무엇을 긔대하얏든 경관은 고개짓을 하면서 또한번 외첫다.
보일 것을 다 보엿스니 미인은 주섬주섬 행장을 수습하야 가지고 그 자리를 니러섯다.
애매한 모던껄 그의 상자 속에 들엇스면 무엇이 들엇단 말인가. 공여히 대로상에 양말을 흔들며 속웃을 내보이며 수치만 당한 것이 분한 듯이 미인은 의긔넘치게 거리를 향하야 덩거장 넓은 마당을 건넛다.
경관들은 머리를 한데 뭇고 검은 수첩을 들여다보면서 무엇인지 의론이 분분하얏다. 전문가이니만큼 그들의 눈치는 뭇사람의 그것과는 달으다. 우리들의 생각지 못할만큼 날카라운 눈을 그들은 가지고 잇다.
넓은 마당을 다 건너간 미인의 동수에 날카라운 시선이 가자 그들은 미인을 다시 불넛다.
열 번이고 백 번이고 간에 두말 업시 싯키는 대로 해야하는 것이 국경이다. 법이다.
그러나 열 번 열어도 백 번 열어도 그 바스켓이 그 바스켓이지 그 동안에 신긔한 긔적이   니러 낫단 말인가 다른 무엇이 또 들엇슴 것인가.
여전히 수건 화장품 오페라 빽 양말 드로스 참외망태가 길바닥에 쏘다저 나왓다. 아무 긔적도 니러나지는 안엇다.
「참외」
참외 만흔 회녕바닥에서 그래도 참외가 탐나는 지 경관은 망태 속에서 참외를 하나 집어냇다. 시들어 쪼그러진 참외 그 것이 그다지 탐나는가. 하기는 길거리의 싱싱한 놈보다도 시들엇슬망정 미인의 수중에 저장한 것이니 탐도 날 것이다. 금방 입에다 너을 듯이 탐을 내면서 그들은 시든 참외의 향긔를 맛헛다.
「안돼요. 그것은」
미인은 별안간 참외를 그들의 손에서 빼슬랴 하얏다.
「집의 어린 것 줄 것얘요.」
「집의 어린 것?」
경관은 의미잇게 우섯다.
「안되요 그것은」
이 실네를 치는 판에 가엽다 손에 들엇든 참외 하나 땅에 떠러지고 말엇다. 떠러저 부서진 참외-그 속에서 한 자루의 샛감안 무긔가 나왓다. 『뿌로닝』식인지 『콜트』식인지 혹은 『모젤』식인지 퍽도 귀여운 무긔이다.
계쇽하야 또한개 떠러트린 참외-그 쇽에서는 샛감안 콩알이 우수수 헤여젓다.
뎡거장 넓은 마당에 근대뎍 력학미롤 띄운 푸른 총신과 검은 콩알이 국경의 석양을 바더 무섬게 빗낫다.
역시 석양을 바더 반작이는 경관의 시선이 평범치 안은 모던 껄의 얼골을 날카랍게 쏘앗다.<40><35-40>
