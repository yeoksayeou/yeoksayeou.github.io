Difficulty of Writing

If you look at magazines in Chosŏn now, there are none where a single essay or creative piece exceeds 10 pages. Most are within 5 or 6 pages.
This likely arises from a sales policy aimed at listing as many names of well-known people as possible in the table of contents, but it causes great pain for writers. While other magazines often provide 50 or 60 pages or more for a single work, for us, a work of 50 or 60 pages must be dragged out for nearly a year. For the writer, it is not satisfactory to write, and for the reader, it is not satisfactory to read, but due to the publisher's policy, it seems there is nothing that can be done.

Moreover, the very life of the magazine is unstable. Because of this, writers, especially literary artists among them, hesitate to publish their major works. In literary works, pieces of 50 or 60 pages are the most common type, and if possible, one would want to publish them in a single issue, but due to the publisher's sales policy, they must be dragged out for over 10 installments. On top of that, since one does not know when the magazine might cease publication, it is difficult to readily offer one's major work.

Magazines are in this state, and newspapers, also due to newspaper policy, welcome long serialized stories but refuse <23> creative works of 10 or 20 installments.
Even if published in a newspaper, the work is ruined on the pages of Chosŏn newspapers, which are crudely produced, omitting commas, periods (피료-드), and other various symbols/marks (諸「込物」) due to printing technology.
In such a literary world of Chosŏn, how can a major work emerge? If one were to emerge, that would rather be a miracle.
This is our pain.

However, even under these impossibilities and misfortunes, purely through our efforts alone, we have laid this foundation, however poor it may be today.
This is our pride. <24>

**Summary**

The author laments the severe constraints faced by writers in 1920s Chosŏn. Magazines prioritize short articles (5-6 pages) to list many famous names for sales, forcing authors to serialize longer works over many months, often up to a year. This serialization is frustrating for both writers and readers. Furthermore, the instability of magazines makes authors hesitant to submit their major works. Newspapers, while accepting long serialized stories, reject moderately long creative pieces and suffer from poor printing quality that omits crucial punctuation, degrading the work. Despite these difficult conditions, the author expresses pride in the foundation that has been built for Korean literature through sheer effort. For historians, this article offers a valuable glimpse into the practical challenges of literary production under colonialism, highlighting the underdeveloped publishing infrastructure, the commercial pressures shaping literary formats, and the resilience of Korean writers striving to create amidst limitations.

執筆難

지금 조선 안의 雜誌를 보자면 한 가지의 論 혹은 創作이 10頁 以上되는 것이 엄슴니다. 대개는 5, 6頁 以內이외다.
이것은 知名人의 일홈을 目次에 할 수 잇는대로 만히 나렬하랴는 販賣政策 上으로 나온 것이겟지만 이 것이 執筆家의게는 큰 苦痛을 줌니다. 남의 雜誌들은 한 作品을 위하여 5, 60頁이상을 흔히 제공하는데 우리는 5, 60頁되는 作品은 近1年을 끄을어야만 됨니다. 執筆者의게는 쓰기도 싀언치 안코 讀者의게는 닑기도 싀언치 안치만 出版者의 政策 上 엇지 할 수가 업는 모양이외다.
게다가 더구나 그 雜誌의 生命조차 不安定하외다. 그런지라 執筆家들은 그 가운데도 文藝家는 자긔의 力作을 發表키를 주저함니다. 文藝作品에 잇서서는 5, 60頁의 것은 가장 普通型의 것으로서 할 수만 잇스면 한회에 발표하고 십지만 出版業者의 販賣政策上 10餘회를 끄을지 안으면 안될지오. 게다가 그 雜誌가 언제 廢刊될 지 모르니까 자긔의 力作을 덜컥 제공하기가 힘들겟슴니다.
잡지가 이러한 現像이오 新聞은 또한 新聞政策上 長篇講談은 환영하지만 1,20回짜리의<23> 創作物은 거절합니다.
설혹 新聞上에 揚載된다 할지라도 印刷技術上 컴마와 피료-드와 其他 諸「込物」을 畧해 버리는 粗製品인 朝鮮新聞紙上에는 그 作品을 버림니다.
이러한 조선의 文壇에서 엇지 力作이 나오겟슴니까. 나온다 하면 그것이 오히려 奇蹟이겟슴니다.
이것이 우리의 苦痛이외다.
그러나 이러한 不可能과 不遇의 아레서도 이 것이야말로 순전히 우리의 努力뿌능로 貧弱하나마 오늘 만한 基礎는 닥거 노앗슴니다.
이것이 우리의 자랑이외다. <24>
