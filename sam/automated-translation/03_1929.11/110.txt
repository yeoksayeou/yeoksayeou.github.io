Article Title	雜談室 (Chat Room / Miscellaneous Talk)

雜談室
Hŏ Hŏn Says:

When I went to America, one day a young Korean man I didn't know came looking for me at the hotel where I was staying. When he came in, without any particular sign of being glad to see me, nor any sign of looking down on people, his very first words were a question about how the political situation was back home. I found this strange and asked who he was, but he didn't give his first name or surname, only saying he was from a certain province and not mentioning which school in America he attended, so in my heart, I just assumed he was a detective deliberately sent from somewhere.

Still, he eagerly kept asking about the situation in Korea. It wasn't just one or two visits; he came 7 or 8 times, and with each repeated visit, he became even more earnest. Eventually, feeling it wasn't an ordinary matter, I asked Chang Tŏksu and other students, describing the appearance of the person who kept visiting me and asking who he was. They all said in unison, "There is such a person in America, but he never associates with us students at all and wanders around all by himself; he's an eccentric. However, he's not the type to be a detective or anything like that." From then on, I became unaccountably close with that person. Getting to know him gradually, I found he was quite deep and tight-lipped. I later found out his name is said to be "Soeul." As my departure neared, he visited even more often, and we went for walks together and also went to restaurants to eat together. When he heard I was going to England, he told me this.<12>

"There aren't really any students worth seeing in America, and even if you go to England or France, there aren't really any kids worth much there either. However, when you go to London, make sure to go to the Chinese Students' Association (中國留學生會) and seek out a young man named 'Chŏng Myŏnggu.' He is our compatriot and will become a great talent for the future of Chosŏn."

Following his words, when I arrived in London, I didn't particularly seek out anyone else but first went to the Chinese Students' Association to look for the young man named "Chŏng Myŏnggu." Truly, in both character and talent, he is one of the human resources who will shoulder the future of Chosŏn. I couldn't help but admire him. When I met Pak Sŏgyun, who is at Oxford University, he also spoke highly of "Chŏng," saying he is indeed a talent. Anyway, I consider meeting the eccentric Mr. "Soeul" in America and the brilliant Mr. "Chŏng Myŏnggu" in London to be the greatest pleasure and harvest of my round-the-world trip.

Hong Myŏnghŭi Says:

My main concern these days is writing the novel "Im Kkŏkjŏng Chŏn" (林巨正傳). The two hundred fifty or so installments written so far have reached exactly the halfway point. I plan to write about that much more going forward.

Yi Kwangsu Says:

My standard for looking at women is uninterestingly high, so even when walking through the city streets, I have yet to see a woman who makes me think, "If it were that kind of woman!" and stirs my passion. I hear that among kisaeng, there are many said to be beauties of the first rank, but having never frequented kisaeng houses, I haven't had the opportunity to critique them.
Therefore, I have tried hard, at least in literature, to depict the kind of beautiful, pure, and sublime woman I earnestly long for, but I don't know if it turned out well.

I plan to write another 30 or 40 installments of "Tanjong Aesa" (端宗哀史) and then bring it to a close. When a serialized novel exceeds 200 installments, not only do the readers get bored, but the author also becomes weary.

Regarding Pyŏkch'o's "Im Kkŏkjŏng Chŏn" or my "Tanjong Aesa," people sometimes say the "tempo" is slow, while others say so-and-so's work has too fast a "tempo." Which tempo do you suppose suits the temperament of the Korean populace?

Looking at newspaper novel illustrations, Sim San's work in the Chungoe has a purely oriental color, Sŏgyŏng's in the Chosŏn is "modern" and refreshing, Ch'ŏngjŏn's in the Tonga shows a sincere attitude of putting his heart and soul into everything, and Yi Sŭngman's contributions in the Mae'il display talent on the page.
Among them, the one I like best is the brushwork in No Sim San's oriental painting style.

The other day, Yuktang came by and I heard him criticize the blind (簾) hanging at the eaves of my house for being old and cheap, but somehow I don't pay much attention to such things. It would be nice if the front yard were tidily planted with flowers and well-tended, but thinking about how much better it would really become, I just let it pass casually.

Pak Ilbyŏng Says:

There is a hidden genius in Chosŏn. He is Mr. Yi Wŏnch'ŏl, currently a young professor at Yŏnjŏn (延專). His talent is so extraordinary that in America, where he studied abroad, he is publicly acclaimed as the foremost genius among Orientals. Primarily specializing in astronomy, he discovered a star not yet found in the world, and it's said that among famous astronomers abroad, they have even named the star "Wŏnch'ŏl Star" (源喆星) and don't cease to revere him. (14)

**Summary**

This article compiles brief remarks from four prominent Korean intellectuals in late 1929. Hŏ Hŏn recounts meeting an eccentric but insightful Korean student ("Soeul") in the US who recommended seeking out another brilliant student, Chŏng Myŏnggu, in London, whom Hŏ found impressive. Hong Myŏnghŭi mentions his ongoing work on the lengthy novel "Im Kkŏkjŏng Chŏn." Yi Kwangsu discusses his high standards for female beauty, his progress on the novel "Tanjong Aesa," muses on the appropriate narrative tempo for Korean readers, and comments on newspaper illustrators, favoring No Sim San's style. He also dismisses concerns about his home's appearance. Pak Ilbyŏng praises Yi Wŏnch'ŏl, an astronomer at Yonhui College (Yŏnjŏn), as a hidden genius recognized internationally for discovering a star. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable glimpses into the interests and networks of Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, their awareness of compatriots abroad (Hŏ Hŏn), their literary endeavors and concerns about audience reception (Hong, Yi), aesthetic preferences in visual art (Yi), and pride in Korean academic achievements on the world stage (Pak). It reflects a blend of cultural commentary, personal anecdotes, and nationalist sentiment regarding talent and potential.

雜談室
許憲氏 曰

美國 갓슬 때에 하로는 엇든 낫모르는 朝鮮청년이 내 잇는 호텔로 차저 왓습데다. 그는 드러오면서 별로 반가워하는 긔색도 업시 또 그러타고 사람을 깔보는 긔색도 업시 그저 첫 마디부터 本國 정치형편이 엇더하냐 하는 질문을 합데다. 나는 이상하여 당신은 누구냐고 무러도 그는 일홈도 성도 다이지 안코 어느 道에 산다는 사람으로 美國 어느 학교에 다닌다는 말도 아니하기에 나는 심중으로 이것은 어느 쪽에서 일부러 보낸 探偵인 줄만 알엇지요.
그래도 그는 열심히 朝鮮형편을 따라다니며 뭇습데다. 그도 한 두번의 來訪이 아니엇고 실로 7, 8차에 미치여 回를 거듭할사록 더욱 열심하더이다. 그래서 나종에는 尋常한 일갓지 안어서 張德秀 君 기타 유학생 諸氏에게 이러이러한 풍모를 가진 사람이 차저오는데 누구냐고 물은 즉 모다 如出一口로 「그런 사람이 美國에 잇는데 도모지 우리 유학생들과는 한번도 交遊를 아니하며 저 혼자 따로 떠도라 다니는 奇人임넨다. 그러나 採偵 등의 인물은 아녀요」 합데다. 그제부터는 그 사람과 알 수 업게 친근하게 되엇는데 차츰 사괴어보니 퍽으나 속이 깁고 입이 무거운 사람입데다. 뒤에 알아본 즉 그는 일홉을 「쇠울」이라고 한다는데 내가 떠날 림박하여는 더욱 자조 차저와 가치 산보도 다니고 「레스트란」에 식사하려도 가치 단녓슴니다. 그가 내가 英國간다는 말을 듯고 이런 말을 하여줍데다.<12>
「美國안에는 별로 볼만한 유학생이라고 업고 또 英國이나 佛蘭西에 가서도 그러케 쓸만한 자식들이라고 업습넨다. 다만 「론돈」에 가시거든 中國留學生會에 가서 「정명구」란 청년을 긔어히 차저보세요. 그는 우리 동포인데 조선의 장차 큰 人材가 될 분임니다」하더이다.

그 말대로 나는 「론돈」에 와서 다른 사람은 별로 아니 차저보고 「정명구」라는 청년부터 中國留學生會에 가서 차저 보앗는데 실로 인격으로나 재질로나 장래의 조선을 떠메일 人材중의 하나입데다. 나는 탄복하기를 마지 안엇는데 「옥스포-드」대학에 잇는 朴鍚胤君을 만낫슬 때에도 그이 말이 「鄭」은 과연 인재라고 극히 칭찬합데다. 아무튼 나는 美國의 기인 「쇠울」군과 「론돈」의 수재 「鄭明九」군을 만나본 것을 世界一週 중 가장 깁분이요 또 수확으로 암니다.

洪命憙氏 曰

나의 요지간 관심사는 소설 「林巨正傳」을 쓰는 일인데 지금까지 쓴 이박오십여회는 꼭 절반에 이르럿다. 압흐로 그만한 분량을 더 쓰려고 생각한다.

李光洙氏 曰

나의 여자를 보는 표준은 멋업시 놉하서 市井을 지나다니며 보아도 「저런 녀자면!」하고 정열을 이르킬만한 여성이라고 아직 보지 못하엿다. 드른 즉 기생 속에는 一色이라 할 분이 만타하나 나는 아직 기방출입이라고 하여본 적이 업서서 그를 비평할 기회라고 가지지 못하엿다.
그래서 문학상으로나 절실히 동경하는 아름답고 깨긋하고 숭고한 그런 여성을 그려보려고 애썻스나 잘 되엇섯는지 모르겟다.

「端宗哀史」는 압흐로 3, 40回를 더 쓰고 끗을 매즈려 한다. 연재 소설이란 200回를 넘으면 독자도 지리할 뿐더러 작자 역시 지리하여지니까.

碧初君의 「林巨正傳」이나 나의 「端宗哀史」를 남들이 간혹 「템포」가 느리다 하고 누구누구의 것은 또 너무 「템포」가 빠르다 하는 말이 잇는데 어느 것이 朝鮮 민중의 性情에 마즐넌지요?

신문의 소설삽화를 보면 中外의 心汕君것은 순수한 동양적 색채가 잇고 朝鮮의 夕影君은 「모던」식으로 淸新하고 東亞의 靑田君 것은 어느 것이나 심혈을 다하는 眞摩한 태도가 보이고 每日의 李承萬君 分은 才氣가 紙表에 나타난다.
그 중에 내가 제일 조와하는 것은 盧心汕君의 동양화식의 그 필치다.

전일 六堂君이 와서 우리집 추녀 끗에 친 발(簾)이 오래되고 헐갑의 것이라고 나모래는 말을 둘엇는데 나는 엇전지 그런데는 별로 관심을 아니한다. 뜰 압헤도 깨끗하게 화초나 심으고 손질을 잘 하엿스면 조켓지만 그런들 얼마나 조와지랴 하는 생각에 그저 범연하게 지나처 버린다.

朴一秉氏 曰

조선에 숨은 천재가 잇슴넨다. 그 분은 只今 延專의 少壯敎授로 잇는 李源喆氏인데 그 분이 엇더케 才操가 비상하든지 留學하든 아메리카에서는 동양사람으로 제일가는 수재라고 公稱한다는데 爲先 天文學을 전공하든 끗에 세계에서 아직 발견하지 못한 별 하나를 발견하여 해외의 유명한 천문학자들 사이에 그 별을 「源喆星」이라고까지 일홈을 지어 崇敬하기를 말지 안는다 함니다. (14)
