Article Title: 詩歌 (Poetry Collection)
Author: 요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

New Day

The wind blows the snow
And the world is utterly dark, but
A single streak of brightness
Rises in the east;
The new day is dawning.
Quickly rise and greet it!

Wandering in darkness,
Limbs were frozen, but
When the sun rises and shines,
New life will be found;
The new day is rushing near.
Quickly rise and greet it!

The sun rises, it ascends.
Like mountain peaks set ablaze,
You who sleep, arise!
Awaken from troubled dreams!
The new day is dawning,
Quickly rise and greet it!

Birds, gather together!
Trees, burst into bloom!<35>

People who wept and sorrowed,
Take strength!
The new day has already come.
Quickly rise and greet it!

Castles, crumble down!
Stone walls, collapse!
Mountains, bow down low!
Rivers, stand still!
The day of glory is coming,
O Samch'ŏlli, greet it!

Heart

Light has no wings, so it vanishes;
Fragrance too fades away;
Flowers fall and dust settles,
Adding only to sorrow.

Tears dry in the wind,
Voices too wear away with sorrow;
Even the "Beje" sent at the time of the new day
Fades even from memory.

My love, neither tears nor flowers
Nor memories can be trusted.
That which renews following the passage of time
Is only the colorless, wordless
"Heart."<36>

**Summary**

This article presents two poems by "Yohan". The first, "New Day," uses powerful natural imagery (darkness giving way to dawn, frozen limbs finding life, sunlit peaks) to call for an awakening. It urges people, birds, trees, and ultimately "Samch'ŏlli" (Korea) itself to rise and greet a coming "day of glory," suggesting a hopeful anticipation of liberation and renewal. The second poem, "Heart," reflects on the transience of beauty, sorrow, and memory, contrasting them with the enduring, unchanging nature of the inner "heart" or "mind." For historians, the first poem is particularly interesting for its overt nationalistic undertones and call for collective awakening during the Japanese colonial period, expressing hope for a radically different future despite the harsh present. The second poem offers a more introspective counterpoint, emphasizing inner constancy amidst external change.

새날

바람은 눈을 날리고
세상은 캄캄도 하나
한줄기 밝음이
동녘에 떠오르니
새날이 새여오도다
어서 닐어 마잣스라

어둠에 헤매이며
사지가 얼엇스나
해나서 비첼 적에
새 묵숨 차즈리니
새 날이 달러오도다.
어서 닐어 마잣스라

해가 뜬다 도다 오른다.
산봉어리 불켯드시
자는 이는 이러나라.
서른 꿈은 깨처 오라.
새날이 도다 오나니
어서 일어 마잣스라.

새들아 모혀들라.
나무들아 피어나라.<35>

울고 설어하던
백성들아 힘내어라.
새날이 이미 왓도다.
어서 닐어 마잣스라.

성들아 깨어저라.
돌담아 문허지라.
산들은 엎드리고
강물은 머젓스라.
영광의 날이 오나니
삼천리여 마잣스라.

마음

비츤 날가 업서지나니
향긔도 스러지나니
꼿은 떠러지고 먼지 안자
서름만 더하나니

눈물은 바람에 말으고
목소리도 설어 가나니
새날 때에 보낸 「베에제」도
긔억조차 스러지나니

님이어 눈물도 꼿도
긔억도 밋지 못할터라
세월을 딸하 새롭는 것은
오직 빗갈업고 말업슨
「마음」으러라.<36>
