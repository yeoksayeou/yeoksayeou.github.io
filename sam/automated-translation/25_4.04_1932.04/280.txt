Article Title: 잠은 나의 배를 밀고 (Sleep Pushes My Boat)

Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Sleep Pushes My Boat
						Kim Kirim

Top of the Public Hall (公會堂)―
The clock's (時計) hour hand (時針) busily rolls past 『1 2』

I turn off the light
Then my bedroom, a small balloon (風船), departs the night's pier (埠頭)

Blood-mixed pupils, glaring whites of the eyes, a knife-like smile (微笑)― oh!
Farewell
O things that ran brilliantly through my daytime
Sleep pushes my boat
And departs far from the Earth (地球)

Leaving behind the Earth (地球) that tumbles like a bubble
In the endless whirlpool of darkness
The boat, flying far, far away
Is drawn by the song of the stars
Gliding, swaying over the sea of blue dreams.＜93＞

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Kirim portrays sleep as a liberating journey away from the harsh realities and stresses of the daytime world. The speaker's bedroom transforms into a boat or balloon, leaving behind the unpleasant memories of the day (symbolized by glaring eyes and forced smiles) and the Earth itself. Guided by starlight, this vessel travels across a dream sea, suggesting peace and transcendence. For historians, the poem offers insight into Korean modernist aesthetics of the 1930s, using urban imagery like the public hall clock. It may also reflect a desire for escapism common during the restrictive Japanese colonial period, seeking refuge in the subconscious realm of dreams. The specific orthography used is also valuable for linguistic studies of Korean from that era.

잠은 나의 배를 밀고
						김기림
公會堂 꼭댁이―
時計의 時針은 『1 2』우흘 분주하게 구르고 감니다

불을 끔니다
그러면 작은 風船인 나의 寢室은 밤의 埠頭를 떠나감니다

피 석긴 눈동자 흘기는 눈자위 칼날가튼 微笑 오-
잘 잇거라
나의 대낫을 찬란하게 달리던 것들이여
잠은 나의 배를 밀고
地球를 멀리 떠나감니다

밋업는 어둠의 물박휘속에
물거품처름 넝구는 地球를 버리고
멀리 멀리나는 배는
별들의 노래에 잇끌리며
푸른 꿈의 바다우흘 드놀며 밋그러저감니다.＜93＞
