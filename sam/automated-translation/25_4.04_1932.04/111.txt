**Body Text Translation:**
Urgent Notice

Although this one article by Mr. Yi Chŏngsŏp (李晶燮氏) is a long essay reaching fifty-three manuscript pages, we apologize to the author and readers that due to unavoidable circumstances (不得已한 事情), we could only publish a part of it like this. However, following the critique of Mr. An Chaehong's (安在鴻氏) course last month and the critique of Mr. Ch'oe Rin's (崔麟氏) course this month, we will continue with major critiques of various individuals' courses in the next issue as well, so please look forward to it greatly.

Samch'ŏlli Editorial Department <55>

**Summary**

This is an editorial notice from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* apologizing for only being able to publish a portion of a long article by Yi Chŏngsŏp due to unspecified circumstances. It assures readers that critiques of prominent figures' viewpoints ("courses"), following those of An Chaehong and Ch'oe Rin in previous issues, will continue in the next issue. For historians, this brief notice is interesting as it points to editorial constraints (perhaps censorship or space limitations) faced by Korean publications under Japanese rule. It also highlights the magazine's role in hosting intellectual debates by critiquing the ideas of significant contemporary Korean figures like An Chaehong and Ch'oe Rin.
