**Body Text Translation:**
Crossroads (交叉點)

If anyone has something they wish to ask someone, please submit it to this column. Our magazine's representative (旗手) will relay the letter to the intended recipient, receive their reply, and publish it in this column.
1. Use a postcard for the paper.
2. Keep the meaning simple.
3. The content should relate to matters of public social concern.

To the Tonga Ilbo:
On November 17th last year, the Sumiyoshi fundraising department for the relief of fellow Koreans in Manchuria sent 31 won 90 jeon to the Tonga Ilbo Company, but we have received no reply. Despite asking several times via return postcard, there has been no response whatsoever, which is extremely frustrating. This time, please be sure to inform us through the Samch'ŏlli Crossroads column.
An Osaka Reader

Received.
It was published in the Tonga Ilbo issue no. 2,922, page 4, dated November 24, Showa 6 (1931). You say you inquired several times via return postcard, but if we had received them, we would have replied. We have no memory of receiving them.
Tonga Ilbo Business Director, Yang Wŏnmo

To the Avant-garde Writer, Mr. P'alp'ong:
1. Regarding Yang Ch'angjun of Kunkisa's XX, do you not have a duty yourself to publicly declare (聲明) your position to the masses? <66>
2. Instead of thinking about the dead child (Kunki, Chiptan - Group), how about paying more attention to your own health from now on and striving to give birth to sturdy and vigorous sons (Works)? Please let me know your address.
Pugang Pongsŏk

1. Not only regarding the so-called statement (所謂 聲明) by Yang Ch'angjun published in the former "Kunki," but also along with Min Naehwi's, it is of course the duty of myself, currently a KAPF committee member, or Mr. Pak Yŏnghŭi, more than anyone, to criticize and expose its falsehood (虛僞) and reactionary (反動) nature before the masses. Therefore, last summer, I attempted to publish an article in the magazine "Irŏt'a," but the manuscript was confiscated (압수) during censorship (檢閱), so I could not achieve my goal.
2. Kunki may have died, but the group (Chiptan) did not die and is now growing by leaps and bounds (비약적으로). Your attitude of referring to the organization's magazine as an individual's "child" is not good (좃치 못하니); please correct it (곳치시오). And thank you for the advice (勸告) to put out works. My address is Kyŏngsŏng, Pongik-dong 11, unit 11. P'alp'ong

To Lawyer Mr. Kim Pyŏngno:
I am a 22-year-old man. Some years ago (年前), I became acquainted with a certain (某) woman, and last March, she gave birth to a son (生男).
My parents, completely unaware (不知) of this fact, ordered me to marry someone from another place (他處) in mid-September this year. What should I do about the child's (아히) family register (호적)? However (但), I have no intention (의향) of registering him as an illegitimate child (사생아). But if I try to register him as the eldest son (長子), my parents (兩親) absolutely will not consent (승낙). What should I do (엇지할까요)? <67> I earnestly hope (千萬伏望) you will provide detailed instruction (下敎) through the April issue of Samch'ŏlli.
A Susŏngsang-dong Reader

If you submit an acknowledgment report (認知屆) for the born child, he will be recorded (記載) in the family register (호적) as a son born out of wedlock (庶子男). Then, subsequently (爾後), a son born from the legal wife (本妻) will become the legitimate son (嫡出男) and the eldest son (장남).
Lawyer Kim Pyŏngno

To Mr. Pak Ch'anghun:
Teacher, if I want to acquire common knowledge about medicine, what kinds of magazines and books are necessary (필요함닛가)? I earnestly desire (切望耳옵니다) that you instruct me (下敎) on the titles (題號), prices (定價), and places of sale for the books, one by one.
November 7, 1931
Manchuria, Pak Chongch'ŏn respectfully

Please request the "Chosŏn Language Medical Lecture Notes Sample Copy and Rulebook" (朝鮮 文의학 강의록 견본부 규칙서) from the Kyŏngsŏng Medical Training Institute (京城의학 講習所) within Ch'unam Clinic, Noryangjin, outside Kyŏngsŏng City. Pak Ch'anghun, M.D. (醫博), replies.

Who is the Anonymous Writer?

Who is Ch'angnang'gaek, the writer (필자) of the "Open Letter to Mr. Chang Tŏksu" published in your esteemed magazine last autumn? Besides that, there are sometimes (각금) other writings under the name Ch'angnang'gaek. Who are they all?
An Ŭiju Reader

You've asked at just the right time (마츰). Because a certain (某) magazine company, without investigation, simply stated that the writer of that open letter was Mr. Kim Myŏngsik in Osaka, it caused Mr. Kim Myŏngsik considerable trouble (미안). Ch'angnang'gaek is the anonymous name (匿名) of our company's Mr. Kim Tonghwan. Besides that, among pen names (雅號) and aliases (變名) that seem unfamiliar, there are many (만이) writings by Mr. Kim.
Samch'ŏlli Editorial Department <68>

To Mr. Hong Chongin:
I heard that you did much of the writing (집필) for the newspaper extras (號外) at the time of the Yŏ Unhyŏng incident. Can they still be obtained (어더볼 수 잇슴니까) now?
Kimhae, Pak Kyŏngsik

Regarding the extras (號外) from that time (當時), I don't know about those who may have kept them, but they would be difficult to obtain now. Afterwards (기후), the Chungoe Ilbo fell into a state of chaos (혼란상태), so it doesn't seem likely (잇슬 듯 십지 안으나) they exist, though perhaps a library might have them. I also do not have any saved copies.
Hong Chongin

Where are the Nursing Training Centers?
Please tell me the names and addresses of the nursing training centers (養成所) in Chosŏn, wherever they are (잇난지).
Hŭich'ŏn Samch'ŏlli Branch Office

There are two places in Kyŏngsŏng. One is the Severance Union Medical College Affiliated Nursing Training Center (세부란쓰 의학전문학교 부속 간호부 養成所) on Namdaemun Street, Kyŏngsŏng, and the next one is the Kyŏngsŏng Government-General Medical College Affiliated Nursing Training Center (京城총독부 의학전문학교 부속 간호부 養成所). Besides these, there is also one at the Hamhŭng Provincial Hospital (咸興道立병원).

To Mr. Chŏng Sŏkt'ae:
I hope for the ever-increasing prosperity (隆昌) of your company. My age is 21 (height 5 chi 1 ch'on 5 pun), but perhaps due to incomplete overall physical development (全身 발육 불안전), my height is relatively short (比較적 身長 短少). Because of this, I have been living in daily pessimism (일상 悲觀). Then, the other day (前日), according to a certain newspaper report (某신문 보도), I saw an advertisement (광고) for the physical training height increase method (체육신장법) from the Nomura Company (野村상회) in Kyōbashi-ku, Tokyo City (東京市 京橋區). Teacher, can anyone freely achieve the goal (목적) of completely promoting growth (발육증진) and treatment (治療) at home (자택) with that (其) physical training height-increasing (체육신장) device?
Puk Migŭm-ch'ŏng, Pessimist Student (悲觀生)

I cannot approve (찬성) of the physical training height-increasing device method you mentioned. A more suitable (적합) exercise method would be necessary, and it would be better (조훌 것임니다) not to think about various other advertising techniques (其他 여러 가지 선젼술). Respectfully, Chŏng Sŏkt'ae.

As a Working Woman
Teacher, what I, your humble servant (소생), wish to inquire (문의하오랴 하옵는 것은) about is the adornment (치장) of modern women (especially working women), which truly (참말로) surprises our eyes and ears (耳目). Please instruct (下敎) me frankly (숨김업시) as to what kind of (如½•한) meaning their dressing up (모양냄) holds. Respectfully, A Reader (Details omitted - 여불비상셔 一讀者上)

I have read this all day (종일 생覽하엿스나), but have only barely reached (겨우 어덧슬 뿐입니다) the conclusion (결론) that perhaps (아마) it is because they are young.
Respectfully, Mokpyŏngjŏng.

When Does a Woman Conceive?
Regarding pregnancy (임신), some say (云云者도 有) it happens before menstruation (월경 전), and others say after menstruation (월경 후). Which one (何者) is correct (올슴잇가)? Also, whether it's the former (前者) or the latter (後者), please inquire with a specialist doctor (전문의사선생) about how many days before (前幾日間) menstruation or how many days after (後幾日間) menstruation it is, and instruct me accurately (정확히 下敎하심을 바람니다) on the conception period (임신기일).
The end. Sŏul, Mr./Ms. K <69>

A woman can become pregnant (임신) whenever there is any kind of (何等) sexual intercourse (房事). However (然이나), centering on (잡어서) the time of menstruation (월경시), the pregnancy rate (임신율) increases (만아지는 것임니다). Since ancient times (自古이래로), the period from 3 days before to 3 days after menstruation (前3 後3) has been recognized in practice (실제상 否認하지 못할 일이외다) as the easiest time (容易한 시기) to conceive. Especially, looking at the experiments (실험) and statistics (통계) of numerous (多數) scholars (학자), the period of several days after menstruation (월경 후 수일간) is the easiest time to conceive. However (然이나), menstruation is not absolutely (절대) necessary for pregnancy. Occasionally (간혹), women with amenorrhea (無월경한 婦人) can also conceive. Pak Ch'anghun, M.D. (醫博), replies.

**Summary**

This article is a Q&A column titled "Crossroads" from the April 1932 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine. Readers submitted questions via postcard on various topics, which the magazine relayed to relevant experts or figures, publishing both the questions and answers. Topics include inquiries about a donation receipt from the *Tonga Ilbo*, a literary dispute involving KAPF members and the defunct *Kunki* group, legal advice on registering a child born out of wedlock, recommendations for medical books, identifying an anonymous columnist, locating old newspaper extras about Yŏ Unhyŏng, finding nursing schools, the efficacy of a height-increasing device, the meaning behind modern working women's fashion, and the most fertile period for conception. The column provides a glimpse into the concerns and interactions of Koreans during the colonial period, featuring prominent intellectuals, writers, doctors, and lawyers responding directly to reader queries.
