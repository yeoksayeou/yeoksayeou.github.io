Heart Seeking Spring
						Mo Yun-suk

Wanting to see the dew-맺친 spring grass,
I climbed the rear mountain (뒤ㅅ山) and sought the green turf,
But in the wide plain (平野), only snowstorms blow,
And the sad news (悲聞) that an old man with a bundle (봇다리) died crossing the pass.

Though I long so dearly for the southern spring that passed,
Only unseasonal snowflakes knock at my window.
It is April, when even the potted plant by the window froze to death;
Today too, my heart weeps, holding the broken pot.

My withered, cold breast also longs for the spring light,
Seeking and wandering in the quiet breeze,
Why does the spring news not come? How cruel the cold wind is!
My heart stands trembling, clutching the hem of my skirt tightly. <92>

Let's sing a spring song—news (消息) came from a friend in the Southern Land (南國),
Of iris flowers, water parsley flowers, forsythia flowers, pasqueflowers.
But this is April in a foreign land (胡地) where even weeds do not sprout;
My pining heart burns with sorrow (哀愁) seeking spring.

Exhausted from seeking spring, I return to the guest house (客舍);
On the dark wall, stands a lone Ave Maria (아베메리).
Praying, "Give me spring!", I am awakened by the cold wind.
A spirit longing for spring in a flowerless spring land (無花春胡地).
—From the Northern Region (北域)— <93>

**Summary**

This poem by Mo Yun-suk expresses a deep yearning for spring, symbolizing warmth, life, and perhaps homeland, from a desolate, cold location described as the "Northern Region" and "胡地" (foreign land). The speaker contrasts the memory and news of a vibrant southern spring with the harsh reality of lingering winter, snow, and death in April. The imagery of frozen plants, cold winds, and a solitary Ave Maria emphasizes feelings of isolation, despair, and unfulfilled longing. For historians, the poem offers insight into the emotional landscape of Koreans potentially displaced or living in northern regions like Manchuria or Siberia during the colonial period. The contrast between the longed-for "South" (likely Korea) and the bleak "North" ("胡地"), along with the profound sense of sorrow (哀愁), could reflect the broader experience of loss, displacement, and the suppressed desire for national restoration under Japanese rule.

봄을 찻는 마음
						毛允淑
이슬맷친 봄풀이 보고 십허서
뒤ㅅ山에 올나 푸른잔듸 찻엇으나
넓다란 平野앤 눈보래만 날니고
봇다리낀 늙은이가 고개넘다 죽엇다는 悲聞이야

지내간 南쪽봄이 하그리 그립건만
때아닌 눈발만 창까를 두다리네
들창엽헤 화분마저 얼어죽은 四月이어
오늘도 깨여진 화분안고 우는 내마음이야

시드러진 찬가슴도 봄빗이 그리워
한적한 바람결을 찻어서 헤매건만
봄소식 웨 안이오나 찬바람 무정코나
치마자락 꼭 붓안고 떨고 섯는 마음이야＜92＞
봄노래 부르자 南國벗의 消息왓네
창포꼿 메나리꼿 개나리꼿 할미꼿
실타고 안돗는 胡地의 四月이니
봄 찻는 哀愁의 애타는 마음이야.
봄을 찻다 시진한 몸 客舍로 도라드니
컴컴한 바람벽에 홀노섯는 아베메리
봄을 주소! 빌다가 찬바람에 늣겨우네
無花春胡地에 봄그리운 영혼이야.
―北域에서―＜93＞
