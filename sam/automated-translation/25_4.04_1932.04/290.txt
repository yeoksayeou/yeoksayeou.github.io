Publication Date: 1932년 04월 01일
Article Title: 님과 孤寂(외 5편) (The Beloved and Solitude (and 5 others))
Author: 金一葉
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

님과 孤寂
						金一葉

님과 孤寂 (The Beloved and Solitude)

우리집 손님이란 님과 다못 孤寂이라
님가시자 孤寂와서 떠날 줄을 모르는가＜93＞
아마도 나의 孤寂은 님의 선물인가!

The guests at my house are just the Beloved and Solitude.
As soon as the Beloved leaves, Solitude comes and doesn't know how to depart?<93>
Perhaps my Solitude is a gift from the Beloved!

겨을밤 (Winter Night)

겨을밤 길다기에 감긴회포 풀재떠니
첫구비도 찻기전에 새벽빗이 새로워라
그럴 줄 알어떠면 더 감지나 말 것을

They say the winter night is long, so I tried to unravel my pent-up feelings,
But before I could even find the first bend, the dawn light was new.
If I had known it would be like this, I shouldn't have held them in more.

詩調數章 (Several Sijo Poems)

哀別 (Sorrowful Parting)

어지어 내일이니 인제는 홀이로다
人生의 險한 길을 나어이 혼자 갈가
님이야 사괼님 만흐니 외로시다 하릿가

Ah, tomorrow has come, and now I am alone.
How can I walk life's perilous path by myself?
You, my love, have many friends to meet; would you say you are lonely?

花園에서 (In the Flower Garden)

꼿이란 꼿이어니 꼿이어든 미을소냐
桃花꼿 머례꼿고 오야꼿 손에 들어
장미밧 지나가서 海棠花를 보리라

A flower is a flower; if it's a flower, how could I dislike it?
Picking peach blossoms and pear blossoms, holding plum blossoms in hand,
I pass the rose field to see the Sweet Brier (海棠花).

靑春 (Youth)

따슨볏 니불삼아 풀우에 누엇으니
적은새는 노래하고 가는 바람 키쓰한다
무심한 저 구름은 시샐 줄을 모르는가

Using the warm sunlight as a blanket, I lie upon the grass.
Small birds sing, and the passing breeze kisses me.
Do those indifferent clouds not know how to be jealous?

님에게 (To the Beloved/Lord)

나의 어린靈이 님의 말슴 밋사옵고
方向좃차 모르고서 가노라고 가지만은
힘업는 지축거름 언으때나 님뵈릿가

My young spirit trusts the Beloved's word,
And though I go, saying I am going, without even knowing the direction,
With powerless, faltering steps, when shall I see the Beloved?

님께서 부르심이 千年前가 萬年前가
님의 말슴 늣길 때는 금시 님을 뵈옵는 듯
法悅에 뛰놀다가도 돌처보면 거기로다 ＜94＞

Was the Beloved's calling a thousand years ago or ten thousand years ago?
When I feel the Beloved's word, it is as if I see the Beloved right away;
Though I leap about in dharma joy (法悅), when I turn back, I am still there. <94>
<93-94>

**Summary**

This collection of six sijo poems by Kim Il-yŏp explores themes of love, longing, separation, solitude, and spiritual searching. The poems move from the lingering presence of loneliness after a beloved departs, through reflections on fleeting time, the pain of parting, the simple appreciation of nature's beauty in youth, to a deeper yearning for union with a "Beloved" (님), which carries strong spiritual or religious connotations, reflecting the author's path towards Buddhism. The final poems depict the paradox of feeling spiritually close yet physically distant from the divine object of devotion. From a historical perspective, these poems are interesting as they showcase the work of a pioneering female intellectual and writer during the colonial period, using the traditional Korean sijo form to express intensely personal and increasingly spiritual emotions. Her evolving perspective, hinted at in the shift towards a potentially divine 'Beloved', reflects her later life decision to become a Buddhist nun, offering insight into the personal spiritual journeys undertaken amidst the social and political constraints of the time.
