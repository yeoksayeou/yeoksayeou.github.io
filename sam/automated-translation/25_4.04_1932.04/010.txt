Magazine Title: 三千里 제4권 제4호
Publication Date: 1932년 04월 01일
Article Title: 三千里 전체회의, 無風的인 現下局面打開策 -文化運動과 消組運動에 注力- (Samch'ŏlli General Meeting, Measures to Break Through the Current Stagnant Situation - Focusing on Cultural Movement and Consumer Cooperative Movement)
Author: 宋鎭禹 (Song Chin-u), 李如星 (Yi Yŏ-sŏng)
Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Interview/Roundtable Discussion)

Cultural Movement and Consumer Cooperative Movement
Song Chin-u

Reporter... What kind of strategy should be adopted to break through the currently stagnant situation of the national movement and make it newly healthy and active? Of course, the scope we intend to discuss is limited to legal movements, and the strategy should also address only the points necessary at the current stage.

Song... There is indeed a strategy to break through the stagnant situation. First and foremost, the standard approach is to first establish a strong central organization that unites the power of the entire nation in one place. Without it, political movements cannot exist. No matter how hard individuals try for a hundred days, what do you think can be achieved? Without a movement conducted through a large organization representing the will of the entire nation, a vast political movement cannot arise. However, I believe there is currently no political movement organization of this kind in our society. (Omission - original text) At the current stage, the measure we should take is to first establish this central organization. <2>

Reporter... Then, do you see the formation of that central organization as having no prospect?

Song... I know it is an extremely difficult task. In the current situation, it is very difficult for a central organization to be created. Therefore, we cannot observe that a political movement will easily occur for us.

Reporter... What is the reason for that?

Song... The primary condition cited as a reason why the formation of a central organization is impossible is the point that the thought of the Korean people is not unified and is divided. On one side, nationalism is prevalent; on another side, there is socialism; and on another side, there are various other 'isms', large and small, long and short, positive and negative, divergent and convergent, utterly chaotic and inconsistent. Therefore, even if an association were formed, it would inevitably contain the seeds of division from the outset and would be forced. Thus, internal strife constantly arises, there is no growth, and ultimately, its lifespan is short.

Reporter... Why do you observe that it must necessarily be so?

Song... The Sin'ganhoe provided us with the best recent example (Omission - original text). Look. When we try to do something, isn't there a powerful opposing force sternly confronting us? We should pour all our wisdom and strength into countermeasures against it, but when internal strife like this arises, we are too busy managing internal affairs, so how can there be an opportunity for anything to be accomplished? If someone does something, rather than covering for it, people are already busy criticizing, pursuing, questioning, and examining it point by point, like prosecutors or judges. This is entirely a harmful effect stemming from the disunity of thought.

Reporter... If so, to eliminate that disunity of thought?

Song... Only through the awakening of the masses and the improvement of the level of culture.

Reporter... Setting that aside, what is the second reason why the central organization cannot be formed?

Song... There might also be the reason that influential and capable individuals are exercising self-restraint and remaining inactive.

Reporter... Why do they not step forward? Are they afraid of suppression?

Song... No. It is to avoid useless sacrifice for now.

Reporter... What does that mean?

Song... There must be many circumstances related to the currently entangled thought relationships and the complex surrounding environment.

Reporter... Then, what should be the future course of the national movement?

Song... It lies in conducting the fundamental movement for political action. As preparatory work for that, a cultural movement must inevitably be initiated. <3>

Reporter... A cultural movement?

Song... Enriching educational institutions, developing knowledge through newspapers, magazines, and lecture meetings, and also initiating consumer cooperative and cooperative association movements to open ways for economic self-support, thus providing practical training culturally and economically.

Reporter... Is there no other way besides that?

Song... In the current environment, nothing else can be said.

Reporter... As with initiating any movement, isn't it true that to break through any situation, firstly an organization, secondly leaders, and thirdly money are needed? Regarding the formation of an organization, I understood your intention through our earlier conversation, but what are your thoughts on the second issue, the leaders? Wasn't the inconsistency in the skills and objectives of various past leaders due to the lack of disciplined training? Therefore, isn't it an urgent task for the supreme control organ to quickly rebuild something like a People's University (民立大學) to foster and produce standardized personnel?

Song... That is necessary. However, will a People's University be easily established?

Reporter... If Mr. Kim Sŏng-su once again rises with iron determination, wouldn't the public outside strongly support that project and bring it to completion? I definitely see such momentum maturing in our society.

Song... Mr. Kim Sŏng-su probably has such thoughts too. However, as long as Mr. Kim's thoughts have not yet been publicly announced, I cannot say anything.

Reporter... Thirdly, the funds? I believe such a thing can be imagined now. Couldn't Messrs. Kim Sŏng-su, Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak, Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl, etc., create a trust company (信托會社) worth several million won? In this situation where land throughout Korea is being sold off cheaply from the hands of financial cooperatives (金融組合), the Industrial Bank (殖銀), the Oriental Development Company (東拓), and other lenders, couldn't the aforementioned trust company buy up that land or provide rescue methods, thereby creating permanent funds through its agricultural output? This would also help prevent the economic ruin of Korean farmers.

Song... If the interests of the capitalists align, that might be possible. However, even people with money have different positions and conflicting interests, so realization will likely be difficult.

Reporter... I heard that Dr. Yi Sŭng-man in North America had a plan years ago to buy several large merchant ships to travel around the world engaging in commerce to raise funds. In any case, shouldn't there be some unique measure? Don't they say that in Ireland, they had scientists discover and mine many gold mines to use for funds?

Song... However, I believe that point is not such a cause for groundless worry. <4>

Overview of Overseas Movements
Yi Yŏ-sŏng

Reporter... Let's look at just one aspect of overseas movements, concerning tactics and strategy. First, let's examine the Indian movement. If we were to define the Indian movement, is it a proletarian liberation movement, a bourgeois democratic [movement], or a national [movement]?

Yi... It should be called a national [movement].

Reporter... If so, where is the central force of this national movement, and also, with which strata did it start as allies?

Yi... Today, the decisive force in the Indian movement is the National Congress Party (국민회의파). The constituent elements of the National Congress Party are India's middle class. Although this middle class is currently collapsing at high speed, it still represents the largest, broadest, and deepest-rooted force. Besides this, the large landowners, big capitalists, and other bourgeois strata, along with the Princes, gather under the Muslim League's (回敎徒의 「새-풀」 旗下 - lit. under the banner of the Muslims' 'Sapru') banner, forming the right-wing force. Also, the proletarian force is mainly organized around the urban workers in coastal areas like Madras (마도라스), Bombay (뽐페이), Calcutta (칼컷타), etc. The proletarian movement force, while exposing the 'petty bourgeois mentality' (小뿔조아 根性) of the National Congress Party, seeks cooperation for the anti-British movement. Since the right-wing 'Sapru faction' (「싸-풀일파」) is also in a position of fearing the Gandhi faction, the main force of the Indian national movement is, of course, the National Congress Party. Although there are various fields of movement in India, one can clearly see the left-wing proletarian movement, the centrist Gandhi movement, and the right-wing Sapru-Jinnah (?) movement proceeding side-by-side.

Reporter... If so, please tell us about the strategy of the National Congress Party. How was the National Congress Party organized? What means of struggle has it employed?

Yi... Even if we speak broadly of the National Congress Party's movement, it refers to the movement since welcoming the famous commander-in-chief Gandhi in 1920. The movement before that, i.e., before 1920, is not worth discussing. Before Gandhi, the Indian national movement was a compromising, cooperative, sectarian, and localized political movement <5> aimed at obtaining self-government. However, once Gandhi took control (일본말로 하면 놋도리(乘取り) - 'nottori' in Japanese, meaning takeover) of the National Congress, the principle of the movement became non-cooperation and non-resistance, transcending sects and encompassing all of India. Therefore, the movement of the National Congress before Gandhi, while rejecting the pro-British stance of the All-India Muslim League (全 印度回敎同盟), aimed at achieving self-government through compromise and sought to improve the political status of Indians. Also, before Gandhi, there was no pan-Indian mass national movement. It was through Gandhi that Hindus, Muslims (回印 兩敎徒), and other various religious followers first united into one body, initiating an unprecedented general political struggle for India—for example, tax refusal (납세XX), boycott of public officials (公官吏XX), boycott of public school classes (공립학교수업XX), boycott of British goods (英貨뽀이곳), etc. Then, in 1926, they declared independence as their program objective, and in 1929, reaffirming this, they developed the Swaraj (스와라지) movement—facts still fresh in our memories, aren't they?

Reporter... Why did the British rulers permit the National Congress Party, which aimed arrows at the heart of the ruling country? Was it because the Indian masses had acquired freedom of speech, assembly, and association through their own power?

Yi... No. Initially, British politicians, for colonial policy purposes—that is, to use it as a thermometer for ruling India—allowed only right-leaning elements and pro-British individuals to form something called the 'National Congress' (國民議會). It was a semi-governmental, semi-private, insignificant body without even the power of resolution. However, after experiencing the Great European War, Gandhi and his faction, indignant at Britain's betrayal (failure to grant self-government - 자치제 불승정), took it over ('nottori' in Japanese). Then, its power grew so much that the British government could do nothing about it. It was as if a tiger cub had grown up completely. Thus, it has now transformed into the headquarters of the mass national movement. Actually, until then, Gandhi had been engaged in 'legal relief movements' (법률적 구제운동) while traveling around places like South Africa (南阿). During the Great War, he was pro-British, gave speeches recruiting soldiers for Britain, and even received the Kaisar-i-Hind Medal (카이잘이힌드 勳章), didn't he? That he then fiercely initiated the Satyagraha movement (眞理把持運動 - truth-holding movement) in 1920 was partly due to Gandhi's own greatness, but the Indian situation at the time also made Gandhi prominent.

Reporter... Generally, India experienced mass mobilization struggles over long periods, the first time from 1920-22 and the second time from 1929 to the present. The funds for these movements must have been enormous; where did they come from? Was it foreign aid, the result of members' long-term savings, or subsidies from domestic capitalists?

Yi... I too have looked into that point with considerable interest, and I recently saw an article by an influential foreign newspaper reporter in a certain foreign-language paper. According to him, the Indian movement actually requires surprisingly little funding. India's pioneers are mostly religious believers with a strong spirit of martyrdom, <6> so they often don't ride trains, subsist on plain clothes and simple food, and wherever they go, the villagers gather food and treat them, hence funds are needed only minimally.

Reporter... Is the Indian capitalist class indifferent to funding the movement?

Yi... I don't know for sure, but things like this happened. For example, didn't India's famous tycoon, Mr. Jamnalal Bajaj (짜-만·랄빠자), serve as Gandhi's financial advisor for many years, providing huge sums of money, only to be discovered later and serve three years in prison? I believe there are many similar examples besides this. When speaking of Indian capitalists, the wealth they possess is truly immense, often owning private airplanes, so if they put their minds to it, funds would not be scarce.

Reporter... How many British residents are there in India?

Yi... It is said to be about 160,000. 160,000 within India's population of 320 million is truly a drop in the ocean (滄海의 一粟). That is, Britain is not occupying India to relocate its surplus population, so it's not a settlement colony. Therefore, the national confrontation between the British and Indian peoples within India might be considered weaker compared to other places. However, since India is Britain's granary, the national and economic confrontation is extremely sharp (尖銳).

Reporter... Who are the current leaders of the National Congress Party?

Yi... Gandhi (간듸), Jawaharlal Nehru (와와하랄, 네루), Naidu (나이두), (Tyabji? 치부지), Sen Gupta (센쿠푸다), Bose (뽀쓰), etc.

Reporter... I would like to ask more about tactics, strategy, and other matters, and also touch upon the situations in other places, but due to space constraints, I will stop here. Finally, just one thing: how do you view Gandhi? Some even see him as someone who sells out the masses.

Yi... I think Gandhi is like Sun Wen (孫文 - Sun Yat-sen) in China.

Reporter... In what sense?

Yi... Their ideologies and principles differ, but their political and social positions are similar, and their role as dictators of the national movement is similar. However, although both are leaders of national movements of weak and small nations, they also differ from figures like Abd el-Krim (압텔그림) or Zaghloul Pasha (자그률 파샤).

The portion discussed by Mr. Yu Chin-hŭi (兪鎭熙) has been omitted from publication. <7>
<2-7>

**Summary**

This 1932 roundtable discussion from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features Song Chin-u and Yi Yŏ-sŏng analyzing the stagnation of the Korean national movement and seeking solutions. Song advocates for focusing on cultural and economic preparation (cultural movements, education, consumer cooperatives) as prerequisites for effective political action, citing the disunity of thought (nationalism vs. socialism) and lack of a unified central organization (like the failed Sin'ganhoe) as major obstacles. He suggests influential figures are hesitant due to the complex situation and potential for futile sacrifice. Yi Yŏ-sŏng analyzes the Indian independence movement, led by Gandhi and the National Congress Party, as a model. He discusses its structure (middle-class core, proletarian and right-wing elements), tactics (non-cooperation, boycotts since 1920), funding (minimal reliance, some capitalist support like Bajaj), and leadership, comparing Gandhi's role to Sun Yat-sen's in China.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into the mindset of Korean nationalist intellectuals during a period of perceived stagnation in the colonial era. Song Chin-u's pragmatic focus on cultural and economic groundwork reflects disillusionment with direct political action and internal divisions, while Yi Yŏ-sŏng's detailed analysis of the Indian movement reveals a keen interest in comparative anti-colonial strategies and leadership models. The discussion on funding, leadership potential (mentioning Kim Sŏng-su), and the role of capitalists provides a snapshot of the practical challenges debated within the movement.

三千里 전체회의, 無風的인 現下局面打開策 -文化運動과 消組運動에 注力-
文化運動과 消組運動
