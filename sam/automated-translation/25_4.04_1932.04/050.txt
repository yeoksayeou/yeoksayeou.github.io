Anatomy of the Large Capital Camp Structure
The Content of the Chosŏn Industrial Bank, a Giant of the Chosŏn Conglomerate
Yi Siwan

In Chosŏn, the bank generally known first would be the Bank of Chosŏn. However, in reality, concerning the exertion of power throughout all of Chosŏn and controlling the economy of all of Chosŏn, a conglomerate surpassing the Industrial Bank (殖産銀行), which will be discussed here, has not yet emerged. The former can boast of its existence and authority as a bank due to being the central bank of Chosŏn with the right to issue convertible notes, but the latter, by holding sway over the economic and financial circles of agriculture-based Chosŏn, not only surpasses the former in practical terms, but moreover, the prosperity or decline of the Chosŏn people's economy hinges on the latter's disposition. <24> Originally, the Industrial Bank, as the successor to the former Korean Agricultural and Industrial Bank (舊한국 農工銀行), came forth with the purpose of promoting Chosŏn's colonial industry (殖産興業), as its name suggests, and is now a large bank with a nominal capital of 30 million won. So, who are the contributors (醵出者) of this 30 million won capital? Firstly, among government offices, the Naejangwon (內藏頭) of the Imperial Household Ministry (宮內省) holds 9,000 shares, and the Government-General of Chosŏn (朝鮮총독) holds 6,598 shares. With Hansŏng Bank's (漢城은행) 20,000 shares and Chosŏn Commercial Bank's (朝鮮商業銀行) 16,304 shares leading the way, there are close to 300 banks, companies, and organizations, including those in Japan and Chosŏn, holding at least one share. Among the 23 individual shareholders owning 1,000 shares or more, the fact that Sŏ Pyŏngjo (徐丙朝) of Taegu is the sole Chosŏn person occupying a seat among them is a peculiar distinction. Among the total 6,457 shareholders, Chosŏn shareholders number merely 388.
These 388 shareholders mostly arose from the pursuit of fame and profit rather than ownership as bank stockholders, thus almost all are shareholders holding less than 10 shares.
The Industrial Bank, formed with such a lineup, is located at Namdaemun-ro 2-chōme, Kyŏngsŏng-bu, and has 60 branches and sub-branches established. Except for the Osaka branch and the Tokyo stationed representative office, all are deployed in key areas across the 13 provinces of Chosŏn.
Looking at the bank's lending situation, because its purpose is Chosŏn's colonial industry promotion, the loans are entirely for that purpose. Broadly speaking, as of the end of Shōwa 6 (1931), loans to public organizations amounted to 80,691,245 wŏn, loans to the Central Bank for Financial Associations (금융조합 중앙금고) were 27,213,252 wŏn, and industrial loans totaled 142,380,783 wŏn, making a grand total of 250,285,281 wŏn in loans. Regarding the details of these loans:

Based on the table above, one can perceive the relationship between the Industrial Bank and the Chosŏn people. That is, 81 million wŏn for agricultural funds, 65 million wŏn for irrigation projects, 15 million wŏn for land improvement expenses, 13 million wŏn for land and housing, 27 million wŏn for the Federation of Financial Associations, and other funds including sericulture and livestock industry are responsibilities directly or indirectly shouldered by the Chosŏn people. Considering the current economic situation of the Chosŏn people in Chosŏn, is the repayment of over 200 million wŏn in debt truly feasible for the future? And what percentage decrease will the future debt burden rate show? Without dwelling long on this, the current reality demonstrates it, and the relationship between the Industrial Bank and the Chosŏn people becomes even more complex. Consequently, with that, the economic situation of the Chosŏn people, whose sole basis is the agricultural economy, will only project a bleak outlook ahead.
Then, how did the Industrial Bank manage to undertake such large loans amounting to over 250 million wŏn with a capital of only 20 million wŏn? Lest the reader harbor doubts, introducing its internal policy here: the Industrial Bank, upon collecting the funds for the various loans mentioned earlier, proceeded to issue debentures. Including the takeover of former Agricultural Bank bonds, as of the end of Shōwa 6, this amounted to 850,000 wŏn. From the first issuance of Industrial Bank debentures (殖産채권) of 2 million wŏn on June 2, Taishō 8 (1919), up to December of Shōwa 6 (1931), there were 111 issuances, totaling 282,518,000 wŏn in debentures. Excluding the redeemed amount of 34,960,000 wŏn, the outstanding debenture amount at the end of last year was 247,558,000 wŏn, which ultimately went out as loans to the private sector. Not only that, <25> deposits from the Central Bank for Financial Associations were 7,644,777 wŏn as of the end of last year, and general deposits, including fixed, current, special current, notice, and miscellaneous deposits, totaled 55,598,733 wŏn. This alone shows the substantial lineup and internal solidity of the Industrial Bank. At the same time, if we glance at the profit and loss situation arising from its lending and borrowing relationship, the holders of the aforementioned debentures were almost entirely Japanese, and the same was true for general depositors. When one realizes that the borrowers of this money were Chosŏn people and the Chosŏn countryside, and that the interest rates on the debentures ranged from a high of 6% to a low of 4.5%, while shareholder dividends were paid at a high rate of 9%, it can be seen as a rare instance for a bank to be able to pay shareholders an annual dividend of 9% amidst the devastating process of the current depression. How much supplementation the existence and function of the Industrial Bank will provide to the actual situation of the Chosŏn people in the future will be deferred to the next opportunity, and we conclude here for now.
—March 17th, 9 AM—

The Hŭngnam Nitrogen Fertilizer Plant, the Orient's Largest as Noguchi's Kingdom
Yang Chaeha

It is a recent event that foreign capital marches into Chosŏn, a colony, or rather, Chosŏn which has not seen the complete development of capitalism. Furthermore, its advance towards Hamgyŏng South and North Provinces, which are abundant in natural resources (天源) and vast in land yet sparse in population (地廣人稀), has been rapid. Also, while the development of Northern Chosŏn (北鮮) has been an important item in the politics of successive Governors-General, on the other hand, large Japanese conglomerates (大財閥) such as Mitsubishi (三菱) and Noguchi (野口) have already invaded and are constructing kingdoms here and there. Among them, we will take a glimpse at the representative Hŭngnam Nitrogen Company, Ltd. (興南窒素주식회사) and its factory in general.

The Hŭngnam Nitrogen Company, Ltd., with the large Japanese conglomerate and the government as its background, is centrally managed by Mr. Noguchi. It occupies an area of one square mile in front of Hŭngnam Station (興南 驛前) in Hamnam (咸南) for its company and factory, and has established a power plant requiring a 10 square mile area on a mountain in Sinhŭng County (新興郡 山上). Spending 110 million wŏn in construction costs (55 million for factory construction, 55 million for power plant installation), it completed the basic construction five years ago. Currently, with 8,000 employees and using 190,000 kilowatts of electricity, it manufactures 500,000 tons of ammonium sulfate or ammonium sulfate phosphate, 80,000 tons of pig iron, and 1.5 million barrels of cement annually, using 20 million tons of sulfide iron ore, 300,000 tons of phosphate ore, 200,000 tons of coal, 1,500 sheets of straw mats (叺 - kamasu), and 5 million gunny sacks (麻袋) as main raw materials and materials. It is the Orient's largest nitrogen factory and, at the same time, a company operating a so-called large-scale capitalist enterprise.

How vast the scale of this company is can be guessed from the factory built at a cost of 55 million wŏn on a one-square-mile site, but to give another example: to obtain electricity for fertilizer manufacturing, a large dam (大堰堤) was constructed on the Changjin River (長津江) at an elevation of 1,500 meters in Sinhŭng County, using 82,000 cubic pyŏng (立坪) of concrete, 80 meters high and 400 meters long, damming 190,000 'kilo' [likely referring to flow rate or capacity units related to kilowatts] of the Changjin River water. This created a reservoir with a circumference of 80 kilometers and a depth of 80 meters. It generates 129,600 kilowatts of power with a drop (落着) of 700 meters. Furthermore, one of the world's foremost cable cars (ケ-ブルカ-), 8 kilometers long, has been installed to easily traverse the high plateau at 1,500 meters elevation.

Not only that, for the import of raw materials and supplies and the transport of manufactured goods, Hŭngnam Port (興南港) was constructed with breakwaters extending 1,000 meters, and its quay allows two 10,000-ton ocean-going vessels, two 2,000-ton vessels, and one 1,500-ton vessel to dock simultaneously. It is said that when the large bridge transporter (大「부릿지, 트란스포-티-」) currently under construction <26> is completed, it will be possible to handle 1.5 million tons of cargo annually. There are complete facilities, including about 2,000 modern-style employee houses accommodating over 8,000 employees, employee dormitories, an affiliated hospital, an elementary school, a club (俱樂部), and a supply store (供給所). A new city has formed around the factory, and factory scrip (工場전표) exists, used like banknotes in the vicinity. All aspects of life are standardized, and not only the factory workers but also the casual laborers of the area wear its factory uniforms, truly constructing a separate world (別乾坤), the Noguchi Free State (野口自由國).

At this factory employing over 8,000 people, work is divided into three 8-hour shifts, running engines day and night. Since everything is large-scale, the lives of the employees enjoy relative stability, and especially for the migrated Japanese, it has become their paradise (樂土). However, among the 8,000 workers, there are about 2,000 Chosŏn employees who, earning wages of 1 wŏn 70 chŏn or more, are all employed in lower-level jobs. Outside the factory gates, about 5,000 casual laborers gather, but their living standards are not comparable to those of the Japanese employees, and needless to say, the Noguchi Free State is not a kingdom for Chosŏn laborers.

Moreover, no matter how much it is the Noguchi Kingdom, where there is capitalist enterprise, a class society of labor and capital (勞資) is formed, and their conflicts cannot be absent. Although the factory interior is absolutely secret and thought control (思想取締) is severe, last autumn, through the cooperation of Japanese and Chosŏn laborers of this factory, red secret societies such as the Chosŏn Communist Party Reconstruction Working Committee affiliated with the Chinese Communist Party (中國공산당系 朝鮮공산당 재건공작위원회), the Chemical Workers' Union (화학노동조합), and the Pacific Labor Union (태평양노동조합) were organized, showing a peculiar distinction in the history of the Chosŏn thought movement. It is also said that this factory and port could be used as a military port and weapons manufacturing plant in times of emergency. Thus, the future of this factory is highly noteworthy not only from a national perspective but also politically and economically. <27>

**Summary**

The first article analyzes the Chosŏn Industrial Bank, highlighting its immense financial power in colonial Korea, surpassing even the Bank of Chosŏn. It details the bank's capital structure, heavily dominated by Japanese interests and government bodies, with minimal Korean shareholding. The article emphasizes the vast loans extended, primarily for agriculture, irrigation, and land projects, placing a significant debt burden (over 200 million wŏn) on the Korean populace, financed largely through debentures held by Japanese investors, while the bank pays high dividends. The second article describes the Hŭngnam Nitrogen Fertilizer Plant, built by the Noguchi conglomerate, as the largest in the East. It details the massive scale of the investment, infrastructure (including a huge dam, power plant, port, and company town), and production. While portraying it as a "Noguchi Kingdom" offering stability for its 8,000 workers (mostly Japanese), it points out the lower status and wages of the 2,000 Korean workers and the presence of around 5,000 precarious casual laborers. It also notes the suppression of dissent alongside the recent formation of communist-affiliated secret labor organizations and the plant's potential military use.

These articles offer valuable insights for historians into the colonial economic structure. They clearly illustrate the mechanisms of Japanese financial domination through institutions like the Industrial Bank, channeling capital into sectors beneficial to the colonial power while indebting the Korean population. The description of the Noguchi plant exemplifies the large-scale industrial projects undertaken, the creation of enclave economies ("kingdoms"), the exploitation of Korean labor within this system, the suppression of dissent, and the undercurrent of organized resistance, including communist activities. The specific financial data and the description of the Hŭngnam complex provide concrete examples of colonial capitalism's operation and impact.

朝鮮財閥의 巨物 殖産銀行의 內容
李時琓
朝鮮에서 은행으로 일반이 아라주기는 첫재 朝鮮은행일 것이다. 그러나 사실에 잇서서 全朝鮮적으로 힘을 버치고 全朝鮮의 경제를 좌우하게 되는 것은 예서 말하려는 殖産은행을 눌너갈 재벌이 아즉까지는 생겨나지 안하얏다. 前者는 朝鮮의 中央銀行으로 兌換券 발행권을 가진 데에서 은행으로의 존재와 권한을 자랑할 수 잇스나 후자는 農本 朝鮮의 경제계 財界를 통터러 執柄하게 된 데에서 전자를 사실로 능가하게 될 뿐 아니라, 더욱 朝鮮人의 경제는 後者의 向背에 興廢가 달니게 된 것이다. ＜24＞ 원래 殖産은행은 舊한국 農工銀行의 後身으로 일홈과 갓치 朝鮮의 殖産흥업을 목적으로 나아온 은행으로 現에 공칭자본금 3,000만원을 가진 대은행이니, 이제 그 자본금 3,000만원의 醵出者들은 누구들인가? 첫재 官衙로는 宮內省 內藏頭에 9,000株와 朝鮮총독의 6,598株를 48,757株와 漢城은행의 20,000株 朝鮮商業銀行의 16,304株를 비롯하야 日本, 朝鮮을 물론하고 최하 一株까지 가진 은행 회사단체로만 300에 가까우며 개인으로 1,000株以上 소유株主만 23인중 大邱 徐丙朝 一인이 그 중에 占席하게 된 것은 一異彩이고 총株主 6,457인중 朝鮮人 株主는 겨우 388인에 불과하다.
이 388인이란 株主다대수는 은행주권으로의 소유보다 名利의 탐구로 生한 것이니 擧皆 10株主滿의 株主들뿐이다.
如斯한 陳容으로 성립된 殖産은행은 위치를 京城府 남대문通 二丁目에 두고 지점과 출장소의 설치는 60개소인 데 그중 大阪지점과 東京출장원 駐在所를 除하고는 전부 朝鮮13道의 樞要지대에 배치하게 되엿다.
그리고 同은행의 貸付상황을 본다면 그 목적이 朝鮮의 殖産흥업이기 때문에 대출이 전혀 그것으로 되얏스니 그 대개를 본다면 昭和 6년말 현재로 公共단체 대출이 8,069만1,245원. 금융조합 중앙금고 대출이 2,721만3,252원. 산업대출이 1억4,238만783원으로 총계 2억5,028만5,281원의 거액을 貸하게 되는바 그 대출의 내용에 잇서서.

野口王國으로 東洋 第一의 興南 窒素工場
梁在厦
식민지인 朝鮮 아니 자본주의의 완전한 발달를 보지못한 朝鮮에 외래자본이 행진곡을 울니고 더욱이 天源의 자원이 풍부하며 地廣人稀한 함경남북도를 향하야 그의 진입이 급속한 것이 최근의 일이며 또한 北鮮개발은 역대 총독정치의 중요항목으로 되여 잇는 일면에는 三菱, 野口 등의 日本 대재벌이 어느듯 침입하야 處處에 왕국을 건설하고 잇는 중인데, 그의 대표적인 興南窒素주식회사와 밋 그 공장의 일반을 엿보기로 하는 것이다.

興南질소주식회사는 日本 대재벌과 정부를 배경으로 가지고 잇는 野口씨의 중심경영으로 咸南興南 驛前 一平方哩 면적에 회사와 공장를 두고 同 新興郡 山上 10평방리 면적을 요한 발전소를 설치하야 건설비 1억1천만원(공장건설비 5천5백만원 발전소 설치비 5천5백만원)을 드려 距今 5년전에 기초공사를 마치고 현재는 8천인의 종사원과 19만「키로왓트」전력을 사용하야 1년간에 2천만톤의 硫化철광 30만톤의 燐鑛 20만톤의 석탄20만, 이 1,500枚의 叺 500만 枚의 麻袋를 주요한 원료와 재료로 하야 50만톤의 硫安 又는 硫燐安 8만돈의 銑鐵 150만樽의 「세멘트」의 주요품을 제조하는 동양 제1의 질소공장인 동시에 所謂 대규모의 자본주의적 기업을 영위하는 회사이다.

이회사의 규모가 얼마나 굉대한 것은 일평방리 地積에 5,500만원 경비로 건축한 공장으로 짐작할수 잇겟지마는 다시 一例를 들면 비료제조용의 전력을 엇기 위하여 新興郡長津 해발 1,500米 지대에 高80米 長400米 8만2천 立坪의 「콘크리트」를 사용하야 長津江水 19만 「키로」를 막은 大堰堤를 축성하야 주위 80粁 深底 80米의 저수지를 맨들어 落着 700米로 129,600「키로왓트」 동력을 발전식히며 해발 1,500米의 고원지대를 容易히 來往할 수 잇는 세계屈指의 「케-불카-」 延長 8粁까지 설치하야 잇는 것이다.

그뿐아니라 원료 及 재료의 수입과 제조품이 수송을 위하야 방파제 延長이 1,000米에 달하고 岸壁에는 10,000돈급 원양기선 2隻과 2,000돈급 2隻 1,500돈급 1隻을 동시에 抵着케한 興南港을 築港하엿스며 目下 築造중에 잇는 大「부릿지, 트란스포-티-」가 ＜26＞ 완성하면 1개년에 150만돈의 화물을 吐呑하기에 가능하다 하고 8,000여명의 종업원을 수용하고 잇는 약 2,000호의 문화식 傭員사택을 비롯하야 傭員합숙소 부속병원 소학교 俱樂部 供給所 등의 완전한 시설이 잇서 공장을 중심으로 신시가를 이루고 공장전표가 잇서 부근에는 지폐로 사용되는 등 모든 생활이 균일되여 공장인은 물론 일대의 자유노동자까지 그의 공장복을 착용한 등 실로 別乾坤의 野口자유국이 건설되여 잇다.
8,000餘人이 종업하고 잇는 이 공장에는 一日을 3분하야 8시간식의 교대작업으로 晝夜업시「엔징」을 돌니며 도든 것이 대규모인 만치 取종업원의 생활이 비교적 안정을 엇고 더욱이 移住한 日本人은 그들의 樂土로 되엇스나 그 8,000인중에는 2,000여명의 朝鮮人 종업원이 잇서 1원70전이상의 勞賃으로 擧皆가 하급에 종업하고 공장 출입외에 약 5,000인의 자유노동자가 운집하야 잇스되 그들의 생활이 日本人 종업원에 비할 정도의 것이 아니며 野口자유국도 朝鮮人 노동자를 위한 왕국이 아닌 것은 다시 말할 것도 업다.
그리고 아모리 野口왕국이라 하드라도 자본주의적 기업이 잇는 곳에 勞資의 계급사회가 성립되고 그의 충돌이 업슬 수 업는 것이라 공장내는 절대 비밀이며 思想取締가 업중하지마는 작년 가을에는 이 공장의 日鮮人 노동자합력으로 中國공산당系 朝鮮공산당 재건공작위원회와 화학노동조합 태평양노동조합 등의 적색비밀 결사가 조직되여 朝鮮思想운동 史上에 一異彩를 보히엇고 이 공장이 港口일반 유사한 時에는 군용항 병기제조 공장으로도 사용할수 잇다 하니 이공장의 장래는 민족상으로 뿐 아니라 정치적 경제적으로도 심히 주목되는 것이다.＜27＞
