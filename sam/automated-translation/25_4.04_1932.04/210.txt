Article Title: 三千里「壁新聞」 (Samch'ŏlli "Wall Newspaper")

Article Type: 소식 (News/Bulletin)

Samch'ŏlli "Wall Newspaper"

How many wealthy people (부호) possessing vast fortunes are there within Chosŏn? This is quite an interesting matter, and according to a recent investigation by the "Imperial Credit Agency" (帝國興信所):

Over 10 million won: 2 people

Over 5 million won: 2 people

Over 4 million won: 1 person

Over 1.7 million won: 2 people

Over 1 million won: 3 people

Over 800 thousand won: 2 people

Over 700 thousand won: 4 people

And it was said that there are approximately 100 people with over 100 thousand won. However, what should be noted here is the fact that the majority of all these wealthy people are Japanese (日本人), and only a few are Koreans (朝鮮人).<77>

**Summary**

This news brief from 1932 reports findings from the Imperial Credit Agency on the number of wealthy individuals in colonial Korea, categorized by asset levels ranging from over 700,000 won to over 10 million won. It notes roughly 100 people possessed over 100,000 won. Crucially, the article points out that the overwhelming majority of these wealthy individuals were Japanese, with only a small fraction being Korean. For historians, this article offers a contemporary data point, however brief, illustrating the stark economic stratification along ethnic lines in colonial Korea, highlighting Japanese economic dominance and the limited wealth accumulation opportunities for Koreans under Japanese rule.
