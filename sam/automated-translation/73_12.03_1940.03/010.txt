Secret Room, The Inner Workings of Our Society
Rumor of Civilian Newspaper Control

The Future of Tong-A, Chosŏn, and Maeil, the Three Papers

According to the "Shinbun Naihō" (Newspaper Insider Report) published in Tokyo, the Government-General intends to strongly control all newspapers throughout Korea. To begin with, seven local newspapers will be abolished, and the four Korean-language papers in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) will be consolidated into two. The most attention-grabbing fact is that the three Korean-script newspapers will also undergo merger and abolition. One rumor suggests that two specific papers (某紙某紙) will be merged to create an economic newspaper focusing on agriculture, commerce, and industry, while general news will be permitted only for one specific paper (某紙). Another rumor suggests only one specific paper (某紙) will be abolished, leaving the other two (某紙某紙) to continue as they are. Their future is drawing intense attention.

Taiwan Also Adopts Mainland-Style Clan Name System (氏制度)

To be Implemented from February 11th
By the decisive action of Governor-General Minami, the "Name Creation and Change" (創氏改名 - Sōshi-kaimei) system was already established in Korea. Now, in Taiwan, for its 5 million islanders, the creation of a mainland-style "Clan Name System" (氏制度 - shi seido) is set to occur starting from the National Foundation Day (建國祭) on February 11th.

Central Council Councilors and the Name Creation/Change Issue

Their Leanings Regarding the Name Creation and Change Issue

On Thursday, December 14th, a large number of Councilors gathered at the Central Council (中樞院 - Chūsūin) and debated for a long time the issue of Koreans adopting Japanese-style names (創氏, 改名) following the revision of the Civil Affairs Decree. Central Council Councilors are generally either nobles holding titles like Viscount or Baron, or former Provincial Governors or officials of Participatory Councilor (叅與官) rank, forming a group that has received the most favor from the Governor-General's administration. Therefore, in many ways, whether these Councilors change their names is attracting the attention of the era. The result of the meeting at that time* was that about 30-40% were individuals who intended to create names and change given names entirely in the Japanese style. There was also a faction that wanted to wait for the detailed regulations to be announced, and other opinions existed, so unanimity was not reached. However, with the implementation period approaching, the actual actions of these gentlemen are said to be drawing significant public scrutiny.

Likely Transferring from Poland

According to rumors originating from the Hsinking (Shinkyō) area, Mr. Pak Sŏk-yun (朴錫胤), after Poland suffered military defeat at the hands of Germany and the Soviet Union, lost his post and has been staying somewhere in Europe. In any case, depending on the developments of the turbulent situation in Europe, Mr. Pak's stage of activity is drawing great attention.

Ch'unwŏn's Royalties Approximately 100,000 Won

Total Royalty Earnings Over Half a Lifetime from One Pen

Ch'unwŏn (春園 - Yi Kwang-su), a master of the literary world and a pioneer of Korea's new culture, has produced numerous works with his single pen over the past 30 years. He has published nearly 30 types of works, including poetry collections, novels like "Mujŏng" (無情) and "Kaech'ŏkcha" (開拓者), and essays. It is estimated that the amount received from selling the copyrights of these various works to bookstores (冊肆) amounts to 100,000 won. Of course, this figure likely includes manuscript fees earned from writing novels for newspapers or contributing articles to magazines and other publications.

"Sarang" Fetches 3,500 Won

Furthermore, the copyright for his recently published novel "Sarang" (사랑 - Love), in two volumes (upper and lower), was reportedly sold to Pakmun Sŏgwan (博文書舘) for 3,500 won around this time.

Epic Novel "Im Kkŏkjŏng" Published

"Im Kkŏkjŏng" (林巨正), written by Pyŏkch'o Hong Myŏng-hŭi (碧初 洪命憙) and serialized continuously in the Chosŏn Ilbo for over ten years, has finally been completed after many years. To mark its completion, the Chosŏn Ilbo publishing department decided to publish it, releasing the first volume in October. It is expected to become a complete collection of about 10 volumes.

3,000 Won in Royalties

**Summary**

This 1940 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine presents a collection of news tidbits reflecting life in Korea under Japanese rule. Topics include rumored consolidation and control of Korean newspapers by the Government-General, the implementation of the Japanese clan name system (Sōshi-kaimei) in Taiwan following its introduction in Korea, statistics on Japanese language proficiency among Koreans, the number of Japanese-Korean marriages, significant Korean and Japanese populations moving into occupied China, details on prominent figures like Pak Sŏk-yun and Wang Jingwei, economic news about bank assets and company bonuses, literary updates including Yi Kwang-su's (Ch'unwŏn) earnings and Hong Myŏng-hŭi's (Pyŏkch'o) novel publication, and personnel movements. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the tightening colonial control (newspaper censorship, Sōshi-kaimei), the promotion of Japanese assimilation policies ("Naeseon Ilche," language statistics), the socio-economic landscape during wartime (economic boom for some sectors, population movements into China), the activities and attitudes of the Korean elite (Central Council members' debate on name changes, Pak Yŏng-hyo's state-funded funeral), and cultural production under constraint (literary publications). Historians might find the specific data on population movements, Japanese language use, intermarriage rates, and the candid discussion of elite collaboration (Central Council) particularly interesting.
