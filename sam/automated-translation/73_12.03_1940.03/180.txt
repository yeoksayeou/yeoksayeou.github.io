Article Title: 朝鮮農民과 滿洲國 開拓 (Korean Farmers and the Development of Manchukuo)
Author: 新京 洪陽明 (Shinkyō Hong Yangmyŏng)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Article)

Korean Farmers and the Development of Manchukuo
						Shinkyō Hong Yangmyŏng
-Visiting the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center-

Starting with the migration conference of the Inner [Japan]-Korea-Manchuria development authorities held in the national capital, Shinkyō, at the very beginning of this spring, several subsequent meetings of the relevant authorities held in Shinkyō and Tōkyō have fundamentally shifted the previous policy regarding Inner [Japanese] and Korean migration. A grand ideal has been bestowed upon the migration policy, which previously felt somewhat limited to economic significance. Today, it is already common knowledge that the development policy has become the most crucial national policy integrating Japan and Manchukuo. However, to reiterate and summarize the ideal of the development policy, as already expressed in the Outline of Development Policy revealed by the Development Plan Council, the highest body for Japan-Manchuria development policy: in order to truly advance Manchuria, the land of the Kingly Way founded on ethnic harmony and a model for the establishment of the New Order in East Asia, politically and economically, it became necessary to deeply and widely infuse Japanese people, who will form its core, quantitatively and qualitatively into all aspects of this emerging nation, thereby epochally promoting the task of achieving harmonious unity and prosperous development among all ethnic groups. As a result, the designation for previous migrants was changed to "pioneer settlers" (開拓民), and the issue of training these pioneer settlers to truly understand the ideal of developing Manchukuo, the pivotal axis of the New Order in East Asia, and to embody the spirit of ethnic harmony, has also gained significant importance. In line with this advancement of the development state system, Korean pioneer settlers, as part of the subjects of the Japanese Empire, were also imbued with national policy significance. Their position evolved from being purely economic migrants, in the sense of leaving Korea because they could not survive there, to one where the self-awareness of their mission as pioneer settlers in the positive sense, carrying out the important <218> national policy of Japan-Manchuria unity for the development of Manchukuo, was demanded. Particularly, the trend towards realizing Japan-Korea unity within Korea added impetus to this, leading to the decision that, in principle, Korean pioneer settlers would be treated identically to pioneer settlers from mainland Japan under the national policy. Accordingly, the issue of integrating Mant'ak (滿拓 - Manchurian Development Company) for mainland Japanese and Sŏnt'ak (鮮拓 - Chosŏn Development Company) for Koreans, which served as local guidance agencies for Inner [Japanese] and Korean pioneer settlers, has already materialized. A unified development policy across Japan and Korea, working hand-in-glove with the Manchukuo development authorities, is becoming a model based on the New Order in East Asia. Against this backdrop, the issue of guiding and training Korean pioneer settlers for their qualitative improvement, which had felt neglected previously, came to be regarded as important. The Kangmilbong (江密峯) Pioneer Training Center is one important indicator of the authorities' efforts towards Korean pioneer settlers amidst this epochal shift in national development policy. In this respect, although the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center is merely a small supply base for peasant leaders, it stands out prominently, 'close-up' at the forefront of the national development policy. From this viewpoint, the reporter, upon receiving an invitation from Sŏnt'ak to attend the completion ceremony for the training center's buildings on the 21st, leaped for joy as if saying "I've been waiting for this" and took the opportunity to visit this place.

◇

Early on the morning of November 21st, the reporter, along with Development General Bureau Director Yūki (結城), the bureau's Second Guidance Section Chief Yun Sangp'il (尹相弼), Chōsen Government-General Section Chief for Development Affairs Tsuji (辻), Matsushima (松島) Embassy's Korean Affairs Section Chief, Aide Kim Ch'ugak (金推珏), and several other gentlemen, departed from Shinkyō Station and arrived in Kirin (吉林). Guided by Sŏnt'ak company director Im Hallyong (林漢龍) and Mr. Kang Sŏngt'ae (姜聖邰), who were waiting for us, we took a break for about half an hour before the group transferred to the Kangmilbong line train, arriving at Kangmilbong around 12 o'clock. From there, we reached the Kangmilbong Training Center, approximately two kilometers away. The group of over forty people walked this two-kilometer distance. Only Mant'ak President Nimiya (二宮), Development General Bureau Director Yūki, and a few others rode in horse-drawn carts specially prepared by Sŏnt'ak. Walking at a leisurely pace across the plain under temperatures more than ten degrees below freezing, breathing in the crisp air, was truly refreshing for me, who, despite being in Manchukuo for a full year, had not set foot in a rural area outside the two cities of Shinkyō and Harbin. In Kirin, Mr. On Chong (溫鍾), head of this newspaper's Kirin main branch, joined us with his camera. The Kangmilbong vicinity seems to be quite densely populated with Korean farmers; along the way, the sight of Korean children in *paji-chŏgori* (바지저고리 - traditional trousers and jacket) and vests, darting about and ice-skating in frozen ditches, and the faces of simple farmers met here and there. A sign posted at a Korean shop on the street reading "Salted pollack roe has arrived" (백하젓왔읍니다) seems an indicator that tastes, even if circumstances change, do not alter easily. Putting aside digressions, I intend to describe the details of the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center, the proceedings of the completion ceremony, and some impressions <219> below.

◇

The location of the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center, to be precise, is Kangmilbong, Yungchi County (永吉縣), Kirin Province (吉林省). Its function, as the name suggests, is a training ground (道場) to cultivate pioneer settler instructors. Training centers of this kind already exist in several locations within mainland Japan and Manchukuo for the core training of mainland Japanese pioneer settlers. There is also a training center managed by the Government-General in Sep'o (洗浦), Kangwŏn Province (江原道) within Korea, and plans are underway to establish six more centers of this type starting next fiscal year. However, the Kangmilbong Training Center is the first of its kind located within Manchukuo. The significance of establishing this training center, as mentioned earlier, lies in demonstrating locally, within Manchukuo, the epochal shift of treating Korean pioneer settlers as national policy pioneer settlers. Furthermore, the current Kangmilbong Training Center holds greater significance than the Sep'o Training Center in Korea because it trains pioneer leaders actually within Manchukuo, the target of the national development policy. It goes without saying that training on-site is more practical and hands-on than training within Korea. It was timely for the Chōsen Government-General, in cooperation with the Manchukuo authorities, to respond to the emphasis placed on pioneer settler training within Manchukuo and entrust its management to the Manchuria-Chosŏn Development Company (滿鮮拓殖會社 - Man-Sŏn T'akchik Hoesa). Of course, depending on the outcome of the comprehensive, concrete plan currently being drafted regarding the division of responsibilities for carrying out the national development policy, its management might be transferred to the Manchukuo development authorities or perhaps to the Concordia Association's (協和會 - Hyŏphwahoe) development section, which is expected to handle the training division, following the integration of Mant'ak and Sŏnt'ak. But the crucial point is not the specifics of administrative jurisdiction. The general necessity arose to cultivate core members among Korean pioneer settlers – individuals possessing a strong spirit, like warriors developing Manchukuo, and reliable practical knowledge. They are not mere salaried workers. The need is urgent for forming a core of pioneer settlers who have embodied the spirit of Manchukuo's construction, grasped the ideal of ethnic harmony, realized that developing and advancing Manchukuo is a sacred task creating a model for the New East Asian Construction, and possess the determination to become friends and providers for the pioneer settlers as warriors for the prosperity of Asia. This becomes self-evident when one considers how difficult it is to mentally and materially stabilize general pioneer settlers coming into Manchukuo, where all circumstances are different. Objectively, the Kangmilbong Training Center exists with this ideal as its cradle.

The natural surroundings near Kangmilbong, quite different from the endless, monotonous plains of Manchuria, evoked images of purple mountains and clear waters (山紫水明) for the author, even though it was early winter with all the withered leaves fallen. Surrounded by small, clustered hills, with clear streams murmuring here and there, the mountains and rivers here are rather closer to those of the Korean peninsula than the typically changeless, potentially wearying Manchurian landscape often perceived as lacking mountains and water. <220> Whether this training center, situated amidst such scenery and accommodating pioneer warriors from the peninsula, was chosen out of deep consideration to mitigate the emotional impact of entering Manchuria resulting from drastic geographical change, I do not know. In any case, this training center, perhaps by chance, is located in a good place. Construction of the buildings began in July of this year, and training commenced in September using partially completed structures, with completion now achieved. It consists of five thatched-roof buildings (草屋) and two brick buildings (煉瓦舍屋 - lecture hall and staff offices) totaling 458 *pyeong* (坪 - approx. 1514 sq meters) at a total cost of 76,000 Yuan. Thus, it's not a large complex, but the trainees' residences were designated as First Farmhouse, Second Farmhouse, etc., prioritizing the same level of simplicity and frugality as actual life in pioneer settlements. It is said that everything, including cowsheds and work areas, replicates the current conditions of Manchurian Korean pioneer farmhouses. Frankly speaking, as the author has not yet visited a Korean pioneer settlement, I cannot say much more. Farming involves assigning a certain plot of land to each farming unit, composed of four trainees, for cultivation. Since it was established in the autumn this year, this could not be implemented; training focused mainly on spiritual cultivation and classroom instruction on practical matters, according to reports. Consequently, the inspection remained rather abstract, which was regrettable, but unavoidable given the center's founding only in September. A summary description of this training center's details is as follows.

First, the policies and goals stated by this training center are as follows:
1. Training Policy = To embody the spirit of Manchukuo's founding, strive for the practice of the development policy, and cultivate the qualities necessary for the construction and guidance of pioneer settlements.
2. Training Goals
    ① Dedication: To instill the noble spirit of dedicating oneself to the sacred task of Manchukuo's construction and the national development policy.
    ② Cultivation: To foster leader-like spirit (氣魄) and pride (矜持) through the entirety of training life.
    ③ Practice: To enable trainees to acquire the knowledge and skills necessary for guiding farm households and rural villages, becoming individuals of practice and action (實踐躬行).

And the admission capacity of this training center is as follows:
1. Cadre Trainees (Men aged 25 or older who have graduated from middle school or higher)
2. Youth Trainees (Men aged 20 or older who have graduated from elementary school)

The training period for Cadre Trainees is approximately ten months. This year, only Cadre Trainees were admitted, and due to the late establishment, they entered on October 7th. Therefore, temporary short-term training will be conducted until March next year, necessitated by the need for them to guide the group pioneer settlers arriving next fiscal year. <221> Thus, the current trainees are all Cadre Trainees, numbering fifty-five. Among them, forty-nine were selected by the Development General Bureau's Second Guidance Section based on a request from the Chōsen Government-General within Korea. According to rumor (仄聞), in the future, all these Cadre Trainees will be selected within Manchukuo by the Second Guidance Section, which seems commendable as it standardizes based on trainees familiar with the local conditions. These Cadre Trainees bear the critical mission of becoming direct leaders of the Pioneer Groups (開拓團 - Kaet'akdan) planned for implementation starting next year. The breakdown of the fifty-five currently in training is: three candidates for Group Leader (團長候補者), two for Accounting Managers (經理擔當者), and fifty for Agricultural Instructors (農事指導者). These Group Leaders are, as the name suggests, leaders of the Pioneer Groups; the Accounting Managers and Agricultural Instructors are responsible personnel assigned to various sections within the Pioneer Group to handle practical affairs. That they are more direct leaders of the pioneer settlers than government officials or any other related personnel is because they, body and soul, literally form the core of the pioneer settlers. That is, they themselves become pioneer settlers, farm the same area of land as other pioneer settlers, acquire land ownership through annual installments, and become permanent settlers. Of course, regarding their hardships and joys, separate allowances are provided: about 150-160 Yuan per month for Group Leaders, and about 60-70 Yuan per month for Accounting Managers and Agricultural Instructors. The Pioneer Group (開拓團) is a new system to be implemented for Korean group pioneer settlers starting next year, following the epochal shift in the ideals of the national development policy. Based on the Pioneer Group Law, which will be enacted and implemented soon, it aims to build a type of special autonomous administrative body during the planning phase. During this period, the Pioneer Group Leader will also perform a kind of administrative role. In the second five-year phase, it will transition developmentally to a town/village system (街村制), with the Group Leader becoming the town/village head (街村長), and the Accounting Managers and Agricultural Instructors changing into town/village officials (役員), aiming for development into a complete local administrative unit of Manchukuo. One Pioneer Group consists of one group of pioneer settlers, and one group of collective pioneer settlers (集團開拓團) is based on a unit of 300 households, meaning one Pioneer Group will form a large settlement of around 900 households. Additionally, each Pioneer Group must have one Group Leader, one Accounting Manager, four Agricultural Instructors, one clinic (病院), one school (學校), and one veterinarian (獸醫) attached. Seen this way, the training of the three Group Leader candidates, two Accounting Manager candidates, and fifty Agricultural Instructors who will stand at the forefront of the Pioneer Group system is a crucial undertaking that foretells (卜する) the future of the group pioneer settlers arriving next year. Although the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center may not be large in scale, its mission is indeed paramount and immense.
◇<222>

The author met one of the Group Leader candidates, Mr. Yi Sangnak (李相洛), a graduate of Yŏnhŭi College (延專), and asked about his impressions of life here. Mr. Yi, whose face seemed filled with a resolute spirit, stated firmly, "Having cast aside my job as a county clerk (郡屬) and come to the continent intending to do something useful, however small, for the development of Manchukuo and the guidance of pioneer settlers, I am determined to become a true pioneer worker." The faces of these fifty-five individuals, absorbing the spirit befitting pioneer settler leaders through an extremely frugal lifestyle, all displayed great spirit (氣宇) and a valiant quality, as if expressing their resolve to overcome any hardship and share fortune and misfortune alike with the pioneer settlers. Their reliable, solid attitude – waking at 5:30 AM, deeply inhaling the fresh continental air of the Kangmilbong plains, honing the grand ideal of Asian prosperity (興亞), the spirit of diligence, and management skills, and diligently engaging in practical work (勞作) – made me, who has mainly interacted with city dwellers suffering from urban maladies (though perhaps I myself am one of them), living self-consoling, empty lives filled with futile romantic dreams and pale sentiments, bow my head.

The significance of the training for these individuals – who will most directly and extensively guide the Korean compatriot pioneer settlers coming to Manchukuo seeking a livelihood, further endowed with the glory of being warriors for the development of Manchukuo, the model for the New East Asian Construction, ensuring they can live prosperously, and on top of that, instilling self-awareness of their mission as construction soldiers, and who will serve as models for the pioneer settlers – should be valued most highly.

◇

The settlement of Korean pioneer settlers next year is planned based on an annual standard of 10,000 households: 2,000 households of group settlers (集團開拓民), 4,000 households of collective settlers (集合開拓民), and 4,000 households of dispersed settlers (分散開拓民). Among these, those selected by the authorities, provided with land and subsidies, and settled under specific controls will constitute the group settlers. Next year's 2,000 households of group settlers are primarily designated for settlement in Peian Province (北安省 - Puk'an-sŏng), and an advance party has already crossed the border and is on site. Thus, gazing far towards the chilly Peian Province swept by northern winds, conscious of their grave mission for the coming year, the hearts of these trainees must constantly experience fresh emotion, akin to soldiers preparing for deployment having foreseen their battlefield. Finally, looking at the provincial origins of the fifty-five individuals currently in training: Kyŏnggi 5, South Ch'ungch'ŏng 2, North Ch'ungch'ŏng 5, South Chŏlla 5, North Chŏlla 14, South Kyŏngsang 9, North Kyŏngsang 5, Hwanghae 1, Kangwŏn 1, South P'yŏngan 1, North P'yŏngan 1, South Hamgyŏng 1. North Chŏlla has the most. This is because the three southern (三南) and six provinces (六道 - likely referring to the southern provinces plus maybe Kyŏnggi, Kangwŏn, Hwanghae?) are generally the reservoir for pioneer settlers. Their assignment is also intended, as much as possible, to match the provincial origins of the settlers they will guide. Regarding age, the youngest is 25 and the oldest is 43. There are three 26-year-olds, seven 27-year-olds, four each of 28, 29, and 30-year-olds, one each of 31 and 32-year-olds, five 33-year-olds, <223>
two each aged 36, 37, and 38, four each aged 39 and 40, one aged 41, and two aged 43, making the average age 29.5. This is because, to make them friends of the farmers rather than recent graduates, people with experience in social life were selected. Regarding education level: 3 graduated from professional schools (專門學校 - Chŏnmun Hakkyo), 43 from agricultural schools (農學校 - Nong Hakkyo), 4 from supplementary agricultural schools (農業補習學校 - Nong'ŏp Posŭp Hakkyo), 3 dropped out of agricultural school mid-course, 1 graduated from commercial school (商業學校 - Sang'ŏp Hakkyo), and 1 from middle school (中學校 - Chunghakkyo). Their previous careers are diverse: 2 provincial/county employees (道郡雇員), 15 agricultural association technicians (農會技手), 2 township technicians/clerks (面技手 及 面書記), 2 township accountants (面會計員), 1 provincial industrial technician (道産業技手), 1 county clerk (郡屬), 2 company employees, 2 teachers, 1 local forestry official (地方森林主事), 1 cultivation cooperative technician (耕作組合 技手), 1 industrial cooperative employee (産業組合 雇員), 1 railway police member (鐵道警察隊員), and 1 farmer (農業).

The completion ceremony began with Sŏnt'ak Director Im Hallyong presiding, starting with the deepest bows to both the Japanese and Manchurian nations. When Sŏnt'ak President Nimiya, a Lieutenant General in the army, began his address, he encouraged the trainees in a solemn yet passionate tone, stating, "Whether it is the mission of the officers and soldiers fighting bravely on the continent for the Japanese Empire and the establishment of the New Order in East Asia, or the mission of the pioneer warriors engaged in the development of Manchukuo as part of the grand plan for Asian prosperity, the means may differ, but the objective is the same. I hope that you, the trainees, will possess the great ideal and spirit of participating in the construction project of Manchukuo – the land of the Kingly Way characterized by ethnic harmony, which can be called a model for the New Order in East Asia – with the same spirit of public service (奉公心) as the soldiers fighting and shedding blood on the battlefield." Sŏnt'ak, led by this idealistic, military-background president, seemed to carry the weight of a thousand *jun* (千鈞 - ancient unit of weight, figuratively immense weight). Next, congratulatory remarks began, starting with Mr. Yūki, Director of the Development General Bureau, the highest executive body for Manchukuo's development policy. Mr. Yūki, who was one of the central figures of the youth movement (青年**) that created the surging new trends just before the Manchurian Incident and became the spiritual matrix for the Concordia Association's vast organization after the founding of the state, is reputed among newspaper reporters as having a "divine nature" (神性), possessing a sublime, leader-like character. He pointedly remarked, "Although various causes are cited for rural impoverishment, the greatest is the lack of rural leaders," emphatically highlighting the grave importance of the pioneer trainees' duties. Following this, the congratulatory message from the head of the Foreign Affairs Bureau of the Chōsen Government-General was read by the Section Chief for Development Affairs, who had come all the way from Keijō (京城 - Seoul) specifically to attend. After congratulatory messages from the Mant'ak President and the Governor of Kirin Province, came the forceful reply from the Training Center Director, Tsubouchi Sakunojō (坪內作之丞). Mr. Tsubouchi, who previously served as an agricultural school principal in Korea and director of the Sep'o Migrant Training Center in Kangwŏn Province, and now became the inaugural director of this Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center, the first of its kind established within Manchukuo, stated straightforwardly and without pretense in a reliable manner that he well understood the gravity of his duties and would do his utmost. The fact that this Kangmilbong village, a corner of Yungchi County in Kirin Province, witnessed today a gathering of virtually all the top officials responsible for development from across Manchuria signified the great importance of this training center's <224> opening. That is, in addition to the aforementioned Sŏnt'ak President Nimiya and Development General Bureau Director Yūki, the presence of Mr. Yun Sangp'il, the de facto top leader for Korean development as the head of the Development General Bureau's Second Guidance Section; Mr. Tsuji Keigo (辻桂五), the top practical official responsible for development affairs from the Chōsen Government-General's side as Section Chief of the Foreign Affairs Bureau's Development Section; the Matsushima Embassy's Korean Affairs Section Chief acting as liaison between these two; and over ten other important figures related to development turned this simple completion ceremony in the small lecture hall into something resembling a general meeting of development leaders. After the ceremony, we toured the buildings and grounds, then received a sumptuous feast (饗應), difficult to prepare in such a mountainous area, prepared to welcome the original guests. Around 4 PM, we set off on our return journey, marching briskly in small groups along the 2-kilometer path back to the station. Sŏnt'ak had specially prepared two horse-drawn carts and encouraged those with tired legs to take turns riding, but the entire group, enveloped in a cheerful mood, hardly used them. The sight of the young and cheerful-looking Chōsen Government-General Section Chief for Development Affairs Tsuji, seated beside Second Guidance Section Chief Yun, riding a horse-cart slowly along the country road, falling behind the walking group, was a comical scene rarely savored by city dwellers.

Thus, the group headed from Kangmilbong Station towards Kirin. Section Chief Tsuji and some others went directly to Shinkyō, while the remaining group attended a reception prepared by Sŏnt'ak in Kirin city. However, the author, along with Matsushima's Korean Affairs Section Chief, had dinner and then promptly went to the station alone, boarding the train for Shinkyō. Politely declining Matsushima's suggestion to get off and rest at Hsiakiutai (下九台), I returned alone to Shinkyō, nodding off, around 11:30 PM. Having stayed in Kangmilbong for only two and a half hours, I frankly confess there is no more material to write about, and conclude here. Lastly, I will introduce the curriculum of the Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center, which I intended to include previously but omitted:
1. Cadre Training
    a. Civics Education (The Way of the Farmer 農民道, History of Manchukuo's Founding 滿洲建國史, Manchukuo Labor Overview 滿洲國勞一般, Manchukuo National Policies, especially Development Policy 滿洲國國策 特히 開拓國策, Rural-related Laws and Regulations 農村關係法規, etc.)
    b. Practical Subjects Education (Rural Management 農村經營, Farm Management 農科經營, General Agriculture 農業一般, Accounting 會說, Bookkeeping 簿記, etc.)
    c. Practical Training (實習)
    d. Manchurian Language (滿洲語 - likely referring to Mandarin Chinese)
    e. Military Drill (校練) (Including Martial Arts 武道)<225>

**Summary**

This 1940 article by Hong Yangmyŏng describes his visit to the newly opened Kangmilbong Pioneer Training Center in Manchuria (Manchukuo), designed specifically for training Korean cadre leaders for state-sponsored agricultural settlements. The author details the center's background within the intensified Japan-Manchuria-Korea development policy (日滿鮮一體), emphasizing the shift from viewing Korean migrants as purely economic refugees to essential "pioneer warriors" (開拓戰士) fulfilling a national mission within the "New Order in East Asia." He describes the center's facilities, curriculum (focused on ideology, practical farming skills, leadership, and Manchurian language), the profile of the initial 55 trainees (background, age, education), and the structure of the planned "Pioneer Groups" (開拓團) they would lead. The article highlights speeches from high-ranking Japanese and Manchukuo officials emphasizing dedication, sacrifice, and the ideal of "ethnic harmony" (民族協和).

For historians, the article is highly revealing of Japanese colonial ideology and practice during wartime mobilization. It explicitly frames Korean agricultural migration to Manchuria not as a response to poverty in Korea, but as a core component of imperial expansion and the consolidation of Manchukuo under the "Kingly Way" (王道) and "Japan-Manchuria Unity." The detailed description of the training center, its curriculum emphasizing ideological indoctrination ("Manchukuo's Founding Spirit," "National Policy") alongside practical skills, and the profile of the trainees selected for leadership roles provide concrete evidence of the mechanisms used to integrate Koreans into the Japanese imperial project as subordinate partners. The language used ("pioneer warriors," "sacred task," comparing settlers to soldiers) underscores the militarized nature of this "development" policy.

朝鮮農民과 滿洲國 開拓
						新京 洪陽明
-姜密峯開拓訓練所를 차저-

今春劈頭에 國都新京에서 開催되였든 內鮮滿開拓當局의 移民會議를 起點으로 繼續하야 數次新京, 東京에서 開催되였든 開催關係 當局議會는 從來의 內鮮移民에 對한 方針을 根本的으로 轉換하야 從來經濟的 意味에 局限되엿든 感이있든 移民政策에 一大理想을 賦與하고 今日에 있어서는 開拓政策은 日滿兩國을 一體로한 最重要國策이되고있는 것은 이미 當識이되고 있다. 그러나 反復하야 開拓政策의 理想을 要約하여본다면 日滿開拓政策의 最高機關인 開拓案議會에서 開明한 開拓政策大網에 이미 表示된바와 같이 民族協和의 王道榮土인 滿洲을 眞實로 東亞新秩序樹立의 한 標本이 된만큼 政治的으로나 經濟的으로 向上前進시킴에는 그 核心이될 日本人을 量的으로나 實的으로나 이 新興國家의 모든 部面에널니 또깁히 浸透시켜서 諸民族의 渾然一致하야 繁榮하고 開習하는 任務를 劃時期的으로 推進시킬 必要를 느끼게 된 것이다. 이 結果 從來의 移民을 名稱까지 開拓民으로 고치게되고 그들이 眞實로 東亞新秩序의 重點的樞軸이될 滿洲國開發의 理想을 理解하고 民族協和의 精神을 體得케하기 爲한 開拓民의 訓練의 問題가 또한 重大性을 띄게되어왔다. 이러한 開拓國體의 前進에따라 日本帝國臣民의 一部인 朝鮮開拓民에도 國策的意義를 賦與하게되고 從來에 朝鮮서살수없어서 滿洲로간다는 式의 純經濟移民으로서의 入場을 一步前進시켜 滿洲國開發을 爲한 日滿一體의 重＜218＞要國策을 遂行하는 積極的意味의 開拓民으로서의 使命의 自覺이 要請되었다. 特히 朝鮮內에 在한 內鮮一體의 具現化傾向은 이에 拍重를 加하야 原則으로써 朝鮮開拓民도 日本內地人과 同一한 國策開拓民으로써 取扱하는 方針이 決定되것이다. 이에 따라서 內鮮開拓民의 現地轉導機關이라고한 內地人에 對한 滿拓朝鮮人에 對한 鮮拓의 統合問題가 이미 具體化하고 內鮮을 通하야 一元化한 開拓政策은 滿洲國 開拓當局과 表裡一體가되며 東亞新秩序에 의한 모델이되고 있다. 이러한 背景에서 從來에 閑却된 感이있든 朝鮮開拓民의 質的向上을 爲한 指導訓練의 問題가 重要視하게되였다. 江密峯開拓訓練所는 이러한 開拓國策의 劃期的轉換에 際하야 朝鮮開拓民에 對한 當局의 努力을 가르키는 重要한 指標의하나이다. 이러한 點에서 江密峯開拓訓練所는 一個의 조고마한 農民指導者의 一兵站에 不過하지마는 開拓國策의 前面에 『크로즈업』한 커다란 存在이다. 記者는 이러한 見地에서 去二十一日이 開拓訓練所 舍屋의 落成式에 參加하라는 鮮拓의 招請을 받고 『기다렸읍니다』하는 것과같이 雀躍하야 이곳을 訪問하는 檂會를가졌다.
◇
十一月二十一日早朝 記者는 結成開拓總局長同局第二指導科長尹相弼氏, 朝鮮總督府辻拓務課長, 松島大使館朝鮮課長, 金推珏援佐其他諸氏와함께 新京驛을떠나 吉林에 到着, 먼저와서 기다리고있는 滿鮮拓殖會社參事林漢龍氏, 姜聖邰氏의 案內를받어 約半時間休憩後 一行과함께 姜密峯行軍에 乘換하야 十二時頃姜密峯에 到着 이곳에서 約二粁되는 距離에있는 姜密峯訓練所에 到着하였다. 이二粁의 路程을 一行四十餘名은 徒步로것도 二宮滿拓總裁 結城開拓總局長外數氏만은 鮮拓에서 特히 準備한 荷馬車를 타서 다가치 느릿느릿한 步調로 氷點下十餘度의 벌판을 爽快한 大氣를 흠벅마시면서 거러가는 氣分은 滿洲國에온지 萬一年동안 新京, 哈爾濱의 二部市以外는 農村에 한발자욱도 못드듸여본 나에게는 眞實로 淸新한것이였다. 吉林에서는 本報吉林總支局長溫鍾氏가 카메라를가지고 同途했다. 姜密峯附近은 朝鮮人農民이 相當히 密集한곳인모양으로 가는 路中에 바지저고리에 좇기를 입고 어름어른 개골창에서 어름지치는 朝鮮兒孩들의 뛰노는꼴 淳朴한 農民들의 얼굴과 얼굴이 이곳저곳에서 부드치인다. 거리의 朝鮮人商店에는 『백하젓왔읍니다』고 써붙여있는곳은 變해도 口味는 좀처럼 不變하는것이라는 것을 가르키는 指標로보인다. 餘談은 그만두고 以上江密峯開拓訓練所의 內容과 또 落成式의 經過와 若干의 感想을＜219＞ 敍述코저한다.
◇
江密峯開拓訓練所가 있는곳은 仔細히말하면 吉林省永吉縣江密峯이오 그하는일은 그 名稱이 가르침과 같이 開拓民指導員을 養成하는 訓練道場이다. 此種의 訓練所는 內地人開拓民의 中緊訓練을 爲하야 이미 內地, 滿洲國內의 여러곳이있고 朝鮮內에도 江原道洗浦에 總督府經營의 訓練所가 있으며 明年度부터 六處에다 이시種類의 訓練所를 新設한 것이 企圖되여 있으나 滿洲國內에 在한것으로는 이 江密峯訓練所가 嚆矢이다. 特히 이 訓練所設置의 意義는 前述한바와 같이 朝鮮開拓民을 國策開拓民으로써 取扱하는 劃期的인 轉換을 現地인 滿洲國에있어서 實證한데있다. 또한 現在의 江密峯訓練所가 朝鮮內洗浦訓練所보담重要한 意義를 가지게 되는 것은 開拓指導者들을 實際로 開拓國策의 對象인 滿洲國內에서 訓練하는데있으니 現地에서의 訓練이 朝鮮內에있어서의 그것보담 實踐躬行的임을 다시말함 必要도 없는 것이다. 朝鮮總督府가 滿洲國當局과의 協力으로 滿洲國內에 開拓民訓練의 重點을 두는 것을 慫應하야 滿洲拓殖會社로하여금 그 經營에 當케한 것은 時宜에 適한일이다. 勿論 開拓國策遂行의 擔當部分이 目下 成案中에있는 全面的 具體案의 結果如何로는 滿拓鮮拓의 統合에따러 이 經營이 滿洲國開拓當局에 또는 訓練部門을 擔當할것이 豫想되고있는 協和會開拓粁로 移管될른지도 몰으지마는 要는 그 事務官轄關係의 如何가 아니다. 滿洲國은 開發하는 戰士와 같은 氣魄을가진 굿건한 精神과 틀림없는 實務의 知識을가진 開拓民의 核心을 形成할 鮮系開拓民中堅分子를 養成할 必要가 一般的으로 要請된 點이다. 單純한 月給쟁이가 아니다. 滿洲國建設의 精神을 體得하고 民族協和의 理想을 把持하야 滿洲國을 開發하고 發展시키는 것이 新東亞建設의 한 『모델』을 맨드는 聖業임을 깨닷고 興亞戰士로써 開拓民의 親友가되고 살림꾼이되겠다는 覺悟를가진 開拓民의 中心을 形成할 心要가 얼마나 切實한 問題인가는 모든 事情이달은 滿洲國內에 들어오는 一般開拓民을 이곳에 精神的物質的으로 安定시키는일이 얼마나 어려운일인가를 想到하면 自明될 것이다. 江密峯訓練所는 客觀的으로 이러한 理想을 태누리로하고있는 存在이다.
滿洲에 끝없는 平野的인 單調와는 무론 風景이달은 江密峯附近의 自然은 枯葉조차 다 떨어지고만 初冬이지마는 筆者로하여금 山紫水明의 『이메이지』를 그리게하였다. 오물조물한적은 뫼들에둘니어 이곳저곳에 맑은시내물이 졸졸흐르고 이곳의 山川은 山없고 물없은것과같은 無變化의 倦怠를 주기쉬운＜220＞ 滿洲에있어서는 오히려 半島山河에 가까운 便이다. 이러한사이에 서있는 江密峯開拓訓練所가 半島의 開拓戰士들을 收容하고있는 것은 急激한 地理的 變化에서 오는 感想的인 入滿氣分을 緩和하려는 深慮에서 選擇된것인지 아닌지는 모르지만 어떠튼 이 訓練所는 偶然인지는 몰으나 좋은곳에 놓여있다. 舍屋은 今年七月에 建築하기 始作하고 九月부터 一部竣工된 一部舍屋을 使用하야 訓練을 開始하다가 이제 完成된것인데 四五八坪의 草屋五棟과 煉瓦舍屋(講堂과 職員室)二棟으로 總工費 七萬六千圓의 것임으로 큰 建物은아니나 訓練生들의 住宅을 第一農家 第二農家의 順序로 區分하야 開拓地에서 實際生活함과같은 꼭같은 程度의 質素와 節儉을 主眼으로 한것이였다. 오양간있고 作業터있고 모든 것을 滿洲朝鮮開拓民農家의 現況그대로 한것이라고한다. 正直하게 말하면 아즉 筆者는 朝鮮人開拓部落을 못가보았음으로 무엇이라고 말할수가없다. 農耕은 訓練生各四人으로 形成된 農耕에 對하야 一定한 土地를 配定하고 作業케한 것이다. 今年에는 秋季에 創始된만큼 實行하지못하고 主로 精神上訓練과 實務에 對한 授業이 主가되였다고한다. 따라서 視察은 形而上的의 끄치게된 것은 遺憾이였으나 이는 이 訓練所가 九月에야 創始되였음으로 不得已한일이다. 이에 揭記的으로 이 訓練所의 內容을 一■하면 다음과같다.
첫재 이 訓練所가 表示한 方針과 目標는 다옴과 같다.
一, 訓練方針=滿洲國建國의 精神을 體하야 開拓政策의 實踐에 邁進하고 開拓部落의 建設 및 指導에 必要한 資質을 鍊磨함을 方針으로함
二, 訓練目標
① 挻身, 滿洲國建設의 聖業 및 開拓國策에 殉하는 崇高한 精神을 體得케함
② 訓練生活의 全般을 通하야 指導者다운 氣魄 및 矜持를 涵養케함
③ 農家農村經營指導에 必要한 知識技術을 習得케하야 實踐躬行의 士가되게함
그리고 이 訓練所의 收容定員은 다음과같다.
