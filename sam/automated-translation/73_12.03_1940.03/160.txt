**Summary**

This article from the March 1940 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine lists numerous prominent organizations active in Korea at the time. The list spans various sectors including business (mining, retail, textiles, manufacturing, printing), media (newspapers, publishers), cultural and professional associations (writers, language, film, painting, law, medicine, business), religious institutions (Cheondogyo, Christian, Buddhist, Sicheongyo), and educational institutions (colleges, middle schools, high schools, commercial schools). While titled "Personnel Directory," the provided text only lists the organizations themselves, suggesting the actual personnel details followed. For historians, this list provides a valuable snapshot of the institutional landscape of colonial Korea in 1940, revealing the presence and activities of significant Korean-led enterprises, cultural bodies, religious groups, and educational establishments operating under Japanese rule. It highlights areas of continued Korean social organization and economic activity despite the colonial context.
