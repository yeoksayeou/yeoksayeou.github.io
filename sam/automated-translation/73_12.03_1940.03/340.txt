"The best savings are war bonds."

"Nock the 9th arrow. Subscribe to the war bonds."

"Jewelry and Gold Purchase Week is from the 16th to the 23rd."

"Germany's future is in the hands of our children. Gather funds for the German Infant and Toddler Protection Fund."

"Provide domestic rabbit fur! Fur merchants and breeding associations are essential for the army."

"Our losses! 20% of production areas - 10% of the population - one-third of coal production - one-quarter of the total production of flour and potatoes - four-fifths of iron ore - all colonies and merchant ships. Announced the day after the signing of the peace treaty."

"The homeland is in danger. <295>
Defending the East requires a large amount of funds. Let us save it immediately; speed is required. In Russia, destructive radicals are extending their influence, and its repercussions threaten our eastern border. All savings banks and branches of the Great Bank of Berlin accept donations under the slogan 'Aid for the East' at any time."
For recruiting reserve soldiers for the East to prevent extremism.

The people of Holland cry out!
"Paper money is useless; I only need to receive the goods you make. Coal, potash, ironware, machinery, etc., are needed. Otherwise, we cannot give you bread, butter, pork fat, other goods, or raw materials."
Therefore, German workers, manufacture goods. Announced on the 15th, 1919.

French Side Propaganda

It is said that the propaganda of the Allied side at the time was particularly skillful in France.
It is said that the special characteristic of French propaganda was that it did not cause turmoil among the people over the five years from the start of the war to peace.
Among the famous slogans were:
(a) "Let's try to win!"
(b) "Look at that!"
(c) "Now, we've won!"
(a) was used on the first New Year's Day after the war began,
(b) was used when Germany was repulsed during the 5th offensive,
(c) was the slogan used on the day the peace treaty was signed.
Ah! One cannot but highly praise the propaganda skill that guided the five years with these three slogans.

"Subscribe to French bonds for world freedom!"

"To hasten victory and so that we may meet again... please subscribe."

"Standing firm in the trenches!
Bathed in the dawn light, the soldier dreams of victory and thinks of his home. To show that they will return home victorious, subscribe to the 3rd National Defense Bond!"

"Don't forget to subscribe!... For victory, for the triumphant return."

"Two poisons! One is Germany, the other is tuberculosis. Defeat Germany! Tuberculosis is the same."

British Side Phrases

Britain is, needless to say, a democratic nation. Therefore, its propaganda is self-aware and conviction-based, <296> attempting to make the people voluntarily endure the great sacrifices demanded by the state out of a sense of responsibility for national unity and cooperation.
The rational and reasonable aspect is a characteristic of Britain.

Lord Kitchener said, "Men, materials, and money are needed right now"... If there are none who respond to the appeal to the people's duty, there is no choice but to execute it by compulsory order.

"Think sincerely and know that the reason you state (for avoiding volunteer service) is merely to excuse your own inaction."

"The key to unfolding the situation." Are you turning this? Munitions, men, gold—are you among these?

"Go forth! Towards victory, subscribe immediately."

American Side Phrase Collection

Touching the subtleties of the human heart and skillfully manipulating mass psychology is the specialty of American propaganda. Propaganda posters often feature images using women.

"To achieve victory, buy more and more bonds."

"If you don't want to go to war, come into the Navy."

"Will you fight, or will you buy Liberty Bonds?"

"25,000 trainee nurses are needed immediately."

"The United States Army needs you."

"'I wish I were born a man.' Then I would have already enlisted in the Navy. Gentlemen, be manly and go to the United States Navy recruiting office to apply."

"Wireless Technician!" Help the Navy capture German submarines.
1,000 wireless technicians and naval reservists are needed.

"To an extent not unbecoming of your status,"
Please buy the 3rd Liberty Bond.

"The world cries out, 'Give us food!'" Promote domestic agriculture.

Save for freedom.<297>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* compiles and analyzes World War I propaganda slogans from Germany, France, Britain, and the United States. It presents examples from each nation, commenting on the perceived national character reflected in their propaganda styles: German directness and emphasis on duty, French cleverness and long-term emotional guidance, British appeals to reason and responsibility within a democracy, and American skill in manipulating mass psychology, often using images of women. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it was published during the height of Japanese wartime mobilization (leading into the Pacific War). It implicitly provides models of national mobilization through propaganda, potentially reflecting or informing the contemporary situation in Korea under Japanese rule, where similar mass mobilization efforts were underway. The discussion of different national styles might have offered subtle points of comparison or critique relevant to the colonial context.

서광을 입으면서 병사는 승리를 꿈꾸고 자기 집을 생각한다. 그들이 승리하여 가지고 다시 집에 도라가게 됨을 보이기 위하여 제3 國防公債에 응모해라!」

「응모할 것을 잊지마라!... 승리를 위하여 凱旋하기 위하여.」

英國側의 文句

英國은 말할 것도 없이 민주정치인 國이다. 따라서 선전같은 것도 자각적 신념적인 <296> 것이어서 국민 일치 협력의 책임으로 하여 국가의 강요하는 대희생을 자발적으로 감수하려고 하고 있다.
사리의 當코 합리적인 점은 英國의 特長이다.

깃체나-卿 曰 「사람과 물자와 금전이 지금 곧 필요하다」...국민의 의무에 호소하여 응하는 자가 없는 경우엔 헐 수 없으나 강제적 명령으로써 실행할 따름이다.

「성실히 생각하여서 汝가 말하는 바(義勇兵 避忌)의 이유가 汝의 自行自止를 변호함에 불과함을 알어라.」

「시국을 전개케 할 錠.」 당신은 이것을 돌리고 있읍니까. 군수품, 사람, 금, 너는 이 중의 드러 있느냐.

「나가라! 승리를 향해 즉각 응모해라.」
