A Novel Divorce Request

Recently, an American new woman, "Mari Purangkka," filed for divorce in a New York court and won. As a result, the husband lost his wife and, on top of that, was ordered to pay fourteen *wŏn* per week in alimony (扶助料 - support money). The reasons for the divorce listed in the lawsuit are as follows:

March 7, 1932, morning: My husband slapped my left cheek hard.

April 8, 1932, night: My husband pinched my leg.

September 12, 1933, lunchtime: My husband threw a clock at me, and it hit my chest.

July 5, 1933: He hit me with an electric lamp glass, and also tore my skirt.

October 7, 1933, middle of the night: My husband lifted up the bed I was lying in.<94>

**Summary**

This short article reports on a divorce case in New York where an American woman, "Mari Purangkka," successfully sued for divorce, citing specific instances of physical abuse by her husband on dated occasions. The court granted the divorce and ordered the husband to pay weekly alimony. From a historical perspective, the article highlights the Korean interest in Western social phenomena like the "New Woman" and modern legal practices regarding divorce during the colonial period. Detailing specific, itemized grounds for divorce based on physical cruelty might have been novel to Korean readers and potentially influenced discussions about women's rights, marital abuse, and legal recourse within Korea at the time.

기발한 이혼요구
요사히 미국 신녀성 「마리부랑까」는 뉴-욕재판소에 하야 리혼소송을 제긔하여 익이엇다. 그래서 결국 그 남편은 녀편네 이러버리고 그 우에 일주일에 십사원씩의 부조로 (扶助料)를 주기로 되엇다. 소송장에 나타난 그 리혼 리유를 들면
1932年 3月 7日아츰 남편이 내 왼쪽편 빰 한개를 철석 갈기다.
1932年 4月 8日밤, 남편이 내 다리를 꼬집다.
1933年 9月 12日 점심때 남편이 내게 시게를 던저 그것이 내 젓가슴에 마전다.
1933年 7月 5日, 전긔등유리로 나를 박다, 또 치마를 찟다.
1933年 10月 7日, 밤중에 남편은 내가 눕은 침대를 번제노타.＜94＞
