Collection of Rumors
Starting this month, we have decided to put all sorts of rumors (風聞) circulating in the world onto this red paper column (紅色紙欄). And this red paper column will continue every month. Ch'osa <185>

Hara Takashi's Remark and Yŏ Unhyŏng's Attitude
Although it's an old story from 14 or 15 years ago, it's so strange that I'm writing it down as I heard it rumored. If, decades later, a second Yun Paengnam appears and compiles an unofficial history collection (野史全集), perhaps this might be specially recorded as someone's political secret history (政治秘史) at some time—
Setting aside idle talk, the time was the Kimi year (1919), the place was Tokyo.
The people were Yŏ Unhyŏng and Hara Takashi.

Yŏ Unhyŏng, who was in Shanghai, overcoming various objections from his Shanghai comrades, at the invitation of the then Minister of War, General Tanaka Giichi (who later became Prime Minister), and the Director-General of the Colonial Bureau, Koga Renzō, stroked his Kaiser Wilhelm mustache and went to Tokyo by ship directly from Nagasaki and then by train.
In Tokyo, with Chang Tŏksu, a young political figure exiled to some island off Cheju Island, leading the way, he invited the executives of Britain's Reuters News Agency and Tokyo's leading newspapers to the Imperial Hotel and gave a speech. At that time, the public buzzed that he gave a pro-Japanese speech for Chōsen XX, but the truth is said to be that he spoke about the principles of the "right to survive" (生存權).
Also, it's said that the speech was not initially given in English. What Yŏ Unhyŏng said in Korean was interpreted as is by Chang Tŏksu so that the people of Tokyo could understand. After the speech ended, when foreign reporters fired off questions freely, Mr. Yŏ is said to have answered directly in English.
A reporter from the Yomiuri Shimbun, who arrived late just then, saw the questions and answers happening in English and wrote a special feature in the Yomiuri Shimbun as if Mr. Yŏ had given the entire speech in English from the beginning, which is why everyone came to believe he gave an English speech.
However, this is not the strange new rumor (所聞). So, what is the new rumor?

Since I haven't yet verified the truth with Mr. Yŏ Unhyŏng, I cannot definitively state it as fact, but considering the atmosphere (雰圍氣) of the time, it seems likely such words were indeed spoken. Thus, the rumor collector writes exactly as heard.
One of the most important purposes of going to Tokyo was to meet the then Prime Minister.
Mr. Yŏ met Prime Minister Hara Takashi. At that time, Hara reportedly told Mr. Yŏ,
"We will grant autonomy (自治) to Chōsen, <186> so don't entertain futile, impossible thoughts. Go straight to Kyŏngsŏng on this path, meet with the 33 signatories, and discuss the autonomy movement. Please also strive to have the independence activists in Shanghai all join this autonomy movement."
So, what did Mr. Yŏ reply then? Judging by the fact that he rejected the suggestion to go to Seoul and meet the 33 signatories and went straight back to Shanghai, it's self-evident, but anyway, it seems that Prime Minister Hara did have a plan (腹案) to grant autonomy to Chōsen at that time, according to the reminiscences (懷舊談) of a certain political figure.

Since we're talking, let's add one more rumor (風說). It's said that when Mr. Yŏ was in Shanghai, during the day he would go out to -, and in the evening and at night, he would invariably go out as a sock seller. That is, when going to -, he would put on a fine Western suit, stroke his Kaiser Wilhelm mustache, and go out, but when the time came to return, he would always carry a basket containing socks, face powder, stationery, envelopes, handkerchiefs, etc., and visit the homes of foreigners in the French Concession and the International Settlement, selling even a single pair of socks or a pack of cigarettes. It's said he covered his personal living expenses with the small amount earned from this peddling (行商). Although he had many opportunities to handle large sums of money, he never touched any for personal use, living by selling socks.

The New President of Tongil Bank
Regarding the current situation of Tongil Bank, which is in disarray (粉料中), Mr. Min Taesik submitted his resignation at the last shareholders' meeting, stating he would take full responsibility and step down as President (頭取). Although it hasn't been accepted yet, Mr. Min Taesik's intention to resign is firm and seems difficult to reverse. Consequently, they are in a position where they must eventually bring in a successor President. However, the promotion of Managing Director Sŏng from the Hoseo faction is difficult to realize due to various delicate (떼리케-트한 - delicate) internal issues. There's also talk that a representative from the Min clan might come in, but although the Min family's deposits in Tongil Bank are substantial, that too seems unlikely to be easily realized due to internal and external relations, according to the current situation. <187>
However, amidst this difficulty in finding a successor President, according to information from a most reliable source within our company's secret intelligence network,
a major figure, beyond the imagination of the entire Chōsen public, appears poised to take office in one leap. This person in question has reportedly already acquired a portion of Tongil Bank's shares and is just waiting for the opportunity. It is rumored that behind this figure lies the understanding (諒解) of the President of the OO Bank.
So, who is this figure? He is said to be a certain young businessman in his 40s, soaring in the financial world of the new academic sphere (新學天地), whose personal wealth is rumored to be of a geometric progression figure.
In any case, it is a fact that the behind-the-scenes battle surrounding the successor President is gradually heating up.

Ch'oe Rin and the Former Minister of War's Q&A
Mr. Ch'oe Rin met many people when he went to Tokyo last time. Among them were Privy Council President Ichiki, former Prime Minister Count Kiyoura, and Count Makino, as well as several other senior statesmen (重臣). However, it seems he couldn't meet the elder statesman (元老) Prince Saionji. It also appears he returned without meeting Seiyūkai President Suzuki, Minseitō President Wakatsuki, or Kokumin Dōmei President Adachi. But according to rumors (所聞), he met Viscount Saitō, a former Prime Minister and former Governor-General of Chōsen. At that meeting, Mr. Ch'oe reportedly said,
"In the past, when you were Governor-General, I caused the - incident and was arrested by your subordinates and imprisoned, but today we meet again like this."
As he delivered this first greeting, Viscount Saitō burst into laughter (破顔大笑).
He also met former Minister of War Araki. At that meeting, Mr. Ch'oe reportedly said,
"In the twenty-odd years since annexation, it's true that Chōsen has seen great progress, with new roads and railways built where there were none, irrigation developed, barren mountains turned green, much grain produced from the land, and large three or four-story brick buildings erected in every city. However, what the Chōsen people desire now is not just such systems and material civilization...(remainder omitted in original)"
After listening attentively for a while, Mr. Araki opened the window, pointed to the garden splendidly laid out in front of his house, and said, <188>
"You are right. Look at that garden. A garden cannot be made solely of pine trees, nor can it be a complete garden just by planting flowers and grasses. Only when there are pine trees, bamboo, flowers, grasses, a pond, and rocks can it finally become a complete garden (完全한 동산). Similarly, within the Japanese nation (日本國家), only when various peoples live while exhibiting their respective characteristics (諸特色) can the entire nation develop completely. Chōsen people too must be able to live with the rights (權利) and duties (리무 - duties?) corresponding to Chōsen people. Only when all various peoples do so can the entire nation bear complete fruit (完全한 결실). I have listened well to your words. I too will study the Chōsen problem deeply (깁히 硏究하겟소이다)."
There were said to be many other questions and answers, but I will omit them here.

No Paengnin Carrying a Bag of Potatoes
When No Paengnin, who died in Shanghai, was struggling greatly, he cooked for himself (自炊) in the French Concession and often took the tram out to the International Settlement market to buy potatoes. One day, as he jumped off the tram, the bag of potatoes he was holding burst open, and the potatoes scattered, rolling on the asphalt street. No Paengnin felt so regretful (악갑든지) about those potatoes that he ran here and there, frantically picking them up one by one, two by two. Because of this, passing cars all stopped, even the tram stopped, and the traffic policeman kept blowing his whistle for obstructing traffic.
Thus, hundreds of onlookers gathered in no time.
Having collected all the potatoes, Mr. No laughed heartily (껄껄 웃고) at the crowd, hugged the bag again, transferred to another tram, and headed home – an episode illustrating his magnanimity (豪憺).

Pak Hŭngsik's Opportunity for Success
According to rumors (風說), the annual profit (一年間의 利益) of Sŏnil Chimul Trading Company (鮮一紙物株式會社), managed by Mr. Pak Hŭngsik, is ** <189> 403,000 won. For a company with a capital (資本金) of 250,000 won to earn over 400,000 won, more than its capital, in one year – indeed, paper business (조히 장사) is an amazing business.
However, it wasn't like this from the beginning, it is said. Listening to the story of how Mr. Pak amassed his fortune, one might call him lucky (好運兒) or say he seized the opportunity (機會) well. Ten years ago, the place he first focused on was the paper business. So, he went around paper companies in Osaka and Tokyo, observing the market conditions (時勢), and then went to see Mr. Kim Sŏngsu of the Tong-A Ilbo. "I will supply the newsprint rolls (新聞卷紙) used by the Tong-A Ilbo at a cheaper price than others, so please use mine," he requested a contract (契約).
Generally, no matter what, the place that uses the most paper (조히) is a newspaper company (新聞社). Even using 5 or 6 rolls a day, at 40 won per roll, they use 200-300 won worth daily. Moreover, about 10 years ago, newspaper publishers completely ignored paper prices. The price was whatever paper stores (紙店) like Chūgai or Maruzen charged; for a while, a roll of paper cost 100 won, eventually dropping to 70 or 60 won. Mr. Pak offered to charge several won less per roll and asked the Tong-A Ilbo for a contract.
At first, Mr. Kim Sŏngsu had no reason to believe him. Considering a month's usage worth 3,000-4,000 won, and a year's worth 40,000-50,000 won (at that time), if they signed a contract and Mr. Pak failed to deliver, the newspaper wouldn't be published that day. It was their first meeting, and moreover, Mr. Pak's creditworthiness (信用程度) was unknown.
However, after arranging some guarantee deposit (保証金), they finally signed a 6-month contract. He delivered the paper daily, but towards the end, the paper market price (時勢) in Tokyo and Osaka fluctuated, and the cost price (原價) rose above the contract price (契約價). Furthermore, Mr. Pak's warehouse inventory (在庫品) ran out.
Continuing delivery meant a huge loss (損害), but he couldn't break the newspaper contract. After much thought, he rushed to the Ogiwara Paper Store (荻原紙物店), bought paper paying 10 won more per roll, and fulfilled his delivery obligation to the Tong-A Ilbo. In other words, he fulfilled the contract while losing several tens of won daily.
After completing this contract period, he gradually signed contracts with the Chosun Ilbo, Chungang Ilbo, Keijō Nippō, Maeil Sinbo, Chōsen Shimbun, and over 20 local newspaper companies.
Then, instead of buying paper from Tokyo, he decided to import it from Switzerland (瑞西). Using a letter of credit (信用狀) from the Bank of Chōsen, he signed a contract with a Swiss paper company for 1.5 million won worth of paper in one go and imported it. Subsequently, he directly imported (直輸入) paper from America (米州) and Canada (加奈多), making a large profit (大利) from that, leading to today's success, it is said. Does fortune (運) exist for people? Or is it due to one's own competence? This question can be posed to today's Mr. Pak just the same. <190>

Rumor of Kim Chun'yŏn as Bureau Chief - Will OO Newspaper Company Recruit Him?
There is a widespread rumor (風說) that the OO Ilbo company will recruit Mr. Kim Chun'yŏn, who was recently released from prison (가즈나온), as its editor-in-chief (編輯局長). Perhaps the origin (出處) of this rumor lies in the past when Mr. Kim was at the OO Ilbo company. When the MB Party incident (MB黨事件) broke out and he was detained (被拘), the then company president (社長) X rushed to the Chongno Police Station, guaranteed his identity (身元), and requested continued investigation without detention (不拘束取調). At that time, he reportedly even offered to guarantee that
"If he is truly not involved in the incident and is only under suspicion, he will henceforth completely refrain from actual movements (實際運動) and only pursue an academic life (學究生活)."
However, later the truth of the incident was revealed, and Mr. Kim was sentenced to a heavy penalty (重刑) as one of the leaders (首領). Because of this past, President X highly values (앗김) Mr. Kim and intends to bring him back. Even if asked, the person himself wouldn't give a clear answer (明答), so for now, we just relay the rumor as it is. Furthermore, informed sources (消息通) explain in more detail: no matter how much President X wants him, could they really bring back someone just released from prison as editor-in-chief right away, wary even of the mother-in-law's glare (시어미 눈살을 꺼려서라도)? Perhaps they initially plan to invite him under the title of advisor (顧問) or guest member (客員), and then realize this secret plan (腹案) when a good opportunity arises over the long course of time (기나긴 歲月). Is it really so, or not? Heaven's secrets must not be revealed (天璣不可洩), but it sounds unbelievable, yet also believable; I don't know yet.

The 100 Million Won Unsan Gold Mine - For Sale, Or Not For Sale?
Everyone knows (알니라) that the gold mine (金鑛) producing the most gold in Chōsen is the Unsan Gold Mine in North P'yŏngan Province (平北 雲山金鑛). That gold mine yields several million won worth of gold annually. The facilities (設備) are incredibly extensive (굉쟝한지): rails (레-루) laid underground extend for tens of ri; there's a power plant (發電所) providing electricity (電燈) to all places thousands of feet inside the mine (坑內); furthermore, the mine has elevators (에레배-다), mine cart (밀車 - 토록코/torokko) facilities, and a powder magazine (火藥庫) for storing dynamite (다이나마잇트). The mine office (鑛山事務所) is also well-built with reinforced concrete (鐵筋콩그리-트). <191> Several famous engineers (技師)—high-salaried engineers paid thousands of won per month—have been invited from the United States (米國). Anyway, from start to finish, it is said to be complete (完全하다).
With talented engineers, all sorts of scientifically invested equipment, and still inexhaustible gold underground, it is truly, without doubt (틀님이 업다), Chōsen's number one gold mine.
However, this gold mine is managed by Americans (米國사람). The mining permit (採掘許可) was granted back in the days of the old Korean Empire (舊韓國政府). At that time, ignorant high officials (高官) of the government, while struggling because the national treasury (國庫) lacked money, didn't think of operating such a fine gold mine as a state enterprise (國營). Instead, they received tens of thousands of won from Americans and carelessly (덜넝) granted the mining rights (採掘權) for a hundred years, or perhaps several decades.
The pumpkin fell to the big-nosed American friends, and for the subsequent several decades, who knows how many millions or tens of millions of won in huge profits (大利) they have made sitting idly by.
Recently, however, as the mining fever (鑛業熱) swelled greatly (잔뜩 膨脹), the Nihon Kōgyō Kabushiki Kaisha (日本鑛業株式會社), primarily backed by the Mitsui (三井) and Mitsubishi (三稜) conglomerates (財閥), put forward a certain giant (某巨頭) from the Chōsen world as an intermediary and initiated a buyout movement (買收運動) for this Unsan Gold Mine. The American mine owner is willing to consider the buyout, but the price—don't be shocked (놀나지말아)—
was stated as one hundred million won (金一億萬圓也).
After several months of negotiation (交涉) over this outrageous asking price (呼價), recently, it is said they have come down to offering to sell for
30 million won (3千萬圓).
Still, the buying side considers it expensive (빗사다고), and the negotiation has not been concluded. If some friend from the streets (江湖의 친구) rolls up their sleeves, skillfully persuades both the American mine owner and the buyer side, the Nihon Kōgyō Kaisha, and manages to conclude the negotiation, the intermediary commission (中間口文) would truly be enormous (巨額).
Even in Seoul's real estate agencies (복덕방), when buying a house or finding a room, they customarily receive a commission (口文) of 2-3% (100分之2,3). Applying that ratio, even if the price settles at, say, 20 million won, one million won would materialize. Considering the shares received by Ch'oe Ch'anghak, Pang Ŭngmo, Kim Sangsŏl, Ko Ilch'ŏng, etc., who knows how many hundreds [of thousands?] of won might fall? According to rumors (들니는 풍설), they say 3 million won will be given if the sale is concluded. That too sounds truly plausible (참말일상 십다). <192>

**Summary**

This article from the September 1934 issue of Samch'ŏlli compiles various rumors circulating at the time. These include: Yŏ Unhyŏng's alleged 1919 meeting in Tokyo where Prime Minister Hara Takashi supposedly offered autonomy to Korea; the unclear succession plan for the presidency of Tongil Bank; Ch'oe Rin's conversations with Japanese officials like Saitō Makoto and Araki Sadao regarding Korea's situation and desires beyond material progress; a poignant anecdote about independence activist No Paengnin's poverty in Shanghai; the story behind Pak Hŭngsik's remarkable success in the paper import business through bold contracts and seizing opportunities; speculation about the recently released Kim Chun'yŏn potentially rejoining a newspaper; and the ongoing, high-stakes negotiation for the sale of the American-owned Unsan Gold Mine to Japanese conglomerates, with a rumored asking price dropping from 100 million to 30 million won. The article touches upon significant political interactions between Korean figures and Japanese authorities, the dynamics of colonial-era business and finance, the personal struggles of independence activists, and the immense wealth generated by Korean resources like the Unsan mine. Historians might find the detailed accounts of rumored political discussions (like Hara's alleged autonomy offer or Araki's analogy), the insights into business practices (Pak Hŭngsik's paper trade), and the specifics of the Unsan mine negotiations particularly interesting for understanding the complex political, economic, and social landscape of colonial Korea in the mid-1930s.

風聞集
이달부터 세상에 흘러다니는 온갓 風聞을 이 紅色紙欄에 담기로 하엿슴니다. 그리고 이紅色紙欄은 每月 繼續하옴니다. 草士＜185＞

原敬의一言과 呂運亨態度
별서 14,5年前 지나간 녯날 이약이지만은 이약이가 하도 신긔하기에 들니는 風說대로 적는다. 이것이 몃십년后 第二尹白南氏 나와서 野史全集을 꾸밀 機會 잇다면 아모때 아모개의 政治秘史라하야 特書記入될지도 모르니까-
잡담 제하고 때는 긔미 당시 곳은 東京.
사람은 呂運亨과 原敬 .

上海잇든 呂運亨이 그 當時의 陸軍大臣 田中義一大將(뒷날 總理大臣까지 한 사람)과 拓殖局長官 古賀廉造의 招請으로 上海同志의 여러가지 反對를 물니치고 攸攸 카이젤수염을 쓰다듬으며 長崎直航의 배를 타고 船車로 東京에 갓것다.
東京서는 濟州島 어느섬엔가 流配되어 잇는 靑年政客 張德秀를 압세우고 帝國호텔에서 英國 로이텔通信社 이하 東京 各 一流新聞의 記者幹部을 招請하여 노코 一場演說을 하엿는데 世間에서는 그때 朝鮮XX의 X日演說을 하엿다고 떠들엇스나 實相은 「生存權」에 對하야 原理原則을 말하엿다 한다.
그리고 演說도 처음부터 英語로 한것이 아니엇다 한다. 呂運亨氏가 朝鮮말로 한것을 東京사람들이 아라듯게 張德秀氏가 그대로 通譯하엿고 그 演說이 끗난뒤 自由質問이 外人記者로부터 連發될 때에 呂氏가 直接 英語로 答辯하엿다고 한다.
그것을 때마츰 讀賣新聞 記者가 늣게 왓다가 英語로 受問受答하는 것을 보고 ²˜음부터 呂氏英語演說한 줄로 讀賣新聞에 特書大筆하엿기 까닭에 모다 英語演說하엿거니 알게 되엿다 한다.
그런데 이것이 신기한 새所聞되잘 것은 업다. 그러면 새所聞이란 무엇인고?

아직 呂運亨氏에게서 眞相을 따저보지 못하엿스니 꼭 事實이라고 確言할수업스나 當時의 雰圍氣로 보아 그만한 말씀은 應當 잇섯슬 듯 하기에 風聞子 드른 대로 正聞正筆하는 바이다.
東京간 目的中 가장 重要한 하나인 當時의 總理大臣과 맛나보든 일.
呂氏는 곳 首相原敬氏를 맛낫다. 그때 原은 呂氏더러
『朝鮮에다가 自治를 줄 터이니＜186＞ 공연히 안될 딴생각 말고 이길로 곳 京城에 나가서 33人들과 맛나 自治運動의 相議를 하시오. 上海잇는 獨立運動者도 모다 이自治運動에 合流하도록 努力하여 주시오』
하엿더라 한다. 그러면 그때 呂氏는 무슨말로 對答하엿든가고? 그가 서울 가서 33人 맛나 보라는것을 물니치고 一路上海로 간것으로 보아 자명하거니와 엇잿든 그때는 原首相의 意思는 朝鮮에 自治을 許與할 腹案은 잇섯든 듯 하다고 某政客의 懷舊談.

말하든까리니 風說 한가지만 더 添付하자. 上海에 잇슬 때의 呂氏는 낫에는 -에 出動하고 저녁과 밤에는 반드시 양말장사로 나섯다고 한다. 즉  -에 갈 때에는 조흔 세비로 잡붓고 카이젤 수염을 쓰다듬으며 出動하지만 時間이 되야 도라나오면 반드시 양말, 치분 편지지 봉투 수지 등을 너흔 빠스켓을 들고 佛蘭西租界와 共同租界의 外國사람 집을 차저 양말 한개도 팔고 담배 한갑도 팔어 私生活費는 그 行商으로 남는 少額으로 지내엇다 한다. 巨額의 돈을 제 손으로 움지길 기회 여러 번 잇섯스면서도 私用에는 一切 손을 안대이고 양말행상하여 산 氏엇다 한다.

東一銀行의 新頭取
粉料中에 잇는 東一銀行의 現狀으로 말하면 閔大植氏는 지난번 株主總會 때에 一切의 責任을 지고 頭取을 辭免하겟다고 辭表를 提出하엿스나 아직 受理는 되지 안코 잇는 中인데 閔大植氏의 辭意는 確固하야 到底히 飜意하기 어려움으로 結局 後任頭取를 마저 드려야할 處地에 잇스나 그러타고 湖西系의 成專務의 昇任은 行內의 諸般 떼리케-트한 問題로 實現되기 어럽고 閔氏一族으로부터 어느 代表者가 드러온다는 說도 잇스나 閔氏系의 預金이 東一銀行에 만히 잇기는 하지만 對內對外 關係로 그도 容易히 實現될 것 갓지 안는 現狀에 處하여 잇다 한다.＜187＞
그런데 이러케 后任頭取難에 陷하고 잇는 此際에 本社機密機關의 가장 信用할만한 系統으로부터의 消息에 의하면
全鮮世人의 想像以外의 一大人物이 一躍就任할 氣勢가 보인다는데 그 疑問의 人物은 벌서 東一銀의 株의 幾部分을 買收하여 入手하고 機會만 보고 잇다는 바 그 人物의 背景에는 ㅇㅇ銀行總裁의 諒解가 잇다는 說이 잇다.
그러면 그 人物이란 誰某인가. 新學天地의 財界에서 雄飛하는 40代의 壯年實業家 某氏로 그의 私財도 幾何級數的 數字를 가지고 잇다고 傳한다.
아무튼 后任頭取를 싸고 도는 內幕戰은 漸漸 白熱化하여 가고 잇는 것만은 事實이다.

崔麟氏와 前陸相問答
崔麟氏가 지난번에 東京 가서 맛나고 온 사람이 만흔데 그 中에는 樞密院議長 一木氏도 잇고 前首相淸浦伯도 잇고 牧野伯도 잇고 그박게 여러 重臣들도 잇섯다 한다. 그런데 元老西園寺公僧은 맛나지 못하고 온 모양이요. 政友會의 鈴木總裁도 民政黨의 若槻總裁도 國民同盟의 安達總裁도 모다 맛나지 안코 도라온 모양이라 한다. 그런데 들니는 所聞에 의하면 前首相이자 前朝鮮總督이든 齊藤子爵을 맛낫는데 그 席上에서 崔氏가
「나는 녯날 당신이 總督으로 나와 잇슬 때에 -事件을 이르켜 당신 部下손에 붓잡히어 감옥에 가 잇섯지만 오늘은 다시 이런 몸으로 서로 맛나게 되엇소이다」
하고 人事의 첫마듸를 건즉 齊藤子爵은 破顔大笑하더라고.
또 荒木前陸軍大臣을 맛낫는데 그 席上에서 崔氏가
「합병한 뒤 이십여년 사이에 朝鮮은 그야말로 업든 新作路와 鐵道가 생기고 水利가 開墾되고 마른山이 靑山이 되고 땅에서 穀食이 만히 나고 都市마다 3,4層 큰 벽돌집이 만히 서서 크게 進步한 것만은 事實이지만은 그러나 朝鮮사람이 지금 바라는 것은 그러한 制度와 物質文明뿐이 아니라(하략-원문)」
하엿더니 荒木氏는 귀를 기우려 한참이나 듯고 난 뒤 창문을 열고 자긔집뜰 압헤 버젓하게 차려논 동산을 가르키며＜188＞
「당신 말이 올소이다. 저 동산을 보시요. 저 동산에 소나무만 잇서도 동산이 될 수 업고 花草만 심어서도 완전한 동산이 될 수 업지요. 소나무도 참대나무도 花草도 연못도 바위도 다 잇서야 비로소 完全한 동산이 되드시 日本國家안에는 여러 民族이 다 諸特色을 發揮하면서 살아야 全國家가 完全히 發達될 것이올시다. 朝鮮사람도 朝鮮사람에 該當하는 權利와 리무를 가지고서 살게 되어야 하지요. 여러 民族이 다 그리하여야 全國家가 完全한 결실을 맷지요. 당신 말슴 잘 들엇소이다. 나도 朝鮮問題를 깁히 硏究하겟소이다」
하더라고 그박게 問答하엿다는 말이 만헛스나 여기서는 略하여 버리노라.

감자봉지 든 盧泊麟
上海에서 죽은 盧伯麟氏가 한참 困窮할 적에 佛蘭西租界에서 自炊하는데 늘 電車를 타고 共同租界市場에까지 감자 사려 나가것다. 하로는 電車에서 뛰어나리다가 가슴에 안은 감자봉지가 탁 터지는 바람에 감자가 애스팔트우 거리에 데글데글 굴너 흐터젓다. 盧伯麟氏가 엇더케도 그 감자가 악갑든지 이리 뛰고 저리 뛰며 그 감자 한 개씩 투 개씩 모다 주서 모으느라고 精神이 업섯다. 그 때문에 가고 오든 자동차가 모다 서고 電車까지 서고 交通巡査는 交通妨害한다고 연해 호각을 불고.
이리하야 구경꾼까지 數百名이 삽시간에 몰려왓다.
감자를 다 주은 盧氏는 群衆들을 向하야 껄껄 웃고 다시 봉지를 안고 다른 電車에 밧구어 타고 제집으로 向하엿다고 그 豪憺을 말하는 한 에피소-트.

朴興植氏의 成功의 機會
風說에 들니는 말에는 朴興植氏가 經營하는 鮮一紙物株式會社의 **＜189＞ 一年間의 利益이 40萬3千圓이라 한다. 資本金 25萬圓會社에서 一年동안에 資金보다도 더 만흔 40餘萬圓을 버으럿다 하니 과연 조히 장사란 놀나운 장사다.
그러나 처음부터 이러케 數가튼 것은 아니라 한다. 朴氏가 돈 모으게 된 來歷을 들으면 好運兒라면 好運兒고 機會 잘 탓다면 機會 잘 탓다고도 할 것이다. 10年前에 처음 着目한 곳이 조히 장사라 그래서 大阪東京의 紙物會社를 도라다니며 時勢을 살피고 나온 뒤 東亞日報의 金性洙氏를 차저 갓다. 「東亞日報에서 쓰는 新聞卷紙를 내가 남보다 더 싼갑으로 대어 드릴터이니 내 것을 써주시오 하고 契約을 請하엿다.
