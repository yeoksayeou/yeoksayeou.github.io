갈바람은 산들산들
						岸曙
갈바람은 산들산들
갈바람은 산들산들 넓은벌 휘돕니다,
이하로도 해는넘어 西山에 붉은노을,
눌한닙이 나풀나풀 東山에 쌔입니다,
지낸세월 꿈이라니 이대로 떠나릿가.

갈바람은 산들산들 바다를 쓰칩니다,
오가든배 어데가고 물결만 찰랑찰랑,
떠날몸이 설길래로 갈매기 워칩니다,
모든것을 꿈이라고 우리도 떠나릿가.

갈바람은 산들산들 구슬비 휘돕니다,
山을넘어 들을돌아 바다를 건넙니다,
갈바람은 산들산들 모도다 꿈이외다.
이져녁에 가는님아 사랑도 꿈이든가.

집을떠나 나도는 님의것일가.

가을철도 인제는 늣즌탓이리
우수수 부는바람 몸에숨이네
도리독닥 내옷은 뉘가지을고
도드락 도드락은 이내뜬시름.

불다가 부든대로 슬어지길래

Autumn Wind Blows Gently
						An Sŏ
Autumn wind blows gently,
Autumn wind blows gently, swirling round the wide field,
Already the sun sets beyond the West Mountain (西山) with a red glow,
Yellowed leaves flutter, piling up on the East Mountain (東山),
Past times are but a dream, shall I leave just like this?

Autumn wind blows gently, brushing the sea,
Where have the coming and going ships gone? Only waves lap, lap,
As if saddened by the departing self, the seagulls cry out,
Shall we too leave, calling everything a dream?

Autumn wind blows gently, swirling the misty rain,
It crosses mountains, circles fields, crosses the sea,
Autumn wind blows gently, everything is a dream.
Oh, my love departing this evening, was love also a dream?

Fulling Block
Startled awake by the todŭrak todŭrak sound,<251>
Was it from sleeping curled up? A shiver runs through me,
The lampwick flickers dimly by itself,
Tori-tok-tak, this neighbor is fulling clothes.

Opening the window absently, a white moon in the sky,
It's the eighteenth day, night has fallen again,
Tori-tok-tak, whose clothes is she fulling so?
Could they be for her beloved, wandering away from home?

Perhaps autumn is now late,
The rustling wind blows, seeping into the body,
Tori-tok-tak, who will make my clothes?
Todŭrak todŭrak is my floating sorrow.

The Sailor's Wife
My longed-for beloved, why do you not come?
Every day I wait at the port (浦口),
On the sea, only white water, endlessly vast,
There's no way to know news (消息) of my beloved.

A white sail flickers far out on the rough sea,
Hoping it's the ship my beloved boarded,<252>
It passes distantly beyond the sea's edge,
My beloved's ship is nowhere to be seen.

When I call for my love with this cruel heart (心思),
Seagulls circle the sky, crying alone,
At the shore (가슭), the chap-sak chap-sak sound of waves,
My longed-for beloved gives no answer (對答).

Sighing Willow
Willow drooping, drooping, its trailing branches,
The swirling spring wind is a wind of love,
My body is the willow branch, you are the wind,
I go wherever the wind blows.

Willow swaying, swaying, the blowing wind,
Does it wander endlessly, having a set direction (定向)?
My body is the willow branch, you are the wind,
I go wherever the wind blows.

Willow drooping, drooping, its trailing branches,
The swirling spring wind has a fickle heart (變키쉬운맘),
Blowing as it blew, then fading away,
The willow, its heart (心思) sad, bowed its head.<253>
<251-253>

**Summary**

This collection features four lyrical poems by An Sŏ (岸曙) published in 1934. The poems explore themes of nature's passage (autumn wind, spring wind), transience, longing, and separation. "Autumn Wind Blows Gently" reflects on the ephemeral nature of time and love. "Fulling Block" depicts a lonely night filled with the sound of ironing, evoking thoughts of a distant loved one. "The Sailor's Wife" portrays the sorrowful waiting of a woman whose husband is at sea. "Sighing Willow" uses the metaphor of a willow swayed by a fickle spring wind to represent a love that fades. For historians, these poems offer a glimpse into personal sentiments and common literary motifs during the colonial period. While not overtly political, they convey a pervasive sense of melancholy, longing, and perhaps instability, reflecting the emotional landscape of the time through traditional Korean imagery (like the 'dadŭmi' fulling block) and nature metaphors, using the Korean language orthography common in the 1930s.
