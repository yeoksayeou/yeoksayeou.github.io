May Morning
						Haeng Sŏk-chŏng

May Morning
As the rain clears, the barley color is especially green
The early morning pheasant calls sound ever more serene.

Dewdrops bead upon the simple spider web
Sunlight falls on new leaves, making the green May shine more brightly.

Does the sky turn blue reflecting this green earth?
Thinking it endless, the sky becomes even higher.

A pair of swallows in the sky seem incredibly close!
This morning, my heart flutters like a baby bird!

Meditation
The night sky is faraway and quiet
The distant starlight is even quieter.<254>

When will I ever forget myself
And become that quiet sky and star?

Night

On this deep night, the wind goes far away, not knowing how to rest.

From the distant pond water, the sound of ducks is faintly heard,
From whose house does a young lamb cry so piteously this night?

My love— night is when the sky is a secret meeting place for the stars,
Shall we not sit by that quiet lakeside (湖畔) and eavesdrop
on their whispering (속세임)?
1934, May 14th Afternoon<255>

**Summary**

This document presents three short poems by Haeng Sŏk-chŏng: "May Morning," "Meditation," and "Night." The poems use vivid natural imagery – a spring morning after rain, the quiet night sky, a windy night with sounds of ducks and a lamb – to evoke feelings of peace, wonder, longing for transcendence, and romantic intimacy. The speaker observes nature closely, connecting personal emotions to the scenes observed, such as a fluttering heart like a baby bird or a desire to merge with the quiet cosmos. Historians might find the poems interesting as examples of nature poetry during the Japanese colonial period, a genre often used for personal expression or subtle escapism. The specific language and orthography (e.g., '한울', '보리ㅅ빗', '여서') are valuable artifacts of Korean language usage in the 1930s.

五月아침
						幸夕汀
五月아침
비 거드니 보리ㅅ빗 유난히 푸르고
이른 아침 꿩소리 더욱 閑雅허이

簡素한 거믜줄에 이슬비 구슬피고
새닢새에 해ㅅ볏드니 푸른 五月 더 빗낫다

이 푸른 땅이 비처 한울이 푸르던가
끝이 없다 생각하니 한울 더욱 높아지네

제비 한쌍 한울에 무척 갓든하고나
이 아침에 내마음은 새색기처럼 파다거리여!

冥想
밤한울 머얼리 고요한데
아드-ㄱ한 별빗 더욱 고요하네＜254＞

언제나 으히 나를 잊어바리고
저 고요한 한울과 별이 되오리

밤

이 깊은 밤에 바람은 쉬여갈 줄도 몰으고 멀리 가네

머언 못물에서 오리소리 가안간히 들려오고
누 집에서 어린羊이 간얇피 이밤을 저러케 우는고?

님이여- 밤은 한울을 密會場으로 별들이 만나는 떼이어니
그들의 속세임을 우리도 저-고요한 湖畔에 여서
이약이 하며 엿듯지 않으려는가?
1934, 5月 14日 午後＜255＞
＜254-255＞
