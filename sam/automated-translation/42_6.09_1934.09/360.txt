Comings and Goings in the Literary World
						Ch'obyŏngjŏng

Dissolve KAPF (갑프). Have you forgotten that the duty of the likes of you (鄕等) has already been completed? Embracing an empty vessel without substance and blustering in the style of "Hey you horn-sprouting literati!" (이놈들 뿔조아文士야) is now nothing more than a source of bitter laughter.

Dissolve the Theater Arts Association (劇藝術協會) too. Has not the opportunity come to liquidate recent activities that are like an extension of student theater, detached from the masses?

Dissolve the Group of Nine (九人會) too. Above all, we wish to avoid it in the sense that it cultivates factionalism in the literary world. All separate organizations should follow suit entirely; the Ballad Association (歌謠協會) too, and all others likewise, should be dissolved.

After that, let all writers come together and form one big mass. Let's establish a unitary and absolute large literary organization to initiate a comprehensive literary movement for Chosŏn. Under the great banner of national literature—

Following Pak Hwasŏng's year-plus rest, Kang Kyŏng'ae's sudden appearance in the women's literary scene is a very welcome event. However, the title "Human Problems" (人間問題) is mundane, and again, mundane. This might be scientific, but it's an unartistic title; it might be philosophical, but it's an unemotional (*情的) title; it might be masculine, but it's an unwomanly title. Art is the product of emotion (情緖), the product of tears, laughter, and sighs. Things that are tough, stiff—generally far from laughter and tears, whether in the title <224> or the content, we do not accept. The fair lady of the deep chamber (深窓의 佳人) should reconsider this.

Not a single proletarian writer (푸로文人) attended Sŏhae's memorial stone unveiling ceremony (立牌式). At Ubo's farewell ceremony (永訣式), only a few literary figures participated. What lamentable customs are these among writers who should be the most affectionate and friendly?

Among those itching to write long novels before their test are Pak Yŏnghŭi, Yi Hyosŏk, and Yu Chino. We urge newspaper companies to have the magnanimity to give these three an opportunity. Discovering new talent is good, but today's newspaper pages have too many newcomers. Start with those who have weight, those who have capability.

We wish to establish friendly relations with figures from the Chinese literary world like Zhou Zuoren (周作人), Lu Xun (魯迅), Guo Moruo (郭沫若), and Tian Han (田漢). Will they not visit? We await them with craned necks (鶴首苦待).

Kim Kirim's poetry, from sensation to sensation, plunges alone into profundity. Yi Sang, likewise, leads readers into places unknown like a deep forest (深林). Write simply. Isn't the "Poet's Way" (詩人之道) to write simply, so it can be easily understood and easily recited?

I thought it had been 17 or 18 years since Ch'unwŏn wrote "Heartlessness" (無情), but according to his recent words, it was serialized in the newspaper starting from January of Taishō 5 (1916), making it exactly 20 years this year. For now, I correct the number of years thus.

The Chosŏn Ilbo announced the serial publication of short stories by 15 writers, a very delightful and grand undertaking (壯擧). One regrettable point is the omission of Messrs. Yi Kwangsu, Yŏm Sangsŏp, Kim Tongin, and Hyŏn P'ingho. <225>

**Summary**

The author, writing under the pseudonym "Ch'obyŏngjŏng," calls for the dissolution of existing literary factions like KAPF, the Theater Arts Association, and the Group of Nine, advocating for a unified national literary organization. The piece comments on recent literary events, praising Kang Kyŏng'ae's return but critiquing her title choice, lamenting the lack of solidarity among writers shown at recent memorials, urging newspapers to publish established authors like Pak Yŏnghŭi, Yi Hyosŏk, and Yu Chino, expressing a desire for exchange with Chinese writers, criticizing the obscurity of poets Kim Kirim and Yi Sang, correcting the publication date of Yi Kwangsu's "Heartlessness," and applauding the Chosŏn Ilbo's short story series while noting key omissions. From a historical perspective, the article offers a valuable snapshot of the Korean literary scene in 1934, revealing intense debates about factionalism versus unity, the struggle between proletarian and nationalist literary ideals, the influence of Chinese intellectuals, and the ongoing development of modern Korean literature under colonial rule. The call for dissolving groups like KAPF reflects the political pressures and internal shifts within the literary world during this period.

文壇歸去來
						草兵丁
갑프 解散하라. 鄕等의 任은 이미 完了됨을 이젓는가. 無實한 虛器를 擁하고 「이놈들 뿔조아文士야」式의 洞喝은 이제는 苦笑거리 以外의 아모것도 아니다.

劇藝術協會 亦 解散하라. 大衆과 流離된 學生劇의 廷長가튼 近業, 淸算의 機會오지 안엇는가.

九人會 亦 解散하라. 무엇보다 文壇에 黨派性을 培養한다는 意味에서 吾人은 避하고 십다. 分立된 諸團體는 一切로, 歌謠協會 亦 餘皆倣此 解散할 일.

그런뒤 모-든文人이 한데 뭉처 큰덩어리 이루자. 朝鮮의 全面的 文藝運動을 이르키기 爲하야 一元的의 또 絶對的의 큰 文學團體를 結成하자. 民族文學의 大旗幟下에-

朴花城 休息年餘의 女流文壇에 姜慶愛 突現 甚히 快事, 다만 標題의 「人間問題」平凡에 又 平凡, 이는 科學的일지 모르나 非文藝的 題요. 哲學的인지 모르나 非*情的 題요. 男性的인지 모르나 非女人的 題다. 藝術은 情緖의 産物, 눈물과 웃음과 咏嘆의 産物, 억세고 뻑뻑하고-대개 웃음과 눈물과 距離가 먼 것은 標題든,＜224＞ 內容이든 吾人不取, 深窓의 佳人 一考할 일.

曙海 立牌式에 푸로文人 一人도 不叅, 牛步 永訣式에 文壇人 叅加는 不過 數人, 가장 多情하고 友愛하여야 할 文人사이에 이 무슨 慨歎할 風인고.

長篇 쓰이고 십흔 試驗前의 人에 朴英熙, 李考石, 兪鎭午 잇다. 新聞社는 이 세 분에 機會 줄 雅量가지기를 勸한다. 新人發掘도 조치만 今日의 諸紙面에는 너무 新人이 만타. 무게잇는 이로 力量잇는 이로부터.

周作人, 魯迅, 郭沫若, 田漢 等 中國文壇의 諸人들과 交歡맷고 십다. 來遊하지 안으려는가. 吾人鶴首苦待.

金起林의 時 感覺에서 感覺으로 혼자 深奧한데 突入, 李箱 亦, 深林속가치 同路 모를 곳으로 讀者를 引入, 쉽게 쓰소. 쉽게 알고 쉽게 읇프게 쉽게 씀이 「詩人之道」아닐가.

春園 「無情」 쓴지 17,8年된 줄 알엇더니 다시 氏의 말에 의하면 大正5年 正月부터 新聞에 連載하엿다니 今年이 꼭 20年, 爲先 年數를 이러케 訂正하여 둔다.

朝鮮日報 作家 15氏의 短篇連載를 發表, 甚히 愉快한 壯擧. 한가지 섭섭한 일은 李光洙, 廉想涉, 金東仁, 玄憑虛의 諸氏 빠진 点.＜225＞
