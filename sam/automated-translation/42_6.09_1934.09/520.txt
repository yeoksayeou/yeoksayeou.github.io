Gratitude and Apology

This world is about money. Where there is no money, there are no heroes, no righteous people, no businesses, nothing at all. It is the way of our world today that countless talented people fall because they lack money, while insignificant, worthless people swagger around boasting, all due to the power of gold. Crying because of money, laughing because of money, being employed because of money, fighting because of money, dying because of money – that is this world, indeed.

But look here, besides money, we must realize that there is "sincerity" (정성). If there is sincerity, single-minded devotion (일편단심), a single heart (一心), then perhaps it can accomplish two or three things when money accomplishes ten. When money moves a hundred people, sincerity might move about ten people. Good heavens, what foolish thing have I said – no, no, sincerity does what money cannot. Even if money has moved ten thousand people, it is temporary; even if money has accomplished ten kinds of projects, who knows what will become of those projects the day the money runs out? However, a project built with sincerity will never collapse even after hundreds of millions of years, and comrades bound by sincerity will share life and death together until they die.

When the whole world cries "Money, money!" I will put forth sincerity. When people from east, west, south, north, men and women, young and old, are dazzled by the glitter of money, I will solely hang out the drawing* and meat/fish representing my single-minded devotion. "Samch'ŏlli" (삼천리) is the product of such earnest sincerity. Please recognize at least this much. I too have breathtakingly received the suffering, the humiliation, the persecution that comes from having no money, but my conviction (신렴) is that I want this one magazine to bloom like a single lotus flower (련꼿) in a murky corner of the world, a clean flower made of sincerity.

Rather than expressing sorrow that we do not have many universities, we intend for even this small magazine to fulfill the role of a university; we await your hoping that you will remember the sincerity that grows month by month. <300>

**Summary**

This editorial piece contrasts the overwhelming power of money in the world with the enduring value of sincerity (정성). The author laments how money dictates success and failure but argues that sincerity can achieve things money cannot, creating lasting works and loyal bonds. The magazine "Samch'ŏlli" itself is presented as a product born purely of this sincerity, despite the hardships faced due to lack of funds. The author asks readers to recognize this effort, comparing the magazine to a lotus flower blooming in murky waters and hoping it can serve a role akin to a university. From a historical perspective, the text reveals the economic hardships faced by Korean intellectuals and cultural producers under colonial rule. The emphasis on "sincerity" over money could be interpreted as a form of moral resistance or a coping mechanism in a situation where financial success, especially for nationalist-leaning ventures, was difficult. The desire for the magazine to act as a substitute for universities highlights the perceived lack of higher educational opportunities for Koreans and the ambition of the press to fill that void.

感謝와 謝罪
이 세상은 돈이외다, 돈 업는 곳에 영웅도 의사도 사업도 아모 것도 업슴니다, 허다한 인재가 돈 업기 까닭이 타락하고, 말고 보잘 것 업는 사람이 황금의 위력 때문에 내로라하고 고개질하는 것이 오늘날 우리 세상 일이외다, 돈 때문에 울고 돈 때문에 웃고 돈 때문에 부리우고 돈 때문에 싸우고 돈 때문에 죽는 것이 이 세상,일이외다 그려.
그러나 여보세요, 돈 이외에 우리는 「정성」이 잇슴을 깨달어야 하겟슴니다, 정성이, 일편단심이, 一心이 잇스면 돈이 열 가지 일할 적에 두 세가지는 할 수 잇겟지. 돈이 백 사람 움지길 적에 정성이 열 사람쯤은 움지길 수 잇겟지요, 앗불사 내가 무슨 철 업는 말을 하엿나- 아니  아니 돈이 못하는 일을 정성은 함니다. 돈이 만 사람을 움지겻다 하여도 그는 일시지요, 돈이 열가지 사업을 하엿다 하더래도 그 돈이 떠러지는 날 그 사업은 엇이될는지 모르지요, 그러나 정성으로 된 사업은 억만년을 가도 문허지는 법 업고 정성으로 된 동지는 죽을 때까지 생사를 가치하는 법이외다.
세상이 모다 돈돈 할적에 나는 정성을 내세우겟서요, 동서남북서 남녀노소가 돈의 광채에 현혹할 적에 나는 오직 내 일편단심을 그린*와 고기를 내어걸겟서요, 「삼천리」는 이러한 이미의 정성의 산물이외다. 그 줄이나 알어주시오. 나도 돈 업는 데서 오는 고통 돈 업는 데서 오는 수모, 돈 업는 데서 오는 핍박을 긔막키게 밧여옴니다만은 나의 신렴은 흐린 세상 한모통이에 한 떨기의 련꼿이 피듯, 이 잡지 하나쯤은 정성으로 된 깨긋한 꼿이 되어 피이고 십다고 생각함니다.
우리는 대학이 만치 못한 설음을 발한 때가 아니라, 이 조고마한 잡지나마 대학의 소임을 하려, 다달이 커가는 그 정성을 잇지 말어주기를 바라주심을 기다리나이다.＜300＞
