三千里文藝-文藝雜感
						春園 (Ch'unwŏn)
Towards a Higher Love

I believe that people are not equal. Although there are countless differences in intelligence, willpower, and wealth, I especially believe this to be true regarding the power to know and feel "what is right" and "what is beautiful." And I do not grieve over this. Rather, this very distinctiveness among people promises infinite improvement and evolution, allowing us to believe that even a worm striving for betterment can become a Buddha. Therefore, even a dirty and foolish sentient being like myself can walk this arduous path of life with the joyful hope of desiring the perfection of Buddha.

I see that outstanding individuals sometimes emerge from among us sentient beings. These are great figures like Shakyamuni Tathagata, various Bodhisattvas, or Jesus. When I learn that they too were originally sentient beings like me, when I am taught, "You too can become like me," I feel boundless gratitude and joy. I believe it is possible to become a "person" possessing the most beautiful body, the most beautiful voice, the highest wisdom, and limitless love, power, and merit, receiving the adoration of all sentient beings and becoming a source of joy, strength, and salvation for them. I believe one can become the loving will for sentient beings through the eternal life of the Great Vow (대흥서원). People, could there be any greater hope than this?<214>

I believe that all this improvement and evolution occurs solely through the karma (업) we create. The gracious God created this universe to be governed by the law of cause and effect (인과률). Even the smallest "deed" (karma) done by us worm-like sentient beings is recorded in my deposit account without fail. These savings accumulate and determine the me of tomorrow, the me of my next life, the me after thousands of kalpas. This truly is God's great grace. If, in this world, a fertilized rice paddy could yield less than an unfertilized one, how difficult would our lives be? If eating food could sometimes leave you hungry, or if lighting a fire could make a room colder, how could we live? That a cause inevitably brings an effect—what gracious providence from the Ruler is this?

I believe that love is the most mysterious and sublime among all phenomena of life for sentient beings (유정물). However, just as the same carbon can become charcoal or graphite, but also diamond (금강석), I believe that even within what we call love, there are countless levels and qualities. The same applies to love between sexes. The love of lewd men and women cannot be viewed the same as the love of wise men and virtuous women; between them lies a difference in value as vast as heaven and earth.

Love aiming for physical union might be the most common, but that is no different from the fact that there are more worms than humans in the biological world. Love that pairs longing for the spirit with physical union perhaps first earns the right to be called human, but it is humanity's pride that there exists a love that has climbed a step higher, completely detaching from desire for the flesh. This can only arise from recognizing the "eternal existence" within our temporary bodies. He Bo (河伯), who had never seen the ocean, believed the waters of the Yellow River's tributaries were the largest in the world.<215> A person who has not seen such love (사랑) considers love that overlooks the body merely empty talk; for them, a time will be necessary to encounter Copernicus and awaken to a new universe.

Speaking of the pinnacle of love, it would undoubtedly be indiscriminate, equal love. That is the love of a Buddha. It would be a love that cherishes all sentient beings like a lover, like an only son. However, on the path to reaching that state, there might also be a stage where one loves even just one person apart from the flesh.

To leave behind carnal passion (육정) means to leave behind animalistic instinct. That phrase is also equivalent to leaving behind all "selfish desire" (이기욕). Only when one loves "solely for their sake," completely abandoning the "greed" of "for my sake," does it become love tinged with the golden color of "compassion" (자비심).

I wonder if, in literature up to the present day, harsh emotions such as resentment, jealousy, greed, hatred, and anger have been dealt with and emphasized too much. These brutal emotions always become the root of misfortune and evil for people. Even with the gentle emotion of love, much literature has liked to pair it with harsh emotions. This might be for the convenience of literary techniques like contrast or catering to popular psychology, but surely the gentle emotions are what people want to have and develop. Love, sympathy, joy, sorrow, etc., etc. There have been those in the past who created literary works solely with such gentle emotions. Various Buddhist tales or, in modern times, Tolstoy's short stories from his later years are examples.

Just as each person has their own humble cottage (오막사리), each person possesses their own world (세게). However, just as those cottages are all unimpressive, people must always strive<216> to leave the world they inhabit and seek a larger, wider world.
Striving "to seek endlessly higher love and improve"—everyone, isn't this also a beautiful theme?

Bedside Diary (病床日記)

January 23rd (Sun)
On the table beside my sickbed for my intractable illness (難治의 病床), the flower vase (花甁) holds carnations, narcissus, and lilies. The carnations were brought by P. The narcissus were given by R, K, and C. The lilies were arranged by niece C. The vase was bought by W.
The colors are beautiful, and the fragrance is beautiful. They are the most beautiful flowers among Earth's products. Such beautiful flowers should be offered before Buddhas and Bodhisattvas. They are undeserved by a dirty person like me.
However, more beautiful than these flowers are the hearts that brought such flowers for me in my illness. That person, that compassionate heart (慈悲心). The most beautiful thing in this Universe (宇宙) is precisely this compassionate heart. Something beautiful beyond all praise. This heart is itself Buddha. It is God.
At night, read Matthew chapters 5 and 6. A pure heart. The parable of the lilies, etc. The flowers at my bedside are limitlessly<217> beautiful and fragrant, and my heart is limitlessly joyful. P, who read the Bible to me, also said he was joyful. A rare joyful night in a lifetime! (End)

January 25th (Tues) Cloudy
Trimmed the stems of the flowers in the vase. I trimmed them yesterday too, and they absorbed water well, reviving the wilted freesia and narcissus. The white carnation just won't revive.
The plum blossom (梅葉 - likely refers to a branch with buds/leaves) dried up during the night. Why? It will need to gather strength for some time to bloom again.
Of the three lilies, one has bloomed. The second one seems likely to bloom tomorrow.
Burned the incense given as a gift by S. I knelt before the Buddhas of the Ten Directions (十方諸佛前), burned incense, and joined my palms (焚香合掌). I prayed (念하다) that this fragrance may spread throughout the Ten Directions and purify the minds of all sentient beings (一切 衆生). One stick of incense (一炷香)! May my body become one stick of incense. But this body will emit a foul odor!
Last night, someone asked P to read John chapter 12 and Psalm 37. "The righteous shall inherit the land... the offspring of the wicked shall be cut off." O you of little faith, do not fear. O foolish one, O you of little faith!
Read the Expedient Means chapter (方便品) of the Lotus Sutra. "The words of the Buddhas are without difference. Towards the Dharma spoken by the Buddha, one should give rise to great power of faith (大信力)." "One should give rise to great joy (大歡喜). Knowing oneself will become a Buddha (自知當作佛)." Oh, ignorant child that I am, unable to listen to or believe the words spoken by the elders, without knowing when.

January 26th Sunny, Cold
Gazed at the reddish dawn sky through the east window. The Universe is always young, they say.
Read "Wishing to cause sentient beings to open the Buddha's wisdom-seeing, enabling them to attain purity (欲令衆生. 開佛智見. 使得淸淨)."<218>
Read the Manifestation of Dharma chapter (明法品) of the Avatamsaka Sutra. "Adornment of the physical body (色身莊嚴), adornment of speech (語言莊嚴), adornment of mental activity (意行莊嚴), adornment of the Buddha-field (佛刹莊嚴), adornment of radiance (光明莊嚴), adornment of the retinue (眷屬莊嚴), adornment of spiritual powers (神力莊嚴), adornment of the Buddha's teaching (佛敎莊嚴), adornment of the ground of Nirvana (涅槃地莊嚴), adornment of upholding the Dharma (持法莊嚴)."
Adornment of the Buddha-field (佛刹莊嚴) means eliminating afflictions (煩惱). Eliminating my afflictions. Eliminating even the traces of afflictions. When my mind is pure (淸淨), the entire Dharma realm (法界) is pure.
"How is the adornment of the Buddha-field attained? When one thought is pure, the myriad dharmas are pure (云何佛刹莊嚴得. 一念淸時萬法淸)."
Last night, someone read John chapters 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16. "Fear not, little flock!"
P came. Said he believes in cause and effect (因果) but not in the three lives (三生). I told him to first believe in the cause and effect of today and tomorrow. Then he will eventually come to believe in the cause and effect of past and future lives (前生 來生).
Though I intend and try to contemplate and practice "Constantly performing Buddha deeds (常行佛事)," it is difficult to attain Adamantine Concentration (金剛定), and afflictions arise violently.
The second lily bloomed without my noticing when. Though I kept it by my pillow and looked at it constantly, I didn't see it bloom. My Bodhicitta (菩提心) was like that too.<219>

**Summary**

This article by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu) presents miscellaneous thoughts on literature and life, emphasizing spiritual growth. He argues that human inequality allows for evolution towards higher states, like Buddhahood, guided by karma. He discusses the hierarchy of love, placing selfless, spiritual love detached from physical desire as the highest form, akin to Buddhist compassion (慈悲心). He critiques literature's focus on negative emotions, advocating for works centered on gentler feelings like love and sympathy, citing Buddhist tales and late Tolstoy. The latter half consists of diary entries from his sickbed (January 1938), detailing gifts of flowers, reflections on their beauty versus the givers' compassionate hearts, and readings from both the Bible (Matthew, John, Psalms) and Buddhist scriptures (Lotus Sutra, Avatamsaka Sutra), contemplating faith, purity, and the path to enlightenment.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its syncretic blend of Buddhist and Christian ideas, reflecting the complex intellectual landscape of Korea under Japanese rule. Ch'unwŏn's focus on individual spiritual cultivation and finding meaning through suffering (illness) could be interpreted in the context of seeking inner resilience or transcendence amidst the political and social constraints of the colonial period. His emphasis on universal love and compassion, drawing from multiple religious traditions, offers insight into the philosophical coping mechanisms developed by intellectuals at the time.

三千里文藝-文藝雜感
						春園
높은 사랑을 향하여

나는 사람의 평등되지 아니함을 믿는다. 지력으로나 의지력으로나 재력으로나 다 천차만별이 있지마는 그 중에도 「옳은 것」, 「아름다운 것」을 아는 힘, 느끼는 힘이 있어서 더욱 그러함을 믿는다. 그리고 나는 이것을 슲어하지 아니한다. 도리어 사람의 이 자별이야말로 무한한 향상과 진화를 약속하는 것이니 벌러지가 향상하기를 힘써 부처님이 될 수 있음을 믿을 수 있는 것이다. 그러길래 나같이 더럽고 어리석은 중생도 부처님의 완전을 바라는 기쁜 히망으로 이 고달픈 인생의 길을 걸어갈 수가 있는 것이다.
나는 우리들 중생 중에 때로 뛰어난 사람이 나오는 것을 본다. 석가여래라든가 여러 보살이라든가 예수라든가 하는 어른들이시다. 나는 그이들도 본래는 나와 같은 중생이셨더니 라고 배울 때에, 너도 나와 같이 될 수 있나니 라고 가라치심을 받을 때에 한량없는 고마움과 기쁨을 느낀다. 나는 가장 아름다운 몸과 가장 아름다운 음성과 가장 놓은 지혜와 한량없는 사랑과 힘과 공덕을 가진 「사람」이 되어서 모든 중생의 사모함을 받고 그들에게 기쁨과 힘과 구원이 될 수 있음을 믿는다. 나는 대흥서원의 영원한 생명으로 중생의 사랑의 의지가 될 수 있음을 믿는다. 사람들아, 이에서 더한 히망이 또 있겠는가?<214>
나는 이 모든 향상과 진화가 오직 우리가 짓는 업으로 되는 것을 믿는다. 고마우신 하나님은 이 우주가 인과률에 의하여 다스려지도록 지어주셨다. 우리네 벌러지와 같은 중생이 하는 조고만 「일」(업)도 하나도 슬어짐이 없이 내 예금구좌에 기입이 되는 것이다. 이 저축들이 모이고 모여서 내일의 나, 내 생의 나, 천겁 만겁 후의 나를 결정하는 것이다. 이야말로 하나님의 크신 은혜다. 만일 이 세상이 걸음 준 나락이 걸음 안 준 나락보다 못되는 일도 있다고 하면 우리네 살아가기가 얼마나 힘들 것일까. 밥을 먹어도 배고픈 수도 있고 불을 때일수록 방이 더 치워 가는 일도 생긴다면 우리는 어떻게 살아갈까? 원인 있으면 반드시 결과가 온다는 것-이것이 어떻게나 고마우신 섭리자의 은헨가?
나는 사랑이 일체 유정물의 생명현 중에 가장 신비하고 또 가장 숭고한 것임을 믿는다. 그러나 꼭 같은 탄소로도 숫도 되고 석묵도 되는 반면에 금강석도 되는 모양으로 다같이 사랑이라 하더라도 천차만별의 게단이 있고 품이 있는 것을 믿는다. 이성간의 사랑에 있어서도 마찬가지다. 음탕남녀의 사랑과 현사숙녀의 사랑과를 같이 불 수는 없는 것이니 그 사이에는 하눌과 땅 만한 가치의 층등이 있는 것이다.
육체의 결합을 목적으로 하는 사랑이 가장 많겠지마는 그것은 생물게에 사람보다도 벌러지가 많다는 것과 다름없는 것이다. 육체의 결합과 아울러 정신에 대한 사모를 짝하는 사당이야말로 비로소 인간적이라는 이름으로 불러질 자격을 갖이겠지마는 한층 더 올라가서 육체에 대한 욕망을 전연 떼어버린 사랑이 있는 것이 인류의 자랑이 아닐 수가 없다. 그것은 일시적인 우리 육체 속에 있는 「영원한 존재」를 인식하는 데서만 생길수 있기 때문이다. 바다를 못 본 하백은 황하의 개천 물을 세상에 가장 큰물로<215> 안다. 이러한 사량을 보지 못한 사람은 육체를 안보는 사랑을 공손으로만 생각하거니와 그에게는 어느 때에나 한번 코페르니쿠쓰를 만나서 새 우주를 깨달아야 할 시기가 필요할 것이다.
사랑의 극치로 말하면 무론 무차별, 평등의 사랑일 것이다. 그것은 부처님의 사랑이다. 모든 중생을 다 애인같이 외아들 같이 사랑하는 사랑일 것이다. 그러나 거기까지 가는 노중에는 어느 한 사람만이라도 육체를 떠나서 사랑하는 대목도 있을 것이다.
육정를 떠난다는 것은 동물적 본능을 떠난다는 말이다. 그 말은 「이기욕」을 일체로 떠난다는 말과도 같다. 완전히 「나를 위하야」라는 「욕심」을 떠나고 「오직 그를 위하야」사랑할 때에 그것이 비로소 「자비심」의 황금색을 띤 사랑이 되는 것이다.
오늘날까지의 문학에는 원망이라든가, 질투라든가, 욕심이라든가, 미움이라든가, 성냄이라든가, 이러한 사나운 감정이 너무 많이 취급되고 강조되지 않았는가 한다. 이러한 추폭한 감정은 늘 사람에게 불행과 악을 주는 근본이 된다. 사랑이라는 부드러운 감정조차도, 많은 문학에서는 사나운 감정을 곁드리기를 좋아하였다. 이것은 대조라든가, 대중의 심리에 마춘다든가 하는 문학적 기술의 편의를 위함도 있겠지마는 역시 사람에게 있고 싶고 발달되고 싶은 것은 부드러운 감정일 것이다. 사랑, 동정, 기쁨, 슬픔, 들, 들. 이러한 부드러운 감정만으로 문학적 작품을 만든 이가 과거에도 없지는 않았다. 불교의 려러 설화라든가, 근대에도 톨스토이의 말년의 단편 설화 등은 그러타다.
사람은 저마다 제 오막사리 한 간을 갖이고 있는 모양으로 저마다 제 세게 하나를 갖이고 있다. 그러나 그 오막사리들이 다 대견치 못한 것임과 같이 사람은 항상 제가<216> 들어앉은 세게를 벗으나서 더 크고 넓은 세게를 찾아야만 한다.
「끝없이 높은 사랑을 찾아 향상하려」는 애씀-여러분이어, 이것이 또한 아름다운 제목이 아닐가.
