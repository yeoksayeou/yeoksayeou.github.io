Publication Date: 1938년 11월 01일
Article Title: 夜哨線 (Night Sentry Line)
Author: 葉紫 -支那人作家- (Ye Zi -Chinese Writer-)
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Fiction)

Night Sentry Line
						Ye Zi -Chinese Writer-

It had already been three days since they arrived and stationed themselves near the small village where the enemy's barbed wire was strung—tomorrow, according to rumors, they were finally departing for the front line.
Cho Tŭk-sŭng (趙得勝) felt an extraordinary sorrow in his heart, and his face suddenly turned bright red. His eyes just blinked, blinked, and from his downturned lips, saliva dripped, drawing thin lines on the ground.
He managed to find Sergeant Major Yu (劉曹長) and requested him to write a long-term leave request (長期休暇願) on his behalf. Then, he sought out his Squad Leader (班長) and hurried to prepare the request form to be presented before the Duty Officer and the Company Commander.
Perhaps because the march was imminent, after dinner, the Squad Leader, Assistant Squad Leader, and everyone else seemed to have gone somewhere unknown. So Cho Tŭk-sŭng, with a restless heart, started searching frantically, running aimlessly through fields and paths.<283>
"Hey, you idiot, who are you looking for? Fool!"
Turning around, he saw Squad Leader Yang (楊班長) of the Third Squad following him with a somewhat stern face. Cho Tŭk-sŭng forced a smile and said,
"Yes, I... I am trying hard to find our Squad Leader, but the Squad Leader..."
"Are Hae Ro-sam (海老三) or Wang Taep'o (王大砲) likely to be in a place like that? Fool! You pathetic fool!"
Squad Leader Yang raised his hand and pointed towards the corner of the western wall. Cho Tŭk-sŭng quickly put on a smile and ran in that direction.
Indeed, the Squad Leader and Assistant Squad Leader were whispering about something in that corner by the wall.
"What is it? Huh?"
Squad Leader Wang's voice was loud as always.
"Yes, yes, I, I... uh..."
Cho Tŭk-sŭng felt somewhat embarrassed.
"It's that troublesome leave request again!"
"Yes! Yes! Squad Leader!... I..."
"What a troublesome fellow!"
Seeing the Squad Leader stand up abruptly, Cho Tŭk-sŭng was startled and stepped back a few paces. He knew the Squad Leader was known for his loud yelling, nicknamed "Cannon" (大砲), and felt genuinely scared.
"My... my mother, it seems she won't live even two more days..."
"Is that the reason? Is that all the reason? You damned fool—after I explained it so much yesterday!"
"Please, just submit this request form!"<284>
"You, you wretched bastard! Damn you!"
The Squad Leader, crumpling the piece of paper, rushed forward in anger as if to slap his cheek. Cho, startled and frightened, looked like he was about to cry.
Assistant Squad Leader Ri Hae-sam (李海三) quickly stepped in front of Wang Taep'o, blocking him.
"Mr. Wang! There goes your 'cannon' again!"
Squad Leader Wang smirked slightly, looked back at Ri Hae-sam, and said,
"Huh, Ri, what use is this fellow anyway? He looks like he's crying before I even hit him."
"I... I... I just want the Squad Leader to submit this request to the superior officer. My... my mother..."
"You brat! Talking back again! Damned fool!"
"Alright, alright, Cho, I understand!"
Ri stepped forward one pace and firmly grasped Cho's wrist.
"Look, you don't need to be afraid of the Squad Leader. He's just a 'cannon'. You keep talking about leave, leave, but do you still not understand? That we are departing tomorrow?"
"Yes! Assistant Squad Leader! I know that well."
"So, who do you think cares about your leave?"
"I saw Mr. Ho Mun-bin (胡文彬) leaving early this morning too!"
"Hmph, Ho Mun-bin is the Company Commander's relative, that's why!"
Ri Hae-sam repeated this meaningfully several times, then continued, "Let me tell you, even if you plead with the Company Commander, it won't work. You've already been denied leave two or three times. At a time like this, who would consider your mother's life or death an issue? Especially when we're marching tomorrow. Didn't the Squad Leader tell you several times yesterday? That no matter how much you want to go, there's simply no way. So, endure it! Be patient! No matter what, you..."<285>
"I... I don't care about that. Squad Leader, I just desperately want to go. If I can't get leave no matter what, I'll just have to stop marching!"
"Stop marching! If you're caught, it's immediate execution by firing squad!"
Cannon Squad Leader started shouting again.
"Running away during a march isn't that easy!"
Ri Hae-sam continued,
"There are many people around, do you think they'd let you get away?"
"I... I don't care about anything!"
"Why would you do such a foolish thing?"
Ri Hae-sam repeatedly tried to soothe him, turning down a winding path, explaining at length why the leave request was absolutely impossible and why stopping the march was not an option. Hearing this, Cho Tŭk-sŭng's large eyes finally welled up with tears. He put the request form back and walked away.
Wang Taep'o's face reddened slightly as he sat down.
"To think such a brat became a soldier! No matter how much you fret, it won't work!"
"Fretting! That's perfectly natural!"
Ri Hae-sam let out a slight chuckle.
"You say such a person isn't qualified to be a soldier, but what about yourself? Do you believe you are qualified to be a soldier?"
"I never knew such reasons from the start! If only that Farmers' Association (農民協會) hadn't disappeared, hmph..."
Wang Taep'o often remembered, not without pride, that he was once the chairman of the Farmers' Association in his old hometown.
"Farmers' Association! You cannon-mouth!"<286>
Even at this, Wang Taep'o remained silent. He rolled his round eyes and stared blankly at the evening glow sinking below the western horizon.
(Could it be true!) Wang thought somehow. (Did Cho Tŭk-sŭng originally not want to be a soldier?... He probably wasn't involved in the Farmers' Association, but from what he says, he too was once an honest farmer. Just like I myself am this kind of person...)
However, he didn't want to think about such things again. After a moment, he lifted his head, ground his teeth, and then, very kindly, patted Ri Hae-sam's shoulder.
"Ri, what should we do? If we go to the front line, we're bound to clash with those bandits (班人 = 共匪 = )!"
"The front line? You don't need to be so hasty!"
Ri Hae-sam looked up at Wang and smiled once more. Wang looked embarrassed and blushed slightly. Although Wang was an old soldier who had been in the Special Service Company (特務中隊) for over four years, he had never once experienced actual combat. He instinctively glanced at Ri Hae-sam again.
Ri Hae-sam had been in the military registry for over ten years and, moreover, had been captured twice by the "bandits." His overall experience was far superior to Wang's. For that reason, Ri Hae-sam had been transferred from the Brigade's Special Service Company to this 3rd Regiment, 1st Battalion, 3rd Company, and since becoming Assistant Squad Leader, he handled all matters in consultation with Wang. The two got along quite well. Whenever Squad Leader Wang was about to act rashly, Ri Hae-sam would kindly explain things. Especially regarding the front line and being captured—
"Why are you always so impatient?"
Whenever Wang Taep'o pressed him intensely, Ri Hae-sam would always counter with such words.
Ri Hae-sam knew well. He was the extremely impatient type, and if not restrained even slightly, he might unexpectedly commit violent acts.<287>
Now too, hearing this retort from Ri Hae-sam, he blushed and said nothing.
After a short while, he finally opened his mouth,
"I'm certainly not impatient! But pitiful things like that Cho Tŭk-sŭng should just disappear quickly."
Ri Hae-sam stood up from his seat.
"Come on, let's go. Let's quickly go and dissuade him properly before he unexpectedly causes trouble. If something happens, it will be serious!"
"Well..."
By the time they returned to the barracks and met Cho Tŭk-sŭng, the evening sun had already sunk below the horizon.

The next day, the Company Commander ordered his subordinates to be fully prepared and ready to move out to the front line immediately upon receiving orders from above.
The soldiers waited in the barracks. Morning passed, lunchtime passed, yet the departure order seemed unlikely to come. Wang Taep'o's heart grew increasingly tense.
"Hmph, could it be false? Surely they wouldn't depart just like that!"
"Perhaps so! Seeing as there's been no order until now."
Someone nearby chimed in.
"What's the use of hurrying so much!" Ri Hae-sam continued, "Wouldn't it be better if we didn't depart? You fools, if you keep wishing for it like that, you'll surely end up as ashes!"
"Should you be saying things like that? Assistant Squad Leader Ri," said a soldier from the sixth squad. "If we really don't depart, that would certainly be good. But wondering whether we'll depart or not is rather agonizing. If we're not allowed to leave here, we'll just be guarding this smelly barracks forever."<288>
"No matter how much you press, do you think there's some miraculous solution? In your opinion—"
Several others started arguing noisily again, but no one had any way of knowing whether they would depart or not. Wang Taep'o suddenly started shouting in the middle of the barracks. Cho Tŭk-sŭng, his face perpetually filled with worry, didn't say a word.
Evening came before they knew it. The soldiers had spent the entire day idly in the barracks.
Three days passed, then four days... Still, no departure order came down. The weather grew hotter daily, and the stifling heat made it hard even to breathe. The inside of the barracks gradually filled with a stuffy odor that stung the nose, and the soldiers felt sluggish, their heads aching. Sweat trickled down, soaking their clothes through.
"Damn it! To hell with it! Can't even die!"
If Ri Hae-sam hadn't forcibly restrained him, Wang Taep'o would have caused a major commotion right there in the barracks.
All the other soldiers also wore expressions of sullen anger.
After a short while, the Company Commander and Platoon Leader, observing the soldiers' expressions, said:
"If the superior's order is not to depart, then you can run out of the barracks or do whatever you please! Until then, endure it!"
However, they still remained annoyed, filled with complaints.
Cho Tŭk-sŭng, hearing the Company Commander's words that they might not depart after all, felt a new strength welling up from a corner of his heart. He took out his leave request form again and once more pushed it before the Squad Leader.
"Squad, Squad Leader! If we really aren't departing, please, I beg you..."
"This brat is here again! You damned fool—damn you!"
Cho flinched back in fear, quickly put the paper scrap away, and just turned back. Through eyes clouded with a mix of sorrow and fear, he glanced sideways at Ri Hae-sam standing to his right.
"Hey, they say we're not departing, isn't that truly great!" Ri Hae-sam forced a smile onto his face, trying to<289> console him.
But suddenly, early on the morning of the fifth day—just when the Brigade headquarters had reported that the main enemy force had moved far away—the departure order came down right at first light.
"All Brigade personnel, proceed to your assigned positions and swiftly advance towards the enemy for reconnaissance!"
Also, fearing falling into an enemy trap, the entire brigade was divided into three routes to advance, preparing to take an encircling attack posture against the enemy.
The first and second regiments took the left and right flanks respectively and moved out rapidly in unison. The third regiment, along with the brigade headquarters, advanced slowly along the central road.
The plan was to encircle the enemy flawlessly, leaving them no room for any stratagem.
All preparations were complete. By the time the departure order was given, the morning sun had already risen above the mountains.
Cho Tŭk-sŭng's heart, caught in the marching column, felt heavier than the grief of his parents' death. His mind was in turmoil; he wanted to scream and cry out loud right there in the ranks.
He occasionally blinked his large eyes, glancing furtively at Squad Leader Wang. The Squad Leader seemed incredibly cheerful. Not just the Squad Leader, but everyone else seemed to have a more cheerful expression than he did. Only he felt as though he had abandoned his aged mother, his wife, and children... even his father's ox (中 - likely a typo or placeholder for 牛, meaning ox/cow). But the purpose for enduring all this suffering and becoming a soldier was to avenge his father, kill the enemy, settle the score, become a respected official, and return home in triumph. Yet, what was his situation now? After two or three years, wasn't he still just a common soldier? He finally realized clearly that becoming a soldier was not the path he should have taken. Far from becoming an official and avenging his enemy, there were times when he faced the severe danger of losing his own life, and now he deeply regretted becoming a soldier. Thus, when others looked cheerful or treated him like a child, he felt the pain in his heart even more intensely. Originally, being treated like a child was deeply distressing for him.<290>
The Company Commander wouldn't grant leave, the Squad Leader opposed stopping the march midway, and his mother back home was critically ill, urging him to return quickly... His mind was torn in a thousand directions, unable to find clarity.
The column advanced step by step. With each step, Cho's heart ached anew. The heavy footsteps of the other soldiers felt as if they were trampling on his chest, making him dizzy and feel like collapsing at any moment.
Squad Leader Wang continued to walk along, joking with the soldiers with his usual cheerful laughter.
There wasn't a single cloud in the sky. The heat radiated powerfully, like the sun rising in the eastern sky. Dust rose like a thick fog underfoot with every step, creating what looked like a long trail of smoke. The vast, wide plain gradually turned into a desolate wasteland. Far off, not a single human shadow could be seen. Occasionally, one or two stray dogs or cats would dart out from collapsed graves on the field ridges, only to run meaninglessly across the rough, empty field towards the other side.
As the march continued, the heat grew increasingly severe. The sun, like a ball of fire hanging in the center of the sky, seemed poised to scorch the earth. Large beads of sweat rolled down the soldiers' faces, dripping like rain onto the furnace-hot ground.
Their feet burned—burned—on the brutally hot earth. It was difficult to bear the stinging heat without quickly lifting their feet off the ground. The powdery dust kicked up more with each step, clinging to their sweaty faces, making it hard to breathe. Yet, they had to open their mouths to breathe.
"Damn it! To hell with it! Let us burn to death!"
Even the gear on their shoulders and backs felt like burning embers. Of course, the military blankets, ammunition belts, canteens—everything felt like smoldering charcoal, growing heavier and heavier. Wang Taep'o, completely drenched in sweat, was constantly yelling—yelling. He cursed the sky and damned the earth. He felt as if blue flames were shooting from his throat and gulped down his canteen water in one go. But what use was complaining?
"Mr. Wang, isn't the march pleasant!" Ri looked at him and laughed. Wang Taep'o said nothing, just rushed over and snatched Ri Hae-sam's canteen too, draining it in one breath.<291>
The column had, at some point, passed through several relatively orderly villages. The roadside scenery was utterly desolate, no different from primitive times. There were no people, and not even any living creatures could be seen. All the houses stood empty. They had clearly fled. The collapsed houses, the houses with dark holes gaping open—just looking at them evoked a sense of their sorrow.
"How about that! Most of those who fled from here probably joined the bandits (반인). This situation is entirely due to that reason!" Thus Ri Hae-sam explained to Wang Taep'o, gesturing at the scenes along the route as they passed.
As noon approached, the sun seemed ready to burn them from head to toe. Several soldiers collapsed on the spot due to dizziness. White foam bubbled from the mouths of those collapsed on the ground, their faces deathly pale, clearly having stopped breathing completely.
"Ahhh—Ah!"
Only then was the order finally given, and the unit ate lunch on a relatively cool hillside.

In the afternoon, scattered clouds appeared in the sky, and occasionally a south wind rustled across the plains, making it quite cool.
The soldiers' spirits lifted somewhat, and they marched onto the endless road with renewed, strong steps. The desolate wilderness became increasingly bleak the further they marched. Gradually, not even a single dugout hut could be seen. Only heart-chilling phrases written in lime on the branches of roadside trees met their eyes.
"Tsk! Quite strong words they wrote!"
Seeing the writing on the branches, the soldiers burst into hollow laughter. Some, seeing the lime letters, even cursed as if they couldn't believe it.
"It can't be true!" Some adopted a skeptical attitude.
Wang Taep'o and Ri Hae-sam said nothing. It was as if they were meditating on the truth hidden within those words. Cho Tŭk-sŭng, with his mournful<292> face, did not chime in with a single word.
The column had already passed through about ten villages. In the distance, a small mountain rose up. The point scout (尖兵) company, seeming to have spotted the target, rapidly increased its speed. The following units quickly followed suit.
Messengers (傳令), out of breath, ran here and there. The enemy was right ahead; the soldiers' minds tensed.
Wang Taep'o, in a highly excited tone, asked Ri Hae-sam,
"Ri! Haven't we clashed with the enemy?"
Ri gave no reply. As they charged close to the foot of the mountain, suddenly—
Pang-pang!
Gunshots from within the point scout unit. A new fear gripped the soldiers' hearts, causing painful pressure. Cho Tŭk-sŭng, dazed by this single gunshot, barely managed to keep his head steady and looked ahead.
Pa-pa-pang! Pang-pang! Tak-tak Kwang!
The point scout unit, along with the 1st Company, had already spread out to the left and right. The target seemed to be the small mountain directly in front. However, the gunshots heard from the front were slow—tak—tak—and didn't sound like they had engaged the enemy. From the mountain, the target of the assault, no enemy counter-fire could be seen. Following behind, the Battalion Commander ordered the 3rd Company to spread out as well. The soldiers' eyes glinted like demons'. Each one lay prone, gripping their rifles tightly, disengaged the safety, and listened intently for the officers' orders.
"Fire your rifles into the air!" the officers' laughter-tinged command came through.
"What's going on?..." The soldiers were all puzzled. "What on earth is the reason for this? Damn it!"
The soldiers carefully looked ahead. They pointed their rifles skyward and pulled the triggers—tang-tang-tang. They advanced, taking an encircling posture towards the mountain.<293>
Pang-pa-pang! Tuk-tak-tuk-tak... Tak-tak-tak...
They gradually reached a point about two hundred meters from the mountain. The bugler, without knowing the reason, blew the "Charge" call.
Tang-tang-tang, Tang-tang! Tang...
Waaa—ah! Waa!
The soldiers, not knowing what was happening, followed with shouts of "Charge!" When the strongest 3rd and 4th Companies of the first battalion charged to the foot of the mountain, they saw not a single enemy soldier on the mountain. The Battalion Commander rode his horse up the mountain and surveyed the battle. Following him, all the soldiers also charged up the mountain.
"What on earth is this?" The soldiers looked down into the valley beyond the mountain, bewildered. And then—
Oh! What could this be? A raging sea—a sea of humans! In the narrow valley below the mountain, a teeming crowd of people was boiling. Men and women, old and young, were milling about. Some led oxen, some herded sheep, some carried broken pot shards on their shoulders. Daughters cried, searching frantically for their fathers, yet their terrified eyes kept looking up at the mountain.
"Aren't those all civilians!" The soldiers turned their astonished gazes. Then, charge, again charge—the 3rd and 4th Companies charged down beyond the mountain to the valley floor.
The civilians surged forward like waves in a great sea, pouring out of the valley entrance—Waa! Children, women, and the elderly collapsed onto the ground, their sounds of weeping and groaning so loud they seemed to shake heaven and earth.
"Surround them! Encircle them! Search every single one without letting anyone escape! There must be remnants of the bandits (반인) hidden among them!"
The Battalion Commander's order was relayed to the Company Commanders, and from the Company Commanders to the Platoon Leaders. In an instant, the soldiers had surrounded the civilians.
The civilians cried out like pigs having their throats slit.
"Search them! Search them, I say!"<294>
The Squad Leaders gave orders to the soldiers, but Wang Taep'o alone just said,
"What is the meaning of this! Innocent civilians! Damn it!" He stood there blankly, seemingly unaware of Ri Hae-sam gesturing to him several times. The civilians began a unanimous, tragic, pleading outcry.
"Soldiers! We... we are just civilians! There are no such 'bandits'!"
Pa-pang!
"Listen! You damned fool!" Fists rained down like lightning on the cheeks of those who hadn't raised their hands quickly enough.
"Soldier sir. Th-this is my life savings! Please spare me!"
Pang-pang! Even pleading for life earned slaps on the cheek.
"Ah! Older sister!"
"Ah! Mother!"
The Battalion Commander's orderlies dragged two young women out from the crowd.
"Soldier sir. What use is the ox... ox to you! Please leave it alone!"
"Hand it over. You old pig!"
Pa-pang! Ta-dang! Pang-pang!
Wang Taep'o, his eyes wide like a cow's, just watched, having ordered the soldiers of his entire squad not to move an inch. This was truly the first time he had ever witnessed such a scene, and it felt like a bonfire had been poured into his chest. In his anger, he roared at the soldiers.
"You bastards! You damned bastards!"
Ri Hae-sam's heart pounded. "This is trouble! What an impatient fool!" He tried to cover Wang's mouth, but by then, Squad Leader Wang, holding his rifle, had already rushed towards the Battalion and Company Commanders.
"You damned fool! Get lost!"<295>
Pa-pang! Tang-tang!
The Platoon Leader of the 3rd Platoon rushed over and wrestled Wang Taep'o down. He fell, rifle and all, becoming covered in dirt.
"Reporting to the Battalion Commander!" The Platoon Leader, planting one foot on Wang Taep'o's waist, declared, "This, this man has violated military regulations (軍規) and defied orders!"
"What is his name!" came the Battalion Commander's thunderous voice.
"3rd Company, 1st Squad Leader, Wang Chi-bin (王志斌)!"
"Hmm, bind him!"
Ri Hae-sam trembled all over, unable to utter a word. Ri, holding his rifle, shouted in a loud voice,
"Fellow soldiers! Like those civilians, our lives are in imminent danger! Our Squad Leader!"
Pang!
Assistant Squad Leader Ri's right hand and the rifle barrel suddenly dropped limply. Smoke rose from the Company Commander's pistol. Seeing this spectacle, Cho Tŭk-sŭng felt his head spin. Looking at the civilians filling the mountain valley, his own sorrow seemed to deepen.
"Cho Tŭk-sŭng! Run over and take that old man's ox!" The Platoon Leader ordered, seeing an old man leading an ox pass in front of Cho.
Cho was startled, snatched the ox's reins from the old man's hand, causing him to collapse with a thud.
"Soldier sir! What will you do with this skinny ox? Please let it go!"
"Take it! Cho Tŭk-sŭng!"
The Platoon Leader shouted sharply from behind Cho.
"Soldier sir! I... I beg you! Please!"
Cho didn't know what to do. He considered turning his rifle on the old man. But suddenly, his vision went black. His hands tingled as if struck by electricity, and he almost dropped the rifle he was holding.<296>
Tears streamed down from his eyes like rain. The old man, who had been prostrated on the ground pleading, seized this opportunity and led the ox away.
Pang!—
"What's the matter? Cho Tŭk-sŭng!"
The Platoon Leader fired a shot at the retreating old man and then questioned Cho.
Seeing the Platoon Leader, Cho quickly said, "I... I got sand in my eyes."
"Damned fool!" followed by the sound of a gunshot.
Cho Tŭk-sŭng saw the old man who had been leading the ox moments before collapse, bleeding. His heart felt like it was being shredded into a thousand, ten thousand pieces. Looking left and right, he saw the fleeing civilians, the bound squad leaders... His whole body trembled violently in shock.
The mountain valley was pacified in an instant. The cries of the civilians faded into the distance. Only then did the Battalion Commander, looking triumphant, issue orders.
"The 1st Company will guard the valley behind this mountain. The 2nd and 3rd Companies will escort the prisoners and, together with Battalion HQ, proceed to station south of the mountain!"

About five or six hundred meters from the mountain, a dilapidated fortress gate stood firmly shut. Inside, thirty or forty captured soldiers and deserters were spending the night en route to Brigade Headquarters under escort. The sun had long since set beyond the western mountains; it was a dark night. It was a night like poured ink. Even the stars high in the sky were hidden by clouds. The utterly exhausted soldiers were snoring this night.
At this time, about two hundred meters in front of the dilapidated fortress gate, on the sentry line (步哨線), Cho Tŭk-sŭng stood with his rifle slung over his shoulder, head hanging limply.
(The bitterness of three years as a soldier, the numerous battles and marches! The arrogant Squad Leader Wang, the kind Ri Hae-sam, the leave request,<297> the civilians, the old man leading the ox, the children searching for parents...)
All these things surfaced disconnectedly in his mind, and he felt an electric current run through his body. Thinking about all the events that happened during the day made his head spin. He felt he was on the verge of madness.
(Mother is sick and asking me to come back, the Squad Leader says I can't slack off on the march...)
He suddenly thought of his Squad Leader. Wang Chi-bin, bound and severely beaten; Ri Hae-sam, with a large hole in his right hand, bleeding red blood—just thinking about it made his teeth chatter.
(I don't want to stand here ever again! Ah! Who knows if I might end up like that too.)
He abruptly looked back at the fortress gate two hundred meters behind him. As if having made a great resolution, he scanned his surroundings. All around was dead quiet, and in the pitch darkness, nothing could be seen. His heart suddenly grew bold. He stealthily put down his rifle, unfastened his ammunition belt, took off his backpack and canteen, and thought,
"Damn it! Let's desert."
He prepared to flee, lightened, into the world of darkness.
"Desert? No! This area is surrounded by barracks. One wrong move and my head could roll off without warning!" He changed his mind again. He picked up the rifle. He put on the backpack. Cold sweat instantly drenched his body.
(What on earth should I do!)
He closed his eyes and thought. The next moment, he walked towards the fortress gate. He thought of a plan regarding the guards (반병).
"Password (口令)?"
"Safe! (安!)"
"Cho Tŭk-sŭng, what's the urgent matter?"
"There are four guards in total, right? Go quickly! The Company Commander says he has urgent business on the sentry line!"
"The Company Commander? What for?"
"You'll find out when you get there. And he told me to relieve you in the meantime."<298>
The four men left, half-believing. Only after seeing them go did Cho Tŭk-sŭng, elated, enter the fortress.
"Squad Leader Wang!"
"Who is it?"
"It's Cho Tŭk-sŭng!"
"Why are you here?"
"Shh! Quietly!"
Snip! With one cut, the rope binding Squad Leader Wang was severed. Snip, snip! Ri Hae-sam, now free, asked Cho Tŭk-sŭng,
"What happened? The guards outside?"
"Don't shout! I tricked them to come in here, they'll be back soon! We need to figure out how to deal with them!"
"They're here!"
Several men waited in the darkness. It seemed the four guards were approaching.
"Password?"
"Safe!" they replied, coming closer. "Cho Tŭk-sŭng, we didn't see the Company Commander!"
"How could the Company Commander have come!"
The four men came closer in confusion. In an instant, all four were seized and bound.
"Want to die! Or surrender!"
"Squad Leader Wang! P-please..." The four men dropped their rifles and trembled uncontrollably.
"Right!" was Ri Hae-sam's reply. "Everyone, grab a rifle! And you, lead the way and attack the two sentries ahead."
"Yes!" ... Several dozen deserting soldiers began to move in the darkness.
An utterly silent night! A night as solemn as awaiting a volcanic eruption. (End)<299>

**Summary**

The story "Night Sentry Line" follows Cho Tŭk-sŭng, a soldier in the Chinese army desperate for leave to see his dying mother. Denied permission just as his unit moves to the front, he witnesses the harsh realities of war, including the brutalization of civilians mistaken for enemies and the severe punishment of his sympathetic NCOs, Wang and Ri, for dissent. Disillusioned and horrified, Cho seizes an opportunity while on sentry duty to free Wang and Ri. Together, they overpower the guards and lead a group of fellow soldiers in deserting their posts under the cover of darkness.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this story, written by a Chinese author and published in a Korean magazine in 1938, is intriguing. Its depiction of military dissent, brutality, and desertion within the Chinese army could have served multiple purposes under Japanese censorship: potentially as propaganda highlighting the perceived weakness or chaos of China, or perhaps as a veiled commentary on oppressive authority and the suffering of common people that might resonate with Korean readers experiencing colonial rule and forced mobilization. The specific use of terms like "Panin" equated with "Kongbi" (Communist bandits) also reflects the complex political landscape of the Sino-Japanese War era.

夜哨線
						葉紫 -支那人作家-


적의 철조망이 처진 조고만 마을에 갓가이 와서 주돈(駐屯)된 지가 벌서 사흘-내일은 들니는 말에 드듸여 전선(前線)을 행하여 출발한다는 것이다.
조득승(趙得勝)은 마음 가운데 비상한 비감을 늣겼든지 갑작이 얼골이 발가케 질닌다. 두 눈은 그저 끔벅 끔벅 할 뿐, 양쪽으로 쑥 처진 입술로서는 춤을 질, 질 흘여서 땅 우에 가느다란 선을 긋고 있다.
그는 간신히 유조장(劉曹長)을 차저서 그의게 장기휴가원(長期休暇願)의 대서를 의뢰했다. 그 다음 또 반장(班長)을 차저서는 그 원서를 당직사관과 중대장의 앞에 내여 놓기로 준비를 서둘넜다.
아마 곧 행군이 있을야는지 저녁식사가 끝나자, 반장 부반장 할 것 없이 모다 어데를 갓는지 알 수가 없다. 그래서 조득승은 들뜬 마음으로 풀밭과 길을 가리지 않고 발 닷는대로 마구 달이면서 찾기 시작했다.<283>
「얘 이놈 누구를 찾느냐, 바보가!」
도라다 보니 삼반(三班)의 양반장이 그의 뒤를 약간 무서운 얼골을 하고 따러 온다. 조득승은 억지로 웃는 얼골을 지어가며
「예 저는 저... 저는 우리 반장님을 차저 뵈일야고 애씀니다마는 반장님은 반...」
「그런 곳에 해로삼(海老三)이나 왕대포(王大砲)가 있으니 있나! 바보가 이 못난 바보!」
양반장(楊班長)은 손을 들어 서편쪽 담벽켠을 가르킨다. 조득승은 얼는 얼골에 우슴을 지워가며 그 편쪽으로 달여갓다.
본즉 반장과 부반장은 서로 그 담벽 귀통이에서 무슨 이야기를 소근거리고 있다.
「무슨 일이야? 응.」
왕반장의 말소리는 언제와 같이 커다란 말소리였다.
「네, 네, 저, 저...는 저-.」
조득승은 다소 붓그러운 생각이 들었다.
「또 귀찬은 휘가원이야!」
「네! 네! 반장님!... 저는...」
「참 구찬은 자식이야!」
반장이 썩 이러스는 것은 본, 조득승은 깜작 놀나면서 멫 발자욱 뒤로 물너섰다. 반장은 잘 떠들기로 유명한 「대포」별명을 가진 위인인 줄을 아는 그는 저윽히 무서웠다.
「저... 저의 어머니는 이제 단 이틀도 살아있을 것 갓지가 앉어서...」
「그만한 이유야. 그래 그만한 이유란 말이지? 이놈 이 망할 놈-어제 그만치 네게 말해 들였는데도!」
「제발 이 원서를 듸려 보내 주십시요!」<284>
「마, 마, 망할 자식! 에익 망할 자식!」
반장은 그 조히 쪼각을 마라쥔 채 분김에 달겨들어 그의 뺨을 후려갈길야 했다. 조는 겁결에 놀나서 울상해젔다.
부반장 리해삼(李海三)은 얼는 달겨와서 왕대포의 앞을 막어스며
「왕씨! 또 예의 대포가 시작됨니다 그려!」
왕반장은 한번 힐쭉하니 우스면서 리해삼을 도라다보며
「응 리군, 이눔은 대체 어데다 쓰는 놈이야. 미처 때리기도 전에 우는 상을 하네.」
「저 저는... 저는 다만 이 원서를 상관께 밧처주기를 반장님에게 원할 뿐임니다. 저 저의 어머님은.」
「이놈! 또 떠드러! 망할 놈!」
「응 잘 알었어 조!」
리는 한거름 앞으로 닥어스며 조의 손목을 꼭 잡고
「자네는 그래 반장을 무서워 할 필요는 없네. 그 사람은 대포이니까. 자네는 그저 휴가 휴가하지만 그래 아직 잘 모르는 모양이지? 내일 출발할야는 것을.」
「네! 부반장님! 잘 알고 있읍니다.」
「그래 누가 자네 휴가쯤 알어줄상 싶은가.」
「저는 오늘 아츰 일즉암치 또 호문빈(胡文彬)씨가 떠나가는 것을 보았읍니다!」
「흥 호문빈은 중대장의 친척이니 그렀치!」
리해삼은 의미있는 듯이 이 말을 멫 번이나 되푸리하더니 뒤이어 「자네한테 말이지만 중대장에게 아무리 원해도 안될걸. 벌서 두 세번 휴가를 맛터스니까. 이런 때에 누가 자네 어머니의 생사를 문제나 삼을 것인가. 더욱이 내일은 행군하는 날이 아닌가. 반장님도 어제 멫 번이나 말하지 안튼가. 아모리 가고 싶어도 도모지 방법이 없다고. 자-참께 참어! 암만해도 자네는...<285>
「저는 저는 그런 것을 상관치 안슴니다. 반장님 그저 작구 가고만 십슴니다. 아무리 해서라도 휴가를 얻지 못하면 행군을 중지할 것뿐입니다!」
「행군을 중지한다고! 붓잡히면 당장 총살이다!」
대포반장은 또 짓거리기 시작이다.
「행군 중에 도망질 치기는 그렇게 쉬운 일이 아니야!」
리해삼은 또한 말을 니어
「주위에 사람들이 많은데 자네를 놋칠상 싶은가?」
「저는 저는 아무 상관없이!」
「그런 바보짓을 웨 할야는가?」
리해삼은 재삼 그를 달내면 꺽긴 길을 돌아스면서 휴가원이 도저히 안될 것과 또 행군을 중지할 수 없는 사실을 죽-설명했다. 이 말을 듣는 조득승은 비로소 크다란 두 눈에 눈물이 핑그르 돌며 그 원서를 도루 넣고 가 버렸다.
왕대포는 얼골이 약간 붉어지며 자리에 걸터 안는다.
「저런 자식도 다 - 군인이 됐어! 아모리 속을 태여도 안될걸!」
「속을 태인다구! 그게 당연한 일이지요!」
리해삼은 한번 힛쭉 우슴을 지우면서
「당신은 그래 그런 작자가 군인될 자격이 없다마는 그러면 당신자신은 어떻게 생각하시우. 군인이 될 자격이 있다고 밋소?」
「나야 본래부터 그런 리유는 모르니까! 이것이 만일 저저 농민협회(農民協會)가 없어지지 않은 때였다면 흥...」
왕대포는 자기가 옛날 향리의 농민협회 임원장이였든 것을 각금 잊지 않고 기억하는 것이였다.
「농민협회! 대포쟁이가!」<286>
이 말에도 왕대포는 말이 없었다. 그는 뚱그런 눈동자를 돌니면서 서편쪽 지평선으로 떠러지는 저녁노을은 물끄럼이 바라보고 있다.
(참말일가!) 어떻게 왕은 생각하였다. (조득승은 본래 병정이 되고 싶은 생각은 없었을가... 그는 농민협회에는 관게하지 않었겠지마는 본인의 말을 들어보면 그도 옛날에는 한사람의 착실한 농민이였다구. 맛치 내 자신이 이런 인간인거와 같이...)
허지만 그는 두번다시 그런 일을 생각하고는 싶지 않었다. 얼마 있드니 머리를 처들고 이를 뿌드득 갈녀니 극히 친절하게 리해삼의 억개를 뚝뚝 치면서
「리군 어쩔테야. 만약 전선에 나간다면 꼭 그 반인(班人) = 共匪 = 들하고 맛붙을 터인데!」
「전선(戰線)? 당신은 그렇게 성급해 질 것은 없지 않어요!」
리해삼은 왕을 처다보고 또 한번 우서 보였다. 왕은 열쩍은 듯이 약간 얼골을 붉힌다. 왕은 특무중대(特務中隊)에 들어가서 4년이 지나는 늙은 병정이였스나 입때까지 한번도 전쟁을 해 본 일이 없었다. 그는 저도 모르게 다시 한번 리해삼을 처다 보았다.
리해삼은 벌서 10년 넘께 군적에 있었으며 더구나 「반인」들에게는 두번씩이나 포료가 된 일이 있었다. 모든 견식이 왕보다는 훨신 높다. 그런 까닭으로 해서 리해삼은 려단의 특무중으로부터 이 3련대 1대대 3중대에 전근되여 와서 부반장이 된 이래, 모든 일을 왕과 함께 잘 상의해서 처리해 가고 있다. 그들 두 사람은 무던히 사히가 조왔다. 왕반장이 각금 무리를 하랴 할 때에는 리해삼은 친절이 잘 설명하고는 하였다. 더구나 전선과 포료에 관해서-
「당신은 그떻게 늘 성급해서 어쩔템니까?」
언제든지 왕대포의 추문이 심할 때이면 리해삼은 늘 이런 말로 반문하는 것이였다.
리해삼은 잘 알고 있다. 그는 비상히 성급한 축이여서 조금만 압제하지 않어도 뜻박게 란폭한 행동을 저지를 넌지도 모른다.<287>
지금도 또한 그는 리해삼에게 이런 반문을 듣고는 얼골을 붉히면서 아무 말도 없었다.
조곰 있드니 비로소 입을 열어
「나는 결코 성급하지는 않은데! 군 조득승 따위 그런 불상한 것은 얼는 없어지는 것이 좋을텐데」
리해삼은 자리를 이러서며
「자 - 갑시다. 뜻박게 소동을 이르키기 전에 얼는 가서 그를 잘 만류하두록 합시다. 만일 일이 이러났다가는 큰일입니다!」
「글세...」
그들이 병사로 도라와서 조득승을 맛났을 때는 저녁해가 벌서 수평선 넘어 떠러젔을 때이다.



다음날 중대장은 부하에게 각자 충분한 준비를 갓추고 있어 우으로부터 명영이 떠러지는 대로 직시 전선에 출동하도록 명하였다.
부하의 군사들은 병사에서 기달렸다. 아츰시간이 지나고 점심시간이 지나도 출발명령은 좀체로 네려질 것 갓지가 안타. 왕대포의 마음은 긴장할 대로 긴장해젔다.
「흥, 거짓이 아닐가! 설마 그렇게야 출발할야구!」
「아마 그런가 보우! 지금까지도 아무런 명령이 없는 것을 보니」
옆에서 누가 한목 끼우는 말이다.
「그리 급히 서둘너서는 뭐 해!」 리해삼은 말을 이여 「출발하지 안는 편이 도리혀 났지 않을가? 바보들이 너이들이 그렇게 좋와하다가는 필연 재가 되고 말걸!」
「그런 말을 해서는 안됩니까? 리부반장님」 제육반의 병정 하나가 하는 말이다. 「참말 출발하지 안는다면 그야 물론 좋치요. 어떻게 출발한다 안한다 하는 것은 도리혀 참기가 괴롭습니다. 만일 여기에서 나가는 것을 허하지 안는다면 언제든지 이 구린내 나는 병사를 직히겠지요」<288>
「아무리 조르면 무슨 신통한 방법이 있을 상 싶어? 자네의 의견에는 -」
여렀으 또 와-하고 론쟁을 시작했으나 어느 누구 하나, 출발허고 안허는 것을 알어낼 방법은 없다. 왕대포는 갑작이 병사가운데서 떠들기 시작했다. 조득승은 늘 수심이 가득한 얼골로 말한마듸 없었다.
