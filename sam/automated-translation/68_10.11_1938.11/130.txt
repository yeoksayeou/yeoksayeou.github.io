**Body Text Translation:**
Dream of the Tale of Ch'unhyang
						Ma Hae-song, President of Modern Japan, Tokyo

When I was young, among my beloved books were good ones published by Shinchōsha (新潮社), such as "The Sorrows of Young Werther," "Manon Lescaut," "Pierre and Jean," and "Love and Death." It was my dream to think of adding the "Tale of Ch'unhyang" (春香傳) to that series.

I also dreamed of staging this as a revue at the Tōgeki (東劇) or perhaps the Tōhō (東寶) stage. I also had the desire to try making this into a dance or music.

Surely, there isn't anyone born in Korea (조선) who doesn't hold an attachment and lingering affection for the "Tale of Ch'unhyang." Furthermore, it would be only natural for an intellectual to have had such a dream.
This is the kind of work the "Tale of Ch'unhyang" is.

Regarding the recent performance, I could not help but be surprised by the meticulous preparations, and consequently, I cannot help but respect it.
The scenery of Kwanghallu Pavilion (廣寒樓), where the sky-blue and cloud colors were all distinct, the elegant costumes, and the actors' every single movement truly evoked the feeling of thinking of celestial maidens (선녀). A strange fragrance drifted not only on the stage but to every corner of the theater, leading one, I believe, to a state as if dreaming for a brief moment.

I would say this to anyone:
"Have you seen it? The Tale of Ch'unhyang— If you haven't seen it, there's nothing more to say."<94>

**Summary**

Ma Hae-song, the president of the Japanese magazine "Modern Japan," expresses his long-held dream of seeing the Korean classic "Tale of Ch'unhyang" adapted into various modern forms like inclusion in a Japanese literary series, a stage revue, dance, or music. He praises a recent performance of "Ch'unhyang" for its meticulous preparation, beautiful aesthetics (scenery, costumes), and evocative atmosphere, strongly recommending others see it. This article is interesting from a colonial history perspective as it shows a Korean intellectual (Ma Hae-song, despite his association with a Japanese publication) deeply invested in promoting a Korean cultural artifact like *Ch'unhyangjŏn* during the colonial period. His desire to see it adapted into modern, even Japanese-associated formats (Shinchōsha, Tōgeki, Tōhō revues), reflects the complex cultural interactions and aspirations of Koreans under Japanese rule, navigating between preserving tradition and engaging with modernity, potentially even seeking recognition within the dominant colonial culture's frameworks.

春香傳의 꿈
						동경, 모던 일본 사장 馬海松
연소했을 때 愛書에 新潮社에서 나온 「젊은 웰테르의 슲음」「마논 *스크」「피에르와쟌」「戀愛와 死」 등의 곻은 책이 있었다. 그 총서 중에 「春香傳」을 써 넣었으면 하고 생각한 것이 내 꿈이였다.
나는 다시 이것을 東劇이나 혹 東寶 무대에 레뷰 - 로서 상연했으면 하는 꿈도 꾸여 보았다. 또는 이것을 무용으로 음악으로 맨드러 보고 싶은 마음도 있었다.
적어도 조선에, 태여난 자로서 「春香傳」에 애착과 미련을 안 가진 자 없으리라. 더구나 지식인으로서 이와 같은 꿈을 가젔다는 것은 당연한 일일 것이다.
이러한 「春香傳」이다.

이번 상연에 있어서 주밀한 준비에 놀나지 않을 수 없었고 따라서 존경하지 않을 수 없다.
하늘색 구름색 모다 제각금인 廣寒樓의 정경과 우미한 의상 배우들의 일거 일동 꼭 선녀를 생각케 하는 느낌이였다. 무대는 물론 극장 구석구석까지 이상한 향기가 떠돌아서 잠간 사이는 무슨 꿈이나 꾸는 듯 하는 경지에 이르러라고 믿는다.

나는 누구에게나 이렇게 말하리라.
「자네 그걸 보았든가 春香傳을 -  그걸 못 보았다면 말이 안되네」<94>
