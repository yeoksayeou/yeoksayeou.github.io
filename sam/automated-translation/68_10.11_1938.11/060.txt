**Body Text Translation:**
An Open Letter to Comrades Regarding Our Political Line
						In Chŏng-sik

Comrades! With this beautiful, passionate word that expresses human friendship to the highest degree, I intend to call upon you once again.
And within this term, "comrades," I intend not only to include the communists of Chosŏn (Korea) of the past but also broadly encompass the nationalist elder warriors (老 戰士 諸氏). I appeal to you with my sincere words and await your esteemed criticism.

I am not unaware that between you nationalists and you communists, there certainly exist various differences in principle and political oppositions that cannot be reconciled. However, no matter what opposition and conflict may strictly exist, as long as you remain faithful to your fundamental ideas and principled assertions of the past, I believe that when faced with the new situation in the Far East, which is shaking the heavens and earth of this East Asia where we live so turbulently, you will surely transcend all these oppositions and align yourselves with a single, common political stance and policy objective.

More than a year has passed since the outbreak of the China Incident (支那事變), and during this time, the process of reorganizing East Asia with the Empire of Japan as the sole and absolute leader (맹주) in economic and political terms has proceeded at a truly astonishing, unprecedented scale and with unparalleled rapidity, changing the entire map of Asia. No matter how tenaciously the so-called scorched-earth resistance (焦士 抗戰) by the anti-Japanese national front—comprising the Kuomintang<50> warlords of the Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) faction and the Chinese Communist Party led by Chu Te (朱德), Mao Tse-tung (毛澤東), and others—may unfold henceforth, it is absolutely impossible to reverse this already decided new tide in East Asia. This must be the unanimous conclusion of all intellects capable of calmly judging the objective situation.

Thus, all peoples of Asia must clearly recognize this irreversible new tide and, simultaneously, follow the path along which this tide progresses to seek the happiness and prosperity of their respective nations.

Esteemed comrades! For the past ten-odd years in the record of our lives, the central task we sought to resolve has always remained unchanged. Was it not our greatest concern, unforgettable even in dreams, to hope for the eternal happiness and prosperity of the Koreans as a single nation, and the amicable resolution of the Asian problem, which must necessarily include the happiness of Koreans?

If that is indeed so, then there is nothing that draws more immense interest for us than today's China Incident. Like lions listening to a waterfall, we must incline our passionate ears to the sound of distant thunder echoing throughout the Yangtze River (長江) region. And then, we must urgently decide our political stance and, according to the conviction thus decided, courageously charge forward. Reality always despises opportunism. All political indifference and policy hesitation are attitudes that thinkers should never adopt.

What political stance do you indeed demand from the Korean masses (民衆) today, and what social happiness do you intend to promise the Koreans of tomorrow?

I dare to hope for a solemn judgment and cool-headed decision from you regarding this immensely important, immediate problem. At the same time, having undergone much internal anguish (苦憫) and painful contemplation since the outbreak of the incident, I wish to express my own conviction and eagerly await your esteemed critique.

I pray that this open letter does not become a dividing marker that separates you and me forever. To discard one's entire past, built with blood and sweat over ten-odd years, and the comradely friendship forged behind iron bars like worn-out shoes (蔽屢) would surely not be a pleasant matter for any fool (愚夫). However, when one clearly realizes that the past achievements built up were filled with errors and the path walked in the past was a devil's road (魔의 길) leading the masses astray,<51> then, as long as we are at least thinkers, we must know how to courageously and boldly advance (邁進) towards new truth and conviction, like a fierce eagle (猛鷲) gazing at the sun. In modern terms, people call such a change in conviction a so-called conversion (轉向, *chŏnhyang*). However, a true conversion must fundamentally exclude all opportunism and political indifference. Therefore, conversion for a former communist ultimately means nothing other than a shift to another camp opposing the red camp—in other words, a decisive transition to the anti-communist camp (防共 陣營).

I want it to be forever recognized by you that this stance of mine was based thoroughly on solemn and cool-headed judgment. Even if there are those who cast base conjectures (忖度), that is the fault of the conjecturer, and it is not something I, the subject of the conjecture, am concerned about in the least. Even if a day comes when a bloody fight unfolds between us because you cannot help but cling to the past, I intend to remain faithful to my new conviction until the very end.

But, but, I wish once again that this open letter does not become a dividing marker that separates you and me forever. And if only we could, as thinkers and warriors in a new sense leading the Asia of tomorrow, once again gather all our will, all our passion, and all our wisdom in one place, following the national policy line indicated by the leader (盟主) of East Asia—

Note - In the following description, I assume a person named A. Through dialogue with this A, I intend to express my conviction more clearly.

A. So, what does this "tide of East Asia" (東亞의 대세) you speak of generally mean?

Me. The tide of East Asia means the progression of the reorganization process of the entire Far East (極東) by the power of the sole and absolute leader (盟主), who can directly or indirectly guide or dominate the economic, political, and cultural lives of each nation in East Asia.

A. In that case, which of the great powers does this sole and absolute leader generally refer to?

Me. Until before the outbreak of the China Incident, we could primarily point to the three major powers of Great Britain, Japan, and the Soviet Union (英日蘇) as the opposing forces contending for hegemony in the Central Plains (中原). As Mr. Sun Yat-sen (孫文) once lamented, modern China (支那) was by no means a fully independent nation. As Sun Yat-sen designated,<52> it was a vast semi-colony (半 植民地). Even the so-called national unification movement, most forcefully promoted over the last ten-odd years by the Chiang Kai-shek Kuomintang regime, could not completely overcome this semi-colonial state. Moreover, this unification movement was by no means based on the political trust of the masses towards the central government; it merely attempted to achieve unification by expediently resolving and mitigating the contradictions among the major and minor warlords who constantly clashed with each other. This was indeed the fundamental contradiction inherent in the national unification movement led by the Chiang Kai-shek regime. In any case, as long as it could not escape the semi-colonial state, modern China always had to directly or indirectly rely on one of the great powers. The senile and cunning British Empire (英 帝國) constantly sought opportunities for aggression through routes passing through the southwestern borders like Sin'gang (新疆), Tibet (西藏), Yunnan (雲南), and Kwichu (貴州) to reach the Central Plains, or through direct routes based in Hongkong (香港). The Soviet Union (「쏘비엣트」 연방) of Red Imperialism (赤色 帝國主義) contended for hegemony in the Central Plains through numerous Red routes leading from Sin'gang through Kansu (甘肅) and Shensi (陝西) to the heartland of China. Confronting these two major aggressive forces, our Empire of Japan (日本 帝國) has, for the past ten-odd years, truly exercised patience and self-restraint (隱忍 自重), continuously advocating for the establishment of East Asian peace (東亞 和平) through close cooperation between Japan and China (日支). However, the central government of China, which considers "allying with distant states while attacking nearby ones" (遠交近攻) its supreme national policy, not only failed to comply with this sincere proposal but instead leaned towards the efforts of Red Russia (赤露) and Old Britain (老英), completely transforming into an anti-Japanese, indeed, Japan-selling (賣日) China. The outbreak of the China Incident truly finds its cause in this utterly foolish (愚妄) self-destructive policy of anti-Japanese China. The entire responsibility for the calamity of war must naturally be borne by the leaders of this anti-Japanese China. Regarding this, we can cite numerous proofs. Moreover, the fact that the Chinese Soviet government (中華 쏘비엣트 政府) already issued a declaration of war against our Imperial government in July of Shōwa 8 (1933) confirms their suicidal challenge and must remain an indelible, eternal stain on the history of modern China.

Thus, the Empire's military mobilization was initiated to enlighten the foolish masses of anti-Japanese China, thoroughly chastise their political leaders, and simultaneously establish the eternal peace and prosperity of East Asia on a bedrock foundation.<53>
More than half a year has already passed since Shanghai (上海) and Nanking (南京) were occupied, completely destroying the entire apparatus of the Chekiang (淅江) financial clique and severing the heart of modern China from its whole. The stubborn resistance in the Suchou (徐州) area, the ancient stronghold of Hsiang Yü (項羽), was easily crushed, and the fall of the Wuhan (武漢) triple cities, where the final defenses are being solidified, is already imminent. Today, in North China (北支) and South China (南支), the influence of the new regimes under the indirect guidance of the Empire is expanding and strengthening daily. Thus, it cannot but be affirmed that the glorious position as the sole and absolute leader of East Asia is already secured in the hands of our Empire.

A. As the leader of East Asia, can our Empire truly promise eternal happiness and prosperity to the people of China and all the peoples of Asia? If this is impossible, wouldn't the position of leader instead become the root of all evil towards Asia?

Me. Correct! Bismarck (「삐스마 - 크」), famous as the Iron and Blood Chancellor (鐵血 宰相), insightfully stated, "Might is right" (Macht ist r icht). However, for power to become justice, this power must necessarily be used solely for the welfare of the vast majority of the people. Power without justice means incompetence, but similarly, justice without power merely signifies oppression.

From this perspective, I dare to assert, with all my conscience, that the power of the Empire as the leader of East Asia precisely aligns with the welfare of the entire populace of the Far East (極東 全 民象). Let's look at a simple example. As is well known, the fertile loess region of the Yellow River (黃河) basin is not only an inexhaustible source of modern industrial raw materials like iron, coal, and petroleum but is also world-renowned for the excellent quality and quantity of its cotton production, which forms the basic element of the textile industry. However, under China's past economic conditions, this inexhaustible natural resource wealth (富源), far from being sufficiently developed under modern production techniques, saw cotton production shrink year after year due to the incessant rapacious extortion (苛斂誅求) by major and minor warlords and the decline in production technology. Mr. Madial (「마듸알」氏), an authority on Chinese rural economy, lamented this shrinkage of agricultural production, saying, "In such regions, humans displace horses (馬), and hoes and sickles preclude the introduction of tractors (「트락타 - 」)."<54>
Furthermore, he traced the cause of this miserable state—where agricultural productivity declines year after year, production techniques show a downward trend, and human labor is treated more contemptuously than that of oxen and horses (牛馬)—to the despotic tyranny (專制 的 虐政) and lawless extortion of the warlords. According to sincere investigations and reports by researchers of the Chinese countryside, there are many regions where the number of taxes borne by a single Chinese peasant actually exceeds 200 types. Moreover, in the North China region, collecting several years' worth of future taxes within the current year has reportedly become a custom under warlord rule.

The process of reorganizing the Chinese economy with our Empire as the leader takes as its primary mission, first and foremost, the complete overthrow of the despotic warlords and the strict prohibition of all exploitation of the masses by them. Thus, with the feudal shackles (桎梏) on agricultural production completely removed, and through the introduction of modern improved agricultural tools (改良 農具) and technical guidance for farmers, a series of developmental processes will be promised: modernization of production techniques, astonishing increase in productivity, national rationalization of irrigation projects, enhancement of land fertility (土地 豊沃), and unprecedented prosperity (未曾有의 번영) in peasant life.

This, of course, is not only observable in cotton production; the same happy prospect can be presented for every sector of Chinese agricultural production. The numerous experiments carried out by Japan-China joint venture companies (日支 合資 會社) have already practically proven the validity of this established theory.

A. If Chinese peasants can develop and prosper like this, what relation does this have to the national economy of our Empire, which leads them?

Me. Let's examine the example of cotton again. Our country's basic industry, or rather key industry (鍵鑰 産業), is the well-known textile industry (纖維 工業). However, we have hitherto imported the main raw material for this textile industry from America (亞米利加). American cotton, of course, has a much higher production cost than Chinese cotton, so its production price cannot but be much higher. Also, in terms of transportation costs, tariffs, etc., our country's textile manufacturers have always had to bear enormous burdens. In short, the fact that the price of these imports could not but be much higher than Chinese cotton has, in turn, diminished the competitive ability in the international arena for cotton fabrics produced in our country's large factories, and it has also created more unfavorable conditions for the Chinese masses, the main consumers of Made in<55> Japan cotton fabrics.

Therefore, if Japan-China economic cooperation proceeds smoothly now, and Chinese cotton completely replaces American cotton, it will not only make the economic life of the Chinese masses, as both producers and consumers, much more advantageous, but it will also resolve various contradictions of our country's capitalism—such as the narrowness of commodity markets (商品 市場의 狹隘) and the scarcity of raw material sources (原料 泉源의 狹小)—at one stroke (一擧에), charting an infinite upward line of development for its future.

In short, if agricultural China and industrial Japan cooperate and collaborate closely like this, it will indeed pave a great path of co-existence and co-prosperity (共存 共榮), leading not only the peoples of Japan and China but the lives of all peoples in Asia towards unprecedented prosperity.

Established theories such as "Japanese capitalism is now undergoing a process of rapid collapse" or "Only Japanese and American capitalism are walking a path of slight ascent" were the baseless theories of the Comintern (「콤민테룬」), which confuses hope with reality and constantly curses the prosperity of Asia. We certainly cannot lend an ear to such chants. That Japanese capitalism is by no means undergoing a process of rapid collapse, but rather drawing a line of rapid ascent, has been displayed all too clearly to our eyes, especially since the Manchurian Incident (滿洲 事變), hasn't it?

A. The rapid development of Japanese capitalism is believed to have a significant impact on the political life of our Korean nation (朝鮮 民族) as well. What is your observation on this point?

Me. Correct. It must not be overlooked that since the outbreak of the China Incident, truly unprecedented changes have been brought about in our political life in Chosŏn. Firstly, the fatal defeat of Chosŏn's past nationalist movement (民族主義 運動) and communist labor movement (共産主義的 勞動 運動) catches our attention. As capitalism developed rapidly, the social foundation for the national movement and communist movement was completely annihilated. The large-scale conversions (大量 的 轉向) of nationalists and communists, along with the rapid national awakening (國民的 自覺) of the general masses, have drastically accelerated the process of complete unification between the Yamato people (大和 民族) and the Korean people (朝鮮 民族)—in other words, the process of *naesŏn ilch'e* (內鮮 一體, Japan-Korea unification). It has become the common realization of all Koreans who truly hold the greatest interest in the present and future of Chosŏn that the interests of the Korean nation can be advanced step by step only by fully cooperating with the Empire's national policy (國策) and faithfully fulfilling one's duties as a subject of the Empire (帝國 臣民), not through XX means [likely 'rebellious' or 'anti-Japanese'] opposing the Empire of Japan,<56> and that doing so is the only path towards the prosperity of East Asia, which includes the happiness of the Korean nation.

Firstly, we need to clearly recognize that the Japan-Korea Annexation (日韓 合併) itself was by no means merely a historical fact of conquest (征服的 史實). In the process of annexation, the co-existence and co-prosperity (共存 共榮) of the Japanese and Korean peoples and the eternal prosperity of East Asia were certainly considered as the greatest factors. Above all, it is necessary to glance at the actual results of the history of governance in Chosŏn (朝鮮 統治史) from the Japan-Korea Annexation to the present day. According to the principles of Marxist formulaism (맑스主義的 公式論), since the annexation, the rural areas of Chosŏn, under the overwhelming influence of foreign capital, should have experienced a rapid acceleration of the process of rural class differentiation (農村 階級 分化), accompanied by the concentration and accumulation of land property at one extreme, the separation of general peasants from land at the other extreme, the flooding of surplus rural population (農村 過剩 人口), the gradual fragmentation (逐次的 細分化) of the average cultivated area per household, and the extreme impoverishment (極度의 貧乏化) of general rural life—a series of miserable processes should have been rapidly promoted.

However, the reality of the rural areas thoroughly rejects the application of the principles of this formulaism.
According to statistics, the cultivated land area (耕地 面積) of Chosŏn increased from 847,000 *chōbo* (町步) of paddy fields (畓) in Meiji 43 (1910) to 1,670,000 *chōbo* [NB: This seems wrong, likely 1,670,000 *chōbo* total, not just paddy, or the first number is wrong. Assuming the text meant 1,670,000 total paddy area, which is still a huge increase] in Shōwa 9 (1934), doubling the paddy area. Also, the cultivated area per farm household, combining paddy and dry fields (田), increased from an index of 100 to 142 during the same period. Furthermore, along with the increase in cultivated land area, the unprecedented development of agricultural production technology led to a remarkable increase in the index of rice production (米穀 生産). The harvest yield index (收穫高 指數) from Meiji 42 (1909) to Shōwa 10 (1935) increased from 100 to 172, and the harvest yield index per *tanbo* (反步) leaped astonishingly from 100 to 447 [NB: 447 seems impossibly high, likely a typo or misinterpretation in the original text, maybe 147?]. Looking again at the production value (生産 價額) of rice, it shows an increase from index 100 to 137 during the same period. All the above figures most concisely indicate the general prosperity of Chosŏn's rural areas and the improvement process of peasant life, and they cannot but serve as evidence (證左) of the true significance of the Japan-Korea Annexation.

Therefore, the fact that after the outbreak of the China Incident, we Koreans, regardless of whether inside or outside the country (海의 内外), truly mobilized the entire nation to cooperate<57> with the national policy and began rushing towards the path of *naesŏn ilch'e* was due to this level of economic development achieved in Chosŏn society since the beginning of Chosŏn governance, and the absolute trust of the masses based on this development. For a cultural nation (文化民族) with its own history of at least 4,000 years to strive for complete unification (一體化) with another nation cannot be explained solely by the power of the governing authorities. The social factors underlying this historical confluence process of *naesŏn ilch'e* are the absolute trust in Imperial rule experienced by the Korean nation since the annexation and the unprecedented prosperity within Chosŏn society.

A. You said that Chosŏn's nationalist and communist movements suffered a fatal defeat. Then, what was the cause of this defeat?

Me. People often try to point to the harsh arrests and suppression (峻烈한 檢擧와 彈壓) by the authorities as the cause of this defeat. However, this is merely a formal observation that sees only one side of the situation. If the movement in Chosŏn truly possessed historical rationality for its existence, no amount of suppression by the authorities could have completely halted its development. The decisive factor causing the fatal defeat of the movement in Chosŏn lies in the national awakening (國民的 自覺) of the Korean nation and the national development and prosperity that made this awakening possible. The Korean masses of today do not demand any XX [likely 'rebellion' or 'independence']. They intend to cooperate thoroughly with the Empire's national policy. And thus, as loyal subjects of the Empire (忠實한 帝國의 臣民), they intend to advance and realize their national happiness step by step.

A. But the Comintern (「콤민테룬」) will persistently demand the continuation of the struggle from the communists in Chosŏn. As long as the support and guidance of the Comintern continue, won't the nationalist or communist movement in Chosŏn still preserve its life?

Me. No. It should not be forgotten that since the Manchurian Incident, all the Comintern's red routes leading into Chosŏn have been fatally severed. Moreover, since last year, with the occupation of North China (北支) and Shanghai (上海), the communists within the country have fallen into a state of complete blockade, and those scattered abroad have completely lost their bases of activity. The Comintern will still order the continuation of the struggle. The true hidden intention (魂膽) of the Comintern is still simply to weaken the national strength<58> of our Empire, even through cruel means. It is said that Lenin (「레 - 닌」) once invited Yi Tong-hwi (李東輝) to Moscow (莫斯料) and offered the following encouragement:
"Even if it's a XX struggle [likely 'futile' or 'doomed'], it doesn't matter. Whoever it may be, as long as they resist Japan, we have an obligation to actively support them," he reportedly said.

Whether Chosŏn society perishes or thrives, whether the young men of Chosŏn are imprisoned en masse and suffer any physical sacrifice—that is not the slightest concern (關知하는 바가 아니다) of the Comintern. Their true hidden intention is that it is sufficient as long as we Koreans become some element of resistance against the Empire of Japan, even if it means the entire nation perishes.

Furthermore, even if any instructions and support from the Comintern were possible, it should not be forgotten that, considering the current situation, the activities of communists have fallen into a state of complete impossibility.

A. Then, what is the political demand you advocate for the Korean nation?

Me. I unconditionally insist on cooperating with the Empire's national policy line and faithfully fulfilling our duties as subjects of the Empire. First, we must fulfill our duties as nationals (國民). Let's demand rights after fulfilling duties! The Koreans of today are by no means pitiful colonial subjects (植民地 百姓). We must not forget that we are, at the very least, members of the Imperial nation advancing towards the leadership and domination of all of East Asia (全 東亞). Only in this way can *naesŏn ilch'e* and the complete equalization (完全한 平等化) of the Japanese and Korean peoples be realized, can it not? Indeed, a blessed prospect lies before the Korean nation today. End.<59>

**Summary**

In this 1938 essay, In Chŏng-sik argues forcefully that Koreans, both nationalists and communists, must abandon their past resistance against Japan. He claims the ongoing Sino-Japanese War proves Japan's inevitable role as the sole leader ("맹주") of a reorganized East Asia. He dismisses Chinese resistance as futile and asserts that cooperating with Japan's "national policy" (*kokusaku*, 국책) is the only path to prosperity for Chosŏn and all of Asia, citing alleged economic progress since the annexation as proof of Japan's benevolent intentions. He urges Koreans to embrace their role as loyal subjects of the Empire (*naesŏn ilch'e*), fulfill their duties first, and anticipate eventual equality, dismissing past movements and the Comintern as irrelevant or malicious.

This article is highly significant for historians studying colonial Korea as a primary source articulating the logic of collaboration (*ch'inil*, 親日) from the perspective of a Korean intellectual. It vividly demonstrates how collaborators framed cooperation not just as pragmatic surrender but as a positive, historically necessary step towards a modern, prosperous future within the Japanese-led East Asian order, justifying *naesŏn ilch'e* and denouncing anti-colonial resistance as misguided and detrimental. The specific economic arguments used (however dubious) and the timing during the height of the Sino-Japanese War provide valuable insight into the justifications employed to promote assimilation and support for Japan's imperial project.

我等의 政治的 路線에 관해서 同志 諸君에게 보내는 公開狀
						印貞植
동지 제군! 인간 우정을 최고도로 표현하는 이 아롬다운 정열의 말로서 나는 다시 한 번 제군을 불르려 한다.
그리고 이 동지 제군이란 말 가운데 나는 종래의 조선의 공산주의자 제군만이 아니라 널리 민족주의적 老 戰士 諸氏까지도 포함하야 나의 진심으로서의 말을 제군의 앞에 호소하는 동시에 제군의 고명한 비판을 기대하려는 바이다.
민족주의자로서의 제군과 또 공산주의자로서의 제군의 사이에는 물론 서로 조화될 수가 없는 여러 가지의 원칙상의 차이와 정치상의 대립이 존립하고 있다는 것을 나는 몰르는 배도 아니다. 그러나 여하한 대립과 또 상극이 엄존한다 할지라도 제군이 의연히 종래의 근본 사상과 원칙적 주장에 충실하기를 마지 안는 한 우리들이 사는 이 東亞의 천지를 너무도 소란스럽게 잡아 흔들고 있는 극동의 新사태에 직면하게 될 때 제군은 반다시 이러한 모든 대립을 초월하여 한 개의 공통된 정치적 태도와 정책적 목표에 일치되리라고 밋는다.
支那 사변이 발발되여 이미 1년이 넘는 그 동안은 일본 제국을 경제상의 또는 정치상의 유일 절대의 맹주로 하는 東亞의 재편성 과정은 실로 놀낼만한 공전의 대규모와 미증유의 급격한 속도로 진행되여 亞細亞의 전 지도를 변하고 말었다. 蔣介石 일파의 국민당<50> 군벌과 朱德 毛擇東 등을 수반으로 하는 중국 공산당의 항일 민족 전선에 의한 이른바 焦士 항전이 금후 여하히 집요하게 전개된다 할지라도 이미 결정된 이 東亞의 새로운 대세를 역전할 수는 절대로 불가능하다는 것이 객관적 정세를 냉정하게 판단할 줄 아는 모든 지성의 일치되는 결론이 아니면 안될 것이다.
이리하야 亞細亞의 모든 민족은 이 역전될 수 없는 새로운 대세를 명확히 인식하는 동시에 이 대세가 진행하는 노선에 순응하여 각자의 민족의 행복과 또 번영을 꾀하고 있는 것이다.
경애하는 동지 제군 과거 십 수년 동안 우리들의 생활 기록에 있어서 우리들이 해결하려한 중심의 과제는 언제든지 변함이 없었다. 한 개의 민족으로서의 조선인의 영원한 행복과 번영을 기대하며 또 조선인의 행복을 반다시 포함하지 않으면 안 되는 亞細亞 문제의 원만한 해결만이 우리들의 꿈에도 잊을 수 없는 최대의 관심사가 안이었드냐.
과연 그러타면 금일의 支那 사변과 같이 우리들에게 있어서 막대한 관심을 끄는 일은 다시  없을 것이다. 폭포를 듯는 사자와 같이 長江 일대에 울리는 遠雷의 소리에 우리들은 정열의 귀를 기우리지 않으면 안 된다. 그리하야 일각이 밥부게 우리들의 정치적 태도를 결정한 후 결정된 소신에 따라 용감하게 돌진하지 않으면 안될 것이다. 현실은 언제든지 기회주의를 멸시한다. 一切 정치적 무관심 정책적 주저은 결코 사상인의 취할 바 태도가 아니다.
제군은 과연 금일의 조선 민중에 대해서 여하한 정치적 태세를 요청하며 또 明日의 조선인에 대해서 여하한 사회적 행복을 약속하려 하는가.
나는 감히 제군 앞에 이 중대하기 짝이 없는 당면에의 문제에 관해서 엄숙한 판단과 냉정한 결정이 있기를 바라는 동시에 사변 발발 이후 幾多의 내적 苦憫과 고통의 사색을 경유하야 지어진 나의 소신을 피력하여 제군의 고명한 비평을 待望하려는 바이다.
바라건대 나의 이 공개장으로 하여금 제군과 나를 영원히 메별케 하는 분기의 도표가 되지 안케하여 주소서. 과거 십 수년 동안 피와 땀으로 쌓어 온 자기의 전 과거와 철창에서 맺어진 동지적 우정을 蔽屢와 같이 버린다는 것은 어떠한 愚夫에게 있어서도 결코 유쾌의 일은 안일 것이다. 그러나 쌓어 놓은 과거의 업적이 오류에 찬 것이며 걸어온 과거의 노선이 민중을 글으치는 魔의 길이였다는 것을 명확히 자각하게 될<51> 때에는 우리들이 적어도 한 개의 사상인인 한으로 태양을 擬視하는 猛鷲와 같이 새로운 진리와 신념을 향해서 용감하고 대담하게 邁進할 줄을 알어야 한다. 요새 말로 말할 때 사람들은 이러한 신념상의 변화를 가르처 소위 전향이라고 한다. 그러나 참된 의미의 전향이란 것은 一切의 기회주의와 또 정치상의 무관심을 근본적으로 배제하지 않으면 안 된다. 그럼으로 종래의 공산주의자로서의 전향이란 결국 적색 진영에 대립되는 다른 진영에로의 推移 다시 말하면 방공 진영에로의 결정적 이행을 의미하는 것 이외에 아모 것도 업다.
나의 이러한 태도는 끝까지 엄숙하고 냉정한 판단을 기초로 하였다는 것을 제군에게 영원히 인정되고 싶다. 만일 야비한 忖度를 던지는 자가 설사 있다 하드래도 그것은 忖度하는 者 자신의 잘못이며 忖度 당하는 나의 조곰도 關知할 바가 아니다. 만일 제군이 의연히 과거에 집착되기를 마지못하여 우리들의 사이에 설사 피투성이의 싸홈이 전개되는 날이 있다 할지라도 나는 나의 새로운 신념에 끝까지 충실하기를 마지 않으려 한다.
그러나 그러나 다시 한 번 원컨대 이 공개장으로 하여금 제군과 나를 영원히 결별케 하는 분기의 도표가 되지 안케 하여 주소서. 그리하야 우리들로 하여금 명일의 亞細亞를 리드하는 새로운 의미의 사상 전사로서 東亞의 맹주가 지시하는 국책적 노선을 따라 우리들의 전 의욕과 전 정열과 전 지혜를 다시 한 번 한곳에 뭉켜볼 수가 있다면 -
註 - 이하의 서술에 있어서 나는 A라는 사람을 가정한다. 그리하야 이 A와의 대화를 통해서 나는 나의 소신을 보담 명백하게 피력하려 한다.
A. 그래 네가 말하는 東亞의 대세란 일반적으로 무었을 의미하는 말이냐.
나, 東亞의 대세란 것은 東亞의 각 민족의 경제적 정치적 내지 문화적 생활을 직접 혹은 간접으로 지도 또는 지배할 수가 있는 유일 절대의 맹주의 세력에 의한 전 極東의 재편성 과정의 진행을 의미함이다.
A. 그러타면 이 유일 절대의 맹주란 대개 열강 중의 어느 세력을 지칭함이냐.
支那 사변이 발발되기 전까지에는 중원의 패권을 다토는 열강의 대립 세력으로서 우리는 주로 英日蘇의 3대 세력을 불 수가 있었다. 孫文 氏가 일즉이 개탄한 바와 같이 현대의 支那는 결코 완전한 독립 국가가 아니였다. 그것은 孫文의 지명한 대로<52> 한 개의 광대한 반 식민지였다. 蔣介石 국민당 정권에 의해서 최근 십 수년간을 통하야 가장 강력적으로 추진되여 온 소위 국가 통일 운동으로서도 이러한 반 식민지적 상태를 완전히 지양할 수는 없었든 것이다. 더욱이 이 통일 운동이란 것은 결코, 중앙 정권에 대한 민중의 정치적 신망을 기저로 한 것이 되지 못하고 서로 서로 상극하여 마지 안는 대소 군벌간의 모순을 임기 응변적으로 해결 완화함에 의해서 달성하려 한데 불과하였다. 이것은 실로 蔣介石 정권에 의한 국가 통일 운동에 내재하는 기본적 모순이였다. 어째든 반 식민지적 상태를 면치 못하는 한 현대의 支那는 항상 열강 중의 어느 세력에 직접 혹은 간접으로 귀의하는 안을 수가 없었다. 노쇠하고 교활한 英 제국은 新疆과 西藏 雲南과 貴州 등 西南의 변경을 거처 중원에 달하는 「루 - 트」를 통하야 또는 香港을 기지로 하는 직선적 「루 - 트」를 통해서 항상 침략의 기회를 엿보아왔으며 赤色 제국주의의 「쏘비엣트」 연방을 新疆에서 甘肅 陝西를 거처 支那 본부에 이르는 數多한 赤色 「루 - 트」를 통하야 중원의 패권을 다토고 나온 것이다. 이 양대 침략 세력에 대항하야 우리 일본 제국은 과거 십 수년간 실로 隱忍 자중하면서 日支 양국 간의 緊密한 제휴에 의한 東亞 和平의 확립을 끈임 없이 제창하여왔다. 그러나 遠交近攻을 최고의 국책으로 하는 支那의 중앙 정권은 이 성의 있는 제창에 순응하지 안었을 뿐 아니라 도리혀 赤露와 老英의 노력에 귀의하여 완전한 항일 내지 매일의 支那로 일변되고 말었다. 支那 사변의 발발도 실로 이 항일 支那의 이처럼 愚妄하기 짝이 없는 자멸적 정책에 그 원인이 있는 것이다. 전쟁의 참화에 대한 전 책임은 당연히 이 항일 支那의 수령들이 지지 않으면 안될 것이다. 이에 관해서도 우리는 數多한 실증을 예시할 수가 있다. 더욱이 昭和 8년 7월에 중화 쏘비엣트 정부가 이미 우리 제국 정부에 대해서 선전 포고를 발하였다는 것은 그들의 자살적 도전을 확증하는 사실로서 현대 支那史上에 지울 수 업는 영원한 오점이 안이면 안된다.
이리하야 항일 支那의 우매한 민중을 계몽하며 그들의 정치적 수령을 철저히 응징하는 동시에 東亞의 영원한 평화와 번영을 반석의 기초 우에 확립하기 위하야 제국이 군사적 동원이 개시된 것이다.<53>
上海와 南京이 점령되여 淅江 재벌의 전 기구가 여지없이 파괴되는 동시에 현대 支那의 심장부가 그의 전체로부터 절단되고 만지 이미 반년이 넘었으며 項羽의 古城인 徐州 일대에서의 완강한 저항도 容易히 破粹되고 최후의 방비을 굳게하는 武漢 3鎭의 함락도 이미 목전에 임박된 금일 北支와 南支에는 제국의 간접적 지도에 의한 신 정권의 영향이 나날이 확대 강화되고 있으니 東亞의 유일 절대의 맹주로서의 광영의 지위는 이미 우리 제국의 손에 점유된 것으로 단언되지 않을 수 없다.
A. 東亞의 맹주로서의 우리 제국은 과연 支那의 민중에 대해서 또는 亞細亞의 전 민중에 대해서 그들의 영원한 행복과 번영을 약속할 수가 없을까. 만일 이것이 불가능하다면 맹주로서의 지위는 도리혀 亞細亞에 대한 전 죄악의 근본이 될 것이 아니냐.
그러타! 鐵血 재상으로 유명한 「삐스마 - 크」는 「권력은 정의」(Machtist r icht)라고 갈파하였다. 그러나 권력이 곳 정의가 되지만 이 권력이 반다시 多大數의 민중의 복리를 위해서만 사용됨을 요하는 것이다. 권력 없는 정의란 무능을 의미하지만 다시 정의 없는 권력도 또한 한 개의 압제를 의미함에 불과하다.
