From the Poetry Collection "Tonggyŏng" (憧憬 - Yearning)
Kim Kwang-sŏp (金珖燮)

A Foolish Talk on Self-Selected Poems
When I receive letters from friends far away or from readers I don't know, it's interesting how their preferences differ from various angles, but it seems quite foolish to choose for oneself and say which ones are good.
The few pieces selected here are not too long and are ones that I wanted to represent several tendencies.

There are days when emotions are extremely clear. At such times, the poet's eye is like a small window looking out at beautiful nature. If it rains all night, the window cannot be opened. But when morning clears, nature is fresh and reflects vividly in this window.
One morning, after the rain cleared, I went out into the field, looked up at the sky, and stood by the small pond at my house. It seemed as if the sky had descended into the pond, and under the surface covered with willow leaves, laying on the green shade, small goldfish darted about briskly. Their rhythm was like a musical score, but to my eyes, it seemed to paint a poem of nature, leading to the feeling of the following five-line poem.

A Summer Morning After Rain

On a day the rain has cleared
The clear sky descends into the pond
Creating a summer morning
Green shade becomes the paper
And goldfish write poetry.

The form of the Sijo (時調) felt too rigid and restrictive, so I wanted to create shorter poems with more freedom.
It might have been long ago, but I had someone dear and lovely. We walked along a deserted<230> street where snow fell fiercely. Soft snowflakes covered her hair endlessly and gently. I wrote much romance onto those snowflakes. Returning home, the twelve o'clock silence, like a bird taking flight, descended upon my nerves, landing on the branches of the tree behind the house. My eyes filled with longing and loneliness in Apollo's cup. A poet couldn't be without a verse, so I wrote this short poem.

Romance

Returning on the night path after writing on snowflakes
And letting them fly onto hair
Silence flows quietly
Filling Apollo's cup with nostalgia (香愁).

One quiet, warm spring day, the car ran alongside the East Sea (東海). The East Sea is my heart, my hometown. I wanted to fly to the distant horizon where eternal mystery lies, at the border of the blue sea. I became a seagull. Looking at the blue sea, my consciousness flew. The white waves of the blue sea crashing against the rocks were so refreshing that I lost my way and fell into the water. The sea was so deep that I fell deeper and deeper. Wouldn't one want to dig for the burning coral at the bottom of the sea? In a diving posture, my consciousness burned within the sea. There is a flight of consciousness possessing intuition, so this poem contains the rhythm of my spirit.

Going to Dig Coral

Borrowing the wings of a seagull
I fly over the blue East Sea.

Towards the horizon where the sea embraces the sky
Toward the eternally hidden world

Into the white water crashing against the rocks
I fall deeper and deeper.

The sky is blue, the sea is blue
The waves are blue, and the heart is blue on such a day

Following the story of the sky, land, and sea<231>
I fall into the white foam, deeper and deeper, going to dig coral.

On Christmas night, a single carnation given by a friend gradually withered, speaking words to me each day. One evening, trying to maintain a cozy heart, I had a conversation with this single flower, discussing youth, love, and beauty. There was doubt, hope, yearning (憧憬), ecstasy, and perhaps even disappointment. In the end, where a lightless wind passed, like a sunflower bowing its head to the earth, I felt the desire to bow my head and pray. Thus, "Twilight" (黃昏) is the poem I love most, and I wanted to weave the noble flame of youth into a commonplace title. But what can I do about the fact that the more beautiful something is, the more I anticipate its completion, yet fail to achieve it? As youth is absurd and cannot end merely in lyricism, I added a bit of intellect.

Twilight

After all the light of the sky has leaned onto the western mountain ridge and burned away, around the time shadows falling from the clouds push in from the forest, on my desk, a single flower, pursing its red lips, reveals a scene where a fervent flame burns down a nation.

Even if it's just half a day past noon for the infinite will to live
Now, sitting in this place where light has turned yellow and dim
Sad words come to mind; this is an island seeking spices
Night after night seems to fall like this.

Although this single flower may not be seeking the sermon that it will inevitably wither
What words will beauty use to bring tears when it finally opens its dry lips?
What words will remain for the day when the wonders of heaven and earth gathered in this single flower?
And where shall we read the sacred text of love?
I ask, is "Beauty" (美) just words? Then all words will hide.
I ask, is "Love" (愛) a sad tomorrow? Then tomorrow will try not to come at all.
Blue sky, spring breeze, stars and moon, lonely sun
Did not this perpetuity gather to bloom this small single flower?<232>

Ah, how carefully must we cherish this single flower, we who will soon all fall from the nest of time?
Shall we, drawn by a sigh, exaggerate the few beautiful days and make them the glory of youth?
On the day the flower falls, deep nostalgia (香愁) falls to the ground.

The single sunflower that turned its face following the sun now bows its aspiration to the earth.
The sun sets, and a lightless wind passes by.
Shall I fall onto the warm bed and be buried in a single thought?
Ah, where does the brilliant wonder flow? When twilight comes, night after night seems to fall like this.

I have many nights when I cannot sleep properly. Why, for what reason, am I robbed of sleep, lose sleep, go out into the garden in the middle of the night, or wander restlessly along the mountain path of Ch'wiun-jŏng (翠雲亭), I myself do not know. An owl lived in my heart. It seemed like a symbol of myself. Its sleepless, round eyes were precisely my eyes, owl eyes. Opening those eyes, to quench my thirst, I went to the spring and drank the water stored secretly during the day; it felt like the spring of poetry. Even in this meaningful bird, there is the language of great nature, but all is silent, and it even leads to blindness in the bright daylight. Here lies a greater doubt and anxiety. This is my symbolic poem, and in it lies the world of misfortune. Next time, I am preparing a poem to symbolize the world by wearing the clothes of a crow.

[Untitled Poem - Commentary refers to Owl]

Is there a silent world, an eternally buried land, where you, gathering your bedding at night, become a child of the night, opening the door from your dark bed, and at its entrance
Prick up anxious eyes and ears full of doubt, counting time on the empty earth?

Soon, fluttering from the periphery of silence, you descend into a deep, deserted mountain valley
Lift your heavy mouth, peck at the small gate, and drink a sip of spring water<233> quenching your thirst with myths from underground; the spring water flows eternally into darkness.

All the dark reefs of antiquity are contracted in your breast
All the meditations of the primitive age reside darkly in your spring
Is the night story of great nature narrated within your small breast, within a single stream of spring water?

Ah, on the day the eternal mystery of night desired expression and obtained your body
Nature equipped you with an infinite vocabulary that burns silently
Now, avoiding the blinded daylight, do you seek to hear alone the calling voice of God?
The above five poems are from the collection "Tonggyŏng" (憧憬)<234>

From the Poetry Collection 『Hyŏnhaet'an』 (玄海灘 - Genkai Sea)

Im Hwa (林和)

Nostalgia (鄕愁)

Homeland
Now on the distant Peninsula (半島)
As if casting it off
I left it behind

Even memory
is faint
And old things
are painful
the more I think of them

Ah! It is now May
the prime time

Skylarks
like stones thrown from a sling
straight
shoot upwards
and dew drops
doze
on the green wheat field

As if caressing
a gentle breeze blows
sunlight
flows like pollen

Spreading both hands
chasing butterflies
the dream by the ditch
is still
in the fishbowl<235>
clear like a goldfish

But now it is May
the prime time

On the city pavement
where a shower has passed
in a line of water

Ah! I
seeing the blue sky
of May
as if flailing
chase the unforgettable
butterfly.

Because of You Alone

Is the only thing that exists
Glory alone?
Is there truly no Humiliation?
Or does it exist but is treated as non-existent?
If only there were no struggle...

But if there were no struggle
Neither would exist
Struggle indeed
is the mother of Glory and Humiliation
The everything among all things

The blood of Defeat
brewing the wine of Victory
Humiliation
distilling the spice of Glory
All are in the wide sea of struggle.

The sea
has no width or depth
Where Victory freely
scoops up the pearls of its pleasure
And Defeat, to the death,<236>
learns the noble price of its pain

Of irrecoverable
humiliation
- Do you gentlemen know the meaning of this word?
Where the painful, painful wound
red blood
blooms like a rose bush

Ah! Because of you alone, you alone
I even love Humiliation.

Where Was Happiness?

With both hands thrust in pockets
You wear a yellow raincoat
Looking up at the sky, on your shoulders
Night dew already rests, white and heavy.

The home to return to is far
The path to walk is distant
My young heart.
On the platform of a desolate suburb
What dream do you follow?

The small heart startled by first love
Has now entertained immense thoughts.

Who was the person you waited for?
Is the time for them to come still far off?
Looking at the watch
Occasionally counting the stars
You know the moon is bright
The sky is blue
And the breaking water drops

Are more beautiful than pearls
At the seaside of your hometown
Do you wander like a child?

The night is deep
And they finally did not come.<237>
Driven away as if pursued
Under the dark sky
Above even the starlit Tokyo Bay (東京灣)
Ah, you
Must find a place for a night's rest
Anywhere.

Where is the warm roof
Under which to lay your small five-foot body?
The dense houses
And the pedestrians on the bright streets
Are too unfamiliar
And cold strangers.
Only fearful eyeballs
Red like sparks
Follow your wandering back
As if shooting darts.

Ah, if
The one awaited never comes
And the home to return to is disordered down to the bedding mat
And harsh fate sweeps down
Over your head

My heart—
You, who were only as lovely as a single rose
Are now
A lost child.

Pitiful heart—
The salty
Foreign wind
Feels cold on your thin skin as if penetrating.
Sad night
Rising and falling in your mind
In the pupils of dear people
What did you see?

The path of wandering that leads nowhere
Was it all a warm cradle?
Wherever they went<238>
Did they not discover a homeland?
With no clear promise of returning
To the homeland's cradle someday
Did not the heart of the eternal wayfarer
Settle as if shadowed?

Inside, it was sadder than a frozen lake
But deep as the sea.

Truly, both they and I
Only memories of childhood
Like acorns
Scattered on the mountain slopes and fields of home
With no one to pick them up
Somehow, we in the wind and rain are lonely
Like a single row of young trees
But the irrepressible happiness and joy
Are not above, nor beside, but solely
In the single devotion
Stretching towards the difficult future!
(Postscript: Since it seems I don't sing of things other than myself, it feels like purely my own property. Therefore, my heart always yearns for beautiful lyric poetry.)<239>

From the Poetry Collection "Hyangsu" (鄕愁 - Nostalgia)

Cho Pyŏg-am (趙碧岩)

The Glacier of Nostal-gy

After publishing my humble poetry collection "Hyangsu" (鄕愁), I sent a copy to a friend, and received a letter asking why there was no preface. I hesitated for a long time to write a reply, but somehow the question lingered, so I once wrote back as follows:
"In aiming to grasp naked human value, the writer's position differs from that of people engaged in other work. Yearning (憧憬) always floats about clearly in the world, yet the feeling of yearning, its expression, and the agony and sorrow of human life exist precisely because it cannot be clearly grasped. Though I am ashamed of what I have done, I dislike writing things like prefaces that come close to self-justification by clearly stating reasons. Nor do I feel like writing one. Knowing it's tedious, I wanted instead to strip off even the outer garments and show the bare body itself, just as it is."
I am not one who likes to engage in such self-defense and self-criticism. This applies to literature as a whole, and especially to poetry. However, I took up my pen thinking this much might not be irrelevant. This is because my attitude has been to write truthfully about what arises unhindered from the deep inquiry into life at each moment.
Though few and insignificant, it is enough to murmur without falsehood or pretense the true meaning of life and the sorrow of living. If my posture of seeking the truth hidden like a gem within such ordinariness and murmuring it simply can be seen, that alone would be satisfaction.
A stream of life, rooted in antiquity, flows down<240>, always floating a fragment of weariness, heading towards the sea, towards the sea. Along the way, poetic inspiration somehow swells up—at such times, I am most solitary. It is a time of fatigue from travel.
Leaving the chaotic and miscellaneous streets (life), when I grope for a lonely starlit place, I heroically start out as if boldly commanding Great Nature, but then get exhausted and collapse, often captured by helpless, solitary sorrow.
It is at this time. The nostalgia (鄕愁) that persistently follows and nags me torments me unbearably. This is the realm of poetry drifting on the glacier of nostal-gy.
The pieces included in my humble poetry collection "Hyangsu" are also imbued with this scent, and the few pieces excerpted here are part of that same family. Am I not perhaps a foolish person who always holds a puzzling roof tile fragment (瓦片) in hand, soliciting all sorts of stories in the forest of silence?

Roof Tile Fragment (瓦片)

Stories full of resentment
Joyful stories
Sad stories

Stories, stories, stories
Endless, bottomless—those countless stories

Even after receiving so many wounds all over your body
Your fidelity, kept firmly shut

Is intention truly this sharp?
Is silence truly this heavy?

Today too, I soothe you
Pestering for old stories.

Window Facing the Sea

Suddenly rising from the desk
Stretching, I lean against the window
And gaze outside aimlessly.

Nothing is visible in the flowing darkness<241>
So it is deeper and farther.

The islands that always blocked my view
And the obstructing horizon are gone.

Attaching the sail of contemplation to my childlike heart
I freely send it off onto this dark night sea.

Yet it's not a boat yearning painfully for a loved one.
Nor is it a boat wishing to clearly establish its purpose, but
A heart wanting to send it off endlessly
Exhausted by an unknown loneliness.

With the palm that caressed my fluttering chest
I grope and wipe the dark windowpane.

Vine (넌추리)

Planting a vine dug up
Giving it water morning and evening
A heart waiting distantly.

Thinking butterflies might still come searching
Trapped inside this double-curved glass window
The small vine grew up deceived.

Clearly knowing butterflies cannot come to it
My sentiment that stubbornly watered and raised it—
Even if I myself were better than a butterfly to it
What affection could permeate it?

Am I too a terrifying tyrant like the world?

Rather, from now on, I want to stop watering it
And see it wither before it even blooms.

Then
Without a large mirror
My own form seems likely to be reflected clearly.<242>

Andong Tea House (安東茶寮)

Though the scene seems extremely gentle
A cold, sad undercurrent flows within

On the table, forsythia prettily decorates spring
But the foreign girl, Tasha (다-샤-), seems to wither aimlessly like a reed.

In this place where I came to listen sideways to the rusted tradition of even a dented Huqin (胡弓)
The anxiety of tilting vodka (웍카-) I don't know how to drink.

Pressing my cheek, hot as charcoal fire, against the marble table
I cast the fishing line of yearning (憧憬) into the "Abyss of Silence"

What if I get completely drunk? I feel like crying, about to cry.
Like this, just like this, I want to leave on the eternal path of wandering.

Nostalgia (鄕愁)

Travel sorrow, stinging saltily like seawater whenever dusk falls
Today too, entrusting the traveler's loneliness to the east window
Always like a newly dropped calf
Painfully ruminating on unforgettable nostalgia (鄕愁)

In a misty village where cozy nests settle
I long to fly in fluttering like a bird seeking a warm home.<243>

From the Poetry Collection "San chebi" (山제비 - Mountain Swallow)

Pak Se-yŏng (朴世永)

Now I will briefly write down the motivations behind writing these few poems.
"Mountain Swallow" (산제비) was written when I went to Mount Sŏngni (俗離山). At that time, I spent a night at Sanggŏ-sa Temple (上庫寺) and woke up at dawn. Although it was summer, a rather cool air filled the room, chillingly. The monk living alone in the small hermitage seemed already awake, preparing our breakfast. Opening the shuttered door and going out into the garden, I saw the panorama of the Sŏngni mountain range spread out like a folding screen! White, red, and silently dark, its brilliant colors were incomparable to those seen from the fields. I felt like my body could fly away and land there in one breath. After eating breakfast, I started climbing again towards Munjang-tae Peak (文壯臺). It was a dense forest where one couldn't see the sunlight, almost like a primeval forest. No matter how far I went, it seemed endless, and wherever the path was lost, red earth was dug up. They said this was the work of wild boars. Trees large enough to embrace lay fallen here and there, emitting the smell of mushrooms. It was past noon when I finally reached Munjang-tae with difficulty.
From this mountain range, the Kŭm River (錦江) flows west, and the Naktong River (洛東江) flows south. Truly, the world below looked hazy, and the rivers curved and flowed like threads. At that time, the "Mountain Swallows" (山제비) flying freely in the sky seemed to release my constricted heart.
They must always feel what I first felt; is it any different from ascending one step from the shackles of reality to seek the truth?
Thus, there are those who yearn (憧憬) for the realization of grand ideals, and comparing this with the rampage of fierce beasts in the dark dense forest, one imagines how bright and sublime the life of the "Mountain Swallow" is. I decided that I too would try to possess something this sublime in my life<244> with firm conviction.
Elegy (悲歌), There was a time when I shared innocent conversations with a village girl in a place where strawberry vines covered the stream bank and the shade of camellia trees was deep. In the lonely mountain village, only the sound of water flowed murmuringly, and occasionally the chirping of mountain birds echoed in my ears. My mood felt like pleading for something, and the strawberries held in my hand seemed blurry, as if invisible.
Six years have passed since those days of dipping feet in the stream and pouring out pure feelings, and I have returned to the countryside again.
There are rumors that the girl who tied a knot in my heart has drifted far away, and also that she is gone forever. At that time, my feelings turned entirely melancholic and became too poignant. Thinking back on those days with her, I felt regretful as if I had lost some treasure, like half of my life had been taken away.
Looking around, the camellia shade is still there, but the place where we sat and played has been trampled by calves, and unfamiliar girls are just doing laundry. And the thumping sound of the water mill felt like it was tearing my heart, thud by thud.
Nostalgia (鄕愁) An old saying goes, "Autumn sorrows the scholar" (秋士悲). For some reason, just hearing the sound of falling leaves makes one contemplate life, and the heart burns in the bleak atmosphere. At such times, how can those long away from their hometown not feel heartache? Although I have never been to Manchuria (滿洲), and the North and South are different, even in Mainland China (支那本部), on the mountain-less green plains, there must have been more than one or two times when yearning for the hometown was incessant.
Seeing muddy water reminds me of home; seeing only blue clothes makes me want to see my hometown clothes; hearing only clumsy speech makes me want to hear my hometown's speech too.
If even a bird longs for its nest, how much more so for my hometown that gave birth to me? I pour out my emotions, wanting those wandering the plains of Manchuria to feel this same feeling.
"Warrior of Haral" (하랄의 勇士)
Passing through the streets in early summer, I suddenly looked at a display window. A scene caught my eye. I found myself bowing my head to that naked young warrior.
His arm, thinner and more emaciated than my own insignificant one, held a rifle.
The tattered clothes on his body, his sparse hair—he, like an orphan, was receiving instruction from a regular soldier.
I think<248> about the beating blood in his chest, trying to ignite the chaotic battlefield with the flame of righteousness.
Oh, young boy soldier!
Warrior of Haral, leaving beloved parents for the battlefield!
For your small life, unknown to you!
Hot tears suddenly welled up in my eyes.
But even if flocks of eagles fly above your head, and the wind of poison gas seems to reach before you, let there be victory! Let there be no bending! Oh, young Warrior of Haral!<249>

Mountain Swallow (산제비)

Did you come from the South?
Did you come from the North?
On the mountain peak, the highest peak
Swallow nesting where one cannot climb higher.

You are indeed like the embodiment of freedom.
Who can hold your body?
Who can claim familiarity with your body?
Truly, the sky seems yours, the earth seems yours.

Looking down upon the world with eyes like green beans
With your fist-sized body, piercing the sky like an arrow
Circling horizontally and vertically like a magician's wand on the mountaintop
Oh, swallow
You are magnificent.

Laugh at me, who, after panting up for a whole morning, a whole day,
Looks down upon the world and feels things.
I rather wish I could spread wings like you.
Darting in one breath, soaring in one breath
I wish I could become like you, mysterious beyond further flight.

When the morning red sunbeams shine on the white rocks like spears planted
You will sit on that peak and preen your feathers
And hear all the secrets flowing out of the primeval forest.

When the wild boar digs up the red earth
You will think of flying to the stars.
When the striped tiger prowls, seeking weak prey to fill its belly
You will be the thousand-li bird (千里鳥)
That carries the sad news of humans
From this country to that country.
Mountain swallow, fly!
Fly like an arrow!<246>

The ground is cracked like a tortoise shell.
Fly, you all fly!
And for the poor peasants
Can you not gather the clouds?
Fly, circling round and round, vertically and horizontally, soaring and darting
Come with clouds trailing your tail.

Mountain swallow, fly!
Fly like an arrow!
Part the clouds, push through the fog.

Elegy (悲歌)

By the stream flowing, washing pebbles
Under the shade where camellias grow thick
You and I pleaded our hearts.
When we walked by the streamside strawberry vines
Your hands were full of strawberries
Dipping feet in clear water, whispering love
Our love was passionate like strawberries, we said
"Rhwa" (로화), she was my love.

Gone now is the love of those old days
Gone as a pure maiden.
When I wander alone in the camellia shade
Only the water mill thumps, pounding my heart
And unfamiliar girls energetically do laundry.
The streamside strawberry vines are trampled by calves
And the sun sets on the slopes of Chatbong Mountain (잣봉산)
"Rhwa" (로화), she was my love.

Nostalgia (鄕愁)

Ah—how I miss my hometown
Autumn when the ripe fields ripple like waves
When I see the yellow fields and the deep blue sky
I think only of my hometown.

Medicinal water in the mountain valleys
A blue river in front of the village
Old days of floating a boat on the river and catching fish
My hometown was this beautiful.
Here without mountains<247>
In this land of muddy water
The wandering traveler's body is lonely.
Now, the moon rising from the Ch'usŏk (秋夕)-moonless horizon
My heart is just restless at the sound of wild dogs barking in North Manchuria (北滿).

Even the birds flying in the hometown sky, the beasts crawling on the ground
Would be dreaming sweet dreams in their warm homes now
But like a sold slave
Like a driven-out bird, this body is sorrowful.

Rooftops red with drying peppers
Blue gourds hang like treasures on vines
When the sound of the pounding mill echoes
Those welcoming this autumn, this Ch'usŏk
Ah—how many are there in my hometown?

Though no one told me to go, why couldn't I live if I didn't leave?
The fields are ripe and yellow, why couldn't I live without starving?
Like parting from a beloved lover
It's already ten years since I left my hometown.

Is it just my body?
Village maidens too left shedding tears
Village young men too resented the hometown and ran away.
My dear hometown is also rustic.

The Ch'usŏk moon caught on sorghum tassels
Wild geese strolling by the sleeping lakeside
Now, the sound of fulling cloth will be heard far away
Ah—how I miss this hometown of mine!

Warrior of Haral (하랄의 勇士)

Warrior of Haral, oh young boy soldier
I see the rifle resting on your arm, dry like a stockfish.

The tattered clothes on your body
Your sparse hair, like an orphan
You are receiving instruction from a regular soldier.

I think<248>
About the beating blood in your chest
Trying to ignite the chaotic battlefield with the flame of righteousness

Oh, young boy soldier!
Warrior of Haral, leaving beloved parents
For the battlefield
For your small life, unknown to you!
Suddenly, hot tears welled up in my eyes.

But even if flocks of eagles fly above your head
And the wind of poison gas seems to reach before you
Let there be victory!
Let there be no bending!
Oh, young Warrior of Haral!<249>

From the Poetry Collection 『Punhyang』 (焚香 - Burning Incense)

Yi Ch'an (李燦)

The editor requested 4 or 5 of the best pieces from the recently published "Punhyang" (焚香), but this is as difficult as judging the merits of one's own children, and even if I were to actually select them, it's questionable whether they would meet public consensus. Therefore, I intend here just to fill the number of pieces as my hand leads and attach brief impressions to each—.
"Tea Room Elise" was said by several literary friends in Seoul (在京) at the time of its publication in "Chogwang" (朝光) to be a realistic expression of our circumstances back then—so I suppose it is. "Melancholy" (憂愁) truly murmured my own extremely gloomy feelings, as eloquently stated by the couplet, "If there were no flower fragrance remaining to guard this night / Who could stop the heart of the mole digging graves?" "Gravestone" (墓碑) lamented the shallow human feelings that have recently penetrated deeper into the marrow. "Hope" (希望) expressed the beauty of that which cannot be abandoned even in the weary dreams of a dark night when the path is lost. Finally, "Playing House" (소꼽노리) is a poem memorable even as a memento of that period when I calmly observed one aspect of womanhood through my own poignant experience.

Tea Room Elise

Frustration with nowhere to vent
Becomes smoke

Midori, Cocoa
Becomes smoke

Tea Room Elise—

Tonight too, you are<250>
Milky white like the forehead of a newly made-up bride.

Light-lost chandelier
Even so, you have nothing particular to be melancholic about.

Our beloved poet's head is heavy, heavy
It's long since he lost the habit of gazing up in rapture.

Only the table cover, shiny with handprints, is hateful.
Why is it reflecting his time so futilely and nervously?

Oh lovely waitress
Isn't a lullaby good on a night like this?

That sweet and ticklish melody
That languid and cozy reverberation

Let it rather call sleep
Call sleep—

Outside it's December, the road is long—
Our poet hates the cold most of all.

Melancholy (憂愁)

On a night when melancholy settles like fog
Even the distant village lamp sobs, weeping
The sound of the stream flowing listlessly
Forms rapid after rapid in my heart—

Fresh green May, weeping willow, slender branch
What are you searching for, dipping your hand in this place?
Only cold stone and sand brush past dismissively...
No way to even touch the back of a soft loach

Ah, if there were no flower fragrance remaining to guard this night
Who could stop the heart of the mole digging graves?
On the threshold path cluttered with footprints
A shabby grave stood trembling.

Gravestone (墓碑)<251>

In these disrespectful times when flies dare to mate on one's nose tip
The grass and trees grown thick in the cemetery have further lost reverence.

Beneath the midsummer heat, shunned even by birds of passage
Oh gravestone, standing gloomily, in what sorrow are you immersed?

As futile time silently flows over your tilted stone cap
Even the cozy fragrance of remembrance that sometimes seeped into your heart
Has stealthily hidden its trace

Ah, do you only now contemplate, in the infinite emptiness
That poignant affection of the transient human world?

Hope (希望)

Hope is dragonfly wings
Whose every edge burns towards the sky.

Dragonfly wings
That lift even the sun high into the blue sky.

Dragonfly wings
That cannot be folded and put away even in weary dreams on a dark, pathless night.

Ah, dragonfly wings that may lose life in the ordeal of the spiderweb
But leave themselves spread open.

Therefore
Dragonfly wings are beautiful.

Playing House (소꼽노리)

Enjoying playing house
Seems to be a woman's innate nature.

Unable to forget even after growing up
That pointless mischief

Here too lies a fragment of a hand-stained clay vessel
Played with and then abandoned without a second thought.
- From the Poetry Collection Punhyang (焚香) -<252>

**Summary**

This article from the November 1938 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* presents self-selected poems by five prominent Korean poets of the era: Im Hwa, Kim Kwang-sŏp, Cho Pyŏg-am, Pak Se-yŏng, and Yi Ch'an. Each poet introduces a small selection of their work, often from published collections like Kim Kwang-sŏp's *Tonggyŏng*, Im Hwa's *Hyŏnhaet'an*, Cho Pyŏg-am's *Hyangsu*, Pak Se-yŏng's *San chebi*, and Yi Ch'an's *Punhyang*. They provide brief commentary on the poems' origins, themes (ranging from nature, love, nostalgia, and melancholy to philosophical reflections and artistic struggle), and personal significance. The collection offers insights into the poets' creative processes and preoccupations during a challenging period. For historians, the article showcases the continuation of vibrant Korean literary production under Japanese rule, reflecting both personal lyricism and subtle engagement with broader themes of displacement (nostalgia for home, references to Manchuria), hardship, and resilience (e.g., Pak Se-yŏng's "Warrior of Haral"). The poets' reflections reveal their individual voices and aesthetic concerns within the constraints of their time.

詩集 「憧憬」에서
金珖燮

自選詩愚話
멀니 게신 벗에게서나 혹은 알지 못하는 讀者에게서 오는 편지를 보면 좃타는 것이 제각기 각도가 달라서 흥미있지만 자기 스스로 골라서 어느 것이 좃타고 하기는 퍽 어리석은 일 갓다.
여기 몇 篇 골라 내인 것은 너무 길지 않은 것으로 몇 개의 傾向을 表하고 싶은 것들이다.



感情이 몹시 맑은 날이 있다. 그런 때면 詩人의 눈은 아름다운 自然을 내다보는 작은 창과 갓다. 밤새끗 비가 오면 窓은 열 수 없다. 하나 아츰 개이면 自然은 淸新하야 이 窓에 산 듯이 빗친다.
어느 날 아츰 비가 개여서 들 안에 나서 하늘을 처다 보다가 나의 집 작은 못가에 섯드니 하늘이 못 속에 나려온 듯 하였고 버들 닢사귀가 덮힌 못 속에서 綠陰을 깔고 작은 금붕어들이 경쾌히 쏘아 단인다. 律動이 樂譜같으나 나의 눈에는 自然詩를 그리는 것 갓해서 다음 五行詩의 感覺이 되였다.

비개인 여름 아츰

비가 개인 날
맑은 하늘이 못 속에 나려와서
여름 아츰을 일우었으니
綠陰이 조희가 되야
금붕어가 詩를 쓴다.



時調의 形式이 너무 고집스럽고 不自由해서 나는 좀더 자유로운 短詩를 맨들고 싶었다.
어느 옛날일지는 몰라도 내게는 귀여운 사랑스러운 사람 하나가 있었다. 눈이 펄펄 쏘다지는 人跡없는<230> 거리를 거닐었다. 軟한 눈송이가 그의 머리 우에 하염없이 보드러히 덮인다. 나는 그 눈송이에 많은 로-맨스를 썼다. 집에 도라온 열두시 새가 난 듯한 寂寞이 뒤나무가지에 나리는 나의 神經에 나린다. 눈(眼)이 「아폴로-의」겁에 그리운 情과 외로움이 가득찻다. 詩人 詩한 句節 없을 수 없어서 쓴 短詩가 이것이다.

로-맨쓰

눈송이에 글을 써서
머리카락에 날린 밤 도라온 길
고요히 寂寞이 흘러서
「아폴로-」의 컵에 香愁가 차다.



어느 고요하고 따슷한 봄날 東海바다를 끼고 車가 달었다. 東海바다는 나의 마음이요, 나의 故鄕이다. 恒久한 神秘가 있는 먼 地平線과 푸른 바다의 接境에 날어 가고 싶었다. 나는 갈매기가 되였다. 푸른 바다를 보며 나의 意識은 날었다. 바위에 부다치는 푸른 바다의 힌 물결 너무도 시언해서 그만 가든 길을 일코 물 속에 빠젔으니 바다가 하도 깊어서 빠저서 빠저서 들어갓다. 海底에서 타는 珊瑚가 캐고 싶지 않으랴. 다이빙하는 姿勢로 나의 意識은 바다 속에서 탓다. 直觀을 가진 意識의 飛翔이 있으니 이 詩에는 나의 精神의 律動이 있다.

珊瑚 캐러가다

갈매기 나래를 어더
푸른 東海바다를 날다.

바다가 안긴 하늘까 地平線
永遠히 秘藏된 世界로 向하다가

바위에 부다치는 힌 물 속으로
나는 빠저서 빠저서 들어간다.

하늘도 푸르고 바다도 푸르고
물결도 푸르고 마음도 푸른 날

하늘과 땅과 바다의 얘기를 따라 가든 길<231>
나는 힌 머루에 빠저서 빠저서 珊瑚캐러 간다.



크리스마스 밤 동무가 준 한 송이 카-네이슌이 차츰 시들어가며 나에게 날마다 하는 말들이 있었다. 않윽한 마음을 가지랴든 어느 날 저녁 나는 이 한 송이 꽃으로 더부러 맛치 靑春과 愛와 美에 대한 대화를 한 일이 잇다. 거기에는 疑心도 있었고 希望도 있었고 憧憬도 있었고 恍忽도 있었고 혹시는 失意도 있었다. 나종에는 빛없는 바람이 지나가는 곳에서 나는 머리를 땅에 숙이는 해바래기 같이 고개를 숙이고 祈禱할 마음을 가젓다. 그래서 「黃昏」은 내가 가장 사랑하는 詩篇이며 凡常한 제목에 고은 靑春의 意炎을 짜고 싶었다. 하나 가장 아름다운 것일수록 내가 가장 完成을 기대하며 가장 완성을 하지 못함을 어쩌랴. 靑春이 황당하야 抒情많에 끄칠 수 없어 약간의 知性을 넣었다.

黃昏

왼 하늘의 光明이 西쪽 山머리에 기우러저 타고 난 다음 구름에서 떠러진 그림자가 숲에서 밀려오는 때쯤 하면 나의 床 우엔 한 송이 꽃이 빨간 입술을 옴치리고 炎炎히 한 나라를 태우는 情景이 드러남니다.

無限한 生에의 意念이 白日을 지나온 한 나절 엘지라도
이제 여기 光明이 黃迷한 자리에 앉었으면
슬픈 말들이 드노니 이것은 香料를 求하는 섬
밤은 밤마다 이렇게 저물어 가나봄니다.

이 한송의 꽃이 암만해도 시들어 버릴 說法을 나아가 求함이 않일지로되
아름다움은 나종에 그 마른 입술을 열어 무슨 말로 눈물을 진이리끼.
하늘과 땅의 驚異가 이 한송이에 모였든 날을 위하야 또한 무슨 말이 남으리까.
또한 어데서 우리들은 사랑의 聖스런 글을 읽으리까.
뭇노니 「美」는 말뿐입니까 그러면 말은 모도 다 숨으리다.
뭇노니 「愛」는 슬픈 來日입니까 그러면 來日은 모도 다 오지 않으랴 하리다.
푸른 하늘 봄바람 별과 달 외로운 해
이 永續性이 모아서 작은 한송이를 피우지 않었읍니까.<232>

아, 不遠에 모도 다 時間의 둥이에서 떠러질 우리들이 한 송이꽃을 어떻게 삼가 아껴야 하릿까.
한숨에 끌려서 아름다웁던 몇일을 誇張하야 靑春의 光榮으로 삼으릿까.
꽃이 지는 날 깊은 香愁는 땅에 짐니다.

해를 따라 얼골을 돌리던 하낫 해바래기는 이제 志向을 땅에 숙입니다.
해가 저서 빛 없는 바람이 지나갑니다.
溫床에 떠러저서 하낫 생각에 뭇치리까.
아. 燦爛한 驚異가 어델 흘러 갑니까 黃昏이 오면 밤은 밤과 다 이렇게 저물어 가나 봄니다.



밤을 바로 자지 못하는 밤이 내게는 몹시 많다. 웨 이렇게 무슨 때문에 이렇게 잠을 빼았기고 잠을 일코 밤중에 뜰 안에 나서며 혹은 翠雲亭 山길로 퍼덕 퍼덕하고 단이는지 나도 몰을 일이다. 나의게는 마음 속에 올뱀이가 살고 있었다. 그것이 나 自身의 表象 갓했다. 잠입는 둥그런 눈이 곳 나의 눈이였고 올뱀이 눈이었다. 그 눈을 뜨고 목을 축이랴고 샘물터에 가서 白日에 秘藏해 놓은 샘물을 마시니 그것은 詩의 샘갓기도 했다. 이 한마리의 意味한 새에도 大自然의 言語가 있으나 모도 다 沈黙되였고 나아가 光明한 白日이 失明케까지 되였으니 여기에 더 큰 懷疑가 있고 不安이 있다. 이것은 나의 表象詩이요 거기에는 不幸이라는 世界가 있으니 요담에는 나는 가마귀의 옷을 입고 世上을 表象化해 볼 準備의 詩다.



말 없는 世界 永遠히 파뭇친 나라가 있어서 밤에 자리를 거두는 그대는 어두운 寢床에서 門을 열고 밤의 아들이 되야 그 어구에서
不安한 눈 疑惑 많은 귀를 기우려 空寞한 大地에 時間을 헤이느냐.

이윽고 寂寞의 末梢에서 퍼덕여 그대 人跡 없는 깊은 묏골에 나려와
무거운 입을 들어 작은 門을 쫏고 한목음 샘을 먹음<233> 地下의 神話로 목을 축이나니 샘물을 永永 어둠으로 흘은다.

太古의 모는 闇焦이 그대의 가슴에 緊縮되였고
原始의 모든 黙想이 그대의 샘 속에 陰居되였으니
작은 가슴 한줄기 샘 속에 大自然의 夜話가 敍述되였느냐.

아, 永遠한 밤의 神秘가 表現을 希望하야 그대의 肉體를 어든 날

노스탈지-의 氷河

拙詩集 「鄕愁」를 出版하야 어느 친구에게 한 卷 보냈드니 어찌하야 序文도 없느냐는 片紙가 왔었다. 나는 오래동안이나 그 答狀 쓰기를 躊躇하다가 어짠지 그 말이 잊어지지를 안어 다음과 같이 써 보낸 일이 있다.
「赤裸한 人間價値를 把握하랴는 점에서 글쓰는 사람은 다른 일에 從事하는 사람의 立場과는 달다. 憧憬이란 언제든지 세상에 확실히 떠돌고 있으면서도 그것이 明瞭히 붓잡혀 지지 않는 데에 憧憬의 感想이 있고 표현이 있으며 人生의 苦憫과 哀愁가 있는 것이다. 나는 내가 한 일이면서 붓그러워는 할 망정 明確히 이유를 들어 自己辯明에 갓차운 序文 따위를 쓰기는 실타. 또 쓸것 같이도 역여지지 않는다. 그리하야 지지한 줄은 알면서도 그나마 것옷을 볏겨버리고 알몸 송두리채를 그 생김 생김대로 뵈이고 싶었기 때문이다.」
나는 이 같이 自己辯護와 自己批判을 하기 좋아하는 편이 아니라. 文學 전체에 있어서도 그러하려니와 특히 詩에 있어서는 더욱 그러하다. 그러나 요만한 것은 무관치 않을가 하고 붓을 들었다. 그때 그때의 生活深求 중에 거리낌없이 우러나오는 것을 如實히 써 온 것이 나의 태도이였기 때문이다.
적고 오즉지는 않으나마 거짓없이 率直히 人生의 眞意와 생활의 苦愁를 읍조리면 그만이라는 것이다. 그러한 平凡한 속에서 眞球같이 숨으 眞理를 차저 素朴하게 읍조리는 나의 姿態가 뵈여지게까지 된다면 만족일 따름이다.
創古에 뿌리를 박고 흘너나리는 人生의 시내 하나<240> 있어 언제든 倦怠의 쪼가리 하나를 띄우고 바다로 바다로 향하야 가는 도중 읏지 읏지 한게 제에 붑푸러 오른 詩想- 나는 이런 땔수록 孤獨하다. 旅簑에 疲困한 때다.
搔亂하고 雜된 巷中(生活)에서 떠나 孤寂의 星地를 더듬을 때 나는 英雄格으로 大膽히도 大自然을 호령이나 할 듯시 서들다가 짓처 도리혀 困憊하야 씨러지고 마는 無援孤愁에 사로잽히는 적이 많다.
이때이다. 쫄쫄히 따러단이며 봇채는 鄕愁는 나를 안타갑게도 괴롭힌다. 이것이 노스탈지-의 氷河에 떠도는 詩의 境地이다.
나의 拙詩集 「鄕愁」 속에 실인 것들도 다 이러한 냄새를 겻들인 것들이며 지금 이 곳에 몇 편 抄한 것도 그러한 同族들의 일부이다. 나는 언제든 수수꺾이의 瓦片을 손에다 놓고 沈黙의 森林 속에서 왼갓 얘기를 졸러 보는 어리석은 者가 아닐가?

「산제비」는 내가 俗離山에 갔을 때 쓴 것이다. 그때 上庫寺에서 하로밤을 자고 나는 새벽녁에 눈이 띄여젔다. 여름이것만 제법 찬 기운이 방안에 서리고 冷冷하였다. 조그만 庵子에 홀로이 사는 僧은 우리들의 아츰을 지여주느라고 벌서 깬 모양이다. 덧문을 열고 庭園에 나가보니 屛風같이 둘린 俗離連峰의 파노라마! 희기도 하고 붉기도 하고 黙黙이 거뭇도 하야 그 燦然한 빛이란 恒用 田野에서 바라보는 그것과는 비교가 않된다. 내 몸이 한숨에 날려가 앉고 싶기도 하였다. 나는 朝飯을 먹고 다시 文壯臺를 향하야 올라가는 것이다. 했볓을 볼라야 볼 수 없는 密林, 마치 原始林 사이로 얼마를 가도 한이 없고 길섬헤는 곳곳이 붉을 흙을 파헷처 봣다. 이것은 묏도야지의 所爲라고 한다. 아람들이 나무가 척척 쓰러진 채로 버섯냄새를 풍기는 것이다. 대낫이 넘어서야 艱辛히 文壯臺에 올났다.
이 山줄기로부터 西으로 흘러 錦江이 되고 南으로 흘러 洛東江이 되었다. 그야말로 天下가 아믈아믈하고 江이 실오래기처럼 굽어 흐른다. 때에 空中에서 自由로히 날러 헤매는 「山제비」들은 나로 하여금 졸라매진 가슴을 탁 풀어주는 것 같었다.
저이들은 내가 처음 느낀 바를 언제나 느낄 것이요, 現實이란 桎梏에서 한 거름 올라가 眞理를 探究하는 것이나 무었이 다르랴 싶어다.
그리하야 遠大한 理想의 實現을 憧憬하고 나가는 자도 있으며 暗黑한 密林 속의 慓悍한 猛獸의 橫行을 對照해 볼 때에 얼마나 「山제비」의 生活은 明朗하고 崇高한가를 상상케 된다. 나는 나의 생활에 있어서서도 이렇듯 숭고한 무었을 가저 보리라고<244> 굳은 信念을 가지고 있는 것이다.
悲歌, 딸기 넝쿨이 시냈가에 덮이였고 동백나무 그늘이 으슥이 진 곳에서 마을의 처녀와 나는 純眞한 이야기를 한 時節이 있었다. 寂寞한 山村에 물소리만이 쫄쫄 흐르고 이따금 山새소리가 귓전을 울린다. 나의 氣分은 무었을 呼訴하는 것 같이 되고 손에 드렀든 딸기도 눈에 어리기만 하는 듯이 보이지 않있다.
시내물에 발 잠그고 純情을 쏫든 그 時節 어느 듯 6年이 지나간 뒤에 나는 다시금 시골에 도라왔다.
나의 가슴에 매듭을 매여 준 그 處女는 멀리 흘러 갓다는 말도 있고 그리하야 永永이 갔다는 말도 있다. 때에 나의 느낌이란 모든 것이 憂鬱해젔고 너무나 애틋해젔다. 지나간 날 그와의 시절을 생각함에 무슨 寶物을 잊은 듯이 서운해지고 나의 生의 半을 헐어간 것과 갔다.
바라봄에 동백나무 그늘은 여전하것만 그와 앉어 노든 곳은 송아치가 짓밟어 버리고 낯모를 處女들이 빨래만 하는 것이다. 그러고 쿵쿵 울리는 물방아 소리는 이내 가슴을 쿵쿵 찢는 것을 느끼였다.
鄕愁 옛 글에 秋士悲라 하였다. 어째 그런지 落葉 소리만 드러도 인생을 瞑想하게 되고 肅條한 기분에 마음은 불타는 것이다. 이럴 때 故鄕을 떠난 지 오랜 이들은 어찌 마음이 안타갑지 않으랴. 내가 滿洲는 간 일이 없으되 南北이 다르되 支那本部 역시 山 없는 푸른 大地에서 故鄕을 憧憬하야 마지 않은 때도 한 두번이 아닐 것이다.
흐린 물을 봄에 고향 생각 푸른 옷만 보며 내 고향의 옷이 보고 싶고 서투른 말 소리만 드르메 또한 내 고향의 말이 듯고 싶은 것이다.
새로 제 집이 그립다거든 어찌 나를 나어준 내 故鄕이랴? 나는 나의 感情을 쏘다 滿洲 벌판을 헤매이는 저들에게 이 느낌같이 가지려는 것이다.
「하랄의 勇士」
나는 初夏의 街頭를 지나다가 문득 진열장을 보았다. 나의 시선을 있그는 장면 나는 그 헐벗은 어린 勇士에게 머리를 숙이게 되는 것이였다.
그 팔뚝이라니 보잘 것 없는 내 팔뚝보다 더 야위고 瘦瘠한 그가 鬪志에 볼타는 마음으로 멀리 戰場을 志向하고 있는 모양 그러나 한편 말할 수 없는 느낌이 스스로 내 머리를 지나갈 때 저윽이 인생의 裏面이 얼른거리여 모르는 中여 내 눈도 얼른 얼른거리는<245> 것이였다. 그리하여 나는 이 붓을 든 것이다.
