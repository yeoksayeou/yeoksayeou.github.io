Recollections and Memoirs

The Short-Lived Na Tohyang

An Sŏgyŏng

Though it was long ago, thinking of it now feels like yesterday, and one cannot help but feel the utter transience of human affairs.
It feels as though Tohyang is still in this world, yet his soul must surely laugh at this unfaithful friend who does not even know where his remains lie. He who had much affection also had many tears. It must also be sorrowful that no one thinks of him after he departed; even if life is such that one simply leaves after living in this world, how he must lament the unkindness of people.
When he was in the world, he loved everyone, and especially for those who fell into pitiful fates, his pen did not rest, yet this world has forgotten him.
It thinks of him emptily.
Even when loving a woman, he was sorrowful; even when serving friends, he was sorrowful; even his parents did not let him into the house, so he was one who was born into the world, lived solitarily, and departed. He laughed often, but it was laughter in place of tears; he liked songs, but it was to overcome all suffering. Why did he like suffering and sorrow?<85> Not that he liked it, but why was there so much suffering and sorrow for him? This was because he was too intelligent, and for that reason too, he departed this world early.
His piece "Hwanhŭi" (幻戱) depicted his own short life, and "17 wŏn 50 chŏn" (17원 50전) depicted human sorrow. That he viewed this world too much only through tears hastened his demise.
Even when playing with friends back then, he would laugh alone and also shed tears alone, which was him foretelling his own short future. He said he was the most unfortunate person in this world, and also boasted of being the greatest genius.

Now, Tohyang's broad face appears before my eyes – that image comes to mind. That face, which always looked at me, is still clearly visible now.
Finding it hard to bear looking at that sorrowful face, I open the window, and the autumn sky appears. The autumn sky that Tohyang liked –
That autumn sky, he used to say, holds sorrowful music.
I followed that music. The five grains ripened and bowed their heads. Also, the sound of Tohyang's footsteps seems to come carried on the wind.
Tohyang, standing with his cane, meditating, stood beside me.
— If I had just painted pictures, I would have become a Millet...
When his artistic desire surged, he would stand tall, gaze at nature, and whisper to himself. Even when looking at a woman, if he wanted to put her into his work, he had a habit of staring embarrassingly.
— How pitiful. Why do women look only pitiful? Autumn will come upon that beautiful woman's body. And she will become remains (觸* - likely 屍). And she will go into the earth –
Tohyang, who was too sentimental – had he lived until now, he would have changed greatly. Entering his prime, he would have produced robust literature, but because he matured too early and achieved fame too early, there is a feeling that he spent a person's entire life in one morning and one night.
Though I still think of Tohyang as a friend, I do not love his sentimental works. Perhaps it is because I have already aged, know people, and know what art should be.
However, completing what Tohyang left unfinished is also up to me, and knowing that Tohyang wished for humans to be good, I too know I must be so. That is Tohyang's legacy.
Still looking upon this world with open eyes, Tohyang, close your eyes and rest peacefully.<86>

The Much-Regretted Yun Simdŏk

Yi Sŏgu

Yun Simdŏk Ŏnni!
The sky is high, the moonlight is clear, and the cold wind blows the leaves from the trees, making thoughts of old acquaintances well up.
I used to jokingly call you Ŏnni. Therefore, in this writing today, I intend to call you Ŏnni. Since you passed away without refusing "Ŏnni," I trust you will have no objection.
For a man to call a woman "Ŏnni" sounds quite out of place, and moreover, for me, older than you, who was young, to call you "Ŏnni" might seem nonsensical, but anyway, between you and me.
"Ŏnni"
"Tongseng (younger sibling)"
We got along extremely naturally like this, so what reason would there be not to be "Ŏnni"?

Ŏnni, when you returned from Tonggyŏng (Tokyo) and said you would act in plays, I, as a newspaper reporter, met you often. When you appeared in the T'owŏrhoe (토월회), as a member of the T'owŏrhoe, I also met you often. And when you went to make your final recordings at Nitto (日東) Records, as a member of the literature department, I worked alongside you. Thus, during the most turbulent period of your short half-life, your Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) life, I almost always followed you around like a shadow!
Ŏnni!
When you and I were deciding the tracklist for the Nitto Records recording, you never mentioned, not even in your dreams, recording "Saui Ch'anmi" (死의 讚美 - Praise of Death), did you?
Playing innocent, you went all the way to Osaka (大阪) and hastily deceived the head office literature staff, who didn't know Korean, and secretly recorded "Saui Ch'anmi," didn't you?
Some time after you passed away, when the test pressing (試聽盤) of the records you recorded came out, we finally heard "Saui Ch'anmi" and wept once again.
So, it really was a planned love suicide (情死).
"The plan (프란) for a love suicide existed from the time she left Tonggyŏng."
Such talk arose. I was not the only one who cried upon hearing this "Saui Ch'anmi."<87> Mr. Yi Kise (李基世) cried, his wife cried, I cried, and Mr. Chang Yŏngsun (張英涥), who was part of our group (크릅), also cried.

Ŏnni!
Even thinking about it now, it’s vivid before my eyes. In a room on the third floor of the Okuda Shashinkan (奧田写真館) in Suŭnjŏng (授恩町), you and Mr. Kim Ujin (金우鎭) spent time discussing the empty affairs of the world, occasionally opening the south window, the two of you sitting side-by-side looking out at the street – that image is still vivid before my eyes. Perhaps now, you two are opening a window in heaven and looking down side-by-side upon this Sea of Suffering (苦海).
Ŏnni!
Do you remember playing the protagonist in "Tongdo" (東道) on the T'owŏrhoe stage?
Deceived by a fake shaman (거즛 사당), entering into a false marriage unacceptable to the spirits, the protagonist carrying the seed of sin unknown to her father – she is ultimately chased out of the world. Didn't you cry on stage, as if it were your own fate, embodying the tragic destiny of "Anna" who ran out even into the great river where drifting ice broke lives?

Ŏnni, you viewed the world too simply.
A young lady like a naive girl (선모슴) just out of girls' school! The world watched your actions with interest, perhaps, but did not view them fondly. Not only that.
"Madwoman (밋치괭이)"
"Debauchee (난봉패)"
Such malicious criticisms were thrown at you, and I believe your pure heart was ultimately trampled by those who did not know how to respect women, those who thought they could do anything as they pleased just because they had money.

Ŏnni, who graduated from the Ueno Music School on a government scholarship and returned home in glory, becoming a girls' school teacher – the dark shadows targeting that Ŏnni.
Ŏnni!
You already had your lover (애인) Kim Ujin in Tonggyŏng, who intended to even die with you, so what were you lacking that you sought a second lover?
Ŏnni!
I know well the painful story of this second lover of yours. Ŏnni, you had a "dream." To establish a music academy, gather many talented individuals (才操軍), and raise the spirit of musical Chosŏn – a great ambition!
Ŏnni, you were not a meek woman content to teach do-re-mi-fa... to girls in uniform in the shadow of a blackboard. In short, you were not a bureaucratic, static figure, but a political, dynamic one. Therefore, you always set great goals for the future<88> and cherished a "shining dream."
Ŏnni!
You were too hasty in realizing this dream. That's why you went seeking patrons (파토돈) with world-unaccustomed steps. Capitalists of the world, encountering Ŏnni, a blooming young musician who especially enjoyed dressing herself splendidly, desired Ŏnni's shapely bosom, which held those ideals, more than the ideals themselves. Ŏnni's life after running away from school was like a breached treasure chest. You set up house (살님) in Hwanggŭmjŏng (黃金町), but did a new household without heart satisfy you? You tried throwing away a fur coat... but did that truly soothe your wounded heart? Unable to endure, you ran off to Harbin (할빈), didn't you?

Ŏnni!
How long could a modern young woman, having run away from the lecture hall, sustain herself under the lamplights of Harbin, passing the time with cocktails (칵렡) and jazz (짜쓰)? Drink after drink, the alcohol didn't intoxicate; laugh after laugh, the un-sorrowful life offered no joy. Ŏnni must have surely faced "Death" at this time. Therefore, your love suicide with your lover (愛*) Kim Ujin on the Toksu Maru in the Hyŏnhae Sea (玄海) can be seen as the second chance. You intended to die, but perhaps you couldn't bring yourself to die without seeing Kim Ujin again.
Ŏnni!
More than anything, you were born at the wrong time. Nowadays, whether one wears bright Western clothes, has bobbed hair, or walks around with bare calves, the world, having become tolerant, wouldn't even glance sideways, but back then, it was an old era. Just wearing high-heeled shoes caused trouble. In such circumstances, dressing colorfully and dashing about noisily (왈가닥 달가닥), how could rumors about you possibly be good? Here, Ŏnni, you became nothing more than a spectacle among the heartless crowds of the world.
Ŏnni!
Here I once again deeply lament that you were born in the most unfortunate era. In the midst of ladies who, though perhaps rotten inside, pretended to have the weight of a thousand catties, engaging in all sorts of irresponsible acts behind the scenes but having to act demurely in public, only Ŏnni jumped and played as she pleased. How could you be accepted in a society that practiced deception? By the time you realized the vast gap between what your heart desired and the ways of the world, everything was already<89> ruined – how regrettable it is, and how resentful you must have been.

Ŏnni!
I later heard that you recorded "Saui Ch'anmi" while weeping. There may be countless singers who have recorded sad songs in the world, but you are probably the first in this world to have decided to die and then sung
"Saui Ch'anmi"
and recorded it on a record. A song sung with the determination to die –
Listening to the lines sung like "When I die, that's the end," the feeling of weeping while singing resonates in the listener's heart. I still have this record. Tonight too, to write this manuscript, I deliberately put on one track and am writing now as if squeezing my heavy heart.

Ŏnni!
When you were leaving Kyŏngsŏng Station, I was at the platform head:
Me: "My desired gift is a necktie."
Ŏnni: "A necktie isn't difficult, but if I die on the way, I can't bring it."
Me: "If you're going to die, buy it there, send it, and then die."
Ŏnni: "Well, that's not difficult."
Wasn't it this kind of joke? Only later did I realize that even these words hinted at a fragment of your resolve. Was that all? Just as you said, you bought the necktie, sent it, and then departed this world.

Two or three days after hearing the sad news that you and Kim Ujin had committed a love suicide by throwing yourselves into the Hyŏnhae Sea, the necktie you bought in Osaka and sent finally arrived, and the entire staff of the Nitto Chosŏn Literature Department shed tears twice over. Cheerful and sensitive Ŏnni, though more than 10 years have already passed since you left this world, stories about you still linger. Please, take care of yourself in heaven, and ensure there are no mistakes a second time. 13, 10, 12.<90>

**Summary**

These two memoirs from 1938 offer poignant reflections by friends on the short, tragic lives of prominent Korean cultural figures: writer Na Tohyang and singer Yun Simdŏk. An Sŏgyŏng remembers Na Tohyang's profound sensitivity, melancholy, artistic passion, early death, and the lingering sense of loss and forgotten legacy. Yi Sŏgu provides a more intimate, detailed account of Yun Simdŏk's life, recalling her vibrant personality, artistic ambitions (including her famous recording of "Saui Ch'anmi"), societal struggles as a "new woman" clashing with conservative norms, her relationship with Kim Ujin, and their eventual double suicide. For historians, these pieces offer valuable insights into the personal struggles and psychological burdens faced by artists in colonial Korea, the restrictive social environment, particularly for women challenging conventions like Yun Simdŏk, and the cultural atmosphere of Seoul (Kyŏngsŏng) in the 1920s and 30s, including glimpses into the worlds of literature, theatre (T'owŏrhoe), and the early recording industry (Nitto Records).

夭折한, 羅稻香

安夕影
퍽 오래된 옛 일이라 해도 지금 생각하면 어젯일 같해서 사람의 일이 너무도 덧없음을 느끼지 않을 수 없다.
稻香이 지금도 이 세상에 있는 것 갈으되 그 유해나마 어느 곳에 있는지 모르는 이 信實치 못한 벗을 그의 영혼은 얼마나 우스랴. 정이 많으니 눈물도 많은 그다. 그가 간 뒤에 그를 생각하는 이 없음이 또한 슲을 것이니 이 세상에 살다가 가면 그만인 인생이라 해도 얼마나 사람이 착하지 못함을 한탄할 것이냐.
그가 세상에 있을 때 모든 사람을 사랑했고 더구나 가엾은 운명에 떠러진 자를 위하야 붓을 쉬지 않었으나 이 세상은 그를 이젔도다.
그를 허잘 것 없이 생각하는도다.
여인과 사랑을 해도 그는 슲었고 벗에게 이바지해도 슲었고 어버이도 그를 집에 드리지 않었었으니 세상에 나서 쓸쓸히 살다 간 그다. 그는 우숨이 많었으나 눈물 대신 우슨 것이요 그는 노래를 좋아했으나 모든 괴롬을 익이고자 한 바다. 웨, 그는 괴로움과 슲음을 좋아했는가.<85>  좋아한 것이 아니라 웨 그에게 괴로움과 슲음이 많었든고. 이것은 그가 너무도 총명해서 그렇고 또한 그런 까닭에 일즉이 이 세상을 떠난 바다.
그의 「幻戱」일편은 그 자신의 짧은 일생을 그린 것이요 「17원 50전」은 사람의 슲음을 그린 것이라. 그가 너무도 이 세상을 눈물로만 본 것이 그의 목슴을 재촉한 것이다.
그 때 벗들과 놀 때에도 혼자 우스면서 또한 혼자 눈물을 흘린 것이 그가 자기의 짜른 압날을 점친 바 있었음이니 그는 이 세상에서 자기가 가장 불행한 사람이다 하였고 또 가장 천재인 것을 자랑하였다.

지금 내 눈앞에 稻香의 넙쭉한 얼골 - 그 모습이 떠오른다. 항상 나를 바라보든 그 얼골, 그것이 지금도 뚜렸이 보힌다.
나는 그 슲은 얼골을 참아 바라보기 어려워 창문을 여니 가을 하늘이 보인다. 稻香이 좋아하는 가을 하늘 -
저 가을 하늘에는 슲은 음악이 흐른다고 하든 그 가을 하늘.
나는 그 음악을 따라 들어섰다. 오곡이 익어 머리를 숙이었다. 또한 稻香의 발자최 소리가 바람결에 들러 오는 것 같다.
단장 집고 서서 명상하는 稻香이 내 곁에 섰다.
- 나는 그림이나 그리였드면 밀려 - 나 되는 것을...
그는 예술적인 의욕이 복밧치면 그는 웃득이 서서 자연을 바라보고 혼자 속삭이는 것이다. 여자를 볼 때도 그 여자를 자기 작품에 집어넣고 싶으면 면구하도록 바라보는 버릇이 있었다.
- 가엾어라. 여자는 어째서 가엾이만 보히나. 저 어엽분 여자의 육체 우헤 가을이 오겠지. 그리고 觸*가 되겠지. 그리고 흙 속으로 가겠지 -
너무도 애상적이였든 稻香 - 지금까지 그가 사렀드라면 몹시 변하였을 것이요. 장년기에 드렀을 그는 건실한 문학을 낳을 것이로되 그가 너무도 조숙했고 너무도 일즉 출세를 하였기 때문에 그는 사람의 일생을 하로 아츰과 하로 밤으로 보낸 느낌이 잇다.
내가 稻香을 벗으로 지금도 생각하되 그의 애상적인 작품은 사랑치 안는다. 벌서 내가 나희가 먹고 사람을 알고 예술은 어떠해야 한다는 것을 안 까닭일 것이다.
그러나 稻香이 하다가 간 그것을 완성하는 것도 내게 있고 稻香이 인간을 착하기를 바라든 것도 또한 나도 그러해야 할 것을 안다. 稻香의 유업이 그것이다.
지금도 눈을 뜨고 이 세상을 바라다보고 稻香 눈을 감고 편안히 쉬라.<86>

多恨한 尹心悳

李瑞求
尹心悳 언니!
하늘은 높고 달빛은 맑아 찬바람이 나무잎을 날이니 옛 사람 생각이 소사 오롬니다.
나는 당신더러 농담 삼아 언니라고 불넜읍니다. 그럼으로 오늘 이 글에도 당신을 언니라고 불느랴고 합니다. 「언니」를 물느지 않고 그대로 세상을 떠나갔으니 이의는 없으실 줄 압니다.
남자가 여자보고 「언니」라는 말이 몹시, 동에 닷지 않고 더욱히 나가 어린 당신 나 많은 내가 「언니」라 부르는 게 당치도 않은 소리 같었으나 어쨋든 당신과 나 사히에는.
「언니」
