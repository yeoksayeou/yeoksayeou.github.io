"Chaplin Appears in Court" - Sued by Female Secretary

On December 1st, Ch'wap'ŭring (Chaplin) appeared at the Westminster Commercial Court (商業法院公判廷) in Rondon (London). The reason was that his female secretary, Miss Mei Sip'wadŭ (May Reeves), had filed a lawsuit (告訴) claiming that despite providing special service (싸-비스) during Ch'wap'ŭring's travels, he did not give the special compensation (1,000 won) she requested for it. Ch'wap'ŭring, looking gloomy (憂鬱) for about 15 minutes, agreed to pay the requested compensation and the litigation costs (訴訟費) as the plaintiff demanded.<82>

**Summary**

This short article reports on Charlie Chaplin appearing in a London court on December 1, 1931. He was sued by his female secretary, May Reeves, for unpaid compensation (1,000 won) related to "special services" provided during his travels. Chaplin ultimately agreed to pay the demanded sum and court costs. For historians, this piece reveals the reach of international celebrity news into colonial Korea and contemporary interest in Western legal proceedings and social affairs, even translating foreign currency amounts into Korean won for context.

「촤프링이 法政에 나타나」-여비서에게 소송되여서
12월1일에 론돈의 웨스트민스타- 商業法院公判廷에 촤프링이 낫타낫다. 그 원인은 氏의 여비서- 메이·시퐈-드 양이 촤프링이 여행하는 중에 특별한 싸-비스를 하여 주엇슴에도 불구하고 거기에 대해서 특별한 보수(1천원)을 청구햇는데 주지 안는다는 것으로 告訴하엿든 것이다. 촤프링은 한 15분간 憂鬱한 표정을 가지고 잇다가 원고의 요구대로 보수금과 訴訟費를 다- 갑하주기로 했다.<82>
