Learning Esperanto

What is the use of learning Esperanto (에쓰어)? If it is useful to us, please introduce a proper self-study book.
Yongt'ae Saeng (龍台 生)

What is the use of learning Esperanto (에쓰페란토어)? What is the use of telegraphs and telephones as means of communication? It is not something that requires debating whether it is necessary or not. One should learn Esperanto even just to be able to read with little time and effort. For a self-study book in Korean:
Kim Ŏk (金億) author, 『Esperanto Self-Taught (에쓰페란토 독학)』
Published by Hansŏng Toso Hoesa (漢城圖書會社), list price 1 wŏn 50 chŏn.
Ch'obyŏngjŏng (草兵丁)

Inquiry<98>

Where is Miss Yu Sinbang (柳新芳), who starred in "Pŏngŏri Samnyong (벙어리 三龍)," and what is she doing now? Is Miss Yi Aerisu (李愛利秀) of the theater market still unmarried? Ch'obyŏngjŏng (草兵丁) must answer!
Kanggu 1 Film Person (江口 1 映畵人)

Miss Yu Sinbang (柳新芳), who starred in 『Pŏngŏri Samnyong (벙어리 三龍)』, has left the film world. She is now at Kŭmgangsan (金剛山), having shaved her head to become a nun (爲僧), renounced the secular world, and is cultivating the path to enlightenment while chanting Buddhist prayers.
Miss Yi Aerisu (李愛利秀) is still making a name for herself as a star in the theater market, and rumor has it that she is no longer a Miss.
So'wŏl (笑月)

Motion Pictures

1. How many film companies have distributed films in Chosŏn (조선)?
2. Are motion picture dramas and live-action plays (實演劇) useful for social education?
3. Does the Chosŏn (조선) film industry have the potential to become more widespread in the future? Please instruct.
RCM in Aichi Prefecture (愛知縣)

1. Chosŏn Kinema (조선 키네마), Kyerim Yŏnghwa Hyŏphoe (鷄林映畵協會), Taegyŏng Yŏnghwa Sa (大京映畵社), Eksŭ Kinema (엑스키네마), Wŏnbanggak Sa (圓方角社).
2. Of course. The educational value of films is generally felt. In places like Russia, they are said to educate more through film. Live-action plays (實演劇) also have similar results.
3. Regardless of how one looks at it, Chosŏn (조선) film will see powerful dissemination in the future. Western films<99>

**Summary**

This article is a Q&A section from the January 1932 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine, answering reader inquiries. Questions cover the whereabouts of writer Yi Hyosŏk, the editorial staff of the Chungang Ilbo newspaper, the utility and study materials for Esperanto, the current status of actresses Yu Sinbang (star of "Pŏngŏri Samnyong") and Yi Aerisu, and the state and future prospects of the Korean film industry, including key production companies and the educational value of film and theater. The answers provide specific addresses, names, book recommendations, updates on celebrities (one becoming a nun, another rumored married), lists film companies, and affirms the educational potential and bright future for Korean cinema.

For historians, this piece offers a snapshot into the cultural interests and information networks of Koreans during the colonial period. It reveals curiosity about intellectuals (Yi Hyosŏk), media organizations (Chungang Ilbo), international trends (Esperanto), popular culture figures (actresses Yu, Yi), and the burgeoning domestic film industry. The specific details about individuals, publications, film companies, and the confident outlook on Korean cinema provide valuable data points about urban culture, media consumption, and national cultural aspirations under Japanese rule. The mention of Esperanto reflects a particular intellectual current of the time.
