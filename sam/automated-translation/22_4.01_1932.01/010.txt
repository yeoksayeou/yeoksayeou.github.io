Oath
						P'ain
Grasping the sword and gazing,
The whole world seems mine.

In this wide world,
Is there any reason to live crying?

If I raise the sword and strike those clouds,
A new sun and moon will appear.

**Summary**

This short poem by "P'ain" (Kim Tong-hwan) expresses a powerful resolve. The speaker, holding a sword, feels a sense of dominion over the world and questions the necessity of sorrow. The final lines suggest that decisive action—striking the clouds with the sword—can bring about a fundamentally new reality, symbolized by a "new sun and moon." From a historical perspective, written during the Japanese colonial period, the poem is significant for its metaphorical expression of resistance and hope for liberation. The imagery of forcefully revealing a "new sun and moon" likely represents the desire to overcome the oppressive colonial regime and usher in an era of Korean independence, making it of interest to historians studying colonial-era literature and nationalism.
