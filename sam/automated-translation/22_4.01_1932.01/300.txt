Crossroads
If anyone has something they wish to ask someone, please contribute to this column. Our magazine's standard-bearer (旗手) will relay the letter to the intended recipient, receive their reply, and publish it in this column.
1. Use a postcard for the paper.
2. Keep the meaning simple.
3. The content should relate to matters of public social interest.

To Teacher Yi Kwang-su (李光洙):

Teacher, I am a student currently enrolled in a middle school. My constitution is on the weak side, and my temperament is calm, so from early on, I haven't put effort into exercise but have focused on reading, and I have read many of the books you have authored. Thus, I developed an interest in literature from an early age and intend to major in literature going forward. What sequence should I follow? Teacher, is there no correspondence school for literature? Or is it not possible to receive correspondence instruction from you, Teacher? Please guide this young student well.
Chŏnju (全州), Yi Chong-sŏk (李鍾錫)

To Mr. Yi Chong-sŏk (李鍾錫) of Chŏnju (全州):<95>

Thank you for avidly reading my works. If you wish to become a literary writer, I believe you should first follow the steps (階梯) to complete the literature department at a university. While there are those who became writers through self-study, that is due to unavoidable circumstances, and needless to say, their painstaking efforts are truly dreadful.
For correspondence instruction, the "Literature Department Lecture Records" (文學科 講義錄) published by the publishing department of Waseda University (早稻田大學) in Ushigome (牛込), Tōkyō (東京), would likely be most suitable. I possess neither the qualification nor the ability to provide correspondence instruction.
Beyond that, there is one more thing I wish to mention that I believe is necessary for becoming a literary writer. It is to constantly study and appreciate nature and human life through your five senses (五官) and your understanding. This, along with reading, seems to be the artist's vocation and primary (第一義的) training.
Yi Kwang-su (李光洙)

To Teacher Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰):

Teacher Chu, from what age did you start composing poetry?
By the way, I really want to see the first works (처녀작) you wrote during your practice period! I apologize for the imposition, but could you possibly show them to me?
Sunan (順安), Ko Tong-hwan (高東煥)

I tried writing poetry in Japanese (日本文) at age 17, and tried writing in Korean (朝鮮文) at age 19. I cannot recall which was my first work (처녀작).
Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰)<96>

To Teacher Kim Ŏk (金億):

1. I wish to self-study the 'S' language (에쓰어 - Esperanto) independently. Please provide detailed instruction (下敎) in the A, B, C order for a beginner (初讀者).
Book title, author name, publishing bookstore name, list price, and also the most necessary dictionaries and reference materials...
2. Is it not possible to buy the magazine "Musanja" (無産者 - Proletarian) published in Tōkyō (東京)?
Please indicate the bookstore name and address for purchasing, and the magazine price (誌價).
Yŏngju (榮州), Kim Ch'.t. (金ㅊㄷ)

1. Although there might be a point concerning pronunciation if you know Japanese (日文), the following book should be fine (關찬슴니다):
Kosaka (小坂) author, *Esperanto Quick Course* (エスペラント捷徑). List price 50 chŏn (전).
For a dictionary, the *New Translation Esperanto-Japanese Dictionary* (新譯 エス和 사전). List price 60 chŏn (전).
Publisher: Japan Esperanto Institute (日本エスペラント學會)
Postal Transfer (振替) Tōkyō (東京) 11325
2. I do not know (몰으겟슴니다) about the magazine "Musanja" (無産者) published in Tōkyō (東京). However, among Esperanto magazines, there are monthly publications in this field such as *Nova Epoko* (annual fee 2 wŏn (원) 50 chŏn (전)) or *Sennaciulo* (around 5 wŏn (원) annually). These are entirely in Esperanto (에쓰어). The Japan Esperanto Institute (日本 エスペラント學會) accepts advance payments/pre-orders (선금예약 밧슴니다) for them.
Kim Ŏk (金億)<97>

**Summary**

This article from the January 1932 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features a Q&A column titled "Crossroads" (交叉點). Readers pose questions to prominent intellectuals: Yi Kwang-su receives an inquiry about pursuing a literary career, Chu Yo-han is asked about his earliest poems, and Kim Ŏk is questioned about learning Esperanto and obtaining a leftist magazine. The responses offer advice on education (university preferred over self-study or correspondence), acknowledge early writing efforts (though Chu Yo-han claims not to remember his first work), and provide specific resources for learning Esperanto, including recommending Esperanto-language publications with potentially leftist leanings (*Nova Epoko*, *Sennaciulo*) as alternatives to a Japanese one the writer couldn't locate. The column highlights the desire among young Koreans for guidance from established figures, the perceived importance of formal education, and contemporary interest in international languages like Esperanto, even touching upon access to potentially sensitive political publications during the colonial period. Historians might find Yi Kwang-su's emphasis on university education and sensory experience, Chu Yo-han's vague recollection, and Kim Ŏk's practical advice on Esperanto and related publications indicative of the cultural priorities, personal styles, and underlying political awareness among leading intellectuals and their readership in 1930s Korea.

交叉點
누구시든지 어느 분에게 뭇고 십흔 말슴이 잇거든 본란에 투고하여 주십시요. 본사 旗手가 수신할 분에게 그 편지를 分傳한여 그 회답을 바더다가 본란에 발표하겟슴니다.
1. 용지는 엽서에
2. 의미는 간단하게
3. 내용은 사회공중에 관계되는 것.

李光洙 선생께

선생님, 저는 한 중등학교에 재학중인 학도임이다. 신체는 약한 편이고 성질은 침착하여 일즉부터 운동엔 힘을 안 쓰고 독서에 힘을 써 선생님께서 저작하옵신 책도 만이 읽엇나이다. 그래 일즉부터 문예에 취미를 갓게 되어 이후 문예를 전공할녀고 하온대 엇더한 순서를 밟어야 할까요? 선생님, 문예통신학교는 업슴닛까. 안이 선생님한테 통신교수를 밧을 수 업슴잇까. 어린 이 소생을 잘 인도하여 주옵소서.
全州 李鍾錫

全州 李鍾錫씨에<95>

生의 저작을 애독해 주심은 감사하옵니다. 문예작가가 되랴고 하시면 먼저 階梯를 밟아 대학의 문과를 맛치도록 하실 것인가 합니다. 독학으로 작가가 된 이도 업지 아니하나 그것은 사정이 부득이한 것이오, 그 고심이 참담함은 말할 것도 업습니다.
通信敎授로는 東京牛込 早稻田大學 출판부 발행인 「文學科 講義錄」이 가장 적당할가 합니다. 生은 통신교수할 자격도 능력도 업습니다.
그박게 문예작가 되는데 필요하다고 밋는 것 한가지를 말슴하고 십픔니다. 그것은 자연과 인생을 당신의 五官을 통하야 당신의 이해력으로 끈힘업시 연구하고 감상하는 것입니다. 이것은 독서와 아울러 예술가의 천직인 동시에 제1 義的 修學인가 합니다.
李光洙

朱耀翰 先生任에게

朱耀翰<96>
