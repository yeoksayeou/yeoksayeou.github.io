Zeal Regarding "Compatriots in Manchuria"

Because our readers contributed the monetary donations listed below, however small, to alleviate the suffering of our "Compatriots in Manchuria," our company receives them with tears of gratitude and will send them along the very best path. We will continue to receive donations going forward, so regardless of the amount, we hope our compatriots will show their affection.

We offer our condolences to the victimized compatriots in Manchuria.

We are poor workers selling our labor in the overseas labor market, far from our homeland.
All day long (10 hours), we endure hard labor, driving our minds and bodies under dangerous machinery while smelling noxious chemicals. However, earning meager wages of only 80 or 90 chŏn up to 1 wŏn, we barely sustain our lives, yet live under a mountain of anxiety, worrying whether our wages might be cut or we might be fired amid constant talk of "Recession! Recession!"
Therefore, we hoped that perhaps some good news might come from the homeland—that there was a good harvest! Or that good jobs had appeared! Or that the Sŏbuk Kando region was good for Koreans to live in!—or any such news. But then, completely unexpectedly, we heard the tragic report of an indescribable disaster, "Suffering for the Korean compatriots residing in Manchuria..." and we cannot suppress the feeling that our insides are being torn apart.
More than just ethnic affection! We feel that the misfortune of our wandering brothers, who are in the same circumstances, is a misfortune that directly affects us personally. Thus, choked with tears of grief and indignation, we thought about it in various ways and concluded that since this is something that happened to our brothers, we must save ourselves with our own hands. Yes—let's save them! Although we are only just making ends meet, let's express our condolences to our compatriots in Manchuria, even if it means skipping a meal. Especially through the initiative of the young worker Han Pok-su (17 years old), several Korean brothers at the Okamoto Enamel Factory have gathered donations according to their means and are sending them via the Samch'ŏlli Company. Please forgive the delay, and may this provide even one ten-thousandth part of solace to our brethren in Manchuria crying out in hunger.
December 8, 1931
Respectfully submitted by the Korean Workers of the Okamoto Enamel Factory, Tokyo<24>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the January 1932 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* acknowledges donations received for Korean compatriots suffering hardships in Manchuria. It lists contributions from various individuals and groups, including students from a school in Hamgyŏng Province and individuals from Kyŏngsang Province. A significant portion is dedicated to a poignant letter and donation from Korean factory workers at the Okamoto Enamel Factory in Tokyo, who describe their own harsh working conditions, low pay, and precarious employment while expressing deep empathy and solidarity with the Koreans in Manchuria, donating despite their poverty. For historians, this article is valuable as it showcases the transnational solidarity among Koreans during the colonial period, linking the plight of laborers in Japan with the struggles of settlers in Manchuria. The letter from the Tokyo workers provides a raw, first-hand account of the difficult lives of Korean migrant laborers in Japan, their anxieties about economic depression, and their strong sense of shared identity and compassion for fellow Koreans facing crisis elsewhere, initiated even by a teenage worker.

在滿被害同胞들에게 慰問함니다.

우리는 고국을 떠나 머-ㄹ니 해외의 노동시장에 품파리 하는 가난한 직공들임니다.
하로종일 (10시간) 극약 냄새를 맛흐며 위험한 기계 밋헤서 심신을 驅使하야 노역을 감당함니다. 그러나 8,90전 내지 1원이라는 갑의 임금을 밧으며 근근히 연명이나 하면서도 불경기다! 불경기다! 하는 바람에 임금 値下나 아니될까? 馘首나 아니될까? 하야 憂愁 태산으로 지냄니다.
그럼으로 행혀나 故土로부터 조흔 소식이나 들녀오면―풍년이 들엇다! 혹은 조흔 일자리가 생겻다! 또는 西北間道는 조선사람 살기 조타!―든지 그타 소식이라도 전하여주면 하고 지내오든 중 천만 뜻박게 『滿洲在留 조선동포들에게 수난...』이라고 아조 말못할 災變의 慘報를 듯게 됨에 간장이 찌저지는 듯한 회포를 금하지 못하겟습니다.
우리는 동족애! 보다도 갓흔 처지에 처한 遊離의 형제의 그- 변고는 직접 우리의 신변에 당한 변고가 아닌가 생각됨니다. 그리하야 우리는 비분강개의 눈물을 먹음고 여러 가지로 생각하여 본 결과 우리 형제의 당한 일인만치 우리의 손으로 우리를 구원하여 주지 안으년 안되겟다. 자- 구원하자! 우리는 오직 연명하는 형편이나 비록 一食을 闕하고라도 在滿同胞들에게 위문의 意를 표하자. 특히 소년공 韓福洙(17세) 군의 발기로 岡本琺瑯工場 朝鮮兄弟 幾人의 응분의 희사를 모아 三千里社를 통하야 부송케 되오니 시일의 遲晩된 것을 용서하여주시고 기아에 呼哭하는 在滿동포형제에게 만분지 일이라도 위안이 되게 하여주옵소서.
