歲拜 (Sebae)

Everyone, let us laugh heartily once. Louder, very loudly, very, very loudly, let us laugh. And let us continue this laughter,
and live this year always peacefully, always cheerfully, always actively!

New Year's Day, 1932
Samch'ŏlli-sa Kim Tong-hwan
Pak Myŏng-hwan
Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi
Sin Sun-sŏk
And all other staff members<58>

**Summary**

This short editorial from the Samch'ŏlli magazine staff serves as a New Year's greeting for 1932. It emphatically encourages readers to laugh loudly and to carry that spirit of laughter throughout the year, living peacefully, cheerfully, and actively. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its simple, yet forceful, call for positive emotions (laughter, peace, cheerfulness, activity) during a difficult period in Korean history under Japanese colonial rule. While not overtly political, this focus on maintaining a positive spirit could be interpreted as a subtle form of cultural resilience or perhaps a necessary coping mechanism promoted by the publication.
