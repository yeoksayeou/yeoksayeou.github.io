Article Title: 笑話 (Jokes)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

During a pastor's sermon:
"Those among you who wish (십흔) to go to heaven, please stand up (이러서십시요)." Upon this, the whole congregation stood up straight, but just one person remained seated (안저잇다).
Pastor: "Don't you want (십지 안어요) to go to heaven?"
Congregant: "I don't particularly want to go right now (지금 곳... 십지는 안은테요)."

A: "Whenever I intend to travel (여행을 할여면), I invariably get sick the night before. I don't know (몰으겟네) why that is."
B: "In that case, wouldn't it be better (좃치 안켓나) to depart one day (하로) earlier?" <38>

**Summary**

This article presents two short, simple jokes. The first involves a play on words regarding the timing of wanting to go to heaven during a church service. The second joke offers an absurd solution to the problem of getting sick before travelling. From a historical perspective, the article provides a small glimpse into the type of humor published in a popular Korean magazine during the colonial period. The first joke references Christianity, indicating its presence and normalization in Korean society to the point of being subject matter for casual humor. The second joke reflects everyday concerns like travel and illness. The use of older Korean orthography (e.g., 십흔, 이러서십시요, 안저잇다, 할여면, 몰으겟네, 하로, 좃치 안켓나) is also of linguistic interest to historians studying the evolution of the Korean language.

笑話
목사가 설교하는 중에
「여러분 중에 천국에 가고 십흔 분은 이러서십시요.」한 즉 일동이 쭉 이러서는대 꼭 한 사람이 가만히 안저잇다.
목사 「당신은 천국가고 십지 안어요.」
교인 「지금 곳 가고 십지는 안은테요」

갑 「나는 여행을 할여면 그 전 날 밤엔 꼭 병이 생기게 되니 웬일인지 몰으겟네.」
을 「그러면 하로 먼저 출발하는 것이 좃치 안켓나?」＜38＞
