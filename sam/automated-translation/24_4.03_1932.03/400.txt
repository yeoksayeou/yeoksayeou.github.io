戀愛片紙印刷 해서 公開, 英國의 文學批評家 쇼―翁이

Bernard Shaw (봐나-드 쇼翁), who had been in a deep love relationship with the famous British actress Ellen Terry (에렌테리-), recently printed and made public the important letters exchanged between them after his lover had died. Consequently, Terry's son, the famous stage artist Gordon Craig (골든 크레그), became indignant (*慨해서) about this and attacked only Old Man Shaw (쇼-翁), which became the trigger for an intense debate in the literary world about the rightness or wrongness of Old Man Shaw's actions. **Summary**

The article reports on the controversy surrounding British literary critic Bernard Shaw's decision to publish his private correspondence with the late famous actress Ellen Terry. This act provoked indignation from Terry's son, stage artist Gordon Craig, leading to public attacks against Shaw and igniting a fierce debate within London literary circles and newspapers regarding the ethics of Shaw's actions. From a historical perspective, this brief news item highlights the awareness and interest among the Korean readership of the 1930s in international cultural events and celebrity gossip, even amidst colonial rule. It demonstrates the reach of global news networks and the role of magazines like *Samch'ŏlli* (三千里) in disseminating such information, offering a glimpse into the cultural consumption patterns of the time.

戀愛片紙印刷 해서 公開, 英國의 文學批評家 쇼―翁이
