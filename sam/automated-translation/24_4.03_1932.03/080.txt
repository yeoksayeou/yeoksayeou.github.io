謝告
						三千里編輯局

Introducing the leaders of the next generation is a very meaningful endeavor, yet at the same time, it was exceedingly awkward to handle, and gathering materials was also difficult. However, believing it is better than not doing it at all, we first introduce the initial group as shown above and intend to continue introducing them sufficiently in the future. We ask for the understanding of related organizations and public figures. We only endeavored to be impartial (不偏不黨) in this first installment. Furthermore, we cannot guarantee that there will be no errors regarding the ages, brief histories, and the like of the various individuals. For that, too, please forgive us.

Samch'ŏlli Editorial Bureau <31>

**Summary**

The editorial board of Samch'ŏlli magazine issues an announcement regarding their recent feature introducing "leaders of the next generation." They acknowledge the significance of the task but also the difficulties faced in gathering information and the awkwardness of the selection process. They apologize in advance for potential errors in details like age or biographies and ask for the readers' and related parties' understanding, promising to continue the series more thoroughly while stressing their commitment to impartiality (不偏不黨). From a historical perspective, this piece reveals the challenges faced by a colonial-era Korean magazine in identifying and profiling potential future leaders, hinting at sensitivities and possibly limited access to reliable information. The explicit mention of striving for impartiality (不偏不黨) is noteworthy, reflecting an awareness of potential political divisions or sensitivities within Korean society under Japanese rule.
