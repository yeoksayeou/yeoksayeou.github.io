**Body Text Translation:**

March Literary Arts
Pioneer - Several Pieces for Quiet Thought = Thinking quietly, *There are marked trails through the brushwood. The Odes lament the customs.* (*擗有標. 詩*질*風*)

Yang Chu-dong

1 Pioneer

Because this heart surges so with indignation,
I rise in the middle of the night, beat the floor,
And weep bitterly for the pioneers who shed [Blood] for us,
Then curse them once again.

Oh, Pioneer, in a time lacking the people's awareness, training, and organization,
Only your courageous name stood out clearly.
Ah, you are the tragic sacrifice and accursed symbol
Of this powerless people's ignorance and insensitivity.

Alone, yet bravely forging ahead,
Oh, man whose blood boiled with righteous anger! Oh, martyr! Oh, hero!
How much must you have resented these people,
Or rather, pitied and felt sorry for them.

Why, for these people wandering without direction,
Did you alone gaze at the goal and go?
Why did you shed [Blood] and go your way first?
How tragic and heartless, our pioneers. <98>

Ah, Pioneer, when we thirst,
We may drink the [Blood] you spilled at every step and go on,
But on the day the masses advance together, among us,
There will be none found bearing the name Pioneer, search as we might.
—From the poetry collection "Chosŏn's Pulse" (朝鮮의 脈博)

2 Hero and Patriot

Last night, they say a hero emerged from the Alps,
And steered the world's history as he pleased—
Comrade, is this true?

This morning, let's say patriots gathered on Mount Paektu,
And set Chosŏn's future course straight—
Friend, could this also be true?

Masses, let us deny the hero!
Let us clearly recognize their falsehood!
Masses, let us send off the so-called patriot!
Let us expose their worthless true nature!

Who asks who the creator of history is?
Is not everything within our grasp?
Who asks who the leader of the future is?
Our leader is only among our own hands!

Ah, let us solely cultivate our strength!
Let us stop kneeling before the vain illusion of the hero,
Let us stop being moved by the cheap tears and rhetoric of the patriot!
Truly, while the masses lack strength,
Know that no matter the magic of heroes and patriots,
"Something" cannot possibly come from "nothing." <99>

Masses, know that the day Guan Yu (關羽), Zhang Fei (張飛), and Zhuge Liang (諸葛亮) disappear
From our minds, our new day will dawn.
Know that the day the so-called heroes and patriots are expelled
From the history we create,
Our day of victory has arrived.
—Ibid.

3

I want to offer my whole body to you, the red heart within my breast,
Reality steals it away in some crevice.
Oh, you, shatter the reality before me,
With your arms, strong as iron.

The red passion directed towards you,
Reason spitefully makes it rot.
Oh, you, dry up the reason within me,
With your blood, hot as sulfuric acid.

Ah, Principle of "Righteousness" (義), brighter than sunlight,
I want to offer my whole body to you—
Crossing the night pass of reality, traversing the twilight path of reason.
Ah, let this body proceed only before the altar of practice (實踐).
—Ibid. Above 3 pieces written February 1930.

Mountain Path 1

I walk the mountain path, wordlessly,
Alone, I walk the mountain path.

The sun sets, bird songs cease, <100>
The sounds of animal footsteps are faintly heard.

I walk the mountain path, wordlessly,
Alone at night, I walk the mountain path.

2
Silent night,
Dark forest.

Go on and on, a rugged forest,
A dark forest where stars are unseen.

The mountain path is rough,
The mountain path is long.

3
On the dreamlike mountain path,
A single pile of burning leaves (화토ㅅ불).

(When will this pathless mountain trail end?
When will this pitch-dark night dawn?)

On a rock,
A single pile of burning leaves.
—Ibid. From Part 1, "Eternal Secret" (永遠한 秘密)

Moonlit Night - To a Friend

Just because it's hidden in the clouds,
Don't you dare think the moon has set—
The moon is still in mid-sky,
Let's play all night until dawn before going.
—Ibid. Written 1926. <101>

Towards the Dark South Street

Yi Ŭng-su

Through the New Year's night street battered by the northern country's (北國) snowstorm,
He, the '[Anarchist]', and I walked, discussing the world.
Listening to the 'February frog' sound crunching underfoot,
Brushing off the ice that repeatedly formed before our noses.

Through the New Year's night street battered by the northern country's snowstorm,
He, the '[Anarchist/Activist?]', and I walked, discussing tomorrow,
How many times did we retrieve and put back on our hats,
Which the fierce wind mercilessly ripped off and carried away.

Pushing through the bustling New Year's crowd,
Two young men disappear into the dark south street (南쪽 거리),
Into the dark south street...
—End—

Song of the Defeated

Myŏng Hwan

Oh night, come quickly,
I long for the land of dreams. <102>

Though I cry out until my throat is bloody and dry,
There is no answer.
Falling and rising again,
Though I chase, I cannot reach it.
The fragrant fruit of the fig tree,
Ah, reality is relentless.

Pour wine into the 'glass' (그라쓰),
And add poison.
Set my heart ablaze,
And pump the bellows in yours.
Let's resurrect the gasping living corpse,
Dreams are a beautiful world.

Coming back from [Two Characters Redacted - Original]—
An old man shivers in the 추위 (cold).
A young child cries from hunger.
[One Line Redacted - Original]
Where has the beautiful ivory tower gone?
Pok-man, Ok-hŭi, my body, too, is tired.

Afraid of the dazzling sun,
Hating to hear the roaring voices.
A dark room,
Oh, a dark room.
Lower the 'curtain' (커퇸),
Ah, the opium pipe is here.
—From a sickbed— <103>

**Summary**

This collection features poems by Yang Chu-dong, Yi Ŭng-su, and Myŏng Hwan, exploring themes of disillusionment with leadership, the role of the masses, revolutionary fervor, personal struggle, and despair in colonial Korea. Yang Chu-dong critiques the idea of solitary "Pioneers" and "Heroes/Patriots," arguing true progress comes from the awakened and organized masses, rejecting figures like Guan Yu as models. He contrasts this with poems expressing intense personal commitment to "Righteousness" and the difficulty of navigating a metaphorical dark "Mountain Path." Yi Ŭng-su depicts two young intellectuals, possibly radicals given the censored terms, discussing the future amidst a bleak, cold setting. Myŏng Hwan's "Song of the Defeated" expresses profound weariness, disillusionment with reality, and a longing for escape through dreams or oblivion (opium), hinting at the harshness of life and failed aspirations.

For historians, these poems offer insights into the intellectual currents and emotional landscape of 1930s colonial Korea. Yang's poems reflect the ideological debates about revolutionary strategy—individual heroism versus mass mobilization—common among leftist or nationalist intellectuals. The explicit rejection of traditional heroes (Guan Yu, etc.) signifies a desire for a modern, mass-based movement. Yi's poem, with its censored political labels ([Anarchist]?), points to the active presence and suppression of radical ideologies. Myŏng Hwan's poem starkly reveals the deep despair and psychological toll the colonial situation and perhaps failed political activism could inflict, even leading to thoughts of escapism through substance abuse. The censorship itself is historically significant, indicating state control over expression.

2 英雄과 志士 어제ㅅ밤 알프스에서 英雄이 나와,

압길의 指導者 뉘라 뭇는가,

갑싼 志士의 눈물에, 辯舌에, 感激을 그치자!
진실로 大衆이 힘업는 동안,
