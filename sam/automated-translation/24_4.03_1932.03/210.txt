交叉點
If there is anything you wish to ask anyone, please submit it to this column. Our company's standard-bearer (旗手) will forward the letter to the intended recipient, receive their reply, and publish it in this column.
1. Use a postcard for the submission.
2. Keep the meaning concise.
3. The content should relate to the public interest.

To Mr. Yu Kwang-yŏl (柳光烈)

I admire you, sir. I read your writings in newspapers and magazines and renew my awareness. Recently, I heard news from someone <52> that you had left the Chosŏn Ilbo Sa (朝鮮日報社). Is this true? If true, where do you intend to go next?
Apologies, but also your current address...
Hamhŭk Kyŏngsŏng Kim Wŏl-ak (咸北 鏡城 金月岳) respectfully

After leaving the Chosŏn Ilbo Sa (朝鮮日報社), about two months passed, and since November 16th, I joined the Chungang Ilbo Sa (中央日報社) and am currently working there. My current address is Kyŏngsŏng-bu (京城府) Iksŏn-dong (益善洞) 166-ŭi-82.
Yu Kwang-yŏl (柳光烈) respectfully

To Mr. Sin Im (申琳)
1. Why was the magazine Taejung Kongnon (大衆公論), which you were editing, suspended? And when will it resume publication?
1. What is your current address?
1. There is a rumor about taking over Chosŏn chi Kwang (朝鮮之光); is this true?
Yi Hye-gyŏng (李惠卿)

1. Taejung Kongnon (大衆公論) was suspended for a while due to some unavoidable circumstances (Reason omitted - original text), but we intend to resume publication as soon as possible.
1. Kyŏngsŏng-bu (京城府) Samch'ŏng-dong (三淸洞) 54-so-1
1. Regarding Chosŏn chi Kwang (朝鮮之光), I negotiated several times with Mr. Yu Chin-hŭi (兪鎭熙), who is handling the matter, about taking it over, but we haven't reached a decision because the conditions demanded by both sides do not match.
Sin Im (申琳)

To Mr. Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯)
Mr. Kim? Under civil law, what is the age of a minor! <53> Is it irrelevant if one doesn't pay back money borrowed during one's minor years?
Sinŭiju Kukkyŏng Koju (新義州 國境 孤舟)

(Reply) The age of majority is 20 full years. Debts incurred through actions taken as a minor can be voided.
Lawyer Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯)

To Mr. Chŏn Mu-gil (全武吉)
The novel "Yŏkkyŏng" (逆境) that you were writing in the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報) last spring, is it true that Mr. Pak Hŭi-do (朴熙道) was the model? I read it with great interest, but why was it suddenly stopped? It's rumored, but even when I asked the Tonga Ilbo Sa (東亞日報社), there was no reply, so I wish to ask the author.
Kyŏngsŏng (京城) Kim Ok-tŭk (金玉得)

That is not correct. As I briefly mentioned when writing a review in the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) the other day, even if certain aspects resemble a particular individual, if the intention throughout the entire work lies elsewhere, it cannot be called a "model" novel. Every time I hear such things, I feel both apologetic and unpleasant.
The suspension occurred simply because, while depicting the devastation of rural villages, it was halted under the pretext of disturbing public peace (치안방해); any mention of interference, etc., is completely unfounded.
Chŏn Mu-gil (全武吉) <54>

**Summary**

This article from the March 1932 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features a Q&A column titled "Intersection Point" (交叉點), where readers pose questions to public figures. The questions cover journalist Yu Kwang-yŏl's move from the Chosŏn Ilbo to the Chungang Ilbo, the suspension and potential resumption of the magazine *Taejung Kongnon* edited by Sin Im, clarification on rumors of Sin Im taking over the magazine *Chosŏn chi Kwang*, a legal question about the age of majority and debt responsibility answered by lawyer Kim Pyŏng-no, and an inquiry to author Chŏn Mu-gil about the abrupt end of his novel "Yŏkkyŏng" in the *Tonga Ilbo* and whether it was based on a real person. Historically, this piece is interesting as it provides a snapshot of reader engagement with intellectuals and creators in colonial Korea, reveals details about the careers of prominent figures, sheds light on the challenges faced by publishers (suspensions, negotiations), and explicitly mentions censorship ("disturbing public peace" - 치안방해) affecting literary works depicting social realities like rural hardship.
