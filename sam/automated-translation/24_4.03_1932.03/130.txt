Public Notice

Public Notice = We were unavoidably unable to publish an article criticizing An Chae-hong's course (코-쓰), written by a Hanyang intellectual (漢陽學人).

Furthermore, going forward, we will continue in every issue to publish severe critical articles regarding the "courses" (「코-쓰」) of various prominent figures (諸大家) from political, ideological, economic, and literary standpoints.

Samch'ŏlli Editorial Department <38>

**Summary**

This public notice from the Samch'ŏlli magazine's editorial department announces that they could not publish a critical essay on An Chae-hong's "course," written by an unnamed Seoul intellectual. However, the magazine pledges to publish similar critical analyses of works by various prominent figures from different perspectives in future issues. This notice is interesting for historians as it hints at potential external pressures or internal editorial caution (possibly censorship) during the colonial period, forcing the magazine to withdraw a specific critique. Yet, it simultaneously asserts an intention to continue critical discourse, showcasing the complex environment faced by Korean publications at the time.
