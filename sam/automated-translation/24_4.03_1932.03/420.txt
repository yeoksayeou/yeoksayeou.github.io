**Body Text Translation:**

Orion and the Apple - A Piece from "The Skill of the Apple"-
						Yi Hyosŏk

A
I couldn't help but be surprised when I realized that Naomi, despite having joined the association only two weeks ago and thus having attended the research group meeting only twice, was already showing an attitude flowing in a direction completely unforeseen. While the movements of human emotion are hard to predict, the fact that she had come to harbor such feelings towards me in such a short time was entirely unexpected, and I couldn't help but feel bewildered as much as surprised. Truthfully, from the first day Naomi joined, introduced by S, I had received more of a feeling of "woman" from her than that of "comrade." This wasn't just because Naomi was currently a female clerk at some department store and thus her attire was thoughtful and luxurious, but rather because the overall impression of her physique and appearance was exceedingly soft and luxurious. Her body was very slender, her mouth light, and the expression in her eyes was far too rich. Although I had occasionally heard through S that her distant uncle had been a stalwart XX in the past and was now incarcerated, there was not a single point of connection between this knowledge and my impression of Naomi; like weeds floating on water, they were separate things. It was like two flowers of naturally different colors, a red one and a blue one, naturally attached to the same branch. However, just because her impression was delicate didn't mean her future couldn't be promised. Therefore, when S, a true member and trustworthy comrade, introduced her, we were not in the least bit hesitant to approve her membership. Yet, the more I met her, the feeling of "comrade" faded, and the feeling of "woman" became almost the entirety of the impression I received from her. Meanwhile, her attitude and actions toward me were intensely suggestive. Of course, I only realized this after the following incident occurred. It was the day Naomi attended her second research group meeting after joining. As the half-dozen members, ranging from S, a factory girl, to students and clerks, naturally couldn't adhere strictly to the meeting time, and because the text "..." which we were reading and discussing by translating and comparing with the German original contained many difficult passages, we couldn't cover much material. Moreover, everyone would be tired after the meeting finished, so it was customary to gather as early in the evening as possible and break up before the night deepened. That night too, we finished early and left S's house, <105> and since our directions home were the same, I ended up walking with Naomi again. "How is it? Have you come to understand the general mood of us comrades somewhat?" Concerned that she might feel an awkward disharmony and loneliness entering an unfamiliar group, being the only one of different blood among the members, I tossed out these words as we walked out of the dark alley, partly to hear her thoughts and partly to console her. "Of course, I understand! And I really like this atmosphere. The hearts of the comrades who welcomed me are good too, and towards you, teacher, I've especially come to feel much more intimacy." "If that's the case, I'm glad—because there are still occasional instances of mistakes being made due to misplaced prejudice regarding kinship." "It must be due to a lack of understanding—anyway, I don't feel a single point of awkward discomfort in our meetings—my heart feels this happy and good." As if genuinely pleased, Naomi swayed her slender body slightly and laughed aloud. Recognizing her subtly shifting gaze on my side profile, we emerged from the alley and reached the crossroads. Following my usual habit, I stopped by the corner bookstore to browse the new arrivals for a moment, and even as we left the bookstore, Naomi's smile hadn't faded. As we passed the fruit stand next to the bookstore, Naomi directed that smile squarely at me, looked up at me with a complex expression, and proposed, "I want to eat an apple!" "An apple?" Since it was an unexpected proposal from her, I looked at her while asking back. "If only I could bite into a fresh apple!" Naomi pressed close to me, looking straight at me as if I myself were an apple, instead of the apples at the fruit stand. I tossed a few silver coins and handed the bag of apples I received to Naomi. Walking along, Naomi, without any hesitation on the bright street, ate the bright red apple voraciously, skin and all. "You're bold." "How about it? Eating an—apple—on the main street—isn't it proletarian?" Naomi's white teeth flashed in a smile amidst the apple. "Asceticism isn't proletarian morality—isn't expressing honest emotions frankly what being proletarian is about?" However, the scene of a beautiful woman voraciously eating an apple on a bright night street looked less like something proletarian and more like a beautiful "modern" landscape painting. To that extent, Naomi's beautiful figure lacked even a single proletarian point, and it was quite doubtful how much of a proletarian fighter she would become in the future—she was far too beautiful, luxurious, and "modern," this Naomi. "Do you like apples?" "Who dislikes apples?" "Because we are all sons of Adam and daughters of Eve"—"Well then, have one." Still smiling, Naomi placed an apple in my hand. "—That's right, we can't sever our ties with the apple, loved since the time of our ancestors. Everyone likes apples, and they will probably be good forever—an apple that shines brightly, transcending space and time, just like that star 'Orion' in the sky, shining for ages and ages. <106>" "The philosophy of the apple?" "You could call it that—so, even for a proletarian fighter, the apple is by no means a forbidden fruit, right? Where is the law that says a fighter, who must eat rice, shouldn't eat an apple?" Naomi's suggestion sounded to me like an overt confession. Therefore, I couldn't help but acutely raise my shield. "While it's true that this is the truth, the issue lies in value and effect. And also, we must have a certain boundary and self-control (節制). No matter how beautiful the apple, if indulging in it harms the class cause, wouldn't that be a worthless act?"

B
After this incident, for some reason, I always came to associate Naomi with apples, so whenever I thought of her or met her, the association of an apple would invariably flash through my mind first. Thus, there were times when she seemed almost like an incarnation of an apple. Of course, after the following event, that impression deepened further. Was it about two weeks later? A merger issue with another association regarding certain actions that had long been under consideration suddenly reached a decision. Due to the need to quickly inform the members of this development, I had no choice but to visit each member's home with the report. The last person I sought out that evening was Naomi. Since I went directly to her workplace, the department store, rather than her lodgings, I couldn't give her the lengthy news right there. So, amidst the displayed cosmetics, I could only convey the simple report in a few whispered words. However, Naomi, who stood listening quietly to my report, leaning in closely with a cute smile as if facing a dear lover rather than an unfamiliar customer or even a comrade, gave me a meaningful glance as my words ended, left her spot, and beckoned me to follow her. Not knowing what was going on, I feigned indifference while suspiciously following her. We took the elevator together. Getting off on an upper floor, Naomi climbed the stairs and, upon reaching the rooftop garden, led me again to a quiet corner by the iron railing. "What is it?" Predicting that something unusual might have happened, I asked impatiently upon reaching the spot. "I have something to give you, teacher." Leaning her tired body against the iron railing and brushing back stray strands of hair, Naomi answered slowly, without any sign of haste, looking squarely at me. "What do you mean?" "What do you think it is?" "Well—" But Naomi didn't answer right away. With a gesture that seemed intensely weary, she adjusted her disheveled clothes and appearance and sighed. "Making us work more than ten hours a day, I'm so tired I can't stand it." "That's why we cry out." "'Absolutely oppose labor over ten hours!'—But having spent time here, there isn't a single sharp kid among them. Ultimately, the necessity of organizing a place like this seems not yet to have reached the stage of suggestion." "—Let's leave that aside for now. What is it you have to give me?" "Right, I must give you what I have to give." <107> Saying this, Naomi put her hand into the pocket of her jet-black one-piece dress. "The other day, I received an apple from you, teacher—so I should give you an apple too." In her right hand rested a single, bright red apple. "An apple?" "Why, are you disappointed? Could there be anything in the world as precious as an apple?" As if seeking agreement, Naomi looked directly at me. "Look down there. What are all those countless people wandering and crawling in the bustling streets ultimately searching for—isn't it a bowl of rice and an apple? This bird's-eye view (俯瞰圖) of the bustling street seems like a search map (探索圖) for beautiful apples." Saying this, Naomi turned her body slightly towards the street and held the apple in her hand high. The brilliant evening sun reflected off a few stray strands of her hair, the contours of her side profile, her soft legs, and the apple in her hand, making it seem as if golden sunlight emanated from her entire body. Her figure appeared like a sacred and mysterious painting, like Eve holding the apple. "Please accept the apple." The "modern" Eve, clad in her striking one-piece, held out the single apple before me. Bewitched by her appearance and actions, I stood silently. Misinterpreting my silence perhaps, she placed the single apple between her two hands and applied force. "In the Caucasus region, they say that during a wedding, they split a single apple in two, and the bride and groom each eat one half on the spot," Naomi said, handing me one half of the split apple and bringing the remaining half to her own lips. Leaning against the iron railing, gazing sideways at the bird's-eye view of the darkening street while eating her half of the apple, Naomi's figure, in stark contrast to the earlier sacred painting, appeared only as a mundane and ordinary earthly (地上的) scene.

C
"—So, what do you think of Naomi?" "You mean Kollontai herself?" "More about Wasillisa." "In the heart of Wasillisa, who formed various red loves, there was surely the firm control of reason, but perhaps there was more obedience to boiling blood and emotion—in this respect, I too can like and admire Wasillisa." "Isn't it brave how she adhered to the creed 'Work first, love second' no matter what?" "But wasn't 'work first' ultimately just a shield and an excuse for Wasillisa? When moving from one man to another, wasn't there first the dictate of personal feelings of like or dislike, rather than the beautiful surface signboard of work? Ultimately, at the root, wasn't it 'emotion first, work second'? Love—as long as it's love and not trifling—cannot possibly enter solely through work; it enters first and foremost through mutual sight (視覺)." "Even so, isn't it a bit harsh to judge Wasillisa's actions straight away as 'emotion first, work second'?" "That's the frank judgment. Without judging like that, understanding Wasillisa's actions would be difficult. And wouldn't Wasillisa's own true heart actually prefer to receive such a judgment?—Ultimately, one could say Wasillisa greatly loved apples, and she frankly expressed that feeling of love. It's just that, being very cunning and clever, she used work as a shield to express it, skillfully camouflaging herself and trying to preserve her dignity." Flushed from her passionate eloquence, Naomi's face seemed to burn even brighter, reflecting the candlelight on the desk <108>. Beneath her dark eyebrows, her pupils, filled with passion, cast a sharp brilliance like an animal's, and her hair, tinted by the flame, seemed to distinctly emit a surrounding halo of red passion! "Ultimately, it's the apple, then." "Of course. Without the apple, nothing can be explained." "Ah, the apple—" Although I had my own opinions and judgments, I avoided elaborating on them and was about to end the conversation with such a short sigh and a feigned yawn when the watch on my arm suddenly caught my eye. "The time has long passed, what could be the matter?" "I know. Perhaps there's some trouble at the factory." "What about the other members?" The starting time for the research group meeting had long passed, and even though we were in S's room, only Naomi and I, both members, had been waiting for a long time. Now that the topic of Kollontai had ended, it seemed strange that S herself, let alone any other members, had yet to appear, and I couldn't suppress my anxious and impatient feelings. "They said the atmosphere at the factory was ripe for explosion; perhaps it finally erupted." "Well, maybe that's why S is late—" Feeling impatient and also bored, I muttered and casually cast my gaze upon the biography of "Rosa" lying open on S's desk, skimming through it meaninglessly. "Speaking of apples, Rosa too—" Naomi, drawn in, also cast her gaze upon Rosa's biography and continued the topic, changing its direction. "When she returned to her home country, she had to perform a strange play for policy reasons related to her work, and there was an incident where she unexpectedly picked an apple, but if you dig deep down, that too probably wasn't an entirely unintended apple—at least, that's how I want to think." Drawn by Naomi's words, I couldn't help but involuntarily shift my gaze, along with Naomi's, to Rosa's portrait hanging on the wall above the desk—to the portrait of Rosa looking down thoughtfully at the dimly candlelit, impoverished room and the young woman within it speaking of her, after the electric light had been cut off. Then, for some reason, suddenly! Unexpectedly, Rosa's portrait, as if rejecting our gaze, abruptly left its spot and fell onto the desk. In an instant, the glass of the portrait frame shattered against the corner of the desk, and in the same moment, the candle placed beneath the frame was knocked over, plunging the room into sudden darkness. "Oh my!" Startled by the sudden event, Naomi clung to me almost reflexively. "Could she be angry that we casually made disrespectful remarks about her?" Taken aback by the sudden turn of events, feeling this instinctively, and momentarily unsure of what to do, I was then subjected to something even more surprising—suddenly, I felt an unexpected warm and soft touch on the nape of my neck and face. And the scent of blood enveloped my entire body, making it feel hot. The next moment, the soft touch on my nape changed into a firm pressure, and a suffocating warmth and fragrance flowed over my face as if scalding blood had been poured on it—and burning lips met mine. And, of course, simultaneously, Naomi's trembling, pleading voice, fragmented along with the rapid beating of her heart, brushed past my ear, phrase by broken phrase. "Hold me! Please hold me tight!"
—February 10th—<109>

**Summary**

This short story by Yi Hyosŏk depicts the narrator's complex relationship with Naomi, a new, strikingly "modern" female member of his socialist-leaning study group. The narrator finds himself increasingly drawn to Naomi as a "woman" rather than just a "comrade," a feeling amplified by symbolic encounters involving apples. Their intellectual discussions about figures like Kollontai and Rosa Luxemburg intertwine with their burgeoning personal tension, culminating in an abrupt, intimate embrace initiated by Naomi in a moment of darkness and confusion. Historically, the story offers a window into the lives of Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule, navigating imported ideologies like socialism and modernism, challenging traditional gender roles (a female department store clerk in a study group), and exploring the conflict between political commitment ("comrade," "proletariat") and personal desire ("woman," "apple"). The coded references (XX) and discussions of revolutionary figures highlight the era's political undercurrents and intellectual engagements.

의외에도 로오사의 초상이 우리들의 시선을 거부하느 듯이 럴엇든 그 자리를 떠나서 별안간 책상우에 떠러 젓든 것이다. 순간 책상 모슬기에 부드친 초상화 판의 유리가 밧삭 부서지고 가튼 순간에 화판미테 니운 촉불이 쓸어지며 방안은 별안간 어둠속에 잠겨 버렷다. 「에그머니!」 돌연히 놀난 「나오미」는 거의 반사뎍으로 나에게 밧삭부텃다. 「그에게 대햐야 공연히 불손한 언사를 희롱한 것을 노여함이 아닌가」 돌연한 변에 뜩금하야서 이럿케 직각 뎍으로 늣기며 엇지 할 바를 몰나 잠시 잠잣고 잇든 나는 그러나 더 놀나은 것을 당하엿다―별안간 나는 목덜미와 얼골우에 의외의 따뜻하고 부드러운 촉감을 바덧든 것이다. 그리고 피의 향긔가 나의 전신을 훅군하게 둘너쌋다. 다음 순간 목덜미의 부드럽든 촉감은 든든한 압박감으로 변하고 얼골에는 전면 뜨거은 피를 께언는 듯한 확근한 김과 향긔가 숨차게 흘너오고―입술에는 타는 입술이 와서 맛타엇다. 그리고 물논 동시에 다을과 갓흔 니는 「나오미」의 애원하는 목소리가 후둑이는 그의 염통의 고동과 함께 구절구절 찟기면서 나의 귀를 스첫든 것이다. 「안어 주세요!
