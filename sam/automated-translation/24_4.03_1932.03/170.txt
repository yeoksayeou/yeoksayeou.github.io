交叉點 (Intersection Point / Q&A Corner)
If there is anything you wish to ask anyone, please submit it to this column. Our company's standard-bearer (旗手) will forward the letter to the recipient and publish their reply in this column.
1. Use a postcard for the paper.
2. Keep the meaning simple.
3. Content should relate to the public interest (社會公衆).

To Mr. Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿氏)
Q1: What is the impact of the German reparations issue on the capitalist empires? Chinju (晉州), Pak Sŏnggyŏng (朴聖敬)
This is an extremely complex issue that cannot be answered simply. If you refer to my article "How Does the War Debt Reparations Moratorium Affect the World Economy?" (戰債賭償延期案=世界經濟에 影響이 如何), serialized over 12 installments in the economics section of the Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報) from July 3rd, Shōwa 6 (1931) to July 16th of the same year, I believe you will gain a general understanding. Sincerely, Sŏ Ch'un (徐春拜)

Q2: The relationship between the suspension of the British Gold Standard and Japanese trade<45>
Western goods were in a competitive position in various Eastern markets. Previously, Japanese goods were about 10% cheaper than British goods, so they always held a position of dominance over British goods. As a result of Britain suspending the Gold Standard, the Pound Sterling could be sold about 30% cheaper, completely reversing the position of the two countries' goods in various Eastern markets. Now that Japan has also banned gold exports and the value of the Won has fallen by about 30%, this disadvantage has been completely eliminated, and Japanese goods have returned to a more advantageous position than British goods. Sincerely, Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿拜)

Economics Section
Q: What reference materials are needed to gain enough knowledge of economics to freely read the economics section of a newspaper? Also, are there any books that explain the terminology used? Please teach me. Andong-hyŏn (安東縣), Ch'oe Ch'angsik (崔昌植)
Of course, having reference books is better than not having them, but the only way to gain the economic knowledge to read the economics section freely is to diligently read the daily economics section without cease. If you read diligently, you will naturally come to understand it within half a year or a year. The day will surely come when you have a sudden, clear understanding (轄然貫通), as they say in university. Think about how a young child learns to speak. They manage to speak fully by the age of three without reference books. There are some books in Japanese called "How to Read Economic Articles" (經濟記事讀方), but for a Korean person to read, those "How to Read" books are even more difficult. In the near future, the Tong-a Ilbo economics section will begin<46> simple explanations of issues and terminology, so please refer to that. Sincerely, Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿拜)

Q1: What is Mr. Kim Ansŏ's (金岸曙氏) address and age?
Q2: Please fill "Samch'ŏlli" (三千里) as much as possible with literary works rich in "nonsense" (넌센스) flavor. How much do we, the white-clad masses (白衣大衆) struggling on the front lines of life, yearn for laughter rather than monotonous theory?
From K M Saeng (K M 生) in Tokyo (在東京), Sincerely

A1: Ansŏ Kim Ŏk's (岸曙 金億氏) age is 38, and his current address is c/o Yuil Yŏgwan (唯一旅舘內), 132 Nagwŏn-dong (樂園洞), Kyŏngsŏng (京城).
A2: Henceforth, we will gradually place more emphasis on this area as well.
Mok Pyŏngjŏng (木兵丁拜) <47>

**Summary**

This article is a Q&A column titled "Intersection Point" from the March 1932 issue of the magazine "Samch'ŏlli". Readers submit questions on various topics, which are then answered by experts or the editorial staff. The questions cover international economics (German reparations, the British gold standard's impact on Japanese trade), how to understand newspaper economics sections, and personal details about the writer Kim Ŏk (Ansŏ). There's also a plea for more humorous content in the magazine. For historians, the article offers insights into the concerns of Korean readers during the colonial period, including their awareness of global economic events and the practical difficulties they faced in accessing and understanding complex information like economic news. The request for "nonsense" highlights a desire for escapism and humor amidst the likely hardships of colonial life and the perceived dryness of intellectual discourse.

西國상품은 동양 諸시장에 경쟁적 위치에 잇섯슴니다. 종래는 日本품이 英國품에 비하야 약 1할이나 저렴햇든 故로 항상 英國품을 압도하는 지위에 잇섯슴니다. 英國이 金本位制停止의 결과로 磅貨가 3할이나 저렴하게 팔 수가 잇게 되닛가 동양 諸시장에서의 양국품의 지위는 전혀 전도되엿슴니다. 지금와서는 日本도 금수출을 금지하야 원화가치가 약 3할 低落하고 보니 그 불리점은 완전히 제거될 日本품이 도로 英國품보다 유리한 지위에 서게 되엿슴니다.  徐椿拜
