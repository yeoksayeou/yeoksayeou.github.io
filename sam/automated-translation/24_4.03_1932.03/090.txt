**Body Text Translation:**
Samch'ŏlli Wall Newspaper: Relief Fund for Compatriots in Manchuria
(Third Announcement of Donations Received by Samch'ŏlli Company up to February 20th)

Mr. Yi Chonggap (李鐘甲) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Sin T'aeho (申泰鎬) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Cho Sŏngwŏn (趙性元) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Hwang Inch'un (黃仁春) (Hoeryŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Chŏng Pyŏnghŭi (鄭秉熙) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Kyŏng Chaeho (景在昊) (Hoeryŏng Pot'ong Hakkyo) 5 Chŏn
Mr. Yi Yonghŭi (李容喜) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Sin Myŏnghun (辛明薰) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn
Mr. Yi Hŭisang (李熙相) (Kyŏngsŏng) 50 Chŏn

Above total: 4 Wŏn 5 Chŏn
Amount from previous month: 35 Wŏn 88 Chŏn
Grand total: 39 Wŏn 95 Chŏn

Thus, our company receives with tears of gratitude the donations (義損金) sent by our dear readers out of concern for the well-being of our compatriots in Manchuria (在滿同胞), and we will deliver them via the best possible route in the near future. We will continue to receive donations going forward, so we ask that you please send them regardless of the amount (多少 不拘). <31>

**Summary**

This article is an announcement from the magazine Samch'ŏlli, listing donations received by February 20, 1932, for a relief fund aimed at helping Korean compatriots residing in Manchuria. It details the names of donors, their locations (mostly Kyŏngsŏng/Seoul and Hoeryŏng), the amounts donated (mostly 50 Chŏn), and the total collected so far (39 Wŏn 95 Chŏn). The magazine expresses gratitude and pledges to deliver the funds soon, while encouraging readers to continue donating. For historians, this piece is interesting as it reflects public concern and grassroots fundraising efforts within colonial Korea for Koreans in Manchuria, a region of increasing instability after the Manchurian Incident (1931). The small individual donation amounts also hint at the economic realities faced by ordinary Koreans during this period.
