**誌上 三千里全體會議, 第一部門=民族問題, 現下의民族主義勢力과社會主義勢力 (Samch'ŏlli Magazine General Meeting, Part 1 = National Problem, Current Nationalist and Socialist Forces)**

**民族主義的勢力의 系列 (The Lineage of Nationalist Forces)**
**An Chaehong**

Reporter: As of today, what organized nationalist strata forces that have already been formed can we point to?
An: Speaking of nationalist forces, it's true that due to various objective circumstances that inevitably befell us, there was no time or opportunity to form a powerful organization, and thus our strength remained dispersed. Therefore, unlike labor unions or peasant unions formed under definite discipline through collective training, there aren't many such tightly-knit bodies. Nevertheless, it is a fact that considerable forces that can be called nationalist and groups that can be seen as nationalist organizations exist robustly in various places, and not only exist but are growing vigorously.

Reporter: We used to see the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) as the leading force of the dispersed nationalist strata. Now that it is in the process of dissolution, can it only be evaluated as a "vanished past force"?
An: That's right. It would be correct to view the power of the Sin'ganhoe as now dispersed.

Reporter: In that case, where do you see the main forces residing now? <2>
An: Generally, in religious organizations.

Reporter: Religious organizations?
An: The three largest religious organizations in Korea are Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎), Christianity (基督敎), and Buddhism (佛敎). Whether they declare themselves nationalist organizations or not is a separate issue, but based on their roles in the very recent past history, the general public defines them as such without major error, I believe.
Specifically, Ch'ŏndogyo, with the Ch'ŏng'udang (靑友黨) as its vanguard, is strengthening internal control effectively, and its numbers are said to be considerable. Originally, Ch'ŏndogyo was influential due to its established control. In past instances, the leadership and control by figures like Son Ŭiam (孫義庵) and others were excellent, which is why it received greater efficacy and recognition. At that time, it is said the characteristics [of strong organization] were actually more prominent on the Christian side. (Seven lines omitted - original text)

Reporter: Then what about Christianity and Buddhism?
An: Recently, Christian forces across various regions are uniting under the Korean Protestant Council (朝鮮耶蘇敎聯合公議會). Many talented individuals who had gone overseas are returning, and new organization and training are about to take place. Buddhism, too, has established the Central Ecclesiastical Council (中央敎務院) as an incorporated foundation with one million won, organized the Buddhist Youth General Federation (佛敎靑年總同盟), and appears to be making great efforts in education and industry.

Reporter: The first thing I'm curious about is the evaluation of the "Korean Production Promotion Society" (朝鮮物産獎勵會). Does the Production Promotion Society stand in the position of an auxiliary force for nationalism?
An: Isn't the Korean Production Promotion Society an organization for economic movement? However, in a place situated in a special region, it's difficult for any movement to be achieved without any connection to political movement. In this sense, the Production Promotion Society can be seen as a partial force within the political movement.

Reporter: If so, should it be seen as capable of fulfilling a special role like that of consumer cooperatives or cooperative unions in foreign lands during emergencies?
An: The time is still early. I believe it itself has not matured to that point yet.

Reporter: Then what about newspaper companies? Furthermore, would it be correct to view the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) company as a nationalist force?
An: Newspapers, through their essential mediating power, have great objective value in closely connecting movements and the populace. However, I believe the time when the company itself had to become the mainstream of the movement and act has already passed. (Five lines omitted - original text)

Reporter: In which regions and among which strata are the main forces of the Chosŏn Ilbo located?
An: They are distributed throughout Korea and overseas, centered around the newly emerging forces (新興勢力層). Revealing specifics by region is not appropriate.

Reporter: Then what about the Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報)?
An: It's awkward to comment on another newspaper company, but I believe the influence of the Tong-a Ilbo is certainly large as well.

Reporter: I will avoid any mention of nationalist forces overseas, but just one thing: how do you view the Hŭngsadan (興士團) lineage, which has roots in Korea? Especially in relation to the Tong-a Ilbo company.
An: That too is a matter difficult to disclose publicly.

Reporter: From the above, after the dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe, the main nationalist forces reside in the Ch'ŏndogyo, Christian, and Buddhist strata. The Production Promotion Society is also a major force, and separately, the three newspapers Chosŏn, Tong-a, and Chung'ang (中央), along with various magazines, act as mediating forces. Is this the entire lineage of the currently assembled nationalist forces?
An: Shall we say so? However, one cannot view the entire system as a unified force under any <3> control. One might say they constitute various sectors capable of concentration as coexisting nationalist forces. And I believe that even within the labor and peasant strata, in a new sense, they will eventually base themselves on left-wing nationalism.

**社會主義勢力의一瞥 (A Glimpse of Socialist Forces)**
**Chŏng Unyŏng**

Reporter: What is the current camp and number of organized workers? If the Labor Federation (勞總) hasn't accurately investigated, how large are the forces currently gathered under the umbrella of the General Federation of Labor (勞働總同盟)?
Chŏng: Since its founding, the General Federation of Labor's organic activities have been entirely blocked due to various circumstances. However, even amidst these unfavorable conditions, 23,000 trained workers are currently affiliated, and in terms of organizations, 57 have joined. The unit of affiliated organizations often involves the federation itself joining in some regions; that is, federations combining 10 or 20 unions participate as a single unit, so the number of labor unions is likely much higher. And the aforementioned 23,000 union members are all organized, conscious workers.

Reporter: Then what about the forces of the General Federation of Peasants (農民總同盟)?
Chŏng: The Peasant Federation (農總), too, has had its meetings banned, no different from the Labor Federation. Yet, even so, it holds immense power with over 50 affiliated organizations and 37,000 members. They are mostly organized agricultural proletariats consisting of poor peasants from the tenant farmer class.

Reporter: Then what about the forces of the Youth Federation (靑總), currently embroiled in dissolution theories?
Chŏng: With over 50 affiliated organizations and currently over ten thousand members, it too is a force composed solely of conscious elements.

Reporter: So, where do you see the leading power of the current socialist forces residing?
Chŏng: Of course, I believe the forces gathered in the camps of the aforementioned three Federations constitute the entirety. There might be other organizations and union members not yet affiliated with the General Federations, but they are extremely few, and it seems they will all affiliate soon, so they can be considered the same force.

Reporter: If that's the entirety, isn't the All-Korea Peasant Union (全朝鮮農民組合) force led by Mr. Yi Sŏnghwan (李晟煥) included in the socialist forces?
Chŏng: It cannot be. It is a collective of the social democratic faction (社民系). Also, geographically, the constituent members of that association are mostly confined to North P'yŏngan Province (平北).

Reporter: However, according to our investigation, the affiliated peasant unions number in the dozens, and members number in the tens of thousands. How can this be seen as a small force?
Chŏng: Wasn't there a report in the newspapers not long ago about denouncing and attacking the All-Korea Peasant Union faction in Tanch'ŏn (端川), South Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡南道)? And besides <4> that, haven't statements from conscious individuals in various places attacking this social democratic faction organization been frequent? From this perspective, I believe this force is on a downward path that will gradually be XX-ed [likely 'eliminated' or 'weakened' - original text obscured] as the populace awakens.

Reporter: Then, within the same peasant proletariat lineage, isn't there Ch'ŏndogyo's Nongminsa (農民社)? Shouldn't this force be incorporated into the socialist strata forces?
Chŏng: That is even less of an issue, for the same reason.

Reporter: Then, are there no auxiliary forces for the proletariat?
Chŏng: Perhaps the left-wing nationalist stratum...

Reporter: Are the All-Korea Peasant Union faction and the Nongminsa faction then in an antagonistic position relative to the three Federations' forces?
Chŏng: They are merely [?] forces (x動勢力 - likely 반동세력 'reactionary forces' or similar, first character obscured/unclear).

Reporter: Currently, the Labor Federation and Peasant Federation comprise only peasants and workers. However, Korea has a long coastline, and the value of fishery products is enormous, suggesting a considerable number of fishery proletariats. Is this force completely excluded from calculations?
Chŏng: No. The Labor Federation is in the process of absorbing the fishery proletariat as well. Calculating the number of organized fishery workers, as widely reported in the newspapers recently, there are the *haenyŏ* (海女) – mainly centered around Cheju Island (濟州島) and the entire South Sea coast villages – numbering about 7,000. This force is also entirely incorporated within the Labor Federation.

Reporter: So, to calculate the organized socialist strata forces in Korea currently, the leading force lies with the approximately 60,000 members of the three Federations, and besides the left-wing nationalist stratum as an auxiliary force, there is nothing else to count. This refers only to overt movements, of course.
Chŏng: That is the reality.

Reporter: This is a separate issue. The main intention was to ask about the socialist strata forces. However, as an aside, I'd like to ask one more thing to know your view. How do you see the current nationalist strata forces in Korea?
Chŏng: The concentrated power of nationalism is gathered solely in religious organizations. However, looking at Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, or Buddhism, their power ebbs and flows; there is no fullness or progress. As evidence, if you go to churches or temples, you cannot see the shadow of conscious youth; only old men and women or very young children are there. As the populace awakens, this trend will increasingly show signs of decline at an accelerating pace.

Reporter: Then what about the power of newspaper companies?
Chŏng: Although it's a dispersed force that hasn't undergone control and training, I would see its influence as comparatively large. Especially regarding the Tong-a Ilbo, since it seems to have close contact with the Hŭngsadan, various aspects of its efforts to expand its sphere of influence can be clearly observed.
Recently, raising the issue of Admiral Yi Chungmugong's (李忠武公) tomb and the relief issue for compatriots in Manchuria, they attempt a national mobilization, even if only spiritually. Such subtle influence should not be underestimated, I believe. The Chosŏn Ilbo is also similar.

Reporter: Do you see any potential for the nationalist strata forces to grow further in the future?
Chŏng: No. I believe there isn't. There will only be decline. <5>

**兩勢力의比較 (Comparison of the Two Forces)**
**Kim Yaksu**

Reporter: Please compare the two major forces of the current social movement and national movement. When we say forces, we limit it to organizations with clear guiding principles and strength derived from training, excluding unorganized, dispersed forces. Conversely, underground forces not visible to us are also excluded.
First, I'll ask about the nationalist camp. Where do you see the central force of this camp today?
Kim: Generally, a comparative study of the two forces is not an easy task. Social conditions are extremely complex and multifaceted, making it difficult to distinguish which force is which at a glance. Moreover, in a colony, these two forces often possess overlapping characteristics in the process of the anti-[Imperialist] (反X) movement. However, let's try to answer simply within the scope we can discuss today. Whether it is the central force of the national movement is not yet time to say, but as an organization striving to grasp organization, training, and guiding spirit, there is the Hŭngsadan (興士團).
If discussing An Ch'angho's (安昌浩) Hŭngsadan as an overseas entity is inappropriate, we could point to the Tong'uhoe (同友會) of Yi Kwangsu (李光洙) and others, which shares the same root but is a different branch. Both the Hŭngsadan and the Tong'uhoe are not militant organizations but rather groups composed only of leaders and staff members sitting in the rear. Furthermore, with only about 500(?) members, they cannot be called such a formidable force. If their movement has any significance, it would be nothing more than the selection of personnel to be deployed in the future. The result will be self-isolation from the entire nation. In other words, it could be called the eternal refuge for nationalists. However, in reality, the existence of the Hŭngsadan organization is indeed a fact.

Reporter: Isn't the significance of the Hŭngsadan's existence due to its satellites? Isn't it because there are many influential satellites scattered around the organization, orbiting it?
Kim: There is that point. Those are a certain newspaper company (某新聞社) and a part of Christianity, etc. Aren't there many people in both the Tong-a Ilbo and the Tong'uhoe who have a foot in both camps?
Also, the spirit and actions of the two places are quite similar, and I understand that the influence of the Hŭngsadan has significantly penetrated today's Christianity. It's a fact that the An Ch'angho faction (安昌浩系) and the Yi Sŭngman faction (李承晩系) have historically long infiltrated Christianity.
However, regarding their influence, the latter [Yi Sŭngman faction] is said to be much larger. In this way, perhaps the newspaper, its management layer, a part of Christianity, the P'yŏngan Province (平安道) merchant/industrialist stratum, etc.—all these external forces might have magnified the Hŭngsadan's existence, but anyway, within the nationalist camp, it could be called a sort of questionable group. <6>

Reporter: How do you view the religious organizations?
Kim: Religious organizations can also be seen as a contingent of the national camp. Among them, I believe Christianity is the most influential. Others seem to rank Ch'ŏndogyo first for its orderly control and thorough training, but in my view, Christianity seems strongest. On what grounds do I see it that way? It is judged so through its evenly spread influence in the regions, various activities, and personnel.

Reporter: The Production Promotion Society (物産獎勵會)?
Kim: I believe it is not yet an entity deserving significant evaluation.

Reporter: Anything else besides that?
Kim: Probably not much else. If there are, it would be newspaper companies and the like. They raise issues like Admiral Yi Chungmugong's (忠武公) tomb and the relief problem for compatriots in Manchuria (在滿同胞救濟問題) to heighten national consciousness. However, this cannot be seen as a concentrated, formidable force of one side.

Reporter: Then please tell me about the forces in the social movement camp.
Kim: As things stand now, the forces of the labor-peasant stratum reside in the Labor Federation (勞總), Peasant Federation (農總), Youth Federation (靑總), Kŭn'uhoe (槿友會), etc. This is commonly acknowledged by anyone discussing overt movements, so I believe there's no need to reiterate it.

Reporter: And also?
Kim: Besides that, various class-based forces (階級的 勢力) are encompassed, forming one force. For example, anti-religious organizations (反宗敎的 團體), or various associations of class-conscious artists, musicians, writers, etc., standing in one line to form something like a Proletarian Cultural Federation (푸로 文化聯盟), could possess broad influence.

Reporter: And also?
Kim: And then, a contingent of the national left-wing (民族左翼) exists as an auxiliary force.

Reporter: Beyond the three Federations, there are other camps of class struggle. The Korean Peasant Society (朝鮮農民社) faction and Korean Labor Society (朝鮮勞働社) faction within Ch'ŏndogyo, Mr. Yi Sŏnghwan's (李晟煥氏) All-Korea Peasant Union (全朝鮮農民組合) faction, and other cooperative unions, consumer unions, etc. How do you view these?
Kim: I believe they will amount to nothing more than forces that cannot be entirely disregarded. Especially concerning the cooperative union movement, in the current situation today, it is merely deception.

Reporter: The merchant/industrialist stratum and landlord class, gradually declining and going bankrupt due to the economic depression—couldn't they, out of the sorrow of disillusionment and rebellious psychology, form a potent force?
Kim: It would be right not to expect it. The more that happens, the more they lose strength and will only resort to flattery. (Remainder omitted - original text) <7>

**Summary**

This 1932 roundtable discussion features interviews with An Chaehong (nationalist perspective), Chŏng Unyŏng (socialist perspective), and Kim Yaksu (comparative perspective) analyzing the strengths and structures of nationalist and socialist movements in colonial Korea. An identifies religious organizations (Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, Buddhism) and newspapers (Chosŏn Ilbo, Tong-a Ilbo) as key nationalist centers post-Sin'ganhoe dissolution, albeit dispersed. Chŏng highlights the organized socialist power within the Labor, Peasant, and Youth General Federations (三總), claiming around 60,000 conscious members, while dismissing social democratic groups like Yi Sŏnghwan's union and Ch'ŏndogyo's Nongminsa as marginal or reactionary. Kim Yaksu offers a nuanced comparison, pointing to the Hŭngsadan/Tong'uhoe as an intellectually significant but isolated nationalist group linked to the Tong-a Ilbo and parts of Christianity, while affirming the three Federations' centrality for socialists, supplemented by cultural groups and the "national left-wing." Both socialist commentators predict the decline of nationalist forces, especially religious ones.

For historians, this article offers valuable contemporary insights into the perceived landscape of Korean political movements shortly after the Sin'ganhoe's collapse. Particularly interesting are the differing assessments of religious groups' roles (central nationalist forces vs. declining entities), the detailed breakdown of socialist organizations' membership claims, the explicit identification of factionalism (e.g., An Ch'angho vs. Yi Sŭngman factions in Christianity, socialist rejection of social democrats), and the acknowledged links between organizations like Hŭngsadan/Tong'uhoe and the Tong-a Ilbo. The discussion reflects the intense ideological competition and organizational efforts shaping the anti-colonial struggle during this period.

安...민족주의적 세력으로 말하면 그동안 우리에게 不可避的으로 닥치어온 여러 가지 客觀的 情勢때문에 강대한 조직을 가질 시간과 기회가 업시 그냥 力量이 분산된 대로 지내어 온 것이 사실이엇슴니다. 그러키에 노동조합이나 농민조합에서 가지는 것 가튼 一定한 규율미테 이루어진, 단체적 훈련을 통한 집결체는 몃낫이 아니 되지 만은 그러터래도 각지각처에 민족주의자라고 부를 수 잇고 민족주의단체라고 볼 수 잇는 巨然한 세력들 蔚然히 존재해 잇스며 또 존재할뿐더러 맹렬히 성장되어 나가고 잇는 것은 사실임니다.
記者...新幹會를 諸分散된 민족주의층 세력의 領導勢力으로 보아 오든 터인데 이제 解消過程에 처한 오늘에 잇서서는 아조 완전히 「消失된 過去의一勢力」으로 밧게 더 평가할 수 업겟슴니까.
安...그러치요 新幹會로서의 세력이란 인제다 분산된 것으로 보는 것이 올켓지요.
記者...그러타면 지금의 主要勢力은 어듸에 잇다고 보겟슴니까.＜2＞

記者...宗敎團體라면?
安...朝鮮에서 第一큰 宗敎團體를 헤자면 天道敎, 基督敎, 佛敎의 셋인데 그들이 민족주의단체라고 표방하고 아니하고는 別問題로 하고 最短時日안의 過去史에서 그네의 역할을 본배 잇슴으로 一般은 이러케 규정짓는대로 큰 틀님이 업슬 줄 암니다.

記者...그러면 基督敎와 佛敎는?
安...基督敎는 최근에 朝鮮耶蘇敎聯合公議會아래 각지의 세력이 한데 뭉처지고 잇슴니다. 海外에 나갓든 인재들도 만히 도라오고, 새로운 조직과 훈련이 잇스려 하는 중이며, 佛敎亦 百萬圓의 中央敎務院인 財團法人을 결성하여 가지고 또 佛敎靑年總同盟까지 조직하고서 교육에 산업에 큰 노력을 하려하고 잇는 모양입니다.

記者...第一 궁금한것은 「朝鮮物産獎勵會」에 대한 평가임니다. 物産獎勵會는 民族主義的補助勢力의 地置에 서고나 잇슴니까.

記者...그러타면 그를 마치 外地의 消費組合, 協同組合이 非常時에 하는 특수역할 가튼 것을 마틀수 잇는 것인 줄로 볼 것이겟슴니까.

安...신문은 그 本質的인 媒介力에 의하야 운동과 민중을 密着식히게 하는 객관적 가치는 크지만은 社가 직접운동의 主流가 되어 움지겨야 하리라고 볼 時機는 벌서 지나간 줄노 암니다.(五行略-원문)

安...全朝鮮과 海外를 통털어 新興勢力層을 중심으로 분포되어 잇슴니다.  지방별로는 公開할 성질이 아닙니다.

安...남의 신문사를 云謂하기는 거북한 노릇이지만 東亞日報의 세력도 勿論 큰줄로 암니다.
記者...海外의 민족주의적 세력에는 一切 言及을 피하겟슴니다만은 다만 한가지 朝鮮안에 뿌럭지를 가지고 잇는 興士團系를 엇더케 보심니까.  더구나 東亞日報社와의 관련성에 잇서서.

兩勢力의比較
金若水

記者...現下의 社會運動과 民族運動의 兩大세력을 비교하여 말슴하여 주세요. 세력이라 하야도 그것은 確然하게 指導情神이 서고 훈련을 통한 力量을 가진 단체에 限할 것으로 아직 未組織分散勢力은 論外로 할 것임니다. 또 그 반대로 吾人의 시야에 비최지 안는 地下的 勢力 가튼 것도 亦論外로 하고 말슴임니다.
먼저 민족주의진영에 대하여 뭇겟슴니다. 오늘 이 진영의 중심세력이 어듸 잇다고 보심니까.
金...대체로 두 세력의 비교연구는 容易한 노릇이 안이외다. 그것은 사회사정이 대단히 復雜多端하야 一瞥만으로는 이것이 이 쪽 세력 저것이 저 쪽 세력이라고 구분하기 곤란 때가 만슴니다. 더구나 植民地에 잇서 反X運動上 이 두세력은 때에 따라 과정상의 互交的 특질도 만히 가지고 잇슴으로. 그러나 오늘 우리들이 말할 수 잇는 범위안으로만 간단하게 대답하여 보기로 하지요. 민족운동의 중심세력인 與否까지는 아직 말할 시기가 아니지만 그 조직과 훈련과 지도정신을 붓잡으랴하는 단체로 興士團이 잇다고 하겟슴니다.
安昌浩의 興士團을 海外의 것이라고 거론하기 滋味업다면 同根異枝인 李光洙 等의 同友會를 가르치겟지요. 興士團이나 同友會는 모다 투쟁적 단체가 아니고 後方에 안저 지도자 참모자들로만 집결된 단체일 뿐더러 회원도 겨우 5백(?)을 가진 점으로 보아 그러케 巨然한 세력이라 할 수 업슬 뿐 아니라 그 운동에 意義가 잇다면 미래에 배치할 인물의 選擧 그것 밧게는 아무것도 업슬 것이오. 그 결과로 全民族으로부터 自己孤立化를 초래하게 될 것이올시다.  말하자면 민족주의자의 영원한 隱遁所라 할 것이나 그러나 하여간 현실에 잇서서 興士團이라는 단체가 잇는 것만은 사실이올시다.
記者...興士團의 존재가 큰 것은 거기에는 衛星이 잇는 까닭이 아니겟슴니까 그 단체의 주위에 흐터저 잇스면서 그를 싸고 도는 有力한 衛星이 만히 잇는 까닭이 아니겟슴닛가.
金...그 點이 잇지요. 그것은 某新聞社와 基督敎의 일부 等이지요. 東亞日報와 同友會에는 위선 가튼 사람이 兩者에 발한 쪽식 드리어노코 잇슨이가 만치 안슴닛가?
또 두 곳의 정신과 동작이 어슥비슥하고 그리고 오늘의 基督敎내부에도 興士團의 세력이 상당히 드러와잇는 줄 암니다. 원래 基督敎안에는 安昌浩系 李承晩系가 역사적으로 오래 침투되어 잇는 것이 사실임니다.
그런데 그 세력에 잇서서는 後者가 훨신 크다함니다. 이모양으로 혹은 신문 그 신문의 경영자층 또 基督敎 中 一部 平安道 商工層 등이 모든 外國의 세력이 興士團의 존재를 더 크게 한 점이 잇겟스나 엇잿든 민족주의의 陣營에서는 일종의 의문집단이라 하겟지요.＜6＞

記者...宗敎團體는 엇더케봄닛가?
金...宗敎團體도 民族陣營의 一部隊라고 볼 수 잇겟지요. 그중에도 基督敎가 가장 有勢한 줄로 암니다. 남들은 天道敎를 통제의 整然, 훈련의 周倒한 점에서 첫손가략에 꼽는 모양이나 내가 보기에는 亦基督敎가 第一 성한 것 갓슴니다. 무슨 근거로 그러케 보느냐고? 그것은 지방에 펼처잇는 골고루의 세력과 여러 가지 사업과 인물을 통하여 그러케 판단됨니다.

記者...物産獎勵會는?
金...그것은 아직 그러케 중요평가할 존재가 아니라고 봄니다.
記者...그밧게는?
金...별로 업슬걸요. 잇다면 新聞社 등이 잇지요. 忠武公의 墓所問題도 끄으러들고 在滿同胞救濟問題 가튼것도 끄으러들고서 민족의식을 고조하는 일이 잇지요. 그러나 이것은 집결된 一方의 雄的勢力이라고 볼수 업겟지요.
記者...그러면 사회운동의 진영세력에 대하야 말슴하여 주세요.
金...지금 현상으로 말한다면 勞農層의 세력은 勞總, 農總, 靑總, 槿友會 등에 잇지요. 이것은 표현운동을 말하는 이로 누구나 다-공통하게 인정하는 바이니 새삼스러히 말치 안어도 조흘 줄 암니다.
記者...또 그리고?
金...그 밧게 여러 가지 階級的 勢力이 망라되어 그것이 한 세력을 이를 터이겟지요. 즉 反宗敎的 團體 등이나 또는 階級的 美術家, 音樂家, 文學家 等 諸多의 結社가 한 계열에 서서 푸로 文化聯盟을 형성하는 것 가튼 폭이 널분 세력을 가질 수 잇겟지요.
記者...또 그리고는?
金...그리고는 民族左翼의 一部隊가 보조세력으로 잇게 되지요.

記者...三總이외에도 계급투쟁의 진영이 잇지요. 天道敎내의 朝鮮農民社系, 朝鮮勞働社系及李晟煥氏의 全朝鮮農民組合系 기타 協同組合 消費組合 등도, 이것은 어떠케 보심니까.
金...그것은 全然 算外로 칠수는 업슬 세력이란 程度에 끄칠 것인 줄노 암니다. 더구나 協同組合運動에 이르러는 그것은 오늘 현상에 잇서서는 오직 기만일 따름이외다.
記者...經濟恐慌에 차츰 몰락파산되어 가는 商工層, 地主階級은 환멸의 비애에서 반발적 심리로 한 有力한 세력을 이를 것가치도 생각할 수 엄겟슴니까.
金...기대치 안는 것이 올켓지요 .그네는 그럴사록-힘을 일코 오직 阿諛가 잇슬 것 뿐이니까. (下略-원문)＜2＞
