Cultural Appreciation Notes of Ewha Womans College Literature and Music Department Students
						Yi Yŏng-su et al.

Our company has recently decided to hear the cultural appreciations from students in the literature and music departments spanning various colleges and universities within Korea (朝鮮內). As the very first installment, we have brought students from the literature and music departments of Ewha Womans College (梨花女子專門學校) to appear in this magazine. Going forward, we plan to feature cultural appreciation notes from students of other schools such as the literature department of Keijō Imperial University (京城帝國大學), the literature department of Yŏnhŭi College (延專), etc. We note here that among the Ewha students this time, some have written under anonymity.

Literature Dept., Yi Yŏng-su (李英述)

Literature

"Three Daughters" (딸 삼형제): This novel depicts the characters of three <180> daughters, but its expression, such as dialogue, feels somewhat unnatural. As I kept reading, there were many parts that felt tedious. I didn't look forward to the next part; rather, I finished reading it out of reluctance. The beginning of the novel is written in a report style (報告式), making it rather uninteresting. However, it effectively expressed the differences in the three characters.

Film

"Wayside Stone" (路傍の石): The protagonist, a child, grows up in a difficult home from a young age, facing continuous misfortune. Unable to pursue the studies he desires, he works as a servant in someone else's house, enduring abuse from his master until he finally quits and, though still a child, sets out on a new path. It truly expresses the customs of this world humorously (?).

Literature Dept., Kim Ok-sun (金玉順)

Literature

"The Story of Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人傳): I could further learn from the wife's genius and struggling efforts, burning with patriotic fervor.
"The Good Earth" (大地): I am moved by the wife who embraced her husband with a broad and large heart even when the once-innocent husband became dissolute.
"Gone with the Wind" (風と共に去りぬ)<181>
"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): I resonate with Chŏng-guk's view on love.
"Moonlit Night" (달밤): It writes about Mr. Yi's (李先生) life, making me want to glimpse his life, befitting a literary man.

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): I could understand what kind of women contemporary men desire, and I am moved by the principal's character.

Literature Dept., Kim Ŭi-su (金義洙)

Literature

"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): Chŏng-guk's lively view on love was quite moving.
"Bruck Theater" (부륵 劇場): I felt the futility of love and art.

Film

"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成 交響樂): The agony of lost love was consoled by music.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의 少女): I was impressed by the innocent Durbin's (떠-빈) sincere love for her father and her musical passion.

Literature Dept., Chŏn Su-yŏn (全壽宴)

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): A child, upon seeing good and beautiful things, simply feels "It's good" with an overflowing heart, without restraint. The inability to sort out various feelings is because the limbs (四肢) cannot yet move evenly. Laughter and crying are also vague. The mindset of girls who dislike distance, that too is an ironic aspect forged by environment; there, inevitably, the voice crying out for the freedom of a pure heart (純情) is heard. A life growing up without the love of mother, father, siblings, and teachers, a life not receiving the love of the environment—there, only the terribly twisted hearts of girls grow; there is no true laughter and tears of life. But truth lives everywhere—laughter and tears that can bloom from love, a pure heart (純心) blossoming. Reformation lies not in suppression, power, and moldy (곰팽이 쓴) conventions, but solely resides in living love. Yvonne's (이반누) living words, "Teach them to live, to laugh," surely, teaching finds its meaning in nurturing burgeoning life (生). The desire to live out youth, which comes but once, must be a wish for salvation. Youth calls for freedom—where laughter blooms and tears also flow. The expression of laughter and tears, the expression of life trying to save freedom and youth. Nellie (네리) would have lost her youth and freedom without Yvonne (이반누). One cannot blame innocence (天眞)—. Unspeakable joy, sorrow, all are beautiful.<182> Yvonne's (이반누) sorrowful face saying, "The prisoners are not the girls in the reformatory; it is us," shining because of the reformatory work—no, shining because of that living thought—is heartachingly desolate and beautiful.

Literature Dept., Mun Hae-ra (文海羅)

Literature

"The Story of Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人傳): I admired Madame Curie's talent (才操) and ceaseless effort.
"The Good Earth" (大地): I was surprised by Pearl Buck's (펄퍽) meticulous observation skills and learned something about China (支那).
"Portrait of a Mother" (母の肖像): I resolved to learn from Pearl Buck's (펄퍽) mother. A splendid woman.
"Gone with the Wind" (風と共に去りぬ): Mitchell's (밋첼) transformation was very interesting. It was a very interesting novel, written naturally.
"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): I liked Chŏng-guk's thoughts and disposition. If a woman's life is like that, I don't want to get married.

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): I agreed with the teacher's educational policy. I held back tears seeing Wedd's(?) character naturally changing (for the better).
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오케스트라의少女): Stokowski's (스토코우스키) conducting and Durbin's (떠-빈) singing were good.

Music

"Kreutzer" (크로이싸아): I watched, completely absorbed.
"Im Sang-hŭi" (任祥姬), "Kye Chŏng-sik" (桂貞植), "Yi Ae-nae" (李愛內): Their spirits (혼) were very good.

Literature Dept., Sŏng Pok-sun (成福順)

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): It was a film worth watching as a woman. Seeing the life in the reformatory, I realized a lot.
"Wayside Stone" (路傍の石): I regained hope and reflected upon myself. The desire to strive was rekindled.

Literature Dept., Chŏng Sun-hŭi (鄭順姬)

Literature

"The Story of Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人傳): The fact that Madame Curie devoted herself solely to research, disregarding her own well-being, and achieved success is worth learning for us women.
"The Good Earth" (大地): That a Westerner depicted Eastern China (支那) so well.
"Young People" (若い人): The psychology between teacher and student.
"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): It well depicted the three sisters living unhappily or happily in their respective different circumstances.

Film<183>

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): It showed that what reforms people is, above all else, love.
"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成交響樂): The poor musician Schubert (슈-벨트), despite receiving patronage from an aristocratic family, absolutely refused to be bound (매우지 않고) by them and focused solely on his music.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의 少女): Stokowski's (스도코푸스키-) conducting and Deanna Durbin's (데이아나, 떠-빈) music remain in my impression.
"Wayside Stone" (路傍の石): A boy born into an unfortunate family becomes a servant (使人) in another house, yet strives endlessly for the studies he hopes for. I anticipate that boy's success will come in the future (後日).

Music Dept., Ch'a Chŏng-nyŏ (車貞女)

Music

"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): Amidst singing many operas based on popularity and technique, it is good that he sings many weighty and substantial art songs (歌謠), especially German Lieder (獨逸 *-드).
"Kreutzer" (크로이싸아): His interpretation of the musical pieces was relatively good. His technique was good, and I thought his pedaling was very clean. The solemn opening chord of Beethoven's Pathétique Sonata (파테틱 쏘나타) permeated deeply into my heart (肺腑). Perhaps due to aging compared to his performance four years ago, the effect was lesser. I actually think Leo Sirota's (레오시로타) performance was better.
"Chopin" (쇼팡): Although he has fewer works than other composers, his unique style is what I like best. Truly the poet of the piano. I wonder who could have fully and subtly expressed the piano's capabilities if Chopin hadn't existed.
"Miura Tamaki" (三浦環): She is someone who desecrates the sacred music stage. I hope she never shows that base attitude again.
"Pak Kyŏng-hŭi" (朴景嬉): I hope she comes out after much effort and study.

Music Dept., Yi Sun-nyŏ (李順女)

Film

"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成交響樂): Although it was quite successful as a film work, I am not particularly impressed by the content.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의少女): Durbin's (떠-빈) singing is good from a musical perspective.

Music

"Kreutzer" (크로이싸아): I was impressed by a performance much more powerful than I had anticipated, assuming he would be aged.
"Miura Tamaki" (三浦環): Shall we say she is too emotional for her age?
"Beethoven" (베토벤): The deep music of the Sage of Music (樂聖) becomes more interesting the more I listen (드를수록).
"Kye Chŏng-sik" (桂貞植): As a senior, I revere her, but as for art...<184>
"Yi Ae-nae" (李愛內): Her artistic and research-oriented attitude is admirable.
"Chopin" (쇼팡): Because his works are so feminine and poetic, they tend towards excessive sentimentality.

Music Dept., Ch'ae Ki-ae (蔡基愛)

Literature

"Ignorance" (無明) (Yi Kwang-su 李光洙): The protagonist's character is alive.
"Moonlit Night" (달밤) (Yi T'ae-jun 李泰俊): There is a sense of reality throughout, and especially in "After Planting the Flowers" (꽃은 심어 놓고), the feelings of one leaving their hometown are better expressed.

Music

"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): His classical and composed posture was truly good. I too wanted to sing once.
"Kreutzer" (크로이싸아): I felt a sense of mystery.
"Zimbalist" (진바리스트): Impressed by his technique.
"Kye Chŏng-sik" (桂貞植): The subtlety of tone color, musical interpretation.
"Yi Ae-nae" (李愛內): Her musical touch gave a sense of mystery.

Music Dept., Chŏng Sam-yŏng (鄭三英)

Music

"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): He sings many German songs, and it's always powerful, seemingly piercing the audience's heart (肺腑), which is very good.
"Chopin" (쇼팡): The works of Chopin, a poet and musician, are sentimental, all melodies are beautiful, and I never tire of hearing them.
"Kreutzer" (크로イ싸아): When he played the piano, it was good how he made the piece his own and played freely and beautifully.
"Im Sang-hŭi" (任祥姬): She sings many operas, and whenever I hear her songs, my heart feels peaceful.

Music Dept., Ŏm Sŏng-ŏp (嚴成業)

Literature

"Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人): I saw the brilliant victory of that lady, shining for all ages after twists and turns (曲折). But isn't that also a victory predicated on innate genius?
"Love" (사랑): Isn't it an ideal love that cannot be found in reality?
"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): The contradiction between innate personality and reality, or Chŏng-guk's audacity during her student days, also inevitably clashes (부닦일) with reality...
"Moonlit Night" (달밤): The author's insight (透察) into one aspect of society and satire through the work.

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): The different (?) beauty of women and the dual life of men.
"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成交響樂): The end/fate (末途) of heartbreak and the male protagonist's romantic life.<185>
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의少女): In art, honor and gold must be powerless.
"Warm Current" (暖流): I admired the progress of Japanese cinema.
"Sŏnghwangdang" (城隍堂): It well depicted the reality of present-day Korea (朝鮮).
"Song of Love" (愛戀頌): The cruel joke (惡戱) of love, money, and misunderstanding.
"The Wanderer" (나그네): Whose fault is it really that the male protagonist has no choice but to set off on the path of an endless wanderer?

Music

"Fujiwara" (藤原): Isn't it ignorance (모름이) of Korea's (朝鮮) musical level?
"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): A very composed and artistic musician.
"Miura Tamaki" (三浦環): Her music and expressions are incomprehensible.
"Im Sang-hŭi" (任祥姬): Was very good.
"Pak Kyŏng-hŭi" (朴景嬉): Is it a concert of true intention (眞意)?
"Kye Chŏng-sik" (桂貞植): A performance for the sake of music.
"Yi Ae-nae" (李愛內): An expression of many years' effort.

Music Dept., Ch'oe Pok-nyŏ (崔福女)

Literature

"The Story of Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人傳): I could better take inspiration from her genius talent and struggle.
"Young People" (若い人): I think being single as an educator is not effective, and suppressing emotions is too impossible.
"Three Daughters" (딸삼형제): I strongly resonated with Chŏng-guk's view on love.

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): The humane scenes were too sorrowful and elicited sympathy.
"Bruck Theater" (부륵劇場): Just feel more the futility of humanity and art.
"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成交響樂): The agony of heartbreak and the solace of music are quite reassuring.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의少女): I felt more the sincere devotion of the innocent Durbin (떠-빈) for her father, and I am thankful.

Music

"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): Not only his classical posture, but I envy his thick vocal volume and passionate intensity.
"Chopin" (쇼팡): His elegant compositions are very good.
"Im Sang-hŭi" (任祥姬): As she is our teacher, I find nothing but perfection in her. Her tone color is beautiful, and her expression and passion entirely draw the listener into the music. Opera is even more so.
"Pak Kyŏng-hŭi" (朴景嬉): Her tone color is beautiful.
"Kye Chŏng-sik" (桂貞植): She is the musician type (타잎). It's a feeling that lingers after listening.<186>

Music Dept., Pae Chŏng-sun (裵貞順)

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): This film is truly humane, and the appearing characters, though newcomers, expressed emotions very well.
"Unfinished Symphony" (未完成交響樂): This is a musical yet truly interesting (滋味있는) film. However, I wished there was more music.
"One Hundred Men and a Girl" (오-케스트라의少女): Durbin's (떠-빈) young and beautiful expression is good. However, I wish there was more music.
"The Wanderer" (나그네): This is a Korean (朝鮮) film that well depicted our circumstances. We also wanted to live by improving our lives somewhat.

Music

"Chŏng Hun-mo" (鄭勳謨): He sings many German songs, and I was greatly moved by his large vocal volume and passionate performance.

Literature Dept., Kim Kŭm-nan (金金蘭)

Literature

"The Story of Madame Curie" (キユリ夫人傳): A genius scholar burning with patriotic fervor, she is the possessor of an angelic beautiful heart, thus a wise mother and good wife.
"Gone with the Wind" (風と共に去りぬ): Scarlett's (스카-렛도) character is good. Padora's (?) (파도라) wild personality too.
"Love" (사랑): Could such love exist in the human world? For us common folk (俗人), it feels too much like fantasy, hence a sense of emptiness.

Film

"Prison Without Bars" (格子なき牢屋): A group finding happiness from misfortune. A woman abandoning worldly affairs and dedicating herself to her work; she was called a saint. Many (뭇) men fear such women.

Music

"Beethoven" (베토벤): No matter what, he is the Sage of Music (樂聖).<187>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* compiles brief cultural reviews (literature, film, music) from students in the Literature and Music departments of Ewha Womans College in Seoul. The students comment on a mix of Korean, Japanese, and Western works, including novels like "Three Daughters," "The Good Earth," and "Gone with the Wind," films like "Prison Without Bars," "Unfinished Symphony," and "Wayside Stone," and musical performances by figures like Leonid Kreutzer, Miura Tamaki, and Korean artists Chŏng Hun-mo and Im Sang-hŭi. Their comments reflect personal tastes, moral judgments, and aspirations, often touching on themes of love, hardship, art, patriotism, and the ideal roles for women and artists. For historians, the article offers a valuable glimpse into the cultural consumption patterns and intellectual concerns of educated Korean women during the late colonial period, revealing the complex interplay of Korean, Japanese, and Western cultural influences, as well as nascent feminist ideas and social commentary embedded within their appreciations. The direct commentary on Japanese films and performers, alongside Korean ones, within the context of colonial Korea is particularly noteworthy.

梨花女專 文科 音樂科 學生의 文化 鑑賞記
						李英述 외
금번 본사에서, 朝鮮內 각 전문학교와 대학을 亙하여 문과, 음악과의 학생들로부터 그 문화 감상을 듣기로 하는 바, 그 제일착으로 梨花女子專門學校 문과, 음악과의 학생을 본지에 동원 등장 시켰다. 앞으로는 京城帝國大學 문과, 延專 문과 등 諸校의 학생 문화 감상기를 실리기로 한다. 그런데 今回 梨專 학생 중에는 익명으로 집필한 이가 있음을 이에 말하여 둔다.

文科 李英述

文學

「딸 삼형제」 이 소설은 3인의 딸의 성격을 나타낸<180> 것인데 그 표현에 있어서 대화라든지가 좀 자연스럽지 못하다. 자꾸 읽어가는 데 좀 지루한 데가 많다. 다음 것이 기다려지지 않고 싫여서 읽고 만다. 소설 시작은 보고식으로 되어 좀 재미없다. 만은 3인의 성격의 相違點을 잘 표현시켰다.

映畵

「路傍の石」 주인공 아이가 어려서부터 어려운 집에서 자라나 끝끝내 불행만 닥처온다. 자기가 원하는 공부도 더 못하고 남의 집에서 고용사리를 해가는 데 그 주인의 학대를 받다 못해 나종에는 그것을 집어치고 어린 아이로 새 출발의 길에 나선다. 참 이 세상의 풍습을 해학적으로 표현했다.

文科 金玉順

文學

「キユリ夫人傳」 애국열에 불타는 부인의 천재적이면서도 분투 노력한 것을 한층 본받을 수 있었다.
「大地」 순진했든 남편이 방탕했을 적에도 넓고 큰마음으로 포옹한 부인에게 감격한다.
「風と共に去りぬ」<181>
「딸삼형제」 정국의 연애관에 共鳴한다.
「달밤」 李선생의 생활을 썼는데 문학가다운 그의 생활을 엿보고 싶어진다.

映畵

「格子なき牢屋」 現世 남성들이 어떠한 여성을 요구하는지 알 수 있었고, 園長의 인격에 감동된다.

文科 金義洙

文科 全壽宴

映畵

「格子なき牢屋」 어린애는 좋은 것 아름다운 것을 보면 함부로 가슴 벅차게 「좋다」하고 느낄 뿐이다. 느껴움을 가지 가지 추려 세우지 못함은 아직 四肢가 고루 움지겨지지 않는 까닭. 웃고 울음이 또한 막연하다. 별로 거리를 두기 싫은 소녀들의 심경, 그도 환경이 빚어낸 皮肉的 面, 거기엔 반듯이 순정의 자유를 부르짖는 소리가 들린다. 어머님 아버지 또 형제 그리고 선생의 사랑이 없이 자라는 삶 환경의 사랑을 못받는 삶, 거기엔 지지리 비틀어진 소녀들의 마음이 자랄 뿐 인생의 참된 웃음과 눈물은 없음이다. 그러나 진실은 어디고 산다― 사랑에서 눈 틀 수 있는 웃음, 울음, 피어나는 純心. 감화는 억제와 권력 그리고 곰팽이 쓴 규모에 있지 않고 오로지 살아나는 사랑 속에 깃든다. 「이반누」의 「저들에게 사는 것을, 웃는 것을 가르켜 준다」는 살아있는 말, 모름지기 가르킴이란 생성하는 生을 키워주는데 의의가 있을 것이다. 두 번 없는 청춘을 살려보려는 마음은 구원의 願望일 것이다. 청춘은 자유를 부르는 것― 웃음이 피어나고 눈물도 덛어 흐르는 것. 웃음과 울음의 표현, 자유와 청춘을 살리려는 인생의 표현. 「네리」는 「이반누」 없이는 그의 청춘과 자유를 상실했을 것이다. 天眞을 責할 수도 없고―. 말 할 수 없는 喜, 悲 모두 아름다운 것이다.<182>
「囚人은 感化院의 소녀들이 아니오. 우리들이라는」 「이반누」의 서글픈 얼굴로 感化院 사업으로 해서 빛나는― 아니 그 산 생각으로 해서 빛나는― 가슴쓰리게 처량한 아름다운 것이다.

文學

「キユリ夫人傳」 큐-리 부인이 자기 몸을 돌아보지도 않고 연구에만 충실하여 성공까지 이루운 점은 우리 여성으로 배울만 합니다.

「쇼팡」 다른 작곡가보다 작품은 적으나 그의 독특한 作風은 내가 제일 좋와한다. 참 피아노의 시인이다. 쇼팡이 없었든들 피아노의 성능을 완전히 미묘하게 표현할 자가 누구일가 생각된다.
「三浦環」 신성한 음악 무대를 더럽히는 자이다. 그 저열한 태도를 다시는 보이지 말기를.
「朴景嬉」 많이 노력하고 공부한 후 나오기를 바란다.

音樂科 李順女

映畵
