**Body Text Translation:**
Editorial Postscript

We cannot help but feel celebratory thinking of the establishment of the new Nationalist Government in Nanjing (南京) and the advance, in one respect, of the new order in East Asia (東亞). Furthermore, on April 7th, the fifth ceremony summoning the spirits of the war dead was held at Yasukuni Shrine (靖國 神社), and we express our condolences for these many heroic warriors who died for their country and became the cornerstone (柱礎) of the Sacred War (聖戰).

On the occasion of the convening of the important conference of the 13 provincial governors to decide various wartime policies, politics being reality, we have collected and published proposals and opinions from various gentlemen outside of government to reflect the popular will (民意) in this conference; we believe this feature deserves the public's attention. Also, the article on the conference of military instructors who are nurturing spirited and brave youth (勇兒) is also considered timely (詩宜).

The magazine exposition on "Culture and Industry" is also an expression of our sincerity (誠意) in wishing to contribute to construction at this time when the literary fortunes (文運) of this land are poised for a leap forward. We hope you will read it carefully. Furthermore, gathering and publishing 7 or 8 second works at once by the literary talents selected by the three major newspapers in the capital (都下) this spring (今春) is one of this month's points of pride for this magazine (本誌).

Finally, due to paper conservation (用紙 節約), we cannot increase the number of lines further, so we have decided to edit based strictly on the principle of emphasis (重點 主義的). The meaning of the "principle of emphasis" is to focus only on the most important topics and concentrate our editorial efforts on points of emphasis (力點). Also, from now on, we can only print a fixed number of copies (一定 數部), so we hope you will become a semi-annual or annual subscriber in advance. We were unable to send the March issue and last month's issue to those gentlemen (諸氏) who ordered late.

Publishing Department Communication (出版部 通信)

Our company's publishing department is now arranging its capital (資金) and personnel (人材) and intends to begin business on a large scale (大大的). As previously announced, the "Great Patriotic Speech Collection" (愛國 大演說集) is the first sign of this, and we will continue to publish 5 or 6 renowned works (名著) that will truly be beneficial (稗益) to the world. There are some currently under censorship (檢閱) and some already in printing; we will refrain from announcing them in advance, but their scope will extend to various fields such as politics, industry, culture, and literature/arts (文藝). We just do not know if progress will proceed as planned due to the paper shortage (用紙難), but our publishing department will devote its full effort (全力 傾到).<350>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the May 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* celebrates the establishment of the Wang Jingwei regime in Nanjing and commemorates Japanese soldiers enshrined at Yasukuni, framing both within the context of Japan's "Sacred War" and the "new order in East Asia." It highlights magazine features, including reader opinions on provincial governance, military instructors, culture/industry, and new writers. It also announces paper shortages leading to print run limitations and editorial focus changes, urging readers to subscribe early, and promotes upcoming publications from its publishing department, including a "Great Patriotic Speech Collection."

For historians, the article starkly reveals the magazine's pro-Japanese stance during the late colonial period, openly endorsing Japanese militarism and expansionist rhetoric ("Sacred War," "new order"). The mention of collecting "popular will" for the governors' conference, alongside the promotion of patriotic materials and discussion of wartime austerity (paper shortages), illustrates the complex interplay of state propaganda, controlled public discourse, and wartime mobilization efforts within colonial Korea. The casual references to censorship and the specific mention of the "Great Patriotic Speech Collection" further underscore the political climate.

編輯後記
南京이 신국민 정부가 수립되어 새로운 東亞의 질서가 일단 진전된 것을 생각하고 축하하는 마음을 금할 길이 없으며 또한 4월 7일 靖國 神社에 제5회의 영령 초혼식이 잇섯는데 聖戰의 柱礎가 된 이 여러 순국 勇士의 애도의 意를 표하엿나이다.

전시 하의 제 정책을 결정하는 중대한 13 도지사 회의가 개최됨에 제하야 정치는 현실인지라 이 회의에 민의를 반영시키려 재야 諸씨로부터 진언 의견을 수집 게재한 이번 기사는 세인의 주목에 値할 줄 아옵니다. 그러고 의기 용아를 양성하고 잇는 군사 교관 諸씨의 회의 기사 또한 詩宜를 어든 바인 줄 아나이다.

「문화와 산업」에 대한 誌上 박람회, 또한 이 땅 文運이 바야으로 약진하려는 이 때에 건설에 첨가하려는 우리의 성의의 표출이외다. 숙독하여 주실지며 또한 今春 都下 3대 신문사에 입선된 문단 수재의 제2작을 일거에 7, 8편을 모아 실은 것도 이 달 本誌의 자랑 중한 가지인 줄 아옵니다.

끝으로 용지 절약 때문에 항 수를 더 늘일 수는 업서 그야말로 重點 主義的 편집을 하기로 하였으니 重點主義란 뜻은 가장 중요한 제목에만 치중하며 역점 편집이 주력하려는 뜻이외다. 그러고 앞으로는 일정 數部 밧게 인쇄 못 하오니 미리 반년 혹은 1년 독자되여 주시기를 바라나이다. 지난 달과 3월호도 나종에 주문하신 諸씨에게는 보내 드리지 못 하엿나이다.

出版部 通信

본사 출판부는 이제 자금과 인재를 정비하고 대대적으로 사업을 시작하려 하나이다. 이에 예고한 바와 가치 「愛國 大演說集」은 이 첫소리이며 계속하여 진실로 세상에 稗益될 바 잇슬 명저 5, 6종을 계속 발간하겟나이다. 지금 검열 중의 것도 잇고 이미 인쇄 중의 것도 잇사온 바 미리 예고는 피하오나 정치, 산업, 문화, 문예 등 각 방면에 그 범위가 미치겟나이다. 다만 용지難 때문에 예정대로 進捗될른지 모르겟스나 우리 출판부로서는 전력을 傾到하겟나이다.<350>
