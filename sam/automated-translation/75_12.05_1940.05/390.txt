**Summary**

This document is the table of contents for a special feature in the May 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine, presenting a virtual "Chosŏn Culture and Industry Exposition". It outlines sections covering the political situation (focusing on Korean members in the Japanese Diet), the wartime volunteer soldier system (including propaganda elements like blood oaths and a visit by Yi Kwangsu), Korean culture (media, libraries, publishing, museums, historical academies), entertainment (film and theater), and a detailed province-by-province overview of local products, industries, and cultural/scenic landmarks across all 13 provinces of colonial Korea. For historians, this table of contents is valuable as it reflects the Japanese colonial administration's efforts to simultaneously promote assimilation and the war effort (volunteer system, emphasis on national industries like sardine fishing) while also cataloging and showcasing Korean culture and resources. The structure, blending political propaganda with extensive cultural and economic documentation, reveals the complexities of colonial knowledge production and control during the late 1930s and early 1940s.
