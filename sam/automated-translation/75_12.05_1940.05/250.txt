Notes from a Burning Heart
						P'ain

How could we part ways like this, on a path we happened to meet upon—
Certain you would call out before ten steps, sure you would seek me before twenty paces, yet while I hesitated, your form vanished into the waves of the street. How could I have been so foolish? Why does the blood of a true man fear so many things in heaven and earth? Sitting alone, I sigh in lament.

Oh, my beloved, I wish to see you as a crane (학두룸)—
As a crane that flies high in the sky, never setting its feet upon the earth; why would I even hope to see you as a Bodhisattva (陽者)? Perhaps I might just overhear the sound of your wings beating within the clouds. Truly, may you remain high above, becoming neither someone's wife nor mother, like that woman eternally leaning on a bridge in Florence, may your body and soul remain utterly pure and lofty.<144>

When the sun's scorching light strikes the earth, even the cooled soil reflects heat—
Likewise, even if you have grown cold, please let there be at least one reply to ten letters, please come even just one *ri* when the path to find you is a hundred. Though my own light is dim, when will it, like the sun, stir your heart and show a reaction of warmth? Ah, even so, O Sun, lend me your burning heat for just one day, just for her, I wish to become sunlight for a single day, to become it and shine upon the cooled earth.

If you cast me away, saying you don't know me, you don't know this desperate person—
Even if the waters of the Han River (한강) are the milk that nourishes all the million households of the capital (長安), though my lips burn and my heart turns to ash, parched and crackling, I will sever any thought of drinking even a single drop of water, and I will quietly flee this street, vanishing down a dark side path.

Truly, if you still don't know me, if this is all there is—
Without ever having danced once in this wide courtyard, without having called out to heaven even once unable to dance, I will cast away all my still-young life, and like an owl in the mountain fortress (山城) deep in the night, I will partner its cry, open the black fortress gate, and like a sinner, just disappear. Truly, if this is all there is. I—<145>

Is eternity a thousand years? If I cannot see the one I must see, even ten thousand years are futile. Is eternity just a single day? If I could be by her side, even if I died in antiquity, would that not be living eternally? Do you know this heart? Do you know this truth? Do you know this philosophy of a man boiling with passion? Therefore, if even giving longing is a sin, how can you even tell me to forget, casting away eternity?

It is late. They tell me to sit back. Though missing you yesterday was late, will not letting today pass just as it is also be regrettable tomorrow? Though this moment is later than yesterday, is it not earlier than tomorrow?—
Let the matter of being late or early be left to the mountains and rivers. Though it may be late, now I will wait without aging. At the street corner of youth, holding a full bouquet of flowers, I will always stand guard and wait. Even in the far-distant future, I will stand aside on the path, and after others, those happy others, have all passed by praising you with laughter, only then, perhaps as flower petals flutter down, I will stand back at a distance and quietly watch you. Watch you, and then leave.

Oh, you, you, my beloved you—
Now I cast down my brush and close my book. This sad heart, unable even to speak of longing to the one I long for, I close the pages of the longed-for chronicle. If '16, '17 were nightmares, until when will this dream torment me? (Night of the 15th)

**Summary**

This piece, "Notes from a Burning Heart," is a passionate, lyrical expression of unrequited love and despair written in 1940. The author, P'ain, laments his hesitation in approaching his beloved, idealizes her purity to an almost divine level (comparing her to a crane and a figure in Florence), and expresses intense longing, frustration at her coldness, and a desire to disappear if rejected completely. He questions the nature of time and eternity in relation to love and resolves to wait indefinitely, even if only to catch a glimpse of her from afar. From a historical perspective, the article is significant as a piece of personal, emotional Korean literature published during the late colonial period, a time of increasing cultural suppression. Its focus on intense individual feeling, using traditional poetic language mixed with modern references (Florence), showcases the resilience of Korean literary expression and the exploration of modern themes of love and alienation, potentially offering insights into personal coping mechanisms and artistic continuation under duress.

心火抄
						파인
어쩌다가 뵈온 길을, 이대로 갈라질 법 있사올가―
열 발자곡 채 안가서 부르올 것을, 스무 자곡 채 못넹겨 찾사올 것을, 망서리는 사이에 그 모습 그만 거리의 물결 속에 사라지셨네. 어쩌문 이리도 못났던고. 사내 대장부의 피, 천지에 두려워 하는 것 웨 이리 많던고, 두러앉아 혼자 탄식하옵네.

그리운 이 사람아, 내사 그대를 학두룸으로 뵈옵고저―
두 발 흙 우에 안 붙이시고 저 하늘 높이 나는 학두룸으로요, 양자인들 어찌 뵈옵기 바래랴. 행여 구름 속서 나래 치는 그 소리나 엿듣고저. 정말일네 높이 계셔서 남의 안해도 엄마도 안 되시고 후로렌스 돌다리에 영원히 기대 선 그 여인같이 육신과 마음이 모다 깨끗하게 높이 계서지이다.<144>

태양 뜨거운 빛, 대지를 쏘면 식었든 흙도 열을 반사하거든―
그렇게, 식으셨더라도 열장 편지에 단 한 장이나마, 찾는 길 백리일 적에 와주시는 길 단 1리나마 있어주소. 이 몸의 빛 적사오나 어느제면 태양 같이 그대 가슴 흔들어 열의 반응 뵈옵고 말리. 아아 그렇더래도 태양이여, 그 뜨거운 열 하루만 빌려 주소, 그이 위해 단 하루만 햇빛 되고저, 되여 식은 흙쏘아 보고저.

이렇게 그리운 사람을 모른다 모른다 내치신다면―
저 한강물이 장안 백만가호 골고로 추겨주는 젖물이라해도, 이 입설 타오르고 이 가슴이 재돼 바삭바삭 말라들지언정 단 한 방울의 물 마실 생각 끊고서, 나는 이 거리로 고요히 피하리다, 어두운 오솔길로 사라지고 마오리다.

정말일네, 이제 모르신다면 이 뿐이라면―
이 너른 마당에서 춤 한 번 추어 못보고, 못 춰 본채 저하늘서 기엘 한번 불러 못 본채, 아직도 살 젊음을 다 버리고 야밤중 산성의 부엉 소리나 짝하여 검은 성문 열고 죄인 같이 그만 사라져 버리리다. 정말 이 뿐이라면. 나는―<145>

영원이란 천년인가, 뵈올 이 못 뵈오면 만년도 헛되옴을, 영원이란 단 하론가. 그이 곁에 계셔주면 고대 죽사온들 그것 영원을 산 것 아니올가― 이 심정 아실는가, 이 진리 아실는가, 정열에 끓는 사내의 이 철학 아실는가, 그러기 그리움 주는 것도 죄이려든 어찌 잊으라 조차하시뇨, 영원을 버리시뇨.

늦었다. 두러 앉으라시노, 어제 못 뵈왔슴 늦었으나 내일 날엔 오늘 그냥 보냈음 또한 후회되지 않사올가, 이 자리가 어제보다 늦었으나 내일보다야 일르지 않사올가―
때에 늦고 일름이 있기야 산천에나 부치소서, 비록 늦었대도 이제는, 늙지 않고 기대리리다. 청춘의 길모통이에 꽃다발 가득 싸안고 언제든 지켜 기다리리다. 먼-훗날까지 그렇더래도 내사 오솔길에 피해 섰다가 남드리, 그 행복한 남드리 다 웃음으로 그대를 찬미하고 지나간 뒤 그제사 행여 꽃입살 날리올가, 저만치 물러서 가만히 뵈오리다, 뵈옵고 가오리다.

그대여, 그대여, 그리운 그대여―
이제는 붓을 던지고 책을 덮네, 그리운 이에, 그립단 말 조차 못하는 슬픈 이 심정, 그리든 연대기 책장을 덮네, 16,7년도 악몽이려든 이 꿈이 어느날까지 나를 괴롭히려는고.(15일 밤)
