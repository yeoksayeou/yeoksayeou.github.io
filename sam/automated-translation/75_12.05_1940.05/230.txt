**Body Text Translation:**
The Longevity and Rise/Fall of Human Life
						Pae Sangch'ŏl

Fifth, Noble Features (貴相)

Those whose face is dark but body is white, whose face is rough but body is fine, whose legs are short but hands are long, whose body is small but voice is large, whose fingers are longer than their palm, or whose face is short but eyes are long – all have noble features. They will achieve their desires whether seeking office, wealth, or fame.
A forehead that is strong, without lines, moles, or flaws, and gently slopes backward (like a liver lobe) is called a "tiger head" (虎頭). A chin that is slender like a swallow's beak is called a "swallow jaw" (燕頷). Having this "tiger head and swallow jaw" is a sign of great nobility (大貴格). If the Sun and Moon horns (日月角, areas on the forehead) protrude, or if the length of both hands reaches past one's knees, or if a fist can fit in the mouth and the tongue can touch one's nose tip (準頭), or if one walks leisurely (tiger's pace, 虎步), or walks without the body swaying (dragon's gait, 龍行), these are also signs of great nobility.
Bones rising straight up to the left of the Sun horn and right of the Moon horn are called "Gold Castle Bones" (金城骨) in physiognomy books; those with these bones will reach the rank of the Three High Ministers (三公, i.e., top officials). Bones rising on both sides of the center of the forehead (中正) are called "Dragon Horn Bones" (龍角骨); these also indicate great nobility. A bone rising from the glabella (印堂) to the celestial court (天庭, upper forehead) is called the "Celestial Pillar Bone" (天柱骨), and a bone rising straight from the celestial court to the crown of the head is called the "Hidden Rhinoceros Bone" (伏犀骨). Those with these two types of bones will reach the rank of the Three High Ministers. If the beard is like iron wire, or the ears are whiter than the face, or the eyes are black and clear, or the body is longer in the upper part and shorter in the lower, or the mouth is like the character "四" (Four-Character Mouth (四字口), mouth corners bright, neither upturned nor downturned but seeming slightly lifted)<136>, or one has 36 teeth, these indicate mid-level nobility (中貴格). If the celestial court is high and prominent and the chin (地閣) is square and round, or if urine flows like a stream of pearls and feces are square and fine, or if the teeth are white and large and the eyebrows clear and eyes fine (眉淸目秀), or if the mouth is shaped like a bow (Bow-Shaped Mouth (彎弓口), corners turned up, philtrum clear), and the lips are like vermilion, or the waist is round and back thick, or the bridge of the nose stands tall and the cheekbones (顴骨) on both sides are appropriately formed, these all indicate minor or mid-level nobility (小貴/中貴). Those whose appearance is imposing and fierce (威猛之相) or clear and refined (淸秀之相) will become greatly noble. If the body is short but the face long, or the face is square and long, or the shoulders and back are thick and heavy, or there are raised bones on the head, these all indicate nobility. Dragon eyes (龍眼) and phoenix eyes (鳳眼) are also signs of nobility. Dragon eyes are long and large, slightly upturned at the outer corners, with clear black and white parts, and look spirited. Phoenix eyes are long, with clear black and white, bright pupils, slightly recessed, and have a clear spirit, but are not as large as dragon eyes. If either dragon or phoenix eyes have yellow pupils or red veins, it breaks the noble pattern. If the voice is clear and resonant, or the hands and feet are like jade, one is noble. Those with strange and ancient-looking features (古怪之相), if not noble, will be wealthy. Identifying noble people can sometimes be unexpectedly difficult; people with pale faces and eyes and noses that seem crowded together often achieve mid-level nobility. Therefore, among noble people, there are light ones, heavy ones, clear ones, refined ones, rough ones, fine ones, thin ones, and fat ones, but nobility lies in having a refined charm (秀媚). Even if the form, bones, parts, and positions are good, without this refined charm, one may be noble but will not reach the level of a king or ruler.
Noble people have little hair on their heads, while base people have no hair on their bodies. If the bone structure looks extraordinary and the eyes have a clear spirit, one is a noble person. A square face and large ears indicate the potential for high office (first rank). Upright eyebrows and long eyes indicate the potential for the highest ministerial positions (三台). Furthermore, if a Korean person looks like a foreigner, or a Westerner looks like an East Asian, or an East Asian looks like a Westerner, it is a noble sign; they will make their name known through literature, society, politics, windfall fortune, or administration. The methods for judging nobility are numerous in the miscellaneous discussions below, so I will omit further details here, but the reader should keep in mind that great nobility is often found in the 8 Great Physiognomies (8大相), 8 Small Physiognomies (8小相), 5 Long Physiognomies (5長相), 5 Short Physiognomies (5短相), and 5 Small Physiognomies (5小相).

Sixth, Base/Common Features (賤相)

Base features often resemble poor features, but there are also quite a few differences. A nose bridge that is sharp like a sword blade and eyes exactly like bee eyes (蜂睛) are ominous and base. A head that is pointed with protruding bones, like a roe deer's head (獐頭), and eyes round and bulging like a rat's (鼠目) are base features. A large mouth with thin lips, uneven teeth (參差), a face lacking definition (輪廓), rough palms, and many lines on the forehead are the features of an entertainer (actor), a base type (賤格). Red eyes, coarse eyebrows, rough bone structure, a lack of mental presence whether talking, laughing, sitting, or lying down, many lines on the forehead, and horizontally stretched skin and flesh indicate a lowly base person (下賤之相). A flat or sunken back of the head, and a flat or sunken mountain root (山根, between eyes) or year/longevity bridge (年壽, nose bridge) also indicate a base type. A large body<137> that is turbid and lacks energy, with rough hands and feet, indicates a base person. A crooked, low, or flat nose and eyes without luster are signs of a slave. A small head, sheep's eyes (羊眼), and uneven three sections (三停, forehead, midface, lower face) all indicate a base type.

Seventh, Poor Features (貧相)

Poor features are primarily found in those who are lazy, lack resourcefulness and drive in any task, forget others' help, have a fickle mind, have a sunken mountain root, and have pointed, thin ears. A flat back, pointed shoulders, resembling a chicken sitting quietly under a tree in the rain, signifies inescapable poverty.
A small head with a narrow forehead, thin ears with rough skin, a small mouth, and a face completely lacking vibrant vitality are signs of poverty (貧乏). If the mountain root of the nose is sunken and flat, the nostrils are flared upwards, and the teeth are exposed, one will be poor. If one walks swaying the body like a snake or hopping like a sparrow, they will ruin their family and always be poor. If the voice is cracked like a broken gong (破聲), the waist is thin, the legs are long, the eyebrows are short (not reaching the outer corner of the eye), the chin (地閣) is pointed and thin, the lips are like a sheep's, and the face has much grime (塵埃), one will be poor. If the chest is shorter than the face, or the lips and mouth are pointed, or the tongue is white, or the back is sunken, or the neck is too thin, or the palms are too thin and dry (乾枯), or the ears are large but without rims (無輪), or the waist is small but the face large, or the person is large but the voice small, or the face is white but the body dark, or the belly is large but the lower abdomen does not protrude, or the voice comes from the root of the tongue rather than the abdomen, or the ribs are prominent on the chest, or the body is top-heavy (上重下輕), or the face is thin but the hands large, or the person is small but the legs large, or the face is red and the tongue small – these are signs of poverty.
A pointed head, pointed face, and pointed mouth (嘴, snout/mouth area) are called the Three Points (三尖); this configuration indicates a bad and poor person. If all six offices (六府, cheekbones, jaws, temples) appear sunken and sharp, it is called the Six Sharps (六削); such a person is cunning, deceitful, and lives in poverty.
A large but crooked nose; a face that looks like it's crying even when it's not; constantly shifting the body while sitting; leading with the head bent forward when walking; frequent changes in facial color; a thin and sharp back of the head (腦後); smelly hair; having a chest but no back (有胸無背); overly thin breasts (乳); dull eyes (無光); a thin and pointed nose tip (準頭); prominent veins on the back of the hand (露筋); white lips and yellow teeth (唇白齒黃); disproportionate three sections (三停); fleshless tops of the feet (脚背); turbid spirit (神濁); a large face with a small nose (面大鼻小) – committing even one of these indicates inescapable poverty. There are many more, but they will be detailed in the miscellaneous discussions in the next part, so I will omit them here.

Eighth, Longevity and Short Life (壽와 夭<138>)

A classic text says, "Among the secrets of physiognomy, longevity and short life (壽夭) are the most difficult [to judge]." This shows how ancient masters deeply lamented the extreme difficulty in determining a person's lifespan. However, if carefully observed, judging longevity and short life is not that difficult. The main difficulty lies in frankly telling someone when you perceive signs of a short life; thus, predictions often go unvoiced. Below are a few important points; judge carefully and do not make light pronouncements.
A person has four aspects: Vital Energy (氣, Ki), Form (形), Spirit (神), and Color (色). Through these, one can infer a person's longevity or short life. Vital Energy includes both bones/veins (骨脈) and voice (聲音). Form includes blood/flesh (血肉). Spirit encompasses the combined essence (精力) of the good and bad aspects of Vital Energy and Form. Color includes the presence or absence of illness and the harmony or disharmony of mind and body. Therefore, someone lacking vibrant Vital Energy is deficient in bones/veins and voice. If bones/veins and voice are deficient, it is an inevitable rule that their physical body will not endure (耐久). Someone with much flesh but few bones (肉多骨少) is said to have "Excess Form but Deficient Vital Energy" (形有餘而氣不足). The reason those with much flesh do not live long is due to this deficiency in Vital Energy.
Someone with deficient Form means their bones overcome their flesh (骨勝肉); this is often seen in thin people (瘦瘠者). This means a deficiency in blood and flesh. If blood and flesh are deficient, it signifies an imperfection in the body's internal structure. It is a very clear principle that one cannot live long with an imperfect body.
Spirit is the essence of Vital Energy and Form. Blood nourishes Form, Form nourishes Vital Energy, and Vital Energy nourishes Spirit. Only when Blood is complete can Form be complete; only when Form is complete can Vital Energy be complete; only when Vital Energy is complete can Spirit be complete. Spirit resides in the flesh, and its form cannot be seen. However, if Vital Energy is strong and Blood is harmonious (氣壯血和), the Spirit will be stable and firm. Strong Vital Energy and harmonious Blood manifest in a person's eyes and skin. The *Maui Physiognomy Book* (麻衣相書) states, "Nourish Blood with Form, nourish Vital Energy with Blood, nourish Spirit with Vital Energy," but this is mistaken. Only after Blood is sufficient can Form be sufficient; it is not that Form becomes sufficient and then Blood becomes sufficient. Therefore, stating that Form nourishes Blood is absolutely incorrect. Vital Energy manifests according to the harmony and balance of Form; it is not that Vital Energy becomes strong following the sufficiency or harmony of Blood. That is, Blood exists first, then flesh, then Form, and then the presence or absence of energy within that Form. Thus, the argument that Blood bypasses the intermediate Form to directly nourish Vital Energy is an impossible theory.
If the eyes are not exposed or vacant, the black and white parts are clear, and the facial skin has little turbidity and much clarity, it means the Spirit, the essence of Vital Energy and Form, is strong, and longevity is certain.
Regarding Color, someone with a chronic illness (宿病) will have turbid blood, unable to show a good complexion; it might be yellowish or whitish and appear dull and weak. It is clear such a person will not live long. Someone whose emotions frequently flicker across their face reveals much inner turmoil and an impatient nature. How can someone whose seven emotions (七情) are unbalanced live long? Longevity or lack thereof <139> can be estimated from these points alone, but determining the specific number of years is much harder. If the head is large and the neck small, death occurs before 30. Losing teeth in robust middle age indicates sufficient longevity. Having only 26 teeth indicates a short life. Clear eyebrows and fine eyes (眉淸目秀) are noble signs, but excessively charming and fine eyebrows indicate a short life. A clear philtrum (人中), like a split bamboo, indicates longevity. Hard fingernails and hard teeth indicate longevity. A weak nose and a low or sunken year/longevity bridge (年壽上) indicate either poverty or a short life. If the facial skin is taut like a stretched drumhead (太急), even if the philtrum is long, life will be short. If the eyes lack spirit, even if the nose bridge is high, life will be short. If the nasolabial folds (法令) are tight and indistinct, death occurs at 49. If the eyes are round and protruding like fish eyes (魚目) and lack luster, life is short. Many spots on the face indicate a short life. Having no lip definition (稜), no spirit in the eyes, exposed nostrils, and no rims on the ears (輪) is called the Four Reversals (四反); such a person faces disaster around age 30. Becoming obese in youth with shortness of breath leads to death at 36. Everted lips, exposed teeth, and shortness of breath lead to death around 30. A thin, sunken back indicates a short life. A delicate body that is also light indicates poverty and premature death (夭). If the five peaks (五岳, forehead, chin, nose, cheekbones) are broad and grand, the nasolabial folds clear, long hairs grow in the eyebrows (should appear after 40), there is excess skin on the neck (項), there are horizontal bones on the forehead, the facial skin is broad and thick, the voice is clear and resonant, the back muscles are broad and thick, the head is round, the occipital bone grows horizontally (耳後骨, bone behind ear), the cheekbones protrude, posture in walking and sitting is upright and dignified, both eyes have spirit, the forehead is square, hair recedes from the forehead top, one eats quickly but defecates slowly, the chin (地閣) is square and thick, the back is like a turtle's shell, the voice is deep and resonant, the forehead is high like a phoenix's, the ears are thick and firm, the year/longevity bridge is prominent, the disposition is generous, and one is prone to righteous anger – these are all signs of longevity. If a fat person has tight skin, or becomes breathless when walking, or walks like a snake (蛇行) or a sparrow (雀步), or sleeps with eyes open (不閉), or sleeps with the mouth constantly open (常開), or has small or low-set ears, or a pointed head and flat forehead (額塌), or flesh that is soft like mud, or a short voice, or a blue face and black lips (面靑唇黑), or a large body and small head, or a crooked nose and parched lips (鼻曲唇焦) – all these indicate a short life. If the belly and navel protrude, or the eyebrows meet, or a fat person has a prominent Adam's apple (結喉), or the philtrum is high and thick, life is short. Eating little and having a dull spirit leads to death by forty. A small head and small forehead lead to death at age 3, 6, 9, 12, or 16. A beautiful face with indistinct facial contours and eyes with much white and little black (白多黑小) that look vacant (浮虛) leads to death at 11, 12, or 13. Prominent blue veins on the face and forehead lead to death at 8 or 9. A sharp back of the head and no occipital bone behind the ears lead to death at 8 or 16. A face like a peach blossom and thin facial skin lead to death at 19 or 25. Eyes with much white and little black, a thick mountain root, and low-set ears lead to death at 20. Short eyebrows and a compressed face lead to death at 25. Sparse eyebrows, eyebrows meeting at the glabella (印堂), upturned lips, and spiritless eyes lead to death at 26. Small eyes, a large nose, a coarse <140> bone structure, and a long, upturned lower face section (下停) lead to death at 26 or 27. Spiritless eyes and rolled-up lips lead to death at 27, 28, or 29. Short, thin, cold-looking eyebrows but a long chin lead to death between 31 and 40. A large face with a prominent Adam's apple, or a fat person with small bones and much flesh, leads to death at 36. Eyes that are exposed with a piercing glare (光炎射出), even if the mountain root is high, lead to death at 39 or 41. An improperly formed nose bridge and a deeply sunken mountain root mean one won't pass 40. Small eyes and compressed eyebrows combined with a thick mountain root lead to death at 41. High eyebrows and low cheekbones lead to death at 46 or 47. An upper lip that is thin and short but not retracted leads to death at 53. Short, cold, thin eyebrows but a long chin lead to death at 51 or 61. A drooping nose (垂, bent downward) and a shallow, small philtrum lead to death at 51. A high nose and low cheekbones, with the left cheekbone flawed, lead to death at 46 or 47. Large ears but bulging lips lead to death at 56 or 57. Bones rising at the temples (精舍) and a flat navel lead to death at 65. A protruding back and bulging lower abdomen but a small forehead lead to death at 54. A fat person with short or sparse teeth leads to death at 40. A fat body with a thin face leads to death at 62. A fat person with a long neck leads to death at 50. A shallow philtrum and long nasolabial folds lead to death at 49. A thick, large nose tip (準頭) pressing down on the upper lip means water star (Mercury, mouth) is covered by earth star (Saturn, nose), leading to death at 52, 53, 54, or 55. If the voice originates from below the navel and is long, the bones are fine and spirit clear, the face is square (面方), and the head bones are square, one will live a long life past 90. A fat person whose flesh is not exposed, or a thin person whose bones are not exposed, will have a very long life. A bright and deep celestial court (天庭) indicates longevity. If longevity eyebrows (壽眉) appear at 20, death occurs at 30; if they appear at 30, death occurs at 40. After 30, longevity eyebrows are good. Becoming obese before or during one's 20s leads to death at 31 or 32. Long hairs inside the ear (耳內長毛) are a sign of longevity, but they must not be plucked for life to be long.

Ninth, Features of Failure (敗相)

Failure (敗) refers to ruining one's family (敗家) or personal failure. Tapping one's feet while sleeping, being thin with a pointed chin, and having frivolous behavior (輕佻) leads to dissipating family wealth in youth or middle age. Premature baldness also indicates one failure in life for both men and women. If someone born in spring or summer becomes bald early, they will certainly fail and live in poverty in later years. If someone born in autumn or winter becomes bald early, they will instead achieve success (達) and live well in later years. Hair is like the vegetation on a person; if vegetation scatters and falls in spring or summer, the tree is damaged and unusable. Humans are the same. It is natural for vegetation to lose leaves in autumn and winter when it's in season; thus, for someone born in autumn or winter, early baldness is also considered appropriate (得格). Someone lacking the Law Courts (廷尉) and Admonition Terraces (諫臺) areas <141> beside the nose wings will fail. A nose bent to the left is called "Reverse Chant" (反吟), and bent to the right is "Prostrate Chant" (伏吟, sometimes a bumpy bridge is also called this); such a person will harm their spouse (克妻) and experience one failure in life. If the mountain root is too large and smoothly connects down to the nose tip (準頭), one cannot accumulate wealth and will experience one failure. If both upper and lower lips are upturned and the philtrum is contracted (頭縮), one will ruin their family and have a short life. Ears with a heavenly rim (天輪) but no earthly lobe (地輪) indicate one failure. Eyes with too much white and a dull, sunken spirit (神沈) cannot accumulate wealth. Eyebrows that meet, short jawbones (腮骨), and no lower beard lead to bankruptcy. Exposed teeth and retracted lips indicate one failure. Missing left teeth indicates failure in middle age. Having a lower beard but no upper mustache indicates poverty and inability to accumulate wealth. Stiff fingers with large gaps between them lead to dissipating wealth. A voice like a broken pot leads to failure and few descendants. Constantly jiggling one's knees while sitting or idly swaying the body left and right while seated leads to financial ruin (財敗) and inability to hold onto money. If red veins (赤脈) invade the pupils, one experiences great failure in early years. If red threads (紅絲) cross the eyes, one will only manage to live in rented houses and will not be able to acquire or keep land and houses. A pointed and thin chin (地閣) leads to losing ancestral property and difficulty inheriting it. A crooked and dull-colored chin leads to losing land (田土). A chin that recedes sharply indicates no land ownership. Dark tear troughs (淚堂) indicate selling off land. A crooked nose and everted ears lead to losing land and wealth. Nostrils that are excessively flared and exposed (露大) lead to early ruin of the family. A crooked nose leads to financial loss (破財). A nose with three bends (三曲) leads to family ruin, loneliness, and few descendants. Thin ears pointing forward lead to selling off all fields and gardens. Ears flipped back like they are facing the wind (張風) lead to losing ancestral property. Walking with a tilted body leads to losing ancestral property and leaving one's hometown. Ears black like ash indicate lifelong poverty and hardship. A mouth puckered as if tightly pursed (口如撮聚) leads to bankruptcy and lack of integrity. A face that looks like it's crying indicates lifelong poverty and hardship. Flaws in the border areas (邊地, sides of face) lead to bankruptcy and one failure. A full face that is pointed at the top leads to failure in middle age. Lines around the mouth converging towards the lips indicate drifting and starving to death.
A needle-like vertical line in the glabella (印堂針紋) indicates (uterine problems for women) losing a spouse or financial loss in early years. Exposed, large nostrils (鼻孔露大), though ambitions may be great, mean wealth disappears as soon as it arrives (入手輒消). Constantly tapping or shaking one's feet, or shaking one's knees while sitting in a chair, inevitably leads to failure. A person with little facial flesh (Wood type, 木體) who deals in grains (米豆) or rice will fail. A hoarse voice like a creaking axle (水牛牧 진 이) leads to great financial loss in early and middle years. A face that is fleshless and dark indicates unavoidable failure in middle age. A fat body and round face with yellowish skin and no appearance of strong energy (強氣) indicates great hardship in later years. Very low cheekbones (顴骨太低) and narrow Law Courts (廷尉) and Admonition Terraces (諫臺) lead to financial ruin in middle or later life. Large hands and long <142> fingernails with an impatient and greedy nature (性急慾大) lead to failure. Walking frivolously (깝신깝신), speaking rapidly, and interrupting others to speak first leads to failure. Smiling while speaking but being quite stubborn leads to failure. A tilted-looking body, small head, and light voice (頭小聲輕) lead to failure and no future prospects. A man who looked beautiful like a woman in his youth (少時) and was talented will fail in middle age. A pointed forehead (額尖), reversed ears (耳反), and disordered eyebrows (眉毛敗亂) lead to financial loss in middle age and great hardship in old age. Someone who neglects their own affairs and those of their parents and siblings, seemingly attends diligently to others' affairs but doesn't actually do so properly, and yet slavishly obeys their wife's family and wife's words will fail and be poor their whole life. A pale face, short stature, and stubborn nature make failure in middle age and hardship in old age difficult to avoid (難免).
A long, pale face with weak, insipid-looking facial muscles (面肉無力) indicates many failures throughout life (early, middle, late). Those who avoid spending their own money and try to deceive others to use their money for their own schemes (運動), and those with much ambition for fame but lacking a generous heart, frequently encounter failure. <143>

**Summary**

This article by Pae Sangch'ŏl delves into the traditional Korean practice of physiognomy (kwansang), detailing how facial and bodily features supposedly determine a person's destiny. It categorizes appearances into noble, common, poor, long-lived/short-lived, and failure-prone types, providing extensive lists of specific physical traits (head shape, eyes, nose, mouth, bones, posture, voice, skin color, hair) and even behaviors (gait, fidgeting, sleeping habits) associated with each category. The author presents these interpretations as near-certain predictors of social status, wealth, lifespan, and success or failure, occasionally referencing or critiquing classical sources like the 'Maui Physiognomy Book'.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects the persistence and perceived relevance of traditional divination practices like physiognomy in colonial Korea (1940). The detailed, deterministic links drawn between physical appearance and socio-economic destiny offer insight into contemporary anxieties and aspirations regarding status, wealth, and longevity. Particularly revealing is the section discussing how Koreans looking like foreigners, or vice-versa, is considered a sign of nobility and potential success, hinting at complex views on identity, foreignness, and pathways to advancement during a period of foreign rule. Historians might find the text useful for understanding popular beliefs about fate, social hierarchy, and identity construction in late colonial Korea.

人生의 壽命과 盛敗
						裵相哲
第五, 貴相

面黑身白하거나 面粗身細하거나 脚短手長하거나 身小聲大하거나 손가락이 손바닥보다 길거나 面短眼長하거나, 한 이는 모두 貴相이니 求官이나 求財에나 求名에 모두 如意하니라.
이마가 유력하여 紋痣와 결함이 없이 되고 뒤로 슬그먼이 자빠진 (肝葉과 如히) 것을 虎頭라고 하고 턱이 제비턱과 같이 나부죽한 것을 燕頷이라고 하는 바 이 虎頭燕頷이 된 이는 大貴格이오, 日月角이 불끈 나오거나 두 손의 길이가 자기 무릅을 지나거나 입에 주먹이 드나들고 혀가 자기의 準頭에 닿거나 걸음을 설넝설넝 걷거나(虎步) 걸음 거를 때에 몸 하나 깟댁 않고 것거나(龍行)하는 이는 또한 大貴格이다.
日角의 좌와 月角의 우에 骨이 있어 直起된 것을 相書에 金城骨이라고 하나니 이 金城骨이 있는 이는 벼슬이 三公(대신)에 이르고, 額의 中正 양변에 소슨 뼈를 龍角骨이라고 하나니 또한 大貴하며, 印堂에서 뼈가 소서 天庭까지 이른 것을 天柱骨이라고 이름하고 天庭으로부터 頂上까지 直起한 骨을 伏犀骨이라고 하나니 이 두가지의 骨이 있는 자는 지위가 三公에 이르고, 수염이 鐵線 같거나 귀가 얼굴보다 희거나 눈이 검고 또렀또렸하거나 몸이 上長下短하거나 입이 四字口(口角이 광명하고 口角이 不上, 不下하되 약간 仰한 듯함)<136>로 되었거나, 이가 36齒거나 한 이는 中貴格이다. 天庭이 高聳하고 地閣이 方圓커나 小便이 구슬 방울 같고 大便이 方細하거나 이가 희고도 크고 眉淸目秀거나, 입이 활 형상(彎弓口니, 兩角이 仰하고 人中이 분명함) 같고, 입술이 珠紅같거나 腰圓背厚하거나 코의 樑柱가 立하고 좌우 顴骨이 기한 이는 모다 小貴 혹은 中貴로 되며 相에 威猛之相이나 淸秀之相으로 된 이는 크게 귀하며 身短面長커나 面이 方하고도 長커나 肩背가 중후하거나 頭에 立骨이 있거나 한 이는 모다 貴格이오, 龍眼과 鳳眼도 모다 貴格이니 龍眼은 눈이 길고도 크고 끝이 우으로 조금 들리고 흑백이 분명하고 정신이 있어 보이며 鳳眼은 눈이 길고 흑백이 분명하며 눈에 광채가 있고 조금 들렀으며 神이 淸하되 龍眼과 같이 크지는 않으며 龍眼 鳳眼이 모두 黃睛이나 赤脉이 있으면 破格이니라. 聲音이 청량커나 수족이 옥과 같은 이는 귀하며, 古怪之相도 귀하지 않으면 富하고, 귀인을 가리는 법은 의외에 어려운 곳이 가끔 있나니 얼굴이 파리하고 눈 코가 다닥다닥 붙어 보이는 사람이 中貴를 하는 이가 많으니라. 그럼으로 귀인에는 輕한 이도 있고 厚한 이도 있고 淸한 이도 있고 秀한 이도 있고 粗한 이도 있고 細한 이도 있고 瘦한 이도 있고 肥한 이도 있고 하나 모다 秀媚한 맛이 있는 데에 貴가 있나니 形, 骨, 部, 位가 잘 되고도 秀媚한 기가 없으면 귀하되 왕좌가 되기까지는 不及하나니라.
귀인은 두상에 髮이 적고 賤人은 신상에 털이 없으며 골격이 비범해 보이고 눈에 神이 淸하면 귀인이오 面方耳大하면 일품 고관격이오 眉竪目長하면 三台 고관격이니라. 그리고 朝鮮 사람으로서 외국인의 얼굴 같거나 서양인으로서 동양인 같거나 동양인으로서 서양인 같어 보이면 貴格이니 문필, 사회, 정치, 횡재, 관리의 그 어느 것으로서든지 그 이름을 드날리나니라. 귀인 감정법은 이하 잡론에 多함으로 이만 생략하려니와 8大相, 8小相, 5長相, 5短相, 5小相에 큰 貴가 많은 것을 독자는 명심할 지니라.

第六, 賤相

賤相은 대개 貧相과 흡사한 점이 많으나 그 중에도 약간 다른 점이 많이 있나니라. 鼻梁이 削하야 도검과 같고 眼睛은 흡사히 蜂睛이 되었으며 흉하고도 천하며, 頭削骨露하야 마치 獐頭와 같고 눈은 鼠目과 같이 동그랗게 되고 凸하면 賤相이오 口大脣薄하고 이가 울눅블눅(參差)하고 面에 輸廓이 없고 손바닥이 粗(거칠고)하고 이마에 紋波가 많으면 광대의(배우)의 상이니 賤格이오, 눈이 붉고 눈섭이 粗하고, 골격이 粗하며 談笑에나 坐臥에 모다 정신든 곳이 없고, 額上에 紋이 많고 皮肉이 橫佈되었으면 下賤의 相이다. 뒤통수가 陷平하고 山根, 年壽가 平陷된 자도 賤格이며, 몸이 富大코도<137> 濁하고 기가 없으며 手脚이 粗하면 천인이오. 鼻曲低塌하고 눈에 광채가 없으면 노예의 相이오 頭小와 羊眼과 三停이 평균치 못한 이가 모다 賤格이니라.

第七, 貧와 相

貧相은 첫째 고로 게으르도 무슨 일에 아모 주변성과 활동력이 없고 겸하야 타인의 공을 잊어바리고 마음이 浮輕하고 山根이 陷하고 귀가 尖薄한 자에 많으니라. 등이 납쪽하고 어깨가 尖하며 우중에 수목의 下에 가만이 앉은 닭(鷄)의 형상같으면 영낙없이 빈궁하니라.
頭小하고도 額部가 狹窄하며 귀가 얇고도 귀 가죽이 粗하고 입이 적고 얼굴이 팔팔한 생기가 하나도 없는 이는 貧乏의 상이오 코의 山根이 陷平하고 코구멍이 번적 처들이고 겸하야 이빨이 드러나면 빈하고 거름 거를 때에 몸을 흔든 흔들하야 마치 배암의 걸음 것듯 하거나 참새 거름같이 깡총깡총 뛰어 걸으면 敗家하여 늘 빈한하고 소리가 깨진 꽝막이처럼 破聲이 나고 허가 가늘고 다리는 길고 눈섭이 짧으며(눈섭이 눈초리까지에 不及한 것) 地閣이 尖薄하고 입술이 羊의 입술 같고 얼굴에 塵埃가 많으면 빈한하고 가슴이 얼굴보다 짧거나 입술과 입이 뾰족하고 혀(舌)가 백색이 되거나 등이 陷하고 목이 너무 가늘거나 손바닥이 너무 얇고 乾枯커나 耳大無輪하고 腰小面大커나 사람은 큰데 음성은 적거나 얼굴은 흰데 몸은 검거나 배는 크되 아래배가 나오지 못했거나 聲音이 腹中에서 못 나오고 舌根에서 나오거나 가슴에 露骨이 되며 몸이 上重 下輕커나 얼굴은 가늘고 손은 크거나 사람은 적게 생겻으되 다리는 크거나 面赤舌小한 자는 貧相이니라.
頭가 尖하고 面이 尖하고 嘴(주동이)가 尖한 것을 三尖이라고 하는 바 이 三尖이 된 相은 不良코도 빈하고. 六府가 모두 陷한 듯 하고도 削한 것은 六削이라고 하는 바 이 六削이 된 자는 奸하고 교활하고도 빈한케 사는 相이니라.
코가 크되 빗두러지고. 얼굴은 울지 않되 우는 것 같고. 앉었을 때에 늘 몸을 흔들고. 거를 때에 머리가 먼저 앞으로 굽게 나가고. 얼굴 빛을 가끔 변하고 腦後가 薄削하고. 모발이 냄새가 나고. 有胸無背하거나 젖(乳)이 너무 가늘거나 兩目이 無光커나 準頭가 薄尖하고 手背에 露筋커나 唇白齒黃커나 三停이 불균하고 脚背에 無肉커나 神濁하고 面大鼻小커나 이상에 하나라도 범한 이는 빈곤을 면치 못하나니라. 이외에도 퍽 많으나 下篇 잡론 중에 자세히 말하겠으므로 畧한다.

第八, 壽와 夭<138>

經에 曰호대 「相中訣法은 壽夭가 最難」이라고 했으니 古人 * 사람의 壽夭를 감정키가 지극히 어려움에 深嘆했음을 가히 推知하겠다. 그러나 壽夭도 그것을 잘 살펴보면 그대지 難한 것은 아니다. 다만 단명할 相을 看破코도 그것을 본인에게 직언키가 難해서 맞후지 못하는 것이 많으니라. 이제 중요한 것 몇 가지를 下記하노니 너무 輕斷치 말고 신중히 판단할 지니라.
사람에게 氣와 形과 神과 色의 이 네가지가 있어서 그 사람의 壽하고 단명한 것을 능히 추측할 수가 있나니 氣中에는 骨脈 양자와 聲音이 포함되고 形중에는 혈육이 포함되며 神 중에는 氣形의 두 가지의 잘 되고 못된 종합적 精力이 포함되고 色 중에는 有病無病의 두가지와 심신의 화평과 불화평이 포함되어 있는 것이다. 그럼으로 매친 氣가 없는 이는 骨脈과 聲音이 부족됨이니 骨脈, 聲音이 부족하면 그 육체가 耐久치 못할 것은 필연의 定則이다. 肉多骨少한 이를 形有餘而氣不足이라 하나니 肉多한 이가 장수치 못하는 것은 즉 氣부족의 소치니라.
形이 부족한 이는 즉 骨이 肉을 勝함이니 瘦瘠者에 많이 있다. 즉 血과 肉이 부족함이니 혈육이 부족하면 신체의 내부 구조에 있어 한 불완전함을 이름이니 신체가 불완전코 능히 장수할 수가 없는 것은 너무 명백한 이치이다.
神은 氣와 形이 골자이니 血은 形을 養하고 形은 氣를 養하고 氣는 神을 養하는 바 血이 全한 후에야 形이 全하고 形이 全한 후에야 氣가 全하고 氣가 全한 후에야 神하나니 神은 肉에 居하야 그 형체를 보지 못하나니라 그러나 氣壯血和하면 神이 安固하나니 氣壯血和는 그 사람의 眼目과 피부에 나타나나니라. 麻衣相書에는 「形으로써 血을 養하고 血로써 氣를 養하고 氣로써 神을 養한다」 하였으나 그릇된 말이다. 무릇 血이 풍족한 뒤에야 形이 풍족될 것이지 形이 풍족한 뒤에 血이 풍족됨이 아닌 즉 形으로써 血을 養한다 함은 절대로 불가한 말이오. 形이 調和得中됨을 따라 氣가 現할 것이지 血이 풍족하거나 調和得中됨을 따러 氣가 유력케 됨은 아니니, 즉 血이 있은 후에 살이 있고 살이 있은 후에 形이 있고, 形이 있은 후에 그 形의 유기력, 무기력함이 있는 것인 즉 血이 그 중간에 在한 形을 걸러서 즉시 氣를 養한다함은 불가한 論이니라.
안목이 不露, 不虛하고 흑백이 분명하고 面의 피부가 濁少淸多하면 그는 氣와 形의 골자인 神이 유력케 된 것이니 壽할 것은 물론이오.
色에는 그 사람이 宿病을 가진 자는 血이 탁하야 좋은 빛이 나지 못하고 黃하거나 白하거나 하고도 無光無力함이 보일 것이니, 이런 이가 壽하지 못할 것은 분명한 일이오, 희노를 면모에 乍現乍沒하는 이는 속에 감정이 많고 성질이 급한 사람이니 七情이 편벽된 자-어찌 壽를 하리요. 壽하고 못함은<139> 이것만으로도 짐작하려니와 그 長短의 年限은 이보다 훨신 어려우니라. 頭가 大하고 項이 小하면 30前에 死하고 壯年에 落齒가 되는 이는 壽가 족하고 齒가 26개밖에 안되면 短壽하고 眉淸目秀하면 貴格이지만 눈섭이 너무 媚秀하면 壽하지 못하고. 人中이 분명하야 대쪽 뻑앤듯한 이는 壽하고 손톱이 단단하고 이빨이 단단한 이는 壽하며 코가 단단치 못하고 年壽上이 低陷한 이는 貧乏하지 않으면 단명하며. 面皮가 마치 북메인
