Spring Wife

Yi Koryŏ

The ticklish nape of the neck is sad
Smile quickly, try to laugh.

Your hometown too is Ch'ungch'ŏng Province
Forced shy lips, wrinkle upon wrinkle, burn with sorrow.

A body that barely writes the three characters of a name
The dark night rolls on.<260>

Hey girl
You are thin like India.

Blue Wall

Two legs
Two legs
Tired before the Gate locked with the iron of remoteness (迂遠)
Fallen leaves, aged over countless years (憶年), roll away.

Blood on the toenails,
Blood under the nails,
Fragrant oil clinging to the scratched limbs.
The sound of curses (呪文), terribly, terribly frightening.<261>

Tears released in dreams dry up
Woong—
The sound of a gong wrinkles up world peace
A sick ghost prostrates itself here.

Whose darkness is it that tears away?
When the transparent red jewel (紅瓊) pillar stands before the eyes
Wadeudeu—the grabbed hem of the skirt!

That,
O, Female Image (女像) of Anguish, disappear!
Just fallen before the Palace of Darkness (冥暗)
I will scratch, climb, and burn the Wall of Still Emptiness (靜虛).<262>

Dispatch from Utopia

Ham Hyŏngsu

A voice cries out in the wilderness, saying,
Repent, for the Kingdom of Heaven is near! — New Testament

1. There shall be countless cafés and diners and restaurants and dance halls on every street.
Elementary school students shall treat their teacher, thin as a chicken leg today, to liquors of thirteen nations.
And from their teacher, they shall surely receive fruits and sweets and coffee and rice cakes and bread.
"Teacher, that woman, skinny as a sorghum stalk, looks just like my mother."
"Teacher, this child, with one eye big as a saucer, looks just like<263> my sibling who starved to death."
"Teacher, that woman with freckles thick as fly droppings looks just like my older sister."
"Teacher, that child with one crooked lip looks like the one I threw into the sewer last year."
"Teacher, Teacher
Let's drink liquor, please have some sweets
Today let's cry our fill and laugh our fill
And today let's love our fill."
Today, drinking liquor for the country
Today, dancing for the country
Today, spending the country's money
Today, the day of eating the country's bread (팡)—

Oh, elementary school students
Those girls like angels, like demons
Call them "mother," "sister" on this day, call them thus, call them thus.

2. Reception rooms in every house—
Completely wallpapered (塗壁한) with the Bible and Buddhist scriptures.<264>

Conversation
Lady: "They say in the bygone era, there was a funny game (遊戱) called love."
Gentleman: "They say in the bygone era, there was also noisy morality called law."
Gentleman & Lady: "Hahaha hahaha hahaha hahaha..."

3. Their own happiness, their son's happiness, their mother's happiness—
Having forgotten it all
Those dirty kin (族屬) called merchants
Mount them on iron horses (쇠말) and send them on an Everest expedition, send them thus, send them thus.

4. Scholars shall research more thoroughly gliders and wings.
A little heavier than a clod of earth (들뗑이), a little heavier than a lump of lead (鉛땡이), and not quite as heavy as the Earth<265>
This body
Making it fly
To Mars, to Saturn, to Jupiter, to Africa, to America
Is your effort.

Oh—the day when countless new angels
Will flood (橫溢할) the sky.

5. On every street, there shall be exceedingly many children practicing standing upside down.
This is an "antithesis" to the old spirit of gymnastics, so do not stop them, do not stop them, do not stop them.

6. The day the breath of a new god
Enters the breasts of the lions, tigers, and wolves said to be ferocious (獰惡하다는)
They will flock to the streets
And beg for milk, bread (팡), and sweets.
While doing their funny buttock (궁뎅이) dance and singing their tasteless humming (코노래)—.<266>

Collect the guns.
Collect the guns.

7. Tired grandmothers and grandfathers shall be put in hammocks (함모끄) and sent to the mountains, to the seaside (바다까).

Shall I read "Water Margin" (水許誌) to you?
How about "Robinson Crusoe"?
You like Persian drinking songs, don't you?
Or shall I just sing you a lullaby (자장가나 불러드릴 깝쇼)?

8. Confucius as a home tutor
Laozi as a university professor
Jesus Christ as a foreign language instructor

How about it, everyone?<267>

9. Oh—let's make the flag a "Nine-Stranded Dragon" (아홉가달龍).
Let's make it a "Nine-Stranded Dragon."

10. Launch warships onto the streets
Launch automobiles and trains onto the sea
Fly mountains and houses into the sky
Let clouds descend and settle on the earth

Set off firecrackers like goblin (독까비) laughter
Sound celebratory cannons like the trumpeting (우름) of elephant (코키리) herds
Like a fairy tale
Like Heaven
Like laughter
Like terror

An unprecedented festival

Let's hold it tomorrow.
Let's abandon it tomorrow.<268>

**Summary**

This feature presents three poems by Yi Koryŏ and Ham Hyŏngsu, winners of major newspaper literary awards in Spring 1940. Yi Koryŏ's "Spring Wife" portrays a sorrowful young woman, while "Blue Wall" uses stark imagery of confinement, suffering, and a desperate yearning for escape. Ham Hyŏngsu's "Dispatch from Utopia" is a long, satirical poem depicting a bizarre, inverted society presented as an ideal future, featuring absurd social interactions, exiled merchants, flying humans, begging predators, and a chaotic festival. From a historical perspective, the poems, particularly Ham Hyŏngsu's "Dispatch from Utopia," are highly interesting. Its biting satire and dystopian vision, published during a period of intensified Japanese colonial control and wartime mobilization, could be interpreted as a veiled critique of the imposed modernity, social dislocations, or the absurdity of the political situation, using the guise of a fantastical "utopia" to express dissent or deep unease. Yi Koryŏ's poems, with their themes of sorrow and confinement, might also reflect the suppressed atmosphere of the times.
