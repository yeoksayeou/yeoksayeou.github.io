**Summary**

This article from the May 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine provides a list of telephone numbers for major governmental, public, commercial, educational, media, and financial institutions in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul). The list includes key administrative bodies like the Government-General and Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural Office, major newspapers (Korean and Japanese), universities and colleges, hospitals, hotels, prominent department stores (Japanese and Korean-owned), banks, publishing houses, and major companies. The phone numbers are distinguished by exchange prefixes (光 for Kwanghwamun, 本 for Ponch'ŏng). For historians, this list offers a snapshot of the key institutions shaping life in the colonial capital, revealing the structure of governance, commerce, education, and communication. The presence of both Japanese and Korean entities (though under colonial rule), the prominence of Japanese department stores and banks alongside Korean ones, and the very existence of such a directory in a popular magazine highlight the modernization and integration of Kyŏngsŏng into the Japanese empire's infrastructure while also showing the persistence of Korean institutions.
