35,000 People to Siberia

Farmers are Being Relocated
The Soviet Union is relocating a large number of farmers to the Far Eastern Siberia region. Last year, the number of relocated farming families reached over ten thousand, and this year, there are plans to relocate thirty-five thousand people to the Chita-Irkutsk region. <16>

Name Changing (創氏) Exceeds 40,000 Cases

Kyŏngbuk Leads with 24,000

The "Name Changing System" (創氏制度), which began on February 11th, sees an increasing number of registrations daily, reaching 40,150 by March 20th. According to an investigation by the Government-General's Civil Affairs Section into the registration numbers by province, Kyŏngbuk (North Kyŏngsang) was overwhelmingly high with 24,044 people, accounting for more than half of the total. Kyŏngnam (South Kyŏngsang) was second with 2,628 people, and Kyŏnggi Province was third with 2,488 people. The lowest were Ch'ungbuk (North Ch'ungch'ŏng) with 493 and Hwanghae Province with 675. The name change registration numbers by province are as follows:
Kyŏnggi 2488, Kangwŏn 891, Ch'ungnam (South Ch'ungch'ŏng) 2227, Hambuk (North Hamgyŏng) 1207, P'yŏngnam (South P'yŏngan) 1544, P'yŏngbuk (North P'yŏngan) 1064, Hwanghae 675, Kyŏngbuk 24044, Kyŏngnam 2626, Chŏnnam (South Chŏlla) 753, Chŏnbuk (North Chŏlla) 1126, Total 40,150

Kyŏngbuk Name Changers Subsequently Reach 27,060

As name changing (創氏) bears the great fruit of Japan-Korea Unity (內鮮一體), the number of people changing their names is rapidly increasing daily amid interest from various quarters. As of April 5th, the number of name changers within Kyŏngbuk Province reached 27,060 households, leading all of Chosŏn. As the number of name changers increases daily, Inspector Im Hŭng-jae (任興宰), head of the Kyŏngbuk Police Training Center, took "P'ungch'ŏn" (豊川) from his hometown of P'ungch'ŏn, Hwanghae Province, changing his name to P'ungch'ŏn Kyŏng-sam (豊川敬三). Subsequently, Lieutenant Ch'oe Sŏk-hyŏn (崔錫鉉) of the Kyŏngbuk High Police Section took the "mountain" (山) from "Ch'oe" (崔) and changed his name to Yamamoto Shōji (山本祥資).

16,000 People in Hambuk

The number of households registered for name changing and their family members in North Hamgyŏng Province up to March was 2,220 households and 16,111 individuals. The registration status by city (府) and county (郡) is as follows:
Ch'ŏngjin City 114 households, 659 people; Najin City 39 households, 232 people; Kyŏngsŏng County 247 households, 1525 people; Myŏngch'ŏn County 138 households, 1,205 people; Kilchu County 339 households, 2,073 people; Sŏngjin County 353 households, 2,677 people; Puryŏng County 32 households, 191 people; Musan County 199 households, 1,553 people; Hweryŏng County (likely 曾寧郡) 228 households, 1,674 people; Chongsŏng County 170 households, 1,219 people; Onsŏng County 146 households, 992 people; Kyŏngwŏn County 119 households, 735 people; Kyŏnghŭng County 196 households, 1,376 people.

Kyŏngsŏng City Exceeds 1,100 Cases

The fervor for name changing in Kyŏngsŏng City (Seoul) has intensified, reportedly surpassing eleven hundred cases now.

Petition to Punish Corrupt Merchants (奸商) Under Military Law

Farmers' Union to the Military Minister

According to a dispatch from Mito, the Industrial Cooperative and Agricultural Association of Urizura-machi, Naka-gun, Ibaraki Prefecture, has <17> submitted a petition to the Army and Navy Ministers regarding the fertilizer issue. Due to difficulties in obtaining fertilizer because of poor distribution, unscrupulous merchants are engaging in black market transactions (闇取引), sucking the lifeblood (膏血) of the farmers. Deeming this unforgivable and akin to aiding the enemy, they requested severe punishment under military law (軍刑法) rather than ordinary criminal law. This matter has gained the sympathy of the vast majority of farmers, and its outcome is being closely watched.

The newly established National Government [of Wang Ching-wei] has launched an official newspaper, the "Chung Pao." The founder is Wang Ching-wei (汪精衛), the chairman is Chou Fohai (周佛海), and the president is Luo Junqiang (羅君强). It is said to have about thirty reporters.

Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng Returns to Kyŏngsŏng; Mr. Song Chin-u Still in Tōkyō

Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨), who had been staying in Tōkyō for about 20 days, completed his business and returned to Kyŏngsŏng in early April. Mr. Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹) is still staying at the Imperial Hotel (帝國ホテル) in Tōkyō, and his return date is unknown.

A certain Korean volunteer in Shanghai is reportedly compiling and editing a directory of prominent peninsula people residing in China. This is considered a timely endeavor, and Shanghai correspondence indicates it will likely be published sometime in April.

Details of the Public Trial of the Assailants of Messrs. Wang and Tseng

On March 18th, just as the government headed by Mr. Wang Ching-wei was about to be inaugurated, the public trial of Huang Ju-chiao (黃如喬?), the ringleader who attacked Mr. Wang's hideout in Hanoi a year prior and assassinated Mr. Tseng Chung-ming (曾仲鳴), was held in a Hanoi court, resulting in a life imprisonment sentence. According to correspondence from Hanoi, during the trial, the plaintiff's lawyer, Mr. Tanbel(?), argued: "In times of war, the responsibility of the people ends with their own sacrifice, but the responsibility of a leader, beyond their own sacrifice, encompasses the fate of the nation and the desire for the country's benefit. This was the view of Messrs. Wang Ching-wei and Tseng Chung-ming. China has already lost many provinces, and the farmers across the country and soldiers on the battlefield are plunged into dire suffering. If one calmly considers without vainly inflaming the war effort through newspapers, fire, or words, Wang Ching-wei's assertion, 'If there is hope for peace and its conditions do not hinder the nation's independence and freedom, what is wrong with seeking peace?' can be understood. Mr. Wang's goal is the revival of China. China must be wary of the Communist Party's current aims. That is, the Communist Party seeks to expand its influence by exploiting China's difficulties. The Communist Party speaks of world peace, but in reality, they are the ones who most enjoy war; their war is a capitalist, imperialist, class war, and further, a war for war's sake, intending to make the flower of Communism bloom on the post-war land. The patriotic intentions of Messrs. Wang and Tseng also lie herein. Even though Mr. Tseng Chung-ming has died, Mr. Wang Ching-wei is still alive. His high discernment and noble character are evident from his past negotiations with Japan and the contents of his messages to Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石); he is striving for the construction of a New China. We firmly believe that the soldiers currently fighting the war will be moved before the grave of Tseng Chung-ming, who became a cornerstone for peace and happiness." <18>
Thus he argued forcefully, demanding the defendant be severely punished according to French (佛國) law. Prosecutor Modeum(?) then stated in his closing argument: "Based on the evidence, the defendant's crime was attempting to harm the life of a Chinese leader and showing contempt for French law by carrying out a political assassination. Criticizing a person vacuously without knowing them simply because their principles differ is a kind of sin. How much more unforgivable is the crime of attempting to harm the life of a useful talent for the nation."
Thus he argued, demanding life imprisonment. The defendant's lawyer then attempted a plea of innocence, but following deliberation by the jury, the crime was unanimously acknowledged, resulting in a verdict of life imprisonment. It is also said that before the trial began, numerous telegrams demanding severe punishment were sent to the Hanoi authorities from various Chinese organizations and groups.

Financial and Other Fields

The Peninsula's First Banks

Their Capital and Loan Amounts, etc.

The history of banks being established in Chosŏn spans about 40 years. Looking at the records, the very first ones appeared in Meiji 28 (1895), that is, when the Sino-Japanese War ended and the Kabo Reforms (甲午更張) brought new civilization into Chosŏn. At that time, there were four banks:
Imperial Bank (帝國銀行), founded Meiji 28
President: Min Pyŏng-han (閔丙漢)
Investors: Unclear
Ch'ŏnil Bank (天一銀行), founded Meiji 29 (1896)
President: Formerly Min Yŏng-gi (閔泳琦), later Min Pyŏng-sŏk (閔丙奭)
Investors: Mainly the Royal Household (天室) and aristocracy
Chosŏn Bank (朝鮮銀行), founded Meiji 28 (1895) [Note: This name was later used by the central bank, but this appears to be an early, unrelated bank]
President: Sim Sang-hun (沈相薰)
Investor: Yi Kŭn-bae (李根培)
Hansŏng Bank (漢城銀行), founded Meiji 28 (1895)
President: Sim Sang-hun (沈相薰)
Investors: Yi Sŭng-ŏp (李承業), Han Ch'i-jo (韓致朝)

Loans Reaching 500,000 Won

Although their capital was small at the time, looking at the extent of business these banks conducted, the Ch'ŏnil Bank, for example, had its highest loan amount in Meiji 37 (1904), reaching 500,000 won.

Textbooks Reduced by 20%

The paper shortage is gradually worsening, now affecting even the Ministry of Education's (文部省) textbooks. Starting from April this year, the publication volume is set to be reduced by 20%. This pertains to the situation in mainland Japan, and the situation in Chosŏn is still unclear. <19>

Film and Theater Fields

Actress Mun Ye-bong's Fee: 1,000 Won Per Film

It is said that star actress Mun Ye-bong's (文藝峯) fee for starring in "Water Lily" (水仙花) (late Kim Yu-yŏng's (金幽影) work) and "Volunteer Soldier" (志願兵) (An Sŏk-yŏng's (安夕影) work), both set to be released soon, was 1,000 won each. Although now various companies are practically fighting a contract war(?) over Mun Ye-bong's popularity with fees in the thousand-won range, there were also times before "The Wanderer" (나그네), such as for "The Tale of Ch'unhyang" (春香傳), when her fee was merely over 100 won per film.

Rumor of Chosŏn Film Industry Merger through Government Mediation

It is a well-known fact that authorities, starting with the filmmakers' roundtable hosted by the Chosŏn Army Information Department, and including the Government-General, are preparing a new system for film, such as promulgating the Film Law. However, according to one rumor, there is talk of consolidating all the small-scale production companies and establishing a national policy company, similar to Germany, Italy, or Manchukuo. For this reason, it seems there have already been several meetings with certain business tycoons, such as the president of a certain life insurance company. The capital for this company is said to be 5 million won, fully paid-in, and existing companies with considerable facilities and equipment, like Cho-Yŏng (朝映) or Koryŏ (高麗), will reportedly be acquired through fair valuation.

Wealthy Businessman Han Hak-su Actively Supporting "Theater Troupe Kohyŏp"

Mr. Han Hak-su (韓學洙), the young master of the Han family, known as a multi-millionaire magnate, owner of the Han Ch'ŏng Building (韓靑삘딩?) on Chongno 4-ga, is said to have decided to actively support "Theater Troupe Kohyŏp" (劇團.高協) going forward. Besides what has already been reported in some newspapers, he has reportedly provided a small ranch for the families of the Kohyŏp Village members, and plans to lend the entire production cost for a film featuring all troupe members during the summer. Mr. Han, who also invests in education as the manager of Kyŏngsŏng Girls' Commercial School (京城女商校), is praised as an entrepreneur for extending his support to a theater group like this.

"Theater Troupe Kohyŏp" Earns 11,000 Won Profit from Hamgyŏng Line Tour

"Theater Troupe Kohyŏp," supported by the aforementioned businessman Han Hak-su, perhaps aided by the handicap that most of its members are from Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道), reportedly achieved astonishing earnings of 11,000 won from 43 performances during its tour of the region in March. <20>

Revue Comes to Kyŏngsŏng! Yoshimoto Kōgyō, Taking Over the Hwanggŭmjwa (Koganeza), Introduces It
Yoshimoto Kōgyō (吉本興業) is widely recognized as the most modern entertainment concern that dominates the entertainment world in Japan's Kansai (關西) region. This Yoshimoto announced its entry into Kyŏngsŏng by taking over the management rights of the movie theater Hwanggŭmjwa (黃金座) from the end of last March. It is predicted that this company's advance will clearly define a certain stage in the peninsula's entertainment business. First, it is reported that they will introduce things like musical revues (레뷰-). Before long, the calves of revue girls, like autumn radishes, will appear in Kyŏngsŏng.

**Summary**

This article from the May 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* compiles various news snippets concerning Korean society under Japanese rule. Key topics include widespread rumors and official Japanese discussions (cited from Japanese publications but with text omitted) about the potential consolidation or closure of the major private Korean newspapers, *Tonga Ilbo* and *Chosŏn Ilbo*. It extensively reports on the progress of the forced name-changing policy (Sōshi-kaimei), providing detailed statistics highlighting Kyŏngbuk province's high participation rate and naming specific officials who changed their names. Other news includes Soviet farmer resettlement, peasant petitions against price gouging in Japan, the establishment of Wang Ching-wei's government in China and the trial of his attacker, notes on early Korean banks, paper shortages affecting textbooks, and extensive updates on the Chosŏn film and theater scene, covering actor fees (Mun Ye-bong), potential industry mergers under government guidance, financial backing for theater troupes (Han Hak-su supporting Kŭktan Kohyŏp), the arrival of Japanese entertainment companies (Yoshimoto Kōgyō), film association memberships, box office results ("Tuition Fee"), and the movements of film personnel.

The article is historically significant as it directly reflects key aspects of late colonial policy implementation, particularly the coercive Sōshi-kaimei (創氏) campaign and the tightening control over Korean media (newspaper consolidation fears) and culture (film industry guidance, promotion of "Japan-Korea Unity" themes). The detailed statistics on name changes, especially the regional breakdown and emphasis on Kyŏngbuk, offer valuable data for historians studying the policy's reception and enforcement. The film industry news illuminates the structure, economics, and personalities within this cultural sphere operating under colonial constraints and influence.

汪, 曾 兩氏 襲擊 犯人 公判內容

汪精衛씨를 수반으로 하는 정부가 탄생할여는 去 3월 18일 1년전 汪씨의 河內隱家를 습격코 曾仲鳴씨를 암살한 首魁 黃如喬 공판이 河內 법정에저 행하여저 州日 징벌의 판결이 내렸는데 河內로부터의 통신에 의하면 同 공판에서 원고측 변호사 탄벨씨는 전쟁의 時 국민의 책임은 자기의 희생으로 끄치나 지도자의 책임은 자기의 희생 이외에 국가의 운명을 점하고 조국의 리익을 원할 것이다. 汪精衛 曾仲鳴 兩氏의 의견은 이것이었다 .支那는 이미 많은 省을 잃고 전국의 농민 전장의 병사는 도탄의 苦에 陷入되고 있다. 헛되히 신문이며 불이며 입으로 항전을 고조하는 일 업시 냉정히 고찰하면 汪精衛의 「만약 화평의 희망이 있어 그 조건이 국가의 독립 자유에 장애 없으면 和半을 강구하는 것이 何 고로 불가한가」라는 주장이 양해될 것이다. 汪씨의 목적은 支那의 부흥이다. 支那는 현재 공산당이 노리고 있는 것에 주의하지 않으면 안된다. 즉 공산당은 支那의 곤궁에 承하여 이 세력을 확대하려고 있다. 공산당은 입으로 세계평화를 말하나 그 實 가장 전쟁을 즐기는 자로 그 전쟁은 資本主義적 帝國主義 계급적 전쟁이며 다시 전쟁을 위한 전쟁으로 전후의 토지에 共産主義의 꽃을 피게 할려고 하고 있는 것이다. 汪, 曾 兩氏의 구국의 의도도 亦이에 있는 것이다 曾仲鳴씨가 사망하여도 汪精衛씨가 건재하다. 그 식견 높고 인격 고매함은 종래의 日本과의 교섭 蔣介石에의 전문 내용으로 명백하며 同씨는 新中國의 건설에 매진하고 있다. 吾人은 현재 항전 중의 장사가 和平幸福의 초석이 된 曾仲鳴씨의 墓前에 감격할 것을 미더 마지<18> 안는다
라고 역설하고 佛國의 법률에 의하여 피고를 엄형에 처할 것을 요구하자 검사 모데움은 피고의 죄상은 증거에 의하여 피고는 支那 領袖의 생명에 위해를 가하려하고 또 佛國의 법률을 경멸하여 정치 모살을 실행하였다. 主義不同의 고로써 그 사람을 알지 못하고 헛되히 이를 비판함은 일종의 죄악이다. 하물며 국가에 있어 유용한 인재의 생명에 위해를 가함에 있어저는 그 죄가 용서할 수 없는 것이 있다.
라고 논고하고 무기징역을 구형 끝나자 피고측 변호사가 무죄의 변론을 試하였으나 배심원회의의 결과 전원일치 그 죄를 인정하고 무기징역의 판결로 된 것으로 또 同 공판 開延 前 支那 각 기관단체로부터 河內 당국에 엄벌을 요망하는 전보가 다수 들어왔다 한다.
