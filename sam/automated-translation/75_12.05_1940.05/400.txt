**Summary**

This article from the May 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine provides a directory of Korean cultural institutions and media outlets operating under Japanese colonial rule. It lists major newspapers and a wide variety of magazines (general interest, literature, religious, specialized industry, etc.), providing publisher names and representatives. It also includes lists of significant libraries with their holdings (divided into Japanese/Chinese and Western books), major publishing houses with addresses and phone numbers, museums (including those established by the colonial government and Yi Royal Family), and surviving traditional Neo-Confucian academies (Seowon) dedicated to prominent historical figures.

For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of the intellectual and cultural landscape of Korea in 1940. It reveals the structure of the media and publishing industries, showing both Korean-owned enterprises (like Chosŏn Ilbo, Tonga Ilbo, Samch'ŏlli Sa) and institutions under direct colonial influence (like Maeil Sinbo, Government-General Library/Museum). The extensive list of magazines highlights diverse areas of interest and activity, including language preservation (Han'gŭl magazine by the Chosŏnŏ Hakhoe), religious life (Christian and Buddhist publications), and specialized fields, despite the tightening controls of the wartime period. The inclusion of traditional Seowon points to the persistence of historical memory and cultural heritage structures.
