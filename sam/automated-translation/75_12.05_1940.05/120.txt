Magazine Title	三千理 第12卷 第5號
Publication Date	1940년 05월 01일
Article Title	戰爭은 아직 멀었다, 板垣 參謀長의 警告 (The War is Still Far Away, Chief of Staff Itagaki's Warning)
Author	Not Specified (Quotes Itagaki Seishirō)
Article Type	논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

In the modest reception room of the China Expeditionary Army General Headquarters, located in the imposing former Nationalist Government Ministry of Foreign Affairs building on Nanjing's Zhongshan Road, Chief of Staff Itagaki spoke thus. After first congratulating the establishment of the new government [referring to the Wang Jingwei regime], he said:

"However, the people must not feel relieved because of this. On the front lines, large-scale battles comparable to the last Great War in Europe are being fought against the anti-Japanese army, so I hope you will further strengthen your unity. The Japanese people have also made considerable sacrifices in the current Incident, but they have not suffered any direct ravages of war. In this regard, the Chinese populace is making even greater sacrifices, so I hope you will think of this and endure. Although supplies are short in the homeland (内地), in a few years, self-sufficiency will be achieved between Japan, Manchuria, and China (日滿支), so it is a matter of enduring just a little longer."

"As has already been declared to the world by the Konoe Statement, this war is not about taking territory or receiving money; the sole war aim lies in constructing eternal peace in East Asia through Japan-China cooperation (日支 제휴).
However, Japan-China cooperation must be cooperation that flows sincerely from the heart (衷心). It becomes a heartfelt alliance when both sides respect each other and trust each other. Therefore, on this occasion, all Japanese people need to cultivate a character worthy of respect from the Chinese people, and for that reason, spiritual education, moral education within the country, and the self-restraint (自肅) of Japanese people on the ground are necessary."<83>

**Summary**

This article reports on remarks made by Japanese Army Chief of Staff Itagaki Seishirō in Nanjing in 1940. He emphasizes that despite the establishment of a new collaborationist government in China, the war against anti-Japanese forces continues intensely, requiring ongoing sacrifice and unity from the Japanese people. He contrasts Japan's lack of direct war damage with China's suffering, urging endurance and promising future self-sufficiency within a Japan-Manchuria-China bloc. Itagaki stresses that the war's goal is not conquest but establishing lasting East Asian peace through sincere Japan-China cooperation, which requires mutual respect and necessitates Japanese individuals cultivating respectable characters and exercising self-restraint. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as propaganda published in a Korean magazine, conveying the Japanese leadership's message directly to the colonized population. It frames the ongoing war as a necessary sacrifice for a greater regional order, urges Koreans (as part of the Japanese Empire) to endure hardships, and subtly reinforces the idea of Japanese moral leadership, which Koreans were expected to emulate or at least accept.

戰爭은 아직 멀었다, 板垣 參謀長의 警告
南京 中山路의 장대한 舊 국민정부 외교부 자리에 있는 支那 파견군 총사령부의 質素한 응접실에서 板垣 참모장은 이렇게 말하였다. 爲先 신정부의 성립을 祝한 뒤
「그러나 이로서 국민은 안심하여서는 못 쓴다. 현지에서는 항일군과의 사이에 저번의 歐羅巴大戰에 비할 대규모의 전투를 하고 있음으로 일층 결속을 굳세게 하여 주기를 바란다. 금번 사변으로 日本 국민도 상당한 희생을 내고 있지만은 그러나 직접의 戰禍는 조금도 입고 있지 않다, 이 점은 支那 민중이 더욱 큰 희생을 하고 있음으로 이 일을 생각하여 참어 주기를 바란다. 內地는 물자가 부족하고 있다 하나 수년 후에는 日滿支間에 自給自足이 되게 될 것이니 조금만 참으면 되는 일이다.」

「이번 전쟁은 近衛聲明으로 천하에 이미 성명한 바와 같이 토지도 취하지 않고, 돈도 받지 안는 것으로 오직 한가지의 戰果는 日支 제휴에 의하여 東亞 영원의 평화를 건설함에 있다.
그러나 日支의 제휴는 衷心으로 흘러나오는 제휴가 아니면 안다. 서로가 존중히 하며 서로가 신뢰하여 가는 데서 마음의 제휴가 되는 것이다. 그러기에 이 기회에 모든 日本人은 支那人으로부터 존경을 받을 만한 인격을 만들 필요가 있고 그 때문에 국내의 정신 교육, 도덕 교육과, 그리고 현지의 日本人의 자숙이 필요한 것이다.」<83>
