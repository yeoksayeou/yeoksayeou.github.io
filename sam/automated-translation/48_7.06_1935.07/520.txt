Treasured Mirror of Medicine and Pharmacy, (Life, Disease, Health)
						By Former Professor at Keijō Medical College, Former Lecturer at Keijō Imperial University Faculty of Medicine, Doctor of Medicine, Pak Ch'anghun

We always want to be healthy. We always want to be full of vigor, to live actively and pleasantly through these 60 years of life. I am a medical scientist. From a medical scientist's perspective, our Korean people are unhealthy. That is, there are many diseases. Therefore, my wish is to find a way to make the Korean people free from illness, to always live in a world of health. With this thought, under the topic presented in the title, I intend to write a "Theory of Health" from a medical standpoint. As the very first step, I wish to inform you first of the knowledge of medicinal prescriptions, which is the most essential knowledge for our daily lives, and thus decided to first list a "Prescription Compendium."

Popular Practical Prescription Compendium (Reprinting and Publication Prohibited)

(Foreword) 1. This section is intended to be practically useful not only for general patients but also to serve as a reference for drug merchants, medical practitioners, medical students, candidates for the physician examination, and even physicians, and will be described continuously in a general manner.
2. Within the text, only the drug names are written in Katakana for the practical convenience of the readers. The reason is that it is easier for you to actually <238> buy medicine when you go to the pharmacy.
3. Please be aware in advance that among the various medicines listed in the content, potent and toxic drugs are difficult to obtain without a doctor's certificate.

Experimental Prescription Compendium (Reprinting and Publication Prohibited)

Part 1: Internal Medicine Section
Chapter 1: Medicaments for Respiratory Diseases
Section 1: Expectorants (袪痰劑)

This agent aims to loosen phlegm (喀痰) and facilitate its expectoration (喀出), and is primarily used when expectoration is difficult.
The causes of difficult expectoration are:
First, because the phlegm is viscous (粘稠).
Second, because the phlegm is excessive (過多), making expectoration impossible through physiological coughing alone.
Third, when the cough is weak (咳嗽 薄弱).
Among the above, in the first case, mucus dissolving and diluting agents such as ammonia preparations (アンモニア劑) and iodine preparations (沃度劑) are used.
In the second case, cough-inducing agents (催咳性劑) containing saponin (サポニン), such as Senega root (ゼネガ根) and Onji root (遠志根), are used, and depending on the situation, secretion-inhibiting and antiseptic agents (制泌防腐劑) like hydrated terpin (抱水テレビン) are used.

**Summary**

This article, written by Dr. Pak Ch'anghun, a former professor at Keijō Medical College, expresses concern over the poor health of Koreans in 1935 and aims to provide practical medical knowledge. It begins a series titled "Treasured Mirror of Medicine and Pharmacy" focusing on life, disease, and health. This first installment introduces a "Popular Practical Prescription Compendium," starting with expectorants for respiratory conditions. It lists 16 detailed prescriptions, including ingredients (often using Japanese Katakana names for pharmaceuticals), dosages, preparation methods, and indications like bronchitis, pneumonia, and tuberculosis.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects a Korean medical professional's view on public health during the colonial period, acknowledging Koreans' general unhealthiness. It shows the dissemination of Western/Japanese medical knowledge (indicated by the pharmaceutical names like セネガ根, 沃度カリウム, ヂオニン, フストール, etc., written in Katakana, and referencing German products) through a Korean-language magazine for a general audience. The practical instruction to use Katakana names at pharmacies highlights the linguistic and commercial realities of accessing modern medicine in colonial Korea. The mix of traditional herbal ingredients (like 杏仁, 遠志根, 桔梗) with modern chemical compounds is also noteworthy.

第一章 呼吸器病에 對한 藥劑
第一節 袪痰劑

本劑본   本본본본本本 본본저ㅔ는 喀痰을 緩解하야 喀出을 용이케 하는 목적에 供하나니 주로 被痰이 困難한 時에 사용하는 약제이다.
被痰困難의 원인도
第一은 喀痰이 粘稠한 까닭으로
第二는 喀痰이 과다하야 생리적 咳嗽로는 도저이 喀出이 不可及하는 경우
第三은 咳嗽 薄弱한 때
以上中 제일의 경우에는 アンモニア劑, 沃度劑 等의 粘液溶解 稀釋劑를 사용
第二의 경우에는, ゼネガ根, 遠志根 等과 如한
サポニン含有의 催咳性劑를 사용하며 형편에 의하야는 抱水テレビン等의 制泌防腐劑를 사용함.
