Introduction to New Businesses for Intelligent Youths with Small Capital

1.  A Mobile Movie Theater that can be started with 100 Won
2.  A "Play Guide" that can be started with 10 Won
3.  A Used Magazine Business that can be started with 6 Won
4.  A Newspaper Reading Club that can be started with 5 Won
5.  A Record Listening Club possible with Several Tens of Won
6.  An Economy-Focused Magazine Subscription Reading Club

Economy-Focused Magazine Subscription Reading Club

As a cultural institution that disseminates taste and knowledge to households (定庭), responding to the demands of the era with an economy-first motto, the new business, the Magazine Subscription Reading Club, has recently been welcomed. The special characteristic of this business is that it meets the demands of readers who wish to read through about 10 books for the price of purchasing just one or two magazines, thus conforming to society's needs.
The current management method, membership fees, and other details of the Subscription Reading Club are explained as follows. (Method) Desired new magazines are delivered to the member's home or a designated location for perusal. The designated number of books is 10 per month, and these are rotated for reading, exchanging one book every 4 days. However, the number of books can be reduced, and the exchange interval extended to 7 days.
(Membership Fee) 1 Won 30 Chŏn per month, collected from the 11th to the 16th of each month. For months with special supplementary issues (정기할인 더하는 달 - likely referring to magazines with supplements), an additional 20 chŏn each is added for April, May, July, September, October, and December, and 30 chŏn is added for January.<226>
(Circulation Area) Throughout the entire city and adjacent counties, delivery is prompt upon application.
△ Back issues (달느진 - 月遲) are sold separately at about half the cover price. A catalog price list will be presented.
Other key points are "No Security Deposit Required," joining is based on foundation, trust, and accuracy first at our club—
Printed materials detailing the above information, an application form, and magazine titles are printed on postcard-sized paper (엽서 倍版). Titled "Grand Recruitment for Circulating Reading Members," the front of the application is made in the style of a private postcard (私製葉書體), with fields established for writing the applicant's address (district, block, number) and name, addressed to "Such-and-such Magazine Circulating Reading Club." These printed materials are distributed to each household as inserts (折込廣告) in newspapers.
Thus, if the membership fee becomes 1 Won or more, the scope of the reader's magazine circulation must be broadened, the number of magazines enriched, and selections made. If the hypothetical fee is lowered to 1 Won, the regulation of 10 books per month could be adjusted to a circulating scope of 6 books. That aside, to fully satisfy the readers' desires regarding the list of magazines (書目), efforts must be made to increase the number of available magazines as much as possible. For its types, if it's a somewhat known magazine, list various kinds such as politics, finance, economics, ideology, hobbies, knowledge, law, diplomacy, industry, business, vocational studies, education, religion, history, geography, literature, arts, literary arts, science, photography, sports, language, medicine, health, home, women, cooking, young boys, girls, youth, students, storytelling (講談), film, music, art, poetry (詩歌), etc. Also, for those published more than twice a month, attach publication frequency codes like 2 or 3 at the beginning of the magazine title, and allow members to freely designate their required 10 types from among them. Listing all the various magazine titles here is omitted, but it is necessary to present about 150 types of magazine titles from the categories mentioned above in the advertisement space. This business can be done by anyone and does not necessarily require a bookstore. As for capital, just 100 Won (一金 100圓) is sufficient. At the start of the business, purchase about 150 magazines for circulation; even if the average purchase price (仕入價) per book is 50 Chŏn,<227> 150 books would cost 75 Won. Since the advertising print is small, postcard-sized, 10,000 copies would cost 15 Won. If you entrust the newspaper insertion to the distribution agencies (賣捌店) in the target area, even for 10,000 copies, entrusting this to 4 or 5 agencies would cost 2 or 3 Won per agency; for 5 agencies, 10 Won would cover distribution to the desired areas. Therefore, based on the calculation above, it can be managed with 100 Won. Even if only 2 percent (2보 - 步 means percent here) become members from the 10,000 advertisements, that's 200 members. Even in these recent times of economic hardship, if it doesn't exceed 2 percent, one can acquire a minimum of 100 members, which is 1 percent (1보). With 100 members, the club's income is 130 Won. If the magazines that have finished circulation are also sold, the total income could reach around 152 Won.

A 16mm Mobile Movie Theater that can be started with 100 Won

The term "16mm" has become so well-publicized that it's even used in newspaper gossip column headlines (미다시). Focusing on the fact that it hasn't been commercialized beyond being a hobby for some, how about trying to run this 16mm mobile movie theater?
A 16mm movie theater, which is most interesting, refined, educational, and yet absolutely safe using non-flammable film, is sure to be a huge success. The method is, first, prepare a portable screen (뽀-다불 - probably from 'portable') on the luggage rack of a bicycle, one 16mm movie projector, about 5 reels of somewhat worn film (mainly cartoons and interesting things), plus drawing paper (模*紙 - likely 模造紙, mōzōshi), a brush, and an ink pot. With just this preparation, travel around the rural villages (농촌). In a village, find a vacant lot like a clothes-drying area, establish it as a base, and put up posters made nearby.
Then, as the sun sets in the west, connect the electricity, place the machine stand on the bicycle's luggage rack. One technician-cum-musician operates the gramophone, provides narration, and makes people laugh—this can be a great success. For admission fees, it's fine to accept money, but if accepting money is impossible in a region, it's also fine to receive a bag of rice per person, just as Ch'ŏndogyo (천도교) collects spoonfuls of consecrated rice (성미<228>). Now, if we break down the capital (자금), it's as follows:
16mm projector 45 Won, 5 reels of somewhat worn film (후리루무 - film) 35 Won, a worn portable screen (뽀-다부르) 10 Won, a worn bicycle (자동차 - here likely meaning 自轉車, bicycle) 8 Won, paper, brush, ink, etc. (지필묵즙 등) 2 Won, Total 100 Won. Thus, touring villages and doing rice-begging movie screenings can be done without much difficulty.
Special points to note:
1. Know the regulations concerning movie screenings thoroughly.
2. Use censored film (후이리무).
3. Obtain the qualifications for a narrator.

A Street "Play Guide" that can be started with 10 Won

In Ponjŏng (본정), there is a proper business called a Play Guide, which operates on the roadside, next to a building, or beside a cigarette vendor's stall. It's called a street Play Guide. Of course, being a Play Guide, it operates near places like movie theaters or performance theaters.
This business, unlike others, doesn't require large facilities, so it doesn't cost much money. First, get one stool, buy about two kerosene boxes (석유궤ㅅ작), cover their exterior with something like an old blanket for decoration (裝蒒), and create compartments inside to make it splendid. Purchase 10 tickets with an original price (票原價) of 20 Chŏn each for a first-rate theater (1流舘) from about 2 theaters. If these cost 25 chŏn [to resell? context implies buying at a discount and reselling slightly higher, or buying regular price and selling higher - likely the latter or buying wholesale], you make a small profit. Next, purchase tickets for a second-rate theater (2流舘) at 15 Chŏn and sell them for 20 Chŏn, and buy tickets for a third-rate theater (3流舘) for 5 Chŏn and sell them for 10 Chŏn, making a 5 Chŏn profit. How does it work? Without needing to shout much, just attach an easy-to-see headline (미다시) and a price card board saying which theater and how much, and that's it. If you set up shop on a Saturday or a holiday (祭日) on the roadside near Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇場), Tansŏngsa (團成社), or Umi Kwan (優美舘), you should sell quite a few. If you sell 20-30 tickets on weekdays and about 80 on holidays, even a 5 Chŏn profit per ticket is not insignificant. Thinking this way, this is by no means a laughable business<229> but a decent occupation (好職業) for a 10 Won capital.

A Used Magazine Purchasing Business that can be started with 6 Won

As the world becomes shallower (얏타질수록 - implies more superficial or perhaps economically tighter), people want to buy and read new magazines cheaply by paying less. They don't mind reading magazines that are about a month old if they can buy them cheaply. Students and women, in particular, have this mentality. Thus, recently, used 'tsukiokure' (ツキオクレ - Japanese for month-late/back issues) magazines from secondhand dealers (고물상) are reportedly selling quite well. Even if they are old magazines, those older than a month won't do. Ones that are 25 or 30 days past the publication date are less interesting. Magazines like Chung'ang (중앙), Samch'ŏlli (三千里), or Sin Kajŏng (新家庭) would be good. Capital of about 6 Won would suffice. Create introductory cards (人事狀) like name cards using a mimeograph (謄寫版) or something similar, visit each household, and say, "Please sell me your new magazines."
Anyway, the end of the month or the beginning of the next month is fine. Also, go to the magazine company after the 15th, around the 25th, buy magazines priced at 30 Chŏn for 20 Chŏn, 15 Chŏn, or even 10 Chŏn, and if you receive just 25 Chŏn each, this is also a business where you can gather quite a bit.

A Newspaper Reading Club that can be started with 5 Won

Reading about one type of newspaper is usually enough. However, some people are not satisfied unless they read two or three types. Salarymen (사라리맨) read one type upon waking up in the morning, read another for about 10 minutes before leaving for work after breakfast, and feel satisfied only after reading about one more upon returning in the evening. This is a specific case, but people living in retirement or on pensions want to read various types even if they are a day late. Every afternoon, distribute today's A and B newspapers, yesterday's C and D newspapers, and the day before yesterday's E and F newspapers. Then, collect the ones distributed the previous day. Next, to another person, distribute today's C and D newspapers and the day before yesterday's A and B newspapers in the afternoon. Employ distribution methods like this. This is just one example; if there's a better combination method, it's fine to adopt it. This way, one always gets to read several preferred newspapers between the afternoon and the afternoon of the next day.<230> In any case, being able to read two types of the current day's newspapers in the afternoon is splendid. This requires a minimum capital of 5 Won to deliver papers like the Chosŏn (朝鮮), Tong'a (東亞), Chung'ang (中央), and Maesin (每申 - likely Maeil Sinbo).

A Record Listening Club Sufficient with Several Tens of Won

If records retail for an average of 1 Won 50 Chŏn, a purchase cost (仕入價) of 1 Won per month can come into hand. Initially, you probably won't be able to purchase them outright one by one. Attach a 'watari' (와다리 - likely means an adapter or connection for multiple listeners, or perhaps refers to a deferred payment system - context suggests the latter) to the gramophone. When there's an order, purchase that record and pay on 30-day or 60-day terms. This is only at the beginning; soon, as it prospers, this [profit margin] will remain. Next, print the purpose of this club and distribute it via newspaper inserts (折込). The cost for 3,000 copies is 10 Won. Make it a bi-fold private postcard style (2折私製葉書型), with one fold being the order form for the customer. Gramophone companies will advertise new records (新譜) in newspapers around the 10th of each month, so if possible, insert and distribute them in the newspapers at that time. Since new records can be known well before the newspaper advertisements, stealthily copy (인* - likely 印寫, inswa, print/copy) the lists from each gramophone company. Monthly * fee (月*種 - typo, likely 月定金, wŏljŏnggŭm, monthly fee or similar), with 4-day rotation per type, 1 Won per month (*金 1원 - typo, likely 定金, chŏnggŭm, fixed fee).
From the vicinity of this business, one can do side jobs (내직) like selling gramophones or radios on installment plans. Initially, it's in the form of a sales agent (외교원) for a gramophone merchant, but based on this, it can gradually grow larger. Therefore, initial newspaper insertion cost 20 Won, transportation and letter (렛데르代 - letter cost, likely postage/stationery) costs 7-8 Won, total about 30 Won is needed.<231>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine outlines six low-capital business ideas targeted at educated ("intelligent") young Korean men facing economic hardship. The businesses include a magazine subscription club, a 16mm mobile movie theater for rural areas, reselling theater tickets ("play guide"), dealing in used magazines, a newspaper rotation club, and a record listening club. Each description provides estimated startup costs (ranging from 5 to 100 Won), operational details, potential profits, and marketing suggestions like using newspaper inserts. The article reflects the economic conditions of colonial Korea, the growing popularity of modern media like magazines, films, and records, and the entrepreneurial spirit aimed at the urban and educated youth demographic. Historians might find the specific cost breakdowns, the suggested business models (like subscription/rotation services and installment plans), the mention of specific media outlets (theaters, newspapers), the focus on "intelligent youth" as entrepreneurs, and the blend of Japanese loanwords and older Korean interesting for understanding urban life, popular culture, and small-scale commerce during this period.

經濟本位의 雜誌購讀會

定庭의 취미와 지식을 보급식히는 문화기관으로서 시대의 요구에 응하야 경제본위를 못-도로 한 新商業, 雜誌購讀會가 최근에 환영밧고 잇다. 이 사업의 特長은 단지 잡지 1, 2책의 구입할 돈으로 10책 내외를 통독할 수 잇다는 독자의 요구에 의하야 조직된 것만큼 사회에 迎合된다.
購讀會의 현재 경영하고 잇는 방법 及 회비 기타의 事况을 설명하면 左와 如하다. (방법) 희망하는 신간잡지를 회원의 가정 혹은 지정장소에 배달하야 열람케 한다. 그리하야 지정 책 수는 1개월 10책으로 하고 이것을 4일만에 1책식 교환하야 廻讀한다. 단 책수를 減하야 교환일을 7일식 연기할 수도 잇다.

(순회) 全市 及 接續郡에 亙하야 申込次第 신속배달한다,

100원으로 될 수 잇는 十六미리 移動映畵舘
