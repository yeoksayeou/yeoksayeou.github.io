Publication Date: 1935년 07월 01일 (July 1, 1935)
Article Title: 三千里 社交室 (Samch'ŏlli Social Room)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous writing/jottings)

三千里 社交室 (Samch'ŏlli Social Room)
Starting this month, we have established a Social Room. This is intended to be a place similar to a "smoking room," where figures from all classes of the peninsula—thinkers, financiers, writers, educators, etc.—can converse lightly and freely without any constraints. Therefore, this month, we have primarily recorded, in no particular order, snippets gathered during conversations between our company's reporters and various notable figures. Next month, we will change the format, selecting pairs of figures who fit well together in combination, and publish one dialogue from each pair.

General Suetsugu's Yi Sun-sin

Mr. Song Chin-u says—Although it was widely reported in the Ōsaka Mainichi Shinbun, when Admiral Suetsugu was the Commander-in-Chief of the Combined Fleet, he gave a speech at a public hall somewhere, either in Osaka or Tokyo. At that time, he highly praised Yi Sun-sin's strategy and tactics as rare in all ages, saying, "Yi Sun-sin of the East (東洋) is a greater commander than Nelson of the West (西洋)." Perhaps it's a case of a great commander recognizing another great commander.

Plan for the Realization of a Second "Sŏul"

Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik says—Unbeknownst to anyone in the world right now, I have purchased 100,000 p'yŏng of land<201> X at a certain location near Sŏul. This is part of a plan to build a large city here, about the size of Sŏul. As for the nature of this city, it is still a secret within a secret (秘中의 又 秘事), so I cannot speak of it externally. However, just as Osaka has Takarazuka (寶塚), an ideal land (이상향) will arise here that will provide spiritual comfort and material supply to the people of Sŏul. To realize this, funds amounting to several million or tens of millions of won are needed. In any case, the detailed plan (복안) is all set, but since it is not yet time to announce it, I will stop here.

Eight Banks Merged

Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl says—The Commercial Bank (商業銀行) recently acquired the Pusan Bank (釜山銀行), but this doesn't mean the capital of the Commercial Bank increases. Since Pusan Bank was simply paid-in (拂込한) at 750,000 won, and we paid 750,000 won to acquire it, the nominal capital (公稱資本) of 10 million won remains the same. Pusan Bank had 8 branches, so they all changed their signs to become our branches. For now, it's just the contract; the complete handover is scheduled for early next month, and I will also go to Pusan then. Anyway, with the Commercial Bank acquiring Pusan Bank, it means we have merged or acquired eight banks so far.

On the Marriage of Ewha Womans College Graduates

Mr. Yi In says—Today, while returning in a car after visiting Ewha Womans College (梨花女專), I discussed this with fellow passengers Mr. Song Chin-u, Mr. Paek Kwan-su, and Mr. Kim Chun-yŏn. Where could all the graduates of Ewha Womans College, who studied literature and music in such a splendid academy, possibly find places to marry? It seems too far removed from the reality of Chosŏn (朝鮮).

Chungdong Foundation Progressing Well

Mr. Ch'oe Kyu-dong says—Many people kindly inquire about the establishment of the Chungdong School (中東學校) incorporated foundation after its separation from the Kogyŏ Academy (高啓學園) foundation. Originally, the land, buildings, and equipment/books (器書) currently in Susong-dong (壽松洞) are worth about 200,000 won. Therefore, externally, only about 100,000 won more is needed. Funds of that amount are progressing smoothly, and the 300,000 won incorporated foundation will be completed soon (未久).

Osaka Asahi and Sŏl Ŭi-sik

Mr. Pak Ch'ang-hun says—Among all newspapers, the one I read with the greatest interest is the Ōsaka Asahi (大阪朝日), and even then, it's mainly for its "Tensei Jingo" (天聲人語) column rather than all its articles. The writer of that column can truly be called a genius—the humor, the harmless satire, the pithy, cutting phrases (寸鐵殺人)—it's truly astonishing writing. Also, I hear that Mr. Sŏl Ŭi-sik, the editorial director of the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), writes its "Hoengsŏl Susŏl" (횡설수설) column. There are many brilliant phrases there that make one listen intently (赤 귀를 기우리고) and sigh thrice in admiration (三嘆). Even if I can't read other articles, I meticulously read those two columns in those two newspapers every single day without fail, laughing and admiring them to myself afterwards.<202>

Tonga Securities' 500,000 Won

Mr. Chang Hŭng-su says—(Director (取締役), Tonga Securities Stock Company (東亞證券 株式會社))—Our company mainly focuses on stock brokerage. The nominal capital is 500,000 won with 125,000 won paid-in. The president is Cho Chun-ho; Directors (取締役) are Kim Hŭng-gyŏm, Chang Hŭng-su, Nakano Yūkichi, Hwang Yun-su; Auditors (監査役) are Kim Tong-jun, Kim Tong-sŏk. Since our founding last January, we have been growing significantly. We have also constructed a new company building...

Definition of a Hero

Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi says—My goodness, what is this world coming to? Everyone boasts that they are a hero (英雄), except for those who think, "I am certainly not a hero." It's truly dumbfounding, really dumbfounding (긔가 막힐세)!

Always Wondering About Sŏul

Mr. Hŏ Hŏn says—Although there's nothing special happening in Sŏul, when I'm staying in the countryside, I can't help but feel curious, as if something must be happening in Sŏul. While staying in the countryside, you wouldn't believe how eagerly I wait each day for the newspapers that arrive two or three days late.

Students Abroad Are High-Spirited

Mr. An Chae-hong says—When I visited Tokyo (東京) recently for the first time in over 20 years, it was truly surprising. Every single person was tense, and the city streets were splendid and well-ordered—and what's more, I was invited by the Meiji University (明治大學) alumni association, gave a lecture one evening, and afterwards had intimate talks (交膝而談) with the Chosŏn students there. They were all very high-spirited (意氣張大), seeming quite reliable.

Publish Old Literary Works

Mr. Yi Kwang-su says—If someone with purpose among publishers would step forward to collect and republish works like Silla (新羅) hyangga, Ch'unhyangjŏn, Simch'ŏngjŏn, etc., it would be a great help to our culture. Is there no one who will do so?

What I Learned This Time

Mr. Kim Yak-su says—Previously, when I went to study in Tokyo (東京), I learned about "Class" (階級). This time, having returned from Tokyo, I have come to understand "Nation" (民族).

Revival of Local Folk Songs

Mr. Yi Ki-se says—The popular songs circulating on the streets these days are becoming increasingly decadent; some are truly unbearable to the ear. And are there any popular songs with long lives? Not really. They just appear suddenly and are discarded just as suddenly—it's truly lamentable. The conclusion drawn from many years of experience is that, no matter what, we must revive songs based on our own folk songs (民謠).<203>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine introduces a new "Social Room" feature, presenting short remarks from prominent Korean figures of the era on various topics. These include praise for Admiral Yi Sun-sin by a Japanese admiral, Pak Hŭng-sik's ambitious plan for a "Second Sŏul," a bank merger, concerns about Ewha graduates' marriage prospects, updates on the Chungdong School foundation, commentary on newspaper columns, business news from Tonga Securities, reflections on heroism, the experience of being away from Sŏul, impressions of high-spirited Korean students in Tokyo, calls for publishing classical Korean literature and reviving folk songs, and Kim Yak-su's notable shift in focus from "class" to "nation." The article offers a snapshot of the interests, activities, and evolving thoughts of the Korean elite under Japanese colonial rule, touching upon economic development, cultural identity, social change, and political consciousness. For historians, Kim Yak-su's statement about prioritizing "Nation" over "Class" is particularly revealing of the ideological shifts occurring among Korean intellectuals facing intensified assimilation pressures in the mid-1930s. Pak Hŭng-sik's large-scale urban development plan also highlights the ambitions of Korean capitalists within the colonial economy.

三千里 社交室
이달부터 사교실을 두엇습니다. 여기에는 반도의 사상가 재정가 문인 교육자 등 온갓 계급의 인사가 아모 구속받음이 업시 가비엽게 또한 자유롭게 서로 이약이하는 「스목-킹, 룸」 비슷한 처소로 만들엇슴니다. 그래서 이번달은 주로 본사 기자가 諸 명사와 대화 중 어든 담편을 순서업시 채록키로 하엿는데 이 다음달은 형식을 밧고아 서로 어울니는 콘비네-슌적 인물 두 분씩 택하여 서로 서로의 대화 한쌍식 싯기로 하겟습니다.

末次將軍의 李舜臣

宋鎭禹氏 曰-大阪每日新聞 기사에도 크게 난 일이지만 末次大將이 연합함대사령장관으로 잇슬 적에는 大阪선가 東京선가 어느 공회당에서 연설한 일이 잇섯는데 그때 「東洋에 李舜臣은 西洋의 넬손보다 더 나은 名將」이라고 李舜臣의 전략전술을 고금에 드문 명전략이라 하야 激稱한 일이 잇섯지요. 可謂 명장이 명장을 안다 할 일일넌지요.

第二「서울」實現設

朴興植氏 曰-나는 지금 세상 사람 아모도 모르게 서울 근방의 모처에<201> 땅X 10만평을 삿는데, 이것은 여기에다가 장차 서울만한 큰 도시를 하나 꾸밀 계획이여요. 이 도시는 엇든 성질의 것인가 하면 아직 秘中의 又 秘事니까 외부에 말할 수는 업지만 大阪에 寶塚이 잇드시 여기에도 서울 사람에게 정신적 위안과 물질적 공급을 줄 이상향이 서질 것임니다. 이를 실현하기 위하여는 수백만원 수천만원의 자금이 듬니다. 엇잿든 복안은 다 섯스나 아직은 발표할 시기가 아니기 이만 하기로 하지요.

八個銀行이 集成하여

朴榮喆氏 曰-商業銀行에서 이번에 釜山銀行을 매수하엿는데 그러타고 商銀의 자본금이 증액되는 것은 아니지요, 그냥 75만원을 拂込한 釜山銀行이니까 우리가 75만원을 내어서 매수하엿스니까 1,000만원의 公稱자본은 그대로 잇지요. 釜銀 지점이 8개소가 잇기에 모다 우리 지점으로 간판을 곳첫는데 아직은 계약뿐이고 來月 초에 전부 인계 밧기로 되어 나도 그때 釜山 가겟소이다. 아무튼 우리 商業銀行은 釜山銀行까지 매수하니 그동안 여덜개의 은행을 합병 혹 매수한 셈이얘요.

梨花女專 出身의 出嫁는

李仁氏 曰-오늘 梨花女專 구경을 하고 도라오는 자동차 안에서 동승한 宋鎭禹, 白寬洙
金俊淵씨 등과도 이약이 한 일이지만 그러케 호화찬란한 학원에서 글배우고 음악 배우든 梨花女專 출신들은 모다 어대로 시집갈 곳이 잇슬는지 朝鮮의 현실과는 암만해도 너무 거리가 먼 것 갓해요.

中東財團은 良好進行

崔奎東씨 曰-高啓學園 재단과 갈나진 뒤 中東學校의 재단법인 성립여부를 호의로 뭇는 인사가 만흔데 원래 현재 壽松洞에 잇는 기지와 건물과 *器*書만 약 20만원어치가 잇스니까, 밧갓흐로는 약 10만원만 잇스면 되겟는데 그 정도의 금액은 순서롭게, 진행되고 잇서 未久에 30만원의 재단법인이 완성될 것이외다.

大阪朝日과 薛義植

朴昌薰氏 曰-나는 온갓 신문 가운데 가장 흥미를 가지고 읽기는 大阪朝日인데 그것도 全 기사라기 보다 그의 「天聲人語」의 1난 때문이외다. 그 난의 필자만은 실로 천재라 할 것으로 유-모어와 뼈업는 풍자와 寸鐵殺人의 경구와-참 놀나운 필치여요. 그리고 東亞日報의 「횡설수설」을 그 社 편집국장 薛義植씨가 쓴다는데 거기 赤 귀를 기우리고 三嘆할  명구가 만해요. 나는 다른 기사는 못보아도 그 두 신문의 두 가지 난은 매일 빼지 안코 숙독하고 나서 혼자 웃고 우스며 찬탄 하기를<202> 마지아니다.

東亞證券의 50萬圓
