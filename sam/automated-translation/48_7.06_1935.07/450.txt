The Skill of Smiling/Laughing

They say there isn't a great man (大丈夫) who doesn't melt when a peerless beauty (絶代佳人) smiles sweetly. So then, how many facial muscles (얼골筋肉) does a person use when laughing? It's merely seven, they say.<216>

**Summary**

This brief article remarks on the captivating power of a beautiful woman's smile, claiming it can disarm any "great man." It then presents a supposed fact: smiling only requires the use of seven facial muscles. From a historical perspective, the piece offers insight into the popular culture of 1930s Korea, reflecting prevalent gender tropes (the powerful effect of female beauty on men) and the dissemination of simplistic physiological information. The language itself, with its older spellings and grammatical forms, is also of linguistic interest to historians.
