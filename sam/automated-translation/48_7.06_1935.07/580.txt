잡지명	삼천리 제7권 제6호
발행일	1935년 07월 01일
기사제목	우리님 (Our Beloved)
필자	萬海 韓龍雲
기사형태	시 (Poem)

우리님
						萬海 韓龍雲
대실로 비단 짜고
솔닢으로 바눌 삼어
「萬古靑靑」 수를 노아
옷을 지어 두엇다가
어집어 해가 차거든
우리님게 드리리라<270>

Our Beloved
						Manhae Han Yongun

Weaving silk with bamboo threads,
Taking pine needles for a needle,
Embroidering "Eternal Verdure" (萬古靑靑),
I made clothes and put them aside.
Alas, when the year grows cold,
I shall offer them to our beloved.<270>

**Summary**

This short poem by Manhae Han Yongun describes the act of painstakingly creating a garment. The speaker weaves silk from bamboo fiber, uses pine needles as a needle, and embroiders the phrase "萬古靑靑" (Eternal Verdure), symbolizing constancy and eternity. This specially made clothing is set aside, intended as a gift for "our beloved" (우리님) when the year ends or grows harsh ("해가 차거든"). From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period by a renowned independence activist and Buddhist monk, carries significant weight. "Our beloved" is widely interpreted not just as a personal love interest, but allegorically as the lost nation of Korea, or perhaps the Buddha or an absolute truth. The themes of enduring creation using natural elements, patient waiting, and devotion ("Eternal Verdure") amidst difficult times ("어집어 해가 차거든" - Alas, when the year grows cold) would have resonated deeply, offering a subtle expression of hope and unwavering loyalty to Korea's identity and future.
