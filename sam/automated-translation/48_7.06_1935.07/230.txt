기사제목	美人薄命哀史, 人世無情·蒼波有情의 尹心悳 (A Tragic History of Beauty Fated to Die Young: Yun Simdŏk of the Heartless Human World and the Compassionate Blue Waves)
필자	綠衣童子
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellany)

美人薄命哀史, 人世無情·蒼波有情의 尹心悳
						綠衣童子
"Sister, aren't your eyebrows too thin?"
Sŏk Kŭmsŏng, sitting beside her, spoke kindly to Yun Simdŏk, who was applying makeup.
"Could you draw them for me? What would a novice know?"
Yun Simdŏk leaned her face toward Sŏk Kŭmsŏng's chin and smiled prettily.
"Your lips are too red."
Kŭmsŏng again skillfully touched up the clumsily applied rouge on her lips.
"My heart is pounding, I don't know if the words will come out right."
Simdŏk couldn't contain her worry. It was the first time in her life stepping onto a stage. This was the predecessor to Yun Simdŏk the vocalist. The stage might be the same, but the gestures! The hand movements she had never done before!<132> How would she manage the sorrowful laments without musical accompaniment?
"Even so, once you get out there, you become bold, find unexpected spirit, and it all just flows smoothly."
Kŭmsŏng tried to offer comfort with a composed smile.
"Well, I wish it would turn out like that."
Simdŏk forced a smile too.
"Alright – the stage is ready! Miss Yun Simdŏk, please put on your costume."
The young director looked into the dressing room and called out. Today was the day the ill-fated beauty Yun Simdŏk, a graduate of the voice department of Tokyo Ueno Music School and former teacher at Kyŏngsŏng OO Women's Higher Common School, joined the T'owŏrhoe theater troupe and stepped onto the stage for the first time! The time was the first autumn of Taishō 14 (1925)! The place was Kwangmutae Theater in Koganechō 3-chōme! Already, calls for Yun Simdŏk were raining down from the audience.
"Those people are calling for me just to insult me."
Yun Simdŏk wiped away tears as she changed her clothes.
"Look, whether the audience insults or praises, what does it matter? When many people gather, it's natural there will be all sorts of opinions." The author of today's play featuring Yun Simdŏk in the lead role, Mr. Yi Kyŏngson, patted her back, trying to soothe her again.

"What sin have I committed? Is it right that the man who seduced an innocent girl, who didn't even know what a lie was, got her pregnant, and then abandoned her like a worn-out shoe, receives no punishment, while I alone must bear all the blame?"
Once on stage, Yun Simdŏk poured out the tearful complaints of "Anna," the protagonist of *Tongdo*.
"Damn it! She's crying so much, you can't hear her words clearly. An actress should calmly deliver sorrowful lines to make the audience cry, but she cries first, choking up, so what enjoyment is there for the audience?"
The director standing behind the backdrop clicked his tongue. Her debut performance that night was deemed a complete failure because she shed too many tears. From the moment the play *Tongdo* was chosen, Yun Simdŏk had prepared for today's tears. For her, playing the protagonist in a sad story of a young girl who offered her life deceived by false love, today's tears were perhaps inevitable.
"Anyway, you worked hard. Starting tomorrow night, just cry beforehand and then go out."
The troupe leader, Mr. Pak Sŭnghŭi, silenced the murmuring crowd.
"Here! They said to give this to you."
A small boy held out a bouquet of flowers! A white slip of paper was attached to the end of the pink ribbon.
"Wow! Real flowers!"
"Where did these roses come from in autumn?"
"They must have been grown in a greenhouse."
The crowd buzzed again. Yun Simdŏk accepted the flowers, but as soon as she saw the writing on the paper slip, her expression changed instantly. She threw the bouquet to the ground and stomped on it repeatedly.<133>
"Go back and tell them I trampled the flowers like this."
The flower shop boy who had delivered them ran away frightened.

"Seriously, what nerve does that person have to send me flowers?"
Yun Simdŏk fumed alone until dawn. She felt wronged.
"After ruining someone's life..."
She wept and sighed repeatedly.
After studying abroad as an OO exchange student, dreaming of a bright future, she went to Tokyo to major in voice. Upon her return, her fame as a blossoming flower on stage, a 'modern girl,' grew day by day. She was naturally cheerful. She didn't discriminate between men and women in her friendships and didn't consider safety or danger wherever she went, roaming the streets of Kyŏngsŏng like a lark singing freely in the clear sky. During this time, the tempting snares reaching out from here and there entangled her frail body like a spider's web. By the time she realized her own situation had grown dark, she had reached a point of no return.
"All men are thieves! Especially rich ones are scoundrels! Thinking they can buy people with money, thinking they can get away with deadly deeds just by paying!"
She eventually came to curse the wealthy. Feeling stifled by the restrictive school life, she yearned to break free into the world and establish herself as a vocalist. Sensing this, the wealthy Yi Yŏngmun (pseudonym – original text) living under OO, employed several intermediaries to win her favor.
"Mr. Yi is a wealthy gentleman who admires your talent as a vocalist and wishes to support you to promote the music scene of the peninsula. Why not take this opportunity to shake hands with Mr. Yi?"
Hearing this, Yun Simdŏk's heart leaped. Performing on stage once a month, gathering beloved students to teach singing – how enjoyable that life would be. And didn't Mr. Yi say he would provide necessary funds with noble intentions? Mr. Yi was a millionaire. What could hold her back? Yun immediately had her first formal meeting with Mr. Yi at the Chosŏn Hotel. On her way back, she promptly sent a long telegram to Tokyo. In Tokyo was her lover, Kim Ujin, with whom she had promised her life. She sought her lover's consent. This was a necessary step for someone in love. From Tokyo, an "OK" arrived that very night. A letter followed:
"I don't know what kind of person Mr. Yi is, but since you trust him, I will trust him too."
Kim's heart was 'nickel-plated' (니콜; likely meaning 'reflected' or 'in tune with') to Yun's heart. This is the channel of hearts for those in love.
"Who is Mr. Yi that he would do so much?"
Without even doubting once, his straightforward heart, trusting simply because Yun trusted, must have felt more secure to Yun than a millionaire.<134>

Yun left the school, arranged a house, and then began visiting Mr. Yi's house under OO to discuss business matters. However, for some reason, Mr. Yi deliberately avoided meeting Yun during the day and preferred to meet at night.
Yun: "Please be there during the day tomorrow."
Yi: "Well, I have business at various companies during the day, what can I do? Tomorrow night, I will definitely be back by nine o'clock sharp, so please come then. I'll have the money ready and will surely give it to you."
Yun: "Yes, then I will definitely trust you."
Buying a piano, enlarging the house, preparing for concerts – it required a large sum of money. Thus, an initial 5,000 won was to be given by Yi to Yun the next night.
The following night—
At 9 o'clock sharp, Yun went to Yi's house. Tonight, strangely, there was no trace of the usual bustling visitors, and the lights in the reception room were off. It was the quietest night since she had started visiting this house.
"Is the master in?"
She asked. A housemaid greeted her.
"Yes, the master is unwell and lying in his bedroom. He asked that you be shown in immediately upon arrival."
Yun went straight to his bedroom. It was deep autumn. Curtains (카-덴) were drawn tightly on all sides, the faint light from a red-shaded stand (스탠트) dimly illuminated the gloomy surroundings of the bed, and the perfume scent wafting from all directions was more inviting than a spring breeze. Lying wrapped in a feather duvet, Yi's face was obscured by the dim light, making it impossible to tell if he was sick or well. The maid left, and Yun, feeling inexplicably uneasy, couldn't bring herself to sit, standing stiffly by the door.
Yi: "Please sit down."
His voice, though strained, was louder than that of a sick person, and it trembled.
Yun: "Where are you feeling unwell?"
Yi: "... "
In any case, that night in that room, there were sounds of scolding, sounds of weeping, and sounds of knocking on the door. But in a Western-style house built high up at the edge of OO, with the door closed, curtains drawn, and the entrance door locked from the outside by the maid who had led her in, she was a rat in a trap. Shouting and crying were useless.

With what face could she meet Kim in Tokyo, and how could she face the world? Yun finally began to despair. Fine, be Yi's mistress! Let's just live extravagantly now. Art, hope, honor – all buried in a single night's dream. But the world was not ready to dismiss the matter so simply.
"They say Yun became Yi's mistress."
"Yi is lecherous."
"Yun is a fool."<135>
When these words spread, a letter breaking off the relationship arrived from Tokyo. Though she expected it, that night Yun drank alcohol she couldn't handle and confronted Yi, weeping and throwing a tantrum. The next day, Yun obtained final travel expenses, turned her back on her homeland, and left for Harbin in the north, embarking on a path of wandering. A year passed. By now, the rumors must have subsided. Missing her older sister and younger siblings, she suddenly returned to Kyŏngsŏng. Upon her return, she immediately joined the T'owŏrhoe as an actress. The person who sent the flower bouquet on her debut stage was none other than the man who had ruined her life, Yi.
Life as a T'owŏrhoe actress couldn't soothe her ailing heart. Kim Ujin, who had come from Tokyo specifically to ascertain Yun Simdŏk's true situation, met her several times. During these meetings, Kim finally came to acknowledge Yun's ordeal.
"Look, it was just a disgrace suffered under violence from someone you didn't care about. Why make such a fuss?"
Kim's words were clear and bright. Thus, the love between him and Kim, which had temporarily cooled, was rekindled. It was as if Yun's love was fueled by gratitude, and Kim's love was enhanced by the gunpowder of compassion.
"Let's die. Let's leave this troublesome world behind!"
From whose lips did such words emerge?
"Yes, that's good. How should we die?"
Such was the reply. And so, the decision for the two to commit suicide together was already made in Room 1, 3rd floor of Yun's apartment in Suŭn-dong.

Yun composed and sang a piece titled "Sae Ch'anmi" (The Praise of Death; 死의 讚美) for Nitto Chōsen Records. To record this piece, she went to Osaka. On her return journey, she met Kim Ujin at Shimonoseki, boarded the ship *Tokujumaru* (德壽丸), and while crossing the Genkai Sea (玄海), they finally left behind the troublesome and painful world and departed on the path to heaven.

Sae Ch'anmi (The Praise of Death)

1. Oh life running in the vast wilderness
Where is your destination?
Lonely world, harsh sea of suffering
What are you trying to find?

2. In this world made of tears
If I die, it will all be over
Oh lives seeking happiness
What you seek is suffering

3. The smiling flower and the crying bird
Their fate is the same
Pitiful life engrossed in living
You are one dancing on a knife's edge

4. Oh life leaping about, lost in vanity<136>
Do you know you have been deceived?
Fundamentally, the world is emptiness to you
After you die, the world does not exist

5. Living well, living poorly, are fleeting moments
The menacing reefs are drawing near
This way or that way, it's still one life
Money, fame, even my beloved, I detest them all

6. The more I live, the more painful; the further I go, the harsher
Only the wind brings the peace of death
When I hide this body from the world
Pain and bitterness will vanish

Some say Yun and Kim are alive in Italy. But that is an unbelievable story. Given the investigation by the ship's captain at the time and the announcement by the Water Police, it must be an unbelievable, albeit welcome, rumor.<137>

**Summary**

This 1935 article recounts the tragic life story of Yun Simdŏk, Korea's first professional female soprano. It details her promising beginnings, her career aspirations, the devastating incident where she was allegedly assaulted by a wealthy patron named Yi Yŏngmun (pseudonym) which led to social ruin and the temporary break-up with her lover Kim Ujin. It describes her return to Korea, brief stint as an actress, reconciliation with Kim Ujin, and their eventual joint suicide by jumping off a ferry crossing the Genkai Sea after she recorded her famous song "Sae Ch'anmi" (The Praise of Death), the lyrics of which are included. From a historical perspective, the article is significant for its portrayal of the challenges faced by 'New Women' in colonial Korean society, caught between modern ambitions and traditional constraints. It offers a contemporary, albeit potentially biased, account of the pressures, exploitation (particularly by wealthy men), and lack of agency that could lead such figures to despair, reflecting the social anxieties and gender dynamics of the era.
