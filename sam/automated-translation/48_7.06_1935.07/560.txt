Regarding Parasites That Harm Children's Nutrition
						By Sŏk Yi-gyŏng

"Children are the treasure of a family and also the treasure of a nation." The future master of the household is the child, and the future master of the nation is also the child; truly, they are the treasure of the family and the treasure of the nation. Fortunately, the birth rate of children in our country increases every year, but recent statistics show that the child mortality rate in our country is extremely high, which is truly lamentable for the future of the nation. No matter how many children there are, if many perish half-developed or disabled, it will have no beneficial effect; without healthy children, there will be no benefit for the family or the nation. They grow up to become individuals who contribute labor to society. Comparing them to timber, they become logs (丸太), ridgepoles (棟木), or foundations, thereby becoming useful materials. However, if a seedling (苗木) is eaten by worms and becomes bent, even if that seedling grows large, its timber cannot be used.

Similarly, humans also suffer much harm from worm infestations (虫蝕). Looking at the children in our homes today, many children have worm infestations, just like peach trees or chestnut trees have worm infestations (虫蝕). Originally, when a child lacked energy or had abdominal pain (腸痛), it was attributed to worms (충). Also, common people say things like 'everyone has worms in their belly' or 'using roundworm medicine (蛔虫藥) and forcefully expelling roundworms is harmful to the body'—all implying that everyone has roundworms. However, what medical experts refer to as a child with a worm infestation (虫蝕) means living organisms, or 'worms' (버러지), that invade the human body from the outside and cause harm. There are various types of worms that invade the human body and cause harm; there are worms easily visible to the naked eye and worms invisible to the naked eye. I wish to speak about the worms that parasitize the intestines and harm children, which can be identified by examining stool.

Not only does infestation by worms, i.e., parasites (寄生虫), during childhood harm physical development, but it also causes significant impairment in children's mental development (精神上發育), leading to incomplete academic progress.

The worms that parasitize children's intestines and cause harm do not arise spontaneously within the intestines but invariably invade the human body from the outside. They then mature within the intestines, produce an extremely large number of eggs (卵), and these eggs are passed out with feces (糞便), allowing them to invade again<252>. Since there is no need to explain where parasites develop or through what routes they enter the human body, I wish to speak about common parasites and a few specific types.

1. Roundworm (蛔虫) = This worm is well-known to everyone, a white, large worm about 5 or 6 *ch'on* [approx. 15-18 cm] long, especially common in children. When these roundworms are in the intestines, they not only take nutrients from the human body but also secrete a type of toxin, causing children to experience sudden abdominal pain, fever, vomiting, and sometimes hives (두두럭이). Children can become irritable, experiencing dizziness (眩暈), acute convulsions (急癎), or seizures (癎), and in severe cases (*度), children may die. The reason these roundworms develop in the intestines is that the roundworms residing there produce numerous eggs (卵), which are expelled with feces. In moist soil during summer, within 20 days, small larvae (仔虫) develop inside the eggs. When these eggs are ingested with food or vegetables, they enter the intestines, shed their shells, and larvae (幼虫) emerge. These do not immediately grow into adult worms (親虫) in the intestines; instead, they first penetrate the intestinal wall, enter the bloodstream, travel to the lungs (肺臟), grow a bit there, then enter the trachea (기관), return to the intestines, and become adult worms. Therefore, infection with roundworms can also affect the lungs, sometimes causing children to develop pneumonia (폐렴). Furthermore, as roundworms grow in the intestines and consume the nutrients ingested by the person, the person becomes malnourished, fails to gain weight, and suffers from frequent abdominal pain and severe vomiting.

2. Hookworm (十二指膓虫) = This worm is smaller than the roundworm, about 4 or 5 *pun* [approx. 1.2-1.5 cm] long, and thin. This worm parasitizes the upper part of the small intestine (小腸), pierces the intestinal wall to absorb nutrients, causes bleeding, and also releases a type of toxin. Therefore, children infected with this worm become anemic (빈혈), their development is poor, their skeletons do not harden completely, and in severe cases, they develop a bluish complexion (靑氣), their fingernails change, they have diarrhea mixed with blood, their appetite changes, causing them to eat things like wall plaster (壁土) or other strange objects. Because they contract hookworm in childhood, their physical and mental development is incomplete upon reaching adulthood. Hookworms, like roundworms, produce numerous eggs (卵), but their eggs are expelled with feces into the environment, gradually develop, and hatch into small larvae (幼虫) after a few days. These larvae grow in fields (田地) and on vegetables. If they come into contact with skin (皮膚), they can enter the human body through it, or they can invade the body with vegetable matter. Like roundworms, the larvae first enter the bloodstream, go from the lungs (폐장) to the lungs [Note: repetition in original, likely meant bloodstream -> lungs -> trachea -> throat], pass through the trachea (기관) to the larynx (喉頭), are swallowed (嚥下), and then spread into the lower intestines (下腸內) to become adult worms (混虫) [Note: likely meant 親虫 - adult worm].

3. Pinworm (蟯虫) = This worm is white like the hookworm, about 3 or 4 *pun* [approx. 0.9-1.2 cm] long, and mostly resides in the lower part of the small intestine. When it's time to lay eggs (卵), the worm curiously crawls out of the anus (肛門) and generally lays eggs on the surrounding skin (皮膚). Because of this trait, [text missing/corrupted: likely describing anal itching (症)]... children cannot [text missing/corrupted: likely 'sleep' or 'endure it'], becoming irritable. These worms are frequently passed out in large numbers with feces, spreading onto fields (田地) and vegetables, and then re-enter<253> the body through the mouth (口中) with food, eventually becoming adult worms (强虫) [Note: likely meant 親虫 - adult worm] in the intestines.

4. Whipworm (鞭虫) = This worm is also white and thin like the hookworm, about 4 or 5 *pun* [approx. 1.2-1.5 cm] long, a small worm that parasitizes the lower part of the small intestine and the cecal region (맹장부위). It attaches to the intestinal wall and feeds by sucking blood (흡혈), causing children to become anemic (빈혈), develop a bluish complexion (청색), and gradually weaken, though not as severely as with hookworm. The eggs (卵) of this worm exit the body and, in water (水中) or very moist places (濕氣), develop into larvae (仔虫) within the egg. They then enter the intestines along with food and become adult worms (混虫) [Note: likely meant 親虫 - adult worm]. To prevent these fearsome parasites!
1. Do not eat unclean food or food contaminated by flies (蠅).
2. Do not eat raw vegetables.

As described above, these frightening and dreadful worms parasitize the intestines and constantly shed eggs (卵) in the stool. The presence and type of parasites can be determined from these eggs. To know the presence or absence of these parasite eggs (寄生虫卵), a stool examination is necessary. Since we cannot see parasite eggs with our naked eye, examining the stool with a microscope (현미경) can clearly reveal the presence or absence of eggs and their type. Therefore, I believe it is extremely necessary to have children's stool examined. If the examination confirms the presence of parasite eggs, I hope that appropriate deworming medicine (驅虫藥) for each type of worm will be used so that children can become strong and healthy.<254>

**Summary**

This 1935 article by Sŏk Yi-gyŏng emphasizes the severe impact of intestinal parasites (roundworm, hookworm, pinworm, whipworm) on children's physical and mental development, nutrition, and even survival in Korea. It explains the life cycles of these common parasites, detailing how they are contracted (often through contaminated food, vegetables, or soil) and the specific health problems they cause, including malnutrition, anemia, abdominal pain, and developmental delays. The author strongly advocates for preventative measures like food hygiene and avoiding raw vegetables, and crucially promotes modern medical diagnosis through microscopic stool examination to identify infections, followed by treatment with appropriate deworming medications, moving away from traditional assumptions about worms.

From a historical perspective, the article highlights pressing public health concerns in colonial Korea, particularly high child mortality rates and widespread parasitic infections. It reflects an effort to disseminate modern scientific knowledge about disease transmission and treatment to the public, contrasting scientific methods (microscopy, targeted medication) with folk beliefs. The linkage of children's health to the nation's future ("treasure of a nation") was a common trope in nationalist discourse of the era, framing public health as vital for national strength and progress, a point of interest for historians studying colonial modernity, public health campaigns, and nationalism in Korea.

이와 맛천가지로 사람에게도 虫蝕의 해독을 만이 받는대 現今 우리 가정의 어린이를 보면 복송아나무 밤나무에 虫蝕이 잇는 것과 갓치 虫蝕이 어린이가 만이 잇다. 원래 虫이라고 함은 자고로 소아가 기운이 좃치 못하든지 혹은 腸痛잇으면 충으로 因함이라고 하얏다. 그리고 보통사람들이 말하기를 사람에 배속에는 누구나 虫이 잇다든가 혹은 蛔虫藥을 사용하여, 엉지 회충이 나오면 신체에 해롭다는 등, 이모도가다- 사람에게는 회충이 잇다하는 말이다. 그러나 의학자들의 말하는 虫蝕의 소아라는 것은 버러지」가 외계로부터 인체에 침입하야 害하는 생물을 말하는 것인대 인체에 침입하야 해하는 충도, 여러종류가 잇서서, 육안으로 용이히 볼 수 잇는 버리지와 또는 육안으로 볼 수 없는 버러지가 잇으니 대변을 검사하야서 腸內에 기생하여서 어린이를 해하는 虫에 대하야 말하고저 한다.
소아시에 虫卽寄生虫의 침해로 인하야 신체의 발육을 해할뿐 안이라, 어린이의 精神上發育의 막대한 고장이 생김으로 인하야 학업상 발달도 완전치 못하게 되는 것이다.
어린이의 腸內에 기생해서 해를 주는 충은 장내에서 자연히 발생함은 안이고 반다시 외계로부터 인체내에 침입하야 그리고 腸內에서 성숙하야 비상히 다수의 卵을 生하고 其 卵이 糞便과 갓치 침입하기도<252> 한다. 기생충이 何處로부터 발육하며 엇떤 경로를 통하야 인체내에 들어옴을 말할 필요가 없음으로, 보통적 기생충과 몃가지에 대하야 말 하고저 한다.

상술한 바와 갓치 무섭고 꿈직한 버러지가, 장내에 기생하여 대변 중에 늘 卵을 배출하는 것인대, 此卵으로 기생충의 유무와 종류을 알 수가 잇는대 此寄生虫卵의 유무을 알나면 대변검사가 필요하다. 우리들의 육안으로는 기생충란을 볼 수 없음으로 대변을 현미경으로 검사하면 卵의 유무며 종류를 명확히 알 수가 잇음도 반다시 어린이의 대변검사를 하여줌이 대단히 필요하다고 생각한다. 검사의 결과 기생충란의 증명이 확실하면 各虫에 대한 驅虫藥을 사용하여 튼튼한 어린이들이 되기를 바라는 바아다.<254>
