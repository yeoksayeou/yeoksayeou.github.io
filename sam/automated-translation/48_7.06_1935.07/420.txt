Magazine Title: 三千里 제7권 제6호
Publication Date: 1935년 07월 01일
Article Title: 사람의 心臟을 (Regarding the Human Heart)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Don't people in love talk about the "heart" (하-트) this and that? It refers to the heart (심장), which they call "heart" (하-트).

If you take a heart out of the human body, does it die right away? Not so. According to the research of Dr. "Kuburayu" (구불아유) of Russia (露西亞), it is said to live for 30 hours.<210>

**Summary**

This short piece reflects on the common term "heart" used in romance and contrasts it with a scientific claim. It reports that according to research by a Russian doctor named "Kuburayu," a human heart can survive outside the body for up to 30 hours. From a historical perspective, the article shows the dissemination of (potentially simplified or sensationalized) Western scientific news, specifically from Soviet Russia, into colonial Korea. The use of mixed script (Hanja and Hangul), older spellings like "그러치안코" and "함니다," and the transliteration of foreign terms like "하-트" (heart) are typical linguistic features of the period, making it interesting for historical linguistics and studies of cultural reception.
