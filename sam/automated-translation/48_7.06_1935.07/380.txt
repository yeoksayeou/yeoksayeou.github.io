**Body Text Translation:**
Actress Whose Salary is More Than the President's Monthly Salary

In the United States (美國), those who know are aware that a movie actor's salary is several tens of times greater than the president's monthly salary.

Greta Garbo (구레타, 칼보): 18,000 Wŏn per week
Will Rogers (월, 롯쟈스): 15,000 Wŏn per week
Maurice Chevalier (슈-바리에): 15,000 Wŏn per week
Bennett (베넷트): 14,000 Wŏn per week
John Barrymore (쫀, 바리모아): 13,000 Wŏn per week
Norma Shearer (노-마, 쇨라): 12,000 Wŏn per week
Ppa-, Selmelsŭ (빠-, 셀멜스): 12,000 Wŏn per week
Harding (하-딩그): 12,000 Wŏn per week
Ppiari (삐아리): 10,000 Wŏn per week
<200>

**Summary**

This short piece from 1935 highlights the extraordinarily high weekly salaries of top American film stars, noting they earn many times more than the US President's monthly salary. It lists nine actors, including Greta Garbo, Will Rogers, and Maurice Chevalier, along with their weekly earnings in Won (presumably Korean Yen). From a colonial history perspective, the article reveals Korean interest in and awareness of American popular culture and its economic scale. The comparison with the US President's salary and the listing of specific, large sums in the local currency would have emphasized the immense wealth disparity and the allure of Hollywood, potentially offering a stark contrast to the economic realities in colonial Korea.
