**Body Text Translation:**
The Academic World, Prospering Day by Day
						Yi Sukchong

The capital city of my old school days! It had been three years since I last saw Tōkyō, which I think of with much fondness and ardent feeling, when I set out to see Tōkyō again at the end of last March. It was a trip taken wondering how much Tōkyō had changed, having heard rumors that it became newer year after year, even though I had seen it several times since graduation. Though I believed it wasn't too far away and the journey not unfamiliar, setting out after several years felt quite tedious and tiring. The blue waves of the Hyŏnhaet'an (Genkai Sea) met the sky as the day ended, and being violently shaken on the tedious ferry, I felt extreme fatigue and realized it was not an easy long journey. Outside the window, lush forests in vast plains stretching for thousands of *ri* and the rich array of fields and plains seemed to melt in self-praise and ease; green forests and golden fields stretched endlessly. Inside the window, from every corner, discussions were bustling about Shinkyō! Manchuria! – topic after topic, person after person. Immersed in various thoughts from seeing and hearing things, I reached Tōkyō before I knew it. I visited old teachers, saw exhibitions, observed schools, and also toured Tōkyō. I saw a Tōkyō where brilliance! splendor, abundance, prosperity, progress, and various other things intersected. Culture (文物)! People's standing (人位)! Everything – the Tōkyō I saw had changed so much. Education had also become Westernized (洋風化), and the people, material things, lifestyle systems, and even human feelings – everything had become Westernized. The Tōkyō we once knew, the Tōkyō we saw, was the capital and the academic capital (學都) of ten years ago. However, today's bustling Tōkyō seemed made of extreme luxury (奢偖) and pleasure-seeking (逸樂). It felt like a Tōkyō that had lost its inherent character, lost its local flavor (卿土風 / 郷土風). One scholar remarked that Europe lags behind, while Tōkyō becomes newer, calling it a "New Europe" <192> and praising it as convenient because the language is fluent [presumably for the Japanese] and better because it's closer – that's how much it has developed. However, I longed for the distinct flavor of Tōkyō as Tōkyō. I thought it would be good if it retained some flavor of the Edo Castle site (江戶城趾). And then, the grand buildings (삘틩), theaters, splendidly flashy department stores, dance halls (딴쓰장), the Western clothing (洋裝) of gentlemen and ladies! And their makeup (化粧) – a charming yet bizarre modern scene. Brilliant and dazzling Tōkyō felt very bewildering and also unsettling, but one cannot help but be amazed by the Tōkyō that has become splendid, become Greater Tōkyō, the Tōkyō of the academic center (學府), the Capital Tōkyō (都東京) – its large-scale organization and extreme development, driven by grand spirit (浩氣) and unity (結束), knowing no bounds in its direction and progress. Their determined resolve, effort, and discipline (團束) were deeply enviable. Seeing the school institutions, I felt even more envious. However, consoling myself (自慰) with the thought that "it's just the trend of the times (從時屬)," implying a return to diligent training and self-cultivation (精勵修煉) in a three-*kan* thatched cottage, returning to substance (質實), I turned my steps back to my lodging, bundled my travel gear (行具), and set off on the return journey (同程). <193>

**Summary**

Yi Sukchong describes her visit to Tōkyō after a three-year absence in 1935. She notes the city's immense development, modernization, and Westernization in architecture, lifestyle, and even education, comparing it unfavorably to the Tōkyō she knew a decade earlier, feeling it has lost its traditional character. While overwhelmed and somewhat unsettled by the scale, luxury, and perceived superficiality, she expresses admiration for the Japanese people's spirit, unity, and relentless drive for progress, particularly evident in their organization and educational institutions. The article reflects a Korean intellectual's complex reaction to Japan's imperial power and modernity during the colonial period. Historians might find interesting the explicit mention of "Shinkyō! Manchuria!" as common topics of conversation, reflecting the atmosphere of Japanese expansionism, the author's ambivalence towards Westernized modernity (seeing it as both impressive and a loss), and the subtle expression of longing for simpler "substance" perhaps hinting at a desire for cultural preservation or alternative path for Korea.

녯 學窓에 都邑! 퍽 반갑고 熱達히 생각되는 東京을 본지 3년 만에 지난 3월말에 다시 東京구경을 떠낫섯다. 졸업 후 몃 번 본 東京이나 年年이 새롭다는 소문이 엇더케나 되엿나 하고 떠난 길이다. 그다지 먼 곳이 아니오 또 생소한 노정이 아닌 줄 밋든 길이나 몃 해만에 나서 서보니 퍽 지리하고 괴로웟다. 현해탄에 푸른 파도가 하날과 맛단 채로 하로를 맛치고 지리한 차에 몹시 흔들니어 극도에 피로를 感할 때 수헐치 아닌 원정인 것을 알엇다. 수천리 평야에 창창한 鬱林이며 田野에 풍부한 배열이 自誇과 安逸에 숨 녹이는 듯 窓外에는 綠林과 金田이 끈닐 줄 몰으고 窓內에는 이 구석구석에서 新京! 滿洲! 화제마다 사람마다 분분한 의론이다. 가지가지 보고 듯는 생각에 저즌 채로 어느 듯 東京을 다다럿다. 녯 선생님도 찻고 전람회도 보고 학교도 참관하고 또 東京 구경도 하엿다. 찬란! 화려 豊定 盛興 전진 이외 여러 가지가 교착된 東京을 구경하엿다. 문물! 人位! 다 - 내가 본 東京은 퍽이나 변하엿다. 교육도 洋風化하고 인물도 물질도 생활제도도 인정도 다 - 서양식화 하엿다. 녯 우리가 아든 東京 보든 東京은 10년 전 수도요 또 學都이엿다. 그러나 금일에 흥성겨운 東京은 극도에 奢偖와 逸樂으로 된 듯 하엿다. 고유한 東京의 자태를 일흔 즉 卿土風 일흔 東京갓핫다. 엇던 학자 한 분에 말이 신구라파라고 할만치 구라파는 뒤떠러지고 東京은 새로워지며<192> 또는 언어가 능통하니 편하고 갓가우니 더 좃타는 평을 할이만치 대발달은 되엇다. 그러나 東京이면 東京다운 풍미가 그리웟다. 江戶城趾에 풍미를 좀 남기고 나간다면 조흘이라고 생각하엿다. 그리고 웅대한 삘틩, 극장, 화려 야한 백화점, 딴쓰장, 남녀신사숙녀에 양장! 그리고 그들에 화장, 묘미하고도 기이한 현대상이다. 찬란코 황홀한 東京이 몹시도 얼떨떨하고 또 불안하엿다마는 화려한 東京이 되고 大東京이 되고 學府에 東京 都東京 그 호기과 결속으로 갈바 긋칠 바를 모르는 대규모 조직 극극한 발전이 또 놀나지 안닐 수 업다. 그들에 맷친 결단 노력 단속이 퍽 부러웟다. 학교기관을 보고 또 한층 더 부러웟다. 그러나 從時 屬이라고 三間草屋에 精勵修煉하여 質實에 도라가게 함이라는 자위로 발길을 돌치어 숙소에 와서 行具를 몽치어 同程한 것이다<193>
