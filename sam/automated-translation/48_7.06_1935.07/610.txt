初夏隨筆, 꾀꼬리를 기다리다
						李無影

Has spring already gone? I suddenly have this thought.
Because, before you know it, the sound of frogs grows clamorous once the sun sets.
It seems the day the oriole sings from the willow branch by the lotus pond is not far off.
Spring, which leaves as soon as it arrives, is endlessly regrettable.
But I wait for spring to go. Because after spring goes, the bright yellow oriole comes to visit the willow branches, lushly flowing with deep green foliage.
"Kkwekkol! Kkwekkol- Kkwekkweggol"
As if telling me to get up already, I wish for the day to quickly come when it wakes me, calling towards the door of my room, not even two *kan* wide.
The green shade is lovely, but in the charmingly decorated garden, on the weeping willow accustomed to the blue-water lotus pond where goldfish play,
the sight of a desirably bright yellow oriole perched is also an early summer scene of the mountain villa, no less beautiful than the green shade.
And doesn't the oriole sing for us with its cheerful voice on a May morning?...
No matter how good the summer at the mountain villa is, if that bird were not there, how could it have been so delightful?...

May is called the month of joy. May is also said to be the spirit of the poet's hope. If so, what shall we call this bird that visits in May?
Lying flat on a blue grass mat, looking up at the May azure sky shining with hope is good... Lying on my stomach on the lawn and watching the goldfish in the lotus pond is also good...
Casting a fishing line into the lotus pond and dozing off wouldn't be strange either...
As the green shade deepens, the oriole's color will surely become more yellow.
The Golden Bird (黃金鳥), shining brilliantly amidst the dark green foliage, hindered by nothing, soaring high to its heart's content, singing beautifully to its heart's content—the oriole will surely come and sing again this summer... <281>
Ah, who is it that would buy and hang a birdcage in this garden, this mountain villa?...
(At the Mountain Villa)<282>

**Summary**

This lyrical essay by Yi Muyŏng expresses anticipation for the arrival of summer, specifically marked by the appearance and song of the oriole (꾀꼬리). The author finds spring fleeting and waits eagerly for the vibrant yellow bird whose presence enhances the beauty of his mountain villa (山莊), with its lotus pond and willow trees. He poetically reflects on the joy the oriole brings, associating it with the hope and beauty of May, and contrasting the freedom of the wild bird with the idea of captivity in a cage. For historians, the piece offers insight into the aesthetic sensibilities and connection to nature maintained by Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. The focus on apolitical, personal reflections on nature might represent a subtle form of cultural expression or escapism amidst the political realities of the time, showcasing a continuation of traditional appreciation for seasonal beauty.

봄도 갓나? 문득 이런 생각을 해본다.
어느듯, 해만지면 개고리소리가 요란해진 때문이다.
蓮塘앞 버들가지에서 꾀꼬리 울날도 머지 않은 듯.
오자마자 가버리는 봄이 한련 야속도하다.
그러나 나는 봄이 가기를 기다린다. 봄이가고 나면, 진초록 녹음이 칠칠흐르는 버들가지에 새노란 꾀꼬리가 찾아오기 때문이다.
「꾀꼴! 꾀꼴- 꾀꾀꼴」
그만 일어나라는 듯, 두 間통도 못되는 방문에다 대고 잠을 깨워주는 그날이 얼른 왔으면 한다.
녹음도 좋거니와 아담하기 꾸며논 정원, 金魚노는 碧水蓮塘에 길드린 수양버들에
탐스럽도록 새노란 꾀꼬리가 앉은 것도 녹음만 못지 않은 山莊의 初夏風景이다.
그러고도 꽤꼬리는 5월의 아츰 그 명랑한 목청으로 노래를 불러주지 않는가?...
산장의 여름이 좋다기로니, 저 새가 없었든들, 그 어찌 맛있었으라?...

5월을 일러서 기뿜이 달이라 한다. 5월은 시인은 희망의 넉이라고도 한다. 그럴진대 5월에 찾는 이새의 이름을 뭣이라 할꼬?
파아란 草席에 번듯이 누어 희망에 빛나는 5월의 碧空을 우러러 봄도 좋을시고... 잔디밭에 배를 깔고 蓮塘의 金魚의 구경도 좋을시고...
蓮塘에 낙시던지고 조을어 봄도 怪치 않으리...
綠陰이 지터갈스록 꽤꼬리 빛도 더 노라지리라.
검도록 푸른 綠陰叢中에 燦然히 빛나는 黃金鳥, 아무도 꺼리낌이라 마을껏 높이 마음껏 곱개 꽤꼬리는 올여름에도 찾아와 울어주리라... <281>
아, 이동산, 이 산장에 새장을 사걸란게 누구든고...
(山莊에서)<282>
