Intellectual Circles, Academia

Kim Yaksu's View on Fascist Trends

Mr. Kim Yaksu (金若水), who recently returned after staying in Tōkyō for about two months, spoke as follows about the intellectual circles in Tōkyō. Among his remarks, his observations regarding the ebb and flow of fascist power were particularly interesting.
"While in Tōkyō, I mainly stayed at the house of Takatsu Masamichi (高津正道) in Honkyō-ku (本鄕區). It happened to be the time when the Emperor of Manchukuo was visiting Tōkyō, so the Metropolitan Police Department's security was extremely strict. However, I was not subjected to preventive detention; instead, a uniformed police officer would come by once a day just to check on my movements and leave. Tōkyō has changed from before; the activist camps are clearly divided into legal and illegal factions, and there isn't such severe suppression against the legal activists. For example, during the visit of the Emperor of Manchukuo, it seemed most people in the illegal camp were preventively detained, but otherwise, they were just monitored, and detention wasn't carried out.

Based on the stories I heard from various people during my two-month stay in Tōkyō, as well as my own investigations and observations, it seemed that the fascist power there is unlikely to advance further and that the forces of liberalism—or parliamentarism—are gradually regaining ground. Generally, fascist power seemed to have reached its peak for a time after the Manchurian Incident, through the May 15 Incident. However, since General Araki (荒木) stepped down as Minister of the Army and General Hayashi (林) took office – and with the successful<26> purchase of the North Manchurian Railway (北滿鐵道) and the temporary easing of the danger of war in the Far East, which had been seen as critical after the Manchurian Incident, etc. – due to various domestic and international circumstances (諸情勢), fascist power shows a tendency to gradually decline. Another factor is that there is a tendency among the elder statesmen (元老重臣) to dislike fascist politics, a theory suggests this is largely influenced by the example of Italy (伊太利). There was also a rumor that the large financial cliques (大財閥) domestically oppose fascism... ... etcetera."

Another New Rumor, The Million Won Donation Theory

Recently, a tremendous rumor is circulating aimlessly in Seoul like the wind. Investigating the source of the rumor doesn't reveal much, nor does probing its certainty. However, because it's a rumor being spread so plausibly, I write it down as heard:
"Mr. OOO, a wealthy man from P'yŏngan Province (平安道), who supposedly earned three million won from gold mining yet hasn't spent significant money on cultural projects for Korean society, keeping the money only in his safe (金꼬), has decided, taking the opportunity of Mr. OOO's emergence, to donate one million won for industrial or educational projects, entrusting the aforementioned Mr. OOO to carry out the work." Etc.

"Street Newspaper" Emergence Theory

In Korea, where there are already four newspapers—Tong'a (東亞), Chosŏn (朝鮮), Chungang (中央), Maeil Sinbo (每申)—and the newspaper market seems saturated these days, there is a rumor that another newspaper is about to emerge. The source of this rumor is also unknown, as is its certainty, but it is nonetheless a rumor circulating vigorously among knowledgeable people, so I introduce it as follows:
"The newspaper coming out this time will differ in format (체재) and content from the existing four papers. How will it do so? It will be a kind of 'street newspaper,' priced at around 50 chŏn per month, and its content will be news-oriented (뉴-쓰 본위). For example: 'This morning, twin daughters were born at someone's house in such-and-such neighborhood in Seoul,' 'A dog bit a female student's leg at the An'guk-dong (안국동) intersection,' 'A fire broke out in the kitchen of someone's house in Chongno (종로) 3-chōme, with damages amounting to 1 wŏn 76 chŏn,' etc., publishing very small news items directly related (즉한) to daily life. So who is planning this newspaper? There's a theory that it's Mr. OOO, a young Korean businessman renowned today like an ever-victorious general, succeeding in one venture after another, his name shining. However, as mentioned earlier, its truth or falsehood (眞否) is unknown."

Memoir of a Defeated General

Thirty years after the Russo-Japanese War, the memoir (回顧錄) of the Russian Army Commander-in-Chief, General Kuropatkin (쿠로포토킨), has been made public.<27> In a passage written by the general himself in the book, there is this section:
"Many elite (精銳) officers were gathered (叅集) in the army, but society was extremely indifferent to the outbreak of war in the Far East. Hundreds of commoners volunteered for military service themselves, but the sons of the upper class, such as nobles, merchants, and scholars, did not desire (慾치 안엇다) it. Among our tens of thousands of students, excluding medical students, only a few volunteered for military service. Russia was not only indifferent to the army, but numerous revolutionaries diligently pursued tactics for our defeat, attempting thereby to achieve their own conspiracies."
From this, one can understand that the sentiment of the Russian court and country (朝野) at the time was fragmented (四分五裂).

Chosŏn Ilbo Gathers Five Presidents

The Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) company recently brought in Mr. Minse An Chaehong (民世 安在鴻) and Mr. Ch'unwŏn Yi Kwangsu (春園 李光洙) as company guests (社賓格). Minse is contributing "Minse P'iltam" (Minse's Talk), and Ch'unwŏn is contributing "Ilsa Ilŏn" (One Matter, One Word) and the novel "Kŭ Yŏja ŭi Ilsaeng" (That Woman's Life), showcasing their unique literary styles daily in the paper. In any case, this brings the Chosŏn Ilbo company to house one current president, three former presidents, and one former vice-president. That is, the current president Mr. Pang Ŭngmo (方應謨); former presidents currently serving as directors (取締役) of the company, Mr. Cho Mansik (曹晩植) and Mr. An Chaehong (安在鴻), who was also a former Chosŏn Ilbo president; Mr. Pyŏkch'o Hong Myŏnghŭi (碧初 洪命熹), author of Im Kkŏkjŏng Chŏn and former president of the Sidae Ilbo (時代日報); and at the vice-president level, Mr. Ch'unwŏn Yi Kwangsu (春園 李光洙). Thus, historical giants from the arena of journalism (筆政壇上) have coincidentally gathered under one roof (一堂一傘下).

Rumor of Several Ten Thousand Won Investment and New Power at Chungang Ilbo

A major change has occurred at the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報). Vice-president Ch'oe Sŏnik (崔善益), Business Director Hong Chŭngsik (洪增植), and Sales Manager Pak Ton'sŏ (朴敦緖), three key executives, have resigned. Replacing them are Mr. Kang Wijŏng (姜偉情) as Vice-president, Mr. O Kiju (吳基周) as Business Director, Mr. Han Changsŏl (韓章卨) as Assistant Accounting Director and Head of Accounting, and Mr. Ch'oe Sŏkha (崔*河) as Assistant Business Director and Head of Sales. The reason for such a large reshuffling of executive-level staff, happening like lightning (疾風迅雷的) without even the citizens of the capital knowing, and the structure of the new financial group (新財團), are reported internally as follows: Essentially, although Chungang Ilbo completed its establishment as a stock company (株式會社) by this spring, old investments were counted as new shares (新株), and due to various other factors, there was very little cash investment. The stock company existed in name only, and in reality, shortly after its establishment, it was already in a position where it couldn't properly pay employee salaries. Consequently, the executives anxiously engaged in fundraising (金策), resulting in Mr. Kang's joining the company this time. According to rumors, the new financial group will invest several tens of thousands of won. As for the leader (盟主) of the new financial group, Mr. Kang Wijŏng, who is he? He is a young intellectual in his late 20s,<28> 27 or 28 this year. His father in Chinnanp'o (鎭南浦) ran Samhwa-cho (三和組), a transport labor group (運輸勞働單體) and local products dealership (物産商), becoming a self-made (自手) man worth about 300,000 won in his time. Mr. Kang Wijŏng attended an economics department at a university in Tōkyō. Mr. O Kiju, who was the Chinnanp'o branch manager, and Mr. An Pyŏngju (安炳珠) from Anju (安州) in South P'yŏngan (平南), are both his friends. They joined the company several months ago as Mr. Kang's advance guard (前哨) and have now respectively occupied key (樞要) positions. Furthermore, word is that with this reshuffle, an application is being processed to change the editor and publisher (編輯兼 發行人), which was Ch'oe Sŏnik, to the name of President Yŏ Unhyŏng (呂運亨). The resigned Messrs. Ch'oe Sŏnik and Hong Chŭngsik are said to retain their directorships. It is also predicted that there will be changes in the staff composition due to the entry of this new financial group. (Photo is of Mr. Kang)

OO Newspaper Twelve-Page Increase

Currently, the private newspapers are nearly evenly matched (伯仲) in influence, fiercely waging a battle for supremacy (爭覇戰) through either financial power or human resources. A recent fact, heard as a secret within secrets (秘中의 又秘事), is that a certain major newspaper is secretly preparing to increase its current 10 pages to 11 pages. Sensing this move, another paper is also rumored to be planning a twelve-page increase (亦十二頁 增頁說), suggesting that ultimately three newspapers might soon all become 12-page papers. It is said to be a matter of note which newspaper will first make the bold decision and execute it (勇斷實行).

Novelist Likely to Become President

American writer Sinclair (싱글레아) previously ran in the California gubernatorial election and gained significant popularity but suffered a narrow defeat (惜敗) to the opposing party. Afterwards, Mr. Sinclair emerged with the new political platform (政綱) of the "EPIC plan" and is now active throughout the United States. It seems he intends to run in the next presidential election and is considered very promising.

Financial World, Business World

Kim Sŏngsu, Kim Yŏnsu's Ten Thousand Won

Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik Hoesa (京城紡績會社), the largest textile company managed by Korean hands, located in Yŏngdŭngp'o (永登浦) outside Seoul, which previously had a fully paid-up capital (全額拂込資本) of one million won, has recently increased its capital (增資) by another two million won, creating a large company with a total capital of three million won. Of the new two million won shares, the company's major shareholders, Messrs. Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) and Kim Yŏnsu (金年洙), subscribed (申込) to 2,000 shares. Since each share is fifty won, they invested one hundred thousand won. Furthermore, the company is reportedly proceeding with major expansion construction as indicated on the left page.

Sudden Wealth of 200,000 Won (一躍 20萬圓의 卒富)

Recently, a dream-like event has occurred in Seoul, startling people. Namely, an old man over sixty, identified as Mr. X living in Chongno (鍾路) 2-chōme, Kyŏngsŏng, an illiterate ignoramus (無識쟁이) unable to write a single character, happened to buy a mine for 350 won last year that was auctioned off by the Hoengsŏng County Office (橫城郡廳) in Kangwŏn Province (江原道) for unpaid taxes. Pooling all his household assets, he began digging. Late last year, he finally discovered a good mineral vein (鑛脈). A buyer appeared, and he sold it for 200,000 won, becoming rich overnight (一躍富者). Then, the person who bought it for 200,000 won discovered yet another vein, and recently, even when offered 3 million won, ten times the 200,000 won, he reportedly refused to sell.

Reception for Minse and Ch'unwŏn

It is reported that the Chosŏn Ilbo company provides remuneration of 150 wŏn per month to Mr. Minse An Chaehong, who joined as a company guest, and a similar amount to Mr. Ch'unwŏn Yi Kwangsu.

First-Class Aviator Sin Yongmuk's Aerial Wedding

Mr. Sin Yongmuk (愼鏞ꝯ), thirty-five this year, had been living as a lonely widower after his wife passed away several years ago. Recently, he formed a marital tie (侍緣) with Miss Ch'oe Kyesun (崔頁順) of Ch'angsin-dong (昌信洞), Seoul. On May 5th, they held a grand aerial wedding ceremony (空中結婚) aboard three passenger planes flying over Kyŏngsŏng Airfield, riding separately (分乘).
Afterwards, the couple reportedly established their home (쓰잇홈) in a Western-style two-story cultural house at 11, 7-chōme, Misakichō (罔崎町).

Rumored Relocation of Sŏn'il Paper Goods (鮮一紙物 移轉說)

Something that always attracts public interest is the fate of the former Chongno Police Station site. After the Chongno station moved to the former courthouse location, the site remained vacant for a while. Then, after the Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店) was destroyed by fire, it moved its temporary store to this building this spring, as previously reported. According to recent news, this building is likely to change hands once again soon. That is, when the construction (築) of the former Hwasin Department Store building, owned by Mr. Min Kyusik (閔奎植), is completed this autumn in September, Hwasin will move there. It is reportedly internally decided that Sŏn'il Paper Goods Stock Company (鮮一紙物株式會社) will move into the current temporary store location, namely the former Chongno Police Station site.
Thus, it is said that paper (조히) will now occupy the site of the police station detention cells (유치장), which held political prisoners, thought criminals, and holds much history.

"Ch'ŏn'il" Invites 240 People

"Ch'ŏn'il Yakpang" (天一藥房), located in Yejidong (禮智洞), Seoul, invited 240 local merchants from all over Korea who sell their company's medicines to Seoul in late April. They provided services (써비-스) such as visiting the exposition and sightseeing in Seoul. The condition for the invitation was one invitation ticket sent per 100 units of Ch'ŏn'il Yakpang medicine purchased this time by a local seller. In effect, they sold at least 24,000 won worth of medicine at once and hosted 240 people at the Seoul headquarters.

Five Million Won at Kyŏngsŏng Station

These days, tourist groups and exposition visitors are flooding into Seoul, with their numbers reportedly not falling below 30,000 daily. In any case, the current crowd (雜踏) at Kyŏngsŏng Station (京城驛) is astonishing. Due to this busyness and the heavy traffic of people and horses, Kyŏngsŏng Station's revenue has increased significantly. Even in a normal year, it reaches 5.1 million won <31> annually. Therefore, it is observed that in a year like this with such good business conditions, it might exceed 6 million won. Seeing that the number of people getting on and off (乘降) trains during the past year was 3.76 million, it seems likely to exceed 4 million this year.

Dr. Chŏng's Hospital Undergoes Major Expansion

Dr. Chŏng Sŏkt'ae (鄭錫泰), formerly a German-trained medical doctor and an authority (權威) in internal medicine and pulmonary tuberculosis, recently undertook a major expansion of his hospital in Ch'ajach'ŏng (茶屋町). The expansion makes it about three times the size of the old building. The completion ceremony (落成式) was held grandly on April 27th, inviting over 100 guests from the capital, including various doctors from Keijō Imperial University.

Film World Secret Tidbits (映畵界秘聞片片)

Several welcome pieces of news are heard in the film world, which had been stagnant (波滯) for a long time.
First, Mr. Na Un'gyu (羅雲奎), the famed actor of "Arirang," who had been wandering and touring locally with theatrical performances (演劇巡廻) for a long time, recently came to Seoul. He is said to be currently preparing to film a new work, joining hands again with former colleagues like Mr. Yun Pongch'un (尹逢春) under the Yewŏnjwa (藝苑座) system.
Second, Mr. Pang Hanjun (方漢駿), who works as a director at Shōchiku Kinema (松竹키네마), is reported to be collaborating with Mr. Pak Sun'ok (朴淳玉), owner of P'yŏngyang Cheil Kwan (平壤 第一館), to establish a large film studio and simultaneously begin work on new productions.
Third, Mr. Yi Wŏnyong (李源鎔), who gained fame with the fine work (佳作) "Ch'ŏngch'un ŭi Sipchaga" (Crossroads of Youth), is reportedly preparing to film a new work and has commissioned Mr. Yi Sŏgu (李瑞求) for the original story.
Thus, it is expected that outstanding works will delight the eyes of movie fans this autumn.<32>

**Summary**

This "Samch'ŏlli Secret Room" column from July 1935 compiles various news snippets and rumors circulating in Korea, primarily Seoul, and Tokyo. Topics include observations on the decline of fascism in Japan by Kim Yaksu, rumors of large donations for Korean industry/education, speculation about a new tabloid-style newspaper, a historical note on Russian morale during the Russo-Japanese War, the gathering of prominent writers and former newspaper presidents at the Chosŏn Ilbo, major personnel and financial changes at the Chungang Ilbo, potential newspaper page increases, faculty lists from Seoul's professional schools (Severance Medical College featured), the political ambitions of Upton Sinclair, business news involving Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik and a mining fortune, broadcast call signs, an aviator's aerial wedding, property changes at the former Chongno Police Station site, business promotions, booming traffic at Kyŏngsŏng Station, a hospital expansion, and burgeoning activity in the Korean film industry. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable glimpses into the socio-political atmosphere of colonial Korea: observing Japanese political trends (fascism's perceived decline), the dynamics within the Korean press (personnel shifts, competition, financial struggles), the nascent Korean business world (textiles, mining), cultural developments (film), and everyday life details (rumors, social events, infrastructure). The commentary on Japanese fascism and the mention of preventive detention reflect the political anxieties and realities of the time.

金若水氏의 팟쇼趨勢觀

최근 약 두달동안이나 東京에가서 逗留하다가 일전에 도라나온 金若水氏는 東京 사상계에 대하야 아래와 가치 말하는데 그 중에도 팟쇼 勢力의 消長에 대하여 흥미잇는 관찰을 하고 잇섯다.
『東京으로 가서는 주로 本鄕區의 高津正道집에 잇섯는데 마츰 滿洲國皇帝가 東京에 오는 대목이되야 警視廳의 경계가 심히 엄중하엿스나, 나는 예비검속은 당하지 안코, 매일 한번씩 정복경관이 왓다가는 동정만 보고 가더라. 東京은 예전과 달너서 운동진영의 합법적과 비합법적으로 완연히 갈라저잇서 합법적 운동객에 대하여는 그리 심한 탄압은 하지 안터라. 예하면 滿洲國皇帝御來朝時에도 비합법적 진영에 잇는 사람은 대부분 예비검속된 모양이나 그러치 안으면 그양 감시만 하고, 검속까지는 이르지 안터라.

이번에 두달동안 내가 東京에 잇스면서 이사람 저사람의 이약이를 듯고 또 실지로 조사도 하고 목도한 여러가지 점으로 보아 지금 그 곳에는 팟쇼세력이 이 이상 더 나아갈 것 갓지안코 차츰 自由主義-또는 議會主義 勢力이 만회되어갈 것가치 보엿다. 대체로 팟쇼세력은 滿州事變 後 5.15사건을 거처 한동안 전성한 것 가치 보엿스나 荒木大將이 육군대신의 직에서 물너가고 林大將이 신임하면서부터 - 또 北滿鐵道의 매수가 그런데<26> 성립되고 滿州事變 後 위기에 잇다고 보아오든 극동에 전쟁의 위험이 일시 완화하여지는 등, 국내 국외의 諸情勢문에 팟쇼세력이 차츰차츰 꺽기어가는 경향이 잇스며  한가지 원로중신에서 팟쇼정치를 꺼리는 경향이 잇다하니 그것은 伊太利의 예를 보아 暗示밧든 점이 만하여 그런단 설이 잇고 그리고 국내에서도 大財閥들이 팟쇼를 반대한다는 설이 잇섯다... ...云云』

「이번 나오는 신문은 현재 잇는 네 신문과는 체재도 다르고 내용도 다르게 하는뎨 그것은 엇더케 하는가 하면 일종 「거리의 신문」이라 할 것으로 갑도 한달 50전 정도로 하고 내용도 뉴-쓰 본위로 하되 가령 「서울 어느 동 누구의 집에 오늘 아츰 딸 쌍동이 낫고」
「안국동 네 거리에서 개가 여학생의 다리를 물엇고」
「종로 삼정목 누구의 집 부억에서 불이 낫는데 손해 1원76전이라고」
等等 아조 일상생활에 즉한 小新聞을 내리라는 바 그러면 누가 이 신문 설계를 하고 잇는가 하면, 오늘날 조선사람 청년실업가로서 常勝將軍가치 이 사업 저 사업에 모다 성공하여 그 일홈이 빗나는 000氏라느 설이 잇다. 그러나 앗가 말한 모양으로 그 眞否는 모르겟다.』
