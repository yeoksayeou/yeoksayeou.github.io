Magazine Title: 삼천리 제7권 제6호
Publication Date: 1935년 07월 01일
Article Title: 朝鮮文化와 民衆과 新聞, 三大新聞編輯方針論 (Korean Culture, the Masses, and Newspapers: A Discussion on the Editorial Policies of the Three Major Newspapers)
Author: 薛義植(東亞日報編輯局長), 金炯元(朝鮮日報編輯局長), 金東成(朝鮮中央日報編輯局長)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Interview)

朝鮮文化와 民衆과 新聞, 三大新聞編輯方針論
東亞日報編輯局長 薛義植
朝鮮日報編輯局長 金炯元
朝鮮中央日報編輯局長 金東成

In our society today, the direct readership of the three major private newspapers exceeds 300,000, and the indirect readership under their influence likely numbers in the millions. What policies, then, do these three papers, possessing such absolute cultural power, follow in editing their newspapers? How do they intend to guide public opinion, and how do they intend to reflect the world? As cultural institutions belong to the "public," obtaining answers based on the following guidelines from the three leading figures wielding the pen that commands these "public institutions" and reporting them to the world is intended to lay an important "cornerstone" for the comprehensive advancement of our culture.
(Editor)<42>

Regarding Political Articles

A. Among the various political reports from around the world, what events or figures do you emphasize in your reporting? (For example, the progress of disarmament conferences, various special movements in outlying territories, your general policy approach)

Sŏl Ŭi-sik (Tonga Ilbo Editor-in-Chief)

As you can see from the political pages of the Tonga Ilbo, our newspaper strives to reflect the "focal point of the moving world political stage." Therefore, as the European political situation is currently eventful, we are dedicating all our efforts to conveying its fluctuations. Germany is one example. Yesterday, when the Chinese Red Army was sweeping through the Sichuan area like a storm, we focused our reporting on the battle between the Red and White forces in China. Four or five years ago, when Gandhi was active, we reported on British-Indian relations. The Manchurian Incident, the League of Nations, etc.—if any ripple affected the world political situation, we placed special emphasis on it. We strove not only to report the fluctuating temporal aspects but also to explain the causes and effects through commentary, news, and photos, either expansively or concisely, so that readers could thoroughly grasp the truth of the incident. We also pay attention to and report on figures whose every move is of interest from our perspective.<43>
In short, we apply the measure of news value to the events we are most interested in and the events we ought to cover. Whether an event originates in Europe, America, China, or the Soviet Union, they are all interconnected and related. Where is the focal point we are most interested in? The public knows this well. Newspaper reporters often take cues from the public (about where their interest lies) and then form their own judgments.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn (Chosŏn Ilbo Editor-in-Chief)

While it's not that we have no policy whatsoever, in the current situation where newspapers are essentially retailers of "news" distributed through controlled communication networks, it is almost impossible for an editor to gather news freely. Therefore, we simply report most prominently what is objectively most important among the day's news and what has the most direct bearing on us.

Kim Tong-sŏng (Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo Editor-in-Chief)

Lord Northcliffe of the London Times famously said, "When a dog bites a man, that is not news, because it happens so often. But if a man bites a dog, that is news." Rather than reporting on things that routinely happen, when something unexpected occurs, our Chungang Ilbo dedicates all its efforts to reporting that event swiftly. When Germany, thought to be dead, suddenly rose up, the world's attention was captured. We quickly caught onto this, and recently, it feels as though only Germany exists in the world, as we expand our coverage of Germany across all pages. There are many such examples: when a weak force fights a strong one, or when movements between nations cause waves like those in the Pacific or Atlantic oceans...
However, sometimes while editing the political pages, we reach difficult situations requiring editorial judgment. As you know, all Korean newspapers receive foreign telegrams from "Dentsū, Rengō Tsūshin, etc.," and there are many times when we doubt how much to trust these foreign reports. Just the other day, it was reported that Zhu De had been killed in action and Mao Zedong had become the commander-in-chief in his place. This was based on Chiang Kai-shek's forces capturing and interrogating Red Army soldiers, who supposedly confirmed Zhu De's death and stated that the entire army had held a funeral ceremony with rifle salutes. I don't know how many dozens of times foreign telegrams have reported Zhu De dead. Didn't they say Lenin died seventy-two times during his lifetime?
So, the foreign telegrams reported Zhu De dead, but our paper alone did not print it as is. There are times when making such judgments is agonizing. Later reports in foreign papers confirmed that Zhu De was indeed alive. Generally, even agencies like Dentsū or Reuters are subject to censorship by the Nanjing Government, so one must anticipate that considerable political maneuvering is mixed in.
In any case, we always pay close attention to and report on the actions of prominent politicians<44> on the world stage.

B. Why has the coverage of the situation in China receded in recent pages (whereas 3-4 years ago, Chinese issues covered entire pages), while Germany has emerged prominently, and why is there such close attention paid to trends in Tōkyō politics (which wasn't the case 7-8 years ago)?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

The reason Tōkyō political news, which was treated minimally 7-8 years ago, is now handled with great importance probably dates back to when Korea's "rice issue" was being debated in Tōkyō. Also, 8-9 years ago was when China's unification under Chiang Kai-shek was being completed, which was more astonishing, so it's natural that most political news leaned that way. Generally, any single decree originating from Tōkyō now directly or indirectly affects the entire colony, so we cannot help but treat it as important. The reason German news is now dominating the pages is likely because the feeling of exhilaration and the subsequent potential turmoil are generating intense interest and attention.

Kim Tong-sŏng

The group most keenly interested in the movements of Tōkyō politics are the merchants and industrialists (南工階級人). These individuals know that if a political change or dissolution of the Diet occurs, the prices of their textiles, rice, and stocks will fluctuate, leading to opportunities for great profit or ruinous loss, so they watch with fiery eyes. Just the other day, news of Chiang Kai-shek's supposed death seemed to reach stock market circles in Sŏul from Tōkyō even before the newspapers, causing great commotion.
Besides the merchant and industrial class, everyone has now begun to take a great interest in Tōkyō politics. Tōkyō is one of the important hearts of Asia, so its political and economic movements inevitably affect every individual in Asia. How could it be otherwise?
Since foreign telegrams about China issues have decreased, coverage of China issues on the political pages has quieted down recently, but China always remains an object of our interest and concern. Readers are all watching China issues closely.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

We do not neglect the situation in China. However, the European situation centered on Germany is more urgent and larger, so we have momentarily shifted our focus to where the news value is greater. The China issue is inherently the Asia issue and the world issue, so our greatest interest and attention remain fixed on it, just as they were ten years ago. We only temporarily shift our perspective according to the trends of the moment; the focus of our interest in the world situation remains constant. And the reason we report on trends in Tōkyō politics is partly because they have an immediate impact on our real lives, but also because, for several years now, Tōkyō's political ******* movements are a ripple effect of **********, and therefore part of world politics *********** world trends. ******** Tōkyō affairs, London affairs, or ******** ******** are all interconnected global phenomena.<45>
Therefore, we must always understand any event not individually, but holistically, relationally, and synthetically. In this sense too, we must pay close attention to the trends in the nearby Tōkyō political scene.

C. What measures are taken on the pages to provide "political education" to readers, and are you undertaking any ** initiatives?

Kim Tong-sŏng

I believe there is no particular need to provide political education specifically to Koreans. Everyone is interested in politics and constantly studies it. If you go to the countryside, there isn't a single house where people don't gather in the main room (사랑방) to discuss world affairs, and everyone has their own opinion on issues like Germany or China. This is because the writings of Confucius and Mencius are largely political theories, and for five hundred years, they have constantly engaged in political struggles, so they have a keen interest in politics.
Therefore, the entire political section implicitly serves as interesting material for these people, making specific political education unnecessary. However, to supplement, guide, and stimulate this, we strive to include things like commentary on international issues.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

"Education" might be too strong a word. We constantly endeavor to explain fragmented news to aid the reader's convenience. Not only does the Special Essay on page 1 of our paper fulfill this role, but recently we have established a "Topic Explanation" (話題解說) column on page 1 of the evening edition and continue to provide daily explanations.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

We publish explanations of various issues on the political pages as timely as possible. While presenting and criticizing the core of issues daily through editorials, columns like 'Horizontal and Vertical Talk' (橫說竪說), and other commentary can provide considerable understanding of international situations and trends, for more complex issues, we immediately publish explanatory articles to help general readers understand. Also, there are points of emphasis implicitly shown during editing; these can all be seen as the editor's provisions for political education.

D. Regarding the paper's main principles constantly emphasized in editorials, and others.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

Since we have a chief editorial writer, it's difficult for me to speak about editorials, but our company's principles and spirit are the following four points:
1. Upholding Justice (正義擁護) 2. Building Culture (文化建設) 3. Developing Industry (産業發展) 4. Impartiality and Fairness (不偏不黨 - corrected from 不偏不薰)

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

The principles and spirit of the Tonga Ilbo are clearly written in the first issue of its publication.
And to this day, we have consistently adhered to that spirit as the foundation of our company policy.
The editorials up to now prove this, without need for separate elaboration.<46>

Kim Tong-sŏng

If you pay close attention to our editorials, you will understand, but in essence, we emphasize the principles by which we can thrive (*道理). We especially strive to promote our company's principles regarding education and industry whenever the opportunity arises.

Regarding Social Articles

A. Efforts seem apparent in various papers to expand coverage of the bright side of Korean society and conceal the dark side as much as possible. What is the reason behind this?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

It is human nature to delight in brightness and dislike darkness. Especially today, when newspapers have become a daily necessity for cultured people, placed on desks morning and evening, presenting readers with pages filled only with darkness is regrettable and, I think, harmful. Moreover, to instill vitality and bring light into our society, which is in many ways powerless and lacking cheerfulness, I believe we must deliberately seek out the bright side.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

Firstly, because it is a society yearning for light, we tend to pay attention to this aspect as much as possible.
Because materials representing brightness are scarce in our society, we treat them as precious items and introduce them carefully.
Even while talking about light, light, our reality easily leads us to wander in darkness, so we deliberately emphasize light, light, uncovering, digging up, and spreading the bright side. This is partly a technical consideration to make the newspaper pages fresh, elegant, and cheerful, but I see it as something Korean reality demands. Like searching for water in a desert. However, such an approach carries the risk of making people complacent with the status quo. But I trust that contemporary newspaper professionals are not without this level of caution.

Kim Tong-sŏng

Indeed, compared to newspapers from 5-6 years ago, today's pages have a certain余裕 (yoyū - ease/room) and humor. And in the articles handled, there is a brightness. For example, if an educator donates money, or some volunteer undertakes poverty relief work, we write about it prominently (特書大書) to show that such dedicated individuals are striving in our society, trying to bring "light." If you only talk about darkness being dark, it just gets darker. No matter how dark the moonless night, there is always a way for a single ray of light (一條光明) to appear. We must now show bright light to our readers. I believe it is the editor's duty to create such an atmosphere.

B. Regarding social articles, are there any "fundamental articles" where you actively or dynamically imply the paper's principles and devote reporting efforts toward a certain principle? (e.g., emphasizing industrial facilities, compulsory education, abolition of corvée labor (夫役廢止), promotion of domestic products (物産獎勵), promotion of physical education (體育獎勵), etc.)<47>

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

Yes, there are. Since "food is heaven" (食以爲天), promoting industry is paramount; since we must know things, promoting education is the first priority. Therefore, we focus our efforts on industry and education. Outside of political or social movements, these are practically the only areas available. Also, these areas are the most crucial among the sectors where we can possibly raise our voices. Among these, regarding education, we campaigned through our pages with slogans like "Implement Compulsory Education," "Eradicate Illiteracy," which not only awakened the governing authorities but also stimulated the public's passion for education. The fact that schools are now being established everywhere stems from this, I believe. It goes without saying for areas like physical education. We have further plans for this area, but we will refrain from public disclosure for now.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

Based on our company's principles, we emphasize matters related to education, livelihood, and health.

Kim Tong-sŏng

While not neglecting other areas, our company policy is decided on putting special effort into physical education (體育). We lament our weak physical strength. Years ago, I went to the Hamhŭng shrine (咸興本宮) and was astonished at the size of the clothes Yi T'aejo wore as a young man, the sword he carried, and the hat he used – they were so large. In Sŏul too, when I visited an old house of about four hundred kan belonging to a certain Ch'amp'an (參判 - Vice Minister rank) in what is now Asahi-machi (旭町), formerly Hoe-dong (膾洞), I couldn't help but be amazed. It was a house built several hundred years ago, and out of 400 kan, only two rooms had ondol (heated floors); all the others had wooden floors (널마루방). Our ancestors certainly did not live with their backs pressed against warm ondol floors like us. Because they were healthy, they lived in rooms with wooden floors. But look at us now – our small physiques, lack of endurance; truly, our weakness is shameful. We must all build strong bodies. That's why we have established a separate physical education department to heighten enthusiasm for sports not only among general readers but throughout society.
As a first step, we invited the famous football team from Shanghai, renowned in the East. We consider this a success in the sense that it further emphasized the importance of physical education to Koreans. We will continue to focus our efforts in this area.

C. Types of articles deliberately omitted (e.g., rape (强姦), gold mine failures (金鑛失敗談), etc.)

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

Any incident acquires reportorial value based on its "news" value. Therefore, things with little news value are naturally omitted. Moreover, purely private matters are resolutely not published. Not only that, but for all incidents, there are things to publish and not publish based on the measure of "degree." However, for incidents like "rape" (强姦), where just seeing the characters can evoke unpleasantness,<48> we sometimes change the term to "assault" (暴行) or omit it entirely. As for things like gold mine failure stories (金鑛失敗談), if publicizing the failure story can serve as a reference for others and does not adversely affect personal reputation or private life, I think it might be acceptable to publish them.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

There are numerous articles deliberately ignored, deliberately avoided. The types are so numerous, I cannot list them all. There are also many types we cannot list even if we wanted to. To summarize briefly: things whose reporting would not benefit our society, things not honorable, things worthless or meaningless even if reported, so that general society would disregard them (置之度外), things the authorities prohibit publishing, things that violate the law, etc., etc.
For reference, I attach our company's News Gathering Guidelines (取材綱領).
一. Appropriately respect others' lives, property, and human dignity.
一. Strictly distinguish between others' public/private lives and public/private interests.
一. Observation and stance should always be objective.
一. News gathering and investigation should thoroughly adhere to the principle of being on-site (臨場主義).
一. Strive for factual realism (寫實), but avoid darkness.
一. Report right and wrong faithfully, but consider the impact.
一. Aim for accuracy, but prioritize speed.
一. Be concise, but do not lose the main points.
一. Be detailed when necessary, but avoid complexity.
一. Respect the explanations and direct statements of the parties involved.
一. Ensure no entanglement of relatives or acquaintances beyond the involved parties.
一. Do not pursue inquiries beyond the scope of the relevant incident.
一. Always keep the following four points in mind:
    Things contrary to social justice and the trend of the times.
    Things conflicting with all laws regarding newspapers.
    Things related to the company's principles and prestige.
    Things detrimental to the welfare of the majority of the populace.
一. The five elements of an article:
    When, Where, Who, Why, How.

Kim Tong-sŏng

Articles concerning human relations (人倫) are handled with caution. However, if they have social impact, we do not hesitate to clarify them through our pages.
Beyond that, matters concerning others' honor are given particular and repeated consideration.

Regarding Economic Articles

A. Are there specific areas where you focus efforts, such as promoting Korea's indigenous industries or striving to open up markets in outlying territories? <49>

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

Striving for industrial development is one of our company's principles. Therefore, we pioneered the establishment of an industry section separate from the economy section, and we are currently working for the Korean economy and Korean industry, and will continue to do so in the future. Organizing an industrial inspection team as part of the 15 major projects commemorating the completion of our new building, providing an opportunity to tour Tōkyō, Ōsaka, and other advanced industrial cities, also stemmed from this intention.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

We have established an independent section called the industry section and dedicate efforts to this area. Even outside this section, or without specialized articles on industry or specific academic theories, there are many ways to encourage or promote this field. This is something we constantly pay attention to throughout all pages.

Kim Tong-sŏng

From issues like Manchurian millet (滿洲粟) down to smaller matters like beekeeping (養蜂) and poultry farming (養鷄), we provide guidance on breeding methods, introduce good products, and make continuous efforts. Generally, brightness for Korea will come from the promotion of industry. In this respect, we intend to focus our efforts on the industry pages.

B. Besides articles on rice and beans (米豆記事), what are your plans for revamping the economy pages to make them more relatable to the general readership?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

One aspect of highly developed capitalist economies is the settlement system (淸算制度). Therefore, detailed reporting on stock or futures rice settlements (期米) on the economy pages today is by no means merely encouraging a speculative gambling mood, as some misunderstand. While it's true that few readers yet fully understand and utilize this section, considering just one point – how the daily rice exchange rate directly affects our daily lives – we cannot afford to disregard settlement market prices (淸算時勢). In addition to settlement news, we aim to report more faithfully on rice conditions, price trends, capital movements, etc., which have close ties to Korean life, as well as the general economic situation of Koreans.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

From the term "rice and bean articles" (米豆記事), I can guess the questioner's thoughts. But what are commonly called rice/beans (米豆) and stocks (株式) are not gambling. If gambling is taken in a broad sense, then the entire business world, the entire economic world, indeed, all worldly affairs involve an element of gambling. Unless the social structure or economic system changes drastically, under the current system, the "transaction" (取引) system of regular settlement markets (定期淸算市場制度) is the most developed, and current commercial transactions center around this. It's not just a matter of interest for those who go to the market and clap hands (make deals). Rather, the economy pages must become even more faithful to conditions and numbers in reality. Disregarding this section as dry and uninteresting (乾燥無味) is because Koreans are still unscientific and non-numerical in their practical lives. We are making efforts to make it as understandable and relatable to the general public as possible. Generally, detailed plans ("belly plans" - 腹案) for newspaper pages are not publicly announced until they are revealed through the pages themselves.<50>

Kim Tong-sŏng

We are striving to write about the world economic situation in an easily understandable and popular style so that everyone can comprehend it. Nowadays, unlike in the past, a war, political upheaval, or natural disaster in some corner of the distant world inevitably affects the entire global economy to some extent. Therefore, we will endeavor to report and explain the epicenter of these waves faster than others. In this way, we strive to rescue the economy pages from isolation, stiffness (生硬), and dryness (無味乾燥).

Regarding Literature and Arts (文藝)

A. Couldn't you make more concrete and larger efforts to introduce the spiritual culture of ancient Korea or preceding dynasties? What are your detailed plans (腹案) for this?

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

Our newspaper has striven so much to unearth ancient Korea and ancient culture that we might even be criticized for doing it excessively. We are dedicated to re-recognizing and re-promoting our unique culture with a new breath. There's no need to list numerous examples, but recently, during our 5,000th issue commemoration, we showed a part of this effort. Korean color (朝鮮色), Korean spirit (朝鮮心) – this is the consistent principle and goal that our Tonga Ilbo pays the most attention to. Because of this, there seem to be some uncomprehending criticisms. But this is because we always believe that "while all cultural achievements of Korea are ultimately part of the world and cannot escape the global framework, Korea still has its own uniqueness."

Kim Tong-sŏng

We have decided to include at least one item every day on the literature and arts page, covering everything from the clothing and rituals of preceding Korean dynasties.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

We have done so in the past and are doing so currently. I believe there is no need to state detailed plans here.

B. What efforts will you make to discover new writers? Also, do you have any plans for the promotion of literature and arts on the peninsula?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

It is a well-known fact that newspaper arts departments solicit masterpieces from new talents annually or several times a year with prize money, but it is disappointing that no particularly outstanding works are seen. We constantly think about making newer efforts for the sake of newcomers and the promotion of literature and arts, but we cannot announce such plans.

Kim Tong-sŏng

If excellent literary works such as poems, novels, or literary criticism come in, we are prepared to provide as much space as needed. However, we lament the recent lack of truly astonishing major works. And for unknown writers, we have been making efforts through New Year's prizes or by occasionally offering large sums for prize-winning novels.<51>

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

At the start of every New Year, we seek new works and new writers. At other opportunities too, whenever possible, we devise and implement plans to encourage and recommend them. Such matters are announced to the world through the pages at that time.
There may be many other ways not yet known, but I think there is no need to publicly state them until they can be announced through the pages.

C. What percentage of the total readership do you estimate are readers of serialized novels?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

The era when newspaper novels were thought to be read only by housewives is over. I believe that anyone living a complex modern life seeks solace, and in our society where opportunities for solace are few, newspaper novels are the most universal material providing them comfort. It's difficult to answer numerically what percentage of all readers are novel readers. This depends on the excellence of the serialized novel – readership can be high or low. However, currently, for the Chosŏn Ilbo, I believe 90 percent of all readers are also devoted readers of the novels.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik (misprinted as 蒔義植, should be 薛義植)

I haven't analyzed statistics, so it's hard to give exact numbers, but if we categorize readers, I believe readers in this area are the most numerous. You didn't ask for the reason, so there's no need to explain, but there is one thing I'd like to add. When we say "total readership" (全讀者), it's easy to associate it with "total circulation" (全發行部數), but especially in Korea, I see a huge difference between circulation and readership. It's common for one copy of a newspaper to be read not only by the entire family or all shop employees but also circulated throughout an entire village (一洞里). For this reason, even if, say, the head of a household doesn't read novels due to different tastes, perhaps four or five other family members specifically read the novels.

Kim Tong-sŏng

I believe the readership of serialized novels in our newspaper is gradually increasing. I can't give an exact number, but I think it's about 50 percent of the total readership.

Regarding Others

A. The era of influencing readers solely through news or page content seems to have passed. Please tell us if you have concrete plans for "what comes next," such as sports, film movements, publishing, etc. – specifically, "Mahayana-like" (大乘的 - grand scale) plans to bring even non-readers under your influence.

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

A newspaper company is a place that publishes newspapers, and newspaper reporters consider everything other than making newspapers to be secondary. You say, "The era of influencing readers solely through page content has already passed,"<52> but I consider striving to create excellent pages my greatest mission.
It is a well-known fact that our company has been promoting literacy and health movements for years and has sponsored or supported various sports, striving for the construction of a "strong Korea" (억센 조선). We intend to continue these efforts steadfastly in the future to meet the expectations of the public. However, if the main newspaper itself is poor, no matter how good the projects are, they cannot succeed as intended. In this sense too, I adhere to the "Page First Principle" – first, make the newspaper well, and then do the projects well.
It goes without saying that the publishing business has a great connection to cultural movements. Our company, as one of the projects commemorating the completion of our building, is establishing a publishing department and is currently preparing on a large scale to contribute in this area alongside the newspaper.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

That's right. It is no longer the time for a newspaper company to limit its efforts solely to the production of the "newspaper," given the power of its organization, facilities, and publicity. If we examine the path newspapers have tread, they moved from being commentary-centric to reportage-centric. However, going forward, I think they must fulfill a role as social service institutions beyond being purely news delivery organs. Not just for the newspaper's own publicity and utilization of surplus capacity, but to fulfill the role of true social service, a true public servant of society, "service on the pages" alone is far too insufficient. This is demanded by the times and the public, but in a society like Korea where business institutions are weak, reliance on the organizational power of newspapers is even greater. Therefore, leading newspaper companies now have departments not directly related to newspaper production (planning dept., business dept., service dept., etc.) and organize or support various projects either directly or through affiliates. I believe this area will develop even further in the future – and it must – but since the meaning of a newspaper company's existence lies in producing the newspaper, forgetting this fundamental meaning would also not be the right path. Even if projects are absolutely necessary, in reality, they are secondary and ancillary. And they should be.

Kim Tong-sŏng

As mentioned in the previous section, our newspaper company intends to particularly highlight one thing to serve even the non-reader base. Thus, as mentioned earlier (앗가 말슴한 바), we have newly established a special sports department within the company and decided to focus all efforts on improving physical constitution (體質改造).
Besides this, we currently have a magazine department as an ancillary business of the newspaper company, publishing two magazines, "Chungang" (中央) and "Sonyŏn Chungang" (少年中央). There are various detailed plans (腹案) currently under consideration, which we intend to announce later, but in essence, we organize and carry out events like the Lantern Viewing Group (觀燈團) to Sŏgwangsa (釋王寺) or Kaesŏng (開城) on April 8th, and hold lectures and film screenings (映畵會) as needed. For our company, these stem from the sole intention of serving readers while progressing alongside the news.<53>

B. Among Tōkyō and Ōsaka newspaper professionals, whom do you consider accomplished or outstanding 'star reporters' or 'star editors'?

Kim Tong-sŏng (misprinted as 金烔元, should be 金東成 based on context/previous answers)

Among star reporters, people like Uēda Sekizō of Dentsū produce three or four times the output of others and gather the most good material. Among seniors, it would be Tokutomi Sohō. In political commentary, individuals like Baba Tsunego are distinguishing themselves. And perhaps a 'star editor,' anyway a treasure of the newspaper world, is Sugimura Sojinkan. This person created and perfected the organization of the research department (調査部) in the newspaper world, wrote well, was good at devising things; in any case, he was a person of great merit.

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

Truly, a wealth of talent (濟濟多士), it's hard to single anyone out. But if we choose someone who is truly a pure newspaper reporter with skill, fame, and achievements, it would be the veteran Sugimura Kōtarō (currently editorial advisor at Tōkyō Asahi, pen name Sojinkan - 楚人冠).

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn (misprinted as 金東成, should be 金炯元 based on context/previous answers)

Sugimura Sojinkan and Suzuki Bunshirō of the Asahi.

C. Among Tōkyō and Ōsaka newspapers, which do you consider the best organized and having superior editing technique?

Kim Hyŏng-wŏn

Tōkyō Asahi Shinbun, Ōsaka Asahi Shinbun

Sŏl Ŭi-sik

**Summary**

This 1935 interview features the editors-in-chief of Korea's three major newspapers (Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo) discussing their editorial policies. They cover political news selection (emphasizing global hotspots like Germany, China, and Tōkyō's impact), social news (a conscious effort to highlight positive aspects over negative ones to uplift society), economic reporting (promoting industry and debating the relevance of market news), cultural content (preserving Korean heritage, fostering new writers, the popularity of serialized novels), and the evolving role of newspapers beyond print (sports promotion, publishing, community events). The editors acknowledge the constraints of controlled news sources (Dentsū, Rengō) and censorship, express admiration for Japanese newspapers like the Asahi Shinbun, and reveal priorities like national strengthening through physical education and industrial development.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the media landscape and intellectual concerns in mid-1930s colonial Korea. The editors' articulated desire to promote national culture, industry, and even physical strength (體育) within the constraints of colonial rule highlights subtle forms of nationalism and adaptation. Their discussion of balancing 'dark' and 'bright' news reflects the psychological pressures of the era, while the focus on Tōkyō politics underscores the colony's dependence. Historians might find the explicit mention of controlled news agencies, the editors' strategies for navigating information (like questioning reports on Zhu De), and their views on the newspapers' role in social guidance and cultural preservation particularly interesting.

薛義植(東亞日報編輯局長)

東亞日報의 정치면 지면을 보시면 아시려니와 우리 신문은 「움지기는 그 세계 정계의 초점」을 반영하여 노키에 힘씀니다. 그러기에 요지음은 歐洲政局이 多端하기에 그 파동을 전하기에 전력을 다하고 잇습니다, 獨逸은 한 예지요. 四川一帶에 中國紅軍이 長驅風雲을 이르키든 작일에는 中國紅白勢力戰의 보도에, 간디-움지기든 4,5년 전에는  英印關係의 보도에, 滿洲事變, 國際聯盟等等 모다 그 一波가 세계정국에 미치는 것이라면 특히 역점을 너허, 기복하는 시간적 光景은 물론, 원인 결과를 알리기 위하야 해설로, 뉴-쓰 로, 사진으로, 확대적 또는 집약적으로 보도하야 독자로 하야곰 철저히 그 사건의 진상을 파악하도록 하기에 노력하엿습니다. 인물도 우리 處*로 안저 그의 一動一靜에 관심을 가질 것이라면 늘<43> 주의하여 보도함니다.
요컨대 우리가 가장 관심하는 사건, 해야할 사건에 뉴-쓰 벨류의 척도를 두는 것입니다 사건의 발단이 歐洲든 米洲든, 또는 中國이든 蘇聯이든 그것은 다 서로 인과가 되고 서로 관련이 되는 것임니다. 우리가 가장 관심을 가지는 초점은 어듸에 잇는가? 그것은 민중이 잘 아는 것임니다. 신문기자는 오히려 민중의 암시를 (어느 점에 관심을 가진다는) 밧어가지고 自家의 판단을 세우는 것임니다.

다만 정치면을 편집하다가도 편집자로써 판단하여야 할 난국에 도달하는 때가 잇습니다. 아시다십히 朝鮮의 신문은 너나 할것 업시 「電通, 聯合通信等」에서 外電을 차저오는데, 그 외전을 어듸까지 밋어야 할가 의심나는 때가 만허요, 전일도 朱德이 전사하엿기에 毛澤東이 그 代로 총사령이 되엇다고 낫서요. 그는 홍군병졸을 蔣介石軍이 捕虜하여다가 심문하여본즉 朱德이 확실히 죽어서, 그 장례식까지 전군대가 모여 밧드러 총!으로 지냇다고 하는 것임니다. 朱德이 죽엇다는 外電은 오늘까지 몇 십차인지 몰나요, 레-닌도 생전에 일흔 두번을 죽엇다고 하지 안어요.
즉 外電은 朱德이를 죽엇다고 報하는데, 우리紙만 그대로 내지 안엇서요. 이러한 판단을 하기에 고심하는 때가 잇슴니다. 그뒤 後報에 朱德은 과연 살아잇는 것이 확실타고 外字紙가 報합데다. 대체로 電通이나 로이텔通信 가튼 것도 南京政府의 검열을 맛는 것이니까 거기에 정치적 공작이 만히 석기는 것을 미리 아러채여야지요.
어잿든 인물도 두각이 뛰어난 각국정계의 정치가들<44> 행동은 늘 주목하야 보도하여옴니다.

B. 최근의 지면에서 中國情勢가 머러지고 (3,4년 전에는 中國問題가 전지면을 덥헛는데) 獨逸이 전면적으로 등장하고 잇스며 又 東京政界의 推移를 極力注目 (7,8년 전에는 그러지 안터니) 하는 그 이유?

金炯元
