**Samch'ŏlli Secret Chamber**

**Intellectual Circles, Domestic and Foreign Situation**

**The "Sin Saeng" Incident and the Organ Theory**

While the "Sin Saeng" (新生) magazine lèse-majesté incident that occurred in Shanghai has been repeatedly reported in the newspapers recently, the content was merely described as "an article disrespectful to the Japanese Imperial Family," lacking detail. However, during a question-and-answer session on July 12th in Tokyo, when the Seiyūkai's Committee for the Thorough Implementation of Clarifying the National Polity visited Navy Minister Ōsumi at the Navy Ministry, the following phrase emerged, revealing for the first time that the "Sin Saeng" lèse-majesté incident was related to the issue of the Organ Theory (天皇機関説).

Committee Member: "The 'Sin Saeng' incident is an issue of Japan's Organ Theory that arose in China. China has already dealt with it, so why is Japan neglecting it (the Emperor Organ Theory)? Does the Navy Minister have any intention to advise the Prime Minister to achieve results?"
Navy Minister: "I will do my best. I am fundamentally opposed to the Organ Theory out of conviction."

**Korean Officers in Manchukuo**

According to Lieutenant Colonel Yi Ryang (李亮) of the Manchukuo Army, who visited our office on July 17th, it seems that many Korean officers will be entering the Manchukuo military in the future. Currently, seven are already serving as commissioned officers, and it is further reported that there are several Korean students at the army training center in Fengtian (奉天), which can be considered an army military academy.
Lieutenant Colonel Yi Ryang is said to have been stationed in Harbin (哈爾賓) as the chief of staff for Manchukuo's 2nd Military District since this spring.<18>

**Japan Accustomed to Britain and America**

Each year, the U.S. Navy's institutional journal publishes a prize essay selected by the leadership; this year, an essay titled "The Pacific Problem and the Course of War" was chosen. With the expiration date of the naval treaty approaching, the appearance of this essay is drawing attention as it indicates the trend within a segment of the U.S. military. Its main points are as follows:

**Internal Motives for War**
Japan is like pre-war Germany, but its need for territorial expansion is quite significant. The Japanese people possess a strong conviction as a rising nation and biologically have the divine right to eliminate surplus population. Their advance into Manchuria and the national aspiration to become the leader of East Asia are based on this. Britain and America, threatened by Japan's advance, cannot help but adopt cooperative policies, both hard and soft, but the possibility of an alliance relationship is weak.

**External Motives for War**
A war within two years triggered by the denial of naval parity to Japan; a war triggered after the completion of Manchuria's military and economic structure; a war triggered by seizing an opportunity involving British and Soviet participation. Regarding these three possibilities, even if a treaty-less warship construction race materializes next year, Japan's power relative to Britain and America will drastically decrease compared to the completion of British and American naval ports and shipbuilding two years later. Therefore, Japan's stance regarding the London Conference will either be to turn its back on Britain and America, secretly prepare its military, and recklessly provoke war, or demand equal rights but maintain relative effort as much as possible without completing treaty-level shipbuilding in a short period. Ultimately, the discussion revolves around whether to avoid war within two years. However, the result remains the status quo, and there is a great risk that Japan will not wait for the approaching two years but will strike a blow.

**Japan's Position**
The naval power of rising Japan is quite superior compared to the defensive Britain and America. Although there are partial weaknesses such as the demilitarization of mandated territories and poverty in heavy oil resources, its fleet power is balanced, the people's patriotism is strong, and if it collaborates with a certain European power as rumored, it can block British and Soviet advances. If Ethiopia's racial friendship becomes an alliance and Britain's Far East power is sufficiently suppressed, China will have no choice but to follow Japan. It is natural for Japan to focus its diplomatic efforts on this. The only flaw is the lack of an economic foundation for unlimited protracted war against the major powers. Considering this tactically, it is disadvantageous for Japan to engage the enemy fiercely in its nearby waters. Rather than treading the path of Germany's two fronts, it should boldly move its offensive line and decide the victory or defeat in one go.

**The Soviet Union's Position**
The Soviet Union is vigorously promoting its own military buildup, but without an alliance with naval powers, it cannot defeat Japan alone and would likely be pushed back to the vicinity of Baikal.<19> Therefore, it is not thought that they would try to align with Britain and America until sacrificing the entire Maritime Province.

**Britain's Position**
Britain is superior to Japan overall, but because Japan concentrates its entire fleet power in the Pacific and Britain possesses vast global territories, it faces a confrontation where it cannot concentrate its fighting power in one area. Its current position relative to Japan is inferior even compared to the Anglo-German relationship before the Great War.

**America's Position**
The annihilation of the British fleet means the collapse of the nation, but America has the strength of abundant resources domestically even if its Pacific territories are seized. It is superior in terms of fleet balance, bases for operations in the East, and the quantity of trained sailors, but inferior in quality compared to Japan. Its current position in the Pacific is worse than Britain's. Therefore, tactically, it might allow Japan to occupy the Philippines, suffer enormous sacrifices, and adopt a defensive, protracted strategy from the beginning of the war to break East Asian naval power during that time. However, the risk lies in the trend of domestic public opinion, having suffered difficulties in the Spanish-American War. It is thought that Pacific operations in the East will also be greatly hindered by this.

**Conclusion**
Viewed this way, America's future decidedly does not permit optimism. The morale of the expansionist nation Japan is soaring. The discretionary policy for the Anglo-Saxon naval powers to adopt at this juncture is to show full goodwill towards Japan, solidify Japan's position in the Pacific, while progressively completing the 5:5:3 treaty ratio and maintaining cooperation—this is the best service for both sides.

**Press and Academic Circles**

**Scene from the Chosŏn Ilbo Branch Managers' Conference**
On July 7th, a branch managers' conference was held in the large hall on the 4th floor of the newly built company building. The number of attending branch managers reached 300, a turnout unseen since the Mumyŏnghoe's All-Korea Journalists' Conference. At 1:00 PM sharp, President Pang delivered an address, followed by an explanation of the company's current status by General Affairs Director Kim Ki-bŏm. The gist was that until two years ago, the company suffered an annual deficit reaching 46,000 won, but now not only are revenues and expenditures balanced, but financial resources for new business ventures are gradually emerging. Following this, Business Director Mun Sŏk-chun gave instructions regarding business matters, and then Assistant Editor Ham Sang-hun provided cautionary notes on news gathering and communication. Afterwards, P'yŏngyang branch manager Kim Pyŏng-yŏn gave a response representing the branch managers. With this, the conference convened by the head office ended. Subsequently, an emergency branch managers' conference initiated by the branch managers themselves began, the main points of which are as follows:<20>

**Part Two: Emergency Branch Managers' Conference**
With Sim Sang-ryŏl, branch manager of Sin Sang, South Hamgyŏng, presiding as chairman, the following proposals were resolved and demanded of the head office:
1. The head office shall pay each branch 5 won per month as branch expenses.
1. The allotment of free copies (無價紙), recently reduced to only 5%, should be restored to around 10%.
1. All branch managers shall contribute 50 chŏn each to present a commemorative gift to President Pang.
1. Changes in branch managers should be handled with greater care.
1. The discount rate for advertising fees should be further increased.
1. A permanent provincial correspondent should be stationed in each special province.
1. Local news should be treated with more importance.
Following this, as the discussion turned to the most attention-grabbing issue of the recent Chosŏn Ilbo vs. Posŏng College (普專) problem, it was halted by attending police officers. Subsequently, a proposal to make this branch managers' conference a permanent body meeting once a year was also stopped by police intervention, and the meeting adjourned.

**Anecdotes from the Chosŏn Ilbo Building Completion Ceremony**
President Pang read his ceremonial address from a prepared scroll. While officiating a wedding sometime ago, his voice was barely audible even to those nearby, but this time, his loud voice resonated throughout the hall. His volume was ample, and the voice of Mr. Sŏ Ch'un, interpreting President Pang's address, showed the skill of a veteran (百戰之士), with excellent modulation of pitch and tone. The resonant and weighty sound carried authority.
Government-General Political Affairs Superintendent Imaida also read a prepared congratulatory speech, which was smooth and level like a school graduation address.
Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho, whose youthful face and fervent speech kept the audience tense, seemed to show that his spirit from those days had not yet waned (當年 意氣 尙不衰).
Mr. Kwŏn Tong-jin, in contrast to Mr. Yun, looked diminished in spirit, as if asking, "Where is the spirit of those days now?" (當年 意氣今安哉). Being already a guest of 70 years, age must take its toll—.
Next, the explanations by Messrs. Kim Ki-bŏm, Mun Sŏk-chun, and Ham Sang-hun were all clear and logical.
At the post-ceremony banquet, three cheers of "Chosŏn Ilbo Manse" were led by Mr. Cho Man-sik, but as the loudspeaker was not used, there was a suspicion that the sound did not carry thoroughly across the vast square.
This completion ceremony was a rare spectacle, attended by figures from all walks of life—various private organizations, government circles, the military, etc., seemingly everyone of note was gathered. Yet, among fellow journalists, only Maeil Sinbo Vice President Yi Sang-hyŏp attended. Neither President Yŏ of the Chungang Ilbo nor President Song of the Tonga Ilbo, nor executives from either company, were present, giving a strange impression. Rumor has it that invitation letters were not sent to the Tonga Ilbo due to the ongoing conflict between them.

**Note on Mr. Hŏ Hŏn and the Alumni Association Relationship**
Mr. Hŏ Hŏn was elected to the executive committee regarding the Chosŏn Ilbo matter at the Posŏng College Alumni Association meeting. However, as he has recently been engaged in mining in the Yŏnghŭng area of South Hamgyŏng, it is said he resigned from the committee, stating he could not fulfill the duties.<21>

**Business World, Literary Scene**

**Rumor of a 500,000 Won Rayon Company Appearing**
With the textile industry experiencing a boom, exemplified by Kyŏngsŏng Pangjŏk in Yŏngdŭngp'o increasing its capital to 3 million won, certain individuals are reportedly proceeding with plans to establish a 500,000 won rayon (人造絹) company purely with Korean hands. Specific details about investors and the business plan will be reported in the next issue.

**Dividend Rates of Various Banks**
Due to inflationary policies and the prosperity of the munitions industry, the peninsula's financial world is experiencing a significant boom, and the semi-annual dividends of various banks are expected to be as follows:
Bank of Chosŏn: Dividend of 5% (5分). Likely to be decided at the regular general meeting around August 10th.
Industrial Bank of Korea: Dividend of 9% (9分). Likely to be decided at the regular general meeting around August 15th.
Commercial Bank: Dividend of 3% (3分). Decided at the shareholders' meeting on July 24th.
Savings Bank: A dividend of 6% (6分) is expected to be decided. The shareholders' meeting is around July 25th.
Hansŏng Bank: Likely to be decided at the regular shareholders' meeting on July 25th, dividend rate unclear.
Tongil Bank: Likely to be decided at the regular shareholders' meeting on July 22nd, dividend rate unclear.

**Yet Another Building Appears**
At a time when new 4- or 5-story buildings like Hwasin, Hanch'ŏng, and Yŏngbo are appearing one after another, it is internally decided that another surprisingly large building will rise at the Kwanghwamun intersection under the hand of Mr. 000, causing a strange sensation in the construction world. The new building is planned as a 5-story structure costing 300,000-400,000 won, intended for office rentals, with rumors of construction starting this fall in September.

**Rumor of a Major Film Company Appearing**
According to persistent rumors, a large film company is expected to emerge in the long-stagnant peninsula film industry. This is reportedly affiliated with the Tokyo XX Motion Picture Co., Ltd., with funding coming from there, supplemented by a considerable subsidy from the Government-General for film control and promotion of domestic films. It aims to gather prominent male and female actors counted among Korea's best, and it seems likely to materialize this fall.

**Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn Coming to Seoul**
Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn is the great boxing champion produced by the peninsula, ranked 4th<22> in the world boxing scene. Having left his homeland and stayed in America, he has now gained fame and fortune. It is decided he will return home in glory (金衣還鄕), and news arrived from his friend, Hwang Ŭl-su of the Tongyang Kwŏnt'uhoe in Sup'yo-dong, Seoul, that he departed San Francisco on July 15th. Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-gwŏn's arrival in Seoul is expected around mid-August, and he is likely to stay in Seoul for some time.

**Mr. Chang Tŏk-su's Engagement**
Mr. Chang Tŏk-su, currently Vice President of the Tonga Ilbo, who went to America and has been gazing at the cold moon of a foreign land for over ten years, recently sent a letter (一狀 書札) to his mother in Seoul.
"Your humble son (迷息), having thought deeply, intends to marry a young lady named Pak Ŭn-hye, who is currently studying abroad in America. I humbly inquire if Mother grants her permission."
This was the gist. His mother immediately wrote back.
"Receiving your letter brings me joy, and I am very happy that you intend to marry. Though I do not know this Miss Pak, you are a grown man over 40, so surely you have thought carefully and decided. I think well of it, so fulfill your wish."
It is said that the two immediately got engaged upon receiving this letter. Whether the wedding ceremony will take place upon returning to Korea, or whether the two, whose youthful prime in their 30s and 40s is already passing like an arrow (光陰이 如矢―), will hastily marry and even establish a sweet home (쓰윗트홈) in America, is unknown.

**Two Corrections**
Mr. An Chae

思想界, 內外情勢
「新生」事件과 機關說
上海에서 이러난 잡지 「新生」不敬事件이라 하면 근래 신문에 屢屢 보도된 바이나 그 내용은 다만 「日本皇室에 대한 不敬記事」라 할 뿐으로 상세치 못한 바 7월 12일 東京에서 政友會의 國體明徵貫徹實行委員들이 海軍省에 大角海軍大臣을 방문하고 문답한 중 이러한 一句가 잇서 비로소 「新生」불경사건이란 機關說문제임을 알엇다.
委員  「新生」사건은 中國에 이러난 日本의 機關說문제올시다. 中國에서조차 처분하엿는데 日本은 何故로 그(天皇機關說)을 等閑에 付하고 잇는지요. 海相은 총리대신에 進言하여 실적을 擧하도록 할 생각은 업슴니까.
海相  盡力하겟슴니다. 나는 어듸까지든지 신념으로서 機關說 반대올시다.

滿州國에 朝鮮人 將校
7월 17일에 본사를 來訪한 滿洲國 육군중좌 李亮씨의 談에 의하면 압흐로는 滿洲國 군대에 朝鮮사람 사관이 만히 드러가게 될 듯한 바 현재 벌서 장교로 일곱명이 재직하고 잇다 하며 더욱 육군사관학교라고 볼 奉天의 육군훈련소에도 朝鮮人 학생이 여럿이 잇다 전한다.
李亮中佐는 今春부터 滿洲國 第2軍管區의 참모로 哈爾賓에 駐在 中이라 한다.＜18＞

日本은 英米에 익인다
매년 米國海軍機關誌는 首腦部의 심사에 의한 현상논문을 게재하든 바 금년은 大平洋 문제와 전쟁의 경로라는 題의 논문이 당선되엿다. 지금 해군조약의 無効期를 압두고 이 논문의 출현의 관측은 米國 일부 군부의 동향을 표시한 것으로 주목되여 잇다. 그 요지는 次와 如하다.

戰爭으로의 內面動機
日本은 戰前의 獨逸과 갓흐나 영토 팽창의 필요성은 자못 중대하다. 日本人은 신흥국민으로서의 강한 신념을 가지고 잇고 생물적으로 과잉인구를 제거할 天職의 권리를 가지고 滿洲의 진출 또한 東亞의 맹주되랴는 국가적 願望은 此에 기초되여 잇다. 일본의 약진에 의한 脅威를 밧는 英米는 硬軟共히 협력정책을 취하지 안을 수 업스나 동맹관계의 가능성은 박약하다.

戰爭으로 外面的 動機
日本에 대하야 均 해군권을 부인한 까닭으로 야기될 2년내의 전쟁, 滿洲의 군사경제적 기구완성한 후에 야기될 전쟁 英, 蘇의 참가를 기회 보와 야기될 전쟁 此 3종의 가능에 대하야 明年 무조약 建艦경쟁이 실현되여도 2년후 日本의 對英米세력은 英米의 軍港 及 建艦의 완성보다 激減되는 고로 論敦會商에 대한 日本의 입장은 英米에 佛面을 향하맀´서 비밀히 군비를 정돈하야 猪突的으로 전쟁을 야기하든가 혹은 均等權을 요구하여도 條約量 建艦을 단기간에 완성치 안코 될 수 잇스면 상대적 노력을 유지할 것이다. 결국 2년내의 전쟁을 회피하겟는냐의 의논이 된다. 然이나 결과는 현상대로 하고 日本은 來迫할 2년을 기다리지 안코 일격을 가할 위험성이 多大하다.

日本의 位置
신흥日本의 해군력은 방어적 英米에 비하야 자못 우세로서 委任統治地域의 비무장 重油資源의 빈곤 등의 부분적 결함은 잇스나 艦隊力은 均勢되고 국민의 애국심은 강하고 소문과 갓치 歐洲 某强國과 提携하면 英, 蘇의 진출을 저지하고 에치오피야의 인종적 友誼가 동맹관계가 되고 英國의 극동세력을 넉넉하게 억압하면 支那도 日本에 추종할 수 밧게 업슬 것이다. 日本이 외교공작을 이에 傾注하는 것은 당연하다. 유일의 결함은 열강을 상대하는 무제한한 持久戰, 耐久戰에 경제적 기초를 缺한 것이다. 이런 관계로 日本은 전술적으로 이것을 고려하면 日本 근해에 적군을 激擊하려는 것은 불리하다. 獨逸의 두 무대를 발지 안이하고 차라리 당당히 공격진을 動하야 一氣에 승패를 決할 것이다.

蘇國의 位置
蘇國은 자국의 군비충실을 極力 선전하고 잇스나 해군국의 동맹이 안이면 단독으로는 일본에 勝할 수 업고 바이가루＜19＞ 부근가지 격퇴될 것임으로 沿海州 일대를 희생할 때까지 英米에 組하려고는 생각되지 안는다.

英國의 位置
英國은 전체적으로 日本보다는 勝하나 日本이 全艦隊力을 太平洋에 집중함으로 세계적으로 넓은 영토를 가젓슴으로 英國은 전투력을 一方에 집중치 못할 대결함이 잇다. 大戰 前의 英獨關係 以上에 금일의 對日勢力은 劣勢로 잇다.

米國의 位置
英艦隊力 전멸은 국가의 붕괴를 의미하나 米國은 太平洋上의 영토를 탈취되여도 국내에 풍부한 富源을 가진 强味가 잇다. 함대력의 均整 東洋作戰의 根據地 海員의 養成數量에 잇서 勝하나 質에 잇서서 日本만 못하다. 현재 太平洋의 위치는 英國보다도 못한 고로 전술적으로는 日本에 比島를 점령식혀서 막대한 희생을 당하고 개전당초부터 방어적 持久術을 세워 이 사이에 동양의 해군력을 꺽것스나 그 위험성은 국내 여론의 동향으로서 米西전쟁에 신고한 경험이 잇다. 太平洋 동양작전도 이에 阻害되는 일이 多大하리라고 생각된다.

結論
이럿케 보면 米國의 前途는 단연 낙관을 불허한다. 팽창국가 日本의 사기는 冲天의 勢요 此際 안그로색손 해군국으로서 採取할 裁量 정책은 日本에 대하야 전폭적 호의를 가치고 太平洋에 대한 日本의 위치를 견고히 하는 一方에는 5, 5, 3의 조약비율을 점진적으로 완성하고 협조를 保持함은 一面類의 最良의 봉사다.

言論界, 學界
朝鮮日報 支局長大會 光景
7월 7일에 새로 지은 社屋 4層廣堂에서 支局長大會를 개최하엿는데 출석지국장 수가 300명에 달하야 無名會의 全朝鮮記者大會 이래 처음 보는 성황이엇다. 오후 1시 정각에 方社長의 훈사가 잇고 총무국장 金箕範씨의 본사 현황의 설명에 입하엿는바 요지는 재작년까지는 年年 缺損 4만 6천원에 미첫스나 이제는 수지가 마즐 뿐더러 신규사업을 경영할 재원도 차츰 생기는 중이라 하엿고 뒤이어 영업국장 文錫俊씨의 영업 관계에 대한 지시가 잇섯고 그 다음 편집차장 咸尙勳씨의 취재 及 통신에 대한 주의가 잇슨 뒤 평양 지국장 金炳淵씨가 지국장을 대표하야 답사가 잇섯다. 이로써 본사 측에서 소집한 대회는 곳 끔나고 뒤를 이어 지국장들끼리 發案하여 개회하는 긴급 지국장 대회가 잇섯는데 그 要領은 如左하다.＜20＞

方사장의 式辭는 미리 조히에 쓴 것을 들고 낭독하엿는데 언젠가 결혼식 주례할 때에 보니 겻헷사람도 잘 들니지 안을 성량으로 하더니 이번에는 고음으로 장내가 쩡-쩡-울렷다. 성량이 풍부하엿고 方사장 式辭를 통역하는 徐椿씨의 말소리는 百戰之士라 高低抑揚이 모다 조왓다. 걸-찍하고 묵-찍한 음향이 무게 잇섯다.

張德秀氏 約婚
現在 東亞日報 副社長의 현직에 잇스면서 米國 건너가 벌서 십여년을 異域의 찬달을 바라보고잇는 張德秀씨로부터 얼마 전에 서울잇는 그 慈堂께 一狀 書札이 왓다.
「迷息은 금번 깁히 생각한 바 잇서 현재 米國 건너와서 유학하고 잇는 朴恩惠라는 令孃과 결혼할가 하오니 어머님께서 허락하여 주시올는지 삼가 엿주어보나이다.」
이러한 뜻이엇다. 그 어머니께서는 즉시 답장을 썻다.
「편지를 보니 반가울 뿐더러 결혼할 뜻을 가젓다니 몹시 깃브다. 그런데 나는 朴氏라니 모른다만은 40넘은 장성한 네가 어련히 생각하고 결심하엿겟느냐 나는 조케 생각하니 네 뜻대로 이루워라.」
하엿다. 이 편지를 밧은 두분이 즉시 약혼하엿단 말이 잇는데 그 결혼식은 朝鮮에 도라와서 시작할는지 혹은 光陰이 如矢―라 3, 40台에 올은 꼿가튼 청춘의 고개를 이미 지나려하는 두분인지라 불이야 불이야 米國서 결혼하여 쓰윗트홈가지 이룰넌지 모른다.
