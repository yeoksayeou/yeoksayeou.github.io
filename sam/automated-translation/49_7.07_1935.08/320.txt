Notes on Listening to Mr. Sin Pulch'ul's Mandam, The Magnanimous Husband
						One Reporter
Prefatory Remarks
If we were to say that a clear distinction can be drawn between linguistics and literature, then *mandam* (comic dialogue) and *manmun* (comic writing) fundamentally differ in their form. What this reporter first felt upon hearing Mr. Sin Pulch'ul's *mandam* is that it can only be expressed through "speech" and absolutely cannot be expressed through "writing." If one were to try expressing *mandam* by borrowing the form of *manmun*, it would, of course, belong entirely to the realm of impossibility, and moreover, when it comes to the characteristic true flavor (眞味境) that *mandam* possesses, it is truly <237> as if it belongs to a separate world, forbidding even the slightest imitation of its form. Although I will discuss the content of the works Mr. Sin Pulch'ul has published under the same title in a separate manuscript, Mr. Sin Pulch'ul's artistic talent, seen through his "verbal skill," makes him truly a man of talent (才士). Although some people in the world, having only heard the jokes recorded (吹込) on records (레코-드), might misunderstand *mandam* as being only that sort of thing, it is actually not so, and the expectations for the *mandam* artist Mr. Sin Pulch'ul are great. Although he has recently presented numerous *mandam*, we begin publishing in this issue one of his older *mandam* pieces, "The Magnanimous Husband." As mentioned before, it is questionable how we can possibly capture its essence with "writing" instead of "speech," so considering this merely as presenting the content, I first offer my apologies to Mr. Sin Pulch'ul for any inadequacy. (Responsibility for the text lies with the reporter)

『...Do you really love me?』
Since it was the wife speaking, already over a year after their marriage, one might be startled by the unexpected question, but it was well past midnight (子正), and moreover, in a place where they shared a pillow. Naturally, for a woman cherished by her man, it was a charming outburst mixed with affection and coyness, and at the same time, a lamentation of her most anxious feelings.
『Of course, I love you. But asking so suddenly feels rather abrupt, doesn't it?』
Beneath the husband's moved reply and sly embrace, the wife, feeling some overwhelming physiological impulse, said,
『Darling?』
『Hm?』
『Uh, if you really, really love me, then I'll build a sky-high barrier (障壁) forever separating me from that useless world of vanity—』
It's unlikely the husband didn't understand his wife's meaning, but pretending to be obtuse, he deliberately asked,
『What is vanity?』
『Vanity is just that, isn't it? Top-grade cosmetics, diamond (다이야몬도) rings, fox fur scarves, silk stockings, modern houses (문화주택), and what-not – all that is vanity, isn't it?』
『So, if I truly love you, all that vanity becomes unnecessary?』
『Of course!』
『Really?』
『Uh-huh!』
『Even if you wear worn-out shoes and shabby clothes...』
『As long as your love is true and unchanging...』
『Really?』
『Uh-huh!』

The next day
On the company ledger (帳簿), the husband's fountain pen (鐵筆), moved by his cheerful mood, was dancing. However, the life of that fellow called joy is originally not so long, and the next moment, when the unwelcome yet precious salary envelope was placed in his hand, he couldn't help but bitterly cry again at the cold reality <238>.
Rice cost!
Water bill!
Side dish cost!
Firewood cost!
Suit cost!
Shoe cost!
Newspaper bill!
Light bill!
Medicine cost!
Land rent!
House rent! etc., etc., etc...
It was a situation where he couldn't avoid swimming in waves of deficit, and now even his choked sighs had become chronic, only increasing his very nonchalant nerve. So, thinking that if he hesitated while holding the salary envelope, the creditors (빗쟁이) would take it all, leaving him with an empty envelope to make into blotting paper (吸淚紙), his situation was indeed pitiful. As a last resort (窮餘의 一策), he staged a rather audacious act of what could be called criminal theater, cleverly deceiving the creditors' eyes. Wiping the sweat from his brow with the still unopened (封切되지 않은) salary envelope, he ran a track-and-field race towards home, where his wife was waiting (긔대리는).
Seeing her husband sitting there panting, out of breath from running headlong, the character for surprise leaped out from the wife's wide eyes.
The husband, painting a wry smile like one who had bitten into an unripe chestnut onto his sweaty face, said,
『Darling, since I got paid, let's go out for a walk together.』

Feeling terribly sorry for this wife who had acted as a cook for this worthless salaryman husband for a whole month, the husband, before hearing her reply, turned his gaze towards the woodshed, which had not a single tree, to hide the tears welling up in his eyes.
As soon as the suggestion to go for a walk was made, the wife had already finished her makeup, neatly changed into her outing clothes, and was bustling about looking for her parasol again.

So, after strolling here and there, they eventually entered a certain large department store (백화점). Of course, in this department store, luxury items embodying the height of splendor, such as top-grade cosmetics, diamond rings, silk stockings, fox fur scarves, silk fabrics, etc., were piled up like mountains, inevitably dazzling (眩慌케) the eyes of onlookers.
The wife, perhaps acting childishly pleased, flew into the department store as if floating, but the husband, being a penniless pauper, felt as if he had committed some crime bringing his wife into such a place. Pointlessly wearing the expression of having mistakenly bitten into a lump of soybean paste thinking it was meat, he shambled pitifully like a rain-soaked fellow, trailing behind his wife into the store.
『Oh my, how!』
『Oh, look at that!』<239>
『Oh, what about that one!』
『Oh, look at this!』
『Oh, how startling!』
『Oh...!』
『I wish I could buy this』
『That too』
『Oh, all of them!』
Indeed, it wasn't just speaking with one voice (이구동성), but rather one mouth speaking variedly (동구이성), as the wife continuously uttered exclamations of admiration, igniting a fire in her husband's eyes.
As the wife, who had been chattering away like a mad person while gulping, caught her husband's eye, she finally recognized herself. At the same time, the thought of the promise she had clearly made to her husband the night before surfaced, and instantly, a feeling of shame seemed to light a bonfire on her face.
No matter how dazzling the goods piled up in the department store were, and even if 'seeing tempts the heart' (견물생심) is human nature (常情), when she thought, "What disgraceful behavior is this for me, who declared my withdrawal from vanity (虛榮脫退) to my husband last night at home, nailing it down with words?" her heart must have hurt as if pierced by the sharp blade of humiliation (恥辱).
Seeing his wife's behavior like this, the husband's heart must have hurt even more. That was because, despite all the goods in this department store being things that could be bought if only one had money, he thought, "Why can't I buy those things that my wife, whom I cherish (애지중지) so much, desires so badly?" At that moment, the fact that he himself was a penniless, poor husband painfully gripped his heart, and the foundation of their bitter livelihood (살님사리), with their lives contained within a blood-stained salary envelope, clearly stamped itself before his eyes, making his heart ache, and subsequently, the whole world ache.

Due to such trivial matters, the lump of pain created in the hearts of that husband and wife becomes a seed planted in the middle of their livelihood, sprouting roots, growing stems, putting forth branches, producing leaves, dropping flowers, and bearing fruit. The name of that fruit ends up becoming the cold fruit called divorce.

So, does this mean a penniless husband cannot live with his wife? Actually, that's not the case either. Then, by what method can one manage (繰縱) the wife thoroughly, without any such worries (杞憂) or the tail-flicking shadow of that dreadful breakdown (破綻)?

First, from the moment they enter the department store:

The husband should not keep the salary envelope himself but should give it to his wife, effectively granting her full financial authority (재정전권). Then, once the money becomes the wife's, when entering the department store, he should not <240> trail behind pitifully like a rain-soaked fellow, but should now enter several steps ahead of his wife, barking orders like autumn frost (秋霜같은 號令),
『Ah, why are you dawdling like that? Hurry up and follow me!』
Acting all puffed up as he enters, he then says,
『Wow- that's really splendid! Darling, shall we buy that one? Shall we buy this one? How about that?』
Even without this, she was already overcome with desire, her greed rising to her chin. Hearing her husband's words, joining in with immense spirit (豪氣萬丈) and urging her on, it might seem likely that the wife would readily open the money envelope and buy those items. But the reality is not so. Even if the wife truly wants the items in her heart, the thought of taking out her own money to buy them feels like too much of a waste, so in the end, she cannot buy a single thing and leaves the department store empty-handed.
Returning home, the husband puts on airs once more.
『Darling! If those things were necessary for our household, you should have bought them. Why didn't you?』
When he pretends to reprimand her like this, inevitably, the wife, without fault, pouts broadly and, as if she has already given up, speaks loudly to her husband.
『What are you saying? Buy trivial things like that? Goodness, I'm speechless... Back when I was immature, I wanted to buy everything I saw, but do you think I'm still like that? Besides, we even made a promise last night...』

Everyone!
If things turned out just like this, how would it be?
Not spending a single penny,
Getting endless credit for generosity,
The wife becoming a different person on her own,
Without hurting the wife's feelings in the slightest,
No fear of divorce.

Isn't that quite a plausible clever method? However, if we ask whether such a tactic truly makes for a magnanimous husband, the answer is absolutely not.
It is nothing more than the basest sort of prank. It is merely a hateful stopgap measure (미봉책) that temporarily plasters over (塗糊) and fawns upon (訝謟) the wife's pathological psychological outburst—in other words, her frivolous vanity, unable to calmly face the reality of life. It is not the act of a person living with a somewhat greater humanity, and such a husband must be dismissed as entirely inhuman (비인간).
Then, what kind of husband can truly be magnanimous...?

**Summary**

This article introduces Mr. Sin Pulch'ul, a popular *mandam* (comedic storytelling) performer of the era, praising his verbal artistry while lamenting the difficulty of capturing its essence in print. It then presents the beginning of one of Sin's *mandam* stories, "The Magnanimous Husband." The story depicts a couple: the wife professes to reject materialism if her husband truly loves her, while the husband struggles with poverty. A visit to a department store exposes the wife's lingering desire for luxury goods, causing tension. The article proposes a "trick" where the husband gives the wife the salary, making her too frugal to buy anything, thus saving money while appearing generous. However, the author immediately condemns this as a base, inhuman tactic, not true magnanimity, promising to reveal the real solution in the next issue.

For historians, the article offers insights into popular entertainment (*mandam*) and social commentary in 1930s colonial Korea. It reflects anxieties about consumerism (department stores, luxury goods like diamond rings, fox furs), economic hardship (meager salaries, debt), and gender dynamics within modernizing urban marriages. The tension between professed ideals (love over vanity) and material desires, framed within the context of financial strain, provides a window into the everyday struggles and aspirations of middle-class Koreans under Japanese rule. The critique of the husband's "trick" hints at evolving ideas about sincerity and humanity in relationships.

첫머리의 말
語學과 文學과의 確然한 구별이 서 질 수 있다고 할 것 같으면 만담과 漫文과는 그 형식을 원칙적으로 달니 하는 것임니다. 申不出씨의 漫談을 기자가 처음 듯고 늣긴 것은 「말」 노서뿐 표현할 수 있는 것이고 「글」 노서는 도저히 표현식힐 수 없다는 것이 있읍니다. 만일 漫文의 형식을 빌어가지고 漫談을 표현하랴면 그것이야말노 전연 不能에 속하는 일인 것은 물론이려니와 况且 그 漫談이 가지는 특징적인 眞味境에 이르러서는 實노 別界에＜237＞ 것인양 하야 그 모습의 한 구퉁이나마 시늉하기를 불허하는 것이외다. 申不出씨가 발표한 作品들의 내용상 同題는 稿를 달니하야 말하겟지만 「말재조」를 통해 보는 申不出씨의 예술적 소질이야말노 문자 그대로의 才士외다. 이 申不出씨를 세상에서는 레코-드에 吹込한 우슴거리들만을 듯고 혹시 漫談이란 그런 것이나 않인가 하고 오해하는 사람들도 있겟지만 실은 그러치 않이하야 만담가 申不出씨에게 대한 기대는 큰 것임니다. 최근에 그가 발표한 漫談도 數多하지만 그 녯날 발표하엿든 漫談에 하나로 「관대한 남편」을 이번 호부터 기재하게 되엿는데 먼저도 言及한 바와 같이 「말」이 않인 「글」노서 엇더케 감히 그 면모를 살닐까 하는 것이 의문이매 다만 그 내용만을 발표하는 셈으로 하고 우선 申不出씨에게 미안함을 謝하는 바외다. 『...정말 나를 사랑하우?』
결혼한지가 벌서 1년이 넘는 안해의 말인지라 뜻박게 질의에 愕然하기도 하겟지만 워낙 子正이 훨신 넘은때요. 더구나 베개를 같이 한 자리에서라 응당 남자에게 귀염받는 女人으로서는 아양과 응석이 범으러저서 나오는 하나의 어엽분 발작인 동시에 가장 안타까운 심정의 하소이기도 한 것임니다.
『그야 사랑하다 뿐이겟소. 그런데 새삼스럽게 물어보는 것이 도리혀 정소하지 않소?』

한 달 동안이나 이 벤벤치 못한 싸라리멘인 남편을 위해서 밥장수 노릇을 해준 그 안해가 너무도 가엽슨 생각이 나서 그 남편은 안해의 대답을 듯기 전 눈물이 핑 돈 눈을 감추느라고 나무 한 가치 없는 헷간 쪽으로 視線을 돌녓습니다.

이따위 하치않은 일노 말미암아서 저질너진 그 남편과 그 안해의 가슴에 압흠이란 덩어리가 그들의 살님사리 복판에다가 씨가 되어 백혀 가지고 뿌리를 뻣고 줄거리가 올노오고 가지가 솟고 닢이 피고 꽃이 떠러지고 열매가 맺는데 그 열매의 일흠은 이혼이란 차디찬 과실이 되여 버리고 마는 것임니다.
