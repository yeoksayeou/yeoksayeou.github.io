Profiles of Giants Planning a Comeback
						By Ch'angnang Kaekki

**Mr. Sin Sŏgu, Who Says One Must Now Prepare a Million Won to Step Forward**

Kanch'ang Sin Sŏgu (干蒼 申錫雨) has already been lying low for four years. How could such a spirited, ambitious, and fiery personality endure confinement within his gates for four long springs and autumns? Mr. Sin claims he threw away 400,000 Won, while others say it was 100,000 Won, a huge sum, for the sake of the Chosŏn Ilbo <76> and dedicated 10 years to the press world, only to then advocate the "Three Nos" principle—not listening, not speaking, not seeing—and build a wall against the world, leading to the present day. However, as worldly affairs have become complicated, simmering internally and with harsh storms brewing externally, people longing for influential figures gather here and there, occasionally speaking of Kanch'ang. So, is Mr. Sin quietly plotting a comeback (捲土重來) behind closed gates, as rumored among people these days? And is the era of Sin Sŏgu about to return?

To solve this riddle, on a scorching mid-summer day, this reporter visited Mr. Sin's house in Sup'yojŏng (水標町).

"Confining yourself behind closed doors (杜門幽居) surely isn't your true intention; you've been putting on a facade to the world for too long, making your blood boil. It seems the time for your return to the public stage has come. Are you still waiting?" I fired the first shot. With a broad smile breaking on his large-framed, boyish face, he replied:

"To try my hand at worldly affairs again, two kinds of preparations must be complete, but I haven't achieved even one yet. The first is money. Things are different now; whereas in the past one might think of 100,000 Won as business capital, now it's hard to proceed without having about 1,000,000 Won. While grasping a million Won in one's lifetime might not be such a difficult (難) task for a man, it's not easy either."

"So, if your goal is one million Won, do you currently have about half, say four or five hundred thousand Won?"

"It would be nice if I had that much, but I've only just managed to settle all past debts. My debt originally amounted to about three hundred sixty or seventy thousand Won, and in the roughly four years since leaving the newspaper company, I had no peaceful days (寧日) settling this. Through various negotiations (담판), the three hundred sixty or seventy thousand Won was reduced to a total of about 250,000 Won. During that time, I already settled about 100,000 Won of minor personal debts, and one large debt to the Siksan Bank (殖産銀行 - Chosen Industrial Bank) remained, which is now being settled. It should be completely finished within this month."

"When it's finished, how much will be left in total?"

"Well, perhaps tens of thousands of Won will be left. Enough to live on."

"Then, financially, you've already been resurrected, haven't you?"

"I've barely escaped being a debtor (빗쟁이)." he said, smiling sadly.

"Money preparation aside, what else—"

"Next, one needs to know something. <77> In these times when the world situation is changing so drastically, having turned my back on worldly affairs (世事) for several years, am I not already a man behind the times (時代遲)? Therefore, I am trying to learn a bit about current issues, see things, cultivate my insufficient self, clear my mind, and read books."

"Couldn't you do that after returning to the public stage?"

"That doesn't work well. During the 10 years I was tied to the Chosŏn Ilbo, between internal administration, external relations, financial preparations, etc., I truly had no spare time. For 10 years, though I always had access to train passes, I couldn't even once visit Kŭmgangsan (금강산), which is only a day or two trip away. If I were to step out again, I would have to do so with the resolve and premise that there would be absolutely no time for studying, reading, or improving my health. If that's the case, fortunately, I am idle now. I intend to use this time for repeated cultivation of both mind and body."

"If so, how many years are you planning for?"

"Well, now that the debt (負債) is settled and the worries of many years (永年) have been washed away, I expect to have more mental leeway from now on. That would require about four years. The past wasn't recuperation. From now on, I will truly engage in recuperation and preparation."

"So, that makes eight years in total?"

"Is eight years too long? Even if I wait another four years, I'll only be 44 or 45. If I'm starting anew, would I change my career path a second time? The business I take up again now must be the final business of my life (終生). So, these days I'm thinking like this: I'm considering buying a thatched cottage somewhere quiet, about several tens of ri from Sŏul, reachable in about an hour by car, and spending my mornings and evenings strolling amidst pleasant natural scenery, perhaps doing some reading (看書) I wish to do."

At this, knowing that the rumors of "comeback, comeback" (권토중래, 捲土重來) were not unfounded, I fired the second shot.

"Will the business you undertake this time be a newspaper company again? Isn't the newspaper business something anyone ambitious would pursue, being glamorous and masculine, targeting millions, even tens of millions of people?"

"Indeed, it is. Especially in our current situation, the newspaper business is the most effective and brilliant among all spiritual and cultural enterprises. However, I will never set foot (投足) in the newspaper world again."

"Why is that?"

"If I do something, I've decided to do a different business."

"A different business, you say?"

"I'd rather not say yet."

"As a veteran campaigner (老鬪將) of 10 years in the newspaper world, <78> if you were to apply that experience and knowledge now, wouldn't you be able to produce a superior newspaper with less money than others?"

"That may be true. It's not necessarily required for someone running a newspaper to possess great knowledge. One can invite and gather many scholars or prominent commentators (論客). However, it's my nature (천성) that I cannot keep anything hidden in my heart, and I tend to speak bluntly even things others dislike, and I don't hesitate to wield my pen forcefully! Considering various points, this and that, I don't see newspapers as the business I would choose as my final, lifelong endeavor." he says. This seems likely influenced by his reflections on the unfolding external situation and the recent state of affairs within the press world. From the landscaped mound (築山) lush with flowers and plants in the garden before the window, a cool breeze, making one forget the summer heat, was pouring through the stones (石?). <79>

**Mr. Sin Hŭngu, Engaged in Reading and Contemplation with "Positive Faith" on His Mind**

Passing through the dense woods where many Western-style houses (문화주택) are nestled in Sajik-tong (社稷洞), one eventually comes upon a tasteful semi-Western style house (半洋製). Crossing the garden of this house, I sat facing the master of the house, Mr. Sin Hŭngu (申興雨), in a bright and clean American-style (亞米利加式) Western drawing room.
Mr. Sin resigned from his position as General Secretary of the Central YMCA Federation (중앙기독교청년회 연합회) this past April. Since then, his planned trip abroad has been halted, and it's curious what he has been doing to pass the time recently.

"Uh- the plan to go to America (미국) was halted because the provincial governor declared it 'Difficult to deliberate' (詮議し難し - Japanese phrase), and the travel permit was denied (不許可). So, there was nothing I could do but give it up. Also, among my dear friends who care for me, some advised against going, saying that going abroad and returning would take time, and upon return, work would have to start <79> from scratch, only delaying the business. So, perhaps it's better not to go. My feeling (심경) now is that it's fine whether I go or not; it's just like that."

"Then, will you push forward more actively with the 'Positive Faith' (積極信仰) you were engaged in for a while?"

Taking off and wiping his strong prescription glasses, Mr. Sin pondered for a moment, then said:
"Well, that's a good thing too. Originally, the reason for using the characters for 'Positive' (積極) was that many people think of religion as something where one should refrain from doing things and just remain still, rather than actively doing something. Thus, even if a reform movement were started in Christianity today, it would only encounter disputes, so it's better to just stay still. Since the Great War (大戰 - WWI), our psychology tends towards the negative (消極). Therefore, we used the characters for 'Positive' to signify abandoning the idea of just staying still.
As you know, the trend among the world's youth at the end of the 19th century was burgeoning in all directions, reaching its peak during the Great War. After the Great War, it shed its reformist colors and has been actively advancing in various ways. At such a time, our Christianity should not flow only in a static direction. Therefore, believing that gathering like-minded individuals (分子) was the top priority, the Five Principles of Positive Faith came into being. The desire to strive for its proper growth is the same now as it was then."

Through the banana trees (芭蕉나무) of the southern land in the courtyard, a cool breeze constantly flows, sweeping through the room.

"Now that you've found an opportunity for recuperation (精養), why not compile into writings all that you've seen—the people, landscapes, and civilizations—during your numerous travels (遊歷) to various foreign countries over the past 10-plus years?"

"Well, I do have such thoughts. Whenever I traveled abroad, I jotted down fragmentary notes, but I've never systematically organized them. It would be nice if I could do it during this opportunity. However, that task is not currently on my program (푸로그람). If I have time, I'll do it this year if possible, if not this year, then next year, if not next year, then the year after."

"Whether it's Kagawa Toyohiko (賀川豊彦), Yamamuro Gunpei (山室軍平), or Dr. Nitobe (新渡戶博士), whenever any religious figure returns from abroad, they invariably publish a book or two, but in Chosŏn (조선), there are too many scholars without publications, too many religious figures without publications. I hope you will pioneer that field. Furthermore, even if not necessarily about foreign countries, you must have ascended and descended the pulpit thousands of times; wouldn't collecting those sermons also be good?"
"That's right. Though unworthy, since I am not currently responsible for any position, I have decided to try doing my own work."

"What field are you reading in these days?" <80>
"Mainly history, philosophy, and religion. Moreover, among these, I have started translating something and make efforts towards that daily."

Finally, as I came because of the article's theme, I couldn't help but fire the final shot.
"Hasn't the time come for you to re-enter the public stage?"

"Re-enter? Now, I intend to try doing something myself. No matter how large the institution or how important the position one holds, the day inevitably comes in life when one must leave that position. Even if one has strived for 10 years, it ultimately amounts to running errands for others. There is nothing that is one's own work. This is a general rule (通則) applicable not only to Christianity but to any society."
"Therefore, why not step forward with a venture centered on yourself, teacher?"

"In the current situation in Chosŏn, organizational movements are difficult. Therefore, for now, the urgent task (급무) is to convey the truth I believe is right (올타는) to others and welcome like-minded friends (동무); it is, so to speak, a time for a spiritual movement."

"What about the Positive Faith Group (積極信仰團)?"

"That too was likely intended as a research club (硏究俱樂部) seeking the union of hearts and minds. It must have been a gathering of apostles of truth, wondering how best to spread the truth of Christ (基督) into the hearts of young people."
"How long will it take for the Positive Group (積極團) to become what you envision?"

To this question, he smiled without answering (笑而不答). The result of my sounding out (打診) today suggested that the direction of his comeback (권토중래) seems different from before (녜전), and the timing appears yet undecided (末定).
(July 17th, 1:00 PM). <81>

**Summary**

This article features interviews with two prominent Korean figures, Sin Sŏgu (formerly associated with the Chosŏn Ilbo) and Sin Hŭngu (formerly of the YMCA), discussing their plans after periods of withdrawal from public life in 1935. Sin Sŏgu emphasizes the need for significant financial resources (1 million Won) and a period of study (4 years) before potentially returning, hinting he might not re-enter the newspaper business due to its constraints and his own nature. Sin Hŭngu, whose travel plans were thwarted by authorities, focuses on his "Positive Faith" movement, personal study (history, philosophy, religion), writing, and translation, suggesting a shift from large organizational roles towards influencing individuals spiritually and intellectually rather than through direct organizational action.

Historically, the article offers insights into the perceived financial scale needed for major public endeavors in colonial Korea (1 million Won), the personal reflections and desire for self-cultivation among intellectuals/leaders after setbacks, and the potential shifts in strategy—from institution-building or mass media to more personal, preparatory, or spiritually focused work. The mention of travel restrictions ("Difficult to deliberate" by the governor) and the cautious discussion around future plans subtly reflect the political atmosphere under Japanese rule. The context surrounding the Chosŏn Ilbo and the YMCA's "Positive Faith" movement are also noteworthy for historians of the period.

捲土重來를 策하는 巨人群像
						滄浪客記
이제부터는 나서자면 百萬圓臺 準備잇서야 한다는 申錫雨氏
干蒼 申錫雨씨는 벌서 雌伏四個年. 그러케 패기 만코 野心勃勃하고 불덩이가치 격하기 쉬운 성격으로 엇더케 장장 4春秋나 門內에 幽居할 수 잇섯뜬고. 씨는 자칭 40만원, 타칭 10만원 하는 거금을 조선일보를 위하야 던지엇고＜76＞ 또 언론계에 10년을 헌신하여 오다가 다시 不聽, 不語, 不見의 三不主義를 표방하고 세상과 담을 싸어 오늘에 이르럿다. 그러나 世事가 복잡하여저 안으로 끌코 밧갓으론 눈바래가 심한 오늘이 되엇스매 인물을 그리워하는 世人들 모여 안진 자리자리에서 각금 干蒼 이약이를 하게 되엇다. 그러면 요지음 世人들 소문모양으로 씨는 고요히 대문을 닷고 안저 捲土重來를 策하고 잇는가, 또 장차 申錫雨 시대는 다시 오려하고 잇는가.
이 수수걱기를 풀고저 삼복 여름날 기자는 水標町 씨의 집을 찻다.

「杜門幽居가 본심이 아니실 터인데 피를 썩혀가며 너무 오래 세상에 대하야 위장을 하고 게심니다. 인제 재출마의 시기가 왓다고 보아지는데 아직도 대기중임니까」
하고 一矢를 發하니 巨軀童顔에 파안일소하며
「다시 세상일 좀 하여 보자면 두가지 준비가 다 되여야 하겟는데 나는 아직 한가지 준비도 이루지 못하고 잇슴니다. 첫재는 돈인데 지금은 전과도 달너 녯날에 사업자금으로 10만원 대를 생각하든 것이면 지금은 100만원대를 가지지 안코는 하여 나가기 어렵슴니다. 그런데 男兒 一代에 100만원쯤 쥐는 것이 그러케 難사업도 아닐 것이나 그러나 그러타고 또 쉬운 일도 아니지요.」
「그러면 100만원을 목표 잡는다면 지금은 한 절반 사오십만원은 되어 잇슴니까.」

『積極信仰」을 머리에 언고 讀書·思索 中의申興雨氏

社稷洞 문화주택이 만히 드러안진 鬱密한 숩 사이를 지나 한참 가노라면 蕭洒한 半洋製집 한 채가 나진다. 나는 이 집 정원을 지나 亞米利加式으로 명랑하고 깨긋하게 차린 양옥 응접실에서 이 집 주인공 申興雨씨와 마조 안젓다.
씨가 중앙기독교청년회 연합회 총무의 직을 辭한지가 今春 4월이라 이래 외국가겟다든 거름도 멈추고 요지음 무엇으로 소일하는지 궁금하다.
「어- 미국가려고 하든 일은 도지사로부터 「詮議し難し」라고 하야 여행권이 不許可 되엇기에 엇지할 도리 업서 그만 두엇지요. 또 나를 앗기는 고마운 동무들 중에는 외국갓다가 오느라면 세월이 걸닐 터인데 도라와서도 일은 처음부터 시작하여야＜79＞ 하겟스니 사업만 느저질터이니 차라리 가지말난 분도 잇서요. 나의 심경은 이제는 가도 조코, 아니가도 조코 그저 그러웨다 그려.」
「그러면 한동안 착수하시든 積極信仰」을 끄을고 좀더 활발하게 나가겟슴니까.」
强度의 안경을 버서 닥그며 씨는 한참 沈思하더니
「글세요, 그도 조흔 일이지요. 원래 積極이란 문자를 쓴 뜻은 종교에 대하야 무엇이나 하자는 것보다 하지 말고 가만 잇자는 것으로 생각하는 이가 만슴니다. 그래서 오늘 기독교라도 개혁운동을 이르킨다면 시비만 맛날 것이니 차라리 그저 가만 잇자 그뿐外라 大戰 이후로 우리의 심리가 消極으로 작고 쏠려짐니다. 그러니까 우리는 가만 잇자는 생각을 버리자는 의미에서 積極 문자를 갓다가 쓴 것이지요.
원래 아시다십히 19세기 말엽에 세계 청년들 사조는 온갓 방면으로 발흥하여 大戰때에 와서 全盛하엿다가 大戰이후에 이르러는 개량주의적 色彩을 버리고 무엇이든 적극적으로 약진하고 잇는터가 아님니까. 이때에 잇서 우리 기독교도 靜적 방면으로만 흘너서는 안 되겟스니 그러자면 뜻을 가치하는 分子끼리 모히는 것이 급선무라 하여 積極信仰 5개조가 생겨난 것이지요. 압흐로 이것을 잘 자라가게 힘쓰고저 함은 그때나 지금이나 마찬가지지요.」

들압 남국의 芭蕉나무 속으로 서늘한 바람이 항상 흘너 드러와 방안을 씨처준다.
「이번에 틈을 어더 精養하는 기회를 만낫스니 그동안 10여년 내로 여러번 해외 각국을 遊歷하며 인물과 산수와 문명들을 보고오신 것을 모다모다 저술이나 하시지요.」
「글세요 그러한 생각도 잇서요. 늘 외지로 도라다닐 때마다 단편적으로 적어 모아노은 것은 잇섯스나 언제 한 번 체계 잇게 만들어 본 적이 업는데 이번 기회에 할 수 잇게 되엇스면 조켓서요. 그러나 지금은 푸로그람에 그 일은 업서요. 틈이 잇는대로 금년에 못 되면 明年에 明年에 못 되면 再明年에 하기로 하지요.」
「賀川豊彦이나 山室軍平이나 新渡戶博士나 모든 종교가 치고 외국에 갓다오면 반드시 책 한두 개는 나오는데 조선서는 저서 업는 학자, 저서 업는 종교가들이 너무 만허요. 그 방면을 개척하여 노키를 바라며 더욱 반드시 외국에 관한 것이 아니라 하여도, 그동안 강단에 수천 次 오르고 내렷슬 터이니 그를 모으는 것도 조치 안켓서요.
「다 올해요. 변변치 못하나마 그런 것은 지금 내 몸이 책임진 곳이 업스니까, 내 자신의 일을 하여보기로 결심햇습니다.」

「요지간은 무슨 방면의 독서를 함니까.」＜80＞
「주로 역사와 철학과 종교 방면이외다. 더구나 그 중에는 번역도 시작한 것이 잇서 매일 그 노력도 함니다.」

마즈막으로 표제의 기사 때문에 차저온 터인지라 최후의 一矢를 發하지 안을 수 업섯다.
「이제는 재 출마할 때가 오지 안엇스리까.」
「재 출마? 이제는 나 스사로가 무엇이던 하여보랴고 하겟슴니다. 아모리 큰 기관에서 아모리 중요한 자리에 안저 일한다 할지라도 어느 날은 그 자리를 떠날 날이 인생에게는 오는 법이외다. 10년을 애썻다 해도 그는 결국 남의 심부름한 일이지요. 내 자신의 일은 아모 것도 업지요. 이것은 비단 기독교 뿐 안이라 아모 사회에나 마찬가지 通則일 것이외다.」 「그러기에 그러면 선생 중심의 사업을 들고 나서지요.」
「지금 조선형편이 조직운동은 어럽슴니다. 그러기에 아직은 내가 올타는 진리를 남에게 전하여 가튼 동무를 마지자 하는 것이 급무요 말하면 정신운동의 시기지요.」
「積極信仰團은요?」
「그도 마음과 마음의 결합을 꾀하는 硏究俱樂部이엿슬 것이지요. 엇더케 하면 청년들 가슴에 基督의 진리를 펴저줄가, 하는 진리의 사도의 회합이엿슬 것이이지요.」 「압흐로 얼마나 가면 선생이 생각하신대로 積極團이 될 것이오니까.」
이 말에는 笑而不答 하엿다. 내가 오늘 打診 결과는 씨의 권토중래의 방면은 녜전보다는 다른 곳인 듯 하엿고 그 시기는 아직 末定인 듯 하엿다.
