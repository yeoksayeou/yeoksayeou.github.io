A Nine-Year Record of Wandering Between Life and Death - My Prison Memoir
						Yi Su-tak

May 21st, Shōwa 2 (1927)!
On this day cursed and filled with resentment!
Accused of having poisoned and killed my father (置毒殺父), the heavens spun, my hair stood on end (毛骨), and merely calling for my father like a madman, I became confined in the cold, unconvicted detainee cell of the prosecutor's office. How could one not say that the Way of Heaven (天道) is indifferent!

Every day, I wanted to kill myself, but if I were to die bearing a stigma so horrifying even to hear, and so utterly unjust, who after my death would clear me of this original sin, and who would carry on my father's legacy (父業)? No, wouldn't people throughout the world rather say, "They say Yi Su-tak killed his father! What a wicked man! Can there really be such a person in the world!" Isn't it natural that they would heap unjust slander and insults upon me for countless generations? When such thoughts surged in my chest, I clutched my heart, writhed, and cried my fill.

Each time, biting down on the resentment and sorrow welling up amidst the grinding of my teeth (切齒), I resolved: If I cannot quickly shed this original sin and go out to avenge myself against those scoundrels, how can I face my departed father in the afterlife (地下)! This determination frosted my bones more than once or twice.

March 19th, Taishō 13 (1924) (Lunar Feb 14th), my father, who was lying ill with a cold, passed away suddenly on the 25th of that month (Lunar Feb 20th) at 2 AM, without recovering even after taking three packets of medicine (Gami Yangwi-tang 加味養胃湯) prepared by his son, however ignorant and immature Su-tak may be. As the waning crescent moon leaned over the western mountain and the clear cry of the first rooster spread through the night sky of this village, he departed on the eternal road from which he could never return. Who could have dreamt that this final farewell <130> would become the thread that spun out a life of confinement (囹圄) for Yi Su-tak, trapped behind iron bars through nine springs and autumns, nine years filled with sorrow!
The more I think about it, the more my heart feels like it's breaking.
However, thinking about it, on one hand, it was my fault for being ignorant and not astute, and also because of the rotten notion of being a son born of a concubine (庶子)!
I cry out again; I have now keenly felt once more that the corrupt custom passed down to this day, the principle of keeping a concubine while having a wife (有妻蓄妾), was the great cause that created my tragedy today.
If only I hadn't been a *sŏja* with a concubine mother, even my stepmother, Cho Kŏn-sik (趙健値), however stubborn and rich in jealousy, wouldn't have been so deceived by villains greedy for gold, and wouldn't have done this to me, the son of Yi Kŏn-ho (李健鎬), even if born of a concubine!
Before sending curses and the blade of revenge towards the demonic hands (魔手) fiercely vying like a pack of wolves over my father's inheritance, I could only lament my own misfortune first.
"Oh, unfortunate one in the world, thy name is *sŏja*!"
However, I am perhaps the most unfortunate among the unfortunate.
Born as the only son of a millionaire, receiving my father's special affection all over my body, due to my father's stubborn insistence, I started reading the Thousand Character Classic (千字文) at age seven and only attended a village school (書堂) until seventeen, never even seeing a modern school. Isn't it natural that I was foolish and ignorant?
Even well past the age of 20, I knew nothing of the world, only receiving my father's affection, and coaxed by friends, I just wandered around playing, wasting about ten thousand won and naturally causing my parents worry. This was all the fault of old-fashioned and stubborn parents. I realized many times that the pitiful person in the world must be the ignorant one.
All these conditions led me to allow villains like Cho In-ok,
O Kyŏng-jae, Yu T'ae-jin, and about ten others to squander my father's inheritance. No matter that she was a concubine! No matter that I was a *sŏja*! No matter how ignorant I was! How could any person with sense have just remained silent!

Recalling these bitter facts of the past, the more I do, the more my fists clench and my teeth grind!
"You wicked and lawless scoundrels of the world! No matter how much you love gold, to accuse me even of killing my father, to try and snatch gold even by taking <131> a person's life...!" At such times, unbeknownst to me, hot tears of blood, steeped in curses and resentment, would well up around my eyes and trickle down the hollows of my pale, gaunt cheeks.
Moreover, thinking of my mother and wife (Kim Yŏng-ja, 金英子), who had lived so luxuriously and beloved, now suffering unjustly in confinement (囹圄) just like me, my heart felt even more like tearing apart.
"Mother, should I die? Or should I live?... People will call me a patricide! What should I do about this... Yes!" Like this, I buried my face in my mother's bosom and wept my fill. I cried out loud!
"Hey! Su-tak! Why on earth talk about dying! Does it make sense to just die bearing that unjust and resentful accusation...? You must live, even if you have to clench your teeth! Yes, live no matter what! You must avenge yourself against those scoundrels and die only after clearing your resentful stigma! Otherwise, know that even your father in the underworld will frown!" Mother gripped my hands tightly.
Waking up, it wasn't reality but a dream.
It wasn't just once or twice that I had such dreams in my weary sleep.
When I first started prison life, it was still quite chilly and cold as the chill hadn't fully left, but during the passing days, spring seems to have already arrived in the outside world. Peeking out through the narrow southern iron bars, avoiding the guard's eyes, I see red and yellow flowers blooming abundantly in the small flowerbed, and the warm air, even through the narrow window, gently caresses the prisoner's heart! Hearing the chirping of birds from somewhere occasionally, why does this world and that place, separated only by a wall close at hand, feel so distant and longed for? How stifling must my heart be now, as a young man in the prime of life, unable to fully drink in the fragrant light of spring in nature!

Time flows like water--.
While continuing this monotonous life day after day, the bright autumn moon already hangs beyond the iron bars of my cell. As days passed and months changed since I came in here, spring passed, the summer that felt like scorching flesh passed, and it seems late autumn, where only a desolate wind seeps in, has arrived.
During this time, how many times did I clench my teeth and shoot arrows of curses and resentment at the wicked people of the world, and clutch my resentment-filled chest! Also, how many times did I lament the heartlessness of the world <132> and the indifference of Heaven! Looking back, all past events just seem like a dream.
During this time, perhaps there was humanity even in this prison cell, as various people offered words of comfort, while some regarded me with suspicious eyes.
I grew to dislike the communal cell where many people stayed. I disliked talking with various people altogether! I pleaded with the guards and the chief guard many times, but they ultimately did not grant me a solitary cell.
Meanwhile, countless people came and went. People leaving after a month, people becoming convicted prisoners after several months, people happy after receiving light sentences, people boasting about coming to this prison, as if visiting a relative's house, for the umpteenth time – I gradually came to dislike all these people. Especially when looking at the form of a "death row inmate" who received "life imprisonment" or "death penalty" and will soon become dew on the "guillotine" without appeal, I felt as if I myself would soon tread the same path of fate. The "death row inmate's" expressionless, senseless pale face, the trembling body, the eyes soaked in trepidation and fear, felt exactly like my own reflection. Instantly! I found myself trembling uncontrollably with trepidation and fear, and tears blurred my vision.
Each time, the final moment of the "death row inmate" going to the gallows would come to my mind.
I shook my head many times trying to forget this dreadful thought.
"Indeed, a person's life is like that of a fly! Death is indeed so close! Even if one lives a full seventy years, dying is a sad thing, but for a body in the fresh bloom of youth like a flower bud to offer its life to the gallows in one morning! Ah! Is the world truly so dreamlike and futile?"
All that night, I couldn't close my eyes, and endless thoughts wandered aimlessly in my hazy mind!
Some time after that, I received a verdict of "death penalty" from the Kyŏngsŏng District Court under the terrifying charge of "patricide (弑父犯)". In that instant, I couldn't see anything before my eyes! What use would begging be!
What use would my honest confession, knotted with resentment, be! The heavy iron hammer of the law mercilessly struck my head down. How could one not say Heaven is indifferent!
"Still, if the gods of heaven and earth are watching over me, they wouldn't kill this clean, innocent Yi Su-tak..."
That night, lying prone in a corner of the dark cell, <133> unaware of the deepening night, I sobbed with feeling.
"Even if it goes to the third trial (三審), if the law and the universal principle of the world is to kill the guilty and save the innocent, there must still be a way to live!"
Whenever my heart burned and sorrow choked me, I always cried out like this many times.
Thinking about how much my mother, wife, and young child, who used to live so luxuriously, must be suffering now, sending me food from outside ("사식") like this! Even though they are in the outside world, their pale faces rise one by one in my mind!
My mother, staring blankly at my wrists bruised blue by the iron handcuffs, couldn't say a single word to me and spent the fleeting moments of the visit just crying. In that interval, tears just rolled down my cheeks, drop by drop!
Still, in this life, reading books and visits from my mother and family provide some comfort.
Each time, I feel even more sorry for my family who must live on, enduring unspeakable hardships and public criticism!

One year, two years, spring with blooming flowers, autumn with bright moon, sweltering summer, winter with falling snow, repeated again and again, how many times? I received the "death penalty" even at the appeal court (覆審) and, without delay, came to await the trial date at the third instance (三審).
During that time, I spent eight years wearing iron handcuffs. Could I have slept comfortably even once? Could I have eaten peacefully even once! Even now, blue marks are imprinted on my wrists.
Also, it wasn't just once or twice that I was beaten by harsh guards and kicked, nor was it just once or twice that my hands and feet froze in the cold winter.
Writing down such trivial matters would be endless, so I will stop.
It was the night before the third trial (三審) was to open.
"Su-tak! Tomorrow you will be found innocent and released. Don't worry about anything. When you go out into the world, try not to do bad things to people..."
That night too, I heard my deceased father's words in a dream. If only this wasn't a dream but reality-- My heart pounded, and I felt terribly anxious.
Still, if Heaven is not indifferent, it wouldn't punish me! On the other hand, I always held onto this thought.
Finally, the morning of the trial day arrived, and unable to calm my mind, I stood in the courtroom.
"Not guilty-" Although these words clearly came from the judge's mouth, I couldn't tell if it was a dream or reality. My mind went blank, and it felt like the ground was spinning. No matter how I thought about it, it only felt like a dream, not reality.
I pinched myself several times. It was undeniably real! My whole body just trembled uncontrollably!
Ah! Thinking back, it was a precarious situation and a chilling affair!
Spring winds, autumn rains! After suffering under a false accusation for nine years, if things had gone slightly wrong, I would have walked the path of a resentful death. Perhaps with the help of my deceased father's spirit, I found the path of "life" at the crossroads of "life" and "death". How could this not be the just conclusion of human morality! (End) <134>

**Summary**

This harrowing memoir recounts the nine years Yi Su-tak spent in prison, from 1927 to 1935, after being falsely accused of poisoning his father. He describes his initial shock, despair, thoughts of suicide, and eventual resolve to survive and clear his name. Yi reflects on the perceived injustices contributing to his plight: being the son of a concubine (*sŏja*), his lack of modern education (having only attended a traditional *sŏdang*), and the greed of relatives and associates who allegedly framed him for his inheritance. He details the harsh realities of prison life, the emotional toll of seeing other prisoners (especially those condemned to death), the pain caused to his family, and the brief solace found in reading and family visits. After receiving the death sentence twice, he was finally acquitted in his third trial, an outcome he attributes possibly to his father's spirit and the ultimate triumph of justice.

For historians, this article offers a poignant first-person perspective on the colonial Korean legal system's fallibility (or susceptibility to manipulation), the brutal conditions within prisons, and the profound social discrimination faced by *sŏja* even within wealthy families. It vividly illustrates the personal cost of patriarchal traditions like concubinage and limited education access, framing them as root causes of individual tragedy within the broader context of colonial society.

生死彷徨九年記, -나의 獄中記-
						李洙倬
소화2년 5월 21일!
이 저주에 떨니고 원한에 사모치는 이날!
치녹살부(置毒殺父)하엿다는 고소(告訴)를 당한 나는 천지가 아득하고 모골(毛骨)이 송연하여 다만 미친듯이 아버지를 부르고 차듸찬 검사국 미결감방에 갓친 몸이 되엿사오니 엇지 천도(天道)가 무심타 안 하오릿가!
매일가치 자결도 하고 십헛스나 내가 듯기에도 끔찍끔찍한 억울하기 짝이 업는 루명을 쓰고 죽는다면 나 죽은 뒤에라도 어느 누가 이 원죄를 푸러주며 또 어느 누가 부업(父業)을 이을것인가? 안이 온 세상사람들은 도로혀 나를 「리수탁이는 아비를 죽엿다지! 천하에 고약한 놈이군! 세상에 그런 놈도 잇담!」 이러케 천추 만대를 내려가면서 억울한 비방과 모욕을 퍼부을 것이 당연한 일이 안이냐? 이런 생각이 나의 가슴에 치미러 올나올 때에는 가슴을 쥐여뜨드며 몸부럼을 치며 슬컷 울어도 보앗슴니다.
그럴때마다 나는 절치(切齒)속에 솟아오르는 원한과 서름을 깨물면서 이 원죄를 하로밧비 벗고 나가 그놈들의 원수를 갑지 못하면 어찌 시하(地下)에 도라간 망부를 대할 것인가! 이런 결심이 나의 뼈속에 서리여 올나온 때도 한두 번이 안이엿슴니다.

대정 13년 3월 19일(음2월 14일) 감긔로 누우신 아부님이 다만 미런하고 철업는 수탁이라 할지라도 자식의 지어온 약 세첩(加味養胃湯)을 잡수시고도 소생하실 줄을 몰으시고 무심키도 급기야 그 달 25일(음2월 20일) 오전 두시, 이즈러진 쪼각달이 서산에 기우려저 잇고 교교한 첫닭 우름소리가 이 마을의 밤하날에 퍼질 때, 한번 가시면 다시 도라오지못할 영원의 길을 떠나시고야 말앗스니 이 마지막 하직이 장차의 리수탁으로＜130＞ 하여곰 춘풍추우 구개성상의 한만흔 세월을 철창속에 갓겨서 영오의 생활을 만들어낼 실마리로 될 줄이야 엇지 꿈인들 꾸엿스리요!
생각하면 생각할수록 가슴이 쪼개지는 것 갓슴니다.
그러나 생각하면 한긋흐로는 나의 무식함과 똑똑치 못한 탓이엿고, 또한 첩의 몸에서 난 서자(庶子)라는 썩은 관렴때문이엿지요!
나는 다시금 부르짓슴니다, 오늘날까지 내려오는 썩어질 관습인, 유처축첩(有妻蓄妾) 주의가 나의 오늘날 이 비극을 만드러내고야만 큰 원인임을 이제 다시금 절실이 늣겻슴니다.
내가 만약 소실의 어머니를 가진 서자(庶子)가 안이엿드라도 아무리 고집과 질투심이 풍부한 큰어머니 조건식(趙健値)이라 하드라도 이러케까지 황금을 노리는 악당들에게 속아너머가, 아무리 첩의 몸에서 난 아들이라 할지라도 리건호(李健鎬)의 자식인 나의게 이러케까지는 하지 안헛슬것을!
나는 아버님의 유산을 보고 이리떼 모양으로 무섭게 날뛰는 마수들에게 저주와 복수의 칼날을 보내기 전에 먼저 내 자신의 불행함을 뉘우칠 뿐이엿슴니다.
