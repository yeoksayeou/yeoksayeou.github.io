**Body Text Translation:**
Techniques of Seduction at the Beach

1
There are many men who strike up a conversation by pretending to have mistaken you for someone else. This is a method most commonly used.
Saying something like,
"Aren't you Mr./Ms. XX's younger sister?"
There are many men who persistently approach like this.
In such cases,
there is no need to even say,
"N-no."
Just furrow your brow deeply and pretend not to see him.
If you carelessly open your mouth, even if nothing happens right then and there, the next day or so,
he might say something like,
"I was terribly rude yesterday,"
and somehow start a conversation... you really can't let your guard down.

2
When you are trying to swim freely and refreshingly, letting your guard down, <188> some shameless fellow intentionally follows you, kicking up water with a crawl stroke, bumps into you, and says,
"Ah- excuse me."
A single apology should be enough, but then when he encounters you again later, he approaches, saying,
"Ah, earlier was truly...!"
Then he starts making small talk, saying things like, "You swim quite well," and so on. With such characters, pretending not to see them at all is the best policy.

3
These types of men tend to be rather ill-mannered. Those with truly good manners usually do not approach so persistently—.
Seeing women nearby, they somehow enter the water and just grab and pull their legs. Usually, one ends up swallowing a mouthful of water at this time. Then they make various <189> excuses, saying they mistook you for someone else... It's a situation where you absolutely cannot swim with your guard down.

4
When a girl is alone and lonely with no friends to play with, men intentionally pass by in front of her in boats (뽀-드). If she looks blankly without any thought, they invite her,
"Would you like a ride?"
In such cases, of course, one should not agree, but one should also refrain from acting shy or blushing. If you show such an attitude, you are bound to get caught somehow.
Showing demureness is, above all, detrimental.

5
On the wide, white sandy beach, they set up a portable (포-타불) gramophone and play it, constantly looking around as if to say, "Look at this," or "Listen to this sound." If you carelessly let your guard down and senselessly start singing along to the tune, you have already fallen 30 percent into the opponent's evil hands (?).
This misuse of music! Women who like songs should be doubly cautious.

6
Gazing at the waves rolling in gently over the blue horizon, when three men try to entice one girl to play a game of mahjong (마-쨩) on the beach, a woman who enjoys mahjong is bound to have her heart swayed. Without even realizing her own body is being wagered (賭). -Isn't that right?

7
At summer resorts, if you happen to rent neighboring houses, you somehow get to know each other before long.
However, the longest stay is barely two months.
One should not become friendly in a careless or frivolous manner. When autumn comes, you won't know where they've gone off to. It's simply because he was the young man next door.

8
The type who pretends to be looking for a house.
"Isn't there a house belonging to someone named XX around here?"
Like this, pretending to look for a non-existent house, they strike up a conversation to get acquainted somehow.
Since they approach with considerable politeness, it's hard not to answer, which makes this situation a bit difficult.

9
When the men's and women's changing areas (탈의장) are separated by only a thin plank (板), there might be a woman who carelessly hangs her removed swimsuit near the boundary plank. A bold man hides it, waiting for the woman to say something.
Then, feigning seriousness, he says, "Oh- wasn't this Ms. XX's?" and bows politely two or three times, using it as a means to become friends. There are perhaps even more plausible versions of this type, but—.

10
A man who deliberately walks in front of a woman, drops something, and walks past pretending not to notice.
"Ah- you dropped something."
Around this point, she will probably call out to him.
The idea is that in any situation, if the woman speaks first, there is a seven-tenths (七分) possibility (a 70% chance) for him. <191>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine advises women on navigating unwanted advances ("seduction techniques") from men at seaside resorts. It outlines ten common scenarios, such as men pretending misidentification, feigning accidents while swimming, using props like boats or portable gramophones, inviting women to mahjong, or exploiting proximity in rented summer houses. The primary advice is for women to remain aloof, ignore the advances, avoid showing shyness (which is seen as detrimental), and crucially, not initiate conversation, as this is perceived as granting the man a high chance of success. From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into changing social dynamics and gender interactions in colonial Korea, particularly the emergence of new leisure spaces like public beaches where traditional rules of separation between sexes were being tested. It reflects anxieties about female vulnerability in these modernizing environments and showcases the adoption of Western leisure activities (swimming, gramophones) alongside Asian ones (mahjong), painting a picture of cultural transition and the negotiation of new social norms for women in public spaces.

『XX씨의 누이동생이 안이심니까』
라고 하는 등, 이러케 추군 추군히 걸치는 사내가 만타.
이런 때에는
『아-니요』
라고도 말할 필요가 업다. 거저 잔뜩 골살을 찝흐리고 본체 만체 하여 버릴 것이다.
잘못 입을 열엇다가는 그 자리에서는 그냥 일 업시 지나치드래도, 그 다음 날 쯤 되여서는,
『어제는 대단 실례 갓슴니다』
라는 둥, 어느 사이에 말을 걸기 시작하여서...사실 마음 노흘 수도 업게 된다.

마음을 푸-ㄱ노코 상쾌하게 헤염을＜188＞ 치자면 어느 사이에 염체 업시 구로-ㄹ·스토로-그로 물거품을 일으키며, 우정 따라와서 툭 부듸치면서,
『아-ㅅ 미안함니다』
하고, 한번 변명을 하면 고만인 것을 괘-니 뒤에 다시 맛나서는,
『앗가는 참으로...!』
하며 닥어 슨다. 그리고는 꽤 헤염을 잘 치는데요 라는 둥 이러쿵저러쿵 말을 걸기 시작한다. 이런 작자에게는 아예 못 본체 하는 것이 상책이다.
