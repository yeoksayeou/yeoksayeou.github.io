Section 2: Cough Suppressants (鎭咳劑)

Cough suppressants are drugs that act by paralyzing the cough center to calm coughing. Coughing is originally an indispensable physiological action for expelling sputum (喀痰). However, excessively severe coughing not only causes suffering to the patient but also hinders the healing of lesions (病竈) due to drastic changes in intrathoracic pressure, easily leading to complications like hemoptysis (각혈) and pneumothorax (氣胸). It can also induce insomnia, vomiting, and even the spread of lesions. In such cases, using cough suppressants is advisable. Conversely, for patients with a large amount of sputum that is easily expectorated, cough suppressants are harmful and useless, so it is advisable to avoid them as much as possible.

There are the following three types of cough suppressants:

First is the Belladonna group (莨菪及其制劑), with atropine (アトロピン) as the main component.
Products like Loidesuki (ロㅣドエスキ, 莨菪越幾斯) and Hyoscyamus extract (ヒヨス越幾斯) belong to this group. They are indicated for spasmodic coughs (痘攣性咳嗽), painful coughs, and cases where the patient suffers from violent coughing despite a large amount of sputum (e.g., wet catarrh (濕性加答兒), wet cough (濕性咳嗽)). Also, for severe coughs due to laryngeal diseases, using an inhalant (吸入料) is effective (奏效).<176>

Second is the Morphine group (モルヒネ屬), which includes Morphine (モルヒネ), Pantopon (パントポン) (Japanese-made is Narcopon/ナルコポン), Codeine Phosphate (燐酸コテイン), Fuscojin (フスコヂン), etc. Generally, they are applied for acute catarrh (急性加答兒), when sputum is not abundant (不多), and for painful coughs. However, caution must be exercised as Morphine, Pantopon (Narcopon), etc., have a habituation effect (慣習作用).

Third is the Hydrocyanic acid group (靑酸屬), including Apricot water (杏仁水), Bitter almond water (苦扁桃水), Bakuchi water (バクチ水), etc. However, the cough-suppressing effect of this type of drug is uncertain, so recently, Apricot water is only used as a flavoring agent utilizing its fragrance.

These three types of cough suppressants are generally mixed with expectorants (袪痰劑) mentioned in Section 1, and cases of single use are infrequent.

Section 3: Asthma Treatments (喘息劑)

Asthma therapy comprises two types: therapy during attacks (발작시의 요법) and therapy during intermittent periods (間歇시의 요법).

**Summary**

This 1935 article by Dr. Pak Ch'anghun provides practical medical guidance on treating coughs and asthma. It details the function, types, and uses of cough suppressants (鎭咳劑), including belladonna, morphine derivatives (like codeine and heroin), and hydrocyanic acid preparations, noting potential habituation. It also outlines asthma treatments (喘息劑), differentiating between managing acute attacks (using injections like morphine, atropine, adrenaline, or oral drugs like aspirin) and long-term therapy between attacks (using iodine, endocrine, arsenic, or calcium preparations). Numerous specific prescriptions (處方例) are listed with dosages and indications. For historians, the article offers a snapshot of Western pharmacological practices in colonial Korea, showing the availability and recommended usage of potent drugs, including narcotics. The frequent mention of Japanese drug names (e.g., Narcopon, Pantopon) and the direct incorporation of Japanese particles and grammatical structures (e.g., シ, ニ, ヨリ) into the Korean text underscores the significant influence of Japanese medicine, pharmaceutical industry, and language during this period.

ロㅣドエスキ(莨菪越幾斯, ヒヨス越幾斯 등이
