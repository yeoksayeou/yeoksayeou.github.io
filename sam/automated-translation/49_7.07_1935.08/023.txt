**Body Text Translation:**
My View on the Various Aspects of That Tactic
						Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng

It is not that I have investigated its contents (內容), nor do I view it as an event (事件) important enough to warrant such investigation. However, from the perspective of us third parties, if the reporting by the *Chosŏn Ilbo* is not factual, then the authorities (當局者) of Posŏng College (普成專門) should hold them accountable (問責) and demand a correction (訂正). Yet, the responsible parties at Posŏng College remain silent (沈黙), as if it were true, and the fact that the Alumni Association (校友會) has emerged to dispute (是非) the *Chosŏn Ilbo* seems to indicate a weakness (弱點) on the part of the Posŏng College authorities. The Alumni's sentiment toward their alma mater is nothing more than a form of obligation (義理). They are people who have already entered society and live under different life circumstances (環境); there is no reason why their interests (利害) should always align (一致) with those of their alma mater. Nevertheless, if the Alumni Association voluntarily (自進) stepped forward, we may express respect (敬意) for their sense of obligation, but ultimately, it is merely a single ×××. Evidence of this is the lack of <59> vigor in the Alumni Association's method of protest (抗爭方式), which speaks for itself.

Despite the event escalating (擴大) to this extent, why are the school authorities unable to directly clarify (辦明) the issue or hold anyone accountable? That is the point. Furthermore, the *Chosŏn Ilbo* brought up the Chungang High School (中央高普) issue, criticized (批難) the *Tonga Ilbo*'s reporting on it, and launched a full-scale attack (全面的攻擊) against the Kim Sŏngsu-affiliated organizations (金性洙系機關). Yet, the Chungang High School authorities also say nothing, the *Tonga Ilbo* also says nothing, and their attempt to counter (對抗) the *Chosŏn Ilbo* merely by reporting on the Posŏng College Alumni Association's activities raises the suspicion (疑惑) that there is a weakness somewhere.<60>

**Summary**

The author, Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng, critiques the handling of a public dispute involving the *Chosŏn Ilbo* newspaper and institutions linked to Kim Sŏngsu (Posŏng College, Chungang High School, *Tonga Ilbo*). Chŏng argues that the silence of the official authorities (Posŏng College, Chungang High School, *Tonga Ilbo*) in the face of accusations by the *Chosŏn Ilbo*, and the reliance on the Alumni Association to respond, suggests a weakness or culpability on their part. The author views the Alumni Association's intervention as based on obligation rather than direct interest and considers their response ineffective. Historically, this article is interesting as it reflects the media rivalries (*Chosŏn Ilbo* vs. *Tonga Ilbo*) and institutional politics among prominent Korean organizations during the colonial period. It offers a contemporary commentary on the tactics and perceived weaknesses within the Korean elite establishment associated with Kim Sŏngsu when facing public controversy.
