A Thousand Ri of Scenery in the Western Province - Via Songdo to Paekch'ŏn
						Kim Sŏngmok
Mr. XX!
I arrived at Paekch'ŏn Hot Springs three days later than planned. I will tell you the detailed reasons when I return to the Company (社). To let you know the gist beforehand, it is like this: 1. The weather was unfavorable, and thinking that returning without seeing Kaesŏng, a place of scenic beauty and historic sites, would be like a blind man visiting Namdaemun Gate, I sought out and viewed each one, which is how this delay happened. Leaving behind the complex and noisy Seoul, by the time I reached around Sinch'on, the fragrant scent of wildflowers carried on the breeze and the soft smell of grass made my nostrils flare. Gazing at the green mountains steeped in lush greenery and the white clouds rising like puffs in the summer sky, I stuck my head out the train window like a dazed person; though my body was inside the train, my mind was adrift like the white clouds. What one never tires of seeing are the mountains, and shifting my gaze from mountain to mountain, how wonderful it was. However, as they say, "Good things face many obstacles" (好事多魔), the coal dust spewing from the running train's nostrils struck my face and obscured my eyes, preventing me from fully enjoying the natural scenery. Having no choice, I closed the window and tried to read a book I had brought, but drowsiness crept upon me (쌀러럭 와서), and I missed the fine scenery, waking from my dream to find the next stop was Kaesŏng. So, hastily putting my book and towel into my bag, the train, as if taking a long breath, slowly pulled into Kaesŏng Station.

Ah! Kaesŏng! Historic Kaesŏng! A model city of commerce! The special product region for ginseng! Kaesŏng, where people eat ginseng to live long, conduct business well, cultivate a fine spirit, and will write the second page of history! With these thoughts, I emerged onto the platform, took a shared taxi (승합자동차), got off in front of Namdaemun Gate, left my luggage at the Chosŏn Inn, and went out for some fresh air and to see Namdaemun. Namdaemun is now located in the central area. In the old days, the population was small, but now, with 19,000 households and 52,526 people <196> residing here, what was once Namdaemun (South Gate) is now centrally located with houses built around it. Kaesŏng's Namdaemun is famous not for the size of the gate itself, but because it houses a giant bell made during the reign of King Ch'ungmok of Koryŏ, one of the four great bells of Chosŏn. The bell at the Bell Pavilion in Kyŏngju, the bell in Kwangju, the bell at Ponwŏnsa Temple in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), and this bell placed above Kaesŏng's Namdaemun are the four famous bells of Chosŏn. After viewing this bell, I returned to the inn and called Older Brother Yi of the Kaesŏng branch office. He came, shared good news with me, and promised to guide me around Kaesŏng tomorrow. After spending the night, I waited for him the next morning and then set out to find the famous sites of Kaesŏng.

Songdo Industrial Training Center (松高實習場)
Someone said that Kaesŏng is a commercial city, full of solidarity, self-awareness, and people who not only know how to make money but also how to spend it. I merely listened with one ear and today first sought out the Songdo Industrial Training Center (松高實習場), the only one of its kind in Kaesŏng. Stepping into the grand factory complex deeply nestled in the woods, I presented my name card at the office and waited. An office boy (小使) told me to enter. I exchanged greetings with Director U Sangsun (禹相淳) and said I wanted to tour the factory. He readily agreed and led the way. Following behind him and listening to his words, I learned that this factory started in Meiji 39 (1906) as an attached factory to Songdo Higher Common School (松都高等普通學校) with 15 treadle looms (足踏機). It gradually expanded and expanded until January of Showa 4 (1929), when Songdo Higher Common School decided not to manage it anymore. Local dignitaries then organized it into a stock company (주식회사) with 200,000 Won capital. Now comprising the first factory, second factory, engine room, warehouse, and office, it has become a place where every visitor to Songdo comes to see at least once. Following the guide into the first factory where work was underway, I saw nearly 200 lovely country girls, seemingly around 17 or 18 years old, playing with threads as if teasing a breeze. I walked past them, observing each one closely. I felt overwhelmed (울넝하고 녁이 들더이다). As I passed by one young lady, I caught the scent of facial lotion (미안수) and perfume. A young woman let her lush hair hang down, and her appearance from behind as she worked was even lovelier. Some girls glanced sideways at me as if finding my passing strange, and one young bride (싀악씨) seemed to send an amorous glance (秋波) with a gentle smile when I wasn't looking... Leaving this place, I asked about the annual production value. They said it was about 80,000 Won for 270,000 yards(?) [Original: 27碼, likely 27만 마 - 270,000 yards] of woven fabric and about 100,000 Won for 70,000 dozen pairs of Western socks (洋襪). Hearing that Songdo woven and dyed goods are absolutely colorfast and incomparably superior, I gritted my teeth and spent a hefty 3 Won on fabric for a Western shirt and two pairs of socks, then left the place.

To Kang Kamch'an's Stone Pagoda (姜邯瓚石塔)
Next, I went to see the stone pagoda of Kang Kamch'an (姜邯瓚), a valiant general of Koryŏ. During the Koryŏ Hyŏnjong era, when the country was suffering from Khitan invasions, the heavily pockmarked and unattractive Kang Kamch'an, with resourcefulness in his pockmarks (얼근 구멍에 궁리가 들고) and courage, utterly scattered the strong Khitan army, achieving victory and securing peace for the Koryŏ nation. A monument commemorating this stands in front of Namsŏng Hospital. <197> This is a monument of righteous valor that imparts courage to later generations.

To Manwŏldae (滿月臺)
Hurrying my steps again, I sought out the ruins of Manwŏldae. In the place ravaged by fire, only foundation stones (주초돌) remained here and there, and only young cherry trees fluttered (팔낭 팔낭) in the wind. Only young girls picking and eating bitter greens in the grassy fields were playing around, making the once splendid palace a desolate scene with no one even to ask where it had gone. In An Ch'im's (安琛) poem, it is said:

The traces of five hundred years ago are already dust, moss seals the deserted path, woodcutters have made trails,
How many times has the green of Songsan Mountain renewed? Weeds grow wild in the abandoned polo ground in spring.

The music and songs of the rear palace are now silent and lonely, about the endless past events, whom can we ask?
The boats and oars on the East Pond (東池) have long sunk, only the moon remains above the platform, a single wheel.

Thus it was said, who could come here and not reminisce about the old days? Also, in Hŏ Ch'im's (許琛) "Flower Garden Poem":

Blood and treasure achieve nothing, leading to self-reproach, gathering carts cannot block the path,
Ultimately, the energy of war surrounds the hooked chain [armor?], wielding spears in vain, killing people wrongly.

Fortune departed, ruler and subjects shed tears together, in panic, the dream of prosperity ended at once,
Evil floats between heaven and earth, allowing no place for the body, in the ruined garden, leftover flowers face a desolate spring.

Thinking of these various writings, my heart became desolate (살난해지드이다).

On the Way to Ch'aehadong (彩霞洞)
It being early summer, beads of sweat dripped from my head. Having decided beforehand with Older Brother H from Changdan, who was accompanying me, to eat lunch amidst the forest of Ch'aehadong, we hurried our steps despite slight hunger, passing Ssangp'oktong (雙瀑洞) and Kŭmchodae (琴釣臺), going through Pusandong (扶山洞), and arriving at Ch'aehadong. After resting on a rock and drying our sweat, we ate lunch, and energy I hadn't felt until now seemed to surge.

In Ssangp'oktong, soaring rocks are submerged in green shade,
As soon as the footsteps of scenery seekers arrive here,
An unexpected gust of pure wind embraces them fully.

At Kŭmchodae's clear water, casting a fishing line,
Distracted by the white clouds, I lost the bait,
This place being called Kŭmchodae (Zither Fishing Platform) must be true.

Truly, with every tree, every stone, and the clear water trickling by, a body weary from the city feels refreshed enough to live a hundred years longer here. It wasn't just me immersed in delight; the people of old must have been the same.

To Sŏnggyungwan (成均舘)
Hundred springs gush, wind rises under the arms, no need for a mountain monk to forcefully pull the sleeve,
Pine mist drifts, greenness drips within, wild flowers and crying birds know how to detain people.

Descending from the mountain, enjoying the sounds of birds crying in the forest and the cuckoo's call, I reached Sŏnggyungwan. Sŏnggyungwan is where portraits of the 72 sages of the Song state were painted and hung. During King Kongmin's reign, Master Yi Saek (李穡) and Master Chŏng Mongju (鄭夢周) served as instructors (敎官), educating talented individuals. <198> It was what we would call a university today, the highest educational institution of the old days. Thus, even now, statues of the Five Sages and Ten Philosophers are enshrined, and ancestral tablets of various sages throughout history are present. It was a place heavily endowed with property during the Koryŏ King Ch'ungnyŏl's reign to facilitate rituals for the sages. Master Yi Saek's poem says:

Drum beats, ranks align exactly at the fifth watch, the jeweled crown shines brightly in the deep hall,
Officials in robes and holding tablets attend the spring rite, bells and chimes resonate grandly in the great courtyard.

The Sage's Way hangs like the sun and moon in the nine heavens, sweat flowed, I remember attending the master's court,
Imperial offerings shine for myriad ages in red and blue, white-haired, how can I forget pursuing the single classic?

Thus, the literary figures and ministers of the left and right of olden times emerged from and gathered at this place.

Juvenile Reformatory (少年形務所)
It is said that young people whose determination (立志) is not firm, who fall onto the wrong path and make bad friends (동모), eventually come into contact with the law and are sent here to receive moral influence before leaving. The youths here are not put in shackles (손에 포성을 거러지 아니하고 - lit. 'ropes are not hung on hands'?) but are given some freedom to plow fields (밧갈고) and weed (김 매게 한다고 함니다). The juvenile inmates wear pitch-black clothes and weed the fields. If a villager saw this scene, they would not identify them as criminals. This reminded me of my first year in elementary school when the teacher wore a military sword (軍刀) and supervised us during practical training time. I found myself thinking about the state of Chosŏn education when I was 8 or 9 years old. Lost in such thoughts, I arrived at Sŏnjukkyo (善竹橋) before I knew it.

Sŏnjukkyo (善竹橋)
Taking one step, then another, I came to see a red memorial pavilion beside drooping willows. Deeply carved into the stone monument, narrowly written above, were the words "Monument to the Consummate Virtue of Koryŏ Loyal Subject P'oŭn Sŏnsaeng" (高麗 忠臣 圃隱 先生 成仁碑). Below that, in large letters across two lines, it read: "Loyalty and Righteousness of an Era, Cardinal Principles for Myriad Ages" (一代忠義 萬古綱常). This inscription is said to be by Master Han Ho (Sŏkpong) (韓護(石峯)) and remains perpetually damp throughout the four seasons, which I saw as evidence of the mystery of loyal righteousness here. Beside it stand three other monuments: these commemorate the loyal subjects who followed P'oŭn Sŏnsaeng (Chŏng Mongju) in death and Recorder Kim Kyŏngjo (金慶祚). We paid 1 Kŭm 2 Chŏn each to the gatekeeper to enter and view the P'oŭn Sŏnsaeng memorial pavilion west of Sŏnjukkyo. Inside, a large stone turtle carried a stone monument nearly two kil (approx. 6 meters) high, carved with King Yŏngjo's (英祖大王) own composition and calligraphy: "Virtue and Pure Loyalty Mutual for Myriad Ages, Mount T'ae's High Integrity, P'oŭn Kong" (道德精忠互萬古泰山高節圃隱公). There was also another large monument with King Yi T'aewang's (李太王 - Emperor Kojong) composition and calligraphy: "Endangered Loyalty, Solitary Integrity, Old Universe, Our Way in the East Relies on the Duke" (危忠孤節老宇宙吾道東方賴有公). Leaving this place, I went to Sungyang Sŏwŏn (崧陽書院) to see P'oŭn Sŏnsaeng's portrait and the black bamboo staff (烏竹杖) he used while alive. Approaching the master's portrait respectfully, I bowed once, twice, thrice, and thought of these lines:

The loyal subject sits silently, his meaning deep, deep,
With the first bow, I approach, with the second bow, draw near,
Asking his intent with the third bow, silently, only Sŏnjukkyo <199>
The seven-jointed black bamboo staff, each joint his integrity,
If the straight bamboo is like this, can there be change?
Especially where his hand's touch remains here and there.

After reciting this, I passed by the museum, saw Kwandŏkchŏng Pavilion, Kyŏngdŏk Kung where swings were ridden, the Military Training Center (훈련원) where soldiers were trained in the old days, and many other scenic spots. But I will conclude the Kaesŏng sightseeing here for now, and write about Pagyŏn P'okp'o (朴淵瀑布), where Pak Tong (朴童) played his flute, on another occasion.

Journey to Paekch'ŏn Hot Springs (白川溫泉行)
The reason for resting several days (몃날) in Kaesŏng was not simply to rest, but because the rain made it impossible to go out. So, I rested for several days and left for Paekch'ŏn the day after the rain stopped. It seemed as if nature itself was greeting us as we sought out scenic places! Mr. XX, this place is truly a different world, outside the human realm (별유쳔지 비인간이니), how could one not linger and play? As the sun set behind the western mountains (日落西山) and the moon rose over the eastern ridge (月出東嶺), I returned, having obtained the "Nine Bends Poem" (九曲詩) from Kiun Chŏng Pavilion (起雲亭).

First Bend: Tracing the Source Spring (一曲泝源泉)
Trickling spirit spring surges between rocks, tracing the nine bends of the valley to find the source,
It gathers into clear jade, churns into rapids, endless scenery on the mountains of both rainy banks.

Second Bend: Five Heroes Platform (二曲五豪臺)
Five heroes played on the Five Heroes Platform, past traces fade with the years, no trace of horses,
Below, a long stream flows to the sea, mountains and streams remain serene as of old.

Third Bend: Jade Rinsing Cave (三曲漱玉洞)
Clear stream winds through the cave heaven opening, scooping water rinses like spraying jade,
Flat paving stones and white rocks are piled, heart and mind suddenly feel cleansed of worldly dust.

Fourth Bend: Stone Cliff Waterfall (四曲石涯瀑)
The stone waterfall is called Sŏgae (Stone Cliff), one suspects the sound comes from among the clouds,
Hanging like white silk, scattering like mist, pointing remotely to two or three houses by the stream.

Fifth Bend: Zither Playing Platform (五曲彈琴臺)
On the Zither Playing Platform, playing the zither softly, who again harmonizes with the lofty and vast [music]?
Water blue, mountains green, the primeval heart, pine wind and zither sounds send clear notes.

Sixth Bend: Awakening Spring (六曲覺爽泉)
Try drawing the spirit spring, cold and fragrant, the whole town's paper jars compete to draw it,
Awakening clearly, one feels the dusty bowels cleansed, poor and healthy alike taste it equally.

Seventh Bend: Silk Rinsing Ford (七曲浣紗磯)
Village women abound on the silk rinsing ford, sound after sound of pounding laundry, green mountains in spring,
Along the long embankment, white clouds dry in the sun, flowing water, floating flowers, birds sing naturally.

Eighth Bend: Ear Washing Stream (八曲洗耳溪)
Mount Ki (箕山) and the Ying River (潁水) offer woodcutting and fishing, happier still that the clear stream is named 'Washing Clean',
Roaming beyond worldly affairs, joy abounds, this place truly fits Hsü Yu and Ch'ao Fu's dwelling.

Ninth Bend: Worthies Gathering Platform (九曲會賢臺)
By the clear stream's bank, the Worthies Gathering Platform, the elegant style of the elders' village (耆洞) is now distant,
Peaks sharp, streams bright, valley winds around, visitors annually come to see the flowers.

Mr. XX,
Perhaps the Nine Bends scenic area (九曲幽地) also felt neglected? They have piled up long and short stones to create a garden (庭園), built a Lotus Hall (蓮堂), and planted weeping willows. Intoxicated by the atmosphere, words came out naturally (절노 나오더이다). <200>

**Summary**

This travelogue by Kim Sŏngmok describes his journey from Seoul to Kaesŏng and his subsequent explorations of the historic city before heading towards Paekch'ŏn Hot Springs in the summer of 1935. Delayed by weather and a desire to see Kaesŏng's sites, the author recounts his impressions of the train ride, the city's atmosphere as a historic commercial center famous for ginseng, and visits to Namdaemun Gate (noting its famous bell), the Songdo Industrial Training Center (describing its modern setup and young female workforce), Kang Kamch'an's pagoda, the ruins of Manwŏldae palace (evoking Koryŏ's fall), scenic spots like Ch'aehadong, the historic Sŏnggyungwan academy, a juvenile reformatory, and finally, the poignant Sŏnjukkyo bridge and Sungyang Sŏwŏn honoring the loyalist Chŏng Mongju. The piece concludes with anticipation for Paekch'ŏn and includes a classical-style poem about the "Nine Bends" near Kiun Chŏng pavilion.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its blend of traditional Korean cultural appreciation (Koryŏ history, poetry, scenic beauty) and the descriptions of modern, sometimes Japanese-influenced, elements. The mention of the Songdo factory's founding year in the Meiji era, the use of the Japanese term '取締役' (Torishimariyaku/Director), the depiction of disciplined young female factory labor, and the description of the modern juvenile reformatory provide glimpses into the societal structures and modernization efforts under Japanese rule. The author's perspective seems primarily focused on tourism and history, but these details offer context to the period. Historians might find the description of the Songdo factory, the mention of specific Japanese era names, and the observations about the reformatory useful for understanding industrial development, colonial administration's presence, and social institutions in 1930s Korea.

XX선생!
白川溫泉에는 예정한 날보다 사흘이나 늣게 도착햇슴니다. 그 이유는 社에 도라가서 자세한 말을 하오리다. 그 中 대략 미리 알니자면 이럿슴니다. 1. 日氣가 不順한 것과 勝地古蹟의 開城이라 아니보고 도라온다면 소경 남대문 구경하든 격이 될가하야 일일이 차져 구경하노라니 이러케 된 것임니다. 복잡하고 요란한 서울을 버서나 신촌즘 오니 여긔서부터는 들꽃을 씨처온 향긔로운 냄새와 부드러운 풀냄새가 코를 크게 만들고 녹음에 무루저진 靑山과 뭉게뭉게 피여오르는 여름 하늘에 白雲을 바라보너라고 머리는 차창에서 내밀고 얼빠진 사람처름 나 자체가 차 안에 잇서면서도 마음은 白雲갓치 떠 잇섯슴니다. 언제든지 보아도 실지 안는 것은 山과 山으로 눈을 옴기니 얼마나 조흔지요. 그러나 好事多魔라드니 이 자연에 풍경도 마음끗 못보랴고 달니는 기차 코구멍에서 내뽑는 석탄 가루가 내의 얼골을 따리고 눈을 가리움니다. 할 수 업시 창문을 따고 가지고 간 책을 좀 보느라니가 조우름이 쌀러럭와서 물 조흔 풍경을 못보고 꿈 속에서 깨고보니 다음이 開城이라지요. 그래서 주슴주슴 가방에 冊과 수건을 집어 넛코 나니 어녀듯 車는 긴 숨을 쉬는 듯 츤츤히 개성역에 드러서고 잇슴듸다.
아! 開城이로구나 역사적 開城 商業界 模範都市 人蔘의 特産地! 人蔘먹고 장수하야 장사도 잘하고 조흔 정신을 함양하야 역사의 제2 페이지를 꾸밀 開城이로군 하고 푸렛드 홈에서 나와 승합자동차로 남대문 앞헤 내려 조선여관에 짐을 두고 바람도 쏘이며 남대문 구경으로 나갓슴니다. 남대문은 只今은 中央地에 잇슴니다. 녯날에 인구가 적엇스나 지금은 1만 9천호에 52,526人＜196＞ 거주하니가 녯날엔 남대문이나 지금은 남대문을 중앙에 두고 돌녀 집을 지은 것임니다. 開城에 남대문은 문이 커서 유명한 것이 아니라 高麗 忠穆王 당시에 만든 巨鍾이 잇는데 조선에 4대 巨鍾에 하나인 것임니다. 경주 鍾閣에 鍾과 광주의 鍾과 경성본원사의 鐘과 이 개성 남대문 우에 노힌 종은 조선의 4대 名鍾임니다. 이 종을 구경한 다음 집에 도라와 개성 지사의 李兄의게 전화를 그니 형은 와서 나의게 조흔 소식과 개성 안내을 明日에 하여 주기로 하고 그 밤을 지낸 후 그 잇흔 날 아침 兄이 오기를 기달녀 개성 명승지를 차젓슴니다.

松高實習場
개성은 상업도시요. 단결심 만코 자각이 만코 돈 모을 줄만 아는 것이 아니라 돈 쓸 줄도 아는 사람들이라고 뉘가 그르기에 나는 한 귀로 듯기만 하고 오날의 갈 길을 먼츰 개성에 오직 하나인 松高實習場을 차젓나이다. 숩싸이 푹 파무친 웅대한 공장으로 발을 더려노하 사무실노 명함을 통한 후 기달리니 小使가 드러오라 한다. 그래서 取締役 禹相淳씨와 인사를 하고 공장을 구경하겟다 하니 그는 쾌희 승낙하며 압서셔 안내를 한다. 뒤를 따라가며 그의 말을 귀에 넛노라니 이 공장은 明治 39년에 松都高等普通學校 부속공장으로 足踏機 15臺로 하든 것이 점점 증설에 증설을 가하다가 昭和 4년 1월에 松都高普에서 경영치 안키로 내여노하서 지방 유지들노서 20만원 주식회사로 조직케 되고 지금에 제1 공장 제2 공장과 기관실, 창고, 사무실노서 松都에 오시는 손님은 한 번식 와주게 되엿다고 한다. 안내의 뒤를 따라 작업하는 제 1 공장으로 더러서니 10이라 7, 8세의 어엽뿐 시골 처녀가 실바람을 가지고 희롱하는 듯 200명에 갓가운 처녀와 아가씨들 엽으로 지나며 일일히 살펴 보앗슴니다. 울넝하고 녁이 들더이다. 엇든 아가씨 엽흘 지나러니 미안수 냄새 향수 냄새도 나고 처녀는 탐쓰려운 머리쳬를 너려터리고 일하는 뒤 모양이 더욱 어엽버더이다. 지나가는 나를 이상하다는 듯 엇든 처녀는 살짝 나를 훔처보는 어엽뿐 이도 잇고 엇든 싀악씨는 秋波를 던지는 듯도 하게 방그레 우슴을 내가 보지안는 사이에... 그리고 이 곳을 나오면서 1년 간 생산고가 얼마인가하니 직물이 27碼에 약 8만원 洋襪이 7만打에 약 10만원 이라하며 松高織染色物은 絶對 不變色인 것과 優秀無比하다 하기에 大枚 3원을 눈 질긋 깜고 내여 와이싸쓰감과  양말 2족을 싸가지고 이 곳을 나왓슴니다.

立志가 굿지 못한 어린 사람이 잘못 길올 들고 잘못 동모를 사괴여서 마츰내 法에까지 접촉되면 이 곳에 와서 감화를 밧고 나간다 함니다. 이 곳에 잇는 소년은 손에 포성을 거러지 아니하고 좀 자유를 주어 밧갈고 김 매게 한다고 함니다. 소년 죄수는 생카만 의복을 닙고 기임을 맵니다. 이 모양을 촌사람이 본다면 누가 죄인이라고는 아니 보리다. 내가 소학교 1학년때에 선생이 軍刀차고 실습시간에 감독하든 생각이 니여나와서 나는 내가 8, 9세 시절에 조선 교육 정도를 생각게 되엿슴니다. 이런 생각을 하면서 어너 사이에 善竹橋에 다다렷슴니다.

善竹橋
한 거름 두 거름 나아가 수양버들 너러진 그 엽헤 불근 碑閣을 拜見케 되엿슴니다. 돌비 속 깁히 색여 그 우에는 가늘다케 高麗 忠臣 圃隱 先生 成仁碑라 쓰고 그 밋헤 크다라케 두 줄노 「一代忠義 萬古綱常」이라고 색엿는데 이 글은 韓護(石峯)先生의 筆題로 四時長濕하야 잇다 하니 여긔에도 忠義의 神密이 잇는 것이라고 보앗나이다. 그 겻헤 또 다른 碑가 세 개가 잇는데 이것은 圃隱先生을 뒤따러 殉死한 충신들과 錄事 金慶祚의 碑가 잇슴니다. 우리는 善竹橋 서편 圃隱선생 비각을 拜見코저 1金 2錢식을 문직이에게 주고 들러가보니 그 곳엔 큰 돌노 만든 거북이가 두 길이나 거반되는 碑石을 업고 잇는데 英祖大王의 御製御筆노서 「道德精忠互萬古泰山高節圃隱公」이라고 색인 碑와 李太王의 御製御筆로 危忠孤節老宇宙吾道東方賴有公」이라고 색인 큰 碑石 2개가 잇더이다. 이곳에서 나온 나는 다시 圃隱 선생의 畵像과 살아 게실 때 집고 단기시든 烏竹杖을 보려 崧陽書院으로 가서 선생 화상 압헤 공손히 나어가 재배 삼배로 보이면서 한 마듸 글을 생각햇슴니다.

이 글을 얼코나서 박물관으로하야 觀德亭으로 그네 뛰는 敬德宮도 구경하고 녯날에 兵士를 敎練하든 훈련원이며 그 밧게도 승지를 만히 만히 보앗건만 대강 여긔까지하고 또 朴童의 피리불든 朴淵瀑布도 다음 긔회에 쓰기로 하고 여긔까지만 開城 구경을 햇슴니다.

白川溫泉行
開城서 몃날 쉬는 것은 쉬려 그러케 한 것이 아니라 비가 와서 나단기지 못하겟기에 몃날을 쉬고 白川으로 가기는 비온 뒷날 이엿슴니다. 나 勝地를 차저드는 우리보고 인사 하노라고 그르는 듯! XX선생이 이 곳이 별유쳔지 비인간이니 아니놀고 어이리가 日落西山되여 月出東嶺할 때 도라오면서 起雲亭에 九曲詩를 어더왓슴니다.
