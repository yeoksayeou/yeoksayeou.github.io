University Life Boiling with the Blood of Youth
Profile of a German University Student
Yi Ha-sŏng, Berlin University

A
Through the hazy tobacco smoke, like fog, the dim light of an electric lamp illuminates the small café room.
One student playing the violin and another playing the piano are engrossed in performing music they might not even know, <256> lost in a daze. A serving boy in a white apron (에푸롱) absentmindedly spins a pewter (眞鑄) goblet, occasionally letting out a big yawn, his mind lost, blankly staring up at the dimly lit ceiling.
This was a bar (빠-) frequented by young, penniless music students.
"Boy, bring a glass of water!"
"What would you like to order, sir?"
"Water, just water!"
"Yes, sir."
Walter is already drinking his fourth glass of water. The foam from the beer (삐-루) he drank first must have vanished somewhere in his stomach long ago.
In front of the other students too, empty beer glasses are neatly lined up in a row.
"Why has Friedrich's behavior changed so much lately?"
"Don't even mention it... Within that, the great..."
"Hmph, forget it! Jazz is most suitable for the Charleston Meister."
Rudolf puffs out clouds of cheap tobacco smoke.
"It's true! Just dancing the Charleston won't do."
Emma shakes her blonde hair vigorously as she interjects, offering a gentle smile.
Just then, the door (또아-) swings open, and the considerably tall Johann slowly walks in, holding his hat.
"Hallo, Johann, I knew you'd be here already, I was waiting," Walter says in a gruff but dignified voice, straightening his back. The others look at each other, grinning strange smiles.
"Well, how is it?" Johann glances around the room once.
"Looks like business is good!"
"To honor Johann, shouldn't we raise a toast?"
Walter winked one eye, firmly stomped on the tip of the shoe of the person next to him, and energetically shouted.
Everyone simultaneously gave some kind of unintelligible reply.
"Agreed!"
Clara, who had been sitting quietly until then, lost in thought, raised her hand, her face showing deep color (빗츨).
The waiter, still with a preoccupied look, carries beers one by one.
The two students who had been playing the piano and organ (올간?), perhaps finished with their performance (奏樂), stare blankly this way.
"Prosit!"
"Prosit!"
The beer mugs (곱부) clink together.
"Clara, what are you going to be for the Masquerade Ball (마스켄발) this time?"
"You shouldn't make a fuss about it now."
"Tell me, just a little, I need it."
"I know very well what your 'need' (필오) is." <257>
Johann quickly puts down his mug and nudges Walter hard in the ribs.
"Why isn't Franz here?"
"Oh, that guy, he's completely smitten with Gerta right now!"
"With Gerta? That can't be right, eh?"
At that moment, Friedrich, who had been affectionately whispering with Emma, bursts into loud laughter.
"It would be good if that fellow came, seems he's got quite a bit of money thanks to Gerta."
"Huh, really? If I'd known that earlier, I would have grabbed him and brought him along."
Johann looked regretful.
"..."
This group has already been in the café for over three hours, yet none show any sign of wanting to get up.
This happens several times a month for them, but since everyone's pockets are empty (비여저), time just keeps flowing by as they gaze at each other's weary faces.

That very night, Berlin (伯林) City Streets.
In a dark corner of a small park, two slender shadows are moving.
"What are you thinking so hard about..."
One shadow asks.
"Hmm..." the other shadow quietly follows, replying.
"Are you thinking about Gerta again?"
"Tonight, I'm breaking off ties (緣) with Gerta for good!"
"Looks like you fought... What a foolish person..."
"It's not like that, I'm just tired of it now!"
"Of Gerta!"
"No, she's tired of me..."
"Looks like you received your sentence!"
"It seems like it might happen tonight!..."
"Greatest Romantist! (Ah! 위대한 로만틔스트여!)"
"Well then, treat me to a beer. You can guess how I feel at times like this!"
"Are you really that broke? That can't be right."
"Yeah, looks like from today I'm back to being the penniless student I used to be!"
After a while, they finished their beer at the café, and one kindly offered to walk the other to the woman's house. The church bell tolled nine times. Of course, the woman's house door (the apartment (아파-트) door, as a rule, is always locked at 8 PM) was chained shut. It couldn't be opened without a key.
Franz whistled two or three times to announce his arrival. A window on the 4th floor slid open silently, and a woman's head appeared.
Upon spotting Franz, the woman waved her hand. <258> A few moments later, a small white bundle landed before the two men's eyes. It was a key wrapped in paper (조이) and tied to a string, lowered down.
Franz quickly untied the bundle. Something was written on the paper. As he read it, overcome with joy, he looked up at the woman and blew her a kiss. The woman blew a kiss back.
"It worked! The sentence hasn't been passed yet!"
He muttered in a low voice, handed the piece of paper he held to his friend, and vanished inside the door in an instant. The note contained words that seemed to mock his friend.
"Bitte, du allein, mit keinem Pegleiter. (Please, you alone, with no companion.) Your friend is disliked. Only you may enter."

B
The clinking of wine glasses (크라스/곱부), the popping of champagne (샴뻰) corks, jazz (쨔-즈), red (赤), blue (靑), green (綠), purple (紫), yellow (黃) five-colored streamers (데-푸), strange masks (가면), the thick mingling scents of women's perfume – amidst this symphony (교항악), the curtain rose on the State Higher Music School (官立高等音樂學校) Masquerade Ball (마스켄발).
The Jazz Meister, holding a violin bow (줄) in his right hand and a wine glass in his left, dances overcome with excitement, shouting something, but it's inaudible, buried in the noise (騷音) of the hall (쌀).
Half-naked (半裸體) girls and strangely attired men embrace and dance. But because of the masks, no one can tell who is who. The five-colored streamers dance in the air, then scatter underfoot.
As time passes, the effects of alcohol seem to heighten the excitement.
For them, there was nothing but pleasure (향락).
New music starts again.
"Well, shall we dance a bit?"
"Leave me alone. Tonight is the Masquerade Ball."
"I know that, come on, let's dance."
"No way, not with a bad dancer (下手) like you!"
"What!"
The man tries to grab the girl's body (몸둥아리) and tightly grips her wrist. The girl screams fiercely and runs away.
Amidst this, all the men and women somehow embrace each other and dance.
Their dancing continues for hours.
"You're Clara, aren't you?"
"Ah... How do you know?"
"Why ask if I know, eh!"
"Ah, it hurts..."
The girl grimaces and burrows into the man's chest. Then the man tightly holds the girl's body with both hands and leaves the dance floor, going up <259> to the second-floor private room (셋빠레-) overlooking the hall.
"Clara, take off your mask. I'll take mine off too."
"No way, are you crazy!"
"Come on, nothing will happen. Then, do you know who I am?"
"I know."
A roar of cheers erupts from the hall.
A professor, holding a champagne bottle, offered it to several students.
"Hey, waiter (급사), bring champagne!"
"Me too." (Clara's words)
"You drink well, I didn't know you could drink like that."
"I don't know who you're talking about."
"Hahaha... Come sit on my lap."
"Pussierstengel!" (Playboy/Dandy?)
A group of drunkards (주정군), blowing paper trumpets (조이랍팔), comes dancing down the narrow corridor (廊下) in front of the private room with strange movements.
One girl lies stretched out long on the corridor floor, refusing to get up even when her wrist is pulled, just singing something resembling a popular song (유행가). Another group of men and women comes crowding out and trips over the girl. The girl screams "Kkyaek!" Instantly, a roar of laughter (우슴소리) erupts.
"Prosit!"
"Prosit!"
Another champagne cork pops.
Suddenly! The lights in the hall all go out with a 'pak' sound, then come back on the next moment. At this time, the music starts making strange sounds again.

Pleasure, wild dancing (난무), and drunken revelry (광취) deepen, continuing until dawn.

Cambridge University Student Life
Ch'oe Yŏng-ch'un, Oxford University

An American university was once criticized by a certain sharp-tongued (險口家) German: "A university is a suburban club (구락부) where young men and women can enjoy a more pleasant life." <260>
Rather than criticism, it's a sharp piece of sarcasm (皮肉).
However, in the atmosphere of the so-called 'college life' of American universities, it truly might be a pleasant college life.
Young men and women boiling with the blood of youth – since most universities implement co-education systems – undoubtedly have a pleasant university life, holding hands and frolicking together. There, male students find female students as their good companions, and female students find male students as their good playthings (완구); they discover each other.
This is indeed what the Germans mock as the "pleasant suburban club."
Compared to this, the mood (정조) of British university student life is centered on the emotion, form, and color of 'college life' itself, refined from eroticism (에로틔시즘) and nonsensicalism (넌센니즘).
Truly, if one wants to appreciate the scenery of university life in the context of British university student life, it naturally refers to the two universities, Oxford (牛津) and Cambridge (劒橋).
If someone were to ask if there is truly traditional 'chonppol' (쫀뽈 - perhaps meaning 'genuine' or 'authentic'?) university life at the University of London (倫敦大學), that person would undoubtedly be asking as foolish a question (愚問) as asking if Mussolini is British.
The University of London cannot represent the university life of the English (Englishman). No matter that it is in the British capital, London (英京 倫敦), and has a regional connection like being the central point of disarmament conferences, just as Columbia University (코롬비야 대학) in New York (紐育) has errors (誤謬), deficiencies (不足), flaws (缺陷), and anomalies (異態) that prevent it from representing American university life, the University of London lacks it.
Indeed, Oxford and Cambridge, these two universities, are truly the universities of England. This is because the English-style 'college life' persists there in a dignified form.
Here, nature and architecture seem to possess a hidden charm that captures one's heart to some extent.
A long time ago (when I was in England), when I visited Oxford and then Cambridge, the moment I saw only the exterior of those universities, I asked myself inwardly:
A university having the right natural environment and the correct architectural position – that is truly a 'college town'.
This, I thought, created everything about the consciousness of life for the students of British universities.
A 'university town' (대학가) does not yet exist in our East Asia. Rather than saying it doesn't exist, it's perhaps better to say it cannot exist yet in the East. But it exists at Oxford and Cambridge. On the university campus (캰나쓰 - campus?), where elm (楡) and oak (檞) trees grow lushly, the bell tower symbolizing the college (Calledge) stands tall and quiet, as if sleeping. The hallowed ground of grace (은혜의 성지) is far <261> removed from the mundane world (浮世); there exists only a living environment solely for the university. That area is the so-called 'college town'. There, the unique color (色), form (型), and emotion (感情) of British university life boils over.
When rousing oneself from a long, deep sleep, a sound like the prayer bells of a monastery (수도원) flows into the head, still not fully awake from sleep, feeling as if the whole body is being sharpened (쌔워 벼리는 것) with silk.
It is truly enveloped in unspeakable tranquility (정적).
Looking out the window at the dormitory yard wet with autumn rain, a few birds with yellow beaks are frolicking among the dense thickets (숩사히).
It is truly literal tranquility (정적함).
Wearing a gown (가운) when going to the dining hall or to the classroom is difficult and troublesome, but it is strictly observed. Quiet dormitory life is a characteristic feature (특장) of British university life, but outdoor sports (戶外競技) are also the most pleasant other side of university life.
Classes are held in the morning, and usually, there are no classes in the afternoon. From the afternoon onwards, students spend their time in sports (스포-쓰), games (께임), tea time (차마시기), clubs (구락부); university students truly pass their time in a pleasant atmosphere of discipline and nurturing (훈육).
To put it extremely, I think British university life has a more pleasant castle life (城廓生活) than American university life.

French University Life
Pak Nak-chŏng, Sorbonne University

Sorbonne (쏘루본누) university life – this might not be much different from the university student life in other countries, nothing particularly remarkable.
That the university is quiet, seemingly dry and uninteresting (무미건조), rigid, and also has a courageous aspect is an unavoidable reality for Sorbonne University as well.
Once they pass the baccalauréat (바가로레아) (entrance qualification exam), they become full-fledged university students.
On Saturdays and Sundays, they invariably flock to the dance halls (딴스장) to frolic joyfully.
If you toss in 6 francs (푸랑) and enter, there are plenty of beautiful women who greet them with smiles.
However, these dance girls (딴스껄) are not the lowly and vulgar girls like those we might think of here; they are all either female students from the Sorbonne or students from other schools – children from respectable families. <262>
Therefore, even if some incident or relationship arises there, it never leads to unhappy consequences.
April brings Pâques (빠-크) (Easter). At this time, there is a week-long holiday, so everyone leaves for travels. The weather is fine, nature is beautiful, so everyone sets off on a journey to the countryside they had been thinking about for a month or two beforehand.
If asked about their purpose, they would answer like this: If we go to the countryside, many country girls will be waiting there... moreover, since we are Sorbonne students...
Wherever you go, women, women, women are so numerous in France (佛蘭西) that men become cherished everywhere. Especially when it is a foreigner – mysteriously, they show favor.
Furthermore, on July 14th, as the whole country celebrates the Republic (공화제), they spend the time frolicking, shouting, and fighting.
Because they grab female students indiscriminately, 14 or 15 at a time, stuff them into a truck (일대의 자동차), and go around shouting "Wah- wah- wah-" from street to street, frolicking, various chances (챤쓰) are created for them at such times.
However, the female students there are generally very firm-minded (마음이 구더서); since it's co-educational, they make many friends and play well together, but problems never arise from these things.
In any case, the Sorbonne is a playground (노리터) for young people singing the joy of youth. Everyone is passionate (열정적이요), everyone is natural (자연스럽다).
And they all want to become excellent people... all want to get good women... (End)

The Spirit of American University Students
Yŏm Myŏng-sik, Washington University

One day in September, Shōwa 8 (1933), from a corner of a bustling downtown shopping street in a metropolis where electric lights flickered and sound waves and human waves intersected, a group of black people wearing evening wear (야회복) with thick stripes came walking, shouting enthusiastically, making noise as if some big commotion had occurred.
Walking with me through the bustling shopping street, when we reached under the bright red sign of a 15-sen uniform price store (15仙均一店), M, seeing it, exclaimed in Korean with a startled look, loud enough for the white people walking nearby to turn around.
"A riot! A Negro riot! (黑人의 騷動)" <263>
However, M's observation was not as accurate as predicting the weather by observing cloud movements at sea.
No gunshots were heard from anywhere, no police machine gun squad cars rushed over, nor was the sound of shattering glass heard. Moreover, no cries of fear erupted from the passersby. On the contrary, passersby were flocking towards the direction of the commotion.
This was because M didn't know about the American students' initiation (이니테이에-숀). He was my classmate who had first crossed over to America (미국) that spring from here.
So, what is initiation? It also has the meaning of admission, etc., and in the case of students, by undergoing this, they first acquire the qualification as a student. In short, it belongs to the category of entrance ceremonies (입교식), but its content is entirely different from the entrance ceremonies here.
For students who have finished high school (하이·스쿠-ㄹ), passed the university preparatory exam (大學豫備誠驗 - likely meant 시험, 'exam'), and are about to enter new university life for the first time, the most pleasant, and on the other hand, fear-inducing thing they carry in their hearts is the initiation received from upperclassmen.
It was generally similar to the lectures (설교) from upperclassmen I first encountered when I became the middle school student I had envied since elementary school, walking around with shiny gold buttons (금 댄추); those lectures only instilled a thumping fear deep within my body. A fearsome-looking member of the sports club committee (운동부 위원) would bark orders, "Salute the upperclassmen! Above all else..." and I even saw cases where violators were immediately subjected to fist punishment (鐵拳制裁).
However, American initiation, while not entirely free of such barbaric customs (蠻風) as mentioned above, does not lose the uniquely American exhilarating sense of ingenuity (奇拔心) amidst the fear. The white-walled building rising high on the hill where lilac (라이락) flowers bloom, northwest of Washington (와신돈), is the new alma mater the entering students are heading towards.
Gazing at the splendid school buildings (校舍) towering here and there, feeling the vibrant happiness of life and the joy of youth, the students walk up to the university office, gather in front of the blackboard (흑판), and chatter noisily.
WARNING.
1. Initiation (이니데이에-숀) will be held today at 7 PM on the school grounds (교정), all freshmen (푸렛슈만) must assemble.
2. School song chorus (교가합창)
3. Baptism ceremony (세례식)
4. Procession through the city after the ceremony (식후 시내 행렬)
Attire: Pajamas (파쟈마) must be worn according to the school's Old Custom.
Violators, for example, just as the people who did not believe in God were punished by Noah's flood, divine punishment (神罪) will soon follow, be warned in advance.
Freshmen seeing this notice (揭示) muttered "Gee! My Goodness," <264> chattering amongst themselves, seemingly pleased, wishing for night to come quickly.
After a while, as the evening sun tragically sets behind the western building's (삘딍) clock tower and the campus grounds gradually darken to violet (자색), just like American Indians (아메리카, 인듸안) making noise while performing rituals after capturing white people as prisoners (포로), groups wearing pajamas flock onto the campus from all directions, sounding alarm-like sirens (警笛), arriving in cars available for $5. On one side of the campus, a bonfire (모닥불) is lit and begins to blaze vigorously, creating an atmosphere reminiscent of natives' (토인) nighttime mountain camps.
After a while, once everyone lines up (整列), a liquid boils furiously in a large oil can (石油鑵) over the blazing fire.
That liquid is brought to an altar (제단) built high at the front, and following the lead of an upperclassman, likely the chairman (위원장), the school song is sung in chorus, and then the baptism ceremony is performed.
One by one, they come forward and have the liquid applied to their heads. The furiously boiling coal tar (고-ㄹ타-ㄹ) liquid drips onto the fine hair praised by lovers as golden, and people on either side smear chimney soot (煙突구멍의 재) onto their peach-colored faces. Students who try to avoid this or show dislike are considered very ill-natured (마음 낫분 눔).
"Don't do that! I don't Want! Don't touch me! Gee! Ha! Ha! Whopee!"
Amidst such screams, laughter (우슴소리), and clamor (喧騷), the students all turn into a group of black people from some society.
For what reason do they imitate black people? This is a question incomprehensible to us East Asians.
Ah! It's surprising. The fervor for worshipping black people might be a precursor to sweeping across all of America.
Judging from facts like these, some argue vehemently that black people will marry white people in 50 years, while others counter that 100 years is more than enough time, causing a big commotion.
On Halloween night or Christmas night, and even on New Year's Eve (새해의 전난 발?), when they dress up in costumes (가장), they invariably make themselves look black. Al Jolson's (아-ㄹ죠루숀) "Sonny Boy" (사니-뽀이) from the blackface minstrel show (와- 나뿌로-즈 - Vaudeville?) is gaining popularity on stage.
"In the future, America will be filled with mixed-race children (雜種兒) of black and white people."
This seems to be a headache for America's patriots (우국지사), who are said to be busy investigating black families from morning till night. Black people resembling large merchants (거상) walk stealthily along the dark roads (가도). Wherever one looks, only black people are walking. One is even startled by the appearance of a policeman standing under a streetlamp (가등).
"Ryder, way, Ikorgot about the initiation." (This seems like misheard/misremembered English). Shaking their heads, shiny like they're covered in bird droppings (새 똥), they push into the center of the bustling city.
Streetcars (電車) and automobiles (自動車) encountering this group (一團) are all forced to stop. When a streetcar comes, <265> they line up horizontally (횡대) to block it, and when a car comes, they gather around it from all sides, chanting "Etsha, etsha" (엣샤엣샤 - a rhythmic chant like 'heave-ho'), pushing the driver out.
In no time, streetcars line up continuously on both sides of the street. 'Dang-ga-dang dang-ga-dang ppang-ppang' – the streetcars clang their bells (고동), and cars honk their horns (경적), causing great chaos (대혼란). At this point, if policemen rush in on motorcycles (자동 자전차) to restore order, everyone shouts "Waa-" and runs away.
Next, they link arms in a line (일례) and push towards the front of a theater (극장). That night is truly hell for the theater management. Shouting "Waa-" at the entrance, they barge in, pierce through (께뚤느고 - likely 꿰뚫고) to the exit, pushing everything aside, causing considerable distress (두통) to the audience (관객).
The next day's newspaper (신문) carries a major editorial (논설) about 'Obstruction of Business' (興業防害), but society treats it with indifference (냉담). Everyone considers it an exception, a custom (습관).
When the next day arrives, all male freshmen wear yellow neckties and go to school with large pieces of paper (조이) attached to their backs. On the paper slips, faces of women or phrases like "Kiss me again" are scribbled (낙서).
Female students wear shoes of different colors on each foot, like one white and one red, or one black and one red, and attend school. They chatter like little children, run around like birds, and enjoy their youth to the fullest.
After commuting in such attire for a certain period, they are accepted by the upperclassmen as full-fledged college men (칼레지, 만). Truly, American universities offer a cheerful life. If that life were tedious (치근치근한), it would be impossible for a self-supporting student (고학생) who crossed over from the East to endure for long.
Many sons (子息) of millionaires (백만장자), reluctant to leave school and enter the real world (실사회), enjoy student life for over 10 years under the pretext of obtaining an M.A. degree (데구리-). Among uncomprehending laborers (노동자), there are still white people who mock (嘲笑) East Asians, but among these students, one sees many who show great interest in the East and ask questions.
Recently, besides baseball (야구), which receives enthusiastic welcome among American students, rugby (라꾸비-) has become very popular. The character (성질) of these students is mostly rough and boisterous (괄괄한 축).
Just like hefty athletes (퉁퉁한 운동선수) here carry large bags (빽) with sports equipment (운동구) as if showing them off, shoulders raised (억개가 올나가서 - likely 어깨가 올라가서), passing by girls' school gates (여학교의 문전), American rugby players also carry their equipment (도구) tucked under their arms, faces patched with plaster (膏藥), shoulders squared (역개를 벌니고 - likely 어깨를 벌리고), and walk around girls' school gates.
There are students with gentler hearts (마음이 좀 부드러운 학생) who, knowing nothing about music (음악의 一部도 몰으면서), carry a saxophone (색쓰폰) with an expression as if introducing music as the first art form presented to the world (世人), entering the school gate (교문). Following them are students wearing wide sailor pants (세-라-빤즈) so broad that both legs could fit comfortably into one side, <266> flapping like women's skirts (스카-트) as they walk.
Students who came over from England (영국), perhaps educated in the English style (영국풍), politely greet each morning when encountered:
"How are you feeling this morning?"
Truly the most polite (점잔흔) morning greeting.
"How you are" is common, but most American students, perhaps reluctant to open their mouths every morning, usually just wink one eye slightly, tilt their head a bit, and pass by when they meet.
Although some people make a fuss, saying that processions (행렬) are a barbaric custom (야만적 습관) and should be abolished, thinking the ultimate purpose of life lies in pleasure (향락), the issue doesn't seem to become very big, perhaps because it's mostly the children and daughters (영양) of wealthy families (큰 부호) who participate in the processions. However, just as the custom of tying empty cans (空鑵) to the back of a car during weddings (혼례시) and dragging them around was banned as excessive mischief (작란) due to the noise (喧騷), the thought that this enjoyable school tradition (교풍) of American university students might disappear before long brings a sense of regret (섭섭한 감).

Chinese Universities and Student Life
Kim Sang-gyun, Shanghai University

"University is a tomb (墳墓)."
I remember seeing this line sometime in an editorial in a Shanghai newspaper.
"University is a paradise (樂園)."
This sounds like something Madam (매담님 - likely 'madam' or 'lady') who likes American cinema (씨네마) might say.
Beiping (北平), Tianjin (天津), Nanjing (南京), Guangdong (廣東), Wuhan (武漢), etc. – these are the locations of major universities in China (中國).
(There was Dongbei University (東北大學) in Liaoning (遼寧) (Fengtian, 奉天)), but it should be noted that it disappeared after the Manchurian Incident (滿洲事變).)
In Beiping, the number of schools with 'University' (大學) in their name numbers in the dozens (수십을 헤이게 되며); among them (就中), National Peking University (국립북경대학), Yenching University (燕京大學), Hebei University (河北大學), Tsinghua University (淸華大學), etc., are famous universities.
In Tianjin, there are Nankai University (南開大學) and Beiyang University (北津大學 - likely 北洋大學, Beiyang University). In Nanjing, there are Nanjing University (南京大學) and Central University (中山大學 - likely 中央大學, Central University). In Shanghai (上海), there are Nanyang University (南洋大學), Jiaotong University (交通大學), Daxia University (大夏大學), Fudan University (復旦大學), and below them Hujiang (滬江), St. John's (쎈트·쫀스), Soochow (東吳) etc. universities. In Guangdong, there are Sun Yat-sen University (中山大學) and Lingnan University (嶺南大學). In Wuhan, there is the university that caused a stir during the Tang Shengzhi (唐生智) era.
Besides these, there are not a few regional universities like Qingdao University (靑島大學) in Qingdao (靑島), Qilu University (濟魯大學) in Jinan (濟南), etc., but listing them all one by one would probably require going as far as the Philippines (比律賓).
With this, we have generally located <267> them. Next, let's look into the hometowns where these university students grew up. Of course, it's undoubtedly China. But looking at the map once more, Heilongjiang (黑龍江), then Mongolia (蒙古), Xinjiang (新彊), Tibet (西藏) down to Yunnan (雲南), Guangxi (廣西) – this line marks the so-called author's utopia, the "Great Northwest" (大西北) and the "National Border" (國境).
If one were to write a topography (風土記) about this region, it would be fascinating (흥미진진), but it's deeply regrettable (심히 유감되다) not to have that leisure.
Next, looking at the students enrolled at Nankai University by region, the numerical order is: Hebei (河北), Guangdong (廣東), Jiangsu (江蘇), Zhejiang (淅江), Anhui (安徽), Shandong (山東), Liaoning (遼寧), Sichuan (四川), Jingbei (京北 - likely area around Beiping), Fujian (福健), Henan (河南), Jilin (吉林), Hubei (湖北), Gansu (家肅 - likely 甘肅), Hunan (湖南), Heilongjiang (黑龍江), Shaanxi (陝西), Jiangxi (江西), Guizhou (貴州), Rehe (熱河), Guangxi (廣西), Yunnan (雲南), Mongolia (蒙古). Besides these, looking at the fact that people from abroad including Japan (일본), America (미국), Mexico (멕시코), Panama (파나마), England (영국) (Korea (조선)) were also enrolled – according to a survey from Shōwa 4 (1929) – from the phenomenon revealed here, we can generally grasp the level of education in each province and region of China. This is merely based on statistics from a survey of a single university, but it is sufficient to infer from this.
Here, we can easily imagine such scenes. Scholars (士/섬배) eager for learning (好學), driving camels (駱駝) from the vast Mongolian desert (蒙古沙漠) where the fierce Siberian (시베리아) wind blows sandstorms (砂塵), finding their way (차자드는) to the distant Chinese mainland... Or people coming down to Shanghai riding rafts (떼목/筏) and junks (쨩그/克戒 - likely 舢板, sampan?) from the perilous mountains and deep valleys (險山幽谷) of Sichuan... Also, the farmer's son finding his way (차자드는) to the distant capital (首都) along the desolate single road (荒凉한 一本道) of Gansu, battered by the cold wind (寒風)... All these scenes are unique things that can only be seen (어더볼 수 업는) in China.
On the other hand, among Chinese university students, there are quite a few children (자제) of warlords (軍閥), politicians (政治家), and wealthy merchants (富豪). These so-called children of local tyrants and evil gentry (土豪劣紳) come to see their birth as 'intelligentsia' (인테리) only through the 'university'.
While this fact might be just a new custom (풍습) in present-day China, for them, having nowhere else to advance (더 나아갈 데가 업는), the 'university' being the place they must inevitably end up (떠러저야 할 곳) – what else could this be but a sad phenomenon (슲은 현상) of the vast land of China!
The college march (칼레이지·마-취) performed by such orchestra members (오-케스트라·멤버-) certainly cannot be harmonious music, and one can glimpse from Chinese university student life that there is no jazz-like melody (쨔-즈와 갓흔 메로듸-) either.
Children of capitalists who spend money like water (금전을 물쓰듯 하는); students who sell hemp cloth (麻布) made by their mothers back home (鄕里) to pay for tuition (학비); students who rarely attend school (좀체로 출석도 안허며) and lose themselves (정신을 파라버리는) in sports (스포-쓰) and theater-going (관극) – these are probably the same in any country.
This reflection (반영) is a fact that can be seen particularly well in Shanghai.
-End- <268>

**Summary**

This 1935 article compiles accounts from five Korean students studying abroad, describing university life in Germany (Berlin), Britain (Oxford/Cambridge), France (Sorbonne), the USA (Washington), and China (various locations, focusing on Shanghai). The German section depicts bohemian student poverty and social interactions in cafés and at a masquerade ball. The British section contrasts UK "college town" life (tranquil, traditional, sport-focused) with American perceived superficiality. The French account highlights relatively carefree student socializing, dancing, and travel. The American section details the boisterous and sometimes bizarre "initiation" rituals (hazing involving costumes, tar, soot, and public disruption) and the generally energetic, perhaps superficial, student culture. The Chinese section notes the vast geographical diversity of students, the mix of poor scholars and wealthy elite children, and suggests a less cohesive or joyful student life compared to the West, particularly in Shanghai.

From a historical perspective, the article offers fascinating glimpses into the experiences and comparative viewpoints of Korean colonial elites studying overseas during a period of intense global change and Japanese colonial rule. The descriptions reveal varying degrees of assimilation or observation of Western/Chinese cultures, anxieties about identity (e.g., American fascination with blackface), and reflections on modernity and tradition. The mention of Korean students grouped under 'England' in the Nankai University survey is a subtle reminder of their colonial status. The different tones – bohemianism in Berlin, tradition in Oxbridge, carefree socializing in Paris, boisterousness in the US, and a more complex, somewhat somber view of China – provide rich material for understanding Korean intellectual perspectives in the 1930s.

牛津大學 崔泳春

아메리카의 대학을 어떤 險口家인 독일인이 『대학이란 것은 젊은 청춘남녀가 좀 더 유쾌한 생활을 즐길 수 잇는 교외 구락부다.』라고 비평한 일이 잇다.＜260＞
비평이라기 보담도 날카로운 皮肉의 말이다.
그러나 아메리카의 대학이 소위 카레이지·라이푸의 분위기로는 그것이 진실로 유쾌한 카레이지·라이푸일 것이다.
젊은 청춘의 피끌는 남녀- 대부분의 대학에서는 남녀공학의 제도를 실시하고 잇슴으로-들이 서로 손목을 마주잡고 뛰노는 유쾌한 대학생활임에 틀님이 업다. 거긔에는 남학생들은 그의 조흔 반려자로서의 여학생을, 여학생들은 그의 조흔 완구로서의 남학생들을 그들은 서로 발견하고 잇다.
이것이 실로 독일 사람들이 비웃는 「유쾌한 교외 구락부」일 것이다.
이에 비하면 영국의 대학생 생활의 정조는 에로틔시즘과 넌센니즘에서 세련된 카레지·라이푸 그것의 감정, 형태, 색채가 中樞가 되여잇다.
참말로 대학생활의 정경을 영국의 대학생 생활에서 감상할야며는 그것은 말할 것도 업시 牛津(옥쓰포-드)와 劒橋(켐부릿치)의 兩大學을 의미하게 된다.
만약 倫敦大學에 참말로의 쫀뽈 전통의 대학생활이 잇는야고 물는 사람이 잇다면 그 사람이야말로 뭇쏘리니-는 영국인임니까하고 질문함과 갓흔 愚問임에 틀님이 업슬 것이다.
倫敦 大學은 영국(Englishman)의 대학생활을 대표할 수는 업다. 아무리 英京 倫敦에 잇서서, 그 곳이 군축회의의 중심지점과 갓흔 지역적 관계가 잇다고 하드라도, 마치 紐育(뉴-욕)의 코롬비야 대학에 아메리카의 대학생활을 대표할 수 업는 誤謬와 不足과 缺陷과 異態가 잇는 것 가치 倫敦大學에는 업는 것이다.
실로 옥쓰포-드와 켐부릿치의 兩大學, 그것야말로 참말로의 영국의 대학일 것이다. 여긔에는 영국(Englishman)풍의 카레-지·라이푸(Calledge life)가 엄연한 형태로서 존속하고 잇슴으로써이다.

여자 편은 구두를 백색과 적색이든가 흑색과 적색이라든가 반쪽식 다른 빗의 구두를 신고 등교한다. 그들은 마치 어린 아이들과 가치 떠들어내며 새들과 가치 뛰여단이며 청춘을 마음것 향락한다.
이러한 복장으로 어느 일정한 기간을 통학하면 그들은 상급생들에게서 완전한 칼레지, 만으로써 인용된다. 실로 미국의 대학은 쾌활한 생활을 할 수 잇다. 만약 그 생활이 치근치근한 생활이라면 동양에서 건너간 고학생에게는 오래 계속할 수가 업슬 것이다.
백만장자의 아들(子息)들은 학교를 나와서 실사회에 뛰여 들기가 슬혀서 10년 이상을 M,A,데구리-를 엇는 것을 구실로 학생 생활을 향락하고 잇는 자들이 만타. 몰이해하는 노동자들 중에는 아즉도 동양인을 嘲笑하는 백인이 잇스나 이들 학생간에는 동양에 대하야 만흔 흥미를 가지고 질문하는 학생들을 만히 보게된다.
近者 미국학생들 간에는 열광적으로 환영을 밧는 야구 외에 라꾸비-가 대단히 성행한다. 그들 학생들의 성질은 대개가 괄괄한 축들이다.
그것은 마치 이 곳의 퉁퉁한 운동선수들이 크다란 빽 속에 운동구를 너허가지고 이것을 좀 보라는 듯이 억개가 올나가서 여학교의 문전을 지나듯이 미국의 학생들도 라꾸비의 선수들은 도구를 엽헤끼고 얼골에 膏藥을 부치고 역개를 벌니고 여학교의 문전을 돌아단인다.
마음이 좀 부드러운 학생들은 음악의 一部도 몰으면서 색쓰폰을 들고 음악이 예술을 世人에게 소개한 최초의 것이다, 라고 하는 듯한 표정으로 교문을 들어오는 학생이 잇는가 하면 그 뒤에는 양쪽 다리를 한 쪽에다 미러＜266＞ 너허도 넉넉할만치 널는 세-라-빤즈를 입고 여자들의 스카-트 모양으로 펄넉펄넉 나붓기며 거러오는 학생도 잇슴니다.
영국에서 건너왓다는 학생들은 영국풍으로 교육을 바더서 그런지 매일 아츰 마주치게되면 공손히
How are you beeling this morning?
참으로 제일 점잔흔 아츰 인사이다.
How you are가 보통이지만 미국 학생들은 거개가 매일 아츰 입을 열기가 실흔지, 만나게 되면 으레히 한 쪽 눈만 살작 감고 고개를 약간 갸웃하고 지나갈 뿐이다.
인생의 궁극의 목적은 향락에 잇다고 생각하는 미국인에게 잇서서 행렬은 야만적 습관이라고 하야 폐지할 것이라고 일부의 인사들은 매우 떠들지마는 대체로 큰 부호의 자식들과 영양들이 하는 행렬이기 때문에 문제는 그리 크게 일지 안는 모양이다. 그러나 혼례시에 空鑵을 자동차 뒤에 처매여 놋코 서로 끄러단이는 습관도 喧騷에도 심한 작란이라고 하여 금지되버리듯이 이 유쾌한 미국 대학생들의 재미잇는 교풍도 오라지 안어 업서지리라는 것을 생각하니 좀 섭섭한 감이 든다.

中國 大學과 學生 生活
上海大學 金想均

『대학은 墳墓이다.』라고
그 어느 때인가 상해의 어떤 신문의 논설에 실닌 것을 본 기억이 난다.
『大學은 樂園이다.』
이 말은 아메리카의 씨네마를 조와하는 매담님들의 함직한 소리이다.
北平, 天津, 南京, 廣東, 武漢 등 이만한 곳이 중국의 주요한 대학 소재지들이다.
(遼寧(奉天))에는 東北大學이 잇섯스나, 滿洲事變 後에는 업서지고 말엇음을 말하여 둔다.)
北平에는 大學이란 이름이 붓는 학교수가 무려 수십을 헤이게 되며 就中 국립북경대학, 燕京大學, 河北大學, 淸華大學, 등등은 유명한 대학들이다.
天津에는 南開大學, 北津大學이 잇고 南京에는 南京大學, 中山大學, 上海에는 南洋大學, 交通大學, 大夏大學, 復旦大學 이하 滬江, 쎈트·쫀스, 東吳 등 대학이 잇고, 廣東에는 中山大學, 嶺南大學, 武漢에는 唐生智時代에 한때 떠들든 대학이 잇다.
그 외에도 靑島에는 靑島大學, 濟南에는 濟魯大學등 이와 갓흔 지방적 대학도 적지 안흐나, 일일이 그것을 적어 가자면 아마 比律賓까지 가지 안으면 안될 것이다.
이로써 대체 이들의 소재지는 차저본＜267＞ 셈이다. 다음에 이들 대학생들의 잘아난 고향을 알어보기로 하자. 그야 물론 중국임에는 틀님이 업다. 그러나 다시 한 번 지도를 살펴보면 黑龍江, 다음에 蒙古, 新彊, 西藏으로부터 雲南, 廣西에까지, 이 一線이 소위 필자의 유토피아 「大西北」과 「國境」이다.
만약 여긔에 대한 風土記를 쓴다고 하면 흥미진진할 것이나 그만한 여유를 못가졋슴이 심히 유감되다.
다음에 南開대학의 재학생을 지방별로 살펴보면 河北, 廣東, 江蘇, 淅江, 安徽, 山東, 遼寧, 四川, 京北, 福健, 河南, 吉林, 湖北, 家肅, 湖南, 黑龍江, 陝西, 江西, 貴州, 熱河, 廣西, 雲南, 蒙古 등의 수자적 순서로 이외의 국외로부터 일본, 미국, 멕시코, 파나마, 영국(조선)-昭和 4년의 조사-등 사람들까지 들엇슴을 살펴볼 때, 여긔에 나타난 현상으로서 우리는 대체로 중국 각성 각부의 교육정도를 살필 수가 잇슬 것이다. 이것은 다만 한 개 대학의 조사에 의한 통계에 지나지 안으나 이로 미루워 짐작하기에 넉넉하다.
여긔에 잇서서 우리들은 이런 것을 넉넉히 상상하고도 남을 것이다. 저- 시베리아 세찬 바람 砂塵을 날니는 廣漠한 蒙古사막에서 駱駝를 몰아 머나 먼 중국 본토로 차자드는 好學의 士(섬배)들을...또는 四川의 險山幽谷에서 떼목(筏)과 쨩그(克戒)를 타고 上海까지 나오는 사람들을...또한 甘肅의 荒凉한 一本道를 寒風에 시달니우면서 머-ㄹ니 서울(首都)를 차자드는 農夫의 아들을...이러한 모든 풍경은 중국이 아니고는 어더볼 수 업는 특유한 일이다.
그런 반면에 중국의 대학생에게는 軍閥, 政治家, 富豪의 자제들도 적지안타. 이 소위 土豪劣紳의 자제들은 『대학』에 의하야 비로소 한 사람의 「인테리」로서의 탄생을 보게된다.
이런 사실은 현 중국에 잇서서 한갓 새로운 풍습일넌지는 몰을지나, 다른 데로 더 나아갈 데가 업는 그들의 당연히 떠러저야 할 곳이 「대학」이라는 것은 저- 넓고 넓은 토지를 가진 중국의 한 슲은 현상이 아니고 무엇이랴!
이러한 오-케스트라·멤버-에 의하야 연주되는 칼레이지·마-취는 물론 여긔에 잇서서 조화된 음악이 될 수는 업고, 쨔-즈와 갓흔 메로듸-도 업슴을 중국 대학생 생활에서 엿볼 수가 잇다.
금전을 물쓰듯 하는 자본가의 자식들이며, 鄕里에 잇는 어머니가 만드신 麻布등을 파라서 학비로 쓰는 학생들이며 학교에는 좀체로 출석도 안허며, 스포-쓰에 관극에 정신을 파라버리는 학생들은, 아마 어느 나라나 마찬가지일 것이다.
이러한 반영은 특히 상해에 잇서서 제일 잘 볼 수 잇는 사실이다.
