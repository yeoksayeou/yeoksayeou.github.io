Editor's Postscript

This July 7th marks the Fourth Anniversary of the China Incident (支那事變). To commemorate this significant day, marking four full years of the Holy War (聖戰) to create a New Order in East Asia, we have collected and published commemorative writings for the Fourth Anniversary of the Holy War from representatives of various religious groups and cultural organizations that bear the heavy responsibility for the spiritual and cultural life of our society.

Furthermore, to commemorate this day, we have featured a special report on the tragic situation of the anti-Japanese capital, Chungking, which has turned into a living hell. Despite the fact that anti-Japanese China has reached such extremes of exhaustion and ruin, we are able to live stable lives within the Imperial territory without even seeing enemy aircraft. This is solely due to the grace of the Emperor (皇恩) above and the benefit of the efforts of the Imperial Army officers and soldiers (皇軍將士) fighting bravely on the front lines. We cannot suppress our feelings of gratitude. To repay this kindness, we must further demonstrate our worthiness as subjects by achieving results on the home front (銃後) through Workplace Service (職域奉公) with One Hundred Million Hearts as One (一億一心).

Publishing Department Communication

The collection of travel essays, "Peninsula Mountains and Rivers" (半島山河), which went on sale last month, has garnered great acclaim right from the start (發賣劈頭); please place your orders before it sells out (賣書되기). Also, for laborers in farming, mountain, and fishing villages who can only read pure vernacular Korean script (純諺文), the historical popular novel "Sabi River" (泗泚水), set in the era of King Ŭija of Paekche (百濟義慈王時代) by the storyteller Mr. Sin Chŏng-ŏn (申鼎言氏), published in pamphlet style (팜푸렛트式) with Size 4 movable type, has been released and is drawing explosive popularity.<212>

**Summary**

This editor's postscript from the July 1941 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* commemorates the fourth anniversary of the Sino-Japanese War (referred to as the "China Incident" and "Holy War"). It strongly promotes the Japanese war effort, expressing gratitude to the Emperor and Imperial Army for protecting Korea from the perceived chaos engulfing China (specifically Chungking). It urges Koreans to support the war through dedicated work ("Workplace Service") on the home front. The postscript also includes promotional announcements for books published by the magazine, notably a collection of travel essays and a historical novel in pure Hangul aimed at rural laborers. For historians, this text is a stark example of pro-Japanese propaganda disseminated through Korean cultural mediums during the colonial period. It reveals the explicit framing of the war effort as righteous and beneficial to Koreans, the emphasis on Emperor worship and gratitude towards the Japanese military, and the specific targeting of different segments of the Korean population (including rural laborers) with tailored cultural products to ensure ideological conformity and mobilization.

編輯後記
今 7월 7일로써 支那事變第四周年을 마지하게 되었나이다. 東亞의 新天地를 창조하는 聖戰에 이미 네돌을 마지하게 되는 이 뜻깊은 날을 기념하기 위하여 我社會의 精神生活及文化生活의 重荷部門을 擔當하고 있는 各宗敎團體의 대표와 및 文化團體의 대표로부터 聖戰第四周年記念文을 蒐集하여 실렀나이다.

더욱이 이날을 기념코저 生地獄化한 抗都重慶의 慘狀을 特輯하였으며 抗戰支那는 이렇듯 乏廢의 극에 이르렀음에도 불구하고 我等은 帝國領土內에서 敵의 飛行機조차 볼 수 없이 安定된 生活을 보내게 됨은 오로지 우으로 皇恩과, 및 第一線에서 奮戰하고 있는 皇軍將士의 노력의 惠擇임에 삼가 감사의 念을 不禁하는 바이며, 이에 報恩코저 我等은 더욱 一億一心 職域奉公으로 銃後의 實을 걷우므로 국민된 자격을 잃지 말아야 할 것이외다.

出版部通信

去月부터 發賣를 개시한 紀行文集 「半島山河」는 發賣劈頭부터 大好評을 博하고 있으니 賣書되기 전에 注文하시옵소서. 그리고 農, 山, 漁村의 純諺文만 읽을 수 있는 勞務者를 위하여 百濟義慈王時代를 舞臺로 한 野談家 申鼎言氏의 歷史大衆小說 「泗泚水」(팜푸렛트式四號活字造版)가 出刊되여 瀑風的 人氣를 끌고 있나이다.<212>
