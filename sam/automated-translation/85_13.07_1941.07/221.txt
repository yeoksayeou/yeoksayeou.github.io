Prevention and Treatment of Beriberi, Common from Now On - Advice for Susceptible Individuals and on Food/Drink
						Okabe Taku, M.D.

From now through midsummer, many people suffer from beriberi (腳氣) every year.
Beriberi is a metabolic disease caused by a quantitative imbalance between ingested carbohydrates and vitamin B within the body. It varies depending on one's physical constitution (體質), with some having a relatively susceptible disposition (素質) and others not.

Big Eaters or Those with Weak Digestive Organs

Especially those with weak intestines who are particularly prone to flatulence, pregnant or postpartum women (姙産婦), and breastfeeding women (授乳中의 婦人) are of a susceptible constitution (■質 - likely 素質).
If you consume nutritious foods like fish (魚), meat (獸肉), eggs (卵), taro/potatoes (芋), beans (豆類), etc., far exceeding the necessary amount, the digestive organs cannot handle the burden (負擔). Consequently, the food mass (食塊) putrefies and ferments (腐敗醱酵) within the intestinal tract, and simultaneously, the ingested vitamin B is also decomposed (分解) and fails to be utilized (活用) within the body.
Even if you eat a lot of rice, a grain (穀食), it is fine if it is 70% polished rice (七分搗米) rich in vitamin B. However, if you eat white rice (白米) or the imported rice (外米) common these days, the result is consuming a lot of carbohydrates while lacking vitamin B. Therefore, individuals with a susceptible disposition should pay careful attention from their usual routine (平素),

Strengthen the Stomach and Intestines

and prevent putrefaction and fermentation within the intestinal tract. To do this, avoid overeating nutritious but easily putrefied foods like meat, fish, and taro/potatoes, and on the other hand, consume as much anti-beriberi (抗脚氣性) vitamin B as possible. These individuals have high body metabolism, leading to high consumption of vitamin B, thus easily falling into deficiency. Needless to say, vitamin B supplementation (補給) is necessary. As a method for preventing and treating beriberi, ingesting germinated rice (胚芽米) or barley rice (麥飯) is good for supplying ample vitamin B, but an inexpensive (低廉), highly effective (効果), and simple (簡易) method is taking "Wakomoto Tablets" (『錠劑와까모도』). Furthermore, it strengthens the stomach and intestines from their very tissues (組織), improves digestion and absorption (消化吸水), and has a very powerful (强力) direct effect (直接) on beriberi, making it more suitable (適當) as a preventive or therapeutic drug for beriberi than a single (單一) vitamin B preparation (劑).

Treatment of Diarrhea (泄瀉)

Diarrhea (泄瀉) is a state where inflammation (炎症) occurs on the inner surface of the intestine because something toxic (有毒) has formed within the intestinal tract. Therefore, simply trying to stop it using anti-diarrheal medicine is not good. In such cases, first use castor oil (ヒマシ油) to sweep out the toxins (毒素), then consume easily digestible things like porridge (죽), thin rice gruel (미음), meat broth (肉汁), or vegetable juice (野菜汁), and simultaneously take "Wakomoto" (「와까모도」). Especially when it has become chronic (慢性化), this kind of fundamental (基礎的) treatment is necessary.<126>

**Summary**

This 1941 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* discusses the causes, prevention, and treatment of beriberi (Kakki), a vitamin B deficiency disease common during summer. Written by Dr. Okabe Taku, it identifies dietary habits, particularly the consumption of polished white rice over less refined grains, and individual constitution as key factors. It recommends dietary adjustments, strengthening digestion, and specifically promotes the Japanese pharmaceutical product "Wakomoto Tablets" (containing yeast and Aspergillus fungi) as an effective and inexpensive remedy for both prevention and treatment, even including its use for diarrhea. A testimonial by Hirabayashi Yasushi recounts recovering from severe beriberi and associated digestive issues using Wakomoto.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about public health concerns (nutritional deficiencies like beriberi) in colonial Korea. It highlights the tension between traditional dietary staples (rice) and modern nutritional science (vitamin B), reflecting changing foodways, possibly influenced by the availability of imported or highly milled rice. Furthermore, it showcases the aggressive marketing of Japanese pharmaceutical products like Wakomoto within the colony, using scientific language (metabolism, vitamin B, medicinal fungi) combined with personal testimonials to appeal to consumers seeking modern medical solutions. The specific mention of "foreign rice" (外米) and pricing in Won/Chŏn situates it firmly within the colonial economic context.

昨年八月中旬頃부터 脚氣로써 脚部에 浮腫이 生기고, 步行도 困難하게되고 指先에가벼운 麻痺를 느끼게되었습니다, 그後九月一日의 저녁에 突然下腹部에 심한아픔을 느껴 걸으랴해도 다리가서지않고어쩔수얹이 匍匐하게되어 約한時間쯤식벽어있는 아픔에 마른땀이 밧작나는것같었습니다---드듸어 病院에 入院하여 나는이 腹痛과 泄瀉는 脚氣의 副産物임으로 第一에이것을 治療하여야되겠다고 生覺하여 마침그때 新聞廣告로 『錠劑아까모도』가 脚氣에 効果가있다함을 알고 服用하였습니다. 그런즉그처름심하든 大腸가다루도 次次順調롭게되고 服用後○日後에 便通도 常態로도라서게되었습니다. 여기에 元氣를얻어 더욱 繼續하여 服用하던中 겨우 步行되기까지 되었습니다, 나는 生覺하였습니다.
脚氣에 걸리게되면 胃腸이나뻐진다 그러나 지금은 胃腸도 恢復된터이니까 脚氣의 攻畧도그렇게 至難한것이아니겠다고 그래서 「錠劑아까모도」를다시 一回에 入錠식 服用하기로하고 醫師의 注射를 받었습니다. 그러니까 脚氣는 날로나서 脚氣衝心같은것도 完全히막었을뿐아니라 指先의 麻痺같은것도 次次가벼워졌습니다. 다리는 아즉은 步行하기 多少苦痛을느끼나마 이것도 不日內로 恢復되리라 生覺합니다.＜127＞
