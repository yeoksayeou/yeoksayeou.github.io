Chongqing Rice Price 180 Yuan

Selby Unoka, the Far East manager of the Reuters news agency, who stopped in Hanoi on his way back to Shanghai from Chongqing, spoke about the recent situation in Chongqing as follows.
"In Chongqing, I met key figures such as Chiang Kai-shek, K'ung Hsiang-hsi, and Chou En-lai, but everyone is keeping silent on the Nationalist-Communist issue. The gasoline (까소린) shortage in Chongqing is extremely severe, so transportation is by rickshaw and palanquin (駕籠), and the fares are exorbitant. Rice costs 180 yuan per *sŏk* (石) (5 *tu* 7 *sŭng* - approx 103 liters), which is 100% (10割) more expensive than in Shanghai. On the way back, I passed through Kunming via the Burma Road (비루마·루-트), and it took 10 days by truck (추럭) to reach Lashio. The bridge over the Salween River (살우인河), which the Japanese army bombed, is located in a mountain valley (산골째기) and was just wide enough for one car to pass; the bomb hit it directly. Currently, they are moving cars across using ferries (渡丹), and hundreds of cars are waiting. I also waited there for 2,000-some hours <100>."

US, Poll on Entering the War

According to an announcement by Dr. George Gallup (쬬지·카라푸), director of the American Institute of Public Opinion, generally recognized as the barometer of US public opinion, the number of people saying the US would enter the war if Japan advances into the Dutch East Indies (蘭印) has increased. According to that poll:
February Poll
Pro-War Entry: 39%
Anti-War Entry: 46%
Undecided: 15%
March Poll
Pro-War Entry: 40%
Anti-War Entry: 39%
Undecided: 21%
This trend of US public opinion toward Japan becoming so hardline in just a few weeks seems to be due to mounting antipathy towards Japan further consolidating its position through strengthening the Axis (樞軸强化).

US, Army Monthly Salaries

The US Department of War recently announced the monthly salaries for officers and soldiers as follows:
First 4 months of enlistment: $21
After 4 months: $30
Next: $36
Corporal (伍長): $54
Sergeant (軍曹): $72
Same rank, technical/guard field (同技衛方面): $84
Master Sergeant (曹長): $126
Second Lieutenant (少尉): $135
Captain (大尉): $150
Major (少佐): $200
Lieutenant Colonel (中佐): $350
A Colonel (大佐) has an annual salary (年俸) of $5,000, and General Officers (將官) receive from $6,000 to $8,000.

Construction of 65,000-Ton Ships

According to a US House naval expert, the 5 battleships to be built as part of the US Two-Ocean Navy plan will be 65,000 tons.

Tobacco Famine in Britain

Recently, it is said that the tobacco famine is severe in Britain too. Often, one cannot obtain cigarettes even after visiting two or three shops. It is said to be worse in the provinces. There were places here and there that had closed their shops, displaying signs saying "Sold Out" (品切). According to Tobacco Controller Mr. Maxwell (마크스웰), before the war, 195 million pounds (폰드) of tobacco were put on the market, so it is strange that there should be shortages. If one digs into the causes, firstly, since the start of the war, more women have entered society to work, leading to increased smoking among women; secondly, demand for tobacco has increased due to night work (夜業) in wartime industries; thirdly, the biggest reason seems to be hoarding (買蓄).

**Summary**

This article from July 1941 compiles several news briefs about the international situation during World War II. It covers the harsh conditions in Chongqing (high rice prices, fuel shortages, transport difficulties on the Burma Road), shifting US public opinion towards entering the war against Japan, details on US Army salaries and plans for massive new battleships, and a severe tobacco shortage in Britain attributed to increased demand and hoarding. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it reflects the information landscape available to Koreans under Japanese rule. It presents news about Japan's enemies (China, US, Britain), highlighting their difficulties (Chongqing's economy, UK shortages) but also their growing resolve and military preparations (US opinion shift, naval buildup), potentially filtered through a pro-Axis or Japanese lens, though the reporting seems relatively factual based on Allied sources (Reuters, Gallup). The details on wartime economies and societal changes offer insight into the global impact of the conflict as perceived in colonial Korea.

重慶米價 180元

重慶으로부터 上海에 돌아오는 길에 하노이에 들렸든 로이텔通信社 極東支配人 셀삐이·우노카는 重慶의 근황을 다음과 같이 말하였다.
「重慶에서는 蔣介石, 孔祥熙, 周恩來 등의 要人을 만났는데 누구나 國共문제에는 입을 담을고 있다. 重慶에서 까소린 부족은 대단히 심하여 교통은 인력거와 駕籠으로 하는데 운임은 턱없이 비싸다. 쌀은 一石(5斗7升)180元으로 上海보다 10割이나 비싸다. 돌아오는 길에 昆明으로 비루마·루-트에 들렸는데 라시노까지 추럭으로 10일간을 허비하였다. 日本軍이 폭파한 살우인河의 다리는 산골째기에 있어 자동차 1대가 겨우 통할 넓이로 폭탄이 바로 명중되었었다. 현재는 渡丹로 자동차를 옴기고 있는데 수백대의 자동차가 기다리고 있다. 나도 2,000數時間이나<100> 거기서 기다렸다.

米, 參戰의 輿論調

米國輿論의 바로메-타라고 일반에 인정되는 米國輿論調査所의 所長 쬬지·카라푸 박사의 발표에 의하면 만일 日本이 蘭印에 진출하면 米國은 참전하리라고 하는 자가 증가되었다고 한다. 그 표의 의하면
二月調               三月調
開戰贊成者 39％      40％
不贊成者   46％      39％
贊否不明   15％      21％
겨우 數週間中에 그와 같이 强硬하게 된 米國對日輿論의 경향은 日本이 樞軸强化로써 더욱 地步를 견고케 하는 데 대한 반감이 몰려들기 때문이라고 보여진다.

米國陸軍省에서는 최근 장병의 월급을 다음과 같이 발표하였다.

六萬五千噸艦建造

米國下院海軍專門家의 말에 의하면 米國兩洋艦隊計劃의 일부로써 建造할 5隻의 戰艦은 65,000噸이라고 한다.

英國에 담배 飢饉

최근 英國에도 담배 飢饉이 심하다고 한다. 두세집을 돌아도 얻지 못하는 일이 많다. 지방에 가면 더 심하다 한다. 품절이란 표찰을 내걸고 매점을 닫친데도 군데군데 있었다.
煙草統制官 마크스웰씨 말에 의하면 開戰前 1億9千5百萬폰드의 煙草를 시장에 내놨는데 품절이 된다는 것은 이상한 일이 아닐 수 없다 한다.
여기에 원인을 캔다면 첫째 開戰 이후 사회에 나와 일하는 부인이 많음에 따라 부인의 喫煙이 많아진 것, 둘째, 戰時産業의 夜業으로 煙草의 需要가 증가된 것, 셋째, 제일 큰 원인이 되는 것은 「買蓄」하기 때문인 듯 하다는 것이다.
