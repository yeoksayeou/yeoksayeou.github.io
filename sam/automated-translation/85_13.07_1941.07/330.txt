Poet's Inner Thoughts
If you were to have a study, what would you name it? Or what have you named your current one?

As Pach'osil (芭蕉室 - Banana Plant Room)

Wŏlt'an Pak Chonghwa (月灘 朴鍾和)

I would like to be called the "Master of the Pach'osil (芭蕉室主人)." I originally have an affinity for banana plants (芭蕉), so I planted four or five stalks by the garden. This is because I like the fluttering, green leaves that fully embrace the sentiment of the southern lands. Doesn't it evoke an East Asian poetic charm?
I currently go by "Master of the Chosuru (釣水樓主人)." There is a half-acre square pond by the garden. In this place, young golden and silver scaled fish leap about energetically. Sometimes I cast a fishing line. But can one really catch fish? I just let down a straight fishing hook. As it happens, a plaque with the calligraphy "Chosuong (釣水翁 - Old Man Fishing in the Water)" by Master Ch'usa (秋史先生) is hanging there.

As Maehwaok (梅花屋 - Plum Blossom House)

Karam Yi Pyŏnggi (가람 李秉岐)

I have one room of my own. It cannot be called a study (書齋) or a detached house (別堂). And since I don't know when something that suits my heart might be realized, I don't engage in such fantasies. However, on one wall of my room, I hang a plaque that says "Maehwaok (梅花屋)." This is the calligraphy of Kim Ch'usa (金秋史), in a strange style, neither seal script (非篆) nor clerical script (非隷). Everyone who sees it asks doubtfully, "Is that calligraphy?" Still, I like it,<176> and I like the characters "Maehwaok," and I like that it was hand-carved by my friend Mae Hŏn (梅軒). I use this as my house name (堂號) and have also engraved a book seal (藏書印) that reads "Maehwaok Chinwan (梅花屋珍玩 - Treasured Curios of the Plum Blossom House)." And I spend my time in this room with daffodils (水仙), plum blossoms (梅花), orchids (蘭), old books (古書), and the kŏmun'go (玄琴).

As Soyojŏng (逍遙亭 - Leisurely Stroll Pavilion)

Kim Ansŏ (金岸曙)

Generally, if you want to hear opinions on such matters, it would be proper for the Samch'ŏlli Company (三千里社) to build even a small place and then ask what the house name should be. To persistently bother someone about something that doesn't exist or has absolutely no possibility of coming into being is, firstly, a waste of time, and secondly, makes it awkward to give any answer. However, since you've asked after all this trouble, I think I'll call the house Ansŏ Soyojŏng (岸曙逍遙亭). There's no particular reason, it just somehow feels right to me.

"Chimnuru" (枕流樓 - Pillow Stream Pavilion)

Chŏng Chiyong (鄭芝溶)

To lay one's head high on a pillow facing the murmuring stream and sleep—

As Ch'ohyangam (草香庵 - Grass Fragrance Hermitage)

Yi Hyosŏk (李孝石)

Shall I call it Ch'ohyangam? I intend to build the house by piling up natural stones or, if not, by stacking logs, and I will let weeds (雜草) grow thickly around the house. Weeds not only have a rich fragrance (馥馥) but also possess a fine atmosphere. If annual wildflowers (雜花) are also allowed to grow profusely (簇生) among the weeds, wouldn't the charm of the study hermitage (書庵) be unsurpassed?<177>

**Summary**

Five prominent Korean writers of the colonial era respond to a survey question from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine about what they would name (or have named) their study. Pak Chonghwa chooses names related to nature (banana plants, fishing pavilion). Yi Pyŏnggi describes his actual "Maehwaok" (Plum Blossom House), filled with culturally significant items like Ch'usa's calligraphy and traditional objects. Kim Ansŏ offers a somewhat sarcastic reply, suggesting the magazine should provide a study before asking for its name, but proposes "Ansŏ Soyojŏng." Chŏng Chiyong gives a brief, poetic name "Chimnuru" (Pillow Stream Pavilion). Yi Hyosŏk envisions a rustic "Ch'ohyangam" (Grass Fragrance Hermitage) surrounded by fragrant weeds and wildflowers. The article provides insight into the personal tastes and cultural inclinations of these writers during a difficult period. For historians, the piece reveals the enduring connection to traditional aesthetics (nature, calligraphy, scholarly items) and perhaps subtle forms of cultural resistance or escapism amidst the pressures of colonial rule, while Kim Ansŏ's response hints at the underlying material difficulties or a critique of the inquiry's relevance.

詩客心懷
書室을 하나 가지게 되면 이름을 무엇이라 짓겠습니까? 또는 현재 무어라고 지으셨습니까?

芭蕉室로

月灘 朴鍾和

「芭蕉室主人」이라 가지고 싶읍니다. 原來性情이 芭蕉를 좋아해서 庭畔에 四五株芭蕉를 심었습니다. 그 너울너울하고 靑靑한 南國情趣를 담쑥이 껴안은 입새가 좋은 때문이올시다. 東洋詩趣가 나지 않음니까.
『釣水樓主人』이라구 현재 가지고 있습니다. 庭畔에 半畝方塘이 있습니다. 이곧에 어린 金鱗銀尺이 펄덕펄덕 뛰놉니다. 때로 낙시대를 던집니다. 그러나 고기야 잡을 수가 있습니까. 고든 낙시를 드리우지오. 마침 扁額에는 秋史先生의 글시 『釣水翁』이 부터있습니다.

梅花屋으로

가람 李秉岐

나는 나의 방 한간을 가지고 있습니다. 書齌라든가 別堂이랄 것도 없습니다. 그리고 마음에 맞을 그것은 언제 實現될는지 모르므로 그런 공상도 아니합니다. 그러나 나는 나의 방 한쪽  壁 우에는 「梅花屋」이라는 懸板 하나를 걸어두고 봅니다. 이건 金秋史의 글시인바 非篆非隷, 이상야릇한 글십니다. 보는 이마다 「저게 글신가」하고 疑訝를 하는 것입니다. 그래도 나는 그것이 마음에 들고<176> 또는 「梅花屋」이라는 文字도 마음에 들고 또는 나의 親舊 梅軒의 手刻이 마음에 들읍니다. 나는 이것으로 堂號도 삼고 「梅花屋珍玩」이라는 藏書印도 사겼습니다. 그리고 水仙, 梅花, 蘭, 古書, 玄琴과 함께 이 방에서 지내는 바입니다.

逍遙亭으로

金岸曙

대체 이런 것에 대한 意見을 들으랴고 하거든 三千里社에서 조고만한 것이나마 하나 지어주면서 집이름을 어떻게 지을 터이냐 하는 것이 옳은 일일가 합니다. 공연이 나치도 아니한 또는 낳을 가능조차 바이 없는 것을 가지고 이러니 저러니 하면서 사람을 졸라대이니 첫재에는 時間이 아깝고 둘재에는 무어라고 대답하기가 거북하외다. 그러나 이왕 모처럼 물으섰으니 집이름을 岸曙逍遙亭이라고 할가 합니다. 이유는 아모것도 없고 그러는 것이 엇재 그런지 맘에 들기 때문이외다.

「枕流樓」

鄭芝溶

시내물 지줄거리는 向으로 벼개를 높이 하고 자기 위하야-

草香庵으로

李孝石

草香庵이라고 할까요. 집은 自然石을 싸어올니거나 그러치 않으면 토막나무를 싸어서 짓되 집 주위에는 雜草가 욱어지게 하렵니다. 雜草라는 건 향기도 馥馥하거니와 운치가 좋은 것입니다. 잡초 속에 1年生의 雜花를 또한 되구말구 簇生케 한다면 書庵의 風致가 그에 더함이 없을까 함니다.<177>
