**Summary**

This entry lists the contents of a feature section in the July 1941 issue of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*, titled "Collection of Poems and Writings on Travel in Korea from Various Mainland Japanese People". It includes pieces by prominent Japanese figures Tsuku'i Tatsuo (on "The Problem of Korea"), Tomiyasu Fūsei (haiku poet, on Kyŏngju), Ishii Baku (modern dancer, on Korean dancer Cho T'aegwŏn), and Kōen Kyoshi (likely the haiku master Takahama Kyoshi), along with a news clipping about former Governor-General Ugaki and a poetry collection by the Korean writer Yi Sŏk-hun. For historians, this collection is interesting as it showcases Japanese intellectual and artistic engagement with colonial Korea during the height of wartime mobilization (1941). The inclusion of topics ranging from political commentary and travel impressions to critiques of Korean dance and Japanese haiku composition provides insight into the diverse, yet Japan-centric, perspectives being curated for a Korean audience by this magazine.
