Magazine Title: 삼천리 제7권 제1호
Publication Date: 1935년 01월 01일
Article Title: 갈매기, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集(1), 名作詩篇 (Seagull, Masterpiece Selection from 20 Years of the Peninsula's New Literary World (1), Masterpiece Poems)
Author: 鄭芝溶
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Looking back, there isn't a single hill. Not a single pine tree, not a single trembling blade of grass.
The sun boils in the middle of the sky like a platinum (白金) cauldron.
The round sea now spins like a top. Seagull. Seagull. You make the sound of a cat.

Could a cat live in a place like this. Where were you born. Your neck is so white. Your wings are white too. Your claws are clean. You bite the leaping fish.
When the white waves surge in, when the blue water's curves settle down.

Seagull. Seagull. As if knowing, as if not knowing, you came into being. As for me, they say I was born before a small oil lamp when the black night rain cried on the stepping stone?
As for me, I have a mother. I have a father too. Their hair is white.

I am a youth with a slender waist. There is one I have longed for, all alone.
They say I left behind the village where the jujube trees bloom.

Seagull. Seagull. You wind the waves with your neck. You push with your claws.
You enter the water. You soar, you drift. You fly sideways.

Do you live without eating rice? My hands are still swollen.
The clouds above the horizon (水平線) are strange. The wind in the sail is strange.
Arms crossed, eyes closed. The sea's loneliness is felt like this black necktie.
(1927. 8)<177>

**Summary**

This poem by Chŏng Chiyong contrasts the speaker's human existence, marked by family ties, longing, and a sense of displacement, with the seemingly free and natural life of a seagull against the vast, indifferent backdrop of the sea and sky. The speaker reflects on his own birth, his family, the home he left behind, and a profound sense of loneliness, symbolized by the sea and felt tangibly like a "black necktie." From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem's themes of alienation, rootlessness ("돌아 보와야 언덕 하나 업다"), and yearning for a lost home ("대추나무 꼿피는 동네 다 두고 왓단다") resonate strongly with the experiences of Koreans during the Japanese occupation (poem written 1927). Historians might find the stark expression of individual loneliness and the contrast between the constrained human world and the perceived freedom of nature particularly reflective of the psychological landscape of the era.

갈매기, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						鄭芝溶
돌아 보와야 언덕 하나 업다. 솔나무 하나 떠는 풀닙 하나 업다.
해는 한울 한 복판에 白金독 아니처럼 끌코.
똥그란 바다는 이제 팽이처럼 돌아간다. 갈메기야. 갈메기야. 늬는 고양이 소리를 하는구나.
고양이가 이런데 살이야 잇나. 늬는 어데서 낫니. 목이야 희기도 희다. 나래도 희다. 발톱이 깨긋하다. 뛰는 고기를 문다.
흰 물결이 치여들 때 푸른물ㅅ구비가 나려 안질 때.
갈메기야 갈메기야. 아는 듯 몰으는 듯 늬는 생겨낫지. 내사 검은 밤ㅅ비가 섬돌 우에 울 때 호롱ㅅ불 아페 낫다더라?
내사 어머니도 잇다. 아버지도 잇다. 그 이들은 머리가 희시다.
나는 허리가 간은 청년이라. 내 홀로 사모한 이도 잇다.
대추나무 꼿피는 동네 다 두고 왓단다.
갈메기야. 갈메기야. 늬는 목으로 물결은 감는다. 발톱으로 민다.
물속을 든다. 솟는다 떠돈다. 모로 날은다.
늬는 쌀을 아니 먹고사나? 내 손이 야짓 부푸러젓다.
水平線 우에 구름이 이상하다. 돗폭에 바람이 이상하다.
팔뚝을 끼고 눈을 감엇다. 바다의 외로움 이 검은 넥타이처럼 만저진다.
(1927. 8)＜177＞
