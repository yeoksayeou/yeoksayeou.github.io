Wandering Beijing, Peninsula New Literary Scene: A Collection of Masterpieces from the Last Twenty Years
						O Sangsun (吳想殉)

Seeing in a dream a friend forever parted from my hometown,
Saying he followed me well all the way to Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul),
Feeling this dimly as I woke this morning,
Immersed in an unspeakable sorrow—

Having sent far away to his country a friend
with whom I shared hardship and joy in the same foreign land,
a friend unable to see "spring,"
Whenever I look at the bear-like black long leather coat he left behind,
Thinking, where might you be wandering now, dragging your cane,
the loneliness of the heart!

Behind the rushing cart,
A seven or eight-year-old threadbare Chinese girl
threw aside her hands and chased after it.
I tossed her a single copper coin,
But hoping for another,
She followed for nearly five *ri*, competing with the coolie,
under the sun beating down like fire—
The moment I noticed, my heart felt constricted.

Sitting high atop a man-pulled cart,
A gentleman wrapped in silk came along, nodding his head. Before him,
A Chinese boy of about ten years old
prostrated himself, touched the ground with both hands,
Struck his forehead on the ground again and again, drawing blood—
The gentleman passed by as if he hadn't seen or heard,
without any expression of feeling.

With one hand,
Clutching a suckling infant held to her breast,
The other hand held high and open,
A Chinese wife with bound feet running after a horse carriage—

Having given my entire fortune, a single silver dollar (洋銀 一圓),
to an old begging woman,
Looking back at myself, penniless,
Just as the hypothetical temptation arises, thinking I should have kept it aside,
I hide myself in a dark, narrow alleyway overgrown <170>
with prison-like walls.
As if someone were looking into my heart.

In the middle of a dusty crossroads
where one's feet sink in,
Whenever I see a Chinese dog sleeping its afternoon nap,
its four legs sprawled carelessly,
I want to cry.
Whether a cart brushes past its mouth,
Or an automobile comes driving, honking its horn,
That appearance, as if saying "I know nothing,"
Seems also most grand.
At the same time, I think of the Chinese coolie.
And then I imagine the entire Chinese people.
- From Beijing, 1918 - <171>
<170-171>

**Summary**

This poem by O Sangsun, written in 1918 but published in 1935, captures the poet's melancholic observations during his time in Beijing ("Wandering Beijing"). He contrasts the memory of a deceased friend with vivid depictions of extreme poverty and suffering among the Chinese populace: a desperate young girl chasing a cart for money, a boy kowtowing bloody for naught, a mother with bound feet running after a carriage, and the general destitution symbolized by a listless dog. The poet feels both pity and a sense of shame or unease about his own reactions, hiding when tempted by regret after an act of charity. The poem ends linking the image of an indifferent dog to the perceived state of Chinese coolies and the Chinese people as a whole. For historians, the poem offers a Korean intellectual's perspective on China in the late 1910s, portraying Chinese suffering with a mix of sympathy and perhaps a sense of shared hardship or detached observation under the broader context of foreign influence and instability in East Asia. The depiction of Beijing and its inhabitants through a colonial Korean lens provides insight into inter-Asian perceptions during a period of significant upheaval.

放浪의 北京, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						吳想殉
故鄕에서 永遠히 여윈 벗을 꿈에 보고
잘도 京京까지 딸하와 주엇다고
희미하게 늣기면서 잠께운 오늘 아츰
말하기 어려운 悲哀에 잠겨-

가튼 旅域에 苦와 樂을 한가지 하던
「봄」 못하는 벗을 멀리 그의 나라로 보내버리고
그의 버서두고 간 곰 가튼 검은 長革 바라볼 적마다
그대 집행이를 끌고 只今쯤은 어느 곳을 彷徨할고 생각
하는 마음의 외로움이어!

달려가는 수레 뒤로
손 버리고 쪼차오든
일곱 여덟(살)의 헐버슨 中國사람의 딸
銅錢 한푼을 던저 주엇더니
또다른 것을 바라고
거의 五里나 되는 길에 『쿠리』와 다투어
불 가티 나리 쪼이는 太陽 밋헤
딸하 오던 少女-
發見하던 瞬間의 나의 마음 메여지는 듯 하얏다.

사람이 끄는 車 우에 놉히 안저
고개ㅅ짓하며 오는 비단에 요싸힌 紳士보고 그 압헤
업드려 두 손으로 땅을 집고
니마로 땅을 찌코 또 찌어 피흘려 내던
열살 前后의 中國사람의 아들-
紳士는 보고도 못드른 듯이 아모 感覺의 表情도 업게
지나가고 말앗다.

한 손으로
가슴에 안키어 젓빠는 갓난애기 부둥켜 잡고
다른 손을 놉히 들어 벌리고
馬車 뒤로 달여가는 발 좁힌 中國사람의 안해-

온 미천이던 洋銀 一圓을 빌억질하는
老婆에게 주어노코
無一分의 나를 돌아보아
쪽애 두엇드면 하는 假想誘惑이 일어나랴 할제
나는 監獄 가튼 담들이 욱어진＜170＞
컹컴한 좁은 골목길 몸을 감추운다.
누가 나의 마음 족을 드려다 보는 듯해서.

발이 빠지는 몬지싸힌
네거리 한복판에
네 발을 되는대로 뻐더 바리고
낫잠 자는 中國개 볼 때마다 울고십다.
수레가 그 입흐로 시치고 지나가나
自働車가 소리를 지르며 몰아오나
「나 모른다는」듯한 그 꼴은
가장 偉大한 듯도 하다.
나는 同時에 中國苦力을 생각한다.
그리고 또 中國사람 全體를 聯想한다.
-1918年 北京서-＜171＞
