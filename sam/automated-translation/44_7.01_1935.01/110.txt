笑話 (Jokes/Funny Stories)

Appetite
"Somehow, I dislike 'eating'."
"Why is that?"
"Because when you eat, your appetite disappears, and can you imagine a life without appetite?"

A Spot-on Fortune
Fortune teller: "If you live for a very, very long time, you will become greatly famous."
Young man: "For what?"
Fortune teller: "For living long!"

Lesson
Mother: "I told you not to use such vulgar words."
Sŏnhŭi: "Oh, Mother, Shakespeare (쎅스피어) said these words."
Mother: "From now on, don't play with kids like that." <81>

**Summary**

This article presents three short, simple jokes. The first reflects ironically on appetite, the second is a play on words about achieving fame through longevity, and the third depicts a humorous misunderstanding where a mother confuses Shakespeare with a neighbourhood child influencing her daughter. From a historical perspective, these jokes illustrate the type of light, everyday humour consumed by the Korean public during the colonial period. The mention of Shakespeare, even in a context of misunderstanding, hints at the gradual influx of Western cultural figures into popular awareness, while the overall tone provides a glimpse into common social interactions and humour styles of the 1930s.

笑話
食慾
「나는 엇전지 『먹개질』이 실테그려.」
「그건 또 엇재서.」
「먹으면 식욕이 업서지니까 식욕 업는 인생을 자네 생각 할 수 잇는가」

꼭 맛는 占卜
術장이 「당신은 훨-신 오래 살면 크게 유명하게 되겟서요.」
청년 「무엇으로?」
占術장이 「장수한 것으로!」

敎訓
어머니 「글세 그러케 얄구즌 말을 쓰지 말나니까.」
善姬 「에그, 어머니두, 이 말은 쎅스피어가 한 말이야요.」
어머니 「이제부터는 그런 애들과 갓치 놀지말어라.」＜81＞
