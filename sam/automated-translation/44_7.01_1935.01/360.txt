I Want to Go, Anthology of Masterpieces from the Peninsula's New Literary Scene of the Last 20 Years
						Yi Ŭn-sang (李殷相)

I Want to Go

My hometown's southern sea, its blue water appears before my eyes
How could I forget, even in dreams, that calm home sea
Even now, those seabirds must be flying; I want to go, oh, I want to go
Those friends I played with as a child, I miss them
Wherever I may go, how could I forget those friends I ran and played with at home
What are they all doing today? I want to see them, oh, I want to see them

Those seabirds, those friends, are all in my hometown
Why is it that I have come to live away somewhere?
Shall I cast everything aside and go back, go back?

Go back, gather together, and live like the old days
Dress my heart in colorful clothes, laugh and laugh as I pass the days
Let's seek that time, that time without tears, let's seek it

A Night at Sŏngbul Temple (成佛寺)

Deep in the night at Sŏngbul Temple, the faint sound of the wind chime
The head monk is asleep, and the guest listens alone
Oh, bell, sleep too, let me weep alone

When it rings 'ding-dong', I worry if it will ring more
When it ends, I wait for the sound, hoping to hear it again
All night long, accompanied by the wind chime's sound, I cannot find sleep
(1931. 8. 19)<168>

**Summary**

The text presents two poems by Yi Ŭn-sang (李殷相). The first, "I Want to Go" (가곱하), expresses a deep yearning for the poet's hometown, specifically the southern sea and childhood friends, reflecting feelings of nostalgia and displacement. The second poem, "A Night at Sŏngbul Temple" (成佛寺의 밤), depicts a sense of lonely contemplation, focusing on the sound of a temple wind chime heard throughout a sleepless night. From a historical perspective, these poems offer insight into personal sentiments and lyrical expression during the Japanese colonial period. They reflect themes of longing for a lost past or a place of belonging, and a quiet, introspective melancholy, which might resonate with the broader experience of Koreans living under colonial rule, even when not overtly political.

가곱하, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						李殷相
가곱하
내 고향 남쪽바다 그, 파란물 눈에 보이네
꿈엔들 이즈리오 그, 잔잔한 고향바다
지금도 그, 물새들 날으리 가곱아라 가곱하
어린제 가치 놀든 그 동무들 그리워라
어대 간들 이즈리오 그, 뛰놀든 고향동무
오늘은 다 무얼하느고 보곱하라 보곱하

그, 물새 그 동무들 고향에 다 잇는데
나는 웨 어이다가 떠나살게 되엇는고
온갓 것 다 뿌리치고 돌아갈까 돌아가

가서 한댸 얼려 녯날 갓치 살고지라
내 마음 색동옷 입혀 웃고웃고 지나고저
그날 그 눈물업든 때를 찻아가자 찻아가

成佛寺의 밤
成佛寺 깁흔 밤에 그윽한 풍경소리
주승은 잠이 들고 客이 홀로 듯는구나
저손아 마자 잠들어 혼자 울게 하여라

댕그렁 울릴 제면 더 울릴까 맘졸이고
끗인젠 또 들리라 소리나기 기다려서
새도록 풍경소리 더리고 잠 못 일워 하노라
(1931. 8. 19)＜168＞
