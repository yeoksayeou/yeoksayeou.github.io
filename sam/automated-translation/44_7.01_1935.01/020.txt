Current Strength of the Sijunghoe (時中會)
Currently, the total membership of the Sijunghoe (時中會) is about 250 members, and the province where its influence is most "greatly flourishing" (大振) is P'yŏngan Namdo. Thus, physician Yi Tong-yŏng, and also Kim Hŭng-ch'an have joined (rumors were heard that Kim Nŭng-su, Vice Chairman of the P'yŏngyang Chamber of Commerce, and lawyer Yi Ki-ch'an had joined, but they reportedly haven't yet). After P'yŏngan Namdo comes Kyŏngsang Namdo, followed by Kyŏngsang Pukto, and then Ch'ungch'ŏng Pukto, Chŏlla Pukto, Hamgyŏng Namdo, Hamgyŏng Pukto, and Hwanghae-do in that order. In Ch'oe Rin's home province of Hamnam (咸南), Provincial Vice-Chairman Yi Hŭi-byŏn, Pang Ŭi-sŏk of Pukch'ŏng, and Yŏm Kyŏng-hun of Tanch'ŏn have all joined. In Hambuk (咸北) too, government-appointed provincial assembly members Hwang Chong-guk and Pang Nak-sŏn of Ch'ŏngjin have joined.
Generally speaking, about one-third of the 250-plus members are public officials such as members of Provincial Assemblies (道議), County Councils (郡評議), or City/Township Councils (府面協議員). It is reported that the association is currently endeavoring to establish an incorporated foundation of one million won, or at least several hundred thousand won.

The Power of the Presbyterian Church, Spending 1.2 Million Won Annually
Some say that after the March 1st Movement (己未運動), the influence of Christianity waned somewhat, while others say it is growing even stronger.<18>
This observation may differ depending on the observer's perspective, but in any case, leaving aside the Methodist Church (監理敎), if we look just at the power of the Presbyterian Church (長老敎會) alone, we can see that it is indeed growing astonishingly. Currently, there are about 3,000 Presbyterian churches throughout all of Korea (全朝鮮), and there are almost no places without church members across 218 counties (郡), 2,493 townships (面), and 28,240 villages (洞里). In terms of church distribution and placement, it is said that there is more than one church per township on average.
The number of adherents reaches 281,918, the number of pastors is 456, and the number of elders is 2,429. What is most astonishing is that in the approximately 10 years since the Kimi year (己未年 - 1919), the number of baptized members increased by 22,000, and catechumens by over 70,000, totaling 100,000 new converts.
Also noteworthy is that last year alone, excluding all mission support funds coming from foreign countries, the money spent purely by the Presbyterian Church on evangelism and education projects amounted to 1.2 million won.

A certain incident involving Ms. 0 and Mr. 0, which had long been gossiped about in the capital's streets (京巷), was recently completely resolved with the withdrawal of the complaint. Hŭngsadan Translating "Three Principles of the People"
The Hŭngsadan (興士團) intends to produce a complete translation of Sun Yat-sen's (孫文) "Three Principles of the People" (三民主義) and began the translation work this past summer. For this reason, a certain great expert in Chinese literature is commuting daily to the library near Namdaemun and diligently writing. In any case, the complete work "Three Principles of the People," into which Sun Yat-sen poured his entire spirit, has mostly appeared in Korean discourse (半島論壇) thus far only in excerpted translations of excerpted translations. This time, it is said that a complete, unabridged translation will be released, raising great expectations in the reading society.

Soviet Far East Military Strength
1935 begins today. According to what has been gathered from a certain informed source, the military preparedness of the Russian Army (露西亞陸軍) in the Far East (極東) is as follows:
Infantry: 10 Divisions, Cavalry: 2 Divisions, KGB(?) troops (께, 베, 우, 軍隊 - *possibly NKVD troops or border guards, unclear abbreviation*): 8 Regiments, Settler-Soldier Troops (屯田兵 軍隊): 3 Divisions. Total troop strength above: 200,000 men.
Aircraft: 500 planes, Tanks: 650 vehicles, etc.
Also, it is said there are several submarines in Vladivostok (海參威), with about 10 more under construction.

Federation Vice-General Secretary Goes to Shanghai
Mr. Hyŏn Tong-wan, who was the Vice-General Secretary of the Christian Youth Association Federation (基督靑年會聯合會) located in Chongno, finished the 70th birthday celebration for Chwaong Yun Ch'i-ho (佐翁 尹致昊) and abruptly left for Shanghai the next day, returning a few days ago. After returning from Shanghai, Mr. Hyŏn is reportedly still working as Vice-General Secretary.

Newspaper Company Profits
The Chosŏn Ilbo is now breaking even, the Tonga Ilbo is said to make a profit of at least a few hundred won a year, and the Chungang Ilbo is also approaching the break-even point. Looking abroad, the "London Times" (倫敦타임쓰) is said to make a profit of 7 million won annually, and even the newly rising "Yomiuri Shimbun" (讀賣新聞) in Tokyo reportedly sees a net profit of 20,000 won per month.

The Boy Philosopher's "Kantō no Kan"
The essay "Kantō no Kan" (岩頭之感 - Thoughts from the Cliff Top) by the suicidal boy philosopher, Ichikō student Fujimura Misao, gave a great shock to the Japanese intellectual world around Meiji 36 (1903).<21> As it is a rare document, the full text, along with commentary by his uncle, Dr. Naka (那珂博士), a professor at the Higher Normal School, and the famous critic of the time, Kuroiwa Ruikō (黑岩淚香), are included here in the original text. (Fujimura Misao committed suicide by throwing himself into Kegon Falls (華嚴瀑布) on May 21, Meiji 36.)

Bungei Shunjūsha and Korean Writers
According to word from a certain informed source, the Bungei Shunjūsha (文藝春秋社) group in Tokyo is reportedly planning to select, <22> translate, and publish works by prominent writers, including central figures from the Korean poetry scene and the Korean fiction scene. However, when this will be realized and whose works will be published are said to be top secrets (秘中의 秘).

Population Concentration in Cities
As various industries related to military demand inflation (軍需인푸레슌) have had a considerable impact on Korea as well, the population in each city is rapidly increasing. There is a strong trend of farmers leaving their villages due to floods and other reasons, gathering in cities, and becoming industrial proletarians, causing the population of each city to swell significantly. According to a recent survey:
Kyŏngsŏng: 400,000, Pusan: 150,000, P'yŏngyang: 140,000, Taegu: 100,000, Inch'ŏn: 70,000, Sinŭiju: 50,000, Hamhŭng: 40,000, Wŏnsan: 40,000, Chŏnju: 38,000, Chinnamp'o: 38,000, Mokp'o: 34,000, Kwangju: 33,000, Masan: 27,000, Kunsan: 26,000, Haeju: 23,000.

Korean Youth as a Lighthouse Keeper
The job of a lighthouse keeper sounds quite romantic, an occupation where the wings of imagination of young, sensitive men and women fly. However, according to a recent survey, there is one Korean person working as a lighthouse keeper: a Mr. Han Sŭng-ch'ŏl at the Chukbyeon Port Lighthouse (竹邊港 燈臺).

Financial World, Mining World Insider News

Hwashin Hires Twenty
Hwashin Department Store (和信百貨店) decided to hire about 20 new employees from among the students graduating from various professional schools this coming March, and even held the entrance examination in early December. These 20 people are to be hired with 5 people each from the following four schools:
Posung College (普成專門學校)
Yonhi College (延禧專門學校)
Higher Commercial School (高等商業學校)
Law College (法學專門學校)
The procedure was first to gather 10 recommended students from each school principal, totaling 40, then give them an entrance examination again, and hire those who passed. The standard starting salary is said to be around 45 won for professional school graduates and around 60 won for university graduates.

The Great Mine Owner with 72 Mines
It is a frequently reported fact that the mining fever among Koreans grows stronger day by day. Now, according to inquiries, looking at who among the several hundred mine owners possesses the most mining claims, it is Mr. U (禹氏), who formerly ran a cloth business at the An'guk-tong intersection in Seoul, who holds the most. The total number of applied-for and permitted claims reaches 72.<23> Assuming application and registration fees cost 300 won per claim, that expense alone would amount to over 20,000 won.

Mr. Pang Ŭi-sŏk's Kyŏngsŏng Taxi
Mr. Pang Ŭi-sŏk, a prominent businessman from Pukch'ŏng in Hamgyŏng Province, has been managing the Hamhŭng Taxi Co., Ltd. (咸興택시주식회사) for some time. Recently, intending to start a large-scale taxi business in Seoul, he organized the Kyŏngsŏng Taxi Company (京城택시회사) and was constructing a two-story Western-style office building in Hasegawa-chō (長谷長川町) in the city. It was completed not long ago and has begun operations. With 50-60 taxis, it is said to have the momentum to seize dominance in the Seoul taxi industry. Mr. *Pyŏng* (*秉*) has reportedly been appointed as the manager. (*Illegible character in name*)

Cheonil Pharmacy Building Costs 30,000 Won
On the side street leading from Chongno 4-chōme towards Kogane-chō (黃金町) intersection, a little further in from the Suyŏngsa (秀英舍) printing house managed by Mr. Han Tong-su, a large, three-story brick building has been newly constructed. This is the famous Cheonil Pharmacy (天一藥房). Completed with an investment of 30,000 won in construction costs, it is observed that, taking the opportunity of this new building's completion, the pharmaceutical world seems poised for renewed activity, as if a fierce battle for supremacy might break out again.

A Proposal for a General Meeting
To Mr. Song Chin-u, president of the Tonga Ilbo; Mr. Pang Ŭng-mo, president of the Chosŏn Ilbo; and Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng, president of the Chungang Ilbo: Regarding the future affairs of our nation, we propose the following matters at the dawn of this New Year. We know that most of the individuals representing our organization and intellect are leading figures connected to the main and branch offices of newspaper companies. We request that you convene a joint meeting of reporters from the three companies this spring, gathering not only from the 13 provinces within Korea but also the bureau chiefs from Manchuria (滿州), China, Tokyo, etc., hundreds of individuals in one hall. Furthermore, we ask you to invite members of the Bar Association and leaders from Cheondogyo (天道敎), Christianity (基督敎), Buddhism (佛敎), and other various organizations to join this gathering, to discuss the urgent and important issues facing our nation, such as:
1.  Consultation on various measures concerning the large contingent of over one million emigrants moving to Manchuria.
2.  Fundamental establishment of relief measures for the flood disasters in the three southern provinces (三南), the Osaka flood disaster, and other poverty relief.
3.  Establishment of urgent countermeasures to achieve more active and rapid results in the literacy movement.
4.  Discussion of opening a people's library and other various issues piling up domestically and internationally.
To break through the stagnant, windless atmosphere of this land since the dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) and the cessation of Samch'ong (三總) and Kŭnu (槿友), we earnestly desire to hold such a gathering, permissible by law, that can synthesize the opinions of the largest possible number of people.
Great trends sometimes arise on their own, but historical examples often show they depend greatly on the efforts of those with purpose. For several years, we have been dispersed, wandering in fragmentation, having lost the focal point for our energy, like scattered grains of sand, and lost the unification of thought. Fortunately, purposeful scholars and commoners (布衣寒士) still fill the mountains and rivers of the peninsula. How could gathering their eyes, ears, and mouths together about once a year be considered meaningless? We earnestly request this of the three gentlemen who represent the vast majority of public opinion.<25>

**Summary**

This article from the January 1935 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine presents a series of short news items and commentaries covering diverse aspects of Korean society under Japanese rule. Topics include the membership and activities of the Sijunghoe political group, the growth and financial strength of the Presbyterian Church, historical notes on the Kim Okkyun incident and Fujimura Misao's suicide note, cultural news about translations (Sun Yat-sen, Korean authors), intelligence on Soviet military buildup, business updates (newspaper profits, Hwashin hiring, new taxi company, pharmacy construction, mining boom), demographic shifts towards cities, and a list of high-ranking Korean officials in the colonial administration. The article concludes with a strong proposal urging the heads of the three major Korean newspapers (Chosŏn, Tonga, Chungang Ilbo) to convene a large-scale meeting of journalists and leaders from various sectors to address pressing national issues like emigration to Manchuria, disaster relief, literacy, and the general fragmentation of national purpose since the dissolution of organizations like the Sin'ganhoe.

For historians, this piece offers valuable snapshots of colonial Korean society in the mid-1930s. It reveals the continued activities of Korean organizations (religious, nascent political like Sijunghoe, cultural like Hŭngsadan), economic developments driven partly by Japanese industrial policy ("military demand inflation"), the scale of Korean participation within the colonial bureaucracy, and anxieties about national direction and unity expressed through the final proposal. The demographic data on urban growth and the specific details on businesses and individuals provide concrete socio-economic context. The final proposal is particularly interesting, reflecting a desire among intellectuals or activists to find new avenues for coordinated national effort within the constraints of colonial rule, leveraging the existing network of newspaper personnel after the forced dissolution of broader nationalist organizations.

全體會議를 爲한 一提議
東亞日報 사장 宋鎭禹, 朝鮮日報 사장 方應謨, 中央日報 사장 呂運亨 3氏에게 우리민족의 將來之事에 대하야 吾人은 新春劈頭에 左記 事項을 제의하나이다. 즉 우리의 조직과 두뇌를 대표하는 人士 중 대부분이 신문사 本支社에 관련 가지고 잇는 首惱者 諸氏 인줄 아오니 請컨대 今春에 3社연합 신문기자 대회를 소집하시어 13道海內는 물론, 滿州, 中國, 東京 등 支局長 數百氏를 一堂에 회합케 하고 가지고 여기에 辯護士協會 諸氏와 天道敎, 基督敎, 佛敎 등 기타 각 단체의 수뇌자까지 합석을 청하야 우리민족의 時急且 중대한 안건인
1. 滿州에 이주하여 가는 백여 만 大部隊 이주민에 대한 諸方策 협의.
2. 三南 水災, 大阪 水災, 기타 빈민구제책의 근본적 수립.
3. 文盲打開運動의 一層 활발, 且 速度적 효과를 得키 위한 緊切한 대책 수립.
4. 民立도서관 개설, 기타 국내 국외에 山積한 諸問題의 토의를 위하기를 청하나이다. 新幹會 해소, 三總, 槿友의 閉絶 이래, 침체된 無風帶적 이 땅 공기를 타개하여 노키 위하야 吾人은 適法에 의한, 가장 다수 인민의 의사를 종합할 수 잇는 이러한 집회를 切切히 가지고 십허 하나이다.
무릇 大勢란 저절로 오는 法도 잇스나, 史上의 例를 보면 흔히 뜻잇는 이의 노력에 만히 달녓더이다. 數年來로 우리는 분산되엇고, 支離滅裂에서 헤매어 마치 모새알가치 에넬기의 集中處를 일엇고, 思想의 歸一을 일엇슴니다. 다행히 뜻잇는 布衣寒士, 아직 半島山河에 충만함애 年一度程度로 그 눈과 그 귀와 그 입을 한데 모아봄이 엇지 뜻 없는 일이라 하오리까. 여론을 가장 대부분 대표하고 안즈신 3先生에게 이를 切切 勸請하나이다.＜25＞
