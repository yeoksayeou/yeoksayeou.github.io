발행일	1935년 01월 01일
기사제목	등대 (Lighthouse)
필자	岸曙
기사형태	시 (Poem)

등대
						岸曙

등대는 반듯 반듯
등대는 감웃 감웃
왼밤을 혼자새는 외로운 등대
心思를 둘 곳 업서 헤매는 등대.

등대는 반듯 반듯
등대는 감웃 감웃
누구를 못내 반겨 네가 웃느냐.
누구가 못내 그려 네가 우느냐.

등대는 반듯 반듯
등대는 감웃 감웃
밝으면 외론 맘에 빗이 들다도
어두면 위론 맘이 더욱 설거니.＜14＞

Lighthouse
						An Sŏ

The lighthouse flashes bright, flashes bright
The lighthouse flickers dim, flickers dim
Lonely lighthouse, staying up alone all night (왼밤)
Lighthouse wandering, with no place to rest its thoughts (心思).

The lighthouse flashes bright, flashes bright
The lighthouse flickers dim, flickers dim
Whom do you welcome so joyfully that you laugh?
Whom do you long for so deeply that you cry?

The lighthouse flashes bright, flashes bright
The lighthouse flickers dim, flickers dim
Even if light enters the lonely heart when it brightens,
When it darkens, the lonely heart grows even more troubled. <14>

**Summary**

This short poem by An Sŏ personifies a lighthouse, depicting it as a lonely figure enduring the night. It experiences fluctuating emotions, flashing brightly and dimming low, metaphorically laughing while welcoming someone and crying while longing for someone. The poem contrasts the slight comfort light might bring with the deepening sorrow or unease that comes with darkness. For historians, the poem's themes of loneliness, endurance through darkness, and ambiguous waiting ('welcoming'/'longing') could reflect the atmosphere of the colonial period in Korea—a sense of isolation and perhaps suppressed anticipation or sorrow under Japanese rule. The personified lighthouse could symbolize an entity (individual or collective) steadfastly enduring hardship.
