In Front of the Hall
						P'albong

Here and there, large holes show in the earth-fallen Walls (壁),
And the pillars, seemingly rotten and pitch-black, lean to one side,
Above them,
Weeds on the roof covered with old tiles are even more Pitiful (恨心),
Oh, the shape of this Desolate (荒凉) Hall (會館), I see it before my eyes again today.

Oppa! More than ten years ago, seeming like yesterday yet far passed—
My beloved younger sister Hyoju—you tapped my shoulder, saying
—Look, haven't the foundation stones been laid, the pillars erected, the roof completed?
This Hall (會館), a Crystalization (結晶) of hot blood, our red hearts!
Will they ever leave here, the young men and women of this land, the Masters (主人) of this house?
This Hall (會館) grows with them beneath the blue sky—you said.

Countless oppas of girls like me, brave men like you, oppa,
Gathered here day and night, losing track of time, exchanging words, those grand thoughts—
Like fire, each fiery phrase seemed to stir the Hearts (心臟) of the crowd,
Parting the Darkness (暗黑), unfolding a bright, clear world—

One by one, two by two, like you, oppa, the brave men left this place,
The years changed, Time (歲月) flowed by... The Gate (門) shut firmly,
The magnificent voices can no longer be heard in this large Room (房),
The dark faces and glossy hair, chests puffed out, ears inclined, have erased their shadows.

Now, battered by the pouring rain,
Standing alone as if Excluded (除外當한) from the coming-and-going street, having lost its master,
The empty house, the Hall (會館) of old, leaks from the roof, forming a Waterfall (瀑布),
And on the floorboards, muddy water flows windingly like a River (江),<174>
Ah, oppa, who left on a road from which one never returns!
Your sister Hyoju wiped her tears with her fist.

But oppa! Hyoju, your dear sister, who has grown for ten years since this house was built,
I am indeed the young woman of this land succeeding you, oppa, your Successor (繼承者) carrying the embers!
Do not Worry (念慮)! I intend to repair this house anew, use it worthily, and make it shine,
I intend to engrave these ten years of blood, ten years of tears, within this heart.

Ah, oppa gone Forever (永遠)! Please listen, within this land,
The places where brave men like you gathered and worked, where loud voices shouted, where lamps burned bright,
—The Youth Halls (靑年會館) that were built evenly throughout the South and North (南北)—
Now, as if fallen into the same Fate (運命), they crumble away like this, but
Do not Worry (念慮)! Your sister, I, am strong, and young blood like mine has been newly ignited,
Oh, why wouldn't the crumbling Youth Halls (靑年會館) be built anew!
(1934. 8. 28)<175>

**Summary**

This poem by P'albong (Kim Ki-jin) uses the image of a dilapidated community hall (會舘), likely a former youth center (青年會館), to lament the decline of nationalist fervor and communal spirit among Korean youth under Japanese colonial rule. The speaker, Hyoju, contrasts the hall's vibrant past, filled with her older brother ("oppa") and other passionate young men, with its current state of decay and abandonment. Despite the sorrow for the lost generation and suppressed activities, the poem concludes with Hyoju's declaration of resilience. As a successor, she vows to rebuild the hall and carry forward the spirit, suggesting hope for a future revival led by the new generation, even amidst widespread disillusionment symbolized by similar crumbling halls across the country. For historians, the poem offers a poignant snapshot of the atmosphere in the mid-1930s: the memory of earlier, more active nationalist youth movements (likely suppressed by the colonial government), the resulting sense of loss and decay, and the nascent determination of a younger generation to persist despite the oppressive circumstances. The specific mention of Youth Halls (靑年會館) points to the importance and subsequent decline of these community spaces as hubs for social and potentially political mobilization.

會舘 압혜서
						팔봉
흙 떠러진 壁 군대군대 커드란 구녕이 보이고,
썩온 듯이 새카만 기둥들은 한편으로 기우렷는데,
그 우에
낡은 개와 덮인 집웅에 雜草 더욱 恨心한,
오오, 荒凉한 이 會館 모양, 오늘도 내 눈앞에 보입니다.

옵바! 어제런 듯 멀리 지나간 十餘年 전일-
당신의 사랑하는 동생 효주-저의, 억개를 치시며
-보아라, 주추가 놓이고, 기둥이 서고, 집웅이 다 되지 아니하엿늬.
이 會館, 뜨거운, 피의 結晶, 우리의 붉은 마음!
한時인들 여기서 떠나랴, 이 땅의 젊은 男女, 이 집의 主人들,
이 會館도 그들과 함께 푸른 하늘 아래 커나간다-하시엇지오.

옵바와 같이 씩씩한 사내들, 나와 같은 젊은애들의 數많은 옵바,
낮에나 밤에나 이 곧에 뫃여서 해 가는 줄 모르고 주고 받든 말, 그 장한 생각-,
불가치 타는 듯 한 마듸 한 마듸 뭇사람의 心臟을 살구드니,
暗黑을 헤치고 화-ㄴ하게 밝은 세상 펄치어 보이드니

하나식 둘식, 옵바와 한가지로, 씩씩한 사내들 여기서 떠난 後,
해가 박귀고 歲月이 흘러 갓서요... 門은 굳게 다치고,
雄장한 목소래 이 큰 房에서 다시는 더 드를 수 없으며
가슴을 벌리고 귀를 기우리는 검은 얼굴과 윤택한 머리 그림자를 지웟습니다.

지금, 함박으로 퍼붓는 비를 마즈면서,
오고가는 거리에서 除外當한 듯 호을로 서서, 主人을 잃은
텅 뷔인 집, 녯날의 會館은, 집웅이 새여서 瀑布를 이루고,
마루짱 우에는 江인 듯이 흙탕물 구비구비 흘러요,＜174＞
아아, 한번 가고는 다시 못 오는 길로 떠나신 옵바!
당신의 동생 효주는 주먹으로 눈물을 씻엇습니다

그러나 옵바! 이 집을 지은 後 十年을 자라난 효주, 당신의 귀여운 동생,
저야말로, 옵바의 뒤를 닛는 이 땅의 젊은 女子, 불씨를 품은 당신의 繼承者!
念慮마셔요! 이 집을 다시 뜻어곤치고, 값잇게 쓰며, 빛나게 할 터이니,
피묻은 十年, 눈물의 十年을 이 가슴 가운대 색이려 합니다.

아아, 永遠히 간 옵바! 드러주셔요, 이 땅 안의
당신과 같은 씩씩한 사내들 뫃여서 일하든 곧, 큰 소래 질르고, 燈불이 밝든 곧,
-南北에 골고루 이루어지엇든 靑年會館들-
지금, 꼭같은 運命에 빠진 거같이 이 모양으로 허러저 갑니다, 그러나
念慮마셔요! 당신의 동생 제가 튼튼하고, 또 저 같은 젊은 피 새로히 불댕겻스니,
오오, 허러저 가는 靑年會館쯤 다시 이루어지지 아니하리까!
(1934. 8. 28)＜175＞
