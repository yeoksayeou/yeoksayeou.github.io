Sigh, Peninsula New Literary Scene Twenty Years' Masterpiece Collection
						Kim Myŏngsun

Burying my face in a round lotus leaf,
The night deepens with unfulfilled dreams.
A sigh, standing alone in the empty courtyard,
Trembles like moonlight on the lotus leaf,
Was it the passing wind? Let out a sigh.

Lonely maiden, lonely maiden, turned pale,
Burying her face in the lotus leaf, the lotus leaf.

Before the mirror, night after night,
With candles lit on left and right,
Wanting to forget endless boredom,
Applying pale powder like moonlight,
She dreams of her mother's precious embrace.

Precious maiden, precious maiden, become a sorrowful fate,
Night after night, before the mirror.

Small shadows, black and red,
Lightning struck, snow came to my heart that drove flocks of sheep,
Like fragments of torn red flower petals,
Rising and falling in a whirlwind,
Sighing upon the abandoned stage.

I ask the countless dark red children,
Oh, sorrows that tried to grasp the void (虛空),
Fragments of a mirror smashed by the whip of fury (憤怒),
Children soaked even in blood,
Are you still seeking warm blood?

Ah, you painful children in my heart,
Just so, my heart is broken, even its blood.
My children, you bodies that must live in ice,
Do not melt uselessly when snow falls,
Go North, head North, like the blue sky, like crystal,
Cling somewhere, condense, and condense again! <180>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Myŏngsun, titled "Sigh," expresses deep sorrow, loneliness, and a sense of fragmentation. The speaker laments unfulfilled dreams and endless boredom, using imagery of a pale maiden, a mirror, moonlight, and scattered petals. The latter part addresses metaphorical "children" born of sorrow and anger, urging them to endure hardship ("live in ice") and seek resilience and clarity by heading "North," symbolized as a place of purity like the sky or crystal. For historians, the poem offers insight into the emotional landscape of colonial Korea, reflecting feelings of despair, alienation, and perhaps the personal struggles of a female writer in that era. The call to head "North" could be interpreted as a metaphor for seeking strength, endurance, or perhaps even a form of internal or external exile away from the current suffering, reflecting a complex response to the pressures of the time.

탄식, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						金明淳
둥그런 련입헤 얼골을 뭇고
꿈 이루지 못하는 밤은 깁허서
뷔인 뜰에 혼자서 서른 탄식은
련입헤 달빗 가치 허득여드러
지나가든 바람인가 한숨지어라.

외로운 처녀 외로운 처녀 파랏케 되여
련닙헤 련닙에 얼골을 뭇어.

거울 압헤 밤마다 밤마다
좌우 편에 촉불 밝혀서
한업는 무료를 닛고지고
달빗 가치 파란 분 발느고서는
어머니의 귀한 품을 꿈꾸려.

귀한 처녀 귀한 처녀 서른 신세되여
밤마다 밤마다 거울의 압헤.

검고 붉은 적은 그림자들
번개치고 羊떼 몰든 내마음에 눈와서
조각조각 찌여진 붉은 꼿닙들 가치도
회오리 바람에 올낫다 떠러지듯
내어둔 舞臺 우에 한숨 짓다.

나는 무수한 검붉은 아해들에게 뭇노라
오오, 虛空을 잡으려든 서름들아
憤怒에 매마저 부서진 거울조각들아
피마저 피에 저즌 아해들아
너희들은 아직 땃듯한 피를 구하는가.

아아, 너이들은 내맘에 압흔 아해들이
그럿틋이 내마음은 피마저 깨엇노라
내 아해들아 너히는 어름에서 살몸
부질업시 눈내려 녹지말고
北으로 北行하야 파란하늘가치 수정가치
어데서 붓허서 맷치고 또 맷치라!＜180＞
