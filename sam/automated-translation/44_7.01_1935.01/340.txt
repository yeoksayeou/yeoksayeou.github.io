Song of the Homeland, A Collection of Masterpieces from the Peninsula's New Literary Scene of the Last Twenty Years
						Kim Ansŏ

Drizzle
Drizzling, drizzling
in Hwangp'o,
the fine rain,
today too, for half a day,
has only made the grains of sand weep.

It doesn't come when I wait,
but on a sorrowful day, it drizzles, drizzles—
could it be the tears
of that person I parted from without meeting?

On a sorrowful day, it drizzles, drizzles;
the seagulls of Ŏyŏngdo too,
their wings heavy and wet,
fly in, flocking together.

A body drifting three hundred *ri* by sea route,
the boatman of Namp'o,
where might he be right now (只今),
his eyes growing heavy with tears in the drizzle?

Autumn
Above the port where the sea route is cut off,
only a lone seagull
sings a sorrowful song.

The chilly autumn wind
raises ripples on the water
and goes off somewhere.

"Ga-ga," crying its throat out,
when the seagull weeps,
even the clear sky becomes teary.

The autumn wind blows gently, gently;
everything is a dream within a dream—
will it all scatter just like this?<166>

**Summary**

This selection presents two poems by Kim Ansŏ, "Drizzle" and "Autumn," both evoking themes of sorrow, separation, and wandering through melancholic natural imagery. "Drizzle" connects the persistent rain with tears of parting and the loneliness of a drifting boatman. "Autumn" depicts a desolate port scene with a lone, crying seagull and fleeting winds, questioning the transient nature of existence. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in 1935 during the Japanese colonial period, might reflect the pervasive sense of loss, displacement, and quiet despair experienced by Koreans. The imagery of cut-off sea routes, weeping landscapes, and aimless wandering could subtly mirror the constraints and anxieties of life under colonial rule, making them poignant artifacts of the era's literary sentiment.

故鄕의 노래, 半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						金岸曙
보실비
黃浦에 보슬보슬
내리는 실비는
오늘도 한나절을
모래알만 울려 노핫소.

기다릴 때엔 안 오다가도
설은 날에는 보슬보슬
만나지도 못하고 갈라진
그 사람의 눈물이든가.

설은 날에는 보슬보슬
魚泳島 갈매기도
나래가 축진해서
떼를 지어 날아드오.

水路 三百里를
배ㅅ길로 떠도는 몸이라
南浦의 사공님
只今쯤은 어느 곳에서
보슬비에 눈물 겨울고.

가을
배ㅅ길 끈허진 浦口 우에
갈매기만 혼자서
설은 노래 부름니다.

싸늘한 갈ㅅ바람은
물 우에 잔물쌀을 돗치고
어대론지 가고 맙니다.

「가가」 목을 노하서
갈매기가 울으면
맑은 하늘도 눈물집니다.

가을 바람은 산들산들
모든 것은 꿈속에 꿈이라
이대로 흐터지고 말릿가.＜166＞
