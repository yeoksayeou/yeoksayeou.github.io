半島 新文壇 二十年來 名作選集
						韓龍雲

Your Letter

Because they said your letter came,
I put down the hoe I was weeding the flower bed with and opened it.

In that letter, the writing is fine and the lines are many,
but the message is simple.

If it were a letter written by the Beloved,
though the words might be few, the message would be long.

Beloved

In the windless air,
making vertical ripples, quietly falling, the paulownia leaf,
whose footprint is it?

At the end of the tiresome long rain,
through a torn gap in the fearsome dark clouds driven by the west wind,
the blue sky, glimpsed now and then,
whose face is it?

On the deep tree with no flowers,
passing through the green moss, brushing against the quiet sky above the old pagoda, the unknowable fragrance (香氣),
whose breath is it?<181>

**Summary**

This article presents two poems by Han Yongun (韓龍雲), titled "Your Letter" and "Beloved," selected as part of a collection of masterpieces from Korea's recent literary scene. The poems express a deep sense of longing and searching for an absent "Beloved" (님, Nim). Natural imagery—a falling paulownia leaf, a glimpse of blue sky through clouds, an unseen fragrance near an old pagoda—is used to evoke the presence or trace of this Beloved. For historians, these poems are significant as Han Yongun was a prominent Buddhist monk, poet, and independence activist. His "Nim" is often interpreted allegorically, representing not just a personal lover or Buddha, but also the lost nation of Korea under Japanese colonial rule. The selection of his work in a "masterpiece" collection during the colonial period highlights his enduring literary and cultural importance despite his resistance activities.
