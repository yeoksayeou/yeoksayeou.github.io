Publications: Russia is First

While it is said that the volume of publications is directly proportional to the civilization of that country, looking at the number of publications from various countries around the world for the year 1930:

First Place: Russia (露西亞) - 34,000 types
Second Place: Germany (獨逸) - 26,000 types
Third Place: Japan (日本) - 22,000 types
England (英吉利) - 15,000 types
France (佛蘭西) - 14,000 types
Poland (波蘭) - 12,000 types
Italy (伊太利) - 11,000 types
United States (米國) - 10,000 types <85>

**Summary**

This short news item from 1935 lists the number of publications released by various countries in 1930, ranking them accordingly. Russia leads significantly, followed by Germany and Japan. The article prefaces the list by stating that the volume of publications reflects a country's level of civilization. For historians, the high ranking of Japan (third place) is notable, potentially used implicitly to bolster Japan's image as a 'civilized' nation comparable to Western powers during the colonial period. The relatively low number listed for the United States (potentially due to a typo) compared to European nations and Japan is also interesting.
