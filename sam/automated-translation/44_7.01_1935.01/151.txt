Publisher's Notice

Regarding a certain issue (某問題), the forty-seven pages of manuscripts by the twenty-four individuals including Messrs. Sin Hŭng'u, Song Chin'u, Yi Kwangsu, Pang Ŭngmo, Yŏ Unhyŏng, Kim Ch'angje, Sŏl T'aehŭi, Chŏng In'gwa, etc., will not be published.<92>

**Summary**

This brief notice from the publisher of Samch'ŏlli magazine announces the non-publication of 47 pages of manuscripts submitted by 24 prominent individuals (including Sin Hŭng'u, Song Chin'u, Yi Kwangsu, Yŏ Unhyŏng, etc.) concerning an unspecified "certain issue." The article is significant for colonial Korean history because it points towards potential censorship or sensitive editorial decisions made under Japanese rule. The fact that contributions from such well-known figures, representing diverse political and intellectual backgrounds, were withheld suggests the controversial nature of the undisclosed topic, offering historians a glimpse into the constraints on public discourse during that era.
