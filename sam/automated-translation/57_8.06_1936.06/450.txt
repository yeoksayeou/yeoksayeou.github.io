Magazine Title: 삼천리 제8권 제6호
Publication Date: 1936년 06월 01일
Article Title: 初夏의 文藝 (Literature of Early Summer)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Cloud

Ch'ŏllyŏng (鐵嶺) high pass
Reaching up
Passing cloud
I gaze upon it

Though my words were right
They do not stand
This rotting heart
I wish to set afloat on the clouds
-P'ain- <225>

**Summary**

This short poem, titled "Cloud," by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan), expresses a sense of weary resignation and a desire for release. The speaker observes clouds passing over the high Ch'ŏllyŏng Pass and reflects on how their own righteous words or beliefs have failed to take root or prevail ("서지 안으니"). Feeling disheartened ("썩는 이 가슴" - this rotting heart), the speaker wishes to cast away their sorrow, letting it drift away like the clouds. For historians, the poem reflects a common sentiment of frustration and helplessness among Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, where asserting truth or justice felt futile against the prevailing political reality. The mention of Ch'ŏllyŏng Pass, a significant historical and geographical landmark, grounds the feeling of despair within a Korean context.
