査問會, 安基永·金顯順 戀愛事件
						一記者
(Inquiry Meeting, The An Kiyŏng·Kim Hyŏnsun Love Affair Incident
						One Reporter)

Casting off honor and position, kicking aside all the world's criticism and ridicule, two young people who set off on a distant path of wandering, carrying a passionate romance forged through "song," have now, after four years have passed, returned to the embrace of this land following the spring news just a few days ago.

These two, a man and woman who sang a "love's triumphal song" avoiding the world's eyes on a spring day four years ago, have now stepped onto their native soil again secretly after four years. During that time, they must have indeed walked many paths of hardship, and also must have unfolded a haven of love burning with pure affection. Also, at times, the strange feelings of nostalgia must have surfaced, and they must have missed the friends with whom they held hands under their hometown sky.

Four years of wandering! How many times did they shed tears? How many times did they wash away melancholic feelings with song?

Is that all! How much learning must there have been from all the vicissitudes of society, and how much realization must there have been? In a word, what did these two young people gain from the four years of wandering, and furthermore, what is the path they intend to walk in the future?

I intend to seek out these "wandering singers," who once caused such a stir in this society, trace the uneven path they have walked in the past, and inform the young people of this land who are passing their days amidst excitement and impatience.

On a certain day in early April, as a gentle spring rain silently fell, hastening the flower news in Hanyang Fortress (Seoul), I unhesitatingly visited the one-room thatched hut where Mr. An Kiyŏng and Ms. Kim Hyŏnsun had set up their haven of love (愛의 巢).

Mr. An Kiyŏng, who had studied music in America, returned in glory, and as a professor in the music department of Ewha Women's College, gathered the admiration of the peninsula's youth<168> all upon himself. Ms. Kim Hyŏnsun, born the cherished daughter of a millionaire, grew up beautifully unaware of the ways of the world, making the hearts of countless young men pound! If these words were applied to them four years ago, isn't their life today too poor and desolate? As I knocked on the door of the single-room hut, the "nest of love" where the An-Kim couple stayed at 31 Pak Kwandong, Sŏdaemun, I realized this thought suddenly passed through my mind.

I obtained the opportunity to sit face-to-face with Mr. An Kiyŏng, whose face looked pale and gaunt, seemingly much worn out from the long wandering life, yet affably plump-cheeked, and Ms. Kim Hyŏnsun, smiling brightly and dressed plainly like a housewife. I decided to hear the story of their memories from the four years of wandering, the path walked last month.

Me - Both of you must have suffered quite a lot during the four years of wandering, right? Of course, there must have been happy times more than once or twice...

Kim - (Smiling slightly, her eyes open wide)

An - Of course, we suffered a lot. However, we both gained much and learned much from the long wandering life. When I went to America to study music, I always worked my way through school, so I had tasted the hardships of the world early on, but how would this Hyŏnsun ever have known hardship? Seeing such a person endure hardship, sometimes living without a single penny in hand, I realized a woman's heart is not weak... But what's the use of bringing up these useless past stories now, only to incur antipathy from people<169> and cause problems! It seems society is always watching my every move and every word now, so it's not easy to bring up these stories again. However, the path we walked was not just a union born of "love," but a union arising from the pure desire to dedicate our two bodies to art (music), so I want to say that we never forgot our passion for "art" even for a moment!

Me - On that spring day exactly four years ago, when you both, one after the other, firmly made promises and sounded the first signal of wandering, where did you intend to go?

Kim - Well... (Looking intently at Mr. An) Please speak. It's the same whether I say it or he says it... Hoho.

An - Hahaha... Our path was one of wandering. It was a path of escape (逃避) seeking love and art, so how could we have had much money? Therefore, with the thought that we two would just study art, we intended to definitely go to Milan, Italy, at least once... Truly, our hopes back then were huge! We would cross over to Italy, the land of art, Milan, the city of music, study music diligently, and make the existence of Koreans shine in the European music scene. After that, we would surely return to this land where I was born, Chosŏn, and dedicate our two bodies for the sake of Chosŏn's music scene! With such passionate, strong, firm promises and shining hopes filling our hearts, we set off on the path of wandering, pretending not to know the world's tumultuous discussions! But do all things in the world ever go as planned?

Me - What was the course you traveled during the four years of wandering? Where did you go?

An - After leaving Kyŏngsŏng, I briefly stopped in Harbin. At that time, since I had left without money, I thought that if I just went to Russia, I could study music even without money, so for a while in Harbin, I tried hard to go to Russia. However, the political situation at that time was also complicated, and there was absolutely no possibility, so I went straight to Shinkyŏng (Hsinking/Changchun), met Hyŏnsun, and we went to Peking together. We stopped in Peking because Hyŏnsun's older brother ran a hospital there, intending to get some money from him. However, he treated us very coldly, so we just stayed at an inn (려과)<170> for several days, and then I left alone for Shanghai first. Hyŏnsun also came down soon after. While in Shanghai, we made various efforts to go to Milan, Italy. The reason we stayed in Shanghai was also because of the thought of going to Italy. The reason we endured all sorts of hardships there for a year and a half was because of this... Indeed, among the four years of wandering, the time we suffered the most was this Shanghai period. Sometimes we lived on just a few pennies a day, so how must it have been!

Me - While suffering so much, you must have shed tears many times?

Kim - Yes- (Bows her head deeply)

An - Women cry easily! There were more than one or two times she shed tears.

Me - At times like that, did you ever regret, thinking, "The path we are walking now was wrong! We shouldn't have chosen this path!"?

An - Although we had many material and spiritual difficulties, we never once felt "depressed" or regretted it.

Me - When was the time you suffered the most?

Kim - We suffered the most on the ship coming from Tokyo to Chosŏn this time... Hoho.

Me - No. That kind of hardship is hardship too, but wandering from place to place in foreign lands, with not a penny in hand and no one to look after you, how difficult and lonely must that be?

An - When we first left, we had a little money, but after coming from Peking to Shanghai, the money ran out. So, the year and a half in Shanghai was truly a time of great hardship. The Shanghai period was the most difficult. During that time of hardship, at a certain dance hall, the black singer had just moved to another place offering a higher salary, leaving a vacancy. Someone introduced me, offering a monthly salary of 450 won to come, but I flatly refused. The people there thought I was a fool. However, my ideal at that time was solely the desire to go to Italy and study art. How could I sell my artistic conscience so easily (쉽사리), no matter how dire my living situation was? That was my thinking. But if it were now, I might give it some thought, but back then, I didn't even consider it.

Me - What was the most pleasant time you spent while in Shanghai?<171> What kind of life did you generally lead?

An - During the Shanghai period, whenever dusk approached, we always took walks together in Jessfield Park (쩨스필 公園). Under the shade of the overgrown trees, we yearned for our hometown sky, and we always sang our favorite songs softly. Walking together on the dark park path, we always sang Tchaikovsky's "Troika" (Russian Sleigh) - also known as "November" (November in the North Country). Also, in early spring, on the wide fields and green lawns of Siccawei (서가회) (this is a suburb of Shanghai), we would roll around together, imagining the shade under the overgrown palm trees of the South Seas, picturing the vast desert plains of Arabia, and sometimes picturing Siberia in the snowy north, endlessly galloping through the land of boundless imagination. Then, on the lawn, we would compose music and sing our favorite songs, gazing towards the distant sky, picturing our hometown's mountains and rivers. Also, sometimes I would compose, and Hyŏnsun would gather things like shepherd's purse, spinach (시굼치), and wild chives (달내갓흔) from the fields, and that evening we would return, season (뭇치고) and pan-fry (지저서) them to eat.

Kim - Truly, our life had no "depression," no "complaints," and no "worries." If there was ever a lonely time, it was when we missed seeing the faces of our parents back home and the friends we were close to, and longed for the beautiful mountains and rivers of Chosŏn, where flowers bloom, birds chirp, and the sound of water is clear. But autumn in Shanghai was really nice. In the evening, when the bell sound from the Catholic church in Siccawei (서가회) faintly drifted in, gazing blankly at the red glow of dusk, spending the whole day on the yellowing lawn – I still remember it clearly.

Me - So, what happened with the plan to go to Italy?

An - The Koreans in Shanghai were too insignificant (미미해서), so we negotiated with Westerners and Chinese people many times, but everything was because of money, so what could we do! At this time, we finally realized how great the power of gold is in the world today. However, having no other choice, the place we turned our steps to again was Tokyo. But when we actually arrived in Tokyo, our life was still difficult (고달프지요). Since I was affiliated with the American system (미국게통), I didn't know anyone in the Tokyo music scene, and the vocal scene (male) here seemed rather inferior to us, so I couldn't find my hopes here. Then, some friends advised that I should go to Chosŏn now and repay the path walked in the past through music again,<172> so we came to set foot (듸려 노케) on our homeland soil once more.

Me - What were your feelings when leaving Kyŏngsŏng Station for the first time four years ago, compared to arriving back at Kyŏngsŏng Station after four years?

At this moment, the sound of a baby crying is heard from the next room. It was clearly the cry of the beloved daughter obtained during the four years of wandering. The baby's mother stands up and leaves.

An - Our actions back then were not based on ordinary thinking. The burning passion forged by "art" and the identical wandering nature (방랑성) of both of us made us do it!
Sitting here now, what use is there in regretting that the path I walked was wrong! However, I don't even think it was a wrong action. During that time, we didn't just whisper "love," we also studied music!

Me - What path do you intend to take in the future?

An - We have firmly vowed (맹세하엿서요) to dedicate our lives for "art" (music). This is the only single path for us to repay Chosŏn society. Therefore, fearing that due to the natural law of "maternal love," Hyŏnsun's passion might shift from music to the child, I will always support her, and we will take turns (번가러) looking after the child while forging our path forward. We just don't know how much Chosŏn society will treat us, or how they will judge (귀정지울지) our actions! Our path forward is only to devote our lives for the sake of Chosŏn's music scene. We have decided to hold a concert featuring the two of us this time as well. There are people criticizing this already, but this concert is not about raising our heads saying "I am great!", but merely to inform (알니우자는데) the public that "We have studied music this much during that time."

Me - And regarding the children from your previous wife, some are grown now and constantly look for (차즈며) their father and shed tears. What are your thoughts about those children?

An - I am not as heartless (몰인정한) a person as the world thinks. Of course, during the four years of wandering, where was there any leeway (여유)? However, just a short while ago, I saw my eldest daughter (맛딸) (who entered girls' high school - 녀자고보 - this year) and comforted (달내엿지요) her, saying, "Don't feel sad at all, just study hard! Would I forget<173> my duty as a father (애비)?" And these days, my older children often come over to our house to play.

The end of his words (말매듸는) is filled with benevolent paternal love (父性愛). However, when considering the issues of family, marital love, maternal love, law, and morality, one could clearly (역역이) see a wisp of melancholy cloud (구름짱) drifting across his face.

I intended to criticize (批判) the hero and heroine of this "love affair," but I couldn't bring myself to do it. How do you gentlemen (諸君) intend to inquire (査問)?<174>

**Summary**

This 1936 article interviews An Kiyŏng, a US-educated musician and former Ewha professor, and Kim Hyŏnsun, daughter of a wealthy family, four years after their scandalous elopement. They recount their difficult "wandering" years through Harbin, Peking, Shanghai, and Tokyo, driven by a desire to study music in Milan but hampered by lack of funds. Despite hardships, including poverty in Shanghai, they claim never to have regretted their decision, sustaining themselves with their love and dedication to art. Now back in Korea with a child born during their exile, they face societal judgment but vow to dedicate their lives to developing Korean music, starting with an upcoming concert. An also addresses his relationship with the children from his previous marriage. The article highlights the conflict between personal passion (love and art) and societal norms (family obligations, reputation) in colonial Korea. For historians, it offers insights into the lives of Western-educated elites, the challenges faced by artists, the social scandals of the era, the mobility (and precarity) possible within East Asia even under Japanese rule, and attitudes towards love, marriage, art, and responsibility during that period. The mention of specific locations like Jessfield Park and Siccawei in Shanghai provides texture to the experience of Koreans abroad.

査問會, 安基永·金顯順 戀愛事件
						一記者
명예와 지위를 버서버리고 모든 세상의 비난과 조소를 거더차면서 「노래」로 매저진 정렬의 「로맨스」를 실고 멀니멀니 방랑의 길을 떠낫든 두 젊은이가 4년이란 세월이 흘너간 오늘 바로 봄소식을 따러 다시금 이 땅의 품 속에 안겨 버린지도 멧츨이 지낫다.
4년 전에 어느 봄날 세상 사람들의 눈을 피하야 「사랑의 개선가」를 부르든 이 두 남녀가 새삼스럽게 4년이 지난 오늘 또한 아무도 몰내 고토를 밟게 되는 그 동안에는 실로 여러 가지 고난의 길도 걸어 보앗슬 것이며 또한 순정에 타오르는 사랑의 복음자리도 펴 보앗슬 것이다. 또한 어떤 때는 「노스탈치아」의 야릇한 회포도 떠올낫슬 것이며 고향의 하날 밑에서 가치 손목을 잡든 동무들도 그리웟섯슬 것이다.
유랑 4개년간! 이들은 멧 번이나 눈물을 흘녓든가? 멧 번이나 「멩랑코리」한 심회를 노래로 씻처버렷든가.
