Magazine Title: 삼천리 제8권 제6호
Publication Date: 1936년 06월 01일
Article Title: 봄 (Spring)
Author: 申不出
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

It's spring, they say it's spring, a spring only the young lady knows,
Oblivious to flowers, birds, and grass flutes,
With closed lips and sealed eyebrows, spring pools and flows.

Her attire loosened, her grooming grown lazy,
So unbearably pretty, she feigns an unwilling, annoyed air,
Shall I pretend to strike her with a peach blossom spray?

How hateful she is, hateful once more,
Not knowing what's in another's heart, smiling so thoughtlessly,
Oh, this wretchedly hateful person, how I, this man, like you!<288>

**Summary**

This poem by Sin Pulch'ul captures the feeling of spring as embodied by a young woman. The speaker observes her seemingly indifferent beauty and charm, her lazy grooming, and her feigned annoyance, which only increases his affection despite his playful protests of finding her "hateful." The poem expresses a tender, slightly frustrated admiration for the beloved. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into personal sentiment and lyrical expression during the colonial period, showcasing the use of older Korean orthography (e.g., '곻여', '푸러저서', '어엽버', '몹슬') and traditional poetic themes of spring and love, providing insight into cultural continuity and linguistic evolution amidst the political context.

申不出
봄이요 봄이라니 아가씨만 아는 봄이
꽃도 새도 풀피리도 아랑곳 아니하고
담은 입 감은 눈섭 봄이 곻여 흐르네

매무시 푸러저서 단장도 게을너라
어엽버 못견듸게 억지로 미운양을
복송아 꽃송이로다 짐즛 때려 볼거나

많이 미운지고 다시 한번 미운지고
남의 속 모르구서 철 없이 웃고 있는
이 몹슬 미운 사람아 내 사네가 좋쿠나<288>
