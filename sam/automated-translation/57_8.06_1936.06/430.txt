Sijo Lecture: Sijo Appreciation and Composition (Continued)
						Yi Pyŏnggi
Author Introduction

Mr. Yi is a scholar and writer born in Chŏlla North Province, currently 45 years old. He is presently a teacher at Hwimun Kyobo School (徽文敎普校), deeply researches Sijo and Hangeul, and uses the pen name "Karam" (가람), under which he has published many sijo works.
He is a member of the Korean Language Society (朝鮮語學會).

Three, Appreciation

The Big Dipper turns, the moon not yet set.
Rowed boat, how far have you come? Night is already deep.
On the wind, a few sounds of *ch'im* (砧) are heard; have we arrived, I wonder.

Date is unknown.
Author is Yi Yŏnjin (李延鎭).
*Puktusŏng* (北斗星) is the Big Dipper (Ursa Major). *Susŏngch'im* (數聲砧) is a few sounds, the sound of cloth fulling. *Mich'ŏ* (미처) means yet or still. *Ŏnma* (언마) means how much.
This depicts the scene of returning by boat at night. The Big Dipper has turned, and the moon hangs low in the western sky. Rowed boat, how far have you come? Look at that Big Dipper and the moon. Night has already deepened—<215> While saying such things to the boatman, the boat goes on and on, the splashing waves glitter at the bow, the wind blows gently, and a few faint sounds of cloth fulling are heard. Now we must have arrived.
Imagining this scene is delightful, but how much more so for someone who has battled wind and waves on that vast waterway, nearly died, and then, not even in daylight but in the dead of night, returns safely to hear those sounds of cloth fulling?

Seeing you, do I detest you? Not seeing you, do I forget you?
Either you never existed, or I never knew you.
Rather, I will die first, making you long for me.

Author is unknown.
*Sŭlmip* (슬밉) means dislike and hate. *Monjŏ* (몬저) means first. *Swiŏsŏ* (싀어서) means dying.
Seeing you once, seeing you twice, seeing you many times, do I grow tired and hateful of seeing you? Or, longing for you and then unable to see you, do I forget you? Or, if you had never existed in this world, or even if you existed, if I had never known you—would I then long for you like this, my insides burning? If I were to lose you, could I survive even for a moment? Rather, I will die before you, making you long for me.
Indeed, it depicts the chaotic emotion of burning with poignant love with just a few simple words, leaving nothing more to add. And yet, it is not monotonous but intricate and coherent.<216>
No matter how many times I read it, I do not grow tired of it.

Luxury is also false, and wealth and rank are but a dream.
On the hill of Pukmangsan (北邙山), when the sound of the *yoryŏng* (搖鈴) ceases,
No matter how much one regrets and grieves, there is no way to recover it.

Work by Kim Such'ang (金壽長), from the reign of King Sukchong of the Yi Dynasty.
*Hwahwa* (豪華) means living luxuriously. *Pugwi* (富貴) means being wealthy and holding high office. *Pukmangsan* (北邙山) is a desolate place with numerous mounds of tombs. *Yoryŏng* (搖鈴) is a handbell, or *solbat* (솔밭), used for military orders or warnings, but here it refers to the bell shaken by the lead pallbearer (*subŏn*, 首番) at the front of the funeral bier (*sang'yŏ*, 喪輿) during a funeral procession (*ch'ulsang*, 出喪), leading the chant of the pallbearers (*sangdugun*, 샹두군). *Nwiut* (뉘웃) means regret. *Aedalp* (애닯) means grieving pitifully (literally, intestines wearing out).
This speaks of the impermanence of human life.
Once a person dies, the luxurious life they lived becomes futile, and the wealth and rank they enjoyed are like a day's dream. Even if thousands of mourners in hemp clothes (*pokin*, 服人) and attendants (*hojang*, 護葬) follow the splendid funeral bier, and dozens of pallbearers chant mournfully, "ŏhŏngdŏhŏng," once they reach the lonely hill of Pukmangsan and the sound of the handbell ceases, no matter how much one regrets and grieves, how could such things possibly be recovered? Will it not merely become, as they say, "The solitary tomb formed, the guests first scattered, the mountain silent, the moon yellow at dusk" (成孤墳客初散 山寂寂月黃昏)?

**Summary**

This article is a continuation of a lecture on *sijo* appreciation and composition by Yi Pyŏnggi. It presents three *sijo* poems—one by Yi Yŏnjin about returning home by boat at night, an anonymous love poem expressing intense longing and a desire to die first to make the beloved grieve, and one by Kim Such'ang reflecting on the vanity of worldly luxury and status in the face of death. Each poem is followed by Yi Pyŏnggi's commentary, explaining the vocabulary, context, and emotional depth, highlighting the artistry of expressing complex feelings concisely within the *sijo* form. The piece concludes with an editor's note apologizing for a previous publication error. From a historical perspective, the article demonstrates the continued engagement with and appreciation of traditional Korean literary forms like *sijo* during the colonial period. Yi Pyŏnggi's role as a teacher, scholar of Hangeul, and member of the Korean Language Society (朝鮮語學會) underscores the efforts to preserve and develop Korean language and culture amidst Japanese colonial rule. The focus on Korean literary heritage and linguistic study within a mainstream magazine like *Samch'ŏlli* is significant.

三, 鑑賞

北斗星 돌아가고 달은 미처 아니젓다.
녜는배 언마나 오야 밤이 이믜 깊엇도다.
風便에 數聲砧 들리니 다왓는가 하노라.

年代는 未詳.
作者는 李延鎭.
北斗星은 北斗七星. 數聲砧은 두어 가락, 다듬이 소리. 미쳐는 아즉. 언마는 얼마.
이건 밤에 배를 타고 돌아오는 情景 北斗七星이 돌아지고 달은 西天에 기울어 있다. 저어가는 배는 어대까지나 온 것이냐. 저 北斗七星과 달을 보아라. 밤은 벌서 깊어젔다-<215> 이러한 말을 沙工 더불어 하든 중, 배는 가고 가며 튀어나는 물결은 배ㅅ머리에 번적어리고 바람은 솔솔 불어오고 두어 가락 다듬이 소리가 미미하게 들닌다. 인자는 다 왔구려.
이 情景을 想像하여도 반가운데 하물려 그 杳茫한 水路를 風浪과 싸우며 죽을 번 하다가 게다가 낮도 아닌 야밤중에 無事히 돌아오자 들니는 그 다듬이 소리에야 여북이나 하였으랴.

보거든 슬밉거나 못보거든 니치거나
네 가지 말거나 내 너를 모르거나
차라리 내 몬저 싀어서 네 그립게 하리라.

作者는 未詳.
슬밉은 싫고 밉다는 말. 몬저는 먼저. 싀어서는 죽어서.
한 번 보고 두 번 보고 하여 여러 번 보고 나면 보기가 싫고 밉기나 하든지 그리다가 못보게 되면 닛히기나 하든지 또는 네가 이 世上에 아여 생기지를 말든지 생기었다 하드라도 내가 너를 몰랐든지-하면 이처럼 나의 肝腸이 타도록 너를 그리워하겠느냐. 만일 너를 여힌다면 내가 잠시라도 부지할 수 있겠느냐. 차라리 내가 너보다 먼저 죽기나 하여 네가 나를 그립게 하랴한다.
과연 그 애틋하게 사랑에 불타는 어수선한 心情을 그 간단한 몇 마디 말로서 다시 더할 나위 없이 그려내었다. 그리고도 單調하지 않고 구성스럽고 줄거리 차게 되었다.<216>
암만 읽고 읽어도 싫증이 아니나게 되었다.

豪華도 거짓것이오 富貴도 꿈이 오데
北邙山 언덕에 搖鈴소래 그쳐지면
아모리 뉘웃고 애닯아도 및을 길이 업나니

李朝 肅宗朝人 金壽長의 作.
豪華는 호강스럽게 사는 것. 富貴는 형세가 넉넉하고 벼슬이 높은 것. 北邙山은 累累衆塚이 있는 荒蕪한 곳. 搖鈴은 搖鈴, 또는 솔밭, 즉 軍命이나 警告에 쓰이는 것이로되 이는 出喪할 때 喪輿 앞에서 首番이 샹두군 소리를 머기며 흔드는 것. 뉘웃은 後悔하는 것. 애닯은 애가 딿는 것.
이는 人生의 無常함을 말 한 것이다.
人生이 한 번 죽어지면 그러케 豪華롭게 사든 것도 헛되이 되고 그러케 富貴를 누리든 것도 한 날 꿈과 같다. 흘란한 喪輿에 服人과 護葬하는 이가 몇 천 명이 따르고 몇 십 명 상두군들이 어헝더헝 구슬푼 소리를 하고 간다드라도 호젓한 北邙山 언덕에 가서 搖鈴 소리만 그치게 되면 제 아모리 後悔를 하고 애를 딿는다 하여도 다시 그러한 일을 어찌 回復할 수 잇으랴. 그야말로 成孤墳客初散 山寂寂月黃昏이나 될 뿐이 아니랴.
