Love's "Hiking Courses" to Take Your Lover To
						Yi Sŏgu
Spring has come. Let's go out for spring fun, to the mountains and waters. Flower viewing and drinking parties will surely spring up everywhere. A flood of people, a great chorus of spring songs – escaping the noise of spring, leaving the stares of the crowd, one person, one person's loving hands clasped together – where are the places laid out for just the two to enjoy the green grass and clear sky, places for just the two to sing? By the kind request of the Samch'ŏlli editor, let me try introducing a few places that come to mind, centered around Kyŏngsŏng.

Take the streetcar. The one bound for Tongdaemun – get off at Tongdaemun and enter the two-story Western-style building rising to the south. It's the station for the narrow-gauge train (긔동차) going to Ttuksŏm. It departs once every 30 minutes. 10 chŏn to Ttuksŏm Resort! You arrive in 25 minutes. At Ttuksŏm Resort, cross the sandy field for five or six chŏng (町 - approx. 109m each), and there's the Han River, flowing like oil. Instead of flower petals, white sails will beckon. Willow branches will offer greetings, and the spring breeze will bring smiles.<94> Take the ferry boat. It's 3 chŏn per person to cross! To the north, a corner of Samgaksan Mountain will rise like a picture. In the village across the river, shrouded in haze, if the scene of figures in dimly visible crimson skirts doing laundry appears, it will be like flowers placed upon silk. When you get off the ferry, ask the white-haired old man mending nets by the riverside for directions. If the white-haired old man isn't there, ask the boatman. If the boatman is young, please, the gentleman lover shouldn't ask, but the lady companion should ask. This I earnestly request. Oh, dear, I forgot to say what you should ask for...

"Pongŭn Temple (봉은사)"

It's a temple. Across the water, Pongŭn Temple – lotus flowers bloom in the front yard, there's the Hall of a Thousand Buddhas (千佛殿), spring water above the Main Buddha Hall (大雄殿), and beneath the zelkova tree, golden grass; lift your head, and luxuriant pine trees soar freely into the blue sky...
The magnificent embrace of Pongŭn Temple nestled in the woods. By the pond, there's a house selling meals. Though you won't hear the voice of a lovely young lady saying
"Have a drink,"
and you can't smell grilling fish, there are clean rooms, tasty vegetable dishes, and friendly "service" (써비쓰) prepared. The meal price is 70 chŏn per person. As you walk from the ferry landing, you cross one hill and there's also a field, albeit narrow. It's close enough that even a lady walking in high-heeled shoes won't get sore feet, let alone ankles. If just buying a meal seems dull, or if you want a snack while the meal is being prepared, it seems they also have quince tea (모과수통), beer bottles, and cider.

Streetcar fare: 20 chŏn
Narrow-gauge train: 40 chŏn
Ferry fare: 12 chŏn
Meal cost: 140 chŏn
Total: 212 chŏn

For 2 wŏn and 12 chŏn, you can have a day's spring outing. Whether there's a human landslide at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn or a chaotic crowd heading to Uidong, there's likely no better place than this for a spring outing spot where just two people can stroll without hindrance. Why couldn't you race on the sandy field? And why couldn't you sing while crossing the hill? It's a situation where you can relax, stretch out, and enjoy a day's spring outing, a hike of love. It would be a touching thing for the love of two people to be tied early, praying before the great statue of Sakyamuni Buddha to let them meet at the wedding ceremony hall (전안청), applying rouge and wearing the groom's cap (사모). It would also be meaningful to pray fervently at the Hall of a Thousand Buddhas for stubborn parents to quickly change their minds and approve the love of the two. Those who have such needs should definitely prepare about 20 chŏn in coins separately. Nowadays, it costs money even to make a request to the Buddha. There's a wooden box covered with a latticed lid; when you toss money in,
*Clang!*
it makes a sound as the money rolls in. The
*Clang!*<95>
sound, the more lively and loud it is, the more generous the heartfelt blessing (心祝) of the head monk listening will likely be. I think it might have a greater effect than inserting paper money that makes no sound.

Another course. Namsan Mountain, which you look up at morning and evening.
"I wish I could go up there just once."
"How far could it be?"
Anyone might harbor such thoughts. Samgaksan and Pugaksan are not unattractive, but they might be tiring for a lady's pace. So, those gentlemen who lack the strength to carry their lady down the mountain should choose the Namsan course. If the lady weighs over 12 kwan (貫 - approx. 45kg), even the strongest man will find it painful to carry her down a rough mountain. If fate turns cruel and you tumble, it's two corpses. Two deaths without even a will –
a love suicide (情死)!
Listeners might find it intriguing, but wouldn't it be regrettable once you're dead? At Kyŏngsŏng Shrine on Namsan, turn west, and there's the Land Survey Monument (土地調査記念碑), and below the hill behind it is a spring called "Puŏng Bawi" (부엉바위 - Owl Rock). It's been a famous spring since the 500 years of the Yi Dynasty. First, quench your thirst. As you walk up holding hands, sweat might have formed on your hands; dipping them in the clear water refreshes even the heart. Ask the child selling snacks at Puŏng Bawi,
"Where is Waryongdang (와룡당)?"<96>
They will point and say,
"It's right back here."
Namsan Waryongdang is a shrine for praying for blessings, a place once highly regarded by the powerful families of the northern and southern villages, and even now, women coming to offer devotional prayers (치성) are ceaseless. After going up to Waryongdang and praying,
"May it not rain..."
"May my lover's heart always be like today's,"
find the mountain path bit by bit and head up towards the signal fire mound (봉수). It's not unknown to anyone, but climbing one corner reveals more of the plain, and conquering one hill makes the capital city seem that much lower. Once you reach the midway point, human presence fades, and only the sound of the wind in the pines and birdsong reach your ears. Even if a squirrel startled by your footsteps gets its tail accidentally stepped on, please don't try to catch it. Spring is surely a good thing for them too. Even resting on the way, you can easily reach the Namsan signal fire mound within an hour. Though the path isn't wide, it's clearly visible, so you can leave worries about getting lost at home. I don't know if the shaman's house (당즉이집) is still there after the National Shrine (국사당) at the Namsan signal fire mound disappeared, but the spring couldn't possibly have dried up. Spread out the packed sandwiches (산도윗치 - sandwich), fruits, and snacks, take the gourd flask that was hanging from the gentleman lover's backside, and draw water to drink. The gentleman lover should find a place to sit, and the lady should go fetch the water. Although the signal fire mound looks pointed from below, when you go up, you find level ground amidst overgrown old trees, and in the gaps of the golden grass, yellow and red flowers are smiling shyly. Looking south, Kwanaksan Mountain crawls into view right before your eyes, and looking north, Samgaksan Mountain lies spread beneath your feet. What always catches the eye first are Ch'angdŏk Palace, the French Catholic Church (불란서 교당), and whether or not you hear the plaintive spring laments of those sighing in Sŏdaemun Prison, the cherry blossoms filling the base of Namsan, the dense cherry blossoms in Ch'anggyŏngwŏn, the flower clouds hanging over the hill behind Kyŏngsŏng Middle School – even without looking at them, it would be a top-tier spring outing spot. Take a nap on the grassy field. Climb a tree and play hide-and-seek. Who is there to stop you? Whose gaze would hinder your fun? Who would say anything if you act as if you're playing in your own backyard?
"Oh my goodness. There's another couple in the shade of that tree over there!"
"Look, there's another one beneath that collapsed fortress wall!"
Love's hike knows no 'us' or 'them'. The desire to enjoy it just between two is common to everyone, so what does it matter if there's another group over the ridge? Is that all? They are people walking the same path of youth and love. If one group plays a harmonica or sings a song, wouldn't it be wonderful if everyone gathered together smiling from this forest and that hillside, and perhaps even did the social dancing that's causing such a stir downtown?<97> Play at the Namsan signal fire mound until about an hour before sunset, then follow the fortress wall eastward, pass the site of Namsomun (South Small Gate), and through the pine forest path, Changch'ungdan Park comes into view. Descending to Changch'ungdan and returning by streetcar is fine. Or, if time permits and your pockets are heavy (around 2 Wŏn), head towards Hangang-ri and descend the southern mountain slope, where young boatmen and newly built ferry boats are waiting. Renting a boat and singing boat songs as you head down to Hangang Bridge or Samgae Ferry would also add to the excitement.

Bentō (벤도 - boxed lunch): 120 chŏn
Fruit: 20 chŏn
Chocolate (초코렛트): 20 chŏn
Drops (도롭프 - candies): 10 chŏn
Boat fare: 200 chŏn
Total: 370 chŏn

If you don't take the boat and return by streetcar, it costs 1 wŏn 80 chŏn. Put the drops in your mouth instead of rock candy while hiking. If, by mistake,<98> a leftover candy the gentleman was eating happens to fly into the lady's mouth, since there are no witnesses, submitting a protest has no chance of success.
"Oh my, that's delicious!"
Preparing to laugh cheerfully in advance is considered proper, both morally and hygienically.

Another course. For those who cannot spend the whole day playing, a simple course. Get off the streetcar in front of the former Kyŏnggi Provincial Office site (경긔감영) (Takezoe-chō crossroads - 竹添町四거리). Go over the hill path with rows of modern houses (문화주택) in the back alleys of Naengdong (冷洞). You will come out at the site of Yŏnhŭi Palace (연희궁터). Crossing the hill from the Yŏnhŭi Palace site and walking the path through the dense woods is the climax (크라이맑스) of this course. Ewha Womans Professional School (리화뎐문), Yonhi College (연희뎐문), and the nursery/orphanage (육아홈) are all nestled within these woods. Here and there among the pine trees, on cushions of golden grass with azalea folding screens set up, swarms of butterflies will flutter about as if welcoming the bride and groom; choose a spot as you wish and talk to your heart's content. Argue, reconcile, flirt, cry and then laugh; when you get hungry, find Sinch'on Station and take the train (trains run frequently) back to Kyŏngsŏng. If you have time left, take the narrow-gauge train (긔동차) to Tangin-ri (唐人里) and try walking upstream along the Han River. Shepherd's purse flowers abound, weeping willows have put out their green curtains, and on the Han River flowing like silk, the poetic mood will ripen with boat songs drifting from Panghwado Island. As you walk up along the riverbanks through Hajung-ri (賀中里), Kamŭndol (玄石里 - Hyŏnsŏk-ri), T'ojŏng-ri (土亭里), scenes unfamiliar to your eyes at every ferry landing will surely provide you with 120% satisfaction.

Another course. Take the streetcar to Ch'ŏngnyangni. Then, enter the side road by the police substation (순사주재소) and take the main road going to the Forestry Experiment Station (림업시험장). Go over "Ojum Kogae" (오줌고개) – do you all know Ojum Kogae? If not, go ask at the nearby motor pool (자동차부). To the west side, there's a narrow path through the woods. Enter this path, and the affairs of Seoul are forgotten.
"Ah, this is nice."
Such words will surely escape from the lips of one of the two. Walk along this cozy path through the woods, where flowers bloom, butterflies fly, and birds sing, as if dreaming, singing songs. Wherever you end up, be it a rice paddy ridge or a hillside slope, if you don't head west, you'll emerge in front of Posŏng College (보성뎐문학교) in Chongam-ni before covering even 5 ri (里 - approx. 2km). Here lies the tomb of Princess Myŏngon (명온공주), and in the large tile-roofed house resembling her tomb shrine, hangs a plaque reading "Yŏnsu Pavilion" (延壽閣). This place offers baths and simple Japanese and Chinese cuisine. It's clean and quiet. For about 1 wŏn 50 chŏn per person,<99> you can spend several hours playing, have a meal, and return. On the hill behind Yŏnsu Pavilion, there's a gently sloping hill covered in golden grass, truly well-maintained like a golf course (꼴푸장). Rolling around here for just an hour will make the fatigue from walking from Ch'ŏngnyangni vanish like clouds. What burden is the 15-minute walk back to the streetcar line?

Another course. Take the streetcar. Get off at the Hyojadong terminus and go outside Chihamun Gate. Apple blossoms are in full bloom, and clear water flows over a bed of jewel-like white rocks. Even worries that burdened you while crossing the pass will surely be cleared away by just one listen to this water's sound. Segŏmjŏng is also nice, and going beneath the hill enshrining the stone Buddha to pledge that the two will not change their minds is also a memorable act. Don't worry if your clothes get wet while catching crayfish in the rock crevices. The gentle spring breeze will surely dry a wet silk skirt hem in no time. Around here, it seems there won't be much scolding even if you pick some flowers to take back; feel free to pick a handful of red azaleas or rhododendrons and return. There's also one place that sells meals. However, I advise you to ask the price beforehand and then eat. For the return, go out via Hongjewŏn and take the bus (뻐쓰); crossing Muhakchae Pass brings you to Hyŏnjŏdong. The fare, including the city streetcar transfer ticket, is 8 chŏn. For enjoying the charm of beautiful rocks and water (수석) and the scenery deep in the mountains, it gets full marks! However, although the path isn't very long, the round trip might be about 15 ri (approx. 6km), so I advise you to demand grim determination from a young lady wearing high-heeled shoes. Of course, for this course, you can reverse the starting and ending points. Well, this seems to cover most of the worthwhile places to go from Kyŏngsŏng. Now, have fun and return safely.<100>

**Summary**

This 1936 article by Yi Sŏgu in Samch'ŏlli magazine suggests several "hiking courses" near Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul) suitable for young lovers seeking privacy and natural beauty during spring. It details routes involving combinations of streetcars, narrow-gauge trains, ferries, and walking to places like Pongŭn Temple, Namsan Mountain (including Waryongdang shrine and the signal fire mound), the Sinch'on/Yŏnhŭi area, the Forestry Experiment Station near Ch'ŏngnyangni, and the area beyond Chihamun Gate towards Segŏmjŏng. The author provides practical advice on transportation, costs, food options (including packed lunches or local eateries), and activities, emphasizing quiet spots away from crowds where couples can enjoy nature and each other's company, even suggesting prayers for their relationship's success or playing hide-and-seek. The tone is lighthearted and encouraging, offering specific directions and estimated expenses for each outing.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a fascinating glimpse into middle-class leisure activities and dating culture in colonial Seoul. It reveals the transportation infrastructure (streetcars, trains, buses, ferries) available and its cost, the blend of traditional Korean sites (temples, shrines, palace grounds, historical markers) with colonial/modern elements (Western-style buildings, Japanese place names like Takezoe-chō, Kyŏngsŏng Shrine, Forestry Experiment Station, modern colleges, mentions of Sŏdaemun Prison, Japanese food like 'bentō'), and changing social norms allowing young men and women to go on unaccompanied outings. The mention of specific costs, the suggestion of praying for parental approval for relationships, the casual reference to "social dancing," and the description of picnic spots reflect the evolving urban lifestyle and aspirations under colonial rule.

愛人 다리고 갈 사랑의 『하이킹 코-스』
						李瑞求
봄은 왓다. 산으로 물노 봄노리를 가자. 꼿구경 술타령은 곳곳이 버러지리라. 사람의 홍수 봄노래의 대합창- 봄의 소음을 버서나 무리의 눈총을 떠나 한 사람, 한 사람의 사랑의 두손길- 프른 잔듸 맑은 하늘 두리만 즐길 곳, 두 사람 만이 노래 부를 곳은 어대어대 벌녀 잇나. 삼천리 편즙장의 은근한 주문- 생각나는대로 몃 곳, 경성을 중심으로 소개해 보기로 하자.
뎐차를 타시요. 동대문행- 동대문서 나리시거든 남편에 소사잇는 이층 양관으로 드러가시요. 뚝섬가는 긔동차의 정거장임니다. 30분에 한 번식 발차기 됨니다. 뚝섬유원디까지 10전! 25분만에 도착함니다. 뚝섬유원디에서 모래밧을 오륙뎡 가로 건너가면 기름 가치 흐르는 한강. 꼿닢대신 흰돗대가 손짓을 하오리다. 버들가지가 인사를 드리고 봄바람이 우슴을 치오리다.<94> 나루배를 타시요. 한 사람 건느는데 3전! 북으로 삼각산의 한모통이가 그림갓치 소사잇사오리다. 아즈랑이에 자옥싸힌 강 건너 마을에는 어렴풋이 다홍치마 자리들의 빨내하는 풍경이 보히면은 비단 우에 꼿을 언즌 세음되리다. 나루배를 나리시거든 강가에서 그물을 꾀매는 백발 노인에게 길을 무르시요. 백발 노인이 안게시거든 배사공에게 무르시요. ㅣ배사공이 점거든 사랑양반이 무러 보시지 말고 안악양반이 무러 보십시요. 이것만은 신신 부탁임니다. 아차, 차, 무엇을 무르시라는 말슴을 뺏슴니다 그려...
『봉은사』
절임니다. 물 건너 봉은사이라면 압뜰에 연꼿이 피고 천불전(千佛殿)이 잇고 대웅뎐 위에 약물이 잇고 느틔나무 밋헤는 금잔듸 고개를 들면 프른 하늘에 마음것 소슨 락락장송...
숩속에 안겨 잇는 봉은사의 장엄한 배포. 연못가에는 밥파는 집이 잇슴니다. 어엿븐 자근 아씨네의
한 잔 드십시요.
소리는 업고 고기 굽는 냄새는 맛흘 수 업스나 깨끗한 방과 맛가로은 채소와 다정한 『써비쓰』가 갓추어 잇슴니다. 밥갑은 일인분 70전- 나루에서 거러가시는 데로 언덕도 하나 넘고 좁으나마 벌판도 잇슴니다. 안악네가 굽놉흔 구쓰로 거러도 발 커냥 발목도 아프지 안을 만콤 갓갑슴니다. 밥만 사먹기 심심하시거나 밥 지을 동안 주전부리를 하시랴면 모과수통도 잇고 맥주병 사이다도 잇는 모양임니다.
