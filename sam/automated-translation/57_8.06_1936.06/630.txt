Magazine Title: 삼천리 제8권 제6호
Publication Date: 1936년 06월 01일
Article Title: 야릇한 마음 (A Strange Heart / A Peculiar Feeling)
Author: 申不出
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A Strange Heart
						Sin Puch'ul

In the flower shade under moonlight, secretly hiding and standing,
My beloved, unable to find me, burns with anxiety and calls out,
I want to see the sight of them getting angry and crying<290>

**Summary**

This short poem expresses the speaker's peculiar desire to hide from their lover and derive a hidden pleasure from watching the lover's distress – their anxiety, calling out, and eventual anger and tears – upon being unable to find the speaker. The poem captures a complex and slightly voyeuristic, perhaps mischievous, emotion. For historians, the piece is interesting as an example of personal lyrical poetry from the colonial era, showcasing emotional nuance and using Korean language forms and spellings common in the 1930s (like '몰내' for '몰래').
