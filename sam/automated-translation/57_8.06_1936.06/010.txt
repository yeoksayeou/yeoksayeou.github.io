**Thought Circles, Press Circles**

**Snippets of Mr. An Ch'ang-ho's Southern Tour**

Mr. An Ch'ang-ho (安昌浩), known as Tosan (島山), who came to Seoul in mid-February and stayed at the Chungang Hotel, departed on February 20th with Mr. Kim Ch'ŏl-chung (金鐵中), the General Affairs Manager of the Tong-a Ilbo Company, for a tour of the southern provinces (南鮮). Covering the Honam Line and Kyŏngbu Line areas swiftly during a brief 10-day period, he completed his very busy journey and arrived back at Kyŏngsŏng Station by night train on February 30th [Note: likely a typo for 29th or March 1st].
Looking at his itinerary for this trip, one can roughly grasp the nature of his journey.
Leaving Kyŏngsŏng Station on the night of the 20th, he stopped at Taejŏn Station, where he had lunch at the station restaurant, welcomed by local figures. He then proceeded to Iri, arriving to rest for the first night. The next day, after viewing the farming villages near Iri, a welcoming party was held by various figures from Iri the previous night. Leaving Iri, he arrived in Kunsan. Here, he particularly visited private civilian schools, and again, a welcoming party by local figures was held during his one-night stay.
From there, he stopped at Kimje (金溝), staying overnight at the residence of Mr. Chang Hyŏn-sik (張鉉植). Mr. Chang Hyŏn-sik then accompanied him to Kwangju. He also rested for two nights in Kwangju. Here too, he heard from local figures about private educational institutions and <18> the living conditions of the general populace. When traveling past Mokp'o to Sunch'ŏn, Pastor Ch'oe Hŭng-jong (崔興琮) from Mokp'o joined him. In Sunch'ŏn, he visited the leper treatment center in Sinp'ung-ri and proceeded to Yŏsu by automobile.
During this time, he observed the Pui Company and Pui Farm along the west coast line, and the land reclamation situation in the tidal flats along the Mangyŏng River. Then, by automobile via Hadong and Chinju, he stayed another night in Masan. From there, he went to Taegu, arriving on the 29th. After resting for a day in Taegu, he returned to Seoul on the 7:30 PM train the next day. At every place he stayed overnight during this trip, there was a welcoming party held by local figures.
For Mr. An, who had never toured the southern provinces before, how must the life of the urban poor, the state of private education, and the life of farmers in the rural villages have appeared? It is clear that he must have been deeply moved by many things.

**Two Million Won in Public Funds for Education**

Recently, the fervor for education has been tremendous throughout the capital and provinces, and much pure wealth (淨財) from people of high intention is being contributed as educational funds, earning the respect of all for their beautiful and noble intentions. According to an investigation by our company, the amount of pure wealth donated towards education in recent months reaches approximately 2,000,000 Won. First to be counted is the 560,000 Won for Anak Ordinary Higher School (安岳普), 400,000 Won for an Ordinary Higher School (高普) in Kyŏngju, South Kyŏngsang Province, 300,000 Won for an Ordinary Higher School in Suwŏn, 300,000 Won for an Ordinary Higher School in Chŏlla Province, and 300,000 Won for a commercial school in Najin, North Hamgyŏng Province. Just those publicly announced amount to about 2,000,000 Won. Besides these, it seems possible that several more schools might be founded, starting with the new establishment of an Ordinary Higher School in South Hamgyŏng Province, which is still under consideration (腹案中).

**Social Figures Express Gratitude to Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek**

Upon receiving the happy news that Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek (金祺宅), a young businessman from Najin, North Hamgyŏng Province, had decided to establish a new commercial school and contribute 300,000 Won to society, about 30 figures in Seoul, including Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Yun Ik-sŏn (尹益善), Kim Ch'ang-kwŏn (金昌權), Kim Ki-dŏk (金基德), Kim Yŏng-gŭn (金永根), O Ok (吳億), Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥), gathered at Yŏngdosa Temple (永導寺) on May 9th and reportedly sent a formal letter of appreciation to the founder, Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek. <19>

**Aftermath for Writers Involved in the KAPF Incident**

Pak Yŏng-hŭi (朴英熙) = Mr. Pak, who spent last year imprisoned in Chŏnju Prison under the so-called "New Construction Incident" or "Chŏnju Incident," has been greatly missed since his release. Just before his imprisonment two years ago, he had withdrawn from KAPF (캅프) and was developing new literary theories, making his future writing activities a focus of general attention. Recently, he has been secluded in his home in Ch'ŏnyŏn-dong, Seoul, engrossed only in English original texts. To a friend who visited him, he reportedly said, "From now on, I won't write short pieces. I want to write something that, once written, will have lasting value as literature for later days. In the past, I focused on guiding literary theory, but from now on, I intend to focus on creative writing..."
Yi Ki-yŏng (李箕永) = Mr. Yi, who shone like a giant star in the Korean literary world after "Hometown" (故鄕), immediately upon his release went to his home in Nuha-dong, Seoul. While barely maintaining his frail health, he is an energetic and prolific writer, continuing to publish creative works in various publications in addition to writing the long novel "Human Training" (人間修業). A certain publisher is said to have already made a firm promise with Mr. Yi to publish "Human Training," currently being serialized in the Chungang newspaper, as a standalone book around early autumn this year.
Paek Ch'ŏl (白鐵) = Mr. Paek was very active as a young critic. After returning from Chŏnju, he went back to his quiet hometown countryside in P'ihyŏn, North P'yŏngan Province, where he is recovering his health while steadily wielding his sharp critical pen.
Yi Kap-ki (李甲基) = Mr. Yi, a critic and painter, returned to his hometown Taegu immediately after leaving Chŏnju. Busy resting his weary body for a while, he then came up to the capital and is currently a guest staff member at the Chosŏn Ilbo Company. Using the pen name "Kimhwa" (金華), he displays his talent through "Contemporary Caricatures" (時專漫畵) while also taking up his pen earnestly to develop new literary theories.
Song Yŏng (宋影) = Mr. Song, a playwright, after returning from there, has been staying in a quiet thatched cottage outside Ch'angŭimun Gate, rarely entering the city, and diligently focusing on creative writing. Having published 5 or 6 works including "Mr. Wŏlp'a" (月波先生), his energy is also quite remarkable.
Kim Yu-yŏng (金幽影) = Mr. Kim, a film director who showed his skill in "Fiery Wheels" (火輪), remained quiet after returning from Chŏnju. Recent news is that he has been invited to join C.C.M. Film Company<20> as a director and is busy preparing his first work. Much is expected from him in the future.
Yun Ki-jŏng (尹基鼎) = Mr. Yun was very active with creative writing and criticism during the early New Tendency School era. Just before this incident occurred, he had been somewhat silent in the literary world, focusing only on family matters. After returning from Chŏnju, he has remained quiet so far. What thoughts occupy the mind of Mr. Yun, who currently spends his days in Such'ang-dong, Seoul?
Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) = Ms. Ch'oe joined the Chosŏn Ilbo Company a few months ago and is working there while also focusing on creative writing.

**Yi Yong-ik Lawsuit Ultimately Appealed**

The case concerning the one million Won deposit of Yi Yong-ik (李容翊) at the Dai-Ichi Bank (第一銀行), which has continued for three years under public scrutiny, awaited the judgment of the Tokyo Court of Appeal, scheduled for April 25th. Mr. Yi Chong-kwan (李鍾涫), grandson of Yi Yong-ik, was set to travel specially from Seoul to Tokyo for this day. Reporting on the scene at the Tokyo Court of Appeal on March 24th: The presiding judge questioned the witness Shinoda (篠田), Chief Secretary of the Yi Royal Household Office (李王職長官), who appeared that day:
Judge: "Did the witness know that Yi Yong-ik, former Minister of the Imperial Treasury (前大藏院卿), had a deposit at the Dai-Ichi Bank?"
Shinoda: "Yes, I knew."
Judge: "How did you know?"
Shinoda: "The case was so large and widely reported in the newspapers that I learned about it from them."
Judge: "There is a claim that the money was public funds (公金). What does the witness think?"
Shinoda: "When I was Vice-Minister of the Yi Royal Household Office, I heard such talk from Chief Secretary Yun Tŏk-yŏng (尹德榮), but that was Mr. Yun's personal opinion. I do not know whether it was public funds or not."
Judge: "Then, the Dai-Ichi Bank says it paid the money to the Yi Royal Household Office. Did the Yi Royal Household Office receive it?"
Shinoda: "I have served in the Yi Royal Household Office since Taishō 12 (1923), but to my knowledge, no such event occurred."
With this, the hearing concluded. In essence, the core of this trial is that since the Supreme Court (大審院) established a precedent that the statute of limitations does not apply to current accounts (當座預金), the bank's claim of prescription is invalidated. Next, the bank claims it paid the money to the Yi Royal Household Office, believing it to be public funds. However, Chief Secretary Shinoda's recent testimony seems to prove this false. So, what will be the outcome of this trial? Truly,<21> everyone can only gather their eyes and ears and await the result of the appeal judgment.

**The Nine Day Club Revolving Around Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl**

Centered around Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl (朴榮喆), president of the Commercial Bank and councilor of the Central Council (中樞院叅議), a social group called the Nine Day Club (九日會) was founded in Seoul several years ago. This club requires members to gather without fail on the ninth day of each month, with the location decided ad hoc each time. The location and arrangements are decided through consultation between the permanent secretary and two monthly secretaries, who then notify each member. What is the purpose of this club? Besides fostering friendship among members, the slogan they promote is:
"Research and Practice Things Necessary for Life" (生活上 必要한 것을 硏究實行)
In other words, aiming for life improvement,<22> members present their opinions, which are then formed into the collective will of the Nine Day Club, and each member strives to implement them. The office of the Nine Day Club is at Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl's residence at 144 Sogyeok-dong (昭格町). The members are as follows (no particular order):
Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl (朴榮喆), Kim Myŏng-jun (金明濬), Sŏk Myŏng-sŏn (石明瑄), Pak Ch'ŏl-hŭi (朴喆熙), Yu Il-ŭi (柳一宜), Kim Pyŏng-?? (金炳*), Chang Hong-sik (張弘植), Pak Chun-ho (朴俊鎬), Cho Pyŏng-hak (曺秉學), Yang Chae-ch'ang (梁在昶), Yi Sŭng-u (李承宇), Sim U-sŏp (沈友燮), Cho Pyŏng-sang (曺秉相), Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak (崔昌學), Kim Ye-hyŏn (金禮顯), Yi Hong-jong (李洪鍾), Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協), Kim Sŏng-hwan (金成煥), Mun Sŭng-t'ak (文承卓), Sŏng Hong-wŏn (成洪遠), Kang Nak-wŏn (姜樂遠), Min Kyu-sik (閔奎植), Kim P'il-su (金弼洙), Kim Su-hak (金秀學), Pak Ch'ang-hun (朴昌薰), Yi Sŏn-gŭn (李先根), Kang Chin-su (姜振秀), Kim Yong-jin (金容鎭), Yi Han-bok (李漢福), Pak Hŭng-sik (朴興植). **Company President's Monthly Salary: Five Hundred Won**

A discussion arose at some gathering about which newspaper president receives the highest monthly salary. After much debate, guessing this person or that, the conclusion was reached that the Maeil Sinbo (每日申報), being subsidized by the Government-General (總督府), probably pays the most. As a result of investigating the Maeil Sinbo president's monthly salary, it turned out to be 500 Won. The president is a Mr. Tokizane (時實), formerly the Governor of Kyŏnggi Province. It is said he receives 500 Won as president of the Keijō Nippō (京城日報) and 500 Won as president of the Maeil Sinbo, totaling 1,000 Won per month.
The Vice President of Maeil Sinbo is Mr. Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協), pen name Hamong (何夢). Including allowances and housing expenses, his monthly income is reportedly 350 Won.

**Erection of a Monument for Sin Ch'ae-ho**

For Tanjae (丹齋) Sin Ch'ae-ho (申采浩), whose spirit scattered into the vastness of that distant foreign castle along with the clouds and mists of Lüshun's (旅順) sky, his close relatives and acquaintances reportedly intend to erect a memorial stone at his grave. <23>

**May Day and Children's Day**

In Tokyo, where martial law (戒嚴令) has not yet been lifted following the February 26th Incident (二·二六 事變), May Day (메이데이) processions on May 1st were completely banned due to the state of emergency. It seems May Day was also entirely prohibited in Korea. However, Children's Day (어린이 날) was reportedly held as usual on the first public holiday of May 3rd.

**Mr. Sin T'ae-ak Has No Connection to Paegakhoe**

Regarding the article in last month's issue stating that lawyer Sin T'ae-ak (辛泰嶽) had joined the Paegakhoe (白岳會), Mr. Sin subsequently informed us that he had not joined. We hereby publish his letter as follows:
To Samch'ŏlli Company: Respectfully, Sin T'ae-ak (辛泰嶽拜)
Your esteemed magazine's April issue published that I seem to have a connection with the Paegakhoe. However, I not only have no connection whatsoever, but am entirely unaware of this matter. I request that your magazine retract this.
Two Corrections = In this magazine's April issue, regarding the arrival of the remains of Sin Ch'ae-ho (申采浩), who died in Lüshun (旅順), at Kyŏngsŏng Station, the name of Tong-a Ilbo's Editor-in-Chief, Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植), who attended representing the company, was omitted from the list of names. We correct this here. Also, in the article about Paegakhoe, the phrase "it is said they are using money procured through the mediation of Mr. Yi Kak-chong (李覺鍾氏가 周旋하여 가저오는 돈을 쓰고 잇다 한다)" is corrected to "it is said they are using money donated by Mr. Yi Kak-chong (李覺鍾氏가 내는 돈으로 쓰고 잇다 한다)."

**Summary**

This "Confidential Room" news section from the June 1936 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* covers a wide range of topics concerning Korean society under Japanese rule. It details nationalist leader An Ch'ang-ho's recent tour of southern Korea, highlighting his interest in education and the lives of ordinary people. It reports on significant philanthropic donations towards establishing schools, updates on the lives and activities of prominent writers (formerly associated with the disbanded proletarian arts federation KAPF) after their imprisonment, the ongoing legal battle over Yi Yong-ik's sizable bank deposit, a list of influential fascist organizations in Japan, the membership and activities of a Seoul social club (Kuilhoe) composed of elite figures, brief updates on various individuals' movements and careers, salary details for the president of the government-backed newspaper *Maeil Sinbo*, plans for a memorial to historian Sin Ch'ae-ho, the suppression of May Day while allowing Children's Day celebrations, corrections to previous articles, and concludes with a list of prominent Seoul figures and the estimated value of their private automobiles. The article offers valuable glimpses into the colonial elite's social life, philanthropic activities, and interactions with the Japanese authorities, the surveillance and constraints faced by intellectuals and activists (like An Ch'ang-ho and the ex-KAPF writers), the growing influence of Japanese right-wing ideology, and socio-economic trends like the increasing adoption of automobiles by the wealthy Korean class in Seoul. Historians might find the specific names, organizations, financial details, and the juxtaposition of political/social developments particularly interesting.

安昌浩氏의 南遊片片

지난 2월 중순 서울에 올너와 中央호텔에 게시든 島山 安昌浩氏는 2월 20일에 東亞日報社 庶務部長 金鐵中氏와 함께 南鮮遊歷의 길을 떠낫섯는데, 10일간의 쩔본 時日에 湖南線 京釜線 一帶를 얼는얼는 지나치시면서 심히 밧부신 旅程을 끗맛치고 2월 30일 밤차로 京城驛에 내리시엿다.
그런데 氏의 이번 旅行의 旅程을 보면 이분의 이번길을 대강 짐작할 수 잇겟다.
20일 밤 京城驛을 떠나 大田驛에 들여서 그곳 人士들의 歡迎으로 驛 食堂에서 點心을 잡숫고, 그길로 裡里에 到着되여 첫날밤을 쉬다 다음날 裡里 近隣의 農村을 구경하다, 裡里의 여러 人士들의 歡迎會가 전날밤 잇섯다. 裡里를 떠나 群山에 到着되다. 이곳에서는 民間私立學校 등을 특히 보시고, 하룻밤 이곳에서도 當地人士들의 歡迎會가 잇섯다.
그 길로 金溝에 들여 張鉉植氏 宅에서 하룻밤을 머물느고, 張鉉植氏도 함께 光州에 이르럿다. 光州에서도 이틀 밤을 쉬엿다. 亦是 民間敎育機關과 <18> 一般細民들의 生活狀態를 이곳 人士들에게 들으셧다. 木浦를 지나 順天에 올 때에는 木浦에서 崔興琮牧師가 함께 왓다. 順天에서는 新豊里에 잇는 癩病患者治療所을 구경하시고 自動車로 麗水에 들니다.
그동안 西海岸 沿線의 不二會社, 不二農場 등을 보시고 萬頃江沿線의 干瀉地開拓狀况 등을 구경하다, 그길로 自動車로 河東晋州를 거처 馬山에 또 하룻밤 머물느다, 그길로 大邱에 갓섯는데 그날이 바로 29일이엿다. 大邱에서 하루 쉬여서 다음날 午後 7시 半 차로 서울로 돌아오셧다, 이번 旅行에 하룻밤씩 들여서 쉬시는 곳에은 모다 當地 人士들의 歡迎會가 잇섯다.
아직 한번도 南鮮地方을 구경하신 일이 업다는 氏의게는, 南鮮의 都市細民層의 生活이며 또 民間敎育狀况이며, 農村의 農民生活 등이 모든 것이 어떠케 비초엿슬넌지, 아마 氏로서는 퍽으나 늣끼심이 만흐셧슬 것이 分明하다.

李容翊裁判은 結局上告

一世의 注目 속에서 數 3년 裁判이 繼續되어 가는 李容翊氏의 第一銀行 預金 百萬圓事件은, 東京控訴院의 判決이 4월 25일로 迫頭하엿다. 이날 서울로부터는 李容翊氏의 孫 李鍾涫氏가 일부러 東京으로 갈 터인 바 이제 지난 3월 24일, 東京控訴院의 法廷 光景을 報導하건대 裁判長은 當日 出頭한 證人인 篠田 李王職 長官에게 대하야
裁 「前大藏院卿 李容翊이 第一銀行에 맛긴 預金이 잇섯든 줄을 證人은 아럿든가」
篠田 「아럿슴니다」
裁 「엇더케 알엇든가」
篠田 「事件이 너무 커서 新聞에서 여러번 떠들엇기 그것을 보고 알엇슴니다」
裁 「그 돈이 公金이란 說이 잇는데 證人는 엇더케 생각하는가」
篠田 「나도 李王職次官으로 在職하든 때에 長官 尹德榮氏로부터 그런 말을 드럿스나 그것는尹氏 個人의 생각이고, 公金인 與否는 나는 모르겟슴니다」
裁 「그러면 第一銀行에서는 그 돈을 李王職에 내어 주엇다고 말하는데 李王職에서는 밧은 일 잇는가」
篠田 「나는 大正 12년부터 李王職에 在職하엿스나 내 아는 한에는 그런 일이 업섯소이다」
이것으로 結審하여 버린 것이다. 요컨대 이 裁判의 核心은, 當座預金에는 時效가 아니걸닌다고 大審院서 判例를 만들엇슨즉 銀行側에서 主張하는 時效는 不成立되엇고, 그다음은 銀行側에선 公金인줄 알고, 李王職에 내어 주엇다고 말하는데, 그것이 篠田 李王職長官의 이번 證言으로 虛僞인 것이 判明된 셈인데, 그러면 이 裁判의 結果는 엇더케 되어지는고, 진실로<21> 萬人이모다 耳目을 한데 모으고 上告判決의 結果를 기다릴 박게 길이 업겟다.

長安 「名士」 自動車

서울 長安에는 하루에도 수업시 「아스팔트」 우으로 구으러 단이는 新型 『씨보레』 流線型自動車가 이 거리에 쏘단이는 市井人들의 말쑥말쑥한 옷자락에 몬지를 피우며 달아나고 잇다.
그런 중에는 서울 안 「名士」들의 自家用 自動車가 한 둘이 아니다. 멧 해 전만 하드래도 그런 줄 몰으겟든데, 요즈음에 와서는 長安에서 누구누구하는 「名士」들이란 거지반 自家用 自動車를 한 臺쯤은 가지고 잇다.
