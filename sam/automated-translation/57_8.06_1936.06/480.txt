Impressions of Meeting Literary Giants
A Few Figures of the Tōkyō Literary Scene

Yu Chin-o
I have never met any overseas writers, so I have no impressions of encounters. I have met a few writers in Tōkyō (Mr. Yokomitsu, etc.), but I have no particular impressions.

Eroshenko & Dr. Einstein

Kim Pok-chin
Years ago, when I was in Tōkyō, I met Einstein and the Russian poet Mr. Eroshenko. I saw Einstein from afar, sitting down while he gave a lecture at the Public Hall (公會堂), so it's difficult to say what my impression of him was.
However, Mr. Eroshenko gave some lecture at the Meiji Hall (明治會館) in Kanda, and I attended the lecture together with him for one night there, and we talked for a long time that night. He seemed very much like a poet in his language, behavior, and everything. Among the foreigners I've seen, he probably made the best first impression on me.<231>

Indian Poet Sage Tagore

Kim Sang-yong
I met the Indian poet sage, old Mr. Tagore. It was right after the Great Kantō Earthquake (震災), and although it was just once, the impression was quite deep. During my student days, I had avidly read his dreamlike, mystical poems like "The Crescent Moon" and "Gitanjali," so when a lecture meeting was held by invitation of Tōkyō Imperial University, I rushed there. At first glance, he looked like a literary giant. His white hair and youthful face, his appearance and voice, reminiscent of a saint or prophet... his voice, too, resonated like a bell amidst the quiet. It rang out sonorously enough for the audience of 400-500 people to hear clearly.

Son Chin-t'ae
I also saw him speak at the Waseda University Auditorium during my Tōkyō days, and my impression was more of a saint than a poet.

Hong Ki-mun
I also saw him when I was in China. He received a great welcome in Tōkyō, but in China, he faced opposition. Perhaps his mystical thought clashed with the progressive tendencies of the Chinese intellectual world at that time. (Continued on page 234).<236> (Continued from page 236) So, he was rejected by university students in places like Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou and was driven away. I watched from afar as he continued his speech amidst the students' jeers ("yaji") in the auditorium of Peking University. Perhaps because I had seen the flyers (삐라) with anti-Tagore statements spread throughout the country, I didn't feel any great emotion from the man himself, other than the thought that he was an old poet from afar.<234>

Austrian Biologist

Sŏ Hang-sŏk
I haven't really met any famous people from overseas. If any, they were scholars I occasionally saw during my student days. Once, when a biologist from Vienna University in Austria (I can't recall his name now) came to Tōkyō, I met him once. Other than that, none from the literary side.

Brazilian Poet Mr. Bopp

Chŏng In-sŏp
Years ago, when I went to Tōkyō, I met the South American Brazilian poet Mr. Bopp, who was serving as Consul General in Kōbe. He had such an excellent memory; he could recite Milton's "Paradise Lost" by heart. His achievements hold a significant place in the history of Brazilian literature; he mainly wrote epic-like poems based on the legends and myths flowing through the Amazon and Cobra River (고부라江) basins. He seemed like a very contemplative and meditative poet.

British Poet Mr. Blunden

Chŏng In-sŏp
Also, when I went to Tōkyō, I met the British poet Mr. Edmund Blunden. I heard his speech at the Tōkyō University of Literature and Science auditorium. And I also met the famous Chinese great scholar Mr. Ku Hung-ming (고鴻銘氏), and at that gathering, I also met the Greek female writer Ms. Metaxa (메탁사氏). I didn't have any particularly deep impressions, but they were people who gave a refined and gentle feeling, not coarse.<240>

**Summary**

Several Korean intellectuals share brief anecdotes about meeting famous international figures, mostly in Tōkyō during the 1930s. They mention encounters with Yokomitsu Riichi, Albert Einstein, Vasily Eroshenko, Rabindranath Tagore, an unnamed Austrian biologist, Brazilian poet Raul Bopp, British poet Edmund Blunden, Chinese scholar Ku Hung-ming, and Greek writer Alki Metaxa. The impressions range from non-existent (Yu Chin-o) to deeply positive (Kim Pok-chin on Eroshenko, Kim Sang-yong on Tagore) or critical (Hong Ki-mun on Tagore's reception in China). The article showcases the contributors' awareness of and engagement with global intellectual and literary circles, often experienced through the colonial metropole, Tōkyō. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it reveals the mobility of Korean intellectuals within the Japanese empire and East Asia (Tōkyō, China), their participation in international cultural events, and their varied perspectives on globally renowned figures like Tagore, reflecting both personal admiration and an awareness of broader political contexts (like Tagore's reception in China).

文豪를 만난 印象
東京文壇의 數三人을

兪鎭午
해외작가는 맛난 일이 업스니 會見印象도 업슴니다. 東京의 作家를 數三氏(橫光氏 등) 맛난 일이 잇스나 별다른 인상이 업습니다.

에례시뻬코 아인슈타인博士

金復鎭
나는 年前 東京 잇슬 때 「아인슈타인」과 露西亞의 시인 에레시뺑크氏를 맛낫지요. 아인슈타인은 公會堂에서 講演하는 것을 멀리 안저서 보앗기에 그 분의 인상을 어떠타고 말하기는 곤란하군요.
그러나 에레시뺑코氏는 神田에 잇는 明治會館에서 무슨 강연이 잇섯는데 그곳에서 하룻밤 갓치 강연도 구경하고 그날밤 오래도록 서로 이야기도 해 보앗지요. 그런데 사람이 퍽으나 언어나 행동 모든 것이 시인답게 뵈이드군요. 아마 내가 본 외국 사람 중에서는 가장 첫 인강이 좃케 뵈입데다.<231>

印度詩聖 타-골

金尙鎔
나는 印度詩聖 타-골翁을 맛나 보앗지요. 그것이 바로 震災 후였는데 단 한 번 뿐이엇지만 퍽으나 인상이 깁드군요. 학생시대에 그의 「新月」 「끼탄자리」 등 夢幻的, 神秘的 詩歌를 耽讀하엿드니 만치 東京帝大 초청으로 강연회가 열니자 달녀갓지요. 一見에 그분은 文豪갓해요. 聖人이나 豫言者를 연상하게 하는 白髮童顔과 그 風采며 말소리... 말소리도 고요한 가운데 鍾인 듯 울니지요. 4, 500명 모힌 청중이 다 들니도록 쟁쟁하게 울려나오드군요.

孫晋泰
나도 東京시대에 早大講堂에서 말하는 양을 보앗는데 詩人이라기 보다 聖者라고 印象되어지드군요.

洪起文
나도 中國 잇슬 때 보앗는데 東京서는 퍽으나 환영을 밧엇지만 中國서는 반대를 밧엇지요. 아마 그 神秘主義的 思想에 急進傾向이 잇는 그 당시 中國思想界와 충돌하엿든 게지요. (234項에 續).<236> (236項에서 계속) 그래서 上海, 南京, 杭州 등지의 대학생들에게 배격을 당하여 쪼기어 갓섯는데 나는 北京대학 강당에서 학생들의 「야지」속에 연설을 계속하고 이슨 것을 먼-발치에서 처다보앗지요. 전국에 펴진 타-골반대 성명의 삐라를 보고난 뒤인지 그저 遠來의 老시인이라는 생각 이외에 별 큰 감격을 그 인물에서 밧지 못햇서요. <234>

墺太利의 生物學者

徐恒錫
나는 별로히 해외의 유명한 사람이라고는 맛나 본 일이 업서요. 혹 잇다면 在學時 간혹 보든 학자들이지요. 한번은 墺太利의 위-ㄴ大學의 생물학자(지금 그 분의 일흠은 기억하지 못하겟스나)가 東京에 왓슬 때 한번 만나본 일이 잇지요. 그 박게 文人측으로는 전혀 업서요.

뿌라질 詩人 뽑푸氏

鄭寅燮
나는 年前 東京 갓슬 때 神戶總領事로 잇든 南米 뿌라질 詩人 뽑푸(Bopp)씨를 맛낫지요. 엇더케 聰力이 조흔 사람인지 밀톤의 「失樂園」을 암송하고 잇서요. 그분의 功績은 뿌라질 文學史上에 큰 지위를 가지고 잇는데 주로 아마존과 고부라江 유역에 흘너 잇는 전설과 신화를 基調로 敍事詩가튼 것을 만히 씨섯지오. 퍽으나 沈爭하고 冥想的 氣風잇는 詩人이드구만요.

英國詩人 불란든氏

鄭寅燮
또 東京 갓슬 때 英國詩人 엣먼드 불란든(EDmund Blunden)氏를 맛낫지오. 東京文理科大學講堂에서 하는 연설도 듯고 그리고 中國의 유명한 碩儒 고鴻銘氏도 맛낫고 그때 會席에서 希臘 女流作家 메탁사(Metaza)氏도 맛낫는데 별로 깁흔 인상은 업섯스나 粗野하지 안코 端麗하고 부드러운 感觸을 주는 분들입데다. <240>
