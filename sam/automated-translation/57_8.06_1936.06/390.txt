**Body Text Translation:**

Jokes

A Clever Idea

"There isn't a single 'closet' (벽장) in this house? What kind of way is this to build a house!"
Said a guest visiting a friend's newly built house for the first time.
"Yes, that's right... But this is also the result born from my painful experience. In my previous house, my wife would often invite other men over, and when she sensed I was returning, she would hide them in that 'closet'..."

Going to Heaven

Captain: "Everyone! There's no hope now! The boat is about to sink immediately! Is there anyone among you who is good at praying <193>?"
Steward (Triumphantly): "I am excellent at it!"
Captain: "Is that so? Then you pray well! Everyone else, tie a rubber belt around your waist and jump into the sea! We happen to be short exactly one!"

The Maid's Art of Economy

Mistress: "The first condition for a maid who wants to run errands at our house is that she must be an economist (經濟家)..."
Maid: "Oh my, Ma'am! At my previous house, I was kicked out because of that?"
Mistress: "Why? Why would an economist get kicked out?"
Maid: "Well, Ma'am, because I was always thinking about economy, I didn't wear my own clothes and always borrowed the mistress's clothes..." <194>

No Exceptions

"Doctor, you sometimes advise patients that they absolutely must not have children, right?"
Doctor: "Oh, certainly, plenty of times. I always say things like that to unmarried patients."

It Can't Be Helped, It's Heredity!

She: "Sometimes you seem quite masculine, but looking at you another way, it seems you also possess quite a lot of femininity! Ho ho."
He: "Well, that's due to heredity (遺傳). As you well know, half of my ancestors (先祖) were male, and the other half were female. Haha..."

For Practical Use!

"I am a famous magician. First, <195> I grab a goblet, pour water into it, and suddenly, by a wondrous method, that water changes into 'whiskey'."
"Haha! Is that so? Then, you must come to our house party tonight."
"Sure, to entertain the guests?"
"N-no, to entrust you with the job of making the drinks!"

Teeth That Have Come In

Dentist: "...This concludes the lecture (講議) on the development of teeth (齒). Now, let's ask you all some questions. Miss Hyeko (惠子), which teeth were the very last ones to come in?"
Hyeko (惠子): "The teeth that have come in (해너흔 니)!"
(Translator's Note: This is a pun. 해넣은 이 literally means 'teeth put in' or 'teeth that have emerged/come in', but it sounds like 해 넣은 이 meaning 'dentures' or 'artificial teeth' in this context, contrasting with the lecture about natural tooth development.)

Latest Fashion

"These shoes (靴) don't fit me well. They need to be a bit wider in the 'ball' (幅). <196>"
"But, this year's latest fashion (最新流行) is narrow in the ball (幅) and long; that's what's in style..."
"Really!? But look here, my feet (足) are still the same as last year!"

Revenge

"Just how many times do I have to tell you? When do you intend to return the money you borrowed? This is already the sixth time-"
"But, do you know how many times I begged and pleaded when I borrowed the money from you?" <197>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of eight short jokes or humorous anecdotes covering various everyday situations: home design influenced by marital suspicion, a cynical take on saving lives during a shipwreck, a misunderstanding about thriftiness, a doctor's specific advice, a play on heredity, a magician hired for practical skills, a pun during a dental lecture, outdated fashion, and delayed debt repayment. From a historical perspective, these jokes, likely adapted from or inspired by Western humor magazines common at the time, reflect the modernization and cultural shifts occurring in 1930s Korea. They showcase interactions related to new housing styles ('closets'), modern professions (ship captain, steward, doctor, dentist), social events (parties), consumer goods (shoes, fashion), and even abstract concepts like heredity, presented in a lighthearted, accessible format for a magazine audience. The use of older Korean spellings and Hanja mixed with Hangul is also characteristic of the period.

笑話
名考案

「이집에는 「벽장」이 하낫도 업군요? 이런 집을 짓는 법이 어데 잇단 말이요!」
하고 새로 지은 어느 동무의 집을 처음 차저간 손님의 말에
「네 그럿슴니다...그러치만 이것도 나의 쓰라린 經驗이 맨드러낸 結果이지요. 이전 집에서는 나의 안해가 종종 딴 남자를 불여드렷다가는 내가 돌어가는 눈치가 나면 그 「벽장」 속에다 감추는구려...」

天國行

船長 「여러분! 인제 아무 가망이 업서요 뽀-드는 금방 까라안즐 것이야요! 이 가운데 누구든지 祈禱를<193> 잘 하는 이는 업는지요?」
事務長(意氣揚揚해서) 「내가 훌륭하게 잘 하지요」
船長 「그런가. 그러면 당신 잘 祈禱를 드리시요! 다른 사람들은 모다 고무전대를 허리에 띄고 바다로 뛰여드시요! 마츰 한 개가 모자라는 판인데!」

下女의 經濟術

主婦 「우리집에서 신부름 할야는 下女는 第一條件으로 經濟家일 것...」
下女 「참 그런데 마님! 저는 전에 잇는 집에서는 그것 때문에 쪼겨낫는데요?」
主婦 「웨? 經濟家가 웨 쪼겻난단 말이야?」
下女 「그러치만, 마님 저는 늘 經濟를 생각햇든 까닭에 自己의 옷은 안 입고 그 댁 마님의 옷만 늘 빌여 입엇섯지요...」 <194>

例外는 업지요

「선생님, 선생은 때때로 患者에게 대해서 결단코 자식을 만들어서는 안된다고 忠告하시는 일이 잇스시겟지요?」
醫師 「암 그야 얼마든지 잇지요. 나는 未婚者인 患者들에게는 으레히 그런 말들을 해주지요」

할 수 업는 遺傳인걸요!

彼女 「때로는 퍽으나 男性的인 분으로도 뵈이지마는 당신은 또 어찌보면 무던히 女性的인 데도 다분히 가지고 잇는 듯 해요! 호호」
彼氏 「그야 遺傳의 탓이지요, 당신도 잘 아시겟지마는 우리 선조(先祖)는 절반은 男性이고, 그 다음 절반은 女性인 탓이지요. 하하...」

實用에 쓸야고!

「나는 유명한 요술쟁이지요, 위선<195> 곱부를 쥐고, 거기다 물을 부으면 별안간에 그 물이 「휘-스키」로 변하는 기묘한 방법이 잇지요」
「하하! 그런가. 그러면 자네 오늘밤 우리집 宴會에 꼭 와주게」
「그러지, 손님들을 위로식힐야고?」
「아-니, 술 맨드는 일을 맷길야고!」

해너흔 나(齒)

齒科醫 「...以上으로써 니(齒)의 발생에 관한 강의(講議)는 끝낫슴니다. 그러면 이제부터 좀 여러분한테 물어봅시다. 惠子씨, 그 중 맨 나종에 나오는 니(齒)는 어느 니엿든가요?」
惠子 「해너흔 니(齒)지요」

最新流行

「이 구두(靴)는 내게 잘 맛지가 안는군요, 좀 더 볼(幅」)이 넓어야<196> 하겟서요」
그러치만, 금년의 최신류행(最新流行)은 볼(幅)이 좁고서 기* 것이 류행이람니다...」
「그래요!? 그러나 여보세요. 내 발(足)은 작년의 그냥 그대로인걸요!」

復讐

「대체 멧 번이나 말을 해야. 취해간 돈을 줄 작정인가? 이번까지 벌서 여섯번째네-」
「그러치만, 내가 자네한테서 돈을 취한 때는 멧 번이나 빌구 사정햇는지 아는가?」<197>
