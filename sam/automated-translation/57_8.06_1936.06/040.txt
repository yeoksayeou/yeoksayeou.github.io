**Body Text Translation:**
Stories of Mishaps During Travel

An Incident in San Francisco (桑巷)

Chŏng In'gwa (鄭仁果)

This happened in San Francisco. I was heading to the station carrying two large handbags when, along the way, the handle of one bag broke off. I couldn't carry one and sling the other over my shoulder, so I was hesitating when an expressman (運搬人) ran up and offered to take the bags to the station. I entrusted them to him. When we arrived at the station, he demanded a fare of about 5 *wŏn* when only about 50 *chŏn* would have been sufficient. It was a real problem. In broad daylight at the station, he grabbed me and made a fuss; where could there be a more pitiful and awkward situation? After thinking it over, I decided to drag him to the nearest police substation (警察官 派出所). However, this expressman's attire (服裝) was similar to a police officer's uniform (警官의 服裝), so at first glance (*번 보아서는), it was hard to tell the difference (區別). Therefore, on the street we were walking, another expressman dressed similarly pretended to be a police officer, stubbornly insisting and ordering (呼令) me to pay the unfair amount (抑鬱한 金額). Looking closely (* 처다보*), I barely realized he wasn't a police officer and dragged the first man to the substation. I explained the facts, and they reprimanded the fellow and sent me safely on my way. Only then did the cold sweat (식을 땀) that had beaded on my forehead flow. I really had a hard time! <33>

An Incident Suffered at Port Said, Egypt

Chŏng In'gwa (鄭仁果)

Another time, something like this happened. It was when I disembarked at the port (港口) of Port Said in Egypt (埃及). At that time, I was traveling under the guidance (案內) of the British-run Thomas Cook Company (英國사람이 經營하는 『토마스쿡會社』). As soon as I landed at the port, a man wearing the clothes of a Thomas Cook Company workman (人夫) appeared, carried my luggage, and then demanded payment (賃金) right there on the street (길거리). A Thomas Cook Company workman, as a rule (原則), wouldn't ask for money midway but would go to the inn (려관) where the owner of the goods was staying and receive the money in a gentlemanly (紳士的) manner. Seeing him demand money midway, I realized he wasn't a Thomas Cook Company workman and reprimanded (質責) him. He then confessed (告白) the truth: he wasn't a Thomas Cook Company workman, but since he had carried my luggage, I should pay him the fare (삭전). He demanded an excessively unfair amount of money, making it quite a difficult (困難) situation. While I was being berated (詰難) in Arabic (아라비야 말), fortunately, a Syrian man I had befriended on the ship appeared. Dressed up from somewhere in a Turkish hat (土耳其帽子) and wielding a large club (몽둥이), he cursed furiously. The man seemed frightened by the hat and club and simply ran away. Afterwards, I asked the Syrian man about it. He explained that because Egypt had been subjugated (먹키워) by Turkey (土耳其) for a long time, Egyptians are still afraid even at the sight of a Turkish man's hat and cane (집팽이).
Some days after that, having finished my travels and preparing to leave Egypt, I went to the station to board a train. Coincidentally, I ran into the same man from whom I had suffered the verbal abuse (힐難) days before. I was quite alarmed (황겁한). I was berated again for some time. Just then, a British gentleman (英國사람 紳士) standing nearby stepped forward and cursed (육을 하니) at the man, who then meekly ran away. It seems that these days, they are unconditionally afraid (닷자곳자로 무서워하는) if someone is British. <35>

On the Way to Toronto, Canada

Yi Tae'wi (李大偉)

Some years ago (年前), when I was in the United States (米國), I set off with several famous American Christian scholars (米國의 유명한 基督敎學者들) to attend the World Congress of Christians (世界基督敎人大會) being held in Toronto, Canada (北米 카나다 토론토 市). When the train arrived (當着) at the US-Canada border (國境), it was the middle of the night, and most passengers were asleep. Suddenly (난대업시), a police officer (警官) rushed in, woke me up, and asked if I had a travel permit (旅行券). At that time (當時), I had a US travel permit but not a Canadian one, which put me in a very difficult situation (困難한 境遇). I explained my circumstances (事情) and that my purpose (目的次) was to attend the conference, but he wouldn't listen (듯지를 안치요). It seemed I would have to get off the train, but he said it would be alright if there was something that could vouch (保證) for me. I spoke to the American Christian scholars sleeping in the next compartment (엽방), and they kindly vouched (保障) for me, allowing me to overcome (突破) the difficulty (難關). <37>

**Summary**

This article recounts three separate travel mishaps experienced by Koreans abroad in the 1930s. Chŏng In'gwa describes being nearly cheated by an expressman in San Francisco, who was only deterred by involving the police. He also details two incidents in Port Said, Egypt: being extorted by a man impersonating a Thomas Cook employee, who fled when confronted by a Syrian friend leveraging the local fear of Turks, and later being harassed by the same man, who was scared off by a British gentleman. Yi Tae'wi narrates being detained at the US-Canada border for lacking a Canadian travel permit while en route to a conference in Toronto, only managing to proceed after American colleagues vouched for him. These anecdotes highlight the vulnerabilities of Korean travelers during the colonial period, navigating unfamiliar foreign systems and relying on intermediaries or the perceived authority of other nationalities (Americans, Turks via Syrians, British) to resolve conflicts or difficulties. Historians might find interest in the portrayal of international travel by Koreans, the specific challenges faced (scams, bureaucratic hurdles), and the implicit commentary on global power dynamics and prejudices as perceived through Korean eyes at the time (e.g., Egyptian fear of Turks and deference to the British).

旅行中 失敗하든 이야기
桑巷에서 생긴 일

鄭仁果

「쌘푸랜씨스코」 에서 생긴 일인데 停車場에까지 나갈야고 커다란 손가방 두 개를 쥐고 나가다가 도중에서 가방 한 개가 손잽이가 떨어저서 한 개를 쥐고 한 개를 메고 갈 수도 업고 해서 망서리고 잇노라니 「엑스푸레스·맨」 (運搬人)이 달여오드니 停車場까지 가방을 가저다 주겟다고 하기에 그냥 맷겻더니 停車場에 와서 賃金을 請求하는데 50錢가량이면 족한 것을 5圓 가량이나 달나고 하지요. 참 큰일 낫지요. 白晝 停車場에서 사람을 붓잡고 야료를 하니 이런 딱하고 거북스러울 데가 어데 잇겟서요. 그래서 생각다 못해 그놈을 끄을고 갓가운 警察官 派出所로 가자고 햇지요. 그런데 이 「엑스푸레스·맨」의 服裝이 警官의 服裝과 비슷해서 *번 보아서는 얼는 區別하기 어렵드군요. 그럼으로 가든 길가에서 갓흔 「엑스푸레스·맨」이 경관인체 하고 막 울뜨며 내게 억울한 金額을 支拂하두록 呼令을 하드군요. 착실이 처다보* 경관이 아님을 겨우 알어차리고 派出所까지 끄을고 가서 이러이러한 사실을 이야기하엿드니 그 놈을 책하고 무사히 돌여보내드군요. 그때에야 이마에서 식을 땀이 흐흡데다. 혼낫지요.<33>

埃及 포싸이드港에서 당하 일

鄭仁果

또 한번은 이런 일이 잇섯지요. 埃及 포싸이드港口에 내렷슬 때의 일인데 그때 나는 英國사람이 經營하는 『토마스쿡會社』의 案內로 旅行하든 때인데 港口에 내리자 토마스·쿡會社의 人夫가 입는 옷을 입은 사람이 나타나서 내 짐을 운반하여 주고는 길거리에서 賃金을 請求합데다. 토마스·쿡會社의 人夫라면 도중에서 돈을 청하지 안코 그 물건 주인이 머물너 잇는 려관에 가서 紳士的으로 돈을 바더 가는 것이 原則인데 도중에서 돈을 청구하는 것을 보니 토마스쿡회사의 人夫가 아닌 것을 알고 質責하니 사실대로 告白하기를 토마스·쿡會社의 人夫가 아니나 당신의 짐을 운반하엿으니 삭전을 내라는 것이외다. 넘우나 억울하게 만흔 돈을 요구함으로 이때에 여간 곤난한 경우가 아니엿섯지요. 한참 아라비야 말로 힐난을 당하는 판에 마츰 배 안에서부터 친하엿든 시리야 사람 하나이 나타나드니 어데가서 차려입엇는지 土耳其帽子에다 커다란 몽둥이를 휘둘느며 막우 욕살질을 하 그 帽子와 몽둥이에 무서워서 그냥 도망치드군요. 그런 뒤에 그 시리야 사람에게 물엇드니 埃及은 土耳其에게 오랜 동안, 먹키워 잇섯든 관게로 土耳其사람의 帽子와 집팽이만 보아도 埃及사람들은 아직도 무서워한다는 것이외다.
그런지 멧츨이 지난 다음 旅行을 끗맛추고 埃及을 떠나랴고 汽車를 탈야든 때 停車場에 나갓다가 공교롭게도 또 전날 힐난을 당하든 그 놈을 맛낫섯지요. 여간 황겁한 것이 아니드군요. 또 한참 힐난을 당하엿지요. 이럴 때 마츰 겻 잇든 英國사람 紳士 하나이 나스면서 육을 하니 그 놈은 피실피실 도망을 가드군요. 오늘날은 英國사람이라면 닷자곳자로 무서워하는 모양입데다.<35>

加奈陀 토론토로 가든 길에

李大偉

나는 年前 米國에 잇슬 때 米國의 유명한 基督敎學者들 멧 분과 함께 北米 카나다 토론토 市에서 열니는 世界基督敎人大會에 出席할 양으로 떠낫섯지요. 米國과 카나다와의 國境에 汽車가 當着하엿슬 때는 마츰 밤중인데 손들은 거개다 잠을 자고 잇는 판인데 난대업시 警官이 달여오드니 잠자는 나를 일으키며 旅行券을 가젓느냐고 뭇기에 나는 當時 米國의 旅行券은 잇서도 카나다의 旅行券은 못가젓든 때인대 일이 대단 困難한 境遇엿지요. 여러가지로 事情과 大會出席目的次로 간다는 것을 잘 말하엿스나 듯지를 안치요. 하는 수 업시 車에서 내리게 될 판이대 어떠케 당신을 保證할 만한 무엇이 잇스면 된다고 하기에 엽방에 가치 자든 米國基督敎學者들에게 말하엿드니 그분들이 잘 保障하여 주워서 겨우 難關을 突破하엿서요.<37>
