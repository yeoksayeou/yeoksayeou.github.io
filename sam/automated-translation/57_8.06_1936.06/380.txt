Talk Room (談話室)

Mr. Min Tae-sik and Sweets (菓子)

Mr. Min Tae-sik (閔大植), who received the foremost affection of the late multi-millionaire Min Yŏng-hwi (閔泳徽) and ranked first among all sons and nephews in the distribution of his assets! He may not be the wealthiest man in Chosŏn, but he would surely fly into a rage if someone didn't offer him a top-tier position. On top of that, his name resounds throughout the land of three thousand *ri* as the president (頭取) of Tongil Bank (東一銀行), a king of finance! Furthermore, within the Min chaebol (閔氏財閥), Mr. Min Tae-sik is an embodiment of power that even Hitler or Mussolini couldn't match! This man is also distinguished for how diligently frugal and thrifty he usually is. He always adheres to his lifelong "motto" (「못도」) even within his own household. According to what I heard recently, at a certain bar (某빨에) (following three lines omitted - original text) ... lately, he finally built a "sweet home" (「스윗홈」) in a certain suburb, spending every day and night there reading, strolling, and planning business. One time, a visitor came and went.
"Darling, why did you put out three sweets (菓子)? Since it's just me and one guest, two would have been appropriate."
"Still, how could I put out exactly two? 'Anata no kechippuri hidoi wa.' 'Kondo dake wa shikata nai wa yo.' (「아나다노게찌부리히도이와」 「곤도다게와시가다나이와요」... 'Your stinginess is terrible.' 'Just this once, it can't be helped.')"... Such was the reply of the Cherry Blossom Beauty (櫻花美人)... To all those in the world who indulge in luxury and extravagance, how about this example of thrift and planning? Isn't it a fine example, a sharp rebuke (頂門一針)?...

Ford and Mr. Sin Sŏk-u

Mr. Sin Sŏk-u (申錫雨), a truly magnanimous man who unsparingly threw vast amounts of his private wealth into Chosŏn's cultural endeavors, newspaper businesses, the New Korea Society (新幹會), and others, wishing only for their growth without regret. Lately, he has withdrawn from all those businesses. Perhaps as preliminary preparation for realizing grand future ambitions, he entrusted the management of Kyŏngsu Automobile (京水自働車) to his younger brother Sin Yong-u (申用雨). Since the end of last year, he frequently visited the Kangwŏn Province (江原道) area, and recently, word has it that he has grasped the freight truck transport rights for the entire Kangwŏn area in one hand and has already started operations from a temporary office in Kimhwa (金化). Furthermore, this "truck" (「추럭」) operating right is said not to be limited to one region but covers the entire Kangwŏn Province. <194> If that's the case, once full operations begin, there will be hundreds of main and branch lines running vertically and horizontally. The day is not far off when he will hold the freight transport of the entire Kangwŏn area in his hand, able to grasp and release it at will. What sharp wit (嶮口).
"Hey, America's Ford became a tycoon by manufacturing automobiles, and you'll become a tycoon with automobiles built by Ford. Shall we call you the second-generation Ford?..."
Mr. Sin, smiling broadly, said,
"What kind of sharp wit is that? If I become the second-generation Ford, then you'd be the third-generation type, haha..."
Anyway, Sir! Whether you are the first or third-generation Ford, please focus on securing the funds for your future soaring contributions to Chosŏn's cultural endeavors and all other businesses...

Famous Actor Sim Yŏng and "Kutsu-naoshi" (Shoe Shining)

Although Sim Yŏng (沈影君) is now a famous actor and handsome man of the era, receiving the adoration of young women while making audiences of thousands laugh and cry heartily on the splendid stage, looking back on his past reveals many crossings of thorny patches filled with desperate tragedy (悲絶慘絶).
One story to recount here: Sim Yŏng's upbringing was in a family that was not very well-off (餘裕). Harboring the commendable ambition to study, he went across to Tōkyō, but his day-to-day life was uncertain (漠然). Having thought it over and finding no other way, he inevitably started working as a shoe shiner (구쯔나오시 - kutsu-naoshi) right in the middle of a Great Tōkyō intersection, experiencing all sorts of hardships (苦生).
Unable to forget those past days, it's surprising that his "great business" (「大事業」) now involves operating a shoe shine business (靴磨營業) in a corner of the entrance hall of the Hanch'ŏng Building (韓靑삘딍) on Chongno (鍾路), the most prosperous intersection in Seoul (長安). But it's even more surprising that the initial motivation for this arose from such circumstances.

Yu Kwang-nyŏl Receiving Mistaken Greetings

Although Mr. Yu Kwang-nyŏl (柳光烈氏) and Mr. No Cha-yŏng (盧子泳氏) are complete strangers, separated by a thousand *ri*, they often cause confusion because they both have long rectangular faces and slightly dark complexions, making them look almost like brothers. Consequently, when Mr. Yu Kwang-nyŏl travels to the provinces, people frequently offer him utterly nonsensical greetings like, "Welcome, Teacher No (盧先生)!" One day, he came to the Samch'ŏlli office (三千里社) and explained the situation as described above, saying, "Both Mr. No and I wander around (쏘단이는) similarly, and our faces are alike, but registering the fact that we are actually different people is pointless. Yet, I can't very well walk around (단일 수도) with a sign on my back saying 'I am not Mr. No.' It's truly awkward (딱하다고)," he remarked.

Reason for the Change in Mr. Pak P'ar-yang's Pen Name <195>

Poet Pak P'ar-yang (朴八陽氏) used to have the pen name (雅號) Kim Yŏsu (金麗水). Recently, however, he has been signing the poems he occasionally publishes simply as Yŏsu (麗水), dropping the character Kim (金). Sensing there might be a reason, someone close to him asked about it. Mr. Pak replied, saying it happened quite some time ago when he went to Kaesŏng (開城) for a literary lecture. At that time, the organizers submitted his name as Pak P'ar-yang but wrote Kim Yŏsu on the lecture program (푸르그람). The attending police officer (臨席警官) questioned why Kim Yŏsu was lecturing when the notification said Pak P'ar-yang. It became a hassle (말성거리가 되여), nearly preventing the lecture. After forcefully explaining (力說) that Pak P'ar-yang was indeed Kim Yŏsu and even checking with the Chongno Police Station (鍾路署), the lecture fortunately proceeded without incident, but it caused considerable trouble (싯그러움을 당하엿고). Furthermore, literary youths from the countryside often come up to Seoul looking for him and inquire after the well-being of "Poet Kim Yŏsu." Finding this utterly baffling (어이 업서서), Mr. Pak would smoothly talk his way out of it (슬슬 넨기고 말엇다는 바) with impromptu eloquence (臨時雄辯). Due to these various inconveniences, although he regretted giving it up, he felt compelled (不得己) to drop the "Kim" character for a while, intending to reattach it when the opportunity arises. When he does reattach the Kim, he's thinking of applying for pen name registration (雅號登記申請) at the courthouse (裁判所), but if such a procedure isn't legally possible, he apparently plans to take out a three-column or larger pen name advertisement (雅號廣告) in the newspaper.

Is This the Breakdown of a 70-Year Life?

According to statistics recently published in Nice (니-즈), France (佛蘭西), out of a 70-year life, one-third, or 23 years, is spent sleeping, and 9 years are spent on desire (愛慾) and love. The breakdown of 70 years is as follows:
Sleep time... 23 years
Talk time... 13 years
Eating time... 6 years
Desire time... 9 years
Socializing, Entertainment time... 14 years
Grooming time... 5 years

How Much Do Hollywood (聖林) Actors Earn?

Who had the highest income in Hollywood (허리웃드) last year? According to a report based on the assessment of the American (아메리카) Tax Agency (稅務省), Mae West (메-·웨스트) of Paramount (파라마운트) was at the top with $339,166 as her income for one year. Next was Bing Crosby (빙그·크로스피-) with $191,000, United Artists (유나이뎃트·아듸스트) Vice President Ar Laidŭman (아-ㄹ·라이드만 - likely Earl Carroll or similar) with $161,089, and Chaplin (촤푸링) with $143,000. Some might think, "It's not particularly impressive, is it?" (「모다 별로히 신통할 것이 업지 안흔가」) But no matter how much one stars in films costing $800,000 to $1,000,000 per picture, salaries don't just pour <196> in so easily.
Mae West's $339,000, when converted to Won, amounts to about 1.2 to 1.3 million Won (百二三十萬圓), which is undeniably astonishing.
The famous newspaper king Hearst (하-스트) reportedly has an annual income of $500,000. He distributes "Cosmopolitan Pictures" (「코스모포리탄 映畵」) through Warner (와-나-) and "Hearst Metrotone News" (「하-스트·메도로돈·뉴-쓰」) also through Warner.
Marion Davies (마리온데·븨스), who is personally associated with him, receives an annual income of $104,000 as the president of Cosmopolitan (「코스모포리탄」).
So, how much do the actors below Mae West and Crosby, as well as the heads of film production, receive per year? Let's list them here. (By the way, Mae West's earnings became exceptionally high because she received both writing fees (原作料) and performance fees (出演料)).

"Great Tōkyō" Seen Through Its Dance Halls (「땐스홀」)

How many dance halls (「땐스홀」) are there in Great Kyŏngsŏng (大京城 - referring to Tokyo here, not Seoul) with its population of 3 million (人口 300萬)? This is also one issue worth looking into.

8 locations within the city.
8 locations in the suburbs.
Among them, there is a distinction between first-class (一流), second-class (二流), and third-class (三流). Let's examine the 8 city dance halls one by one.
First-class are:
Teito Butōjō (帝都舞踏場) (Location: Shinjuku 新宿)
Florida Dance Hall (푸로리다땐스홀) (Location: Tameike 溜池)
Shinbashi Dance Hall (新橋땐스홀) (Location: Shinbashi 新橋)
These are the 3 locations.
Second-class are:
Izumibashi Dance Hall (和泉橋땐스홀) (Location: Izumibashi 和泉橋)
Union Dance Hall (유나온땐스홀) (Location: Hirokōji 寬町 - likely Ueno-Hirokōji)
Nichibei Dance Hall (日米땐스홀) (Location: Kyōbashi 京橋)
These are the 3 locations.
Third-class are:
Kokka Dance Hall (國華땐스홀) (Location: Kyōbashi 京橋)
Ginza Dance Hall (銀座땐스홀) (Location: Ginza 銀座)
These are the 2 locations.
So, what distinguishes first, second, and third class? They can be divided by:
First, interior facilities (場內設備)
Second, number of dancers (땐사-數)
Third, fees (料金), etc.
Speaking of first-class, the number of dancers ranges from over 150 to nearly 300, the facilities are splendid, and the fee is 25 sen or 22 sen per hour (nighttime 夜間). Second-class halls have around 100 dancers, facilities less complete than first-class, and fees are around 20 sen per hour (nighttime). Third-class halls are the most populist (大衆的); the facilities are the least complete, and there are only about 40-50 dancers, but because the fee is 15 sen per hour (nighttime), they are used by the largest number of people.
Also, the daytime fee (晝間料金) is half the nighttime fee (夜間料金). Dance hall hours are generally from 3 PM to 6 PM for daytime and from 6 PM to 11 PM sharp for nighttime. Due to concerns about public morals (風紀紊亂上), permits for dance halls are strictly granted only for buildings of three stories or more. To operate one dance hall requires a minimum investment (最小限度) of 100,000 Yen (10萬圓).
One more thing to mention here (여긔에서) is that the second-class Union Dance Hall (유니온땐스홀) is financed by Pak Ch'un'gŭm (朴春琴), and the famous third-class Ginza Dance Hall (銀座땐스홀) is managed by Yi Tong-hwa (李東華).
Suburban halls include:
Kawasaki Dance Hall (川崎땐스홀)
Kawaguchi Dance Hall (川口땐스홀)
Tōyoko Dance Hall (東橫땐스홀)
Kagetsu Dance Hall (花月땐스홀)
Shangrila Dance Hall (샨구렐땐스홀)
Urawa Dance Hall (浦和땐스홀) (2 locations)
Chiba Dance Hall (千葉땐스홀), etc.
So, how many people use these 16 dance halls encompassing Great Kyŏngsŏng (Tokyo)? According to the rough estimate (槪算) of a certain informed source (某消息通), at least 300,000 Tōkyō citizens use them.
What an astonishing phenomenon this is! <198>

**Summary**

This "Talk Room" article from the June 1936 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* presents a series of anecdotes and observations about prominent figures and cultural trends. It touches upon the wealth and notable frugality of financier Min Tae-sik, the entrepreneurial shift of Sin Sŏk-u into the transportation business in Kangwŏn Province, the humble past of famous actor Sim Yŏng as a shoe-shiner in Tokyo, the humorous case of mistaken identity involving Yu Kwang-nyŏl and No Cha-yŏng, poet Pak P'ar-yang's reasons for altering his pen name, statistics on life allocation, a detailed list of Hollywood celebrity salaries, and an extensive look at the thriving dance hall scene in Tokyo, including classifications, fees, locations, and notable Korean involvement in ownership/management. For historians, the article offers valuable glimpses into the lives and preoccupations of the Korean elite under Japanese rule, economic activities like banking and transportation, the cultural scene involving actors and writers, the pervasive influence of Japanese culture (language, entertainment like dance halls), and a surprising degree of awareness about global popular culture, specifically Hollywood finance. The mix of Korean and Japanese names and places, along with direct quotes in Japanese, also reflects the linguistic reality of the period.

閔大植氏와 菓子

千萬長者 故閔泳徽氏의 第一 寵愛를 밧고 그 財産分配에 잇서서 諸子侄 중에 首位에 參例하야 朝鮮의 甲富는 못되여도 一流의 자리를 내노으라면 怒發大發 하실 분, 거기다가 東一銀行이라는 金融 國王의 頭取로 일흠이 三千里 江山에 휘날이는 閔大植氏! 더구나 閔氏財閥에서는 히틀러, 뭇소리니도 당할 수 업슬 만한 權의 化身인 閔大植氏! 이분은 平時에 엇찌 勤儉節約을 하시는지 여기에도 一頭角을 내시는 분이다. 그 平生 「못도」를 自身의 家庭에서도 늘 직혀 나가시는데 近日 듯는 바에 의하면 某빨에 (以下三行略-원문) 요지음 드듸어 郊外 某處에다가 「스윗홈」을 만들러 노시고 每日每夜 거기서 지내시며 讀書와 散步와 事業計劃으로 지내시는데 어느 때 來客이 왓다가섯다.
「여보, 菓子는 웨 三세를 노아 내보냇소, 나하고 손님 한 분이면 두 개가 맛당한데」
「그래도, 어떠케 똑 누 개만 노아 내보낸단 말이요, 「아나다노게찌부리히도이와」 「곤도다게와시가다나이와요」... 櫻花美人의 所答如此...世上의 모든 豪華奢侈로 일삼는 분들 이 儉約과 規模에 이만하면 어떳소? 頂門一針의 조흔 標本이겟지?...

名優 沈影君과 「구쯔나오시」

지금은 華麗한 舞臺우에서 수천 수백 觀衆을 相對로 하두 잘 웃기고 하두 잘 울니기도 하여가면서 젋은 女子들의 戀慕를 한 몸에 훔벅 받고 잇는 當代의 名俳優요 美男인 沈影君도 지난날을 더듬어 보면 悲絶慘絶한 가시덤불 고비가 많엇든 것이다.
그 중에서 한 가지 여긔에 적으려 함은 沈影君의 자라나든 家庭은 그리 餘裕는 잇지 못하여 勉學의 갸륵한 뜻을 품고 東京에 건너 갓섯스나 그날그날의 生活이 漠然하여 생각다 못하여 하는 수 없이 大東京 네거 복판에서 구쯔나오시를 하기 始作하면서 가진 苦生을 다 격거 보앗다고.
그리든 지난 일을 이즐 수가 업서서, 지금 서울 長安에서 제일 繁昌한 鍾路 네거리에 잇는 韓靑삘딍 玄關 入口 한모퉁이에서 靴磨營業을 하게 된 것도 沈影君의 「大事業」이라면 놀나운 일이지만 그 動機도 맨처음은 이러한데서 생겨저 나온 것이라면 더욱 놀나운 일이다.

헛人事 밧는 柳光烈

柳光烈氏와 盧子泳氏는 相距千里나 되는 남남사이면서도 두 분이 다 얼굴이 長方形으로 생긴 것이라든가, 얏간 검스으름한 點이 비슷하야 맛치 兄弟間이나 되는 듯한 까닭에 柳光烈氏가 혹시 地方으로 가시는 때면 盧先生 잘 오셋슴니가 하는 千萬 뚱단지 갓흔 人事를 듸리는 분이 종종 잇는 바 一日은 三千里社에 오세서 以上과 갓흔 사정을 말삼하고 가로대 盧氏와 내나 다 갓지 쏘단이는 처지로서 얼굴은 비슷하나, 실지는 다른 사람이란 登記를 하엿대야 필요 업고 그럿타고 나는 盧氏 안이요 라는 表를 등에 써지고 단일 수도 업고 일은 참 딱하다고 하시드라고.

詩人 朴八陽氏는 金麗水라는 雅號를 가젓든 바 最近에는 氏가 각금 詩를 發表하든 署名을 麗水리고만 쓰고 金字는 떼여버리는 것이 무슨 까닭이 잇는 듯하야 氏와 친한 분이 氏의게 그 理由를 물은 즉 氏 答曰 벌서 오랜 일임니다만은 開城에 文藝講演을 갓든 일이 잇는바 그 때 主催者側에서 朴八陽이란 일흠으로 届出하고 講演 푸르그람에는 金麗水라고 썻든 바 臨席警官으로부터 웨 朴八陽이 講演한다고 届出하고 엉뚱한 金麗水가 講演하는냐고 말성거리가 되여 講演을 못할 번 하다가 朴八陽은 卽 金麗水라는 것을 力說하고 鍾路署에 照會까지 하야 다행 講演은 無事히 진행하엿스나 퍽 싯그러움을 당하엿고 또는 시골 문학청년들이 上京하여 氏를 찻고는 詩人 金麗水氏가 잘 잇슬가 하고 문안하는 분 만흔 바 氏는 하두 어이 업서서 어름어름 臨時雄辯으로 슬슬 넨기고 말엇다는 바 이런 여러 가지가 불편하여서 참아버리기는 앗가우나 不得己 한동안 「金字」만은 데여 두엇다가 期會 보와서 다시 붓치리라는 바 다시 金字를 붓치게 되는 때면 裁判所에서 雅號登記申請이라도 할 생각이라는 바 만약 法規上 이런 手續을 할 수 업게 된다면 新聞에 三段以上 雅號廣告라도 낼 모양이라고.

둘재 땐사-數
셋재 料金 등으로 나눌 수가 잇는데 一流로 말하면 땐사-의 數가 150명 이상 300명 가까히 되고 場內設備가 훌륭하며 料金은 1시간(夜間) 25錢 乃至 22錢式이며 二流는 땐사-數가 100명內外에 設備가 一流보다는 不完全하고 料金도 1시간(夜間) 20錢式의 程度이고 三流로 말하면 가장 大衆的이여서 設備도 가장 不完全하고 땐사-도 불과 4, 50명 박게 안되지마는 料金이 1시간(夜間) 15錢式임으로 가장 많은 사람이 使用하고 잇다.
또한 晝間料金은 夜間料金의 半額이며 「땐스홀」의 時間은 대개 午後 3시로부터 午後 6시까지가 晝間이고 오후 6시로부터 오후 11시 定刻이 夜間인데 「댄스홀」의 許可는 風紀紊亂上 반듯이 3층 이상의 건물에 許可한다 하는 것으로서 「땐스홀」 한 개를 經營하자면 最小限度로 10萬圓이란 金額이 必要하다는 것이다.
여긔에서 한마듸 더 말해둘 것은 二流의 「유니온땐스홀」은 朴春琴의 經濟이고 三流의 有名한 「銀座땐스홀」은 李東華의 經營이라는 점이다.
