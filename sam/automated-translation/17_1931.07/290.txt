交叉點 (Crossroads)
Starting this month, "Crossroads" (交叉點) has been newly established. This is a place that mediates questions you wish to ask various public figures in society. Whatever you wish to ask, please inquire through our company (本社), and our company will convey the letter (便紙) to the relevant individual (當該氏) and publish their response (回答) in this paper (本紙). Please use postcards (葉書) for submissions, one question per person.
- (Contributions Welcome) -

To Mr. Lee Kwang-soo (李光洙氏)

I hear you are planning to write a novel based on Admiral Yi Sun-sin (忠武公) for the Tong-A Ilbo. How do you intend to write it? That is, will you write it faithfully based on facts in a biographical style (傳記體)? Or will you create most of the material (題材) yourself?
Mokp'o, Kang Sa-sŏng (姜士成)

Biographical Style (傳記體)

I intend to write the novel about Admiral Yi Sun-sin (忠武公) as faithfully as possible to historical facts (史實) in a biographical style (傳記體). However, the actual records (實傳), letters (書翰), anecdotes (逸話), etc., of Admiral Yi Sun-sin that I have gathered are so numerous that it is difficult to select from them. From this perspective, I believe and rejoice that a faithful biography will emerge.
Tong-A Ilbo Company, Lee Kwang-soo (李光洙) bows.

To Hŏ Chŏng-suk's Mother (許貞淑母親)<70>

There is word that Hŏ Chŏng-suk (許貞琡) of the Kwangju Incident (光州事變) had her parole (假出獄) canceled and has been imprisoned again. If so, what happened to her newborn baby? Did she perhaps take the baby with her, mother and child entering prison together?
Pusan, Kwŏn Ok-hŭi (權玉姬)

She Entered Alone

After giving birth, while recuperating from childbirth (産後), she was imprisoned again about 10 days ago. She entrusted the baby to its grandmother. The baby, six months post-partum (産後), is said to fret and cry constantly.
Samch'ŏng-dong, City, A Close Friend of Hŏ (許) bows.

To the Standard-Bearer of Crossroads (交錯點旗手足下)

In the past, the Sin'ganhoe Headquarters (新幹會本部) dispatched executives (幹部) to the provinces (地方), employed paid and unpaid (有無給) standing committee members (常務), rented an office in a large building on Chongno Street, and surely incurred many expenses for stationery (紙筆墨) and other office costs (事務費). Generally, who covered (判出) these costs? No matter how I think about it, it seems difficult to manage solely through the collection of membership fees (會費收拾), which are often nominal (有名無實).
P'yŏngyang, O Ch'ang-ha (吳昌夏)

The Chairmen Did

It is true that the expenses of the Sin'ganhoe Headquarters (新幹會本部) were significant. It is also true that these were not sustained by collecting membership fees (會費) from members (會員). Then, where did that money generally come from? According to the investigation by the Crossroads Standard-Bearer (交錯點旗手), it is said that mostly the executives (幹部), especially the chairmen (主席), bore much of the cost themselves (自擔). Word has it that the former Executive Committee Chairman (執行委員長) Hŏ Hŏn (許憲) spent about 4,000 won until he was imprisoned due to the Kwangju Incident (光州事件), and his successor, the second Executive Committee Chairman <71> Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯氏), spent close to 6,000 won one way or another (이럭저럭).
K Standard-Bearer (K 旗手) replies.

Please Show Us Some Handwriting (筆蹟)

Could you please show us the handwriting (筆蹟) of our various figures in the discourse forum (論壇 諸氏)?
Wŏnsan, Pak T'ae-in (朴泰仁)

First, a Description (描寫)

The handwriting (筆蹟) of a manuscript (原稿) is extremely (大端히) interesting in that it reveals the person's character. Therefore, our company (本社) is currently (方今) planning to display the handwriting, manuscripts (原稿), and books (著書) of figures from various fields at the "Modern Chosŏn Culture General Exhibition" (近代朝鮮文化總展覽會), which will be held soon (不遠), and is currently (昨今) actively (盛히) collecting (蒐集) them. However, as you seem eager for an answer, we offer this brief description (草記) to quench your thirst (解渴).
Hong Myŏng-hŭi (洪命憙) writes very neatly, modestly, and orderly (規模께인), like a letter written by a woman, perhaps always like a letter sent to her in-laws (사돈님), whether it's a manuscript or a letter (便紙), showing great care (操心이 갑은). Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹氏) writes in a roundish, striding style. Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨) writes very fluently (暢達한) in a sky-and-sea expansive style (天空海濶式), flowing in semi-cursive script (半草), and his English writing is even better. Lee Kwang-soo (李光洙氏) writes clearly and flowingly (流麗하게), as if a lady from a noble house (閨中婦女) had practiced calligraphy on decorated paper (畫箋) for 10 years, each character flowing in a rectangular shape. An Chae-hong (安在鴻氏) writes some characters better than renowned calligraphers (名筆) can emulate, while other characters are worse than a first-grader's (小學校 1年生), but overall, his manuscript handwriting tends to be messy (지버분하게) and spreads outwards<72> outwards, asserting extraterritoriality (治外法權). Hŏ Hŏn (許憲) writes in an old style (舊式), like an old Royal Academy scholar (翰林學士), but very neatly (整然하게), with a refreshing breeze style (凉風徐來式) between the strokes (字劃間). Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿氏), no matter how favorably one tries to view it, writes like a gnarled old pine on a flat rock (盤石우 老松), rambling and barely forming characters (僅成文字), almost a textbook example of bad handwriting (惡筆). Yi Kwan-gu (李寬求氏) writes energetically, clearly carving each small character tightly. Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭氏) writes with bony characters (뼈글시), each stroke revealing its core. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟氏) writes beautifully enough to be put on decorated paper (畫箋), large, very fluid (流麗하게), and pleasant to look at. Yi Yŏ-sŏng (李如星氏) writes with the top light and the bottom heavy, yet broad and refreshing (상快하게), like items neatly packed in a chest (궤속에 짜너흔 것가치). Hŏ Yŏng-suk (許英肅氏) has a style similar (갓게) to Lee Kwang-soo's (李光洙氏) handwriting, difficult to distinguish (分練하기 어럽게) at first glance, both rounded (圓滿하고도) and flowing (流麗하게). Kim Se-yong (金世鎔氏) constructs strokes and dots like architecture (建築物), orderly (規模잇게) and with resonance (餘韻잇게). O Se-ch'ang (吳世昌氏) writes letters (書翰) etc. in the style of a renowned calligrapher/painter (名流書畵家體), forcefully and solidly, giving the impression not of characters but of iron and stone (鐵石). Myŏng Che-se (明濟世氏) writes characters that seem rough but are not, seem well-written but not quite so. Hong Sŏng-ha (洪性夏氏) possesses truly beautiful handwriting (眞是玉筆), pleasant to look at, vigorous, and fluent (流暢한) enough to be written on a folding screen (屛風書). Yi Chŏng-sŏp (李晶燮氏) writes like a scholar of high integrity (高節의 士) or a crane (학두룸), stridingly, coolly (선선하게), with a master's hand (名筆). Han Chang-gyŏng (韓長庚氏) writes without much flourish (餘裕업스나) but also without deficiency (不足이 업게), in a somewhat hurried (다라가면서?) style. The rarest form of bad handwriting (惡筆) belongs to Hyŏn Chin-gŏn (玄鎭健氏); less like characters and more like architectural sketches (建築物뎃산), a Mongolian desert sandstorm (蒙古沙漠的 狂風) whirls through all his characters. Han Ki-ak (韓基岳氏) writes like lightning (번개불), finishing strokes in an instant, the strokes so thin they seem to require a microscope (顯微鏡), like drawings of insect models (昆虫模型). Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植氏)<73> writes solidly like a reinforced concrete Western-style house (鐵筋공그리드 洋屋가치), pressing down once, pressing down twice, squarish and neat. Yi Chong-nin (李鍾鱗氏) writes as if the characters are smiling (微笑하는드시), rounded (圓滿하고도) and bright (爽英하게). Yi Chong-ho (李鍾浩氏) writes flowingly (流麗하고) and orderly (規模잇게), like examination script (科擧글씨). **Summary**

This article introduces a new reader Q&A section called "Crossroads" (交叉點) in the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*. It presents several exchanges: Lee Kwang-soo discusses his plan to write a historically faithful biographical novel about Admiral Yi Sun-sin; an update is given on the re-imprisonment of Hŏ Chŏng-suk after childbirth, with her baby left with its grandmother; the funding sources for the nationalist Sin'ganhoe organization are revealed to be primarily personal contributions from its leaders like Hŏ Hŏn and Kim Pyŏng-no, rather than membership fees; and finally, a lengthy and colorful description is provided of the handwriting styles of numerous prominent Korean intellectuals and cultural figures of the time.

From a historical perspective, this article offers valuable glimpses into colonial Korean society. It documents Lee Kwang-soo's approach to a significant national figure, highlights the personal sacrifices and hardships faced by political activists like Hŏ Chŏng-suk under Japanese rule, sheds light on the operational and financial realities of nationalist movements like the Sin'ganhoe, and provides contemporary, subjective, yet revealing character sketches of major figures through the unusual lens of graphology. The detailed descriptions of handwriting offer cultural historians insights into how these individuals were perceived by their contemporaries.

交叉點
이달부터 「交叉點」를 新設하엿슴니다. 社會의 여러 公人에게 뭇고 십흔 말을 仲介하여 드리는 곳임니다. 무슨 말슴이든지 本社에 經由하여 무러주시면 本社에서는 當該氏에게 그 便紙를 傳하야 그 回答을 本紙上에 發表하여 드림니다. 用紙는 葉書, 一人에 一問式.
- (投稿歡迎) -

李光洙氏에게

先生이 忠武公을 題材로 한 小說을 東亞日報에 쓰신다 하는 바 엇든 式으로 쓰실 작정임니까. 卽 傳記體로 事實에 充實하게 쓰시겟슴니까. 그러치 안코 題材의 大部分을 創造하여 쓰시겟슴니까.
木浦 姜士成

傳記體로

忠武公에 대한 小說은 되도록 史實에 充實하게 傳記體로 쓰려고 함니다. 그런데 내손에 모여진 忠武公의 實傳, 書翰, 逸話等은 非常히 만허서 오히려 추리기에 困難할 地境이외다. 이 點으로 보아 充實한 傳記하나가 생겨질줄을 밋고 깁버하는 바외다.
東亞日報社 李光洙 拜

許貞淑母親에게<70>

光州事變의 許貞琡이 假出獄이 取消되어 다시 入獄하엿다하는 말이 잇스니 그러면 그 갓난 아해는 엇지하고 드러갓슴니까. 혹 대리고 母子가 드러갓는지요.
釜山 權玉姬

혼자 드러갓소

解産한 뒤 産後의 몸을 靜養하고 잇다가 10여일전에 다시 入獄이 되엇소. 아해는 그 할머니에게 맛기고 갓는데 産後 6개월의 애기는 늘 보채고 운담니다.
市內三淸洞 許의 一親友 拜

交錯點旗手足下

新幹會本部에서 過去에 각금 地方에 幹部도 派遣하고 또 有無給 常務도 두고 鍾路 큰 거리에 빌딩도 事務所로 엇고 그밧게 紙筆墨 等 事務費의 入費도 만엇겟는데 그것을 大體 누가 判出하엿슴니까. 아모리 생각하여도 흔히 有名無實한 會費收拾만으로는 어로울 터인데요.
平壤 吳昌夏

委員長들이

新幹會本部의 經費가 만흔 것은 事實임니다. 그러고 그것이 會員의 會費를 거두어 드리어 지텡하여 갓든 것이 아닌 것도 사실임니다. 그러면 그 돈이 大體 어데에서 나왓느냐 할 것이외다. 여기 대하야 交錯點旗手가 調査한 바에 의하면 大槪는 幹部 그 중에도 主席들이 만히 自擔하엿다하는데 들니는 말에 前 執行委員長 許憲이 光州事件으로 드러가기까지에 約 4천원을 썻고 그뒤를 이는 第二次執行委員長<71>金炳魯氏가 이럭저럭 6천원 갓가운 돈을 썻다고 함니다.
K 旗手 答

筆蹟을 좀 보여주시오

우리 論壇 諸氏들의 筆蹟을 좀 보여주실수 업겟슴니까.
元山 朴泰仁

爲先 描寫로

原稿의 筆蹟은 그분의 性格을 보여주는 點에서 大端히 興味잇는 일이외다. 그러기에 本社에서는 方今 計劃 中에 잇서 不遠에 開催할 「近代朝鮮文化總展覽會」의 會場에다가 各方面 人士의 筆蹟과 原稿 及 著書들을 陳列코저 昨今 盛히 蒐集 中에 잇슴니다 만은 爲先 뭇는 말슴이 急하신 모양이기에 解渴삼아 草記하여 드림니다.
