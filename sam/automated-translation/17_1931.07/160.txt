Magazine Title: 삼천리 제17호
Publication Date: 1931-07-01
Article Title: 妓生女給과 男便이 戀愛할 때 妻의 態度 (The Wife's Attitude When the Husband Has an Affair with a Kisaeng or Waitress)
Author: 최의순 (崔義順 Ch'oe Ŭisun), 황신덕 (黃信德 Hwang Sindŏk), 윤성상 (尹聖相 Yun Sŏngsang), 박경희 (朴慶姬 Pak Kyŏnghŭi)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

The Wife's Attitude When the Husband Has an Affair with a Kisaeng or Waitress
In the cities, traditional kisaeng and modern waitresses have established themselves in cafés and restaurants, tempting many married men and sending flirtatious glances. Consequently, many once-peaceful homes are experiencing turmoil due to the unwelcome waves of triangular love affairs. In such situations, what attitude do New Women intend to take as wives?...

I Will Not Permit Even One Night's Pleasure

Ch'oe Ŭisun
To the sad, a word of comfort is sweet as honey, and a single smile is more precious than treasure.
Those who have failed in business, those disappointed in love, and besides these, those weeping from the loneliness that everyone experiences as a human being, are seeking only warm comfort and gentle smiles.
How could there be a distinction between men and women in this human state of mind?
When an intellectual man and an emotional woman feel the same sorrow and pain, although the colors and lines of the patterns engraved on their respective minds may differ, there will surely be no difference in their longing for a counterpart for solace.<33>
However, for us Korean women (朝鮮女子), the reality is that such a "garden" to fulfill such desires has not yet been prepared.
Still, those who are properly operating under this special privilege at present are certainly only men.
Kisaeng and waitresses – sources of comfort, no, a kind of splendid plaything for many men!
Café waitresses who purely sell smiles and charm! Kisaeng at amusement places who are said to sell songs and dances along with themselves!
While there might seem to be some differences in their signs and skills, and the types of men who seek them out may also seem somewhat different, from the perspective of soothing the broken hearts and loosened minds of men hurt by sorrow and pain, kisaeng and waitresses can be seen as exactly the same.
Therefore, men in pitiful circumstances who borrow the power of kisaeng and waitresses must be suffering people, but they must also be lonely people.
They must be those who have alcohol, friends, and of course money, but lack just one thing: a loving wife to whom they can entrust their hearts and bodies.
If someone, as another woman's husband, sought even one night's comfort or pleasure with a kisaeng or waitress, he has, during that time, publicly proclaimed his wife's powerlessness. At the very least, it would be a time when he has already lost affection for his wife.
In this sense, one who is another's wife is drawn to the feeling of wanting to block her husband's steps towards restaurants or cafés.
In the current situation where restaurants and cafés are indispensable social venues and meeting places in the sphere of men's activities, allowing one's husband to participate might be an extremely natural course of action.
However, it is also a natural fact that the wife is always shrouded in anxious fog, worrying that her husband might frequent such places purely for amusement, solely driven by a desire for entertainment.
Of course, the husband finding a lover among the kisaeng or waitresses and seeking that woman out is an exceptional case, a significant problem involving even the issue of divorce. But if he merely seeks the "service" of a kisaeng or waitress for temporary amusement, it is something that the wife absolutely cannot permit.
If kisaeng and waitresses were true artists (藝人格) or servers (뽀이格), a man who is someone else's husband would have no reason to go alone. They should definitely accompany their wives, watching, eating, and enjoying together.
When there are upsetting matters at home, the husband seeks solace in the alcohol or tea and songs provided by kisaeng and waitresses, while the wife often stays stuck at home, taking out her frustration on the innocent children, merely lamenting her lack of money and bad fortune,<34> and letting it pass carelessly.
However, one must realize that such moments are precisely the critical junctures where the bond between husband and wife can either be nurtured or broken.
Therefore, a woman who is another's wife must absolutely ensure her husband does not keep a wife and a lover separately. That is, the ideal is to play together and laugh together only with recreational objects and hobbies that are not female targets besides herself.
Consequently, if they are "kisaeng" and "waitresses" simply because they are women, I cannot help but think that they are absolutely inappropriate objects for my husband to be close to.
31.6.24

Meet That Woman

Hwang Sindŏk
If a husband frequents kisaeng houses while his wife is alive and well...? And not just frequenting, but with another purpose, say, for the sake of having an affair?
I have never anticipated such a thing happening to me, nor have I ever been in such a troublesome situation in the past, so I don't have a ready answer, but...
Overall, it would be a matter of degree, wouldn't it?

If he has completely fallen for the kisaeng (or waitress), that is, fallen for the kisaeng so much that he is unaware of his wife and children at home – completely, completely fallen – then there's nothing to be done. Can love that cannot be helped by human strength be turned back with cheap tears? I would separate boldly. I believe the matter can only be resolved by inevitably dragging it to the point of divorce.

However, if he has fallen, but still retains some sense, when the husband is merely suffering with feelings of a light love affair in his heart, then the wife, before things completely fall apart, should exert all her effort to restore that love.
As a method, persuading him to go and traveling to another region for a month or two would be good. While seeing different mountains, rivers, plants, and trees, his heart will return to being the "upright husband" of the old days.
Traveling when there is suffering is certainly an effective remedy.

If not, she should separate from her husband. Thinking about it, separating when another woman is waiting on the side might seem even more dangerous,<35> but if one lacks the self-control to endure even that much danger, how can one spend a lifetime serving him as a husband?
Therefore, perhaps separating for ten days or a month, she should give the husband a chance to reflect quietly.
Besides that, I believe there are many other ways to restore love.

And there is a need to definitely meet the other party, that woman. Meet her and see if her appearance is better than mine, her physique excellent, her education and refinement considerable, and whether she loves my husband passionately or not. If she is indeed passionate and superior to me, then I must realize that this love affair will not end quickly, and as the first wife, I must make a significant resolution. That is, realizing "I am the one to be defeated in this triangular relationship," it is best to then withdraw decisively.
Clinging to the husband and begging that woman to give him up is something a New Woman with dignity cannot bring herself to say. And it wouldn't work anyway!

In any case, one must face the situation to know.

Even a Good Wife Gets Angry at "Erotic Play"

Yun Sŏngsang
Like thread follows the needle, I generally accompany him on outings, especially since H is very weak when it comes to alcohol (alcohol and women are easy to follow), and even if H occasionally has close friends or welcome companions visit and there's an occasion for drinks, at our house, we always get alcohol delivered and sit around drinking together, or else we mobilize everyone and go to a restaurant. Perhaps it's because we're running a haphazard household, to put it badly, but I don't have much experience with the "fuss" of getting involved with waitresses or kisaeng.
But occasionally meeting friends on the street or being dragged by someone to a café or kisaeng house to have a few drinks while receiving their smiles is probably not a big issue.
It's even better if I go along too. This is a brief digression, but I find it a bit strange that among so-called modern women, there aren't many brave (?) wives who think, "Is there any place my husband goes that I can't go too?"
However, if it's truly "erotic play (에로노름) for fun," deceiving the wife's eyes,<36> or even if it's not erotic play, if he's out every day, then even all the so-called "good wives (良妻)" wouldn't just sit still. But a wise "modern wife" probably wouldn't engage in a vulgar fight like suffering in silence.
Anyway, the issue must be faced when it arises. No matter how contemptible the act, will it be forgiven "if you love, then..." (愛すればてそ - Japanese phrase)? Or will even a single act that isn't so contemptible bring about some breakdown that reason cannot permit? Who knows?

Just Leniently Urge Reflection

Pak Kyŏnghŭi
I trust my husband's (밧갓 어룬 - lit. 'outside elder') character. I firmly believe that he will never set foot in taverns and brothels (酒肆靑樓) for frivolous amusement, and that he will never, ever receive even the slightest interest or emotional stirring from café waitresses or kisaeng. His conduct was clean like this even before marriage, and during our 3-4 years of married life together, he has been gentlemanly.
Since I fully understand and believe that his character is not frivolous like that of contemporary men, I have never until now entertained thoughts like, "What would I do if he developed a light romantic interest?" Especially, I have never had even a hair's breadth of thought that the object of that romance could be a kisaeng or waitress who makes a living from romantic games with various men.

However, he too is a man. What wife can guarantee that he won't be tempted by a courtesan (遊女) at some time, in some place?
In such cases, I believe the problem naturally splits into two paths. First, if the husband's love for that woman is already very deep, reaching a point where he neglects his wife and family, this is not a matter of a triangular love affair but has passed that sentimental limit and must proceed to the issue of "annulment of marriage" or "negation of marriage." However, as your company's question does not seem to point to this, I will refrain from discussing it further here.

Then, the second case – when the wife notices signs that her husband harbors feelings similar to love for another woman, and particularly, as is common these days, for a café waitress (웨트레쓰) or a kisaeng (기녀) from a kisaeng house (기방) – what attitude should she take?
I would be very bold and leave him alone. I would keep a distant, subtle watch while leaving him alone until he returns to his former right mind. While doing so,<37>
I would strive to show through my eyes, through my expression, hints like:
"I secretly saw that woman's photograph from your pocket."
"I saw all three letters sent by that bitch hidden in the book you carry."
Never, never let such words come out of my mouth.
If such words were spoken aloud, the husband would feel ashamed and also initiate a fight out of rebellious feelings. Doing so takes away the opportunity to appeal to his conscience and deprives him of time to reflect. Anyone would be the same, right? Therefore, once I know he is facing a crisis, I think I should not resist but try to awaken his temporarily dormant conscience, his original love for his wife.
Isn't it true that even young children, if you tell them not to play with fire and take away the matchbox, will surely find a matchbox somewhere and start a fire? Love affairs, especially with kisaeng who sell smiles and bodies on the street (街路), are just momentary playing with fire. To think too much of it and become flustered and panicked (狼狽蒼慌) is not, I believe, the attitude we New Women, who have received even some modern education, should take.

Love! It can arise anytime, anywhere. But since double love cannot exist, as long as one knows the husband is not a frivolous person, the wife can remain calm and composed.
Because I realize and am confident that we wives possess a flame of intense love strong enough not to lose our husbands to the likes of kisaeng... .<38>

**Summary**

This 1931 survey article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine presents four educated Korean women's perspectives on how a wife should react if her husband becomes involved with a kisaeng (traditional entertainer) or a café waitress. Ch'oe Ŭisun advocates for preventing even casual encounters, viewing them as a sign of lost affection. Hwang Sindŏk suggests assessing the seriousness: divorce for deep love, or attempts at reconciliation (travel, separation, confronting the other woman) for lighter affairs, urging dignified withdrawal if the rival is superior. Yun Sŏngsang expresses personal inexperience but implies intolerance for deceitful "erotic play," preferring open communication or accompaniment, while acknowledging the unpredictability of emotions. Pak Kyŏnghŭi trusts her husband but suggests patient, non-confrontational observation if an affair occurs, believing quiet pressure allows his conscience and original love to reawaken, dismissing such affairs as temporary "playing with fire" unworthy of panic from an educated "New Woman."

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the social dynamics of colonial Korea. It highlights the anxieties and strategies of educated "New Women" grappling with marital infidelity in a modernizing urban setting where traditional figures like kisaeng coexist with new types of female workers like café waitresses, both seen as potential threats to marital stability. The differing responses showcase the ongoing negotiation between traditional values, modern ideas about love and marriage, female agency, and the perceived character (or lack thereof) of men and "other women" during this period of transition.

하로밤의 歡樂조차 不許하노라

崔義順
슬픈 이에게는 한 마대 위로가 꿀가치 달며 한 떨기의 웃음이 보배보다 더 귀하다.
사업에 실패한 그네들, 사랑에 실망한 그네들, 이외에도 누구나 사람으로서 갓게되는 적막증에 울고 잇는 그네들은 오직 따뜻한 위로와 보드러운 웃음만을 구하고 잇는 것이다.
사람의 이러한 심경에야 어찌 남녀의 구별이 잇스랴.
이지적인 남자와 감정적인 여자가 가튼 비애와 고통을 늣길 그 순간이라면 각각 그들 맘바탕에 색여지는 문의(紋)의 색과 선에는 다릅이 잇슬지언정 안위의 상대물을<33> 갈망하는 점에는 아모런 틀림이 업슬 것이다.
그러나 우리 朝鮮女子에게는 사실 그러한 소망을 일우어 볼 「까든」이 아즉 가추어지지 못하고 잇는 터이다.
그래도 현재 이러한 특전알에 제대로 동작을 하고 잇는 이는 확실이 남자들 뿐이다.
뭇남자들의 위로거리 아니 일종의 훌륭한 노리개인 기생과 여급!
순전히 웃음과 애교를 파는 카페의 여급! 노래와 춤과 아울러 판다는 놀이터의 기생!
이들의 간판과 기술에는 다소의 차이도 잇술 돗도 하며 또 차저주는 남자의 類도 좀 다를 듯도 하지만 남자의 슬픔과 아픔으로 허러진 가슴속과 「루스」해진 그들의 머리속을 녹녹히 매만지고 잇는 분수로는 기생이나 여급이나 똑갓다고 보겟다.
그럼으로 기생과 여급의 힘을 빌려하는 가엽슨 처지의 남성들은 괴로운 사람이면서도 반듯이 외로운 사람이에야 할 것이다.
술도 잇고 친구도 잇고 물론 돈도 잇스나 다만 한 가지 맘과 몸을 매낄만한 사랑하는 안해만이 업는 이들에야 할 것이다.
만약 남의 남편으로서 기생 혹은 여급과 함께 하로 밤의 안위 또는 환락을 어드랴고 햇다면 그는 그 동안만은 자기 안해의 무력함을 公明히 호소한 것이다. 적어도 그는 안해에게 향한 애착을 이미 일허버린 동안이겟다.
이러한 의미에서 남의 안해된 이는 요리점이나 카페로 향하는 자기 남편의 거름을 막고 시퍼지는 심정에 끌리는 것이다.
요리점과 카페가 남자의 활약 지대에 업지 못할 사교장이며 의논석이 되는 現형세에 딸하 자기의 남편으로 하야곰 참가케 한다는 것은 극히 자연스러운 처단일 것이다.
그러나 안해되는 사람은 자기 남편이 행여나 일종의 道樂삼아 오로지 유흥적 기분에 끌리여 그런 곳에 출입을 하게 될 때가 올가하야 항상 걱정스러운 안개에 싸이게 되는 것만도 亦是 자연스러운 사실이다.
물론 기생이나 여급 중에 연인이 생겨서 남편이 그 여성을 찻는다는 것은 어데까지 예외의 일로 이것은 이혼문제까지 껴지는 적지 안흔 문제이지만 단순히 일시적 오락으로 기생이나 여급의 「써비스」를 바란다면 안해된 사람으로서는 도저히 허락할 수 업는 일이다.
기생과 여급이 진실한 藝人格이고 뽀이格이 된다면 남의 남편되는 사람이 참아 혼자만 갈 이치가 업다. 반듯이 그들은 안해와 동반하야 가티 보고 먹고 가티 질겨야 할 것이다.
집안에 화나는 일이 잇서 남편은 기생과 여급들의 주는 술 혹은 차와 노래에서 위안을 찻고 잇슬 때 안해는 흔히 그대로 집구석에서 애메한 아히나 따리는 것으로 화풀이를 하면서 단지 돈 업고 팔자 사나웁다는<34> 한탄이나 하고 무심히 넘기여 버린다.
그러나 이러한 고비일스록 진실로 남편과 안해 두 사람 사이의 정분을 키울 때도 되고 또는 깨칠 때도 되는 切線이 되는 것을 알어야 할 것이다.
그럼으로 남의 안해된 이는 남편에게 절대로 안해와 연인을 따로 두지 못하게 할 일이다. 즉 어데까지 자기 이외에는 여자 대상이 아니되는 오락물과 취미거리에 가티 놀고 가티 웃게 되는 것이 이상이다.
딸하서 단순히 여자이기때문에의 「기생」과 「여급」이라면 자기 남편과 천부당 만부당 갓갑게 못할 대상이라고 불 수 밧게 업스리라고 나는 생각한다.

그 女性과 맛나보고

黃信德
안해가 싯퍼러케 사라 잇는데 그 남편이 긔생집으로 출입한다면... ? 그것도 그저 출입하는 것이 아니라 딴 목적이 잇서서 이를 터이면 연애를 하기 위하여서라면?
나는 아직까지 이런 일이 내 압헤 닥처 오리라고 예상하여 본 젹도 업섯고 과거에 또한 그러한 성가신 경우에 노여본 적도 업스니까 얼는 대답할 말이 업지만은
도대체 그것은 정도 문제겟지요.

긔생(혹은 녀급)한테 아주 반하엿거든, 즉 긔생에게 반하여 집안에 처자가 잇는 줄도 모르게 아주 아주 반하엿거든ㅡ 그런 때야 할 수 업지요. 인력으로 엇절 수 업는 사랑을 갑싼 눈물로 돌닐 수가 잇겟슴니까. 아조 대담하게 갈나저 버리지요. 그것은 필연적으로 리혼문제에까지 끄을고 가서야 일이 해결될 줄 암니다.

그러치만 반하기는 반하엿스되 아즉 제 정신이 남어잇슬 때 다만 남편이 가비여운 연애의 감정을 가슴에 품고 괴로워할 때 그 때에는 안해된 사람은 일이 아조 글너지기 전이니깐 전력을 다하야 그 사랑의 회복에 노력하여야지요.
