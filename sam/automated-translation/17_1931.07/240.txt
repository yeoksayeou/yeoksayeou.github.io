**Body Text Translation:**
British Imperialism and the Indian Movement
						Science Research Institute
The Indian Movement and the World Political Situation- From 1930 to the present year has been a period of storms for the capitalist world.<56> All the major capitalist powers of the world are without exception engulfed in the vortex of a severe economic panic, and the economic panic shows signs of transforming into a political crisis. The colonies, which were the reservoirs of imperialism, are receiving an even more severe panic than the home countries, and far from just a political crisis, a direct revolutionary situation is gradually raising a fiercer torch.
In this sense, the present state of India cannot but be said to be the most noteworthy time. The area of British India, including the island of Ceylon, is 4.9 million square kilometers, which can be seen as half the total area of Europe. The population is 330 million, three-quarters of the population of Europe. The awakening of such a large territory, such a large populace, is in itself a world astonishment.
However, to correctly judge the significance of the Indian Revolution in the world political situation, we must investigate the foundation upon which the current world political situation is unfolding. Currently, two different systems of nations confront each other in the world: the capitalist countries and the Soviet Union.
Just as Lenin said that the colonies are the reserve army of imperialism, they are an indispensable source of exploitation for the existence of the great capitalist nations. The national liberation movement in these colonies is, needless to say, collapsing the foundation of the capitalist system.
India, the center of the British Empire, is the central colony of the British Empire. It is said that the sun never sets on British territory. The majority of that territory forms a shape that protects the surroundings with India at the center.<57>
Gibraltar, Malta, Suez, and Aden serve as guardians of the route from Europe to India; South and East Africa are prepared points on the sea route rounding the Cape of Good Hope; Singapore is a key point on the Far East shipping route; and the Australian continent is also connected to the southeast of India.
What tribute does India offer to the home country, Britain? India sends troops for the home country, pays taxes, pays interest on capital, and buys goods, sending this huge treasury to the home country.
Some portion of these profits is not expressed in numbers.
Excluding this, let's see how much of an enormous tribute Britain forcibly demands from India year after year.
How much does British governance cost? In 1924-25, the expenses in Britain amounted to about 140 million won. The expenses for India are even greater than this. In other words, the cost of British rule that India must bear is 300 million won.
Next, India's trade is completely monopolized by British merchants. In 1925, the goods India sold abroad amounted to 800 million won, and the goods bought from abroad amounted to 870 million won (of which 850 million won were British products). Calculating the profit margin on those goods at 10%, 150 million won goes into the pockets of British merchants.
Third, the industrial profit remaining from the goods sold to India, calculated at 15%, amounts to 120 million won annually.
Fourth, since the capital invested in India amounts to about 10 billion won, the profit rate is very high, at least 10%.
Therefore, the capital return is 1 billion won annually. Additionally, India's shipping is almost monopolized by British vessels, and its insurance passes through the hands of British insurance companies. The profit from this category is difficult to pinpoint precisely, but it is estimated to be around 100 million won.
Summing these up leads to the following conclusion:
Even calculating most conservatively the extent of what Britain exploits from India, expressible in numbers, it amounts to 1.67 billion won annually. (Based on Varga's "India, the Center Point of the British Empire" in the "World Economic Yearbook," Second Collection.)
If India disappears, what happens to Britain? The British bourgeoisie itself knows well how necessary the profits exploited from India are for the existence of the British Empire. Lord Rothermere, who recently formed an opposition faction within the Conservative Party and stirred up the "United Empire Party" movement, says:
"India is the target of the British Empire. If we lose India, the Empire must inevitably collapse <58>

**Summary**

This 1931 article argues that the ongoing Indian independence movement is a critical manifestation of the global crisis facing capitalism and imperialism. It highlights India's vast size and population, emphasizing its strategic and economic centrality to the British Empire. The author details the immense financial exploitation of India by Britain through governance costs, trade monopolies, industrial profits, capital returns, shipping, and insurance, estimating the annual drain at 1.67 billion won. The article contrasts the decaying capitalist world, plagued by economic panic and colonial revolts, with the alternative system represented by the Soviet Union, framing India's struggle as a key factor in the potential collapse of the British Empire and the weakening of global capitalism, citing British figures like Lord Rothermere who acknowledged India's vital importance.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it shows a Korean perspective (published in a Korean magazine, though the author is cited as a "Science Research Institute," likely a pen name or collective) analyzing another major anti-colonial struggle. It reflects the shared experience of being under imperial rule and the keen interest in liberation movements elsewhere. The detailed breakdown of economic exploitation in India would have resonated with Koreans experiencing similar economic structures under Japanese rule, offering both a comparative analysis and perhaps inspiration or validation for their own anti-colonial sentiments. The framing of the struggle within a global conflict between capitalism and an alternative (Soviet) system also reflects contemporary ideological currents influential among Korean intellectuals.

						科學硏究所
印度運動과 世界政局- 1930년으로부터 今年에 이르기까지에 時日은 資本主義 世界에<56> 잇서서 暴風의 時期이엿다. 世界中의 主要 資本主義 强國은 남김업시 深刻한 經濟恐慌의 渦中에 쌔워잇고 經濟恐慌은 政治的 危機에 轉化될 形勢를 뵈이고 잇다. 帝國主義의 貯水池이엿슬 植民地는 本國 以上에 激甚한 恐慌을 바더 政治的 危機는 커냥 直接的 革命情勢가 漸漸 熾烈한 炬火를 들고 잇는 中이다.
印度의 現在는 이런 意味로 가장 注目될때라고 말치 안을 수 업다. 英領印度의 面積은 쎄이론島를 아울너 490萬粁平方, 歐羅巴의 全面積 半分으로 볼수가 잇다. 人口는 3억3천만, 歐羅巴人口의 4分의 3이다. 이럿케 큰 領土, 이럿케 만은 民衆의 覺醒은 그것만으로도 世界的 驚異다.
그러나 印度革命은 世界政局에 잇서서 意義를 바르게 判斷하기 위하야 現在의 世界政局이 엿더한 土臺 우에서 展開되여 잇는 것을 究明치 안으면 안될 것이다. 現今 世界에는 두가지의 틀닌 體制를 가진 諸國이 對立하고 잇스니 그것은 資本主義의 國家等과 쏘비엣트同盟과이다.
植民地는 帝國主義의 豫備軍이라고 레-닌이 말한것과 갓치 大資本主義 諸國의 存立에는 不可缺될 搾取源泉이다. 이 植民地에 잇서서 民族解放運動은 말할것도 업시 資本主義體制의 土臺를 崩壞식히는 것이다.
大英帝國의 中心 印度는 大英帝國의 中心 植民地이다. 英吉利의 領土로부터는 해가 지지안는다고 말한다. 그 領土의 大部分은 印度를 中心으로해서 그 周圍를 護衛하고 잇는 形狀이 되고 잇다.<57>
지불알탈, 막르다, 스엣스, 아덴은 歐羅巴로부터 印度에 通하는 길직힘이 되고 南及東아푸리카는 希望峯을 迂廻하는 海絡의 要備이고 싱가폴은 東洋航路의 要衝이고 오스트라이야大陸도 印度의 南東과 連絡되고 잇다.
印度가 本國英吉利에 밧치는 貢物은 무엇인가? 印度는 本國의 軍隊를 보내고 租稅를 밧치고 資本의 利子를 支拂하고 商品을 사서 이 巨額의 金庫를 本國에 보내는 것이다.
이런 利益中에 엇던 部分은 數字에 表現식히지 안는다.
이것을 除하고도 年年이 얼마마한 巨額의 貢物을 英吉利는 印度로부터 强要하고 잇는가를 보자.
英吉利의 統治費가 얼마나 드느냐 하면 1924-25년, 英吉利에 잇서서의 費用이 約1억4천만원이 되여잇다. 印度에 대한 費用은 이것보다도 만타. 다시 말하면 印度가 負擔치 안으면 안될 英吉利의 統治의 費用이 3억원이다.
다음으로 印度의 貿易은 完全히 英吉利商人에게 獨占을 當하고 잇다. 1925년에 印度가 外國에 판 商品이 8억원, 外國으로부터 산 商品이 8억7천만원,(中 8억5천만원은 英吉利製品)이다. 그 商品利潤을 1割노 보아서 1억5천만원이 英國商人의 품속으로 드러간다.
第三에 이 印度에 판 商品으로부터 남는 工業利潤은 1割5分으로 보아서 年1억2천만원이다.
第四에는 印度에 投下된 資本이 約100억원이나 되니가 그 利潤은 非常한 高率로 젹어도 1割은 된다.
그러니가 資本收益은 年10억원이다. 其他 印度의 海運은 거히 英國船舶에 獨占이 되고 그 保險은 英國保險會社의 손을 通하여 잇다. 이 種類에 利潤은 的確히 指摘키은 어려우나 1억원程度는 되리라.
이것을 合計한즌 다음과 갓흔 結論이 된다.
英吉利가 印度로부터 搾取하고 잇는 것을 數字로 表示식힐 限度를 가장 內部的으로 計算하고도 年16억7천만원이나 된다. (바르가 大英帝國의 中心點 印度「世界經濟年報」 第二輯의 의함.)
印度가 업서지면 英吉利는 엇지되나? 이 印度로부터 搾取하는 利益이 大英帝國의 存在에 잇서서 엇더한 必要가 잇는지는 英吉利의 뿔조아自身이 잘알고 잇다. 近來 保守黨內에 反對派를 形成해서 「合衆帝國黨」運動을 捲起식히고 잇는 로사미-아鄕은 말한다.
