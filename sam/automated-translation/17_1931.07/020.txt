**Body Text Translation:**

What is a Council Organization?
						Kim Myŏngsik (金明植)

On May 16, 1931, the dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) was decided at the Sin'ganhoe General Assembly. This is a great victory for the Korean masses. We should celebrate this victory and commemorate this day.

Now, the direction the Korean masses must take has been determined, and the obstacles lying before them have been removed. (Omission - original text) It has been judged by fact which class bears the heavy responsibility.

The March 1st Movement was the first step for Koreans to know the world. It was also the first movement to make Korea known to the world. From this, Koreans came to know the world, and the people of the world came to know Korea. Thus began the history of the new life movement for Koreans. And there are two things Koreans learned about the world. One is liberal thought, and the other is Marxist thought. Through the former, they learned about the individual and the nation, and through the latter, they learned about workers and peasants. That is, they learned about class. They imported it as an idea and understood it through life. Thus, they realized it was a mirror reflecting themselves, a photograph of themselves. However, in importing it, understanding it, or realizing it, much time and even more effort—blood and tears—had to be spent. Henceforth, even more effort will have to be expended.

The history of the new life movement for Koreans spans merely a dozen years. However, in such a short history, the history of the struggle of Marxists against nationalists is not short, nor was the object of struggle singular. Initially, they fought against chauvinistic nationalists, and then against capitalistic nationalists. Those have already become one. Subsequently, they fought against right-wing nationalists (petty bourgeoisie - 小뿌르조아지). In this fight, victory has now been achieved. This victory must be secured until the very end.

Great victory unfolds great hope, but it is invariably accompanied by extraordinary danger. Therefore, now more than anything, the danger must be identified and overcome. Otherwise, not only will we fail to prepare for the victory of the next class, but we cannot guarantee that the hard-won victory will not, conversely, become the cause of defeat. And what is the danger? Complacency is the danger, stability is the danger, opportunism (機會主義) is the danger, and thus the emergence of a Second Sin'ganhoe is the danger. They [the nationalists] have declared this to be inevitable and stated they will not be used by class activists again.

Is this recent victory due to our strength being formidable, or the opponent's strength being weak? Or is it not that the opponent's strength was weak, but rather that their strength was not concentrated? All three are likely facts. Firstly, it is a fact that our strength is relatively formidable, and it is also a fact that the opponent's strength is weak. Among these, it was also a fact that their strength had hitherto been dispersed, and their total power had not been concentrated in the Sin'ganhoe. Therefore, if a Second Sin'ganhoe were to emerge now, under the traditional concept of nationalism, the left and right wings of the bourgeoisie would unite, forming a bloc (뿌룩크) of various bourgeois strata and feudal remnants (landlords, religious groups - 地主 宗敎團) <2> that claim not to be utilized by their so-called 'objects' [i.e., the class activists]. Simultaneously, the right-wing nationalist group, a bourgeois group, would not be isolated but would leap to occupy the throne of leadership.

Originally, the left and right wings of nationalism possess conceptually different ideological systems, but when it comes to practical issues from a national standpoint—especially at the current stage, in the third phase of capitalism—they tend to proceed in unison. Furthermore, if the left-wing nationalist group becomes isolated, there is a danger they might unconditionally surrender to the right-wing nationalists. Therefore, at this time when organizational relationships have drastically changed, one must not lose faith in the fundamental policy or decide on a cowardly retreat.

One of the immediate urgent tasks is to prevent the union of the left and right wings of the national bourgeoisie, isolate the right wing, and draw the left wing towards our side. To do so, there must be an organization that can embrace them, and that organization must be related to a council organization (協議會組織). Now, they stand at a crossroads where they must either take a step forward or a step back. If they take a step forward, from an anti-XXXX perspective, they will become a group of propertyless citizens (無産市民) and be absorbed into one wing of the class organization. If they take a step back, they will become a fascist-like group, an anti-class organization, serving as a special detachment for the bourgeoisie. Herein lies the gravity of the issue.

"Political strategy and military strategy are, in German terms, 'border territory' (Grenzgebiet - meaning inseparable)." Among the principles established by military experts, I believe there are many that can yield significant gains when applied to our current problems. Following the guidance of a senior, I present the second of the four principles by the great German strategist Clausewitz (크라우체위취):
"In any situation, relaxing tension means pushing the achievement of the objective far away. Even in cases where victory seems certain, failing to exert maximum effort to ensure definite success is the most foolish thing. Because such effort will never be ineffective."
This is, of course, extremely rudimentary, something simple that everyone knows. However, I believe it is necessary to emphasize this principle once again at this time.

Regarding the Sin'ganhoe issue, we have certainly won. However, we must not relax our tension, satisfied with that victory. We must exert maximum effort to ensure definite success. It will certainly not be without effect. And to secure the victory, we must establish our basic organization and place the left-wing nationalist group under our leadership. Under their influence are parts of the peasantry. Therefore, their secession alone is not a small issue, but if they secede, a portion of the peasantry will follow, and thus the fundamental goal of the Sin'ganhoe dissolution—the completion of a unified national X-line—cannot but be interrupted midway. Even if, due to the severity of the objective situation, we are forced to take a defensive posture, concerning this issue and in this direction, we must not forget that "the best defense is an attack." Is it not clear that the consolidation of the national bourgeoisie—though not inevitable—will emerge from the conscious efforts of their various strata soon?

Our basic organization has three types: the head organization (頭部組織), the trunk organization (胴體組織), and the limb organization (肢體組織). The heroes of the Second International (第2인터나수날) say, "The trunk organization is not the basic organization." And viewing the significance of the Sin'ganhoe dissolution solely as the consolidation and arrangement of the limb organization is a grave error. Why? Because the consolidation and arrangement of the limb organization alone can never organize a unified X-line. Then, what is the trunk organization? It is the council (alliance) of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens (勞働者農民 無産市民協議會(同盟)). This organization can and must exist in any society, in any era.

There are two misconceptions about the trunk organization. One view is that it is limited to emergency organizations, and the other is that it inevitably transforms into a worker-peasant party. This latter view stems from a lack of understanding of international guidance and a lack of trust in the advanced class, a planar, mechanical observation method. It is a groundless fear (杞憂). Furthermore, the objective situation <3> does not permit its transformation into a worker-peasant party even under current conditions. Moreover, the domestic situation only serves to increasingly sharpen the fundamental confrontation between the two major classes day by day. Consequently, it is fully guaranteed that no intermediate political organization can arise in the future. And the trunk organization does not have a fixed form, nor is its task fixed. Its form is determined by its task, and its task is determined variously by the diverse aspects of the objective situation. Therefore, this organization takes a constructive form in an era of construction, an emergency form in times of emergency, and a preparatory form in times of preparation.

Mr. Kuusinen (크-시넨) said, "In various countries, under various situations, a uniform organizational form like the one I have shown here probably does not exist," and as an example of such an organizational form, "The XXXX of workers = peasants can take the form of regularly held, carefully prepared joint meetings and congresses of representatives from XXX peasant leagues or committees and labor unions. Under certain circumstances, organizing an XX action committee to coordinate the actions of worker = peasant groups and direct various mass actions, etc., is also appropriate to the situation. Finally, one of the important tasks of XXX in the XX of XX will be to organize the XXXXX of workers and peasants... (Overseas Theses)". And Mr. * [Kuusinen] in a section of the same report speech, explained, "At the stage where XXXX has not yet been organized as an XX organ, where that time has not yet been reached, by what means will the tasks performed by XXXX in the Russian (露西亞) Revolution during the intermediate period between XX and XX be carried out? This is the question of the form of XXXX and the organizational form for winning the masses in the preparatory stage of the movement prior to the immediate XX. What kind of organizational form can be conceived that mobilizes not only the proletariat, but also broad masses including the peasantry? It is an organization that the masses recognize as their own representative body, perhaps because they elected it themselves, or for other reasons, and through which not only XX masses but also non-XX masses, even reformist X-oriented masses, can be embraced. It is an organizational form wherein XXXX activities are carried out within their ranks, where our ideological struggle against X is conducted before the eyes of these organizationally united masses, thereby liberating the masses from petty-bourgeois illusions and the influence of reformists..."

Currently, the overall international situation can be classified into four types:
1.  Nations or peoples where XX has succeeded and construction is underway.
2.  Nations or peoples where XX is currently progressing.
3.  Nations or peoples where the momentum for XX has reached its peak.
4.  Nations or peoples still in the preparatory stage.
Therefore, the worker-peasant and propertyless citizen alliance will be expressed in four types of organizational forms under the various situations above. Consequently, the only task now is to investigate which type of organizational form is suitable for the current situation in Korea (朝鮮).

As long as exploitation by capitalists and landlords and XX exist, the struggle of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens cannot but exist, and if their struggle exists, their struggle alliance cannot but exist. And the principle of organization is never spontaneous generation; in the true sense, it is purposeful and conscious. Furthermore, in a stage where the objective situation is extremely perilous, leaving various nationwide XX struggles to spontaneous generation is a complete surrender to the perilous situation and simultaneously means disarming oneself for the benefit of the opponent's success. Also, in such a situation, if the leadership (指導部) and the action units (行動部) do not take organic action through separate organizations, the establishment of the leadership will become mere fantasy. Therefore, we must make the worker, peasant, and propertyless citizen masses understand the significance of the alliance, make them unfold realistic joint struggles, and thereby absorb and organize their demands. Especially since, with the dissolution of Sin'ganhoe, the joint XX substance of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens has effectively disintegrated, if the organizational entity of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens is not constituted at this time, the dissolution of Sin'ganhoe will be a retreat, definitely not an advance. And currently, national organizations of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens (the former Sin'ganhoe can substitute for the national organization of propertyless citizens) <4> exist, and the central organs of their respective local organizations are almost established nationwide – with slogans for the daily XX of workers, peasants, propertyless citizens and the recapture of Sin'ganhoe members – the subjective and objective conditions for the substantive organization of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens are maturing. And if one understands the Worker-Peasant, Propertiless Citizen Council (勞働者農民, 無産市民 協議會) abstractly, thinking that workers' struggles are in labor unions, peasants' struggles in peasant unions, proletarian citizens' struggles in street organizations, and the unification of their struggles is in X, therefore the Worker-Peasant, Propertiless Citizen Council will end up as a temporary expedient organization, then this is not only a misunderstanding of the council but, under the current Korean situation, cannot but become a conscious destruction of X. Consequently, even with good intentions, it amounts to nothing more than terrorism. Now, the vanguard X cannot come out and act before the masses, and without a mass organization as a reservoir—a trunk (胴體) between the leadership and the limb organizations (肢體組織)—to embrace the broad masses under its guidance and act before the masses, XXX cannot become mass-based, let alone establish itself even as a sectarian (쎅트的) existence. Even if that were possible, without an organization for mass action, it would inevitably have to無理하게 come out and act before the masses from time to time, and consequently, suffer慘혹한 destruction repeatedly. In 1928, after the old Labor-Farmer Party (舊勞農黨) in Japan (日本) was dissolved and before the new party was organized, the issue of organizing a Labor-Farmer Council (勞農協議會) was raised by the Proletarian News (無産者新聞). At that time, the legalism-is-everything faction of the Labor-Farmer Party dismissed the proposal for two reasons: that the Labor-Farmer Council was limited to emergency organizations and that it negated the legal Labor-Farmer Party entirely. Thus, the New Labor-Farmer Party (新勞農黨) emerged, and eventually, its glorious tradition was buried in the mud. For a long time, XXX became a sectarian existence, unable to penetrate the masses, yet because it inevitably had to 無理하게 emerge before the masses from time to time, it left behind a record of even more慘혹한 X during that period. And if even in an advanced capitalist country, such a慘敗 was suffered due to indecisiveness regarding organizational issues and misunderstanding of the significance and task of the council, it is clear that if such history repeats in Korea, our慘敗 will be even more profound. In advanced capitalist countries, the working class has a foundation proportional to capitalist development in terms of numbers, training, and base, while simultaneously the power of peasants and propertyless citizens weakens, allowing the working class to secure hegemony (헤게모니) over the backward classes, and its guidance can thoroughly penetrate all strata. However, in colonies (殖民地) and semi-colonies, the history of working-class development is extremely short, and due to the extreme limitations on industrial development, the number, training, and base of the working class cannot but be limited. Consequently, the guidance of the advanced class over the various backward class strata cannot be thorough, making it difficult to secure hegemony. Therefore, especially concerning the colonial and semi-colonial problem, the aforementioned exceptional thesis arose, and it has been practically proven that under the current situation, it is suitable not only limited to colonies and semi-colonies but also widely applicable in advanced capitalist countries. And thinking of the Worker-Peasant, Propertiless Citizen Council as a perpetually legal organization is a mechanical view regarding organizational issues. Under Korea's perilous objective situation, unless it is a consciously reactionary organization, even petty-bourgeois political or ideological organizations cannot maintain perpetual legal existence. How much less can one claim that the perpetual legal existence of a class organization is not a fantasy? Thinking only in this way leads to the rigid theory that it will become a Labor-Farmer Party or transform into a petty-bourgeois political organization. However, the reality is that once expressed, it cannot but go underground, yet this does not mean it cannot fulfill its tasks and roles. And once it is expressed and its existence becomes known to the masses, it can enter XX and perform many roles and tasks. The only issue is making its existence known to the masses; perpetual legal formal existence is not the issue. Japan's Zenkyō (全協), being underground (地X), is not unable to conduct activities comparable to Rōsō (勞總) or Rōren (勞聯). Through its activities, the massification of XXX progresses, but because it does not stand directly before the masses, and under its guidance, Zenkyō stands at the forefront of mass action, it does not suffer the 無X한 XX of being rounded up all at once as before.
In essence, the separation of the class leadership and action units is an even more crucial and significant issue under the current situation, and the fundamental significance of the Sin'ganhoe dissolution must lie herein.<5>

**Summary**

This 1931 article by Kim Myŏngsik argues that the dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe represents a victory for the class-conscious masses over nationalists but warns against complacency. Kim contends that the immediate danger is the potential formation of a unified nationalist front (a "Second Sin'ganhoe") led by the right-wing bourgeoisie, which could absorb the left-wing nationalists (petty bourgeoisie). To counter this and secure the gains, he advocates for the urgent creation of a new "Council Organization" (協議會組織) – a broad alliance of workers, peasants, and propertyless citizens under Marxist leadership. This "trunk organization" is presented as essential for unifying the masses, guiding left-wing nationalists away from the right-wing, and serving as the necessary link between the vanguard leadership ("head") and the basic units ("limbs"), especially in the repressive conditions of colonial Korea where open vanguard activity is difficult.

For historians, this article offers a valuable window into the ideological debates and strategic thinking within the Korean left immediately following the controversial dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe. It showcases a Marxist analysis of Korean society, the perceived threat of resurgent nationalism, the complex relationship between class and national liberation movements, and the influence of Comintern ideas (like those attributed to Kuusinen) and Japanese left-wing organizational experiences on Korean activists. The discussion of different organizational forms ("head," "trunk," "limb") and the adaptation of revolutionary strategy to colonial conditions is particularly noteworthy.

協議會組織은 엇더한 것인가.
						金明植

1931년 5월 16일 新幹會全體大會에서 新幹會의 解消를 決定하얏다. 이것은 朝鮮民衆의 偉大한 勝利다.
이 勝利를 祝福하고 이날을 記念할 것이다.
이제 朝鮮民衆이 갈길은 方向이 一定하야젓고 그압헤 노혓든 障碍物은 除去되엿다. (中略 - 원문)의 重任을 擔負한 者가 어느 階級이라고 그것이 事實로써 判斷되엿다.
3.1運動은 朝鮮人이 世界를 아는 첫거름이엿다. 또 朝鮮을 世界에 알리는 첫 運動이엿다. 이로부터 朝鮮人은 世界를 알앗고 世界人은 朝鮮을 알앗다. 그리하야 朝鮮人의 新生活運動史가 始作하얏다. 그리고 朝鮮人이 世界에 대하야 안 것은 두가지다. 하나는 自由思想이오 하나는 맑스思想이다. 前者에 의하야 個人과 國家를 알앗고 後者에 의하야 勞働者와 農民을 알앗다. 卽 階級을 알앗다. 그리고 그것은 觀念으로 輸入하고 生活에서 理解하얏다. 그리하야 그것이 自己自身을 反映하는 거울이오 寫眞인줄을 알앗다. 그러나 그것을 輸入함에 잇서서 또는 理解함에 잇서서 또는 實現함에 잇서서 만흔 時間을 또 보다 만흔 努力- 피눈물을 쓰지 아니치 못하얏다. 압흐로 또 다시 더만흔 努力을 쓰어야 할 것이다.
朝鮮人의 新生活運動史는 겨우 十數年의 年代를 가젓다. 그러나 그와가티 짤은 歷史에서 맑스主義者의 民族主義者에 대한 鬪爭史는 짤은 것이 아니오 또 鬪爭의 對象도 하나이 아니엿다. 처음에 國粹主義的 民族主義者와 싸왓고 이어 資本主義的 民族主義者와 싸왓다. 저들은 이미 하나이 되엿다. 또이어 右翼民族主義者(小뿌르조아지)와 싸왓다. 이 싸움에서 이번 勝利하얏다. 이 勝利는 最後까지 確保하지 안흐면 안된다.
偉大한 勝利에는 偉大한 希望이 展開되고 또 반다시 非常한 危險이 隨伴한다. 그럼으로 이제 무엇보다도 먼저 危險을 發見하고 그것을 克服하지 안흐면 안된다. 그러치 아니하면 다음 階級의 勝利를 準備하지 못활뿐 아니라 모처럼 穫得한 勝利가 도리혀 敗北의 原因이 되지 안흠을 保障할수 업다. 그리고 무엇이 危險이냐 하면 滿足이 危險이오 安定이 危險이오 機會主義가 危險이오 그리하야 第2新幹會의 出現이 危險이다. 이것을 저들은 必然이라하고 또 다시는 階級運動者에게 利用되지 안는다고 言明하얏다.
今般의 勝利는 우리의 힘이 强大함인가 相對者의 힘이 微弱함인가 或은 相對者의 힘이 微弱함이 아니오 그들의 힘이 集中되지 못하얏든 까닭인가. 이 세가지는 모다가 事實일 것이다. 첫재 우리의 힘이 相對的으로 强大한 것이 事實이오 또 相對者의 힘이 微弱한 것이 事實이오 그런 中에도 從來 그들의 힘이 分散되여 그들의 總力量이 新幹會에 集中되지 못하얏든 것도 事實이엿다. 그럼으로 이제 만일 第2新幹會가 出現한다하면 民族主義라는 傳統的 觀念下에서 뿌르조아지의 左翼右翼이 結合하야 저의 所謂 客體에 利用되지 안는다는 뿌르조아 各層 밋 封建遺物各層(地主 宗敎團)의<2> 뿌룩크가 組織될것이오 그와 同時에 右翼民族主義者크릅이라는 뿌르조아크릅은 孤立이 되지안코 一躍 領導的 地位의 王座를 占有할 것이다.
元來 民族主義의 左翼右翼은 觀念的으로는 各各 다른 思想的 體系를 가지고 잇스나 民族的이라는 見地에서의 實際問題에 이르러서는 더욱히 現段級에 잇서서는- 第3期 資本主義時代에 잇서서는 同一한 步調로 나가게 되고 또 左翼民族主義者크릅이 孤立하게 되면 그들은 無條件的으로 右翼民族主義者에게 屈服할 危險性이 업지 아니하다. 그럼으로 組織關係가 激變한 이때에 잇서서 根本方針에 대한 信念을 일커나 또는 卑怯한 退却을 自決하야서는 안된다.
當面急務의 하나는 民族뿌르조아지의 左翼과 右翼의 結合을 阻止하야 그 右翼을 孤立케 하고 그 左翼을 우리의 쪽으로 끄려오는 것이다. 그러함에는 그들을 包容할 組織이 잇서야 하고 그 組織은 協議會組織과 關聯이 되지 안흐면 안된다. 이제 저들은 一步를 前進하거나 그러치 아니하면 一步를 退却할수 밧게 업는 岐路에 서서 잇다. 그리고 저들이 一步를 前進하면 反XXXX的 見地에서 無産市民의 크릅이 되여 階級組織의 一翼에 吸收되겟고 一步를 退却하면 파쓰씨즘的 크릅으로 反階級的 組織이 되여 뿌르조아지의 別動隊가 될 것이다. 여긔에 問題의 重大性이 잇다.

『政治戰略과 軍事戰略은 獨逸語로 이른바 「國境의 領土」(不可分의 意)다』 軍事專門家에 의하야 樹立된 諸原則中에는 우리의 當面問題에 適用하야 만흔 收穫이 잇슬것이 잇슬줄로 안다. 先輩의 啓示에 의하야 獨逸의 偉大한 戰略家「크라우체위취」의 4個原則中에서 第2原則을 提示한다.
『엇더한 境遇에라도 緊張을 弛緩케 하는 것은 目的의 達成을 멀리 저쪽으로 돌려보냄을 意味한다. 비록 勝利가 確實히 보이는 境遇에 잇서서라도 確實한 成功을 期約하기 위하야 最大의 努力을 쓰지 아니한다는 것은 가장 어리석은 일이다. 웨그러냐하면 그러한 努力은 決코 無效果가 되지 아니할터이니까』
이것은 勿論 極히 初步인 簡單한 누구든지 다 아는 일이다. 그러나 이 時期에 잇서서 또한번 이 原則을 高調할 必要가 잇다고 생각한다.
新幹會問題에 잇서서 우리는 確實히 勝利하얏다. 그러나 그까지의 勝利에 滿足하야 緊張을 弛緩케 하야서는 안된다. 또 確實한 成功을 期約하기 위하야 最大의 努力을 쓰지 안흐면 안된다. 그것은 決코 效果가 업지 아니할 것이다. 그리고 勝利를 確保함에는 우리의 基本組織을 確立하고 우리의 領導下에 左翼民族主義者크릅을 두지 아니하면 안된다. 저들의 影響下에는 農民의 一部가 잇다. 그럼으로 저들의 離脫만도 적은 問題가 아닌대 만일 저들이 離脫하게되면 따라서 農民一部가 離脫할것이니 그리되면 新幹會解消의 根本目的인 民族的 單一X線의 完成은 中途半斷이 되지아니할 수 업다. 설사 客觀的 情勢의 險惡에 因하야 守勢를 取하지 아니할 수 업다고 할지라도 이 問題에 잇서서는 그리고 이 方面에 대하야서는 「最善의 防禦은 進擊」인 것을 이저서는 안된다. 엽흐로 곳 民族뿌르조아지의 結束이- 必然은 아닌대 - 저들 各層의 意識的 努力에서 出現할 것은 分明한 일이 아니냐.
