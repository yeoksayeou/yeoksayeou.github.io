**Body Text Translation:**
Watering Cart
						P'yŏnsŏkch'on

The clock on the pointed roof of the University Hospital (大學病院)
shows nine in the morning—

Damn bastard—
The sun has sprawled in the sky above the street, red with anger.

Lately, that bastard too has a sickness of bothering the Earth whenever it gets angry,
an utterly uncontrollable ruffian.

Today too, the watering cart, bearing the brand (烙印) of the Prefecture (府), thoughtlessly pisses and goes.
From nine in the morning until five in the afternoon on this main road, like a spring (『바네』온가티), it follows the watering cart driver's rear end, going back and forth without rest.

As if railroad tracks (鐵路) were laid—
It is an endless road. His life too, his daily work.

The watering cart driver's body is a broken radiator (『라듸에-터』).
Having lost its cool, the secretions (分泌物) of his skin cling like homesickness (鄕愁) to his bare soles—having lent his rubber shoes to his wife going to sell rice cakes—on the way back.

That road, always cool like Ramune (『라무네』),
the Chevrolet (『씨보레-』) going along wagging its numbered black tail is utterly a prostitute (賣淫婦).

Listing its hidden contents (包藏物):
First, there are eyeglasses. Then, some foreign books (原書), shoes, and a base creature (蠢動物) composed of protein (蛋白質), lime (石灰), moisture (水分), etc.

The surprising thing is the existence called Doctor (博士), a composite (合成物) of these things, looked at the clock—where did he learn that?—and felt the urgency (切迫) of the broadcast time (放送時間).

After all, that there are likely no tears inside that substance (物質)
is where we, the spectators' (觀衆), opinions agreed.

Today too, the watering cart driver
sows seeds of liquid, of sweat and water, upon this street. Wordlessly, pressing his nose to the earth (大地),
farming work (農事) he might never harvest—

But the burning beak of the sun, that pest bird (害鳥),
gobbles it all up before it even falls (떠러도).

Grabbing June—
the damned sun's two cheeks (두빰) just bulge (뽈룩하기만 하다).
When will that bastard's sickness be cured (달너날가)? <85>

**Summary**

This poem vividly portrays the grueling, repetitive labor of a water cart driver in colonial Seoul, contrasting his dehumanized existence with the indifference of nature (the oppressive sun) and the privileged elite (a doctor in a Chevrolet). The driver is depicted as a "broken radiator," endlessly spraying water ("pissing") on the hot streets, his sweat mingling with the water in a futile effort likened to farming barren ground. The poem uses harsh, cynical language to critique the social disparities and the exhausting reality of urban labor under modernization, symbolized by the water cart, the Chevrolet, and the radio broadcast time the doctor is concerned about. Historians may find the poem valuable for its raw depiction of lower-class urban life, its implicit social critique of class divisions during the colonial period, its use of modern technology symbols (car, radiator, radio) juxtaposed with manual labor, and its reflection on the harshness of the physical environment adding to the worker's burden.

필자	片石村
기사형태	시

撒水車
						片石村
大學病院의 뾰죽집의 時計가
아츰 아홉 時-ㄴ데

망할 자식-
太陽은 街路의 上空에 잡바저 빨가케 성이 낫다

最近의 그 자식도 성만 나면 地球를 성가시게 구는 病이 잇서서
아주 엇질줄 모를 막난이야

오늘도 府의 烙印을 걸머진 撒水車는 주착업시 오좀을 싸고 간다.
이 큰길을 午前아홉時부터 午後다섯시까지 『바네』온가티 撒水車夫의 궁뎅이로 쪼츠며 쉴새업시 往復한다.

鐵路를 노앗스면 하도록
그것은 끗이 업는 길이다. 그의 人生도 每日의 일도

撒水車夫의 몸동아리는 깨여진 『라듸에-터』다
冷靜을 일허버린 그의 皮膚의 分泌物은 도라오는 길에는 떡팔러가는 안해에게 고무신을 빌려준 벌거버슨 그의 발바닥에 鄕愁와 가티 달러붓는다

언제던지 『라무네』와 가티 써늘한 그길을
番號를 단 검은 꼬리를 내저으며가는 『씨보레-』는 전혀 賣淫婦다

그 내부의 包藏物을 列擧하면
爲先 眼鏡이 잇다. 그러고 若干의 原書와 구두와 蛋白質과 石灰와 水分等으로 構成된 蠢動物이 잇다.

놀라운 일은 이것들의 合成物인 博士라는 存在는 어대서 배홧는지 時計를 처다보고는 放送時間의 切迫을 늣겻다.

畢竟 그 物質의 內部에는 눈물이 업스리라는데
우리들 觀衆의 意見은 一致되엿다.

오늘도 撒水車夫는
이 거리 우헤 땀과 물의 液體의 씨를 뿌리며 간다. 말 업시 大地 우헤 코를 박고
언제 거들지도 모르는 農事를-

하지만 害鳥인 太陽의 타는 주둥아리는
떠러도 지기전에 그것을 다 집어 먹는다

六月을 잡아서는
망할 자식 太陽의 두빰은 뽈룩하기만 하다.
어느때가 되면 그 자식의 病은 달너날가? <85>
