Magazine Title: 三千里
Publication Date: 1931년 07월 01일
Article Title: △中國人士에게 謹告 (A Respectful Notice to Chinese People)
Author: 三千里社
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Notice/Editorial Postscript)

Regarding the unintentional incidents (非本意의 事變) that occurred in early July in P'yŏngyang, Inch'ŏn, Kyŏngsŏng, and other various places on the Peninsula (半島), which caused unfortunate disasters (不祥의 災害) to our neighboring Chinese compatriots (隣邦 中國同胞) with whom we have maintained close affection (至密한 親愛) and friendship (友誼), this magazine, Samch'ŏlli (三千里), representing tens of thousands of readers (數萬讀者), offers deep apologies (深謝) and condolences (慰問). Furthermore, we hope that the friendship (友愛) between our two peoples (民族) will be eternal (永遠).

July, Year Sinmi (1931)
Samch'ŏlli Sa

**Summary**

This editorial note from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* expresses deep apologies and condolences to the Chinese people for violent incidents targeting them in P'yŏngyang, Inch'ŏn, Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), and elsewhere in Korea in early July 1931. The magazine, writing on behalf of its readers, calls the events "unintentional" and "unfortunate disasters," reaffirming the traditional friendship between Koreans and Chinese and expressing hope for its eternal continuation. For historians, this article is significant as a contemporary Korean public response (published under Japanese colonial rule) to the anti-Chinese riots that erupted across Korea following the Wanpaoshan Incident in Manchuria. It reflects an attempt, possibly constrained by censorship, to smooth relations and express regret for the violence against Chinese residents in Korea.
