Rumbling Thunder 殷其雷
The rumbling sound of thunder cries 殷其雷
Still unable to leave that mountain top 在南山之陽
Why have you set off on the road like this? 何斯違斯
I don't think you lack the time 莫敢或遑
Oh, this beautiful master of my house 振振君子
Come back! Why don't you come back? 歸哉歸哉

The rumbling sound of thunder cries 殷其雷
Still unable to pass by that mountainside 在南山之側
Why have you set off on the road like this? 何斯違斯
I don't think you lack the time 莫敢或遑
Oh, this beautiful master of my house 振振君子
Come back! Why don't you come back? 歸哉歸哉 <224>

The rumbling sound of thunder cries 殷其雷
Still unable to subside below that mountain 在南山之下
Why have you set off on the road like this? 何斯違斯
I don't think you lack the time 莫敢或遑
Oh, this beautiful master of my house 振振君子
Come back! Why don't you come back? 歸哉歸哉

Dew on the Path 行露
Bright, bright dew shining on the path 厭浥行露
Though there's nothing to dislike morning or evening 豈不夙夜
Why is this path so dewy? 謂行多露

Don't say the sparrow has no horns 誰謂雀無角
If it has none, how could it pierce my eaves? 何以穿我屋
You, a man, have not performed the betrothal rites (納幣) 誰謂女無家
Why do you bring a lawsuit, calling me your wife? 何以速我獄
Though I face a lawsuit, I have no worries 雖速我獄
Even in dreams, there is no way I will become your wife 室家不足

Don't say the rat has no molars 誰謂鼠無牙
If it has none, how could it pierce my wall? 何以穿牙墉 <225>
You, a man, have not performed the betrothal rites (納幣) 誰謂女無家
Why do you bring a lawsuit, calling me your wife? 何以速我獄
Though I face a lawsuit, I have no worries. 雖速我訟
Even in dreams, there is no way I will become your wife 亦不如湜

Plums are Falling 有梅
White plum blossoms are falling 標有梅
Leaving fruit not even seven *bak* 其實七兮
You who think me precious 求我庶土
Choose a good day and take me away 迨其吉兮

White plum blossoms are falling 標有梅
Leaving fruit not even three *bak* 其實三兮
You who think me precious 求我庶土
The time has come now, so take me away. 迨其今兮

White plum blossoms are falling 標有梅
Let's go gather them with a basket (광우리) 頃筐墍之
You who think me precious 求我庶土
Keep your promise, time is passing. -其謂兮

Small Stars 小星
Twinkling, twinkling small stars in the dark sky 暳彼小星
As the Three and Five stars shine in the east 三五在東
How pitiful! My body, after the night has passed 肅肅宵征
I leave my lord's place before dawn 夙夜在公
Oh, the sad fate (身勢) of a body different from the Queen's 寔命不同

Twinkling, twinkling small stars in the dark sky 暳彼小星
As Orion shines in the western sky 維參與昻
How pitiful! Oh, my body, after the night has passed 肅肅宵征
I return, carrying the bedding (이부자리) 抱衾與裯
Oh, the sad fate (身勢) of a body not like the Queen's 寔命不猶 <227>

**Summary**

This article features Korean translations of four poems from the ancient Chinese *Classic of Poetry* (*Shijing*), provided by the translator An Sŏ (岸曙). The poems—"Rumbling Thunder," "Dew on the Path," "Plums are Falling," and "Small Stars"—are presented with their original Chinese lines alongside the Korean renditions. The themes range from longing for an absent husband and rejection of an unwanted suitor to the urgency of marriage and the lament of a royal consort. For historians, this publication in *Samch'ŏlli*, a major popular magazine, reflects the continued engagement with classical East Asian literature within colonial Korean society and the effort to render these classics into contemporary Korean vernacular, complete with 1930s orthography. The translation choices and the identity of "An Sŏ" might offer insights into cultural production and intellectual currents of the time.
