Article Title	玄淑 (Hyŏnsuk)

玄淑
						羅蕙錫 (Na Hyesŏk)

The two met after half a year.
The man had been invited by the woman, but he had been waiting for such an opportunity anyway. Of course, they talked about various things, the words of their peers.
Now, facing her, her fragrant, rich cheeks, lips like azalea flowers, breath warm like mahogany – things he had long forgotten – gave him unparalleled excitement.
She certainly wasn't the woman from half a year ago. Her figure, capable of digesting the desires of any member of the opposite sex, seemed poised, her beckoning index finger waiting without restraint **.
"...Anyway, your attitude was tasteless. Quitting A Trading Company after just three days, becoming a waitress at a cafe..."
"...It's because I couldn't stand it even one more day. It's because being a waitress seemed suitable for me. And I went daily as a model to the Western painter Mr. K's house. Mr. K, well, Mr. K always talks about his work only to you, Sir.<239> The teacher often makes me uncomfortable, but there's much work that can't be done without me..."
"Ah, is that so. Well, let's drink."
He directed the woman's attention to the black tea placed there.
"And these days, I've become a cash register—simple, effective, clear—100% response somewhere, hahaha..."
Seeming slightly frivolous, she blew on the hot tea two or three times*
With an expression that seemed to say, "You must have been quite lonely during the half-year you were away from me, right? Come back to me now," she looked squarely into the man's face.
The man facing the woman—complexion fair at 23, nape of the neck darkish, clothes fitting well—was a reporter for some newspaper. It was still early morning, nine o'clock, at a small coffee shop (喫茶店) near Namdaemun Station.
"I brought a good plan (푸랑) today. But you mustn't harbor the same heavy jealousy as before. Haven't you gotten sick of it already?"
"Well, I don't know! Since you say you won't step into it this time, does it matter what it is?" He smiled briefly.
The woman's plan was about a coffee shop (喫茶店) currently for sale. The location was Chongno 1-ch'ŏm. She wanted to take it over and manage it, but needed 400 won. Thus, one share (口) was 10 won, with interested parties (有志) requested to subscribe for 10 shares or more. She had waited for an appropriate night when just the two of them could discuss this.
"Isn't it better to work with people you've been close to until now? So, how many shares have been taken?"
"About 25 or 26 shares. Everyone just talks about the recession."
"So, how many people are involved?" The man opened his eyes wide.
"That's the thing, it's a gentleman's agreement (紳士契約)! Everyone thinks they are the only one! As for you, you haven't lost anything from the start, right?" Haha... The woman burst into laughter.
"But didn't you just say I live like a resistor? So, for example, for a man with 10 shares, about 10 shares' worth, and for a man with 20 shares..."
"Not very smart—if you dislike service (싸부이스) so much, you should take the maximum number of shares. So, just make it 30 shares. Paying in two installments is fine too... How about it? Huh?"
"Someone was saying you found a good patron (빠도통)? How enviable it is to have a patron!"
"Well, not really. Just a bourgeois (뿌르조아) old man (翁) occasionally takes me to Chŏngja House Restaurant (丁子屋 食堂) for lunch, that's all."
He thought about that old man. It seemed like a lump sum of money might materialize if he spoke to him—The woman realized the man had approved her plan. And so, she tossed several letters she had onto the table.<240>
"Are there love letters (을너브 을네터) there too?" the man asked.
"Yes, there are many love letters, but that's not the issue. I want you to write replies for me—these days, I can't write even a single line or phrase in response to letters from pure-hearted young men. Even if I try to think of phrases and write well, I can't. Please? Write them for me! I beg you!"
The woman wasn't lying. Indeed, she couldn't write even a line or phrase, and had dragged out replying until today.
Meanwhile, the man read one letter. It was from a man lodging in the same place as the woman.
"I speak of my love for you. I have endured for a long time already, but I cannot endure it any longer. Even this heartfelt letter, I will not decide tonight but wait for tomorrow..."
That was the gist.
The man frowned. He pulled a fountain pen from his pocket. Simultaneously, the woman quickly took letter paper (을네터 페이퍼) from her handbag and, so he could write immediately, described her current lodging life. The young man was in the room below, she was in the room next to his, and next to her room was an Old Poet (老詩人). The young man came up from the countryside two or three months ago and is preparing for submission to the Sŏn Exhibition (鮮展); she asked him to write wishing him success by all means.
The reporter smoothed the creases on the letter paper and began writing. His perception was indeed sharp.
"Like you, I love you too, but I am busy day and night seeking a room (팡). Even writing this letter now, I do not have ample time." Thus, meticulously, he wrote the letter using feminine language.
"Make a clean copy of this."
"Yes, it turned out well. I'll make a clean copy. You really are a liar."
"You, who utilizes that liar, are the bigger liar."
The woman read the reply 쭉쭉 (quickly). At the end, it said, "Missing you from a friend's lodging." After that... writing something like that would be funny, so he added such a line.
"Hey, if you write something like that, I'll die."
"It won't be like that, this is fine. He'll read this reply breathing heavily. His heart will be wounded. If you had written 'at that time,' it would be funny. At that precise moment (딱닥디리면), he'll finally be able to breathe deeply."
"What, if read the other way, the heart gets agitated (동*하는)!"
"Since that's your handwriting (肉筆), he'll think this is written by someone else <241> and won't trust it."
"Then isn't it better not to reply at all? That properly expresses the feeling as it is." The more the woman thought about the young man's racing heart, the more she ultimately wanted to head in the opposite direction. And so, she put the folded letter paper into a Western envelope (서양봉투).
Could he have changed this much? The man's eyes took out his watch.
"Let's meet tonight around 7 o'clock at the Chongno intersection," he said. The two stood up.

2
The OO boarding house (하숙) in Anguk-tong was dim on a cloudy, rainy autumn day. The Old Poet's room was piled high with old newspapers and magazines, leaving no space to step. The poet himself sat in the middle, using a traveling trunk (行李) instead of a desk, talking loudly to three students accompanying him. The three students, wearing the uniforms of a provincial higher common school, addressed the poor yet famous Old Poet with utmost respect*
"Sir, the score for the school song (校歌譜) you composed for us half a year ago was finally completed recently. It was composed by Mr. S. Today, we came as representatives of the student association (校友會) to report to you, Sir. We plan to go and announce it to all the students immediately. Sir, we will sing it."
The three respectfully sang the school song in low voices. The Old Poet, with a drunken face, marked the beat (拍子) with his second finger. But frankly speaking, the Old Poet had completely forgotten he had composed it, listening as if to someone else's song. However, excited by their fluent singing, there were two or three phrases (個所) he remembered.
"Yes, that! That! That's definitely it!" The Old Poet stroked his bald head and nodded repeatedly.
"It's truly a fine melody. I adore Byron. This school song has the scent of Byron's poetry—Please sing it once more. Let me learn it with you."
Encouraged by the Old Poet's passionate words, the students' voices grew louder and higher. The Old Poet became increasingly elated. At that moment, the young painter in a thin, stained shirt, who had been completely ignored in a corner until then, abruptly stood up.
"Sir, let me treat you."
He opened the torn window and placed the 5 or 6 bottles of beer (삐루) he had put there before the Old Poet's trunk.
"Mr. L, thank you for your trouble. It's alright to drink, right?" The Old Poet squinted and said yes.
"Mr. L, I'll have a lot of drunken complaints for you later."<242>
The Old Poet had become a model (모텔) for Mr. L. Their schedules hadn't matched for 3 or 4 days, and today, meeting the students put him in a good mood, so he had bought beer for the modeling fee (모텔료) in advance. Of course, Mr. L, wanting to please the Old Poet, brought out all the beer he had brought.
At the Old Poet's urging, the students each had a glass. The Old Poet tried to make them stay longer, but they said they had to leave, so the Old Poet, staggering drunkenly (醉步), followed the three out to the street (街道).
Mr. L was left alone. The chaotically strewn old newspapers were rough. L drank, muttering
"...Youths full of hope..." two or three times under his breath, then again felt the misfortune of his present, where his own hope was distant.
Hyŏnsuk's reply (返信)... Why hadn't he gauged Hyŏnsuk's feelings sooner? Because he hadn't, he had inquired about her heart. And wasn't Hyŏnsuk's reply sent to mock him like that? Thinking about it that way, he couldn't possibly be angry (怒할) with her.
"Hyŏnsuk says she's very busy, just like in her letter. But Hyŏnsuk's reputation (세평) is very bad."
With an aching (압흔) heart, he felt regret over the occasional rumors about Hyŏnsuk that reached his ears.
The current situation, where the Old Poet, Hyŏnsuk, and himself were living together like family (親身) in the same lodging house, though coincidental, was sometimes uncomfortable. The Old Poet, whenever drunk and out of money for alcohol, would starve for days (몃칠이라도).
"A gave me poetry. He talks as if alcohol has drained all his energy, but even at this age, my blood is still hot."
Having remained single past 50, he wore a desolate expression.
Hyŏnsuk had once bought the Old Poet's poetry collection from a bookstore (冊店) and read it devotedly (愛讀한), so she paid attention to his well-being and, whenever she had money, would invariably buy alcohol and urge him to drink. Thus, the lonely Old Poet's life became brighter thanks to Hyŏnsuk's kindness. Consequently, the lives of the three became inseparable. This year, expecting L to be selected for the Sŏn Exhibition (鮮展), the Old Poet had become his model.
"Who would normally act as a model, but for you, I'll make an exception—So, will you give me alcohol every day? If you paint me gulping it down, I'll feel spirited."
And so, L made a last stand (背水의 陳). He thought he would throw away his brush (畵筆) if he failed the exhibition this year. He pawned (典當하야) his read books and clothes, bought a No. 50 canvas (림파스), art supplies (화구), and two dozen (두다스) beers. It wasn't beer season, but just seeing the beer <243> excited his spirits.
2 hours a day, 3 bottles of beer. Painting title (畵題): "Y, the Old Ruined Poet." It was selected by the Old Poet himself. For the first 4 or 5 days, things proceeded according to plan, and good progress (호색이 났다) was made. The Old Poet, after drinking the prescribed 3 bottles, would say,
"Ah—that was delicious," and go outside. Hyŏnsuk, the one among the three lodgers from whom a gentle breeze always blew, said,
"Yes? Sir, I know how to sew too. Sir's clothes are dirty (더렀어요)."
As Hyŏnsuk spoke, she peered into the messy room, then darted to L's side like wind blowing on a hill. L, intoxicated by her charm, tumbled around again playfully (둥글둥글 뒤굴었다).
"I opened your door just a little while ago (조곰악가). You were writing something like a diary. Everyone thinks so, right? Huh? So, the reply (返信) I sent was quite interesting (滋味있었지), wasn't it? Honestly, accounting (회계) over emotion, that accounting thing... Huh? What are you thinking about... It's good for the entrance to love to start from accounting. Truly, I've failed everything by entering through emotion until now. Therefore, dealing with a pure-hearted young man like you is the most difficult and scary thing."
"I am satisfied just lodging together with you, Miss Hyŏnsuk."
"But Mr. L, I plan to leave this house soon (근일 내로)."
"..."
"Such a disappointed expression. You mustn't be disappointed. I have shed many tears, but I haven't despaired. Now, I'll give you and the teacher good news—I'm planning something very interesting right now. I have to go out again. There's something I need to forget (이저바릴) for a bit." Once more, Hyŏnsuk adjusted the hair falling on her neck, narrowed her pretty (입분) eyes, and preened (꿈이고) herself before the mirror.
"I'll be back this evening," she muttered to herself and went out the main gate.

3
The next morning (翌朝), the Old Poet woke early (일즉이) and was smoking a cigarette when he heard footsteps. Thinking it might be the woman, he asked,
"Is that Hyŏnsuk?"
But Hyŏnsuk didn't answer and went into her own room.
"Drunk again. Something must have happened to the teacher again." Saying this, L became very worried and peeked through the door crack. The teacher came out and went to Hyŏnsuk's room. Hyŏnsuk lay down on the bedding L had spread out for her and stared <244> at the ceiling, saying,
"Sir, may I drink alcohol too? How I wanted to drink... Yes? Sir, what should I do?"
Before finishing her words, she turned sideways and sobbed. Hyŏnsuk wanted to forget (이즈려고) the unpleasant events from last night until this morning... Painter Mr. K had also broken the new contract with Hyŏnsuk. This was also because he couldn't help imagining 4 or 5 men behind her plan. More than that, someone had spread out bedding in her room upon her return.
"Thank you! Thank you. Sir, please tell him to remember these tears of mine."
Whether she was crying from the bitterness of drunkenness or from loneliness, the Old Poet couldn't tell at all (도모지), but anyway, he went outside, filled a basin with water, came back, soaked a towel, and placed it on Hyŏnsuk's forehead. Hyŏnsuk mumbled that the cold water was dripping onto her neck and splashed the water away.
"Really doesn't know how," the Old Poet felt helpless.
Just then, L entered. He stood for a moment watching Hyŏnsuk's bizarre drunken state, then whispered to the Old Poet.
"Something happened with the Master (大家) K somewhere. It's somehow strange. K might do something like that. We need to find out for sure. In any case, we must prevent her from falling into ruin (타락만은)." The Old Poet looked sternly at Hyŏnsuk with a solemn expression.
The next afternoon, without consulting L, the Old Poet rushed to Master K's house in Sajik-tong. The Old Poet slowly brought up the subject of Hyŏnsuk.
"Hyŏnsuk has changed a lot lately. Considering various things, you must take responsibility for Hyŏnsuk. Didn't she drink here late into the night yesterday?"
"No, you seem to have misunderstood something."
The plump and dignified K touched his balding (갈는) head unpleasantly and asked,
"What exactly do you mean by 'responsibility'?"
"Should you be asking me that?"
"No matter how I look at it, you seem mistaken. That Hyŏnsuk knows various painters and receives 3 won, 5 won, 10 won as a model fee. I can't say I know nothing, but Hyŏnsuk should certainly not place responsibility solely on me. No, she couldn't possibly say that."
"Don't make such excuses. Hyŏnsuk is a modest woman. If you are a man, how about guiding that woman onto a decent path? Seeing Hyŏnsuk return this morning (오날 아참에), I felt she must have become ruined (墮落해진) because of that."
"This is truly strange. I bear no such responsibility. There were several men behind Hyŏnsuk; she shouldn't be in difficulty.<245> You blame only me, but do you even have the right to do so?"
"What?"
The Old Poet's cheeks flushed red, and he abruptly stood up from his chair.
"Anyway, leave. If it's about money—"
K pulled out a crumpled banknote from his slightly stained vest (족긔). It was a 10-won note.
"These days, your poetry is outdated and doesn't sell well, so you must be looking for something to latch onto, I suppose. Humph."
Hearing this, the Old Poet flared up like fire. Tearing the banknote K offered and throwing it onto the desk, he simultaneously knocked over the chair and stormed out the door. The Old Poet's chest (가삼) pounded.
"He insults not only Hyŏnsuk but me as well. Just wait and see. I won't let him get away with pretending to be a master (大家인체 하난꼴)—I can't just stand by while he trifles with someone's daughter—"
Fuming with rage (노긔등등하야), he went into a nearby bar and drank for 4 or 5 hours. When the Old Poet finally emerged onto the street, he was staggering drunkenly (근들엉근들엉). By the time he returned to his lodging, it was already past midnight. Hyŏnsuk and L were both awake, struggling to fall asleep. The Old Poet arrived at the threshold supported by someone else, his face and head bandaged, his overcoat (두루막이) and socks covered in mud. A neighbor explained that he had fortunately been pulled out of some ditch he had fallen into. Hyŏnsuk got up from where she lay, wiped the Old Poet's limbs clean, and helped him into bed. While doing so, the Old Poet mumbled half-consciously (반엄을 거리는 소리로),
"That bastard, that bastard wasn't anything special either—Once he sheds the artist's mask, I'll peel his skin (껍*을) right off."
Watching him repeat the curse, Hyŏnsuk knew instinctively.
"The teacher must have gone to Mr. K's house."
And Hyŏnsuk unexpectedly felt tears welling up, unable to stop them. As Hyŏnsuk changed the Old Poet into his nightclothes, she wiped her tears. For some reason, dirt got into her eye. It was from the hem of the Old Poet's overcoat. Hyŏnsuk laughed.
"What's funny (우수워)?" The Old Poet snapped open his heavy, drunken eyes.
"Look at this (브서요). I somehow wiped my tears with the hem of your overcoat, Sir. Look here (보서요). Isn't there dirt all over it like this?"
Hyŏnsuk rolled around (대골대골 굴느며) laughing. L, beside them, joined in, chuckling softly.
The next morning (翌朝에), Hyŏnsuk stood with a pale face (얼골로), looking dazed, staring out the window.<246> Just then, the Old Poet came in, still in his nightclothes, and spoke in a fatherly tone,
"Poverty (가난*란) is truly苦勞 hardship, isn't it? Having to bow your head to dog-like bastards, having to do things you hate—Yes, thinking about things woke me up early. Hyŏnsuk, too, from now on, shouldn't associate with useless men." He spoke with effort.
"Yes? Sir, I don't mind hardship (苦勞하지 안해요). I live recklessly (엉벙하고). Isn't there no other way to live?"
"Mm, that's right."
"Therefore, I am more composed than you think, Sir... This woman called me doesn't want to cry unless it's for something grateful. I don't cry just because someone is cruel (야숙하게)!"
"Mm, because we are paupers, we cry when we need to cry. When we cry, our hearts empty out somewhat!"
And so, the Old Poet smiled, as if understanding the young woman's heart. He returned to his room to sleep a bit more. Hyŏnsuk, having slept enough, got up to put on makeup, unable to help muttering,
"What a wonderful teacher he is." ...The things the teachers do without saying anything are amusing, but if the teacher knew about my current life... I worry needlessly, but the teacher might despair over me...

4
It was the afternoon several days later.
"Sir!" Hyŏnsuk said while packing her belongings.
"I am constantly walking diligently towards hope. So, even if I leave here, please don't worry. I believe there will be cause for celebration within a few days."
Hyŏnsuk said she would let them know her address later and quietly moved out.
Though expected, L was truly disappointed. The Old Poet, just drinking and coming and going, ultimately failed as L's model. L, waiting daily for a letter from Hyŏnsuk, received a thick letter without an address or sender's name. Opening it, he found two envelopes inside. One had L's name written on it, and the other had nothing written, just "Bearer (持參人): Mr. L."
L first (爲先) opened the one addressed to him. It said something about wanting to meet the recipient (大兄 - big brother, a respectful term) regarding Hyŏnsuk; Hyŏnsuk is passionate if it's the 'brother'. Come tomorrow afternoon at 3 o'clock to the marked place (표기처로) with the enclosed letter...
L didn't know what scheme (웬심인지) this was. But guessing that Hyŏnsuk's recent situation was hidden within this letter, he didn't know what else to do (엇절 줄을 몰나) and went to the designated place (지정소로) before 3 PM the next day.<247>
Arriving there, he indeed found the designated place and knocked (두다렷다) on the door. Listening closely, he heard sounds from inside, and shortly (未久에) the door opened. As he was thinking it was a stranger (몰느는 남자), the person standing right in front of him was Hyŏnsuk. Ah! Startled, the two stood staring at each other.
"Ah! It was you? Who told you (가라처 줍데가) to come here? How did you come here when I didn't even tell you! What is this!" Hyŏnsuk spoke with displeasure.
As L took a step forward to explain he came because of the addressless, nameless letter, Hyŏnsuk suddenly slammed the door shut. And so, L hastily threw the strange letter in front of Hyŏnsuk.
The door closed (닷첫다). L stood blankly before the door for 3 or 4 minutes. Meeting Hyŏnsuk unexpectedly here, Hyŏnsuk being very angry (노한)... He didn't know what scheme (웬세음인지) this was... What on earth is going on...? Hyŏnsuk seems to misunderstand something, or else she's disregarding friendship too much... He thought about knocking again and confronting her, but he hesitated (들떠섰다), feeling powerless, about to turn back. Just then, from behind,
"Wait! Mr. L," someone called.
L didn't look back. Hyŏnsuk, who had chased after him, grabbed L's hand and pulled him into the room.
"Look here (여보서요), Mr. L. I had someone I absolutely had to meet at three, so I didn't have time to talk with you. But then I found out that person sent you instead. Come on in quickly. I have much to tell you."
And so, urged by Hyŏnsuk, L entered. The single room seemed very cramped, perhaps because furniture was placed there. The southern window let in light, filling the room with a summer feeling. Hyŏnsuk, wearing a light (*은) jeogori and a navy skirt (감장치마), sat and told L to come sit beside her. A wicker chair (藤椅子) was placed next to her.
"This is my bedroom and study. How is it? Quiet and nice, isn't it?... I don't just invite anyone into this room."
L turned on the light and glanced around the room. Unlike the Old Poet's adjacent room, this place was bright and tidy (정하였다). A handsome dressing table stood there, its mirror shining like a prized possession (家財), with cosmetic bottles large and small (大小) arranged neatly. For some reason (엇전지), L felt uneasy seeing this.
"...Look here (여보서요). I realized after seeing this letter. I ran out thinking you had left. It turned out well, you coming instead. This letter went to you, right? This person is someone I already broke ties with. Please read this letter. Yes?"
Hyŏnsuk forced L to look at the letter he had thrown down. The 3 or 4 pages were crumpled. It looked like Hyŏnsuk had crushed them in her grip.<248>
"My dear Hyŏnsuk!
I suddenly have to go to the Yŏngnam region. I will come up to Seoul occasionally. But the interesting (滋味스러운) times the two of us spent together until now have become impossible. Moreover, I am sad that I cannot make it tomorrow afternoon at 3 o'clock either.
But I thought about it. I thought of the young man Hyŏnsuk likes, the young man she loves, Mr. L. You love L, but in your current life, you are avoiding getting close to him. And so, I thought I would bring you and Mr. L closer together.
Miss Hyŏnsuk, doesn't L naturally have this much right (귄리)? This is L's vested right (旣得權) to possess you forever from this work. I intend to execute this vested right. Surely Miss Hyŏnsuk will clap her hands (손벽을) and rejoice in L's right. If you are human too, I imagine this will please you, and I smile inwardly.
Miss Hyŏnsuk! I brought this letter with that meaning. I now offer my wholehearted (滿腔의) blessings for the two of you. Bravo! Bravo!"
Hyŏnsuk stood beside L, who was reading the letter by the window. Her intense, ignited (點火한) eyes stared blankly (멀건이) at L's eyes, which were following the characters. Thinking of (생각키여) the pleasant moment when L's dark, fresh eyes swiftly scanned the sloping surface (傾斜面) in one go (一氣), [her gaze] rushed towards Hyŏnsuk [L? - Text says Hyŏnsuk, likely typo for L].
The two embraced. As if the trigger (契機) had been set long ago. "Yes? I couldn't have imagined that the entrance of accounting I mentioned would bring happiness to us two so quickly. Our feelings for each other were already fully prepared! So now, it doesn't matter how excessive our emotions are. Mr. L! I won't call you Mr. L anymore. Instead, I'll call out Bravo for you. Bravo! Bravo!"
However, a large lump seemed stuck in L's throat (咽喉). It was a joy unknown until now. He had no choice but to swallow it now.
"...And you, please come here at 3 PM!"
"I'll always leave the key with the landlord, so the two of us can't live here. Please comfort the Old Teacher well. Yes? It's best not to tell the teacher about what happened between us for the time being. Let's have a secret relationship for half a year. After half a year, we'll need to think again about a new contract. We need to prepare for that in advance first (爲先)."
"It's funny (우숩지) if you put it that way."
L smiled, trembling with desolate joy.
Of course, there was no need to prepare for such things in advance. Hyŏnsuk spread her arms wide and bravely extended her warm hands towards L. (End) <249>

**Summary**

This short story by Na Hyesŏk depicts the complex relationships and struggles of Hyŏnsuk, a young woman navigating life in 1930s Seoul, and the men around her: a journalist, a young aspiring painter (L), an impoverished Old Poet, and unseen patrons. Hyŏnsuk, having left jobs and become a model, seeks financial independence by planning to open a coffee shop, soliciting investments from multiple men simultaneously under a "gentleman's agreement." She uses the journalist to write replies to love letters she can no longer bring herself to answer. Meanwhile, L, who loves Hyŏnsuk, struggles with his art and poverty, using the Old Poet as a model fueled by beer. The Old Poet confronts painter K about his treatment of Hyŏnsuk, resulting in humiliation. Hyŏnsuk eventually moves out, leaving L desolate until a contrived letter from another man (likely the journalist or K) orchestrates a meeting between L and Hyŏnsuk, leading to their union, framed by Hyŏnsuk as the "accounting" finally aligning with emotion.

From a colonial history perspective, the story offers glimpses into the precarious lives of artists and intellectuals (the poet, the painter L, the journalist) facing economic hardship ("recession," pawning items) and navigating social hierarchies (reliance on patrons, the "Master" K). Hyŏnsuk's pragmatic, almost transactional approach to relationships ("accounting over emotion," seeking multiple investors) reflects both a potentially shifting view of female agency and the desperate measures potentially required for survival or advancement in a constrained environment. The use of loanwords (푸랑, 삐루, 빠도통, 을너브 을네터) indicates cultural mixing and the influence of Western/Japanese modernity. The Sŏn Exhibition (鮮展) points to the official art scene operating within the colonial structure.
