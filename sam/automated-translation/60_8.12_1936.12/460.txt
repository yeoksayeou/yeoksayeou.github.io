A Letter to Our Esteemed Readers

As the Pyŏngja Year (丙子年) is drawing to a close, we present this as the year-end issue. Just as all human lives move step by step from youth to young adulthood, from young adulthood to maturity, and from maturity to old age, so too do nations and peoples take a step forward using the year's end as an opportunity. They grow one step at a time, become braver one step at a time. We cannot know what sentiments the sound of this year's New Year's Eve bell (除夜), carrying this eventful peninsula's mountains, rivers, society, and populace, will leave us with, nor what dreams it will bid us dream for tomorrow as it departs.

On the occasion of sending off the eighth year of our magazine, *Samch'ŏlli* (三千里), and preparing to greet the ninth year with the upcoming issue, I wish to express a fragment of my deep feelings. During the eight years this magazine has walked its path, we stood alongside colleague magazines (僚誌) such as *Kaebyŏk* (開闢), *Chosŏn chi Kwang* (朝鮮之光), *Tonggwang* (東光), *Cheil Sŏn* (第一線), *Pyŏl Kŏn'gon* (別乾坤), *Tongbang P'yŏngnon* (東方評論), *Taejo* (大潮), and *Chosŏn Mundan* (朝鮮文壇). Sharing in their joys and sorrows like flourishing pines and happy cypresses (松茂栢悅), we spread our silver wings side-by-side on the frontline of our cultural movement. Yet now, there is no way to find their traces, and we are left only with a few journals like *Pip'an* (批判), *Chogwang* (朝光), *Sin Tonga* (新東亞), *Chungang* (中央), *Sahae Kongnon* (四海公論), *Yadam* (野談), *Wŏlgan Yadam* (月刊野談), and *Chosŏn Munhak* (朝鮮文學) as companions. Just as the flickering fortunes (明滅) of human affairs are said to be unpredictable (數奇), how numerous indeed have been the changes (何幾多變遷) in the fate of monthly magazines! Thus, our magazine has naturally become the longest-running one. As the editor, I feel unending shame (慚愧) and self-reflection (自省) for occupying this position without having made any particular contribution to Chosŏn (朝鮮) culture during that time.

When I founded this magazine eight years ago, I established these two principles and have strived to uphold them to this day:
"Let's not slander others; let's not expose the dark side of our people."
"Let's not receive financial aid from others."
The meaning of "Let's not slander others" was my vow to spare no effort, however small, to help bring forth as many "Giants, Heroes, Geniuses, Leaders" as possible in our society. The meaning of "Let's not expose the dark side of our people" was tied to the effort of striving, by consequence, to focus on excavating the brilliant ancient culture of old and introducing superior foreign peoples. Secondly, the meaning of "Let's not receive financial aid from others" was that, in order to protect the sanctity of the press enterprise, I would do everything with my sincerity, my effort, my conscience, and my talent and principles (才操) to prevent the distortion (丕曲) of journalism, and to carefully nurture this magazine without causing financial trouble (페) to others. As long as *Samch'ŏlli* continues to exist and I am overseeing this magazine, I intend to uphold these two principles as much as possible in the future.

Starting next month's issue, it will be the New Year. We intend to proceed with utmost sincerity in the spirit [or pride] (怜持) of the ninth year, so we ask all of you, our readers, to contribute good guidance, light, and strength for the growth and development of this magazine. <250>

**Summary**

The editor of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine reflects on completing its 8th year (1936) and entering its 9th. He notes the disappearance of many contemporary Korean magazines, leaving *Samch'ŏlli* as the oldest, a position he feels unworthy of. He reaffirms his founding principles: avoiding slander and negativity towards Koreans, focusing instead on promoting positive figures and cultural heritage, and maintaining financial independence to ensure journalistic integrity. He asks for continued reader support for the magazine's future. For historians, this piece offers insight into the precarious state of Korean publishing under Japanese rule, listing numerous defunct and surviving magazines. It also highlights the editor's conscious strategy of promoting national pride and avoiding internal criticism or exposing societal problems, likely a necessary survival tactic and a reflection of nationalist sentiment navigating censorship. The emphasis on financial independence points to concerns about external influence on the press.

讀者諸賢에 보내는 便紙
丙子年도 歲將暮己로 이것으로서 송년호를 삼슴니다. 모든 인생들도 소년에서 청년으로, 청년에서 장년으로, 장년에서 노쇠기로 한 계단식 옴겨 드딤에 따라 국가도 민족도 모다 歲暮를 기회 삼아 한 거름씩 내드딥니다. 한 거름씩 성장하고, 한 거름씩 용감하여저 감니다. 아지 못게라 多事하든 반도의 이 山川과 사회와 민중을 실을 이 한해의 除夜 소리는 엇든 감개를 우리들에게 남기고 또 장차 엇든 꿈을 내일날 우리들로 하여금 꾸게 하면서 가려 하는고.

본지 「三千里」의 제8년을 보내고 이제 來號로써 제9년을 맞이하려함에 際하야 나의 감개의 일단을 말코저 함니다. 본지 *8년 동안 거러오는 사이에 「開闢」 「朝鮮之光」 「東光 「第一線」 「別乾坤」 「東方評論」 「大潮」 「朝鮮文壇」 등 諸잡지를, 僚誌로서 볏하야 松茂栢悅, 그 희비를 가치하면서 우리 문화운동 선상에 銀翼을 나라니 펴고 있었더러니, 而今에 그 자최를 차즐길 없이되고, 오직 「批判」 「朝光」 「新東亞」 「中央」 「四海公論」 「野談」 「月刊野談」 「朝鮮文學」 등 數誌로 벗히게 되니 인세의 明滅도 數奇하다 하 ¤니와 월간잡지의 운명 또한 이엇지 何幾多變遷이런고 이리하야 본지 저절로 최고의 잡지였으니, 그 동안 朝鮮 文化에 공헌한 바 별로 없이 이 자리를 차지하게 되매 편집자로서 慚愧와 自省을 不己하는 바입니다.

나는 8년 전 이 잡지를 창간할 때에
「남을 헐지 말자, 민족의 암흑면을 들추지 말자」
「남에게서 자금 원조를 받지 말자」
이 두 가지의 신조를 세워 오늘날까지 직혀오느라고 애썻습니다. 남을 헐지 말자는 뜻은, 아모조록 「巨人, 英雄, 天才, 指導者」를 우리 사회에서 많이 나게 하도록, 나의 적은 노력이나마 한사코 앗기지 말자고 맹세한 것이요 민족의 암흑면을 들추지 말자는 뜻은 저절로 고대의 燦然*든 녯 문화의 발굴과, 諸外 우수 민족의 소개에 힘쓰잔 결과에 결부*느라 애썻고, 둘재 「남에게서 자금원조」를 받지 말자는 뜻은 언론사업의 신성을 직히기 위하야 내 정성과 내 노력과 내 양심과 내 才操를 다해서 그 언론의 丕曲을 막을 것이요 다른 분에게 금전상 페를 끼치지 말고 고히고히 이 잡지를 키워 나가려 하였든 것이외다. 이 두 신조를 앞로도 「三千里」의 생명이 지속되고 내가 이 잡지 일을 보고 있는 이상은 될수록 직혀가려 함니다.

來月號부터 신년이외다. 제9년의 怜持 속에서 가진 정성을 다하여 가려 하오니 여러분께서도 본지의 성장과 발전을 위하야, 좋은 길과 빗과 힘을 보태여 주옵소서.＜250＞
