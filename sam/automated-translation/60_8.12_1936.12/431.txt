Reason for Recommending This Work
						Lee Hyo-sŏk (李孝石)

Although I take up my pen at the editor's request, writing a recommendation letter is not my true intention. It is merely that, having happened to read the submitted work, I thought its quality was decent and its technique had reached the literary world's standard, so I took on the effort of presenting and introducing it. Therefore, the result is something for the readers themselves to judge; I am merely playing the role of an intermediary.

While it is not a large work, its skill is considerable, and most importantly, the effort put into expression and the clear traces of refinement are precious. Just because something is crude or rambling does not necessarily make it great literature; not treating even a single word or phrase carelessly should be the upright attitude of a literary person. If one closely examines the great literature of the past, the misuse of characters does not catch the eye. Furthermore, in Flaubert (플로오벨) and Gide (지이드), we can see the model for this. The greater the subject matter, the more it should stand upon the foundation of polished characters. This is by no means a useless topic that everyone has already graduated from, and regarding this, I think that no matter how many words are spent, it is still insufficient.

Among those who submit work, the author is one of the most literary individuals in terms of cultivation, attitude, spirit, and passion. I hope that, along with refining expression, they will set their sights on larger and extraordinary subject matter and strive further on the path of literature. If this serves as an opportunity, there could be no greater fortune. This is what I wish for in brief words. <231>

**Summary**

Lee Hyo-sŏk (Yi Hyo-sŏk) explains his reasons for recommending an unnamed literary work. He emphasizes that while the piece isn't grand in scale, its technical skill (手法) and evident dedication to refining expression (表現의 鍊磨) make it worthy of introduction. He argues that meticulous attention to language is crucial for any writer, citing Flaubert and Gide as examples, and believes this focus is never outdated. He praises the author's literary attitude and encourages them to tackle more significant themes while continuing to polish their craft. From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into the literary standards and critical values emphasized in colonial Korea during the 1930s, particularly the high regard for technical refinement and careful expression. It also showcases the role of established writers like Yi Hyo-sŏk in mentoring emerging talents and shaping the literary scene.

이 作品推薦理由
						李孝石
編輯氏의 분부로 붓을들기는하나 推薦辭를씀이 本意는아니다 우연히 들어온 作品을 닑어본결과 된품이 웬만하고 手法이 문단수준에 올나다엇다고 생각함으로 發表紹介의 努를 마텃슴에 지나지안음으로 다 成果는 讀者가 스스로 判斷할것이요 나는 다만 仲介의 役割을한데 지나지안는 것이다.
큰 作品은 아니나 기수리 相當이며 무엇보다도 表現에애쓰고 鍊磨의자최가 또렷이 보임이 귀한일이다. 粗잡하고 잘황하다고반다시 大文學이아닐것이니 한마듸의 語句라도 허루로녁이지안음이 文學者의 謹直한테도여야된다. 過去의 大文學을 삿삿이들처야 文字의 濫用은 눈에뜨이지안는다. 더욱이 플로오벨이나 지이드에게서 우리는 그 典型을 볼수잇다. 偉大한 素材일수록에 탁마된 文字의 주초우에 서야할 것이다. 이것은 決코 누구가다졸업하야버린 쓸데업는 話題가아닌것이며 여긔에 關하야서는 아무리만흔말을 허비하야도 오히려 不足하다고 생각한다.
作者는 처저오는분들가온데에서 敎養, 態度 心操 熱情에잇서서 가장 文學的인한 사람이다. 모름즉이 表現의 鍊磨와 아울러 더크고 非凡한 素材에 着眼하야 文學道에 더욱 精進하기를 바란다. 이것이 한 機綠이 된한면 더 多幸한일은멀다. 短言으로 비는바이다.＜231＞
