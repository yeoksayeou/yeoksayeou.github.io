古典文學의 鑑賞과 硏究, 九雲夢의 價値
						梁白華

*Kuunmong* (九雲夢)—
As a novel, it is a masterpiece. This can be known from the fact that the names of the protagonist Sŏngjin (性眞) and the Eight Fairies (八仙女) have long been on the lips of old and young alike. Whether as a novel or a play, it has left a very deep impression on the Korean people. The writer, too, when he first obtained and read this novel in his youth, was truly中毒 (addicted) to it, so to speak, for several days. Who would not praise the romantic tale (艶史) of Yang Soyu (楊少游) enjoying his life with the Eight Beauties? However, at that time, the writer knew nothing of the ways of the world. Now, encountering this novel again, it is nothing more than a diversionary tale (消閑傳奇) written as a pastime (遊戱筆墨) by a literary man; it is literature of a bygone era. Although the original motive for writing this novel is said to have been to provide entertainment for the author's respected mother's boredom (심심푸리), it may indeed serve as a way to kill time (破閑거리), but it is not something to be placed on our desk (案頭) as a noble literary work. If there is anything we should take from this novel, it would be the charm of its prose (文章의 情趣) and its structure (構想); no matter how much the author, for convenience of production (製作便宜相), set the stage (舞臺) in China (中國), the conception (想) itself can be called grand. I hear that the original author was Sŏp'o Kim Manjung (西浦金萬重), and his grandson Pukkan Kim Ch'unt'aek (北幹金春擇) translated the original Hanmun (原 漢文) into the vernacular (諺譯하얏다고). <219>

**Summary**

The author, Yang Paekhwa, reflects on the classic Korean novel *Kuunmong*. While acknowledging its immense historical popularity and admitting his own youthful fascination with its romantic plot (Yang Soyu and the Eight Fairies), he now views it critically as merely an entertaining diversion written for amusement, lacking the depth of "noble" literature suitable for serious study. He concedes its literary merit lies in its prose style and grand conceptual scope, despite being set in China for convenience. He notes the accepted authorship of Kim Manjung and the vernacular translation by his grandson, Kim Ch'unt'aek. For historians, this article offers insight into the evolving literary critical standards in colonial Korea, the way classical works (even those with foreign settings) were being re-evaluated, and the tension between popular entertainment and perceived high literary value during that period. The mention of the work's enduring popularity also speaks to its cultural significance.

						梁白華
九雲夢-
小說로는 명작이다. 主人公 性眞이가 八仙女의 이름이 오래ㅅ동안 늙으니 젊으니닙에 올라단이는 것으로 보아 알 것이다. 小說로 劇으로 朝鮮 사람에게는 인상이 매우 깁흔 것이다. 筆者도 어렷슬 때에 이 小說은 처음 어더 보고 나서는 몃칠 동안 그야말로 중독이 되다십히 하얐섯다. 楊少游가 八 美人을 대리고 일생을 향락하든 그 艶史를 그 누가 稱*치 아니하랴. 그러나 筆者는 그 때에 世間의 물정을 도모지 몰랏든 것이다. 지금에 이 小說은 다시 대하면 文人의 遊戱筆墨으로 한 消閑傳奇에 지내지 못하는 것이며 지낸 날의 文學이다. 원래 이 小說을 지은 동기가 作者가 그 母夫人의 심심푸리에 供하기 위하야 지엇다고 하지마는 아닌 게 아니라 한 破閑거리는 될지언정 高貴한 文學作品으로 우리 案頭에 노하둘 것은 못된다. 다만 우리가 이 小說에서 취할 것이었다면 그 文章의 情趣와 構想이라고 할 것이니 아모리 作者가 製作便宜相 舞臺를 中國에 두엇다 할 지라도 그 想만은 크다고 할 것이다. 듯건대 原作者는 西浦金萬重인데 原 漢文을 그 孫北幹金春擇이 諺譯하얏다고 한다. ＜219＞
