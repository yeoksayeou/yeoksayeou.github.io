**Body Text Translation:**
Samch'ŏlli Confidential Room, Survey of Autumn Harvests of the Wealthiest in Chang'an [Seoul]

In Western countries or other nations, the development of commerce and industry is remarkable, so one must look to the commercial and industrial sectors to find most large capitalist conglomerates. However, in the East, particularly in Chosŏn (Korea), agriculture has been primary. Consequently, to trace the lineage of millionaires, one must inevitably focus on agriculturalists (though recently, some engage in various businesses). Thus, since ancient times in Chosŏn, it has been specially said, "Agriculture is the great foundation of the world." In this way, in Chosŏn, being wealthy immediately became synonymous with someone who harvests a lot. So, who among the people of Chosŏn harvests the most? Leaving the provinces for later, we will first start with the old families (those known as wealthy for a long time) or prominent figures in Chang'an [Seoul] and gradually investigate and record this. Of course, recently, there are newly rich families (成金家) like Mr. So-and-so who became millionaires worth several million through mining, especially gold mining. However, they are excluded from this autumn harvest report for now, to be introduced later after they have purchased much land. Also, since this autumn harvest record involves secretly exploring others' assets, it is utterly impossible to calculate it precisely down to the thousand, hundred, ten, or single *tu* (斗, a unit of volume) like a tax collector (秋收官). In this regard, we ask readers for their generosity and understanding in the sense of "it seems approximate." We also note that land purchased within the last one or two years has not been added, as there is no basis (憑籍) for it.
(To avoid disputes over the amount of harvest) <17>

First to appear is not only the wealthiest man in Chang'an but also the wealthiest in Chosŏn, the sons (令胤) of the late Min Yŏnghwi (閔泳徽). Whether excluding the royal family (宮家) or not, he was the number one figure among old families or anywhere else. Having inherited his estate, the autumn harvest gathers from all parts of Chosŏn, and it is said that the harvest administrators (秋收官) alone number over several hundred, so giving him the first place seems appropriate.

Min Hyŏngsik (閔衡植) - As the successor (to Min Yŏnghwi), his harvest might seem large, but it is not that much, seemingly totaling only around 2,200-2,300 *sŏk* (석, stone; a unit of grain volume), thus failing to rank higher. This constitutes the entirety of his harvest.

Min Taesik (閔大植) - The younger brother (令弟) of Hyŏngsik and a direct blood relative of Min Yŏnghwi. It was once rumored his harvest might exceed ten thousand *sŏk*, but due to some setbacks, it is currently estimated to be around 6,000-7,000 *sŏk*.

Min Kyusik (閔奎植) - The youngest brother (令季氏) of Taesik. Among the gentlemen mentioned above, he certainly stands out, nearing 9,000 *sŏk*. Besides this, he possesses much cash and real estate, amounting to a fortune of several million *won*.

Han Yangho (韓亮鎬) - Son (令胤) of the late Prime Minister (參政大臣) Han Kyusŏl (韓圭卨). As an old family, he is not inferior to the Min family. His wealth indeed has a long history, and he is a considerable landowner. Therefore, when the Prime Minister was alive, remaining secluded and focusing on financial management (理財), there were rumors his harvest might reach 20,000 *sŏk*. However, the reality was different, around ten thousand *sŏk*, so public opinion suggests it might be around 7,000-8,000 *sŏk* now.

Kim Chongson (金鐘善) - Although not well-known in our society, he is also from an old wealthy family in Chŏlla Province (全羅道). His assets under his own name alone easily exceed 4,000 *sŏk*, and including those he manages (due to his elder brother's (令伯氏) passing, the heir entered his household registry), it totals a considerable 8,000-odd *sŏk*.

Sin Sŏgu (申錫雨) - Known to everyone for his immense contribution to our society's cultural projects, there were loud rumors (喧籍) that he had dissipated (蕩盡) his family fortune. However, he has actually consolidated everything over several years. Through development projects and other means, it has now recovered. His current harvest is around 3,000 *sŏk*, but it is rumored that once the farms in places like P'och'ŏn (抱川), Kangwŏn Province (江原道) are completed, it will easily reach 10,000 *sŏk*. <18>

Sŏ Pyŏnghak (徐丙學) - He is also a leading tycoon competing for the top spot in Taegu (大邱). Chang Kilsang (張吉相) and Sŏ Pyŏnghak were the twin pillars of Taegu, and who had more money was indeed a major topic of conversation. According to what is heard, his harvest is far superior to Mr. Chang's, exceeding ten thousand *sŏk*. He is also the number one figure in Taegu for cash holdings and is said to hold sway (牛耳を 잡었다 - hold the cow's ear, meaning to lead) in the Taegu financial world.

Ch'oe Iksŏn (崔益善) - Also a gentleman who, along with Sin Sŏgu, made huge contributions (巨功) to Chosŏn's cultural projects. It is said his harvest decreased somewhat due to this, but it is around 2,500-2,600 *sŏk*.

Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) - From an old family in South Chŏlla (全南), he has made many contributions to Chosŏn's business, education, and cultural sectors, showing great vigor (萬丈氣熖). It was said his adoptive family's (生養家) harvest amounted to 40,000 *sŏk*, but now, having spent much on business funds, his actual income is around ten thousand *sŏk*.

Kim Kyesu (金季洙) - The biological younger brother (生令季氏) of Sŏngsu, said to be on par with his elder brother.

Kim Chaesu (金在洙) - Sŏngsu's younger brother (令季), somewhat further down, around 2,000 *sŏk*.

Ku Ch'angjo (具昌祖) - From an old family known for wealth (富名) in Inch'ŏn (仁川). After leaving Seoul, he purchased much land through profit-making (殖利) and now far exceeds 5,000 *sŏk*.

Chang Kilsang (張吉相) - Now deceased. Said to be the wealthiest man (首富) in Taegu, he divided his assets among his siblings, then lived with his wife in Kyŏngsŏng [Seoul] before passing away. If one calculates his harvest from various sources now, it is said to exceed 6,000 *sŏk*.

Im Hyŏngsang (林衡相) - Famous for money lending (貸金業), he was already known for wealth, and now has a harvest of around 8,000 *sŏk*.

Im Hosang (林昊相) - From an old family, his wealth remains consistent (如一), claiming 4,000 *sŏk*.

U Chonggwan (禹鐘觀) - Came up to Seoul from Chŏlla Province, famous as a hidden wealthy man. He also is said to have no less than (不下) 3,000 *sŏk*.

O Chŏnggŭn (吳正根) - From an old family, having served as a high official (大官) and minister (判書) in the Old Korean Empire (舊韓國). His harvest surpasses 5,000 *sŏk*, and he reportedly has cash amounting to several hundred thousand *won*.

Yun Ch'iho (尹致昊) - This gentleman is too famous. His wealth, dating back to his father's time, amounted to several thousand *sŏk*, and even now it is no less than 5,000 *sŏk*.

Kim Sŏnghwan (金成煥) - An original resident (元住者) of Ahyŏn <19>. Since his father's time, he made his fortune (致富) through brassware (鍮器). Now, he reportedly receives a harvest easily exceeding 8,000 *sŏk*.

Let's stop here for now. Recently, a few individuals suddenly acquired several thousand *sŏk* within a few years through gold mining. Let's list a few of them below.

Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學) - Made a fortune (致富) of 3-4 million *won* from gold mining. It is estimated that the land he has acquired yields about 3,000 *sŏk*.

Pang Ŭngmo (方應謨) - Also made his fortune (成家) through gold mining and is now active in cultural projects. Through land purchase (買收) or reclamation projects (干拓事業), he harvests around 2,000-odd hundred *sŏk*.

Wŏn Yunsu (元胤洙) - His family made their fortune (成家) as fruit merchants (果物商) in the Namdaemun Market (南大門 市長) since the previous generation. Later, they struck gold (百年 금광 - lit. 100-year gold mine, perhaps meaning a long-lasting mine?) and became even wealthier (致富). The income from the land he currently owns is around 1,500-1,600 *sŏk*, but it is said if he buys land with mining profits, he could easily surpass ten thousand *sŏk*.

Yun Hŭijung (尹希重) - From an old family in Nonsan (論山), he has contributed much to Chosŏn's cultural projects since coming to Seoul. Having spent money here and there on cultural projects, his harvest is said to be around 1,100-1,200 *sŏk*.

We will stop at this point for now and announce more details later.
Furthermore, this autumn, one *sŏm* (섬, bag/sack, roughly equivalent to a *sŏk*) of rice is about 14 *won*. So, for example, a harvest of a thousand *sŏk* means an annual income (年收) of 14,000 *won*.

Pak Hŭngsik's Rumored 200,000 *Won* Profit
Due to the surge in Pukson Paper Mill shares

Mr. Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植), who runs the Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店) and also manages a chain store, has his name known in various fields of business. His name was among the shareholders of the recently established Pukson Paper Company (北鮮製紙會社), and now it is said he has made a huge gain because of those Pukson Paper shares.
The reason, reportedly, is that he bought several thousand shares of Pukson Paper when they were circulating cheaply on the stock market at a low price of twelve or thirteen *won*. Recently, as the company's substance improved and general stock prices rose, the price of these shares surged, resulting in a large profit (大利) of about 200,000 *won*. Although he hasn't sold the shares yet and holds them firmly in his grasp (掌中), so it's not yet cash profit, it's considered a rare windfall (橫財) in these circles, drawing envy (羨선), or so they say. <20>

Yu Yŏngsŏp's Educational Endeavor
Mr. Yu Yŏngsŏp (劉永燮) of Hwadong-jŏng (花洞町) in the city recently voluntarily donated (自進喜捨) five hundred *won* towards the new construction costs of Kŭnhwa Girls' School (槿花女學校). At a time when philanthropists (篤志家) donating tens of thousands of *won* for education are emerging one after another, one might dismiss 500 *won*. However, anyone who understands the origin of Mr. Yu's 500 *won* cannot help but express gratitude for his special intention (特志). Mr. Yu was originally extremely poor (赤貧), working as a clerk or market agent at the Kim T'ae Store (金太店) in the Inch'ŏn rice futures market (仁川期米市場). He barely managed to support his large family (數多食率) on a meager salary, never having a peaceful day (寧日). Then, Mr. Yu, possessing a sharp mind (明敏), took the few hundred *won* he had saved penny by penny (分分錢錢) and invested it in rice futures (定期米). Using a steady strategy of starting small and growing big (極小成大), he fought persistently over a long period, eventually making a profit of tens of thousands of *won* and becoming known today as a hundred-thousand-*won* millionaire (십만장자). In short, he built today's success through blood, sincerity, and diligence (血과 誠과 勤). Thus, 500 *won* is a large sum (大金) for him. Mr. Yu, your pure wealth (淨財) will surely be more than enough to support this academy (學園)!

What Will Become of Andong Detached Palace?
Divided among the Office of the Yi Royal Household, Ch'oe Ch'anghak, and Min Taesik

In days gone by, Andong Detached Palace (安洞別宮) was one of the royal palaces commanding reverence and respect, marked by signs reading "All persons, high or low, dismount" (大小人員皆下馬). It was a hall of joy (기뿜의 殿閣) and a pavilion of celebration (慶祝의 閣宇) where princes and other members of the royal family, including close relatives (宗室宗親), held splendid wedding ceremonies (華觸의 盛典) and pledged eternal life (萬壽의 約). After the wedding ceremony of Emperor Sunjong (純宗) was the last, its gates closed. It passed through dozens of springs and autumns (數十 星霜) amidst desolate winds (소슬한 바람). More recently, it became a residence for court ladies (內人), but even that, due to the circumstances of the times (時運의 所致), was sold off into the hands of city dwellers (市井人). It has already revealed its sold state and appearance, making indifferent passersby (無心한 行客) look back twice.
The red railings and splendid pavilions (朱欄華閣) of Chŏnghwadang (正和堂), Kyŏngyŏndang (慶衍堂), and Hyŏngwangnu (顯光樓) have all been torn down by laborers (土工), leaving no trace of their former glory. Now, what will occupy the site (基址) where this detached palace was demolished?
The gold mine king (金鑛王) Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學), whose name flutters throughout the capital and countryside (京鄕), a multimillionaire (천만장자), purchased several hundred *p'yŏng* facing the road (道路 正面) and is constructing a gold mining company building at the cost of tens of thousands of *won*, while also building a "building" (삘딍 - building). Behind it, a section of several thousand *p'yŏng* will become the site of an elementary school (普通學校) funded by 330,000 *won* donated by Min Taesik's (閔大植) mother (母堂). Furthermore, the several hundred *p'yŏng* behind that remain under the ownership <21> of the Office of the Yi Royal Household (李王職), where new residences for the court ladies (內人) who lived within the palace grounds are being built. The Detached Palace is now divided into three parts (三分), making it impossible to find its former position. In any case, large buildings will soon stand densely packed (林立) there.

Cho Chunho's Rumored 200,000 *Won* Profit
Mr. Cho Chunho (趙俊鎬), president of the East Asia Securities Company (東亞證券株式會社), known in our Chang'an [Seoul] for his youthful resourcefulness (小壯 手腕家) and as a businessman (實業家), entered the speculative world (投機界) just over three years ago. Through his meticulous brain (치밀한 두뇌) and sharp, agile intellect (민첩한 理智), he has already subjugated many traders (取引員) considered seasoned veterans (百戰老將) in the field for decades. Repeating dynamic activities, charging east and thrusting west (東衝西突縱橫無盡), he has now established an unshakable foundation (確乎不拔의 지반) in both the stock market (株式家) and the rice futures market (期米界). Thus, there is talk that he skillfully manages the company's huge capital (巨資) of 2 million *won*. Now, according to rumors spread through hearsay (風聞) or informants (소식통), since the famous February 26 Incident (2.26사건), Mr. Cho, using his sharp mind in the rice futures market, has reportedly earned a massive profit of 200,000 *won* in just 6-7 months. 200,000 *won*! Who can fathom (寸度) the painstaking effort (苦心) buried within earning such a huge sum? In any case, we hope he remains prudent (自重) and healthy (健在), representing our people in the financial world and displaying great vigor (萬丈氣熖).

First Female Doctor of Medicine
Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk Currently Writing Thesis

Ms. Hŏ Yŏngsuk (許英肅女史), who left Seoul this spring for a planned one and a half years (18 months) to study academics and clinical practice at the Tōkyō Red Cross Maternity Hospital (東京亦十字産院), has reportedly progressed remarkably (日就月將) in her studies during this time, astonishing the hospital's medical doctors (學博士). Initially entering as just a research student, she has now even been appointed as a physician at the Red Cross Maternity Hospital. The lady doctor is currently writing a thesis on "The Relationship between Pregnancy and Internal Secretions (內汾泌)". It is expected that upon completion, she will submit this thesis to the university and request a Doctor of Medicine degree. It seems likely she will emerge as the first female medical doctor (女醫博士) within a few years (不出幾年). Furthermore, the lady doctor plans to return to Seoul next autumn (明秋) and establish a maternity hospital in Hyojadong (孝子町) to operate on a large scale (대대적으로 經營).

Completion of Sin Sŏgu's Residence, Paengnyongjang

Mr. Sin Sŏgu (申錫雨), pen name Kanch'ang (干蒼), selected an auspicious site (吉地) below his ancestral tombs (先塋下) in a place of high mountains and beautiful water (山高水麗), about 5 *ri* (里, approx. 2 km) from Ŭijŏngbu (議政府). Starting construction last autumn (昨秋) to build a splendid and magnificent residence (邸宅), after over a year and spending tens of thousands of *won* (數萬金), it is already completed, and a housewarming ceremony (落成式) will be held <22> soon. The residence comprises a main building (本館) of over 60 *kan* (間, a unit of space between pillars) and auxiliary buildings (附屬建物) of over 20 *kan*, making it a grand structure (宏大) of fine materials (甲材巨屋) rarely seen near Kyŏngsŏng [Seoul]. While the beauty and brilliance (美麗燦爛) of the red railings and splendid pavilions (朱欄華閣) inspire awe (驚異의 감), the superb natural scenery (天然의 景槪가 絶勝) of the secluded valley (幽谷) and the lushness (鬱蒼) of flowers, plants, and trees in the garden offer more than enough solace to the minds and bodies (心身) of city dwellers. The radiance (燦然함) of the lotus pond pavilion (蓮堂) is said to further enhance (도읍는다 - archaic form of 돋운다) the scenery. Mr. Sin named the residence Paengnyongjang (白龍莊), meaning White Dragon Villa, because his mother (慈親) reportedly saw a white dragon (白龍) in her dream when conceiving (孕胎) him. The signboards and pillar couplets (懸板柱聯) are said to be modeled after Chinese masters (中國巨匠).

Dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's Fortune of 20,000 *Won*

Dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi (崔承喜女史), currently in Tōkyō, having completed her third performance, her popularity now truly in the ascendant (旭日昇天の勢), is reportedly planning to tour about 60 cities for recitals after finishing her Tōkyō performances. It is heard that before establishing her own research institute, she received only 200 *won* total for a 10-day appearance with the Tōhō Masters Association (東寶名人會). Nowadays, however, she reportedly does not perform for less than 300 *won* per night. Furthermore, when she performed at the Ōsaka Theater (大阪劇場) last October,
she received an astonishing performance fee of 3,000 *won* for one week.
Her trip abroad (洋行) will likely be around next spring (明春頃).
Moreover, the frequency and popularity of her performances continue to reach new heights (絶頂). Rumors are widespread (소문이 놉흐다) that she has already earned 20,000 *won*, built a Western-style house (洋屋), and established a research institute.

Ch'oe Sŏnik and English

Mr. Ch'oe Sŏnik (崔善益), pen name Mongch'o (夢初), after extensively touring scenic mountains and rivers (勝地江山) or spending the summer idly (閒居) diverting himself with calligraphy (書筆로 消遣), seems to have been deeply moved (感) by something. According to rumors (風聞), since early autumn (初秋), he has invited Professor Kim (金敎授) from Ewha Women's College (梨專 - Ihwa Chŏnmun Hakkyo) and is diligently studying English every Tuesday and Thursday, either at his home or Professor Kim's residence. There are rare tales (珍談) of achieving great success (大成) after entering university at 50. For Mr. Ch'oe, nearing the age of forty (不惑 - age when one is free from doubts), to be so devoted to learning with aspirations for future great endeavors (雄飛) is a rarely seen strong will (壯志). As he possesses extraordinary talent (非凡한 才分), his acquaintances (知舊間) are watching with admiration and praise (刮目相讚), expecting his mastery of English to be a matter of the near future.

Master Painter Simsan's Major Work

Master Painter No Simsan (盧心汕畵伯) has recently been commissioned (委囑) to create a mural (壁畵) for the large hall of the Meiji Confectionery Company (明治製菓會社) located on Chŭngogae (즌고개 - likely present-day Chungmuro area) in Seoul. He is currently contemplating a theme (畵題) that expresses the sentiment of the Peninsula (半島情緖) and is actively working on its production (製作中). It is expected to be completed soon (不遠에 竣工). Furthermore, the remuneration (報酬) received from the company is said to be 300 *won*. <23>

**Summary**

This 1936 Samch'ŏlli magazine article surveys the wealth, primarily measured by autumn rice harvests (*ch'usu*), of prominent families and individuals in Seoul (Chang'an) and other regions. It contrasts Korea's agriculture-based elite with the West's industrial capitalists, listing estimated harvest sizes for figures like the Min clan heirs, Han Yangho, Kim Sŏngsu, and others. It also notes the rising wealth from gold mining (Ch'oe Ch'anghak, Pang Ŭngmo) and includes shorter news items on stock market gains (Pak Hŭngsik, Cho Chunho), philanthropic donations (Yu Yŏngsŏp), the redevelopment of Andong Palace, the academic pursuits of Hŏ Yŏngsuk (aspiring female doctor) and Ch'oe Sŏnik (studying English), the success of dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, and an art commission for painter No Simsan.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a fascinating glimpse into the Korean elite under Japanese colonial rule. It highlights the persistence of land-based wealth derived from traditional agriculture alongside emerging fortunes from modern industries like mining and finance. The detailed listing of names and estimated wealth provides insight into the social hierarchy and economic structure of the time. Furthermore, the mentions of cultural activities, education (including women's higher education and study abroad), and philanthropy illuminate the social lives and aspirations of this class amidst the colonial context. The casual mention of Japanese locations (Tokyo, Osaka) and entities (Meiji Seika, Toho Meijinkai, Red Cross Hospital) alongside Korean affairs underscores the integrated, yet complex, reality of colonial Korea.

三千里機密室, 長安甲富 秋收 調査
歐米 각국이나 또는 其他諸國은 모다 상공업의 발달이 현저하야 대개의 大資本閥을 차저 보랴면 상공업계에서 뒤저 보아야 하지만 동양 특히 朝鮮은 농업이 주가 되었음으로 따라서 갑부 백만장자의 舊稱를 뒤저내자면 불가불 농업가(근래는 모든 업을 겸한 분도 있지만)를 주체로 삼었던 것이다. 그래서 古來로 朝鮮에서는 「農者는 天下之大本」이라고 特稱하였다. 이같이 朝鮮에서는 부자라면  卽時 추수를 많이 하는 사람의 대명사처럼 되었으니 그러면 대체 朝鮮사람들 중 어떤 분이 추수를 많이 하는가?  지방은 후일로 미루고서 먼저 長安에서도 舊家(옛날부터 부자 소리듣는 집)혹은 저명한 인사로 비롯하야 점차로 이에 조사 기록하기로 한다. 勿論 近日에 와서는 광업 특히 금광으로 某某氏같이 수백만의 長者가 된 금광 成金家가 있기는 하지만 아즉은 이 秋收狀에서는 제외하야 뒷날 토지를 많이 산 뒤에 紹介하려 하며 또一方이 秋收記는 남의 재산을 비밀히 탐색한 것인지라 秋收官처럼 幾千幾百幾十幾斗式으로 계산하기는 도저히 불가능하니 此邊에는 讀者諸位도 雅量을 가지고 「近似하구나」 하는 식의 양해을 바란다. 특히 1,2년 내로 더 산 것은 憑籍할 곳이 없어 가산치 않었음도 附記하여 둔다.
(단 秋收多少로 시비를 피하기 위하야)＜17＞
먼저 第1着으로 등장되는 분은 長安갑부일 뿐 아니라 朝鮮갑부라는 故 閔泳徽氏의 令胤들이다. 宮家를 빼놓고든지 어째던지 舊家로나 어디로나 제1일인자이든 氏의 상속을 받은 터이라 秋收도 全朝鮮 각지에서 모혀들어 秋收官만 하야도 수백인을 넘는 형편이라 하니 첫자리를 주어도 손색이 없으리라고 보는 바이다.
閔衡植氏-相續者(閔泳徽氏의)인 만큼 추수도 많을 것 같으나 그다지 많치 못하니 그럭저럭 합하야 2천 2,3백석 밖에 次例에 못 간 듯하고 따라서 그것이 氏의 추수 전부요,
閔大植氏-衡植氏의 令弟로 閔泳徽氏의 직접혈속인지라 한때는 만석을 넘을 듯 하다더니 多少 실패가 있어 현재는 6,7천석 내외일 것이라 하며,
閔奎植氏-大植氏의 令季氏로서, 以上 諸氏 중에 당연 두각을 들어내어 9천석을 넘을 지경이라하며 그 외 현금과 가옥이 만하 수백만원의 富라 한다.
韓亮鎬氏-故 參政大臣 韓圭卨氏의 令胤이니 舊家로도 閔씨에게 지지 않는 분이다. 그야말로 오랫동안의 내력이 있는 재산이고 보매 토지로도 상당한 부호이다. 그래서 參政大臣이 在世時에 두문불출하는 남어지 理財에 힘을 써 一時는 2만석의 추수가 있으리라는 설까지 있었으나 실상은 그렇지 못하고 만석 내외이었다 함으로 지금쯤은 7,8천석의 정도일 것 같다는 것이 세간의 평이다.
金鐘善氏-氏는 우리사회에서 그다지 이름을 들날린 적이 없어 일반은 모르나 역시 全羅道 부자의 舊家로 지금 자기 名義만의 것으로도 4천석은 무난하고 자신이 관리(令伯氏별세로 所生者가 入戶)하는 것까지 합하면 無慮 8천여 석이라 하고.
申錫雨氏-누구나 잘 아는 우리사회의 문화사업에 거대한 공헌이 있는 氏는 그간 세간에서 가산을 蕩盡하였다고  喧籍하였었으나 그 실은 그것을 數個年來에 모다 정리하였고 그간 개척사업 기타로 이제는 복구되어 현재 추수로는 3천석 내외나 江原道 抱川等地의 농장이 완성되는 날이면 1만석은 무난하리라고 소문이 있다.＜18＞
徐丙學氏-氏亦 大邱에서 1,2위를 다투는 부호이다. 張吉相, 徐丙學은 大邱의 쌍벽으로 그야말로 누가 돈을 많이 가젓느냐가 큰 화제가 되었다. 듣는 바에 의하면 張씨보다는 훨신 상위인 만석 이상의 추수가 있고 또 현금 많기로는 大邱에 제1인자이며 大邱財界에서는 牛耳를 잡었다고 한다.
崔益善氏-亦 申錫雨氏 함께 朝鮮文化事業에 巨功을 끼친 氏는 이로 인하야 다소 추수가 줄었다고 하나 2천 5,6백석 내외라 하며,
金性洙氏-全南舊家로 朝鮮事業界와 교육계 문화사업계에 공적이 많어 萬丈氣熖을 토하고 잇는 氏는 元來 生養家의 추수가 4만석 운운하였으나 지금은 사업자금에 많이 써서 실수입은 만석 내외라 한다.
金季洙氏-性洙氏의 生令季氏로 역시 伯氏와 伯仲間이라 하며,
金在洙氏-性洙氏의 令季로 좀 뚝 떠러저 2천석 내외.
具昌祖氏-仁川에서 富名 듣든 舊家로 서울을 나와서 그간 殖利로 많은 토지를 사서 지금은 5천석을 훨신 넘는다 하며,
張吉相氏-지금은 故人이 되었다. 大邱의 首富라더니 그간 형제에게 分財를 해준 후 京城에서 부인과 동거하다가 作故한 분이니 지금 氏의 추수를 이것저것 처보면 6천석 이상은 넘으리라고 한다.
林衡相氏-貸金業으로 유명하던 氏는 元來도 富名을 듣든 터라 지금은 8천석 내외의 추수가 있다 하며,
林昊相氏-氏는 舊家의一로 지금도 그 재산이 如一하야 4천석을 稱한다 하며,
禹鐘觀氏-全羅道에서 서울 울너온 분으로 숨은 부자로 유명하니 氏 亦 3천석을 不下한다고하며,
吳正根氏-舊韓國 大官으로 判書에 있었던 舊家로서 氏의 추수는 5천석을 돌파하는 외 現金이 수십만원에 달한다고 하며,
尹致昊氏-이분은 너무나 유명한 분으로 그의 재산은 氏의 부친 때부터 수천석을 하는 분인데 지금도 5천석을 不下,
金成煥氏-阿峴의 元住者로＜19＞ 부친 이래 鍮器로 致富한 분인데 지금은 8천석을 무난히 받는다고 한다.
여기쯤 하고 근래 돌연 금광으로써 數年來에 수천석을 하게 된 2,3분을 적어보면 아래의 數氏일 것이다.
崔昌學氏-금광으로 3,4백만원의 致富를 한 분인데 氏는 그간 토지를 작만한 것이 3천석은 되리라는 평이오,
方應謨氏-亦 금광으로 成家하야 지금은 문화사업에 활약하는 분으로 買收 혹은 干拓事業으로 2천 수백석의 추수를 하는 터이며,
元胤洙氏-先代로부터 南大門 市長에서 果物商을 하야 成家한 분으로 그후 백년 금광을 얻어 더욱 致富하였다. 지금 가지고 있는 토지의 수익은 1천 5,6륙백석 내외나 鑛의 수익으로 토지를 산다면 만석 이상은 돌파하리라고 한다.
尹希重氏-이분은 論山의 舊家로 서울온 이래 朝鮮 문화사업에 공헌이 많은 분인데 이럭저럭 문화사업에 쓰고 추수는 1천 2,2백석 내외라고 한다.
이번은 이만한 정도에 끄치고 다시 後期에 상세한 것을 발표하기로 한다.
더욱 금년 가을은 벼 한 섬에 14원 정도에니 가령 천석 추수라면 年收 1만 4천원인 셈이 된다.

朴興植氏의 20萬圓 利得說
