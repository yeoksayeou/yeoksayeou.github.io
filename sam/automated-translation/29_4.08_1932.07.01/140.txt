Upon Seeing the All-Korea Children's Artwork Exhibition, A Fragment of Feeling
						By a Female Reporter

The All-Korea Children's Artwork Exhibition was held for three days on the 8th, 9th, and 10th of this month in the classrooms of the Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School in Ch'ŏngjin-dong, downtown. Although I attended and viewed it, I am neither a researcher of children's art nor do I know the theories of childcare, so I acknowledge it would be quite foolish of me to write a critique of the submissions, saying this or that about them. Therefore, I will refrain from doing so and instead briefly jot down the feelings I had while inside the venue, having viewed the exhibition.

The first place I stepped into was where the children's works were displayed, and I had an entirely different impression from what one feels in an art exhibition hall. A childlike innocence welled up, my heart softened, and a smile spread across my face, because the self-expressions drawn by their small fingertips were so endearing and lovely. There were many pretty, lovely, and skillfully drawn pieces, but some were so rendered that one could hardly distinguish what was what. What was particularly amusing, endearing, and lovely was a picture hanging on the wall directly across from the entrance. Truly, I couldn't tell if it was drawn like a dog's paw or a cow's hoof (개발인지 소발인지); lines were just scribbled here and there with a pencil, and circles were drawn too. For some reason, I stood lingering the longest in front of that picture, picturing the child who drew it. I muttered to myself that if I were a judge, I would have attached a "Green Sheep Award" (綠羊賞) tag to that picture.

In any case, everything looked good to my eyes. There were times I suspected that an adult's hand might have touched the skillful and well-done pieces, but what was somewhat disappointing was the matter of the judging panel teachers. Among the nearly 1,000 numerous works, the only ones with a piece of paper attached that read "Green Sheep Award" were "Moonlit Night" by Han Pok-kun (5 years old), "Streetcar" by Ko Kwang-hun-kun (6 years old), and "Race," "Swing," and "Slide" by Kim Chu-hŏn-kun (6 years old). (If I were giving out awards, I would have given them to all!) I rather wished I hadn't seen the "Green Sheep Award" tags at all.

After passing the Korean children's works display area, there was the Japanese children's works display area. I didn't linger there very long and looked for the section of works by other foreign children. However, as I couldn't see it anywhere, I asked an attendant, who replied that there were no works by foreign children. Just as I was about to pass by, I saw some displayed in a corner. But they didn't seem particularly remarkable.

There were also several invitational submissions from children's art researchers. However, with my eyes, I had no way of knowing which were good works. I only felt that they were cleaner and more skillful than the children's works.

Displayed in the next area were entirely works by students of the Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School. I viewed these with a somewhat different feeling than when viewing the children's works, perhaps because I had the preconceived notion, "Well, they are adults." And I couldn't find the innocence. Moreover, what caught my eye disagreeably were the calendars made by the second-year students. There was no originality at all; they all seemed to have been copied from easy-to-draw Japanese picture books like "Kodomo no Kuni" (子供の國) or similar types, which left me feeling displeased. Perhaps I saw it wrong, but that's how I felt at the time, so nothing can be done about it. The bean crafts (豆細工), shell crafts (貝殼細工), woodcrafts (細木), etc., as well as the Green Sheep Amusement Park (綠羊遊園), Green Sheep Ranch (綠羊牧場), Green Sheep Bridge Side (綠羊橋畔), etc., seemed quite interestingly made.

In the very last area, there were statistics concerning kindergartens throughout Korea, and photographs of kindergartens from various countries were displayed. By the time I had toured this far, the clock already showed two o'clock. As I hurriedly stepped outside, the remaining feeling in my head was that a great deal of manual effort (手功) had gone into all the arrangements. <41>

**Summary**

A female reporter reviews the All-Korea Children's Artwork Exhibition held at the Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School in 1932. She finds the young children's art endearing and naively charming, particularly favouring a seemingly random scribble which she playfully wished to award. However, she criticizes the limited number of official awards given. She briefly notes the presence of Japanese children's art and the near absence of other foreign works. The reporter expresses disappointment with the works of older students from the Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School, finding them lacking in originality and purity, specifically pointing out copied illustrations from Japanese children's magazines like "Kodomo no Kuni". From a historical perspective, the article offers insights into the colonial Korean art education scene, cultural comparisons (Korean vs. Japanese children's art), the limited international exposure, and anxieties about originality versus imitation, particularly the visible influence of Japanese popular culture ("Kodomo no Kuni") on Korean students. The critique of older students copying Japanese sources highlights concerns about cultural identity and authentic expression under colonial rule.

全鮮幼兒作品展覽會를 보고서, 늣김의 한 쪼깍을
						婦人記者
全鮮幼兒作品展觀會가 本月 8, 9, 10일 3일간 시내 淸進洞 京城보육학교 교실에서 열니게 되여서 관람한 일은 잇스나 필자가 童畵연구가도 안이고 또 유아보육의 이론조차 알지 못하는 것만큼 무엇이 엇저니 하고 출품에 대한 평을 쓴다는 것은 퍽도 어리석은 일 인줄 자신이 수긍하는 바이니 그만 두기로 하고 이왕 관람햇스니 장내에서 늣긴 바나 잠간 적어보려고 한다.
처음 바를 듸려 노흔데는 유아들 작품이 진열된 장소엇는데 미술전람회 진열장에서 늣기는 바와는 전혀 다른 感想을 가지게 되엿다. 동심이 끌어 올나서 맘이 柔해지며 얼골에 유슴을 띄우게 되엿스니 그것은 어린 그들의 적은 손끗헤서 그려진 자기들의 표현이 너무도 귀엽고 사랑스러웟든 까닭이다. 엡부고 곱게 묘하게 그린 것도 만엇지만은 엇던 것은 무엇이 무엇인지 분별할 수 업스리만큼 된 것도 잇엇스며 더욱히 우습고도 귀엽고 사랑스런 것은 會場 입구에서 바로 건너다 보이는 편쪽에 붓흔 그림이엿다. 정말 그애말로 개발인지 소발인지 몰으게 연필노 이리저리 쭉쭉 선도 끄코 똥그람이도 그려 노앗섯다. 필자는 웬 일이엿든지 그 그림압헤 제일 오래 머물너 서서 그것을 그린 어린이의 자태를 눈압헤 떠올엿다. 내가 심사원이 되엿드면 그 그림에 綠羊賞이라는 標를 부처 주엇을 것을 하고 혼자 중얼걸엿다.
엇잿든 내 눈에는 모도가 조왓다. 묘하고 잘된 것은 어룬에 손이 것치지 안엇을가 하는 의심을 가진 적도 이섯지만은 그런데 좀 섭섭한 것은 심사원 선생님들의 일이다. 약 1000점이나 되는 그 만흔 작품 중에 綠羊賞이라고 쓴 조희쪽을 부처 노흔 것은 한복君(5세)의 「달밤」 高光勳君(6세)의 「전차」 金周憲君(6세)의 「競走」, 「근네」, 「미끄럼대」 등 뿐이엿다.(필자가 상을 준다면 모조리 줄텐데) 차라리 綠羊賞이라는 標가 보히지 안엇더면 하는 생각을 가젓다.
조선 아동작품 진열장을 지나면 일본 아동작품 진열장이엿다. 그곳에선 별로 발이 오래 머믈지 안코 다른 외국 아동작품부를 차젓다. 그러나 아모데도 보히지 안엇슴으로 係員에게 물엇드니 외국아동작품은 업다고 대답하길네 근양 지나칠여고 하엿드니 한쪽 귓텡이에 얼마간 진열되여 잇섯다. 만은 별로 신통한 것 갓지 안엇다.
童畵硏究家 찬조출품도 몃개간 잇섯다. 그러나 필자의 눈으로는 어느 것이 조흔 작품인 것 조차 알 길이 업섯다. 단지 어린이들 작품보다 깻끗하고 묘하다는 것만 늣겻을 뿐이엿다.
그 다음 장소에 진열된 것은 전부 京城보육학교 생도들의 작품이엿는데 아동작품 관람하든 때와의 기분과는 좀 달은 기분을 가지고 보게 되엿스니 그것은 아마 「어룬들이닛까」하는 선입견을 가젓기 때문이엿는가바. 그리고 순진미를 차즐 수 업섯다는 것이다. 더구나 필자의 눈에 거슬니게 된 것은 2년생들의 카렌다엿다. 창작은 하나도 업고 죄다 그리기 쉬운 일본 「子供の國」라든가 하는 그 종류의 그림책을 보고 그린 것 갓허서 긔분이 좃치 못햇다. 내가 잘못 보앗는지는 몰으지만 그 때에 그럿케 늣겻스니 할 수 업는 일이지. 豆細工 貝殼細工 細木 등이라든가 綠羊遊園 綠羊牧場 綠羊橋畔 등은 퍽 자미스럽게 된 것 갓햇다.
맨 나종 장내엔 全鮮유치원에 관한 통계가 잇섯고 또 각국 유치원 사진이 전열되엿섯다. 여기까지 둘너 나오니 시게는 벌서 두 점이엿다. 급히 문 밧게 나선 내 머리에 남은 늣김은 모-든 설비에 만흔 手功이 드럿다는 것이엿다.＜41＞
