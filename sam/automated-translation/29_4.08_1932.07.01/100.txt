Article Title: 금주법이 실시되면 장안 주객은 將奈何 (If Prohibition Were Enacted, What Would the Drinkers of the Capital Do?)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Questionnaire)

If Prohibition Were Enacted, What Would the Drinkers of the Capital Do? (將奈何)

If a law decreeing "You cannot drink alcohol" were passed, what would the capital's lovers of drink, who seek out the Apricot Blossom Village (杏花村 - poetic term for a tavern) every dusk, intend to do? What would the worriers about the world (憂世家), who kept the wine cup close due to discontent, use to resolve the fire in their chests? And what would the poets, who composed works through the excitement of drink, use to enhance their inspiration? If Prohibition were enacted? If Prohibition were enacted! Indeed, it would be a big problem. Haha.

Floating a Boat in the Inch'ŏn Sea

Lawyer Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯)

I can drink two *toe* (되, a unit of volume) of alcohol. And I also enjoy drinking with meals, so I suppose I belong in the ranks of what the world calls lovers of drink (애주가).

But what if Prohibition were enacted now? That's unlikely to happen. And there probably isn't anyone who would tell me not to drink. When drunk in times of sadness, the sadness seems to wash away, and when drunk in times of joy, that joy becomes even greater. That's why, from ancient times (自古) to the present (及 今), for thousands of years, from kings and generals down to the common folk on the streets (街頭走卒), people have enjoyed and loved alcohol. People who cannot drink alcohol truly live a life too bland and dry (무미간조하게).

If "Prohibition" were enacted, I don't have the courage (용지) to pierce through the net of the law just to drink. Alcohol doesn't hold that kind of absolute importance for me.

That said, looking at "America," which supposedly enacted Prohibition, there probably isn't any populace in the world that drinks as much alcohol as Americans. If they want to drink, they float a boat on the sea and drink, or else they secretly brew it (밀조) in their basements and drink...
If it comes to that for me too, maybe I'll float a boat in the Inch'ŏn sea and drink. Haha.

Moonshining and Drinking Alone (獨酌)

Ch'a Sangch'an (車相瓚), Head of Kaebyŏk Publishing Company (開闢社 主幹)

Even if Prohibition were enacted, if you have money, there's no worry. In America too, they say people with money go out to sea on boats or drink up in the air in airplanes. If Prohibition were enacted in Chosŏn, it would be a big problem.
Because a penniless person like me can't ride airplanes or boats.
Anyway, if things get desperate, some means will surely be devised.
You want an answer now?
Haa—I wasn't going to tell anyone, planning to watch the spectacle of people wandering around unable to drink after Prohibition is enacted, while I alone drink my fill, so what would happen if I told you?
Well then, Mr./Ms. Samch'ŏlli reporter, please keep this strictly to yourself. I plan to secretly drink with my wife at night.
You ask where I'd buy it since there wouldn't be any shops selling alcohol in Chosŏn?
I'll make it at home. And if a policeman comes to search the house and I get caught, I'll pour him five or six glasses. Then he'll get tipsy and won't even think of arresting me.

Secretly Infiltrating Houses That Sell Illicitly
Critic Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿)

The world already knows I'm a spiteful person, so if someone else does something I cannot, I get so angry my eyes could pop out, and I can't live.
Even without that, there's something that makes me furious these days. I don't have money, so I can't have even a single glass of alcohol, but seeing others drinking the night away at places like Myŏngwŏl kwan (明月舘) or Siktowŏn (食道園) is so hateful I could just die. Just when I was thinking to myself and hoping that Prohibition would be enacted, your company asks this question, which makes me feel glad and want to express my gratitude.
Even if Prohibition were enacted, there would probably still be places selling alcohol secretly. If I happen to get some money, I've firmly decided to go alone secretly and drink my fill.

Floating a Boat on the Han River

Kang U (姜友), Ch'ŏndogyo Ch'ŏngudang (天道敎 靑友黨 - Heavenly Way Religion Youth Friendship Party)

If not a single drop of alcohol remains in Seoul, what will the discontented people, myself included, use to vent their frustrations in the future? It is truly pitiful.
However, even if "Prohibition" is enacted, I have a way to drink. That is, I'll smuggle alcohol. Cross one river, and Chinese *soju* can come in; cross just one sea, and there's Western liquor, so I could probably secretly smuggle bottles of alcohol.
Having done that, if I can't drink openly in the Seoul city center (시중), I'll team up with like-minded friends and go boating (배ㅅ노리) on the Han River (漢江). Carrying the alcohol bottles secretly, of course. If the crackdown (취체, 取締) on the Han River is severe, I'll row a small boat out beyond Wŏlmido Island (月尾島) near Inch'ŏn. There, looking out at the vast, billowing Yellow Sea with its boundless green waves (만경창파), on moonlit nights, I'll hold my cup and ask the moon (把酒問月), and in the season when flowers bloom, I'll sing "Below the Mountain, It Opens" (山下開 - likely a drinking song/poem reference) while having one cup, another cup, and yet another cup (一杯一杯 復一盃). Isn't that wonderful?
But there's one thing I must confess. Although I do drink, I don't belong in the ranks of heavy drinkers (酒豪); I'm merely in the ranks of alcohol lovers (애주가) who don't miss their drink with meals (반주) on any given day. Therefore, even if I undertake the adventure of smuggling, I won't need such large quantities, so I believe I'll manage to find a way to drink somehow.

Should I Learn to Make Carbonated Drinks (카보네)?

Man of Letters Hyŏn Chin'gŏn (玄鎭健)

Since I can't drink because I have no money, it seems better to just quit, and I've already been trying to quit. Despite that, people recognize me as a lover of drink, which is truly pitiful. The fact that your company, setting aside many other figures, asks me specifically, I understand is because you deliberately inquire, assuming I like alcohol.
You ask what I would do if I really wanted to drink?
It's questionable whether I could easily discard a learned skill (재조) like that. If I really-really want to drink, I think I'll have to take up the job of making carbonated drinks (카보네) to get by.

Get a Job at a Russian (露西亞) Brewery (釀酒場)

Pak Hŭido (朴熙道)

Originally, I didn't particularly like alcohol, and because of illness, I don't drink at all these days, so even if a Prohibition bill is enacted, it doesn't concern me.
However, it's human nature to want to do more of what is forbidden. So, if Prohibition were enacted, I might want to drink alcohol.
If I had money, when I really-really wanted to drink, I'd smuggle it in and drink, but since I have no money, I can't do that either!
It seems there's no choice but to move to a country where Prohibition hasn't been enacted.
In my view, Russia (로서아) doesn't seem likely to enact Prohibition so easily, so it seems the only way is to drag my entire family of eleven there.
You ask what I'll do if I take all eleven family members and there's no work?
That certainly is a difficult problem. But, if I were to go drink whenever I felt like it, the money would be spent without measure, so what can be done?
When I move to Russia, I plan to get a job right at a brewery (釀酒場). Not just me, but I'll have the entire family work at the brewery, and as compensation, I'll receive alcohol and drink my fill all by myself.

If There's No Alcohol, Something Else
Novelist Yi Ik-sang (李益相)

Before answering your company's survey question, I want to complain. Asking me, "If Prohibition were enacted, what would you, a 'drinking immortal' (酒仙), do?" seems to treat me purely as a drunkard (주객), which is quite displeasing (괫심하지). Why, out of so many people, did you have to come specifically to me and ask? No matter how I think about it, it's incomprehensible— hahaha. However, I am not that much of a person who likes alcohol. It's fine if I don't drink, and fine if I do, so I think there's no need to drink alcohol in violation of the law after Prohibition is enacted. Perhaps an alcoholic (알콜 중독자) would look for a secret way (군영) to drink even if Prohibition were enacted, but since I only drink for socializing, if alcohol disappears, another method of socializing will emerge, so I think it won't matter even if I can't drink alcohol.

Decide to Quit Completely on This Occasion
Yi In (李仁)

Others might see me as a drunkard (주객), but I am not such a drunkard. At most, I drink only about four *hap* (사합).
And it's not that I drink because I see alcohol and crave it. It's because others at drinking gatherings keep urging me (작고 권하니까), so I end up having one or two glasses repeatedly. Also, when a friend who enjoys drinking truly comes from afar (有朋自遠方來), as the host, I can't help but share (논우는) a few glasses with them. Therefore, I cannot be called a lover of drink (애주가).

One can live without drinking alcohol, but since I'm not in a position where I completely abstain from alcohol either, if "Prohibition" were enacted now, it would be fine (좃슴니다) to quit alcohol entirely.
Even if the trend of drinking alcohol is eradicated from our society, there will be many advantageous points in various aspects.

From what I've heard (내 드른 즉), they say that among people in the world who drink and get drunk and disorderly (주정하는 사람), there are none like Koreans (조선 사람밧게 업다고 합데다). Westerners, when they drink, have just one or two glasses to feel stimulated, and that's enough. Chinese people, when they drink, supposedly never drink beyond their capacity and cause a drunken disturbance. But only Koreans gulp down alcohol by the bowlful (사발노 드리키고는), wander the streets drunk and disorderly, or neglect their duties for several days and cause trouble (행패할 일이 만치요). We must eradicate such bad customs (페습).<39>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine humorously surveys prominent Korean figures (a lawyer, publisher, critic, religious figure, writers, etc.) on how they would react if Prohibition were enacted in Korea. Responses range from defiance (planning elaborate smuggling or moonshining operations, even moving to Russia) and finding loopholes (drinking on boats offshore, bribing police) to resignation (using it as a chance to quit, finding other ways to socialize) and even approval (seeing it as a way to curb excessive drinking perceived as a Korean problem). The article reveals contemporary drinking culture, attitudes towards law and authority under colonial rule, awareness of international events like American Prohibition, and varying personal philosophies regarding alcohol, money, and social customs among the Korean elite of the time. Historians might find the candid, sometimes defiant or critical, responses interesting as reflections of social commentary and coping mechanisms within the constraints of colonial society, as well as the self-perception of Korean drinking habits compared to other cultures.

금주법이 실시되면 장안 주객은 將奈何
「술 먹지 못한다」하는 법령이 내린다면 해질가리마다 杏花村을 찻든 장안 애주가들은 장차 엇더케 하려는고 불평이 잇서 술잔을 갓가히 하든 憂世家는 가슴의 불길을 무얼로 해소하며 취흥을 통하여 작품을 짓든 시인들은 장차 무엇으로써 그 흥을 도드려 하는고. 금주법이 실시되면? 금주법이 실시되면! 과연 큰 문제라 할 것이외다 그려. 呵呵.

인천 바다에 배 뛰워 노코

변호사 金炳魯

나는 술을 두 되는 함니다. 그리고 반주도 즐겨 하는 편이니 세상에서 이르는 바 애주가의 행렬에 들 것이외다.

그러치만 이제 금주법이 실시된다면? 그럴 리가 업겟지요. 또 날더러 술 마시지 말나고 할 사람이 업겟지요. 술은 슬푼 때에 먹으면 그 슬품이 씨슨 듯 사라지고 깁분 때에 먹으면 그 깁붐이 한층 더 독거지지요. 그러기에 自古 及 今 수천년에 왕후장상으로부터 街頭走卒까지 술을 즐기고 술을 사랑하는 것이지요. 술 못 먹는 사람은 그야말로 한 세상을 너무 무미간조하게 지내버리는 것이지요.

만일 「금주법」이 실시된다면 그 법망을 뚤코까지 내게는 술 먹을 용지가 업슴니다. 술이 그러케 절대성을 띈 것은 아니니까요.

하기는 금주법을 실시하엿다는 「아메리까」를 보아도 아마 세게 중에 아메리까 사람갓치 술 만히 먹는 백성은 업슬 걸요. 술 먹고 십흐면 바다에 배 뛰워 노코 먹고 그러치 안으먄 지하실에서 가만히 밀조하여 먹고...
나도 그러케 되면 인천 바다에나 배 뛰워 노코 먹을는지 몰으지요. 呵呵

밀조하여 獨酌

開闢社 主幹 車相瓚

금주법이 실시되드래도 돈만 잇으면 걱정 업지요. 아메리카에서도 돈 잇는 사람들은 배를 타고 바다에 나간다든가 혹은 비행기타고 공중에서 먹는다드군요. 조선에 금주법이 실시된다면 큰일인데.
나갓흔 돈 업는 자는 비행기도 탈 수 업고 배도 탈 수 업는 일이닛까.
엇잿든 정 급하게 되면 엇든 수단이든지 꿈여낼 수 잇게 되겟지요.
지금 대답하라구요.
