Magazine Title: 삼천리 제4권 제8호
Publication Date: 1932년 07월 01일
Article Title: 釋王寺吟 (Song of Sŏkwangsa Temple)
Author: 李應洙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A cold rain falls in the forest of Sŏkwangsa Temple (釋王寺).
How could the rain be different anywhere else, yet
Somehow, the rain at Sŏkwangsa Temple seems different.

Into the dense and silent pine forest,
As I leisurely enter like a hermit,
My heart also deepens, becoming dense.

On a night when the gloomy rain makes the mountains and rivers weep,
Hiding under an umbrella with a loved one,
We quietly strolled the dark mountain path.

The rain does not cease in the secluded Sambang Gorge (三防幽峽).
The water of Sesimch'ŏn Stream (洗心川) in Sambang Gorge surges.
The hidden sorrow within my heart swells up.

Clearly posted in front of Songhakkwan Hall (松鶴舘),
The advertisement for Mr. No Chayŏng's (盧子泳) romance novel,
Pitiably soaked by the rain, has collapsed and fallen over.

Afflicted with bodily illness, wandering south and then north,
On this day, at this time, I came to stand by the sea.
Is the small boat on the horizon a symbol of life?

On the sandy beach of the coast where a light rain falls,
Where the sky weeps and the sea weeps,
As I walk forlornly, my footsteps weep along.

As evening fell, coming out to the temporary seaside,
After bathing very calmly,
There was a person calmly putting on clothes and leaving.

From the back shore, *wooo wooo splash*,
At the sound of the earth and sea biting at each other,
A fly that had been sitting on the window lifts its front legs.
(From an old manuscript) <51>

**Summary**

This poem, "Song of Sŏkwangsa Temple," by Yi Ŭngsu captures a mood of melancholy and contemplation set against the backdrop of nature – specifically Sŏkwangsa Temple, Sambang Gorge, and the sea coast, often under the rain. The speaker reflects on sorrow, fleeting moments like a romance novel ad decaying in the rain, personal illness, the symbolism of life found in nature, and a sense of weary observation of the world. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into the personal and perhaps national psyche during the colonial period, reflecting themes of displacement ("wandering south and then north"), sorrow ("hidden sorrow within my heart"), and a quiet sense of resignation or observation amidst perceived decay (the fallen ad, the weeping landscape). The mention of a contemporary romance novel advertisement adds a specific cultural touchpoint of the era.

釋王寺吟
						李應洙
釋王寺 숲 속에 찬비가 내림니다
어느곧 비가 다를 리 잇으리만
엇전지 釋王寺 비는 다른 것 같음니다

森森하고 寂寂한 松林속을
隱者인 듯이 悠然이 들어갈 제
내 마음이 또한 森森하게 깊어지다

음산한 비가 산천을 울리는 밤
사랑하는 이와 양산 아래 숨어서
검어직직한 山道를 조용이 거닐도다

三防幽峽에 비가 끊이지 않소
三防幽峽의 洗心川 물이 소
숨은 내 가슴 속의 애수가 볼어나오

松鶴舘 앞에 뚜렷이 내여붙인
盧子泳씨의 연애소설 광고가
가련이도 비에 저저 쓰러저 너머젓네

身病을 얻어 남으로 또 북으로
이날 이 때는 바다에 와서 섯네
수평선의 쪽배는 인생의 상징인가

보슬비 오는 해안의 백사장의
한울이 울고 바다가 우는 곧을
내 憔然이 걸어갈 제 발자욱이 따라 우네

해 저물 臨時 바다가에 나와서
아조 침착하게 沐洛을 하고 나서
침착하게 옷을 입고 가는 사람 잇엇다

뒷 해안에서 우우우 찰삭하고
흙과 바다가 물어뜻는 소리에
窓에 앉엇든 파리 앞발을 드려놋타
(오랜 舊稿에서)＜51＞
