Chang Ch'ŏmji (張僉知)?
An unknowable smile began to spread across the face of the man sitting alone on the *maru* (wooden floor veranda) of the master's detached quarters (*sarangchae*).
Holding the pipe he had been smoking, he slowly nodded. Yesterday at the hot springs resort, as he recounted past events one by one with the Madam, he had felt an unexpected tingling sensation seize his whole body, and a warm energy had enveloped him.

Chang Ch'ŏmji's Madam, having registered her name on the gisaeng rolls (妓案托名) since childhood, possessed a beauty that seemed to change morning and evening as she aged. Furthermore, her exceptional talent in singing and dancing (歌舞學習) progressed rapidly day by day. From the time she was barely twenty, all the favor of the government officials enveloped her. Whenever magistrates (府使) arrived for their posts, she alone monopolized the duty of attending them (*such'ŏng*), this old gisaeng (老妓), Ch'osŏn (楚仙).
And so, even now, she held the position of a millionaire in Tongnae (東萊).

Chang Ch'ŏmji couldn't help but replay the events that occurred since yesterday, the seventh day of the seventh lunar month (七夕), when he had accompanied the Madam to the hot springs resort. He was overcome with joy and pleasure.
The Madam, bathing in the family bath (家族湯), suddenly, as if struck by a thought, had called him into the bathhouse. At the Madam's call, he had hesitantly entered, still fully clothed. Then she told him to go out again, undress, and come back in. Though they were both old, they were still man and woman, and moreover, the custom dictating she was the master and he the servant made him unable to enter readily; he hesitated, unsure what to do. As he dithered, she urged him on, saying time was passing. Pressured, he had no choice but to strip naked and hesitantly enter, huddled over. As soon as he entered, she asked him to scrub her back. As he scrubbed her back, although her aged skin lacked elasticity, it was still the skin of the opposite sex. As skin met skin, rubbing against each other, he felt drawn by the lingering charm of the opposite sex, an inexplicable impulse welling up inside him. She braced herself with her arms and legs, leaning forward awkwardly, telling him to come closer and scrub with all his might. Then she lay flat on the floor, telling him to climb onto her back and scrub. Then, she abruptly stood up, saying she would scrub him, and plopped down. As all these actions grew progressively bolder and the mounting tension of her various hints made him instantly realize she was rushing towards the gratification of some final desire—
Chang Saengwŏn (張生員)!
—she flashed a slightly awkward, licentious smile, spread her arms, rushed at him, hugged his neck tightly, and collapsed... As his thoughts reached this point, his shoulders involuntarily hitched upwards again and again.

And he thought about how today, long past his sixtieth and even sixty-first birthdays, his vigor was still strong, perhaps because he had lived without knowing much about drinking and women since his youth, and how the effects of the ginseng, deer antlers (三用), and restorative medicines (補藥) the Madam constantly took were truly evident. An inscrutable, heavy smile played on his lips. (Continued on Page 16)<39>
Not only that, but the thought that his fate had changed overnight—he who until yesterday had been wandering around as someone else's servant, enduring all sorts of contempt, had now become the husband of a millionaire—truly made him unable to sit still.
Time flows like water. A day passed, then two, then ten, then twenty days flowed by. A month passed, then two, and now it was the <16> last market day of the third month.
However, Chang Ch'ŏmji's ecstatic dream, waiting expectantly for some sign or change, had seen no indication of realization even by today.
On this day every year, the Madam paid the annual wages (새경) to all the servants. After breakfast, she called them in one by one, as usual, to settle their accounts (會計). Yet, towards Chang Ch'ŏmji, there wasn't a single word.

It was then. Chang Ch'ŏmji thought,
'That's right!'
'Of course, that's how it is!'
'If things were as before, I would have been among them, but since there's no word, she must surely have something else in mind.'
'Surely, it can't just end like this!' Two days had already passed since he first thought this.
The New Year was approaching, and the situation of his wife and children at home, who depended solely on him, was pitiable. Moreover, especially after Ch'ilsŏk, because of his merrymaking, creditors were starting to dun him for the drinks he had consumed on credit while wandering here and there. Having no other choice, he went inside and, after hesitating before the door, finally said,
"Well—it's the end of the year settlement time, shouldn't I look after things at home a bit?"
Hearing this, the Madam opened the door, looked out, and said,
"Chang Saengwŏn (張生員)!"
"Are you quitting this year?"
"What—have you forgotten what happened at the Onch'ŏn river this summer?" (이것베릿는기요?)
She smiled with her eyes, an enigmatic, faint smile, and then,
"What?" <17>

**Summary**

This short story depicts the inner turmoil of Chang Ch'ŏmji, an elderly servant to a wealthy former gisaeng named Ch'osŏn. After an unexpected and intimate encounter initiated by his mistress at a hot spring, Chang Ch'ŏmji becomes convinced his social status has dramatically changed, believing he is now her partner. However, months pass, and during the annual servant wage settlements, she treats him no differently than before. When he finally confronts her indirectly, asking for leave to tend to his family, she dismisses his implied expectations, mockingly reminding him of their encounter and asking if he intends to quit, shattering his delusion.

From a historical perspective, the story offers insights into the complex social dynamics of colonial Korea. It portrays the significant wealth and autonomy a former top gisaeng could achieve, challenging simple narratives of victimhood, while also highlighting the rigid master-servant hierarchy. The casual reference to *such'ŏng* reflects the lingering legacy of the gisaeng system. The story's exploration of class, aging, sexuality, and dashed hopes within this specific social context could be of interest to historians studying social mobility (or lack thereof), gender roles, and the psychological landscape of individuals navigating the stratified society of the period.

장첨지의 주인 마님은 어렷슬 때부터 긔안에 탁명(妓案托名)한 뒤로 한 살 두 살 나히가 들어갈사록 아름다워지는 얼골은 아참 저녁으로 달라 보일 뿐 아니라 가무와 학습(歌舞學習)에 잇서서도 남달니 뛰여나는 재조가 일취월장이엿슴으로 이십이 될락 말락할 때부터 관가의 모든 총애가 왼몸을 싸고돌앗던 것이며 부사(府使)들의 도임이면 동네 동네마다 수청은 혼자 독차지하고 거행하엿던 로긔 楚仙이엿다.
그리하야 지금에도 동래(東萊)에서 백만장자의 자리를 차지하고 지나는 터이엿다.

장첨지는 어제가 칠월 칠석(七夕)날이라서 온청장까지 주인 마님을 모시고 딸아갓던 때부터 생긴 일을 차츰차츰 생각에 되풀이하지 안코는 그저 잇슬 수가 업슬맛치 깃부고 즐거웟던 것이엿다.
가족탕에서 목용하던 주인 마님이 갑작이 무슨 생각이 폭발되엿던지 자긔를 목용탕 안으로 불러들이던 것. 주인 마님이 불음으로 주저주저하면서 옷도 입은 채로 그냥 들어갓던 것. 다시 나가서 옷을 벗고 들어오라던 것. 다가티 늙은 터이엿지만 그래도 남녀가 달을 뿐 아니라 그는 상전이오 자긔는 하인이라는 인습이 좀처름 들어가지 못하게 함으로 어찌할 줄을 몰라 두루 망서리든 것. 그럴사록 시간이 지나간다고 재촉하던 것. 그 바람에 마지못하야 옷을 벌거벗고 알몸동이로 옴추리고 들어갓던 것. 들어가던 길로 등을 문즐러 달라던 것. 등을 문즐을 때에 늙은 몸동이라 탈력조차 업서진 살결이엿스나 그래도 이성의 살결이라 살결과 살결이 읏슥읏슥 마주치고 문즐러질 때 이상하게도 살아진 듯이 남아잇는 이성의 매력에 끌니어 아지 못할 충동이 치밀고 올으던 것. 두 팔과 두 다리를 집고 엉거주춤하니 업듸리서는 갓가히 닥아서서 힘대로 문즐이라던 것. 또 땅바닥에 막우 업드러서는 등을 걸타고 문즐이라던 것. 그러다가는 벌떡 일어나 자긔를 문즐러주겟다고 펄석 안즐 때까지 차츰차츰 로골화하는 모든 동작과 긴장되여 가는 가진 암시가 무슨 최후의 욕망을 향락하고야 말여고 날뛰는 것인 줄을 즉각적으로 깨닷게 되자마자
