Apology Notice

Regarding the "cut" (캇트) used on page 8 of this magazine's Midsummer issue (仲夏호), due to carelessness during printing, we ended up using a copperplate (동판) from the "7-person KAPF Writers Collection" (캅프작가 7인집) published by Chiptansa (集團社) as it was. This has resulted in a protest from the KAPF Secretariat (캅프 서기국) and caused a misunderstanding regarding your esteemed publishing business. We apologize for this mistake.

Samch'ŏlli Company Editorial Department <42>

**Summary**

The Samch'ŏlli magazine's editorial department issues an apology for mistakenly using a printing plate ("cut") belonging to Chiptansa, specifically from their publication "7-person KAPF Writers Collection," in the magazine's midsummer issue. This error prompted a protest from the KAPF (Korea Artista Proleta Federacio) Secretariat. The magazine apologizes for the carelessness and the resulting misunderstanding caused to Chiptansa's publishing business. For historians, this short notice is interesting as it highlights the interactions (even through conflict/error) between different publishing entities in colonial Korea, including the prominent leftist literary organization KAPF and a major magazine like Samch'ŏlli. It also provides a glimpse into the practical issues of printing and copyright/ownership of materials during that period.
