A Collection of Poems by Talented Ladies
The several individuals gathered here are Talented Lady Poets (閨秀 詩人) currently enrolled in certain girls' schools in Seoul. In the future, with the intention of encouraging the still sparse literary circle of women writers, we wish to gather excellent works (佳作) from various Talented Ladies (閨秀 諸氏), so we hope you will send many submissions.

『A Child’s Heart』

Kang Suk-kyŏng

A child’s heart is pure.
Untouchable by these hands of hatred,
It possesses a precious heart, a holy heart.
My wicked heart of today disappears.

A child’s heart is also valiant.
Unbreakable by these ignorant hands,
It holds unseen strength abundantly
And awaits the future of Chosŏn.

Precious and beautiful child’s heart,
While today it plays joyfully with laughter,
The great tasks of tomorrow already approach,
And it prepares to carry the heavy burden.

Ah, fickle human hearts!
Please do not put thorns in their hearts.
Wherever they fight the perilous world (險世) with righteousness,
Grant them victory.

『Night Road』

As I walk the quiet night road,
My heart, into the boundless expanse,
Is falling and wandering,
Trying to sever this crimson heart
That sought to work for this land.

Carrying curses and despair in my chest,
As I walk the road alone,
Only wordless darkness befriends me,
Throwing great strength to fight for this land
Into my heart before leaving.

Though reality has kicked me away and left,
Remembering that vow sworn with blood
On the dark night road,
Resolving for victory in the righteous fight,
I turn back from the quiet night road.<37>

**Summary**

This article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* introduces a collection of poems by female students ("Talented Ladies") attending schools in Seoul in 1932. It includes two poems: "A Child’s Heart" by Kang Suk-kyŏng, which praises the purity and strength of children's hearts, seeing them as holding the hope for Chosŏn's future and needing protection from the harsh world; and "Night Road" (author unnamed), which expresses feelings of despair and isolation but ultimately finds renewed resolve to fight for "this land" despite hardship. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it showcases literary expression by young Korean women during the colonial period. The themes of hope for Chosŏn's future, the struggle against harsh realities ("perilous world," "reality has kicked me away"), and the determination to fight righteously, even when couched in poetic language, reflect underlying nationalist sentiments and the difficult social conditions faced by Koreans under Japanese rule. The magazine's effort to foster a female literary circle is also noteworthy.

閨秀詩人集
여기에 모힌 몃 분은 지금 서울 어떤 녀학과에 재학하고 잇는 閨秀 詩人이외다. 아프로도 소條한 여류 문단을 북돗는 의미로 閨秀 諸氏의 佳作을 모려 하오니 작품을 만히 보내 주심을 바라나이다.

『어린이 마음』

姜淑卿

어린이의 마음은 순결도 함니다.
이 증오의 손으로 만질 수 업는
귀한 마음 거룩한 마음을 갓추엇나니.
내 오늘의 악한 마음 살어지고맘니다.
어린이의 마음은 씩씩도 함니다.
이 무지한 손으로 꺽을 수 업는
보이지 안는 힘을 가득히 품고
조선의 미래를 긔다리고 잇음니다.
귀하고 아름다운 어린이 마음
오늘은 우슴으로 기뿌게 놀 때
내일의 큰 일은 벌서 닥처 와
무거운 짐을 지고 가려 함니다.
아- 변하는 人心이여
그의 마음에 가시를 주지 마사이다
險世를 義로 싸우는 곳마다
승리를 던저 주사이다.

『밤길』

고요한 밤길을 거니노라니
마음은 한업시 광활한 속으로
업더지고 허메이고 잇서서
이 땅을 위하야 일하러 하든
이 붉온 마음을 끈으려 함니다.
저주와 실망을 가슴에 품고
외로히 길을 거니노라면
말 업는 어둠만이 벗해주면서
이 땅을 워하야 싸울 큰 힘을
나의 마음에 던저 주고 감니다.
현실은 나를 차버리고 갓지만
피로서 맹세한 그 언약을
어둔 밤길에 생각하면서
의로운 싸움에 승리를 결심하며
고요한 밤길을 돌처스고 맘니다.<37>
