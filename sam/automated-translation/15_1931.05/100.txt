Editorial Notice
						Editorial Department

"Moscow's (露都) May Day" by Kim Se-yong (金世鎔)
"The Difficult Homeland Movement" by India's Tagore (인도 타고아)
"The People's Rally and Other Incidents" by Yi Chong-nin (李鍾麟)

The above three pieces were subjected to deletion (삭제) and we were unable to publish them; we express our apologies to the authors and readers for this. <19>

**Summary**

This editorial notice from the May 1, 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine informs readers that three articles have been censored ("deleted") and could not be published. The censored articles dealt with Moscow's May Day, India's independence movement (attributed to Tagore), and a "People's Rally." This notice is significant for historians as it provides direct evidence of Japanese colonial censorship targeting specific topics: communism/socialism (Moscow's May Day), nationalist sentiment or comparison with other independence movements (Tagore/India's movement), and potentially organized popular resistance (People's Rally). It starkly reveals the limitations placed on expression and the press under colonial rule.
