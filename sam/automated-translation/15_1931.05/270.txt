Fifteen Short Poems
						Yi Ŭng-su

The clock weeps, noon has come to Changan (長安)
Thousands, tens of thousands of clocks weep
Oh—when will the day come that tens of millions of souls cry out at once?

The world felt so very unstable (不穩不穩)
Perhaps even the heavens have become unstable and stirred things up
Today, upon this land, a water-thunderbolt descends.

Covering her face lest others see
A kisaeng (妓生) walks crying down the street in broad daylight
By what man was she insulted?

On a morning when all friends seem hateful
Sitting on the wooden floor, shaving my beard
I banter with my own face in the mirror.

A madman stands in the middle of the road
A crowd gathers around him
Today too, the heavens mock the city street.

In the latrine of a collapsing rural village
Scattered fragments of a broken hand mirror (面鏡)
Reflect my face each time I go to relieve myself.

On a pine branch at Sŏgwang Temple (釋王寺) in the severe winter snow and cold
A fan (扇) painted with a picture of blue waves (滄波圖) hangs
Is it a gift left behind by a summer visitor from some year past?

Seeing a firelight at the mountain foot (山麓) across the field
I walked over on an evening stroll
And heard an old woman's (老婆) voice say, "Hey, let's wash the dishes."

Into a small bowl, the blue waves (蒼波) of the great sea
I scoop them for a while and place them on my desk
Loving eternity, I broaden my sea of the heart (心海).

Standing on a rock, the sound of breath exhaled
That breath sound goes away forever.
And then the next breath sound, and the next, and the next sound too.

Whoosh, whoosh, the sound of the wind rushing past
Its destination is also called eternity
Does this friend named eternity never know fullness (滿腹)?

If only I could turn the heavens upside down once
Life would feel so lukewarm here
I lift the foundation stone in the yard, then put it down.

Even for just a moment, the shoes that met my feet
Are getting rained on in the gallbladder container (?)
Seeing it as I pass by, my heart feels unwell.<57>

**Summary**

This collection of fifteen short poems (*tanga*) by Yi Ŭng-su offers fragmented glimpses into life in 1930s Korea. The poems move between observations of urban life (clocks marking time, a crying *kisaeng*, a madman on the street), rural decay (a collapsing latrine), natural imagery (a storm, a temple in winter, the sea), and personal, existential reflections (hating friends, shaving, contemplating breath and eternity, feelings of unease and powerlessness). The overall tone is melancholic, marked by a sense of instability ("不穩不穩") and quiet despair. For historians, the poems provide insight into the atmosphere of the colonial period, reflecting social anxieties, the contrast between modernizing urban centers and struggling rural areas, and individual feelings of alienation and a search for meaning amidst uncertainty. The mention of the weeping *kisaeng* and the general sense of instability are particularly evocative of the era's social conditions.

短歌十五章
						李應洙
시계가 우네, 長安에 정오가 와
數千數萬의 시계가 울어나네
오- 數千萬生靈이 一時에 웨칠날은?

세상이 너무 不穩不穩하드니
한울까지가 不穩하여 젓는지
今日의 이 강산엔 물베락이 나리시네

남이 볼가봐 얼골을 가리고서
대낫거리로 울면서 가는 妓生
엇던 산아이한테 모욕을 당햇슴인가

모든 벗들이 미워지는 날 아츰
마루에 안저 수염을 깍으면서
거울안의 내 얼골과 희롱을 하노이다

미친사람이 길가운데 섯고
그의 주위에 군중이 모여서서
오늘도 都會 길은 한울이 비웃어라

쓰러저가는 농촌의 便所깐에
面鏡깨어진 파편이 흐터지어
뒤보러 갈제마다 얼골을 비처주네

嚴冬雪寒의 釋王寺 솔가지에
滄波圖그린 붓채(扇)가 걸렷슴은
어느해 避暑客의 잇고 간 선물인고

들건너 山麓에 불비치 보이길내
황혼의 산보로 거러가 보앗더니
「야, 설거지하자」는 老婆소리가 나더라

곱부안에다 大海의 蒼波를
한참 담어다 책상우에 노코서
영원을 사랑하며 心海를 넓히이네

바위에 서서 뿜어노은 숨소래
그 숨소리는 영원히 가버린다.
또 그다음 숨소래 다음에 다음의 숨소래도

회회회하고 스처간 바람소래
그의 간곳도 영원이라고 하니
영원이란 친구는 滿腹이란 업는가

한울을 한번 뒤집어 보앗스면
삶이 이리로 미즈근 할데라고
뜰압헷 주칫돌을 들엇다가 노아라

단하로라도 내발에 대든 신이
쓸개통에서 비를 맛고 잇는게
지나치며 보랴. 마음이 안조터라＜57＞
