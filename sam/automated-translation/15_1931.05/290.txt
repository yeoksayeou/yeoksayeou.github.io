An Account of Encountering Misfortune Through Theft at the Ends of the Earth, A Close Call in Hong Kong
						Yŏ Unhong

About 5 or 6 years ago, I had some business in Shanghai and went to Hong Kong. The bustle of the Shanghai docks was considerable, but the coming and going of people and horses at the Hong Kong docks was also astonishingly complex.

I was about to disembark onto the pier, holding 4 or 5 bags divided between my left and right hands. As an aside, I adhere to the habit of never entrusting my luggage to others when traveling, whether on a train or a steamship.

Why is that? Well, living in a complex and chaotic city like Shanghai, there are many robbers and thieves. There are truly frequent instances where, if you entrust your luggage to laborers, they snatch the whole lot and run off. Moreover, even if you take considerable care not to lose your luggage, <61> I felt such intense displeasure, to the point of being fed up, with them demanding an exorbitant luggage fee even for moving a fist-sized bag just two or three steps. Therefore, I maintained the principle of carrying my own luggage myself under any circumstances.

As many of you who have been to Shanghai may know, you can encounter great misfortune just by mistakenly choosing the wrong ferryboat to get from a steamship anchored in the nearby sea to the docks. The boatmen, even while rowing, might suddenly point a six-shooter at a passenger's chest, rob them of all their money, strip them of the clothes they are wearing, and in the most extreme cases, even secretly take their life and dump the body into the waters of the Hwangp'ot'an'gang (黃浦灘江).

In a place like this, vast in territory and population, and where the net of the law is not so well organized, such things are perhaps unavoidable.

Anyway, holding five bags divided between my two hands, and having no choice but to hold my ship ticket in my mouth, I was just about to step onto the pier. In front of me, a crowd of people milled about, and behind me, many Western passengers were lining up to disembark. At that moment, I realized there was a strange sensation near my pocket.

As if struck by lightning, I quickly dropped my luggage and swung my hand behind me. There, I grabbed someone else's wrist.

He was a Chinese coolie. He already had my wallet grasped in his fingertips. Had I been a moment later, wouldn't I have suffered the great hardship of being penniless in this foreign land where I knew no one?

I slapped him once across the cheek. Then I let go of the wrist I had grabbed. He muttered some curses and disappeared into the crowd. It was truly a hair's breadth escape from danger. <62>

**Summary**

The author, Yŏ Unhong, recounts a close call he had with a pickpocket upon arriving at the docks in Hong Kong several years prior. He prefaces this story by explaining his distrust of hiring laborers to carry luggage in Shanghai, citing the high risk of theft and even violence, including robbery and murder by ferrymen. He describes the chaotic Shanghai environment as justification for his habit of always carrying his own bags, which ultimately saved him from losing his wallet in Hong Kong. From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into Korean perceptions of China, particularly Shanghai, as a dangerous and lawless place during the colonial period. It highlights the risks faced by travelers and the author's personal strategies for navigating such environments, reflecting broader anxieties about safety and order in treaty ports.

天涯萬里에서 盜難逢變記, 香港에서 하마터면
						呂運弘
5,6년전 上海에서 볼 일이 잇서 香港으로 간 일이 잇섯다. 上海 부두의 雜踏도 여간한 것이 아니지만 香港 부두의 人馬의 왕래도 놀납게 복잡하엿섯다.
나는 汽船에서 가방 4,5개를 좌우 두 손에 갈너쥐고 부두로 내려가려 하엿다. 딴말이지만은 나는 여행할 때에는 결단코 짐을 남에게 맛기지안는 습관를 직힌다. 기차에서나 기선에서나.
그것은 엇재 그런가하면 上海가튼 복잡하고 亂脈의 都會에 살자면 强窈盜가 만허서 노동자들에게 짐을 맛기면 송두리채 집어먹고 다라나는 弊가 정말 頻頻하고 또 여간 주의를 하여 짐을 일치안는다 할지라도＜61＞ 주먹만한 짐을 두세발자곡 옴기여 노코도 짐싹을 굉장히 쫄나내는 그에 진저리가 나도록 불쾌를 늣기엇슴으로 나는 엇든 경우에든지 내손으로 내짐을 운반한다는 주장을 가젓든 터이다.
上海에 가보신 이도 만흘 터이니 아시겟지만 上海近海에 정박한 기선에서부터 부두로 오르는 종선을 잘못 골나타도 큰 변을 맛난다. 선부들은 놀질하다가도 갑작히 船客의 가슴에 六穴砲를 드리대이고 잇는돈을 깡그리 강탈하며 입엇든 옷벌까지 벗기고 아조 심한 자에 이르러는 생명까지 몰내 죽이어서는 黃浦灘江물에 그 시체를 처너코 만다.
地廣人多할뿐더러 法網이 그리케 잘 정비되지 못한 이런 곳에선 엇절수 업는 일이리라.
엇잿든 나는 가방 다섯개를 양손에 갈나 쥐고 船票를 엇절수 업서 입에 물고 바야으로 부두에 내리려할 때 내압헤도 구릅가튼 사람이 오락가락하고 내뒤에도 만혼 西洋人船客들이 列를 지어 나리려 할 때 무엇인가 「포켓트」부근에 이상한 감촉이 잇는 것을 깨달엇다.
나는 번개에 마긴 듯 얼는 짐을 떠러트리고 손을 뒤에 돌녓다. 거기에는 엇든 딴 놈의 팔목이 쥐이엇다.
그는 中國人苦力이엇다. 벌서 내 돈지갑을 손끗에 쥐고 잇섯다. 하마하드면 이 아는 사람하나업는 外地에서 無一錢의 큰 고통을 당하지 안엇겟는가.
나는 그의 빰을 한 개 갈기엇다. 그러고 잡엇든 팔목을 노아주엇다. 그는 무에라고 중얼중얼 욕을 하며 사람들 틈에 사라지고 말엇다. 실로 위기일발의 순간이엇다.＜62＞
