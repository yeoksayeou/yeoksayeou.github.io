Setting Sun
						Han Yongun

The setting sun
Is like the end of a successful hero,
Both beautiful and sad.

Its vast remaining light
Shines upon the high mountains and distant rivers,
Adorning a splendid finale.

Suddenly, with the thin cloud's red sleeve,
It stealthily covers its distinct face
And wears a smile of farewell (訣別).

The swift current of the great river sings a dirge (輓歌),
And the slanted shadows of the myriad mountains write a history of the final moments (臨經).<64>

**Summary**

This poem by Han Yongun uses the imagery of a setting sun to evoke feelings of beauty and sorrow associated with an ending. The sun's final moments are depicted as a grand, albeit melancholic, spectacle, compared to the dignified demise of a successful hero. Its light illuminates the landscape one last time before it hides behind clouds, accompanied by the river's mournful flow and the mountains' shadows recording the event. From a historical perspective, penned during the Japanese colonial period by a prominent Buddhist monk and independence activist, the poem's themes of a beautiful but sad end, a final glorious display, and farewell could be interpreted allegorically. Historians might find interest in how the poem subtly conveys a sense of loss, the end of an era, or perhaps a quiet dignity in the face of decline, reflecting the constrained atmosphere for expressing nationalist sentiments at the time.

지는 해
						韓龍雲
지는해는
성공한 영웅의 末路갓치
아름답기도 하고 슬프기도 하다.

蒼蒼한 남은 빗치
놉흔 산과 먼 강을 빗치여서
絢爛한 최후를 莊飾한다.

忽然히 열븐 구름의 붉은 소매로
뚜렷한 얼골을 슬쪅가리며
訣別의 미소를 띄운다.

큰강의 급한 물ㅅ결은 輓歌를 불으고
뭇山의 비낀 그림자는 臨經의 역사를 쓴다.＜64＞
