**Summary**

This article from the May 1931 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine presents a hypothetical cabinet lineup for a Japanese Proletarian Party government, as imagined by an unnamed Japanese commentator. It lists prominent socialist and labor movement figures of the time assigned to various ministerial posts, vice-ministerial positions, parliamentary councilor roles, and key administrative positions, including colonial governorships. For historians of colonial Korea, the inclusion of proposed Governors-General for Korea (Abe Isoo) and Taiwan (Yamakawa Hitoshi), both well-known socialist figures, is particularly interesting. It reflects contemporary speculation about how a leftist government in Japan might approach colonial administration and potentially alter the political landscape in the colonies, offering a glimpse into alternative political possibilities imagined during that period.
