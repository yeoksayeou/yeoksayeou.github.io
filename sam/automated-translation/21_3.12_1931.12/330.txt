Magazine Title: 삼천리 第3卷 第12號
Publication Date: 1931년 12월 01일
Article Title: 그들과 우리 (Them and Us)
Author: 金若水
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

그들과 우리
						金若水

They are on our side
What are we seeking?

They, squeezed in the gap between nature and society
Are those who have become unable to avoid choosing one among
theft, beggary, or death.

They are like shipwrecked sailors (水夫)
Stripped naked, crying out in anguish
They are those who wander the crashing waves.

Thus, they are witnesses who expose social evils
before us, or rather,

They are the very first—at least—
Who kicked away all the spiderweb-like
Deceptions (긔만) and temptations of the old world (구세게).

They are fearsome—like a fire set in the dark night<96>
To hinder the repose (안면) of —. Is that all?

They kill people and sometimes throw away even their own deaths,
They are sons of strong and beautiful men, dwelling in unrepentant faces.

Oh, they are on our side
What are we seeking?<97>
<97-98>

**Summary**

The poem "Them and Us" portrays a group referred to as "they" who are marginalized outcasts forced into desperate situations (theft, beggary, death). They are depicted as victims ("shipwrecked sailors") who expose societal evils. However, they are also hailed as pioneers who rejected the deceptions of the old world and possess a fearsome, even violent, unrepentant spirit ("like a fire in the night," "kill people"). The poet, Kim Yaksu (金若水), declares "they" are "on our side" and rhetorically asks what "we" are seeking, implying solidarity with this group. For colonial history, this poem likely reflects the socialist or anarchist sympathies of the author, a known activist. "They" could represent the proletariat, marginalized Koreans under colonial rule, or revolutionary figures pushed outside societal norms by oppression, offering insight into radical ideologies and sentiments among some Korean intellectuals facing Japanese colonialism and capitalist exploitation in the 1930s.
