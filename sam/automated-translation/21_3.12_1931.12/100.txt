Samch'ŏlli "Wall Newspaper" No. 5: The Secret Story of the Dong-a Ilbo's Founding

As Governor-General Terauchi's silent and coercive military rule (무단정치) changed, and talk spread that Governor-General Saitō's so-called "cultural rule" (文化標榜 정치) was beginning, on the third day after the new Governor-General's arrival, a Korean youth, short in stature and of quite unremarkable appearance (風釆), aged about 25 or 26, came looking for the Governor-General.

With a logical (조리정연) and clear (명석한) tone, the young man emphatically argued that the Korean people must be given freedom of the press (언론자유). After hearing him out (言畢廳 후에), Mr. Saitō said one thing:

"それちや 新聞の 許可願ひを 出しなさい" (Sore ja, shinbun no kyokaganai o dashinasai - Well then, submit an application for permission for a newspaper.)

Hearing these words, the young man immediately turned and left, submitting an application for permission to publish a newspaper the very next day (翌日).

This was soon (未久) permitted and came out, becoming the first private newspaper (민간신문) since the annexation (합병): the "Dong-a Ilbo" (東亞日報).

This bold (대담하고도) young man whose meritorious service will never be forgotten (功勞不滅) was Mr. Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協) of that time.<26>

**Summary**

This brief article recounts the anecdote of how the Dong-a Ilbo newspaper received permission to publish in 1920. Shortly after Saitō Makoto replaced Terauchi Masatake as Governor-General of Korea and announced a shift from "military rule" to "cultural rule," a young Korean man named Yi Sang-hyŏp boldly approached Saitō. He argued for freedom of the press, prompting Saitō to tell him to simply submit an application, which was quickly approved, leading to the founding of the Dong-a Ilbo. For historians, this piece offers a contemporary (1931) perspective on the founding myth of the Dong-a Ilbo, highlighting the perceived abrupt shift in colonial policy under Saitō and crediting Yi Sang-hyŏp's initiative. It reflects the narrative of the "cultural rule" period allowing limited Korean expression, albeit framed within the colonial structure.

三千里 「壁新聞」 第5號 東亞日報 發行 祕話
寺內總督의 침묵 且 무단정치가 갈니고 齋藤總督의 文化標榜 정치가 시작한다고 말이 흐르든 新總督 着任 제3일만에 키가 적고 風釆가 심히 드러나지 안는 25,6세의 一조선청년이 총독을 차저왓다.
그 청년은 조리정연하고 명석한 어조로 조선민에게 언론자유를 주어야 한다고 역설하엿다. 言畢廳 후에 齋藤씨는 한마듸
「それちや 新聞の 許可願ひを 出しなさい」
이 청년은 그 말을 듯고 곳 도라나와서 그 翌日에 신문발행 허가원을 제출하엿다.
이것이 未久에 허가되여 나오니 합병 이후 최초의 민간신문인 「東亞日報」엿다.
이 대담하고도 功勞不滅의 청년이란 당시의 李相協씨엿다.<26>
