Oh, God!
						By Pang In'gŭn

The automobile carrying the prisoner (always hearing the nickname 'paulownia carriage') came from the red brick palace at the foot of Muhakchae Pass, carrying within it one prisoner dressed in red clothes, and dropped him off at the Chŏngdong courthouse. He was a young man named Kim Sŏkpo, an attempted robbery and murderer.

At dusk, when the red sunset glow danced in the western sky, the automobile picked up the prisoner again and passed before the prison gate. Sŏkpo had received a sentence of 15 years in prison today.

As if the world were turning upside down, Sŏkpo, still wearing the prisoner's hood (yongsu), lowered his head deeply and let out a sigh as if the ground would collapse.

—Fifteen years!

It was a long stretch of time. Although he had expected to receive such a sentence when he was caught, now that he had actually received it, the heavens and earth seemed to grow distant. For the first time, regret that he had committed the crime arose.

Suddenly, he missed his parents, wife, and child, and he missed the world.

Through a gap in the prisoner's hood, between the swaying curtains hanging on the automobile's window, he looked outside.

"There is Okch'ŏndong, where my house is," he cried out inwardly.

When the automobile reached Sŏktari bridge, the driver suddenly yelled, "Ugh!" and stopped.

Beneath the automobile, a young child had been hit. When pulled out, his skull was cracked and his belly burst open; dark red, clotted blood flowed out, staining the dry ground crimson. The child was a boy of about six years.

People cried out "Wah—!", each pair of startled eyes widening, faces grimacing as they looked down upon this small corpse. Their hundreds of hearts pounded wildly.

To them, the world seemed futile! The death of a human life is thus unfathomable! They felt uneasy, as if they themselves stood in a perilous place.

A short while later, the child's grandmother and mother, only just finding out, rushed over frantically, snatched up the blood-stained child, and began to wail.

Although the child lay limp, his dead face seemed to gaze resentfully at the sky.

The child's grandmother, her mind overturned, became almost like a madwoman, her face twitching uncontrollably, her limbs twisting as she only trembled violently. The child's mother cried out, piercing the listeners' hearts as if with a dagger,

"Poktong! Poktong! What is this talk of you dying!"

Sŏkpo, sitting inside the automobile, was startled terribly.

Poktong was the name of his own son. The owner of the voice calling Poktong was clearly his wife. Sŏkpo felt as if his chest was collapsing with a bang! He jumped up abruptly. The guard grabbed him and forced him back down.

Poktong was taken to the Red Cross Hospital. However, no renowned doctor could save him; he had already stopped breathing.

Poktong had been pestering his mother, who lived as a servant in the outer quarters (haengnangsar), asking her to buy him a bicycle, and had ended up getting soundly smacked. He desperately, desperately wanted to ride the child's bicycle that the master's son rode, but although he was allowed to push it from behind, he was never once told he could ride it.

That day, he had made a bold decision, took the bicycle out when no one was around, and was joyfully riding it on the street when he was hit and killed by the automobile his father was riding in.

Sŏkpo, back in prison, beat his chest, tore at his hair, and cried and cried again all through that night.

"If I hadn't committed the crime, that automobile wouldn't have passed that place at that time and killed my child... I am the murderer who killed my own child."

"Poktong! Poktong!"

He calls out, weeping. He could see the face of his plump and clever-looking son.

"Oh— God!"

For the first time in his life, he cries out, pierced by heartfelt repentance (통회).

The morning's red sunlight streamed smilingly through the crack in the window. <100>

**Summary**

The story recounts the tragic fate of Kim Sŏkpo, a man sentenced to 15 years for attempted robbery and murder. While being transported back to prison after sentencing, the vehicle accidentally hits and kills a young boy. Inside the vehicle, Sŏkpo realizes with horror that the victim is his own son, Poktong, and that the grieving mother is his wife. Poktong had secretly taken out a bicycle he desperately wanted but wasn't allowed to ride. Overwhelmed by guilt, Sŏkpo views himself as his son's murderer, a consequence of his own crime, and experiences profound repentance in prison. The story highlights the stark realities of life and sudden tragedy in colonial Seoul, depicting specific locations like Muhakchae Pass, Chŏngdong courthouse, Okch'ŏndong, and the Red Cross Hospital. It touches upon the justice system (prison transport, sentencing), social stratification (the mother's servant status - *haengnangsar*i), and the devastating intersection of fate and personal culpability under challenging circumstances, offering historians a glimpse into personal tragedy amid the broader societal backdrop of the 1930s.

오-하느님!
						方仁根
죄수를 태운 자동차(언제나 오동마차의 별명을 듯는)는 무학재 고개 밋 붉은 벽돌 대궐에서 붉은 옷 입은 죄수 하나를 태내 가지고 정동 재판소로 와서 쏘다 노핫다. 그는 살인미수강도「김석보」라는 청년이엿다.
붉은 「놀」이 서ㅅ녁 하눌에서 춤추는 저녁 때 자동차는 그 죄수를 다시 태워가지고 개명 압흘 지나갓다. 석보는 오늘 15년 징역의 언도를 밧엇다.
세상이 꺼구로 서는 것 가티 석보는 용수를 쓴 채 고개를 폭- 숙이고 땅이 꺼질 듯한 숨을 쉬엿다.
―15넌!
길다란 세월이다. 잡힐 때도 이만한 선고는 밧을 것을 예상하엿지마는 막상 밧어노코 보니 천지가 앗득하엿다. 비로소 죄를 아니 지엇더면하는 후회가 니러낫다.
감작이 부모처자가 그리워지고 세상이 그리웟다.
그는 용수 틈으로 자동차 유리창에 매달닌 커-틘의 흔들니는 사이로 밧글 내다보앗다.
「저긔는 우리집이 잇는 옥천동이다.」
하고 그는 속으로 부르지젓다.
자동차가 석다리에 다엇슬 때 갑작이 운전수는 「으악!」 소리를 치고 정거를 하엿다.
자동차 밋헤는 어린 아이가 치웟다. 끄내여보니 머리통이 깨지고 배가 터저서 붉은, 선지피는 마른 땅을 검붉게 물드리며 흘럿다. 아이는 여섯살 쯤 되는 사나희엿다.
사람은 와- 하고 저마다 놀낸 눈을 크게 뜨고 얼골을 찡그리고 이 조고만 시체를 드려다보는 것이엿다. 그들의 수 백개 심장들은 팔닥팔닥 뛰엿다.
그들에게는 세상이 허무하다! 인생의 죽엄은 이러케도 측량키 어려운 것이다! 하고 자긔 자신도 위태한 곳에 섯는 것 가치 불안스러웟다.
조곰후에 이 아이의 할머니와 어머니는 그제야 알고 허둥지둥 뛰여와서 피뭇은 아이를 번적 안고 통곡을 하엿다.
아이는 척 느러저 죽은 얼골이지만 하늘을 원망스럽게 처다보는 것 가텃다.
이 아이의 할머니는 정신이 뒤집혀 거의 미친 사람처럼 얼골이 실눅실눅 해지며 사지가 뒤틀녀 벌벌 떨기만 하엿다. 아이의 어머니는
「복동아! 복동아! 네가 죽다니 웬말이냐!」
하고 듯는 사람의 가슴을 비수로 예여내게 소리첫다.
자동차 안에 안젓든 석보는 소스라치게 놀래엿다.
복동이는 자긔 아들의 일홈이다. 그 복동이를 부르는 목소리의 임자는 분명히 자긔 안해이다. 석보는 가슴이 쾅! 하고 문허지는 것 가텃다. 벌덕 니러낫다. 간수는 붓잡어 안치엿다.
복동이는 적십자병원으로 다려갓다. 그러나 아모런 고명한 의사도 살닐 수 업시 발서 숨이 끈어젓다.
복동이는 행낭사리를 하는 어머니에게 자전거를 사달라고 졸으다가 그만 실컷 마젓다. 주인아들이 타고 다니는 아이 자전거가 몹시도 몹시도 타고 십헛스나 뒤를 밀어달나고는 하되 한 번도 타라고는 아니하엿다.
그 날은 용단을 하여 아모도 업는 사이에 자전거를 가지고 나와서 길에서 조화라고 타다가 아버지가 타고 가는 자동차에 치워 죽은 것이엿다.
석보는 감옥으로 와서 그 날 밤이 새도록 가슴을 치고 머리를 쥐여 뜨드며 울고 또 울엇다.
「내가 죄를 짓지 아니하엿더면 그 자동차가 그 시간에 그곳을 지나서 내 자식을 죽이지 아니하엿슬 터인데... 나는 내 자식을 죽인 살인범이다.」
「복동아! 복동아!」
그는 울면서 불은다. 그 토실토실하고 영리하게 생긴 아들의 얼골이 보엿다.
「오- 하누님!」
그는 생전 처음 통회에 사못치는 소리를 질은다.
아츰 붉은 햇발이 창틈으로 우스며 드러왓다.(끗)<100>
