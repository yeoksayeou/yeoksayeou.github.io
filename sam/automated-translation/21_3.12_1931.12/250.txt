交叉點
누구시든지 어느 분에게 듯고 십흔 말슴이 잇것든 본사에 투고하여 주십시요. 본사 旗手가 수신할 분에게 그 편지를 分傳한여 회답을 바더다가 본지에 발표함니다.
1. 용지는 엽서에
2. 의미는 간단하게
3. 내용은 사회공중에 관계되는 것

Crossroads

If there is anything you wish to hear from anyone, please submit it to our company. Our company's standard-bearer (旗手) will forward the letter to the recipient, receive a reply, and publish it in this journal.
1. Use a postcard for the paper.
2. Keep the meaning simple.
3. The content should relate to the public interest.

宋鎭禹 先生에게

貴東亞日報社에서 매년 개최되는 全鮮 녀자정구대회 때는 스포-쓰맨의 정신을 발휘하야 주시는 일일까요. 불황시대 우리들은 굴머 죽는다고 떠드는 때에 자동차로 戰地에 선수들을 운반하는 것은 좀 더 고려할 필요가 잇지 안을까요.
京城 寒村生

To Mr. Song Chin-u

Is it an act demonstrating the spirit of a sportsman when your esteemed Dong-A Ilbo company holds the All-Korea Women's Tennis Tournament each year? In this era of depression, when we are clamoring that we are starving to death, isn't there a need to reconsider transporting athletes to the battleground (戰地 - likely meaning 'venue') by automobile?
From Hanch'on Saeng, Kyŏngsŏng

苦戰할 선수를 피로하지 아니하게 하랴는 뜻이니 결코 無用한 일이 아님니다. 본사에서 운동장까지는 10리나 됨니다.
宋鎭禹 拜<71>

It is intended to prevent the athletes who will fight hard (苦戰) from becoming fatigued, so it is by no means a useless act. It is a distance of 10 ri from our company office to the sports field.
Respectfully, Song Chin-u <71>

方仁根씨에게

1. 선생께서 東亞紙에 실니든 怪靑年은 엇지 되엿슴니까?
2. 나는 문학에 多大한 취미를 가지고 지금까지 수 편의 소설, 소품, 희곡, 감상문 등을 지엿스나 발표할 길이 업슴니다.
발표하는 동시, 평을 밧으랴면 엇던 길을 취할가요?
(주의) 나는 지금 중등학교 4년에 재학중이올시다. 물론 대작품은 못될 줄 밋슴니다. 다만 진보의 길을 찻고저 헤메는 중임니다.
現城 답답생

To Mr. Pang In-gŭn

1. What happened to "The Strange Young Man" (怪靑年) that you, sir, were publishing in the Dong-A newspaper?
2. I have a great interest in literature and have written several novels, short pieces, plays, impressions, etc., but I have no way to publish them.
What path should I take to get published and simultaneously receive critiques?
(Note) I am currently a 4th-year student in middle school. Of course, I believe they are likely not great works. I am just wandering, seeking the path of progress.
From Taptap Saeng, Hyŏnsŏng (presumably Kyŏngsŏng/Seoul)

1. 마지막 회가 압수되엿슴니다. 내용은 말삼하기 어렵슴니다.
2. 쓰신 작품을 잡지사나 혹 개인에게 보내십시요. 그러나 성의것 보아드린다고는 할 수 업스나 조흔 작품이면 발표될 껏임니다. 그러타고 발표 아니되여도 끗까지 참고 습작을 게속하십시요. 발표 후에 평은 月評에도 나오나 역시 어떤 개인에게 부탁하는 수 박게 업슴니다.
方仁根 拜

1. The final installment was confiscated (압수되엿슴니다). It is difficult to speak about the contents.
2. Please send the works you have written to a magazine company or perhaps to an individual. However, I cannot say they will look at it sincerely, but if it is a good work, it will likely be published. Even if it isn't published, please endure to the end and continue your writing practice (습작). After publication, critiques may appear in monthly reviews (月評), but otherwise, there is no way but to ask some individual.
Respectfully, Pang In-gŭn

To Teacher Yi Kwang-su

I respect you, Teacher. And indirectly,<72> I receive love from afar. Your work, Teacher, "Prince Maui" (麻衣太子), has become half of my being. I hope you will complete the other half of my being, and for the sake of us all—finally, your current address—
Teacher Ch'un'gwang, Hŭngsang, South Hamgyŏng Province

李光洙

Reply to Mr. Ch'un'gwang
It is pleasant to hear that my work "Prince Maui" moved you deeply. I believe this is not due to my writing skill but is the result (所致) of the historical facts themselves and your sense of justice. I strive throughout my life to devote all my heart and strength (全心力) to writing good books, but I always regret that my strength is insufficient. My address is 127 Sungin-dong [崇三洞 is likely a typo or older name for 崇仁洞 Sungin-dong], Kyŏngsŏng.
Yi Kwang-su

**Summary**

This "Crossroads" column from the December 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features reader questions directed to prominent Korean figures and institutions, with their replies published. Topics include the Dong-A Ilbo's spending on a women's tennis tournament during the depression, the censorship of a serialized novel, advice for an aspiring writer, admiration for Yi Kwang-su's work, delays in a promised Dong-A Ilbo publication, the status of a women's vocational school, the activities of intellectuals like Ch'oe Rin and Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, the lack of Korean-language English materials, and concerns about Korean casualties in Manchuria following the recent Manchurian Incident. The column reflects reader engagement with contemporary social, cultural, and political issues, featuring direct interactions with leading intellectuals and journalists. For historians, it offers glimpses into public concerns (economic hardship vs. cultural events), censorship (Pang In-gŭn's novel), the literary scene (Yi Kwang-su's popularity, aspiring writers), educational matters, the movements of key figures, and the immediate anxieties surrounding the Manchurian Incident and its impact on Koreans living there. The use of staff pen names (Mok Pyŏngjŏng, Sŏk Pyŏngjŏng) for some replies is also notable.
