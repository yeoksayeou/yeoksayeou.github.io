**Body Text Translation:**
A Visit to a Vanguard Women's Group, The Formation of the Buddhist Young Women's Association - Said to be Expanding its Organization across the Nation in the Future -

It was noon on a day deep in autumn. Hearing a rumor that the Chosŏn Buddhist Young Women's Association Hall was near Kakhwangsa Temple in Susong-dong, I soon went looking for this hall.
The hall was right across from Kakhwangsa Temple—the former site of Posŏng High Common School. Although I had passed by its front more than once or twice, it was only belatedly that I learned the Buddhist Young Women's Association existed within that building.
Whatever the guiding purpose of this association might be, since it was already an organization working for the liberation of Chosŏn women, I thought it was truly regrettable for me, as a woman, to have learned of its existence so late, and I stepped through the main gate. The wide courtyard was unusually steeped in autumn colors, and the old wooden (木製) building, coated in paint (뺀끼칠), stood showing an appearance that disliked the chilly autumn wind. Of course, I didn't know which side the office was attached to. When I asked the janitor, he said there was a room a short way down the corridor on the left, and that was the room.
Having had the experience of failing several times when visiting women, whenever I visit women, I always have the thought, "What if they don't agree to meet me?" This day was also the same, with the same thought—hesitatingly, just like a child going to an unknown place for the first time—I went up to the door labeled "General Alliance Office," cleared my throat once, and gently opened the door. The office interior was spacious. Inside the large room, one man was sitting and attending to business; a scene entirely different from what I had thought before opening the door appeared.<71>
There, I felt the disappointment of "Another failure, I suppose."
However, wondering how things turned out this way, with the thought of going in and asking anyway, I hesitantly approached the man attending to business.
"Is this the Buddhist Young Women's Association office?" I asked.
Before answering my question, he first asked whom I was looking for. Rather than naming a specific person, I told him the reason I sought the place (thinking he would then find someone suitable for me to meet). At that point, he showed signs that he might help me achieve my purpose, so I felt relieved and took out my business card to give to him.
He also took out one of his business cards and gave it to me, showing a kind attitude.
His business card indicated he was Secretary-General (書記長) Mr./Ms. Chŏng. Feeling more reassured, I began asking, one by one, the several questions I wanted to know.
This association was founded early last April, he said, and its platform is:
1. Experience of the Buddha's Spirit
2. Establishment of Rational Religious Governance
3. Realization of Buddhism for the Masses
He said there are no affiliated organizations yet, and it is attached to the Chosŏn Buddhist Youth General Alliance.
Therefore, in its internal organization as well, it is said to have the same platform and regulations as the General Alliance.
Its organs are the General Assembly, the Central Executive Committee, the Central Inspection Committee, etc., and the officers are said to be as follows:
Executive Committee Chair: Chŏng Kwiin
Secretary-General: Kim Sukkyŏng
Director: Ch'oe Sunja
Head of Culture and Education Dept.: Kim Ilyŏp
Head of Physical Education Dept.: Kim Pokyŏng
As for its projects, it currently operates Myŏngsŏng Girls' School (明星女學校), Taeja Kindergarten (大慈幼稚園), Chamyŏng Night School (慈明夜學校), and the Myŏngsŏng Girls' Common School Research Course. Aside from a small subsidy from Buddhist organizations, it is said to be run purely by this association. The members number merely twenty-two, but one could not help but be surprised that they manage these four or five considerable educational institutions.
As the purpose of this association's work is for all propertyless children (전무산아동), the members themselves were personally taking up the teacher's pointer.
The total number of students, including kindergarten children, is seventy, and there are about fifty night school students, which is also not a small number.
I asked Mr./Ms. Chŏng again about the management method. Mr./Ms. Chŏng's response was still that the various executives work purely out of enthusiasm (Continued on page),<72> that the results are not poor, and that it is not particularly difficult either.
As they answered thus, there was nothing more I could say, so leaving behind greetings that I had imposed on their busy time for too long, I stood up from that spot and came out.
Warm sunlight shone peacefully in the wide courtyard, and the building, which had looked bleak, showed a courageous spirit as if to pierce the beautifully clear, fine sky.<75>

**Summary**

This 1931 article reports on a visit to the newly established Chosŏn Buddhist Young Women's Association, located in the former Posŏng High Common School building near Kakhwangsa Temple. Founded in April 1931 and affiliated with the Chosŏn Buddhist Youth General Alliance, the association aims to realize Buddhist principles, establish rational religious governance, and promote Buddhism for the masses. Despite having only 22 members, the group actively runs several educational institutions—a girls' school, kindergarten, night school, and a girls' common school research course—primarily for underprivileged children, with members serving as teachers driven by enthusiasm. The author expresses surprise at the scale of their work relative to their small membership and notes their stated plans for future nationwide expansion.

For historians, this article offers a valuable glimpse into the organized activities of Korean women during the colonial period, particularly within a religious framework. It highlights the role of Buddhism not just as a faith but as a platform for social activism and education, specifically targeting the poor ("propertyless children"). The organizational structure, connection to a larger (male-dominated) Buddhist youth group, the specific educational projects, and the names of female leaders like Kim Ilyŏp (a prominent figure) provide concrete details about women's social engagement and the challenges (implied by the small membership and reliance on "enthusiasm") they faced under Japanese rule. The mention of future nationwide expansion also speaks to the ambitions of such nascent organizations.

前衛 女性團體 訪問記, 佛敎女子靑年會의 陣形 -장차 전선에 조직을 확장한다고 한다 -
깁허가는 가을 어느날 정오엿다. 수송동 각황사 부근에 조선녀자불교청년회관이잇다는 소문을 듯자 곳 이 회관을 찻게 되엿다.
회관은 바로 각황사 마즌 편에 보이는―녯날 보성고보교 자리엿다. 내가 그 압프로 지나기가 한두 번이 안이엿스나 그 건물 속에 불교녀자청년회가 존재하고 잇다는 늣게야 알엇다.
이 회의 강영 목적의 여하는 엇잿든 벌서부터 조선 녀성의 해방을 위해서 일하는 단체인 것 만큼 녀성인 나로써 그 존재를 늣게 알엇다는 것은 너무나 미안한 일이라고 생각하면서 정문으로 바를 듸레노앗다. 넓다-란 마당엔 남달니 가을색이 농후햇스며 뺀끼칠 한 날근 목제(木製) 건물은 싸늘한 가을바람을 실허하는 빗츨 띄우고 서 잇다. 나는 몰논 사무실이 어느쪽에 부터 잇는 줄도 몰낫다. 수부에 가서 물으니 왼편쪽 랑하로 얼마 안가서 방이 잇는데 바로 그 방이라고 한다.
녀성 방문에 몃번 실패한 경험이 잇섯든 나는 언제나 녀성 방문을 할 때면 「맛나주지 안으면 엇지나.」 하는 생각을 가지게 된다. 이날도 역시 맛찬가지 생각을 가지고- 맛치 어린애가 모르는 곳을 처음 가는 때 가치 주춤주춤 하면서- 총동맹 사무실이라고 써 붓친 문 압헤까지 가서 한번 기침을 깃츤 후에 살그머니 문을 열엇다. 사무실 내는 넓엇다. 넓은 방안에는 남자 한 분이 안저서 사무를 보고 이섯스니 내가 문 열기 전에 생각햇든 것과는 전연 다른 장면이 나타나게 되엿다.<71>
나는 거기서 「또 실패로구나.」 하는 실망을 늣기게 되엿다.
그러나 엇더케 된 셈인지 들어가서 물어나 볼여는 생각으로 어름어름 사무보는 남성 압흐로 갓가히 가서.
「여기가 불교녀자청년회 사무실입닛가?」 하고 물어보앗다.
그는 나의 뭇는 말에 대답하기 전 먼저 누구를 찻는냐고 뭇는다. 나는 누구라는 일홈을 지적하기 보담 그에게 내가 그곳을 차즌 까닭을 말하엿다.(그러면 그가 내가 맛날 만한 사람을 차저 줄 줄 알고) 거기서 그는 내 목적을 달성식혀 줄 듯한 눈치를 보엿기 때문에 나는 안심하고 명함을 꺼내어 그분에게 주엇다.
그분도 나에게 한 장의 명함을 꺼내여 주면서 친절한 태도를 보여준다.
그의 명함에는 서긔장(書記長) 뎡씨라고 씨여 이섯다. 나는 더욱 안심하고 내가 알여든 몃가지의 질문을 하나식 둘식 시작햇다.
이 회는 지난 봄 사월 초순에 창입되엿다고 하며 이 회의 강령은
1. 佛陀精神의 體驗
2. 合理宗政의 確立
3. 大衆佛敎의 實現
이라 한다. 아직까지 연합단체는 업스며 조선불교청년총동맹에 부속되여 잇다고 한다.
그러기 때문에 내부조직에 잇서서도 총동맹과 맛찬가지 강영 규약을 가지고 잇다고 하며.
긔관은 전체대회, 중앙집행위원회, 중앙검사위원회 등이며 임원들은 다음과 갓다고 한다.
집행위원장  鄭貴仁
서긔장      金淑經
리사장      崔順子
문교부장    金一葉
체육부장    金寶經
사업으로서는 현재 명성녀학교(明星女學校)와 대자유치원(大慈幼稚園), 자명야학교(慈明夜學校), 명성녀자보통학교 연구과 등을 경영한다는데 불교긔관에서 나오는 약간의 보조 외에는 순전히 이 회에서 경영을 해간다고 한다. 회원은 겨우 스믈두 명이라는데 이러케 적지 안은 네다섯 교육긔관을 운반해 나가는데는 놀나지 안을 수 업섯다.
이 회의 사업의 목적이 전무산아동을 위한 것인 것 만큼 회원들이 친히 교편을 잡고 잇는 것이엿다.
학생 수는 유치원 아동까지 합해서 전부 칠십 명이고 야학생이 오십 명 가량 된다고 하니 그도 적지 안은 수다.
나는 다시 뎡씨에게 경영방힘을 물어보앗다. 뎡씨의 대답은 여전히 간부 여러분들이 순전히 열성만으로 일해간다 하며(제  頁에 續)<72> 성적도 불량지 안코 또 그닥지 곤란하지도 안타 한다.
이럿케 대답하는데 더 무에라고 말하기는 안되여서 밧부신데 오래 실례햇다는 인사를 남기고 그 자리에서 니러서 나왓다.
넓은 뜰에는 따뜻한 햇볏치 조용히 쪼이고 시산해보이든 건물도 곱게 개인 맑은 하늘을 찌를 뜻이 용감한 긔색을 보혀주엇다.<75>
