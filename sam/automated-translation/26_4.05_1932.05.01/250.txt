A Sad Song of Youth Whose Lover Was Stolen
Losing My Lover to My Younger Sister

An Elegy of a Tuberculosis Patient, Kim Chŏng-sŏn

The sunset at M Port in the south – beneath a sky dyed a gentle pink, the warm, late spring breeze, and the vast blue sea. I sat idly on the towering pier, gazing at the sentiment of the harbor where the waves freely danced.
Over there, seagulls danced in the empty air, and the faintly heard, heart-wrenching boat songs of the sailors seemed to tear my ailing heart ten, twenty times over. How could I not say it was agonizing!
Of course, others besides me might see this harbor sentiment – this scenery – as beautiful poetry or song, and also see it cheerfully. But to me, filled with suffering and wrapped in sorrow, everything looked like nothing but the mischief of demons.
I will now tell the story of my bitter history. If you, dear readers, would read it once, no, twice, or even three times, I would be grateful.

Three years ago from now – as soon as I left the gates of S School, I came to have a lover named Hanmin.
His face closely resembled that of the movie actor Charles Rogers; how could one not call him handsome?
And he had many talents; he could draw pictures and also knew how to write romantic poems.
I would go out with him to Ch'ŏngnyangni, avoiding people's eyes, we embraced, and we watched small birds sitting paired on tree branches, chirping away, bursting into laughter full of hope – this happened not just once or twice.
But the god of fate is mischievous and snatched this "romance" away from me.
I had a cute and beautiful younger sister named Mari. Unlike me, she held proletarian ideology to some extent. So she even went to reading groups night after night.
In my eyes, Mari was truly innocent and young. But Mari was a budding young woman.
One day, Hanmin, at the urging of a Marxist friend, also attended the reading group Mari went to, without even time to tell me.
Here, the tragedy of fate began. My dear Mari, unaware of the relationship between Hanmin and me, came to feel a certain passion for Hanmin. And Hanmin, likewise unaware that Mari was my sister, couldn't help but be drawn to the allure of Mari, who passionately approached him. Without my knowledge, they secretly whispered sweet nothings for almost a month.
Unaware of this fact, I saw Hanmin not meeting me as before, and his cold attitude growing day by day. I secretly agonized alone, staying shut in my room, only crying.
Sometimes Mari would come into my room and ask worriedly,
"Sister, is something troubling you? Why don't you tell me in detail?"
But I couldn't bring myself to tell the innocent Mari, so I couldn't speak.
However, as time passed, it became apparent even to me that Mari, whom I had only seen as innocent, was strangely taking great care in her appearance. So one day, I asked Mari like this:
"Mari, why are you dressing up so much these days? You used to tell me I was too extravagant and should fix my ways."
"........."
"You must have something fun going on these days. Huh, Mari?"
Hearing these words, Mari's face suddenly turned red, and she turned away sharply.
"Sister, you're just teasing me for no reason. What fun could there be?"
"But I know everything!"
At my words, bluffing that I knew when I didn't, Mari didn't know what to do.
"Sister, how do you know?"
"Well, there are ways of knowing."
"Sister, I'll tell only you. Please keep it a secret."
"Okay-"
"Well... I'm engaged. But he keeps saying he can't get engaged to me just like that, and he seems troubled. I don't know why. But it's not like he dislikes me either."
"So, who is this person?"
"Well..."
"Go on, tell me. Do you have secrets from me too?"
"Why don't you tell me yours, sister?"
"If you tell yours first, I'll tell mine."
"I'm embarrassed! Well... uh... it's a person named Hanmin who lives in Ke-dong."
"Eh- Mr. Hanmin?"
The response came out involuntarily. How could I have known that Mari would deliver such news to me, who was even lying sick in bed because of him? Though I tried hard to hold back the flowing tears, tears inevitably streamed from my eyes. Mari asked,
"Sister, why are you crying?"
What could I answer? I could only say I was crying because I was overjoyed.
Still wondering if perhaps it was a different Hanmin, I asked,
"So, what does he look like?"<27>
"I have his picture, shall I show you?"
She took out her lover's photograph from her handbag. Seeing the photo, I was even more at a loss, because it was the very same photograph Hanmin had given me.
After forcibly controlling my emotions, I sent Mari out and continued to weep silently for some time.

A few days later, quite unexpectedly, Hanmin sent me a letter.
"Miss Chŏng-sŏn, I hear you are unwell. I am truly sorry. I won't speak at length. Only yesterday, hearing Mari's words, did I learn that you and Miss Mari are sisters. Miss Mari was so innocent that she said she would love me even if she had to sacrifice her life. Being weak-hearted, I was drawn to Mari's charm and betrayed you during that time.
I am tormented and intend to leave for somewhere far away. A place where I won't see you, nor Mari. That would be the best course of action, wouldn't it? I have not told Miss Mari about my past with you. My conscience bothers me too much— Well then, I will end here."
From Hanmin
After reading the letter and thinking it over— I decided to sacrifice myself and gently entrust the man I had loved to my dear sister Mari for her sake. So I wrote the following final letter.

Mr. Hanmin—
How much you must be suffering? I do not resent you either. I merely laugh scornfully at the strange fate of this world. I do not intend to write at length either. I wish to conclude with just one request. Mr. Hanmin, if you do not grant my request, this body will repay you with death. I have already forgotten you. But Mari loves you deeply. If you were to abandon Mari and leave, Mari would not be able to live. Therefore, please enjoy eternal happiness with Mari. May you always be well.
From Chŏng-sŏn
What must my heart have felt like throwing such a letter at him, but I intended to sacrifice myself for the happiness of my beloved sister.
My illness worsened progressively after that. When I saw a doctor, they declared it was tuberculosis, which is how I ended up drifting to this port.
My mother waits, thinking I will return after recovering from my illness in a place with good air, but I only become more tormented and melancholic amidst the heart-wrenching sentiment of this port.

Today is Hanmin and Mari's wedding day, so after sending a congratulatory telegram at eight in the morning, I lay down wrapped in my blanket. Coming out to the seaside, the time was sunset. The mood of the harbor at this time seems only sorrowful and agonizingly painful.
Picturing Mari's wedding ceremony before my eyes, and on the other hand, thinking of my own death which is not far off, my heart felt as if it were rotting away.
Could I ever have imagined, even in my dreams, that my life would end so wretchedly?
The sea waves surged, white clouds floated in the distant sky, and even the 'poong-na' sound of a steamship's siren felt all the more sorrowful. -The End-

"Waiting a Lifetime for a Fiancé" Is it alright not to keep promises made in youth?

Yun Sun-bok

That summer – three years ago, as the hot sun dipped below the western mountains and the sunset glow dyed the western sky red, he brought me a cluster of bright red roses.
And so, receiving the red roses from the handsome man, my tender girlish heart began to flutter!
If my mother had been there then – and if my older brother had been there – the tender bud of first love between him and me would not have sprouted—
In my heart, which grew up ignorant of any worldly storms, did the flame of love ignite so quickly!!
From the day he first brought me the flower cluster, I began to feel tormented, picturing only him before my eyes.
My older brother knew that I was agonizing over him like this. So one day, my brother apparently said this to him:
That he was entrusting his younger sister to him.
But he replied that since his father had passed away, he was constrained financially and in other aspects as well, and thus could not bear such a great responsibility.
At that time – three years ago – it was when he had stopped school due to his father's passing and returned to his hometown.
Even after hearing from my brother that he hesitated to get engaged to me, I simply dismissed it as words of modesty and came to feel an even greater passion for him.
And he didn't seem reluctant to meet me either, often creating opportunities for us to meet.
He even told me stories about some female siblings competitively trying to court him, and sometimes just the two of us went for walks in the countryside. Thinking back now, it was all just a clear, beautiful phantasm (幻影).

Not long after that, he went back to school and left for Tokyo. Alone, I could only replay the short records of the past and wait for even a single letter from him. But he nonchalantly sent letters only to my older brother.
That letter ruthlessly trampled my expectations – no, my beautiful dream.
"I hold favorable feelings towards your sister, but I cannot ask her to wait until I graduate." This sentence appeared blurry to my tear-filled eyes.
I knew well that he had delivered the verdict that he couldn't get engaged to me. Yet, I waited for him for the long period of three years.
But after those agonizing three years passed, and though there was no change in my circumstances, he did not convey any happy news to me. During that time, I entered the workforce and experienced life as a working woman. To wait for him. And to avoid many suitors. For his sake, I was a stronger woman than anyone.
Knowing he was someone who wouldn't come, I still clung to a small hope and waited for him. In my heart, the red rose he gave me still blooms. But then I heard news that felt like my heart was bursting – that my mind went blank. He was going to continue his studies for another three years.
I just want to abandon myself to passion and run off to where he has gone.
Must I wait again for the long period of another three years?
The third spring has come, but the one I wait for shows no sign of coming.
With haze shimmering on the mountains and the river flowing blue, spring has undoubtedly arrived. Though when wouldn't it be painful?
This time, when birds sing in the trees and butterflies flutter around flower clusters, is the most painful for me.
I have no intention of hindering his studies. I wish and hope that he studies as much as possible to cultivate a splendid character. My only wish is that if he would just give his consent to an engagement, all the resentment of my life would be resolved. I want to know if it's because he holds no favorable feelings towards me, or if he truly cannot permit an engagement for the sake of his studies. I have sent letters<28> letters many times, but there is not a single reply.
Picturing only the distant, fading shadow before my eyes, should I spend the painful days ahead in tears, cursing my unfortunate fate? Who could have known that the bud of love, secretly sprouting in my young girlish heart, would be trampled so wretchedly?
As memories fade, they only become fresher; what should I do!?
(Night of April 1st, 2:00 AM)
From Kahoe-dong

I Want to Go Back to My Maiden Days—The Fading Shadow of Marriage

Han Sin-hye

It was long after the icicles under the eaves had melted, and the wearying chilly wind had ceased.
That day too, he and I – the two of us, with hearts brimming full of the beautiful dream of first love, spent the whole day together with the vibrant spring, wandering through the quiet suburbs of Tokyo.
When we returned to the boarding house, the electric lights shone brightly – it was around nine in the evening. The landlady handed me a letter, saying,
"It's Sunday today, so you're having a good time. I didn't know such a late sleeper could wake up so early just because it's Sunday," she laughed, and I acknowledged her words weren't unreasonable.
"When did this letter arrive?"
"It already came during the morning delivery time."
The letter was from B, a friend I was closest to during my higher common school days. As there had been no news for a whole year, I had worried at times, so I was extremely glad to receive it.

Sin-hye! It’s truly been a long time. The time when we cried and lamented, parting as if we couldn't live separated, has already flowed by for a long year. During that time, many changes must have occurred for us too. Especially for me?!
Sin-hye! I hear you're engaged? I'm happy for you. I congratulate you. I await your wedding day.
No! No! I resent your engagement. I am tormented. And I fear the approaching wedding day.
Sin-hye! You, as you are now, might think of me as an enemy for saying this. But—when the time comes, you will realize that Sŏng-hye's letter—words from a certain time—were the "truth." Sin-hye! Don't curse me!!
You know well too. That I had my wedding ceremony the day after graduation!
With a man handsome, high-status, and wealthy—I achieved a grand wedding amidst the blessings and envy (欽望裡) of many. Consequently, B herself and I felt a happiness that only we could savor. But now, for what reason—for what cause—I have come to the point where I must send you such a letter, I don't even want to say. Since telling you now would have no effect anyway! Sin-hye! I won't write at length. Today, hearing about your engagement from a certain friend, I unknowingly picked up my pen and have written this far. Well then, stay well. If there's a reply, I'll write again—.
Sŏng-hye, turned into a demon!

After reading the entire letter, I thought for a while. Why would Sŏng-hye send such a letter to me? Was she abandoned by her husband?
Even if she had been abandoned, would there be a reason to send such a letter to me? If abandoned, she would resent her husband and lament her own situation, wouldn't she? Why then... and wouldn't she rather bless my marriage— As I had these thoughts, I simultaneously began to harbor a certain suspicion.
I suspected if there hadn't been some relationship between Sŏng-hye and B in the past! And so, I read Sŏng-hye's letter again. It seemed plausible. I wanted to tear up Sŏng-hye's letter, and if she were beside me, I wanted to bite her. My jealousy exploded to that extent.
When day broke, I would lay Sŏng-hye's letter before B and ask him— no, rather than asking, I wanted to writhe before B, crying about why he deceived me.
The night of anger and jealousy passed. Though I had thought I would run to B as soon as day broke, once it actually became light, I found myself hesitating for some reason.
As usual, I prepared my book bundle, deciding to go to school at the scheduled time, and since B always dropped by on his way from school, I decided to meet him after returning from school.

However, my head was completely filled with the matter of Sŏng-hye's letter. I tried to endure and study until the last class, but I couldn't calm my mind, so I returned to the boarding house and just waited for B to come.
I didn't know what to do. I felt like I was going crazy. While B seemed detestable, my passion for him only grew stronger.
Before long, B arrived. Upon meeting him, tears flowed before anything else, making me unable to speak, so naturally B, not knowing the reason, became anxious. I finally managed to start speaking.
"Do you know a woman named Kim Sŏng-hye?"
"Why?"
"I'm asking if you know her?"
"I'm asking why you're asking?"
Instead of answering whether he knew her or not, B's tone of questioning me back seemed strange, making me even more suspicious. I threw Sŏng-hye's letter, which I had put in the desk drawer, in front of B.
B didn't say a single word like "Don't cry" to me weeping, but just kept reading the letter, which made me resent him terribly.
"Look here, Sin-hye! How can a person be so foolish? You look at this letter, fail to grasp what kind of letter it is, and misunderstand me. You are endearing though!"
"..."
"Is there any reason for me to know this woman named Sŏng-hye? I came straight here right after graduating from P'yŏngyang, so how would I know her? Didn't this woman likely fail in her own marriage and send this letter out of jealousy, seeing you happily married?"
B's words matched my initial assumption. Thereupon, my misunderstanding of B melted away like ice in the spring sun.
On the other hand, I couldn't help but feel an even stronger sense of hatred towards Sŏng-hye. Because her intention, as a friend, to try and shatter her friend's happiness seemed so cruel!!

The February sun in Tokyo was incredibly warm. Although the cherry blossom viewers weren't out yet, the sound of footsteps seeking out Hibiya, Ueno, Asakusa parks, Inokashira, etc., was loud. B, who would be leaving Tokyo in a few days, and I also walked together through deeply quiet places where sunlight reflected off green leaves, and we freely visited famous scenic spots—crowded places where people commonly go.
And so, the two of us became inseparable, making it impossible for me alone to return to Korea.
Of course, B had no intention of leaving me behind and returning to Korea, and I too felt it would be difficult to live alone and lonely in this foreign land without him, so without my parents' permission, I decided to drop out of school and return to Korea together with B.

When we left Tokyo, March was almost over, and it was early April.
The cherry blossoms were in full bloom as they pleased, dyeing the entire scenery of Tokyo in a gentle pink hue, further stirring the hearts of moving people and luring them out onto the streets.
Though Tokyo's spring was so wonderful, as I left that place with B, there wasn't the slightest<29> feeling of reluctance to part.
If anything filled my head, it was only the thought of having the wedding ceremony upon returning to Korea.
Wearing a veil, a habutae skirt and jacket, strangely colored high-heeled shoes, a diamond ring, and thinking about living happily with P in a two-story Western-style house playing the piano, the train's speed somehow felt frustratingly slow, making me anxious.
Nevertheless, without noticing the tediousness, we arrived in Kyŏngsŏng.
I got off in Kyŏngsŏng, and P decided to go straight to his hometown, P'yŏngyang.
Returning after a full year, I was joyfully welcomed by my parents and siblings, which was very pleasant, but it was merely momentary joy. For me, it felt as though there was no eternal pleasure outside of P. So, day after day, I was busy with preparations, wanting to hold the wedding ceremony quickly. Neighborhood people gathered around and enviously said,
"How did you manage to marry your daughter off into such a fine family?"
"The groom is a doctor, they say."
"They studied together in Tokyo."
"Looking at the photo, he's so remarkably handsome," and so on, praises abounded, making me secretly feel conceited that there was no happier person in the world than myself.

The awaited wedding ceremony ended, and our newlywed life began. We led a life more enjoyable than expected—a joyful and pleasant life beyond others' imagination.
We spent the spring without noticing its passing, and when the summer season arrived with leaves intensely dyed green, we went to the Wŏnsan beach resort.
At times when I thought of the people wandering, sweating profusely in the boiling city air, I would shudder intensely; perhaps it was because I myself was passing the whole summer without shedding a single drop of sweat.
We passed the summer, which everyone finds agonizing, without feeling any hardship at all. When the cool breeze began to blow and the leaves on the trees started to dry, we returned to our home in P'yŏngyang. P treated me with sincerity, just as he did during our Tokyo days, without the slightest change.
Our house, built with the clinic and residence attached, had enough trees to be called a park and boasted fine scenery. The young newlywed couple, like a pair of doves, hated to be apart even for a moment.
The autumn season, which had made me intensely melancholy and sentimental in my maiden days, was now spent only joyfully. P often repeated stories from our Tokyo days, making me recall those enjoyable times and blooming with laughter.
It was when all the leaves in our yard's trees had fallen, and white snow settled on the bare, skeletal branches. P's attitude— P, who hated to leave my side until 11 AM, needing the errand person to come in two or three times calling that a patient had arrived before he would go out— his heart strangely seemed to show signs of changing. He would wake up in the morning, have breakfast without much conversation, and then just go out to the clinic.
As P's attitude changed, I began to feel tormented about it and started paying close attention to his every action.
Day by day, as time went by, I began to discover his undesirable behaviors one by one. I learned he was a dreadful demon who violated the chastity of the nurses working with him. Yet, still dissatisfied with something, he wouldn't return home until two or three in the morning. Of course, he wasn't at the clinic either. I thought he must have gone out with some nurse, but upon finding out, that wasn't the case either. He frequented kisaeng houses. So he offered all his material wealth and passion to those women.
However, I merely wished inwardly that he would become the loving P I once trusted again, and I hesitated to say things he disliked hearing. But P's attitude only continued to change, and he came to be spoken of not just by me, but by others too, as an unprincipled person—a degenerate human being. I endlessly detested his actions, but then memories of the past—no, less than a year ago—of our time in Tokyo, or things we did at Wŏnsan beach—stories about how when we first met, walking on the sandy beach under the bright moonlit evening, our hearts just fluttered and we couldn't utter a single word; talks about the future plans for the baby still in my womb, not yet free from being a lump of flesh—when these came to mind, I missed him so much it felt like I would go mad. But as the days went by, he only hated me more, making the world seem only ruthless.
I wanted to read again the letter Sŏng-hye sent me when I was in Tokyo. But back then, I had torn that letter so thoroughly that not a single letter was legible, crumpled it, and finally even burned it, so there was no way to read it again.
The letter Sŏng-hye sent me— I clearly realized it was by no means sent out of cursing my happiness, and at the same time, I felt my face flush.
Among the men of the world, Sŏng-hye's husband—and P—could there possibly be other demons like them?
Blaming my own foolishness and ignorance, I cannot help but resent all men in the world, and I cannot help but curse the contradictory social system.
Meanwhile, time passed relentlessly; the winter when white snow fell also became the past, and the warm sunlight of spring gently pushed up the budding tree sprouts, and the catkins on the willow trees grew large, yet he didn't even know spring had come. And the spring season arrived, shining down with the meaning of the past. He didn't even know how to recall that spring when new grass grew deep blue in the flowerbed (花단). (The End)
- (Night of March 25th) - <30>

**Summary**

This article presents three first-person accounts from young Korean women in 1932, detailing their heartbreaks and disillusionment with love and marriage. The first, by Kim Chŏng-sŏn, recounts losing her lover Hanmin to her own younger sister, Mari, leading to Kim's illness (tuberculosis) and despair. The second, by Yun Sun-bok, describes waiting faithfully for three years for a man who subtly rejected engagement due to his circumstances and studies in Tokyo, only to learn he needs another three years, leaving her in limbo. The third, by Han Sin-hye, narrates her seemingly blissful marriage to P, whom she met in Tokyo, only to discover his infidelity with nurses and kisaengs, shattering her dreams and making her regret dismissing a warning letter from a friend. All three stories portray the emotional turmoil, societal pressures, and vulnerability faced by modern women navigating relationships, betrayal, and shifting expectations in colonial Korea.

From a historical perspective, these accounts offer intimate glimpses into the personal lives and emotional landscapes of educated, urban young women during the Japanese colonial period. They reveal the complexities of modern courtship, the influence of Western concepts like "romance" and "engagement," alongside traditional pressures. The mention of Marxist reading groups (Kim Chŏng-sŏn's story), education abroad (Tokyo features prominently in Yun Sun-bok's and Han Sin-hye's stories), and the emergence of "working women" (Yun Sun-bok) highlight the social transformations occurring. The raw expression of female suffering, disillusionment with male partners, and critique of societal structures (Han Sin-hye) provides valuable insight into evolving gender dynamics and female consciousness in 1930s Korea.

애인을 빼앗긴 靑春悲歌
누이동생에게 愛人을 빼앗기고

폐병환자의 에레지 金貞善

남쪽 M항구의 석양- 연분홍으로 곱게 물듸린 하눌 밋헤 따뜻한 느진 봄 실바람 그리고 항망한 푸른 바다 자유롭게 물결 뛰노는 항구의 정서를 시름업시 웃득소슨 부두에 안저서 나는 바라보앗슴니다.
저-편 어구에서는 허공에 갈멕이 춤추고 은은히 들여오는 水夫들의 애끈허지는 듯한 뱃노래가 병든 나의 가슴을 열 번 스무 번 찌저 놋는 것 갓흐니 엇지 괴롭다고 하지 안으리오!!
물논 나 이외의 다른 사람은 이 항구의 정서- 풍경을 고흔 시나 노래로 볼 것이오 또한 명랑하게 볼 것이오나 괴롬만코 비애에 싸힌 나로서는 모-도가 악마의 작난으로 박게 보이지 안엇슴니다.
나는 이제부터 쓰라린 나의 력사를 이약이하겟스니 여러분은 한번 아니 두번 세번이라도 읽어 주신다면 고맙겟슴니다.

지금부터 3년 전- S교문을 떠나자 나는 한민이라는 연인을 가지게 되엿슴니다.
그의 얼골은 흡사히 영화배우- 챨쓰로-자-만히 갓헛스니 엇지 미남이라고 안할가요.
그리고 재질이 만허서 그림도 그리고 로맨틕한 시도 쓸 줄 알엇슴니다.
나는 청량리로 그와 동반하야 나가서 사람의 눈을 피하며 포옹도 하야보고 나무가지에 적은 새가 짝을 짓고 안저서 재재거리는 것도 바라보면서 희망에 가득 찬 우슴을 터지여 본 적도 한두 번이 안이엿담니다.
그러나 운명의 신은 심술구저서 나에게서 이러한 「로맨쓰」를 빼앗서 갓섯지요.
나는 마리-라는 귀엽고 아름다운 누이동생을 가지고 잇섯슴니다. 그는 나와 달나서 어느 정도까지의 푸로레타리아 이데오로기를 가젓슴니다. 그래서 독서회에도 밤마당 갓섯슴니다.
나 보기에 마리- 는 정말 순진하고 어렷슴니다. 그러나 마리-는 움터가는 처녀엿담니다.
어느 날 한민이도 맑키쓰트 동무의 권유로 말할 사이도 업시 마리-가 가는 독서회에 참예하엿다구요.
여기에서 시운의 비극이 출발되엿슴니다. 한민이와 나와의 사이를 몰으는 귀여운 마리-는 한민에게 대해서 엇더한 열정을 늣기게 되엿스며 한민이 역시 마리-가 나의 동생인 줄 몰낫슴으로 열정으로 덤벼드는 마리- 의 인력에 끌니지 안을 수 업섯든지 나 몰비 거진 한 달 동안이나 비밀한 사랑을 속삭엿든 것임니다.
이러한 사실을 몰으고 잇는 나는 한민이가 전과 갓치 못나지도 안코 또는 랭정한 태도는 날이 갈수록 더하여 감으로 남몰내 혼자서 속만 태우고 방안에 드러 안저서 울기만 햇담니다.
각금 마리- 는 나의 방에 드러와서 근심스리운 듯이
「언니 무슨 걱정이 잇서요? 좀 자세 이약이 해 주시면 엇대요.」
하고 말햇스나 순진한 마리- 에게는 참아 말할 수가 업서서 이약이도 못 해 주엇서요.
그러나 시일이 흘으는 사이에 순진하게만 보앗든 마리- 가 웬일인지 몸단장을 몹시 하는 눈치가 나에게도 알여젓슴니다. 그래서 나는 엇던 날 마리- 에게 이럿케 물엇담니다.
「마리-야 넌 요새 웬 모양을 그럿케 내고 단이니. 날 보고 너무 사치한다고 뜨더 곤치라고 하드니.」
「.........」
「아마 요새 자미잇는 일이 잇는가보다. 응 마리야.」
이 말을 듯던 마리-는 갑작이 얼골이 붉어지며 픽 도라서면서.
「언니는 괜이 놀니네. 자미는 무슨 자미얘요.」
「멀 나는 다-아는데!」
몰으면서도 안다고 넹게치는 내 말에 마리-는 엇절 줄 몰으며
「언니 엇더케 알우?」
「글세 아는 법이 다 잇지.」
「언니 내가 언니에게만 말할게 비밀을 직혀주오.」
「그래-」
「저- 나 약혼햇서요. 그런데 그이는 작고만 나하고 약혼까지는 할 수 업다고 하면서 괴로워 하는데요 웬일인지 몰으겟서요. 그럿타고 해서 나를 실허하는 것도 아니라우.」
「그런데 그 사람이 누구야?」
「저...」
「말 하려무나. 내게도 비밀이 잇느냐?」
「언니는 웨 말하지 안이요.」
「네가 먼저 하면 나도 할테다.」
「난 북그러워! 저- 어- 게동에 잇는 한민이라는 사람이얘요.」
「엣-한민씨?」
하고 나오는 대답은 자발적이엿다. 그로 해서 병석에까지 누어 잇는 나에게 마리-가 이러한 보고를 전해줄 줄이야 엇지 알엇슬가요. 억지로 흐르는 눈물을 참는다고 하지만 나의 눈에서는 눈물이 흐르고야 말엇스니 마리는
「언니 웨 우시우.」하고 물어봄니다.
무엇이라고 대답하릿가? 나는 너무 깃버서 운다고밧게 할 수 업엇답니다.
나는 그래도 혹시나 다른 한민이 안인가 십허서
「그래 그의 얼골이 엇더헌냐?」<27>
「나한태 사진이 잇는데 보혀 줄가요?」
하며 핸드빽 속에서 자긔 연인의 사진을 내여 노앗슴니다. 나는 그 사진을 보고 더구나 엇절줄 몰낫스니 그것은 한민이가 나한테 준 바로 그 사진이엿든 까닭이엿슴니다.
억지로 감정을 제어한 후 마리- 를 내여보내고 나는 얼마동안 소리도 업는 울음을 게속하엿슴니다.

몃칠 후에 너무도 이외로 한민이가 나에게 편지를 보내엿슴니다.
「정선씨 편치 안으시다지요. 죄송하외다. 길게 말하지 안켓슴니다. 어적게 비로소 마리-의 말을 듯고 정선씨와 마리-씨와 형제간인 줄 알엇슴니다. 마리- 씨는 너무도 순진하엿든 까닭이엿든지 목숨을 밧처라도 나를 사랑하겟다고 햇슴으로 맘이 약한 나는 그만 마리- 의 매력에 끌니게 되여 그동안 당신을 반역햇슴니다.
나는 괴로워서 머-ㄹ니로 떠나렴니다. 당신도 안뵈고 마리- 도 안뵈는 곳으로 가렴니다. 그것이 제일 조흔 상책하겟지요. 나는 당신과의 과거를 마리- 씨에게 이약이하지 안엇슴니다. 너무나 양심이 붓그러워서요―그러면 이만 끈침니다.」
한민으로부터
나는 편지를 다 읽고 생각한 결과― 나를 희생하고 귀여운 나의 동생 마리-를 위해서 내가 사랑햇든 사람을 그에게 곱게 맛길여고 햇섯슴니다. 그래서 다음과 갓흔 최우의 편지를 썻담니다.

한민씨―

동경의 이월 볏은 몹시도 따뜻햇다. 아즉 사꾸라 구경군은 업서도 히비야 우에노 아싸꾸사 공원 이노가시라 등을 찻는 발자최 소리는 요란스러웟다. 몃츨 안이면 동경을 떠날 B와 나도 함끠 푸른 입사귀에 태양빗치 반사하는 그윽히 고요한 곳도 단엿스며 사람들이 혼히 가는 명승지―복잡한 곳에도 맘대로 단엿다.
그리하야 두 사람은 떠러질내야 떠러질 수 업는 사이가 되엿스니 나 혼자만 도저희 귀국하지 못하게 되엿든 것이다.
무론 B도 나를 떠러트리고 귀국할여고 하지 안엇스며 나 역시 그가 업고는- 이역에서 혼자 외롭게 지내기는 어러울 것 갓햇슴으로 나는 부모의 승락도 업시 학교를 중지하고 B와 함끠 귀국하기로 결정햇다.

우리가 동경을 떠나든 때는 3월도 다-가고 4월 초승을 잡아서엿다.
사꾸라가 제 맘대로 필 대로 피여서 동경의 전경은 연분홍 빗츠로 곱게 물드러 잇서서 움즉이는 사람의 맘을 더 한층 움직이게 하야 거리로 꾀여낸다.
동경의 봄이 그럿케 조왓건만 B와 함께 그곳을 떠나는 내 맘엔 잔별하기 앗가운 늣김이 조곰도<29> 업섯다.
다만 머리에 가득 찻다면 귀국하면 결혼식한다는 생각 뿐이엿다.
면사포 쓰고 하부다이 치마 저고리에 굽 놉흔 이상한 색구두 금강석 반지 그리고 이층 양옥에서 피아노치면서 P와 자미잇게 지낼 생각을 하니 긔차의 속력이 오히려 엇전지 안타가웁게 더딘 것갓해서 갓급하엿다.
그러치만 지리한 줄 몰느는 사이에 우리는 경성에까지 니르게 되엿다.
나는 경성에 내리고 P는 자기 고향인 평양으로 바로 가기로 햇다.
만 1년만에 도라온 나는 부모와 형제들의 깃브게 마저 주어서 하도 유쾌하엿다만 그것은 한갓 찰나의 깃븜에서 지나지 못할 뿐이엿다. 나에게 잇서서는 P외엔 영원한 즐거움이 업는 것 갓치 늣겨 지엇다. 그래서 하로 밧비 나는 결혼식을 속히 할 양으로 준비에 분주햇섯다. 동리 사람들이 모혀 와서는 부러운 듯이
「엇지면 따님을 그럿케 조흔 곳에 시가를 보내심잇가?」
「실랑은 의사래요.」
「동경서 갓치 공부햇대요.」
「사진을 보니 인물이 엇더케도 똑똑한지요.」등등의 칭찬이 자자함으로 나는 남몰내 세상에 나 이외엔 행복자가 업다는 자만심을 가지게 되엿다.

기다리든 결혼식도 끈나고 신혼의 살님이 시작되여서 우리는 예상 이상의 자미잇는 생활-남들이 상상하는 이상의 즐겁고 유쾌한 생활을 하게 되엿다.
봄도 가는 줄 몰으게 보내고 푸른 입사귀가 몹시 푸르게 물듸러진 여름철이 오게 되자 우리는 원산 해수욕장으로 가게 되엿다.
도회의 쩔쩔 끌는 공긔 속에서 땀을 철철 흘니며 헤매는 인간들이 종종 생각나게 되는 때 몹시도 몸소림을 치게 되엿스니 그것은 아마 내 자신이 한 여름동안 땀 한 방울 흘니지 안코 지나는 까닭인지.
누구나 괴로워 하는 한여름을 우리는 조곰도 괴로운 줄 몰으고 지나고 서늘한 바람이 불기 시직하야 나무 입헤 물이 마르기 시작하는 때 우리는 다시 평양 집으로 도라왓섯다. P는 동경시대나 이때나 조곰도 다름업시 나에게 대해서 성이끗 해 주엇다.
병원과 사택을 함께 부처서 신축한 우리 집은 공원이라고 할 만하게 나무가 만헛스며 경치가 조왓다. 신혼의 젊은 부부는 한 쌍의 비듧기 모양으로 조곰도 떠나기를 실혀햇다.
처녀때의 나를 몹시 메란고리-하고 애상적으로 만드러 놋튼 가을씨-슨도 이제는 즐겁게만 지내엿다. P는 종종 동경 시대때의 이약이를 다시 되푸리하며 녯날의 자미잇는 때를 회상하게 만들어 우슴의 꼿츨 피엿다.
우리집 마당의 나무입들이 다- 떠러지고 뼈만 남은 엉성한 나무가지에 힌 눈이 안저슬 때엿다. 아츰 11시 되도록 내 겻흘 떠나기 실혀서 심부럼하는 사람이 병자가 왓다고 두세 번 불느라 들어와야 나갈 줄 아든 P의 태도― 마음은 이상하게도 달너지는 눈치가 뵈엿스니 그것은 아츰에 니러나서 별 이약이도 업시 밥만 먹으면 병원으로 나가는 것이엿다.
P의 태도가 달너진 만큼 나는 거기에 대해서 고민을 늣기게 됨에 따라 그의 행동을 일일히 주의하기 시작햇다.
하로 잇틀 시일이 갈사록 나는 그의 좃치 못한 행실을 하나 둘식 발견하게 되엿다. 그는 자긔와 함께 일 보는 간호부를 헤여가면서 정조를 짓밟는 몹슬 악마엿음을 알엇다. 그러면서도 또 무잇이 불만햇든지 밤이면 두 점이나 석점이 되여야 집에 도라올 줄 알엇다. 무론 병원에도 업섯다. 나는 생각하기를 어느 간호부와 함께 나갓으리라고 햇스나 정작 알고보니 그러치도 안엇섯다. 그는 긔생 집으로 단엿다. 그래서 물질과 정역을 게집에게 다- 바치엿다.
그러나 나는 그가 다시 녯날과 갓흔 애정잇는 내가 밋든 P가 되여지기를 맘으로 은근하게 빌 뿐이엿고 그에게 대해서 듯기 실혀하는 말도 하기를 주저하엿섯다만은 P의 태도는 점점 변해갈 뿐이고 나 뿐 안이라 다른 사람들에게도 비인격자- 타락된 인간이라는 욕설을 듯게 되엿다. 나는 그의 행동을 업시 미워하다가도 녯날- 안이 1년 못되는 녯날- 동경 시대쩍 일 또는 원산 해수욕장에서 지내든 일- 달 밝은 전녁에 명사의 해변을 발브면서 첫번 맛나든 때에 서로 가삼만 울넝거리게 하며 말 한 마듸 못햇다는 이약이. 아직 핏덩어리에서 버서나지 한은 배ㅅ속에 잇는 것의 장래 사업문제까지 이약이하든 일이 머리에 떠오를 젠 그가 오히려 그리워서 밋치기까지에 니를 듯 십헛다. 그러나 그는 점점 날이 갈사록 나를 미워할 뿐이엿스니 세상이 악착스럽게밧게 생각되지 안엇다.
나는 동경 잇슬 적에 성헤가 보내준 편지를 다시 읽어보고 십헛다. 그러나 그 때에 나는 그 편지를 글자 한 자 뚝뚝히 보이지 안토록 찟고 부비고 나종엔 불에까지 살너 바렷스니 다시 읽어볼 길이 업섯다.
성헤가 내게 보낸 편지- 그것은 결코 나의 행복을 저주하는 데서 보낸 편지가 안이엿슴을 학실히 깨닷는 동시에 나의 얼골이 붉어짐을 늣기엿다.
세상의 남자 중에 성헤의 남편- 그리고 P 이외에도 또다시 그들과 갓흔 악마가 잇을 것일가.
나는 나의 어리석고 미련하엿슴을 원망하면서 세상의 모-든 남성을 원망하지 안을 수 업스며 모순된 사회제도를 저주하지 안을 수 업다.
이러는 사이에 세월은 덧업시 흘너서 하얀 눈 나리든 겨울도 과거가 되여지고 양춘의 따뜻한 햇발이 움트는 나무 싹을 조용히 올니밀고 버드나무에 벼들강아지가 크게 자라서도 그는 봄온 줄도 몰나섯다. 그리고 녯날의 뜻나려 쪼여주는 봄씨-슨이 왓섯다. 꼿담(花단)에 새풀이 파-랏케 깁든 그 봄을 회상할 줄도 몰나섯다.(끗)
