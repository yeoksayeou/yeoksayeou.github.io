Magazine Title:	三千리 제4권 제5호
Publication Date:	1932년 05월 01일
Article Title:	無産黨, 政友會, 民政黨 三政黨 中에 우리는 어느 政黨에 關心을 가질 것인가(諸氏見解) (Among the Three Political Parties—Proletarian Party, Seiyūkai, Minseitō—Which Party Should We Be Interested In? (Various Views))
Author:	諸氏見解 (Various Views)
Article Type:	설문 (Survey)

Still, the Proletarian Party

Hŏ Hŏn

The question of which party among the Seiyūkai, Minseitō, and Proletarian Party one should be interested in is quite an interesting problem. The existing established political parties were embodiments of XX-ism and parties centered on Diet members for the benefit of the privileged classes. These parties can be seen as having fully played their role as parties of the XX-ist state. And how much merit or demerit these established parties have brought in their political duties is a fact well known to the public, so there is no need to repeat it here. Generally, politics should aim ultimately to protect the interests of the populace, and concurrently, political parties should be institutions representing the interests of the populace. Now, the Proletarian Party has emerged. The Proletarian Party, according to its declared platform, differs from the established parties which were tools of the privileged classes until now, and claims to protect the interests of the proletarian class, which constitutes the majority of the populace; its significance of emergence can be seen in this point. Therefore, if it thoroughly breaks down the past monopoly politics (壟斷政治) and politics of concessions (利權政治) by representing the interests and demands of the masses, and realizes its platform for the popularization of politics, establishment of the people's livelihood, and protection of the entire interests of the populace, then that party can indeed be seen as having fulfilled its political duty.
Therefore, while acknowledging the political value declared in its platform to some extent, we pay some attention and interest to the Proletarian Party regarding its future political realization.

Just Watching Closely

Kim Myŏngsik

Among the three parties, Seiyūkai, Minseitō, and Proletarian Party, we are not interested in any of them, but rather watch closely as these bourgeois parties engage in factional strife. Of course, their factional strife occurs within certain limits, but it is not unnecessary to utilize even that.

Seiyūkai, Minseitō, and Proletarian Party are the Same

Wŏn Sehun

Among all the current political parties, which one should we have even a slight interest in? Regarding this question, firstly, I personally have never been interested in such parties until now, and secondly, referring to "we" as a majority rather than myself personally presents some points to avoid. However, I would like to state a few words of my personal opinion here.
Currently in Chosŏn (朝鮮), there seems to be a strong tendency to be interested in the Proletarian Party above all. Of course, Chosŏn is a Chosŏn where the entire nation is becoming proletarianized. For such a Chosŏn, if one were to have any interest in any political party, it would not be unreasonable to be interested in the Proletarian Party.
However, when I look at the policies toward India (印度) by all the cabinets of Britain's Conservative Party, Liberal Party, and Labour Party, I get the feeling that they are almost cut from the same cloth (一丘의 狢), and in practice, I could not find any points that could be called significantly different. It was common for anyone to approach the colonies based on the national policy of the British Empire (大英帝國). How much more so when the future of the Proletarian Party, even if it tries to become like the British Labour Party, is uncertain? Furthermore, recently, various factions of the Proletarian Party have openly shifted to the right, becoming separated from the National Essence Faction (國粹派) by a distance of fifty steps versus a hundred steps. From this perspective, it can be said that there is not a single political party among all of them worthy of even slight interest. However, rather than interest, there are points worth watching regarding Japan's (日本) political situation and national administration. In other words, what are the trends in its domestic affairs and diplomacy? What are the trends in the ideological world towards the extreme left or extreme right? There is a need to watch these as part of the general Far Eastern situation. Its domestic financial and economic issues and diplomatic issues such as Japan-China, Japan-Russia, Japan-US relations all have a general chain relationship with the overall Far Eastern political situation. However, this is off-topic here. (April 5, 1932) <3>

Not Much to Expect

Song Chinu

Of course, regarding the Seiyūkai (政友會) and Minseitō (民政黨), but also the development of the Proletarian Party (無産黨) might cause some changes in the political situation, so there might be some points worth noting, but there probably isn't much to expect. (Latter part omitted in original)

Interest in All Three Parties

Sin T'aeyak

It seems the question is somewhat unclear. Thinking about it, perhaps it means which established Japanese party we Chosŏn (朝鮮) people would or should favor. If so, the author wishes to state clearly that he cannot favor any of them.
And if answering by focusing on the given phrase "interest," then I would say that We should take great interest in each of Japan's political parties, the reason being the same meaning as the saying from the *Three Strategies* (三略), "The essential of military affairs is first to investigate the enemy's situation (必先察X情)," or from the *Six Secret Teachings* (六韜), "The art of military victory lies in secretly observing the enemy's movements (密察X之機)."

In Terms of Platform, It's the Proletarian Party

Sŏl Uisik

Looking at the political platforms and policies of the parties themselves, of course the Proletarian Party (無産政黨) can draw more of our interest, but as a "political party," it is too powerless, so perhaps the question of interest doesn't arise. And the Seiyūkai (政) and Minseitō (民) are like spots on a leopard [idiom: similar but slightly different]! If we become interested due to our minor immediate matters of gain and loss, then we become interested in both; if we don't care about such things at all, then both are irrelevant, and the difference in interest might not even be an issue.

The Proletarian Party is More Dangerous

Kim Yaksu

It goes without saying that the Seiyūkai (政友會) and Minseitō (民政黨) are long-standing representative bodies of landlords and the bourgeoisie, but the so-called Social Democratic Party (社民黨) and Mass Party (大衆黨), self-proclaimed Proletarian Parties, are also groups of petit bourgeoisie hungry for money power (金權), so not only are they not worth the slightest interest, but their danger is actually more severe than the former two.

Established Parties are Not an Issue

Kim Pyŏngno

The established political parties are not an issue. As for the Proletarian Party (無産黨), in its current state, nothing can be expected, but seeing how it transforms into some new trend, its future seems likely to hold much interest.

Seiyūkai and Minseitō Are...

Sŏ Sangil

The Seiyūkai (政友會) has the backing of the industrial capital clique (産業資本閥). Just as the Minseitō (民政黨) is likely a puppet (傀레) of the financial capital clique (金融資本閥), the Proletarian Party (無産黨), although transforming into a fascistic (팟쇼적으로) form... (Omitted in original)

If Interested, Then in All; If Not, Then in None

Yun Hyŏngsik

Whether regarding the Seiyūkai (政友會), Minseitō (民政黨), or the Proletarian Party (無産政黨), if one is interested, one would be interested in all—equally; if not, one wouldn't be interested at all. Since the current Proletarian Party is also a crystallization of the fascisticization (팟쇼化) of the petit bourgeoisie, special interest in it is probably unnecessary. As for the Seiyūkai and Minseitō, being identical bourgeois established parties, is there anything more to say?

There Are Interests Involved, So Interest Arises

Yi In

Looking at real life, Chosŏn (朝鮮) people cannot help but be interested in the ebb and flow, rise and fall of the various political parties in Tōkyō (東京). Seiyūkai (政友), Minseitō (民政), Proletarian (無産), they are all the same, but still, one will likely have somewhat more interest in parties with relatively progressive political thought (be it feudal or XXXX, still among them). Why? Because their policies impact the lives of Chosŏn people, making one feel the stakes involved. As for me, I thought I couldn't help but be interested in the Proletarian Party (無産黨) four or five years ago, but looking at the recent Proletarian Party, it mostly seems to be walking a path of degeneration rather than reaction, alienated from the masses. Moreover, hearing the recent news of its transformation into so-called national socialism has caused such revulsion (鱗逆症) in me that although I am interested in political parties, I cannot hold special interest in them as parties. If I still had to mention one that holds slightly more interest among them, I would have no choice but to say the Proletarian Party. This Proletarian Party does not refer to the currently formed one, but if one were to be formed in the future, that is.

Seiyūkai and Minseitō are Homogeneous

An Chaehong

Seiyūkai (政友) and Minseitō (民政) are entirely homogeneous, with no difference in interest. Even assuming the Social Democratic Party (社民黨) develops to the point of taking power, there would be nothing more than the recent 'MacDonald' [referring to Ramsay MacDonald and the UK Labour Party's perceived compromises]. Other left-wing parties might be somewhat different, but currently, not much can be expected, nor are they worthy of great interest.

Still, Perhaps the Proletarian Party?

Im Wŏn'gŭn

Even if there's nothing particularly remarkable, still, for us, it would be the Proletarian Party (無産黨) that draws interest.

Interest in All Three Parties

Yi Kwangsu

I am interested in all three parties.

The Proletarian Party is Also Becoming Fascistic and Powerless

Yi Yŏsŏng

One cannot help but have the greatest interest in the Proletarian Party (無産黨), but the fact that a whiff (냅새) of the recently fashionable 'Fascio' (팟쇼) has begun to emanate from a part of it, and its overall powerlessness, still fail to draw our significant attention.
Even if the Proletarian Party doesn't become like the British Labour Party (英國 勞働黨流), even if its quality is superior, its quantity is not small.

On the Rise and Fall of the Proletarian Party

Kim Kyŏngjae

If asking which of the three parties—Seiyūkai (政友會), Minseitō (民政黨), Proletarian Party (無産黨)—Chosŏn (朝鮮) people might have some interest in, whether nationalists or socialists, they would likely be interested in the rise and fall of the Proletarian Party (無産黨).

Not Yet Worthy of Interest

Yi Chŏngsŏp

Looking at the current situation, there is very little worth being interested in among the Seiyūkai (政友), Minseitō (民政), or Proletarian (無産) parties. If anything, it would still be the Proletarian Party (無産黨), but its division and suggestion of fascisticization (팟쇼化를 是事하니) make it a non-issue <4> There is no difference in political views regarding the governance of Chosŏn (朝鮮) between the Seiyūkai and Minseitō parties. They have no political views [on Chosŏn]. Only on the day when the issue of what to do with Chosŏn arises between these two parties and becomes part of each party's policy will the question of our interest arise.

Their Roles are All the Same

Hong Yangmyŏng

I am not particularly interested in any political party. In party politics, the roles of the two [major parties] are identical. Just as a whale living in the sea, a wolf roaming the mountains, or a cat kept at home differ in habitat and appearance but are ultimately all mammals, so it is [with these parties]. By this, I am not praising dictatorial politics. Moving towards dictatorships of both extremes is the important trend in world politics currently. Consequently, parliamentary politics is losing its light.

Theoretically the Proletarian Party, But...

Ham Sanghun

Theoretically, it is right to be interested in the progressive Proletarian Party (無産黨). However, in the current situation where the Proletarian Party is so weak (萎微) that it has no time to look after even its own internal affairs, where can one find interest? <5><3-5>

**Summary**

This 1932 survey in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine gathers opinions from various Korean intellectuals on which Japanese political party—the established Seiyūkai and Minseitō, or the emerging Proletarian Party—deserved their attention. Most respondents express deep skepticism towards all three. The Seiyūkai and Minseitō are dismissed as bourgeois parties representing privileged classes and capital cliques, offering no meaningful difference or benefit to Koreans. While some theoretically favor the Proletarian Party for its stated aim of representing the masses, they lament its current powerlessness, internal divisions, and alarming rightward drift towards nationalism and fascism, making it ultimately unworthy of significant hope or interest. A few pragmatically note the need to watch all parties due to their impact on colonial Chosŏn, while others dismiss parliamentary politics altogether. The article reveals a widespread disillusionment among Korean intellectuals with the Japanese political system and its parties, even the nominally progressive ones, highlighting their perceived irrelevance or even danger to Korean interests during the colonial period. Historians might find the candid critiques, the specific references to party factions (like 社民黨, 大衆黨) and ideologies (fascism, national socialism), and the comparisons to British politics valuable for understanding Korean perspectives on Japanese governance and the complex ideological landscape of the 1930s.

政, 民, 無 三政黨 중 어느 정당에 관심을 가지겟느냐 하는 것은 자못 흥미잇난 문제이다.  在來의 기성정당은 XX주의의 權化로서 특권계급의 이익을 위한 의원중심의 정당이엇다. 이 정당은 XX주의 국가로서의 역할을 완전히 하고난 정당이라고 볼 수 잇다. 그러고 이 기성정당은 과연 그 정치적 임무에 잇서서 얼마나한 功過를 가저 왓난지는 여기에서 重言하지 안해도 일반이 잘 아난 사실일 것 갓다. 대개 정치란 것은 민중의 이익을 옹호하는 것으로써 그 究竟의 목적을 삼어야 할 것이며 그와 동시에 정당이란 것도 민중의 이익을 대표하난 기관이 되여야 할 것이다. 이제 無産黨이 출현하엿다. 無産黨은 그 標榜 政綱에 의하야 지금까지의 특권계급의 도구이든 기성정당과 類를 달니하야 민중의 대부분인 무산계급의 이익을 옹호한다고, 그 점에 출현의 의의가 잇다고 볼 수 잇다. 그러기에 만일 대중의 이익, 요구를 대표하야 과거의 壟斷政治 利權政治를 철저히 타파하고 정치의 민중화, 민중의 생활확립 등 민중의 全的 이익의 옹호를 위한 政綱을 그대로 실현한다면 그 정당은 과연 정치적 임무를 이행하얏다 볼 수 잇난 것이다.
그러함으로 우리난 政綱과 표방한 정치적 가치를 어느 정도로 인정하난 동시에 그 장래의 정치적 실현에 대하야 無産黨에 다소 주목하고 관심을 가지게 되난 바이다.
