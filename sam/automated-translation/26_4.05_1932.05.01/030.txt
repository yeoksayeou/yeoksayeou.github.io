Magazine Title: 삼천리 제4권 제5호
Publication Date: 1932-05-01
Article Title: 中國은 列强의 領土分割과 共同管理를 免할가 (Will China Escape Territorial Division and Joint Management by the Great Powers?)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Will China Escape Territorial Division and Joint Management by the Great Powers?

Territorial Division and Joint Management Are Already a Shattered Dream

Yu Chin-hŭi (兪鎭熙)

The formation of the world proletariat! The workers within each imperialist nation, the workers in colonies and semi-colonies, the countless proletarian army latently containing proletarian characteristics, and their internal evolution and internal organization show the absolute might of the world proletariat's formation. Right now, this absolute force is showing absolute pressure on the heads of the imperialists. This pressure defends the Soviet Union and protects China as a candidate country for XX. The day is near when things like independent countries or administrative districts created by external forces will dissipate like a bad dream. No matter how much the tributaries, distributaries, side streams, and countercurrents originating from external forces may throw the land of China into turmoil, the main current (本流), flowing ceaselessly, is gaining speed in its fixed riverbed. In any case, the future struggle among imperialist powers over territorial division holds great significance in that it is the self-destructive action of imperialism itself and the deathbed cry of capitalism itself.

Joint Management Cannot Happen

Yi Chŏng-sŏp (李晶燮)

I believe there is no chance that China will suffer territorial division by the powers, nor will it be placed under their joint management. Why? Since initiating the national revolutionary movement, China, except for Manchuria, has gradually recovered the vested rights of the great powers, establishing national sovereignty while national unification has gradually progressed towards completion amidst numerous internal wars. In short, the total capacity of the nation has become very strong. This trend may be accelerated in the future, but it is unbelievable that it would diminish. Furthermore, the fact that the United States, truly the world's financial kingdom, insists on respecting China's sovereignty is something that cannot be lightly dismissed. The reason the US tolerated Japan's military actions in the Manchurian Incident was not out of affection for China, but because the day Japan obtained billions of tons of coal, iron, and timber from Manchuria would be a major threat to the US. Therefore, the importance of America's policy towards China (對中政策) will likely increase as time passes. Isn't America's slogan towards the great powers, "Hands off China"? For national defense and for the eastward expansion of the American economy, the US cannot help but shout this.

Depending on China's Own Awakening

Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹)

The question of whether or not China will escape territorial division or joint management by the powers will ultimately be decided according to the degree of China's own awakening.

Partition Theory is Past, Joint Management is Reality

Wŏn Se-hun (元世勳)

The question of whether China can avoid partition (派分) by the great powers or international joint management in the future is an issue that many people have discussed for a long time. Briefly put, the theory of China's partition was popular before the European War [WWI], and the theory of internationally managing China jointly became prevalent after the Great War. With the recent Sino-Japanese Incident, it seems both theories are gaining currency.
However, it does not seem to be a truly pivotal issue of the current situation. Discussing the aspect of partition theory, it has already been over 10 years since the Outer Mongolia (外蒙) region became independent under the influence of the Soviet Union (소베트 연방), and due to the recent Sino-Japanese Incident, the Four Northeastern Provinces (東北 4省) have become independent. If this situation persists, there is a non-negligible concern that the Tibet (西藏) region might become independent under British influence.
If such things are called partition, then one could say the stage of partition is already set. However, whatever the internal circumstances, this situation is referred to as the formal separation of border regions (邊方), not partition by the great powers. If so, what kind of partition is the current issue? This must refer to the possibility that the great powers will divide China proper (中國 本部). For example, the partition theory before the European War was that spheres of influence (勢力權) were established – Yunnan (雲南) for France, Guangdong (廣東) for Britain, Fujian (福建) and South Manchuria (南滿) for Japan, Shandong (山東) for Germany, North Manchuria (北滿) and Outer Mongolia for Russia – and upon the first opportunity, they would partition it all at once. Even now, such bygone dreams and 19th-century ambitions might linger in the minds of the imperialist powers. However, there are now two great powers, the United States (合衆國) and Italy (伊太利), which did not participate in such partitioning efforts. Moreover, the awakening of the Chinese nation and the post-Great War world trend (世界 潮流) absolutely do not tolerate such partitioning actions by the great powers. Consequently, even from the standpoint of the capitalist powers, it has long been realized that territorially partitioning and occupying China is practically useless. If there are still those who haven't realized this, they must be underdeveloped capitalist powers.
The capitalist powers, having realized the lack of real benefit (實益) in partition, and the imperialist powers, fearing that useless partitioning actions would only provoke disastrous wars (戰爭的 慘禍) amongst themselves, came up with a countermeasure (對策) other than partition. This new countermeasure was established after the Great War, and it is the policy of internationally managing China jointly. With this policy, the great powers have dominated (軍臨) China from immediately after the Great War until today. Looking at the reality of this policy, although the name 'joint management' is absent, the substance of joint management has long been practiced.
In short, no matter how the situation changes due to the Sino-Japanese Incident now, while there might be temporary separations of border regions, there will be no territorial partition by the great powers. Under the specious name (美名) of jointly assisting China's territory and independence, they might try to make the real benefits and scope of joint management more effective or expand them compared to the present, but they will likely not go so far as to hang up a signboard explicitly reading 'international joint management'. To summarize again, the theory of China's partition is merely an old theory from before the European War, while joint management is the reality being practiced after the Great War. China's complete independence is something to be discussed only after escaping this substantial joint management. Its current independence is nominal only; in reality (其實), it is a joint colony of the great powers.

It Will Be Avoided

Yi Kwang-su (李光洙)

I believe it will be avoided.

International Linkages

Yun Hyŏng-sik (尹亨植)

The resolution of the China problem has international linkages, so it will not be completely resolved by China's power alone; I believe it can only be resolved through the force of strong developments in internationalism (인터나쇼날리쯤).

Intervention in China Cannot Happen

Hŏ Hŏn (許憲)

The theory of territorial division or joint management of China by the powers has been advocated by politicians among the imperialist powers for over a decade. It originated from the anachronistic (時代錯誤) argument, based on the pretext that in the past, China lacked a unified established government, warlord (軍閥) private struggles (私鬪) were intense, international faith (國際信義) was not respected, and the lives and property of foreigners in China were persecuted. In recent years, as China's national movement became vigorous and consequently a unified government was established, theories like intervention in China naturally vanished. Instead, the great powers came to have much sympathy and assistance for the reviving China. Not only that, but with the end of the Great European War as a turning point, the aggressive policies of the powers towards China noticeably softened, and the Washington Treaties (華盛頓 諸條約) further pledged to uphold China's independence and territorial integrity, while also establishing various principles regarding the improvement of China's international status.
Furthermore, interpreting the League of Nations Covenant (國際聯盟規約) and the Kellogg-Briand Pact (不戰條約), even if China cannot escape economic exploitation by capitalist powers, it will certainly not fall into the fate of political territorial division and joint management.
This is all the more true given that the powers cannot adopt a unified stance due to their differing political and economic interests (利害關係) regarding China.

Concern That Divided Rule Will Become Substantial

Yi Yŏ-sŏng (李如星)

If China becomes 'Sovietized' (소베, 트 中國化), the powers will surely scramble to divide its territory or impose joint management. However, as long as China remains a capitalist China pursuing an anti-communist policy, the theory of partitioning or jointly managing all of China seems unlikely to emerge in the near future. This is because it could become a cause for world war, risking the rapid collapse of the entire order of third-stage capitalism. However, under the expanded interpretation of protecting residents (居留民保護) and defending vested interests (旣得權益擁護), there is a great concern, I think, that partial territorial dispersion (離散) will recur, eventually leading to the substantialization and rationalization (合理化) of divided rule. Therefore, the existence of a capitalist China is not necessarily a way to avoid partition; rather, it depends solely on the awakening of the Chinese people (人民).

Joint Management is Impossible

Sin T'ae-ak (辛泰嶽)

The world's great powers will likely be unable to carry out territorial division or joint management of China. The reasons are: (1) China itself is not the China of yesterday, (2) the great powers exercise mutual restraint (互相牽制) upon each other, (3) capitalist imperialism no longer solely demands the temporary expansion of limited (or to be limited) territory. That is to say, the world's imperialist powers have well realized that expanding their respective economic spheres of influence across all of China is more advantageous than the limited, temporary benefits derived from temporary territorial division or balanced joint management.
(Note) Regarding this, more detailed explanation is needed, so I intend to elaborate on another occasion later.

There Will Be No Partition

An Chae-hong (安在鴻)

Focusing on China proper, partition or joint management will likely not happen for the time being.

The Powers' Own Circumstances Do Not Permit It

Kim Yak-su (金若水)

Regarding China's territory, partition or joint management by the powers is further disallowed by the internal circumstances of the powers themselves.

Joint Management Will Not Happen

Yi In (李仁)

In the vast and abundant great market, since each power is frantically busy (血眼奔忙) pursuing its own interests, achieving cooperative, unified action would be exceptionally difficult. Even if they were to act in unison, it would likely amount to nothing more than temporary military action. Thus, mutually hesitating and being locked in a stalemate (牽牙相支), territorial division or joint management will likely not occur. Another reason might be that the so-called five great powers, still bearing the unhealed wounds (創痍) from the Great War, lack the capacity to advance into the East Asian political situation.

Difficult to Avoid the Powers' Control

Im Wŏn-gŭn (林元根)

Predicting the future path of China in its state of chaos (混沌狀態) is one of the most difficult tasks. However, its actual situation today, although lacking formal structure, is not far from joint management by the powers. As for territorial division, considering that numerous crucial domestic routes have long departed from self-governance, it is no exaggeration to say that partial realization has already been seen. In any case, as long as a complete unified government based on the populace's consciousness of life fails to emerge in China, it will be difficult to avoid the so-called control (콘트롤) of the great powers.

If National Sovereignty is Firmly Maintained, It Can Be Avoided

Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植)

If firm national sovereignty (國權) is maintained under complete centralization (even if there is partial<6> loss of territory), and if there is no internal strife, then even with the strategy of 'using barbarians to control barbarians' (以夷利夷策), joint partition and joint management can likely be avoided. Originally, the differing interests (利害) of the powers make it difficult to reach such a point, but if it were to happen, the global situation might change entirely.

There Will Be No Partition

Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯)

The Republic of China will not be subjected to territorial division by the powers or joint management by the powers. This is because China's patriotism (愛國性) today is incomparable to that of the past.

Compromise Among Powers is Doubtful

Kim U-p'yŏng (金佑枰)

Will China be able to avoid territorial division by the powers or joint management by the powers in the future? Today, the sense of conflicting interests (利害感) among the so-called great powers is extremely acute, leaving not much room for compromise (妥協). The attitudes of the great powers centered around the recent Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents prove this.

Already Experiencing It Now

Kim Kyŏng-chae (金璟載)

I see China as currently suffering territorial division by the capitalist powers and also being bound under joint management. If China is to completely escape this, it will be when a Soviet regime takes central power in China.

The Kuomintang Regime is More Dangerous

Kim Myŏng-sik (金明植)

As long as the Kuomintang (國民黨) regime persists, China cannot avoid becoming the target (對衆) of territorial division or joint management by the powers. However, the Kuomintang regime will not last long in the future.
Simultaneously, the influence of the powers will be swept away (一掃) from China. This will be realized through a Japan-US, Japan-Britain war, and since a Japan-US, Japan-Britain war is an inevitable fact, this is by no means mere fantasy.
Otherwise, China cannot be saved.

Dangerous If Resorting to War

Hong Yang-myŏng (洪陽明)

If the currently acute confrontation of interests among the great powers is resolved by resorting to war, there will be territorial division and re-division (再分割). If resolved through mutual compromise, it will become joint management by the powers. The current situation makes the latter possible. It is also actually progressing in that way now. The former [war] is unlikely to materialize for the time being due to the common sense of threat (脅威感) towards the Soviet Union (소베트 연방), the need for each capitalist nation to avoid war due to its own difficulties, and the current actual circumstances of the powers.
The day China escapes both the former and the latter will be the day the world's political and economic structure completely transforms, leading to different forms of politics and life.

Realization Difficult Due to Conflicting Interests

Ham Sang-hun (咸尙勳)

Due to the conflicting interests (利害相反) of the powers, joint partition and joint management cannot happen. The awakening of the Chinese populace and their traditionally crafty (老獪) diplomatic tactics will likely navigate the international situation well.<7>

**Summary**

This 1932 article presents a survey of opinions from various Korean intellectuals on whether China could avoid territorial partition or joint international control by imperialist powers. Responses vary widely: some believe China's growing national strength, US policy, or the rise of the proletariat will prevent partition; others see partition as a past threat replaced by the current reality of *de facto* joint control masked by nominal independence; several emphasize China's fate depends on its own national awakening and unity. Some foresee increasing danger, potentially linked to Sovietization or conflict between powers like Japan and the US/UK, while others think the powers' conflicting interests make unified action impossible. The Manchurian Incident is a key backdrop.
For historians of colonial Korea, this article is valuable as it showcases the diverse viewpoints among Korean intellectuals regarding regional geopolitics and imperialism during Japanese rule. It reflects their engagement with international affairs, their analyses of power dynamics (imperialism, nationalism, communism), and their varied prognoses for China, which indirectly mirrored concerns about Korea's own fate under colonialism. The range of opinions, from Marxist analyses (Yu Chin-hŭi) to nationalist hopes (Yi Chŏng-sŏp, Song Chin-u, An Chae-hong, Kim Pyŏng-no) and realist assessments (Wŏn Se-hun, Hŏ Hŏn, Yi Yŏ-sŏng, Im Wŏn-gŭn, Hong Yang-myŏng), offers insight into the intellectual currents of the time. The explicit mention of potential US-Japan conflict (Kim Myŏng-sik) is also noteworthy.

中國은 列强의 領土分割과 共同管理를 免할가
領土分割 共同管理는 발서 께어버린 꿈이다

兪鎭熙

세계푸로레타리아-트 構成! 제국주의 각국 내의 노동자 식민지 반식민지의 노동자와 푸로레타리아적 特質을 잠재적 형태에서 내포하고 잇는 무수한 푸로레타리아軍 그러고 그들의 내면적 진화와 내면적 편성은 세계푸로레타리아-트 구성의 절대한 위력을 뵈이고 잇다. 지금 이 절대적인 세력은 제국주의자의 頭上에 절대한 압력을 뵈이고 잇다. 이 압력은 소련을 防衛하고 XX후보국으로서의 중국을 수호하고 잇다. 外力에 의한 무슨 독립국 어느 管理區 가튼 것은 一場의 악몽으로 雲散霧消되는 날이 갓가워온다. 外力에서 오는 支流 分流 傍流 逆流가 중국 천지를 아무리 荒亂하고 잇서도 滾滾히 흘너 마지안는 本流는 일정한 河床에서 속력을 더하고 엇다. 하여간 영토분할 때문에 하는 今後의 제국주의간의 투쟁은 제국주의 자체의 自壞작용이오 자본주의 자체의 임종의 悲鳴인 점에서 큰 의의가 잇다.

共同管理는 아니된다

李晶燮

中國은 列國에게 영토분할을 당할 리도 업고 列國의 공동관리 하에 잇슬 경우도 업스리라고 봄니다. 웨? 웨라니 국민혁명운동을 이르켜서부터 오늘날까지 中國은 滿洲 이외에서는 열강의 기득권을 徐徐히 회수하야 국가의 주권이 바로 잽히는 동시에 국가의 통일도 許多 內亂 중에 점차로 완성의 域에 進하야 간단히 말하면 국민의 총 역량이 매우 굿세어 젓는 바 이러한 경향은 장래에 잇서는 혹은 가속도적으로 촉진될 수는 잇서도 도리어 減勢될가는 미더지지 안슴니다. 더구나 세계의 그야말로 금융 왕국인 米國이 中國의 주권존중을 주장함과 가틈은 경시할 수 업는 일임니다. 滿洲事變에 米國이 日本의 군사행동에 容吻한 것은 中國이 그처럼 사랑스러워서 그런 것이 아니라 滿洲에서 日本이 기십억噸의 석탄과 철과 목재를 엇는 날 그것이 곳 米國에 대한 일대 위협이엇기 때문이라면 米國의 對中정책은 시일이 갈사록 그 중요가 증가될 것임니다. 米國의 열강에 대한 표어는 『中國에서 손 떼어라』가 아닐가요? 國防上으로 米國 경제의 동양진출로나 米國은 이것을 부르짓지 안흘 수 업슬 것입니다.

中國 自體의 覺醒에 따라서

宋鎭禹

中國이 列國의 영토분할이나 공동관리를 면할가 아니할가하는 문제는 결국 中國 자체의 각성하는 정도 여하에 의하야 결정될 문제임니다.

泒分說은 過去, 共管은 現實

元世勳

中國이 아프로 열강의 派分이나 혹은 국제적 공동관리를 면할 수 잇슬가? 하는 문제는 발서 오래 전부터 여러 사람이 논하든 문제이다. 略論하자면 中國 파분의 說은 歐洲戰爭 전에서 유행하든 말이요 중국을 국제적으로 공동관리한다는 說은 大戰 이후부터 성행하는 말이다. 그러든 것이 금번의 日中事變으로 인하야 兩說이 共히 성행하는 모양이다.
그러나 그다지 시국의 중추적 문제는 되지 못하는 듯하다. 派分說의 방면으로 논하여도 外蒙一帶가 소베트 연방의 세력하에서 독립한지가 발서 10년이 지낫고 금번의 日中事變을 인하야 東北 4省이 독립하얏고 이 현상이 持久한다면 西藏一帶가 英國의 세력하에 독립할 우려가 不無하다.
이러한 것을 派分이라 할진대 派分의 局의 旣定하얏다 할 것이다. 그러나 이러한 局勢는 내용이야 여하튼지 열강의 派分이 아니라 변방의 형식상 분립이라고 한다. 그러타면 어떠한 派分이 現下의 문제일가? 이것은 中國 本部에 대하야 열강이 분할하리라는 말일 것이다. 예컨대 雲南은 法國의 것, 廣東은 英國의 것, 福建과 南滿은 日本의 것, 山東은 獨逸의 것, 北滿과 外蒙은 露國의 것 등으로 각각 그 세력권을 확정하여 두고 一朝에 기회만 有하면 일시에 派分하리라든 것이 歐洲戰爭 이전의 派分說이다. 至分에도 제국주의 열강의 腦中에는 그러한 昔日의 夢과 19세기적 야욕이야 不無할 것이다만은 지금은 그러한 派分의 한목에 참가하지 못한 合衆國과 伊太利라는 2대강국이 잇다. 그리고 중국민족의 자각과 대전 후의 세계 조류는 그러한 열강의 派分的 행위를 도저히 容認하지 아니한다. 따라서 자본주의 열강의 입장으로 논하야도 中國을 영토적으로 派分하야 점령하는 것이 실제에서 무익한 것을 발서 覺悟한 지가 오랏다. 아직도 각오하지 못한 자가 잇다면 그것은 발달되지 못한 자본주의적 强國일 뿐일 것이다.
派分의 實益이 無한 줄로 覺悟한<5> 자본주의의 열강 無益한 派分적 행동에서는 전쟁적 慘禍만을 互相間에 야기하고 말 것을 겁내는 제국주의의 열강은 派分 이외의 대책을 案出하게 되얏다. 새 대책이 大戰 이후에 확립되얏나니 이것이 곳 中國을 국제적으로 공동관리하자는 대책이다. 이러한 대책을 가지고 열강은 大戰 직후로부터 오늘까지 中國에 군림하는 것이다. 그 대책의 실제로 논하야도 공동관리라는 名만 無할 뿐이지 공동관리의 실질은 발서 擧한 지 오랏다.
요컨대 이제 日中事變으로 因하야 局勢가 如何히 변한다 할지라도 변방의 시간적 분립은 有할지언정 열강이 영토적으로 분할하는 派分은 無할 것이며 中國의 영토와 독립을 공동적으로 원조한다는 미명하에서 공동관리의 실익과 범위를 현재보다 좀 더 유효하게 또는 확대할야고 할지언정 名義까지 국제적 공동관리라는 간판 걸야고 하지는 아니할 것이다. 다시 요약하야 말하자면 중국분할의 說은 歐洲戰前의 舊說에 불과하고 공동관리는 실제에서 大戰 後에 행하고 잇는 현실이다. 중국의 완전독립이란 것은 이러한 실질적 공동관리를 면한 뒤의 말이다. 현재의 독립은 名義 뿐이고 其實은 열강의 共同的 식민지이다.
