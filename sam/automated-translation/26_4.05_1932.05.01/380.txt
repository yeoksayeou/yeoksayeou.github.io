Magazine Title: 삼천리 第四卷 第五號
Publication Date: 1932년 05월 01일
Article Title: [냄새 업는 마른 꼿을 이젓든 책 속에서 보왓다…] (I saw a scentless dried flower in a forgotten book...)
Author: A. S. Pushkin (Original), 蘇民 (Translator)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A. S. Pushkin (Original), Somin (Translator)

A scentless dried flower
I saw in a forgotten book.
This already feels quite fantastical,
My head is filled with thoughts.

What spring? When?
Where did it bloom?
And did it bloom long?
By whom was it picked?
By the hand of a dear one?
Or perhaps another person?
And why—did it come
To be here?

Was it remembering a happy meeting?
Or else a fateful parting?
Or perhaps a lonely stroll?
In the shade of the woods, in the quiet fields?

Did they live thus, and will they live on?
In what corner are they now?
Or perhaps they have already withered,
Like this nameless flower? <43>

**Summary**

This poem, a translation of A. S. Pushkin's work by Somin, reflects on finding a dried, scentless flower pressed within a forgotten book. The discovery prompts a series of questions about the flower's origin, the circumstances under which it was picked and preserved, and the fate of the person associated with it. The flower becomes a symbol of faded memories, past moments (whether joyful meetings, sad partings, or solitary walks), and the transience of life itself, comparing the potential withering of the people involved to the state of the nameless flower. For historians, this article illustrates the translation and consumption of foreign literature, specifically Russian poetry, in colonial Korea during the 1930s. It shows a channel of cultural inflow and intellectual engagement beyond the immediate Japanese colonial influence, suggesting an interest among Korean intellectuals like the translator "Somin" in broader world culture.
