Collection of Modern American Negro Poetry
						By Kim T'aesŏn from Washington

Henceforth, issue by issue, I intend to select renowned poems by Black poets from modern North America. Of course, the translated poems treated here are from the original poetry collections, and I also promise to attach brief biographies of the original poets. - Translator -

Spring in "New Hampshire"

By C. McKay

April's spring grasses are too green.
The silver-dappled sky is too blue. Whenever the happy wind laughs and passes by, ah! For me, who lingers, I only spend precious time indoors wiping window panes and mopping the floors.
April nights are too amazing.
The first May flowers are too fragrant. The stars sparkle too beautifully. When the stream murmurs and the fields are fresh, for me, spending this night,
There is only worry, exhaustion, and hazy slumber.

The Tired Worker

By C. McKay

Ooh, whispering, ooh, my heart— this afternoon is tilting towards evening— Softly whispering! Peace, oh, my rebellious heartblood! The moon will soon rise from the hazy "veil"! Be patient, weary body. Soon the night will quietly wrap a black single blanket around you and, with a soft voice, will invite you to rest your tired hands and aching feet.
The wretched day is theirs. The night is my night. Come, soft sleep. Hold me in your bosom. What is that wine-colored red thing blowing out grey clouds? Oh morning! Oh dreadful morning! Oh let rest! Me! My tired veins, my head, my life— Have pity. No! Again to that pier, to the hateful city.
Note=‘New Hampshire’ is the name of a state.

Biography of the Original Author

Mr. McKay was born in 1889 on the island of Jamaica in the West Indies. His only education was elementary schooling received from his elder brother. Later, around 1912, he immigrated to North America and studied while enrolled at Kansas Agricultural College, but he left school midway and subsequently tasted all sorts of worldly hardships. He worked as a hotel servant, a job often held by ordinary Black people, and for a time, he was also a train boy. However, Mr. McKay currently works as an associate editor for the monthly magazine called "The Liberator" (解放者). He has two poetry collections, (Songs of Jamaica, Spring in New Hampshire), both of which are worth reading. <30>

**Summary**

This article from the May 1932 issue of the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* introduces modern Black American poetry to its readers. The translator, Kim T'aesŏn (writing from Washington), presents translations of two poems by Claude McKay: "Spring in New Hampshire" and "The Tired Worker," along with a brief biography of the poet. Kim promises to continue featuring notable Black American poets in future issues. For historians, this article is interesting as it demonstrates Korean intellectual engagement with international literature, specifically from a marginalized group in America, during the Japanese colonial period. The choice of Claude McKay, whose work often explores themes of alienation and struggle, might suggest a subtle resonance with the experiences of Koreans under colonial rule, showcasing an early instance of cross-cultural awareness and potential solidarity expressed through literary introduction.

「뉴·함쇌」에 봄

씨·맥케이 作

4월의 春草은 너무나 퍼럿슴니다.
은색으로 점 박은 하눌은 너무나 靑靑함니다. 행복스렵은 바람이 웃고 지나칠 때마다 아! 머물머물거리게 하는 나의게는 유리창 닥기와 마루를 걸네질하면서 집안에서 귀한 시간을 보낼 뿐임니다.
4월의 밤은 너무나 놀납소이다.
첫 5월의 꼿들은 너머도 행기럽슴니다. 별들은 너무도 아름답게 빤작거림니다. 냇물은 솰솰 드을은 신선할 때 이 밤을 보내는 나의게는
근심과 피페 불선명한 조름 뿐이외다.

피곤한 노동자

씨·맥케이 作

으오 속살그림 오오 내 맘이여― 이 오후은 저녁으로 기우러가고 잇나니― 부드럽게 속살그림이여! 화평 오오 내 不服의 心血이여! 달은 미구에 몽롱한 「뻴」로부터 장차 떠오르러하도다! 참을지여다. 시달픈 몸이여 미구에 밤은 고요히 검은 홋니불을 몸에 두르고 그리고 가늘한 소리로 그대를 청할거시니 그대의 피곤한 손과 압흔 발을 쉬게할 냥으로 .
몹쓸 낫은 저들의 것이다. 밤은 나의 밤이라. 오라 부드럽은 잠. 당신의 가슴에 안아주소서. 잿빗 구름을 불너내는 포도주 빗의 붉은 것이 무엇이냐? 오 아츰! 오 무서운 아츰! 오 쉬게하라! 나를! 곤한 나의 혈관들 나의 머리 나의 삶― 동정하라. 아니다! 또 다시 그 埠頭로 보기 실흔 도시로.
註=『뉴·함쇌』은 一洲名

원작자의 약력

백케이씨는 1889년 西印度 짜메카島에서 출생햇다. 교육이란 오직 氏의 사형으로부터 밧은 초등 그것 뿐이며 其後에 1912년경에 北米에 이민으로 와서 『캔사쓰』농업대학에 재적해 공부하다가 중도 학교를 떠난 후 그는 온갓 世苦를 다 맛보앗든 것이다. 보통 흑인이 만히 일하는 여관 하인노릇도 해 보앗스며 한때는 기차 뽀이로도 잇섯다. 그러나 氏는 현재 『解放者』라는 월간잡지에서 연합편집인으로 일을 보고 잇다. 그의 시집으로(Songs of Jamaica Spring in New Hampshire) 2권이 잇는데 모다 읽은 만하다. <30>
