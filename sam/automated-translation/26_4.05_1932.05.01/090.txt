**Body Text Translation**

Joke
Divorce Scene

Ch'unshik: "How is it? Is their divorce proceeding well?"

Kwang'il: "Yes, it seems to be going very well, but the matter of the young child hasn't been resolved."

Ch'unshik: "Why is that?"

Kwang'il: "Well, it's because neither side wants to take the child."<9>

**Summary**

This short piece presents a humorous dialogue titled "Divorce Scene". Two characters, Ch'unshik and Kwang'il, discuss a couple's divorce. The punchline reveals that the proceedings are smooth except for the child, not because both parents want custody, but because neither parent wants the child. From a historical perspective, this joke, published in 1932 Korea, might reflect changing social dynamics and perhaps a cynical view of family responsibilities amidst modernization or hardship during the colonial period. It offers a glimpse into popular humor and potentially evolving attitudes towards divorce and parenting, making it interesting for historians studying social norms and everyday life under Japanese rule.
