Article Title: (笑話)再婚理由 (Joke: The Reason for Remarriage)
Author: Unspecified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Lawyer: "In this will, it says that if you remarry, all of the deceased's property is to be given to his younger brother."

Widow: "Yes, that's why I intend to marry that younger brother."<11>

**Summary**

This short piece presents a joke about a widow's clever response to a condition in her late husband's will. The will stipulates that if she remarries, the inheritance goes to the husband's younger brother. To circumvent this and keep the fortune indirectly, the widow declares her intention to marry the brother himself. From a historical perspective, this joke, brief as it is, subtly touches upon inheritance laws and the constraints placed upon women (specifically widows) regarding remarriage and property rights in colonial Korea. It highlights a woman finding a loophole within a patriarchal system, potentially reflecting societal attitudes or commentary on such matters through humor.
