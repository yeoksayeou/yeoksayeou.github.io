交叉點 (Crossroads)
If there is anything anyone wishes to ask someone, please submit it to this column. Our Company Representative (本社旗手) will pass the letter to the intended recipient, receive their reply, and publish it in this column.
1. Use a postcard for the paper.
2. Keep the meaning concise.
3. The content should relate to the public interest.

To Teacher Kim Tu-bong (金枓奉):

Teacher, since authoring *Kiptŏ Chosŏn Malbon* (깁더조선말본) in Shanghai, it seems you have had no other publications. Where have you been and what have you been doing during this time?
I would like to know your current address, place of family origin (原籍), and age. When will you announce your subsequent research on the "Horizontal Writing of Han'gŭl" (한글가로쓰기)?

T'aein (泰仁), Moebop

My elder brother is still in Shanghai. Since the publication of *Kiptŏ Chosŏn Malbon*, he devoted all his energy to *Malmoi* (말모이), and recently he is serving as the Principal of the Shanghai Insŏng School (上海仁成學校). Regarding research on "Han'gŭl," I believe there will be an opportunity to announce it. His place of origin is Kijang (機張), Tongnae County (東萊郡), South Kyŏngsang Province (慶南). His age is 43 this year.
(Younger Brother) Kim Tu-baek (金枓白), replying on his behalf.

1. What is the difference between Esperanto ("Essŭŏ," 에쓰어) and English?
2. Is it possible to self-study ("tokhak," 독학) Esperanto? If possible, please inform me of books and other reference materials.
From a Reader in South Kyŏngsang Province (慶南), Respectfully

1. English and Esperanto ("Essŭŏ") are similar in nature. If it takes five years to learn English, half a year or one year is more than enough for Esperanto. Knowing one foreign language makes any other considerably easier.
2. There are many self-study books ("toksŭpsŏ," 독습서) for Esperanto in Japanese, but pronunciation might be difficult.
*Esperanto Kōgi* (エスペラント講義) (Price 50 sen [錢]), Published by the Japan Esperanto Institute (日本 エスペラント學會 發行)
For reference, let me mention that I also have one called *Essŭp'erando Tokhak* (에쓰페란도독학, Self-Study of Esperanto) (Price 1 Wŏn 50 chŏn [圓 錢]).
Ordering it through Samch'ŏlli-sa (삼천리사) should be fine.
Kim Ŏk (金億), Respectfully

A Few Questions to Ask

1. Where is the Tonggwang (東光) magazine company located now?
2. Who is the editor-in-chief ("chugan," 주간) of your company?
3. In Chosŏn, what constitutes contemporary reading material ("讀物") for youth?
4. How many readers does your company's publication have just in North and South Manchuria?
From Puk'ansansaeng (北寒山生) in Qiqihar (齊齊哈爾)

1. The Tonggwang Company (東光社) is at Chongno (鍾路) 2-chome 92, Kyŏngsŏng (京城).
2. Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥).
3. It is somewhat difficult to pinpoint specific books, but in any case, please read many works by authoritative figures in various fields.
4. Our company's publications have quite a large number of readers.
Mokpyŏngjŏng (木兵丁), Respectfully

To Chu Yo-han (주요한):

Teacher, when you publish writings in various magazines, you always sign your name in Korean script (조선문). What is the reason for this?
From an Avid Reader in Ch'ŏrwŏn (鐵原), Respectfully

I am a "Pure Korean Script Advocate" (순조선문주의자). Except in unavoidable circumstances, I insist on using only Korean script. I hope such comrades increase quickly so that the "Abolition of Hanja" (한짜쳘페) can happen soon.
Chu Yo-han (주요한)

To Teacher Mun Il-p'yŏng (文一平):

I (소생) intend to study Korean history from ancient times (상고) to the modern era (근세). Please introduce the most suitable books. Please also inform me of the publisher (發行所), author (著作), and price (定價金).
From Wŏn Hun (元勳) in Samsu (三水), Respectfully

Regarding Korean history, there are several types for reference and textbook use, but among them, *Chosŏn Yŏksa* (朝鮮歷史), authored (撰) by Mr. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善), is easy to understand even for self-learners. It is overly summarized up to the middle ages and becomes very detailed in the modern era, so the structure seems a bit unbalanced, but the historical facts (史實) are accurate and the narration is plain, allowing the reader to grasp it at a glance. *Chosŏn Yŏksa*, authored by Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (崔南善撰 朝鮮歷史). Publisher (發行所): Tongmyŏngsa (東明社), Chongno (鍾路) 6-chome 1, Kyŏngsŏng-bu (京城府). Price (定價金): 1 Wŏn (圓). Postage (郵送料): 14 Chŏn (錢).
Mun Il-p'yŏng (文一平)<35>

**Summary**

This article from the May 1932 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine features a Q&A column titled "Crossroads" (交叉點), where readers pose questions to prominent intellectuals and receive public replies. Questions cover the activities and whereabouts of linguist Kim Tu-bong, the nature and study of Esperanto, the location of the Tonggwang magazine, recommended reading for youth, the magazine's readership in Manchuria, Chu Yo-han's reasons for exclusively using Hangul in his signature, and recommendations for Korean history books. The replies provide updates on figures like Kim Tu-bong (working on *Malmoi* and as a school principal in Shanghai), information on Esperanto study materials, details about Tonggwang-sa, and book recommendations like Ch'oe Nam-sŏn's *Chosŏn Yŏksa*.

From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as it showcases the role of magazines as interactive public forums during the Japanese colonial period, connecting readers (including those in Manchuria, like the one from Qiqihar) with leading Korean intellectuals. It reflects contemporary interests in Korean linguistics (Kim Tu-bong's work, Han'gŭl horizontal writing, *Malmoi*), the international language Esperanto (seen as easier than English), Korean history (interest in comprehensive texts like Ch'oe Nam-sŏn's), and the ongoing debate about national script, highlighted by Chu Yo-han's passionate advocacy for Hangul-only writing and the abolition of Hanja. The mention of specific individuals, publications, and locations provides valuable context for the intellectual and social landscape of 1930s Korea.

泰仁 뫼봅
