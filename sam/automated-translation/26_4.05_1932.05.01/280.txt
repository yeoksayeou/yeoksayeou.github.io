A Glimpse of Yi Su-hŭng's Face Seen for the Last Time Before Execution
						山外山人
Prologue

Having watched a Chaplin motion picture at the Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇場) and leaving around 10:30 PM, female reporter Ch'oe Ŭn-hŭi (崔恩喜) was casually passing by the front of the Chongno Police Station when police cars, sirens blaring, rushed out towards the Tongdaemun direction, startling even the people watching the night market (夜市) so much that their eyes widened.
The police officers in the cars were all armed with pistols.

A newspaper reporter's sixth sense struck Ch'oe Ŭn-hŭi's mind like lightning. Reporter Ch'oe immediately dashed off to the house of Min T'ae-wŏn (閔泰원), who was the managing editor at the time, and informed him that a major incident had just occurred.

Immediately, the vigorous activity of resourceful field reporters began, bright electric lights were turned on in room after room of the newspaper office's editorial department, the factory's rotary press had already started spinning, and about 10 minutes later, newspaper extras (號外), like long sheets of paper, were scattered like falling rain onto the streets of Seoul by delivery boys.

This extra was the prelude, reporting that Yi Su-hŭng (李壽興) had first appeared at the pawnshop in Suŭn-dong near Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮), pointed a gun at the owner, and demanded money and valuables. An iron-wall-like emergency cordon (非常線) was stretched out across the entire city by armed policemen. The number of policemen mobilized that night was 1,200!

Whether he turned into wind and slipped through the alleys or turned into a cloud and rose to the sky, the culprit's identity vanished, piercing through such an extraordinary security net. As the anxiety of the 300,000 citizens and the impatience of the police intensified, three days later, a second extra circulated.

It reported that he had shot and seriously wounded two police officers standing guard at the police substation inside Tongsomun Gate and vanished again. The general public felt a chill down their spine at his elusive appearances and disappearances.

Time flowed like water (如流), and his whereabouts remained unknown again. Then one day, a third incident occurred: an attack on the Oriental Development Company (東拓) branch office and the police substation (駐在所) in Yongin, Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道 龍仁), resulting in serious injuries. It goes without saying that the police of the entire province surrounded the mountain where the criminal was said to have fled and began a search.

After countless hardships, this audacious criminal was caught, revealing himself to be Yi Su-hŭng, who had entered from Manchuria (滿洲) carrying some kind of mission. I attended his final trial at the High Court as a reporter for the M Newspaper.

At that time, Yi Su-hŭng's face was gaunt from prison hardship, his upper lip had a dark stubble, and his hair was a bit long. Only his eyes shone brightly (炯炯), piercing through people.

Before the presiding judge, he admitted to all the facts of his crimes without the slightest concealment. And when the judge dismissed the appeal and sentenced him to death according to the first trial's verdict, he neither cried nor laughed. He was calm (平然).

A few days later, I heard the following story from the mouth of Prosecutor B, who had suddenly made a trip to the prison and returned.

When they were about to carry out the execution and asked the prisoner if he had any last words, he shook his head side to side (설네설네) and said he had nothing to say.<32>

**Summary**

This article recounts the sensational case of Yi Su-hŭng, a man who carried out a series of armed robberies and attacks against colonial authorities in Seoul and Kyŏnggi Province in 1932. It details the initial crime, the massive police mobilization, Yi's elusive escapes, subsequent attacks on a colonial enterprise (Oriental Development Company) and police substations, his eventual capture, trial, and calm demeanor facing the death sentence. The author, a reporter at the time, provides a first-hand perspective on the media frenzy (newspaper extras) and Yi's stoic attitude during his final trial and before execution. For historians, the article offers insight into public reaction to anti-colonial resistance acts (even if framed purely as crime here), the scale and nature of the Japanese colonial police response, the role of the media in disseminating news during crises, and the symbolic significance of targets like the Oriental Development Company. Yi Su-hŭng's portrayal as a determined, almost stoic figure facing death, despite the brief description, hints at the underlying political motivations often obscured in contemporary reports framing such acts solely as criminal.

死刑前 마즈막 본 얼골 李壽興의 一瞥
						山外山人
푸로- 로크

朝鮮劇場에 찻푸링 활동사진을 보고 밤 10시 반이나 하여 파하여 도라나오든 여기자 崔恩喜씨는 무심코 종로경찰서 압흐로 지나올 때에 夜市보든 사람들도 모다 놀나서 눈을 뚱그릴 만치 경찰서 압헤는 경적을 울니고 동대문 방면으로 질주하는 경찰 자동차가 분주히 출동하엿다.
자동차에 탄 경관들은 모다 삐스톨을 찻섯다.
신문기자의 육감은 崔恩喜씨의 머리를 번개가치 따리고 지나갓다. 최기자는 그길로 곳 줄다름질을 하여 당시 편집국장으로 잇는 閔泰원씨 집으로 달려가서
지금 중대사변이 돌발한 것을 알렷다.
즉시 민완의 외근기자의 대활동이 개시되고 신문사 편집실에는 밝은 전등불이 방방에 켜지고 그리고 벌서 공장의 윤전기는 돌기 시작하고 그러고 그로부터 약 10분 후에는 배달부의 손으로 장지가튼 호외가 비빨치듯 서울의 거리거리에 뿌리어 지엇다.
이 호외가 李壽興이 처음 창덕궁 압 수은동의 전당포에 나타나 주인에게 총을 노코 금품을 강청한 서막이엇다. 全市에는 철옹성가튼 비상선이 무장경관들 손으로 벌니어젓다. 當夜 동원한 경관 수천 2백 명!
바람으로 화하여 골목으로 빠젓는지 구름으로 화하여 하늘로 올넛는지 그러케 비범한 경계망을 뚤고 범인의 정체는 사라지고 마러서 30만 시민의 불안과 경관의 초조가 자심하여가는 그 뒤 사흘되는 날.
동소문 안 경찰관 파출소에서 망을 보는 경관 두 명을 삐스톨로 사격하여 중상을 입히고 또 종적을 감추어 버렷다는 제2 호외가 돌 때에 일반 世人은 그 신출귀몰에 가슴을 서늘히 하엿다.
세월이 如流하여 다시 종적이 묘연하다가 하로는 京畿道 龍仁의 東拓출장소와 주재소를 습격하여 중상자를 내인 사건이 또다시 제3차로 돌발하엿다. 全道의 경관이 범인이 도주하더라는 산을 포위하고 수색을 개시한 것은 물론이다.
이 대담한 범인은 그 뒤 천행만고 끗헤 잡고 보니 滿洲로부터 무슨 사명을 띄고 드러온 李壽興이다. 그의 고등법원의 최후의 공판을 余는 M신문기자로써 보앗다.
그 때의 李壽興은 얼골이 옥고로 여위엇고 웃수염이 꺼뭇꺼뭇하게 나섯고 머리도 좀 길엇다. 다만 눈빗만 炯炯하여 사람을 쏘앗다.
재판장 압헤서 그는 조곰도 숨김업시 범행사실을 일일히 시인하엿다. 그리고 재판장이 상고를 기각하고 1심 판결대로 사형을 선고할 때에도 그는 울지도 웃지도 안엇다. 平然하엿섯다.
몃칠 뒤 형무소로 돌연히 출장하엿다가 도라온 B검사의 입으로부터 나는 이런 이야기를 들엇다.
사형을 집행하러고 하야 죄수에게 무슨 할 말이 업느냐 하니까 그는 고개를 설네설네 흔들며 아모 할 말이 업다고 하엿다.<32>
