Magazine Title: 三千리 제10권 제8호
Publication Date: 1938년 08월 01일
Article Title: 文士가 말하는 名 映畵 (Famous Films Discussed by Literati)
Author: Survey Responses
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Famous Films Discussed by Literati
1. The most outstanding work and famous actor in Korean cinema.
2. The best work and famous actor in Western cinema.
3. A work by our literati that one would like to see made into a film.

**Yi Hyosŏk**

1. It seems "Nagŭne" (The Wanderer) has been most talked about recently, but there are many points in the reality depicted in the work with which I cannot sympathize. The actor Mun Yebong seems to possess good qualities, and her acting appears likely to improve further in the future, so I have expectations. There are numerous Western masterpieces, too many to list individually, but perhaps I'll mention "Anna Karenina" and Garbo's masterful performance.
2. On the day the Korean film business becomes more corporatized, there will surely be many works by currently active writers worth adapting into films. Of course, first, one must have the eye to discover those good works.

**Chiyong**

1. I have only heard that the Korean film "Yŏro" (The Journey) is the best, and I only know that Mun Yebong has a good reputation.
2. "Ppieenaŭi Hwanghon" (Twilight in Vienna? Likely *Mayerling*) and the man who played the doctor there; the French film "Ch'unhŭi" (Camille) and the actress who played Camille, "Yvonne!" something [likely Yvonne Printemps]; "Yakch'o Iyagi" (Little Women) and Katharine Hepburn who starred in it — generally, those people were good.
3. Pyŏkch'o's Im Kkŏkchŏngjŏn (The Tale of Im Kkŏkchŏng).

**Paek Ch'ŏl**

I will omit discussion of Korean films. I recall seeing recent works like "Omongnyŏ," "Sunjŏng Haehyŏp" (Pure Love Strait), and "Nagŭne," but none left an impression in the sense of being a masterpiece; rather, saying so might be unpleasant for conscientious directors and actors. In other words, there seems to be a great distance between the aforementioned works and masterpieces.<244>
Regarding Western films, there are many works that left a relatively deep impression. Listing them as they come to mind: "Yŏindo" (Women's City? Likely *La Kermesse Héroïque*), "Kŭmnamŭi Ka" (House of Forbidden Men? Likely *Mädchen in Uniform*), "Kahyangbok" (False Happiness? Likely *La Belle Équipe*), "Uridŭrŭi Tongnyo" (Our Comrades? Likely also *La Belle Équipe*), "Mokkyŏkcha" (The Witness? Likely *The Informer*), etc... Besides these, slightly different in nature, are "Aksŏng Pet'oben" (Beethoven the Great Composer? Likely *Un grand amour de Beethoven*), "Rembrandt," etc. However, here I will single out only "Kahyangbok" to offer some brief thoughts. This is not because "Kahyangbok" is the most outstanding among the works listed above, nor does it mean it's a great masterpiece, but rather because, like recent French cinema which tends towards sincerity regarding the conscience of intellectuals suffering in the modern era, this work is certainly one that represents that trend, and it is a work that could resonate deeply within our hearts for that reason.

One artistic height that recent films seem keen to pursue is the effort to rescue oneself from sentimental emotion. For example, scenes where, unlike in past films, a kiss would conventionally occur, but the audience's expectation for that scene is betrayed, going right up to the critical moment and ending without a kiss; or scenes where tears would conventionally be shed, but those tears are disregarded – such instances are representative impressions from watching recent Western films, and I received such impressions frequently in "Kahyangbok." The male protagonist, released from prison, is moved by the handkerchief the woman left in his hand and intends to kiss it, but then stops. The later scene where he tries to meet the woman's lips was unexpectedly the catastrophe just before their eternal separation. Scenes like this, tending to liquidate sentimentalism, were phenomena expressed not only in "Kahyangbok" but also in "Mokkyŏkcha," "Uridŭrŭi Tongnyo," etc.

Next, regarding actors, I also choose "Charles" [likely Charles Boyer], the male lead of "Kahyangbok," [Translator's Note: The original lists "샤루", "루보와 이웨 - ". "샤루" likely refers to Charles Boyer who starred in *La Belle Équipe*. The latter part "루보와 이웨 -" is unclear, possibly referring to other actors or a misinterpretation/misremembering]. He always adopts an expression of unspoken indifference, scorning the world. It's true that because he forcibly tries to make the expression of despising and cynically mocking today's reality seem like his actual personality, his expression has the flaw of being "affected" (氣取る) and exaggerated, but I believe that much exaggeration should be permitted for an artist. And above all, his ambiguous<245> expression, vague, hazy, seemingly unable to distinguish black from white, like that of an idiot, is good. For a young man who has lost purpose in reality and fallen into the melancholy of the age, an intellectual who doesn't know the world's happiness and pleasure, what else could characterize his expression but such a foolish one? His affectation (氣取니) will likely become calmer and more natural as he ages.

Postscript: Regarding survey question 3, I cannot think of anything suitable, so I will omit it.

**Yi Sŏnhŭi**

1. Among Korean works, Arirang and Sin Ilsŏn from that time. Among Western works, "Kog'yŏtan" (Variety Troupe? Likely *Variety*) and Emil Jannings.
2. Yi Kwangsu's "Mauidaeja" (Crown Prince Maŭi).

**An Hoenam**

1. Among Korean films, I enjoyed watching "Nagŭne." As for actors, also Mun Yebong who appeared in the same film. Among Western works, "Morok'k'o" (Morocco) is a masterpiece; I dislike seeing all the male actors, but conversely, all the female actresses are good.
2. Among Korean literary works, I haven't yet noticed any that would be good to adapt into a film.

**Chŏng Naedong**

1. I haven't seen many Korean films; among recent ones, I only recall "Nagŭne," and the actors are those who appear in the stills I see every day. For Western films, it's "Rŏbu P'aradŭi" (Love Parade) and its star Jeanette MacDonald.
2. There are two types of Korean literary works for film adaptation: historical ones and contemporary works. For historical material, Tanjong Aesa (The Tragic History of King Tanjong); among contemporary writers, the works of Mr. Yi Muyŏng and Mr. Yi T'aejun seem likely to succeed if made into films.

**Pak T'aewŏn**

1. I haven't seen many Korean films, so I don't know. The same applies to actors.
2. Among foreign films, Chaplin's works, René Clair's works, Duvivier's "Hongbal" (Red Hair? Likely *Poil de Carotte*), Willy Forst's "Morinakaŭi Silchong" (Morinaga's Disappearance? Likely *Maskerade*), several works by Lubitsch, King Vidor's "Maekch'u" (Barley Harvest? Likely *Our Daily Bread*), Feyder's "Mimoja Kwan" (Pension Mimosas) and "Yŏindo," several works by Sternberg, Capra's "Ŏnŭ Pam e Irŏnan Il" (It Happened One Night)... Not all are first-rate works, but I've listed those that remain in my memory.
3. I haven't paid particular attention. <246> Hold on. I'll write a good scenario myself now.

**Yi Kiyŏng**

1. Among Korean films, the ones that left an impression are "Nagŭne" and Mun Yebong. Among Western works, "Miwansŏng Kyohyanggok" (Unfinished Symphony) and the actors who appeared in it.
2. Regarding Korean literary works, there are quite a few feature-length works I haven't read, so it's difficult to point out which one is good; nothing comes readily to mind.

**Kim Ansŏ**

1. While dithering about films, I've fallen behind the times, so what I've seen is, by necessity, recent. As someone who hasn't watched many films, it would be ridiculous for me to comment this way or that about stars of bygone days, so I'll refrain from stale talk. This is better than pretending to know what I don't.
2. If there are Korean works to be filmed, although the work itself and the film are separate things, wouldn't there be something among the recent works of Mr. Tongin [Kim Tongin]? If it had been completed, something like T'aep'yŏnghaeng would be very good. And "Chŏlmŭn Kŭdŭl" (Young Ones) wouldn't be bad either. I also hold the humble opinion that Ch'unwŏn's [Yi Kwangsu's] "Hyŏngmyŏnggaŭi Anhae" (Wife of a Revolutionary) might not be bad.

**Kim Kwangsŏp**

1. I've seen several Korean films, but there aren't many that remain unforgettable; Na Un'gyu's "Arirang," whether good or bad, is memorable. Among Western films, "Ch'oe wa Pŏl" (Crime and Punishment) was good, "Aksŏng Pet'oben" was alright; among recent ones, Greta Garbo's performance in "Ch'unhŭi" (Camille) was good, and Popesco's [Elvira Popescu?] clear and pretty voice as the famous actor [Maurice Chevalier] in "Syubarie Hyuhaenga" (Chevalier the Playboy? Likely *Folies Bergère de Paris*) is fresh in my memory daily. The final scene of "Naeil Puri" (Tomorrow Never Comes? Likely *Make Way for Tomorrow*) was also good in its reflection on the meaning of life.
2. Anyone could probably make them [Korean literary works into films].

**Yu Chino**

1. There are many works with established reputations as "masterpieces" that I haven't seen, so it's difficult to say, but among those I have seen, "Sangson Renasiti" (Merchant Ship Renacity? Likely *Renegades*) was extremely good. As for actors, Greta Garbo - of course.
2. I don't know. This is regarding acting [likely referring to Korean actors/actresses].<247>

**Summary**

This 1938 survey captures the opinions of prominent Korean writers (Yi Hyosŏk, Paek Ch'ŏl, Pak T'aewŏn, Yi Kwangsu via others, etc.) on Korean and Western cinema. While acknowledging the nascent state of Korean film, with *Nagŭne* and actress Mun Yebong frequently mentioned but critically assessed, the writers show considerable awareness of international cinema, particularly French (*La Belle Équipe*, *Mayerling*, *Camille*) and American films (*Anna Karenina*, *Little Women*, *Morocco*, *Love Parade*, *It Happened One Night*), alongside admiration for stars like Greta Garbo, Katharine Hepburn, Charles Boyer, and Emil Jannings. Suggestions for film adaptations primarily focus on historical narratives (*Im Kkŏkchŏngjŏn*, *Tanjong Aesa*) and works by established authors like Yi Kwangsu and Kim Tongin. For historians, the article reveals the cultural consumption patterns of Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule, their critical engagement with both domestic and foreign films, the perceived gap between Korean and Western cinematic achievements, and specific aesthetic preferences, such as Paek Ch'ŏl's noted appreciation for the anti-sentimental tendencies in contemporary French cinema.

기사형태	설문

文士가 말하는 名 映畵
1. 조선 영화 속에 가장 우수한 작품과 名優.
2. 서양 영화 속에 가장 나흔 작품과 名優.
3. 우리 文人 작품으로 영화화하고 십흔 것.

李孝石

1. 「나그네」가 아마도 昨今의 화제에 가장 만히 오른 듯 하나 작중 현실에는 동감 못되는 점이 만슴니다. 배우 文藝峯은 좋은 소질을 가진 듯이 보이며 연기도 앞으로 더 올늘 것 가터서 기대됨니다. 서양 명화도 허다하니. 일일히 들기도 어려우나 한 편 「안나 카레니나」와 갈보의 名技 쯤을 들어 둘까요.
1. 조선 영화 사업이 더욱 기업화되는 날에는 현역 작가의 것으로만 하드라도 영화화 시킬 만한 작품이야 만치오. 물론, 그 좋은 것을 발견할만한 눈이 첬재 잇서야 겟지요.

지용

1. 조선 영화는 「旅路」가 제일 좋다는 말슴을 들었을 뿐이요, 文藝峯이가 평판이 좋은 줄 알 뿐이외다.
1. 「삐에나의 黃昏」과 거기 의사로 나왔던 사람, 불란서 영화 「椿姬」와 거기 椿姬로 나왔던 「이보!」무엇이라 하는 女優, 「若草物語」와 거기 주연으로 나왔던 캐떠린, 헤편 - , 대개 그러한 사람들이 좋읍데다.
1. 碧初의 林巨正傳.

白鐵

조선 영화에 대해서는 略합니다. 최근 작품으로는 「五夢女」 「純情 海峽」 「나그네」 등을 본 기억은 있읍니다만은 어느 것이나 명화라는 의미로서 인상을 남는 것은 도리혀 양심 있는 감독과 배우에게 불쾌한 일일 것 갓습니다. 말하면 右揭의 작품들과 명화 사이에는 큰 거리가 있어 보인다는＜244＞ 말입니다.
서양화에 대해서는 비교적 깊은 인상을 받은 작품이 많읍니다. 기억나는 대로 적어보면 「女人都」 「禁男의 家」 「假幸福」 「우리들의 同僚」, 「目擊者」 등... 그밖에 上揭 작품과는 성질이 약간 달너집니다만은 「樂聖 베토벤」 「렘브랜트」... 등입니다. 그러나 여기서 나는 오직 「假幸福」하나만을 빼서 약간의 감상을 말할까 합니다. 그것은 무슨 「假幸福」이 上揭 작품 중 가장 우수한 것이라는 때문도 아니오, 또한 큰 명작이라는 의미도 아닙니다만 근래의 불란서 영화와 같이 현대에 고민하는 지식인의 양심에 성실성을 가지려는 경향이 주라고 하면 이 작품은 확실히 그 경향을 대표한 작품의 하나이요 또 그만치 우리들의 가슴에 공명을 기대해도 좋은 작품이엇기 때문입니다.
근년의 영화가 즐겨서 가지려는 예술적 경지의 하나는 감상적인 감격에서 자기를 구해내는 노력인 것 갓습니다. 예를 들면 과거의 영화 같으면 의례히 接吻을 해야 할 장면인데 그 장면에 대한 관중의 豫期를 배반하고, きりきりの 순간까지 가서 接吻을 하지 않고 마는 장면 등과, 또는 의레히 눈물을 흘녀야 할 장면인데, 그 눈물을 묵살하고 마는 장면 같은 것 이런 것은 근년 洋畵를 보는 인상 중 대표적인 것인데 이 「假幸福」에는 그런 인상을 받은 곳이 많엇습니다. 감옥에서 나온 남주인공이 자기 손에 남기고 간 여인의 손수건에 감격하야 接吻를 하려다가 그대로 그만 두어버립니다. 나종 장면에서 그 여인의 입술과 합하려든 장면은 의외로 영원히 헤여지는 장면의 직전의 캐타스트로히었읍니다. 이와 같이 감상주의를 청산하려는 경향의 장면은 이 「假幸福」만이 아니라 「目擊者」 「우리들의 同僚」 등에도 표현되여 있는 현상이었읍니다.
다음 배우에 대해서는 역시 「假幸福」의 남주인공인 「샤루」, 「루보와 이웨 - 」를 擇합니다. 나는 그가 언제나 세상을 冷蔑하고 있는 無言 不笑의 표정을 취합니다. 금일의 현실을 侮蔑하고 冷嘲하는 표정을 무리로 성격 그것과 같이 보이고저 하는 때문에 그 표정에는 「氣取る」한 흠이 있고 과장된 점이 있는 것은 사실이나, 본래에 있어 예술가에게는 그만한 과장은 許與해야 할 것인 줄 압니다. 그리고 무엇보다도 그의 명확치＜245＞ 못한 표정, 막연하고, 몽롱하고, 흑백을 가릴 수 없이 보이는 백치와 같은 표정이 좋읍니다. 현실에 意를 잃고 세기의 우수에 빠진 청년, 세상의 행복과 즐거음을 모르는 인테리의 표정을 나타내려는 특색은 그런 바보와 칼은 표정일 수밖에 없지 않겟슴니까. 그의 氣取니도 연령이 차감을 따라서 침착해지고 자연스럽어질 것입니다.
