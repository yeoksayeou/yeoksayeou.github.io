**Wartime Speeches**
The Emergency Situation and Household Economy
Hwang Sin-dŏk

1. Re-recognizing the Emergency Situation
Looking back, a year has already passed. At the time the North China Incident broke out last July, the atmosphere of national general mobilization surged throughout the capital and countryside, with people saying, "Ah, it's war! Let's make national defense donations. Let's go see off the departing soldiers. Let's pull out our hairpins. Let's take off our rings." However, in the war with China, with the peerless loyalty and courage of our Imperial Army, achieving victory in every battle—one victory, then two victories, a hundred victories in a hundred battles—Beijing, Nanjing, and finally Xuzhou were splendidly captured.<189> Because we have experienced several instances of long lines of students running joyfully, raising celebratory national flags high and singing victory songs, hasn't the tension of the emergency situation somewhat eased, replaced by the joy of the victor and the feeling of "なんだ相手はなんでも, ないぢやないか!" (What, the opponent is nothing, aren't they!)? This is indeed a matter of great concern.
Unless we are great politicians or prophets, we do not know how long this war will last or how it will conclude. However, as citizens on the home front, we must prepare for a long-term war and act accordingly. Even if the war were to end tomorrow, our resolve and preparations must be such that we need not worry even if it drags on for five or ten years. The entire nation must mobilize in advance to overcome this emergency situation. Isn't this time truly an emergency within an emergency?

2. The Duties of Women on the Home Front
In the past, war was thought to be something only soldiers who went directly to the battlefield with guns or swords did. And indeed, in those times when weapons were undeveloped, it would not be an exaggeration to say that war was solely the affair of the soldiers who went off to fight. However, today's war is not just a war of the soldiers fighting on the front lines; it is a war of the entire nation. Some may ask, "What does that mean?" Everyone! Do you know the reason why China, despite having countless troops and the handicap of fighting on its own territory, suffers crushing defeats against the small Imperial Army unfamiliar with the foreign terrain? It is! It is because there is a difference in two great powers. One is the difference in the spirit of public service (奉公), dedicating oneself to His Majesty the Emperor and the nation. The other is the fact that their developed weapons technology, spanning the army, navy, and air force, lags behind ours. Where did the sacrificial spirit of the Imperial Army arise from? And were the proud armaments of the army and navy achieved overnight? The women on the home front! They are precisely the mothers and wives of soldiers.
Without the solemn maternal lesson of "Do not return alive" and the strength of wise wives who ensure not a single worry about looking back, would the glorious present of the Imperial Army have been possible? And wouldn't it be doubtful whether such weapons could have been prepared without the crystallization of the efforts of countless citizens in ordinary times? In other words, our mindset and material resources in dealing with the emergency situation directly impact the war; conserving or not conserving even a single sheet of paper or a single nail<190> is precisely war. Everyone sees magnificent airplanes flying in the sky. We cannot know what kind of malfunction might occur because one nail is missing in some part of that precise machinery. Perhaps today, because one rice ball was lacking, a crucial soldier might be lacking energy. In this sense, today's war is a war of national general mobilization, and accordingly, I believe the duty of us women on the home front is crucial and great (重且大).

3. Household Economy for National Service (報國)
As stated above, if we consider every single action of the citizens on the home front as indirect warfare, we can readily understand where the path of national service (報國) lies for us women in this emergency situation.
I would like to add a few words about the Savings for National Service Emphasis Week (貯蓄報國强調週間). We women, while being good mothers and wise wives at home, should serve the nation through the household economy that is managed by our hands. This is our duty as subjects (臣民) of the Empire, and also the path of national service where we women can demonstrate the greatest efficiency. Usually, when discussing household economy, it means diligence and thrift, but today's household economy requires somewhat different study and attitude than usual, especially because the war situation is long-term, and if our lives are not adjusted accordingly, we don't know what kind of difficulties we might fall into at any time; indeed, this is an extraordinary emergency situation.
First, there is something we must know: knowledge about the wartime economy. That is, why do prices rise during wartime? Everyone has experienced since the Japan-China Incident that the price of beef has risen, and coal, firewood, rubber shoes—everything—has become terribly expensive, to the point where ordinary people can hardly keep their senses. Furthermore, purely gold hairpins and rings are prohibited, and even for fabrics, purely cotton ones are not woven; instead, staple fiber is mixed in. While some may just worry, wondering what on earth is happening, this is not due to any other reason than that among our daily necessities, there are goods needed for the war. That is, starting with iron, fuel, paper, hides, etc., there is nothing unrelated, directly or indirectly, to the war. Since the raw materials or products for these munitions are not infinite, and the war's end cannot be determined, even if goods haven't run out immediately, if they were to disappear, it would bring ruin to the war effort and national <191> life. Therefore, as a warning to the general public to conserve and use resources sparingly in advance, the government may raise prices as a policy measure. There might also be disturbances caused by unscrupulous merchants (奸商輩) exploiting such times, or perhaps, in reality, some items have indeed become scarce.
Therefore, those of us who are housewives must take a step further from the vague notion of simply having to be diligent and thrifty in normal times. We must study the economic situation under the wartime system more concretely. 1. What raw materials or products can be produced in our country? 2. What are the most important essential items for the war? What munitions are imported from foreign countries? Can they be supplied through our efforts? We must learn at least the common sense aspects of these questions and manage our household economy accordingly.
Even if we make a fuss, learning blackout procedures (消燈法) and stopping the noon gun (午砲), calling it a war and an emergency, as long as we haven't seen bleeding soldiers before our eyes or experienced bombs falling from the sky, our general populace can still be said to be living a peaceful life compared to the time of the European War. During the Great War in Europe, it is said that the most taxing task for German women was obtaining daily food for their families and evenly distributing the single piece of bread they acquired. Even that period seemed happy later on, as eventually, not even a crumb of dark bread came their way, and they supposedly boiled seaweed sprinkled with salt to stave off temporary hunger. It is said that the government regulated the people's lives so meticulously that a decree was issued imposing fines on those who peeled potato skins.
Although we are fortunate enough not to have experienced such bitterness yet, we must absolutely not be complacent here. We must anticipate the worst-case scenario and make material preparations sufficient to endure even if this war lasts five or ten years. If we manage our household economy with this recognition and resolve, not only individual households but also the national economy will be able to overcome the difficulties without faltering.

4. Concrete Methods for Household Economy
The question of how to wisely manage consumption with a fixed income to create surplus is undoubtedly a matter of daily concern for us women, but in reality, isn't our lifestyle still primitive <192> as it has always been?
First, looking just at the three necessities—food, clothing, and shelter—we have long felt the irrationality of our housing system. Isn't it our way of life to maintain a messy household with a water jar buried in an earthen floor kitchen, even though we know we could live quite comfortably without needing a maid if the main room (안방) and kitchen were directly connected, allowing the housewife to work directly wearing slippers? Don't we still adhere to the same methods passed down for thousands or hundreds of years regarding clothing and food, even though we know we could save time and resources considerably through more research and improvement? This is indeed evidence of falling behind in today's civilized world, and in this emergency situation, such contradictions represent an extreme phenomenon. Therefore, we believe that simplifying and improving our lifestyle is the first step in household economy. However, since this cannot be achieved overnight, it should always remain our homework in the back of our minds. The attitude we must adopt requires more urgency: conserving war supplies (軍需品). In other words, we must absolutely refrain from wastefully using or discarding things like fuel, cotton, iron, and paper. Furthermore, we should use our heads to research and utilize whatever items we have in the house as much as possible, conserving and cherishing them to the extent feasible.
We must realize that discarding even a piece of cotton or a single needle hidden away in some corner of the house is not the attitude of a woman facing these times. Next, we must absolutely refrain from using luxury items. Under this principle, the government is imposing taxes on gold hairpins and records, and prohibiting the import of foreign cosmetics. No matter how nice our clothes or abundant our food may be, when we think of our soldiers fighting fierce battles on the front lines, possessing overly fancy clothes or accessories is not, I believe, a national attitude. Therefore, our lives today should be frugal, buying and using only absolute necessities.
Finally, one method is saving. Who would starve and die today? But we must not forget that some emergency could arise even tonight. You experienced it not long ago, didn't you? An emergency alarm suddenly sounded in the middle of the night. Fortunately, nothing happened, but we need to be prepared with somewhat ample supplies of these four things—rice, wood, matches, and water—at all times, so that even if transportation is cut off due to some incident, <193> our young children will not go hungry. There might be cases where money cannot buy things, and we must anticipate prices soaring several times higher than today, saving both goods (現品) and money as much as possible. In other words, the points are: first, conserve war supplies; second, avoid luxurious living and live frugally; third, make material preparations so as not to panic even if faced with a worst-case scenario.
In short, let us thoroughly recognize today as an emergency within this emergency situation (非常時局). As women on the home front, let us strive not only with mental readiness but also on the material front, under the notion of "even one piece of paper for the nation!" to achieve tangible results for national service through household economy, which is most suited to our role.

The Situation and the Awakening of Women
Yu Kak-kyŏng, General Secretary, Chosŏn Women's Youth Association (朝鮮女子靑年會)

Current society holds much interest in women in general. The reason is that since ancient times, notions and critiques regarding women have been inconsistent. Women have constantly heard dishonorable phrases like being appendages of men or "Frailty, thy name is woman." However, on the other hand, there are also sayings like "The hand that rocks the cradle rules the world," and "The strength or weakness of a nation can be known by looking at its mothers." Various things like these are said. What I wish to say here is not to judge whether women are weak or strong, or whether they are appendages or not.
Rather, I wish to state that while men are individuals with personhood and women are also individuals with personhood, the duties (本分) of these two personalities are distinct.
It is in the difference of the duties of these two personalities that their noble aspects lie. Only when each individual fully upholds their duty can they help each other, and only when this help combines can the nation and society be maintained, forming the foundation for national development.
In the current emergency situation (非常時局), what should women do? This is a problem for us women to consider carefully. It is easy to think that rushing out to the battlefield (戰地) with a gun, keeping pace with the officers and soldiers (將兵), is what makes a splendid heroine. However, in my opinion, as mentioned above, women still have women's duties. <194>
Entrusting the task of shouldering guns and going to the battlefield to repel the enemy to men, allowing them to display utmost loyalty and courage (忠勇) with manly spirit (意氣), while women firmly protect the home front (銃後) from within so there are no worries about looking back—this, I believe, is the duty (本分) of women. Deeply realizing that this loyalty (忠義) towards the nation lies in striving in that direction is what I consider the woman's role.
Then, how to protect the home front becomes the next stage.
War is a temporary thing; it does not last forever. Regarding the current incident, I believe we will see a great victory for the Imperial Army (皇軍) sooner or later, thanks to the spirit (意氣) of the deployed officers and soldiers and their sophisticated weapons (武器). Therefore, let us entrust the fighting to the men. As for what women on the home front should do, firstly, the most important thing is to protect the home well, preserve family assets (家産), and raise the second-generation citizens (제2세 국민) to be possessors of noble spirit and healthy bodies. This, I know, is the most crucial task among those women must resolve to undertake. Furthermore, considering that today, with the implementation of the volunteer soldier system (志顧兵制度), the future rise or fall of the nation solely depends on the mental and physical health of the second-generation citizens, the mission is grave. Accordingly, as mentioned above, it is something to be proud of that the hand rocking the cradle holds the prerogative to sway the world.
Secondly, I wish to speak about the spirit of diligence and thrift (勤儉 節約). Thriftiness is necessary even in ordinary times, but especially during wartime, I know it requires even more attention. During the Great War in Europe, French women reportedly formed a "Short Skirt Society" (短袴會), shortening their skirt lengths to alleviate hardship. Now that we face an emergency, it feels like we finally understand the frugal spirit of those French women.
Shortening skirt lengths by 1 or 2 inches (寸), when compared to the immense military expenditures and sacrifices, is indeed like a single hair from nine oxen (九牛一毛).
I heard something like this: Regarding the bedding (이불/침구) used in Korean households, do you know why it has a neckband (동정), a collar (깃), and is made in blue and red? It is said that in the past, if a war broke out, soldiers would tear up those blankets to make clothes, and tie the neckband around their shoulders like a red sash before going out to fight. Also, concerning the brassware (鍮器) used in general households, it is said this was done so that during wartime, it could be used to make guns and other military supplies (군수품). If things were like this in the past, how much more should be the resolve of housewives today? As mentioned earlier,<195> I believe that the unique and meticulous spirit of women lies hidden in the French women's effort to shorten their skirts, wanting to contribute even a little to rescuing the nation from hardship. Though we may not know the extent of the impact that single inch of skirt length, born from the sincere hearts of women, had on France's economy during that extraordinary time, let us protect the home front, taking the sincerity (赤誠) of the members of this "Short Skirt Society" as the driving force that saved France from hardship.
Therefore, today, women on the Peninsula (半島 여성) on the home front should also change luxurious clothing to simple ones, promote plain-colored clothes (色衣 - *likely means plain/undyed, contextually*), save both money and time simultaneously, and act with the sincerity (赤誠) of leaving behind achievements like those French women. A Korean proverb (俗語) says, "When the house is poor, one thinks of a wise mother; when the nation is disordered, one thinks of a wise minister." This shows that, regardless of time period, overcoming difficulties requires general mobilization, or success will be elusive. Therefore, realizing that the responsibility of women on the home front facing an emergency like today is even greater, let us unite as one body (內鮮一體化) and faithfully implement national policies starting from the home. This is the duty of citizens on the home front, and consequently, we will be able to become wise mothers overcoming the crisis and benevolent mothers (慈母) to the officers and soldiers.

Women's National Service (報國) in the Emergency
Yi Suk-chong, Principal, Sŏngsin Women's School (誠信女子學校)

Today, various women's organizations have united to hold this Lecture Meeting on the Situation (時局講演會). From the standpoint of women who are subjects (臣民) of the Great Japanese Empire, the purpose tonight—to re-recognize this grave situation, understand what duties and awakening are required, and how we should respond—is, needless to say, imbued with a profound mission and duty. The war will likely drag on for a long time. Are we prepared as citizens to cope with this long-term war? This spirit must become the resolve of the general public regarding the current situation, and action must follow. Since ancient times, the duties of men have been clearly divided between internal and external affairs, while women have borne the fundamental responsibilities for humanity and the nation. The power to create humanity and the nation, the power to control happiness, has placed women in a position greater than men, and this position has become established as if by the most natural order. The power to nurture generals (將士) and loyal retainers (忠臣) lies here, and the responsibility for the bright governance of a family and a household is also a great power held by women. As fellow subjects of Japan,<196> if men have the duty to wear armor and helmets for the entire nation and go to the front lines to offer their lives, what national service must we women perform in their stead? That strong nations have strong soldiers, and strong armies have the virtue of wise mothers and wives, is a consistent fact taught by any era or history. Now, when the nation has commenced war, women must take charge of consolidating internal strength (內部 實力) on the home front (銃後), bearing the grave duty of complete internal defense so that the soldiers who have gone to the front lines have no lingering concerns (後顧). The conditions under which our Imperial Army (我 皇軍) is fighting bitterly in the remote foreign lands of North China, Central China, and South China, amid extreme cold and scorching heat, are something we, living safely on level ground, cannot possibly even imagine (想像). You are all well aware of this. Nearly a year has passed since this bitter struggle began. That our Imperial Army is achieving increasing superiority everywhere, astonishing the world, is primarily due to the loyalty, courage, and invincible martial arts (兵術) spirit (意氣) of our Imperial Army, confronting the enemy not with weapons but with spirit (靈魂), and it is undeniable that the complete reassurance provided by the united home front of the entire populace has played a significant role. Though it's a story of defeat, it is said that the French people, defeated in the Franco-Prussian War 10 (*actually ~70*) years ago, endured hardship and training (忍苦 鍛鍊), practiced extreme diligence and saving (勤儉 貯蓄), and soon paid off all reparations to Germany, with their remaining strength making them a leading nation today. And didn't the German people, who suffered a crushing defeat in the World War you all know, recover their former state by enduring hardship to the point of skimping on food and even saving on clothes? The situation has arisen from the incident between our Japan and China, causing Japan's national fortune (國運) to develop further, and Japan is now striving to firmly and resolutely fulfill its righteous mission. Jubilation and emotion, shaking heaven and earth with successive victories, have boiled over like water, but we citizens must realize that complacency and overconfidence from this are absolutely forbidden. The situation has entered a second stage of tension, and as the mission of our Japanese Empire grows larger, we must believe in tempering ourselves for greater duties through the strong resolve of the people, united as one in a general mobilization. As citizens, and particularly from the important position of women, we must further awaken to what Peninsula women (半島 女性) should do and aspire to. The teaching that "All Japanese citizens are soldiers" (日本國民皆兵) instructs both the soldiers going to the front and the citizens on the home front to realize their common duty. At the same time, from the standpoint of women who are Imperial subjects (皇國 臣民) on the Peninsula, what further awakening and action must there be? Everyone,<197> in awakening to and acting upon anything, there must be righteous courage and strength, self-confidence, and perseverance from beginning to end. With righteous courage, we awaken, and from this, we gain the strength to act. Therefore, we must awaken with great courage, recognizing our grave position as Imperial subjects and this situation, and proceed with a firm sense of duty based on a sincere attitude towards the situation. Sincerity (至誠) in unity and solidarity, a steadfast spirit of national service (報國) on the home front—this, I believe, should be the mission of Peninsula women facing this situation. Even though we are said to share ancestral roots (同祖 同根) from ancient times, having lived apart in different places for a long time, differences in language, customs, and practices led to somewhat different ways of thinking and feeling, causing difficulties at times. Just as siblings living separately in the same house can become distant in some ways, leading to mutual misunderstandings, it is true that in a similar relationship, there were periods where, with insufficient understanding and narrow consciousness, the national duty was neglected in some quarters. However, since the Manchurian Incident, and especially seizing the opportunity of this current incident, various organizations and religious groups have taken the lead in the Imperial Way movement (皇國運動), uniting as one to achieve a complete recognition as Imperial subjects and awaken to their national duty. With this confident self-awareness stemming from complete understanding replacing insufficient comprehension, I firmly believe there will be steadfast progress in realizing our duties as Japanese citizens. I have spoken about the spirit of national service (報國) appropriate for women of the Japanese Empire facing the situation. Furthermore, the fundamental leadership for the three great spirits of the recent educational ordinance revision (敎令育改定)—Clarification of the National Polity (國體明徵), Unity of Japan and Korea (內鮮一體), and Perseverance and Training (忍苦鍛鍊)—lies specifically with women. It resides in the education of children, the improvement of education, the home, guidance in reverence for the gods and ancestors (敬神崇祖) essential to the national polity, and the fundamental duty of service through consumption reduction and perseverance and training. Realizing the crucial position of women in these areas, we must also feel the responsibility that the home must first fulfill, especially considering the immense Imperial grace (聖慮) shown in establishing the volunteer soldier system, granting the military service obligation (兵役義務) to Peninsula people (半島人) for the first time, and revising the education system. From now on, as mothers and wives of soldiers, we have gained a share in the duty that "all citizens are soldiers" (國民皆兵). As you well know, the entrance ceremony for the 200 initially selected volunteers out of 3,000 applicants in the recent selection was held on the 15th of last month. While renewing our resolve as Imperial subjects, we must strive to fully repay our duties.
Finally, among the important things we must realize is consumption reduction. While this is crucial for every household even in peacetime,<198> with the military expenditures due to the current China Incident reaching 7.38 billion yen, and future trends unpredictable, if it drags on longer, further increases in military spending will be unavoidable. When such vast sums of money and materials are consumed due to war, it goes without saying that the entire nation must mobilize and unite with enduring perseverance to conserve materials. However, we women, while not having the opportunity for direct combat, must feel the even greater importance of our duty: ensuring that small contributions through consumption reduction and waste utilization (廢物利用) accumulate to become airplanes, cannons, and warships, thereby providing a sufficient supply of materials without hesitation, no matter how long the situation lasts.
Under the awakening to these three points I have mentioned, if the unity of women on the home front is complete, no matter how long this situation drags on, the resolute mission of our Japanese Empire will shine brighter, and all this strength, I know, lies in the awakening and action of women.
Now, I will conclude, emphasizing the spirit of women's national service, and especially urging Peninsula women to awaken to their grave position and responsibility. - (End) - Showa 13 (1938), June 24th.

National Service (報國) and Moderation (節制)
Chang Chŏng-sim, Women's Theological Seminary (女子神學校)

In this time when the situation is persistently marked by incidents, I believe the authority of the Empire has been further proclaimed to the world since the North China Incident. This is because every subject of the Empire is mobilizing entirely, dedicating their full strength to national service (報國).
Our Imperial soldiers (皇國 將士) who went to the front lines fought bravely, offering their lives. Therefore, the fact that various regions of North China have been occupied today, less than a year since last July, cannot but be considered the result of the soldiers' countless hardships and bloody battles (血戰). Indeed, their labor (苦勞) is known to be like the pains of childbirth (産母の辛苦) rather than just the role of a midwife (産婆). The brave soldiers who left their wives, children, and homes went to the battlefield (戰地) and devoted themselves wholeheartedly to loyalty (忠義). Besides this, it is said they share joys and sorrows, taking good care of the wounded.
All citizens on the home front (銃後), under the principle of Clarification of the National Polity (國體明徵), with nationwide unity (擧國一致) and cooperation, and a spirit of perseverance and endurance (堅忍持久), men and women, old and young, are serving the nation. Especially housewives, dedicating their spirit, materials, and time for their husbands and children dispatched to the front,<199> have achieved such great results today. However, as time drags on, there is concern that the national spirit might somewhat relax, and anxieties about the upcoming harsh heat are not absent. At such a time, for us general housewives possessing the spirit of national service, recognizing this moment well is a duty we must fulfill. Thus, to re-emphasize that spirit, government and people have united to act. This current activity should not merely end as an enlightenment and propaganda campaign but should further emphasize the spirit of general mobilization to push forward to the very end. In other words, it is about steadily proceeding with daily life to achieve the final victory (勝捷).
Until this incident ends, I know it is the fundamental duty (本分) of us women of the Imperial nation (皇國) to do our utmost for national service.
The issue of women on the home front will likely become unexpectedly intense. Mothers of the newly advancing brave soldiers will increasingly ensure that their beloved sons going forth for national service do so joyfully. The one who should bless the path taken for the sake of the Imperial nation is, more than anyone, his benevolent mother (慈母).
I read something like this in a children's picture book (兒童畵集).
A man named Takemori (竹森), a Superior Private (上等兵), had previously been stationed in Manchuria and was dispatched to the North China Incident this time. When he left, he certainly did not expect to return alive. Some time after his departure, news came home that he had died in battle (戰死). So, his mother and father opened his desk drawer and found a document. They asked a familiar Army Minister (陸軍大臣) to read it together. The letter said:
"As a soldier, being able to fulfill my loyalty (忠義) to His Majesty (陛下) is truly a happy thing for me. This is also filial piety (孝誠) towards my parents. I do not fear death and intend to give my life. Even if I die in battle, please never grieve or be disheartened." The Army Minister who read this and the parents who heard it, saying it was only fitting for a soldier, shed tears and were deeply moved. There is a story I heard before about Spartan mothers. Spartan mothers would give their beloved sons being sent to battle a large plank (널쪼각), blessing them by saying, "Either fight to the death for Sparta and come back lying on this plank, or achieve victory, sound the victory drums, and return riding a horse."
Facing this emergency situation, one cannot help but think of these two types of mothers regarding the soldiers sent to the front. If we are all to become citizens devoted to national service (報國의 신민), we cannot avoid moderation (節制).
With the implementation of the volunteer soldier system (支援兵 制度), enabling our households too to send Imperial soldiers (皇軍 兵士), we keenly feel the necessity of that spirit even more today.<200>
It is proper for each household to conserve what should be conserved, utilize what should be utilized, and cherish what should be cherished. Following the general trend of the times, especially by conserving items necessary for military use (軍用品) starting from each household and organization, everyone, high and low, should unite in general mobilization so that military supplies do not become scarce (乏絶).
These include fuel (연료) like petroleum (석유) and coal (석탄), leather (革), cotton (木綿), iron (鐵), paper (紙), gasoline (キハツユ - *likely 揮発油 kihatsuyu*), heavy oil (重油), etc. (*Note: キハツユ/揮発油 and ガソリン/가쏘린 both refer to gasoline.*)
Starting from the home, we must first comply with national policy (國策) and cooperate with financial and economic matters. In this emergency situation, housewives should not strive to buy clothing fabrics or recreational items with money they don't have; amidst high prices, this is inappropriate. Moreover, if clothes are made, they only get eaten by moths (좀); this money should be saved to supplement financial resources (財源). Household economy is exactly the same as government economy. Therefore, let us cooperate under at least three conditions:
1. Utilizing Waste Materials (弊物 利用)
Let's not waste even a drop of oil or a handspan of thread. By utilizing these well, large things can be made, and crucial needs can be supplied. Use staple fiber (ステブベバ - *likely ステープルファイバー, staple fiber*). Do not burn or discard paper, nails, or wood scraps. If you think about utilization before discarding, there is nothing that cannot be utilized.
2. Savings (貯蓄) and Deposits (貯金)
Even tens of thousands of gold (*currency unit*) start with 1 *chŏn* (錢).
One who does not save even 1 *chŏn* cannot accumulate 1 *wŏn* (圓). It is said, "Save when young, use when old." For us Imperial subjects facing such an emergency, saving is incredibly necessary. That's why there is this maxim:
"The best friend of the poor is savings."
1. Earn well
2. Save well
3. Spend well
This is the secret regarding money.
3. Conserving Time (時間을 節制)
Time is said to be more precious than money. Wasting time is said to be the greatest loss among all wastes. Whether for household chores or victory in the current situation, victory lies in the final <201> five minutes; let us realize how precious five minutes of time are. "If you lose an hour in the morning, you will spend the whole day busy trying to catch up," it is said. Housewives and working women should conserve time to make time for housework, children's education, other self-cultivation, reading, and attending meetings of the Gold Hairpin Society (金釵會), National Defense Women's Association (國防 婦人會), lectures (講演會), training sessions (講習會), Patriotic Women's Association (愛國婦人會), etc.
The home is the beginning of all order (法).
The home is the model for society.
The home is the introduction agency (紹介所) for society.
The problems of national service and moderation will be resolved starting from the home.
Moderation (節制) is one of the four virtues (四德).
Thus, moderation is a virtue in no way inferior to wisdom, courage, and justice. In other words, moderation is the most sincere virtue among women's virtues (婦德).
Just as the saying goes, "United we stand strong, divided we fall weak," let us advance with one heart and mind in unity. In wartime, a drop of oil is said to be more precious than a drop of blood. Through moderation, let us help the Empire facing wartime, even if only by one ten-thousandth (1万分之一). It is said that those who lived without moderation die without honor. Let us all live each day with diligence and thrift, fulfilling the spirit of national service.
Let us now emphasize the spirit, unite the entire nation, persevere through the national crisis (國難), endure hardship (艱苦), and strive together so that homes and society may achieve the final victory for the Empire.
Showa 13 (1938), June 24th, 8 PM
At the Central Youth Hall (中央青年會舘)

The above are all speeches from a lecture meeting held at the Seoul Central Youth Hall by various leading women figures in our society. As the current situation becomes increasingly grave, they rose up together for the purpose of forging iron-like unity among the entire populace, determinedly concluding this incident with victory, thereby achieving the national policy of peace in East Asia. The crowd that night reached over a thousand, creating extraordinary emotion.
Samch'ŏlli Editorial Department, Paek <202>

**Summary**

This article compiles four speeches delivered by prominent Korean women (Hwang Sin-dŏk, Yu Kak-kyŏng, Yi Suk-chong, Chang Chŏng-sim) at a lecture meeting in Seoul in June 1938, published in the August issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine. The speeches unanimously urge Korean women to support Japan's war effort in China (Second Sino-Japanese War). Key themes include recognizing the prolonged "emergency situation," embracing the crucial role of women on the home front, practicing extreme frugality and saving resources (especially war materials like metal, cotton, fuel), utilizing waste, managing household economies efficiently for "national service" (報國), fostering national unity under the *Naissen Ittai* (內鮮一體) policy, raising children with Imperial spirit, and supporting the soldiers, including the newly implemented Korean volunteer system. The speeches frame these actions as essential duties of Imperial subjects and the most effective way for women to contribute to Japan's victory and the establishment of East Asian peace under Japanese leadership.

For historians, this article is a significant example of pro-Japanese collaborationist discourse promoted by elite Korean women during the colonial period. It vividly illustrates the mobilization rhetoric used to integrate Koreans, particularly women, into the Japanese wartime system, emphasizing sacrifice, austerity, and ideological conformity (*Kokutai Meichō*, *Naissen Ittai*). The speeches reveal the pressures and expected roles for women under wartime mobilization, linking domestic activities like saving and household management directly to the imperial war effort and highlighting the co-option of traditional feminine virtues (motherhood, thrift) for state purposes. The reference to the new volunteer soldier system underscores the increasing militarization of Korean society and the push for Koreans to demonstrate loyalty through direct participation in the Japanese military.

戰時 演說
비상 시국과 가정 경제
黃信德

1. 비상 시국의 재인식
회고하건댄 어언간 1년이란 세월이 흘러같읍니다. 작년 7월 北支사변이 발발되었을 그때야말로 「아- 전쟁이로구나! 국방 헌금을 하자. 출전 군인 見送을 나가자. 비녀를 뽑자. 가락지를 버서라」 京鄕을 물론하고 국민 총동원의 공기가 창일하였읍니다. 그러나 支那와의 전쟁은 我 황군의 忠勇 무쌍한 사기로서 一戰一勝, 二戰二勝, 백전이면 백승의 기세로 북경 함락, 남경, 徐州까지 드디어 보기조케 함락을 시키고야＜189＞ 말었읍니다. 여긔에 우리는 축하의 국기를 높이 달고 승전가를 부르면서 기뿜에 날뛰는 장사진의 학생 행렬을 멫 차레나 격게 되기 때문에 비상 시국으서의 긴장미보다는 「なんだ相手はなんでも, ないぢやないか!」 다시 말하면 勝戰者로서의 희열과 아울러 극도로 긴장했든 기분이 다소 풀리지 않었나 하는 것이니 이것이야말로 크게 우려할 문제가 아닌가 합니다.
우리는 大政治家나 先知者가 아닌 이상 이 전쟁이 언제까지 갈 것이며 또 어떻게 귀결이 될는지 모릅니다. 그러나 우리 銃後의 국민으로서는 장기전을 矛想하고 그 대책에 임하지 않으면 않될 것입니다. 설사 전쟁이 내일 끝이 난다 할지라도 우리의 각오와 준비만은 5년을 가고 10년을 끈다 할지라도 염려가 없을 만큼 미리 미리 국민이 총동원하야 이 비상 시국을 극복하여야 할 것이니 이때야말로 비상시 중에 비상시가 아니오니까!

2. 銃後 부인의 임무
옛날에는 전쟁이라 하면 총이나 칼을 가지고 전장에 직접 싸우러 나가는 병정만이 하는 것으로 알어왔고 또 사실에 잇어서 무기가 발달되지 못하였든 그 시절에는 출전 군인만의 전쟁이었다구 해도 과언이 아니었겠읍니다. 하나 오늘날의 전쟁은 제 일선에 출전한 군인만의 전쟁이 아니라 국민 전체의 전쟁이올시다. 그것은 무슨 소린가? 하고 질문하실 분이 게시겠지요. 여러분! 저 支那가 수 만흔 兵隊를 가지고 자기 국토에서 싸운다는 핸드캡을 가지고도 異域 地理의 익숙지 못한 소수 皇軍에게 참패를 당하는 그 원인이 어데 잇는 줄 아십니까? 그것은! 그것은 오직 두가지 큰 힘의 차이가 있기 때문입니다. 하나는 천황 폐하께와 국가를 위하야 이 몸을 바친다는 奉公의 情神의 차이, 또 하나는 육, 해, 공군을 통하야 발달된 무기 기술이 우리보다 떠러저 있다는 사실 그것입니다. 皇軍의 히생적 정신은 어데서 생기었으며 육해군의 자랑할만한 備置는 一朝 一夕에 된 것이겠습니까. 총후에 있는 부인! 그는 곳 군인 어머니 않해입니다.
「살아서는 도라오지 말어라」하는 엄숙한 어머니의 교훈과 後顧의 염려를 일호도 없게 해주는 현명한 안해의 힘이 없섯든들 황군의 광휘있는 今日이 있었을는지 모를 것이오 평소의 수많은 국민의 노력의 結晶이 아니었든들 저만한 무기가 준비되었을가가 의문이겠읍니다. 다시 말하면 비상시국에 처하는 우리의 마음과 물질이 직접 전쟁에 영향을 주는 것으로써 조이 한장 못 한개를＜190＞ 아끼고 아니 앗기는 그것이 곳 전쟁입니다. 여러분 웅장한 비행기가 공중에 날고 있는 것을 봅니다. 그 정밀한 기게 속 어느 부분에 못 한개가 빠젓기 때문에 어떠한 고장이 생기는지 알 수 없읍니다. 오늘 아츰 주먹밥이 한 덩어리 부족하기 때문에 어느 중요한 군인 한 분의 기운이 부치어 있는지도 모르는 것입니다. 이러한 의미에서 오늘의 전쟁은 국민 총동원의 전쟁이요 따라서 銃後에 있는 우리 부인의 임무가 重且大 하다고 생각합니다.

3. 가정 경제 報國
이상의 말슴한 바와 같이 총후 국민의 일거수 일투족의 행동이 전부 간접적 전쟁이라고 생각할 것 같으면 우리 부인으로서 이 비상시국에 있어서 報國의 길이 어데 있다는 것을 넉넉히 알 수가 있을 줄 압니다.
貯蓄報國强調週間에 대해서 몇 마듸 첨가하겠읍니다. 우리 부인들은 가정에서 착한 어머니와 어진 안해가 되는 동시에 우리의 손으로 좌우되고 있는 가정 경제로써 보국을 하는 것이 帝國의 臣民으로서 할 의무요 또 우리 여성으로서 가장 능률을 발휘할 수 있는 보국의 길이라고 생각합니다. 보통 가정 경제를 운운하면 근검 절약한다는 말이지마는 오늘날 가정 경제는 특히 戰局이 장기인 것, 이에 따라 우리 생활에 조절이 없으면 어느 때 어떠한 곤경에 빠질는지 모르는 그야말노 비상한 이 시국인 고로 평소보다는 좀 더 다른 연구와 태도를 가지고 임하여야 할 것입니다.
몬저 우리가 알어 두어야 할 것이 있으니 전시 경제에 대한 지식입니다. 즉 전쟁이 되면 웨 물가가 올라가는지? 여러분은 日支사변 이후로 소고기 값이 오르고 석탄 장작 고무신 무었할 것 없이 무척 비싸저서 웬만한 사람은 도무지 정신을 차리지 못할 지경이오 더구나 순금으로 많드는 비녀 가락지는 禁하고 포목에도 순전한 무명것은 짜지를 안코 모다 스테불 파이버를 석거서 짜냅니다. 대체 이것이 무슨 일일가 하고 다만 걱정만 하고 있는 이가 있지마는 이것은 다른 까닭이 아니라 우리네 일상 생활품 중에 전쟁에 필요한 물품이 있는 까닭입니다. 즉, 鐵, 燃料, 紙, 毛皮 등을 위시하야 어느 것이든지 전쟁과 직접 간접 관련되지 않은 것이 없읍니다. 그런데 이러한 군수품의 원료나 제품이 무한하고 있는 것이 아닌데다가, 전쟁은 언재까지 갈는지 작정할 수 없는 것이 되기 때문에 당장 물건이 떠러진 것은 아니라 할지라도 만일 없어지면 전쟁과 국민＜191＞ 생활에 파탄을 가저올 터이니까 미리 미리 일반 국민에게 앗겨서 사용하라는 경종으로 정부에서 정책적으로 물가를 올리는 것도 있고 이러한 시기를 악용하는 奸商輩들의 作亂도 있고 혹은 사실에 있어서 품절이 된 것도 없지 아니할 것입니다.
그러므로 우리 가정 부인되는 이들로서는 평소에 막연한 생각으로 근검 절약하여야 한다는 관렴에서 한 거름 나와 전시 체제 하에 있는 경제 상태를 좀 더 구체적으로 연구하야 1. 우리나라에서 생산할 수 있는 원료나 제품은 무었인가? 2. 전쟁의 필수품으로 가장 중요한 것은 무었인가? 군수품으로 외국에서 수입되는 것은 무었인가? 우리의 노력으로 충당할 수 있는가 하는 등을 상식적으로남아 배워 가지고 내 한 가정에 경제를 조절해 나가지 않으면 않되겠읍니다.
이른바 전쟁이다! 비상시다! 하고 消燈法을 배우고 午砲를 停止하며 떠든다 하드래도 아직까지 우리 눈앞에 피흘리는 군인이 뵈이지 안코 공중에서 폭탄 떠러지는 것을 구정하지 못한 일반 우리 국민은 아즉도 歐洲전쟁 당시에 비하야 태평한 생활을 하고 있다 할 수 있읍니다. 구주전쟁 때 독일 부인의 가장 머리를 쓰는 일은 그 날 그 날 가족의 식료품을 어더오는 일 어더온 한 조각의 빵을 가지고 평균하게 분배하는 일이었다 합니다. 그 시기도 오히려 행복스러웠든 것처럼 나종에는 검은 조각도 빵 한 조차레에 도라오지 아니하야 톳밥을 주어다가 소곰을 뿌려 익혀서 일시적 주림을 면하였다 합니다. 정부로서도 감자 껍질을 벗기는 자에게는 벌금에 처한다는 법령까지 나리도록 세밀하게 국민의 생활을 조절시켯다 합니다.
우리는 아즉까지 이와 같은 쓰라린 맛은 다행이 모른다 할지라도 결코 여기서 안심하여서는 않될 것입니다. 만일의 경우를 矛想하고 이 전쟁이 5년 10년을 간다 하더래도 넉넉히 버티어 나갈만한 물적 준비를 해 두어야 할 것입니다. 이러한 인식과 각오 밑에서 우리의 가정 경제를 요리한다면 한 가정 물론하고 국가의 경제도 흔들리지 안코 난관을 돌파해 나갈 수 있을 것입니다.

4. 가정 경제의 구체적 방법
어떻게 하여야 일정한 수입을 가지고 지헤있게 소비하야 여유를 많들 수 있을가? 하는 문재는 일상 우리 부인들의 관심하는 바임에는 틀림없으나 사실에 있어 우리들의 생활은 아즉까지 원시적 생활 그대로가＜192＞ 아닌가 생각됨니다.
첫재 의식주 세 가지많으로 보드래도 가옥 제도의 불합리를 느껴온 지 오랩니다. 안방과 부엌이 반듯이 통해서 스립퍼를 끌고 주부가 집접 일을 할 수 있으면 하녀 한 사람을 두지 안코도 넉넉히 알뜰하게 살 줄 알면서도 토방 부엌에 물독을 파묻어 놓고 구지지한 살림을 하는 것이 우리의 생활이 아닙니까! 의복 음식 할 것 없이 좀 더 연구와 개선을 가해서 시간적으로나 물질적으로 얼마든지 경제를 할 수 있으리라고 알면서도 아즉까지 몃 천년 직 백년 나려오는 그 방식 고대로만 직히고 있지 않은가 합니다. 이야말로 문명한 오늘날에 뒤떠러진 증거요 이러한 비상 시국이 모순도 극한현상이라 아니할 수 없읍니다. 그러므로 우리는 우리의 생활을 간편하게 개선하는 것이 가정 경제의 제일보라고 생각하나 이것은 一朝一夕에 일우어질 일이 아님으로 언제든지 우리의 숙제도 염두에 둘 것이지마는 우리들로서 취할 태도는 그것보다도 좀 더 긴급을 요하는 軍需品을 절약할 것입니다. 다시 말하면 연료, 木綿, 철, 紙 같은 것을 허피 쓰거나 버리거나 하는 일은 절대로 말어야 할 것입니다. 그 박에 집안에 있는 어떠한 물건이든지 머리를 써서 연구하야 이용할 수 있는대로 이용하고 절약할 수 있는 정도 및 절약 애호할 것입니다.
이와 같이 집안 어느 구석에 박혀있는 한 조각의 솜과 한 개의 바늘이라도 버리는 것은 이 시간에 처하는 부인의 태도가 아니라는 것을 알어야 할 것입니다. 다음으로 사치품은 절대로 사용하지 말 것입니다. 정부에서는 그러한 취지 밑에서 금비녀의 세금이 붓고 레-코드에 세금이 붙고 외래의 화장품 수입을 금지하고 있읍니다. 아무리 좋은 옷과 밥이 만타 하드래도 제일선에서 악전 고투하는 우리의 군인을 생각하면 너무 화려한 옷이나 장식품을 가지는 것은 국민적 태도가 아니라고 생각합니다. 그러므로 우리의 오늘날 생활은 검소하게 절대 필수품만을 사고 쓰고 할 것입니다.
끝으로 한 가지 방법은 저축입니다. 오늘 굶고 죽이 어디 있겠습니까 마는 우리는 지금 오늘밤에라도 어떠한 비상한 일이 생길넌지 모른다는 것을 잊어서는 않됩니다. 얼마 전에도 여러분 경험하었지요? 밤중에 갑자기 비상 경적이 울려왔읍니다. 다행이 무사하였지마는 어떤 때 어떠한 일을 당해서 교통 차단이 되드래도 어린 아기들을 굼주리지 안토록 언재든지 쌀과 나무와 성랑 물 이 네 가지는 좀 넉넉히 준비해 둘 필요가＜193＞ 있읍니다. 돈을 가지고도 살 수 없는 경우도 있을넌지 모르고 오늘보다 멫 배나 더 물가가 騰貴될 것까지를 矛想하야 現品과 돈을 될 수 있는 대로 저축하여야 할 것입니다. 다시 말하면 첫재로는 군수품의 절약 둘재로는 사치한 생활은 금하고 또 근검한 생활을 할 것 셋재로 만일의 경우를 당하더래도 당황해 하지 안을만한 물적 준비를 하여야 한다는 말습입니다.
요컨대 이 非常時局의 비상시인 금일을 철저히 인식하고 銃後 부인으로서 심적 준비는 물론, 물적 방면으로 「한 조각의 조이라도 나라를 위하야!」라는 관념 밑에서 우리의 분에 가장 적당한 가정 경제에서 보국의 실을 거두도록 노력합시다.

朝鮮女子靑年會 總務 兪珏卿

현 사회에서는 일반 여성에게 많은 관심을 가지고 있읍니다. 그 이유는 예로부터 여성에 대한 관념이나 비평이 일정치 못하야 여자는 남자의 종속물이니 「弱者여. 네 일홈은 여자」이니 하는 불명예스러운 말을 항다반 듯고 있으나 其 반면에 「童車를 끄으는 여자의 손이 세계를 움즉인다」는 말도 없지 않으며 一國家의 强弱은 그 나라의 어머니를 보아 알 수 있다. 이와 같은 여러 가지 말들을 함니다. 본인이 여긔서 말하고저 하는 것은 여자는 弱하너 强하니 從屬物이 아니라는 판단을 하려는 것이 아니라.
남성도 一介의 인격이오 여성도 일개의 인격이나 그 두 인격은 그 본분이 각각 다름을 말하고저 함니다.
이 두 인격의 본분이 다름에 비로소 두 인격의 고귀한 점이 있으며 각자가 충분히 그 본분을 직힘으로써 서로 도음이 되고 이 도음이 합할 때에 비로소 국가 사회는 유지되고 국가 발전의 기초는 이루어지는 것임니다.
現下 非常時局에 있어 대체 여성은 무엇을 하여야 하겠읍니까. 이것은 우리 여성으로서 신중히 생각해 볼 문제임니다. 급한 대로 직접 총을 메고 戰地에 나가서 將兵과 보조를 같이 하는 것이 훌융한 여걸이나 되는 것처렴 생각하기 쉽슴니다. 그러나 본인의 생각에는 우에 말한 바와 같이 역시 여자에게는 여자의 본분이 있다고 봄니다.＜194＞
총을 메고 戰地에 나가 적을 물리치는 것은 남자에게 맛기여 남자다운 의기로 忠勇을 다- 하게 하고 여자는 안에서 後顧에 근심이 없도록 총후를 굿게 직히는 것이 여성으로 국가에 대한 忠義라는 것을 깊히 깨닷어서 其 방면에 진력하는 것이 여성의 본분이라고 생각함니다.
그러면 銃後는 어떻게 직히겠느냐는 것이 다음 階段이겠읍니다.
전쟁이라는 것은 일시엣 것이오 늘- 있는 것이 아님니다. 금번 사변으로 말하여도 出征한 장병의 意氣와 정예한 武器로서 조만간에 皇軍의 대승리를 보리라고 밋음니다. 그러니까 전쟁하는 것은 남자에게 맛기고 총후 여성의 할 바로 말하면 첫재로 제일 중요한 것은 가정을 잘 직히여 家産을 보관하고 제2세 국민을 고상한 정신과 건강한 육체의 소유자로 길너놋는 것이 여성의 각오할 바 중에 가장 중요한 일인줄 압니다. 더욱이 오날에 있어 志顧兵이 실시되여 국가의 장래의 성쇠가 오로지 제2세 국민의 심신 건강 여하에 좌우한다는 것을 생각하면 그 사명이 중대하며 따라서 우에서 말한 바와 같이 童車를 미는 손이 세계를 좌우할 특권이 잇음을 자랑할 만한 일인줄 압니다.
둘재로는 근검 절약적 정신에 대해서 말하고저 함니다. 평소에도 절약이 있어야 하는 것이지마는 특히 전시에 있어서는 일층 주의할 것인줄 압니다. 歐洲대전에 佛蘭西 여성들은 「短袴」會를 조직하여 치마의 길이를 짤게 하여 곤란을 구하였다는 말이었는데 금일 우리가 비상시를 당하고 보니 비로소 佛蘭西 여자들의 근검한 정신을 깨다른 것 같음니다.
치마 길이를 1, 2寸 짤게 한다고 이것을 막대한 군비와 희생에 비교한다면 실로 九牛의 一毛와 같은 것임니다.
나는 이런 말을 들었음니다. 우리 조선 가정에서 쓰는 이불(침구)로 말하면 엇재서 동정이 잇고 깃이 있고 푸르며 붉은 것으로 만들어 쓰는 줄 아심니까. 그것은 예전에 만일 전쟁이 나면 그 이불을 뜨어서 병정들이 옷을 하여 입으며 그 동정을 끈어서 홍띄 모양으로 억개에 걸치고서 나가서 싸웟기 때문이었다 함니다. 또 우리 일반 가정에서 쓰는 鍮器로 말하면 전쟁이 날 적에 그것으로 총도 만들고 군수품으로도 쓰기 위해서 그리함이라고 함니다. 예전에도 이러하거든 하물며 오늘 우리네 주부의 각오이리까. 앗가도 말한 바와 가치＜195＞ 佛蘭西 여자들이 치마를 짤게 하여 국난 구조의 한 도음이라도 주겠다는 여긔에 여자 독특하고 정밀한 정신이 숨어었다고 나는 생각함니다. 여자의 진정에서 울어난 이 치마 기리 한 치가 당시 佛蘭西의 非常前, 경제에 얼마나한 反響이 있는지 알냐고도 아니하나 만일 이 「短袴會」 회원의 赤誠이 佛蘭西의 곤란을 구하는 원동력이 되여 가지고서 총후의 가정을 직켜 나갑시다.
그래서 금일 총후에 半島 여성도 사치한 의복을 질박한 것으로 곳치며 色衣를 장려하며 금전과 시간을 동시 절약하야 저- 佛蘭西 여성과 같은 업적을 남긴다는 赤誠으로 행하여야 겠읍니다. 조선 俗語에 집이 가난하면 어진 어머니를 생각하고 국가가 어지러우면 어진 재상을 생각한다고 한 것을 보면 고금을 물론하고 곤란을 당하면 총동원이 없이는 成事가 어려운 것을 깨닷겠읍니다. 그런 고로 今日같은 비상 시기를 당한 銃後 여성의 책임이 더욱 중대한 것을 각오하고 內鮮一體化하야 가정으로 국가 방침 하에 충실히 실행하는 것이 총후 국민된 의무요 따라서 時難 극복에 어진 저 어미가 되고 장병에 慈母될 수 있을 줄 압니다.

시국이 항구적으로 사변이 많은 이때에 北支사변 이래로 제국의 권위는 더욱 더 세계에 선양되였다고 생각합니다. 이는 제국 국민된 자로써 누구나 총동원하야 報國의 전력하는 까닭입니다.
제일선에 나아간 우리 황국 장사들은 생명을 바처 용감하게 선전하였으므로 작년 7월 이후로 아직 1년밧게 안된 오늘에 北支에 여러 지역을 점령하게 된 것은 용사들의 천신만고의 혈전이라고 생각 아니할 수 없읍니다. 마치 그 苦勞는 산파의 역할보다도 산모의 신고라고 압니다. 처자를 두고 본가를 떠나간 용사들은 戰地에 나가 전심으로 충의를 다하며 其外에도 부상자를 잘 보호해야 동고 동락한다 합니다.
총후의 전국민은 國體明徵 하에 擧國一致의 협력과 堅忍持久의 정신으로 男女老幼가 報國하는 중에 더욱 더 가정의 주부로써 戰地에 출정한 남편과 親子를 위하야 정신과 물질과 시간을 다 드려 오늘의 저만한 위대한＜199＞ 업적을 거둔 것입니다. 그러나 시일이 長長하게 되며 국민 정신이 다소간 완화되지 않을지 모르고 또한 앞으로 혹독한 더위에 염려가 없지 아니한 이때에 報國의 정신을 가진 우리 일반 가정 주부들로써는 이때를 잘 인식할 의무가 있다고 생각해서 한번 더 그 정신을 강조키 위하야 관민 일치로 활동하게 되였읍니다. 이번 이 활동은 단순히 교화 선전 운동에만 끝칠 일이 아니라 앞으로 더욱 더 총동원의 정신을 강조하야 끝까지 나아가는 것입니다. 다시 말하면 일상 생활에 꾸준이 나아가 최후의 勝捷을 획득하려는 것입니다.
이 사변이 끝날 때까지 우리 황국 부녀로써는 최선을 다하는 것이 報國의 본분인 줄 압니다.
