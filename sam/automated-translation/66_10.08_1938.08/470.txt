Feature-Length Youth
						Yi Kiyŏng (李箕永)
One. The Night After the Birthday
The story begins in a certain countryside village some ten-odd years from now.

1
That day too, at Councilor Kim’s (金叅事) house, people bustled in and out, making a commotion all day long.
Because the very day before was Councilor Kim’s birthday, not only family members from near and far, influential relatives by marriage and blood, and acquaintances from nearby and distant villages, but even tenant farmers living far away had been busy bringing gifts for several days already, seeking to make a good impression on the landlord master on this occasion.
The rich man's birthday feast continued until the next day. If the main day was the feast for the gentry, the following day could be called the feast for the servants. That day, many lower-ranking people crowded in. <254>
Thus, Councilor Kim’s birthday feast was commemorated successfully this year as well, but because everyone, high and low, had been fully occupied with the big event for two days, they were all thoroughly exhausted. The masters were tired from entertaining guests, eating well, and enjoying themselves; the servants were tired from preparing food and setting up the feast – they had all practically stayed up all night the previous night.
And yet, they spent today in the same way all day long.
As the sun began to set, the feast finally ended. The house, having finished the big event, was as quiet as a house after a shamanistic ritual (굿). After eating dinner, they were each busy preparing for bed. Having eaten and drunk their fill, they had no wish other than sleep. Moreover, the short spring night made them feel extremely weary. Drunk on alcohol and full from food, they were about to succumb to drowsiness and drunkenness from sleep.
The maids in the kitchen, finishing the dishes late, yawned repeatedly and nodded off. The voices of the servants, chatting in twos and threes, faded away one by one. Before ten o'clock at night – the towering gate (소슬 대문) of Councilor Kim’s house was bolted shut from inside and out, and from this room and that, the loud sound of snoring, like thunder, arose.
And so, the whole house, if someone had quietly come in to look at this time, would truly have been a sight – each person collapsed in various postures, snoring in various sounds.
- Sshik! Saek! Ppik! P'ulttŏk! Tŭrŭrŭng... In the large master's room (사랑방), the electric light brightly illuminated the long room. A cover with colorful tassels was placed over the lampshade.
The room, freshly papered and floored like a powder box after removing the wall hangings, was dazzlingly fresh.
Councilor Kim had spread out his silk bedding (금침) in the warmest part of the room and was sleeping alone as usual.
He too was no less tired than anyone else from holding the feast yesterday and today. Moreover, being the host, he was mentally hazy from dealing with so many people. He was not originally a heavy drinker. Still, having accepted small amounts of alcohol offered by various people, he had remained thoroughly drunk, with no time to sober up between drinks. So, he had spent the whole day in a drowsy state, still suffering from the lingering effects of alcohol (숙취).
His snoring, exacerbated by nasal congestion (비색증), made his nostrils flare like bellows made of dog skin. But for some rhythm, he suddenly turned over and stopped snoring abruptly. At such times, when all sounds of nature (만뢰) were quiet, it seemed the only living thing was the ticking <255> sound of the clock hanging on the wall.
Councilor Kim, sleeping soundly, suddenly startled and sat up with a cry of "Uh-," like a frightened child. He had heard some strange sound between sleep and wakefulness.
Was it a dream? Reality?
The clock stood precisely at eleven.
The electric light in the room was still on.
But what was this?
Before his eyes, a dark figure appeared out of nowhere. As if sprung from beneath the earth. Who was it?
"Stay alert!"
The dark person, whose entire face was covered with a black cloth, stood aiming something shiny right at Councilor Kim’s chest.
"A masked robber (복면강도)!"
Finally snapping to his senses, Councilor Kim felt his hair stand on end and his courage fail. Dumbfounded (망지소조), he trembled all over, solely gripped by fear.

2
The intruder, maintaining his shooting stance, took another step closer.
"Shout and there will be no mercy. If you value your life, listen to me!"
Councilor Kim didn't know what he should say. Alas, he lacked the courage to yell. So he just lowered his head and remained silent like a prisoner.
"I am someone who came listening from afar. You should know my purpose for coming by now. Give me the money!"
He demanded calmly.
"Yes... but... where would there be money in the middle of the night? If you give me a few days' grace (말미), perhaps!"
Councilor Kim could finally show a slight sign of relief. Pretending to answer, he raised his head to look. No matter how he looked, he couldn't figure out who the strange man was, his entire face covered with a black cloth. <256>
"What kind of rascal could he be?... Is he really a robber driven by the times? Or is he a local?"
Councilor Kim pondered as he fastened his belt.
"How can I catch this thief like a rat in a trap?"
"What did you say? Are you saying you have no money in your house right now?"
"Yes! There's only loose change, no large sums."
Councilor Kim feigned ignorance (우엉을 깟다) as if he were telling the truth.
"Hmph, none? Let's see, open the safe (금고)!"
Councilor Kim’s heart skipped a beat. "This rascal knew about it!"
In fact, the safe contained over ten thousand won from selling land in Kyŏngsang Province just the other day. The day he withdrew the money was the afternoon before last; since it was a Saturday, time ran late, and he couldn't deposit it in the bank, ultimately leading to this disaster. The strange man had noticed this. Amidst his fear, Councilor Kim thought this and couldn't help but feel deep regret.
He had planned to buy a large farmstead in Chŏlla Province all at once by adding to the money from the Kyŏngsang land sale.
"The telephone! The telephone! If only I could make a call at a time like this... Isn't anyone awake?"
Councilor Kim felt extremely anxious inwardly. But wasn't the house, inside and out, dead silent?
"Is money dearer than your life? Still won't open it?!"
The strange man prepared to pull the trigger.
"One minute, two minutes! Three minutes... If you don't open it in three minutes, I'll shoot."
Councilor Kim had no choice but to unlock the large padlock on the closet door and then push open the safe door.
"Take out everything in there!"
Councilor Kim did as he was told. The strange man continued to stand aiming the pistol, monitoring his actions. Every time Councilor Kim took out money and placed it on the floor, he glanced sideways, looking for an opportunity, but the strange man gave him no such chance.
The weapon seemed real too.
The night visitor stuffed bundles of ten-won and hundred-won bills from among the various documents (문권) Councilor Kim brought out into his overcoat pockets and suit trouser pockets <257> as much as they could hold. He then called back Councilor Kim, whom he had sent to the upper part of the room, and ordered him to wrap the remaining money in newspaper. Councilor Kim obeyed the command like his servant. Having only ever ordered others around, experiencing such a situation for the first time in his life, he felt more shame than anger at having the large sum stolen.
The strange man picked up the bundles of bills Councilor Kim had tied, stood up, sent him back to the upper part of the room, meticulously inspected the inside of the safe, and then said,
"Thank you. Let's meet again later. Go ahead and report immediately that I visited."
He then calmly opened the sliding door and went out. He was wearing 'jikatabi' (지까다비 - Japanese work boots). As soon as the sound of him stepping down from the porch was heard, accompanied by the creak of the main gate opening, the strange man disappeared into the darkness.
Councilor Kim sat blankly for a while, staring at the middle gate through which he had exited.
Suddenly! He came to his senses and ran barefoot to the telephone room installed in the small inner room.
"Ah!"
Councilor Kim, opening the small room's door, suddenly gasped and cried out. The meticulous strange man had already cut the receiver's cord at some point. At the bold and audacious actions of the strange man, Councilor Kim could only be dumbfounded.
"Thief! There's been a robbery! Yŏnguk-ah!"

3
The first person awakened by Councilor Kim’s commotion (설네발) was Yŏnguk. Being a faithful head servant (상노) who handled Councilor Kim’s errands, the sound of his master calling made his ears sharp, like a worker hearing a siren.
Yŏnguk, hearing Councilor Kim's voice in his sleep, jumped up, rubbed his eyes, and ran to the large master's room. The middle gate was open, and the master's room door was wide open too; the master stood on the porch, breathing heavily like an ox.
"Something must have happened!"
Yŏnguk, having been startled awake by his name being called last, didn't know a robber had entered.
"You called, sir!" <258>
"You sleep soundly without knowing a thief entered? Eh-"
Councilor Kim, despite having slept unaware of the thief entering his own room, loudly blamed his innocent servant.
"Yes?! A thief entered, you say!"
Yŏnguk is startled and bewildered (망지 소조).
"A robber... a robber broke in."
"Ah!... A rob-ber-?"
Yŏnguk steps back, his mouth agape as if screaming.
"But when?"
"Just now! Hey... quickly wake up the eldest master! And... wake up Nŭsoe's father and quickly get the rickshaw! And is everyone dead inside the inner quarters? Wake everyone up! Ah- A major incident happened in the house, yet everyone inside and out is just sleeping? Huh?"
"Where are you planning to go, sir?"
"Stop talking nonsense and hurry up and tell them! Don't we need to report it to the police station?"
A little later, T'aeksu, who was sleeping in the smaller master's room, came over wearing only his inner jacket (동저고리ㅅ 바람). From the inner quarters too, clamorous sounds were heard, and one by one, people came through the door leading from the inner quarters to the master's quarters. Councilor Kim’s wife, trembling all over, came to her husband and stood with her mouth open.
"Oh my, what is all this?"
"What do you mean, what is all this! A robber enters the house, and you don't even know? How were the doors secured! Huh?"
Councilor Kim throws a fearsome glance at his wife.
"A robber broke in?"
T'aeksu too felt a chill and exclaimed.
"I told them to lock the main gate from early evening! Hey! Where is Nŭsoe's mother? Who locked the main gate!"
Councilor Kim’s wife passes the anger received from her husband onto the servant.
"Her husband locked it early in the evening!" <259>
Nŭsoe's mother answers barely, her voice trembling. Behind her, T'aeksu's wife, the seamstress, the nanny, and female relatives stand in line, holding their breath, pressed against the back wall.
"Master, Madam, the rickshaw is ready,"
Nŭsoe's father announced, bowing low (고패) before the porch. Councilor Kim hastily put on his clothes.
"Then how did the thief get in? Didn't any suspicious person come during the day!"
"No such person came, sir."
Yŏnguk scratches the back of his head with one hand.
"Truly, I've seen everything now. They say even the dogs don't bark when you're about to meet a thief. But why was the safe door open?"
"Why else would a robber come in! Why are you standing there talking nonsense!"
"What kind of weapon did he have?"
As his son asks, Councilor Kim, dressed and coming out onto the porch, says,
"He had a pistol. He took all the money from the land sale. Hoo – That rascal even cut the telephone line somehow."
"Yes?"
At the mention of a pistol, the people were startled all at once, their fear intensifying.
"I'm going to file a report, so you stay awake!"
Behind Councilor Kim’s rickshaw, besides Yŏnguk, two or three men from the servants' quarters followed closely behind and ahead, rushing like the wind in a group effort (두패 잡이).

4
The next day, the S County (S군) Japanese police were turned upside down, as if a sudden crisis had occurred.
The police station conducted an emergency summons (비상 소집) for officers even from each township substation (주재서원), and starting with the chief, dozens of officers were fully mobilized.
The police contingent, looking intimidating just by sight, all pulled down their chin straps, wore leggings (각반), and clanked their military swords (군도), guarding every key point on the streets without leaving any gaps.
"Pistol-wielding robber appears!"
Anyone hearing just this phrase would be terrified, but moreover, the fact that the pistol-wielding robber, who knew somehow, had attacked the house of Councilor Kim, the most influential figure and <260> great landlord in this town, and escaped after robbing over 20,000 won in cash, was a major incident, rare in recent times. Given the current situation, the surprise among officials and civilians was considerable. The wealthy felt anxious, unable to sleep soundly at night.
Naturally, the authorities were even more concerned. The duty officer, upon hearing Councilor Kim's report, immediately called the chief and the section chief, who soon rushed over and first carefully listened (청취) to Councilor Kim's detailed account of the crime.
After asking Councilor Kim to leave the traces of the crime (범적) at the scene untouched, they secretly discussed the search strategy while issuing an emergency summons for the officers. Designating the police box at the train station as the headquarters, the chief and all personnel began a full mobilization in all directions. Simultaneously, they alerted the entire network via security telephone and arranged for close coordination, especially with neighboring counties, directing all efforts towards the apprehension of the criminal.
However, they failed to catch the culprit that night. Police officers guarded Councilor Kim’s house.
Before dawn the next day, the chief of the Higher Police section (고등게 주임) led a team of detectives to conduct an on-site inspection (림검) of the crime scene.
"Ah! Please come up – You are working too hard so early in the morning."
Councilor Kim came out onto the porch to greet them, appearing truly grateful. Awakened from a deep sleep and having stayed up all night, their faces were puffy and their lips chapped.
"Ah! Not at all."
The section chief took off his cap, bowed politely, and then led his team first into the telephone room.
"Was the culprit wearing gloves?"
At the section chief's question, Councilor Kim tilted his head and thought for a moment.
"Yes, he wore black gloves."
"Hmph! What was he wearing on his feet?"
"He wore jikatabi."
However, no footprints remained. Although muddy footprints should have remained on the floor, after the robber left, Councilor Kim had entered the room, and due to the commotion (설네발) he raised, people from the house followed and bustled about, trampling <261> the traces underfoot, so no jikatabi prints could be found despite searching.
They meticulously examined the inside and outside of the room, but couldn't find even a fingernail-sized piece of physical evidence.
Therefore, they tried to find out where the culprit entered by examining the house's surrounding wall (墻垣) inside and out. Whether he climbed over the back wall or climbed the wall in front of the master's quarters gate and stepped into the flower bed, footprints should have remained somewhere. Moreover, where the culprit climbed onto the wall and jumped down inside, footprints should have been clearly imprinted. But strangely, no matter how thoroughly they searched, like looking for lice, such traces (자최) were not found.
"Hmm! The culprit did not come over the wall. Definitely not. He entered through the main gate during the day and hid."
The section chief exclaimed this after pondering something for a while.
"How could he enter during the day?"
At Councilor Kim’s question, the section chief answered with more confidence,
"Many people came and went yesterday too, right?"
"Yes, yesterday was also busy as it was the end of the feast."
"Then he must have come in amidst the crowd, hidden somewhere, and waited for nightfall!"
"Ha! Ha..."
Councilor Kim nodded his head.

5
The Higher Police section chief, perhaps having found a ray of light in his investigation strategy, barred outsiders and entered the room to begin a secret interrogation of Councilor Kim.
After asking for the addresses and names of everyone who came yesterday, he inquired,
"Did any unfamiliar travelers or beggars come?"
"They say no such person came. Let me ask the servants once more."
"Yes, please do!"
"Shall I call them here?" <262>
"Yes!"
Councilor Kim called loudly for Yŏnguk. A long answering voice came from inside, and shortly after, Yŏnguk appeared before the porch, bowing low (고패) with his hands clasped.
The Higher Police section chief asked in clumsy Korean,
"Yesterday, did any unfamiliar person or any beg-gar come?"
As Yŏnguk hesitated for a moment, Councilor Kim, seeming impatient, shouted sharply.
"He's asking if any travelers or beggars came!"
"Yes... No travelers came, but many beggars came."
"Are you sure no strangers came?"
"Yes, no unfamiliar people came."
"Ah, then good!"
The section chief gestured for him to leave. Yŏnguk scratched the back of his head and went outside.
The section chief! To Detective Ri (리 형사)! a sharp-eyed, tall man! ordered him to quickly return after meticulously recording the addresses, names (씨명), occupations, ages (년녕), kinship relationships (친²™ 관게 - likely typo for 친척 관계), and financial status of all those who came to the birthday feast over the past two days – the day before yesterday and yesterday. Having obtained no material clues, they withdrew from the crime scene.
Armed police officers stood guard at the main gate of Councilor Kim's house since dawn, monitoring everyone entering and exiting.
The Higher Police section chief returned immediately, reported to the police chief, and then dispatched detectives in all directions to investigate shops in the city that sold jikatabi yesterday.
According to his thinking, the culprit's only lead was the jikatabi-wearing young man. Judging by the fact that he wore a mask, he was clearly a local. If he had come from afar, there would be no need to wear a mask, and more importantly, he wouldn't know the layout of the house so well. If so, wasn't the culprit a resident of a nearby village who frequently visited Councilor Kim’s house?
That's why he must have come before sunset yesterday, hidden somewhere, and appeared at night. Moreover, the large courtyard inside the main gate – with stacks of firewood and straw piled under the wall, and two large pagoda trees serving as shade trees – offered many hiding places. <263>
"But where did he get the pistol?"
Seeing that the culprit had a pistol, he might also be a political offender (시국 범인), or even if not directly involved, he might have indirect connections with them. This was certainly not a lone act.
If so, even if he is a local, he must be a person under surveillance (요시찰인). The unfortunate thing was that Councilor Kim couldn't recall the culprit's appearance at all. Even though he wore a mask, there should have been some idea of his height, build, or voice, but Councilor Kim, awakened from sleep while drunk and thoroughly frightened, had no recollection; his words were utterly incoherent.
The detectives sent to investigate the shops selling jikatabi returned shortly. According to their reports,
nothing conclusive could be found from that alone.
However, the suspicion that the culprit was a local – especially a person under surveillance – seemed increasingly likely to be a strong lead. Therefore, the section chief waited for Detective Ri, who was tasked with investigating the identities of those who attended Councilor Kim's birthday feast, to return. They planned to select persons under surveillance from the list, secretly trail their recent activities and daily lives, and launch a large-scale operation, striving desperately (매진) to search for the culprit.
In that case, the arrest of the culprit was only a matter of time. (Continued in the next issue 次號續) <264>

**Summary**

The story begins the day after a lavish birthday celebration for Councilor Kim, a wealthy landowner. Exhausted from two days of feasting, the household falls into a deep sleep. Late at night, a masked man armed with a pistol breaks into Kim's room, forces him to open his safe, and steals over 10,000 won acquired from a recent land sale. The robber cuts the telephone line before escaping in jikatabi (Japanese work boots). The police investigation, led by the Higher Police section chief, focuses on the theory that the robber was likely a local familiar with the house, possibly someone who attended the party and hid. They suspect the culprit might be a 'person under surveillance' (요시찰인), a term indicating individuals watched by the colonial authorities, and begin investigating the party attendees.

This chapter offers insight into colonial Korean society, highlighting the stark contrast between wealthy landowners like Kim Ch'amsa and their tenant farmers and servants. The incident reveals anxieties about crime, particularly armed robbery, in the period. The police response showcases Japanese colonial methods, including emergency summons, the role of the Higher Police (often involved in political surveillance), and the category of 'persons under surveillance,' reflecting the political tensions and control exerted by the colonial regime. The blend of traditional Korean customs (birthday feasts) and modern elements (telephones, safes, police procedures) paints a picture of a society in transition under colonial rule.

一. 생일 뒷날 밤
이야기는 지금부터 십 수년 전 어느 시골에서부터 시작된다.

1
그 날도 김 참사(金叅事) 집에서는 안 팍 없이 사람들이 들낙 거리며 왼 종일을 두고 벅석을 노았다.
바로 그 전날이 김 첨사의 생신인 까닭에 대 소가의 집안 식구와 번화한 인아 족친 간은 물론이요 원근동의 친지 할 것 없이 멀니 사는 작인들까지도 이런 게제에 지주영감의 눈에 잘 뵈이랴고 벌서 몇 칠전부터 선물을 드리기에 분주하였다.
부자집 생일잔치는 그 이튼날까지 게속되였다. 당일을 양반들의 잔치라면 그 이튼날은 하인들의 잔치라 할가. 그 날은 아랫사람들이 많이 들끄럿다.＜254＞
이렇게 김 참사의 생일잔치는 올에도 성황으로 기렴(記念)하였는데 아래 위 사람을 물론하고 이틀 동안이나 큰 일을 치르기에 붓배기로 매달린 까닭에 그들은 모두다 피로할 대로 피로하였다. 주인네는 손님 대접하고 잘 먹고 잘 노느라고 하인들은 음식 만들고 잔치를 설비하기에 - 그들은 모두들 어제 밤을 새다 싶이 하였든 것이다.
그런데 그들은 오날도 종일 또 그렇게 지났다.
이럭저럭 해가 어슬핏하자 잔치는 비로소 끗이 났다. 큰 일을 끗낸 집안은 굿하고 난 집 같이 괴괴하였다. 저녁을 먹고 나자 그들은 제각금 잠자리를 보기에 밧벗다. 실컷, 먹고 마시고 하엿스니 인제는 잠 박게 소원이 없었다. 더구나, 봄날의 짜론 밤은, 그들을 한껏, 뢰곤하게 하였다. 술에 취하고 밥에 취한 그들은 다시 식곤증이 나서 잠에 취하기 시작할 판이다.
부엌에서 뒤늦게 설에질을 하든 행낭 어멈들도 연해 선 합품을 하며 꿉벅꿉벅 조럿다. 두세 두세 하든 하인들의 목소리도 하나 둘식 없어저 갓다. 밤 열 시가 되기 전에 - 김 참사집 소슬 대문은 안 팟그로 첩첩히 잠겨지고, 이 방 저 방에서는 우레같은 코고는 소리가 높이 낫다.
그리하야 왼 집안은 이런 때에, 누가 가만이 드러와 보았으면 참으로 구경거리가 될 수 있을 만콤 - 각 사람은 가진 형국으로 씨러저서 가진 소리로 코를 골며 잔다.
- 씩! 색! 삑! 풀떡! 드르릉... 큰 사랑방에는 전등불이 이칸 장방을 환히 비첫다. 전등가사위로는 채단술을 매다른 보재기를 씨웟다.
방장을 갓으로 떼여내고 새로 도배 장판을 분통같이 꾸며놓은 방 안은 눈이 부시게 신선하다.
김 참사는 아래목에다 금침을 펴고 전과 같이 혼처 자고 있었다.
그도 어제 오늘 잔치를 치르기에 누구만 못지않게 피로하였다. 더구나 그는 주인인 만콤 뭇 사람에게 취해서 정신이 얼떨떨하였다. 그는 원래, 주객은 아니였다. 그래도 여러 사람이 권하는 바람에 조곰식 바더 마신 술이라 이 참 저 참, 깰 동안이 없을 만콤, 장취하고 있었다. 그래 그는 오히려 숙취가 미성하야 취기(醉氣)가 몽롱하게 왼 종일을 지났었다.
취중에 고는 코 고는 소리가 비색증까지 들녀서 개가죽 풀무처럼, 콧청을 풀딱거렸다. 그러나 무슨 박자(拍子)로인지 그는 별안간 몸을 뒤치며 콧소리를 뚝 끈첫다. 그런 때는 만뢰가 구적한데, 오즉 사러있는 것이라고는 뚝딱거리고 ＜255＞ 가는 벽에 걸닌 시계소리뿐인 것 갔다.
정신없이 자든 김 참사는 별안간 깜짝 놀내며 경풍한 아해처럼, 「어-」 소리를 치고 이러낫다. 그는 비몽사몽간에 무슨 괴상한 소리를 드럿든 것이다.
꿈이냐? 생시냐?
시계는 열 한시에 딱 섯다.
방안에는 전등불이 여전히 켜젓다.
그런데 이게 웬일인가?
그의 눈 앞에는 난데없는 웬 씨커면 사람이 나타낫다. 마치 땅 밑으로 소서오른 것 같이 누군가?
「정신차려!」
검정 수건으로 얼골 전체를 가린 거문 사람은 번쩍! 빛나는 것을 김 참사의 앙 가슴에 견우고 섯다.
「복면강도!」
비로소 정신이 펄쩍 난 김 참사는 머리끗이 쭙삣하고 간담이 서늘하엿다. 망지소조한 그는 오즉 공포감에 전신을 떨고 있었다.

침입자는 사격의 자세를 잃지 않고 한 거름 더 닥아서며
「소리치면 용서 없다. 목숨이 앗갑거든 내 말을 드러라!」
김 참사는 무에라고 대답해야 좋을는지 몰낫다. 우황, 그는 고함칠 용기는 없었다. 그래서 그는 다만 고개를 숙이고 죄수와 같이 묵묵히 있을 뿐이였다.
「나는 먼데서 드리 온 사람이야 그만하면 내가 온 목적은 알 것이다. 돈을 내라!」
그는 태연하게 부르짓는다.
「네... 그러시지만 아닌 밤 중에 돈이 어... 어디 있읍니까? 몇 일 말미를 주신다면 모르지만은! 」
김 참사는 그제야 저윽히 안심하는 기색을 띠울 수 있었다. 그는 대답하는 체하고 고개를 처들어 보았다. 거문 수건으로 왼 얼굴을 폭! 둘너싼 괴한은 아모리 보아도 누구인지 아러낼 수는 없었다. ＜256＞

김 참사는 허리띠를 잠매며 생각해보았다.
「어떻게 하면 이 도적놈을 독 안에 든 쥐처럼 잡을 수 있을가?」
「뭣이 어째? 늬 집에 지금 돈이 없단 말이냐?」
「녜! 잔돈푼 박게 많은 돈은 없어요」
김 참사는 진정인 것처럼 우엉을 깟다.
「흥 없나 어디 보자, 금고문을 여러라!」
김 참사는 가슴이 선뜻하였다. 「이놈이 그 속을 알고 왔구나!」
사실, 금고 속에는 엇그제 경상도 땅 판 돈이 만 여원이 드러 있다. 돈 찾든 날은 그적게 오후, 그 날은 마치 토요일이기 때문에 시간이 느저서 미처 은행 예금을 못하고 두엇다가 필경, 이런 화를 당하고 만 것이다. 괴한은 이 기미를 눈치 챗다. 김 참사는 무서운 중에도 이런 생각을 하고 은근히 복통하기를 마지 않았다.
그는 경상도 땅 판 돈에 보태서 전라도에다가 한 구레로 큰 농장을 작만할 게획이였다.
「전화! 전화! 이런 때에 전화를 거렀으면... 누가 잠을 깨지 안나?」
김 참사는 심중으로 초조하기 마지 않았다. 그러나 집안은 안 팟으로 죽은 듯이 고요하지 않은가!
「목숨보다 돈이 앗가우냐? 그래도 못 열겟늬?!」
괴한은 방아쇠를 제치랴한다.
「1분, 2분! 3분... 삼 분만에 열지 않으면 쏘아 버린다.」
김 참사는 할 수 없이 벽장문에 잠긴, 큰 잠을쇠를 열고 나서 금고문을 여러 제첫다.
「거기 있는 것을 죄다 끄내 노아라!」
김 참사는 식히는 대로 하였다. 괴한은 권총을 여전이 견우고 서서 그의 행동을 감시하였다. 김 참사는 돈을 끄내여 다가 방바닥에 놓을 때마다 어뜬 기회를 엿보랴고 곁눈질을 해보았으나 괴한은 도모지 그런 여가를 주지 않었다.
무기도 진짜 갓다.
밤사람은 김 참사가 끄내다 놓은 여러 가지 문권 속에서 십 원권, 백 원권의 지폐 뭉치만 외투 주머니 속에 양복 바지 봉창 ＜257＞ 속에 드러갈 수 있는 대로 집어넣고 남는 것은 그  동안에 윗 목으로 보낸 김 참사를 다시 오래서 신문지로 싸노라고 명하였다. 김 참사는 마치 그의 하인처럼 명령에 복종하였다. 그는 이제까지 남을 부리기만 하다가 이런 꼴을 평생 처음 당하고 보니 대금을 뺏기는 분함보다도 창피한 생각이 더욱 낫다.
괴한은 김 참사가 묵거논 지전 뭉치를 마저들고 이러서자 그를 다시 윗 목으로 보낸 후에 금고 안을 일일히 검사해 보고는
「고맙소. 이담에 또 맛납시다. 내가 단여 갓다고 지금 곳 고발하시요」
하고 태연 잔약히 미다지를 열고 나간다. 그는 「지까다비」를 신었다. 마루를 내려서는 기척이 나자마자 일변 대문을 여는 삐드득 - 소리를 남기고 괴한은 어둠 속으로 사라저 버렸다.
김 참사는 어안이 벙벙하니 한동안 우두커니 앉어서 그가 나가든 중문을 바라보았다.
순간! 그는 딴 정신이 펄석 나자, 버선발로 골방에 가설한 전화실로 뛰여 갓다.

「도적이야! 강도 낫다! 영국아!」

영국이는 잠결에 김 참사의 목소리를 듯고 벌덕 이러나서 눈을 비비며 큰 사랑방으로 뛰여 갓다. 중문이 열니고 사랑방문도 활작 열어 제첫는데 주인 영감은 마루로 나와서 황소숨을 쉬고 섯다.

영국이는 맨 나중의 자기 일홈을 부르는 소리에 놀나 깼기 때문에 강도가 든 줄은 모르고 있었다.
「소인 불너겝시요!」 ＜258＞

김 참사는 정작 자기 방에 드러오는 도적을 모르고 잠이 드렀으면서도 애꾸진 하인에게만 큰 소리로 책망한다.

「지금! 얘... 얼는 큰 서방님 깨워라! 그리고 저... 느늣쇠 에비를 깨워서 빨니 인력거를! 그리고 안에서는 모다 죽었단 말이냐? 모두들 깨워라! 아 - 니 집 안에 큰일이 났어도 안 팟없이 잠들만 잔단 말이냐 응」

김 참사의 인력거 뒤에는 영국이 외에도 행낭 사람 두 서넜이 앞 뒤로 따러 서서 두패 잡이로 풍우같이 모라갓다.

그 이튼난 S군 일경은 발칵 되집혀서 별안간 난리가 난 것 같다.
경찰서는 각 면 주재서원까지 비상 소집을 식히고 서장 이하로 - 수 십명이 총출동을 하였다.
경관대는 보기만 해도 무시무시하게 일제히 모자끈을 내리고 각반을 치고 군도를 절컥거리며 거리 거리의 요소마다 물샐틈 없이 경비를 하고 있었다.

누구나 이 한 마듸만 드러도 무시 무시한데 더구나 권총 강도는 어데서 알엇는지 이 고을에서 다시 없는 세력가요＜260＞ 대지주인 김 참사집을 습격하야 현금 이만 여원을 강탈 도주했다는 것은 근래에 드문 중대 사건이였다. 시국이 시국이니 만콤 관민간에 놀나움은 여간 크지 않었다. 부자들은 마음놓고 밤잠을 자지 못할 만콤 그들의 마음은 송구하였다.

그들은 김 참사에게 범행 장소의 범적(犯跡)을 그대로 가만 두어달라고 부탁한 뒤에 일변 서원을 비상 소집을 식혀 가지고 수색 방침을 밀의하였다. 총본부를 정거장 파출소로 정하고 서장 이하 전원이 각 방면으로 총출동을 시작한 것이였다. 동시에 경비 전화로 전선에 수배하고 특히 린군 각군에서는 밀접한 련락을 취하게 하야 범인 체포에 전릭을 경주하게 하였다.
그러나 그 날밤으로 범인을 체포하지는 못하였다. 경관은 김 참사집을 직히고 있었다.
이튿날 식전에 고등게 주임은 형사대를 인솔하고 범행 현장을 림검하러 왔다.
