Alas, Nanjing Has Fallen
						Soong Mei-ling (via Cheollisa)

China's (支那) self-proclaimed Joan of Arc (짠다크), Soong Mei-ling. As soon as the Incident [referring to the Second Sino-Japanese War] broke out, she volunteered for the key post of Aviation Committee Chairman, literally rushing east and west, sometimes pounding the table at the Supreme Military Council meetings emphasizing long-term resistance, sometimes flying to the front lines to command the Direct Line Army (直系軍), becoming one arm of Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) and mobilizing a large force of over two hundred thousand soldiers. The "leader in the shadows," Soong Mei-ling, also became the "commander" of China's secret chamber, using crafty radio waves for skillful counter-propaganda slandering Japan, drawing sympathy from European and American nations (歐米), deceiving the people, and continuing a fruitless resistance.

This piece is a hard-to-obtain manuscript she gave to a reporter from the American magazine "Forum" (米誌 「포 - 람」) one day shortly before escaping Nanjing, penned while boldly gazing upon the state of the capital, Nanjing, being mercilessly destroyed by the fierce bombing of our [Japanese] navy planes from her secluded official residence at the Central Military Academy within the city walls. In its every word and phrase, we feel we can hear the groans from the death throes (斷末魔) of the collapsing Nationalist government and see the frowning face of Soong Mei-ling, anguished by defeat. (Reporter) Below is the manuscript:

The air raid siren has already been sounding for 15 minutes. However, it seems Japanese planes haven't appeared over Nanjing yet. Looking out the window, the sounds of commotion and hubbub from the streets, which I now regard as commonplace, seem to be the clamor of refugees – a roaring sound like a rushing tide reaches my ears. And the soldiers guarding the official residence shout, "Air raid!" "Japanese planes!" and run off to their respective posts. While waiting for the Japanese air raid, I shall write. It has already been three months since the Japanese army began its attack on Nanjing. The damage our side has suffered during this time is beyond words. Foreign military advisors greatly <102> lament that our army's armament is insufficient, praising the Japanese army's effective air strikes and accurate shelling as something unseen even in the Spanish Civil War (西班牙戰爭) or the Great European War (歐洲 大戰), while also commenting that our army endures hardships that are humanly unbearable. During the World War, air force bases were several hundred *ri* from the front lines, so even if they wanted to hinder the defending enemy, bombers could barely make about two round trips to the front lines per day. However, while Nanjing's defending air force is so meager it might as well not exist, the Japanese army possesses a superior air force, the pride of the world, and only needs to fly about 5 *ri* to load bombs for air raids on our front lines and cities. Therefore, Japanese bombers fly strangely high, beyond the range of our weak anti-aircraft fire, dropping countless bombs as they please before leaving.

Foreigners unfamiliar with the situation might wonder why we couldn't muster an air force to counter the over 400 Japanese planes that seized air superiority over greater Shanghai (上海). But in truth, the Chinese air force is barely five years old since its inception, inexperienced in using new weapons, with most of the time spent on research. For that reason, the Nationalist government has been ordering large quantities of aircraft from America since the Incident began, eagerly awaiting, as if each day were three autumns (一日如三秋), the arrival of superior American-made aircraft and well-trained, highly skilled pilots. What do we expect from Japan? That is clear. Even looking at it more pessimistically, America... "Ah, air raid!"

"Jimmy" (지미-), who was assigned lookout duty because his eyes are said to be as sharp as a hawk's, shouted. Jimmy's eyes, able to spot the enemy bombers making a tremendous roar as they fly high, are now extraordinary.

Japanese heavy bombers visible!
"Three planes! Six planes! Ah, nine planes!"
Jimmy looked at me, his eyes wide. The time was 2:42 PM.

In the clear sky, clouds were gathered, and above them, here and there, were more clustered clouds. Three bombers appeared from within those clustered clouds, heading south. Another three appeared. With a "thump, thump" sound that resonated deep in the stomach, black <103> smoke suddenly enveloped the first three planes. The anticipated anti-aircraft barrage (花蓋) had begun. Smoke billowed around the following three planes as well. Ah, another three appeared. Just as Jimmy said, nine in total. The sound of anti-aircraft guns firing from here and there is deafening.

Near the army airfield, the target of the enemy planes, the fire spat by our anti-aircraft guns is visible. Ah – fighter planes have appeared. They seem to have been hiding in the clouds until now. The sound of machine guns can be heard as they seem to have begun combat with the Japanese planes. The nine Japanese planes are flying leisurely over Nanjing city. They do not change course, intending to bomb their target.

The leading three have already reached the southern city wall. 2:26 PM – a flash that pierces the eyes! A huge mass of earth, smoke, and debris shoots high into the air. Finally, several bombs have been dropped.

Our fighter planes' full-scale attack has begun. 2:50 PM – a dogfight continues in the western sky. One enemy plane, pressed hard by our "Curtiss Hawk" (카 - 지스폭) fighter, dives steeply and disappears beyond Purple Mountain (紫金山). The lead bombers, the first three that dropped bombs, have finished their mission and their silhouettes disappear as they head towards their base in Shanghai. The remaining five, their formation disrupted by the pursuing group of our fighters, are still circling in the clouds towards the south to confirm the location of their target. Each time they turn, their silver wings glint.

2:52 PM – Thick smoke and flames rise in the city's southwestern area. Ah – ah, more bombs have fallen!

2:55 PM – Our Curtiss Hawk fighters pursue, the sound of their machine guns echoing in the northern sky, while the enemy bombers seem to have already dropped their black eggs (黑卵) [bombs] on the airfield.

2:56 PM – More bombs were dropped near the airfield. In the western sky and over the city center, another thrilling air battle unfolds.

Our Curtiss Hawks or the Japanese bombers chase each other, repeating maneuvers like turns, rolls (橫轉), diving steeply only to ascend in an instant, ascending only to suddenly change shape, the sound of them firing at each other audible. One of our Curtiss Hawks <104>, looking as if it was hit, skillfully evaded by turning its left fuselage, but in an instant, black smoke spewed from its engine section, turning into an orange fireball and falling like a meteor into the most densely populated area near the south gate. Looking at the Japanese planes, they are hatefully circling, still in attack formation.

2:58 PM – The Japanese planes began bombing the city center. It's admirable, even if they are the enemy, how they skillfully avoid foreign legations and meticulously destroy only our military aircraft. Black smoke and crimson flames rise from two or three locations. It seems fires have started.

Our Curtiss Hawks, flashing the Blue Sky with a White Sun emblem (靑天白日의 「막」), sped off towards another battle point. Air combat continues incessantly in the northeast-northwest sky.

3:10 PM – One of our Curtiss Hawks dives steeply with a roar. Three enemy planes appeared from the clouds and gave chase, but its speed was faster, and it disappeared from view along with one Japanese heavy bomber (重爆).

3:17 PM – The Japanese bombers are already gone, only the fading sound of their engines can be heard. Black smoke still rises from the crash site of the Curtiss Hawk. Whose father, whose lover, I pray for the soul of the brave pilot who became a sacrifice for the defense of the motherland with eyes closed.

3:20 PM – The roar of the enemy planes that swept through Nanjing's sky and terrified the people has faded. The air raid lasted about 40 minutes.

3:30 PM – With the all-clear siren for the air raid warning, people who had hidden in basements come out one by one, relieved. Jimmy seems surprised to hear that I had remained alone, watching from the most dangerous second floor. I am now hardened to air raids and not that frightened. However, to change my mood, I sprayed my favorite Coty (코티) perfume generously on my chest with an atomizer and, as always, hurried the driver, who was still trembling from the earlier air raid, to drive the car to inspect the damage sites and investigate our and their losses. Crowds, pale-faced from the severe air raid, throng the streets. Thus, the car I was in was stopped several times.

Having received the baptism of black eggs (黑卵), the ammunition depot, barracks, hangars, etc., are still burning with unpleasant sounds. The damage investigation official and <105> policeman accompanying me say the extent of the damage cannot be known without clearing the destroyed areas, but the losses this time seem considerable. After circling the bombed areas once, I arrived at the crash site of the Curtiss Hawk. I was unexpectedly impressed to see the people who witnessed the fall of that fiery monster, those who suffered from the resulting fire, even women and children, watching with surprisingly calm expressions.

Several firefighters with hoses (호 - 스) and buckets (드레박) are busy extinguishing the fire. After a while, the fire was put out, and I entered the house where the plane had fallen, pushing through the billowing white smoke. Someone who looked like the fire chief pointed and said, "That's it," and what I saw was a惨담한 (brutal/pitiful) sight beyond description. What seemed to be barely a part of the engine (엔진) and propeller (푸로페라) was buried in the earth, only half visible, making it hard to believe this was the fighter plane that had been chasing the enemy heavy bomber an hour ago. Someone pointed out that the crushed head of the pilot could be seen beneath the dirt-covered engine, but I had no desire to look. I found out after returning to headquarters that three other Curtiss Hawk planes of ours were shot down, while two enemy planes were shot down. Furthermore, it was reported that one enemy plane crashed due to our anti-aircraft fire before reaching the skies over Nanjing. The report that day stated that a total of 9 enemy aircraft – 6 twin-engine heavy bombers (發動機2基 장비의 敵 重爆機) and 3 fighter planes – dropped over 50 bombs over Nanjing, with 7 returning. Our losses were 4 planes shot down, 2 crash-landed, and about 10 casualties including pilots and gunners.

The framed picture of Sun Wen (孫文) hanging on the wall had fallen onto the blanket at some point, the glass shattered into pieces. I immediately replaced it with another frame, thinking Zhongzheng (中正) [Chiang Kai-shek's given name] would be displeased if he returned, but somehow it felt like an ominous ** [text missing/censored? - likely 'omen' or 'sign']. Outside, dusk is falling. Let me continue writing what was interrupted by the Japanese air raid.

From the most pessimistic viewpoint, the fact that America might prohibit arms exports to China (支那) and refuse to issue passports to prevent American advisors from coming to China is an unexpected development.

Tonight, the Japanese air force will likely rain black eggs (黑卵) on our heads again. Poor Nanjing's defenses cannot completely prevent this. As has already been partly reported in foreign newspapers, it is true that a momentous decision to relocate the capital (遷都) has been made among the government leadership. Will the place we retreat to be Hankou (漢口)? Or Chongqing (重慶)? Anyway, Nanjing, farewell. <106>

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean translation of what is claimed to be a first-hand account by Soong Mei-ling, wife of Chiang Kai-shek, describing a Japanese air raid on Nanjing in late 1937, just before the city fell. Prefaced by a pro-Japanese Samcheolli magazine reporter who frames it as evidence of China's collapse, the main text details the bombing's intensity, the inadequacy of Chinese defenses, the desperate aerial combat, pilot casualties, and the foreshadowing of the capital's relocation to Hankou or Chongqing. For colonial Korean history, this piece is interesting as it shows a major Korean magazine publishing content related to the Sino-Japanese War. The reporter's preface clearly reflects the colonial government's pro-Japanese stance, using derogatory terms for China ("支那") and framing Soong Mei-ling's account as the "death throes" of the enemy. Publishing Soong's dramatic, albeit ultimately pessimistic, account might have served multiple purposes: demonstrating Japanese military prowess, highlighting Chinese weakness and suffering (thus justifying Japanese actions), and potentially showing Korean readers the grim realities of war while subtly reinforcing the idea of Japanese dominance in East Asia. Historians might find the juxtaposition of the pro-Japanese preface and the translated enemy perspective revealing about wartime propaganda and information control in colonial Korea.

鳴呼 南京 陷落
						宋美齡천리사
支那의 자칭 짠다크 宋美齡. 사변이 발발하자 자진 항공위원장의 요직으로 문자 그대로 동분서주 혹은 최고군사위원회 석상에서 테 - 불을 두드리며 장기 항전을 강조하며 또는 제일선에 비행기로 直系軍을 지휘하는 등 蔣介石의 한 팔이 되며 이백 수 십만의 大兵을 동원케 하는 「그늘의 지도자」 宋美齡은 또한 괴전파로 일본 비방의 교묘한 역선전으로 歐米 제국의 동정을 끄을고 국민을 기만하며 무익한 항전을 계속케 한 支那의 기밀실의 「사령관」도 되였다.
此 一篇은 彼女가 남경 탈출 직전에 엇던 날 대담하게도 성내 중앙군관학교의 깊숙한 관저에서 과감한 我 해군기 맹폭격에 무참이도 파괴되는 수도 남경이 모양을 응시사면서 펜을 들어 米誌 「포 - 람」의 기자에 준 엇기 어려운 수기로 우리는 그 一言 一句 속에 멸망하는 국민정부 斷末魔의 신음 소리를 듯고 패전의 고민하는 宋美齡의 찌프린 얼골을 보는 것 같이 생각된다. (기자) 이하 수기

벌서 15분 전부터 공습 경보가 들녀온다. 그러나 아직 일본 비행기는 남경에 나타나지 않은 듯 창을 열고 보니 인젠 예사로 생각하는 거리나 떠들고 법석하는 소리는 피난인의 떠드는 소리인 듯 와 - 하고 조수 밀니는 소리가 귀에 들녀온다. 그리고 관저 경비의 병사들은 「공습」 「일본기다」라고 웨치며 각기 부서의 나가느라고 다름질친다. 일본기의 공습을 기두르는 동안 나는 쓰자. 일본군이 남경 공격을 개시하여 벌서 3개월이 된다. 그 동안 我方에 받은 피해는 말할 수 없다. 외국인 군사 고문은 아군의 무장이 불충분하다고 대단＜102＞ 통탄하며 일본군의 효과적의 공습과 견양이 정확한 포격을 賞讚하기를 西班牙전쟁 歐洲 대전에서도 못 본 사실이라고 하며 또는 아군이 인력으로서는 참을 수 없는 고난을 참는다고 비평한다. 세계 대전 때에는 공군 근거지는 전선으로 몇 백리 되는 곳에 있었음으로 방비하는 적군에게 방해코저 하나 폭격기는 1일 2회 가량 겨우 전선을 왕복할 정도였으나 남경 아군은 방비 공군이 전연 없다고 해도 좋을 빈약한데 대하여 일본군은 세계의 자랑하는 우수한 공군을 가젔으며 我 전선 공습 及 도시 공습에는 폭탄 적재키 위하여 한 5리 비행하면 된다. 그러므로 일본 폭격기는 아군의 微力한 고사포의 사격단 외에 묘하게 비상하며 제 생각대로 무수한 폭탄을 떠러터리고 간다.
사정 모르는 외국인들은 대 상해의 제공권을 잡은 400여 대의 일본 비행기에 대항할 공군을가지지 못하였을가 하고 의문시할 것이나 사실 支那 공군은 아직 탄생 이래 겨우 5년으로 신무기 사용의 무경험으로 대부분은 연구에 시일이 들었다. 그런 이유로 국민 정부는 사변 이래 미국에 비행기 대량 주문하게 되고 일각이라도 빨니 우수한 미국제 비행기와 훈련한 기술 우수한 비행사의 도착을 一日如三秋로 고대하였다. 우리가 일본의 기대하는 것은 무었? 그것은 명료하다. 더 비관적으로 보아도 미국이 「앗, 공습이다!」
매같이 눈이 밝다고 위정다려다 망을 보게 한 「지미-」가 웨첫다. 대단한 폭음을 내며 비상하는 적의 폭격기를 알어내는 지미-의 눈은 인젠 보통은 아니다.
일본의 대형 폭격기 보인다!
「3대 6대 아 9대다!」
