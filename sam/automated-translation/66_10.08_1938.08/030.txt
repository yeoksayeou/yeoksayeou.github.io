**Body Text Translation:**
The Movement for Korean Suffrage and the Response of the Imperial Diet

Excerpt from the Proceedings of the House of Representatives Petition Committee Chairman: Regarding the matter of implementing the House of Representatives election law in Chosŏn, I request Representative Pak Ch'un-gŭm (朴春琴), the introducing member, to take the floor.
Representative Pak Ch'un-gŭm (朴春琴): This petition concerns the implementation of the House of Representatives election law in Chosŏn. Since Taishō 9 (1920), petitions have been submitted to the Prime Minister 16 times. Concurrently, since Taishō 9, petitions have been submitted 8 times, and among those, it has been adopted by the Petitions Committee 4 times. As you know, from a continental perspective, we hope this petition is implemented swiftly. Seeing how all 23 million people of the peninsula are greatly rejoicing since the establishment of the volunteer soldier system, premised on granting the duty of military service, I keenly feel that Japan's national policy must henceforth involve the outer territories (外地). Furthermore, considering the relationship with North China (北支) since the Manchurian Incident, I believe it is national policy to grant suffrage to Chosŏn, fundamentally uniting Naichi (內地 - mainland Japan) and Chosŏn (內鮮), and striving together for the nation. Generally, every cabinet until now has spoken of national unity (擧國一致). However, when considering if the politics of even a national unity cabinet are truly unified, I acknowledge there are some shortcomings.

As you know, it has been 28 years since the Japan-Korea annexation until today. Regarding the suffrage movement, as you know, since Taishō 9, Min Wŏn-sik (閔元植), the chairman of the National Association (國民協會), Chosŏn's sole pro-Japanese party, <40> came to Naichi for this movement and was assassinated by malicious elements, namely anti-Japanese elements, while staying at the Station Hotel. Although the National Association has continued to exist until today, this petition is different from previous ones. Until now, Naichi residents living on the peninsula did not approve of this petition, but today, even Naichi residents living on the peninsula express great approval, suggesting submitting this petition through Naichi-Chosŏn unity. Previously, the National Association submitted petitions with 3,000 or 4,000 joint signatures, but this time, representatives from each province of Chosŏn, the provincial council members – one Naichi resident and one Chosŏn person each, totaling 26 representatives for all of Chosŏn – submitted this petition. This can be said to represent the 23 million people of the peninsula. Kim Myŏng-jun (金明濬) and 28 others from the National Association who submitted this petition can be considered such people. Since the Japan-Korea annexation, when thinking of Emperor Meiji's words of "Impartial Benevolence" (一視同仁), I thought that politics of impartial benevolence must surely be extended to Chosŏn, but I believe there were many things said but not done. This is because, in Taishō 9, the ideology in Chosŏn was not favorable, and also considering culture and various other points, I think it could not be realized immediately at that time. Even now, I am not asking for the House of Representatives election law to be implemented exactly the same as in Naichi; I think we should ask for what is feasible. Only then can true national unity be achieved, and it would also be good from Japan's continental perspective. That is, Chosŏn consists of 13 provinces. If, for example, each province sends one or perhaps two representatives to the Imperial Diet, I think the majority of them might turn out to be Naichi residents. If the qualification is paying 5 yen or more in national tax, similar to Japan's old election law, Naichi residents pay more tax, so naturally, I think the majority would be Naichi residents. If that happens, having those people attend the Imperial Diet and reflect the situation of the outer territories in the Diet – (this is not to say everyone is like that, but because some people might be) – making them aware of the conditions in Chosŏn would be very beneficial from a national perspective. Furthermore, as mentioned earlier, the volunteer soldier system was recently established as a premise for granting military service duty, and since then, the 23 million people of the peninsula, as people behind the lines (銃後人), have actively participated in national defense donations, and their patriotism today is extraordinary. Even the Army authorities, the Chosŏn Army Commander, <41> when I visited recently, mentioned that the sentiment on the peninsula has greatly improved. I firmly believe that people born on the peninsula might even have a stronger "Yamato Spirit" (大和魂 - Japanese spirit) than the Japanese people. He even requested me, "Mr. Pak, when you return to Naichi, please tell those who lack understanding about Chosŏn the actual state of its governance." Until now, government committee members have always said it is still premature. However, looking at the recent situation, it is needless to say that patriotism is flourishing on the peninsula as mentioned before. Moreover, I believe the primary way to quickly cultivate the Japanese spirit in Japanese people born in Chosŏn is precisely to grant them the right to vote in House of Representatives elections. At the same time, there must be great inconvenience for Naichi residents as well. For example, one has suffrage up to Shimonoseki, but upon crossing to Pusan, which is also Japanese territory, that suffrage disappears, which Naichi residents also find inconvenient. In reality, even though many Naichi residents live in Chosŏn, they feel like they are living with dual nationality. From this point of view, and also considering the future relationship with Manchuria and North China, I think it is inevitable that something like what I just described will happen regarding the 23 million Japanese [subjects] on the peninsula. This is not just a matter of numbers, but a matter of sentiment (氣分). It is the duty of all Japanese people to overcome this emergency period for Japan with good sentiment and true Naichi-Chosŏn unity. I hope you will listen to the government's sincere reply on these points and kindly adopt [the petition].

Committee Member Itagaki (坂東): Let us hear the opinion of the government representative.
Government Representative Itō (伊禮): The fundamental policy of Chosŏn governance, as Mr. Pak just stated, is Impartial Benevolence (一視同仁). Successive Governors-General, based on this great principle of Impartial Benevolence, have strived to advance culture, promote the people's welfare, and realize the harmonious fusion of Naichi and Chosŏn (內鮮渾融). Therefore, implementing the House of Representatives election law in Chosŏn and granting suffrage to the peninsula is, of course, in line with the fundamental principle of this governance, and there is no disagreement with the spirit of this petition as an ideal. Furthermore, regarding the display of patriotic fervor by the residents of the peninsula during the current Incident, <42> it is truly a joyous matter.

However, although successive Governors-General have endeavored in cultural facilities, industry, and various other facilities, achieving remarkable results, upon examining the specific circumstances in various areas, we see that there is still a considerable gap and difference between Naichi and Chosŏn.
For example, regarding the local government system, as you know, a general system of self-government has not yet been proclaimed. There are differences in the system, such as partially indirect elections or restricted elections. There are also significant differences in the prevalence of education. Currently, the school enrollment rate among school-age children barely reaches 31%. Consequently, the percentage of residents who understand the national language [Japanese] is merely 10%. Not only are there institutional issues, but there are also points requiring thorough study regarding the practical implementation of this law. Furthermore, regarding the implementation of such an important law as this one, sufficient preparation must be made to ensure there are no errors or miscalculations after implementation. The government believes that the conditions for implementing this law are not yet met at present.

Representative Pak: I would like to hear the opinion of the government representative from the Ministry of Home Affairs (內務省). Isn't suffrage generally related to the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務省)?
Government Representative Kimura (木村): As the representative from the Ministry of Colonial Affairs, Mr. Itō (伊禮), just stated, the Ministry of Home Affairs generally thinks the same. The question of which laws implemented in Naichi should be implemented in Chosŏn, and to what extent, is, as Mr. Pak explained, a matter that must be decided by looking at the level of culture and the actual conditions of the people's lives in various aspects.
Furthermore, while we cannot but offer congratulations for the fact that the people of the peninsula have awakened to their duty as subjects of the Empire and devoted their utmost sincerity (赤誠) to the home front during the current China Incident, the question of whether the purpose of implementing critical laws like the Conscription Law or the House of Representatives Election Law in Chosŏn, just as in Naichi, would truly be achieved at such a time is a matter requiring sufficient consideration. As written in this petition, we believe that election laws or conscription laws should only be implemented after confirming that there would be no regrets in achieving the goal of strengthening national unity and solidarity between Naichi and Chosŏn. Under the current circumstances, <43> the House of Representatives Election Law has not yet been implemented even in Karafuto (樺太 - Sakhalin) or Taiwan (臺灣). Regarding implementing the election law in these regions as well, I believe no one would disagree that each place will eventually reach the time for implementation.
Therefore, the Ministry of Home Affairs currently has no intention of implementing the House of Representatives Election Law in Chosŏn under the present circumstances. We believe this is an issue where a decision should be made only after carefully observing future developments, thoroughly studying whether the outcome of implementation would truly achieve the objective stated in this petition, and confirming that there would be no regrets.

Representative Pak: Since this is a difference of opinion between us, I will not pursue it forcefully. I only wish to say, thanks to all of you, I am fortunate to occupy a seat in the House of Representatives here in Naichi Japan, but I am not asking for the election law to be implemented in Chosŏn exactly the same way as in Naichi. Nor is it a matter of numbers. For example, like the recent volunteer soldier system, it was only 400 people out of 23 million. The fact that the 23 million people of Chosŏn rejoiced in their hearts over those 400 shows, I believe, Japan's grand national policy. Therefore, what I am suggesting is, among the 23 million, there are 13 provinces, so I am talking about 13 representatives. The number is small, but its influence would be great. We casually say that Manchuria is a neighbor, or that North China is too. For instance, those who obstruct Japan or spread malicious propaganda against Japan often point to matters concerning the peninsula. They talk about what Japan is doing to Chosŏn after the Japan-Korea annexation. If this [suffrage] is implemented, even with a small number, I believe it would be very beneficial from a national perspective, somehow for Japan's national policy. Another point I particularly want to address to the Home Affairs government representative is that I believe the Ministry of Home Affairs has a significant misunderstanding. When people from the peninsula come to Naichi, if they don't have a travel permit (旅行證明), they are forcibly prevented from crossing at Pusan. There is much discontent about this. However, those people are the same subjects [of the Empire], so why restrict travel within the same country? I receive criticism about this every time I go to Chosŏn. Whenever there is an opportunity, I intend to raise this matter, but considering the general situation in Naichi, there are many things <44> that Chosŏn people say which we cannot fully convey to you all in the Diet. However, regarding those born in Chosŏn who now reside in Naichi, numbering 700,000, traditionally, according to Chosŏn custom, they visit Chosŏn at the year-end or Ch'usŏk (Autumn Eve festival). They would go, spend 3-4 months with their parents, and then return to Naichi. But now, if they go to Chosŏn, they cannot come back to Naichi a second time, so they absolutely do not leave Naichi. If this [restriction] were abolished for people coming from Chosŏn, perhaps 5,000 or 10,000 more might come initially, but if things don't work out as they wish after coming to Naichi, they will return voluntarily. I think the deterrent effect (師傳 - perhaps misprint for 示傳 or similar, meaning lesson/example?) of that would be significant. Because this is prohibited, those who would go cannot, and calculating that, I think the number of those staying in Naichi has increased. Regarding this matter too, speaking from the spirit of Naichi-Chosŏn unity, I truly think it is a contradiction that subjects within the same country cannot travel to Japan without a travel permit. From the perspective of the Japanese people issue (日本人問題 - population issues?), I do not hope that many people from over there come here to live. However, because of merely 5,000 or 10,000 people, I see a very large ideological backlash against the Japanese national polity (國體). However, I think the Home Affairs authorities need to study this carefully. If you ask me frankly which is better, I think it should be abolished. The Government-General of Chosŏn also finds this difficult. The Ministry of Home Affairs says they shouldn't be sent, blocking them... Now, regarding the House of Representatives Election Law, I am not proposing it be submitted to the Diet immediately, but since it has already been adopted multiple times, I firmly believe that collaborating with the Ministry of Home Affairs and the Ministry of Colonial Affairs and implementing it, even with a small number, would be for the good of the country. I hope you will study this well in the future.

Committee Member Higashi (東): I believe there are many points worth acknowledging in the explanation by the introducing member, Mr. Pak. I hope the government will also study this sufficiently so that this request can be realized even one day sooner. In that sense, I hope this proposal will be adopted.
Chairman Shida (信太): As chairman, I too, as you know, this proposal was adopted in the 64th and 70th Diets. As Mr. Pak just stated, I believe its realization is a fundamental policy that will become a great foundation for Naichi-Chosŏn harmony. <45> Therefore, I sincerely hope that Chosŏn strengthens all these aspects and thus reaches the opportune moment for implementing the election law proposed herein. If there are no objections, shall we decide to adopt it? (Someone says, "No objection.")
Chairman Shida (信太): Then, it is decided to adopt [the petition].

Baron Sakatani and the Minister of Colonial Affairs Q&A - Discussion in the House of Peers Plenary Session -

Baron Sakatani Yoshirō (阪谷芳郞): I would like to say a word to express my respect and gratitude to our brethren on the Korean peninsula. I would be very grateful if the Minister of Colonial Affairs (拓務大臣) would answer my question.
It seems the situation in Chosŏn has greatly progressed in recent years, and it is gratifying that manufacturing industries, agriculture, commerce, industry, and various other fields are developing significantly. I believe this is entirely the benefit of the Japan-Korea annexation (日韓合邦). This Japan-Korea annexation is undoubtedly a fortunate event for both Naichi and Chosŏn, and I believe it is something that can maintain peace in East Asia and, further, world peace.
What I especially want to mention on this occasion is that since the Sino-Japanese Incident (日支事變), the patriotic fervor displayed by our brethren on the Korean peninsula, united as one nation, cannot but move us to tears.
Also, not long ago, the volunteer soldier system was implemented, and not only did many applicants participate, but also, before long, the education system is said to become indistinguishable between Naichi and Chosŏn. This is also a very good thing. Is this not something arising from the unity of Naichi and Chosŏn? I earnestly request the Minister in charge to speak about the actual situation. (Remainder omitted in original)
Minister of Colonial Affairs Ōtani (大谷): I will answer Baron Sakatani's question. Since the Japan-Korea annexation, the Chosŏn brethren, embracing the spirit of Impartial Benevolence (一視同仁), have seen successive Governors-General achieve administrative successes. Various industries, commerce, agriculture, and everything else have greatly developed, and at the same time, the Chosŏn people's self-awareness as Japanese subjects has deepened considerably. Triggered by the Manchurian Incident, this awakening became significant, and <46> after the outbreak of this [current China] Incident, their understanding of the times has grown further. With pride and fervor as subjects of the Empire, they have demonstrated complete national unity through national defense donations, procurement of military supplies, sending off soldiers departing for the front, comforting their families, and various other forms of cooperation on the home front, fully displaying the spirit of national unity and showing the fruition of Naichi-Chosŏn harmony without reservation, which fills us with indescribable emotion. This is indeed a joyous matter for the Empire and a delightful matter for the peace of East Asia. Not only do we deeply appreciate their sincerity, but the government also understands that we must give much thought to this point.
And regarding the volunteer soldier system you asked about, observing the current situation in Chosŏn, we intend to implement it shortly. Preparations are currently underway steadily, and the unity of Naichi and Chosŏn is clearly materializing.
The same applies to the issue of reforming the education system. We are currently busy with preparations. At a time when the situation is so turbulent, we truly expect the loyal hearts of the Chosŏn brethren, as subjects of the Empire (皇國), to contribute to the prosperity of the nation through united cooperation.
Baron Sakatani Yoshirō (阪谷芳郞): I deeply appreciate receiving a clear answer from the Minister in charge. Originally, being a part of the Japanese Empire, the Korean peninsula's actions might seem natural. However, considering that not even half a century has passed since the annexation, and yet, in this Incident, their patriotic sincerity and passion are in no way inferior, and perhaps even greater, compared to Naichi residents who possess a 2,600-year history, I cannot help but feel profound respect and gratitude towards our Chosŏn brethren. I am confident that all gentlemen present here share this sentiment.
I request that the Minister in charge strive diligently with policies for sufficient progress and improvement regarding the administration of the Korean peninsula henceforth. And finally, one more word: I believe the time is not far off when the Minister in charge must seriously consider the issue of suffrage on the Korean peninsula. Please be aware of this. <47>

**Summary**

This article from August 1938 presents excerpts from discussions in the Japanese Imperial Diet regarding granting suffrage (the right to elect representatives to the Diet) to Koreans. Pro-Japanese Korean Diet member Pak Ch'un-gŭm argues strongly for limited representation (e.g., one representative per province) citing the principle of "Naissen Itchi" (Japan-Korea Unity), the loyalty shown by Koreans through the new volunteer soldier system and war contributions, and the need to improve Japanese understanding of Korea. Government officials (from the Ministry of Colonial Affairs and Ministry of Home Affairs) counter that it is "premature," pointing to perceived developmental gaps in education, Japanese language proficiency, and local autonomy in Korea, emphasizing the need for caution and further study, and noting that other colonies like Karafuto and Taiwan also lack representation. Baron Sakatani Yoshirō, in the House of Peers, echoes the sentiment of gratitude for Korean loyalty and urges the government to consider suffrage soon. The House of Representatives Petition Committee ultimately votes to adopt Pak's petition, though government opposition remains clear.

The article is historically significant as it captures the complex discourse surrounding colonial assimilation policy during wartime. It highlights the gap between the official rhetoric of "Impartial Benevolence" and "Naissen Itchi" and the actual reluctance to grant political rights. It reveals the paternalistic and often discriminatory attitudes of Japanese officials towards Koreans, justifying unequal treatment based on perceived cultural and developmental inferiority. The prominent role of Pak Ch'un-gŭm exemplifies the activities of pro-Japanese Korean collaborators working within the Japanese system, while also showing his attempts to leverage Korean cooperation (like the volunteer soldier system) to argue for greater rights, albeit limited ones. The discussion on travel restrictions further exposes the discriminatory reality faced by Koreans within the Empire.

기사제목	朝鮮參政權運動과 帝國議會의 反響
기사형태	논설

朝鮮參政權運動과 帝國議會의 反響
衆議院 請願 委員會 議事 抄 위원장. 조선에 중의원의원선거법 시행의 건으로 紹介議員 朴春琴군의 등단을 바람니다.
朴春琴 위원. 이 청원은 조선에 중의원의원선거법 시행의 건이외다. 大正 9년으로부터 내각총리대신에 대하야 建白하기를 16회이외다. 동시에 大正 9년부터 청원을 제출하기는 8회로 其間 4회 청원위원회에 채택하였읍니다. 아시는 바와 같이 본 청원은 大陸見地로 기 어히 빨리 시행하기를 바라며 일체 반도 2천 3백만의 여러분은 今回 병역 의무를 부여할전제로 지원병제도가 제정이래 대단히 깃버하는 점으로 보더래도 일본의 국책은 금후로 외지가 아니면 않되리라고 절실이 늣기고 있읍니다. 더욱 만주사변 이래 今回의 北支의 관계로 보더래도 조선에 참정권을 부여하여 內鮮이 근본으로 일체가 되여 국가를 위하여진력하는 것이 국책이 않일가 생각합니다. 대체 지금까지 어느 내각이나 거국일치라고 합니다. 擧一일치 내각이라도 참으로 그 정치가 거국일치냐고 생각하는 때는 다소 결함이 있다고 인정합니다.
아시는 바와 같이 日韓병합 이래 至 금일 20有8년이 되였읍니다. 참정권운동으로 인하야아시는 바와 같이 大正 9년 조선유일의 친일당, 國民協會 회장 閔元植이＜40＞ 내지에 이 운동으로 와서 「스테이슌 호텔」에 숙박 중 불량분자 즉 排日黨분자에게 암살당한 이래, 國民協會가 금일까지 계속 존재하였지만 今回의 청원은 종래에 청원과 달라 지금까지는 반도 在住 내지인이 이 청원에 대하여 찬성치 않었으나 금일은 반도 在住 내지인도 이에 대하여 대단한 찬의를 표하며 內鮮일치로 本청원을 제출하자는 데서 이전은 3,000명, 4,000명 連署로 國民協會가 해왔으나 그보다도 조선 각도의 대표를 하고 있는 道會 의원된 분이 - 이것은 내지인 1인, 조선인 1인씩 全鮮 26명의 대표가 이번 청원낸 것이외다. 즉 이것은 반도 2천3백만의 대표라 할 수가 있는 것이오. 본청원을 하고 있는 國民協會의 金明濬 외 28명이 즉 그런 사람이라고 할 수있읍니다. 나는 日韓병합이래, 明治大帝의 一視同仁이라고 하신 말삼을 생각할 때에 반 듯이 一視同仁 정치를 조선에 해 주어야 하겠다고 생각하였으나 말코 행치 못할 일이 많었다고 생각하는 것은 大正 9년 당시는 조선의 사상이 좋지 못하였고 또는 문화 기타 여러점으로 말해도 그 당시는 곧 실현할 수 없었다고 생각합니다. 지금에도 내지와 同樣으로중의원의원선거법을 시행하여 달나는 것이 아니고 나는 될 수 있는 일을 해달나고 생각합니다. 그래야 참으로 擧國일치요, 또는 일본의 대륙적 견지로 보아도 좋으리라고 생각합니다. 즉 조선은 13도로 되여 있는데 이 13도에 대하야 예컨대 1도에 1인 혹 2인으로 하여제국의회에 대표를 보내게 되면 몰나도 其태반은 나는 내지인이 되리라고 생각합니다. 국세 5원 이상으로 일본 昔日 선거법과 같이 한다면 국세 밧치는 자는 내지인이 많으니 당연 내지인 大半 이라고 생각합니다. 그렇게 된다면 그 자람들이 제국의회에 참석하여 조선에 대한 인식을 - (이것은 일반이 그러타는 것은 아니나 혹 그런 사람 있기에) - 외지상황을 의회에 반영케 하는 것만이라도 국가적 견지로 대단 좋으리라고 생각합니다. 더 욱 최근 먼저 말한 바와 같이 병역의무를 부여 전제로 지원병제도가 제정되였으며, 그 후로 반도 2천3백만인은 銃後人으로 국방헌금도 성행하고 금일에 애국심으로 말하면 비상한것이 있읍니다. 육군 당국, 조선군 사령관께서도 요＜41＞전 내가 갓슬 때에도 반도사상이 대단 좋아젔으며 실제 반도에서 출생한 사람은 일본 민족에 「大和魂」이라는 것이 도로혀강할넌지 모는다고 나는 마음 튼튼하게 생각하고 있으니 朴군 내지에 돌아가면 조선에 대한 인식 부족한 사람에게 대하여는 조선통치의 실정을 말하여 달나구 당부까지 하시였읍니다. 지금까지는 정부위원 여러분은 아직 시기상조라고 언제나 말코 있읍니다. 그러나 최근 정세로 보와 반도에도 前述과 如히 애국심이 왕성한다든 것은 多言不要하며, 더욱 나는 조선에서 출생한 일본인에게 일본정신을 속히 함양시킬 요소는 즉 중의원의원선거권을가지게 하는 것이 나는 제일방책이라고 생각합니다. 동시에 내지인에게도 대단한 불편이있을 것이올시다. 예컨대 下關까지는 선거권이 있으나 같은 일본영토인 부산에 건너서면그 선거권이 없에 되는 것이 내지인에게 대하여서도 불편을 느끼고 있읍니다. 실제 내지인 여러분이 조선에 많이 在住하여도 이중국적으로 생활하는 기분을 가주고 있음니다. 이점으로  보아도 또 금후에 만주, 北支에 관계로 말터래도 반도 2천3백만 일본인에 대하여이제 말한 바와 같은 일이 되지 않을 일이 없다고 생각합니다. 이것은 단지 수 문제가 아니고 기분 문제이외다. 좋은 기분으로 그리고 참으로 內鮮이 일체되여 이 일본의 비상시를 돌파하려는 것이 서로서로에 일본국민에 의무라고 나는 얻습니다. 이 點點에 대한 정부에 성의있는 御답변을 들으시고 부듸 채택하여 주심을 희망한는 바이올시다.
坂東 위원. 정부위원의 의견을 들읍시다.
伊禮 정부위원. 조선통치의 근본방침은 지금 朴군에 말삼과 같이 一視同仁이올시다. 역대 총독은 이 一視同仁 大義에 의하여 혹은 문화를 진보케 하고 혹은 民福을 증진하야 內鮮이渾한 융합을 구현키에 노력하여 왔읍니다. 딸아서 조선에 중의원의원선거법을 시행하여 반도에 대하여 참정권을 付興할 것은 물론 이 통치에 근본방침으로 보아 이 청원에 있는정신은 이상으로 異論은 없읍니다. 더욱 금번 사변에 있어도 반도주민열성의 애국심의 발동에 대하여서는＜42＞ 참으로 기뿐 일이올시다.
그러나 금일까지 歷之 총독이 문화의 시설 혹은 산업 기타 各般시설에 대하여 劣力하여 비약적 효과를 보았으나 구체적으로 各般사정을 고찰하면 아직 內鮮간에게 상당한 懸隔 相異가 있다그 봅니다.
예를 들면 지방제도 같은 것에 대하여 보면 아시는 바 같이 일반적의 자치제도는 아직 선포되지 않었읍니다. 一 혹은 간접선거, 혹은 제한선거, 이와 같이 제도상에 相違도 있읍니다. 또는 교육보급에 대하여도 대단한 相違가 있읍니다. 금일 취학아동은 學齡아동에 겨우31%에 달하는 형편이올시다. 딸아서 국어를 이해하는 주민은 겨우 10%에 불과하는 현상이올시다. 제도상 관계 뿐 아니라 사실상 본법시행에 대하여는 십분 연구할 점이 있읍니다. 더욱 본법과 같은 중요법시행에 대하여는 시행 후에 과오없에 오산없도록 충분한 준비를 하지않으면 안 되리라고 믿습니다. 현재에 있어 본법 실행에 대하여 아직 구비 못되였다고 정부는 생각하는 바이올시다.
朴 위원. 내무성 정부위원 御의견을 듯고저 합니다. 천거권이라는 것은 大體 拓務省 관계 아니니까요.
木村 정부위원. 지금 伊禮 拓務 정부위원의 말삼이 있었읍니다만은 대체 내무성에서도 그렇게 생각합니다. 내지와 조선에 어떤 법률을 시행할까 즉 내지에서 시행하는 바 법률을 어느 정도에 朝鮮에 시행할가 하는 문제에 있어서는 이것은 朴군에 설명에 있었읍니다만은문화이 정도 국민各般생활의 실정을 보아서 결정치 아니면 아될 문제라고 생각합니다.
더욱 이번 支那사변에 반도민이 제국신민의 본분을 자각하려 총후의 赤誠을 다한데 대하여는 경하를 마지 안는 바이오나 이럴 때에 병역법, 혹은 중의원의원선거법과 같은 중대법률을 내지와 같이 조선에 시행한다면 참으로 시행에 목적이 달성되겠느냐고는 충분이 고려할 문제라고 생각합니다. 이 청원에 씨어있는 바와 같이 內鮮거국일치, 국민적 단결을공고케 할 수 있게 이 목적을 달성함에 유감이 없다고 확인한 후 선거법이나 병역법을 시행치 않으면 안되리라고 생각합니다. 현재 상황으로서는 樺太,＜43＞ 臺濟에도 아직 중의원의원선거법은 실시되지 않었습니다. 이런 지방에도 선거법을 시행하는 점으로 말삼하면 어듸나 이 시행시기에 도달하는 것은 누구나 異存이 업스리라고 생각합니다.
그러므로 내무성에서도 지금 형편상 조선에 중의원의원선거법을 시행하겠다는 생각은 없읍니다. 금후의 추이를 잘 보고 그 시행결과가 이 청원에 있는 바 같흔 참으로 그 목적 달성 興否를 충분 연구하여 그런 후 유감없으리라고 확인 후 비로소 그 생각을 결정할문제라고 생각함니다.
