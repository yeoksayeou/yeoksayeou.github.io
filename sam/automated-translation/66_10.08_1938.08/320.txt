1. Memorable moments, happy or sad, during location shoots.
2. Difficulties and feelings when performing love scenes.
3. Memorable masterpieces of cinema?

Mun Yebong
1. I haven't had any experiences joyful or sorrowful enough to remain particularly memorable.
2. What I dislike the most are these love scenes. Consequently, the most difficult thing is also this "love scene." It's awkward, uncomfortable, and makes my face tingle, often leading to numerous retakes ("ヤリナオシ" - yarinawoshi). I worry greatly because I just can't seem to fully become the character in these scenes.
3. For some reason, "Beongeori Samnyong" (벙어리 三龍) remains in my memory.
The late Na Un'gyu's performance as an old man in "Nimja ŏpnŭn narutpae" (님자없는 나룻배) was brilliant, I think.
"Queen Christina" (크리스틔나 女王)
And "Greta Garbo" (그레타 가르보) who appeared in it.

Kim Pokchin
1. I haven't appeared in a film yet, so how could I have gone on a "location shoot"?
2. Again, I might know if it were theater, but I have no experience with this yet.
3. Among Korean films, "Arirang" (아리랑), which I saw long ago. Na Un'gyu (羅雲奎) who starred in it.
Sin Ilsŏn (申一仙). Among Western films, "Captain Blood" (海賊 뿌랏트) starring Errol Flynn (에 로 - 루푸링), "Mazurka" (마즈르카) with Pola Negri (포라네그리), "Taras Bulba" (隊長 뿌-리바) starring Harry Baur (아리보루), "Ramona" (라모나) with Loretta Young (로렛다 영), "Romeo and Juliet" (로미오와 줄리엣) starring Norma Shearer (로마 샤라).<175>

Yi Ung
I have gratefully read your company's kind inquiry.
1. I haven't been on a location shoot yet. I plan to leave soon, and I think it will be very interesting.
2. Among the various scenes, they say the love scene is the most difficult, and indeed, it was tough. Having suffered repeated defeats, I plan to avoid them as much as possible in the future.
3. Memorable Korean films? It's quite disappointing. No good impressions remain, only unpleasant ones, isn't that a worry? As for Western films, it's not Euro-American worship, but truly, immortal masterpieces are abundant. Haven't all the literary masterpieces since the dawn of history been made into films? However, I really dislike American films. German and French works are good, and recently, Czechoslovakian works are also very excellent. Our films should also follow the German, French, and Czech style. Among those that captured my heart are "Volga Boatmen" (쁠카의 船歌), "Mazurka" (마쯔루카), "Crime and Punishment" (罪와 罰), "Sach'unjo" (思春調), "Hŭllŏgan ch'ŏtsarang" (흘너간 첬사랑), "Taras Bulba" (隊長 부리바). As for actors:
Harry Baur (아리보-ㄹ), Emil Jannings (에밀야닝스), Buster Keaton (키-통), Pola Negri (포라네그리), Françoise Rosay (후란노아로-제), Mártha Eggerth (마르다에겔트).

Kim Ch'igŭn
1. It was during the location shoot for "Aeryŏnsong" (愛戀頌) in P'yŏngyang this time. On Nŭngnado Island (綾羅島), under the director's guidance for the first time, I was creating a "new world." After Mun Yebong-ssi and I finished a period of intense acting, the entire Nŭngnado area was, before we knew it, filled with countless onlookers, yet it was so quiet you couldn't even hear a single breath. This was more joyful than anything else. I realized anew that this must be a joy only actors can feel. There weren't particularly sorrowful moments, but when I thought, "What if we disappoint those who were so engrossed in our acting, even if just for a moment?", <176> that was truly distressing.
2. Emotion, unlike a product, cannot be counterfeited. Thus, no matter how much of an actor one is, this (professional love affair) that does not spring from the heart is truly difficult. Therefore, if the co-star doesn't appeal in any way, that "love scene" ultimately becomes the most bland thing. This suffering likely arises from the fact that actors are also human.
3. I haven't had the chance to see Korean films, so I have no basis to comment. Among Western films, "Crime and Punishment" (罪와 罰), and as for actors, Charles Boyer (샤르르·보아웨).

Kim Tonggyu
1. Because I have only lived on the stage, I have never been on a location shoot. Also, I have never played the role of a young man in love. There was just one time I did it. It was when the person who was supposed to do it couldn't due to prior circumstances, so I filled in briefly. It can't really be said that I performed it, but I write a word in response to your query. It was for a script called "Chindallaekkot p'il ttae" (진달내꽃 필 때) by Yi Unbang-ssi (李雲芳氏). I was to play the role of a young man named Sangp'il (相弼). To describe one scene from this role: on Sangp'il's birthday, Sangp'il gathers his friends and reminisces about his courtship with his wife Pokhŭi (복히) several years prior, using gestures, expressions, and speech as he remembers them, becoming Sangp'il and Pokhŭi from their courting days. It was a play within a play, so to speak. If done well, it could be a very romantic scene, but perhaps because I have always played old man roles, no matter how much effort I put in and studied diligently, I ultimately failed. Realizing this, I understood I was fated never to be able to portray romance.
2. Memorable Korean films and actors: "Se Tongmu" (세 동무 - Three Friends), which I saw in my hometown when I was much younger, and Pok Hyesuk-ssi (卜惠淑氏) who appeared in it. Among Western films, Charles Laughton (짠르스르롱) in "Les Misérables" (噫 無情) was good; Fredric March (푸레데릭마취) was also good. And Harry Baur's (아리보르) acting was also good.

Also, it seems much harder to make it feel real compared to other scenes, so I feel like love scenes <177> should have an exclusive patent granted to Western actors.

Sim Yŏng
1. Several years ago, during the production of "Panga t'aryŏng" (방아 타령), we went on location. Working all day and then in the evening, the filming crew would gather and perform makeshift jazz with "whistles, tin cans, and wooden pillows" (회파람 생철통 목침) – thinking of it now, it's an unforgettably joyful memory. Winter location shoots are truly dreadful.
2. In films, I haven't yet had the experience of a proper love scene, but I've had countless ones on stage. I have a habit of unknowingly embracing my counterpart too forcefully, so I often get scolded by the actresses playing opposite me. There are many times when I feel the urge to kiss them, if only there were no audience.
3. a. Hmm, let me see.
b. "Mokkyŏkcha" (目擊者 - The Witness)
c. Charles Boyer (샤루르·보아와에).

Kim Soyŏng
1. Going out to film while breathing the morning air and returning after finishing the day's work were enjoyable.
Filming all day without rest under the scorching sun was somewhat distressing.
2. I haven't yet appeared in a scene that could be called a proper love scene, so I don't have any particular difficulties or feelings about it.
3. Especially since the advent of talkies, I haven't seen any films that left a deep impression. As for famous actors, Mun Yebong (文藝峯) is quite good.
Among recent Western films, only "Unfinished Symphony" (未完成 交響樂) still remains vivid in my memory. For foreign actors, Mártha Eggerth (마르다·에겔트) who appeared in this film is good, and Marlene Dietrich (마루네·데이르히) captivates me more than anyone else.

Kim Yŏngok
As someone with no film experience yet, I'm unsure what I should write, but since it's my first experience appearing even in a minor role (俳品 - paep'um), I will speak.
1. The first film I appeared in was "Sunjŏng haehyŏp" (純情 海峽). The most enjoyable time was when we went on location to the Naktong River (洛東江). That scene was a more charming love scene than any other, and furthermore, the weather was good, so that day's location shoot finished satisfactorily. The feeling of returning home singing together with everyone <178> was both happy and joyful. More than distressing things, there were often unpleasant moments.
Why? Because when I'd diligently put on makeup to go on location, finding out it was cancelled due to various reasons was both distressing and unpleasant.
2. When doing a love scene with a partner I've never even met before, I find myself struggling without realizing it.
As for my feelings, whatever role I play, I don't feel any particular emotion on set (演席 - yŏnsŏk). It's after it's all done that the feelings, as asked, are not unusual.
3. Among Korean films, the one that remains in my impression is "Nagŭne" (나그네). As yet, no actor remains particularly in my impression.
Foreign films that captured my heart were "Unfinished Symphony" (未完成 交響樂) and "Yenn mosŭp" (옛 모습). The actress is the heroine of these works, Mártha Eggerth (마르다 에겔트).

Han Ŭnjin
Regarding your company's survey, I will substitute a response with a few brief words below.
1. I have no experience appearing in films yet, so I haven't done a "location shoot."
2. Other aspects are similar, but especially when encountering a "love scene," it's quite a headache each time. I think I could do better, but perhaps due to my still immature acting skills, I myself feel it seems very awkward. I do try very hard. When facing a difficult love scene, it was somewhat easier to perform with an experienced, seasoned actor as the partner.
3. I believe there must be many Korean films, but I haven't had the chance to watch them all. It's embarrassing. However, among the Korean films I have seen, though it was a long time ago, Na Un'gyu-ssi's (羅雲奎氏) "Arirang Part 1" (아리랑 前篇) remains in my impression. Its theme, departing from unrealistic works that were not Korean, was good, and Mr. Na Un'gyu's powerful acting, driven by a mass of passion, was good. And among recent ones, "Nagŭne" (나그네) was good. Watching it, I felt that if all conditions were favorably met, we too could create works not inferior to Japanese mainland films. As for actors, as mentioned before, Na Un'gyu-ssi from Arirang, and Pak Chehaeng-ssi (朴齊行氏) from "Nagŭne" remain in my impression. Pak Chehaeng-ssi is someone I also respect personally (私事 - sasa). Foreign films? Oh dear, there are too many. There are far too many good works and good actors. Briefly mentioning individual ones: "Mazurka" (마즈루가), "Mädchen in Uniform" (處女 湖水 - Ch'ŏnyŏ hosu?), "Poil de carotte" (닌징), "Pension Mimosas" (미모자 舘), "La Bandera" (外人 部隊). These are just some approximate ones. As for actresses: Simone Simon (시모느·시몽), Annabella (아나·베라), Loretta Young (로렛·양), Greta Garbo (그레타·갈보). And male actors: Charles Boyer (솨르르·보아웨), Gary Cooper (께리·쿠파), Jean-Pierre Aumont (짠·삐엘오몽).<179>

**Summary**

This 1938 survey in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine asks prominent Korean actors and actresses about their experiences with location filming, the challenges of performing love scenes, and their favorite films and performers. Many express discomfort and awkwardness with love scenes, sometimes wishing Western actors had a "monopoly" on them. Location shooting is recalled with mixed feelings – joy from camaraderie and audience appreciation, but distress from weather or logistical issues. Favorite Korean films mentioned include classics like "Arirang," "Beongeori Samnyong," and the more recent "Nagŭne," with Na Un'gyu being a frequently praised actor. There's a strong awareness of and admiration for European cinema (French, German, Czech), with notable mentions of actors like Charles Boyer, Greta Garbo, Harry Baur, and Mártha Eggerth, and films like "Mazurka," "Unfinished Symphony," and "Crime and Punishment." Some express disappointment with the state of Korean cinema or dislike for American films.

For historians, this article offers valuable first-person perspectives from key figures in the colonial Korean film industry. It reveals anxieties about performance (especially love scenes, potentially reflecting social mores), working conditions (location shoots), influences from foreign cinema (particularly European over American), and aspirations for Korean film's development compared to Japanese and Western standards. The specific films and actors mentioned provide a snapshot of cinematic tastes and cultural exchange during this period. The candid comments about difficulties and preferences offer a glimpse into the personal and professional lives of these cultural figures under colonial rule.
