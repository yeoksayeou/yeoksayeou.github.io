Modern Women's Bad Taste

An Sŏkchu
1. Frequently visiting beauty salons.
2. Liking popular songs (jazz, etc.).
3. Carrying a wallet in hand.
(The writer is a painter, film director, and writer)

Pak Ch'anghun
1. Excessive decoration and luxury in clothing.
2. Excessive sightseeing/spectating.
3. Excessive visits to fortune-tellers (問卜).

Sim Yŏng
A. Wearing Western clothes unsuitable for one's physique and getting a permanent wave unbecoming to one's face.
B. The epidemic trend of smoking among young women.
C. The sight of walking around chewing gum (쥬잉껌).
(The writer is an actor and theater critic)

Chŏng Sŏkt'ae
1. If they observed their husband's psychology more and rationalized their methods of handling them, it would become the driving force for family peace, but one feels that modern women lack humility and meekness and are derailing into self-importance and conceit.
2. Forgetting the noble and refined customs of their ancestors, adopting bizarre hairstyles and strange colors, and turning patterned clothing into the norm truly gives the impression of pandemonium (百鬼晝行).
3. It would be desirable if each person considered their economic situation and adopted a corresponding lifestyle, but it is frustrating that everyone dreams of seaside bathing, hot springs, Western-style houses, music, entertainment, etc., as indispensable conditions.
(The writer is a Doctor of Medicine)

Kim Sangyong
1. The sight of pretending to know what one doesn't know.
2. Shallow talkativeness.
3. Makeup that makes one ugly (醜化).
(The writer is the Dean of the Literature Department at Ewha Womans [Professional] School)

Kim Kwangbyŏn
1. Strolling for the sake of "show windows."
2. Valuing the watch more than the time itself.
3. Disregarding the possibility of harm.
(The writer is a teacher at Chungdong School)

Yi Kise
Respectfully replying, as someone who cannot even discern what kind of tastes modern women possess, it is factually impossible for me to critique or point out their good or bad points.
Please forgive me (恕諒).
(The writer is a theater critic) <155>

**Summary**

This article compiles short opinions from several prominent Korean male intellectuals and professionals in 1938 on what they consider "bad taste" among contemporary women. Criticisms range from fashion choices (Western clothing, perms, makeup, luxury), behaviors (frequenting beauty salons, smoking, chewing gum, liking jazz, carrying wallets openly, window shopping, talkativeness), attitudes (pretentiousness, lack of humility towards husbands, disregarding tradition, excessive desire for modern luxuries like beaches and Western houses), to perceived moral failings (visiting fortune-tellers, disregarding potential harm). One contributor demurs, stating he cannot judge. The article reflects the anxieties and criticisms directed at women adopting aspects of modernity—influenced by Western and Japanese trends—within the context of colonial Korea. Historians might find it revealing of conservative male perspectives on changing gender roles, the negotiation of tradition and modernity, class aspirations, and the perceived negative influences of consumer culture and Westernization on Korean women during this period.
