Magazine Title: 삼천리 (三千里)
Publication Date: 1938년 08월 01일 (August 1, 1938)
Article Title: 現代 男性의 惡趣味 (Modern Men's Bad Tastes)
Author: Hwang Sindŏk, Yi Sŏnhŭi, Kim Soyŏng, Pok Hyesuk, O Sohwa, Han Ŭnjin
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

Modern Men's Bad Tastes

Hwang Sindŏk
1. Drinking alcohol and smoking cigarettes.
2. Eating out more than necessary and liking *kisaeng*.
3. Visiting the red-light district.
(Ms. Hwang is a reporter for the *Tonga Ilbo*)

Yi Sŏnhŭi
1. Wandering the night streets and only finding their way home after midnight.
2. Hating to wear darned socks.
3. Speaking condescendingly (하대ㅅ말) to their own wives.
(Ms. Yi is a reporter for the *Chosŏn Ilbo*)

Kim Soyŏng
1. Above all, having a wife whom one loves the most, yet casting glances at different women must be a bad taste.
2. And while one must maintain appearances to some extent, it seems many men consider it their daily duty, which they absolutely must perform, to get a permanent wave, or to slick down their hair with oil like a drowned rat, parting it sharply, and spraying cheap perfume on their bodies. I wish they would quickly abandon such bad tastes.
3. Also, struggling with a meager salary yet frequently visiting entertainment spots like cafés (카푸에) cannot but be called a bad taste; it is pathetic that they cannot come to their senses regarding the living standards of Korean people.
(Ms. Kim is an actress)

Pok Hyesuk
1. Unbridled life of pleasure-seeking.
2. Unprincipled personal attacks.
3. Disorderly style of personal adornment (裝身 樣式).
(Ms. Pok is an actress)

O Sohwa
1. Praising the beauty of *kisaeng* in front of new women.
2. At the beach, deliberately capsizing a boat carrying women they don't know, and then making a fuss trying to rescue them – such detestable behavior.
3. A grown man carrying a flowerpot to visit a woman's house.
(Ms. O is an actress from a certain theater troupe)

Han Ŭnjin
1. Whether it's "k'ap'i," "k'op'i," "k'o-hi," or "ko-hi," I don't know which word is correct, but flirting with tea room waitresses (茶娘) while pretending to be good at English.
2. Tucking a Western handkerchief into the breast pocket of a suit (洋服) and acting pompous.
3. Why wear dark glasses when their eyes aren't even bad?
The question is tricky, so I don't know. Even this, I barely managed to write after discussing it with several people here.
(Ms. Han is an actress at the Chosŏn Film Studio) <51>

**Summary**

Six Korean women (journalists and actresses) writing in 1938 list modern men's "bad tastes." Criticisms range from traditional vices like drinking, womanizing (including visiting *kisaeng* and the red-light district), and mistreating wives, to more modern concerns like excessive spending at cafés despite low salaries, adopting affected Western styles (perms, oiled hair, suits with handkerchiefs, sunglasses), flirting in English, and general superficiality or lack of principle. The article offers a glimpse into societal critiques and changing gender dynamics during the colonial period. Historians might find the specific blend of traditional and modern criticisms interesting, reflecting anxieties about consumerism, Western influence, gender roles, and economic hardship under Japanese rule, as voiced publicly by prominent women.
