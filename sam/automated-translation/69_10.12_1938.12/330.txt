Gilt Bas-relief
						Kim Myŏngsun

When the five-colored threads were drawn upon that breast,
A young woman stood at the Taedonggang River wharf.

Fluttering, fluttering
The hems of her white silk clothes,
She cast a momentarily desolate gaze
Across the river to the long forest.

The rhythm of nature,
Spoken by wind and waves—
How could one mark it all with musical notes?

Beneath the golden bas-relief of that scorebook,
Nŭngnado Island, below it Pubyŏngnu Pavilion.

Like tuning the strings of a zither,
With the refreshing gait of youth <208>
As she climbs the banks of Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk cliff,
Her lofty song filled the world.

The numerous names on the walls
Left by tourists visiting the famous site—
Shall I call this fame? <209>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Myŏngsun depicts a vibrant young woman standing by the Taedong River near P'yŏngyang's famous landmarks, Nŭngnado Island and Pubyŏngnu Pavilion. Her presence is described as a beautiful, almost sculpted image ("gilt bas-relief") against the natural scenery. Her youthful energy and song contrast with the fleeting nature of fame represented by the graffiti left by tourists on the walls of the scenic spot, questioning the true meaning of achievement or renown. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into the aesthetic sensibilities of the time, blending traditional imagery (silk clothing, famous landmarks) with a modern female figure embodying youth and vitality. It subtly elevates Korean natural beauty and perhaps individual spirit over the superficial markings of transient visitors, which might resonate with sentiments during the colonial period.

浮金彫
						金明淳
그 가삼에 五色線을 그을때
젊은 女人大同江阜頭에 섯다

하이한 綾羅의
옷나래를 풀날니며 풀날리며

江건너 長林에 던지었다

바람과물결이 아뢰이던
自然의 韻律을
어이다 音符로 標하리오

그音符冊의 金色浮彫
밑으로羅綾島 을로浮碧樓

琴線을調律하듯
靑春의 爽快한 거름거리＜208＞
淸流壁 기슭으로 오를제
드높은노래 天下에 찾다

名勝地遊 客들의
수다한 壁面姓名들어
功名이라 傳하리오＜209＞
