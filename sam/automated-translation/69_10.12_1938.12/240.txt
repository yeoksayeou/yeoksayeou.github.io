**Body Text Translation:**

A Newcomer's Aspirations, Visiting Ch'oe Nam-ju of Chosŏn Film Co., Ltd.
						Ch'oe Nam-ju

The Path of Chosŏn Film

What will be the future path for Chosŏn Film, which captured the ripening sentiment and culture of this land in films like *Arirang*, *Nagŭnae* (Traveler), and *Han River*, making them a source of pride both at home and abroad? Here is a young entrepreneur who has newly emerged brightly, shouldering a huge sum of 500,000 wŏn.

The Chosŏn film world, which had merely been spinning its wheels, saying things couldn't be done due to lack of money or couldn't be made due to lack of technology, is now seeing a considerably abundant wind blowing. Recently, films like *Sim Ch'ŏng*, *Han River*, and *Tosaengnok* have already been released. Furthermore, the Kŭk Yŏn (Theater Research) Film Department's first work *Aeryŏn Song* (Love Song), Kŭkkwang Film Company's *Ŏhwa* (Fishing Fire), and besides these, *Kwich'akchi* (Destination) are expected to premiere soon. Also, the newly established Koryŏ Film Co., Ltd. dispatched a filming crew to the border region last month. In such times, Chosŏn Film Co., Ltd., the most prominent presence in the Peninsula's film industry, has developed very smoothly after fully establishing its identity as a serious enterprise last summer. Starting with its first work, Ch'unwŏn's *Mujŏng* (Heartless), it is preparing to produce *Tae Ch'unhyang Chŏn* (Great Tale of Ch'unhyang) by inviting Mr. Murayama Tomoyoshi from Tokyo, while Mr. Yi Kyu-hwan, known to the world for directing *Yŏro* (Journey), is reportedly filming *Sae Ch'ulbal* (New Start) in a place called Hyŏnp'ung, Kyŏngnam Province. Seeing all this, not only the Chosŏn film world but society in general feels invigorated. On a day when fallen leaves were fiercely scattered by the wind, this reporter visited Mr. Ch'oe Nam-ju, the president of Chosŏn Film, who was staying at the Bando Hotel,<173> to hear about his abundant and vibrant grand business plans and strategies.

Lest readers imagine him having a beard, being plump, tall, and considerably old, the reporter must first briefly describe Mr. Ch'oe's appearance.
He is not tall, not plump—rather chubby—and though he might have a beard, his age seems a bit insufficient for growing one—he was clean-shaven. No matter how you look at him, he appears not yet thirty, very affable, yet also seemingly quite quirky and reserved, putting on airs of solemnity, but even that is not awkward at all and seems fitting. The reporter hesitated for a moment, wondering what to start discussing with this young president, then asked, "What was the motivation for establishing Chosŏn Film Co., Ltd.?"
As if he already knew what I would ask, the president replied,
"I made it because it didn't exist."
"There are many things that don't exist; why did you specifically choose to make that?"
I had no choice but to ask again like this.
"There are many things that don't exist, but if it's a business where I don't lose money by investing and it benefits others, then in Chosŏn right now, the film business is the best."
The reporter thought Mr. Ch'oe's words seemed right and changed the subject.
"When will *Mujŏng* be released?"
"Director Pak Ki-ch'ae finished all the editing in Tokyo and returned, and it's currently being dubbed."
"Will *Sae Ch'ulbal* also be released within this year?"
"Yes, it will. It's currently being filmed."
"It's being filmed in Hyŏnp'ung, right? How many people went there?"
"Director Yi Kyu-hwan and 20 others went."
"I heard several actors joined the company recently?"
"Yes, after the merger with Sŏngbong Film Garden this time, actors Mun Ye-bong, Kim Han, Sŏ Wŏl-yŏng of Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa, Chŏng Ch'un-hŭi, a graduate of Kyōto Dōshisha Higher Girls' School, and Yi Ye-ran, a graduate of Osaka Higher Girls' School, have joined the company and are currently working."
"I heard you were recruiting for Ch'unhyang; did you find a suitable person?"
"I don't know well. It seems many people are applying."
"What exactly happened regarding the relationship with Sŏngbong Film Garden? Is it as reported in the newspapers?"
"There's no need to talk at length about it now; to state the result, Sŏngbong has essentially come into Chosŏn Film. It's like two households merging into one. As a result, the family<174> has grown, and the household affairs are being managed on a large scale, so the office has also moved to a spacious location on the 3rd floor of the Imga Building, 71 Chongno 1-ch'ome."
"So, Sŏngbong wasn't dissolved, but rather Sŏngbong continues to exist as Sŏngbong?"
"That's right. Sŏngbong is, so to speak, a directly managed production unit of Chosŏn Film. As I mentioned at the beginning, it seems we are now fully prepared to handle anything without difficulty going forward. The film studio will also be completed before long. When you see the studio, you'll understand, but we are striving to make it no less impressive than the Tokyo Film Village. We are planning to provide company housing (사택) not only for directors and executives but also for exclusive actors."
"How much are the actors' salaries?"
"Since we must at least ensure their financial stability, we are estimating an average of up to 200 wŏn per month."
"Who are the exclusive actors currently?"
"We don't have any men yet; for women, there are Han Ŭn-jin, Mun Ye-bong, and others."
"What about the others?"
"They will soon become exclusive."
"I heard Mr. An Sŏk-chu joined the company?"
"It's not confirmed yet, but you will likely see him working [with us] in the future. Our company's policy is centered on talent, whether it's actors or directors. We won't keep many exclusive contracts. Those with talent will get less work [if they slack? unclear phrasing, likely means 'will be kept busy'], and those without talent will eventually leave. Therefore, even directors and actors who are not exclusively contracted with our company can be hired if they have talent. For instance, if the company determines that a certain director can brilliantly make a film based on a selected script, we can call upon that director and entrust them with the work, even if they are not part of our company. We can invite directors like this, and since those directors will choose actors they favor, people not exclusively contracted with the company can also be employed."
"What kind of tendency [genre/style] of works do you plan to produce?"
"We intend to strive to fully capture the Chosŏn sentiment. Therefore, if possible, we will try to work on ancient novels."
"How far do you anticipate the sales market extending?"
"I'm not sure about other places yet, but we plan to distribute as much as possible to places where many Chosŏn people live. We are initially targeting Hawaii, Manchuria, and the Japanese mainland."<175>

**Summary**

The article interviews Ch'oe Nam-ju, the young president of the newly capitalized Chosŏn Film Co., Ltd. (朝鮮映畵株式會社) in late 1938. Ch'oe discusses the company's formation, recent merger with Sŏngbong Film Garden (聖峰映畵園), current productions like *Mujŏng* (無情) and *Sae Ch'ulbal* (새出發), talent acquisition including famous actors like Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰) and plans to hire based on merit, intentions to build a large studio comparable to Japanese ones, provide company housing and stable salaries for staff, and focus film content on Korean sentiment, potentially adapting classic literature. He aims to distribute films to Korean diaspora communities in Hawaii, Manchuria, and Japan. From a historical perspective, the article reveals the significant capital investment (500,000 won) and ambition within the colonial Korean film industry, its connections with Japanese film (inviting director Murayama Tomoyoshi, editing in Tokyo), the strategy of consolidating smaller studios, and the goal of creating culturally specific content for both domestic and overseas Korean audiences, reflecting both burgeoning national consciousness and the realities of operating within the Japanese empire.

朝鮮映畵의 길

아리랑, 나그내, 漢江 등 이 땅의 무르녹는 정서와 문화를 영화에 담어 널니 내외의 자랑을 삼은 朝鮮映畵의 앞날의 길은 장차 어떠할는고 이제 50만원의 거재를 질머지고 새로히 빗나게 등장한 청년 사업가.

돈이 없어 안되느니 기술이 모자라 못하느니 하고 거저 매암만 돌든 朝鮮映畵界도 이제 자못 풍성한 바람이 불어서 昨今 이래로 「沈淸」 「漢江」 「圖生錄」이 이미 나오고 이제 또 劇硏 映畵部 窮1회 작품 「愛戀頌」 極光映畵社의 「漁火」 또 그밖게 「歸着地」 등이 불원간에 封切될 것이며 또 새로 창립된 高麗映畵株式會社에서도 月前부터 국경방면에 촬영대가 떠났다. 이러는 때 半島映畵界에 가장 뚜렸한 존재인 朝鮮映畵株式會社에선 작년 야름에 본격적 기업회사로서의 면목을 완전히 한 후 아주 순조롭게 발전되여 제1회 작품 春園의 「無情」을 비롯해서 東京으로부터 村山知義氏를 초빙하야 「大春香傳」 제작하려고 준비하는 한편 「旅路」의 감독을 해서 세상에 알여진 李圭煥氏의 「새出發」도 지금 慶南 玄風이라는 곳에 가서 촬영중이라 한다. 이러고 보니 朝鮮映畵界 뿐 아니라 일반사회가 활기를 띈 듯한 느낌이다. 기자는 나문입이 바람에 몹시 떠러지는 어느날 半島호텔에 유숙중인 朝映의 사장 崔南周씨를＜173＞ 방문하고 풍성하고 자못 活氣滿滿한 씨의 위대한 事業設計策을 듯기로 하였다.
氏는 수염이 나고 뚱뚱하고 키가 크고 또 나이가 어지간할 것이라고 독자 여러분이 이렇게 짐작하실가 봐서 기자는 먼저 氏의 인상을 대강 말슴해야겠다.
키가 못 크고 뚱뚱하지 못하고 ― 똥똥하고 수염이 나긴 했갰지만 아직 수염 길느기엔 나이가 좀 모자랄 듯 ― 빡빡 깍그섯는데 아모리 봐도 설흔은 못 넘은 양 몹시 상양스러우시나 또 여주 엉뚱하시고 내숭하신 편인 듯 무책 점잔을 빼시는데 그것 역시 도모지 서을지않고 자리가 잡혔다. 기자는 이 젊은 사장에게 어떤 것부터 이애기 해볼까 하고 한참 주저하다가 「朝鮮映畵株式會社를 맨둘게 된 동기가 어디 있읍니까」
하고 물었다. 그랬드니 사장은 내가 뭇는 말을 미리 알고 게신가 처럼
「없으니까 맨들었지요」
하는 것이다.

「이제 와서 길게 이애기할 필요는 없구요 결과를 말슴한다면 聖峰이 朝映 안에 들어온 셈이죠 두살님이 한테 합하게 된 셈이지요. 그래서 식구도＜174＞ 많고 살님도 대규모로 버리젔음으로 사무실도 鍾路1丁目 71蕃地 任家삘딍 3層 넓은 데로 옮았입니다」
「그러니같 聖峰이 해체된 것이 아니라 聖峰은 聖峰대로 있군요?」
「그렇지요 聖峰은 말하자면 朝映의 직영 푸로덕숀이지요. 머리 말슴디리는 것같음니다마는 앞으로는 무엇이나 어렵지 않게 해나갈 준비가 다-된 것 같습니다. 오라지 않어서 촬영소도 落成될 것입니다. 촬영소를 구경하시면 아시겠지만 東京映畵村만 못하지 않게 해놀여고 노력하고 있읍니다. 감독 중역은 물론 전속 배우들도 사택을 줄 생각입니다」
「배우들 봉급은 얼마식 주시나요」
