Publication Date: 1938년 12월 01일
Article Title: 春園 近作 「사랑」을 읽고 女主人公 석순옥씨에게 올니는 글 (A Letter Addressed to the Heroine Sŏk Sunok After Reading Ch'unwŏn's Recent Work "Love")
Author: 毛允淑 (Mo Yunsuk)
Article Type: 문예평론 (Literary Criticism)

A Letter Addressed to the Heroine Sŏk Sunok After Reading Ch'unwŏn's Recent Work "Love"
						Mo Yunsuk

Ms. Sŏk Sunok! On a winter night when the Big Dipper has tilted, the night is tediously long, and sometimes sleep comes slowly. Please do not think it too strange that I have taken up my pen like this towards you, Ms. Sunok. I know well that a worldly woman like myself lacks the qualifications to be friends with a woman like you. However, isn't it strange how people, when they discover qualities or beautiful aspects in others that they themselves lack, seek out that person with admiration and kindness?

Based on this principle, I have dared to take up my pen towards you.

Such simple and clear times are only the night hours when one has lost sleep. Though the passing winds have struck the windowpane several times, I have forgotten to prepare the curtains and just stare at the ceiling.

Ms. Sunok! I have just finished reading the entire first part of the novel "Love" by Master Ch'unwŏn. <197> Deeply moved by Oknam's character, when I reached the scene of her departure from this world, my sympathy was solely focused on the woman named Oknam. Thus, I could not feel as much sympathy for your existence, Sŏk Sunok. This was because I thought Oknam's death might have been due to your cruel good deed. Ms. Sunok! Although I know you are not the type to think ill of me for saying this, my heart feels apologetic as I say it.

Could it be that this thought arose because I understand you less? No. While I want to understand you in your own way, how can I deny that the capacity to recognize the woman named Oknam arises sufficiently without any conflict with the capacity to understand you?

Oknam is not truly a genius or a miraculous personality. She is just an unremarkable woman qualified as a member of simple civil society.

Since women with the character and refinement of Madam Oknam are rarely seen in reality, Madam Yi Oknam cannot but be a precious existence. She is, I think, a ** pioneer who broke through the highest principles of good conduct (善律) in this human world.

She deserved to be blessed and should rightfully have occupied her husband's love entirely. Forgive my words, but looking only at the first part of "Love," you, Sŏk Sunok, were outlined only faintly like a shadow of Good, whereas Oknam sculpted the best of herself onto the earth, onto her reality.

You are still one who walks a beautiful path in imagination; Oknam was a woman who lived sprinkling beautiful fragrance upon life.

If you are the illusion of Good, Oknam was a woman who, even on the stage of a stony field that other women often cannot manage, did not forget the duties and patience she possessed. Oknam was a woman close to perfection like this, yet she was one who could not possess her husband entirely. Oknam struggled to comprehend the sublime light of her husband's life, and she lacked the ability to understand the boundless aspiration of that sublime life's power to soar.

An Pin seems like someone who should never have married in the first place. Because the structure of his inner spirit operates under different laws than the structure of marriage, you see. <198>

An Pin is a man with a broad compassion capable of showing all human kindness, effort, and affection to the woman he married. However, he is not one who can darken the path of his own life or spirit merely to satisfy the desires of that one woman. I suspect this might have been the lonely sorrow Oknam could not speak of and agonized over inwardly. In this sense, I wonder if a woman like Oknam might not have built a more harmonious family had she been united with someone of a slightly more worldly character than An Pin. Oknam is a respectable, ordinary woman. And I say this within the context of this distorted social custom; shouldn't this world become righteous only when all of us women possess such heart and refinement?

Ms. Sunok! Let's talk about you now.

I can guess the various thoughts that arose in your mind from graduating from a professional school and working as a high school teacher up to the moment you put on a nurse's uniform. In any case, I understand that your admiration for An Pin as a woman is not mere infatuation, nor is it a longing for his character based on a momentary mood. This is because you possess the nature to resolve life and refine your spirit alone in a spiritual inner chamber, away from the phenomena of this life that we Chosŏn (朝鮮) women often live well without paying attention to. When you saw An Pin, you did not see An Pin the man, but merely sensed your lonely life, your sleeping spirit, uttering something. In such a case, I know your heart seeking An Pin must have stemmed from the true consciousness emitted by a pure white life. Therefore, your admiration is not for An Pin as Oknam's husband, but for An Pin as a member of the universe, arising from a spiritual instinct seeking to fulfill a part of humanity's high aspirations. To say you admire An Pin, Oknam's husband, cannot be but a great insult. However, Ms. Sunok! Wasn't it impossible for Oknam, following you, to misunderstand you precisely because you yourself are so noble? This is solely due to the sublimity of your character.

Sunok! You, who possess a love capable of loving beyond all these things, may face trials that you must endure somewhat humanly. <199>

You cannot help but love An Pin. The idea of not being able to love An Pin is impossible as long as your life can exist.

It is the religion and belief that guides your world. Ms. Sunok! I feel a strong resonance with your inviolable, lofty love. However, Ms. Sunok! Thinking on the other hand, could it be that your heart understanding and loving An Pin too broadly, too highly, too deeply might become the cause generating a somewhat pitiful fate? The fact that because you found An Pin, you found light and gained strength, enabling you to think of this world as your own body, belongs to the realm of extremely noble deeds.

Sunok, who can only love An Pin!

When nothing else in this world besides An Pin can emit meaning, when you can live leaning only on An Pin as a staff, what logic is there that Sunok, Sunok must seek another support?

As Ms. Sunok gets older, how can the world leave Ms. Sunok alone? Warnings and urgings will come from all directions to participate in worldly customs. Moreover, in Ms. Sunok's case, getting married not for her own sake but for the sake of the beloved An Pin! How difficult it is to understand! Yes. An Pin, as a marriage partner, is absolutely unacceptable within your current imagination. He absolutely cannot be a marriage partner.

Then Sunok! Sunok, don't you possess a fate destined to be driven far away from the worldly law that marriage is the extension of love? A love that can only live by separating love and marriage.

The strength to transcend marriage in order to be faithful to love. This cannot but be an exceedingly difficult thing. However, Ms. Sunok! Ms. Sunok boldly undertakes this. What a strong woman you are!

You, who pass over marriage, the most important event of a lifetime, without any research. Your heart, filled with the flash of life received from An Pin, <200> when would it have time to contemplate marriage? The beautiful secret heart that seeks not to profane An Pin's honor!

Therefore, you plan to become Hŏ Yŏng's wife. Ms. Sunok! However, for me, it is extremely difficult to understand, based on common ways of love, that Sunok intends to marry Hŏ Yŏng, whom she does not love. Ms. Sunok! Really, if An Pin were to marry you, could he be at peace?

Look here, no matter how much An Pin is An Pin, would he understand Sunok's intention to marry another man as purely stemming from consideration for himself? Isn't An Pin also human? He must be an ordinary human being who wants to improve. What can one improve apart from life? He wouldn't be a person seeking perfection in the void, having left the stage of life.

Thinking from the perspective of An Pin the human, would he have the strength to understand Sunok so nobly? Although An Pin might externally support Sunok's marriage and adopt an attitude as if it were natural, inwardly, could he have remained ordinary about Sunok leaving? If he harbored even a little bit of disappointment, it would have been difficult for him to understand Sunok's spirit. The author will likely explain An Pin's attitude later, but who knows about the author? Perhaps they will naturally treat An Pin's inner state broadly, like that of a saint. But isn't this An Pin's inner self something only God knows better than the author? Sunok! Truly, Sunok, I know well that your circumstances and situation lead you to intend to marry someone else for An Pin's sake. Even if tens of thousands of readers deny it, I feel I alone can understand it well.

However, Sunok! Isn't An Pin a middle-aged man over 40? To him, a person with much experience and trials in the world, Sunok must seem like a green youth exuding the scent of childhood.

Looking at the novel, An Pin doesn't seem as pure-hearted as Sunok, nor does he show expressions of feeling love. He merely keeps Sunok nearby, barely not rejecting her. From this perspective, Sunok's love seems not one dealing with passion, but a love close to holiness, dealing with spirit and truth. <201>

Sunok! Sunok, you are a woman with the conviction that life's vitality is eternal. Accordingly, it seems An Pin is the companion of the force that seeks to keep that life eternally, and to make that life vibrant within an unbreakable joy, overcoming its suffering.

If so, then there is no contradiction in An Pin being an eternal existence for Sunok. It seems no one besides An Pin can delight Sunok's soul.

Sunok! Even if Sunok marries not just once, but several times, isn't An Pin's existence one that cannot be disregarded? With this faith, you intend to go as Hŏ Yŏng's wife. From a reader's perspective, it's dumbfounding. But even if you don't speak of it, I believe you must have endured considerable life's pain. Here, regarding the innocent Sunok marrying off so unjustly, the author introduces the truth of the law of cause and effect between past and present lives to resolve this human suffering. Even if Sunok knows all this, wouldn't there still be hot sorrow in her heart when taking her pure body to another person's side? In our readers' thoughts, it seems God's law would be right only if no one in this universe besides An Pin could possess Sunok's precious body. Only then does it seem spring would arrive in this world, but the author seems intent on expounding a greater truth by denying this principle, so it seems Sunok will suffer terribly ahead, and I worry in advance.

Since I haven't seen the latter part, I don't know if you actually marry Hŏ Yŏng or not, but as a reader, I wish you wouldn't marry and live alone. However, hearing you are getting engaged makes me fearful.

If you do marry, Sunok, there will be times when your life writhes in loneliness, you will have to suffer pain you shouldn't have to bear, and make sacrifices you shouldn't have to make, living on; what will you do about that?

When a crown of thorns is woven in your heart, and you must depart from An Pin's existence! What will you do about the pain of your suffering soul in life? Sunok! What will you do when, at times, an unbearable loneliness of life violates Sunok's pure and flawless thoughts? Therefore, follow my words: don't marry, not for An Pin's sake, but for your own. <202> Live alone for An Pin's sake and become a worker who helps society and the world for him. Since you are currently an unmarried woman, you seem not to know how crucial marriage is to life. And why don't you understand An Pin's feeling of not taking a new wife after his wife died? Isn't it proof that, having already lived through half of life, he saw no great efficacy even in the union of people who loved each other deeply? Sunok, of course, I know you are not marrying for happiness, but who knows if the marriage ceremony performed truly for An Pin's sake might, at its root, become a cause of sadness and distress for An Pin? This is not to underestimate An Pin's great, broad character. The greater, broader, and deeper a person An Pin is, wouldn't the desire to possess one life completely be stronger? Isn't great and strong love inherently narrow? The sun eternally illuminates and gives life to the universe with the heat burning from a single focal point.

I don't know about An Pin, but you, Sŏk Sunok, will likely find value in existence only while living with your life buried in An Pin's heart. Sunok! Sunok, you must not doubt An Pin during his lifetime. If you do, the position of your life will also be shaken. Even if there are things about An Pin you cannot interpret, you, Sunok, cannot be suspicious of him or betray him. If you were to do so, Sunok's love would become impure from that moment on. If Sunok herself is living with a sublime spirit, is there any reason An Pin would appear clouded? Sunok! It's past two in the morning. How happy I would be if you could receive this letter from wherever you are in space! But you are a woman from a novel. A maiden who only dreams of An Pin.

My concern is, An Pin should eternally call and love you only in your dreams; if the time comes when he breaks through the castle of dreams and calls upon the human Sunok, I wonder what Sunok's attitude will become. Sunok! I call affectionately, Sunok, the heroine of beautiful sorrow! Please take off your nurse's uniform quickly and go to bed. And study to transcend youth, and resolve to endure any hardship in this world for An Pin's sake. Sunok! When you appear in the latter part, please do not meet Hŏ Yŏng. It seems best not to marry. Finally, I want to call the title of this book "Waltz of Life."

Midnight, from your unknown friend, Yunsuk. <203>

**Summary**

Mo Yunsuk writes a letter to Sŏk Sunok, the fictional heroine of Yi Kwangsu's novel "Love," expressing admiration for Sunok's spiritual devotion to the male protagonist, An Pin, while contrasting it with the conventional goodness of An Pin's deceased wife, Oknam. Mo questions the wisdom and implications of Sunok's decision to marry another man, Hŏ Yŏng, supposedly for An Pin's sake, viewing it as a potentially tragic self-sacrifice born from an overly idealized love. She strongly advises Sunok against this marriage, urging her instead to remain single and dedicate her life to An Pin in a purely spiritual manner, serving society in his name. The article provides insight into how Korean intellectuals during the colonial period grappled with modern concepts of love, sacrifice, spiritual versus societal expectations (particularly marriage), and the role of women, all through the lens of contemporary literature. Historians might find the intense engagement with a fictional character, the discussion of Yi Kwangsu's influential work, and the exploration of female agency and complex emotional landscapes within the constraints of colonial modernity particularly interesting.

春園 近作 「사랑」을 읽고 女主人公 석순옥씨에게 올니는 글
						毛允淑
석순옥씨! 북두칠성이 기우러진 한밤 중 겨을밤은 지리하게도 길어서 간혹 잠이 더대오는 수도 있음니다. 내가 순옥씨를 향해서 이렇게 붓을 든 것을 너머 의아하게 생각지 마십시요. 나 같은 속된 여자야 당신 같은 여자와 동무될 자격이 없음을 잘 암니다. 그러나 사람은 이상하게도 자기가 가지지 않은 성품이나 아름다운 점이 다른 사람에게서 발견될 때는 憧憬과 친절을 다하여 그 사람을 찾고저 하는 것이 아님니까?
이런 이치에 빛이여서 나는 감히 당신을 향해 붓을 드렀음니다.
이렇게 단순하고 맑은 시간이란 다만 잠을 놓저 버린 밤시간임니다. 몇번이나 지나가든 바람들이 문창을 치고 갓것만 나는 커틘 준비를 닞어 버리고 천정만 처다 봅니다.
순옥씨! 나는 방금 春園선께서의 소설인 『사랑』의 전편을 끝막었음니다. 옥남이의＜197＞ 인격에 너머 감동된 남어지 그가 세상을 하직하는 장면에 이르러서는 오로지 동정이 옥남이란 여성에게만 쏠였었음니다. 그래서 당신 석순옥이란 존재에겐 그토록 동정이 이러나지 못했음니다. 옥남이가 죽은 것은 당신의 잔인한 선행때문이 아닌가 생각되였기 때문임니다. 순옥씨! 내가 이렇게 말한다고 마음 앞이 생각할 그대가 아닌 줄 알면서도 이 말하는 내 맘은 미안함니다.
당신을 덜 이해해서 이런 생각이 났겠어요 아님니다. 당신은 당신대로 이해하고 싶으나 옥남이란 여성을 알어 줄 수 있는 힘이 당신을 이해하는 힘과 아모 충돌함이 없이 충분이 솟고 있음을 무엇으로 거부함니까?
옥남은 진실노 천재적이거나 기적적인 성격을 갖인 여성은 아님니다. 그저 단순한 시민사회에 일원자격을 갖인 무난한 여성임니다.
옥남여사만한 인격과 교양을 갖인 여자도 매우 드물게 보여지는 현실이니까 이옥남여사야말노 귀한 존재가 아닐 수 없음니다. 그는 내가 생각건대 이 人生界에서 최고의 善律을 돌파한 **의 선*자임니다.
그는 복을 받어야 하고 남편의 사랑을 호을노 차지하여야 맛당함니다. 미안한 말이나 『사랑』 前篇만으로 보아서는 석순옥 당신은 善의 그림자같이 히미하게 윤곽을 지였을 뿐이나 옥님은 땅우에 자기 현실 우에 최선을 조각처럼 싹여 놓았음니다.
당신은 아직 상상으로 아름다운 길을 것는 자요 옥남은 생활 우에 아름다운 향수를 뿌리며 산 여자임니다.
당신을 善의 환상이라면 옥남은 다른 여자가 흔이 할 수 없는 돌작밭 무대 우에서도 제가 갖인 바 의무와 인내를 잊지 않은 여자임니다. 옥남은 이렇게 완전에 갓가운 여성이었으나 남편의 전부를 소유할 수 없는 자였음니다. 옥남은 남편의 숭고한 생명의 빛갈을 分間하기에 힘이 드렀고 그 숭고한 생명에 세력이 끝없이 비약하려는 要望을 이해할 능력이 부족하였음니다.
안빈은 원래 혼인을 안했어야 옳을 사람같이 생각됨니다. 그의 내부 정신설치가 혼인 구조와는 法이 틀니니까 말이지죠.＜198＞
안빈은 혼인한 여자에게 인간적인 모든 친절을 다할 수 있고 노력과 애무를 다할 수 있는 넓은 자비심을 갖인 자임니다. 그러나 그 한 여자의 욕망을 편하게 하려고 자기 생명이나 정신의 앞길을 어둡힐 수는 없는 자임니다. 이것이 옥남이가 말 못하고 속으로만 번민한 외로운 슲음이였을가 함니다. 이런 의미에서 나는 옥남이같은 여자는 안빈보다 조곰만 속된 性格者와 결합하였드면 더 원만한 가정을 일우지 않었을가 함니다. 옥남은 존경할 수 있는 한 평범한 여자임니다. 그것도 이 찌그러진 사회습관속에서 하는 말이지 우리 여자가 다 그런 마음과 교양을 갖어야 이 세상은 바로 될게 아님니까?
