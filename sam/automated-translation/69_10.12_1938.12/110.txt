Magazine Title: 삼천리 제10권 제12호
Publication Date: 1938년 12월 01일
Article Title: 東京遊記 (Tōkyō Travelogue)
Author: 朱雲成
Article Type: 기행문 (Travelogue)

A Fondness for Tōkyō

On July 25th, at 4:15 PM, catching the "Akatsuki," I once again headed for the Eastern Capital (東都). This trip, initially planned for two weeks, stretched, and I became a traveler staying in Tōkyō for a full three months. This was to successfully complete my business. Fortunately, without failure, I returned to Kyŏngsŏng [Seoul] on October 18th at 1:30 PM. At the request of Brother Pa-in (巴人) to write about my impressions of Tōkyō, I don't have much special material to write about, but I will try to jot down some of my feelings from traversing Tōkyō, the stage of my aspirations (憧憬), for 90 days and nights.

I hold a fondness for Tōkyō in the sense that it is the "London" of the East and the headquarters of New Asian Culture. Whenever I arrive at Tōkyō Station, I feel my chest broaden and somehow feel my energy surge. So, when I think that this was the land where Mr. Kim Ok-kyun (金玉均) once strode, I suddenly transform into a heroic man. This time too, counting the money I spent in Tōkyō at <78> thousand yen, I walked cheerfully, and upon returning to Chosŏn [Korea], everyone greeted me saying my complexion (身光) looked good. Clearly, I feel we need to shed our old skins more and strive for a cheerful life. Seeing how 90 days of refreshing breathing thus changed my appearance!
What evokes my fondness more than anything is that Tōkyō is "a place that recognizes people." During this trip too, I received noble hospitality (優待) several times and was deeply moved by the purity of those who love passion and justice. I also felt that if one could taste peace and freedom, even to some extent, it would only be in Tōkyō. Thus, I felt the gentle air and harmonious fragrance (薰香) soothe the traveler's sorrowful heart (愁心).

Pilgrimage to Tōkyō's Outskirts

On this occasion, I was able to tour the famous Japanese sites I had always wanted to see. One day in late August, I departed from Asakusa Kaminarimon Station (淺草*門驛) towards the scenic spot, Nikkō. Around 1 PM, I reached Nikkō City; because travelers just pass through, the city streets showed no signs of prosperity, truly just a small town. Riding a tour bus through the famous spots, the Sacred Bridge (神橋) and Tōshōgū Shrine (東照宮), which I had seen in Japanese language readers as a child, looked familiar. Getting off the bus at Kaeshiuma Station (返馬驛), it was time to ride a mountain cable car up to the summit reaching the sky. Similar to a tram, the cable car groaned ("엉엉") as it climbed the mountain, steep as a wall (壁上); it seemed magnificent but also pitiful. Even though it was a machine, it looked like a living creature working very hard. Getting off the cable car, this time, there was an aerial cableway strung between this mountain peak and another, moving back and forth. As I had never ridden an airplane, I rode this aerial cableway with the excitement of riding an airplane. Next, I rode a bus again, winding around, and ah—how magnificent! The pride of Nikkō: Lake Chūzenji (中禪寺湖), the great waterfall (大瀑布), and the majestic figure (雄姿) of Mt. Nantai (男體山) at an altitude of 8,000 feet. I endlessly exclaimed in admiration. Truly, this must be the deliberate playfulness (作戱) of the Creator; otherwise, how could it be? Atop a high mountain peak 8,000 feet above sea level, there was a great lake of blue waves stretching ten thousand leagues (蒼波萬頃), a vast sea stretching beyond sight (一望無際). We took a steamship to tour this great lake and listened to the guide's explanations of historic sites. The great temple called Chūzenji (中禪寺) was a brilliantly glittering (玲*燦爛한) building, and it seemed every visitor entered once to pay respects. After seeing the lake, we took the "mountaintop elevator" and descended straight down over 500 feet. It was said that the infirm (病者) couldn't watch; the terrifying great waterfall, plunging and pouring down from the ninth heaven (九天), spewed white mist with a force that seemed to overturn heaven and earth. <79> I was so satisfied that I leaped and exclaimed wildly (狂躍感嘆). Taking the elevator back up, I rode a car, gazing up at the majestic peak (雄峰) of Mt. Nantai, descended the winding mountain passes, and headed straight for Kinu-gawa Onsen (きぬかわ온천). The spectacular sight here was the rows of 500-year-old cedar trees (杉木群) standing side by side for 20 *ri* (里); it was truly a sight from a dreamland. Kinu-gawa Onsen was a gathering place for esteemed guests from around the world, a village of hotels (ホテル촌). I too stayed at a first-class hotel and returned to Tōkyō the next day.
On the next occasion, I traveled along the Pacific coast to Yokosuka. As this is the naval base, the sight of sailors swimming was splendid. The late Fleet Admiral Tōgō's (東鄕元帥) victorious warship served as a very meaningful exhibition hall; entering the ship (艦內), I viewed the rare historical reality of the Russo-Japanese War. Seeing the flag torn in five pieces by cannon fire, bearing the words "The fate of the Empire rests on this one battle" (皇國興廢 此一戰), I felt something stir within me. I keenly felt that the heroic stance (雄據) of Fleet Admiral Tōgō, who had faced death like this, was a good lesson for the peoples of the East. Leaving Yokohama, passing through Hayama, I respectfully viewed (拜觀하였다) the Hayama Imperial Villa (葉山御用邸). Then, arriving at Yokosuka Port (橫須港), gazing at the vast waves (洋洋) of the Pacific Ocean, I toured the exquisite park and had a meal amidst Westerners at the "Grand Hotel" before returning to Tōkyō.

Various Aspects of Tōkyō Under the Current Situation (時局下)

Anyone would find it strange to see Tōkyō under the state of emergency (非常時下) being so solemn and calm (肅然無風), with everything and everyone in a state of peace. Whether it was the Hitler Jugend (히틀러 유겐트), the Italian delegation, or the Kaiser's grandson (카이젤令孫), they all said the same thing: Tōkyō had absolutely no wartime atmosphere. Only occasionally does one see signboard advertisements on the streets, announcing lectures by right-wing groups or government-sponsored speeches. This time too, I attended several lectures held at the Hibiya Public Hall (日比谷公會堂) and saw the faces (面影) of Army Minister Itagaki (板垣陸相), Education Minister Araki (荒木文相), Agriculture Minister Arima (有馬農相), and Navy Minister Yonai (米內海相) several times. The most interesting lecture I heard was the "Meeting to Oppose the Election of the Tōkyō Metropolitan Governor" (東京都長官選反對演說會). On that day, the lion's roars (獅子吼) of dozens of impassioned Diet members (熱辯代議士), including Andō Masazumi (安藤正純), Yamaji Jōichi (山道襄一), and Kawakami Jōtarō (河上丈太郞), were truly worth hearing. Besides that, events like the "Meeting to Respond to the Soviet Union" (對蘇膺應演說會) hosted by the Kenkokukai (建國會) presented quite a tense and tumultuous scene, and at the lecture commemorating the 15th anniversary of the Great Kantō Earthquake (震災), I was greatly impressed by the dignified lectures of the government speakers.

The Issue of a Full-Time Foreign Minister and Matsuoka Yōsuke (松岡洋右)

Foreign Minister Ugaki's (宇垣外相) sudden resignation caused a great sensation (쎈세이슌) in Tōkyō society. The selection of a full-time Foreign Minister became the focus <80> of everyone's attention. Prime Minister Konoe (近衛首相) agonized over numerous recommendations and candidates, but ultimately, the renowned Prime Minister Prince Konoe is said to have made his first personnel mistake and gave up on appointing a full-time Foreign Minister.
Specifically, the Army central command recommended Mr. Matsuoka Yōsuke (松岡洋右), while the Foreign Ministry circles recommended Ambassador to Italy Shiratori (白鳥駐伊大使), former Foreign Minister Arita (有田元 外相), Ambassador to Britain Shigemitsu (重光 駐英大使), and Ambassador to the US Saitō (齊* 駐米大使). Although the public considered Mr. Matsuoka Yōsuke the most likely candidate, Finance Minister Ikeda's (池田藏相) disapproval (不肯) made Matsuoka's appointment difficult (難하였다). Eventually, formal negotiations went to Mr. Shigemitsu, but due to Mr. Shigemitsu's firm refusal (固辭), the Prime Minister ended up concurrently holding the post (兼攝) and moving forward (邁進케 되었다). Since the winter of Shōwa 8 [1933], I have had a comradely connection (同지적 結緣) with Mr. Matsuoka Yōsuke, becoming a member of the Alliance for the Dissolution of Established Political Parties (旣成政黨解消聯盟 聯盟員). I published my articles several times in its official journal (該 기관지). Even after Mr. Matsuoka became President of the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵總裁), we met whenever he came to Kyŏngsŏng. The key point of my camaraderie with Mr. Matsuoka was the slogan "Realization of youth politics" (청년정치의 실현). Indeed, isn't our desire being realized just like that? During the Foreign Minister issue this time, Mr. Matsuoka was staying in Tōkyō, and as one of Matsuoka's fans (팬), I actively encouraged and advised (積極 激勵進言) him, but I believe it will undoubtedly be realized in the not-too-distant future.

The Funeral of Minister Nagai's Mother and the Scene That Day

My relationship with Communications Minister Nagai (永井遞相) was written about in the August issue of this magazine. This time, I mentioned to Minister Nagai at his private residence that I was staying at the Kanda Hachimankan (神田八幡館) in Kanda. Courteously, Communications Minister Nagai sent a greeting in his own handwriting (친필서한). Knowing the Communications Minister, swamped (忙殺된) with national policies on electricity, aviation, and transportation, had no spare moment (寸暇), I did not meet him. Then, on September 29th, seeing the obituary notice of Communications Minister Nagai's mother's passing in the newspaper, I expressed my condolences (조의를 표하였고). On October 2nd, I attended (참열하엿다) the funeral held at Aoyama Cemetery (靑山齋場). That morning, the trams and buses in Tōkyō city were packed (만원이여서), so I had no choice but to take a taxi (택씨). It felt as if Mr. Nagai's popularity mobilized all of Greater Tōkyō. The funeral was Buddhist style, and literally, civil and military officials and Korean elders gathered like clouds (雲集). Mr. Nagai's mother's 80 years of struggling through sewing (裁縫苦鬪) had resulted in such great success (大成).

Tōkyō Scenes from the Hitler Era

What was particularly noteworthy about Tōkyō seen during these three months was the reflection of the Hitler era. Since the arrival of the Hitler Jugend (히틀러유겐트) in Tōkyō, the city was decorated with Nazi flags, and theaters spent a month featuring Hitler-centric films and musical dramas. From September 3rd, at Ueno Park (上野公園), <81> ****** opened, allowing insights into the peak of Nazism ****** Chancellor Hitler's eloquent talkie (토키) captured the ears*** of 6 million citizens. How pleased ****** would be to see first-rate beauties praising Hitler? There might never be another hero who received such *** unprecedented * renown as Mr. Hitler. Returning to the *inn, I asked the maids (女中) if they were fans of Mussolini* or Hitler. They answered in unison (一口如出) that they were *** **** fans. And when Chancellor Hitler's lion's roar (獅子吼) regarding the Czech issue resounded throughout Japan ***, the Hitler Jugend at the Dai-ichi Hotel cheered, "Ah, it's the sound of our ***! Ah, how moving! Oh, we swear allegiance!" The sudden resolution of the Sudetenland issue through the Munich conference of the four leaders truly made people recognize Hitler as a great man again. The noise overflowing from Tōkyō's streets* was filled only with "Heil Hitler" (ハイルセツトラ) **. Adolf Hitler was truly the **** of the 20th century, an unparalleled man of the hour (風雲兒) reigning over all humanity.

Sketches of Tōkyō Streets 1 & 2

One thing anyone walking the streets of Tōkyō can feel is what might be called the era of surplus women. Wherever you go, groups of stylish women (花形女群) roam about (橫行이다). Perhaps the economic and educational circumstances of mainland Japanese men delay marriage, thus producing this era of a flood of unmarried women (處女洪水時代). One day (하로는), when I was wearing a morning coat (모닝코트) and caught in the rain, two modern young women (新女性) passing by with an umbrella noticed (洞察하고) my difficulty and kindly sheltered me under their umbrella, guiding me safely (安保하야) to a car. I can never forget this memory of experiencing such warmth and courtesy. Thus, the streets (街路) of Tōkyō are streets of youth, and the excitement of rising passions (血潮) sometimes seems to turn into typhoons (風) and earthquakes.
What is especially worth writing about this time was a tremendous overnight typhoon (*一夜*風*었다). With strong winds of over 40 meters [per second] (40餘 메돌), the inn where I stayed couldn't sleep a wink, trembling with anxiety (戰戰兢兢). The fear from collapsing nearby houses (倒*), 500,000 fallen roadside trees (橫倒), suspension of transportation*, and danger to human life was truly the most terrifying storm (風塵) I had ever experienced in my life (生來 初有).
Also, the recent trend (動向) among Tōkyō citizens was economic-first-ism (經濟 第一主義). Even a small child (一小童) seemed to make every move (一擧手 一投足) with a commercial mindset. Therefore, even in bookstores (冊店), books on politics, philosophy, or biographies of heroes don't sell at all. The fact that a new book <82> by Mr. Kobayashi Ichizō (小林一三) titled "What Will Happen After the War?" (「戰後はとうなる?」) sold over 40,000 copies within a week of publication gives a glimpse into the economic desires of Tōkyōites. Just as Tōkyō shrinks under the wind of controlled (統制風) peacetime industry, concerns (懸念) about the post-war economy deepen. Even the zaibatsu (財閥) of the Marunouchi district (丸の內區) are paying attention to the national policy of gold production (産金國策), and mining fever is boiling (沸騰).
Furthermore, one could also glimpse an atmosphere where the momentum for constructing a new culture was growing strong (濃厚). Since the inauguration of Education Minister Araki (荒木文相), there has been a trend towards emphasizing emotional education (情操敎育) through the promotion and * of art. The Yūrak座 theater began to see unprecedented visits from cabinet ministers. When Agriculture Minister Arima (有馬農相) attended a play one night, Education Minister Araki also attended another night. It is said that talk about * plays even came up during cabinet meetings (閣議席上). A play script (戲曲*本), painstakingly written by Communications Minister Nagai (永井遞相) over three years, has been completed, and now a scramble (爭奪戰) has broken out (버러져서) in the Tōkyō theater world, causing Minister Nagai a headache, they say. Besides this, ** and Shōchiku (松竹) are sending girls' troupes (少女團) to Europe and America; in any case, trends towards the epoch-making construction of East Asian art are evident. Looking at political movements, the government and public were busy (奔忙中) preparing a new system (新體勢整備). Starting with the new party movements of Ministers Suetsugu (末次) and Shiono (鹽野), new party movements by figures like Akiyama Teisuke (秋山定輔), Akita Kiyoshi (秋田淸), and Maeda Yonezō (前田米藏), and the movement to form a party of Industrial Cooperative Youth (産業組合靑年 結黨運動) which Agriculture Minister Arima is passionate about – various currents are flowing beneath the surface (暗流中). Public movements centered on continental policy were also active. Just among those I observed were the formation of the Industrial Technology Alliance (産業技術聯盟結成), the Holy War Accomplishment Alliance (聖戰貫徹同盟), the National Volunteers Rally (國民有志大會), and the Imperial University Purification Alliance (帝大廓淸同盟); new organizations were sprouting like mushrooms after rain (雨後竹筍). The Central League of the National Spiritual Mobilization (國民精神總動員 中央聯盟) also underwent a major reorganization, and youth group movements are daily adopting new structures (新態勢). Generally speaking, one could only witness the phenomenon of Greater Tōkyō moving in pursuit of a new economy, new politics, and new culture. The era of Pacific civilization has arrived! The tomorrow of the new East Asia will be built with Tōkyō as its womb (母胎). This autumn, I tell myself that we youth must rise resolutely (奮然히 蹶起하야) and fulfill Heaven's mandate (天命). — (End) — <83>

**Summary**

The author, Chu Un-sŏng, recounts his extended three-month stay in Tōkyō in late 1938. He expresses deep admiration for the city, calling it the "London of the East" and finding it invigorating and welcoming. He describes sightseeing trips to Nikkō and the Yokosuka/Yokohama area, impressed by Japan's natural beauty and naval power. Chu observes Tōkyō under the "current situation" (Sino-Japanese War), noting its surprising calmness despite wartime, the pervasive influence of Nazi Germany (Hitler Jugend visit, Hitler's popularity), and the active political scene with numerous lectures and emerging political/nationalist movements aiming for a "new system." He also touches upon social aspects like the visibility of women, an instance of kindness, a severe typhoon, the strong focus on economics, and budding efforts in cultural promotion, particularly in theatre. The article concludes with a fervent call for youth to embrace the coming "Pacific civilization era" centered on Tōkyō.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing for its unabashedly pro-Japanese tone written by a Korean intellectual during the colonial period. Chu's admiration for Tōkyō, Japanese figures like Matsuoka Yōsuke (with whom he claims a "comradely connection"), and the perceived cultural and political dynamism of wartime Japan offer insight into the mindset of certain segments of the Korean elite who either collaborated or found ways to accommodate themselves within the Japanese empire. His observations on the influence of Nazism in Tōkyō, the focus on economic concerns even amidst war mobilization, and the burgeoning nationalist/statist movements provide a contemporary snapshot of Japan in 1938, reflecting the complex socio-political currents of the time that directly impacted colonial Korea.

東京遊記
						朱雲成
東京에의 愛着心
지난 7월 25일 오후 4시 15분 「아까쓰끼」를 잡어타고 余는 또 東都를 향하였다. 이번 여행은 처음 예정 2주일간이 늘*저 만3개월간 滯東의 나그네가 되었다. 그것은 용무를 성공해 가지고 오기 위함이었다. 다행으로 실패치 않고 10월 18일 오후 1시 30분에 귀경하였다. 巴人 兄으로부터 東京 印象을 쓰라는 부탁을 받고 별다른 쓸 재료를 갖이 못했으나 다만 余가憧憬하는 무대인 東京을 90晝夜 踏破하던 소감의 일단을 적어보련다.

東京은 동방의 「런던」이오 新亞細亞 文化의 총본산이라는 의미에서 余는 애착을 가진다. 余一 東京驛에만 도착하면 가슴이 넓어짐을 느끼며 어쩐지 기운이 용소슴침을 느끼군 한다. 그래서 昔日 金玉均氏의 活步헌 땅이 여기였거니 하면 더구나 쫓자 俄然 호걸남아로 化한다. 이번에도 東京에 뿌린 돈이＜78＞ 千圓을 算하며 유쾌한 거름을 것다가 조선에를 오니 사람마다 余의 身光이 좋다는 문안을 받었다. 분명히 우리는 좀더 탈을 벗고 명랑생활을 圖할 필요를 느낀다. 90일간의 爽快한 호흡이 이렇게 余의 풍채를 변하게 한 것을 보니 !
東京은 「사람을 알어주는 곳」이라는 점이 무었보다도 余의 애착심을 자아낸다. 이번 여행에서도 余는 여러번 고귀한 優待를 받었고 열혈과 정의를 사랑하는 그네들의 순수성에 감격하였다. 또한 평화와 자유를 다소간이라도 맛볼 수 있다면 동경밖에 없다고 느꼇다. 그리하야 부드러운 공기 和스런 薰香이 客子의 愁心을 풀어줌을 느꼇다.

東京 近교의 巡禮
日常 한 번 보고 싶던 일본 명소를 이번 기회에 一巡할 수 있었다. 8월 하순 어느날 淺草*門驛을 출발하야 명승지 日光을 향하였다. 오후 1시경 日光市에 다다르니 유객이 지나기만 하는 탓으로 시가는 번창할 줄 모르고 그야말노 一小市이다. 유람뻐스를 타고 명소를 달니는 도중은 어려슬 적에 국어독본에서 보던 神橋와 東照宮 등이 낯이 익었다. 返馬驛에서 뻐스를 나리니 하늘에 다은 高山頂까지 등산 케불車를 타어야 할 판이다. 전차와 비슷한데 壁上과 같은 高山을 「엉엉」 하면서 기어 올나가는 케불車는 장하기도 하나 불상해 뵈였다. 아무리 기계라 하나 대단히 수고하는 생물과 같이 뵈였다. 케불車를 나리니 이번에는 이 산정에서 저 산정간에 건너 매인 줄에 공중케불이 왔다 갔다 하는데 余는 비행기를 타보지 못했는지라 이 공중케불을 비행기 타는 흥미를 갖이고 탓다. 다음에는 또다시 뻐스를 타고 휘도라드니 아- 장엄하도다. 日光의 자랑인 中禪寺湖와 大瀑布와 海拔 8,000尺의 男體山의 雄姿이다. 余는 한없이 감탄을 連呼하였다. 이야말노 조물주의 고의적 作戱가 아니고는 이럴 수 없으리라. 해발 8,000尺 上高山頂에 蒼波萬頃의 大湖水가 있어 一望無際의 大海였다. 我等은 기선을 타고 이 대호수를 一巡하며 안내자의 고적 설명을 청취하였다. 中禪寺라는 大寺가 玲*燦爛한 건물인데 내객은 누구나 한번식 드러가 참배하는 광경이었다. 호수 구경을 마친 후 「山上 엘네베타」를 타고 直下 500여 척을 내리 달아나서니 *병자는 못볼새라 구천에서 떠러저 쏫아지는 무서운 대폭포는 천지를 뒤집을 ＜79＞형세로 白霧를 뿜는다. 余는 너무 만족하여 狂躍感嘆 하였다. 다시 승강기를 타고 올나가서 자동차를 타고 男體山의 雄峰을  앙망하면서 구비구비 산령을 내려와서 一路きぬかわ온천으로 향하였다. 여기의 장관은 500년 묵은 杉木群이 20리간을 병립했는데 이야말로 꿈나라의 관광이였다. きぬかわ온천은 세계 귀객의 운집처인데 ホテル촌이었다. 余도 일류 ホテル에 투숙하고 翌日에 東京으로 도라왔다.
다음 기회에는 태평양 연안을 휘돌아 橫須賀에 다다르니 여기는 해군의 근거지인지라 해병의 수영 광경이 훌늉하며 故 東鄕元帥의 戰勝艦이 아조 의미있는 전람회장인 바 艦內에 드러가서 진기한 日露戰爭의 역사적 진상을 일람했고 포탄에 五裂된 「皇國興廢 此一戰」의 기*를 보고 은연중 余는 늣김이 잇섯다. 이렇듯 一死를 맛본 本鄕元帥의 웅거는 동방민족의 좋은 교훈임을 통감했다.  橫濱賀를 떠나 葉山을 지나가면서 葉山御用邸를 拜觀하였다. 그리고 橫須港에 다다라 태평양의 양양한 물결을 바라보며 絶勝한 공원을 일순하야 「끄랜드 호텔」의 서양인 와중에서 식사를 하고 東京으로 도라왔다.

時局下 東京의 諸相
누구나 비상시하의 東京이 너무나 肅然無風함과 物物人人의 평온상태를 보고는 이상히 역이지 않을 수 없으리라. 「히틀러 유겐트」나 伊太利 사절단이나 카이젤令孫이나 다 一口如出로 한 말이지만 조곰도 戰時 기분이 없는 東京이다. 다만 街頭에 종종 입간판 광고를 볼 뿐인데 이는 우익단체 등의 연설회와 또는 정부 주최의 연설 등이다. 余는 今回도 日比谷公會堂에서 열린 연설회를 여러번 參聽한 바 板垣陸相, 荒木文相, 有馬農相, 米內海相 등의 面影을 수차나 보았다. 연설회 중 가장 재미있게 들은 것은 東京都長官選反對演說會였는대 이날은 安藤正純, 山道襄一, 河上丈太郞氏 외 수십 인의 熱辯代議士들의 獅子吼는 가히 들을만 하였다. 그밖에 建國會 주최의 對蘇膺應演說會 같은 것은 퍽으나 緊張騷然한 광경을 呈하였고 震災 15주년 기념 연설회에는 정부측 연사들의 점잔은 강연에 감명이 컸다.

專任外相 문제와 松岡洋右
宇垣外相의 疾風的 辭職은 동경사회에도 크다란 「쎈세이슌」을 이르켰고 專任外相의 人選에는 만인 주시의＜80＞ 的이였오며 近衛首相으로서도 다수한 추천과 후보자 등으로 고심하던 남아에 결국 명재상 近衛公도 처음으로 인선 실수를 나타내고 그만 專任外相을 단념케 되였다 한다.
즉 육군 중앙부에서는 松岡洋右씨를 추천하였고 외무당국계에서는 白鳥駐伊大使, 有田元 外相, 重光 駐英大使, 齊* 駐米大使 등을 추천이였는데 일반은 松岡洋右씨를 가장 유력시 하였으나 池田藏相의 不肯으로 松岡씨는 難하였다. 결국 重光씨에게로 정식 교섭이 갔—ˆ으나 重光씨의 固辭로 드디어 수상 兼攝으로 邁進케 되었다. 余는 昭和 8년 冬 이래로 松岡洋右씨와 동지적 結緣을 하야 旣成政黨解消聯盟 聯盟員이 되어 該 기관지에 수삼차 余의 논문을 발표하였고 松岡씨가 滿鐵總裁 취임 후에도 京城 올 적마다 회견하였다. 즉 余가 松岡씨와 동지된 요점은 「청년정치의 실현」이란 일구 표어였다. 과연 我等의 요망은 그대로 마저 나가고 있지 않느냐? 이번 외상문제 時에도 松岡씨는 동경 滯在으로 余는 松岡 팬의 1인으로 적극 激勵進言이었으나 여하간 불원한 장래에 실현될 것은 틀님없을 줄 믿는다.

永井氏 母堂 葬儀와 當日 光景
永井遞相과 나의 관계는 지난 8월호 본지에 썻거니와 이번에 가서로 永井씨 사저에 余가 神田八幡館에 유숙하는 旨를 言及이러니 정중하게도 永井遞相의 친필서한으로 문안해 주었다. 전력, 항공, 교통운수 국책에 忙殺된 遞相의 寸暇 없음을 알고 회견은 하지 않었다. 그리다가 9월 29일 신문에서 永井遞相 母堂 逝去의 부음을 보고 조의를 표하였고 10월 2일 靑山齋場에서 거행되는 장의에 참열하엿다. 이 오전중에는 동경시의 전차뻐쓰는 만원이여서 하는 수 없이 「택씨」를 타고 갓던 것이다. 永井씨의 인기가 대동경을 총동원하느 감이 있었다. 장의는 佛式이었고 문자 그대로 문무백관과 조선원로가 雲集이였는데 永井씨의 母堂이 80평생의 裁縫苦鬪의 결과가 이렇듯 大成을 함이었다.
