Ten Minutes in the President's Office
Hwasin's Young President, Full of Spirit to Conquer the Financial World

If one cannot command a million soldiers, isn't it the work of a true man to become a millionaire (長者) worth ten million Won and unfold his own plans as he pleases? Becoming a ten-million-Won millionaire is something to achieve like Lord Cecil (세실卿) of South Africa (南阿) in one's youth, in one's 20s or 30s, grasping great wealth in one's own hands. What is so grand about possessing hundreds of millions after one's bones have grown old? This young president, who became the president of Sŏn'il (鮮一) at 20 and built the great Hwasin (大和信) at 30, is already a figure destined to go down in history for that alone. Now, having established a large company worth 8 million Won and preparing to enter the active stage of international trade, dealing with Great Britain, America, Germany, and France (英米獨佛), his spirit exhales immense vigor for the peninsula's financial world, truly making the people of the time cheer.

I was guided to the president's office on the 4th floor of the new building, a room that even hosted His Highness Prince Yi (李王殿下) this past spring.
"How do you maintain your health while battling numbers like that from morning till night?"
"That's why these days I go play golf (꼴푸) at six in the morning. I've decided not to drink or smoke, and to maintain regular daily routines (起居)."
"As you expand the Hwasin organization (花信機構), are you also welcoming new personnel?"
"Yes, an American and an authority on international trade who was an executive at Murai Bank (村井銀行) have decided to join the Chain Store Department (連鎖店部). We plan to export peninsula products (반도산품) like beans from Manchukuo (만주국) and sardines (정어리) from North Korea (北鮮), and import paper products (紙類) and other goods (物産) from foreign countries in return."
"It's remarkable that you've managed to create an 8 million Won company in this era of wartime controls."
"Initially, the plan was for 10 million Won. In any case, I intend to try doing business with the world as our counterpart."
During this time, documents needing approval flew in like a shower of rain (빗발 날니듯), arriving continuously, and executives had been waiting beside him for a long time. He was that busy. <10>

President Pang, A Hero of the Peninsula's Press Dividing the Realm, Finding Leisure Amidst Busyness

One day in early winter (초동 모일), with the north wind blowing, I visited the president's office on T'aep'yŏngt'ong (太平通) street in Seoul, only to be told he had gone down to the sales director's desk and I should wait. Sitting in a corner of the aristocratically (귀족적) quiet and elegant (閑雅) president's office, adorned with calligraphy by Wich'ang (葦滄) and Simjŏn (心田), I idly imagined the appearance (풍채) of this room's master (어룬). His grand and bold (豪憺) spirit (氣慨), investing 500,000 Won into the press business immediately after gaining wealth of over a million Won from mining; his pioneering (선구자적) vision (복안) for constructing an ideal rural village (이상농촌건설) in the future through a 20-year forestry (養林) plan on 12,000 chŏngbo (정보) of forest land in Yŏnghŭng (永興); his devoted (眷眷) and pioneering (개척자적) skill (수완) in having nearly succeeded in the reclamation (개간) of 320 chŏngbo of uncultivated land (미간지) in Suwŏn (水原). I get the feeling (늣김) that this elder is also a figure who appears perhaps once in a decade or even once in a century. Just then,
"Sorry to have kept you waiting so long,"
says Mr. Pang (方氏), a man of spirit (氣骨), as he enters.
"How busy must you be, running a newspaper amidst wartime paper controls (용지통제) and press controls (언론통제)?"
"Not at all. We carry on according to the tension (긴장) of wartime if it's wartime, and as usual in peacetime if it's peacetime. The newspaper is one thing, but how have you managed to keep 'Samch'ŏlli' (삼천리) going for 10 years?"
"Yes, thank you. By the way, you undertake many brilliant (빗나는) projects like the music competition (음악콩쿨), film competition (영화콩쿨), and the Local Culture Survey Team (향토문화조사대). Could you, sir, perhaps make these permanent and establish something like a Culture Prize (문화상) or Arts Prize (예술상) to serve as a driving force for promoting (推獎力) culture in this land?"
"Well, I always have such thoughts. Anything can be done if there are just three things: talented people (인재), money, and sincerity (정성). Let's wait and see."
Even during this brief exchange, it seemed visitors (내객) were constantly arriving, as a female employee (여급사) brought in a new business card (명함). Promising to return later (후기), I hurriedly withdrew (물너나오다). <11>

**Summary**

This article presents brief interviews with two prominent Korean figures in 1938: Pak Hŭng-sik, the young president of Hwasin Department Store, and Pang Ŭng-mo, the president of the Chosun Ilbo newspaper. Pak discusses his ambitious plans to expand Hwasin, including international trade despite wartime restrictions, and his personal health regimen. Pang reflects on managing his newspaper under colonial controls and his significant investments in cultural and development projects, such as forestry and land reclamation, alongside cultural initiatives like competitions and surveys. The interviewer encourages Pang to establish permanent cultural awards. For historians, the article offers insights into the mindset and activities of elite Korean businessmen and media figures under Japanese rule. It highlights their adaptation to wartime conditions, their engagement in large-scale capitalist ventures (including international trade and resource development), and their efforts to foster Korean culture through private initiatives, revealing complex dynamics of collaboration, resistance, and nation-building within the colonial context.

社長室의 十分間
財界 制覇의 氣魄에 찬 和信의 靑年社長
백만의 병사를 거느리지 못하겟거든 차라리 천만원의 長者가 되어 제 경륜을 제맘대로 펴보는 것이 엇지 사내 대장부의 일이 아니랴. 천만원 長者되는 것도, 저 南阿의 세실卿 모양으로 2·30세의 젊은 시절에 大金을 제 손에 휘-감어 쥘 일이라, 老骨이 다 된 뒤에 억만금을 가진들 무에 壯하랴, 20세에 鮮一社長, 30세에 大和信을 건설한 이 靑年社長은 그것만으로도 이미 史上에 오를 인물이어든, 이제 800만원의 큰회사를 창설하고, 英米獨佛를 상대로, 국제무역의 活舞臺에까지 등장하려 차림하고 잇는 氏의 氣宇는 반도재계를 위하야 만장의 기염을 토하야, 正히 時人을 환호케 한다.
今春에는 李王殿下까지, 이 방으로 모신 적 잇다는, 신관 4층의 사장실에 안내를 밧자
「그러케 아츰부터 밤까지 숫자와 싸우시면서 엇더케 건강을 유지하서요?」
「그러기에 요지음은 아츰 여섯시에 꼴푸하려가요, 술 담배 안 하기로 하고, 그리고 규칙잇는 起居를 하기로 하엿지요」
「花信機構를 늘니는 만치 새로운 인물를 또 영접합니까」
「네, 連鎖店部에는 米國사람과 또 村井銀行의 중역으로 잇고 국제무역의 권위인 한 분이  오시기로 하엿서요, 만주국의 콩과 北鮮의 정어리 등 반도산품을 내보내고 외국에서 그 대신, 紙類와 다른 物産을 드려 오려해요」
「이 전시통제시대에 용하게 800만원 회사를 만드섯군요」
「처음엔 1,000만원 작정이엿서요, 아무튼 세계를 상대하고 장사하여 보려함니다」
이 사이에 결재서류는 빗발 날니듯, 연해 드러오고, 중역들은 겻헤 와 기다린지 오래다, 그러케 밧부섯다.＜10＞

三分天下하야 半島言論의 雄인 忙中有閑인 方社長
삭풍 날니는 초동 모일, 서울 太平通 사장실을 즉한즉 마츰 영업국장석에 내려가섯다고 기다리라 한다. 葦滄의 書, 心田의 書로 차린 귀족적 閑雅한 사장실 一隅에 안저 나는 無聊한대로 이방 어룬의 풍채를 공상하여 보다. 백여만원을 광산에서 득재하자, 50만원을 언론사업에 투한 그 豪憺한 氣慨, 永興에 12,000정보의 임야를 20개년계획으로 養林하여 장차로는 이상농촌건설의 그 선구자적 복안, 水原의 320정보의 미간지개간을 거지반 성공식힌 眷眷한 개척자적 수완, 이 어룬도 10년만이나 100년만에 한번 나타날 인물이란 늣김을 밧는다, 이럴때에
「오래 기다리서서 미안함니다」
하고 드러오는 이, 氣骨의 士 方氏라
「전시하로 용지통제, 언론통제의 속에서 신문하여 나가기 얼마나 밧부심니까」
「무얼요, 전시면 전시의 긴장대로, 평시면 평시대로 하여나가지요, 신문도 신문이려니와, 「삼천리」를 엇더케 10년이나 끄으러 왓어요?」
「네, 감사함니다, 그런데 음악콩쿨 영화콩쿨 향토문화조사대 등 빗나는 사업을 만히 하시는데, 이제 선생께서 그를 항구화 식허 문화상 예술상 가튼 것을 정하여 이 땅 문화의 推獎力을 삼게하여 주실 수 업슴닛까,
「글세요, 늘 그런 생각도 가지고 잇서요 모든 일이란 인재와 돈과 정성의 세가지만 잇스면 되니까요, 어데 두고 봅세다」
이 사이에도 내객이 연해 잇는 듯, 여급사가 새 명함을 갓다드린다, 나는 후기를 약하고 급급히 물너나오다.＜11＞
