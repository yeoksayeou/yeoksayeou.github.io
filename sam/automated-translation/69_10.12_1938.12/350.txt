Article Title: 受難의 記錄(5) (Record of Suffering (5))
Author: 兪鎭午
Article Type: 소설 (Novel)

Record of Suffering
						Yu Chin-o
Synopsis up to the previous issue
Semin, a young scholar researching "The Problem of Korean Agriculture" in a university research lab, and Aera, a female reporter for Chungang P'yŏngnon Sa (中央評論社), come to admire each other. However, when Aera mentions Semin's former classmate, Haerim, who is now in prison, Semin comes to think of Aera as Haerim's lover. Furthermore, being a married man with a wife and child, concerns about various matters prevent their relationship from developing further.
As the deadline for leaving the research lab approaches, Semin contemplates his future path. His professor, aware of Semin's leanings yet valuing his talent, one day suggests recommending him for a position as a professor at K High School of Commerce <214> and encourages him to write a thesis for it. Semin hesitates between pursuing the path he desires and accepting the professor's offer but decides to follow the professor's advice for the time being.
Aera, blessed with natural beauty (天稟) and gaining fame (評判) for a novel she began writing after being released from prison, receives advances from several men. Among them is a critic named Ilsu, who, after repeatedly failing to seduce Aera, resorts to vile slander out of spite. Deeply hurt by this, Aera's pre-existing tuberculosis worsens, and she eventually coughs up blood (咯血). While nursing her, Semin and Aera share their first kiss (接吻).
After that day, their relationship rapidly deepens, but being solely intellectual (이지적) types, Semin and Aera's connection seems confined to the realm of ideas (관념). Meanwhile, Ilsu reappears, this time seriously attempting to win Aera over, not just out of simple ambition. Intimidated by his forcefulness, Semin declares his intention to get a divorce and formally marry Aera, but Aera demurs, citing her health.
Despite this, Ilsu's courtship of Aera continues relentlessly. Semin initially feels only displeasure and hostility towards him, but gradually begins to feel a suffocating sense of being overwhelmed by Ilsu's superhuman physicality. One day, Ilsu sends Aera an utterly bizarre love letter. Feeling unbearable disgust at its primitive, beastly (야수적) scent, Semin leaves Ilsu in Aera's room and returns to his own home.
After a sleepless night, Semin goes to see Aera early the next morning. Aera, lying down with her head wrapped, bursts into tears upon seeing Semin. Ilsu arrives and insults (侮辱) Semin like a victor, leading to a fistfight (白薄戰) between the two men. However, Semin is no match for Ilsu and is easily thrown out of the room (축출).
Still wanting to know Aera's true feelings, Semin tries every means possible, but for some reason, Aera avoids meeting him. <215>

Returning to his desk in the research lab, Semin felt unbearably uneasy. Why couldn't Aera understand his feelings? Why wouldn't she frankly tell him about the incident with An Ilsu? No, why did she even avoid creating an opportunity to talk about it? Perhaps it was difficult for Aera to speak of it, and perhaps hearing it would only hurt Semin more. But whatever had happened, Semin desperately wanted to hear the story from Aera's own lips. He wouldn't mind suffering the pain of having his flesh scraped away for it. He wouldn't mind banging his head against a wall. What did such things matter?
Perhaps—he wondered. Had Aera's affection for Semin already cooled? Such a thing wasn't impossible either. Contrasting Semin's and Ilsu's personalities, it even seemed plausible that Ilsu might be a more fitting partner for Aera. With Semin, they could become close initially, but it might be difficult to go further, while <212> conversely, with Ilsu, they might hate each other to the end yet find it impossible to separate. But, Semin thought again. Isn't this just desk-bound fantasy? Even if something had happened overnight, wasn't it unimaginable that Aera's heart would shift entirely to Ilsu in an instant? He recalled, one by one, the various moments he had shared with Aera since last winter. The night they first kissed, the time they discussed plans for marriage, and the cozy little dinner party on Christmas Eve—all were ordinary, perhaps infinitely so, but the quiet sincerity within them seemed undeniable. Even if there wasn't the blazing passion of a furnace, wasn't there affection like the bright sunlight?
"The memories of the joyful times I've spent since knowing you, sir, will never be forgotten..."
Semin recited the final letter he received from Aera once more, but could find nothing in it but a heart-wrenching plea.
Finally, Semin closed his book, stood up, and went out into the courtyard.
It was already mid-February, and the campus, where the tip of his nose had stung just a few days prior, was filled with warm spring light. It was examination time, so the entire school was as quiet as underwater. A few students lay on the clover, basking in the sun (해볓), reading their notes. Over there, someone had covered their face with notes and fallen asleep right on the grass.
Spring is here! The thought swelled up, filling his chest completely. And then, Semin suddenly remembered the school greenhouse, which he hadn't visited all winter. Until last autumn, whenever he felt tired from reading, he would often go and gaze into it for a while. Semin slowly crossed the yard and rounded the corner of the law faculty building.
The greenhouse at the bottom of the slope also seemed asleep, bathed in warm sunlight. Through the cleanly wiped glass panes, hyacinths, cyclamens, and others were in full bloom—pink, white, purple, jade green—a dazzling array of colors shone before his eyes like a beautiful dream. For quite a while, Semin stood peering into the greenhouse as if engaged in deep research, but the brilliant colors and lovely flower shapes merely passed across the distant sky of his mind, never penetrating the thick fog enveloping his thoughts.
As he stood there like a man lost in thought,
"What are you looking at so intently?"
Someone suddenly tapped him on the shoulder (억개) and spoke. Startled, he turned around to see Yasuda from the Civil Law research lab. <213>
"They bloomed so beautifully—"
Semin replied with a faint smile.
"They certainly are lovely,"
Yasuda, drawn in, also peered into the greenhouse for a moment.
"Ah—it's really warm,"
He yawned, stretched, and said,
"Are you busy now? If not, let's go grab some coffee (고-히)."
Yasuda's face was beaming, as if something good had happened.
"I'm not busy. Let's go."
Semin replied, and together with Yasuda, they crossed the campus again towards the cafeteria in the medical faculty compound.
Yasuda had graduated from the Tōkyō Imperial University faculty, served as an assistant there for a year, and transferred here last spring. A formidable talent, Yasuda, who had been highly regarded there, naturally wouldn't have moved here without reason, and it was widely assumed he would be promoted to lecturer or assistant professor once his term was up. Consequently, since coming here, he hadn't particularly associated with fellow researchers but rather mingled with the professors, though Semin was an exception, and they maintained a relatively close relationship. A primary reason was that Yasuda's specialty, economic law—attempting to study law from the perspective of economics—was closely related to Semin's field of study. Also, Yasuda seemed to hold sympathy and curiosity for Semin, whose academic record at this university was said to be outstanding. Yasuda, reputedly the son of a wealthy family even in Tōkyō, had already dragged Semin around the nightlife of Sŏul two or three times. <214>
The contrast between the two men sitting opposite each other drinking tea was as stark as light and shadow. Yasuda, his sturdy frame smartly clad in a British homespun (홈스팡) suit, a light jade-colored pinstriped dress shirt, a flamboyant necktie he said he bought for eight won at "Zaitō-ya" (좌이도-야), topped by a well-groomed, healthy-complexioned face. Facing him, Semin, whose health wasn't even good, looked all the more shabby. Sunken cheeks, a soiled collar, a wrinkled suit, and more than that, the indelible shadow of deep anguish clouding his face—
Yasuda gulped down his coffee in one go, lit a cigarette, and said,
"Since we happened to meet (맛났으니) like this, let me tell you, my matter is now officially decided."
"Hmm?"
When Semin didn't understand,
"My position at this university, I mean."
"Really?"
Semin said, gazing at Yasuda's face across the table as if dazzled. Yasuda continued,
"It shouldn't be announced externally yet, but it was formally approved at the faculty meeting yesterday, and the president's final approval came through today."
"That's wonderful. I knew it would happen, but is it Civil Law?" <215>
"Yes, but just for this year, Professor S is traveling abroad, so they asked me to teach Labor Law instead."
"Well, that doesn't matter much. Anyway, congratulations."
Semin congratulated him verbally, but his heart grew darker and darker. It wasn't out of envy or jealousy of Yasuda's success—if that were the case, Semin would have gratefully accepted the position at K High School of Commerce his supervising professor had tried to secure for him. Rather, it was envy of the shadowless, sunlit happiness of Yasuda's life. Though they had graduated university in the same year and were the same age, one was like a tropical palm tree shooting up to pierce the sky, while the other was like a flimsy reed emerging from the snow in the harsh northern plains, clinging frozen to the ice.
"How many hours?"
"Four hours."
"What's the title? Assistant Professor (조교수)?"
"Lecturer for the time being, they say."
"What does it matter if it's lecturer? Anyway, well done. Blessings."
Parting ways in the research lab corridor, Semin returned to his own lab, but his agitated mind couldn't find its bearings. Aera was avoiding him, and the professor who offered to recommend him for the K High School post had become angry. True, if he resumed writing the "Ricardo Study" now and presented it to the professor, there might still be some way. But for Semin at this moment, forget being a high school professor; even if offered a university professorship alongside Yasuda, he lacked the energy to write a single line.
As if searching for something, but actually without any purpose, he sat rummaging through his bookshelf when there was a knock at the door, and Yasuda, from whom he had just parted, entered.
"Are you busy? If you're not busy, let's just head out. The weather's nice too."
"Are you treating?"
"I am. Let's go have dinner somewhere, maybe some 'honbura' (strolling aimlessly)."
"Dinner won't be enough. It's a treat for becoming at least a university lecturer."
"Lecturer treat? Hahaha, are you being cynical?" <216>
"Not really, but I feel like drinking. If you're treating, buy me alcohol."
"Alcohol?"
Yasuda looked at Semin's gloomy face.
"Is there ever a time you ask for alcohol first?"
"I sometimes feel like drinking too."
Semin spoke nonchalantly, but inside, he felt like bursting. Alcohol! That clear liquid with its yellowish hue tempted Semin's heart at that moment like the breath of a mysterious seductress. He felt like if he could just guzzle down alcohol, lie down in some roadside ditch, and shout at the top of his lungs, his insides would feel refreshed.
Yasuda and Semin went "honbura," ate chicken dishes at a place called "Kikuya," and by the time they had visited a couple of bars, it was already around eleven o'clock. Yasuda was still quite sober, but Semin, having drunk a bit too much, felt extremely unwell inside. The anguish he wanted to forget refused to be forgotten; the drunker he got, the clearer it became, until eventually, it felt as though nothing else existed in the world but that anguish. If he were alone, without Yasuda, he felt like collapsing in a ditch and just shouting.
Passing by a secondhand bookstore in M-jŏng, Yasuda took out his watch.
"Now's the perfect time. Hey, let's stop by one more place."
"Oh god, I'm suffering. I can't do any more."
"No, just one more place. This time I'll guide you to a really good spot. It's a habit I've picked up; when I drink, I feel like dancing (딴스)."
"Dancing? Is there dancing in Sŏul?"
At that time, rumors were rife about whether dance halls would be permitted in Sŏul or not, but Semin knew they weren't yet allowed.
"Of course, there is. I discovered it by chance the other day. The girls are pretty, and it's absolutely fantastic."
"Well—"
The mention of dancing piqued Semin's curiosity, and he followed Yasuda. <217>
Yasuda led the way, winding through the back alleys of M-jŏng. It was a narrow, dark, and dirty area where one wouldn't expect to find any place of amusement. According to Yasuda's explanation, the bar allowed waitresses and customers to dance, for which it had already been fined twice. However, because dancing attracted so many customers, the profits far exceeded the fines tenfold, so the owner, having gotten a taste for it, continued to let customers dance while avoiding the police's eyes.
"They'll probably have their license revoked soon, but who knows? Before that, we might as well get some dancing in."
Yasuda, walking ahead and talking, suddenly gestured "Shh-" and stopped in front of a stone-pillared gate. Looking up, Semin saw a square red lantern hanging above the pillar with the words "Bar Parisienne" (빠-파리젠느). But the building itself didn't look like a bar at all. Farther in, a Western-style entrance resembling that of a wealthy residence was visible. Between the entrance and this side gate ran a path paved with coarse gravel mixed with cement, and on both sides of the path, evergreen trees like fir, juniper, and arborvitae were densely planted.
"How about it? Pretty grand, right? Apparently, some kabuki actor (가부야?) struck it rich (나리깅) and built it showing off (호풍)."
Thick curtains covered the windows, obscuring the view inside, but as they approached the entrance, the slow melody of a waltz mixed with muffled voices drifted out.
Feeling as though entering some den of vice, his steps hesitated slightly, but summoning his courage, he followed Yasuda inside.
The pungent smell of alcohol, thick cigarette smoke, the shadows of people dancing embraced by women—at first, Semin was so bewildered he couldn't make anything out. Only after settling into a soft armchair at a table in a corner did he slowly begin to survey the room. A marble counter, a luxurious chandelier whose light reflected dazzlingly off various liquor bottles of all colors, a deep crimson carpet (털뇨) covering the floor—everything was on a scale of luxury unimaginable in Sŏul.
"Was there really a place like this in Sŏul?"
Semin marveled, wiping his hands with a towel brought by a woman in a red satin evening dress (이브닝).
"Impressive, isn't it? I was surprised too when I first came. Even in Tōkyō, there aren't bars like this."
There seemed to be at least twenty customers. Only six or seven couples were dancing, but the hall wasn't very large to begin with, so even that made the whole room feel like it was spinning. Those not dancing sat at their respective tables, chatting loudly, drinking, smoking. It was pandemonium. Among the customers were a few Semin could recognize. The painter P, a professor at KS Professional School <218> and a millionaire; the famous playboy R, who supposedly went all the way to Paris to pursue his debauchery... Everyone seemed drunk, indulging as if today was the end of the world.
"Where's Yuri-chan?"
Yasuda asked the waitress assigned to their table, named Kazuko.
"She's over there. Shall I call her over?"
Kazuko pointed towards a corner in the distance. Through a gap between potted plants, a woman in a maroon evening dress with a large red rose pinned to her chest could be seen, drunk, eyes closed, half-leaning against a customer.
"Drunk again."
"Of course, she is. Customers keep pouring strong Western liquor down her throat, a girl who can't even hold her drink. How could she not be? It's her fault too for accepting when offered, but..."
"What's wrong with that?"
"If it's not wrong, how can her body hold up? Every evening when the bar (가개) closes, she inevitably has to be carried and put to bed—"
She seemed unconditionally sympathetic towards the woman called "Yuriko."
Just then, the music started again. "Dark Eyes," a Russian folk song arranged in a slow tango rhythm.
From here and there, couples embracing each other flowed onto the center of the floor.
"Shall we dance once?"
Yasuda also stood up, took Kazuko in his arms, and spun into the crowd.
Semin, feeling sick from the alcohol and his head reeling from the chaotic scene as if struck by a mallet, was utterly disoriented. Amidst this, fierce self-loathing arose for finding himself sitting amidst such a scene. Glancing over, he saw Yasuda dancing with considerable enthusiasm amidst the crowd. Beside him, striking the uniquely erotic pose of the tango, held in the arms of a large Western man, swayed the woman they called Yuriko, like a willow branch bending this way and that in the wind. She might have been drunk, but the sight of her, waist tightly held, body yielded to the man, stepping dreamily to the sensuous rhythm, was almost unbearable to watch with sober eyes. <219>
As he watched, the flow of dancers gradually brought Yuriko's couple towards Semin's table. In an instant—
"Ah!"
Semin almost cried out in shock. The woman called Yuriko was none other than Matsuura Chiyoko, whom he had briefly met at Aera's house just three months ago! At first, Semin doubted his eyes, but no matter how much everything else had changed, it was unmistakably Chiyoko.
However, Chiyoko didn't look his way and continued to drift towards the other side, still held in the man's arms as if dreaming.
"Ah, excuse me. Here, drink up."
When the music ended, Yasuda returned to the table and offered Semin a drink.
"Yes,"
Semin lifted his glass and asked,
"By the way, that woman, Yuriko... what kind of woman is she?"
"Why, have you fallen for her? I've fallen for her too, this is trouble! Ah well, fall for her if you want. She's the number one here. Pretty, isn't she? This place is thriving all thanks to her."
"Since when did she start working here?"
"Well, about a month, maybe?"
From the side, Kazuko interjected,
"Oh my, please have a drink and talk. I'm a woman too, why are you saying I'm useless?"
"Hey, what's this jealousy? You have to pour the drink for me to drink it."
Judging by their banter, Yasuda seemed quite experienced in these matters. Yasuda downed another freshly poured glass and continued,
"By the way, I meant to explain earlier but couldn't. It's a lie that I've fallen for her, but it's true I'm interested in her. Until just recently, forget alcohol, she was so shy she seemed like a complete virgin, but in just a few days, the way she's rapidly changing is... how should I put it? Anyway, it's astonishing. She guzzles alcohol, lets customers embrace her. She's completely decadent (떼카당) now. When she's not drunk, talking to her reveals she's quite intelligent and intellectual, so there must be some reason. Let's just watch <220> and see how far she'll fall."
Just then, the music struck up again. This time, it was a kick step (킥스텦).
"I'll bring her over here later,"
Yasuda said, then stood up again, took Kazuko, and went to dance.
Left alone at the table, Semin's head spun again because of Chiyoko. The memory of meeting Chiyoko for the first time last late autumn felt as vivid as yesterday. The Chiyoko then, fresh as an apple, and the Chiyoko now, in this secret dance hall (무도장), offering her drunken body to countless men (뭇사내), dancing madly until dawn—what sort of transformation was this? Chiyoko's partner this time was another unfamiliar man. It was a kick step, so not as lewd a dance as before, but the light taps—tuk tuk tak-tadak tuk tak-tadak tak-tadak—of her steps seemed like those of someone who had honed her skills through months or even years of dancing. Watching Chiyoko dance, Semin recalled the story of her circumstances he had heard from her back then. Then, he suddenly remembered her words of self-abandonment as they parted at the streetcar stop:
"It'll work out somehow. Would a girl starve to death somewhere?"
"Hmm—"
Only then did the path Chiyoko had trodden during that time seem to connect (련락), however faintly. Thinking about it, perhaps it wasn't something to be viewed with surprise, but rather a natural course.
Semin's eyes followed Chiyoko's movements. Her hair, cut short at the nape (목우?) and scattered carelessly; her lips, painted cherry red, half-parted; her eyes, also half-closed as if dreaming—was this her habit when dancing? Her evening dress trailed to the floor, and beneath it, her high heels (하이힐) occasionally peeked out. When her partner whispered (쏘군쏘군) something in her ear, Chiyoko would glance up and smile faintly.
When the dance ended, Yasuda indeed brought Chiyoko over to Semin's table.
"You didn't come, did I not come?"
Chiyoko seemed to be chiding Yasuda for not visiting the bar often.
Semin remained seated, unsure whether he should look at Chiyoko or not. He wanted to lower his head and hide his face, or if possible, just slip away. <221>
As she sat down next to Yasuda on the opposite sofa, Chiyoko's gaze finally met Semin's.
"Ara!"
Her face showed great surprise.
"What's this? I bring her over intending to make introductions, and you already know each other?"
Yasuda, also surprised, looked back and forth between Semin's and Chiyoko's faces.
"And yet you pretended not to know and asked me about her earlier. Tsk, honestly."
"It's not like that, just once before—"
As Semin tried to explain, Chiyoko gave him a look to stop him.
"Yes, I saw you somewhere before, didn't I? Somewhere—"
Her attempt to cover up was remarkably skillful.
"So Yuri-chan is deceiving me too?"
"Deceiving? What deceiving? Here, give me a drink."
Looking extremely tired, she slumped onto the sofa and held out her glass to Yasuda.
"Alcohol! Of course, I'll give you some. Sorry it's not whiskey."
"Hahahaha, then please give me whiskey."
"Really?"
"Really."
Yasuda called the waiter (뽀이) and ordered whiskey.
"Since when have you been here?"
Semin asked in the meantime.
"A little over a month."
Her reply was listless.
"Before that?" <222>
"I was at home."
Chiyoko emphasized the word 'home'. Wasn't this the Chiyoko who had previously lamented having no place to belong, neither in heaven nor on earth?
The whiskey arrived.
Holding the glass (그라스) filled to the brim with whiskey, Chiyoko said,
"Forgive me,"
and as soon as she put it to her lips, she gulped it all down in one go. The muscles in her throat moving with each swallow strangely caught Semin's eye*. After finishing the alcohol, she picked up a glass of cold water and gulped down several mouthfuls.
Just then, the music started again.
"Your turn to dance this time,"
Yasuda urged Semin to dance.
"I don't even know how to hold someone."
"Who knows how to dance when they dance? Just dance however you like. Give it a try."
"No, I really don't know how."
"Then dance with me,"
Yasuda stood up, holding Chiyoko.
Watching the crowd of dancers, Semin was again lost in various thoughts about Chiyoko. It was entirely conceivable that a woman with nowhere to turn might step onto such a path when cornered. But when did she learn to dance? When did she learn to drink like that? Her heavy makeup, impossible to look at directly except under dim light; her dismissive way of speaking—she was now completely a flower that blooms at night, and the most alluring flower at that. The member of the secret reading circle from three months ago and the dancing Chiyoko of tonight—there was a gap between them that couldn't be bridged by environmental forces alone. Semin replayed his memories from three months ago. The various conversations at Aera's house, the time they walked together to the streetcar stop, and then suddenly, the image of her face at the moment she boarded the streetcar, turning back to smile faintly at Semin who remained behind, rapidly "closed up" in his mind. That expression at that moment, impossible to manufacture, ****** which gave the beholder an impulse to chew her up—thinking back, perhaps Chiyoko possessed the nature of a femme fatale (妖婦) from birth*? <223>
When the dance finished, Yasuda brought Chiyoko back to the table again.
"Ah, I'm so tired. Give me another drink."
Though her legs were unsteady, Chiyoko sat on the sofa and immediately asked for more alcohol.
"Sure, sure."
Chiyoko unhesitatingly accepted and drank the whiskey Yasuda poured. As Semin stared intently, she said,
"Hahaha. I drink well, don't I? Like a crazy woman, right? Hahaha."
It was the laugh of self-abandonment, tinged with hysteria.
"You've changed a lot."
"I have changed. Hahaha, but," she spat out the words,
"Isn't that just life? Right?"
"Well—"
"What's all this philosophy of life? Here, pour more drinks. Pour more drinks."
As Semin watched, Yasuda and Chiyoko exchanged words and downed another glass each.
Just then, another customer loudly called for "Yuri-chan," and Chiyoko went over there.
"Does she always drink like that?"
Semin asked.
"I wouldn't know, but tonight she's actually quite composed. When she gets worked up, it's total chaos—the other night, she even slapped a customer's cheek. Apparently because he kissed her while dancing, or something like that."
"Slapped his cheek?"
"And yet, when she's in the mood, she burrows right into men's arms, so who knows what her deal is."
"Hmm."
Semin picked up his drink. <224>
"But then again, when she gets really drunk, she often just sobs and sobs. Once she starts that, who can stop her?"
The jazz music started again, loud and boisterous, and people began dancing once more. As the night deepened, "Bar Parisienne" increasingly became a crucible of frenzy. People's steps were now staggering, treading on partners' feet, bumping into neighbors' shoulders; the dancing crowd resembled a tangled, spinning ball of yarn. Semin searched for Chiyoko amidst the throng. Her partner this time was the Westerner she had danced with earlier. The potent whiskey coursing through her body, Chiyoko's steps now lost all form, landing haphazardly anywhere. Her partner, seizing the opportunity, held her tighter, breathing heavily, then suddenly switched to rapid, rough steps, spinning Chiyoko around recklessly like playing with jacks. Over there, someone broke a goblet; here, someone shattered a flowerpot—
"Let's go now."
Seeing Yasuda returning to the table, Semin suggested leaving.
"Wait, just one more dance."
After another round of dancing finished,
"Let's go now."
He urged again.
"Wait, just one dance with Chiyoko—"
And so, it was nearly one o'clock when Semin and Yasuda finally got up to leave.
The crowd was becoming even more frenzied and playful. Chiyoko had been dancing with the Westerner who hadn't let go of her for a while, but somehow she saw them, pushed him away, and hurried over.
"Leaving already?"
Her legs wobbled, but her face was smiling.
"It's because Yuri-chan didn't come over."
"There you go again. What could I do? He wouldn't let go."
"Well, see you again." <225>
Following Yasuda, Semin nodded goodbye to Chiyoko with his eyes and headed out the door.
"Well then, goodbye. Please come again."
From behind, Chiyoko bid them farewell and quickly slipped something into Semin's pocket. Touching it, he felt a small piece of paper (조히쪼각).
Inside the car, Semin stealthily took it out. On the inner wrapper of a "Pigeon" (피죵) cigarette pack, written in pencil, was:
"I'd like to see you quietly. Would you come to Ch'ŏngmoktang tomorrow at one P.M.? I'll be waiting."
Though he didn't know what she wanted to say, Semin felt a sweet yet fading stimulation course through his entire body.
(To be continued) <226>

**Summary**

This installment continues the story of Semin, an intellectual struggling with his feelings for Aera and his professional anxieties. After being rejected by Aera and failing to secure a stable academic position, Semin feels increasingly alienated. His friend Yasuda, a successful colleague, gets a university lectureship, highlighting Semin's precarious situation. Semin asks Yasuda to buy him drinks, leading to a night out culminating at a clandestine dance bar, "Bar Parisienne." There, Semin is shocked to find Matsuura Chiyoko, a young woman he met briefly before, now working as a popular, hard-drinking, seemingly cynical bar hostess named Yuriko. The encounter deepens Semin's sense of confusion and despair about the paths lives can take. The chapter ends with Chiyoko secretly passing Semin a note asking to meet him privately.

From a historical perspective, the story offers glimpses into urban Korean society under Japanese rule in the late 1930s. It portrays the anxieties of Korean intellectuals navigating limited opportunities within the colonial system, contrasted with the relative success of Japanese colleagues like Yasuda. The mention of tuberculosis (a common affliction then), the dilemma over divorce, the emergence of Western-style nightlife (secret dance bars operating despite restrictions), and the stark social descent potentially faced by women like Chiyoko provide insights into the social pressures, changing mores, and economic hardships of the era. The depiction of the bar—its luxury, mixed clientele (Koreans, Japanese, Westerners), and atmosphere of hedonism and desperation—reflects the complex social dynamics and modernization occurring in colonial Seoul.

受難의 記錄
						兪鎭午
前號까지의 梗槪
대학연구실에서 「朝鮮農業問題」를 연구하고 있는 젊은 학도 세민은 中央評論社 여기자 「애라」와 서로 사모하는 사이가 된다. 그러나 「세민」의 그 前同窓요 지금 옥중에 있는 「해림」의 이야기를 애라가 한 것이 機緣이 되여 세민은 애라를 해림의 애인으로 생각하게 되고 또 그에게는 처자가 있는 몸이라 이것저것에 대한 掛念으로 두사람의 사이는 그 이상 발전이 되지 안는다.
세민은 연구실을 나올 기한이 박두해 그 후의 처신에 대해 여러가지로 생각하고 있는데 그의 경향을 알면서도 그의 재간을 사랑하는 대학교수는 어느날 세민에게 K高商 교수로 추천해줄 뜻을＜214＞ 말하고 그것을 위한 논문을 쓰라고 권한다. 세민은 스스로 나가고 싶은 방면을 쫓일까 교수의 말을 들을까 생각했으나 우선 교수의 권고를 듣기로 한다.
애라는 天稟의 미모와 출옥 후에 쓰기 시작한 소설이 評判이 되어 여러 사내들로부터 유혹을 받는다. 그 중에 「일수」라는 평론가가 있어 여러번 애라를 유혹하다가 안되니까 화푸리로 애라에 대해 가진 야비한 中傷을 다한다. 그것에 몹시 상심한 애라는 전부터 있든 폐결핵이 도저서 드디어 咯血을 한다. 이 병간호가 기연이 되어 세민과 애라는 최초의 接吻을 한다.
그날 이후 두 사람의 사이는 급속도로 친밀해젔으나 오로지 이지적인 세민과 같은 타잎의 애라와의 사이는 암만해도 관념의 세계를 추구할 뿐이다. 그러는 중에 다시 일수가 낱아나 이번에는 단순한 야심이 아니라 본격적으로 애라를 획득하랴 대어든다. 그 迫力에 겁이 난 세민은 이혼을 단행하고 애라와 정식으로 결혼할 뜻을 말했으나 애라는 건강을 이유로 대답을 흐린다.
그러는 중에도 애라에 대한 일수의 求戀은 끈기차게 계속된다. 세민은 처음에는 그에게 불쾌와 적의를 느낄 뿐이었으나 나종에는 차차로 그의 초인적인 육체성에서 숨맥히는 壓倒感을 느낀다. 어느날 일수는 기괴하기 짝이 없는 러브레터를 애라에게 보낸다. 그 원시적 야수적인 냄새에 참을 수 없는 불쾌를 느낀 세민은 일수를 애라의 방에 남겨둔 채 자기 집으로 도라간다.
不眠의 밤을 지내고 이튼날 아첨 일직이 세민은 애라를 찾어간다. 애라는 머리를 싸고 누었다가 세민을 보자 느껄 울기 시작한다. 그곳에 일수가 와서 승리자나 같이 세민을 侮辱한 것이 원인이 되어 두사람은 白薄戰을 한다. 그러나 세민은 일수의 적이 아니라 문제없이 방밖으로 축출을 당한다.
그래도 애라의 진정을 알고 싶어 세민은 가진 수단을 다 썼으나 일웬인지 애라는 세민을 맞나기를 피한다.＜215＞

연구실 책상 앞으로 도라왔으나 세민은 마음속이 편치않어 견딜 수 없었다. 애라는 웨 자기의 마음을 몰나주는 것인가 웨 탁 터놓고 안일수와의 사건을 말해주지 않는 것인가 아니 웨 그런 말을 할 기회조차 맨들기를 피하는 것인가. 애라로서는 말하기 어려운 일인지도 몰으고 듯는 세민으로서도 점점 더 마음만 상케 될는지도 몰으기는 한다. 그러나 무슨 일이 있드라도 세민은 꼭 애라의 입으로부터 이야기를 듯고 싶었다. 그 때문에 살을 깎어내는 괴로움을 겪어도 좋다. 머리통을 벽에 부디처도 좋다. 그런 것이 무엇이랴.
혹은 ― 하고 생각해 본다. 애라는 이미 세민에 대한 애정을 시켜버린 것이나 아닐까. 그런 일도 있을 수 없는 일은 또한 아니다. 세민과 일수의 성격을 대조해 본다면 애라의 상대자로서 도리혀 일수가 인연이 있을상 싶기도 하기는 하다. 세민과는 우선은 가까운 사이가 될 수 있으되 그 이상은 나가기 어려우나 이와＜212＞ 반대도 일수와는 끝까지 서루 미워하면서도 도리혀 떠러질 수 없는 사이가 될 수 있을상 싶기도 하다. 그러나 하고 세민은 다시 생각해 본다. 이런 것은 책생 우의 공상이 아닌가. 하로밤 동안에 무슨 일이 있었어다 해도 애라의 마음이 단번에 일수에게로 옴긴다는 것은 상상할 수 없는 일이 아닌가. 그는 지난 겨울이후 애라와 지나온 가지가지 일을 차레로 생각해 보았다. 처음으로 그들 키스하든 날 밤 결혼의 설게를 이야기하든 때 그리고 크리스마스·이브의 소꼽질 같은 만찬회 모든 것은 평범하다면 한없이 평범한 일이지만은 그 속에 있는 은은한 성실은 아무래도 의심할 수 없는 바였다. 용광로의 불같은 타오르는 정렬은 없었다 해도 양명한 해빛같은 애정은 있지 않었는가.
「선생을 아온후 질거운 세월을 보낸 기억은 내내 이처지지 않겠사오며...」
세민은 또다시 애라에게서 온 최후의 편지글발을 외어 보았으나 그곳에서는 역시 애귾는 호소밖에는 찾어낼 수 없었다.
내내 세민은 책을 덮고 이러나 뜰로 나갔다.
이월도 벌서 중순이라 멫일전까지도 코끝이 맵든 교정에는 따뜻한 봄빛이 가득하였다. 시험중이라 물속같이 고요한 왼학교 크로버 우에 학생이 두서넜 해볓을 받고 누어 노-트를 읽고 있었다. 저편에는 노-트를 얼골에 덮고 풀밭 우에 누은채 그대로 잠드러 버린 사람도 보인다.
봄이로구나! 하는 생각이 낀-하고 가슴이 가득하게 메어 올나온다. 그리고 보니 문득 세민에게는 겨우내 가본 일 없는 학교 온실 생각이 났다. 작년 가을까지 책을 읽다가 머리가 피곤하면 가끔 가서 한참식 드려다 보곤하든 온실이다. 세민은 천천히 마당을 가루질너 법학부교실 모퉁이를 도라갔다.
언덕 밑에 있는 온실도 따뜻한 해볓을 받어 잠든 듯 하다. 깨끝하게 닥기운 유리창 넘어로 히야신스 시크라멘 등속이 한참 피어 분홍 힌빛 자주 옥색 가지가지 찬란한 색갈이 아름다운 꿈같이 눈에 빛인다. 한참이나 세민은 무슨 깊은 연구나 하는 사람같이 온실 속을 드려다 보고 있었으나 화려한 빛갈도 귀여운 꽃모양도 그의 정신의 저편 하늘을 지나갈 뿐 지튼 안개에 싸인 머리속까지는 내내 들어오지 않었다.
정신나간 사람모양으로 섰는데
「무얼 그렇게 보구있나」
누군지 별안간 억개를 툭 치며 말을 걸었다. 깜짝 놀나 돌녀다보니 민법연구실에 있는 「야스다」였다.＜213＞
「하두 곱게 피었길내-」
세민이 빙그레 웃으며 대답하니까
「거 참 고은데」
야스다도 끌니어 한참이나 온실 속을 들여다 보드니
「어- 참 따뜻허다」
하품을 하며 기지개를 켜고
「자네 지금 바뿐가 안 바뿌면 고-히나 한잔 먹으러 가세그려」
말하는 야스다의 얼골은 무슨 좋은 일이 있는지 벙글벙글 웃고 있다.
「바뿔거 없어 가세」
세민은 대답하고 야스다와 함께 도루 교정을 가로질너 의학부 구내에 있는 식당으로 갔다.
야스다는 동경제국대 학부를 졸업하고 일년동안 그곳 조수로 있다가 작년 봄에 이곳으로 옴겨온 사람이다. 굉장한 수재로서 그곧에서도 앞길을 촉망되든 야스다가 까닭없이 이곳으로 옴겨올니는 물논 없는 것이오 연한만 차면 강사나 조교수로 승임하리라는 것은 누구에게나 짐작되는 바였다. 그런 관게로 그는 이곳으로 온 후에는 같은 연구실원들과는 별로 교분이 없고 교수들축과 추축하는 것이었으나 세민과는 예외로 비교적 가까운 사이였다. 야스다의 전공이 경제법이라는 경제학의 입정에서 법률을 연구하랴는 것이었으므로 세민의 전공과목과 관게가 깊은 것도 전가지 원인이었으나 이곳 대학에서 성적이 대단히 좋았다는 세민에게 야스다는 동정과 호기심을 갖인 듯이 보이기도 하였다. 동경서도 유명한 부자집 아들이라는 야스다는 지금까지도 두서너번 세민을 끌고 서을의 밤거리를 도라다니기도 한 것이였다.＜214＞
마주앉어 차를 마시는 두사람의 대조는 빛과 어둠의 차이같이 기묘한 것이었다. 건장한 몸을 영국제 「홈스팡」양복에 맵시있게 싼 야스다 가는 줄이 도친 연옥색 와이샤쓰은 「좌이도-야」에서 팔원 주고 삿다는 화려한 넥타이 그 우에 올나앉인 곱게 손질을 한 혈색 좋은 얼골 그와 마주 대해노니 건강조차 조치못한 세민은 한칭 더 초라하게만 보인다. 쑥 드러간 두볼 때뭇은 칼라 꾸기꾸기한 양복 그보다도 씨슬 수 없이 얼골에 어리운 깊은 오뇌의 거림자―
야스다는 켜피를 단숨애 쭉 드리켜고 담배에 불을 붙이며
「마침 이렇게 맛났으니 말일세만 내 일은 인제 아주 정식으로 결정됐다네」
「음?」
세민이 알어듯지 못하니까
「이 대학에서의 내 지위 말야」
「그래?」
말하며 세민은 테-불을 격해 앉인 야스다의 얼골을 눈이 부신 듯이 바라다 본다. 야스다는 게속해
「안직 외부에 발표해서는 안되지만은 어저께 교수회의에서 정식으로 가결이 되구 오늘 총장의 결재까지 나왔다네」
「거 잘됐네. 그럴 줄은 알었지만 민법인가」＜215＞
「응 헌데 올 일년만은 S교수가 양행중이라서 노동법을 대신 허래」
「그것쯤야 무어 어쨋든 감축허네」
세민은 감축하다고 입으로는 말했으나 마음속은 더욱더욱 암담해갔다. 그것은 무슨 야스다의 출세를 부러워하거나 시기해서가 아니라 ― 그러타면 세민도 주임교수가 힘써 주든 K고등상업학교 자리나마 고맙게 받었슬 것이다 ― 그런 것보다는 야스다의 그럼자 없는 해빛같은 생활의 행복을 부러워서였다. 같은 해에 대학을 나오고 나이도 같은 두사람이었지만 이를테면 한사람은 하눌을 찌를 듯이 쑥쑥 자라는 열대지방의 야자나무와 같고 한 사람은 북쪽나라 거친 벌판의 눈속에서 나와 어름 속에 조자붙는 쫄댁이풀과도 같은 것이다.
