Japan's Cultural Projects in Shanghai and Nanjing
Shanghai Natural Science Research Institute
As the largest international cultural institution in Central China, the Shanghai Natural Science Research Institute, located in the middle of the combat zone, continues its research work with the full cooperation of its members.
Regarding this, Director Shinjō, who passed away suddenly, spoke thus in Nanjing last July:
"Due to the stationing of Chinese troops, cultural facilities in the vicinity of Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou have suffered considerable damage. Books, machinery, or precious specimens collected through the painstaking efforts of scholars over many years are severely scattered, or buried in mud, which is truly regrettable. Our research institute is dispatching specialist scholars and consulting with military authorities, endeavoring to become an important element in China's cultural revival."
This statement makes the situation clear. Recently, at the request of the Central China Expeditionary Army, a Committee for Books and Documents was established to investigate documents remaining in various cultural institutions and government offices within the occupied territories.
Since the beginning of this year, members of the Shanghai Natural Science Research Institute, together with members of the Tōa Dōbun Shoin (East Asia Common Culture Academy) and the Shanghai Branch of the South Manchuria Railway Company, have been active in the preservation work of books and documents.

1. Shanghai Section
Investigation and collection of books from government-run universities and offices, excluding the two foreign concessions in Shanghai. Many universities, libraries, and other cultural institutions near Shanghai were reduced to ashes by the flames of war, and it can be said that almost none of the fortunately <181> spared institutions retain their original form. Therefore, the books, documents, academic specimens, and machinery remaining in these buildings were damaged beforehand by the Chinese parties involved or looted by rioters, suffering significant losses. Only the libraries of Hujiang University (滬江大學) in Wusong (吳淞) and Datong University (大同大學) in the southern part barely retain some semblance of their original state.

2. Nanjing Section
Collection took place at over 70 locations, amounting to approximately 650,000 yen. The collected items have been stored at the Geological Survey and Geological and Mineral Exhibition Hall on Zhujiang Road.

3. Hangzhou Section
Although many cultural institutions, as well as confiscated public and private assets in Hangzhou, were taken away by the Chinese side across the Qiantang River (鐵塘江), many famous historical sites and temples centering around the city center and West Lake still preserve their old appearance. Collection sites alone number 25.

4. Other Cities between Shanghai and Nanjing
Efforts are being made for collection in various cities such as Suzhou (蘇州), Zhenjiang (鎭江), Wuxi (無錫), Jiading (嘉定), Taicang (太倉), Changshu (常熟), and Kunshan (崑山).

In addition to what is written above, under the same purpose, an Academic Specimen Arrangement Committee was established, and preservation of valuable academic specimens was carried out in the Nanjing and Hangzhou areas. In Nanjing, focusing on the "Central Research Institute's Geological Research Institute (中央硏究院 地質硏究所)," over 20,000 items including geology, minerals, rocks, and soil, and over 10,000 items including zoological and botanical specimens, ceramics, pottery, stone tools, and archaeological items were collected and preserved.
In Hangzhou, activities centered around the "West Lake Museum (西湖博物舘)" and the "Zhejiang Construction Department (淅江建設廳)," steadily producing results.
Beyond concentrating current efforts on the aforementioned book preservation work and academic specimen preservation work, attention is also being given to epidemic prevention. <182>

Tōa Dōbun Shoin (East Asia Common Culture Academy)
The Tōa Dōbun Shoin, whose historic school buildings and valuable documents and books were entirely reduced to nothing (烏有) by the arson and destruction of the Chinese army, immediately began reconstruction. It moved its school building to the Chinese National Chiao Tung University (交通大學) on Haigelu (海格路), bordering the French Concession, started classes from the new academic term this year, and with a spirit of new beginnings, is engaged in guiding the research, refinement, and education of young students who will soar across the Chinese continent.
The burning by the Chinese army occurred in early November last year when our South China mop-up operation began. Afterwards, many school tools and books from the Dōbun Shoin were found abandoned by the Chinese army in the Zhongshan Hall (中山舘) in Huxi (滬西) and the City Government building in Nanshi (南市).
The Dōbun Shoin moved to Chiao Tung University on April 16th and began classes on October 18th. Chiao Tung University was a place that once housed as many as 35,000 refugees.
Now, the academy accommodates 486 students with a faculty of over 30 members, inevitably expanding with the progress of the Continental Policy. The number of entering students increased from 120 last year to 140 this year, and is expected to show exponential growth to perhaps 200 next year. All students live in dormitories under the dormitory system, and annually go on practical research trips to Central China, North China, South China, Manchuria, the South Seas, Siam, and even the Malay Peninsula. <183>

**Summary**

This 1938 article reports on Japanese efforts, primarily through the Shanghai Natural Science Research Institute and the Tōa Dōbun Shoin, to "preserve" cultural assets like books, documents, and scientific specimens from Chinese institutions in occupied areas like Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou following the Sino-Japanese War. It portrays these actions as a benevolent contribution to China's "cultural revival," rescuing materials damaged or scattered during the conflict. The Tōa Dōbun Shoin's relocation and expansion are also highlighted as part of Japan's growing influence. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is revealing because it showcases the Japanese imperial narrative of bringing order and culture to occupied territories, a narrative also applied to Korea. Historians would find interest in the justification used for seizing cultural property (framing it as preservation), the scale of the collection mentioned (650,000 yen worth in Nanjing alone), and the role of academic institutions like the Tōa Dōbun Shoin in supporting imperial expansion ("Continental Policy").

上海 南京의 日本文化事業
上海 自然科學硏究所
中支에 있어서의 최대의 國際的 文化機關으로서 上海 自然科學硏究所는 전투지역 한복판에 있으면서 전원 협력하여 연구공작을 계속하고 있다,
여기에 대하여 急逝한 新城所長은 지난 7월 南京에서 이렇게 말하였다.
「上海 부근, 南京, 杭州는 모다 支那軍隊 駐屯때문에 문화시설이 상당한 손해를 受하여 혹은 도서, 기계, 혹은 학자가 多年 心血을 注하여 모집한 귀중한 標本類가 몹시 散亂하고 혹은 泥濘에 파무처 몹시 았가웟다. 我 연구소는 전문학자를 파견하고 군부방면과 협의하면서 支那의 문화부흥에 중요한 요소가 되려하고 있노라」
함을 보아도 알만하다. 금번 中支 파견군의 위촉에 의하여 圖書 文獻 委員會의 성립을 보아서 점령지역내 각지 문화기관 及 관청에 잔존한 문헌의 조사를 하고 있다.
이제 금년 초부터 上海 自然科學硏究所員은 東亞同文書院과 南滿鐵道株式會社 上海出張所員들과 함께 도서 문헌의 보존 공작에 활동하엿다.

1, 上海의 部
上海의 兩租界를 제한 官立大學 관청의 도서 조사, 접수, 上海 부근의 대학, 도서관, 기타 문화기관은 兵火로 灰燼에 歸한 것이 않었고 다행히＜181＞ 燒失을 면한 것도 구형을 갖이고 있는 것은 거지반 없다고 하여도 可하다, 그러기에 이들 건물에 잔존한 圖書, 文, 獻, 學術標本, 기계류는 사전에 支那측 당사자 손에 훼손되고 또는 暴民에 略取되어 많은 피해를 입었다. 겨우 吳淞에 있는 滬江大學과 南方에 있는 大同大學의 양 도서관이 약간 원형을 갖이고 있을 뿐이다.

2, 南京의 部
蒐集 장소 70여개소 약 650,000圓의 대수집 그것은 珠江路에 있는 地質調査所 地質鑛業陳列舘에 보관 종료.

3, 杭州의 部
杭州에 있어서의 각종 문화기관은 물론, 公私의 逆産은 支那측의 손에 의하여 鐵塘江 저쪽에 가저가 버린 것도 많으나 城內 及 西湖를 중심으로 한 名勝 舊蹟 寺院은 의연히 구태를 보존하고 있는 것이 많다, 수집해둔 장소만 25개소.

4, 上海 南京간의 其他 各 都市
蘇州, 鎭江, 無錫, 嘉定, 太倉, 常爇, 崑山의 각 도시에 모다 수집에 진력하고 있다.

이상에 쓴 것 외에도 같은 취지하에 學藝標本整理委員會를 設하고 南京 杭州 방면에서 귀중한 학술표본류의 보존을 행하였다, 南京에서는 「中央硏究院 地質硏究所」를 중심으로 지질, 광물, 암석, 토양 20,000점 이상, 동물 식물의 표본류, 자기, 도기, 석기, 고고학 등 10,000점 이상의 수집 보존에 당하였다.
杭州는 「西湖博物舘」 「淅江建設廳」을 중심으로 활동을 행하며 착착 성적을 내고었다.
이상 圖書保存工作 及 學術標本保存工作에 현재의 주력을 집중하는 외에 防疫 방면에도 뜻을 두고 있다.＜182＞

東亞同文書院
支那軍의 防火 破壞에 의하여 歷史있는 校舍, 貴重한 文獻, 圖書 一切가 烏有에 歸한 東亞同文書院은 즉시 부흥에 착수하여 校舍를 佛租界의 경계, 海格路의 支那國立交通大學에 移하고 本年의 신학기부터 수업은 개시하며 新興氣分으로서 支那 대륙에 웅비하는 청년학도의 硏讚 訓育의 지도에 당하고 있다.
支那軍에 소각당한 것은 我 南支 掃蕩戰이 개시된 작년 11월 초순이었다. 그 뒤 滬西의 中山舘과 南市의 市政府 등에 同文書院의 校具와 도서의 다수가 支那軍에 의하여 유기되어 있었다.
그런데 同文書院이 交通大學으로 이전한 것이 4월 16일 수업을 개시한 것이 10월 18일 이었다. 交通大學은 一時는 피난민을 35,000이나 수용하였든 곳이다.
지금 서원은 486명의 학생을 수용하고 30여명의 교수진을 펴고서 大陸政策의 진전에 따라 필연적으로 팽창하고 있다. 작년 입학자 120명이 금년은 140명으로 증가하고 내년도은 아마 200명으로 비약적발전을 보이기로 되었라. 학생은 모다 寄宿舍主義로서 기숙사에 수용되고 있는데 학생은 中支 北支 南支와 滿洲 南洋 샤무 방면과 馬來半島에까지 매년 실지조사연구로 떠난다.＜183＞
