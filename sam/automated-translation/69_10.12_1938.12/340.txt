Publication Date: 1938년 12월 01일
Article Title: 江南의 봄 (Spring in Kangnam)
Author: 春園
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Spring in Kangnam
						Ch'unwŏn

The willow branches sway
In the soft spring breeze of Kangnam
In the hazy atmosphere of the water country (水國)
And in the milky sunlight
The willow branches flutter

The skylark's song goes on endlessly
The noon rooster of a Qing person's (淸人) house crows
The skylark sings again, the wind blows again
The marching bugle (行軍喇叭) of the Boy Scouts (童子軍) is heard
Ah, the spring of Kangnam that makes one weary
(Taishō 9, July) <210>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu's pen name) depicts the languid, almost overwhelming sensory experience of spring south of the river (Kangnam). It evokes images of swaying willows, hazy air, milky sunlight, and sounds like the endless song of skylarks, a rooster's crow, and a distant Boy Scout bugle, culminating in a feeling of pleasant weariness. From a historical perspective, the poem, dated 1920 (Taishō 9), offers a glimpse into the early colonial period's atmosphere through the eyes of a major literary figure. The mention of "淸人" (Qing person/Chinese) and "童子軍" (Boy Scouts) reflects the presence of different communities and the introduction of modern Western-style organizations in Korea at the time.

江南의 봄
						春園
버들가지가 흔들린다
부드럽은 江南의 봄바람에
뽀얀 水國의 大氣속에
그러고 젓빗 가튼 日光속에
버들가지가 나비낀다

종달의 소리가 끗도안나서
淸人의집 낫닭이 운다
종달이 또 운다 바람이 또분다
童子軍의 行軍喇叭이 들린다
아아, 사람을 困케하는 江南의 봄이여
(大正 9년 7월)＜210＞
