Spring Moon
						Zhang Ruoxu, Yohan (Trans.)

Spring Moon
The river water overflows
Filling the sea
Following the endless waves
Rises the spring moon.

In the flower garden, by the waterside
The bright moon flows in
White frost, white sand
Cannot be distinguished.

Above the river, the clear sky
In the sky, that high moon
Who first saw it?
Whom did it first shine upon?

Life goes on and on
Only the moonlight remains.
The one who watched the moon is gone without a trace
Only the river water flows on.<206>

The clouds do not rest
On a lonely boat at "Ch'ŏngp'ungp'o" (淸風浦)
The traveler who left home
Cannot overcome thoughts of hometown.

In the house back home, the high attic
Where the lowered screen has been lifted
Why does the bright moonlight not go?
I wish I could block it with my hand.

Unable to convey this burning heart
Shall I try shining along with the moon?
Only the wild geese fly far away
And the fish in the water sink deep.

Falling flower petals in last night's dream
The loved one not arriving though spring is half gone
Spring passes with the flowing water
And even the moon is about to set.

Deep fog envelops the moon
On the distant road of "Kalsŏkshyosang" (갈석쇼상)
Will the longed-for loved one come?
Oh, Spring Moon, it seems you know my unbearable tears.<207>

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean translation of the classic Tang Dynasty Chinese poem "Spring River Flower Moon Night" (春江花月夜) by Zhang Ruoxu (張若虛). The poem explores themes of the beauty of nature under the spring moon, the vastness of the universe, the transience of human life contrasted with the eternal cycles of nature (the river, the moon), and feelings of longing and homesickness experienced by a traveler. From a historical perspective, the publication of a translated famous Chinese poem in a popular Korean magazine during the late 1930s is noteworthy. It reflects continued cultural engagement with the classical Chinese tradition amidst growing Japanese colonial pressures, possibly offering readers an aesthetic or emotional escape rooted in a shared East Asian heritage, separate from contemporary political realities.

						張若虛, 요한(譯)
봄달
강물은 넘처올나
바다에 가득한데
끗업는 물결따라
도다 오는 봄달이여

꼿밧에도 물가에도
흘너드는 밝은달은
힌서리 흰모래를
분변치 못할너라.

강우에 맑은하늘
하늘에 노픈저달
누가 먼저 보앗던고
누를 먼저 비첫든고

인생은 가고가고
달빗만 남는고나.
달본이 간데업시
강물만 흘너간다.＜206＞

구름은 쉬잔는데
「청풍포」 외론배에
집떠난 나그내는
고향생각 못이겨라.

고향집엔 노픈다락
드린바을 거덧는데
밝은 달빗 웨안가노
손으로나 막아보리.

타는가삼 못전하니
달을따라 비처볼가
기럭이만 멀니날고
물에고기 잠겨드네.

밤꿈에 지는꼿닙
봄이 반에 안오는님
흘는물에 봄다가고
달조차도 가려한다.

기픈안개 달을싸니
「갈석쇼상」 머나먼길
그린 그님 올가보냐
봄달아 겨운눈물을 네아는듯.＜207＞
