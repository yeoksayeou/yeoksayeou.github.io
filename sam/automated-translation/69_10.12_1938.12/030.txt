A Letter Sent by Governor-General Minami to Elder Yun Ch'iho

Governor-General Minami (南總督), who is promoting Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e (내선일체 - Japan and Korea as One Body), met with Mr. Yun Ch'iho (윤치호) on the past September 5th and spoke of these three matters. This is a fact known only to a very select few individuals. Translating the full text now reveals:

1.  Regarding the guidance of Peninsular youth, cultivate a cheerful personality where words and actions are one (一言一行致一).

2.  The core of the construction of the East by East Asians lies in the perfection of Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e (내선일체).

3.  The foundation of Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e lies in fostering the substance of being loyal and good Imperial subjects (忠良한 황국신민).<14-15>

**Summary**

This short news piece reports on three key points conveyed by Governor-General Minami Jirō to the prominent Korean figure Yun Ch'iho during a meeting on September 5, 1938. The points emphasize cultivating obedient and unified character in Korean youth, positioning the complete assimilation of Korea and Japan (Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e) as central to building East Asia, and defining the basis of this assimilation as the creation of "loyal and good Imperial subjects." This article is significant as it documents direct communication between the highest colonial authority and a leading Korean intellectual, explicitly outlining the core tenets of late-1930s Japanese assimilation policy: character molding of the youth, the ideological necessity of Nae-Sŏn Ilch'e for Japan's regional ambitions, and the ultimate goal of transforming Koreans into loyal subjects of the Japanese Emperor. Historians would find the direct articulation of these policy goals to a figure like Yun Ch'iho particularly revealing of the pressures and expectations placed upon the Korean elite during this period of intensified assimilation efforts.
