Confidential Room, The Various Inner Workings of Our Society
Financial World, Gold Mining Industry Insider Report
Year-End Bonuses at Banks and Companies
This year's financial world has seen many upheavals under the long-term war. Whether in the cotton yarn and cloth industry, the stock market, or the mining sector, there were few places where large waves did not rise and fall. Consequently, this year's year-end bonuses, following the tide of the times, seem likely to be much higher at prosperous banks and companies, while conversely, they will be meager where business performance was poor, resulting in a significant difference. Now, we will detail the situation regarding various banks, companies, and newspapers in our society.

Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik: Rumored 100% [Bonus]
It is rare to find a place that has achieved sudden wealth like Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik (京城紡績) in Yŏngdŭngp'o, whose president is Mr. Kim Yŏn-su (金䄵洙), who surprised the world by donating 500,000 wŏn to the military about a month ago, riding the favorable wave of the wartime economy, much like the boom times of Taishō 7-8 [1918-1919]. Isn't it astonishing that a joint-stock company with only 500,000 wŏn in capital earned over 1,000,000 wŏn in the first six months? This was primarily due to the price of gray cotton cloth skyrocketing from around 180-190 wŏn to the 400 wŏn range, resulting in a windfall while sitting still. Furthermore, they built a large factory in Shijiazhuang (石家莊) in North China and advanced into the spinning industry, gaining geographical advantage there and achieving consecutive victories, earning a huge profit (巨利) of over 1,000,000 wŏn.<13>
Based on the experience of giving all employees a special bonus of 100% (i.e., 10 times the monthly salary) after settling the accounts for the first half of the year and donating 500,000 wŏn, it is observed that 100% is undoubtedly expected at the end of this December as well. This company's bonus will likely be the highest in Chosŏn society.

Hwasin: Rumored 200% [Bonus]
Rumors abound that Hwasin (和信) also made good profits this year. Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店), Hwasin Chain Stores (和信連鎖店), Taehan Hŭngŏp (大同興業), and Sŏnil Chimul (鮮一紙物) all performed well, with total sales reportedly reaching the 5,000,000 wŏn mark this year.
Moreover, even the chain store joint-stock company, which had reported losses of 50,000-60,000 wŏn or 70,000-80,000 wŏn per period in its early days, turned profitable this year, generating a net profit of 80,000 wŏn in the first half and likely around 70,000-80,000 wŏn in this second half. Is there any need to further discuss the other divisions that have always reaped large profits (大利)? Especially since cotton yarn and cloth were all profitable this year, Hwasin established a new Textile Department<14> on a large scale, and as a result, sales from the Textile Department alone surpassed the 2,000,000 wŏn mark, so it seems factual that the profit would be substantial. Thus, even assuming a 20% profit on total sales of 5,000,000 wŏn, that's 1,000,000 wŏn. Even after paying a 15% dividend on the paid-in capital of about 3,000,000 wŏn, they would still have seen a profit of 500,000-600,000 wŏn. Consequently, there are rumors that this year's Hwasin bonus (뽀-너스) will also be favorable. Since they only give bonuses once a year, it is said to likely average around 200% [of monthly salary].

Taedong Kwangŏp: Rumored 300% [Bonus]
The treasure trove of Taedong Kwangŏp (大同鑛業) is, without a doubt, the Changjin Gold Mine (長津金鑛) in South Hamgyŏng Province. Although electrification facilities are still insufficient, preventing full capacity operation, this year's production value still reached about 1,400,000-1,500,000 wŏn. Furthermore, a gold deposit comparable to the Changjin Mine<15> has been discovered nearby and is currently being actively mined. Assuming the gold production value is 1,500,000 wŏn, and considering mining costs—namely, labor wages, dynamite costs, transportation, and other miscellaneous expenses (諸入費)—as 30% consumption, the net profit is estimated at about 1,000,000 wŏn. This company, which has moved its office building to the former Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報社) site and is renewing its spirit, is reliably said to be considering employee bonuses of roughly 300% [of monthly salary].

Chosŏn Ilbo: Rumored 200% [Bonus]
Private newspapers can be seen as taking a defensive stance (守勢) this year, as their activities have been considerably restricted by various circumstances, such as paper conservation and prohibitions on capital increases. Therefore, the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), under the long-term war, has not shown as much vitality this year as seen in previous years. Consequently, it is observed that this year's bonuses are unlikely to exceed last year's level. Last year's rate was generally 200%.

Tongil Bank: Rumored 150% [Bonus]
Due to poor business performance, it came under the supervision of the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行). After many changes in key positions, including the manager, its business condition is said to be gradually recovering, so it should begin to ease its worries slightly from the second half of this year. There is talk of a 150% bonus for the second half. Compared to Kyŏngjŏn's (京電) 400% and Siksan Bank's (殖産銀行) 300%, this is small, but it is heartening that the day of recovery is gradually approaching.

Posŏng College: 100% [Bonus]
It is said that noble poverty (淸貧) accompanies scholars. Perhaps for this reason, professors (푸로펫샤級) have always been distant from the monetary society, and even this year-end bonus, the one opportunity per year, is subject to austerity policies at each school. Some have completely abolished it, while others have drastically reduced it to just 50-60%. However, at Posŏng College (普傳), it is considered reasonable to expect about 100% this year.

Tong-A Securities: Rumored 200% [Bonus]
There has never been such intense volatility in the stock market as in the past two years. The major market fluctuations surrounding the fall of Shanghai and Nanjing, and again the recent sharp shocks surrounding the invocation of Article 11 of the General Mobilization Law just before and after the fall of Hankou—all have brought much rise and fall to joint-stock companies. Thus, amidst this turbulence, Tong-A Securities (東亞證券) of Meiji-chō (明治町), managed by Mr. Cho Chun-ho (趙俊鎬), which has been very active, is seen by many as likely to give bonuses of about 200% this year.

Tong-A Ilbo: Rumored 100%-plus% [Bonus]
Although it cannot be said that much time has passed since its re-publication, thanks to the president and the entire staff uniting and exerting great effort (大馬力) <16> for the restoration of its former influence, they have mostly recovered the difficult advertising networks in Tokyo and Osaka. The readership network has also been reorganized with a system of branches and sub-branches across the 13 provinces, steadily progressing with a principle of soundness. The Tong-A Ilbo's (東亞日報) performance in the second half is also favorable, and it is said there will be bonuses of over 100% (10數割) for the staff. The inability to adopt an active policy due to paper conservation and press controls was the same as for the Chosŏn Ilbo.

Government-General and Other Public Offices: 150% [Bonus]
In these times when calls for consumption reduction are loud and budgets other than military expenses are being cut as much as possible due to the long-term war, bonuses at the Government-General (總督府) and lower public offices are expected to be around the 150% standard.

Maeil Sinbo: Rumored 150% [Bonus]
Established this year as a large 1,000,000 wŏn company and having secured the position of a national policy newspaper under the support of the authorities, the Maeil Sinbo (每日新報), which has shown significant progress in its advertising and sales networks, is rumored to be giving bonuses of about 150% this year.

Chosŏn Yŏnghwa: Also Rumored 100% [Bonus]
Gathering contemporary popular actors like Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰) and Han Ŭn-jin (韓銀珍), along with leading modern directors such as Pak Ki-ch'ae (朴基采), An Sŏk-yŏng (安夕影), and Yi Kyu-hwan (李圭煥), Chosŏn Yŏnghwa (朝鮮映畵), capitalized at 500,000 wŏn, has jumped onto the advancement track of diverse film production with works like *Mujŏng* (無情), *P'ungnyŏngi* (豊年記), and *Ch'unhyangjŏn* (春香傳). Although no works have yet been released (封切) in Kyŏngsŏng, the debut film *Mujŏng* will soon come out as a New Year's feature, and it seems likely that 7-8 films will follow annually. Since this company fundamentally operates based on public popularity and is a glamorous place, the bonus for actors and staff in its second year of founding, despite being in its initial stages (初創中), is reliably said to be no less than 100%. According to hearsay, the studio site of over a thousand p'yŏng purchased in Ŭijŏngbu has increased several times in value compared to the purchase price, and the mining performance of President Ch'oe Nam-ju's (崔南周氏) properties in Samnam is also reportedly very good. Therefore, considering this company's thriving fortunes, the bonus is unlikely to fall below 100%.

Kyŏngsŏng Broadcasting Station: Also 200% [Bonus]
The Broadcasting Station, quietly making good money, especially with the number of Korean listeners for the second broadcast service exceeding 40,000, showing great prosperity, is expected to have bonuses of 250% just for the second half of the year (bonuses are given twice a year).

Tongyang Theater: Rumored 100% [Bonus]
Tongyang Kŭkchang (東劇), on the path of advancement, has its theater troupes Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa (靑春座) and Hoahwa-sŏn (豪華船) touring all of Chosŏn. Meanwhile, it absorbs the masses by occasionally screening short film programs at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall (京城府民館). Now, it is even moving into film production, joining hands with Mr. Yi Ch'ang-yong (李創用氏) of Koryŏ Yŏnghwa (高麗映畵). Thanks to the excellent leadership of the talented Mr. Ch'oe Sang-dŏk (崔象德氏), <17> Tongyang Theater has repaid most of its past debts and is now set on an upward trajectory towards a promising future. Therefore, there is talk of a 100% year-end bonus this year. It is said that Tongyang Theater, with its numerous popular actors and directors, has rarely had such good performance as this year.

Hwasin 5 Million Wŏn Sales Rumor
There is growing speculation that Hwasin will merge the companies currently in the same group—Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店), Hwasin Chain Stores (和信連鎖店), Sŏnil Chimul (鮮一紙物), and Taehan Hŭngŏp (大同興業)—into an 8,000,000 wŏn Hwasin Trading Joint-Stock Company (和信商事株式會社). In any case, Hwasin's performance this past year was very favorable, with total sales reaching approximately the 5,000,000 wŏn mark. Broken down, this is roughly:
Hwasin Textile Dept.: approx. 2,000,000 wŏn
Hwasin Department Store: approx. 1,000,000 wŏn
Hwasin Chain Stores: 1,200,000 wŏn
Sŏnil: 80,000 wŏn [Note: Likely a typo in original, should probably be 800,000 wŏn to sum near 5M]
It is said that if profit is calculated at 20% of 5,000,000 wŏn in sales, it amounts to 1,000,000 wŏn; even calculated conservatively at 10%, it amounts to a huge profit (巨利) of about 500,000 wŏn.

Seven or Eight People in Seoul Become Rich from Cotton Yarn and Cloth

A time when cotton cloth and yarn merchants earned such huge profits (巨利) as this year has been rare, encountering boom times similar to those during the Great European War in Taishō 7-8 [1918-1919]. Thus, among the major merchants in Kyŏngsŏng city this year, few failed to earn profits of several hundred thousand wŏn, and it is said there are many who secretly grasped unimaginably large profits (大利).
Generally, how much fabric—mainly cotton cloth, cotton yarn, and rayon—is traded within Chosŏn annually? It reaches about 100,000,000 wŏn, of which about 40,000,000 wŏn passes through the hands of Seoul merchants for distribution (賣捌). Looking for the major cloth merchants (布商) in Seoul now:
Kim Hŭi-jun Store (金熙俊商店) in Chongno (鐘路)
Mr. Ch'oe Nam's (崔楠氏) Tong Sun-dŏk (東順德)
Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik's (朴興植氏) Hwasin Textile Department (和信織物部)
Mr. Cho Chun-ho's (趙俊鎬氏) Tong-A Textile (東亞織物)
Chŏngja-ok's (丁子屋) Sŏnman Textile (鮮滿織物)
and so on, totaling 8 or 9 establishments. Looking at this year's sales figures, Kim Hŭi-jun Store is said to have the highest at about 3,000,000 wŏn, Mr. Ch'oe Nam around 2,500,000-2,600,000 wŏn, Hwasin 2,000,000 wŏn, and others also in the 1,000,000 to 2,000,000 wŏn range.<18>
Looking at gray cotton cloth (廣木) prices, what was 198 wŏn soared to the 400 wŏn range, and even now that it has come down to the official price, it is around 280-290 wŏn, still a rise of over 100 wŏn compared to pre-incident prices. On the manufacturing side, the one that profited greatly was Mr. Kim Yŏn-su's (金䄵洙氏) Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik (京城紡績), earning about 1,000,000 wŏn, of which 500,000 wŏn was famously donated as national defense contribution. And, of course, the various Chongno spinning mills (鍾紡) owned by Koreans throughout the country also reaped huge profits (巨利).

Kyŏngsŏng-Kŭmgangsan Tram Plan Underway by Certain Koreans

To go from Seoul to Kŭmgangsan (金剛山), one goes via Ch'ŏrwŏn (鐵原) and Kimhwa (金化) to enter via Changansa (長安寺), which naturally takes 6-7 hours. Everyone has unanimously anticipated how many more visitors coming to Kyŏngsŏng could explore Kŭmgangsan if this travel time were shortened. Now, here is some good news (快 뉴-쓰) about this.
This is a plan for a direct electric tramway between Kyŏngsŏng and Kimhwa. Planned early on by certain Koreans (某某氏), it reached the stage of starting as a joint-stock company, but was temporarily halted due to the sharp rise in steel prices caused by the Incident [Sino-Japanese War]. If this tramway is built, it is said that one could comfortably reach Kŭmgangsan from Kyŏngsŏng in 3 hours.

Press and Thought Circles
64,000 Wŏn Annual Subsidy - Man-Sŏn Ilbo Renovates and Expands
Under the government's press control, the Man-Sŏn Ilbo (滿鮮日報), the sole Korean-language newspaper in Manchukuo, is reportedly set to renew its internal structure and attempt an advance across Manchuria and Chosŏn. The gist of this organizational reform is that Ch'oe Yukkang (崔六堂), currently a professor at Kenkoku University (建國大學), will hold the title of advisor but have considerable control over determining the company's direction (社是). Mr. Kim Kyŏng-jae (金璟載氏), an executive at the Concordia Association (協和會) Capital Headquarters, will become a commissioned writer (囑託) and one of the editorial writers. Additionally, Mr. Pak Sŏk-yun (朴錫胤氏) from the Foreign Affairs Department, Mr. Chin Hak-mun (秦學文氏) from the Internal Affairs Department, and Mr. Yun Sang-p'il (尹相弼氏) from the Kwantung Army are all expected to contribute writings as honorary guest writers,<19> positioning many commentators externally. The newspaper is also expected to become a 14-column morning and evening paper and begin active efforts regarding sales and advertising networks. It is said that government subsidies have also been decided at 64,000 wŏn annually starting this year.

Yŏm Sang-sŏp Becomes Bureau Chief - Hong Yang-myŏng as Political Editor
Simultaneously, the internal structure has been renewed. Mr. Yŏm Sang-sŏp (廉尙燮) (pen name Sangsŏp 想涉), formerly the chief editor, has become the Editor-in-Chief (편집국장) (the chief editor position abolished). Mr. Hong Yang-myŏng (洪陽明氏), former political editor at Man-Sŏn Ilbo, has become the Political and Economic Editor. Mr. Pak P'ar-yang (朴八陽氏), former social affairs editor at the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報), has been appointed Social Affairs Editor. Below them, the editorial department is reportedly organized with 18 capable reporters (敏腕記者). This newspaper, having gathered such elite individuals, is expected to show vibrancy starting from its New Year's issue.

Over 10,000 Wŏn for Lifestyle Improvement - Expenses for Dispatching Fifty People Nationwide
The Government-General dispatched 50 individuals to all 13 provinces for the lifestyle improvement movement, initiating the campaign through lectures and round-table discussions. It is reported that each dispatched committee member received a daily allowance of 20 wŏn, and the total expenditure for this initiative amounted to over 10,000 wŏn.

Literary, Film, and Theater Worlds
Ch'unwŏn's "Sarang" First Edition Royalty: 900 Wŏn
Ch'unwŏn's (春園) novel "Sarang" (사랑, Love) has been published as the first volume of the Contemporary Masterpiece Novel Collection by Pak Mun Sŏgwan (博文書館). The royalty for the first edition (first volume only) was acquired by Mr. No Ik-hyŏng (盧益亨氏), the owner of Pak Mun Sŏgwan, for 900 wŏn. Furthermore, the royalty rate for this novel collection is said to be around 50 chŏn per manuscript page (400 characters). Mr. Kim Tong-in (金東仁氏) is also reportedly writing a new work.

"Mujŏng" Film Rights: 500 Wŏn
Ch'unwŏn's "Mujŏng" (無情) is being made into a film by Chosŏn Yŏnghwa Hoesa (朝鮮映畵會社) and is set to be released (封切) in the new spring. It is said that 500 wŏn was paid to Ch'unwŏn for these film rights. Compared to Tokyo, where authors like Kikuchi Kan (菊池寬) and Kume Masao (久米正雄) receive 5,000-6,000 wŏn or even 10,000-20,000 wŏn per work, this is a very small amount. Nevertheless, the fact that film rights fees are being attached to the works of Chosŏn writers is considered an astonishingly progressive and commendable phenomenon.

"Nagŭnae" Goes to France
The recent masterpiece "Nagŭnae" (나그내, The Wanderer) received extraordinary acclaim while touring all of Chosŏn. In the Mainland [Japan], it played in all 630 theaters of the SY chain. Now, it is set to debut on the international stage, with English and French subtitles added for screenings in Paris and other major cities.

*Tusaengnok* 2,000 Wŏn, *Ŏhwa* 1,800 Wŏn
Two recently released Chosŏn films, *Tusaengnok* (圖生錄) and *Ŏhwa* (漁火), have been released (封切) and are currently being screened throughout the country. The fees received for the rights to screen them for one week at a movie theater in Seoul were:
*Tusaengnok* at Wakakusa Theater (若草劇場): 2,000 wŏn
*Ŏhwa* at Wakakusa Theater (若草劇場): 1,800 wŏn
To elaborate slightly, the production company that made the film initially sells the premiere screening rights (封切權) for one week to a theater owner in Seoul. After this initial Seoul screening, the rights are then sold to movie theaters in various regions.<21> This is an irregular system stemming from the fact that production companies do not directly own and operate theaters.

70,000 Wŏn from "Arirang"
"Arirang" (아리랑), the debut work of the late Na Un-gyu (古 羅雲奎氏), a pioneer of the film world who achieved great success but died young, is still occasionally screened throughout Chosŏn, drawing tears from multitudes with the poignant romance woven by the famous duo Sin Il-sŏn (申一仙) and Na Un-gyu. Regarding "Arirang," production costs were around 3,000 wŏn, as it was the silent film era when expenses were low and film stock was cheap. However, the money earned by this single film as it circulated various places reportedly reached the huge sum of about 70,000 wŏn. Although the ownership rights of this film passed from Mr. Na's hands long ago, the certain distribution agent (配給業 某氏) who has earned huge profits (巨利) in the meantime should indeed erect a splendid memorial stone (立碑) to console Mr. Na's spirit in the afterlife.

Shinkyō Symphony Orchestra Coming? World-Famous Shinkyō
Following the great success, both commercially and critically, achieved by inviting the Shinkyō Gekidan (新協劇團) from Tokyo for *Ch'unhyangjŏn*, which also contributed significantly to the Chosŏn New Theater Movement, there is now a plan underway by a certain individual (某某氏) to invite the Tokyo Shinkyō Symphony Orchestra (東京新響), conducted by Konoe Hidemaro (近衛篤麿*) (younger brother of the current Prime Minister Konoe), which holds a prestigious position even among Western orchestras in Berlin, Washington, etc., for performances in Chosŏn next spring. If successful, this will mark an epoch-making event in the history of music on the peninsula.

*Tsuzurikata Kyōshitsu* and *Hamlet* Coming? Following *Ch'unhyangjŏn*, from Shinkyō Gekidan
Following their performance of "Ch'unhyangjŏn," there is a plan, also being advanced by certain individuals (모모씨), to bring Tokyo's Shinkyō Gekidan (新協劇團) back to Chosŏn for a second time with performances of "Tsuzurikata Kyōshitsu" (綴方數室) and "Hamlet." It seems likely to be realized early in the New Year.

Mun Ye-bong Receives 150 Wŏn for "Ch'unhyang"
In the talkie "Ch'unhyangjŏn," filmed several years ago by the Chosŏn Film Production Studio (朝鮮映畵製作所) in Honmachi, Seoul, which received much acclaim, Mr. Han Il-song (韓一松氏) played Yi Toryŏng and Ms. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰女史) played Ch'unhyang. Ms. Mun's fee upon completion of that single work was reportedly 150 wŏn.

Ch'ae Kyu-hwa Compensation Claim: 6,000 Wŏn
Ms. Ch'ae Kyu-hwa (蔡奎嬅氏), a singer contracted to Columbia Records, who suffered in Taejŏn Prison for a long time before being acquitted and released, has reportedly filed a claim with the court for 6,000 wŏn in compensation. This amount is calculated based on 5 wŏn per day of imprisonment, totaling 6,000 wŏn.<22>

**Summary**

This 1938 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine provides insights into the economic climate of colonial Korea under the "long-term war" (Sino-Japanese War). It focuses heavily on anticipated year-end bonuses across various sectors like banking, textiles (Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik, Hwasin), mining (Taedong Kwangŏp), media (Chosŏn Ilbo, Tong-A Ilbo, Maeil Sinbo), education (Posŏng College), and entertainment (Chosŏn Yŏnghwa, Tongyang Theater). The piece highlights the significant profits made by some businesses, particularly those involved in textiles and wartime industries, contrasting with struggles in others. It also touches upon cultural news, including film production, literary royalties, planned cultural exchanges with Japan (Shinkyō Symphony, Shinkyō Gekidan), and social issues like the status of "thought criminals" and government-led "lifestyle improvement" campaigns. The article reveals the uneven economic impact of the war, the growing influence of Japanese capital and culture, and the state's increasing control over media and thought, offering historians a glimpse into the socio-economic and cultural dynamics of late 1930s colonial Korea. The specific bonus figures and company details provide concrete data points, while the sections on thought prisoners and media control underscore the political realities of the time.

機密室, 우리 社會의 諸內幕
財界, 産金界 內報
銀行 會社의 年末賞與
금년의 재계는 長期戰 下에 있느니 만치 파란이 만했다. 綿絲綿布界라거나 주식시장이라거나 광산방면이라거나 어느 곳에나 크다란 물결이 기복하지 안은 곳이 드물다. 그러느니만치 금년의 연말상여는 시대의 물결에 따라서 몹시 흥한 은행회사에는 훨신 많고 그 대신 업무성적이 시언치 않은 곳에는 박하야 그 차이가 자심한 바 있슬 모양이다. 이제 우리 사회의 여러 은행 회사 신문사에 대하야 그 상세한 것을 적으리라.

京城紡績은 百割說
50만원을 약 한달 전에 군부에 헌금하야 세상을 깜작 놀래인, 金䄵洙씨가 사장인 영등포의京城紡績같이 전시경기의 좋은 물결을 타서 마치 大正 7·8년의 호경기 때 같은 졸부를 이룬 곳이 드물니라. 겨우 50만원의 주식회사로서 상반기 6개월 동안에 100여 만원을 버을었다니 이 아니 놀나운 일인가. 이것은 주로 광목 시세가 백팔구십원 하든 것이 400원대로 폭등하는 바람에 앉은대로 돈벼락을 맛기도 하였거니와 또한 北支 石家莊에다가 큰 공장을 짓고 紡績業의 진출을 하였는데 여기서 地利를 득하여 연전연승격으로 100여 만원의 巨利를＜13＞ 내인 터이다.
지난 상반기의 결산을 마치고 50만원을 헌금하고 나서 사원에게 모도다 百割 (즉 월급의 10배)의 특별상여를 준 경험에 비추어 이번 12월말에도 百割은 틀림업는 일이라고 관측된다. 이 회사가 조선사회의 상여로는 최고봉일 것이다.

和信은 二十割說
和信도 금년 장사는 잘 남었단 소문이 창창하다. 和信百貨店
和信連鎖店

春園의 「無情」이 朝鮮映畵會社의 손으로 이번에 영화화되어 신춘에 封切되리라는데 이 영화화의 권리로서 春園께 500원을 드렸다고 한다. 동경같으면 菊池寬 久米正雄 등은 모다 1作마다 5,6천원 내지 1,2만원을 받는다는데 비하면 몹시 적은 금액이나 그래도 조선문인의 작품에 영화화권리금까지 붙는다하는 것은 놀나운 진보적 경하할 현상이라고 한다.

「나그내」가 佛蘭西로
근래의 명작 「나그내」가 전조선 각지를 돌면서 비상한 환영을 밧엇거니와 內地에 있어선 SY系의 630관을 모조리 돌고 그리고 이번에 국제무대에 등장하기로 되여 영어자막과 佛蘭西말 자막을 넣어 巴里 기타 주요 도시에서 상영하게 되었다한다

圖生錄 二千圓  漁火 千八百圓
요지간에 나온 조선영화의 2작 圖生錄과 漁火가 封切되어 방금 각지로 상영 중인데 이제 서울서 1週間日 동안 영화상영관에 상영시키는 권리금으로 받은 금액은
圖生錄이 若草劇場에 2,000원 漁火가 若草劇場에 1,800원
으로 되었다고 한다. 이 말을 좀더 해설한다면 영화를 제작한 제작회사측에서는 처음 서울서 封切權을 1주일 동안 어느 극장주에게 파는데 이렇게 일단 서울서 상영권을 파라서 封切하고 난 뒤 다시 각 지방의 영화관에 파는 제도로 되여있다.＜21＞ 이것은 제작회사가 직접 직영의 극장을 갖이고 있지 못한데서 오는 변태제도이다.

「아리랑」으로 七萬圓
영화계의 선각자이요 또한 큰 공적을 끼처 놓고 요절한 古 羅雲奎씨의 출세작 「아리랑」은 지금도 각금 全鮮 각지에 상영되어 申一仙 羅雲奎 두 명컴비가 비저내는 애곳은 로-맨스에 만인의 눈물을 자아내거니와 이 「아리랑」으로 말하면 그때는 갑이 적게 드는 무성시대였고 필님갑도 싼 시절이 되어 제작비가 3000원 정도엇다고 하는데 이 영화 1편이 각처로 도라다니며 버은 돈이 약 7만원의 거액에 달하였다고 한다. 이 영화소유권이 나씨의 손으로 떠나서 다른 사람에게 간 것은 벌서 녯날 일이지만 모름직이 그동안 巨利를 얻은 配給業 某씨는 찬란한 立碑라도 하여 세워 지하의 나씨靈을 吊하야도 좋을 일 일네라.

新響交響樂團 來? 世界的으로 有名한 新響
新協劇團을 초빙하여 춘향전을 통하야 흥행적으로도 큰 성공을 얻거니와 내외에 큰 호평을 얻었고 朝鮮新劇運動에도 많은 기여를 한 뒤를 이어서 이번에는 세계적 교향악단으로 일즉 伯林 華盛頓 등 구미 악단에까지 숭고한 지위를 갖이고 있는 近衛篤*(近衛 현 수상의 季씨) 지휘하는 東京新響이 명춘를 기하야 조선으로 초빙 공연할 계획이 某某씨 손으로 진행되고 있는데 이에 성공하는 날에는 半島음악사상 획기적 일을 이루리라한다.
