**Body Text Translation:**
Mr. Yi Kwangsu's View on Love, Reaching the Highest State of Buddha's Love

-. We would like to hear your view on love, Mr. Yi.
Yi. My view on love? Well. How should I put it? A heart that seeks endlessly higher love and strives upward! Since this is the only thing left for me now, I wonder if I have an appropriate answer to that question.

-. What do you mean by endlessly higher love?
Yi. It is the love of heavenly beings. That is to say, a love close to that of the Buddha.

-. Could you speak more concretely, for example?
Yi. I think love is the most mysterious and most sublime<49> thing of all. However, just as the very same carbon can become charcoal or graphite, but also diamond, I believe that even though we call it love, there are myriad levels and qualities. In love between the sexes, likewise, can we view the love of lewd young men and women the same as the love of wise gentlemen and virtuous ladies? Truly, between them lies a difference in value as vast as that between heaven and earth.

-. So, you mean that the most mysterious love, the most sublime love – borrowing your words, the love of heavenly beings – can only exist when it completely departs from the desires of the flesh?
Yi. That's right. In the world, love aimed at physical union is likely the most common. Of course, even if it isn't the love of heavenly beings, it must be a love that values longing for the spirit alongside physical union, but what I am speaking of goes a step further, saying that the love of the flesh must be entirely cast off.

-. Can such a mysterious love – a noble love – exist for humans who are neither Buddha nor God?
Yi. It is possible for those who recognize the eternal existence within our temporary physical bodies.

-. Where could such people be found in the world? In Mr. Yi T'aejun's novel "Hwang Chini" (黃眞伊), there's a story about a certain ascetic monk (道僧) who sought the high love that you idealize, Mr. Yi, abandoning everything, even forgetting entirely about eating and drinking, truly becoming almost a real Buddha, yet was seduced by Hwang Chini. Was that because he failed to recognize the "eternal existence"?<50>
Yi. There are many such stories. Not only that ascetic monk, but even a monk like Kyŏnghŏ (鏡虛) in the old days suffered greatly because he could not leave behind the desires of the flesh. It's said that once, upon seeing a pretty lady on the roadside, he ran up and kissed her. When his disciple questioned him about this, asking how someone as enlightened as the master could do such a thing, Kyŏnghŏ reportedly replied, "What difference is there between thinking it in the mind and carrying out what was thought?" Also, another time, he visited a certain Great Master, asked him to bring alcohol, drank it, and after drinking, demanded that a prostitute be brought. The Great Master who bought and served the alcohol could manage the alcohol but couldn't possibly provide a prostitute, so he asked, "Master, is it right for someone as enlightened as you to behave this way?" At this, Kyŏnghŏ's face reddened, and he replied,
"Sudden passion is hard to equate with Buddha; ingrained habits from many lives run deep (頓情難同佛多生習氣深)."
It must be so for everyone. Though one puts on a human body and desires and hopes in their heart to become a Buddha, I know that ingrained habits (習生) carried over a long time are truly difficult to break.

-. So, does that mean a person wearing a human body cannot become a Buddha?
Yi. Beings like Shakyamuni Buddha (釋迦如來), various Bodhisattvas (菩薩), or Jesus (예수) were people who emerged wearing human bodies.

-. Did they have absolutely no desires of the flesh?
Yi. Absolutely none. Because one cannot serve others while harboring desires of the flesh.

-. But didn't Shakyamuni Buddha marry and have a perfectly fine wife?
Yi. He did marry, but after attaining enlightenment, he took that wife as his disciple.

-. If everyone in the world thought like you, Mr. Yi, it seems there would be no joy in living.
Yi. That's spoken from lack of experience [lit. from being less burned]. Spoken from being less deceived. Anyone who hasn't realized "eternal love" thinks that way. But for one who has realized this "eternal" "love," there is nothing more futile. It's all a dream. Like clouds, like wind, like waves, it flows and blows away, but unaware of this, people in the world cry and laugh. It's all child's play. The other day, when my daughter Chŏngnan was away from her mother and staying with me, I asked her how long she would stay with her father. What she said<51> was, "Even after the season of flowers blooming and birds singing passes, until the cold winter when snow falls and wind blows, I will stay with Daddy." But then, after sleeping just one night, she missed her mother, and as if the firm promise she made with me yesterday never happened, she said, "Daddy, I want to go to Mommy." At that time, I thought: The promises of people in the world are just like the one our Chŏngnan made to her daddy. When Chŏngnan promised me, she wasn't trying to lie in the slightest. Her heart truly felt that way, so she said she would live with Daddy for a long, long time, but because that feeling didn't last long, she broke the promise. Yet, she went off to her mother nonchalantly, without regretting it or anything. Another night recently, I had another realization because of Yŏnggŭn's "slingshot." Yŏnggŭn said he was making a slingshot and wouldn't sleep even when told to, insisting he had to finish it. Left with no choice, I agreed to make it for him and put him to sleep. Our affairs are exactly like that. Let's say there are young men and women who are deeply longing for and loving each other right now. Those two cannot live apart even for a moment, and they feel like they would surely die without that person, insisting "We will love each other no matter what anyone says." However, that is exactly like our Yŏnggŭn being unable to sleep without finishing his slingshot. It's fine up to that point, but Yŏnggŭn tossed aside the slingshot I made for him less than three days later. Could this possibly make sense based on how he felt initially? If someone tried to take away or even touch<52> the slingshot then, wouldn't there have been an immediate fight and uproar? But look how his interest faded in less than three days. Everything in the world is just like that. There's nothing special to be gained by being deceived further, so please awaken quickly.

-. What can one do if, no matter how hard they try to think that way, it just doesn't work?
Yi. Well, I keep saying, that's because you lack experience [lit. are less burned]. Because you are young. A clever person seeks to walk the shorter path rather than taking the long way around, so it seems better to awaken right now rather than realizing it after living another 20 or 30 years.

-. Mr. Yi, it sounds like you're speaking without knowing what "love" is. Even if it's not the love of Buddha, can't we humans achieve a love close to Buddha's among ourselves? If, because of love, one becomes more beautiful and elevated, isn't that a heart close to God? I still cannot understand your statement that one must necessarily leave the body behind to be beautiful and supreme. Didn't you yourself praise love that wasn't the love of heavenly beings in works like "The Pioneer" (개척자), "Rebirth" (재생), or "The Heartless" (무정)?
Yi. Well. Back then, I didn't know. I said that because I hadn't awakened.

-. Then, are all the world's writers and artists today praising love as beautiful and sublime simply because they don't know?
Yi. That's right. Love that is not "eternal love" – this love is the love of Buddha – cannot be supreme, cannot be beautiful. What kind of sublime love, what supreme love can it be, when these loves are broken more easily than quitting smoking?<53>

**Summary**

In this 1938 interview, Yi Kwangsu argues that true, sublime love transcends physical desire and mirrors the detached, universal love of the Buddha. He dismisses worldly romantic love as transient and immature, comparing it to childish attachments like his daughter's fleeting promise or his son's brief obsession with a slingshot. He believes that this higher, spiritual love, free from carnal desire, is attainable by recognizing the "eternal existence" within the temporary body, citing figures like Buddha and Jesus as examples, despite acknowledging the difficulty of overcoming ingrained human habits (citing the monk Kyŏnghŏ). He now repudiates the passionate love depicted in his earlier famous novels, claiming he wrote them out of ignorance before attaining spiritual enlightenment.

From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it captures Yi Kwangsu, a leading intellectual and novelist, articulating a shift towards Buddhist spiritualism and detachment during a period of intense Japanese colonial rule. His rejection of worldly attachments and passionate love, advocating instead for a transcendent, almost ascetic ideal, could be seen as a personal philosophical evolution, perhaps a form of escapism or adaptation to the oppressive political climate. His dismissal of his earlier influential works championing modern romance ("The Heartless," etc.) marks a significant, and perhaps controversial, turn for a figure central to Korean modern literature, potentially reflecting the broader pressures and ideological shifts occurring among Korean intellectuals in the late 1930s.

기사제목	李光洙氏의 戀愛觀, 至高한 부처님 사랑의 境地에까지
기사형태	대담·좌담

李光洙氏의 戀愛觀, 至高한 부처님 사랑의 境地에까지
-. 선생님의 연애관을 듯고 싶습니다.
李. 연애관이요.  글세요. 어떻게 말슴하면 좋을까요. 끝없이 높은 사랑을 찾어 향상하려는 마음! 이제 내게 남은 것이란 오직 이것뿐이매 그 무름에 摘應한 대답이 없을가 합니다.
-. 끝없이 높은 사랑이라니요?
李. 하늘 사람의 사랑입니다. 즉 부처님에 갓가운 사랑이란 말이죠.
-. 가령 구체적으로 말슴하신다면?
李. 나는 사랑은 모-든 것 중에 가장 신비하고 가장 숭고한＜49＞ 것이라고 생각합니다. 그러나 꼭 같은 탄소로 숫도 되고 석묵도 되는 반면에 금강석도 되는 모양으로 다같이 사랑이라 하드래도 천차만별의 계단이 있고 품이 있는 것이라고 믿읍니다. 이성간의 사랑에 있어서도 마찬가지로 음탕 청년남녀의 사랑과 賢士淑女의 사랑과를 같이 볼 수는 없* 것이 아니겠읍니까. 그야말로 그 사이에는 하늘과 땅만한 가치의 차이가 있는 것입니다.
-. 그러니까 *장 신비한 사랑 가장 숭고한 사랑 - 즉 선생님의 말슴을 빈다면 하늘사람의 사랑은 육체의 욕망을 全然 떠나는 데서래야만 있을 수 있단 말슴이지요?
李. 그렇죠. 세상엔 육체의 결합을 목적으로 하는 사랑이 가장 많을 것입니다. 적어도 하늘사람의 사랑이 아니라 하드래도 육체의 결합과 아울너 정신에 대한 사모를 깍하는 사랑이 아니면 안될 것은 물론이겠지만 그보다도 내가 말하는 것은 한층 더 올나가서 육체의 사랑을 全然 떼어 버려야 한다는 것입니다.
-. 부처님도 아니요, 하느님도 아닌 사람에게 그런 신비한 사랑 - 고상한 사랑이 있을 수 있읍니까.
李. 그것은 일시적인 우리 육체 속에 있는 영원한 존재를 인식하는 사람이면 될 수 있읍니다.
-. 세상에 그런 사람이 어디 있겠읍니까. 李泰俊씨 소설 「黃眞伊」에 보면 어떤 道僧이 선생님이 理想하는 높은 사랑을 찾어서 온갓 것을 다 버리고 심지어 먹고 마시는 것까지 全然잇고 정말 그야말로 참 부처님이 다되여 가댓는데 黃眞伊에게 유혹받었다는 이얘기가 있어요. 그것은 「영원한 존재」를 인식치 못했든 까닭일까요?＜50＞
李. 그런 이얘기가 많지요. 그 道僧 뿐 아니라 옛날에 鏡虛같은 중도 육체의 욕망을 떠날 수가 없어서 퍽 괴로워했다니까요. 어느때는 길가에서 엡븐 색씨를 보고 달여가서 입을 마쳤다나요. 그래서 그 제자가 여기에 질문하여 스님같이 道가 높으신 이가 그것이 어쩐 일입니까 하고 말한즉 鏡虛의 말이 마음으로 생각하는 것이나 생각한 것을 시행하는 것이 무엇이 다르겠느냐고 했다는 말도 있고 또 어느때는 어느 大師를 찾어가서 술을 가저오라 해서 술을  먹고 술을 먹은 후엔 또 갈보를 대려오라고 했다드라구요. 그러니까 그 술을 사다 대접한 大師가 술까지는 사다 대접했지만 갈보만은 하는 수 없었든 까닭에 역시 스님같이 道가 높으신 이가 이래서 쓰겠읍니까 하고 질문한즉 鏡虛가 얼골이 붉어지며
「頓情難同佛多生習氣深이」라 대답했다는 것입니다. 누구나 그럴 것입니다. 사람의 육체를 쓰고나서 마음으로 부처님이 되기를 원하고 바라지만 오래가젔든 習生은 참 끊어 버리기가 어려운 것인 줄 알어요.
-. 그러니까 육체를 쓰고 난 사람으로선 부처님이 될 수 없잖어요?
李. 석가여래든가 여러 보살이라든가 예수라든가 하는 이들은 사람의 육체를 쓰고 나온 사람이였으니까요.
-. 그이들은 육체의 욕망이란 것이 전연 없었을까요?
李. 전연 없었읍니다. 육체의 욕망을 간직한 채로는 남을 위할 수가 없으니까요?
-. 그래두 석가여래는 결혼을 해서 안해가 훌융히 있지 않었읍니까.
李. 결혼하시긴 했지만 道를 깨달은 뒤엔 그 부인을 제자로 삼었읍니다.
-. 세상사람이 모다 선생님같이 그런 생각만 가진다면 세상에 살 자미가 없을 것 같애요.
