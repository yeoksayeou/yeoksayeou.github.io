Confidential Room

Youth Training Centers Established Widely in the City - Hwasin Also Prepares Wooden Swords and Other Equipment -
The Hwasin Department Store group in the city, which has over 1,000 employees and clerks, has decided to implement military drills for general clerks under the current situation. Having already established a youth training center under the guidance of the military command for some time, they had been struggling with a shortage of training equipment such as bayonets and wooden swords (木刀). Now, they are preparing these on a large scale and plan to extensively implement national defense ideology and citizen training.

Chosŏn Clothing Company Also Shows Good Results with 60 Participants in Youth Training Project
Also, the Chosŏn Clothing Manufacturing Joint-Stock Company (朝鮮被服工作株式會社) located in Yeji-dong in the city established a youth training center to provide military training to over 100 male and female laborers. It has been operating for a long time, and under the leadership of executives like Mr. O Yongt'aek, the results are said to be exceedingly favorable.

Many Missionaries and Others Withdraw from Korea - 600 Depart from Hong Kong on Nov. 3rd for Repatriation -
Due to U.S. national policy (國策), Americans residing in the East have decided to return to their home country. Many Americans in China, such as those in Shanghai and Nanjing, as well as Americans in Tokyo and Yokohama engaged in non-essential business, have already departed. It appears that a similar directive has come from the relevant consulates to Americans within Korea, and many Americans engaged in missionary work and education have already departed or are preparing to depart.
Specifically, on November 3rd, 600 people gathered in Hong Kong to return via a special ship. It is said that many Americans from Seoul, P'yŏngyang, and other areas left Korea intending to board this ship.

The Truth of the Ch'oe Ch'anghak Incident - Detained Due to Complaint by Assemblyman Pak -
Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak (崔昌學) (name change: Songsan - 松山), Korea's "Mining King" (鑛山王), was arrested by the Kyŏngsŏng District Prosecutor's Office (京城地方檢事局) on October 17th on suspicion of mining fraud. He is currently forcibly detained (強制收容) in Sŏdaemun Prison (西大門刑務所), undergoing strict investigation by the Prosecutor's Office. The complainant in this case is Mr. Pak Ch'un'gŭm (朴春琴), Korea's sole Assemblyman (代議士), and the defendant is Mr. Songsan Ch'anghak (松山創學), known as Korea's "Mining King." Both are representative figures of Korea. The details of the case, set against the backdrop of the gold rush frenzy in the Korean mining industry under the Incident, paint an interesting picture revealing a glimpse of both the glamorous surface and the bizarre, dark underside. The reasons for the complaint are:
1. Around March of Showa 3 (1928), Mr. Pak Ch'un'gŭm purchased the Ch'ungman Mine (忠滿鑛山), a gold and silver mine located in Nam-myŏn, Ch'osan-gun, North P'yŏngan Province (平北 楚山郡 南面). While negotiating the purchase of some remaining mining sections, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak, aware of the mine's potential, arranged some funds to acquire mining rights for that section and agreed to co-manage it. However, after Mr. Pak Ch'un'gŭm returned east, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak allegedly deliberately neglected the development of the entrusted Ch'ungman and Taech'ang (大昌) mines, leading Mr. Pak Ch'un'gŭm to believe the mines were worthless. Ch'oe then advised Pak to transfer them to Mr. Kim Kihong (金基鴻) of Unjŏng-chō, Sinŭiju (新義州 雲井町), selling them for 75,000 won and allegedly defrauding (騙取) Pak of this amount. Later attempts to negotiate the return of the mines were met with evasion and refusal.
2. In September of Showa 10 (1935), Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak advised Mr. Pak Ch'un'gŭm that the Namsŏk mine (南石鑛山) in Unsi-myŏn, Pyŏktong-gun, North P'yŏngan Province (平北 碧潼郡 雲時面), owned by Pak, was a poor mine (貧鑛) and better abandoned. Mr. Pak, whose management situation was unfavorable at the time, forgot his previous distrust, resumed association with Ch'oe, and trusted Ch'oe's promise to provide aid for political funds, agreeing to sell for 30,000 won. However, it was discovered that just before this, a major ore vein had appeared in the Namsŏk mine, making its future prospects bright <14>, and that Mr. Ch'oe had already sent engineers to investigate the Namsŏk mine before negotiating with Mr. Pak.
This is the content of the complaint. Furthermore, it is said that these Ch'ungman, Taech'ang, and Namsŏk mines, along with dozens of nearby sections, were later sold to Nihon Kōgyō (日本鑛業 - Japan Mining) for 5.8 million won.

Suspicion Over the Whereabouts of Gift Certificates? Will it Spread to OO Circles?
Since Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak's forced detention, various rumors (途聽途說) have been circulating, but as the case is still in the hands of the judiciary (司直), nothing can be definitively stated. One rumor suggests that besides the aforementioned fraud case, a latent bribery incident (贈賄事件) might exist. This pertains to his purchase of a large amount of gift certificates from the OO Department Store in the city last winter. There is speculation whether these gift certificates might have been distributed in OO-related circles. If this is true, the personal affairs of OOOOO could also be shrouded in suspicion (疑雲), drawing considerable attention.

How Much Money Did Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak Spend on Society?
How much money did multi-millionaire Ch'oe Ch'anghak donate (義捐) to society during that time?
1. Recently announced a donation of 100,000 won for the establishment of a technical school (實業學校) in his hometown, Kusŏng (龜城), North P'yŏngan Province (平北).
2. Donated 100,000 won as funds upon the establishment of the Spiritual Mobilization League of Korea (精神總動員朝鮮聯盟).
3. Acquired shares worth tens of thousands of won in the Maeil Sinbo newspaper company (每日新報社).
4. Other national defense contributions and situational donations ranging from 1,000 or 2,000 won to tens of thousands of won each.

10,000 Won for Mr. Yi Sanghyŏp
Mr. Ho Mong Yi Sanghyŏp (何夢 李相協), who contributed greatly to making the Maeil Sinbo (每日新報) what it is today, recently resigned from the position of Vice President and became an auditor (監査役). The company reportedly presented him with 10,000 won as severance pay.
Furthermore, his salary during his tenure was said to be 600 won per month.

Managing Director Kim, 500 Won
The monthly salary of Mr. Kim Tongjin (金東進), the head of the general affairs bureau of the Maeil Sinbo company, who was newly appointed as Managing Director (常務取締役), is said to be about 500 won.

Korean Mine Owners Apparently Considering Advancing into French Indochina and the Dutch East Indies
Among Korean mine owners, the phenomenon of some developing intentions to advance into French Indochina (佛印) or the Dutch East Indies (蘭印), if possible, to develop the underground resources there using the skills and capital honed within Korea, is noteworthy. <15>

Financial World, Mining Industry, Others
Telephone Finance Reaches Two Million Won
- Total Amount in Seoul City -
The value of telephones in Seoul city has soared past 2,000 won each, currently drawing significant public interest. Looking at the situation of small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists in Seoul, many are borrowing money by pledging their privately owned telephones to financiers. According to an investigation by a certain source, the number of pledged telephones is about 2,000 to 3,000 units, and the total amount borrowed against them exceeds 2 million won.

Savings Reach 25 Billion Won
Finance Minister Kawada (河田藏相) has repeatedly mentioned the "freedom of savings" (貯金の自由性). This was intended to alleviate fears that the amount one could withdraw from bank savings might be limited, officially stating that such a thing would absolutely not happen. Currently, the total amount deposited in ordinary and savings banks nationwide reaches the massive sum of approximately 25 billion won.

One Million Won in Net Profit. Chosŏn Yusen Doing Well
The golden age of the maritime transport industry has arrived, and the net profit for the first half of the year for the Chosŏn Yusen Company (朝鮮郵船會社 - Korea Mail Steamship Co.) reportedly reached the large sum of 980,000 won.

160 Americans in Kŭmgangsan; 30,000 Sightseers a Month
Around the time of the mid-autumn full moon and the autumn foliage season, Kŭmgangsan (金剛山 - Diamond Mountains) is attracting numerous sightseers from both within Korea and abroad. Recently, since September, the number has reached around 30,000 per month. Among them are many foreign tourists. During the month of September alone, there were:
Americans: 165
Germans: 140
And many other nationalities.

Einstein Becomes a US Citizen
Dr. Einstein (61), originator of the theory of relativity, who received a warm welcome from academic circles upon visiting Tokyo, disagreed with Germany's Nazi party and left his homeland. After spending several years in the United States, he recently became a naturalized US citizen.

Film and Theater World, Others
Filmmakers Conform to the New Order
The Government-General (總督府) plans to gather all filmmakers and hold lectures at the Communications Business Hall (遞信事業會館) around mid-November. The subjects include National History (國史), National Policy (國策), Eastern Spirit (東洋精神篇), and Western Spirit (西洋精神篇). Instructors are expected to be professors from Keijō Imperial University (城大), such as Prof. Karashima Takeshi (辛島驍). It is said that only filmmakers with the best results in these subjects will be able to complete registration procedures.

Official Association of Korean Filmmakers
The Government-General <17> renamed the existing Chosŏn Filmmakers Association (朝鮮映畵人協會) to the Official Association of Korean Filmmakers (映畵人公認協會). Messrs. An Chonghwa (安鍾和), Yi Chaemyŏng (李載明), and Yi Ch'angyong (李創用) were invited to decide on various matters. Mr. An Chonghwa is reportedly the managing director (常務理事), and Mr. Kim Chŏnghyŏk (金正革) is the secretary-general (書記長). From now on, producing films or undertaking any activity among filmmakers will require permission from this Official Association (公認協會). For instance, even submitting a script for censorship requires prior certification from the Official Association before the censorship department will accept it. The office is likely to be located in the Government-General's censorship office.

Rumor of Establishing a Two Million Won Film Company
There is a rumor that a 2 million won film company is being established through the mediation of Central Council Advisor (中樞院參議) Han Sangnyong (韓相龍), Kawakita Nagamasa (川喜多長政) of Tokyo's Tōwa Shōji (東和商事), Manchurian Film Association (滿映) figures like Negishi Kan (根岸寬), Koryŏ Film's (高麗) Yi Ch'angyong (李創用), and other film-related individuals from the broadcasting station, Financial Cooperative Federation (金融組合聯合會), etc.

Works to Be Released Soon
Yi Kyuhwan's (李圭煥) work, Tolswoe (돌쇠), Ch'oe In'gyu's (崔寅圭) "Homeless Angel" (집없는 天使), and "Wife's Ethics" (안해의 倫理), produced by Sŏ Hangsŏk (徐恒錫), are expected to premiere soon.

"Festival of the People" Long Run
The Olympia "Festival of the People" (民族の祭典 - Minzoku no Saiten) ran for one month at the Kyŏngsŏng Takarazuka Theater (京城寶塚劇場). This is the first time a film has had such a long run in Korea. Attendance reached 100,000, and total revenue was 80,000 won.

Manchurian Performing Arts Association
The Manchurian Performing Arts Association (滿洲演藝協會) will now handle the distribution of plays and films to Manchuria through this association. A branch office has been established in Korea at the O.K. Record Company on Namdaemun-ro 1-chōme (南大門通 一丁目), with Mr. Yi Ch'ŏl (李喆) appointed as Director (所長).

Mr. An Ikcho's Career Change
Former Manchukuo Army Medical Major (滿洲國軍醫少佐) Mr. An Ikcho (安益祚) has decided to manage the Columbia Musical Troupe (콜탬비아 악극단). Everyone hearing this news is reportedly wide-eyed at this 180-degree turn.

Kim Soyŏng's Performance Fee: 1,000 Won
Kim Soyŏng (金素英) is appearing in "Spring on the Peninsula" (半島の 봄), produced by Choyŏng (朝映 - Chosŏn Film Co.), and her performance fee amounts to the large sum of 1,000 won.

Yi Hyangran's Visit to Korea <18>

Manchurian Film Association's (滿映) popular star, Yi Hyangran (李香蘭 - Ri Kōran), is scheduled to come to Korea in mid-November. While screening her starring film "Beautiful Sacrifice" (아름다운 犧牲), she will also perform attractions (live stage appearances). The duration is said to be one week.

Visit of Manchurian Permanent Movie Theater Owners
A group of 10 owners of permanent movie theaters in Manchuria visited Kyŏngsŏng's permanent theaters for inspection on October 21st, guided by Mr. Kubo (久保) of the Manchurian Film Association's Second Distribution Department (滿映第二配給部員).

Net Profit of the Film "Daffodil"
Choyŏng's (朝映) production "Daffodil" (水仙花) is said to be the first Korean film produced with low costs. Just from its screening in Kyŏngsŏng, it generated 16,000 won in revenue, resulting in a net profit of 4,000 won after deducting the production cost of 12,000 won.

Personnel News
Filmmakers who recently went to Tokyo include: Yun Pongch'un (尹蓬春) and Yi Sinjae (李信哉) of Hanyang (漢陽); Yi Ch'angyong (李創用) of Koryŏ (高麗); Yi Chaemyŏng (李載明), Okamoto Seitarō (岡本淸太郞), and Kim Soyŏng (金素英) of Choyŏng (朝映). Mr. Pak Kich'ae (朴基采) amicably resigned from Koryŏ Film Company (高麗映畵會社). Mr. Chu Yŏngsŏp (朱永涉), a Waseda University (早大) graduate who studied for one year at Tōhō's Kyoto studio (東寶 京都 撮影所), is expected to begin work soon on his first film after returning to Korea. <19>

Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi Arrives in Yokohama - Coming December 3rd, Letter from Chile -
Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi (崔承喜), the peninsula's dancer who was touring Europe and the Americas, introduced Korean art through her genius to personalities in various countries without reserve, and is set to arrive in Yokohama on December 3rd after three years. Here is a recent letter sent during her performance tour in Chile, South America:
"Because war broke out in Europe (歐羅巴), I left Paris (巴里) and came to New York (紐育), USA, where I spent half a year touring major cities on the North American East and West coasts. Then, I reached Central and South America, conducting a dance tour, and came to Chile (智利). The places I passed through include Brazil (뿌라질), Argentina (阿然丁), etc. From now, I plan to tour Peru (베루-), Colombia (컬넘비아), Panama (巴奈馬), Mexico (멕시코), etc., and then head back towards my beloved homeland. Currently, South America has become famous as a stage for Western artists because of the war in Europe.
As you may know, the Latin American (라텐·아메리카) nations were pioneered by descendants of Spain (西班牙) and Portugal (葡萄芽) and have a history of only 400 years. However, having received the cultural traditions of the Latin people for several thousand years, I had heard that the audience level is very high, and indeed it was so. I performed for 3 to 4 days in each place. Not only in large cities with populations over 2 million like Buenos Aires (베노스) or São Paulo (산페드로) where many resident Japanese nationals (在留邦人) live, but even in Santiago (산체코) with 600-700 thousand people, all performances were sold out for three consecutive days. Since my performance was the first Eastern dance performance in this region, they watched very eagerly and provided sincere critiques.
Due to daily performances, I couldn't leisurely savor the exotic atmosphere (異國情調) of each place, but the magnificent view of the Andes mountains (오데재로 산맥 - Andes?) and the sweet, sorrowful, yet passionate Spanish (西班牙) melodies encountered everywhere left a deep impression on me as a traveler. And the sight of young women with dark hair and dark eyes rushing to the dressing room (樂屋) after performances asking for autographs (싸인) reminded me inevitably of when I performed in Tokyo three years ago.
It has already been three years since I came abroad. Having concluded this endless world performance tour for now, I am happy to think of meeting you all this winter. I pray for the health and prosperity of all the resident Japanese (內地人) and Korean compatriots (朝鮮人 同胞) in various parts of South America who gave heartfelt support to this immature younger sister (弟 - used humbly for oneself), despite everything.
- October 27th, from Chile (智利) - <20>

**Summary**

This collection of news snippets from December 1940 covers various aspects of Korean society under Japanese rule during wartime. Topics include the mandatory military-style youth training being implemented even in private companies like Hwasin Department Store, the departure of American missionaries due to US policy changes, the high-profile fraud and potential bribery case involving the "Mining King" Ch'oe Ch'anghak and pro-Japanese figure Pak Ch'un'gŭm, Ch'oe's philanthropic activities, personnel changes at the Maeil Sinbo newspaper, economic news (telephone pawn value, national savings, shipping profits), tourism statistics for Kŭmgangsan, developments in the increasingly controlled film industry (new official association, production news, star visits like Yi Hyangran), and the impending return of world-famous dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi from her international tour. From a historical perspective, the article vividly illustrates the intensification of wartime mobilization (youth training, home front slogans, Spiritual Mobilization League), the tightening state control over culture (film association), the colonial economic dynamics (mining scandals, Japanese corporate involvement), the impact of international affairs (US withdrawal), and the complex lives of prominent Korean figures navigating the colonial system (Ch'oe Ch'anghak, Pak Ch'un'gŭm, Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi). The details on the film industry regulation and the Ch'oe Ch'anghak case offer specific insights into colonial governance and elite interactions.

機密室
市內에 靑訓, 多數設立-和信에서도 木刀等器具準備-
一千餘名의 社員과 店員을 가지고 있는 시내 和信百貨店系에서는 이번에 時局下에 있어어 일반 점원에게 군사적 조련을 실시하기로 되여, 일직부터 군사령부의 지도를 받어가며, 청년훈련소를 실시하였든 중, 총검, 木刀 등의 조련기구의 부족으로 애써오다가, 이번에 대대적으로 준비하고, 크게 국방사상과 국민훈련을 실시하리라 한다.

朝鮮被服會社서도 六十名 靑年訓練事業의 成績良好
또 시내 禮智町에 있는 朝鮮被服工作株式會社에서도 남녀 노무원 100여명에게 군사훈련을 주기 위하여 청년훈련소를 두고, 오래전 부터 실시하여 오는데 吳龍澤씨 등 간부의 선도로 성적이 대단히 양호하여 간다고 한다.

宣敎師等多數退鮮-11월3일의 육백명의 香港發로 歸國-
米國 國策에 의하여, 동양에 있는 米國人들이 본국으로 귀국하기로 되여, 이미 上海 南京 등 在支米人 다수와 東京 橫濱의 米國人들도, 不急의 業務에 종사하는 자는 다수 퇴거하였는데, 朝鮮내에 있는 米國人에게도 關係 領事들로부터 그런 通達이 온 모양으로, 선교방면과 학교방면에 종사하는 米國人이 이미 出發, 혹은 출발준비를 하는 자가 多數하다.
특히 11月 3日에 香港에서 600명이나 집합하여 特別船便으로 돌아가기로 되어, 이 船便에 타고저 서울, 평양 등지의 米國人이 조선을 떠난 자가 여럿이 있다고 한다.

崔昌學氏 事件眞相-朴代議士의 告訴로 收容中-
朝鮮의 鑛山王 崔昌學씨(創氏松山)는 지난 10월 17일에 鑛山詐欺被疑事件으로 京城地方檢事局에 검거되어 방금 西大門刑務所에 강제수용되고 檢事局의 手로 준엄한 취조를 진행하는 중인데 同사건은 고소인이 조선에 유일한 代議士 朴春琴씨이고 피고인은 조선의 鑛山王이라는 松山創學씨이다. 양쪽이 다 조선의 대표적 인물인데 사건의 내용은 事變 下 黃金홍수의 물결에 광분한 朝鮮鑛業界의 호화로운 표면과 기괴한 암흑의 裏面의 일단이 그리여 있는 한 흥미있는 이야기 거리가 되었다. 고소 이유는
1. 昭和 3월경 朴春琴씨가 平北 楚山郡 南面 소재의 金銀鑛山 忠滿鑛山을 買收 일부 殘鑛區의 買收교섭 중에 崔昌學씨가 同광산의 유망한 것을 알고 同광구의 광업권을 획득하려고 일부자금을 융통하여 공동경영하기로 되었는데 朴春琴씨가 歸東 후 崔昌學씨는 위탁받은 忠滿 광산과 大昌 광산의 개발을 고의로 내버려 두어 朴春琴씨로 하여금 광산은 무가치한 것으로 생각게 하여 新義州 雲井町 金基鴻씨에게 양도하기를 권하여 75,000원에 팔고, 이것을 騙取하였다. 후일 광산의 반환교섭을 하였으나 좌지우지하고 응하지 않었다.
2, 昭和 10년 9월 崔昌學씨는 朴春琴씨 소유의 平北 碧潼郡 雲時面의 南石광산은 貧鑛이니 내버리는 편이 좋다고 권하여 朴씨는 당시 경영상태가 자미없는 데다 전번의 불신을 잊고, 다시 交遊를 하며 정치자금의 원조를 하여 준다는 崔씨의 말을 신용하고 3만원에 매각을 승낙하였다. 그런데 南石광산은 바로 그 전에 一大광맥이 일어나 장래 유망하게＜14＞ 되었을 때 崔씨는 朴씨와 교섭 전에 이미 同광산에 기사를 보내며 南石광산을 조사한 것이 판명되었다.
이런 고소장의 내용이다. 더욱이 이 忠滿, 大昌, 南石광산은 부근 일대의 수십구를 합하여 그후 日本鑛業에 580만원에 팔었다 한다.

商品券行衛에 疑惑? OO방면에도 飛火하는가
崔昌學씨가 강제수용된 뒤 여러 가지 途聽途說이 분분하나, 아직 사건은 司直의 손에 있으므로 무어라 할 수 없는데 일설에는 前記 사기사건 외에 贈賄事件이 伏在하여 있는 듯 하다는데 그것은 작년 겨울에 氏가 시내OO백화점에서 다액의 상품권을 산일이 있는 바, 이 상품권이 OO관계방면에 흩어지지나 않는가, 이것이 사실이라면 OOOOO의 신변도 疑雲 중에 싸여 있다고 볼 것으로 매우 주목을 끄은다.

崔昌學씨는 사회에 얼마나한 돈을 썼나?
천만장자 崔昌學씨가 그동안 사회에 얼마나한 돈을 義捐하였는가.
1, 고향인 平北 龜城에 實業學校가 설치된다는 데에 돈 10만원을 의연하겠다고 최근에 발표하다.
2, 精神總動員朝鮮聯盟이 설치되자 그 기금으로 10만원을 의연.
3, 每日新報社의 주식 수만원어치를 引受.
4, 기타 국방헌금과 시국적 기부가 혹은 1,2천원으로부터 數萬圓式있었다.

李相協씨에 萬圓
每日新報의 금일의 大를 만드는데 있어 공로가 많은 何夢 李相協씨가 금번 부사장의 직을 辭하고, 監査役이 되었는데, 同社에서는 만원을 퇴직금으로 呈하였다 한다.
더욱 氏의 재직시 보수는 월 600원이었다고 한다.

金常務五百圓
새로 每日新報社 常務取締役으로 선임된 仝社 총무국장 金東進씨의 월보수는 약 500원이 된다고 한다.

여러분도 아시다싶이 라텐·아메리카 諸國은 西班牙, 葡萄芽의 자손들로 의해서 개척된 4백년밖에 되지 않는 역사를 가졌지만 몇 천년간 라텐 민족의 문화의 전통을 받은 관계로, 그 관객층의 수준이 매우 높다는 말을 들었었는데, 실로 그러하였습니다. 나는 각지에서 3일내지 4일간의 공연을 했는데, 인구 2백만 이상의 대도시 베노스나, 在留邦人이 많은 산페드로는 물론, 6,70만인의 산체코까지라도 3일간 모다 滿員을 계속하였습니다. 그것은 동양무용의 공연은 이 방면에서는 내가 최초이었음으로 非常히 열심히 觀賞하여, 진실스럽게 비평하여 주더이다.
