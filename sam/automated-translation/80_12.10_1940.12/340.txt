Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제10호
Publication Date: 1940년 12월 01일
Article Title: 東洋解放 (Eastern Liberation)
Author: 주요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

From the first light on the Pacific waves
To the sunset over the Arabian sands,
There is the pain of the Eastern peoples,
There is the prayer of the Eastern folk.

For the door closed for a thousand years to open,
For the God asleep for a thousand years to awaken,
The angered god with thunder,
The merciful god with tears,
The destroying god with fire,
The establishing god with patience <218>
Scolds the East,
Considers the East,
Revives the East,
Establishes the East.

Making the Heavenly God (天神) emerge from the rock cave.
Welcoming the Great King (大王) from the Divine City (神市).
The people of Muhammad,
The disciples of Buddha,
The followers of Lao Tzu,
Upon their shoulders
The errand of the new day has been loaded.

Iron with iron,
Gunpowder with gunpowder,
Engine with engine,
At last, the foundation work begins.
At last, upon the chosen foundation <219>

Upon the foundation hardened with fire and blood,
A new order arises.

The whip of the East
Is not meant to bind,
But to set free.
The endurance of the East
Is not meant to submit,
But to salvage.
That pain is the pain of healing,
That prayer is the prayer of salvation.

From the first light on the Pacific
To the evening glow of Arabia,
There is the joy of the Eastern peoples.
There is the prayer of the Eastern peoples. <220>
<218-220>

**Summary**

This poem by Chu Yohan, titled "Eastern Liberation," uses powerful imagery to call for a radical transformation and unification of Asia, stretching from the Pacific to Arabia. It portrays a divine force awakening the East from a long slumber, using both destruction (thunder, fire, blood) and creation (tears, patience, foundation work) to establish a "new order." Various religious and founding figures (Japanese, Korean, Islamic, Buddhist, Taoist) are invoked as bearers of this new era. The poem reframes suffering as a necessary step towards healing and liberation, suggesting that the perceived harshness ("whip") is ultimately for freedom. From a historical perspective, the poem is significant as it reflects the Pan-Asianist rhetoric heavily promoted by Imperial Japan during this period (1940), framing Japan's expansionism as a liberation of Asia from Western influence. Historians might find it a clear example of collaborationist literature, showcasing how cultural figures were mobilized or coerced to support the ideology of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere, using themes of shared Asian identity and destiny.

東洋解放
						주요한
太平洋 물결의 갖밝이로부터
亞剌伯 모래의 해질녁까지
東洋의 겨레의 알픔이 있고
東洋의 백성의 빌음이 있다.

千年 다첬든 문 열라고
千年 잠들었든 神 일라고
노하신 신은 우뢰로서
자비의 신은 눈물로서
문히는 신은 불로서
세우시는 신은 인내로서＜218＞
東洋을 꾸짖고
東洋을 혜가리고
東洋을 살우고
東洋을 세우신다.

天神을 바위 굴에서 나오시게 하다.
大王을 神市에서 마지하다.
무하멧의 백성
부다의 신도
라오쯔의 제자
그들의 어깨에
새날의 심부름이 씨웠나니.

쇠는 쇠로써
화약은 화약으로써
엔진은 엔진으로써
이윽고 터 다짐이 시작된다.
이윽고 골라진 터 우에 ＜219＞

불과 피로서 다진 터 우에
새로운 질서가 선다.

동양의 채쭉은
묵그려 함이 아니요
풀어 놓으렵이다.
동양의 참음은
업데려 함이 아니오
건지려 함이다.
그 아픔은 나으려는 아픔이요
그 빌음은 구원하는 빌음이다.

태평양의 갖밝이로부터
아라비의 저녁노을까지
동양의 겨레의 기쁨이 있다.
동양의 겨레의 빌음이 있다. ＜220＞
