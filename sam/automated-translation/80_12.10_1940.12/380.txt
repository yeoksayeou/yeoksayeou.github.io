On October 14th last, Governor-General Minami (南總督), the new president of the newly established National Total Mobilization League (국민총력연맹), announced the outline for the new national organization of the Peninsula (半島) to immediately respond to the new system (신체제). On the 16th, at the provisional provincial governors' meeting, there were again instructions regarding this National Total Mobilization Movement. The guiding principles of this New Organization Movement (新編運動) are national general training (국민총훈련) and expansion of production capacity. We, the people, must exert ourselves further and conform to this policy, praying that an even greater light (광명) may shine upon the land of the Peninsula.

A roundtable discussion listening to the various aspects of war-torn Europe (戰亂歐洲) with Mr. Pae Un-sŏng (裵雲成), the world-renowned painter who returned home after 19 years, driven out by the flames of the Second European War which broke out this time, Mr. Kim Chae-wŏn (金載元), the venerable scholar who returned after over 10 years, and Mr. Chŏng In-sŏp (鄭寅燮), who toured Europe early on, will surely be good reading material (好讀物) this autumn. Furthermore, the meeting of the three Council Members (審議員) speaking on their aspirations and experience regarding compulsory education, an epoch-making great event (一大盛事) in Peninsula policy, is believed to be a distinctive feature (이채) of this month's issue.

In Arts and Sciences (學藝), the "Roundtable Discussing the New System and Literature" is surely a most timely gathering for these times. That Mr. Chŏng In-t'aek's (鄭人澤) pleasant work (快作) "Good People," the poetry of Ch'unwŏn (春園) and Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰), and furthermore, adaptations of the masterpiece "Olenka" by the world-famous literary master Chekhov and the masterpiece "Strait is the Gate" by André Gide, handled by the leading figures of our literary world (我 문단), Yi T'ae-jun (李泰俊) and Yu Chin-o (兪鎭午), are published is something we are confident is a pride of this month's issue. <250>

**Summary**

This editor's postscript from the December 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine highlights the establishment of the National Total Mobilization League under Governor-General Minami, urging readers to support the "new system" focused on national training and increased production for the Japanese war effort. It also promotes upcoming articles, including discussions with Koreans returned from Europe, a feature on compulsory education policy, a roundtable on literature's role in the "new system," and literary contributions, including original works and adaptations of European classics by prominent Korean writers like Yi T'ae-jun and Yu Chin-o. From a historical perspective, the article starkly reveals the magazine's explicit endorsement of Japanese colonial mobilization policies ("new system," "National Total Mobilization League," conforming to policy). Simultaneously, it showcases the continued activity and prominence of Korean intellectuals and artists, illustrating the complex reality of cultural life under intense colonial pressure during wartime, where collaboration and cultural production coexisted.

編輯後記
지난 10월 14일 새로 탄생한 국민총력연맹의 신총재인 南總督은 신체제에 卽應할 半島의 신국민조직의 大綱을 발표하였음, 16일 임시 도지사회의 석상에서 다시 이 국민총력운동에 고나한 훈시가 있었는데 이 新編運動의 지표는 국민총훈련과 생산력 확충인 바, 국민된 我等은 더욱 분발하여 此 정책에 순응하므로써 더욱 큰 광명이 半島天地에 비쳐지기를 빌어마지 않는 바입니다.

금차 勃發된 제2차 歐洲大戰의 전화에 쫓겨 19년만에 귀국한 세계적 화가 裵雲成씨와 10여년만에 귀국한 古老學者 金載元씨와 일찌기 歐羅巴를 유람하고 온 鄭寅燮씨 세 분을 모시고 戰亂歐洲의 諸相을 듣는 좌담회는 今秋의 好讀物일 것이며, 반도정책의 획기적 一大盛事인 의무교육에 관하여 그 포부와 경륜을 말하는 審議員 3씨의 회의도 이 달 본지의 이채일 줄 믿나이다.

學藝에 있어서는 「新體制와 文學을 말하는 座談會」는 이 시국에 가장 적절한 모임이 아닐 수 없으며, 鄭人澤씨의 快作 「착한 사람들」과 春園·朱耀翰씨의 詩歌에 그 밖에 당대에 명성이 높은 세계적 문호 체홉의 걸작 「오렝카」와 앙드레 지드의 걸작 「좁은문」을 我 문단의 중진 李泰俊씨와 兪鎭午씨가 번안하여 실린 것은 이 달 본지의 자랑임을 자신 하나이다.＜250＞
