Military, Government, and Civilian Volunteers Gather to Discuss Korea's "New Order" and the "Leaders and the Masses" Issue

Date & Time: October 19th, 1:30 PM・Location: Hantō Hotel
Sponsored by: Kokumin Kunren Kōenkai (National Training Support Association)・Supported by: Chōsen Shinbunsha, Mainichi Shinpōsha, Keijō Nippōsha

Attendees:
Director of the Government-General Education Bureau: Shiōbara Tokisaburō
Doctor of Medicine: Pak Ch'anghun

Secretary of the Kokumin Kunren Kōenkai: Kanazawa Isamu <78>
Professor, Keijō Imperial University: Karashima Takeshi
Former Chairman of the Chōsen Bunjin Kyōkai (Korean Literati Association): Kayama Mitsuo (Yi Kwangsu)
Vice President, Chōsen Shinbunsha: Nozaki Shinzō

General Secretary, Kokumin Kunren Kōenkai: Fukuda Ryūtaku

President, Samch'ŏlli Company: Kim Tonghwan
Chief Editor, Mainichi Shinpō: Sŏ Ch'un
Managing Director, Hwashin: Chu Yohan

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: The term "New Order" (新體制) has become quite familiar, but while the Taisei Yokusankai (Imperial Rule Assistance Association) has emerged in the Mainland (內地), here on the Peninsula (半島), the Kokumin Sōryoku Chōsen Renmei (National Service League of Korea) has also been powerfully born – meaning the core of the total strength movement centered around His Excellency the Governor-General (總督閣下) has emerged. There is no need for me to elaborate further on this matter, but generally speaking, we stand at a golden opportunity, based on the spirit of the nation's founding (肇國), to realize the construction of a new order in East Asia (東亞), and extending further, the construction of a new world order – in other words, to realize the founding ideals. We feel honored and deeply moved that each of us can mutually offer our assistance (翼贊). However, we believe there might still be some among the general populace who have not fully grasped the purpose (趣旨). Therefore, the Kokumin Kunren Kōenkai requested the gathering of those in pivotal positions within the Government-General or the military, as well as influential figures from various fields, to have a thorough discussion here, further clarify the true spirit of this total strength movement, and deepen the understanding and cooperation of the masses. This is the objective under which this roundtable discussion is being held. Furthermore, as Chairman Nomura Kōen is ill, I will serve as the moderator here.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: This is a roundtable discussion regarding the New Order, which is an extremely broad topic. Today, I would like to hear your opinions, generally speaking, or perhaps more in terms of principles, or somewhat touching upon the spiritual aspects. <79> First, I would like to ask Director Shiōbara, who established the Sōryoku Renmei (Total Strength League), to briefly explain the purpose behind its creation.

○Shiōbara, Director of Education Bureau: The true spirit of the New Order lies in creating the highest level of military preparedness. It is about arranging the structure to enable this. From every field, things that are not useful, no matter how small, should be thoroughly eliminated, and those that promote this goal should be vigorously adopted. To achieve this, a spiritual structure was created in the Mainland as well, enabling concentration on that single point – this, I believe, is the Taisei Yokusankai. Ultimately, it is solely for the purpose of arranging a structure where the total strength of the nation can be focused on completing the strongest and highest level of military preparedness. If considered from a liberalistic perspective, various dissatisfactions might arise – although the rather lofty goal of constructing a New East Asian Order is presented, its completion absolutely cannot be achieved by continuing as before. It cannot be done without adopting the current structure, and therein lies the fundamental concept of the New Order.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: It seems the concept is understood to some extent, but there may still be various difficulties in putting it into practice. Here, first, ideology; second, national training; third, expansion of production capacity – I have listed them one, two, three, but this is not an order representing importance. I believe these three form the backbone of the New Order.

○Chu Yohan: The Sōryoku Renmei might be similar to the Kokumin Seishin Sōdōin Renmei (National Spiritual Mobilization League), but the latter tends to give the impression of overemphasizing only the spirit. When it becomes the Sōryoku Renmei (Total Strength League), it expresses a stronger feeling of confronting challenges not just spiritually but with total strength, giving a reassuring impression that everyone's total power is concentrated in that direction.

○Pak Ch'anghun: The power resulting from the combination of spiritual strength and physical strength is much greater.

○Shiōbara, Director of Education Bureau: We aim for the maximum effort – the nation's total strength must be directed towards the highest fulfillment of national defense. It was for this purpose that the name was changed like this.

○Kim Tonghwan: According to Director Shiōbara's words just now, I understand the main points of the New Order are, first, ideological unification; second, national training; and third, expansion of production capacity. Regarding ideology, looking at the current situation in the Mainland, both the left and right wings are well unified under the national ideology. In Korea as well, the populace has all converged under the great policy of *Naissen Ittai* (內鮮一體 - Japan and Korea as One Body), those resisting *Naissen Ittai* have been annihilated, and those avoiding *Naissen Ittai* have also been left behind by the new era, resulting in a very good outcome. I believe this momentum will lead to even further unification of the national ideology, regardless of Mainland or Korea, but for now, it seems the unification of thought is still insufficient.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: Generally speaking, the Sōryoku Renmei in Korea will likely require considerable effort to be placed upon *Naissen Ittai*.

○Chu Yohan: I am involved in commerce. Among merchants and the general public, there are people who freely criticize government policies, saying things like, "What the officials are doing is self-righteous," or "They said this year's coal supply would be fine, but none of it reaches our hands. There's no way we can stay in business like this." These criticisms play a very significant role in the proper guidance of thought. If things continue like this, the economic New Order cannot possibly succeed either. There is a risk that unless these thoughts are fundamentally corrected, they will bring about <80> negative consequences.

○Shiōbara, Director of Education Bureau: Just as the saying goes, people must cultivate themselves until death; achieving perfection in anything is quite difficult. Making such criticisms out of selfishness is highly inappropriate. Constructive criticism, suggesting "We should proceed this way," or "I wish they would do it this way," is greatly welcome.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: Mr. Nozaki, as a newspaperman, do you have any requests for the Government-General, or any thoughts on the New Order?

○Nozaki Shinzō: Both the Taisei Yokusankai in the Mainland and the Kokumin Sōryoku Renmei's New Order in Korea require a great force welling up from below. Communication from top to bottom, and from bottom to top – those at the top must clearly perceive the situation below, and the subjects below must also diligently obey the intentions from above. This connection must be appropriate, with both sides mutually advancing and cooperating along the same line. Truly becoming one body from top to bottom, one hundred million hearts as one – how to complete the Advanced National Defense State – we must make earnest efforts regarding this. We are now in a transitional period facing this New Order, and various criticisms may appear, but perhaps we don't need to lend an ear to such things. I think we must endure the anxieties that accompany them.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: The essential point is that the government and the people, those who lead and those who are led, must become one body and put the Sōryoku Renmei system into practice. The entire nation needs to develop that mindset.

○Shiōbara, Director of Education Bureau: We must be cautious about self-righteousness. Even when mobilizing with a single command, if it doesn't truly resonate with the hearts of the people, it will end up being detached and useless.

○Kanazawa Isamu: Regarding the New Order, since various things about the New Order in the Mainland started appearing in newspapers and magazines even before the Taisei Yokusankai was formally announced, those on the front lines have been considerably impacted upon learning about them through these channels. For example, a commonplace instance: during the recent election of officers in the neighborhood associations (町會), efforts were made with these things in mind, resulting in quite a few new faces, but the outcome remained unchanged from the old ways; it was merely a replacement of the previous members. Thinking through this, the core issue is the extent to which individuals recognize the current situation and bear the responsibilities of the home front (銃後) with enthusiasm. Considering this, I was struck by a very lonely feeling that finding true leaders is quite difficult. I hope that the government will pay attention to these matters and cooperate somehow in finding new people who can overcome societal challenges, people with true leadership ability. I am confident that many people who can work to prepare the lineup for the New Order, people useful on the front lines of activity, lie hidden.

○Staff Officer Yamanouchi: It is necessary for such people to be elevated from above, from spheres like the Government-General, but on the other hand, unless the voice of the people enters such a mood and the people push them forward, it is not truly genuine. Hitler, I believe, was brewed from the enthusiasm of seventy-five million citizens seeking to overthrow the Versailles system (ペルサイユ體制), and the citizens themselves campaigned and brought him forth. While it can be seen that Hitler emerged due to his own greatness, it can also be seen that the enthusiasm of the entire nation brought him out. Napoleon (ナボレオン) was also the same. <81> The general populace still lacks the strength to pull the total strength structure; the power to push it forward is also insufficient. I keenly feel the necessity for a greater transformation. Taking the opportunity presented by the creation of the Sōryoku Renmei, I hope you will demonstrate a leap in the exertion of total strength. If a powerful leader emerges, they must engage with a sacrificial spirit and self-annihilation for the public good (滅私奉公). As for the general public, each person must concentrate on their own role and push forward. Cultivating this kind of atmosphere is what I believe is most necessary.

○Kanazawa Isamu: The crucial point relates to the leaders who stand above the masses. These masses are extremely pure-hearted and follow the leaders' words well, and they readily come along, as proven by past experience. However, the leaders too must grasp the ideology of the current situation well and guide the masses. To put it bluntly, there seem to be cases where guidance is given by citing the example of Hitler or Mussolini (ムツソリーニ). If I may say so, the enthusiasm possessed by Hitler or Mussolini might certainly be参考 (a reference point), but I believe it is necessary to uphold Japan's traditional spirit, the spirit of *Kannagara* (惟神の精神 - following the way of the gods), and strive forward with special effort. Japan has the spirit of *Kannagara*. It is sufficient for us, the populace, to uphold this traditional spirit and practice it thoroughly in our daily lives. Consequently, the need to desire heroes like in foreign countries does not arise.

○Chu Yohan: With training sessions like today's, lasting about one hour once a month, I think it's impossible to achieve this goal. I would like to see the practical implementation of setting aside appropriate time every Sunday, perhaps having a religious figure speak for an hour and an important person speak for an hour, totaling about two hours. The current situation where many babysitters and mothers gather at regular meetings (常會) means training effectiveness isn't high. Also, merchants and businesses have a custom of closing on Mondays, but the difference in business between closing on Mondays and closing on Sundays when there are many people out is quite large. Even so, for the sake of national health, holidays should utilize Sundays. On this day, everyone should close shop and dedicate it to national general training. The current situation where eating rice curry has become the Sunday event is unacceptable, I think.

○Staff Officer Yamanouchi: National training needs to be done even daily. In my area of responsibility at the Army Command, every morning I gather all my subordinates and give instructions for the day: "Today we will do this," "You do that," "This should be researched with such-and-such attitude," and handle all other coordination. Then, each person tackles their assigned task using their full ability. Thoroughly instilling this mindset is extremely crucial, I believe. Before leaving the office, gather them once more to wrap up the day's work. Essentially, communicate in advance anything that needs coordination for tomorrow's tasks. Even in regional areas, the master who stands in a position of leadership should meet face-to-face with the head clerk or employees every morning, decide "Today's work is thus-and-so," and then exert full effort towards the indicated tasks. Exerting and carrying out total strength training in one's respective post regarding daily work – isn't this the most beneficial approach?

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: In the context of creating the structure for an Advanced National Defense State, from the perspective of national defense issues, may I ask Staff Officer Yamanouchi for a word?

○Staff Officer Yamanouchi: The details are complex and cannot be fully explained in brief, but as indicated by His Excellency the Army Commander in his statement published in the newspapers during the recent Provincial Governors' Conference, the result of the Tripartite Pact (三國條約) essentially means that against the countries that have been confronting Japan with cunning <82> economic warfare, it is absolutely necessary for us to secure our national defense resources sufficiently and enhance our military preparedness to the highest degree in order to win this economic war. Should we lose this resource war, the construction of the New East Asian Order, for which we have endured all sorts of sacrifices, will fall into disadvantage. In short, securing resources – just because Japan, Germany (獨逸), and Italy (伊) have concluded the pact does not mean we can rely on Germany; it is absolutely unacceptable. We must pursue self-sufficiency to the utmost. Acquiring resources from the Dutch East Indies (蘭印) – this we leave to our diplomatic efforts. As for the general populace, each person must work in their respective post, endure whatever hardships necessary, and exert every possible effort to acquire all things considered important resources for national defense. This is the reason why the expansion of production capacity has become a major objective in the New Order. Regarding this expansion of production capacity, the entire nation must help each other. Those in literature should spiritually encourage (鞭撻) this. To carry this out, ultimately, the unification of thought and national training must accompany it as the foundation. Organizations must become one with their leaders; those engaged in mining must devote their entire capacity towards it. As the Government-General hopes, for the areas most needed by the state, whether agriculture or industry, organizations should cooperate as much as possible on a national level to boost production, creating such a structure. Obtaining the sacrificial cooperation of private businesses so that they can exert their utmost capacity – this is what the military most desires to see adopted.

○Professor Karashima: Isn't an accurate intellectual grasp of Japan's world position necessary above all else? Another point is, internally, an accurate recognition of Japan's national polity (國體). Through this, determining the path we should take is the duty of us subjects (臣民), I believe. Herein also lies the significance of the Taisei Yokusankai. That is, literature too must be literature of the subjects. We cannot continue with the feelings of the past. Simultaneously, isn't the demand for ethics necessary in our lives? We must reform our past lives and at the same time strive to build a new life. For a new life, self-annihilation for the public good (滅私奉公) is necessary. Earlier, Mr. Kanazawa mentioned that the frontline driving leadership elements are lacking. The new life movement has begun in all sectors. The only issue is, like adding a bit of salt when water freezes to make it freeze faster, the need to organize the people and to provide more guidance to the youth. Contact with all strata is also necessary. In this way, the thinking of various factions should be integrated towards the same goal. Those who were relatively indifferent or lax should also return to the national perspective. In short, to acquire leaders, the organization must first be renewed.

○Kayama Mitsuo: In Korea, the Government-General takes the central role in operating the Sōryoku Renmei, but isn't the organization of the core body (中核體) ultimately its lifeblood? If this is established, life enters the core body. The core body is ultimately a matter of people. For example, even in places within the thirteen provinces of the Peninsula where thought is corrupted and culture is low, due to the rapid changes in the world of thought, new ideas are being injected. The thinking of a month ago and the thinking today are vastly different, showing rapid changes daily. Therefore, practically speaking, even if it requires some budget allocation, with Director Shiōbara at the center, recruit reliable individuals, people who can become the core body of Imperial subjects (皇國臣民). <83> Train those gathered thoroughly to create a solid core body. I am confident that within about a year, around a thousand reliable individuals can be gathered. Use these to form an unofficial core body. Also, release them into the field to find new comrades. Train the organization formed by gathering these people to make it the core body. Another approach is to create model, ideal instructors and establish model regular meetings (常會). If tens of thousands of these are created, they will exert tremendous propaganda effects. If possible, creating model towns, villages, districts (模範邑、面、里) might prove effective. For this, we must request strong guidance from the Government-General.

○Shiōbara, Director of Education Bureau: That was very informative. Looking at the Yokusankai in the Mainland as an individual, it seems to be divided into three types: first, representatives from various fields; second, influential figures; and third, the true driving elements.

○Kim Tonghwan: The younger driving elements seem more powerful and reassuring. I hope that in the future, more and more elements from that sphere will be brought to the front line.

○Nozaki Shinzō: Training the twenty-three million people of the Peninsula is quite a difficult problem, but to put it very simply and understandably, make them resolve that "You are an officer, you are a soldier." From there, train the ideology of self-annihilation for the public good and prioritizing the public interest.

○Staff Officer Yamanouchi: Each person advancing with the mindset of a warrior – this is most necessary. Taking up arms is the very last resort. More importantly, advancing through fire and water towards the goal set from above, without hesitation – this I believe is the characteristic of the military.

○Chu Yohan: Looking at the actual situation of my employees, there is a huge difference in all aspects depending on whether they have had military training or not. At the moment, I deeply regret that we don't have rifles available.

○Major Kaba: I believe rifles are not necessary. As long as the mental preparedness is there, the goal of training can be sufficiently achieved even unarmed. As I mentioned previously during the roundtable discussion attended by the Chief of Staff at the inauguration ceremony of the Hwashin Youth Training Center, the most important thing is to embody the training learned at the center in actual daily life. If what is taught and its embodiment in daily life are separate, like oil and water, there will be no effect.

○Sŏ Ch'un: Recently, there were exercises for middle schools throughout Kyŏnggi Province, which were very effective. Students slept outdoors (?). They underwent and experienced training hardships they had never tasted before. They keenly felt that the hardships of the officers and soldiers on the actual front lines must be even more intense. They tell these experiences to their parents. The parents who hear this also come to recognize the hardships of the front line. This achieves two effects with one stone. Gathering representative students who gained valuable experience participating in such exercises, holding a roundtable discussion, and publicizing this in newspapers and elsewhere could be very effective, I think. Next, regarding Mr. Kayama's earlier suggestion about creating model villages, I believe this is already being done in Korea. Simultaneously with creating model villages, just as Christ spread Christianity, it will be necessary for pioneers and influential people to engage in propaganda in parallel. Then, regarding the issue of training -- building the Keijō subway through volunteer labor service (勤勞奉仕) – training the populace accustomed to liberalism and capitalism with this mindset – <84> it might sound like tyranny – but in Korea, during the Paekche (百濟) era, once males reached the age of fifteen, they were already involved in the state's public missions, building castles and going to battlefields. This might relate to national land planning, but wouldn't making people perform physical labor be most effective for national training?

○Kanazawa Isamu: Regarding training, just for reference, the United Youth Group (聯合靑年團) went to Hyoch'ang Park (孝昌公園) in Aoba-chō several times, taking turns, and also visited the Gokoku Shrine (護國神社). The results were very good; we were able to gain the experience of sacred labor through a life of sweat. Lectures and such didn't show significant effects. I am convinced, based on experience, that character building and training are most effective when they involve practical action. With the Shūyōdan's (修養團) White Ethics movement, practices like quiet sitting (靜座) and recitation of Imperial poems (御製) were conducted and achieved great results. I keenly feel that the effects gained through practical training are extremely significant, just for your reference.

○Major Kaba: National training must be carried out through mutual drilling and polishing. Ideology, in particular, is something to be most mindful of. Although I myself was educated amidst liberalistic and individualistic thought, I have since purified and trained myself. Liberalistic and individualistic thought must be corrected and purified by oneself. It is crucial to firmly establish one's own footing and advance with a spirit of service towards the goals of Nation First, National Defense First, and Production First.

○Kim Tonghwan: Looking at the lineup within the New Order in the Mainland, one sees people with considerable leadership experience who were involved in the left-wing movement (左翼運動). In Korea too, among those who were involved in the nationalist movement in the past, if there are outstanding individuals who have liquidated their past thoughts and truly become Imperial subjects (皇國臣民), isn't today the most perfect opportunity to give them a chance to be greatly useful?

○Chu Yohan: From the perspective of expanding production capacity, regarding the establishment of the Advanced National Defense State in the industrial sector – educate industrialists more proactively, pushing them from the Government-General and military perspectives. For those who seem unlikely to be educated, find new industrialists through leader training. Today, among general industrialists, some are uncertain about what is necessary and how they must change their management methods. For these people, I hope a firm direction will be shown.

○Fukuda Ryūtaku: Regarding the issue of promoting the national language (Japanese - 國語普及) and youth training in Korea, what are your thoughts for the future? May I ask Major Kaba?

○Major Kaba: The national organization based on the New Order, spiritually speaking, is ultimately the result of integrating the individual mindset or attitude of each citizen under the establishment of an Advanced National Defense State. Therefore, looking at the current situation on the Peninsula, it is necessary to break away from the conventional profit-oriented, liberalistic spirit and reset the spirit towards service based on one's profession/duty (職能奉公). For this, I believe training is particularly necessary. Also, conducting training with interpreters is problematic, so I think the promotion of the national language (國語普及) is an urgent task. Therefore, I believe we must somehow enhance the youth training currently underway and strengthen and expand the efforts of Mr. Nomura Kōen, chairman of the Kokumin Kunren Kōenkai, who is diligently implementing national language promotion based on the New Order's public interest principle using his private funds. I think this issue is the most critical matter, both from the perspective of the Peninsula's future and from a national defense standpoint. <85>

**Summary**

This article features a roundtable discussion among high-ranking Japanese military officials, government administrators, and collaborating Korean elites in 1940 about implementing the wartime "New Order" (新體制) in colonial Korea. The discussion centers on achieving ideological unity through the *Naissen Ittai* (Japan-Korea One Body) policy, intensifying national training (including physical labor and daily reinforcement), expanding production for the war effort (explicitly linked to the Tripartite Pact and resource acquisition), and finding suitable "leaders" to mobilize the masses effectively. A second section presents brief survey responses from Japanese figures regarding their efforts to explain Korea in Japan and their views on intermarriage between Japanese and Koreans. The article starkly reveals the colonial administration's focus on total mobilization for Japan's war effort, the co-option of Korean elites, the emphasis on ideological indoctrination (*Naissen Ittai*, *Kōkoku Shinmin*), and the explicit goal of creating an "Advanced National Defense State." Historians might find the frank discussion on leadership (including seeking former leftists/nationalists turned loyal), the practical methods of training proposed (daily routines, labor), the link made to global events (Tripartite Pact), and the survey on intermarriage particularly revealing about colonial policies and attitudes during this period.

○福田龍鐸 新體制と云ふ言葉は大分聞き慣らされて居りますが、內地に於きましては大政翼贊會と云ふものが出來、又我半島に於ても國民總力朝鮮聯盟と云ふものが力强く誕生を見たのであります-總督閣下を中心とした總力運動の根幹を成すものが生れた譯でありまして、此の事に關しては縷縷私から申上る迄もありませんが大體我々と致しましては、肇國の精神によりまして、東亞の新秩序建設、延ひては世界の新秩序建設を實現する-つまり肇國の御理想を實現すると云ふ絕好の機會に立至つて居るのでありまして、各自我々が俱に翼贊致すことの出來る事を光榮に思ひ且感激して居る次第でありますが一般民衆の中には未だ十分に趣旨を徹底してゐない向きもあるやうに考へる次第でありますので國民訓練後援會に於きましては總督府或は軍部の樞要な地位に居らるゝ方々、又各方面の有力な方々にお集りを願つて、こゝで十分にお話を致し益々此の總力運動と云ふものゝ眞の精神を明らかにしまして、より大衆の理解と協力を深めたいと云ふ、目的の下に、本座談會を開催した次第であります、尙會長野村弘遠氏が、病氣の爲私が玆に、司會の役を果したいと存じます。
○福田龍鐸 新體制に對する座談會となつて居りますが之は甚だ廣汎に亘る問題でありまして今日は一般的と云ひますか、總論的と云ひますか多少精神的な方面に亘つて御意見を拜聽したひと存じます。＜79＞先づ最初に總力聯盟を作られました䀋原閣下に、この總力聯盟を作られました御趣旨を簡單にお願ひ致します。
○鹽原學務局長 新體制の本當の精神は最高の軍備を作ると云ふことにあります。それを作り得る樣な態勢を整へる、どの方面からも多少に不拘役立たないものはどしく除外し去つて之を助長するものはどしく取上げて行く、之をする爲に內地でも精神的に、その一點に集注し得る樣に精神態勢を作られたのであり之が大政翼贊だらうと思ひます。つまる所最强最高の軍備を完成する爲國民の總力を集注し得る樣な態勢を整へる爲に他ならない、自由主義的に考へるならば、いろいろ不平も生れて來るかも知れぬが-東亞新秩序の建設と云ふ寧ろ非常に高い目標が示されて居るけれども之が完遂の爲にはどうしても從來の儘では出來ない、今の樣な態勢を採らねば出來ないのであり、そこに新體制の根本理念があるのだと思ひます。
○福田龍鐸 理念は或る程度理解して居る樣ですが矢張り之を實踐して行くと云ふ上に於てはいろく困難な問題も御座ぃませう。
玆に先づ第一に思想、第二に國民の訓練第三に生産力の擴充、一、二、三と並べましたが之は重要性を表した順序ではありません-此の三つのものが新體制の中に筋金を成すのだらうと信じて居ります。
○朱耀翰 總力聯盟と云ふのは國民精神總動員聯盟と同じ樣なものかも知れませんが、然し精神總聯盟は精神のみに偏重した印象を受け易く、總力聯盟となれば精神のみでなく總力を以てぶつ突かると云ふ强い感じを現して、總力を皆か其の方に集中すると云ふ賴もしい印象を與へられる樣です。
○朴昌薰 精神上の力と體力とを二つのものが合致された力がもつと大きな譯で、
○鹽原學務局長 最大限度にやらう-最高の國防充實に國民の總力が對つて行かなければ、-此の目的から此の樣な名前に改めたのです。
○金東煥 只今の鹽原閣下の御話に新體制の主眼とする處が第一に思想統一、第二に國民訓練、第三に生産力擴充のことゝ承はりましたが、思想のことは內地の現狀を見ましても左右兩翼が國家思想にうもく統一されて居るし、朝鮮としましても內鮮一體の大方針に民衆が皆歸一され內鮮一體に抗ふものは擊滅され內鮮一體を避ける者之亦新時代に取殘される非常に良い結果となりましたが、此の勢ひを今後は內鮮を問はず尙一層國民思想が統一されることゝ存じますが、今のところ未だく思想の統一が不充分の樣に見受けられる樣です。
○福田龍鐸 大體に於て朝鮮の總力聯盟は內鮮一體の上に少からぬ努力が注かれねばならないでせう。
○朱耀翰 私は商賣に携つて居りますか、商人や一般の人々にはよく政府の政策のことを批判したり、又役人のやつてる事は獨善的だ、今年の炭は大丈夫と云つても我々の手には少しも這入らないじやないか、これではどうしても商賣が立つて行かないとか、平氣で話してゐる人があるが、之等の批評は思想の善導と云ふことに非常に重大な役割を持つてゐる、斯くては經濟上の新體制もうまく行く筈はない、之等の思想を根本的に是正してかゝらねば惡影響を齎す結果となる虞れが＜80＞ある樣です。
○鹽原學務局長 人は死ぬ迄修養を積まねばならぬ假令の通り、何でも完全無缺と云ふことは仲み難しい。利己主義からそうした批評をするのは甚た宜しくない。斯く進まねばならぬ、斯くして慾しいと云ふ如き建設的な批判なら大ひに結構だが。
○福田龍鐸 野崎さんに新聞人として總督府に對して希望して居らるゝことなり、又新體制に對する御感想なりありましたら、一つ、
○野崎眞三 內地に於ける大政翼贊會も朝鮮に於ける國民總力聯盟の新體制も下から盛り上る偉大な力と云ふものがなくてはならない。上からの下達と云ふこと、下からの上達と云ふこと、上に於ては下情關係をよく達觀し、下、臣民は亦上意をよく遵奉して、この結合宜しきを得、兩々相俊つて同一の線に協力をする、眞に上下一體となつて一億一心如何にして高度國防國家を完成するかと云ふことに就て眞劒な努力を拂ふ、今はこの新體制に立向ふ過渡期であり色々な批評も現れませうが、そうしたものに々耳を籍さなくともいゝじやないか、それなどの不安が附總ふのは忍ばなければならないじやないかと思ひます。
○福田龍鐸 要は官民が、指導する方も、指導される方も一體となつて總力聯盟の制度を實踐に移して行く、國民全體がそう云ふ氣持ちになつて行く必要があるでせう。
○鹽原學務局長 獨善と云ふことは注意しなけれぱならぬ、一聲の號令で動かす場合でも、本當に民衆の心になつて行かなければ、之が遊離して終つては駄目な譯です。

私自身旣に獨身なりしかは半島婦人慾しかりした
京城日報學藝部長 寺田瑛

一 講演などの鹿爪らしいことをした機會はありませんが、友人知己には極力朝鮮といふものに對して認識を新にせねばならぬといふことを樣々の例を引いて話して來たつもりです。
二 私には娘があります、緣談の場合、結局婿選びをするには優秀な靑年を對象とすることは勿論です。半島靑年でも優秀であれば對象となり得ることは當然ですたゞその場合兩者に於て異る風俗や習慣生活に融ち込んで行く努力は絶對に必要です。娘に强制する意志はありませんが、私はその點に差別は設けないつもりです。私自身現に獨身であつたら優秀な半島婦人を迎へるかも知れません。
