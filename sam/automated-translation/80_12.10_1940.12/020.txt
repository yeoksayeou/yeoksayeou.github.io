Extreme Punishment for Economic Crimes

Interest among Koreans regarding Germany (獨逸) has deepened further since the establishment of the Japan-Germany-Italy Tripartite Pact (日獨伊 三國同盟). The Government-General Police Affairs Bureau (總督府 警務局) has taken the opportunity of the arrival of a Hitler Youth (히틀러유-겐도) leader in Korea (來鮮) to inform the public about the state of enforcement of domestic laws in Germany under wartime conditions. The important points are as follows:

One, anti-state crimes, without exception, receive extreme punishment such as the death penalty or permanent loss of honorary rights.

One, heavy penal servitude (重懲役) is imposed on those who disseminate theme broadcasts from the French Strasbourg (佛領 슈도라스불쿠) broadcasting station.

One, a housewife who listened to a German-language broadcast from a foreign country in her kitchen for 10 minutes received a 9-month prison sentence.

One, violators of the Wartime Price Ordinance (戰時價格令) and labeling regulations face business closure (營業閉銷).

One, a butcher couple involved in the crime of black market dealing (密賣買) were both sentenced to 10 years of penal servitude (懲).

One, a criminal who used various blackout measures (각종 방위장치) to forcibly steal money from the pocket of a young girl passing by was severely punished with the death penalty and permanent loss of civil honorary rights (市民名譽權永久喪失).

One, a criminal who broke into an empty house in Vienna (뷔인) to plunder received the death penalty.

One, a criminal committing fraud against the family of a soldier away at the front (出征家族) received 8 years of penal servitude.

One, a criminal who embezzled (橫領) soldiers' parcels (軍人小包) received 5 years of penal servitude.<19>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* reports on severe wartime punishments enacted in Nazi Germany, disseminated by the Japanese Government-General's Police Affairs Bureau in Korea. Examples include the death penalty for anti-state crimes, robbery during blackouts, and looting, along with lengthy prison sentences for listening to foreign broadcasts, violating price controls, black marketeering, defrauding soldiers' families, and stealing soldiers' mail. The article highlights the extreme nature of Nazi Germany's legal enforcement during wartime. From a historical perspective, the timing (shortly after the Tripartite Pact) and the source (colonial police) are significant. It suggests the Japanese authorities were using Germany's harsh measures as a model or justification, likely aiming to intimidate the Korean populace and signal increased wartime control and severity within the Japanese Empire itself.

經濟事犯에 極刑
朝鮮人의 獨逸에 대한 관심은 日獨伊 三國同盟의 성립이래 一層 더 깊어졌지만 總督府 警務局에서는 히틀러유-겐도 指導者의 來鮮한 것을 기회로 戰時下 독일국내 법규의 집행상황을 일반에 알리도록 하게 되었다. 그 중요한 것은 다음과 같다.
一, 반국가범죄는 그 진 例外없이 사형, 또는 永久名譽權 상실의 극형
一, 佛領 슈도라스불쿠放送局의 테마방송을 유포한 자의게는 重懲役
一, 외국에서 獨逸語로 방송하는 것을 부엌에서 10분간 청취하는 주부에게 9개월 징역
一, 戰時價格令 及 表記 위반자에 營業閉銷의 처분
一, 肉類商은 密賣買의 범행은 부부가 함께 懲 10년
一, 각종 방위장치를 이용 통행중의 소녀의 주머니에서 金錢을 强奪한 범죄자는 사형 及 시민명예권영구상실의 엄벌
一, 뷔인 집에 뛰어들어가 약탈하는 범죄자에게는 사형
一, 출정가족에 대한 사기범죄자에게 징역 8년
一, 軍人小包 橫領犯罪자에게는 징역 5년＜19＞
