Sixteen-Yen New Group Weddings - Highly Popular in North China -
Since last spring, the Social Bureau of the Tianjin Special City Public Office, aiming to abolish vain formalities by improving the wedding ceremony, which had become too bound by old customs, adopted the merits of the group wedding ceremonies held two or three times during the Ji-Cha Regime (冀察政權時代) era. To encourage new group weddings, the first such ceremony was held on June 11th, producing 11 new couples. Subsequently, when applicants were sought for the second group wedding, 48 people, forming 24 couples, applied from various places including Tianjin, Beiping, Baoding, and Tangshan. The Social Bureau, busy with preparations, chose the Mid-Autumn Festival, September 16th, for the second group wedding ceremony. It was held grandly starting at 10 AM at the Hebei Beining Public Office <160>, with representatives from the Japanese military and civilian sectors, including Honma Unit Commander (本間部隊長) and Usui Civil Group Leader (臼井民團長), also attending.
This group wedding ceremony requires only 16 yen in expenses per couple, with the overall cost of the ceremony being covered by the Social Bureau. The ceremony begins with the wedding march played by a band. The groom, wearing a black long coat (長衣) with a rose pinned to his chest, and the bride, dressed in pure white with a long veil, enter the hall. After their names and personal histories are announced, the bride and groom exchange three bows, give wedding rings, and then the marriage certificate conferment ceremony takes place. Following greetings from the mayor and congratulatory addresses from guests, the ceremony concludes.

The Dictators' Salaries
President Hitler's income: The salary provided by the government is 40,000 pounds per year, but President Hitler has declined this salary since taking power, covering all his expenses with the royalties from his book "Mein Kampf." <161> The income from these royalties is substantial; an investigation several years ago revealed it amounted to one million pounds. Furthermore, all of the President's daily living expenses are reportedly covered by the state.

Premier Mussolini's Income
Italian Premier Mussolini's annual salary is 1,500 pounds. Like President Hitler, he reportedly has nearly the same amount of income each year from his published works. Also, his official newspaper, "d'Italia," has the largest circulation, bringing in significant income from that source. Additionally, all expenses, including his social expenses and miscellaneous costs, are paid by the state.

Stalin's Income
Stalin, the master of the Kremlin in the Soviet Union, is likely the poorest among the dictators. His salary is unclear but estimated to be around 500 pounds. His lifestyle is described not so much as simple, but rather as one where <162> food, clothing, and shelter are treated as matters of indifference. All social and other expenses are covered by the state, and he supposedly carries only a small amount of money in his pocket, just enough to buy cigarettes. Stalin also has many books, with sales surpassing 1.5 million copies, but he receives not a single penny in royalties.

Chiang Kai-shek's Income
Chiang Kai-shek, who calls for resistance against Japan, is perhaps the wealthiest among the world's dictators. His annual salary is a small sum of 600 pounds, but all his expenses, starting with living costs, are entirely covered by the state. Moreover, he can withdraw any amount of money he wishes to spend from the state treasury as provisional expenses. Additionally, his wife, Song Meiling (宋美齡), comes from the Song clan (宋一族), known as the wealthiest in the Far East, and is rumored to have vast sums deposited in foreign banks under her name. <163>

Stalin's Eighteen Body Doubles
It is rumored that the Soviet dictator Stalin has many body doubles (影武者), and the story of a doctor examining several Stalins is famous. Recently, this same story has resurfaced in the United States, featured with a cartoon in the Sunday Mirror newspaper. The story goes as follows:
Dr. K. Kuenkebacher, an Amsterdam physician, was summoned to Moscow to examine Stalin. Upon entering the Kremlin and waiting with his stethoscope (聽誘器), out came ten Stalins! Each looked identical, like twins. After examination, two of them were found to have high blood pressure. The doctor reportedly said upon returning to Holland (오란다) that one of those two was probably the real Stalin. <164>

**Summary**

This article from late 1940 covers several unrelated topics. It begins by reporting positively on Japanese-sponsored group weddings in Tianjin, China, emphasizing their low cost and modern appeal. It then compares the incomes and lifestyles of four contemporary dictators: Hitler, Mussolini, Stalin, and Chiang Kai-shek. Hitler and Mussolini are portrayed as deriving income from book sales alongside state support. Stalin is described as personally poor but state-supported. Chiang Kai-shek is depicted negatively as the wealthiest, using state funds freely and benefiting from his wife's family fortune. Finally, it recounts a rumor about Stalin using numerous body doubles. For historians, the article offers insight into propaganda during the Japanese colonial period in Korea. The comparison of dictators subtly favors the Axis-aligned leaders (or at least portrays them less negatively than Chiang Kai-shek), reflecting the geopolitical alignments of 1940. The positive portrayal of Japanese initiatives in occupied China and the repeating of rumors about Soviet leadership also serve colonial narratives.

色箱子
十六圓의 新集團結婚 -北支에서 大流行-
天津特別市公署 社會局에선 지난 봄부터 虛禮廢止의 意味에서 너무나 舊來의 風習에 잡히여 있는 결혼식이란 것을 改善하고저, 冀察政權時代에 2, 3회 가량 시행된 集團結婚式의 美點을 取入한 新集團結婚을 獎勵하느라고 지난 6월 11엔 그 第1回 集團結婚式을 擧行하고, 11組의 新夫婦을 내게 되었는데, 그 뒤에 다시 第2回 集團結婚 希望者를 募集한 결과 天津을 爲始하여 北京, 保定, 唐山의 각지에서 48명 24조의 申込이 들어왔음으로, 社會局에선 분주히 그 준비에 着手함은 勿論, 9월 16일 中秋佳節을 擇하여 第2回 集團結婚式을 午前 10시부터 河北 北寧公署에서＜160＞ 盛大히 開催했는데, 日本側에서도 本間部隊長, 臼井民團長 等 軍官民代表가 參席하였다.
이 集團結婚式은 1조에 겨우 16圓의 經費만이 必要하고, 式 全般의 費用은 社會局의 負擔이 되어 있다. 式은 먼저 樂隊의 結婚行進曲이 시작되고, 검은 長衣에 장미꽃을 가슴에 꽂은 新郞과 純白의 服色에 긴- 베-일을 쓴 新婦가 式場에 들어오면, 各各 그 姓名, 履歷이 署名된 후에 新郞 新婦가 서로 三鞠의 禮를 交하고, 結婚반지를 준 뒤에 結婚證書 授與式이 있고 市長의 인사와 來賓의 祝辭가 있은 다음 閉會한다고.

獨裁者의 月給
힛틀러 總統의 收入은, 政府에서 주는 歲費는 年 4萬磅인데, 힛틀러總統은 政權을 획득하면서부터 歲費를 辭退, 그 비용은 전부 저서인 「나의 투쟁」의 인세로써 한다고 하는데,＜161＞ 그 인세수입이 대단하여, 수년전의 조사만 하여도 百萬磅이나 되었다고 한다. 더욱 總統의 일상생활비 全部는 國家가 負擔한다고 한다.

뭇소리니 首相의 收入
伊太利首相 뭇소리니 首相의 年給은 1,500磅인데, 히틀러 總統 모양으로, 매년 거이 同額의 出版저서의 收入이 있다고 한다. 또 同首相의 機關紙 「데이타리」는 최대의 발행부수를 가지고 있음으로 거기로부터 들어오는 收入도 많다. 又 同首相의 社交費 其付 一切 費用은 國家가 支給한다고.

스탈-린의 收入
소聯의 쿠레무린의 主人 스타-린은 獨裁者 중에 가장 가난뱅이리라. 그 俸給은 分明치 않으나 대략 500磅 정도이며, 그의 생활은 簡素하다고 하기보다 의식주는 아무게라도 괜찮다는＜162＞ 式의 생활을 하고 있다. 社交費 기타 一切의 費用은 모도다 國家가 負擔하고 있으며, 겨우 煙草나 사는 조고마한 돈을 호주머니에 지니고 있을 뿐이다. 또 스탈린에게는 저서가 많이 있는데, 그 판매도 150만부를 돌파하고 있는 바, 印稅는 一錢도 들어오지 않는다.

蔣介石의 收入
抗日을 呼하는 蔣介石은 富者이기로는 世界의 獨裁者 중 가장 웃듬이리라, 그의 年給은 600磅의 小額이나 생활을 위시하여 온갓 費用은 全部가 國家의 負擔으로 되어 있다. 그리고 그가 쓰고 싶은 돈이면 얼마든지 臨時費로써 國庫로부터 支出할 수가 있게 되었다. 그리고 그의 안해 宋美齡은 極東 제일의 富者라하는 宋一族의 출신이어서 그의 名義로 外國銀行에 거액의 저금이 있다고 전한다. ＜163＞

十八의 스탈-린
소련 독재자 스탈린에게는 많은 影武者가 있다고 傳하며 醫師가 몇 사람의 스탈-린을 診察했다는 말은 有名한데, 최근에도 이와 또같은 말이 米國에서 返復되고 있다. 近著 싼데-미타-紙에, 漫畵와 함께 실리었다. 그 이야긴 다음과 같다.
암스텔담醫師 K우엔케밧하博士는 스탈린을 誘察하고저 모스코바에 불리워 가서 크레무린에 參入하여 聽誘器를 들고 기다리고 있은 즉 나오는 것은 열사람의 스탈-린이 아닌가. 어느 것이나 쌍둥이 모양으로 똑같었는데, 診察의 結果 그 中 두 사람이 高血壓이었다. 그런데 그 두사람 중 하나가 아마 정말 스탈린인 모양이라고 同醫師는 오란다에 돌아가서 이야기했다는 것이다.＜164＞
