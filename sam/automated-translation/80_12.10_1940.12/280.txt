Magazine Title: 삼천리 第12권 第10호
Publication Date: 1940년 12월 01일
Article Title: 文學도 大轉換, 國家的 意義있는 題材를 (Literature Also Needs a Great Transformation, Towards Subjects with National Significance)
Author: 菊池寬 (Kikuchi Kan)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

Even Regarding Literary Activity, Authors Are Each Currently Deepening Their Agony Through Intense Self-Reflection. Regrettably, I Acknowledge That Not a Single Eye-Catching War Novel Nor a Masterpiece Vividly Depicting Life on the Home Front (銃後) Has Emerged. Authors Often Intend This and Make Efforts, but Since Literature Itself Arises Naturally, It Cannot Be Achieved Solely Through the Author's Efforts.

Consequently, Even If Authors Refine New Concepts and Intentions to Align Literary Activity with the New Order (新體制), Whether Such Masterpieces Will Truly Emerge Easily Is a Separate Issue.

At the Very Least, I Believe the Direction to Be Taken in the Literary World Lies in the Following Points I Mention, Which Are Already Being Discussed in Various Quarters.

1.  Abandoning the Overemphasis on Realism and Shifting Towards a Romantic Spirit Is Considered Foremost. Romanticism Is Believing in Life's Possibilities. Literature Too Must Have Faith in the Potential of the Japanese People (日本民族) and Contribute to the Achievement of Their Ideals with New Passion.

2.  Pure Historical Novels Set in Ancient Japan (古代日本) Should Appear Continuously, and Through the Authors' Research, the Beautiful Aspects of Ancient Japan Should Be Written About Even More Beautifully to Be Read.

3.  There Are Plenty of Subjects Centered on Individual Life Focused on Love and Desire (愛慾). Subjects That Have the Most National and Social Significance Should Be Handled. Subjects Should Be Sought Where the Nation—in Other Words, Society—and Individual Life Are Connected.

4.  Instead of Taking Subjects From Urban Street Life—Apartments, Coffee Shops (喫茶店), Bars (빠-)—Border Life in Manchukuo (滿洲國), Pioneer Immigrant Villages (開拓移民村), and the Interactions and Activities Between Peoples on the Chinese Continent (支那 大陸) Should Become the Objects of Subject Matter.

However, It Is Not an Easy Thing to Expect Outstanding Works to Emerge Overnight Through Such a Transformation. Facing a Great Period of Transformation for the Nation, It Is Natural That Literature Also Undergoes a Self-Sacrificing Transformation. Furthermore, Writers Should Create Works Capable of Guiding the New Order (新體制). <199>

**Summary**

Kikuchi Kan, a prominent Japanese author, argues in this 1940 editorial for the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* that literature must undergo a "great transformation" to align with Japan's "New Order." He advocates abandoning realism and individualistic themes (like love and urban life) in favor of romanticism (defined as faith in the Japanese nation's potential), historical novels glorifying ancient Japan, and stories focusing on nationally significant topics, particularly those related to Japan's imperial expansion in Manchukuo and China. He urges writers to find themes connecting individual life to the nation and society, effectively calling for literature to serve state ideology. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is highly revealing. It showcases the direct pressure exerted by Japanese cultural figures on Korean literature to conform to imperial wartime ideology, demanding a shift away from potentially critical or independent themes towards state propaganda glorifying Japan and its empire. Historians would find this a clear example of cultural policy during the late colonial period aimed at mobilizing Korean intellectuals for the Japanese war effort and promoting the assimilationist "New Order."

文學도 大轉換, 國家的 意義있는 題材를
						菊池寬
지금은 文學活動에 대해서도 作家는 제각금 激烈한 自己反省에 苦悶을 더하고 있는 중이다. 遺感이지만 눈에 번찍 띄이는 戰爭小說 한아 또는 銃後의 生活을 活寫한 傑作도 나오지 않었다는 것을 認定한다. 作家들은 많이 이것을 意圖하며 努力도 하지만 文學 그것이 自然的 發生인고로, 作家의 努力만으로는 도저히 되지 않는 것이다.
따라서 作家가 文學活動 우에 어떻게 新體制에 卽應하려는 새로운 構想과 意圖를 練磨하여도 정말 그런 傑作이 容易히 나오겠느냐는 것은 別問題이다.
적어도 文壇에서 取扱할 方向은 임이 各 方面에서 내가 말하는 다음의 諸點에 있지 않을가 생각하고 있다.
一. 現實主義 偏重을 버리고 로만틱精神에의 轉換이 第一이라고 생각한다. 로만틱主義라는 것은 人生의 可能을 믿는 것이다. 文學도 또 日本民族의 可能을 確信하고 새로운 熱情을 가지고, 그의 理想의 達成에 貢獻할 것이다.
一. 古代日本을 舞臺로써 純粹한 歷史小說이 續續 登場하여 作家의 硏究로써 古代日本의 아름다운 姿態를 더 아름답게 써서 읽힐 것이다.
一. 愛慾 中心의 個人生活이라는 題材는 얼마든지 있다. 가장 國家的 社會에 意義가 있는 題材를 取扱할 것이다. 國家 다시 말하면 社會와 個人生活과의 關聯하는 곳에서 題材를 求할 것이다.
一. 都會의 市井生活 아빠트·喫茶店, 빠- 等에서 題材를 取하는 代身에 滿洲國에 있어 國境生活, 開拓移民村, 支那 大陸에 있어서 民族과 民族과의 交涉 活躍하는 것 等이 題材의 對象이 되야 할 것이다.
그러나 이런 轉換으로써 하로 밤도 지내지 않어 훌륭한 作品이 나오리라는 것은 容易한 일이 아니다, 國家의 一大 轉換期에 대하야 文學도 역시 捨身의 轉換을 하는 것은 當然하다. 그리고 文學者로써는 新體制를 指導할 만한 作品을 創建해야 할 것이다.＜199＞
