Literary Squad and "Volunteer Soldiers"

On October 12th, guided by Major Kaba of the Army Command, 38 members of the Chosŏn Literary Squad arrived at the Yangju Volunteer Soldier Training Center. Under the guidance and explanation of Colonel Kaida, they experienced a one-day enlistment before returning. Immediately after this grand event, we requested the impressions of the participating literary figures and record them here.

People Useful Enough to Be Offered to the Emperor
Ch'unwŏn
This is the second time I've seen the Volunteer Soldier Training Center, and each time, what strikes me most is the transformation of body and spirit. The transformation of the digestive system, the transformation of muscles, the transformation of skin—this is something the volunteer soldiers are universally grateful for, but perhaps even greater is the transformation of the spirit achieved through the transformation of habits. When they complete their military life and return, they become entirely new people. This becoming new people seems to be an inevitable and natural process that all 23 million [Koreans] must go through. To put it in one phrase: becoming "people useful enough to be offered to the Emperor."

Conquer with "Truth"
Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi
Volunteer soldiers! Your arms, legs,<60> and chests are strong as copper iron. Your movements in sitting and standing are extremely vigorous and agile. Your voices are exceedingly resonant. You are readily sufficient to become soldiers.
However, there is one thing I absolutely want to say to you all. Please do not conquer others merely through strength, but conquer them through "Truth." Everyone bows their head before "Truth." The reason Director Kaida has you kneel facing your hometowns and kneel before photographs of your parents is to teach this "Truth."

That Training of Perfect Order
Yu Chino
Having observed the Volunteer Soldier Training Center, I was once again deeply impressed by its order and discipline. Unfortunately, due to unavoidable business that afternoon, I could not observe the actual training drills. However, merely seeing the dormitories, classrooms, dining halls, and other real scenes was enough to impress me with the perfect orderliness. I believe that the sturdy strength for constructing the new order in East Asia will also emerge from this training of perfect order (一絲不亂).

Silent Prayer Towards the Hometown
Chŏng Insŏp
Watching the meal scene, I felt the thoroughness of human cultivation (人間修業). I was particularly deeply moved by the fact that not a single grain of rice is wasted and by the silent prayer offered towards the place where their hometown parents reside.
When they performed wooden sword exercises bare-chested in the plaza, one could surmise their sound minds and bodies. Moreover, their weighty steps, which seemed capable of reaching the Earth's axis, demonstrated an unwavering power (確乎不拔).
Director Kaida's sincere character (頓篤한 品格) and Major Kaba's<61> fervent sincerity, along with the kind guidance of each instructor, left us with many gains.
Finally, the scene where 40 literary figures rested under the shade of an orchard, peeling and eating pears, was a beautiful sight rarely seen in recent years.

Military Life Also Necessary for Women
Yi Sŏnhŭi
Walking down the long corridor (廊下) of the training center, I thought that military life, with such training and discipline (規律), is necessary not only for men but also for women to correctly establish the framework of their bodies and minds.
I admired everything about the volunteer soldiers—their sincere attitude, their steel-like bodies—and felt that their willpower must also be accordingly firm. And thinking that when the time comes, they will arm themselves and participate gallantly on the battlefield, I felt that the value of each and every one was limitless. I particularly thank Director Kaida for his kindness.

The Spirit of Endurance and Discipline
Ch'oe Yŏngju
I saw that their purpose and training were not merely for becoming soldiers, but the sincere and solemn effort befitting any man. Throughout this day, I was painfully ashamed of the weakness of my own untrained body and mind.
I have never once in my life produced from my own body such confident and passionate voices as they did when responding. Not to mention their practice of the spirit of endurance (忍苦) they willingly embrace and the discipline (規律) of communal life for the collective good. If permitted, I wished to have even just a few days of this moving life. Then, I thought, my perpetually lukewarm body and mind would surely become passionate and strong.

Chŏng Tongam's Diligence Badges
Pang In'gŭn
Seeing their thoroughly rule-based life made me keenly feel how lacking in tension our own irregular daily lives are. And their unfailingly energetic and lively attitude looked reassuring, and I envied their youth. Furthermore, I was deeply moved by the Director's introduction of Mr. Chŏng Tongam, who had repeatedly earned four Diligence Badges (精勤章) (red stripes on the sleeve)—harder than plucking stars from the sky—at the Nanam Regiment (羅南聯隊). Their perseverance in enduring this difficult life, separated from their hometowns and parents and siblings,<62> was tear-inducing.

Bodies Shining Under the Sun
Mo Yunsuk
Your voices were so resonant that my heart pounded violently. It was the first time it pounded so thrillingly.<63> Strong arms, powerful legs I had not seen in Peninsula people—you showed them to me now under the red sun. An orderly life, regular drills (敎練)—you alone were the fortunate sons of the Peninsula (半島).

Much Praise for Director Kaida's Labor
Chairman, National Training Support Association

Shouldering a Rifle, Singing a March
Chi Pongmun
For the first time, I encountered our future soldiers—valiant, lively young men. Truly magnificent. What prevents them from standing shoulder-to-shoulder with those of other countries? They would be several times stronger, more vigorous. When I saw the reality of their disciplined, orderly training, I immediately wanted to jump into their midst. I wanted to present arms, sing a marching song (行進歌), and await strict orders (命令). My heart surged fiercely. But I lamented that I would not be given such qualifications.
On the way back, I bought these things. A foot soldier's (步兵) uniform, a drum, a rifle, and lead soldier figurines (兵丁). I bought them for my son. Feeling a father's pride while looking upon my son, a fearsome sense of responsibility flared up like fire within my heart, unbeknownst even to me.<64>

Everyone Should Pass Through the Training Center Gate
Im Yŏngbin
Folding a single blanket, putting daily necessities in a box, hanging a single towel—there is nothing that is not orderly and disciplined. For the Peninsula Youth (半島靑年), raised indulgently, doing things carelessly, just as they please, this training center is a good place to show something orderly (整齊). An indulgent life is a spiritually dying life; a life of orderly discipline (規律整然) is a spiritually living life. This training center teaches our Peninsula Youth discipline, enabling them to live a vital life, and consequently provides an opportunity to repay even one ten-thousandth of the vast Imperial Grace (皇恩). Not only because they are subjects of the Empire (帝國臣民), but also to vitalize the life of each individual, I believe Peninsula Youth must pass through the gates of this training center.

The Spirit of Undivided Loyalty and Courage
Ch'oe Yŏngsu
There is only deep emotion. Furthermore, Director Kaida's kindness and every phrase he spoke moved me to tears.
That imposing physique of iron bone and sturdy leg, that posture—truly seemed overflowing with the spirit of undivided loyalty and courage (忠勇一路). It was truly a day of deep emotion.

Orderliness of Instruction and Meals
Ch'oe Pyŏnghwa
I consider having the opportunity for a "one-day enlistment" at the Army Volunteer Soldier Training Center an unsurpassed honor, and at the same time, I would like to write down a few impressions from my enlistment, which was filled with emotion and admiration.
1. The surrounding scenery of the training center is beautiful (佳麗) and suitable for training mind and body.
2. The reddish-bronze (赤銅色) flesh symbolizing the health of the soldiers receiving valiant and cheerful training (敎鍊) bare-chested under the direct sun is enviable.
3. Mastering collective training during instruction and meal times, they are lively yet silent and taciturn, and absolutely obedient to superiors' orders.
4. I cannot suppress the impulse, if only my life circumstances permitted, to also become a volunteer soldier, possess a healthy body, and become a brave warrior (勇士).

Duties Increase in Wartime
Ham Taehun
Respected Volunteer Soldiers!
You have been strictly selected as Chosŏn's Special Army Volunteer Soldiers and enjoy the privilege<65> of being able to fulfill military service obligations. Although the four months of training is a short period, I believe the orderly and systematic training is something difficult to obtain even through more than a year of ordinary education. Now that the battlefront (戰線) has expanded and the entire Japanese nation is heading to this battlefield, it is deeply moving that our Peninsula Youth can also bear this glorious duty. Moreover, you are important beings who will carry out this duty, so become splendid soldiers in no way inferior to homeland soldiers and display the spirit of Peninsula Youth on the battlefield. That an exemplary volunteer soldier like Private First Class Chŏng Tongam of the Nanam unit (羅南部隊), who has already received four Diligence Badges, is a graduate of your institution will, I believe, give a huge impact to you juniors. Also, emulate the loyal martyrdom (忠烈) of Private First Class Yi Insŏk, who has already died in battle. I hope your military achievements (武勳) will shine as part of the powerful army.

Cultural Figures Also Need Enlistment
An Sŏgyŏng
Although the observation of the Volunteer Soldier Training Center lasted only one day, I received an impression that will never fade from my heart. The words of the instructors who taught with enthusiasm are also fresh in my memory, and I believe that here, where the youth of the Peninsula receive spiritual and physical training, they can become pillars of the state (國家의 干城) and realize the duties they must perform as subjects of the Empire (皇國臣民). I hope that in the future, even university graduates will be able to enter this training center. And cultural figures need to observe this training center at least once.

Become Pillars of the State
Hong Hyomin
Uphold the "Imperial Rescript" (勅諭) well, do not lose "Sincerity, Honesty, Vigor" (誠實·正直·元氣), offer loyalty to His Majesty the Emperor (聖上陛下) above, and become pillars of the state (國家의 干城) below. Of course, always love and guide the Korean people (朝鮮人), stand in the position of leading, demonstrate the spirit befitting soldiers on the front line, and become beacons for all the people—this I desire unceasingly.

Like a Flourishing Forest
Pak Wŏnsik
It felt as if newly planted trees had already become a forest (樹林) stretching towards the sky, and we were walking through that forest.
Thinking that all this fine timber (良材) will now become splendid pillars (棟樑) in constructing a great house, I feel endlessly reassured and fortunate.<66>

Equal Benevolence for the People of the Peninsula
Chŏng Pisŏk
Having observed the Army Volunteer Soldier Training Center, I realized the boundlessness of the Sacred Virtue (聖德) and felt the following impressions:
1. If all the youth of Chosŏn pass through the gates of the Training Center at least once, a new light will shine upon Chosŏn. The volunteer soldier system is undoubtedly the crystallization of His Majesty's equal benevolence (一視同仁) bestowed upon the people (民草) of the Peninsula.
2. Would that Greek culture (希臘聞化) have been able to blossom so splendidly without Spartan-style education (スパルタ式教育)?
3. We must quickly correct the mistaken perception, traditionally held as if something to be proud of, that literary figures (文人) are inherently weak (弱質).

Heightening of the Martial Spirit
Kim Tonghwan
Wishing to heighten the martial spirit (尙武精神), I observed the Volunteer Soldier Training Center from morning till evening with the mindset of a one-day enlistment. Upon leaving, the thought keenly arose that if only I were 10 years younger, I would have wanted to enter and train for 4 or 5 months.
The number of robust men selected here is 1,000, but the number of young men who applied to become volunteer soldiers throughout Chosŏn is said to have numbered 84,000. Knowing that this signifies the atmosphere of this land, which had drifted into effeminacy (文弱), is now leaping forward on the single path of enriching the country and strengthening the military (富國强兵), my heart feels endlessly reassured.
I hear that in Kando (間島), a special unit (特設部隊) composed solely of Koreans has been organized; that in Manchukuo (滿洲國), the implementation of a conscription system (徵兵制度) will soon occur, meaning the number of compatriots there taking up arms will count in the tens of thousands; and that in Chosŏn, volunteer soldiers like these are set to be formed at a rate of 3,000 per year. Thus, the spirit (意氣) of Chosŏn youth, both at home and abroad, is rising so high with the sole determination to devote themselves to the sovereign and country (君國), truly I cannot suppress my joyous thoughts.
There is a saying that "the pen and the bullet (彈丸) are made of the same iron." We, who unfortunately passed our youth without any opportunity for military training, keenly felt this day that we must devote all our efforts from behind the lines (銃後) with brush in hand, promoting this image of the volunteer soldier through poetry and novels.<67>
<60-67>

**Summary**

This article compiles the impressions of numerous prominent Korean literary figures after a one-day visit to the Yangju Volunteer Soldier Training Center in October 1940. The writers uniformly praise the discipline, physical transformation, and perceived spiritual strengthening instilled by the military training. They extol the virtues of the volunteer soldier system, viewing it as a path for Korean youth to become "useful subjects of the Emperor," contribute to the "new order in East Asia," demonstrate loyalty, and overcome perceived national weaknesses like "effeminacy." Several authors express regret at being too old to enlist themselves and urge wider participation, even suggesting mandatory training for all youth or cultural figures. The piece serves as overt propaganda, encouraging Korean participation in the Japanese military effort during wartime. For historians, the article is a significant example of collaboration by Korean elites, showcasing their active role in promoting Japanese imperial ideology and militarization within colonial Korea, reflecting the pressures and choices faced by intellectuals during the final, assimilationist phase of Japanese rule.

文士部隊와 「志願兵」
10월 12일, 軍司令部 蒲少佐의 案內로 朝鮮文士部隊 3十8名이 楊州志願兵訓練所에 이르러 海田大佐의 引導, 說明을 받어가며, 1日 入所를 하고 돌라왔다. 이 壯擧가 있은 卽後, 當日 參加하였던 文人 諸氏의 所感을 청하여 여기 記錄하는 바이다.

天皇께 바쳐서 쓸 데 있는 사람
春園
志願兵訓練所를 보는 것은 두 번짼데 볼 때마다 가장 많이 느껴지는 것은 신체와 정신의 改造입니다. 消化器의 개조, 筋肉의 개조, 피부의 개조, 이것은 志願兵들이 共通으로 감사하는 바여니와 습관의 개조를 통하여서 되는 정신의 개조는 그 이상인가 합니다. 그들이 軍隊생활을 맟우고 오는 날은 전혀 新人이 되는데 이 新人化이야말로 2천3백만이 모주리 통과하여야 할 必然當然의 過程인가 합니다. 一言以蔽之曰 「天皇께 바쳐서 쓸데 있는 사람」이 되는 것입니다.

「眞實」로 이기라
崔貞熙
志願兵! 당신들의 팔과 다리와＜60＞ 가슴은 구리쇠같이 강합디다. 앉고 서고 하는 동작은 무척 씩씩하고 敏活합디다. 그 음성은 몹시 우렁찹디다. 당신들은, 쉬이 한사람의 병사가 되기에 넉넉합디다.
그런데 이제 저는 여러분께 꼭 한마디 말슴을 디리고 싶습니다. 여러분은 힘으로서만, 남을 이기지 말고, 「眞實」로서 남을 이겨달라는 것입니다. 「眞實」앞에는 누구나 머리를 숙입니다. 海田所長이 여러분께, 고향을 향해 무릎을 꿇게 하고, 부모님 사진 앞에 무릎을 꿇게 하는 것이 이 「眞實」을 가르치고저 함에서입니다.

一絲不亂의 그 訓練
兪鎭午
志願兵訓練所를 견학하고 그 질서와 규율에 다시금 歎服하였다. 공교로히 그날 오후 나는 불가피한, 事務가 있어 훈련의 實况은 견학하지 못하였으나 宿舍 學科 食事 등의 實景을 보았을 뿐으로도 그 질서의 整然함에 歎服하였다. 그 一絲不亂의 훈련 속에서 東亞新秩序建設의 굳센 힘도 울어나올 것으로 믿는다.

故鄕을 向해서의 黙禱
鄭寅燮
식사하는 광경을 보고 人間修業의 철저함을 느꼈다. 특히 밥 한 알도 버리지 않는다는 것과 고향부모 있는 곳을 향해서 黙禱하는 것은 깊이 감동되었다.
광장에서 상반신을 맨몸으로 하고 木刀體操를 할 때의 건전한 심신을 추측할 수 있었을 뿐 아니라 地軸에까지 비겨 전할 만큼한 무게 있는 步調는 確乎不拔한 動力을 보여주었다.
