**笑話** (Jokes)

Ten Years of Hardship

Painter: "I truly struggled over this painting. You see, it took ten years."
Woman: "What, ten years! You mean you agonized over painting this one picture for ten years?"
Painter: "No, the painting itself took 30 days, but the remaining nine years and several months were spent trying to sell the painting."

That's Likely

A: "My wife just nags for money day and night, it gives me a headache."
B: "What on earth does she use the money for when she takes it?"
A: "How would I know? I've never once given her any, so I don't know what she uses it for."

**Summary**

This short article presents two simple jokes. The first anecdote humorously highlights the long struggle an artist faces, not in creating art, but in selling it. The second joke relies on a common trope about a wife constantly asking for money and a husband who never provides any. For historians, these jokes offer a small window into the popular humor and social commentary of 1930s colonial Korea, reflecting everyday concerns and stereotypes related to artistic livelihoods and marital relationships in a lighthearted manner.
