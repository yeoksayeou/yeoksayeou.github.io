Magazine Title: 삼천리 제4권 제6호
Publication Date: 1932-05-15
Article Title: 우수운 작란(一幕) (A Ridiculous Prank (One Act))
Author: 김영팔 (Kim Yŏngp’al)
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

A Ridiculous Prank
						Kim Yŏngp’al
-(Characters)-
Kim Insu (Free laborer) Late 40s
Kim Sosi (Owner of a cheap tavern) 52 years old
Pak Ch'ŏmji (Servant of a wealthy family) 60 years old
Ch'oe Sŏngnyŏ (Pak Ch'ŏmji's wife) 50 years old
Others A, B, C, D, young child, etc.

-(Time)-
One winter

-(Place)-
A cheap tavern (Kim Sosi’s house)

Stage... A tavern bar in an alley quite far from the main street of Pukch'on. For dry snacks, there are only a few bags of crackers (크랏카-); for wet snacks, there are only a few mung bean pancakes (빈자떡) and a few pieces of pressed ox head meat (쇠머리 편육). However, in front of the bar, ox bones that look like they just came out of bathing in soup broth are scattered widely. Seeing this, one can quickly tell it's a place selling makkeolli by the bowl.
When the curtain opens, Kim Sosi is dozing at the bar, holding a soup ladle (술국이). At this time, A, B, C, and D enter.
D... Hey, it's cold. Auntie, are you sleeping like that?
A... Auntie, please pull yourself together.
Sosi... Oh, you startled me. Welcome. Goodness, I dozed off holding the soup ladle.
B... Ah, you must be that sleepy.
Sosi... Well, it's a big problem. Maybe because I'm getting older, but it's getting worse starting this year. Gosh, what kind of mind is this? If someone says something, I forget it right away. Also, whenever I sit down, I nod off, so it looks like I won't be able to run this business anymore.
A... Heh, Auntie, you haven't even heard the saying yet. (In a singing tone) An old general is useless, there’s no prospect of seizing the opportunity—. Don't you know this saying?
Sosi... Aigo, you put it so well. Truly, an old general is useless. But is it really so? They say the body ages but the mind doesn't, heh heh.
D... Now, Auntie, ladle us about four or five bowls of soup. It's too cold, I can't stand it.
Sosi... Yes. (Comes down from the bar and ladles soup)
A... (Drinking the soup broth) Ah, I feel alive now. Aish, I'm never going to that wench's house again. Gosh, calling that alcohol, alcohol? She's a thief.
D... Hey, did you go to that wench's house without knowing what she's like? What did I tell you? Didn't I say it's getting robbed with your eyes wide open?
C... You're all saying things that'll strike you dead. The guys who are better at playing around with girls are saying such things. (To B) Isn't that right, pal?
B... Obviously. They really play well. Aish, those dirty mouths always seem to be sucking, and what kind of affection is that? Have you really lived together even once? (Looking at A) Is it really such affection? And what kind of talk is that?
C... I reluctantly went Dutch (가부시끼는)—let those who played well pay their share.
B... Me too. I'll pay for what we ate here. Auntie, bring out the alcohol.
Sosi... Makkeolli (말껄니)?
B... Yes, isn't makkeolli the best for a hangover cure?
A... No, hey (to B), is that how it works? You mean you won't pay just because we agreed to go Dutch? And you'll treat us to a hangover cure? Really?
B... No, then did we order two rounds and call it three? And one kettle of alcohol is barely five cups—. Is that all? She pretends to pour alcohol, spills it over the rim of the cup, and collects it in a bowl—. You knew perfectly well, but because you were occupied with the girl, when I spoke up, you scolded me, didn't you? Telling me to be dignified—. Where is there dignity for us? We're craftsmen. How did we earn this money? Don't you remember getting slapped by the supervisor?
D... Hey, are these guys drunk? What's the point of talking about that here? We all went because we liked it, what else? Come on, let's just drink.
(Everyone drinks)
A... Anyway, hey (looking at B), I paid for that side, so you pay for this hangover cure here.
B... Okay, that's fine. You know well that there's a proper way (경오) to drink, don't you? Come on, put out the snacks—. Let's drink refined rice wine (약주) this time—. Auntie, bring four cups of yakju—.
(At this moment, Kim Insu, with a pale face, enters holding a young child)
Insu... (Shivering) Oh, how is it so cold today? (The young child cries) Oh, don't cry—. Don't cry. Huh? (Rocks the child to soothe it) Oh, don't cry—.
Sosi... Why did you come out with a young child in this cold morning? What will you do if it catches a cold—.
Insu... (Sighs without saying anything) Give me a bowl of soup. Make it quite hot.
Sosi... (Ladles soup and gives it to Insu)
Insu... (Receives the soup, holds the child tightly with one arm, and drinks the <37> soup broth with the other hand) Give me a glass of makkeolli—. (Downs the makkeolli in one breath) Give me one more glass. (Drinks)
(The young child starts crying again) Oh, don't cry. Huh. Ah, there are many cruel mothers. Oh, don't cry—. Let's go to mommy—. Oh, don't cry, huh?
Sosi... Hey, did the child's mother die?
Insu... No.
Sosi... Then, is the child's mother sick?
Insu... No. If she were at least sick, would I resent her as a cruel mother?
Sosi... Then what happened?
Insu... She ran away. Leaving this little one, barely two months old, and me, who is sick. Thinking about it, she's a pitiful person too. Until today, let alone living comfortably like others, she only suffered hardship like eating rice cakes made of suffering. Oh, don't cry—. Let's go to mommy. (Wipes tears with one hand)
Sosi... Oh dear, how pitiful. So, you mean you don't know where she went?
Insu... No, I don't know. She said she couldn't live in Seoul on wages, but what sharp solution would there be if she went to the countryside even more so?
B... (Listening for a while) So, she must have left without saying anything to you, elder brother?
Insu... Yes, that's right.
Sosi... Oh, how can people have such nerve? Gosh, how could she even think of running away, leaving behind such a bundle of flesh?
Insu... It must be because she couldn't bear the life worn down by poverty anymore. And since I fell ill, when we already lacked enough to even make watery porridge, trying to figure out how to nurse her husband and live in his stead, wouldn't she have had all sorts of thoughts? So I held onto my wife and told her many times that I was sorry. When I saw her coming in shivering, wearing torn and stained clothes, how could I not feel sorry—? Don't cry—. (Goes to the brazier and sits down) Oh, I'm saved now. Two glasses of makkeolli and two bowls of soup, and I'll survive. It was freezing so badly— (Looks down at the child) What should I feed this one to raise it? (Bows his head and thinks)
A... It's truly a pitiful situation. So, what kind of illness is it?
Insu... Yes, I am a laborer (로동자).
D... Yes. Well, I'm a laborer too.
Insu... No, I'm a hod carrier (목도꾼). Thinking back, if I had died when I fell then, my fate would have been better. Just like the saying, "Who would carry three *sŏk* of chaff to die?" (욱어지 석독을 누가치-고 죽어라는 말과 가티), asking whom I should entrust this bowl of suffering to before dying, that too doesn't come in order. But—. (Startled) Aigoo. The day has completely dawned.
Sosi... Aigoo, how pitiful. So you fell while carrying stones up to build that two-story house—. Oh, how scary.
Insu... Aigoo, two glasses of alcohol are making me drunk. Is it because I drank on an empty stomach? Auntie, I need to go somewhere nearby for a moment, but I have to ask you something regrettable. As you know, my situation is like this—.
Sosi... (With a stern face) You mean you don't have money for the alcohol?
Insu... Yes—. So—.
Sosi... No, how can you be so shameless, early in the morning before breakfast?
Insu... Yes—. I was so hungry that it turned out this way without thinking. I'll bring it back later.
Sosi... I don't know. We don't give credit (외상도 놋치 안쿠)—. This old woman works all day long, and it's hard enough to make watery porridge. If I just give away alcohol for free, are you telling me to starve to death? Can't be helped. Pay up and go.
Insu... I'll definitely bring it later. Don't worry.
A... Auntie, please let him do that. If he doesn't pay, I'll pay. Me. Don't you trust me either?
Sosi... What trust or no trust? Think about it. Alcohol requires definite cash payment (맛둔) to get it from the pub in the morning. In winter, everything costs more, so can I offer credit?
Insu... I know that well, but please let it go just this once.
Sosi... Then bring it back for sure later—.
Insu... Yes, thank you. (Exits quickly)
(A, B, C, D watch Insu's retreating figure)
C... He's pitiful (도조니다에나이네 - likely meant to be Japanese どうしようもないね *dōshiyō mo nai ne* or similar, meaning 'nothing can be done/it's hopeless' or perhaps pitiful).
B... Do we know when we might end up like that too?
A... Well, we don't know.
D... What sympathy? Sympathy? Talking like that here is cheap sympathy.
B... Well, that may be true, but it's really a big problem. Everyone's life among Koreans is like that.
Sosi... People should have some sense. Is asking for credit at dawn something reasonable? Bad luck again today. Business is already slow.
A... What good does it do to grumble after he's gone? Alright then— Let's say we drank two cups each? That's ten jeon. Here, Auntie.
Sosi... Aish, forget it.
A... Ah, please take it.
Sosi... Since you're giving it, I have to take it, but I feel too sorry. If I were just a little better off, I wouldn't be like this. But lately, I'm so hard-pressed (쪼ㅅ처서) that sometimes I have no alcohol to sell and just sit there blankly.
B... That's right. Because of the terrible recession.
C... Well—. Let's get going too.
A... Wait. We drank four cups each, right? We should drink five. Come on—pour one more cup.
Sosi... Just one more cup?
A... No. I meant pour another round of four cups.
Sosi... Yes.
(At this moment, Pak Ch'ŏmji enters)
Pak Ch'ŏmji... (Coughing) Tch, what a strange world this is. Nowadays, these girls (기집에들), as soon as they give birth to a child (식), they abandon it and trouble others.
Sosi... Oh my, what is all this talk, Pak Ch'ŏmji?
Ch'ŏmji... Gosh, it's absurd. Well, it's obvious. Must be a misbehaving female student. Who else would do such a thing?
Sosi... Hey, what is all this about?
A... Who abandoned a child, sir?
Ch'ŏmji... Right. What you said is exactly right.
B... Hey, where was it abandoned, you mean?
Ch'ŏmji... To trouble people, why did they have to send it into our house of all places? Aish, hey, pour me a glass of makkeolli.
Sosi... Hey, why is this Ch'ŏmji taking his anger out on me? So, was it a boy? Or a girl (계집애)?
Ch'ŏmji... Anyway, she's a bitch who deserves to die. Whichever bitch it was, just sticking to this guy and that guy like a dog in heat (홀내 개모양으로), and then abandoning the baby.
Sosi... Hey, when did this happen? This guy just talks like a fortune teller, making me desperately curious.
Ch'ŏmji... How would I know? When I heard a baby crying "peh peh," I went out, and they had sent it inside (처듸려 보내ㅅ드구면). It's absurd.
A... Then it couldn't have been long ago. If it had been a long time, would it have survived?
Ch'ŏmji... Ah, hurry up and pour a glass. You seem out of your mind. Hey, I need to drink one quickly and go report it to the police substation.
Sosi... So, what happened to the baby?
Ch'ŏmji... Someone brought it inside—. I have to go check again. I'll pay later.
Sosi... What?
Ch'ŏmji... Why are you yelling again? Since early morning, aigoo. What a fierce wife too. (Exits)
A... Indeed, it seems there are many female students with loose morals.
B... It's worse these days.
D... They're modern people, free people.
C... Well, it's not like all female students are like that, but once the water is muddied, whenever something like this happens somewhere, they immediately point fingers that way—. Well— let's get going now.
A... Wait a minute, man. Let's hear the story after he comes back from the police substation. Auntie, pour one more glass.
D... Let's stop drinking.
B... Let's just drink more since we're at it.
(Kim Insu enters)
Insu... (Putting down 10 jeon) Please take the payment for the earlier alcohol. I was too rude.
Sosi... Actually, I think I spoke too harshly earlier. Please understand that my temper is originally like that, okay? And I already received the money from that customer, so please give it to him.
Insu... Oh, is that so? Thank you very much. Then please accept this.
A... No. The reason I gave it to Auntie was not to get money from you.
Insu... No, sir. Still.
A... Ah, if you insist, let's have a drink together, one glass each.
Insu... Thank you for the kind words, but if I drink any more alcohol on top of this, it will be a big problem.
A... Hey, what do you mean, a big problem?
Insu... I think I might get rowdy (주정).
A... Well, people drink alcohol to get drunk, and when drunk, isn't it charming to have a little bit of drunken behavior?
Insu... But really, I can't drink alcohol. If I know I will suffer after drinking, it's better not to drink such alcohol. Now— please accept this money.
B... What's there to be so standoffish about?
Insu... It's not about being standoffish, but my situation is not one where I can drink alcohol with a peaceful mind. Please forgive me. Now, please accept the money.
(As Insu gives the money and is about to leave, Pak Ch'ŏmji re-enters)
Pak Ch'ŏmji... (Startled upon seeing Insu) Hey, it's really been a long time.
Insu... (Coldly) Is it you, Uncle? Have you been well? Is Auntie well too?
Ch'ŏmji... Yes, everyone is fine. So, is it true you got hurt falling while working on something?
Insu... Yes. It's much better now.
Ch'ŏmji... So, is your wife well? I heard from someone the other day that she had a safe delivery?
Insu... Yes.
Ch'ŏmji... So, is her body healthy?
Insu... There were no problems after childbirth.
Ch'ŏmji... Bring her to our house sometime. You probably don't know our house, right? If you ask for Minister Ri's residence (리참판댁) at XX-dong XX-beonji, there's no one who doesn't know it.
Insu... Minister Ri's residence? (Startled)
Ch'ŏmji... Yes. Minister Ri's residence. Definitely come visit once. It's really good to see you. We may have practically cut ties due to a petty argument out of fickleness, but what's the point of that? Do I have a son? Do I have a daughter? There's only that one niece (족하딸자식). Please (부대), both of you, come visit once. If you're too busy to come, tell your wife to come visit. Please do.
Insu... Thank you, Uncle—. But my wife has disappeared.
Ch'ŏmji... Disappeared? Do you mean she died?
Insu... She probably didn't die.
Ch'ŏmji... "She probably didn't die," you say?
Insu... I don't know either.
Ch'ŏmji... (Looking at Insu with suspicion) If you don't know, then who does?
Insu... She left somewhere without my knowledge, so how would I know?
Ch'ŏmji... Then the baby?
Insu... She left the bundle of flesh and me, the sick person.
Ch'ŏmji... What? She left even the baby? So, she must have run off with some guy, abandoning her husband and child. Aish, what a detestable bitch—. Hey, madam. Pour just two glasses of alcohol.
Sosi... Yes—. (Pours alcohol) By the way, what happened with the police substation matter?
Ch'ŏmji... Heh. It's absurd. The policeman glared at me so fiercely. That's the kind of unexpected misfortune (휭액) that isn't in one's fate.
A... So what did he say?
Ch'ŏmji... What a damned bitch, doing something others wouldn't dare. He told me to bring the baby to the police substation again.
Sosi... Sosi An old woman's complaints are also dreadful.
Insu... (Sits beside the brazier, warming himself by the fire)
D... Come on, let's all go now.
B... Auntie, how much is it all together?
Sosi... It's twenty glasses in total.
B... Twenty glasses, so that's one won. Okay— give us some snacks. <39>
(At this time, Ch'oe Sŏngnyŏ enters hurriedly, holding the baby)
Ch'oe Sŏngnyŏ... Oh— hey, you need to come to your senses too. The house is turned upside down, and you're here fooling around (노다거리고) in a tavern, huh?
Ch'ŏmji... Hey, why are you chasing me around (쫏차단이며) making such a fuss? What big trouble now—.
Sŏngnyŏ... That darn baby was gasping (깔낙깔낙), and then it just went—.
Ch'ŏmji... Gasping, and then went where?
Sŏngnyŏ... Aigoo, you can't even understand words. It died, it died, don't you see? Inside, the master (나리) said it was dreadful and ordered me to take it to the police substation immediately, so I brought it out.
Ch'ŏmji... What? Died?
Insu... (His face turns pale) The baby died?
Sŏngnyŏ... Who is this? Isn't it Insu?
Insu... Yes. That's right. Please give me that baby. —(Takes the baby and presses his tear-streaked face to the baby's mouth. The Ch'ŏmji couple, Kim Sosi, and the drinkers A, B, C, D stare in astonishment.) It finally died after all. There are many unexpected, ridiculous pranks in this world.
(The day has fully dawned. Insu, holding the baby, quietly leaves the tavern. Everyone watches him.)
-(Curtain)-<40>

**Summary**

This one-act play, "A Ridiculous Prank," depicts a grim morning in a cheap tavern in colonial Seoul. Laborers gather for drinks and hangover soup, discussing their hardships and recent experiences. Kim Insu, a distraught laborer whose wife abandoned him and their newborn after he was injured, enters seeking warmth and solace. Meanwhile, Pak Ch'ŏmji, a servant from a wealthy household, arrives agitated after finding an abandoned baby near his master's house. The plot tragically converges when Pak's wife brings the now-deceased baby back to the tavern, revealing it to be the same child abandoned near the house where Insu's wife presumably worked or sought help, and Insu carries his dead child away. The play starkly portrays the extreme poverty, desperation, social breakdown (abandonment), and precariousness of life for the lower classes in 1930s Korea.

For historians, the play offers insights into the social conditions of urban poor in colonial Seoul, including labor exploitation (mention of injury, supervisor's slap), the struggle for survival, the stark class contrasts implicitly shown via Pak Ch'ŏmji's connection to the "Minister Ri residence," and the social issue of infant abandonment driven by poverty and desperation. The casual use of Japanese phrases ("도조니다에나이네") by a Korean character also reflects the linguistic environment of the time.
