Recent Harvests of the Fiction Scene
1. Newspaper Novels
Samch'ŏlli Side…… Although seven or eight years ago, which could be called the golden age of the Chosŏn literary scene, literary critique meetings (文人合評會) and the like were occasionally held through the Chosŏn Mundansa (朝鮮文壇社) or Kaebyŏksa (開闢社), giving much vitality to the literary world, it is regrettable that such gatherings have been absent recently. With the wish for the prosperity of Chosŏn literature, we organized this meeting tonight. We express our gratitude to the many who came despite the rain.
Among those who were supposed to attend tonight but couldn't due to unforeseen circumstances were eight individuals: Yi Kwangsu, An Sŏkchu, Kim Yŏngp'al, Chu Yohan, Yi Ŭnsang, Yŏm Sangsŏp, Kim Hyŏngwŏn, and Ch’oe Ŭisun.
Now, let's get to the main topic. Please critique the serialized novels recently published in the four newspapers. Have you seen Yi Kwangsu's "Hŭk" (흙 - Soil) and Kim Tongin's "Agane" (아가네) in the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報)?

Sŏhae…… I only saw the beginnings of both, and they seemed more like interesting stories (이약이) rather than full-fledged novels. Looking at them through the lens of Ch'unwŏn's [Yi Kwangsu's pen name] old works like "Mujŏng" (無情 - Heartlessness) and "Kaech'ŏkcha" (開拓者 - The Pioneer), or Kim Tongin's "Kamja" (감자 - Potato) and "Myŏngmun" (明文 - Masterpiece), these can only be called stories.

Kim Iryŏp…… At my house, we don't read the Tonga Ilbo, only the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), so I haven't seen either novel.

Pinghŏ…… I've sort of seen both novels, sometimes reading them, sometimes not, so I haven't read them thoroughly and can't say much. However, the writing style (行文) is certainly fluent, showing further progress (進境) for both authors.

Kim Tongin…… From my experience, among us, we probably don't tend to read serialized works written by others very well, do we?

Samch'ŏlli Side…… It seems most haven't read them. Even so, through these two serialized works <14>, can't we see changes in the literary thought or advancements in the technique of both Mr. Ch'unwŏn and Mr. Tongin?

Tokkyŏn…… Hey, that question is difficult. How can one say anything without having read them thoroughly?

Ch’unhae…… Especially Kim Tongin's current "Agane," it seems like nothing more than a children's novel.

Samch'ŏlli Side…… Then please talk about the Chosŏn Ilbo. For serializations, there was Han Int’aek's "Sŏnp'ung Sidae" (旋風時代 - Whirlwind Era), the former first-prize winner of the reality contest (懸實一等當選), and the second-prize winner "Che Him" (제힘 - Own Strength) being published, and An Sŏkchu's "Sŏnggun" (星群 - Star Cluster) is currently running. How did you view them all?

Sŏhae…… I started reading Han Int’aek's novel out of interest because he's from the same place, and it was quite a good, weighty work. Hearing Sŏgyŏng's [Kim Sŏgyŏng?] words, it seems much was corrected, but you can't achieve that just by correcting; I think the original work must have been much better. There wasn't much dialect in the writing either. It was an excellent work for recent times.

Ansŏ…… Using dialect in a novel isn't something to find fault with. Even when setting a story in Seoul, dialect might appear, you know.

Sŏnghae…… Using local dialect in dialogue is not something to criticize at all, but if the descriptive text (지문) is written in dialect, I oppose it. Firstly, general readers cannot understand it, so isn't there a concern that the work's effect will be halved?

Ch’unhae…… That's right, dialect should only be permitted in dialogue. And as a principle, it would be correct to avoid putting dialect into the work as much as possible. Also, as the level of education spreads, dialect will naturally be eliminated.

Tongin…… In novels written by Arishima Takeo (有島武郞) set in Hokkaidō (北海道) or works by Tanizaki Jun'ichirō (谷崎潤一郞) depicting Osaka (大阪), how much dialect there is! But everyone considers them masterpieces, so the use of dialect might not be such a decisive factor in whether a work is good or bad.

Tokkyŏn…… I read "Sŏnp'ung Sidae" initially and then read it sporadically. I felt it might be a failure as a newspaper novel. The connections weren't well-established, and the technique was lacking. However, the admirable point is that in its description, there's an originality that couldn't be seen without that author. I haven't read "Che Him" yet, so I can't say anything.

Samch'ŏlli Side…… In the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報), the proletarian (푸로) leading writer Yi Kiyŏng is publishing "Kŭndae P'unggyŏng" (近代風景 - Modern Landscape), written as a recent major effort. How do you view it?

Tokkyŏn…… Overall, compared to Han Int’aek's work, it wasn't as good for a newspaper novel. That kind of technique probably doesn't work well for newspaper novels. When I went to the provinces, there were many readers of "Sŏnp'ung Sidae," but few people said they read "Kŭndae P'unggyŏng." Even if they started, they stopped midway without finishing.

Sŏhae…… Generally, there's a lot of explanation. In Mr. Kiyŏng's novels, explanation always outweighs description.

Samch'ŏlli Side…… What about "Muhwagwa" (無花果 - Fig) in the Maeil Sinbo (每日申報)? This work began with "Samdae" (三代 - Three Generations) and is being produced as the first part of a trilogy. How did you view it?

Ansŏ…… A work doesn't necessarily have to be a trilogy. After Émile Zola's (에밀,라) "Paris" (巴里) and other trilogies appeared, trilogies became fashionable in the world, it seems.

Sŏhae…… That's true. It doesn't have to be a trilogy. However, I've only seen about the first 70 installments out of 180-something, so I can't say much, but there are many points where I admire it as truly the work of a master (大家). However, this "Muhwagwa" has one flaw: in order to capture the reader's interest by developing the plot complexly, the protagonist's character fails to generate the events; instead, the events drag the protagonist forward. There are rough patches like that.

Pinghŏ…… The writing style (筆致) is astonishingly mature (老熟). The prose is powerful, and the vocabulary is rich.

Tongin…… In psychological description, he's unrivaled in this era (當代獨步), especially in the technique of depicting psychological conflict (갈등).

Samch'ŏlli Side…… Is there a step forward compared to "Sarang kwa Choe" (사랑과 罪 - Love and Sin)?

Ch’unhae…… In terms of interest, "Sarang kwa Choe" is better. Of course, whether it's welcomed by the general reader or not, the work must proceed as a work.

Ansŏ…… Although the title of "Sarang kwa Choe" doesn't match the content, as a work, it's chewy (질깃질깃하여); the more you chew, the deeper and more compelling it becomes.

Pinghŏ…… Is it possible the title doesn't match the content? It's the content that doesn't match the title (Everyone bursts into laughter - 一同哄笑).

Tokkyŏn…… It seems written much more easily than "Sarang kwa Choe."

Sŏnghae…… Right, there is that point. And the fact that it's a weighty work is admirable.

2. Novels Published in Magazines
Samch'ŏlli Side…… Since last autumn, many good works have been published in various magazines like Pip'an (批判), Tonggwang (東光), Chiptan (集團), Hyesŏng (헤星), Sin Yŏsŏng (新女性), Sin Tonga (新東亞), Tongbang P'yŏngnon (東方評論), Samch'ŏlli (삼천리), etc. Which works left a deep impression on you all?

Tokkyŏn…… I saw Kim Tongin's controversial work "Palkkaragi Talmatta" (발가락이 닮엇다 - The Toes Resemble). When I was in the countryside, people were talking about it in various ways, so when I came to Seoul, I sought it out first. Its description, the depth of its theme—it's the finest (白眉) among Mr. Tongin's works. Even setting aside the talk about "models" (모탤 云云說), it was a good recent short story.

Tongin…… Ch'unwŏn's short story "Suam ŭi Ilgi" (壽岩의 日記 - Suam's Diary), published in Samch'ŏlli, was too different from expectations for a work by Ch'unwŏn shown after a long time.

Samch'ŏlli Side…… How did you view Chang Hyŏkchu's "Agwido" (餓鬼道 - Path of the Hungry Ghost), which won the selection for "Kaejo" (改造 - Reconstruction)?

Pinghŏ…… Great! However, I saw the review by Hirotsu Kazuo (廣津和郞), who said it stood out less as a novel and more for the power of its "facts" (사실). Truly, the power of the facts was too strong.

Sŏhae…… From our perspective, there weren't many points to agree with, but anyway, it was quite a powerful work (力作). The description is tough and rough yet strikes one's heart; the theme, arising from the tearful aspects of Southern Chosŏn farming villages, is a work that meets and exceeds the standard of the Chosŏn literary scene. Only the conclusion, being tied up too weakly, was dissatisfying.

Samch'ŏlli Side…… Does that work clearly reveal the reality of Chosŏn (朝鮮現實相) or Chosŏn sentiment (朝鮮情調)?

Sŏnghae…… Not really. Rather, there were many points that seemed like exaggerations.

Works by the Group of Female Writers
Samch'ŏlli Side…… How do you view Kim Iryŏp's recent works?

Sŏhae…… I read a short story by Iryŏp about a husband returning from Tokyo (東京)... It was good, but there were parts where the connection wasn't well established.

Iryŏp…… Didn't Mr. Ch’oe write a review (評文) somewhere?

Tokkyŏn…… Iryŏp's works seem close to essays (수필).

Ch’unhae…… In Iryŏp's works, there's a unique aspect that couldn't be depicted if not by a woman.

Tokkyŏn…… I saw Ch’oe Chŏnghŭi's "Chŏngdanghan Sŭp'ai" (正當한 스파이 - The Righteous Spy), and it lacked a "ざっ" [zatsu - rough/crude?] feeling; I read it as a very good work. Just, if possible, it would have been better if the spy's psychology was written in more detail. Anyway, among the works by female writers I've seen, it stood out the most.

Pinghŏ…… I saw it too and received a shock (쇼크). While reading, there's a part that makes your heart jump startlingly, a "ヒヤリトスル" [hiyaritosuru - to feel a chill/be startled] moment. I viewed it very favorably, and was surprised by the captivating power (魁力) of the pen that pulls the reader along to the end.

Ch’unhae…… I saw it too. For a work encountered first in print (活字), it was a powerful piece with迫る [semaru - pressing/urgent] force.

Sŏnghae…… Although Kim Wŏnju hasn't published it yet, I once saw a piece she wrote, similar in nature to an exam answer when she was joining some newspaper company, titled "Thoughts on the First Morning a Certain Woman Steps onto the Path of a Profession" (엇든 女性이 職業線上에 나서는 첫 아츰 感想). That was indeed very well written. The psychological description of that first morning, burning with anxiety, impatience, and hope, could be called top-notch (上乘).

Tokkyŏn… That's right. Kim Wŏnju's works don't lapse into sentimentality (센치) and are very good. Especially the piece "Thinking of Friends" (동무들 생각하는 글) published in "Sin Yŏsŏng" (新女性), and the article in Wŏlgan Munye (文藝月刊) where she captured Sŏhae for a writer's impression piece—those have a unique writing style. They could be called representative essays.

Sŏhae… Kim Wŏnju's works are intellectual (이지적). They lack the sentimentality that women easily fall into.

Samch'ŏlli Side… What about Song Chuwŏl's works?

Sŏhae… Song Chuwŏl doesn't yet have any works that are particularly surprising. Ultimately, we'll have to wait and see about her future.

Tokkyŏn… That's right. There's a bit of "**********" [censored or illegible part - likely describing a flaw, perhaps 'artificiality' or 'forced construction'] in the works, as if deliberately attached.

Ch’unhae… There was a person named Pak Hwasŏng, who is said to be in P'yŏngyang (平壤) or Tokyo (東京) now. As someone who won selection in the old Chosŏn Mundan (朝鮮文壇), she probably cannot be excluded as a capable writer. I haven't heard much news recently, though.

Sŏhae… That's right. Pak Hwasŏng was an outstandingly talented person.

Samch'ŏlli Side… What about Kang Kyŏngae, who is writing a novel in "Hyesŏng" (헤星)?

Tokkyŏn… It didn't seem like anything remarkable.

Tongin… I also saw it occasionally. Now and then there are interesting expressions, but it has the flaw that connections are not well established.

Dramatic Works
Samch'ŏlli Side… Recently, comparatively many plays have come out. Yun Paengnam, Kim Unjŏng, Kim Yŏngp'al, Ch'ae Mansik, etc.

Tongin… I saw Yun Paengnam's "Annae e Chu urin Sanae" (안해에 주우린 사내 - The Man Kept by His Wife?), published in the May issue of Samch'ŏlli. Generally, the expression was excessive, damaging the life of the work. For instance, it would have been good if the fact that the protagonist is sexually impotent was briefly expressed in passing, but because it was repeated 3 or 4 times, it made the reader fed up and unpleasant.

Samch'ŏlli Side… And works by other authors?

Tokkyŏn… Ch'ae Mansik has been publishing quite a lot recently, but they mostly give the impression of reading reflections (感想) or essays (隨筆).

Hidden Writers with Promising Futures
Samch'ŏlli Side… It seems few have seen the plays, so we'll skip that. Aren't there any hidden writers whose future we can look forward to (囑望)?

Tongin… There's a person named Chŏng Ilsu in Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙道). While there's a point where he doesn't quite understand the short story form, his pen has strength, and he seems likely to grow considerably.

Tokkyŏn… In this year's New Year issue of the Chungang Ilbo, a prize-winning story was published under the name Kim Po-ok. I heard rumors that it's the anonymous name (匿名) of Ms. Kim Malbong. Anyway, she seems like a writer with a future (압날).

Sŏhae… Pak T'aewŏn is also good. Also, Chŏng Ik-t'aek's work published in M Newspaper Company (M신문사) last year had something sparkling about it.

Poetry Scene, Sijo Scene
Samch'ŏlli Side… Do you novelists generally look at poetry (詩歌) works, even though they are the same literary works?

All… We generally look at them. We mostly read poetry (詩) and Sijo (時調).

Samch'ŏlli Side… Recently, Yi Ŭnsang published the "Kyŏngsan Sijo Chip" (驚山詩調集 - Kyŏngsan Sijo Collection). How did you view it?

Pinghŏ… In Kyŏngsan's sijo, there are many exquisite lines (入神의 묘) and masterpieces (絶唱). Truly magnificent (훌융해요).

Sŏhae… I used to think the sijo form felt restrictive, but looking at Mr. Yi Ŭnsang's sijo, not only does he handle it as fluently and smoothly (流麗暢達) as new poetry (新詩), but the content also has a very fresh taste.

Ansŏ… It's true. There are many masterpieces within it. It's a great harvest of recent times. Especially the points like breaking the 3-5-7 syllable count (調) of the sijo's final line (종장) and pioneering new territory, or the first attempt at a 2-line sijo—it's amazing.

Tokkyŏn… I also discovered many good things.

Samch'ŏlli Side… What about Yang Chudong's "Chosŏn ŭi Maekpak" (朝鮮의 脈搏 - The Pulse of Chosŏn)?

Ansŏ… Mr. Yang misread the "Pulse of Chosŏn." In his preface (卷頭自序), he claims ideological poetry (思想詩) is his forte (本領) and speaks lightly of lyrical short pieces (抒情小曲), but Mr. Yang's forte is definitely in the light short pieces. The ideological poetry, seemingly modeled after Ishikawa Takuboku (石川琢木), is not impressive at all.

Samch'ŏlli Side… What about Chu Yohan's recent poetry trends?

Pinghŏ… Now that specialized skill seems required, Ansŏ, why don't you take charge and speak comprehensively?

Ch’unhae, Tokkyŏn… That's right, the right to speak (言權) in this gathering can only go to Ansŏ.

Ansŏ… This is hard for me—
I saw Mr. Chu's recent work, the express train one. The technique of writing small and perhaps separate (작고 고) things, like 'the train runs, runs over mountains, plants, thatched houses,' seems quite powerful and good. However, because he deliberately tries hard to insert "ideology" (이데올로기) into his works, it often spoils them.

Sŏhae… There's definitely that aspect. "Arŭmdaun Saebyŏk" (아름다운 새벽 - Beautiful Dawn) is probably Mr. Yohan's forte. The works from that time were better.

Tokkyŏn… Does the poetry association (詩歌 同人會) receive many poems?

Ansŏ… Yes, they come in well. It's remarkable.

Ch’unhae… We should also write poems now and get critiques through the association.

Ansŏ… You have to bring thirty-five jeon (전 - coin unit).

Pinghŏ… Hey, won't drinks do?
(Everyone laughs)

Samch'ŏlli Side… What about newly emerging poets?

Sŏnghae… Sin Sŏkchŏng's poetry is very beautiful.

Ansŏ… That's right. I see him as a writer who has grasped East Asian mystical thought well. His writing style is refined.

Tongin… Though not new, Sowŏl's [Kim Sowŏl] poems were soft, lovely, and very good. It's regrettable that he stopped writing poetry.

Ch’unhae… Cho Un is like that too. Cho Un's sijo should also be highly evaluated, but it's regrettable that he has recently discontinued writing.

Tokkyŏn… The poems of the late Yi Changhŭi were also good.

"Below this, there are topics discussed such as critique of literary trends and the issue of models in novels," "Writing time, writing speed," "Age and work title of debut," "Total amount of manuscript fees received in the past," "Most satisfying works among past works, etc."
There were many discussed topics, but as it becomes too long, we will stop here for this time and continue in the next issue of this magazine, published on June 1st. Esteemed Readers, please look forward to the next issue." <16>

**Summary**

This article transcribes a roundtable discussion among prominent Korean writers in May 1932, hosted by *Samch'ŏlli* magazine. They critique recently serialized newspaper novels by authors like Yi Kwangsu, Kim Tongin, Han Int’aek, and Yi Kiyŏng, discussing aspects like entertainment value versus literary merit, realism, use of dialect, and writing techniques. The conversation also covers notable short stories and novels published in magazines, including works by Kim Tongin and Chang Hyŏkchu, and evaluates the contributions of female writers such as Kim Iryŏp, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi, Kim Wŏnju, Pak Hwasŏng, and Kang Kyŏngae. They briefly touch upon recent plays and then shift to poetry, praising Yi Ŭnsang's sijo collection and commenting on the works and trends of poets like Yang Chudong, Chu Yohan, Sin Sŏkchŏng, Kim Sowŏl, and Cho Un. The discussion highlights contemporary literary tastes, debates on style and substance, the influence of Japanese literature (Arishima, Tanizaki, Hirotsu, Ishikawa), the emergence of female voices, and the perceived quality of works across different genres.

For colonial history, this text is valuable as it provides a snapshot of the Korean literary scene under Japanese rule, revealing writers' engagement with both traditional forms (sijo) and modern genres (novels, plays), their awareness of Japanese literary trends and figures, and internal debates about language (standard vs. dialect) and the representation of Korean reality ("Chosŏn sentiment," rural suffering). The discussion about "ideology" in Chu Yohan's poetry and the mention of proletarian writer Yi Kiyŏng hint at the underlying social and political currents influencing literature during this period. The commentary on female writers also sheds light on their growing presence and the critical reception of their work.

最近小說壇의 收獲
1. 新聞小說
本社側……조선문단의 전성기라고 부를 수 잇든 칠팔년전만 하여도 朝鮮文壇社나 開闢社를 통하여 文人合評會가튼 것이 각금 열니어서 퍽으나 文壇에 활기를 주든일이 잇섯슴니다만은 근래에는 이러한 모힘이 업는 것을 유감으로 생각하고 조선문학의 번영을 비는 마음으로 오늘 저녁 이 모임을 개최하엿더니 雨中임을 不拘하시고 多數히 오서주심에 謝意를 표함니다.
오늘저녁 출석하려든 분중에 不意에 볼일이 잇서 못오신 이로 李光洙, 安碩柱, 金永八, 朱耀翰, 李殷相, 廉想涉, 金炯元, 崔義順의 여덜분이 잇섯슴니다.
이제부터 본론에 드러가겟슴니다, 최근 네 신문에 발표되는 連載小說을 평하여 주서요. 東亞日報의 李光洙씨 「흙」과 金東仁씨 「아가네」를 보섯슴니까.
曙海……나는 두 가지를 다 첫머리만 보앗는데 그것은 본격적 소설이라기 보다, 한개의 자미잇는 이약이로 밧게 더 아니 보이드군요. 春園의, 녯날 「無情」「開拓者」와, 金東仁씨의 「감자」「明文」을 보든 눈으로 보면 이것은 한 이약이라고 박게 더 할 수 업서요.
金一葉……나는 우리집에선 東亞日報를 아니보고 朝鮮日報만 보기 문에 두 소설을 다 보지 못하엿지요.
憑虛……나도 두 소설을 다 보기도 하고 안보기도 한 셈으로 정독을 못하엿스니까 무에라 말할 수 업지만 역시 行文이 流麗하기는 두 작가에 더한 進境이 잇더군요.
金東仁……내 경험으로 보아서는 우리들 가운데선 아마 피차에 남이 쓴 連載物이라고 잘 아니 읽지걸요.
本社側……아니보신 이가 대부분임니다 그려, 그러터래도 두 連載物을＜14＞ 통하여 春園, 東仁 兩씨의 문예사상의 변천이라든지 수법의 進境이라든지가 보이지 안슴니까.
獨鵑……여보게 그 문제는 어렵네. 정독을 못하엿는데 무어라고 하나.
春海……드구나 金東仁씨의 이번 아기네는 그것은 소년, 소설밧게 더 아니 되더군요.
本社側……그러면 朝鮮日報를 말슴하여 주세요. 連載物로는 전일 懸實一等當選의 韓仁澤씨, 「旋風時代」와 二等當選의 「제힘」이 실녀잇고, 安碩柱씨의 「星群」이 실녀가는 중이외다. 모다 엇더케 보섯슴니까.
曙海……韓仁澤씨의 소설은 갓흔곳 사람의 것이라는 흥미에서 처음 보기 시작하엿는데 퍽으나 조흔 무게잇는 作이엇슴니다. 夕影말을 드러서는 만히 곳처내엇다 하지만 고처서만 가지고는 그러케 안되지, 원작이 훨신 조흔 것으로 생각되는데 그 行文에도 사투리가 별반 업섯슴니다. 근래의 우수한 작품이엇지요.
岸曙……소설에 사투리 쓴다는 점으로 흠을 잡을 것이 못되지요,서울을 배경삼는데도 사투리가 나올걸요.
星海……대화에 잇서 그 지방 사투리를 쓴다는 것은 조곰도 나무랄 일이 못되지만 지문을 사투리로 쓴다하면 그는 나는 반대해요, 첫재 일반독자가 아라볼수 업스니까 작품의 효과가 반감할 우려가 잇지안녀요.
春海……그러치요 사투리란 대화에만 허락될 것이지요. 그러고 원칙적으로는 작품 가운데 될 수록 사투리를 아니집어 넛는 것이 올겟지요,  교육정도가 보급되면 사투리는 자연히 일소될 토이니까.
東仁……有島武郞이 北海도를 제재로 하고 쓴 소설이나 谷崎潤一郞이 大阪을 그린 작품중에는 어서더케나 사투리가 만흔지요, 그러치만 그것들을 누구나 大作이라고, 치니까 사투리의 사용여부가 그러케 작품의 好不好에 결정적 원인이 아니되겟지요.
獨鵑……「旋風時代」를 나는 처음에 읽고 그러고는 가담가담 읽엇는데 신문소설로는 실패가 아닐가 하는 감이 잇섯서요, 연락이 잘 닷지 안코 그러고 기교가 부족하엿서요. 그러치만 존경할 점은 그 묘사에  그 作者아니고는 볼 수 업는 독창적인 점이 잇서요. 그다음 「제힘」은 아직 읽지 못하엿스니까 무에라고 말할 수 업서요.
本社側……中央日報에 푸로 中堅作家 李箕永씨가 근래의 力作으로 쓴 「近代風景」이 실니고 잇는데 엇더게 보심니까.
獨鵑……대체로 신문소설로 보아서는 韓仁澤씨 것만 못하엿지요, 신문소설은 그러한 수법으로는 잘 안될걸요. 지방에 가보니까, 旋風時代의 독자는 만엇지만 「近代風景」을 본다는 사람은 적더군요, 보다가도 까지 보지안코 중지하엿더구만.
曙海……대체로 설명이 만치요. 箕永씨 소설에는 어느 작품이나 묘사보담 설명이 勝하니까요.
本社側……每日申報의 「無花果」는? 이 作은 「三代」로 비롯하야 3部作의 1部로서 제작되어가는 것인데 엇더케 보섯슴니까.
岸曙……작품은 반드시 3部作이라야 되는게 아니겟지 에밀,라의 「巴里」등등 3部作이 잇슨뒤 세상에는 3部作이 돌드구만요.
曙海……그야 그러치요. 3部作이라야만 되는게 아니겟지요. 그런데 나는 이 소설이 180여?를 난중에서 처음 70여?밧게 못 보앗스니까, 무에라고 말할 수 업지만 과연, 大家의 作이구나 하고 敬服할 곳이 만허요. 그러치만 이번 「無花果」에는 한 가지 결점이 잇더구만 독자의 흥미를 을기 위하여 사건을 복잡하게 전개식히느라고 作의 주인공의 성격이 사건을 낫지 못하고 사건이 오히려 주인공을 을고 압서가는 거츠른 대목이 잇더군요.
憑虛……筆致는 놀날만치 老熟하드군 문장이 힘잇고 어휘가 풍부하고.
東仁……심리묘사에는 當代獨步야, 더구나 심리의 갈등을 그리어 내는 수법에 잇서서는.
本社側……「사랑과 罪」에 비하여 일단의 약진이 잇슴니까.
春海……흥미로 본다면 「사랑과 罪」가 낫지요. 그야 일반독자에게 환영밧거나 말거나 作은 作대로 가야하겟지만.
