Article Title: 五月禮讚 (Praise to May)
Author: 늘샘
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

五月禮讚
						늘샘

When the May wind blows coolly into the bosom,
The fine fragrance brushing past the tender colors of new green (新綠),
Cleanses the heart roughened by worldly troubles (世辛).

The May sunlight is so warm,
The round barley heads swell plumply with grain,
And the beauty's (麗人) basket of herbs is full of fragrant scents (香薰).

May's clear wind, May's warm sunlight,
Clear and warm, filling and overflowing the mountains and fields,
Let them satisfy all living souls (生靈) of this land.

From Yonghwa Temple (龍華寺) on Mirŭk Mountain (彌勒山), T'ongyŏng (統營) <38>

**Summary**

This poem by Nŭlsaem, titled "Praise to May," celebrates the sensory beauty and gentle abundance of the month of May. It evokes the cool wind carrying the scent of new greenery, the warm sun ripening the barley, and the fragrance of gathered herbs. The poet expresses a heartfelt wish that the natural bounty of May fills the land and satisfies all its inhabitants. From a historical perspective, the poem offers a glimpse into non-political cultural expression during the colonial period. While focusing on pastoral themes, the final lines wishing for the satisfaction of "all living souls of this land" (이 나라 모든 생령) might subtly reflect an underlying yearning for well-being and abundance in a difficult era, making "this land" (이 나라) a potentially significant phrase for historians studying national consciousness.

五月禮讚
						늘샘
5월에 부는 바람 품에 선들 들제마다,
신록의 연한 빗흘 스처오는 고흔 향기,
世辛에 거츤 가슴을 맑숙하게 하왜라.

5월 햇살은 따스기도 하올세라,
동올은 보리모개 소복소복 알을 배고,
麗人의 나물 광주리 香薰 가득하왜라.

5월의 맑은 바람 5월의 따슨 햇살,
맑은 채 따스한 채 산에 들에 차고 넘어,
이 나라 모든 생령을 배불니게 하여라.

統營, 彌勒山 龍華寺에서＜38＞
