The Blood and Tears of Female Writers
A Man's Heart, Ever-Changing Like a Reed Swaying in the Wind, Son Chu-ri-na

I could not say "No" to Mr. Kim's sincere marriage proposal (求婚). Therefore, I married him.
At this point, you might wonder what kind of great woman I am to be writing such presumptuous words so clumsily.
Anyone, during their young maiden days, might have thoughts about "what kind of man they want to choose" when seeking a partner, but I think there was probably no other girl with as many serious fantasies about it as I had.
It was just last spring. Having graduated from the liberal arts department of R Women's Professional School, I dreamed of becoming a distinguished female writer (女流文士). However, pressed by financial difficulties, I was busier searching for some kind of job rather than pursuing that path. During my student days, I naively dreamed of traveling abroad and envisioned myself as a world-renowned female author, but in the end, I ended up confined as an office worker at a life insurance company for a monthly salary of 30 won. It's truly pathetic.
But what could I do? It is an inevitable consequence arising from the contradictions of the capitalist social system...
After some time, fortune came my way: Kim, an executive at this company, incessantly poured his affection onto me.
Unaware of the world's hardships – and in the bloom of youth – I could not ignore that person's – Kim's – affection. Thus, with the heat of love exceeding one hundred percent, I rejected other suitors and said "Yes" to Kim's proposal.
Immediately after marriage, I cast aside the title (가다가 - katakki, likely 片仮名 for 肩書き, *katagaki*, meaning 'title' or 'status') of "office worker," built a love nest, and started our married life. After getting married, I somehow began to treat my husband Kim rather plainly, but Kim remained the same, showering me with fiery affection – truly, I had become his life. I, too, felt he was the only loving, good husband in the world. If I were the queen of our new home, it would be no exaggeration to say Kim was my slave.
If I happened to say to Kim,
"Somehow, I've grown tired of seeing you," he would blink his tear-filled eyes and unconditionally kneel down and apologize to me.
On his way back from the company, he would buy things I liked. When he came home, he would not only do whatever I asked but even put my socks on for me on more than one or two occasions. And Kim didn't go to the cafés I disliked, nor did he drink alcohol. He entrusted his salary (사라리-) entirely to me.
When I gave him streetcar fare, Kim would thank me many times over.
As Kim's passion grew so intense, I too poured genuine affection towards him. Feeling it was frivolous to keep calling him Mr. Kim, I began using the respectful term "Sŏbangnim" (서방님 - husband/master), and after reforming all my previous habits, I tried hard to become a sincere wife to him.
From then on, our new life proceeded along an even happier course.
However, this happiness could not last long. It was late spring in the south, when even the springless Arctic feels a touch of spring; a time when all the fully bloomed flowers had fallen, and only one or two petals fluttered among the green leaves.
While dusting (*) the spring coat my husband had taken off and left behind, I discovered a letter in the pocket.
"...Sŏbangnim! I definitely received the money you sent the other day. Thanks to you, the little child is growing well. Now, the child naturally follows its parent. Mother is always waiting for the day you come. Summer vacation – for me, it is the season of unparalleled joy. I am just waiting for that time to come quickly."
It was undoubtedly a letter addressed to Kim. The sender (發信人) was clearly a woman. What must my heart have felt upon discovering this? How could I have known Kim was another's father and husband? A dreamlike reality – how many times, how many times did I pinch my own flesh, wondering if this was just a dream? But it was an undeniable fact – a letter from Kim's hometown.
Kim, who had revered me like a queen, but seeing such a letter, how could I trust him? Yet, on the other hand, I also thought I couldn't doubt Kim based on just one letter.
But a dreadful premonition kept knocking repeatedly at my heart. Therefore, keeping silent towards Kim, I wrote a letter to my friend C in Kim's hometown, requesting a full investigation.

Kim's affection remained unchanged, just as before. Unable to bear it, one evening I asked him about the matter troubling me. "Have you never known another woman besides me?" I asked!
Kim just flatly denied it, asking why I was suddenly asking such a thing. I had trusted Kim like God and intended to believe him no matter what happened, but until the letter arrived from friend C, my heart churned, and I couldn't sleep at night.
My husband – Kim – the woman he loved – the woman who loved Kim – Ah! Even if such a fact existed, I wanted to deny it.

The day of judgment arrived. The contents of the letter from friend C were as follows:
Kim was a man with a wife and son, moreover, it was a marriage born of a love affair. I couldn't even finish reading friend C's letter with such content before I fainted.
Sometime later, when I opened my eyes, I found myself lying on a hospital bed.
Kim, sitting with his head bowed, asked me when I seemed to have regained some consciousness, "How are you feeling?" But without answering his words, I just poured tears onto the white bedsheet.
Day after day, night after night, Kim sat before me, begging for forgiveness. His state seemed rather pitiful, and an even hotter affection than before became entangled. Was this the psychological trick of a weak woman?
In this way, a long year passed, and wrinkles formed on my forehead. Another pitiful being was added – Kim's illegitimate child, that is, my dear son.
Complex problems only increased day by day, but having fallen into such misfortune, I tried to endure and live on. However, a fact occurred that I could no longer bear.

I learned that Kim had a lover, the wealthy widow K in B-dong, Seoul, and had also seduced a student at the school my younger sister attended, even driving her to suicide.
It would have been fortunate amidst misfortune if the incidents had ended there, but eventually, he completely withdrew all affection for me. Consequently, carrying the pitiful illegitimate child on my back, I began living in a single rented room, wandering the streets of Seoul, searching for work several times a day. But finding a job was not easy, and in the end, I became a waitress (웨트례쓰 - waitress) at the R.W. Café.
Less than a month after becoming a waitress, I learned to drink well and also learned how to play around with men.
It was a certain Saturday. From early evening, every booth in the café was packed with customers, so the waitresses were extremely busy.
Alcohol consumed every evening – drunk every evening – when I was heavily intoxicated, I spotted a man resembling Kim in a corner far off.
Rubbing my eyes and looking closely, he was unmistakably the Kim I had loved in the past – Kim, my enemy now.
He was hugging and kissing one waitress after another – any waitress he could grab.
At that moment, the record playing on the phonograph was the tune from Rigoletto, the song "Like a reed swaying in the wind, a woman's heart is ever-changing." Feeling like I would go mad, I went to where the phonograph was, smashed the spinning record, threw it down, and collapsed on the spot.
When I regained consciousness, it was four in the morning. The baby, wanting to nurse, had cried so much throughout the night that its voice had become hoarse, and its cries were barely audible.
Even so, people search for the joy of life.
When I think of myself, I feel so pitiful. The tears of a young intellectual! I even want to scoff.
How could I have known that the result of studying with great aspirations for fourteen years would be so futile?
The autumn wind blowing through the windowpane will mercilessly cause the blue-green leaves to fall. -(End)-

A New Record of Tragic Love
I Cannot Bear to Steal My Friend's Fiancé!

Paek Hye-ryŏn

There is probably no other woman in the world as naive as I am. I truly had no feelings about this world. It might seem like I'm boasting, but since I intend to write the truth as it is, it can't be helped.
My academic performance at school was also quite good.
They say that usually, those with unattractive faces study well, but upon my friends' recommendation, I even sent in a photograph when the XX Newspaper hosted the Miss Chosŏn contest.
However, my family was poor. Due to malnutrition while studying for exams, my body weakened extremely, and as days went by, my illness worsened.
Thus, inevitably using the summer vacation, I went with friend K to Sŏgwang Temple for recuperation.
Friend K had a fiancé named B. I don't feel the need for a lengthy explanation here. I merely intend to reveal a few passages from my sorrowful diary. You will understand my painful situation, won't you?

July X
The rain that started late last night hasn't stopped even by morning and is still falling now. What was that unexpected event last night? For the first time since I was born into this world, I embraced and exchanged kisses with someone of the opposite sex (異性) – him (K's fiancé). Somehow, it feels nothing more than a dream. He was drunk. But… despite his lover K – my friend K – being in the same room, he crawled into my bedding and gave me kiss after kiss, repeatedly. I feel endlessly sorry towards friend K, but I couldn't help but remain quiet because I was afraid friend K would find out. He talked to me about various things, but I couldn't hear anything. All I remember now is just him saying he was sorry. Today, I became hesitant to meet B.

July X
Whenever I meet B, my heart somehow flutters. But no matter how much I think about his feelings, I cannot understand them. When friend K goes somewhere and I am left alone with him, I feel my heart flutter. Why is that?

X day
I certainly love him. Deceiving friend K's eyes! I should dislike him, but the affection flowing towards him increases day by day, which is agonizing. Our fate is not destined for marriage, yet deep inside, I secretly wish he would abandon my friend K and marry me. What am I if not a sinner?
I must not become any closer to B than this. No matter how I think about it, I must exercise restraint.

X day <30>
Last night, a light rain fell heavily. Friend K and I pulled the covers over us from early evening and lay down, talking about our school days. Friend K, unaware of anything (between B and me), chatters endlessly in a cheerful tone. Hearing B's sighs intermittently from the room above, my heart just pounded violently. My conversation with friend K continued for a long time, but whatever we talked about, hardly anything remains in my memory.

August X
K is the daughter of a wealthy family. B is the son of a poor family. B told me, taking advantage of a moment when friend K was briefly out, that while he truly loves K, he only pretends to love her because of money. Could it be true? If so, I feel sorry for friend K. And yet, after hearing those words, my heart feels quite cheerful. Is it because I realized B truly loves me?

August X
Today, B showed me a letter sent to him by friend K's father. The content of the letter was this:
He would send 200 won, asking B to make every effort regarding the weak K's recuperation during the summer.
After I finished reading the letter, B stared at me for a while and said, "I am a slave to money," then gave me a passionate kiss and a strong embrace (포응).

August X
K doesn't suspect the relationship between B and me in the slightest. If B says he's unwell and cannot go for a walk, I also don't want to go out, so I somehow make up an excuse and send friend K out alone. As days go by, this happens repeatedly, but K doesn't notice at all and goes out and comes back alone. This morning too, K went out, leaving only B and me. B again, as usual, gives me countless embraces and kisses. More than his embraces and kisses, I wish he would talk about the future. I wish he would tell me his plan (푸랭 - p'uraeng, likely from French 'plan') – what he will do about K and what he will do about me.

August X
Today, it's been extremely hot since morning.
K insists she won't go for a walk alone. My heart pounded, wondering if she perhaps found out about us. But K still seems unaware.
There were quite a lot of people at the mineral spring. B, I, and K also went to drink water, but B – too! – like the others, didn't want to drink the water for the sake of getting well.

September X
Somehow, the summer has passed, and the people gathered at the mineral spring have dwindled one by one.
A letter arrived from K's family in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), telling her to come up to the capital around the end of this month (末日). That means there are only about 20 days left. Then, it's fate that I cannot help but part from B. My greatest sorrow! My most regrettable thing is parting from B. B, firmly embedded in my naive heart, how could I ever forget him?
I am anxious, wondering if B knows my feelings as I face parting from him soon, not knowing if we will meet in Kyŏngsŏng or not.
As the weather cools, K doesn't go out as much as before, so B and I cannot find opportunities to talk. I am very curious if B's nights are the same as before.

September X
I cried intensely. Crying secretly so K wouldn't know made it difficult to cry. B and K's wedding ceremony is on the sixth of October! K is jumping for joy and even asks me to be her bridesmaid. B hasn't shown his face all day, so I don't know what he's doing in his room. Today, I want to see his face even more. But I'm afraid I might scream and cry when I see his face. K goes in and out of B's room several times. It seems they are discussing the wedding. I want to know what attitude B is taking and what thoughts he has.

September X
After a long time, K went out for a morning walk. The moment K disappeared from the doorway, B rushed down like fire to the lower room! Where I was. Seeing his face, only hot tears flowed.
B also cried, with what felt like a man's weeping. Could what he said be true? That he will break off the engagement with K and marry me! Though I can't believe it, I feel a sense of joy. When B's eyes reddened and my eyes were swollen from crying so much, K returned. There's only a week left until B and K's wedding day. Tomorrow is the sorrowful day I leave alone.

October 6
It's a brightly clear morning. For a whole week, my anxiety while waiting only for B's letter made me look more frightening than a patient who has been sick for a year.
B and K's wedding hall – beside them as they walked side by side, I too, as a bridesmaid, walked together at the same pace. Standing before the pastor, making their eternal vows, turning, and linking arms, with B and K ahead, my legs trembled, and my vision blurred. I was even worried I might collapse on the spot. Beneath the brilliantly hanging five-colored tapes (떳부 - ttŏppu?) – blue tape, red tape – as the two walked, savoring the anesthesia of happiness, white and red streamers of blessing were tangled here and there. The remaining tangled streamers also hung down on my head and shoulders. At that moment, I felt terror and trembling. Why did B look so desolate? Biting my lips, with thoughts of curses and jealousy, I congratulated (?) their happiness.
Oh – the record of days gone by! -(End)-

A Fallen New Woman's Lament: True Love Exists Only with Parents!

Yi Hye-suk

Mother!
I have taken up my pen. You must be surprised that I, who haven't sent a single letter in three years, am now holding a pen addressed to you, Mother. My hand holding the pen – very thin and gaunt – trembled.
Mother! Today too has drawn to a close. Hyesuk also gazed blankly for a while at the sky dyed red in the west. And then I cried. Recalling the path of hardship I have trodden until now.
Pitiful Mother!
You used to say this, didn't you? "Our Hyesuk has become a teacher at a Seoul normal school." The image of you boasting happily to every villager – everyone you met – still hasn't faded from my mind. And Mother, you said,
"Hyesuk! How could I have ever dreamed that you would earn a salary and even manage a household in Seoul?" Mother! I remember it well.
Mother! Four years have already passed since then, but Hyesuk, who rejoiced at being educated! And Mother, who rejoiced at the thought of managing a Seoul household! I still haven't been able to bring you joy.
Mother! Please forgive me. During those four years, all Hyesuk has given you, Mother, is only cause to shed tears.
Kind Mother!
Having lost my father when young, you raised us two siblings, and despite being pressed by poverty, you worked day and night doing sewing for pay (삭바느질) to make us complete individuals, even enabling Hyesuk to finish girls' normal high school.
Now, lying sick in bed and groaning, Hyesuk has realized how pitiful you are, Mother. My heart aches as if it will tear, no matter how much I cry and cry again.
Kind Mother!
I remember often. How, when I was sick, you would stay up all night sitting beside me! But now, Hyesuk has no one to nurse her. I am crying alone in a small, dark room on the second floor of a bar (술집 - sulchip) / café (카페-). How could I not wish to run into your arms, Mother? But having wandered here and there for three years, what I ultimately gained is illness. If I were to visit you, Mother, dragging this sick body, you would surely welcome me warmly, Mother, but thinking of your sad situation (비경), fearing the ridicule of the villagers and relatives, I remain a lonely and desolate sick guest in a foreign land.
Pitiful Mother!
I hear it well. That the villagers ridicule, saying, "They say XX's daughter is working as a kisaeng in Kyŏngsŏng!"
And that when relatives make a fuss, saying, "They say that wench Hyesuk is working as a café waitress – where else could such a family-ruining thing happen!", you cry, Mother, and defend me, saying, "It's not Hyesuk's fault, it's just my fault. It happened because I raised her poorly. It's definitely not Hyesuk's sin." I have also heard rumors like this. I have realized that the only one who sheds tears of blood for me is you, Mother.
Mother!
You probably know better than I that I was not fundamentally a bad person. I was definitely not a bad person. Since you, Mother, already know well the motive (둥기) why Hyesuk came to be pointed at by people, I do not wish to repeat the story here.
Mother! When people curse me and ridicule you, Mother, please just endure it silently. Of course, how great must that suffering be, but it is the last request of the dying Hyesuk, so please grant it.
Mother!
The bird that always cries at dusk has come again today and is crying. Whenever I hear the monotonous cry of that nameless bird, I become more acutely aware of my withered existence.
Mother! As I try to write more, I gradually lose strength, and the pen I held slips from my grasp.
May 7, 1932
From lonely Hyesuk
From her sickbed

**Summary**

This article presents three first-person accounts from educated Korean women ("intellectual women," "new women") in 1932 who, despite graduating from institutions like Girls' Normal High School or Women's Professional School, ended up in socially marginalized positions such as café waitresses or facing personal ruin due to tragic relationships and societal constraints. Son Chu-ri-na recounts marrying a seemingly devoted executive who turned out to be deceitful, leading her to become a waitress. Paek Hye-ryŏn details her painful experience falling for her friend's fiancé during a summer retreat, ultimately witnessing their marriage while grappling with her guilt and unrequited love. Yi Hye-suk writes a poignant letter to her mother from her sickbed, expressing remorse for disappointing her family's hopes after becoming a teacher, acknowledging the rumors of her working as a kisaeng or waitress, and reflecting on her downfall caused by circumstances she doesn't detail but implies were beyond her control.

From a historical perspective, these accounts are significant as they provide intimate glimpses into the lives of educated Korean women during the colonial period. They highlight the limited opportunities available even to graduates of higher education, the economic hardships driving women into precarious professions like café waitressing (often associated with sex work), and the social pressures and double standards they faced, particularly concerning relationships and marriage. The narratives underscore the clash between modern aspirations ('New Woman', 'intellectual') fostered by education and the harsh realities of colonial society, patriarchal norms, and economic instability, making them valuable sources for understanding female experiences, social mobility challenges, and the complex emotional landscape of modernizing Korea under Japanese rule. The specific mention of salary (30 won), company executives, cafés, and the contrast between educational achievement and subsequent fate offer concrete details about urban life and social stratification in 1930s Seoul.

필자	孫朱利奈, 白惠蓮, 李惠淑
기사형태	회고·수기

인테리 여성의 비극, 그 여자는 女子普高를 졸업하고 엇재서 기생과 여급이 되엿나?
						孫朱利奈, 白惠蓮, 李惠淑
여류문사의 血淚
바람에 불니는 갈대와 갓치 항상 변하는 남자의 마음, 孫朱利奈
金의 성의 잇는 구혼(求婚)에 나는 「노-」라고는 할 수 업섯습니다. 그럼으로 나는 그와 결혼을 하엿담니다.
여기에 여러분은 어마나 훌융한 녀성이기에 건방진 말을 서투리에다가 써 놋느냐고 하실 것임니다.
누구를 물논하고 나 어린 처녀 시대엔 상대자를 요구하는 데 잇서서 「엇더한 남성을 택하고 십다」는 생각을 가지고 잇겟지만 나처럼 거기 대해서 심각한 공상이 만흔 게집애는  다시 업섯슬 것 갓해요.
바로 작년 봄이엿나이다.  R녀자전문학교 문과를 졸업한 나는 한 사람의 훌융한 녀류문사(女流文士)가 되려고 엇지요. 그러나 생활 곤란에 들니는 나에게는 그 길노 기보담 엇던 직업을 구하기에 더욱 분망하엿슴니다. 학생 시절엔 철 업시 양행도 엇스며 세게적 녀류작가도 눈 압헤 그려보앗지요 만은 종결이 니르러서 생명보험회사 사무원으로 우러급 30원에 구속되고 말엇스니 참 한심한 노릇임니다.
그러나 엇더케 함닛가. 자본주의 사회제도의 모순에서 발생되는 필연적 결과임을.........
얼마 후에 나에게는 행운이 닥처 왓스니 그것은 이 회사의 중역인 金이 나에게 부절이 애정을 쏘다 주엇든 것임니다.
세상의 파란을 몰으는 나- 피는 청춘이라 그 사람- 金의 연정을 등한시하지 안케 되엿드람니다. 그래서 사랑의 열을 백파-센트를 초월하야 다른 구혼자들을 차 버리고 金의 구혼애 「예-쓰』라고 하엿담니다.
결혼 후 즉시 사무원이라는 「가다가」는 내버리고 사랑의 보금자리를 미고 살님을 시작햇슴니다. 결혼을 하고 보니 엇전지 남편 金에게 대해서 평범하게 대해젓스나 金은 맛찬가지로 불갓흔 애정-확실히 나는 그의 생명이 되고 말엄슴니다. 나 역시 오직 세상에 둘도 업는 사랑스러운 조흔 남편이라고 늣겻습니다. 내가 신가정의 녀왕이라고 하면 金은 나의 노예라고 해도 과언이 안이엿나이다.
나는 혹시 金을 대해서
「나는 엇전지 당신이 보기 실혀젓서요.」라고 말하면 그는 눈물 어린 눈을 박질하여가면서 무조건으로 무릅을 고 안저 나 안태 사과한담니다.
그는 회사에서 도라오는 길에 내가 조화하는 물건을 사다주엇스며 집 안에 도라와서는 내가 식히는 일이면 무엇이나 다 들어준 것은 물논이고 심지어 양말까지 신켜준 가 한 번이나 두 번이 안이엿나이다. 그리고 金은 내가 실혀하는 카페-도 가지 안엇스며 술도 먹지를 안엇습니다. 「사라리-」는 그대로 맷겻담니다.
내가 전차비를 주면 몃 번이나 고맙다고 인사하는 金이엿습니다.
金의 열정이 이다지도 놉하갓슴으 나도 그에게 대해서 진정한 애정을 쏘다 부엇지요. 지금까지 金씨라고 불으른 것이 경솔한 듯 십허서 「서방님」이라는 존경어를 쓰게 되엿스며 전에 하든 일절의 풍습을 개랑한 후 나는 그에게 대해서 진실한 안해가 될여고 애썻담니다.
이로부터 우리의 새 살님은 한층 더 행복스러운 과정을 밟고 진행하엿지요.
그러나 이러한 행복은 오래 게속되지를 못햇습니다. 봄 업는 북극에도 봄 긔운이 도는 남의 느진 봄이엿스니, 필대로 피엿든 들도 다- 러지고 푸른 입사귀 틈에 한 송이나 두 송이의 입피 나풀거리는 엿습니다.
남편이 버서놋코 간 스프링에 몬지를 쓸*가 포켓트에서 나는 한 장의 편질ㄹ 발견하엿습니다.
「.........서방님! 일전에 보내주신 돈을 틀님업시 밧엇습니다. 덕택으로 어린 아희도 잘 크고 잇습니다. 이제는 천연스럽게도 어버이를 어 갑니다. 어머님서는 늘 서방님 오시는 날만 기다리고 잇습니다. 여름 휴가-나에게 잇서서는 다시 업는 깃븜의 씨-슨임니다. 속히 그가 오기만 기다리고 잇습니다.」
金에게 온 편지임이 틀님업섯나이다. 발신인(發信人)은 확실히 녀자엿슴니다. 이것을 발견한 내 가삼이 엇더하엿겟습닛가. 金이 남에 아버지요 남편인줄 엇지 알엇겟습닛가. 꿈갓흔 사실-몃번이나 몃번이나 이것이 꿈이나 안인가 하고 내 살점을 꼬집으면서 정신을 차려도 그것은 어김업는 사실-金의 고향에서 온 편지엿습니다.
나를 녀왕이라고까지 숭배하든 金이엿스나 이려한 편지를 보고 엇지 그를 밋을 수 잇습닛가 만은 또 한편으로는 한 장의 편지로 金을 의심할 수 업다고도 생각햇담니다.
그러나 두러운 예감은 나의 가삼으로 연겁허 두다리고 잇섯습니다. 그럼으로 金에게는 함구무언하고 金의 고향에 잇는 나의 동무 C에게 일절 조사를 의레하는 편지를 써너엇지요.

白惠蓮

세상에 나만큼 순진한 녀성은 또 업슬 것임니다. 너무도 이 세성에 대해서 아모 늣김도 업섯든 것이외다. 내가 내 자랑을 하는 것 갓지만은 사실 그대로 쓰려고 하니 할 수 업는 일이 아님닛가.
나는 학교에서 공부 성적도 상당히 조왓슴니다.
대개 얼골이 못난 사람이 공부 잘 한다는데요 나는 그대로 동모의 권고로 XX신문사 주최의 미쓰 조선을 뽑을 때 사진까지 보낸 일도 잇담니다.
그러나 우리 집은 가난하엿습니다. 시험 공부할 제 영양 부족으로서 몸이 극도로 쇠약하야 날이 갈수록 신병이 중하여 젓슴니다.
그리하야 할 수 업시 열음 방학을 리용하야 나는 K동무를 따라서 석왕사에 수양하려 갓섯슴니다.
K동무는 B라는 약혼자를 가지고 잇섯슴니다. 나는 여기서 다른 설명을 길게 할 필요를 늣기지 안슴니다. 다만 나의 애닯은 일긔의 몃 절을 공개하려는 것임니다. 여러분은 나의 쓰라린 사정을 리해하여 주지시겟지요.

7월 X일
밤이 깁허서 오기 시작한 비는 아참이 되여도 끈치질을 안코 지금도 나리고 잇다. 어제 밤에 생각지도 안튼 사건이 웬일이엿을가. 나는 세상에 태여난 후 처음으로 이성(異性)-그이(K의 약혼자)와 포옹하엿스며 키쓰를 밧구웟다. 엇전지 꿈갓흔 늣김밧게 업다. 그이는 술에 취햇섯다. 그러나 …. 갓흔 방에 자긔의 애인 K-나의 동무 K-가 잇슴에도 불고하고 그는 내 자리에 기여 들어와서 몃 변이나 몃 변이나 키쓰를 던저주엇다. 나는 K동무에게 미안한 생각이 끗치업스나 조용히 잇지 안을 수 업섯슴은 K동무가 알가 무서웟슴이다. 그는 나에게 여러 가지 이약이를 햇스나 나는 조곰도 들니지 안엇다. 지금까지 긔억되는 것은 단지 미안하다고 하든 것 뿐이다. 나는 오늘 B와 맛나기를 주저하게 되엿다.

X일

돈 2백원을 부처 줄테니 한여름 동안 몸 약한 K의 수양에 대해서 극역 노력해 달라는 것이엿다.

어머님!
저는 펜을 잡엇슴니다. 삼년 동안이나 한 장의 편지도 업든 제가 지금에 잇서서 어머님을 향하야 펜 잡엇슴을 어머님은 놀나실 것임니다. 몹시도 마르고 여윈 손-펜 잡은 저의 손-은 떨리엿나이다.
어머님! 오늘 해도 저무럿슴니다. 헤숙도 서쪽에 붉게 물드린 하늘을 한참이나 물끄럼이 내다보앗읍니다. 그리고는 울엇슴니다. 지금까지에 밝버온 역경에 길을 회상하고서요.
불상한 어머님!
어머님은 이러케 말슴하섯지요 「우리 헤숙이가 서울 보통학교 선생이 되엿담니다」 동리 사람-맛나는 사람-마다 보시고 자랑하시면서 깃버하시던 형상이 지금도 머리에서 사라지지를 안엇담니다. 그리고 어머님은.
「헤숙아! 네가 월급바다서 서울살림까지 하게 될 줄 엇지 꿈앤들 생각햇겟니」 하섯지요. 어머님! 잘 기억하고 잇슴니다.
어머님! 그때는 벌서 흘너서 네 해가 지나고 마럿습니다, 만은 공부식혀 준다고 깃버하던 헤숙이도! 서울살림 하신다고 깃버하시던 어머님도 아직것 깃부게 해드리지 못햇슴니다.
어머님! 용서하여 주십세요. 사년이란 그 동안에 헤숙은 어머님에게 드린 것은 단지 눈물을 훌리시게 한 것뿐이람니다.
자애하신 어머님!
어려서 아버지를 이러버린 저의 두 형제를 빈곤에 쫄녀가면서도 완전한 사람을 만드실녀고 밤이나 낫이나 삭바느질하여서 헤숙이를 고등보통학교까지 맛추어 주섯지요.
병석에 누어서 신음하고 잇는 지금에 헤숙은 어머님이 불상하시다는 것을 깨다럿슴니다. 울고 또다시 울어도 시원치 못할만큼 저에 가삼이 찌저질 듯이 압품니다.
친절하시던 어머님!
저는 때때로 생각남니다. 아프면 밤을 주무시지 안코 제겻헤 안저게시던 일을! 그러나 지금에 헤숙은 간호해 주는 사람도 업담니다. 좁고 어두운 술집-(카페-) 이층방에서 혼자 울고 잇슴니다. 어머님 품으로 뛰어가고 십흔 생각이 엇지 업스리오만은 삼년동안이나 이리저리 방황하여서 결국 엇은 것이 병이니 병든 몸을 끌고 어머님을 차저간다면 어머님이야 그래도 반갑게 마저주시겟지요 만은 동리 사람 친척들에 비우슴을 두려워하실 어머님에 비경을 생각하여 타향에 외롭고 쓸쓸한 병객이 되여 잇슴니다.
불상하신 어머님!
저는 잘 듯고 잇습니다. 동리 사람들이 「XX집 딸은 경성서 기생 노릇을 한다는대」 하고 비웃는다는 것을!
그리고 친척들이 「헤숙이란 년은 카페 녀급으로 잇다는대-이런 집안 망할 일이 어대 잇단 말인가」 하고 야단이 나면 어머님은 우시면서. 「헤숙의 잘못이 안이고 단지 저에 잘못이람니다. 제가 잘못 길너서 그러케 된 것이지요. 결코 해숙의 죄는 안이람니다」 라고 변명하여 주신다는 소문도 드럿습니다. 저때문에 피눈물을 흘려주시는 이는 어머님 한 분밧게 업다는 것도 깨다럿습니다.
어머님!
제가 근본적으로 못된 인간이 안이엿던 것만은 저보담 어머님께서 더 잘 아실 것이겟지요. 저는 결코 못된 인간이 안이엿읍니다. 헤숙이가 세상 사람들의 손가람질을 밧게 된 둥기는 어머님이 임이 잘 아시니 여기에 또다시 이야기하고 십지 안습니다.
어머님! 세상 사람들이 저를 욕하고 어머님을 비웃을 때 어머님은 가만히 참고 견대여 주십시오. 물론 그 괴롬이 얼마나 하시겟습니까,만 죽어 가는 헤숙의 마지막 부탁이오니 드러주십시요.
어머님!
언제나 황혼이면 우는 새가 또 오늘도 와서 울고 잇읍니다. 일홈 모를 저 새의 단조스런 우름 소리를 드를 적마다 시드러진 나의 존재를 더 똑똑이 인식하게 됩니다.
어머님! 더 쓸려니 점점 기운이 엄서저서 잡엇던 펜대가 노처집니다.
