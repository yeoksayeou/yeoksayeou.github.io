感激으로 듯는 깁분 消息
						兪鎭泰

In times past (往時), Taejonggyo (大倧敎) flourished, and there was no one who did not know of Taejonggyo. That it was interrupted (中絶) in the world for a long time was something I considered truly regrettable, but that people nowadays are saying Taejonggyo must be revived again is very joyful news.

All of our politics and teachings (政敎) were first opened by Tan'gun (檀君), so all the traces (痕影) left upon our ancestors (先民) originate (由因) from Tan'gun's primary emergence. Therefore, I believe that all of us, without exception, must admire (響慕) and pursue this.

Although we are called a people who observe etiquette (禮儀), in the true sense, we are ignorant of our ancestors. When Confucianism and Buddhism flourished, scholars were merely intoxicated with those of China (支那) or India (印度), and in modern times (近世), they are similarly immersed (沈潛) only in other European and American (歐米) thoughts, treating their own ancestors with contempt (賤待).

However, that in recent years (近年) the reconstruction of Tan'gun's Tomb (檀君墓改築) occurred, and that now Taejonggyo is being revived again, is a matter of course for descendants (後裔) of Tan'gun, an event that cannot but be called grand (盛事) and welcome news.<143>

**Summary**

The author, Yu Chin-t'ae, expresses great joy at the news of efforts to revive Taejonggyo, an indigenous Korean religion centered on Tan'gun. He laments that Taejonggyo was interrupted for a long time and criticizes Koreans for neglecting their own heritage and ancestor Tan'gun, instead becoming absorbed in foreign ideologies from China, India, and the West (Europe/America). He sees the recent reconstruction of Tan'gun's tomb and the movement to revive Taejonggyo as natural and significant events for the descendants of Tan'gun. From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects a growing nationalist sentiment and desire to reclaim indigenous Korean identity and history through religion (Taejonggyo) and the foundational myth of Tan'gun during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the conscious effort to resist cultural assimilation by emphasizing native traditions over imported or imposed ones.

感激으로 듯는 깁분 消息
						兪鎭泰
往왕왕往時에는 大倧敎가 盛하야 누구든지 大倧敎를 모르는 사람이 없섯습니다. 오랫 동안 세상에 이것이 中絶되야 퍽으나 遺憾으로 생각하든 바이 온대 요지음 세상에서 다시 大倧敎를 이르켜야 한다는 것은 매우 깁분 일이 올시다.
우리의 모든 政敎는 檀君이 비로서 열었사오니 우리 先民에게 끼친 모든 痕影은 檀君이 首出하심에 由因한 것이올시다. 그리하오니 우리는 누구를 물론하고 이를 響慕하고 추구하여야 되겟다고 봄니다.
우리는 禮儀를 차리는 民族이라고 하나 진실한 의미에 잇서서는 조상을 모르는 것이올시다. 儒敎, 佛敎가 盛할 때는 학자들이 支那 내지 印度의 그것에만 도취하고 近世에는 또 다른 歐米思想에만 沈潛해서 자기의 조상을 賤待하고 잇는 것이올시다.
그그런대 近年에 와서 檀君墓改築을 그리고 요지음 다시 大倧敎를 이르킨다는 것은 檀君의 後裔으로서는 당연한 일이나 盛事라 않을 수 없고 반가운 일이올시다.<143>
