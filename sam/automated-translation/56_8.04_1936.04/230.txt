Passionate Argument, Passionate Argument, Passionate Argument
Production and Consumption and Our Awakening

Speech by Mr. Cho Man-sik
This problem concerns how, by what policy, we can rescue those who are in hardship of living.
I believe this issue is very important and urgent, and at the same time, an interesting problem. However, it is also a fact that it is quite a difficult problem<116>.
Since the problem expresses poverty and takes it as a premise, there is likely no need to state the evidence of that poverty.
However, if one insists, the following few points can be raised.
First is the difficulty of living. This, more than anything, is a fact that we witness and experience daily in reality.
Second is living in debt. Besides the debts of merchants and industrialists, the debt of farmers alone is said to be between four and five hundred million won.
Third is the phenomenon of possessions gradually becoming scarce, such as the drastic decrease in the area and quantity of land and houses due to their natural price increase.
The first issue we need to consider is the countermeasure for this, and when thinking about this countermeasure, we feel the need to examine the causes of poverty. Just as when treating an illness, one must first know the root cause of the illness—
Then let us find out what the causes of poverty really are.
First, we can point to the boundlessness of money lending. As our lives have special circumstances, this becomes related to unlimited money lending, so at the very least, there should be a policy centered on the Korean people (朝鮮人 本位).
Next is the inadequacy of the general level of the people (一般民度). Whether to call it the inferiority of the people's level or the insufficiency of the standard, I believe this is an issue we each must always strive deeply for.
Next is the lack of productive enterprises (製産事業). This is an issue requiring the rise (蹶起) of property owners and the combination of small capital.
Also, there is the trend of waste, luxury, and excess (濫用, 奢侈, 過度). This is likely a phenomenon stemming from the lack of the concepts of saving and frugality that we always cry out for.
Next is the trend of natural population increase, showing an annual growth rate of 300,000, for which birth control (産兒制限) will also be impossible.
Regarding the causes observed above, there are two types of countermeasures: desirable plans (希望案) and feasible plans (可能案/務圖安). Desirable plans are merely hopes and thus have little possibility, while feasible plans are mostly possible.
Then, what are the desirable plans?
We hope for the existence of a policy centered on the Korean people. What is that policy? It involves the protection, promotion, establishment, and freedom of various business organizations for Koreans. For example, protection of commercial institutions, promotion and freedom of establishment of financial institutions, freedom to establish press organizations, freedom to establish<117> educational institutions, restrictions on the invasion of mainland zaibatsu (內地財閥), implementation of a tariff system, reduction of various taxes and public charges, issues regarding the appointment of government officials and public servants, pensions, salary increases, etc., can be mentioned.
Next are the feasible plans. These feasible plans are precisely the solutions to the causes of poverty. However, they may not be entirely feasible.
So, what are the solutions to the causes of poverty? I will explain below.
As a countermeasure for the inadequacy of the general level of the people, farmer guidance and enlightenment (農民指導啓發), that is, a rural revitalization policy (農村振興策), will be most necessary. This is because for those wandering in ignorance and unenlightenment (愚味未開), there is nothing else but the path of light and life.
Next is the propagation of the Gospel (福音宣傳). This propagation of the Gospel is not just one condition for rural revitalization, but in saving many people, it will be the only "Alpha" and "Omega." That is, it will be total.

Next is the realization of the 'One Village, One Church' principle and the great expansion of Sunday Schools (need to train Sunday School workers). We should strive to realize 'One Village, One Church' with a 10-year plan. Quantity is important, but especially from now on, we must pay attention to quality.
Dr. Pieters once said that when he was evangelizing in Korea, it was more desperately needed to enable Koreans to attain salvation in present social life, that is, salvation in practical life, rather than for the salvation of their souls in heaven in the afterlife. Indeed, it is a necessary fact that for the sake of the people, tearful precursors, passionate religious figures, loving workers, and emotional preachers must be produced in large quantities.
There are numerous actual examples of advocating for the necessity of rural enlightenment through Gospel propagation like this, and a good example is the three villages of Ich'ŏn (Ich'ŏn-ni, Ch'uŭlmi-myŏn, Taedong-gun, P'yŏngan Namdo).
One village = Very few believers... Two taverns... Extremely poor, 30-40 households
One village = Half believers... One tavern... Slightly poor, 40-50 households
One village = Almost all believers... No taverns... Somewhat wealthy, 40-50 households
It shows the phenomenon of the proportions mentioned above. We can roughly guess how great the power of the Gospel affecting us is. This call to propagate the Gospel is not the usual empty clamor (갱구아리식), but rather, truth lies in frequent assembly.
Next is the training of leaders (指導者 養成), which of course means guiding the farmers. Improving agriculture, encouraging side jobs, organizing cooperatives, eradicating illiteracy, promoting culture<118>, spiritual guidance, material guidance, scientific guidance—these three guiding tasks of the leaders are truly immense for rural enlightenment and revitalization.
Although these issues of Gospel propagation and leader training may seem slow, unlike the immediate famine problem faced by an individual, in order to save the masses, there is no choice but to proceed by enlightening on one hand and guiding on the other, different in nature from saying that chanting prayers is unnecessary for a person on the verge of starvation ('Give them bread').
Thus, we must pay attention to and strive towards the construction of ideal villages.
Next is the countermeasure for the lack of productive enterprises: we must strive for the promotion of productive enterprises (製産事業振作). This means urban revitalization (都市振興). Are we not just buying and using thousands of things for daily life? Isn't Korea, despite having abundant necessary conditions for production such as raw materials, labor force, power, etc., and special strengths, merely providing these necessary conditions to others, not producing itself, and becoming just a consumer market for others' goods? At this juncture, Koreans must pool small capital and large capital must rise (蹶起) to undertake production. Thus, we must prevent the outflow of money. This is the spirit of self-production and self-sufficiency (自作自給). The way to live lies here.
According to recent reports, necessary factories run by us Koreans include:
In Osaka, there are over 140 factories producing celluloid, ebonite, aluminum, etc.
In Kanghwa, artificial silk was woven in every household, and recently a 500,000 won stock company was established.
In P'yŏngyang, besides rubber factories and sock factories, recently hosiery factories, brick factories, cement factories, glass factories, etc., have emerged.
In Yŏngdŭngp'o, Kyŏngbangjik Company (京紡織會社) is said to manage even wide cloth weaving and spinning.
Looking even briefly like this, we can see how much need there is for productive enterprises for us.
At the same time, it is also true that many productive industries are being taken away by mainland large zaibatsu (內地 大財閥). Ramie cloth (苧布), hemp cloth (麻布), silk fabric (明紬), etc., are also said to be taken over by mainland large zaibatsu.
One thing to mention is that until our factory industry develops, we should turn homes into factories, that is, household handicraft (家庭 手工業). Weaving silk and cotton for household use is necessary, and it wouldn't hurt to make and wear straw shoes (草靴) instead of rubber shoes.<119>
Therefore, citing successful examples of small industry, i.e., household industry, even just looking at P'yŏngyang, the production of tanned leather rubber wheels for Western shoes, sterilized chopsticks, laundry soap, ink, and various crafted accessories is so numerous that it almost forms a monopoly.
Speaking of our domestic consumption of industrial goods within Korea in Showa 8 (1933), it amounts to 565 million won.
Looking at the breakdown of this consumption amount:
Amount produced in Korea: 365 million won
Amount brought in from mainland Japan: 288 million won
Amount imported from foreign countries: 30 million won
Total: 683 million won
Export amount: 35 million won
Amount shipped out: 83 million won
Total deduction: 118 million won
Actual consumption in Korea: 565 million won
According to the table above, the value of industrial goods produced by our hands is merely (roughly) 65 million won, so ultimately 500 million won has become an outflow of our money.
In other words, if we produce it with our own hands, not only would this 500 million won not leave, but isn't it something we should be producing? As mentioned above, aren't the large zaibatsu invading because the necessary conditions for production are equipped in Korea? However, our rice amounts to 8 million sŏk (石), which, even at an average of 25 won per sŏk, is only 200 million won, and even if there is some shipping out of raw materials for other industrial goods, this is not even an issue.
Here, unless we industrialize the cities and rural villages, we absolutely cannot survive.
Ch'unwŏn (春園), in his Principles of Life, also listed three conditions to overcome the poverty and weakness of Koreans:
1. Increase in the productive power of rural villages and the rationalization of their consumption
2. Industrialization of Korea
3. Large-scale participation in the development of resource wealth within Korea
These three conditions were listed. This, too, concerns productive industry, that is, manufacturing.
Adding to this, while commerce should also be done on a large scale, banking and finance are desperately needed, as money lending often ends up in the hands of manufacturers as payment for goods or especially in the hands of bankers and financiers. Therefore, pooling our<120> assets to organize banks is very urgent, but I understand it is a major issue because it requires great wealth.
However, the investment amount of mainland large zaibatsu in Korea is said to reach about 2 billion won, how large is that?
There is no further need to say that there must be many banks, i.e., financial institutions, run by Koreans.
Besides that, development of resource wealth such as electricity, fishing, marine products, forestry, mining, etc., will be necessary.
Next is the countermeasure for waste, luxury, and excess, which is to promote the ideas of saving and frugality (節約과 儉素).
Saving and frugality are things that should naturally be practiced by any individual and any society, but I believe they are desperately needed especially for us Koreans who are poor and have low production capability.
Examples of our waste include:
Excessive spending on banquets and ceremonies (宴禮費過用), proliferation of empty formalities (虛禮熾盛), excessive ceremonial items, too many Western clothes chests, wardrobes, large mirrors, furniture, etc., wasteful spending on entertainment (遊興費濫用), excessive spending on children's items. And as for our extravagance (夥侈), we can point to facts such as excessive makeup for both men and women, purchasing gramophones (蓄音機) in every household, constant debts at restaurants (料理屋), etc.
Of course, the improvement in the standard of living, including housing, clothing, food, and everything else, is undoubtedly fortunate. However, if we follow the preferences of our eyes and ears and the comfort of our minds without considering the level of our possessions and our special circumstances, this is waste and luxury, which means pleasure-seeking (亨樂) and decline (隋落). If even the wealthy and wealthy societies should beware of waste and luxury, how much more so for us who are poor!
Therefore, what I advocate throughout my life is saving and frugality. The reasons are:
Because Koreans are low in production but bold in consumption.
Because they sell off land and houses to buy daily necessities.
Because they spend more than their income.
Economically prudent.
Because from the points of thought, morality, and character, waste and luxury are the acts of the unconscious.
In short, it seems to be a fact that modern Koreans lack the consciousness of life, the desire for life, think only of living day by day, and seem unable to think of the life of myriad future generations.
Generally, the recent extreme waste and luxury, and the particularly severe pleasure-seeking and decline,<121> are perhaps phenomena where, on one hand, the chaotic atmosphere (零園氣) of the environment and the disordered public morals (紊亂한 風紀) of society confuse the general public,
and on the other hand, due to the trend of so-called inflation leading to a boom (好景氣), waste and luxury are becoming prevalent in Korea due to speculative businesses in gold mining, land, rice and beans, and stocks.
However, looking at the overall situation in Korea, regarding whether the so-called boom truly gives us a good influence, I think it conversely has a bad influence, giving Koreans the bad habits of waste and luxury.
To stop such waste and luxury and to promote the ideas of saving and frugality and realize them, I believe the following few things are necessary.
First, those in leadership positions (teachers, pastors, social institution personnel, youth guides, etc.) must take the lead and practice daringly.
Second, the 300,000 Christians must unite and practice under control.
Third, socially, various organizations must unite and control this to be carried out daringly.
Fourth, large-scale construction of ideal villages everywhere to practice saving and frugality, etc.
Next, as a countermeasure for the unlimited outflow of money, it would be resolved by the hope mentioned above, the policy centered on the Korean people. Regarding this, I believe it is absolutely impossible without the spirit of self-production and self-sufficiency through the promotion of Korean products (朝鮮物産獎勵).
Next, as a countermeasure for natural population increase, although calling it a countermeasure might be misleading, since we are not in a position to practice birth control just because we are poor, we need to feel the necessity to control population growth through industrial promotion (殖産興業) and simultaneously pioneer and emigrate to places like that "Mirudong" (미루동).
("Mirudong" is the border area of Sin'gye, Koksan, Changyŏn, etc., counties)
With the above, though incomplete, I believe poor Korea can be rescued.
In short,
The general masses need to thoroughly possess the consciousness of survival, and centralized consolidation around an idealistic great figure is also necessary. Among these, the great propagation of Christianity and the thorough awakening of the 300,000 believers are considered the most urgent priorities. (Reporter's Notes)<122>

What Leaders Should Do

Lecture by Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng
1. At Tonggi-ri Church (Seventh-day Adventist)
1. Night of January 30, Showa 11 (1936) (From 7:30 PM to 8:30 PM)
1. Hosted by the Volunteer Youth Missionary Society

I have a cold, and having used my vocal cords repeatedly, they've broken down. The doctor told me to speak softly, and even at home, not to use my vocal cords but indicate with writing. However, since this was requested long ago and I promised this evening's time, I came, but it's a great regret that my throat hurts and I cannot speak as I wish.
I just came to say hello. I'll postpone what I prepared a bit at home for the next opportunity. Since many young people have come here, I'll just make a few requests of the youth.
My nature is peculiar; I don't fear what others fear, and I fear what others don't fear. People often fear power (權勢), but using power improperly and abusing it, I want to confront it wherever it may be. Money! It's good if you use money well, but using it for bad things or just hoarding it tightly, a miser (守錢奴)! That is, why bow your head to a petty thief who guards money?
I just get scared and don't know what to do when I see young people or boys like those sitting in front here. When I meet them, somehow a fearful thought arises, and I scrutinize myself all over. Am I wearing my Western suit properly, is my necktie tied straight? I'm afraid they might emulate my mistakes, and I can't bear the fear. Really, more than anything in this world, I fear the youth the most. And when I meet them, somehow I feel happy<123> and reassured. Being at the newspaper company, 40 or 50 visitors come each day. Among them, I am most glad when young people come. When they come, they all talk energetically, sometimes we arm wrestle, and after that, my mind feels refreshed. When I see them doing good things, I want to run up and hug them, but when they act annoyingly, I wish I could slap them on the cheek.
Having been abroad for a long time, I returned to Korea 5 or 6 years ago. When I encounter our youth, I feel happy, but on the other hand, I always feel regretful. That is, the youth of Korea seem somewhat lacking in vigor and energy, looking sloppy. Seeing them lack the drive to move forward, lack self-respect, have weak characters, engage only in following trends (*縱), and become pessimistic, tears of blood come from my eyes. Your ancestors were never inferior to others in brain or body. In the past, the Chinese called Korea Tongi (東夷), which wasn't the character for barbarian, but because they were people with big bows (大弓), they called them I (夷). (夷 is a combination of 大 and 弓) Our grandfathers had great strength and courage, carried big bows, and shot and defeated the northern barbarians. They were also very intelligent, and many figures with great strategic wisdom emerged. Seeing world-class athletes constantly emerging in the sports world lately, I can tell that the good blood of our ancestors still flows in your veins. Thinking of you makes me feel sorry, but you should remember the words of Alexander of Greece. Didn't he lament, "If my father conquers the whole world, what is there left for me to conquer?" Look. If your society were in favorable circumstances (順境), would there be work to do? Isn't it because you are in difficult circumstances (逆境) that you have much work to do? Therefore, you should consider having much work to do as happiness.
Last year, I heard that students were roaming around bars and cafes at night. Wondering if it was true, and since a friend paid the expenses, I raided all the cafes in the city—well, it was astounding! Most were young people, all drunk, with bloodshot eyes—literally, alcohol flowers (酒花) bloomed in their eyes. With the youth who bear the rise and fall of a society in this state, what will become of our society's future? In one place, it was around three or four in the morning, students wearing square caps (四角帽) were sitting around drunk and making a scene. Since the smell of alcohol and cigarettes gives me a headache, I ordered milk and drank it, watching those fellows' antics. Then one guy<124> said, "Hey old man, if you came to drink, just drink. Why are you staring at people?" I couldn't just watch that. I struck him on the shoulder blade hard enough to break it, and he crumpled. Grabbing him tightly by the collar and pulling him up, "Student! What is this behavior? Wasting money sent by your parents through hard work on drinking..." "Ah! Isn't it Mr. Yŏ?" "Well, whoever I am, don't do this kind of thing next time." Then the student just plopped down and started sobbing. "Do you think we enjoy doing this? There are truly unspeakable, unjust circumstances, that's why." "If there are such unjust circumstances, come and discuss it with me tomorrow after you sober up," I said, and indeed, he came the next day. Listening to his story, it really seemed understandable why he would drink and vent his anger. Haha.
Once, a friend* couple who had returned from America suggested going boating on the Han River (漢江). We bought several watermelons and got on a boat. Over there, Japanese mainland kids were rowing their boats among themselves, ignoring us. But on our side, dozens of Korean kids stood in a line, yelling "Hey, nice! That woman is quite pretty," and catcalling (히야가시)! A few guys among them wearing square caps rowed their boat towards ours. So I quickly ducked my head under the boat and hid. When they got close to our boat, they said, "Let's ride the boat together." At that moment, I suddenly stood up. These guys went "Ah! Mr. Yŏ..." not knowing what to do, all trying to back away. "You rascals, how did you come here, and why are you leaving now... Look at those Japanese mainland kids over there... Aren't you ashamed?" "We were wrong." "Alright, since you came anyway, come here and eat some watermelon before you go." "No, sir. It's fine," they said, trying to leave. "No. You can't leave until you eat at least one slice of watermelon." So I made them eat a slice of watermelon each before sending them off. Another time, passing through an alley in Susong-dong (壽松洞), a bunch of kids surrounded a female student (elementary school) and were catcalling her. I couldn't just pass by. I knocked off this one's hat, knocked off that one's hat, put them in my pocket, and gave each a slap. They said they were wrong, so I gave them their hats back and sent them away. When I see such things, I don't know how upset I get.
Young people sitting here, don't you know that if you do well, Korean society will shine, and if you do wrong, Korean society will be dark? I don't want to just blame you. Many elders are present here too, but to guide the youth, the predecessors must first do well themselves. If they themselves do wrong, no matter how much they tell the youth only with words to do well, do you think they will listen well?<125> Hypocrites (僞善者) who wear sheep's clothing but act like wolves... There is nothing as detestable as those guys who talk and write well but do all sorts of nasty things in the back alleys. A leader (指導者) is not just someone who tells others what to do with words or writing, but someone who personally takes the lead, cuts through the path ahead whether it's painful or difficult—that's a true leader. Thinking about this, even I who am speaking here feel ashamed myself (스사로 붓그러워요). If you ask if there are true leaders in Korea today, I think there are none. Thinking about this, I don't know how pitiful the future path of the youth sitting here is. Once, I went to a meeting (會席) in Kyŏngsŏng (경성) where several people were discussing the issue of youth guidance. Everyone was just speaking ill of the youth. As I remained silent, someone must have asked, "Why? Mr. Yŏ, why are you silent without saying a word?" "My perspective is different from yours, so I'm not speaking." "If your perspective is different, speak about the difference." "I need to find fault with you first. You find fault with the youth, but you should do well first. Therefore, unless we predecessors do well first, it's simply impossible." Someone walking over snow, thinking of the person coming behind, couldn't walk carelessly, so they carefully placed their steps, it is said. The person coming behind always steps in the footprints of the person who went ahead. It is unreasonable for a teacher to do wrong while telling students to do well. At a farewell party for a school's academic affairs head, the students were contributing 2 won 50 chŏn (錢) each for the fee. When I said the fee was too much, they said even that wasn't enough if they were to drink alcohol and call several kisaeng (기생). Look, if things are like this in the educational world, how regrettable is that?
Also, recently, without distinction between Seoul and the countryside, I don't know how many trivial popular songs (流行歌) are spreading in the streets. Do you know what kind of influence a single line of song, a single color, has on people? Don't you know that a single line of a dirty song, a single color, can break the spirit (氣槪) of the youth? These wicked popular songs must definitely be prevented. Probably before long, the wicked sounds from radios or gramophones on the streets will be banned. Looking at the situation in Korea today, one cannot help but think about youth guidance. It has become very easy for youth to fall into confusion and wander. Then what should be done? You must become your own leaders. What does this mean? It means that you shouldn't blindly follow those called predecessors, but leaders must emerge from among yourselves and carve out the path ahead; that's the current situation (現今의 情勢).<126> Going one step further, leaders of the student world must come from students, leaders of factories must come from factories, and leaders of rural villages must be farmers. Think about it. How can someone who just wears Western clothes and writes possibly experience the real life of a farmer? Without actual experience, how can one become a true leader? The main point of my talk this evening is also here. Since there are no true leaders in Korea today, youth must judge for themselves and forge their path ahead.
And so, quite some time has passed. My request is to the youth in this place! I hope you will advance vigorously and energetically. That is all. <127>

**Summary**

The article presents two distinct pieces from 1936: Cho Man-sik's analysis of Korean poverty and Yŏ Un-hyŏng's lecture on youth leadership. Cho attributes poverty to factors like exploitative lending, lack of Korean-owned industries, extravagance, and population growth, proposing solutions like Korean-centric policies (desirable but unlikely) and feasible actions like rural development via Christian outreach, industrialization (starting with household crafts), promoting frugality, and organized emigration. Yŏ criticizes the perceived apathy and moral laxity among Korean youth, contrasting it with ancestral vigor, while also condemning the hypocrisy of current leaders. He urges youth to find strength in adversity, reject harmful influences like drinking and popular songs, and importantly, to become their own leaders, emphasizing that true leadership must arise from direct experience within the group being led (e.g., farmers leading farmers).

From a historical perspective, these texts offer valuable insights into Korean nationalist thought and social concerns under Japanese colonial rule. Cho's piece highlights the economic marginalization felt by Koreans, the desire for economic self-sufficiency (物産獎勵 - promotion of Korean products), the perceived negative impact of Japanese capital (內地財閥), and the role of religion (Christianity) in social mobilization and moral reform. Yŏ's lecture reflects anxieties about the younger generation's character and direction, the perceived inadequacy of the existing elite, and a call for grassroots, authentic leadership development, revealing internal critiques and aspirations within colonial Korean society. Historians might find the specific economic data cited by Cho, the emphasis on moral/spiritual solutions alongside economic ones, and Yŏ's candid critique of both youth and established figures particularly interesting.

熱辯, 熱辯, 熱辯
生産과 消費와 우리 覺悟

曺晩植氏演說
本問題는 生活難에 잇는 사람을-어떠한 方策으로 救濟할 수 잇느냐 함임니다.
본문제는 매우 중요하며 緊切한 同時에 또한 興味잇는 問題인 줄로 암니다. 그러나 퍽 難題인 것도 한 사실일<116> 것 임니다.
問題가 貧窮한 것을 表明하야 此를 前提로 하엿스매 그 貧窮한 証據를 말할 必要는 없을 것임니다.
그러나 일부러 此를 强要한다면 以下의 몇 가지를 들 수가 잇슴니다.
첫재 生活 困難임니다. 이것은 무엇보다도 우리들이 日常 實際로 보고 當하는 事實인 것이며
둘재 負債生活인데 이는 商工業者의 負債말고도 農民負債만이 사만원 내지 오억원이나 된다는 것이며
셋재로 所有漸乏의 現象인데 土地家屋의 自然騰貴로 인한 그 面積 數量의 大縮되는 것 등입니다.
우선 우리가 생각할 問題는 즉 그에 대한 대책인데 이 대책을 생각할 때는 其 貧窮의 원인을 考察할 필요를 놋끼게 됨니다. 맛치 病을 치료할여며는 먼저 그 병의 근원을 알어야 하는 거와 같이-
그러면 貧窮의 원인은 과연 무엇인가를 알어보기로 합시다.
첫재로 金錢貸出의 무한성을 들 수 잇슴니다. 이는 우리의 생활이 特殊 事情인 만큼 金錢을 無限貸出케 되는 관게로 해서 적어도 여긔에는 朝鮮사람 本位의 政策이 잇서야 할 것이며
그 다음으로는 一般民度의 未及인데 民度의 低劣이라 할가 정도의 不及이라 할넌지 이것은 우리들 각자가 늘 깊이 힘써야 할 문제인 줄 암니다.
그 다음에는 製産事業의 不作인데 이는 財産家의 蹶起와 小資本의 결합을 要하는 문제이며
또 濫用, 奢侈, 過度의 風인데 우리가 늘 부르짓는 바 節約, 儉素의 관념의 결핍으로의 현상일 것이며
그 다음에는 人口自然增加의 傾向인데 年 30만의 증가율을 나타내는 현상으로서 産兒制限도 불가할 것임니다.
이상에서 보아온 바의 원인에 대한 대책에는 希望案과 可能案(務圖安)의 두 가지가 잇는데 希望案은 다만 희망함에 불과하므로 가능성이 거이 없고 可能案은 거이 가능한 바임니다.
그러면 希望案은 무엇무엇인가 하면,
朝鮮人 本位의 정책이 잇서지기를 바라는데 그러면 그 정책이란 무엇인가 하면 朝鮮人의 各種 事業機關의 保護, 助長, 設置, 自由 등인데 예컨댄 商業機關의 보호, 金融機關의 助長 及 設置自由, 言論機關 設置自由 敎育機關<117> 設立自由, 內地財閥侵入制限, 關稅制施行, 各種稅金及公課金輕減, 官公吏登用及恩給, 加俸等改正問題等을 들 수가 잇슬 것임니다.
다음에는 可能案인데, 이 可能案은 즉 빈궁한 원인에 대한 해결책임니다. 그러나 全然 가능한 것은 안일 것임니다.
그러면 貧窮한 원인의 해결책은 어떠한가? 함을 이하에 설명하겟슴니다.
一般民度未及의 대책으로서는 農民指導啓發 즉 農村振興策이 가장 필요할 것임니다. 그것은 愚味未開하야 헤매이는 자에게 光明과 生命의 道外에는 다시 없슴으로 써이외다.
다음에는 福音宣傳인데 此福音宣傳은 農村振興의 一條件만이 아니라 여러사람을 구함에 오직 「알파」요 「오메가」일 것임니다. 즉 全的일 것임니다.

다음에는 一洞一敎會主義 實現과 主日學校 大擴張(主日學校 事役者 養成 必要) 인데 10년 계획으로 一洞一敎會 實現을 꾀할 것이며 數量도 數量이려니와 특히 從此以後로는 質에 유의할 것임니다.
일즉이 삐드워드 博士는 말하되 내가 朝鮮에서 傳道할 새 朝鮮人은 來世靈魂의 天堂救援을 위함 보다 現在의 사회적 구원 즉 實際生活에서 救援을 得케하려 함이 더욱 절실히 필요하다는 말을 하엿슴니다. 과연 백성을 위하야 淚의 前導者, 熱의 宗敎家, 愛의 事役者, 情의 說敎家가 大量 産出되여야 함이 필요한 사실임니다.
이러틋 福音宣傳으로 農村啓發의 필요를 부르짓게 된 實例는 數多한 것으로서 그 좋은 例로는 利川의 三洞(平南 大同郡 秋乙美面 利川理)이 그것임니다.
一洞=信者極少……酒家有二…極貧3,40戶
一洞=信者半數……酒家有一…少貧4,50戶
一洞=信者擧盡……酒家無…稍富4,50戶
우에서 말한 比例의 현상을 나타내고 잇는 것임니다. 얼마나 福音이 우리에게 미치는 바 그 힘의 큼을 가히 짐작할 것임니다. 이 福音宣傳하자는 말은 보통으로 갱구아리식의 것이 아니라 오즉 眞理는 자주 會集함에 잇는 것임니다.
그 다음에는 指導者 養成인데 물론 농민을 지도함일 것임니다. 農事改良, 副業獎勵, 組合組織, 文盲退治, 文化<118>促進등 精神的 指導와 事物的 指導, 科學的 指導, 이 세가지의 指導者的 任務가 農村啓發振興에는 실로 막대한 것임니다.
此等 福音宣傳과 指導者養成問題는 事 緩晩한 듯 하나 一個人의 당장에 당하는 바 飢饉 問題와는 不同하야 多數民衆을 구함에는 우선 『빵을 주어라』餓死之境의 人에게 念佛이 불필요하다함과 其趣가 달너서 一便으로 啓發식히며 一便으로 指導하야 진행할 수 박게 없슬것임니다.
그리하야 理想村 建設에 留意邁進할 것이외다.
그 다음에는 製産事業不作의 대책인데 製産事業振作을 꾀하여야 할것임니다. 즉 都市振興을 의미하는 것이 겟슴니다. 우리는 일상생활의 千種萬物을 買用할 뿐이 아님니까? 朝鮮은 製産에 관한 필요조건 즉 原料, 勞働力, 動力 等의 豊富와 特長을 가지고도 此等 필요조건을 타인에 제공만 하고 製産은 아니하면서 타인상품의 소비시장으로 化해버리고만 형편이 아님니까. 此際에 朝鮮人은 小資本은 뭉치고 大資本은 蹶起하야 製産에 착수해야 할 것임니다. 그리하야 金錢의 유출을 방지할 것임니다. 自作自給의 정신이 곳 이것이요. 活路가 여긔에 잇다할 것임니다.
近者의 보도로 알아보면 우리 朝鮮人의 경영하는 필요한 工場으로는
大阪엔 세루로이드, 에보나이트, 알미니움 등 製産工場 140餘 處가 잇다하며
江華엔 人造絹을 家家戶戶에서 織造하더니 近者에는 50萬圓株式會社가 생기엇다고 하며
平壤엔 고무工場, 洋襪工場은 물론, 近者 메리야스 工場, 煉瓦工場, 세멘트 工場, 硝子工場등이 생기엿고
永登浦엔 京紡織會社에서 廣木織造, 紡績까지 경영한다고 함니다.
이러틋이 대강만 살펴보드라도 우리에게 製産事業으로서 과연 얼마나 필요함이 만흔가를 알것이외다.
동시에 內地 大財閥에게 앗기우는 製産業도 역시 많은 것이 사실임니다. 苧布, 麻布, 明紬등도 內地 大財閥이 하게 된다고 함니다.
한 가지 말할 것은 우리의 工場工業이 발달되기 까지는 家庭 手工業을 즉 家庭을 工場化하도록 하야야 하겟다는 것임니다. 家庭用으로 明紬 木綿 등 織造가 필요하고 고무靴 대신에 草靴를 맨들어 심음도 無妨할 것임니다.<119>
그러함으로 小工業 즉 家庭工業으로의 成功例證을 擧한다면 平壤에서만 볼지라도 洋靴用 製皮고무輪, 消毒箸, 洗濯石鹼, 잉크, 各種 細工的 附屬品製造가 甚多하야 거지반 獨占의 형태를 이루고 잇는 형편임니다.
昭和8년도 朝鮮內 우리의 공업품 년 소비고를 말한다면 5억 6천 5백만원이 됨니다.
이 消費額의 內譯을 살펴보면
朝鮮에서의 生産額  365百萬圓
日本內地에서 移入額  288百萬圓
外國에서의 輸入額  30百萬圓
計  683百萬圓
輸出額  35百萬圓
移出額  83百萬圓
計  118百萬圓減分
實565萬圓也 朝鮮서 消費高
右表에 의하면 우리의 손으로 製産한 工業物의 價額은 僅히 (으수리)6천 5백만원에 불과할 것이니 결국 5억원은 우리 金錢의 流出이 되고 말엇슴니다.
다시 말하면 우리의 손으로 제작하면 이 5억원은 나가지 안흘 뿐더러 우리가 제작해야 될 바가 안임니가? 이상에서도 말한 바와 같이 製産의 필요조건이 朝鮮에는 具備하엿기 때문에 大財閥이 侵入하는 것이 아님니까? 그런데 우리의 米는 8百萬石으로 평균 1石 25圓치고도 2억원에 불과한 것이며 기타 공업품의 원료도 多少移出이 잇다 하지마는 이것은 문제도 안되는 것임니다.
여긔에서 우리들은 도시와 농촌을 공업화 하기 전에는 도저히 못 살것임니다.
春園도 生의 原理中에서 朝鮮人의 貧弱을 克服하는 원리중에
1. 農村의 生産力의 增加와 그 消費의 合理化
2. 朝鮮의 工業化
3. 朝鮮內의 富源開發에의 大量的 參與
이상 세가지 조건을 들엇슴니다. 이것 역시, 製産業 즉 공업에 대한 말임니다.
여긔에 添附해서 말해두는 바는 商業도 大規模로 하려니와 銀行業, 金融業이 절실히 필요한데 金錢의 貸出은 工業品으로 商品代로 또는 특히 銀行, 金融業者들의 손에 만히 들어가는 것임니다. 그러하매 우리의<120> 財産을 한데 뭉처서 銀行을 組織함이 매우 긴급하지마는 大財를 要하기 때문에 大問題인줄로 암니다.
그런데 內地 大財閥의 朝鮮에의 投資額은 약 20억원에 달한다 하니 얼마나 큰 바임니까?
朝鮮人의 經營인 銀行, 즉 金融機關이 많어야 할 것은 다시 더 말할 필요가 없음니다.
그 밖에도 電氣業, 漁業, 水産業, 林業 鑛業등의 富源開發이 필요할 것임니다.
다음에는 濫用, 奢侈, 過度의 對策인데 이는 節約과 儉素의 思想을 鼓吹할 것임니다.
節約과 儉素는 어떤 개인과 어떤 사회를 물론하고도 당연히 행할바이나 특히 貧窮하고 또   製産의 低能한 우리 朝鮮人에게는 절실히 필요한 일인줄 암니다.
우리의 濫用하는 것을 例擧한다면
宴禮費過用, 虛禮熾盛등의 禮裝物夥多와 洋服장, 衣장, 大體鏡, 家具 등 太多와 遊興費濫用, 兒童用物過費을 들 수가 잇스며 또 우리의 夥侈로서는 남녀간의 化粧하는 것 등이며 家家에 蓄音機購入등이며 料理屋에 恒時滯負등의 사실을 指摘할 수 잇는 바임니다.
물론 家屋, 衣服, 飮食, 其他 모든 생활정도의 향상은 물론 當幸의 事임에는 틀님 없으나 그러나 우리의 소유재산의 정도와 우리의 특수사정을 고려치 아니하고 耳目의 所好와 心志의 安樂을 딸아간다면 此는 濫用이요 奢侈이니 이는 즉 亨樂과 隋落일 것임니다. 富饒한 자도 富饒한 사회도 濫用과 奢侈는 경계할 바 이어든 又況 貧窮한 우리라!
그럼으로 余는 一生을 통하야 主唱하는 바는 節約과 儉素 그것임니다. 그 이유로는
朝鮮人은 生産은 低能하면서 消費에는 大膽함으로써 이고
土地, 家屋을 放賣하야 日常用品을 買用함으써 이고
수입에 초과되는 지출을 함으로써 이고
經濟的으로 삼가고
思想, 道義, 人格의 諸点으로 보아 濫費와 奢侈는 無意識者의 所爲임으로 써이외다.
要컨댄 現代 朝鮮人은 生의 意識, 生의 慾望이 缺如하며 그날 그날의 生만 생각하고 子孫萬代의 生은 생각치 못하는 듯함이 事實인가 함니다.
대개 近者에 濫用, 奢侈가 極甚하고 亨樂, 隋落이<121> 尤甚함은 環境의 混亂한 零園氣와 사회의 紊亂한 風紀가 一般人으로 迷惑케하는 一方,
또 한편으로는 所爲 인푸레이슌의 경향으로 好景氣하는 관계로 因하야 朝鮮에는 金鑛, 土地, 米豆, 株式의 投機事業으로 因하야서 濫用과 奢侈가 盛行되여 가는 현상인가 함니다.
그런데 朝鮮大勢로 보아서 물론 好景氣라 하는 것이 과연 우리에게 好影響을 주는 것이냐 함에 잇서 吾人은 反히 朝鮮人에게 濫用과 奢侈의 惡習慣을 주는 惡影響이라고 思惟하는 바임니다.
이러한 濫用과 奢侈를 放止하고 節約과 儉素의 사상을 鼓吹하야 此를 실현케 함에는 이하 몇 가지가 필요할 줄로 암니다.
첫재, 指導者級 (敎員, 牧師, 社會機關人, 靑年引導者등)이 率先하야 實踐敢行할 것
둘재, 30만 基督敎徒가 團合實行하도록 統制할 것
셋재, 사회적으로 각 團體가 聯合하야 此를 敢行토록 統制할 것
넷재, 各處에 理想村建設을 대규모로 施行하야 節約, 儉素를 實踐케할 일, 등일 것임니다.
그 다음에 金錢無限流出의 대책으로서는 우에서 말한 바 朝鮮人 本位의 政策이라는 희망이면 해결될 바임니다. 此에 대하야는 특히 朝鮮物産獎勵의 自作自給의 정신이 없이는 도저히 불가능할 쭐로 믿슴니다.
그 다음 人口自然增加의 對策으로서는 對策이란 것도 語弊가 잇슬 것이나 貧窮하다고 해서 産兒制限도 할 形便도 아니매 우리는 殖産興業함으로 인구증식을 조절해 나아가는 동시에 彼 「미루동」같은 곳을 開拓하면서 移住할 필요를 느끼게 됨니다.
(「미루동」은 新溪, 谷山, 長淵, 等郡의 境界地域임니다)
以上으로서 不完全하나마 貧窮 朝鮮을 救濟할 쭐로 믿슴니다.
