Article Title: 바람은 불어도 (Though the Wind Blows)
Author: 金岸曜
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Though the Wind Blows
						Kim Anyo

On the path where the soft spring wind whirls,
The snow melts and sprouts are green in the field.

Where the soft spring wind crosses over,
Flowers bloom on branches, grass grows on the mountain.

Though the soft spring wind turns through the heart,
Why is this heart only desolate?<114>

**Summary**

This short poem contrasts the renewal of nature brought by the spring wind—melting snow, green sprouts, blooming flowers, and growing grass—with the persistent desolation in the speaker's heart. Despite the gentle beauty of spring surrounding them, the speaker questions why their own spirit remains untouched and sorrowful. For historians, this poem offers a glimpse into personal sentiment during the colonial era. The juxtaposition of external natural beauty and internal sadness might reflect a broader sense of unease, displacement, or suppressed melancholy prevalent among Koreans during that time, even amidst seemingly peaceful moments like the arrival of spring.

바람은 불어도
						金岸曜
보드라운 봄바람 휘도는 길에
눈은녹고 들판에 속닙 프르다.
