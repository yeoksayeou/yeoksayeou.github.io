Film Adaptation of Female Literary Master Ouida's Work, Pitiful Friend

In the countryside not far from the city of Andŭwap'ŭ (안드와프市) in Paegŭi (白耳義 - Belgium), an old man named E-han (에-한老人) lived together with his twelve-year-old grandson, a boy named Nero (네로).

While living in poverty, the boy Nero constantly thought of the famous painter Rubens (루-벤스) from P'uranda-sŭ (푸란다-스 - Flanders), who painted without a moment's rest.

Nero became very close friends with Maria (마리아), the daughter of the nearby wealthy man Ko-gujŭ (고-구즈). Maria secretly hoped for Nero to succeed as a painter.

One year, Nero decided to submit his work to the Rubens (루-밴즈) Prize Painting Competition. He thought that if he managed to win a prize this time, he could take the prize money and enroll in the art school in Puratssel (부랏쎌 - Brussels).

One day, while returning from pulling a cart in the street, Nero and his grandfather found a Shepherd-breed (세빠-트種) dog by the roadside that had been severely abused and was near death. They rescued it and brought it home. The dog, recovering thanks to Nero's devoted care, came to be called Reo (레오). It became Nero's close friend and was good at running errands. When Nero's grandfather lay sick in bed, it pulled the cart together with Nero.

Maria's father, Ko-gujŭ, had decided on P'it'a- (피-타-), the son of the wealthy Pa-ndarŭt'ŭ (바-ㄴ다-ㄹ트), as his daughter's future son-in-law, arranged for Maria to socialize with him, and forbade Maria's association with Nero.

Later, one night, Ko-gujŭ held a ball with P'it'a- as the guest of honor. That night, Nero's grandfather's illness worsened, and tragically, he passed away.

Unable to find the money for the funeral expenses, Nero ended up selling the painting he intended to submit to the competition to P'it'a-. P'it'a- submitted it under his own name and won first prize. However, through Maria's testimony, the truth was clearly revealed, and a day of bright happiness came to them.<252>

**Summary**

The article summarizes the plot of "A Dog of Flanders" (though attributing it to Ouida, it uses different names like Nero, Reo, Maria, and E-han, and drastically changes the ending). It recounts the story of a poor boy, Nero, aspiring to be a painter like Rubens, his friendship with a girl named Maria, his rescue of a dog named Reo, his grandfather's death, and his thwarted attempt to win an art competition. However, unlike the original tragic story, this version ends happily with the antagonist P'it'a-'s deception being exposed, leading to a "bright happiness" for Nero and Maria.

From a colonial Korean history perspective, this article is interesting as it shows the introduction and adaptation of Western literature and potentially film into Korea during the 1930s. The altered, happy ending is particularly noteworthy; it might reflect a desire for optimistic narratives during a difficult period, or perhaps a deliberate choice by the writer or the film adaptation source to make the story more palatable or hopeful for the contemporary audience. The themes of poverty, artistic struggle against odds, and class conflict would likely have resonated with Koreans under colonial rule.

女流文豪 위더女史 作의 映畵化, 불상한 동무
白耳義 안드와프市에서 그리 멀지안은 시골에 「에-한老人」은 열두살 나는 손자 「네로」라는 소년과 둘이서 살고 잇섯다.
가난한 살님을 하면서도 「네로」 소년은 잠시도 쉬지 안코 그림을 그린 「푸란다-스」 출신의 名畵家「루-벤스」를 늘 마음 속에 그리고 잇섯다.
「네로」는 근방에 사는 富豪 「고-구즈」의 딸 「마리아」와 퍽도 갓가운 사이가 되엿다. 「마리아」는 「네로」가 화가로서 출세하는 것을 속으로 은근히 바라고 잇섯다.
어느 해 「네로」는 「루-밴즈」 賞繪畵競技會에 출품하기로 되엿다. 이번에 긔어히 입선만 되면 그 장려금을 타가지고 「부랏쎌」에 잇는 미술학교에 입학되리라 생각하엿다.
어느날 「네로」는 거리에서 마차을 끄을고 돌아오다가 길가에서 「세빠-트」種의 개 한마리가 몹시 虐待를 밧고 거이 죽을 지경에 이른 것을 자긔의 하라버지와 갓치, 구해가지고 집으로 대려 왓다. 「네로」의 극진한 간호로써 회복된 개는 「레오」라는 일홈으로 부르게 되엿다. 「네로」의 친한 벗이 되어 심부름도 잘 하엿다. 「네로」의 할아부지가 병상에 누웟슬 때에는 「네로」와 갓치 마차를 끄으럿다.
「마리아」의 아부지 「고-구즈」는 딸의 장래 사위깜 될 사람으로 富豪 「바-ㄴ다-ㄹ트」의 아들 「피-타-」를 정해 놋코 「마리아」와 교제식히고 「마리아」와 「네로」와의 교제를 금하엿다.
그 뒤 어떤 날 밤 「고-구즈」는 「피-타-」를 主賓으로 하는 무도회를 열엇다. 그날 밤 「네로」祖父는 병이 되져, 애처롭게도 그만 세상을 떠나버렷다.
「네로」는 장사(葬事)의 비용을 구하다 못해 마츰내 競技會에 출품하랴든 그림을 마츰내 「피-타-」에게 팔아버리고 말엇다. 「피-타-」는 그것을 자긔의 일홈으로 출품하여 일등상을 어덧다. 그러나 「마리아」의 증언으로 사실이 료하게 드러나서 그들에게는 명로운 행복의 날이 차저왓다.<252>
