Seeking the Contemporary Recluse (1), Visiting Mr. Hong Myŏnghŭi, a Contemporary Recluse Content in Honorable Poverty

While murmuring "歸去來兮 歸去來兮 田園將蕪胡不歸..." ["Returning Home! Returning Home! The fields and gardens are growing wild, why not return?..."], thinking busily of the "utopia" realm of Tao Yuanming (陶淵明), whose name is rare and precious throughout the ages, who, after one year in office, cast everything aside like worn-out shoes, returned to his village, built the "Oryuch'on" (五柳村), and dedicated his life to leisurely clouds and wild cranes, befriending morning farming and evening reading—I went to visit Mr. Hong Myŏnghŭi (洪明憙), who lives the life of a recluse "living in the dust, yet apart from the dust" (居塵離塵), content in honorable poverty (淸貧樂道). The time I took those light steps was a warm early spring day when the yang energy was circulating, just after "Usu" [Rain Water, 2nd solar term].

Seeing this elder, like the saying of the ancients, "The great man hides in the city" (大人은 隱於市), choosing not the quiet nature of the countryside but rather to "hide in the city" (隱於市), I thought he must certainly be a "great man" (大人), and I nodded my head <64> a couple of times to myself and smiled faintly.

Why has this elder, who until some time ago carried an extremely heavy burden on the cultural front of Peninsula (半島) society, rushing east and west amidst the complex waves of the world, recently come to live the life of a recluse (處士), merely observing trivial matters while remaining in the marketplace? While having such curious thoughts, I sought out the house where he is said to always be, in Tongmangnae-ri (東幕下里).

Seated serenely amidst folding screens adorned with calligraphy and paintings, the elder's gleaming broad forehead seemed to speak first of him being a venerable elder who has meticulously experienced the numerous worldly affairs of his 50 years of life.

On one wall hung two pieces of calligraphy by O Sech'ang (吳世昌), and on the desk were piled dark, old books (古書). Then again, next to them, one could see Balzac's complete works lined up neatly.

I felt completely captured by an atmosphere as if I had entered the study of some famous scholar.
I decided to ask this elder sequentially about his views on life, art, reading, and his recent state of mind.

Reading and Hobbies

Reporter: Sir, what kind of books have you been reading frequently recently?
Hong: I don't specialize in research or anything like that, I just read whatever comes to hand... so there aren't really any books worth mentioning specifically. Although that set of Balzac's complete works is over there, I read all of it some time ago. Nowadays, both in the East and West, everyone seems to regard him as the pinnacle of bourgeois writers, and there seems to be a huge fuss in Russia too, talking about "re-evaluating classic literature" (古典文學의 再吟味) or whatnot... Besides, for the past year, due to illness (身病), I wandered here and there, and also because I'm writing a so-called novel for the newspaper, I have hardly had any time for reading at all.

Reporter: When will "Im Kkŏkchŏngjŏn" (林巨正傳) be finished?
Hong: It should be finished within the next year.

Reporter: After that novel is finished, will you continue writing novels?
Hong: As things stand now, there isn't really anything else for me to do, so naturally, I'll likely lean in that direction. If I start writing novels now, I intend to stop writing historical novels and try my hand at creative works depicting reality <65>, like today's young writers do. And if I were to read, I'd like to read works by foreigners in their original language as much as possible. You can never really grasp the true flavor through translation (譯). So, even now, I have a mind to study a language like German, French, or Russian. Some might laugh, asking what language study can be done at the age of 50, but compared to some Western literary figure who, in order to study Greek art, began learning Greek only after turning 60 and achieved great success, I still have plenty of time. It's just that in the East, especially in our Chosŏn (朝鮮), there's a lot of premature aging (早老症).

Reporter: Apart from your daily reading or writing, do you have any hobbies during your leisure time?
Hong: I have no hobbies at all. Since I live somewhat away from the city center like this, close friends (親友) I used to associate with frequently in the past occasionally visit, and then I pass the time chatting with them. Otherwise, I sometimes go out to the banks of the Map'o River (麻浦江) for a walk or to fish.

Reporter: Do you enjoy fishing?
Hong: Not really. I just do it sometimes when I'm too bored. Speaking of fishing, this happened right after the Manchurian Incident (OO사변). At that time, I was in Seoul, but since there wasn't much for me to do if I just stayed in Seoul, I went down to my hometown Kwangju (廣州) for a while. Being bored, I spent time fishing by the riverside. Then, a friend I used to associate with in Seoul wrote me a letter, writing "Living apart from the dust, separated from the dust" (去塵離塵), making it seem like I had become a hermit... ha ha... That makes me remember! Heh heh...

Recent State of Mind, View on Life

Reporter: Sir, you were once the principal of Osan High Common School (五山高普校長), you were also the president during the Sidae Ilbo (時代日報) days, and not long ago you were the Vice Chairman of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會). Indeed, everyone knew your position in our society. But recently, you have truly become someone who "hides in the city" (隱於市), completely staying indoors (杜門不出). So, people are saying that you are a "recluse" (隱士), a "lay scholar" (處士), spending idle days content in honorable poverty (淸貧樂道). What do you think?
Hong: Heh heh..... How could I possibly be content in honorable poverty <66> or become a "lay scholar" (處士) or "recluse" (隱士)! I am someone who constantly frowns with worry over all worldly affairs (世事), spending gloomy days amidst poverty and all sorts of discontent! It's just that in the past, the overall social situation, the objective circumstances, made me step into such positions, and today, the objective circumstances have also made me like this! No matter what anyone says, a person is bound to be governed by their surrounding environment... Moreover, a person like me is undoubtedly, in today's slang (時體말), petite bourgeoisie (小뿌루주아), intelligentsia (인테리켄챠), so it suffices to say that worldly affairs have made me this way. However, just because I distance myself from worldly affairs doesn't mean I am ignorant of the world situation or forget the circumstances of our society, intending to live the life of a true "recluse" (隱士). Just last summer, due to illness (身病), I went to a quiet Zen monastery (禪院) in Kŭmgang Mountain (金剛山) for several months. The monks (僧) there kept telling me I should forget the mundane affairs of the secular world and become a monk (僧) forever to practice Zen meditation, but I firmly refused. Is "recluse" (隱士) the right word? "Lay scholar" (處士) is even less so.

Reporter: Do you ever miss those days when you worked vigorously?
Hong: Heh heh.... How could I never miss them! But now there are absolutely no opportunities to gather together in one place like that, so I can hardly ever meet the friends (友人) I used to associate with frequently! People say someone like Hŏ Hŏn (許憲) went somewhere in Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道) to run a mine (鑛山), someone like Cho Pyŏngok (趙炳玉) went far away to do some kind of business, and so-and-so went somewhere to do something... Truly, the people I used to hold hands with in the past have all scattered to go their own ways. If I don't stay indoors (杜門不出), then who would I go out to meet, and where? Worldly affairs (世事) just unfold as they will! Goethe (꾀-테) once said something like, "There is no one as foolish as one who can be found on any pedestal (*떠한 석상)." Since I have nowhere to go out to and just sit quietly in my room all day long, I probably won't get scolded by someone like Goethe! Heh heh.... Right now, my only task is probably to rummage through old and new books and take up the brush to express my inner thoughts! Even so, if someday <67> a friend or some organization comes and asks me to step out, holding my wrist, I might rise again like in the past..... If this society calls, I won't hesitate! But, as was true for me in the past, I might participate in something like a cultural organization, but what can I do if my disposition, my strength, is lacking for other fields! Well, one can only follow worldly affairs as they unfold. It's also true that my energy is genuinely diminishing now...

Within the gentle and scholarly words of this elder, the benevolent atmosphere of having guided countless young disciples in the past still seemed to flow silently.
Thinking I might be wasting too much of the precious time of this elder, who is said to keep manuscript paper and a brush before him even if he doesn't take up the brush, I stood up from my seat. Stepping outside the gate, the sun had already tilted to the west, and the early spring evening breeze blowing from the banks of the Map'o River (麻浦江) was still quite chilly. Mentally praying for this elder's health and for better news in his future, I moved my hurried footsteps away.<68>

**Summary**

The article is an interview with Hong Myŏnghŭi, a prominent Korean intellectual and writer, conducted in early spring 1936. The interviewer visits Hong at his home in Tongmangnae-ri, Seoul, finding him living a quiet life, seemingly withdrawn from public affairs, which leads to descriptions of him as a modern "recluse." Hong discusses his current reading (Balzac), the ongoing serialization of his historical novel "Im Kkŏkchŏngjŏn," and his desire to write contemporary fiction and learn European languages. He dismisses the labels "recluse" or "lay scholar," attributing his withdrawal not to personal choice but to "objective circumstances" and the scattering of his former colleagues like Hŏ Hŏn and Cho Pyŏngok. He expresses a sense of resignation but states he would re-engage if society called, particularly in cultural activities, while acknowledging his declining energy.

For historians, the article offers a valuable snapshot of a major intellectual figure navigating the constraints of the colonial period. Hong's explicit mention of "objective circumstances" forcing his withdrawal hints at the political pressures limiting public activity for nationalists and socialists after the dissolution of groups like the Sin'ganhoe. His self-identification as "petite bourgeoisie, intelligentsia" reflects contemporary social analysis, and his comments on reading Balzac and wanting to learn European languages reveal the ongoing engagement with global literature and ideas despite his seclusion. His candid remarks about poverty, discontent, and the dispersion of his activist cohort provide insight into the atmosphere of disillusionment and adaptation among Korean intellectuals in the mid-1930s.

기사형태	소식

當代處士 차저(1), 淸貧樂道하는 當代處士 洪明憙씨를 찾어
『歸去來兮 歸去來兮 田園將蕪胡不歸...』를 부르짖으면서 在官 一年에 모든 것을 廢履같이 집어던지고 향촌에 돌아와 『五柳村』을 건설하고 閑雲野鶴에 朝耕暮讀을 벗삼아 일생을 바치던 萬古에 그 이름 稀貴한 陶淵明의 「유토피아」境을 부지럽시 머리ㅅ속에 그리면서, 나는 「居塵離塵」의 隱士生活로 淸貧樂道하시는 洪明憙氏를 찾어 가벼운 발자국을 옴겨 놋흔 때는 바로 「雨水」도 지나 양기가 떠도는 따스한 일흔 봄의 어느 날이었다.
『大人은 隱於市』라는 옛사람의 말과 같이 이어룬 역시 한적한 전원의 자연 속을 擇하지 안흐시고 「隱於市」하심을 볼 때, 또한 「大人」임에는 틀림이 없으려니 생각하면서 나는 제절로 고개를 <64> 두어번 끄덕이며 미소를 빙그레 띄어 보았다.
한 동안 까지는 半島社會의 文化線 우에서 지극히 무거운 짐을 지시고 東奔西走 多端한 世波 속에서 지내시던 이 어룬이 웨 이 近者에서는 이다지도 市井에 있으면서 細事를 다만 바라만 보시는 處士의 생활을 하시는지? 이런 굼굼한 생각도 一便 가지면서, 東幕下里로 늘 게시다는 宅을 찾젓섯다.
書畵屛風 쭉두룬 속애 은은하게 정좌하신 이 어룬의 윤기도는 넓은 이마에는 50평생의 許多한 世事를 세세히 경륜하신 거룩하신 어룬임을 먼저 말하고 있는 듯하다.
한편 벽에 吳世昌氏의 글씨가 두쪽으로 걸려저 있고 책상 우에는 거머죽죽한 古書들이 가뜩이 샇여저 있다. 그런가 하면 또 그 옆에는 「빨작크」의 소설전집이 나란히 노여저 있음을 볼 수 있었다.
나는 어떤 유명한 학자의 서재실 속에 들어 선듯한 그러한 분위기 가운데에 사로 잡히고 말었다.
곳 이 어룬한테서 그의 인생관, 예술관, 독서, 최근의 심경 등을 차레로 묻기로 하였다.

讀書와 趣味

記-선생은 최근 어떤 종류의 서적을 만히 보십니까?
洪-무슨 전문적으로 연구한다거나 하는 것이 없고 거저 손에 쥐이는 대로 읽게 되니까...  별로히 이렇다고 할 만한 책이라고는 없어요. 저게 「빨작크」전집이 있지요마는 얼마전    에 그것은 다-읽어 보았지요. 지금은 동서양을 물론하고 모두들 부루조아 작가의 최고봉   으로 처서 고전문학의 再吟味니 뭐니...해서 로시아에서도 굉장히 들떠드는 모양입데다      마는-더구나 작년 1년 동안은 身病으로 해서 여기 저기로 돌아단였고 또 신문에 소설이    라고 쓰는 관계로 도무지 독서할 틈이라고는 별로 없어요.
記-「林巨正傳」은 언제쯤이면 끝이 납니까?
洪- 今後 1년 이내로는 끝이 나겠지요.
記-그 소설이 끝나시면 계속해서 소설을 쓰시겠읍니까?
洪-현재 보는 限에 있어서는 별로히 다른 일도 없고 하니 자연히 그 방면으로나 기우러지게 되겠지요. 나는 이제 소설을 쓰기 시작한다면 인제 역사소설을 그만두고 지금 젊은 작  가들처럼 현실을<65>그려내는 창작을 해  보려고 하지요. 그리고 독서를 한다면 외국 사람의 작품은 되도록 원서로 읽어보고 싶어요. 譯으로서는 도저히 그 참 맛을 모르게 되니까요 그래서 이제부터라도 獨語나 佛語나 露語나 한가지쯤 어학 공부를 하고 싶은 생각이 있어요. 50이 되는 나이에 무슨 어학공부가 되겠느냐고 혹 어떤 사람은 웃을른지도 모르지만, 서양 어떤 문학가는 希臘예술을 연구하기 위하야 60이 지나서 비로서 希臘語를 배우기 시작해서 크게 성공한 사람에 비하면 나도 아직은 넉넉하지요. 東洋, 더*나 우리 朝鮮에는 早老症이 많어서 그러하지마는.
記-선생이 매일 看書나 집필하시는 이외의 여가에는 아무런 취미도 없으십니까?
洪-취미라는 것은 통 없지요. 이렇게 시외에 좀 떠러저서 내가 있으니가 그 전날에 자주  사괴이던 親友들이 간혹 찾어오면 담화로나 소일하고 그렇지 않으면 麻浦江ㅅ가로 나가서 산보나 고기잡이나 더러 하게 되지요.
