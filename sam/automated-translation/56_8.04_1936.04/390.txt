Chaliapin Impression
						Tōkyō Chang Nosŏng

On February 6th at the Tōkyō Hibiya Kōkaidō (日比谷公會堂), I was fortunate enough to attend the third solo recital of Chaliapin, a visitor from afar.

That day, it was snowing heavily in Tōkyō, said to be the first such snowfall in 49 years. I boarded a streetcar (市電) to go to the venue, but it stopped midway, so I walked the rest of the way, braving the snowstorm.

Of course, one cannot grasp his entirety solely through Chaliapin's voice on stage (스테지). For someone like me, lacking musical talent (소질), perhaps it's more accurate to say it's impossible to grasp even a side (側面) of him. The low tone (低音) emanating from his colossal frame, standing 6 feet 3 inches tall, was particularly impressive to me.

Finally, there were songs like *Sŏngno* (船路), *Ch'ŏnyŏ e chugŏm* (처녀에 죽엄), *Mosŭk'o e norae* (莫斯科에 노래), *Chebiriya Ribalssa* (제비리야理髮師), *P'elssiya Yŏn'ga* (뻴시야戀歌), and *Wŏlga e paennorae* (월가에 밴노래). Among them, *P'elssiya Yŏn'ga* (뻴시야戀款) (by Rubinstein) - last spring when Rubinstein came here, I had heard his piano solo.

I do not intend to recount Chaliapin's personal history (覆歷) here. However, the mere fact that he spent his unfortunate (不偶한) youth together with Gorky (콜키), the great master (巨匠) of the Russian literary world (露文壇) today, suggests his past was not exactly ordinary (평범하였다고).

It is said he was once a Volga (월가) boatman (배사공)<238> and also a singer in a poor (빈약한) regional touring troupe (지방순회극단). Was becoming a singer in a regional touring troupe fortunate for Chaliapin? Or can we say that Feodor Chaliapin, who became a world-class (세계적) singer today, is happy? That, I cannot know.

On the stage where the fixed (不動) gaze (시선) of several thousand audience members (聽衆) was concentrated (집중한), Chaliapin, singing the *Wŏlga e paennorae* (월가에 배노래), absolutely did not look like a 65-year-old man (노인). When that low tone (低音) flowed out, nothing but the sound of the deepening night could be heard; not even the sound of breathing (숨소리) was audible (들리지 않었다).

Some years ago (年前), I encountered Chaliapin on the projection screen (映寫幕), but today's impression was new (새로웟다). (February)<239><238-239>

**Summary**

The author, Chang Nosŏng writing from Tokyo, recounts attending a Feodor Chaliapin concert at the Hibiya Public Hall during a major snowstorm in February 1936. Despite professing a lack of musical expertise, the author was deeply impressed by Chaliapin's powerful bass voice and commanding stage presence, particularly during his performance of the "Song of the Volga Boatmen." The author briefly mentions Chaliapin's humble past, including his association with Maxim Gorky, contrasting it with his current world fame. Historically, this article is interesting as it documents a Korean intellectual's experience engaging with international high culture (a famous Russian singer) within the heart of the Japanese empire (Tokyo) during the colonial period. It reflects the complex cultural interactions and the presence and activities of Koreans in Japan at that time.

시야리핀 印象
						東京 張路星
2월 6일 東京 日比谷公會堂에서 나는 遠來의 客인 시야리핀의 제3회 독창회를 다행히 들을 수 있었다.
그날은 49년만에 처음이라고 하는 힌눈이 동경에 나리었었고 會場으로 가려고 市電을 탔었으나 그것이 중도에서 불통이 되어 나는 눈보라를 맞으며 그곳까지 갔었다.
물론 스테지에 선 사야리핀에 음성만으로는 그에 全貌를 알 수는 없을 것이다.
음악에 소질이 없는 나로서는 더구나 그에 側面이라도 알기 불가능하다고 함이 正當하지나 않을른지. 6척 3촌이나 되는 그에 巨體로부터 發聲되는 低音 그것은 특히 나에게는 인상적이었다.
최후에 船路, 처녀에 죽엄, 莫斯科에 노래, 제비리야理髮師, 뻴시야戀歌, 월가에 밴노래 등이 있었다.
그 중에 뻴시야戀款(루빈슈타인曲) 去年 봄에 루빈슈타인이 이곳에 왔을 때 나는 그에 피아노獨奏를 들었었다.
나는 여기에서 시야리핀에 覆歷을 말하고저 하지는 안는다. 그러나 오날 露文壇에 巨匠인 콜키와 不偶한 청년시대를 같이 하였다는 것만하여도 그에 과거가 그다지 평범하였다고 말할 수는 없다.
그는 월가에 배사공을 한 때가 있었다하며<238> 빈약한 지방순회극단에 가수가 된 때도 있었다고 한다.
지방순회극단에 가수가 되었었던 것이 시야리핀에게 행복하였을까. 혹은 오늘에 세계적 가수가 된 프에오돌 시야리핀이 행복하다고 할 수 있을른지 그것은 나로서는 알 수 없다.
數千 聽衆 不動에 시선이 집중한 스테지에서 월가에 배노래를 부르는 시야리핀이 65세에 노인으로는 도저히 보이지 않았다.
그 低音-이 흘러나올 때 깊어가는 밤 소리 외에는 숨소리도 들리지 않었다.
年前 나는 映寫幕에서 시야리핀을 대한 때도 있었으나 오날에 인상은 새로웟다.(2월)<239><238-239>
