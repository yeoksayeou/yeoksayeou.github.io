Soliloquy / Inner Thoughts
						P'ain

Kwach'ŏn's Kwanak Mountain,
Has its breath run out?
No matter how I look,
I see no flame.

Unable even to spew flames,
This burning inside,
Burning, yet pretending not to burn, this aching inside,
Would you understand?<115>

**Summary**

This short poem by P'ain uses the image of Kwanak Mountain, supposedly no longer showing flames, as a metaphor for the speaker's suppressed inner turmoil. The speaker feels an intense, hidden pain ("burning inside," "aching inside") that they conceal ("pretending not to burn") and despairingly questions if anyone ("you") could comprehend their suffering. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem can be interpreted as an expression of stifled national sentiment or personal anguish under Japanese rule. The dormant mountain might symbolize the suppressed spirit of Korea, and the speaker's hidden pain reflects the inability to openly express the suffering caused by colonial oppression.

과천에 관악산은

아모리 바라봐도
불길이 안보이데

타면서 안타는체, 쓰린이속을
