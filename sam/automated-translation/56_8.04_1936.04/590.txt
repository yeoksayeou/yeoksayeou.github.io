The Sorrow of the Black Man (7)
Stowe (Original Author), Yi Kwangsu (Abridged Translator)

20

However, Tom firmly believed that the spirit of his beloved master St. Clare (크렐) had entered God's (하나님) kingdom and would joyfully meet Eva (에바), so he did not grieve excessively. But one day, it occurred to him: the body of St. Clare, who had granted his freedom, was already buried in the earth; who now would grant him freedom? Thus, <380>
“Oh God. Let everything be done according to Your will.”
He could only wait for God’s disposition.

Once the shield named St. Clare passed away, the merciless Marie’s (마리부인) harsh curses and beatings planed the slaves’ flesh like a wood plane and pierced their hearts like an awl. Yet, there was nowhere else to turn for refuge, so they could only await the day of their death, like running on thin ice.
When Rosa (로자) talked back to the mistress over a trivial matter and was taken away to a house that made its living by beating slaves, Mrs. Ophelia (오베리아부인), unable to bear watching the pitiful sight, tried everything to persuade Marie, but the stubborn mistress would not listen, and the wretched Rosa was dragged away to have her blood drawn by cruel, painful blows.

Two or three days later, Tom heard that Marie, after consulting with St. Clare’s brother and a lawyer, was selling the house and all the slaves to return to her parents' home. For a moment, he felt breathless, but then he thought that his only hope now was Mrs. Ophelia. He decided he must tell her about this and seek her help. He immediately went to her room and found her busy preparing for her departure.
“Ma’am. What should I do about this? The master wrote this certificate saying he would free me. Please speak well for me, Ma’am, and save me. Perhaps if she knows the master’s last wish like this, she might listen.”
“Yes, I’ll try speaking to her. I’ll do my best, but since it’s Mistress Marie (마리마님), I don’t know if she’ll listen or not.”
She went to Marie’s room and, after relating Tom’s story, said,
“Think about it carefully and let him go.”
“Oh, sister. Don’t even say such things to me. He’s the one who will fetch the most money; you want me to let him go?”
“But isn’t it pitiful for Tom? He longs so much for freedom.”
“Don’t say such things. What good is freedom to them? It’s enough just to beat them hard and work them; I am strongly opposed to freeing slaves.”
“That may be what you think, but how much must he want to gain freedom? He has worked well until today. And if he meets a bad master, where could be more pitiful than that?”
“Oh, why say that? I grew up in the South (남부) too, but Southern people are all good. If they do wrong, they beat them, of course, but where can they go where they won’t be beaten?”
“Even so, St. Clare also spoke of it before he died, and Eva also said to free Tom. Shouldn’t you <381> consider that a little?”
At this, Marie, seemingly extremely upset, squeezed tears from her handkerchief and said,
“Oh, is there anyone as ill-fated and pitiful as I am? My only daughter dies, then my husband dies too. Is there anyone who feels sorry for me? No matter what I say, nobody listens. Why do even you, sister, torment this pitiful person so unbearably?”
And shouting, “Oh, my head hurts! Oh, my chest hurts!” while calling for Mammy to spread the bedding and bring water, and on the other hand calling for the doctor, saying she was losing consciousness, amidst the commotion, Mrs. Ophelia too could do nothing but withdraw. As a last resort, she wrote a letter to Mrs. Shelby (셀비부인), asking her to buy Tom back.

However, this too was in vain. Before this letter could even reach Mrs. Shelby, the very next day Tom’s body was already in the hands of a slave trader. Together with five or six other slaves, he spent the night at the slave auction site. The light of freedom that had briefly appeared before Tom’s eyes vanished back into dark clouds, and the devil of hell, from which he had been distant for a while, once again tightly gripped the gentle Tom’s neck and pressed down. At the auction the next day, Tom, along with a lovely quadroon girl (퇴기 게집) of about 15, a girl named Lucy (루시), and two male slaves, was sold to a man named Legree (레그리). This man was a fiend capable of scraping the flesh off a living person. Could this be called a human, or a beast? Perhaps a ferocious beast wearing human skin? Compared to him, Hare would indeed be called a great man and a gentleman.

As Legree rummaged through Tom’s belongings, he saw the good clothes Tom used to wear at the Shelby house and, pointing at Tom's clothes, said,
“Filthy! What is all that you’re wearing? Here, change quickly. Take that off and leave it.”
Tom changed his clothes as Legree ordered and took off the clothes he had been wearing. This time, Legree searched Tom’s body and found a Bible and a hymnal.
“Crazy fool! What is all this? Ha… so you believe in Jesus (예수), do you? Hmmph… ha… ridiculous!”
“Yes, I believe in Jesus. Whoever…”
“Hey, you scoundrel! None of your insolent talk! From now on, I’ll make sure you can’t believe in Jesus or Besus! On my place, worship, hymns, prayer, things like that are strictly forbidden. You scoundrel, you’d better listen well to my words. Just let me see you mention Jesus again. You’ll be dead on the spot.”
Taking Tom’s bag, he went out onto the deck.
“Son of a whore! How presumptuous, carrying a bag!”
He took out all the items inside and sold them, then put his own things into the bag. Hearing this and seeing this, Tom muttered to himself, <382>
“No matter what command he gives, can he take away my faith? Even if he carves and sells my flesh, can he sell my soul?”
And he offered a prayer to God in his heart.

After snatching Tom’s bag, Legree went over to the lovely Emmeline (에메리). This Emmeline was a quadroon (퇴기), with a pretty face, able to read, and had even studied the Bible.
“Hey, let me take a look.”
He placed his hand on her shoulder, but Emmeline moved away, her face blushing.
“Hmph. An unwilling wench, aren’t you? Acting all demure. When I say something, you should at least try to smile.”
In his anger, he raised his fist.
“You bitch! Take a look at what this is!”
He struck the shoulder of Tom, who was standing beside him, as if to break it.
“With this much force, wenches like you would be beaten dead in one go… Hahahaha!”

Getting off the boat onto the shore, the two young women were loaded onto a cart, which Legree rode along with them. Tom and the other male slaves followed behind the carriage (마챠), heading step by step along the endless field path towards the dreadful cotton fields of Simon (시몬).
After going some distance, Legree turned around with a smile.
“Hey Emmeline, come over here.”
He took Emmeline’s hand, which rested on her lap.
“It’s not far to the house now.”
However, Emmeline showed no sign of pleasure; rather, her face changed as if frightened, and she pulled her hand away, leaning towards the slave seated beside her. Anger flickered across Legree’s face, but then he smiled again, touching Emmeline’s reddish ear.
“Have you ever worn earrings?”
“No,” she replied, trembling.
“Hmm, then I’ll buy you some. Hmph, you’re quite pretty. You’ll live with me now.”
Around this time, the carriage reached the gate of Legree’s property.

Legree’s estate (가대) was initially very fine, but because he overworked the slaves excessively, none of them tended to it diligently. Weeds grew thick in the fields, and the buildings were dilapidated.
As the carriage reached the gate of Legree’s house, three or four fearsome-looking dogs that had been lying in the yard (다당) ran out barking to meet them. Two slaves dressed in rags (누덕이) and looking fierce tried to restrain them with strange noises. Legree turned to the newly bought slaves, <383>
“You scoundrels, where do you think you can run off to? With these dogs here. Even if you run away, these dogs will follow and find you instantly, you scoundrels.”
The slaves in rags were Sambo (산보) and Quimbo (*보니); they were the overseers of the slaves working in the cotton fields, and scoundrels worthy of being loaded onto a single cart.
Legree said to Sambo,
“Sambo, I bought a new wife (새악씨) for you. Here, take her.”
He pushed the old woman towards Sambo, saying,
“Here, she’s yours.”
The old woman staggered and said,
“Master (서방님), what are you saying? I have a husband back in New Orleans (늬유를네안).”
“What’s that? You won’t listen to my words?”
“I will obey any other command, but how can I have two husbands? Please…”
“You wench! What insolent talk is this? You won’t listen to me?”
He raised his horsewhip and struck her with all his might. The old woman (로파) lost consciousness and collapsed onto the ground. However, Legree pretended not to see this and went over to Emmeline with a smiling face.
“Come, let’s go together, hmm, missy (아씨야).”
Emmeline was dragged away by Legree, while Tom followed Sambo into the quarters (우리). It seemed the place hadn’t been swept once since it was built; in one corner were rags rotting in sweat, in another, dried and stuck snot (코라). Just looking at it made one want to vomit, but his fate was tied to this place, so he had no choice. He first cleared away some of the mess, took the Bible from his bosom, placed it on a shelf (선반), and then was led by Sambo to the work site.

21

That day, after finishing work and returning for dinner, the only food was a few half-cooked potatoes (감자) with the dirt barely removed. In a decent house, even animals wouldn’t be fed such things, but still, valuing his life, he ate them heartily. Then, overcome by exhaustion, he fell asleep. In a state between waking and sleeping (사몽비몽간에), he found himself sitting on a rock by the shore of Lake Pontchartrain (폰다톤 호수까), and beside him, lovely Eva was reading the Bible (성경).
“Whether in water or fire, I will be with you. You are my Lord, Jehovah of Israel (이스라엘), the one who saves me.”
Gradually, this voice grew fainter, then from somewhere unknown, utterly clear music was heard. Eva looked up to heaven (하날) and offered a peaceful prayer. After a while, shining wings sprouted from Eva’s body, and she flew flap, flap, up high into the heavens, ascending towards the holy throne of God.<384>
Tom felt overwhelmed with joy, but suddenly awoke to find it was a dream.
Perhaps it was the spirit of Eva, who had left the kingdom of heaven and come down to earth to comfort the lonely and sorrowful heart of Tom.

Legree too, seeing Tom’s diligence and faithfulness in all matters, gradually considered giving him a position like an overseer. However, Tom’s only flaw was that he was too kind, treating everyone with friendliness and knowing neither how to curse nor how to beat them. For this reason, Legree tried every trick (수란 수) and all his might (힘이란 힘) to make Tom like himself, but our Tom did not emulate his master’s teachings in the slightest, only firmly believing in God’s will.

One day, Tom was picking cotton in the fields (밧헤서) with other slaves. Beside him was a woman he hadn’t seen before, appearing to be about 40. Due to terrible hunger and excessive labor (로*), her flesh was all wasted away, her face lacked even a drop (한땀도) of blood color (피긔운), and furthermore, she seemed afflicted with some deep illness, looking as if she might collapse at any moment. The young male slaves picking cotton nearby jeered,
“That wench has finally come.”
“Ugh, look at that wretched state (꼴악산이).”
“What can she possibly pick?”
“Tonight, she’ll surely get beaten till she’s half dead.”
They hurled all sorts of curses at her, but the dying woman (大여가는 그) acted as if she didn’t hear, neither trying to protest nor complain, only occasionally whispering prayers in a faint voice. Tom felt even more pity for her and took cotton from his own basket, putting it into the woman’s basket. The woman said,
“Oh, please don’t. What will you do if the overseer sees…”
Sure enough, Sambo, who had seen from afar, came running with his whip (혁편) tucked under his arm.
“You sons of bitches (개색기들)! What are you doing?”
He kicked the old woman (로파) hard enough to break her, shoving her aside, and struck Tom across the face with the whip. Tom, being sturdy, stood back quietly despite his bleeding face. But the old woman, originally frail, staggered and was about to collapse (걱굴어지러늘). Sambo took a needle from his pocket.
“You wench, I’ll fix you, just lie still!”
He rushed forward and jabbed her hard in the head. The unconscious old woman screamed in pain and tried to get up (닐이나거늘).
“You wench, get up quickly (냉큼 닐어나서) and work!”
At these words, she endured the pain and started picking cotton again.
“You wench, mark my words (알아 차려라). Tonight, you’re dead!” said Sambo.
“Please, I beg you, kill me now.”
She looked up to heaven.
“Oh God, why do you not save me?” she prayed.

**Summary**

This excerpt, part seven of Yi Kwangsu's 1936 abridged translation of Stowe's "Uncle Tom's Cabin," depicts Tom's despair after St. Clare's death. Despite promises of freedom, St. Clare's widow, Marie, refuses to honor them and sells Tom at auction. He is bought by the brutal Simon Legree, who immediately tries to crush Tom's spirit and Christian faith, confiscating his Bible and threatening him. Tom witnesses Legree's cruelty towards other slaves, including the young Emmeline and an elderly, sick woman mercilessly beaten by the overseer Sambo. The chapter ends with Tom clinging to his faith amidst the horrors of Legree's plantation, witnessing the depths of suffering and questioning God's inaction.

From a historical perspective, Yi Kwangsu's translation during the Japanese colonial period is significant. Publishing a story about the suffering of slaves and their yearning for freedom, centered on Christian faith as a source of resilience, could subtly resonate with the Korean situation under Japanese rule, offering parallels between American slavery and colonial oppression. The focus on themes of injustice, brutality, endurance, and hope through faith might have provided Koreans with a narrative framework to understand their own hardships, making it an interesting piece for historians studying colonial-era literature, censorship (note the asterisk suggesting potential self-censorship of 'labor'), and the role of translated works in shaping consciousness.

20

그러나 톰은 그 사랑하는 주인 크렐의 령온이 하나님 나 에들어가 에바와 반가이 맛날 줄을 굿게 밋는 고로 그리 슬퍼하지 아니하더니 하로는 생각하야 본즉 제 자유를 맛흔 크렐의 몸이 이믜 따 속에 무첫스니 이제 누라서 나에게 자유를 주리오 하야, 다만<380>
『하나님이시여. 모든 것을 하나님의 뜻대로 하서지이다.』
하고 하나님의 처분만 기다릴 뿐이더라.
크렐이라는 방패가 세상을 떠나자 무졍한 마리부인의 혹독한 욕과 매가 대패모양으로 종들의 살을 깍고 송긋 모양으로 종들의 마음을 찌르나 다시는 돌아가 의지할 바가 업스매 살 얼음 우에 달음질하는 모양으로 죽을 날만 기달리 뿐이라.
