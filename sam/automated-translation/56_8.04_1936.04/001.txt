**Summary**

This section consists of captioned photographs from the April 1936 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine. The images cover: Korean figures in Paris from a past era; scenes related to geopolitical tensions in East Asia including Shanghai, the First Sino-Japanese War in P'yŏngyang, German troops in Beijing, and contemporary Chinese student protests in Nanking, Peiping, Wuhan, and T'ienchin; and prominent Korean women, dancer Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi and activist Pak In-dŏk, traveling internationally. For historians, the piece is interesting as it juxtaposes memories of Korean exile political activity (Paris group including Kim Kyu-sik), contemporary regional instability (China), and the international activities and prominence of Korean cultural figures (Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi) and social activists (Pak In-dŏk) during the colonial period, reflecting complex interactions within the Japanese empire and beyond.
