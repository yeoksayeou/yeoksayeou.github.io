Homeland Flight Also an Empty Dream! The Tragic Secret Love Story of the Ill-fated Aviatrix Miss Pak Kyŏngwŏn
The story goes back three years from now, to August 7th, Shōwa 8 (1933).
As a mere frail woman, moreover in a rigid family and an uncomprehending society with no one looking after her, truly after a hard-fought struggle, Miss Pak Kyŏngwŏn, marking 10 full years since entering the Japanese female aviation world, was set to embark on the grand journey of the Korea-Manchuria flight she had long pictured in her head and cherished in her heart.
However!
Just as Miss Pak was about to grasp the laurel wreath of fortune and rush towards the gate of victory, why was the mischief of "fate" so<280> pitiful? So transient!
Overcoming all hardships and bitterness, Miss Pak's dream, bravely stepping forth before that gate to fulfill a 10-year cherished desire, was pitifully shattered forever.
Boldly casting her frail body and spirit into the blue sky, the strength she dedicated to our desolate female aviation world was great, and Miss Pak, who gained no joy from it, has now passed away, the years fleeting, already three years! The sorrowful sentiment is still fresh.
Her life, which was too arduous!
Her life, which was too short!
No matter how cruel the whirlwind of fate, could it pluck a flower bud yet to fully bloom so it cannot blossom!
Time flows transiently, counting three years by the calendar, yet the traces of the departed Miss Pak feel ever newer!
Now, searching through the diary of Miss Pak from her lifetime, and that of student S, an ordinary aviation student at the time with whom Miss Pak secretly and quietly forged a pure love unknown to the world, I transcribe anew the sad story of the secret love Miss Pak carried with her forever, offering it before her spirit.

Shōwa 8 (1933), August Xth.
The day the civilian funeral of the late Miss Pak Kyŏngwŏn was held at XX Hall in Shiba, Tōkyō.
An extremely minor and trivial incident occurred, but no one paid particular attention to it and let it pass.
At that time, unexpectedly, there was no one who knew about it.
Why was that? Because it was too small an event, not even worth discussing—.
—Amidst the reading aloud of eulogies from representatives of various fields, a student in university uniform rushed in and made a request to have his eulogy read.
At that time, the person in charge of reading the eulogies flatly rejected the request, saying that eulogies not registered in advance were not allowed.
However, the university student implored the person again and again, but ultimately could not achieve his goal, and the university student forlornly turned and withdrew from his place.

One year after such a very small incident occurred, it was a day in the summer of the following year, Shōwa 9 (1934).
From a room in a certain boarding house near T Airfield, where Miss Pak had lodged for a long, long time while she was in Tōkyō, and from<281> a corner of a hangar at XX Aviation School, two bundles of diaries appeared.
Who would have thought these two diaries contained the sad tale of the tragic love that the departed Miss Pak had cherished in her heart during her lifetime!
One was Miss Pak's, and the other belonged to S-gun (the student who requested his eulogy be read on the day of the civilian funeral), a member of the OO Aviation League.
Holding these two diaries in hand and supplementing and compiling them with my knowledge and imagination, I believe it will unexpectedly solve the mystery of that civilian funeral day.
So, what was S-gun trying to say that day? What was the relationship between Miss Pak and S-gun?
I think it suffices to say that these two diaries speak of the untold miserable training and the anguish and sorrow secretly cherished by Miss Pak, the No. 1 female aviator of this land, unknown to the people of the world.

A, Miss Pak's Diary

Month X, Day X
Today, M-ko's solo flight celebration party was held at the hangar, and I ate quite well.
It was a spirited celebration.
—Do you have the resolve to keep flying from now on?
—Yes, of course, but I'm satisfied just to have gained one more bit of accomplishment (敎養).
M-ko's thoughts were also like this. Returning alone and lonely to my boarding house room, a letter came from H-ko saying,
"I am marrying T. Although I quit learning to fly midway, it was rather beneficial for me in various ways."
Another female pilot vanished from the airfield. H-ko, who spoke of such firm resolve, also got married! It was sad that M-ko, who started flying so spiritedly, also couldn't think of flying as anything more than just one accomplishment (素養) for a modern woman.
However! Among the three sponsors surrounding M-ko, I always think, even just one would be nice.

Month X, Day X
If I write down today's amount,
Avro...... 120 hours 23 minutes
Anrio...... 64 hours 25 minutes
Salmson...... 48 hours 52 minutes
Total...... 233 hours 40 minutes
This becomes the total. Out of this, excluding the first 100 hours studied by my own effort (自力), the remaining 133 hours were flown with the help (助力) of sponsors.
Thinking that this flight time is merely the compensation (代償) for Kyŏngwŏn's<282> eight years of youth from 24 to 32... now that I have become a fully-fledged pilot capable of undertaking the longed-for homeland visit flight (訪鄕飛行), not a single person remains who could become a sponsor!
(The cost of flight practice, ah, is thought to be expensive!)

Month X, Day X
From the sponsorship association in X Castle (X城),
"Come home once, some amount can be procured (調達)."
I received a letter that seemed like a casual remark.
Tomorrow, I depart Tōkyō.

B, S-gun's Diary

Month X, Day X
Made an emergency landing (不時着) while flying to cheer for my alma mater's baseball game.
When I reached the edge of the deep green (深綠) Tamagawa, the engine (發動機) suddenly stopped.
From the front seat, the instructor looked back with a pale face. As the ground rushed towards us (肉迫) at a ferocious speed—
Thump—.
Just that, after that I knew nothing.
Sometime later, I realized my body was laid (뉘여져) in a room of a large farmhouse nearby.
There was a pretty (에여쁜) girl, and I heard that in the 8th inning, with 2 outs and 2 runners (走者), X School's batter got a good hit (好打), and we lost 7 to 6.
My head was only slightly injured (다첬을 뿐), and soon after, I returned to the training camp (合宿) by car.
When K-ko (Kyŏngwŏn) returns from Chosŏn (朝鮮), let's boast about the emergency landing experience!

Month X, Day X
Received mail from G.
Losing narrowly (惜敗) in the final match of the season against X School was a great lesson in many ways.
Being subjected to sarcastic remarks about the failure by Mrs. P—a fan, the wife of a senior (先輩) from the same hometown—who has looked after me for a long time, is something I cannot forget for my entire life.
I suddenly feel a somehow fierce anger (憤怒) towards sponsors.

In the evening, after finishing practice, I was ridiculed (비웃음) again at the hangar. They said I couldn't even get up after falling down (너머저서)?
It must be because I became friendly with the pretty Y-ko after the emergency landing!
Y-ko!...Y-ko!
Whatever the case, it's not that I'm not happy.

C, Miss Pak's Diary

Month X, Day X
Returned after two months.
The sponsorship association raised OO Won,<283> but OO Won is still insufficient. For the airplane, I want a Puss Moth if not an R3 Type (R3型), but it's absolutely impossible!

Month X, Day X
The Salmson finally came into my hands. I repaired it cleanly and named it "Bluebird" (靑鳥號).
The money needed for gasoline costs (까소링代), travel expenses (旅費), etc., to Shinkyō (新京) is now less than OO Won, and as I was thinking blankly (멍-하니) with no solution,
—Come to my house, there might be good news—
I was tempted (꾀임) by M-ko. Wanting to talk with the cheerful M-ko, I followed her. Seeing her luxurious (豪奢스러운) life and looking back (도라다 보니) at my own splendid (화려하었던) youth, I felt a bit lonely.
—Shall I introduce you to a good sponsor?
—Who?
—If you're interested, how about it? Mr. F, I mean.
I was surprised to hear the name Mr. F.
—With him, I think he'd cover the fuel costs (燃料代) for the Chosŏn flight. Come on, how about it?
M-ko kindly urged me several times, but I didn't give an immediate answer (卽答) and just returned.
I thought about it many times! It was because I was thinking about S.
M-ko, who knows well that the plan I've built up to this point is floundering due to a small amount of funds, means well (好意) by offering to introduce Mr. F, but for me, who has known S, such courage (勇氣) simply doesn't arise.
But then again, if I refuse Mr. F's sponsorship, there's nowhere else to get the funds!

At night, returning from M-ko's house, as I was about to go up to the second floor, two shadows were clearly cast above my door.
Startled, I stopped in my tracks, and I clearly saw the two shadows gradually drawing closer (닥아 붙는 것이)!
—Good evening.
When I called out (질르니), S jumped out first. In the room was a cute-looking girl, whom I immediately recognized as Y-ko, from the house where S had stayed when he had his emergency landing.
They said they had both come to visit but were just waiting because the owner wasn't home.
To them, who had come all this way, I made the excuse (핑게) of having a headache and sent them away (돌려보내고<284>).
Having returned after giving up (단념하고) on the introduced Mr. F, when I saw the unusual closeness between S and Y-ko, I felt a suffocating shock (충동).
Barely managing not to lose consciousness and collapse (쓸어진) on the spot, I managed to exchange simple greetings, but seeing the young and pretty Y-ko, I didn't have the courage to sit together (對座) with the three of them for long.
(Do I have the right to seek love from S?!)
Although this question has an unspoken answer, I now began to agonize over it anew as a fresh problem.
No matter how many times I try to forget, try to change my mind, it's no use!

Month X, Day X
The "Bluebird" (靑鳥號) completed the Bureau's inspection (檢査).
I flew it boldly, and the engine's performance (機能) is OK enough. When I got out of the plane, the students praised me, saying,
—With that level of skill (技倆), crossing the Hyŏnhaet'an (玄海灘) is no problem at all.
The joy of owning my own airplane for the first time is special (各別하다).
After finishing the test (테스토) and completing the plane's decoration (化粧), I rubbed my cheek against the "wing," resilient (彈力) like a maiden's skin, and thanked my beloved machine (愛機) for flying freely.
(My lover (戀人) is the airplane. The blue sky (蒼空) is my happy home (家庭). Bitter things, unpleasant things, all are forgotten when I am high up in the sky (하날)!)
—Where, don't you want to go? To Mr. F's place.
Shoulder to shoulder (억개를 나란히 하고) with M-ko, I returned energetically to my boarding house room.

Month X, Day X
—Ah, you've come.
Mr. F treats me quite cordially (여간 반갑게 대해주지를 안는다).
—Are the preparations for the flight all done?
—Yes, -but,
—I understand, I have something in mind.
Mr. F looked younger than when seen in public settings (공개된 자리). After a while, I followed Mr. F into a room on the second floor of the X Hotel.

Three hours later, my exhausted body lay limply on the lonely floor of my boarding house room. Having resolved (各悟) myself to everything, what is there to regret (後悔) anew now, whom should I resent (怨望)!
(As long as my plan is achieved (達成), that's all that matters.<285> With this, the preparations are complete. Now I can fly! I can fly!)
Clutching a cheque (小切手), I cried out (부르짖었다) alone like a madman (狂人).

Month X, Day X
Tomorrow is decided as the departure from Haneda (羽田). Went to say farewell (作別) to Mr. N.
—My, the preparations went well.
—Yes, ...somehow.
Hot tears streamed (쭈루루 흘러내렸다) down from the corners of my eyes (눈가슭)...
At the single remark from Mr. N, who knew well all of Kyŏngwŏn's hardships (辛勞) over 8 years, tears flowed spontaneously (제절로).
Mr. N, who had been listening silently (잠잠히) to my words babbled (짖거리던) amidst excitement while shedding tears, said,
—Do you think of yourself as a sad woman?
—N-no, right now I'm just happy to achieve the goal I planned. I think "sentimentality" (센치멘탈) is nothing but arrogant (건방진) behavior.
I said forcefully.

D, S-gun's Diary

Month X, Day X
The takeoff (離陸) was truly splendid (훌륭하었다).
Hakone (箱根) suddenly clouded over, making visibility (視度) impossible, so considering the situation (狀況), I flew with the resolve to go around Izu (伊豆). The airfield was boiling with a crowd of over ten thousand (萬餘), waving handkerchiefs (한켓치) and hats and shouting Banzai (萬歲).
Y-ko and I waved handkerchiefs (한캇치) and sent her off until the fuselage (機體) became like a grain of rice (쌀악만치) and disappeared into the southern sky.
After that, news of K-ko (Kyŏngwŏn) completely ceased.
She was scheduled to arrive in Ōsaka (大阪) at noon (正午), but did not arrive. Telegrams (電報) of inquiry (照會) were sent (첬으나) to various points (各地) along the course (코-스), but the reply telegrams (返電) from everywhere were the same: "Aircraft not sighted (機影이 안 보인다)."
Anxiety (不安) gradually mounted. People from the school gathered in front of the hangar until 10 p.m., waiting for information (情報), but still, there was no news at all.
10:30 p.m., gave up (斷念) and dispersed (散會).

Month X, Day X
10 a.m., the first report (第1報) came from a passenger plane (旅客機) that arrived in Ōsaka: "Airplane crashed (墜落) in the mountains of Izu Gendake (伊豆玄嶽)." Subsequently, from Shizuoka (靜岡) Prefecture A Police Station (A署), there was a confirmed report (確報) of Kyŏngwŏn's tragic crash death (墜落慘死).
In the evening, information came from the scene (現場).
Kyŏngwŏn, leaning her upper body (上半身) forward from the seat (座席), had died instantly (卽死) brutally (無慘히). Due to rainfall (降雨), she couldn't cross Hakone and was circling Izu when visibility (視界) became completely nil,<286> and it seems she crashed (衝撞) into the mountainside (山腹) of Gendake while flying blind (盲目飛行).
Who could have even dreamed (꿈이나 꾸었으랴) that she would be so brutally crushed (지터벌일 줄이야) at the threshold (門壓) of her cheerful Korea-Manchuria visit flight!
She departed with full confidence (自信) based on her skill (技倆) and experience, but unfortunately, the sudden change (急變) in weather (天候) brought Miss Pak the worst (最惡) tragedy (悲劇).
For K-ko (Kyŏngwŏn), who died for her cause (殉死) on the yearned-for (憧憬) homeland visit flight, perhaps there were no regrets (不足이 없었을는지), but she so wanted to achieve the first Japan-Korea connection (內鮮連絡), the soaring across (翔破) the Hyŏnhaet'an, by a woman's hand.
In K-ko's boarding house room, as if she had anticipated this misfortune, everything was neatly arranged (整頓). Opening her desk drawer (책상설합) idly, a white square envelope (角封套) emerged.
To me
If I fail and cannot return, please read this. Was written on it.
Unexpectedly (意外에) calming my heart, I slowly opened (뜯었다) the envelope.

Month X, Day X
Today is K-ko's civilian funeral.
Having learned for the first time from her final letter about the hardships (辛酸) K-ko endured during 8 years of self-supported training, I wrote a long eulogy (弔詞) and rushed to the ceremony hall. But the ceremony was already almost halfway over, and ultimately, the eulogy was not read. I pleaded earnestly to have it read, but it was still denied (틀렸다).
Several eminent figures (名士) praised K-ko's glamorous side (一面) or lauded her contributions (功績) to the female aviation world in their eulogies, but no one, not a single person, touched upon (接觸) the hardship (苦心) of earning the expensive 230 hours of practice fees with a woman's frail hands.
Returning from the ceremony hall, I read the eulogy once more.
Thinking that the record of 230 hours was the crystallization of 8 years of youthful blood, I can only bow my head (머리가 수그러질 뿐이다) before K-ko, who dedicated her whole being solely to the blue sky (蒼天)...
Still, I cannot suppress my indignation (激憤) towards the sponsors of the world. What did Mr. F do, making the provision of fuel costs a condition (條件)? Did he have no kindness (好意) to let K-ko fly freely, away from other complicated (煩雜) problems!
Before the death of K-ko, who departed so transiently and pitifully, the sponsor surely bears responsibility (責任) too.

Month X, Day X
Sent message (發信) to G.
Today marks 35 days since K-ko passed.
Only recently have I finally come to understand K-ko's heart. I have promised Y-ko to suspend our relationship (交際中止) for one year.
—End—<287>

**Summary**

This 1936 article recounts the tragic life and death of Pak Kyŏngwŏn (Park Kyung-won), Korea's first female civilian pilot, focusing on her final, fatal attempt at a flight from Japan to Korea and Manchuria in 1933. Through excerpts purportedly from her diary and that of an admirer, student pilot "S," the article reveals her immense struggles against financial hardship and societal constraints, her determination over ten years, and hints at a secret, possibly unrequited love with S. It culminates in her crash due to bad weather and S's grief and anger, particularly towards sponsors, implying one sponsor, "F," may have exploited Pak's desperation for funding. For historians, this piece offers a poignant look into the challenges faced by pioneering Korean women under Japanese colonialism, the difficult path of ambition within limited means, the complex dynamics of patronage and potential exploitation, and the social lives of Korean students in Japan. The raw emotional narrative and focus on personal sacrifice provide valuable insight into the human cost of pursuing dreams during this period.
