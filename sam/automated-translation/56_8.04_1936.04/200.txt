Sigh of the Butterfly
						Kim Anyo

Bunch by bunch, on the yellow petals of the radish flower,
Softly, softly, a fine rain falls all day long.

Caught in the rain, this body, so lonely,
Dimly, dimly, wandering through this evening,
The butterfly, flying and flying, sighs.<114>

**Summary**

This short poem describes a butterfly struggling in the fine rain, unable to rest on the radish flowers and wandering into the darkening evening with a sigh. The imagery evokes feelings of loneliness, desolation, and being lost. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted as an allegory for the suffering or sense of displacement felt by Koreans under colonial rule. Historians might find its subtle expression of melancholy through nature imagery interesting, potentially reflecting the constrained environment for overt expressions of national sentiment.
