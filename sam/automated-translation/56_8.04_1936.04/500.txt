How Much Did the Zoo Animals Cost and Where Did They Come From?

It was 1 PM on the 6th when I visited the Changgyeongwon (昌慶苑) zoo, which had aged another year with the arrival of the Byeongja year (丙子年)! Entering through Honghwamun Gate (弘化門), the leafless trees stood sparsely, evoking a sense of desolation. I went straight to the office below the tall house, and through the introduction of Mr. Chŏn Chŏng-nam (全正南), I was guided by Mr. Higuchi (樋口) and received detailed explanations from Chief Asami (淺見), allowing me to view all 600 animals one by one. Going up from the staff guardhouse (係員詰所), the first thing that catches the eye are several horses (馬). Next to them is a single speckled zebra (班馬), currently 7 years old. Perhaps feeling homesick, having come from faraway Africa (亞弗利加) to a foreign land, it stands ignoring people even when they approach. "How much did that one sell for?" I asked. "3,500 Won (원)," came the unhesitating reply. Indeed, the feeling arises—it’s more expensive than a person's value. If a woman sold on the human flesh market (人肉시장) typically goes for 350 Won, this is ten times that. Truly, being born as a special beast is better than being a person sold on the human flesh market, I thought. Next were four Korean roe deer (노루), Chosŏn (朝鮮) native; their diet is like that of cattle and horses, and the price per head is 10 to 25 Won, decidedly cheaper than the zebra. Above them are Korean deer (朝鮮鹿), handsome fellows among the animals that resided in the mountains of Hamgyŏng North and South Provinces (咸鏡南北道 山中), priced at 200 to 300 Won per head. Currently 10 years old, there are five of them in total! Next are five Korean water deer (朝鮮가모鹿); these fellows have molars (억음니). The price per head (一匹代價) is about 120 Won. There are five Manchurian red deer (滿洲赤鹿), priced at 250 to 350 Won per head. Look at those antlers (角)! They look like broken-off cinnamon tree branches attached to them. Among the small deer, they are said to be very wild and difficult to tame. At 15 or 16 years old, they are like young men in human terms.

There is also a deer called 'kasyo' (가쇼), this one is cheap at about 120 Won. There are five of them. Next, in a den, is the Korean raccoon dog (朝鮮タヌキ). These fellows just stay inside their burrows. There are currently 4, at 50 Won per head (1匹代), and their diet is meat (肉食). Next is the mountain cat (山고양이), 20 to 30 Won per head. The Korean fox (高麗狐) is 20 to 30 Won per head. The raccoon (浣態狐), native to North/Central America (北中亞米利加産),<267> costs about 175 Won per head, and there are two. There is one arctic fox (靑狐) at 375 Won; this one, when dead, wraps around ladies' bodies. It's from Hokkaidō (北海道産). The red fox (金狐), this one is also quite expensive. Don't be surprised. It's 650 Won. There is one. There are 5 lynx (大山猫), 50 to 100 Won per head. From here, turning right to the next enclosure, there are sheep (羊) that came from Mongolia (蒙古), priced at 100 Won per head (一首在 價格). Next to them are mountain goats (山羊), 6 head (首) priced at 50 Won (圓). The camel (駱駝), as you know, is a traveler of the Mongolian desert (蒙古砂漠) and a valuable riding animal. Its special characteristics are: 1. It has fatty humps on its back, which serve as nutrient storage; these shrink during long journeys or extreme fatigue. People commonly say water is stored there, but this is mistaken. 1. [sic] Its stomach has water sacs (水囊) structured to store a large amount of water, allowing it to endure thirst for a long time. 1. [sic] Its senses are keen. Its sense of smell is most superior, allowing it to detect oases (오아시쓰) from afar in the desert. 1. [sic] It has nostrils that can open and close freely, and the pads (褥面) of its toes (指趾) are wide, making it suited for desert travel. Its diet is grass (草食), it produces one offspring per birth (一産一兒), its coat (被毛) is importantly used for woolen textiles, and its price is 200 to 300 Won. A couple is currently living together, comforting each other through hardship. The ostrich (駝鳥) is a bird inhabiting desert regions such as Africa (亞弗利加), Arabia (아라비아), Syria (시리아), Mesopotamia (메소보다미아), and is currently the king among birds. Its height exceeds 2.4 meters (米), weight 140 kilograms (瓩), the female's body color is dirty gray (汚灰色), the male's is black (黑色), with white tail and wing feathers (尾羽, 翼羽). When running, it covers 96 kilometers (粁) per hour, and one stride leaps 7.5 meters. Its diet consists of desert plants (砂漠性 植物), their seeds, and insects. It lays eggs in the sand (砂中); the parent birds incubate them at night, but during the day, they are left to hatch on their own by the sun's heat. Its feathers are used for crafts (工藝品). There are two currently. The price per bird is 1,500 to 1,700 Won. The hippopotamus (河馬) inhabits the rivers of the Congo Basin (곤고盆地) in Africa and the upper reaches of the Nile River (나일河). Its body length exceeds 4 meters (米), making it the king after the elephant among land-dwelling animals (陸棲動物). People say this fellow sweats blood profusely, but that's not the case; it's related to the secretion of a type of crimson liquid (鮮紅色에 1種液을 分泌), certainly not blood (血液), so don't be alarmed. Its diet consists of potatoes (馬齡薯), carrots (胡蘿萄), and hay (乾草). Its daily food intake is 2 kwan (貫) of hay (or 8 kwan if fresh grass), and 2 kwan of potatoes. Its body is large, and it eats a lot too. There are currently 4, priced at 5,000 Won per head (1匹代), and one of them is an elder, so to speak, having been here since the zoo's founding (創設 當時).

Elephant (象) - there are currently two. Being the largest of the land-dwelling animals, the price per head (1匹) is 6,500 to 7,500 Won.<277> They inhabit the lush forests (森林) of India (印度), the Malay Peninsula (馬來半島), etc. Their shoulder height (肩의 高) exceeds 3 meters, body length from trunk tip to tail base (鼻端부터 尾基部) is 7-8 meters, and weight exceeds 3 tons (噸). Although these animals originally prefer tropical forests, they avoid the daytime sun and do not come out onto the plains. Their diet is grasses (草類), and they spray water with their trunks. Solitary males (牡), males with calves (仔兒을 가진 牡), and males in mating season (交尾期) are dangerous. They are polygamous (一夫多妻主義者), and after a gestation period (姙期) of 19 to 22 months, they give birth (分娩) to one calf (1仔). Elephants commonly live long lives of 120-130 years, reaching maturity at 30, producing an average of 6 calves by age 90, and when their strength fails (긔력이다 하면), they bury themselves in the ground. In the regions of India, they are used for labor (使役) in logging (材木運搬用) and farming (耕作用). They are fed potatoes, bread, brown sugar (黑砂糖), soybean paste (된장), and hay.

Tiger (虎) - there is 1 head (首), price is 1,000 Won per head. The female gives birth to 2 to 6 cubs per litter (1産). They are common in tropical regions. Leopard (표범) - there are 3 head, 200 to 300 Won per head. Diet is meat (肉食).

Lion (獅子) - there are currently 3 head. Price per head (1首代) is 750 Won. Also a tropical animal, its majestic head (風彩) makes it seem like the king of all carnivores (全肉食獸). Black bear (黑熊) - 2 animals (匹), price 150-200 Won per head. These fellows are mischievous (작난구럭이). They climb trees and sometimes raid beehives (蜜蜂의 巢) to eat honey (蜜). Brown bear (히熊) - 2 animals, price 300 Won per head. Hyena (하이에나) - 3 animals, 175 Won. Puma (뿌마) - 2 head present (首在), price per head (1首價) 1,000 Won. Wolf (럭대) - 4 head present, price per head 30 to 40 Won. Monkeys (猿類) total 36 head, price varies by species. There are currently 9 species, the highest priced is 350 Won (クモサル - spider monkey), the lion-tailed macaque (獅子猿) is 325 Won, and the lowest is down to 25 Won. Cat (猫) - 120 Won, 2 head present. Snake (蛇) - 1 specimen (尾), 450 Won, length 3.5 kan (間), only one remains now. Crocodile/Alligator (ワニ - wani) - there is one, 40 Won. Giant salamander (싼쇼한 - sanshō'uo) - 4 present, around 12 Won. Turtles (龜) - 3 head present, received as donations, so their value is unknown.

Next, what's interesting is the kangaroo (강가루). It's been over two months since it gave birth, but it still keeps the baby hidden in its pouch, refusing to show it no matter how much one wants a glimpse.

Next, visiting the Aviary (鳥類園):
Heron family (鷺科) - 3 species, 4 birds (羽), 5 Won per bird, total 20 Won.
Heron family, Spoonbill (고노鳥 - probably 고니=swan or 저어새=spoonbill?) - 20 Won. Anatidae family (雁鴨科) Pelican (베루강 - pelican?) - 2 birds, 250 to 300 Won. Other types in this family, over 10 species, more than 100 birds are flying and honking on a winter day, prices go down to a minimum of 3 Won. Accipitridae family (鷲鷹科) - currently 5 species, 8 birds. Price: small eagle (小鷲) 10 Won, large eagle (大鷲) 20 to 40 Won, common kite (野솔개) 3 Won or less. Phasianidae family (雉科) Korean pheasant (고려콩) - 15 head (首), price per head 2-3 Won. Golden pheasant (모추락이) 2-5 Won. Rallidae family (秧鷄科) Gallinule/Moorhen (靑鷄) - 6 head, 8-10 Won. Gruidae family (鶴科)<278> - 8 species, 40 head. Red-crowned crane (단정학, denoted as 단조) 200 Won, Hooded crane (감모리鶴) 425 Won, Indian crane (印度鶴) 200 Won, down to a minimum of 30 Won for those caught in Korea (朝鮮서 붓잡은 것). Columbidae family (鵨科) Dove/Pigeon (都鳥) - 2 birds present. Laridae family (鷗科) Black-tailed gull (海猫) - 15 head. Common gull (유리鷗)? - 1 head, around 4 Won. Regrettably, there is no Cuckoo (杜鵑). If there were, it would be priced around 15 Won, they say. Is there anyone among Samch'ŏlli (삼천리) readers who might be interested (有意者無乎也)?
Strigidae family (梟鴟科) - 3 species present, price around 3-4 Won. Picidae family (ꟓ木鳥科 - typo for 啄木鳥科?) Red woodpecker (赤啄) - 2 birds, around 5 Won. Alaudidae family (雲雀科) Skylark (종달새) - 2 birds present, 1 or 2 Won per bird. Pycnonotidae family (鵯科) Brown-eared bulbul (효鳥 - probably 히요도리/직박구리) - 1 bird, 3 Won. Turdidae family (鶇科) - 4 species, around 2-3 Won, nearly 20 birds present. Laniidae family (鵙科) Large shrike (大型) - 1 bird, 5 Won. Oriolidae family (黃鳥科) Korean Oriole (高麗앵무새 - likely꾀꼬리, not parrot) - 5 birds, around 5-6 Won. Paridae family (40雀科 - typo for 四十雀科?) - two birds, 1 Won or less. Sturnidae family (掠鳥科 - typo for 椋鳥科?) Starling (묵鳥 - probably 찌르레기) - 3 Won. Fringillidae family (雀科) Hawfinch (시매 - probably 되새/콩새?) - 1 Won or less. Although only 7-8 species are listed here, prices go down to 30 chŏn (錢), they say. Next, among the guests from foreign countries: Turkey (七面鳥) - 5 birds, 3 Won per bird. White goose (白雁) - 3 birds, 15 Won. Silver pheasant (銀鷄) - 15 Won, totaling 30 Won. Snow goose (島白雁) - 1 bird, 40 Won. Serau/Serow? (세랄) - 1 present, 200 Won. Peacock types (孔雀類): White peacock (白孔雀) - 7 birds, price per bird 125 Won. Swan (白鳥) - 2 birds, 40-50 Won. Other imports (外來品) 15-20 Won. Reeves's pheasant (尾長雉) - 4 present, 75 to 80 Won. Macaw (마고 - likely Macaw/앵무새) - 3 birds, price per bird 200 Won. One type is lovely, speckled as if raised in the mountain valleys, with a red beak (주둥이), green wings (나래), and bright red legs (샛빨가코). Smaller than a magpie, its name is Fairy Pitta (山娘 - 팔색조). 3 birds are living amicably; they were bought for 15 to 20 Won per bird. Budgerigars (섹세이숀) or Japanese white-eyes (小靑鳥) are small and lovely. The talkative Myna bird (규강, 앵무) says "Ohayō" (오하요 - Japanese for Good morning) when you enter, "Ohayō" again when you look, and "Ohayō" when you leave—such is an animal!

**Summary**

This 1936 article from Samch'ŏlli magazine details a visit to the Changgyeongwon Zoo in Seoul. The reporter, guided by zoo staff including Japanese personnel like Chief Asami, lists numerous animals, their origins (Korea, Manchuria, Africa, India, etc.), and their purchase prices in Korean Won. Notable prices include 3,500 Won for a zebra, 5,000 Won for a hippopotamus, and 6,500-7,500 Won for an elephant. The article also describes animal characteristics, diets, and some long-serving inhabitants. It concludes with a count of animals by type as of the end of fiscal year 1934 (Showa 9). From a colonial history perspective, the article is revealing due to the casual mention of Japanese staff (Higuchi, Asami) and Japanese terminology (Ohayō, animal names like 'wani', 'sanshō'uo', 'sekiseiinko'), indicating Japanese management or influence at a major Korean cultural site. The stark comparison of the zebra's high price (3,500 Won) to the value of women sold in "human flesh markets" (350 Won) offers a glimpse into the harsh social realities and economic disparities of the time, potentially reflecting colonial exploitation or general poverty. Historians might find the detailed price list valuable for economic history, the mix of Korean and foreign animals indicative of trade/collection networks, and the social commentary reflective of the period's mindset.

動物園 動物은 어듸서 얼마에 팔려왓나
丙子年을 마저 한 살 더 먹는 昌慶苑안 動物園을 차저가기는 지난 6일 오후 1시! 弘化門을 드러서니 닢없는 나무는 엉성하게 서서 쓸쓸한 감회를 자아낸다. 나는 바로 놉흔 집 밋 사무실을 차저 全正南씨의 소개로 樋口씨의 안내와 主任 淺見씨의 자세한 설명으로 600여 동물을 일일이 구경하게 되엿다. 係員詰所에서 우으로 올너가면 첫 번 눈에 듸우는 것이 몃 필의 馬이다. 그 다음에 알눅달눅한 한 마리의 班馬가 잇는데 當年 7歲다. 亞弗利加에서 멀니 타국에 온 만큼 望鄕心이 나든지 사람이 가도 본체만체하고 서 잇다. 저것은 얼마에 팔녀왓슬가요. 한즉 「3500원」임니다. 서슴잔코 하는 말이다. 과연 사람의 갚보다도 빗사구려-하는 감이 생긴다. 人肉시장에 팔녀나가는 여자가 보통 350원이라면 열배가 낫구나. 과연 별다른 즘생으로 태여나는 것이 人肉시장에 팔니는 사람보다 낫구나 하게 되엿다. 나는 그 다음 노루 朝鮮産으로 4匹인데 잡숫는 것은 牛馬와 갓고 한 마리의 값은 10원으로 25원 정도 班馬보다 단연 눅거리다. 그 우가 朝鮮鹿인데 咸鏡南北道 山中에 게시든 동물 중 美男兒로서 그 값은 한 마리에 200원 내지 300원임니다. 當年 10세 童, 그 동모는 모다 다섯! 그 다음이 朝鮮가모鹿 5마리가 잇는데 이놈은 억음니가 잇슴니다. 一匹代價 120원 정도, 滿洲赤鹿 다섯 마리가 잇고 한 머리의 값이 250원 내지 350원 정도임니다. 저 角을 보시요. 桂樹나무를 꺽거 붓치고 단기는 것 갓지요. 좀 사슴 중에는 야성이 만코 길들기가 힘이 든담니다. 當年 15, 6세에 사람으로 치면 청년이외다.
또 가쇼라는 사슴이 잇는데 이 놈은 120원 정도에 눅거리외다. 다섯 마리가 잇지요. 다음 窟속엔 朝鮮タヌキ임니다. 이 놈은 구멍속에서만 지나지요. 지금 4匹, 1匹代 50원, 食物은 肉食. 다음은 山고양이 一匹代 20원 내지 30원 정도. 高麗狐 일필 20원 내지 30원 浣態狐 北中亞米利加産으로<267> 一匹代 175원 정도로 2匹이 잇슴니다. 靑狐가 一匹 375원 이 놈은 죽어서 부인들의 몸을 감고 단임니다. 北海道産이외다. 金狐 이놈도 상당이 갚이 빗삼니다. 놀나지 마서요. 650원 이외다. 한 마리가 잇슴니다. 大山猫가 5匹 一匹代 50원 내지 100원. 여긔서 다시 올흔 편으로 돌아 겻 洞里에 羊이 蒙古에서 왓는데 一首在 價格100원. 겻헤 山羊도 잇다. 6首 價格50圓. 駱駝는 아시다싶히 蒙古砂漠旅行者로서 乘用物로 귀중한 동물이외다. 이 놈의 특질은 1. 體背部에 脂肪質의 峯우리가 잇는데 여긔에는 養分貯藏庫로서 長途旅行 혹은 大端이 疲勞하면 적어짐니다. 보통사람이 말하기를 그 속에 水를 저장한다 하지만 이것은 잘못안 것이외다. 1. 胃의 水囊중에 다량의 水를 貯水하도록 구조가 되여서 오래 목마르지 안캐 됨니다. 1. 감각은 예민함니다. 후각은 가장 優越하여 사막지방에서 遠方에 「오아시쓰」 잇는 곳을 냄새맛슴니다. 1. 開閉自由에 鼻孔을 가지고 잇고 指趾의 褥面은 넙적해서 사막여행에 적당하게 생겻슴니다. 食物은 草食이고 一産一兒를 하며 被毛는 모직물로 중요하게 쓰고 값은 200원 내지 300원임니다. 지금 부부가 괴로운 중에도 서로 위로하고 지난담니다. 駝鳥는 亞弗利加, 아라비아, 시리아, 메소보답아 등 사막지방에 棲居하는 鳥로서 현재 조류 중 大王이외다. 身長의 2.4米 이상 체중 140瓩이고 體色은 雌가 汚灰色, 雄이 黑色으로 尾羽, 翼羽가 白色임니다. 다라날 때는 一時間 96粁(キロ) 한 발자욱이 7.5米를 건너 뜀니다. 食物은 砂漠性 植物, 其 種子, 昆虫을 먹고 産卵은 砂中에 하는데 밤엔 親鳥가 품어두고 낫에는 태양열에 제멋대로 짜게 버려 둔담니다. 그 羽毛는 공예품으로 이용함니다. 지금 두 마리가 잇지요. 한 마리의 값은 1,500원 내지 1,700원임니다. 河馬는 亞弗利加의 곤고盆地의 하천과 나일河 상류에 棲居, 體長이 4米 이상으로 陸棲動物 중 象의 다음 王이다. 이놈이 지긋지긋하게 血汗을 흘닌다고 하지만 그런 것이 아니라 선홍색에 1種液을 分泌하는 관게이지 결코 血液은 아니니 놀나지 마시요. 食物은 馬齡薯, 胡蘿萄乾草 등을 주지요. 하로 먹는 것이 乾草가 2貫(靑草면 8貫) 감자가 2貫 몸도 크지만 먹기도 만히 먹슴니다. 지금 4匹이 잇는데 1匹代가 5천원이고 이 중 한 마리는 이 動物園된 創設 當時부터 잇는 말하자면 원로임니다.
