What Stage Do We Want to Entrust to Mr. An Ch'ang-ho?

Tosan completed his parole period, which ended last year on November 18th, and is now a free man. During this time, while recuperating from illness and studying "Practical Korean Studies" (現實朝鮮學) at a temple below Taedo Mountain (大到山下) in P'yŏngan Province, he suddenly came up to Sŏul at the beginning of the new spring and frequently met with figures from various circles. Consequently, various rumors surrounding his personal affairs have abounded, and it seems undeniable that about a third of the recent topics of conversation in Chosŏn society have become stories about An Tosan. In any case, our attention going forward concerns what he intends to do and what we would like him to do. To solve this riddle, our company sought answers to the title question from dozens of leading reporters from various newspaper companies in Sŏul, as well as figures from educational, religious, and intellectual circles (we noted that anonymity was acceptable due to the nature of the question, and indeed, there were anonymous responses, which are presented as received).

Activity in Practical Education

O Kŭng-sŏn
The undertaking I hope for and wish to entrust to An Tosan, considering the current situation, is for him to be active in practical education, which is the urgent task for the Chosŏn populace.

First, Break Down the P'yŏngan Province Monroe Doctrine

Oesŏng Hagin
People call Tosan someone with P'yŏngan regional fervor. Whether Tosan himself was conscious of it or not, I don't know, but I lament that his footsteps after his parole, for nearly a year, were all<41> confined only to P'yŏngan Province.
Then, looking at the personal affairs section of today's newspaper, it says he took a scenic trip (雪月花 旅行) to the Chŏlla Province region; I am pleased by this point. From now on, please travel widely through the mountains and rivers of Horyŏng, Yŏngdong, and Kwanbuk, seeing all the sights of the 13 provinces, and then show the practical example that says, "I definitely, definitely do not love only the Taedong River of P'yŏngan Province, but I love every blade of grass and every tree of the 13 provinces."
After that, the issue of "An Sŏnsaeng's Stage" will arise, and after that, I believe it will be right to discuss the undertakings we want to entrust to Tosan.
In short, the immediate urgent matter for Tosan himself and the people around Tosan to pay attention to is the single fact that Tosan is not someone who holds to a P'yŏngan Province Monroe-ism (平安道몬로-主義).<42>

Follow Tao Yuanming to the Countryside

Ch'ŏngsan Su
Tosan is a truthful person. There are no lies in Tosan's words, and there are no corners in Tosan's actions where he tries to hide himself. Seen as a human being, he is a sincere person from one to ten.
Currently, in our society, there are few places for a sincere person to settle down. Because there is too much scheming (權謀) and maneuvering (術數) here, wouldn't Tosan also be helpless if he entered this murky (沌濁) atmosphere?
Therefore, the path for Tosan to take is to follow Tao Yuanming, who sought the mountains singing "Let us return! The fields and gardens are overgrown," leave the mundane world (俗塵), go to a place with fine mountains and water, and enjoy the "beauty of springs and rocks" (泉石의 美). Wouldn't this be the way to avoid the "shame of remaining years" (餘生의 辱)?

His Qualities Are Unknown

Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng
Although I can guess at his character, I do not know his current ideals or what kind of field he would be suited for, so it is difficult to answer.

Gather Money

Posin'gak Kŏsa
Right now, people keep clamoring about education and industry, but what can be done without money? In a world where moving a single finger or foot requires money from one to ten, shouldn't this money be gathered first? Therefore, the task I wish to entrust to Tosan is, first, to put his full effort into gathering money. Set a clear goal of several hundred thousand won or several million won, and with a plan of about 3 years, I hope he takes the lead in pooling money. After that, I believe discussions about a stage for education, a stage for industry, or stages for various other tasks can take place.

Continue That Life As Is

Im Wŏn-gŭn
Unless the fundamental structure of his brain is changed, he will likely just continue the same X-litical (X治的 - likely political) life he is currently treading. Therefore, new hopes for him seem useless.<43>

Please Do Not Become a Newspaper President

Ttakmokcho
Centered around the Chungang Hotel near Kwanggyo Bridge in Sŏul, among the various rumors circulating in the world these days, there is talk that you will become the president of a certain newspaper company. It's unclear whether this is self-promotion or slander from someone with ulterior motives (黑心). However, generally speaking, isn't "Newspaper President" a good lure for a "famous person" (名士), and aren't there few famous people who do not desire the position of newspaper president? Conversely, are there not few who serve as newspaper presidents and do not act like famous people? Therefore, many people fear and suspect that you too might end up sitting in the newspaper president's chair.
However, you know the limits of our press today. You also know the practical examples of money controlling the press. But ultimately, if you become a newspaper president, much disgrace (辱) will come your way, and you will likely end up staining your upright character. So my request is that you refrain from becoming a newspaper president.

To a Stage That Raises Education

T'an'gŭm Taein
If there are people meant for the front line and people meant for the second line in our society, I believe it is right to have Tosan stand on the second line. Therefore, I would like to entrust Tosan with the task of traveling around the 13 provinces, skillfully persuading (勸設) unknown wealthy individuals (無名財産家) in various regions to donate the money hidden away (死藏) in their hands, ensuring it is dedicated to educational and public interest projects. Although much money has flowed from the hands of the rich (富豪) in the name of education or public interest projects in the past, there have been numerous instances where that money fell into the hands of unscrupulous schemers (不純한 策士) and was misused. Therefore, I believe such important undertakings can only be safely entrusted to the man of utmost sincerity (至誠), An Tosan. Furthermore, with Tosan's persuasion (權誘), given his character, sincerity (至誠), and eloquence (辨說), I believe it will surely be possible.

Just Sing Lullabies

Song Pong-u
If there is an undertaking I would like to entrust to Mr. An Ch'ang-ho, it is the task of singing "lullabies" (子守歌) in his home and looking after his children or perhaps grandchildren.<44> If that is an undertaking, it would be the most suitable undertaking for him. Socially, there is not a single thing we want to entrust to him.

Just Rebuild Taesŏng School

Nŭngna Toin
Considering the current circumstances, the task that would be best entrusted to Tosan-sŏnsaeng is this: 30 years ago, Sŏnsaeng established Taesŏng School and nurtured many promising talents (幾多有爲). I hope he rekindles the passion of that time and devotes himself to educational work.

A Media Outlet Seems Most Suitable Anyway

Chu Un-sŏng
Considering Tosan-sŏnsaeng's background, the fields of education and youth training would be most suitable, but given the current times (時勢), such fields seem difficult. I believe there is no other undertaking to offer him besides a media outlet. In Chosŏn, it's almost customary to nominate such people as newspaper presidents, and inevitably (不得不), it seems there's no other choice. Therefore, I wish that a major newspaper company (大新聞社) would recruit someone like Sŏnsaeng.

Go Away

Akch'ŏn
A one-line letter addressed to Tosan:
"Go away."
"Go far, far away."

I Am Just a Blank Sheet

Ku Cha-ok
Regarding your question this time, it's a blank sheet.<45>

Past Expectations Were Too High

Il Silmangsaeng
Before meeting An Ch'ang-ho, my expectations for him were quite high, no less than anyone else's. However, having had the honor of meeting Mr. An closely on several public and private occasions, the resulting impression is merely the realization that my past expectations for Mr. An, who is after all just one element of Chosŏn, were too high. Therefore, I don't know which field would be the most suitable (適宜) stage for his activities. However, if it is an absolute command (絶對命令) that I must say something, I think acting as a leader of an X group (X士團 - likely émigré nationalist group) overseas, like in Sanghae (Shanghai), might be the most suitable role (最適任) for Mr. An himself.

To the Northwestern Monroe-ism

Il Chŏkp'yŏngsaeng
Sling the Hŭng X dan (興X團 - likely Hŭngsadan) signboard over your shoulder and go pray before Grandfather Heaven (하날아바지).<46>

**Summary**

This 1936 survey in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine gathers diverse opinions on the future role of An Ch'ang-ho following his release from parole. Respondents offer varied suggestions: some advocate for practical education or fundraising, others urge him to avoid politics or newspaper leadership due to the constrained environment and potential for corruption. A significant concern raised is An's perceived regional bias towards P'yŏngan Province, with calls for him to demonstrate love for all of Korea. Some express deep disillusionment, suggesting he retire to the countryside, focus on family, leave Korea, or lead émigré groups abroad, while others see no suitable role or express outright hostility. Historically, this article is valuable for showcasing the complex and often critical views held by contemporary Koreans towards a major nationalist figure like An Ch'ang-ho during the colonial period. It reveals anxieties about regionalism within the nationalist movement, the perceived limitations of action within Korea under Japanese rule, and a degree of cynicism or disappointment regarding established leaders, reflecting the difficult political and social landscape of 1930s colonial Korea.
