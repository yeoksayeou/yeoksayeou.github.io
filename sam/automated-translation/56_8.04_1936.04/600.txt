Gratitude and Apology

Counting by the year, eight years have passed since this magazine (本誌) began publication, and looking at the number of magazine issues published during that time, the total cumulative circulation (延總部數) has surpassed 5 million copies. Now, striving for 50 million copies, ten times the 5 million, we offer this special feature (特輯) in the spirit of self-celebration (自祝).

The "Complete Works of Korean Literature" (朝鮮文學全集) is a project of truly great brilliance, even in its historical significance. Its first distributed volume (配本) will be the Collection of Short Stories by Yŏm Sangsŏp (廉想涉), set to come out in early March.

The first and second distributed volumes (配本) of the "Complete Works of Yi Kwangsu" (李光洙全集) were both well-received. The third volume will be a collection of travelogues (紀行文集) titled "My Peninsular Mountains and Rivers" (나의 半島江山). We know this will be a fine gift to offer readers (讀書子) during the pleasant season (好節季) of warm spring (陽春) in March. <400>

**Summary**

This editor's postscript from the April 1936 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine celebrates reaching a cumulative circulation of 5 million copies over eight years and expresses ambition for 50 million. It also promotes upcoming literary publications: the first volume of the "Complete Works of Korean Literature" featuring Yŏm Sangsŏp's short stories, and the third volume of the "Complete Works of Yi Kwangsu," a travelogue collection titled "My Peninsular Mountains and Rivers." From a historical perspective, the article highlights the magazine's success and ambition within the colonial media landscape. The promotion of collected works by prominent Korean authors like Yŏm Sangsŏp and Yi Kwangsu, framed as a significant cultural undertaking ("Complete Works of Korean Literature"), is noteworthy, showcasing efforts to consolidate and disseminate Korean literature despite the colonial context.
