The Full Picture of Keijō Imperial University
As a first step in visiting the highest institution of learning in Chōsen (Korea), we look at the Imperial University. In the next issue, we will visit the various public and private professional schools.

Location: Keijō-fu (Kyŏngsŏng-bu) Tōshō-chō (Tongsung-dong) (Law & Literature), Rengen-chō (Yŏngŏn-dong) (Medicine), Kyŏnggi-dō Kōyō-gun (Koyang-gun) Kōtoku-men (Kongdŏk-myŏn) Kakei-ri (Hagye-ri) (Science & Engineering)

History: November, Taishō 12 (1923), Keijō Imperial University Establishment Committee founded. May, Taishō 13 (1924), Preparatory School opened. April, Taishō 15 (1926), Faculties established. March, Shōwa 4 (1929), First graduating class of the Faculty of Law and Literature. Subsequently, March, Shōwa 5 (1930), First graduating class of the Faculty of Medicine. January, Shōwa 7 (1932), Degree regulations established, designating three types of degrees to be awarded: Law, Literature, and Medicine. April, Shōwa 13 (1938), Faculty of Science and Engineering added.

Enrollment Capacity: Law & Literature=80, Medicine=80, Science & Engineering=40.

The university is located northeast of the city center, facing Keijō's famous Ch'anggyŏngwŏn garden. The Faculty of Medicine's附属醫院 (Affiliated Hospital) stands tall, surrounding the Hwicheonwŏn (回春苑), with the Faculty of Medicine and the main building nearby. Including the 12-ken (approx. 21.8 meters) road, the Faculty of Law and Literature, the library, and the university headquarters are situated with Nakt'a Mountain (Naksan) at their back. This 12-ken road is commonly called Taehangno (大學路 - University Road), and although there are no trams, buses from the Keijō Electric Company (京城電氣會社) run frequently, making transportation extremely convenient. Nearby are the Medical Professional School (醫專), Higher Technical School (高工), Mining Professional School (鑛山專), Higher Commercial School (高商), and Women's Medical Professional School (女子醫專), forming Keijō's student district. The Faculty of Science and Engineering is located about 2 ri (approx. 8 km) northeast from here and is currently under construction. Transportation is via the Kyŏngch'un railway line from Ch'ŏngnyangni, where the University Preparatory School is located.

Faculty Overview: Keijō Imperial University consists of three faculties—Law & Literature, Medicine, and Science & Engineering—with an attached Preparatory School. Therefore, the vast majority of students entering the faculties have completed the Preparatory School.

The Faculty of Law and Literature is divided into four departments: Law, Philosophy, History, and Literature. The Law department is further divided into Class 1 (Judicial), Class 2 (Administrative), and Class 3 (Economic). Students in each department must complete 27 credits, including required and elective courses. The Philosophy department is divided into Philosophy, Ethics, Psychology, Religious Studies, Aesthetics, Education, and Chinese Philosophy, requiring 24 credits. The History department is divided into National History (Japanese), Korean History, and East Asian History. The Literature department is divided into Japanese Language & Literature, Korean Language & Literature, Chinese Language & Literature, and English Language & Literature, requiring 24 credits, the same as the Philosophy department.

The Faculty of Medicine is largely similar to medical faculties at other universities, requiring completion of all subjects: Anatomy (including Histology and Embryology), Physiology, Medical Chemistry, Microbiology, Parasitology, Pathology, Pharmacology, Hygiene, Preventive Medicine, Forensic Medicine, Internal Medicine, Surgery, Orthopedic Surgery, Obstetrics & Gynecology, Ophthalmology, Pediatrics, Otorhinolaryngology, Dermatology & Urology, and Neurology & Psychiatry. In addition, lectures on Military Medicine and Medical Jurisprudence are given.

The Faculty of Science and Engineering is divided into <126> seven departments: Chemistry, Physics, Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Mining & Metallurgy, and Electrical Engineering.

Student Association (學友會 - Gakuyūkai)
The purpose of the Keijō Imperial University Student Association (Gakuyūkai) is "to cultivate the minds and bodies of members, promote friendship, and foster understanding of ideas, thereby invigorating the academic spirit." Its affairs fall under the jurisdiction of the Student Affairs Division (學生課). All university staff and students are members. Membership is divided into three categories: Special Members (Staff), Regular Members (Students), and Supporting Members (Alumni). The President is the University President, the Vice Presidents are the Faculty Deans, and department heads (professors) serve in roles. Additionally, there are two Staff Directors (Head of Student Affairs Division, Head of General Affairs Division), eight Student Directors, standing committee members, and general affairs/accounting committee members.
The Association has various divisions: Headquarters, Lectures, Orchestra, Mandolin, Traditional Japanese Music (邦樂), Mutual Aid (共濟), Kendō, Jūdō, Kyūdō (Archery), Tennis, Table Tennis, Baseball, Soccer, Rugby, Rowing (端艇), Basketball, Water Polo, Ice Skating, Track and Field, Horseback Riding, and Mountaineering. These divisions have achieved considerable success in their respective fields. Furthermore, directly under the Headquarters is the "Jōdai Gahōkyoku" (城大學報局 - University Newspaper Bureau), which distributes a newspaper to members once a month. Other affiliated cultural sections promote student cultural activities and carry out various projects. The membership fee for student members is 10 Yen per year, with an entrance fee of 3 Yen.

Faculty of Medicine Alumni Association: Established January, Shōwa 5 (1930). Members consist of Faculty of Medicine staff, graduates, and students. It was founded with the aim of "deepening mutual friendship among members, exchanging knowledge, and promoting the advancement of medicine." The president is the Dean of the Faculty of Medicine. Activities include holding a general meeting combined with a welcome party for new members every spring, organizing scenic trips (探勝旅行) in the autumn, and periodically holding lectures, round-table discussions (座談會), academic colloquia (學術集談會), and publishing a member directory. The annual fee is 1 Yen, with an entrance fee of 1 Yen.

Student Organizations
Clubs (同好會 - Dōkōkai): 1. Film Club (映畵同好會) - Holds film viewings, critique meetings, and conducts technical research. Established June, Shōwa 12 (1937). 2. Western Painting Club (洋畵同好會) - Holds exhibitions twice a year in spring and autumn, and regularly holds workshops. Established November, Shōwa 11 (1936). 3. Science Club (理學同好會) - Conducts research and presentations in science. Established April, Shōwa 11 (1936). 4. Photography Club (寫眞同好會) - Holds exhibitions twice a year. Established February, Shōwa 11 (1936). 5. Poetry Recitation Club (詩吟同好會) - Established November, Shōwa 9 (1934). 6. Tanka Club (短歌同好會) - Holds monthly Tanka meetings. Established April, Shōwa 13 (1938). 7. Haiku Club (俳句同好會) - Established April, Shōwa 14 (1939).
Literary Magazine (同人誌 - Dōjinshi): Jōdai Bungaku (城大文學 - University Literature) (critiques, creative writing, poetry, Tanka, Haiku, etc.) Published 6 times a year.
Religious Groups: Christian Association (キりスト教 年會) - Established May, Taishō 15 (1926). Risshōkai (立正會) - Established May, Taishō 15 (1926). Buddhist Youth Association (佛敎靑年會) - Established May, Shōwa 4 (1929).

Off-Campus Facilities
Pharmacognosy Research Institute (生藥硏究所): Kaesŏng-bu (Kaesŏng City), Kyŏnggi-dō. Located southeast of Kaesŏng City, famous for ginseng, this facility includes a medicinal herb garden (藥草苑) of about 40,000 pyeong (approx. 132,000 sq meters) and a research institute for the study and production of crude drugs (生藥). The director is Professor Sugihara Noriyuki (杉原德行) of Pharmacology.
Jōdai Mountain Villa (城大山莊): Pujŏn Plateau (赴戰高原), Hamgyŏngnam-dō. Located about a day's journey <127> from Keijō, on a branch of the Nangnim Mountain Range (狼林山脈), known as the roof of Chōsen. Situated near where the range meets the Sea of Japan, overlooking a large artificial lake created by damming water that flows towards the Yellow Sea (黃海). The area offers grand, continental-scale scenery and expansive views. The facility can accommodate approximately 80 people.

Student Facilities
Student facilities are extremely meager due to the small number of students and the university's relatively recent establishment. The campus store (學內賣店) and the Mutual Aid section (共濟部) temporarily sell notebooks at the beginning of the academic year. They also periodically facilitate the exchange of used books (古本). The Personnel Consultation Office (人事相談所) refers to the Student Affairs Division Head and the office itself, where various consultations are handled. Financial Aid (貸給費): There is no permanent, established financial aid system, but the Student Affairs Division introduces and brokers aid from奨勵會 (possibly 'Encouragement Associations' or specific named groups) and benevolent individuals (篤志家). Assembly Halls (集會所): There are large and small assembly halls within the Faculty of Law and Literature premises, used for study groups, discussions, etc. Mostly, the second floor of the university cafeteria located within the Faculty of Medicine premises is used. Campus Cafeterias (學內食堂): There are two, the University Cafeteria (大學食堂) and the Shōgo Cafeteria (昭五食堂), always operated by designated merchants. Recreation Room (娛樂室): Located within the Faculty of Law and Literature premises. Equipped with Go (碁), Shōgi (將棋 - Japanese Chess), and Chosŏn Changgi (朝鮮將棋 - Korean Chess).

**Summary**

This 1941 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* provides a detailed overview of Keijō Imperial University (Kyŏngsŏng Imperial University), presented as the premier institution of higher learning in colonial Korea (Chōsen). It covers the university's history, location, faculties (Law & Literature, Medicine, Science & Engineering), enrollment caps, curriculum structure, student association (Gakuyūkai), alumni groups, various student clubs, off-campus facilities like a pharmacognosy institute and a mountain villa, and student support services, which are noted as being "meager." The article concludes with an extensive list of the university's Japanese faculty members, organized by department.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it details the structure and operation of the top colonial university, explicitly modeled after Japanese imperial universities. The overwhelming presence of Japanese names in the faculty list starkly illustrates the Japanese dominance in higher education administration and staffing in colonial Korea. The inclusion of departments like "National History" (referring to Japanese history) and the structure of Korean and East Asian studies under Japanese academic leadership reflect the colonial context. The mention of meager student facilities also hints at potential disparities or limitations within the colonial education system, even at its highest level.

京城帝大의 全貌
조선의 최고 學府를 순례하는 첫걸음으로 帝大를 바라보며 이 다음 號에는 각 公私專門學校를 찿겠습니다
所在地  京城府 東崇町(法文) 蓮建町(醫) 京畿道 高陽郡 孔德面 下溪里(理工)
沿革 大正 12년 11월 京城帝國大學創設委員會 설립, 13년 5월 豫科 개교, 15년 4월 학부를 개설, 昭和 4년 3월 法文學部 제1회 졸업을 보내고 계속해서 5년 3월 醫學部 제1회 졸업생을 보내다 7년 1월 학위규칙을 제정하고 수여할 학위를 法, 文, 醫의 3種으로 하다. 13년 4월 理工學部를 증설하다.
收容定員  法文=80, 醫=80, 理工=40.
대학은 도심의 東北에 위치하여 京城의 명승 昌慶苑에 相對하여 醫學部 附屬醫院이 솟사있어 回春苑을 싸고 의학부, 본관이 있다. 12間도로를 포함하여 駱駝山을 등진 法文學部, 도서관, 대학본부가 있다. 이 12間도로를 세상에서 대학로라 칭하고 전차는 없지만 京城電氣會社의 빠스가 통하여 교통의 便은 지극히 좋다, 부근에는 醫專, 高工, 鑛山專, 高商, 女子醫專 등이 있어 京城의 學生街를 일우워있다. 또 理工學部는 여기를 떠나 東北 약 2리의 地에 설립 중인데 교통기관으로서는 大學豫科의 소재지, 淸涼里로부터 京春철도로 간다.
學部槪况, 京城帝大의 학부는 法文醫理工의 3학부로 되여 豫科가 부설되어있음으로 대체의 진로는 豫科수료자가 대부분을 점령하고 있다.
法文學部는 法學, 哲學, 史學, 文學의 4學科로 법학과는 1類(司法) 2類(行政) 3類(經濟)로 논이어 있어 各科와 함께 필수선택 합하여 27단위를 이수하게 되어 있다. 철학과는 철학, 윤리학, 심리학, 종교학, 미학, 교육학, 支那철학으로 分하여 24단위를 이수한다. 사학과는 국사학, 조선사학, 동양사학으로 논이였다. 문학과는 국어, 국문학, 조선어, 조선문학, 支那語, 支那文學, 영어, 영문학으로 논이여 있어 24단위를 이수하는 것은 철학과와 같다.
의학부는 다른 대학 의학부와 大差가 없이 (組織, 胎生을 含함) 生理, 醫化, 微生物, 寄生蟲, 病理, 藥理, 衛生, 豫防醫, 法醫, 내과, 외과, 정형외과, 산부인과, 안과, 소아과, 이비인후과, 피부비뇨기과, 신경정신과의 全科를 이수하기로 되었다. 이 외에도 軍陣醫學, 醫事法規의 강의를 행한다.
理工學部는 화학과, 물리학과, 토목공학과, 기계공학과, 鑛山冶金학과, 전기공학과의<126>의 7科로 分하여 있다.

學友會
경성제국대학 學友會 「회원의 심신을 陶冶하고 친목을 도모하여 사상을 通融함으로써 학풍을 振作함」하는 것이 학우회의 설립의 취지가 되어 있다. 사무는 學生課의 所管에 속하고 本學職員, 학생은 總히 회원이 되기로 되어있다. 회원을 分하면 특별회원(직원) 정회원(학생) 贊助회원(本學출신자)의 3종으로 되고 회장은 총장이 되고 부회장(학부장) 각부부장(교수) 외에 職員理事 2명(학생과장, 서무과장) 학생이사 8명, 상임위원, 서무회계위원이 있다.
本會는 本部, 講演, 관현악, 만도링 邦樂, 共濟, 검도, 유도, 궁도, 정구, 탁구, 야구, 축구, 라꾸비, 端艇, 농구, 水球, 氷滑, 육상경기, 승마, 산악의 諸部가 있다. 각 방면에 상당한 업적을 올리고 있다. 더욱 이 외에 본부에 직속으로 「城大學報局」이 있어 회원에 매월 1回 신문의 배포를 행하고 있다. 기타 여기에 附隨하는 文化部에서는 학생문화운동의 추진력이 되어 各般의 사업을 행하고 있다. 회비는 학생회원은 年拾額圓, 入會費는 3圓이다.
의학부 동창회 昭和 5년 1월 설립, 회원은 의학부 직원, 졸업생, 학생으로써 조직하고 「회원 상호의 친목을 두터히하고 지식을 교환하며 의학의 향상을 促함」 목적으로 설립하였는데 회장은 의학부장이다. 사업으로도 매년 春 신입회원환영회를 겸하여 총회를 개최하고 가울에는 探勝旅行을 행하여 隨時講演會, 座談會, 學術集談會, 회원명부발행을 하고 있다. 회비는 年 1圓, 入會金 1圓으로 되어있다.

學生施設
학생시설은 학생수의 근소한 것과 창립한지 오래지 안어 극히 빈약하다. 학내매점, 共濟部가 학년초 임시로 노트의 판매를 한다. 又 정기적으로 古本의 교환을 한다. 人事相談所라는 것은 학생과장 及 同사무실에서 여러 가지의 상담을 응한다. 貸給費, 특별한 常設的인 貸給費 제도는 없지만 獎勵會, 篤志家로부터 貸給費를 학생과에서 紹介 取次한다. 集會所, 법문학부 구내에 大小 二集會所가 있다. 연구회, 좌담회 등에 이용한다. 대개는 의학부 구내에 있는 대학식당 2층을 이용한다. 학내식당, 대학식당, 昭五식당의 둘이 있어 언제나 指定商人이 경영하고 있다. 오락실, 법문학부 구내에 있다.  碁, 將棋, 朝鮮將棋가 구비해 있다.
