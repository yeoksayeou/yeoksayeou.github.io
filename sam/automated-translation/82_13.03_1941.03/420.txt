A Short History of the Arang Theater Troupe

In August of Shōwa 14 (1939), when the management of the Tongyang Theater changed hands, comrades loyal to the former manager, Mr. Ch'oe Sangdŏk, joined hands and bid farewell to the Tongyang Theater (東劇). They bid farewell in tears. After wandering aimlessly, they borrowed the strength of a certain supporter and gathered huddled together like motherless chicks in the attic on the 2nd floor at 116, Chongno 6-ch'ŏme.

The people gathered here roughly included Pak Chin as director, Im Sŏn'gyu as playwright, Wŏn Ujŏn for stage design, and the acting lineup drawn from Ch'ŏngch'unjwa (靑春座) and Hwahwasŏn (豪華船), featuring Hwang Ch'ŏl, Sŏ Ilsŏng, Yang Paengmyŏng, Kim Tuch'an, Maeng Man-sik, along with over ten other male actors, and actresses such as Ch'a Hongnyŏ, Pak Yŏngsin, Kim Sŏnch'o, Mun Chŏngbok, Yi Chŏngsun, and Ŏm Mihwa, also numbering around ten.

It was here that Arang (阿娘) cried out and was born. Arang's birthday is the bright-mooned, clear-winded Ch'usŏk Holiday (中秋佳節). They held their debut performance at the Taegu Theater on Ch'usŏk day with Im Sŏn'gyu's work "Life Theater" (인생극장). Now, well past its first birthday, it is three years old by East Asian age reckoning. It can eat by itself and run around. It's in perfect health. Since its debut performance, it has digested over 10 scripts, including Im Sŏn'gyu's works like "The Widow" (未亡人), "Kim Okkyun" (金玉均), and "Windy Days" (바람 부는 시절). In the little over a year since its first birthday, the performances held in the capital and provinces number a staggering 400 days in quantity.

Busy with preparations for the performance commemorating the formation of the Chosŏn Theater Association to be held at the Pumin'gwan (府民舘) starting January 27th, they are gathered again today upstairs at Chongno 6-ch'ŏme. Today, they are not chicks. They are big chickens. Big chickens that flap their wings and crow.<201>

**Summary**

This article recounts the founding and early history of the Korean theater troupe "Arang" (阿娘). Formed in August 1939 by members leaving the Tongyang Theater after a management change, the troupe, led by figures like director Pak Chin and playwright Im Sŏn'gyu, debuted on Ch'usŏk day that year. By early 1941, the troupe had performed extensively throughout Korea, staging around ten plays (many by Im Sŏn'gyu) for approximately 400 days. The article concludes by noting their current preparations for a significant performance commemorating the formation of the Chosŏn Theater Association. For historians, this piece offers valuable insight into the dynamics of the colonial Korean theater world, including personnel movement between troupes (Tongyang, Ch'ŏngch'unjwa, Hwahwasŏn, Arang), the names of key practitioners (Pak Chin, Im Sŏn'gyu, Hwang Ch'ŏl, etc.), the repertoire performed, and the organizational efforts within the arts community under Japanese rule, specifically the establishment of the Chosŏn Theater Association. The troupe's name "Arang" itself might also be significant, potentially referencing a traditional Korean legend.

劇團阿娘小史

昭和 14年 8월 東洋극장의 경영자가 바뀌이매 전경영자 崔象德씨에 殉하는 동지들은 손에 손을 잡고 東劇을 하직하였다. 울며 하직하였다. 정처 없이 헤매이다가 某有志의 힘을 빌어 鍾路 6丁目 116번지 2층 다락 우에 에미 잃은 병아리 모양으로 오굴오글 뫃여 앉었다.
여기에 뫃인 사람들을 대강 꼽아 보면 연출에 朴珍, 작가 林仙圭, 장치 元雨田, 그리고 靑春座와 豪華船으로부터 빠저나온 연기진영으로는 黃澈, 徐一星, 梁白明, 金斗燦/pers>, <pers>孟晩植 외에 十數을 넘는 男優와 車紅女, 朴永信/pers>, <pers>金仙草, 文貞福, 李正順, 嚴美花 등 역시 十指로 꼽을 만한 女優이었다.
이곳에서 응아하고 탄생한 것이 阿娘이다. 아랑의 생일은 달 밝고 바람 맑은 中秋佳節이다. 추석날 大邱극장에서 林仙圭 作 인생극장을 가지고 탄생공연을 가젔으니 이제는 돌도 훨신 지나서 동양 나이로는 세 살이다 제 손으로 밥도 먹고 뛰기도 한다. 건강만점이다. 탄생공연 후로 未亡人, 金玉均, 바람 부는 시절 등 林仙圭 作을 비롯하야 10여 편의 각본을 소화하고 한 돌이 조금 지나는 동안에 중앙과 지방에서 가진 공연은 양으로 보아도 무려 400일이다.
1월 27일부터 府民舘에서 가질 朝鮮연극협회 결성기념공연 준비에 바뿐 그들은 오늘드 鍾路  6丁目 階上에 뫃였다. 오늘의 그들은 병아리가 아니다. 큰 닭이다. 홰를 치고 우는 큰 닭이다.<201>
