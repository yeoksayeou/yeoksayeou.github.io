Anthology of Western Province Local Poems
Just as there are unique poems and songs of the Kansai region in the Western Provinces (西道), the Yeongnam region (嶺南) has its Yeongnam style, and the Kwanbuk region (關北) has poems and songs fragrant with the Northern style. Now, we have decided to receive and publish poems deeply imbued with local color from poets originating from that region [the Western Provinces].

Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk Road (Western Province Mood)

Chu Yohan

The Taedong River ferryboat,
Do not board it.
The strains of the Susimga,
I cannot bear to hear.

O boatman of Taenggyŏng Gate,<260>
Do not hurry the way.
At Ch'ŏngnyu Cliff in the evening sun,
A river wind rises.

The name Susangsŏn (수상선 - Water-Top Boat)
is fine too, but
Looking closely, I see it's a pitch-black
coal barge.

When the sluggish pine raft
comes down,
Even the willows of Nŭngna Islet
are excited.

Behind Moran Hill,
The Ch'ŏngnyu Cliff road,<261>
Where it ends,
is the tavern district.

On the left hand,
a field of young pines;
On the right hand,
the vast Taedong Plain.

In the young pine field,
locusts swarm everywhere;
The Taedong River twists and turns, passes
Mandalsan Mountain.

Walking the lower path,
Hey, you donkey driver there,
Clanging and clanking,<262>
Isn't walking alone lonely?

That Ch'ŏngnyu Cliff road,
Holding the autumn grass,
The time when my weeping love wept,
I long for it.

Scholar of Sŏngch'ŏn,
Boast not of your Autumn Wind song.
Right now, the Sŏdo maiden
Is making bird sounds.

Ch'ŏngnyu Cliff road, Ch'ŏngnyu Cliff road,
The name is sorrowful.
Since old love has gone,
Let's seek a new love.<263>

Snowy Night

Kim Ansŏ

Outside, snow.
On a night when snowflakes furiously pour down,
Even the whining children have fallen asleep before long.
Whom does this footstep search for, wandering the streets?

The person I completely forgot and turned away from<264>
Would surely not come again on this snowy, sleeping night, yet
Receiving the white snowflakes, flake by flake,
Why can I not suppress these confused thoughts?

Ah, should I just flop down right here on the ground?
Should I stay up like this through the whole night?
Should I embrace a stone and pour out this heart?

As snowflakes pour down, flake by flake,
They grip this heart, expose it, make it shudder,
"Even the affection of fifteen years was all a dream;
What can't be trusted is this human heart, melting like snow."<265>

Siblings

No Ch'ŏnmyŏng

The dawn when my older sister, weeping and weeping, scolded me
Through the night,
Was like receiving "Holy Absolution" (聖赦).
Tears rained down my thin cheeks.

Even now, thinking of that time makes my eyes burn.

The day my sister, whom I missed, left,
Folding a thousand leagues (千里) into one, I didn't know the distance.<266>

The South where persimmon trees stand red by every house,
The speech is rough, seeming like a foreign land,
But because my sister lives there,
That place is always yearned for in my heart—

Today too, a long letter came from the South.
Reading it again and again, the sorrowful tidings:

"Are you warming the room properly?
Leaving you all alone,
On nights when the wind is cold and the glass doors (瑠璃門) cry, I cannot sleep."

Still holding the durumagi coat,
Tears welled up.<267>

Ripe Persimmon

Ch'unsŏng

Dyeing thin arrowroot vines yellow, blue, and red,
In a finely woven flower basket,
What is the meaning of sending a basketful of redly ripened sangt'u persimmons?

Having grown, beautifully absorbing rain and dew,
Is it that you give me your heart, like that persimmon,
Wholly,<268> right from the stem?

Burned red by the sunlight all summer long,
Is it that you pour your passion, like that persimmon,
Unsparingly onto me?

Hanging abundantly beside the wall of your house,
When those sangt'u persimmons shone like rubies (紅寶石),
My heart wanted to pick one or two.
The persimmons are welcome, and the heart that sent them even more so.<269>

A Small Scene at the Sandpit

The sand is white, white like snow.
I walk barefoot. Footprints, footprints appear.
Walking, I write letters with my hand.
"I will always love my beloved."

Indigo water rushes like a snake
And erases the written letters; the wind also blows.
White seagulls fly like flags.

In the blue sky, like ducks floating on the lake's heart,
Beautiful clouds bloom white and drift along.
On the river water, a yellow sailboat glides.<270>

I am a small seagull.
Splash, splash, I swim in the water.
And floating on the water, I write letters in the sky.
"When will my beloved come?"
(End)

Street with a Hot Spring

Chang Man-yŏng

A street where a warm spring gushes, pungent with the smell of sulfur (琉黃).
At night, fog descends like rain.
Mountains, fields, everything buried in the dense fog.
The voices of Gisaeng (妓女) being called to the inns (旅舘),
The horns of the rickshaws (일력거) carrying the Gisaeng.
Late into the night, until dawn,
I have no sleep at all.<285>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of six poems by five different poets, framed as representative works capturing the local color of Korea's Western Provinces (Seodo, primarily P'yŏngan Province area) in 1941. The poems explore themes of nostalgia for specific locales like P'yŏngyang's Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk, melancholic reflections on love and loneliness (often set against natural backdrops like snow or rivers), the warmth of familial bonds, the symbolic interpretation of nature (persimmons), and observations of local life, such as a bustling hot spring town. From a historical perspective, the publication itself in Korean, focusing on regional identity through "local color" poetry during a period of heightened Japanese assimilation pressure (1941), is noteworthy, potentially representing a subtle form of cultural preservation. The depiction of elements like Gisaeng, rickshaws, and specific regional names provides glimpses into the social landscape and cultural geography of colonial Korea.

西道에 關西獨特의 詩歌가 있고 嶺南에는 嶺南調의 또 關北에는 北方調의 香氣 흐르는 詩歌가 各各있는 법이외다. 이제 우리는 그 지방출신의 詩客들로 더부러 鄕土色이 진하게 무르녹는 詩篇들을 받어 실기로 하였슴니다.

淸流壁街道(西道情調)

주요한

대동강 나룻배
타지 마소.
수심가 가락을
차마 드르리.

댕경문 뱃사공아<260>
길 재촉 마라라.
청류 벽 석양에
강바람 난다.

수상선 이름도
조치마는
알고 보니 새깜안
석탄 배라.

찌꾸둥 솔깡배
나려올 제는
능라도 버들도
제흥이로다.

모란봉 뒤
청류벽 가도야<261>
끝난 데는
주막거리로구나.

왼손에는
애솔밭이요.
바른손은
후얼적 대동벌이라.

애솔밭엔
황충이 천지요.
대동강 구비구비 흘터
만달산이라.

아렛길 것는
여보, 저 나귀장수
왈그랑 절그랑<262>
혼잣길 설찌안쏘?

청류벽 그 가도야
가을풀 잡고
우시든 님 우시든 때
그립수에다.

성천 선비야
추풍곡 자랑마라.
시방은 서도 색시
새소리 한다.

청류벽가도, 청류벽가도
이름 섧고나
옛사랑 가섰으니
새사랑 차자보자.<263>

눈오는 밤

金岸曙

밖에는 눈.
눈송이 펄펄 퍼붓는 밤의,
보채든 아이들도 어느덧 잠을 들고
누구를 찾아 이 발길이 거리를 헤매는고.

내닞노라 싹 쓸고 돌아선 사람<264>
잠든 이 눈밤에 두 번 다시 올 바 안연만
송이송이 흰눈을 그대로 받으며
어즈럽은 생각을 무어라 못내 禁는가.

아하, 바로 이대로 땅 우에 펄떡 주저 앉을가.
이 한밤을 요대로 깡그리 새여를 볼가.
돌을 안고 이 心思 하소를 해볼가.

송이송이 흰눈을 펄펄 퍼부며
이 마음을 붓잡고 들추고 몸서리치네,
「열 다섯해 지낸誼도 모도 다 꿈의
못믿을 건 요 人心 눈이런듯 녹고 만다고.」<265>

同氣

盧天命

언니가 울며 울며 나를 꾸지저
밤을 밝히든 새벽은
「聖赦」을 받는 것 같아
내 야윈 뺨엔 눈물이 비오듯했다.

지금도 그때일 생각하면 눈이 뜨거워

언니가 보고 지워 떠나가는 날은
千里길을 주름잡아 먼 줄을 몰라<266>

감나무 집집이 빠알간 南쪽
말들이 거시여 異邦도 같것만
언니가 산대서
그곳은 늘상 마음에 그리운 곳-

오늘도 南쪽에서 온 기인 편지
읽고 읽으면 구슬픈 사연들

「불이나 뜨뜨시 때고 있는지
외따로 너를 혼자 두고
바람이 차 瑠璃門들이 우는 밤엔 잠이 안온다」

두루마지를 잡은 채
눈물이 피잉 도랐다.<267>

紅柿

春城

가는 싸리에 노랑 파랑 빨강 물드려
고이 역근 꽃바구니에
붉케 연든 샹투감을 한바구니 보내신 뜻은?

비 이슬 곱게 먹고 자란
그 감같은 님의 心臟을
송토리 채 내게 주신단 말슴입니까?

여름내 해빛에 타고 붉은<268>
그 감같은 님의 情熱을
내게 아낌없이 쏜는단 말슴입니까?

당신 집 담 옆에 우렁주렁 매달려
그 샹투감이 紅寶石 처름 빛날때
한두개 따고싶든 이맘이외다
감도 고맙고 보내신 마음 더욱 고맙소.

砂場小景

모래는 히오 눈 온 듯 히오.
맨발로 것소. 자욱자욱 자욱이나오.
것다가 손으로 글자를 쓰오.
「난 님을 언제나 사랑한다고」<269>

쪽빛물이 배암처름 달려와
그 써논 글자를 지우오 바람도 부오.
힌 갈메기가 旗폭처럼 날아오우.

푸른 하눌엔 湖心에 뜬 오리같이
고은 구름이 히게 피어 둥둥 떠가고
江물우엔 노랑 돗단배가 밋그름치오.

나는 적은 갈메기오
첨벙첨벙 물 속에서 헴치오
그리고 물 우에 떠서 하늘에 글자를 쓰오.
「님은 언제나 오시느냐고」
(끝) <270>
