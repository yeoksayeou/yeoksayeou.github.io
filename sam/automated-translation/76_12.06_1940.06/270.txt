A Touching Story of Patriotism, (Miss Chŏng, Who Dedicated Her Body to the Sovereign State as an Angel in White in Place of a Brother Who Could Become a Volunteer Soldier)

At a time when the fever for volunteering for military service is rising daily across the peninsula, there is a young woman who resolutely stepped forward with an ardent spirit to serve the country loyally (盡忠報國), even as a woman, upon hearing the worries of her parents who lamented their inability to serve the nation because they had no son. She is Miss Chŏng Chŏng-sim (鄭正心), the 19-year-old eldest daughter of Mr. Chŏng Kŏn-mo (鄭楗謨), a farmer in Hwahak-ri (和鶴里), Purim-myŏn (普林面), Kangso-gun (江西郡), P'yŏngan Namdo (平南). She had always heard her father sighing,
"If only we had a son, I would have him volunteer. With only daughters, how can we serve the country!"

After hearing this, she pondered if there was any way for a woman to serve the country loyally. Finally, around October of last year, she resolved to become a nurse. Without informing her parents, she quietly purchased lecture materials and studied independently. However, as self-study in the rural village proved difficult in many ways, Miss Chŏng Chŏng-sim, firm in her resolve to volunteer as a nurse, went directly to the P'yŏngyang Police Station on March 9th last. There, she inquired about how to volunteer as a nurse, submitting a letter written in her own blood expressing her sincere devotion (赤誠). Deeply moved by the blood letter and the overflowing sincerity of the rural girl's pure heart, Assistant Police Inspector Matsukawa (松川警部補), the chief of the P'yŏngyang Station's Sanitation Section (衛生係) (currently the Police Affairs chief at Taedong Station (大同署)), and Constable Kimura (木村巡査) immediately replied,
"To become a nurse, it is faster to enter a nursing school and study rather than self-study. If you come to P'yŏngyang (平壤), we will take care of everything, so please come to P'yŏngyang once (1次 來壤)."

Receiving this letter, Miss Chŏng leaped for joy and sought her parents' permission. Her parents, having read the newspaper articles about their daughter's blood letter and determination, readily consented. Afterwards, Miss Chŏng (continued on page 144)<192> came to P'yŏngyang, visited the P'yŏngyang Station, and under the kind guidance of Constable Kimura, learned various ways to prepare for the entrance examination. They had even arranged in advance for her to become a trainee (견습생) at a certain hospital should she fail the exam. Both Miss Chŏng and Constable Kimura kept secret the fact that she was the woman of sincere devotion (赤誠) who had written the blood letter, and she took the exam. Her burning patriotic sincerity (赤誠) finally came to fruition; despite fierce competition, she passed with ease, taking the first step toward becoming the angel in white she yearned (憧憬) for. It is said that Miss Chŏng works cheerfully every day. <144>

**Summary**

This article reports on Chŏng Chŏng-sim, a 19-year-old woman from a rural area in P'yŏngan Namdo, who is inspired to become a nurse to serve the Japanese Empire ("Sovereign State"). Lacking a brother to become a volunteer soldier (支援兵), and motivated by her father's expressed regret and her own patriotism (盡忠報國), she writes a letter in blood to the P'yŏngyang police expressing her desire. Impressed by her sincerity (赤誠), the police officials help her prepare for and pass the nursing school entrance exam. The article portrays her story as a touching example of female patriotism fulfilling a familial and national duty. For historians, this piece is interesting as it clearly reflects Japanese colonial propaganda efforts in the late 1930s/early 1940s, promoting loyalty and service to the Empire, even among women, framed within traditional notions of filial piety and substituting for male military duty. The use of terms like 盡忠報國, 君國, and 赤誠, alongside the narrative structure emphasizing sacrifice and imperial service, highlights the ideological climate being fostered.

愛國 美談, (白衣의 天使로 志願兵될 오빠 없는 代身에 君國에 몸을 바친 鄭孃)
반도의 지원병熱이 날로 높아 가고 있는 이때에 자기 집에 아들이 없어서 나라를 위해 힘 쓸 수가 없음을 한탄하는 부모의 근심을 듣고 비록 여자의 몸으로라도 盡忠報國하겠다는 열렬한 정신으로 분연히 나선 처녀가 있다. 그는 平南 江西郡 普林面 和鶴里에서 농사하는 鄭楗謨씨의 장녀 鄭正心양으로 금년 19세의 어린 몸이나 항상 그 아버지가
『우리 집에도 사내자식이 있으면 지원병을 시킬텐데. 딸 자식으로마는 나라를 위할 수가 없으니 어떻거나!』
하고 탄식하는 소리를 듣고는, 여자의 몸으로는 盡忠報國할 길이 없을까 하고 생각하던 끝에 작년 10월경에 간호부가 되기를 결심한 후 부모에게는 알리지 않고 가만히 강의록을 구입하여 독학하여 왔었는데 농촌에서는 독학하기가 여러가지로 매우 불편하므로 간호부 지원의 결심이 굳은 鄭正心양은 지난 3월 9일 平壤경찰서로 직접 그 赤誠의 뜻을 피력한 혈서와 함께 간호부 지원의 방법을 문의했던 바 그 혈서와 및 진심으로 끓어넘치는 농촌 처녀의 순정에 감격한 平壤署 衛生係주임 松川警部補(현재는 大同署 警務주임)와 木村巡査는 즉시로
『간호부가 되는 데는 독학보다도 간호부 양성소에 입소하여서 공부하는 것이 빠르니 만일 平壤에 오기만 하면 모든 것을 돌보아 줄 터임으로 1차 來壤하라』
는 회답을 하였던 것이다.
이 편지를 받은 鄭양은 기뻐 날뛰며 부모에게 허락을 구했더니 부모도 新聞紙上에 발표된 딸의 혈서와 결심의 기사를 읽고 쾌히 허락했던 것이다. 그 후 鄭양은(144頁에 계속)<192> 平壤에 와서 平壤署를 방문하고 친절한 木村巡査의 안내로 입소시험 준비의 방법을 여러가지로 배우고 있었으며 만일 不各格되는 때에는 견습생이라도 되도록 어떤 병원에 미리 소개까지 하였었다. 그리고 鄭양이나 木村巡査는 혈서까지 썼던 赤誠의 여자임을 숨기고 수험했던 바 불타는 애국의 赤誠은 드디어 이루워지게 되어 경쟁이 심함에도 불구하고 유유히 합격되어 憧憬의 백의천사의 제일보를 밟게 되었다. 그리고 鄭양은 매일 명랑하게 일한다고 한다. <144>
