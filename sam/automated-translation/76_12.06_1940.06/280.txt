Demand for a Ticket System Among Intellectual Women

With the appointment of Ms. Yoshioka Yayoi and Ms. Kōra Tomiko as new members of the Ministry of Commerce and Industry's Central Committee on Price Formation (商工省價格形成中央委員會), joining the incumbent Ms. Yamada Waka, the number of women representatives addressing the price issue has become three. On the 16th at 2:45 PM, a "Meeting Regarding Hopes for the Price Formation Committee," primarily featuring these three members and hosted by the Tokyo United Women's Association (東京聯合婦人會), was held on the 8th floor of the Sanshin Building (三信삘딩).

Attendees, besides the three committee members, included representatives from various women's groups such as Kiuchi Kiyo, Principal of Shimura Daiichi Elementary School (志村第一小學校長); Ōtsuma Kotako, Principal of Ōtsuma Girls' High School (大妻高女校長); Kawaguchi Aiko, Principal of Koishikawa Girls' High School (小石川高女校長); Ōe Sumi, Director of Tokyo Kasei Gakuin (東京家政學院長); Ideno Ryūko, Director of Ōfūkai (櫻楓會理事長); Furukawa Yae, Advisor to the Great Japan United Women's Association (大日本聯合婦人會囑託); and, as the sole man among them, Mr. Hongō Hisatsugu, Chief of Section 2, Price Bureau, Ministry of Commerce and Industry (商工省物價局 제2부장 本鄕壽次氏), totaling about 40 people. First, Mr. Hongō explained the role of the Price Formation Committee. Then, the three committee members stated, "We have been given a very difficult role and are concerned, but from now on, we will truly exert all our strength and act as spokespersons for women, without regard for whether someone is a soldier or an official." Soon after, the "Petitions to the Committee" began.

First, Ms. Kiuchi Kiyo spoke, describing the plight of female teachers. Then, Ms. Ōtsuma Kotako, citing various examples, emphasized the need for essential goods (必需品) to be reliably rationed. Following this, various opinions were expressed by Ms. Yabuki Toyo, representing housewives, Ms. Ōe Sumi, and others. Responding to these, committee member Ms. Yoshioka Yayoi said, "The ticket system (切符制度) is utterly impossible to implement in Japan without proper coordination between the various ministries (各 省). It's regrettable that the government is just imitating Germany (獨逸) without understanding the actual situation." Committee member Ms. Kōra Tomiko then stated,

"We cannot control prices by our own power, but we must not give up; we must assert the consumer's standpoint. Production and consumption, urban and rural areas, have conflicting interests (利害相反), but if the government explains the situation well, mutual reflection (相互反省) can lead to progress. Given Japan's pride in its complete family register (戶籍) system and comprehensive police network (警察網), I believe implementing the ticket system (切符制度) would not be impossible." Thus, heated opinions were voiced regarding the price issue. Finally, Mr. Hongō offered an explanatory defense regarding these demands, and the meeting adjourned after 5 PM. Overall, the call for a ticket system is loud among intellectual women.<193>

**Summary**

This 1940 news report from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* details a meeting held in Tokyo by the Tokyo United Women's Association. Attended by prominent Japanese female educators, association leaders, and three female members of the Ministry of Commerce and Industry's Price Formation Committee, the discussion centered on wartime price controls and shortages. Attendees strongly advocated for the implementation of a rationing or "ticket system" (切符制) for essential goods, citing hardships faced by consumers, particularly women like teachers. While one committee member expressed skepticism about the government's ability to implement such a system effectively compared to Germany, another argued Japan's robust administrative infrastructure (family registry, police network) made it feasible. The article highlights the growing demand among Japan's intellectual female circles for more direct government intervention in managing wartime scarcity. For historians, this article offers insight into the socio-economic anxieties in wartime Japan, the emerging public role of prominent Japanese women discussing national policy, and the specific debates around rationing systems. Its publication in a Korean magazine underscores how news from the Japanese metropole was disseminated within the colony, reflecting the integrated yet hierarchical nature of the empire.

智識 女性間에는 切符制를 要望한다
商工省價格形成中央委員會 新任委員 吉岡彌生 高良富子 爾여사와 또 留任하고 있는 山田와까 여사를 加하여 물가문제에의 婦人代辯者는 3인이 되었는데 16일 오후 2시 45분부터 三信삘딩 8階에서 이 3인을 主로 한 東京聯合婦人會 주최 「價格成委員會에 희망하는 會」가 개최되었다.
출석자는 3위원 외에 志村第一小學校長 木內기요, 大妻高女校長 大妻고다子, 小石川高女校長 河口愛子, 東京家政學院長 大江스미, 櫻楓會理事長 出野柳子, 大日本聯合婦人會囑託 古川八重 등의 각 婦人圖體 代表 중에 黑一點, 商工省物價局 제2부장 本鄕壽次씨까지 40여 명, 먼저 本鄕씨의 價格形成委員會에 관한 설명이 있은 다음, 3위원으도부터 「매우 어려운 역할을 받은 만큼, 걱정스러우나, 인제부터 정말 힘을 다하여 군인이거나 관리거나 헤아리지 않고 부인의 대변자로서 활약하겠다」고 말하고 이윽고 「위원에의 請願」이 시작되었다.
제일 먼저 木內기요-씨가 말을 끄내어, 여교원의 悲境을 고한 즉 大妻고다까씨는 여러 가지 실례를 들어서 필수품만은 반드시 배급해 달라 강조, 다음에 定庭부인 대표로서 矢吹도요씨, 大江스미氏 등으로부터 여러 가지 의견이 나왔는데 위원의 吉岡彌生씨 여기에 답하여, 切符制度는 日本과 같이 각 省의 연락이 잘 되지 않고서는 도저히 실행할 수 없다. 정부는 실정은 모르고 獨逸의 흉내만 내는 것이 안되었다」고 말하니, 위원의 高良富子씨는
「물가는 우리들 힘으로 어떻게 할 수 없고 단념말고, 소비자의 입장을 주장하지 않어선 안 된다. 생산과 소비 都會와 농촌에선 利害相反해 있으나 정부가 실정을 잘 설명한다면 상호반성하여, 진행될 것이다. 切符制度도 호적의 완전, 경찰망의 완비를 자랑하는 日本에서 한다면 못할 것도 없지 않으리라 믿는다」라고 말하는 등, 각각 붉은 氣焰을 토하여 물가문제에 이르렀고 최후로 이들 注文에 대하는 本鄕씨의 변명적 설명이 있어서 5시 지나 散會했는데 대체로 지식여성 사이에는 切符制 요망의 소리가 높다.<193>
