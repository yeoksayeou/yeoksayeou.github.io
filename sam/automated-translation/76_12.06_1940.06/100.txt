The Volunteer Soldier System and Hopes for the Peninsular People
						By Ho Hun, Army Major, Chosŏn Army Information Department

In general, for those who enjoy life in Our Imperial Nation (我皇國), facing national defense is a duty based on the equality of the four classes of people; thus, military service (兵役) is not only an obligation for the people but also a precious and honorable right. Consequently, Our Army (我軍隊), as the Imperial Army under the august command of the Emperor, and simultaneously as the national army, shines even more brilliantly.

The Emperor, with awe-inspiring reverence, has declared the officers and soldiers to be His limbs (股肱). The officers and soldiers, looking up to the Emperor as the Head (頭首), uphold the traditional spirit of loyalty and service to the nation (殉忠報國) [Translator's Note: Original text indicates "2 lines of Japanese follow here," but they are not provided in the source.] Starting with the Sino-Japanese and Russo-Japanese Wars, and through incidents like the Manchurian Incident and the current Holy War, they have established numerous military merits. Herein lies the fundamental purpose of the establishment of Our Imperial Army, and indeed, the prosperity of the Imperial Nation cannot but be attributed to this.

Therefore, the implementation of the Army Special Volunteer Soldier Ordinance (陸軍特別志願兵令) in Chosŏn, opening a path for fellow countrymen of the peninsula to take on the duty of national defense, is truly something to be celebrated endlessly.

It is almost too awe-inspiring to mention that the adoption of this glorious volunteer soldier system stems from the sacred consideration (聖慮) of impartial benevolence (一視同仁) demonstrated by Emperors throughout the ages, starting with Emperor Meiji. However, regarding the fact that the fellow countrymen of the peninsula have come to bear the supreme and greatest duty of national defense as subjects of the Imperial Nation, I believe they must deeply reflect on its significance and truly renew their resolve.

While I rejoice wholeheartedly that through this system, a most vigorous step forward has been taken in the realization of Japan-Korea Unity (內鮮一體), I would like to list the points I especially wish to emphasize to you, gentlemen, hoping for a moment<74> of reflection and urging you to respond faithfully to the sacred consideration behind the establishment of this system.

1.  Although this system is not the application of a complete conscription law, once one bears the honor of military service through volunteering, the treatment of one's status and service is identical and without discrimination compared to soldiers enlisted through general conscription. Furthermore, the path to promotion to non-commissioned officer or officer is also open, making it decisively different from the colonial armies of European nations.

2.  Among some individuals, there are those who speak harshly about this current system, even mentioning political participation rights. However, such talk not only violates the essence of the Imperial Army and ignores the purpose behind the enactment of the Volunteer Soldier Ordinance, but also, stepping further, greatly defiles the noble spirit and pure heart of the young fellow countrymen of the peninsula who intend to take on the duty of national defense.

3.  The fellow countrymen of the peninsula should exert their full capabilities through this system to the highest degree, thereby looking upward to respond faithfully to His Majesty's sacred consideration, while looking downward to strive not to disappoint the expectations of the 100 million fellow countrymen of Japan and Korea. Thus, the success of this system's implementation should serve as a wedge to pioneer the appropriate momentum for its expansion, and furthermore, I believe the resolve is necessary to make it the key (鐽錀) that clearly demonstrates to the world that our fellow countrymen of the peninsula are possessors of the qualities capable of fulfilling any duty as subjects of the Imperial Nation.

4.  Looking comprehensively at the situation of applicants from the past two recruitment rounds, it is truly regrettable that the number of applicants from middle-class or higher households is so negligible that one could almost say there were none.
    At the very least, military service in Our Nation (我國) should be the pride and principal duty of any man born into the Imperial Nation, regardless of wealth or social standing.
    Considering this, how can those from middle-class or higher households, who acknowledge themselves as the core class of the peninsula and stand in leadership positions, face the masses without producing volunteers? Not only will the populace not follow without leading by example, but will they truly have the face to meet the young fellow countrymen who will serve in the military, train their minds and bodies, become fine soldiers and good citizens, and return to their hometowns? I cannot suppress a deep sense of apprehension (危懼). I desire that this point be deeply considered.<75>

**Summary**

This editorial by Ho Hun, an Army Major in the Chosŏn Army Information Department, strongly advocates for Korean participation in the newly established Army Special Volunteer Soldier system in 1940. He frames military service not just as a duty but as an honorable right granted through Imperial benevolence, signifying progress towards *naisen ittai* (Japan-Korea Unity). Ho argues that service under this system offers equal treatment and opportunity compared to Japanese conscripts, dismissing criticisms and linking participation to proving Koreans' worthiness as Imperial subjects. He expresses significant disappointment and concern over the lack of applicants from middle and upper-class Korean families, urging them to lead by example for the sake of national unity and future legitimacy.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing for several reasons. It starkly illustrates the Japanese colonial authorities' (and collaborating Koreans') efforts to mobilize Koreans for the war effort under the banner of Imperial loyalty and unity, portraying military service as a privilege rather than coercion. The author's specific criticism of the Korean elite's reluctance to volunteer highlights internal class dynamics and potential resistance or apathy within colonized society, even among those ostensibly in leadership positions. It serves as primary source evidence of the propaganda used to encourage voluntary enlistment before full conscription was imposed on Koreans later in the war.

志願兵 制度와 半島人에 希望함
						朝鮮軍報道部 陸軍少佐 蒲勳
무릇 생을 我皇國에 享한 것은 四民 평등으로 국방에 임하는 것임으로 兵役은 국민에 대하여 의무인 동시에 귀중하고도 명예스러운 권리이다. 그리하여 我軍隊는 천황의 御統率하옵시는 황군으로써 동시에 국군인 점에서 더욱 광휘를 발하는 것이다.
천황께서 황공하옵게도 장병을 股肱이라고 宣하신 장병은 천황을 頭首에 仰奉하여 「(이하 2줄 일본어)」의 전통적 殉忠報國의 정신으로써 日淸, 日露 戰役을 始하여 혹은 滿洲事變에 혹은 今次의 聖戰에 幾多의 무훈을 수립하고 있는 것이다. 여기에 我皇軍의 건설의 本旨가 있고 황국 융성도 실로 여기에 있다고 하지 않을 수 없다.
고로 陸軍特別志願兵令이 朝鮮에도 시행되여 반도 동포들도 국방의 任에 當할 途를 개척하게 된 일은 실로 경축하여 마지 않는다.
이 광휘있는 지원병제도의 御採用이 明治천황을 비릇하와 역대 천황이 御敎示하옵신 一視 同仁의 聖慮에 기하는 것임은 말하기도 황송하옵거니와 반도 동포가 적어도 황국 신민으로서 至高至大한 국방의 임무를 負荷하게 된 것에 대해서는 깊이 그 의의를 三省하여 참으로 각오를 새로이 하지 않으면 안 될 줄로 생각한다.
나는 本 제도에 의해서 內鮮 一體의 구현에 가장 힘찬 일보를 내여 드디게 된 일을 衷心으로 기뻐하는 동시에 특히 제군에게 강조하고 싶은 점을 열거하여 편히 일단의<74> 반성을 원하며 本 제도 御制定의 聖慮에 응하여 받들기를 빌어 마지 않는다.
1. 本 제도는 완전한 병역법의 적용은 아니나 지원에 의하여 일단 병역의 榮譽를 질머진 후에는 그 신분의 취급 及 복역에 관해서 일반 징병에 의한 兵員과 동일 무차별한 것이며 또 하사관 혹은 장교에 진급하는 길도 열려 단연 저 歐洲 諸國의 식민지 군대와 같은 것과는 전혀 그 撰을 달리하고 있는 것이다.
2. 일부 人士간에는 今回의 本 제도를 가지고 直時 심하게 말하는 자는 참정권을 云謂하는 자가 있으나 그러한 것은 황군의 본질을 유린하고 또 지원병령의 제정의 취지를 몰각할 뿐만 아니라 한 걸음 나아가서 국방의 任에 當하려는 반도 청년 동포의 숭고한 정신과 순결한 심정을 汚毒하는 바 크다고 생각한다.
3. 반도 동포는 全能을 本 제도를 통하여 최고도에 발휘하여 우으로 폐하의 聖慮에 仰奉하는 동시에 아래로 內鮮 1억 동포의 기대에 어그러지지 않도록 기할 것이다. 그리하여 本 제도의 실시의 성과는 이를 확대하는 바 타당한 기운을 개척하는 楔도 되고 또 나아가서 我 반도 동포가 황국 신민으로서 여하한 임무에도 服할 수 있는 자질의 把持者인 것을 천하에 明證하는 바 鐽錀이 되게 할 각오가 필요하다고 생각한다.
4. 過般 2회의 지원자의 상황을 종합하여 보면 중류 이상의 가정으로부터는 지원자가 없다고 해도 관계가 없다고 까지 僅少한 것은 실로 유감이라고 생각한다.
적어도 我國의 兵役은 빈부 귀천의 차별이 없이 황국에 생을 받은 남자의 本職으로 자랑을 삼을 바이다.
이를 생각할 때 반도의 중견 계급으로 자인하고 스스로 지도적 지위에 서 있는 중류 이상의 가정에서 지원자를 내지 않고 어찌 대중에게 임할 수 있을가. 솔선수범 없이는 민중은 쫓아오지 않을 뿐 아니라 장래 병역에 服하여 심신을 단련하고 훌륭한 良兵 良民이 되여서 고향에 돌아온 청년 동포에게 대하여 과연 면목이 설 수 있을가. 심히 危懼의 念을 금할 수 없다. 이 점을 깊이 숙고해 주기를 원하는 바이다.<75>
