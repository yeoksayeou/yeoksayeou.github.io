Publication Date: 1940년 06월 01일
Article Title: 香怨 (Hyang'won) (Fragrant Resentment / Ch'unhyang's Lament)
Author: 月灘 (Wŏlt'an)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Fragrant Resentment 1

On Kwanghallu's red pillars, a white dream flows,
The water winding round Ojakkyo Bridge, warm, spits out the spring.
The grassy field spreads wide, a green blanket spanning ten thousand rooms.
The light green willows, trailing thread by thread,
Are a shimmering, shy, blue-green mist.
Suddenly I turn, and hear the oriole's song;
Truly, it seems sunk deep in spring sorrow.<148>

When will you return, oh when will you ever return?
My departed love, when will you ever come back?
The face mirror (面鏡) you casually gave me when we parted at Orijŏng Pavilion—
Its gleam reflects only my tear-streaked red eyes.

That night you came, the blue silk lantern swaying,
That night the sound of your *t'aesahye* shoes was so dignified,
Your booming voice, your trustworthy, bright eyes,
Brushing against the hem of your *topo* robe, the rustling *kŏmun'go* zither
Stood just there.
Empty now, I cannot sleep,
Unable to sleep, I cannot even dream.
Ah, even hearing the sound of wind scattering blossoms makes me think you are just arriving.<149>

Fragrant Resentment 2

My love, whom I counted the days waiting for, sends no word, lost to the distance,
The newly appointed magistrate drags me off, demanding I serve him.
Since this body was kept for my love, would I yield even in death?
Inside the Three Gates (三門), the red staff (朱杖), the heavy club (棍杖), even branding hold no fear for me.
Fiercely I will guard it, coldly I will keep it;
This single piece of loyal heart, forever and ever I will protect.

Fragrant Resentment 3

Wearing the cangue, entering prison, I wake with a start,<150>
Through the cold prison window (獄窓), the autumn wind blows high.
In the frost-laden empty sky where wild geese cry out,
A sliver of the waning moon is fiercely cold.
Eyes open, I see my love's face; eyes closed, I see my love's image.
All I hear is the rustling sound of falling leaves rolling.

Forget everything, close your eyes and forget it all.
Cleanly forget everything and just await death.
There can be no grudge or resentment;
Dreams and love, all are cast aside.
Only my heart, my heart like a cold, blue dagger (匕首),
Wishes to become that cold moonlight hanging in the sky.<151>

**Summary**

This three-part poem, titled "Fragrant Resentment," retells the famous Korean story of Ch'unhyang from her perspective. The first part expresses her deep longing for her absent lover, Yi Mongnyong, recalling their moments together amidst the beauty of spring at Kwanghallu Pavilion. The second part describes her defiance against the unjust demands of the new magistrate, affirming her unwavering fidelity even under threat of torture. The final part depicts her despair and resolution in prison, choosing to numb herself to suffering and transform her heart into something cold and sharp like the moonlight, awaiting death while preserving her integrity. For historians, the poem's publication in 1940 is significant. Retelling the Ch'unhyang story, a symbol of Korean virtue, loyalty, and resistance against corrupt authority, could function as a subtle form of cultural nationalism and expression of resilience during a period of intensified Japanese colonial assimilation policies.

香怨 1

廣寒樓 붉은 기둥엔 보이얀 꿈이 흐르고
烏鵲橋 감도는 물은 다수어라 봄을 뱉다.
잔디풀 좌르륵 萬間드리 靑담뇨다
실실이 드리운 軟頭빛 버들은
아믈아믈 수집은 푸른 안개라.
홀제 돌아보니 꾀꼬리 소리
짐짓 봄시름에 잠기웠구려.<148>

언제 돌아오리 어느 때나 돌아오리
가신 님 다시 어느 때나 도스시리
五里亭 이별할 때 넌짓이 주신 面鏡
빛이 나니 눈물 방울 서리운 붉은 내 눈 뿐이다.

靑紗초롱 흔들거려 찾아오던 그 밤이야,
태사신 끄는 소리 으젓하던 그 밤이야,
우렁찬 목소리 믿음직한 밝으신 눈,
님의 道袍자락에 스쳐, 스르렁 거리던 검은고
그저 그 곳에 섰다,
허전하고야 잠 못 이루오
잠 못 이루어 꿈도 못 뀌오,
아사사, 꽃 날리는 바람 소리만 들어도 님이 곧 오시는 양 하구료.<149>

香怨 2

손 꼽아 기다리던 님, 깜아득 소식 없고
新延 사ㅅ도 나를 끌어 수청 들라 잡아가오,
님 위해 지킨 몸이니 죽어진들 굴피리까,
三門안 朱杖 棍杖 단근질도 무섭잖소
매웁도록 지키오리 싸늘하게 지키리다
붉은 마음 한 조각은 길이 길이 지키리다.

香怨 3

칼 쓰고 옥에 들어 소스라처 깨여 보니<150>
싸늘한 獄窓엔 가을 바람 드높으오
기러기 울부짖는 서리찬 허공엔
조각 그믐달이 매섭도록 차겁구료
눈을 떠도 님의 얼굴 눈감아도 님의 모습
들리느니 바스스 落葉 구느 소리라.

萬事를 모두 잊소, 눈감아 모도 잊소
깨끗이 모두 잊어 죽음만 기다리오,
한과 원 있을 리 없고
꿈과 사랑 다 버리오,
오직 내 마음, 싸늘하게 푸른 비수(匕首)같은 내 마음
저 하늘에 걸린 차거운 저 달빛이 되랴 하오.<151>
