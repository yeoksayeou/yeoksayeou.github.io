Viewing the Berlin Olympics Film "Festival of Nations"
						Son Ki-chŏng

It was in Shōwa 11 (1936) that, receiving the overwhelming support of the people from my homeland, I participated in the 11th Olympic Games held in Berlin and won the laurel wreath of honor. This was a tearful moment of emotion for me, and now, four years later, to see those scenes again through film cannot but be a new kind of emotion.

There is one thing I would like to say here. If the 12th Olympic Games were to be held this year, I was determined not only to forgo employment but even marriage, dreaming of participating again and achieving consecutive victories. However, predicting that the 12th Olympic Games would likely not be held due to the outbreak of the Second European War, I proceeded with employment and marriage. Thus, my burning ambition ended in vain, which is incredibly disappointing, but I am dreaming of the future once again.

The 11th Olympics film is divided into Part 1 and Part 2. Part 1 is "Festival of Nations," and Part 2 is "Festival of Beauty."

This Olympia film was produced by the German government, which formed the Olympia Film Association with Ms. Leni Riefenstahl as the head of production, dedicating all efforts to filming the record of the Games. The entire German film industry was mobilized for this, and it is said that 44 first-rate cameramen were involved in the filming alone. It particularly showcased the essence of modern German camera technology. From recording to editing, Ms. Leni Riefenstahl spent over a year, and the 44 cameramen filmed from every possible three-dimensional angle.<132>

"Festival of Nations" (Fest der Völker) consists of 12 reels, starting from the lighting of the sacred flame on Olympia Hill (올림피아·쿠-로스丘) and concluding with the finish of the marathon, covering all track and field events. During filming, there was also aerial photography using balloons, i.e., bird's-eye view shots.

Musician An Pyŏng-so said he saw it twice in Germany before returning home and that it was very good. Kim Wŏn-gwŏn hyŏng also saw it last year during his expedition to Finland in Europe and said it was very good. Of course, this might partly be due to the emotional impact of seeing scenes of a fellow countryman's victory.

In any case, I too cannot suppress my emotions, and within this emotion lies the overflowing "love" from all of you, which I can never forget has always encouraged me, both then and now.

Due to space limitations, I cannot write both the review of the Olympia film "Festival of Nations" requested by Samch'ŏlli Sa and the memoir of my expedition journey to the Berlin Games together. Therefore, this time I will write about the Berlin Games expedition journey, and the review of "Festival of Nations" will be postponed to the next issue.

Hanyang, Farewell

Border Scene at Manjuri

It was June 4th, Shōwa 11 (1936). That afternoon at 3 o'clock, receiving a grand farewell from many teachers and public figures, I set off on the long road to Berlin.

Upon departure from Kyŏngsŏng, numerous thoughts and tearful emotions surged within my heart, but circumstances urged me forward, so I cannot record them one by one here. I will simply say, "I departed, my heart pounding, heading north, north." That night at eleven, seeing the late-June crescent moonlight shimmering on the roadside trees, I arrived in my hometown, Sinŭiju. Due to train connections, I continued straight to Andong County (安東縣), where I got off the train and met family and friends who had come to the station to see me off. The next morning, I disembarked in Pongch'ŏn, toured the city and the North Tombs (北陵), and then headed towards Harbin. En route, there was a grand welcome in Sin'gyŏng. Thus, the welcomes in various places were very effective in helping us forget the fatigue of travel. And each time, we keenly felt the weight of our responsibility.

After staying one night in Harbin and practicing a little, we headed again towards Manjuri. Along the railway line<133>, small mountains dotted the landscape, and deserts were also visible here and there. As the train approached Manjuri, the border atmosphere gradually intensified. Guards rode along on the train, and at night, all curtains were drawn. We arrived in Manjuri on the morning of the 9th. After a short rest at the inn, we went outside the city to practice, and the feeling at that time was indescribable. The fact that the two countries, Manchukuo and the Soviet Union (滿蘇 兩國), faced each other just a few *li* apart gave a strange sensation. Moreover, it is said that White Russians (白系 露人), seeing their homeland right before their eyes, were unable to return.

Many Women Laborers

Siberia, Repairing the Railway

On the night of the 9th, we crossed the border from Manjuri into Siberia. The Russian train was supposed to come to Manjuri, but the border customs inspection was indeed strict.

From then on, a monotonous journey crossing the Siberian plains continued for five days. The vast plain stretched endlessly, and the forests were simply astonishing. Apart from the large cities here and there, not many houses were visible along the line, but herds of cattle, horses, and sheep were everywhere in the fields. The railway track was much wider than in Chosŏn, and the scale was larger, but it was infuriating that the trains did not keep to the schedule. Sometimes the train stopped for 30 minutes, allowing us to get off onto the platform and practice.

Since we couldn't drink cold water, waiting for the train to arrive at a station to go get hot tea water (茶물) in a large bottle presented quite a peculiar sight.

The reason the schedule couldn't be kept was said to be due to the poor condition of the tracks, preventing the train from reaching the intended speed. Also, on the Siberian railway, there were few passenger cars and generally many freight cars. We passed Omsk on the afternoon of the 12th at 2 o'clock. Still, it was just the vast, seemingly uninhabited expanse of the Great Siberian plains; unlike Chosŏn's Kyŏngbu and Kyŏngŭi lines, one couldn't see anything that could be called a mountain.

Numerous laborers were seen working along the railway line, and among them, the sight of women repairing the railway tracks was particularly surprising.

Sightseeing in Moscow

From There to the War Zone, Berlin<134>

We arrived in Moscow on the night of the 14th, spent the night in the train compartment, and the next morning visited the embassy to request a guide for city sightseeing. As Moscow is the capital of Soviet Russia, various modern facilities were splendidly established. The large buildings and well-maintained roads were especially impressive. The Kremlin Palace, various temples, and the famous Red Square were all magnificent.

What particularly caught the eye were the faces of the country's politicians created with flowers in the parks. At 11 PM, we departed Moscow and headed for Poland. We crossed the Soviet-Polish (蘇波) border at noon the next day, the 16th, again undergoing a strict inspection. We arrived in the Polish capital, Warsaw, at 8 PM that day. After about two hours of sightseeing by car, covering the main sights, we departed for the actual war zone, Berlin, our hearts filled with tension.

Adoration for the Leader

Tens of Thousands in the Crowd, As If Wild

Crossing the Polish-German border in our sleep, the next day dawned, and on the morning of the 17th at nine o'clock, we finally arrived in Berlin. Guided by the embassy, we went straight to our inn. After spending several days scouting the course and sightseeing in the city, it was decided that we would enter the Olympic Village on June 21st.

Upon arriving at our destination, a sense of anxiety about the Games grew stronger, and I couldn't feel settled at all, so even sightseeing couldn't be done properly. The Olympic Village was truly a pleasant place. Facilities such as a practice track (400m), a cinema hall, and bathhouses were fully equipped, and cheerful interactions with foreign athletes made everything pleasant. Moreover, the accommodation, apparently newly built for future use as army barracks, was very convenient. Thus, becoming accustomed to the pleasant life in the Olympic Village, practicing little by little became the daily routine. It takes 40 minutes by bus from Berlin to the Olympic Village.

There, we cut ties with Berlin for the time being, immersed ourselves in this Village, and simply waited for the Games. The opening ceremony on August 1st, the marathon on the 9th, the closing ceremony on the 16th – the Games program proceeded smoothly. However, as details about the Games have already been extensively reported through newspapers and films, I will omit them here.

After the marathon was finally over, feeling as if a heavy burden<135> had been lifted, I could slowly tour the streets of Berlin. Everything operating under state control, moving like a single machine, seemed to be a major characteristic of Germany. The city's infrastructure also reflected this well.

Spacious, large-scale parks were established throughout the city, and the road system was most precisely regulated, with pedestrians, horse riders, cars, carriages, and trams all moving on separate paths, which was truly ideal. Also, the fact that all shops closed their doors and ceased business at 7 PM was something rarely seen elsewhere. Another characteristic of Germany worth noting is the fervent adoration among the people for Chancellor Hitler.

Not only do people greet each other with "Heil Hitler," but when Hitler appeared at the stadium, it was truly tremendous. The sight of tens of thousands of spectators filling the venue, raising their right hands high and shouting "Heil, Hitler!" was truly spectacular.

Competing Against Denmark

Flying Through the Clouds

On August 19th at 10 AM, we went to the Berlin airfield and, along with 5 or 6 fellow athletes, headed for Denmark. Flying high in the sky, passing through the clouds towards an unknown country, was truly exhilarating. After flying for about 2 hours, we landed in the capital, Copenhagen, around noon. The city was a port built on one side of an island, a neat and beautiful metropolis in every aspect. I had already heard that Denmark was a model agricultural country, but seeing the cooperatives, farms, and schools firsthand, I was again amazed by their systematic methods. On August 21st, there was a competition against Denmark, and the next day, August 22nd, at 11:30 AM, we departed from Copenhagen airfield and headed for Paris.

Paris Without Extravagance

Crossing the Dover Strait Again to London

After departing Copenhagen, we took a short break midway in Hamburg and arrived in Paris at 5:30 PM the same day. In Paris, too, guided by the embassy,<136> we toured the important places in the city. What was surprising was how different the atmosphere was from what I had previously heard. I had only thought of Paris as a very extravagant and glamorous city, but in reality, it was rather calm and composed. During my two stays in Paris, I visited most of the famous landmarks, among which the grandeur of the Eiffel Tower and the Arc de Triomphe was particularly astonishing.

After finishing the Japan-France competition in Paris on the 23rd, we departed Paris the next morning at 7 AM, heading straight for London. It took 5 hours by train to the French port of Calais (카레港), and the crossing of the Dover Strait (도-바 해협) by ship took two hours. Landing at the English port of Dover, it was about an hour's train ride to London. We disembarked in London at 3 PM, finished sightseeing the city that day, and departed London the next morning at 9 AM.

London, being truly one of the world's largest cities, its grand scale could only evoke admiration. We visited most of the famous sites like the River Thames (쥄스河), the Tower of London (倫敦塔), the Houses of Parliament (국회 의사당), Westminster Abbey (웨드민스타 사원), and the museums. A distinctive feature of London is that, unlike other European cities which have right-hand traffic, it has left-hand traffic like in Chosŏn. City planning was also excellent; while most major European cities are well-planned, London stood out for its cleanliness. Departing London as scheduled, we retraced our route via Dover-Calais and arrived back in Paris that afternoon at 5 PM.

Oh, Beloved Ancient Civilization

Clear Traces of the Great Roman Era

On August 27th at 6 AM, we took a train departing from Paris and headed for Italy. We crossed the French-Italian border around 6:30 PM that day. This area is enclosed by the Alps mountain range (알푸스 산맥), and the border crossing was through a tunnel that took about 20 minutes by train.

Around 8 PM that evening, we arrived in Turin and checked directly into a hotel. On the morning of September 1st at 10 AM, we took a train departing from Turin and headed for the capital, Rome. Arriving at our destination, Rome, that afternoon, we began sightseeing the city the next day with guidance from the embassy. Throughout the city, magnificent structures from the ancient Great Roman era were visible, and the abundance of Catholic temples was a major characteristic of Rome. Furthermore, the grandeur of Europe seemed to narrate how splendid Roman civilization once was.<137>

On September 3rd at 10 AM, we departed Rome, arrived at the port of Naples in the afternoon for a short rest, and at 3 PM, headed for Pompeii by car. Pompeii was a city buried by the eruption of a nearby volcano around the 1st century AD. Recently, it has been mostly excavated, preserving its old form and visually narrating the culture of that time. It was also extraordinary that the water systems used back then remained intact. Beyond the city, the volcano was still emitting smoke.

Finishing about 2 hours of sightseeing in Pompeii, we returned to Naples by car around 7 PM.

Six Days on the Indian Ocean

Autumn in Europe, Summer Here

After spending one night in Naples, on September 4th at 3 PM, we boarded the Italian ship Conte Rosso departing from Naples and set off on the journey back home. After three days of sailing through the Mediterranean Sea, we docked at Port Said (뽀-드, 사이드) in Egypt (埃及) on the night of the 7th, stayed overnight, and departed again the next morning. Passing through the Suez Canal (스에즈 운하), we arrived at the port of Suez (스에즈港) on the night of the 8th. The Suez Canal is said to be 161 km long, but because the water is shallow, the ship couldn't go at full speed, requiring 17 hours to pass through. After staying overnight in Suez, we departed from the port on the morning of the 9th. Docking at Aden at the entrance of the Red Sea (紅海) on the night of the 13th, we then completed another 6 days of sailing across the Indian Ocean and entered the port of Mumbai (孟買) in India on the morning of September 17th. The journey in between was extremely monotonous, with nothing but the vast ocean on all sides. What I particularly want to note is the heat of the Red Sea and the Indian Ocean. This kind of heat was the first I had ever experienced. When we left Europe, it already felt like autumn, but here it was high summer. During the day, the temperature reached 103 degrees [Fahrenheit]. Even with four fans running in the small cabin, sweat constantly flowed. Coming out onto the deck, the hot wind blowing from the sea was indescribably agonizing.

Hong Kong's Scenery, Full of Forts

Seeing Indian Culture Thoroughly After All

In Mumbai, due to a breakdown of the ship, we were scheduled to stay for a week.<138> This was quite disappointing for us, who wanted to return home as quickly as possible, but instead, we managed to finish sightseeing almost all of Mumbai. India generally seemed to have a lower level of culture than Chosŏn. Although there were relatively large buildings on the streets, shops were mostly barrack-style (빠락式). The city infrastructure, being managed by the British, seemed very advanced. Here, due to the heat, theaters and such reportedly opened from around 10 PM until 2 or 3 AM. After about a week's stay, the ship's breakdown was repaired, so on the morning of September 24th, we departed Mumbai, docked midway at Colombo, and entered the port of Singapore on the evening of the 27th. After some brief sightseeing and attending a welcoming reception there, we departed Singapore (新*坡) again at 12 PM and headed for Hong Kong. We arrived in Hong Kong early on the morning of October 1st, where there was also a welcome reception. What was particularly noticeable here were the numerous forts lined up everywhere and the many warships anchored in the harbor.

Last Night in Shanghai

Returning to Seoul by Airplane

Departing Hong Kong at 3 PM that day, we landed in Shanghai on the afternoon of the 3rd, stayed overnight, and on the afternoon of the 4th at 11 PM, we boarded a Canadian ship, landing in Nagasaki on the morning of the 6th. There, we attended a welcome reception, then took the 5 PM train from Nagasaki, arriving at Kobe port at 6 PM on the night of the 7th.

On the morning of the 8th at 9 AM, we finally arrived in Tokyo. The team disbandment ceremony was held that day. I left Tokyo on the 16th and returned to beloved Kyŏngsŏng by airplane on the afternoon of the 17th.

With this, I have roughly recorded my journey spanning four months. I intend to write about it in more detail when another opportunity arises.<139>

**Summary**

This article by Son Ki-chŏng, the 1936 Berlin Olympics marathon champion, begins with his reflections on watching the Olympic film "Festival of Nations" four years later, expressing disappointment that WWII prevented his participation in the potential 1940 Olympics. The main part is a travelogue detailing his journey from Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) to Berlin via Manchukuo, Siberia, Moscow, and Warsaw in June 1936, his experiences in the Olympic Village, brief post-competition travels through Denmark, Paris, London, Italy (Rome, Naples, Pompeii), and his return journey via the Suez Canal, India (Mumbai), Singapore, Hong Kong, Shanghai, and Japan (Nagasaki, Kobe, Tokyo), finally arriving back in Kyŏngsŏng by plane in October 1936.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as a first-hand account by a celebrated Korean athlete during the Japanese colonial period. His description of traveling as part of the Japanese delegation, the welcomes received (implicitly under Japanese auspices), his observations of Nazi Germany (including the Hitler cult), and his travels through various parts of the world offer insights into the complex position of colonial subjects on an international stage. His mention of fellow Koreans like An Pyŏng-so and Kim Wŏn-gwŏn, his pride in victory, and his concluding return to "beloved Kyŏngsŏng" subtly underscore his national identity despite the political circumstances. Historians might find value in his descriptions of travel infrastructure, cross-cultural observations, and the atmosphere in Europe on the cusp of war, all from a unique Korean perspective.

伯林 올림픽 映畵 『民族의 祭典』을 보고
						孫基禎
昭和 11년(1936년), 분에 넘는 故土 여러분의 성원을 받자와, 伯林에서 열린 제11회 오림픽 대회에 출정하여 영예의 월계관을 획득하였던 일은 나로서도 눈물겨웠던 감격이거니와, 이제 4년이 지난 오늘에 와서 나는 그 때의 광경을 필림을 통하여 다시 보게 된다는 것은 또한 한 새로운 감격이 아닐 수 없습니다.
저는 여기에서 한 마디 말씀드리고저 하는 것은 만일 금년에 제12회 올림픽 대회가 열리게 된다면 나는 취직은 무론, 결혼까지 하지 않을 결심이였었으며, 재차 출정하여서 연승을 꿈꾸었던 것이 제2차 歐洲 大戰의 발발로 도저히 제12회 올림픽 대회가 열릴 것 같지 않을 것을 예측하고 취직과 결혼을 하게 되었습니다. 이리하여 내 불 붙던 야망은 그만 수포로 돌아갔음은 섭섭하기 짝이 없는 일이나 나는 다시 앞날을 꿈꾸고 있습니다.
제11회 오림픽 영화는 제1부, 제2부로, 노니여 있는데 제1부는 『民族의 祭典』이고, 제2부는 『美의 祭典』입니다.
이 올림피아 영화는 獨逸 정부에서 레-니·리-펜슈타-루 여사를 제작 수반으로 하여 올림피아 영화 협회를 결성하여 대회 기록의 영화 채록에 전력을 다하기로 한 것입니다. 여기에는 獨逸 영화계가 총동원하였으며, 촬영만도 제1선의 카메라맨이 44명이라 합니다. 특히 현대 獨逸의 카메라의 정수를 다한 것으로, 그 수록으로부더 편집에까지 레-니·리-펜슈타-루 여사는 1개여 년이란 시간을 소비하였으며, 44명의 카메라맨은 총동원하여 모든 입체적 각도에서<132> 촬영한 것이외다.
『民族의 祭典』(Fest der Volker)은 전 12권으로 올림피아·쿠-로스丘의 성화로부더 시작하여 육상 경기 전부와 마라손의 결승으로 막을 닫게 됩니다. 그리고 촬영 중에는 기구에 의하여 공중 촬영, 즉 俯瞰 촬영도 있습니다.
음악가 安炳昭씨가 귀국하기 전에 獨逸에서 두 번이나 보았는데 그도 매우 좋더라고 말하며, 金源權형도 작년 歐洲 필랜드에 원정갔다가 보았는데 매우 좋더라고 합디다. 그것은 무론 같은 동포의 勝戰 장면이 있어서 감격했던 까닭도 있겠지요.
어쨌든 나로서도 감격을 不禁하는 바이며, 이 감격 속에는 여러분의 넘치는 『사랑』이 잠재하여서 그 때나 이 때나 늘 나를 격려하여 주고 있음을 잊을 길 없습니다.
지면 관계로 三千里社에서 부탁한 올림피아 영화 『民族의 祭典』의 감상기와, 伯林 대회 원정 여행 회상기를 다 함께 쓸 수가 없어서 금회는 伯林 대회 원정 여행기를 쓰기로 하고, 『民族의 祭典』 감상기는 차호로 밀리기로 합니다.

냉수를  먹을 수 없음으로 기차가 정차장에 도착되기를 기다려 큰 병에다가 茶물 가질너 가든 모양은 몹시 珍妙하었다.
이렇게 시간을 잘 지킬 수 없었음은 선로가 납바서 생각대로 속력을 낼 수 없었든 관계라고 한다. 또 西伯利亞 철도에는 객차는 적고 일반으로 화물차가 많었다. 「옴스크」를 지낸 것은 12일 오후 2시었다. 역시 가도가도 大西伯利亞의 無人之境 같은 광막한 평야 뿐으로 朝鮮의 京釜, 京義線 같이 산이라고 볼라야 볼 수 없었다.
철도 沿線에는 다수한 노동자가 일하는 것이 보였는데 그 중에도 부녀자가 철로 레-루 수선을 하고 있는 모양에는 오직 놀났다.

거기에서 伯林까지 와서 당분 緣을 끊고 이 村에 드려 잠기어 다만 대회를 기다리기로 하였다. 8월 1일 개회식, 9일 마라손, 16일 폐회식 등과 대회 푸로그람은 순조로 진행되었으나 대회에 대하여는 이미 신문 영화 등에 의하여 상세히 보도되었을 것임으로 여기서는 略한다.
마라손이 다 끝난 뒤 겨우 重荷를 버서 버리고 난 듯한 기분이 나서<135> 백림 시가로 천천히 구경할 수 있었다. 모든 것이 국가 통제의 下에 마치 한 개의 기계가 움지기 듯 움지기고 있는 것은 아마 獨逸의 일대 특색이라고 생각한다. 도시의 설비도 이를 잘 표현하고 있었다.
시가지 곳곳마다 너르고 대규모의 공원이 시설되어 있고 도로의 설비는 가장 규칙이 정확하여서 모다 보행차, 말탄 이, 자동차, 마차, 전차가 모다 各異한 길을 것고 있는 것은 실로 이상적이었다. 또 오후 7시가 되면 모든 상점이 모다 문을 다치고 휴업하는 것도 他處에서는 보기 드문 일이었다. 獨逸의 특색으로 더 한 가지 헤려하는 것은 국민간에 히틀러 총통의 숭배열이 왕성한 것이다.
개인간의 인사에도 「하일 히틀러」를 부름은 물론, 스타지움에 히틀러가 한번 나타나면 실로 굉장하다. 장내를 덥고 있는 수만의 군중이 오른편 손을 높이 들어 큰 소리로 「하일, 히틀러!」를 부르는 것은 실로 장관이었다.

大羅馬 時代의 歷歷한 자최

8월 27일 아츰 6시 巴里發의 기차로 伊太利에 향하였다. 佛利 국경을 넘은 것은 그날 오후 6시 반경 이 부근 일대는 알푸스 산맥에 마키여 국경은 기차로 약 20분이 걸리는 턴넬이 되어 있었다.
그날 오후 8시경 토리노-에 도착, 즉시 호텔에 들어갔다. 9월 1일 아츰 10시 토리노-發 기차로 首府 「羅馬」로 향하였다. 동일 오후 목적지인 「로-마」에 도착하여 그 이듬날 대사관의 안내로 시가 구경을 시작하였다. 市中 도처에 엣날 大羅馬 시대의 웅대한 건축물이 보이며 또 천주교의 사원이 많은 것은 羅馬의 일대 특색을 짓고 있었다. 더욱 「유-롭」의 장대한 품은 羅馬의 문명이 어떻게나 찬란하든 것을 이야기하고 있었다.<137>
9월 3일 아츰 열시 羅馬를 출발하여 오후에 나포리 항구에 도착하여 小憩하고 오후 3시 자동차로 폼페이에 향하였다. 폼페이는 부근 화산의 폭발에 의하여 기원 1세기경에 매몰되었든 도시로 근래는 거지반 모다 발굴되어 舊態를 남기고 있어, 당시의 문화를 목전에 이야기하고 있었다. 당시 쓰이고 있든 水道가 그대로 남기여 있는 것도 희한한 것이었다. 도시의 저쪽에는 아직도 화산이 연기를 뿜고 있었다.
약 2시간의 폼페이 구경을 내끊고 자동차로 「나포리」에 돌아온 것은 오후 7시경이었다.

印度洋上의 六日

歐洲선 가을이 여기는 여름

나포리에서 하로 밤을 밝힌 뒤 9월 4일 오후 3시, 아조 나포리 출범의 伊太利 기선 곤데로-스號를 타고 귀국의 길에 올렀다. 그로부터 3일간의 地中海 航行을 끝 마추고 7일 夜 埃及의 뽀-드, 사이드에 寄港, 일박하고 翌朝 다시 출범, 스에즈 운하를, 통과하여 8일 夜 스에즈港에 도착하였다. 스에즈 운하는 全長 161粁라 하는데 수심이 엿기 까닭에 십분 속력을 낼 수 없고 통과에 17시간을 요한다고 한다. 스에즈에 1박하고 9일 朝 同港을 출범, 13일 夜 紅海의 입구 아덴에 寄港, 다시 6일간의 印度洋 航行을 마친 뒤 9월 17일 아츰, 印度 孟買港에 입항하였다. 중도의 여행은 사면이 모다 망망한 대양으로 실로 단조를 극하였든 것이나 특히 記코저 하는 것은 아마 紅海 及 印度洋의 더위일 줄 생각한다. 이런 더위는 나는 나서 처음의 경험이었다. 歐羅巴를 출발할 때에 이미 가을 같아였는데 여기서는 아조 盛夏 白畫에는 103도까지 올나 가고 있었다. 적은 선실에 4개의 선풍기를 놓고도 땀이 작고 흘렀다. 갑판에 나와서 더운 열풍이 바다로 부러 오는 것이 무에라고 말 할 수 없이 괴로웠다.

砲臺 많은 香港 風景

印度 文化를 두루두루 본 뒤

孟買에선 기선의 고장 때문에 1주간 체류하기로 되어 하로라도 속히<138> 고국에 돌아가려 하는 우리들에게는 여간 실망이 아니었으나 그 대신 孟買의 구경은 거지반 완전히 끝내었다. 印度는 대체에 있어 朝鮮보다도 문화 정도가 나즌 듯이 보였다. 거리거리에는 비교적 큰 건축도 있었으나 상점 등은 대개 빠락式이었다. 도시 설비는 英國人의 경영이니 만치 매우 진보된 듯 하였다. 여기서는 더위 때문에 극장 등은 오후 10시경부터 오전 2, 3시까지의 사이에 開演한다고 한다. 약 1주일 체재 후 기선의 고장도 다 수선되었음으로, 9월 24일 아츰, 孟買를 출범하여, 중도 코롬보에 寄港하여 27일 오후 8시 싱가폴에 입항하였다. 거기에서 간단한 시가 구경과 환영회에 임한 뒤 오후 12시 다시 新*坡를 출발하여 香港으로 향하였다. 香港에 도착된 것은 10월 1일 아츰 早朝, 여기서도 환영회가 있었다. 여기에서 특히 눈에 뜨이는 것은 도처에 포대가 나열하여 있고, 또 많은 군함이 정박하고 있는 것이었다.

上海서 마즈막 一泊

서울에는 飛行機로 돌아오다

香港을 그날 오후 3시 출발 3일 오후 2시 上海에 상륙하여 하로 밤 자고 4일 오후 11시 카나다 기선을 타고 6일 아츰 長崎에 상륙하였다. 거기에서 환영회에 갔다가 오후 5시 長崎發 7일 夜 6시 神戶 입항.
8일 오전 9시, 아조 東京에 도착, 解團式은 그날 있었다. 나는 16일 東京을 떠나 비행기로서 17일 오후 2시 그리운 京城으로 돌아왔다.
이것으로 나의 4개월에 및이는 여행을 대강 기록했는데 다른 기회 보아 좀 더 자세히 쓰고저 하노라.<139>
