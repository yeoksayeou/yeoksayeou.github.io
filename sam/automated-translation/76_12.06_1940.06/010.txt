Confidential Room, Various Inner Workings of Our Society

Chosŏn Chaebŏl Advancing into Northern Manchuria

Mr. Cho Chun-ho Establishes 500,000 Won Company in Mudanjiang

Advancing into Forestry Products, Automotive, and Other Businesses

{Mudanjiang Dispatch} The attention of Chosŏn chaebŏl is concentrated on Eastern Manchuria. Major heavy industry plans, such as the Manchuria Rock Drill Co., Ltd. (滿洲 鑿岩機株式會社) and large ironworks in Mudanjiang, are already progressing. Now, another Chosŏn chaebŏl, Mr. Cho Chun-ho (趙俊鎬氏), is establishing a 500,000 won stock company to engage in a wide range of businesses including logging and timber sales, agricultural product and processed goods sales, automotive transport business management, sales of various tools and machinery, and trading of general domestic and foreign products. Registration procedures were reportedly completed on April 19th. It is said that once this is completed, it will create a major sensation in the Mudanjiang commercial world.
Furthermore, according to the company's plans, it will also operate a large brick factory in the Yehe (液河) area, becoming a great boon to the Eastern Manchurian construction industry, which has been suffering from a shortage of bricks.

Min Chaebŏl Also Operating Ironworks

Rumors of 200,000 Won Investment for Northern Manchuria Advance

Concurrent with the establishment of the Manchuria Rock Drill Company, a large ironworks company backed by the Min Chaebŏl (閔財閥) of Chosŏn is reportedly being established in Mudanjiang city. It is said that they have already acquired a certain ironworks from the Mudanjiang Railway and have begun partial operations. In the near future, they plan to form a 200,000 won company, expanding and strengthening the company's scale to dominate the Mudanjiang ironworks sector.<13>

*Ch'angssi* Fever Overseas and Domestically

Kando Governor Yi and Others Undergo *Ch'angssi*

Numerous *Ch'angssi* Among Korean Officials in Manchukuo

At a time when Governor-level officials within Chosŏn are successively undergoing *ch'angssi* (name changing), the movements of high-ranking Korean officials within Manchukuo have been closely watched. Recently, former Kando Provincial Governor Mr. Yi Pŏm-ik (李範益氏), Civil Affairs Bureau Chief Mr. Yu Hong-sun (劉鴻洵氏), and numerous other figures have successively undergone *ch'angssi*. Reporting comprehensively on the recent situation: Kando Provincial Governor Mr. Yi Pŏm-ik, signifying his past service as governor of both Ch'ungch'ŏng and Kangwŏn provinces, adopted the surname Ch'ŏngwŏn (淸原). The Civil Affairs Bureau Chief Mr. Yu Hong-sun, stating he is a descendant of Chungwŏn (中原), adopted the surname Chungwŏn (中原). Next, Mr. Hong Yŏng-sŏn (洪永善氏), head of the management section in Pongch'ŏn City, adopted the surname "Taesan" (大山) and the given name "Hwabang" (和邦), recently completing the procedures. Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-hyŏn (崔昌鉉氏), head of the industry section in the Special City of Sin'gyŏng, adopted the surname "Moksan" (牧山). At the Pongch'ŏn City Office School Association, the entire staff underwent *ch'angssi* simultaneously, setting a record since the establishment of the surname system. The registrar (主事) here, Mr. Cho Wŏn-hwan (曺元煥氏), adopted the surname "Yamamoto" (山本). Also among those who recently underwent *ch'angssi* is Mr. Kim Ku-gyŏng (金九經氏), a professor at the National Pongch'ŏn Agricultural University, who sent out notices to various parties with a lengthy explanation, adopting the name "Sinma Chin" (新馬晉).

Manchukuo Lieutenant Colonel Yi Ryang (伊亮氏), who was widely active in places like Beijing and Hong Kong, has reportedly been staying in Shanghai for some time.

P'yŏngbuk: 12,000 Cases

The *ch'angssi* fever in P'yŏngan Pukto is high. According to a survey by the P'yŏngan Pukto Local Affairs Section, as of the 20th, the number of *ch'angssi* registrations filed within the province was 12,352 households, with 12,322 associated name changes (改名). Among these, 693 were government office employees, 28 were employees of public organizations, and 820 were public officials. Furthermore, within Ch'angsŏng county alone, there are 1,700 prospective registrants. The *ch'angssi* fever in P'yŏngbuk has now reached its peak.

Slandering the "*Ch'angssi* System"

One Year Prison Sentence Handed Down

There has been an incident where an intellectual youth <15> received a prison sentence for slandering the *ch'angssi* system. On May 6th, at the Ch'ungju Branch of the Taejŏn Court, Kim Han-gyu (金漢奎, 30) from Kŭmgŭm-myŏn, Ch'ungju, who had dropped out of Paejae High School and returned to farming, was sentenced to one year in prison for making seditious remarks slandering the *ch'angssi* system and damaging the great policy of Naisen Ittai (內鮮一體 - Japan and Korea as One Body) while at a friend's house. He is currently serving his sentence.

Minister of Education Gives 13,000 Yen

As Prize Money for the Film "Soil and Soldiers"

The films "Soil and Soldiers" (土と兵隊) and "Soil" (土), both produced by Tokyo's Tamagawa studio, have received much critical acclaim. In response, the Minister of Education has sent 13,000 yen as the "Minister of Education Prize" (文相賞金) to the actors and director of the studio.

Ideological Warfare Exhibition

To Be Held in Various Locations Starting with Kyŏngsŏng

The Chosŏn Anti-Communist Association (朝鮮防共協會) plans to hold the second Ideological Warfare Exhibition (思想戰展覽會) in major cities throughout Chosŏn, starting with Kyŏngsŏng in late May. This exhibition will feature materials from ideological warfare campaigns previously shown in various regions of the homeland [Japan] by the Cabinet Information Bureau (內閣情報部), as well as dioramas (ジオラマ) specially produced by the Chosŏn Anti-Communist Association, and many other valuable materials. **Summary**

This article from June 1940 compiles news briefs highlighting Korean activities under Japanese colonial rule. Key themes include the economic expansion of Korean conglomerates (*chaebŏl*) into Japanese-controlled Manchuria, and the widespread, seemingly enthusiastic adoption of Japanese-style names (*ch'angssi kaemyŏng*) by prominent Koreans, including high-ranking officials in Manchukuo and Korea, businessmen, intellectuals, and even entire government offices and police stations. The article also reports on the suppression of dissent against the name-changing policy, the enforcement of derogatory terminology for Chiang Kai-shek's Chongqing government by the Chosŏn Army Command, cultural news regarding film awards, and plans for anti-communist "Ideological Warfare Exhibitions" across Korea. For historians, the extensive lists of individuals undergoing *ch'angssi* provide concrete examples of the pressures and responses related to the assimilation policy (*naisen ittai*). The reports on business ventures, propaganda efforts, and suppression illustrate the multifaceted nature of late colonial control and Korean participation/coercion within the Japanese empire.
