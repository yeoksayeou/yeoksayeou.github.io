Article Title: 長安 社會客인 夫君들의 生活을 報告하는 書(四氏近況) (A Report on the Lives of Husbands Who Are Socialites in Changan [Recent Conditions of Four Gentlemen])
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

A Report on the Lives of Husbands Who Are Socialites in Changan (Recent Conditions of Four Gentlemen)
These well-known figures, who show their vibrant selves on the streets of Changan (長安) as socialites—what are their recent lives like? We decided to take a perspective from the home and have their wives write reports on their husbands' lives. <140>

Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng - Lately, He Spends His Days Walking and Reading

Mrs. Yŏ Un-hyŏng (Chin Sang-ha)

That gentleman's life these days isn't particularly noteworthy, as he doesn't have any specific work. Before, when he worked outside, even after returning home, he would cheerfully chop firewood, wipe the wooden floors, and tend to the flowers in the garden. But lately, he has put all that aside and just spends his time taking walks (散策). Often, after staying up all night reading books, he seems to have a headache during the day, says nothing, pulls the covers over himself, and spends the whole day like that. When he's deeply engrossed like this, he won't answer even if I ask him something. When his mood improves a bit and I ask, he says he's getting angry because he's come to hate himself. Previously, he used to hike mountains and travel, doing everything systematically, but lately, he hasn't been able to do that.
However, it seems he still prefers his past life to his current one, occasionally widening his eyes and saying, "Should I really be living like this?" In the most recent few days, his depression has gradually lessened, and he goes out more often. When he returns, I hear stories that he visited places with fresh air like the Han River (漢江) and Ch'ŏngnyangni (淸凉里).
Now, he says that when our eldest child, who is in Tokyo (東京), returns for the upcoming summer vacation, he will shoulder a backpack with the child and wander through mountains and rivers. Indeed, even during the last spring vacation, that gentleman, who had been listlessly passing the days, became so cheerful after the eldest child returned. He went hiking, which he never did, and even when at home, he would arm wrestle, wrestle, or talk about something with the child. Anyway, to borrow his words, he says he is happy because he met a friend he gets along well with. It seems that gentleman's friends are children. He seems happiest when playing with children rather than anyone else. He uses a completely laissez-faire (放任主義) approach with the children, so they don't see their father as someone difficult or scary,<141> but rather regard him just like one of their own friends.

Mr. Chu Yo-han - The Department Store's Intense Work Sacrificing Private Life, and Family Time on Holidays

Mrs. Chu Yo-han (Ch'oe Sŏn-bok)

Monday.
The department store is closed for the holiday, so this is the day he calls "paying back the debt to the family." Every time, before anyone comes looking for him, he hurries out early in the morning to Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮). Since it's a Monday, the three older children are all at school, so he only takes the two younger ones, Tong-il (東日) and Tong-sŏl (東卨).
Tŏksu Palace is crowded. They all seem to be department store office workers and clerks. It seems fun, but in another way, it feels amusing and silly to be lounging around on a Monday when everyone else is working.
He plays catch with a big ball on the lawn with the children, and sits by the pond watching the children play with the ducks, playing along with them. He looks just like a child himself. It seems he'll probably keep a child's heart until the day he grows old. Whether he's playing catch with the children or sitting by the pond watching the ducks, he doesn't seem like an adult at all, looking exactly like the children. When I see this, I think perhaps it's because he has the heart of an artist, but I still burst out laughing.

X Day.

He goes to work at 9 AM and is supposed to leave at 6 PM, but his temperament is strange; he doesn't think of leaving his seat before finishing his work. So, it seems he usually works several hours longer than others, and rarely comes home earlier than scheduled. When he worked at the chain store (Hwasin 和信), he had more spare time, and if he came home at 6 PM, he would write poetry or read books. But since moving to the department store a while ago, he seems distressed because he has absolutely no time to write poetry.
He always says that he'll work just a little longer, get the department store work settled, then quit, stay <142> home, write poetry, and live a more organized family life.

X Day.

If it looks like he'll be home late in the evening, he calls to tell me to feed the children first. When he comes home early, he just comes straight home, has dinner together with the children, and then puts on a record, sings songs, and dances with the children. I always tell him to stop, saying it looks unsightly. Then he teases me, calling me someone who doesn't know how to live. He often tells the children educational stories, and also just funny and amusing tales.

Sunday.

Since he has to go to the department store even on Sundays, he rarely gets to go to church. However, he makes sure that I and the children go. He especially urges me to have the children attend Sunday school as much as possible. His insistence is on instilling faith from childhood.
Although this is supposed to be a report of a week's life, since he lives the same way every day, I hope this suffices to fulfill the responsibility.

Mr. Yu Ŏk-kyŏm - Prudence, Prayer, Exercise - Devotion to Serving the Nation Through Thought and Religion

Mrs. Yu Ŏk-kyŏm (Yun Hŭi-byŏn)
Being naturally a prudent (謹愼) person, he wakes up at 6 AM, washes his face, immediately performs obeisance towards the Imperial Palace (宮城 遙拜), then prays to God, and afterwards reads newspapers and magazines. Whatever happens, he seems to read through 3 or 4 kinds of newspapers for several hours each, and also appears to read almost all the magazines presented (進呈) to him without skipping much. Because of this, if he happens to return home late in the evening, he wrestles with newspapers and magazines until late at night.
Lately, his mother has fallen ill, so every morning after breakfast, he goes straight to the main house (큰댁), where he decocts medicine for her or calls for a doctor. Since his mother is elderly, he seems very anxious (焦燥). I think that as people age, their feelings towards their parents become greater.<143>
On the 1st of every month, he goes to the Thought Protection and Observation Center (思想保護觀察所). On this day, he leaves early in the morning. Upon arriving at the center, he first pays respects at the Shinto Shrine (神宮 參拜), and then from 4 PM onwards, they hold a standing committee meeting within the Thought Protection and Observation Center. This committee has departments like Culture, Current Affairs, Welfare (厚生部), and Cultivation (修養部). That gentleman is apparently the head of the Cultivation Department. So, after the committee meeting ends at 4 PM and dinner is over, he then begins his lecture, they say. Anyway, on the 1st of every month, he leaves early in the morning and only returns late at night. Not just that day, but as it is a time of emergency, he probably visits the Shinto Shrine 3 or 4 times a month.
Next, a place he often goes to is athletic meets (운동회). Because he originally likes sports, he never misses an athletic meet. Although he played rugby (럭비) and was a rowing (뽀-트) athlete during his student days, he doesn't personally participate in any sports now. However, he goes to the sports grounds so often that I heard his acquaintances worry that something might have happened to him if he isn't seen there. When I ask him what's so interesting about sports, he says that besides being fun, there's much to learn about self-cultivation while watching sports, so it's quite good. In other words, it's not just the competing athletes who get excited and run around unaware of anything else; even the spectators find it hard to maintain composure. He says it's interesting to not lose oneself at such times, but to always watch over and control oneself. Today too, he went to the championship tournament said to be held at Hwanghakchŏng (黃鶴亭). Although he doesn't know how to shoot a bow, because he serves as an advisor to the Physical Education Association (體育協會), he acts as a judge, or so they say. In archery (弓道), the judge is called a "Toch'ŏng" (都廳), so when he left this morning, I chuckled and said, "You're off to be the Toch'ŏng."
And another place he goes once a month is the YMCA board meeting (기독청년회이사회). He goes to the Chongno (鍾路) YMCA on the third Friday of every month and to the Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町) YMCA on the first Monday of every month; he is an honorary director at the Hasegawa-chō YMCA. Anyway, that gentleman's life these days seems to be solely about serving the nation through thought (思想報國) and serving the nation through religion (宗敎報國).<144>

Mr. Yi Kŭk-ro - A Lofty Scholar's Life - Seems Unaware of Even Youth and Love

Mrs. Yi Kŭk-ro (Kim Kong-sun)

His life is very simple, the same every day, so there isn't much material to report. When our house was in Anam-chŏng (安岩町), the distance was far, so there were times he wouldn't come home for several days. He was more like a guest in our house than the master of the household.
Then, after moving to this house—right below the Language Society (어학회)—I started to feel like he was the head of the family. Even so, he doesn't consider his duties as the head of a household very important; he is absorbed in the thought that he must work, work until the day he dies.
The Language Society's rule is supposedly to start work at 9 AM and finish at 5 PM, but it's common for him not to come down until late at night. If he takes time off during the day for some errand, he has to use the night hours to make up for the time taken during the day. He can't take even one trip in a whole year. Going to Ch'anggyŏngwŏn during cherry blossom (벗꽃) season with the children is a major holiday for our family. I don't know how bland and dreary (을스냥스러울지) our life must look to outsiders. But knowing that the lives of all scholars were probably that bland and poor, I accept it willingly.
Besides his work, the only other thing he thinks about is achieving mental concentration for 5 or 10 minutes after waking up from bed, before starting the morning's work. He performs this every morning without fail, no matter what. At that time, it seems he completely clears his mind (염두) even of work-related matters. Although he is such an unromantic person, he very much enjoys thinking about things like the mysteries of the universe or the existence of God. Because of this, he wakes up in the morning as soon as dawn (먼동) seems to be breaking. Actually, according to him, waking up early is a habit from childhood, but he keeps time better than a clock. In other words, he's too precise. That's why, even when he occasionally tells stories about past love affairs, they don't sound believable. Then, perhaps finding it amusing that I <145> don't credit his words, he might put on a stern expression and say, "Even though I'm like this now, back in my student days in Germany (獨逸), I had a passionate affair with a German girl." But all I can think is that even during his student days, he probably just stuck to his desk.<200>

**Summary**

This article presents vignettes of the daily lives of four prominent Korean men in 1940 Seoul (referred to poetically as Changan), as reported by their wives. Yŏ Un-hyŏng appears withdrawn, spending time reading and walking, seemingly depressed about his inactivity, but finding joy in his children. Chu Yo-han is consumed by his demanding job at a department store, sacrificing personal time and artistic pursuits (poetry), though he cherishes moments with his family on his day off. Yu Ŏk-kyŏm leads a highly structured life involving prayer, exercise, attending sporting events, and active participation in organizations like the YMCA and the state-sponsored Thought Protection and Observation Center, focusing on "serving the nation through thought and religion." Yi Kŭk-ro is portrayed as a dedicated scholar devoted entirely to his work at the Korean Language Society (likely the Chosŏnŏ Hakhoe), living a simple, almost ascetic life, detached from typical family routines or leisure, much to his wife's resignation.
From a historical perspective, the article offers glimpses into the lives of Korean elites under late colonial rule. Yu Ŏk-kyŏm's activities, such as shrine visits and involvement with the Thought Protection and Observation Center, illustrate the pressures and forms of collaboration or co-option faced by Koreans within the Japanese imperial system's institutions aimed at ideological control ("思想報國"). Conversely, Yi Kŭk-ro's intense dedication to the Language Society highlights the quiet cultural resistance and preservation efforts occurring simultaneously. The contrast between Chu Yo-han's absorption in modern commerce (department store) and Yŏ Un-hyŏng's apparent disillusionment and withdrawal also reflects the varied responses and pressures experienced by intellectuals and public figures during this period.

呂運亨氏 - 散策과 讀書로 하로 보내는 最近의 그 분

呂運亨氏 夫人 陳相夏

그 양반의 요즘 생활이란 그렇게 뚜러지게, 하시는 일이 없음으로 보고할 바가 못 됩니다. 전에 밖앗에서 일을 보시는 때는 집에 돌아오셔서도 유쾌하신 얼굴로 장작도 패시고, 마루 걸레도 치시고, 꽃당에 꽃도 가꾸고 하시더니 요새 와선 그런 것 저런 것 다 집어치우고, 거저 散策하며 지내심니다. 한밤을 꼬박 새워 가시며 책을 읽으시군, 낮이면 머리가 아푸신 탓인지 아무 말씀없이 이불을 뒤집어 쓰시고 하루 종일을 지내시는 일이 많습니다. 한창 그러실 때는 물어도 대답을 안 하시겠기에 좀 기분이 나아진 다음에 물으면, 인제 자기 자신이 미워져서 화증이 나는 것이라 말씀하십니다. 전에는 등산도 하시고 여행도 하시고, 一居 一動을 규율적으로 하셨는데, 요지음 와선 그렇지를 못합나다.
그렇지만, 역시 요새의 생활보다, 과거에 하시던 생활이 좋으신 모양으로, 가끔 「내가 이렇게 살아서야 될 말이야」하시면서 눈을 크게 뜨신 답니다. 가장 최근 몇 일채는 차츰, 차츰, 우울증이 덜해 가시며, 외출도 자주 하심니다. 돌아오신 뒤에 이야길 드르면 漢江으로, 淸凉里로, 이렇게, 공기가 신선한 곳을 찾아 다니셨다고 함니다.
인제 오는 여름 방학에 東京 가 있는 큰 아이가 돌아오면 그 애와 함께 바랑을 질머지고 산으로 강으로 돌아다니시겠다 합니다. 아닌게 아니라, 지난 봄 방학에도, 그렇게 활기없이 그날 그날을 지나시던, 양반이 큰애가 돌아온 뒤부터 어떻게 유쾌하시든지 전혀 가지 않던 산에도 가시고 집에 계실 때만 하드래도, 그 애와 팔씨름을 하신다, 씨름을 하신다 또는 무슨 이얘기를 하신다, 어쨋던 그 말씀을 빌어 한다면 마음에 맞는 동무를 만난 것이 즐거워서 그러신다는 것입니다. 아마 그 양반의 동무는 아이들인 모양입니다. 누구와 보다도 아이들과 노시는 때가 제일 즐거운 것 같습니다. 아이들에게 전혀 放任主義를 씀으로 아이들도 아버지를 어려워한다거나 무서워 한다기 보다,<141> 똑 저이 동무같이 역이군 합니다.

朱耀翰氏 - 私生活을 犧牲하는 百貨店의 激務와 休日의 家庭

朱耀翰氏 夫人 崔善福

月曜日.
백화점, 공휴일이라, 이 날은 그이가 가정의 빗을 갚는 날이라 해서 어느 번이나 꼭꼭, 누가 찾기 전- 아츰 일즉이 서들어 德壽宮엘 가는데, 월요일인 까닭에 위로 큰 아이들 셋은 다-들 학교에 감으로, 아래로 두 아이- 東日, 東卨이만, 데리고 간다.
德壽宮엔, 사람이 많다. 모다 백화점 사무원, 점원들인 듯하다. 재미있는 것 같기도 하고 또 어떻게 생각하면 남들이, 다 일하는 월요일에 번둥번둥 놀라다니는 것이 웃읍고 싱거운 것 같기도 하다.
그이는 아이들과 잔디밭에서 크다란 공받기도 하고, 또 연못가에 가 앉아서 아이들이 「오리」를 보고 노는 것을 함께 놀아주기도 한다. 그러는 것이 꼭 아이들과 같아 보인다. 그이는 아마 늙는 날까지도 애기의 마음을 가지고 있을 모양인가봐. 아이들과 같이 공을, 받는 때거나, 못가에 앉아 「오리」를 흘리는 때거나, 도무지 어룬 같지 않고, 그 아이들과 똑같이 어린  것을 보는 때, 나는 예술가의 마음이라서 그런가 부다고 생각하면서도 웃음이 터진다.

X曜日.
