Famous Writing
						T'urŭk'e-nep'ŭ

Suddenly, the hunting dog dashed towards the base of the birch tree. Right. Seeing that something else had happened, what was reflected in the eyes of Turgenev, who stared intently, was a pitiful baby sparrow trembling because of the hunting dog that had suddenly snarled and rushed at it. At the very moment he hurried to stop the dog, something else fell abruptly like a swift bullet from a sparsely spread branch above his head, right in front of the dog. Before he could even think, "It must be the mother sparrow," the black shadow, trembling all over in despair, was preparing to struggle against the dreadful enemy with all its might, ready to die.

It was truly a solemn and tragic event that happened in an instant. Thinking, "What an astonishing thing! The mother sparrow, out of love for its young, does not even avoid the fear of death like that," hot tears welled up in his eyes at that moment as he called the dog back. (From Hunter's Diary (獵人日記)) <17>

**Summary**

This article is a Korean translation of an excerpt from Ivan Turgenev's "A Sportsman's Sketches" (specifically, the story "The Sparrow"). The narrator, Turgenev, witnesses a dramatic scene where a mother sparrow bravely confronts a hunting dog to protect its fallen chick, disregarding its own safety. The narrator is deeply moved by the power of maternal love and sacrifice shown by the small bird. For historians, this piece is interesting as it shows the translation and publication of classic Russian literature in a Korean magazine during the colonial period (1929). It reflects cultural exchange and potentially resonated with themes of sacrifice or resistance, albeit in a non-political context here. The use of older Korean orthography and vocabulary is also noteworthy.

名文章
						투르케-넵흐
돌연히 산양개는 백화나무 아래로 줄다름질을 처갓다. 올치. 또 무에 생겻고나 하고 뚱그러케 떠서 보는 투르게-넵의 눈동자 속에 비최인 것은 불이에 으르렁거리고 달려드는 산양개때문에 몸을 떨고 잇는 가련한 한 마리의 참새색기엇다. 그가 분주히 개를 말니려할 그 찰나 이번에는 또 머리 우에 엉성하게 버더나간 나무가지로부터 마치 급한 탕환모양으로 개압헤 돌연히 떠러지는 것이 잇섯다. 「어미 참새 엇구나」생각할 새이도 업시 그 거문 그림자는 절망에 온 몸을 떤면서 두러운 강적을 향하야 죽을 긔운을 다하야 몸부림을 할 마련을 하고잇섯다.
그것은 실로 무엇이라 말할 수 업는 엄숙 비장한 일순간에 생긴 일이엇다. 「놀라운 일이라 어미 참새는 제 삭기를 사랑하는 마음에서 저럿틋 죽엄의 무서움도 가리지 안는 것구나」하고 생각하면서 그 개를 도로 불러 드리는 그의 눈에는 그 순간에 뜨거운 눈물이 돌고 잇섯다. (獵人日記에서) ＜17＞
