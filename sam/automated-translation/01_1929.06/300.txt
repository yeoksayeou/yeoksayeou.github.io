Article Title: 雜談室, 天力의 努才 (Chat Room, Heavenly Strength's Effortful Talent)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

It is said that genius is another name for effort, and indeed, Mr. "Pal Perek" (발 베륵)—not only a famous piano master from Switzerland (瑞西) but whose fame resounds throughout the European (歐羅巴) music world (樂壇)—reportedly would not go out for a public performance (公演) unless he had played a newly composed piece (樂曲) at least 1,500 times. 1,500 times! Even if you assume practicing fifty times a day, that truly takes a whole month. The effort of a genius! How beautiful it is. <43>

**Summary**

This short piece emphasizes that genius is achieved through immense effort, citing the example of a renowned Swiss pianist, "Pal Perek," who allegedly practiced a new composition 1,500 times before performing it publicly. The author marvels at this dedication, presenting it as beautiful. From a colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it promotes the virtue of hard work and perseverance, aligning with broader self-strengthening or modernization narratives often encouraged or imposed during the period. It presents a Western figure as a model of diligence, potentially serving as an aspirational example for the Korean readership within the context of colonial modernity.
