If One Had 100,000 Won?
Survey - If someone offered you 100,000 Won unconditionally, what would you use it for?

I would use it for the construction of the headquarters building and operational funds.
Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭)

If I had 100,000 Won, although the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會) headquarters building should naturally be constructed, what is more urgent and important is to use it for our association's operational funds. Of course, in every matter, there are few things accomplished merely by will without money leading the way, but especially in a place like ours where the scope of our physical movement is strictly limited, money is even more precious. Without putting it first, work becomes exceedingly difficult and time-consuming. (Middle part omitted - original text)
And the operational funds I mean are for such meaningful work that if you invest 100,000 Won today, a return of several tens or hundreds of times that amount will surely come back the next day. 100,000 Won may not be a huge amount, but with even that much, work of that scale could proceed.

I would dispatch several tens of talented individuals to various countries in Europe and America.
Hŏ Hŏn (許憲)

If such a sum of 100,000 Won came into my hands, I would select thirty or forty individuals known as geniuses in Chosŏn (조선) and dispatch two or three each to countries like Britain (英), America (美), France (佛), Germany (獨), Russia (露), Italy (伊), Ireland (愛蘭), Turkey (土耳其), India (印度), Switzerland (瑞西), Czechia (첵크), Australia (濠洲), etc., to have them meticulously investigate and research those countries' national or social systems, human sentiments and customs, industrial conditions, national spirit, and so on. For example, even Japan sends many students abroad each year to countries like Turkey (土耳古). Is it because Japan has something to learn from Turkey? Rather, it's because that country has its own unique characteristics, and they want to understand them. Moreover, based on what I've seen traveling through various countries (各國), Soviet Russia (勞農露西亞) dispatches several tens of outstanding technicians from shipbuilding yards and machine factories to Anglo-American countries each year, and besides that, countries like Britain, America, Germany, and Japan (英美獨日) also select outstanding individuals to explore and research the civilizations of other nations. Especially in our situation, where everything lags behind, there is an urgent need to accurately understand the level of civilization in various countries, and at the same time, we must fully inform them of our circumstances. Therefore, I believe sending such individuals to various countries (各國) is a very urgent task, and if such money becomes available, I intend to spend it all on dispatching talented individuals (俊才派遣).

I want to print primers and give them to four million people.
Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協)

I understand that today in our Chosŏn, there are about four million illiterate people. That is, four million people represent 20% of the entire nation, and I believe that educating these people somehow is the most urgent task for statesmen (經世家). If 100,000 Won comes into my hands, <2> I intend to use it entirely as funds for this illiteracy eradication movement. The method would be to print about one million copies of a "Primer" (讀本) and distribute them widely, while simultaneously mobilizing young people from the intellectual class in rural areas to teach our script. At first glance, 100,000 Won might seem like a large sum, but in reality, when trying to use it for a large project, it's quite insufficient. For instance, doesn't managing a newspaper company or a large factory require several hundred thousand Won? Moreover, looking at the example of Japan, even newspaper management is now in a situation where it's difficult to operate unless large-scale capitalist enterprise funds flow in. If there were hundreds of thousands of readers, it could be done without needing vast funds, but is the situation in Chosŏn like that? Therefore, I want to spend all that money on the most fundamental project, the illiteracy eradication movement.

I would educate unfortunate geniuses.
Han Ki-ak (韓基岳)

If someone placed 100,000 Won in my hands, I would use it for the project of educating Chosŏn's unfortunate geniuses. This isn't something I'm saying anew now; it's something I've always had a plan (腹案) for, intending to manage it someday when I have money. Looking back at our situation today, there are many urgent tasks, but I know this project of educating geniuses is indeed one of the important and urgent matters. This is because how many young people in Chosŏn, born with excellent natural talent (天禀), are unable to study due to lack of school funds (學資)? Geniuses who graduate from middle school but cannot go to university, those who graduate from primary school but cannot attend middle school—educating all these people is a great national task. Of course, things like illiteracy eradication or social education are also good, but rescuing the unfortunate geniuses, who can be counted in the tens or hundreds of thousands, is also a good and refreshing project that casts a great light upon our future.

I would use it as funds for establishing a university.
Ch'oe Kyu-dong (崔奎東)

If someone placed 100,000 Won in my hands, I would immediately use that money as the basic fund for establishing a university. How shameful and regrettable is it that today we lack even one university built by ourselves? Truly, the task for us today is none other than nurturing outstanding individuals, there being no more urgent and important matter, yet the current situation is truly pathetic.
However, establishing one university requires no less than (少不下) three million Won, but wouldn't three million Won just require thirty benefactors donating 100,000 Won each? I have already decided on the university site in my mind: it's that wide field in Yongduri (龍頭里) near the entrance to Yŏngdo Temple outside Tongdaemun Gate (東大門밧 영도사). Build the university in that spacious plaza, also build the affiliated middle and primary schools, and create a University Town.
I hear that Takikawa Middle School (瀧川中學校) in Kobe (神戶), Japan, is run by a donation of seven million Won from some benefactor. Goodness, they even send middle school teachers to study abroad. So, it can't help but be a substantial education, can it?
However, if the aforementioned university plan is too distant, I would immediately like to implement even this kind of project with that 100,000 Won. That is, deciding to establish at least a professional school, and sending about ten sharp-minded (頭惱明折) teachers currently holding teaching positions in various middle schools to study in Europe and America. This is for nurturing future professional school professors. Wouldn't that be good?

Construction of a Pure People's Hall and Establishment of Daycare Centers in Each Province.
Chŏng Chong-o (鄭鍾嗚)

If I had 100,000 Won, I would build a large hall where the masses (民衆) can gather and deliberate, and then also establish several dozen daycare centers (托兒所) in major cities of each province (各道), starting with Sŏul (서울). The reason is, taking Sŏul for example, while there are places where many people can gather, most are owned by organizations like Ch'ŏndogyo (天道教), <3> Christianity (기독교), or Buddhist groups (불교단체), which is inconvenient. Therefore, building a large hall that the masses can always use, and moreover, where they won't be exposed to any strange atmosphere, is known to be a very necessary task. Secondly, regarding daycare centers, recently, the number of Korean women (조선녀성) coming out to work in places like factories has greatly increased. Consequently, they are often troubled by their young children, so establishing and managing daycare centers in every factory district to temporarily look after the children of laboring women is known to be a necessary task. (This is completely different from an orphanage (고아원), you know.)
After that, if there are remaining funds (餘在), I would like to manage something like a women's newspaper for the ten million women, even if on a small scale.

I would produce films and manage a theater.
Pak Yŏng-hŭi (朴英熙)

Among all branches of artistic movements, film and theater movements are paths we must absolutely exert all our efforts on, considering the great effect they have on the masses.
However, with merely 100,000 Won, could such a large enterprise be carried out completely? But first, with that money, assuming the theater itself would be rented from someone else, I would set aside some tens of thousands of Won for nurturing outstanding directors, stage actors, and all other technicians, while also materially treating (優遇) playwrights well to encourage them to produce masterpieces capable of guiding the contemporary masses. Also, if several tens of thousands of Won were spent on the actual costs of production, the theater movement could be carried out splendidly for several years. Moreover, it would be good to organize several mobile theater troupes (이동극반), which can be done even with minimal expense, and travel around places where the working masses gather, such as factories and fisheries, to perform.
Then, with the remaining two or three tens of thousands of Won, I would set up a modest studio (스타지오), nurture film actors, produce major films, and widely disseminate the produced films to the masses through the most effective methods possible. These movements are things that can be done if only there is money. I look forward to that day with conviction. <4>

**Summary**

This 1929 survey in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine asked prominent Korean figures how they would spend 100,000 Won. The responses reflect key concerns of the colonial era: national development, education, and social welfare. Kwŏn Tong-jin prioritized funding the nationalist Sin'ganhoe. Several respondents focused on education: Hŏ Hŏn proposed sending talented individuals abroad for study, Yi Sang-hyŏp aimed to combat illiteracy by printing primers, Han Ki-ak wanted to support gifted but poor students, and Ch'oe Kyu-dong dreamed of founding a Korean university or at least training future professors. Chŏng Chong-o highlighted social needs, suggesting a public meeting hall and daycare centers for working women. Pak Yŏng-hŭi focused on cultural influence through establishing film production and theater management. The article offers historians insight into the aspirations and perceived priorities of Korean intellectuals and activists under Japanese rule, emphasizing nation-building through education, organization, social improvement, and cultural development as key strategies for progress and overcoming colonial limitations. The diverse proposals underscore the multifaceted approach deemed necessary for national advancement.

돈 十萬圓이 잇다면?
說問-누가 돈 십 만원을 무조건으로 제공한다면 엇더한 일에 쓰겟슴니까?

본부회관 건축과 사업자금에 쓰겟다
權東鎭

돈 십 만원이 잇스면 新幹會 본부의 회관도 건축하여야 할 터이나 그 보다도 더 급하고 중요한 것은 우리 會의 사업자금으로 쓸 일 이외다. 무론 每事하고 돈이 압서지 아니하고 뜻과 가치 되어 주는 일이 적지만은 더군다나 우리네와 가치 몸을 움지기는 범위가 까다롭게 제한되어 잇는 곳에서는 더욱 돈이 귀한 것이니 그를 앞장세우지 안코는 일이 무척 어러워지고 날자가 걸니는 터이외다.(中簡畧-원문)
그러고 내가 의미하는 그 사업자금이란 오늘 십 만원을 들이면 다음날에는 그 몃 십 배 그 몃 백 배의 수획이 반드시 도라오고야말 그러케 뜻잇는 일에 쓰고저 함이외다. 십 만원 돈이 만흔 것은 아니겟지만은 그만치라도 잇스면 그만한 정도의 일은 되어 나갈 수 잇겟지요.

俊才 수십명을 구미각국에 派遺하겟다
許憲

나는 그런 돈 십 만원이 내 손에 드러온다면 조선에서 수재라고 일컷는 인물 삼사십명을 뽑아서 英, 美 ,佛, 獨, 露, 伊, 愛蘭, 土耳其, 印度, 瑞西, 첵크, 濠洲 등 각국에 이삼명식 파견하여 그 나라의 국가나 사회의 제도라든지 인정풍속이라든지 산업상태 국민정신등을 정밀히 조사 연구케 하겟슴니다. 가령 일본에서 土耳古가튼 나라에도 매년 유학생을 만히 보냄니다. 일본이 土耳其로부터 무엇을 배울 것이 잇서 그리겟슴니까만은 그 나라 독특의 무엇이 잇슴으로 그것을 알려고 그리는 것이외다. 더구나 내가 各國을 도라다니며 본 바에 의하면 勞農露西亞에서는 매년 우수한 조선소, 기계공장의 기술자 수십명을 英美諸國에 파견하고 잇스며 그 밧게 英美獨日 等 各國서도 서로 우수한 인물을 뽑아서 저쪽 나라의 문명을 탐색 연구케함니다. 더구나 우리가치 모든 것이 뒤저잇는 처지에 잇서서는 각국의 문명정도를 정확히 알어둘 필요가 時急이 잇는 동시에 우리 형편도 저쪽에 충분히 알려주어야할 터인즉 그런 인물을 各國에 波送하는 것이 아조 급무인 줄로 알고 그런 돈이 생긴다면 俊才派遣에 다써버릴가 함니다.

讀本을 인쇄하여 사백만 명에 주고십다
李相協

오늘날 우리 조선에는 약 사백만 명이란 일자무식이 잇는 줄로 압니다. 즉 사백만 명이라면전민족의 二割인데 이 사람들을 엇더케든지 교육하여 놋는 것이 經世家의 가장 급히 하여야 할 일인 줄 압니다. 나는 십 만원의 돈이 내 손에 드러온다 하면＜2＞ 온전히 이 문맹타파운동의 자금으로 쓰고저 합니다. 그 방법은 약 백만 부의 「讀本」을 인쇄하여 녈니 배포하는 동시에 농촌의 지식계급적 청년들울 모다 동원식혀서 우리글을 가르치게 하도록 하겟슴니다. 얼는 들으면 십 만원이라면 큰 돈가치 생각되나 실상 큰 사업에 쓰자면 그것이 여간 부족하여야지요. 가령 신문사나 큰 공장 하나를 경영한다 하여도 멧 십만 원이 잇서야 하지 안슴니까. 더구나 일본의 예를 볼지라도 신문경영에까지 이제는 대규모의 자본가의 기업자금이 밀려들지 안코는 경영이 곤란한 형편에 잇습니다. 그도 수십만의 독자가 잇다면 방대한 자금을 필요하지 안코라도 하여 나갈 수 잇스나 어듸 조선형편이 그러함니까. 그럼으로 가장 기본적 사업인 문맹타파운동에 그 돈을 전혀 써버리고 십슴니다.

韓基岳

누가 돈 십 만원을 내 손에 쥐어준다면 나는 조선의 불우한 천재들을 교육하는 사업에 쓰겟슴니다. 이 말은 지금 새삼스럽게 하는 것이 아니고 나는 언제나 돈이 잇서지는 날에 기어히 경영하여 보려고 腹案을 꾸며가지고 잇든 것이외다. 오늘날 우리 형편을 도라다보면 급하게 하여야 할 일도 만치만은 이 천재교육의 사업도 실로 중하고 긴급한 일 가운데 하나인줄 아는데 그것은 學資가 업서서 훌융한 天禀을 타고난 청년들도 공부를 못하는 사람이 조선에 엇더케나 만슴니까. 중학교를 졸업하고 대학에 못가는 이, 소학교를 졸업하고 중학교에 못가는 수재, 이 모든 사람들을 교육식혀주는 것이 민족적 큰 일인줄 압니다. 물론 문맹타파나 사회교육화 가튼 일도 조흐나 수만 수십만으로 헤아릴 수 잇는 불우수재를 구제하는 것도 우리의 압혜 큰 광명을 던저주는 조코 시언한 사업인 줄로 압니다.
