**Body Text Translation:**
Points of Convergence and Difference between Nationalist Literature and Proletarian Literature

The influence and effect that all branches of Chosŏn literature today—poetry, novels, drama, etc.—have on society and the masses are considerable. However, observing the state of our literary movement, which is entrusted with such a crucial task, we see two movements: the nationalist literature movement rooted in the nationalist movement, and the proletarian literature movement rooted in the proletarian class movement. As the ranks of writers are naturally divided in this way, the readership is also dividing accordingly. Whether this situation is a joyous or sorrowful one is a matter we leave to others' critique; first, we must clearly understand the points of difference and convergence between the two movements. Of course, if this were American or French society, this wouldn't necessarily be a new literary issue, and even within Chosŏn, had it been just three or four years ago, it might not have been an issue. However, we believe that today's overall circumstances urgently necessitate clarifying this once more, and thus we have decided to publish the esteemed opinions of various figures in the literary world. (P'ain)

Historical and Geographical Aspects are Shared; Philosophical and Ideological Aspects Differ
Yi Kwangsu

I. Points of Convergence
Nationalist literature and proletarian literature share the same historical, geographical, ethnic, and aesthetic (meaning literary aesthetics) conditions. To elaborate further:
First, the language is the same. As writers who grew up in the same land, they use the same "kagyagŏgyŏ" [Korean alphabet basics] to convey thoughts and feelings to the same Chosŏn people.
Second, the form is the same. The emerging proletarian literature also adopts existing forms in terms of literary genres like poetry, novels, drama, etc. (襲用하고 잇다).
Third, the disposition is the same. Disposition refers to the direction in which thoughts and feelings move. For instance, if the national character of a people is generally optimistic (樂天的), the works will largely be based on that tone. Conversely, if it is pessimistic (厭世的), a pessimistic trend will naturally form the basis. Ignoring this disposition, the work will fail to be cherished by Chosŏn readers.
Fourth, the customs are the same. Even proletarian literature, if depicting a family, cannot ignore the traditional customs and habits of a Chosŏn family (朝鮮가정) and start by depicting a Japanese family (日本가정).

II. Points of Difference
The two literatures differ in philosophical and ideological conditions as follows:
First, proletarian literature must "necessarily" be materialist, whereas nationalist literature can be materialist, but it is fine even if it isn't necessarily so.
Second, proletarian literature must "necessarily" be oriented towards class struggle, whereas for nationalist literature, while such aspects may be necessary, it is not "necessarily" limited<32> only to that.
Third, proletarian literature must be propagandistic (宣傳的), but nationalist literature is "not necessarily propagandistic" (不必宣傳的).
Fourth, proletarian literature must necessarily be rebellious (反抗的) (in the present). That is, temporally, it must rebel against tradition, and spatially, it must rebel against the current system (現 制度). Nationalist literature, however, while rebellion is fine, does not necessarily have to be so.
Fifth, proletarian literature must necessarily be internationalist, whereas nationalist literature is often patriotic. However, these can be compatible, as seen in the works of Tolstoy, hailed as great Russian (露西亞) national literature, which are not confined to that one country (一國) but are widely renowned as world literature. I believe these are generally the differences.
(Responsibility for the text lies with the reporter)

Weak Differences and the Convergence of Both Literatures
Kim Tongin

Nationalist literature and proletarian literature find their point of convergence only in arguing back and forth over trivial matters.
Their differences are like distinguishing the sex of a crow (가마귀의 雌雄)—impossible to tell.

Essentially, the Two Literatures are Like Water and Charcoal
Kim Yŏngp'al

I. Points of Difference and Convergence between Nationalist Literature and Proletarian Literature
I find the topic above extremely difficult and awkward to address. This is because nationalist literature and proletarian literature have entirely different directions and also different ideologies.
If we speak broadly of literature, simply referring to literary arts alone might be one thing, but when we talk about nationalist literature, proletarian literature, or any specific "literature," it must necessarily presuppose some kind of literary movement. Then, the term "difference," being a passive term, becomes too distant. Consequently, a point of convergence is utterly undiscoverable, and I think anyone attempting to find one could be called foolish.

Nationalism is Conservative; Proletarianism is Internationalist
Yun Kijŏng

Regarding your inquiry about "Points of Convergence and Difference between Proletarian Literature and Nationalist Literature," I hold the following view:

I. Points of Convergence
Essentially, they are opposed, like water and fire which cannot mix (水火不相容). Therefore, a point of convergence absolutely cannot be found.

I. Points of Difference
Proletarian literature is a transitional literature for all humanity based on internationalism. Nationalist literature, being regional and conservative, can only be recognized as a reactionary literature (반동문학) from the perspective of the proletarian literature movement. <33>

**Summary**

This 1929 article from *Samch'ŏlli* presents a survey of opinions from prominent Korean writers (Yi Kwangsu, Kim Tongin, Kim Yŏngp'al, Yun Kijŏng) on the relationship between nationalist literature (Minjok Munhak) and proletarian literature (Musan Munhak/P'ŭro Munhak). Yi Kwangsu argues for shared ground based on common language, literary forms, national disposition, and customs, while differences lie in ideology (materialism vs. not necessarily), focus (class struggle vs. broader themes), purpose (propaganda vs. not necessarily), stance (rebellion vs. not necessarily), and scope (internationalism vs. patriotism). Kim Tongin dismisses the distinction as trivial. Kim Yŏngp'al and Yun Kijŏng assert a fundamental incompatibility, viewing them as ideologically opposed (like water and fire), with Yun characterizing nationalist literature as conservative and reactionary from a proletarian, internationalist viewpoint.

The article is historically significant as it captures the intense ideological debates within the Korean literary scene during the Japanese colonial period. It reflects the growing influence of Marxist thought (proletarian literature) challenging the established nationalist sentiments, revealing deep divisions among intellectuals about the purpose and direction of literature under colonial rule. Historians might find the varying perspectives—from seeking common ground (Yi) to outright rejection of compatibility (Kim Y., Yun)—valuable for understanding the complexities of cultural resistance and intellectual currents in 1920s Korea.

民族文學과 無産文學의 合致点과 差異点
오늘날 시나 소설이나 극 가튼 모든 부문의 조선문학이 사회민중에게 끼처 놋는 바 영항과 작용이 여간 크지 안타. 그런데 이러케 중대한 일을 맛하하고 잇는 우리의 문예운동선상을 살펴보건대 거기에는 민족주의뎍 운동에 뿌리를 박은 민족문학운동과 무산계급운동에 뿌리를 박은 푸로문학의 두 운동이 잇다. 글쓰는 문사의 진형이 저절로 이와가치 갈라저 잇슴애 독자의 분야도 그에 따라 갈라지고 잇다. 이 형세가 깁분 일인가 슬푼 일인가 하는 것은 딴 분의 비판에 맛기려니와 위선 우리는 두 운동이 엇더한 뎜에 차이가 잇고 또 엇더한 뎜에 합치가 잇는가 함을 잘 알어 두어야 하겟다. 물론 이것이 아메리카나 불란서 사회라면 문학상의 새삼스러운 문뎨가 되잘것도 업겟지만 또 가튼 조선이라도 만일 삼사년 전만 하여도 문뎨될 바 업겟지만 오늘의 모든 정세는 또다시 한번 이것을 명확하게 밝혀둘 필요에 절박하엿다고 우리는 보고 이에 문단제가의 고견을 싯기로 한 바이다. (파인)

歷史的 地理的이 갓고 哲學的 主義的이 다르다
李光洙

一. 合致点
민족문학과 무산문학은 그 역사적, 지리적, 종족적, 예술론적(즉 문학상의 미학을 가르침)의 諸 조건이 가튼 것이니 좀더 자세히 말하건대
첫재 어학이 갓다. 가튼 땅에서 자라난 작가들이니 만치 가튼 조선사람에게 사상감정을 전함에 잇서서 가튼 「가갸거겨」로써 한다.
둘재 형식이 갓다. 신흥하는 무산문학도 시가, 소설, 극 등 그 문학상의 형식에 잇서서는 재래의 형을 襲用하고 잇다.
셋재 기질이 갓다. 기질이라 함은 사상감정의 움즈기는 방향을 가르치는 것이니 가령 그 민족의 민족성이 대체로 樂天的이라하면 작품도 대개 그를 기조로 삼아서 되어질 것이요, 그 반대로 厭世的이라 하면 역시 염세사조가 자연히 기조를 이루어질 것이니 이 기질을 무시하고는 그 작품은 조선인에게서 애독을 밧지 못하고 말 것이다.
넷재, 풍습이 갓다. 무산문학이라도 가정을 그리자면 朝鮮가정의 전래하는 풍속습관을 무시하고서 日本가정부터 그릴 수는 업스리라.

二. 差異点
두 문학은 철학적 주의적 諸 조건에 잇서서  알에와 가치 다르다.
첫재 무산문학은 「반드시」 유물론적이어야 하고 민족문학은 유물론적인 것도 잇겟지만은 반드시 그러치 안어도 조타.
둘재 무산문학은 「반드시」 계급투쟁적이어야 할 것이로되 민족문학은 그런 것도 필요하나 반드시 그런＜32＞ 것에만 限하는 것이 아니다.
셋재 무산문학은 宣傳的이어야 하나 민족문학은 「不必宣傳的」이다.
넷재 무산문학은 반드시 反抗的(현재에 잇서서)이어야 한다. 즉 시간적으로는 전통에 반항하고 공간적으로는 現 制度에 반항하여야 하나 민족문학은 반항도 조흐나 또 반드시 그러치 아너도 조타.
다섯재 무산문학은 반드시 세계적이어야 하나 민족문학은 흔히 애국적이다. 그러나 이것은 어울닐 수도 잇는 것이니 露西亞의 위대한 국민문학이라 일켯는 「톨스토이」 작품도 반드시 그 一國에 끄치지 아니하고 널니 세계적 문학으로 일홈이 흐르는 것을 보아도 알 것이다. 대개 이상과 갓다고 생각하다.
(文責在記者)
