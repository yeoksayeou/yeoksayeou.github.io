The Last Moments of a Death Row Inmate
						Judge K

The condemned man dragged out to the execution ground was a youth with sturdy (屈强한) muscles, but his complexion was pale and wet, and his eyes, devoid of sight, were merely staring blankly into the distance. However, unlike other condemned men, he showed no signs of weeping, wailing, or struggling; instead, he displayed an unusual composure. An official whispered,
"He has considerable nerve."
"When a condemned man weeps, wails, and resists, it's actually easier to carry out the execution, but when they are composed like that, one somehow hesitates."

While the execution order was relayed and the chaplain (敎戒師) spoke of the afterlife, urging him to go peacefully to heaven, he kept his eyes tightly shut, silently pondering something deep within his heart. An official urged a final decision, asking,
"Is there anything you wish to say? Is there anything you desire?"
After a long pause, the youth opened his eyes wide and said,
"I have nothing to say, but I would like to eat a little bread (麵麭)."
As it was customary to grant small farewell gifts (餞別) to the dying for wishes up to a certain extent, bread was immediately provided. He received it and chewed it listlessly for a moment, putting it in his mouth, but he looked as though he had no energy at all, as if eating grains of sand.

Having barely finished the bread, the condemned man then said,
"I want to smoke tobacco."
That too was given. He bowed his head and slowly, slowly puffed on the tobacco, but seemed unaware that he was smoking, listlessly staring only at a crack in the wooden planks. After finishing it some time later, he asked for one more piece of bread. Again, bread was given, and he chewed it, seemingly finding it even more tasteless than before.

As he put the last piece of that bread into his mouth, the condemned man requested one more puff of tobacco. However, the official refused, saying,
"Not anymore."
He pleaded, but was still refused. At that moment, as if teasingly, one of the officials asked the condemned man,
"How was it? Did it taste good?"
At these words, the condemned man, in a listless voice, as if mocking himself, tossed out the words, "Neither the bread nor the tobacco had any taste at all. But thinking that I would die after finishing the bread, I couldn't bear not eating something."

Finally, another piece of bread was given. He stared blankly at it for a long while, then gradually, a light returned to his eyes. Suddenly, as if attacking an enemy, he bit into it fiercely, swallowed it quickly, closed his eyes, and then, as if being robbed of something, cried out, "Do it quickly! Quickly, quickly..." (End) <15>

**Summary**

This article, written by "Judge K," recounts the final moments of a young, muscular death row inmate who displays unusual composure before his execution. Despite his calm demeanor, his repeated requests for bread and tobacco reveal a desperate attempt to delay the inevitable, admitting that the items have no taste but that the act of consuming them provides a temporary distraction from the thought of imminent death. His final moments are marked by a sudden burst of desperate energy and a plea for a swift end. From a historical perspective, the article offers a rare, albeit anonymized, glimpse into the psychological experience of a condemned prisoner and the execution process under the Japanese colonial administration in Korea during the late 1920s. The perspective of a "Judge" (potentially Korean or Japanese) observing the scene provides insight into the detached yet humanly affected viewpoint of officials involved in the colonial judicial and penal system.

사형수의 최후
						K판사
사형집행장에 끄을니어 나온 그 사형수는 屈强한 근육을 가진 청년이엇스나 안색은 창백하여 젓고 눈도 시력이 업시 그냥 먼-히 뜨고만 잇섯슬 뿐이다. 그러나 다른 때의 사형수와가치 울며 불며 뛰는 일이 업시 도로혀 이상하도록 침착을 보이고 잇섯다. 係官은
「담이 상당히 큰데」
라고 속살거렷다.
「사형수가 울며 불며 반항하면 도로혀 집행하기 쉬우나 저러틋 침착하면 엇전지 주저 됨니다.」
사형집행이 전달되어 敎戒師가 내세를 말하여 천국으로 잘가라고 권하는 사이에 그는 눈을 꼭감고 고요히 마음 속에 무엇울 삭이고 잇는 드시 잠잠하고 잇섯다. 係官은
「무엇이나 하고 십흔 말이 업느냐? 무에나 욕심나는 것이 업느냐?」
라고 최후의 결심을 催促하엿다. 청년은 한참만에 눈을 번드시 뜨더니
「아모 말도 할 것이 업스나 빵「麵麭」를 조곰 먹고 십허」
라고 하엿다. 어느 정도까지의 소망에는 죽어 가는 자에 대한 조고마한 餞別로 허락하여 주는 법이 엇슴으로 즉시 빵은 주게 되엇다. 그는 그것을 바더 잠시 입에 너코 오물오물 씹고 잇스나 마치 모래알을 먹는드시 조곰도 긔운이라고 업서 보엿다.
겨우 그것을 다 먹은 사형수는 이번에는 또
「담바를 먹고 십다」
라고 하엿다. 그것도 주엇다. 그는 고개를 숙이고 조곰식 조곰식 담바를 빨고 잇스나 제가 담바를 먹는 줄도 이젓는지 긔운업시 널빤지쪽의 틈만 물끄럼히 처다보고 잇섯다. 한참 만에 그것을 다먹고 나더니 이번에는 또 한 개의 빵만 더 달라고 하엿다. 또 빵을 주니 그는 앗가보다도 더 맛이 업는드시 씹고 잇섯다.
그 빵의 최후의 한쪼각을 입에 녓허니 사형수는 다시 한목음의 담배를 빨고 십다고 요구하엿다. 그러나 係官은
「이제는 안된다」
라고 거절하엿다. 그래도 졸낫스나 여전히 거절을 당하얏다. 그 때 가린한드시 係官의 한사람이 사형수에게
「엇더냐 맛이 잇더냐」
라고 물엇다. 그 말에 사형수는 긔운업는 소리로 자기 자신을 조소나 하는 모양으로 말을 던젓다. 「빵이나 담배나 조끔도 맛은 업섯소. 그러나 빵을 다 먹고 나면 죽겟거니 생각하면 무에나 먹지 안코는 견듸지 못하겟소.」
최후에 또 한개의 빵을 주엇스나 한참 그것을 먼히 치어다 보고 잇더니 점점 그의 눈빗은 빗을 띄어 왓다. 불시에 적에게나 하는 드시 와직끈 씹어 그것을 얼는 삼켜 버리더니 그리고는 눈을 감더니 무엇을 배앗는드시 부르지젓다. 「빨니 하여주오 빨니빨니...」 (끗) ＜15＞
