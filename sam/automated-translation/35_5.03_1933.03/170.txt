My First Love Record
The Image of Madam Nam Syura from Russian Territory

Hong Yang-myŏng

The editor's request was to write a "first love story," but rather than saying such sweet tales are currently out of stock, I am a person without particularly remarkable experiences. Therefore, as a substitute for the request, I intend to repay my literary debt by introducing a woman residing abroad whom I once respected. This also seems preferable.
Regarding Madam Nam Syu-ra, whom I intend to introduce, those who have traveled to the Russia area might have met her often directly, and even if they haven't met her, she is an extremely popular figure about whom stories are likely known. However, it is natural that most of you gentlemen would not know about the existence of this Korean elder sister who has never set foot on Korean soil.
Nam Syu-ra! Nam Syu-ra! Her name is undoubtedly one of the shining "names" in the women's society of Soviet Russia today.
What could have made her name shine so brightly?

Madam Nam Syu-ra, due to her ancestors being Russian immigrants, was purely born in Russia, received Russian education, and is a Korean woman who has never stepped on soil other than Russia's. She is a Korean woman who always works for the Soviet state from the perspective of Koreans, for the enlightenment of the backward Korean women residing within the Soviet Union. She once taught outstanding male cadres as a lecturer at the Political School established in <30> Irk'usŭk'ŭ (Irkutsk) City to train cadres of the Far East in a short period, held responsibilities in military and political aspects of the Ch'itta (Chita) Government, and is a woman with a formidable theoretical system in social sciences. Afterward, she worked bearing important responsibilities regarding Korean women in political organs of the Far East region.
She is now a woman of 36 or 37 years with vigorous activity; her extraordinary organizational skills and logically coherent eloquence make her not just the foremost figure in the Korean female society in Russia, but there would likely be no one to match her even if she came to Korea. She is a woman who grew up amidst the waves of X and XX, and was hardened like X through that experience. It is said that after receiving education during the Russian Imperial era, she held various jobs and wandered through various places. For her, Russian is her native language and Korean is like a foreign language, but although she has made efforts with Korean, she manages to express her intentions quite well with a bizarre pronunciation mixed with incomprehensible dialects. Moreover, while not knowing Chinese characters, as a result of painstaking learning, her casual mixing of difficult academic terms like "surplus value" (잉여가치) or "production administration" (생산 행정) was quite a spectacle compared to her clumsy Korean.

I first met her already 9 years ago, during a bitterly cold winter! It was at a Club (側樂部) in a certain northern city. At this Club, the Korean youths gathered there were practicing a play for the year-end party (忘年會). I, too, was a member of this group, dreaming of being a comedic actor (道化役者). Outside, the terrifying snowstorm (吹雪), a specialty of Russia, was roaring as if to overturn the whole world, and it was a bitterly cold night where the heat from the Club's "pechka" (베지까) could hardly suppress the dreadful cold.
While we were engrossed in imitating clumsy actors, a couple quietly entered and sat nearby. After a while,
"Is it alright if we watch?"
asked a woman—her small body wrapped in a black fur overcoat, her eyes dark but shining sharply and intellectually, her pale face refined as if having experienced all the world's hardships.
"It's alright. Thank you so much for coming to watch our practice on such a cold night."
I said, offering her a chair. The woman, rolling her affectionate eyes, quietly sat beside us and watched. When we were leaving, she had gone out without us noticing. It was then that I learned this woman was the famous Nam Syu-ra. I learned that the man who came with her was the famous Nam Man-ch'un, her older brother. That day, they had arrived here from X City, and came to the Club partly out of boredom and partly to see the gathering of Korean youths.

After that, I met her frequently. She would ask about difficult Korean words or, when translating them, would come looking for me if she got stuck with Korean. As our acquaintance deepened, her character became clearer. Whenever a theoretical point arose, if the other party was inconsistent or made a mistake, she would attack like fire. Her sparkling, pitch-black eyes and somewhat dark, pale face possessed an indescribable charm stemming from intellectual brilliance rather than physical beauty. When she stood on the platform, the world was hers, and Russians would shout "Bravo!" (뿌라뽀) at her fluent Russian discourse, which flowed like the sound of steel without obstruction, forming a rhythmical harmony with her masculine, strong expression and firm intonation. She was definitely an agitator (아지테이터) and a strong woman.
However, she was still a woman. Moreover, <31> her private life was extremely lonely. She had a child born between her and a husband from a marriage conducted under old customs (녯싸―에 관습). But for her work, she abandoned both husband and child, drifting east and west following her work. Therefore, her life had no settlement. Possessing an extremely strong side, yet also a womanly side, even more sentimental and full of sorrow than ordinary women, she would, according to time and place, sow many seeds of "love" in the hearts of many members of the opposite sex.
"For me, who became a human being dedicated to work rather than a faithful sexual slave to one man, the liberation of sex is a natural thing." Thus she proclaimed and practiced.
Because of this, much criticism was directed at her. There were more than one or two men and women who pursed their lips saying, "Tsk! That kind of thing for a Korean?". However, whether this was right or wrong is not for me to discuss, but her courage in distinguishing between "work" and "private matters" was admirable. It was true that she had many "partners" (파―트ナー), but her "love" was a "love" measured by her "ideology" and "work."

In her spare moments, she enjoyed singing "sentimental" (쎈치멘탈) songs. Having grown up abroad and never set foot on her native soil (故土), she would occasionally turn to me and say,
"To Korea! Ah—how can I get to see Korea? Ah—I miss Korea,"
agonizing like a child. Whenever she felt the loneliness (孤寂) of life, she would sing an extremely sentimental song called "Vast Siberia" (茫茫한 시비리) and wear an expression of sorrow, as if about to cry.
I discovered that indeed, women cannot escape the characteristics of women, even in this strong woman, Ms. "Nam Syu-ra."
She would occasionally throw gentle words at me, like to a younger brother, saying "You must be thinking of home at times like this." She was a reliable older sister (누님).

Spring winds and autumn rains have passed, nine years already. Since then, while I changed my life moving from Tokyo to Seoul to Shanghai, I occasionally heard that there had been many changes in her life too. There was talk that she was ill and staying at a sanatorium in South Russia's "Crimea" (크리비야), or that she had gone to the Outer Mongolian Government (外蒙古政府) where her brother was working, or that she was still working for the organization. There is no way to ascertain her news. However, my only wish is that the long OO time and little fatigue have hopefully not taken away the genius flash and indescribable charm from her sparkling eyes and pale face, and that health and activity always remain before this older sister.

Pure Love and Separation

Ham Tae-hun

When speaking of first love, one naturally refers to the love of two young people drawn to pure affection. The tender pure love shown in Turgenev's (투르계―넵흐) work "First Love"! The unending passion depicted in Gorky's (고리키―) work "First Love"! < 32 > The whisper of first love towards another's wife, like mine, depicted in Gorky's "Foma Gordeyev" (피마·고르뎨―옙흐)!
First love is truly pure affection and passion itself.
However, my first love, though perhaps a whisper of pure affection, could not be called a love burning with such passion.
I came to know that woman in December of XX year. An introduction at a certain place became the opportunity, and a few days later, I went to the Wŏlmido (月尾島) coast in Inch'ŏn (仁川). It was December 29th! It was a leisurely time during the winter vacation. Thus, we became acquainted through frequent meetings.
Meeting each other frequently, the flame of love ignited between us. However, an event that made us ache with separation occurred: the new semester began, and I had to go back to Japan (日本).
After we separated, love letters entwined with intangible emotions went back and forth.
This continued for 3 years. But since we were both students, we only met once during summer vacation! Just as the Cowherd and the Weaver Girl meet once a year on the seventh day of the seventh month by the Milky Way riverbank, we met briefly during the summer vacation that returned once a year. Although we met once a year, we didn't have the chance to whisper our affections even for a single day. This was because I attended a professional school (專門) and she was in a girls' school (女學校), so my vacation started early and her vacation period was late. When I came out to Seoul, she was still in the midst of her first semester exams... Thus, we barely saw each other's faces before separating and returning to our respective hometowns. Throughout the summer, only love letters went back and forth! We couldn't even go somewhere to escape the heat during the summer vacation... At that time, both she and I thought that going on summer trips was nothing but the pleasure (亨樂) of the bourgeoisie. So, we spent the whole summer with only love letters going back and forth, and when the new semester started again, we would meet briefly in Seoul.
Three years passed like this! With her graduation approaching and my graduation approaching too, that autumn, we met on the bank of the Han River (漢江畔) under a star-twinkling sky, our hearts pounding.
The blue water of the Han River slapped against the riverbank, *chalssadak chalssadak*, and the twinkling star shadows in the distant sky seemed as if they were celebrating our love.
But who could have guessed that it would be the time of separation?...
After parting that night, the woman and I lost contact with each other. For a long time after that, I could not forget her.
Now, even the image (面影) of that woman has faded from my memory, but she was a very beautiful woman and also a pure maiden of passion. She was like a woman raised in a birch forest in a Russian (露西亞) village.
That passionate and beautiful maiden! I heard that she has now become the wife of some man...
Oh! A fragment of a poignant memory from days gone by... in this budding spring... Upon receiving the editor's request to write this first love romance, I came to write this piece while thinking once more of that maiden from long ago.

Love, Love, Love

Yi Chong-myŏng

Can that also be considered a kind of love? Being just an Ordinary School (普通學校) student, still with snot under my nose, meeting an older girl student, like an older sister, on the way to and from school every morning and evening, my heart pounding for no reason, face flushing red, feeling shy, but then, if our paths don't cross for a few days and we don't meet, just quietly forgetting about it?
Or, can that also be seen as an expression of love? A delinquent middle school student who likes movies, harmonica, and football, deliberately tears his school cap to wear it, considers singing class the most important subject, visits his cousin's house every evening to write clumsy letters to a certain female student who comes there to play, gets a stern scolding from his cousin, and eventually even gets grounded?
But if all these are considered love, then one could say that everyone loves at least a thousand times in their lifetime.
However, can that be called love? A professional school student with long hair and a square cap, who became close to a girl by translating and reading poems by Heine (하이네) and Byron (빠이론) to her, even allowing physical intimacy, secretly setting up house like playing house (솟곱질) without their parents knowing, only to be discovered eventually, forcibly separated, and then carrying around a bottle of Calmotin (칼모찐) when she was to marry another man?
Or can that be called love? A relationship with a certain prostitute that became very intense, leading to being forbidden from going out by the family elders, upon which the woman cut off her finger (斷指) as a sign of reflection and protest, but it eventually fizzled out anyway?
It's six of one, half a dozen of the other. Whether it's the image of an unknown girl seen casually on the street lingering in my mind for one night, or crying and wailing while separating after living together for months or years, they are the same in the sense that the separation happened. Looking at it this way, although I am still a young man who has reached the age of twenty-five, it seems I have loved quite a lot and experienced all sorts of things with women, yet in another way, it feels like I haven't experienced real love yet.

If I Had Known Love Then

Sŏ Kwang-je

It was when I was eighteen, attending the 4th year of commercial school. <34> I wasn't good at school studies, and what I enjoyed was tennis, so after school, I would lose track of time on the sports field until dusk.
Through the introduction of a fellow tennis player two grades below me, I got to know his younger cousin sister.
When I first met her and greeted her, I couldn't lift my head. My face turned red like rouge from embarrassment.
I didn't even know what romance was, and sitting in the same place with a pretty girl student for the first time in my life, my heart pounded like a drumstick, and my head felt heavy as if covered by a mortar.
Two or three days after meeting her, I received a letter from her. I don't even remember now what was written.
At that time, she was a girl known as a "modern girl" at Ewha School (리화학교).
Even after receiving such a love letter, I didn't know what kind of reply would be good to send.
Then another letter came from her.
Gathering my courage, I tore a page from my notebook (I didn't even have letter paper), folded it without an envelope, and gave it to her with trembling hands.
After that, whether going to school in the morning or after school finished, I reached a point where I couldn't bear not seeing her.
Even when eating, my heart fluttered, and I couldn't eat well; during study time, her face would appear every time I turned a page.
But I didn't even know that this was love. To be honest, I never even properly held her hand, nor did I ever kiss her.
She had awakened to sex much earlier than I had at that time. Although she was a year younger, when I once said, "Isn't it too young for a middle school student to be in love?"
Her response was blunt: "You should start dating from now." My face turned even redder.
When I visited her house, her cousin brother would deliberately leave the room, but I didn't know how to take advantage of it.
It was only when a rival for her love appeared that I first tasted the bitterness of love.
She asked me almost daily to get my father's permission for marriage.
But being treated like a child when I went home, I couldn't bring myself to say I wanted to get married.
While I was hesitating like that, the distance between her and me grew.
Thinking back now, I definitely didn't know what love was then. It's certain that she must have thought I was a fool.
Even now, whenever I occasionally meet her on the street, I feel how slow my awakening to sex was back then, and I feel quite sorry for her, who tried to send her poignant love towards a fool like me.
Anyway, thanks to her, I felt the motivation to read Turgenev's "First Love," and also thanks to her, I failed the second-semester exams that winter.
When will I ever be able to find again that innocent and beautiful heart of mine, which didn't know the taste of love and just wanted to see her face?... ...(End)... <35>

Alumni Magazine "Yŏngnan"

An Tŏk-kŭn

For me, living lonely in a foreign land (異鄕), what good would it do to reminisce about the sweet first love of days gone by?
Will your esteemed company send me back to those times? Unless the earth reverses its rotation, you wouldn't be able to do that, surely—
But if it's an order, I'll tell you.
Four years ago from now, that is, in the autumn of the year I turned 22, I got the chance to experience something called love for the first time.
At that time, I had an irreplaceable close friend and my only senior, whom I'll call C. Mr. C had a younger sister named XX, then a 4th-year student at the Girls' High School (高女). Mr. C loved his sister very dearly and would unconsciously praise her from time to time.
Although I had never met Miss XX, through Mr. C, I could imagine her personality and tastes to some extent, at least in outline, and I wanted to see the so-called "real thing" (實物), what kind of person she was.
Thinking back now, perhaps this was the budding of affection (戀心).
Then, that autumn, I saw Miss XX for the first time when she participated in a tennis match (庭球試合戰) against an elementary school team on the Girls' High School ground.
Her shy demeanor seemed to express innocence (純眞), and her neat attire showed the beauty (美) that comes from upbringing. But I harbored no particular ambition. I merely had the feeling, "She seems nice!".
Sometime later, needing to consult the Alumni Association Magazine "Yŏngnan" (校友會誌「令蘭」) published by the Girls' High School Miss XX attended, I sent my very first letter to Miss XX, along with my first greeting, asking to borrow "Yŏngnan."
"Yŏngnan," of course, and no reply came even after a week. Since I had only stated my purpose clearly and there were absolutely no improper (love-seeking) words, I was surprised by the unexpected fact of no response.
Then, one Sunday morning, a small, neat-looking envelope and a book were delivered; it was the "Yŏngnan" in question and a gracious letter from Miss XX expressing apologies for the delay and embarrassment that there wasn't much to see.
This was the first time I received a letter (레-타) from someone of the opposite sex who wasn't a relative, so my joy (喜悅) at that time was truly immense. I felt like jumping around like a child.
The reader might wonder why she delayed for over 10 days if she was going to lend it anyway, but according to what Miss XX told me later, there was a reason.
"Unsure whether she should grant the request (所請) or boycott it <36>, being the first time she encountered such a thing, she couldn't resolve it herself. She reported the details (顚末) to her older brother, the aforementioned Mr. C, and asked what she should do. Mr. C reportedly replied as follows:
'Mr. A is not a frivolous worldly youth (世俗靑年). Therefore, I believe he wouldn't ask to borrow 'Yŏngnan' as a means to toy with you. However, I have no right to tell you whether to lend it or not, so do as you please according to your free will!' "
The above process caused the delay of over 10 days. After reading the borrowed Yŏngnan, I wrote back expressing my gratitude and impressions, sending it back to her along with the said "Yŏngnan."
This became the starting point; letters came and went every few days (literally, 'lest three days pass'), and my suitable literary books did likewise.
In this process, the love between us entered its second stage, and as we were about to pass the second stage and enter the third, we walked quietly on Namsan (南山) on moonlit nights, making and remaking "plans" (푸랜) for the future, repeatedly making resolute decisions.
But how could I have known? Due to "difficult circumstances" that cannot be disclosed even now, four years later, she and I had to swallow our tears and come to a painful breakup.
She left my presence forever. Some time ago, I heard through the grapevine (風便) that she is abroad, bravely making her way as an independent (히도리마에노, 一人前) XXXX. It's delightful. I'm glad (깃부외다).

Unforgettable Miss Kang

Han In-t’aek

You command me to write a first love story, so I have taken up my pen, but it's a difficult matter. Is it reasonable to expect an interesting story from me, who has never experienced romantic love (련애)? When one speaks of love, one associates it with the overflowing happiness and joy of passion between the sexes, or else with agony and sorrow, and it's an undeniable fact that all these things revolve around it. Especially in first love, one of these feelings firmly occupies their deep heart and will never be forgotten throughout their lives. Therefore, since I, who have never been in love, lack such experience, I do not possess the qualification (?) to write a first love story. Let's end the prelude here. What I intend to write here, instead of a first love story, is about how, when I was young, I worried my young heart alone for one person of the opposite sex and adored her; to put it more precisely, a story of my one-sided love (짝사랑). Even now, there are times when I still cannot forget her and miss her.
It was 10 years ago from now, when I was in the 2nd year of middle school, a time when I was innocent and didn't know what love was. The place where I boarded was in Tongsung-dong (東崇洞); I've forgotten the house number as it was long ago. At that time, I boarded together in one room with Yi, who was one year behind me, i.e., a 1st-year student. And in the room below, separated by a wall, were the main character in question, Miss Kang (姜孃), and another girl—I've forgotten that girl's name now. (Continued on page 75) <37>
(Continued from page 75) Thus, my secret adoration and longing for her continued not just until she left that boarding house, but I adored her for several years afterward.
When Kang was leaving the boarding house, I felt as if I had lost something, my heart felt empty, and I had no desire to stay there anymore, so Yi and I moved together to another boarding house. After she moved, I never once met her again. There was no way to even know her whereabouts. I still don't know even now. I only learned by chance back then that Kang's hometown was Kowŏn (高原). Even now, whenever I take the Hamgyŏng line and pass Kowŏn station, the memory (긔억) of Kang resurfaces, and the events of that time float in my head, making me smile to myself more than once or twice.
Anyway, although it's an old story, it doesn't easily disappear (싸러지지) from my memory, so when asked to write a first love story, the matter of Kang came to mind, and I decided to write this amusing and insignificant story.
However, although I still remember Miss Kang's name, I will refrain from mentioning it.—There were two of them. Even though we were in the same boarding house like this, because it was a young and innocent time, shame and shyness came first when seeing the opposite sex, so while unable to look directly at them (치어다보지), deep inside I greatly adored the person named Kang. Thinking back now, Kang wasn't particularly beautiful then. If speaking of beauty, the other girl who was with Kang was perhaps prettier. But, unknowingly, I came to like Kang, who was not the beauty. Although I stayed there with the two of them for a year, and adored Kang so much, I couldn't even exchange greetings, let alone speak to her. This was because of my shy nature. Even if they initiated conversation, I would barely manage a reply out of embarrassment, not knowing what to do. Thinking about it now, those times seem both amusing and happy. <75>

**Summary**

This article from the March 1933 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine compiles six short memoirs titled "My First Love Record" by various authors (Hong Yang-myŏng, Ham Tae-hun, Yi Chong-myŏng, Sŏ Kwang-je, An Tŏk-kŭn, Han In-t’aek). The pieces range from admiration for a politically active Korean woman in Soviet Russia (Nam Syura), nostalgic recollections of youthful romances often interrupted by studies (in Japan) or circumstance, cynical reflections on infatuation, to tales of unrequited or awkward adolescent crushes. The authors reflect on the nature of love, memory, and the passage of time from their perspectives as young Korean men in the 1930s.
From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the social and emotional lives of educated Koreans during the colonial period. Hong Yang-myŏng's account of Nam Syura is particularly striking, revealing connections between the Korean diaspora and Soviet political life, and touching upon themes of female emancipation and social critique within that context. The other stories illustrate experiences common to students of the era, including studying abroad (Japan), evolving dating norms, interactions shaped by institutions like Ewha School and boarding houses, and the general atmosphere of youthful longing and reflection against the backdrop of colonial modernity.

나의 첫사랑 記
露領南슈랴女史의 面影

洪陽明

「첫사랑 이약이」를 쓰라는 것이 편집자의 주문이엿스나 이런 달콤한 이약이는 마침 품절이라는 것 보담도 별로 신통한 경험이 업는 나임으로 주문의 代用으로 나는 이곳에 내가 일즉이 존경하든 해외에 잇는 한 여성을 소개하고저 함으로써 文債를 갑흐려 함니다. 또한 이 편이 나흘 듯도 함니다.
내가 소개하려는 南슈―라 女士에 대하야는 露西亞 方面에 여행한 일이 잇는 이들은 직접 만히 만나기도 햇슬 터이오 맛나지 못햇드래도 그에 대한 이약이 쭘은 생각되는 지극히 포푸라―한 인물임니다. 그러나 朝鮮의 따를 발버 보지 못한 이 朝鮮의 누님의 존재에 대하야는 대부분의 諸君은 모르는 것이 당연할 것임니다.
南슈―라! 南슈―라! 그의 일홈은 쏘베트露國의 여성 사회에 잇서서는 오늘날 확실히 빗나는 「일홈」의 하나임니다.
무엇이 그로 하야금 그의 일홈을 빗나게 하얏겟슴니가?

南슈―라 女士는 그의 선조가 露國移住民이엿든 관계로 순연히 露西亞에서 나고 露西亞 敎育을 밧고 露西亞 이외의 따를 밥지 못한 朝鮮女性임니다. 그는 쏘베트聯邦 내에 거주하는 뒤떠러진 조선여성의 교화를 위하야 항상 조선인의 입장에서 쏘베트國歌의 일을 하고 잇는 朝鮮의 여성임니다. 그는 一時 「일구스크」市에 極東의 일군을 단시일에 양성하기 위하야 ＜30＞ 설립한 政治學校의 강사로써 우수한 남자 일군들을 가르킨 일도 잇고 치따政府의 軍事 政治 方面의 책임을 가진 일도 잇고 사회과학에 잇서서도 쟁쟁한 이론의 체계를 가진 여성임니다. 그는 그 후에 極東 方面의 정치기관의 朝鮮女子에 대한 중요한 책임을 지고 일하엿슴니다.
그는 지금은 36,7세의 활동력의 왕성한 여자로 그 비범한 조직 능력과 논리 정연한 辯舌은 露西亞에 잇는 朝鮮人 女性社會에 잇서서 제일인자임에 끄치지 안코 朝鮮에 오드래도 그를 당할 사람이 업슬 것이외다. 그는 X은 XX의 파도 속에 자라고 또 그 속에서 단련된 X과 가치 구더진 여자임니다. 그는 露西亞 帝政時代의 교육을 바든 후에 여러 가지 직업을 가저 各地로 유랑한 일도 잇다함니다. 그에게 잇서서는 露西亞말이 本國말이고 朝鮮말이 외국어인 셈이지마는 朝鮮語도 그의 애쓴 것도 잇지마는 종잡을 수 업는 사투리 석긴 기괴한 발음으로 제법 의사는 다 표시함니다. 더욱이 漢文글자도 모르면서 애써 배혼 결과로 「잉여가치」니 「생산 행정」이니 하는 등 어려운 학술상 용어를 제법 예사로 석거서 하는 것은 그 서투른 朝鮮말에 비하야 가관이엿슴니다.

내가 처음 그를 맛나기는 발서 9년 전의 차듸 찬 겨울! 북쪽 나라의 엇던 도시에 잇는 側樂部에서 엿슴니다. 이 俱樂部에서는 이곳에 잇는 조선 청년들이 忘年會에 할 연극을 연습하노라고 모힌 때엿슴니다. 나도 이 그룹의 한 멤―버가 되여 道化役者의 꿈을 꾸고 잇섯슴니다. 밧게는 露西亞의 명물인 무서운 눈보라(吹雪)가 왼 세상을 뒤집어 업흘 듯이 호통을 하고잇서 俱樂部안의 「베지까」의 열기쭘으로는 이 무서운 추위를 조곰도 억제치 못하는 차듸 찬 밤이엿슴니다.
우리들이 서투른 役者의 흉내를 정신업시 내고 잇슬 때에 一組의 남녀가 가만히 드러와 엽헤 안젓슴니다. 얼마 잇다가
「구경해도 괜찬슴둥」
하고 뭇는 여자―검은 수피 털외투 속에 적은 몸을 싸고 캄캄하나 예민하게 이지적으로 빗나는 눈 세상의 모든 풍파를 다 격근 듯이 세련된 창백한 얼을 가진―가 잇섯슴니다.
「괜찬슴니다. 이 추운 밤에 연습하는 구경을 오섯스니 너무나 감사함니다.」
하고 나는 의자를 내여 노앗슴니다. 그 여자는 다정다감스런 눈등자를 굴니며 엽헤 가만히 안저 구경하다가 우리가 헤여질 때는 가는 줄도 모르게 그는 나가 바렷섯슴니다. 이 때에 이 여자가 바로 유명한 南슈―라고 하는 것을 아럿슴니다. 그 함께 온 남자는 바로 유명한 南滿春으로 그의 오라버니라는 것을 아럿슴니다. 이날 낫에 그들은 X市에서 이곳에 도착하야 심심하고 朝鮮靑年들이 모힌 것도 볼 겸 俱樂部에 왓든 것이엿슴니다.

그 후에 나는 자조 그를 맛나게 되엿슴니다. 그는 어려운 朝鮮말 갓흔 것을 뭇기도 하고 또는 그를 번역하다가 朝鮮말이 맥히면 나를 차저 오고 햇슴니다. 交際가 깁허 감에 따러 그의 성격은 또렷이 드러낫슴니다. 무슨 이론이 나올 때마다 상대방에 不徹底하든지 틀닌 말이 잇스면 熱火와 가치 공격하엿슴니다. 그 반짝이는 샛캄안한 눈 좀 검은 듯이 창백한 얼골은 육체적인 美보담도 지식의 閃光이 주는 형언할 수 업는 매력이 잇섯슴니다. 그가 壇上에 올너설 때 천하는 그의 것이오 수천의 그의 남성적인 강렬한 표정 굿세인 抑揚으로 리즘이칼한 諧調를 일우면서 쇠소리와 가치 맥힘 업시 흘너오는 유창한 그의 露語談說에 露人들은 「뿌라뽀」하고 절규하는 것이엿슴니다. 그는 확실히 아지테이테요 강한 여자엿슴니다.
그러나 역시 그는 여자엿슴니다. 더욱이 ＜31＞ 그의 사생활은 지극히 쓸쓸한 것이엿슴니다. 그는 녯싸―에 관습으로 行한 남편 사이에 나흔 자식이 잇섯슴니다. 그러나 그는 일을 위하야 남편도 자식도 바리고 일을 따러 東으로 西으로 흘너 다니는 것이엿슴니다. 그럼으로 그의 생활에는 정착이 업섯슴니다. 지극히 강한 일면에 여자다운 오히려 보통 여자보담 일층 더 多情多恨한 일면을 가진 그는 때를 따러 곳을 따러 또한 만흔 「사랑」의 씨를 만흔 이성의 가슴에 뿌리는 것이엿슴니다.
「한 남성의 충실한 성적 노예가 되는 것 보담 일을 위하는 한 개의 인간이 된 나에게 잇서서 性의 해방은 자연한 일이다.」 이러케 그는 부르짓고 또 실행하엿슴니다.
이로 인하야 만흔 비난이 그에게 집중되엿슴니다. 「체! 朝鮮의 고론 따이?」하고 입을 빗죽이는 남녀는 한 둘이 아니엿슴니다. 그러나 그 시비는 나의 論할 바 아니나 그가 「일」과 「私事」를 용감히 구별하는 것은 感服할 만 하엿슴니다. 그에게는 만흔 「파―트너」가 잇는 것은 사실이엿스나 그의 「사랑」은 그의 「사상」과 「일」로써 저울때질하는 「사랑」이엿슴니다.

그는 틈틈이 「쎈치멘탈」한 노래를 부르기를 조화하얏슴니다. 외국에서 자라 故土를 발부처 보지 못한 그는 틈틈이 나를 향하야
「朝鮮에! 아―엇더케 하면 朝鮮에 가볼 수가 잇을가? 아―朝鮮이 그리워」
하고는 어린애와 가치 안타까워하는 것이엿슴니다. 틈틈이 생활의 孤寂을 늣길 때마다 「茫茫한 시비리」라고 하는 극히 감상적인 노래를 부르고는 울 것과 갓흔 애수의 표정을 하얏슴니다.
나는 역시 여자는 여자의 특성을 벗지 못하는 것을 이 강한 여성 「南슈―라」氏에게서 발견하엿슴니다.
그는 틈틈이 나를 향하야 「이럴 때는 고향 생각이 날 테이지」하고 어린 동생게에 대함과 갓흔 부드러운 말을 더져주엇슴니다. 그는 밋엄즉한 누님이엿슴니다.

春風秋雨는 어느듯 9년이 기나 나는 그 후 東京으로 서울로 上海로 생활을 밧구는 동안에 그에게 만흔 생활의 변화가 잇다는 것을 간혹 듯기도 하엿슴니다. 或 병나서 南露「크리비야」에 잇는 療養地에 가 잇단 말도 잇고 혹은 그의 형이 일하고 잇는 外蒙古政府에 가 잇단 말도 잇고 그대로 긔관 일하고 잇단 말도 잇고 그의 소식을 종잡을 길이 업슴이다. 그러나 오즉 바라는 것은 그의 반짝이는 눈, 창백한 얼골에 OO한 긴 시간이 적은 疲勞가 원컨댄 그의 천재적인 섬광과 형언 못할 매력을 빼서가지 말고 건강과 활동이 항상 이 누님의 압헤 잇서 주기를 바랄 뿐이다.

純情과 別離

咸大勳

첫사랑이라 하면 누구나 순정에 끌어올르는 두 젊은 남녀의 사랑을 말할 것이외다.「투르계―넵흐」의 「첫사랑」이란 작품에 나타난 그 고흔 순정! 고리키―의 작품 「첫사랑」에 낫타난 그 칠줄 몰으는 열정! ＜ 32 ＞ 고리키―의 「피마·고르뎨―옙흐」에 낫타난 나 만흔 남의 안해에 대한 첫사랑의 속삭임!
첫사랑은 그야말로 순정과 열정 그것이외다.
그러나 나의 첫사랑은 순정의 속삭임은 되엿슬지언정 그러케 열정에 불타는 사랑은 못되엿다는 것이외다.
내가 그 녀자를 알게 되기는 XX년 십이월 이엿슴니다. 엇떤 장소에서 그 女子와 인사한 것이 기회가 듸여 수일 후 仁川 月尾島 海岸에 간 일이 잇슴니다. 그 때는 12월 29일! 동기휴가가 되여 한가한 때엿슴니다. 그리하여 자조 맛나는 사히가 되엿슴니다.
서로 자조 맛나는 사히에 서로 사랑의 불길이 타올르게 되엿슴니다. 그러나 우리에게는 서로 別離에 애타는 일이 발생하엿스니 그것은 신학기가 되여 다시 나는 日本으로 가게 되엿다는 것임니다.
우리가 서로 別離한 뒤에는 無形의 정서가 얼킨 사랑의 편지가 오고 갓슴니다.
이리하기를 3년 간 계속하엿슴니다. 그러나 서로 學生時代이니 만큼 夏休이나 한번式 만나게 되엿슬 뿐! 견우와 직녀가 7月 7夕에 1년 1차 은하수 강가—서 맛나듯이 우리는 1년 1차 도라오는 夏休에 잠간식 맛나든 것이되다. 1년 1차 맛나는 두 사람 사이엿마는 하로나마 연정을 속삭일 기회를 갓지 못하엿슴니다. 그것은 나는 專門에 다니고 그 女子는 女學校이든만큼 나의 방학은 일으고 그의 방학기일은 느진 때문으로 내가 서울에 나왓슬 때는 아즉도 그 女子는 1학기 시험때 엿슴이외다... 그리하여 겨우 얼골만 대하고는 서로 헤여저 각기 고향으로 돌아가 한녀름을 사랑의 편지만이 오고 갓슬 뿐! 夏休 때에 피서도 못갓슴니다... 그 때는 그 女子도 그로코 나도 또한 그러커니와 피서가는 것이란 뿌르조아들의 亨樂이라는 것 밧게 생각키우지 안헛섯슴니다. 그러하야 우리는 한녀름을 두고 사랑의 편지만 오고가다가 다시 신학기가 되엿슬 때는 서울서 잠간 맛나곤 하엿슴니다.
그리는 것이 3년이란 세월이 흘럿슴니다! 그도 졸업기를 압두고 나도 졸업기를 압두고 그해 가을에 우리는 漢江畔에서 별 반짝이는 하늘 아래 서로 두근거리는 가슴으로 맛나게 되엿슴니다.
漢江의 푸른 물은 찰사닥 찰사닥 강 언덕에 부듸치고 먼 하늘에는 별 그림자의 깜박이는 냥이 마치 우리의 사랑을 축하하는 것 갓기도 하엿슴니다.

강이 그 하숙을 떠나 가게될 때 나도 무엇을 일허버린 것 가티 마음속이 텅 비인 것 갓고, 그곳에 잇고 십흔 생각도 업고 하야, 가치잇는 李와 함께 다른 하숙으로 올마간 일까지 잇섯다. 그 녀자가 올마간 후에는 한번도 그를 맛나보지 못하엿다. 소식조차 알 바 길이 업섯다. 지금까지도 알지 못하고 잇다. 다만 강이 고향이 고원(高原)이라는 것만, 그때 우연한 긔희에 알엇슬 뿐이다. 지금도 함경선을 타고 고원역을 지낼 때마다 강이 긔억이 새로워지며 그 때의 일이 머리에 떠돌아서 혼자 우슬 때가 한 두번이 아니엇다.
엇더턴 오래 전 일이나 긔억에서 좀처럼 싸러지지 아니함으로 첫사랑 이야기를 쓰라고 할 때도 강의 일이 머리에 떠돌아서 우습고 아모것도 아닌 이야기를 쓰기로 한 것이다.
그러나 강씨의 일홈만은 지금도 긔억하고 잇스나, 그것은 말하지 안키로 하겟다.―둘이 잇섯다. 이러케 한 하숙에 잇스면서도 어리고 순진한 때라, 이성을 보면 부끄러운 생각과 수줌은 마음이 압서서 그들을 바로 치어다보지 못하면서도 마음속으로는 강이라는 이성을 퍽 사모하엿다. 지금 생각하여보아도 강은 그때 그러케 미인은 아니엿다. 민인이라면 차라리 강과 가치잇는 다른 녀자 편이엿다. 그러나 나도 알지 못하게 미인이 아닌 강이 조케 생각이 되엿던 것이다. 그 둘과 일년이나 그곳에 함께 잇스면서도 또 강을 그러케까지 사모하면서도 서로 인사는 고사하고 말도 하여보지 못하엿다. 그것은 내 마음이 수접은 까닭이다. 그들이 말을 먼저 부치어도 부끄러워서 대답을 겨우하고, 엇쩔줄을 모를 때이엿스니, 지금 생각하면 그 때의 일이 우습기도 하고 행복스러워 보이기도 하엿다. ＜75＞
