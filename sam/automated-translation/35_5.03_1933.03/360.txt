**文人書翰集**
『Impressive Scraps of Paper』

**Yi Kwangsu**

I inherently have a habit of not being able to collect anything, so I've never kept even a single letter received from friends.
However, among the many letters, there are a few that still remain in my impression, so I'll write about them.
This was already 15 or 16 years ago, when I was in Tōkyō and Mr. Hong Myŏnghŏn was in the South Seas (Namyang).
I don't remember the letter from beginning to end, but there were two Han poems (漢詩), which were about having read *Mujŏng* (*The Heartless*), which I wrote. The gist of the phrases written below that was as follows:
『The South Seas are a good place, a more comfortable region than anywhere else on earth. If I just had 4,000 won, I feel like I could live peacefully for the rest of my life doing coconut farming (椰自農事), but it doesn't work out as I wish.
For me, that farming seems most suitable. You don't plant, and you don't weed; how good would that be for lazy me?
Ch'unwŏn! Bring just 4,000 won and rush over to this good place to live.
However, one annoying thing is the nuisance of mosquitoes (蛟)』.
A pure classical Chinese letter (純漢文片紙) with phrases like the above, neatly written on three sheets of fine, white paper, still appears before my eyes.
Next is a letter from Mr. Yi Yunjae, written while teaching "Han'gŭl" in Kaesŏng, recording his impressions as they were. I was so moved upon receiving that letter that I thought I would keep it carefully, but I can't find it even when I look, which is regrettable. <88>
Every phrase seemed full of passion and sincerity.
Besides these, I received many letters from various writers, but I cannot write them all down individually. Finally, I will introduce just one bland letter.
A letter from novelist Kim Tongin.
『My wife has given birth and we have nothing to make soup with, so please send some manuscript fees』—I remember this simple letter being bland. I surmised that it was quite rare for a novelist to write like that.

**From Mo Yunsuk to Pang In'gŭn**

Ch'unhae Teacher! You say the green shade of early summer has arrived. In this land where misfortune has rolled in, it's a spring where one still cannot freely admire the colors of flowers.
I am in such a terrible mess that I have no words to say. Encountering a terrifying thief for the first time in my life, and even contracting a difficult illness, the history of nearly parting with the world for the long period of three weeks in a corner of a North Manchuria (北滿) hospital is hard to speak of again now. How I managed to survive and see the world's sunlight again feels like a dream.
These days, I miss the Southern Sky (南天) so much that I just want to cry until my throat is hoarse. No matter how I think about it, I don't think I can live here. It's been four full days since I was discharged from the hospital.
I still lack strength, so I can't do everything as I wish.

My heart, withered by the mad wind (狂風), feels as if broken by waves; I intensely miss the old dreams of Sŏul. Poignantly so. Tonight, a tiny crescent moon has risen white. There are no mountains here, so even the crescent moon doesn't set.
Teacher, thinking about it, life is sorrowful (비애로운 生). All the surroundings here give me nothing but world-weariness (염세증) and sorrow (哀愁).
Where should I row the sail of aimless fate? The more I think, the darker it seems.
Sŏul must be very nice these days. Here, not only is there no natural scenery (自然景慨), but there are no conversation partners either, so only the cry of silence (沈黙의 울음) strikes the void daily and emits sorrowful sounds (애닯은 음향).
If one seeks a river, there should be a clear river. As if angry at something, the muddy Tuman River (豆滿江) stream, tumbling and tossing, roars and flows nearby. Would the moon's reflection even shine in the muddy water? When you go boating (船遊) on the spring sea at Sŏul's Inch'ŏn Harbor, please look carefully at the moon's reflection shining down. Perhaps you might think of the distant Moon Shadow (月影).
Please send my regards to the members of the Old Youth Association (엣웟靑年會). I intend to send a letter of greeting to the Association members soon.

Is everything going well? And is H-kun also well? Does life have to tread such a prelude (序曲)? When I recall the faint past, which seems like having overcome the riddle of tragedy and comedy (戱悲), bittersweet laughter and unknowable tears flow again. Teacher, it is now 10 PM. The North Star is right above my window, watching over me. How far away it is. I shall stop writing now. Please write me a letter. (From Yongjŏng Village, Pukkando)
Mayloth moon Shaolow

**From Chu Yohan to Yi Chongmyŏng**

Thank you for sending your esteemed manuscript. I always send the magazine continuously, but I don't know what happened. I am sending the latest issue again, so please check and receive it (査收하시옵소서).

**From Yun Paengnam to Pang In'gŭn**

When you visited my humble abode (鄙巢) the other day, I was very rude. Regarding the matter of employment from a certain foreign company in Yokohama (橫濱), negotiations were underway previously, and although I had agreed assuming it would be concluded by the end of this month at the earliest, unexpectedly, I have to depart for Tōkyō around the 8th of the coming month and stay in Tōkyō for about 10 days. Since things have turned out this way, it has become difficult to attend the Popular University Lecture (通俗大學講演), which is not only extremely regrettable (遺憾) but also incredibly embarrassing and apologetic towards the organizers. What should I do? Although it might be called my lack of insight (不明), since the circumstances are unavoidable (事勢不得), please forgive me entirely (萬分容恕). In my humble opinion (愚見), how about negotiating with Unjŏng Kun (雲汀君)? (Remainder omitted - original text)
Yun Paengnam

**From Yi Hyosŏk to Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi**

Snow has been falling softly since morning. Imagine the sentiment of the snowy North. Today is Yook's 100th day since birth. I'm on my way to Najin (羅南) to buy a present. I barely managed to send the manuscript. It's been a while since I held a pen, so the tip felt scratchy, and I just managed to write it. It seems the one-page story (一頁小說) became a two-page story.
I've set the pen name as written on the manuscript. From now on, I'll go by that name. Please call me by that name from now on. Will I be able to visit in winter? If I can't, Chŏnghŭi-ssi, please come visit here. Being rushed, I'll end these few words here.
November 27th
At the Post Office

I received your kind letter (惠書) well.
You must be very busy, taking charge of the entire magazine. Autumn must have come to Sŏul too. Try going out to Sŏngbuk-tong. You can feel 100% of Sŏul's autumn there. Here, as soon as you open your eyes, as soon as you step outside the door, everything that meets your eye is autumn. The ancient castle wall (古城壁) cutting into the distant sky, the hazy sea horizon next to it, and the fields on this side are all expansive autumn companions.
I am enclosing a couple of pages of the manuscript "Around My 20th Year". I always intend to write other manuscripts too, but I don't have the leisure to write. Is Ikcho growing well? Tongson has started to laugh. (Remainder omitted - original text)
September 29th
Yi Hyosŏk

**From Yang Kyedong to Pak P'aryang**

Pak Hyŏng,
There is a place requiring correction (訂正) in my unpublished manuscript (拙稿 未揭載分), so I wish to impose upon your trouble (煩累).
I don't know which page, but about 2/3rds of the way down, <90> on the second page (裏頁), first line, there is a passage saying (Middle part omitted - original text) and so on (云云). Please revise it as follows—
「To do so, a revision (修正) of the philosophical perspective (哲學的 見地) for perceiving things is needed first, and furthermore, fundamentally, the shift of the ideological standpoint (思想的 立脚地) becomes the issue.」
Since I cannot remember the exact preceding and succeeding words, please fix it generally with the meaning as above (如右) and connect the sentences logically.
January 4th
Respectfully, Yang Che

**From Yu Chino to Kim Yuyŏng**

Kim Hyŏng! Have you been well? Please forgive your younger brother (弟) who received your letter but couldn't reply for so long.
Are all things going well there? Since it was a path you deliberately set out on, leaving everything behind, I especially pray that everything goes well.
You mentioned the cultural level (문화 레벨). Truly, as you say, Hyŏng, once you step onto Japanese soil, everything in Chosŏn seems pathetic beyond measure. Isn't it our responsibility to raise this lagging culture of Chosŏn not just to Japan's level, but to the world's level? This isn't empty talk (空言).
It's a fact, so our responsibility is truly grave. Of course, it's not just culture, but anyway, please strive to quickly establish a foothold (地盤) there. For you, for us, and for all of Chosŏn.
I also visited Kyōto twice, when I was 17 and 4 years ago, staying for 2 or 3 days each time. Thinking back now, the Kyōto scenery, literally "purple mountains and clear waters" (山紫水明), comes to mind like a painting. The clear water of the Kamo River (鴨川), the pavilions and bridges (橋) painted red and blue, the green-shaded mountains, the bright streets; the memories of years ago, strolling through Kyōgoku (京極) and Gion (祗園) after dinner, wearing the inn's yukata (裕衣), become fresh again now. Hyŏng! Please study diligently.
I've only met Chŏnghŭi-ssi just once since you left, Hyŏng. It's awkward for either Chŏnghŭi-ssi or me to deliberately create opportunities to meet, and there are no natural opportunities either, so although I meant to visit occasionally, it ended up like this. Literature isn't made solely through knowledge and friendship. Just please encourage her to read much and write much (多讀多作). The most dangerous thing would be to drift into journalism (쩌-낼리슴). If that happens, one lacks one's own ability, needlessly looks down on others' work, and fails to study classics and masterpieces diligently ("あちいて" - likely a typo for 熱心に?). Since Chŏnghŭi-ssi is at the magazine company, such dangers might be many. The cool touch of a glass (破璃의 곱부), the milky white reflection of a silver vessel (銀으로 맨든 그릇), the fresh taste of soda water felt through a straw (스트로-끄테), the contrast between ice and bright red strawberries, beer with froth (거품 끌은 맥주)—summer deepens here.
Hyŏng, you were worried about spending such a summer with Kyōto's film people (영화인). But as long as our reality, which is always unsatisfactory, exists, how can a moment's pleasure (一時의 歡樂), a cup of beer, break our will? In a word (蔽一言), establish your foothold there as soon as possible (하로밧비). All problems come after that.
Please tell me about Kyōto sometimes.
June 23rd Yu Chino

**From Yi Kapyong (?) to Yi Chongmyŏng**

Chongmyŏng-ssi, greetings (冠省). Busy with the New Year, aren't you? When does the December issue come out? What about *Yŏin* (*Woman*) December issue? Has Sŏ Kwangje-ssi returned to Kyŏngsŏng (歸京)? By the way, since they say the War Theory (戰爭論) is dead, please make sure to keep the deleted manuscript, or even a copy (복사) would be fine (좃슴니다).
Respectfully read your reply, Hyŏng <91>

**From Sim Hun to An Sŏkkye**

It seems you read my last letter, but there's no reply, so I'm very curious. Are you not overworked with duties, and are all friends well (無告)? I wish to know. I'll send the preview material for the novel in 1 or 2 days and about 10 days' worth by mail, so please check and receive it. I wrote the title and content to be suitable for publication starting January 1st, so I would be even more grateful if you could serialize it starting from the New Year issue. I'm writing a few words as you might be curious.
Send a postcard or something to this person stuck in the countryside. Do you see Yi Hot'ae Hyŏng? I'll write again soon.

**From Chŏn Yŏngt'aek to Pang In'gŭn**

I received your second postcard gladly. I know you must have been very frustrated due to the lack of news during that time. My situation is beyond words. Even on hot Sundays (主日날), I walk tens of ri, give sermons two or three times, return only on Monday, go out again on Wednesday and return on Thursday. During that time, I was busy with the Summer Children's Bible School (夏期兒童聖經學校), teaching personally, and now I'm in charge of school work, teaching one class—at home, I look after the child, clean up excrement (똥치고), sweep the room, and even clean the church building (禮拜堂); that's my situation. Food is absolutely out of the question, so my body is utterly exhausted, and I'm out of my mind.
I thought I sent a letter, but I guess I didn't; I was so out of it. I've just started the *Kibo* (基報 - likely a Christian publication) manuscript, so I'll send it within 4 or 5 days.
Still, you seem to be doing fine, visiting Wŏnsan and Sŏgwangsa! I'm just sweating and walking on roads! Still, since I live in the countryside (농촌), from a hygiene perspective, perhaps there's no need (心要) for me to go to such places.
Chŏn Yŏngt'aek, Pongsan-ŭp

**From Ch'oe Sŏhae to Kim Tonghwan**

Sorry to bother you when you're busy, but I hope you can drop by for a bit.
Come tomorrow at 11 AM. If you can't make it then, drop by the day after tomorrow (7th) afternoon.
It's Room Ha (ハ) on the 2nd floor of the Medical School Main Building (醫專本舘). Not number eight (八), but Ha (ハ) from I-Ro-Ha-Ni.
July 5th Ch'oe Sŏhae <92>

**Summary**

This article compiles excerpts from personal letters exchanged between prominent Korean writers and intellectuals in the early 1930s. The letters cover a range of topics, including personal hardships (illness, poverty, childbirth), reflections on life and literature, experiences abroad (Japan, Manchuria, Southeast Asia), manuscript submissions and corrections, travel plans, and general camaraderie. They offer glimpses into the writers' daily lives, creative processes, anxieties, and connections within the colonial literary sphere. For historians, the letters provide valuable insights into the material conditions, personal networks, geographical mobility (including experiences in Manchuria and Japan), and intellectual concerns (like cultural comparisons between Korea and Japan, the role of literature) of Korean elites during the Japanese colonial period. The casual mentions of financial struggles, health issues, and feelings of displacement or cultural inferiority offer a ground-level perspective on life under colonialism.

나는 본래부터 무엇이나 모아 둘 줄 모르는 습관이 잇서서 친구에게서 밧은 편지 한 장인들 모아둔 일이 업섯다.
그러나 만흔 편지 중에 인상에 아직도 남어잇는 편지 몃 개가 잇스니 그 이약이나 적어보겟다.

金兄! 그간 안녕하심닛가. 편지를 밧고도 너무나 오래 답장도 못드린 弟를 용서하십시오.
그곳에서의 모든 일은 잘 되어 나가는지요. 모든 것을 다 물니치고 일부러 떠나신 길임으로 특별히 모든 일이 잘 되어 가기만 祝禱함니다.
문화 레벨을 말슴하섯지요. 사실 兄의 말슴가티 한발 日本 땅에 들어서면 朝鮮의 모든 것이 한심하기 짝이 업슴니다. 이 뒤느저진 朝鮮의 문화를 日本의 레벨까지 아니 세상의 레벨까지 끌어올니는 것은 우리들의 책임이 아니겟슴닛가. 이것을 空言이 아니라.
사실이니 우리의 책임은 정말 중대라 할 것임니다. 하기야 하필 문화 뿐이겟슴닛가 만은 이런 것이야 엇잿든 얼는 그곳에서 地盤을 잡도록 노력해 주십시요. 형과 우리들과 朝鮮 全體를 위해서.
京都에는 弟도 두 번 17才때와 4년 전에 들니어 2,3일식 묵은 일이 잇슴니다. 지금도 생각하면 글자대로의 山紫水明京都風景이 그림가티 머리에 떠오름니다. 鴨川의 맑은 물, 붉고 푸르게 丹靑칠한 누각과 다리(橋), 綠蔭지튼 산, 밝든 거리, 저녁을 먹고 여관의 裕衣를 걸치고 京極으로 祗園으로 漫步하는 年前의 기억이 지금 새로워 짐니다. 형! 충분히 공부해 주십시오.
貞熙氏는 형 떠나신 후 겨우 한번 맛낫슬 뿐임니다. 貞熙氏나 弟나 일부러 맛날 기회를 맨드는 것도 우습고, 자연 맛날 기회도 업고해서 각금 차저뵈오랴면서 그리 되엇슴니다. 문학이란 지식과 交友로 되는 것이 아니나. 그저 多讀多作을 勸해 주십시요. 제일 위험한 것은 쩌-낼리슴에 흘으는 것이겟지요. 그리되면 자기의 실력은 업스면서 공연히 남의 것을 경시만 하고 古典과 傑作을 「あちいて」 공부하지 못하게 되기 때문임니다. 貞熙氏는 잡지사에 게신이 만치 그런 위험이 만켓지오. 破璃의 곱부의 싸늘한 촉각, 銀으로 맨든 그릇의 乳白色反射 스트로-끄테 늣기는 신선한 소-다水의 미각, 어름과 샛발간 딸기의 대조, 거품 끌은 맥주―의 녀름은 이곳에서 지터감니다.
형은 京都의 영화인들과 이러한 녀름을 지낼 것을 근심하셧지오. 그러나 항상 흡족하지 못한 우리의 현실이 잇는 이상, 一時의 歡樂 한 곱부의 맥주가 엇더케 우리의 의지를 꺽겟슴니가. 蔽一言하고 하로밧비 그곳에서 地盤을 잡으십시오. 모든 문제는 그 후로.
京都이야기 각금 들녀 주십시오.

田榮澤氏로부터 方仁根氏에게
두 번재 엽서는 반가히 바덧소. 그 동안 소식 업서서 퍽 답답햇슬 줄 아오. 내 형편은 말할나위업소. 더운데 主日날도 몃 10리식 길을 것고 두 번 세 번 설교를 하고, 월요일에야 도라오고, 수요일에도 나갓다가 목요일에 도라오고, 그 동안 夏期兒童聖經學校를 하느라고 친히 가라치고 지금은 학교 일을 마터서 한 반 가라치고―집에서는 아이 보고, 똥치고, 방 쓸고 禮拜堂 소제까지 내가 하는 형편이오. 먹는 것은 아조 말할 것 업고 하니, 몸은 고단 할 때로 고단하고 정신업소.
편지 한 줄 알엇더니 아니햇구만 정신업서 基報 原稿는 지금 시작햇스니 4, 5일간 內로 부치겟소.
그래도 元山도 다니고, 釋王寺도 다니고 팬치 안엇구만 나는 땀만 흘니고 길만 것는데! 그래도 나는 농촌에서 지내니 위생상으로 보아서 그런데 갈 心要가 업겟지요.
