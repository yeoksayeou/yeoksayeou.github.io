Publication Date: 1933년 03월 01일
Article Title: 봄마지 (Welcoming Spring)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Welcoming Spring
						P'ain

Holding hand in hand
That young lady in yellow shoes
Where are you coming from?

Where the willow trees hang low
Because they said spring has come (봄이 오섯다기)
I came out to meet spring (봄 마증 나왓슴네), where is spring (봄 어대 게시온가)?

Spring, you say spring,
Would spring be somewhere separate (봄이 따로 잇사오리)?
It has already come and sits upon your smiling brow (아미에).

**Summary**

This short poem by P'ain uses a dialogue format to depict the welcoming of spring. A speaker encounters a young woman ("각시") who has come out seeking spring after hearing it arrived. The speaker poetically responds that spring isn't a separate entity to be found elsewhere but is already present in the woman's own joyful expression, specifically her smiling brow. For historians, the poem provides an example of lyrical expression during the colonial period, focusing on nature and beauty. While not overtly political, the theme of spring's arrival might subtly resonate with underlying hopes for renewal or change during a difficult era in Korean history. The use of slightly archaic spellings and grammar also offers linguistic data from the 1930s.
