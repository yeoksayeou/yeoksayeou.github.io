A Beautiful Woman's Self-Portrait, Apart from the Trends
						Cha Sa-baek

They say people live for the thrill of their own self-importance. Even when just two or three people gather, you can see them competing to boast about themselves. Isn't this something that stems from the instinctive iron self-esteem of human life? If I too lacked self-esteem, perhaps I might have laid my head on the rails (로 궤도로 벼개하엿슬넌지). Perhaps I might have bathed forever in the deep waters of the Han River. How could one possibly live knowing they are inadequate? Seeing as my self-esteem is stronger than anyone else's, I too would like to boast strongly about myself. However, whether good or bad, I intend to honestly confess about myself. And so, I will now list a few things.

1.  My self-esteem is strong. Therefore, I try to do all the work myself and hate saying I can't do something to others. Also, when I intend to do something, I try to finish it right away in one go. I hate seeing things drag on sloppily (흘게 느진 것).

2.  My sense of responsibility is extremely strong. If it's a task I've taken on, I cannot bear not doing it.

3.  I am nervous, so if something happens suddenly, I break out in a cold sweat, and staying up all night is commonplace. Still, I am also famous for sleeping well, but if I miss just one night's sleep, I'm practically dead.

4.  I lack preparedness, so I have a habit of scrambling to do things only when they are immediately upon me. <4>

**Summary**

The author, Cha Sa-baek, reflects on the human tendency towards self-importance, rooted in self-esteem, admitting their own is very strong. They then proceed to list their personality traits: intense pride leading to wanting to do everything themselves and finish tasks quickly, a very strong sense of responsibility, nervousness coupled with an ability to sleep well unless deprived, and a lack of foresight resulting in last-minute scrambling. From a historical perspective, this personal reflection offers a glimpse into individual self-perception and expression during the colonial period. While not overtly political, the frank discussion of personality, including flaws like nervousness and lack of preparation, presented in a popular magazine, provides insight into the personal lives and psychological states individuals might have navigated and publicly shared within the constraints of the era. Historians might find the candid tone and focus on individual psychology notable for a 1933 Korean publication.

사람은 저 잘난 맛에 산다고 함니다. 두 세 사람 모인 데서라도 다투어 가며 자기 자랑하는 것을 볼 수 잇습니다. 이것은 인생의 본능적인 철 자존심에서 나오는 것이 아님니까. 나도 자존심이 업섯드면 로 궤도로 벼개하엿슬넌지. 한강수 깁흔 물에 영원히 목욕하엿슬 넌지도 모르겟슴니다. 못난 줄을 알고야 엇지 살수 잇겟슴니까. 자존심이 누구보다도 강한 편으로 보아서는 나를 힘것 자랑하고도 십슴니다. 그러나 조흐나 그르나 나는 솔직하게 나를 고백해 보랴고 함니다. 그래서 이제 몃 가지를 들려고 함니다.
1. 자존심이 강함니다. 그래서 일은 제가 다하랴고 하며 남에게 못한다는 말을 하기 시려함니다. 그러고 하려든 것은 꼭 단번에 끗을 내려고 함니다. 느럭느럭하는 흘게 느진 것은 보기 실혀 함니다.
2. 몹시 책임감이 강함니다. 자기 마튼 일이면 아니하고는 견댈 수가 업슴니다.
3. 신경질이 되여서 척하면 진땀을 쭉 흘니며 밤새우기를 예사로 함니다. 그래도 또 잠 잘자기로 유명한데 하로 밤만 못 자면 거반 죽지요.
4. 예비성이 업서서 무슨 일이든지 당장에 하노라고 쩔쩔 매는 버릇이 잇슴니다. ＜4＞
