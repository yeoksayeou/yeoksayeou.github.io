기사형태	소식 (News)

文壇小話

It is said that at a literary roundtable discussion hosted by the Tonga Ilbo, Mr. Kim Tongin (金東仁氏) stated that writing novels for newspapers is done to earn money, not out of artistic impulse. The next day, the Tonga Ilbo's "Horizontal Talk Vertical Talk" (橫說竪說) column carried a piece lamenting the decline in writers' integrity, and at the same time, the company reportedly decided as a matter of policy not to publish Mr. Kim's writings in the Tonga Ilbo anymore.

Thinking about it, there is fault on both sides. The view that newspaper novels must necessarily be written at a low level is unpleasant. That is a matter of the writer's natural talent (天分); while focusing on entertainment can be an attribute of popular novels, it is not the whole story. As a real example, look at Mr. Yamamoto Yūzō's (山本有三氏) "Woman's Life" (女の一生) currently running in the Taichō (大朝).

Though it's a serialized novel, how profoundly each word and phrase stirs our heartstrings! How artistically fragrant (郁芳) it is! And yet, it does not lose its popular appeal in the slightest. I believe the time has come to reconsider the popular novels in various Chosŏn newspapers, which, emphasizing entertainment, entertainment, tend to flow towards a low tone.

Furthermore, the Tonga Ilbo has two faulty attitudes. First, it bows too much to money. Offering manuscript fees (稿料) of only 2 or 3 Wŏn, it holds its nose too high, having the bad habit of trying to settle everything with paltry sums of money. Second, it exposes its utter lack of understanding regarding Mr. Kim Tongin's art. Mr. Kim Tongin has been writing for the Tonga Ilbo for 5 or 6 years. Dozens of his novels and essays have been published. Given this, it only makes sense that they published his work knowing his art. Despite this, upon hearing his single remark today, they fuss about whether to publish him or not from now on—it's just like children fighting. Thinking about it, regardless of what Mr. Kim Tongin said at that meeting, if they had correctly viewed his art, wouldn't it be right to continue receiving his work with humble deference (低頭卑辭) and publish it for the long term?

Recently, the issue of female writers (여류작가) has arisen. Some say, "I never called myself a female writer, but others lift me up only to criticize me." Others say, "You barely have a few works, what kind of writer are you?" etc.—both sides. They sit around engaging in yawn-inducing talk. "Female writer" is merely an occupational title like lawyer, newspaper reporter, or bank clerk. If one writes, they can be called a writer. As for the argument that one doesn't have many works, that is a matter of degree (程度問題). In short, it's something to be dismissed as idle talk by idle people (閑人의 閑話).

Many male and female writers appeared in the performance of the Drama Arts Research Society (劇藝術硏究會) held at the Public Hall (公會堂). A good trend. If there were just one Osanai Kaoru (小山內薰), the new theater movement on the peninsula wouldn't be this desolate (蕭條).<98>

**Summary**

This article reports on literary gossip from 1933 Korea. It covers the controversy surrounding writer Kim Tongin's statement that newspaper novels are primarily for money, leading the Tonga Ilbo newspaper to blacklist him. The author criticizes both Kim's view (citing Yamamoto Yūzō's high-quality serialized novel as a counterexample) and the Tonga Ilbo's overreaction and alleged stinginess. It also dismisses the ongoing debate about the legitimacy and definition of "female writers" as trivial. Finally, it notes the positive trend of writers participating in theater but laments the poor state of the new theater movement in Korea, wishing for a figure like Japan's Osanai Kaoru.

From a historical perspective, the article reveals tensions between art and commercialism within the colonial Korean literary scene, particularly concerning the popular medium of newspaper serialization. It highlights the power dynamics between writers and influential media outlets like the Tonga Ilbo. The discussion on female writers points to the emerging visibility and accompanying scrutiny of women in the public literary sphere. The reference to Yamamoto Yūzō and Osanai Kaoru underscores the significant cultural influence of Japan on Korean arts during this period, while also expressing a desire for indigenous development in areas like modern theater.

文壇小話
金東仁氏가 東亞日報 主催의 文士座談會席上에서 신문에 소설 쓰는 것은 돈 벌기 위한 노릇이요, 예술적 충동으로 그리하는 것이 아니랫더니, 그 이튼날의 東亞紙橫說竪說에 문인의 節操의 타락을 개탄하는 一誅의 글이 실니는 동시에 東亞紙에는 金氏 글을 아니 실기로 社의 방침이 정하여젓다 한다.
생각컨대 두 편에 다 잘못이 잇다. 신문소설이라고 반드시 低級하게 써야 된다는 견해에는 불쾌를 늣기게 된다. 그는 필자의 天分問題이지, 흥미 중심이 통속소설의 속성은 될 수 잇스나 전체는 아닐 것이다. 實例로 요지음 大朝에 나는 山本有三氏의 「女の一生」를 보라.
연재소설이면서 一言一句가 엇더케나 심오하게 우리의 심금을 울니는가. 예술적 향기가 郁芳하든가. 그러면서도 대중성을 조곰도 일치 안는다. 이 기회에 흥미 흥미하고 작고만 低調에 흘너가는 朝鮮 各紙上의 통속소설은 고려할 때가 왓다고 본다.
또 東亞日報에 잘못된 태도 두 가지가 잇다. 一曰 돈에 너무 姿勢한다. 2, 3圓式 주는 稿料에 너무 코이 놉허, 돈푼으로 모든 일을 規結지으려는 惡弊가 잇다. 二曰 東仁氏는 예술에 대하야 너무 이해가 업는 것을 폭로한다. 東仁氏는 東亞日報에 집필한 지 5, 6년은 된다. 소설, 수필류가 실니운 것도 수십 편이 된다. 이만하면 東仁氏의 예술을 알고 실엇대서야 말이 된다. 그런 것을 今日에 이르러 氏의 一言을 듯고 이제부터 싯네, 아니싯네―도모지 어린 아히 싸홈갓다. 생각컨대 東仁氏야 그 席上에서 무어라 햇든 氏의 예술을 올케 보앗다면 장래 長久히 低頭卑辭로 밧어다가 실어야 올치 안을가.

최근에 여류작가 문제가 들닌다. 曰 나는 언제 여류작가라고 자칭한 적도 업는데, 남들이 치켜올녀 세워 노코 辱을 먹인다. 又曰 작품 몃 낫도 업는데 네가 무슨 작가냐 云云―두 편이다. 하품나는 이약이들을 하고 안젓다. 여류작가란 변호사, 신문기자, 은행원 가튼 직업적 名稱에 불과하다. 글쓰니 작가라 할 것이요, 또 작품이 몃낫 안 된다고 云云하지만 그는 程度問題라. 요컨대 閑人의 閑話로 돌닐 일.

公會堂에서 열닌 劇藝術硏究會의 출연에 남녀 문인들이 만히 등장하엿다. 조흔 경향. 一人의 小山內薰이 잇스면 半島新劇運動이 이러틋 蕭條하지는 안으련만.＜98＞
