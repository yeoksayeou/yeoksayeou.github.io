**Body Text Translation:**
Samch'ŏlli Confidential Room
Taxes Skyrocket for Capital's Wealthiest

Yi: "Hey, Director-General of Political Affairs Ōno says Korea will also see tax increases, just like mainland Japan. They plan to collect about 4 million more wŏn. If that happens, how much more tax will the wealthy tycoons in the capital end up paying?"
Kim: "Right. That's a good topic. But since the tax rates to be applied in Korea haven't been announced yet, let's assume for now that the increase rate will be the same as in the mainland (and it probably will be) and talk about the inheritance tax for the capital's wealthy. Of course, inheritance tax refers to the property that automatically rolls over to the son when the father dies. Now, listen up. The tax increase rate by Finance Minister Baba this time is 100%. For example, if the late Min Pokuk, who was rumored to have 10 million wŏn in assets, distributed (synonymous with inherited) this wealth to his descendants like Messrs. Ch'ungsik, Taesik, and Kyusik, before this decree was announced, they would have had to pay 180/1000, or 1.8 million wŏn out of 10 million wŏn, to the national treasury. But now, it's 400/1000, so they'd have to pay 4 million wŏn, almost half, as inheritance tax."
Yi: "The more money wealthy people pay to the country, the better, right? If they pay a lot and that money is used for social policy facilities to help us 'Western-suited loafers'<15> live better, wouldn't that be great? —But instead of using examples of gentlemen who have already passed away, give me some real examples of living people."
Kim: "Alright— According to the Kyŏngsŏng Municipal Income Tax Survey, 19th-ranked Mr. Min Kyusik is still a Korean tycoon with about 3 million wŏn. If he were to pass away 50 or 80 years from now, the inheritance tax that elder would pay to the national treasury would be around 1.1 million wŏn. If we talk about Mr. Yun Ch'iho, rumored to have a net worth of 2 million wŏn without any debt, it would be about 700,000 wŏn. Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl, who entrusted all his land assets to the Chosŏn Trust, valued at 1 million wŏn, would pay about 300,000 wŏn. Mr. Kim Kyesu, said to have more money than his brother with a real worth of 2 million wŏn, about 700,000 wŏn. Mr. Min Ch'ungsik, with 2.5 million wŏn, about 900,000 wŏn. Mr. Min Taesik, with a harvest of 6,000 sŏk, valued at 120* wŏn [per sŏk? value unclear], so about 400,000 wŏn. Mr. Han Yangho, also with 7,000 sŏk and other assets totaling 1.4 million wŏn, even if reduced*, about 400,000 wŏn. Mr. Kim Chongson, with 2 million wŏn, 700,000 wŏn. Mr. Sŏ Pyŏnghak of Taegu, with about 1 million wŏn, 200,000 wŏn. Mr. Ch'oe Sŏnik, who used to run the Chungang Ilbo, with 1 million wŏn, 200,000 wŏn. Mr. Im Ch'ungsang, with 1 million wŏn, 200,000 wŏn. Mr. Im Hosang, with 1 million wŏn, 200,000 wŏn. Mr. U Chonggwan, with 600,000 wŏn, about 100,000 wŏn. Gold Mine King, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'anghak, with 3 million wŏn, 1.1 million wŏn. Mr. Pang Eungmo, with 1.5 million wŏn, 400,000 wŏn. Mr. Wŏn Yunsu, with 6 million wŏn, 2.4 million wŏn. Mr. Yun Hŭijung, with 1 million wŏn, 200,000 wŏn..."
Yi: "Hey, that's enough. You're rattling them off like you're talking about 1 or 2 wŏn, but for someone with a weak heart like me, just hearing it makes me dizzy. Stop it. Anyway, how much more net money is expected to roll into the mainland* due to this 100% increase in inheritance tax?"
Kim: "What used to be a total of 30 million wŏn last year has doubled, so it's roughly 60 million wŏn."

Tax Increase Amount for Banks and Companies

Yi: "I understand about individual rich people, but what about the taxes paid by the large banks and companies in Korea, which are said to be raking in money like crazy due to the best economy seen since the Great European War? How much more will they increase?"
Kim: "Alright, this year, take a deep breath, prevent dizziness,<16> and listen with a continental heart. For banks, stock companies, limited partnerships, and general partnerships, the income tax rate has been significantly increased. Wait a moment... which company should I mention first?"
Yi: "Talk about Hwasin, that's easy."
Kim: "Right, that's easy— Of course, this assumes the tax rate will be the same as the mainland. Based on that— Among Hwasin companies, the easiest to understand is 'Hwasin Chain Store Stock Co.' According to President Pak and Executive Director Chu, if each of the 300 chain stores across Korea has annual sales of 10,000 wŏn, the total sales volume is 3 million wŏn. Now, after deducting all operating expenses and personnel costs, if they make a profit of, say, 5%, the Type 1 corporate income tax is raised from the current 5% to 10%, so how much is that..."
Yi: "Isn't it risky to bring up Hwasin, Tongil Bank, or Kyŏngsŏng Textiles as examples just because they're easy to talk about? Might it affect the financial market?"
Kim: "That's true too. Investigating and publishing the profit figures of other companies is really something to refrain from, unless you're the Tax Office. Then, shall I use examples of large companies in mainland Japan, where there's no such concern?"
Yi: "That would be better. What if 'Samch'ŏlli' runs into a boycott*? Wouldn't that be pitiful?"
Kim: "Even a fool's thousand thoughts may yield one gain (愚者千慮 必有一得). Sometimes you actually talk sense. Hahaha... Alright, let's look at the mainland side.
The Industrial Bank of Japan (勤業銀行) has an annual net profit of 13 million wŏn. According to the new tax rate, it's about 10%, amounting to 1.6 million wŏn (an increase of 800,000 wŏn). The Hypothec Bank of Japan (興業銀行) has an annual profit of 4 million wŏn, so 430,000 wŏn. Mitsubishi Bank, with a capital of 130 million wŏn, has a capital tax of 200,000 wŏn, and with a net profit of 10 million wŏn, about 4 million wŏn [likely corporate income tax, not just capital tax]. Sumitomo Bank (住友銀行) is roughly the same, 10 million wŏn profit, 1 million wŏn [tax].
Nippon Yūsen (日本郵船) has 6 million wŏn profit, 600,000 wŏn tax. Kanegafuchi Bōseki (鍾淵紡織), profit amount unclear, but tax is 1.4 million wŏn.
Tōyō Bōseki (東洋紡織) has 1.2 million wŏn profit, 1.1 million wŏn tax.
Dai Nippon Bōseki (大日本紡織) has 2.7 million wŏn profit, 2 million wŏn tax."
Yi: "Hey, that's enough. Somehow I feel like I've become the president of a big rich company myself, just getting bloated on air."
Kim: "If you have no money, you should at least have a strong heart— That's why you've never held even 1,000 wŏn in your life and came into this life wrongly. Hahaha... Just you wait and see. Next time, when the tax rates to be implemented in Korea<17> are announced, I'll tell you all about the profit amounts and income taxes of all the banks and companies in Korea, down to the last detail..."

Department Store Sales Reach 3 Million Wŏn

Last year-end, not only were bonuses from the Government-General and various government offices and companies generally larger than usual, but it was truly said to be the best economy since the Great European War. As such, the business climate in Seoul's shopping districts showed a boom, averaging a 30% increase compared to previous years. Among these, department stores saw the biggest gains. The five department stores in Seoul - Hwasin (和信), Mitsukoshi (三越), Hirata (平田), Minakai (三中井), and Chōjiya (丁子屋) - started their year-end grand sales with prizes on December 1st. In the approximately 20 days until December 20th, total sales reached 1.4 million wŏn, and from the 21st to the 31st, they reached another 1.6 million wŏn, breaking a total of 3 million wŏn. Averaging this among the aforementioned 5 department stores, it amounts to a huge sum of 600,000 wŏn for the single month of December, meaning they sold 20,000 wŏn worth of goods each day.

Prosperity of the Sannō Hotel

The Sannō Hotel in Tokyo's Shiba Ward, which served as the headquarters for the rebel forces during the 2-26 Incident, has drawn significant public attention since the event. Travelers eager to stay there at least once have flocked to it, leading to great prosperity with crowds at its gates (門前萬客來). An executive from a certain newspaper company who went over from Seoul also recently stayed there overnight. According to what this gentleman related, the hotel's female manager and maids said that people assume the leading officers of the rebel army would look very fierce and rugged, but that wasn't the case. They were handsome young officers, even using polite language like "そうですか" (Sō desu ka - Is that so?) towards the maids, and whenever they asked for an errand, they always gave a tip of 1 or 2 yen.

Lieutenant Colonel Yi Yang to the Army War College

Mr. Yi Yang, a Lieutenant Colonel in the Manchukuo Army who was serving as a military attaché in Peiping (北平), recently entered the Military Officer School (軍官學校) in Hsinking (新京). This Military Officer School is Manchukuo's highest military education institution, comparable to Tokyo's Army War College (陸軍大學), and graduating from here offers the prospect of becoming a Major General. The term is 6 months, so Lieutenant Colonel Yi Yang will graduate next spring. If fortune smiles upon him, he might even leap to Major General after graduation.

Dancer Ch'oe's Farewell Dance

Miss Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, the genius dancer acclaimed as the "Dancer of the Peninsula," is scheduled<18> to embark on a dance tour to Europe this spring, in April when flowers bloom and larks sing, to widely introduce the "Classical Art of the Peninsula" to the world. Prior to that, she wishes to perform a farewell dance in her homeland. She will return to Seoul on February 21st and dance like a butterfly to her heart's content at the Pumin'gwan (府民舘) for two days, the 22nd and 23rd.

"Ch'ŏngch'unhwa" Troupe's Business Success

The "Tongyang 극장" (東洋劇場 - Oriental Theater) outside Seoul's Sŏdaemun Gate, managed and directed by Messrs. Hong Sunŏn and Ch'oe Tokkyŏn, houses several exclusive troupes like Ch'ŏngch'unhwa (靑春座), Hŭigŭkchwa (喜劇座), and Yŏn'gŭk Howasŏn (演劇豪華船), performing alternately in Seoul and the provinces. Among them, the most popular and commercially successful is "Ch'ŏngch'unhwa." It always plays to full houses during its Seoul performances and is popular in the provinces too. On its recent provincial tour along the Honam Line, it reportedly earned a net profit of 6,000 wŏn after deducting all expenses over about 40 days and returned.

Hwasin Hires 200 New Employees

Hwasin Department Store on Chongno, having rented the entire new 7-story building constructed by Mr. Han Haksŏu to establish its West Wing (西舘), thus completing its new setup after the great fire two years prior, hired 200 new employees for the opening of this new wing. Even if calculated at just 40 wŏn per month salary, this is a significant increase in personnel costs, amounting to 8,000 wŏn per month and 90,000 wŏn per year.
Rent 2,600 Wŏn - So, how much rent does Hwasin, having rented this new West Wing building, pay per month? It's 2,650 wŏn monthly, totaling about 30,000 wŏn per year. On the other hand, how is the building valued? The land is 240 p'yŏng, located at the Chongno intersection, so at 1,000 wŏn per p'yŏng, the land value alone is 240,000 wŏn. The building construction cost is calculated at 150 wŏn per p'yŏng, and with a total floor space of about 1,000 p'yŏng, its value is 150,000 wŏn. Thus, the total valuation is around 400,000 wŏn. Since the annual rent income from the building is close to 10% of its value, it's said that in just 10 years, the land cost and construction expenses will be fully recovered, and after 10 years, the land and building will essentially be free and clear.

Booming Tea Houses in Seoul

Tea houses (喫茶店), which can be called the "parks of the street" in modern cities, are gradually increasing in Seoul, and they are all reportedly doing well. For starters, Venus (매담 Ms. Pok Hyesuk) in Insadong (仁寺町) has average daily sales of 10[?] wŏn, about 1,000 wŏn per month. Nangnang (樂浪) (매담 Ms. Kim Yŏnsil) in Chang*yong-dong (長*用町) also averages 40 wŏn daily, bringing in 1,200 to 1,300 wŏn per month.<19>

**Summary**

The article, presented as a dialogue and news snippets, discusses the anticipated impact of Japanese tax hikes (particularly inheritance tax) on wealthy Korean individuals and businesses in 1937 Seoul (Kyŏngsŏng). It details specific fortunes and estimated tax burdens for prominent figures and contrasts Korean circumstances with examples from mainland Japan. The piece also covers miscellaneous topics: booming year-end sales at Seoul department stores, the unexpected popularity of Tokyo's Sannō Hotel after the 2-26 Incident, the career advancement of a Manchukuo military officer, dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's upcoming European tour and farewell performance, the success of a Korean theater troupe, Hwasin Department Store's expansion and associated costs/profits, and the thriving business of Seoul's modern tea houses.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into the economic anxieties and realities of the Korean elite under Japanese colonial rule, particularly concerning taxation policies mirroring those in Japan. It provides concrete (though sometimes speculative) figures on wealth concentration, business activity (department stores, banks, theaters, tea houses), real estate values (Chongno), and the growing consumer culture in colonial Seoul. The mention of specific individuals, businesses, cultural figures like Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, and events like the 2-26 Incident provides context for the social and political atmosphere of the time.

三千里機密室
長安 甲富의 稅金激增

二.二六事事 때의 叛亂軍 總本部이었든 東京芝區의 山王台호텔은 事件 후 世上의 人氣를 크게 集中하야 한번 가서 그 집에서 잠을 자보겠다는 旅客이 踏至하야 門前萬客來로 크게 繁榮하고 있다 하는데, 서울서 건너간 某新聞社 幹部도 요전에 거기 가서 一泊하고 왔다 한다. 그런데 그 氏가 전하는 말을 듯건대, 그 집 女매담과 下女가 이약이 하기를 叛亂軍 首腦士官들이라하면 몹시 우락부락하게 생긴 줄 아나 그러치 않어서 보기에는 美貌의 젊은 靑年士官들로서 말소리도 下女들에게 대하여까지 꼭꼭 「そうですか」의 敬語를 쓰고, 그리고 무슨 심부름을 시키면 반다시 1, 2圓의 팁을 주더라고.
