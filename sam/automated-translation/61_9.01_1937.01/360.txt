Arinari Tune of Twilight
						Ham Hyŏngsu

As if startled, as if pursued, on the riverbank at twilight,
A huddled, elegant group.
Oh, unable even to cry out loftily,
They just dig at the dark earth
And weep with pitiful snorts.

Against the sunset-stained, blood-colored sky,
Holding erect your precious antlers, O deer,
On the darkening Arinari riverbank,
Close your eyes downwards and suppress your anxiety.

Ah, on the riverbank, the sun has suddenly set.
Your delicate legs,
Stop stamping them small, O deer,
And listen intently to the flow of distant history. <289>
Note: Arinari—Ancient name for the Yalu River (鴨綠江)
—(For Dance)<290>
<289-290>

**Summary**

This poem by Ham Hyŏngsu paints a melancholic scene of deer huddled on the banks of the Arinari River (ancient name for the Yalu) at twilight. The deer, symbols of elegance yet vulnerability, are unable to cry out and instead anxiously dig at the earth. The poem urges the deer to suppress its anxiety and listen to the flow of history as darkness falls. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's somber tone, the imagery of vulnerable creatures at a significant border river (the Yalu), and the call to reflect on history could be interpreted as an allegorical representation of Korea's plight under Japanese rule—a sense of being trapped, anxious, and needing to connect with a deeper historical consciousness amidst the encroaching darkness. The specification "(For Dance)" suggests it might have been intended to accompany a performance, perhaps adding another layer to its potential expression of cultural identity or sorrow during that period.

黃昏의 아리나리曲
						咸亨洙
놀란들 쫓긴듣 黃昏의 江畔에
옹송그리는 優雅한 무리.
오오 높다라히 울지도 몯하고
검은 땅만 파헤치며
구슬피 코우름 운다.

노을진 피빛 하눌에
貴로운 뿔 고추드러 사슴아
저므는 아리나리江畔에
눈 나리감고 焦燥를 눌러라.

아아 江畔에 해는 깜박 저무렀다.
연약한 네 다리
작고만 구르지말고 사슴아
아득한 歷史의 흐름에 귀기우려라. <289>
註 아리나리―鴨綠江의 古稱
―(爲舞踊)<290>
