Article Title: 雪上二篇 (Two Pieces on Snow)
Author: 尹克榮
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Two Pieces on Snow
						Yun Kŭk-yŏng

Snow descends and covers the ground
Upon it, footprints
One

Three...

The sun has risen, and the wind blows
Scatters the snow
The footprints fade away

My Memory (記憶)
Digs into the endless Strata (地層), this sorrowful Past (過去)
Runs—like an arrow—...<323>

**Summary**

This short poem contrasts the temporary nature of footprints left in the snow, easily erased by the wind, with the persistent, piercing quality of memory that digs into the layers of a sorrowful past. The footprints vanish quickly, but memory actively seeks out and penetrates the past like an arrow. From a colonial history perspective, the imagery of fading footprints could symbolize the erasure of Korean identity or experiences under Japanese rule, while the act of memory digging into a "sorrowful past" might represent the struggle to retain or uncover a suppressed history. The poem's melancholic tone and focus on disappearing traces versus persistent memory could resonate with the experiences of Koreans during that era.
