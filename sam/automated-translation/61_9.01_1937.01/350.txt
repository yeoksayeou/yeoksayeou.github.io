Ode to Goryeo Celadon
						Ham Hyŏngsu

In infinite Time and boundless Space,
Your one unmovable, sublime position.
There lies the heavy accumulation of Art,
There lies the shining revelation of Art.

Neither fierce nor frail,
Your mysteriously rounded curves.
There surges a profound undulation,
There pools a beautiful jade-green light.

Neither dark nor bright,
Your wondrous, dusky blue-green color.
There frosts a noble contemplation,
There dwells an immortal spiritual energy.

In bounded Space and limited Time <288>
Your one distinct, beautiful solid form.
There slumbers a solemn unity,
There lies a grand resolution.
—(For Dance)<289>

**Summary**

This poem by Ham Hyŏngsu is a lyrical tribute to the aesthetic qualities of Goryeo celadon. It praises the pottery's timelessness, sublime form, unique curves, mysterious color (often described as 'bisaek' or jade-green), and the sense of profound artistry and spiritual depth it embodies. The poet contrasts the infinite and the finite, positioning the celadon as a fixed, beautiful object bridging these concepts through its artistic perfection. From a historical perspective, celebrating a pinnacle of traditional Korean art like Goryeo celadon in 1937, during the height of Japanese colonial rule, can be interpreted as an assertion of Korean cultural identity and artistic achievement. It implicitly highlights the distinctiveness and value of Korean heritage in a period marked by assimilation pressures, making it a subtle expression of cultural pride and potentially resistance. The note "For Dance" suggests an interdisciplinary artistic interest, possibly linking this traditional art form to contemporary performance.

高麗磁器頌
						咸亨洙
無限한 時間과 無邊한 空間속에
하나 움지길 수 없는 崇高한 네 位置.
거기에는 무거운 藝術의 蓄積이 있고
빛나는 예술의 啓示가 있다.

사납지도 않고 연약하지도 않고
두리두리 구브러진 네 神祕로운 曲線.
거기에는 幽玄한 仰揚이 구비치고
아름다운 비치 어리었다.

어둡지도 않고 밝지도 않고
우중충 푸른 네 不可思議한 色彩.
거기에는 高貴한 思念이 서리었고
不死의 靈氣가 어리었다.

旣邊한 空間과 旣限한 時間속에 <288>
하나 뚜렸한 아릅다운 네 立體.
거기에는 嚴肅한 統一이 잠겨있고
거창한 解決이 누어있다.
―(爲舞踊)<289>
