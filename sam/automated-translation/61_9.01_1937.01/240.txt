**Body Text Translation:**
New Film: Omongnyŏ

A small port town on the East Coast.
Still, it's a village with a *myŏn* office and a post office, a coastal market where quite a lot of people gather from nearby towns on market days.
In this village, the house of Chi Ch'am-bong, famous enough that everyone knows it, is located a little way off to the north of the village.
He was famous for being good at fortune-telling, but he was even more on everyone's lips because he lived with only one other family member, Omongnyŏ, the prettiest girl in the village. Although it's said he bought her for 100 *nyang* when she was twelve and raised her, nobody knows the exact details. When the Ch'am-bong's first wife was alive, she was regarded only as an adopted daughter, but lately, rumors have even spread that the two are living together. Whatever the truth of these rumors, Omongnyŏ grew up spoiled under Chi Ch'am-bong's indulgence, loved snacks like sweets (菓子) since childhood, and recently became an expert (選手) at stealing.
Pickpocketing a blind person was commonplace for her, but lately, she started seeking out unattended boats tied up at the shore, stealing fish, selling them, and making funds for snacks. The person who suffered the most damage from this was the village bachelor, Kim Tol-i.
Angered by the repeated damage, Kim Tol-i kept watch one night by the seashore. Unexpectedly, when he saw that the culprit was the pretty young woman Omongnyŏ, being a young bachelor, he lacked even the strength to catch her. From that day on, Kim's once peaceful mind began to get tangled up because of this matter.

Kim Tol-i wasn't the only one captivated by Omongnyŏ. Among them, Nam Min-gu, who ran a confectionery business (菓子商), and Master Im, the owner of the bicycle shop (自轉車舖), were the ones most tormented because of Omongnyŏ.<178>
Master Pak, unable to think of anything else, decided to sell his bicycle shop and run away with Omongnyŏ. Nam Min-gu, noticing this, reported it to Chi Ch'am-bong. This was because it was better to leave her entrusted to Chi Ch'am-bong than to lose her completely because of Master Pak. Chi Ch'am-bong, hearing this from Nam-ga (南哥), bought five locks that night, locked every single door, left only the kitchen (부억) door open where Omongnyŏ might enter, and waited for her return. As soon as she came in, he planned to lock the door, put in the hairpin he had prepared three years ago, put her hair up, and make her his woman (제 게집). The night deepened. Footsteps were heard. Chi Ch'am-bong held his breath and waited. The footsteps entered through the door. The door was locked. He felt relieved, thinking, "Now Omongnyŏ is mine," but the person who stepped inside was not Omongnyŏ, but Nam-ga, who harbored feelings for her. Furious, Chi Ch'am-bong grabbed Nam-ga by the collar and yelled, demanding Omongnyŏ. (Nam) ("He sold the bicycle shop today, so she must have run away with Master Pak.")
However, that wasn't it either.
Master Pak, having sold the bicycle shop, packed his belongings, and came to Chi Ch'am-bong's house to take Omongnyŏ away, had just arrived at the gate. The sound of a rooster crowing was heard. At that moment, Omongnyŏ had entered the bachelor's boat to steal fish. The empty boat moved, startling Omongnyŏ. As she tried to jump out, she saw Kim Tol-i rowing the boat. The boat was already far out. What use was it to struggle desperately now? The boat, carrying only the bachelor and the young woman, departs heading towards an uninhabited island. (End)

**Summary**

This text is a synopsis for the 1937 Korean film "Omongnyŏ," based on a story by Yi T'ae-jun and directed by Na Un-gyu. It describes Omongnyŏ, a beautiful young woman living with the elderly Chi Ch'am-bong in a coastal village. Raised indulgently, she turns to petty theft, particularly stealing fish from Kim Tol-i's boat. Several men, including Kim Tol-i, confectionery seller Nam Min-gu, and bicycle shop owner Master Pak, are infatuated with her. Master Pak plans to elope with her, leading Nam Min-gu to warn Chi Ch'am-bong, who then plots to lock Omongnyŏ in and make her his wife. However, Nam Min-gu walks into the trap instead. Meanwhile, Omongnyŏ is caught stealing fish again by Kim Tol-i, who rows her away towards an uninhabited island. The article concludes with film production credits. From a historical perspective, the synopsis reveals social dynamics in colonial Korea, including the ambiguous status of women like Omongnyŏ (bought, potentially coerced cohabitation), the competition among men for her affection, and hints at rural poverty driving petty crime. The detailed film credits also provide valuable information about the personnel and studio involved in the Korean film industry under Japanese rule.

新作映畵 五夢女
東海岸에서 적은 浦口
그래도 面事務所가 있고 郵便所가 잇는 海岸市場으로 장(市)날이면 近邑에서 사람이 꽤 많이 뫃이는 洞里다.
이 洞里에 名物노 누구가 모르는 사람이 업는 池叅奉네 집은 이 동리 북편 좀 떠러진 곳에 있다.
그는 점 잘치기로도 유명했지만 더구나 그가 여러 사람 입에 오르나리기는 洞里에서 第一 어엽뿐 계집애 五夢女와 단두 食口가 살기 때문이다. 열두살 될 때에 百兩주고 사다가 길 다하지만 그 內容을 確實히 아는 사람은 없었다. 叅奉 본처가 사리있을 때는 수양딸노만 역였던 것이 요새 와서는 둘이 같이 산다고 하는 風說까지 들닌다. 이 風說의 內容이야 얻더케 되였든 五夢女는 池叅奉엉석 속에 함부로 자라서 어렸을 때부터 菓子갓튼 군것질을 조아하다가 요새 와서는 도적질에도 選手가 되였다.
장님 주머니 털기는 여사지만 近來에는 海邊에 매어는 主人업는 배를 차저가 생선을 훔쳐다가 팔어서 군것질 미천 맨들기가 始作되였다. 이 被害를 第一 많이 받은 사람이 洞里總角 金돌이다.
金돌이는 매번 당하는 피해에 화가 나서 하롯밤은 해변에서 직혓다. 意外에도 적이 어엽뿐 처녀 五夢女인 것을 볼 때에 젊문 총각인 그는 잡을 힘조차 없었다. 그날부터 金의 이 평화하든 머리는 이 일로 얼크러지기 시작하였다.
五夢女 때문에 마음 홀닌 사람은 이 金돌이 뿐이 안니다. 菓子商하는 南民九와 自轉車舖 주인 林서방이 기중에도 第一 많이 五夢女 때문에 애 태는 사람들이다.<178>
朴서방은 생각다 못해서 自轉車舖를 팔이가지고 五夢女와 같이 도망하려고 했다. 이 눈치를 안 南民九는 이 일을 池叅奉에게 일넜다. 朴서방 때문에 아조 잃어버리는 것보다는 池叅奉에게 멧껴두는 편이 낫기 때문이다. 南哥에게서 이 말을 들은 池叅奉은 그날 밤 잠을쇠 다섰 개를 사다가 문이란 문은 다 잠궈노코 五夢女가 드러올만지(부억) 문만 여러노코 五夢女 도라오기를 기다렸다. 드리만오면 문을 잠궈버리고 3년 전부터 준비해 두었던 비녀를 끼여 머리를 언꼬 제 게집을 맨들녀는 것이다. 밤이 깁헛다. 발자죽 소리가 들닌다. 池叅奉은 숨을 죽이고 기다린다. 발자죽 소리는 문안으로 드러왔다. 문은 잠것다. 인제 五夢女는 내 것이다고 안심했으나 문 안에 드러선 사람은 五夢女가 아니요 마음을 드리든 南哥다. 성이 날때로 난 池叅奉은 南哥의 멱살을 잡고 五夢女를 내노라고 소리를 진느니(南) (自轉車舖를 오늘 팔엇는데 朴서방과 같이 도망한 게로군요)
그러나 그것도 아니다.
自轉車舖를 팔어가지고 行裝을 꾸려 가지고 五夢女 빼내리 온 朴서방이 池叅奉집 문전까지 왔을 때다. 닭 우는 소리가 들닌다. 그 때 五夢女는 총角의 배로 생선훔치러 드러간 때다. 그 빈배는 움즉인나 五夢女가 놀나서 뛰여나리랴고 보니 金돌이가 배를 젓는다. 배는 벌서 멀니 나왔다. 인제야 발악을 한들 무슨 소용이 있으랴. 총각 처녀 단 둘만 실은 이 배는 무인도를 향하고 떠간다.(끗)
