**Body Text Translation:**
Haesamwi Naval Port
						By Yŏnhaejuin

While it is difficult to grasp the exact current state of the Soviet Navy based at Haesamwi (海參威) port, judging by referring to what is recorded in the "Daily Telegraph" magazine, it is said to be generally as follows:

Four 36,000-ton dreadnought-class battleships, all with 12 12-inch guns, speed 23 miles [knots]. These are over-age ships (艦令超過艦) that have been refitted with modern equipment; their funnels have been lengthened and bent backwards to avoid smoke from the bridge.

Four new-type cruisers. 6,700 to 7,900 tons, 15 5-inch guns, 8 high-angle guns, 12 torpedo tubes, speed 30 knots.

Three old-type cruisers. All refitting completed. Performance roughly the same as before, loaded with one catapult and one aircraft (發行機 一 臺).

27 destroyers. 1,300 tons, 4 4-inch guns, 9 torpedo tubes, speed 28 to 30 knots. Besides these, there are 70 torpedo boats.

Excluding submarines, the general situation is roughly as described above. These numbers alone are not particularly frightening, but the Soviet Navy has advanced inch by inch with the premise of advancing foot by foot. If the current strength as mentioned above were dispersed among the four locations of the Baltic (빨직쿠), Black Sea (黑海), Caspian Sea (裏海), and the Far East (極東), it would be laughable, but this would be a short-sighted observation of the present numbers.

Until 2 or 3 years ago, it was not even known that submarines<189> were stationed at P'oyŏmsŭdŭk (浦鹽斯德 - Vladivostok). However, in just 2 or 3 years, about 50 state-of-the-art submarines (新銳潛水艦) have been gathered in the P'oyŏm (浦鹽 - Vladivostok) naval port. These were assembled in P'oyŏm from materials transported via the Siberian Railway (西伯利亞鐵道). Recently, it is said that world-class shipyards and ordnance factories have been established, starting with P'oyŏm.

Since the appointment of Viktorov (비구도로후) as commander of the Red Pacific Fleet (赤色太平洋艦隊), he has exclusively commanded the submarine fleet, guarding [the region]. The total number of submarines in the Soviet Union reaches 75, of which 60 were built in 1930, with a displacement of 900 to 1,000 tons, one 4-inch gun, and 8 torpedo tubes. The 50 currently patrolling the coast centered around Haeyŏm (海鹽 - Vladivostok) all boast being state-of-the-art (新銳), and it is said that these alone can rival Japan's (日本) large fleet.

The three main principles of Soviet naval construction are:
1.  To be carried out within the scope permitted by the national budget, ensuring no adverse impact on the overall budget.
2.  In selecting ship types, they must be defensive (防禦的) in nature.
3.  In shipbuilding, exclusively use Soviet industrial technology and labor.
An important policy is implied here. That is, item 2, "defensive ship type," clearly indicates making submarines the core of the navy.

The Bitter Cup of the Russo-Japanese War Era

Even during the Russo-Japanese War (日露戰役) in Meiji (明治) 37-38 (1904-1905), the Haeyŏm fleet, a detachment of the Russian (로시아) Far East Fleet based around the three ships Gromoboi (구로모포리), Rurik (류릿쿠) [Translator note: The text names 3 ships but lists only two; the third was likely the Rossia], was active everywhere, threatening Japan's army logistics transport (兵站輸送) and playing a crucial role in dividing the strength of the Japanese fleet. Consequently, the Tōgō Fleet (東鄕艦隊), wary of this, had to allocate (割受) ships from its command.

Haeyŏm's military facilities and defenses are being constructed according to top-tier Soviet [policy]*, executed without even regard for cost-effectiveness. More advanced than during the Siberian Intervention (西伯利亞 出兵當時), they utilize natural mountains for solid defensive installations, with barracks and ammunition depots underground. It's said that mountain slopes have been cut into, creating facilities from which a large number of aircraft can sortie. The base is located in America Bay (아메리가灣), deep inside Ussuri Bay (우스리灣). The fortress locations are mainly in the southern Ussuri region (우스리 方面), Bonett'ŭ (본엣트 - Posyet?) and Sŭrabwaan K'a (스라봐안카 - Slavyanka?) areas. Besides these, the installation of gun batteries, rear batteries (背面砲臺), etc., is said to be complete. Thus, huge 35cm fortress guns are reportedly lined up, menacingly facing the Sea of Japan.

Meanwhile, the total number of naval personnel is approximately 3 million [Translator note: This figure seems extraordinarily high for 1937 naval personnel and may be a typo or refer to total mobilized potential], of which 2,500 are officers or higher. Also, at the time of the Manchurian Incident (滿洲事變) in September, Shōwa 5 (1931), the Soviet Far East Army consisted of only 40,000-50,000 regular troops, but in the subsequent few years, 310,000 Red Army (赤軍) soldiers are said to have been deployed in the Far East.<190>

**Summary**

This 1937 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* reports on the perceived growing strength of the Soviet naval base at Vladivostok (Haesamwi). Citing the "Daily Telegraph," it details the surface fleet but emphasizes the rapid build-up of a modern submarine force (around 50 vessels), assembled locally from parts shipped via the Trans-Siberian Railway. The author notes the Soviet focus on submarines reflects a defensive naval strategy. The article contrasts this buildup with the earlier Russo-Japanese War era and describes extensive fortification efforts around Vladivostok, including underground facilities and large coastal guns facing Japan. It concludes by mentioning a significant increase in Soviet Far East ground forces since the Manchurian Incident. For historians, this piece offers insight into contemporary Korean perceptions (under Japanese rule) of the Soviet military threat in the Far East, particularly the focus on Vladivostok's naval and defensive capabilities, and reflects the rising geopolitical tensions in Northeast Asia leading up to WWII. The sourcing from a British publication and the specific details on Soviet naval strategy and fortifications are notable.

海參威 軍港
						沿海州人
海參威港을 基點으로 한 蘇聯의 海軍 現勢는 正確히 把握하기는 어려우나 이제 「데-리-. 데러구구라후誌」에 記載된 것을 參照하야 判定한다면 大體 左와 如하다고 한다.
三萬六千 噸級 弩*戰艦 4隻, 모다 12吋砲 12門, 速力 23哩. 艦令超過艦을 改裝하야 近代裝備를 한 것으로 艦橋의 煤煙을 避하고저 煙筒을 길게하야 後方으로 꾸부렷다.
新型 巡洋艦 4隻. 6千 7백噸 乃至 7천 9백噸으로 5吋砲 15門, 高角砲 8門, 發射管 12門, 速力30節.
舊型 巡洋艦 3隻. 全部 改製完了. 性能土略同前, 카타팔트及 發行機 一 臺를 積載.
驅逐艦 27 隻. 1천 3백 噸, 4吋砲 4門, 發射管 9門, 速力 28 乃至 30節. 外에 水雷艇 70隻이 있다.
潛水艦을 除한 以外는 大體 以上과 如한 것으로 이 數字만으로는 그다지 두려울 程度는 아니나 그러나 蘇聯 海軍은, 漸次 尺進하랴는 前提로 寸進한 것이다. 前記와 如한 現勢를 빨직쿠, 黑海, 裏海, 極東 4處에 分散되면 그것은 可笑할 것이지만 이것은 目前의 數字에 對하야 近視眼的 의 觀察일 것이다.
2,3年 前까지 浦鹽斯德에 潛水艦이<189> 떠 있었는 것까지 몰낫든 것이다. 그런데 不過 2,3年間에 約 50隻의 新銳潛水艦이 浦鹽軍港內에 集聚하게 되였다. 이것은 西伯利亞鐵道로 運搬하여 온 材料를 浦鹽에서 組成하야 놓은 것이다. 最近 浦鹽을 비롯하야 世界에서도 有數한 造船廠, 兵器廠이 있다한다.
赤色太平洋艦隊 司令官 비구도로후 就任 以來 專혀 潛水艦隊를 統率하야 守護하여 온 것이다. 潛水艦의 數는 蘇聯 全部 75隻에 達하고 其中 60隻은 1930年의 建造로 排水量 九百 乃至 1천噸, 4吋砲 1門, 發射管 8門을 가지고 現在 海鹽을 中心으로 한 沿岸에 倣廻하고 있는 50隻은 總히 新銳를 자랑하는 것으로 이것만으로도 日本의 大艦隊와 拮抗할 수 있다고 하는 것이다.
蘇聯海軍의 建艦 3原則은
1. 國家豫算의 許하는 範圍內에서 行할 것으로, 豫算全般에 惡影響을 미치는 일이 없을 것
2. 艦型撰擇에 當하야는 어듸까지나 防禦的일 것
3. 造艦에 當하야는 專혀 蘇聯工業技術 及 勞働力을 使用할 것
이라는 것인데 여기에 重要한 方針이 含蓄되여 있는 것이다. 卽 第 2項의 「防禦的 艦型」이라는 것은 潛水艦으로써 海軍의 中軸을 삼는 것을 明示한 것이다.

日露戰爭 當時의 苦杯

明治 37,8年 日露戰役 當時에도 露國 東洋艦隊의 支隊인 로시아 구로모포리, 류릿쿠 3艦을 基幹으로 하는 海鹽艦隊가 縱橫으로 活躍하야 日本의 陸兵 兵站輸送을 威脅하야서 日本 艦隊勢力 兩分의 役割에 奏切하였든 것이다. 그래서 東鄕艦隊가 此로 因하야 그 것을 警戒코저 所屬艦隊를 割受하였든 것이다.
海鹽의 軍事施設 乃至 防備는 그 採算까지도 無視하고 執한 蘇聯 一流의 *에 依하야 施設되는 것이다. 西伯利亞 出兵當時 以上으로 進步되여 天然의 山을 利用하야 堅고한 防禦裝置를 한 우에, 兵營, 彈藥庫가 地下에 있고 山腹을 끊어 그 속에서 大量의 飛行機가 出發할 裝置가 되여 있다 한다. 그 根據地는 우스리灣속에 쑥 드러간 아메리가灣에 있고 要塞 所在는 南部 우스리 方面 본엣트 及 스라봐안카方面이 主라하며 이 以外에도, 砲臺, 背面砲臺 等의 設備가 完結되였다고 한다. 그래서 35糎의 巨大한 要塞砲가 줄느런히 日本海 方面을 바라보고 하품을 하고 있다 한다.
그리고 一方 그 海軍의 總 數는 約 3百萬 人으로 그 中 2千5百 名은 士官 以上이라한다. 또 昭和 5年 9月 滿洲事變 當時는 蘇聯 極東軍은 正規軍 4,5萬 名에 不出하든 것이 爾後 數年來에 31萬의 赤軍이 極東에 整備되여 있다 한다.<190>
