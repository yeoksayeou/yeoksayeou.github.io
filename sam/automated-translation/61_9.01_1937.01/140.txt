Magazine Title: 삼천리 제9권 제1호
Publication Date: 1937년 01월 01일
Article Title: 長安 紳士淑女 스타일 漫評 (Satirical Critique of the Styles of Seoul's Gentlemen and Ladies)
Author: 卜惠淑 (Pok Hye-suk), 覆面客 (Pokmyŏn'gaek - Masked Guest)
Article Type: 세태비평 (Social Commentary)

長安 紳士淑女 스타일 漫評
						卜惠淑, 覆面客
Critique of the *Tonga Ilbo* Bunch

Masked Guest (Hereafter M.G.) - Let's thoroughly scrutinize, dig into, sweep up, trim down, and critique every single one of those self-important "first-class gentlemen" who strut around as if the streets of the capital (長安) are too narrow for them, and those so-called "ladies" who mince along with their skirts fluttering in the east wind (東風).
Pok Hye-suk (Hereafter P.H.S.) - Well, even if it's unpleasant, getting some complaints off my chest might lift my spirits in these gloomy year-end times. Where should we start? Like a drunkard staggering along, nodding this way and that?
M.G. - Let's start with the *Tonga Ilbo* crowd, who've become the talk of the town amid rumors of whether the newspaper will live or die.
P.H.S. - If Song Chin-u were just a little taller, he'd look quite decent in a Western suit, but in his Savile Row suit (세비로), he looks round and broad, not chic (씨-크). Traditional Korean clothes (조선 웃) would suit him better, in the style of a Chŏlla Province gentleman.
M.G. - His accent isn't strong either. For example, when calling Kwanghwamun 550 (光化門 550番), instead of saying "Kkokamong," he says "Kogamung." Also, when answering the phone saying, "私し 宋鎭禹ですが" (Watashi Sō Chin'u desu ga - This is Song Chin-u), he says "Wadasi Sojing'udesŭga" <104> and from the first sound to the final "desŭga," it's all voiced consonants (濁音), without any pitch variation, just flowing straight through. What about Kim Chun-yŏn (金俊淵), who studied in Germany?
P.H.S. - Someone told me he sometimes dances (딴스) well, but my first impression was just that of a scholar (學者). If he went to Germany where there are blonde female students, he should have had a romance before coming back. Then he might dress more smartly in Western clothes, but his physique is Eastern style, so he doesn't really give off the vibe of someone who's been abroad (양행꾼).
M.G. - Maybe he learned to dance (딴스) from his occasional trips east? The new managing director, Yang Wŏn-mo (梁源模), also dances quite well, I hear.
P.H.S. - He's neat and cares about his appearance. The former managing editor, Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薛義植), dresses plainly but has a bit of a nasty temper—we once had a huge fight, calling each other names. When was that? It was when Sŏ Pŏm-sŏk (徐範錫), who's now in Manchuria, was in that newspaper's editorial department. One day, during the night shift, I called and asked someone to get a person who had gone into the factory to set type. Some man answered the phone, incredibly unfriendly and stiff. He seemed very busy and annoyed that I called. After some back and forth, he started spewing curses like "You bitch!" (이 년- 저 년), so I got angry too and retorted with "You bastard!" (이 놈- 저 놈). During the fight, I asked, "I'm Pok Hye-suk. Who on earth are you?" and he replied, "I'm Sŏl Ŭi-sik." At that moment, I thought, "Oops (앗불사), I've messed with the wrong person," but I held my ground. We still meet occasionally, and when we do, we curse at each other and then part laughing. When you get to know him, he's actually quite a gentle (상양한) gentleman, but his stubbornness, rigidity, and nonchalance (텀텀이) are flaws.
M.G. - What about Pak Ch'an-hŭi (朴讚熙)?
P.H.S. - Physique-wise, he probably belongs with Yi Yŏ-sŏng (李如星), who has a large nose and a Continental (大陸風) look, among the handsome men (호남아). But Pak Ch'an-hŭi sometimes applies black dye to his white hair, and once applied, it lasts three or four months. However, when it starts to wear off, it becomes hazy (부-여코) and whitish (희슥희슥하여), quite a sight. It ruins (台なし) his image as a handsome man (色男).
M.G. - What about Hyŏn Chin-gŏn (玄鎭健)?
P.H.S. - His complexion is fair, his eyes are clear, and if his body <105> hadn't become so plump and spread out, he might resemble someone with the refined air of an immortal (仙風道骨), but alas, that's his flaw. Still, among the *Tonga Ilbo* crowd, he's probably head and shoulders above the rest in terms of style. His only fault is sometimes becoming a heavy drinker (모주).
M.G. - What about the styles of the female reporters like Hwang Sin-dŏk (黃信德), Ch'oe Ŭi-sun (崔義順), and Kim Cha-hye (金慈惠)?
P.H.S. - A man like you would know women's styles better. How would I know, being of the same feather? But beauty is in the eye of the beholder. Hwang Sin-dŏk shouldn't wear Western clothes (洋裝). Kim Cha-hye, with her slender body having streamlined (流線型) potential, should definitely wear Western clothes. Ch'oe Ŭi-sun has a naturally beautiful foundation, walks gracefully, and has a lovely appearance from behind (뒷맵시), so she looks good in Western clothes, and also suits the schoolgirl look in a black skirt and white jeogori. She'd also suit being a classic lady with her hair in a bun, a long skirt trailing behind her heels, wearing yellow shoes (노랑갓신). She's probably number one (남버 원) among all female reporters throughout history!

*Chosun Ilbo* Bunch

M.G. - What about Pang Ŭng-mo (方應謨) of the *Chosun Ilbo*?
P.H.S. - He looks plain, his face is manly and swarthy, and he's quite tall and slender (후리후리) – but one flaw is, I once saw him officiating a wedding, and his voice was so faint that even people nearby couldn't hear him. I wonder if it's the same when he addresses his employees... It's frustrating.
M.G. - What about the chief editor, Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿)?
P.H.S. - He looks Chinese. He dresses carelessly, has a dark face, and wears dark-rimmed Lloyd glasses (로이도 안경) on his dark face – he doesn't look like a scholar (선비) who lives by the pen (붓대). More like a fighter (鬪士), or someone with a masculine nature (陽性)!
M.G. - Who among the *Chosun Ilbo* executives has 100% perfect style then?
P.H.S. - Probably Ham Sang-hun (咸尙勳). He has good facial features, a good physique, and any Western suit looks good on him; he's probably the best. The managing editor, Kim Hyŏng-wŏn (金烱元), seemed like a very clear-cut (선명) person! His speech isn't vague (흐리터분한), his accent is clear, and he seems to handle everything decisively, though perhaps a bit cold.
I haven't seen him many times, but the society section chief, Yi Sang-ho (李相昊), seems like a person who always looks worried and prefers silence (沈黙)! Their faces generally look good, blessed by the spirit of the Yeongnam mountains and rivers (嶺南山川).
M.G. - Don't you know the new business manager, Kim Kwang-su (金光洙), who came from Osaka (大阪)?
P.H.S. - I haven't met him yet.
M.G. - What about the female reporters?
P.H.S. - Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) is showing signs of age (나티) now, but just a few years ago, she was a rarely seen beauty – there's a funny story. Once, Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi came looking for me, and my younger sibling Chŏng-ja saw her. <106> Chŏng-ja had heard that Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi was old, looked rather aged (늑수구레하고), and plain-faced, thinking she must look like an insurance canvasser (외교원). But she was surprised by her beauty, her style, her gentle voice, and her cheerful personality (明朗性).
M.G. - Haven't you met Yi Ŭn-sang (李殷相) or Ham Tae-hun (咸大勳)?
P.H.S. - No. Those overseas literature types (海外文學派) generally don't set foot in my house.
M.G. - What about An Sŏk-yŏng (安夕影), though he's left now?
P.H.S. - Like the late Sim Hun (沈薰), they were both quite talkative, loved to have fun, dressed neatly (암전하고), drank well; anyway, they were modern (모-던) young gentlemen.
M.G. - How about Hong Yang-myŏng (洪陽明)?
P.H.S. - Absolutely Chinese (촤이니스)! His gait, the way he always walks slightly hunched over, and his incessant talk, mixing sharp and rotten words – he's like the Chinese (촤이니쓰) you often see in the back alleys of Shanghai (上海).

*Joongang Ilbo* Crew

M.G. - Yŏ Un-hyŏng (呂運亨)?
P.H.S. - Imposing and dignified, like a true leader. If he were presented as a general at a military review, he wouldn't fall short of Yi Yong-ik (李容翊), who was famous for his imposing presence in the past. Even without his eloquence or the honor of his past, his beard, presence (풍채), and style alone could make him famous. Having lived in Shanghai (上海) and Nanjing (南京) for many years, his way of wearing Western clothes is impeccable and well-established. His appearance wearing a morning coat (모-닝) at Sim Hun's funeral was truly splendid.
M.G. - I haven't met him, but what about Sŏng Nak-hŏn (成樂憲), the former managing director of Tongil Bank (東一銀行), who supposedly put up 100,000 won?
P.H.S. - Good. He looks weighty and has a fine presence (풍채). He doesn't flaunt his wealth, <107> and though rich people often dress ostentatiously as if dripping with oil, he doesn't... Both Yŏ Un-hyŏng and Sŏng Nak-hŏn have imposing presences. Probably the best among the three newspaper companies.
M.G. - What about Kim Tong-sŏng (金東成), who is again rumored to be a candidate for president?
P.H.S. - Hopelessly Chinese (촤이니스). He's in the same category (사동갑이야 - figuratively) as Sŏ Ch'un, Hong Yang-myŏng, and also Yang Paek-hwa (梁白華) of the *Maeil Sinmun*. Especially when you see him walking briskly, slightly hunched, wearing an overcoat with a yellowish (누-런) otter fur (수달피) scarf – his appearance from behind and front is entirely Chinese. He looks like the kind of person you often see in San Francisco's (桑港) Chinatown (中國街).
M.G. - What about Ch'oe Sŏn-ik (崔善益) and Yun Hŭi-jung (尹希重)?
P.H.S. - Ch'oe Sŏn-ik is a watertight, tightly-wound person. His whole body is neither thin nor thick, neither tall nor short – a slender style. He always dresses neatly whenever I see him, and so does Yun Hŭi-jung. Whenever I see him, his trousers are always ironed (아이통질하여) with a crease, his necktie is new, and his collar is pure white (純白).
M.G. - His clean collars and shirts must be thanks to having a good wife.
P.H.S. - No matter how much a wife tries to dress up her man, if the man himself is negligent (게티부러하면), it won't work.
M.G. - The former female reporters of this newspaper?
P.H.S. - I saw Kim Mal-bong (金末峰) the other day, and her body has become quite bulky (부대하여저서); she's become a middle-aged woman. From photos, Pak Hwa-sŏng (朴花城) also seems quite old (나 먹고) and stout (부대한).
M.G. - Other people at the *Joongang Ilbo*?
P.H.S. - I mostly recognize their faces but don't know their names, so let's leave it at that.

*Maeil Sinbo* Bunch

M.G. - Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協)?
P.H.S. - The more he tries to dress up, the more stylish he looks (모양 날이지). He seems to be getting bulkier (부대해지드구만) lately. Until a few years ago, he was on the slender side, and the way he walked quietly with his eyes lowered had a certain air (태).
M.G. - Yu Kwang-nyŏl (柳光烈) or Yŏm Sang-sŏp (廉尙燮)?
P.H.S. - Yu Kwang-nyŏl is just easy-going (쑬쑬하지). Yŏm Sang-sŏp, typical of a drinker, always has a flushed face. His body certainly has a healthy beauty (건강미).
M.G. - Chŏng In-ik (鄭寅翼) is a handsome man (호남아).
P.H.S. - His style is fresh (淸新), as if sprung from the North (뿍 - likely typo for 북). He might be the archetype (典型) of the capital's handsome men.
M.G. - Kim Ki-jin (金基鎭)?
P.H.S. - Although tall (키 크다리지만), he's not insipid (싱겁지), gentle (상양하고), and his style barely gets a passing mark (급제점).

Hwashin Crew

M.G. - Alright, now let's move (올마서) to the business world (상계). How is Pak Hŭng-sik (朴興植) of Hwashin?
P.H.S. - His looks and physique are all good, but his gait (거름거리) is childish (어리광치듯) <108> and lacks dignity, which is a shame. Especially his appearance from behind (뒷맵시), he seems to stagger slightly (빗슬빗슬하지) at a 30-degree angle, as if he's had a couple of glasses of whiskey (우이스키).
M.G. - Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰)?
P.H.S. - Someone called him a "kangaroo" (강가-루), and indeed, his head is small and he's tall, so his proportions aren't quite right (격에는 안 드러맛지). However, having spent a long time in Shanghai (上海), his way of wearing Western clothes suits him well. The way he strides quickly with his hands behind his back clearly resembles a zoo kangaroo.
M.G. - Managing Director Yi Ki-yŏn (李基衍) and other figures?
P.H.S. - Unfortunately, I haven't met them yet, so I'll have to skip them.

Tongyang Theater Crew and Actors

M.G. - Theater owner Hong Sun-ŏn (洪淳彦)?
P.H.S. - As the hidden husband (비장 남편) of the famous actress Pae Ku-ja (裵龜子), his style is modest and good. More so, the manager Ch'oe Tok-kyŏn (崔獨견), who almost became a handsome man, looks stylish like a newlywed groom (새 신랑가치) when he walks past Gwanghwamun Street (광화문*) swinging his walking stick (스틱) on a summer day.
M.G. - Please critique the styles of the male and female actors in the Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa (靑春座) or Hŭigŭk-jwa (喜劇座) there.
P.H.S. - That's difficult. Since they are all actors, they take meticulous care (거두지) of their own bodies. They cultivate their appearance and keep their faces flawless (티 하나 끼지 않게). Moreover, Chi Kyŏng-sun (池京順), Cha Hong-nyŏ (車紅女), and Kim Sŏn-ch'o (金仙草) are all beauties, and having received physical education in girls' schools, they are modern girls (모던껄) with beautiful curves (曲線美) and legs (脚線美). <109> Even if placed in Paris (巴里) or London (倫敦), they would be first-class.
M.G. - Among the men, Kang Hong-sik (姜弘植) must be good.
P.H.S. - When he appears on stage singing about going to gather herbs (나물 캐러 간다고) for "Virgin Bachelor," his six-foot (六尺) height and 20-kwan (貫 - approx. 75kg) build don't quite fit the part, but he is an imposing and handsome man (호남아). Even if sent abroad, he wouldn't lag behind their first-class gentlemen one bit.
M.G. - While we're at it, critique some actresses. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰) or No Chae-sin (盧載信) or Sin Il-sŏn (申一仙).
P.H.S. - Judging from her appearance on screen, when Mun Ye-bong twists her body, her shy demeanor (羞態) is quite capable of provoking (노살시키게) men, just like in a Heine poem... No Chae-sin also has clear facial lines and large eyes; her pose (포-즈) gets eighty points.

Male and Female Singers

M.G. - Wang Su-bok (王壽福), Sŏnu Il-sŏn (鮮于一扇)?
P.H.S. - Wang Su-bok's neck is a bit short (짤르고), and her body looks stocky (밧틈밧틈하게), as if cut top and bottom (아래위를 잘너낸 것가치). In contrast, Sŏnu Il-sŏn is slim and slender (날신하고 가늘신한 것이), possessing a fresh charm like a Gangnam swallow (江南 제비) gliding through the water amidst weeping willows (수양버들) by the riverside (강가). Though her record popularity seems to be declining.
M.G. - What about Victor's Kim Pok-hŭi (金福姬)?
P.H.S. - Although her face isn't that beautiful, her tall and slender (후리후리한) height and the pose (포-즈) of her well-balanced (均齊) body (肉體) are good.
M.G. - Which actress has a well-proportioned (均正된) style comparable to Irie Takako (入江たか子)?
P.H.S. - Probably none. Just when a decent star (스타-) emerges, some fellow (년석들이) whisks her away because of a love affair, and she disappears from the silver screen (銀幕) – it's frustrating that there's no way to even guess (취측하여)!

Bank and Company Executives

M.G. - Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl (朴榮喆) of the Commercial Bank (商業銀行)?
P.H.S. - His style lacks much curve (屈曲), round (두리둥둥) like a main beam (대들보), balding (버서지고) forehead, grave (묵신하고), possessing what you might call giant beauty (巨人美). A thousand li apart (千里相距) from charming (아기자기한) beauty.
M.G. - Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙) of Kyŏngsŏng Textiles (京城紡績)?
P.H.S. - The exact opposite (正反對) of Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl, he's a smallish (조고만치), solidly built person. His presence (풍채) falls far short (떨어지지) of his name.
M.G. - Min Tae-sik (閔大植), Min Kyu-sik (閔奎植)?
P.H.S. - I've never met them, so I don't know at all.
M.G. - Then who among the prominent figures (名士) in the business world do you know?
P.H.S. - Han Sang-nyong (韓相龍) of Chosŏn Trust (朝鮮信託) has a lucky face (福相), like a turtle's (거북이).
M.G. - This is a style critique (評), why bring up lucky faces? Don't force it if you don't know. Try critiquing school principals or other famous gentlemen and ladies (名士淑女) as you please.
P.H.S. - Criticizing school teachers would immediately impact hundreds of students, so out of Buddhist compassion and good heart, I cannot bear to do it. Shall we talk about the various gentlemen (僉君子) who fit nowhere? <110> Judging from his novels, Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) should be a slender (간얄핀) handsome man (好男兒) like Mei Lanfang (梅蘭芳), but the actual person (實物) has a large frame (骨格), is imposing (壯大), and blunt (무뚝하고). Yun Sŏng-sang (尹聖相), when wearing Western clothes, had a good style like Clark Gable (구로― 벨 - likely Gable), with his seemingly thin (간듯한) waist and long leg lines (脚線), but always wearing Korean clothes lacks prudence (知身의 明 - perhaps 知人之明, knowing others?). Yu Kak-kyŏng (兪珏卿) is short, and carrying a large handbag dragging on the ground goes against the way of a lady (淑女之道). Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng (丁七星) has sparkling eyes (영채), speaks beautifully, and is tall and slender (후리후리), an Eastern classic beauty, but her old-fashioned gait is not good. Na Un-kyu (羅雲奎), viewed from front, back, left, or right, has a substantial (알이 훔신 든), weighty physique. Han Il-song (韓一松), who played Yi To-ryŏng in the movie "Ch'unhyangjŏn" (春香傳), has a good face and presence (風采); probably few can match his style.
M.G. - If you're just giving curt (외마디) critiques, then stop. Looks like you've run out of material (미천). Finally, shall I chime in with a few words? Yu Chin-t'ae (兪鎭泰) of the Education Association (敎育協會) looks like an old monk holding an iron sheet (철 한장 - stubborn?) watching the Four Elders of Mount Shang (商山 사호) play Go. Yun Ch'i-ho (尹致昊), old or young, transcends age and always has an air like the "Song of Sunny Spring" (楊昌曲 - likely 陽春曲). Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨), no matter how dignified he tries to be, is hopelessly a contemporary playboy (호활냥이). Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭) is leisurely (悠長하기) like the scenery of Gangnam (江南風月), resembling Jiang Ziya fishing by the Wei River (渭水). Kim Hwal-lan (金活蘭) always looks like an innocent (無雅氣) 17 or 18-year-old girl, but the energetic 'ddal, ddal, ddal' sound (딸, 딸, 딸, 구으는 것) when she walks shows excessive energy (元氣超過). Han Yong-un (韓龍雲) resembles an old monk crossing Dabal Pass (단발嶺) at sunset. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟) moves slowly as if mountains are rolling when he walks. Pak In-dŏk (朴仁德) is refreshing (선선하기) in speech and action, like a Gwacheon melon (果川참배 - likely 참외). Hyŏn Sang-yun (玄相允) looks like a Taoist ascetic (道士) who has faced a wall for nine years (面壁九年) and is about to attain Buddhahood (成佛). Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Cho Man-sik (조晩植), Sin T'ae-ok (辛泰獄), Kim Pyŏng-no (金炳魯)...唉 (에그)! There are still clouds of figures to count, but hold on... Phew (에있), I'm sweating. If I keep talking carelessly (주서대다가) like this, won't I get cursed back?
P.H.S. - Haha, you don't know the term 'satirical critique' (漫評). It's like a wandering marketplace singer (장타령패) standing at the gate, drumming out a rhythm (품파품파), and chattering all sorts of nonsense (된 소리 않된 소리). Because it's a satirical critique, even those who got cursed will laugh it off, and even if my words displease someone, they'll just think, "Tsk, what a naughty girl (惡少女)," and forget it over a New Year's drink (歲酒) at dawn. Knowing this, I've freely indulged in gossiping about the flaws of Seoul's gentlemen and ladies. Well then! Let's celebrate the New Year (過歲), and next time, let's try a truly substantial (알이든) critique! (End) December 23rd <111>

**Summary**

This article, presented as a dialogue between actress Pok Hye-suk and an anonymous "Masked Guest," offers a gossipy and highly subjective critique of the physical appearance, fashion sense, and mannerisms of prominent figures in 1937 Seoul (termed 'Changan'). Covering individuals from major newspapers (*Tonga Ilbo*, *Chosun Ilbo*, *Joongang Ilbo*, *Maeil Sinbo*), business (Hwashin, banking, textiles), the arts (theater, film, music), and other public life spheres, the critique is candid, often harsh, and reveals contemporary standards of style, blending traditional Korean aesthetics with admiration for Western fashion and 'modern' attributes, while also frequently resorting to stereotypes (e.g., 'Chinese' appearance). For historians, the article provides a unique, informal snapshot of the colonial elite's social world, perceived hierarchies of style, prevailing attitudes towards public figures (including judgments based on regional origin, education abroad, physique, and perceived personality traits), and the cultural milieu of the time. It highlights the personalities associated with key colonial institutions and offers insights into contemporary notions of masculinity, femininity, and modernity within Korean society under Japanese rule.

東亞日報 축들

覆面客- 제 잘난 체, 장안네거리 좁은 듯 네 활개치고 다니는 일류신사나, 치마자락에 동*풍 나풍기며 아장아장 지나 다니는 내로라하는 숙녀아씨들을 모조리, 깡그리 알뜰이 살트리 뒤지고 우비고 쓸코 다듬고 어디 있는대로 그 비평이나 하여 볼가.
卜惠淑- 글세 싫건 욕이나 한바탕 하고 났으면 세대목 울적한 판에 긔분이 훠 풀릴 것 같네 그려, 어디, 말탄 양반 꺼덕꺼떡, 소탄 양반 꺼떡것떡 주정방이 거름으로 주서 섬겨볼가.
覆- 신문이 죽느냐 사느냐 하고 소문거리 자진 동아일보축부터 건드려 보지.
卜-송진우는 키가 조금만 더 컷더면 양복 입어도 그럴 듯 어울리겠는데 세비로 입으면 둥굴넙적하여 씨-크 하지 못해, 차라리 조선 웃이 어울날 걸, 전라도 양반 식으로.
覆- 말씨도 악센트가 *세지 못하지. 가령 光化門 550番이라고 부를 때 「고까몽」할 것을 「고가뭉」하지. 또 「私し 宋鎭禹ですが」라고 전화 받갸는 보면 「와다시 소징우데스가」 <104> 하고 첫자부터 마즈막 「데스가」에 이르기까지 줄곳 濁音바처 고저장단없이 그냥 쭉 훌른단 말이야, 독일 유학하고 온 김준연(金俊淵)은 어때?
卜- 누구 전하는 말에 각금 딴스 잘 한다는데 첫 인상이 그저 학자(學者)더구나. 금발 녀학생 있는 독일로 갔거든 언애나 하고 도라오지. 그러면 좀더 산듯하게 양복 입으련만 그 분은 체격이 동양식이 되어 「양행꾼」이란 티가 별로 없어.
覆- 「딴스」는 동*에 각금 다녀오더니 그런지 새전무 양원모(梁源模)도 꽤 추거든.
卜- 깨끗하고 모양 보는 분이지. 예전 편집국장 설의식(薛義植)은 사람이 옷채림은 수수한데 심술이 좀 있거든--나하고 언젠가 이놈저놈하고 대판 싸움을 했지. 그게 언제든가 지금 만주 가 있는 서범석(徐範錫)이 그 신문 편집국에 있을 때인데 어느 날 야근때 공장에 판 짜러 들어갔다는 것을 좀 불러 달라고 전화했더니 웬 사내가 전화를 받는데 불친절하고 딱딱하기 한량없단 말이야. 저는 몹시 바뿐 모양인데 전화거니 성가시어 골을 잔뜩 낸 모양이야, 그래서 설왕설래 끝에 저쪽에서는 이 년- 저 년 하고 개진창으로 욕을 마구 퍼붓기에 나도 화나서 이 놈- 저 놈하고 한바탕 드리대었지. 싸우다가 「나는 복헤숙이다. 너는 데체 누구냐?」하고 물었더니 나는 설의식이다 하겠지. 그 때 앗불사 된 손에 걸렸구나 했지만 그냥 버티였지. 그 뒤에도 각금 만나는데 만나면 욕 하다가 웃고 갈라지지. 알고 보면 퍽으나 상양한 신사이나 좀 고집과 틀과 텀텀이 있어 탈이야.
覆- 박찬희(朴讚熙)는 어때.
卜- 체격이야 아마 코이 큼직하고 大陸風으로 생긴 이여성(李如星)이와 같이 호남아 축이겠지. 그러나 박찬히는 힌 머리털에 각금 거머지는 약을 바르고 다니는데 한 번 바르면 서너 달씩은 가거든. 그러나 그것이 차츰 벗기어질 때는 부-여코 희슥희슥하여 장관*지. 色男 台なし야.
覆- 현진건(玄鎭健)이는?
卜- 살결 히고 눈빛이 맑고 몸만 좀 <105> 뚱뚱하게 가르 퍼지지 말었으면 선풍도골(仙風道骨) 가음인데 아차 그만 탈이야. 그래도 아마 누구누구해도 동아일보 축에선 스타일 좋기로 일당백일걸. 술이 각금 모주 되어 탈이지만.
覆- 황신덕(黃信德)이고 최의순(崔義順)이고 김자혜(金慈惠)고 하는 부인기자들은 스타일이 어떤고?
卜- 여자 스타일이야 자네 같은 사내가 보아야 잘 알지. 초록동색이라고 내가 알 수 있나? 그러나 제 눈에 안경이라고 황신덕이는 양장(洋裝)하지 말 일 김자헤는 차라리 날신한 몸에 유선형(流線型) 소질이 있은 즉 아모쪼록 양장할 일 최의순이는 원체 바탕이 미인인데다가 거름거리 곱고 뒷맵시 고아서 양장도 어울리고 검정치마 힌저고리 바처 입으면 여학생풍으로도 어울리고 머리 쪽찌고 긴치마 발뒤꿈치에 질질 흘니며 노랑갓신 바처 신은 고전적(古典的) 아씨 되어도 어울리고, 아마 역대 부인기자 중 남버 원이야!
