Expansion of Turmoil Throughout China, Safety of Resident Compatriots!!
Ambassador Kawagoe Warns Against Reddening

Conveying Japan's Grave Concern
(Shanghai, 28th, Dōmei) Ambassador Kawagoe, based on instructions from Foreign Minister Arita, is heading to Nanjing to visit Minister Zhang Qun at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Representing the Imperial Government, he is expected to express deep condolences for the misfortune of the neighboring country. At the same time, he will reiterate the true meaning of our side's advocacy for anti-communism, expressing the earnest hope that "the Nationalist Government, in its preoccupation with overcoming the immediate crisis, does not misjudge the hundred-year plan for East Asian peace. If the Nationalist Government were to accept Zhang Xueliang's demands, implement tolerance for communism, and bring about the undesired result of China's reddening, the Imperial Government, which holds a grave concern for the stability of the East Asian political situation, cannot remain silent." This sentiment is reportedly expected to be conveyed.<44>

Divisions Among Universities in Beiping

Split Between Anti-Zhang Faction and Pro-Central Government/Chiang Faction
Universities in Beiping are holding daily student assemblies to discuss and deliberate their stance on the current situation. Universities such as Beiping University, along with Qinghua and Yenching Universities, advocate for the resolute subjugation of Zhang Xueliang. However, other universities argue against subjugation. Here, they are divided into two factions: the Anti-Communist Kuomintang-affiliated National Front faction and the Anti-Chiang Communist Army People's Front faction. The outcome is attracting attention.

Urgent Telegram Sent to Mao Zedong

Will He Indeed Join Forces with Zhang Xueliang?
(Beiping, Dōmei) According to reliable information received by a certain key figure of the Shanxi clique here, Zhang Xueliang, simultaneously with initiating the rebellion, sent a telegram to Mao Zedong, leader of the Communist army in northern Shaanxi Province, stating that there were matters to discuss and requesting a visit to Xi'an. In response, Mao Zedong reportedly presented the following conditions:
1. First, clearly demonstrate the Northeastern Army's stance on reddening.
2. If there are matters to discuss, Zhang Xueliang himself should come.

A Word from Colonel House

Warning from an Elder of American Diplomacy
I sincerely rejoice in Japan's development. Japan's future is bright but also fraught with difficulties. What result will the Japan-Germany alliance produce? It will create momentum for Britain, France, and Russia to form an alliance. Following that, it will facilitate Russia's military buildup. In one year's time, the military preparedness of Britain, France, and Russia will be enhanced. I hope Japan navigates the objective circumstances of its gradualist policy well.<45>

Chiang Kai-shek's True Intentions Towards Japan

At the Huaqing Pool in Xi'an, formerly Chang'an where Emperor Xuanzong of Tang enjoyed the glory of a supreme ruler with Yang Guifei, Chiang Kai-shek fell into Zhang Xueliang's trap, and all of China once again faced the danger of great turmoil akin to the Warring States Period. To understand the true nature of this turmoil, we first need some preliminary knowledge. That is, did Chiang Kai-shek intend to fight Japan in resistance, as is commonly rumored? Consequently, did the Nationalist Government faction truly intend to fight Japan? It is necessary to investigate this point first. Why? Because it is said that Chiang Kai-shek being shot (or imprisoned) and Zhang Xueliang rising up was solely due to anti-Japanese resistance and tolerance for communism.
Nakano Seigō, leader of the Kokumin Dōmei in Tokyo, met Mr. Chiang some time ago and asked,
"In the event of a contingency, do you intend to move the government to Chengdu in Sichuan Province and fight Japan by borrowing the power of Britain, America, and Russia?" He prefaced by saying that since he was a military man, matters concerning war are quickly judged.
"I know that Japan is also making considerable preparations against the Soviet side's searchlights and air force on the northern Soviet-Manchurian border. However, I surmise that the Soviet Union, having concerns about its rear on the European border, will definitely not be able to launch an offensive against Japan. Regarding Britain, now that they are withdrawing to Singapore rather than Hong Kong for defense, Britain will not easily head towards the Far East with military force. The Soviet Union, unlike Russia during the old Imperial era, possesses strategies in both military affairs and diplomacy no less sophisticated than Britain's, and will unlikely take actions like<46> those in the Russo-Japanese War. In such circumstances, if Japan and China fight, Japan, swift in seizing the initiative, will occupy key locations in China with its military power. Consequently, China will suffer enormous damage. If this happens, I too will rise and stand at the forefront. Then, the patriotic movement throughout China, as well as bandits, vagrants, and communist bandits, will all mobilize against Japan. To prevent this full-scale anti-Japanese resistance, Japan will be forced to deploy its precious army throughout China. If this happens, the economy and industry of the entire nation [Japan] will fall into chaos, and Japan's economy towards China will also be completely destroyed. Japan will waste much wealth and soldiery. Thus, China will verge on ruin, and Japan will reach exhaustion. Only then will the Powers, which have been eyeing the Far East covetously (虎視耽耽), make their move. If this happens, it is not only the Republic [of China] that will suffer calamity, but Japan will also not escape it. Therefore, considering the interests of Japan and China and the interests of the East, there should not be a war between Japan and China."
This was his statement. In a word, whether winning or losing the war, both countries would be ruined, and British, American, and Soviet influence would spread in the East, so war should be avoided – this is said to be the view of Chiang Kai-shek and the current leadership of the Nationalist Government.

Chiang Kai-shek's Son Weeps Bitterly

Mr. Chiang Wei-kuo Studying in Berlin
Chiang Kai-shek's second son, Chiang Wei-kuo (age 20), came from Paris to the University of Berlin in November to major in economics and is currently lodging with an aristocratic family. Upon receiving the urgent news about his father, he reportedly cannot stop weeping bitterly (Berlin Cable).<47>

Actual Strength of the Chinese Communist Army

The Chinese Communist Army is located in northern Shaanxi Province and parts of Gansu Province, behind Zhang Xueliang's army. Originally, the Communist Party of China arose in Shanghai 16 years ago from now and organized the Chinese Soviet government in Ruijin, Jiangxi Province, at the end of Shōwa 5 (1930). Then, pursued by Chiang's suppression army, they abandoned Ruijin at the end of October, Shōwa 9 (1934), moved along the Guangdong provincial border to the Guangxi provincial border, and from there passed through Yunnan and Guizhou to Sichuan, setting up camp in the aforementioned two locations [Shaanxi and Gansu]. Their military strength is said to be 80,000-90,000, with some saying slightly more, but beyond that is unclear. Besides the army, they possess the strong point of being able to rally various strata of the People's Front forces.

Tens of Thousands of Korean Compatriots Too

So, what about the Korean people? Since the Shanghai Incident, about 2,000 Koreans who had their base in Shanghai have left; 500-600 remain in Shanghai, and most have moved to Nanjing. It is said that about 400 reside in Zhenjiang, a city between Shanghai and Nanjing. Besides that, in places like Wuhan, Nanchang, Guangdong, Hankou, etc., there are 10-20 to 50-60 people living there. Generally, among Koreans residing in the south, there are many figures involved in ideological matters; several individuals, including Kim Kyu-sik, are said to be near Nanjing currently. Also, in Shanghai and Nanjing, there are many Korean students in various universities – 70-80 in each major university – but for various reasons, they live without revealing they are Korean. Furthermore, there are students even in military academies and army air corps, but all are reportedly in a situation where they do not reveal their names.
As for Northern China, the place where the most live is Beijing; there are about 2,000 here, the majority of whom are said to be merchants involved in smuggling and opium. The several hundred in Tianjin, Shanhaiguan, Qingdao, etc., also mostly engage in similar occupations. However, if the Chiang-Zhang war zone expands, or if the Soviet Army or Mao Zedong's Communist forces sweep in from the Outer Mongolia direction, the flames of war will likely reach the entire North China region, causing considerable concern for the safety of resident Koreans.<49>

**Summary**

This 1937 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine reports on the Xi'an Incident, where Zhang Xueliang detained Chiang Kai-shek, sparking fears of wider conflict and potential communist influence in China. It details Japan's diplomatic response, expressing concern about China "reddening" and warning the Nationalist government against aligning with communists, framing this as a threat to East Asian stability. The article discusses divisions among Chinese universities, Zhang's communication with Mao Zedong, and includes an alleged account of Chiang Kai-shek's rationale for avoiding war with Japan, fearing mutual destruction and exploitation by Western powers and the USSR. It compares the military strengths of Zhang's forces and the Central Army, notes the distress of Chiang's son in Berlin, briefly outlines the history and strength of the Chinese Communist Army, and concludes with anxiety over the safety of Japanese and Korean residents throughout China, providing population statistics for Japanese nationals and describing the precarious situation of Koreans, many involved in sensitive activities or hiding their identities, particularly in North China. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is revealing about the sizable Korean diaspora in various parts of China, their diverse (sometimes illicit) occupations, and their vulnerable position amidst Sino-Japanese tensions and Chinese internal conflicts. It highlights the presence of Korean nationalists (like Kim Kyu-sik) and students involved in Chinese military and political affairs, often covertly, reflecting the complex dynamics of resistance and survival under Japanese rule. The Japanese concern over Korean safety is presented alongside statistics for Japanese nationals, implicitly positioning Koreans within the framework of the Japanese empire's interests, while also acknowledging their unique and often hidden circumstances.

蔣介石의 對日眞意

唐明王이 楊貴妃로 더부러 萬乘天子의 榮華를 누리든 옛날 長安 지금의 西安 華淸池 못가에서, 蔣介石이 張學良에 속에 陷하자, 全中國은 다시 戰國時代와 같은 大動亂의 危險이 直面하게 되었다. 그런데 우리는 이 動亂의 眞相을 알기 爲하여 爲先 한개의 豫備知識을 가질 必要있다. 그것은 蔣介石이 世人의 所傳과 같이 抗日一戰하려 드렸드냐? 따라서 國民政府一파가 果然 對日 一戰하려드렸드냐 라는 點부터 究明할 必要가 있다. 었재서 그러냐하면 蔣介石이 銃殺(或은 監禁) 당하고 張學良이 이러선 것이 오직 抗日과 容共때문이었다 하니까.

「貴下는 萬一의 境遇에 四川省 成都에다가 政府를 옴기고 英米露 勢力을 비러 日本과 一戰하려 하느냐 한즉 그는 나는 군인출신이기에 戰爭에 對한 일이면 곳 判斷된다고 前提하고
「北方 蘇滿國境에 蘇聯側의 토-치가 及 空軍에 對抗하여 日本도 相當한 準備를 하고 있는 줄 안다. 그런데 歐洲國境에 後顧의 憂가 있는 蘇聯이 決코 日本에 對하여 攻勢에 나올 수 없으리라고 推測한다. 英國에 對하여도 그는 香港보다는 新가坡에 물너가서 防備하고 있는 이즈음 英國이 容易하 武力으로서 極東에 向해 오지 않으라, 蘇聯은 舊帝國時代의 露西亞와 달나서 軍事에도 外交에도 英國에 뭇지 않는 計策을 가지고 있어 좀처럼 日露戰爭과 같은<46> 行動을 取하지 않으리라. 이러할 때에 四圍의 情勢로, 日中相戰한다 하면, 機先을 制하는데 敏한 日本은 그 武力으로 中國의 要所所要을 占領하리라. 그래서 中國은 莫大한 損害를 보리라, 이리되면 나도 이러나서 陣頭에 나서리라, 그러하면 全 中國의 愛國運動도 土匪도 流*도 共匪도 모다 日本에 向하야 움지기리라. 이 全面的 抗日을 막기 위하야 日本은 貴重한 軍隊를 全 中國의 두지 않이치 못하리라. 이리되면 全國民의 經濟도 産業도 混亂되어, 日本의 對中經濟도 全滅하리라, 日本은 財와 兵을 않이 濫費하리라, 이리하야 中國은 破滅에 頻하고 日本은 피폐함에 이르러 그때에야 말로, 極東에 대하야 虎視耽耽하든 列國은 움지겨 나오리라. 이리되면 이 때 禍亂을 입을 것은 民國뿐 아니고 日本도 또한 免치 못하리라, 그런 즉 日中의 利害와 東洋의 利害를 따저 볼 때 日中間에 戰爭이 있어선 않된다.
함이었다, 한마듸로 말하면 戰爭은 해서 익이든지 지든지 兩國은 亡하고, 東洋에는 英米蘇勢力이 삐칠 터이니 戰爭을 避하겠다 하는 것이 蔣介石 及 現 國民政府 首腦部의 見解라 한다다.
