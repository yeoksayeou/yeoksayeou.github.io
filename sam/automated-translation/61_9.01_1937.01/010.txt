Information on the Two Newspaper Companies
The Two Major Newspapers Still Under Suspension – Why Can’t They Resume Publication Yet?

Current Situation of the Tonga Ilbo

Despite the general speculation that the suspension of the *Tonga Ilbo* should have been lifted by now, given that Mr. Song Chinu (宋鎭禹) took ultimate responsibility and resigned as president, thus receiving a certain degree of punishment, why are the newspaper company's rotary presses still not running? We will report on the internal circumstances behind this. The course of events was as follows:
As a result of an executive meeting, the leadership of the *Tonga Ilbo* appointed Mr. Yang Wŏnmo (梁源模) as managing director to act as president in place of Mr. Song Chinu, and initiated negotiations with the Government-General (總督府) for the lifting of the suspension<9>. However, as some figures acquainted with the newspaper world might guess, putting Mr. Yang Wŏnmo forward was deemed insufficient to fulfill the requirement of holding the executives accountable. This is because Mr. Yang was seen as merely an extension of Mr. Song, leading everyone to anticipate some future personnel change, viewing Mr. Yang as provisional. Simply put, Mr. Yang was seen as a figure who would move exactly as Mr. Song dictated. Furthermore, who could guarantee that Mr. Song would not reappear as his successor on the day Mr. Yang stepped down? Moreover, Mr. Yang Wŏnmo was the editor and publisher of the magazine *Sindonga* (新東亞), making him directly responsible for the Japanese Flag Erasure Incident (日章旗抹消事件), and he was also an influential executive at the *Tonga Ilbo* at the time of the incident. Due to all these points, despite repeated negotiations, they reportedly failed to obtain the Government-General's understanding.

Consequently, as a second plan, the executive team was reorganized. Namely, lawyer Mr. Kim Yongmu (金用茂), currently a pillar of the Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) concern and president of Posŏng Chŏnmun School (普成專門學校), was nominated as president; Mr. Ko Chaeuk (高在旭), currently a political reporter in the editorial department, was appointed editor-in-chief; and Mr. Yi T'aero (李泰魯) of Kyŏngsŏng Spinning Co., Ltd. (京城紡績株式會社), another wing of the Kim Sŏngsu concern, was appointed business manager. Negotiations were held with the Government-General with this new lineup. However, according to highly confidential news, the Government-General reportedly still refused to give its understanding, stating that the fundamental spirit of the *Tonga Ilbo* was wrong because it did not open its doors to society as a whole and reorganize based on talent, but instead stubbornly persisted in staffing primarily with individuals from Chŏlla Province (全羅道) and the Kim Sŏngsu network. Thus, negotiations for lifting the suspension are currently said to be in a state of delay. Regardless of whether these individuals would be acceptable to the Government-General or not (which remains unknown), the opinion among informed private citizens is that the Chŏlla faction (全羅道閥) should be dismantled on this occasion. They hope that the paper will liquidate its factional, petty mindset, seek talent broadly from society, and achieve a truly genuine rebirth of the *Tonga Ilbo*.

Two Views on Lifting the Suspension = The *Tonga Ilbo* was suspended on August 27th, and by New Year's January, a long period of six months will have passed. It seems that suspensions become longer with repeated offenses. Looking at the past record where the suspension period increased progressively from 40 days to 60 days, and then from 60 days to 4 months with the first, second, and third suspensions, there is speculation that this time it might last even longer. Some deeply pessimistic observers hold the quite hopeless view that if the rotary presses are kept idle for such a long time, salaries will eventually become impossible to pay. Then, employees will naturally disperse like Zhang Liang's (張子房) soldiers scattering at the sound of the jade flute (玉銅蕭), dealing a major blow to the finances and naturally leading to [bankruptcy? word obscured/censored]<10>. The basis for this view is rumored to be a new policy from a certain quarter (某邊) suggesting that having only the *Maeil Sinbo* (每申) as the official organ and the *Chosŏn Ilbo* (朝鮮日報) as the private paper coexisting would suffice. However, believing that the ruling authorities would surely not be so narrow-minded, we anticipate that the suspension will eventually be lifted after receiving firm commitments. As for the timing, might the suspension be lifted at the beginning of the New Year, when auspicious energy (瑞氣) fills the world and Governor-General Minami's (南總督) administration enters its second year, in the spirit of "rejoicing together with the people" (與人民共樂)? Even if it is deemed that reflection is still insufficient and a later date is awaited, we predict the suspension will be lifted by the Kigensetsu (紀元節 - Empire Day) on February 11th at the latest, which marks three years before the 2,600th year of the imperial era (皇紀).

Mr. Chang Tŏksu's Place of Rest?

Though it may be a rumor, there was an attempt to appoint Mr. Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), who returned after 14 years in America, as president and reorganize with a new lineup. However, it is said that there was a hint from a certain quarter (某邊) suggesting that, in the spirit of "shared destiny" (一蓮托生), even the position of vice president should be reconsidered, let alone president. If so, where will Mr. Chang go? Given that he, as a scholar, is hesitant even about the vice presidency of a newspaper company, attention is drawn to whether a professorship at Posŏng Chŏnmun School (普成專門學校) would suit him. According to some recent news, since he is a Christian and reportedly traveled around various churches in North America preaching while in the US, there is also speculation that he might perhaps re-emerge as the president of the Korean Christian Youth Association Federation (朝鮮基督敎靑年聯合會), which is currently facing a crisis due to a lack of personnel.

The Difficult Birth of the *Chungang Ilbo*

Then why is the *Chungang Ilbo* (中央日報) also stuck in a state of difficult birth? To put it simply, the *Tonga Ilbo*'s difficulty stems from having money but lacking suitable personnel, while the *Chungang Ilbo*'s difficulty stems from having personnel but lacking money. Listing the major shareholders now = The individuals holding financial control over the *Chungang Ilbo* are:
Ch'oe Sŏnik (崔善益) 7,800 shares
Sŏng Naghŏn (成樂憲) 9,000 shares (of which 4,000 are ghost shares (幽靈株), so effectively) 5,000 shares
Yun Hŭijung (尹希重) 3,000 shares
Kang Wijŏng (姜偉情) 800 shares
One share is fully paid-in at 20 won, so converting to monetary value:
Ch'oe Sŏnik (崔善益) 156,000 won <11>
Sŏng Naghŏn (成樂憲) 100,000 won
Yun Hŭijung (尹希重) 60,000 won
Kang Wijŏng (姜偉情) 16,000 won
However, there is difficulty in selecting the top executives. That is, the old financial faction (舊財閥派), including Yŏ Unhyŏng (呂運亨) and Ch'oe Sŏnik, tried to nominate Mr. Kim Tongsong (金東成), the former editor-in-chief of the same company, as president. But this also failed to gain the Government-General's understanding, being seen as an extension of the Yŏ and Ch'oe lineage, leading to a standstill. On the other hand, the new financial faction (新財閥派), such as Mr. Sŏng Naghŏn, intends to put up the problematic 80,000 won and quickly resume publication with Mr. Sŏng as president. However, they assert that unless Ch'oe Sŏnik's side completely abandons all its past claims (舊債權) and relinquishes its right to speak on future newspaper management, they cannot confidently proceed with running the newspaper under a unified structure. But Ch'oe Sŏnik's side insists that their past investment of 160,000 won must be acknowledged, and a new foundation should be created by adding capital on top of that. Due to this fundamental gap between the two sides' positions, the situation remains utterly unresolved. According to one rumor, upon hearing that the newspaper company was in trouble, Mr. Kang Wijŏng of Chinnamp'o (鎭南浦), who had once considered taking over the management but backed out, came to Seoul a few days ago shouldering 150,000 won and negotiated with the current newspaper authorities. However, due to the insistence from Ch'oe Sŏnik's side on recognizing past debts amounting to 170,000-180,000 won, he reportedly broke off the three days of negotiations and returned to Chinnamp'o. In conclusion, the author's conjecture is that the *Chungang Ilbo* might resume publication only after a major capital reduction (大減資) of the current 500,000 won = eventually reducing all past claims proportionally to around 100,000 won, and then a new foundation comes in with at least 110,000 won (100,000 won plus 10,000 won more) to secure a controlling majority interest. This is the observation. In any case, the major obstacle of recognizing old debts (舊債) delays the emergence of new capital. Therefore, if this problem is not resolved through capital reduction, perhaps a second step <12> might be taken: the current executives liquidate the old Chungang Ilbo Co., Ltd. (舊株式會社 中央日報社) cleanly. They sell the current asset, the 80,000 won office building, distribute the proceeds pro-rata among the shareholders. Then, new capital comes in to establish a new, solely owned stock company, inheriting and continuing under the *Chungang Ilbo* title. To save the *Chungang Ilbo*, there seem to be only two paths: either adopt a policy of drastic capital reduction, a true "devaluation" (平價切下), reducing the current 500,000 won capital to about one-fifth, or, in the sense of clearing away past chronic problems (痼疾), completely dissolve the old stock company and establish a new company in its place*. The finances of both newspaper companies are currently severely hit*. Both *Chungang* and *Tonga* have been unable to pay even a penny of employee salaries since November. Employees are left to scatter as they will, and many have already resigned from the newspaper companies. Furthermore, a prominent commentator (論客) like Mr. Pae Sŏngnyong (裵成龍), who was the political editor at *Chungang Ilbo*, has already changed jobs to become the editor-in-chief of the weekly newspaper *Chungang Sibo* (中央時報). If this situation continues for another 2-3 months, following the natural law that "eating comes first" (食而爲大), "sightseeing Mt. Kŭmgang after eating" (食後金剛山求景), and "one must live to go even to remote Kapsan" (사라야 山水甲山 간다), there will be those who leave the journalism field*. Thus, it is also questionable how these two newspaper companies, having lost so many talented reporters, will be able to compete against the editorial and business lineups of the *Chosŏn Ilbo* and *Maeil Sinbo* even if they resume publication.<13>

**Summary**

This 1937 article details the prolonged suspension of Korea's two major private newspapers, the *Tonga Ilbo* and *Chungang Ilbo*, by the Japanese Government-General. The *Tonga Ilbo*'s delay is attributed to the Government-General's dissatisfaction with personnel appointments, specifically rejecting figures seen as extensions of the former president or part of the dominant "Kim Sŏngsu / Chŏlla faction," demanding a broader, talent-based restructuring. The *Chungang Ilbo*'s suspension persists due to financial deadlock between old and potentially new investors over acknowledging past debts, hindering the infusion of necessary capital. Both papers face severe financial crises, unable to pay salaries since November 1936, leading to staff departures and raising doubts about their future competitiveness even if publication resumes. The article speculates on potential resumption dates, possibly early 1937 or by Kigensetsu (Feb 11th), while noting pessimistic views about the papers' survival. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the Government-General's methods of press control (beyond direct censorship, influencing personnel and finances), the internal politics and factionalism within Korean-owned enterprises, the precarious financial state of colonial media, and the specific impact of the 1936 Japanese Flag Erasure Incident.

東亞日報現在事情

宋鎭禹씨가 최고 책임을 지고 사장을 사임하였으니 받을 벌을 어느 정도까지 받고 낫다을 東亞日報는 벌서 해금이 되염즉 하리라고 일반이 추측함에도 불구하고 엇재서 여전히 신문사의 윤전기는 돌아가지 못하고 있는가. 여기 대한 내면 사정을 보도하리라, 즉 그 경위는 이러하였다.
東亞日報 수뇌부는 중역회의 결과로 宋鎭禹씨에 대함에 梁源模씨로 하여 사장 직무를 대행케 하는 전무로 천하여서 總督府에 해금 교섭을<9> 하였다. 그러나 웬만한 신문계 호흡을 마신 일부 인사는 짐작 하드시 梁源模씨를 내세워서는 간부 引責의 實이 거해지지 않는다 함이니 梁씨를 宋씨의 一延長으로 보아서, 梁씨는 잠정적이고, 「그 뒤에 오는 엇던 인사사태」를 누구나 예측하였기 때문이다. 쉽게 말하면 梁씨는 宋씨의 寸方대로 움지길 인물이라 보았다. 또 梁씨 退하는 날 宋씨 또한 그 후계로 나서지 않는다고 누가 보증하랴. 더구나 梁源模씨는 잡지 「新東亞」의 편집겸 발행인으로서 日章旗抹消事件의 당면의 책임자이요 또한 동아일보의 사건당시의 유력 간부임에랴, 이 모든 점에서 재삼 교섭했슴에도 불구하고 總督府의 양해를 엇지 못하고 말었다 한다.
이에 第二段方策으로 간부를 개조하였으니 즉 사장에 현재 金性洙 컨체른의 一支柱요, 普成專門學校校長으로 있는 변호사 金用茂씨를 擁立하고, 현재 편집국 정치부기자인 高在旭씨를 편집국장으로, 또 같은 金成洙 컨제른의 일익인 京城紡績株式會社의 李泰魯씨로 하여금 영업국장으로,―이러한 진영을 차리고 總督府에 교섭하였으나, 극비중에 들니는 소식에 의하면 總督府에서는 東亞日報가 전 사회에 문호를 개방하여 인재본위의 개조를 하지안코 의연히 全羅道 及 金成洙系 인물로만 다지려 하는 그 근본정신이 틀녓다 하야 終是 양해를 주지않어 지금은 해금 교섭이 遲遲한 형세에 있다는 *설도 있다. 좌우 *민간 일반식자의 견해도 總督府에서 容許하는 인물일는지 안일는지 그는 未詳하나, 全羅道閥만은 이 기회에 타파하여, 파벌적 소아병적 그 정신을 청산하고, 널니 인재를 세상에 구하야 정말 참다운 東亞日報의 更生을 희망하고 있다.
解禁에 대한 二觀測=東亞日報는 8월 27일에 정간 이래 新春 1월까지 헤면 장장 6개월의 장기에 미첫다. 屢犯일수록 장기인 모양으로 제1차 정간, 제2차 정간, 제3차 정간으로 정간 때마다 40일간이든 것이 60일로 60일이는 것이 4개월로 그 정간일수가 遞增된 과거기록으로 보아 이번은 좀더 장기에 미치리란 설도 있는데, 심하게 비관하는 이는 이렇게 작고 장기로 윤전기를 못 돌니게 하면, 결국는 봉급을 못 주게되야 사원은 張子房의 玉銅蕭 소리에 사산하듯, 저절로 분산하여 버릴 것이요, 그래서 재정에 큰 타격을 바더 자연 00에까지 이르게 하리란<10> 아조 절망적 견해 가진 이도 있는데 이 說의 근거는 기관지로 每申, 민간지로 朝鮮日報만 兩立식혀 노오면 그만 아니냐 하는 某邊의 신방침이라고 전하기도 한다. 그러나 吾人은 통치 당국이 결코 이렇듯 狹量하지 않으리라고 믿음으로 결국 단단한 다짐을 밧고 해금이 되여지리라고 보는데 그 시기는 宇內에 瑞氣가 차고 南總督施政 제2년을 맛는 신년정초에 與人民共樂의 의미로서 해금이 되지 않을가, 만일 아직도 반성이 부족하다 하여, 후기를 기다리게 한다 할지라도 느저도 皇紀 2,600년을 3년 후에 마지할 2월 11일의 紀元節에는 해금이 되어지리라고 관측한다.

張德秀氏의 安住의 地?

풍문인지 모르나 米國서 14년만에 도라온 張德秀씨를 사장으로 擁立하고 新陳容을 整齊하려 하였으나, 某邊의 의향이 一蓮托生의 의미로 사장은 고사하고 부사장까지 고려하란 암시가 있었다 한다. 그렇면 張씨는 어데로 갈고, 물론 학자로서 普成專門學校 敎授로 강단에 서재도 신문사 부사장조차 마다하는 터에 敎授의 자리도 었덜가 주목된다. 그래서 요지간 일부의 소식에 의하면 그가 기독교인에 귀의하여 米國있을 때는 北米 각지 교회로 도라다니며 전도하였다 하느니만치 오히려 지금 인물이 없어 위기에 처하여 있는 朝鮮基督敎靑年聯合會의 회장쯤으로 부활하지 않을가 하는 관측도 있다.

中央日報의 難産
