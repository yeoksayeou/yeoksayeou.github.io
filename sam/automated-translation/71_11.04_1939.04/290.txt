Magazine Title: 삼천리 제11권 제4호
Publication Date: 1939년 04월 01일
Article Title: 기다림(외 9편) (Waiting (and 9 other poems))
Author: 春園 (Ch'unwŏn)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Waiting

When my hair-like body comes, I adorn myself beautifully,
Washing, applying makeup, changing into freshly starched clothes,
With a fluttering heart, I sit, going in and out.

Waiting for the Beloved, sitting as if painted beneath the lamp,
The short summer night feels like a thousand years,
As the rooster crows three times, tears flow naturally. <224>

Oh dear, was I that foolish yesterday?
I forgot to open the door latch for the Beloved's arrival.
Seeing the footprints left behind, I break down and weep.

Henceforth, forget all else, even adornment,
The door latch will be left wide open,
How could I risk letting the Beloved, who came again, slip away?

Had You asked me to open, I would have run on stockinged feet,
But without a word, the departed Beloved is gone.
When will You come again? I fear I will grow old. <225>

Shall I leave to search? Leaving home and all behind?
Shall I wander, searching far and wide for the Beloved's traces?
Let the place where I collapse on the way be my grave. <226>

Humble Me

Why would the Beloved favor me?
Like showing pity to a beggar standing at the gate,
Saying You favor this one, I too feel audacious.

Though I shed fine clothes and adorn myself in every way,
When I stand before the Beloved, my appearance is shabby.
Yet You still touch me, and I am choked with tears. <227>

Coming to the Beloved's house in worn-out straw shoes*,
Just gazing at the gate and turning back,
Is because I cannot bear to show this wretched state to You. <228>

I Cast Off Adornment

Now, having no Beloved to see in this world,
Shall I apply powder? Shall I sprinkle perfume?
I cast off adornment, I wear rags.

There was a time I believed my face was beautiful.
Now that I know I am like a crow or a toad,
Would I ever dare to look in a mirror again? <229>

Seeing a warty toad crawling in the garden, suddenly
I reflect on my mindset of the past half-life.
Like me, the toad lives on, pleased with itself. <230>

Even Without a Home

Even without a home, even without arms and legs,
As long as one is alive, can there be one without a Beloved?
Let's see by following our own Beloveds.

Is there any way to follow the Beloved other than devotion?
Offer body and soul, bound together,
And go wherever the Beloved leads, I say. <231>

Rather than choosing a new Beloved, follow the one you have.
If you follow and follow the existing Beloved to the very end,
If a new Beloved is meant to come, they will come naturally, I think.

Oh, seeker of the true Beloved, seeking this one and that one,
Giving and receiving affection for a day or two,
How could you know the deep true love of the Beloved?

Once you meet one Beloved, do not kill them [in your heart].
They are not just any other; that one is indeed the Beloved.
Whether one becomes the Beloved or not depends on me, I say. <232>

Is it Vain Worry?

If I truly believed You would seek me,
I would not refuse to wait even for a thousand kalpas,
But sometimes I despair, wondering if You are a Beloved who has forgotten.

The prospect of seeing You is remote, connections are severed,
There is no way to know the Beloved's intention,
So I cast lots, and my face flushes, I say. <233>

The vow You made, that You wouldn't change even if heaven and earth vanished,
Surely You wouldn't change in this world?
Is it just my anxious self? Am I worrying in vain, I wonder? <234>

God
(Luke 12)

Though You are All-knowing, I always failed to trust You more than my own mind;
Though You are All-powerful, I always failed to trust You more than my own strength;
I am one who failed to trust the One who should be trusted.

You nurture and raise, feed and clothe,
Shine light upon, soothe and touch,
Always holding me in Your bosom, yet I claimed I wasn't held. <235>

Calculating the value of doing well when I do well, and the value of failing when I fail,
Without adding or subtracting, You measure precisely,
And distribute evenly, yet I called You unfair.

If the Beloved were not here, how could I live in this dark world?
Moreover, on the path of death, who else is there to rely on?
Truly, because the Beloved is here, my heart is steadfast.

Is there a single strand of my hair You do not know?
Each day of my life is prepared by You.
Not even one bird in the sky is forgotten by You. <236>

Since the spring rain has fallen, I should sow the seeds You gave;
Since the leaves have grown, I should weed the fields;
Making things ripen and mature is something the Beloved will do by hand.

Postscript: "O ye of little faith." Little faith was my foolish pride. "Fear not." Daily there is anxiety, and hourly fear arises! What happened to the pride that refused to believe? You and I cry out daily, "for the morrow," worrying "What shall we eat? What shall we wear?" What we gain is only suffering and death. Our outcry is the sound of *losing faith, so you and I have reached the day to call for the salvation of faith. <237>

Long, Long Dream

If I part again this time with the Beloved met over countless kalpas,
How many more countless kalpas must I wander to meet You again?
Even if this body turns to dust, I cannot let go.

You are the true master, destroying and creating the universe,
The intention that I should govern all sentient beings (微塵衆生),
I forgot, and wandered through the Three Realms and Six Paths (三界六道). <238>

Mistaking Ignorance (無明) for light, in the sea of birth and death,
As I bobbed along the dark waves,
Somewhere a drum sounded, awakening me from a long, long dream. <239>

Forgotten Intention

Who but the Beloved knows the intention held within me?
Knowing even the intention I held yet did not know myself,
You awaken me from time to time, Your grace is profound.

The vow made so firmly before the Beloved back then,
Forgotten, all forgotten, even forgot that I forgot.
If the Beloved had not reminded me, I almost would have forgotten forever. <240>

If my birth and growth were not by my strength but the Beloved's,
How could matters of life and death be by my strength?
Just calling out "Beloved!" seems to be my only strength, I think. <241>

Heaven and Earth

A day during a long hospital stay. The day is hot, the body aches, there is no money, my affairs and others' affairs don't go as intended, and I cannot even walk around freely outside. In such times, complaints about the suffering of life (人生苦) arise playfully.
However, realizing it is all one's own fault and one's own mind, the song of suffering breaks apart before its melody is even complete.

They say heaven and earth are vast, but what is so vast?
There's no place to put this body, barely larger than a span,
Chased by sorrow and pain, I know not where to go. <242>

Once, even a small room felt desolately spacious,
Yet at times, the entire Heaven and Earth feel suffocatingly narrow.
Ah, I realize, this way and that way are just my mind, I think.

When my mind is at rest, even a storm feels tranquil;
When it surges once, heaven and earth leap together.
I have grasped the hidden pulse of Creation (造化), I declare. <243>

It feels as if the Beloved has left me behind. It feels like You abandoned me in this lonely, painful world and went to find another beautiful beloved. How unfair! How resentful! Falling into the Three Evil Paths (三惡途) due to various desires and karmic affinities (諸慾因緣), wandering through the Six Realms (六趣), experiencing all sufferings and torments (苦毒), due to lack of virtue and fortune (薄德少福), losing the way in the forest of wrong views (邪見), taking delusion (虛妄) for truth, refusing to let go no matter what, yet still thinking oneself supreme, all-knowing, and correct, with a mind twisted and false, unable to enter the right path even after countless kalpas (千萬劫) – such are the sentient beings impossible to save (濟度 못할 衆生). (Lotus Sutra 法華經)
I too am such a sentient being, but sometimes I awaken from the dream and have moments where I feel embraced in the Beloved's arms, gazing upon the Beloved's beautiful face. These poems are from such moments.
When will this "Moment" become my "Eternity"? <244>

You say the Beloved left? Is the Beloved one who would leave me?
Unable to truly leave, You hold me thus in Your arms,
Yet in a dream, the self leaves the Beloved and knows not how to return.

Not knowing the dream is a dream, believing it real,
Trying to grasp, refusing to let go, weeping and laughing over illusions,
Crying out that the Beloved abandoned me, I break down and weep.

When the time comes and I awaken from the dream, the Beloved's face is as before.
That joy, that sorrow, what lived, what died,
Was all that just a dream, while held in the Beloved's embrace? <245>

Summer Sun

What among all Creation (萬物) is not the Beloved's child?
The various ways You soothe me, looking upon me,
The brightness and warmth of the summer sun are especially so.

On that day, receiving the command at the Vulture Peak Assembly (靈山會) and withdrawing,
Where did I wander and what did I do during those three thousand years?
Today, suddenly awakened, it must be the Beloved's call. <246>

If I close my eyes, if I block my ears and nose,
Perhaps the incoming light and sound might cease,
But the voice arising from within—there's no way to stop it.

I want to sever this mind, pull it out by the roots,
Burn it with fire until even the ashes are washed away.
Once empty and tranquil, then I shall hear that voice. <247>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su) explores themes of intense spiritual longing, devotion, and the struggle with faith and doubt. The speaker addresses an unnamed "Im" (Beloved/You/Master), expressing feelings of unworthiness, the pain of perceived separation, and the ultimate solace found in recognizing the Beloved's constant presence, whether understood through Christian ("하나님"/God, Luke 12) or Buddhist (Lotus Sutra, kalpas, Six Realms) frameworks. The poems, framed by prose passages reflecting personal hardship (illness, poverty) and spiritual searching, depict moments of despair and doubt alternating with moments of profound connection and surrender to a higher power or reality, often described as awakening from a dream. Historically, the work is interesting as it showcases Yi Kwang-su's complex spiritual syncretism during the Japanese colonial period, blending Christian and Buddhist concepts in his personal expression of suffering and search for meaning. This reflects the broader intellectual and spiritual currents of the time among Korean intellectuals grappling with modernity, tradition, and national hardship.

기다림(외 9편)
						春園
기다림

숱같은 몸이 오매 단장하다 고우리만
씻고 바르고 빨대빈 옷 가라입고
설레는 마음을 앉고 들락날락 하오라

불 밑에 그린듯이 앉어 임을 기다릴 제
여름 짧은 밤이 천년 만은 한지이고
세 홰를 재우처 우니 눈물 절로 흘러라 <224>

어지어 내일이어 그리도 어리던가
임 듯일 문 빗장을 열을 줄을 잊었서라
오섯던 자욱 뵈옵고 몸을 부려 우노라

이후란 다 말고서 단장도 마옵고서
문 붙어 여올 것이 활작 여러 놓을 것이
다시야 다 오신 님을 놓칠 줄이 있으랴

여러라 하실진댄 보선 발로 여올 것을
한 말슴 없으시고 가신 님도 가신 님이
어느 제 다시 오시리 다 늙을가 하노라 <225>

찾아 떠날가나 집도 모도 다 버리고
임의 종적을 두루 찾아 헤맬가나
가다가 쓰러진 곧이 무덤인가 합소서. <226>

초라한 나

임이 나를 혀오시니 무삼보고 혀오시리
문전에 선 걸인을 어였브이 여기심이
이 님을 혀오다하니 나도 무엄 하여라

고은 옷 떨떨이고 갖은 단장 다 하와도
임 앞에 서올 때에 초초하온 행색이어
그래도 만저주시오니 눈물 겨워 하노라. <227>

허위 *신으로 임의 집을 찾아 와서
문전만 바라옵고 그저 도라가는 뜻은
차마도 초라한 이 꼴을 못 뵈와서 임니다. <228>

단장을 버리나이다

내 이제 이 세상에 뵈올 님 없아오니
분인들 발르리까 향물인들 뿌리리까
단장을 버리나이다 누더기를 입나이다.

제 얼굴 고운 줄로 믿던 적도 있었것다
가마귀 두꺼비와 같은 낸줄 아옵거든
다시야 거울 맛나기로 비처볼 줄 있소리까. <229>

뜰에 긔어가는 옴두꺼비 보고 문득
지난 반생의 내 마음이 생각혀라
날같이 두껍이 저도 잘난 맛에 살아라. <230>

집도 다 없어도

집도 다 없어도 팔 다리도 다 없어도
살아 있는 사이로 임 없는 이 있소릿가
저마다 제 임 따라가 보면 알까 하노라.

임을 따르는 길이 정성 밖에 또 있는가
몸도 맘도 함께 싸고 묶어 바치고서
그 님이 끄시는대로 가 보소서 하노라. <231>

새 님 고르느니 있는 임을 따를 것이
있는 님 따라따라 끝간 데를 가량이면
새님이 오실 님이면 절로 올까 하노라.
