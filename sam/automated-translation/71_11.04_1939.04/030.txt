Roundtable Discussion in Shanghai with Military, Officials, and Civilians
A Plan for the Advancement of the Korean Populace into New China

What are the conditions like in Shanghai, Nanjing, Hankou, Suzhou, and Hangzhou after the Incident? If the Korean populace were to advance commercially or in other fields, could they succeed? We decided to invite key figures currently in Shanghai to hear their thoughts on all these issues. We plan to continue holding these on-site roundtable discussions regarding the South China and North China regions in the future.
Date and Time: February 26th, 4:00 PM
Location: Shanghai Club (in Hongkou) <30>

Attendees

Colonel Hong's Opinion

Special Correspondent = It is deeply gratifying to have this opportunity for figures from the military, the Government-General authorities, the Civil Association leadership, and distinguished individuals from the financial, business, and media worlds of mainland Korea to gather in one place like this after the Incident, looking out over the 400-some provinces of the newly rising China and discussing plans for the advancement of the Korean populace onto this new continent. The impressions of those who have come from the mainland after inspecting the sites in Shanghai, Nanjing, Hankou, etc., and the thoughts of the military and Government-General officials here in Shanghai who are considering national policy, will surely be listened to with great attention by our fellow countrymen back in mainland Korea and will be of great help for future advancement into China.
It is somewhat regrettable that, with the war still ongoing, delicate international relations and the military situation may prevent completely frank discussion. However, we hope you will speak as much as possible to satisfy the people back in mainland Korea.
Now then, for the sake of proceeding with the agenda, perhaps Colonel Hong could begin by introducing himself and speaking about the general situation in the Central China region centered around Shanghai.
Colonel Hong = We cannot contain our pleasure at having such a large number of influential figures from the financial and media worlds of mainland Korea visit Shanghai for an inspection this time. Following the moderator's request, I will first introduce myself and speak about the conditions in the Central China area after the Incident. My hometown is Kyŏngsŏng, Kyŏnggi Province. That was already 30 years ago, in Meiji 42 (1909), when I went to Tokyo, graduated from the Army Cadet School, and have since served in the military until today. I came to Shanghai late the year before last, that is, late in Shōwa 12 (1937), right after the fall of Nanjing.
I'll stop with the personal story there. Having come here and seen and felt things, this area around Shanghai along the Yangtze River has historically been a place of unceasing warfare throughout ancient, medieval, and modern Chinese history.
You all occasionally hear sayings like "Wu and Yue in the same boat" (吳越同舟) or "The struggle between Wu and Yue" (吳越相爭), don't you? That state of "Wu" (吳) refers to Suzhou near Shanghai, and the state of "Yue" (越) refers to Hangzhou over there. <32> Suzhou and Hangzhou were always bases for warfare. Also, considering the Three Kingdoms period, and especially that Nanjing was the capital of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom, the entire Shanghai, Nanjing, Suzhou-Hangzhou area, wherever you go, is the site of old battlefields.
And as you well know, this place Shanghai is the great artery of the Yangtze River. The upstream and downstream regions are truly China's great natural treasury, where 60 percent of all China's industry is gathered.
The total trade of Central China is about 5 billion yen, and this trade centers on the triangle formed by Shanghai, Nanjing, and Hangzhou. The resources abundant in the Yangtze River area include iron, *, and tung oil (*Note: the asterisk represents an illegible character in the original text).
From this perspective, the economic value of Central China is truly immense. Therefore, it is highly significant for our resource-poor Japan to advance into this area. In contrast, Chiang Kai-shek's anti-Japanese policy ultimately provoked the Incident.
Special Correspondent = Yes, I understand. By the way, what is the situation regarding foreign capital in Shanghai?
Colonel Hong = Looking at the amount invested in Shanghai, Great Britain has 1.4 billion yen, Japan 400 million yen, and the United States 200 million yen.
Therefore, British influence was the greatest in the past. Consequently, the fact that our Empire is facing various difficulties with the British regarding post-war construction issues is occasionally reported in the newspapers.
Special Correspondent = After experiencing the Incident, that is, in the post-war present, what is the current state of the Chinese people?
Colonel Hong = Because Shanghai and Nanjing endured urban warfare, the devastation of their cityscapes, as you have seen, was extensive. However, since public order has been maintained by the hands of the Imperial Army, they are now focusing all efforts on reconstruction.
However, the first-rate Chinese figures and institutions from the financial and business worlds that existed before have all fled.
The circulation of goods in Shanghai is insufficient and not proceeding as desired, causing some hardship for the law-abiding citizens. Nevertheless, our Japan is steadfastly working to eradicate the Chiang regime and aid the good citizens in their reconstruction efforts. <33>
Special Correspondent = What is the status of the activities of the national policy companies in Central China?
Colonel Hong = Yes, the Central China Economic Development Company (中支經濟開發會社) was established in March of last year and began operations in April. It has about 10 affiliated companies, each active in its field. Namely:
1. Iron Ore Company (centered on the Yangtze River basin)
2. Electric Power and Waterworks Company (controls within the occupied territories)
3. Telecommunications Company (Sino-Japanese joint venture)
4. Shanghai Real Estate Company (urban planning, port facilities, railway matches, etc.)
5. Sericulture Company (approx. 150 silk reeling factories)
6. Inland Steamship Company (Yangtze River area, Japanese-Chinese steamships)
7. Fisheries Company (currently handles about 100,000 yen worth of sales)
8. Urban Bus Company (Shanghai-Nanjing route, Sino-Japanese joint venture)
9. Gas Company
10. Airline Company

Besides these, financial issues are difficult and currently under study.
In short, all major businesses are Sino-Japanese joint ventures. In addition, private sector investment from Tokyo, Osaka, and other parts of the mainland (Japan) has recently reached about 300 million yen.
Special Correspondent = Please tell us a bit more specifically about the investment situation.
Colonel Hong = In the industrial sector, spinning is the largest, and although many places were destroyed in the Incident, they are being restored. Businesses like tobacco and matches are also being run as Sino-Japanese joint ventures. Commercially speaking, the Yangtze River, which was the main artery, had been blockaded by the Chinese army but is now being progressively occupied and developed by the Imperial Army. This region has a dense population and high culture, comparable to the Nagoya-Osaka * area in mainland Japan. <34> The area between Nanjing and Shanghai is a plain, so the population is quite dense, and the disparity between rich and poor is not that great.
Regarding the situation of the Chinese people, about 80% have returned since the Incident, and the farming villages haven't changed excessively. In places like Suzhou, the population has actually increased compared to before the Incident.
Crop yields per tan are recovering to about 70-80% of pre-Incident levels, and it is believed that they can generally recover to pre-Incident levels within the next two years.
Samch'ŏlli Special Correspondent = What is the attitude of the Westerners here?
Colonel Hong = The British and French Concessions are within the Western sphere of influence, so things don't always go according to our intentions. They also suffered considerable setbacks due to the Incident and fear their rights and interests are being taken away. They were secretly aiding Chiang Kai-shek, but since the capture of Hankou and Guangdong by the Imperial Army, there is a tendency for them to bow their heads to us.
Samch'ŏlli Special Correspondent = Then, finally, what about the most critical issue: the relationship between Central China and Korea?
Colonel Hong = Yes, that is an important issue. Looking at past facts, the economic relationship was not very significant. If anything, it was limited to Korean ginseng. However, after the Incident, a Korean Products Exhibition was held with the support of the Government-General, and subsequently, marine products and other goods needed by the Chinese are being imported. From this side, silk fabrics from Hangzhou and other places are entering Korea.
Special Correspondent = What is the distribution status of Koreans?
Colonel Hong = Many Koreans have advanced here after the Incident. As a result, trading companies have been established, and Koreans have entered banks and steamship companies, occupying significant positions and engaging in great activity. The total population here includes mainland Japanese, whose numbers in Shanghai alone increased from 26,000 before the Incident to 37,000 now. Besides these, the floating population is about 45,000. In Nanjing, there are 10,000. Anyway, in the three cities area, there are about 60,000.
Now that the overall situation is stabilizing, we hope that many influential Koreans will advance here. <35>

Opinion of the Government-General Administrative Official

Special Correspondent = With this, we have clearly understood Colonel Hong's opinions. Now, Mr. Harada, who is dispatched to Shanghai as a Government-General Administrative Official, please speak about the activities of Koreans.
Government-General Dispatched Administrative Official = I am pleased that this meaningful roundtable discussion is being held today. Now, let me briefly speak about the situation of Koreans in Shanghai.
Before the Incident occurred, there were many instances where Koreans caused unpleasant feelings. However, since the Incident began, these have all been resolved. Now, thanks to the guidance of various leaders within Korea and the self-awareness of the Koreans here locally, perceptions have greatly changed.
In terms of population distribution, before the Incident, 1,700 Koreans were concentrated in Shanghai, with not many in Nanjing or Hangzhou. But since the fall of Nanjing the year before last, the number of Koreans has gradually increased, even reaching the Hankou and Wuchang areas.
So now, there are about 4,000 Koreans, roughly double the pre-Incident number:
Shanghai: 2,500
Nanjing: 1,500
Many are also found further in the interior, and notably, there are many women. Also, unlike before, people are generally undertaking steady businesses, and their enterprises are prospering, leading to the emergence of many capitalists with assets around 100,000 yen. Furthermore, subversive political movements have disappeared, and consequently, incidents like being threatened by comrades have ceased, and the living conditions of individuals have generally improved.
The number of children attending school, which was only seven or eight before, now exceeds 40. In the future, the Government-General also intends to implement various facilities for Koreans. In short, those currently active in the Central China region must resolve to become the foundation, unite as one, create economically sound organizations, and gradually build up operational units to solidify their base. On this favorable occasion, when you return to Korea, please convey the local situation well and encourage many to come as part of the continental development force. Moreover, Colonel Hong, being in the highest echelons of the military <36> and exerting great effort in the economic sphere, especially in construction, will surely be able to provide convenience to you all.

Trade and Other Relations

Special Correspondent = Please tell us more about the commercial and trade relations between mainland Korea and this Shanghai, Nanjing, Hankou region. Many influential figures in the financial and business worlds back in the mainland wish to advance into the Central China area, but many hesitate because they do not fully understand the situation.
Korea Trade Association Shanghai Branch Manager = That's correct. Let me speak about the trade relations between Korea and Central China after the Incident.
The total value handled from April last year until today was about 245,000 yen. Fishery products coming from Inch'ŏn, Pusan, etc., were the most significant item. Besides that, there were fruits, soy sauce, soybean paste, kimchi, and similar items from Chinnamp'o, Hwangju, etc.
Maeil Sinbo Editor-in-Chief Kim Hyŏngwŏn = As I recently came here for a Central China inspection tour, I am well aware of the recent situation in mainland Korea. Generally, there are many prominent businessmen who intend to advance into the Central China region after the Incident. However, the biggest obstacles here are funding and the exchange rate situation. How is that handled? To come here and start a business, one must bring funds, but currently, exchange regulations prevent bringing in more than 1,000 yen. How should we think about this relationship?
Government-General Administrative Official = Regarding this issue, it's a problem shared by both those gentlemen wishing to come from mainland Korea and the resident peninsular people already here. This is not just a hardship for Koreans; the entire Japanese nation faces the same difficulty.
However, regarding this matter, those of you in Korea should reach a good understanding with the Government-General authorities. Also, if you consult well with the Bank of Chosŏn and the Oriental Development Company, I believe you can find accommodation. Furthermore, since the manager at the Bank of Shanghai was appointed from Taegu, I trust he will be quite understanding. If the nature of the business is sound and has potential for development, we also intend to provide convenience by liaising with the Bank of Chosŏn, Oriental Development Company, Bank of Shanghai, etc. Please convey this intention clearly to the people in Korea. <37>
Shanghai Korean People's Association President Yi Kabyŏng = On this good occasion, let me also introduce a couple of things. After the Incident, with the assistance of the Military, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and the Government-General of Korea, the Korean People's Association (朝鮮人民會) was organized. Influential local figures formed a group, establishing a Council (參事會) and Advisory Board (評議員會). We also hold National Language (Japanese) Seminars (國語講習會) offering free instruction, and besides that, we are considering various industrial matters. We hope that Shanghai will become a base point for the great development of Koreans extending into Nanjing and the Hankou area in the future.
Kim Han'gyu = I previously traveled through the Shanghai-Nanjing area, staying two nights before the Incident. At that time, anti-Japanese posters were plastered along the railway lines, and during a single day's travel, I was inspected by officials over 60 times. Then, less than two weeks later, the Incident broke out.
Touring the Central China area this time, I was immensely pleased to see it heading straight towards reconstruction.

Recent Returnees to North China

According to what was heard from military sources, the recovery situation of civilians returning to North China after the capture of Hankou was as follows. (Note: The text stops here without providing the details).

Income of Shanghai Koreans

Though it's dishonorable to say, before the Incident, the occupations of Shanghai Koreans often included dealing opium, pimping for prostitutes (阿片괭이 갈보장사), etc. After the Incident, however, some have started managing companies and factories, and others have become legitimate merchants. Many are employed by banks or companies, earning monthly salaries of over 100 yen. Thus, their lives have become relatively stable. According to hearsay, there are quite a few who have amassed fortunes of over 100,000 yen, and several people earn monthly salaries of 500-600 yen. <40>

Korean Cuisine in Shanghai

Many Korean women, from 17-18 to around 30 years old, work as waitresses (女給) in various cafes, and many women earn 300-400 yen as dancers (딴사-) in dance halls (딴스홀). Korean restaurants (朝鮮料理店) are also increasing. On the other hand, in the Hongkou District (虹口地區), mainland Japanese-style restaurants (內地料理店) are very prosperous. Their types include:
Sushi shops (壽司屋), Oden shops (オテンや), Shiruko shops (しるこ), etc.
There are five dance halls run by mainland Japanese, and countless cafes and restaurants.

Accommodation Fees in Beijing

For sightseeing in Beijing, if one wants to stay in a decent hotel or inn run by mainland Japanese, options include:
Nikka Hotel (日華ホケル), Yenching Hotel (燕京ホケル), Yamato Hotel (大和ホケル), Fusōkan (扶桑舘), Fukushimakan (福島舘), etc. Accommodation fees are around 5 to 15 yen. Western-style hotels include:
Peking Hotel (北京飯店), Central Hotel (中央飯店), Grand Hôtel des Wagons-Lits (六國飯店).
Besides these, there are also Korean inns where one can stay for 2-3 yen per night.

Recent Situation in Shijiazhuang

Known to Koreans because Mr. Kim Yŏnsu of Kyŏngsŏng Spinning (京城紡績) established a Kyŏngbang factory there, Shijiazhuang has recently seen a small increase in its Korean population. Originally the starting point of the Shijiazhuang-Taiyuan Line (石太線), with Zhengding (正定) to the north and the Jingxing Coal Mine (井陉炭坑) to the south, it is the most important transportation hub in southern Hebei Province (河北省). The climate has extreme differences between cold and heat, making it difficult to endure for those who are not very healthy.

Scenery of Hangzhou

Mr. Ch'oe Sŏnik and others were sightseeing in Hangzhou city when it got late at night, and on their way back to their lodging, they were cautioned by a sentry. While walking around late at night requires some consideration, public order has now generally stabilized. It takes about 5 hours by train from Shanghai. It is famous as the scenic spot of West Lake (西湖). Writing brushes, inksticks (筆墨類), and damask silk (緞子) are produced here in abundance. It is also the home of Shaoxing wine (紹興酒). There aren't that many Koreans, but the mainland Japanese population, which was only about 20 before the Incident, is now over 800, with 37 mainland Japanese police officers stationed there. <41>

Recent Situation in Suzhou

Suzhou can be reached in about 2 hours by taking a Nanjing-bound train from Shanghai North Station (上海北停車場). Getting off at the station and taking a rickshaw (黃包車) for 15 minutes brings you inside the city walls (城內). It is the location of Hanshan Temple (寒山寺), famous from the line "Moon sets, crow cries, frost fills the sky" (月落鳥啼 霜滿天). Canglang Pavilion (滄浪亭), the Confucius Temple (孔子廟), and Beisi Pagoda (北寺塔) were all worth seeing. Koreans are gradually increasing here. The mainland Japanese population, which was about 70 before the Incident, is now over 500, with 32 mainland Japanese police officers stationed there.

Koreans in Nanjing

Many Korean women have gone to Nanjing. It is said there are about 200 of them. Various city areas like Zhongshan Road (中山路), which were destroyed by the fires of war (戰火), were all undergoing reconstruction.
The previously heard-of Observatory on Purple Mountain (紫金山天文台) and the site of the former headquarters (舊大本營跡) were worth seeing. Historically famous sites like the Fuzi Temple (夫子廟) in the Qinhuai (秦淮) district, Yuhuatai (雨花台), Jiming Temple (鷄鳴寺), Mochou Lake (莫愁湖), and Xuanwu Lake (玄武湖) were all restored enough for strolling. Koreans are also entering, while the mainland Japanese population, which was small before the Incident, is now about 2,000, with 115 police officers stationed there. <42>

**Summary**

This 1939 roundtable discussion, held in Shanghai and reported by Samch'ŏlli magazine, brought together Japanese military personnel (including a high-ranking Korean officer, Hong Saik), colonial administrators, and Korean business and media figures. They discussed the conditions in Japanese-occupied Central China (Shanghai, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Suzhou, Hankou) following the "Incident" (Second Sino-Japanese War) and explored strategies for Korean economic advancement into this region, framed as "New China." Topics included post-war reconstruction efforts led by the Japanese military, the significant economic potential (resources, trade, industry), the role of Japanese national policy companies, challenges like capital controls and lingering Western influence, and the increasing presence and changing activities of Koreans in these cities, shifting from previously "dishonorable" trades to more formal businesses and employment facilitated by the new colonial order.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the complex position of Koreans under Japanese rule. It explicitly shows Korean elites participating in discussions about capitalizing on Japanese military expansion into China, viewing the occupation as an opportunity for economic advancement. The perspective of Colonel Hong Saik, a Korean in a high position within the Japanese Imperial Army actively involved in economic planning for occupied China, is particularly noteworthy for historians studying collaboration and identity during the colonial period. The article also provides valuable details on the socio-economic conditions and Korean demographics in occupied Chinese cities at the time.

洪大佐의 意見

특파원=사변후 이와 같이 軍部와 總督府 官憲과 民會측 간부와 그리고 朝鮮內地로부터 이러틋 財界 實業界 言論界의 名士 諸氏가 한자리에 회합하여 新興하는 支那4백여 州를 바라보면서 이 신대륙에 향하여 朝鮮민중의 進出策을 협의하는 이 자리를 가지게 되었으니 심히 깁부올시다. 上海, 南京, 漢口, 등 현지를 시찰하고 도라온 內地로부터 오신 여러분의 그 感想이나 이 곳 上海 현지에서 國策을 생각하고 게시는 軍部總督府 官憲 諸氏의 소감은 朝鮮 內地에 있는 여러 동포들이 몹시 주목하여 드를 것이며 또한 장래 支那方面 진출에 큰 도움이 될 줄로 생각함니다.
다만 아직 전쟁이 진행중이 되야 떼리케잇트한 국제관계와 군사형편으로 솔직한 말슴을 하여 주시지 못할 것은 다소 유감으로 생각함니다만은 될 수 있는데로 朝鮮 內地의 人士들에게 만족이 가도록 말슴하여 주시기를 바람니다.
그러면 議事진행상 洪大佐께서 몬저 자기 소개를 겸하여 上海를 중심으로 한 中支那 一帶의 情形을 말슴하여 주섯스면 좋겟음니다.
洪大佐=금번 朝鮮 內地로부터 財界 言論界의 유력한 人士 다수가 上海시찰로 와 주시니 우리는 반가운 생각을 금할 길 없읍니다. 司會의 말슴에 쪼차 내가 몬저 자기소개를 겸하여 사변후 中支 一帶의 형편을 말슴코저 함니다. 나는 고향이 京畿道 京城임니다. 그것이 벌서 30년 전인 明治42년에 동경에 드러가서 陸軍士官學校를 마치고 그 뒤 군대에 근무하여 오늘에 미첫는데 上海에 오기는 재작년 말 즉 昭和12년 말 南京이 함낙된 卽後였음니다.
이만침 一身上 이야기는 곤치기로 하고 여기와서 보고 늣긴 바이지만 이 上海를 중심한 揚子江 일대의 地는 원래 支那上古, 中古, 근대의 전 역사를 통하여 늘 전란이 不絶하든 곳이였어요.
여러분도 「吳越同舟」라거나 「吳越相爭」이란 말슴을 각금 듯지 안으서요. 그 「 吳나라」라는 것이 上海 근처에 있는 蘇州요 「越나라」라 함이 저 杭州를 가르친 말이엇어요. <32>蘇州 杭州가 늘 전란의 근거지였어요. 또 三國時代라거나 더구나 太平天國의 首都가 南京이었으니 上海, 南京, 蘇杭州 일대는 어데로 가든지 옛 戰場이든 터이지요.
그리고 여러분도 잘 아시겠지만 이 上海란 곳은 陽子江의 大動脈이라 할 것으로 상류와 하류 지역은 진실로 支那의 천연적 큰 富庫로서 全支那의 산업의 60 파-센트가 여기 모여 있지요.
中支那 전체의 무역은 약 50억원이요 이 무역은 上海 南京 杭州를 삼각형으로 하여 그 중추를 삼었는데 陽子江 일대의 産物로는 鐵, *, 桐油 등의 풍부한 자원이 있어요.
이런 점으로 보면 中支那의 경제적 가치는 참으로 커요. 그러기에 자원이 빈약한 우리 日本이 이 방면에 진출하는 것은 크게 의의가 있는데 반하여 蔣介石의 抗日정책은 끗끗내 事變을 이르켜 노았어요.
特派員=네 알겠음니다. 그런데 上海에 있어서의 외국의 자본관계는 엇더함니까.
洪大佐=上海에 투자한 額을 보면 英吉利 14億圓 日本 4億圓 米國 2億圓
그러기에 英國 세력이 과거에 제일 컸어요. 따라서 我帝國이 戰後 건설문제에 있어 英人과 種種의 곤란을 격고 있는 것은 신문지상에도 각금 보도되여 있지요.

崔善益씨 등 여러분이 杭州市街을 구경타가 밤 늦게 되어 숙사로 도라오다가 보초에게 주의를 밧은 일이 있거니와 夜深하여 다니기는 좀 생각할 정도이나 그러나 이제는 대개 치안이 안정되였다. 上海에서 차를 타고 약 5시간 西湖의 勝地로 유명한 곳이다. 여기*는 筆黑類와 緞子가 많이 난다. 또 紹興酒의 본바닥이 여기다. 朝鮮人은 그러만치 못하나 內地人은 事變前20餘人이든 것이 지금은 800餘名이요 內地人 경찰관 37 명이 배치되여 있다.<41>

蘇州의 最近

蘇州는 上海北停車場에서 南京行의 기차를 타고 2시간이면 도착한다. 역에서 내려 黃包車를 타고 15분이면 城內에 드러 간다 月落鳥啼 霜滿天으로 유명한 寒山寺의 소재지다. 滄浪亭, 孔子廟, 北寺塔이 모다 볼 만 하였다. 여기는 차츰 朝鮮사람이 늘어가고 있으며 內地人은 事變前 70餘名이든 것이 지금은 5백餘名 日本 內地人 경찰관 32명이 배치 되여 있다.

南京의 朝鮮人

南京에 많이 가 있는 것은 朝鮮 여자들이다. 약 200 명이나 된다든가 戰火로 하여 破毁되었든 中山路 등 여러 市街가 모다 復興 도중에 있었다.
옛듯든 紫金山上의 天文台 舊大本營跡도 볼만 하거니와 역사적으로 유명한 奏淮의 구릭그에 夫子廟 雨花台 鷄鳴寺 莫愁湖 玄武湖 등 모다 산책하도록 부흥이 되여 있었고 朝鮮人도 드러가고 있거니와 內地人은 事變前은 少數더니 지금은 약 2천 명 경찰관도 115 명이 배치되여 있다.<42>
