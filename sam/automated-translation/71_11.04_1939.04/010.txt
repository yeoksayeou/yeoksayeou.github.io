**Manchukuo Army: 100,000 Soldiers**

The Manchukuo Army (滿洲國軍) is under the command of the Emperor. To manage this, the Ministry of Public Security (治安部) serves as the central organ. The Minister of Public Security oversees military command and administration (軍令及軍政), and also serves as the staff organ (帷幄機關) regarding command.
As for regional command structures for the Army, excluding Hsingan Province (興安省), the entire country is divided into six military districts (六軍管區), where one military district generally covers two provinces under the new administrative divisions. Military District Commands (軍管區司令部) are established here to oversee military command and administrative matters within their jurisdiction. Within each military district, considering command convenience and administrative divisions, it is further divided into several zones where Garrison Commands (警備司令部) are placed to command the troops within the zone. Separately, Garrison Commands (警備軍司令部) are established in the Hsingan provinces (興安各省) to command the Garrison Army (警備軍) composed solely of Mongolian soldiers (蒙古兵). The Military District Commanders and the Hsingan Garrison Army Commanders are all directly subordinate to the Emperor (皇帝直隷).
The total strength of these various forces is approximately 100,000. The main combat branches (戰鬪兵種) are infantry (步), cavalry (騎), and artillery (砲). The strategic units are the Mixed Brigade (混成旅) and Cavalry Brigade (騎兵旅). Regarding equipment, due to the need for joint operations with the Japanese Army (日滿軍協同作戰) and maintaining public order, and sharing the supply source with the Imperial Army (皇軍), efforts have been made since the army's founding to completely standardize major weapons (主要兵器) and heavy firearms (重火器). As a result, currently, almost all have been converted to the Japanese style (日本式).
Furthermore, the Border Surveillance Corps (國境監視隊), newly established in Shōwa 11 (1936), considering the internal and external situation, has partly been reorganized into the National Army (國軍), with its main force transferred to the Security Bureau (保安局) to become the border guard force. The Peace Preservation Corps (治安隊), two years after its establishment, was largely transferred to the Prefectural Police (縣警察) last March to adapt to reforms in the state structure and changing circumstances.
Recruitment of personnel (兵員), since last fiscal year, is based on the New Recruitment Guidelines (新募兵要綱) under the jurisdiction of the Minister of Military Affairs, recruiting relatively high-quality young men from rural areas nationwide to replace old soldiers (舊老兵). Therefore, in the near future, it will be possible to establish a form comparable in all aspects to the armies of other nations (列國軍).
Now, six years since its founding, under the guidance of the Imperial Army (皇軍), it has shed the abuses (陋弊) of the old army era, reformed internally, strived for training (陋練 - likely a typo for 熟練, training/skill), and improved both in substance and appearance.<17>

**General Wu Peifu Rises**

**Doesn't Take Concubines, Doesn't Love Money, Doesn't Enter Concessions**

Initially, Tang Shaoyi (唐紹儀) and Wu Peifu (吳佩孚) were considered as leaders (主班) for the new Chinese regime (支那 新政權), but only Wu Peifu remained. And finally, Wu Peifu has risen (蹶起). At the end of last year, representatives from various quarters urging him to emerge (出馬) flooded his residence daily. The normally quiet alley of Shimian Huayuan (什綿花園) became congested with cars, overwhelming Wu Peifu. Requests for his emergence from various organizations nationwide piled up on his desk. Amidst this, Wu Peifu slowly inclined towards the path that many observers desired. At that time, a close associate described Wu Peifu's state of mind: "If it is truly for peace and national salvation (和平救國), General Wu's long-held conviction is not to worry about personal reputation or criticism (一身의 毁譽褒貶). Although he hasn't expressed any intention regarding the nationwide requests to emerge, if this is truly the will of Heaven and the will of the people (天意요 民意), he will certainly break his decade-long silence, resolutely emerge, and fight like an Asura (阿修羅) for peace and national salvation." Indeed, the statement that he would emerge without regard for himself and fight like an Asura is not the least bit false.
Now, it is no exaggeration to say that everything from the organization of the Central Government of China (支那中央政府) to the cooperation between the Japanese and Chinese peoples (日支兩國民族提携) rests on his shoulders (双肩).
Since September 18, Shōwa 2 (1927), when he was miserably defeated by Chiang Kai-shek's (蔣介石) northern expedition army (北上軍), left his temporary residence in Jinan (濟南) with his wife and barely a thousand soldiers (手兵 千), and fled to Nanyang (南陽), Wu Peifu had been abandoned by the political forefront for over a decade. But now he has re-emerged as a leader for the construction of a New East Asia (新東亞建設).
Wu Peifu was born in Penglai County, Shandong Province (山東省蓬萊縣) and is 70 years old this year. In his youth, as a soldier (軍士), he led troops of the Zhili Army (直隷軍手兵) in several revolutions (제1, 제2, 제3等 數革命), suppressing forces in Shanxi (山西), Hunan (胡南), Sichuan (四川), Yunnan (雲南), etc., achieving great merit (偉功). He eventually became the commander-in-chief (總帥) of the Zhili Army. In 1922, he started the Zhili-Fengtian War (奉直戰爭), drove Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) to Manchuria (滿洲), established the golden age of the Zhili clique (直隷派), and his influence extended from the Hebei-Jiangsu-Yangtze region (河蔣長江 일대) to Fujian (福連 - likely Fujian 福建).
After being heavily defeated by Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥) in the Second Zhili-Fengtian War (第2奉直戰) in 1924, he passed through Tianjin (天津), Shanghai (上海), Hankou (漢口) and fled to Henan (河南), looking for a chance to rise again (再擧).
Later, he allied with Zhang Zuolin, creating an era of Fengtian-Zhili cooperation (奉直합작시대), but suffered the bitter cup of defeat again due to Chiang Kai-shek's northern expedition army.
He has a sharp appearance (精悍風貌) but gives a gentle impression. Despite his advanced age, he is very healthy (健全). However, perhaps due to spending half his life in hardship or disappointment, a trace of loneliness (寂寞) lingers. He is a thorough nationalist (國家主義者) who normally adheres to the "Three Nos" (三不主義): "Doesn't love money" (不愛錢), "Doesn't take concubines" (不納妾), "Doesn't enter foreign concessions" (不入租界). This shows his integrity and uprightness (淸廉剛直).<22>

**Wang Ching-wei's Movements**

**His Eventful Career**

Among those called Chinese revolutionaries (支那革命家), there is probably no one else with such a turbulent and winding (波瀾曲折) life. Moreover, his career itself can be said to have shaped the history of the national revolution (國民革命史), as he played such an important role.
He was born and raised in Guangdong (廣東), but at the age of 19, he went to Japan (日本), and while studying at Hosei University (法政大學), he was influenced by Sun Yat-sen (孫文), a senior from his hometown who was in exile at the time. Joining the Tongmenghui (同盟會) marked the start of his revolutionary activities. He quickly became a cadre (幹部) and simultaneously worked as a reporter for the confidential paper "Min Bao" (機密紙「民報」) published in Tokyo. Then, following Sun Yat-sen, he returned to China and initiated uprisings (暴動) in Guangdong, Guangxi (廣西), etc. After failing, he infiltrated Beijing (北京) and daringly attempted to assassinate the Prince Regent Zaifeng (攝政王 載灃), father of the Xuantong Emperor (宣統皇帝), single-handedly. He failed and was even sentenced to death, but Prince Su (肅親王), recognizing the young man's talent (才), spared his life. While imprisoned, the First Revolution (第1革命) occurred, and he was immediately released.
After the establishment of the Republic (民國), he was appointed Governor of Guangdong (廣東都督) and Minister of Justice (司法部長), but he accepted neither, instead going to France (佛蘭西) to study law again for three years. After returning, he held important positions in the Kuomintang (國民黨) and the Guangdong Government (廣東政府), always sharing hardships under Sun Yat-sen. Thus, he, Hu Hanmin (胡漢民), and Liao Zhongkai (廖仲愷) were called Sun Yat-sen's "Three Greats" (三羽鳥).
Among them, his character was considered the best, and Wang was popular even among party members (黨人). When he later inherited Sun Yat-sen's mantle (衣鉢) and became a pillar (柱石) of the Kuomintang, public trust in him was immense.
However, when Sun Yat-sen reorganized the Kuomintang, creating the Zhongguo Kuomintang (中國國民黨), and simultaneously organized a new army with Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) taking military command, Chiang's power suddenly grew strong. When Chiang tried to carry out the Northern Expedition (北伐) before Sun's death, Wang advocated that the time was premature, leading to a split with Chiang. Thereafter, Chiang's power grew even greater with the success of the Northern Expedition. A distance developed between him and Chiang. During that time, he tried to gain influence alongside Yan Xishan (閻錫山), Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥), etc., who opposed Chiang's dictatorship (獨裁), but failed. His last stand was opposing from the Guangdong Government. Since compromising in 1932, he had always maintained pace with Chiang.

His political career (政治的 閱歷) has truly been extremely checkered (數奇), and at times even his political principles (政治的 節操) have been questioned. However, his convictions (信念) are quite strong. In Shōwa 10 (1935), in his capacity as Foreign Minister (外交部長), he was almost hit by an assassin's bullet (兇彈) for supposedly conceding to Japan regarding the China Incident (支那事變). But this too stemmed from his strong convictions. Therefore, although the world calls him pro-Japanese (親日派) or leftist (左派), he is neither. Rather, he is likely a true patriot (眞正의 愛國者). It is no exaggeration to say that currently in China, apart from Wang Ching-wei, there is no one who can be counted as a statesman of great stature (大政治家). His recent actions too stemmed from his fervent patriotism (熱烈한 愛國心), observing the larger situation (大局) and seeking to save China from the ravages of war (戰褐 - likely 戰禍, ravages of war) through political means.
He turns 54 this year but appears perpetually youthful (萬年青年), always looking handsome and giving a soft impression like a woman. However, once he ascends the podium (演壇), he not only charms the masses but captures them with his eloquence and passion (雄辯과 熱). In short, he is a born revolutionary (革命兒). It is unknown what his fate will be henceforth, but he will probably follow the path of a revolutionary for his homeland until the very end.<23>

This April 1939 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine presents a collection of news snippets under the title "Secret Room, Various Inner Workings of Our Society." It covers diverse topics including projected gold production figures for prominent Korean mine owners (Yi Chongman, Pak Yongun, Kim Chunsang, Kim Pong'o) under the Government-General's national policy, contrasting them with potentially retiring older figures (Ch'oe Ch'anghak, Wŏn Yunsu). Col. Yi Ryang expressing disillusionment and hopes for peace. It reports on the Manchukuo army's structure, colonial army strengths, extensive anti-spy warnings detailing alleged foreign espionage activities targeting Japanese and colonial assets, and news from literary/film circles (potential writer dispatch to China, Yi Kwangsu's novel copyright sale, activities of actresses Chŏn Ok, Ch'a Hongnyŏ, Han Ŭnjin, and Hollywood debut of An Ch'angho's son Philip Ahn). Finally, it profiles Chinese figures Wu Peifu and Wang Ching-wei, discussing their potential roles in the ongoing Sino-Japanese conflict from a perspective favoring Japanese-led peace initiatives.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is revealing in several ways. It highlights the active participation of Korean capitalists and industrialists within the Japanese colonial economic framework, particularly in strategic sectors like mining (driven by wartime gold demand) and manufacturing, even forming joint ventures. The piece on espionage reflects the heightened wartime paranoia and surveillance state permeating the Japanese empire, including Korea. The profiles of Chinese leaders Wu Peifu and Wang Ching-wei, presented sympathetically as potential collaborators for peace, align with Japanese propaganda efforts to establish alternative regimes in China. The cultural notes, like the fundraising for Wani Shrine and the activities of Korean writers and film stars (including Philip Ahn in Hollywood), provide glimpses into the cultural life and transnational connections of Koreans during this period, operating within the constraints and opportunities of the colonial system.

李鍾萬씨가 사장인 大同鑛業會社의 所有鑛인 長津金鑛에서 금년 중에 얼마나한 産金을 내일가함은 세간에서 모다 주목하는 사실인데 여기 대하야 전일에 總督府 殖産局 産金課에서 朝鮮안 유수한 鑛業者들을 불너 14년도의 예정액을 査定하였는데 長津鑛山의 産金額은 518돈 197만원으로 되였다. 물론 國策에 의하여 파내는 것임으로 이 예정액대로는 어떠한 경우가 있을지라도 산출키로 되었음으로 금년에는 이 산액에 꼭 도달될 것이라고 보여진다.

滿洲國陸軍十萬名

滿洲國軍은 황제의 통솔하는 바로 여기에 統轄하기 위하여 中央機關으로서 治安部를 두고 治安部 大臣은 軍令及軍政을 統轄하며 또 統師에 관해서는 帷幄機關을 겸임하고 있다.
陸軍側 地方統轄機關으로서는 興安省을 除하고 전국을 六軍管區(一軍管區는 대개 新行政區에 의하는 二省을 포함함)에 分하여 여기에 軍管區司令部를 설치하여 관내의 軍令軍政事項을 統轄식혀 각 軍官區內에 在하여는 指揮統御의 便과 行政區劃을 고려하고 약간의 地區로 구분하여 여기에 警備司令部를 두고 地區內軍隊를 지휘식히고 있다. 또 따로 興安各省에 警備軍司令部를 두어 蒙古兵만으로 된 警備軍을 統轄식히고 있다. 軍管區司令官及興安各警備軍司令官은 어느 것이나 皇帝直隷다.
이상 諸軍의 總兵力은 약 10만으로서 戰鬪兵種은 主로서 步, 騎, 砲의 3종으로 하고 戰略單位로서 混成旅及騎兵旅가 있어서 그 장비는 日滿軍協同作戰竝治安維持上 皇軍과 그 補給源을 같이 하는 관계상 건군 이래 전혀 主要兵器 竝重火器의 정비 통일에 노력한 결과, 현재에 있어선 대개 全數를 일본식에 改裝하고 있다.
그리고 또 昭和 11년에 신설한 國境監視隊는 내외 정세를 살퍼 일부를 國軍으로 개편하고 주력을 保安局에 이관하여 국경경비대로 하였다. 治安隊는 창설이래 2개년으로서 작년 3월 국가기구의 개혁과 정세 변화에 순응기 위하여 그 태반을 縣警察에 移管하였다.
兵員의 보충은 1년 昨年度以降 軍部大臣의 統轄하는 新募兵要綱에 기하여 전국적으로 비교적 소질 우량한 지방 청년으로부터 모집하는 舊老兵과 更新하고 있음으로 오라지 않은 장래엔 各般에 있어서 列國軍과 逕庭한 형태를 정비 할 수 있을 것이다.
지금 建軍이래 6개년 皇軍指導하에 舊軍시대의 陋弊로부터 脫却하여 내용 改하고 陋練에 노력하여 내용 외관이 함께 향상하고 있다.<17>

이제 支那中央政府의 조직으로부터 日支兩國民族提携에 이르기까지 모다가 그의 双肩에 매달닜다고 해도 과언이 아니다.
昭和2년 9월 18일 蔣介石의 北上軍까닭에 무참히도 패한 뒤 부인과 함께 겨우 手兵 千을 이끌고 濟南의 假住居를 뒤로 하고 南陽에 피한 때부터 10여년, 전혀 政界표면에서 버리운 吳佩孚도 新東亞建設의 지도자로 다시 이러나게 되였다.
吳佩孚는 山東省蓬萊縣에서 났는데 本年 70세. 젊어서 軍士로 제1, 제2, 제3等 數革命에 늘 直隷軍手兵을 이끌고 山西, 胡南, 四川, 雲南 등을 토벌하여 偉功을 세워 드디여 直隷軍의 總帥가 되고 1922년에는 奉直戰爭을 이르켜 張作霖을 滿洲에 쫓아 直隷派의 전성 시대를 싸코 威勢는 河蔣長江 일대에서 福連에까지 미쳤다.
1924년 제 2奉直戰에서 馮玉祥에게 大敗한 후 天津, 上海, 漢口를 지나 河南에 피하여 再擧의 기회를 였보고 있었다.
그 후 張作霖과 結하여 奉直합작시대를 작성했으나 蔣介石외 北上軍 때문에 또한 패배의 고배를 들게  되였든 것이다.
그는 精悍風貌에 온후한 인상을 줄 뿐 아니라 노령임에도 불구하고 매우 健全해 부이나 실의의 半生을 보낸 탓인지 일말의 적막한 빛이 떠돈다. 철저한 국가주의자로 평소 「不愛錢」「不納妾」「不入租界」의 三不主義를 奉하고 있는데 여기에서 그의 淸廉剛直이 였보인다.<22>

汪兆銘의 動向

波瀾滿丈의 그 經歷

아마 支那혁명가라 칭하는 사람들 중에도 그처렴 波瀾曲折이 많은 사람은 별로 업슬 것이다. 더구나 그 경력 그대로가 국민혁명사를 만들어 왔다고 하리만큼 그는 중요한 역할을 演하고 왔다.

그 외 정치적 閱歷은 진실로 數奇를 극하여 때로는 정치적 節操까지 의심바더 왔으나 그의 신념은 자못 강한 것으로 昭和10년 外交部長의 자격으로서 支那事變에서 日本에 양보했다고 하여 兇彈을 바들변한 일도 있기는 하나 이것 역시 그의 강한 신념에서 생긴 일이였으니까 세상에서 그 친일파 혹은 좌파라고 불르나 어느 편도 아니다. 오히려 진정한 애국자일 것이다. 현재 支那에서 大政治家다운 정치가는 汪兆銘을 빼놓고 헬 사람이 업다고 해도 과언은 아니다. 이번 그의 행동도 그가 大局을 관찰하여 정치적 수단으로 支那를 戰褐에서 구하고저 하는 열렬한 애국심에서였다.
그는 금년으로 54세가 되나 만년청년으로 언제 봐도 곱고 여자와 같이 부드러운 감을 준다. 그러치만한 번 演壇애 오르기만 하면 대중을 매료할 뿐 아니라 웅변과 열로서 대중을 捕促하고 만다. 요컨대 그는 선천적으로 革命兒으로 今後 그의 운명이 어떻계 될지 모르나 아마 그는 조국을 위하여 최후까지 혁명가로서의 길을 밟을 것이리라.<23>
