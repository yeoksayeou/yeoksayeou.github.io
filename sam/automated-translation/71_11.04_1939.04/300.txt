**Body Text Translation**
Ireland Yeats Poem Selection
						I Hae-yun

To a Young Girl

Dear girl, dear girl,
Why your heart beats so fiercely,
I know better than anyone.
Even the mother who bore you
Could not know the reason as well as I.
He shattered the heart I offered him.
Thoughts maddeningly painful
(Though he says it wasn't so,
and I've forgotten now)
Made his blood rush through his whole body
And brought a gleam to his eyes.

Discarding the Floweriness for the Substance <248> (去華就實)

Though the leaves are many, the root is one.
While young, living amidst all falsehoods,
I shook leaves and flowers in the sun;
Now, it's fine to wither in truth.

The Sorrow of Lost Love

Pale forehead, quiet hands, and black hair,
I had a beautiful friend.
I thought old disappointments would vanish and new love would bloom,
But it was a dream. One day, he examined my heart,
Saw your reflection there,
And just wept and left.

A Deeply Pledged Vow

Because you didn't keep that deeply pledged vow,
Unlike you, I gained a friend.
Yet whenever I face death,
When I crawl to the summit of high sleep,
Or when I am drunk with wine,
Suddenly I face your countenance. <249>

The Collarbone of a Wild Hare

I shall launch a sailboat upon the water and sail away
To where numerous kings went searching,
To where numerous princesses went searching.
Yes, upon a grassy field where beautiful trees flourish,
Where they dance while playing the bagpipes, I
Shall learn the best thing:
Receiving a loved one while dancing,
And repaying kisses only with kisses—
From the other side of the water, I shall search
For the collarbone of a wild hare,
Licked by the waves, thin and delicate.
I shall pierce this with an awl and look through
At the old, sorrowful world that binds marriages in churches.
Yes, beyond the calm waves,
I shall laugh at the shadow of marriages bound in churches,
Through the white, thin bone of a wild hare. <250>

**Summary**

This article presents Korean translations by I Hae-yun of five poems by the Irish poet W.B. Yeats: "To a Young Girl," "The Coming of Wisdom with Time" (interpreted as "去華就實"), "The Sorrow of Love," "A Deep-Sworn Vow," and "The Collar-Bone of a Hare." The translations capture themes of love, loss, disillusionment, aging, and a longing for a different reality, rendered in the Korean language of the late 1930s. For historians, this publication is significant as it demonstrates the introduction and reception of prominent Western literature, specifically Irish poetry, within colonial Korea. The choice of Yeats, a figure associated with Irish nationalism and cultural revival, might have resonated subtly with Korean intellectuals navigating their own cultural identity under Japanese rule, representing a quiet engagement with external literary traditions during a period of restricted expression.

						異海潤
어린 處女에게

귀여운 처녀야 귀여운 처녀야
웨 네 가슴이 그러틋 몹시 뛰는지를
누구보다도 내가 잘 알고 있느니
너를 나하준 어머니라도
그 까닭을 나만치 알 길이 없으리
그에게 바첬든 내 마음을 그는 깨트렸노라
미칠 뜻 괴로운 생각이
(그는 그러치 않다고
지금은 이젔지마는)
그의 피를 온 몸에 끌어오르게 하고
그의 눈에 光彩를 도덧섯것만

去華就實<248>

넙흔 많어도 뿌리는 하나
젊었을 제 온갓 虛僞로 사는 동안
나는 입파리와 꽃을 했볕에 흔들었으매
이제 眞實 속에서 시들어도 좋으리

失戀의 설움

창백한 이마, 고요한 손과 검은 머리
나는 한 아름다운 벗이 있어
녜전 失望은 사라지고 새로운 사랑이 꽃필줄 알었드니
꿈이러라 그는 어느날 내가슴을 삷혀
거기 비친 그대 그림자를 보고는
그만 울면서 가버리였노라

깊이 言約한 盟誓

그러틋 깊이 言約한 盟誓를 그대가 직히지 안하
달니 내게는 벗이 생겼것만
언제나 내가 죽엄을 눈 앞에 볼 때
높은 잠의 山頂에 내가 기어오를 때
혹은 내가 술에 醉할 때
忽然히 나는 그대 얼골을 對하노라 <249>

들토끼의 鎖骨

나는 물 우에 帆船을 띄워 달녀가 보리
數많은 왕이 찾어가든 곳으로
數많은 王女가 찾어가든 곳으로
그래 아름다운 樹木이 茂盛한 잔듸밧 우에
風笛를 불며 춤추는 곳에서 나러
내 배호리라 가장 좋은 것을
춤추는 동안 사랑하는 사람을 받고는
또 키쓰를 오직 키쓰로 갑는-
물 저편 가에서 나는 찾어 보리니
물결에 할티워 가늘고 얇아진
들토끼의 鎖骨을,
송곳으로 이것을 뚫으고 드러다 보리
聖堂에서 혼인을 맷는 낡고 괴로운 세계를
그래 나는 고요한 물결 넘어로
聖堂에서 혼인을 맷는 그늘을 우스리라
희고 가는 들토끼의 뼈를 통하야. <250>
