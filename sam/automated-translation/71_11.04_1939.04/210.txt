Life of Beijing University Students, Five Universities Have Opened
						By O Ch'op'o in Beijing

Just as it's said that Tokyo in Japan and Beijing in China have the most schools in the world, Beijing truly has many schools.
Before the recent Incident began, there were 12 universities and 14 vocational colleges alone. Even after the New Government was established, the schools continuing to operate are:
National Normal University (國立師範大學),
The affiliated Beijing University (同北京大學),
Privately run Zhongguo University (中國大學)
The American-affiliated Yenching University (燕京大學)
The German-affiliated Fu Jen University (輔仁大學)
These five schools have over 5,000 students combined. The students are all children of wealthy families and intellectuals, all standing in leadership positions.

Student Life Is

In a word, student life can be described as boarding house (公寓) life; it has a deep connection with the boarding house (公寓). A boarding house (公寓) is, simply put, like an apartment. Rooms are separated by wooden partitions (板子), and each room is equipped with three items: 1 table (卓子), 1 chair, and 1 washstand with legs (脚세면대), provided for general student use. The room rent (房貰) is around 6 or 7 won, and meals cost from 8 won to 12 or 13 won a month.
Boarding houses (公寓) come in various sizes; some accommodate 12 or 13 people, while larger ones can house 300 or 400 people.<173> Not only students but also outsiders (外來者) are sometimes allowed temporary use.
The American-affiliated Yenching University, a stately hall located to the right of the motor road leading to Longevity Hill (萬壽山), the Summer Palace of Empress Dowager Cixi (西太后), in the western suburbs of Beijing, has excellent dormitory facilities similar to the Normal University, making boarding house life unnecessary. However, for 80-90% of the students at the other three schools, around 3,000 people, this boarding house life is the only option. Besides this, some commute while staying in houses built by seniors from their hometowns, such as the Guizhou Hall (貴州會館) or Guangdong Hall (廣東會館).

University Student Attire Is

Regarding attire, unlike students in Seoul or Tokyo, they do not wear uniforms (制服). Especially after Chiang Kai-shek's New Life Movement, unadorned Chinese clothing (支那服) itself became the uniform. Among them, only students with surplus money might wear Western clothes, but generally, Chinese clothing (支那服) is preferred. All universities are co-educational (男女共學), have a strong liberal arts college atmosphere, and the rules are quite lenient.
Once classes are over, until the next morning, it's their world. In groups of threes and fives, some go to the library, some to the sports field, some invite friends for meals, some immediately return (곳 도라가는) to the boarding house, and some go to watch movies or plays. Their lives appear quite free and lively.

Popular Actors Are

The students' passion for theater is incomparable to that of Chosŏn students in Seoul. When a famous actor performs, the universities and boarding houses buzz with talk about them. The actors popular among students are:
Mei Lanfang (梅蘭芳) leading the list,
Shang Xiaoyun (尚小雲)
Gou Huisheng (苟慧生)
Cheng Yanqiu (程硯秋) – these four great actors (名優); besides them, Ma Lianliang (馬連良) and others are all popular.
Regarding films, as major Chinese film companies like Lianhua Mingxing (聯華明星) continuously produce (뒤이여 작고 제작함에) excellent purely Chinese films (純 支那映畵), and also with the influx of foreign films, especially American ones, the majority of viewers are male and female students. Currently, companies like Zhenguang (眞光), Pingan (平安), and Zhongyang (中央) do not handle Japanese films at all, and movie theaters are always full on Saturdays and Sundays. What is particularly noteworthy is the strong influence of American films reflected in the students.

Whose Literary Works

Next, among the widely read authors, Lu Xun (魯迅) goes without saying. Other popular writers include Mao Dun (矛盾), Ba Jin (巴金), Lao She (老舍), and Lin Yutang (林語堂). Female writers (閨秀作家) include Xie Yongxin (謝泳心), Xie Shuiying (謝水瑩), and Chen Yin (陳因). Sports are not as vigorous as in Chosŏn. Basketball is said to be strong, but only among specific players. Tennis, soccer, and rugby are also limited to a very small portion. However, in winter, the sound of skating echoes loudly in Zhongyang Gongyuan (中央公園) and Beihai Gongyuan (北海公園), drawing large crowds (人多). Calculating general student expenses: boarding house 6-7 won, meals around 10 won, coal (石炭) for the stove (暖爐) 5 won, laundry, haircut, bathing 5 won, tobacco (煙草) 3 won, tuition (學費) 10 won, clothing (被服) 12-13 won – altogether, one needs about 50 won.<174>

**Summary**

This 1939 article by O Ch'op'o describes the lives of university students in Beijing under the Japanese-sponsored "New Government." It notes five universities are open (National Normal, Beijing, Zhongguo, Yenching, Fu Jen) with over 5,000 students, mostly from wealthy backgrounds. Student life revolves around communal apartments ("kong'yu"/公寓), costing 6-7 won monthly, with meals adding another 8-13 won. Total monthly expenses are estimated at 50 won. Students wear simple Chinese clothing rather than uniforms, enjoy considerable freedom after classes, and are passionate consumers of Chinese opera (admiring stars like Mei Lanfang) and films, particularly American movies, while Japanese films are shunned. Popular authors include Lu Xun, Mao Dun, Ba Jin, Lao She, and Lin Yutang. Sports are less popular than in Korea, except for winter skating.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its comparison between student life in occupied Beijing and colonial Seoul (e.g., clothing, passion for theatre, sports). It provides a Korean viewpoint on Chinese society during the war, noting the affluence of students, their cultural preferences (favoring American films over Japanese), and the cost of living. The author's presence in Beijing and reporting for a Korean magazine under Japanese rule offers a glimpse into the interactions and observations of Koreans within the broader Japanese empire. Historians might find the details on daily life, expenses, cultural consumption patterns, and the implied political undertones (mention of the "New Government," shunning Japanese films) valuable for understanding the complexities of the period.

北京大學生生活, 大學이 五校가 開校
						北京 吳草坡
世界 中에 학교가 가장 만키로는 日本의 東京과 支那의 北京이란 말이 잇드시 北京에는 참으로 학교가 만타.
이번 事變이 나기 전에 大學만 12校 專門學校가 14校나 있었다. 新政府가 수립된 뒤에도 계속하여 開校하고 있는 곳이
國立師範大學,
同北京大學,
私立으로 中國大學
米國系로 燕京大學
獨逸系로 輔仁大學
의 다섯 학교로 이 다섯 학교의 학생 수가 5천명을 넘는다. 학생들은 모도 다 부유한 계급의 자제들이며 또한 인테리들로서 모다 지도적 지위에 서 있다.

學生들 生活은

학생생활을 한 마디로 말하면 公寓生活이라고 할 것으로 公寓와는 인연이 깊다. 公寓라는 것은 쉽게 말하면 아파-트와 같은 것으로 房과 방 사이는 板子로 가르막엇는데 방마다 卓子1 의자1 脚세면대1 이 세 가지가 비치되여 있어 일반 학생을 사용케 하고 있는데 房貰가 6, 7원 정도 식사도 한 달에 8원에서부터 12, 3원을 한다.
公寓는 大小 여러 가지가 있어 혹은 12, 3명 수용하는 데도 있고 좀더 큰 것은 3, 4백 명을 수용할 수 있는 것도<173> 있다. 학생 뿐 아니라 外來者들에게 임시로 사용시키는 수도 있다.
米國系인 燕京大學은 北京 西郊에 있는 西太后의 離宮 萬壽山으로 가는 자동차 도로의 우측에 向한 당당한 殿堂으로 師範大學과 同樣 훌륭한 기숙사의 설비도 있음으로 公寓생활의 필요도 없으나 그 他 3校의 8, 9 割의 학생 3천 人 내외는 이 公寓생활을 하는 수 밖에 없다. 이 밖에 貴州會館에 라거나 廣東會館이라 하여 제고향 선배들 지은 집에 留하면서 통학하는 수도 있다.

大學生들 服裝은

복장은 서울이나 東京 학생들 모양으로 制服을 입는 것이 아니고 蔣介石의 新生活운동 後는 特히 장식이 없는 支那服이 그대로 制服이 되었다. 그 중에도 돈이 餘裕있는 학생들만 或은 洋服도 입지만은 대개는 支那服 그대로 좋다. 대학은 모다 男女共學으로 自由學院의 색채가 강하고 규칙도 퍽이나 관대하다.
학생들은 수업만 지내면 이튼날 아침까지는 그네들의 세상으로 三三五五 도서관에 가는 이, 운동장에 가는 이, 食事로 동무 부르는 이 公寓에 곳 도라가는 이 영화 觀劇하러 가는 이 그네의 생활은 퍽으나 자유롭고 활달하게 보인다.

人氣 잇는 俳優들은

학생들이 연극을 좋아하는 熱은 서울 朝鮮學生의 比가 아니다. 有名한 배우가 출연하면 대학에서나 公寓에서나 그 이야기로 가득찬다. 학생간에 인기 있는 俳優는
梅蘭芳을 筆頭로
尙小雲
苟慧生
程硯秋의 4대 名優로 그 밖에도 馬連良 등은 다 人氣가 좋다.
영화에 대하여는 支那의 聯華明星 같은 큰 영화회사가 우수한 純 支那映畵를 뒤이여 작고 제작함에 따라 또한 외국 것 특히 亞米利加 것의 진출에 伴하여 관람자의 대다수는 남녀학생이며 현재 眞光平安中央같은 회사에서는 전연 日本映畵를 취급치 않고 있으며 土曜 日曜의 映畵館도 언제든지 滿員이다. 特筆할 것은 亞米利加 영화의 영향이 학생에게 강렬하게 反映되여 있는 바이다.

文藝作品은 누구 것

그 다음에 많이 읽히는 작가로는 魯迅은 더 말할 것도 없고 기타 大衆作家로 하여는 矛盾巴 金老舍 林語堂이요 閨秀作家에는 謝泳心 謝水瑩 陳因 등이다. 운동은 朝鮮같이 굳세지 못하다. 籠球가 강하다고 하지만 특정의 선수에 限하여 있으며 庭球 蹴球 럭비도 아조 일부에 限하였으며 다만 冬季는 스케-트 소리가 中央公園이나 北海公園에서 많이 열이여 人多를 끄은다. 일반학생들 경비를 따지면 公寓가 6, 7원 식사가 10원 내외 暖爐에 때이는 石炭代 5원 洗濯 理髮 沐浴代 5원 煙草代 3원 學費 10원 被服代 12, 3원 이럭저럭 합계 50원은 있어야 한다.<174>
