Secret
						Kim Kwangsŏp (金珖燮)

In this world, man too was a beautiful secret
In this world, woman was an even more beautiful secret

As this secret unraveled, word by word, dreams scattered between the two
When those dreams were wound back onto a single thread, love emerged there

Like petals surrounding the pollen in the center of a flower, words upon words
Bloomed from the center of the heart, making the lips redder still

Therefore, even if there was loneliness, sorrow, and even pain
Thinking itself was already happiness, and gladly, one knocked on that door, agonizing

A land dreamed of before the leaves of the whole world entered the moonlit night
Smiling, upon the bright youth, an eternal image revolved <252>

Thus, under the sun, moon, and stars, hearts fluttered endlessly
Until finally, a night came when the secret remained unsolved, only as a dream

Words hard to find again and dreams hard to have again shared the same space
Like a guest who came to a lonely lakeside, they loved too cruelly

The man had a wish and finally achieved his desire
The woman had regrets and finally gained her tears

That night, somewhere, a man who loved that woman bowed his head
That night, somewhere, a woman trying to forget that man despaired

The secret of trying harder to forget while loving without knowing why
The secret of loving more while trying to forget, unable to stop

In this world, both man and woman were a beautiful secret, but
In this world, both man and woman were a sad secret. <253>

**Summary**

The poem "Secret" by Kim Kwangsŏp explores the complex and often contradictory nature of love between men and women. It begins by framing both sexes as beautiful mysteries whose gradual unfolding leads to dreams and love, accompanied by heightened emotions and sensory experiences. However, this journey also involves loneliness, sorrow, and pain. The poem culminates in a sense of disillusionment and unresolved mystery, where love leads to separation, regret (tears for the woman), and despair, transforming the initial 'beautiful secret' into a 'sad secret'. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the lyrical introspection prevalent in Korean literature of the late 1930s. Amidst the constraints of colonial rule, writers often turned to universal themes like love, nature, and existential sorrow, sometimes as an indirect way to express deeper cultural or personal angst, focusing on the inner world rather than overt social commentary. Historians might find the poem's focus on personal emotion and the nuanced portrayal of male and female experiences indicative of the period's literary trends and perhaps subtle expressions of constrained desires or loss.

秘密
						金珖燮
이 세상에서 사나이도 한 아름다운 비밀이었고
이 세상에서 여자는 더 아름다운 비밀이었다

이 비밀이 한마듸식 풀여서 두 사이에 꿈이 흐터지고
그 꿈이 한 실마리로 도루 감기면 거기서 사랑이 났다

꽃같이 한가운데 花粉을 둘러싼 花辨같은 말과 말
가슴속 한가운데서 피여 나오기에 입술을 더 붉게 했다

그러기게 외로움이 있고 슲음이 있고 아픔까지 이슬시라도
생각함이 임이 幸福이엇고 즐겨서 그 門을 두다려 고민하였다

모든 世界의 입사귀가 달인 드러서기 전부터 夢想하는 나라
微笑하야 光明의 靑春 우에 永遠한 모습이 돌았다 <252>

이리하야 해와 달과 별 아래서 마음과 마음이 설레이든 끝
끝끝내 꿈만으로 그 秘密이 다 풀이지 않은 밤이 왔다

다시 있기 어려운 말과 다시 었기 어려운 꿈이 자리를 함끼 하야
외론 湖水ㅅ가에서 온 손님같이 너무도 殘忍하게 사랑하였다

사나이는 원함이 있어 드듸어 그 마음을 일우었고
여자는 았김이 있어 드듸여 그 눈물을 얻었다

그날 밤 어듸선가 그 여자를 사랑하는 한 사나이가 고개를 숙였고
그날 밤 어듸선가 그 사나이를 이즈랴는 한 여자가 절망을 하였다

알 수 없이 사랑하면서 더 이즈랴하였다는 秘密
할 수 없이 이즈랴하면서 더 사랑하였다는 秘密

이 세상에서 사나이도 여자도 한 아름다운 秘密이었으나
이 세상에서 사나이도 여자도 한 슲은 秘密이었다. <253>
