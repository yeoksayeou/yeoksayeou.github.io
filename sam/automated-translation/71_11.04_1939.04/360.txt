**Summary**

This document outlines the contents of a special feature in the April 1939 issue of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*, focusing on the "Korea Problem." It compiles essays from high-ranking Japanese officials (Governor-General Minami, Army Commander Nakamura), prominent Japanese intellectuals (Tokutomi Sohō, Kobayashi Ichizō, Hayashi Fusao, Kikuchi Kan), and a Korean writer known for collaboration (Chang Hyŏkchu). The titles suggest themes of integrating Korea into Japan's "New East Asia," emphasizing Japanese leadership, Korean gratitude and contribution (resources, art), appealing to Korean intellectuals, and reflecting on Japanese experiences in Korea, all framed within the context of Japan's wartime mobilization and colonial policy. For historians, this collection is significant as it showcases the coordinated effort by Japanese authorities and collaborating intellectuals (both Japanese and Korean) to shape public opinion and justify colonial rule in Korea during a critical period of imperial expansion, highlighting the specific propaganda narratives being disseminated.
