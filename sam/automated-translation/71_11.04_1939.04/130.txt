Love Stories of Famous Kisaeng in the Capital
						Paekcho Rang
A Love Rival Becomes Love's Guardian—The Pure Heart Swelling in a Short-Lived Woman?

Yŏnsuk and Okchŏng were companions in tears and also companions in laughter. They shared thirty hardships together and laughed together about future hopes... The two were like shadows, never knowing how to leave each other.
However, there was a big difference between Yŏnsuk's frail health and Okchŏng's robust health. Yŏnsuk was the type to inevitably fall ill if she stayed up even a little late at the restaurant (요리집). The cost of medicine given to the doctor was more than the hourly fee earned overnight, and the loss from not being able to work the next day was even greater. Okchŏng didn't know what best to do about Yŏnsuk's delicate constitution. Even when staying up all night together, Okchŏng always tried hard to send Yŏnsuk home first or even let her sleep using her lap as a pillow. But tonight, Yŏnsuk showed no intention of sleeping or leaving; even though it was already 4 AM, she just sat there blankly, shivering.
"Yŏnsuk, it would be best if you just went home and slept."
Okchŏng spoke loudly, as if intending for the customer to hear as well.
『I don't want to! Why are you always trying to chase me away?』
Unexpectedly, Yŏnsuk gave this reply. Yŏnsuk, who usually heard the words "go and sleep" with utmost gratitude, today took them almost resentfully; there could be no greater surprise. However, Okchŏng was not one to misunderstand the meaning behind Yŏnsuk's response.
"Don't worry. I will look after the Young Master (도련님) faithfully!"
"No, I don't want to. This time, I can't trust even you."
Yŏnsuk's lips trembled slightly.
"Hmph, that's all just nerves."
"For me, this is a dead end."
She bit her red lips with her white front teeth and sighed softly.
It seemed Yŏnsuk was finally in love with the young nobleman called "Young Master." Thus, knowing her own fading health, she appeared to have made a solemn decision to rely on this lover as her last, for her entire life.
"By the way, what does this gentleman do?"
Okchŏng, resigned, pressed the "Young Master," who had gone across to a friend's table in the next room, to encourage Yŏnsuk to leave early.<117>
"I'll go check."
Yŏnsuk stood up hastily and went out. It was late at night, and there were only two groups of customers left. As Yŏnsuk left, gossip started among those seated.
"This time she's being quite aggressive."
"Not aggressive, desperate."
"He doesn't even know the Young Master's situation, sigh!"
Hearing this, Okchŏng shouted sharply.
"Why wouldn't he know the Young Master's situation? Just this morning, a long letter came to Yŏnsuk."
"That letter... if you knew the story, it's astounding."
As one guest abruptly said this, the two sitting next to him burst into loud laughter. Okchŏng couldn't fail to notice this. Clearly, there was some other story behind that letter. Okchŏng recalled the contents of the Young Master's letter she had read at Yŏnsuk's house.
「You are a potato fallen on the streets of Chongno.
A soil-covered potato grown by a farmer's hand in the countryside.
A lonely one fallen while being transported to market.
People walking by will kick it with their feet.
No, they will trample it.
But I will pick up that potato with my hand.
Brush off the dirt
And find its color
Thus, I will make it my treasure」
A "potato" is destined to be born from the soil and go to a home. Just like a country girl getting married... But when that potato falls onto the street, its fate of being kicked and trampled is pitiful, akin to a kisaeng or a neglected wife. However, the potato representing Yŏnsuk's fate would be held in the Young Master's hand and come to enjoy a new world... Seeing this letter, Yŏnsuk cried out of excessive joy. Okchŏng, who laughed and cried with her, naturally cried too. And so, one way or another, today, here, the Young Master and the three companions revolving around him ended up spending the night together.
To write this one letter, the Young Master had spent three days, discarding hundreds of sheets of paper. He tried writing this way and that, experimenting with various styles and myriad contents, before finally deciding to utilize the "philosophy of the potato."<118>
Once Okchŏng understood the Young Master's pure heart, she felt even more confident and willing to entrust Yŏnsuk's future to him.
"Yes, with this, Yŏnsuk's remaining half-life is secure."
She inwardly considered it fortunate. Yŏnsuk, who had gone to the next room, returned after a while, supporting the heavily intoxicated Young Master. Whether he was truly drunk or pretending, the Young Master used Okchŏng's lap as a pillow and immediately fell fast asleep. Okchŏng, lending her lap, looked apologetically at Yŏnsuk.
The Young Master was the youngest son of a certain nobleman. He wasn't called Young Master just because he was born into an aristocratic family; although he was a young man of twenty-eight, he was not yet married, and moreover, everything he did was rather childish, hence the nickname. Yŏnsuk, too, was a kisaeng with a disposition just as fastidious as the Young Master's, but upon meeting him, her high-towering pride fell to the ground. Crushed, she became unable to control herself in front of the Young Master, turning into a shy, self-conscious slave of her heart. The Young Master didn't know money. Rather than not knowing money, he couldn't carry it around. He merely had the freedom to run tabs at department stores or restaurants; these debts were usually paid secretly by his mother behind his father's back.
Since the Young Master became engrossed with Yŏnsuk, his visits to restaurants grew increasingly frequent. If he had met Yŏnsuk right after returning from Tōkyō, some sort of conclusion would surely have been reached long ago, but he returned in the fall and only met Yŏnsuk in the winter.
Therefore, Yŏnsuk missed the glory of being the first page in the history of the Young Master's amorous adventures in the pleasure quarters, but regrettably, she was undoubtedly the second entry. Then who was number one?
Before there was time to wonder, the door burst open as if struck by lightning, and Ch'osŏn stormed in.
"Ah— Do you think this restaurant is an inn?"
Her first words were confrontational. Yŏnsuk didn't flinch, but the Young Master, who had been lying down senseless, sat bolt upright almost like a mechanical doll.
"Hey, is this world only for you to live in?"
Okchŏng protested on behalf of the group.
"Hmph, how can I live alone with bitches like you stealing other people's men..."
This was clearly a criticism of Yŏnsuk.<119> A challenge.
"Hmph, what's there to steal? As if he's a child's piece of rice cake... The customer is free to like this kisaeng or that kisaeng."
Okchŏng had no intention of yielding till the end.
"Young Master... Please come with me today."
Ch'osŏn grabbed the Young Master's arm and pulled.
"Ah— Why are you doing this? Huh? You shouldn't do this."
A friend sitting nearby tried hard to release his arm, but Ch'osŏn's grip seemed fixed, hardened onto the Young Master's wrist.
"Ah— Please go now before you are embarrassed."
"Go where?"
"Since you promised to love me forever, shouldn't you be with me until death?
"What utter nonsense are you talking? When did anyone promise to live together forever?"
The friend sitting beside him interjected.
"Hmph, third parties should just drink their alcohol. Matters between people should be resolved by the parties themselves..."
The Young Master remained silent. But it didn't seem like he couldn't speak because he was drunk. Yŏnsuk could bear it no longer. Almost like a mad person, as if chased by a demon, she rushed out and returned home.

The beautiful kisaeng surrounding the Young Master—he was a rare case of pure affection in the demimonde, a young man with money, from a family with no one formidable to fear, and moreover, handsome and not firm in affection.
Therefore, the Young Master's appearance was undoubtedly a major sensation among the kisaeng. One after another, resourceful kisaeng emerged, each wanting to monopolize the Young Master's pure heart and enjoy a drive down the happy road of marriage.
To list their names explicitly here would be inappropriate for propriety's sake. Even if the Young Master's list of lovers is suppressed, the relationships with Ch'osŏn, Yŏnsuk, and Okchŏng must inevitably be told. Okchŏng, visiting the bedridden Yŏnsuk, peeled a pear, placed it in her hand, and sighed.
Ok: Well, what can be done...
Yŏn: I'm sorry.
Ok: I thought the Young Master would surely be yours...<120>
Yŏn: What did Ch'osŏn say?
Ok: What's the point of hearing that?
Yŏn: If the Young Master keeps acting like this, he'll fall soon too.
Ok: Ch'osŏn, Wŏrhŭi, Yŏngok, Kŏmnan, all of them are throwing themselves at him. From the Young Master's side, it seems he probably doesn't care either way.
Yŏn: But they said they'd live together.
Ok: She clung to him while he was drunk, desperately pleading, so he just said whatever... whatever...
Yŏn: Then, was the letter sent to me also just like that?
Ok: No. They said he struggled immensely while writing the letter, getting stuck often.
Yŏn: Even so, how can my heart be at ease when they make such a fuss?
Ok: We just need to hurry things along immediately so he stops frequenting the restaurants you know.
During this, the Young Master came to visit. Yŏnsuk didn't know what to do.
It's strange that such a naive person was among the kisaeng... Okchŏng marveled inwardly. That day, the Young Master explained the matter concerning Ch'osŏn to Yŏnsuk. Of course, Yŏnsuk understood it too. Okchŏng, listening beside them, was almost entranced by the Young Master's silk-like heart.
Whatever happens, please don't abandon Yŏnsuk. The day you abandon her will be the day she dies. She's a lonely child raised by her maternal grandmother, not knowing the faces of her mother or father. Though called a kisaeng, she still has the air of a schoolgirl, and although not ill, her body is frail, so she needs to quit this profession quickly.
Okchŏng pleaded with the Young Master, her voice choked with tears. Yŏnsuk sniffled, shedding tears, and somewhere along the line, the Young Master's eyes also turned red. Okchŏng tactfully excused herself. Eventually, only the two of them remained.
That day, Yŏnsuk was led by the Young Master to a restaurant. Okchŏng hadn't arrived yet, but somehow, Ch'osŏn, along with Wŏrhŭi, Yŏngok, and Kŏmnan, had gathered and entered the room as if summoned. The atmosphere in the room became extremely hostile.
Since we weren't called, we certainly won't accept an hourly fee, so don't worry.
Kyŏngsŏn, the last to enter, glared at Yŏnsuk with venomous eyes and sat down next to the Young Master. The seat next to the Young Master, which everyone had hitherto refrained from taking,<121> was readily occupied by the final intruder as if it had been saved just for her. Indeed, the atmosphere in the room became such that if anyone touched it, an uproar would surely ensue. The assembled faces were all top-class figures who held sway in Seoul, so the situation was tense enough to feel murderous. The Young Master, completely intimidated, just smoked cigarettes. Yŏnsuk wished she could run away immediately with the Young Master to some island. The faces of the kisaeng sitting around her seemed like demons attacking with poison to shatter her good fortune. However, Yŏnsuk, who hadn't been a kisaeng for long and lacked both eloquence and smooth talk, had absolutely no ability to lead the atmosphere of this room. She wished Okchŏng would arrive quickly at such a time. Because Yŏnsuk had emerged as a so-called intellectual kisaeng, and due to her gentle nature and frail disposition, she often acted aloof even towards her companions, she had been called
A cold bitch
An arrogant bitch
An unworthy bitch.
The resentment from that had burst forth like a flood today.
What's the point of falling for dissolute men who frequent restaurants... foolishly. Do you think they have pure hearts? Even if you believe your own feelings are pure, in reality, it's nothing more than affection stained with "money" and "vanity."
This was Yŏnsuk's attitude when criticizing her companions' love affairs. That's why the kisaeng gathered here today had all come thinking, "Let's see how that Yŏnsuk bitch's love affair turns out." At the same time, they came with the grim determination, "We must snatch away Yŏnsuk bitch's love and trample it." Of course, if the "Young Master," not being a child day and night, was truly sincere in his love for Yŏnsuk, what could a thousand armies do? But the Young Master himself had little experience in the demimonde and was a nobleman not particularly steadfast in love. Yŏnsuk's heart felt like it was on fire, but the Young Master pretended not to notice, accepting and smoking a cigarette offered by Wŏrhŭi. Just when it seemed rain or wind might break out, Okchŏng entered. As she entered, she grabbed the wrists of the Young Master and Yŏnsuk and pulled them outside. After seating the two in a separate room, Okchŏng returned to the room where her companions were.
Hey Okchŏng, how much commission are you getting for playing matchmaker?<122>
Kŏmnan was furious. Okchŏng readily sat down next to Kŏmnan and opened her mouth.
"Hey everyone, think about it. No matter how deceptive the world of kisaeng is, why should we trample on someone's innocent love? I know you're doing this because Yŏnsuk is arrogant, difficult, and pretends to be somebody, making her hateful and annoying. But Yŏnsuk's true heart is by no means that of an arrogant and insolent girl. I believe she is a girl as fine as silk. It's just that she doesn't know the path of a kisaeng... I know she has merely deviated from your ways. That child has no parents, her body is weak, she has no talent for being a kisaeng, and moreover, this is undoubtedly her first love. Now that a good match like the Young Master has appeared, let's present Yŏnsuk, saying 'we kisaeng also have such a bride candidate.' What about you all? Is it so difficult and painful for you to give and receive love, whether it's from wealthy customers or handsome ones? Therefore, I will bring Yŏnsuk here and have her apologize before you all. Please forgive her readily and bless the future of our pitiful companion."
Okchŏng's heartfelt plea, mixed with her blood, immediately resonated among the chivalrous kisaeng. And so, in the end, Yŏnsuk, solely focused on protecting her love, performed a deep bow of apology before her elder sisters, without even knowing what crime she had committed.

After that—indeed, Yŏnsuk became exclusively the Young Master's and moved into a newly built culture house (문화주택) at the top of Kyedong. Occasionally, when the Young Master was dragged out to a restaurant by a friend, the kisaeng who had attended that love truce meeting would, upon seeing him, immediately say,
Please go home now. Isn't Yŏnsuk waiting?
Their words made them seem just like guardians of love. <123>

**Summary**

This fictional story from 1939, titled "Love Stories of Famous Kisaeng in the Capital," narrates the romantic entanglements surrounding Yŏnsuk, a frail and sensitive *kisaeng*, and a naive young nobleman known as "Toryŏnnim." Deeply in love and seeing him as her last hope due to her poor health, Yŏnsuk faces intense jealousy and competition from other prominent *kisaeng*, particularly Ch'osŏn. Yŏnsuk's loyal friend, Okchŏng, acts as her staunch defender, eventually mediating a peace pact among the rivals through a heartfelt appeal to their camaraderie. The story concludes with Yŏnsuk living with the Toryŏnnim in a modern "culture house," her former rivals now acting as protective "guardians" of their relationship, urging the Toryŏnnim to return to her whenever they see him out.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a glimpse into the social world of elite *kisaeng* in colonial Seoul (referred to as Changan). It portrays their complex relationships, involving friendship, fierce rivalry for patrons, perceptions of love versus economic necessity, and a code of conduct amongst themselves. The characterization of the naive nobleman and the mention of a "culture house" (문화주택) reflect aspects of class dynamics and creeping modernization during the period. The story, while fictional, provides insight into contemporary ideals, anxieties, and social commentary regarding *kisaeng*, love, and social mobility in 1930s Korea.

長安名妓情話
						白鳥郞
戀敵이, 사랑의 守備兵되어-한 떨기 薄命女性의 붓는 純情은?

연숙이와 옥정이는 눈물동모인 동시에 우슴동 모도 되였다. 서른 사정을 같이하고 압날의 희망을 같이 웃고... 두 사람은 그림자같이 서로 떠날 줄을 몰났다.
그러나 연숙의 병약한 점과 옥정의 건강한 점에는 큰 틀님이 있었다. 요리집에서 밤 좀 새면 긔어히 병이 나고 마는 연숙이었다. 밤새 버른 시간비보다는 의사에게 주는 약갑이 더 많었으며 그 이튼날 벌지 못하는 손해가 더 많었다. 연숙의 섬약한 체질에는 옥정이도 었재야 조홀지를 몰났다. 같이 밤을 새다가도 옥정이는 반다시 연숙이를 몬저 보내거나 무릅을 베어서라도 잠을 재우랴고 애를 써왔다. 그런데 오늘밤만은 연숙이가 자랴고도 하지 않코 갈 생각도 하지 않고 발서 새벽 4시가 되얐것만 오드만히 앉어서 발발 떨고 있는 것이다.
「연숙아 너는 고만 가 잣으면 좃켓다.」
옥정이가 손님까지 드러보라는다시 크게 떠드렀다.
『실타 얘! 왜 사람을 좇이랴고만 그러니.』
의외에 연숙이는 이같은 대답을 하는 것이다. 전 같으면 가서 자라는 수리를 지극히 고맙게 듣든 연숙이가 오늘은 숫제 야속히 역이니 이런 의외의 일은 없다. 그러나 옥정이야 연숙의 이 대답의 의취를 모를 니는 없다.
「글세 걱정마러. 도련님은 내가 신성하게 모실게!」
「이애 실타. 이번만은 너도 못 믿겠다.」
연숙의 입살은 가볍게 떨닌다.
「흥 그게 다 신경과민야.」
「나에게는 이게 막다른 골목이니가.」
하얀 압니로 불근 입살을 물고 가늘게 한숨을 쉰다.
연숙이는 필경 『도련님』이라 부르는 귀공자와 사랑을 하나니 보다. 그래서 병에 시드는 자긔의 신상을 아는이만치 이번 애인만은 최후의 애인으로 일생을 의지하리라는 비장한 결심이 있는 모양이다.
「대관절 이 냥반은 무었을 하실가」
옥정이는 하는 수 없이 도련님을<117> 안동을 해서라도 연숙이를 일즉 보내랴고 았가붙어 건너방 친고 좌석에 다니러 간 『도련님』을 채근했다.
「내가 좀 가 볼테야.」
연숙이는 부리났케 이러나 나갔다. 밤이 늦어서 손님이라고는 단 두 패받게는 없지 않었다. 연숙이가 나가자 일좌에서는 숙덕공론이 났다.
「이번에는 아조 적극적인데.」
「적극적이 안니라 결사적일세.」
「도련님 사정은 아도 못하고 흐!」
이 소리를 드른 옥정이는 소리를 빡 질넜다.
「왜 도련님 사정을 몰나요. 오늘 아츰에도 연숙에게 만리장서가 왔는데」
「그 편지는... 알고 보면 기맥키지」
한 손님이 불숙 이 같은 소리를 하니가 옆에 앉었든 두 사람도 같이 박장대소를 한다. 옥정이가 이 눈치를 채지 못할 니가 없다. 필경 그 편지에는 다른 곡절이 있는게 분명했다. 옥정이는 지금 연숙의 집에서 읽어 본 도련님의 편지 사연을 회상해 보았다.
「당신은 종로 길거리에 떠러진 감자요.
시골서 농군의 손에 자다난 흙 무든 감자요.
장으로 실녀가다가 떠러진 외톨이요.
길 걷는 사람은 발길노 차고 가리다.
안이 짓밟고 가겠지요.
그러나 나는 그 감자를 손으로 집겠소.
흙을 털고
빗갈을 찾겠소
그리해서 나의 보배를 삼겠소
「감자」는 흙에서 나서 가정으로 가는 게 작정된 팔자이다. 맟이 시골 처자가 싀집을 가듯이... 그러나 그 감자가 거리에 떠러지는 때에는 발길에 채고 짓밟히는 운명이 긔구하기 기생이나 추없부에 갔갑다. 그러나 연숙의 운명을 대포하는 감자만은 도련님의 손견에 당겨서 새로운 세상을 즐기게 될 것이 않인가... 이 편지를 보고 연숙이는 너머 깃버 우렀다. 웃고 울기를 같이하는 옥정이도 물론 같이 우렀다. 그래서 이럭저럭 오늘 이 자리에서 도련님과 믿 도련님을 싸고도는 일행 세 사람이 함께 어울녀서 밤을 새게 된 것이다.
이 편지 한 장 쓰기에 도련님은 사흘 동안을 수백 장의 편지지를 버린 것이다. 이리 썼다 저리 썼다 가지가지 형식과 별별 사연을 다- 써 보다가 결국은 「감자의 철학」을 리용하기에 니른 것이다.<118>
옥정이가 도련님의 순정을 알고 나서는 더 한층 연숙의 장래를 믿고 맷기고 싶었다.
「오냐 이만하면 연숙이의 남은 반생은 안심이다.」
혼자 속으로 다행히 녁였다. 엽방으로 갔든 연숙이가 한참만에 술이 몹시 취한 도련님을 붓들고 도라왔다. 도련님은 취했는지 취한 체를 하는지 옥정의 무릅을 베고는 그대로 고라 떠러지고 마렀다. 무롭을 빌닌 옥정이는 연숙에게 미안한 얼굴을 했다.
도련님은 어는 귀족의 망내 아들이었다. 귀족의 집에 태어나서 도련님이라 부르는 게 안이라 이십 팔 세가 된 장년이었만 아즉 장가도 들지 않고 그 우에 하는 것이 모도가 앳데서 지은 별명이었다. 연숙이도 이 도련님에게 지지 안는 성미 깔금한 기생이었으나 도련님을 맞나며 그의 고소대 같은 자부심은 땅에 떠러진다. 스러지고 그저 도련님 앞이라면 몸을 간으지 못하는 수집고 몸조심하는 마음의 노예가 돼지고만 것이다. 도련님은 돈을 몰났다. 돈을 모른다기보다 돈을 갖이고 다니지를 못했다. 다만 백화점이나 요리점에 외상거래가 자유로 될 뿐이요 그 외상은 대개 도련님의 어머니가 아버지 몰내 뒤길노 값아주는 처지었다.
도련님이 연숙에게 열중되며붙어 요리집에 드나드는 수효는 점점 심해젔다. 동경서도 나오는 길노 연숙이를 맛났드면 그동안 무슨 끗장이든지 넉넉히 났슬 것을 그는 가을에 도라와 가지고 겨을에야 연숙이를 맛난 것이다.
그럼으로 연숙이는 도련님의 화류정화의 력사의 첫 페이지에 오를 광영을 놋치고 유감이지만 제이호적 존재임에 틀림없었다. 그럼 그 제일호란 누고인가?
궁금할 새도 없이 방문이 벼락같이 열니드니 「초선」이가 드러 닥첬다.
「아- 이건 요리집을 려관으로 아나」
첬 마디가 시비조였다. 연숙이는 꼼작도 하지 안었스나 정신 없이 누었든 도련님은 거의 긔계인형 모양으로 벌컥 이러났다.
「이애 이건 너 혼자 사는 세상이냐」
옥경이가 일좌를 대표해서 항의를 했다.
「흥 나 혼자 살재니 살 수가 있늬 남의 서방을 뺏는 년들 등살에...
이것은 분명히 연숙에 대한 비난이다.<119> 도전이다.
「흥 뺏을 게 어듸 있어 어린애 갖은 떡이든가바... 손님 임의로 이 기생 좇아 저 기생도 좃타지.」
옥경이는 끗까지 굴할 생각은 없다.
「도련님... 오늘은 나고 좀 가십시다.」
초선이는 도련님의 팔을 잡아 끈다.
「아- 너 왜 이러니 응 이래선 못써」
옆에 앉었든 겻애가 팔을 풀냐고 애를 썼으나 초선의 손아귀는 도련님의 팔목에 부튼 채 굿어버린가 싶었다.
「아- 챙피하시기 전에 어서 가서요.」
「가기는 어딀 가」
「평생을 사랑하신 댓스니가 죽기 전에는 나와 같이 있어야 할께 안이겠소.
「별 당토 않은 소리를 다 하는구나. 언제 누가 평생 살자 약속야.
