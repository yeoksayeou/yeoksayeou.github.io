Editor's Postscript

As the domestic and international situation grows increasingly tense, and the storm clouds (風雲) over the Pacific gradually become more perilous, we, the 24 million people of Korea (조선), have reached a time for establishing a decisive war footing (決戰體制), where we must break through this unprecedented national crisis (國難) by offering our blood and serving the nation with righteous courage (義勇奉公). At such a time, it is truly a matter for celebration (慶賀) that 250,000 volunteer soldiers have emerged from the youth of the Peninsula (반도). We are thus publishing a special feature with writings by various individuals (諸氏) to send off (壯行) these volunteer soldiers.

Economic prosecutor (경제검사) Kang Chungin's (姜仲仁) "Recent Economic Crimes (經濟事犯)" and the "Reminiscences of Military Academy Days and Tales of Going to War (出征譚)" by Colonel (大佐) Pak Tuyŏng (朴斗榮) and other officers from the Peninsula are very necessary reading materials (讀物), and we also believe the "Roundtable Discussion with Lead Actresses from Masterpiece Films" is good reading (好讀物).

We will now publish the second installment of the On-Paper Cultural Lecture Series, which was scheduled for the previous month's issue but could not be published due to space constraints (지면관계). This time, we have mobilized the faculty of Yŏnhŭi College (延禧전문학교) to present new fields (文獻) such as economics, science, law, religion, and physical education, which were not seen in the first lecture series, and we are confident this will be a great help (도옴이라) to our cultural sphere.

Also in the Literature and Arts Section (文藝欄), we are publishing in this month's issue the special feature on overseas war literature, which could not be published previously due to space constraints. We believe the translations by Chu Yohan (朱요한) and others (諸家) of war novels and war poems from various nations such as Germany (獨逸), Italy (伊太利), France (佛蘭西), and the United Kingdom (英國), as well as "This Year's Harvest (收獲) in the Literary World," will also be good reading material (讀物).<155>

**Summary**

This editor's postscript from the December 1941 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine highlights the escalating wartime atmosphere, celebrating the 250,000 Korean youths who volunteered for the Japanese military effort as the Pacific situation intensified. It promotes articles related to the war, including memoirs of Korean officers in the Japanese army and features on war literature from Axis and Allied nations. It also mentions articles on economic crime, a roundtable with film actresses, and the continuation of a cultural lecture series featuring Yonhui College professors. For historians, this piece is revealing of the propaganda efforts within colonial Korea aimed at mobilizing the population for Japan's war, framing participation as patriotic "義勇奉公" (righteous public service). The specific mention of 250,000 volunteers and the selection of content (war literature, officer memoirs) clearly reflect the pressures and priorities of the late colonial period on the brink of the Pacific War.

편집후기
內外情勢는 더욱 긴박해가며, 태평양의 風雲이 점차 험악해 가는 이 때에, 我等 2천 4백만 조선민중은 피를 바치여 나라에 義勇奉公하므로써 未曾有의 국난을 돌파하여 할 決戰體制 확립의 시기에 이르렀다. 이러한 때에 반도 청년층에서 25만이라는 지원병을 내게 되였다는 것은 실로 慶賀할 일로, 이 지원병 壯行을 위한 諸氏의 글을 特輯하는 바이다.

경제검사 姜仲仁氏의 「최근의 經濟事犯」과, 朴斗榮大佐 이하 반도출신의 諸 장교의 「사관학교시대 회상과 出征譚」은 매우 필요한 讀物이며 「명작영화 주연여배우 좌담회」도 好讀物이라 생각한다.

前月號에 게재키로 하다가 지면관계로 발표치 못한 제 二回 誌上文化講座를 발표하기로 한다. 今回는 延禧전문학교 교수진을 동원시켜 경제, 과학, 법률, 종교, 체육 등, 제 일회 강좌에서 보지 못하던 새로운 文獻으로 我문화계에 큰 도옴이라 자신한다.

文藝欄에 있어서도 역시 지면관계로 발표치 못했던 해외 戰爭文學 특집을 今月號에 발표하는 바, 朱요한氏외 諸家의 筆墨으로 번역된 獨逸, 伊太利, 佛蘭西, 英國 등, 諸國의 전쟁소설과 전쟁시를 비롯하여 「금년도 문단의 收獲」도 좋은 讀物이라 생각한다.＜155＞
