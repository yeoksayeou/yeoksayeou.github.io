Patriotic Anecdotes, Selected by the Editorial Board

From a Prestigious Noble Family, Volunteer Applicants Emerge, Deeply Moving Home Front Korea

Kim Tae-yŏng, grandson of Baron Kim Sŏk-ki (金奭基) of 31 Suhajŏng (水下町), Kyŏngsŏng, along with his brother-in-law (husband of his paternal aunt) Yi Uk (李煜) (25), visited the Honmachi Police Station (本町署) on the 11th and deeply moved the officials, saying, "The deadline was yesterday, but if possible, we also wish to participate as honored volunteer soldiers, so please permit us to volunteer." Kim Tae-yŏng and Yi Uk, having received education beyond middle school, reportedly sought out various comrades (등모) in their village as the volunteer recruitment deadline approached, urging them to volunteer together, resulting in about ten of their peers also applying to volunteer. When his grandfather, Baron Kim, was visited, he spoke as follows:
"To me, he is my only grandson, and he is my only son-in-law, but to the nation, they are young men more important than my love for them. Since they themselves said they would shoulder the honor of peninsular youth and volunteer, I considered it a great glory for our family (가문) and readily approved."

Mining Technical School Student Volunteers

The spirit (의긔) of peninsular youth boils toward the military gates, toward the military gates, and now another volunteer soldier has emerged from the Mining Technical School. Around 9 PM on the 10th, the deadline day for volunteers, a young man appeared at the Tongdaemun Police Station (東大門署) with an excited tone of voice, submitted his volunteer application, and left. That young man is Sinjŏng In-gyu (新井仁圭), currently a third-year student in the Mining Department (採鑛科) at Kyŏngsŏng Mining Technical School (京城鑛專). He finished Ch'ŏngju (淸州) Middle School in Shōwa 13 [1938] and is currently in his graduating year. Although he had wished to become a volunteer soldier from early on, he had been unable to achieve his goal until now because his family in his hometown of Yangch'on-ri (兩村里), Taesan-myŏn (代山面), Haman-gun (咸安郡), South Kyŏngsang Province (慶南), did not give permission. However, unable to endure the currently heightened volunteer soldier fever (지원병열) among the educated class (유식층), he finally took advantage of last Sunday, the 9th, to go down to his hometown and actively persuade his strict father, Mr. Pyŏng-ŭi (炳*), explaining the purpose (취지) of volunteering, and finally obtained ready consent (쾌락). Thus, he rushed straight from Kyŏngsŏng Station to the police station, deeply moving the concerned officials (관게자).

Donating Two Won Monthly

Mr. Sinbon Hŭng-nae (新本興來) of 84 Sinp'ungjŏng (新豊町), Suwŏn-ŭp (水原邑), an elderly man of 61 this year, resolutely quit smoking immediately since the outbreak of the Incident (事變 勃發) and has continued to donate 2 Won monthly as a national defense donation (국방 헌금) to this day. On the 15th, he again entrusted (기탁하여) 2 Won to the local police station, deeply moving the concerned authorities (관계당국). Also, the women's joint work groups (부인 공동작업반) from P'unggari (風歌里), Tokchiri (禿旨里), and Chihwari (芝花里) in Songsan-myŏn (松山面), Suwŏn-gun (水原郡), reportedly each entrusted 3 Won as national defense donations.
-End- <96>

**Summary**

This article compiles three anecdotes presented as examples of Korean patriotism during the Japanese colonial period in late 1941. The first story highlights a nobleman's grandson and son-in-law volunteering for the Japanese army, framed as a family honor. The second details a technical school student overcoming family opposition to volunteer, spurred by the "volunteer soldier fever" among the educated. The third praises an elderly man for quitting smoking to donate monthly to the war effort since the start of the "Incident" (Sino-Japanese War), alongside donations from women's work groups. For historians, the article is a clear example of wartime propaganda promoting Korean assimilation and support for the Japanese war effort through military enlistment and financial contributions. It reveals the pressure, particularly on educated youth and elite families, to demonstrate loyalty, and showcases the types of actions lauded as "patriotic" by the colonial regime and collaborating media.

愛國美談, 編輯局選
貴族의 名門에서도 志願兵을 出願한 銃後朝鮮感激諾
京城 수하정(水下町) 31번지 남작 김석기(金奭基)씨의 손자 김대영(金大泳)군은 그 고모부 이욱(李煜)(25)군과 함께 11일 본정서를 차저와 『마감 기일은 어제였으나 될 수 있으면 우리들도 영예의 지원병에 참가하고저 하오니 지원병을 허락하여 주시요』라고 말하여 게관을 감격시켰다. 김대영군과 이욱군은 중등 이상의 교육을 바든 터로 지원병 모집 기일이 박두하자 손수 마을 안에 있는 여러 등모를 차저다니면서 가치 지원병이 되기를 권유하여 그들 동료 가운데서도 십여 명이나 지원병을 출원하였다한다. 그의 조부 김남작을 찾으니 다음과 가치 말한다.
나에게는 둘도 없는 손자요 또 둘도 없는 사위나 나라에 있어서는 내가 사랑하는 이상으로 더 중요한 청년들이다. 저이들이 스스로 반도청년의 명예를 질머지고 지원을 하겠다 하므로 그것은 우리집 가문의 크나큰 영광으로 생각하고 쾌히 승낙한 터이다.

鑛專學生의 志願兵
군문으로 군문으로 반도청년의 의긔는 끌어 이제 또 광업전문에서도 지원병이 나섯다. 지원병 마금날 10일 오후 9시경에 한 청년이 흥분된 어조로 동대문서에 나타나 지원병 지원서를 접수시키고 갓다. 그 청년은 현재 경성광전(鑛專) 채광과(採鑛科)3년 생 신정인규(新井仁圭)군으로 청주(淸州) 중학을 昭和13년에 마치고 현재 졸업반에 있는데 진즉부터 지원병이 되고저 하였으나 향리인 경남 함안군 대산면 양촌리(咸安郡 代山面 兩村里)의 가정에서 허락을 하지 안흐므로 여즉것 뜻을 이루지 못하고 있었다. 그런데 바야흐로 고조된 유식층의 지원병열에 도저히 참지 못하여 드듸어 지난 9일 일요일을 틈타서 고향으로 나려가 엄부 병의(炳*)씨에게 지원병의 취지를 적극적으로 설파시켜 드듸여 쾌락을 어덧스므로 경성역에서 이처럼 곳 경찰서로 달려온 것으로 관게자들을 감격케 하였다.

每月二圓式 獻納
水原邑 新豊町 84番地 新本興來씨는 금년 61歲의 노인인데 事變 勃發 이래 즉시 금연을 단행하여 매월 2원式 국방 헌금을 금일까지 계속하여 왔는데 지난 15日에도 金2圓을 當地 경찰서에 기탁하여 관계당국을 감격케 하였다. 그리고 水原 郡松山面 風歌里 禿旨里 芝花里 부인 공동작업반에서도 각각 3圓式을 국방헌금으로 기탁하였다한다.
