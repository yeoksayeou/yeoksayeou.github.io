Magazine Title: 삼천리 제13권 제12호
Publication Date: 1941년 12월 01일
Article Title: 今年 一年間의 我文壇의 收獲 (This Year's Harvest in Our Literary World)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

This Year's Harvest in Our Literary World

1. Among the works you read this past year, which ones did you think were good?
2. What works did you yourself write this past year?

Yi Kwangsu (李光洙)
1. The full-length novel *Kyŏngsu Hanŭn Saramdŭl* (耕す人人, People Who Plow) (by Hong Chongu 洪鍾羽).
2. *Tongp'o ege Kisŭ* (同胞に寄す, To My Compatriots), *Naesŏn Ilch'e Suisangnok* (內鮮一體隨想錄, Thoughts on the Unity of Japan and Korea), the novel *Pom ui Norae* (봄의 노래, Song of Spring).

Kim Tongin (金東仁)
1. I have read nothing.
2. The only thing I have left behind this year is shame (恥辱).

Chu Yohan (朱耀翰)
1. Among short stories, the work that comes readily to mind now is Ch'ae Mansik's (蔡萬植氏) *Chip* (집, House).
Among poems, the works of Yi Yongak (李庸岳), O Changhwan (吳章煥), Sŏ Chŏngju (徐廷柱), Kim Kwanggyun (金光均氏), etc.
2. *Ch'ŏt P'i* (첫피, First Blood) and 2 or 3 similar pieces <120> *P'iyang Sŏng* (피양성, P'yŏngyang Fortress), and other folk song style pieces.

Kim Ansŏ (金岸曙)
1. Works I have read:
*Pando Sanha* (半島山河, Mountains and Rivers of the Peninsula), *Samŭidang Sijip* (三宜堂 詩集, Poetry Collection of Samŭidang).
2. My own works:
Poetry collection *Ansŏ Sicho* (岸曙詩抄, Poetry Selection of Ansŏ)
Collection of essays *Sisim* (詩心, Poetic Mind).

Pak Chonghwa (朴鍾和)
1. Chiyong's (芝溶) *Paengnoktam* (白鹿潭, White Deer Lake).
Sanghŏ's (尙虛) *Musŏrok* (無序錄, Record Without Preface).
2. *Chŏnya* (前夜, The Eve), *Tajŏng Pulsim* (多情佛心, Affectionate Buddhist Heart), *Ch'ŏngt'aejip* (靑苔集, Blue Moss Collection).

Pak Yŏnghŭi (朴英熙)
1. I think there has been no year I was unable to read as much as this year, being busy with miscellaneous tasks (雜務). While rummaging through magazines from time to time, I did read 5 or 6 works, but I cannot readily recall their titles now.
2. I merely published 5 or 6 essays (論文) in the *Maeil Sinbo* (每日新報), *Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo* (京城日報), *Kungmin Munhak* (國民文學), etc.

Pak T'aejun (朴泰俊) - *Note: This is likely Yi T'aejun (李泰俊) based on the works mentioned, possibly a typo in the original magazine.*
1. I believe the monument (紀念塔) of our literature this year is Chŏng Chiyong's (鄭芝溶) second poetry collection. How pleased we were by the 10 new works published in the New Year issue of *Munjang* (文章)!
2. *Sasang ui Wŏrya* (思想의 月夜, Moonlit Night of Thought) was discontinued, and for short stories, there are only *T'okki Iyagi* (토끼이야기, Rabbit Story) and *Twitpang Manaem* (뒷방마냄, The Old Woman in the Back Room).

Yi Hyosŏk (李孝石)
1. Never mind the works themselves, it seems to have been an unproductive year (不作) where I couldn't even obtain many publications.
2. Works like *Raok'owŏn ui Huye* (라오코왼의 後裔, Descendant of Laocoön), *Sanhyŏp* (山峽, Mountain Gorge), *Ch'unŭisang* (春衣裳, Spring Clothes), etc., and stand-alone books <121> *Tanp'yŏnsŏn* (短篇選, Short Story Selection), *Ch'anggong Muhan* (蒼空無限, Boundless Blue Sky) (full-length novel), etc.

Pak T'aewŏn (朴泰遠)
1. I haven't particularly (別로히) read other people's works, so I cannot say anything.
2. For short stories, *Sagi wa Nammae* (四季와 남매, Four Seasons and Siblings) (*Sinsidae* 新時代), *Yundo* (倫盜, Ethical Thief) (*Chogwang* 朝光), *Chaeun* (財運, Fortune) (*Ch'unch'u* 春秋), *Chaekka* (債家, Debtor's House) (*Munjang* 文章), etc.
For full-length novels, *Yŏin Sŏngjang* (女人盛裝, Woman in Full Attire) (currently serialized in *Maeil Sinbo* 每新).
For translations, *Samgukchi* (三國志, Romance of the Three Kingdoms) (currently serialized in *Sinsidae* 新時代). I am utterly (못내) ashamed that not only is the quantity (量) extremely meager, but in terms of quality (質), there are no works worth mentioning.

Ch'ae Mansik (蔡萬植)
1. Yi T'aejun's (李泰俊作) *T'okki Iyagi* (토끼이야기, Rabbit Story).
Sŏk Inhae's (石仁海作) *Haesu* (海愁, Sea Grief).
Pak Saengmo's (朴生模作) *Kwasuwŏn* (果樹園, Orchard).
2. *Chip* (집, House) (*Ch'unch'u* 春秋), *Saho Ildan* (四號一段, Section Four, Unit One) (*Munjang* 文章), *Pyŏng i Natkŏdŭn* (病이 낫거든, Once the Sickness Heals) (*Chogwang* 朝光), besides these, there are 5 or 6 works, but they have not yet been published.

Yi Sŏkhun (李石薰)
1. I haven't been able to read comprehensively, so I cannot speak of outstanding works among them, but as publication outlets gradually decrease, I think the works being published can generally be seen as a 'harvest' (收穫).
2. None. Although I wrote a considerable number of pieces that could be called works, there were no truly satisfactory works (會心의 작). I just consider this a transition period (轉換期) and am treating it as a preparatory period for writing new characters (文字), new works.

Cho Yongman (趙容萬)
1. Among those I read, nothing remains in my memory. <122>
2. *Yŏjŏng* (旅程, Journey) (*Munjang* 文章), *Manch'an* (晩餐, Dinner) (*Ch'unch'u* 春秋), *Maebu* (매부, Brother-in-law) (*Chogwang* 朝光).

Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑)
1. Pak Chonghwa's (朴鍾和氏) *Tajŏng Pulsim* (多情佛心, Affectionate Buddhist Heart).
Chu Yohan's (朱耀翰氏) poem *P'i* (피, Blood).
Chi Haryŏn's (池河蓮氏) *Kaŭl* (가을, Autumn).
2. There are utterly (도모지) no works that I have left behind.

Chŏng Int'aek (鄭人澤)
1. Chŏng Chiyong's poetry collection *Paengnoktam* (鄭芝溶시집 白鹿潭).
Various works from the collection of 34 creative writers in the special issue of *Munjang* (文章增刊創作卅四人集中의 諸作).
2. *Yŏsu* (旅愁, Traveler's Sorrow) (*Munjang* 文章)
*Kuyŏkchi* (區域志, Regional Record) (*Chogwang* 朝光)
*Chŏnya Hunhyang* (田野薰香, Fragrance of Fields and Plains) (*Nong'ŏp Chosŏn* 農業朝鮮)
*Pongsŏnhwa* (鳳仙花, Balsam Flower) (*Maeil Sinbo* 每日新報)
*Ch'ŏngnyangni Kyeweg* (淸凉里 界隈, Vicinity of Ch'ŏngnyangni) (*Kungmin Munhak* 國民文學), etc.

Kim Saryang (金史良)
1. More than anything, I consider the emergence (輩出한) of gem-like (珠玉같은) new writers over the last two years to be the greatest harvest (收穫) for our literary world.
I enjoyed reading (愛讀) the works of Kwak Hasin (郭夏信), Sŏk Inhae (石仁海), Chi Haryŏn (池河蓮氏), etc. And also the poetry of O Changhwan (吳章煥), Sŏ Chŏngju (徐廷柱氏).
2. (a) *Kōmei* (光冥, Light and Darkness) (*Bungakkai* 文學界), *Ryūchijō de atta otoko* (留置場서 만난 사나이, The Man I Met in Jail) (*Munjang* 文章), *Chigimi* (지기미) (*Samch'ŏlli* 三千里, *Shinchō* 新潮), *Dorobō* (泥棒, Thief) (*Bungei* 文藝), *Kyōshū* (鄕愁, Homesickness) (*Bungei Shunjū* 文藝春秋), *Yama no Kamigami* (山の神神, Gods of the Mountains) (*Fūzoku* 風俗), *Hana* (鼻, Nose) (*Chisei* 知性), plus 2 or 3 other short stories and a medium-length piece currently being written (執筆중).
(b) Collected Works *Kohyang* (故鄕, Hometown).

Hong Hyomin (洪曉民)
1. Among this year's works, I liked Chŏng Int'aek's (鄭人澤氏) work titled *Ch'ŏngnyangni Kyeweg* (淸凉里 界隈) published (揭載된) in the inaugural issue of *Kungmin Munhak* (國民文學 創刊號), Yi T'aejun's (李泰俊氏) work titled *Sasang ui Wŏrya* (思想의 月夜), and Pak Hyangmin's (朴鄕民氏) play titled *Paettaragi* (배따라기, Following the Boat Song).
2. What I wrote this year is something resembling research (硏究) called *San kwa Munhak* (山과 文學, Mountains and Literature), <123> one critical essay (評論) called *Hŭk Non* (흙 論, Treatise on Soil), and an essay (隨筆) called *Ilgo Subujok* (日高睡不足, Sleeping Poorly Until Late Morning), that's all I've written. But can they become literature worth leaving behind (냉길만한 文獻)? I intend to try harder in the future.

Ham Taehun (咸大勳)
1. Entering this year, the literary world (文藝界) was too (넘우) stagnant (不振). I suppose this is related to the lack of publication outlets (發表機關) as well as the grasp of the ideology (理念把握) of National Literature (國民文學), resulting in a lack of works produced. Nothing remains in my head regarding poetry or novels. For plays, Yu Ch'ijin's (柳致眞作) *Hŭngnyonggang* (黑龍江, Black Dragon River) (in five acts) is considered a National Play (國民戱曲) and has cast (더진) large ripples (波紋) in this field (斯界).
2. I couldn't write a single work this year. Due to work with the Hyŏndae Theater (現代劇場) and the National Theater Research Institute (國民演劇硏究所), I couldn't find even an hour a day for my own creative world. Amidst that, I've written about ten installments of a full-length novel, but it seems I won't be able to finish it this year. Perhaps my achievement (成果) this year is having developed the theory of National Theater (國民演劇理論) somewhat vigorously.

Song Yŏng (宋影)
1. Because I have been confined by a long illness for more than half a year, I haven't been able to read anything (아모 것도). I am ashamed.
2. Likewise, I haven't been able to write either. There are only the staged plays (上演戱曲) *Sŏn'gujŏ* (先驅女, Pioneer Woman) (3 acts), *Yuhun* (遺訓, Dying Instructions) (2 acts), *Yasaenghwa* (野生花, Wildflower) (2 acts), but they haven't yet (아즉) been printed (活字化).

Ch'oe Myŏngik (崔明翊)
1. I couldn't see any full-length novels at all, and among the short stories I read, Yu Chino's (兪鎭午氏) *Mach'a* (馬車, Carriage) and Yi T'aejun's (李泰俊氏) *T'okki Iyagi* (토끼이야기, Rabbit Story) were interesting (滋味있었습니다).
2. I don't know what you mean by "...left behind" (냉기신), <124> but I only wrote one piece, *Chang Sam Yi Sa* (張三李四, Tom, Dick, and Harry).

Kye Yongmuk (桂鎔黙)
1. There are no works that remain in my memory as being particularly notable.
2. For works, there were barely (겨우) three short short stories (掌篇): *Simwŏl* (心月, Moon Heart), *Iban* (離叛, Betrayal), *Sudalp'i* (수달피, Otter Skin), and even then, the latter two were old manuscripts (舊稿).

**Summary**

This 1941 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine asks prominent Korean literary figures about the best works they read and their own creative output over the past year. Responses vary widely: some list specific works (Yi Kwangsu, Chu Yohan, Pak Chonghwa, Ch'ae Mansik, Chŏng Int'aek, Yi Yongak, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi, Pak Kyeju), while others claim to have read little or produced nothing of merit, citing busyness, illness, or simply a lack of notable works (Kim Tongin, Pak Yŏnghŭi, Yi Hyosŏk, Pak T'aewŏn, Yi Sŏkhun, Cho Yongman, Mo Yunsuk, Ham Taehun, Song Yŏng, Kye Yongmuk). Several authors mention works by Yi T'aejun and Chŏng Chiyong. The survey reflects the state of the Korean literary scene under intense Japanese wartime pressure. Historians might find interest in the frequent mentions of inactivity or dissatisfaction, Kim Tongin's response ("only shame"), Yi Sŏkhun's allusion to a "transition period" perhaps hinting at accommodation or silence, Ham Taehun's discussion of "National Literature" ideology and plays, and the mix of Korean and Japanese publications/works listed, particularly by Kim Saryang.
