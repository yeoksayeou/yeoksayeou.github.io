Magazine Title: 삼천리 제13권 제12호
Publication Date: 1941년 12월 01일
Article Title: 日ㆍ米ㆍ英의 太平洋戰爭!! (The Pacific War of Japan, America, and Britain!!)
Author: 崔麟 (Ch'oe Rin), 御手洗辰雄 (Mitarai Tatsuo), 高橋少將 (Major General Takahashi)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Speech Record)

○ Speech Stenography On December 1st, at the Keijō Citizens' Hall (京城府民舘), a Grand Speech Meeting was held in response to the tense situation in the Pacific. This is a stenographic record of that night (Photos above show Messrs. Mitarai, Ch'oe Rin, Matsubara, and Takahashi, from top).

**Denouncing America's Self-Righteousness**
Ch'oe Rin, Head of the Chosŏn Wartime National Service Corps (朝鮮臨戰報國團長)

I do not wish to speak ill of the American people here. This is because they were our companions in the past, but arriving at today, I cannot but denounce the mistaken self-righteousness of the Americans.
Looking back, America pointlessly participated in the First World War, consuming much military force and matériel, dealing a great blow to the national spirit, and yet what did it ultimately receive as the reward for victory? <20> President Wilson went to Paris, held the Paris Peace Conference, and merely fell for the clever manipulations of France's Clemenceau and Britain's Lloyd George, creating only the thing called the League of Nations. This "League of Nations," which could not solve any of humanity's inequalities, was just like a snowman (雪達磨) made on a snowy day; when the sun came out, it melted and melted, and gradually even its form could not be found.

(Scene of the Speech Meeting that Night)

When creating the League of Nations at the Paris Peace Conference, if only they had opened a path for emerging nations to live, giving Germany a way to breathe and survive back then, perhaps today's Second World War might not have occurred. In that respect, the responsibility for this Great War truly lies with America and the Americans (Applause).
There is another aspect to America's self-righteousness. In our East, America sent much money and people to China (支那), building many schools, hospitals, and churches everywhere. However, ultimately today, the numerous believers, schools, and hospitals remain in China, while they alone were driven out. The one who benefited was China; the one who suffered loss was America alone (Applause).
Recently, I hear that Roosevelt and British Prime Minister Churchill held a summit meeting on a warship in the Pacific Ocean. I do not know what conspiracy they hatched, but according to newspaper reports, they decided to provide aid to Chiang Kai-shek even more actively. No matter how much they do so, it will only be America that suffers loss again.
In conclusion, the one responsible for this world turmoil is, more than anyone, America. <21> America holds the notion that only it is superior, that only it is supreme under heaven, and is immersed in its own self-righteousness.
I wish to offer them two pieces of advice from this podium. First, stop all interference in foreign countries, sticking your nose into the East here and the West there, withdraw, and return to the "Monroe Doctrine," guarding only the Americas.
Second, recognize well the true strength of our Japan and the historical destiny of Asia, join hands with Japan, and build a new order for the Eastern and Western worlds.
Truly, now is the time for America to reflect deeply. Time is short, so I will end here (Applause).
×××

**The Thousand-Year Decision of the Pacific**
Mitarai Tatsuo, President of Keijō Nippō (京城日報社長)

The enemies of the world, attempting to drive all of humanity into misfortune and war, are Britain and America. Britain, with the aim of ruling the world, has turned the 440 million people of its colonies, such as India and Egypt, into eternal slaves, and is now extending its dreadful demonic hand even to the East (Applause).
Furthermore, America, under the mask of justice and humanity, has oppressed the various races of the East for the past several decades. If these two countries, Britain and America, do not reflect at this time, the world turmoil will only expand further and further, and the restoration of peace will become increasingly hopeless. In other words, we must not forget that the enemies of world peace are the tyrannical America and Britain (Applause).
What direct actions has America taken against our Japan? After the Russo-Japanese War ended, it plotted Harriman's scheme to seize the South Manchuria Railway (南滿洲鐵道); there was the issue of excluding Japanese schoolchildren in California; and as part of the ideological war, Sanger's birth control advocacy, continuously oppressing the East and interfering in Asia's affairs, one after another without end. Recently, it has oppressed us by abrogating the commerce treaty and freezing assets, and furthermore, it is strengthening the blatant ABCD encirclement against Japan.
Consider Commodore Perry (「베두리」提督), known as the teacher of the oceans, who came to our country eighty years ago. According to American writings, he planned first to occupy Okinawa Prefecture, <22> establish a naval base there, and pressure Edo Castle with military force to open trade. If that's the case, shouldn't we today occupy Panama (巴奈馬), establish a naval base there, exert pressure on New York and Washington, make them cease their oppression of the East, and open trade? Wouldn't that fit their intentions? (Applause).
Gentlemen! Do we not possess Army, Navy, and Air Forces that boast of being invincible in the world? Even if the war takes three years, five years, or ten years, we have the conviction and determination to absolutely not lose to the likes of America. Our Imperial Army (皇軍) bears the grave mission of driving away the common enemy of mankind. Here, the one hundred million people must unite as one and advance on the path to victory.
To do so, we must equip ourselves with steadfast spiritual strength. We must not entertain the notion of "what if we lose?"
There is a saying in our country, "If you act with determination, even demons and gods will avoid you." (断じて行へは鬼神も避けゐ) Truly, to repel weak enemies like Britain and America, we must rise up resolutely with this powerful military strength and spiritual power. I shout this two, three times (Applause).
Ambassador Kurusu (來栖大使) is currently in Washington, conducting final peaceful negotiations. Of course, the future of these Japan-US talks cannot be predicted by anyone, but whether it results in peace or war, we must resolutely advance through this national crisis with indomitable courage (Applause).

**The Time for One Hundred Million Compatriots to Rise**
Major General Takahashi, Chief of Staff, Korean Army (朝鮮軍參謀長)

Today, with the objectives of the Holy War (聖戰) yet unachieved, the situation has reached a critical juncture, a pressing state of affairs that threatens the self-reliant existence of the Imperial Nation (皇國) and decides the rise or fall, the security or peril of the state.
Britain and America originally monopolized world industry and economy through aggressive policies, but recently, fearing the establishment of a European economic bloc by Germany and Italy, and an East Asian economic bloc centered on Japan, they strive desperately to preserve the old order to obstruct their formation. This tendency became particularly blatant after the formation of the Japan-Germany-Italy Alliance (日獨伊同盟), especially with the outbreak of the German-Soviet War (獨蘇戰爭), not only hindering our immovable national policy, the great task of constructing the New East Asian Order (東亞新秩序建 <23> 設), through aid to Chiang Kai-shek but also reaching the point of threatening the Empire's existence with an economic blockade, creating a situation that cannot be taken lightly.
The fundamental ideology behind Britain and America adopting such an attitude is precisely to obstruct the rise of the Empire and East Asia centered on the Empire, resulting from the construction of the New Order, and to keep East Asia under the economic hegemony of Europe and America, i.e., the old order. No matter what excuses they make, this truly goes against natural principles, deviates from humanity, and attempts to reverse the general trend of bloc economies in the world. If the Empire were to yield here, it is clear that the Empire would be increasingly compressed, lose its leading position in East Asia, and eventually find even its own existence difficult.
Therefore, the Empire must somehow break through this obstacle and bring about a major transformation of the situation. So, does our Empire have the strength to break through the situation and carry through its national policy (國是)? I think perhaps the people's conviction on this point might not be firm. This might be partly due to the inability to reveal the full picture because of military or state secrets, but another significant cause is believed to stem from insufficient understanding of the shifts in the situation accompanying the current European war, especially regarding the war potential of the hostile nations. Therefore, I will speak in some detail below about the war potential of various countries.
The Soviet Union (蘇聯) normally maintains 180 divisions with five million peacetime troops. In the German-Soviet war, its mobilized force exceeded ten million, but according to more reliable investigations, it has already lost five to six million. Although it still nominally possesses a force of four to five million in terms of numbers, its actual strength has fallen to less than one-third. The situation regarding airplanes and tanks is even worse. The Soviet army will likely flee east of the Volga River (볼가 河) in the future. In this case, should the German-Soviet war become protracted, the Soviet Union, maintaining a population of sixty million and an army of two million (about 70-80 divisions), even if it skillfully relocates current facilities to rebuild heavy industry and arms manufacturing, will only be able to exist as a second-rate nation, exhibiting merely one-third of its former capacity.
At the start of the German-Soviet war, there was a considerably strong force in the Far East (極東), but after the war began, about one-third of the elite troops, along with over 1,200 airplanes, tanks, etc., were gradually sent west to European Russia. The vacancies were filled with conscripts, so its war potential seems to have rapidly declined. However, the remaining military and civilians in the Soviet Far East are gradually learning of the great defeat of their home country and becoming engulfed in anxiety about the Far East.
Within the military, due to fear of Japan, border defenses were actually being strictly maintained, but due to anxiety about the homeland, poor supplies, excessive duties, etc., desertions and surrenders are occurring continuously. Recently, I inspected the ○○ area and witnessed three deserters just days before, and also the surrender of one non-commissioned officer the day before yesterday. Because of this situation, the Soviet Union <24> will likely strengthen the neutrality pact in the Far East. Therefore, unless the Soviet Far East receives heavy pressure from America or others, there is no reason for it to join hands with the Empire. Furthermore, the steadfast, victory-assured stance of our Great Kwantung Army (大關東軍) and the Korean Army (朝鮮車 - likely refers to the 朝鮮軍, Korean Army Command under Japan), which possesses the history of annihilating an enemy three times its size in the Battle of Zhanggufeng (張鼓峯戰) without using a single tank or airplane, truly serves as a great cornerstone for the defense of the north.
Next is Britain. It is true that they are strengthening the defenses of Hong Kong, Singapore, Burma (빌마), etc., but the British Army received a thorough blow from the German forces in last year's (?) campaign (likely referring to France/Dunkirk or Greece/Crete) and has not yet recovered its strength. On top of that, due to the need for strong defenses in the homeland, Egypt, and various regions of the Near East, they are in a state where they cannot increase their forces in the Far East. Its Navy must concentrate its main strength to secure its lifelines against Germany's blockade operations around the British Isles and Mediterranean operations. That is, both the Army and Navy rely entirely on America to protect the remnants of the old order. This tendency will intensify if Germany begins operations like the Caucasus (코-카사쓰) campaign.
It will become even more severe if the operation to land on the British mainland begins. It is well known that America possesses a superior and still undamaged Navy and Air Force. However, considering the Atlantic front, the technical difficulties of trans-Pacific operations, the excessive distance and scarcity of air bases, etc., the forces it can employ in the East are limited, and its war potential is nothing to fear. Especially its Army, having been tested in the Great European War, is already out of the question. Having outlined the war potential of these countries above, I will now speak about our own war potential.
While carrying out the China Incident (支那事變), we have unreservedly <25> strengthened our Army and Navy readiness out of the necessity to respond at any time to challenges from hostile third countries. That is, regarding the Army, what was consumed in the China Incident was only a portion of weapons, materials, etc. Munitions production has been increased and expanded, and military preparedness has been expanded epochally. Furthermore, with the threat from the north being incomparable to the past, while prosecuting the Holy War, we have ample surplus strength to resolutely carry out operations in any direction whatsoever. And through intense training day and night, the officers and men are truly burning with the conviction of certain victory. Also, the capacity for producing weapons, airplanes, and ammunition has advanced by leaps and bounds, reaching several times, even ten-odd times, that of before the Incident, putting us in a state where we can meet the demands of war at any time. On the other hand, the so-called total war (總力戰) posture, encompassing finance, treasury, industry, etc., essential for war, is being progressively strengthened, putting us in a state of absolute certain victory should a Japan-US war break out at any time. Moreover, even if it becomes a protracted war, based on self-sufficiency within the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (東亞共榮圈), depending on the people's determination, we are poised to forcefully continue for however many years it takes and see the construction of Greater East Asia through to the end.
However, war is prosecuted by the power of will, and the establishment of will comes from self-confidence. Therefore, understanding the international situation I have spoken of above and regarding our war potential, I am convinced that the people need to trust the government and, at the same time, possess the conviction that the Imperial Nation (皇國), as a divine land (神國), will certainly be victorious.
Now, the Empire is considering measures to break through the difficult times with attitudes ready for both peace and war. And whether the means <26> are peaceful or military, the success or failure will decide the national destiny of the Imperial Nation for the next thousand years. Now, the Army and Navy are poised to adapt to any sudden turn of events. The Imperial Army (皇軍) does not exist to fight; it exists to carry through the national policy (國是). Therefore, to prepare this posture, the people must not remain immersed in the old state of affairs but must exert all efforts for the execution of total war.
Originally, whenever the Japanese people faced difficulties, they united from top to bottom to overcome them, thereby protecting our glorious national polity (國體) and achieving ever greater prosperity day by day. The current difficulty is incomparably more profound and intense than the Mongol Invasions (元冠) or the Russo-Japanese War (日露雨役). Therefore, once we overcome this, we shall realize the great ideal of Hakko Ichiu (八紘一宇) here and truly see a great light in our future. Therefore, for a certain period, deeply embracing the mindset of living under wartime conditions, with perseverance and creativity, without thinking of personal or familial interests against each other, with the resolve to starve with the nation and rejoice with the nation, with an iron will for the貫徹 of national policy, let every last one of the one hundred million compatriots firmly resolve to offer themselves to the country and assist and uphold the eternal Imperial Plan (皇謨).
-End- <27>

**Summary**

This article, published on December 1, 1941, just days before the Pearl Harbor attack, compiles speeches from a rally in Keijō (Seoul). The speakers, Ch'oe Rin (a prominent Korean collaborator), Mitarai Tatsuo (Japanese newspaper president), and Major General Takahashi (Japanese military chief of staff in Korea), vehemently denounce American and British "self-righteousness" and "imperialism," blaming them for global conflict and the oppression of Asia. They justify Japan's actions as necessary for self-preservation and the creation of a "New Order" in East Asia, express unwavering confidence in Japan's military strength and spirit to overcome any challenge, including a protracted war with the US, and call for total mobilization and sacrifice from the populace ("one hundred million compatriots," including Koreans) to achieve victory and realize Japan's "divine" destiny under the Emperor. The speeches highlight Japan's escalating anti-Western propaganda, its justification for expansionism (Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere), and its assessment (or misassessment) of Allied and Soviet strength immediately preceding the Pacific War.

For historians, this article is significant as it captures the intense pro-war, anti-Allied atmosphere being cultivated in colonial Korea on the very eve of the Pacific War. The participation of Ch'oe Rin, a high-profile Korean figure heading a collaborationist organization (Chosŏn Wartime National Service Corps), demonstrates the mobilization of Korean elites into the Japanese war effort and the rhetoric used to align Korean interests with those of Imperial Japan against the West. It starkly reveals the pressures and propaganda faced by Koreans during this period, demanding unity and sacrifice for the Japanese Empire's perceived "Holy War." The military assessment by Major General Takahashi provides insight into the Japanese military's mindset and strategic calculations regarding potential adversaries (USSR, UK, US) at that critical juncture.

日ㆍ米ㆍ英의 太平洋戰爭!!


○ 演說速記 十二月一日, 京城府民舘에서, 太平洋의 緊迫한 時局에 呼應하여 大演說會가 開催되었다. 이것은 當夜의 速記(寫眞은 上으로부터 御手洗, 崔麟, 松原, 高橋의 諸氏)


米國의 獨善主義를 排擊
朝鮮臨戰報國團長 崔麟

나는 이 자리에서 米國사람의 辱을 하고십지는안슴니다. 그것은 그들이 과거에 우리들의 동무였기때문이었으나 그러나 오늘날에 이르러는 米國人의 잘못된 獨善主義에 對하여 여기에 排擊치 않을 수 없습니다.
回顧컨대 米國은 第一次世界大戰當時에 空然히 參戰하여서 많은 軍隊와 軍需品을 消耗하고 國民의 精神에 큰 打擊을주면서 結局戰勝의 報酬로 바더드린것이 무엇인가하면,＜20＞ 大統領우윌손이 巴里에가서 巴里媾和會議를열고 佛蘭西의 쿠레만소-와 英國의 로이도, 죠-지의 巧妙한 弄絡에 너머가서 國際聯盟이란 것을 만들었을뿐이었습니다. 人類의 不平等을 아모 解決치도못하는이「國際聯盟」이란것은 恰似히 눈오는날 만들어 놓은 눈사람(雪達磨)과 가태서 해가나자 녹고 녹아서 차츰 그 形骸도 차저볼길이 없는것이있어요.


(當夜演說會場光景)
