Synopsis of World Masterpieces
Selected by the Editorial Office

Enoch Arden
By Tennyson
Enoch and Annie were childhood friends who eventually married. Philip, who also loved Annie, is heartbroken because of this. Enoch fails in business and goes to China (支那) to make money, leaving his wife and children. Annie tried to provide for her three children but failed, and the youngest child dies. At this point, Philip extends a helping hand. After continuing his pure protection for 10 years, Philip finally proposes marriage to Annie. A year later, Annie, resigned to the belief that Enoch is dead, marries Philip and bears his child. Just then, a destitute Enoch returns, learns that Annie has remarried, and wishing for their happiness, dies in solitude clutching a lock of her hair without meeting Annie.

Peer Gynt
By Ibsen
The maiden Solveig hears the empty love confession of Peer and, abandoning her parents and siblings, runs with Peer to his small hut in the mountains. Peer is surprised and happy that Solveig loves him, a poor and violent man pointed at by the villagers. At this point, a woman called the Troll Princess, with whom Peer had previously lived, comes with his child and causes trouble. Therefore, Peer comes down from the mountain, wanting to meet Solveig after cleansing his body.
Ten years pass. Peer, having completely forgotten about Solveig while wandering various parts of the world, becomes homesick as he grows older. Returning to Norway as an insignificant old man, he suddenly remembers Solveig and seeks out his mountain hut. The blind Solveig comes out, leaning on a cane. Solveig has still been waiting for Peer. The Living Corpse (산송장)
By Tolstoy
Out of a dissolute sense of responsibility, Fedya decides to separate <136> from his wife Liza. But Liza loves him. Knowing that his friend Karenin has feelings for Liza, Fedya distances himself for the sake of the two's happiness and loves a woman named Masha. Fedya considered this moral. Yet, he attempted suicide. Masha finds out and stops him. Liza and Karenin, receiving the suicide note, are surprised and go to the scene. Upon arrival, they find his belongings, and soon a corpse floats up. Thus, the two end up marrying. However, Fedya was not dead. He was hiding. Because Fedya revealed this secret to someone, Liza is accused of the crime of bigamy. Liza is arrested—. In court, Fedya speaks of how his good intentions were twisted by a third-party villain and commits suicide before the verdict. To defy the law and set his wife free. <137>

**Summary**

This article, selected by the editorial office of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli*, provides brief synopses of three famous Western literary works: Alfred Tennyson's "Enoch Arden," Henrik Ibsen's "Peer Gynt," and Leo Tolstoy's "The Living Corpse." Each summary outlines the main plot points, focusing on themes of love, sacrifice, betrayal, societal judgment, and moral conflict. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it shows the introduction and curation of Western literary classics for a Korean audience in 1941, during the late Japanese colonial period. The selection, chosen by the editors rather than a specific critic, might reflect an attempt to present relatively uncontroversial narratives focused on universal human drama, perhaps navigating censorship while still offering cultural enrichment.

이노크ㆍ아-덴
테니슨作
이노크와 안니-는 어릴 때 동무인데 結婚에까지 들어갔다. 안니-를 사랑하든 퓔립은 그 때문에 失戀한다. 이노크는 事業에 失敗하고 돈 뫃으기 위해서 妻子를 두고 支那에 간다. 안니-는 세 아이의 生計를 꾀했으나 失敗하고 막내아이가 죽는다. 거기서 퓔립이 救援의 손을 편다. 10년간 깨끝한 保護를 게속해 온 퓔립은 끝내 안니-에게 結婚申請을 하게된다. 1年後, 안니-는 이노크가 죽었다 단념하고 結婚하여 퓔립의 아이이를 낳는아. 거기에 이노크가 低落하여 도라오는데, 안니-가 結婚한 것을 알자, 그들의 幸福을 빌면서 안니다 안만나고 그의 머리카락을 품고 孤獨한 채로 죽는다.

페-ㄹㆍ균트
입센作
