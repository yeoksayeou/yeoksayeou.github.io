Overseas War Poetry Collection, Italy Edition
						Translated by Mo Yunsuk

Tricolor Flag (三色旗)   Paolo Buzzi (파오토ㆍ뿟쯔이)

That subtle pulsation heard on the 'balcony' (빨콘)
The beat of wings, the beat of love
Oh, the resoundingness of ecstatic song.

And the sound, blood boils in the chest
Amidst the thousand sounds of spring in the pasture (牧場)
The first light (光明) harmonizes with the 'goal' (꼬올) hymn (讚歌).

And so, when asleep, oh, Tricolor Flag (三色旗),
So affectionately
From the kiss of the trusted demon that shakes me
The immortal season of the god of glory (榮光) seen in dreams. <144>

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean translation by Mo Yunsuk of the Italian poem "Tricolor Flag" by Paolo Buzzi. The poem evokes nationalistic fervor through imagery of pulsations, spring, light, and the Italian flag, linking them to ecstatic song, boiling blood, and dreams of glory. For historians, this piece is interesting as it shows a prominent Korean literary figure, Mo Yunsuk, translating and publishing poetry from an Axis power (Italy) in a major Korean magazine in December 1941. This reflects the cultural and political pressures of the late colonial period, aligning with or being influenced by the Japanese empire's wartime propaganda efforts promoting Axis solidarity and nationalistic sentiment.
