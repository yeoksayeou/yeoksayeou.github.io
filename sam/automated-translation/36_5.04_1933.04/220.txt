Impressions of Shimamura Hōgetsu and Sumako
						Ch'unwŏn

It was June 26th of the Chŏngsa year (丁巳; 1917), so it's already an old story from over ten years ago. That day, commissioned by M Company (M社), I had boarded a southbound train at Namdaemun Station with the intention of traversing the five provinces of Hoso (湖西) and Yŏngnam (嶺南). It was then, inside the train car, that I met Shimamura Hōgetsu, Matsui Sumako, and their party. They were on their way from Inch'ŏn to Mokp'o and looked very tired. I presented my business card (名銜) and inquired about their impressions of their tour of Chosŏn (朝鮮巡遊). As Mr. Shimamura was a literary man (文學子), he spoke variously about Korean literature (朝鮮文學). Thinking it might be very beneficial, I intend to record the gist (大網) of it.

"Chosŏn has a long history (歷史), so it must naturally possess unique thoughts and feelings (思想感情). However, having long been under the oppression (壓迫) of Chinese civilization (支那文明), these could not fully develop (發育) and ended up withered (凋殘). Furthermore, even if some remnants still exist, they haven't been able to be expressed (發表).

But now is a time when, receiving the new civilization (新文明), a new vitality (新生氣) is emerging in everything, so thoughts and feelings—that is, the spirit (精神)—must also gain new vitality to develop and be expressed (表現). To achieve this, one must look forward to the development of Korean literature (朝鮮文學).

Generally, literature is the only thing (밧게 업스니까) that expresses spiritual life. Therefore, among the new youth of Chosŏn, those interested (有意<46>) in literature need to unite and cooperate (一致協力) to strive greatly for the construction of a new literature.

The content (內容) of literature is thought and feeling, and the tools (機構) for expressing them are language (語) and writing (文). I am unfamiliar (不知) with the Korean language and writing, but it seems likely that its grammar (文法) or literary style (文體) has not yet been perfected (完成).

Therefore, first (爲先), the language and writing (語文) must be put in order (整頓), and next (다음에는), various literary forms (文學上의 諸形式) such as novels (小說), poetry (詩), or drama (劇) must be transplanted (移植) to suit (合하도록) the Korean language and writing.

This is entirely (總히) your responsibility (君等의 책임이니까), so I hope you will exert yourselves wholeheartedly (全心努力)."

He also mentioned having met seven gentlemen in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), including Ch'oe Namsŏn (崔南善) and Chin Hakmun (秦學文 諸氏), and said he held great expectations (多大한 囑望) for them, asking me to convey his regards (文案) when I went to Kyŏngsŏng.

I expressed my gratitude (感謝하는 뜻을 표하엿다) for his earnest words (懇篤한 말).

Mr. Hōgetsu first arranged Sumako's seat, even inflating an air pillow (風枕) for her so she could rest comfortably, before dozing off himself, leaning on his own air pillow. Beside him, a lovely young woman said to be Sumako's adopted daughter (養女) was also dozing, leaning against a chair, occasionally half-opening her beautiful eyes to gaze around the cabin (室內), seemingly lost in thought.

Watching the farmers (農夫) standing intently at the edge of the rice paddies with faces worried about the lack of rain (비가 不足하야 근심하는 낫츠로), I arrived at Choch'iwŏn (鳥致院).

Mr. Hōgetsu's party was deeply dreaming of "Katusha" (까주쌰 - likely referring to Sumako's famous role), so I got off without being able to say farewell (作別人事). I hurriedly hailed a car bound for Kongju (公州行 自動車) and sped off (다라낫다) towards the Kongju Provincial Office (公州監營). <47>

**Summary**

The author, Ch'unwŏn (likely Yi Kwangsu), recounts a train encounter in 1917 with the famous Japanese writer Shimamura Hōgetsu and actress Matsui Sumako. Hōgetsu shares his views on Korean literature, acknowledging its potential rooted in a long history but noting its suppression under Chinese influence and its current underdeveloped state. He encourages Korean youth to develop their language, grammar, and literary forms (novel, poetry, drama) suited to Korean expression, mentioning his hope for figures like Ch'oe Namsŏn. The author observes the personal dynamics between Hōgetsu, a tired Sumako, and her adopted daughter before disembarking. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for capturing a direct interaction between a prominent Korean intellectual and influential Japanese cultural figures during the colonial period. Hōgetsu's comments, while seemingly encouraging, reflect a common Japanese perspective of Korean culture as historically overshadowed and needing modernization/development, fitting within the broader colonial narrative of tutelage, even as he expresses hope for its unique expression. The casual mention of meeting Ch'oe Namsŏn also anchors the encounter within the specific intellectual milieu of early colonial Korea.

島村抱月과 須磨子의 印象
						春園
丁巳 6월 26일 이엇스니 벌서 10여년 전의 녯날 일이다. 그날 나는 M社의 委囑으로 湖西 嶺南의 5道를 踏破할 작정으로 남대문 정거장에서 南行車를 탄일이 잇다. 그때 나는 車中에서 島村抱月 松井 須磨子 一行을 만낫다. 仁川서 木浦를 가는 길이라는데 매우 피곤한 모양이다. 나는 명함을 들이고 朝鮮巡遊에 대한 감상을 들엇다. 氏는 문학자니까 朝鮮文學에 대하야서 여러 가지 말을 하더라. 매우 유익할 듯 하기로 그 大網을 기록할련다.
「朝鮮은 역사가 오래니까 자연 특별한 사상감정이 잇슬 것이다. 그러나 오랜동안 支那文明의 압박을 바다서 그것이 충분히 발육하지 못하고 凋殘하여지고 말엇다. 그뿐더러 設或 아직 남아잇는 것이라도 발표가 되지 못하엿다.
그러다가 지금은 신문명을 바다 모든 것에 새로운 생기가 나는 때니까 사상감정 즉 정신도 새로운 생기를 어더 발육하고 표현되여야 하겟다. 그리함에는 朝鮮文學이 발달하기를 기대하여야 하겟다.
대개 정신생활을 표현하는 것은 문학밧게 업스니까 그럼으로 朝鮮新靑年中에 문학에 有意＜46＞한자는  一致協力하야 크게 신문학 건설을 위하야 힘쓸 필요가 잇다.
문학의 내용은 사상감정이어니와 그것을 표현하는 기구는 語와 文이라. 朝鮮語와 文을 余는 不知하거니와 아마 아직 문법이나 문체가 완성되지 아니하엿슬 듯하다.
그러니까 爲先 어문을 정돈하여야 하겟고 다음에는 소설이나 시나 劇 가튼 문학상의 諸形式을 朝鮮語文에 합하도록 이식하여야 하겟다.
이것은 總히 君等의 책임이니까 전심노력하기를 바란다.」
하고 京城서 崔南善
秦學文 諸氏 7인을 맛낫든 말하며 그네에게 多大한 囑望을 가지니 京城에 가거든 문안을 전하여 달나 하더라.
나는 氏의 懇篤한 말에 감사하는 뜻을 표하엿다.
抱月 氏는 爲先 須磨子의 자리를 잡어주고 風枕에 공기까지 부러너허서 편안히 자게 한 뒤에 자긔도 風枕에 지대어서 졸더라. 그 겻헤는 須磨子의 양녀라는 어엽분 처녀가 이 역시 걸상에 기대어 졸며 잇다금 그 고운 눈을 반쯤 떠서는 아모 생각업는드시 室內를 둘너본다.
비가 부족하야 근심하는 낫츠로 논머리에 우둑헌미 섯는 농부들을 바라보면서 나는 鳥致院에 다다럿다.
抱月氏 일행은 한창 까주쌰의 꿈을 꾸는 중임으로 작별인사도 하지 못하고 나렷다. 밧비 公州行 자동차를 잡아타고 公州監營을 향하야 다라낫다. ＜47＞
