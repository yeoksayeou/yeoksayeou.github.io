**Breaking the Silence of a Spring Night**

Medicine
Calling the wife of a seriously ill man to a place where no one else is present: "Please be relieved. I have driven out all that nasty fever."
"Then my husband won't get better, will he?"
"It's alright. Even if he passes away, the illness will be cured before he goes."

Prerequisite
Man: If you marry me, I'll take out a huge life insurance policy on myself for your future...
Woman: Oh, thank you very much. But what if you don't pass away?

Still Abnormal
Manager: Haha, you must be a married man.
Job Applicant: Not at all. I'm still single. <52>
Manager: Huh? Single? Single, you say? (Briefly looks up at his face) Then what about those fingernail marks on your cheek?

Obstinacy
Gun Shop Owner: Hey, call that new warehouse worker. That rascal was smoking on top of a gunpowder box earlier. That guy's neck is forfeit, his neck!
Clerk: Ah, but it might not be as easy as before.
Owner: What? Not easy? For the owner to cut off a servant's neck?
Clerk: Exactly. It won't be possible. Because that fellow's neck disappeared when the gunpowder exploded earlier.

Needless Worry
Section Chief: Then come starting tomorrow. Among the staff, there are some undiligent people who just stare at the clock during work hours, but you wouldn't do that, would you?
Applicant: Hah. You don't need to worry about that. I have my own watch.

Fatal Wound
Youth: I don't know what to do. Which woman should I marry? There are two good prospects.
Her: Who is the other one?
Youth: You know the other one well.
Her: (Face flushing) No, I don't know!

Habit
He lacked confidence in romance. Still, he was well-behaved. So all his friends advised him, saying:
"Get into the habit of badmouthing women."

Repulsion Method
A: Where are you living now? <53>
B: I'll give you this business card. But I'm moving soon.
A: Hey, look here. Last time we met, didn't you give me a business card and say you were moving soon?
B: I've decided to always do that to bothersome people.

The Truth of Life
It's strange that someone like you is doing so well running an obstetrics and gynecology clinic.
"Why?"
"Have you looked at your face in the mirror?"
"I am, as you say, an ugly man. That's why husbands feel secure sending their wives to me."

Unreasonable Argument
Warden: Do you have any wishes?
Death Row Inmate: I do, but it won't work.
Warden: Tell me what it is. It's your last wish, so I'll grant anything—
Death Row Inmate: Still—
Warden: No, I'll grant anything—
Death Row Inmate: Then I'll tell you, let me take out a life insurance policy.

Like Father, Like Son
Teacher: You all know well that giving is better than receiving, right?
Child: Yes. My father says so too.
Teacher: Ho ho— That's a good father. So, what does your father do?
Child: "A boxer!"

This is Unreasonable
A: Come hang out tonight.
B: But I promised to go see Suil (守一) and Sunae (順愛) today.
A: Hey, it's fine. Bring them both along. <54>

Secret Exchange
Chatterbox: Somehow, I feel worried about you. You won't tell the secret I told you to anyone else, right?
Blabbermouth: Eh, it's fine. Who would I tell? I only exchange it for other secrets.

Too Used To It
"Why is the bill calculation so slow?"
Waitress: What do you mean? Since you teachers always say you don't take the change, I'm not used to it.

Totaling Six
Teacher: Name six wild animals found in Africa.
Student: Two lions and four elephants.

Sheep, Paper, Writer
"What year of the zodiac is a writer born under?"
"The Year of the Sheep (未生)."
"You know that well."
"Of course, I know. Think about it. Besides sheep, paper shops (紙店), and writers, what else eats paper (조희)?"

Seeing is Believing
Son: Father, the newspaper mentions something called 'husband killer'. What is that?
Father: That's when the mother—the father—
Mother: Honey! Telling such things to a child—
Father: It's fine. When the mother—the father!
Mother: Really, why are you doing this? Honey—
Father: What's wrong? It's fine.
Mother: What's fine?
Then the son shouted. <55>
"Ah—I get it! Father, what a 'husband killer' is—"

Letter
Man: Why haven't you replied to my letter?
Woman: I never received any letter.
Man: You never received it?!
Woman: That's right. And I didn't like the phrasing written in the letter either.

?
Father: What needs doing today must be done today.
Son: Then give me all the leftover snacks from earlier today—

Better Than Knocking
Woman: When you enter my room, knock on the door first.
Man: Isn't it better to peek through the keyhole before entering than to knock?

Gentleman and Boy
As a young maiden sat in the park with her little brother, about three years old, a passing gentleman asked the boy accompanying him:
"Hey, how old do you think that maiden is?"
"Change your mind. She already has a child."

Contradiction
A certain tactless fellow went to a friend's wedding and said:
"By the way, why are there more women getting married than men this year?"

What a Waste
Seeing his pretty daughter putting on makeup, her father, a lacquerer, clicked his tongue and muttered to himself:
"If only that one had been born a boy, she would have truly become an excellent lacquerer." <56>

Devil's Gulch
An American went over to England and was strolling through a garden named "Devil's Gulch (악마의 골작)." An old man appeared from somewhere, "Who on earth are you, trespassing on someone else's garden?"
American: Sir, I heard this place was called Devil's Gulch. But I never dreamed I'd meet the owner, sir.

Self-Portrait
In a corner of the theater (극장), two female students (女學生) are deep in conversation.
A: Look at this. That Yŏngsuk (영숙) saw the self-portrait (自畵像) I drew and said it was completely wrong, not even worth 10 points. It's so upsetting.
B: It's alright. Don't worry. If Yŏngsuk had your face (얼골), she probably couldn't have drawn it any better either.

Bill
A guest staying at an inn (려관) for free receives the bill (計算書) from the mistress (주인마나님) and says, "Madam, why is the amount so high? It feels like I've been lodging here for about 10 years without paying a single penny."
Inn Mistress: Looking at it, it seems that way.

Antacid
Neighbor boy: Auntie next door, look at this. The auntie right next door gave me these Japanese snacks (일본 과자). So now my stomach is flat. How can she be such a gentle mother (어머닐가요)?
Auntie next door: Ah, then what a waste (압갑다). I'll bring you some antacid (胃散), take it. <57>

**Summary**

This article, "Breaking the Silence of a Spring Night," is a collection of short, humorous anecdotes and jokes published in the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* in April 1933. The jokes cover various themes including misunderstandings in relationships, observations about professions (doctors, gunsmiths, innkeepers, boxers, lacquerers), wordplay, social interactions, and cynical takes on life, marriage, and death. The humor often relies on simple twists, puns (like the writer/sheep/paper joke), or ironic situations.

For historians, this piece offers a glimpse into popular humor and social commentary during the Japanese colonial period in Korea. It reflects common social archetypes, everyday concerns (jobs, marriage, money), prevailing attitudes (e.g., towards physical appearance in the OB/GYN joke), and the Korean language usage of the 1930s, including older spellings and vocabulary. The mention of Japanese snacks (일본 과자) also subtly points to the cultural context of the time.
