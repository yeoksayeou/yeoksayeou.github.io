Great Actor and the Stage

It was the spring of Taishō 6 (1917), so already an event of over 10 years past. On the splendid stage of Seoul's Umi-gwan (優美舘), a single act of Korean new drama was performed, causing the quiet blood of the capital's citizens to boil. The script at that time was "Changhanmong" (長恨夢), an adaptation of "Konjiki Yasha" (金色夜채), and there was the protagonist Yi Ki-se (李基世), the great performer who brought this drama to life. Yi Ki-se was originally tall and lanky, with a fair complexion and a Seoul accent in his speech; when he appeared as Yi Su-il (李守一), wearing a torn manteau (만또) and a high school cap, and recited his lines (세리푸), it was enough to make the blood of the peninsula's men and women boil.

"Changhanmong" was adapted by Cho Il-che (조일제), incorporating the scenic spots and historical sites of the Taedong River (대동강) into the background, and depicting the conflict between love and gold, which resonated with the public sentiment of the time and drew extraordinary popularity. Finally, to briefly outline the plot of "Changhanmong":

Yi Su-il, attending high school, loved Sim Sun-ae, the daughter of his benefactor. Just when they were set to hold their wedding ceremony upon his graduation, the unexpected appearance of a millionaire named Kim Chung-bae led to his lover being snatched away. Thereupon, Yi Su-il resolved to make money, became a usurer, and fought against a cruel world, before finding a loving home with a repentant Sim Sun-ae in the middle of his struggles, thus bringing the story to a close.<5>

**Summary**

This article recalls the immense success of the play "Changhanmong" (長恨夢), a Korean adaptation of the Japanese novel "Konjiki Yasha" (金色夜叉), staged at the Umi-gwan (優美舘) theater in Seoul around 1917. It specifically praises the performance of actor Yi Ki-se (李基世) in the lead role of Yi Su-il (李守一), whose appearance and delivery deeply moved audiences. The plot, involving a love triangle complicated by wealth and leading to the protagonist becoming a moneylender before eventual reconciliation, is noted for having strongly resonated with the public sentiment of the era. For historians, the article highlights the cultural landscape of colonial Korea, showing the popularity of adapted Japanese narratives within the burgeoning "new drama" (신극) movement. It provides insight into early 20th-century Korean theatre, audience tastes, and the social anxieties surrounding modernization and materialism (love vs. money) during that period.

名優와 舞臺
대정 6년 봄이니 벌서 10여 년 전의 녯날 일이다. 서울 우미*의 화려한 무대 우에 조선의 신극 한 막이 상영되여 장안인사의 고요하든 피를 끌케 한 일이 잇섯다. 그 때의 각본이 「金色夜채」를 번안한 「장한몽」이요, 이 극을 살린 명 연출자의 주인공 리긔세씨가 잇섯다. 리긔세씨는 원래 키가 후리후리 크고, 얼굴이 히고, 말소리 서울태가 되여, 그가 찌저진 만또에 고등학교 모자를 쓴 리수일이 되어 나와서 세리푸를 의일 때 반도 남녀의 피를 끄리기에 충분하엿다.
장한몽은 조일제 씨의 번안이라 배경에다가 대동강의 명승고적을 집어너코 그리고 연애와 황금의 갈등을 그린 점이 그 시대 인심에 투합되여 비상한 인긔를 끄으럿다. 끗흐로 장한몽의 경개를 간단히 적으면
「고등학교에 다니는 리수일은 은인의 따님 심순애를 사랑하엿다. 그래서 학교를 졸업하면 결혼 례식을 거행하기로*지 되엇슬때 불의에 김중배란 백만장자의 출현으로 애인을 *앗기고 말엇다. 이에 리수일은 돈 모으기로 결심하고, 고리대금 업자가 되여 악독한 세상과 싸우다가 도중에 회심한 심순애와 사랑의 보금자리 맛는 것으로 끗을 맷는다.＜5＞
