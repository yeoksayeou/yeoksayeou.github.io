The Current State of the Peninsula's Theater World, "As Seen, As Remembered"
						Han Chi-su (韓志洙)

Chwisŏngjwa (聚星座)
After Kim Sorang's (金小浪) troupe lost its substance, the Chosŏn Yŏn'gŭksa (朝鮮硏劇舍 - Korea Research Theater Company) was founded under the leadership of Chi Tu-hwan (池斗煥). Following that, Mun Su-il (文秀一), who had been an important figure in the Yŏn'gŭksa, withdrew from it – together with Yi Kyŏng-sŏl (李景雪), he carved out the Minjung Kŭktan Yŏn'gŭk Sijang (民衆劇團演劇市場 - People's Theater Troupe Theater Market). The Yŏn'gŭk Sijang (Theater Market), born as the successor to the Yŏn'gŭksa, held its first performance immediately after its founding at the Tansŏngsa Theater (團成社) in the central location of Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul). While the Chosŏn Yŏn'gŭksa, which had been on a regional tour, finished its tour and came up to Kyŏngsŏng, performing for an extended run at the Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇場) – in opposition to the Yŏn'gŭk Sijang – it saw the withdrawal of key figures such as Sin Pul-ch'ul (申不出), Yi Wŏn-jae (李元在), Yi Chong-ch'ŏl (李鍾哲), and Chŏn Kyŏng-hŭi (全景希). The Yŏn'gŭksa left Kyŏngsŏng and set off on the path of a regional tour to Inch'ŏn (仁川). - News spread that Sin Pul-ch'ul, Yi Chong-ch'ŏl, Chŏn Kyŏng-hŭi, and others who had left the Yŏn'gŭksa had joined hands with Kim Sorang, the former head (座主) of Chwisŏngjwa, to revive Chwisŏngjwa, but this rumor disappeared. Instead, a new troupe, Sin Mudae (新舞臺 - New Stage), was founded by the hands of these figures: Sin Pul-ch'ul, Yi Chong-ch'ŏl, Chŏn Kyŏng-hŭi, Yi Wŏn-jae, etc. They held their first performance at the Tansŏngsa, staging Sin Pul-ch'ul's *Arirang Pandaep'yŏn* (아리랑 반대편 - The Other Side of Arirang). Then, they too set off on a regional tour. The theater movement, arising one after another, even reached the Haeoe Munhakp'a (海外文學派 - Overseas Literature School), and through their united efforts, an organization called the Kŭgyesul Yŏn'guhoe (劇藝術硏究會 - Theater Arts Research Society) was founded. It held a theater, film, and exhibition event, and subsequently established a directly affiliated Silyŏm Mudae (實驗舞臺 - Experimental Stage).

Last year's student plays were numerous in performance count.
At the end of October, there was a night for the Sejŏn Pun'gŭk (世專分極 - meaning unclear, possibly Severance Union Medical College drama division). On November 27th, there was also a Yŏnjŏn (延專 - Yonhi College) student play, staging Tolstoy's *Power of Darkness*. The following February 4th, there was the Ihwa Yŏja Chŏnmun Hakkyo (梨專 - Ewha Womans College) Theater Festival, staging Shakespeare's *The Taming of the Shrew*. At the Kyŏngsŏng Poyuk Hakkyo Nokyanghoe (京城保育學校 綠羊會 - Kyŏngsŏng Childcare School Green Sheep Society), there was a Night of Children's Songs and Plays. "The above student plays are cited from the November issue of *Sin Tong-a* (新東亞) as the author lacks reference materials."

Following this, on February 26th, Kŭnhwa Yŏhakkyo (槿花女學校 - Kŭnhwa Girls' School) held the Kŭnhwa Theater Festival at the Hasegawa-chō Public Hall (長谷川町 公會堂) in Kyŏngsŏng, staging the following scripts:
Yi Hae-nam (李海南) - *Majak* (麻雀 - Mahjong) 「1 Act 1 Scene」
Chekhov - translated by Ham Tae-hun (咸大勳) - *The Proposal* 「1 Act 1 Scene」
Galsworthy - translated by Chang Ki-je (張起悌) - *The Winner and the Loser* 「1 Act 1 Scene」

Following this, the leftist mobile small theater troupe (移動式 小型 劇場) was founded.
Advisor: Kang Ch'ŏn-hŭi (姜天熙)
Script Department: Yu Chin-o (兪鎭午), Yi Hyo-sŏk (李孝石)
Directing Department: Kim U-yŏng (金幽影), Ha Puk-hyang (河北鄕)
Acting Department (Male): Sŏk Il-lyang (石一良), Yun Pong-ch'un (尹逢春), etc.
Acting Department (Female): Kim Sŏn-ch'o (金仙草), Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英), Kim Yŏn-sil (金蓮實), etc.
Scripts included *Chŏnsŏn* (全線 - The Whole Line), *Sun-a - Ne Choe-ga Anida* (順아-네 죄가 아니다 - Sun-a, It's Not Your Fault), *Ich'ŭng-ŭi Sanai* (2층의 사나히 - The Man on the Second Floor), Song Yŏng's (宋影) *Hosinsul* (호신술 - Self-Defense), Kim U-yŏng's *Chihach'ŭng Sodong* (지하층 소동 - Basement Commotion), Kim Yŏng-p'al's (金永八) *Puŭm* (訃音 - Obituary Notice), Yu Chin-o's *Pak Ch'ŏmji* (朴僉知), Yi Hyo-sŏk's *Tanan'gi-ŭi Ki* (多難期의 記 - Record of a Difficult Period), etc. were reported to be performed.
A Pangsonggŭk Hyŏphoe (放送劇協會 - Broadcasting Drama Association) was organized centering around Pak Sŭng-hŭi (朴勝熙). In Inch'ŏn, the Kyerim Kŭkhoe (鷄林劇會 - Kyerim Theater Society) was established.

Last year <88>
postponed its first performance because several scripts like *Tanan'gi* (多難期) were disallowed (不許可).
Kang Ch'ŏn-hŭi, who was the advisor for the mobile small theater, again founded the Chungoe Kŭkchang (中外劇場 - Chungoe Theater). With film person Pak Che-haeng (朴齊行), Yun Pong-ssi (尹逢氏 - likely Yun Pong-ch'un), Ha Chi-man (河之滿) - who led the Oyang Kagŭktan (五洋歌劇團 - Oyang Opera Troupe) on a regional tour - and actresses such as Kim Sŏn-ch'o, Ko Yong-nyŏ (高龍女), Sin K'anariya (申카나리야 - Sin Canary), etc.
With scripts like *Sŏllo Kungbu-ŭi Chugŏm* (線路工夫의 죽엄 - Death of a Railway Worker), *Saram Choŭn Hyŏngsa* (사람조흔 형사 - The Kind Detective), *Sarang-i Kip'ŏ Kal Ttae* (사랑이 깁허 갈 때 - When Love Deepens), *Mich'in Nom* (밋친 놈 - Crazy Guy), etc., they held their first performance at the Minado-jwa Theater (미나도 座) in Kyŏngsŏng, but it disbanded after a short while.

Due to the sudden Manchurian Incident on September 18th last year, many compatriots suffered harm.
As donation campaigns for Manchurian compatriots were launched across Chosŏn (朝鮮 - Korea), a Manchurian Compatriot Relief Performance was held, featuring members of the old T'owŏrhoe (舊土月會 - Old Earth Moon Society), achieving great success. The members of the old T'owŏrhoe, having followed the path described above, dissolved the name T'owŏrhoe and, centering around Pak Sŭng-hŭi, organized the new theater troupe T'aeyang Kŭkchang (太陽劇場 - Sun Theater).
Script Department: Pak Sŭng-hŭi, Pak Chin (朴珍)
Directing Department: Yi So-yŏn (李素然), Yi Paek-su (李白水), Pak Che-haeng, Yang No-kŏn (梁路健), Sim Yŏng (沈影), etc.
Female Department: Sŏk Kŭm-sŏng (石金星), Kim Yŏn-sil, Kang Sŏk-yŏn (姜石燕), Kim Sŏn-yŏng, etc.
They held their first performance starting from the Lunar New Year at the Minado-jwa Theater in Kyŏngsŏng. At this time, in the regional city of Kaesŏng (開城), an organization named Kŭkch'ŏnji (劇天地 - Theater World) was organized.
With Hwang Paek-kang (黃白江) in the Script Department,
they held their first project, a theater and film exhibition and a lecture on dramatic arts, at the Kaesŏng Koryŏ Ch'ŏngnyŏn Hoegwan (開城 高麗 靑年會舘 - Kaesŏng Koryŏ Youth Hall).
In Hamhŭng (咸興), the leftist troupe Tongbuk Kŭkchang (東北劇場 - Northeast Theater) was founded, establishing a temporary office in Hasŏ-ri, Hamhŭng-bu (咸興府 荷西里), and preparing for a central performance. Key figures who worked hard at the Chungang Kŭkchang (中央劇場 - Central Theater), founded last December - Im Sŏ-bang (任曙昉), Ha Chi-man, Kim Sŏn-ch'o, Sin K'anariya, Ko Yong-nyŏ, etc. - established a temporary office at the Ch'ŏngjin Yŏgwan (淸進旅舘) in Ch'ŏngjin-dong (淸進洞), Kyŏngsŏng, organized the new troupe Yesuljwa (藝術座 - Art Theater), and left for a study tour of the Japanese art world. The Kŭgyesul Yŏn'guhoe of the Haeoe Munhakp'a held a two-week short course on women's dramatic arts, recruiting 30 members. Additionally, the Silyŏm Mudae, directly affiliated with the Kŭgyesul Yŏn'guhoe, announced its first performance for April and recruited its second batch of students - this time also recruiting a total of 30 male and female students.

The Yŏn'gŭksa, which had - in opposition to - the Yŏn'gŭk Sijang - finished its long run in Kyŏngsŏng and temporarily declined due to the departure of key figures, setting off on a regional tour to Inch'ŏn, enriched its content compared to before, finished its regional tour, and resolutely came up to the central location of Kyŏngsŏng. It began its inaugural commemorative performance at the Cheil Kŭkchang (第一劇場 - First Theater), renovated from the Minado-jwa. The Silyŏm Mudae, affiliated with the Kŭgyesul Yŏn'guhoe introducing overseas literature, was said to hold its first trial performance (試演), which had been in preparation, with Gogol's *The Inspector General* translated by Ham Tae-hun, on May 7th and 8th at the Hasegawa-chō Public Hall in Kyŏngsŏng, but the location and date were changed.
It completed its first trial performance over three days, May 4th, 5th, and 6th, at the Chosŏn Theater in Kyŏngsŏng. While announcing its second trial performance for early June, it recruited its third batch of students, teaching theory and practice of dramatic arts. The mobile troupe Kŭndae Kŭkchang (近代劇場 - Modern Theater) was founded, establishing a temporary office in Tayachō (茶屋町), Kyŏngsŏng, and preparing for a central performance. In Kaesŏng, an organization called Yŏnhwa Sidae (演化時代 - Drama Evolution Era) was formed by a gathering of several comrades. They held their first performance at the Kaesŏngjwa Theater (開城座), staging scripts like *Nŭkchi Mara Ch'ŏngch'un* (늙지 마라 청춘 - Don't Grow Old, Youth), *Chejip Sin-ŭi Chenal* (제집 신의 제날 - unclear title, possibly related to house gods), *Yŏ Samuwŏn* (여 사무원 - Female Clerk), etc., and set off on a regional tour. They were said to have returned and be preparing for a second performance, but news ceased - what happened is unknown. Then, in Ahyŏn-ri (阿現里), Kyŏngsŏng, the leftist troupe Mek'ap'on (메카폰 - Megaphone) was founded. It was said they would hold their first performance <89> for three days starting June 8th at the Chosŏn Theater in Kyŏngsŏng with the following scripts, but perhaps due to prohibition by the authorities (當局), the curtain closed after only one day, June 8th.
Sin Ku-song (申鼓頌) - *Mek'ap'on* 「Sprechchor (슈풀레이콜)」
Müller - *Die Weber* (荷車 - The Cart/Weavers, likely Hauptmann's *Die Weber* or a similar work)
Song Yŏng - *Hosinsul* (호신술 - Self-Defense)
Yu Chin-o - *Pak Ch'ŏmji* (朴僉知)
Kim Hyŏng-yong (金形容) - *Chiok* (지옥 - Hell), etc.

The Silyŏm Mudae, directly affiliated with the Kŭgyesul Yŏn'guhoe, which held its first trial performance on May 4th, 5th, and 6th for three days at the Chosŏn Theater, held its second trial performance from June 28th for three days at the Chosŏn Theater with the following scripts, closing the curtain with great success.
Script: Ervine - translated by Chang Ki-je - *Jane Clegg* (愛人 - Lover, likely *Jane Clegg* or similar)
Grigori (크리고리 - possibly Grigoryev?) - translated by Ch'oe Chŏng-u (崔정宇) - *Ok Mun* (獄門 - Prison Gate)
Pehling (페-링 - possibly Fehling?) - translated by Cho Yŏk-hŭi (曹譯喜) - *Haechŏn* (海戰 - Naval Battle)

The Mun'uhoe (文友會 - Literary Friends Society) of Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun Hakkyo (延禧專門學校 - Yonhi College), which performed a student play last November 27th, staged an outdoor play, Ibsen's *The Lady from the Sea*, on the school grounds on June 25th.

The leftist troupe Mek'ap'on, which finished its first performance at the Chosŏn Theater in Kyŏngsŏng, set off on a regional tour to P'yŏngyang (平壤). While preparing for its second performance there, word came that it had returned to Inch'ŏn and was preparing for its second performance at the Inch'ŏn Aegwan (仁川愛舘). In Masan (馬山), the Kŭgyesa (劇藝社 - Theater Arts Company) was organized by a gathering of several comrades. In Kaesŏng, there was the leftist troupe Taejung Kŭkchang (大衆劇場 - Masses Theater).
With scripts like *Chŏng'ŭi wa K'ambisŭ* (정의와 캄비스 - Justice and Cambyses), *Hach'a* (荷車 - The Cart/Weavers), *Chojŏngan* (조정안 - Arbitration Proposal), *Pak Ch'ŏmji* (朴僉知), etc., they were preparing for their first performance, but it was stopped due to disapproval (不許可) from the authorities. When Taejung Kŭkchang stopped its performance, several comrades who worked at the same theater gathered to form the Kaesŏng So Kŭkchang (開城小劇場 - Kaesŏng Small Theater) and prepared to perform again, but this too was deemed largely the same (大同小異) as Taejung Kŭkchang, and the assembly was prohibited (集會 禁止). Then again, those comrades gathered to form the Sinhyŏng Kŭkchang (新型劇場 - New Type Theater) and were preparing to perform, but this too was disbanded due to assembly prohibition by the authorities.

Yesuljwa, founded last March, which had gone on a study tour of the Japanese art world, returned to Kyŏngsŏng after finishing the tour, skipped the central performance, finished one performance in Inch'ŏn, came to Kaesŏng, finished a performance at the Kaesŏngjwa, returned to Kyŏngsŏng, and merged with Sin Mudae. This is what is called the Hyŏptong Sin Mudae (協同 新 舞臺 - Cooperative New Stage). Yŏn'gŭk Sijang, which held its first performance in the central location of Kyŏngsŏng immediately after its founding, disbanded while on a regional tour due to the departure of many key figures. Its leader, Mun Su-il himself, joined the Yŏn'gŭksa, and several other actors joined Sin Mudae (Cooperative).

T'aeyang Kŭkchang finished its central performance and set off on a regional tour, and there has been no news since.

In Sungil-dong (崇一洞), Kyŏngsŏng, at the Chiptansa (集團社 - Collective Company), the proletarian theater troupe Sin Kŏnsŏl (新建設 - New Construction) was founded and was preparing for its first performance under the departments listed below.
Acting Department (Female): Yi Chŏng-ja (李貞子), Yi Kwi-rye (李貴禮), Ham Kyŏng-suk (咸景淑)

Literature Department: Song Yŏng, Kwŏn Hwan (權煥)

In Iri (裡里), the new troupe Yŏnyangsa (硏揚舍) was organized. Yŏnhŭi Chŏnmun Hakkyo, which performed an outdoor play on its school grounds last June, also presented an English play by the liberal arts students (文科生 英語劇) on November 19th.
Scripts: Thomas Hardy - *The Three Strangers* (三怪人)
John Millington Synge - *Riders to the Sea* (谷影 - Shadow in the Valley, likely *Riders to the Sea*)

The Yŏnjŏn Mun'uhoe held its second drama event on the 10th at the Hasegawa-chō Public Hall, staging this year's Nobel Literature laureate Galsworthy's *Justice* (正義). Hyŏngje-jwa's (형제 座 - Brothers Troupe) Hyŏn Sŏng-wan (玄聖完) troupe is diligently working on a regional tour with several serial plays (連鎖劇).

**Summary**

This 1933 article by Han Chi-su provides a whirlwind tour of the Korean theater scene primarily during 1932-1933. It chronicles the constant flux of theater troupes in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) and other regions like Inch'ŏn, Kaesŏng, and Hamhŭng. The author details the formation, merging, and dissolution of numerous groups (Yŏn'gŭksa, Yŏn'gŭk Sijang, Sin Mudae, T'aeyang Kŭkchang, Yesuljwa, Mek'ap'on, etc.), listing key personnel (actors, directors, writers), their performance venues (Tansŏngsa, Chosŏn Kŭkchang, Minado-jwa), and specific plays staged, including both original Korean works and translations of Western and Russian classics (Tolstoy, Shakespeare, Chekhov, Galsworthy, Ibsen, O'Neill). The article also notes the active participation of student groups (from Yonhi, Ewha, Paejae, Posŏng colleges) and the presence of leftist or proletarian troupes (Mek'ap'on, Sin Kŏnsŏl, Tongbuk Kŭkchang, Taejung Kŭkchang), several of which faced censorship and performance prohibitions (不許可, 集會 禁止) by the authorities (當局). The author concludes by lamenting the disappearance of several prominent actors from the scene.

For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of the vibrant but unstable cultural landscape of colonial Korea. The rapid turnover of troupes suggests economic precarity and perhaps political pressures. The explicit mention of leftist troupes and subsequent censorship highlights the Government-General's control over cultural expression. The mix of Korean and translated Western plays reveals contemporaneous artistic interests and the ongoing negotiation between nationalist sentiment, modern artistic trends, and colonial realities. The mention of a relief performance for Manchurian compatriots connects the theater world directly to the geopolitical turmoil following the Manchurian Incident.
