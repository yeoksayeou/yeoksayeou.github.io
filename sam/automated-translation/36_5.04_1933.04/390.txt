Article Title: 八道處女氣質 (Temperaments of Maidens from the Eight Provinces)
Author: 림원근 (Rim Wŏn-gŭn) [Kyŏnggi-do Section], 朴亨寬 (Pak Hyŏng-gwan) [P'yŏngan-do Section]
Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Questionnaire Response)

八道處女氣質
(Temperaments of Maidens from the Eight Provinces)

Gentle and Affectionate (Kyŏnggi-do Edition)
Rim Wŏn-gŭn

"What is the temperament of Kyŏnggi-do women like?" you ask.
Well now. What should I say in response? It's quite awkward to speak on such a topic. If I say the slightest thing wrong, I'll needlessly get cursed out three generations back by the lovely young ladies, and if not that, I might get scolded from other quarters for praising my own region.
However, there’s no particular need to be overly concerned with such minor constraints. Whatever others may curse or praise, regardless, I intend to give an honest answer as I have seen and know it.
Originally, my birthplace is Kaesŏng (開城).
However, I left my hometown from a young age and grew up in Sŏul from my elementary school days. For that reason, in this place where I am to speak of the temperament of Kyŏnggi-do women, I will specifically choose Sŏul women as representatives. As Sŏul is the capital here, many people from various regions and walks of life gather, and their observations, what they see and hear, will likely differ from ten people to ten ends. Therefore, the various sentiments, observations, and critiques regarding Sŏul women will likely have considerable differences.
However, those I have seen—their temperament, so to speak—is very good and beautiful. To put it in one word, one could say they possess, to a considerable degree, the various conditions a woman ought to have. —Perhaps this is a stereotypically feudal notion— Firstly, their temperament is exceedingly gentle, simple, abundant in human warmth (인정미), and also bright in reason (理智).
It's true that saying this feels a bit apologetic, but in reality, when encountering Sŏul women after meeting women from Hamgyŏng-do, P'yŏngan-do, or the Kyŏngsang-do regions, there are many times one feels as if they have moved almost from interacting with a man to interacting with a woman.<99> As they live in the city, rubbing shoulders with people and being worn down by social interactions, their 'speaking skills' (말솜씨) and 'mannerisms' (몸맵시) and the like must have become much more refined and polished, but still, without their innate disposition (천품), how could they be so beautiful?
Sŏul women often shed tears easily. There is the saying, "Tears are a woman's weapon," but generally, women of this region are quick to sorrow and quick to joy. The reason is evidence that their emotions are better developed and they possess much human warmth. Also, their speaking skills are remarkably good. Whether it's haggling with various side-dish vendors at their gates morning and evening, employing 100 percent humor, or turning away a debt-collecting visitor who dashes in before breakfast, or the scene of making a fuss (허덜갑) upon meeting a welcome guest, everything seems quite plausible.
However, Kyŏnggi-do—Sŏul women—lack thickness in their character as human beings. Somehow, one corner seems empty, and they are somewhat less trustworthy (미덤성). In the very spot where they sat, it seems like honey dripped from their mouths, but turn around, and within ten minutes, the scent of motherwort (육모초) emerges. It's deeply regrettable to only speak ill like this, but they also possess considerable vanity and love luxury (사치). Even if they go home to find nothing for dinner and maybe bedbugs bite doubly hard, when they go out on the street, they disturb the eye (이묵) plausibly with cheap artificial silk (인조견) (which actually counts as luxury). So, in the end, if you weigh the good and the bad, which side is heavier?…
Rest assured. Naturally, the good and beautiful side is much heavier. Firstly, Sŏul women manage households well as homemakers (가뎡 주부). Needlework (침선), cooking (료리), and all etiquette (범절) regarding hosting guests (대인 접객) absolutely do not permit following by women from other regions. Furthermore, their speech is fine and clean, and added to that, their heart, fine as silk, is very considerate, amiable, detailed, and affectionate. Therefore, when I meet my bachelor friends, I recommend they marry a Sŏul lady. Just thoughts as they come, the good and the bad—Oh, Kyŏnggi-do Sŏul ladies, please don't be too angry!

Tough and Tenacious (P'yŏngan-do Edition)
Pak Hyŏng-gwan

The saying "Namnam Puknyŏ" (Men from the South, Women from the North) has existed since old times, and indeed, P'yŏngan-do women, compared to those from the three southern provinces or the northern Hamgyŏng provinces (북선), tend to have many beauties. However, this is an undeniable fact based on appearance and physique; it does not mean their temperament is also superior to women of other provinces.
So, what is the temperament of P'yŏngan-do women like? This will differ according to each person's observation. Perhaps what I intend to write about the temperament of P'yŏngan-do women might be the exact opposite of their actual temperament. However, since this is the temperament of P'yŏngan-do women as I have seen it, even if there are points contrary to the temperament of P'yŏngan-do women, I still consider it valid. Why? Because I do not think there should be a reason why another person's observation and my observation must necessarily be the same.
Firstly, P'yŏngan-do women have few womanly points. Their vocal cords (성대) are high, so their speech lacks softness, and no matter how affectionate the words spoken, one cannot feel an affectionate taste (정다운 맛). Conversely, once they get angry, it is truly a thunderbolt from a clear sky (청천의 벽력). This can be seen when they fight with their husbands or scold their children (아히들). If they cannot persuade the opponent with words, there are many cases where they resort to force to finally obtain submission (항복).
Secondly, P'yŏngan-do women tend to be dull regarding etiquette (례절). Women from Sŏul or other provinces never take off their skirts except when sleeping, but P'yŏngan-do women spend much time indoors without their skirts on. Also, once they are middle-aged or older, they often go to nearby houses without their skirts on. This can be seen as a type of custom, but I think it has much relation (관게) to the woman's temperament.
Thirdly, P'yŏngan-do women are fierce/violent (强暴). If a child does something wrong (Continued on page 68)<100> they resort to beating (매질). Scolding or beating an innocent child for something that could easily be taught with a smile is partly related to a lack of refinement (교양), but because their original temperament is fierce, they must see the child's tears.
These three points are only the unfavorable aspects of the P'yŏngan-do woman's temperament, but there are also points where they surpass the temperament of women from other places.
First, P'yŏngan-do women possess abundant frugality (근검성). Though beauty (미) is said to be a woman's life, P'yŏngan-do women do not know how to apply makeup (화장) to make their beauty more beautiful, nor do they wear silk clothes (비단 옷) to make their bodies more splendid (화려하게). They do not enjoy luxury (사치) beyond a degree appropriate to their standard of living (생활정도). Not only that, but if it is work they can do with their own strength, they never borrow another's hand. Therefore, in P'yŏngan-do, hiring a housemaid (식모) is rare, and the so-called *haengnang* (行廊 - outer servant's quarters, implying resident servants) does not exist.
Second, P'yŏngan-do women are active (활동적).
When inside, they naturally do women's work like needlework (침공), laundry (세탁), cooking (식사), cleaning (소뎨), etc., and whenever they have spare time, they help their husbands. For their husband's rest (휴식), they bravely (용감히) do any kind of work. If they go up the mountain, they chop wood, and if they go to the market, they drive the cattle (소도). In this way, they are not just the homemaker (주부) of a family but also workers (일꾼) not inferior to men.
Third, P'yŏngan-do women are upright/strong-willed (强直).
As they are abundantly frugal and active, they are upright. They know how to fight bravely (싸홀 줄) if something harms them, but they do not know how to deceive (속힐 줄) others with cunning words (간사한 말) and skillful means (교묘한 수단). Therefore, they live their lives solely by their own strength and never try to live by cunning (간교) and flattery (아첨).
Now, I have listed three good things and three bad things about the temperament of P'yŏngan-do women. There might be more unique temperaments beyond these, but from what I see, there is nothing more to add. Thus, I believe that while P'yŏngan-do women might be unsuitable as affectionate wives (안해) for a sweet (달큼한) home, as homemakers (주부) who can support a family (일가), they are the best among Korean women. <68>

**Summary**

This article presents contrasting descriptions of women from Kyŏnggi Province (specifically Sŏul) and P'yŏngan Province in 1933. Kyŏnggi/Sŏul women, according to Rim Wŏn-gŭn, are portrayed as gentle, emotionally expressive, refined in speech and manner, and skilled homemakers, though potentially superficial, vain, and less trustworthy. Pak Hyŏng-gwan describes P'yŏngan women as beautiful but less traditionally feminine, loud, blunt in manner, sometimes harsh, but also exceptionally frugal, hardworking, active in supporting the family economically, and strong-willed or upright. For historians, this article is interesting as it explicitly documents and perpetuates regional stereotypes prevalent during the Japanese colonial period. It reflects contemporary discourse on ideal femininity, contrasting the urban refinement of Sŏul women with the perceived practical strength and resilience of women from the North, touching upon themes of modernization (city life influence), gender roles within the household and economy, and enduring regional identities within Korea.

八道處女氣質
부드럽고 情다워 (京畿道 篇)
림원근

『경긔도 녀성들의 긔질은 엇더하냐?』고요.
글세올시다. 무엇이라고 대답을 하여야 조흘는지요. 도모지 이런 문뎨는 말슴하기가 거복하여서요. 조곰 무엇하다가는 공연히 까닭업시 어엽분 아가씨들에게로부터 삼탭이 욕을 먹겟고 또 그러치 안으면 제 지방 칭찬을 한다고 다른 방면의 꾸중이 잇슬 모양이니까요.
그러나 그러한 적은 구애에 그다지 관심할 필요야 별로히 업슬 일이지요. 남들이야 무엇이라 욕을 하던지 칭찬을 하던지 엇지하엿던 나는 나 본대로 내가 아는 그대로 솔직한 대답을 하렴니다.
본래 나는 출생디가 개성(開城)임니다.
그러나 어려서부터 고향을 떠나서 소학생 시대부터 서울서 잘아낫슴니다. 그런 까닭에 나는 경긔도 녀성들의 긔질을 말슴하려는 이 자리에서 특히 서울 녀성을 대표로 선택하겟슴니다. 서울은 이곳의 수도인 것 만치 각 디방으로부터 각 방면의 사람들이 만히 모혀 들어 잇고 그들의 보는 바 듯는 바의 관찰이 열 사람 열 끗으로 다 각긔 불일 할 것임으로 서울 녀성에 대하는 여러 가지 감정과 관찰과 비판이 모다 어지간한 차이가 잇슬 것임니다.
그러나 내가 본 그들―그들의 긔질이랄 것은 매우 조코 아름다운 것이외다. 한말로 하여 바린다면 그들은 녀성으로서의 가저야 될 여러 가지 조건을 어지간히 가초어 가젓다고 말할 수 잇슴니다. ―상투의 봉건뎍 관렴이랄는지는 모르지마는―첫재로 그들의 긔질은 몹시 부드럽고 담백하고 인정미가 풍부하고 그리고 또 리지(理智)에 밝슴니다.
이러케 말슴하면 좀 미안스럽은 점도 잇는 것이 사실이지마는 실상 함경도나 평안도 혹은 경상도 방면의 녀성을 대하다가 서울 녀성을 대하면 거의 남성에게로부터 녀성에게로 옴겨온 것 갓흔＜99＞ 늣김을 밧는 때가 만슴니다. 그것이야 도회생활을 하고 잇는 것 만치 사람에 부댁기고 사교에 싀달니어 『말솜씨』 『몸맵시』갓흔 것도 훨신 세랜되고 쟁화되엿슬 것이지마는 그래도 자긔네들의 천품이 아니고서야 엇더케 그러케 아름다울 수가 잇겟슴니가.
서울 녀성들은 걸핏하면 눈물을 잘 흘님니다. 『눈물은 녀자의 무긔』라는 말도 잇지마는 도대체 이 디방 녀성들은 잘 슬퍼하고 잘 깃버함니다. 그 까닭은 감정이 보다 잘 발달되고 인정이 만흔 증거이외다. 그리고 또 말솜씨가 긔막히게 좃슴니다. 아츰 저녁으로 가지 각색의 반찬 장사들을 붓들어 가지고 자긔 문전에서 100퍼센트 유-모를 써가면서 눙란한 수작건늬는 것이라던지 조반 전에 달녀든 빗쟁이 손님을 따돌니는 것이라든지 반가운 손님을 만나서 『허덜갑』을 부리는 광경이라던지 모도가 다 그럴 듯 함니다.
그러나 경긔도―서울 녀성들 인간으로서의 선(線)이 굵지 못함니다. 어듸로인지 한편 구석이 뷔인 것 갓고 좀 미덤성이 적슴니다. 금방 안즌 자리에서는 입에 꿀을 담아 부는 듯하나 돌아 안저 십분만 되면 육모초 냄새가 남니다. 이것 작고 이러케 험담만해서 즈윽히 미안함니다마는 그들은 또 어지간히 사치를 조와하는 허영심이 만슴니다. 집에 드러가면 비록 저녁거리가 업고 빈대피 되배는 하엿슬게라도 지리에 나오면 그럴 듯이 갑싼 인조견(실은 사치도로 되지요)으로 이묵을 어지럽게 함니다. 그러면 결국은 조코 낫본 것을 저울을 달아보면 엇던 편이 묵업단 말이야요?...
