Sounding Out Feelings: Do Female Students Desire Birth Control? Sounding Out the Feelings of This Spring's Graduates

One day, this reporter visited a certain girls' higher common school (某女子高等普通學) in the city and asked the students—young women who would receive the honor of graduation this spring and take their first steps into society—to cover the following questions and provide answers.

Out of 120 students, more than half responded, "I don't even know the meaning of the question," and among the remaining young women, about 38 blushed and lowered their heads.

It's understandable, as even now in Korea (朝鮮), anyone feels somewhat hesitant and embarrassed to talk about "birth control" (産兒制限). As such, the response from more than half the students, "I don't even know the meaning of the question," was not entirely unexpected.

Rather than explaining to the students who didn't know, the reporter closely approached the students whose faces were blushing and asked whether they approved or disapproved of birth control.

While some students didn't even understand the meaning of the question, don't be surprised, even answers the reporter hadn't anticipated—surprising and clever ones—began to emerge.

Let's listen to the individual answers of these young women.

"In a true sense, I see it as absolutely necessary. I believe it is one's duty in life to have few healthy children and raise them with all one's might. Even speaking economically, having fewer children is considered a very good strategy."

"It's unpleasant for poor people to have many children. I think parents should have children only to the extent that they can provide them with <97> responsible education. Rather than raising many children haphazardly, raising fewer children well would be a good thing. I know this would be happiness not only for the children but also for the parents."

"Having too many children is economically 불안한 (unstable). Also, since it's easy for them to be crudely produced and excessively made (粗製濫造), raising fewer children with careful attention is better, as eugenics (優生學) has taught us," was another surprising answer.

Besides these, there were answers like, "In a poor country like present-day Korea, where the food problem is complicated, I wish people would have as few children as possible."

Also, "If there are many children, naturally there will be a lot of work, and I feel I wouldn't be able to help my husband with his work," and "Isn't it a bit burdensome to become a mother soon after getting married? At the very least, two or three years should pass after marriage." "Unless there are economic difficulties or the mother's body is physiologically weak, it can't be helped, but doing it for pleasure (享樂) would be wrong." "It is absolutely necessary only for legitimate married couples." "I know it is necessary to some extent for the proletarian class." Their answers were quite earnest (眞劍).

Among them, the most amusing were responses like, "If there are absolutely no harmful effects," or "If things go as planned," and also a demure young woman who coolly remarked, "It would be fine to do it after having about five or six children." More than half of the students, as if they had just understood, chimed in with a word or two from here and there.

Arguments of the Opponents

While there were answers like, "I know birth control has the harmful effect of making humans easily tend towards hedonism and easily leading to moral disorder (風긔문란)," there was also a student who showed a vigorous attitude, powerfully stating, "As a woman, I absolutely oppose it." In any case, countless stories flowed unrestrained from their mouths. <98>

**Summary**

This 1933 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine surveys graduating female students at a Seoul girls' school about their views on birth control (産兒制限, *sana chehan*). Initially, many claimed ignorance or showed embarrassment, reflecting societal taboos. However, those who responded expressed diverse opinions, with many favoring birth control for economic reasons, better child-rearing, parental happiness, national poverty (food scarcity in Korea), allowing women to support husbands, and delaying motherhood. Some even cited eugenics (優生學, *usaenghak*) as a justification. Opposition stemmed from concerns about hedonism and moral decline. The article highlights a range of surprisingly modern and pragmatic views among young educated women, alongside traditional reluctance and moral concerns. For historians, this piece offers a valuable glimpse into the evolving social consciousness of educated Korean women under Japanese colonial rule, particularly their engagement with modern concepts like family planning, eugenics, and women's roles, juxtaposed against prevailing conservative norms. The mention of eugenics and economic hardship specific to Korea provides context about ideologies and conditions influencing personal choices during that period.

心境打診, 여학생들은 産兒制限을 所願하나, 今春 卒業生의 心境打診
엇든 날 기자는 시내 某女子高等普通學에 차저가서 今春에 졸업의 광영을 가지고 사회에 첫발길을 내여 노흐려는 生徒-처녀들에게 다음과 갓흔 문제에 대해서 덥허 놋코 대답해 달나고 말해 보앗다.
120명 중 반수 이상이 「나는 질문의 의미조차 모름니다」라는 대답이엿고 그 남어지 처녀들의 38명 쯤은 얼골이 붉히며 머리를 숙엿다.
그도 그럴박게 아직 우리 朝鮮에 잇서서는 「産兒制限」에 대하야 이약이 하는 것은 누구나 좀 주저해 하고 또 붓그러운 생각을 가지게 된다. 그럴만큼 반수 이상의 생도가 「나는 질문의 의미조차 모르겟다」는 대답도 그리 의외는 아니엿다.
기자는 모른다는 생도들에게 설명하기보다 얼골빗 붉어지는 생도를 압헤 밧삭 닥아서며 産兒制限의 贊否를 물엇다.
질문의 의미조차 모르는 생도가 잇는 한 편에는 놀나지 말어라. 기자도 생각지 못햇든 신긔하고 교묘한 대답까지 터저 나오게 되엿다.
이제 우리는 그 어린 처녀들의 하나하나의 대답을 드러보기로 하자.
「진정한 의미에서 반다시 필요하다고 봄니다. 건강한 자녀를 적게 나어서 전력을 다햐야 길느는 것이 인생의 할 바 임무라고 보아 짐니다. 경제상으로 말하드래도 적게 낫는다는 것은 퍽으나 조흔 방책이라고 생각해요」
「가난뱅이 아희 만히 낫는 것은 실흔 일이지요. 부모로서 자녀에게＜97＞ 책임잇는 교육을 식힐 수 잇을만 정도에서 자녀를 나어야 하겟다고 생각함니다. 만흔 자식을 아모러케나 길느기 보다 적은 아희 잘 길느는 것이 조흔 일이겟지요. 이것은 그 자식에게 뿐 아니라 부모의 행복도 될 것일 줄 아러요」
「너무 자식이 만타는 것은 경제상으로 불안한 일이외다. 또 粗製濫造되기 쉬운 일임으로 적게 나어서 주의를 다해 길으는 것이 좃타는 것은 優生學으로서 우리에게 가르처 주엇슴니다」라는 놀나운 대답도 잇섯다.
그 외에도 「현재 조선과 갓치 가난한 나라에는 식량문제가 복잡하게 되여 잇는데서는 될 수 잇는대로 적게 낫키를 원함니다」
또 아희가 만흐면 자연히 일이 만케 되여서 남편의 일을 도아 못줄 것 갓해」라는 대답 「결혼해서 곳 어린애 어머니 노릇하기는 좀 거복한 일이 아닐가요. 적어도 결혼해서 2, 3년은 지나야 되겟지요」 경제적으로 곤란하다든가 생리적으로 모체가 약하든지 하지 안는 경우에는 할 수 업지만은 享樂을 위해서 한다는 것은 잘못이겟지요」 「정당한 부부에 한해서는 반다시 필요함니다」 무산계급에는 어느 정도까지 필요한 줄 알아요」 그들의 대답은 퍽으나 眞劍하엿다.
그 중에도 제일 우수을 것은 「절대로 엇던 페해가 업다면」이라든지 「생각대로 된다면」 또 「다섯이나 여섯 쯤 난 뒤에 해도 좃치」하고 새침을 똑 떼는 처녀도 잇섯다. 반수 이상의 생도들 중에도 그제야 알엇채엿다는 듯이 이쪽 저쪽에서 한 마듸 두 마듸 말을 던젓다.

反對者의 理論
「産兒制限은 인간이 향락적에 흐르기 쉽고 또 풍긔문란하게 되기 쉬운 페해가 잇을 줄 암니다」라는 대답도 잇섯지만 「여자로서는 단연 반대할 일임니다」라고 힘잇게 대답하는 씩씩한 태도를 보혀 준 생도도 잇섯다. 엇잿든 그들 입에서는 제한 업시 만흔 이약이가 흘너 나왓다. ＜98＞
