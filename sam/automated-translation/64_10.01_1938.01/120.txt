China and the Nature of Composite States

According to recent newspaper reports, the populace of North China is said to be eagerly awaiting the establishment of a new regime. The form of this targeted new regime is reported to be something like a composite state encompassing North China and Central China.

A composite state indicates a state of national union that is contrary to a single, independent state. To list its types:
1. Protectorate relationship
2. Suzerain-Vassal state relationship
3. Personal Union relationship
4. Confederation of States
5. Federation
These five types can be mentioned.

Protectorate Relationship
Examples include the relationship between France and Tunis or Annam, or the Japan-Korea relationship from Meiji 39 (1906) to Meiji 40 (1907) [Note: This likely refers to the period leading up to the 1910 annexation, specifically the protectorate established in 1905].

Suzerain-Vassal State
A vassal state (附庸國) is also called a retainer state (陪臣國家) or a tributary state (공납국가). Famous examples include the early relationship between Rome and its conquered territories, and the relationship between Turkey and Austria up until the World War.

Personal Union Relationship
This is a relationship of union resulting from a monarch coincidentally or legally (by agreement of each country) reigning over two or more countries. Actual examples include the case of the union of Spain and Great Britain [Note: This seems inaccurate; likely referring to earlier unions involving Spain or Britain separately, or perhaps the Hanoverian succession in Britain] or the relationship between Austria and Hungary up until the World War.

Confederation of States
Aimed at preserving the territory of the union and maintaining peace between member states. Actual examples include the Confederation of the Rhine from 1806 to 1813, and the North American league of 13 states from 1776 to 1787.

Federation
This refers to state forms such as America, Germany, Switzerland, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, the Soviet Union, Canada, South Africa, and so on.

What is a Restoration (復辟) Movement?
According to recent special foreign telegrams, a movement is reportedly occurring in a part of Europe to support the former Spanish Crown Prince, His Highness Don Fran [likely Infante Juan, Count of Barcelona], and realize a Spanish Restoration.
Restoration (復辟) means the return of the monarch (辟, meaning sovereign) to their position (位). The term originates from the phrase "伊尹旣政厥辟" (Yi Yin administered the government for his sovereign) in the "Cheng You Yi De" (成有一德篇) chapter within the Chinese book called the Classic of History (書經).
A famous example of restoration in China was the Qing Dynasty revival movement that occurred through ** [text missing/obscured?] in July of the 6th year of the Republic [1917]. This movement declared a restoration by supporting the Xuantong Emperor (宣統帝), but because the restoration army was defeated and retreated, it ended in failure within less than ten days (旬日). <19>

**Summary**

This article from 1938 explains the concept of a "composite state," prompted by reports of discussions about such a political structure for North and Central China. It categorizes composite states into five types—protectorates, suzerain-vassal states, personal unions, confederations, and federations—providing historical examples for each. Separately, it defines "restoration" (復辟), tracing its etymology to the Chinese Classics and citing contemporary efforts in Spain and a failed attempt to restore the Qing Dynasty in China in 1917. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its use of the term "Shina" (支那) for China, common in Japanese discourse at the time, and its discussion of state forms like protectorates (mentioning the Japan-Korea relationship) and potential composite states in China, which reflects the geopolitical context of Japanese expansionism and the formation of puppet states (like Manchukuo) during the Second Sino-Japanese War. The specific mention of the Japan-Korea relationship (1906-1907) under the protectorate example is particularly revealing of the contemporary understanding and justification of colonial relationships.

1776년부터 1787년까지의 북아메리카 13주연맹이 그 실례이다.

연방
