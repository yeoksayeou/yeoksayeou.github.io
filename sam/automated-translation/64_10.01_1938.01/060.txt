Article Title: 戰時中70만 天道敎大衆은 어데로 가나, 新舊兩派首領의 見解 (Where Are the 700,000 Ch'ŏndogyo Followers Headed in Wartime? Views of the New and Old Faction Leaders)
Article Type: 대담·좌담 (Interview/Discussion)

Where Are the 700,000 Ch'ŏndogyo Followers Headed in Wartime? Views of the New and Old Faction Leaders
Ch'ŏndogyo! Ch'ŏndogyo! Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) draws public attention for various reasons. Especially now, in wartime, the direction of Ch'ŏndogyo, which boasts 700,000 followers, attracts much attention both domestically and internationally. We decided to hear about the aspirations from the leaders of both the New and Old factions.

1.  Will the New and Old Factions Reunite?
2.  The Path Forward for the New and Old Factions Regarding the Current Situation

Opinion of Mr. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟) of the New Faction
- According to what we've heard, the New and Old Factions of Ch'ŏndogyo are going to reunite. Is this true?
- Yes, I believe such words were reported in the newspaper the other day, but that was discussion between individuals, and there has been no consultation between the organizations themselves about reunification. However, the thought of reuniting has been around for a long time.

- There was talk of holding a General Assembly of both Ch'ŏndogyo parties (天道敎新舊兩黨總大會) in December to achieve reunification, is that not the case?
- Talk of holding a party assembly in December is baseless, but it is true that the top leaders of both factions are making their utmost effort to reunite as quickly and smoothly as possible. Whether looking at the religion itself or the current situation, reunification is seen as appropriate.

- Then, is it fair to say the leaders of both factions have practically already agreed to reunite?
- Everything concerning the time of the split was well known to the world back then, and things were reported in newspapers and magazines beyond the actual facts, so I don't wish to speak of it anew now. But even back then, there wasn't a major cause; it was mainly an emotional issue. Human emotions naturally dissipate with the flow of time, so the top leaders have already been converging for some time. However, as it involves 600,000 to 700,000 followers, splitting and reuniting is not something that happens easily. It must be carefully considered so that those 600,000 to 700,000 followers can reunite wholeheartedly without any dissatisfaction, so it's not an easy task. I did not participate in the recent discussion about reunification, so I cannot know all that was said then, but I believe they exchanged the thoughts they usually held. We cannot guarantee the completion of reunification based on that. It was merely a promise to strive for reunification together.

- Have there been any discussions regarding the internal structure after reunification?
- Of course not. Whether reunification will happen or not is still uncertain, so how can such things be discussed? Those are all matters to be dealt with after reunification. Therefore, the prerequisite issue is solely making reunification happen. That too will only proceed if it goes well; if it doesn't proceed smoothly, it can't be helped.

- What is the current total asset value of Ch'ŏndogyo, and how is it managed?
- Currently, the assets are worth around one million won. A portion is jointly managed by both factions, and the rest is managed separately by each. The difference between the current two factions probably just lies in handling administrative affairs separately.

- Regarding the recent Japan-China Incident (日支事變), how does Ch'ŏndogyo intend to serve?
- Since the declaration of war was not made in this Japan-China Incident, it is called an incident, but in reality, it is a war. In wartime, the nation must undergo total mobilization and exert all its strength solely for the victory of the war. In terms of personnel, regardless of gender or age, and in terms of quality, regardless of whether it's state-owned or privately owned, everything must be utilized for the war. Therefore, Ch'ŏndogyo also intends to serve the nation in that spirit.

- Then, by what method do you intend to make contributions? Do you not plan to purchase and donate things like anti-aircraft guns (高射砲)?
- I cannot really approve of donating an airplane or buying an anti-aircraft gun. Donating an airplane isn't about directly buying and giving an airplane, but donating the value of one airplane, so ultimately, I believe it amounts to nothing more than giving that much money. Therefore, we in Ch'ŏndogyo intend to vary our methods according to the future progress of the incident in this crisis called war. The contributions made by individual followers so far and by the religion as a whole have not been small, but I believe we will do even more in the future. However, we in Ch'ŏndogyo have served in various ways and will continue to do so, but we know we must never act out of self-interest (功利心) and intend to ensure we do not. In other words, we will ensure there are no actions like donating an airplane hoping the nation or society will recognize it that much, or expecting a reward for having done so. It must be done with the mindset that the nation's affairs are the people's affairs, and the nation's disaster is my personal disaster. Let's say, for example, a fire breaks out in one's own house. At such a time, the desperate struggle to extinguish the fire is not an act stemming from any self-interest; it is seen as an action welling up from the inner heart, an inability to endure without doing so. Likewise, we should not act out of self-interest, seeking some reward or a certain amount of honor by doing such things, but simply do our utmost with sincerity (眞心盡力) to overcome this crisis.

- During such wartime, is there no intention to have Koreans also bear military service obligations?
- Military service obligation is a matter of the state's system, so it cannot be done immediately. I believe it is good to engage in military duties as non-soldiers. That is, organizing and actively participating in Defense Corps (防護團) and Air Defense Corps (防空團) is engaging in military duties. Military duty is not only about attacking the enemy front line; defending against enemy raids in the homeland is also complete military duty. Therefore, I don't think it's necessary to specifically dispatch troops (出兵). So, issues like dispatching troops are thought to be handled according to future developments. Victory in war is not achieved solely by dispatched soldiers (出征兵); it is through home front support (銃後後援) that <12> dispatched soldiers can fight even more bravely and win. Therefore, the people on the home front (銃後), regardless of wealth, poverty, gender, or age, should sincerely fulfill their loyalty according to their duties. The rich will contribute as the rich, and the poor will fulfill their loyalty within their means. Among them, the rich should make significant material contributions during this national crisis. No one among the people receives as much protection from the state as the rich. It may not be an exaggeration to say that many state facilities are primarily centered around the rich. In such times, it is deeply regrettable that the sincerity of the rich is less than that of the poor. If the incident worsens further in the future, I believe their voluntary support will increase. Media organizations, at such times, should correct the misguided thoughts of the populace and ensure the entire nation focuses solely on overcoming the national crisis as one.
Recently, some people worry that Japan will face economic collapse if the Japan-China Incident continues, but I believe there is absolutely no possibility of that. During the World War, Great Britain spent 7.4 billion pounds (75 billion won) over four years without any issue, so I believe even if Japan spends around 10 to 15 billion won on military expenses for a year, there will be no impact. Therefore, we in Ch'ŏndogyo intend to provide home front support, changing methods according to the time, until the day this incident ends, no, until the day China completely surrenders, and we are doing so.

Opinion of Mr. Kwŏn Tong-jin (權東鎭) of the Old Faction
- They say the separated Ch'ŏndogyo is reuniting. When will that happen?
- I heard such talk from Mr. Ch'oe Chun-mo (崔俊模) some time ago, and later saw it in the newspaper, but I am not directly involved in that issue, nor am I particularly striving to make reunification happen. Therefore, I don't know how things have been progressing recently.

- Then, was it the New Faction that first proposed reunification this time?
- Yes, that's right. Actually, some time ago, Mr. Ch'oe Chun-mo came to see me and said that Mr. Ch'oe Sŏng-yŏn (崔성연) from the New Faction had come, unconditionally apologized for all past matters, and proposed reuniting, stating they would follow the Old Faction's lead. He asked for my opinion after consulting, saying he would then convey the Old Faction's view, asking what would be best. I replied, "How can we oppose reunification when the New Faction apologizes for everything and wants to reunite? However, Mr. Ch'oe Sŏng-yŏn is not a top leader of the New Faction, so we cannot know their side's opinion just by hearing his words. And we have suffered the bitter experience of reuniting once in the past only to split again, so it would be best to consider everything carefully to avoid any chance of failure." Then, a short while later, Mr. Ch'oe Chun-mo came again and said, "Mr. Ch'oe Sŏng-yŏn and Mr. Chŏng Kwang-jo (鄭廣朝) from the New Faction, and Mr. Yi Chong-nin (李鍾麟) and myself from the Old Faction, the four of us met at Mr. Chŏng Kwang-jo's house and discussed reunification again. The New Faction said that their past denial of Mr. Pak In-ho (朴寅浩) as the 4th Kyoju (教主) was done merely to cause the split, and they would, of course, recognize Mr. Pak In-ho as the 4th Kyoju and unconditionally follow the Old Faction in all other matters, asking us to work towards reunification. So, we agreed to try doing that." That's all I know about the New-Old Faction reunification issue.

- But sir, you seem unenthusiastic about the reunification issue. Why is that?
- Ha-ha- why would I enjoy seeing one religion split and dislike reunification? However, if we forcefully reunite something that cannot be reunited, leading to incessant internal strife or objections from the entire body of followers, then it's no better than not reuniting at all. The very first split occurred because the New Faction denied Mr. Pak In-ho as the 4th Kyoju. After that, Mr. Ch'oe Rin (崔麟) came to me and said that the past denial of Mr. Pak In-ho as the 4th Kyoju was done back then to cause the split, <13> not because of any other interpretation. He deeply apologized for the mistake and said they would recognize Mr. Pak In-ho as the 4th Kyoju, asking to reunite. Therefore, I too agreed at that time, saying let's tell Mr. Pak In-ho everything and then proceed with reunification, and so we reunited. However, not only did the provincial followers object, but even among the leadership, opinions did not harmonize. Furthermore, at the Party General Assembly (黨總大會) in December of the year we reunited, Mr. Ch'oe Rin and others again stated they could not accept Mr. Pak In-ho as Kyoju, leading to another split. When we split again, the anger of the provincial followers was beyond words. So, for a while, I had a major headache trying to calm things down again. Having tasted such bitter experiences, I don't really trust talks of reunification.
Therefore, if we reunite now only to split again, Ch'ŏndogyo will be ruined, so we cannot reunite so easily. If we are to reunite again this time, I believe not only the top leaders and the followers in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), but also the provincial followers must all be completely harmonized and come to desire reunification. So, the question is whether that will happen.

- If that's the case, isn't reunification quite uncertain?
- It's difficult. But I, too, hope for reunification as much as possible. My only hesitation stems from the past experience, worrying that we might split again.

- Have you, sir, ever met directly with representatives of the New Faction?
- I have not met them yet. I haven't seen Mr. Ch'oe Rin even once since the re-split. And I don't feel the need to meet him either.

- Regarding the recent North China Incident (北支事變), is Ch'ŏndogyo making special contributions, and does it plan to do so in the future?
- Ch'ŏndogyo as an organization will do what it can, and we intend to have individual followers contribute as much as their strength allows.

Opinion of Mr. Chŏng Kwang-jo (鄭廣朝) of the New Faction
- They say the separated Ch'ŏndogyo is reuniting again. Will it happen soon?
- No. I don't think it will be reunited that easily. The talk of reunification didn't come from formal meetings between the two factions; rather, individuals met recently, spoke of reuniting, and promised to strive for its realization in the future. The newspapers reported this, exaggerating beyond the facts. Newspapers always exaggerate facts, so I consider it normal. Of course, it is true that the top leaders of both sides are making their utmost efforts to achieve reunification through the heartfelt harmony of the 700,000 followers as soon as possible. However, since religion is emotional rather than intellectual, not everything can be achieved solely through theory and wisdom. In other words, while theory might dictate a certain outcome, reality sometimes turns out the opposite. Therefore, regarding the current reunification issue, we intend to carefully consider these emotional aspects and strive to make all followers sincerely call for the significance of reunification. The fact that we reunited once before and then split again is also seen as mainly due to emotional conflict. Having trodden that bitter path, we intend to ensure such things absolutely do not happen in the future and achieve a reunification meant for eternal unity. Thus, as discussed in the previous meeting, we have already sent people to the provinces to deeply instill the significance of reunification. Currently, it wouldn't be an exaggeration to say Ch'ŏndogyo is split into three factions rather than two. There is a faction centered in Sariwŏn (沙里院), which recently split off from the Old Faction. So, we sent people there too to explain the significance of reunification, and they seem to understand well. Things appear to be progressing smoothly given the current situation.

- After the split, do the New and Old Factions exist separately in the provinces as well?
- Places where both factions exist together include several locations like Sŏnch'ŏn (宣川), Yongch'ŏn (龍川), and Ch'ŏlsan (鐵山) in North P'yŏngan Province (平北). In other places, where the New Faction <14> exists, the Old Faction does not, and where the Old Faction exists, the New Faction does not. Therefore, there has never been a case of New and Old Factions clashing with each other in one place. In places like Sŏnch'ŏn, like in Kyŏngsŏng, they share an office in the same building and handle administrative tasks. So, the existence of two factions arguably boils down to just handling administrative affairs separately.

- If reunification happens in the future, since there were two Kyojus in the same Ch'ŏndogyo, will that naturally cause changes in the internal structure?
- Of course, if we reunite, there will be one Kyoju, and the internal structure will also change. However, we haven't had the leisure to discuss that issue yet; for now, we intend to devote all our efforts to achieving reunification. After reunification, I believe matters like the internal structure will be resolved without causing major difficulties, however they turn out.

- How does Ch'ŏndogyo intend to serve regarding the current Japan-China Incident?
- We haven't decided on a specific course of action, but rather intend to adopt the best measures according to the progression of the incident.

- There's a rumor that Ch'ŏndogyo will donate about one airplane. What about that?
- Rather than specifying airplanes or anti-aircraft guns, I believe it is right to provide home front support (銃後後援) with utmost sincerity (誠心盡力). Our followers have donated according to their duties and means, and we intend to encourage them to do even more in the future. Our New Faction, as the Ch'ŏndogyo New Faction, has donated one thousand won, but depending on future circumstances, we might do more. So, such matters will likely be decided and executed according to the unfolding of the incident.

Opinion of Mr. Ch'oe Chun-mo (崔俊模) of the Old Faction
- It's truly welcome news that the divided Ch'ŏndogyo is reuniting.
- Not at all. The split didn't stem from a major cause either; it was merely a difference in interpretation regarding the Kyoju succession, so it wasn't absolutely necessary to split. However, once emotions between the two factions clashed, harmony became impossible. That's how we ended up splitting, and six years have already passed since then. The discordant feelings have all faded, and the desire to return to the old days when we worked together in one place has gradually strengthened on both sides, I believe. Then, some time ago, Mr. Chŏng Kwang-jo and Mr. Cho Ki-gan (조기간) from the New Faction, and Mr. Yi Chong-nin and myself from the Old Faction, the four of us gathered, reminisced about the past, discussed the present, and ultimately spoke of reuniting, merely promising to strive together for the realization of reunification. The thought of reuniting wasn't limited to the four of us who gathered that day; I believe all leaders on both sides felt the same way.

- To realize reunification, they say a Joint General Assembly (兩派總大會) will be held. When and how will that happen?
- That's completely unknown. There has been no such talk yet, and we only discussed doing our best to properly convey the intentions (心案) of the top leaders to the followers in the provinces as well, so that they may harmonize.

- Then, has there been no discussion about the internal structure after reunification?
- No. The current issue is whether harmony can be achieved or not; we haven't yet had the leisure to address post-reunification matters.

- What are the current assets, and how are they managed?
- Ch'ŏndogyo's total assets are over one million won. Most are jointly owned by both factions, with only a small portion held separately.

- How does Ch'ŏndogyo receive income?
- The majority of income comes from the "Sincerity Rice" (誠米) offerings from followers. Currently, for both factions, this alone is insufficient to cover office and all other expenses.

- How do you plan to support the Japan-China Incident?
- We don't have any special method; we just intend to provide support sincerely and to the best of our ability. Therefore, the religion as a whole has made donations, and we intend to have individual followers contribute as much as possible.

- Was the fact that Ch'ŏndogyo is reuniting this time directly caused by the need to take the same stance in supporting the current situation (時局後援)?
- Of course, the current situation (시국관계) likely played a role to some extent, but the desire to reunite has existed for some time already. In any case, home front support (銃後後援) will likely be more powerful if done collaboratively.

- Is there no plan to donate something like an anti-aircraft gun or an airplane?
- We don't have a specific plan like that, but we do intend to donate as much as possible within our means so that it becomes a significant force. I believe this thinking is not unique to Ch'ŏndogyo; anyone who is a citizen would feel the same way. <15>

**Summary**

This 1938 article interviews leaders from the New (Sinp'a) and Old (Kup'a) factions of Ch'ŏndogyo regarding the possibility of reunification and their stance on supporting Japan's war effort during the Sino-Japanese Incident. While leaders like Ch'oe Rin (New) and Ch'oe Chun-mo (Old) express optimism and acknowledge ongoing efforts towards reunification, driven partly by the wartime context, Kwŏn Tong-jin (Old) remains cautious due to past failed attempts and lingering mistrust, emphasizing the need for genuine consensus among all followers. Both factions express commitment to supporting Japan's war effort ("home front support") through financial contributions and follower mobilization, framing it as a national duty, though they differ slightly on specific methods (e.g., donating money vs. specific items like planes). For historians, this article offers valuable insight into how a major Korean religious organization navigated colonial rule during wartime. It reveals internal divisions, the complex dynamics of reunification talks influenced by external pressures (the war), and the varying degrees of cooperation or adaptation expressed by religious leaders towards the Japanese state's mobilization demands, particularly the emphasis on "sincerity" (誠心) and national duty in justifying support for the war.

기사형태	대담·좌담

戰時中70만 天道敎大衆은 어데로 가나, 新舊兩派首領의 見解
天道敎! 天道敎! 여러 가지 의미에서 世人의 주목을 끄으는 天道敎, 더구나 시국은 戰時라 이때에 70만 敎徒를 擁하고 잇는 天道敎의 동향은 내외의 視聽을 끄으는 바가만타. 우리는 新舊兩派의 首領에게서 그 포부의 일단을 듯기로 하다.

1. 新舊兩派는 다시 合同하는가.
2. 時局에 대한 新舊兩派의 進路

新派 崔麟氏 의견
- 得聞한바에 의하면 天道敎 新舊派가 合同하게 된다는대 그것이 사실입니까.
- 네, 일전 신문에도 그러한 말이 보도되엿든가 봅니다만 그것은 개인과 개인간에 상의한 말이요 기관과 기관이 서로 합동할 것을 협의한 일은 없섯습니다. 그러한 즉 합동할녀는 생각을 가진지는 오래전부터 인가 합니다.

- 아니요. 그리 십게 합동될 것은 아니겟지요. 합동한다는것도 兩派가 정식으로 서로 만나 회의한것도 아니고 일전 개인과 개인이 서로 만나 합동할 것을 말하고 금후 그 실현을 위하야 노력하야 보자는 것을 약속을 하든 것이외다. 그러한 것이 신문은 사실이상 확대하야 보도하엿습디다. 신문이란 언제나 사실을 확대하야 말하는 것이니 보통으로 생각합니다만 물론 양편 최고수령들은 가급적으로 짜른 시일내로 70만 교도가 마음의 융합으로 합동하도록 할녀고 최선의 노력을 다함은 사실입니다만 종교란 지적이 아니라 감정적인 만치 모든 것이 이론과 지혜로서 많으로는 안되는 것입니다. 다시 말하면 이론으로서는 그리되어야 할 것이로대 사실에 잇서는 정반대로 되는일도 없지 못헤 잇는 것입니다. 그래 今般 합동 문제에 잇서서는 이 감정문제 갓흔 것을 신중히 고려하야 전교도들이 성심으로 합동의 의의를 부르짓게 하도록 할가 합니다. 前年에 1차 합동하엿다 다시 분열된것도 주로 감정의 갈등으로서 엿다고 봅니다. 그러한 쓴 경험을 밟어 온 만치 금후에 잇서서는 절대 그러한 일이 없시 영원한 합동을 위한 합동을 하게 할가 합니다. 그래 전번 모여 이야기 한 대로 벌서 지방에도 사람을 보내여 합동의 의의를 깊게 인식시키기에 힘쓰고 잇습니다. 현재 천도교는 兩派라기 보다는 三派로 되엿다고 보아도 과언이 아닙니다. 沙里院을 중심한 일파가 잇는대 그것은 최근 舊派에서 다시 분열되여 나간 것입니다. 그래 그곳에도 사람을 보내 합동의 의의를 말하엿든바 그곳에서도 잘 이해하는 모양이와 일은 현재의 형편으로 보아 순조로 나가게 될 듯 합니다.
- 분열후의 天道敎는 지방에도 新派舊派가 따로 존재하고 잇습니까.
- 한 곳에 兩파가 다 잇는 곳은 平北宣川, 龍川, 鐵山, 멫 곳이 되고 그 다음 곳은 新派가 잇는데는＜14＞ 舊派가 없고 舊派가 잇는대는 新派가 업게 되엿습니다. 그러니 한곳에서 新舊派가 서로 衝突되는 일은 절대 없섯습니다. 宣川 갓흔곳은 경성과 갓치 한집에 사무소를 두고 사무정리를 하게 됩니다. 그러니 兩派래야 그저 사무처리를 따로 한다는데 끈친다고 하겟습니다.
- 만약 금후 합동된다면 갓흔 天道敎에 교주 두분이 잇든 것이 한분이 되게되니 자연 내부 직제에도 변동을 이르키게 되겟습니다?
- 물론 합동하면 교주는 한분이 되고 내부 직제도 달너지게 될 것입니다. 그러나 아직 그 문제에 대하여서는 논의할 만한 여유를 갓지 못하고 위선 합동하게  됨에 온갓 힘을 다 할가 합니다. 합동한 후면 내부직제 갓흔 것은 엇더케 되든 그리 큰 곤란을 이르큼 없시 될줄 믿습니다.
- 天道敎에서는 이번 日支事變에 대하야 엇더케 봉사할녀 합니까.
- 엇더케 하여야 하겟다는 무엇을 결정하고 하는 것이 아니라 사변의 진전됨을 따라 최선의 방도를 취할가 합니다.
- 天道敎에서 비행기 1대쯤 헌납한다는 설잇스니 엇더합니까.
- 비행기이니 高射砲이니 그리할 것 없시 그저 誠心盡力으로 銃後後援을 하는 것이 올타고 봅니다. 우리 교인들은 자기 직분과 힘이 밋치는데까지 헌납하엿고 금후에 잇서서 일층 더 하게 할가 합니다. 우리 新派에서는 天道敎 新派로서 천원 헌납 하엿습니다만 금후 형편을 보아 더하게도 될가 합니다. 그러니 그러한 것은 금후 사변의 전개됨을 따라 결정 실행 되리라고 봅니다.

舊派崔俊模씨 의견
- 분열되엿든 天道敎가 다시한대 합한다니 그것 참 반가운 일이외다.
- 천만에 말삼이올시다. 분열된것도 그리 큰 원인을 가진 것이 아니엿고 다만 교주세대에 대한 해석의 차이엿든만치 꼭 분열하지 않을 수 없섯든 것도 아니엿지요. 그러나 兩派의 감정이 한번 서로 갈등이 지자 엇지 화합될 수 없섯든 것입니다. 그래 서로 분열되엿든 것인대 그리된지 벌서 6년이 지나 갓스니 평화롭지 못하든 감정은 다 사라지고 한곳에서 갓치 일하든 그 옛날노 다시 도라 갈녀는 마음이 점점 강하야짐은 양편이 다 갓하엿다고 봅니다. 그러하다 얼마 전에 新派에서 鄭廣朝씨, 조기간씨 舊派서는 저와 李鍾麟씨 4인이 한자리에 모여 옛날을 이야기하고 오날을 논하든 끗헤 다시 합동할 것을 말하고 합동의 실현을 위하여 서로 진력하야 볼 것을 약속하엿든 것에 지나지 않습니다. 합동할녀는 생각을 가진 것은 그날 모여 안즌 4인만히 아니엿고 양편 간부는 모다 그러하엿다고 봅니다.
- 합동을 실현하기 위하야 兩派總大會를 한다는대 그것은 언제 엇더케 하게됩니까.
- 그것은 전혀 모를 말삼이외다. 아직 그러한 이야기는 없섯고 될 수 잇는 대로 지방잇는 교도들에게도 최고수령들의 心案을 잘 전달하야 서로 화합하도록 함에 최선의 노력을 다하자는 것을 이야기하엿슬 뿐입니다.
- 그러면 합동 후의 내부직제에 대하여서는 엇더케 하자는 상의가 없섯습니까.
- 없섯습니다. 현재의 문제는 화합되겟는냐 안되겟는냐 하는것이지 아직 합동 된 후의 문제에까지 논급할 여유를 갓지 못하엿습니다.
- 현재 재산은 얼마나 되오며 관리는 엇더게 합니까.
- 天道敎 총재산은 백여만원되며 대부분은 兩派 공동소유이고 소부분만을 서로 따로 갓고 잇습니다.
- 天道敎의 수입은 엇더케 됩니까.
- 수입의 대부분은 교도로부터 드러오는 誠米이겟습니다. 兩派 모다 현재 그것만으로 사무기타 모든 비용을 쓰는대 부족되는 형편입니다.
- 日支事變에 대한 후원은 엇더케 할녀합니가.
- 별다른 방법을 갓고 잇는 것은 없고 그저 성심으로 힘자라는 대로 후원할가 합니다. 그래 교 전체로서도 헌납하엿고 교도 개인으로서도 가급적으로 많이 하게 할가 합니다.
- 이번 天道敎가 합동한다는 것은 時局後援에 잇서 갓흔 보조를 취하자는 것이 직접원인이 되지 않엇습니까.
- 물론 시국관계도 어느 정도까지는 잇서스리라고 봅니다만 합동할녀는 것은 벌서 전부터 이엿습니다. 여하튼 銃後後援은 될 수 잇는대로 협동하여 함이 더 힘이 될가 합니다.
