A View on the Sino-Japanese Incident from Prison, Sano Manabu's Prison Diary
Chapter Five

1. The Autumn of Japan's Rise
Triggered by the North China Incident, a great situation has unfolded that will determine our national destiny. While the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents ended as they did, today's incident will not likely end here but may lead to a confrontation with a massive conflict involving not only China but several other countries as adversaries. Currently, it seems the Chinese are preparing for the future partition of China by themselves. China lacks the money and weapons to fight Japan on its own strength. Chiang Kai-shek, without considering the consequences, borrows money and weapons from foreign countries, and foreign countries readily lend them. Westerners, unlike the Japanese, do not consider China's preservation and Asia's destiny with sincerity, nor do they feel a sense of responsibility.
What will come after China's ruin will be the unified maneuvering of Westerners for the partition of China. Japan, of course, opposes the partition of China and will thus clash with them. For this reason, although China's military strength alone is not worth considering, the international situation developing from the military conflict with China is unexpectedly complex and requires vigilance. War against others is also quite possible. Although the political principles of the Japanese and the Soviets are as different as fire and water, the clash between the two countries is undoubtedly a fated struggle arising from the necessity of survival for both peoples. Even if Tsarist Russia still existed, the crisis of war would likely be intensifying by now. It is uncertain whether China and the Soviet Union will emerge as enemies in the first stage, followed by Britain and America in the second.
The difficulties ahead for Japan are truly great. However, by overcoming these difficulties, Japan will make a truly great leap forward. Great historical achievements can always be attained through the ordeal of extraordinary hardship (困苦). Heaven is now giving Japan countless difficult tasks to make Japan a truly great world leader. Now is truly the autumn of Japan's rise. It is time for the national energy accumulated over 20 years to be unleashed. Only by passing this great ordeal will Japan become a world hero (勇者). Love of adventure (昌險) is the traditional spirit of our nation. We rather welcome the critical urgency of the situation.

2. Regarding War: Active National Defense
When we issued our conversion statement (*tenkō* statement), some people suspected that we might hold a Comintern-like war policy. However, since our conversion was originally a product of the war situation, there is a reason for taking an active stance towards war.
Humans need a strong state to truly develop the various aspects of their lives. The stronger the state, the better. A strong state must first be able to fully assert itself externally. Japan has reached a point where it cannot suppress its impulse for expansion, and it possesses the capability to put that impulse into practice.
Even if this power unfortunately turns into war, it cannot be helped. (Omission - original text) Delaying an unavoidable war even by a day only increases the damage. Japan has the historical mission to liberate the peoples of Asia from European and American capitalism, and the wars Japan wages, large or small, are based on this historical mission.
Therefore, the wars waged by Japan have a progressive nature. Merely defending the nation is insufficient. It must take on an active, offensive character. The bellicose spirit unique to our nation will rapidly develop. Thus, the pacifist weakness (惑弱) uncharacteristic of Japanese people will rapidly disappear. War starting is rather a good thing, also in the sense that it provides an opportunity for Japan's internal reform.

3. Internal Reform Through Nationwide Assistance
As the North China Incident broke out, the people instinctively sensed the crisis of the national destiny and began to show nationwide unity efforts such as donations. I believe that this manifested national energy (에네르기-) should not be dissipated but should be organized to create several political, social, and military units of activity. To win a great war that determines the fate of the nation, carrying out internal reforms is indispensable. War provides an opportunity to exterminate (斷滅) the corruption, degradation, weakening, and peaceful decay (腐朽) existing within Japan.
Winning a war with a decayed internal structure is absolutely impossible.
Through the life-and-death struggle of the people in war, the absolutely necessary spiritual and material common unity, Japanese society will advance to a higher stage by leaps and bounds.

Four. The Soviet Union
Some people might think that since we once belonged to the Comintern, we might still hold goodwill towards the Soviet Union, but that would be a mistaken thought. It seems that becoming today's enemy from yesterday's comrade evokes an even deeper hatred than towards a long-standing enemy. We view with hatred the attitude of the Soviet regime, which always regards Japan with hostility and takes anti-Japanese and insulting actions like the Chinese. The Soviet Union has been experiencing continuous internal strife (內訌相), starting from last year's Zinoviev-Kamenev executions by firing squad to this June's Tukhachevsky execution. Journalists (쩌나미스트) and left-wing intellectuals (좌익인테리) caught up in the current state of affairs seem to say this is precisely the process of strengthening the Stalin regime, but in reality, the fundamental content is the crisis of their communist system. When we see the chaotic commotion in the Soviet Union, we rejoice inwardly (心祝) that our conversion was correct. What a great predicament (낭패) we would have been in if we had sincerely held onto the Communist Party. The fundamental problem boils down to the impossibility of communism. Because they try to realize this impossibility, the Soviet Union always needs deceptive policies (긔민정책). The deceptive policy Lenin carried out was the New Economic Policy. The deceptive policy carried out by Stalin, a figure one step lower than Lenin, is truly complex: pretending to adopt democracy while clinging to communism (the new constitution), trying to use internationalism and nationalism concurrently, deceiving with people's front tactics (인민전술), publicly promising foreign countries to abolish the GPU (게-페,우) while continuing the terror policy (테로정책) with altered mechanisms (카라구리), etc. When it collapses, resolving the situation becomes that much harder. Ultimately, the Russian people are returning to capitalism. It is said that Tukhachevsky and others had a political program to abolish the proletarian dictatorship and establish a New People's Republic (新民공화국). The trend of Russian society returning to capitalism is pressing upon the Russian people with a force beyond human will. That movement will repeat itself many times in the future and eventually lead to the destruction of the Stalin regime. We must also carefully observe the continued bankruptcy of the materialistic worldview. It is a great irony (皮肉) that religion seems to be returning by the hands of the masses themselves. Next will likely be the revival of idealistic philosophy. The inhuman cruelty (慘酷) of factional struggles within the Communist Party is related to the ideology that views humans as objects. The Russians are frantically arming the entire nation, aiming at anti-Japanism like the Chinese<22>. However, it is questionable whether they are as strong as they claim. Historically, the Russian people were slaves to the Mongols for hundreds of years starting from the 13th century. They have never won a war belonging to Europe. Their capital, Moscow, was occupied by enemies several times. Ethnically, they certainly do not belong to the conquering peoples. The conflict between the Japanese and the Russians is a struggle for the survival of both peoples. Seeds of conflict exist everywhere – Manchuria, Mongolia, Xinjiang, Siberia, the Maritime Province, Kamchatka, Sakhalin (*태). The Japanese will likely resolve to occupy even Moscow.

Regarding China
China, after years of shouting anti-Japanese and exclusionist slogans, finally instigated the North China Incident. It is no exaggeration (과언) to say that the mission of the Japanese nation in Asia currently lies in resolving the China problem. Without destroying the conservatism of the Chinese and Indian societies, which occupy vast areas of Asia, comprehensive progress in world history cannot be obtained (엇슬수 없스나), but the India problem is the task of the next generation (次代) of Japanese; the task of the current Japanese is the resolution of the China problem. An accurate understanding (초월) of China is extremely necessary. China is an old, large country (老大國). Its societal corruption, slavish baseness (奴隸的 卑賤), conservatism, and xenophobia (排外主義), which are the results of its long history, will be shattered (打碎될 것이다) by a force like a *, by blind fate. This force, this fate, is none other than Japan. This outcome coincides with China's interests. The anti-Japanese and exclusionist movement directed at Japan is, in essence, the content of **-like xenophobia thrown at foreigners in general.
I remember someone once calmly stating, "The Chinese want to expel every single foreigner from China," and indeed, the blind arrogance (盲想的 尊大) and xenophobia (排外心) of the Chinese nation are profound. Initially, I had deep sympathy for the Chinese revolution and began studying China, but during my research, I discovered that the base, primitive, and negative elements in Chinese society outnumbered the positive ones, and my sympathy turned into disgust (嫌惡). Japanese society has developed to a much higher level (고급하게) compared to Chinese society. Japan and China are too far apart (相遠) in their social foundations and national principles. China interrupted (中繼하야) its autonomous social development, and even today, ancient characteristics persist (잔존하고 잇스나), whereas Japan completely passed through feudal society, continued its consecutive development, and finally reached the modern state. Japan has strong national unity and possesses a state that politically expresses this, whereas national unity does not exist in China, and a state in the true sense does not yet exist.
In China, primitive class antagonism exists, and cruel fratricide occurs endlessly, whereas in Japan, the replacement (更替) of class efforts occurred relatively smoothly (원만히). China's spiritual culture has a brilliant (찬연한) outward appearance (외관), but it was the caste-like (카스트적) culture of a narrow scholar-official (士太夫) stratum, whereas in Japan, culture was already becoming nationalized (국민화) from the Tokugawa era. The reason Japan built a modern state while China could not is, in short (요컨대), that Japan created the objective (객관적) and subjective (주관적) conditions to reach a modern state, whereas China lacked them. Among Japanese scholars, there are those afflicted with the age-old China-worship disease (支那崇拜病者), and political conservatives seem intent on quoting from ancient Chinese classics (綠典), but it would be better to stop such practices and become self-aware (자각함이) of the superiority (우월) of Japan's social development and the brilliant (燦한) essence of our classics (古典). The agricultural structure forming the backbone (척골) of Chinese society has a severe feudal nature (封建性) in terms of land ownership, land rent (地代), merchant capital functions, etc. The state's politics is blatant (노골한) rule by violence, and the temperament (기질) and habits (습관) of the corrupt and degraded bureaucrats (官僚) of the Qing Dynasty (淸朝) still remain (잔존하고 잇다). The family system is strong, but it is irrelevant (風馬牛) to the overall fate of the state and society. It is truly regrettable (참으로 동정할 일이다) that Sun Yat-sen lamented (비애 하엿슴은) the lack of national unity among the hundreds of millions of Chinese people, comparing them to grains of sand. Negative aspects like love of money (愛錢), hypocrisy (僞善), formalism (形式主義), and lack of public spirit (公共心의 결핍) are prevalent in the national psychology. It is said that China is a semi-colony (半植民地). This is a term coined (조어) by the Comintern, but it seems quite apt. The domination by the financial capital of imperialist powers deepens year by year. In the last one or two years, China has made remarkable (괄목적) political and economic progress. This has made the Chinese themselves boastful (뽐내게 되엿스나), but ultimately, it is undoubtedly the imperialist powers strengthening their control by using their collaborators (支配人) to reconstruct the Chinese economy.
Chiang Kai-shek is preparing for the future partition of China; he is not a patriot but a traitor (賣國奴). I believe the Japanese nation has the following tasks regarding China:
1) Japan has the mission to liberate China from its regressive social stagnation (退步的 社會靜態). Concretely, it should transform China's feudal economic structure and assist (援助할 것이다) it in progressing towards a modern state politically. It absolutely should not obstruct it.
2) The warlords (軍閥) and party members (黨人) organizing the current Chinese government are traditional Chinese exploiters (貪慾者). They are exploiters of the people (人民搾取者). They are harmful to China's regeneration (更生), so a war to exterminate (撲滅) them is a righteous war (義戰), not directed against the Chinese people, but rather for their benefit.
3) The Chinese popular masses must not be made enemies. They must be supported. Their impulse to rebel against their exploiters should be ignited. Historically, what enabled dynastic changes and foreign invasions of the Central Plain in China was peasant mass uprisings. If we thoroughly maintain the attitude of "saving the masses" towards the Chinese people and gain their trust (信任), anti-Japanese sentiment will naturally disappear (消滅). China will be acquired (獲得) from below.
4) Progressive vanguard (前衛) elements also exist in Chinese society. We should help them and unite (結合) with them. People like Sun Yat-sen, despite having minor flaws (缺點), were truly respectable figures for their passion (熱情), devotion (獻身), and magnanimity (宏量). Li Hwahun (李火訓), who was executed (形死) in Beijing by Chang Tso-lin, was an acquaintance of mine, and I still recall his character (風格) of sacrificing himself for virtue (殺身成仁) with respect.
5) It is inevitable that Japan will fight against the Western imperialist powers that manipulate (模縱하는) China's traitorous warlords and party members. In this sense, the China problem has existential significance for the Japanese nation.
6) Japan absolutely opposes the partition of China.
7) When China becomes a modern state with Japan's assistance, the two countries, Japan and China, will cooperate (提携) to liberate French Indochina (佛領印度支那) and British India (英印度). The struggle with Westerners is inevitable. The revival (再生) of Genghis Khan's (成吉汗) conquest of the West, led by Japan, is something Westerners themselves fear. The development of the world economy creates the conditions to end (終結) Asia's current state of division. Giving this a vital synthetic form (綜合形態) volitionally (意思的으로) is Japan's mission.
8) If war begins between Japan and China due to the current North China Incident, Japan must, in addition to military attacks, instigate internal disruption (內部攪亂) in China in the most planned and organized manner. In China, which lacks national unity, the current authorities like Chiang and others are ultimately exploiters of the people, and opportunities (모メント) for popular uprisings against the government exist everywhere (至所에 잇는 것이다).
War rapidly and mercilessly overthrows the decayed (腐朽). This is true. This war will produce such effects in Japan as well. This will be especially true in China. In this sense, this war is a kind of heaven's provision (配劑), something to be welcomed. China can only advance (向上) with Japan's assistance. Anti-Japanese actions are acts of China's self-destruction (自滅), and the Chinese have no other way (딴 방도) to save themselves except through Japan's real power (實力).

Regarding my Future Course of Action
One. Since I still have 9 years left in my sentence, speaking concretely about things after my release might not seem very realistic, but broadly speaking, it is as follows:
1) To live as a loyal Japanese (忠良한 日本人).
2) To do something to contribute to our state and society to compensate for past errors (前過).
3) The principles acknowledged in this submitted report (本上申書) - Imperial Household-centrism (皇室中心主義), Nation-first principle (民族第一主義), Statism (國家主義), etc. - will be my unchanging beliefs (不變한 信念) hereafter.
Two. I intend to earn my living (生活의 資) through writing (著述業). For scholarly works, I hope to undertake considerable research (大<23>*한 アルバイト를 것치고) on specific historical parts of Japan and China.
Three. Whether I engage in social or political movements after release depends on the situation (政勢如何). If the situation requires me, I will not necessarily refuse (辭絶까지 하지는 안켓다). Current social movements, large or small, have classist (階級主義的) characteristics. If I were to engage in a social movement, I would base it particularly on nationalism (國民主義) from the standpoint (見地) stated in this report. Also, since I hold no negative opinions whatsoever (何等) towards the existing state and social organization, if I do, I will make a completely fresh start according to the laws of the state, i.e., legally (合法的으로).
Four. In a critical time like today, in other words, in the autumn when true Japan's rise (興隆) will be shown through overcoming hardship (艱苦), sitting idly (헛데이) in prison is an unspeakable pain (苦痛).
If there is anything I can do, I want to devote myself wholeheartedly (全心績的으로) and serve (奉仕) the state. In this report, I stated that if a Sino-Japanese war occurs, besides military attack, internal disruption (內部攪亂) in China—that is, agitation (煽動) and organization to make the Chinese people rebel against Chiang Kai-shek's rule—is necessary and possible. I would like to contribute something in such areas. In that field, I might be one of the experts (エキスパ-ト). I also have considerable confidence in my ability to propagandize the Japanese masses. We who have crossed the spiritual line of death (死線) called conversion (*tenkō*) possess quite intense courage (勇氣).
The above is merely expressing feelings of lament (歎息) out of sorrow for being imprisoned in times like these, and nothing more is meant. <24>

**Summary**

Sano Manabu, a former Japanese communist leader who underwent *tenkō* (ideological conversion), writes from prison in 1938, viewing the Sino-Japanese War as a necessary, fated conflict and a crucial step towards Japan's destiny as the leader of Asia. He argues Japan has a mission to "liberate" Asia from Western capitalism and China from its own internal decay (corruption, feudalism, lack of unity), justifying war as progressive and beneficial for internal Japanese reform. He expresses deep disdain for China's society and leadership (calling Chiang Kai-shek a traitor), criticizes the Soviet Union's communist system post-conversion, and advocates for Japanese superiority and expansionism, including potential conflict with the USSR and Western powers. He pledges loyalty to the Japanese Emperor and state upon release, hoping to contribute to Japan's war effort, potentially through propaganda or organizing internal disruption within China. For historians of Korean colonial history, this article is revealing as it showcases the powerful ideology of Japanese expansionism and Pan-Asianism (under Japanese hegemony) prevalent during the period, written by a prominent intellectual figure. It starkly illustrates the justifications used for Japanese aggression and the deeply ingrained sense of racial and national superiority that underpinned Japanese colonial rule and wartime policies, directly impacting Korea.

日支事變獄中觀, 佐野學 獄中記
第五章
1. 日本興隆의 秋
北支事變을 계기로 하야 우리의 민족적 운명을 좌우하는 위대한 국면이 전개 되엿다. 滿洲上海兩事變은 그러케 종결을 지엇스나 금일의 사변은 이대로 끈칠것이 아니라 이로 말미아마 支那뿐만 아니라 他의 數國을 적대하로한 거대한 갈등에 직면하지 않을것인가. 현재 支那人은 장래에 잇서서의 支那 분할을 자기 스사로 준비하고 잇는 것이라고 생각된다. 支那는 독력으로 日本과 싸울만한 돈과 무기도 갖지 못하엿다. 蔣介石은 앞뒤를 고려하지 못하고 돈과 무기를 외국에서 차용하고 외국은 쾌히 그것을 대부하여 준다. 서양인은 日本人과 갓치 성심으로 支那보전과 亞細亞 운명에 대하야 고려하지 않으며 책임감도 갖고 잇지 않다.
支那가 파멸된 후에 올 것은 서양인의 일치한 支那 분할의 책동일 것이다. 日本은 물론 支那 분할에 반대하니 彼等과 충돌한다. 이러한고로 支那일국의 군사력은 문제할배 못되나 支那와의 군사적 갈등으로부터 전개하는 국제적 국면은 의외로 복잡하야 戒心을 요한다. 他에 對소 전쟁도 퍽으나 가능하다. 日本人의 정치원칙과 소국인의 그것은 불과 물과 갓치 다르나 양국의 충돌은 양민족의 생존의 필요에서 숙명된 투쟁임에 틀님없다. 帝政露西亞가 존속한다 하드래도 역시 인제와서는 전쟁의 위기가 첨예화하고 잇슬 것이다. 제1차로 支那 及 소聯, 제2차로 英米가 적으로 출현할지 알 수 없다.
日本의 전도의 곤란은 참으로 크다. 그러나 이 곤란을 극복하고서 日本은 참으로 위대한 약진할 것이다. 위대한 역사적 업적은 언제나 이상한 困苦의 시련을 통하야 획득 할 수 잇다. 日本을 참으로 위대한 세계적 지도자 될 것으로 하날은 지금 日本에 무수한 곤란의 과제를 주고 잇다. 지금이야말노 日本興隆의 秋이다. 20년래 축적된 민족적정력이 발휘될 때이다. 이 대시련을 파-스하고야 처음 日本은 세계적 勇者가 된다. 昌險에의 愛는 민족의 전통적 정신이다. 우리는 국면의 위기적 切追을 오히려 환영한다.

2. 戰爭에 對하야 적극적 母國防衛
우리가 轉向聲明 하엿슬때 일부의 人은 우리가 콤민테른적 전쟁정책을 가진 것이 아닌가 추측하든 것이엿다. 然이나 원래 우리의 전향은 전쟁정세의 산물이엿스니 전쟁에 대하야 적극적 태도를 취하는 이유가 잇다.
인간은 그 생활의 여러 방면을 진실노 발달식히기 위하야 강력한 국가를 필요한다. 국가는 강하면 강할사로 좋다. 강력한 국가는 우선 대외적으로 충분히 자기를 주장하지 않으면 안된다. 日本은 현실에 잇서 팽창의 충동을 抑刷할 수 없슬만치 되엿스며 그 충동을 실현에 옴길만한 능력을 갖고 잇다.
이 힘이 불행이 전쟁으로 화한다 하드래도 엇지할 수 없는 일이다.(중략-원문) 아모리하야도 피할수없는 전쟁을 하로 연기하면 그만치 손해가 크다. 日本은 亞細亞의 제민족을 歐米의 자본주의로부터 해방식힐 역사적 사명을 갖고 잇스며 日本의 행하는 전쟁은 크나 적으나 이 역사적 사명에 의함이다.
그러하니 日本의 행하는 전쟁에는 진보성이 잇다. 다만 田國防衛로만은로서는 부족하다. 그것은 적극적 공격적 성질을 가질 것이다. 我민족특유의 호전적 정신은 급속히 발전될 것이다. 그래 日本人 갓지 않은 평화주의적 惑弱은 급속히 消滅하여 갈 것이다. 日本의 내부적 개혁의 기회를 갓게 된다는 의미에 잇서서도 전쟁이 시작된단은 것은 오히려 조흔일이다.

3. 擧國的 助力으로써 國內改革
北支事變이 발기되자 국민은 민족적 운명의 위기를 본능적으로 직감하고, 獻金 기타의 거국일치적 노력을 표시하기 시작하엿다. 이에 낫하난 국민의 에네르기-를 방산함이 없시 이것을 조직화하야 정치적 사회 군사적 활동단위를 멧개이고 만들어야 할것이라고 생각한다. 국운을 좌우하는 大戰에 승리하기 위하여서는 국내개혁의 단행은 불가결한 일이다. 전쟁은 日本내부에 在한 부패 타락 나약화 평화적 腐朽 등을 斷滅할 기회를 준다.
腐朽한 내부조직으로써 전쟁에 승리한다는 것은 절대 불가능한 일이다.
전쟁에 잇서서 국민의 생사의 투쟁 절대 필요한 그 정신적 물질적 공동일치 이것을 통하야 日本사회는 비약적으로 높은 단계로 진전한다.

四. 소 聯邦
우리는 한때 콤민테른에 속하엿든 것이니 지금도 소聯邦에 호의를 갖고 잇스리라고 생각하는 사람이 잇슬는지 모르나 그럿타면 잘못된 생각이다. 昨日의 동무로서 今日의 적이 된 것은 재래의 적에 대하는 것보다 일층 심각한 증오를 感하는 모양이다. 우리는 언제나 日本을 적으로 질시하고 支那人과 갓치 抗日侮日적 행위를 취하는 소연방정권의 태도를 증오감으로써 보고 잇다. 소연방은 작년 지노카메 총살을 위시하야 금년 6월 도하총살에 至하기까지 內訌相이 계속되고 잇다. 현상 형태에 사로잡힌 쩌나미스트와 좌익인테리는 이것이야말노 스타-린 정권 강화 과정이라고 하는 모양이나 실제는 그들의 공산주의 체제의 위기가 근본내용이다. 우리는 소聯의 亂痴氣騷動을 볼 때 우리의 전향이 정당하엿슴을 心祝한다. 진심으로 공산당을 갖고 이섯다면 얼마나 큰 낭패를 하엿슬 것인가. 근본문제는 공산주의의 불가능 하다는대 귀착된다. 이 불가능사를 실현코저하기 때문에 소련은 언제나 긔민정책을 필요하는 것이다. 레-닌의 행한 기만정책은 신경제정책이다. 레-닌보다 일단저급한 인물 스-타린의 행하는 기만정책은 혹은 공산주의를 고집하면서 민주주의를 채용하는 척하며(신헌법) 혹은 국제주의와 국민주의와의 병용을 시험도하야 보기도 하고 혹은 인민전술로써 기만하며 혹은 게-페,우를 폐지한다고 諸외국에 대하야 공연하게 약속하면서 카라구리를 변한 依然 테로정책을 수행하는 등 실노 복잡한대 破綻하면 그만치 수습이 더 힘들게 되는 것이다. 결국 로서아人은 자본주의에 복귀하고 잇는 것이다. 토하쳎스키-등은 푸로레타리아독재를 폐지하고 新民공화국을 건설할 정치강령을 갖고이섯다 한다. 露西亞사회가 자본주 의에 복귀할녀는 동향은 인간의사 이상의 힘을 갓고 露國人에 迫到하는 것이다. 그 운동은 今後 몇 번이구 반복되야 내종에는 스타-린 정권의 破滅노 될 것이다. 유물론적 인생관이 계속 파산하고 잇는것도 주의깊게 관찰하지 않으면 안될 것이라고 본다. 종교가 대중자신의 손으로 복귀되고 잇는 듯 한 것은 큰 皮肉이다. 그 다음은 관념론적 철학의 부흥일 것이다. 공산당내 파벌투쟁의 비인간적 慘酷은 인간을 물건으로 보는 사상과 관련이 잇다. 露西人은 전국적으로 군비에 광분하야 支那人과 갓치 항일을＜22＞ 목표로 하고 잇다. 그러나 그들이 말하는 만치 강한지는 의문이다. 역사적으로보면 露西亞人은 13세기로부터 수백년간 蒙古인의 노예가 되여섯다. 歐羅巴에 속하는 전쟁에 승리한 일이 없다. 首府 모스코-는 멫번이나 적에게 점령되엿다. 민족적으로 보면 결코 정복민족에 속하지 않는다. 日本人과 露西人과의 갈등은 양민족의 생존의 투쟁이다. 滿洲, 蒙古, 新疆, 西伯利亞, 沿海州, 캄챡카, *태, 이르는 곳마다 충돌의 씨가 잇다. 日本人은 모스코-까지라도 점령할 의기로 할 것이다.

支那에 對하야
