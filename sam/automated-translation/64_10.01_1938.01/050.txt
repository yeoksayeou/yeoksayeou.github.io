**Body Text Translation:**

Four Questions Survey
1) What newspapers and magazines do you read at home?
2) What do you listen to on the radio at home?
3) What are your favorite records at home?
4) Where are you going on a trip at the start of the New Year?

Pak Yŏngch'ŏl (朴榮喆), President of Chosŏn Commercial Bank
1. Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo, Chosŏn Sinmun, Maeil Sinbo, Tonga Ilbo, Osaka Mainichi, Mansŏn Ilbo, <9> Taishinkyō Nichinichi
2. News, etc., various lectures
3. Shigin (Japanese poem chanting)
4. Paekch'ŏn, Yujŏng Hot Springs (露城溫泉)

Pak Ch'anghun (朴昌薰), Doctor of Medicine
1. The four newspapers Taejo (likely Chosŏn Chungang Ilbo), Tonga, Asahi, Maeil; the seven magazines Samch'ŏlli, P'ip'an, Chugan T'aeyang, Kaejo, Sekai Chishiki, Daiyamondo, Yunyŏn Kurakbu, Shōgakusei 6-nensei, plus medical journals.
2. Various things, that is, too numerous to list one by one (but mainly in the area of music).
3. News and music.
4. None in particular.

Mun Yebong (文藝峰)
1. Chosŏn Ilbo, Yomiuri Shimbun, Chogwang, Chungang Kongnon, Samch'ŏlli, Yŏnghwa P'yŏngnon, Teadoro Gekijō (likely the magazine *Theatoro Gekijō*).
2. Schubert's Serenade (레나-데), Träumerei (트로메라), Caravan (캬라방), Solveig's Song (솔배지, 송), etc.
3. It's not fixed; I check the program day by day and choose.
4. I don't have any.

Yun Sŏngsang (尹聖相)
1. Chosŏn Ilbo, Samch'ŏlli, Chogwang, Yŏsŏng, etc.
2. I prefer vocal music over instrumental music.
3. These days, as the weather has gotten colder, I pay attention to the weather report.
4. In the New Year, I'm thinking of taking a trip to Manchukuo (Manju).

Yi Kŭnjong (李肯鍾), President of Chosŏn Sanggong Sinmun
<9>
1. Chosŏn Sanggong Sinmun, Chosŏn Ilil Sinmun, Chosŏn Ilbo, Kaejo, Shufu no Tomo, and others I buy occasionally.
2. I don't have any particular favorite records, but I like classical opera plays.
3. Lectures, old stories (古談), radio dramas, news.
4. I haven't particularly traveled anywhere at the start of the New Year. This year, I'd like to go to Manchukuo (滿洲) or North China (北支), but who knows what will happen!

Kim Tongjin (金東進), Councilor (叅事) at Sŏnman T'akchik Hoesa (Korea-Manchuria Colonization Company)
1. Three types of Korean-language newspapers (朝鮮文新聞 3종), Chogwang, Yadam, Chungang Kongnon, Shufu no Tomo, Samch'ŏlli, Gaikō Jihō.
2. Russian editions brought from Harbin (哈爾賓서 가저오는 露國版), children's songs.
3. News, weather forecast, lectures, Korean music, Yaseong (traditional storytelling?).
4. Hot springs (Paekch'ŏn or Yujŏng (儒城)).

Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑)
1. Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Chogwang, Yŏsŏng, and foreign magazines besides.
2. I like Western music.
3. I listen to news and entertainment (演藝).
4. It's a hot spring trip.

Ku Chaok (具滋玉), Central Christian Young Men's Association (YMCA)
1. Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Kamnigyohoebo (Methodist Church News); no fixed magazines, I read various ones from time to time.
2. I have no favorite records.
3. No particular genre.
4. I cannot travel. <10>

(Continued at bottom)

Yi In (李仁), Lawyer (辨護士)
1. Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Osaka Asahi, Osaka Mainichi, Tōkyō Nichinichi, Chosŏn Sinmun, Hōsei Shinbun, Hōritsu Shinbun, Samch'ŏlli, Sekai Chishiki, Nihon Hyōron, Gaikō Hyōron (likely Gaikō Jihō?), Shihō Kyōkai Zasshi, Hōritsu Hyōron.
2. I am indifferent to record and radio music; even just reading newspapers and magazines takes up all my time.
3. I sometimes go to hot springs (溫井), but recently, I went hunting once, and for the second year running, I came back having harassed innocent village chickens without seeing a single sparrow. Still, summoning my courage, this year I'll just sit in the guest room (사랑) of a friend's house near Kurye Hwaŏm Temple (구례화엄사) in the Chiri Mountains (지리산) and sing one mountain song (山歌), but that's fun too.

Kim Ch'angje (金昶濟), Professor at Ewha Girls' High School (梨花女高敎授)
1. Chosŏn Ilbo, Osaka Asahi Shimbun.
2. None in particular.
3. None in particular.
4. None in particular.

Paek Kwansu (白寬洙), President of Tonga Ilbo
1. Major newspapers from Tonga and Osaka, major newspapers within Korea, and several magazines related to public discourse.
2. I don't have any particularly favorite genres.
3. Mostly lectures and news.
4. I am too busy to go.

Yi Sŏgu (李瑞求)
1. Maeil Sinbo, Yomiuri Shimbun, Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo.
2. Only the poems I have written myself.
3. Dramas and lectures.
4. Paekch'ŏn Hot Springs (白川溫泉).

Hŏ Hŏn (許憲)
1. Osaka Asahi, Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Kaejo, Chungang Kongnon, magazines related to international law and mining magazines, Samch'ŏlli, etc.
2. None in particular.
3. None in particular.
4. No particular place I am going.

Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi (崔貞熙)
1. For newspapers, I read the Chosŏn Ilbo. For magazines, like Munye, Chungang Kongnon, Kaejo, etc., I buy them if good creative works are published.
1. My records are nothing special. Until now, I've just bought whatever sounded good without any system. Most are simple things like Schubert's lieder and violin solos, so I've grown tired of them. From now on, I intend to listen to more complex and overwhelmingly rich music – although lately, the phonograph is temporarily out, so I can't even listen to simple music.
1. On the radio, I listen to nothing but music. Whether it's famous pieces or popular songs, listening on the radio seems to have a different flavor, so I listen to everything.
1. There are many places I want to go, but will I be able to go?

Kim Myŏngsik (金明植)
1. Newspapers are Taejo, Tonga, etc., and magazines are Kaejo, Chungang Kongnon, Samch'ŏlli, etc.
2. I always listen devotedly to worn-out ("deaf") records.
3. I don't know what kind of genres are on the radio broadcasts.
4. For me, there is no New Year's beginning (正初) and no travel. <11>

**Summary**

This 1938 survey in Samch'ŏlli magazine polls various prominent Korean figures (businessmen, doctors, artists, writers, educators, lawyers, journalists) on their media consumption habits and New Year travel plans. The responses reveal a diverse media diet including major Korean and Japanese newspapers (Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Osaka Mainichi, etc.) and popular intellectual or general interest magazines (Samch'ŏlli, Kaejo, Chungang Kongnon). Radio listening focused on news, lectures, music (Western classical, Korean, Japanese), and dramas, while record choices ranged from Western classical to indifference. Travel plans often involved hot springs (Paekch'ŏn, Yujŏng) or aspirations to visit Manchukuo or North China, though many cited busyness or lack of plans. For colonial history, the article offers a snapshot into the cultural life and perspectives of the Korean elite under Japanese rule. It highlights the blend of Korean and Japanese media consumed, the types of leisure activities considered (hot springs, travel, music), and hints at the political realities (travel to Manchukuo/North China, mentions of Japanese publications, figures associated with colonial entities like Sŏnman T'akchik Hoesa). The range of responses, from detailed preferences to curt dismissals, also subtly reflects individual personalities and perhaps their varying levels of engagement with or distance from mainstream cultural trends of the time.

1. 레코-트가 변변치 못합니다. 지금까지 아모 계통없이 듯기 좋은 좋은 것이면 사군했읍니다. 대부분이 슈-벨트의 가요곡과 바요링 독연등 단순한 것 뿐이여서 실증이 낫읍니다. 이제 앞으로는 좀 더 복잡하고 홍수처럼 풍부한 음악을 드를가 합니다- 요새는 축음기가 잠간 외출한 뒤라 단순한 음악이나마 듯지 못하지만-
1. 라디오에서는 음악외에 아모것도 안듯읍니다. 명곡이거나 유행가거나 라디오에서 듯는 것은 딴맛이 나는 듯 싶기에 다-듯습니다.
1. 가고 싶은 곳이야 만치만 가낼수가 잇겠읍니까.
