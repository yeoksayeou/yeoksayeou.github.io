**Body Text Translation:**
Seeking to Revive Classical "Dance" and "Music," Two Great Masters Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi and Han Sŏngjun Meet

"Ŏrhwa Manso—
Ŏrhwa Taesae-nira"

Could a Westerner, even if they died a hundred times, ever manage such a wonderful song tune? Likewise, who else could perform the "Monk's Dance" (僧舞), danced softly and gently as if crawling or embracing, wearing a peaked hat and a monk's robe (長衫), or the "Sword Dance" (劍舞), danced with spirit, the sword hilt fluttering gracefully? Only we can dance these, and even among us, the eyes of the young generation grow increasingly dim to appreciating them.

It is truly a regrettable thing that the classical music and dance, kneaded from the soil of this land over many thousands, many hundreds of years, are gradually hiding behind the awareness of the people of this era.
Therefore, those who have once been immersed in the fragrance of that art have all come to think, how can we revive this "song tune" and this "splendid dance danced flutteringly"?
In any era, in any place, it is not the masses of the time but only the artists who make such efforts to connect the fading light. Among artists, it is those with the most passion (熱) and sincerity (精誠) who carry this forward. And now, beautiful new light is heard on the first dawn of the new year. It is the appearance of Mr. Han Sŏngjun and Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi.

Meeting at Myŏngwŏlgwan—Researching by Actually Dancing
The very beginning of the matter was some time ago.
One cold winter evening.
The place: Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月舘).
Those gathered:
Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi (崔承喜氏)
Mr. Han Sŏngjun (韓成俊氏)
Mr. Kim Hyŏngjun (金炯駿氏)
Mr. Hyŏn Ch'ŏl (玄哲氏)
Mr. Ch'oe Sŭng'il (崔承一氏)
Mr. An Mak (安漠氏)
and others. That Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi is a dancer receiving the most respect in the world, pouring her creative genius through 'tansu' (dance) or new-style dance, needs no further mention. But more than that, it also needs no further mention that she achieved great significance by creating and presenting various types of Korean dance (朝鮮舞踊) intertwined with the sentiment of the Peninsula (半島情緖), old dances like "Ehera, Noara" and "Arirang," featuring them whenever she performed at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall (京城府民舘) or toured various other regions. Furthermore, as for Mr. Han Sŏngjun, it needs no further explanation that he is the sole great master who, skilled at playing the changgo (장고) from early on, also knows how to perform ancient dances (古무용) passed down from the very, very distant Three Kingdoms period. And Mr. Kim Hyŏngjun, who attended that night, is famous as a folk dancer (民俗무용가) deeply versed (造詣깁흔) in the southern arts buried in the Southern Korea (南鮮) region. Besides them, Mr. Hyŏn Ch'ŏl is also a folklore researcher, and Messrs. Ch'oe Sŭng'il and An Mak are also individuals striving to revive the classical arts of the peninsula.
These various people gathered in one place. They gathered, talked about the new and the old, showed each other, and thus strove to discover the good points. Now, to describe the scene that night, first:

Mr. Han Sŏngjun: "Until becoming 64 this year, I have solely researched Korean Dance (朝鮮舞踊) theoretically and practically, but the Korean (朝鮮) populace still lacks understanding of dance, so dancers have been recognized as a kind of lowly entertainer (藝人). Therefore, naturally, I lacked the courage to widely reveal myself as a dancer before the public, nor did I even have the opportunity to do so. Even the Korean populace, who so lacked understanding of dance, have come to reappraise Korean Dance thanks to Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's astonishing dance, and the joy (깃붐) of that is indescribable. What I wish to entrust (願托) to Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi is for her to inherit the 50-odd types of Korean Dance I have researched to this day and ensure that Korean Dance lives on forever. If that does not happen, the Korean Dance I have researched will die along with my death."

Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi: "What I have researched and performed to this day is the modernization of Korean Dance, that is to say, I have created about 30 types of dance by learning good aspects from Western Dance (西洋舞踊) so that Westerners can also understand them. This is the Korean Dance I intend to introduce on my upcoming trip to Europe and America (歐米)."

After the long exchange of opinions between old master Han Sŏngjun and Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi ended, an actual mutual demonstration of dance began— Many people have seen Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi dancing on stage, but it is rare to see her dancing without makeup, still in Western clothes, tying a towel (수건) and explaining as she dances. Not only the Myŏngwŏlgwan office staff and the 'boys' (뽀이), but even guests and kisaeng (기생) engrossed in amusement in various rooms scrambled to watch, causing a commotion. Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's "Dancing Girl" (舞女) seemed to strike a chord with old master Han Sŏngjun and Mr. Kim Hyŏngjun; both gentlemen repeatedly uttered exclamations of admiration. The "Crane Dance" (鶴춤) by old master Han Sŏngjun, over 60, and Mr. Kim Hyŏngjun's Southern Korean Folk Dance (南鮮民俗舞踊) "Tŏtpaegi Ch'um" (덧배기춤) also seemed to have many points to teach Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi; her whole body became eyes watching.

Establishment of a Music, Dance Research Society?—Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi Donates 600 Won
After the mutual dance demonstration ended, opinions soon aligned, and it was decided to organize the Korean Music and Dance Research Society (朝鮮音樂舞踊硏究會). Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi volunteered to contribute 600 won for the founding preparation fund. However, as Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi was leaving Tokyo (東京) on December 25th for Korean Dance performances (朝鮮舞踊公演) (Continued on page 42, lower section)<43> heading for Europe and America (歐米), she could not become a founding committee member but promised to support indirectly as an advisor during her trip and to be active on the front lines after her return. So, what is the news of the Society's founding thereafter? A sign for the Korean Music and Dance Research Society Founding Office has been hung at Mr. Han Sŏngjun's residence in Kyŏngun-dong (慶雲町) in the city, and the founding committee chairman, Mr. Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, is vigorously active. It is said that the recruited members already number over 60, and they plan to construct a hall and recruit research students by March of next year (明年) at the latest.
After Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi returns, the Society's headquarters will be in Seoul (서울), but the place of activity, meaning the performance venue, will be in Tokyo (東京), and Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi plans to actually perform in major theaters throughout mainland Japan (日本內地).<42>

**Summary**

This 1938 news report details a significant meeting at Myŏngwŏlgwan between the internationally renowned modern dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi and the elderly master of traditional Korean dance, Han Sŏngjun. Driven by a shared desire to revive and preserve classical Korean dance and music, which Han feared would die with him due to lack of public appreciation, they discussed their respective approaches – Han's preservation of ancient forms and Ch'oe's modernization for international appeal. The meeting led to mutual dance demonstrations and the decision to establish the Korean Music and Dance Research Society, funded initially by Ch'oe's donation. Although Ch'oe was departing for an international tour, plans were underway, led by Hyŏn Ch'ŏl, to establish the society with Han Sŏngjun, aiming for a physical space and students by early 1939, with future performances planned in Tokyo upon Ch'oe's return.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it captures the dynamic interplay between tradition and modernity in Korean arts under Japanese colonial rule. It highlights conscious efforts by prominent Korean cultural figures to safeguard and promote national heritage amidst assimilation pressures, showcases the generational dialogue about cultural preservation (Han entrusting his legacy to Ch'oe), and reflects the complex cultural positioning of Korean artists within the Japanese empire, planning activities in both Seoul and Tokyo.

古典「舞踊」과 「音樂」을 부흥식히고저, 崔承喜·韓成俊 兩巨匠 會見
「얼화만소-
얼화 대세니라」

이러한 멋진 노래곡조를 서양사람이 백번 죽은들 해여 볼 길 잇스랴, 마찬가지로 곳갈쓰고 장삼입고 기는 듯, 안는 듯, 연하고 부드럽게 추는 「僧舞」라거나 칼자루 나근나근 나붓기며 기개잇게 추는 「劍舞」라거나 이런 것을 어느 누구 출 길이 잇스랴, 이것은 오직 우리네만이 출 수 잇고, 우리네 중에도 연소한 이들은 그를 보아도 감상할 눈이 점점 어두어간다.

여러 천년, 여러 백년을 두고 이 땅 흙속에서 비저 지어낸 고전적 음악과 무용이 차츰 차츰 시대 사람의 안광뒤에 숨어버린다는 것은 참으로 앗가운 일이다.
그래서 그 예술의 향기에 한번 저저난 사람들은 엇더케 하면 이 「노랫가락」이 「너덜너덜 추는 멋진 춤」을 살려볼 수 잇슬가 하고 모다들 생각하게 되엿다.
엇든 시대 엇든 곳에서든지 이러한 노력을 하어 사라지는 빗을 잇어내는 것은 그때의 대중이 아니고 오직 예술가인 것이다. 예술가 중에도 가장 熱이 잇고 정성이 잇는 이들이 하여온다. 그런데 이제 아름다운 새빗이 새해 첫 새벽에 들니여온다. 그는 韓成俊氏와 崔承喜氏의 등장이다.
