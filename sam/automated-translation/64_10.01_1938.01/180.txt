Masterpiece of War, War and Peace
						By Tolstoy

From 1805 to 1812, spanning about seven years, there were three major conflicts between France and Russia. The Battle of Schöngrabern, the Battle of Austerlitz, and the Battle of Borodino are the three great battles referred to from that time. Among these, the Battle of Borodino, in particular, was a fierce battle where the two great powers of East and West fought without regard for their nation's fate, shining brilliantly as one of the histories in modern military history.

Napoleon, the greatest hero born of France, harbored grand plans to conquer Russia at this time and rushed eastward with bold courage. Meanwhile, the Russian Emperor Alexander the Great, concerned for his motherland's future, initiated a fierce great battle against Napoleon's forces in 1812 at Borodino, outside Moscow.

However, the outcome of this war was such that both armies suffered damage to the extent they couldn't easily regroup, making it difficult to determine victory or defeat at the time. The Russian army temporarily withdrew eastward from Moscow to reorganize its troops. Along with this, the people also abandoned Moscow and fled. Taking advantage of this gap, the French army, greatly distressed and feeling fatigued from the war, was able to quarter its soldiers in the abandoned Moscow and occupy this great city without any effort.

However, fate did not allow them to be permanently fortunate there but made them taste hardship again and again, because not long after the French army entered Moscow, fierce fires broke out on all sides of the city. Thus, in an instant, the fierce flames ended up engulfing the entire city. With their quarters burned, provisions lost, and furthermore, due to the cold northern weather, they found it impossible to sustain their lives and were forced to withdraw the army from Moscow. It is said that the valiant activity of the brave partisans was most effective in repulsing the French army like this. As yesterday's victors, today resembling insignificant beggars, the French army barely gathered its remaining soldiers and headed back to their homeland. Leo Tolstoy's "War and Peace" is a work written with these three great wars as its background and can be seen as a grand epic poem in modern Russian society. Truly, this novel is an outstanding war novel as well as a historical novel.

Many historically famous figures, including Alexander the Great, play active roles in this work. Not only that, but its content also records the reality of the aristocratic society of the time with astonishing brushwork. Even looking at the themes handled here, they display Tolstoy's unique technique unseen in any other writer. It has already been mentioned that this work is a historical or social epic poem of modern Russia, but besides that, this novel indeed includes many problems and features many characters.

Regarding the issue of models, he said,
"Writers often confine themselves to describing only people they have seen or personally know, but if that's the case, where on earth is the meaning of creation?"
Although Tolstoy was extremely reluctant to reveal specifics about the models for his works within the scope of his knowledge, this was not the case for this novel. In this novel, historical figures were particularly presented under their real names. The old Prince Bolkonsky (브루곤스기 父공작) who appears in this work was Tolstoy's maternal grandfather, and Marya (마리아) was his mother. [Translator's Note: Princess Marya Bolkonskaya was inspired by Tolstoy's maternal grandmother; the article seems slightly inaccurate here, potentially confusing the character with Tolstoy's mother, Maria Nikolayevna née Princess Volkonskaya].

It has been established that the heroine Natasha (나-타샤) was Tolstoy's sister-in-law, Tatyana Kuzminskaya (다쟈-나 구즈망스가야) [Translator's Note: Tatyana Behrs, later Kuzminskaya, was Leo Tolstoy's wife's younger sister]. Tolstoy's son Alexander wrote the following interesting record about the aunt who was the model for Natasha:
"The aunt, who lived at Yasnaya Polyana (야스나야 보리야냐) until the age of 75, once told stories of the life she experienced. About going hunting with father, about being tempted by Anatole (아나도-리) (in the novel, it is written that she was actually tempted by Anatole), and also, sometimes when aunt [likely referring to Tatyana] sang, father would, almost unconsciously, sketch aunt's mood and her inner experiences just as they were, which was truly deeply moving."
he stated. Because Tolstoy advocated for art for life's sake in his later years, he negated his past works, but if the "correct reflection of objective reality" is the primary condition determining the value of an artwork, "War and Peace" will certainly not only be the most excellent among all of Tolstoy's works but also the highest peak in world literature. It is said that in his later years, the author considered it a useless work, to the extent that he even forgot its content.
An anecdote remains where one day, hearing a lady acquaintance reading "War and Peace" nearby, he remarked, "I don't know whose work it is, but it's extremely well-written."
However, in reality, it is said that there probably wasn't any work into which Tolstoy himself put as much effort and spirit as this one. It is said that every time he wrote the manuscript for this novel, he put in infinite effort revising it. His wife, without regard for her busy time raising children, helped with the transcription every night, and it is said she rewrote it seven times. Anyway, there is no room for doubt that this novel is recognized as a first-class work in terms of its value. It is worth reading even just for attracting the reader's interest. As already mentioned, it will sufficiently capture the reader's interest not only as a war novel, historical novel, and social novel, but also as a romance novel. If there are readers who avoid this work because its volume is too large, it means a great loss for them. By reading it once, we will surely come to possess the important inner meaning this work holds.
-The End- <30>

**Summary**

This 1938 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* introduces Leo Tolstoy's novel "War and Peace" to its readers. It outlines the historical backdrop of the Napoleonic Wars between France and Russia (1805-1812), focusing on the Battles of Schöngrabern, Austerlitz, and especially Borodino and the subsequent French occupation and retreat from Moscow. The article praises the novel as an outstanding war, historical, and social epic, highlighting its realistic portrayal of Russian aristocratic society and its inclusion of historical figures alongside fictional characters, mentioning the real-life inspirations for some characters like Natasha Rostova and the Bolkonskys. It also touches upon Tolstoy's later disavowal of the work despite its monumental effort (including his wife's extensive help in rewriting) and enduring literary value, urging readers not to be deterred by its length. For historians studying colonial Korea, this article is interesting as it reflects the introduction and reception of major Western literary works during that period. Its framing of the novel—emphasizing historical grandeur, national struggle (Russia vs. France), and the human element—might subtly resonate with or reflect contemporary Korean sentiments under Japanese rule, while the focus on a Russian classic showcases the complex cultural landscape beyond just Japanese or traditional Korean influences.

戰爭名著, 戰爭과 平和
						톨스토이 作
1805년으로부터 1812년까지 약 7년동안 佛蘭西와 露西亞 두 나라 사이에는 세 번의 큰 알륵이 있었다. 시엔구라-뱅戰, 아우스데루릿지戰, 보로지노戰이 곧 그때의 3大戰을 말함이다. 그 중에서도 특히 보로지노戰 같은 것은 동서2대국이 자기나라의 흥망을 헤아리지 않고 싸흔 激戰으로 근대 戰史上에 찬연히 빛나는 역사의 하나다.
佛蘭西가 낳은 최대의 영웅 나폴네온은 이때에 露西亞를 정복하려는 장한 계획을 품고 대담한 勇力을 가하여 동방으로 달었다. 그때에 露西亞皇帝 알넥산더 대제는 모국의 장래를 우려하는 일편 1812년에 나폴네온 군대를 향하여 모스크와 교외 브로지나르에서 격열한 대전을 시작하였다.
그러나 이 전쟁의 결과에 있어서는 양군이 다시 재기하지 못하리만치 서로 손상을 입고 그 당시에는 승패여부를 쉽사리 알수없게 되었다. 露西亞軍은 일시 군대를 정리하기 위하여 모소코봐에서 동으로 **하였다. 이와함께 백성들도 모스코봐를 버리고 다라났다. 이틈을 타서 전쟁에 매우 애*해지고 피곤을 感한 佛蘭西軍은 백성들이 버리고간 모스코봐에서 兵隊를 收容시키고 아모 노력없이 이 대도회를 점령할 수가 있었다.
그러나 운명은 저들을 그곳에서 영구히 福되게 하지 못하고 고난을 재삼 맛보게 하였으니 까닭은 佛蘭西軍이 모스코봐에 입성한지 얼마되지 않어 성 사방에서 화재가 맹렬히 일어났다. 그리하여 삽시간에 猛火는 全市를 덮어버리고 말었다. 宿舍가 타고 양식을 잃고 더욱이 북방의 찬 일기로 인해서 그들은 도저히 생명을 유지해 갈 수 없게 되여 모스코봐로부터 군대를 물니치지 않을수 없었다. 이같이 佛蘭西軍을 격퇴식힌 때에 큰 공로자는 용감한 바루지잔의 활약이 가장 유효하였다 한다. 昨日의 *者로 오늘은 보잘것없는 걸인 모양으로 佛蘭西軍은 자국의 병정을 겨우 거두워 갖이고 고국으로 향하였다. 레오톨스토이의 「전쟁과 평화」는 이 3대전을 **으로하고 저술된 근대 露西亞사회에 있어서는 일대 서사시라 볼 수 있는 작품이다. 진실노 이 소설은 **할 만한 전쟁소설인 동시에 역사소설이다.
이 작품에는 알넥산더대제를 비롯하여 역사상 저명한 인물이 많이 활약하고 있다. 뿐만아니라 그 내용에 있어서는 당시 귀족사회의 현실같은 것이 놀나운 필치로 기록되여 있다. 여기에 취급된「테마」만 보드라도 여하한 작가에게서도 볼 수 없는 톨스토이 독단적 기술을 나타내고 있다 이 작품이 근대 로서아 역사적, 혹은 사회적 서사시라하는 것은 임이 말하였거니와 其 외에도 이 소설은 실노 많은 문제를 포함하고 또는 많은 인간을 등장식혔다고 볼수 있다.
모델문제에 대해서 그는
「흔이 작가들은 자기가 본 사람이거나 혹은 친히 아는 사람만을 묘사하는데 끈치는데 만약 그렇다면 창작의 의미란 대체 어대 있을 것이냐」
고 톨스토이는 자기작품 모델에 대해서 극히 아는 범위내에서 발표하는 것을 꺼려했으나 이 소설에 있어서는 그렇지 않은것도 있었다. 이 소설에는 특히 역사상의 인물(본명)그대로 발표하기도 하였다. 이 작품에 나오는 브루곤스기 父공작은 톨스토이의 어머니의 조부요 마리아는 그의 어머니었다.
여주인공 나-타샤는 톨스토이의 동생 다쟈-나 구즈망스가야인 것이 판명되였다. 톨스토이의 *者 알넥산더는 이 나타샤의 모델인 叔母에 대하여 다음과 같은 자미있는 기록을 쓴 일이 있다.
