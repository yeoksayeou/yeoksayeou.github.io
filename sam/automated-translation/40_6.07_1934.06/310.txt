The Golden Age of Genius - Scientists

Nobel, famous for the Nobel Prize, achieved mass production of nitroglycerin (니도로 구리세링) at age 30.
The invention of dynamite was at age 33.
Edison invented the phonograph at age 30.
Curie discovered radium (라디움) at age 39.<66>

**Summary**

This short article lists the ages at which prominent scientists Nobel, Edison, and Curie made major breakthroughs, suggesting that significant scientific achievement often occurs around one's thirties. From a colonial history perspective, the article reflects Korea's engagement with Western scientific progress and figures during a period of modernization under Japanese rule. It highlights an interest in the concept of 'genius' and the timing of peak productivity, potentially serving as inspiration or a point of comparison for Korean intellectuals and students at the time.
