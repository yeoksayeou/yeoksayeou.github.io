The Peak Period of Geniuses - Politicians

Nap'aryun became Emperor by plebiscite at the age of 35. Mussorini became Prime Minister of It'aeri (Italy) at 36.
Maktonaldu became the leader of the Yŏnggilli (British) Nodongtang (Labour Party) at 35. P'oangk'are of Pŭllansŏ (France) became a Minister at 33.
Hannibal defeated the great army of Roma (Rome) at the age of 31. <62>

**Summary**

This short article lists several prominent historical political and military figures, noting the young age at which they achieved significant power or success (between 31 and 36 years old). It highlights Napoleon, Mussolini, Ramsay MacDonald, Raymond Poincaré, and Hannibal as examples of individuals reaching their "peak" early in their careers. For historians studying colonial Korea, this piece reflects an interest in global political figures and the theme of youthful achievement and leadership during a period when Koreans themselves lacked political sovereignty. It implicitly contrasts the perceived dynamism of these (mostly Western) figures with the constrained political situation in Korea under Japanese rule.
