Magazine Title: 삼천리 제6권 제7호
Publication Date: 1934년 06월 01일
Article Title: 人生揭示板 (Life Bulletin Board)
Author: None Listed
Article Type: 소식 (News/Updates)

For various reasons, these are the people currently living alone (獨居) in solitude (孤獨) in a spacious room, spending their fleeting youth (덧업는 靑春). Here they are:

Poet Pyŏn Suju (卞樹州)

Novelist Ch'oe Tokkyŏn (崔獨鵑)

Painter Na Hyesŏk (羅蕙錫)

Novelist Kim Ilyŏp (金一葉) <37>

**Summary**

This brief news item lists four prominent Korean cultural figures—poet Pyŏn Suju, novelists Ch'oe Tokkyŏn and Kim Ilyŏp, and painter Na Hyesŏk—who were reportedly living alone at the time. The article notes they are spending their "fleeting youth" in solitude for various unspecified reasons. For historians, this piece offers a small but potentially poignant snapshot of the personal circumstances of intellectuals and artists during the Japanese colonial period, hinting at potential social isolation, economic hardship, or personal choices that led to these living situations.
