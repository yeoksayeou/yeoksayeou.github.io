**Body Text Translation:**
Gandhi the Man and His Recent Movement
						Cho Man-sik

The Lord's path opens to the brave
The Lord's path closes to the timid
Offer your life, your all
And praise the Lord's name

He who forsakes children, wife, property, life
All these he abandons
Only he drinks the cup the Lord gives

He who dives for pearls in the Brahma
Does not heed the danger to his life
And enters the depths of the sea

He does not fear death
He forgets all calamities
Standing atop a rock in the great sea<27>
He who does not dive in gains nothing

The path of love is a path made of fire
The timid avoid it
He who leaps into the flames
Receives eternal blessings
He who stays apart, does not approach closely
And only watches from afar, is burned to death by the flame"

Love is endlessly precious
Only through devotion can love be obtained
He who offers devotion obtains love

Only the possessor of heroic inspiration, wrapped in the Lord's love
Is truly a person of love
Heaven speaks, day and night without distinction
Only to that person are the Lord's workings visible

This is a passage from the "Hindu Hymn" that Gandhi sang morning and evening during his historically famous 21-day long fast in October 1924. Indeed, for twenty days, Gandhi did not drink even a drop of water, only sunbathing and singing this song morning and evening. He survived through the power of religion, believing in the Lord.

Placing these words at the beginning, I shall now discuss the character of Gandhi. First, let me briefly recount his<28> family background and path of growth.
Gandhi had a famous statesman for his grandfather. I am unsure from how many generations back, but in any case, for three generations, the position of Prime Minister was held. Gandhi was born the youngest among three sons and three daughters. After finishing elementary school in a place called Rajkot, following his father, he entered higher elementary school at the age of twelve. His grades in elementary school were average, and being quite shy, he kept apart from his friends, befriending only books.
Following Indian custom, he married at the age of 13. He found this extremely painful. Around the time of his marriage, Gandhi eagerly bought and devoured small pamphlets published and circulated at the time, costing 10 or 20 *chon*, on topics like "Marital Love," "Methods of Making Money," and "Marriage with a Wife."
In 1887, he passed the high school entrance examination and went to study abroad in England. Upon departure, before his religiously strict mother, Gandhi pledged three things:
"Not to drink alcohol
Not to associate with women
Not to eat meat."
During his studies in England, he first devoted all his energy to the English language, speaking, seeing, and reading freely. After that, wanting to know the true state of British society, he temporarily abandoned his vow of vegetarianism, frequented dance halls, and even danced.
A characteristic feature of this student period was his self-imposed strictness regarding finances. He meticulously recorded every single *chon* spent in an account book. Looking at his account book, there was nothing missing – newspaper fees, miscellaneous ledger costs, tram fares, everything was there. According to his autobiography, he did this<29>
"So that even when handling hundreds of thousands of won (圓) in public funds, I would manage expenditures strictly and economically, never incurring debt, always showing a surplus, as preparation for the future, to cultivate that habit."
This is essential conduct that anyone should learn, and valuable words indeed.
Consequently, he eventually succeeded in halving his tuition fees. In London, he obtained his barrister's license and returned to his homeland, setting up practice in Bombay.
Then, he decided to go to South Africa and departed. In South Africa, Indians were suffering hardships. There, in his spare time from court appearances, he witnessed the tearful circumstances of the immigrant Indian society and studied them extensively.
After that, he returned to his homeland. And he made a great resolution: to rescue his fellow countrymen from their current misfortune through the paths of religion, education, and politics.
While his history and movement history will be discussed as opportunities arise below, I will first describe the spirit and rules of what can be called Gandhi's fundamental thought and the foundation of foundations, the "Institution for Holding Fast to Truth" (Satyagraha Ashram - in Indian language), which he founded with all his heart and soul, and then state my opinion.<30>

Rules of the Satyagraha Ashram

Purpose

The purpose of this institution is to learn the methods of service and put them into practice.

Distinctions

This institution is divided into three classes: Directors, Probationers, and Students.<30>

Directors

Directors must personally practice the following rules to learn the way of service to India.

1. Vow of Truth (Satya)
It is not sufficient for a person merely to believe in not falling into untruth.
Lies must not be told, even when for the welfare of the nation.
Truth must be believed, even if it means opposing parents or siblings.

2. Vow of Non-violence (Ahimsa)
It is not sufficient merely not to kill animals. One must not harm even those believed to be unjust, must not become angry, but rather must love them.

3. Vow of Celibacy (Brahmacharya)
Without observing the vow of celibacy, observing the above two vows is difficult.
This vow is not sufficiently fulfilled merely by not casting lustful glances at women.
One must prevent animal desires from arising in the heart. Even if married, one must not view one's wife with passion but treat her as a lifelong companion, maintaining the relationship with complete purity throughout.

4. Control of the Palate (Asvada)
Those who wish to serve India must limit their food intake to what is necessary for bodily sustenance and forbid foods that stimulate animal desires.

5. Vow of Non-stealing (Asteya)<31>
It is not sufficient merely to refrain from stealing others' possessions.
Nature provides individuals with necessities, but no more; therefore, using things one does not need is also the same as stealing.

6. Vow of Non-possession (Aparigraha)
One must not possess much, and things unnecessary for the body must not be possessed.
For example, if one can do without a chair, one should live without it. One must deeply consider daily life and simplify it.

Addenda

1. Swadeshi (Use of local goods)
If there is a possibility of falsehood in an object or its maker, using that object is inconsistent with truth. For example, since items made in factories in Manchester, Germany, or India cannot be said to be free of falsehood, believers in truth should not use them. Furthermore, laborers suffer in all these factories, and the firepower used in factories harms the workers' health and shortens their lifespans. Therefore, foreign goods and products of complex machinery should be forbidden to believers in non-violence.
Originally, abandoning hand-woven goods and wearing foreign items is only because they appear aesthetically pleasing due to habit, but one should avoid the artificial beauty of the body and use simple items, simple fabrics. And Swadeshi must be thoroughly applied to all aspects of life.

1. Fearlessness (Abhaya)
Since one who fears cannot uphold truth or anything else, Directors must not fear people, classes,<32> relatives, thieves, robbers, ferocious beasts like tigers, and even "death." One who is truly fearless must protect oneself solely by the power of truth, that is, by the power of the spirit.

3. Hand-spinning Wheel (Charkha)
Directors, believing that one of the main causes of India's poverty is the virtual disappearance of the cotton spinning wheel and handloom, must themselves turn the spinning wheel and strive to revive this industry.

4. Politics
Politics, economics, or anything else should not be thought of as independent disciplines, but all should be considered rooted in "Religion."
The above generally covers the main points. If we listen to Gandhi's speech given in Madras on the day this religious educational institution was founded, February 16, 1916, his fundamental assertion becomes even clearer. The gist of the speech is: "Now, on the occasion of establishing the Ashram (i.e., the said institution), I wish to tell you, gentlemen. What I have constantly realized throughout my public life is that what we desire now, what the nation desires, indeed, perhaps what all the people of the world desire, is truly the transformation of human nature. This matter was the profound ideal of our great patriot, Mr. Tagore. (Applause) I believe you generally know his speeches, but his oft-repeated phrase worth noting is, 'If we have aspirations but lack the corresponding character behind them, even if we achieve them, they have no value, nor can we achieve them.' From this viewpoint, he organized the great association called the 'Servants of India Society' (印度公僕會). In the prospectus of this society, he passionately<33> advocates the necessity of spiritualizing the political world. As you know, he always laments that our average level is lower than that of Europeans. As my political teacher, I do not know whether Mr. Tagore's explanation is correct or incorrect, but among educated Indians, his words are said to be right. In any case, whatever work a person does, however great the enterprise, if it lacks a religious background, it cannot truly prosper. Then the question follows, what is religion? When I speak of religion, I do not mean the religions you find by looking through the scriptures of the world, that is, not a grasp by the brain, but a grasp by the heart. If you do anything in the right way and support it, you can obtain it there."
Thus he climaxed. In essence, the purpose of this Satyagraha Ashram, which creates his extensions – disciples and followers, and also comrades – is to resolve the Indian problem through ascetic life based on religion and religious spiritual power. This truth was discovered through personally confronting, experiencing, striving, learning, and teaching countless social, political, and religious problems; it can be called the synthesized, supreme, and final intention.
Therefore, he is not only disseminating this truth to numerous young Indians but is also upholding it with his own life. Even now, on any given day, he spins the wheel for four hours, contemplating and wishing to save the 350 million fellow people of India through this path.
Speaking of India, with a population of 350 million and an area of 1.8 million square miles, truly accounting for one-fifth of the entire world's population, the future of this India and the direction of its leader, Gandhi, are the focus of global attention. With this, my pen will now turn to Gandhi the man and his movement. **Summary**

This article by Cho Man-sik introduces Mahatma Gandhi, focusing on his background, character, and the philosophical underpinnings of his movement, particularly his emphasis on religion as the basis for social and political action. It details Gandhi's personal vows (truth, non-violence, celibacy, non-stealing, non-possession, control of palate, Swadeshi, fearlessness) as enshrined in the rules of the Satyagraha Ashram, which aimed to cultivate individuals capable of serving India through spiritual discipline and self-reliance (symbolized by hand-spinning). Cho highlights Gandhi's belief, shared with Tagore, that transforming human nature and spiritualizing politics are essential for national progress. The piece sets the stage for a deeper discussion of Gandhi's movement, framing it as a religiously grounded endeavor.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is significant because it shows a prominent Korean nationalist leader, Cho Man-sik (often called the "Gandhi of Korea"), analyzing and presenting Gandhi's philosophy of non-violent resistance, self-discipline, and spiritual grounding to a Korean audience in 1934. It reflects the interest among Korean intellectuals and nationalists in alternative strategies for achieving independence from Japanese rule, particularly those emphasizing moral strength, self-sufficiency (like promoting local products, akin to Swadeshi), and non-violent struggle, mirroring aspects of Cho's own nationalist activities. Historians might find Cho's framing of Gandhi's movement, especially the emphasis on religious/moral character as the foundation for political action, revealing about the ideological currents within the Korean nationalist movement during this period.

하엿다고 한다. 누구나 배워 둘 要緊한 行實이요, 貴한 말이다.

眞理把持敎育所의 規律

目的

本所에 目的은 奉仕의 方法을 배우고 이것을 實行할 일.

區別

本所는 理事者, 候補生 及 學生의 3階級에 分함.<30>

理事者

理事者는 印度에 奉仕하는 길을 배우기 위하야 아래의 規律을 躬行할 일.

1 理眞의 맹세
사람은 그저 不眞理에 빠지지 안키만을 밋어서는 充分치 못하다.
거짓말은 國家의 福利를 위할 境遇에라도 행하지 말일.
眞理는 父母, 兄弟에 背反할지라도 밋을 일.

2 非暴力의 맹세
動物을 殺害치 안는 것만으로는 不充分하다. 不正이라고 信하는 者도 害치면 안되고 怒하면  안되고 도로혀 사랑하지 안어서는 안 된다.

3 獨身의 맹세
이 우 두 가지는 獨身의 맹세를 직히지 안으면 직히기 어럽다.
이 맹세는 婦人에게 秋波을 보내지 안을 程度만으로는 不充分하다.
잘 動物慾을 心中에 이러나지 못하게 할 것이다. 만일 結婚할지라도 妻를 劣情視하지 말고 生涯의 벗으로 하야 그 關係를 完全히 純潔로써 一貫할 것이다.

4 食事의 節制
印度에 奉仕코저 원하는 者는 食事는 그저 身體의 支持에 必要한 程度로 또 動物慾을 자극할 食事는 禁할 일.

5 도적질하지 안을 맹세<31>
항상, 남의 所有物을 도적질하지 말자는 程度로만은 不充分할 일.
自然은 個人에게 必要品을 준다. 그러나 더는 아니 주는 것이기 때문에 쓸데 업는 것을 씀은 亦是 도적하는 것과 마천가지다.

6 不所持의 맹세
만히 所持치 말고 그리고 身體에 不要한 것은 所持하면 아니 된다.
例하면 倚子업시도 괜찬타면 그대로 업시 지내어야 한다. 日常 깁히 考慮하여 그 生活을 簡單히 할 일.

附則

1 國産
物件 及 物件의 製作者에 거짓의 可能性이 잇다 하면 그 物件을 씀은 眞理에 一致치 안는   다. 例하면 만체스타- 獨逸 又는 印度等의 工場에서 된 物品은 거짓이 업다고 할 수 업슴으로 眞理의 信仰者는 이것을 使用치 말 일, 더욱 勞働者들은 이 모든 工場에서 고생하고 잇스며 또 工場에서 쓰는 火力때문에 勞働者의 健康을 傷케 하고 그 天命을 줄이고  잇슴으로 外國品 及 複雜한 機械의 製品은 非暴力의 信者에게는 禁制할 일.
元來 手織品을 버리고 外國品을 입는 것은 다만 習慣上 美的으로 보이는 때문이나 身體의 技巧美는 이를 避하여 簡單한 物品 簡單한 織物을 쓸 일이요. 그리고 國産이 生活의 各 方面에 徹底함을 要할 일.

1 두려워하지 말 일
두려위하는 者는 眞理도 아모 것도 직힐 수 업슴으로 理事者는 人民, 階級,<32> 親族, 竊盜, 强盜, 猛虎와 가튼 猛獸 及 「죽엄」까지 무서워 말일, 진실로 두려워하지 안는 사람은 다만 眞理의 힘에 의하여 즉 靈의 힘에 의하여만 自己를 防護할 일.

3 手織機
理事者들은 이 印度의 貧困의 主要한 原因 中 한가지는 木綿紡織車 及 手織車의 事實上의 消滅인 것을 信條로 하여 스사로 手織機를 돌니며 이 産業을 復活하기에 努力할 일.

4 政治
政治, 經濟 其他 엇던 것이나 獨立한 學科로 생각지 말고 모다 「宗敎」에 뿌리를 두엇다고 생각할 일.
大體以上의 그 要領이다. 이 宗敎敎育所를 創設하든 날 즉 1916年 2月 16日에 「마드라스」에서 한 간듸의 演說을 드르면 그의 根本主張이 더욱 明確하여 질 터이라, 그 演說의 개요는 「나는 이제 「아시람」 (즉 該所)를를 設立함에 際하야 諸君에게 말하고저 하노라. 나의 公生涯中에 不絶히 늘 깨다른 點은 지금은 우리들이 바라는 것이 國民이 바라는 것이, 아니 아마 全世界의 사람들이 모다 바라는 것이 실로 人性의 改造인 줄 암니다. 이 일은 우리들의 大愛國者 타고아氏의 深遠한 理想이엇슴니다. (喝釆)諸君은 氏의 演說을 大槪 아실 줄 암니다만은 注意할 氏의 常套語는 「我等은 願望하지만 그 背后에 이에 相應하는 性格을 가지지 못한다 할진대 이를 到達한다 할지라도 何等의 價値업스며 또 到達할 수도 업는 것이오」라고 함이외다. 이러한 見地에서 氏는 「印度公僕會」라는 大團體를 組織한 것이외다, 이 會의 趣旨書에서 氏는 熱心으로<33> 政治界를 精神的되게 할 必要를 高唱하고 잇슴니다. 諸君은 아실 터이지만 氏는 항상 我等의 平均點은 歐羅巴人보다 낫다고 歎息하고 잇슴니다. 나는 나의 政治上의 스승으로서 타고아氏의 이 說明이 맛고 안맛고는 잘 모르겟슴니다만은 敎育잇는 印度人사이에서는 氏의 말이 올타고 함니다. 엇잿든 엇더한 사람이 하는 일이든지 엇더케 큰 事業이든지, 萬一 거기에 宗敎的 背景이 缺如한다 하면 참으로 繁榮하지 못할 것이라 함이외다. 그러면 宗敎란 무엇이냐라 하는 문제가 뒤에 따라옴니다. 나는 종교라 함을 제군이 세계 中의 經典을 둘처 보고 안 그런 宗敎들 말함이 아니라 즉 頭惱로서의 把持가 아니라 마음으로서의 把持를 가르침이외다. 무슨 일이든지 바른 方法으로 하고 그리고 그것을 支持만 하면 거기에 그것이 어들 수 잇슴니다.」
라고 高潮하엿다. 요컨대 그의 延長인 弟子와 信徒을 만드는, 또 同志를 만드는 이 眞理把持所의 目的은 宗敎的의 禁慾生活과 宗敎的인 精神的 힘으로 印度問題를 解決하자는 것이니 이 眞理을 發見하기까지에는 그는 無數한 社會問題, 政治問題, 宗敎問題를 몸으로서 당하고 體驗하고 애쓰고 배우고 가르킨데서 엇어 된 즉 綜合된 最高最終의 意思라 할 수 잇다.
그래서 그는 이러한 眞理를 多數한 印度靑年예게 보급식히고 잇슬 뿐더러 自己自身이 生命으로써 직히여 가고 잇슴으로 지금도 엇던 날이든지 하로 네시간 물네 저스며 印度 3億 5千萬의 同族을 이 길을 통하야 救濟코저 念하고 願하고 잇다.
印度라 하면 人口 3億 5千萬 面積 180萬平方哩, 실로 人口數에 잇서 全世界의 5分의 1일을 차지하고 잇는 이 印度의 前途와 그의 指導者 간듸의 動向은 世界의 注視의 標的이 된다. 이에 나의 붓은 이제부터 간듸의 人物 及 그의 運動에 미처보리라. (編輯子 附記 紙數關係로 不得已 이번에는 序論에 끗치고 次號부터 本論을 連載하겟슴니다.)<34>
