The Flourishing of Shakespearean Plays in Europe

The flourishing of Shakespearean (沙翁劇) plays on the European continent is quite astonishing. Among these, in Germany (獨逸), the Bavarian National Theater in Munich (뮤눈) is planning to perform the complete works of Shakespearean plays once over the three years from 1934 to 1936. In the first year, they plan to mix in some other plays slightly, but for the two years of 1935 and 1936, designated as a Shakespeare commemoration, they intend to continue performing only Shakespearean plays.

The first play to be staged is said to be "Richard II" (리챠-드2世).<101>

**Summary**

This news brief reports on the remarkable popularity of Shakespearean plays across Europe in 1934. It specifically highlights a major initiative by the Bavarian National Theater in Munich, Germany, which plans a three-year cycle (1934-1936) to perform Shakespeare's entire canon, dedicating 1935 and 1936 exclusively to his works as a commemoration, starting with "Richard II". For historians, this article reveals Korean awareness of and interest in contemporary European high culture, specifically theatre trends in Germany, during the colonial period. It reflects a window into the international cultural information circulating within Korea at the time.
