Magazine Title: 삼천리 第六卷 第七號
Publication Date: 1934년 06월 01일
Article Title: 人生揭示板 (Life's Bulletin Board)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 소식 (News/Tidings)

Life's Bulletin Board

Here are those who are in relationships such as between father-in-law and son-in-law (翁婿間), or wife's brother and husband's sister's husband (妻男男妹), or parents whose children are married to each other (사둔).

Mr. Kim Kyŏngjae (金璟載) is Mr. Sŏ Chŏnghŭi's (徐廷禧) son-in-law.

Kim Yŏnŭi (金演義) is Kang Kidŏk's (康基德) son-in-law (婿郞).

Mr. Pak Sŏgyun (朴錫胤) is Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn (崔南善) and Mr. Ch'oe Tusŏn's (崔斗善) younger sister's husband (妹夫).

Mr. Song Chinu (宋鎭禹) is Mr. Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) and Mr. Kim Yŏnsu's (金年洙) fourth younger sister's husband (녯재 妹夫).

Mr. Song Pongu (宋琫禹) is Mr. Hŏ Hŏn's (許憲) son-in-law (婿郞).

Mr. Ch'oe Kyudong (崔奎東) and Mr. Paek Namgyu (白南奎) are parents whose children are married to each other (사둔간).<39>

**Summary**

This short news piece lists the marital connections between several prominent Korean figures in 1934. It details relationships such as son-in-law, brother-in-law (specifically, sister's husband), and the relationship between the parents of a married couple (sadon). The article highlights the interconnectedness of elite families through marriage. For historians, this piece offers a glimpse into the social networks and marriage alliances among the Korean elite during the colonial period, potentially revealing patterns of influence and power consolidation within certain families and circles. The specific individuals mentioned (like Ch'oe Namsŏn, Kim Sŏngsu, Song Chinu) were significant figures in politics, business, and culture.
