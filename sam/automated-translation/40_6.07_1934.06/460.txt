La Paloma
						Translated by Yun K'ŭg-yŏng

When leaving the port of Habana (하바나)
Tears flow down on their own
You board this ship looking for me
The ship departs seeking a flower-blooming land

If the dove comes seeking you the day after tomorrow
If it comes, call it and softly tell it this
If the dove cries clinging to the window
Call it gently and softly listen to its words

Ah! Ch'inita (치니다)ya
If you have not changed
Come, follow me to that flower-blooming land over there

Ah! Ch'inita (치니다)ya
Will your love for me change?
Come, follow me to that flower-blooming land over there<95>

**Summary**

This text is a Korean translation of the lyrics for the famous Spanish song "La Paloma," translated by Yun K'ŭg-yŏng. The poem speaks of departure from Havana, the sorrow of separation, and the hope of reunion with a beloved ("Ch'inita"). A dove acts as a messenger between the separated lovers, carrying hopes and confirming unchanging love, with the ultimate desire to reunite in a "flower-blooming land." For historians, this piece highlights the inflow of Western popular culture into colonial Korea via translation and adaptation. The themes of departure, longing, and the dream of reaching a better place ("flower-blooming land") might have resonated deeply with Koreans experiencing displacement, separation, or yearning for liberation during the colonial period.
