**Body Text Translation:**
From Paris to New York, (World Tour (Continued))
						Na Hye-sŏk (羅惠錫)

Visiting the House Where El Greco Lived

Toledo is one hour from Madrid by train. Toledo is not only an old city in Spain, but also the place where El Greco, a Greek who made his home here and dedicated his life to the world of painting (畵界), lived. When people from all over the world come seeking Spanish art, they invariably stop here to see Greco's unique brushwork, and the number of those who learn from it increases year after year. Here, there are many (不少) ancient buildings and temples, houses where Arabs lived for 600 years, a painting academy, and the house where El Greco lived in Toledo, which has become like an exhibition hall. Countless works by Greco are displayed. There, I viewed one masterpiece by El Greco in a certain temple. There is a house collapsing by the roadside; they say this is the house where Don Quixote was written. Ah, somehow, Spain exudes a mystical scent. Under the blue sky, unseen in other European countries, and the hot sun, as I walked back on the soil, the distant ancient castle looked like a Greek building. On both sides of the green flowing water, there were strange earthen walls—what kind could they be?—creating a superb view (絶景) nearby. At 7 p.m., I returned to Madrid by train. What a delightful day it was. The next morning at 9 a.m., I left Madrid and headed for France.

Arrival in Paris Again

There were many Spanish people on the train. I didn't realize how talkative they were. Spaniards, in general, are said to be talkative.
From here, we began preparations to return to our home country. We bought ship tickets and checked the dates. Time flies so fast. Suddenly, a year and a half had passed. We saw many sights and spent a lot of money. What, ultimately, have we gained? It's still like mixed rice (비빔밥), impossible to sort out yet. I have no shame about having utilized the time as much as possible while I was there.
Anyone who stays in Paris comes to love it and dislikes leaving the day they realize what a good place it is. Thus, when they have no money to eat but are unwilling to leave, all sorts of tragedies and dramas occur. Such people<134> live irresponsibly and emotionally, routinely deceiving and robbing others. Paris itself is a beautiful place, but foreigners spoil it. Indeed, the Parisian spirit is full of liberty, equality, and fraternity, allowing anyone to live cheerfully, and leaving this place feels like parting from a lover.
I do not know all of Paris. However, I was reluctant to leave. I considered staying longer to study painting, but due to various circumstances, I decided to return via America (米洲).
On September 17th, at 9:50 a.m., I departed from Saint-Lazare station for America, seen off by several friends (知友). How much news of Paris had filled my ears, and how much I had longed for Paris—that too has become the past.

Crossing the Atlantic to New York, USA (7 days)

Arriving in Cherbourg at 3:30 p.m., I boarded a small boat at 5:40 p.m. Around 7:30 p.m., I boarded the *Majestic*, the second largest ship in the world (the first being the *Île de France*), a British-American (英米) ship, and headed for New York, USA.

Life Aboard the *Majestic*

The *Majestic* has a gross tonnage of 56,621 tons. Its total capacity includes first class with grades *, B, C, D, E, F, accommodating 870 first-class passengers, 730 second-class passengers, and 1,336 third-class passengers, totaling 2,636 people. The facilities include beds, wardrobes, tables, long chairs, small chairs, washbasins, and call buttons for male and female attendants. There are salons (응접실) everywhere, a smoking room (스목킹우룸), passenger cabins, recreation rooms, a restaurant (으레스토랑), game rooms, a swimming pool, a children's playroom, and a library. There is also a chapel, giving the feeling of a large hotel.
On board, there are recreational facilities for passengers to pass the time. There is horse racing (경마) every two days (women in colorful hats bring out fabricated horses, and one woman moves them according to called numbers, the one that falls off first<135> wins), dancing (딴스), motion pictures, plays, tennis, ping-pong (뺨뽕), ring toss (輪投), deck golf (덱기꼴뿌), deck billiards (덱기퍼리야드), Go (碁), Shogi (將棋), bridge (뿌리지), mahjong (마장), and quoits (힛지아지). One can play day and night. Indeed, they play cheerfully with vibrant bodies. There is nothing not to be envious of.
The weather was clear, the sky above and below was blue, and black waves sparkled with white spots here and there as the majestic *Majestic* sped powerfully along for 7 days. On the 23rd, at 2 p.m., we arrived at the port of New York City, United States of America. Mr. Chang Tŏk-su (張德秀) and Mr. Yun Hong-sŏp (尹弘爕) came to meet us, and we greeted them happily. At night, I went to the International House and was glad to meet Teacher Kim Mari-a (金瑪理亞).

New York City

New York is a small island at the mouth of the Hudson River, an excellent major port excelling in both geography and scenery. Initially a Dutch colony called New Amsterdam, it was later ceded to England and renamed New York. 100 years ago, the population was merely 100,000, and the city was confined to the southern tip of Manhattan Island. Now, encompassing the surrounding boroughs like Brooklyn and Long Island across from the entire island, Greater New York has been formed.
New York, with a population of 9 million, is the world's largest metropolis. It also ranks first in the world for the number of cars, with one car for every three people; first for the height of its buildings; and first for the amount of money, boasting countless world firsts and taking pride in them.
The roads are divided north-south and east-west, making walking very easy. On either side are buildings of several dozen stories, allowing one to see the sky only directly overhead.
New York City (紐肯市) is the center of North American commerce and finance, as well as the center of world commerce and finance. Its bustling commerce and industry, the frequent arrival and departure (頻頻) of ships, the massive volume of trade—everything here is number one in the world. Among the large buildings in the city center, there are over 20 buildings with 30 stories, housing more than 20,000 office workers daily, essentially forming a small town. The four major bridges connecting the city center, Manhattan, and the opposite shore, Long Island (?), are about 2 miles (20 町) long, allowing warships to pass freely underneath. Airplanes deliver mail. Americans, whose motto in all endeavors is to be number one in the world, have truly built here a great metropolis, the embodiment of civilized essence.
There are many places to see, among them Central Park, Riverside Park (河畔公園), Grant's Tomb (Gnant tomb), the old haunts (舊地) of the writer Washington Irving (Washgton liring), the zoo and botanical garden, and theaters.
Many ethnic groups who immigrated from various European countries live here, making it quite complex. The vast land, abundant resources, sparse population, and free atmosphere attracted Europeans.
The American character is progressive, adventurous, with a strong desire to acquire wealth and a great sense of independence and freedom. They advocate equality, value labor, are cheerful and optimistic, make good use of machinery, and emphasize cooperative work. They enjoy humor (諧謔).

International House

This building was near our lodging. It is a dormitory built solely by the famous Mr. Rockefeller (록구후에드氏) for international students from 67 countries.<136> The facilities are complete, and the atmosphere and discipline (風紀) are cosmopolitan.

Columbia University

Many Korean students attend this university. The library, dormitories, and facilities of each department are large-scale, accommodating thousands of students.

Woolworth Building (Woolwod belding)

This building is 58 stories high. Also called a skyscraper (마천루), it is a unique spectacle of New York, soaring 750 feet above the ground. Going up to the top by elevator (에례베다), the buildings below looked like stacked matchboxes (석냥구비).

Metropolitan Museum (Metroporis musim)

This gallery had many sculptures by Michelangelo (미게란체로) and many old paintings. Among modern paintings, there were many (不少) French works, and American works seemed better in tone (調子) than British ones.
In the evening, I went to the Korean Church (조선 예배당) and ate kimchi and siraegi soup (씨라귀국).

Roosevelt's Birthplace (Rusbelt 出生家)

It was Roosevelt's memorial day, so I went sightseeing. Starting with a lecture by Roosevelt's sister, congratulatory messages sent from various places were read, and guests gave speeches.

Presidential Election Voting (大統領選擧投票)

I happened to watch the voting at a polling station near our house.

New York Times (New yorkimes)

The New York Times is not only the largest newspaper in New York but the largest in all of America. On this day, the vote counts arriving from various places were reported on the outer wall of this newspaper building. A crowd of tens of thousands stood packed without room to move (입추의 여지 업시). Each time a candidate's name and vote count were announced, they would either applaud or cheer loudly, and one woman even sobbed aloud. Who would achieve the final victory?

Bronx Park (Blask park)

This is a park outside the city, containing a zoo and botanical garden. I spent a pleasant day strolling there with a friend (知友).

Paramount Theatre (paramount 活動寫眞舘)<137>

This motion picture theatre is said to be the world's largest in terms of seating capacity and building size. The interior decoration was indescribably fine, and the scale was immense. Films produced by Paramount Company (파라마운트社) are distributed to countries worldwide.

Statue of Liberty (自由神)

This is a bronze statue of a woman erected right at the entrance of New York Harbor, standing tall, reaching for the heavens.

Washington (와신톤 Whastong)

We departed for Washington from Pennsylvania Station. American trains have no classes, and there is one seat per person. The scenery along the line was different from the Midwest; there were forests and houses. Major cities en route included Philadelphia (필나돌피아), Baltimore (마루지모아), and Union Station (유니용). Calm cityscapes were few, and there were few passengers on the train. The American countryside seems desolate compared to Europe. Washington Station was clean. We stayed at the Commodore Hotel (Commoielei) and met Mr. Han So-je (韓小濟) and Mr. Kim To-yŏn (金度演).

Old Korean Legation Building (舊韓國時 韓國公使舘)

The world is small, and people are close. One meets dear friends here and there. Our party—Mr. and Mrs. Han So-je, my husband and I, and Mr. Kim To-yŏn—went for a drive (쭈라이브) in Mr. Han So-je's private car. We stopped at a house pointed out to us—it was the Korean Legation to the U.S. during the old Korean Empire period. On the small Western-style building's main gate, the T'aegŭk national emblem (國表) remained faintly visible. Strangely, I felt both happy and sad.

Art Museum (美術舘 Art musem)

This art museum had many copies (코피) of Michelangelo's sculptures and many paintings. There were more masterpieces (가작) by Corot (고로) than in his home country, France. Because they have much money, there are many foreign items.

Lincoln Memorial (린컨記念牌 Lincon Memou)

This is a building made entirely of marble dedicated to Abraham Lincoln (아부라함 링큰), the father of the revolution for Black Emancipation (黑奴 해방). A bronze statue of Lincoln sits at the front entrance, and in the garden, there is a pond where the reflection (倒影) of the memorial (池塔?) is mirrored, creating an indescribably beautiful scene.

Washington Monument (와상롱記念牌 Washiton Memori)

The Washington Monument (와싱톤 비) is a pointed obelisk (尖碑) standing 555 feet high, facing the Lincoln Memorial.

White House

The presidential residence is entirely white, hence called the White House (백인관). In the building connected to this residence, portraits of successive presidents and their wives, along with artifacts they used, are displayed for public viewing. It is relatively modest.

Central Library (中央圖書舘) [Likely Library of Congress]

Constructed by Congress in 1896 and opened to the public on December 16, 1898. It holds 33,000 volumes of general literature, numerous (不少) foreign language books, totaling 1,417,499 volumes, and 133,597 pictures, making it the world's largest library. It is said to have many branch libraries (分舘) in various regions.

Capitol Building (議會堂 bapitol)

This parliament building is located on Capitol Hill, hence its name, Capitol. It houses the House of Representatives (중의원), the Senate (원로원), and the Supreme Court (대심원). Inside, there are portraits of individuals like Washington, Lincoln, Franklin (뿌랑클닌), Columbus (콜놈보스), etc. Among the murals depicting the Revolutionary War, there are portraits of the representatives from each state who signed the Constitution at the time of the United States' founding. Inside the Congressional Library (仝會議 도서관), there are the Declaration of Independence, the Constitution from the founding<138> of the United States, and photographs of the signatories of the Declaration of Independence.

Congregational Church? (콩고리드쳐치 Church)

Hearing that the President attends worship services here every Sunday, I went to see. When the President entered or exited, the crowd all stood up.

Dr. Sŏ Chae-p'il (徐載弼博士)

Leaving Washington, we stopped midway in Philadelphia (filnaiolpio). We took a car to a hospital located in a quiet area outside the city. As I sat in the reception room, Dr. Sŏ (徐박사), a robust middle-aged man, came out and shook my hand warmly. We briefly discussed Korean issues, then toured the hospital and departed. We arrived back in New York at 1 a.m.

Thanksgiving Day (Uausagwing day)

On this day, November 29th, schools are closed, offices shut down, and people roast and eat turkey (Tuke) together and enjoy themselves.

Christmas Day (耶蘇誕日 Chistmas day)

Last year's Christmas was spent in Germany. This year's Christmas is spent in America. On this day, every household (家家戶戶) decorates pine trees with all sorts of ornaments and celebrates joyfully. I went with Mrs. Park (朴부인) to the largest church to observe. At night, I went to observe the Korean Christian church service.

Departure from New York

On January 12th, at 9:20 p.m., we left New York by train, seen off by several grateful friends. A farewell poem from one friend (知舊) went like this:

Is the eastern sky not yet bright?
The dawn slumber was deep.
Is the wood not yet catching fire?
It was long soaked in great waters.
You who are like the orchid and the iris,
The woodcutter, unknowing, will cut you down.
O, pair of lonely geese heading there,
Go straight on without rest.
Lest the small boat and wind behind you
Soak your clothes, I worry.
Are the nameless mountain sheep too many?
Still, the hunter plays and eats.
Has the water dried up in the pasture?
When will the rain fall?
Is the stream flowing off to one side?
It is a great hindrance to farming.

Ten Days - Waterfall

A pair of lonely souls passed a night in the cramped train carriage. The scenery outside (外景) was a world of white snow. Transferring at Buffalo (빠베로), we arrived at Niagara Falls (나이갈폭포) at 11 a.m. We checked into the Cataract House (Cata raet house).

**Summary**

This article details Na Hye-sŏk's journey from Paris to New York and her experiences in the Eastern United States in late 1933/early 1934. She describes leaving Paris, crossing the Atlantic on the *Majestic*, her impressions of New York City's scale and energy, visits to landmarks like Columbia University, the Woolworth Building, and the Metropolitan Museum. She then travels to Washington D.C., visiting the Capitol, Library of Congress, memorials, and notably, the former Korean Legation building and meeting Dr. Sŏ Chae-p'il in Philadelphia. The piece concludes with her departure from New York towards Niagara Falls. For historians, the article offers a rare first-person perspective from a prominent Korean female intellectual/artist during the colonial period navigating Western societies. Her encounters with notable Korean figures in the US (Chang Tŏk-su, Kim Mari-a, Sŏ Chae-p'il) and her poignant visit to the defunct Korean Legation provide insights into the diaspora's activities and the lingering sentiments surrounding Korea's lost sovereignty.

드레도 살든 집을 차저

드레도는 마드리드에서 기차로 1시간 간다. 드레드는 서반아 중 古市일 뿐 아니라 희랍인으로 여긔 주택을 두고 일생을 畵界에 종사한 에루구레고의 살든 곳이다. 세계 각국인이 서반아의 미술을 차저올 때에는 반드시 이곳에 들너 구레고의 독특한 필법을 보고 혹 배와가는 자 기수 年年 증가한다. 여기난 고대 건물사원이 不少하고 또 아라비아인이 600년 동안 살든 집들이 잇고 그림애가데미가 잇스며 드레도의 살든 집이 잇서 진열관처럼 되여 잇다. 구로도의 작품이 무수히 진열해 잇다. 거긔서 어느 사원에 잇난 드레드의 걸작 하나를 구경하엿다. 길가에 씨러저 가는 집이 잇스니 여긔서 동기호텐 쓰든 집이라 한다. 아, 어듼지 모르게 서반아는 신비적 내암새가 흐른다. 구라파 다른 나라에서 보지 못하든 남청색하눌 뜨거운 볏아래 흙을 밟으며 도라올새 멀니 보이난 古城은 희랍의 건물갓고 푸루게 흐르난 물 좌우편에는 무슨 식인지 이상스러운 토벽문이 잇서 그 近處는 絶景을 일우어 잇다. 오후 7시 차로 도로 바드리드로 도라갓다. 얼마나 유쾌한 1일이엇든고 翌朝 오전 9시에 마드리드를 떠나 불란서로 향하엿다.

다시 파리 도착

車中에는 서반아인이 만핫다. 엇지 말이 만흔지 몰낫다. 서반아인은 일반이 말이 만탄다.
우리는 일노붙터 본국으로 도라갈 준비를 하게 되엿다. 배표를 사고 날자를 조사해 노앗다. 세월도 速하다. 어느듯 1개년 반이 지나갓다. 구경도 만히 하고 돈도 만히 썻다. 대체 얻은 것이 무엇인가. 아직 비빔밥 갓하야 뒤서를 차릴 수 업다. 잇는 동안 이용할 수 잇는대로 이용한 것은 자신에 붓그럼이 업다.
누구든지 파리에 와 잇다가 파리가 조흔 곳인 줄 아난 날은 떠나기 실혀한다. 그리하야 먹을 돈은 업고 가기난 실코 하면 가진 참극 비극이 다 생긴다. 그런 사람들은<134> 무책임하고 기분적으로 살며 남을 속이고 빼앗기를 예사로이 한다. 파리 자체난 아람다온 곳이나 외국인들이 버려놋난 것이다. 과연 파리 인심은 자유 평등 박애가 충분하야 누구든지 유쾌히 살수 잇스며 이곳을 떠날 때는 마치 애인 압흘 떠나는 것 갓다.
나는 파리를 다 알지 못한다. 그러나 떠나기가 실혓다. 좀더 잇서서 그림 연구를 하랴다가 여러 사정으로 인하야 米洲를 들녀 도라가기로 작정하엿다.
9월 17일 오전 9시 50분에 쌍라잘 정차장에서 수인의 知友 전송으로 미국을 향하야 떠낫다. 얼마나 만히 파리 소식이 귀에 젓고 얼마나 만이 파리를 를 동경하든 그것도 과거가 되고 마럿다.

마즈스틱 중에서 지낸 생활

마마마마즈스틱은 총 屯數 56,621屯되는 총정원이 일등만 *, B, C, D, E, F의 등급이 잇스며 1등 인원 870명, 2등 인원 730인, 3등 인원 1,336인 합계인 2,636인이다. 설비는 침상, 의장, 테불,  긴 의자, 적은 의자, 세면기, 남녀 하인 招人단초가 잇다. 處處에 살롱 즉 응접실이 잇고 스목킹우룸, 객실, 오락실, 으레스토랑, 유희실, 수영실, 아동 유희실, 도서실이 잇고 예배당이 잇서 큰 호텔 갓흔 감이 생긴다.
船中에는 승객들이 소일하는 유희 기구가 잇다. 2일에 한 번식 경마가 잇다. (맨든 말을 입분 여자들이 각색 모자를 쓰질 나와서 한 여자가 호수부르는 대로 옴겨노아 먼저 떠러지난 자가 승리가<135> 던다) 딴스(舞蹈)가 잇고 활동 사진이 잇스며 연극이 잇고 테니스, 뺨뽕, 輪投, 덱기꼴뿌, 덱기퍼리야드, 碁, 將棋, 뿌리지, 마장, 힛지아지 등이 잇다. 낫에난 낫대로 놀고 밤에는 밤대로 놀 수 잇다. 과연 그들은 싱싱한 신체로 유쾌히 논다. 어느 것 하나 부럽지 아닌 것이 업다.
일기가 명랑하야 상하 天光이 푸르럿난대 검은 파도가 군대군대 백색 점으로 번적이며 雄壯한 마즈스틱은 기운차게 7일간 다라난다. 23일 오후 2시에 미국합중국 뉴육 항구에 도착하엿다. 張德秀氏와 尹弘爕氏가 출영하야 반가이 맛낫다. 밤에난 인터나소날 하우스에 가서 金瑪理亞선생을 맛나 반가웟다.

뉴육시

유육은 hudsoan河口 중앙에 잇는 小島로 지리 풍치 共히 우수한 일대 양항이다. 최초에는 화란 식민지이여서 New amsterdA이라고 일컷든 것이 후에 영국에 양보하게 된 이래 뉴육이라고 개칭하엿다. 100년전에는 인구가 겨오 10만에 불과하고 시가는 Manhatan島 남단 일부에 불과하든 것이 지금은 이 全島對岸에 Brooklyn Luny Islana島 등 주위부 市를 倂하야 대뉴육을 成하엿다.
뉴육은 인구가 900만명되는 세계 제일 大道인 동시에 3인 압헤 자동차 1대식 잇다는 자동차 만키로도 세계에 제일이오 집 놉기로도 세계에 제일이며 돈 만키로도 세계에 제일이여서 세계의 제일되는 것이 무수하며 세계에 제일되는 것을 자랑하는 곳이다.

紐肯市는 북미의 상업 及 재계에 중심지인 동시에 세계상업 及 금융의 중심지이다. 상공업에 활발한 것 선박 출입의 頻頻한 것 무역의 거액인 것 모든 것이 세계에 제일되는 곳이다. 시중 대건물 중에는 30층집이 20여 개가 잇서 매일 집무자가 2만여 인이다. 就然 一小街를 成하고 잇다. 시의 중추 망하당과 對岸렁크島間에 가설한 4대 철교는 長이 20町 내외잇서 군함이 橋下로 자유로 출입하게 된다. 비행기가 우편물을 배달한다. 모든 사업에 세계 제일을 표어로 하는 米人은 참으로 문명정수의 權化한 대도시를 여기 建設 하엿다.
