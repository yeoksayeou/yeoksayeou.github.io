血盟團에의 述懷

東京地方裁判所에서 審理밧고 잇는 有名한 血盟團의 井上日召는 4月 17日에 裁判廷에서 一首詩를 을펏다.

『一團鐵魂屠元兇 滿身鮮血洗皇國』<106>

---
*(English Translation)*---

Recollections Regarding the Blood Pledge Corps (血盟團)

The famous Inoue Nisshō (井上日召) of the Blood Pledge Corps (血盟團), currently undergoing trial at the Tōkyō District Court (東京地方裁判所), recited a poem in the courtroom on April 17th.

"A single group of iron souls slaughters the arch-villains;
A body covered in fresh blood washes the Imperial Nation." <106>

**Summary**

This short news item reports that Inoue Nisshō, leader of the Japanese ultranationalist group Ketsumeidan (Blood Pledge Corps), recited a defiant poem during his trial at the Tokyo District Court on April 17, 1934. The poem expresses the group's violent ideology, justifying assassination ("slaughters the arch-villains") as a means to purify the Japanese state ("washes the Imperial Nation"). For historians studying colonial Korea, this article is interesting as it shows a Korean magazine reporting on radical political events within Japan proper. It highlights the flow of information concerning Japanese ultranationalism into the colony and the continued heavy use of Hanja in Korean publications during this period.
