Magazine Title: 三千里 제6권 제7호
Publication Date: 1934년 06월 01일
Article Title: 社告數則 (Several Company Notices)
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Company Notice/Editorial Postscript)

We are grateful. For the previous May issue, we have received thunderous praise and affection from both within the country and overseas, and we can only be moved with deep emotion. On this occasion, we pledge once again to dedicate our entire heart and strength to protecting this magazine until these bones give out and this blood runs dry.

One regrettable matter is that the two lecture meetings hosted by our company, which would have been historically proud moments, have been postponed because gatherings were not possible as intended due to the period around "May Day" (메이데이).

Respected readers! We wish that each one of you would please add just one more reader to Samch'ŏlli.<300>

**Summary**

This company notice from Samch'ŏlli magazine expresses deep gratitude to readers for the positive reception of the May 1934 issue and reaffirms the publisher's commitment. It also explains the postponement of two company-sponsored lectures due to difficulties holding gatherings around May Day. Finally, it makes a direct appeal to readers to help increase circulation by recruiting one new reader each. For historians, the notice is interesting as it reflects the publisher's direct engagement with its readership, the challenges faced (implied restrictions around May Day potentially related to labor movements or colonial government control), and the constant need for magazines to expand their subscriber base during this period.
