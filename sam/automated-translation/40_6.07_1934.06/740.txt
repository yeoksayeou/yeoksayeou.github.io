**Body Text Translation:**
Great Fantasy (偉大한 空想)

A certain person who deeply loves their compatriots (同胞) one day decided to treat all twenty million brethren (이천만 형제) to a bowl of soup each. Then, calculating the cost at ten jeon per bowl—don't be surprised—it came to two million won.

And also, deciding to present each with a pack of 5-ri cigarettes (오리 짜리 석양갑), even that would cost a whopping sum (끔직한 금액) of one hundred thousand won.

King of a Thousand Children (子女千名의 王)
From Sternberg's (스탄박-크) film, featuring the somewhat nostalgic atmosphere of that southern country, Morocco (모록코), comes a story from about 200 years ago. When King Moulay (무라이王) passed away and the funeral was held—don't be surprised—548 princes and 340 princesses stood behind the funeral bier (喪擧). Truly a family with unparalleled numerous offspring (絶世의 多子孫家).<178>
<178-178>

**Summary**

The article presents two "great fantasies": the immense cost of providing soup (2 million won) or cigarettes (100,000 won) to the entire Korean population, estimated at 20 million. It then contrasts this hypothetical scale with the real-life example of King Moulay Ismail of Morocco, famed for his vast number of children (548 sons, 340 daughters), referenced via a Sternberg film (*Morocco*). For historians, the article reveals the contemporary perception of the Korean population size ("twenty million brethren"), provides a snapshot of consumer prices (soup at 10 jeon, cigarettes), and reflects on the scale of national welfare or collective needs, framed against an exotic historical anecdote drawn from popular culture (film).
