Song of Youth
						Kim Tong-hwan

The mountains and rivers are old, but we are Youth
Let's not heed the transient rise and fall of fortunes
Looking toward the vast future, let's advance firmly
The surging blood of a young body comes but once.

Life is a Waterloo battleground
If defeated, even Napoleon is merely chased away
The hero unequalled in the world is solely the victor
How enviable that name is; it is our goal.

As the Han River flows and flows through eternity
So the blood of Koguryŏ flows forever in this body
Oh, young men of Hanyang, do not be discouraged
Looking toward the glory of the coming day, let us strive hard!<25>

**Summary**

This poem, "Song of Youth," is an exhortation by Kim Tong-hwan encouraging young Koreans to be strong, resolute, and forward-looking despite the challenges symbolized by the "transient rise and fall of fortunes." It contrasts the aged landscape with youthful vitality, urging them to strive for victory, likened to becoming a world-renowned hero unlike the defeated Napoleon at Waterloo. The poem invokes historical continuity, referencing the "blood of Koguryŏ" flowing in the youth of Hanyang (Seoul) and the eternal Han River, calling for perseverance towards future glory. Historians might find this poem significant for its expression of youthful nationalism and resilience during the Japanese colonial period. The emphasis on historical lineage (Koguryŏ), the call to action ("strive hard"), and the focus on future "glory" can be interpreted as a subtle form of cultural resistance and an attempt to foster national spirit under oppressive conditions.

靑年의 노래
						金東煥
山川은 늙엇스나 우리는靑年
덧업는 흥망성쇠 괘렴치말고
蒼蒼한 압날바라 굿게나가세
젊은몸 솟는피는 두번업나니

人生은 워-털루 싸홈터이라
敗하면 나파륜도 쪼기여갈뿐
絶世의 英雄이란 오직승리자
부럽도다 그일홈 우리목푤세

한강수 흘너흘너 만고에가듯
이몸에 고구려피 기리홀으니
한양의 장부들아 락심치말고
오는날 영광바라 분발합세다<25>
