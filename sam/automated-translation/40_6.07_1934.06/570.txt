The Fates of the Governors of the Thirteen Provinces
						Mok Ch'un Hagin

What were called Provincial Governors (Kwanch'alsa) in the former Korean era followed the old system of the eight provincial governors (Kamsa). The difference compared to the old system (before the Gabo Reform) was that the concurrent posts of Provincial Inspector (Tosunch'alsa), Army Commander (Pyŏngmajŏltosa), and Naval Commander (Sugunjŏltosa) were abolished. Consequently, unlike before, the power to dismiss and punish civil and military officials under their jurisdiction for wrongdoings first and report later (先行後啓) was gone. Also, as administrative power and military power became separate and independent, the governors' authority no longer included delegated command over the army and navy. However, other duties, such as the twice-yearly performance evaluation (殿最) of local magistrates (Suryŏng) (spring and autumn evaluations), and involvement in all administrative and judicial matters including police, judiciary, and tax collection (徵稅), meant they customarily concurrently held positions like Head of the Court (裁判所長) and Tax Supervisor (稅務監). Regarding their appointment and dismissal, they could report directly to His Majesty (陛下) and did not necessarily have to go through the State Council (議政府) (cabinet), demonstrating it was indeed an important post with much authority and power (有威多權). After the governmental system reform (官制改革) in the 2nd year of Yunghŭi (1908), judicial power (法權) and taxation power (稅權) were also separated. The Provincial Governor became merely the head of local administration and police, no different from the current Provincial Governor (Tojisa), with both status (地位) and authority (權限) reduced. However, through a special mandate (特殊委任) by imperial decree (勅令), County Magistrates (Kunsu) within their jurisdiction were appointed exclusively from residents of that province recommended (推薦) by the Provincial Governor, and the central government (中央政府) could not appoint them directly. This system, which continued right up until the annexation (併合), was significantly superior compared to the Provincial Governor (Tojisa) role. Additionally, besides the 13 Provincial Governors, there was a special office in Kyŏngsŏng called Hansŏngbu (漢城府), which followed the old system of the Hansŏngbu Mayor (P'anyun). Compared to the Provincial Governor, its official authority (職權) was lesser as it lacked police power (Kyŏngsŏng's police were directly managed by the Metropolitan Police Agency), but its status and rank (品質) were above the Provincial Governors. Therefore, usually, when referring to local governors (地方長官), it meant the Hansŏngbu Mayor and the Provincial Governors of each province. In this respect, although the name is the same as the current Kyŏngsŏng Mayor (京城府尹), its nature was entirely different. Now, listing the last Provincial Governors of Chosŏn, that is, the local governors immediately before the annexation, they were as follows, as I recall:

Mayor of Hansŏngbu            Chang Hŏnsik (張憲植)
Kyŏnggi                Kim Samuk (金思黙)
Ch'ungbuk                Kwŏn Pongsu (權鳳洙)
Ch'ungnam                Ch'oe Chŏngdŏk (崔廷德)
Chŏnbuk                Yi Tuhwang (李斗璜)
Chŏnnam                Sin Ŭnghŭi (申應熙)
Kyŏngbuk                Pak Chungyang (朴重陽)
Kyŏngnam                Hwang Ch'ŏl (黃鐵)
P'yŏngnam                Yi Chin'ho (李軫鎬)
P'yŏngbuk                Yu Hyŏngno (柳赫魯)
Hamnam                Yi Pŏmnae (李範來)

Hwanghae                Cho Hŭimun (趙羲聞)
Kangwŏn                Yi Kyuwan (李圭完)

These were the 14 individuals, I believe. Speaking of their origins, Chang Hŏnsik was an exchange student in Tokyo, finished an elective course (選科) at Tokyo Imperial University's Faculty of Law, and had influential figures like Nagashima Ryūji (長嶋隆二) (now a Diet member, son-in-law of Duke Katsura Tarō) among his classmates. Having a close associate (親父 - *likely meaning 親友 ch'in'u, close friend*), Chang and Nagashima served as a communication channel (傳語機關) between Prime Minister Yi Wanyong (李完用) of Old Korea and Prime Minister Katsura Tarō (桂太郞) of Japan. From his student days, he put much effort into the political comeback (政界復活) of Yi Wanyong, who was then a politician (政客) out of power. Thus, after returning to Korea, he served under Education Minister (學相) Yi Wanyong as secretary (秘書官), head of the accounting section (會計課長), etc., and then, upon the formation of the Yi cabinet, he was honorably promoted (榮擢) to Mayor of Hansŏngbu in one leap. He later served as Councilor (參與官) in P'yŏngnam and Provincial Governor (道長官) of Ch'ungbuk, then became an administrative official (事務官) in the main office, and again Governor of Chŏnnam. Now, as a Councilor (參議) in the Central Council (中樞院), he receives an annual allowance (年手當) of 2,000 won and a not insignificant pension (恩給), seemingly spending his old age comfortably at Ch'wiunjŏng Kodae (翠雲亭高臺). Kim Samuk, a police veteran with two stints as police chief, became Provincial Governor in Kyŏngnam and Kyŏnggi, and was a man of upright and noble character. His current status, whether alive or dead (存沒), is unknown, but it seems he briefly served as a Councilor (贊議) after the annexation. Kwŏn Pongsu was also an exchange student in Tokyo who gained favor (受知) with Police Advisor Maruyama (丸山) and rose from Security Section Chief (保安課長) in the Home Ministry to Police Bureau Chief (警務局長). While serving as Ch'ungbuk Provincial Governor, his wife died at his post (任地). Misled by the words of a geomancer (地官), he tried to bury her in a propitious site (明堂吉地), but unfortunately, it was a private mountain with an existing owner and grave (有主有墳之民山). Thus, after the annexation, a lawsuit arose, and eventually, in Ch'ŏngju, where he had served as governor (方伯) until the previous year, he became a prisoner in his capacity as an Imperially Appointed Councilor (勅任贊議), serving time in jail (入獄服罪). Since then, he has spent decades in gloomy (悶悶) circumstances near Sajiktong (社稷洞). Yun Kapyŏng is currently an Imperially Appointed Councilor (勅參) in the Central Council. He is from the prestigious Yun clan of Nosŏng (魯城尹氏), holds a red certificate (紅唐紙) for passing the military examination (武科及第), and during the Ilchinhoe (一進會) days, he was a politician who gave wicked (奸惡) speeches (演說) from the platform (壇上). He served as Councilor in P'yŏngbuk and Kyŏngbuk, became Governor of Kangwŏn Province, and then retired from office (退官) during Mr. Shimo'oka's (下岡氏) time. As for Pak Chungyang (朴重陽), as the world knows, during his time as governor and governor-general in Ch'ungnam, Hwanghae, and Ch'ungbuk, problems involving women (女色問題) arose everywhere, leading to his downfall (失脚). He is now passing the time (消日) playing Go (圍碁) in quiet retirement at Ch'imsan Han'gŏ (砧山閑居) outside Taegu.<119> However, when discussing a person, there is no need to dwell on their private conduct (私行). Moreover, traditionally (由來), heroes (豪傑) in the Eastern style (東洋流) considered drinking and womanizing (飮酒好色) the true nature of a hero (英雄本色). Therefore, it is unreasonable to demand that he alone model himself after a moralist teacher (道學先生) and become a character from an ethics textbook (修身敎科書). Setting aside this issue, Pak Chungyang as an individual (個人) has admirable stories (美談) of extreme filial piety (孝心) and deep friendship (友誼), and is an upright (剛直), cheerful (快活), fine man (好男兒). Furthermore, as a local governor, his talent (才幹), capability (力量), skill (手腕), and insight (食見) were all outstanding (出色), earning the fear and submission (畏伏) of his subordinates and producing excellent (優異) administrative achievements (治績). His current situation, lying low (雌伏) as an Imperially Appointed Councilor (勅參), might bring him the regret (憾) of an old thoroughbred in the stable (老騏在櫪), but since his age has already reached sixty (還曆), a comeback (再起) seems hopeless. He can only console himself (自慰) by looking back at his past success and fortune (榮達과 幸運), having risen as a young man entirely bare-handed (赤手空拳), without prestigious family background (門地), other learning (學術), or official career (官歷), becoming County Magistrate of Taegu in the 32nd year, then leaping to Provincial Governor of P'yŏngnam, and moving to Kyŏngbuk the following year (翌年), astonishing (瞠若) the world. Then there is Hwang Ch'ŏl (黃鐵), a maternal cousin (姨從) of Prince Kang (李堈殿下). He spent many years in Japan following the prince and, with the backing (推挽) of Resident-General Itō (伊藤), became Vice-Minister (協辦) of Agriculture, Commerce, and Industry (農商工部) and Governor of Kangwŏn. After retiring from Kyŏngnam, he returned to Japan and never set foot (再踏) on his old homeland (舊土) again, spending his days (送日) with poetry, wine, calligraphy, and painting (詩酒書畵). He was nicknamed "Magyŏng" (魔硬 - perhaps meaning stubborn/tough like goblin leather?), a man of talent (才氣) and exceptional skill (怪腕), and consequently full of ambition (野心滿滿). During Itō's era, he was quite prominent, but to Terauchi (寺內氏), who was meticulous about minor matters (小廉曲謹), fastidious and narrow-minded (潔癖狹量), and the very incarnation (權化) of strict resoluteness (嚴毅), he was an eyesore in many ways and ended up being dismissed (馘首) simultaneously with the annexation. Criticisms aside, he was, in any case, a sort of unconventional (不羈) and jolly fellow (快漢) with a bohemian taste (浪味). Ch'oe Chŏngdŏk (崔廷德) also traveled (遊歷) in Tokyo and, due to his closeness (親近) to Pak Yŏnghyo (朴泳孝) and Song Pyŏngjun (宋秉畯), left a record (記錄) as a 9th-rank Provincial Governor. Now, having left his post as Kyŏngnam Imperially Appointed Councilor (慶南勅參) 10 years ago, he suffers from extreme poverty (貧窮徹骨), wandering poetry gatherings and bars (詩席酒場) near Taegu, living day to day. Among the others, Yu Hyŏngno (柳赫魯), Sin Ŭnghŭi (申應熙), Yi Tuhwang (李斗璜), Yi Chin'ho (李軫鎬), Yi Pŏmnae (李範來), Cho Hŭimun (趙義聞), and Yi Kyuwan (李圭完), these seven were involved as political offenders (國事犯) from the Kapsin Coup (甲申) era up to the assassination of Empress Myŏngsŏng (明成后事件), and had special relationships (特殊關係) with Kim Okkyun (金玉均) and Pak Yŏnghyo (朴泳孝). Their origins, careers, and personalities differ slightly, but they were figures who shared a life in exile (亡命生活) from the same faction and later served as local governors after receiving amnesty (恩赦) and returning to Korea – so-called comrades in different bodies sharing the same goal (異身同志). Most of them continued to serve as Provincial Governors and Councilors immediately after the annexation. Yi Tuhwang has since died (죽고), Sin Ŭnghŭi is also gone (업서 젓스며), and the remaining ones hold posts (所任) in the Central Council or the Oriental Development Company (東拓). Among them, Yi Chin'ho is said to be the poorest, while Yi Kyuwan and Yu Hyŏngno reportedly would have no financial worries (生活의 憂) even otherwise. Finally, one last word: with these 14 as the last, the name of Provincial Governor vanished. Now, after 25 years (春風秋雨 25個 星霜) have passed, many have entered the register of the dead (鬼籍), and the survivors (現存者) <120> are mostly old men (老翁) in their 70s or 80s; the youngest (最年少者) are over sixty (六旬) with few remaining days (餘日업는 사람들). Looking at it this way, the chance to see the image (面影) of these antique (骨董品) relics (遺物) of Korea's final high officials (良二千石) will only last for these few recent (요지음) years. Thinking of them evokes considerable deep emotion (感慨).<121>

**Summary**

This 1934 article recounts the status and authority of the Provincial Governors (Kwanch'alsa) in the final years of the Korean Empire, just before Japanese annexation in 1910. It contrasts their powers with earlier and later positions, lists the last 14 governors (including the Mayor of Hansŏngbu), and provides brief biographical sketches of several, detailing their backgrounds (often involving connections to Japan or reformist movements like the Kapsin Coup), their careers both before and after annexation under Japanese rule (many continuing in colonial administration or the Central Council), and their situations in 1934 – ranging from comfortable retirement to poverty or obscurity. The author concludes with a reflection on the passage of time and the fading presence of these figures who represent the end of an era. For historians, the article offers valuable contemporary insight into the transition period, highlighting the varied fates of the Korean elite who served at the highest provincial levels. It reveals details about their connections, political affiliations (including pro-Japanese groups like Ilchinhoe), personal lives, and how they navigated the shift to colonial rule, providing a human dimension to the historical narrative of collaboration and adaptation.

前 한국시대의 관찰사라 하든 것은 舊時의 8도 감사제도를 그대로 襲用한 것이오, 舊制(甲午前)에 비하야 차이가 잇든 것은 도순찰사와 병마, 수군절도사 등의 겸직이 업서젓슴으로 전일과 가듸 관하의 문무관리를 죄과가 잇다하야 그 파면과 치죄를 선행 後啓하든 권능이 업서진 것과 또 행정권과 병권이 분리독립된 결과로 관찰사의 권한에서 육해군의 위임지휘가 업서진 것이다. 그러나 기타의 직권 예컨대 수령에 대한 年 2차의 殿最 (春, 秋 2기의 성적고과) 라든지 警察, 사법, 징세 등 만반 행정과 재판 사무에까지 관계함으로 재판소장, 세무감 등을 例兼하얏고 그 진퇴에 관하야도 직접으로 폐하께 상주할 수가 잇고 반듯이 의정부(내각)를 경유지 안어도 무방하든 것 만티 실로 有威多權하든 중직이엿섯다. 융희 2년 관제 개혁후에는 법권, 세권이 또한 분리되여 관찰사는 지방행정과 경찰의 수뇌자가 될 뿐이오 지금의 도지사와 다를 것이 업는 1개의 행정 문관으로 지위도 저하되고 권한도 축소되엿스나 다시 칙령으로 특수 위임을 밧어 관내의 군수는 관찰사가 추천하는 그 도내 거주자에 한하야 전형 임용하고 중앙 정부가 직접으로 임용치 못하야섯다. 이것은 병합 직전까지 계속되든 제도로 도지사에 비하야 매우 우월하든 것이다. 그리고 13도 관찰사 외에 경성에는 한성부라는 특수 관아가 잇서 舊時의 한성부 판윤제도를 그대로 襲用한 것인대 관찰사에 비하야 직권은 경찰권이 업든이만티 (경성의 경찰은 경시청에서 직관함) 不及되엿스나 지위와 품질은 관찰사의 수위에 잇섯슴으로 보통으로 지방 장관이라 하면 즉 한성부윤과 밋 각도 관찰사를 말한 것이오 이 점에서 지금 경성부윤과는 일홈은 갓흐나 성질이 전연 다르든 것이다. 이제 조선의 최후 관찰사 즉 병합 직전의 지방 장관을 列錄하면 이러하얏다.

그리하야 귀국후에 李完用 學相의 밋헤서 비서관, 회계과장 등에 역임하고 잇다가 李내각 성립후에 일약 한윤으로 榮擢된 사람이다. 그는 그 후에 평남 참여관, 충북 도장관 등을 다니다가 본부 사무관과 다시 전남지사도 되엿고 지금은 중추원 참의로 2천원의 年 수당과 不少한 恩給을 밧어가며 翠雲亭高臺에서 노후를 질겁게 보내는 모양이다. 金思黙 경무사의 二度 경력을 가진 경찰의 선배로 경남과 경기의 2도에 관찰사가 되엿는데 강직 고결하든 인격자이엿다. 지금은 그 存沒도 알 수 업스나 병합후에는 일시 찬의 노릇을 한 것 갓고 權鳳洙도 동경 유학생 출신으로 丸山 경무고문에게 受知하야 내부 보안과장으로 경무국장까지 되엿섯다. 충북 관찰사로 잇슬 때  임지에서 妻喪을 당하야 지관의 말을 잘못 듯고 명당 吉地에 안장하랴든 것이 불행히 有主有墳의 民山이엿슴으로 병합후에 소송문제가 생기고 필경 그 전년까지 方伯 노릇을 하든 청주에서 勅任贊議의 신분으로 죄수의 몸이 되여 入獄服罪하고 이래 수십년 悶悶한 세월을 사직동 近地에서 보냇다. 尹甲炳은 現 중추원 勅參으로 잇는데 魯城 尹氏의 名閥이오 무과급제한 紅唐紙도 가젓고 일진회 당년에는 단상에서 간악한 연설도 하든 정객이엿다. 평북, 경북의 참여관으로 강원도지사가 되엿다가 下岡氏 시대에 퇴관하얏고 朴重陽이란 사람은 세상에서 알다십히 충남, 황해, 충북의 장관과 지사시대에 도처마다 여색의 問題가 생겨 실각하고 대구교외 砧山閑居에서 圍碁로 소일하고<119> 잇다. 그러나 사람을 의론함에 그 사행을 들줄 필요가 업슬 뿐 아니라 유래 동양류의 호걸들은 음주 호색을 영웅의 본색으로 알든 것인즉 獨히 그에게 道學 선생을 배호와 수신 교과서의 인물이 되라고 하기는 무리한 것이다. 그리고 이 문제를 떠난 朴重陽은 개인으로 극히 효심이 잇고 우의가 깁흔 미담도 잇스며 강직 쾌활한 호남아이다. 더욱이 지방 장관으로 재간, 역량, 수완, 식견이 共히 걸출하야 부하가 畏伏하고 치적이 優異하든 出色의 인물이다. 今에 勅參으로 雌伏하고 잇는 것이 彼로서 老騏在櫪의 憾이 잇겟지만은 연령이 임의 還曆이 되엿스니 재기는 절망으로 볼 것이오 오즉 과거에 잇서 門地 미천하고 다른 학술과 官歷이 업스면서 전혀 赤手空拳의 청년으로 궐기하야 32년에 6품 대구 군수로 일약 평남 관찰사가 되고 다시 翌年에 경북으로 옴겨 一世를 瞠若케 하든 영달과 행운을 도라보고 자위할 것이다. 그리고 黃鐵이라는 사람은 李강殿下의 姨從으로 물건의 權化갓든 寺內씨에게는 여러 가지로 눈에 버서나 병합동시에 馘首되고 말엇다. 비난잇든 것은 비난으로 하고 하여튼지 일종의 浪味잇는 不羈의 快漢이다. 그리고 崔廷德도 동경에 遊歷하야 朴泳孝, 宋秉畯 등에게 친근하든 관계로 9품 관찰사의 기록을 남긴 사람인데 지금은 慶南勅參을 10년전에 떠러지고 빈궁이 徹骨하야 대구 近地에서 詩席酒場으로 다니며 그날
