Recent Transformations in Korea Over 25 Years - (Intellectual Circles) The Rise and Fall of Hwayohoe, Pukp'unghoe, Sin'ganhoe, etc.
						Kim Kyŏngjae (金璟載)

I. Preface

It feels like only yesterday that when I was 7 or 8 years old attending the village school (書堂), I would sit among the village elders, feeling tense for reasons I didn't understand, yet already 25 years have passed.
Thinking this way, time seems to have flowed by so easily, and now I have grown up like this; considering that 25 years is a quarter of a century, it feels like a very long time indeed. To write about the transformations of organizations within Korea during that period is by no means an easy task.
One reason is that the content is exceedingly complex and multifaceted. Even if one tries to establish a framework centered only on overt organizations (表現團體), there are always other organizations and figures behind the scenes (裏面), interacting with them. Factional power struggles (派的 勢力) gave rise to slander (中傷) and exclusion (排斥), and consequently, estrangement (離間), false accusations (謀陷), and jealousy (嫉妬) directly or indirectly exerted enormous influence on the movement.
Therefore, the transformation of ideological groups (思想團體) over the last 25 years will emerge as the most important scene in our modern history (近世史). [Omitted in original text] In any case, there has never been a time like this when we wandered (彷徨), going back and forth in confusion (左往右往), unable to find our way, and it was during this time that we shifted our footsteps under various meanings and consciousnesses.
Generally, before an organization becomes public (表面化), there is a cause (源因), a driving force (動力), and it manifests as a result. However, here, I am asked not about the cause or driving force, but to speak of the transformations centered on the surface organizations, and furthermore, to fill the extremely limited pages to cover the entirety, such that anyone who reads it can grasp it at once, nod, and have the transformations of the organizational movement over 25 years imprinted in their minds—making it concise and very easy to understand. This is the request from Samch'ŏllisa (三千里社), but for me, the writer, it is like an order to insert a triangular iron bar (三角形 鐵棒) into a round hole (圓形).<61> Thus, it is my premature worry as the writer that it will likely fail to meet the standard.

I. Beginning of the Movement

[A section of 7 pages omitted here - original text] The World War, which had been fought for many years, came to an end with Germany's defeat, and its peace conference (媾和會議) was held in Paris.
France, wary of (㥘내는 - likely 'fearing') Germany's revival (復興), sought to use this opportunity to prevent Germany from ever raising its head again. Thus, it accepted the principle of self-determination (自決主義) proposed by America's Wilson and decided to cut off Germany's limbs (手足). The next problem was to prevent rapprochement (接近) between Germany and Russia, leading to the recognition (承認) of the new formations of Poland, Lithuania, Latvia, etc. This wave flowed and eventually reached -.
Because of that event, afterward, Youth Associations (靑年會) emerged in various places in Korea. Their stated purpose was to strive for the development of intellectual, moral, and physical education (智德體 三育), but their character became clearer in 1920 in Kyŏngsŏng, when O Sang-gŭn (吳祥根), Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), and others became central figures in forming the Federation of Korean Youth Associations (朝鮮靑年會聯合會). At the same time, Pak Chunghwa (朴重華), Pak Ilgyu (朴珥圭), Yun Tŏkpyŏng (尹德炳), Sin Paegu (申伯雨), and others centrally organized the Korean Labor Mutual Aid Society (朝鮮勞働共濟會). These were people who, at that time, held the most progressive (進步的) ideas, studied the problems of laborers (勞働者), and had some understanding (解得) of the relationship between the development of capitalism (資本主義) and colonies (殖民地). However, it would be a correct view to see the Korean Labor Mutual Aid Society as a type of cultural organization (文化團體). Student groups (學生團體) also emerged, and lecture tours (巡講) by the Christian Youth Association (基督靑年會 - YMCA) became prevalent (盛行). Then, the waves of various transformations from foreign lands (外地) began to ripple (波及) through.

Progress of Class Differentiation (階級分解)

First Period

Korea's recent ideological movement has been greatly influenced by the ideological trends in Tokyo (東京) and Osaka (大阪). The influence coming from the north is also significant. Accordingly, since the Korean Labor Mutual Aid Society had many individuals among its constituent elements (構成要素) and organizational layers (組織層) who held the most progressive ideas, it was also the most sensitive (敏感) to the influences and repercussions (波動) coming from there. As the factional strife (泒戰) of the overseas movement, such as the Shanghai Faction (上海泒) and the Irkutsk Faction (尼市泒), intensified (激熱), its repercussions finally (드듸여는) spread to the ideological groups within Korea (鮮內). Yun Tŏkpyŏng (尹德炳), Sin Paegu (申伯雨), and others declared (聲明) the dissolution (解體) of the Korean Labor Mutual Aid Society and organized the Korean Labor League (朝鮮勞働聯盟) in 1921. In opposition (對立) to this,<62> the All-Korea Labor-Farmer Congress (全鮮勞農大會) was organized in 1923, with Kim Saguk (金思國) and Kang T'aekchin (姜宅鎭) as its important figures. In the same year, the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance (南鮮勞農同盟) was organized in Chinju (晋州); it was a federation (聯盟體) of various labor and farmer groups scattered throughout Chŏllanam-pukto (全南北), Kyŏngsangnam-pukto (慶南北), and Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-pukto (忠南北). Sŏ Chŏnghŭi (徐廷禧), Chŏng Unhae (鄭雲海), and Yi Yŏngmin (李榮珉) played important roles (役割) in it. In 1922, the General Federation of Korean Laborers and Farmers (朝鮮勞農總同盟) was finally organized, and the aforementioned three groups dissolved. While the Korean Labor League and the All-Korea Labor-Farmer Congress, being located (位置) in Kyŏngsŏng, might have had some political (政治的) significance (意義), in actual practice (實地), they were organizations that failed to exert any real capability (力量). Thus, it was solely (오즉) the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance that, through its actual strength (實力), created the unified body (大同團結體) that was the General Federation of Korean Laborers and Farmers. It encompassed (網羅) a large organization of 118 labor groups and 114 peasant groups, totaling 232 cell organizations (細胞團體) throughout Korea. At its outset (벽頭), it greatly displayed its vigor (氣勢) with a resolution to boycott (非買決議) the Tong-a Ilbo (東亞日報) and through demonstrations (示威運動). Sŏ Chŏnghŭi, Chŏng Unhae, Yun Tŏkpyŏng, Kang T'aekchin, and Ma Myŏng (馬鳴) played influential (有力) roles at the time of the founding (創立) of the General Federation of Korean Laborers and Farmers. Thus, the General Federation of Korean Laborers and Farmers was formed by bringing together the Hwayo Faction (火曜系), Pukp'ung Faction (北風系), and Seoul Faction (서울系), achieving organizational completion. However, its subsequent operation (運用) came to be moved (움지기게) by the hands of the Hwayo and Pukp'ung Factions. So, one must (말하지 안을 수 업다) explain what the Hwayohoe (火曜會), Pukp'unghoe (北風會), and Seoul Youth Association (서울靑年會) were, and when and from what origins (由來) they arose. In 1921, Kim Han (金翰) and Pak Ilbyŏng (朴一秉) became central figures and organized the Proletarian League (無産者同盟), advocating (標榜) a class movement. However, being unable to take action (動態), they organized the New Thought Study Group (新思想硏究會) as an academic research group (學術硏究團體) in 1922, with Sin Paegu, Yun Tŏkpyŏng, Hong Chŭngsik (洪增植), and Ku Yŏnhŭm (具然欽) as cadre-level (幹部級) figures. Later, deeming the "Study Group" too inactive (非活動的) and incompetent (無能), they changed (變改) the organization to Hwayohoe (Tuesday Society) in 1923; Kim Ch'an (金燦), Kim Chaebong (金在鳳), and Yun Tŏkpyŏng were its key players (俳優).
Meanwhile, Kim Chongbŏm (金種範) organized the Kŏnsŏlsa (建設社 - Construction Society) in 1921. The following year (翌年), Kim Yaksu's (金若水) faction (一泒), which had been conducting ideological activities after organizing the Puksŏnghoe (北星會 - North Star Society) in Tokyo, returned (도라와서) and changed the organization of the aforementioned Kŏnsŏlsa to Pukp'unghoe (北風會 - North Wind Society). Kim Yaksu, Chŏng Unhae, Song Pongu (宋琫禹), and Sin Ch'ŏl (辛鐵) were the central figures at that time. The Seoul Youth Association (서울靑年會) was organized in 1920 as a general youth group (一般靑年團體) by Chang Tŏksu, Ch'oe P'aryong (崔八鏞), Kim Saguk, Sin Paegu, and others. However, in the usual (例의) struggle between the Irkutsk Faction (泥市泒) and the Shanghai Faction (上海派), Chang Tŏksu, Ch'oe P'aryong, etc., were naturally eliminated (自然陶汰) for allegedly receiving XX funds (XX費 構領하엿다는 것). Sin Paegu's faction, the so-called Irkutsk Faction, was also pushed out (밀니여 나오고), and simultaneously with it becoming the camp (陣營) of Kim Saguk's faction, it ceased to be merely a regional (地域的) youth group and came to play the role of an ideological group signifying the overall movement (全體運動). Kim Saguk, Chŏng Paek (鄭栢), Yi Chŏngyun (李廷允), and Pak Hyŏngbyŏng (朴衡秉) were its important figures.
Pak Hŏnyŏng (朴憲永), Kim X-ya (金X冶), Cho Pongam (曹奉岩), and Im Wŏn'gŭn (林元根) formed the core (核心) to<63> organize the Sinhŭng Youth League (新興靑年同盟 - New Rising Youth League) in 1923. Although the Sinhŭng Youth League was young (年이 淺하나) in its organization, its existence shone (빗낫다) in the point (點) that it practiced (實踐) a huge role (巨大한 賦役) in the Korean youth movement and ideological movement. At the same time that the Federation of Korean Youth Associations (朝鮮靑年會聯合會) decisively carried out (斷行) an organizational change (變改) to become the General Alliance of Korean Youth (朝鮮靑年總同盟), its conventional (從來) principles and platform (主義 綱領) were [omitted]. Socialist youth took charge of (當하엿으나) the operational pivot (機樞), but by then, factional confrontation (派的 對立) was already (임이) clear (鮮明). Therefore, the Korean Youth General Alliance was always under the leadership (領導) of the Seoul Youth Association faction (서울靑年會系). At this time, the Sinhŭng Youth League served as the vanguard group (前衛團體) for the Hwayohoe faction, and the real power (實權) of the Hwayohoe faction's movement was always controlled (左右) by the hands of the Sinhŭng Youth League. The All-Korea Mass Movement Activists' Congress (全朝鮮民衆運動者大會), proposed (提唱) by Hwayohoe, also saw significant activity (活動이 컷든 것이다) from the Sinhŭng Youth League. The first chairman (委員長) of the Sinhŭng Youth League was Pak Hŏnyŏng, the second was Im Wŏn'gŭn, and the third was Kim Kyŏngjae. During the third term (第3代 時代), due to the first and second Communist Party Incidents (共産黨事件), the aforementioned figures were imprisoned (投獄), and thereafter, the Sinhŭng Youth League naturally could not avoid (免치 못햇다) losing influence (失勢). The formal establishment of international connections (國際關係) for the Communist Party (共黨) and Communist Youth (共靑) began with the 1st and 2nd Party [Congresses/Incidents]. Their organization was born concurrently with the All-Korea Mass Movement Activists' Congress, and in the point that its core force (核心勢力) consisted of the core elements (核心分子) of the Sinhŭng Youth League, the Sinhŭng Youth League played a large role (役割이 컷스며) for its relatively short existence (日淺한 比例). The Hanyang Youth League (漢陽靑年聯盟), which played an oppositional (對立的) role to the Youth General Alliance operated by the Seoul faction, encompassed (包容) 1,020 members in 19 organizations within Kyŏngsŏng city (京城市內) and along the Han River banks (漢江沿岸), but its main force (主要勢力) was the Sinhŭng Youth League.
Although the opposition movement (反對運動) from the Seoul Youth Association against the All-Korea Mass Movement Activists' Congress initiated (發起) by Hwayohoe was also fierce (猛烈), Pukp'unghoe and the General Federation of Korean Laborers and Farmers actively (積極的) supported (支援) it. Although the meeting (會合) could not be held due to a police ban on assembly (警察의 集合禁止), afterward, the ideological movement in Korea finally (비로소) began to show clear colors (色彩가 鮮明하야 왓으니). Until then, most (大槪) local organizations (地方團體) had been satisfied with conducting a kind of cultural movement under vague (漠然) platforms like "development of intellectual, moral, and physical education." However, arguing that those on the same line (갓흔 線) should not distinguish between Hwayohoe and Pukp'unghoe but should seek unification (統一) even one day sooner (하로라도 속히), the four organizations—Pukp'unghoe, Hwayohoe, the Korean Labor Party (朝鮮勞働黨), and the Korean Proletarian League (朝鮮無産者同盟)—held a joint general meeting (合同總會) and attempted (꾀하엿으나) its concrete (具體的)<64> realization (實現), but it ended in failure (未遂). There were 7 Standing Committee members who handled the actual affairs (實際 事務). At this point, the multifaceted (多角的) phenomenon in the Korean ideological movement up to then was settled (淸帳), and the two major oppositions (兩大對立) of Seoul vs. the Four-Group Joint [Committee] (四合) emerged (出現). However, since the organization of the Four-Body Joint Committee was abnormal (畸形的), control (統制) was difficult to achieve smoothly (圓滑), and next, the rupture (破裂) of the underground organization (地X組織 - likely 地下組織) directly affected (影響) the surface organizations (表面團體). Thus, signs (조짐) of a re-split (再分裂) began to appear (뵈이기) in the Four-Body Joint Committee. In the winter of 1925, part of the secret (秘密) was leaked (루설되야서) in Sinŭiju (新義州), finally (드듸여) leading to the arrests (檢擧) of the First Party Incident. Afterward, the operation of the organizations came to be dominated (支配) by relatively newly emerging figures (新進分子) with less experience in the previous factional struggles, and unification once again became the object of general yearning (갈망). Furthermore, the authority (權威) of the underground organization was declining (墜落) day by day (날로), and consequently, its influence (影響) on the surface organizations also became very weak (薄弱). Thus, the atmosphere (氣分) for unification on the surface line (表面線) grew stronger (濃厚) daily (이섯다). In the spring of 1926, a joint discussion meeting (合同懇談會) between the Four-Group Joint [Committee] (四合) and the Chŏnjinhoe (前進會) was even held at Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月舘). And the Four-Group side decided (決議) to participate (參加) in the Central Council of Korean Social Organizations (朝鮮社會團體中央協議會) advocated (唱導) by the Chŏnjinhoe. The calls (소래는) for front unification (X線統一 - likely 戰線統一) grew louder (놉하 가고) day by day (하로하로). Thereupon, the Four-Body Joint Committee formally dissolved (解體), and the Chŏnguhoe (正友會 - Righteous Friends Society) came to be founded (創立케 되엿으니). Its promoters (發起人) included Yi Kyusong and Pae Tŏksu (裴德秀), Paek Kiho (白基浩) from the Pukp'unghoe side; Kim Kyŏngjae, Yi Pongsu (李鳳洙), O Ŭisŏn (吳義善) from the Hwayohoe side; Yi Ch'ungmo and Kim Yŏnŭi (金演義) from the Korean Labor Party; Pak Ilbyŏng from the Korean Proletarian League; and additionally, Kim Seyŏn (金世淵) and Kim Kyŏngt'ae (金京泰) participated as independents (無所屬). Overcoming numerous (幾多) difficulties (難關) under the slogan (口號) of "Front Unification," the unification work (統一工作) progressed step by step (一步一步), leading to the announcement (告하고) of Chŏnguhoe's founding and the completion (畢하니) of the dissolution ceremony for the Four-Group Joint Committee. The Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) and Sidae Ilbo (時代日報) published (載하야) editorials (社說) titled "The Establishment of Chŏnguhoe," praising (비럿다) its significance (有意義함).
Prior to this (先하야), the Seoul Youth Association side had organized the Chŏnjinhoe (前進會 - Advance Society). The reason given was that there were flaws (缺陷) in the structure (組織上) and operational mechanism (機構運用上) for the Seoul Youth Association, a youth group, to also conduct general ideological movements. However, besides such surface (表面) reasons, internal (內面的) circumstances (事情) were also latent (潛在). Among the members (「멘버」) of the Seoul Youth Association, there was a faction (一派) that denied (否定) other work/operations (他工作 - likely underground activities) and a faction that approved (是認) of it, existing in mutual opposition (對立的 氣勢). The Chŏnjinhoe was the group that approved of underground work (地X工作), consisting of Yi Chŏngyun, Pak Hyŏngbyŏng, Chŏng Paek, Kim Wŏlsŏng (金越星), and others.

Third Period

The founding of Chŏnguhoe and Chŏnjinhoe was an epoch-making (劃期的) event in the Korean social movement line, and simultaneously (同時) with the consolidation (整理) of the X-line (likely 戰線 - front line), it was undeniably a great achievement (一大收穫). Thus, signs (徵候) of conscious (意識的) unification grew strong (濃厚하야 왓으니). If the fact that those in the early movement (初期運動) who possessed considerable (多分히) past (過去) feudalistic (封建的) ideology (이데올으기) were leading (領導) the movement was an unavoidable fate (피하지 못할 運命) due to the circumstances (關係上), then as a result (結果) of the First Party Incident arrests, the internal (內部) situation had changed greatly (만이 變햇거니와), and moreover, the objective (客觀的) situation (情勢) promoted (促進) the settlement (淸算) of factional strife (派爭). After that, in 1926, the large-scale arrests (大檢擧) of the Second Party Incident began (開始), and over a hundred people from all over Korea (全鮮) were taken (가게 되고), while many others (其他 多數) fled (닷게 되니) overseas. The situation changed again (再轉), bringing about (招致) a major transformation in organization. By the Irwŏlhoe (一月會 - January Society) of Tokyo—namely An Kwangch'ŏn (安光泉), Ha P'irwŏn (河弼源), Pak Nakchong (朴洛鍾), etc.—Chŏnguhoe was dissolved (解體), and they proclaimed (외첫다) that the movement strategy (運動方略) must shift direction (방향전환) from the hitherto limited economic struggle (經濟X爭 - likely 經濟鬪爭) to an overall front movement (全線的 運動). Although it might be said (할시나) that viewing the conventional Korean social movement as a limited economic struggle was not a correct grasp (정당한 인식) of the facts (事實把握), and that such harm (폐해) arose merely from directly importing (직수입) theories from Tokyo and Osaka, the dissolution of ideological groups following the completion (완성) of the underground organization (地下組織) was a natural course (당연한 노정). And loudly calling for (소래 놉히 부르지즌 것도) a political movement (政治運動) on the entire front line (全線的) as line unification (線의 統一) was achieved (이루워지면서) was also an inevitable path (필연의 도정). At the Central Council of Korean Social Organizations convened (召集) by Chŏnjinhoe, there was debate (논쟁) on this issue. Amidst the oppositional phenomenon (對立的 現象) between permanent (常設) and non-permanent (非常設) establishment, the denial (否定) of the rules (規約) based on the spirit (精神), declaration, and platform (宣言 綱領) of its founding time (發起當時), and the theory of non-permanent establishment gained superiority (優勢). This was also an extremely natural (지극히 당연한) event, and thus, the Korean social movement took another step forward (再一步). The Central Council of Korean Social Organizations was initiated by Chŏnjinhoe. When the joint discussion meeting between the Four-Group Joint Committee and Chŏnjinhoe was held (열니엿을 때에) at the main branch (本店) of Myŏngwŏlgwan at the beginning of 1926 (1926年 正初), the Four-Group Committee side, while participating, had stated (聲明하얏든 바) several assertions (멧 가지의 주장), among which was the expression (表明) of opposition (反對) to establishing a permanent organ (常設機關). The Irwŏlhoe in Tokyo also held this assertion (잇섯스니). The Central Council of Korean Social Organizations held its congress (大會) in May 1927 (1927年 5月), and amidst deepening (深刻化) discussion (討議) on that problem,<66> it ended up being subjected to (當하고 마럿다) a ban on assembly (集合의 禁止). (From this time, the leadership of the Korean movement came to be controlled (左右되엿고) by the hands of what was commonly called (俗稱) the M-L Faction (엠, 엘派), and in opposition (對立的 地位) to it was the Seoul Faction (서울派).)

From Socialist-Nationalist Cooperation (社·民協同) to Re-Division (再分野)

Slogans (슬로-간) such as "A change of direction from the hitherto limited economic struggle (經濟X爭) to an overall front political movement (全線的 政治運動)" inevitably (필연적) gave birth to (나(生)혓다) the necessity (必要) for a cooperative front (協同線) between socialists (社會主義者) and nationalists (民族主義者). What came out of that (그에서 나온 것이) was the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會). Sin Sŏgu (申錫雨), An Chaehong (安在鴻), Hong Myŏnghŭi (洪命熹), Yi Chongnin (李鍾麟), Yi Kwanyong (李灌鎔), etc., were the central figures at the time of its initiation (發起當時). Prior to this, Song Naeho (宋乃浩), Kwŏn T'aesŏk (權泰錫), etc., of the Seoul Faction (서울系) had organized the Minhŭnghoe (民興會 - People's Promotion Society) as a pan-nationalist organization (汎民族主義團體), which was later merged (合倂) into Sin'ganhoe. Following the student incident (학생사건) in Kwangju (光州) and subsequently the mass rally incident (민중대회사건), figures from the Sin'ganhoe leadership level (幹部級), such as Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Yi Kwanyong, Hong Myŏnghŭi, and Cho Pyŏngok (趙炳玉), were arrested (가고). A movement for the dissolution (解消運動) of Sin'ganhoe arose (起하야), and at the Sin'ganhoe convention in 1931, dissolution was approved (解消 可決되엿다). The reason (理由) was that the leadership authority (領導權) of Sin'ganhoe was held (갓고 잇스니) by the petit bourgeois intelligentsia (인테리의 小市民層), who could not display fighting power (鬪X力 - likely 鬪爭力). Consequently, laborers and peasants, through the Sin'ganhoe movement, forgot (망각) their original mission (본래의 사명), and accordingly, even their class consciousness (階級的의 의식) gradually became diluted (점차로 희박하여 진다는 것이다). For those who advocated (표방하고) an overall front political movement and cried out for (부르지즌) a change of direction, the intermediary organization (媒介組織) like Sin'ganhoe could not exert capability (역량), and moreover, the class [-consciousness?] of laborers and peasants was quite insufficient (不足햇다). The signboard (看板) "Conversion from the conventional limited economic struggle to an overall front political movement" (『從來의 局限된 經濟X爭에서 全線的의 政治運動에로 轉換이란』) was splendid (華麗햇으나), but the resulting product (所産物), the Sin'ganhoe movement, was exceedingly inactive and incompetent (無爲無能이엇다). Thus, it even snatched away (빼앗아 버렷다) the conventional economic struggle from the minds of the laborers and peasants. The dissolution (解消) of Sin'ganhoe marked (표시한 것이다) the re-division (再分解) between socialists and nationalists.
Since Sin'ganhoe dissolved, already (벌서) three years (3個年) have passed without the appearance (출현도 업다) of any overt organization (表現團體). However, that being said (그럿타 해서), the dissolution of Sin'ganhoe cannot be seen (볼 수 업다) as an eternal (永遠히) denial (否認) of cooperation (協同) between socialists and nationalists (社, 民) in Korea.
One fact (事實) necessarily (반듯이) gives birth to (낫는다) a new, opposing (反對) fact. Thus, at the next stage (段階), a synthesized result (綜合的 結果) of the aforementioned two facts is born (낫는 것이다).
That Sin'ganhoe, which became unable (업게 된) to display (發揮) any capability (何等의 力量) due to its many (만어서) organizational flaws (組織上 缺陷), had to be dissolved (解消되여야 함은) was a natural (當然) and welcome (반가운 일) event (이엿다). However, we firmly (굿게) believe (밋는) that cooperation (協同) will inevitably be reorganized (再組織되고야 말 것을) by establishing (세우고) a definite sense of purpose (一定의 目的意識) and moving towards that common (共通) demand (要求). (完 - End)
(1934. 5. 6) <67>

**Summary**

This article by Kim Kyŏngjae provides a detailed account of the complex evolution of Korean ideological and socialist organizations during the approximately 15 years leading up to 1934, framed within a 25-year retrospective. It traces the formation, mergers, factional struggles (influenced by trends in Tokyo, Shanghai, and Irkutsk), and eventual dissolution of key groups like the Hwayohoe (Tuesday Society), Pukp'unghoe (North Wind Society), and their unification attempts, leading to the Chŏnguhoe. It also discusses the parallel development of the Seoul Faction and its Chŏnjinhoe. The narrative culminates in the formation and subsequent dissolution of the Sin'ganhoe, a united front organization involving socialists and nationalists, analyzing the reasons for its failure (petit bourgeois leadership, lack of fighting power, dilution of class consciousness) and expressing hope for future cooperation. The article offers a valuable insider's perspective on the intense factionalism (Hwayohoe, Pukp'unghoe, Seoul Faction, ML Faction) that characterized the early Korean socialist movement and the dynamics between overt organizations and underground activities (implied by terms like 地X組織, 他工作). Historians might find the detailed chronology of organizational shifts, named key figures involved in each faction and group, and the author's analysis of the strategic debates (economic struggle vs. political front, permanent vs. non-permanent structures, united front tactics) particularly interesting for understanding the internal complexities and challenges faced by the Korean independence and socialist movements under Japanese colonial rule.

一. 緖言

내가 7, 8歲 때에 書堂에 다닐 때에 洞里 어른들 틈에 끼어 까닭 모를 氣分에 緊張하엿든 일이 어제 갓흔데 벌서 그 사이에 25年의 歲月이 흘넛다.
이러케 생각하니 歲月이 너무도 쉽사리 흘넛거니와 내가 이제는 이러케 자랏스며 25年이라면 한 世紀의 四半年이거니 하매 퍽으나 오란 歲月갓다. 그 사이에 朝鮮 내의 團體의 轉變을 쓰기는 결코 쉬운 일이 아니다.
그의 內容이 넘우도 復雜多端한 것도 理由의 하나이려니와 아무리 表現團體만을 中心으로 하고 體系를 세우려 할지라도 그에는 언제나 裏面에 다른 組織이 잇고 人物이 잇서 그와 서로 交互關係를 갓고 잇스며 派的 勢力의 對立은 中傷과 排斥을 나엇스며 그에 따라서 離間, 謀陷, 嫉妬가 運動에 直接 혹은 間接으로 多大만 影響을 주엇다는 것이다.
그럼으로 最近 25年間의 思想團體의 轉變은 그가 우리 近世史上의 가쟝 重要한 장면으로 등장될 것이다. (畧-원문) 엇지 햇든 우리가 갈피를 잡지 못하고 左往右往하야 彷徨하든 것도 이때와 가튼 때가 일즉이 업섯고 여러 가지 意味 及 意識밋헤 발자최를 옴겨 오든 것도 이 때인 것이다.
大體 한 개의 團體가 表面化하기까지에는 그에는 源因이 잇고 動力이 잇고 그의 結果로 顯現하는 것이다. 그런데 이제 여기에는 原因도 動力도 알배기 아니고 다만 表面의 團體를 中心으로 하고 그의 轉變을 말하라는 것이며 그리고 극히 쩌른 紙面에 그의 全面이러나도록 채(充)워 주고 누구가 보든지 一讀하고 나서는 「올치」하고 25年間의 團體運動의 轉變이 그대로 머리 속에 印처지도록 要領이 잇고 아주 쉬웁게 써달나는 三千里社의 要求인 바 執筆者인 나에게 잇서서는 마치 圓形으로 된 구멍에 三角形의 鐵棒을<61> 접어너허 달라는 注文이나 다름업다. 따라서 格에 合하기는 바라지 못할 것이 執筆者인 나의 지레 근심이다.

一. 運動의 始初

(7枚로 된 一節此間畧-원문) 여러 해를 두고 싸우든 世界大戰은 獨逸의 敗戰으로 終幕을 告하고 巴里에서 그의 媾和會議가 열니어젓다.
獨逸의 復興을 㥘내는 佛蘭西는 이 機會에 獨逸로 하여곰 永遠히 머러를 들지 못하게 하려고 하엿스니 그는 米國의 월손의 提唱한 바의 自決主義를 바더드리어 獨逸의 手足을 잘너 내이기로 햇다. 다음의 問題는 獨逸과 露西亞와의 接近을 防止함에 잇슴으로 波蘭, 리드아니아, 라트바아 等의 새 成立을 承認케 되엇스니 이 물결이 흘너서 -에 까지 옴에 이르럿다.
그래서 그 일이 잇고 그 뒤 朝鮮에는 各地에 靑年會가 出生하엿스니 그의 目的은 智德體 三育의 發達을 圖謀한다는 것이나 그의 色彩는 1920年에 京城에서 吳祥根, 張德秀 等이 中心이 되여 가지고 朝鮮靑年會聯合會가 結成되엇다. 그와 同時에 朴重華, 朴珥圭, 尹德炳, 申伯雨 等이 中心이 되어 朝鮮勞働共濟會를 組織하엿스니 그는 그 때에 잇서서는 가장 進步的의 思想을 가진 그들로서 勞働者의 問題를 硏究하고 資本主義의 發達과 殖民地와의 關係도 若干 解得하는 部類의 사람들이엇다. 그러나 朝鮮勞働共濟會는 一種 文化團體이엿다고 보는 것이 正當한 見解일 것이다. 學生團體도 생기고 基督靑年會의 巡講 等이 盛行하엿다. 그러다가 外地의 諸 轉變하는 몰결이 波及됨에 이르럿다.

朴洛鍾 等에 의하야 正友會는 解體되고 運動方略을 從來의 經濟X爭에서 全線的 運動으로 방향을 전환하지 안으면 아니 된다고 외첫다. 종래의 朝鮮의 社會運動은 국한된 經濟X爭으로 본다는 것은 事實把握에 잇서 정당한 인식이 아니요. 다만 東京 大阪의 理論을 그대로 직수입하려는 데에서 그러한 폐해가 잇섯다 할시나 地下組織의 완성을 따라서 思想團體가 해체되여야 할 것은 당연한 노정이엿고 線의 統一이 이루워지면서 全線的의 政治運動을 소래 놉히 부르지즌 것도 필연의 도정이엿다. 前進會에서 召集한 朝鮮社會團體中央協議會에서 그에 대한 논쟁이 잇고 常設과 非常設의 對立的 現象에서 그의 發起 당시의 精神과 宣言 綱領에 基準한 規約을 否定하며 非常設의 理論이 優勢를 得하얏슴도 그가 지극히 당연한 일이요. 그리하야 朝鮮의 社會運動은 再一步한 것이엿다. 朝鮮社會團體中央協議會가 前進會에서 發起되엿고 1926年 正初에 明月舘 本店에서 四合對 前進會의 合同懇談會가 열니엿을 때에 四合委員會側에서는 그에 參加는 하되 멧 가지의 주장을 聲明하얏든 바 그에는 常設機關을 두자는 데에 反對를 表明햇든 것이다. 東京의 一月會에서도 그 주장을 갓고 잇섯스니 朝鮮社會團體中央協議會는 1927年 5月에 大會가 열니여서 그 問題로 討議가 深刻化하는<66> 中에 集合의 禁止를 當하고 마럿다. (이때부터 朝鮮運動의 領導權이 俗稱 엠, 엘派의 손에서 左右되엿고 그와 對立的 地位에 서울派가 잇게 되엿다.)

社·民協同에서 再分野

『從來에 국한된 經濟X爭에서 全線的 政治運動으로의 방향전환이란 이러한 슬로-간은 그는 필연적으로 社會主義者와 民族主義者와의 協同線의 必要를 나(生)혓다. 그에서 나온 것이 新幹會이엿다. 申錫雨, 安在鴻, 洪命熹, 李鍾麟, 李灌鎔 等이 그 發起當時의 中心人物이엿으며 그보다 先하야 서울系의 宋乃浩
權泰錫 等이 汎民族主義團體로 民興會를 組織하엿다가 新幹會에 合倂되엿다. 光州에 학생사건이 잇고 게속하야 민중대회사건으로 許憲, 李灌鎔, 洪命熹, 趙炳玉 等의 新幹會의 幹部級의 人物이 가고 新幹會의 解消運動이 起하야 1931年에 新幹會大會에서 解消 可決되엿다. 그 이유는 新幹會의 領導權이 인테리의 小市民層이 갓고 잇스니 그는 鬪X力을 발휘하지 못하고 勞働者와 農民은 新幹會 運動으로 하야 自己 본래의 사명을 망각하고 따라서 階級的의 의식까지도 점차로 희박하여 진다는 것이다. 全線的의 政治運動을 표방하고, 방향의 전환을 부르지즌 그들로 新幹會란 媒介組織으로는 역량을 발휘할 수도 업거니와 勞動者, 農民의 階級的 -이 아주 不足햇다. 『從來의 局限된 經濟X爭에서 全線的의 政治運動에로 轉換이란』 看板은 華麗햇으나 그의 結果로의 所産物인 新幹會運動은 넘우도 無爲無能이엇다. 그리하야 從來의 經濟X爭까지도 勞動者와 農民의 머리에서 빼아서 버렷다. 新幹會의 解消는 社會主義者와 民族主義者와의 再分解를 표시한 것이다.
新幹會가 해소하야 벌서 3個年 이래에 아무런 表現團體의 출현도 업다. 그럿타 해서 新幹會의 解消로써 朝鮮의 社, 民의 協同은 永遠히 否認된 것이라고는 볼 수 업다.
한가지의 事實은 반듯이 그와 反對의 새 事實을 낫는다. 그리하야 그 다음의 段階에 와서는 前記 두 가지의 事實이 綜合的 結果를 낫는 것이다.
組織上 缺陷이 만어서 何等의 力量을 發揮할 수 업게 된 新幹會가 解消되여야 함은 그는 當然한 일이요 반가운 일의 하나이엿다. 그러나 一定의 目的意識을 세우고 그 共通한 要求를 향하야 協同이 再組織되고야 말 것을 굿게 밋는 우리다.  (完-원문)
