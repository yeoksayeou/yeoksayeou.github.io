**Body Text Translation:**
Guest Register of Local Dignitaries Visiting Our Office

Mr. Yi Tongjŏng (李東井), Samch'ŏlli North P'yŏngan Province, T'aech'ŏn Branch Manager, visited the office on April 15th.

Mr. Hŏ Hoyŏng (許浩永) (Kyŏnggi Province, Songp'a Branch Manager & Chosŏn Boy Scouts 49th Tiger Troop Executive), visited the office on April 16th.

Mr. Yun Chun (尹埈) (Kyŏnggi Province, Chuan Branch Manager), visited the office on April 18th.

Mr. Yi Pongu (李逢雨) (Former Tōkyō Samch'ŏlli Branch Manager), visited the office on April 19th.

Mr. Hwang Ŭijun (黃義準) (Samch'ŏlli North Kyŏngsang Province, Kimch'ŏn Branch Manager & Kimch'ŏn Consumer Cooperative Executive), visited the office on April 19th.

Mr. Kim Sŭngjin (金勝鎭) (Samch'ŏlli P'yŏngyang Branch Manager), visited the office on April 20th.

Mr. Yi Sŏkkŭn (李錫根) (Samch'ŏlli North P'yŏngan Province, Sŏnch'ŏn Branch Manager), visited the office on April 25th.

Mr. Yi Ch'ŏrŭi (李哲儀) (Samch'ŏlli North Chŏlla Province, Chŏngju-ŭp Branch Manager), visited the office on April 26th.

Mr. Han Sangwŏn (韓相元) (Samch'ŏlli Kyŏnggi Province, Changdan Branch Manager), visited the office on April 28th.

Mr. Ko Sŏngnam (高成男) (Samch'ŏlli Taejŏn Branch Manager), visited the office on April 29th.<125>

Mr. Pae Chongch'ŏl (裴鍾哲) (Samch'ŏlli South Kyŏngsang Province, Kimhae Branch Manager), came up to Seoul and visited on April 30th.

Mr. Cho Kyuhwan (曹圭浣) (Samch'ŏlli Former Kwangju Branch Manager), came up to Seoul and visited on April 30th.<126>

**Summary**

This article is a simple log from the Samch'ŏlli magazine, listing individuals, primarily its regional branch managers from across Korea (and one former Tōkyō manager), who visited the main office in Seoul during the latter half of April 1934. It notes their names, affiliations (including roles in other organizations like the Chosŏn Boy Scouts or a consumer cooperative), branch locations, and the date of their visit. For historians, this piece provides concrete evidence of the magazine's extensive network throughout the Korean peninsula during the colonial period. The list of branch managers and their locations illustrates the magazine's reach, and the mention of affiliations like the Boy Scouts or consumer cooperatives hints at the interconnectedness of social and economic organizations among Koreans at the time.
