Life's Bulletin Board

It has become a Gold Rush (黃金狂時代) era, and many prominent figures (著名人士) are flocking to mines (鑛山). The names of those currently busy running about in all directions (四面八方) prospecting (探鑛) and mining (採鑛) are as follows (如左):

Hŏ Hŏn
Im Hŭng-sun
Kim An-sŏ

Min Pyŏng-ju
Cho Pyŏng-ok
Kim P'al-bong
Nam Sang-il
Yŏm Kyŏng-hun
Cho Pyŏng-sang<38>

**Summary**

This short news item from 1934 reports on a "Gold Rush" occurring in Korea, noting that numerous prominent individuals are actively involved in prospecting and mining ventures. It lists eleven specific names of well-known figures engaged in these activities. From a historical perspective, the article highlights the economic activities and speculative ventures pursued by members of the Korean elite during the Japanese colonial period, specifically the rush towards resource extraction like gold mining, possibly driven by economic pressures or opportunities within the colonial system. Historians might find the list of names particularly interesting for tracking the diverse involvements of notable figures beyond politics or culture during this era.
