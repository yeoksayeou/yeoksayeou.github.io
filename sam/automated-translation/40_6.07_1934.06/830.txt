**Body Text Translation**
The Golden Age of Genius Writers

Shakespeare wrote "A Midsummer Night's Dream" and "The Merchant of Venice" at age 30. "The Taming of the Shrew" at 33, "As You Like It" at 35, "Caesar" at 37, "Hamlet" at age 38.

Hugo's "Lucrezia Borgia" was at 30.
Dickens' "A Christmas Carol" was at 31.
Andersen's "Fairy Tales" was at 30.

Maupassant's "Boule de Suif" was at 31, "Bel-Ami" at 37.
Zola's Rougon-Macquart series started at 31, "L'Assommoir" was at 37.

Baudelaire's "Les Fleurs du mal" (惡의 꽃) was at 36.
Tolstoy's "War and Peace" (戰爭과 平和) was at 35.
Ibsen's "Brand" (푸란토 - likely referring to Brand) was at 38.

Shaw's "Man and Superman" (사람과 超人) was at 38.
Scott's "The Lady of the Lake" (湖上의 美人) was at 39.
D'Annunzio's "Triumph of Death" (死의 勝利) was at 32.
Turgenev's "Rudin" (루-진) was at 30. "A Sportsman's Sketches" (獵人日記) was at 34.
Gogol's "Dead Souls" (죽은 사람들) was at 33.
Pushkin's "The Captain's Daughter" (大尉의 따님) was at 34.

Wilde's "The Picture of Dorian Gray" (도리안그데의 畵僧) was at 35.
Flaubert's "Madame Bovary" (마담 보바리) was at 35.
Whitman's "Leaves of Grass" (풀닙사귀) was at 36.<214>

**Summary**

The article lists numerous famous Western and Russian authors (Shakespeare, Hugo, Dickens, Andersen, Maupassant, Zola, Baudelaire, Tolstoy, Ibsen, Shaw, Scott, D'Annunzio, Turgenev, Gogol, Pushkin, Wilde, Flaubert, Whitman) and the specific ages, predominantly in their 30s, at which they wrote some of their most significant works. It suggests that the 30s represent the "golden age" for these "genius writers." For historians studying colonial Korea, this article is interesting as it reflects the strong influence and admiration of Western literary canons during this period. It implicitly sets a standard or timeline for creative productivity that Korean intellectuals and aspiring writers might have measured themselves against, potentially highlighting a desire to achieve similar world-renowned status or anxieties about perceived delays in the development of modern Korean literature.
