**Summary**

This article is a simple acknowledgment listing the books and various magazines received as donations by the *Samch'ŏlli* magazine, mostly May 1934 issues (with a couple from April). The list includes two novels by prominent Korean authors, several general interest and specialized magazines (covering women, children, farming, industry, religion, arts), a newspaper, and official publications from the Japanese Government-General of Korea. For historians, this list provides a valuable snapshot of the publishing ecosystem in colonial Korea during the mid-1930s. It highlights the range of available reading materials, from Korean literary works and cultural magazines to official government reports and publications originating from Seoul, P'yŏngyang, and even Tokyo, demonstrating the interconnectedness and diversity of print culture under colonial rule. The juxtaposition of Korean cultural publications with those from the Government-General is particularly noteworthy.
