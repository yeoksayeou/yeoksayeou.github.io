Article Title: 全鮮 道會 議員 及 府協 議員, 「創氏改名」錄 = 三月 十日 現在 (Register of Name Changes [Sōshi-kaimei] for All Korean Provincial Council Members and Municipal Council Members = As of March 10th)
Author: 三千里社 (Samch'ŏlli Company)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

Regarding "Sōshi-kaimei" (Name Change), we intended to inquire about the opinions and publish the results of the name changes firstly for all Korean high officials currently serving in government offices, secondly for all thirteen Provincial Council members and Municipal Council members holding public office, and thirdly for influential private citizens in general. Already in last month's issue, our company inquired with thirteen Provincial Governors, Councilors (Sanyōkan), Police Chiefs (Keishi), Police Inspectors (Keibu), Judges and Prosecutors (Han-kenji), Administrative Officers (Rijikan), etc., totaling over fifty individuals from various levels, and made their situations public. Now, we have again inquired with the Chief Secretaries (Kanbō Shuji) of the thirteen provinces and the Internal Affairs Section Chiefs (Naimu Kachō) of twenty municipalities regarding the respective Provincial and Municipal Council members, and are publishing this now. Furthermore, in next month's issue, we intend to continue by publishing the information for general private citizens.
Additionally, because the deadline for this manuscript was extremely tight, we ask for your understanding that the responses from several provinces and municipalities that arrived after the deadline will be carried over to next month's issue.

Furthermore, for reference, according to a newspaper report on the Kyŏnggi Provincial Council meeting of March 4th, an emergency motion was proposed that day suggesting that the Provincial Council members should take the initiative in 'Sōshi' (Creating a family name) to set an example for the populace. The agenda took a detour, painting a truly cheerful scene, and it was passed with unanimous consent. The proposer was Provincial Councilor Hirayama Masao (former name Song Sŏng-jin) representing Kap'yŏng County, who addressed the assembly saying:
'With the recent revision of the Civil Affairs Ordinance, fellow countrymen on the peninsula can now establish Japanese-style "shi" (氏 - family names). While this is significant for the completion of Naisen Ittai (Japan-Korea Unity), I would like to propose that we, the council members who are representatives of the provincial residents, take the initiative in establishing our "shi" and agree to set an example for the general populace.'
Councilor Han Sang-nyong stood up, expressed his agreement, and the assembly unanimously adopted this agreement. Furthermore, Councilor Im Han-sŏn from Kaesŏng proposed, 'Regarding the detailed rules of Sōshi, I would like to invite an authority to ask questions about points of doubt.' As a result, it was decided that the Provincial Council would invite Mr. Iwanaga, the head of the Civil Affairs Section of this government office (Honpu), to give a lecture and hold a Q&A session from 10:30 AM on the 6th. Councilor Hirayama Masao, who proposed this agreement, was formerly named Song Sŏng-jin. He promptly created a family name as soon as the "Sōshi" ordinance was issued. He stated, 'For the people of the peninsula to become true subjects of the Empire, it is imperative that as many people as possible create family names as circumstances permit. We, the provincial councilors, want to take the lead in creating family names,' explaining why he proposed the agreement. Councilor Hirayama said:
'I am overjoyed that my proposal was adopted. I believe that creating family names, as many as possible at this time, is the way to comply with the Imperial Will.'
Mr. Han Sang-nyong stated:
'I strongly agree with Mr. Hirayama's proposal. Although there may be some who cannot create a family name due to family or personal circumstances, it would be better if as many people as possible did so. I also want to create a family name, but since I have been known by the name Han Sang-nyong, couldn't it be done in a way like Hirayama Han Sang-nyong? I am looking forward to hearing the lecture by the head of the Civil Affairs Section of this office on the 6th.'
Councilor Im Han-sŏn from Kaesŏng, who proposed hearing the opinion of an authority on the detailed rules of Sōshi at the Provincial Council, said:<61>
'Regarding Sōshi, perhaps even among the council members, there aren't many who know much about it? Being able to create a family name is a very joyous thing. But how is it done? Thinking that perhaps even those present here do not truly know the details, I proposed the matter of a lecture by an authority. I believe the provincial councilors will gain a considerably new understanding through the lecture on the 6th.'
Mr. Kanemitsu Satoshi (金光 敏), who is both a Provincial Councilor and a Municipal Councilor, had previously changed his former name Kim Min-sik to "Kanemitsu Satoshi." He stated:
'I strongly agree with Mr. Hirayama's proposal. Provincial councilors should all create family names as far as circumstances permit. As a Sōshi pioneer, I am speaking enthusiastically.'

**Summary**

This article from the April 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine compiles lists of Korean provincial and municipal council members, as well as some higher-ranking officials, who had adopted Japanese-style family names (Sōshi) and potentially new given names (Kaimei) by early March 1940, following the implementation of the Sōshi-kaimei policy. It includes official responses from various regional administrative offices contacted by the magazine, detailing the names submitted. The article also highlights proactive efforts within some councils, like Kyŏnggi-do and P'yŏngan-pukto, where members encouraged each other to change their names promptly to set an example for the general populace and demonstrate loyalty towards the Japanese Empire and the policy of "Naisen Ittai" (Japan-Korea Unity). The magazine notes that this is a partial list and more names, including those of private citizens, will be published later.

For historians, this article offers a valuable contemporary record documenting the initial phase of the Sōshi-kaimei policy's application among the Korean elite serving in colonial administrative structures. It reveals the varying pace of adoption across different regions and provides specific examples of individuals changing their names, including the Japanese-style names they chose. The justifications cited by proponents, emphasizing conformity with the "Imperial Will" and setting a public example, underscore the intense political pressure associated with the policy.

全鮮 道會 議員 及 府協 議員, 「創氏改名」錄 = 三月 十日 現在
｢創氏改名｣に就いては第一に現に官公署に職を奉する、朝鮮人高等官全部と、第二に公職に居られる十三道會議員及府會議員全部と第三に一般有力なる民間人士に御意見なり、その創氏結果を發表せむと思ひ、本社に於ては旣に先月號紙上にて十三道知事、參與官、警視、警部、判檢事、理事官、等各層人士五十餘氏の分を問ひ合せ世に公けにせしが、今又十三道官房主事と、二十府內務課長に夫々道、府會議員に就いて問ひ合せ今之を發表するものなり、尙來月號には 引續き 一般民間人士の分をも發表せんとす
尙、本稿は締切を非常に急いたが爲め、締切後に回答が來た數三ヶ道と府の分は來月號に　廻すことにしたるに付き御諒承を乞ふ

尙參考の爲め三月四日京畿道會の新聞記事を記せは此の日道會議員は率先『創氏』をして民衆に範を示さうといふ緊急動議が提出され一村議題は寄り道してまことに明朗な風景を描き滿場一致可決になつた、提出者は加平郡選出の平山正夫道議(舊姓名宋星鎭氏)で
『今回の民事令改正で半島同胞も內地式の＂氏＂を創設し得ることになつたが、內鮮一體の完成上意義あることにながら、道民の代表であるわれわれ議員は率先氏の創定をし、一般民衆に範を示すやう申合せをしたい』
と議場に諮つたち韓相龍議員が起つて、贊成意見を述べ滿場一致この申合せを採擇した、更に開城選出の林漢瑄議員は＂創氏の細則について權威者を招いで疑問の點を質問したい＂と提案し、その結果道會で六日午前十時半から本府岩永民事課長を招いで講演を聽き質疑をすることになつた、この申合せを提議した平山正夫議員は舊名を宋星鎭といつてゐたが、氏は＂創氏＂の令下るや逸早く創氏したが、半島民が眞の皇國臣民たるには是非とも事情の許す限り一人も多く創氏しなけばならぬ、われわれ道議員から率先して創氏したいとこの申合を提議したのだ 平山議員は
『私の提議が採擇になつて喜びに耐へません、この際一人でも多く創氏することが御聖旨に副ふ所以だと存じます』
韓相龍氏は語る
『平山君の提議には大贊成です家庭とか個人の關係で創氏出來ない人もあるかと存じますが出來るだけ多く創氏なさつた方がいゝでせう、私も創氏したいのですが韓相龍の名で通つてゐたのですから例へば平山韓相龍といふやうに出來ないものでせうかね、六日本府の民事課長の講話を聞きたいと思つてゐます』
道會で創氏の細則について權威者の意見を聞かうといふ開城の林漢瑄議員は＜61＞
『創氏については議員の方々でも餘り知つてゐる人がないのでせうか、創氏出來るといふことは非常に嬉しいことです、だがどうするのか、この席の方も本當に詳しく知らないだらうと思つて權威者の講話の件を提議したのです、六日の講話で道議員は隨分認識を新たにすることゝ思ふ』
道議であり府議である金光敏氏はさきに舊名金敏植を＂カネミツサトシ＂と改めた人で、氏は語る
『平山君の提議には大贊成です、道會議員はすべからく事情の許す限り創氏するんたね、創氏先輩として氣焰をあげてゐるよ』
