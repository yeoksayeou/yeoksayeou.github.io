Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제4호
Publication Date: 1940년 04월 01일
Article Title: 長老敎 分裂과 그 意見 (Presbyterian Church Split and Opinions on It)
Author: Chŏng In'gwa (鄭仁果), Ham T'aeyŏng (咸台永), O Kŏnyŏng (吳建泳), Yang Chusam (梁株三), Yi Hun'gu (李勳求)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey/Questionnaire)

Where Lies the Truth of the Split? - The Seminary Issue is Not Worthy of Discussion
Chŏng In'gwa, Korean Presbyterian Church General Assembly Side

It is extremely regrettable that some figures within the Presbyterian Church have recently initiated a divisive movement, causing a stir in the church community. Furthermore, in the current emergency situation, unity is being called for in all domestic affairs. Following this trend, our Christian churches, both Korean and Japanese (內鮮 兩敎會), are expected to unite and see unification in the near future. It is therefore very sad that the Korean Presbyterian Church, which was already one body, is splitting.

Unity comprises three elements: spiritual unity, operational unity, and unity in execution methods. Spiritually, since we adhere to the same doctrines and scriptures, there is absolutely no reason for spiritual division. From an operational perspective, for example, the seminary issue is not considered problematic as the authorities (當局) are implementing an approval system (인가제) and providing guidance.

While the purpose of the divisive movement is unclear, I hear that they intend to split off and merge with the Japanese (내지인) Presbyterian church. What is the point ofわざわざ (wazawaza -わざわざ-) splitting off just to merge? Wouldn't it be more rational to merge with the Japanese church as we are, without splitting?

I Will Remain Silent for Now
Ham T'aeyŏng, Korean Presbyterian Church Splinter Faction Side

You ask for my opinion regarding the recent split of the Korean Presbyterian Church into North and South? <103> Well, it seems the public generally considers me a leader of the separatist faction, and while I am not without opinions, I intend to maintain silence.

Although I am of the Separatist Faction
O Kŏnyŏng, Korean Presbyterian Church Splinter Faction Side

I am one of the committee members of the separatist faction, but I do not wish to state my opinion regarding the separation.

The True Situation is Unclear
Yang Chusam, Korean Presbyterian Church Splinter Faction Side

I have heard that there is a dispute within the Korean Presbyterian Church causing it to split into North and South, but I do not yet understand the reasons for the split well. Therefore, I have nothing to say regarding my opinion.

Separation is a Sign of Weakening - Unite, I Hope They Remain United
Yi Hun'gu, Social Commentator Side (Vice President, Chosŏn Ilbo)

I am extremely saddened to hear that the Korean Presbyterian Church is splitting into two factions. The Methodist Church, which originally had two factions, the Southern Methodist Church and the Northern Methodist Church, united under the name Korean Methodist Church to work as one body. It is profoundly regrettable that the Presbyterian Church, which was already one body, is splitting into North and South.

And if they split, it's a fundamental principle that their strength will weaken. For a Korean organization, whose strength can be seen as weak even when united, how could it not weaken further if it splits? The impact on the church will also inevitably be a weakening of its strength. <104>
<103-104>

**Summary**

This article presents various opinions on the impending split within the Korean Presbyterian Church in 1940. Chŏng In'gwa, representing the General Assembly, argues against the split, citing spiritual unity, the resolution of the seminary issue via government approval, and the broader trend towards unity, including potential unification with the Japanese Presbyterian Church. Representatives of the "splinter faction," Ham T'aeyŏng and O Kŏnyŏng, refuse to comment, while Yang Chusam claims ignorance of the reasons. Social commentator Yi Hun'gu laments the division, drawing a parallel with the Methodist unification and emphasizing that splitting will inevitably weaken the Korean church. Historically, the article is significant as it captures the internal conflict and external pressures (Japanese colonial government control, forced Japanization reflected in the seminary approval system and the talk of merging with Japanese churches) faced by Korean religious institutions during a period of heightened assimilation policy under the "emergency situation." The varied responses, from advocating unity (potentially including merger with Japanese churches) to strategic silence, highlight the difficult choices and positions individuals took under colonial rule.

近者 장로교회의 일부 인사들이 분열 운동을 일으켜 교계에 한 파문을 일으키는 것은 매우 유감스러운 일이외다. 더욱이 현하 비상시국에 처하여 국내 만반 사업에 통일을 부르짖고 있으며, 이 대세에 따라 우리 기독교도 內鮮 兩敎會가 연합하여 불원한 장래에 통일을 보게 될 터인데 이미 한 몸이었던 朝鮮장로회가 분리한다는 것은 매우 섭섭한 일입니다.
통일에는 정신 통일, 사업 통일, 실행방법 통일, 이 세 가지의 요소를 가지고 있는데 정신적으로는 같은 교리와 성서를 신봉하니 정신적으로 분열할 이유가 조금도 없고, 사업적 견지에서 보더라도 예컨대 신학교 문제 같은 것은 당국에서 인가제를 실시하여 지도하고 있으니 문제될 것이 없다고 생각합니다.
분열운동의 목적은 분명치 못 하나 듣는 바에 의하면 분립하여 가지고 내지인 장로교회와 합한다 하니 구태여 분열해 가지고 합할 것은 무엇이며, 차라리 분열하지 말고 합한 그대로 내지 교회와 합류하는 것이 합리적이 아닐까 생각합니다.
