Provincial Council Trends and Name Creation Establishment
Resolutions Passed by Kyŏnggi and P'yŏngan Pukto Provincial Councils

The general society is keenly observing how the name changing and renaming (創氏 改名) advocated by Governor-General Minami is progressing in the various Provincial Councils (道會) of the 13 provinces, which began convening from early March. The trends identified as of today (March 8th) are in Kyŏnggi-do and P'yŏngan Pukto; the situation in the other 11 provinces is unknown. Looking at the situation in these two provinces:
Kyŏnggi-do: On March 4th, during a meeting, <21> Provincial Councilor Hirayama Masao (formerly Song Sŏng-jin), elected from Kap'yŏng, proposed that all provincial councilors take the lead in changing their names. Following a supporting speech by government-appointed Provincial Councilor Han Sang-ryong, the chairman, Governor Kanō, put it to the floor, and it was passed unanimously.
P'yŏngan Pukto = At the Provincial Council meeting on March 2nd, all 23 attending members resolved to change their names with unanimous consent.

400,000 Sturdy Youths

Suffrage Movement Again This Year

Education and Finance Sectors
Over 1,000 'Thousand-Sŏk Earners' in Seoul
The Wealthy from Provincial Cities Keep Moving to the Capital

The phenomenon of wealthy people increasingly gathering within the Seoul city walls has become notably prominent recently, seemingly to avoid the numerous donations and public levies imposed in provincial cities. According to a recent investigation, the number of wealthy individuals in Seoul city who earn over 1,000 sŏk (석, a measure of grain) of rice reaches a high of over 1,060.

Fifty Thousand as Coal Miners
Korean Laborers Gone to the Mainland (Japan)

To overcome the government's coal shortage, it was decided to supplement the labor shortage in mainland (內地) coal mines with workers from Korea. Known as the coal production increase support team, laborers were sent from Korea to the mainland. Already 22,000 (as of early February) have arrived and dispersed to various mines, and it seems a large contingent of 30,000 more will arrive, totaling a massive 50,000 people.

One Won Becomes Ten Thousand Won
Story of the Manchukuo Lottery

Recently, the "Tomikuji" (富籤, lottery) has been implemented, which is similar to the "Man'in-kei" (萬人計). While this is the first time <23> it's being held in our country, in Manchukuo (滿洲國), it has already been widely implemented under names like "Saipiao" (彩票), "Fukumin Shōken" (福民獎券), or "Yūmin Saipiao" (裕民 彩票) to fund poor relief facilities, medical equipment, disaster relief, and social work expenses. They issue 400,000 won worth monthly with 1-won tickets. If you win, you get this kind of money:

Kyŏngju Treasures for Three Thousand Won
When Foreigners First Came

Soon after, the authorities decided to take direct charge of the excavation, leading to today's achievements.

**Summary**

This 1940 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine offers a snapshot of Korean society under late Japanese colonial rule, covering politics, military matters, the economy, and culture. It reports on the controversial name-changing policy (창씨개명) being adopted by prominent Koreans, the dramatic increase in Korean "volunteers" for the Japanese army (including those making blood oaths), and the presence of Korean officers in the Imperial Japanese Army. Economic news includes the concentration of wealth in Seoul, the forced mobilization of tens of thousands of Korean laborers for coal mines in Japan, and details about lotteries. Culturally, it highlights a boom in Korean literature publishing, particularly collected works (전집), the employment of marathoner Son Ki-jŏng, and the reception of Japanese cultural elements like cinema and literary prizes. For historians, the article is valuable for its candid (though likely censored) portrayal of the implementation of assimilation policies like name changing, the scale of wartime mobilization affecting Koreans, the complex roles of Korean elites and military figures, economic shifts driven by colonial needs, and the state of Korean cultural production amidst intense Japanization pressure. The details on publishing figures and the list of military personnel are particularly significant data points.

朝鮮出版部, 博文, 漢圖, 三文, 永昌 學藝社 等 合戰
침체하던 朝鮮 문화 사회에 수3년 내로 출판 문화가 왕성하기 시작하여 유사 이래 처음이라할 活況을 정하여 같은 문학 전집을 들고 일어나서 반도 문단의 거성 전부를 등장시켜 「전집 합전」을 하였는데 이제는 이럭저럭 그 성적을 외부에서 窺知할 수 있게 되었음으로 그 대략을 探報하면 아래와 같은데 본사 조사부에서 조사한 출판 부수는 상세하고도 정확한 것이 있으나 이것은 참아 상업 도덕 상 여기에 수자 공개만은 피하고 그저 「몇 판」 「몇 판」된 것을 記할 뿐이다. 그런데 이 「한 판」이란 것은 대개 천부가 보통 습관인데 그러나 서점에 의하여는 500부를 1판으로 하는 데도 있고 또는 2,000부, 3,000부를 1판으로 하는 데도 있어 그도 균일치 않음을 미리 알고 이 수자를 읽어주기를 바란다.

十五萬圓 資金에 二十萬 冊 賣盡
그 밖에 單行本와 文庫本도 多數
더욱 「문학전집」을 위하여 이상 5社에서 투자한 운전 자금이 약 150,000원이 되며 또한 그 발매된 延部數를 치면 약 70권, 그 책부는 200,000부의 거대한 부수에 달하여 수 3년 내의 朝鮮 시장이 얼마나 흥왕함을 설명하고도 남음이 있는데 이렇게 전집물 외에도 博文 書館의 「博文 文庫」가 있었고 학예사의 「조선문고」가 있어 그도 거대한 부수를 발매하고 있었다. (文庫 분은 차호 계속)
이 밖에도 유수한 단행본으로서 洛陽의 殘價를 비싸게 한 서적이 文章社, 人文社, 三文社 등으로부터도 다수히 발매되었다.

「京日 文化 劇場」 五千圓
一個月의 收益이 好調
朝鮮서 처음인 문화 극장인 京城 日報社 직영의 「京日 文化 劇場」은 소형이지만 상영물이 교화에 適宜한 것이 일반 문화층의 지지를 받아서 그 영업 성적도 대단히 호조로서 지난 1월의 1개월 중 영업 성적을 보건대 총수입 9,872원에 달하여 諸 잡비 일절 경비를 공제하고도 순익 5,000원을 거하였다는데 그 건물은 三中井 百貨店에서 돈을 내여 건축하여 주고 京日서는 임대료를 문다고 한다.
