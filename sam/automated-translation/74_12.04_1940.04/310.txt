Article Title: 喜劇 家和萬事成 二景 (Comedy: Two Scenes of 'When the Family is Harmonious, All Affairs Prosper')
Author: 金元浩, 孫一平, 新春漫談 (Kim Wŏnho, Son Ilp'yŏng, Sinch'un Mandam)
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Comedy: Two Scenes of 'When the Family is Harmonious, All Affairs Prosper'
						Kim Wŏnho, Son Ilp'yŏng, Sinch'un Mandam
Manp'o ... (Man) Kim
His Wife ... (Woman) Choe
Ch'unsik ... (Man) Son

*Bang, bang, bang* - (Sound of knocking on the door)

Kim Wŏnho: Honey... please open the door, I'm asking you to open the door. Aish, it's cold (sneezes). Aish, they say the dawn wind is a cold wind, and it’s as chilly as my wife’s glare.
Honey... Ah? Please open the door. Good heavens, the head of the household has returned, and even if you can't welcome me happily, what kind of greeting is this anywhere in the world, not even opening the door? Honey- Dear- Wife- *Okusan* (オクサソ - Mrs.) - Dear- Okcha (玉子)- Hey- Okcha, open the door.
Aish, it's cold- I'm definitely going to catch a cold at this rate.
Eh- cold (sneezes).
Honey... Ah! Are you unable to open the door because you're sleeping, or are you awake but not opening it because you're sulking? Oh-kay- If I stay out here all night like this, catch a cold, get a fever of 180 degrees, my fingernails and toenails turn black, and I just die, do you think my ghost will just let this resentment go?
Aish- cold (sneezes).
(Sound of a dog barking)
Shh- Hey dog, hmm, still, you're better than the mistress of the house. You came out to greet me even though you were sleeping. So, is your mistress asleep or awake... What- what- she's awake? Crouching just inside the gate? If I go in now, I'll be in trouble, so I should endure it even if it's a bit cold? Get outta here, you mutt! Even if you are a dog, this is truly dog-like behavior (개수작 - mischievous trick). At the very least, my wife is a wise mother and good wife, renowned in the world, a *yasashii, yayayasashii* (ヤサシイ ヤヤヤサシイ - gentle, gentle-gentle) (sneezes) wise mother and good wife. Even if her husband, due to social obligations, unavoidably went to a banquet and, with one drink, another drink, and yet another drink,
*Sake wa namida ka* (サ-ケワナミタカ - Is sake tears?)
*Tameiki ka* (タメイキカ - Or sighs?)
lost track of the night in the wind and returned late, she's not the kind of woman who wouldn't understand that much. You? Hey, you mutt, Patuki, you must have seen wrong, go look again and come back out.
(Woof woof, dog barking sound)
What do you mean, where should I go in? What a stupid dog. You came out through the dog hole, so obviously, you should go back in through the dog hole, what other nonsense is this? (Woof... woof... dog barking sound)
Hey, you annoying mutt! Are you telling me to follow you through this hole too? Firstly, the hole is too narrow for a person to get through. Secondly,<214> I'm scared of my wife, it's difficult. Thirdly, humans are, at the very least, the highest among animals, how could I possibly go in and out through the hole you use? Ugh, I can't do it. Quick, go tell my dear wife that I said so – before I count to one hundred stars twinkling in the sky up there, she needs to open the door. If not, tell her I'll take the whole salary envelope I got today just as it is and go off somewhere, anywhere. Eh- one, two, three, four, (counts up to 70, 80) ninety-nine- Now there's just one last one left. Ah- will this one remaining star guide the husband, shivering in the cold dawn wind, to the warm lower part of the *ondol* floor? Or will it cause him to leave on an aimless journey, salary envelope still in hand? Separated by a single gate, the fate of this young couple is as precarious as a lamp in the wind. Ninety-nine.

Woman: (From inside) Honey- (Loudly)

Kim Wŏnho: Aigoo, you scared me!

Woman: Ah- Go where? Hurry up and send the salary envelope in here.

Kim Wŏnho: What? Just send in the salary envelope? How pathetic. Like leftover *chapch'ae*, worse than a firefly sitting on the roof of a thatched house. So, the dog can go in, and the salary can go in, but I'm the only one who can't enter my own house.

Woman: Stop your whining and just send in the salary envelope.

Kim Wŏnho: Why should I send it in? It's my salary, earned with my own skill, my own strength, spending my own time. Why send it in?

Woman: Why, do you think you earned that salary all by yourself? Don't be ridiculous. Ah- can one go to a salaried job without eating meals? Does one go out to a salaried job without wearing clothes? I, who cook breakfast at dawn for thirty days a month, also have the right to receive that salary, I tell you.

Kim Wŏnho: Right, right. So, the husband is outside shivering from the cold, and when you say 'Just send in the salary,' I should say 'Yes, ma'am-' and send it in? Would that be the right thing for me to do? Forget it, forget it. So what if I came home a bit late and drunk at night? Locking the gate, making the neighborhood noisy, and demanding I push only the salary envelope in – what's that about? Look, because I love you dearly,<215> I'd love to fulfill your wish, push in just the salary envelope, and suffer Patuki's fate outside the gate, but if word of this gets out, do you think the fifty million men of our country will just stand idly by?

Woman: There are fifty million women too, so let's give it a try.

Kim Wŏnho: Oh, she just doesn't understand the state of affairs. Ah- what times are these to be acting like this? It's a national emergency. At a time when we must overcome the national crisis through total harmony and total unity (총친화 총합격), this kind of strife within the family fence between male and female is really unacceptable.

Woman: If you know it's a national emergency, why don't you know that wandering around drunk late at night is wrong?

Kim Wŏnho: Oh, absolutely not, how could I not know? Of course, I know. Who would know if I didn't? Because I know, because I know, that's why I'm standing here at my own house gate seeking your understanding. If I thought I did right, wouldn't I be kicking the door down and shouting orders, not standing here like this? Look- tonight I truly made a mistake, so please forgive me just this once. A banquet is simply a gathering of many people, what can I do if my intentions and thoughts don't get through easily?

Woman: Why is it then, when you go to a banquet, I told you to hide your hat and shoes somewhere inconspicuous near the threshold before going in, and then when the streetcars stop running, slip out pretending to go to the backhouse? Why don't you implement that?

Kim Wŏnho: Look, that's theory, reality isn't like that. Where would there conveniently be a suitable place to hide a hat and shoes? And let's say there was. If I hide them carelessly and they get stolen, who suffers the loss?

Woman: That's why you should take out property insurance on your shoes and hat.

Kim Wŏnho: Stop talking such nonsense. Which insurance company insures shoes and hats?
Aish- cold (sneezes)
Aish- cold. Honey, now that I'm sobering up, it feels even colder.
Look- enough already, open the door. Someone might pass by and see.

Woman: Do you even know what time it is?

Kim Wŏnho: Eh- what time is it now? It looks dark.

Woman: Ah- just listen to the clock striking. (Clock strikes five)

Kim Wŏnho: What- it's only five o'clock now?

Woman: What- *only* five o'clock now?

Kim Wŏnho: Honey, don't I still have to sleep for three more hours before going to work?

Woman: Aigoo, listen to him playing innocent- So you're saying you regret not being able to just sleep out all night?

Kim Wŏnho: Ah- the moonlight is also desolate. They say the crescent moon is like a small boat, but that moon, how thin it has become, like the curve of a bow. Hey- Moon- you must have suffered quite a bit from a nagging wife too. You look absolutely haggard.

Woman: The moon is female. Why do you think that moon <216> became thin from heartache because of a woman? It's because she met the wrong husband and couldn't sleep a wink, waiting for him every night with a stew pot by the hearth – that's why she looks like that.

Kim Wŏnho: Hey- Moon, let me ask you. You would understand my heart. As a wife who pledged with a rock-solid oath to live together for a hundred years and be buried in the same grave even after death, is it right to torment her drunken husband like this? Hey Okcha (玉子), *Tamako* (タマコ)... *Tamako-chan* (タマコチヤソ)! Come out and look at the nameplate. Whose name is written on it? I am, at the very least, the head of this household (戶主), and you treat the head of the household like this.

Woman: Sorry, but there are two heads of household in this house.

Kim Wŏnho: Heavens no, how can there be two heads of household in one house?

Woman: I am also a head of household, if you will.

Kim Wŏnho: What? A woman is also a head of household?

Woman: Humph! When we rented this house, whose money paid the deposit, huh?

Kim Wŏnho: Whose money? It was from selling the gold ring.

Woman: And whose gold ring was that?

Kim Wŏnho: Whose was it? Whose could it be?

Woman: That's my point. The deposit was paid by pawning my ring, so? I also have half the rights to this house – that's what I mean.

Kim Wŏnho: Agh, stop arguing, damn it! Were cement and concrete the only things you traded in?

Woman: Hurry up and send in the salary envelope. If I inspect the envelope and the amount of money is correct, I'll let you in.

Kim Wŏnho: And if it doesn't match, you won't let me in?

Woman: Of course, that's right.

Kim Wŏnho: I won't give you the salary envelope.

Woman: Look at that! You just used the banquet or whatever as an excuse and went around drinking at this bar and that bar with the salary money, didn't you? Look, think about it yourself. In a household with big kids and little kids hanging around, why are you doing this? Huh?

Kim Wŏnho: Ah- who are you scolding? Are you tormenting me? (Kicks the door) Open the door! Open up! The master of this house has returned! Open the door quickly!

Woman: Tell him that in our house, there is no master who comes home late at night drunk on payday.

Kim Wŏnho: Tell her that wasn't the case originally, but tonight, due to social obligations, he was unavoidably late.

Woman: Tell him if that's really the case, he should hand over the salary envelope first.

Kim Wŏnho: Tell her to open the gate so the person can enter together with the salary envelope.

Woman: Tell him he can only come in after the salary envelope inspection passes without issue.

Kim Wŏnho: Aish- her attitude is quite firm now. Tell her not to end up like Poland (폴-랜드) by being pointlessly stubborn.

Woman: Tell him not to act all high and mighty only to be disgraced like Britain (영국).
Kim Wŏnho: Tell her I'm the Hitler (힛틀너) type.

Woman: Tell her I'm the Mussolini (묻소리니) type.

Kim Wŏnho: This is serious. Hey- hey, is anyone around? If there's anyone good at talking,<217> please try to persuade my wife- What did I say from the beginning? I hesitated, saying the woman seemed a bit too stubborn, but that darn Ch'unsik fellow insisted that a bride needs to be a bit stubborn to be reliable. Thinking it might be true, I married her, and now she's so reliable she kicks out her husband and guards the house. Is there any stronger defender than this?

Ch'un: Hey friend, why are you out here instead of going in?

Kim Wŏnho: Who's that?

Ch'un: It's me, Ch'unsik. Ch'unsik.

Kim Wŏnho: Oh- you came at the right time. I was just complaining about you. When you acted as matchmaker for my marriage, what did you say? Huh? What did you say?

Ch'un: What did I say? I said the bride being a bit stubborn makes her reliable, so she's suitable as wife material, that's what I said.

Kim Wŏnho: You really said that.

Ch'un: Of course, I did.

Kim Wŏnho: Get outta here, you enemy-like fellow! Because I fell for your trick, I'm in this mess now.

Ch'un: What- what kind of mess are you talking about... Come on- let's go inside, you'll catch a cold. The madam probably fell asleep waiting for you, what's all this fuss? Madam! Madam!

Kim Wŏnho: Call her all you want, she won't come out. Do you think I can get in before the salary envelope inspection is over?

Ch'un: Madam! Madam!

Woman: Who is it?

Ch'un: It's Ch'unsik. Has my wife come to your house?

Woman: No-o.

Ch'un: Then where did she go?

Woman: Why, where did she go?

Ch'un: Well, there was a company banquet, so I came home a bit late, and she locked the door and disappeared.

Kim Wŏnho: Humph, what a world. One locks the door from the outside and leaves, the other locks it from the inside and won't let me in.

Ch'un: Anyway, please open the door.

Woman: (Sound of door opening) You come in too. But be warned, the salary envelope inspection will be quite thorough.

Kim Wŏnho: Yes- I was cold. Now I can finally live.

Ch'un: Man, why are you always out so late?

Kim Wŏnho: And you?

Ch'un: Me? I had a banquet, that's why.

Kim Wŏnho: Why, do you think I don't have banquets just like you?

Woman: Hurry up and give me the salary envelope.

Ch'un: Is today payday for you too?

Woman: Hurry up and hand it over.

Kim Wŏnho: Hold on- Aish, I'm thirsty. Give me a bowl of cold water, then I'll give it to you.

Woman: Alright, while I go get the cold water, match the salary slip and the money exactly.

Kim Wŏnho: O-K. Hey, friend.

Ch'un: Yeah?

Kim Wŏnho: Your salary envelope is still intact, right?

Ch'un: Yeah.

Kim Wŏnho: Anyway, quickly lend me just 20 wŏn.

Ch'un: Man, I'm short too, I was on my way to borrow from you.

Kim Wŏnho: Man, aren't you our matchmaker? If I don't have the 20 wŏn, the salary will be short,<218> and it'll be a huge problem. Come on- quickly-

Ch'un: This is serious. Here.

Kim Wŏnho: *Arigatou* (アリガトウ - Thank you).

Woman: Here- I brought the water, now the salary.

Kim Wŏnho: Agh, you really don't trust your husband. Here- look- 89 wŏn, 82 chŏn.

Woman: One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight.
(Sound of counting paper money, sound of coins)

Kim Wŏnho: Well? Anything to say?

Woman: I'm sorry.

Ch'un: Madam, that's why one should respect the husband first, not suspect him first. And you too, friend, maintain some dignity as the head of the household. What is this?

Woman: Sir, I'm sorry. I suspected him for no reason.

Ch'un: Well- I'll be going now.

Kim Wŏnho: Okay, go well. Where will you look for your wife?

Ch'un: Isn't it obvious? She'll be at her parents' house.

Kim Wŏnho: Then you're going straight there to look for her?

Ch'un: I have to, what choice is there? If the household is overturned, everything goes wrong.

Kim Wŏnho: Of course. Hurry and go, please.

Ch'un: Um, Madam.

Woman: Yes?

Ch'un: I have a difficult request.

Woman: Please tell me, whatever it is.

Ch'un: Well, it's not that, but as it happens, my salary is a bit short. I'm going to my wife's parents' house to pick her up now, but I'll surely fail the salary envelope inspection. Could you please lend me 40 wŏn?

Kim Wŏnho: You too, man. Don't you have big kids and little kids trailing after you? So how could you? Huh? You need to reassure your wife and children.

Ch'un: It's just for today, I won't do it again.

Kim Wŏnho: You drink a bit too much, and frankly, you're extravagant with your spending money too. So, how must your wife feel?

Woman: Here's 40 wŏn, take it quickly and go reassure his mother. A wife wants to trust and live with her husband, even if she's being deceived.

Kim Wŏnho: Of course. As they say, *kahwa manasasŏng* (家和萬事成 - when the family is harmonious, all affairs prosper). You just have to keep things peaceful, even if it means telling a white lie.

Ch'un: Well- I'm off then. Thank you very much, Madam.

Woman: Go carefully.

Kim Wŏnho: I'll lock the gate.

Ch'un: Hey, friend. Then, out of the 40 wŏn, consider 20 wŏn as repayment for what I lent you earlier, and just give me back the remaining 20 wŏn.

Kim Wŏnho: Whatever you say. Just as they say, *kahwa manasasŏng*, we need to keep the household peaceful first and foremost. Ha...

Ch'un: Ha...
(End) <219>

**Summary**

This short two-scene comedy depicts two husbands, Kim Wŏnho and his friend Ch'unsik, facing domestic strife after returning home late and drunk on payday in 1940 Seoul. Kim is locked out by his wife, Choe (also called Okja/Tamako), who demands his salary envelope for inspection before letting him in. Ch'unsik arrives, revealing his own wife has left him under similar circumstances. Through humorous bickering and negotiation, involving temporary loans between the families facilitated by the wives, both men manage to temporarily patch things up by ensuring their salary envelopes appear full, prioritizing household peace (*kahwa manasasŏng*) over honesty. The play humorously highlights contemporary marital dynamics, financial pressures on salaried men, and the power wives wielded within the domestic sphere, particularly concerning finances. For historians, the references to the "national emergency" (국가 비상시) and calls for "total harmony and unity" (총친화 총합격/총단결), awkwardly inserted by Kim to justify his actions, alongside comparisons to Poland, Britain, Hitler, and Mussolini, vividly reflect the pervasive wartime atmosphere and state propaganda filtering into everyday life and even domestic disputes in colonial Korea during the early stages of World War II. The use of Japanese terms like *Okusan* and *Tamako* also points to the linguistic environment of the time.
