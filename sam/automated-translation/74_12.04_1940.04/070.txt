**Body Text Translation**

Samch'ŏlli Urgent Bulletin Board, Armed Women's Group Among Kim Il Sung Bandit Group, 25 Abductees Return Safely

Of the 140 people abducted by the bandit group led by the bandit leader Kim Il Sung (金日成) from Tamalukou (大馬鹿溝), across the river from Hamgyŏngbuk-to, late on the night of the 12th, 25 individuals (1 Japanese [내지인], 13 Koreans [朝鮮人], 9 Manchus [滿人], 2 White Russians [白系 露人]) were released at 6:00 AM on the 13th after camping overnight on Hill 133, 5 ri northwest of the Tamalukou headquarters, and returned around 11:00 AM the same day.

According to the accounts of those released, the bandit group (匪團) consisted of 150 people, including 7 or 8 armed women (武裝한 婦女子). As border security has become tense, the villagers have evacuated and are on alert. <68>

**Summary**

This news report from April 1940 details the abduction of 140 people near the Hamgyŏngbuk-to border by a group led by Kim Il Sung, referred to as a "bandit leader". 25 of the hostages (including Japanese, Korean, Manchu, and White Russian individuals) were released the next morning after being held overnight. The released hostages reported that the group numbered 150 and included several armed women. The incident heightened security tensions, causing villagers to evacuate. For historians, this article offers insight into the colonial Japanese perspective on Kim Il Sung's anti-Japanese forces, labeling them "bandits." It also provides specific details about the composition of his group (including armed women) and the multi-ethnic makeup of the border region populace affected by such incidents, illustrating the tangible fear and disruption caused by resistance activities.

12일 末明 咸北 對岸인 大馬鹿溝에서 匪首 金日成의 인솔인 匪團에 납치되었던 140명 중 25명(내지인 1명, 朝鮮人 13명, 滿人 9명, 白系 露人 2명)은 大馬鹿溝 본부에서 서북방 5리의 133 고지에서 노영하고 13일 오전 6시에 석방되어 동 11시경 귀환하였다.
석방된 사람들의 이야기에 의하면 匪團은 150명으로 되고 그 중 무장한 부녀자 7, 8명도 섞기여 있다는 사실인데 이 국경 치안이 긴박되여 부락민은 피난하고 경계 중이다.＜68＞
