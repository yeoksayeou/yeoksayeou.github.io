**Body Text Translation:**
A Record of the Esteemed Wives' Circle's Appreciation of the Arts

To what extent does the circle of esteemed wives of prominent figures in our society appreciate literature and the arts? Furthermore, how much do they enjoy and evaluate the works, music, and dance of fellow women? To find this out, we asked several ladies of Seoul society the following four questions.

1.  Which works (author and title) by Chosŏn's female writers (whether by ladies of the past like Hŏ Nansŏrhŏn, Hwang Chini, etc., or those of the modern literary scene) have you read, Madam?
2.  In your household, do you recommend literary works to your children and have them read them?
3.  Which serialized Chosŏn novels in the newspapers are you currently reading, Madam?
4.  Have you had the chance to hear or see the films of actress Mun Yebong, the dances of dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, or the art of musicians Chŏng Hunmo, Pak Kyŏnghŭi, and Im Sanghŭi? And what were your impressions? <130>

Mrs. Kim Ugyŏng, wife of Mr. Ch'oe Rin, Myŏngnyun-chŏng 1-chōme 45

I humbly pray for the prosperity of your esteemed publication.
1. I have heard about and seen some of Lady Nansŏrhŏn's poems. I have never read the works of Lady Hwang Chini.
2. I encourage the children towards mathematics and science rather than literature.
3. I do not read newspaper novels.
4. I have heard and seen the dances of Miss Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi and the songs/music (歌樂) of Miss Pak Kyŏnghŭi. I consider them our great pride.

Mrs. Chŏng Talsŏng, wife of Mr. Yi Yunjae, Ch'angsin-chŏng 633

1. Hŏ Nansŏrhŏn wrote only in pure classical Chinese, so one must be able to read that well. Hwang Chini composed a few *sijo* poems. Works by female writers of the past are all just like this. Although there are many works by modern female writers, people like me are old-fashioned housewives, so I haven't read such things at all.
2. The boy kids (년석들은) are still young and studying in middle school, so I don't want to recommend such things for them to read yet. The girls (계집애들은) have all finished middle school and almost all have married off (거지반 출가했읍니다).
3. I am so busy with household chores (살림살이) that I don't have the leisure to read them.
4. One needs to be free to go and see such things. It's difficult to visit a theater even once a year. However, I sometimes hear the voices of people like Miss Pak Kyŏnghŭi singing solos on the radio. Having no ear for music, I only know the impression that it sounds pleasant.

Wife of Mr. Paek Kwansu, City, Wŏnnam-chŏng 197

1. I read some a few times before, but now it's been too long, and I've forgotten everything. I can't read modern works at all. For some reason, I'm always busy, and there's less and less time (사이) to read books.
2. Before I even recommend them, the children know better and read magazines and newspapers themselves.
3. Again, due to lack of time, I go without reading even one of the many newspapers that arrive.
4. I heard that Miss Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's dancing is so good, but I haven't seen it yet, so what can I say?

Mrs. Yi Chŏngŭng, wife of Mr. Yi Chŏngjae, Kyŏngsŏng-bu Nagwŏn-chŏng 214

1. I have often heard (더러 드렀읍니다마는) stories of Hwang Chini's beautiful *sijo*, but I have not read <131> the books.
2. I don't particularly recommend them to the children, but it seems they read a lot on their own. That they read many works by mainland (Japanese - 내지) authors might be because their proficiency in the Korean language is insufficient.
3. I generally read the serialized novels in the Chosŏn language newspapers.
4. I watch various things as a companion to the children. They were interesting.

Mrs. Pak Ŭnhye, wife of Mr. Chang Tŏksu, Chegi-chŏng 149-4

1. I have read works by Hwang Chini, Pak Hwasŏng, and Kim Malbong.
2. If I had children, I would certainly recommend and have them read.
3. I am reading *Hwasangbo* (華想譜).
4. I have heard and seen them all—and my impression? "How could they!" (『어쩌면!』 입니까) [Ambiguous: could mean amazement or perhaps disbelief/criticism].

Wife of Mr. Pang Ŭngmo, City, Chukch'ŏm-chŏng 3-chōme

1. I haven't read much of anything. I haven't read works by ladies like Hŏ Nansŏrhŏn or Hwang Chini from books, though I have read one or two pieces. I haven't read any modern works either.
2. Since the children are still young, it's mostly just reading aloud things from the children's section (애기란) of the newspaper.
3. Among the novels in the Chosŏn language newspapers, there isn't one I read continuously (게속해). I read when I have spare time.
4. I don't go to movie viewings or music concerts, so I don't really know.

Wife of Mr. Kim Sŏngsu, City, Kyedong

1. None.
2. I don't particularly recommend them.
3. None. I'm too busy with the children and household chores (살님사리) to have time to read.
4. I don't really know.

Wife of Mr. Pak Hŭngsik, City, Kahoe-jŏng

Please allow me to postpone this for another opportunity; I cannot answer your esteemed questions this time. Please understand (양해하오소서).

Mrs. Cho Paekchu, wife of Mr. Yi Sŏnghwan, Myŏngnyun-chŏng 1-33-17

1. I have read works by Lady Hwang Chini, and I have also read works by Lady Pak Hwasŏng and Lady Kim Malbong.
2. I greatly encourage it.
3. I generally read the ones in the three newspapers.
4. I have seen them all once or twice. I think very highly of them and know they are our pride. <132>

Mrs. Kim Kongsun, wife of Mr. Yi Kŭngno, Hwadong 129-1

1. I have read works by Hŏ Nansŏrhŏn, Hwang Chini, etc., included in the *Anthology of Poems by Successive Generations of Chosŏn Female Writers* (歷代 朝鮮 女流 詩歌選, translated and annotated by Sin Kwi-hyŏn).
2. They are still in the lower grades of primary school (소학 저급), so they haven't reached the level where they can read them.
3. I have read *Segye ŭi Ttal* (Daughter of the Century) in the *Tonga Ilbo* and *Taeji ŭi Adŭl* (Son of the Earth) in the *Chosŏn Ilbo*.
4. I have not yet heard or seen Mun Yebong's movies or the vocal music (성악) of Im Sanghŭi and Pak Kyŏnghŭi. I saw Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's dance several years ago. As for Chŏng Hunmo's vocal music, I often heard it because we were classmates at P'yŏngyang Girls' High School (平壤 女高). Moreover, I still haven't forgotten (잇치지 않읍니다) her skillful voice when reading the textbook aloud. I express my respect for the hardships endured by all these ladies active in impoverished Chosŏn, hope they leave strong nourishment for our women's world, and wish they strive for the sake of future generations.

Mrs. Yi Chŏngŭn, wife of Mr. Yi Sangho, Anam-chŏng 175-21

1. I have never read works by female writers of the past. Among modern works, I read Pak Hwasŏng's newspaper serial novel, but I didn't find it greatly interesting.
2. They read before I recommend them.
3. I am reading "Sarang ŭi Sujokwan" (Aquarium of Love) and "Taeji ŭi Adŭl" (Son of the Earth) in the *Chosŏn Ilbo*.
4. I have seen or heard almost all of them. Miss Ch'oe's dance, which captures the Chosŏn color tone, always remains in my impression (남어 있을 뿐입니다).

Mrs. Ch'oe Sŏnbok, wife of Mr. Chu Yohan, Tangju-chŏng 72

1. I haven't been able to read much. I have roughly read the complete collection of women's literature (여류 문학 전집).
2. The children in my house all like reading books so much that I advise them not to read *only* books.
3. Nowadays (이즈음은), I read *Segye ŭi Ttal* (Daughter of the Century - the life of Madame Curie) serialized in the *Tonga Ilbo*.
4. I haven't even seen Miss Mun's movies yet. Although Miss Ch'oe's dance has become so world-renowned (알리워 젓것만), I haven't had the chance and haven't seen it even once yet, but I intend to definitely see it someday. I hear Miss Chŏng Hunmo's voice every day through records. I occasionally hear Miss Pak Kyŏnghŭi's voice via the radio. I definitely attended Im Sanghŭi's solo recital. When I listen to music, I wish that even if I couldn't (못 되였으나), my daughters could become musicians like them.

Wife (室人) of Mr. Ku Chaok, P'irun-chŏng 278-3

1. I haven't read anything.
2. I do not recommend; it is left to their freedom (자유에 막입니다).
3. I sometimes read what is published in the *Tonga Ilbo*.
4. I have not seen them. (Continued on page 128 bottom) <133>

Mrs. Sŏ Kyŏngi, wife of Mr. Pak Pyŏngnae, Chaya-chŏng 185

1. I have not read works by female writers with any particular thought.
2. Besides review, I have the child (兒孩) read things related to historical records (史記) or biographies of successful people (立志傳).
3. I have never read a newspaper novel continuously.
4. As I have never gone to dance performances or music concerts, I have no particular impression. <128>

**Summary**

This 1940 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine explores the engagement of wives of prominent Korean men ("Esteemed Wives' Circle") with literature and the performing arts, particularly works by Korean women. The responses reveal a wide spectrum: some actively read contemporary female authors (Pak Hwasŏng, Kim Malbong) and newspaper serials, encourage reading among children, and attend performances by figures like dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi or singer Pak Kyŏnghŭi, expressing pride in their achievements. Others, however, profess little time or interest due to household duties, lack of exposure, preference for other subjects (like science), or inability to read older works (like Hŏ Nansŏrhŏn's Hanmun poetry). Several mention reading Japanese authors due to children's weaker Korean skills.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the cultural lives and constraints of elite women in late colonial Korea. It highlights the tension between traditional domestic roles and emerging modern cultural consumption, the varying levels of engagement with both classical and contemporary Korean arts, the influence of Japanese culture ("mainland authors"), and the nascent pride in internationally recognized Korean artists like Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, even among those who hadn't personally seen her perform. The frequent mention of being too busy with "salim" (household management) underscores the enduring domestic responsibilities, while the differing attitudes towards literature for children reflect evolving views on education and culture within the elite stratum.

우리 사회, 諸名士의 영부인층은 어느 정도로 문학과 예술을 玩賞하고 게신가, 더구나 같은 여성들의 작품과 음악과 무용을 즐기고 평가하고 게신가, 그를 알기 위해서 이제 서울 사회의 몇몇 분에게 이하 4개조를 물었습니다.
