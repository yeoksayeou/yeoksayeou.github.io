**Article Type:** 대담·좌담 (Interview/Discussion)

**Kim Soyŏng, Full of Sorrowful Regrets, Returns from Tōkyō**

There's an old saying, "Beauty meets misfortune" (美人薄命 - miin pangmyŏng). It truly seems that beautiful women either meet misfortune or face many hardships. Isn't that the case when we look at our star, Kim Soyŏng? Possessing a beauty that could be boasted to the world, she holds tears and sorrows that others haven't experienced.

Seven o'clock in the evening, an evening perfect for a walk as dusk settled.
"When did you return from Tōkyō?"
Thinking that the feel of the pavement underfoot suggested spring, I asked Soyŏng this first question.
"It's been about two months, but I haven't left the house at all. I don't have the face to go out..."
Darkness prevented me from discerning her expression, but I could tell from her voice that she must surely have worn a sad face. We were silent for a while. Only the sound of our shoes continued on the pavement.

**Relationship with Mr. Ch'u**

"How did things turn out with Mr. Ch'u Chŏgyang? Regarding the family register, I mean."<152>
"It's still the same. It seems he heard news about me even while he was there. A letter arrived recently, saying he understood enough, so wouldn't it be better for both of us if I stopped doing such clumsy things and just proceeded with the divorce? And since he's constrained (부자유한 몸) and can't do anything himself, he told me to go to his house, explain, and handle the divorce procedures. But how could his family agree to that when he's not there? So, I went to Taegu Prison to visit him."
"What did you do when you went there?"
"When I went, he said the fault wasn't only mine, but his too. I guess he said that because he's facing several years in prison. When I went, I had prepared all the divorce papers. He affixed his seal to them and told me that since his father isn't there and his mother is the head of the household (호주), I should ask his mother to affix her seal."
"So, are the procedures all finished?"
"No, not yet. I can't go to that house, so I sent an intermediary, but his mother apparently said, 'What's the rush?' Also, my father is absolutely against it. He worries, saying, 'When you got married, you wouldn't listen to your parents' opposition, saying you'd die without him,<153> and now you say you can't live together and demand a seal? What kind of game is this?'"
"Do you absolutely have to get divorced?"
"Well, isn't it spilled water now? Whether I live with him or not..."
"Why should that be the case? If he fully understands?"
"Being human, could it really work out that way? Even if we were to live together again, I think it would be unpleasant. So, now that things have come to this, I think it might be better to just part ways. A close friend of his asked to meet the other day and told me that until he gets out, whether it's 3 or 4 years, I should sustain myself by working at the Tongyang Theater (東洋 劇場) or somewhere. But I said I didn't want to do that. I appreciate his friend's concern for me and for Ch'u, but..."
Our conversation on the street ended there, and Soyŏng and I sat facing each other in an extremely quiet place in Ponjŏng (本町). Under the bright lights, she seemed a bit thinner than before, but this only made her more beautiful. Truly, she was a woman lovely in every aspect. Even if covered head to toe with a cloth, one could likely sense the charm emanating from her. I wondered if this was why she, more than anyone, generated so many problems, and I asked the following question.

**With the Dancer Mr. Cho T'aegwŏn**

"What's the situation with Mr. Cho T'aegwŏn?"
"It's just like that."
"Just like that?"
"Out of mutual prudence, he is doing his work, and I am staying home, studying a bit and being prudent (근신합니다)."
"You haven't completely broken up, have you?"
"We're just waiting for a good opportunity."
"Doesn't he have a wife?"
"He said they divorced. He has two children."
"You'll be prudent for a while, and what will you do after that?"
"I'm just waiting for the time when my mind and body become completely sound, I become a new person, and can make many good films. Right now, I don't have any particular plan (풀랜)."
"So these days, are you just staying at home?"
"That's right. I've seen all the movies in Tōkyō, so there's nothing special to watch. I just stay home and read books."<154>

**Five Months of Tōkyō Life**

"How many months were you in Tōkyō?"
"I was there for five months."
"I heard you joined some film company?"
"Yes, I was briefly involved with Nankō Eigasha (南旺 映畵社), but I quit. Firstly, because I thought I needed to get my mind in order..."
"You must have enjoyed sightseeing in Tōkyō then?"
"Yes, I did. The novelist Mr. Hamamoto Hiroshi (濱本浩) had visited Seoul twice, and since I had guided him around then, he often took me around various places to sightsee. Still, I was always busy doing nothing, and looking back after returning, there were many places I didn't get to see. Mr. Ishii Baku (石井漠) strongly encouraged me to learn dance. But it's difficult if you don't start from childhood. I learned for a little while and then quit. He asked me to join him on a regional tour, but I didn't go."
"You're still young, why can't you? To be an actress, shouldn't you know how to do everything and be able to do everything? ... Anyway, did you meet many famous actors?"
"I saw them all. Though I only went to Shinkō (新興) and Shōchiku Ōfuna (松竹 大船)."
"Who did you meet?"
"Tanaka Kinuyo (田中絹代), Irie Takako (入江 たか子), Saburi Shin (佐分利信), I met them all, but they are very different from how they look on screen. Especially Tanaka Kinuyo, she's quite short and wrinkled like an old person (쪼록 쪼록해요). And I often spent time with Li Kōran (李香蘭), but she's completely different from the screen too. She's short, her face is quite thin and unattractive (숭한 게), covered in freckles (죽은깨), only her eyes are huge like marbles, but she appears so pretty on screen. I was quite disappointed. Still, Tanaka Kinuyo received the best treatment at Ōfuna. All the other actors shared rooms, two per room, but since it was summer, Kinuyo had the largest, coolest room with one apprentice. We took pictures together and had a lot of fun. She treated me kindly."
"So, weren't there any pretty actresses?"
"Mito Mitsuko (三戶光子) was pretty, not much different from the screen. I met Irie at a movie theater, and she seemed very elegant."
"Which male actors did you like?"
"Saburi Shin was good, and Uehara Ken (上原謙) was good too. I took pictures with all of them."
As she spoke of these things, her expression became much brighter than before.<155> However, the inherent sorrow (애수) she possessed did not disappear from her face. Even while laughing, she would quickly turn somber (새므륵해지며) and gaze out the window with her beautiful, limpid eyes. Somehow, I felt as though she carried a sad history from her mother's womb.

**How She Came to Know Mr. Ch'oe In'gyu**

"Since when did you know Mr. Ch'oe In'gyu?"
"Since before the filming of 'Border' (국경) began. It's all my fault that I caused such a public stir, but in the relationship between people, if the mind or body is weary and not enjoyable, I don't think it can be called a proper relationship. While associating with him, I was extremely weary. That man always manipulating people (사람을 늘 이리저리 말하는 게) is unbearable. While I knew him, he absolutely prevented me from appearing in any other movies. If someone asked me to appear in their film, he would immediately block them, preventing me from being in any other movies. So, while I knew him, I couldn't do anything else. When I was briefly with Kohyŏp (高協) and went on a regional tour, just as I was about to go on stage in Hoeryŏng (會寧), a telegram arrived. It said they were going to do the sound recording for 'Border' and I should return to Seoul immediately. And he probably came down to get me right away. I had no choice but to go back up with him, but even after returning, we waited 20 days before doing the recording."
"Doesn't he have a wife?"
"He does. Kim Sinjae (金信載), she's a truly good person. A very virtuous woman. When I think that because of me, that good person is living with heartache, my conscience bothers me so much I can't stand it even for a moment. Especially when her child laughs innocently, I feel like lightning might strike me from the sky. So I always urged him to live happily with Sinjae. But then, looking at the story about me published in *Chogwang* (朝光) Magazine, if that's not a femme fatale (요부), where would you find one? I'm not saying I did well. Of course, what I did wrong is unspeakable. But to pin all the blame on me, as if all the sin is mine, isn't that too pathetic? It's regrettable to speak this way about someone I spent even a short time with, but before Mr. Ch'oe In'gyu knew me, he was already living with some café woman, just providing living expenses for Sinjae. If Sinjae said anything, he would immediately jump on her, criticizing and pressuring (하박) her. What could she do but stay quiet? He wouldn't even meet that café woman once, though letters often came from her.<156> I thought he was a truly harsh-natured man, and how he treated me was the same. If he loved me even for a moment, would he try to harm me like that? Judging by what was published in *Chogwang* Magazine, he looks like a saint, and I'm made out to be an unspeakably wicked woman. But I too have human blood and skin; how could that be? Moreover, knowing how I suffered, missing our Naemi (奈美, daughter's name), he supposedly claims that if I go home, I pinch the child and make her life miserable? Even a tiger knows its own cub is precious; how could a human not know their own child is precious?"
As Soyŏng started talking about the child, tears welled up in her eyes.

**Naemi is in Namhan Sansŏng**

"Where is the child now?"
"She's at his house. In Namhan Sansŏng (南漢山城)... I had her with me until last spring, but they took her, saying they would enroll her in school."
The tears that had gathered streamed down her cheeks.
"You must miss her?"
Perhaps unable to find other words, I blurted this out. After saying it, I felt I might have said something I shouldn't have.
"When I see children her age on the street, my heart stops (가슴이 선득해저요). I just think, 'Our Nami must be about that tall now.' It seems the bond that can never be broken in this world is maternal love (모성애). Wherever I go, thoughts of my child always linger in my heart."
"Doesn't the child ask for her mother?"
"I don't know. No one comes or goes [to tell me]..."
She answered in a voice choked with tears. Then she gently (사르지) closed her eyes, seeming to trace the beloved image (面影) of her child with her eyes.
"How old is the child?"
"She's nine. If we were in Seoul, perhaps I could meet her... If I were to marry, I would marry someone with motherless young children, and with the same heart I think of our Nami, I would treat those children exceptionally well. That's how I would atone for my sins (내 죄를 속하겠어요)."
The night seemed to have deepened considerably, and the sound of footsteps on the street dwindled. Feeling it wouldn't do to make Soyŏng cry more, I told her I hoped she would think positively and live well in the future, and we went outside together.
<157>

**Summary**

This 1940 interview captures Korean actress Kim Soyŏng discussing her recent return from Tōkyō and the personal turmoil surrounding her relationships. She details the difficult process of divorcing her imprisoned husband, Ch'u Chŏgyang, her cautious relationship with dancer Cho T'aegwŏn, and her painful history with director Ch'oe In'gyu, whom she accuses of control and slander. She expresses deep sorrow over being separated from her daughter, Naemi, and reflects on her experiences meeting Japanese film stars in Tōkyō, contrasting their screen personas with reality. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the life of a prominent colonial-era Korean actress, navigating complex personal scandals, career pressures, and the dynamics between Korean and Japanese film worlds. It highlights the social constraints on women, particularly actresses, the personal cost of fame and complicated relationships, and provides firsthand observations on the Japanese film industry and stars like Li Kōran and Tanaka Kinuyo from a Korean perspective during that period.

雪恨이 만흔 金素英, 東京서 도라와서
녯날부터 美人薄命이란 말이 있읍니다. 정말 미인은 薄命하지 않으면 難이 많든지 한가 보아요, 우리의 스-타 金素英을 보드래도 그렇지 않습니까. 세계에 자랑해도 좋을 아름다움을 한 몸에 지닌 그에게는 남이 안 거진 눈물과 서름이 서리워 있담니다.

오후, 일곱시, 한창 것기 좋게 저무른 저녁이다.
「동경서 언제 나오섰지요?」
발 밑에 밟히는 舖道의 감촉이 제법 봄을 느끼게 한다고 생각하면서 나는 이렇게 첫 말을 素英에 물었다.
「한 두어달 됩니다 마는 통 집에서 나오지 않었어요, 나와 다닐 면목두 없구 해서...」
어둠이 가로 막은 탓으로 그의 표정을 살필 수 없었으나 그는 분명히 슬픈 얼굴을 지였으리라는 것은 그의 말소리로서 알 수 있었다. 우리는 한참 말이 없었다, 두리의 신소리만이 포도 우엘 게속될 뿐이었다.

秋氏와의 關係
「秋赤陽씨완 어떻게 되섰죠? 호적상 말슴입니다」＜152＞

길에서의 이야기는 여기까지 끝나고 素英과 나는 本町 어느 극히 조용한 장소에서 마조 앉었다. 환안이 밝은 불빛 아래의 그는 전보다 좀 야윈 듯 했으나, 그럴수록 그는 아름다웠다. 정말 어디 하나 빠진 데 없이 고흔 여성이었다. 머리에서 발 끝까지 보자기로 푹 뒤집어 씨워 놓고라도, 그에게서 새어 나오는 매력을 느낄 수 있을 듯 싶었다. 그가 누구보다 문제를 많이 발생시키는 것도 그 때문이 아닐까 나는 이런 생각을 하면서 다음과 같은 질문을 또 했다.

밤두 어지간히 어두워진 듯, 거리에 사람 발소리도 적어저 갔다, 나는 素英을 더 울리기도 안 되고 해서, 앞으로, 좋은 생각을 가지고, 좋게 살기를 바란다 말하고 함께 밖으로 나왔다.
