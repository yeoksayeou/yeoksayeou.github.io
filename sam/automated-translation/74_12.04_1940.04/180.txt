**Body Text Translation:**
文藝峰 等 當代 佳人이 모여 「紅淚·情怨」을 말하는 座談會
(A Roundtable Discussion where Contemporary Beauties like Mun Ye-bong Gather to Speak of "Crimson Tears and Resentful Love")

Attendees:
Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯), Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑), Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英), Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙), Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕)
Also present: Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求), Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙), Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周)
On the night of the first full moon of the lunar new year, in Changan, Seoul.

Introduction of the Five Attendees:

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯)
After playing the role of Ch'unhyang in Korea's first talkie film, "Ch'unhyang" (春香), she instantly rose to fame as a renowned actress. She subsequently appeared in various works such as "The Wanderer" (나그네) and "New Life" (새生活), monopolizing contemporary popularity along with her beauty. She is the Irie Takako (入江たか子) of Korea, the Dietrich (뎃드릿히) of Korea, the Ri Kōran (李香蘭) of Korea, and her fame has spread far overseas. Her hometown is Hamhŭng (咸興), Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道).

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑)
During her time at Yokohama (橫濱) Seminary, she organized the T'owŏrhoe (土月會) group with Mr. Yŏn Hak-nyŏn (延鶴年) and others, pioneering the path of New Drama (新劇) in this land. Her name became known through "Katusha" (갓추사), and through film, theater, and radio, she has become such a popular and immense presence that there is likely no one in Korea who doesn't know the names of Mt. Kŭmgang and Pok Hye-suk. She can play elderly roles, young girl roles, social portrayals (勢帶描寫)—anything is possible for her. Her hometown is Ch'ŏnan (天安), Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸道).

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙)
Famous for her acting and singing skills during her time at Ewha Girls' High School (梨花女高), she joined the Drama Arts Research Society (劇藝術硏究會) after graduation, acting and singing. For a while, she quit acting and went to Tokyo (東京) to study voice, but things didn't work out, and she returned to the theater world. Still unforgettable is when she played the heroine "Käthie" (케티) in the performance of "Heidelberg" (하이딜·베르히) at the Central Stage (中央舞台), moving the hearts of many.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英)
She made her name as a star of the Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa (靑春座) theater troupe for a long time alongside Mr. Hwang Ch'ŏl (黃撤) and Mr. Sŏ Wŏl-yŏng (徐月影). Her hometown is Seoul, and she graduated from the Women's Commercial School (女子商業). She married sometime ago and is playing the role of a gentle daughter-in-law in Changsŏng-dong (昌成町). When asked if she will act again, she shakes her head, saying she can't because she's afraid of her father-in-law.

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕)
After graduating from Kyŏngsŏng Women's Commercial School (京城女子商業), she went to Tokyo (東京) and spent a long time there, studying dramatic arts extensively. Upon returning to Korea (朝鮮), she was very active in the Sin Kŏnsŏl (新建設) troupe during its heyday. When that group disbanded, she was a suspect in the so-called Chŏnju Incident (全州事件) and suffered for several months at the Chŏnju (全州) police station. Afterwards, she worked at the Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場) for a living, but she is currently not affiliated anywhere. <135>

My Life of Many Affections, Many Sorrows (多情 多恨한 내 半生)

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) - (Representing the magazine) Tonight is the night of the first full moon. Outside the window, the bright snowy moon dimly illuminates the ten thousand houses of Changan (長安). On this quiet night, I am delighted to sit <134> together with all of you, tipping glasses of wine and listening to your words, likely filled with many affections and sorrows. Perhaps back in our hometowns, all the newlywed wives dressed in new clothes are out on the riverbanks tonight performing 'bridge treading' (다리봅기), or else drawing 'pure water' (정화수) and praying to the heavens for their 'life's dream' (인생의 꿈) to become beautiful. We, who have left our hometowns and gathered in the capital city of Seoul, let's think of our beloved hometowns, seek the classic fragrance of old times on this festive day, and talk quietly until the night ends, truly until dawn breaks.
By the way, I think it would be good if Mr. Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) could serve as the moderator this evening. When it's only women gathered, having a gentleman present somehow helps the discussion proceed. If it's just us, we tend to just laugh and not speak much...

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - They say when the road is neatly cleared for the royal inspector's procession (어사행차), some beggar passes by first. It's a bit embarrassing for a man like me to be the first one to speak at a gathering prepared for such beautiful young ladies... Well then, as requested, I will serve as the moderator and proceed with the conversation. Ms. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯), Ms. Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑), and all of <135> you gathered here, do you also have sorrows? People might think that the five of you gathered here—with your beautiful faces, talents, good writing, good dancing, stylishness—have no reason for lament or complaint from any angle. Even though you are contemporary beauties whom others envy, are there times when you shed tears? Are there times when you sigh, feeling frustrated because something isn't working out?
Let's hear the hidden "laments about your lot" (신세 타령) from deep within the hearts of you, who act as "Korea's sweethearts" for tens of thousands of fans through film, stage, and music troupes.
It doesn't quite fit your stature to ask you for "laments about your lot," but hold on, how should I phrase this question? Well then, I'd like to ask a few things in order. First, let's hear about your past experiences. Please tell us a story (一場 說話) about how you came to Seoul and ended up gathering here tonight.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - Oh dear, this is trouble. Asking a young lady like me to tell her life's story? What should I do?

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - Exactly. This is serious. We've really been caught by Samch'ŏlli Magazine tonight.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Alright, let's start with the youngest person first. Who might be the youngest here?

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) - I'm not sure exactly, but from what I see, it seems to be Ms. Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕).

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - But please, don't ask my age. Age is truly our greatest secret, and it even affects our professional interests.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Yes, I'm well aware of that fact. Then, let's have Ms. Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) speak first.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Others know my past better than I do. (Laughs) My hometown is Poryŏng (寶寧), South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸南道), and my father was a pastor. So, I spent my girlhood as a pastor's daughter, attending church diligently and school diligently. My grades in elementary school back then weren't particularly outstanding, but I showed considerable talent in composition and drawing (圖畵) and received praise. Even then, I loved reading novels, and when I graduated elementary school, I was crazy about "Neptune" (海王星), translated by Hamong Yi Sang-hyŏp (何夢 李相協), reading it day and night. Later, at the age of fifteen, I went to Tokyo (東京) and studied at an arts school for 2-3 years. One time, visiting the Imperial Theater (帝國劇場) sealed my fate for today. Of course (母論), during my elementary school days, I never dreamed of becoming an actress; the highest aspiration I had back then was perhaps to become an elementary school teacher. Another dream I had, growing up in a pastor's <136> family, was to study abroad in America (米國). But after going to Tokyo, my aspirations changed. I developed two hopes: to become either a newspaper reporter or an actress.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Haha! So, you've achieved your dream of becoming an actress, but you haven't had a chance to be a newspaper reporter yet. Maybe you should try working as a reporter now.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - It's absolutely too late now! Anyway, at that time, my father came chasing after me to Tokyo, caught me in Asakusa (아사구사), and brought me back to Kyŏngsŏng (京城). Also, when I was in Tokyo, the dormitory supervisor asked me what I wanted to become in the future, and I answered that I wanted to be an actress. Later, when I was performing in Kyŏngsŏng, that same supervisor, who was returning from some business in Dairen (大連), saw my poster on the streets of Kyŏngsŏng, found me, and said, "You succeeded according to your wish." So, I guess you could say I became an actress because I wanted to.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Next, please let Ms. Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) speak.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - My hometown is Sinch'ŏn (信川), Hwanghae Province (黃海道), and my real name is Kim Chin-sun (金鎭順). I also attended Sunday school since elementary school, so whenever we performed Nativity plays (聖劇) at church during Christmas, I always participated and received much praise. After graduating elementary school, I commuted to Holston Girls' High School (好壽敦女子高等普通學校) in Kaesŏng (開城) for two years, then transferred to Ewha Girls' High School (梨花高女) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城). Of course, this life as an actress wasn't my original dream. My dream at that time was—my grades weren't bad compared to others, and I especially had a talent for music—to aim for a future in music. So, from my third year at Ewha Girls' High School, I even took private lessons. And I was so crazy about music <137> that even though it was a mission school and I was supposed to go to church on Sundays, I often skipped it to sneak into the music room and play the piano, getting caught and scolded by the teacher more than once. (She sighs faintly) But even the music I longed for so much, circumstances didn't allow it, and my dream of going to Tokyo after graduating from Ewha Girls' High School was shattered. Through the arrangement of Mr. Ham Tŏk-hun (咸德勳) and the introduction by his older brother, Mr. Ham Tae-hun (咸大勳), I joined the Kŭgyŏn-jwa (劇硏座 - Drama Research Troupe). I played elderly roles then, and later worked at the Ch'unch'u Theater (春秋劇場) for 2-3 months. I finally showed my acting ability playing the lead role in a play adapted by Mr. Yi Un-bang (李雲芳) called "Oppeodihil" (오뻐디힐 -?). Even then, I hadn't given up hope for music, and I entered the theater world because I heard it was the fastest way to get songs recorded (吹入) on records. Later, I achieved my wish and went to Tokyo, where I attended a higher music school (高等音樂學校). Mr. Hong Nan-p'a (洪蘭坡) helped me a lot then. But when I returned home during the summer vacation the following year, I couldn't go back to Tokyo. After that, I worked for a year with the Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa (靑春座), which was affiliated with the Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場). Then, at the Central Stage (中央舞台), which split off from Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa, I finally performed many plays that suited my ideals. My counterparts were mostly Mr. Sim Yŏng (沈影) and Mr. Sŏ Wŏl-yŏng (徐月影).

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - The person who played the lead role of "Käthie" (케티) in "Heidelberg" (하이델 베리히) was none other than Ms. Namgung Sŏn.

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - Oh, really? I saw it back then without knowing who it was. "Heidelberg" was really good.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Why, she also played the role of "Camille" (椿姬) in "La Dame aux Camélias" (椿姬), didn't she? Next, please let Ms. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) speak.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - I don't have much to say. My hometown was Hamhŭng (咸興), and since my father originally dedicated himself to the dramatic arts, I naturally followed him into this path. I started appearing on stage at the age of thirteen, and initially, my Hamhŭng accent caused quite some difficulty. It wasn't that I was unwillingly dragged into it just because my father worked in theater (劇), nor did I enter due to any threat to my livelihood; I actually did it because I loved this path of art. It's still the same now.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Indeed, among those present here, perhaps no one has walked the path of an actor as smoothly as Ms. Mun Ye-bong. In the realm of theater and film art, Ms. Mun can be called a fortunate person from a lineage of performers (各門의 출신자). Her father was understanding, and her current husband, Mr. Im Sŏn-gyu (林仙圭), is a playwright, so he is also understanding. <138> It's truly been a smooth road for her. Next, please let Ms. Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) speak.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - My hometown is Chŏngju (定州), North P'yŏngan Province (平北). I graduated from Kyŏngsŏng Women's Commercial School (京城女子商業學校). Unable to study further, I stayed cooped up (배켜서) at home out of anger (화낌에) and devoured the four volumes of "Ongnumong" (玉淚夢) in 10 days. I really enjoyed reading it then. My debut was as a young lady (令孃) in a play called "The Person Climbing the Hill" (언덕을 오르는 사람) performed at the Cheil Theater (第一劇場), involving Mr. Na Un-gyu (羅雲奎) and Mr. Yi Paek-su (李白水). There was a scene where Mr. Sim Yŏng (沈影) did a "katagoi" (가다고이 - ?). Also, having just graduated from girls' school, I had no experience wearing high heels (하이힐). While coming down the hill built on stage wearing high heels, I fell. Oh, it was extremely embarrassing (챙피 막심하고), and my appearance was so disgraceful (망칙하게) that I couldn't even stand up; I just crawled backstage. (Everyone laughs) Oh, I was so ashamed then. Anyway, my first performance was a failure. After that, I lived as an actress in the Ch'ŏngch'un-jwa (靑春座) troupe affiliated with the Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場). I got married last year and haven't been on stage much since.

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - You haven't been on stage at all since getting married?

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - Last fall, during the performance of a play called "Sacrifice" (犧牲) at the Tongyang Theater, I appeared just once. But my husband's family (偲家) strongly opposed it, so at night, I lied saying I was going to visit my parents' home, then rushed to the Tongyang Theater to perform on stage. It might have been okay for a day or two, but it continued for 5 days, and lying every day about going to my parents' home, how could it not be discovered? Also, among the audience members I knew, some spotted me on stage, so word inevitably got out. So, my husband's family found out, and opposition rained down like hail (빗발치듯); I really had a hard time. It was a situation where I had to perform, but my father-in-law, being rather strict, practically jumped up and down, saying, "How can you go out to such a place while having a father-in-law and call other men (딴 사내들) father? Where in the world is such a law? It's outrageous (해괴망칙한 일)!" I was truly in a difficult situation then. Since then, I haven't been on stage.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - While performing "Sacrifice" (犧牲), you almost became a real sacrifice (희생) at your husband's home (偲家), didn't you? (Everyone laughs)

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) - Do you have any desire to return to the stage?

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - Why not? I really want to go on stage just once more and perform a play that suits my heart.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Next, let's hear from Ms. Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕).

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - I really have nothing to say. My hometown is Ch'ŏnan (天安), South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠南), and I also graduated from Kyŏngsŏng Women's Commercial School (京城女子商業學校). And <139> my real name is Yi Kwi-rae (李貴來). After that, I went to Tokyo (東京) and was involved with the Proletarian Theater (푸로극장) for 4-5 years. After returning to Kyŏngsŏng (京城), I starred in the play "All Quiet on the Western Front" (西部 戰線에 異狀없다) with the Sin Kŏnsŏl (新建設) theater troupe.

First Stage Appearance and Age at the Time (처음 나선 舞臺와 그때의 年齡)

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - I'd appreciate it if you could tell us your age when you first appeared on stage, and which stage it was.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - I started appearing on stage at thirteen, but back then I played children's roles (少兒役). I first played a leading role at age 15, and it was a male role. The stage was likely the Suwon Theater (水原劇場).

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - I first appeared on stage at age twenty, and the stage was the Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場).

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - I first appeared on stage at age 17, and the stage at that time was the Kyŏngsŏng Cheil Theater (京城 第一劇場).

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - I first performed in a full-scale production at age 20, and the stage was the Kyŏngsŏng Theater (京城劇場).

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - Next, Ms. Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑), please speak.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Oh dear! It's so long ago, I can't remember at what age I debuted on stage or which theater it was. The first troupe I was involved with was T'owŏrhoe (土月會), and I think I first appeared on stage around age 17 or 18.

Romance During Travels (旅行 中의 로맨스)

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Now, please tell us about romances encountered during your travels.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - What romance could there be during travels?

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Oh, don't be like that. Please tell us about any sweet romances, touching moments, or unfortunate incidents (봉변) you experienced during your travels. Anything is fine.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - If it's about an unfortunate incident, I can share one story. It was when the Minjung Theater Troupe (民衆劇團), associated with Mr. Yun Paek-nam (尹白南), was touring the provinces. We went to Masan (馬山) and performed, but expenses exceeded income, and we ended up in debt. We couldn't even pay the inn's meal expenses, and a war unfolded under the innkeeper's loud scolding. Being debtors, we couldn't assert ourselves. Helplessly, my theater friends pawned me (전당 잡고) at the inn, saying they would go to Kyŏngsŏng (京城), send money, and then disappeared one by one, running away (달아뀀¬구려 - 달아났구려). A day passed, a week passed, <140> half a month passed, a month passed, but no money arrived. Confined in the inn room, burning with frustration, I couldn't bear it. I sent letters and telegrams worth a staggering 70 or 80 won. Still, for some reason, forget money, not even a single reply came; it was as if there was absolutely no news, like the spot where a pheasant was roasted and eaten (꿩구어 먹은 자리). How could my insides not burn? If the money didn't come, back then they even sold people into bars or brothels, so I grew even more anxious. The innkeeper, worried that I, the collateral, might run away, practically locked me in the room. What could I do? Two months passed with no news. Feeling so frustrated and fiery, and wanting to see the outside world, at night while others slept, I would quietly slip out. Without even putting on shoes—fearing I'd be caught if I did—I wandered barefoot through the streets and alleys of Masan until dawn. Because of this, a rumor spread in Masan about a madwoman wandering at night. One night, I was caught and dragged back by the owner, who tied me up. Feeling even more frustrated and resentful, I screamed and screamed. Then, saying I was truly possessed, they called a shaman, performed an exorcism (굿), cooked coarse millet rice, smeared it all over my face and body (몸슗� - 몸뚱이에?), and beat me with a peach wood stick (복숭아 몽치), claiming it would drive out the spirit. Was there ever such an absurd situation? So, I screamed and yelled even louder. They thought the spirit hadn't left yet and beat me more. Realizing screaming would only get me beaten more, I eventually stayed quiet. Then the shaman, who had been beating me with the peach stick, said the spirit was now gone and stopped hitting me. Oh, I was truly dumbfounded (기가 맥혀서)... After three months, I was fortunately released from Masan. (Everyone laughs)

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - When I was 16, traveling with a theater troupe, a certain man, a friend of my father aged around 38 or 39, acted strangely kind towards me. At first, I just thought he was treating me kindly like a niece because he was my father's friend. But even to my young mind, the way he treated me seemed too strange and persistently bothersome (추군 추군하기에), so from then on, I kept my guard up when dealing with him. Since he was a close friend of my father and there were mutual interests involved, I couldn't say anything. But eventually, he spread rumors in public (항간에) that he was even engaged to me. I felt so wronged, frustrated, and angry I couldn't stand it. Still, we endured it quietly. Truly, strange things happen in the world.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - When I was in Seoul, I continuously received love letters (戀書) from a certain technical college student, but I never replied once. At that time, I already had someone I loved, <141> so I never responded. Then one time, he insisted on meeting me at a certain restaurant. He pleaded so earnestly that I went. He genuinely begged, saying he would grant all my wishes, provide financial stability, and if I would just get engaged to him first, he would wait to marry until I wanted, for the sake of my stage career. He pleaded (懇願), but since I already had someone I loved, I refused, saying I couldn't accept. Ah, but after that, he started spreading malicious rumors, claiming things like "Namgung Sŏn and I are lovers," or "We're engaged." I was misunderstood by my lover and faced an unspeakably difficult situation.
And this is a truly funny, nonsensical episode: another time, I kept receiving earnest love letters from someone. Then once, he even sent a letter written in blood (血書). At first, I was startled seeing the blood letter, but upon closer inspection, it wasn't written with human blood, but with bedbug blood. Hahahaha, oh my, I almost died laughing. (Everyone laughs)

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Goodness, how many bedbugs would you have to catch to get enough blood to write a letter? He must have traded in a huge number of bedbugs then. (Everyone laughs)

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - How could the entire letter be written in blood? Only the crucial climax part was written in blood, clearly done by dipping a matchstick in bedbug blood. No matter how I examined it, it wasn't human blood; it was definitely bedbug blood. (Everyone laughs)

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Even if the blood was from bedbugs, a blood letter is still undoubtedly a blood letter. Ms. Namgung Sŏn, you are quite fortunate indeed. You even received a blood letter, which others don't get... (Everyone laughs)

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - Hey! Don't tease me (히야까지 마세요).

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - They say receiving a blood letter actually makes one lose affection.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - Well, I don't know if receiving a blood letter makes affection that was starting to form disappear, but such people are rather untrustworthy. It's like the passion of a small earthenware bowl (쟁개비) that boils up quickly and then cools down...

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - That's really true. It's hard to believe such a person's love would last long. I've seen a blood letter too, and it was gruesome!

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - So, Ms. Sŏn-yŏng, you've received a blood letter too?

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - No. Not one I received, but I saw one sent to Mr. Kim Tong-gyu (金東圭) at the Tongyang Theater...

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Oh, I thought you meant one you received. (Everyone laughs)

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - That one was really human blood; it was an application letter asking to be made an actor. Wow. Seeing that blood letter made my heart pound. <142>

Places Kind and Unkind (親切한 곳·不親切한 곳)

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - When you tour the provinces, which region is the kindest, and where is the coldest?

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - In the old days, everywhere was kind. When we went to the provinces, the welcome was considerable. Once, when we went to Taegu (大邱) during the time of the Sidae Ilbo (時代日報) newspaper, they even set off welcoming fireworks at the train station; it was quite a commotion. Nowadays, forget welcoming fireworks when going to the provinces; you rarely even see a welcoming party. Then and now, the people from Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道) are intimidating. I had the hardest time in Ch'ŏngjin (淸津). Once, we were performing "Bird in a Cage" (籠中島) in Ch'ŏngjin. Because the stage preparations took time, the intermissions got a bit long. During every intermission, they demanded I come out and dance (딴스). How could I dance Western dances wearing a traditional hairpin (비녀) and tail skirt (꼬리치마)? This was truly a situation to die or faint from absurdity. Lamenting that there was nothing to be done about low-level people, the applause urging me to come out and dance echoed through the hall like waves. Even the director, Mr. Yi Kyŏng-hwan (李京煥), felt sorry for me and told me to hurry out and dance. Seeing even the director lacking understanding, I was so dumbfounded that I confronted him, "Hey! Do you have your senses or not? How can I go out and dance a Western dance in this costume?" Then, in a fit of anger, I stormed onto the stage and yelled, "Everyone, do you have your senses or not? How can I dance some Western dance in this makeup and costume I need for the next act? There's a limit to ignorance! What kind of god-forsaken dance (딴스) is it with a hair bun and a tail skirt?" After the play ended, a swarm of people rushed forward shouting, "Catch that wench Pok Hye-suk!" I ran away to the inn without even putting on my shoes; I nearly soiled myself. (Everyone laughs) Oh, and another time, we went to perform in Yongjŏng (龍井), Kando (間島). People heckled (야질) like a shower of hailstones, shouting, "Hey, you scoundrels! Stop doing romance plays and do social dramas!"—this was during the heyday of Socialism (礊�會主義 - 사회주의)—When the play ended, students rushed in masses, demanding to meet Ms. Pok Hye-suk. Thinking I was going to get beaten again, I ran barefoot back to the inn. A crowd of students followed me to the inn and filled the yard (뜰악); seemed like several hundred people. They kept insisting, "Just let us greet her, bring out Pok Hye-suk!" The director had no choice but to tell me to go out. <143> "Ah, whatever! Let's just go out, prepared to be beaten to death," I thought. Opening the door and stepping out, seeing the yard full of students, my heart truly pounded. But I boldly asked, "What is it?" One student stepped forward and asked, "Why are you hiding when we want to greet you?" I replied, "How can I greet so many students one by one?" Then he said, "It's not about greeting one by one, but one representative greeting you," and extended his hand for a handshake. So, I very boldly extended my hand too and shook it. Then they said, "That's enough," and all left. Only then did I let out a sigh of relief and wipe the sweat from my brow. Although they had criticized us for doing romance plays, they had come to offer a moving greeting to fellow countrymen who had traveled far to a foreign land, and I hadn't realized, getting so scared. Oh, why were there so many representatives back then! Frequently (쩍하면), they made a fuss, claiming to be this representative or that representative.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - Among the places I've toured, Pusan (釜山) was the kindest. There's a specific inn where we always stay when we go to Pusan, and the old man who owns it is truly kind. Whether we arrive early in the morning off the train or late on a cold night, he always says, "Ah, our young ladies have come again. Hurry, come into the lower room," and makes the room very warm, brings seaweed soup before the rice is even ready because he worries we might be cold; he makes quite a fuss. He looked exactly like the French actor Harry Baur (아리볼), so we called him Old Man Aribol.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - Haeju (海州), Sinch'ŏn (信川), Taegu (大邱), Hamhŭng (咸興) were all kind. I had a hard time once in Chinju (晋州) with Mr. Wang P'yŏng (王平). We were performing a play called "Rationalization of Love" (사랑의 合理化) and were all arrested by the police, suffering quite a bit.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - (Taking out a mirror from her handbag and applying makeup) When I left home, I thought I must be the prettiest, but looking in the mirror now, I guess I'm the least attractive. (Laughs)

Protest Against Street Gossip (巷間의 「꼬싶」에 對한 抗議)

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - This time, please offer your protests, explanations, or the truth regarding the gossip (꼬싶) circulating about you all in the world.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - I don't have any material (껀데기) to be gossiped about these days. <144>

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - There was much talk that after Ms. Pok Hye-suk moved out of Insajŏng (仁寺町 - Insadong), the alley became very quiet. And after you moved to Ch'ŏngjinjŏng (淸進町 - Cheongjin-dong), didn't the Ch'ŏngjinjŏng neighborhood association issue an expulsion order because it became too noisy and busy? (Everyone laughs)

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Yes, they said my departure from Insajŏng was both relieving and regrettable, and I received quite a lot of gifts at the end of last year. (Laughs)

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - It seems I'm being gossiped about lately like this: a certain film company (某 映畵社) was going to film a movie called "Portrait of a Virgin" (處女圖), and a certain director asked me to play the lead role. When he first spoke to me, he made it sound not too difficult, said it was suitable for me, and seemed easy. But later, when I read the script describing the content of "Portrait of a Virgin," it was incredibly difficult. And since it was something I absolutely couldn't take on, I declined (謝絶), saying I couldn't do it. It seems there's much criticism now that I'm being arrogant (건방지다고). Actually, I didn't refuse out of pride (뽐내느라고); it was truly something too difficult for me to handle, and therefore, my acting wouldn't be able to bring the film to life as a film. So, I declined out of humility for the sake of the film itself, but they misunderstood and are criticizing me, saying Mun Ye-bong is very arrogant (뽐내고) and extremely presumptuous (건방지게) now. It's truly unfair.

Novels That Impressed Me (내가 感銘받은 小說)

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - Among the novels you've read, please each mention the one that impressed you the most.

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - I was deeply impressed reading Mr. Yi Ki-yŏng's (李箕永) "Hometown" (故鄕).

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Speaking of "Hometown" (故鄕), we thoroughly rehearsed an adaptation of "Hometown" for performance but couldn't stage it. Also, because its setting is Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸道), our homeland, it left a deep impression.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - I was deeply impressed reading Mr. Yi T'ae-jun's (李泰俊) "Second Fate" (第二의 運命), especially the male protagonist remains vividly in my memory. And recently, I read Mr. Yi Kwang-su's (李光洙) "Love" (사랑) with great emotion.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - Recently, the novel that impressed me most was Mr. Yi Kwang-su's (李光洙) "Ignorance" (無明).

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - I don't particularly read many Korean (朝鮮) works. Perhaps, among those I've read recently, a good one was Mr. Pang In-gŭn's (方仁根) <145> "Returning Home" (歸鄕). "Returning Home" was truly good. By the way, there's a work by Mr. Tanizaki Jun'ichirō (谷崎潤一郞) with content like this: there is a great painter renowned worldwide. He commits a grave crime and is sent to prison. As he goes to prison, he says, "My art goes to prison with me." How should this be interpreted? Should society bury his art along with his crime? Or should his crime be treated as crime, and his art still be recognized as art?

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - That interpretation would differ for each individual.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - Of course, crime is crime, and art is art.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Lately, I haven't finished reading any novels, but I am reading one. It's Mr. Yi Ki-yŏng's (李箕永) "Son of the Earth" (大地의 아들), currently serialized in the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報).

Happiness Wished For Beyond This (이 우에 더 바라는 幸福)

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) - Do you consider your current life as actresses to be happy?

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - Yes, I think it's happy.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - I don't think it's unhappy. But I'm not satisfied. Because I have to act due to the threat to my livelihood. When I first entered the theater troupe, it was for bread, and even now, I act for bread; this isn't just me, it's the case for most.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - If you act for bread, isn't that instrumentalizing (도구화) art?

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - That's theory; in reality, the majority act to eat. There's no need to particularly rebuke them for instrumentalizing art when society has made it that way.

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - If your current life as an actress is happy, is there no further happiness you wish for?

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - There is. It's the hope that the film works I appear in get better—yesterday's better than the day before's, today's better than yesterday's, and tomorrow's better than today's. I wish to hear reviews saying my acting is constantly progressing and improving like this. Other than that, I don't wish for any other happiness.

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - That must be the true confession of an artist.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - I too wish for my acting to improve. <146>

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - Of course, that's a common hope everyone desires. And if it happens, it couldn't be anything but happiness.

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - Do you ever sacrifice your body for your art? For example, when touring the provinces, incurring debt and seeing many people suffer, do you sacrifice yourself—to put it bluntly, even sell your chastity?

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - Why wouldn't that happen? Such things occurred quite often before. And women pawned at inns, if the money wasn't sent, often ended up selling their bodies in cafes or bars.

Admired Families in Changan (長安에서 부러운 家庭)

Yi Sŏ-gu (李瑞求) - Among the families of famous people in Changan (Seoul), whose families do you admire?

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - Well, I need to know the families of famous people first.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - Same here.

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - I also don't know many families of famous people well. Among friends' families, I find the family of Ms. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯), who is here, quite nice.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - For me too, it's not necessarily admiration, but Mr. An Ki-yŏng's (安基永) family.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - I find Mr. Yu Ch'i-jin's (柳致眞) family the most delightful.

Thoughts on the Body Aging (肉體가 늙어가는데 對한 感懷)

Pak Kye-ju (朴啓周) - Lastly, do you feel a sad sentiment, like youth passing away? The sorrow and bitterness of a blooming flower fading, perhaps melancholy, regarding autumn coming to the body...

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - Somehow, even though I age, I always feel young at heart.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - I resent getting older and older. I wish I could always stay young.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - I wish to get old quickly. I want to have my 60th birthday celebration (환갑 잔치) soon, and also, when I'm old, I can play elderly roles without makeup. Wouldn't that be great, saving on makeup costs? (Everyone laughs)

Kim Sŏn-yŏng (金鮮英) - Since I'm under my mother-in-law's supervision (슬하에), I can't command the maid freely, so I wish I could get old quickly and wield authority as I please. And I want to be called 'Madam' (마나님) by the maids. (Laughs)

Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi (崔貞熙) - As the body ages (Continued on page 170) <147> <134-147> doesn't something fade?
Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - I worry my husband's heart might change.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - I too only worry about whether my husband's heart might change.

Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯) - I haven't had such worries yet.

Nam Hae-yŏn (南海燕) - I just wish I didn't have any worries at all; that would be best.

Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) - A woman's youth seems too short.

Namgung Sŏn (南宮仙) - That's right. Once you're past 30, it's already over.

**Summary**

This 1940 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine features a roundtable discussion with five prominent Korean actresses of the colonial era: Mun Ye-bong, Pok Hye-suk, Kim Sŏn-yŏng, Namgung Sŏn, and Nam Hae-yŏn, moderated by Yi Sŏ-gu and Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi. They discuss their entry into acting, memorable experiences (including humorous and difficult ones) during provincial tours, encounters with fans and critics, thoughts on specific novels and plays, views on happiness, aging, and the pressures of their profession. Topics range from early career struggles, dealing with public gossip and rumors (including unwanted advances and false engagement claims), financial hardships faced by traveling troupes (leading Pok Hye-suk to be 'pawned' at an inn), encounters with police and political scrutiny (like Nam Hae-yŏn's Chŏnju incident or Mun Ye-bong's trouble in Chinju), balancing career with family expectations (Kim Sŏn-yŏng's difficulties with her in-laws), and reflections on artistic integrity versus economic necessity. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the lives and working conditions of popular entertainers in late colonial Korea, reflecting societal attitudes, the intersection of art and politics (mentions of socialism, police intervention), the challenges faced by women in public life, and cultural consumption (references to specific films, plays, novels, and even Japanese/Western figures like Irie Takako, Dietrich, Ri Kōran, and Tanizaki). The discussion about economic hardship forcing actresses into difficult situations, and the mention of proletarian theater and incidents involving police, subtly highlight the socio-political context of the time.

出席五氏 紹介
文藝峯
조선서 처음 나온 토-기 영화 「春香」에 춘향의 역을 맡어 일약 名優의 이름을 날린 뒤 계속하여 「나그네」 「새生活」 등 여러 작품에 나와, 그 미모와 아울러 당대인기를 독점하고 있다. 조선의 入江たか子요, 조선의 뎃드릿히요, 조선의 李香蘭이로 그 명성은 멀리 해외에까지 흐르고있다. 고향은 咸鏡道 咸興.

卜惠淑
橫濱神學校 시대에 延鶴年씨들과 함께 「土月會」를 조직하여 이 땅에 처음으로 新劇의 길을 연 뒤 「갓추사」로 그의 이름이 날리나, 영화로 연극으로 라디오로 「조선에 금강산과 卜惠淑의 이름을 모르는 이가 없으리만치 표푸라한 巨然한 존재가 되어 있다. 그 役도 老役도 可, 소녀도 可, 勢帶描寫도 可也, 만사가 다 可也다. 고향은 忠淸道 天安.

南宮仙
梨花女高 시절에 연극 잘 하고 노래 잘 하기로 유명하더니 학교를 나오자 이어 劇藝術硏究會에 가입하여 연극을 하고 노래를 부르고 했다. 한동안은 연극도 그만 두고 성악공부를 東京가서 하고 있었으나 여의치 못하여 다시 劇界에 발을 디디게 되었다. 아직 기억에 사라 안지는 것은 중앙무대에서 「하이딜·베르히」를 공연할 때 女主公 「케티」로 분장하여 많은 사람의 가슴 울리 때었다.

金鮮英
黃撤, 徐月影씨 등과 오랫동안 靑春座의 스타-로 이름을 날렸다. 고향은 서울, 학교는 女子商業을 나왔다. 얼마전에 결혼하여 昌成町에서 얌전한 며느리 노릇하는데 인젠 연극을 안하겠느냐고 하면 시아버지가 무서워서 못한다고 고개를 살래살래 내젔는다.

南海燕
京城女子商業을 나온뒤 東京 건너가서 오랫동안 있으면서도 劇藝術에 많은 연구를 하고 있다가 朝鮮돌아와, 한때 전성시대이던 新建設에서 많은 활약을 하다가 그 단체가 없어지든 때 소위 全州사건이라 하는 사건에 험의자로 全州 경찰서에서 여러 달을 고생하든 이로, 그 뒤 생활을 위해서 東洋劇場에도 있은 일이 있으나 지금은 아무데도 소속해 있지 않다.＜135＞

多情 多恨한 내 半生
崔貞熙 - (본사측) 오늘은 정월 대보름날 밤, 창 밖에는 둥두렷한 雪月이 長安 만호를 은은히 비최어 줍니다. 이 고요한 밤, 여러분과 자리를＜134＞ 가치하여 포도주 잔을 기울면서 여러분의 多情 多恨하실 그 말씀을 듣게 되니 기쁘외다. 아마 우리들 시골서는 모두 다 새옷 갈아 입은 각씨들이 이날 밤, 강변에 나가 「다리봅기」를 하든지, 그렇지 않으면 「정화수」를 떠 놓고 「인생의 꿈」이 아름다워지기를 하늘에 빌고 있을 것입니다. 고향을 버리고 서울 장안에 모으게 된 우리들은 그리운 고향도 생각하며 옛날의 고전적 향기도 이 명절날에서 찾으면서 이 밤이 다 새도록, 정말 다 새도록 고요히 이야기하며 밝힙시다.
그런데 오늘 저녁 사회는 李瑞求씨가 맡아하여 주섰으면 좋겠어요, 여성끼리만 모인 자리에선 암만해도 사내 어른들이 있어야 의사가 진행되더군요, 우리끼리만이면 자꾸 웃기만 하고 말씀덜 잘 안해주시니깐...
李瑞求 - 어사행차한다고 길을 말숙하게 닦아 놓으니 웬 거지가 먼저 지나가더라고, 이렇게 어여쁜 아가씨들 오게 한 자리가 작만되자 나같은 사내가 처음 나오게 되니 좀 부끄럽구먼요, ...그러면 말씀대로 사회자되어 말슴을 진행키로 하지요. 文藝峯씨나 卜惠淑씨나 모두＜135＞ 여기 앉은 여러분에게도 슬픔이 있을까요. 세상 사람들은, 여기 모인 다섯분은 얼굴 잘났것다, 재주 있것다, 글 잘하고 춤 잘추것다, 멋들어지것다, 어느 모로 보든지 한탄이나 불평이 없을 줄로 알 것인데 그렇게 남들이 부러워 하는 당대 가인이 되시고도 혹시 눈물 짓는 때가 있어요? 한숨을 쉬고, 무엔가 되지 않어서 애달버 하는 때가 있을가요.
여러 1만명의 팬을 가지고서 영화로 무대로, 음악단으로 「조선의 애인」 노릇을 하는 여러분의 그 어디, 가슴 속 감추어 둔 「신세 타령」을 듣기로 합시다.
여러분더러 「신세 타령」이란 도무지 격에 맞지 않는 말이외다마는, 가만 있자 이 말씀을 어떻게 물을까, 그러면 차레 차레 몇 가지 물을려 하는데 위선 여러분의 지내 온 경로부터 듣기로 합시다. 서울 와서 오늘 저녁 이 자리에 모이기까지 어디 一場 說話를 좀 말씀하여 주서요.
南宮仙 - 아이, 큰일 났네. 새파란 각씨보고 팔자 타령을 하라니 이를 어떻게 해요?
文藝峯 - 글쎄 말이우. 큰일 났는데요. 오늘 저녁은 三千里社에 단단히 부뜰렸군.
李瑞求 - 자, 우선 나이 제일 어린 분부터 시작합시다. 예서 제일 나이 어린이가 누구실까?
崔貞熙 - 아마 자세히는 몰라도 제 보기엔 南海燕씨인 것 같군요.
南宮仙 - 그런데 나은 제발 묻지 말아 주세요. 연령은 실로 우리에게 있어서 최대의 비밀이오 직업에도 그 이해관계가 미치니깐요.
李瑞求 - 네, 그 사실은 미리 미리 알고 있습니다. 그럼 어디 卜惠淑씨부터 이야기 하십시오.
卜惠淑 - 내 과거는 나보다 남이 더 잘 아니까요. (笑) 제 고향은 忠淸南道 寶寧이며, 제 부친은 목사였습니다. 그래서 저는 소녀 시절을 목사의 딸로 교회에도 열심히 다녔고, 학교에도 열심히 다녔습니다. 그 때 소학교 시절의 학과 성적은 그리 훌륭하다고 할 것은 없었으나 작문과 圖畵에는 상당한 소질을 가져 칭찬을 받았었어요. 그때부터도 소설을 읽기 좋와 해서 소학교를 졸업하던 때 何夢 李相協씨의 번역인 「海王星」에 미쳐서 그야말로 밤을 낮으로 열독했죠. 그 후 열 다섯 살때 東京에 건너 가서 기예 학교에서 2, 3년간 학업을 닦게 되었었는데 어느 때 제국극장에 구경간 것이 나로 하여금 오늘날 이 팔자를 만들어 놓고 말았지요. 母論 소학교 시절에는 배우라는 것을 꿈에도 생각 못 했고, 그저 그때 기껏 이상한 희망이라야 장차 소학교의 교사나 되였으면 하였죠. 그 밖에 또 다른 이상이 하나 있었다면 목사의＜136＞ 가정에서 자라나게 된 관계로 장차 米國이나 유학갔다 왔으면 하는 것이었지요. 그러나 東京에 건너간 후의 나의 이상은 달라젔어요. 장차 신문 기자가 아니면 배우가 되려는 두 가지의 희망을 가지게 되었어요.
李瑞求 - 하하! 그러구 보니 배우의 소원은 이루웠는데 아직까지 신문 기자 노릇을 못 해 봤으니 이제부터 어디 기자 생활이나 한번 하여 보시죠.
卜惠淑 - 인젠 아주 틀레마센데스나! 하여간 그때 제 아버지께서 東京에 쫓아와서 「아사구사」에서 나를 붓들어 가지고 京城에 돌아 왔으니깐요. 그리구 東京 있을 때 사감 선생이 너는 장차 무엇이 되려느냐 묻기에 배우가 되겠다고 대답했었는데 그 후 제가 京城에서 공연할 때 그 사감 선생이 大連에 무슨 일로 갔다가 돌아오던 길에 京城 거리에서 포스타-를 보고 나를 찾아 와서 너는 네 소원대로 성공했구나 하고 말씀하시드군요. 그러니까 나는 배우가 되구 싶어서 된 것이라 하겠지요.
李瑞求 - 다음은 南宮仙씨가 말씀해 주십시오.
南宮仙 - 제 고향은 黃海道 信川이며, 제 본성명은 金鎭順이얘요. 저도 소학교때부터 주일 학교에 다녔기 때문에 크리스마스 때 예배당에서 聖劇을 노는 때마다 늘 출연해서 매우 칭찬을 받았습니다. 소학교를 졸업하고 開城 好壽敦女子高等普通學校에서 2년간 통학하다가 京城 梨花高女로 전학을 했었지요. 물론 저는 이 배우 생활이 본래의 이상은 아니었었어요. 그때의 제 이상은, 학과의 성적도 남만 못 하지는 않었으며 더욱이 음악에 소질이 있어서 장래의 목적을 음악에다 두었지요. 그래서 梨花高女 3학년때부터 개인 교수까지 받았어요. 그리구 어떻게도 음악에＜137＞ 미쳤던지 미순 스쿨이니만치 일요일이면 예배당에 가야할 것인데 늘 빠저서 가만히 음악실에 들어가 피아노를 치다가 선생에게 들켜서 꾸지람을 들은 것이 한 두번이 아니얘요. (그는 엷은 한숨을 짓고는) 그러나 그렇게 갈망하던 음악도 사정이 여의치 못해서 梨花高女를 졸업하면 渡東하려든 꿈이 깨트려지고, 咸德勳씨의 알선과, 그의 오빠 咸大勳씨의 소개로 劇硏座에 들어가게 되었습니다. 그 때 노역을 하게 되었으며, 그 후 춘추 극장에서 2, 3개월 일보게 되었고, 「오뻐디힐」이라는 李雲芳씨의 각색인 연극에 주역으로 나와 비로소 연기를 나타내게 되었어요. 그때까지도 저는 음악에 대한 희망을 버리지 못 했으며, 劇界에 들어서게 된 것도 레-코트에 노래를 吹入하려면 劇界에 들어서는 길이 가장 빠르다고 하여서였습니다. 그 후, 저는 제 소원을 이루게 되여 동경으로 건너가게 되었으며, 東京서 고등 음악 학교에 통학하게 되었습니다. 그 때 洪蘭坡씨의 알선도 많이 입었고요. 그리다가 그 이듬해 여름 방학 에 집으로 돌아나와서는 다시 東京으로 건너 가지 못하게 되었어요. 그 뒤 동양극장 전속이었던 靑春座에서 1년간 활약했으며, 그 靑春座에서 분리되어 나간 中央舞台에서 비로소 이상에 맞는 연극을 많이 했어요. 상대역은 대개 沈影씨와 徐月影씨가 많이 하였어요.
李瑞求 - 「하이델 베리히」의 주인공 「케티」로 분장하고 나온 이가 바로 南宮仙씨랍니다.
朴啓周 - 아, 그래요? 난 그때 누구신줄 모르고 보았는데, 「하이델 베히」는 참 좋았어요.
李瑞求 - 왜, 「椿姬」의 「椿姬」로도 분장하고 나왔지요. 다음은 文藝峯씨가 말씀해 주십시오.

卜惠淑 - 옛날이야 다 친절했지요. 지방으로 가면 환영이 상당했고 심지어 時代日報 당시 大邱에를 갔을 때에는 정차장에서 환영포까지 터트리고 야단 법석이었죠. 지금이야 어디 지방에 갔다고 환영포는 커녕 환영회조차도 별로 볼 수 없으니까요. 지금이나 옛날이나 무섭기로는 咸鏡道 사람들인데, 淸津에서 제일 혼났어요. 옛날 淸津서 「籠中島」를 공연할 땐데 무대 준비 때문에 막간이 좀 길어지니깐 막간마다 날더러 나와서 딴스를 하라는군요. 그러니 비녀를 쪽지고 꼬리치마를 입었는데 그 꼴을 해 가지고 어떻게 서양 춤을 춰요? 이야말로 정말 요절할 노릇이 아니면 기절할 노릇이라 레벨이 낮은 인간들은 할 수 없구나 하고 自嘆하는데 자꾸 나와 딴스를 하라고 박수는 파도같이 장내를 울리는 통에 감독 李경환씨까지도 딱해서 날더러 어서 나가서 춤을 추라고 하는군요. 감독까지 이렇게 이해가 없으니 너무나 어처구니가 없어서 감독 더러 「여보! 당신 정신 있소? 없소? 이 꼴 하구 어델 나가서 서양춤 추라는 거요?」 하고 한바탕 대들고는 忿낌에 무대에 뛰어 나가서 「여러분 대체 당신들이 정신 있소? 없소? 다음 막에 나와서 연극해야 할 이 분장으로써 어떻게 딴슨지 서양 춤인질 한 걸 춰요? 무식해도 분수가 있지, 머리 쪽 지고 꼬리치마에 딴스가 무슨 놈의 빌어 먹다가 뒤어질 딴스요?」 하고 악을 썼더니 연극이 끝난 뒤에 卜惠淑 이년을 잡아라 하고 벌이떼 같이 덤벼드는 통에 신도 못 신고 여관으로 쫓겨 다라나는데, 정말 똥을 쌀 뻔 했죠. (일동 笑) 아, 그리구 한번을 間島 龍井에 가서 연극을 노는데 「야, 이놈들아, 연애극만 하지 말고 사회극을 해라!」하고 야질을 빗발 날리듯 퍼부으며-- 그때만 해도 礊�會主義 전성시대 였으니깐요-- 연극이 끝났을 때 학생들이 우루루 달려들며 卜惠淑씨와 인사할테니 내여 놓으라 하는구료. 나는 또 얻어 맞게 되나부다 하고 맨발로 또 여관으로 뛰어 갔는데 학생 무리가 여관에까지 쫓아와서 뜰악에 가득 찼었는데, 수백명이 되는 것 같애요. 어서 인사만 할 테니 卜惠淑이를 내어 놓으라고 독촉을 하는 통에 감독도 할 수 없이 날더러 나가보라고 하드군요.＜143＞ 「에라! 모르겠다. 맞아 죽을 셈치고 어디 나가보자.」하고 방문을 열고 쓱 나섰더니 뜰악 하나에 가득찬 학생 무리를 보니 아닌게 아니라 가슴이 두군거려요. 그러나 담대히 「웨 그러십니까?」하고 물었더니 학생 한 명이 쓱 나서면서 「웨 인사하자는데 이렇게 숨어 돌아가는거요」 하고 묻기에 「이 많은 학생을 내가 어떻게 일일히 인사하느냐?」고 대답했죠. 그랬더니 「일일히 인사하는 게 아니라 대표 한 사람이 인사하는게라」고 하면서 손을 턱 내어 밀면서 악수를 청하더군요. 그래서 나두 아주 가장 담대히 손을 턱 내어 밀어 악수했지요. 그랬더니 그 분들이 됐다고 하면서 모두 돌아가요. 그제야 저는 한숨을 휘우 내어 쉬고 이마의 땀을 씻었지요. 비록 연애극을 했다고 욕은 했을 망정 멀리 異國에까지 찾아 온 동포라고 감격의 인사를 할려고 온 것을 모르고 그렇게 혼이 났댔군요. 아이, 그때는 웨 대표가 그리 많은지! 쩍하면 무슨 대표니 무슨 대표니 하고 꽤들 떠들었지요.
金鮮英 - 저는 지방 순회를 하여 보던 중에서 釜山이 제일 친절하드군요. 우리가 釜山 가면 꼭 留宿하는 일정한 여관이 있는데 그 집 주인 노인이 참 친절해요. 우리가 새벽에 차에서 내려서 들어가거나 혹은 추운 때 밤에 늦게 들어가도 「아, 우리 아씨들이 또 오셨군. 어서 아랫 방으로 들어오라」 하면서 불을 뜨겁게 짚여 주는 등, 밥이 미처 되기 전에도 우리가 추워 할까봐 미역국을 미리 끓여서 드려 오며 야단 법석이지요. 그 영감이 똑 佛蘭西 배우 아리볼씨처럼 생겼기에 우리가 아리볼 영감이라고 불렀지요.
文藝峯 - 海州, 信川, 大邱, 咸興 등지가 다 친절하드군요. 한번 혼나 보기는 王平씨랑 가치 晋州에 가서 「사랑의 合理化」라는 극을 공연하다가 경찰에 모두 붓들려 가서 꽤 고생했지요.
南宮仙 - (핸드빽에서 거울을 꺼내어 화장을 하다가) 집에서 올 때는 내가 제일 이쁘려니 생각했더니 지금 거울을 드려다 보니 내가 제일 못 생겼군 그래. (笑)

巷間의 「꼬싶」에 對한 抗議
李瑞求 - 이번에는 세상에서 여러분을 향하여 여러 말이 있는 꼬싶에 대하여 항의, 혹은 변명, 그 진상 등을 말씀해 주시오.
卜惠淑 - 저는 멀 요새 꼬싶에 오르 내릴 아무 껀데기도 없으니깐요.＜144＞
李瑞求 - 卜惠淑씨가 仁寺町에서 이사하여 가니까 仁寺町 골목 안이 대단히 조용해 졌다고 말이 많던데요. 그리고 淸進町으로 이사가신 뒤에는 淸進町 町會에서 요란하고 분주하다고 추방명령이 내리지 않습디까. (일동 笑)
卜惠淑 - 네, 仁寺町에서 내가 떠난 것이 매우 시원 섭섭하다고 지난 년말에 선물도 꽤 많이 쓸어 들던데요. (笑)
文藝峯 - 전 요새 이런 꼬싶에 오르 내리는 모양인데, 그것은 某 영화사에서 「處女圖」라는 영화를 촬영할텐데 절더러 여주인공으로 출연하여 달라고 某 감독이 부탁하드군요. 그 분이 처음 절더러 말씀할 때에는 그리 어렵지도 않은 듯이 말하고, 또 제가 맡기에 적당하다고 하며, 쉬운 것 같이 말하더니, 그 후 「處女圖」의 내용을 기록한 책을 가저다 보니까 여간 어려운 것이 아니얘요. 그리고 저로서는 도저히 맡을 수가 없는 것이기에 못 하겠다고 謝絶했더니 건방지다고 비난이 많은 모양이얘요. 저는 사실 뽐내느라고 못 하겠다고 한 것이 아니고, 저로서도 도저히 맡을 수가 없는 어려운 것이오, 따라서 제 연기로서는 그 영화를 영화로서 살리지 못 하겠기에 그 영화 자체를 위해서 겸손한 의미에서 사양한 것인데 그 분들이 오해하고 文藝峯이는 지금은 아주 뽐내고, 대단히 건방지게 군다고 비평하니 참 억울해요.

이 우에 더 바라는 幸福

金鮮英 - 저두 역시 제 연기가 나아가기를 바래요.＜146＞
南海燕 - 그야 누구나 다 바라는 공통된 희망이겠죠. 그리고 그렇게 되어 간다면 행복이 아닐 수 없지요.
