Article Title	紀行 南漢山城 (Travelogue: Namhan Mountain Fortress)
Author	朴鍾和

Travelogue: Namhan Mountain Fortress
						Pak Chonghwa

If Puk'an Mountain (北漢山) is a magnanimous mountain with brilliant and handsome masculine beauty, Namhan Mountain (南漢山) is a trustworthy and dependable mountain possessing a mother's embrace, like a mother's breast, offering a soft, ordinary, yet somehow leisurely and generous feel. Its shape resembles an overturned traditional hat (사갓), which is why old records describe its form as like an *angnip* (仰笠, upturned hat).

The guardian mountain (鎭山) Puk'ansan, soaring haughtily in the northeast of Seoul, emitting a spirit (氣焰) of ten thousand *chang* (萬丈), and Namhan Mountain, lying indistinctly beyond the clouds in the southeast as if painted with blue-green eyebrows (靑黛), beckoning the sea-like azure sky (蒼穹), with the belt-like blue meanders of the South and North Han River (南北漢江) flowing between them, form a contrasting pair like a good husband and wife.

Puk'ansan and Namhan Mountain also have a height difference befitting a couple. Puk'ansan's height is a considerable 836 meters <110> above sea level, but Namhan Mountain's height is only 453 meters— indeed, Namhan Mountain possesses the virtue of feminine modesty compared to Puk'ansan.

Just as Puk'ansan is also called Samgak Mountain (三角山), Namhan Mountain is called Ilchang Mountain (日長山). Looking in the *Tongguk Yŏji Sŭngnam* (東國輿地勝覽), it says, "Ilchang Mountain is 5 *ri* south of Kwangju (廣州), also called Namhan Mountain," and separately, "Ilchang Mountain Fortress (日長山城) is the Hwajang Fortress (畫長城) of the Silla period, built by King Munmu (文武王). Inside are six wells and streams; its circumference is 86,800 *ch'ŏk* (feet), and its height is 24 *ch'ŏk* of stone construction (石築)."

From this, we can see that after Silla annexed Paekche, they built a fortress on Namhan Mountain and called it Hwajang Fortress. Consulting the *Samguk Sagi* (三國史記) next, it records: 『In the 13th year of King Onjo (溫祚王), an old woman in the capital transformed into a mountain boy, five tigers entered the fortress, and the Queen Mother (王母) passed away. The king spoke to his ministers, saying, "To the east of our country lies Nangnang (樂浪), and to the north, the Malgal (靺鞨), who encroach upon our borders, leaving no peaceful days. Moreover, recently, ominous signs (요물) have frequently appeared, and even the Queen Mother has passed away. The situation is unstable. We must move the capital. Yesterday, I surveyed the land south of the Han River (漢水), and the land is fertile and good. We should establish the capital there to seek long-term peace." In the 7th month, he went to Han Mountain (漢山), built fortifications (柵), and ordered the households of Wirye Fortress (慰禮城) to move. In the 9th month, he built the fortress and palace (城闕).』

From this, we see that 675 years before Silla built Hwajang Fortress on Namhan Mountain, Paekche had established its capital in the Hanam Wirye Fortress area, that is, around the present-day Namhan Mountain Fortress near the old town of Kwangju, centered south of the Han River, moving from present-day Chiksan (稷山). For nearly 400 years, until King Kŭnch'ogo (近肖古王) moved the capital to Puk'an [Mountain area], Namhan Kwangju was the capital of Paekche.

In any case, Namhan Mountain, along with Puk'an Mountain, is a site of immense historical significance with a long, continuous history of 2000 years. Together with Silla's Kyŏngju (慶州) and Koguryŏ's P'yŏngyang Fortress (平壤城), it formed a tripod (솟발같이) that established the Three Kingdoms period and commanded the world.

Flipping through the pages of 2000 years of rise and fall, the number of major wars fought here, marked by the twang of bowstrings, the thunder of hooves, and the clash of flashing long swords and sharp snake-headed spears (蛇矛鎗) that quickened the blood of valiant men (龍驤虎搏), exceeds forty.

However, in modern times, the final unforgettable war, the indelible sorrowful history (哀史), is the poignant <111> war of the Pyŏngja Horan (丙子胡亂, Qing invasion of 1636) during the reign of King Injong (仁祖王) of the Yi Dynasty (李朝), the siege of Namhan (南漢 籠城). Who among those visiting Namhan Mountain could hear this sad story and not shed tears of remembrance?

Namhan Mountain is renowned as the ancient capital of Paekche, but it stirs our hearts even more because of the Pyŏngja Horan, which occurred 300 years ago from now.

In the 14th year of King Injong, the year Pyŏngja (丙子, 1636), the Jin Dynasty (金國) of Aisin Gioro Nurhaci (愛親覺羅 누루하치; 弩爾哈赤), which had suddenly risen like wildfire in a corner of Manchuria (滿洲), reached the time of his son Hong Taiji (홍타시; 皇太極, also Hong Tasi; 洪多時). He changed the state name to Great Qing (大淸), the Khan proclaimed himself Emperor, and changed the era name to Sungdŏk (崇德).

At that time in Chosŏn, Queen Inryŏl (仁烈王后) of the Han clan had passed away. In Manchuria, they announced in the 2nd month of that year that they would offer condolences (吊表) for the national mourning and sent envoys Yonggoldae (龍骨大) and Vice-Commander Mafudae (馬夫大) leading about 190 attendants (從卒) to Chosŏn. While expressing condolences on one hand, they demanded on the other that Chosŏn internationally recognize the Great Qing Emperor and the newly risen Qing state, and subsequently submit to Qing as a vassal state (藩臣).

However, the Chosŏn court's view of the Qing state at this time was extremely arrogant. Until then, the Qing had been regarded merely as a tribe of Jianzhou Jurchens (建州衛野人), barbarians who had paid tribute (조공) several times since the reigns of King T'aejo (太祖) and T'aejong (太宗), considered insignificant underlings.

For such a barbarian tribe, even if times and circumstances had changed, to suddenly proclaim an emperor, a Son of Heaven, was astonishing. Furthermore, demanding that Chosŏn act as a vassal state was unthinkable.

Eventually, the court debate boiled over. Changnyŏng (掌令) Hong Ik'an (洪翼漢) insisted on beheading the Qing envoy Yonggoldae. Scholars from the Hongmungwan (弘文館) and officials from the Saganwŏn (司諫院) also rose up, demanding to burn Hong Taiji's letter and declare war. They were all young men in their 20s and 30s, full of hot-blooded righteousness (熱血 義氣). However, they lacked the mature political judgment to consider the consequences.

King Injong was eventually swayed by public opinion. He refused to receive the state letter * but felt compelled to receive the condolences. He had a temporary canopy (헛장막) set up at Kŭmgyo Bridge (禁橋) and pretended to receive the condolences there. This was because Yonggoldae's retinue was large and their behavior audacious, raising fears of an incident if they were allowed inside the palace.

During his stay of several days, Yonggoldae was not unaware of the boiling public opinion in the Chosŏn court. Regardless, since he had come, <112> he intended to at least offer condolences, even if delivering the state letter was impossible. At this time, they were still unfamiliar with Chosŏn's true situation and harbored considerable fear of Chosŏn. When they finally arrived at the condolence site (吊祭場) to perform the rites (致祭), they found the site was not the palace but the Kŭmch'unggyo Bridge (금충교), and the ceremonial chairs (交椅) on the altar table (祭床) were fake. As they performed the three kneelings and nine kowtows (三拜 九叩頭), filled with suspicion, the canopy flap (帳 자락) fluttered in the wind, revealing armed guards (刀斧手) hidden (埋伏) behind the curtain. These were soldiers the Chosŏn court had hidden beforehand, fearing Yonggoldae's group might cause trouble (亂).

The already fearful Qing envoy Yonggoldae immediately intuited that this was an attempt to kill them. He threw down the offered cup of liquor, clenched his fists, and started to run. When Yonggoldae ran, Mafudae also fled. When the two commanders fled, the hundreds of attendants, bewildered and startled, also ran.

As Yonggoldae ran, Mafudae ran, and the attendants ran, the onlookers who had gathered in layers also panicked and ran. Towards the fleeing Qing envoys, mischievous children threw stones (돌풀매). Hearing shouts from behind and seeing stones raining down, the Qing envoys became even more frantic. They sprinted away, seized horses near Muakchae Pass (무악재), and fled.

This was the initial spark (도화선) that ignited the Pyŏngja Horan. With things having reached this point, King Injong had no choice. He issued edicts (諭文) to the eight provinces, informing the border commanders of the severance of relations with Nurhaci. During their escape, the Qing envoys intercepted and seized an edict sent from the Pibyŏnsa (備邊司, Border Defense Council) to the Governor of P'yŏngan Province (平安 監司), rejecting reconciliation (斥和), as evidence.

Upon learning of Chosŏn's resolve, Hong Taiji's Manchuria decided to conquer Chosŏn in one fell swoop. Hong Taiji personally led 10,000 troops, including Manchu, Mongol, and Han soldiers, waiting for the Yalu River (鴨綠江) ice to freeze before invading Chosŏn. The vanguard was led by Mafudae and Yonggoldae, the main army by Qing Taizong Hong Taiji, and the rear guard by Prince Yoto (豫親王) and others. On the 12th day of the 12th lunar month of the year Pyŏngja, the dispatch (狀啓) from Ŭiju (義州) Magistrate Im Kyŏngŏp (林慶業) arrived in Seoul with the urgent clanging of horse bells from the pony express (파발). It reported that nine days earlier, the Qing army had crossed the Yalu River via a shortcut (지럼길). On the 13th day, a report (馳報) arrived from Commander-in-Chief (都元帥) Kim Chajŏm (金自點). The massive army, pouring in like a flood, had already reached the vicinity of Anju (安州). On the 14th day, the governor (留守) of Songdo (松都) dispatched another urgent message. The enemy troops had already passed Songdo.

Thus, the enemy army, unable to be stopped even once, surged <113> overwhelmingly like a tide, instantly advancing towards Changdan (長湍), Koyang (高陽), and the Capital City (京城). The court was thrown into confusion (낭패), and the city was in turmoil. Hearing that war was upon them, the people, without any plan (심산), scrambled out of the city gates, weeping.

The state had Yejo Pangsŭngji (禮房 承旨) Han Hŭngil (韓興一) enshrine the ancestral tablets (神主) from the Royal Ancestral Shrine (宗廟) and the Altars of Soil and Grain (社稷) and assigned former ministers Yun Pang (尹昉) and Kim Sangyong (金尙容) to escort (護從) them. Kim Kyŏngjing (金慶徵) was appointed Kanghwa Inspector (江華 檢察使) and Yi Min'gu (李敏求) as Vice-Inspector (副檢察使) to protect the Crown Princess (嬪宮, Lady Kang), the Royal Grandson (元孫), Grand Prince Pongnim (鳳林大君), Grand Prince Inp'yŏng (麟坪大君), princesses, and royal sons-in-law, sending them first to Kanghwa (江華) for refuge. The defense of the Capital City was entrusted to Capital Garrison Commander (留都 大將) Sim Kiwŏn (沈器遠). Only then, that afternoon, did King Injong, accompanied by the Crown Prince (世子), attempt to follow the Crown Princess to Kanghwa.

But alas, the situation had already descended into chaos (수라장). Though he was the ruler of ten thousand chariots (千乘), there wasn't a single horse fit to ride, nor a single royal guard (武藝 別監) to escort him. While wringing his hands and stamping his feet, Naesŭng (內乘, royal stable official) Yi Sŏngnam (李星男) managed to find one horse and held the reins (견마 잡다).

Meanwhile, the vanguard of the Qing army had passed Yangch'ŏl Plain (양철이 벌판, south of Taejo-ri, Ŭnp'yŏngmyŏn, Koyang County) and its leading elements had already reached Hongjewŏn (弘濟院). The Governor of Changdan, Hwang Chik (黃稷), shamefully shorn (멀이 깍기고) and dressed in Manchu clothes (胡服), served as their guide (嚮導官). Another contingent (一技隊) had somehow occupied Yangch'ŏn River (陽川江), blocking the road to Kanghwa. King Injong had no choice but to halt the royal palanquin (玉駕) at the Namdaemun Gate tower (南大門 門樓). General Sin Kyŏngjin (申景禛) formed ranks (結陣) outside the gate. Countless innocent commoners within the city—fathers, sons, brothers, husbands, wives—clung to each other, wailing, their mournful cries filling the city.

King Injong urgently consulted the few accompanying officials for countermeasures. Ch'ŏlsan (鐵山) Magistrate Chi Yŏhae (池如海) grasped his sword, bowed before the throne, and proposed: "The enemy crossed the border merely three days ago and rushed over a thousand *ri*. Their soldiers and horses must be exhausted. If we take 500 riflemen (砲手) to attack (迫擊) them at Moraejae Pass (모래재, vicinity of present-day Hyŏnjŏ-dong; 峴底町) and annihilate (鏖殺) their vanguard, their morale (銳氣) will be broken. Taking advantage of this moment, Your Majesty can proceed to Kanghwa, which would be a perfectly safe plan. I beseech you, grant me just 500 elite riflemen (精砲手)." However, all the other officials shook their heads and remained silent. The idea of repelling the overwhelming enemy force with 500 soldiers seemed unreliable.

Minister of Personnel (吏曹判書) Ch'oe Myŏnggil (崔鳴吉) prostrated himself before the throne and volunteered to ride alone <114> into the enemy camp. He proposed to question their reasons for deploying troops and to waste time by pretending to feast (犒饋) their soldiers with beef and wine (酒肴). During this time, the King should quickly enter Namhan Mountain Fortress (南漢山城) and prepare for battle.

King Injong followed Ch'oe Myŏnggil's advice, appointing the reputed courageous Yi Kyŏngjik (李景稷) as Vice-Envoy and providing 20 royal guards (禁軍) to escort Ch'oe Myŏnggil. However, on the way to Hongjewŏn, the 20 guards, fearing death, scattered and deserted.

While Ch'oe Myŏnggil delayed the enemy with diplomatic wrangling, King Injong turned the royal carriage (車駕) towards Namhan. There wasn't even a single attendant to hold the reins for the Crown Prince. The Prince personally used his whip (챗죽) to urge his horse forward, following the King.

Passing Kurigae (구리개, Hwanggeumchŏng 1-chŏngmok; 黃金町 1丁目) and exiting through Sigumun Gate (시구문; 光熙門), the men and women (士女) of the city, barefoot and wailing (호곡하며), rushed after the royal procession (王駕). They crossed Sinnae Stream (新川, now Sinch'ŏn-ri, *do-myŏn, Koyang County) and the Songp'a River (松坡江) around Sin time (申時, 3-5 PM), and only after Su time (戍時, 7-9 PM) and past Hae time (亥時, 9-11 PM) did they finally reach Namhan Mountain Fortress. How tragic! At this time, the escorting officials numbered only ten. All this happened by the night of the 14th day of the 12th lunar month of the year Pyŏngja.

Only after confirming that the King's procession (御駕) had definitively relocated (播遷) to Namhan did the civil and military officials (百官) begin to gather in twos and threes. An all-night council (어전 회의) was held. Chief State Councilor (領議政) Kim Ryu (金瑬) argued that Namhan was not advantageous terrain and urged the King to slip away (물래) to Kanghwa in disguise (미복). The next morning at dawn, the King, under the pretext of inspecting the fortress troops, went out the south gate of the fortress. The mountain path, where snow had melted, was coated with glass-like ice (어름), and the horses' hooves could not gain purchase. The King had no choice but to descend (나리시다) the rocky path on foot. The path was slippery (밋그럽다), his toes were freezing, making it impossible to move even an inch (寸步), and his feet, blistered (발뿌리) from the previous night, could barely move (꼼작). He finally resolved to make a stand (籠城) and returned to the fortress, possessed by the grim determination to defend the solitary, isolated Namhan Mountain Fortress.

Returning to the fortress, the King personally ascended the Command Post (將臺), issued a proclamation (告諭) encouraging the fortress soldiers, provided them with liquor and food (犒饋), and inquired about each soldier's cold and hunger. The soldiers (士卒) wept with gratitude (감읍), and there wasn't a single officer who didn't draw his sword, eager to fight.

At this time, the civil and military officials who had followed to the fortress numbered over 200; <115> members of the royal family and medical staff (醫局員) numbered over 200; servants (노복) numbered over 300; and soldiers (軍兵) numbered 13,800.

The supplies stored within the fortress consisted of polished rice (백미) and miscellaneous grains (皮雜穀) mixed together (얼러서), barely amounting to 16,000 *sŏk* (섬); silver totaling 7,600 *nyang* (냥); soy sauce (醬) over 200 *sŏk*; salt over 90 *sŏk*; and gunpowder (화약) only 18,000 *kŭn* (근). This was barely enough grain for 13,000-14,000 people to eat for just over a month.

The very next day, the 16th of the 12th month, the enemy vanguard under Mafudae had already deployed troops facing Namhan Mountain Fortress. Outside the capital city walls, the women of scholar-official families (士族) and commoners (서민) who hadn't fled far were all captured by enemy soldiers. Mothers were forcibly kept in the camps, while young children were abandoned outside. In the severe cold of harsh winter snow (嚴冬雪寒), the corpses of children lay strewn about (즐비하였다).

During this time, Chosŏn forces fought fiercely (惡戰苦鬪) time and again. However, the tide of battle (시세) was unfavorable. Eventually, the scholars Hong Ik'an (洪翼漢), O Talje (吳達濟), and Yun Chip (尹集), who advocated war and rejected peace (主戰 斥和), emerged; Ch'oe Myŏnggil (崔鳴吉), who calmly argued for peace (主和), stepped forward; Kim Sanghŏn (金尙憲), the man of eternally righteous spirit who tore up the state document, appeared; Chŏng On (鄭蘊), who lamented being unable to die by falling on his sword (伏劍而死) and stabbed his own stomach, appeared; and Royal Son-in-Law (駙馬) Sin Iksŏng (申翊聖), who drew his sword demanding the beheading of pro-peace officials (主和臣), also emerged.

However, empty-fisted righteousness (의기) alone could achieve nothing. As the first lunar month dragged on, all sorts of supplies within the fortress grew scarce. First, fodder (꼴) for horses and cattle completely ran out (뚝 떠러젔다). If it were summer, no matter how barren the mountain, abundant grass would have eliminated concerns about fodder (馬糧草). But in the harsh winter cold, with ice and snow piled thick and frozen in the mountain valleys, not even a strand of straw (검부 새끼) could be found. Horses and cattle, starved for ten or fifteen days, unable to endure hunger, simply collapsed and died. Seeing the horses and cattle collapse and die, even though these were animals they had lovingly fed and grown attached to, the soldiers scrambled to chew the meat of the dead animals. The soldiers too were starving and desperate (솟중이 나니), with no other recourse. However, the cattle and horses had starved to death, so what they ate was mostly tough sinew and fragmented bones (봉투라지진 뼉다귀).

The cattle and horses that hadn't yet collapsed and were still breathing, starving and with nothing to eat, gnawed at each other's tails (꼬리와 꼬리를 뜨더 먹으며) and fought, bellowing (으흥거려).

Food for the soldiers and officials also dwindled. <116>

On the 21st day of the first month, the Khan (汗) himself, leading the main army, joined the vanguard and encircled Namhan Mountain Fortress. Prior to this, a contingent (一技 大隊) had advanced towards Kanghwa Island. Kanghwa Island, believed to be impregnable (難攻不落), proved useless. Kanghwa Island, thought inaccessible without boats because its surrounding waters didn't freeze, was also useless. They built boats on the mainland, dragged them on sleds (수레) across the frozen Cho River (祖江), and launched (띠웠다) them into the sea before Kanghwa. Kanghwa Inspector Kim Kyŏngjing, trusting in the natural defenses (天府金湯), merely sang idle songs (코노래) and drank, but without drawing a single sword, the entire island of Kanghwa fell into enemy hands in an instant (一瞬). The Royal Ancestral Shrine and the Altars of Soil and Grain also fell into enemy hands; the Crown Princess and the Royal Grandson also fell into enemy hands.

Thousands of scholar-official women (千百 士婦女) vied to become lonely souls in the water (수중의 孤魂, i.e., drowned themselves). When this tragic news reached Namhan Mountain Fortress, not only the King, who had abandoned the shrines and altars, but also the civil and military officials and soldiers whose wives, children, and parents had been sent to Kanghwa, lost their fighting spirit (의기가 悄沈되고) and became utterly demoralized (맥이 풀여 벌였다). A collective wail (通哭 一聲) seemed to knock everyone unconscious, and a pall of gloom (愁雲) enveloped the fortress.

After 45 days of siege and fierce battles (籠城 激전), the King, clad in blue robes and jade belt (藍袍 玉帶 - normally red robes (紅袍) were formal attire, but he was made to wear blue robes as a surrendering offender), harboring eternal resentment (萬古 恨), went to the Manchu camp (胡陣) at Samjŏndo (三田渡) and submitted the instrument of surrender (降書). This was truly the first such humiliation (屈辱) in Chosŏn history. On this day, the King was permitted to return to the palace (환궁). The Three Scholars (三學士) were identified as officials who rejected peace (斥和臣) and taken captive to the Manchu camp. Grand Prince Pongnim, Grand Prince Inp'yŏng, and the Crown Prince were taken as hostages (볼모) to Shenyang (瀋陽).

This is the most sorrowful scene from Namhan Mountain Fortress in recent history.

When the spring sunlight is warm or the autumn leaves are beautiful, if you take a short staff (短笻) and go out to the Kwangjin Bridge vicinity (廣津 橋畔), to the south, the Ilchang mountain range (日長連山) draws a solitary line (孤線) across the sky, emitting spirit (영기) as it lies stretched out. This is precisely Namhan Mountain Fortress, the protagonist of this long story. From Seoul, it's 60 *ri* on foot. Using convenient modern transportation, one can take the tram (기동차) from the Tongdaemun Tram Company (東大門 軌道會社) towards Kwangjang-ri (廣壯里), get off at Kwangjin Ferry Bridge (廣津 津橋), see the Paekche ruins called P'ungsŏng-ri T'osŏng (風細里 土城), pass in front of Sangil-ri Elementary School (上一리 소학교), go through Sinjang-ri (新長里) and Tŏkp'ung-ri (德豐里) to Kwangjiwŏn (光池院), and enter the fortress through the east gate (동문; 松岩亭). To save more walking, one can take the tram from Seoul's Tongdaemun to Sut'o (*島) <117>, get off, take a small ferry (똑닥선) to Songp'a from the riverbank, disembark on the hill, thoughtfully read the Khan's stele (汗의 碑) at Songp'a Ferry (松坡鎭), and then enter the fortress directly through the south gate (남문). If one wants to avoid walking altogether, taking a Kyŏngjŏn bus (京電 뻐스) or a taxi (탁시) from Seoul will take you directly to the Yŏnmu-gwan (演武館) inside the fortress.

Entering the fortress through the east gate, on the left are Chisu-dang (池水堂) and Kwanŏ-jŏng (觀魚亭), and on the right, the red-pillared, colorfully painted pavilions (朱欄畵閣) of Changgyŏng Temple (長慶寺) and Hyŏnjŏl Shrine (顯節祠) are faintly visible amidst the pine grove (솔밭).

Chisu-dang was built by Governor (留守) Yi Sehwa (李世華) during the reign of King Hyŏnjong (顯宗). He dug three ponds to the left and right of the hall and built this structure. However, only two ponds remain today.

Kwanŏ-jŏng is a hexagonal pavilion (六角堂) built on an island in the first pond west of Chisu-dang. It was constructed by Governor Kim Chaechan (金載瓚) during King Sunjo's (純祖) reign. Kim Chaechan's "Kwanŏjŏng Sogi" (觀魚亭 小識, A Small Record of Kwanŏ Pavilion) exists in the *Namhant'ŭk* (南漢忒, Records of Namhan?).

Changgyŏng Temple, to the northeast, was already famous during the Pyŏngja Horan. There were nine temples, including Kaeun Temple (開運寺) and Changgyŏng Temple. Buddhist disciples (불제자) entering these temples practiced austerities (法悅에 들어) and revered the Three Jewels (三寶). However, should an urgent national matter arise, they were places that trained monk soldiers (僧兵) who would draw swords in their black robes (長衫) and go to the battlefield. Kaeun Temple housed the head monk (總攝), under whom 370 monk soldiers practiced swordsmanship and horsemanship even in peacetime. Today, however, only Changgyŏng Temple remains. It is surrounded on all sides (사면) by royal azaleas (躑躅花) and pine forests (松林). Sitting in Chinnam-nu Pavilion (鎭南樓) and gazing at the splendidly blooming azaleas in spring offers a fine experience, but receiving the pine wind (松風) during the fiery heat of midsummer (盛夏) provides an even greater pleasure (快味).

Hyŏnjŏl Shrine honors the three famous scholars (三學士) of the Pyŏngja Horan who opposed peace (斥和臣): Hong Ik'an, Yun Chip, and O Talje. These three were captured and taken to the Manchu court (胡廷), where they achieved righteousness through self-sacrifice (殺身成仁). During King Sukchong's (肅宗大王) reign, the king, deeming their loyalty commendable, had Governor Yi Sibaek (李時白) build this shrine to console their spirits. Their loyalty, piercing the white sun (白日), remains brilliant to this day. Built over 250 years ago, memorial rites (節祀) are held here every spring and autumn.

A little further in is the Yŏnmu-gwan (演武館). It is said to have been formerly called Yŏnmu-dang (鍊武堂). During King Sukchong's reign, Defense Commander (守禦使) Kim Chaeho (金在好) was ordered to renovate this building, after which the plaque "Yŏnbyŏng-gwan" (鍊兵館, Troop Training Hall) was hung. Later, during King Chŏngjo's (正祖大王) reign, it was renamed "Suŏyŏng" (守禦營, Defense Command). It was a place for military training (조련시키든 터).

Walking up a short distance leads to the site of the Temporary Palace (行宮 터). The site of the upper palace (윗 대궐; 上闕) <118> is below Ilchang Mountain (日長山) where the West Command Post (西將臺) stands, in the area behind the current township office (면사무소). It was constructed concurrently with the fortress wall during the Kapcha year (甲子, 1624) of King Injong's reign. Defense Commander Yi Sŏ (李曙) ordered Magistrate (牧使) Yu Rim (柳琳) to build roughly 70 structures, including left and right chambers (房軒) and wing corridors (翼廊), completed the following year, Ŭlch'uk (乙丑, 1625). The lower palace (아랫 대궐; 下闕) was located outside the main gate (三門) of the upper palace and built around the same time; it corresponds to the Sŏnhwa-dang (宣化堂). Outside the left wall of the upper palace is the Chwasŭng-dang (坐勝堂). This was built by Governor Sim Sanggyu (沈象奎) in the Chŏngch'uk year (丁丑, 1817) of King Sunjo's reign. These three buildings were considered important official structures (官廨) and were used as the offices (衙門) of the Governor and Magistrate from the mid-Yi Dynasty onwards. Particularly noteworthy is that the upper and lower palaces served as the temporary residence (行在所) where King Injong stayed for over 40 days during the Pyŏngja Horan.

After experiencing the Pyŏngja Horan, King Hyojong (孝宗大王), harboring eternal resentment, planned a northern expedition (북벌), but suddenly passed away due to an unexpected abscess (*腫) and rests quietly in Yŏngnŭng Royal Tomb (英陵) in Yŏju (驪州). Subsequent kings Sukchong, Yŏngjo (英祖), and Chŏngjo, on their return journeys (回路) from visiting the Yŏju tombs (陵行), reflecting on Injong and Hyojong, would each stop at this temporary palace for a night before returning to the capital (回駕). This occurred in the Muchin year (戊辰, 1688), the 14th year of Sukchong; the Kyŏngsul year (庚戌, 1730), the 6th year of Yŏngjo; and the Kihae year (己亥, 1779), the 3rd year of Chŏngjo.

Chwasŭng-dang was relocated to Kyŏngan-ri (京安里) in Taishō 8 (大正 8年, 1919) and is now used as a police station. Other important buildings of the temporary palace were also sold off (拂下) when the county office moved, and very little remains today.

Moving inside the west gate of the fortress, there is a shrine dedicated to King Onjo (溫祚王), the founder of Paekche (百濟). It is located on the mid-slope (中腹) northwest of Ilchang Mountain. It is a shrine (祭殿) built by King Injong when he was besieged (籠城) in this mountain during the Pyŏngja Horan. It was meant to honor the ancient master of Kwangju Namhan and console his spirit. Yi Sŏ, who was the Defense Commander at the time, was also enshrined here (配享). After King Injong built the Onjo Shrine (溫王廟) and performed rites (祭享), strangely, a few days later, King Onjo appeared (顯夢) in King Injong's dream. "Your Majesty built my shrine, for which I am truly grateful," he said, offering thanks, then added, "However, I am very lonely by myself. I request that you send your retainer Yi Sŏ to me." King Injong readily agreed in his dream, saying, "That is not difficult." But upon waking, it was just a dream (南柯一夢).

A few days later, Defense Commander Yi Sŏ suddenly fell ill. At a time when the outcome of the battle was undecided, Yi Sŏ's illness caused considerable trouble. Eventually, Yi Sŏ died from his illness. King Injong lamented not only losing a great general during the war but also because the dream was so eerily accurate (영악하매), he ultimately had Yi Sŏ enshrined alongside King Onjo in the Onjo Shrine. <119> Thus goes the sad legend.

Panting for breath (숨을 헐덕어려) again, climbing to the highest peak of the fortress, this is the summit (꼭댁이) of Ilchang Mountain, 453 meters high. Here stands the ancient West Command Post (西將臺), displaying venerable character (古色이 창연한). A plaque (현판) inscribed with the three characters "Mumangnu" (無忘樓, Never Forget Pavilion) hangs on the platform (臺上). It signifies the intention never to forget the Pyŏngja Horan. It is a grand two-story building occupying a spacious site of 40 *p'yŏng*. It was constructed by Governor Yi Kirin (李箕鎭) during the reign of King Yŏngjo, making it the largest extant structure within the fortress. This is where the Defense Commander, in full military attire (具軍服) with a decorated hat (蜜花 貝纓), would stand on the command platform (壯臺), draw his sword, and command tens of thousands of troops.

Anyone who ascends this Mumangnu will feel magnanimous and exhilarated (壯快), wanting to stamp their feet and let out a long whistle from an opened heart (豁然한 흉금).

The unobstructed view (眼界) allows one to look down upon all the mountains and rivers of Kyŏngsŏng (京城, Seoul), Yangju (楊州), Yangp'yŏng (楊平), Yongin (龍仁), and Koyang (高陽) in a single glance (一眸). In the hazy distance over the Inch'ŏn (仁川) sea, the evening glow (夕照) boils up like a crimson fireball (시뻘건 불덩이). Turning one's gaze closer, looking down (구버보면) at the Han River below, a couple of fishing boats (漁艇), seemingly indifferent yet purposeful, leisurely yet busy, sail gracefully into view (그림같이 도라든다), their sails (돗대) filled with wind. Raising a hand to point (지점하는) towards the upper reaches of the Han River, ah, that must be where the Khan's stele stands at Samjŏndo and Songp'a! At that moment, the Pyŏngja Horan unfolds vividly before one's eyes—seeing the Three Scholars, seeing Ch'oe Myŏnggil, seeing Ch'ŏngŭm Kim Sangyong (淸陰 金尙容), seeing King Injong—even a man cannot help but shed tears of indignation and sorrow (비분 강개한 눈물).

Suddenly recalling a poem by King Sukchong, grandson of King Hyojong, written at the West Command Post:
回駕直登西將臺 (Returning, I ascend directly to the West Command Post)
君臣說往日昏來. (King and ministers recount past days till dusk descends)
忿然慷慨不能抑 (Resentful and sorrowful, feelings cannot be suppressed)
惟有善承善繼哉. (Only resolution is to uphold well and continue well)

Reciting this verse, and departing with the setting sun as a companion, the day's experiences will be so overwhelming (벅차서) that the impression of Namhan will not fade (스러지지 아니할게다) from memory for years to come. <120>

**Summary**

This travelogue by Pak Chonghwa describes Namhansansŏng Fortress, contrasting its gentle, maternal character with the masculine Puk'ansan. It recounts the fortress's deep history, from its Paekche origins as a capital (Wirye Fortress vicinity) and Silla modifications (Hwajang Fortress) to its most famous and tragic role during the Chosŏn Dynasty: the siege during the Pyŏngja Horan (Qing Invasion of 1636-1637). The author details the events leading to the invasion, the desperate 45-day defense led by King Injong, the hardships faced (starvation, betrayal, capture of Kanghwado), the eventual surrender at Samjŏndo, and the fates of key figures like the Three Scholars (Samhaksa) and Ch'oe Myŏnggil. The latter part guides the reader through the remaining historical sites within the fortress (gates, temples like Changgyŏngsa, shrines like Hyŏnjŏlsa, command posts like Sŏjangdae/Mumangnu, the temporary palace site, Onjo Shrine), evoking the poignant memories associated with each location, particularly the humiliation of the Pyŏngja Horan and the subsequent resolve symbolized by Mumangnu.

From a colonial history perspective, the article, written in 1940, is interesting for its focus on a significant site of Korean national history and tragedy. While ostensibly a travelogue, the detailed recounting of the Pyŏngja Horan—a period of immense suffering under foreign invasion and national humiliation—could subtly resonate with contemporary Korean sentiments under Japanese rule. The emphasis on Korean resilience, historical figures embodying loyalty and resistance (like the Three Scholars and Kim Sanghŏn), and the enduring memory of past sorrows (Mumangnu) could be interpreted as fostering a sense of national identity and historical consciousness within the constraints of the colonial period. Historians might find the specific details chosen for recounting the invasion, the descriptions of the sites in 1940, and the underlying nationalistic tone significant for understanding cultural expression and historical memory during that era.

잡지명	삼천리 제12권 제4호
발행일	1940년 04월 01일
기사제목	紀行 南漢山城
필자	朴鍾和
기사형태	기행문

紀行 南漢山城
						朴鍾和
北漢山이 영특하고 준수한 남성미를 가진 호방한 산이라면 南漢山은 부드러웁고 평범하면서도 어느 구석 느긋하고 너글너글한 맛을 주는 어머니 젖가슴 같은 모성애를 가진 믿엄성스런 듬직한 산이다. 산 모양은 마치 사깟을 재처 논 것과 같기 때문에 옛 사기에 그 형상이 仰笠과 같다 한 곳도 있다.
서울 동북에 萬丈의 氣焰을 배앝으며 우줄거려 소슨 鎭山 北漢山과 띄같은 푸른 물구비 南北漢江 물을 가운데로 두고 동남에 아리잠직 靑黛를 눈섭에 지운 듯 둥두렷이 구름 밖에 누어 바다 같은 蒼穹을 손질해 부르는 南漢山은 한 쌍의 좋은 부부 같은 대조다.
北漢山과 南漢山은 키가 또한 부부다웁게 걸맞게 차이가 지니 北漢山의 키는 해발 836米突이나＜110＞ 되는 수얼치 않은 높이지만은 南漢山의 키는 453米 밖— 되지 않으니 역시 南漢山이 北漢山에 비하여 여자다운 겸양의 덕을 가젔다 할 수 있다.
北漢山의 별명을 三角山이라 부르드키 南漢山의 *명을 日長山이라 불은다. 東國輿地勝覽을 찾어 보면 「日長山은 廣州 南 5리에 있으니 일명은 南漢山이라」 하였고 따로이 「日長山城은 新羅시절의 畫長城인 바 文武王의 축조다. 안에 여섯 우물과 시내가 있으며 주위가 86,800척이요, 높이가 24척의 石築」이라 하였다.
이것으로 보면 新羅가 百濟를 병합한 뒤에는 南漢山에 산성을 쌓고 畫長山城이라고도 불은 것이다. 다음에 三國史記를 참조해 보면 『溫祚王 13년에 왕도의 늙은 할미化하여 산아히 되고 다섯 호랑이 성에 들어오며 王母가 薨하거늘 왕이 신하에게 일으는 말슴이 「국가의 동편엔 樂浪이 있고 북편엔 靺鞨이 있어 변방을 침노하야 편안한 날이 없는 중, 허물며 이 사이는 요물이 자주 뵈이고 국모조차 돌아가시니 형세- 편안치 못한지라 반드시 장차 도읍을 옮기려니와 내, 어제 漢水 남편을 순시해 보니 땅이 기름지고 좋은지라 마땅이 그 곳에 도읍하여 써 오래 편안함을 도모하리라 하고」 7월에 漢山에 나아가 柵을 세워 慰禮城의 民戶를 옮기게 하고 9월에 城闕을 세우다』 라 적혀 있다.
이것으로 보면 新羅가 南漢山에 畫長城을 쌓기 이전 675년에 百濟는 河南 慰禮城 곧 지금 稷山에서 漢江 남안인 南漢山城 일대를 중심으로 하여 廣州 古邑에 도읍을 정하였든 것이다. 邇來 近肖古王이 北韓에 도읍을 옮기기까지 근 400년 동안 南漢 廣州는 百濟의 서울이였다.
암커나 南漢山은 北漢山과 함께 길고 긴 2000년의 유구한 역사를 가진 크나 큰 고적의 터로 저 新羅의 慶州, 高句麗의 平壤城과 어울려 솟발같이 삼국시대를 일우어 가지고 천하를 호령하든 곳이다.
흥망 2000년, 역사의 책장을 뒤적여 보면 이 곳에 활시위가 울고 말굽 소리가 요란하여 번적어리는 긴칼 날카로운 蛇矛鎗으로 龍驤虎搏 丈夫의 피를 뛰게 하는 크나 큰 전쟁 수효만이 40여 회다.
그러나 근세에 들어와 최후의 잊지 못할 전쟁, 씻지 못할 哀史는 李朝 仁祖王때 丙子胡亂의 구슬픈＜111＞ 전쟁 南漢 籠城이 그것이다. 누구나 南漢山을 찾는 이로 이 구슬픈 이야기를 듣고, 회고의 눈물을 자아내지 아니하고 백일 것이냐.
南漢山은 百濟의 古都로도 이름이 높으려니와 지금으로부터 300년 전 丙子胡亂이 있기 때문에 더욱이 우리의 가슴을 뛰놀게 한다.
李朝 仁祖大王 14년 丙子에 滿洲 일각에 새로이 불붙듯 일어난 愛親覺羅 누루하치(弩爾哈赤) 金國은 그 아들 홍타시(皇太極, 또는 洪多時) 때에 일으러 국호를 大淸이라 하고 한(汗)은 스스로 황제라 일커르매 崇德이라 年號를 고첬다.
때마침 朝鮮에는 왕비 仁烈王后 韓씨가 승하하신 때라 滿洲에서는 그해 2월에 국상을 吊表한다 표명하고 使者 龍骨大 副將 馬保大는 從卒 190여명을 거느리고 朝鮮에 나왔다. 그들은 한편으로 吊喪을 표하는 동시에 한편으론 大淸 황제를 또는 신흥 淸國을 국제적으로 승인한 뒤에 朝鮮도 이 淸國에 귀의하여 藩臣 노릇을 하라 요구하였다.
그러나 이때 朝鮮 조정이 淸國을 보는 안목은 극히 오만하였든 것이다. 여태것 淸國은 일개 建州衛野人의 무리 오랑캐로 太祖, 太宗 이래에 여러번 조공을 들어 왔든 허잘 것 없는 눈 아래 보는 무리이였든 것이다.
이러한 야인의 족속으로 세월이 바뀌고 시세가 다르다 할망정 별안간 황제요, 천자라는 것은 기맥히는 소리였다. 더욱이 朝鮮으로 하여금 藩臣 노릇을 하다싶이 하라 하니 될 번이나 한 소리냐.
마침내 조정 의론은 물 끓듯 일어났다. 掌令 洪翼漢은 淸使 龍骨大를 목 베이자 주장하고 弘文館, 司諫院은 모든 학사와 諫官들도 들고 일어나 「홍타시」의 글을 불사른 뒤에 宣戰을 포고하자 날뛰였다. 모도 20과 30 熱血 義氣의 젊은 사람이였다. 그러나 그들에겐 앞뒤를 돌아보고 料量해 볼 노숙한 정치적 요량은 적었다.
仁祖 大王도 마침내 여론에 휩쓸려 *서는 받지 않고 吊祭만은 아니 받을 수 없다 하여 禁橋에 헛장막을 치게 하고 허위로 吊喪을 받게 하였다. 龍骨大의 從者가 하도 많고 하는 수작이 당돌하니 대궐 안에 들였다가는 무슨 사변을 일으킬가 염려한 까닭이다.
龍骨大도 몇일 묵는 동안에 朝鮮 조정의 물 끓듯 일어나는 여론을 모르지는 아니했다. 좌우간 나온 길이니＜112＞ 국서 전달은 고만 두드라도 吊喪이나 하려 하였든 것이다. 이때만해도 그들은 朝鮮의 실정에 어둡기 때문에 꽤 朝鮮을 두려워 했든 것이다. 급기야 致祭를 들이러 吊祭場에 당도해 보니 吊祭場은 대궐이래야 할 것이 금충교 다리 위요, 祭床 交椅에는 허위다. 三拜 九叩頭 절을 하면서도 잔득 의심을 품고 있는 중에 바람에 펄덕 帳 자락이 움직이매 장막 뒤에는 무기를 가진 刀斧手들이 埋伏하여 있었다. 이것은 조선 조정의 혹시 龍骨大들이 난을 일으킬가 저허하여 미리 감추어 두었든 군사였다.

籠城 격전 45일에 드디여 상감은 藍袍 玉帶(원래 紅袍가 정복이연만 항복하는 죄인이라 하여 藍袍을 입으시게 한 것이다.) 로 萬古 恨을 품으신채 三田渡 胡陣에 나아가 降書를 올리시니 실로 朝鮮史에 처음되는 천고의 屆辱이였다. 이 날 상감은 환궁을 허락하고 三學士는 斥和臣이라 하여 胡陣에 묵껴 가고 鳳林大君, 麟坪大君과, 세자 저하는 볼모로 瀋陽까지 가시게 되었다.
이것이 근세사에 가장 구슬픈 南漢山城의 장면이다.
봄 볓이 다스럽고 가을 단풍 고을 녁에 短笻을 이끌어 廣津 橋畔에 나아가면 남으로 日長連山이 천공에 孤線을 그어 영기를 배아트며 가로 누었다. 이것이 바로 이 장황한 이야기의 주인공인 南漢山城이다. 서울서 도보로는 60리요 근자의 간편한 교통기관을 이용한다면 東大門 軌道會社에서 廣壯里로 가는 기동차를 타고 廣津 津橋까지 나아가 風細里 土城이란 百濟의 유적을 구경한 뒤에 上一리 소학교 앞을 거처 新長里 德豐里를 지나 光池院까지 가서 동문(松岩亭)으로 해서 산성에 들어갈 수도 있고 좀 더 걸음을 아낀다면 서울 東大門에서 軌道車를 타고 *島에서＜117＞ 내려서 강가에서 松坡가는 똑닥선을 타고 언덕에 네려 松坡鎭에서 三田渡 한(汗)의 碑를 뜻 깊이 읽은 뒤에 바루 남문으로 들어서 산성에 들 수도 있고 아주 걷지를 아니하랴면 서울서 京電 뻐스나 탁시를 타고 앉으면 바로 산성 안 演武館까지 다을 수 있는 것이다.
산성 동문을 들어서면 왼편에 池水堂, 觀魚亭, 이 있고 오른편에 長慶寺, 顯節祠의 朱欄畵閣이 솔밭 사이 은은하다.
池水堂은 顯宗 때 留守 李世華의 건축한 것으로 당 좌우에 세 곳 연못을 파고 이 집을 지었다.
그러나 지금은 연못이 둘밖에 남지 않었다.
觀魚亭은 池水堂 서편 첫째 연못 섬 속에 지은 六角堂이니 純祖 때 留守 金載瓚이 건설한 것으로 南漢忒에 金載瓚의 「觀魚亭 小識」이 있다.
동북방 長慶寺는 일즉이 丙子胡亂 때도 일홈이 높든 長慶寺다. 開運寺, 長慶寺들의 아홉 군데 절이 있어 이 절에는 입산 수도하는 불제자들이 法悅에 들어 三寶들 받들건만 하루 아침에 나라에 급한 일이 있으면 검은 長衫에 칼을 빼여 들고 전장터에 나갈 僧兵을 길으든 곳이다. 開運寺에는 중의 總攝이 있고 그 밑에는 승병 370명이 평시에도 칼 쓰고 말 달리고 교련하고 있는 곳이다. 그러나 지금엔 長慶寺 한아만이 남었다. 사면은 躑躅花와 松林으로 둘러 쌓았다. 鎭南樓에 앉어 봄에 화려하게 터지는 철죽꽃을 바라보는 멋도 좋으려니와 盛夏 불같은 더위에 松風을 받어 드리는 快味 더 좋을게다.
顯節祠는 저 유명한 丙子胡亂의 三學士 洪翼漢, 尹集, 吳達濟 세 분 斥和臣을 모신 곳으로 三學士는 당시 胡廷에 붓들려 가서 殺身成仁한 어른들이다. 肅宗大王 때 대왕은 三學士의 충의를 가상이 생각하시여 留守 李時白으로 이 사당을 지어 그 영혼을 위로하였다. 白日을 끠뚤는 충의- 지금까지 혁혁하다. 250여년 전의 건축으로 매년 춘추에 節祀를 들인다.
조금 더 들어가면 演武館이다. 옛적엔 鍊武堂이라 불렀다 한다. 肅宗 朝때 守禦使 金在好를 시켜 이 집을 修築한 뒤에 「鍊兵館」이란 扁額을 부첬다가, 그 뒤 正祖大王때 「守禦營」이라 고쳤다 한다. 군사를 조련시키든 터다.
얼마를 걸어 올러 가면 行宮 터가 나온다. 행궁 터 곧＜118＞ 윗 대궐(上闕)은 西將臺가 있는 日長山 아래 지금 면사무소가 있는 후편 일대다. 仁祖 甲子 축성과 동시에 건축한 것으로 守禦使 李曙가 牧使 柳琳에게 명하여 좌우 房軒과 翼廊 등 범 70여채를 건축하여 甲子年 이듬해 乙丑에 가서 준공한 것이요 아랫 대궐(下闕)은 윗 대궐 三門 밖에 있어 역시 한때에 건축한 것으로 곧 宣化堂이 되는 것이다. 윗 대궐 左牆 밖에 坐勝堂이 있다. 이것은 純祖朝 丁丑에 留守 沈象奎가 건설한 것으로 이 세 건물은 官廨 중 중요한 건축이라 하여 李朝중엽 이후엔 留守와 府尹의 衙門으로 사용했다. 이 가운데 특필할 것은 上闕과 下闕은 丙子胡亂때 仁祖大王께서 40여일, 臨御하신 유서 깊은 行在所다.
丙子胡亂을 격은 뒷 날 孝宗大王의 만고 恨을 먹음으시고 북벌을 경영하시자 돌연 뜻 아니한 *腫으로 돌아가시여 驪州 英陵에 고요이 잠드시니 뒤 임금 肅宗, 英祖, 正祖는 驪州 陵行 길 回路에 仁祖와 孝宗을 우러러 생각하시여 한번식 이 행궁에 듭시여 주무시고 回駕하섰다. 이것이 肅宗 14년 戊辰, 英祖 6년 庚戌, 正祖 3년 己亥의 일이다.
坐勝堂은 大正 8년(1919년) 京安里로 옮기여 지금은 경찰서로 사용한다. 행궁 기타 중요한 건물도 군청 이전과 함께 拂下되어 오늘 남은 것은 극히 적다.
산성 서문 안으로 발을 옮기면 百濟 시조 溫祚王을 모신 사당이 있다. 곧 日長山 서북 中腹에 있다. 丙子胡亂 때 仁祖大王이 이 산에 籠城해 게실 때 지으신 제전이다. 廣州 南漢의 옛 주인을 생각하시여 그 영혼을 위로하는 의미였다. 이 곳에는 당시 守禦使로 있든 李曙도 配享을 하였다. 仁祖께서 溫王廟를 짓고 祭享을 지내신 다음 몇일 안 돼서 이상하게도 仁祖 大王 꿈에는 百濟 溫王이 顯夢을 하였다. 「대왕이 내 사당을 지어 주시니 진실로 감사하오,」 하고 사례를 들인 뒤에 「그러나 나 혼자 있기 몹시 쓸쓸하오, 청컨대 대왕의 신하 李曙를 나에게 보내 주시요」 하였다. 仁祖大王은 꿈 속에 快하게 허락하섰다. 「어렵지 않은 일이오이다」하고, 그러나 깨여보니 南柯一夢이였다.
몇일 뒤에 守禦使 李曙가 벌안간 앓기 시작했다. 싸움에 승부가 결단되지 않은 이 판에 李曙의 병은 적지 않게 낭패를 주었다. 마침내 李曙는 병들어 죽었다. 仁祖大王이 전쟁 중에 명장 하나를 잃은 것도 한이려니와 꿈이 하도 영악하매 마침내 李曙를 溫王廟에＜119＞ 配享시키섰다는 구슬픈 전설이 있다.
다시 숨을 헐덕어려 산성의 최고봉을 올라가면 이것이 곧 453米突이나 되는 日長山의 꼭댁이다. 古色이 창연한 西將臺가 있고 臺上엔 無忘樓 석字를 쓴 현판이 달려 있다. 丙子胡亂을 잊지 말자는 뜻이다. 40평이나 되는 넓은 터전을 차지한 집으로 아래 윗 층 장엄한 건물이다. 지금으로부터 英祖朝때 留守 李箕鎭이 건축한 것이니 현존한 산성 속에 가장 그 규모가 큰 집이다. 守禦使가 具軍服 蜜花 貝纓으로 壯臺에 올라 칼을 빼여 수만 군병을 호령하든 곳이다.
누구나 한번 이 無忘樓 우에 올으면 호방하고 壯快하여 스스로 발을 굴러 豁然한 흉금으로 기-ㄴ 회바람을 불어 보고 싶지 않을 이 없을 것이다.
탁 열려진 眼界는 一眸에 京城, 楊州, 楊平, 龍仁, 高陽의 모든 산천이 네려다 보이고 희멀금 仁川 바다엔 夕照가 끓어 올라 시뻙언 불덩이 같다. 다시 눈을 가까이 돌리여 발 아래 漢江을 구버보면 무심한 듯 유심하고 한가로운 듯 바뿐 두어척 漁艇이 돗대에, 바람을 배불리 실고 그림같이 도라든다. 손을 들어 漢江의 상류를 지점하는 동안 아하, 저기가 三田渡 松坡 汗의 비가 선 곳일세 할 제 丙子胡亂이 눈 앞에 역력히 버러진 모양, 三學士가 뵈이는 양 崔鳴吉이 뵈이는 양 淸陰 金尙容이 뵈이는 양, 仁祖大王이 뵈이는 양 사나이면서도 한 줄기 비분 강개한 눈물을 뿌리지 않을 수 없다.
