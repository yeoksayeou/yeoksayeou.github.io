Publication Date: 1935년 12월 01일
Article Title: 첫치마 (First Skirt)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

First Skirt
						P'ain
O cloud crossing the ridge,
Where are you flowing to?

If you must become rain
And fall anyway,

Pass by the pumpkin patch.
My love Suni is there.

If the rain wets even her first skirt,
That child might cry and turn away. <292>

**Summary**

This short poem expresses a speaker's tender concern for his beloved, Suni. Addressing a cloud, the speaker asks that if it must rain, it avoid the pumpkin patch where Suni is, fearing that getting her "first skirt" wet will make her cry and turn away. The poem captures a moment of youthful affection and protective feeling, set against a simple rural backdrop. For historians, the piece offers a glimpse into sentimental expressions of the era, the common use of pastoral imagery, and provides examples of Korean language usage and spelling conventions from the mid-1930s, such as '밧' for '밭' and '직시면' for '적시면'.
