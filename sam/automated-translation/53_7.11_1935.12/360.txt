Choosing "Night's Touch" (밤의 感觸)

Pak P'aryang (朴八陽), Yŏsu (麗水)

I choose it for its conception rather than its present state. It would shine much more if more refined letters were used. In short, since poetry is the "art of words," be sensitive to "words" (or the letters that record them). I bless this author's future and introduce this as the work of a new writer.<222>

Night's Touch (밤의 感觸)

Yi Yunsŏp (李允燮)

Night, oh night, the deep night is quiet.
It is like being thousands upon thousands of fathoms deep in water.

Because it was so quiet, I listened intently,
And heard the sound of footsteps
Of the rustling wind crossing the fence, treading on pumpkin leaves.

Night, oh night, the sleepless night is heart-wrenching.
It seems the person I await might come.

Because it was so heart-wrenching, I opened the window,
And the waning crescent moon silently illuminated the garden,
Bringing tears to my spiderweb-like heart.

Night, oh night, the windy night is lonely.
It is like a solitary wild goose carrying a piece of the moon on its back.

Because it was so lonely, I adjusted my pillow and closed my eyes,
And before I knew it, that person (그 대님) stole in,
Quietly set a fire in my heart, and left.<223>
<222-223>

**Summary**

Pak P'aryang introduces Yi Yunsŏp as a promising new writer, recommending his poem "Night's Touch" (밤의 感觸) while advising the author to refine his use of language, the essence of poetry. The poem itself depicts the profound quiet, anxious anticipation, sorrow, and loneliness of sleepless nights, using imagery like the deep sea, footsteps on leaves, the waning moon, and a solitary wild goose. It culminates in a poignant, perhaps dreamlike, moment where an unnamed beloved ("그 대님") enters and stirs the speaker's heart. From a historical perspective, the article showcases the process of literary mentorship and the introduction of new talent within the colonial Korean literary scene via magazines like *Samch'ŏlli*. The poem's themes of waiting, loneliness, and inner turmoil, expressed lyrically, might subtly reflect the atmosphere and sentiments prevalent among intellectuals or artists during the Japanese colonial period, offering a glimpse into personal emotions within that broader context.

「밤의 感觸」을 골느고

麗水 朴八陽

現在보다도 그 搆想을 取한다. 좀더 洗鍊된 글짜를 썻스면 훨신 빗날것이다. 요컨대 詩는 「말의 藝術」이니 「말」(또는 그것을 記錄한 글자)에 대해서 敏感하라. 이 作者의 將來를 祝福하며 新人의 作品으로서 紹介한다.<222>

밤의 感觸

李允燮

밤도 밤도 깁흔 밤은 고요하외다.
천길 만길 물속과도 갓사외다.

하두 고요하기에 귀를 기우렷더니
울타리 넘는 소슬 바람의
호박닙 위 거니는 발자욱 소리 들닙듸다.

밤도 밤도 잠 못 이루는 밤은 안타까외다.
기다리는 사람이 올 듯도 하외다.

하두 안타까워 창을 여럿더니
이즈러진 그믐달이 말업시 뜰을 비최여
거미줄가튼 내 가슴 속을 눈물지게 하더이다.

밤도 밤도 바람이는 밤은 외롭사외다.
쪼각달 등에 진 외기럭이와도 갓사외다.

하도 외로워 벼개 고치고 눈을 감엇더니
어느듯 몰으게 그 대님이 숨어들어와
가만이 가슴에다 불을 질너 노코 갑데다.<223>
