Internal and External Situation

List of the Wealthy in Seoul (Chang'an), -As Revealed by Individual Income Tax Amounts-

Who are the rich people in Seoul (Chang'an)? If one searches through the east, west, south, and north corners of Seoul where 60,000 households live, one can see huge houses like the Epang Palace towering here and there as if to say, "Look at me!" These are all the residences of the so-called millionaires. However, there are also rich people whose houses are large but empty inside, and rich people who are substantially wealthy inside but keep their exteriors modest. Thus, one cannot possibly know the extent of their wealth based on their residences.
But now there is a fortunate thing, which is the income tax amounts of these people from an investigation by certain quarters, allowing us to glimpse their true substance. That is, how many hundreds of thousands of won did each of these rich people earn during this one year? It is reasonable to consider this income amount as roughly 10% of their capital. For instance, if someone had an income of 300,000 won during Shōwa 10 (1935), it is doubtless that they possess assets of at least 3 million won.
Enough explanation!
Generally, Mr. Min Tae-sik (閔大植) is the number one richest person in the capital (Chang'an). This year, he had an income of 260,000 won. Following him are Messrs. Im Chong-sang (林宗相) and Kim Yŏn-su (金䄵洙). With Marquis Pak Yŏng-hyo's (朴泳孝侯爵) 40,000 won as the minimum, the income tax amounts of about 22 prominent wealthy individuals are as follows. <18>

Tosan An Ch'ang-ho Completes Prison Term

Mr. An Ch'ang-ho (安昌浩), pen name Tosan (島山), whose parole period ended on November 18th, has now become a person of complete freedom, a liberated person. He is currently in the mountains at Sudŏk Temple in P'yŏngnam (South P'yŏngan Province), but it is said he will soon come down from the mountain for good and arrive in Seoul.

Mr. Hong Yang-myŏng to Paris?

Mr. Hong Yang-myŏng (洪陽明), a contemporary civilization critic and foreign news editor at the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), harbors the intention to study abroad in Paris (巴里). He has been diligently studying French by knocking on the door of a certain French scholar in Sŏdaemun-jŏng (西大門町) while applying for a travel permit. As soon as the travel permit is issued, he plans to soon pack and depart for a stay of several years in France.

Recent Personnel Movements

Critic Kim Kyŏng-jae (金璟載)씨 set out from Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) for North Manchuria (北滿) in late October to reclaim several hundred *ilgyŏng* (日耕) of farmland he had previously purchased in the North Manchuria area. He traveled via Tomun (圖們), Yongjŏng (龍井), and Yŏngil (延吉) and is expected to return around the end of this year.
Ms. Pak In-dŏk (朴仁德女史), who had long been planning to depart as the Korean representative to the World Christian Conference held in America (米洲), left Kyŏngsŏng on November 7th, about two weeks later than scheduled due to a breakdown of the reserved steamship on the Pacific route, setting out on the long-awaited grand journey to America!
Mr. Yi Yŏng (李英) - Mr. Yi, who had long stayed at the Chosŏn Education Association (朝鮮敎育協會) in Sup'yo-dong, Seoul, recently went down to his hometown, Pukch'ŏng (北靑).
Mr. Chŏn Il (全一) - While staying in Seoul, he took his family and went down to his hometown in Hambuk (North Hamgyŏng Province) for good.
Ms. Hŏ Yŏng-suk (許英肅여사) went to Tokyo (東京) to study, as previously reported. Ms. Hŏ is studying Obstetrics and Gynecology at the Red Cross Maternity Hospital (赤十字産院) in Tokyo. After staying in Tokyo for 2-3 years, she plans to return <19> establish a maternity hospital, and strive herself for the cause of child health advocacy.
Mr. Yun Paeng-nam (尹白南) - Mr. Yun, a novelist and also a businessman, went over to Tokyo on November 8th on some business. He has taken up lodging at the Chosŏn YMCA (朝鮮YMCA) in Kanda-ku (神田區), Tokyo, and plans to stay for about two weeks.
Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik (朴興植) - Mr. Pak, the president of Hwasin (和信), is reportedly going to Tokyo and Osaka around late December. Mr. Kim Ki-dŏk (金基德) - After suffering from illness for a long time, he is said to have recently recovered well.

Rumor of a Two Million Won Gold Mining Company Emergence

Led by Mr. Pak Yong-un (朴龍雲) of P'yŏngbuk (North P'yŏngan Province) and other major mine operators, a large gold mining company (金鑛會社) with a capital of 2 million won is currently in the process of being established. Since even the promoters' meeting has already been held, it is reported that it will likely be finalized soon. One of the facilitators (産婆役) is said to be Mr. Yi Sŏng-hwan (李晟煥), formerly of the Ch'ŏndogyo Farmers' Association (天道敎農民社).

Pae Ku-ja's Performance Earns Four Thousand Won

The opera and dance troupe of dancer Ms. Pae Ku-ja (裴龜子) performed as the opening act at the Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場) in Takezoe-chō (竹添町), managed by her husband Hong Sun-ŏn (洪淳彦). They performed for ten days from November 3rd to 12th and returned. The box office revenue for the 10 days achieved favorable success, surpassing 4,000 won.

Hwasin New Building is Four Hundred Thousand Won

What draws the attention of people passing through the Chongno (鍾路) intersection in Seoul these days is the model diagram of the Hwasin (和信) new building erected in the middle of the crossroads. As for how this magnificent 7-story (including basement) structure is being built, Mr. Han Hak-su (韓學洙), owner of the Han Cheong Building (韓靑딍), put up 300,000 won, and Hwasin put up 100,000 won, making a total of 400,000 won for construction. Hwasin has signed a 15-year permanent lease contract for this building being constructed by Mr. Han, with the rent being 2,000 won per month. The construction is expected to be completed by June next summer.

Hwasin's Ten Million Won Sales Plan

Hwasin has recently established a plan for annual sales of 10 million won. The content of the plan is to achieve 4 million won from the Hwasin Department Store, 4 million won from its chain stores (連鎖店), and 2 million won from the directly managed branch store (直營支店) in P'yŏngyang (平壤), thus generating annual sales of 10 million won. It is observed that for this plan to be fully implemented, the sales floor must be expanded upon completion of the 6-story new building under construction at the Chongno intersection, and the number of chain stores must increase from the current 360-odd to over 500.

Na Un-gyu Studio Establishment

Famous actor Mr. Na Un-gyu (羅雲奎), who expressed the sentiment of this land <20> 100 percent in the film "Arirang" (아리랑) and moved many people, recently produced a piece called "The Village Across the River" (江건너 마을) and then went back to Tokyo. He intends to purchase an excellent sound recording machine (錄音機), bring it back, and devote all his energy to shooting superior talkie films. And an even more surprising development is that he is reportedly preparing to establish a studio (스타지오) in the Ch'ŏngnyangni (淸凉里) area outside the city.

Emergence of a Seven Million Won Grand Museum

By merging the Government-General Science Museum (總督府 科學舘) located at Waesŏngdae (倭城臺) below Namsan (南山) and the Government-General Museum (總督府 博物舘) within Kyŏngbok Palace (慶福宮), a large Grand Museum is expected to be established within the Government-General compound (總督府 構內) around April next spring with a huge budget (尨大한 豫算) of seven million won.
Considering that Tokyo's Ueno Museum (上野博物舘) cost only 3 million won, one can well imagine how large the scale of the Chosŏn Museum to be newly established will be.

"Ch'unhyangjŏn" First-Day Earnings

Produced by the Kyŏngsŏng Film Studio (京城撮影所), "Ch'unhyangjŏn" (春香傳), which attracted much public attention as Korea's first talkie film, premiered at Tansŏngsa (團成社) Theater in Seoul. Despite admission fees being set at two tiers, 1 won and 70 chŏn, the first day's income reportedly reached over 1,580 won. Compared to the example of major Tokyo theaters earning only 3,000-4,000 won on a good day, this can only be called extraordinarily good results. Anyway, based on this example, it appears there is ample possibility for Korean films to become profitable in the future. Since the total production cost for the aforementioned "Ch'unhyangjŏn" is said to be around 8,000 won, most of it was likely recouped during its 2-week extended run in Seoul.
Also, another film titled "Passion Flowing in the Milky Way" (銀河에 흐르는 情熱) premiered at the Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇장) and reportedly earned over 1,300 won on its first day. Thus, one cannot but say the future of Korean cinema is bright.

Is the Takarazuka Revue Coming?

Rumor has it that the Takarazuka Revue (寶塚歌劇團) from Osaka (大阪), managed by Kobayashi Ichizō (小林一三), might come to perform in Seoul within this year. This is because the Kyŏngsŏng Civic Hall (京城府民舘), currently being built on T'aep'yŏng-t'ong (太平通) in Seoul for 350,000 won, will soon be completed. As its inaugural performance, some manager (마네자-) is said to have formulated a plan to invite the most popular Takarazuka Girls' Opera Troupe. However, to bring the troupe over, the daily expenses and fees would have to be at least 2,000-3,000 won. Considering the audience base of Seoul with its population of 400,000, one cannot but doubt from a financial perspective (珠盤上으로) whether this performance will be realized.<21>

**Summary**

This "Samcheolli Confidential Room" column from December 1935 compiles various news briefs. It begins with a list ranking Seoul's wealthiest individuals based on their estimated income tax, led by landowner Min Tae-sik. It then reports on the release of independence activist An Ch'ang-ho (Tosan), potential travel plans of intellectuals like Hong Yang-myŏng, personnel movements of figures like Kim Kyŏng-jae, Pak In-dŏk, and Yun Paeng-nam, and business developments including a rumored 2 million won gold mining company, the successful run of dancer Pae Ku-ja's troupe, the construction and expansion plans of Hwasin Department Store, and plans for a new film studio by Na Un-gyu. Cultural news includes the box office success of early Korean talkie films "Ch'unhyangjŏn" and "Passion Flowing in the Milky Way," plans for a large new Government-General museum, and speculation about a Seoul performance by Japan's Takarazuka Revue.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a fascinating glimpse into the elite social strata (wealth list), burgeoning cultural scene (cinema, theatre), economic activities (mining, retail), and movements of prominent figures in colonial Seoul during the mid-1930s. The details on income, business ventures like Hwasin's expansion and Na Un-gyu's studio, the financial success of early Korean talkies, and the large-scale colonial project of building a new museum provide valuable data points for understanding the socio-economic and cultural landscape under Japanese rule. The mention of An Ch'ang-ho's release and Hong Yang-myŏng's potential travel also subtly touches upon the political climate and intellectual currents of the time.

들니는 말에 小林一三이 經營하는 大阪의 寶塚歌劇團이 금년 안으로 서울에 公演하려 올듯하다는 바, 그것은 지금 35만원을 가지고 서울 太平通에 짓고 잇는 京城府民舘이 不遠에 落成되는데, 그의 첫 興行으로, 엇든 마네자-가 나서서 가장 人氣잇슬 寶塚少女歌劇團을 招聘할 案을 세운 것이라 한다, 그러나 그 일행을 끄으려 오자면 적어도 매일 經費와 報酬가 2,3천원씩은 되어야할 터인즉, 40만 인구를 가진 서울의 관객층을 想考할 때에 珠盤上으로 이 興行이 實現될는지 엇덜는지 疑向이라 아니할 수 없다.<21>
