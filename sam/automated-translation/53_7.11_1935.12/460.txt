Article Title: 가을밤 (Autumn Night)
Author: 麗水
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Autumn Night
						Yŏsu

The solitude (寂寥) of the autumn night
Seeps into my heart like cold water.

An autumn night, 3 AM, waking from an unprecedented dream
Where a petal of a white lotus flower falls soundlessly
Onto the cold stone pavement (鋪道).

New wisdom (叡智) seems to rise
Within my head like transparent crystal.

Around this time, under a poor house roof, suffering through the night,
The hot head of some lonely widow's (홀어머니) son
Will also cool, and he will finally regain consciousness and open his eyes.<285>

**Summary**

This poem by Yŏsu describes the profound quiet and clarity experienced late on an autumn night. The speaker feels the night's solitude seep into them, leading to a moment of insight, likened to clear crystal. This personal introspection then extends outwards in empathy towards the suffering of others, specifically imagining a sick child under a poor roof finally finding relief as dawn approaches. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects a common theme in colonial-era Korean literature: finding moments of personal insight and expressing subtle social consciousness (awareness of poverty and suffering) within a non-political, lyrical framework, perhaps as a way to navigate censorship or channel feelings of helplessness.

가을밤의 寂寥가 나의 心臟으로
찬 물과가티 숨여든다

흰 蓮꽃의 한조각이 소리도 업시
찬 돌 鋪道우에 떠러지는
전에 업든 꿈을 꾸고 깨인 가을밤 午前세時

새로운 叡智가 나의 머리속에
透明한 水晶과 가티 떠오르는 듯도 하다

이맘때쯤, 가난한 집웅 밋에 밤새여 알는
어느 외로운 홀어머니 아들의 더운 머리도
싸늘하게 식고 비로소 정신을 차려 눈을 뜰 때다<285>
