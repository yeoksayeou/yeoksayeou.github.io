A Gentleman Costs Over 410 Yen, A Lady Around 500 Yen: How Much Money Does It Take to Make a Neat Gentleman and Lady?

The number of neat gentlemen and ladies bustling back and forth on Seoul's Chongno crossroads is countless, and almost all of them step out cleanly dressed in Western attire. While we cannot fathom their inner thoughts, it is an interesting task to find out how much money was spent on their Western clothes. Let's ask for these prices at the Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店), which supplies the latest popular items for young men and women.

**Summary**

This 1935 article from Samch'ŏlli magazine details the cost of fully outfitting a "modern" gentleman and lady in Seoul, based on prices from the Hwasin Department Store. It provides itemized lists for a Western-style gentleman (totaling 410.55 Yen), a Western-style lady (516.05 Yen), and a lady dressed in Korean attire (344.85 Yen). The lists include clothing, undergarments, accessories like watches, pens, and handbags, and cosmetics (specifically Coty brand for women). The article reflects the increasing adoption of Western clothing styles ('Yangjang') in urban colonial Korea, while also providing a comparison point with the cost of traditional Korean clothing ('Chosŏn'ot'), noting that Western style, especially for women including expensive items like fur shawls and imported cosmetics, was significantly more expensive. For historians, this piece offers valuable data on consumer culture, the perceived costs associated with modernity and Westernization vs. tradition, specific goods available (like British suits, Coty cosmetics), and the economic stratification in 1930s Seoul. The use of loanwords and specific fabric names (Shantung crepe, Shusu satin, Kōshū silk) also provides linguistic and material culture insights.
