When the Garden Peas Bloom
						P'ain
1.

When the garden peas bloom,
Suni gets so happy,
Could the skylarks in the sky
chatter that much?

Could the herds of deer at Puksan Shrine
run about that much?
Laughing and chattering,
in and out all day long.

2.

When a young man passes by,
It's usual for her to hide and peek;
If someone says she's pretty,
She curses back at them,

Yet still happy about something,
Chattering away by herself;
When the garden peas bloom,
Suni gets so happy. <295>

**Summary**

This poem by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan) describes the pure joy and lively behaviour of a young girl named Suni when the garden pea flowers bloom. She is depicted as energetic and cheerful, comparing her happiness to skylarks and deer, though she also shows shyness and defensiveness around strangers. Historically, the poem offers a glimpse into the pastoral and lyrical themes prevalent in some Korean literature during the Japanese colonial period. It avoids direct political commentary, focusing instead on innocent rural life, which might reflect either a personal inclination of the author or the constraints on expression at the time. The use of vernacular language and depiction of simple emotions could be of interest to historians studying cultural life and artistic expression under colonial rule.

원두꼿 필 때며는

혼자서 직거리며
