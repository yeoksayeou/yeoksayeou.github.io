A Dirge for Waning Life, (Looking Towards the Age of Fifty)
"The 80-year-old English poet Browning sang, 'Love again, young again,' wishing he could go back to his youth once more, wishing he could love once more. So, what are the feelings of the poets of this land?"

Seeing the Falling Leaves

Chŏn Yŏngt'aek

M hyŏng,
It's truly dumbfounding. For the Samch'ŏlli Company to ask me to write about my feelings looking towards fifty is just too dumbfounding an order. For some reason, when I received this request, I couldn't suppress the feelings of astonishment, regret, and resentment that arose.
I hate even hearing the word "forty," so adding the sound of "fifty" on top of that,<114> how could it not be dumbfounding? If I had researched and produced something, or saved up some money, or at least raised a son and welcomed a daughter-in-law, it might be different, but to say things like looking towards fifty to someone who is still sitting as a mere student, dreaming about what he will try to do now, how could I not be dumbfounded?
Since I stay cooped up at home, I've been living almost unaware of how the seasons changed, but the other day I heard from you, hyŏng, that the harvest is almost done. Today, I put on the padded trousers and jacket the house provided, and seeing the poplar leaves piled up thick on the roadside and only a few withered leaves left on the trees, the thought suddenly arises, "It's winter. This year is almost over too."
It seems I was too indifferent about my own life as well. So, being asked to write my feelings about looking towards fifty feels like a sharp whip striking me, saying, "You rascal, pull yourself together. How old are you now?" I feel grateful for that stinging reminder.

When this year passes, spring will come again, won't it?
I told the children, "I wish warm spring would hurry up and come. I hate winter," and hearing their reply, "Now the snow will come, and then the flowers will bloom, right?" I just laughed heartily.
But the spring of a life once gone will never come again. My friend, I try and try to remember when the springtime of this body passed, but I just don't know. Not having had one passionate love affair, not having run around a sports field like a young man, not having exercised once on the ice, on the mountains, or on the sea, not having roared like a lion before a crowd—to have reached this state, isn't it dumbfounding?
When I think about how I just sit here day and night, mulling over the same things in the same way, I get so angry and frustrated that I feel like snapping this writing brush in half right now.
M hyŏng,
Still, I simply think, "I can work for at least 10 more years. For the next 10 years, I will actively do what I want to do." "Let's live cleanly according to conscience. Let's heal this rotten world a bit. Let's love each other. At least let's love our brothers. Let's live harmoniously and justly. Let's be kind. Let's be kinder. Let's live a little warmer. If possible, let's try to love passionately. Let's love until a fire starts. Let's make this overly lonely world a bit warmer." I intend to shout and cry out to the world like this for just 10 years, and then die. Friend, there are still 10 years left, aren't we grateful?<115> Come—let's hold hands and try to achieve something in these 10 years. Let's do it. For 10 years.

When I Turn Fifty, I Intend to Return to My Old Self

Sŏgyŏng

Being asked what I would do when I turn 50 sounds to me like being told, "When you turn 50, please try to do something worthwhile." As someone who hasn't even established himself yet, I am not fit to tell people what I will do when I turn 50. I am someone who still hasn't grasped my own work.
Bound by household chores and tied down by my job, I am someone in adverse circumstances with weak resolve, unable to find much time for my heart and hands to pursue what I aspire to. When I was over 20, I thought, "When I turn 30..."; now that I've passed the 30-year mark and look towards 40, I think, "When I turn 40..." If someone like me, who still hasn't laid a foundation, turns 50, what refreshing things could I possibly do? First, if I don't create something excellent by the time I'm 40, I intend to withdraw my hand from it. Thus, I want to avoid troubling the eyes and minds of others.
Recently, I have entered such a path. I also had great interest in cinema, and it seemed I might have some talent for it, so I put my mind to it, but I decided to think about myself before fully committing to this path as well. It occurred to me that if the work I do isn't worthy, it might be better to remain a blank slate with nothing accomplished. Therefore, I have no answer to the question of what I will do when I turn 50.
Whether I will even live to 50—that's because my family's tradition is not one of longevity. However, I remember myself as a child, standing on the twilight plains, holding a canvas (캠바스) and carrying a sketch box (스케취뻑스), lost in thought. So perhaps the 50-year-old me, with graying hair, might also stand like that in the old plains.
When I was young, I was greatly influenced by Millet (밀레-).
For that reason, I still cannot forget Millet (밀레-),
And when I think of Millet, I cannot forget the me of my childhood days. Therefore, perhaps when I turn 50, I might experience a life similar to the later years of Millet (밀레-) or Cézanne (세산느). Here, I cannot help but think of the lonely life of my senior, Mr. Ch'un'gok Ko Hŭidong (春谷高羲東先生), who is around 50.<116>

I Will Become a Companion on the Path of History

Pak Sanghŭi

The year Ŭlhae! Shōwa 10 (Western Calendar 1935)
This year, for me, is an extremely precious year, and at the same time, it is also true that it is a year of curse (咀呪).

December, when this year too finally wanes! Perhaps under the sky of my northern homeland (北國), fluffy snowflakes are pouring down heavily, but at this cold window of my lodging away from home (客舍寒窓), only a chilly headwind swirls around!
Ah, this year Ŭlhae (1935) too is ending, with only a few days left!
Go quickly, year Ŭlhae! O year of curse!
Year Ŭlhae, which drove humanity into the turbulent waves of anguish (苦悶) and sorrow (悲哀) through the "anxiety of reality"!
Did the historical process grant you even such ruthless (!), tyrannical actions!

Time truly is flowing water!
"From this year, I'll try to live more meaningfully!"
"No matter how rough the pass, I'll try to live a more conscientious life!"
"No, I'll try to become a stronger human being!"
The day I first greeted this year, making forceful vows, has already passed by in the space of a year!
Why do I cherish this "1935," which I can never find again in my lifetime, and why do I attach myself to it!
As I send off this year, I am already halfway up the hill of life's 50 milestones (人生50路).
Having reached this midpoint of my 50 years in the world, perhaps this is why I flip through the "album" of my "past."

Truly, my past days were those of a vigorous youth whose passion and spirit surged.
I also cried out the "voice of justice" and held "ideals" that XX with the "reality" full of "contradictions."<117>
My youth, woven with discontent towards contradictions and hope for ideals, was indeed a path of hardship and a blood-stained chronicle (班史).
Was this too excessive thinking?
Or was it impudent "mischief"?
No! I will refrain from speaking now!

"I will refrain from speaking now!"
Have I become such a weak-hearted person?
Am I trying to join the pile of "honor" for the losers of reality?
But conscience exists and ideals are great! Still, in some corner of my heart, I sometimes feel the throbbing beat of my heart.
Ah! It's a dumbfounding situation!
My pathetic state (꼴악선이여!), committing day after day to a worthless life and work I don't care for!
But I force a bitter smile (쓰디쓴 우슴) onto my pale face (얼골우에).
Isn't it a much harder and braver thing to cling tenaciously within "adversity" and taste the bitter cup of reality, rather than sketching phenomena of youth imagined in "favorable circumstances"?
But this too is a heartbreaking matter!

I have jumped out of a cozy family. It has been long since I forgot about complex sexual matters (性問題).
Solely, I intend to steadily walk forward in the bitter sea of reality (苦海).
Until this tyrant of the "emergency period" (非常時), full of curse and hatred, withdraws, whenever that may be.
It's alright to meet the end of "life" (生) there!
But when I think about it, it is also the bone-chilling sorrow (悲哀) of an "intellectual" (인톄리).
The sorrow of being an "illegitimate child" (私生兒) created by this generation!

Now the year Ŭlhae (1935) is also drawing to a close. Soon, I must greet the year called "1936". The wheels of human history, turning without rest (轉轉不息), keep rolling on.<118>
Ten years ago was different, one year ago was different. Reality is changing tremendously.
Accordingly, such a huge difference has inevitably come to the "me" of my boyhood and youth!
Now, having reached the midpoint of life's 50 years, as I look towards the long period I must walk again, it is obvious (빤한 노릇) that the "world" will have changed and transformed infinitely.
- How will it change -
Before awaiting my pen, first kneel down (무릅을 꿀고) before the solemn and just stage of history and ask (무러보아라)!
I am merely a companion (伴侶者) tracing the weary path from one historical stage to the next.
Whether I reach 50!
Or 70!
Until my life ends—

"Year Ŭlhae!"
("Year 1935!")
You Neronian tyrant who left the greatest tragedy upon human history, farewell and go quickly!
(Sending off this year)<119>

**Summary**

This article from the December 1935 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* features reflections from three writers—Chŏn Yŏngt'aek, Sŏgyŏng, and Pak Sanghŭi—on approaching the age of fifty. Chŏn Yŏngt'aek expresses frustration at aging without significant accomplishments but resolves to dedicate the next ten years to living conscientiously and trying to improve the "rotten world." Sŏgyŏng feels unqualified to speak of future plans, burdened by daily life and lack of artistic achievement, contemplating a return to youthful artistic inspirations like Millet and Cézanne, while acknowledging the "lonely life" of elder artist Ko Hŭidong. Pak Sanghŭi reflects on the passage of time, particularly the difficult year 1935 ("year of curse"), lamenting his unfulfilled youthful ideals and the harsh realities of the current "emergency period." He resigns himself to being a "companion on the path of history," enduring the hardships until his life ends.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its blend of personal anxieties about aging and career with palpable undercurrents of the socio-political despair of colonial Korea in the mid-1930s. Chŏn's desire to "heal this rotten world" and Pak's explicit references to the "curse" of 1935, the "tyrant" of the "emergency period," the "sorrow of an 'intellectual'," and being a "child of the times" (사생아 - illegitimate child) offer glimpses into the oppressive atmosphere and intellectual disillusionment under tightening Japanese rule and militarization. Pak Sanghŭi's piece, especially with its likely censored passage ("XX여는"), strongly hints at resistance or critique of the political situation, making it valuable for understanding the constrained intellectual climate.

참 기맥히는 일이구려. 三千里社에서 날더러 오십을 바라보는 감상을 쓰라고 하는 것은 너무도 기맥히는 주문을 하는 구려. 웬일인지 나는 이 주문을 받을 때에 기맥히고 원통한 생각과 분한 생각이 이러남을 금할 수 없었오.
「사십」이란 말도 듯기 실흔데 게다가 「오십」소리를 하니<114> 어찌 기맥히지 아니 하겠오. 그래도 무얼 하나 연구해서 내놓았다든지 돈이라도 좀 모아놓았다던지 하다 못해 아들이라도 길러서 며누리라도 마젔다면 모르지마는 아직도 일개 서생으로 앉어서 이제 무얼해 보겠다고 꿈꾸고 있는 사람보고 오십을 바라보느니 어쩌니 하니 어찌 기가 맥히지를 아니하겠오.

하면서 따끔하게 채찍으로 때려주는 것을 고맙게 생각이 됩니다.

五十이 되면 녯날나로 도라가려 합니다

夕影

50이 되면 무엇을 하겠느냐는 말슴은 내게는 50이 되거들랑 보람있는 일을 해 보구료하는 말같이 들립니다. 아직까지 서지 못한 내가 여러 사람에게 나는 50이 되면 무엇을 하겠오 하기에 마땅치 못한 사람이요. 지금까지도 내 일을 붓잡지 않은 사람입니다.
집안살림에 엉매이고 직업에 발목이 잡힌 나는 내가 뜻한 일에 맘이 가고 손이 갈 틈을 많이 얻지 못하는, 말하자면 역경에 있고 노력이 약한 나입니다. 나는 20이 넘었을 때는 30이 되면 하였고 30고개를 넘어서서 40을 바라보게 된 지금에는 40이 되면 하고 지금까지도 기초를 세우지 못한 사람이 50이 된들 무엇으로 그리 시원한 노릇을 하겠읍니까. 우선 나는 40까지 무엇이나 훌륭한 것을 맨드러내지 하하면 거기서 손을 떼이랴 합니다. 그래서 남의 눈과 마음를 어지럽게 하지 않으려 합니다.
근일에 와서 나는 그러한 길로 들어섰읍니다. 영화에 대하여서도 많은 흥미를 가지고 또 어디인지 내게 거기에 대한 소질이 있는 것도 같아서 마음을 써 보았으나 이것 역시 이 길로 아조 나아가기 전 나룰 생각해 보기로 하였읍니다. 오히려 내가 하는 일이 일다웁지 못하면 아무 것도 해 놓은 것이 없이 백지로 있는 것이 나을 것 같은 생각이 든 것입니다. 그러니 50이 되면 무엇을 하겠오 하고 묻는 말에는 나는 대답할 말이 없읍니다.
내가 50을 살게 될지-그것은 우리집 수명의 전통이 오래 살지 못하는 까닭입니다. 그러나 어렸을 때 황혼의 광야 우에 「캠바스를 들고 「스케취뻑스」를 메고 서서 명상에 잠기든 나를 생각합니다. 그래서 머리털이 히뜩히뜩해질 50머리의 내가 또한 옛 광야에 그와 같이 서있을는지도 모릅니다.
나는 어렸을 때 「밀레-」의 영향을 많이 받었읍니다.
그런 까닭에 지금도 「밀레-」를 잊을 수가 없고
그 밀레-를 생각할 때 옛날 어렸을 때의 나를 잊을 수가 없읍니다. 그러니 내가 50이 되면 「밀레-」나 「세산느」의 말년의 생애와 같은 생애를 맛보게 될른지도 모릅니다. 여기서 나는 나의 선배인 50境인 春谷高羲東先生의 쓸쓸한 생애를 생각못게 됩니다.<116>
