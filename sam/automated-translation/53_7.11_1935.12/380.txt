Collection of Works by Newcomers Recommended by Various Houses - Recommended by Yi Ha-yun, The Beach (Poem)
Afterthoughts on Selecting "The Beach" and "Korean Sentiment"

Yi Ha-yun

Other than these two pieces, "September Beach" (九月의 海水浴場) and "Korean Sentiment" (朝鮮의 情調), I have no memory of reading any other works by Mr. Yu Sŏk-sŏng. Nevertheless, I cannot help but mention that I sense a level of skill that makes me hesitant to call Mr. Yu a newcomer.

Of course, it's impossible to rashly offer a critical assessment of the poet as a whole after seeing just one or two poems, and it is difficult to publicly release a recommendation without any knowledge of the author. However, I will entrust rigorous critique to you, the readers, and simply try to jot down a few impressions I felt.

"September Beach" - It wouldn't be an overstatement to say it's a fine piece of landscape poetry, flowing down without any hindrance. In the first stanza, things like "Four-colored villas" (四色의 別莊) and "Pier" (棧橋) felt a bit awkward for me to read, but it's a poem enveloped in a seemingly light yet clean sentiment, where finding such flaws is difficult. The September beach is calling me.

"Korean Sentiment" - "Korean Sentiment," I just don't want to discard it. There's something in it that makes our reading hearts swell, causing us to overlook any minor flaws as poetry.

Based solely on these two poems, I want to hold ample promise for this poet's future. To be honest, Mr. Yu is one of the poets who fall within the scope of those I can appreciate.<226>

September Beach - One Day at Songdowŏn -

Yu Sŏk-sŏng

Midday in September, when fragments of sunlight fall—
Four-colored villas doze, sitting on the sandy field,
And the pier lies across the blue sea water, stretching its back.

In the pine forest, shadows wander alone on the benches,
And by the seaside, a fisherman, head bowed, mends his net.
Seagulls, who lost their hometown all summer long...
Thwack—kicking the waves, they fly.

Who has gathered up all the glory of this place?
A chilly wind brushes my face, making it feel even more deserted.

That solitary fishing boat rounding the island far away—
To the tip of that boat's mast, the lonely sentiment of this beach
I want to send it away.

Korean Sentiment

Yu Sŏk-sŏng

Hearts that awaited the storm,
Like travelers soaked in homesickness,
Took to the dream path in a deep midday nap.

It’s a rain-soaked flag.
It’s a broken brass mirror (퉁경).
A melancholic feeling (沈鬱한 心情), like a lake, overwhelms the heart.

On a moonlit autumn night, who plays the flute?
Play it, ah! Even if you play the flute,
Please, just don't play the old melodies.
Please, just don't play the old melodies. <227>

**Summary**

Yi Ha-yun introduces and recommends two poems by a newcomer, Yu Sŏk-sŏng: "September Beach" and "Korean Sentiment." Despite having read only these two works, Yi is impressed by Yu's skill, finding "September Beach" a clean, evocative landscape poem despite minor awkward phrasing, and "Korean Sentiment" deeply moving, capable of making the reader overlook poetic flaws due to its emotional impact. Yi expresses confidence in Yu's future potential as a poet he personally appreciates. The poems themselves depict desolate scenes—a deserted beach in autumn and symbols of decay (wet flag, broken mirror)—linked to a suppressed, melancholic Korean sentiment under oppressive circumstances, explicitly asking not to be reminded of "old melodies." For historians, this piece offers insight into the literary scene of colonial Korea, showcasing how even officially published works could subtly express nationalistic feelings and melancholy (Chosŏn ŭi chŏngjo) through landscape and metaphor. The act of recommendation itself highlights the process of literary discovery and promotion during that era, while the poems' themes reflect the constrained emotional and political atmosphere.

諸家推薦 新人作品集-異河潤 薦, 海水浴場(시)
「海水浴場」과 「朝鮮情調」選後感

異河潤

劉石星氏의 作品은 이 『九月의 海水浴場』과 『朝鮮의 情調』 오직 두 篇 밧게 달니는 읽어본 記憶이 업습니다. 그럼에도 불구하고 나는 劉氏를 新人이라고 하기엔 疑心을 품고 십흘 만치 孰練된 데가 엿보인다는 것을 말하지 안흘 수 업습니다.
詩 한 篇이나 두 篇쯤 보고 나서 무에라고 詩人全體에 대한 評筆를 섯불리 들 수 업슴은 물론이오 그 作者에 대한 아모런 知識도 업시 推薦文을 公表하기도 힘든 일이지마는 嚴正한 批評은 讀者 여러분 各自에 一任하고 다만 늣깃 바 몃가지 感想을 적어 보랴 합니다.
九月의 海水浴場-아모 거침도 업시 나려간 한폭의 風景詩로 별로 遜色이 업다 해도 過言이 아닐 상 십흡니다. 첫 聯에 『四色의 別莊』이니 『棧橋』니 한 것이 나로서는 읽기에 좀 어색하엿지만 이러타할 缺点을 차저내기 힘든 가벼운 듯하나 깨끗한 情緖가 휩싸고 도는 詩라 하겟습니다. 九月의 海水浴場이 나를 부르고 잇습니다.
朝鮮의 情調-朝鮮의 情調 그저 바리고 십지 안습니다. 읽는 우리의 가슴을 벅차게 해 주는데가 잇서 詩로서의 若干의 缺点쯤을 알 수 업시 지나갈 것입니다.
나는 이 두 篇의 詩만을 가지고서도 넉넉히 이 詩人의 前途를 囑望을 가지고 십흡니다. 正直하게 말하자면 劉氏는 내가 조하할 수 잇는 範圍에 屬하는 詩人의 한사람이외다.<226>

九月의 海水浴場 -어느날 松濤園에서-

劉石星

쪼악볏이 나리는 九月의 한낫-
四色의 別莊들은 모래불에 안저 조을고
棧橋는 푸른 바다물에 가로누어 허리를 편다.

松林속 벤취에는 그늘이 혼저 건닐고
바다가에는 漁夫한분 머리를 숙이고 그믈을 뜬다.
여름한동안 故鄕을 일헛던 갈메기들...
투-ㄱ 물결을 차며 나네.

누가 이곧의 榮華를 모조리 거더갓노?
쌀쌀한 바람이 나츨 스치니 더욱 호젓하고나

저 멀니 섬을 돌아가는 외돗쟁이 고기배
저배 돗대끗헤 이 海水浴場의 쓸쓸한 情調를
실 보내고 십다.

朝鮮의 情調

劉石星

暴風을 긔다리던 마음들은
鄕愁에 저즌 나그내와 가치
함북 午睡에 꿈길을 탓다.

비마즌 旗幅일세.
께여진 퉁경일세.
沈鬱한 心情은 湖水와 가치 가슴에 벅찬다.

달밝은 가을밤에 누가 피리를 부느냐.
부러도 아! 피리를 부러도
옛 曲調랑 제발 불지마러주렴.
옛 曲調랑 제발 불지마러주렴. <227>
