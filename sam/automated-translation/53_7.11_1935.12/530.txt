Magazine Title: 삼천리 第七卷 第十一號
Publication Date: 1935년 12월 01일
Article Title: 달밤 (Moonlit Night)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

달밤
						파인

골안에
안개 나리네

안개 뒤엔
초숭 달이

초승달 뒤엔
강바람이

강바람 뒤엔
피리 소리

피리 뒤엔
에그, 다 말해 무엇하리

셋재야, 이 조흔 달밤
피리 불며 와주럼 <296>

Moonlit Night
						P'ain

In the valley depths
Mist descends

Behind the mist
A crescent moon

Behind the crescent moon
The river wind

Behind the river wind
The sound of a flute

Behind the flute
Ah, what's the use of saying it all

Setchae, on this fine moonlit night
Come playing your flute <296>

**Summary**

The poem "Moonlit Night" by P'ain paints an atmospheric picture of a misty valley evening. It layers sensory details – mist, crescent moon, river wind, and finally a flute's sound – creating a mood of quiet contemplation or perhaps melancholy longing. The poem concludes with a direct, intimate address to someone called "Setchae," inviting them to come and play the flute on this beautiful night. For historians, this poem exemplifies the lyrical and often apolitical style found in some Korean literature during the colonial period, focusing on natural beauty and personal emotion. The use of a pen name (P'ain for Kim Tong-hwan) and slightly archaic language ("조흔", "와주럼") are also characteristic features of the era's publications.

나리네	나리네	Descends/Falls (from 내리다 'to descend'; -네 is an exclamatory ending)
초숭 달	초승달	Crescent moon (Likely an older or regional spelling/pronunciation of 초승달)
강바람	강바람	River wind
피리	피리	Flute (traditional Korean flute)
조흔	좋은	Good, fine (Common older spelling of 좋은)
와주럼	와 주렴	Please come (Contraction/form of 와 주렴, a gentle imperative suggesting 'come and do me the favor of...')
