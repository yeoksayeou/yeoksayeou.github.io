Collection of Works by Newcomers Recommended by Various Houses - Recommended by Pak Wŏlt'an, Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved? (Poem)
1st Place (Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?) · 2nd Place (Twilight) · 3rd Place (Worldly Aspects)

Wŏlt'an Pak Chonghwa

Picking out those that barely reach a level that could be considered poetry, they were one piece each by Mr. Im Chinjun, "Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?"; U Kŭmju's "Twilight"; and Mr. An Sŭngt'ae's "Worldly Aspects." However, none of these are entirely perfect. I just want to affirm that the works of these three individuals possess much potential for writing poetry.
Mr. An Sŭngt'ae's "Worldly Aspects" (The original manuscript said 世上 (Sesang, World), but 世相 (Sesang, Worldly Aspects) is correct) shows a skill that absolutely does not permit imitation by those things spread out as poetry on newspaper pages these days, in its breath of driving the poetic language and the manner in which the rhythm is neatly finished. The technique, carved, touched, polished, and washed, is that of someone who has read much and written much. However, after reading this poem, what kind of feeling must one end up having? The poetic idea is too weak; the reader does not receive much emotional stirring. If it's a sad poem, it should be written to make the reader's nose tingle; if it's a joyful poem, it should be written so the reader's heart pounds. If it's a sigh, as if the lungs would burst; if it's suffering, as if the heart would suffocate. If a poem, no matter how well-refined in form and words, does not possess a poetic idea capable of inciting human emotion, that is poetry's fatal wound.
Compared to the refinement of Mr. An's work, polished like a Go stone, Mr. Im Chinjun's "Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?" seems rather clumsy, but even with just its vocabulary, like surging river waves, it sufficiently overwhelms the refinement of "Worldly Aspects." And the skill of handling the *ki-sŭng-chŏn-kyŏl* (起承轉結) structure by looking this way and that, soothing and touching the single subject "beloved," pretends to be clumsy but is not a clumsy skill. I recommend "Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?" for first place, "Worldly Aspects" for third place, and Ms. U's "Twilight" for second place in between. "Twilight" lacks the vigorous flavor of Im Chinjun's poem and the words seem a bit immature, but the poem's structural arrangement is rather more beautiful and elegant compared to "Worldly Aspects," showing much potential for writing lyrical poetry in the future. <205>

Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?

Im Chinjun

Are you asking about the time my beloved left?
The time my beloved left was the spring when flowers bloomed last year.
Are you asking about the place where my beloved is?
The place where my beloved is is the ice-cold North.

Are you asking for news of my beloved?
I haven't heard news of my beloved even on the wind.
But the beloved's gentle and soft heart,
Why would I not know it!
The beloved's heart that eternally consoles my spirit,
Why would I not know it!

Are you asking about the beloved's appearance?
The beloved's appearance was a plump, chubby sort.
Are you asking about the beloved's hair color?
The beloved's hair color was a darkish yellow.
But when the beloved walked down the path once,
Keeping rhythm, she walked lightly, lightly.
When the beloved's hair fluttered once in the wind,
It sparkled like fine gold thread.

The beloved's face had a harsh impression.
But when the beloved smiled once,
It was soft and lustrous like a peony blossom.
The beloved's eyes were sharp like an autumn sky.
But they were an expression of tender love.

When the beloved came to meet me,
She said no words, only her face reddened.
But it was a symbol of eternal, eternal love.
When the beloved cried out of frustration,
My heart also ached and was pained.
But it was a symbol of eternal, eternal love.

Twilight

U Kŭmju

It's twilight
Twilight, bringing stories in the darkness
Beneath the gold brocade curtain <206>
Having buried the sun-lord
It's the hosts of Nature opening a red feast

Flocks of birds seek their nests
The wind clinging to the bushes also sleeps
Twilight,
which the grey curtain is peeking at
It's twilight, chased by darkness

But alas, the red guests
Guests who will soon disperse and scatter

Worldly Aspects

An Sŭngt'ae

Crying!
Crying!
Sighing!

The world is meaningless
There is nothing to believe in...

The world is meaningless
There is nothing to believe in...

This sound
Repeating how many times
This painful world
Have I grown up in...<207>

**Summary**

Pak Chonghwa, a prominent literary figure, reviews and ranks three poems by newcomers submitted to *Samch'ŏlli* magazine: Im Chinjun's "Why Wouldn't I Know My Beloved?" (1st), U Kŭmju's "Twilight" (2nd), and An Sŭngt'ae's "Worldly Aspects" (3rd). Pak praises Im's poem for its emotional depth and evocative language, despite technical imperfections, placing it above An's technically refined but emotionally weak poem. U Kŭmju's poem is noted for its elegance and potential in lyrical poetry. The review highlights the tension between technical skill and emotional resonance in poetry evaluation, while the poems themselves touch on themes of love, loss, nature's beauty, and existential disillusionment. For historians, this piece offers a glimpse into the colonial Korean literary scene, including critical standards, the emergence of new writers (including women like U Kŭmju), prevailing themes reflecting the era's atmosphere, and the linguistic features of 1930s Korean. An Sŭngt'ae's poem, in particular, reflects a common sentiment of despair or meaninglessness found in literature from periods of occupation or social upheaval.

諸家推薦 新人作品集- 朴月灘 薦, 님을 내 왜 모르릿가(시)
第1席 (님을 내 왜 모르릿가)·第2席 (黃昏)·第3席 (世相)

月灘 朴鍾和

詩됨즉한 水準우에 겨우 올려놀 만한 것을 뽀으니 林鎭俊氏의 「님을 내 왜 모르릿가」와 禹今珠의 「黃昏」과 安升泰氏의 「世相」 各 한편식 이엿슴니다. 그러나 이것이 다 完全無缺한 것은 안이외다. 세분의 것이 多分이 詩지을 素質을 많이 가지고 잇는 것만을 保障하고 십흔 것이외다.
安升泰氏의 「世相」(原稿에는 世上이라 햇스나, 世相이 옳은 것)은 그 詩語를 몰아쓰는 숨씨라든지 결곡하게 리듬을 마치여 노흔 품이 요사이 新聞紙쌍 우에 詩라고 벌려노흔 것들의 도저의 追從을 허락하지 안을 솜씨다. 깍고 만지고 닥고 씻어논 手法이 만히 읽고 만히 쓴 솜씨다. 그러나 이 詩를 읽고 나서 우리는 무엇을 엇떠한 늣김을 갓고야 마는가. 詩想이 너무도 약하다, 讀者는 그다지 큰 感情의 흔들림을 밧지 못하게 된다. 습흔 詩면은 讀者 코뿌리 가재릿가재릿하여 지도록 되어야 하고 깁뿐 詩면은 讀者의 가슴이 벌룽벌룽거리도록 써놓아야 한다. 嘆息이면 허파가 터질 듯키, 苦롭다면 마음이 窒息될 듯키, 만일 詩로써 아모리 型과 말에 잇서 가장 잘 洗鍊된 것이라 할지라도 사람의 感情을 煽動할 만한 詩想을 갓지 못햇다 하면 그것은 詩의 致命傷이다.
林鎭俊氏의 「님을 내 왜 모르릿가」는 安氏의 바득돌같이 닥겨진 洗鍊에 비하야시는 도로혀 좀 서투른 듯하나 그 출렁하는 강물결과 갓흔 語彙만으로도 넉넉히 「世相」의 洗鍊을 壓頭하고 만다. 그리고 「님」 한아를 가지고 이리보고 저리보고 얼느고 또 만저서 起承轉結 굴니는 솜씨가 서투른 체 하면서도 서투른 솜씨가 안이다. 第一席에 「님을 내 왜 모르릿가」를 推薦하고 第三席에 「世上」을 推薦하고 그 중간 第二에 禹氏의 黃昏을 推薦한다. 「黃昏」 林鎭俊의 詩만큼 씩씩한 맛이 적고 말이 좀 어린 듯 하나 詩의 搆造配置가 도로혀 世想에 비하야 고읍고 優雅하야 앞으로 叙晴詩 쓸 素質을 만히 가지고 잇다. <205>

님 떠나신 때 말슴임닛까?

님 게신 곳 말슴임니까.
님 게신 곳은 차듸찬 북쪽임니다.

님의 소식 말슴입니까.
님의 소식은 바람김에도 못드럿씀니다.
그러나 은근하고 부드러운 님의 맘씨를,

내가 외 모를 리가 잇어요!

님의 머리깔및 말씀입니까?
님의 머리깔및은 거머척척한 노랑이엿씀니다.
그러나 님이 한번 길을 거를 적에는

가늘다한 금실처럼 반짝엿어요.

님의 얼굴은 모진 감각이엿씀니다.
그러나 님이 한번 웃을 때에는,
함박꽃같이 부드럽고 윤택하엿씀니다.
님의 눈초리는 가을날씨처럼 날카러웟씀니다.
그러나 연연한 사랑의 表現이엿슴니다.

님이 나를 만나려 왓엇을 때에는
말은 없이 얼골만 붉혓엇습니다.
그러나 永遠한 永遠한 사랑의 表象이엿씀니다.
님이 안타가워서 울 때에는
내마음도 맛쯜하야 苦로웟습니다.
그러나 永遠한 永遠한 사랑의 表象이엿습니다
