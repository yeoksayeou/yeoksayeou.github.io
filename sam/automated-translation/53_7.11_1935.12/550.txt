Magazine Title: 삼천리 제7권 제11호
Publication Date: 1935년 12월 01일
Article Title: 파랑새 (Bluebird)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Bluebird
						P'ain

Covering the whole body in oil
Shall I set it alight?
Then, becoming a bluebird from the ashes
They say one can go where one wishes-.

How sorrowful. You are already another's wife
Only the path to becoming a bluebird remains.
For this body too
Only the path to becoming a bluebird remains. <298>

**Summary**

This short poem expresses deep sorrow and longing for an unattainable love. The speaker contemplates self-immolation as a means of transformation into a bluebird, a symbol of happiness or freedom, believing this metamorphosis is the only way to reach the desired place (implicitly, the beloved who is married to someone else). Both the speaker and the beloved are trapped, leaving only the path of becoming a "bluebird." From a historical perspective, the intense despair and the drastic metaphor of transformation through fire might resonate with the suffocating atmosphere and suppressed desires of Koreans under Japanese colonial rule. The yearning for unattainable freedom or happiness, symbolized by the bluebird, could be interpreted as reflecting broader societal constraints, even when framed as personal romantic despair.
