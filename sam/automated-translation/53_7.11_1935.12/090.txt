**Summary**

This short article lists several prominent tea houses (*tabang*) in Seoul as of late 1935, specifying their locations by district (dong/chō/t'ong). The list highlights popular spots in central Seoul areas like Insadong, Chongno, Hasegawachō (modern Sogong-dong area), Meijichō (modern Myŏngdong), Namdaemun-t'ong (Namdaemun-ro), and Kwanch'ŏl-tong. For historians, the article is interesting as it reflects the burgeoning cafe culture in colonial Seoul. The mix of Korean districts (Insadong, Chongno, Kwanch'ŏl-tong) and Japanese-named districts (Hasegawachō, Meijichō), along with the variety of tea house names (Western transliterations like Ppi-nŏsŭ/Venus, Mekk'isik'o/Mexico, Pposŭt'ong/Boston; Japanese/Korean like Nakrang, Paeghabwŏn), illustrates the cultural fusion and modernization occurring under Japanese rule.
