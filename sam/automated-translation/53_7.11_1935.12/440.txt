A Certain Woman
						Mo Yun-suk

The waterside midnight deepens,
The scent of fragrant trees wafts
Beside the sleeping village.
The moon, leaning over the old fortress wall,
Slips into the house’s small window.

Lying on a torn cotton mattress,
The old woman’s face.
The wind occasionally blows her hair,
Scattering it in disarray,
But the pillow’s dream is deep,
Hiding worries and sighs in the night shade.
The patterns of hardship engraved on her bones<278>

The joyful well path of old days,
The cherished water jug,
The fortunate stories
Left behind in the rows of the cabbage patch—
Now they are but the evening smoke of a hometown passed.
Like the end of leisurely tales
Where memories blossom and fade.

That star shines brightly and heavily
In the impoverished window all night long,
It will fill that woman’s empty cup.
When life, eaten away, makes its final promise,
She will merely prostrate herself upon the evening forest,
Gathering God’s voice through her torn skirt hem.<279>
<278-279>

**Summary**

This poem by Mo Yun-suk portrays an impoverished elderly woman nearing the end of her life. Lying alone at night, she contrasts her current hardship, marked by a torn mattress and worries hidden in the dark, with nostalgic memories of a happier past symbolized by the village well and cheerful stories. The final stanzas suggest a bleak future where only a star symbolically fills her "empty cup," and her final act will be one of humble supplication, seeking divine solace amidst her destitution. For historians, the poem offers a poignant glimpse into the lives of marginalized individuals, particularly women, experiencing poverty and reflecting on loss during the colonial period. It subtly touches upon themes of hardship, memory, and perhaps a quiet, personal form of spiritual endurance in the face of suffering, reflecting the socio-economic conditions and emotional landscape of the time.

어느 女人
						毛允淑
물가의 한밤은 깊어서
향어린 나무냄새 풍기는
잠든 마을옆
낡은 城우로 기우는 달이
그집 들창에 슬어저간다.

헐닌요우에 누운
늙은 女人의 얼골
바람은 이따금 그 머리날여
散亂히 흐터가나
벼개의 꿈은 깊어
근심과 탄식 밤그늘에 숨긴다.
고생의 문채 그뼈에 색인채<278>

녯날 즐겁든 우물길
그리운 물동의
배추밭이랑에 흘여둔
多福한 이야기들
지금은 지나온 故鄕의 저녁연기로다.
記憶에 아람이 피여낫다 사라지는
한가하든 이야기의 끝처럼

저별이 빗나고 무겁게
貧困한 창안에 밤새도록
그 女人의 븬잔을 채우리니
生命이 좀먹어 맞으막 언약은 하올때
그는 한갓 저녁 숲 우에 업들여
헐닌 치마귀로 神의 음성을 뫃으리.<279>
