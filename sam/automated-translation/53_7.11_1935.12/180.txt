Secrets to Wealth of Top-Tier Merchants, First Public Release = Promotion and Advertising Techniques
Promotion Costs Standardized at 6% of Sales

Yi Kŭnjong, Section Chief, Hwasin Department Store

How do the top-tier merchants in Sŏul, the ones everyone counts on their fingers, make their money? What are their promotion techniques? Their methods for sourcing products, or their ways of serving customers—what are all these like? To get a glimpse of these secrets, we have decided to publish insightful articles on this topic in every issue from now on.

In business today, there's nothing that can succeed without the power of promotion. Especially nowadays, when one must attract various customer segments and new products appear one after another almost daily, it's difficult to digest all these products across the purchasing base without widely promoting their quality, use, price, etc. Promotion is truly becoming more necessary by the day.
(The new Hwasin Department Store building, to be completed next June) <120>
However, even with all the talk of promotion, promotion alone is not the secret to life in business. This is what I think: First, trust, and second, promotion! No matter how grandly one promotes, if the goods are not good, customers might be fooled the first time, but they won't be fooled a second time. Therefore, one must first display good, inexpensive products, and then strive to promote them in a way that matches their true value. Currently, our Hwasin adheres strictly to this motto.

Is the 10 Chŏn Uniform Price Sale a Promotion?

As a promotion method, others sometimes hold cheap markets like 10 chŏn uniform price sales. That's something to try occasionally for a very short period, but it's not something to do for long. Also, a 10 chŏn uniform price sale isn't just about selling low-value items (눅거리), but a sale gathering only cheap 10-chŏn items. If you run this kind of sale, customers flock only there, causing the headache that other sections don't sell. Also, since cheap items are displayed, many get broken or damaged, making it easy to end up with a loss.

How About the Year-End Grand Sale?

For instance, in places like Tōkyō and Ōsaka, there are two major sales: the Mid-Year Grand Sale (Chūgen Ōuridashi) and the Year-End Grand Sale (Nenmatsu Ōuridashi). However, in Chosŏn, we can only benefit from one peak season throughout the year, the Year-End Sale. If daily sales are usually 10,000 wŏn, then during this year-end grand sale period, daily sales should reach 100,000 wŏn to be fitting. Holding a raffle draw during this Year-End Grand Sale is one promotional tactic. This raffle is done in the spirit of providing service to customers during the year-end/new-year transition, definitely not by adding more to the regular price of goods. During the year-end grand sale, our department store prints flyers (삐라). We don't distribute them on the streets; instead, clerks and managers all scatter throughout the streets and deliver them door-to-door (分傳). We also send separate letters to households that are regular customers, informing them of new product arrivals.

Are Mannequins Effective?

The use of mannequins is still not very common in Chosŏn these days. Mannequins are meant to promote products and sell them well, but perhaps this method is still premature for Chosŏn. Recently, we tried selling Morinaga (森永) caramels using mannequins. So many onlookers gathered, finding the mannequin novel, that other sections couldn't sell their goods, so it ended up being a failure this time.

Promotional Expenses are 6% of Sales <121>

Besides that, we also advertise in newspapers and magazines. Overall, including flyers, posters, catalogues sent for regional sales, and all other costs, we spend 6% of our sales revenue on promotional expenses. We don't do full-page newspaper ads as they look vulgar.
Also, as a department store, we should always strive to promote using bands or other entertainment, but rather than temporary promotions, we try to consistently promote things steadily. The reason for employing female clerks is that women have a soft and gentle feel, which helps make a good impression on customers.

Opening a Discount Book Market

Kim Chinho, Manager, Hansŏng Book Company (漢城圖書會社)

When selling books, the "good books first" principle is itself the best promotional technique. Unlike other products, we don't need methods like hiring mannequins or installing dazzling neon signs at all.
Now, let me tell you about our company's secret promotional methods. First, we prepare a book catalogue. When someone requests a printed catalogue, we send it to them individually. Also, even if there's only one or two orders, we record the person's name and address on a name card (네임·카드). Whenever a new book comes out, we notify them, and also inform them when we have discount sales, thus providing service to various readers while striving not to lose a single customer.

Special Sales, Group Sales

What are these? Our company has traveled to large cities like P'yŏngyang or Taegu, carrying books, and held temporary special price sales for short periods. Each time, we achieved sales figures beyond expectations. Then there's group selling (집단 판매). We go to places where many people gather, like companies or factories, and sell dozens or hundreds of wŏn worth of books at once. We don't offer prices as low as we do to retailers, usually around a 10% discount. All these have been good promotional methods.

Effectiveness of Newspaper and Magazine Ads

When a published book goes into its second or third printing, we place ads in newspapers. This is a promotional method to cultivate new readers. Simultaneously, we place ads in various prominent magazines within Chosŏn. <122> This also yields significant results. Besides this, as a service to booksellers (賣子), we are distributing a monthly magazine called "Hakdŭng" (學燈) to those on our reader list (讀者名簿).
Overall, the most reliable promotion method, I believe, lies in not losing a single reader once acquired, and fostering enduring relationships planned over decades, even centuries.

Method Where the Medicine Sells Itself

Cho Chongguk, Manager, Chosŏn Patent Medicine Co., Ltd. (朝鮮賣藥株式會社)

Placing huge, full-page ads in newspapers makes viewers feel nauseous. It truly makes one wonder where the money is pouring from, where the excessive profits are coming from, to afford such full-page ads. Otherwise, there would be no reason for it. We think an ad just needs to be seen, and about half a page is actually better to look at. So, we didn't want to run boastful (虛風) ads like others do. Other patent medicine dealers run full-page ads, but we prefer to cut that cost by about half, run about half-page ads, and use the saved money for medicine manufacturing or importing expensive raw materials. This too is our company's actual promotional method.

Let the Medicine Sell the Medicine

From our long experience, the period after the medicine is bought is actually the issue. If someone buys medicine after seeing a grandly advertised product, takes it, and it only momentarily feels refreshing in the mouth, but today is the same, tomorrow is the same, it just seems like it might get better, but the illness ultimately doesn't heal—those advertising tactics for gonorrhea (림질) and syphilis (매독) drugs, even attaching things like letters of appreciation (감사장), seem rather despicable. Isn't there no need to go that far? If the medicine is good, it sells itself without needing to make such a fuss. Therefore, newspaper ads should play a supporting role, and the medicine itself should be the main driver of sales. If the medicine doesn't sell itself, and sales stop the day the newspaper ads cease, then it cannot be called good medicine.

Start with Good Packaging

First, when the buyer opens the medicine packet, the packaging must be clean. If the medicine looks dirty from the packaging, it first <123> causes discomfort for the user. They lose the desire to take the medicine, thinking, "What effect could this possibly have?" This has the detrimental effect of rather diminishing the medicine's efficacy. We pay attention to medicine packaging (藥包裝) and materials (用材), conducting silent promotion (無言의 宣傳).

Ads of About Six Columns Are Appropriate

It's good to run about half-page ads in newspapers, and we tend to run more in spring and summer. In the Taishō era (大正時代), we spent about 10,000 wŏn per year on advertising, and it yielded that much effect (救果). Now, as I mentioned earlier, we stick to that policy, running smaller ads and putting our main effort (主力) into the medicine.

Various Types of Promotion

1.  Peer Monthly Report (同業者月報): This is for mutual promotion among peers (同業者) and is also necessary for promoting those medicines.
2.  Flyers (삐라): We make flyers for promotion. This is promotion targeted at users.
3.  Pharmaceutical Industry Newspapers (藥業新聞): Besides these, promotion mostly targets peers rather than users.
4.  Magazines (雜誌): We place ads in magazines in Chosŏn that have the most influential and largest circulation. We do this to promote directly to users.
Also, the reason we must advertise in newspapers or magazines is that old medicine grows mold and dries out. Therefore, as much as possible, medicine that hasn't been manufactured long ago and shows effects is very effective, so it must be sold quickly. From this perspective, if a company only uses its own promotional materials and doesn't advertise in newspapers and magazines, there's a concern that the medicine will lose its sales percentage as medicine. Currently, our company spends about 30,000 wŏn per year on promotional expenses, and it seems likely we will increase this by about 30-40% next year.

Airplane Promotion is Premature

Hong Sunŏn, Owner, Tongyang Theater (東洋劇場)

The secret to promoting plays or movies is to make them visible to everyone, so you probably have a general idea. First is live promotion (實物宣傳). For instance, when a theater troupe appears in Sŏul, they put a line-up of shabby-looking guys (엉설마진 녀석) and handsome men/beautiful women (美男美姬) in rickshaws, led by a band playing "bungbarabunga," and parade around town. This seems to be the most effective. <124>
I hear the promotional methods used these days in Tōkyō or Ōsaka are truly super-speed style. They supposedly make huge advertising banners from bolts of cotton cloth (廣木匹) saying "Such-and-such troupe has arrived," attach them to the tail of an airplane, and fly the plane over Ginza or Ueno. This way, all 7 million people in Tōkyō see it, so it's a good promotion method, but due to cost issues, it's still premature (尙早) for Chosŏn.
Next are flyers (삐라) and posters. Not only distributing them on the streets but also handing out flyers with the next week's program (푸로그람) to theatergoers is an effective method for constantly attracting regular customers (願客).
However, to see effects in a short period of 2-3 days, newspapers are best. To promote with a deeper impression, magazines, which have a lifespan of at least a month, are much better.

I hear Shōchiku (松竹) in Tōkyō uses this method: They offer prizes to promotion department employees to come up with clever promotion methods. If the method results in profit, the employee gets a cash prize; if it results in loss, they have to pay a fine.

However, no matter how well you promote, if the movie actually shown on the silver screen is lousy, or the play staged isn't worthy of welcome, what effect could there be, even if you promote until your voice is hoarse? Rather, if the audience who saw it first starts negative publicity (惡宣傳), you'll see failure worse than not promoting at all. Therefore, the "work-first principle" (作品第一主義) is always the greatest weapon and demonstrates the best promotional function.

Special Effect of Touring Performances

Kim Sŏnghŭm, Branch Manager (*likely mistake, should be Manager), Okeh Records Company (오-케-레코드會社)

When it comes to the promotional secrets of a record company, the method is fundamentally different from, say, a pharmaceutical company.
For medicine, once an order is taken and sold, that's it for the main office. But look here: records sent out from the main office go around to sales outlets all over Korea (全鮮販賣所), and if they don't sell, they are returned (返品) to the main office. If that happens, it's purely the head office's loss. Therefore, to ensure that products shipped from the main office are all sold and don't come back as returns, you absolutely need truly clever promotion methods. So, our company's promotion method, besides of course putting all effort into producing good works, is to strive to widely promote the fame of the street artists (街頭藝術家) - that is, the recording artists (吹込者) - who sang the songs, <125> through the company. We don't do this for just any singer, but only after assessing the character of singers who have signed a contract with our company and won't be swayed by temptations (誘惑) from other companies.

Effectiveness of Regional Tours

Furthermore, there probably isn't a record company today that doesn't conduct regional tours (地方巡廻). Why does everyone emphasize these tours? If many singers idle around in Sŏul, they naturally engage in temptations and waste money. Another thing is, letting tens of thousands in the audience hear the singer's actual voice, which they usually hear from a record (譜盤), serves as a service to record fans. Also, doing these regional tours means the singers themselves receive a daily allowance (手當金) of 2 to 10 wŏn in addition to their salary, which also helps them financially.

Other Promotion Methods

一. Advertising in newspapers. Especially for announcing events like concerts, fast newspaper ads are effective.
二. Magazine ads. Since these have a lifespan of over a month, we gladly utilize magazine advertising.
三. The main office prints catalogues (型錄/目錄) and sends them to branches.
四. We produce monthly reports (月報), posters, flyers (삐라), and wrapping paper/inserts (貽紙).

Generally, in this day and age, no product can ignore advertising, but among them, the record business is particularly mass-oriented, so it must constantly appeal to the public's eyes and ears. All told, our company's monthly promotion expenses exceed 2,000 wŏn.

Advertising Cost is 10% for a 5,000 Wŏn [Film]

Pak Kich'ae, Executive, Yŏnghwa Sidae Sa (映畵時代社)
The Korean movie "Spring Breeze" (春風) was produced by our own hands. Let me talk about the actual promotion.
"Spring Breeze" is 14 reels in total, and the total production cost came to 5,300 wŏn. The breakdown is:
Film Stock (필님代): 2,400 wŏn <126>
Drawing Paper (原畵紙代): 500 wŏn
Lunch Costs (晝食代): 400 wŏn
Filming Costs (撮影費): 200 wŏn
Developing Costs (現象費): 400 wŏn
Besides this, adding personnel costs (人件費) and advertising costs (廣告費) brings the total to over 5,000 wŏn. Although we started planning over two months ago, the actual filming took only 21 days. Chosŏn is not yet a place with permanently employed actors, nor does it have studio (스타지오) facilities, and aren't personnel issues (人事問題) quite complex? So, there were many unexpected expenses. On top of this, one task remains where we must put the most emphasis, and that is promotion. We are currently researching how to do this promotion, but no particularly good secret method has emerged yet. It's standard practice for any cinema to sign a screening contract with a permanent theater (常設舘) and then use all means—flyers, posters, newspaper, and magazine ads—to inform even one more person. What's the point of repeating this exact same method for 10 or 100 years? So we are thinking of a good plan, but haven't found one yet.

However, in principle, spending about 30% of the production cost on promotion would be right. For movie productions that appeal to everyone's eyes, unlike other things, special emphasis should be placed on promotion. Still, no matter what, newspapers and magazines have the most power in promotion. <127>

**Summary**

This 1935 article compiles interviews with managers from various prominent Korean businesses (department store, book publisher, patent medicine company, theater, record company, film production company) in Sŏul, discussing their advertising and promotional strategies. Common methods include newspaper/magazine ads, flyers, posters, catalogues, and special sales. However, there's a strong emphasis on product quality and customer trust ("good books first," "let the medicine sell itself," "work-first principle") as the foundation. They also discuss specific tactics like raffles, mannequins (deemed ineffective/premature), regional tours for performers, and managing advertising budgets (ranging from 6% of sales for the department store to a suggested 30% of production cost for film). For historians, this piece offers a valuable snapshot of commercial practices, advertising budgets, media choices (newspapers vs. magazines), and the adoption (or rejection) of modern promotional techniques like mannequins and airplane advertising in colonial Korea. It highlights the blend of traditional business values (trust, quality) with emerging capitalist marketing strategies within the specific context of Japanese colonial rule and nascent Korean consumer culture.

一流商家의 致富秘訣, 第一次公開=宣傳과廣告術
宣傳費는 賣上高의 六分標準

和信百貨店 課長 李肯鍾

서울서 누구누구라고 손곱는 일류상가에서는 엇더케 하여 돈을 모으는고. 그 선전술은? 그 상품 仕入術은 또는 고객에 대한 봉사 방법들 온갓 것은 엇더한고 그 비결을 엿듯고저 이제 매호 거기 대한 조흔 기사를 내기로 하엿다.

오늘날 장사치고 어느 것인들 선전의 힘이 들지 안코서 되어가는 것이 업슴니다. 더구나 요지음가치 손님의 각층을 골고로 끄으려야 하고 상품도 하로 건너큼씩 새로운 것이 뒤으로 뒤으로 작고 나오는 이판에 잇서 그 모든 상품을 골고로 구매층에 소화식히자면 그 상품의 품질 용도, 가격 등을 널니 선전하지안코는 어려울 줄 암니다. 선전은 참으로 날이 갈사록 더 필요하여 감니다.

그리고는 레코-드會社치고 오늘날 地方巡廻를 하지 안는 곳이 업슬 듯 함니다. 내남이 엇제서 이 巡廻에 置重하느냐하면 多數한 歌手를 서울서 놀게하면 自然히 誘惑과 돈의 浪費等을 하게 됨니다. 또 한가지는 譜盤우에서 듯는 歌手의 肉聲을 數萬公衆 압헤서 들니게하니까 레코-드 팬에게 써-비쓰도 되며 이러케 地方巡廻를 하게되면 歌手自身으로도 月給以外에 하로 2圓以上 10圓以下의 手當金이 나오게되니까 經濟方面으로도 도와드리는 것이 됨니다.

그밧게 宣傳方法은

一. 新聞에 廣告하는 것 더구나 어듸서 演奏會를 한다든가 하는 것을 알려드리기에는 迅速한 新聞廣告가 効果가 잇슴니다.
二. 雜誌廣告 이것은 한달 以上의 生命을 가지는 것이기 즐겨 우리는 雜誌廣告를 利用함니다.
三. 本店에서 型錄(目錄)을 印刷하여 支店에 보냄니다.
四. 月報-포스타-삐라-貽紙等을 함니다.

大體로 오늘날에 잇서서는 商品치고 廣告를 無視할 수 업지 만은 그 중에도 레코-드장사는 가장 大衆的 이니 만치 恒常 大衆의 耳目에 訴하는바 업스면 아니됨니다. 이럭저럭 우리會社의 한달한달 宣傳費가 2천圓은 突破함니다.

映畵時代社幹部 朴基釆
우리 손으로 이번 朝鮮映畵「春風」이 製作되엇는데 實際宣傳上 이약이를 하지요.
「春風」은 모다 14卷인바 總製作費가 5천3백圓이 먹엇는데 內譯을 보면
필님代    2천4백圓<126>
原畵紙代  5백圓
晝食代    4백圓
撮影費    2백圓
現象費    4백圓
이밧게 人件費와 廣告費을 合치면 5천餘圓돈이 됨니다. 처음 시작하기는 그럭저럭 두달이나 넘엇지만 정작 撮影한 日字는 21日間에 不過함니다. 조선이란 곳은 아직 常備된 俳優가 잇는 곳도 아니요. 「스타지오」의 設備도 된 곳이 아니며 人事問題等 좀이나 複雜합니까, 그래서 豫想外의 入費도 만하여젓는데 이 우에 우리가 가장 역점을 너허야 할 거리가 한가지 남어잇스니 그것이 곳 宣傳이지요. 이 宣傳을 엇더케 할가 함을 우리도 지금 硏究하는 중인데 이러타할 조흔 秘訣이 아직 나지지 안엇슴니다. 어느 키네마에서든지 常設舘과 上映契約을 하고는 곳 삐라, 포스타, 新聞, 雜誌廣告를 通하야 한사람에게라도 더 알녀 노토록 萬端의 힘을 다쓰는 것이 通例지요. 이와 가튼 꼭가튼 方式을 10年이고 100年이고 되푸리 하여서야 엇저겟습니까. 그래서 조흔 案을 생각하는 중인데 아질 發見이 되지 못하엿소이다.

그런데 原則上으로 보면 製作費의 3割쯤은 宣傳費로 쓰는 것이 올켓지요. 萬目에 訴하는 映畵作品가튼 것은 다른거와도 달너 特別히 宣傳에 主力하여야 할 줄 암니다. 그래도 무어니무어니 하여도 宣傳에는 新聞과 雜誌가 가장 威力이 잇지요.<127>
