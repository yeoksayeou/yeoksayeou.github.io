Magazine Title: 삼천리 제7권 제11호
Publication Date: 1935년 12월 01일
Article Title: 귀도 업니, 입도 업니 (Have No Ears, Have No Mouth Either)
Author: 파인 (P'ain)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Have No Ears, Have No Mouth Either
						P'ain

Even in the thousand-year-old Anapchi Pond (雁鴨池), when a stone is thrown
It ripples and answers with a splash.

Hey, eighteen-year-old, this girl!
Do you have no mouth, do you have no ears? <294>

**Summary**

This short poem contrasts the natural responsiveness of Anapchi Pond, which reacts even to a thrown stone after a thousand years, with the perceived silence and unresponsiveness of an eighteen-year-old girl. The speaker directly addresses the girl, questioning if she lacks the ability to hear or speak. From a historical perspective, the poem might reflect traditional gender expectations regarding female expression or silence. Historians might find the direct, somewhat impatient tone towards the young woman ("이 기집애야") and the theme of voicelessness interesting, potentially hinting at frustrations with perceived passivity or lack of agency, though whether this extends beyond a personal observation to broader social commentary is ambiguous in such a brief work.
