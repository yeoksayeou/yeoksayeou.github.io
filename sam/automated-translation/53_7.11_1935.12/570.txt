Thoughts on Sending Off the Year

Alas, Time! This year too passes by so quickly. After youth comes prime, after prime comes old age, and after that old age, may you become young again, so that not a single one of our fellow brethren dies, but lives on for countless generations—

This magazine (本誌), now welcoming its 9th year, intends to dedicate its full energy (全精力) to the upcoming New Year issue (新年號), presenting to our esteemed readers (江湖諸先生) a special feature (特輯) that is magnificent (豪華), brilliant (燦爛), and unparalleled in content (內容 無比). As you likely know, we have never been merchants (商人). Therefore, setting aside profit and loss (利害), we wish only to enter even fire and water (水火속) if there is a way to exalt (讚揚) the culture of the Peninsula (半島文化).

For this year-end issue (送年號), although it is customary (依例) each year (每年) to publish a retrospective special feature (回顧特輯) covering the past year (今年), we have entirely avoided (一切 避하엿습니다) it, as retrospection (回顧) is a futile (부지럽는) task for those who lack pleasant (깁분) memories (回想거리).<300>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the December 1935 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine reflects on the swift passage of time and the end of the year. The editors announce their intention to forgo the usual year-in-review section, citing a lack of pleasant memories to recall. Instead, they promise a magnificent and content-rich special issue for the New Year, reaffirming their commitment to promoting Korean culture ("Peninsula culture") above commercial interests. For historians, the explicit mention of avoiding a retrospective due to unpleasant memories offers a subtle but poignant glimpse into the likely difficult socio-political atmosphere of Korea under Japanese colonial rule in 1935. The emphasis on promoting "Peninsula culture" despite challenges also highlights the nationalist undercurrents within cultural publications of the era.
