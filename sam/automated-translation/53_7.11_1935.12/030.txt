Proposal for Invitation - World Politicians, Thinkers, Literary Greats, Scientists
Just as the Kaizōsha (改造社) in Tokyo invited the renowned scientist Dr. Einstein and the poet Tagore, and the Yomiuri Shimbun invited the American baseball team, if our society were to invite famous overseas scientists, thinkers, or literary figures, who would you wish to select?<26>

Dr. Fosdick, the Religious Leader

Ku Chaok (具滋玉) (Mr. Ku is the General Secretary of the Chosŏn Christian Federation)

As a religious figure, Dr. Harry Emerson Fosdick.
As a scientist, Dr. Millikan.
I would like to invite these two individuals.
Regarding Dr. Fosdick, when I was in America, I often met him and heard many of his speeches; he is truly a great elder.
He is now an old man of about 70, and his reputation shines throughout the world.
When I was in America, he would regularly stand before the microphone once a week to give broadcast sermons.
He now solely manages the Riverside Church in New York City, a world-class church built at the enormous cost of 7 million won. While perhaps artistically inferior to St. Peter's Basilica, it is the most modern style of architecture, equipped with all cultural facilities.
Besides solely managing such a splendid church, he is also a professor at the Union Theological Seminary in the United States (全米聯合神學校). I understand his presence is that of a global giant in the religious world.
Also, Dr. Millikan is a famous American scientist currently serving as a university professor in California.
He has a special friendship with Einstein. When Dr. Einstein was expelled by the German Nazis, he personally took the lead in arranging for Dr. A[instein] to come to America.
I understand he is a famous scientist as well as a famous religious figure.
He once gave a speech at a large gathering of over 3,000 people, where he famously said:
"I am a scientist! For over 60 years until today, I have devoted all my energy to science and accumulated research. However, with the power of science, I absolutely cannot prove God (Jesus). But when I think within my heart, though I cannot express it in words, I somehow naturally come to believe in God! Moreover, I have no choice but to believe."
He is a man who has received 14 honorary doctorates from various countries around the world.<27>

Perhaps Mr. Hu Hanmin

Song Chinu (宋鎭禹) (Mr. Song is the President of the Tonga Ilbo)

There are many faces I wished to see at least once,
And many voices I wished to hear at least once in this world.
However, sitting here, I do not enjoy speaking about such figures in political or ideological terms. There are heaps of people we'd like to meet, people we'd like to invite, far and near, East and West, but if you force me to speak…
Hu Shih (胡適) or Hu Hanmin (胡漢民)
Are figures nearby whom I would like to hear speak at least once.

Perhaps a First-Rate Scientist

Hŏ Hŏn (許憲) (Mr. Hŏ is the former Chairman of the Sin'ganhoe)

When I traveled the world years ago, meeting in person and talking with figures I had only heard about through newspapers and magazines, I felt the powerful impression they made truly required sitting closely and conversing. No matter how much one reads their books and understands their character and thought, how can it compare to speaking knee-to-knee (交膝而語)? From this perspective, if great thinkers and scientists could come, there would be nothing better. However, I will refrain from pointing out any specific names.

Gide and Rolland

Yŏ Unhyŏng (呂運亨) (Mr. Yŏ is the President of the Chungang Ilbo of Chosŏn)
Replying briefly to your company's question, regarding politicians, I refrain from speaking as I am now. Also, in sports, I do not feel the need to invite famous foreign figures.
Then, as thinkers and scholars, André Gide of France and the artist Romain Rolland, these two would seem good, I believe.
These individuals are likely people who best understand "reality" and the "world situation." Regardless of bourgeois scholars (뿌루 學者) or proletarian scholars (푸로 學者) of the world, these two alone bear the name "sober and awake people," thus they are likely people who understand the world well.<28> Therefore, at this time, it would be good to invite them once and listen to their views on art, life, and even the world.
And although there are people better than these two, I will refrain from mentioning them due to circumstances.

The Religious Leader Niebuhr

Sin Hŭngu (申興雨) (Mr. Sin is the former General Secretary of the Chosŏn Christian Federation)

I wish we could invite the world-famous American religious leader, Mr. Niebuhr.
He is currently a professor at Columbia University, a religious figure who also holds broad interest in economic and social issues and conducts much research as a scholar and thinker.
I understand he is someone who studies human social problems with progressive thought and grasps the current world situation well.
I think such a person's words would be particularly necessary for the youth and students of Chosŏn.
Next, the famous British scholar H. G. Wells is also someone I would like to invite to our society sometime.

The Writer André Gide

An Chaehong (安在鴻) (Mr. An is the former President of the Chosŏn Ilbo)

Among the questions asked by your company,
Regarding thinkers, politicians, religious leaders, or activists, not only can I not speak, but I also avoid speaking. I will only speak here concerning scholars.
Then, as scholars, and among them, as writers, André Gide of France and Hu Shih (胡適) of China, I think it would be good if these two could be invited.

The Honest Old Mr. Bernard Shaw

Hyŏn Sangyun (玄相允) (Mr. Hyŏn is the Principal of Kyŏngsŏng Central Normal High School)

Bernard Shaw of Britain.
Although he spoke unfavorably about Ethiopia during the outbreak of the war with Italy last month, he is relatively honest. He seems likely to speak frankly to us without reservation or concealment.<29>
Besides him, a few others come to mind, but they all seem weakened by "power." If invited from the Russian side, it might not be so, but they also risk losing fairness due to their self-promotion.
Ultimately, it seems there is no one who can speak with guts other than old Mr. Shaw.

Someone like the English Literary Great H. G. Wells

Kim Yŏsik (金麗植) (Mr. Kim is the Principal of Kyŏngsŏng Hyŏpsŏng Industrial School)

If there were an institution capable of inviting such famous foreign figures and the time came, I wish we could select people like Tagore of India, Hu Shih of China, Einstein of Germany, H. G. Wells of Britain, Sinclair Lewis of America, etc.

An Authority in the Theological World

Ku Sŏngsŏ (具聖書) (Mr. Ku is an executive of the Chosŏn Sunday School Association)

As I myself am in the religious field, I wish to speak only concerning the religious world.
If our society were to invite a famous foreign religious leader or theologian, who would I wish to invite?
Of course, I believe it is a very good thing and also a necessary and appropriate question.
As the history of religion (Christianity) in Chosŏn is long, many famous overseas religious figures have visited our society several times before, and the strength they imparted to our society was truly great.
However, as the religious world in our society seems to be experiencing various internal conflicts and disturbances lately, perhaps showing some regression, rather than inviting some famous religious figure from abroad, I believe the more pertinent issue in reality is striving for the internal enrichment of our religious world, which is the urgent task of the moment.
I am merely stating a few thoughts that come to mind regarding your company's question.

Even Bearing the Expenses is Difficult

Kim Ch'angje (金昶濟) (Mr. Kim is a Professor at Ewha Women's Normal High School) <30>

All the distinguished figures mentioned in your inquiry would be good to invite to Chosŏn, but I believe there wouldn't be much gained just by hearing one or two lectures from them. Also, given the environment, we wouldn't be able to hear lectures from politicians or thinkers, nor could we afford the expenses to invite them.
Therefore, I dislike engaging in empty speculation. Besides, aren't people who can read already generally familiar with the writings of so-and-so?

The Philosopher Dr. Barth

Hong Pyŏngsŏn (洪秉璇) (Mr. Hong is an executive of the Chosŏn Christian Youth Association, Pastor)

I believe your company's question reflects the most appropriate thinking in Chosŏn society at present.
However, how can a poor society like Chosŏn invite famous foreign figures?
First, it is questionable whether it is economically permissible. Therefore, might it not just be idealistic talk?
But setting this aside, assuming everything is possible, who would I like to invite? In the religious field, especially as a theologian, I would like to invite the world-famous religious philosopher of the German Mystic school, Dr. Barth.
Regarding scientists or politicians, I believe that is a question to be answered after more thought.

The Poet Raymond Schwartz

Kim Ansŏ (金岸曙) (Mr. Kim is a Poet)

No matter how poor one is, they say, "Where can a man not meet another?" (男兒何處不相逢), so it seems plausible one might meet living people if luck is good, but meeting those already dead would be extremely difficult. If spiritualism (降神術) could advance a bit more so that we could meet even as spirits, summoning people like Strindberg or Schopenhauer and having them discuss their views on modern Chosŏn women would be extremely interesting.

And among the living, people I'd like to show the state of modern Chosŏn to include Ferenc Molnár or Raymond Schwartz. It would be worth seeing their expressions as they turn away, smirking wryly.<31>

The Scientist Dr. Einstein

Hong Yangmyŏng (洪陽明) (Mr. Hong is the Foreign News Editor of the Chosŏn Ilbo)

If it were possible to invite them:
Scholar - Not just a scientist but also a progressive thinker, Dr. Einstein.
Politician - President Masaryk of Czechoslovakia (the modern politician with the greatest power of dedication, wisdom, and intuition, and a typical upholder of democracy).
Artist - As writers, Mr. Maxim Gorky or Mr. Lu Xun (魯迅); as a musician, Mr. Paderewski of Poland.

Drs. Wolf and Mott

Hŏ Pongnak (許奉洛) (Mr. Hŏ is an executive of the Chosŏn Sunday School Association)

Regarding the survey question from your company, as someone in the religious field, I refrain from speaking about thinkers, politicians, or artists. Here, I will only mention those from the religious field whom I believe our society must invite.
Dr. Bieder Wolf (삐 덜·울푸)
Dr. Mott (whom we have called "Moktŏk" (목덕) in Korean). I would like to invite these two gentlemen and let the youth of Chosŏn today hear their words.
Dr. Wolf is a famous American religious leader whose passionate and powerful speeches would surely be great spiritual nourishment for the youth of Chosŏn today.
Dr. Mott is also a world-famous religious figure, not just in America. He visited Tokyo years ago, and the government treated him as an honored guest, welcoming him in various ways. If our society were to invite a famous foreign religious leader, I would really like these two to be invited.

Wishing to Hear "Adherence to Truth"

Sin T'aek'ak (辛泰嶽) (Mr. Sin is a Lawyer)

I wish we could invite Mr. Gandhi of India and hear his explanation regarding "Adherence to Truth" (眞理의 把持).<32>
However, is it possible for this to actually happen?

The Musician Schipa

Hyŏn Chemyŏng (玄濟明) (Mr. Hyŏn is a Professor at Yŏnhŭi College)

Thinker: Gandhi
Artist
Musician… Schipa
… Kreisler

Someone Who Would Come on Their Own Accord

Yi In (李仁) (Mr. Yi is a Lawyer)

If our circumstances were just a little better, we'd want to try everything others do, but since we cannot, rather, if there were someone who could dissect the heart of the Chosŏn people, make them cry and anger them, and give some suggestion, I wish they could come. But from what I've seen (내가 본 배론), such people seem to have dried up (씨가 마른 듯하다).
And so, it can't be helped; anyone who might come would be fine, so if possible, I wish they would just come on their own feet so we could meet. If the cost were the same, I wish Mr. Dé Valera or Aguinaldo or Mr. 000 residing in the Netherlands could come and just talk.

Mr. Hastings

Chŏng T'aehŭi (鄭泰熙) (Mr. Chŏng is the Pastor of Sangdong Church)

If Chosŏn society were to invite famous foreign figures, especially from the religious field, the ones I would like our society to invite are the famous American theologians Mr. Hastings and Mr. Wellington Day. I wish we could invite these two gentlemen to speak to the youth of Chosŏn.

The Musician Schipa

Kim Nŭngin (金陵人) (Mr. Kim is the Literary Director of the Okeh Record Company)

I answer your question.
As a world scholar, I'd like to invite Einstein <33> and hear about the indescribable state of mind of possessing unparalleled genius yet losing one's nationality and wandering. As an artist, I'd like to call the Italian opera singer Schipa, put him on the stage of Kyŏngsŏng's Tansŏngsa Theater, and listen to his refreshingly clear voice that feels like it could burst the chest, even just for one night.
I cannot think of any thinkers or politicians I would like to invite.

The Sculptor Meštrović

Chang Pal (張勃) (Mr. Chang is an Artist, Professor at Hwimun Normal High School)

I would invite Jacques Maritain of France, an authority on Scholastic philosophy, as a university professor; G. K. Chesterton and Hilaire Belloc of Britain, along with Mr. Wells, as public debate speakers; composer Igor Stravinsky and sculptor Ivan Meštrović are particular invitations I'd make.
I'd show Mr. Meštrović the Sŏkkuram Grotto in Kyŏngju, and I'd commission Mr. Stravinsky to arrange Aak (雅樂 - court music).

**Summary**

This 1935 article from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* surveys prominent Korean figures (journalists, religious leaders, educators, writers, lawyers, artists, etc.) on which world-renowned politicians, thinkers, writers, scientists, or artists they would like to invite to Korea, mirroring invitations extended by Japanese organizations. Responses varied widely, including scientists like Einstein and Millikan; writers like Gide, Rolland, Shaw, Wells, Tagore, Hu Shih, Gorky, and Lu Xun; religious figures like Fosdick, Niebuhr, Barth, Mott, and Gandhi; musicians like Schipa, Kreisler, Paderewski, and Stravinsky; and even controversial political figures like Mussolini and Masaryk, alongside thinkers like Russell and Radek. Several respondents expressed practical concerns about funding or the political feasibility (censorship) of such invitations in colonial Korea, while others specifically declined to name politicians or thinkers due to the sensitive environment.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the intellectual currents and international awareness among the Korean elite during the colonial period. The choices reflect a desire to engage with leading global figures across diverse fields, indicating aspirations for cultural and intellectual exchange despite colonial limitations. The frequent mentions of figures known for progressive thought, anti-fascism (Gide, Rolland), or non-violent resistance (Gandhi), alongside the explicit reluctance by some to discuss politics, highlight the complex navigating of ideas under Japanese rule. The inclusion of figures like Mussolini, alongside democratic leaders and socialist thinkers, also shows the diverse range of political models being considered or discussed at the time. The practical acknowledgements of financial and political barriers underscore the realities faced by Koreans seeking engagement with the wider world.

招請案-世界的 政治家·思想家·文豪·科學者
東京 改造社에서 著名한 科學者 아인슈타인 博士와 타골 詩聖을, 讀賣新聞에서 米國野球團을 초청하드시 우리 사회에서 해외 著名한 科學者, 思想家 文豪를 招請한다면 엇든 분을 選하고 십슴니까.<26>

宗敎家 포스틕 博士를

具滋玉 (氏는 朝鮮基督聯合會 總務)

종교가로는 포스틱(Dr, Harry Lmerrson Fosdick)博士.
과학자로는 밀니칸 博士.
이 두 분을 招請하고 십슴니다.
포스틕 박사로 말하면 내가 在米時에 자주 맛나 그이의 하는 演說 같은 것을 많이 들어보았는데 이 분은 참으로 훌륭한 어룬임니다.
이 분은 지금 70세 가량된 노인으로서 그이의 聲望은 全世界에 빛나고 있음니다.
내가 在米時에는 1주일에 한 번식은 으레히 마이크로폰 앞에 나서서 放送演說을 하고는 하였지요.
지금은 뉴-욕시 『江邊敎會』를 單獨으로 經營하는 이로 그 교회는 7백만원이라는 거대한 금액을 듸려 지은 世界的大敎會堂으로서 아마도 『聖페트로교회당』보다 美術的으론 못할넌지 몰나도 가장 現代式으로된 건축으로서 그 안에는 모든 文化的設備를 하여 잇음니다.
이렇게 훌융한 교회를 單獨 經營하는 외에 「全米聯合神學校」敎授로도 있어 이 분의 存在는 宗敎界에 있어서는 세계적인 巨物인줄로 암니다.
또 밀니칸 博士는 미국의 유명한 과학자로 지금 「캘니포니아」에서 대학교수로 있는 이외다.
「아인슈타인」과는 특별한 親交를 맺고 있는 이로 전반 아인슈타인 박사가 獨逸 나치스에게서 追放을 당하였을 때에도 남달니 몸소 나서서 「아박사」를 周旋하여 미국으로 다려오게 한 이외다.
이 분은 유명한 과학자인 동시에 또한 유명한 宗敎家인 줄로 암니다.
씨는 그 언젠가 3천여 명이나 모힌 大集會장에서 演說한 일이 있는데 그때 말하되
「나는 과학자외다! 오늘날까지 60여 년 동안을 科學에다 온 精力을 다하여왔고 또한 연구를 쌓어왔음니다. 그러나 과학의 힘으로는 到底히 「하누님」(예수)를 證明할 수는 없음니다. 그러치만 나는 내마음속으로 생각하여 보면 어쩐지 말로는 표현할 수 없으나 제절로 「하누님」을 믿게됨니다! 또한 믿을 수박에는 없음니다.」라고 한 말은 유명한 말이였음니다.
이 분은 세계 각국의 名譽博士만도 14개의 박사학위를 얻은 분임니다.<27>

못트(Mott) 博士. (조선말로 「목덕」이라고 불너왔지만) 이 두 분을 초청해다 오늘날 조선청년들에게 말슴을 들여주고 십슴니다.
울푸박사는 米國의 유명한 宗敎家로 그 熱이 있고 힘있는 연설은 오늘날 朝鮮靑年들에게는 실로 커다란 精神的 糧食이 될 쭐로 암니다.
못트박사도 米國에서 뿐만이 않이라 世界的인 유명한 宗敎家로서 이분은 年前 東京에까지 건너왔든 일이 있는데 政府에서는 貴賓으로 모시는 등 실로 여러 방면으로 환영하였든 일이 있는 분임니다. 만약 리 사회에서 外國의 유명한 宗敎家를 초청한다면 이 두분을 꼭 초청하였으면 조켓슴니다.
