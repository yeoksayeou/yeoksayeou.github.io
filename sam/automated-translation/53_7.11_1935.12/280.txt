童話, 거북이와 토끼
						崔仁化

In a certain deep mountain, there was a wide field. In this field, when spring came, red flowers, white flowers, yellow flowers, flowers of all colors bloomed in abundance.

One warm spring day, the mountain spirit, leading many heavenly maidens (선녀), came to see the flowers in this field. Hearing the news that this spirit was coming, the birds and beasts living in this mountain all hid in their caves and under rocks.

In this field lived a rabbit who was an exceptionally good dancer. This rabbit, wondering how he could show his skillful dance to the spirit, stuck his head out of his hole, glancing around (홀군홀군), observing the spirit's movements.

A short while later, beautiful five-colored clouds suddenly descended from the sky and covered the field. The rabbit, twitching his long ears, watched the brilliant (령룡한), beautiful five-colored clouds with his pretty, glass bead-like eyes. After a little while, the five-colored clouds faded away without a trace, and the mountain spirit, dressed in strange clothes as white as snow with a beautiful sparkle, stood there, surrounded and guarded (옹위해서) by the heavenly maidens with their white wings spread (돗친). The spirit looked at the maidens and said,
"Children, it has been a long time since I came to this flower field. You all, dance in this flower field (꽃밧헤서)."

Twelve heavenly maidens immediately stepped forward and danced marvelously, fluttering their beautiful wings. However, the maidens disliked touching the ground with their feet, so from beginning to end (나종), they danced lightly and nimbly (삽분 삽분) only in the air, without touching the ground.<173> Seeing this, the mountain spirit said,
"I wanted to see dancing on the ground today, but none of you touched the ground. I want to see the dance of the beasts living here, so have the beasts come out and dance."

At this, one of the twelve maidens put a beautiful golden trumpet to her lips and blew,
"Hey—all you beasts living in this mountain! If there is anyone among you who dances well, come out quickly and dance before the spirit. Do-do-do-dot-do-do-"

Hearing the sound of the trumpet, the rabbit hopped out of his hole, shook his long ears, strutted proudly (웃쭐웃쭐), and danced with great enthusiasm.

Having watched the rabbit's amusing dance, the mountain spirit said, "Your dance is quite entertaining. As a reward, I will grant whatever you wish, so state your desire."

The rabbit had been greatly tormented by the monkey, the deer, and the wild boar in this mountain.
"Thank you very much. It is presumptuous of me to say, but I would be grateful if you would make me the king who rules over all the beasts in this mountain."

"Then, as you wish, from today you are the king of this mountain," the spirit declared, commanding a maiden to inform all the beasts living in this mountain that the rabbit had become their king starting today.

As the spirit's words fell, the maiden stepped forward, put a silver trumpet to her lips, and blew,
"Hey—all you birds and beasts living in this mountain! Listen carefully (자세이 아러드러라)! From today, this rabbit has become the king of this mountain. If any among you disobey the rabbit king's command or look down upon (업수이 여기엿다가는) the rabbit king, flying birds will instantly be unable to fly, and crawling beasts will be unable to walk. Do-do-do-dot-do."

The beasts hidden under the rocks and the mountain birds perched hidden on tree branches looked at each other and whispered in low voices,
"That rabbit fellow will surely boast (뽑내겟네) about being king starting tomorrow."

A short while later, the spirit, leading the heavenly maidens, rode the five-colored clouds again and went somewhere.

The rabbit was overjoyed. He leaped onto a rock and jumped down, climbed up a large tree (나무구루) and climbed down, hopping here and there across the wide field, beside himself with joy.

Just then, from under a rock over there, a tortoise came crawling out slowly (엉금엉금).<174>
"Oh, Mr. (생원) Rabbit. You seem to be having a great time. Well then, let's have some fun together today," said the tortoise. The rabbit suddenly puffed out his chest arrogantly, putting on an air of authority, and boasted,
"Hey, you insolent fellow (고현놈). From today, I am the king of this mountain. From now on, you are all my subjects."

"Nonsense, that can't be right."

"You rascal, didn't you just hear the maiden's trumpet?"

"I heard it, indeed. But that was addressed to the birds and beasts living in this mountain. It has nothing to do with me."

"Why not?"

"Why not, you ask? I am neither a bird nor a beast (즘생)."

"Then are you a fish?"

"I am not a fish either."

"Then are you an insect?"

"I am not an insect either. I am a tortoise. A tortoise that lives for ten thousand years."

"Really? When were you born to live ten thousand years?"

"It was 1935 years ago. I was born on the very day Jesus was born in Bethlehem in Judea (유대ㅅ나라 베들네헴). No matter how much you boast about being the king of birds and beasts, I still know your grandfather and father well. Your respected father cried 'k'aeng-k'aeng' for several days (멫을 동안) after a hunter shot his left ear with a gun. And your grandfather lay sick for about ten days after an eagle tried to snatch him and pulled out his tail."

"Hey, look here. Why do you keep digging up (둘추는가) other people's flaws?"

"If I don't, will you play nicely with me?"

"Well..." the rabbit thought about it.
As if a good idea struck him, the rabbit said,
"Hey, tortoise. Instead of this, let's have a race tomorrow morning, you and I. Let's decide that whoever wins will be the superior one (우뜸)."

"What's the point of being superior? I don't care about being superior. What happens if I win?"

"If I lose and you win, I will bow to you a hundred times. And I will give you the position of king. But if you lose, you will be my servant for generations to come."

"Alright," agreed the tortoise. The tortoise and the rabbit parted, promising to meet again the next day.

The rabbit was confident he could beat the tortoise, so he slept soundly (쿨쿨) without any worries. However, the tortoise, no matter how much he thought, couldn't figure out how to win a race against the rabbit. Unable to think of a solution, the tortoise went to find his close friend, the owl (올뺌이), who lived in the forest. The owl listened to the tortoise's story and said,
"My friend, don't worry at all. There's a good way.<175> If you do this, it will work out fine without any worries."
He whispered into the tortoise's ear. Hearing the plan, the tortoise said,
"Right! Right! That should work!" and returned to his home.

The next day, the tortoise and the rabbit came to the place they had agreed to meet (맛나기로), greeted each other, and were about to start the race. The tortoise said,
"Alright then, let's make the base of this pine tree the starting point, and the fir tree over there the finish line. And you run to the right of the large zelkova tree in the middle, and I will run to the left."
The rabbit agreed. Then the tortoise added,
"Now, after we both race, we shouldn't argue about who won. So we need to choose a judge."

"Right. So who should we choose as the judge?"
The rabbit looked around and glanced up at the pine tree. On the pine tree, an owl was looking down, its large eyes blinking (뒤룩뒤룩). The rabbit said,
"Hey, Owl. Please be the judge for our race."

"Yes. Indeed, Your Majesty the King, I am honored. I will do it. Moreover, since my two brothers are on the zelkova tree in the middle and the fir tree over there, one on each, if we three brothers judge, it will be very fair," said the owl, bowing his head from the tree branch.

"Let's start the race now." The owl counted,
"One, two, three!"
The tortoise and the rabbit started running.
When the rabbit, running with all four feet, got close (가슬때) to the zelkova tree in the middle, the owl on that tree called out,
"King Rabbit, please hurry! The tortoise has already passed!"

"Really?"
He ran on, and indeed, the tortoise was already ahead (압서서), running busily. The rabbit thought,
"This is serious!"
He closed his eyes and ran hard. When he got close to the fir tree marking the finish line, the owl on that fir tree shouted (부르지젓습니다),
"King Rabbit, hurry! If you don't hurry, you'll lose!"
The rabbit ran with all his might, but when he reached the finish line, the tortoise had already arrived under the fir tree first.

"The tortoise has won!" declared the owl from the fir tree branch. All the beasts watching from the forest ran out, shouting,
"Long live the tortoise!"
The rabbit was so furious <176> he said,
"Let's do it one more time!"

This time, they decided to start from this fir tree and go to the pine tree over there. And when the owl counted,
"One, two, three!"
The tortoise and the rabbit started running (달녀가기) again. But this time too, when the rabbit got close (갓가히) to the zelkova tree in the middle, the owl on the zelkova branch said,
"King Rabbit, please hurry! The tortoise has already passed!"
As the rabbit ran with all his might towards the pine tree over there, the owl on that pine tree said,
"King Rabbit, please hurry! The tortoise is almost here!"
The rabbit, sweating profusely, ran to the finish line, but the tortoise had already arrived.

The rabbit was so angry he couldn't bear it. He raced again, and again, ten times in total. The rabbit lost all ten times. His legs hurt so much that he had no choice but to surrender. As promised, he had to bow one hundred times for each loss, so the rabbit ended up bowing a thousand times to the tortoise.

The tortoise sat on a rock receiving the bows, while the rabbit bowed before him. The owl on the branch counted (세이엿습니다), "One, two, three, four." The tortoise, pleased, called out,
"Hey, fellow tortoises, come here! The king of this mountain is bowing to me right now. All my friends, come out!"
Then, from under the zelkova tree in the middle, one tortoise came crawling out (엉금엉금), and from under the fir tree over there, another tortoise came crawling out (엉금엉금). These three tortoises, radiating dignity, received the bows together.

The birds and all the beasts in the mountain, realizing the reason why the tortoise had won, burst out laughing (웃섯습니다), "Hahaha!"
The tortoise also found it so funny (우수워서) that he couldn't stand it, clutching his stomach and rolling around with laughter for a while. The tortoise laughed so hard that his back split open. The rabbit bowed so much that his front paws blistered and hurt terribly, becoming almost unbearable (막 터지게 앞어). And his eyeballs bulged out.

"This is a disaster!" They called the mole, who was the doctor of this mountain, to perform surgery. The tortoise had the crack on his back sewn up with thread and fixed (곤첫습니다). That's why, even now, the lines on a tortoise's back are said to be where it cracked back then. The rabbit's front paws were examined, and it was found that germs had entered the toenails, so they had to be shortened (짤는) a little. That's why rabbit's front paws are still said to be a bit short (쩔다고) today. Medicine was put (너엇스나) in his eyes, but they didn't heal (낫지가 안어), and from that time on, his eyes remained bright red and couldn't move (음직여지지) at all.
(End) <177>

**Summary**

This story retells the fable of the tortoise and the hare with significant variations. A mountain spirit grants a dancing rabbit kingship over mountain animals. A tortoise challenges the rabbit's authority, arguing he is neither bird nor beast. They agree to a race: if the tortoise wins, the rabbit bows 100 times and gives up kingship; if the rabbit wins, the tortoise becomes his servant. The tortoise, advised by an owl, arranges for two identical tortoise relatives and three collaborating owl judges to be placed along the race course. The rabbit is tricked into thinking the tortoise is always ahead and loses ten consecutive races, forced to bow 1,000 times. The story concludes by explaining that the cracks on a tortoise's shell are from laughing too hard, and the rabbit's short front paws and red, immobile eyes are results of excessive bowing and the ordeal.

From a colonial history perspective, the story is interesting for its blend of traditional Korean folklore elements (mountain spirit, animal kingdom) with a specific Western/Christian reference (Jesus's birth in Bethlehem, dated 1935 years prior to the story's publication). The themes of challenged authority (rabbit king vs. clever tortoise), the use of trickery and collaboration (tortoises and owls) to overcome perceived power, and the etiological explanations for animal characteristics might reflect subtle commentaries on power dynamics, resistance, or perhaps just creative storytelling incorporating elements from different cultural spheres present in 1930s Korea.

						崔仁化


어떤 깊은 산 속에 넓은 들이 잇엇습니다. 이 들에는 봄이 되면 빨간꽃, 힌꽃, 노란꽃, 가지각색 꽃들이 가득이 피여이섯읍니다.
어떤 따스한 봄날 산신령은 많은 선녀들을 다리고 이 벌판에 꽃을 보시려 오시게 되엿읍니다. 이 신령님이 오신다는 소문을 듯고 이 산속에 사는 새와 즘생들은 모다 굴속과 바위 밑으로 다 숨어버리엿읍니다.
이 벌판에는 춤을 썩 잘추는 토끼 한마리가 이섯읍니다. 이 톡끼는 자기의 잘추는 춤을 어떻게 한번 신령님께 뵈엿스면 하고 구멍으로 머리를 내밀고 홀군홀군하면서 신령님의 거동만 엿보고 이섯습니다.
조곰 후에 하늘 공즁에서 난데없는 아름다운 오색 구름이 펄펄 나려와 이 펄판을 뒤 덮엇습니다. 토끼는 기다란 두 귀를 쫑깃쫑깃하면서 유리알같은 어여쁜 눈으로 그 오색이 령룡한 아름다운 구름를 보고 이섯읍니다. 조곰 후에 오색구름은 간곳이 없이 슬어지고 눈과 같치 히고 어여쁜 빛이 반짝이는 이상한 의복을 입은 산신령과 힌나래를 돗친 선녀들이 신령님을 옹위해서 이섯읍니다. 신령님은
「얘들아 이 꽃밭에 오래간만에 왓다. 너이들 이 꽃밧헤서 춤이나 추어라」
하고 선녀들을 바라보면서 말하엿습니다.
열두 선녀가 곳 나서서 어여쁜 나래를 날리면서 묘하게 춤을 추엇습니다. 그러나 선녀들은 땅우에 발을 딥는 것이 실허서 처음부터 나종까지 발을 땅에 대지 않고 공즁에서만<173> 경쾌하게 삽분 삽분 춤을 추웟습니다. 이것을 보신 산신령님은
「나는 오늘 땅우에서 추는 춤을 보려고 햇는데 너이들은 종내 한사람도 발을 땅에 딥지안는구나. 이곳에 사는 즘생들의 춤을 좀 구경하고 싶으니 즘생들이 나와서 춤을 추게해라」
하엿습니다.
이때에 열두선녀 가운데 한 선녀가 아름다운 금색 나발을 입에 대고
「여-이 이 산속에 사는 즘생들아. 누구든지 춤 잘 추는 이가 잇거든 곳 나와서 신령님 앞에서 춤을 추어 드리여라. 도도도돗도도-」
하고 나발을 불엇습니다.
이 나발소래를 드른 토끼가 구멍 속에서 호닥딱 뛰여 나와서 기다란 두 귀를 흔둘흔둘하고 웃쭐웃쭐하면서 열심으로 춤을 추엇습니다.
이 토끼가 재미잇게 추는 춤을 구경하신 산신령은 「네가 추는 춤이 퍽 재미있다. 그 상급으로 무엇이든지 네가 원하는대로 줄 터이니 네 소원을 말해라」
하고 말하엿습니다.
토끼는 그 동안 이 산속에서 원숭이와 사슴이와 산도야지에게 무척 괴롬을 받고 이섯습니다.
「참 감사합니다. 말슴드리기는 황송하오나 이 산즁 모든 즘생을 주장하는 임금이 되게 해주시면 고맙겟습니다」
「그러면 네 소원대로 오늘부터는 너는 이 산즁에 왕이 되엿다」
하고 말슴하며 선녀에게 이 산에 사는 모든 즘생에게 토끼가 오늘부터는 이 산즁에 왕이 되엿다는 것을 알리라고 명령하엿습니다.
이 신령님의 말슴이 떠러지자 선녀가 나서더니 은나발을 입에대고
「여-이  이 산즁에 사는 새와 즘생들아. 자세이 아러드러라. 오늘부터는 이 토끼가 이 산즁의 왕이 되엿다. 너이들즁에 누구든지 이 토끼왕의 명령을 거역하든지 또는 토끼왕을 업수이 여기엿다가는 당장에 나는 새는 날지를 못하게 되고 기어단니는 즘생이 거러단닐수가 없게 된다. 도도도돗도」
하고 은나팔을 불엇습니다.
바위밑에 숨어잇든 즘생들과 나무가지에 숨어 앉어잇든 산새들은
「고 토끼란 놈이 내일부터는 임금이라고 막 뽑내겟네 그려」
하고 서로 바라보고 적은 목소래로 소근 거리엇습니다.
조곰 후에 신령님은 선녀들을 다리고 다시 오색 구름을 타고 어듸로 가시엿습니다.



토끼는 너무도 기뻐서 바위우에 뛰여 올라갓다 나려왓다 큰 나무구루에 올라갓다 나려왓다하며 그 너른 들판에 이리 뛰구 저리 뛰구하며 혼자 미칠뜻이 기뻐하엿읍니다.
이때에 저쪽 바위밑에서 거북이 한마리가 엉금엉금 기여 나오면서<174>
「어 토끼 생원. 퍽 재미가 조흔 모양일세 그려. 자 오늘 나하고 좀 재미잇게 노라보세」
하고 거북이가 말하니 토끼는 별안간 거만스럽게 가슴을 내밀고 위엄을 보이는 듯이 뽐내면서
「어이 고현놈. 오늘부터는 내가 이 산즁 왕이다. 이제부터는 너이들은 내 부하이다」
「천만에 그럴리가 잇나」
「이놈 너는 지금 그 선녀의 나발소리를 못드럿느냐」
「듯기는 드럿네. 그러나 그것은 이 산에 사는 새와 즘생들에게 말햇지. 내게는 아모 상관이 없네」
「엇째서?」
「엇째서라니. 나는 새도 안이고 즘생도 안일세」
「그러면 고기냐?」
「고기도 안일세」
