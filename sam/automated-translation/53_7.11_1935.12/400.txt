Magazine Title: 삼천리 (三千里)
Publication Date: 1935-12-01
Article Title: 諸家推薦 新人作品集-春城 薦, 月夜의 把守兵(시) (Collection of Works by New Talents Recommended by Various Masters - Recommended by Ch'unsŏng, Moonlit Night's Sentry (Poem))
Author: No Chayŏng (盧子泳 - Recommender), Kim Hongch'ŏl (金洪哲 - Poet)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Regarding the "Samch'ŏlli" New Talent Recommendation - Several poems were submitted to this humble person. However, upon reading them all, there was not a single piece that was particularly refreshing. There was almost nothing I wished to recommend. But, complying with the request to recommend at least one piece, I submit this one work, "Moonlit Night's Sentry" by Mr. Kim Hongch'ŏl. <236>

Moonlit Night's Sentry

Kim Hongch'ŏl

Over the crumbling, sagging ridge,
The distorted autumn moonlight
Gathers and flows down into my little paradise (小樂園).
Blooming alone in the rough field,
The cosmos, smiling a smile full of sorrow,
Oh—the sentry unit (番兵隊) guarding the quiet night garden.
The messenger (傳令使) reporting reconnaissance from the enemy camp (敵陣)—
How many times did it swim
Across the chilly, blue sky?
The cricket's song, murmuring mournfully,
Is the signal trumpet (信號喇叭) for a brave advance—
Whee—whee—
Oh—my garden, finally trampled down,
My sole companion (伴侶者) taken captive (推虜),
The five-colored petals (페달) scattered by my love
Embrace my dreams
And flutter lightly, yet
Where have you headed?
The soft, pale fragrance
Exhaled by red lips
Had boosted my young soul—
But this lonely body, tormented by solitude,
These aimless footsteps,
Where shall they wander?
Oh—my whole life—
The sweet dream of past happiness—
Now my heart, dotted gray (灰色),
What shall I offer to that love in dreamland?
Going to the rough garden, growing old,
Shall I just shed tears of recollection (追憶)? - End<237>

**Summary**

No Chayŏng (pen name Ch'unsŏng), tasked with recommending a new poet for *Samch'ŏlli* magazine, expresses initial disappointment with the submissions but ultimately selects Kim Hongch'ŏl's poem, "Moonlit Night's Sentry." The poem uses the metaphor of a sentry guarding a garden under the moonlight. It describes a scene of beauty (cosmos, moonlight) intertwined with sorrow and loss, employing military imagery (sentry, enemy camp, messenger, signal trumpet, captive) to depict the trampling of the garden (paradise) and the capture of a beloved companion. The speaker laments their loneliness and uncertain future, left only with memories. For historians, the poem's blend of pastoral imagery with stark military metaphors during the height of Japanese colonial militarization (1935) is noteworthy. It might reflect the pervasive atmosphere of control and suppressed anxieties, or perhaps use these metaphors to express personal loss in a politically charged environment where direct expression could be censored. No Chayŏng's commentary also offers a glimpse into the editorial process and standards of literary journals of the time.

「三千里」 新人推薦에 대하며-拙者에게도 멫 篇의 詩가 들어왓다. 그러나 모다 읽어보면 그리 시원한 거시 一篇도 없었다. 推薦하고 싶은 것이 별로 없섯든 거시다. 그러나 기어히 一篇만은 推薦해 달라는 부탁에 의하며 金洪哲氏의 「月夜의 把守兵」 一篇을 보내는 바이다. <236>

月夜의 把守兵

金洪哲

문허저 가는 늘바재울 넘어로
이즈러진 가을달빗치
고이고 흘너내리는 나의 小樂園이며
거츠른 벌판에 외로히 피여
哀愁에 가득찬 微笑를 하는 코스모쓰는
오-고요한 밤 庭園을 직히는 番兵隊
敵陣의 偵察을 報告하는 傳令使는
싸늘싸늘한 푸른 하날은
멫번이나 헤염첫든고-
처량이 읇조리는 귀뜨램이 노래는
勇敢한 進軍의 信號喇叭-
휘-휘-
오-맞침내 짓밟핀 나의 동산아
推虜가 되야 간 나의 唯一의 伴侶者며
님의 뿌리고 간 五色 「페달은」
나의 꿈에 안기며
하늘하늘 나붓기고 있오이다 만은
그대는 어듸로 향하얏나잇가?
붉은 입술이 뿜어내이는
보드러운 뽀-얀 香氣는
나이 적은 魂을 북도다 주웟것만-
孤獨에 시달인 외로운 이몸은
지행업는 이 거름발을
어대로 저어가릿까.
오-나에 一生이며-
지낸날에 幸福의 단꿈이며-
지금의 나의 마음 點點한 灰色
꿈나라 그님에게 무엇을 듸리오릿가?
거츠른 동산에가 늘게서
追憶의 눈물이나 흘여보녀- 끝<237>
