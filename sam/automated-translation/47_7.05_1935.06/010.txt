Intellectual World, Academic World
First Academic Boom Since the March First Movement
This March, schools in Seoul experienced an extraordinary boom, the first seen in over 10 years. That is, from middle schools up to various professional schools, the number of applicants reached seven, eight, even ten times the admission quota, creating a prosperity unseen in recent times.
The reason for this extraordinary vitality is attributed to the recent rise in rice prices in rural areas across the Peninsula, somewhat improving the farmers' financial situation, combined with an enhanced passion for learning, desiring to educate their children. This truly marks the first such boom in over a decade, and significant attention is reportedly being paid to how long this educational fervor will last in the coming years.

Impressive Sun Wen Memorial Service
A grand memorial service commemorating the tenth anniversary of Sun Wen's (Sun Yat-sen) passing, hosted by the Kuomintang's Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) Branch, was held. It took place precisely at 11 AM on March 15th. As a gentle atmosphere currently prevails in Sino-Japanese diplomatic relations, the venue, Pukpong Hall in Kwansu-dong, was properly adorned with floral wreaths and offerings from Governor-General Ugaki and various officials and civilians. Moving into the ceremony, commemorative addresses were given by three individuals: Central Daily News President Yŏ Unhyŏng, Commercial Bank President Pak Yŏngch'ŏl, and Chōsen Army Chief of Staff Ōkushi. Then came the reading of the memorial address, featuring a lengthy lamentation with choked voice and tears (聲淚共下) from the representative of the Chinese residents. Following this, Mr. Pak Kŭm—a Korean who had once received a gift of a full military uniform worn by Chiang Kai-shek for presenting insightful opinions (卓見具陳) on the statesmanship concerning Manchuria and Mongolia—delivered a memorial address lasting over an hour. Its main point concerned the grand design (經倫) of Great Asianism (大亞細亞主義) for East Asian nations based on equality, deeply moving all attendees. Next, Keijō Mayor Idate's memorial address was read. Thus, the ceremony concluded amidst the mourning of over five hundred attendees. Notably, the aforementioned Mr. Pak Kŭm's memorial address was reportedly sent even to the Nanjing government.

Exposition News
People in Seoul don't particularly remember it, but recently, many people from the countryside, hearing there's an exposition in Seoul, are preparing to travel to Seoul with their bundles (봇다리짐) during the April-May season to see the exposition. As for what this exposition is about, it's not hosted by the Government-General this time but is an industrial exposition organized by a newspaper company called Chōsen Shinbunsha located in Namch'on (South Village). Although its name is Exposition, its small scale makes it comparable to a mere exhibition. It seems best not to come up expecting too much.
Also, word has it that they are soliciting exposition advertising towers in four or five locations in Seoul, such as Chongno, Sŏdaemun Street, Namdaemun Street, and in front of the train station, charging 1,000 Won per location. The exposition period is said to be from April 20th to June 10th.

The Missionary Who Baptized Ch'unwŏn
Ch'unwŏn (pen name of Yi Kwangsu) published his essay "New Life Theory," which caused a great sensation in society at the time, and was subsequently excommunicated from the church. The person who originally baptized Ch'unwŏn was a Western missionary, Mr. M, who serves as the principal of P'yŏngyang Theological Seminary. It is said that Mr. M still keeps Yi Kwangsu's name written in his notebook and prays to God for his swift repentance. Hearing this, Ch'unwŏn sometimes sheds tears, remembering the past (넷일) and grateful for the affection (恩情) that never forgets him.

A Lawsuit Worth 500,000 Won
Regarding the much-troubled Kogye Academy issue, Mr. Pang Ŭngmo, with lawyer So Wankyu as his legal representative (訴訟代理人), has filed a lawsuit against Mr. Ko Hŭiwŏn in the Keijō District Court for 500,000 Won or thereabouts. Meanwhile, the defendant, Mr. Ko Hŭiwŏn, has appointed Mr. Kim Pyŏngno as his legal representative, and the trial is currently underway. In any case, it is a major case demanding that Mr. Ko Hŭiwŏn hand over all his land, valued at up to 500,000 Won, to Kogye Academy. The outcome (如何) is drawing much attention, and details are planned for next month's issue (來月號) of this magazine (本誌上).

Ms. Kye Chŏngsik, who returned from Germany and recently settled the concert issue involving the three newspaper companies, is said to be expressing her intention to study Korean folk songs and court music (雅樂) and attend the Yi Royal Household Music Department (李王職雅樂部).

Okp'yŏn and Ch'unhyangjŏn are Number One
The current state of the Peninsula's publishing world is lamentable. If you ask what books sell the most in Chōsen right now, they are still the Okp'yŏn (玉篇 - Hanja dictionary) and Ch'unhyangjŏn (春香傳 - Tale of Ch'unhyang). There is a Wholesalers' Association (都賣商組合) in Seoul composed of wholesalers, and according to their investigation in this area:
Okp'yŏn      Annual    20,000 volumes
Ch'unhyangjŏn      Annual    70,000 volumes
Sim Ch'ŏngjŏn      Annual    60,000 volumes
Hong Kiltongjŏn    Annual    45,000 volumes
Miscellaneous Songbooks (雜歌冊)    Annual    15,000 volumes
etc., it is said. How do you, readers, view this situation? When the sorrow of exposing reality reaches this point, it feels like the curtain might fall on a kind of comedy.
However, while such large numbers of copies are being consumed in the book market as mentioned above, through what channels are these books distributed? It is said they are sold exclusively by about 1,400-1,500 peddlers (봇찜장사) who travel from market to market in the countryside. It is also a noteworthy phenomenon that the sales figures for the aforementioned books are demanded in the same quantities every year.

Commercial World, Business World
Reviving Hwasin Department Store
Due to the major fire it experienced (맛난), some newspapers reported the fire insurance payout received by Hwasin as 450,000 Won, while others reported 500,000 Won, causing inconsistency. However, after our company investigated the most accurate facts, it turns out that even though it's called Hwasin, the East Wing's five-story building was rented property owned by Mr. Min Kyusik, so not a single penny of the insurance money for that building could be claimed. Only the insurance attached (부첫든) to the goods lost from inventory amounted to 300,000 Won. After extinguishing the fire, some partially burnt remaining goods were found, and when all such things were included in the calculation, eventually, a 10% deduction of 30,000 Won was made from the 300,000 Won, and the full amount of 270,000 Won＜30＞ was received (밧엇다). Furthermore, the eight-story large building being constructed in front of the Chongno Police Station at a cost of supposedly 1 million Won will only begin construction this autumn and cannot be used until at least three years later. Therefore, in the meantime, they plan to reopen for business in the old Min Kyusik building where they operated previously (녯날). Currently, that building is being repaired, and the East Wing site is also under new construction. Word has it that this time, they plan to unify the East and West Wings into a single five-story building, with completion expected sometime in May, during the Exposition season.

Female Announcers and Pay
The Keijō Broadcasting Station sometimes recruits female announcers who are graduates of girls' high schools, and their starting salary (初給) is said to be 80 chŏn per day. Also, the remuneration for female clerks at certain large department stores in the city is said to be 90 chŏn per day at best, despite working for over 10 hours.

Starting Salary for Professional School Graduates
Many graduates from private professional schools like Yŏnhŭi and Posŏng College this year found employment in banks. Their starting salary was typically 40 Won, while graduates from government-run professional schools received 45 Won, it is said.

Famous Spots for Early Summer
Tongnae Hot Springs
Located eight ri from the station, reachable in 40 minutes by tram or 30 minutes by car. It is a unique hot spring, particularly effective for gastrointestinal diseases, glandular diseases, skin diseases, etc., making it suitable for patients seeking recuperation. Lodging facilities are available nearby.

Haeundae Hot Springs
Similar to Tongnae Hot Springs but emerges from the coast (海岸湧出), so during the summer, it's convenient to combine it with sea bathing.

Pŏmŏsa Temple
An ancient temple located on the mid-slope of Kŭmjŏngsan Mountain, founded during the Silla King Hŭngdŏk era. The current buildings were rebuilt about 300 years ago. The vicinity features overlapping grotesque rocks (怪岩) and offers exquisite scenery of clean, verdant beauty (淸潔翠碧).
Hyŏnch'ungsa Stele, Oguk Castle Site, Hoeryŏng Shrine, Kando Fuji (likely view of Paektu Mt.), etc.

Kaesŏng, Ancient Capital of Koryŏ
Kaesŏng is the old capital of the Koryŏ Dynasty, also called Songdo. Koryŏ's Wang Kŏn
rose as a general under Kung Ye, possessed great virtue, accepted Silla's surrender (降を納하야), established the capital here, and completed the great task of unifying the peninsula for over 470 years before falling to the Yi Dynasty 540 years ago. This place was famous for Koryŏ celadon, and now the renowned Koryŏ ginseng is produced here. Under the Government-General's monopoly patent, it serves as a precious health tonic (滋養强壯の保藥) in the East and West. It is a bustling city (櫛枇) with a population of over 40,000, and Kaesŏng people have developed to rank first among commercial pioneers throughout all of Korea (全鮮).

**Summary**

This "Confidential Room" section from the June 1935 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine compiles various news snippets covering academia, society, culture, economy, and tourism in colonial Korea. It notes an unprecedented boom in school applications, reports on a Sino-Japanese attended memorial for Sun Yat-sen, discusses a small industrial expo, shares an anecdote about Yi Kwangsu's baptism, includes classical Chinese poems, lists numerous libraries across Korea (both public and private), mentions a large lawsuit involving Kogye Academy, provides updates on literary figures (Yŏm Sangsŏp, Kim Tongin, Yi Kwangsu, etc.) and a musician (Kye Chŏngsik), details surprising bestseller statistics for classic Korean tales like Ch'unhyangjŏn sold by peddlers, reports on the rebuilding of Hwasin Department Store after a fire, contrasts low wages for female announcers/clerks with starting salaries for male college graduates, and promotes tourist spots like Tongnae Hot Springs and Kaesŏng. The article offers valuable glimpses into colonial Korean society: the strong desire for education despite circumstances, complex Sino-Japanese-Korean interactions, the enduring popularity of traditional Korean literature alongside modern cultural activities, the precarious economic realities for some workers versus the growth of businesses like department stores and banks, and the surprisingly widespread (though varied in quality) network of libraries and reading rooms. Historians might find the data on book sales, wages, the specific list of libraries, the description of the Sun Yat-sen memorial (noting Japanese official presence), and the blend of traditional and modern cultural news particularly insightful regarding social trends, economic conditions, cultural consumption, and political undertones of the era.

三千里 機密室 The Korean Black chamber
思想界, 學界
己未以后 初有의 學界活况
今年 3月에 서울에 잇는 학교는 모다 10餘 年來 처음 보는 비상한 活況을 이루엇다. 즉 중학교 이상 各 專門學校에 이르기까지 그 지원자 數爻가 입학정원의 七八倍 十倍에까지 이르러 근래에 업는 성황을 이루엇다.
이러케 비상한 활기를 띄우게 된 원인은 근래에 半島 각지의 농촌에 쌀갑이 올너서 농민의 품이 좀 넉넉하게 되어가자 거기에다가 최근 子弟를 위하야 공부식히려는 向學熱이 향상된 까닭에 실로 十數年來 初有의 活况을 띄이고 잇다는데 금후 몃해까지 이 교육열이 지속할는지 퍽으나 注目을 끄은다고 한다.

印象깁흔 孫文 追悼會

高麗古都開城
開城은 高麗朝의 舊都로서 一名 松都라 云하며 高麗 王建
弓裔가 大將으로 身을 起하야 德望이 厚하엿스며 新羅에 降을 納하야 此地에 都을 定하고 半島 統一의 大業을 完成한지 470여 년으로서 李氏에게 亡함이 지금으로 540년 이전이 됨. 此地에는 高麗磁器라 云하야 高名하엿스나 今亦 有名한 高麗人蔘이 생산됨으로 總督府 專賣特許에 의하야 滋養强壯의 保藥으로서 東西洋에 珍品이 되며 人口 4만여가 居住하난 櫛枇한 市街로서 全鮮에 亘하야 商業先覺者로서 開城人이 首位을 點할만치 發展하게 됨.
