Scenery of the Villa Area of the Wealthy, Sŏngbuk-tong Area

Many rich gentlemen live in Sŏul. Therefore, there are numerous villas of the wealthy in the vicinity of Sŏul. First, the places that catch the eye are the high ground of Chikch'ŏm-chō Sanch'ōme (竹添町 三丁目), the Hakkŭu area beyond Changch'ungdan, and the Myŏngsutae area beyond Yongsan; all are villa zones for multimillionaires and billionaires. However, the place where the most wealthy Koreans live would be pointed out as the Sŏngbuk-tong area, which has fine water and rocks. Now, as the first stop on a pilgrimage to the villa areas, let's first look at the Sŏngbuk-tong area.

Paek Ingi's Residence, Worth Over 100,000 Wŏn
Even within the same Sŏngbuk-tong, there are three secluded deep valleys with fine mountain and water scenery. The house occupying the very best location at the end of the central alley is Mr. Paek Ingi's villa. The rear garden forest alone is said to be over 100,000 p'yŏng, and rumor has it that he is not selling it even when offered 140,000 wŏn. As it is mortgaged to a certain bank (某銀行) for 70,000 wŏn, its vastness, depth, and size are beyond description. The mountain gate at the entrance is built stacked with granite, old pine trees hundreds of years old block the path within the garden, a spring stream flows, and there is a stone bridge. Inside, though somewhat aged, sits a large-scale,<204> grand Korean-style house built over many years. When summer comes, the sounds of the wind, birds, and water make one forget human suffering; it is a fairyland. Coming out a little from there,

The Western-style Villa of Paek Sanggyu
The white, Western-style two-story house nestled among trees and rocks is Paek Sanggyu's villa. In terms of market price, it would be tens of thousands of wŏn; it is a Western-style villa worth no less than that. When I passed nearby, the sound of a piano could be heard; perhaps it was his lover, Ms. Han Kiju, a graduate of the Tokyo Music School (東京音樂學校), playing a tune after a meal.

The Modern-style Villa of Han Yangho
Nearby is the villa of Mr. Han Yangho, the son of Mr. Han Kyusŏl, the Prime Minister during the late Han (Korean Empire) period. As his son, Mr. Han Ch’angsu, is the owner of the Hanch'ŏng Building (韓靑삘딍) and is constructing a large building across from Hwasin Department Store in Chongno, he seems confident in architecture. Although it is unknown who designed it, the architecture of this villa is also outstandingly tasteful. It likely also covers many tens of thousands of p'yŏng.

The Villa of Chŏn Yŏngp’il, Graduate of Nihon University
If it is said that 5,000 wŏn was spent just on stacking the stone wall, one can know the grandeur of its scale. It is a purely Western-style house bought for 50,000 wŏn several years ago from a Westerner, the owner of the Pullae Trading Company (富來商會), and there is news that he refuses to sell it even when offered 100,000 wŏn recently. The owner of this villa, Mr. Chŏn Yŏngp’il, is still a young man around 30 years old who went to Tokyo early on, graduated from Nihon University (日本大學), has his main residence in Chongno Sach'ōme, and is a large landowner and moneylender.

The Villa of Money Lender Im Chongsang
Mr. Im Chongsang, the owner of the house outside Tongdaemun in Ch'angsin-dong, with its walls built up as if to block the sky like the residence of an Annam king, owns a villa in Sŏngbuk-tong that seems even larger than his main residence. Its vast, large, upward-gazing residence, a blend of Korean and Western styles (和洋圻衷), dazzles the eyes of the beholder. This wealthy man is also said to mainly engage in the money lending business (貸金業).

The Villa of Landowner Yi Chongsŏk
There is likely no one who doesn't know Yi Chongsŏk, the rich man of Changgyo, Sŏul. This wealthy man's villa is as magnificent and splendid as a house where royalty might live. Is it because he has no sons or daughters and has no way to handle his excessive wealth that he throws such large sums of money into his house like this?

Those of Other Gentlemen
Besides these, there are several other houses. In any case, it is said that there are 14 villas of multimillionaires in Sŏngbuk-tong, so everywhere one goes, there are villas that dazzle the eyes. The villa of Mr. Yi Yuson, who runs a pharmacy business in Hwangŭm-chŏng, is also here, and the villa of Mr. Kim Kyesu of Haedong Bank (海東銀行), and also the villa of the Sŏul rich man Mr. Kim Tonggyu are all here.
And I don't know if it is right to write this here or not, but the villa of His Highness Yi Kang (李堈 殿下) is also located a good way past Mr. Kim Kyesu's villa in Sŏngbuk-tong. However, the impression was that the house was not a Western house, but a purely Korean-style old building.<205>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine describes the opulent villas of wealthy Koreans in the Sŏngbuk-tong area of Seoul (then Keijō). It highlights specific villas owned by prominent figures like Paek Ingi, Paek Sanggyu, Han Yangho (son of a former prime minister), Chŏn Yŏngp’il (a young landowner and Nihon University graduate), Im Chongsang (moneylender), and Yi Chongsŏk (landowner), detailing their scale, architectural styles (ranging from traditional Korean to purely Western and blended designs), and estimated values. The article emphasizes the concentration of wealth in this area, noting at least 14 multimillionaire villas, and even mentions a villa belonging to Prince Yi Kang. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the lives of the Korean elite under Japanese colonial rule, showcasing their significant wealth accumulation (often through landowning and money lending), their adoption of diverse architectural tastes including Western styles, and the social geography of Seoul during this period. It implicitly contrasts this visible wealth with the likely conditions of the broader population.

富豪의 別莊地帶風景, 城北洞一帶
서울에는 부자양반이 만히 산다. 그러기에 서울 근방에는 부호의 別莊이 수두룩하다. 위선 눈에 띄이는 곳이 竹添町 三丁目의 高台와, 獎忠壇너머 鶴丘 一帶와 龍山너머 明水台 一帶가 모다 十萬長者, 百萬長者의 別莊地帶이라 그러나 조선인 부자가 가장 만히 사는 곳으로는 水石이 조흔 城北洞 一帶를 가르치리라. 이제 별장지대를 巡禮하는 첫길로 城北洞 一帶를 爲先 보기로 하자.

時價十餘萬圓의 白寅基邸
가튼 城北洞이라 하여도 山水조흔 幽閑한 深谷이 세가달 잇다. 그 中央골목 막바지에 第一 조흔 위치를 차지하고 잇는 집이 白寅基 氏 別莊이다. 後園山林만 十餘萬坪이라 전하며 들니는 말에 十四萬圓을 바드란 것을 아니 팔고 잇다든가. 某銀行에 七萬圓인가 저당이 되여 드러가 잇느니 만치 넓고 깁고 큰 것은 이로 말할 수가 업다. 드러가는 山門은 花剛石으로 하여 싸헛고 園內에는 數百年 묵은 老松이 길을 막엇고 泉流가 흐르고 石橋가 잇고 그 속에다가 좀 낡엇지만 대규모의＜204＞ 여러 해의 朝鮮式 크다란 집이 드러안젓다. 여름이 되면 바람소리 새소리와 물소리가 人間苦를 닛게 하는 仙境이 다 거기서 조곰 나오다가

洋製의 白象圭別莊
하얀 洋製 2層 집이 樹石속에 싸인 것은 白象圭 別莊이라. 이도 時價로 치면 몃萬圓이면 몃萬圓이지 그 이하는 안들 洋式別莊이다. 내가 그 近傍을 지날때에 피아노소리 들님은 그의 愛人으로 東京音樂學校出身의 韓琦柱 女史가 食后 한곡조 타심인가.

新式의 韓養浩別莊
그 부근에 저 韓末의 總理大臣 韓圭卨 씨의 令息인 韓養浩 氏 別莊이 또 잇다. 그의 아드님인 韓昌洙 氏는 韓靑삘딍의 所有者로 鍾路和信 건너편에 큰 삘딍을 짓고 잇느니만치 建築에는 자신이 잇는 듯 누구의 設計인지 모르나 이 別莊의 建築도 뛰여나게 風致가 잇다. 基他도 여러 萬坪일 듯

日大卒業한 全營弼別莊
石돌담을 쌋키만에 五千圓을 던젓다하면 그 規模의 豪大를 알니라. 몃해전에 西洋사람인 富來商會主人으로부터 五萬圓에 산 純洋式집으로 요지음 十萬圓에 팔나해도 아니판다는 消息이 잇다. 이 別莊主人 全瑩弼 氏는 아직 30세 전후의 靑年으로 일즉 東京드러가 日本大學을 마춘 鍾路四丁目에 本宅이 잇는 大地主요 貸金業하는 분이다.

貸金業의 林宗相別邸
東大門밧 昌信洞에 安南國王의 邸宅가치 한울을 막을 듯이 올니싸흔 그 담장 집의 所有者  林宗相 氏는 城北洞의 그 本宅보다 더 클 듯한 別莊을 가지고 잇다. 넓고 크고 和洋圻衷의 그 仰視케 하는 邸宅은 보는 이의 눈을 휘황케한다. 이 富豪는 亦貸金業을 主로 한다든가.

地主의 李種奭氏 別莊
서울 長橋富者 李種奭 이라면 모르는 이가 업스리라. 이 富者의 別莊은 꼭 王公이 사는 집가치 豪擧壯麗하다. 아들 딸 업는 분으로 너무 만흔 財産을 處理할 길업서 집에다가 이러케 大金을 던짐인가.

其外諸氏의 것
이 밧게도 여러 집이 잇다. 엇잿든 城北洞안에는 十萬長者의 別莊이 14個所가 잇다느니 만치 간곳 온곳이 모다 눈을 부시게 하는 別莊이다. 黃金町서 藥種商하는 李有善 氏 別莊도 여기 잇고 海東銀行의 金季洙氏 또 서울 부자 金東圭氏의 別莊도 다 여기 잇다.
그러고 여긔 적는 것이 올흘는지 엇덜넌지는 모르겟스나 李堈 殿下 別莊도 城北洞의 金季洙氏 別莊을 지나 한참 드러가면 잇다. 그러나 그집 洋屋은 아니고 純朝鮮式 늙은 建物인 듯이 인상되엿다.＜205＞
