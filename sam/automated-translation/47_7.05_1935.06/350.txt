A Tragic History of Beauty's Misfortune, Ah, It is Better to Return: The Famous Actress Yi Kyŏngsŏl
						By Hongŭi Tongja

As sickness deepened, even her lover grew distant. Oh, just wait until I recover from this illness.
When twilight fell upon her sickbed, the person she had awaited all day long did not come. Time and again, she soaked her pillow with tears and ground her teeth.
"I would gladly die for you," she used to say.
"I would even be your servant," the whisper still lingered in her ears.
"For your happiness, I would spare no sacrifice," she vowed.
Bundles of letters from ardent suitors lay piled by her bedside. However,
as the rumor spread that Yi Kyŏngsŏl's lung disease (肺病) had worsened,<209> one by one, their footsteps began to fade.
Still, surely...
Even the one who said it was fine to face death together stopped coming. Now, there was nothing more to hope for, nothing more to believe in, no one left to wait for.
"When my body was healthy and I stood on stage, they pleaded for my life and death, but now that I am confined by illness, is it all over?"
The promissory notes (手形) issued by her suitors all ended up dishonored (不渡) upon their payment dates. For Yi Kyŏngsŏl, the Queen of Tears and star of popular songs, all that remained now was resentment and bitterness.
Oh, I must live—I have to live. If only to see the final state of those unjust and immoral scoundrels who cast aside their promises like worn-out shoes, I must live.
Being ten times, a hundred times more passionate (激情家) than others, how could she hope for her illness to lighten while such flames of resentment (怨炎) burned in her heart? Destined to fade away like the leaves touched by the first autumn maple colors (丹楓) in the 9th year of Shōwa, at the end of her 24th year (24歲を一期로), she desperately tried to live, almost by sheer willpower alone.

She was born (胎生) in Kangwŏn Province. Her ears were sharpened by the bell sound (鍾) of Naksan Temple (洛山寺) in Yangyang, her heart grew generous from the blue waves of the East Sea (東海), and her character (人品) gained elegance treading the peaks of Pongnae (蓬萊) that appeared through the morning mist under the sunlight. Having lost her parents early (早失父母), she became an orphan (孤兒) with her older sister and was raised by her maternal uncle (外叔父), Ch'oe Hwa-ch'un, at 78 Sinam-dong, Ch'ŏngjin, Hamgyŏng Province. Thus, she who would later become a renowned tragic actress (悲劇의 名優) capable of moving Chosŏn to tears, could not escape becoming a tragic heroine (悲劇의 主人公) in her own life. She attended school (學校) in Ch'ŏngjin. After finishing elementary school (普通學校), she rushed to Kyŏngsŏng to prepare for entrance (入學準備) into a girls' high school (高等女學校), when she was taken by friends to see a play (演劇). It was the troupe (團體) led by Song Hae-ch'ŏn.
"Rather than studying by taking my uncle's hard-earned money, let's become an actress (女俳優) and quickly achieve self-support (自活)."
This was the solemn decision (悲壯한 決心) of the 16-year-old Yi Kyŏngsŏl. Of course, Mr. Ch'oe Hwa-ch'un objected.
"If you end up like that, what will your deceased sister in the underworld (地下) think of me?"
Scolding letters arrived several times. However, Kyŏngsŏl, firm in her resolve (決斷性), swallowed her tears and joined (入團) Song Hae-ch'ŏn's troupe. Who would have paid attention to a 16-year-old apprentice actress (見習女俳優) with blonde hair and short stature, looking like a country bumpkin if one were to find fault? But the troupe leader (座長), Song Hae-ch'ŏn, observed her intently.
"Your eyes are large, your face generous, your expressions (表情) fluid, your voice plaintive yet strong. You should specialize in tragedy (悲劇에 專功) and make the world (世上) weep."
For her, who already bore the sorrow of being doubly burdened by others, being told to cry and make others cry<210>—was this also some kind of fate (宿命)? From the day she joined the troupe, she studied crying, she studied making others cry.

It was several years later that the rumor spread about an actress (女俳優) named "Yi Kyŏngsŏl" who excelled at tragedy (悲劇). Thus, she moved up to the Chw'isŏng Troupe (聚星座) and, alongside Yi Aerisŭ and Sin Ŭn-bong, during the long-run performance (長期興行) for the Chosŏn Exposition (朝鮮博覽會) in the 5th year of Shōwa at the Tansŏng-sa Theater (團成社), her popularity (人氣) soared at will. A stage life (舞臺生活) where she constantly came and went for plays (演劇) and solo songs (獨唱)! Cheers (歡呼) following this fluttering flower! To escape the siege of fans after the play ended, she even reached the point of commuting daily by automobile (自働車). From the Chw'isŏng Troupe, she moved again to the Yŏn'gŭk Sijang (演劇市場 - Theater Market Troupe) and then again to Sin Mudae (新舞臺 - New Stage Troupe). Thus, she always walked the same path with her frequent stage partner (相對役), her lover (愛人) Sŏ Il-sŏng, and Kwanak Sanin, who wrote good scripts (脚本) for her. In this way, she became the star (主演) of numerous famous plays such as *Tongbaekkot* (Camellia Flower), *Choguk ŭi Aein* (Lover of the Fatherland), *Hwangguk Paekkuk* (Yellow Chrysanthemum, White Chrysanthemum), *Piro Tchatchang* (Woven with Blood), *Sarang ŭl Chikhinŭn Abŏji* (Father Protecting Love), *Hwajangsil ŭi Aesu* (Sorrow in the Dressing Room), becoming the protagonist (主人公) of fortune (幸運), enjoying both love and fame. Following this, she signed an exclusive contract (專屬契約) with the Tōkyō Polydor Record Company for popular songs and drama recordings, achieving a level of success (榮達) unsurpassed by any other actress in Chosŏn. Despite playing the role of a flower on the glamorous actress stage, her private life (私生活) was never affluent.
"Since I have Sŏ, I need neither money nor love anymore."
She paid no attention to the courtship of luxurious young men who followed her, offering to be patrons, and was grateful for each day, living frugally on her own income (收入) alone.
This happened when she was with the Yŏn'gŭk Sijang. She got into a heated argument (激論) with the representative (代表者), Mun Su-il, over something, leading to a one-sided dramatic scene (活劇). The performance time (出演時間) was approaching.
"It’s fine even if an actress like you doesn't perform!"
The words exploded from Mun's flushed (上熱) lips. From the packed audience (滿員된 客席), applause (拍手) demanding Kyŏngsŏl's appearance rained down. She was not an actress whose non-appearance would be fine. She was an actress who absolutely had to perform. Her colleague (同志), standing beside her listening, felt resentful and whispered,
"Sister, just go home and lie down. The representative will take responsibility (責任) for what follows." But Yi Kyŏngsŏl wiped away her tears, sat before the mirror stand, and began applying powder (粉칠) to her face (化粧). Everyone was wide-eyed at this unexpected action.
"You were just fighting moments ago, why are you putting on makeup?"
Chŏn Kyŏng-hŭi, a comedy actor, teased jokingly. At these words, Kyŏngsŏl chuckled and said,
"This is why one can't even talk with ignorant (無識) people. Mr. Mun may be the representative (代表) of this troupe, but he isn't the representative of the stage (舞臺), is he? Just because I fought with Mr. Mun, why should I take it out on the audience waiting for Yi Kyŏngsŏl? For an actress, the stage is her home and her grave, so she must perform (演劇)<211>."
At these words, the entire troupe bowed their heads. There remains a touching anecdote (美談) that Mr. Mun also laughed heartily and shook Kyŏngsŏl's hand. How resentful she must have been to leave the world, having left the stage she considered her grave.

During her time with Sin Mudae, she starred in a tragedy (悲劇) by Kwanak Sanin titled *Hwajangsil ŭi Aesu* (Sorrow in the Dressing Room). This script (脚本) was written entirely for Yi Kyŏngsŏl, and even within the play, Yi Kyŏngsŏl appears as an actress named Yi Kyŏngsŏl. It portrays the suffering and sorrowful tale of a Chosŏn actress, and she ultimately commits suicide on stage. Perhaps because this play resonated with her the most, whenever she met colleagues or supporters, she would often say,
"I will definitely die on stage. Dying like in *Sorrow in the Dressing Room* would be the best."
After receiving the diagnosis of her illness, she cut off all ties (一切의 關係) with the world and went to her uncle's house in Ch'ŏngjin to spend days recuperating (精養). Now, love, fame, and money seemed insignificant to her. There was only the single thought (一念) that she must hold onto her life (목숨).

Selling the house in Such'ang-dong that she had barely managed to acquire with her first Polydor recording fee (吹込料) to pay for her treatment... As she left Kyŏngsŏng, her heart must have been filled with a thousand emotions (千感萬懷가 交集하여), making it hard to take a step. From Ch'ŏngjin, she went to Chúil, then Kando (間島), and finally moved to the Koga Hospital (古賀病院) in Tosu (鳥栖), Kyūshū (九州). After going there, good news (喜消息), carried by the spring breeze (春風) across the Hyŏnhae Strait (玄海), arrived saying her condition (病勢) was gradually reversing and her full recovery (全快) was only a matter of time (時間問題).
『Mr. Wang, today I even took a walk (散步). Lying on the green hill, I looked at the western sky (西쪽하늘) and waved. The hospital director (院長) is also happy, saying the day I return to Chosŏn is not far off.
Perhaps I will be able to go for the exclusive recording session this time. This is all thanks to the care and support from everyone at the company.』
This postcard was the cheerful report (快報) she sent in March of Shōwa 9 (1934) to Mr. Wang P'yŏng of the Arts Department (文藝部) at the Kyŏngsŏng Polydor branch. Thus, receiving a certificate of approval (承諾證) from the director, she was discharged (退院) and returned to Kyŏngsŏng in April. Upon arrival, she took lodging at the Anjŏng Temple (安定寺) outside Kwanghŭi Gate (光熙門밧). However, given the nature of her illness, surprisingly few people visited her sickbed (病席).
The only visitors were people from the Polydor company and two or three close friends (親友). This pained her. The people she had counted on visiting, whom she had ticked off on her fingers, not a single one came.
"It was foolish to trust. The one who gets deceived is pitiful, what fault lies with the deceiver?"
Many times, cold tears soaked her pillow. Now, if I die, who will weep for me out of sorrow? Even if my illness heals, who will rejoice for me? The world felt cold and desolate. Having grown up without knowing the affection of a mother or father, the only place she could attach her feelings to was the world (世上). But<212> that world treated her, sick as she was, so heartlessly; what could she believe in, where could she rely on in the future? The only reason she wanted to live now was this bitterness (악).
"Let's see how well you fare after treating me with such contempt (괄시)." It was only this resentful feeling (원억한 마음) that made her want to live a little longer. It was just the thought of standing on stage (舞臺) once more, glaring at the world, and making it weep again. Thus, while preparing for a recording session (吹込準備), just as Sin Mudae was about to stage "Madame X," she volunteered to take the lead role.
*Special Appearance (特別出演) of the Recovered (更生) Miss Yi Kyŏngsŏl*
When this advertisement (宣傳) appeared on the streets, enthusiasts (同好者) in Kyŏngsŏng murmured amongst themselves.
"They said she was about to die, but she's alive again."
"They said her illness was severe, but she recovered quickly."
And so, the opening night was a full house (大滿員). Dressed in black (黑衣), she appeared in the courtroom (法廷) and wept her final plea. However, when the curtain fell, she collapsed onto the stage (舞臺), her body finally giving way after forcing herself to endure. From her mouth, red blood... Trembling hands carried her back to her sickbed (病席), and from the next day, an understudy took her place. This was her final stage appearance. If only she hadn't performed that day? If only she hadn't performed so passionately (熱演), as if wringing blood from her heart, perhaps her condition (病勢) would not have relapsed (再下) so severely, many thought. Thus, pulling herself together again, she set off on the long journey to Tōkyō in late April (下旬) to record songs (吹込). She knew she couldn't survive unless she went back to Koga Hospital (古賀病院). However, going to Koga Hospital required over 500 won, and the amount she had already borrowed from Polydor was considerable, leaving her unable to move forward or backward.
Therefore, thinking it would be better to go for the recording, even if just out of apology, to get some money, she dragged her ailing body onto the path of recording.
"Oh well, let me repay the kindness of the company that treats me so well, and then I can die." This was her state of mind (心境) at the time. From her arrival in Tōkyō, she couldn't sleep at night, continuing her anguish (煩悶).
"Let's die... After the recording is finished, on the ship back, let me throw myself into the Hyŏnhae Strait (玄海) and disappear into the blue waves (滄浪)."
Night after night, while her companions Wang Su-bok and Sin Ŭn-bong slept, she wrote her suicide note (遺書). Mr. Wang P'yŏng, sensing this, finally tore up her note and comforted her with myriad words (萬端으로). Ultimately, through the goodwill (好意) of a Polydor executive (重役), another 1,000 won (千圓) was paid (支佛) to her, arranging for her admission (入院) to Koga Hospital.
"Kyŏngsŏl—the company has agreed (快諾) to provide 1,000 won. Let's go to Koga Hospital."
Hearing the words of Mr. Wang P'yŏng, who entered with a face full of joy (喜色이 滿面하야), Kyŏngsŏl doubted if it was a dream.
"Now I'm saved."
The thought flashed like lightning through her heart. There was no need for suicide. Money had appeared for an illness that could be cured with money, so it was right to seek the path to life.<213>
"But I feel too indebted. When can I ever repay the company's kindness (恩惠)?"
Tears of gratitude (感激) fell like rain. Even if ten thousand, a hundred thousand people applauded her stage performance, could it contribute even one bit to curing her illness now? At this moment, Kyŏngsŏl's remaining life (殘命) hung in the balance of money, and the only way that money could come was through the sympathy (同情) of the Polydor executives. After entering Koga Hospital, her progress (經過) was very good for a time. Just as worried people began to relax, thinking she would recover, an unexpected sad report (悲報) flew in.

ハナシアル マグコイ (HANASHI ARU MAGU KOI - There is something to discuss, come at once)
An urgent personal telegram (親電) from the director of Koga Hospital arrived for Mr. Wang at Polydor.
"What could have happened that they're asking me to come by telegram?"
Mr. Wang rushed towards Tosu (烏栖). When he arrived, Kyŏngsŏl was lying as if dead, and the old woman (老婆) attending her sickroom (病室) could only say, tearfully,
"I am sorry," unable to utter another word. Mr. Wang discovered (發見) a bandage wrapped around Kyŏngsŏl's left wrist as she lay in bed.
"What is this?"
Kyŏngsŏl's reply was tears. She could only weep without making a sound. While her condition had improved in one aspect, the disease eating away at her chest had spread to her intestines (膓), and she had received the verdict that she could no longer survive. Although the doctor did not explicitly say so, the quick-witted Kyŏngsŏl had already sensed it. Figuring that if she was going to die anyway, she would rather suffer less and be less of a burden to others, she
cut her artery (動脈) with a razor blade (面刀칼).
When the old woman sleeping nearby opened her eyes, much blood had already flowed, and her life hung in the balance. At 4 AM, the hospital was in great commotion (大騷動), and although emergency treatment (應急手當) was barely administered, her condition fluctuated (一進一退), making tomorrow uncertain. With the patient seeking death, her situation (身邊) was critical (一時が急急하니), so the hospital advised Mr. Wang to have her discharged (退院) and return home.
"Kyŏngsŏl, let's go. Let's go back to your hometown (故鄕) and see things through, whether life or death." Holding Kyŏngsŏl, who could barely support her body like a withered flower branch, Mr. Wang boarded an airplane (飛行機) and flew over the Hyŏnhae Strait and Ch'up'ung Pass (秋風嶺) back to Kyŏngsŏng. Just then, white clouds (白雲) gathered densely (密集) over Ch'up'ung Pass, and the airplane flew through the clouds.
"Mr. Wang, isn't this nice? It feels like living in the clouds."
Looking down upon the mountains of her homeland (故鄕), the high passes soaring above the blue sky of her homeland, she rejoiced again, as if falling into a lover's embrace!
"Now it feels like I might live. My mind feels clear and my chest feels open."
Her joyful mood (氣分) was pitiful. How long could she live in the clouds? Already, the Han River (漢江), flowing like oil, came into view, and wasn't the white sandy beach (白沙場) of Yŏŭi Island (汝矣島)<214> opening its arms to welcome the beautiful lady (佳人) returning in search of death?

After returning to Kyŏngsŏng, she first rested at the Kim Chin Inn (金進旅館) in Ch'ŏngjin-dong (淸進洞). The Kim Chin Inn was where she used to stay (寄宿) during her glamorous prime (花形時代), full of affections and sorrows (多情多恨튼). The pomegranate tree (石榴나무) in the courtyard, the apricot tree (살구나무) in the backyard—everything was familiar. She deliberately sought out the room she used to stay in. Though it was the room where she had laughed and sung joyfully with colleagues just a few years ago, now she lay there desolate, merely waiting for the day of her death. The only visitor was Na P'umsim.
"Hey, Na P'umsim, if I had just become some rich man's (富者) mistress (妾) when they were all pestering me like that, I wouldn't have caught this illness, nor would I be receiving such contemptuous treatment. But that's useless talk. I, who dedicate my life (生命) to drama (劇), am happy even if I die now. What is there to resent?"
She put on the test pressing (트데스板) of the tragedy *La Dame aux Camélias* (椿姬), which she had last recorded (最後로 吹込한) for Polydor, played it, wept, and coughed up blood.

As her illness worsened, she inevitably went to Ch'ŏngjin, where her uncle lived. She sought out her burial place. Soon, summer passed, and as the autumn breeze began to blow softly in August, her body started to wither away rapidly. When dawn broke on the 28th, she failed to open her eyes from her deep sleep. The doctor sighed and said,
"Giving any more injections (注射) at this point will only cause more suffering for the dying person, so just let her lie still,"
and did not take out the syringe again. As it neared six o'clock,
without any specific word, her lips just parted slightly, and then, carrying a light breath, she uttered a single phrase,
"Ah—I wanted to die on stage,"
and smiled faintly. This was the end, but her peaceful face made it difficult to tell if she was dead or alive. Thus, the famous actress (名女優) who had moved the world to tears finally added another page to the tragic history of beauty's misfortune (美人薄命哀史), ending her life at the age of 24.

The suicide note (遺書) placed by her bedside was opened in the presence of Mr. Wang P'yŏng and her uncle, but its contents were kept secret (祕密), so they remain unknown. Her remains became a lonely soul (孤魂) without a chief mourner (喪主) in the Ch'ŏngjin Public Cemetery (淸津府 共同墓地), left to feel the spring winds and autumn rains (春風秋雨). Having loved the stage as her lover and sacrificed her own glory like worn-out shoes for the sake of drama, the renowned actress of her time passed away, becoming nothing more than a handful of earth, her name fading away from the world. How can one not lament (哀惜) this?

Her funeral (葬式) was conducted by the Polydor Company (포리돌社葬) with a grand farewell ceremony (永訣式) held at the square in front of Ch'ŏngjin Station (淸津驛前 廣場). Representing her friends (友人代表), Ms. Kim U-yŏn, a former actress of the T'owŏlhoe troupe (前土月會 女俳優), read the eulogy (吊文). Na Un-gyu, Pak Yŏn-ik, Wang P'yŏng, Hyŏn Sŏng-wan, and others rushed there to shed tears on her final path. <215>

**Summary**

This article recounts the tragic life and death of the popular Korean actress Yi Kyŏngsŏl (1911-1934). Born an orphan, she rose to fame as a tragic actress in the 1930s, known for her emotional performances on stage and in recordings for the Japanese company Polydor. Despite her success, she lived modestly, devoted to her lover Sŏ Il-sŏng and her craft. Diagnosed with severe tuberculosis, she faced abandonment by former admirers and even her lover. Financial struggles compounded her suffering, leading her to seek treatment in Japan, funded partly by Polydor. A brief recovery was shattered by a relapse after a grueling performance. Despairing, she attempted suicide but was saved, only to succumb to the illness shortly after returning to Korea at age 24. Her funeral was sponsored by Polydor.

From a colonial history perspective, the article highlights the burgeoning popular culture involving stage plays and recordings, bridging Korea and Japan (Polydor, travel to Kyūshū for advanced medical care unavailable or unaffordable in Korea). It underscores the precarious existence of female performers, reliant on popularity and susceptible to abandonment. The involvement of a Japanese company (Polydor) in supporting (and perhaps exploiting) a Korean star, including funding her medical treatment and funeral, illustrates the complex economic and cultural interactions during the colonial period. The narrative of abandonment and financial hardship reflects the societal vulnerabilities, particularly for women, in that era.

잡지명	삼천리 제7권 제5호
발행일	1935년 06월 01일
기사제목	美人薄命哀史, 아아 不如歸러라 名優李景雪
필자	紅衣童子
기사형태	소설

美人薄命哀史, 아아 不如歸러라 名優李景雪
						紅衣童子
病이 드니 愛人도 머러지는고나 오-냐 病만 나어 보아라.
病床에 黃昏이 차저올 때 종일토록 기다리든 사람은 찻지를 안앗다. 그는 번번히 눈물노 벼게를 적시며 이를 가랏다.
당신을 위해서는 죽어도 좃소.
하든 그네이엇다.
나는 당신의 종노릇이라도 하겟소.
하든 속삭임이 아즉도 귀에 남아잇다.
당신의 幸福을 위하야서는 엇더한 犧牲이라도 앗기지 안으리다.
날나드든 求愛者들의 便紙뭉치가 머리맛에 싸혀잇다. 그러나
李景雪 이가 肺病이 심해젓다.＜209＞ 는 소문이 나자 한사람 두사람 발길이 머러지기 시작햇다.
그래도 설마...
죽엄을 갓치해도 좃타든 그 사람마저 발이 머러젓다. 이제는 더 바랄 것도 더 미들 것도 더 기달일 사람도 업다.
내몸이 성하고 舞臺에 오를 때에는 죽여주 살녀주 하드니 病에 누으니 고만이란 말이냐.
求愛者들의 振出한 手形은 支拂期日에 이르러서는 모조리 不渡가 나고 말앗다. 눈물의 女王流行歌의 名星으로서의 李景雪에게 지금 남은 것은 오직 분하고 원통한 생각뿐이엇다.
오-냐 살자-사러야 한다. 信約을 헌신가치 내바린 不義不德한 놈들의 마즈막 꼴을 보기 위해서라도 사러야 한다.
남의 十倍百倍 激情家인 그에게 이가튼 怨炎이 가슴에 타오르고 누엇스니 病이 가비여지기를 엇지 바랄 수가 잇스랴. 24歲를 一期로 昭和 9년 첫가을 丹楓이 머리에 지는 입과 갓치 사라지고 말 것을 그는 거의 입씨긋으로 살겟다고 애를 썻다.

그는 江原道 胎生이다. 襄陽의 洛山寺 鍾소리에 그의 귀는 총명해지고 東海의 풀은 물결에 저의 마음은 너그리워젓스며 아츰 안개속으로 해빗에 낫하나는 蓬萊의 連峯을 밟고 그의 人品은 고화지기를 어덧다. 그는 早失父母하고 언니와 함께 孤兒의 몸이 되야 咸鏡道 淸津 新岩洞七八에 잇는 外叔父 崔和春 이라는 이에 길니우게 된 것이다. 이리하야 將次로 朝鮮을 울이는 悲劇의 名優가 될 그는 그 自身이 임이 悲劇의 主人公됨을 버서나지 못하얏든 것이다. 淸津서 學校를 단녓다. 普通學校를 맛친 그는 京城으로 뛰어 올나와 高等女學校에 入學準備에 밧뿔 때에 동모들에게 걸녀서 演劇求景을 갓섯다. 그것이 곳 宋海天 君의 잇끌고 다니든 團體이엇다.
女俳優 노릇을 하자 外叔父의 잣딴돈을 뜨더다 工夫를 하느니 보다 어서 밧비 自活을 하자.
이것이 16歲때의 李景雪의 悲壯한 決心이엇다. 물론 崔和春氏는 반대를 하얏다.
네가 그 꼴이 되면 地下에 게신 누님이 나를 무엇으로 아시겟느냐.
꾸지람 편지도 여러번 왓다. 그러나 決斷性 굿센 景雪이는 눈물을 먹음고 宋海天一座에 입단을 하고 마랏다. 노란머리 적은 키 흉을 보자면 시골 촌뜩이 가튼 16歲의 見習女俳優 누가 거들떠나 보왓스랴만은 座長 宋海天君만은 유심이히 바라보왓다.
눈이 크고 얼골이 너그럽고 表情이 마음대로 되며 목소리가 구슬프고 억세이니 너는 悲劇에 專功해서 世上을 울녀보와라.
갓득이나 남의 곱되는 서름을 가즌 그로서 울고 울이고＜210＞ 하라니 이것도 무슨 宿命이엇든가. 그는 入團하는 날노부터 우는 공부를 하얏다 울니는 工夫를 하얏다.

「李景雪」이라는 女俳優가 悲劇을 곳 잘 안다는 소문이 나기는 몃해 후이다. 그리하야 그는 자리를 옴겨 聚星座로 躍進을 하고 李애리스
申銀鳳과 함게 昭和 5년 朝鮮博覽會長期興行을 團成社에서 하는 동안에 그의 인기는 마음대로 올나갓다. 演劇으로 獨唱으로 눈코뜰새업시 드나드는 舞臺生活! 이 날나드는 꼿 발뒤를 잇는 歡呼! 그는 劇이 끗나면 팬들의 포위를 버서나기 위하야 每日 自働車로 왕래하기까지에 이르럿다. 聚星座에서 그는 다시 演劇市場으로 또다시 新舞臺로 자리를 옴겨왓다. 그리하야 그는 항상 그의 相對役인 그의 愛人 徐日成과 그에게 조흔 脚本을 써주는 冠岳山人과는 항상 갓흔 길을 거러왓다. 이리하야 동백꼿 祖國의 愛人, 黃菊白菊, 피로짯상, 사랑을 직히는 아버지 化粧室의 哀愁 等 허다한 名劇에 主演이 되야 사랑과 일홈을 아울너 누리는 幸運의 主人公이 되야바렷다. 뒤를 이워 東京포리돌 레코-드社의 流行歌와 劇吹込의 專屬契約이 되니 朝鮮의 女俳優로서는 다시 더 바랄 길이 업는 榮達이다. 그는 가장 호화러워야 할 女俳優舞臺의 꼿 노릇을 하면서도 그의 사생활은 항상 넉넉지 못하얏다.
「나에게는 徐가 잇스니가 다시는 돈도 사랑도 필요치 안소이다」
파토론이 되겟다고 뒤를 따르는 豪華子弟들의 求愛는 거들떠보지도 안코 구차히나마 自己의 收入만으로 그날그날을 感謝해 왓다.
그가 演劇市場에 잇슬 때의 일이다. 그는 代表者 文秀一과 무슨 일에 激論을 하다가 一方活劇을 일웃킨 일이 잇섯다. 出演時間은 닥처왓다.
「너가튼 女俳優 出演 안해도 조화」
소리가 文君의 上熱된 입에서 폭발되얏다. 滿員된 客席에서는 景雪이 나오라는 拍手소리가 비발치듯 한다. 出演 안해도 조흘 女俳優가 안이다. 출연하지 아으면 안이 될 女俳優이다. 엽헤서 듯고 섯든 그의 同志는 분한 마음에
「언니 집으로 가서 누으시우. 다음 일은 代表者가 責任지겟지 하고 속은 거렷다. 그러나 李景雪은 눈물을 거두우고 경대 압헤 안저 얼골에 粉칠을 하기 시작햇다. 一同은 意外의 일에 눈이 둥구래젓다.
「금세 쌈을 하더니 化粧은 왜 할가」
全景希라는 喜劇俳優가 弄 직어리로 지근 거렷다. 이 말끗해 景雪은 픽 우스며
「그러기에 無識한 사람들과는 이약이도 못해. 이 團體의 代表가 文氏이지 舞臺의 代表가 文氏는 안이겟지. 文氏와 싸홧다고 李景雪이를 기다리는 손님의게까지 분푸리를 할게 머요. 女俳優는 舞臺가 집이요 무덤자리이니가 演劇을＜211＞ 해야지」
이 한마듸에 一座는 고개를 수그렷다. 文氏도 벌벌 우스며 景雪의 손목잡고 흔드럿다는 美談이 남아잇다. 舞臺를 무덤으로 아든 그가 舞臺를 떠나서 世上을 떠낫스니 얼마나 원통하랴.

그가 新舞臺 出演때에 冠岳山人의 化粧室의 哀愁라는 悲劇에 主演을 한 일이 잇섯다. 이 脚本은 전혀 李景雪이를 위하야 쓴 각본으로 극 속에서도 李景雪이는 李景雪이라는 일홈으로 女俳優가 되야 나온다. 朝鮮의 女俳優의 受難哀話로 그는 마츰내 舞臺에서 自殺을 하게 된다. 이 演劇이 그에게는 가장 조홧든지 그는 동모들이나 後援者를 맛나면
나는 꼭 舞臺에서 죽을테야. 化粧室의 哀愁와 가치 죽으면 第一 좃치.
소리를 여러번 하얏다. 病이란 宣告를 밧고는 그는 世上과의 一切의 關係를 끈코 淸津外叔父의 집으로 가서 精養의 날을 보냇다. 이제는 사랑도 일홈도 돈도 그에게는 귀치 안케 녁여젓다. 오즉 목숨을 붓잡어 세워야 한다는 一念이 잇슬 뿐이엇다.

포리돌 첫 吹込料로 겨오 작만해 노흔 需昌洞 집을 파라서 病을 곳치러 가게 되니... 京城을 떠나는 그의 가슴에는 千感萬懷가 交集하야 참아 발길이 떠러지지를 안앗스리라. 淸津에서 朱乙로 間島로 나종에는 九州鳥栖에 잇는 古賀病院으로 옴겨갓다. 이곳에 간 뒤부터는 차차 病勢가 도릇켜 全快함에는 時間問題이라는 喜消息이 봄바람을 타고 玄海를 건너왓다.
『王先生님 오늘은 散步도 햇슴니다. 푸른 언덕에 드러누어 서쪽하늘을 보고 손짓을 햇슴니다. 朝鮮에 도라갈 날도 머지 안엇다고 院長도 깃버하십니다.
아마 이번 吹込에는 專屬을 가게 될 가 봄니다. 이것도 모다가 會社 여러분의 愛護해 주신 德澤임니다』
이 한張 葉書는 昭和 9년 3월에 그가 京城 포리돌 支店에 잇는 文藝部 王平氏에게 보낸 快報이엇다. 이리하야 院長에게 承諾證을 밧아 退院을 하야 가지고 4월에 京城에 도라왓다. 오는 길로 그는 光熙門밧 안정寺에다 접사리를 하얏다. 그러나 그의 病이 病인만치 그의 病席을 돌보는 사람은 뜻밧게 극히 적엇섯다.
다만 찻는 사람이라고는 포리돌 會社사람과 2, 3의 親友밧게는 업섯다. 그는 이것이 원통하얏다. 누고누고는 차저 오려니 하든 손꼽든 사람은 한사람도 차저 오는 일이 업섯다.
미덧든 것이 어리석다. 속는 사람이 못낫지 속히는 사람이 무에 잘못이랴.
그는 몃번이나 찬 눈물이 벼개를 적시엇다. 이제 내가 죽으면 누가 원통타 우러나 줄가. 내가 病이 나흔들 누가 깃버나 해 줄가. 世上이 차고 쓸쓸만 해젓다. 어머니 아버지의 情을 모르고 자라난 그로서는 오즉 정붓칠 곳이라는 世上밧게는 업섯다. 그런데＜212＞ 그 世上이 이럿케 病든 自己에게 薄情히 구니 장차 무엇을 밋고 어듸다가 依支해 산단 말이냐. 지금에 그에게 살고 십다는 것은 오직 이 악이 남앗Š¬ 뿐이다.

그리하야 돈을 좀 어드랴니 미안해서라도 吹込은 가는게 올켓다는 생각에 그는 病든 몸을 끄을고 吹込의 길을 떠낫다.
오-냐 고맙게 구러주는 會社의 신세나 갑고 죽어도 죽자. 이것이 그때의 그의 心境이엇다. 東京에 到着하며부터 그는 밤마다 잠을 일우지 못하고 煩悶을 繼續하얏다.
죽자... 吹込이 끗나고 도라가는 배우에서 玄海에 몸을 던저 滄浪에 사라저 바리자.
그는 밤마다 갓치자든 王壽福 申銀鳳 두 동모의 자는 틈을 타 遺書를 쓰고 잇섯다. 이 눈치를 채인 王平氏는 마츰내 그의 遺書를 찟고 만단으로 위로를 하얏다. 그리하야 결국은 포리돌社 重役의 好意로 다시 千圓 돈을 그에게 支佛하야 古賀病院으로 入院을 식히게 되기에 이르럿다.
