The Rebellion in Greece Again

Although rebellions in Greece, where it can be hyperbolically said that the sovereign changes in just a week, are not uncommon, this time the revolutionary conflict has become a focus of worldwide attention because Venizelos (부에늬제로스), a respected elder statesman, declared his support for and mobilized with the anti-government forces.
Historically, Greece has seen continuous power struggles between the Royalist Party faction and the opposing Republican Party faction, and currently, a Royalist government headed by Tsaldaris (주아루다리스) holds power.
The incident originated merely as a rebellion stirred up by officers dissatisfied with the current government, instigated by the ambitious General Plastiras (뿌라스지라스將軍), who had been living in unfortunate exile since the failed coup d'état in Athens (아데네) in 1933 which collapsed in just one day. However, with Venizelos's participation, the strength of the rebel forces today appears considerably large.<125>

Abdication of the King of Siam

His Majesty Prajadhipok (뿌라쟈데이데陞下) of the Kingdom of Siam (사무國) has finally formally declared his abdication. Thinking back, he already held this grim resolve last autumn when he traveled to Britain accompanied by the Queen, citing cataract treatment as the reason.
While there are various conceivable reasons for the abdication, the crux is likely the clash of opinions with his home government on every matter, and for His Majesty, with his proud disposition, finding it unbearable to stand by and watch the sovereignty, which had been in the hands of the Siamese Royal House for generations, become merely nominal with the changing times.
He reigned for 11 years since succeeding to the throne as the 7th King of Siam upon the death of the former King Rama VI (라마6世) on November 26, 1925. Following His Majesty's abdication, his nephew, Prince Mahidol (마히도르殿下), will assume the throne as the 8th King.

Prime Minister MacDonald's Armament Expansion Statement

The efforts towards a European security mechanism, which had been progressing favorably, reaching what could be called the finishing touch in the final stage of the movement to draw Germany (獨逸) in, now show subtle signs of a sudden reversal due to the armament expansion statement by British Prime Minister MacDonald (英首맑토날트氏).<126>
The content of the armament expansion statement was that, until now, Britain (英國) alone has strived for the establishment of permanent peace. Other countries, under the guise of peace efforts, are actually preoccupied with military expansion. In this situation, one cannot feel secure relying solely on the current international peace mechanisms to prepare against war. The British government has therefore resolved at this time to expand armaments across the board – army, navy, and air force. It criticized Germany's rearmament efforts as destructive to international peace. Because of this, the first to become angered was Germany.<127>

**Summary**

This article from June 1935 digests three international events: a significant rebellion in Greece supported by the prominent politician Venizelos against the Royalist government; the abdication of King Prajadhipok of Siam (Thailand), attributed to conflicts with his government and the diminishing power of the monarchy, with his nephew Prince Mahidol succeeding him; and British Prime Minister MacDonald's announcement of broad military expansion, citing other nations' arms build-ups under the guise of peace efforts, which immediately provoked anger from Germany and threatened recent progress on European security agreements. For historians of colonial Korea, this piece shows the kinds of international news Koreans were consuming, filtered through publications potentially influenced by Japanese censors or editorial choices. The focus on political instability (Greece, Siam) and great power tensions (Britain, Germany) might reflect an interest in disruptions to the existing world order or simply standard international news coverage of the time, while the specific Korean vocabulary and orthography are indicative of language usage in the mid-1930s.

世界情勢抄
又復希臘의 叛亂
일주일만 지나면 主權者가 달너진다고 極言할 수 잇는 긔리시야의 일 叛亂은 珍奇치 안으나 今回의 革命戰亂은 聲望이 잇든 政界의 元老 부에늬제로스가 反政府軍에 荷擔을 宣言하고 起動하야 儀然 그 成行은 全世界 注目의 焦點이 된다.
由來 긔리시야는 王黨派와의게 對抗하는 共和黨派가 부단히 政權爭奪을 繼續하고 현재는 주아루다리스을 首班으로 王黨派政府가 政權을 掌握하엿다.
事起는 이미 1933년 아데네에 구데타을 敢行하고 僅一日로서 瓦解한 以來 流謪의 不遇의 생활으로 계속하고 잇슨 野心家 뿌라스지라스將軍의 示唆에 의하야 現政府에 불만을 가진 將校등이 共鳴하야 일우킨 叛亂에 불과하나 부에늬제로스가 참가한 것만큼 今日 亂軍의 勢力은 상당히 크게 보혀진다.＜125＞

사무王國退位
사무國 뿌라쟈데이데陞下은 드듸여 正式退位을 申告하엿다. 생각하면 昨秋 白內障治療를 이유로 王妃同伴 渡英遊時 이미 비장한 결의을 가지고 잇섯다.
退位의 원인은 여러 가지로 생각되나 요컨대 本國政府와의 意見이 매사마다 들이고, 그 昔日, 代代 사무王室손애 잇든 主權이 時代의 推移와 共히 有名無實히 되어 가는 것을 傍觀하는 것이 勝氣의 氣性의 陞下로서 전듸기 어러운 최대의 원인이엿슬 것이다.
1925년 11월 26일 前國王 라마6世의 死去하고 사무 第7代의 王位를 繼하야서부터 在位11年. 陞下가 退位의 後는 甥아닌 바다. 마히도르殿下 第8代의 王位에 就任할 것이다.

맑首相軍擴聲明 好轉又好轉을 繼續하여 온 歐洲安全保障機構工作은 畵龍點晴이라고 할 만한 獨逸引込運動의 최후의 土壇場으로서 英首맑토날트氏의＜126＞ 軍擴聲따문에 形勢俄然逆轉의 微兆가 잇다.
軍擴聲明의 내용은 지금것 恒久平和의 확립에 노력한 것은 英國뿐이다. 英國以外의 各國은 平和工作의 名實로 실은 軍備擴張에 餘念이 업는 이 상태로서는 현재의 國際平和機構뿐으로서는 안심하고 戰爭에 준비하게는 되지 안는다. 英國政府도 잇때 陸海空軍의 전면에 亙한 軍備擴張을 결의하엿다. 獨逸再軍備工作은 國際平和을 破壞한 것이라고 非難하엿다. 이래서 몬점 怒한 것은 獨逸이엿다.＜127＞
