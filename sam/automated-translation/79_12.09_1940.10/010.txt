Confidential Room, Various Insides of Our Society
The Situation of Newspaper Companies

New System at the Maeil Sinbo

Mr. Yi Sang-hyŏp Steps Down, Upper-Level Personnel Structure Reformed

After the Dong-A and Chosun newspapers vanished from the journalism scene on August 10th, the public watched closely to see what the future path of the Maeil Sinbo, now the sole Korean-language newspaper in Korea (朝鮮), would be. In mid-September, Mr. Yi Sang-hyŏp (李相協), who was Vice President and a veteran figure with about 30 years in the newspaper business, suddenly resigned. Around the same time, Managing Editor Mr. Kim In-i (金麟伊) also resigned. Following these changes at the top leadership, a personnel reshuffle in the upper echelons occurred as follows:
The position of Vice President will remain vacant for the time being. Instead, a Chief Writer (主筆) system was established, with Mr. Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿), former chief writer of the Chosun Ilbo, appointed to the post. A General Affairs Bureau was newly created, and Mr. Kim Tong-jin (金東進), who was active as the Tokyo Bureau Chief for the Chosun Ilbo 4-5 years ago and then served as a councilor (參事) at the Chosŏn Manchuria Colonization Company (鮮滿拓殖會社), was appointed as Managing Director (常務) and concurrently head of this bureau. Mr. Chŏng In-ik (鄭寅翼), formerly the Tokyo Bureau Chief for the Maeil Sinbo, was appointed as Managing Editor. Mr. Yi Sang-ch'ŏl (李相喆) remained in his position as Business Manager. The executive lineup after the restructuring is as follows:<13>
President: Ch'oe Rin (崔麟)
Executive Director (專務): Fukae Shikayoshi (福江鹿好) (Former Government-General Library Section Councilor 理事官)
Managing Director & General Affairs Bureau Chief (常務取締役兼總務局長): Kim Tong-jin (金東進)
Chief Writer (主筆): Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿)
Managing Editor (編輯局長): Chŏng In-ik (鄭寅翼)
Business Manager (營業局長): Yi Sang-ch'ŏl (李相喆)

Maeil Sinbo Readership Surges

The Maeil Sinbo, which decided to increase its capital from the previous one million won to two million won, has the personnel structure described above. Meanwhile, there have also been many personnel changes at the directly managed local bureaus (地方 있는 직할지국). As it has somewhat inherited the base of the Dong-A and Chosun newspapers, its readership is said to have surged by 80% in the past month and a half.

The Subsequent Course of the Chosun and Dong-A Companies
- Mr. Song Chin-u Appointed as Representative Director -

Here is a report on the subsequent settlement process for the Chosun and Dong-A newspaper companies, which closed their 20-year history pages on August 10th.

Reorganization of the Dong-A Ilbo

The Dong-A Ilbo Company (東亞日報社), a joint-stock company with 700,000 won in capital and 350,000 won paid-in, decided at a shareholders' meeting to dissolve the company concurrently with the newspaper's closure. Liquidation committee members are currently handling the settlement affairs. Prior to this, the last board of directors at the joint-stock company was reorganized, resulting in Mr. Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹), who had previously held an advisory position, becoming the Representative Director (代表取締役). There were numerous other changes to the lineup as well. Specifically:
Representative Director: Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹)
Director (取締役): Yang Wŏn-mo (梁源模)
Director: Paek Kwan-su (白寬洙)
Director: Kim Yong-mu (金用茂)
Auditor (監査役): Chang Tŏk-su (張德秀)
Auditor: Im Chŏng-yŏp (林正燁)

Final Scene Inside the Company
The liquidation process for the joint-stock company seems likely to extend over the next several months. Until then,<14> Mr. Song Chin-u and a few staff members occasionally come to the office to handle remaining tasks. Consequently, it is said that decisions regarding the disposal of the company building and the liquidation of equipment like the rotary press and fixtures have not yet been made.

Employee Job Placements
Among the employees expected to face mass unemployment, several editorial staff members have transferred to reporter positions at the Maeil Sinbo. About ten business department staff members are said to have found jobs in banks and companies.

The Subsequent Course of the Chosun Ilbo Company

The Chosun Ilbo Company (朝鮮日報社), a joint-stock company with 500,000 won fully paid-in capital, has transformed into the Eastern Culture Institute (東方文化學員), continuing activities in various cultural fields under the leadership of former president Mr. Pang Eung-mo (方應謨). To handle the liquidation of the former Chosun Ilbo Company:
Pang Eung-mo (方應謨)
Sin T'ae-ak (辛泰嶽)
Ch'oe I-bok (崔利福)
These three individuals have taken full responsibility and are currently engaged in liquidation affairs, which also seem likely to take several months.

Four Department Head System Reorganized - Active in Publishing Culture

Although its mission as a daily newspaper has ended, the company has decided on a policy to continue steady activities in the field of publishing culture through monographs and daily magazines. It has reorganized the former Chogwang Company (朝光社) structure as follows:
General Affairs Department Head (總務部長): Ch'oe I-bok (崔利福)
Sales Department Head (販賣部長): Wŏn T'aek-yŏn (元澤淵)

Library Department Head (圖書部長): Pang Chong-hyŏn (方鍾鉉)

Recent Scene Inside the Company
The imposing 5-story building standing prominently on T'aep'yŏng-ro Avenue was initially considered for conversion into a public medical facility, but a final decision has not yet been reached and seems to be under consideration. Currently, President Pang and key employees occasionally come to work as before, handling the wrap-up affairs. The Chogwang Company continues<15> its operations within that building.
It is said that a certain newspaper company in the city made inquiries about purchasing the rotary press, but the deal seems yet undecided.

Four Million Eight Hundred Thousand Won from America - Annual Amount for Mainland Church and Other Projects

According to the newly reformed education system, various schools like Aoyama Gakuin (靑山學院), hospitals, and all other social projects previously managed with aid from the United States are to be handed over to mainland Japanese (內地人) hands. Consequently, all positions previously held by Westerners are being succeeded by mainland Japanese, and the shadow of foreigners is disappearing from academies, churches, and medical institutions. The amount of aid that used to come from foreign countries (mainly the United States) for all these institutions reportedly amounted to about 4.8 million won per year.

Less Than One Million Won in Korea - A New System is Being Established for Korean Christian Society Too

Following the policy in mainland Japan (內地), all Christian institutions in Korea (朝鮮內) are also being transferred from foreign hands to Korean (朝鮮人) hands. So, how much money used to come into Korean society from foreign countries (mainly the United States)? It is said to be less than one million won for schools and other institutions.

Nation's Number One Tuberculosis Sanatorium - Communications Bureau Budget of 1.7 Million Won

To save unfortunate people dying from tuberculosis, the Government-General Communications Bureau (總督府 遞信局) has secured a budget of 1.7 million won to establish a tuberculosis sanatorium. It will acquire 60,000 pyeong (坪) of land in P'yŏnggang (平康), Kangwŏn Province (江原道), a high plateau region at an elevation of 4,000 feet (尺). It is expected to accommodate about 200 patients.<20>

**Summary**

This October 1940 article from Samch'ŏlli magazine details significant shifts in Korean society under Japanese colonial rule, focusing on media consolidation and cultural control. It reports the closure of the Dong-A Ilbo and Chosun Ilbo, the subsequent restructuring and readership boom of the Maeil Sinbo (now the sole Korean daily), and the transition of foreign-funded (primarily American) religious and educational institutions to Japanese or Korean control. The piece also covers movements of prominent Korean figures, lists numerous Koreans working for Manchukuo's Concordia Association (Kyōwakai), and provides updates on cultural productions like films and plays, including budget details, adaptations ("Ch'unhyangjŏn" modernized controversially), and the growing emphasis on state-mandated "culture films."

Historians would find the article valuable for its detailed snapshot of the tightening control over Korean media and cultural spheres in 1940, reflecting the wartime "New System" (新體制). The specific names and positions listed in the Maeil Sinbo restructuring, the newspaper liquidations, and particularly the extensive list of Koreans in the Manchukuo Concordia Association offer concrete data on collaboration and participation within the colonial structure. The financial details regarding foreign mission funding withdrawal and film/exposition budgets also provide economic context to the political and cultural changes.

博覽會 豫算 七十六萬圓 -京城日報의 總預算이-

9월 10일부터 京城 東大門 밖 淸凉里에서 개설하고 있는 京城日報社 주최 「朝鮮博覽會」는 連日 관객이 多數 遝至하여, 어느 일요일 같은 날은 하로 동안에 6만명에나 달하였다는 바, 듣는 바에 의하면 이 박람회의 총 경비는 76만원이라더라.

「無影塔」에 六千圓,  原作者에 三百圓

劇團高協에서 지난 9월 12, 3, 4일을 府民舘에서 「無影塔」을 3일간 상연한 바 그 총결산이 6천원에 달했다 하는데 거기서 준비금으로든, 3천 5백원과 원작자 玄鎭健씨에게 원작료 3백원 드린 것을 제하고 2천여원의 순이득이 있었다 한다.

「春香傳」을 樂劇化

內地와 滿洲에서 크게 환영을 받은 朝鮮樂劇團이 秋夕佳節을 이용해서 서울 府民舘에서 「春香傳」으로 中央公演을 했는데 新體制에 맞게 한다고 해서, 房子가 自轉車를 타고 나오고, 李道令과 春香의 러브 씬에 「一目見た時好きになつたのよ」라는 노래를 불르고 하여 일반 관중들이 불유쾌하게 역였다는 것이다. 역시 「春香傳」은 古典의 美를 잃지 않는데 그 생명 있지 않을까.

「안해의 倫理」에 劇作家 登場

徐恒錫씨가 지휘 중에 있는 藝興社에선 「안해의 倫理」란 영화로서<19> 첫 출발을 하는데 남녀 배우들 출연비가 各 一人 앞에 5,6백원식 된다 하며, 「愛戀頌」 제작 후의 徐씨의 시험인만큼 일반은 많은 기대를 가진다 한다.

新開地, 處女圖에 난관

제작 도중에, 경비문제로 중지하고 있는 작품 중 「新開地」 「돌쇠」 「處女圖」 등이 있다.

「집없는 天使에 二萬八千圓 -文藝峯 出演費 九百圓-

高麗映畵社 「집없는 天使」의 총 경비는 2만 8천원인데, 그 중에서 文藝峯의 출연비가 9백원 金一海가 1일 20원씩이라한다. 그런데 출연 일수는 약 40일 가량이라 하니 총액을 계산하면 8백원의 거액이나 된다고-.

「福地萬里」의 後報

北鮮과 滿洲 등지를 배경 삼어, 1년有餘의 긴 시일을 디려 촬영을 끝낸 「福地萬里」는 그 제작본부인 高麗映畵社에서 버젓이 내여 놓을 수 없는 작품이라 해서 일반 공개를 중지하고 있다고 한다.

夕影이 「民謠集」 -文化映畵로 制作한다-

朝鮮映畵令이 실시된 이래, 비상히 문화영화 상영을 강제하고 있음으로 각 영화회사에선 여기에 적응하기 위하여, 朝映에서는 安夕影감독으로 「民謠集」을 촬영 개시했고 고려에선 「小鹿島」 「勝利의 뜰」을 제작한다는데. 「勝利의 뜰」은 이미 완성되어서 朝鮮서 처음 인증 받는 문화영화라고 한다.
