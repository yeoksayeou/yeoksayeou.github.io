At the Stationery Store

"Give me one pencil."
"Yes, shall I give you one with a hard lead (心)? Or a soft one?"
"Give me a soft one. I'm going to write a letter to my fiancée."

It Depends on How You Think

Executive (重役): "If you hadn't drunk alcohol, you might have become a mayor (市長) by now."
Subordinate: "Yes... but when I drink alcohol, I feel like I've become a minister (大臣)..."

Patience is Best

Maid (어멈): "Sir (영감님), I can't stand how the mistress (마님) nags."
Master (영감님): "Don't be like that, just endure it. Look at me, haven't I endured the same nagging as you for 29 years?" <153>

**Summary**

This short piece consists of three brief, humorous anecdotes. The first describes a man buying a soft pencil specifically to write a love letter. The second contrasts an executive's view of his subordinate's potential ruined by drink with the subordinate's feeling of importance when drunk. The third features a master advising his maid to endure the mistress's nagging by pointing out his own long suffering. From a historical perspective, these vignettes offer glimpses into everyday social interactions, humor, and gender/class dynamics in 1940 Korea. They reflect common tropes about romance, workplace hierarchies, and marital complaints, providing historians with subtle insights into social attitudes and light entertainment of the colonial period.
