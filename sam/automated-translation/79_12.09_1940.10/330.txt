Films of Clear Autumn

Angels Without a Home (집없는 天使)

Koryŏ Film Association Production (高麗映畵協會作品)
Producer: Yi Ch'ang-yong (李創用)
Planner: Kim Chŏng-hyŏk (金正革)
Screenwriter: Sŏ Ku-wŏn-jŏng (西龜元貞)
Director: Ch'oe In-kyu (崔寅奎)
Cast: Mun Ye-bong (文藝峰)
Kim Il-hae (金一海)
Kim Sin-jae (金信哉)
Chin Hun (秦薰)
Yun Pong-ch'un (尹逢春)
Pok Hye-suk (卜惠淑)
Kim Han (金漢)
Chŏn T'aek-i (田澤二)
Pak Ch'ang-hyŏk (朴昌赫)
Hong Ŭn-sun (洪銀順)
Paek Nan (白蘭)
Kim Yu-ho (金裕虎)

Also featuring 20+ children from Hyangninwŏn (香隣園)

Angels Without a Home! Even counting just within Seoul, the wandering children (浮浪兒), numbering in the thousands, are truly a flock of angels without homes.
Startled by the daily threats of the streets and barely growing up under the scrutiny of the public eye, has anyone ever reached out a warm, sympathetic hand to soothe even one of these young souls?
Mr. Pang Su-wŏn (方洙源), on his way back from Tokyo, noticed the tearful fate of these vagrant children at the station head and is a philanthropist who found a place to pour his passion. He took in wandering children he saw, raised them, and reawakened their lost innocence. As Mr. Pang overcame all sorts of difficulties, the public did not withhold praise and support, so the future of those homeless angels can be said to be bright.

Angels Without a Home is a humanistic work based on these facts.

(Synopsis - 梗槪)
Dizzying lights, a chaotic pandemonium of greedy cries, the coming and going of luxuriously dressed ladies and gentlemen. In a corner of Seoul, far removed from such realities, lies the life of wandering children growing up in a different environment.
Yong-gil (龍吉) and Myŏng-ja (明子) were siblings living such a life under the supervision of the "Taishō" (『다이쇼-』). Blaming Yong-gil for a mistake, the "Taishō" intended to sell Myŏng-ja off to a bar, but Yong-gil ran away from the "Taishō's" house and was rescued by Pastor Pang Su-wŏn (方洙源).
Pastor Pang <162> was a passionate man who doubted meaningless prayer and considered guiding young vagrant children his heavenly calling. Overcoming his wife's opposition, Pastor Pang borrowed the warehouse of his brother-in-law An In-kyu's (安仁圭) villa in Han-gangni (漢江里) and left with the children. Here, their new life unfolded, but facing the rainy season, Pastor Pang floundered due to impending financial difficulties. The children, too, amidst the silent mountains, rivers, and trees, began to miss the bustling night streets of Seoul.
Among them, the big-headed Yŏng-p'al (永八) and Hwa-sam (化三) plotted to escape while Pastor Pang was out, but Yong-gil strongly dissuaded them. [Yong-gil?] fell into the flooded river and was near death.
Pastor Pang returned, hurried, and sent Yŏng-p'al to call Doctor An. Pastor Pang trusted Yŏng-p'al. When Yŏng-p'al reached Doctor An's house, Myŏng-ja was also there.
Everyone rushed over, Yong-gil survived, and met his beloved sister.
Yŏng-p'al and Hwa-sam also repented their wrongdoings, and the other children who had run away returned.
With Pastor Pang's efforts, An In-kyu's support, and general public sympathy, the financial difficulties were resolved, and the children grow up happily in a peaceful atmosphere. Pastor Pang says:
"The problem of vagrant children is a major social issue, and my strength is utterly inadequate, so I know I cannot completely solve this problem. However, if only my work could move society and become a catalyst for larger social facilities, there would be nothing more fortunate than this."

The Wife's Ethics (안해의 倫理)

Chosŏn Art Promotion Company Production (朝鮮藝興社 作品)
Producer: Sŏ Hang-sŏk (徐恒錫)
Scenario: Chŏn Tong-min (全東民)
Dialogue: Yu Ch'i-jin (柳致眞)
Director: Kim Yŏng-hwa (金永華)
Cinematographer: Hwang Un-jo (黃雲祚)
Cast:
Pak Chin-ch'ŏl (朴進哲) - Na Ung (羅雄)
Kim Yŏng-suk (金英淑) - Chi Kyŏng-sun (池京順)
Yi Ki-t'aek (李基澤) - Chŏn T'aek-i (田澤二) <163>
Kim Tong-ho (金東昊) - Kim Han (金漢)
Mary (메리-) - Paek Nan (白蘭)
Chŏn Kwang-ho (田光鎬) - An Pok-rok (安福祿)

-Synopsis (梗槪)-
-The world of history is indeed the world of human relations (人倫).
This dark and sad story also has as its historical backdrop the reality of the so-called 40s, where the confusion of morals (모랄) and the decay of ethics (倫理) are announced, and continues as follows.
-Company employee Pak Chin-ch'ŏl (朴進哲) could be happy because he had his wife Yŏng-suk (英淑) and young Hyŏp (協).
However, perhaps because happiness is always precarious, one day, when Chin-ch'ŏl had no choice but to quit his company over a trivial matter, these three people came to face the harsh trials of fate.
Chin-ch'ŏl hid this unfortunate situation from his wife while trying every means to get a job elsewhere, and his wife Yŏng-suk, without dreaming of her husband's unemployment, passed several sorrowful days alone with Chin-ch'ŏl, as if nothing had changed among the three.
A few days after Yŏng-suk learned of her husband's unemployment from Chin-ch'ŏl's close friend Kim Tong-ho (金東昊), as if adding insult to injury, their young son Hyŏp, the embodiment of all memories between the two, past and present, also fell ill. Yŏng-suk, unfamiliar with the ways of the world, solely focused on Hyŏp's treatment and found work amidst the rough atmosphere of a bar (빠-).
The bar's atmosphere, brewed from the body odor of numerous men, alcohol, cigarette smoke, and the mad rhythm of jazz (째-스), always manifested a world of intense sensuality, and Yŏng-suk could not help but be flustered by the persistent temptation of the mine owner Yi Ki-t'aek (李基澤). One night, tempted by Ki-t'aek and Mary (메리-), Yŏng-suk drank late into the night at XX mountain villa, returning home with bloodshot eyes and disheveled hair after the first cock crowed.
-Chin-ch'ŏl, in a complex mix of self-scorn (自嘲), jealousy, and anger, left home. As Yŏng-suk and Hyŏp's dark and lonely life repeated, Chin-ch'ŏl, advised by Tong-ho, decided to reconcile with his wife and sought out his home. On that day, fate played a cruel trick – Ki-t'aek came looking for Yŏng-suk.
Chin-ch'ŏl once again gave up on his wife and left home. Yŏng-suk, weeping in despair, had no choice but to appeal for her innocence through death (死). <164>

(Synopsis - 梗槪)
The wandering boy Tolswe (돌쇠), solitary with no one to rely on (天涯無托), drifting aimlessly—the extremely simple and good-natured Tolswe. Having never been embraced by warm and gentle human affection and only tasting loneliness, Tolswe's eyes opened to love for the opposite sex, but this too was merely unrequited love for him. Nevertheless, the strength to live on resolutely arose within him.

Silla Legend "The Shadowless Pagoda" (新羅傳說 『無影塔』)

5 Acts, 7 Scenes
"Theatre Troupe Kohyŏp" (「劇團 高協」) Autumn Performance
Original Work by Hyŏn Chin-kŏn (玄鎭健), Adapted by Ham Se-dŏk (咸世德)

(Synopsis - 梗槪)
1200 years ago, during the reign of King Kyŏngdŏk (景德王) of Silla (新羅)! Having unified the Three Han (三韓), with successive kings making the propagation of Buddhism the basis of governance, Silla at that time was at the peak of Buddhist cultural prosperity (隆盛).
Thus, wanting to build two pagodas at Pulguksa Temple (佛國寺), they widely sought famous artisans from across the land. At this time, Asadal (阿斯怛), an unknown stonemason (石手) from Paekche (百濟), harboring burning artistic ambition (野望) and grand hopes for brilliant success, left his beautiful young wife behind in Puyŏ (扶餘) and departed for Kyŏngju (慶州).
Thus, three years passed, and Asadal completed the "Tabot'ap" (多寶塔), the pinnacle of sculptural art at the time. On April 8th, the Buddha's Birthday Festival (佛誕祭), when the Sŏkk'at'ap (釋迦塔) was also nearing completion (未久), Chuman (珠曼), the daughter of the Yijon (伊飱 - then a high minister) Yujong (唯宗), who had accompanied the Queen's procession (行次) to Pulguksa, was instantly captivated (혹하고) by Asadal's artwork and appearance.
Meanwhile, Taejung Kim Chi (待中 金旨), who sought to spread Tang learning using the power of the Tang Dynasty (唐朝) as a shield (방패), proposed marriage (請婚) intending to arrange a marriage (定婚) between his son Kim Sŏng (金城) and Chuman.
However, Yujong, who possessed nationalistic (國粹的) ideas revering (崇揚) Silla's traditional Kuksŏndo (國仙道), rejected the proposal due to ideological conflict with Kim Chi and arranged a marriage (定婚) for Chuman with Kim Kyŏng-sin (金敬信), the younger brother of a comrade.
But Chuman's affection for Asadal grew stronger (끓니는 정이 용소슴첬다) day by day. Every night, under the pretext of offering Buddhist prayers, she went to Pulguksa and met (맞났다) Asadal.
Finally (드드듸여), in early August of the year when the Sŏkk'at'ap was just a few days from completion (끝이 난다는), one night, as Asadal and Chuman lamented their unfulfillable love, reluctant to part (떠러지기를) late into the night, Kim Sŏng, whose marriage proposal had been rejected (拒婚당한), formed a gang (徒黨) and attacked (엄습했다) them.
That night, Asadal's wife <165> Asanyŏ (阿斯女), who had been waiting only for her husband's return after he left home promising (期하고) to be back in three years, having received no news even after the promised three years passed, could endure (견듸다) all hardships no longer and traveled the long distance from Puyŏ to Kyŏngju.
She secretly (몰내) entered Pulguksa, where entry was forbidden (듸듸리지안는), wanting to meet (맞나보고저) her husband just once, but the indifferent monks prevented her wish from coming true. Asanyŏ clung (부뜰고) to the pagoda and wept. Then, a monk told her that about 10 ri from there was a pond called Shadow Pond, where any shadow in the world would be reflected (빛이니); if her husband finished building the pagoda, its shadow would be reflected (빛일테니) in the pond, so she should wait there, and when the pagoda's shadow appeared (빛이거든), she should come to meet (맞나러) her husband. Thus, several days passed until the Kabae Festival (嘉俳節 - Ch'usŏk) in August. Chuman's relationship with Asadal was discovered (발각되여), and her rigidly obstinate (頑固一徹) father Yujong put Chuman on the execution platform (磔臺) and executed her (死刑).
The following night, by the lake called Shadow Pond, the exhausted, despairing, and indignant (비분한) Asanyŏ finally attempted suicide and threw herself into the water. Meanwhile, Asadal, led by a monk to find (찾어) his wife, was able to embrace his pitiful (불상한) wife, who had been rescued by villagers.
Asanyŏ endlessly felt (느낀다) embraced in her husband's arms. At that moment, the sound of bells announcing the completion of the two pagodas to all (왼) of Sŏrabŏl (the capital) could be heard. (End) <166>

**Summary**

This article from the October 1940 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine reviews three recently released Korean films: "Angels Without a Home," "The Wife's Ethics," and "Tolswe," along with a synopsis of a stage play adaptation of the "Shadowless Pagoda" legend. "Angels Without a Home" tells a humanistic story based on the real-life efforts of Pang Su-wŏn to care for Seoul's street children. "The Wife's Ethics" portrays the moral struggles and eventual tragedy of a couple facing unemployment and temptation in the modernizing society of the 1940s. "Tolswe" follows a simple, wandering young man finding the strength to live despite loneliness and unrequited love. The "Shadowless Pagoda" play dramatizes the Silla-era legend of the Paekche stonemason Asadal, his wife Asanyŏ, and the tragic love involving the noblewoman Chuman during the construction of Pulguksa's pagodas.

From a historical perspective, the article offers insights into the social concerns and cultural productions of late colonial Korea. The focus on social issues like orphans ("Angels Without a Home") and marital breakdown amidst economic hardship and perceived moral decay ("The Wife's Ethics") reflects contemporary anxieties. The production details (studios, directors, actors) document the colonial film industry, while the "Shadowless Pagoda" synopsis shows the continued interest in retelling national legends, possibly as a form of cultural assertion or exploration of traditional values versus modern influences, even under Japanese rule. The mention of "Tang learning" versus "Kuksŏndo" hints at underlying cultural tensions.

方목사는<162> 무의미한 기도에 의혹을 품고 어린 부랑아의 선도를 천직으로 삼는 정열가였다. 方목사는 부인의 반대를 물리처가면서 처남되는 安仁圭의 漢江里 별장창고를 빌어 어린이를 데리고 떠났다. 여기서 그들의 새로운 생활이 벌려젔으나 장마철을 당한 方목사는 닥처오는 재정난으로 갈팡거렸다. 어린이들도 짹소리도 없는 산과 강, 나무 밑에서 새삼스러히 서울의 분주한 밤거리를 그리워하는 것이였다.
