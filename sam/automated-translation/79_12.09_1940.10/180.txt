Kyŏngsŏng Remodeling Plan
Compared to Tokyo, Osaka, or other major foreign cities, facilities lacking in our Seoul and things wished to be fixed.

Commentator In Chŏng-sik

1.  There is a need to install air-raid shelters, green spaces for air-raid evacuation, etc. Underground facilities for air-raid evacuation are required in front of the Bank of Chosŏn (鮮銀前), in front of the Station (驛前), at the Hyehwamun stop, near Tongdaemun, etc.
2.  A subway line connecting Tongdaemun to Ch'ŏngnyangni is needed. The subway should also serve the purpose of air-raid evacuation.
3.  The old evil practices of "chŏnse" and "sagŭlse" (monthly rent) for Chosŏn houses must be strictly regulated.
4.  Elementary school buildings should be made of wood, reducing construction costs and expanding the number of school buildings.
5.  Social policies are required to properly organize the slum villages in places like Sindang-ri and rehabilitate the dugout-hut dwellers (土幕民) on the mountainsides.
6.  Efforts must be made to urgently alleviate the housing shortage.
7.  I absolutely oppose the relocation of Namdaemun.

Professor Ko Hwang-gyŏng, Kyŏngsŏng Ewha Women's College

The establishment of a Juvenile Court,
a Girls' Reformatory,
and dormitories for working women is necessary.

Yi Kŭng-no, Chosŏn Language Society (朝鮮語學會)

1.  Facilities for educational institutions are urgently needed. From elementary school to university, regardless of the type of school, the fact that there are people unable to study because there aren't enough schools is, frankly, somewhat unbecoming of a modern civilized nation.
2.  Facilities for training scholars are urgently needed. If there is no place for scholars, who are responsible for cultural development, to emerge, that would indeed be regrettable. Scholars do not just appear on their own. Their personal lives must be stable, and they need to sit within libraries (書庫). In a civilized society, there should be many academic research institutions established by the state, and beyond that, many institutions established through the charitable donations (惠施) of wealthy individuals, so that scholars can settle down and work with peace of mind everywhere.

Female Writer Yi Sŏn-hŭi

Putting all other facilities aside, wouldn't it be great if the Prefecture (府) cleaned out the toilets more often and didn't let the trash cans overflow?
This summer, during the month-long rainy season,<94> when the toilets overflowed and blowflies swarmed blackly inside the house and on the streets, I lamented thus:
"Though the culture in my head claims citizenship of Berlin, I am still a woman living in such a dirty and squalid home (집족)."

Professor, Musician Kye Chŏng-sik, Ewha College

①. Complete installation of a sewage system.
②. Facilities enabling flush toilets.
③. Prompt removal of trash cans.
④. Increase the number of streetcars and traffic volume to alleviate congestion.
⑤. As a major capital (大京城), do not show the disgraceful sight of letting beggars roam freely (散在).
⑥. There should be prefectural or government-run agencies to rescue the unemployed and find them jobs, like Germany's Arbeitsamt (알바이텔·암트).

Film Director Pak Ki-ch'ae, Koryŏ Film Company (高麗映畵社)

If we speak of desires, there is no end.
First is the facility for film production.
Whenever a Chosŏn film comes to market, we often hear the lonely (孤高) voices lamenting its inadequacy. However, one must know that the cause of Chosŏn film's inadequacy lies not so much in its capacity or technique, but primarily in the lack of facilities for its production.
Therefore, not under the authority of any individual, but as an institution of the people, the establishment of a film studio capable of producing good movies should be the most urgent (急先) matter for the dignity of a major city.

Professor, Novelist Yi Hyo-sŏk, P'yŏngyang Taedong Technical College (平壤大同工專)

1.  Thoroughly implement a plan for greenification (綠化) by planting many trees on every street. Probably no excellent city in the world lacks lush trees.
2.  Authorities should take the initiative to establish cultural facilities before waiting for public opinion (民意). For example (假營), things like a music hall should be established by the city authorities to actively hold performances at cost (實費) for the citizens.

Composer Hong Nan-p'a, Kyŏngsŏng Broadcasting Station (京城放送局)

Even setting aside world-class major cities, to maintain the dignity of a civilized city, I believe the following five points, at the very least, must be implemented urgently and indispensably (不可不急).
1.  Expansion of large and small parks & creation of new children's playgrounds.
2.  Increase of roadside trees and the protection and cooperation of citizens regarding them.
3.  Implement a 'One Household, One Tree' policy (一戶一木主義), ensuring that not only new constructions but also existing houses plant at least one garden tree (庭木) per household as much as possible (可及的).
4.  Urgent completion of water supply and sewage systems.
5.  Do not accumulate refuse (塵芥) and unclean materials (不潔物) on the streets (路上). Above.

Novelist Ch'ae Man-sik<95>

Night soil carts (糞尿馬車) stroll (潤步) down the middle of Chongno, and eateries raise flies by the "millions" (『百萬마리』씩 가축하고)—isn't the removal of such shameful aspects more pressing (솔직하지 않을까요 lit. more honest/frank) than new facilities?

To insist on a postcard, "Please install this and that in Kyŏngsŏng too..." is utterly frivolous talk (虛浪한 수작). To be even more absurd...
"For people like me who always suffer from lack of sleep, please build a 'Peaceful Eye Hospital' (安眼病院) that can put you to sleep for 4 or 5 days' worth in just 30 minutes!"

Novelist Han Sŏl-ya

Adding more facilities within Seoul? That's like discussing catching an ox inside an eggshell.
So, if I were to become a policymaker (爲政者), I would tear down one-third of Seoul's houses. If not that, I would mobilize the million citizens, truly make them do labor service (근로봉사), and even if just little by little each day, dig away at Pukak and Inwangsan until a level plain (坦坦한 평야) is made.
That would be the greatest facility for Seoul and also a plan for the next hundred years (百年大計). Because adding more facilities to the current Seoul just makes the land too narrow (지대가 좁아저버리니까요).
The foolish adults and people living in Seoul seem to only know that Namsan exists, but they don't seem to know how to look down coolly at the Seoul basin from Namsan; even if they know how to look, they don't seem to know how to think.

Novelist Yi Ki-yŏng

1.  Completion of the sewage system (下水道).
2.  Hygienic facilities (衛生的 設備).
3.  Balance of urban beauty (都市美)—fountain parks, etc.
I think it would be good if these several points were improved first.

Novelist Ham Tae-hun

Even in Tokyo, the streetcars and buses run incessantly without connection breaks, so one never feels inconvenience in boarding; moreover, with the provincial railway lines (省線), the well-organized transportation network is something unimaginable in Kyŏngsŏng. Kyŏngjŏn (京電) seems to be making efforts, but they pack people in like loading pigs (도야지를 싯듯 막 처싯고), and the rude words and actions of the conductors make it impossible for anyone with a mouth not to argue. Above all else (무었보다), Kyŏngsŏng must resolve this bus and streetcar problem. Expansion of parks, cleaning and increase of public toilets are, of course, essential. Also, ensure that night soil (糞尿) & waste (汚物) removal happens at least once a week. Furthermore, the horse-drawn carriage system (馬車制度) seen in Xinjing (新京) and Fengtian (奉天) might be a good means to solve the transportation network problem. Also, in places like Xinjing, facilities for an air-raid-safe, noiseless city (防空, 無騷音都市) are in place; because there are no streetcars but only freight vehicles, buses, and cars, it seems very peaceful. I wish they would create an air-raid-safe, noiseless city with such facilities. (Continued on page 75)<96>

Writer Ch'oe Pong-ch'ik

It is a welcome event (喜事) that in recent years, wealthy individuals (有産人士) from the peninsula have stepped forward, contributing large sums of money to educational institutions (數育機關). However, it is a great regret (遺憾) that they do not pay attention to social facility projects. In a large capital like Kyŏngsŏng, approaching one million people, to rescue and educate (救濟敎養) the annually (追年) increasing number of orphans (孤兒), if a foundation of about one million won were thrown down for a facility like Peking's Tong Sŏn-tang (! 同善堂), truly the kingdom of orphans would descend (臨할 것이다) upon the earth. Next, there are immense drawbacks (弊端), worries, and difficulties with student boarding houses (學生自由寄宿). A facility of about 400,000 or 500,000 won is desired, enough to provide them peace of mind and spur their guidance (敎導). Wouldn't it also be a good thing if the office buildings of the two companies whose publications were recently discontinued (폐간된 兩社) were redirected (轉向) for this purpose?<75>
<75, 94-96>

**Summary**

This 1940 survey from Samch'ŏlli magazine gathers opinions from various Korean intellectuals and public figures on necessary improvements for Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul). Common themes include urgent needs for basic sanitation (sewage, waste disposal, clean toilets), better housing and slum clearance, expansion of public transportation (subways, more streetcars/buses, fewer crowds), increased green spaces (parks, trees), more educational and cultural facilities (schools, research institutes, performance venues), and social welfare institutions (orphanages, job centers, women's dormitories). Some unique suggestions include air-raid preparedness, film production facilities, noise reduction measures, and even radical reshaping of the city's topography. The piece reflects contemporary urban problems exacerbated by colonial conditions and wartime anxieties. For historians, the article provides a valuable snapshot of urban concerns in late colonial Korea, revealing anxieties about sanitation, housing, transportation, cultural development, social welfare, and the perceived backwardness compared to Japanese and other major cities. The mention of air-raid shelters, the German Arbeitsamt, and the oblique reference to the recently closed newspapers (Dong-a Ilbo and Chosun Ilbo) anchor it firmly in its specific historical context of intensifying war mobilization and repression.

1. 교육기관의 시설이 급합니다. 小學으로 붙어 大學까지 어떤 종류의 학교를 물론하고 학교수가 모자라 공부를 못하는 이가 있다는 것은 그 말붙어가 현대 문명국가의 체면에 좀 자미가 없습니다.
2. 學者 양성기관의 시설이 급합니다. 문화발전의 책임자인 학자가 날 곳이 없다면 그야말로 서운한 일입니다. 學者는 절로 되는 것이 아닙니다. 개인의 생활이 안정되고 書庫속에 앉어야 됩니다. 문명한 사회에는 국가의 시설로 된 학술연구기관도 많으려니와 이 밖에 재산가의 개인의 惠施로 된 기관도 많아서 學者가 곳곳이 안심하고 들어앉게 됩니다.

女流作家 李善熙

①. 하수도의 완비.
