German Marriage Encouragement Measures
Marriage Expense Loans

It is the same in any country that setting up a household after marriage requires a considerable amount of money. If men and women of marriageable age (適齡期) do not marry solely for this economic reason, the state cannot simply ignore it.
In Germany, immediately after the Nazi policies were implemented, a marriage encouragement law was enacted. According to this law, interest-free loans up to 1,000 Marks were provided to those without marriage funds.
Repayment of this money is made at 1 percent, or 3 percent if the wife has a job, but the interesting part is that when a child is born, <44> 25% of the total amount is exempted. This marriage loan is not only aimed at encouraging marriage but also carries significant meaning in encouraging childbirth (產兒) that follows.
Of course, this is limited to children born alive; one child exempts 25 percent, and four children exempt 100 percent [translator's note: the text incorrectly states 4% here, likely a typo for 100% based on the context of 25% per child], so the more children born, the smaller the amount to be repaid becomes. Also, if reported within one year after a child is born, a suspension of the repayment is also permitted.
In the four years since this law was implemented, over 800,000 couples married using these loans, amounting to over 500 million Marks. Among these, there have been more than 60 [translator's note: this number seems extremely low, possibly a typo or misunderstanding in the original article; 600,000 or a percentage might be more plausible] cases where the loan was exempted due to childbirth.

Child Allowances

The money repaid from the loans granted under the marriage encouragement law described above becomes aid money for families with many children. There are two types of aid: one-time and continuous.
The one-time aid payment serves as funds to prepare housing facilities and undergarments, which are often neglected in homes with many children. In other words, the objective is to build a sound foundation for the child's future growth and development (成育).<45> This is limited to households with four or more children under 16 years of age, with a maximum limit set at 100 Marks per child and 1,000 Marks per family.
Families receiving continuous child-rearing support are defined as those with five or more children under 16 and an annual family income of up to 1,000 Marks. Besides this, there is an aid system for families with many children and promising children to enable them to receive higher education.
Laws like these arose from the intention within Nazi policy to encourage families with many children and to economically assist households facing hardship due to having many children, thereby improving their livelihood.

Mother's Medals

Mothers who bear many children in compliance with national policy are, in a sense, contributors to the nation. They should receive the gratitude and respect of all citizens. Therefore, like soldiers who achieved merit in war, they are awarded medals. Having four children earns a Bronze (銅) medal, five to seven children an Silver (銀) medal, and eight or more children a Gold (金) medal – their merit is divided into these three classes. If a Hitler Youth member encounters a woman wearing such a medal on the street, they must absolutely perform a hand salute (擧手) to show respect.<46>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* describes Nazi Germany's policies to encourage marriage and childbirth. It details the state-sponsored marriage loan program offering up to 1,000 Marks interest-free, with loan forgiveness of 25% per child born (up to four children). The article also mentions one-time and continuous child allowances for large families based on the number of children and income, as well as educational support. Finally, it highlights the "Mother's Cross" awarded in Bronze, Silver, and Gold based on the number of children (4, 5-7, 8+ respectively), emphasizing the respect accorded to these mothers, including mandatory salutes from Hitler Youth members. For historians, this article is interesting as it showcases Korean awareness and reporting on specific social engineering policies of Nazi Germany during the colonial period. It reflects the global interest in state-led population policies and might offer comparative insights into how similar concepts (like promoting fertility for the state) were viewed or discussed in colonial Korea, potentially influenced by or contrasted with Japanese policies.

獨逸結婚獎勵策
結婚費用貸付

결혼해서 한 살림을 꾸미려면 얼마큼의 큰 돈이 든다는 것은 어느 나라나 마찬가지다. 適齡期의 남녀가 이 경제적 이유만으로써 결혼 안하고 있다고 하면 여기엔 국가도 모른 체 할 수 없는 것이다.
독일에선 나치쓰 정책이 실시되면서부터 곧 결혼장려법이 되였다. 이 법률에 의하면 결혼자금이 없는 者에게는 1천 마-크를 한도로 해서 무이자의 대부금을 교부하게 되였다.
이 돈의 返償은 1파-센트씩, 안해가 직업을 가지고 있는 경우엔 3파-센트씩 갑는데 재미있는 것은 아이가 나면<44> 전액 중 25%가 면제된다. 이 결혼 대부금은 단 결혼을 장려하는 것이 목적이 아니고 여기에 따르는 産兒를 장려하는 의미가 많이 포함되여 있다.
물론 이것은 살어서 나오는 아이에게 限하는데 한 사람이 25파-센트, 네 사람이면 4파-센트로 많이 나면 날수로 返償할 금액이 적어진다. 또 아이가 나서 일년간은 신고만 한다면 返償金의 중단도 용서해 준다.
이 법률을 시행 후 4년 사이에 대부해서 결혼한 者가 80萬組餘 금액으론 5億마르크를 넘는데 그 중에 아이가 나서 대부금 면제 받은 것이 60건 이상이 되어 있다.

小兒扶助金

상술한 바와 같은 결혼장려법에 의해서 한번 대부해서 返償된 돈은 아이가 많은 집의 부조금이 되어 있다. 부조금은 일시적인 것 계속적인 것 두 가지가 있다.
일시적으로 주는 부조금은 아이들 많은 집에 행용 버려 두기 쉬운 주택설비와 속옷 등을 장만하는 자금이 된다. 다시 말하면 아이의 장래 成育 때문에 건전한 토대를<45> 만들려고 하는 목적이다. 이것은 16세 이하의 아이가 4인 이상 있는 집에 限하는데 아이가 한사람에 대해서 최고 백말크 일 가족으로서 천말크까지 규정되였다.
계속적으로 양육비를 받는 가정은 16세 이하의 아이가 5인 이상 가족의 연수입 천말크까지에 정하여 있다. 이밖에 아이가 많은 집 그리고 유망한 아이에겐 고등교육을 받게 하기 위해서 부조금제도가 있다.
이상과 같은 법률은 나치스정책이 多産가족을 장려하고 아이가 많은 때문에 그 생활이 곤란한 가정을 경제적으로 도아서 생계를 높이 하려는 마음에서 생긴 것이다.

어머니의 勳章

아이를 많이 나어 국책에 순응하는 어머니는 일종 나라의 공로자다. 전국민의 감사와 경의를 받어야 한다. 그래서 전쟁에서 功을 얻은 군인과 같이 훈장을 준다. 네 아이를 갖이면 銅, 5인에서 7인까지는 銀, 8인 이상은 金훈장-으로 그 공로는 3단에 난호여 있다. 히틀러 유-겐트단원이 만약 거리에서 훈장을 붙인 부인을 만나면 반드시 擧手의 禮를 하여 경의를 표하지 않으면 안된다.<46>
