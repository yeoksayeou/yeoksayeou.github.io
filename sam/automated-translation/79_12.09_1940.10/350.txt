An Actor's Notebook
						Chi Kyŏngsun
Diary of Filming "Ethics of a Wife"

X Month X Day
There's a saying that ten years is an olden time.
Thinking back, it's embarrassing enough to make me blush, but it's already been ten years since I started my life on the stage.
Looking back now, it's still dim what these accumulated ten years have contributed to me as an actor or as a woman, and how they flowed by.
In any case, since yesterday, I have come to stand not on the stage but before the camera.
My heart, having become a captive of the black and white fantasy land, an uncharted territory of experience, cannot help but feel a strange excitement, like a child standing before an examiner, with a fluttering chest.
It sounds funny, but it seems the attitude of appearing before the camera is awkward and clumsy for me, to the point that it doesn't allow even a hair's breadth of pride from ten years in the arts. Perhaps it's because the role I've taken on is that of the protagonist "Yŏngsuk," who can be seen as controlling more than half of this "Ethics of a Wife." More characteristic than that might be the overall impression and atmosphere of the Yehŭngsa (藝興社) film crew. As expected from a film crew proud of being a young cinema team, all members are like that, but moreover, I cannot help but admire the artistic attitude of Director K, who is in Chosŏn for the first time, with his incredibly meticulous acting guidance and excessive seriousness, which can be irritating at times.<174>
Today too, during the location shoot in Sŏngbuk-chō (城北町), when he shouted "Test!" and stared intently with all his nerves concentrated, he looked so frail that I genuinely feared he might collapse. It was truly frightening. Sometimes, we do tests like this eight or nine times.

X Month X Day
Yesterday, I was tormented by Director Kim's repeated "test" whirlwind, and it had been a while, but mostly I was overwhelmed by the siege of onlookers, so my body felt heavy since morning.
On top of that, today was the beauty salon scene, and I don't know how many tens of kilowatts they were, but the radiation from the light bulbs, truly the size of watermelons, made sweat crawl like worms down my back, making it unbearable. Most of all, my eyes were dazzled, and the electric light shone down even on the hair getting a permanent wave, making my head feel foggy and unable to collect my thoughts.
Ultimately, honing one's craft is truly the penance of age itself; I keenly feel this anew as these near-disasters continue these days. When I said I couldn't stand the total onslaught of the lights because it was too hot, the remark from Production Chief X, who was beside me, was mischievous.
"You don't know that the Ethics of a Wife essentially corresponds to the ethics of a slave! Hahahaha!"
"Then did the ethics of a husband during the matriarchal system correspond to the ethics of livestock? Hohohoho."
Even for a joke, it was too mischievous, so my retort shot back at him, and the Production Chief just went "Heh-" which felt slightly satisfying.

X Month X Day
Today, thanks (!) to the cloudy weather, I could rest at the company during the morning. I told old stories to Hyŏp-i, who plays the role of Yŏngsuk's—that is, my—son, and received a whopping five caramels (갸라메르), for which I was criticized (!) by Mr. X for profiteering.
We gathered in the reception room and had an informal acting critique session, where I received the undeservedly flattering assessment of "Passable" (稍良), which made me feel ashamed in my heart. Can one truly never achieve a performance that satisfies others, let alone oneself? It felt regrettable again. If the agony of expression is the eternal task of an actor, then one must train with utmost devotion, but sometimes I cannot help but doubt my own talent. Whenever this happens, I hesitate, thinking perhaps it would be better in many ways to become an ordinary housewife like so many other women. Truly, there is something in people that cannot be achieved by passion and ambition alone. That is why "Those who can believe in the possible surely possess both the highest pride and happiness that only humans can have."
The sky started clearing up from the evening, so tomorrow we will probably go for location shooting at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑). Thinking about it, I realize anew that even though I live in Seoul, it's been a long time since I last visited Ch'anggyŏngwŏn. <175>

**Summary**

This article is a series of diary entries by the actress Chi Kyŏngsun, reflecting on her transition from a decade of stage acting to her first film role in "Ethics of a Wife" (안해의 倫理) in 1940. She describes the challenges of working with cameras, the intensity of the director (Mr. K, new to Korea), the physical discomfort of studio lights, interactions with the crew (including banter about the film's title), and moments of self-doubt about her acting abilities contrasted with her artistic aspirations. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the colonial Korean film industry through the personal lens of an actress, mentioning a specific production company (Yehŭngsa), the presence of possibly Japanese directorial staff (Mr. K), the blend of cultural elements (Japanese place name Sŏngbuk-chō, Japanese loanword 'caramel'), and the personal reflections on career versus traditional female roles within that specific historical context.

演技者의 手帖
						池京順
「안해의 倫理」撮影日記

X월X일
十年一昔이란 말이 있다.
생각하면 스스로 얼굴을 붉힐 만큼 부끄러운 일이나 무대 우에 생활을 가진지 벌서 10년을 헤이게 된다.
이 10년이란 축적된 시간이 연기자로써 또는 한 여자로써의 내게 무엇을 어떻게 기여하고 흘러갔는지는 돌아보는 지금도 어둡기만하다.
여하간 나는 어제부터 무대가 아닌 카메라 앞에 스게 되었다.
경험의 처녀지인 黑白의 幻想國의 질거운 포로가 된 내 마음이란 시험관 앞에 슨 어린애같이 설네이는 가슴을 이상한 흥분 속에서 느끼지 않을 수 없다.
웃우운 말이지만 藝道 10년이란 毫末의 矜恃조차 許치 않을 만큼 내게는 어색하고 서투른 것이 카메라 앞에 나스는 태도인 것만 같다. 특히 내가 맡은 역이 이 『안해의 倫理』의 過半分을 좌우한다고 볼 수 있는 주인공역인 『英淑』이기 때문인지도 모른다. 그보다도 특징적인 것은 藝興社 촬영반의 전체적인 인상과 분위기에 있다할 것이다. 젊은 映畵陣을 자부하는 촬영반 전원이 그러하려니와 더욱이 朝鮮은 처음인 감독 K씨의 말할 수 없이 치밀한 연기지도와 지나치게 진지한 때는 한편 짜증이 나면서도 예술가적인 태도에 敬服하지 않을 수 없다.<174>
오늘도 城北町 『로케』에서 『테스트』!하고 부르짖으며 전신경을 집중하여 응시할 때 외양이 퍽 약한 분이라 졸도하지 않을가 생각될 만큼 진정 무서웠다. 이렇게. 『테스트』만 여덜번 아홉번식 할 때가 있다.

X월X일
어제 몇번이고 거듭되는 우러 金감독씨의 『테스트』선풍과 오래간만에 시달린 관계도 있었지만 제일 구경군들 포위진에 氣壓되어 오늘은 아침부터 몸이 묵어웠다.
그런데다가 오늘 미용실 장면인 만큼 몇십키로 왓트나 되는지 정말 수박만큼한 電球의 방사선에 등에는 지렁이같이 땀이 기도니 견댈 수 없었다. 제일 눈도 부시려니와 『파-머넨트』하는 머러에까지 電光이 내려쪼여 머러가 머엉한 것이 정신차릴 수 없다.
결국 연기를 닦는다는 것은 나히의 고행 그대로임을 새삼스러히 느낄만큼 요사이는 半逢變이 계속된다. 『라잇트』의 총공격이 너무 뜨거워서 못살겠다고 했드니 옆에 있던 제작주임 X씨의 말슴이 짓궂이다.
「안해의 倫理는 본시 노예의 윤리에 통하는 것을 모르는군! 허허허허!」
「그럼 母系제도 때의 남편의 윤리는 가축의 윤리에 통했습니까? 호호호호」
농담이라도 너무 짓궂이기에 쏴준 내 反問에 제작주임씨 「헤에-」해버러는 것이 약간 통쾌했다.

X월X일
오늘은 曇天의 덕택(!)으로 오전중 社에서 쉬일 수 있었다. 英淑의 즉 내 아들役인 協이에게 옛날이야기를 해주고 무려 『갸라메르』 다섯개를 얻어먹어 暴利행위라고 X씨에게서 비난(!)을 받었다.
응접실에 둘러앉어 비공식 演技합평회가 있었는데 나는 『稍良』이라는 惶悚한 평을 들어 마음에 부끄러웠다. 다른 사람은 물론 스스로 會心할 연기는 가일 수 없는가! 다시 안타가웠다. 표현의 괴로움이란 연기자의 영원한 과제이라면 丹心으로 연마해야 되겠지만 때로는 자기재능을 의심하지 않을 수 없고 이를 때마다 그저 다른 많은 여자와 같이 평범한 가정인이 되는 편이 여러가지 의미에서 옳지 않을가 浚巡케 된다. 사실 사람에게는 정열과 의욕으로만은 이루지 못하는 무엇이 있다. 그러기 때문에 「可能을 신념할 수 있는 사람은 분명히 사람만이 가질 수 있는 최상의 자랑과 행복을 아울러 갖는 것이러라.
저녁부터 하늘이 맑어지니 내일은 昌慶苑 로케를 가게될 것이다. 그러고 보면 서울 살면서도 昌慶苑 가본지도 오래인 것을 새삼스러이 느낀다. <175>
