**Lullabies of Various Countries**

**Japan**

Baby, lullaby lullaby

Baby, lullaby lullaby
Go to sleep quickly
If you cry, from the mountains
A mother pheasant will come

My cute baby's
Plump face
If you cry, the mother pheasant
Will snatch it away

**Germany**

(1) Silver Drizzle

Baby, lullaby lullaby
Rain is falling
Like silver drizzle
Without a sound
Into dreamland tonight
It descends

Baby, lullaby lullaby
Rain is falling
The silver drizzle
Is like silver thread
For baby's toys
It will make them

(2) Memorial Day Night

The Memorial Day evening
Becomes quiet<178>
Softly, softly, snowflakes
Come quietly

My beloved baby
Sleep soundly
Tomorrow morning by your pillow
Toys will be piled high

The Memorial Day evening
Dawns quietly bright
Baby, sleep well
Until the day breaks

**France**

(1) Even the Stars in the Sky

Even the stars in the sky
Are already asleep
In the deep blue sky
Lying face down
In the land of stories
They are dreaming

The flowers in the field
Are already asleep too
The cute insects'
Songs have ceased
Everyone familiar
Is dreaming
Is dreaming

(2) Land Where Lilies Bloom

Dreamland is
A beautiful land
Where lilies are in full bloom
A pure white land

Cute babies
Holding hands
"Good, good,"
Say the angels playing

**USA**

(1) Sleeping Land

The sleeping garden
Is amidst dreams
On the garden hill stands a familiar person
Come, good baby
I'll give you a flower bouquet

The sleeping land
Is amidst dreams
On the round hill
A rabbit climbs up
Our baby is good
Shall I give you rice cakes?

(2) Closing Your Eyes

The toy doll baby
Sleeps at midnight
When you close your eyes
A little horse
Starts walking
When you close your eyes
It starts walking<179>

**Italy**

Baby, Sleep

Baby, lullaby lullaby
Sleep well
Our baby, God
Will protect you

**Russia**

Owl

The owl, the owl
Sits desolately
Hoo, hoo
The night dew falls
To the hooting song
That lulls the baby to sleep
Drizzling rain falls softly
The autumn night has deepened

The owl, the owl
Mother's face
Hoo, tears and dew
Fall down
Baby, let's sleep quickly
Mommy is cold
Autumn has deepened

**Ireland**

Water Bird

Sob, sob
The water bird cries
Searching for its mother
Crossing mountains and seas
Searching everywhere
"Where are you, Mommy?"

Sob, sob
Crying in its dream
The water bird searching for its mother
Is our baby
The mother you search everywhere for
Is right here

**England**

Midnight

In the quiet forest
Even the sparrows are asleep in the forest
Baby, lullaby lullaby, go to sleep quickly

In the quiet night sky
Even the crescent moon sleeps
Baby, lullaby lullaby, go to sleep quickly

**Wales** (께-일)

Snow White Cradle

Snowstorm, don't blow
The night is deep
In the snow-white cradle (or: on the snow-white blanket)
The baby sleeps

Wind, don't blow
Don't shake the door
The baby is already
In dreamland <180>

**Summary**

This article presents translated lyrics of lullabies from various countries, including Japan, Germany, France, the USA, Italy, Russia, Ireland, England, and Wales ("께-일"). Each lullaby reflects common themes of sleep, nature, dreams, parental love, and sometimes gentle warnings, adapted into Korean verse. From a historical perspective, this collection published in 1940 Korea offers a glimpse into cultural interests during the late colonial period. The inclusion of lullabies from both Axis and Allied/neutral nations might suggest an attempt to present universal human themes or perhaps navigate censorship by focusing on seemingly apolitical content like children's songs, while still providing readers with exposure to foreign cultures. The use of older Korean orthography and vocabulary is also notable.
