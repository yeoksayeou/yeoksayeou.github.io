**Summary**

This article is a brief listing of five newly published books in Korea in October 1940. It provides the title, author (if applicable), a short description of the contents for some, the publisher's name and address, postal transfer account number, price, and shipping cost for each book. The books cover humor, historical biographies of Korean warriors, collected letters of famous Koreans, a novel about a female university student, and Christian children's stories. From a historical perspective, this list offers insight into the types of literature being published and consumed during the late colonial period, including works by Korean authors like Pang In'gŭn and topics ranging from humor and modern life (female student) to national history (warriors, famous figures) and religion (Christianity). The inclusion of publisher details and prices also provides economic and social context.
