Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제9호
Publication Date: 1940년 10월 01일
Article Title: 建國 十年의 滿洲國과 朝鮮人 近況, 朝鮮內 資本의 進出과 人物의 集散 等 (Manchukuo After Ten Years Since Foundation and the Recent Situation of Koreans, the Advance of Capital from within Korea and the Dispersion of Personnel, etc.)
Author: 金東進
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

Manchukuo is the stronghold of our Japan's moralistic new continental policy and the base for the construction of the new East Asian order. Without the sound development of Manchukuo, our Japan's national life cannot possibly be maintained, and consequently, we cannot hope for stability in East Asia and peace in the world. Therefore, the development and cultivation of Manchukuo can be said to be one of the grave duties imposed upon our Japanese people.

There may be various ways to fulfill this national duty. Some may exert their strength within the country, and others may devote their sincerity outside the country, but it is natural that a greater responsibility lies with those who strive directly within Manchukuo for the development and prosperity of that nation. Now, I intend to speak about the recent situation of the 1.3 million Korean compatriots in Manchuria who are entrusted with this important role, but first, as a matter of order, I would like to briefly discuss the historical relationship between Korea and Manchuria.

As you all know well, Korea and Manchuria have extremely close ties geographically, historically, and ethnically. Setting aside the geographical relationship of being separated only by the two rivers, the Amnokkang (鴨綠江) and the Tumangang (豆滿江), ethnically, when we consider that Koguryŏ (高句麗) and Paekche (百濟), founded 2,000 years ago, were both tribes of Puyŏ (扶餘) which arose in Manchuria, we cannot help but realize that we have an inseparable blood bond with Manchuria. Historians classify Manchurians, Mongolians, and Koreans, along with people from the mainland (內地人), as belonging to the Tungusic lineage, in other words, the northern Asian ethnic group. Considering that our ancestors, Koguryŏ<64> and Paekche, branched off from the Manchurian tribe of Puyŏ, it seems that Koreans today having a kind of yearning (一種이 동경) for Manchuria, thinking of Manchuria as an extension of Korea, and traveling back and forth is by no means a coincidental phenomenon, but perhaps a revelation from God. Governor-General Minami (南總督) proclaiming "Korea and Manchuria as One Body" (鮮滿一如) might be seen as expressing a part of the sentiment that has sprouted in the hearts of Koreans.

Setting aside such ancient historical facts for now, even looking back at modern times, already in the early Qing Dynasty (淸朝), because Korean farmers migrating to various parts of South Manchuria across the Amnokkang, known as Sŏgando (西間島), gradually increased, the two countries concluded the Jiangdu Treaty (江都會) and respectively guarded their borders to prevent crossing. However, farmers who secretly farmed despite the prohibition (冒禁潛耕) never ceased. But this was mostly in an unstable state called cross-border cultivation (越境경작), referred to as "farming in the morning and returning at dusk" (朝耕暮歸) or "farming in spring and returning in autumn" (春耕秋歸). However, during the early Meiji (明治) era, when a five-year famine continued in Korea, the number of people crossing into fertile Manchuria, defying the strict prohibition laws, increased dramatically. This is perhaps the trigger (*觸? likely 嚆 'start') for the group migration (단체적 滿洲 移住) of Koreans to Manchuria. Eventually, in the 13th year of Meiji, Kyŏngjin (庚辰, 1880), Korea established four counties (四郡) in Tongbyŏndo (東邊道) across the Amnokkang, implementing a self-government system (自治制) for Koreans and governing them. Around the same time, in Pukkando (北間島), i.e., the opposite bank of the Tumangang, the number of people crossing in violation of the national prohibition (國禁) surged, and they settled down on the land.

Since then, although there were temporary fluctuations (消長) due to political and security relations, just as water flows to lower ground, the natural tide pulling people from barren land to fertile land could not be stopped by human effort.

In this way, Koreans made the Pukkando and Tongbyŏndo regions their second Korea (제2 朝鮮). After temporarily recuperating their strength (기력) there, they used these places as bases (근거지로) to advance further: eastward, crossing the steep Chigyŏngnyŏng pass (志格嶺之峻險) and the Mudanjiang River (牧丹江), reaching the Sanjiang Province (三江省) region; northward, up to the banks of the Heilongjiang River (黑龍江邊); and westward, into the East Mongolian (東蒙古) desert regions. They created paddy fields (水田), and the sound of peasants singing happily (擊壤歌) could be heard across the vast fertile plains (沃野萬里). However, the path they walked (행로) was by no means a smooth main road (탄탄한 大路), but a great adventure of pushing through thorn bushes (가시덤불). You probably all know about the miserable state (참상) of Koreans before the founding of Manchukuo, even without me reiterating it here.

Then, in a turn of fate (乾坤一擲), the Manchurian Incident broke out on September 18, Showa 6 (1931), leading to the establishment of Manchukuo. All the previous tyranny and evil were shattered, and under the founding spirit of "Five Races Under One Union" (五族協和), Koreans in Manchuria also became one of the constituent elements (構成分子) of Manchukuo, receiving equal treatment (均等의 대우). As their lives gradually stabilized, the number of people newly crossing from Korea to Manchuria suddenly increased, eventually reaching the figure of 1.3 million.

The total area of Manchukuo is 1,303,000 square kilometers, about six times larger than Korea's 220,760 square kilometers,<65> but the population is only 39.5 million. Looking at this by ethnicity, Han Chinese (漢人) are the absolute majority with 34.7 million, followed by Manchurians (滿洲人) with 1.8 million, Koreans (朝鮮人) with 1.3 million, Mongolians (蒙古人) with 1.02 million, Mainland Japanese (內地人) with 650,000, White Russians (白系露人) with 50,000, and other foreigners in that order. The 1.3 million Koreans is by no means a small number, and although their economic life is meager, since its foundation is firmly rooted (立脚) in agriculture on the land, it can be said that they hold an unshakable position (確乎不拔).

Looking at the distribution of Koreans in Manchuria, the largest number is in Kando Province (間島省), accounting for 80% of Kando's total population, or 520,000 people. This is followed by Fengtian (奉天), Andong (安東), Longjiang (龍江), Mudanjiang (牧丹江), Jilin (吉林), etc. Although they reside in all 14 provinces of Manchuria, the numbers are small, less than a thousand, in places like the three Xing'an provinces (興安 東, 西, 北) and Rehe Province (熱河省). Since the livelihood (生業) of Koreans in Manchuria is agriculture, the rural population is naturally much larger than the urban population. However, in cities that serve as centers for rural areas, like Mudanjiang and Fengtian, around 10,000 Koreans live in each. Places like Andong Station (安東驛) or Tumen (圖們) in Kando Province are border cities, and Yanji (延吉) and Longjing (龍井) are special cities where many Korean compatriots live. The influence (勢力) of Koreans in a city like Mudanjiang is astonishing, and this can perhaps be seen as a microcosm (縮國) showing the growth of Koreans in the newly emerging Manchukuo.

The main occupation (大業) of Koreans in Manchuria is stable agriculture (安定 농업), with 80% of total households engaged in farming. Even among those farming, owner-farmers (自作農) and landlords (地主) are extremely few; most are tenant farmers (小作人), often tenanting land owned by Manchurians. This was because most Korean compatriots in Manchuria crossed over empty-handed (赤手空拳), literally "men carrying bundles on their backs, women on their heads" (男負女戴), and because the former Chinese Northeast regime (舊支那 東北政權) absolutely oppressed (壓迫) Koreans, preventing them from finding ways to improve their lives. However, after the founding of the state, the unjust exploitation (無道한 착취) by landlords disappeared, public order was restored (恢復), and the government provided protection (保護), leading to a remarkable stabilization of life. Furthermore, the Choman T'akchik Chusikhoesa (朝滿拓殖株式會社), established as a national policy institution (國策的 기관) by the Japanese and Manchukuo governments, and its sister organization, the Mansŏn T'akchik Hoesa (滿鮮拓殖會社), have begun creating owner-farmers (自作農 創定) according to government policy. They are also gathering unsettled farmers (浮動농민) and unscrupulous traders (不正業者), many of whom existed before the abolition of extraterritoriality (治外法槪徹廢), into rural areas, and settling new farmers entering from Korea (入植식히여). By distributing an average of 4 chōbu (町步, approx. 4 hectares) of arable land and lending (貸與) farming funds to enable farming (영농케 함으로), the miserable situation of tenant farmers seen in the past will gradually be resolved in the future.

The relationship between Korean farmer migration to Manchuria and the Manchurian colonization companies (滿洲拓殖會社) is as close as lips and teeth (唇運輔車). Moreover, these institutions are indispensable for the migration of farmers without capital. From Showa 12 (1937) until this spring, 14,430 households comprising 61,761 people migrated to Manchuria through these companies. Additionally, 1,047 households with over 3,600 unsettled farming families and unscrupulous traders already living in Manchukuo were gathered and stabilized. Besides these, the number of so-called dispersed pioneers (分散開拓民) who migrate independently without going through the companies may reach a considerable figure, but this cannot be known as there are no clear statistics. Generally, pioneers (開拓民) who migrate<66> through group settlement via the companies receive a subsidy (補助金) of about 80 yen, including 40 yen for travel expenses from the Government-General of Korea (朝鮮總督府), and Manchukuo also provides a support fund (造成金) of the same amount to promote sound development. Therefore, the future of Korean pioneers is considered truly bright (양양한).

Besides these national policy institutions, privately managed (民間經營) institutions related to pioneers are by no means few. There are many large-scale farms accommodating pioneers, led by Man-Mō Sangyō (滿蒙産業) in Harbin (哈爾濱) and Santaku Kigyō (三拓企業) in Xinjing (新京). Currently, out of the 32 million chōbu of arable land (可耕地) in Manchukuo, only 14 million chōbu, less than half, is cultivated (旣耕地). The remaining 18 million chōbu (about four times the total arable land of Korea, 4.9 million chōbu) remains as vast wasteland (荒地). The development of Manchukuo must start with pioneering (開拓). Therefore, the Manchukuo government has established a pioneering policy as one of its three major national policies (3大國策). In response (호응하야), the Imperial Japanese government decided that over the 20 years from Showa 12 (1937), 1 million households of mainland Japanese pioneers (內地人開拓), totaling 5 million people, and 10,000 households of Korean pioneers (朝鮮人開拓民), totaling 50,000 people annually, would be settled (入植) for land reclamation. Manchukuo's policy towards Korean pioneers was based on the Kantōgun's (關東軍) instructions of May 30, Showa 11 (1936), and the decision of the Immigration Affairs Processing Committee (移民事務處理委員會) in January, Showa 12 (1937). The settlement number was limited (制限) to 10,000 households annually, and the settlement area was controlled (統制), decided yearly for the 23 counties in Kando Province and Tongbyŏndo where many Koreans already lived. The consolidation areas for existing Korean pioneers were also limited to 17 counties where Koreans resided heavily, such as Tieling (鐵嶺) and Kaiyuan (開原). However, later, as Koreans in Manchuria exercised self-restraint (自重) and, spurred by the China Incident (支那事變), the patriotic fervor both inside and outside Korea grew, deepening their self-awareness as subjects of the Empire (皇國臣民), this was reflected (反映) to the Kantōgun and Manchukuo authorities. Through the deliberation of the Immigration Affairs Processing Committee on July 27, Showa 13 (1938), the restricted areas were abolished (鐵廢), allowing settlement principally anywhere. Furthermore, according to the Basic Guidelines for Manchurian Pioneering Policy implemented this year, Korean pioneers are now handled in accordance (準據) with mainland Japanese pioneers. For instance, the Manchurian Pioneer Youth Volunteer Corps (滿洲開拓少年義勇軍), previously limited to mainland Japanese, has opened its doors. Already, about 60 youths have completed training at the Government-General of Korea's Manchurian Pioneer Candidate Training Center (朝鮮總督府 滿洲開拓志願者訓練所), received two months of training at the Uchihara Training Center (內地內原訓練所) in the mainland, and will cross to Manchuria like mainlanders. They will enter the Manchurian Pioneer Youth Volunteer Corps Training Center (渡滿開拓청년의용대훈련소) for three years of training, after which they will serve in Manchurian pioneering under the same conditions as mainland Japanese. This is one aspect of the revised treatment of Korean pioneers. Also, the integration (統合) of the Manshū Takushoku Kaisha (滿洲拓植會社), established to handle mainland Japanese pioneer affairs, and the Mansŏn T'akchik Hoesa (滿鮮拓植會社), established for Koreans, stems from this spirit.

Livelihoods of Koreans in Manchuria other than agriculture include rice milling (精米業), land management (土地경영), trading in local specialties (特産物취인), automobile transport (自動車交通運수업), general goods retail (雜貨소매상), restaurants (料理業), mining (礦山), forestry (森林채벌), and government official positions (官公吏). Enterprises like the Nanman Bōseki (南滿紡績), a Kyŏngsŏng textile affiliate with 10 million yen capital under construction in Sujiatun (蘇家屯), Fengtian, are emerging. The increasing investment from capitalists within Korea, starting with the Tonghŭng Bank (東興銀行) in Kando, is a welcome phenomenon. In addition,<67> in cultural endeavors, the Man-Sen Nippō (滿鮮日報) in Xinjing, Manchukuo's only newspaper [for Koreans], holds the beacon for the Korean enlightenment movement (啓蒙運動). Also, in Fengtian, the monthly "Chae-Man Chosŏnin T'ongsin" (在滿朝鮮人通信), supervised by Mr. Sŏ Pŏmsŏk (徐範錫氏), contributes to the edification (敎化) of Koreans.

In the realm of government officials (官公吏), there are many holding considerable positions. Starting with Mr. Yi Pŏmik (李範益) (former Kando Province Governor), who is of Chokuninkan rank (親任級, Imperially Appointed) Manchukuo Councillor (參議), there are also Messrs. Ch'oe Namsŏn (崔南善) (Kenkoku University Professor), Pak Sŏgyun (朴錫胤) (Consul General to Poland), Yu Honghyn (劉鴻詢) (Kando Province Vice-Director), and Yun Sangp'il (尹相弼) (General Bureau of Development Councillor) at the Chokuninkan rank (勅任級, Imperially Commissioned). There are over 50 people at the Section Chief level and below Sōninkan rank (薦任官, Recommended Appointment) (including concurrent posts), and Mr. Chin Hangmun (秦學文氏) is a Managing Director (常務理事) of a Special Company (特殊會社). In the security field, the loyalty and bravery (忠勇) of the Korean Border Guard Unit (朝鮮人國境警備隊) in Kando Province is known throughout the world, and the Korean Gando Special Force (間島特設部隊) in Mingyuegou (明月溝), Kando Province, is a model (貴鑑) for the Manchukuo Army.

A common concern for Koreans abroad, not just in Manchukuo, is the education of their children (子弟). The number of elementary school students (小學 취학생) among compatriots in Manchuria is about 150,000, and the school enrollment rate (취학률) for school-age children (학령아동) is over 50%, a much higher phenomenon than in Korea. However, the facilities of educational institutions are extremely poor (貧약하야), incomparable to those in Korea. With the abolition of extraterritoriality (治外法權철폐), except for the 14 Common Schools (普通學校) managed by the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵) and the two Hamgyŏng Pukto provincial common schools (咸北道立普校) in Kando, all were uniformly transferred (移管) to Manchukuo and became Manchukuo National Schools (國民學校). However, as the educational level of Manchukuans is much lower than that of Koreans, and Manchukuo's financial situation (財政상태) is impoverished, it is difficult to provide superior facilities solely for Korean education. At a time when compulsory education (義務敎育) is about to be implemented in Korea, the educational facilities for Koreans in Manchuria should naturally be improved. This requires the influence of Koreans in Manchuria and substantial aid (援助) from the Government-General of Korea. The recent activities in Seoul of Messrs. Kim Ŭngdu (金應斗) and Hwang Ŭimyŏng (黃義明) as representatives petitioning (陳情代表) for the education of Koreans in Manchuria aim to achieve the general will (總意) of the Koreans in Manchuria. Regarding the relationship between the spirit of Manchukuo, "Ethnic Harmony" (民族協和), and Koreans, there is much criticism about Koreans holding a sense of superiority (優越感) towards Manchurians. This is an issue arising from the fact that mainland Japanese (日本內地人) form the core of the five ethnic groups and hold a leading position, leading Koreans, as fellow Japanese subjects, to also try to assert themselves as Japanese. However, mainland Japanese in Manchukuo generally still lack understanding of Koreans, coupled with the fact that some past actions (去就) of Koreans in Manchuria before the state's founding were treated as anti-state behavior, and also intertwined with the feelings of Manchurians (Han Chinese) towards Koreans, there were concerns that Koreans in Manchuria might face a crisis for a time. But later, as Koreans exercised self-restraint (自重) and embodied the spirit of Ethnic Harmony, and their efforts not to abandon their duties as constituent members became known to other ethnic groups, and also as the perception (認識) of mainland Japanese deepened significantly, the status of Koreans is gradually improving and advancing. They must make further progress from their current high point (百尺竿頭이 更進一步), exercise even more self-restraint, and heighten their magnanimity (裕度) as subjects of the Empire. (Continued on page 50)<68>

(Continued from page 68) Currently, the population growth rate within Korea is about 350,000 people per year. In 35 years, the current population of 24 million will double. In 70 years, the population will approach 100 million. Looking at the current situation, the majority of this surplus population (過剩人口) inevitably has to be sent out to Manchuria. Sending them to Manchuria not only contributes to solving the pressing surplus population problem within Korea but will also contribute greatly to the development of Manchukuo, our empire's lifeline. Thus, we can expect the effect of killing two birds with one stone (一石二鳥). Therefore, the trends among Koreans in Manchuria not only have a great influence on the future of all Koreans but are also deeply related to the Empire's construction of the new East Asian order, a fact that must be deeply borne in mind (명심).<50>

**Summary**

This 1940 article by Kim Tongjin argues for the importance of Manchukuo to Japan's "new continental policy" and the "East Asian New Order." It justifies Korean migration to Manchuria by emphasizing deep historical and ethnic ties, portraying Manchukuo as a natural extension of Korea. The author details the history of Korean settlement, acknowledging past hardships under Chinese rule but highlighting the supposed stability, equality ("Five Races Under One Union"), and opportunity offered under Japanese-controlled Manchukuo, particularly through government-sponsored agricultural settlement programs. While noting the significant Korean population (1.3 million) and their role in agriculture, it also mentions emerging Korean involvement in industry, commerce, and administration. Issues like inadequate educational facilities and friction stemming from Korean "superiority complex" towards Manchurians are raised, urging Koreans towards self-restraint ("자중") and cooperation within the imperial framework. The article concludes by framing continued Korean migration to Manchuria as essential for both alleviating Korea's population pressure and developing Manchukuo, thus serving Japan's imperial interests.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing for its explicit articulation of Japanese colonial ideology regarding Manchukuo and Korea's place within it ("鮮滿一如," Five Races Harmony). It showcases the mechanisms of state-sponsored migration and settlement used to solidify Japanese control and exploit Manchuria's resources, while simultaneously attempting to manage the Korean population. The author's perspective, likely reflecting or influenced by official views, provides insight into the propaganda directed at Koreans, emphasizing opportunity and shared destiny while subtly demanding assimilation and loyalty to the Japanese Empire. Historians would find the details on settlement policies (Choman/Mansŏn T'akchik companies, land allocation, subsidies, integration efforts), the demographic data, the discussion of inter-ethnic relations (Korean-Manchurian-Japanese dynamics), and the rationale for population transfer valuable for understanding the complexities of Japanese imperialism and the Korean diaspora experience in Manchukuo during this period.

기사제목	建國 十年의 滿洲國과 朝鮮人 近況, 朝鮮內 資本의 進出과 人物의 集散 等
필자	金東進
기사형태	논설

建國 十年의 滿洲國과 朝鮮人 近況, 朝鮮內 資本의 進出과 人物의 集散 等
						金東進
滿洲國은 우리 日本의 道義的 新대륙정책의 거점이오 東亞 新질서건설의 기지로 滿洲國의 건전한 발전이 없이는 도저히 우리 日本의 국가적 생명의 유지될 수 없으며 따라서 東亞의 안정과 세계의 평화를 바랄 수 없습니다. 그러므로 滿洲國의 개발 배양은 우리 日本국민에 지워진 중대한 의무의 하나이라고 할 수 있음니다.
이 국가적 의무를 이행함에는 여러가지 방법이 있겠다. 혹은 국내에서 힘을 쓰는 이도 있겠고 혹은 국외에서 정성을 바치는 사람도 있겠지마는 무었보다도 滿洲國 내에서 직접 그 국가의 啓發 번영을 위하야 노력하는 사람에게 보다 더 큰 책임이 있을 것은 물론이니 이제부터 이러한 중요한 역할을 맡어 갖이고 있는 103만 在滿朝鮮同胞의 근황을 말슴하려는데 우선 순서로 古來의 鮮滿관계를 잠간 말슴하려고 합니다.
朝鮮과 滿洲는 지리적으로나 역사적으로나 또는 민족적으로 보아 극히 긴밀한 관계를 갖이고 있음은 여러분도 다 잘 아시는 바라 鴨綠江과 豆滿江이라는 두 江이 새에 막혀있을 뿐이라는 지리적 관계는 막론하고 민족적으로보다도 저 2천년 전에 건국된 高句麗며 百濟가 모두 滿洲에서 발흥한 扶餘의 족속인 것을 생각할 때 우리는 滿洲와 끈흘내야 끈흘 수 없는 피의 엉킴이 있음을 께닷지 않을 수 없읍니다. 역사가는 滿洲人, 蒙古人, 朝鮮人을 內地人과  더부러 「퉁구스」족의 계통, 즉 다시 말하면 亞細亞북방계통의 민족이라고 칭하는데 옛날 우리의 조상이든<64> 高句麗와 百濟가 滿洲族인 扶餘로부터 파생되였다는 것을 생각하며 오늘날에 있어 朝鮮사람이 滿洲에 대한 一種이 동경을 갖이고 滿洲를 朝鮮의 연장같이 생각하고 건너단니는 것이 결코 우연한 현상이 아니라 신의 계시가 안인고도 생각됩니다. 南總督께서 「鮮滿一如」를 부르지즌 것은 朝鮮人의 가슴속에 싹도든 감정의 일단을 표현하여준 것이 아닌가고도 생각됩니다.
이상과 같은 고대의 역사적 사실은 且置하고 근대를 도라 보더라도 淸朝의 초기에 벌서 西間島라고 하는 鴨綠江을 긴너가 南滿洲 각지에 이주하는 朝鮮농민이 점점 많어지기 때문에 두 나라에서는 江都會이라는 조약을 체결하고 각기, 其국경을 직히어 서로 건너단니지 못하게 하였으나 冒禁潛耕하는 농민이 끊이지를 않었읍니다. 그러나 이는 대개 朝耕暮歸, 또는 春耕秋歸라고 하야 오늘의 말하는 바 越境경작이라고 하는 불안정한 상태에 있었든 것이지마는 明治 初年, 朝鮮에 5년 흉년이 계속되매 엄중한 禁法을 무릅쓰고 비옥한 滿洲로 건너가는 사람이 격증하얐으니 이것이 아마 朝鮮人의 단체적 滿洲 移住의 *觸일가 합니다. 필경 이리하야 明治 13년 庚辰에는 鴨綠江 건너편 東邊道에 朝鮮의 四郡을 두고 朝鮮人의 자치제를 펴고 朝鮮人을 다스렸으며 이와 전후하여 北間島 즉 豆滿江 對岸에도 國禁을 犯하고 건너가는 사람이 부쩍 늘어 대지에 발을 붙이고 살게 되었습니다.
其後론 정치적 관계와 치안관계로 인하야 일시적 消長은 있섰으나 물이 나즌 데로 흘러가는 것과 맛찬가지로 척박한 땅에서 비옥한 땅으로 끌리워 가는 자연적 대세에는 사람의 힘으로 막어내는 수가 없었읍니다.
이와 같이 되야 朝鮮사람은 北間島와 東邊道지방을 제2 朝鮮으로하야 여게서 잠시 기력을 휴양한 후에는 다시 그곳을 근거지로 하야 東으로 志格嶺의 峻險을 건너 牧丹江을 건너 三江省지방까지 北으로는 黑龍江邊까지 西便으론 東蒙古 사막지대까지 진출하야 水田을 萍成하야 沃野萬里에 擊壤歌소리가 서로 들리게 되었지만는 그들의 걸어온 행로는 결코 탄탄한 大路가 아니었고 가시덤불을 헤치고 나가는 큰 모험이었으니 滿洲國 건국전의 朝鮮人의 참상에 대해서는 여게서 제가 다시 말하지 안터라도 여러분이 다 아실 것일아합니다.
그러든 것이 乾坤一擲, 昭和 6년 9월 18일에 滿洲事變이 발발한 것을 계기로 滿洲國이 성립되자 종래의 모든 暴惡은 부서저버리고 五族協和의 건국정신 밑에 在滿朝鮮人도 滿洲國 구성분자의 하나로 균등의 대우를 받게되야 그 생활이 점차 안정되였음으로 새로히 朝鮮으로부터 渡滿하는 사람이 갑자기 불어서 필경은 130만이라는 數字로 헤이게 되였읍니다.
滿洲國의 총면적은 130만 3천 平方粁로 朝鮮의 22만 760餘 平方粁보다 약 6배나 되는 넓이를 가지고 있으나<65> 인구는 3,950만명밖에 되지 않음니다. 이것을 민족별로 보면 漢人이 절대다수로 3,470만 명, 고 다음이 滿洲人으로 180만 명, 朝鮮人 130만 명, 蒙古人 102만 명, 內地人 65만 명, 白系露人 5만 명, 기타 외국인의 순서인데 조선인 130만 명은 數字로 결코 적은 것이 아니며 또 그 생활이 경제적으로 빈약하나마 그 기초가 대지에 곧게 立脚하에 있는 농업이니만치 確乎不拔의 지위를 갖이고 있다고 할 수 있읍니다.
在滿朝鮮人의 분포상태를 보면 間島省에 제일 만히 間島 총인구의 8할인 52만명, 그 다음이 奉天, 安東, 龍江, 牧丹江, 吉林 등의 순서인데 滿洲 14省 중 안사는 곳이 없이 들어가 사나 興安 東, 西, 北의 3省과 熱河省같은 곳은 천명 미만의 少數입니다. 在滿 朝鮮人의 생업이 농업인 만큼 도시인구보다도 농촌인구가 훨신 많음은 물론이나 농촌의 중심도시가 되는 牧丹江이며 奉天가튼 데는 각기 만명내외가 생활하고 安東驛이나 間島省 圖們같은 곳은 국경도시로, 또 延吉, 龍井같은 곳은 특수도시로 朝鮮동포가 많이 사는데 牡丹江같은 도시의 朝鮮人의 세력은 놀낼만한 것이니 이는 또 신흥 滿洲國에서의 朝鮮人의 성장을 보혀주는 縮國라고 볼 수 있을가 합니다.
