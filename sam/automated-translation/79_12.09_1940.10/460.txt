Publication Date: 1940년 10월 01일
Article Title: 餞別, 나의 少年이 가든 날 (Farewell, The Day My Boy Departed)
Author: 張萬榮 (Chang Man-yŏng)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The steamship carrying him
Far in the scenery, in a straight line
Is departing.

Receiving no blessings whatsoever,
My boy, for whom tears were unusually common.

In his heart<208>
Carrying sorrow (悲哀) and resentment (傷恨),
To what country is he going now?

The steamship, on the blue water's surface,
Leaving a trail of smoke like sorrow,
Went beyond the horizon.

At that time, I, the tide wetting my feet,
Unaware, was waving a handkerchief towards the distant place.

At that time, I, seeing off the departing one,
With such a heart, shed tears profusely,
My cheeks were entirely soaked with tears.<209>

**Summary**

This poem by Chang Man-yŏng depicts the speaker's sorrowful farewell to a young man, referred to as "my boy," who is leaving by steamship. The boy departs without blessings, carrying sadness and resentment, heading to an unknown destination. The speaker watches, heartbroken and unaware of the rising tide, waving a handkerchief and weeping until the ship disappears beyond the horizon. From a historical perspective, the poem, written in 1940, resonates with the atmosphere of loss and forced departures common during the late colonial period in Korea. While the reason for the boy's departure isn't specified, it evokes potential themes of migration for labor, study, or even conscription, reflecting the hardships and separations faced by Koreans under Japanese rule, making it poignant for historians studying the social and emotional landscape of the era.

餞別, 나의 少年이 가든 날
						張萬榮
그를 태운 汽船이
風景 멀리 一直線으로
떠나가고 있다.

아모런 祝福도 받지 못하고
유달리 눈물만 흔하든 나의 少年.

가슴에는<208>
悲哀와 傷恨을 지닌채
그는 이제 어느 나라로 가는것이냐.

汽船은 푸른水面에
한줄 연기를 슬픔모양 남기고
水平線 등을 넘어 가버렸다.

나는 그때, 밀물이 발을 적시는것도
아지못하고 먼 곳을 향하여 손수건을 흔들고 있었다.

나는 그때, 가는이를 바라주는
그런 마음으로 눈물을 줄줄 흘리며
뺌을 온통 눈물로 적시고 있었다.<209>
