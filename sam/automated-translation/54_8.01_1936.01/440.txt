Love and the Catapult (and 2 others)
						Pyŏnsŏkch'on Kim Kirim
(1) Love and the Catapult

The moment I realized it was love,
I hurriedly assumed a posture to flee.

The impatient catapult
cannot linger in the vicinity of love.

This worn-out "Victorian" "raincoat"–
I must quickly take it off. Yes, I must.

(2) A Certain Love

I learned another pointless habit.<292>
I threw it away along with the burnt-out cigarette butt.

That woman must be a cool comet somewhere today.

(3) Congratulatory Telegram

May the wedding ceremony be as splendid as a wedding ceremony can be.

Now the world can rest assured.
Until the day they realize that what they bought by giving up freedom was too expensive,
the two, grimacing at each other,
will lick the skin of bitter happiness...

Carrying hope wrapped in white and black grave clothes (襚衣),
after the flower car departed,
everyone quite respectfully performed the deepest bow.<293>

**Summary**

These three short poems by Kim Kirim explore themes of love, fleeting relationships, and marriage with a markedly cynical and modernist tone. The first poem depicts an immediate impulse to escape love, comparing it to an impatient catapult. The second reflects on a past, discarded relationship likened to a finished cigarette, with the woman now distant like a comet. The third offers a sarcastic "congratulation" on a marriage, predicting future bitterness and regret over lost freedom, framing the wedding procession as akin to a funeral. For historians, these poems offer insight into the modernist literary trends in colonial Korea, reflecting disillusionment and a critical perspective on traditional institutions like marriage and romantic love during a period of significant social change. Kim Kirim's imagery and detached tone are characteristic of the intellectual currents of the time.

戀愛와 彈石機(외 2편)
						片石村 金起林
(1) 戀愛와 彈石機

戀愛인 것을 깨닷자 마자
나는 慌忙히 달아날 姿勢를 갖인다.

性急한 彈石機는
戀愛의 附近에서 멍서릴 수 없다.

이 해여진 『빅토-리안』의 『레인코-트』는
어서 버서버려야지. 그래야지.

(2) 어떤 戀愛

실없은 習慣을 또 배웟지.<292>
다 타버린 담배 꼭지와 함게 비앗아 버렷다.

그 女子는 오늘 어디선가 씨원한 慧星일게다.

(3) 祝電

結婚式은 結婚式처름 기껏 華麗하려므나.

인제 세상은 安心해도 좋을 게다.
自由를 주고 산 것이 너무 비싼 것을 깨닷는 날까지
두 사람은 서로 얼골을 찡그려가며
쓰디쓴 幸福의 거죽을 할틀게다...

히고 검은 襚衣에 쌓인 希望을 실고
꽃自動車가 떠나간 뒤에서
一同은 자못 정중하게 最敬禮를 하였다.<293>
