**Summary**

This article from the January 1, 1936 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine is a photo news feature highlighting key figures and events at the start of the new year. It presents images of Japanese officials involved with international diplomacy (Naval Disarmament Conference) and domestic politics (Diet opening), alongside prominent Koreans leading major newspapers (Chosŏn Ilbo, Chungang Ilbo, Tong-a Ilbo), religious organizations (YMCA, Ch'ŏndogyo), a major business (Hwasin Department Store), and popular singers. Captions convey messages of hope, plans for institutional growth (literacy campaigns, building expansions), social contribution, and cultural vibrancy.
For historians, this piece offers a valuable glimpse into the public activities and aspirations of the Korean elite under Japanese rule. It showcases their engagement in media, business, religion, and culture, illustrating how Korean civil society functioned and presented itself within the colonial framework. The juxtaposition with Japanese official activities subtly underscores the political context, while the features on cultural figures like singers provide insight into popular entertainment of the 1930s in Korea.
