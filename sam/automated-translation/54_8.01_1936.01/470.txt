At the Han River's Edge
						Kim An-sŏ


Beyond the River (江外) a shadow, lonely I stand.
The water flows, the water flows, just flows,
Time goes by, time goes by, just goes by
Life is short and Hope is large, Hope laments, sighs.

Water goes and time goes, it brightened, it darkened, the day changes
People are born, people die, people too are born and die.
All wither, all flow, the water sings and time laughs.
Lonely I stand on the River's edge where nothing returns,
Vainly casting a shadow upon the water.<304>


Splash, splash, the waves break the bank,
The sand is washed away, the bank is eroded.
Even if this heart of mine isn't the sand of the Han River's edge,
It has all been eroded by the coming and going waves of the World.


My lonely Mind (心思) feels so stifled with no way to fill it
I came to the Han River I’ve long, long drawn in my mind,
But only the vast water bends and bends, flowing down.
With no way to fill this Mind as it was,
Alone, I gaze far away at just the edge of the sky.<305>

**Summary**

The poem expresses the speaker's profound sense of loneliness and existential contemplation while standing by the Han River. Observing the relentless flow of the river and the passage of time, the speaker reflects on the shortness of human life, the cycle of birth and death, and the erosion of the self by worldly experiences, comparing their heart to the riverbank worn away by waves. Unable to find solace, the speaker gazes into the distance, feeling small and unfulfilled against the backdrop of nature's indifference. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, offers a glimpse into personal feelings of melancholy and perhaps quiet despair. While not explicitly political, the use of the Han River—a significant national symbol—as the setting for such introspection could be seen by historians as a subtle reflection of the era's atmosphere, where personal struggles intertwine with the larger national context.

漢江까에서
						金岸曙


江外에 그림자가, 외로이 나는 섯노라.
물은 흘은다, 물은 흘은다, 그저 흘은다,
세월은 간다 세월은 간다, 그저 간다
목숨은 잘고 希望은 크다, 希望은 탄식하다, 한숨쉰다.

물은 가고 때는 간다, 밝앗다 어두웟다 날이 바뀐다
사람은 낫다 사람은 죽는다, 사람도 나고 죽고.
모다 예윈다, 모다 흘은다, 물은 노래하고 세월은 웃는다.
하나도 돌오지 안는 江까에 외로이 나는 섯노라,
한갓되이 물 우에 그림자 어리우고.<304>



찰래찰래 물결은 기슭을 부십니다,
모래는 씻깁니다, 기슭은 패웁니다.
이내 맘 漢江까의 모래는 아니라 해도,
드나는 世上물에 모도다 패웟습니다.



외론 心思 채울 길이 하도 답답해
두고두고 그려오는 漢江왓드니,
펄한 물만 구비구비 흘러내릴 뿐.
그러하든 이 心思는 채울 길 업서
혼자로서 저멀니 하눌 끝만 바라봅니다.<305>
