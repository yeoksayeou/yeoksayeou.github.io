Publication Date: 1936년 01월 01일
Article Title: 履聲滿街 (Shoe Sounds Filling the Street)
Author: 卞榮魯
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

履聲滿街
						卞榮魯

Hweong—hani, the empty street
The street, all asleep and quiet
On the night street where even the streetlights (街燈) cannot endure their drowsiness
Arm in arm with each other
Surprisingly, walking thoughtlessly
Tudaktaktak, the sound of our shoes
The quietness of the sleeping street
Shattering it like a dish
Wishing this road would not end,
Before long, home draws near
And the romancing of the night street too
Disappears like joy.<272>

**Summary**

The poem "Shoe Sounds Filling the Street" by Pyŏn Yŏngno depicts a quiet walk taken by two people arm-in-arm through deserted streets late at night. The speaker cherishes the intimacy of the moment, marked only by the sound of their footsteps shattering the silence, and wishes the walk would never end. However, the pleasant "romancing" fades as they inevitably approach home. From a historical perspective, this poem offers insight into personal lyricism during the Japanese colonial period. Amidst broader national struggles, it focuses on intimate, fleeting moments of human connection and quiet joy, perhaps representing a personal refuge or a different facet of life under occupation, expressed using the distinct orthography and vocabulary of the time.

어느듯 집은 갓가워지어
밤거리의 로만싱도
깃븜같이 사라지다.<272>
