New Year's Three New Wishes

This year, may the crops come to this land, so that not one of our compatriots goes hungry or poorly clothed, and may they live well.

This year, may many sons and daughters who will become great people and geniuses emerge one after another from all the homes of our compatriots.

This year, may light and strength descend into the hearts of all compatriots in this land, and may peace and happiness (辛福) come to all society in this land.<25>

**Summary**

The article expresses three earnest wishes for the new year (1936) in Korea: agricultural abundance ensuring no one suffers from lack or poverty, the emergence of future great leaders and geniuses from Korean families, and the bestowal of inner light, strength, peace, and happiness upon all individuals and society. From a historical perspective, these wishes reflect the deep aspirations of Koreans under colonial rule for basic well-being, national revitalization through future generations, and spiritual/social harmony amidst challenging circumstances. Historians might find the emphasis on collective well-being, future talent, and inner strength particularly indicative of national sentiment and coping mechanisms during that era. The use of "辛福" instead of the standard "幸福" (happiness) is also notable, possibly a typo or a specific nuance.

새해의 세 新願
올에, 이 땅에 農作이 와서 모든 同胞 한분도 헐벗지 말고 잘 살게 해주소서.
올에, 同胞의 모든 家庭에 장차 偉人과 天才가 될 아드님과 따님들이 많이 나와서 줄줄히 벗게 해주소서.
올에, 이 땅 모든 同胞의 가슴에 빛과 힘이 내리고, 이 땅 모든 社會에 平和와 辛福이 와주게 해주소서.<25>
