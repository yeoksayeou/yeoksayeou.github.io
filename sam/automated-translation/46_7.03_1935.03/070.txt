**Body Text Translation:**
Recent Activities of Figures, Czechoslovakia President Masaryk

Dr. Tomáš G. Masaryk (도-마스, 지. 마사릿 博士) was born in 1850 in Hodonín (호도-닌), Moravia (모라바이아), as the son of an indispensable person [likely meaning a serf or lower-class worker, "헐 수 업는 이"]. Initially, he became an apprentice blacksmith (鍛冶屋의 徒第), but later, making a determined effort (發奮), he entered university and graduated by supporting himself through hard work (苦學). In 1882, he was invited to be a professor at Prague (뿌라-구) University, and in '91, he secured a seat (議席을 點得) in the Austrian (오-스도리아) Parliament and fought hard for the cause (同樣 때문에 奮鬪하엿다). Becoming the leader (首領) of the Czech Party (첵고黨), with the establishment of the Republic of Czechoslovakia (지엣고, 스로부아가 공화국) after the World War, he was recommended by the National Assembly (國民議會) as the first president (초대 대통령). From an indispensable blacksmith [apprentice] (헐 길 업는 鍛冶僧) to the president of a newly established republic. Everyone cannot help but admire (감탄을 마지 안는다) this astonishing success story (立身出世). In '28, Masaryk (마시릿구) received the Grand Cordon (大勳位) for his meritorious service (功) in striving (盡力) for friendly relations between Czechoslovakia and Japan (지엣고, 스로부아가 양국 친선).<61>

**Summary**

The article briefly outlines the life of Tomáš G. Masaryk, the first president of Czechoslovakia. It highlights his humble origins as the son of a poor worker and his early apprenticeship as a blacksmith before pursuing higher education through self-support. It notes his academic career, entry into politics in the Austrian Parliament, leadership of the Czech Party, and eventual election as president following World War I and the founding of Czechoslovakia. The article emphasizes his remarkable rise from poverty to the presidency and mentions his receiving of the Grand Cordon from Japan in 1928 for promoting friendly relations between the two countries. For historians studying colonial Korea, the article is interesting for its focus on a 'rags-to-riches' national leader narrative, potentially serving as inspiration or comparison within the colonial context. The mention of the Japanese award (Grand Cordon) to a European leader for diplomatic efforts also subtly reflects Japan's position and activities on the international stage during this period, which indirectly relates to its imperial ambitions and administration of Korea.

最近人物動靜, 지엑고스로부아기아 大統領 마사릿구
도-마스, 지. 마사릿 博士는 1850년 모라바이아의 호도-닌에 엇던 헐 수 업는 이의 아들노 태워낫고 처음에는 鍛冶屋의 徒第가 되엿든 바 후에 發奮하야 하야 大學에 입학하야 고학으로 졸업하엿다. 1882년 뿌라-구 대학교수로 초빙되엿고 91년 오-스도리아 議會에 議席을 點得하고 同樣 때문에 奮鬪하엿다. 첵고黨 首領이 되여서 세계대전 후 지엣고, 스로부아가 공화국 성립되면서 國民議會에 의하야 초대 대통령에 推戴되엿다. 헐 길 업는 鍛冶僧으로 신건설 공화국의 대통령. 이 경탄할 만한 立身出世에는 누구나 감탄을 마지 안는다. 마시릿구는 28년에 지엣고, 스로부아가 양국 친선에 盡力한 功으로 大勳位을 바덧다<61>
