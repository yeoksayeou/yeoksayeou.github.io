大盜傳 (The Story of a Great Bandit)
						尹白南 (Yun Paengnam)
Previous Installment Four

"Father, Father, this son will surely avenge you!"
Muryong repeated this several times, addressing the empty air as if speaking to a living person. The more he thought about it, the more unknowable human destiny seemed. His own life might last only until tomorrow. Why? Because even if he succeeded in his objective tonight and avenged his father, while it might be different for another person, Maeng Hak was the leader of the mountain stronghold (산채); his many subordinates would surely not remain still. It was unbelievable that they would simply stand by, out of loyalty as subordinates, seeing their leader killed by another's hand. He would inevitably be branded a traitor and face the swords of his comrades. Should he then fight his innocent colleagues as enemies...
"However," Muryong reconsidered. Depending on the circumstances and details, there might be no other way. If he explained the intricate truth of the matter to them and they still couldn't understand, misinterpreted him, and attacked, then that would be the end. Until these arms were broken and his eyes blinded, he would cut down anyone who attacked him like demons, cut and cut until bodies formed a mound, and upon that mound, he would have no choice but to kill himself.
Thinking this, Muryong swept every corner of his room clean. After neatly organizing his few belongings and clothes, he took all the scraps of paper and clutter outside and burned them. It was because he hated the thought of being criticized as lazy after his death, whether he died by the leader's sword in return or unfortunately perished after fighting his comrades.
That night was the fourteenth of the lunar month. As the night deepened, the air grew clearer,<203> and the pale moonlight became increasingly bright and luminous.
What was most fortunate for Muryong that day was that more than half of the stronghold's subordinates, taking advantage of the season when the sorghum (高粱) grew thick, had gone out to seek work wherever they pleased, leaving only the leader Maeng Hak and a few others in the stronghold.
Midnight passed. In the already quiet mountains, all surroundings fell into a deathly silence, without even the sound of a breeze. Even the trees and plants seemed asleep, and the flowing water seemed to have stopped its course...
Muryong, his body trembling with courage, gathered his spirit, wrapped a sharp dagger in a towel, tucked it into his bosom, and silently emerged from under the eaves of his hut. Killing the sound of his footsteps, he walked towards the leader's hut.
That night, Pak Tal (朴達) awoke in the middle of the night. He couldn't get back to sleep, thinking restlessly about Nan-yŏng, Muryong, and himself. Just then, he heard the footsteps of someone walking cautiously below his hut. At first, startled by the strange sound of footsteps, he pricked up his ears. But the next moment, realizing it was definitely human footsteps, he quietly got up, gently lifted a corner of the reed blind (簾) hanging on the high window, and looked outside.
Outside, in stark contrast to the inside of the room, the bright moon shone like daylight, illuminating everything.
Muryong, unaware that someone was watching him from behind, walked on, staring intently ahead.
The moment Pak Tal recognized it was Muryong, he couldn't suppress the uncontrollably surging flames of jealousy. He interpreted Muryong's stealthy walk towards the leader's hut at this hour as undoubtedly an attempt to secretly meet Nan-yŏng.
As much as he usually detested Muryong, jealousy poured fire into his head.
Instinctively, Pak Tal took the sword hanging on the wall, held it, quietly opened the door, and stepped out. He carefully followed Muryong from behind.
Confronting Muryong immediately wouldn't have been difficult, but he knew that without definite proof that Muryong was heading straight for the leader's hut, his questioning would lack force. So, holding his breath, he followed for some distance.
After passing several sparsely standing huts, when only the leader's hut remained on the path ahead, Pak Tal called out in a low but firm voice,
"Muryong!"
<204>
Muryong, walking heedlessly, felt a chill run down his spine.
He stopped walking and stood stock-still. Thinking he might have misheard, he strained his ears again.
"Muryong!"
At the second call, he spun around.
"Who is it?"
He demanded quickly.
Pak Tal didn't answer but walked towards Muryong's side.
"Ah, it's you, Brother Pak Tal."
"Where are you going at this time of night?"
Caught off guard, Muryong couldn't answer immediately.
"Just because it's night doesn't mean you should do things others would laugh at."
"What?"
"If you must go, go openly in broad daylight. Sneaking around like a cat through the back door at night isn't something a man should do."
"What are you talking about? Sneaking through the back door?"
Muryong took a step closer.
"The more you talk nonsense, the more you lose face... Aren't you just following the scent of Nan-yŏng? If not, why don't you say so?"
"Whether I meet Nan-yŏng or not, what business is it of yours? You should be comfortably lying down sleeping. Why are you strangely stepping out and sneering? It's rather strange."
"You brat! Do you think words are everything? Just because I spoke kindly to you as a comrade, instead of listening properly, you act like such an arrogant fellow... Honestly, having the likes of you around makes my food indigestible. You'd better turn back, or I'll break your legs."
"You brat? Alright, your way of speaking is fine. Just because I addressed you respectfully as 'Brother' due to your age, have you become completely blind? From now on, I won't listen to you."
"You damn beggar!"
Pak Tal suddenly grabbed Muryong by the collar.
With things having come to this, Muryong couldn't just endure it either. Moreover, his usual resentment surged anew, and with immense hatred, his eyes flashing, he slapped Pak Tal across the cheek.
Struck by the lightning-fast hand, Pak Tal, insulted by someone subordinate to him, completely lost his composure in extreme anger. Being insulted by a subordinate, struck first by a rival in love (戀敵), and hit by someone he hated daily – this piled up multiple layers of fury.
"Ah! Is there such a murderous wretch?"
<205>
With this curse, Pak Tal, eyes blazing, grabbed Muryong by the throat.
The curtain rose on their fight.
Under the pale moonlight, the two masses of flesh became one tangled heap and fell to the ground.
The two men wrestled, grappling and panting, fighting with all their might.
Perhaps because they were ashamed of others finding out, they only traded blows without a single word.
While Muryong didn't harbor such deep resentment, Pak Tal's grudge against Muryong was neither simple nor shallow.
Thus, having always wanted a chance to beat Muryong severely enough to cripple him somewhere, he seized the opportunity when Muryong was pinned beneath him on the ground. Pouring all his body's strength into his arms, he pressed down on Muryong's throat, intending perhaps to make him faint.
However, warm blood flowing from a wound on the pressing Pak Tal's face dripped onto Muryong's face.
Muryong was suffocating, nearing the point of losing consciousness. Realizing the danger to his life, almost instinctively, he pulled out the dagger hidden in his bosom. Aiming at Pak Tal's waist, he thrust it in as if to bury it up to the hilt.
Pak Tal cried out, "Ugh!" and fell backward. Muryong, getting up, rushed towards Pak Tal, who was writhing in unbearable pain, and stabbed him in the chest. Pak Tal, seeming to grasp at the air with both hands, let out a final groan and went limp.
Everything ended in an instant, in silence.
Muryong stood for a moment under the moonlight, holding the blood-dripping dagger, his mind blank. Although it happened in the heat of the moment, things turning out this way was unexpected. Yet, he couldn't suppress the exhilarating feeling of having punished a wicked person with the sword of justice. From somewhere, the mournful hooting of an owl could be heard...
Muryong, as if waking from a dream, collected himself, flicked the blood off the knife a couple of times, and hurried towards the leader's hut. Muryong's excited mind became even more unbalanced after seeing human blood.
Muryong reached the door of the leader's hut, hesitated briefly, but finally opened the door without pause and entered. A dim lamplight illuminated the surroundings, centered on the leader's bed.
<206>
Maeng Hak, unaware that the enemy seeking his life was imminent, was fast asleep, snoring loudly.
Muryong, dagger in one hand, stood looking down at Maeng Hak's face for a moment. Knowing Hak's extraordinary strength (비력), the thought occurred to him to take advantage of his deep sleep and stab him in the heart with a single stroke, giving him no chance to resist. But the next moment, he realized that doing so would be incomparably cowardly, and it was more proper to let the person he was about to kill know why he was being killed.
So, he approached the bedside and kicked Hak's pillow with his foot. No matter how deep the leader's sleep, how could he not wake up from having his pillow kicked? Maeng Hak startled awake.
At the same time, seeing Muryong, pale-faced, standing before him with a dagger, his heart leaped, and his whole body seemed to freeze.
But the next instant, truly like someone shocked by electricity, he shot up into a sitting position on the bed. In that instant, Muryong shouted,
"Vengeance for my father! Take my sword!"
And thrust the dagger towards the leader's neck. Muryong's knife struck empty air. Pulled by his own momentum, Muryong's body collided with Maeng Hak's bed.
Maeng Hak, in a flash, swiftly turned his body to evade Muryong's knife, simultaneously struck Muryong's oncoming arm hard with his fist, leaped off the bed, and shoved Muryong's chest with both arms.
Muryong, unable to regain his balance from the forward momentum, was struck in the chest by the leader's powerful fist. Having no resistance, he staggered backward, dropping the dagger he held in his hand somewhere along the way.
Hak leaped off the bed, picked up the dropped dagger, and, like a wild boar struck by lightning, his fierce eyes blazing, rushed at Muryong.
Even though Muryong possessed youthful blood and a body strong and resilient as steel, his strength was ultimately no match for Maeng Hak, who had weathered countless life-and-death crises amidst thousands of troops. However, the single-minded determination to avenge his parents gave him strength and courage beyond his usual capacity. Thus, aiming for Maeng Hak's chest, he charged like a bullet. And so, a great, silent struggle erupted between the two. At this time, Nan-yŏng, sleeping in the next room, drowsily opened her eyes. Simultaneously, she clearly recognized whose fight the sounds of struggle coming from her father's room belonged to. So, she quickly
<207>
got up and peered through a gap in the curtain separating the rooms.
Bright red blood flowed profusely from Muryong's arm, instantly staining the leader's clothes. And somehow, the dagger was back in Muryong's hand.
Muryong stabbed the leader's shoulder a couple of times. The two wounded men, using all their dying strength, wrestled, tumbling over and over, becoming one struggling mass.
Ah, what a gruesome fight! Nan-yŏng covered her eyes with both hands.
It was a terrifying sight that a woman's eyes could hardly bear to watch directly. Muryong, summoning his last ounce of strength, headbutted the leader in the chest. Maeng Hak staggered back several steps, barely managing to prop himself up against the bed. At the same time, his hand accidentally grasped the water pitcher (자리끼 그릇) by the bedside. The leader picked up the pitcher and threw it, aiming at the charging Muryong. Muryong, having no time to dodge, was struck squarely in the face. With a cry of "Iku!" he fainted on the spot.
Meanwhile, Chief Maeng grabbed the long sword hanging on the wall and took a step towards Muryong.
Alas, Muryong's life now hung like a candle flame in the wind.
It was at this moment. At this very moment, someone dashed into the room like a swift bird (飛鳥) and stood protectively before the leader's eyes.
It was, of course, Nan-yŏng. Nan-yŏng clung to her slightly startled and hesitant father's chest.
"Father!"
"What is this? Ugh, get away!"
"No, Father, please calm down for just a moment. Father, blood is flowing like this from your shoulder wound, what are you going to do? If this continues, Father, you will die from this wound."
"Move aside! Move! I must kill that man!"
"Father, if you intend to kill him, please kill me along with him. Father, you are his enemy. His parents were the Korean couple (고렷사람의 내외) who died at your hands in Oho-gye (오호게) twenty years ago."
"What?"
"The deceased Old Man Kwŏn (권로인) picked him up and raised him. Father, please don't just call him the one to be killed. As a human child, if one doesn't avenge their parents, how can they stand among people?"
Upon hearing Nan-yŏng's words, Wŏnhak finally understood
<208>
the meaning behind Muryong's shout as he charged, "Vengeance for my father! Die by my hand!" Simultaneously, the terrifying reality of karmic retribution (因果應報) sent shivers down his spine. The arms he had raised dropped limply. Blood flowed ceaselessly from the shoulder wound, and as the tension drained from his mind, the pain he had forgotten until now felt as if it were boring into his bones.
Hak pushed Nan-yŏng, clinging to his chest, aside and collapsed sitting on the spot, as if falling.
Muryong regained consciousness. Having momentarily lost his senses after being struck squarely in the face by the brass pitcher, Muryong collected himself again and was resolved to enter the second round of fighting when he unexpectedly saw Nan-yŏng. Seeing her words and the leader's sudden change in attitude, his attacking spirit died down as well.
Muryong could only stare blankly at the actions of the leader and Nan-yŏng.
Maeng Hak, who had slumped down like a collapsing pillar, seemed to catch his short breath for a moment.
"Muryong,"
he called in a hoarse voice.
"If you are indeed that man's son, then seeking revenge is certainly the right thing to do. A commendable thought... Kill me... I have received a wound from which I likely won't survive anyway, and even if I did live, how many more years would I have? But Muryong, I regard you as a true man, and I have one last request... Even if I am your enemy, what fault does this Nan-yŏng have? Please take care of her, so that even if I die right here, I will have no regrets... Hurry, kill me quickly."
Maeng Hak barely managed to speak these words with his failing breath and dry throat. Afterward, he closed his eyes, clenched his teeth, and frowned, trying to endure the pain. From his tightly shut eyes, a single tear streamed down his dark, bruised cheeks.
Although Nan-yŏng had spoken firmly, seeing her own father's agony firsthand, she cast aside cold reason (理性) and wept emotionally. Maeng Hak, being the man he was, possessed a rather warrior-like (무사다운), tragic resolve. Moreover, considering the leader's intention to entrust his daughter's entire life to Muryong, believing in his character despite him being the enemy seeking his life, Muryong, who was naturally prone to emotion and soft-hearted, suddenly felt his eyes well up, and tears flowed.
It was true he had been excited by the hope of fulfilling his duty as a son by avenging his father. But now, taking a step back and thinking again, he couldn't help but feel <209> that he personally held no grudge against the leader. He couldn't deny that a large part of his determination to avenge his father stemmed from a sense of obligation towards societal expectations (체모).
No matter how much he was his parents' enemy, killing the leader who had shown him kindness for decades seemed too heartless and cruel. Ah, I thought wrongly. I should forgive his sin and merely curse my own harsh fate. Thinking this way, the leader's final words pierced his heart even more.
"Leader, please forgive me. This is already sufficient revenge for me. If you can forgive me, a subordinate who raised a sword against his leader, I will ensure your daughter Nan-yŏng is presented admirably to the world. I intend to spend my life with her. Please set your mind at ease about that."
"Oh—hearing just those words brings me satisfaction."
The leader managed to say. A smile of relief (安堵) even appeared on his face, which grew paler by the moment.
However, due to the deep wound and the massive loss of blood, his life gradually faded like a lamp running out of oil.
A fresh wave of sorrow surged in Nan-yŏng's heart, and shaking her father's body, she began to weep aloud,
"Father! Father!"
Maeng Hak seemed about to say something, opening his mouth, but his throat was choked, and no words came out. His mouth twitched in a violent convulsion.
The end had come... Maeng Hak, possessor of a harsh fate who had experienced only hardship for over 60 years, met his end abruptly amidst the turmoil of a single night.
While daughter Nan-yŏng's grief was beyond words, Muryong, although he hadn't personally delivered the final blow, ultimately felt responsible for his death. The pangs of conscience outweighed the triumphant feeling (동쾌한 늣김) of having avenged his parents many times over, tearing at his heart.<210>

**Summary**

Muryong, a young member of a mountain bandit group, plots to kill the leader, Maeng Hak, to avenge his parents' deaths from twenty years prior. His plan is interrupted by Pak Tal, a jealous rival for the affection of Maeng Hak's daughter, Nan-yŏng. Muryong kills Pak Tal in self-defense. He then confronts Maeng Hak, and they engage in a brutal fight. Nan-yŏng intervenes, revealing Muryong's identity and motive – Maeng Hak killed Muryong's Korean refugee parents. The wounded Maeng Hak, acknowledging the karmic justice, asks Muryong to care for Nan-yŏng before dying. Muryong agrees but is overcome with guilt rather than satisfaction. From a historical perspective, the story touches upon themes relevant to colonial Korea: the displacement and vulnerability of Koreans (referred to as "고렷사람," an older term for Koreans) potentially in border regions like Manchuria where banditry was rife, the complex loyalties and violence within marginalized groups, and themes of revenge and fate against a backdrop of hardship. The specific mention of the parents being killed twenty years before 1935 places their deaths around 1915, a period of continued consolidation of Japanese rule and potential instability in peripheral areas.

前篇四

「아버지 아버지의 원수는 긔어코 이놈이 갑하들이겟음니다」
무룡은 산사람에게 대해서 말하듯이 허공을 대해서 몃번이나 되풀이하며 이러케 말햇다. 생각하면 생각해 볼스록 사람의 숙명이란 알 수 업는 것이다. 자긔의 목숨도 길대야 래일 하로 박게 더 업슬것도 갓다. 왜그러냐하면 오늘밤에 다행이 목적을 달하야 아버지의 원수를 갑는다 할지라도 그것이 다른 사람이고 보면 모르거니와 산채의 두목이고보니 여러 부하들이 가만이 잇슬리는 만무하다. 소위 두목이 남의 손에 걸려 죽는 것을 보고 부하된 의리에 그냥 두리라고는 미들 수 업는 일이다. 반듯이 반역자라는 이름으로 여러 동무들의 칼을 아니 밧지못하게 될 것이니 그러타고 아모 허물업는 동료들을 적으로 삼아 싸와야 할 것인가...
「그러치만」하고 무룡은 다시 돌려 생각해 보앗다. 그것도 사세와 경위을 딸하서는 어쩌는 수 업다. 그들에게 사리의 위곡(委曲)을 말해 들려주어서 그래도 그들이 알아듯지 못하고 나를 곡해하고 달려든다면 그때는 마지막이다. 이 팔뚝이 부러지고 눈이 멀기까지는 악귀라 찰가티 닷는대로 버이고 버이고 버여서 시체로 무덕이을 지으고 그 무덕이 우에서 나도 자결해 죽는것 밧게 더 할길이 업다.
무룡은 이러케 생각하고 자긔방안을 구석구석이 정결하게 쓸엇다. 그러고 여간 즙물과 옷을  말끔이 정돈한 후에 휴지조각과 허잡씨러기는 모도 밧그로 내다가 태어버렷다. 두목의 칼에 돌이여 마저 죽거나 또는 동료들과 싸운끄테 불행이 죽는다면 죽은 뒤일지라도 게으른 몸이드란 욕을 밧기 실흔 생각으로 그리한 것이엇다.
그날은 열사흔날 밤이엇다. 밤이 깁허 갈사록 공긔는 맑아가고<203> 창백한 달빛이 갈스록 창창하게 밝앗다.
이날 무룡에게 대해서 가장 형편에 조핫든 것은 산채의 부하 반수 이상이 고량(高粱)이 무성한 절긔를 타서 제각금 맘먹은 곳으로 일거리를 차저서 출타하야업고 산채에는 다만 무목이하 몃몃사람만 남아 잇든일이다.
자정때가 넘엇다. 워낙 고요한 산중이라 사면은 바람한점의 소리업시 죽은 듯한 적막에 빠지고 말앗다. 초목도 잠을 자고 흐르는 물가지 걸음을 멈춘듯이...
무룡은 용긔에 떨이는 몸에 힘을 주어서 정신을 가다듬으면서 날카로운 단검을 수건에 말아서 픔에너코 초믹밋으로 소리업시나섯다. 그래서 발자국소리를 죽여가며 두목의 초막으로 걸어갓다.

원학은 란영의 말에 비로소
「아버지의 원수다. 내 손에 죽어라.」
하고 부르지즈며 달려들든 무룡의 말뜻을 알게되엇다.<208> 동시에 그는 인과응보(因果應報)의 두려운 현실에 전신에 솔음이 끼첫다. 그러고 들엇든 두팔은 힘업시 축 늘어젓다. 엇개의 상처로서는 쉬일 사이업시 피가 흘러나리고 긴장햇든 마음이 탁 풀어지는 동시에 이쨋껏 이젓든 아픔이 뼈로 조이듯이 늣겨건다.
학은 가슴에 매달린 란영을 겨트로 밀어내고 그 자리에 쓸어지듯 안젓다.
무룡은 다시 청신을 수습햇다. 어마지두에 놋그릇으로 얼굴청통을 들어맛고 일시 정신을 일헛든 무룡은 다시 정신을 차리고 제이차에 격투로 드러갈 결심을 하든 차에 그는 뜻밧게 란영을 보게되고 또 그의 말과 두목의 돌변한 태도를 보게되매 그 역시 공격의 심의가 죽어버리엇다.
무룡은 물끄럼이 두목과 란영의 거동을 바로보고 잇는 수밖에 업섯다.
기동이 쓸어지듯 그 자리에 털썩 주저안즌 맹학은 잠시는 갓븐 숨을 돌리는 듯 십헛다.
「무룡아」
하고 쉬인 목소리로 블은다.
「네가 과연 그 사람에 아들이라면 물론 원수를 갑흐려하는 것이 올흔 일이다. 긔특한 생각이다... 나를 죽여라... 나는 어차피 살지못할 상처를 바닷고 또 산다 한들 몃해나 더 살겟느냐. 그러나 무룡아 나는 너를 남자다운 남자로 여기고 단 한가지 마지막으로 부탁할 일이 잇다... 나는 네게 원수가 된다 할지라도 이 이란영이야 무슨 죄가 잇늬. 이것만은 네가 마타서 내가 이 자리에서 죽드라도 한이업게 해다고... 어 얼핏 날 죽여다우」
맹학은 긔진하야 가는 숨과 말라가는 목으로 간신히 여긔까지 말하고 그 뒤는 눈을 감고 이를 악물고 고통을 참려고 눈살을 찝흐렷다. 꽉감은 두 눈초리에서는 한줄기의 눈물이 검푸른 두뺨에 흘러나렷다.
란영은 입으로는 비록 굿센 말은 하얏을지라도 친아버지의 비통을 목전에 보게되매 차듸찬 리성(理性)을 던저 파뭇고 늣겨 울엇다. 맹학은 그의 근지가 근지이니만치 제법 무사(武士)다운 비장한 결심이 잇섯든 것이다. 더구나 자긔를 원수로 여기고 목숨을 빼으려고하는 적(敵)인 것도 불고하고 그의 인격을 밋고 딸자식의 일생을 부탁하려는 두목의 심의를 생각할때에 워낙 정에 격하기 쉽고 눈이 여린 무룡은 금시에 눈ㅅ자위가 뜻거워지며 눈물이 솟아 흘럿다.
아버지의 원수를  갑하서 사람의 자식된 본의를 직키려는 희망에 흥분되엇든 것은 사실이다. 그러나 이제 다시 한거름 물러서서 생각할 때에 그는 자긔 자신으로는 직접<209> 두목에게 아모런 원한이 업는 것을 늣기지 안흘 수 업섯다. 아버지의 원수를 갑는다는 결심가운대에는 세상에 체모를 생각하야서 그리 아니 할 수 업다는 마음이 만흔 부분을 점*한 것도 사실이 아니라고 할 수 업섯다.
아무리 부모의 원수라 할지라도 수십년ㅅ동안 은덕을 입어 나려온 두목을 죽인다는 것은 넘우나 무정하고 랭혹한 일이 아닐가. 아아 나는 글흣 생각하얏다. 그의 죄를 용서하고 다만 자긔의 기구한 운명을 저주할 따름으로 그칠것이엇다. 이러케 돌리어 생각하고보니 두목의 최후에 말이 더욱 가슴을 찔리엇다.
「두목 날 용서해 주십시요. 나는 이만해도 충분이 원수를 갑흔 것이 됏음니다. 부하된 몸으로 두목의게 칼질을 하게 된 나를 용서해 주신다면 나는 따님 란영을 훌융히 이 세상에 내노켓음니다. 평생을 가티하려고 함니다. 그걸랑은 마음을 노십시요」
「오-그 말만 들으면 나는 만족일다」
두목은 간신이 말을 햇다. 그러고 시시각각으로 창백하야가는 얼굴에 안도(安堵)의 미소까지 띄엇다.
그러나 깁흔 상처와 어마하게 흘러나온 피로하야 그의 생명은 점점 기름이 다한 등ㅅ불가티 기울어저 들어갓다.
란영은 새삼스런 슬픔이 가슴에 치밀어 올라서 아버지의 몸을 흔들며
「아버지 아버지」
하고 목을 노코 울기 시작햇다. 맹학은 무슨 말을 하려는 듯이 입을 버리기는 하얏스나 목이 갈려서 말이 나오진 아니하엿다. 격렬한 경련에 입이 실룩어린다.
최후는 왓다... 60여년 동안 구건일만 격거나려 온 긔구한 운명의 소유자 맹학은 하로ㅅ밤 풍파에 어이업시 평생을 마감하고 말앗다.
딸 란영의 슬픔은 이무가론이어니와 무룡은 비록 자긔의 손으로 그의 최후의 목숨을 끈흔 것은 아니로되 결국은 자긔가 죽인것이 되고보니 부모의 원수를 갑핫다는 동쾌한 늣김보다 양심의 가책이 몃배를 더해서 가슴을 우비엇다.<210>
