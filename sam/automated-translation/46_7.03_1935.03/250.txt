Looking Up to Pioneers, Great Elders of History
						An Chaehong, Sŏl T'aehŭi, Yi Tonhwa, Kim Ch'angje, Sin Hŭngu, Hŏ Hŏn, Chŏng In'gwa, Yŏ Unhyŏng, Pang Ŭngmo, Song Chinu, Ch'a Sangch'an, Yi Kwangsu, Pak Ch'anghun

**The Valiant Yi Sunsin**

An Chaehong

Yi Sunsin
Other great points need no further explanation, but among his prudence, meticulousness, intensity, sincerity, brilliance, and valiant courage, the point we must certainly learn is his absolute dedication to responsibility, demonstrating to the utmost limit his ability in all matters, large and small, with all his strength, talent, and sincerity, leaving nothing undone.

**Culturally, Sejong**

Sŏl T'aehŭi

It is difficult to speak about your query. However, as not responding may seem discourteous, I offer a few words below.
Regardless of their current existence or ruin, speaking of contributions made for the nation and people appearing in history, the legacies of Mr. Sŏl Ch'ong's Idu script, King Sejong the Great's Chŏngŭm (Hunminjŏngŭm), Mr. Mun Ikchŏm's cotton planting and weaving machinery, and Mr. Kim Changsaeng's (*Garye Chimnam* - Family Rite Handbook, likely intended; 回禮便覽 seems incorrect) are no small forces that contributed to Chosŏn's culture. And for national defense, there are Messrs. Yang Manch'un, Ŭlchi Mundŏk, and Yi Sunsin.
In political insight, there are Messrs. Hwang Hŭi, Chŏng Tojŏn, Yi I, Chŏng Yagyong, and Pak Chiwŏn. Besides them, there are figures with purpose like Cho Kwangjo, Hong Kyŏngnae, Ch'oe Cheu, and Sŏ Chaep'il (though their methods differed).

**Tonghak's Ch'oe Suun**

Yi Tonhwa

As far as I know, there is none like Mr. Ch'oe Cheu, that is, Mr. Ch'oe Suun. It is a great regret that his spirit is not well understood by the world because he is overshadowed by being a religious founder. His contributions to us are the creative new principle called Tonghak and the call for the peace and stability of the people (安民) through the collective spirit of "Returning to One Body" (同歸一體), which I believe will be an eternal contribution. What we should learn from him are things like becoming new humans with a spirit of rebirth, living united by Returning to One Body, and the incident where he offered his body at the execution ground in Taegu. <153>

**Koryŏ's Chŏng P'oŭn**

Kim Ch'angje

Mr. Chŏng P'oŭn and Admiral Yi Ch'ungmu.
As historical figures of Chosŏn, I revere these two. Their achievements are known to the world, so I trust there is no need to speak of them separately.
Among Westerners, I revere Cavour. He is one of Italy's three outstanding figures.
If there is something to learn from these three, it is loyalty and righteousness (忠義) from beginning to end. A line expressing each of their lives:
1. Though this body dies and dies, dying a hundred times, whether my soul exists or not, my white bones turn to dust, how could this single-minded devotion (一片丹心) towards my lord ever fade? (P'oŭn)
2. The sea pledges, fish and dragons move; the mountains vow, grass and trees know. (盟山草木知) (Ch'ungmu)
3. Italy is my wife I will never another. (Cavour)

**Poland's Kościuszko**

Sin Hŭngu

Mr. Kim Okkyun
Among those who appeared in recent history, I would mention Mr. Kim Okkyun. Generally, pioneers live unfortunate lives and their names shine like the sun and moon after death, but Mr. Kim even suffered the cruel fate at Yanghwajin after passing away.
His unwavering determination (脊脊一念) - there is much we should learn.
Kościuszko.
This man is a pioneer of Poland. In my youth, I avidly read his biography. He is a pioneer I respect and have not forgotten to this day.

**The Great General Yi Sunsin**

Hŏ Hŏn

What words could fully express the respect for that elder whose achievements covered the world (功盖 一世)? Seeing in the newspapers the other day, even in the speech by Admiral Suetsugu, the former Commander-in-Chief of the Combined Fleet, Yi Sunsin was called a great general of the seas (林洋の名将 - likely intended 海洋, meaning 'ocean').

**Israel's Moses**

Chŏng In'gwa

I like Moses the most. He is one who thoroughly carried out the sense of mission received from God, one who never spared himself under any circumstances, and also the creator of laws.

**England's Cromwell**

Yŏ Unhyŏng

England's Cromwell
Italy's Garibaldi
I choose these two. I originally dislike "professionals." I most detest professional politicians and professional soldiers. In this respect, I dislike Napoleon and Hannibal. <154>
However, these two worked truly and sincerely, without thinking of any reward, honor, or anything else. I bow my head to their great character and always respect them.

**The Great Deeds of King Sejong**

Pang Ŭngmo

Among historical figures, I admire Yi Sunsin. There's no need to speak anew about his character and achievements.
And if looking among kings, I would mention someone like Sejong.

**The Saintly Character of Ch'ungmugong**

Song Chinu

The pioneer I fervently love is Admiral Yi Sunsin.
1. His contributions to the state and society are brilliant facts known throughout the world (天下周知), so it seems unnecessary to speak of them anew now.
2. The shining points we should learn from him are his selfless sacrificial spirit for the public good (純公無私) and his magnanimous, saintly character.

**How Great is Admiral Yi Ch'ungmu!**

Ch'a Sangch'an

The elder I most admire and fervently love is Admiral Yi Ch'ungmu. His achievements are already known to the world, so I won't speak of them again, but he is the one who devoted his heart and blood for the nation until the very end, thoroughly and completely. I think that is the point we should most learn.

**The Sole Subject in Five Hundred Years**

Yi Kwangsu

Yi Sunsin
Whether the monarch and government recognized him or not, whether the world reviled him or not, whether there was a chance of victory or not, whether the overall situation had tilted such that there was a sense of a great house collapsing, difficult to support from the east (大厦之傾東難支), he devoted his full strength to his duty, eventually sacrificing his life and rescuing everything. He is the sole great elder of the Yi Dynasty's 500 years.

**The Indomitable Ch'ungmugong**

Pak Ch'anghun

Elder Yi Sunsin is great.
That elder pushed forward with an indomitable spirit (不屈不橈) independently, overcoming all the difficulties disallowed by his surroundings. That point is worthy of looking up to. <155>

**Summary**

This article compiles responses from thirteen prominent Korean figures in 1935 to a survey asking them to name the historical figures they most revere and the reasons why. Yi Sunsin emerges as the most frequently cited figure, praised for his responsibility, patriotism, selflessness, military genius, and perseverance against adversity. Other Korean figures like King Sejong, Ch'oe Cheu (founder of Tonghak), Chŏng Mongju, and Kim Okkyun are also mentioned, alongside international figures such as Moses, Cavour, Kościuszko, Cromwell, and Garibaldi. The respondents highlight qualities like cultural contribution, political insight, loyalty, pioneering spirit, self-sacrifice, and unwavering dedication to a cause or nation.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the overwhelming reverence for Yi Sunsin is highly significant, reflecting a potent symbol of national resistance and integrity during a period of Japanese rule. The inclusion of figures like Ch'oe Cheu and Kim Okkyun points to an interest in indigenous modernization efforts and reformist movements. The selection of specific foreign figures like Kościuszko (Poland), Cavour, and Garibaldi (Italy) – associated with national liberation and unification struggles – subtly mirrors Korea's own aspirations. The diverse choices reveal the intellectual landscape of the time, grappling with national identity, historical legacy, and models for future action under difficult circumstances.

剛勇한 李舜臣

安在鴻

李舜臣
다른 위대한 점은 부연할 것이 업고 그의 謹愼, 周密, 精烈, 眞摯, 英明, 剛勇한 중에도 大小巨細의 온갓 일에 힘것, 재주것, 지성것, 할 수 잇는 최대한의 능력을 남김업시 발휘한 절대한 책임 지상주의가 꼭 배울 점임니다.

文化上으로 世宗

薛泰熙

貴間에 대해서 말하기가 모다 難澁하오이다. 그러나 不答이 恐蹈不恭임으로 左에 略干語를 해듸림니다.
현재 存廢는 不拘하고 역사상에 나타난 국가민족을 위하야 기친 공적으로 말하면 薛聰선생의 吏讀, 世宗大王의 正音 文益漸선생의 種棉製機, 金長선생의 回禮便覽 등 遺業이 조선문화에 기처주신 힘이 적지 안음니다. 그리고 국방에는 楊萬春, 乙支文德, 李舜臣 諸선생이요.
政治 識見에 黃喜, 鄭道傳, 李珥, 丁若鏞, 朴趾源 諸선생이요, 그 박게 뜻잇는 이로 趙光祖, 洪景來, 崔濟愚, 徐載弼 諸賢(但 方法 各異)이외다.

東學道의 崔水雲

李敦化

내가 알기에는 崔濟愚, 즉 崔水雲선생 갓흔 이가 업다함니다. 그는 宗敎敎祖라는 그늘에 뭇처 잇는 까닭으로 그 정신을 세상이 얼마 아라 보지 못하는 것이 큰 유감임니다. 그가 우리들에게 끼친 공적은 東學道라 하는 創造的新主義와 「同歸一體」라는 단체적 정신으로 安民을 부르지즌 것이 영원의 공적이 될 줄로 생각함니다. 우리가 그분에게 배울 점은 再生의 정신으로 新人間이 되라는 것, 同歸一體하야 단합하야 산다는 것, 大邱將臺에서 몸을 밧친 일 등 여러 가지인 줄<153> 암니다.

麗朝의 鄭圃隱

金昶濟

鄭圃隱선생과 李忠武公,
조선의 역사적 인물로는 두 분을 경애합니다. 두 분의 공적은 세상이 다 아는 바이니 별로히 말할 필요가 업슬 줄 밋습니다.
서양인으로는 「캬부어」를 경애함니다. 이는 이태리의 뛰어난 3인중 한 분임니다.
이 세 분에게서 배울 점이라면 곳 시종일관한 忠義올시다. 그들의 일생을 표현하는 말을 한마듸씩 적으면
1. 이 몸이 죽어죽이 일백 번 고처죽어 넉이야 잇고업고 白骨이 塵土되여 님 향한 一片丹心 가실 줄이 잇스랴.(圃隱)
2. 誓海魚龍動, 盟山草木知(忠武公)
3. Italy is my wife I will never anather(이태리는 나의 처라 나는 결코 他를 원치 야니 한다.) (카부어)

波蘭의 고시스꼬

申興雨

金玉均선생
나는 최근 史上에 나타난 분으로는 金玉均 선생을 들겟슴니다. 대개 선구자란 살아서 불우하다가 죽어선 그 일홈이 日月가치 빗날 것이로되 선생은 도라가서까지 梁花津의 慘厄이 잇섯슴니다.
그 분의 脊脊一念-우리는 배울 바가 만타 하옵니다.
고시스꼬.
이 분은 波瀾의 선각자임니다. 나는 소년 시대에 이 분의 전기를 몹시 애독햇슴니다. 지금까지 이처지지 안는 나의 존경하는 선구자임니다.

名將 李舜臣

許憲

功盖 一世하든 그 어룬을 무슨 말로 하면 그 존경하는 뜻을 다 표하겟슴니까. 前日 신문지상에서 보건대 前聯合艦隊司令長官이든 末次 대장의 연설에도 李舜臣을 林洋의 명장이라 하더이다.
