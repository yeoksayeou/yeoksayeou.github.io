Publication Date: 1935년 03월 01일
Article Title: 新女性과 愛情과 貞操觀 (New Women, Love, and Views on Chastity)
Author: 崔貞熙
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Essay)

New Women, Love, and Views on Chastity
						By Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi

I intend to discuss the issue of marital love from the perspective of chastity and, consequently, sexual morality. First, for reference, I will begin with stories arising between married couples in several countries around the world. Although it might seem slightly divergent from the main topic, discussing these allows us to understand their concepts of chastity and sexual morality. So, let me first speak about India. It is said that in India, there are two main religions, Mohammedanism and Hinduism, and their views on the concept of chastity between spouses differ. Specifically, Mohammedanism regards women as very precious, and the husband knows how to love the woman dearly, but Hinduism allegedly certifies women as having no value whatsoever. Among the teachings given to women, it is said there is nothing besides the husband, and whether dead or alive, absolute obedience to the husband and pleasing him constitutes a woman's morality. However, in Mohammedanism, mentioned earlier, it is quite the opposite; wives are said to dominate their husbands and children.<160> What is somewhat inconvenient is that these wives, while knowing how to dominate their husbands, are confined indoors and cannot go outside all day long. It is said that these wives, amidst the ardent affection of men, do not feel this lifestyle to be the least bit restrictive. There is also a frightening story that if either the husband or wife shows even a hint of their affection waning or seeming to seek another partner, they immediately stab them with a concealed knife, drawing bright red blood. Next, I will discuss the issues prevalent between married couples in Turkey. Turkish women, much like Indian women, reportedly did not go out on the streets often, and if they did, they would cover their faces with something. However, after the World War, with 150,000 Russian refugees flocking to Turkey, Turkish women were stimulated by the cheerful behavior of Russian women and underwent a great awakening. Consequently, working women, previously unseen, became numerous, and organizational bodies like women's associations emerged, leading women to adopt much more progressive behaviors.

Accordingly, the previously lax issue of chastity between husband and wife is said to have become quite strict. Turkish women reportedly have the legitimate right to request a divorce without any issue in cases such as when the husband suffers from mental illness for over three years without recovery, fails to provide appropriate love to the wife, or fails to understand the monthly pathological state [menstruation]. However, one pitiful matter is that many men from this country died in the war, resulting in a large number of widows. It wouldn't be an issue if the widows lived alone, but they remarry, leading to situations where three or even four women marry one man. As they all end up living together in one household, you can well imagine the internal strife (암투) that arises without me having to elaborate. Next, let me speak of Russian customs. Russia is a country where women are more liberated than in any other country in the world. Correspondingly, the freedom of marriage, freedom of divorce, and also the concept of chastity are truly at a level that seems to us like they do exactly as they please. Of course, doing so might be the proper and principled way. However, when we see the considerable negative customs (페풍) that sometimes arise from this, it is a matter that requires reconsideration. In Russia, the interference of a third party is considered unnecessary for a man and woman to marry; what is essential is that the two individuals agree and love each other, even if momentarily. When marrying, only a simple procedure at the registration office is needed, and divorce can similarly be done very easily. If either party<161> grows tired of the relationship, they go to the registration office, register it, and notify the partner, completing the divorce procedure. I will stop here regarding the sexual morality or love issues of couples in other countries like America, England, or China, and from now on, I will broadly discuss marital love and sexual morality. However, let me state in advance that I cannot discuss this issue explicitly (로골적으로). I will only speak illustratively (예증적으로). Let me begin first with marital love. Marital love (부부애) is something everyone knows well. Scientifically speaking, it can be divided into two types: one is intrinsic marital love, and the second is spiritual marital love. In any case, it goes without saying that complete marital love cannot be established if either of these two is missing. According to the theories of various scholars, marital love was not equally complete in both aspects—spiritual love and physical love—from the very beginning. That is, it is proven that physical love arose first, followed by the emergence of spiritual love. To prove that this is an immutable fact, let me talk about the marital lives of lower humans—that is, savages (야만인). It has been discovered that savages possess only physical love (육적애), in other words, sexual desire (성욕). However, there is also mention that such examples are not necessarily found only among savages.

Evidence for this includes facts such as feeling romantic attraction towards a beautiful person rather than an unattractive one, ultimately leading to the theory that marital love originates from physical beauty (신체미). Even looking at such arguments, one can surmise that in constituting marital love, the element of physical love outweighs spiritual love. But what makes this physical love play such a necessary role between spouses? It is very awkward to discuss, but if I were to simply cover up even this much, the content might seem too lacking, so let me say a few words. Physical love (육체애), simply put, is sexual love (性的愛). As just discussed, if this plays a vital role in sustaining marital life, it cannot but be considered an issue that should not be treated lightly. It is known that often, marital discord (반목), conflict (충동), and family disharmony largely arise from this problem. Simply adhering to monogamy (一夫一妻制) and strictly observing chastity for the husband cannot define a flawless marriage. Discussing this further feels too explicit (로골화하는 늣김), so I will not elaborate more here; I hope you all will understand. Next is the discussion of spiritual love. Although physical love plays a crucial role<162> in completing marital love, emphasizing only physical love without spiritual love is something we cannot help but consider unfitting for members of today's culturally developed society. Just as physical health, beauty, and graceful movements are necessary, spiritually, couples should always prioritize affection, harmonize their emotions (감정을 융화식히고), and conduct all matters with trust (신임). Since marriage itself came into being to seek happiness, if one has entered married life to obtain happiness, it is a human's proper duty to ensure that misfortune is not brought about. As long as you and I live in reality (헨실), we cannot help but conform to it. Indeed, the theory is plain, but when put into practice, everything tends to go awry, and unless the world is torn down and rebuilt, there seems to be no remedy. If any among you ladies, finding your husband unfaithful and living a dissolute life (방탕한 생활), were to adopt the exact same attitude as your husband, the result would ultimately be nothing but the ruin of your own self, with no gain. Although I lack detailed knowledge of the law (법률) and cannot speak at length, even looking at the current law, marriage aims for the joint life of the couple, thus the wife and husband are mutually obliged to cooperate and preserve the peace and security of their shared life, requiring them to maintain faithfulness towards each other. In other words, above all else, spouses must maintain chastity (정조를 직혀야 된다). However, just because the law, social morality, and customs are so, does not mean you should endure silently despite any suffering in the family or dissatisfaction with your husband. That is to say, if the discord is merely due to temporary emotional issues, clashes of opinion, or misunderstandings arising from small errors, and the discontent lacks deep roots, it's one thing. But if the husband genuinely dislikes the wife and has no intention of spending his life with her, yet she waits pitifully for his heart to turn back, wasting her entire life—there seem to be such people in the world occasionally. For these individuals, separating from each other (서로 갈나지는 것) for the sake of mutual happiness is considered the best path.

And one more thing for mutual caution is that we women have too much jealousy (질투심). Someone said that suppressing jealousy is a beautiful thing, but expressing jealousy can also be beautiful. As that saying goes, there might be times when jealousy becomes beautiful. However, much unhappiness in our homes arises from jealousy. Of course, jealousy itself is a kind of instinct (본능) and is said to necessarily exist innately (先生的) in humankind, but there are often cases where suppressing even this innate instinct is better. It was a story I saw in the newspaper the other day, something that happened in a rural village: a husband, out harvesting, was thirsty and got some water from a woman drawing water. His<163> wife saw this, immediately returned home, grabbed a kitchen knife, went out, and stabbed the woman to death—a horrifying incident. Even hearing such a story, one can realize it clearly proves that women possess much jealousy. Therefore, I mention that this too is not an insignificant issue between spouses. In other words, jealousy arises from the wife not trusting the husband or the husband not trusting the wife. Thus, if the concept of chastity (청조관렴) is strict between them and there is no duplicity (이심), marital jealousy would likely disappear.<164>

**Summary**

This 1935 essay by Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi explores contemporary views on love, marriage, and chastity, particularly concerning the "New Woman." It contrasts practices in India (Hindu vs. Muslim), Turkey (post-WWI changes, polygamy issues), and Russia (liberal marriage/divorce) before delving into the nature of marital love. Ch'oe argues that while both physical (sexual) and spiritual love are crucial for a complete marriage, physical love historically and perhaps biologically came first and remains vital for marital stability, linking its absence to common discord. She cautions against mere adherence to monogamy without genuine connection. While advocating fidelity and warning against female jealousy, she significantly suggests that if a marriage is fundamentally broken with no hope of reconciliation, separation is preferable to enduring lifelong unhappiness, prioritizing individual well-being.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it reflects the ongoing negotiation between traditional Korean values and modern Western/global ideas about gender roles, individual happiness, and sexuality during the colonial period. Written by a prominent female intellectual, it provides insight into the evolving consciousness of Korean women ("New Women") regarding autonomy within marriage. The comparison with other nations, especially the perceived extremes of Russian liberation versus Indian/Turkish tradition, highlights the complex position of colonial Korea. The relatively frank discussion of the importance of sexual compatibility (육체애/성적애) in marriage, even if veiled, is noteworthy for its time and context. Historians might find the blend of traditional moral expectations (chastity, warning against jealousy) with modern considerations (mutual love, importance of sexual fulfillment, the right to divorce for happiness) particularly revealing of the period's social and intellectual currents.

新女性과 愛情과 貞操觀
						崔貞熙
貞操觀에서 본 부부의 애정문제와 따라서 性도덕에 대한 이약이 할 터인대 먼저 참고 삼아서 세계 몃 나라의 부부사이에서 이러나는 이약이 부텀 하겟슴니다. 좀 본 문제에 어그러진 감이 잇을 것갓치 생각되지만은 이것을 이약이하는 중에서 그들의 정조관념이라든지 성도덕에 대한 것을 알 수 잇게 되는 까닭이올시다. 그러면 제일 몬저 印度에서부터 말슴하겟슴니다. 印度에는 모하몃교도와 힌두교의 두가지 교가 잇서서 부부 사이의 정조관념에 대하야서도 다르다고 함니다. 즉 모하몃교에서는 녀성을 아주 존귀한 것으로 알고 그 남편되는 자가  녀자를 한끗 사랑할 줄 알지만은 힌두교에서는 녀자는 아모 가치업는 것으로 인증해 버린다고 함니다. 그리고 여자에게 주는 교훈 중에 남편 이외에는 아모 것도 업다고 하엿으며 죽든지 살든지 남편에게 절대 복종하여서 그를 즐겁게 해주는 것이 여자에 도덕이라고 하엿슴니다. 그러치만 몬저 말한 모하몃교에서는 그와 아주 반대로 부인들이 남편이나 자녀를 지배한다고<160> 함니다. 좀 불편한 것은 이 부인들은 남편을 지배할 줄은 알면서도 방안에 감검되여서 하로 종일 밧게 못 나간다고 하는 것이올시다. 이 부인들은 열열한 남성들의 총애 속에서 이러한 생활이라도 조곰도 부자유하게 생각지 안는다고 함니다. 그들은 남편이나 안해나 어느 편에서 자긔에게 대해서 좀 애정이 떠진다든지 혹은 다른 상대자를 구하는 듯 십흔 눈치래도 뵈인다면 당장에 가슴에 품엇든 칼노 찔너서 싯뻘건 피를 내고야 만다는 무서운 이약이도 잇슴니다. 다음에는 토이긔에서 성행하는 부부사이에 문제를 이약이 하겟슴니다. 토이긔 부인들도 인도 부인과 맛찬가지로 거리에 잘 나단이지 안엇다고 하며 혹 나오는 일이 잇드래도 얼골에다가 무엇을 가리우고 단인다고 함니다. 그러나 세계대전 후에 이르러서는 15만명이나 되는 아라사 피란민들이 토이긔로 몰여드는 통에 아라사 부인들이 쾌활한 행동에 자극을 밧어서 토이긔의 부인들도 대각성을 이르키게 되엿다고 함니다. 그래서 전에 업든 직업부인도 만허지고 부인협회 갓흔 단체긔관도 생기게 되여서 부인들이 퍽으나 진보된 행동을 취하게 되엿다고 함니다.
이럿케 됨에 따라서 남편과 안해 사이에 문란하든 정조 문제도 퍽 엄격하여 젓다고 함니다. 토이긔 부인들은 남편이 3년 이상을 정신병으로 회복 못될 때라든지 또는 안해에게 대해서 적당한 사랑을 해주지 못할 때라든지 또 매월 이러나는 병적 상태를 리해하여 주지 못하는 때라든지 이러한 경우에는 아모 문제업시 부인측에서 리혼을 청구할만한 당당한 권리를 가지고 잇는 것이라고 함니다. 그러나 한 가지 가엽슨 일은 이 나라 남자들은 전쟁에 나가서 만히 죽엇기 때문에 과부들이 퍽 만엇다고 함니다. 과부라고 해서 혼자 살면 문제되지 안을 것임니다 만은 다시 재혼하게 되니가 한 남자에게 녀자가 셋식이나 녯식이래도 시집가게 된다고 함니다. 그래서 모다 한 집안 안에서 살님을 하게되니 거기에 이러나는 암투라고 하는 것에 대해서는 이약이하지 안어도 여러분이 더 잘 취측하실 것이올시다. 다음에는 러시아의 풍속을 말슴하겟슴니다. 러시아라면 세계에 어느 나라보다 부인들이 해방하고 잇는 나라임니다. 그런 것만큼 결혼의 자유라든지 리혼의 자유 또 그 밧게 정조관념이라든지 그야말로 우리가 보기에는 정말 제 마음대로 하는 것갓치 생각할 정도에 足하여 잇슴니다. 물론 이러케 하는 것이 본격적이고 원측이겟지요. 그럿치만 때로는 거기에서 이러나는 페풍도 적지안은 모양을 볼 때에는 또다시 생각할 필요가 잇는 문제인줄 암니다. 이 러시아에서는 남녀가 결혼하는데 잇서서 결코 제 3자의 간섭이 필요하지 안타는 것이고, 순간적이나마 두 사람이 합이적이여서 사랑해야 된다는 것이올시다. 결혼할 때에도 등록소에 가서 간단한 수속만 한면 될 뿐이고 또 리혼도 맛찬가지로 아주 쉽게 할 수 잇다는 것임니다. 어느 편에서든지<161> 실증이 나면 등록소에서 가서 등록해 가지고 상대자에게 통지해 주면 그것으로 리혼수속이 난다고 함니다. 이외에도 아메리카나 영국이나 중국 여러 나라 부부의 성도덕이라든지 애정문제에 관한 이약이는 여기서 그만두고 인제부터는 부부애에 대한 것과 性道德에 대한 것 등을 대강 이약이하겟슴니다. 그런데 미리 말슴해두는 것은 이 문제에 대해서 로골적으로 이약이 못 듸리겟다는 것임니다. 단지 예증적으로 이약이 하겟슴니다. 그러면 몬저 부부애에 대한 것부터 시작하겟슴니다. 부부애라고 하면 누구나 잘 아는 것임니다. 이것은 과학상으로 본다면 두 가지로 난홀 수 잇는데 즉 그 한 가지는 자체적으로서의 부부애요, 그 다음 둘재는 정신적으로서의 부부애임니다. 엇잿든 이 두 가지의 어느 한 가지가 빠진다고해도 완전한 부부애가 성입되지 못할 것은 다시 말슴듸릴 필요도 업는 줄 암니다. 여러 학자들의 학설을 비처보면 부부애라는 것이 최초로부터 양자 즉 정신애 육체애가 똑갓치 완비해잇든 것이 아니라고 증명햇슴니다. 즉 육체적 애가 몬저 발생한 다음에 정신적 애가 생기개 된 것이라고 하엿슴니다. 이것이 움즉일 수 업는 사실이라는 것을 증명하기 위하야 그들 하등 인류-즉 야만인들의 부부생활을 드러서 이약이하겟슴니다. 야만인들에게는 육적애(肉的愛) 다시 말하면 성욕 밧게 업다는 것을 발견하엿다는 것임니다. 그러치만 이러한 예가 또 반듯이 야만들 사이에만 잇는 것이 아니라는 말도 잇슴니다.
