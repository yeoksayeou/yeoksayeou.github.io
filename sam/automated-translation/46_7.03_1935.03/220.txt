Gentlemen and Ladies Background Survey
The Lunches of Three Newspaper Presidents

What does Mr. Song Chinu, President of the Tong-A Ilbo, eat when lunchtime arrives? Sometimes servants bring his meal (진지) from home, but usually, he has Western food, specifically a 50-chŏn lunch, brought in from places like "Umenoya" (梅の屋) inside the "Kwanghwamun Building" (光化門 삘딩) or other establishments.

Mr. Pang Ŭngmo, President of the Chosŏn Ilbo, eats out about twenty days a month due to social engagements. When he is at the company, it is customary for him to order Western food. During the old office days in Yŏngŏn-dong, he truly shared the joys and sorrows with his employees, eating 12-chŏn beef bone soup (설넝탕) and 15-chŏn soup with rice (湯飯), enduring hardships.

Mr. Yŏ Unhyŏng, President of the Chungang Ilbo, being a man with many visitors, sometimes goes to Paegyangwŏn on Chongno around lunchtime and finishes with a 1-wŏn set meal or a simple 50-chŏn Western-style lunch. Occasionally, he drives with guests to the Chosŏn Hotel dining room and has a set meal. After eating, he takes a walk in the "Rose Garden" (로-스까-덴), formulates his management plans for the newspaper, or engages in social activities for the company.<149>

The Lunches of Bank Presidents

Mr. Pak Yŏngch'ŏl, President of the Chosŏn Commercial Bank, goes directly to the nearby Bank Assembly Hall (銀行集會所) as soon as the noon siren sounds. There, Governor Kato of the Chosŏn Bank, President Aruga of the Choksan Bank, and other top bank presidents in Seoul gather and share lunch. Usually, there are three options priced at 3 wŏn, 2 wŏn, and 1 wŏn. President Pak frugally finishes his lunch with a 1-wŏn lunch. Sometimes, he goes to the golf course outside Tongdaemun and has a simple outdoor meal.

Mr. Min Taesik, President of the Tongil Bank, gathers at the Bank Assembly Hall during lunchtime. He sits facing these giants of the financial world, discusses bank management, and finishes his lunch. He also usually opts for a 1-wŏn Western-style lunch.

Mr. Kim Kyehŭi, President of the Haedong Bank, customarily appears at the Bank Assembly Hall at twelve o'clock. The Bank Assembly Hall is where the titans of the financial world, who have dedicated themselves to the banking business, gather. The building, perched on a high ground in Hasegawa-chō (長谷川町), looks unremarkable from the outside, merely three stories including the basement. However, once one steps inside, everyone clicks their tongue at the splendid decorations. The mirrors hung in each room, the chandeliers, the silk brocade (공단) laid in the corridors of every room – it is simply dazzling. And the cuisine, prepared by famous chefs (국수) in Chosŏn, has an established reputation (定評) as the most delicious in the land. Commoners can only be astonished.<150><149-150>

**Summary**

This article details the lunchtime routines of prominent newspaper presidents (Song Chinu of Tong-A Ilbo, Pang Ŭngmo of Chosŏn Ilbo, Yŏ Unhyŏng of Chungang Ilbo) and bank presidents (Pak Yŏngch'ŏl of Chosŏn Commercial Bank, Min Taesik of Tongil Bank, Kim Kyehŭi of Haedong Bank) in 1935 Seoul. It describes where they eat (company, restaurants, a luxurious bankers' club), what they eat (Korean meals, Western lunches of varying prices), and who they dine with, contrasting the relative frugality or opulence of their habits. From a historical perspective, the article offers insights into the lifestyles of the Korean elite under Japanese colonial rule, including the adoption of Western food and customs, the social stratification reflected in meal prices (from 12-chŏn soup to 3-wŏn lunches), the existence of exclusive venues like the Bank Assembly Hall, and the presence of Japanese figures (Kato, Aruga) in high positions within the colonial banking system. Historians might find the specific names, locations, prices, and descriptions of social interactions valuable for understanding the socio-economic landscape of 1930s Seoul.

三新聞社長의 점심

東亞日報社長 宋鎭禹씨는 점심 때에 이르면 무엇을 잡숫는가, 宅에서 「진지」를 하인들이 날너 가저오는 때도 잇지만 각금 洋料理를 그것도 50전 정도의 런치를 「光化門 삘딩」안의 「梅の屋」 기타에서 갓다가 잡수시는 것이 예이다.
朝鮮日報社長 方應謨씨는 한달이면 스무날은 交際 때문에 박갓헤 나가 잡숫는데, 社에 게실  때에는 洋料理를 청하여다가 잡숫는 것이 예이다. 蓮建洞의 舊社屋 시절에는 사원들과 문자 그대로의 동고동락을 하시느라고 12전짜리 설넝탕도 잡숫고 15전짜리 湯飯도 잡수으며 고생하섯다.
中央日報社長 呂運亨씨는 來客이 만흔 분이니만치 점식적에는 각금 鐘路百合園에 이르러 1원짜리 정식이나 50전짜리 간단한 양식 런치로 끗마치는 때도 잇고 간혹은 손님과 가치 자동차를 모라 조선호텔 식당에 나타나 정식을 잡숫는 때도 잇다. 그래서 식후에는 로-스까-덴」으로 산책하며 신문사에 대한 경륜을 마련하시며 또는 社를 위한 교제도 하신다.<149>

銀行 頭取의 點心

朝鮮商業銀行 頭取 朴榮喆씨는 점심 고등이 틀기와 가치 바로 그 근방에 잇는 銀行集會所로 간다. 거기에는 加藤朝鮮銀行總裁와 有賀殖産銀行頭取와 그박게 서울 안에 잇는 일류 은행장들이 모다 모여 점심을 함께 나눈다.
보통, 3원, 2원, 1원의 3가지가 잇는데 朴頭取는 검소하게 1원 정도의 런치로 점심을 끗내인다. 엇든 때는 동대문 外 꼴푸장에 가서 간단한 야외식을 자시는 때도 잇다.
東一銀行 頭取 閔大植씨는 점심시간에는 銀行集合所에서 모여, 은행 경륜에 대하야 이 모든 금융계의 巨星들과 마조 안저 이약이하며 점심을 마춘다. 氏 亦 1원 정도의 양식 런취가 보통이다.
海東銀行頭取 金季浨씨는 열두 시면 銀行 集會所에 몸을 나타냄이 예이다. 은행 집회소란 곳은 은행사업에 몸을 바친 금융계 巨星들이 모히는 곳이라. 長谷川町 高臺에 소슨 그집의 외관은 지하까지 겨우 3층으로 그리 보잘 것이 업스나 1차 그 내부에 발을 도러노으면 그 화려한 장식에 누구나 혀를 챈다. 각방에 거러논 거울, 샨데리, 방방에 깐 공단 복도, 오직 눈부심이 잇슬 뿐이고 그 요리는 조선서 일홈잇는 국수들 손으로 되여지느니 만치 맛남에 천하제일이란 定評이 잇다. 평민들은 오직 놀남이 잇슬 뿐이라 할가.<150><149-150>
