The Diet and the Issue of Korean Political Participation

It is already widely known that a local self-government system has been implemented in Chosŏn, where officially appointed and popularly elected council members sit together to deliberate on the local budgets of the local administrative offices. Also, it was recently reported that this local self-government system is likely to be implemented in Taiwan starting next year. However, at this time, an important event occurred: during the recent "Imperial Diet" held in Tōkyō, a petition titled "Grant Political Participation Rights to Chosŏn," jointly signed by a certain Korean individual and 120 others, was submitted to the House of Representatives Petitions Committee through Representative Pak Ch'un-gŭm. The government policy, however, led to its rejection on the grounds that it was "premature." Subsequently, the current Minister of Colonial Affairs and former Chosŏn Governor-General for Political Affairs, Count Kodama [Hideo], elaborated on the reason, stating, "Due to the low level of popular standards (民度) in areas such as education and industry, political participation rights cannot yet be granted." Thus, the path to central political participation, which has been debated back and forth for 15 or 16 years, has been revealed to still be a matter for the distant future.

Dr. Mott Coming to Seoul from America

There is inside information that Dr. [John R.] Mott will come to Chosŏn from America in mid-March. Dr. Mott is a heavyweight in the World Alliance of YMCAs and is known globally within Christian society, having visited Chosŏn several times before. The purpose of this visit is said to be an inspection of the peninsula's missionary work.<18>

900,000 Won Reappears

Regarding the 900,000 won deposited in the Dai-Ichi Bank by the Minister of Finance, Yi Yong-ik, the bank refused payment, asserting the nonsensical reason that it was not Yi Yong-ik's property but the Emperor's money, the nation's money. Consequently, his eldest grandson, the late Yi Chong-ho, filed a claim lawsuit in the Tōkyō Court with lawyers such as Ōshima as legal representatives. After high officials from that time, like Hayashi Gonsuke, testified and so forth, the case was lost in the first trial and again lost in the second trial. However, as fate would have it, the case went up to the Daishin'in (Supreme Court), where a retrial judgment was issued, and it was decided to re-examine the case. The current situation suggests a strong possibility of recovery, and it seems likely that eventually, either the bank or the court will push for a settlement. Mr. Hŏ Hŏn testifies that the 900,000 won clearly belonged to Yi Yong-ik personally. According to Hŏ Hŏn's explanation: "In the autumn of Meiji 38 [1905], after being invited to dinner by Mr. Hayashi Gonsuke at the Japanese Legation, Yi Yong-ik was set to go to Tōkyō. As the Tōkyō trip was decided suddenly, a messenger was sent to inform his home. Therefore, Mr. Hŏ Hŏn, Mr. Yi Chong-ho, and others brought Western clothes and other travel expenses from his house. They immediately took a train from Namdaemun, boarded a warship in Inch'ŏn, and went straight to Tōkyō. Around December 28th, intending to establish Posŏng College, he returned after purchasing about 30,000 won worth of books. At that time, Yi Yong-ik had a large sum of money in hand, and as soon as the new year began (as banks were closed for the year-end and New Year holidays), on January 6th, he deposited either 130,000 or 160,000 won into the Dai-Ichi Bank. Therefore, there is no reason for it to be public funds."

Pak In-dŏk and Hŏ Yŏng-suk, Born the Same Year, Same Month, Same Day, Same Hour

It is truly a strange fate that Pak In-dŏk and Hŏ Yŏng-suk, both famous for various reasons, were born in the same year, same month, same day, and same hour. The two knew each other during their student days – Ms. Hŏ was at Chinmyŏng and Ms. Pak at Ewha – but had no close relationship. After both Ms. Pak and Ms. Hŏ got married, perhaps when they were twenty-two or twenty-three, during the beautiful spring season in May when barley yellows (大麥黃), skylarks fly over barley fields, and cuckoos call from distant mountains, the two, each accompanied by her new husband, happened to meet at Sŏgwangsa Temple and exchanged greetings for the first time. As it was a scene where two eloquent women met, they talked while crying, laughing, jumping, and chattering.
"When were you born? How old are you, elder sister?"
"Twenty-two."
"Oh my goodness, we're the same age! What about your birthday?"
"It's such-and-such month, such-and-such day."
"What is this? How can it be so uncanny?"
"You mean you too, elder sister?"
"Yes—this is truly a match made in heaven (天生配匹). How strange<19>, that we weren't born as men—if we had been—"
"Really, if we had been, we same-aged pair could have become partners. This is truly a dream—But sister, really, what time were you born?"
"I was born in the Hour of the Tiger (寅時), maybe around the time they boil barley in the evening."
"Look at this providence! I was also born in the Hour of the Tiger."
Thus, the two women, chattering endlessly (喃喃喋喋) about this strange fate arranged by heaven, continued their intricate conversation until the mountain rooster of Sŏgwangsa Temple crowed once, twice, and thrice. Shaking hands by the Taedong River—no, that's Yi Su-il and Sim Sun-ae. The two women shook hands by Sŏgwangsa Temple—falling flowers, flowing water—

Youthful Monk Song Pong-u, "Smiling, Points to a Single Cloud in the Blue Sky"

Mr. Song Pong-u, having had some thoughts recently, has entered the Sŏn Academy in Anguk-tong, Seoul, for mountain retreat and spiritual cultivation. When friends inquire about this change of heart, he offers a quiet smile and quotes a line from an old poem: "(Smiling, points to a single cloud in the blue sky - 笑指靑天一片雲)."

Poet Section Chief's Tale of Heroism During the Great Hwasin Fire

There is a tale of heroism (武勇談) about the great deeds of Mr. Chu Yo-han, known as the poet section chief (詩人課長), during the great fire at Hwasin.
From the rooftop rooms on the 4th floor where the fire started, red flames flared out fiercely. The Chongno area was filled with the blaze reaching the sky, and thousands of onlookers swarmed in from all directions. The sound of shattering glass windows, the noise of goods being pulled out, cries, and shouts filled the air.
At this moment, after all customers had been evacuated, employees started escaping one by one from the fire. Just when people were feeling relieved that all lives had surely been saved, wasn't the desperate cry of a frail woman heard from somewhere?
Witnessing this scene, poet section chief Chu Yo-han pushed aside the several people trembling in fear and single-handedly dashed into the flames, ran up to the 4th floor, found the source of the screams (悲鳴), and rescued a young female employee. This is the tale of heroism.

Tosan's Illness

Mr. An Ch'ang-ho (pen name Tosan), who had gone to Yonggang Hot Springs for treatment, arrived back in P'yŏngyang on the 16th. He met with Mr. Yŏ Un-hyŏng, who came from Kyŏngsŏng, and also Mr. Cho Man-sik, reminiscing about old times, and then headed back to the hot springs. According to what he told a recent acquaintance:
1. From now on, I will devote myself to supporting the youth.
2. I will teach them from my experience.
3. I will uphold my principles (志操) no matter what.
It is said he has a stomach ailment requiring considerable treatment, and also that his teeth (齒) are bad and need replacement, which he might do after coming up to Seoul on March 9th.<20>
Furthermore, it is said his eyesight was damaged from reading in the dim light of his prison cell, where sunlight did not enter well, although they apparently provided him with a special kerosene lamp (유탐부).

Source of the News of Yi Tong-hwi's Passing

When the newspaper report that Sŏngje Yi Tong-hwi had suddenly passed away from illness in Vladivostok, Russia, was conveyed to the world, everyone who heard it, regardless of whether they knew him personally, was greatly surprised by its unexpectedness. As for how the news of his passing reached his homeland, it was learned by a Government-General press reporter through a report from the Japanese Consulate General in Vladivostok and published as a newspaper article. Later, those who know Russian read a similar report in the Soviet government organ "Pravda" (라우다) published in Moscow, confirming definitively that he had passed away.

Initiation of Sŏngje Memorial Service

Upon hearing the news of Yi Tong-hwi's passing, prominent figures from various sectors in Seoul society intended to hold a grand memorial service. The day after the news was reported in the newspapers, a preparatory meeting was held on the 4th floor of the Samch'ŏlli Sa building in Chongno, Kyŏngsŏng. It was decided to hold the memorial service as a joint effort involving representatives from each newspaper company, various ideological groups, and church-related figures. Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho represented all the initiators and took responsibility for the assembly notification. Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi was directly in charge of negotiations and visited the chief of the Higher Affairs Section at the jurisdictional Chongno Police Station the next day to request permission for the assembly. However, due to the authorities' policy stating "there is concern it may disturb public peace (公安)," the assembly was prohibited, and inevitably, the memorial service had to be cancelled. The names of those who gathered at Samch'ŏlli Sa are as follows:
Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng, Wŏn Se-hun, Kim Tong-in, Yu Kwang-nyŏl, Sin Im, Kim Kyŏng-jae, Kim Tong-hwan, and others.

Education Association vs. Pak Hŭng-sik Lawsuit

The long-standing court case between the Chosŏn Education Association, located in Sup'yo-dong, Seoul, and Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik of Hwasin Corporation recently received a verdict in the second trial at the Kyŏngsŏng Court of Appeals. Similar to the first trial, Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik's side lost. The details of the lawsuit go back 6 or 7 years. At that time, Elder Yu Chin-t'ae, who was the Chairman of the Education Association, became the president of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper. In an effort to rescue the deteriorating finances of the newspaper company, a certain individual from the company's leadership used the land and building of the Education Association as collateral with Mr. Pak Hŭng-sik's Sŏnil Paper Company (鮮一紙物 會社) and took paper worth 10,000-odd won. Consequently, Mr. Pak's side legally demanded payment for the goods. Due to complex circumstances during this period, the verdict was reached as described, and now the building and land of the historically traditional Education Association have escaped the crisis.<21>

Academic World, Literary Scene

Ewha College Graduation Ceremony at the New Campus

The new school building for Ewha Womans College, under construction in Sinch'on outside the city with a huge investment of hundreds of thousands of won, is scheduled for completion in March. This year's graduation ceremony is also planned to be held at the new campus. For this reason, the move from the old campus to the new one is set to be completed over a week starting from March 7th, and the graduation ceremony is scheduled for March 30th in the new auditorium. Another noteworthy point is that among Ewha Womans College's departments, only the Home Economics Department has not yet received designation under the professional school ordinance. As influential educators from America and academic inspectors from the Ministry of Education in Tōkyō are all scheduled to come to Seoul in March, their inspections are also planned to take place at the new campus. Thus, the preparations for the new school facilities are proceeding day and night.

30 Musicians Coming from Tōkyō

Musician Kim Mun-bo, who has set up a sweet home at Den-en-chōfu No. 4, Ōmori Ward, Tōkyō City, plans to hold his first recital in Tōkyō this summer, perhaps around May or June, and immediately follow it with a performance tour visiting his homeland. This is not an individual effort by Kim Mun-bo, but is hosted by the recently founded Association of Korean Musicians in Tōkyō. This tour is expected to be a large-scale event involving about 30 performers, and anticipation in the music scene is reportedly high.

Letter from Ms. Na

Ms. Na Hye-sŏk, who bears the reputation of being the Florence of the peninsula and has settled in the legendary city of art, Suwŏn, recently decided to forge ahead solely on the path of art once more to cultivate her natural talent (天分). She has built a quaint three-room thatched cottage (三間草堂) outside Sŏho Castle and is busy turning her colored brush (彩筆) on canvas every day. Recently, signifying her entry into this new life, she sent the following message to her friends: "I have returned after 20 years to Suwŏn, the land where I was born and raised, and have established my residence."
Seeing Suwŏn Castle after seeing the castles of Rome (露馬城) gives an unusually romantic feeling.
Suwŏn possesses Eight Views: Kwanggyo Chŏksŏl (Snow on Mt. Kwanggyo), Hwahong Ch'onbyŏk (Gurgling stream at Hwahongmun Gate), Nagak Taewŏl (Waiting for the moon at Panghwasuryujŏng Pavilion), Tongsan Sŏkpong (Evening beacon fire on the eastern hill), Pyŏng'am Kansu (Stream water at Pyŏng'am Rock), Yuch'ŏn Changje (Long embankment at Yuch'ŏn Stream), Sŏho Nakcho (Sunset glow at West Lake), and Pukchi Sangnyŏn (Lotus viewing at North Pond). Indeed, there are many subjects for painting (畵題) and many places for strolls.
After recuperating my unhealthy body with medicine and rest, I intend to re-enter society and receive guidance from my teachers. I hope for much love and support.
Suwŏn X-tae X-myŏn Chiri 557
Na Hye-sŏk<22>

Recent Activities of Writers

Mr. Chang Hyŏk-chu, combining business related to Kaizōsha with appreciating the early spring scenery of the Kantō region, departed from his hometown Taegu in early March for Tōkyō, where he expects to stay for several weeks.
Female writer Ch'oe Chŏng-hŭi, implicated along with her husband in the Chŏnbuk Sin Kŏnsŏl Sa incident, is currently imprisoned in Chŏnju Prison. Having entrusted her three-year-old child to her mother, she is reportedly distressed due to affection for her beloved child.
Poet Ansŏ, Kim Ŏk, had another daughter in late January.
P'albong Kim Ki-jin went to Namwŏn, a scenic spot in the Yŏngnam region famous for Kwanghallu Pavilion. He didn't go searching for "Ch'unhyang" but was detained at the Namwŏn police station for over a month due to involvement in the aforementioned Sin Kŏnsŏl Sa incident before returning. Friends commented that he must have dreamt of seeing Ch'unhyang (夢見春香).
Female novelist Paek Sin-ae has gone to Panyawŏl, a charming village near Kyŏngju with a name sounding like something Yi T'aebaek might have coined. There, she manages an orchard her older brother established and quietly wields her creative brush.
Female novelist Chang Tŏk-cho went down to Taegu after the Lunar New Year to visit her parents' home, holding her beloved child, and returned after about a week.
Female novelist Pak Hwa-sŏng is diligently wielding her creative brush in a thatched house in Mokp'o, but recently, due to severe nervous weakness, her writing reportedly lacks the force of a galloping horse (奔馬之勢) it once had.

議會와 朝鮮人參政問題

朝鮮에 地方自治制가 실시되어 官選議員과 民選議員이 한자리에 안저 그 지방행정관청의 지방 豫算을 審議하고 잇슴은 己히 世人이 아는 바이요, 又 臺灣에서는 이 地方自治制度가 명년도부터 시행될 모양이란 정보를 드른지도 최근의 일이다. 그런데 이 때에 잇서 한가지 중요한 일은 일전 東京에서 개최되엇든 「帝國議會」에 朝鮮人 某씨 외 120인의 連署로 代議士朴春琴씨 손을 거처.
「朝鮮에 參政權을 실시하여 주오」 하는 청원을 衆議院 請願委員會에 제출하엿든 중 정부의 방침이 「시기상조」라는 이유로 불채택이 되엿고 이어서 現 拓務大臣이자 前 朝鮮政務總監이든 兒玉伯爵이 그 이유를 부연하여 「敎育과 産業 등 民度가 나즌 까닭에 아직는 參政權을 許與할 수 업다」 하엿다. 이리하야 15, 6년 번복하여 오든 中央參政의 길은 아직도 먼 장래의 일인 것이 알여지엇다.

和信 大火時에 詩人課長의 武勇傳
