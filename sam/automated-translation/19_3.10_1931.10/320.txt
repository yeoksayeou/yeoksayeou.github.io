**Contest Announcement, The Betrayed Comrade**

**Cause is Twofold (Prize)**
O Yŏnggŭn (吳永根)

Within our camp, we often witness comrades who betray us. Although I haven't left a good record of struggle on the front lines of the movement, it's true that I've been learning all tactics with all my might and wish to fight on the very front line.
Of course, in a place like Chosŏn (朝鮮), it seems impossible for us to carry out a social movement that would yield sharp results for the entire proletariat. But even so, shouldn't our movement avoid stagnation or regression? Here, we must explore new tactics, and no small number of vanguard elements will have to be sacrificed.
Discussing such matters one by one would lead to unfolding the fundamental theory of the socialist movement, so I will stop here and instead ruthlessly expose the actual cases of two high-ranking (fallen) cadres within our movement camp, whom we (I) trusted most and fought alongside for so long (?), turning traitor.

That comrade (?) never knew how to use any particular deceitful tactics, nor did he possess heroism; he was just a young man among us who said he would simply follow the leader's guidance. Yet he bravely told us several times things like,
"Comrades, you show signs of turning right-wing, so be careful,"
or even that he would have us expelled from the Central Committee of the Congress.
Even for someone like me, whose consciousness was not yet clear, his powerful (?) words instilled a brave spirit, enough to make me unafraid even if a sword were pointed right at me. So now, thinking back, I blush with embarrassment—at that time, I tended to try to solve everything purely through emotional action. Of course, it wasn't just me who acted emotionally; several other comrades like me also acted purely based on emotion.
However, we didn't want to remain mere emotional Marx-boys or Engels-types forever; accordingly, we tried to delve into the fundamental meaning of Marxism. <84>
And the trust we had placed in that comrade (?), whom we had revered as our leader until then, gradually diminished. Not only diminished, but our trust in him completely vanished.
From then on, we paid close attention to his every action, and our eyes could clearly discern his XX. However, we held onto a tiny shred of hope (?) that he wouldn't truly betray us. But he certainly did betray us. He, who was revered as a leader (?), became blinded by money and ended up becoming a minion (끈아풀) of XX. Consequently, we were tightly bound by his strings, so how could there be a way to escape? We ended up having our flesh and fat squeezed out for a long time in dark prison cells.
The leader (?) betrayed us and also caused our flesh and fat to be squeezed out.
Our former leader (?) is now showing his unwavering activity under the splendid name of XX at the XX Police Station.

I intend to confess the facts about another comrade (?). As I write these facts, my hands tremble, and the veins throughout my body stand on end.
Within our entire camp—often, false social activists who wander around chattering about socialism, purely out of selfish—heroic psychology, trying to become vanguard elements (to understand the reality of our movement) just to preserve their own positions, catch our eye.
I should mention now that the comrade (?) I am about to speak of belongs to this category.
He possessed considerable theory (?), at least.
To me, whose consciousness was weak, he seemed a more courageous and combative fighter than the genuine socialists who fought steadily through practical action.
So I always enjoyed listening only to his words, and whenever I met anyone, I would tell them he was our leader. Truly, all my past actions and views were this foolish and narrow.
Was it just me? Our comrades revered him, and whenever we gathered, there were several times we praised him, saying, "XX will become our true leader." However, the opportunity came to realize that this comrade (?) XX had been purely using us. It happened when one of our comrades attended the general meeting of the Youth General Alliance (청년총동맹총회). The story that comrade relayed to us made us tremble with intense anger.
"Hey—our leader (?) deceived us. We were nothing more than his tools (리용물)."
We gathered our heads together, just staring at that comrade's mouth, waiting for the story to continue. He went on,
"The XX we revered is a degenerate (타락분자) who was even expelled from the Youth Alliance last night. He was a degenerate ostracized by all comrades. We didn't even know that and got caught in the net of his sweet words—his schemes. We must think about how to deal with that scoundrel."
After the comrade finished speaking, we were so angry we didn't know what measures to take. Our leader (?) XX, after being ostracized by various comrades, disappeared without a trace. Lately, rumors (?) say he's a SPY for XX...
Having been thus completely betrayed by comrades, we (I) came to the firm resolve to initiate practical action myself, rather than receiving guidance from someone or proceeding theoretically.
September 5, 1931

**Because of a Woman, 8 Years Ago (Prize)**
P'yŏngbuk Inp'ung (平北 仁風)

"The Betrayed Comrade"? Ah! How detestable an affair this is, and how dumbfounding <85> must it be for the person involved. If there is resentment in the world, what could be greater than this? If there is injustice under heaven, what greater injustice could there be than this? When I think that my current situation and circumstances are solely due to the betrayal of a comrade, it is truly difficult to suppress feelings of painful resentment (痛忿).

The time was the autumn of 1924, 8 years ago from now.
My hometown, the border region of Kanggye (江界), was experiencing a period of extreme turmoil (搔亂時代) due to the activities of the XX Group (XX團). At that time, as I was managing a branch office of a certain newspaper (某報支局), I did not neglect my responsibility to investigate and report in detail on the activities of the OO Group (OO團), the pursuits of the Police Units (警官隊), and all the suffering of the populace caught between them.
This also happened that summer. The horrific incident of the massacre of 8 civilian households in one night in Hwach'ang-myŏn (和昌面), Wiwŏn County (渭原郡), and the certain violation (逢辱事件) of over 30 village women in Ŏroe-myŏn (漁雷面) of our county—truly, chilling events like these occurred more than once or twice. Simultaneously, a momentous event intertwined with my life, one I cannot forget, occurred. It was none other than this: Commander Chang (張某), dispatched to the Kanggye region under the orders of the OO Group headquarters at the time, had been trying endlessly to establish contact with me. However, sensing the situation, a certain police inspector (警部) of the Subjugation Unit (討伐隊) [Ellipsis - original text] created an incident where 4 or 5 people were murdered. This was nothing short of a bolt from the blue (靑天霹靂) for me, and it's a fact that I couldn't help but feel a certain sense of responsibility. Thus, my circumstances became such that I could no longer possibly live out even a humble life. [Remainder omitted - original text]

I had a widowed father over 70 and four helpless children. Morally, justly, and responsibly, my situation was by no means one where I could abandon my family, but my extraordinary resolve led my father to tearfully grant permission. However, here I must write about a person fit to be the protagonist of this main issue: a woman named Ch'undo (春桃), with whom I was close. As she knew my situation in detail, she was not unaware of the inner workings of the OO Group. One day, when I told her of my decision to escape across the border, she, though just a woman, expressed a strong desire under a clear resolve to follow me. Perhaps it was the foolish thought that accompanying a woman when undertaking a man's great affairs was absolutely unacceptable, but I refused her in every way possible. Yet, on the other hand, I also felt a certain unease about the difficulties that might befall her after my departure, and consequently, the fear that some secret might be revealed. After considering various things, I agreed to take Ch'undo along, and specifically promised the OO Unit Commander Xp'a (X坡 - likely 白坡 Paekp'a) that while accompanying a woman was improper, it was due to unavoidable circumstances, and instructed him to OO [likely meaning 'eliminate' or 'dispose of'] her at a certain place en route. Thus, Ch'undo came to travel with the OO unit; for the sake of our resolve, the sacrifice of a single woman was not even considered an issue at the time. Afterwards, I alone escaped the border, barely surviving numerous brushes with death (九死一生), after about 10 days.

I reached the OO Group Headquarters, a certain place in Chiptanhyŏn (輯安縣), on November 1st of that year, in early winter. It was a time when even white snow swirled over the mountains and rivers. The surprise and shame upon encountering Ch'undo, unexpectedly alive! I asked Paekp'a (白坡) for the facts. His reply? That he couldn't bring himself to do it out of human feeling? Could I have ever dreamt it? Paekp'a's evil scheme (惡計)! I merely lamented the shame of being the only one accompanied by a woman among hundreds of comrades, all single. Later, on a certain occasion, I learned in detail from Ch'undo the facts that Xp'a (Paekp'a) had OO [likely meaning 'raped' or 'assaulted'] her several times, and that he had schemed to assassinate me under some pretext—claiming that he had brought Ch'undo, whom I had ordered killed—and coaxed her to go with him towards the Kirim (吉林) area.
Ah! What a terrifying betrayal by a comrade this is! Hearing these words, I could only feel disheartened and despairing (落膽失望).

Knowing Xp'a's (Paekp'a's) character, I did not take seriously his betraying a comrade over a single woman. It was the action of one starved for sex for many years, like a hungry person stealing, so I could only laugh bitterly (呵呵苦笑). However, afterwards, he became blatant in plotting against me in various matters. Thinking back, I deeply regretted bringing the woman along, and concluded that to resolve this fundamentally, there was nothing better than dealing with the woman as quickly as possible, even one day sooner. It reached the point where I attempted to shoot Ch'undo with the pistol I carried. At this moment, I was seized by the hands of comrades, failed to achieve my intention yet again, and in the early spring of the following year, February, before the snow had melted, I abandoned all hope and ambition, turned my back on that place full of resentment (恨), and headed for a certain place. Unable to achieve anything as I wished, due to a certain unavoidable incident en route, I underwent the procedures for surrender (歸順). Thus <86> my life ended up leaving only OO [likely meaning 'stain' or 'regret'].

Afterwards, I again wandered in China for several years, but due to the wound once received, everything felt like mere fantasy, and even the life of a hermit in the Kŭmgang Mountains (金剛山) only brought anguish. Today, in my bitter life, quietly recalling the past, I can only resent the comrade's betrayal. Chagwangun (自狂雲), Ch'a Ch'ŏlli (車千里), Paekch'i (白治), and the many others who died unnatural deaths (非命橫死). What indeed was the reason? I heard that Xp'a (Paekp'a) was later expelled (追出) from the headquarters, wandered various places, and was arrested by the North P'yŏngan (平北) Provincial Police Department [Remainder omitted - original text]
Whether he was truly the admirable figure and man of action the world gossiped about is a separate issue, but I will bear the responsibility for my misguided life. If you too have a conscience, myriad feelings (萬感) must be stirring within you now.
Looking at this incident personally, I considered it merely a "dumbfounding affair" (기맥히는 일) and intended to brush it aside (點殺), as 7 or 8 years have already passed. However, thinking that your magazine's special handling of this issue is not merely for sensationalism but perhaps aims to provide some social lesson and benefit, I have taken up my pen.

**Before and After in Tokyo (Prize)**
Chŏngp'yŏng Maeng Hyŏngmo (定平 孟亨模)

I first met Mr. Kang (姜君) in June 1926, at the opening meeting of the Proletarian Students' Association (無産學友會) held at Mr. R's house in the Kanda district (神田區) of Tokyo (東京). I don't fully know your state of mind when you first attended that meeting, Mr. Kang, but since you knew that the Musan Haguhoe meeting at that time was not its founding general assembly, but rather a gathering revived through the enthusiasm of a few comrades after an unexpected period of stagnation, how could you not have known that its main proponents were members belonging to the A group?
You surely knew, which is why you continued attending the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd meetings, and beyond that, even became a member of the Free Labor Union (自由勞働組合) which arose subsequently. At that time, the Musan Haguhoe stood in opposition to the Association of Chosŏn Students Studying Abroad in Tokyo (在東京遊學生學友會). Although the Association of Chosŏn Students Studying Abroad appeared outwardly composed of bourgeois students, it was common knowledge (旣知) in the surroundings that the cadres (幹部連衆) behind the scenes were petit bourgeois elements (프틔브르조아 連衆) of the CS Group (CS團). They formed a group for their own benefit, so we felt we couldn't just be mixed up with them and exploited; we needed our own group for ourselves—that was the sentiment under which it arose. I intend to speak even of its platform. Also, I view the Free Labor Union as purely an AC group. Why? Because regarding the ratio of free laborers (自由勞働者) to professional laborers (職業勞働) among the Chosŏn laborers in Tokyo at the time, although I don't know the exact numbers, the majority were free laborers who, one could see, constantly feared their own unemployment. So, among free laborers, what need was there for a labor union simply named 'Free' without any other designation attached? It was because the petit bourgeois elements of CS, using signboards like the Western Labor Union (西部勞働組合) and Southern Labor Union (南部勞働組合) at the time, used sweet talk and trickery (甘言術策) to deceive ignorant laborers, offering no economic or national salvation whatsoever, merely reciting Marx's jargon (熟語) from the podium—jargon they themselves didn't fully understand—and boastfully advertising their own importance. Who would tolerate that? The fact that the Koishikawa Tōkō Labor Union (小石川東興勞働組合) also inevitably joined the AC group can be seen as one example of this. The Free Labor Union arose necessarily to expose such conspiracies, and the name was attached to distinguish it from other labor unions.
Mr. Kang! Who would have known that you would leave these two organizations and turn reactionary? The incident where a conflict arose with a certain nationalist reactionary group (民族的 反動團體) in Honjo (本所 - assuming 本所 refers to Honjo ward in Tokyo) and we planned a spirited fight (快鬪); the struggle in Ōsaki (大崎) against those CS elements who deceived laborers, talking about 'Bolsheviks' and whatnot—you remember all this, don't you? I have now forgotten the things I planned and did together with you, nor do I intend to write them all down.
Mr. Kang! You wrote a critical essay titled "Reading the Theory and Practice Regarding the Idol Problem" in a certain daily newspaper (某日報) in January of last year, didn't you? Do you think your critique of value within the first volume of Proudhon's *Système des contradictions économiques* (『경제학 모순의 체계』) is correct? Didn't you deliberately distort someone else's writing, talk utter nonsense (헷 뚱단지 가튼 소리), and point out its flaws?
Let me cite your quotation: <87>
"The capacity possessed by natural or productive things in productive general, which humanity uses in the process of its life, can be specifically named use-value."
Mr. Kang! Do you yourself understand what this means? Look at the original text below and my translation. (Or, la capacité qu'ont tous les produits, soit naturels, soit industriels, de servir à la subsistance de l'homme, se nomme particulièrement valeur d'utilité) (p.92) 『The capacity (capacité) possessed by all products, whether natural or industrial, to serve for the subsistence of man, is specifically named use-value.』 Besides this, there are not a few mistranslations and erroneous arguments, but as this is not a critical essay, I will not write further. <126>

**Summary**

The article presents three prize-winning memoirs from a *Samch'ŏlli* magazine contest on the theme "Betrayed Comrade." The first recounts betrayal by two socialist leaders in colonial Korea, one succumbing to money and becoming a police informant, the other revealed as a self-serving opportunist expelled from the movement. The second tells of betrayal within an armed independence/guerrilla group (likely operating near the Manchurian border) where a commander named Paekp'a spares, then allegedly assaults, a woman the author had ordered killed, later plotting against the author, leading to the author's disillusionment and surrender. The third details betrayal within Korean student and labor movements in 1920s Tokyo, accusing a former comrade, Kang, of abandoning leftist groups (Musan Haguhoe, Chayu Nodong Chohap) and turning "reactionary," citing Kang's flawed critique of Proudhon as evidence of intellectual dishonesty accompanying his political turn. These accounts offer historians valuable glimpses into the internal conflicts, ideological struggles (socialism vs. nationalism, factionalism within socialism), personal betrayals, and harsh realities (including police surveillance, infiltration, and violence) faced by Korean activists across different arenas – domestic socialist organizing, borderland armed resistance, and overseas student/labor movements – during the Japanese colonial period. The raw emotions and specific details about group dynamics and individual failures provide a personal counterpoint to more formal historical narratives.

懸賞發表, 背叛한 同志
原因은 두 가지(賞)
吳永根

우리들 진영 내에는 흔이 배반하는 동지들을 목격하는 때가 만슴니다. 비록 내가 운동선상에서 조흔 투쟁 긔록은 남겨 놋치 못하엿지만 모-든 전술을 힘껏 배워가며 제일선상에서 싸호고 십흔 생각을 가지고 잇는 것만은 사실임니다.

「背叛한 同志」? 아! 이 얼마나 가증한 일이며 당사자로 하여금 얼마나 기막힐 일＜85＞이라 할가. 세상에 忿함이 잇다 하여도 여긔서 더한 일이 어대 잇스며 천하에 不義가 잇다 하여도 이에서 더 큰 不義가 어대 잇스리요. 지금 나의 처지와 환경이 오로지 동지의 배반에 인함이라 함을 생각할 때에 실로 痛忿의 念을 금키 難하다.

定平 孟亨模

姜君은 1926년 6월에 東京神田區의 R君 宅에서 모인 無産學友會 開會席上에서 나는 처음 아랏다. 君이 처음 彼 會席에 출석할 당초 심리는 내가 다- 모르나 無産學友會가 당시 모음이 創立總會가 아니라 중간에 의외의 침체상태로 잇다가 약간 同侔의 열성으로 다시 이러난 모음인 줄 아는 君이야 엇지 그의 주동자가 A 그루-프에 속한 분자가 아닌 줄 아라슬가.
君도 역시 아랏기에 1차 2차 3차는 계속 하다가 그 외에 또다시 이러난 自由勞働組合의 同人까지 되엿지. 당시 無産學友는 在東京遊學生學友會에 대립하엿다. 留學生學友會가 외관상 구성분자는 뿌르조아 學生輩라고 볼 수 잇스나 裏面의 幹部連衆은 CS團의 프틔브르조아 連衆인 줄은 주위가 旣知한다. 彼輩는 彼를 위한 그루-프니 우리가 공연이 彼輩에 혼동되여 이용당하는 것 보다 우리도 우리 자신을 위한 그루-프가 업서서는 안 된겟다는 기분 하에서 일어난 것이다. 나는 그의 綱領까지 말할랴고 한다. 또 自由勞働組合을 내가 보기를 純然한 AC의 그루-프라고 본다. 웨 그런냐 하면 당시 東京에 잇든 朝鮮人勞働者가 自由勞働者와 職業勞働의 比例가 엇지 되엇는냐 하면 명확한 숫자는 모르나 대부분이 自由勞働者로서 그의 失業을 그 자신이 항상 두려워한다고 볼 수 잇다. 그런데 自由勞働者 간에 자유라고만 稱名하고 별다른 명칭을 부치지를 아닌 勞働組合의 필요가 어데 잇는냐. 그는 CS의 프틔輩가 당시 西部勞働組合
南部勞働組合 등의 간판을 가지고 甘言術策으로 무지한 勞働者에게 경제적 혹은 민족적 하등의 구원이 업시 다만 席上에서 자기 자신도 잘 이해치 못하는 맑스의 熟語를 낭독하며 자기의 위풍을 과장적으로 선전하니 어느 누가 그를 甘受할가. 小石川東興勞働組合도 할 수 업시 AC 그루-프에 귀합된 것도 그의 한 예라고 볼 수 잇다. 自由勞働組合은 여차한 음모를 폭로하기 위하야 필연적으로 일어난 것인데 他 勞動組合과 구별하기 위하야 부친 명칭이다.
姜君! 君이 두 단체에서 떠나 반동할 줄랴 누가 알가. 本所 某民族的 反動團體와 충돌이 생겨 피차 우리가 快鬪를 계획하든 일 그 뽈세비기 운운하며 勞働者를 기만하는 彼 CS輩에 대항하여 大崎에서 투쟁하든 일 등을 君은 다- 기억하겟지. 나는 君과 갓치 하자 하며 또 하든 일의 기억이 인제 와서는 이저도 바렷거니와 전부 쓰자고도 아니한다.
姜君! 君은 去年 1월 某日報紙上에 『偶像問題의 관한 이론과 실제를 읽고』란 비판논문을 쓴 일이 잇섯지. 君의 프루-돈의 『경제학 모순의 체계』(Gysteine des contradiitton eionomiqve) 제 1권 內의 價値批判을 정당하다고 생각하는가! 군은 남의 글을 고의로 굽혀가지고 헷 뚱단지 가튼 소리 하며 그의 결점을 摘指하지 아니엿는가.
君의 인용구를 예시하자.＜87＞
『인류가 그 생활 과정에 잇서서 자연적인 또는 생산적인 것을 생산적 일반에 잇서서 보유되는 능력을 특히 사용가치라고 명명할 수 잇다』
姜君! 君 자체가 이것이 무슨 소리인지 알 수 잇니. 下의 원문과 나의 번역을 보게. (Or, Ia Capaint'e qv onttovs les prodvits, soit natv els,soit industriels, de servir ci Ia subsistavie de I'homme, se nonme partiwlieremeat valeur utilit'e)(p.92) 자연적이든지 인공적이든지 인류의 생활에 사용되는 생산물을 보유하는 기능(Capoiit'e)을 특히 사용가치라고 명칭한다』 그 외에도 誤譯誤論이 적지 아니하나 이것이 비판논문이 아닌 이상 더 쓸랴하지 안니한다.＜126＞
