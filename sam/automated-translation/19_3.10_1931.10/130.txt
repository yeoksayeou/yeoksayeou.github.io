Announcement of the "Samch'ŏlli Beauty" Results
						Samch'ŏllisa
14 Selected Individuals: Special Selection - 1 person, Second Selection - 3 people, Honorable Mentions - 10 people

Since our company began soliciting entries for the "Samch'ŏlli Beauty," born from our splendid rivers and mountains, the Korean peninsula, a large number of 326 application photos have arrived from both within and outside the country. Therefore, authorities in the aesthetic world (審美界) such as Messrs. Yi Kwangsu, Yŏm Sangsŏp, Kim Ansŏ, An Sŏkchu, Yi Sŭngman, Yi Ch'ŏngjŏn, Hŏ Yŏngsuk, Na Hyesŏk, Kim Illyŏp, Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, (and from our company, Kim Tonghwan, Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi) – writers, painters, doctors, and dancers – conducted solemn and fair judging over several days, and we now announce the results as follows.

Special Selection: Miss Ch'oe Chŏngwŏn, Age 18
Currently studying at Taejŏn Girls' Higher Common School, Ch'ungnam

Second Selection: Miss Pak Pubong, Age 17
Currently attending the Shanghai British Girls' High School in the Shanghai French Concession (上海 佛租界). A sports girl (스포쓰, 껄) who is a track and field and basketball player and won the All-China Women's Championship.
Birthplace is Siberia (西伯利亞), father is a Russian (露西亞) Army officer (of course, a Korean).

To the selected individuals, we will express our company's humble appreciation (微意) by presenting (進程하야) prizes, though they may be modest (略粗하온). (Scheduled for dispatch within October)
And to the 312 individuals who were not selected, we will present each with this month's issue of our magazine and simultaneously return the photos that were sent (보내섯든).
The judges' critiques for the 14 selected individuals, scenes from the "Samch'ŏlli Beauty" judging meeting, public opinion (世評) from social figures regarding our company's "Samch'ŏlli Beauty" announcement, and the judges' manuscripts on their "View of Korean Beauty" (朝鮮美人觀) will be published extensively and continued in next month's issue of this magazine. Please look forward to it (기다려어 주시압).

(Furthermore, if members of the Peninsula's society would also send critiques or impressions regarding the "Miss Korea" (미쓰, 코레아) announced this time, we would be grateful to include them (아울너) in next month's issue.)

Kyŏngsŏng Samch'ŏllisa Paek <39>

**Summary**

This article from the October 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine announces the results of its "Samch'ŏlli Beauty" (三千里一色) contest. It lists the names, ages, locations, and sometimes schools or brief biographical details of the 14 winners (1 special selection, 3 second selections, 10 honorable mentions) chosen from 326 entrants by a panel of prominent cultural figures. The magazine promises prizes for winners, returns photos to non-winners along with a copy of the current issue, and announces follow-up articles featuring judges' comments, public reactions, and essays on Korean beauty for the next issue.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it showcases an early form of a beauty contest ("Miss Korea") organized by a popular Korean magazine during the Japanese colonial period. The list of judges, including well-known writers, artists, and a dancer like Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, highlights the involvement of the cultural elite in shaping popular aesthetics. The diverse backgrounds of the contestants (e.g., one born in Siberia with a father in the Russian army, another a sports champion in Shanghai) reflect the mobility and transnational connections of some Koreans even under colonial rule, while the focus on education (listing schools attended) points to its perceived importance for modern women.
