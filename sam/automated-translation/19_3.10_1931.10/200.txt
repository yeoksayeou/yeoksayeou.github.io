Monopoly Products (專賣品)
The monopoly products of the Government-General of Chosŏn (朝鮮總督府) are four kinds: tobacco, ginseng, salt, and opium. Looking at each category for the past year:

Tobacco ― cultivated in 358 myeon, 70 gun, across 10 provinces, the manufactured quantity was 4,502,468,000 sticks, valued at 12,402,653 won.

Ginseng ― the harvested amount was 165,897 kŭn (斤), with sales reaching 2,481,998 won.

Salt ― salt fields covered 2,446 chŏngbo (町步), the production amount was 309,638,000 kŭn (斤), valued at 1,322,154 won. Besides this, there was privately manufactured salt (私製鹽) from 2,103 chŏngbo (町步), valued at 2,080,081 won.

Opium ― the cultivation area was 753 chŏng (町) and 9 tanbo (段步), the collected amount reached 400 kwan (貫).<53>

**Summary**

This short news item from 1931 lists the four main monopoly products controlled by the Japanese Government-General of Chosŏn: tobacco, ginseng, salt, and opium. It provides specific statistics from the previous year (likely 1930) detailing production quantities, areas under cultivation or production, and their monetary value in won. The article offers a stark glimpse into the colonial economic structure, showcasing the extent of state control over key commodities. For historians, the piece provides concrete data on the scale of these monopolies and is particularly revealing for its open inclusion and quantification of opium production and collection as a state-controlled enterprise during the colonial period.
