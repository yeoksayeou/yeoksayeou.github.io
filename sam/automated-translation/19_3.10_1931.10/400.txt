**Body Text Translation:**
Deep White Clouds (白雲深)
						P'ain (파인)

Deep White Clouds (白雲深)

As the white clouds pass,
that sky becomes wider.
The clouds in this heart,
when will they scatter?
Even those clouds of this land—
Oh, autumn has come,
but in the sky, only white clouds come and go.

Caution (조심)

When people passed on the main road,
like a pumpkin on the stone wall,
I only stuck my head out and hid quietly.<100>
I came cautiously like that.

At the sound of footsteps,
even if the spotted dog barked quickly,
so they'd think it barked at the moon,
I chose such cloudy nights to come.<101>
<100-101>

**Summary**

This entry features two short poems by P'ain. "Deep White Clouds" contrasts the vast, clear autumn sky after clouds pass with the persistent metaphorical "clouds" within the speaker's heart and over the land, expressing a lingering sadness or unresolved state despite the change of season. "Caution" depicts moving with extreme stealth, hiding from passersby and choosing cloudy nights to mask movement, suggesting fear and the need for secrecy. From a historical perspective, the poems subtly convey the atmosphere of anxiety and constraint likely experienced under Japanese colonial rule in 1931. The unresolved sorrow in the first poem and the fearful secrecy in the second could reflect the political climate and the restricted lives of Koreans during that period, making them potentially interesting for historians studying colonial-era sentiment and expression.
