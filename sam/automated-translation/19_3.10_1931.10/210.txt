Cossack Lullaby
						Kim Tongjin (Translator)
This song is a Cossack woman's child-watching song, originally by "Nerŭmonrop'u".

1
Pretty baby sleeps so well
Hush hush, lullaby
In mid-heaven, the bright moon
Above the cradle, our baby
Shall I tell an old tale?
Shall I sing a nice song?
Sleeps so well, pretty baby
Hush hush, lullaby

2
You know the future world too
What will you do in this harsh life?
Bravely shoulder a gun
Mount your horse with a smile
I'll make a saddle of silk brocade (명주나서)
And place it on the horse's back for you
Sleeps so well, precious baby
Hush hush, lullaby

3
You are a hero of the future
How could you forget the soul of the fatherland (조국혼)?
When sending you off to campaign
Do not wave your hand to stop me
On the night I send you away
What shall I do with sorrowful tears?
Sleeps so well, my angel
Hush hush, lullaby

4
My heart is impatient
When will you ever grow up?
During the day, I pray
At night, it is worry
After sending you to war
How will I live, wanting to see you?
You still don't know anything yet
Hush hush, lullaby

5
On the road you go to war
I will give you one token
Pray to God
And keep it hidden deep, deep
When in danger, take it out and look
And think of your mother
Sleeps so well, pretty baby
Hush hush, lullaby<54>

**Summary**

This article presents Kim Tongjin's 1931 Korean translation of a Cossack lullaby, originally attributed to "Nerŭmonrop'u" (Lermontov). The poem depicts a mother singing to her sleeping child, expressing love but also foreboding about the harsh future awaiting him, specifically the life of a Cossack warrior. She encourages bravery, patriotism ("soul of the fatherland"), and remembrance of her, while lamenting the eventual separation and her own worry. From a historical perspective, the publication of this poem during the Japanese colonial period is interesting. While ostensibly a foreign piece, its themes of inevitable hardship, martial valor, national spirit, and maternal sacrifice could have subtly resonated with Korean readers facing their own subjugated reality and uncertain future, offering a veiled space for contemplating such complex emotions. The focus on military preparedness and the "soul of the fatherland" within a non-Korean context might have been a permissible way to touch upon potentially sensitive nationalistic sentiments for historians studying cultural expression under colonial rule.

哥蕯克 자장
						金東進(번역자)
이 노래는 「네르몬로푸」의 原作인 哥蕯克 여인의 아해 보는 노래이다.

1
엡분 애기 잘도 잔다
자장 자장
중천에는 밝은 달님
요람 우엔 우리 애기
옛말이나 하야불가
조흔 노래 불너볼가
잘도 잔다 엡분 애기
자장 자장

2
압날 세상 너도 알지
험한 생애 어이하련
용감하게 총을 메고
웃으면서 말을 타라
명주나서 안장 지어
말 잔등에 치어주마
잘두 잔다 귀한 애기
자장 자장

3
너는 장래 영웅이다
조국혼을 이즐소냐
너희 출정 전송할 때
손 흔드러 막지 마라
너 떠보낸 그날 밤에
설흔 눈물 어히할고
잘도 잔다 내의 천사
자장 자장

4
내의 심사 조급하다
너 언제나 자라겟니
낫이 되면 긔도하고
밤이 되면 근심일다
너를 전쟁 보낸 뒤에
보고 십허 어히 살겐
아직 너야 무엇 알니
자장 자장

5
네가 전쟁 가는 길에
표적 하나 줄 것이니
하나님께 긔도하고
깁히 깁히 간수햇다
위급할 때 내여보고
네 어머니 생각해라
잘도 잔다 엡분 애기
자장 자장＜54＞
