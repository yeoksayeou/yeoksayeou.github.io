Announcement of the Results of the Public Recommendation Poll for the Presidents of Chosŏn Ilbo and Tonga Ilbo

This past spring, our company requested public recommendations from figures across the Peninsula for the presidents of the Chosŏn Ilbo and Tonga Ilbo, important institutions in Korean society. By the deadline set according to regulations, a large number of 1,032 votes were received from within the country and overseas. Believing this reflects, to some extent, the combined will of our society's populace, we hereby announce the results as follows.

However, due to unavoidable circumstances, we have tearfully had to suspend the announcement of the results for the public recommendation of the Singanhoe Central Committee Chairman. We ask for the deep understanding of all voters. Furthermore, because we are similarly unable to announce those publicly recommended as leading figures of the Singanhoe, due to related circumstances, we were unable to present the names of several well-known figures to the public view, resulting in the regrettable outcome of losing balance in the overall presentation of figures. However, as these circumstances are truly unavoidable, we are hereby announcing at least the results for the presidents of the two newspapers, which is possible for now.

**Summary**

The Samch'ŏlli magazine announces the results of a public recommendation poll it conducted for the presidencies of two major Korean newspapers, the Chosŏn Ilbo and Tonga Ilbo, receiving 1,032 votes from Koreans at home and abroad in 1931. An Chaehong received the most votes for the Chosŏn Ilbo, while Song Chinu led for the Tonga Ilbo. The magazine apologetically explains that results for the Singanhoe (New Korea Society) Central Committee Chairman and other leaders are withheld due to "unavoidable circumstances," subtracting those votes from the total count. A list of 50 voters chosen by lottery to receive a free subscription is also provided. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects public opinion (or at least the opinion of the magazine's readership) on prominent nationalist figures during the colonial period, showcasing who held popular sway regarding leadership of key cultural institutions like newspapers. The explicit withholding of the Singanhoe results, citing vague "unavoidable circumstances," strongly hints at the political pressures and censorship prevalent under Japanese rule, making the poll itself and its partial publication a subtle act of navigating colonial constraints.

朝鮮日報 東亞日報 社長公薦 結果發表
今春 본사에서 조선사회의 중요기관인 朝鮮日報 及 東亞日報 社長의 공천을 널니 半島人士에게 요청하엿든 바 규정대로 모집기일까지 전부 1,032 표의 多數한 투표가 海內海外로부터 잇섯사외다. 이것이 어느 정도로 우리들 사회민중의 의사를 결합 반영한 것이라고 밋고 이에 그 결과를 如左히 발표하나이다.

단, 신간회 중앙위원장에 대한 공천결과는 부득이한 사정으로 눈물먹으며 그 발표를 중지하게 되엿사오니 投票諸氏의 심심한 양해를 바라거니와 더욱 이와 가치 신간회의 지도급 인물의 公薦諸氏를 발표하지 못하게 되눀” 관계상 幾多 知名人士의 氏名을 일제히 대중의 시야에 제공하지 못하게 되어 전체의 인물배치상 균형을 이른 유감된 결과를 초래하엿나이다. 그러나 그 역부득이한 사정임으로 위선 가능한 兩 新聞社長 만이라도 이에 발표하는 바외다.
