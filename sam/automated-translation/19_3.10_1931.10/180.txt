Sky of the Hometown
						Scottish Folk Song, P'ain (translator)

The evening sky clears
And the autumn wind blows

Beneath the clear moon
A cricket cries

Thinking of it, it's so distant
The sky of my hometown

Ah, my parents
What might they be doing now<51>

**Summary**

This short poem, presented as a translation of a Scottish folk song by someone named P'ain, expresses a deep sense of longing for one's hometown. The imagery of a clear autumn evening sky, the sound of a cricket, and the distant moon evokes nostalgia and prompts the speaker to wonder about their parents back home. From a historical perspective, the publication of a translated foreign folk song focusing on homesickness during the Japanese colonial period is interesting. It could simply reflect universal human emotions, but it might also subtly resonate with Koreans' feelings of displacement or longing for a lost national 'home' under colonial rule, even if expressed through the medium of a foreign text.
