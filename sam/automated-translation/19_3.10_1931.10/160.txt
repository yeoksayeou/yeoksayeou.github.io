**Summary**

This article lists the major shareholders of three significant banks operating in colonial Korea in 1931: the Chosŏn Bank, the Chosŏn Industrial Bank, and the Chosŏn Commercial Bank. It details the number of shares held by key entities, including the Japanese Governor-General, the Yi Royal Household Office, other banks, and prominent Korean individuals. The article is significant for revealing the financial structure and control mechanisms during the colonial period, showing the heavy involvement of the Japanese colonial government and institutions, alongside participation from the former Korean royal family's office and certain Korean elites in the colonial economy's key financial institutions. Historians might find the specific share distributions useful for understanding capital concentration and the intertwining of colonial power with local financial structures.
