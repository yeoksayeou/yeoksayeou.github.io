편집후기

반도인사 여러분의 격려와 질책 밋헤서 본지는 금월호로서 3주년을 맛슴니다. 그동안 이만치라도 성장하게 한 諸씨의 후의를 深謝함니다.

來월호부터는 배전의 용기와 정성을 가지고 조흔 잡지를 꾸미어 드리겟사오니 끗까지 돌보아 주시압.

今월호인 이 특대호는 다수원고가 그만 부득이 게재하지 못하게 된 것이 잇서 예정보다 빈약하게 되엇슴을 붓그럽게 암니다.-(파인)-

이번달부터 여러분 압헤 나왓슴니다. 「삼천리」를 돌보아 주시는 마음으로 이후 잘못된 점이 잇슬지라도 만히 편달과 지도를 주시압. 더구나 녀성 여러분과는 「만국부인」란을 통하여 서로 친애하는 사이가 되고 십사외다.-(崔貞熙)-＜136＞

Editor's Postscript

Under the encouragement and criticism of the figures of the Peninsula (반도인사), this journal greets its 3rd anniversary with this month's issue. We deeply thank (深謝함니다) the kindness of the various people (諸씨) who have allowed us to grow even this much during this time.

Starting from next month's issue (來월호), we will strive to create a good (조흔) magazine for you with doubled courage and sincerity, so please look after us until the end (끗까지).

We are ashamed (붓그럽게 암니다) that this month's issue (今월호), this special large issue, turned out poorer than planned as many manuscripts unfortunately could not be published.-(P'ain)-

Starting this month, I have come before you all. With the same heart that looks after "Samch'ŏlli," please offer much encouragement and guidance (편달과 지도) even if there are faults hereafter. Furthermore, I wish to become close friends with the women (녀성) readers through the "World Women" (만국부인) section.-(Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi)-<136>

**Summary**

This editor's postscript consists of two short notes. The first, signed "P'ain," marks the third anniversary of the *Samch'ŏlli* magazine, thanking readers for their support, apologizing that the current special issue is smaller than intended due to unpublished manuscripts, and promising improvements for future issues. The second note is by Ch'oe Chŏnghŭi, introducing herself as newly joining the magazine, asking for reader guidance, and expressing a particular desire to connect with female readers through the "Man'guk Puin" (World Women) section. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into Korean periodical publishing in 1931, including acknowledgments of reader feedback, apologies for content limitations possibly hinting at external pressures or resource constraints, the use of pen names, and the notable presence and specific address to female readership by a female staff member, highlighting efforts towards gendered content and engagement during the colonial era. The older Korean orthography is also significant.
