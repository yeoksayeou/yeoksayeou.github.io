社告
						삼천리사

Song Chun-do (宋俊道)
We have specially dispatched the aforementioned individual to the Hamnam region this summer, and hope for everyone's special patronage.

Chŏng Tong-sŏk (鄭東錫)
We have specially dispatched the aforementioned individual to the Hamgyŏng area, and hope for everyone's special patronage.
August 1st
Samch'ŏllisa <73>

**Summary**

This brief notice from the Samch'ŏlli Company (삼천리사) announces the dispatch of two individuals, Song Chun-do to the South Hamgyŏng (Hamnam) region and Chŏng Tong-sŏk to the Hamgyŏng area (likely encompassing both North and South Hamgyŏng), requesting special support or patronage (愛護) from readers for these representatives. For historians, this notice provides insight into the operational activities of a major Korean magazine during the colonial period, specifically its use of dispatched personnel likely for subscription, reporting, or distribution purposes in specific regions. It highlights the logistical network required to maintain a publication business across the Korean peninsula under Japanese rule.
