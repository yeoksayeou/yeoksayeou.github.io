**The Eternal Secret History of the Chŏn Hyŏp Faction That Rampaged Across the Three Thousand Ri; The Truth of This Incident, Which for a Time Captured the World's Attention, Revealed After Ten Years**
**A Recent Grand Historical Drama of Casting the Die (1)**

One night—it was a cold winter night, November 9th. Detective Tosi, who was guarding the outside of His Highness XXXX's residence, wrapped his ears with the collar of his overcoat and, while patrolling secretly several times, suddenly discovered the shadow of a person slipping out silently from the back gate of the XX residence.
"Ah!"
So late at night! Thinking this, Detective Tosi hid behind the two people and followed them.
The two people, not dreaming that such a pursuer was attached to their backs, kept their bodies close together, talking in low voices about something as they walked towards the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch. Looking closely, one person bowed their body each time they spoke, in an attitude like an attendant addressing their master.
"What could this be?" At the moment he thought this, Detective Tosi realized the figure from behind resembled His Highness XX. "That's right, it's definitely His Highness X!"
As he thought this, at that very moment—near the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch—he completely lost sight of His Highness X's figure.
The Police Authorities, upon receiving Detective Tosi's report, were astonished by the unexpected situation.
However, it was too absurd to immediately believe in the disappearance of His Highness XXXX, who was accorded the courtesy due to XXXX, based solely on the report of a single Detective Tosi. The authorities could not help but hesitate about its truthfulness.
No matter how much privilege they had as government officials, it was improper to lightly conduct surveillance regarding His Highness's personal affairs. Moreover, the residence grounds were vast, with 6 or 7 detached palaces, and the police officers providing security were restricted only to guarding the square in front of the main entrance and the perimeter of the residence; entering the house, let alone the rear garden, was absolutely forbidden.
Of course, His Highness X had Secretary Yi XX, who handled official duties, and an Aide-de-camp attached to him, but the secretary was only allowed to enter the inner residence when granted an audience for official business. Also, the Aide-de-camp, by long-standing custom, was in the position of a subject and absolutely could not do anything that might offend His Highness X's venerable thoughts.
Under these circumstances, it was entirely difficult for the authorities to investigate His Highness X's movements.<44> However, if, as per Detective Tosi's report, His Highness X had indeed disappeared! How could the authorities possibly compensate for their negligence? Needless to say, they would have no choice but to rescue him from the hands of the Shanghai Provisional Government.
Therefore, the Government-General authorities immediately summoned Secretary OO Yi XX and ordered him to visit the residence to inquire after His Highness X's well-being and secretly investigate, in order to ascertain the truth of Detective Tosi's report.
Secretary OO Yi XX, surprised by the gravity of the situation, immediately paid respects at the XX Public Residence and requested an audience, but His Highness X was reportedly slightly indisposed, and the audience was not permitted, so he had no choice but to leave the residence.
The top leadership of the Government-General, upon hearing Secretary XX's report, could only look at each other with ominous premonitions.

"If His Highness X is not in residence, it's a major incident concerning governance and consequently a huge issue of the Government-General's responsibility."
The Governor-General spoke, his white-haired, youthful face clouded with deep worry.
"Mr. XX, sorry to trouble you, but please visit the residence once more and inquire about His Highness X's movements. Whether he is in residence or has gone out—that is the crux of this incident."
The Governor-General stated that, this being shortly after the defection of Baron Kim Ka-jin from XX, they needed to quickly ascertain the truth.
Secretary XX again sped off in a car and visited the XX residence.
"Although His Highness X is said to be indisposed, with rumors and speculation spreading throughout the country today, I wish to personally pay respects to His Highness X and inquire after his health."
Secretary XX pleaded several times. The reply then came:
"His Highness went out through the back gate late last night, accompanied by the servant Kim Sam-bok, but he should return shortly, so please do not worry."
The reply, saying he seemed likely to return, was vaguely evasive. And he could sense an air of unease flowing among the various people inside the residence. Secretary XX could guess everything.
The Government-General, realizing Detective Tosi's report was true, became truly tense.
Within that day, urgent telegrams were dispatched in all directions from the Higher Police Section.
Now, emergency lines were stretched across the entire land, as densely as a spider's web, without a single gap.
His Highness XXXX Disappeared!
Thus, the authorities, fearing that something might happen to the person of His Highness X, who might have been lured and abducted by radical Koreans, desperately extended their search efforts with bloodshot eyes.
In the early morning of the next day, November 10th, a report reached the investigation headquarters from a team searching a certain <45> gate in Kongp'yŏng-dong, Seoul: "An empty house formerly inhabited by a kisaeng is the secret meeting place of the conspiracy group!"
Along with the authorities' activities to secure His Highness XXXX, special service agencies throughout Manchuria were ordered to mobilize and undertake special vigilance against the Shanghai Provisional Government.
Nevertheless, why did His Highness XXXX carelessly go out quietly late at night—
And to what place? By whose machinations was His Highness spending his days in what manner? The author will first write about the motive for His Highness X's outing.
Those knowledgeable will remember that when the XX Incident of Taishō 8 (1919) was allowed to be reported in newspapers, the dispute over the fishing rights issue in the XX coastal area, the sole source of income for the XXX family, was reported.
The origin of His Highness X's disappearance stemmed from that fishing rights issue.
The fishing rights of the XXX family extended to the entire coast of Kyŏngsang Province. Regarding the concessions for these fishing grounds, many figures were maneuvering during the period of financial speculation. Moreover, within the financially strapped XXX family, there was discussion of mortgaging a part of it to alleviate the temporary financial hardship.
One day—
A neatly dressed person who came to visit the XXX family presented a letter to His Highness X through the former attendant, Chŏng Un-bok.
"What kind of person is it?"
His Highness asked tersely.
"Yes, he is Yi Min-ha, an agent of a wealthy man named Sŏk Tong."
Chŏng replied respectfully.
"Ah, yes. Did he speak of arranging 30,000 won?"
"Yes, he intends to arrange a loan of 30,000 won using the fishing rights as collateral."
His Highness X listened to Chŏng's reply, considered deeply, and then, hearing Chŏng Un-bok's story that accepting the offer was the only option for the current predicament,
"Do as you see fit,"
he said simply. Chŏng Un-bok, overwhelmed, withdrew from the royal presence and returned to the separate room where Yi Min-ha was waiting.
That Yi Min-ha appeared gentle and sincere at first glance. Chŏng, conveying His Highness's intention, decided to discuss the details and then seek His Highness's approval.
"His Highness has just given his permission, but this matter involves various troublesome procedures, so I wish to conclude it secretly..."
Chŏng Un-bok said, looking at Yi Min-ha's face.
"That is what we desire as well. Then how shall we handle the cash transaction?"
"Well—" Seeing Chŏng Un-bok thinking, Yi Min-ha smiled faintly and said,
"Then how about this? My master could present it directly to His Highness X at my master's secret residence—"
"Well..."
"And if you come around dawn, I believe you can come without anyone noticing."
"At dawn—His Highness, the noble X?"
"Yes, I think that way would be safer than during the day."
"Hmm, that sounds plausible. Then I shall inquire about His Highness's intentions."
Chŏng Un-bok again attended upon His Highness X and relayed Yi Min-ha's words.
"At dawn! That might be good too—"
His Highness spoke thus without any sign of suspicion, so Chŏng Un-bok conveyed this intention to Yi Min-ha.
"Is that so? Then at that time, we shall receive His Highness's signature on a simple document."
"Then which day would be good? The sooner the better, I think." "Let me see," Yi Min-ha seemed to think about something, then said,
"How about the night of November 9th?"
"November 9th..." Chŏng Un-bok also thought for a moment,<46>
"Let's decide on that day. If any circumstances arise on our side, we will send a messenger to inform you."
The two men chatted for a while after that, and the man named Yi Min-ha stated the location of Sŏk Tong's secret residence and took his leave.
Thus, after November 9th, His Highness X, accompanied only by his attendant Kim Sam-bok, quietly went out through the back gate.
Detective Tosi, who was on duty guarding the residence that night, unexpectedly spotted His Highness X's figure from behind.
His Highness X had proceeded to the secret residence of the wealthy man Sŏk Tong, located near the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch.
His Highness XXXX, having gone to Sŏk Tong's secret residence, rested briefly in a separate room, then had Kim Sam-bok negotiate with Sŏk Tong's agent, Yi Min-ha.
Kim Sam-bok, understanding His Highness's intention, negotiated a few points with Yi Min-ha, and as a result,
"Then please give us the money immediately,"
he said. At that, Yi Min-ha made a troubled face and said,
"Actually, I haven't brought it today."
"What? You haven't brought it?"
Kim Sam-bok scolded Yi Min-ha's dishonesty in reneging on his previous words, especially since His Highness had come personally.
"Yes, the truth is, the 30,000 won has been prepared in Kongp'yŏng-dong, but I apologize, I must ask His Highness to go there."
"Kongp'yŏng-dong! Hmm, so if we just go there, will it surely be settled?"
"Yes, there's no doubt. Fortunately, I've also called for a car."
"Wait a moment, I will speak to His Highness."
Kim Sam-bok went out grumbling, then came back in and said,
"His Highness has specially said he will go."
The party, having left Sŏk Tong's secret residence, sped away in a car with the hood pulled down deep.
After a while, His Highness X's party arrived in Kongp'yŏng-dong and was guided into a certain house.
The house was quiet, like an empty house. Kim Sam-bok felt uneasy. However, upon being guided inside, he saw Chŏng Un-bok's figure there, along with 2 or 3 others who came out to greet them, which somewhat relieved him.
Once seated, Yi Min-ha, observing proper etiquette, spoke.
"I, Yi Min-ha, as the agent of Sŏk Tong, have brought the sum of 30,000 won here."
He took out bundles of banknotes from a moneybag and offered them for His Highness's inspection.
"However, Your Highness! We subjects wish to inquire what thoughts Your Highness holds regarding the current XX."
Yi Min-ha's eyes shone intensely. His Highness X, utterly surprised by the unexpected words, remained silent and fixed his gaze on Yi Min-ha.
"Your Highness! Then allow me, Min-ha, agent of the wealthy Sŏk Tong, to remove my mask and speak. Your Highness! I am the one known as Chŏn Hyŏp of the Shanghai Provisional Government."
His Highness X let out a low "Ah!" in astonishment and stood up. Kim Sam-bok moved closer to His Highness X, resolved to protect him with his own body should anyone attempt harm.
"Your Highness! We are by no means attempting to harm Your venerable person. We subjects wish to escort Your Highness to the Shanghai Provisional Government and make you our leader (Original text abbreviated)."
Even His Highness X, who usually remained unmoved by material or emotional matters, upon thinking that Chŏn Hyŏp, whose name was known throughout all Korea, was under the alias of Yi Min-ha..., his complexion became unusual.
Chŏn Hyŏp, seeing this, even smiled faintly and said,
"Your Highness! There is no wealthy man named Sŏk Tong anywhere in the world. However, we subjects will use any stratagem to escort Your Highness. Your Highness! Understanding our humble intentions, please resolve to escape to Shanghai."<47>
However, His Highness Kim(?) did not utter a single word. Kim Sam-bok, his voice trembling from the sheer disrespect, said,
"Insolent fellow! Do you think His Highness would go with the likes of you?"
Chŏn Hyŏp, hearing those words, grinned and said,
"Hmph, don't talk nonsense (Original text abbreviated)."
Kim Sam-bok looked pitifully and bowed his head before His Highness X.
"Your Highness! If you do not make your decision now (Original text abbreviated), death is the only final option for Your Highness."
His Highness X, in his agitation, could not support himself, and Kim Sam-bok supported him. At that time, His Highness spoke these words in a low voice:
"I do not think like you people."
Everyone was startled, but Chŏn Hyŏp alone remained unmoved and, turning backwards, raised one hand as if giving a military signal. Then, suddenly, stalwart men like Song Se-ho, Han Ki-dong, Yi Ŭl-kyu, and Chŏng Nam-yong were there, each holding a revolver.
His Highness X stared intently at the scene, and seeing even Chŏng Un-bok, his most trusted former attendant, apologetically holding the muzzle of a gun, said,
"Chŏng! Even you?" Chŏn Hyŏp raised his voice again,
"Your Highness! If Your Highness does not consent, even Your Highness, we will (Original text abbreviated). Your Highness! Please speak. Please speak clearly."
Chŏn Hyŏp pressed His Highness. His Highness, with all due respect, reached the peak of confusion.
Danger approached his esteemed person with every passing moment.
"Your Highness! Your answer!"
Chŏn Hyŏp, his eyes changed, comes closer, pressing more urgently.
Around His Highness's person, the muzzles of about ten revolvers glinted—.
Kim Sam-bok alone could do nothing. The situation grew increasingly desperate.
"Your Highness, your answer!"
Chŏn Hyŏp again pressed for a reply. His Highness, now at the extremity of his confusion, as if in a dream, let out a sigh of despair, "Ah!"
Chŏn Hyŏp, hearing His Highness's groan-like sound, said,
"Your Highness! Ah, do you consent? (Original text abbreviated) Baron Kim Ka-jin is also in Shanghai. You cannot imagine how eagerly our comrades await Your Highness."
Chŏn Hyŏp, interpreting His Highness's lack of any reply as consent, consulted among themselves on the method of escape.
Thereupon, His Highness X was made to wear a mask and ride in a rickshaw, and was abducted through the late-night streets to a private house in Ŭnp'yŏngmyŏn, outside Ch'angŭimun Gate.
The next day, November 10th, having humbly disguised himself in commoner's clothes, he was escorted by Song Se-ho, Yi Ŭl-kyu, Chŏng Nam-yong, and Han Ki-dong, boarded a third-class train at Susaek Station, and disembarked at Antung County Station.
Therefore, the investigation team that raided the conspiracy group's secret meeting place in Kongp'yŏng-dong early on the 11th arrived at an empty house.
The XX Provincial Police Department, upon receiving the urgent telegram from the Government-General Police Affairs Bureau, devoted all its efforts to searching for those who had infiltrated from the Shanghai Provisional Government.
Then, on the 10th, they received another telegram from the Police Affairs Section.
—"There are signs suggesting His Highness XXXX traveled north by train. Arrange surveillance along the railway line."
Such was the meaning of the telegram.
The XX Provincial Police Department suddenly summoned off-duty police officers and managed to arrange surveillance along the entire line within that night.
That morning—on the morning of November 11th, Police Lieutenant OO of the XX Provincial Police Department, along with a dozen plainclothes officers, mingled among the passengers at Antung County Station, casting their net.
As the train from Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) entered the platform, exhaling white steam, Police Lieutenant OO, having sent two or three subordinates into the train disguised as passengers, stood at the ticket gate as if waiting to meet someone.
Passengers disembarked, seemingly cold. At that moment,<48> Police Lieutenant OO noticed five shabbily dressed men approaching the ticket gate.
"Oh!" The Lieutenant almost cried out. This was because among them, he discovered one person dressed in rough clothes who bore an uncanny resemblance to His Highness XXXX.
The Lieutenant signaled his subordinates with his eyes and approached the five men dressed as farmers near the ticket gate.
"Excuse me, may I ask—"
As he spoke, he paid close attention to the man standing in the very center. Oh, it was unmistakably His Highness X. Though disguised in rough clothes, there was no doubt it was His Highness.
"Your Highness! We have come to escort you!"
Police Lieutenant OO respectfully took a step back in surprise, ignored Song Se-ho and the others, stood at attention, and saluted.
"I am Police Lieutenant OO of the XX Provincial Police Department."
Before the Lieutenant finished speaking, a puff of white smoke rose, and Song Se-ho's right hand went up.
"Bang—Bang—"
Subsequently, revolvers from the hands of the four men fired towards the Lieutenant.
"Wheeeeee!"
The sound of a whistle shook the morning air of Antung County Station.
"Big trouble!"
A dozen police officers dropped to the ground and began exchanging fire.
During this, Police Lieutenant OO approached His Highness X.
"Your Highness, quickly, quickly!"
Thus, almost taking his hand, he guided His Highness to another location.
On the pavement, the gunfire continued fiercely.
"Don't kill them! Capture even one!"
The police officers, emboldened by Lieutenant OO's voice, rose up, shouted "Waaah!", and charged forward.
The culprits sprang up and began to flee.
"Don't let them escape!"
Pursuit! Pursuit! Finally, they captured Chŏng Nam-yong.
Lieutenant OO immediately called for a car, took His Highness aboard, and sped towards the police department.
An urgent telegram was sent from the XX Police Department to the Government-General.
Meanwhile, the Government-General, learning from the telegram from the XX Provincial Police Department that His Highness XXXX had been secured, was greatly invigorated and immediately ordered Police Inspector OOO, along with several officials for His Highness's security escort, to proceed rapidly to Sinŭiju.
Thus, His Highness XXXX, safely escorted by Police Inspector OOO and others, disembarked at OO Station the next morning, the 12th, and was immediately taken by car to Noksŏnjŏng, located on the cliff of the Government-General Official Residence grounds.
This matter was handled with whirlwind speed, catching the public completely off guard. By ensuring His Highness was securely protected, making it absolutely impossible for those radicals to approach him, His Highness's whereabouts remained unknown to the general public for quite a long time.

**Drama Outside Ch'angŭimun Gate**
The conspiracy group headquarters at the Government-General Police Affairs Section, buoyed by the success of rescuing His Highness XXXX from the clutches of evil and securing him, dedicated all efforts to pursuing the radicals.
Following the trail of His Highness X—the strange house near the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch said to be the secret residence of the wealthy Sŏk Tong, the empty house in Kongp'yŏng-dong—they discovered around 2 PM on the day before His Highness's return that he was hiding in a private house outside Ch'angŭimun Gate.
Ch'angŭimun Gate commands a vital point of Seoul's northern gate, flanked by steep mountain paths. His Highness had been temporarily detained in that private house, from where he proceeded to Susaek Station, boarded a train, and traveled north. A detective finally discovered that location. Since several people inside seemed to be quietly discussing something, fearing they might all escape if he rushed in alone, the detective immediately reported it to the investigation headquarters.<49>
"Well, this is an unexpected discovery!" The investigation headquarters immediately summoned over 20 robust plainclothes detectives from various stations, equipped them with pistols for self-defense, divided them into 3 cars, and dispatched them at once.
(This time, I will capture the ringleader!)
Burning with ambition for glory, the 20-odd men stopped their cars at Ch'angŭimun Gate and advanced on foot along the 10-ri mountain path in a single line, rushing towards the private house.
"Yikes!"
The detective at the front let out a slight cry and stopped.
"What is it?"
"It's quite strange."
"What?"
"Look at that!"
What the detective pointed to was a child playing along the path, who, upon seeing their shadows, fled like an arrow.
"What—that?"
The group laughed and started walking again.
"No!"
He offered a slight objection.
"If my thinking isn't wrong, I can say this. That conspiracy group, to guard against our raid, might have bribed children to use them as an intelligence network."
His words strongly resonated in the hearts of the group.
"Perhaps that's possible."
"Let's go!"
The team of 20 immediately broke into a run.
The conspiracy group, alerted to the raid by the child's report, had stationed three men outside the house.
"Be careful!"
The team approached the private house, cautioning each other. However, the suspicious figures at the private house seemed, strangely, almost pleading, as if waiting for them. "Now—" Thinking they shouldn't hesitate, they closed in from all sides and arrested them without difficulty. They immediately entered the house, but nobody was there anymore. Had they escaped? The group returned to the front where the three men were.
"What's your name?"
"Yes, I am Kim Sam-bok, attendant to His Highness XXXX."
"What, Kim Sam-bok!"
"And you!"
"Chŏng Un-bok."
"And who are you?"
"Uh, I'm the owner of this house."
The group felt deflated. Was there anyone else? At that moment, the three men pointed towards the mountainside opposite.
Ah—there, a sturdy man dressed as a porter was climbing over the rocks, attempting to escape.
"Hey, don't let him get away!"
The detectives, burning with ambition, all dashed towards that spot.
"Bang! Bang!"
Warning pistol shots fired towards the sky.
"Come down!"
"If you don't come down, we'll shoot!"
Whether he couldn't hear the detectives' shouts or not, he climbed even faster.
"Letting him escape is a disgrace to the capital's detectives!"
The group chased like monkeys. The suspicious man glanced back briefly, then tried to flee along the rocks.
However, he was no match for the numerous, monkey-like detectives. He was quickly surrounded.
"Alright, come down—"
The captured suspicious man was dragged down.
"What's your name?" The suspicious man kept his mouth firmly shut and didn't say a word.
"Hmph, won't talk—"
The detectives inferred that this man held an important position.
A body search was immediately conducted. Around his waist, he carried dozens of blasting caps (雷管), and a Mauser-type pistol was found concealed on his person.
"Chŏng! Who is this man!"
Chŏng Un-bok answered, trembling.<50>
"It's Hwang Ch'ang-nyul."
Thus, the detectives brought the four men back to the investigation headquarters.
According to Kim Sam-bok, His Highness XXXX's attendant, the bundles of banknotes His Highness inspected at the public house in Kongp'yŏng-dong had real banknotes only on the front and back, with the inside filled entirely with newspaper scraps. Even the investigating authorities clicked their tongues at their vicious and cunning methods.

**Arrest of Ringleader Chŏn Hyŏp**
Snowflakes fluttered down heavily. The endlessly visible streets were covered in white silver. The thatched houses of Koreans on the outskirts were buried in snow.
As the investigation into the conspiracy of the Shanghai Provisional Government progressed, the tension at the investigation headquarters intensified further.
Based on the confessions of Chŏng Nam-yong, arrested at Antung County Station, and Hwang Ch'ang-nyul and Chŏng Un-bok, captured outside Ch'angŭimun Gate, it was learned that the one who abducted Baron Kim Ka-jin was Yi Chong-uk.
However, even fellow members of their group did not know Yi Chong-uk's address. But the authorities, knowing he was hiding within Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) city limits, began activities to raid their base.
The snow, seemingly pressing down on people's hearts, fell without the slightest pause. The anxious night deepened progressively, and even the bustling streets of Kyŏngsŏng fell silent, with footsteps ceasing. The dim, snow-covered streets—the night deepening flavorlessly—not even the sound of dogs barking could be heard, only the snow falling relentlessly.
On the street where human footsteps had ceased, suddenly a human shadow appeared. The human trace walked briskly, then, perhaps seeing something, turned around and ran away.
"Ah!"
A detective, coincidentally on secret patrol, witnessed the strange behavior and immediately gave chase through the snow. The suspicious person disappeared around a certain street corner.
"Hmph."
The detective stood in the falling snow, looking all around, but the shadow of the suspicious person was ultimately nowhere to be seen.
If he had paid a little more attention and searched that area, he might have discovered the strange person's footprints left on the snow, but they were too focused on chasing the culprit to notice.
That person, unaware of this, walked trudgingly. Occasionally looking back, they found nothing unusual.
They walked for about half an hour. The Korean farmer briefly stopped, looked around in all directions, and disappeared into a large residence.
"This strange house, this strange Korean farmer."
The detectives stood before the gate, looking at each other.
"Said he was going to the doctor, hehehe."
The detective who spoke first chuckled contemptuously. Now is not the time to hesitate. They would immediately split into two teams and storm this strange house.
"Open up!"

They broke a windowpane and rushed in.
Inside the house was pitch dark. From within, *tang*, a bullet whizzed past their ears.
"Bang, bang!"
The enemy was armed. The detectives pressed themselves flat against the wall and rushed towards the source of the sound.
"Got him!"
An agile detective caught a man. Encouraged by this, the detectives attacked all at once.
The electric lights were turned on, revealing three men tied up there.
That night, they had rushed into Yi Chong-uk's hideout. Thus, they were able to capture the conspiracy leader Chŏn Hyŏp, along with Yi Chong-uk and Song Se-ho.
Interrogation began immediately at the investigation headquarters, and the next morning, leaders Yi Ŭl-kyu, Song Se-ho(?)*, and Yang Chin were apprehended. Afterwards, it was said that the Shanghai Provisional Government, located in the French Concession in Shanghai, having lost its central figures, would be rebuilt by Na Ch'ang-hŏn, who had escaped from the private house outside Ch'angŭimun Gate, but now (Original text abbreviated).<51>

**Summary**

This 1932 article details the alleged 1919 plot by Korean independence activists, referred to as the "Chŏn Hyŏp faction" associated with the Shanghai Provisional Government, to escort Prince Ŭi (Yi Kang, referred to as X下) out of Korea to become a figurehead for the independence movement. It describes how the activists, using deception involving a fake loan, lured the Prince out of his residence, took him to hideouts in Seoul (Kongp'yŏng-dong, outside Ch'angŭimun Gate), and then attempted to transport him via train towards Manchuria. The plot was foiled by colonial police, particularly through surveillance leading to an armed confrontation at Antung Station where the Prince was "rescued" and key activists were arrested. The article portrays the activists as cunning and radical conspirators and the Japanese colonial authorities and police as vigilant protectors who swiftly dismantled the plot.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its clear pro-colonial bias, depicting independence activists negatively ("radicals," "conspirators," "vicious methods") while glorifying police actions. It reveals details (albeit from a biased viewpoint) about the methods of both the independence movement (using aliases like Chŏn Hyŏp for Yi Chong-uk, deception, network of safe houses, attempts to co-opt royal figures) and the colonial surveillance state (use of detectives, informants like Tosi, telegram networks, railway monitoring, armed police raids). Historians might find the specific names (even obscured ones like X下), locations, and the detailed narrative of the police operation useful, while critically analyzing its perspective and potential inaccuracies or embellishments typical of propaganda from that era. The casual mention of Baron Kim Ka-jin's earlier successful defection provides context for the authorities' heightened state of alert.

잡지명	삼천리 제4권 제8호
발행일	1932년 08월 01일
기사제목	橫行 三千里하든 全協一派의 千古秘史, 한동안 一世의 耳目을 끄을든 이 사건의 眞相이 十年 後에 判明되엇다
기사형태	세태비평

橫行 三千里하든 全協一派의 千古秘史, 한동안 一世의 耳目을 끄을든 이 사건의 眞相이 十年 後에 判明되엇다
乾坤 一擲의 최근 大史劇(1),

一 어느 날 밤-그것은 치운 겨울인 11월 9일 밤이엇다. XXXX下의 邸外를 경계하고 잇든 密偵 土師某는 「오-바」의 목어리로 귀를 싸고 여러 차레 密行하든 중 돌연히 XX邸 뒷문으로부터 소리업시 빠저가는 사람 그림자를 발견하엿다.
「아이!」
이러케 밤이 깁헛는데! 하면서 土師某는 두 사람의 뒤로 숨어서 미행하엿다.
이러한 미행자가 자기의 뒤에 부터잇는지는 꿈에도 생각지 안는 두 사람은 몸을 밧삭 각가히 하여 무엇인가 나즉한 소리로 이약이하면서 明月舘 支店잇는 데로 거러가고 잇섯다. 자세히 본즉 한 사람은 이약이할 때마다 몸을 굽히어 從者가 주인에게 대하는 것 가튼 태도엇다.
「웬일일가」하고 생각한 그 순간 土師某는 그 뒷모양이 XX下에 갓가우신 것을 깨다럿다.   「올치 올해 확실히 X下다!」
하고 생각하자 그 순간 -明月舘 支店 부근에서 X下의 모양을 끗끗내 이러버리고 말엇다.
警務當局은 土師某의 보고를 밧자 사태의 의외에 경악하엿다.
그러치만 XXXX로서의 禮遇를 賜하는 XXXX下의 御失踪을 1個의 土師某의 보고로서 곳 밋기에는 너무 황당하엿다. 當局은 그 진위에 躊躇치 안을 수 업섯다.
아모리 官憲으로서의 특권이 잇다할지라도 輕輕히 殿下의 御身邊에 대하야 御시찰하기는 穩當을 일는 일이라. 더구나 邸內는 廣大하고 別宮도 6,7個所잇서 警衛하는 경찰관은 玄關 前의 광장과 邸의 주위의 경계에만 제한되여 家屋 內는 물론 后庭에 조차 드러가기를 절대로 금지되여 잇다.
물론 X下의 御신변에는 공무를 처리할 李XX 사무관과 御附武官이 잇스나 사무관은 공무로써 拜謁을 許하는 이외 함부로 內邸에 드러옴을 허락치 안는다. 又 御附武官은 古來로부터의 습관으로 臣隷의 입장에 잇서 X下의 尊慮를 모독하는 일은 절대로 할 수 업는 바이다.
이러한 정세의 가운데 X下의 御動靜을 검찰하는 일은 當局으로서는 전혀 곤란한＜44＞ 일이엇다. 그러치만 土師某의 보고와 如히 X下의 御失踪!의 경우에는 當局의 실태는 무엇으로써 갑흐려는가. 더 말할 나위 업시 上海 假政府의 손으로부터 구하여 나오시도록 할밧게 업는터이다.
그럼으로 총독부 當局에서는 즉시 OO李XX 사무관을 招致하여 土師某의 보고의 진위를 캐기 때문에 邸內에 參上식혀 X下의 御氣嫌을 奉伺하여서 暗暗裡에 검찰하시기를 命하엿다.
OO李XX 사무관은 事態의 중대함에 놀나서 直時 XX公邸에 奉伺하여 拜謁을 願하엿스나 X下께서는 御微恙의 御模樣이라는 이유로 拜謁을 許치안음으로 부득이 退邸하지 안으면 안되게 되엇다.
XX사무관의 이 보고을 드른 총독부 首腦部는 불길한 예감에 얼골을 마조대일 뿐이엇다.

「만일 X下께서 御在邸 하시지 안타면 통치상 큰일로 따라서는 총독부 책임상의 大문제이다」
총독은 白髮童顔에 깁흔 우려를 싸안고 말하엿다.
「XX君 수고스럽지만 이제 다시 한번 參邸하여 X下의 御動靜을 奉伺하여 주게 御在邸, 혹은 御他出, 그것은 이번 사건의 岐路이니까」
총독은 얼마 전의 男爵 金嘉鎭의 XX탈출 직후의 일임으로 신속히 진위를 캐어알지 안으면 안될 것을 말하엿다.
XX사무관은 다시 자동차를 날려 XX邸에 參邸하엿다.
「X下 御恙가 게시다 하지만은 엇잿든 流言과 風評이 전국에 흐터저 잇는 今日, 친히 X下에 拜謁하여 問安하고 십슴니다.」
XX사무관은 여러번 간청하엿다. 그 때 대답에는
「X下께옵서는 昨夜深更, 從僕 金三福을 다리시고 뒷문으로 御외출하섯스나 얼마잇지 안어서 御歸邸하실터이니 아모 근심도 마십시요」
御歸邸하실 듯 하다고 하는 대답은 애매하게 흐리엇다. 그리고 邸內의 여러 사람에게는 어데인가 불안한 빗이 흐르고 잇슴을 깨다를 수 잇섯다. XX사무관은 모든 것을 짐작할 수 잇섯다.
土師某의 보고는 진실인 것을 안 총독부는 實로 긴장하엿다.
그날 사이에 고등 경찰과로부터 飛電이 八方에 發하엿다.
이제는 全土에는 비상선이 完膚업스리만치 거미줄 느러서 드시 처서잇다.
XXXX下御失踪!
그래서 當局은 過激 朝鮮人에게 유혹 拉致된 X下의 신변에 만일의 일이잇서는 안되리라고 血眼이 되여서 탐색의 손을 벌리엇다.
翌11월 10일의 早朝 서울 公平洞의 엇든＜45＞ 문을 탐색한 一隊로부터 이전에 妓生이 거주하고 잇든 空家가 바로 陰謀團의 밀회소이다! 하는 보고가 수사본부에 達하엿다.
XXXX下를 御억지하기 위한 當局의 활동과 함께 滿洲 일대의 特務기관은 動員을 命하여서 上海 假政府에 대한 특별 경계에 임하도록 되엇다.
그러타 할지라도 엇재서 XXXX下께서는 경솔하게 深夜 조용히 御외출하섯슬가―
그리고 엇더한 곳에? 누구의 策動에 의하여 殿下는 如何한 御일상을 보내이고 게섯든가. 필자는 위선 X下 御외출의 동기부터 적으리라.
大正 8년의 XX사건이 신문지에 기사 解禁되엇슬 때 XXX家 유일의 財源일 XX沿海의 어업권 문제의 紛紏에 대하여 보도된 것을 識者는 기억하리라.
X下 실종문제의 발단은 그 어업권 문제에 원인을 發하엿든 것이다.
XXX家의 어업권은 慶尙道 沿海의 전부에 及하여잇다. 이 漁場에 대한 利權을 싸고돌며 貨忖期에 잇서 幾多의 인물들이 策動을 하고 잇섯다. 더구나 財政에 窮한 XXX家에서는 그 일부를 抵當으로 하여 일시의 財政的 궁핍을 구하자는 의논이 잇섯다.
엇던날―
XXX家을 방문하여 온 몸차림이 깨끗한 사람이 元 近侍인 鄭雲復을 통하여 一書를 X下께 捧하엿다.
「엇던 사람인가」
X下는 말슴이 적게 물으섯다.
「네 錫東이라고 하는 富豪의 대리인으로 李敏河라고 하는 者이올시다.」
鄭은 죄송하여 하면서 奉答하였다.
「응 그래 3만원의 융통을 말하엿는가」
「네 어업권을 저당으로 하여 3만원의 융통을 하여 드릴려고 하옵니다」
X下는 鄭의 奉答을 드르시더니 깁히 고려하시다가 目下의 窮策으로 그 말을 드르실수 밧게 업다는 鄭雲復의 이약이를 드르시더니
