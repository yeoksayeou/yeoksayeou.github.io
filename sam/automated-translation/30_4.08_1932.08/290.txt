Literary Circle Miscellany

The Death of Sŏhae (曙海)
Ch'oe Sŏhae, a heavyweight (重鎭) of the fiction world, passed away (長逝) at Seoul Medical College Hospital (서울 醫專病院) at the age of 32. When news of his critical condition was first reported, the condolences and visits from various figures of the literary world poured in, showing how much he was cherished and trusted by his friends.

Moreover, when his death (臨終) was near, Sŏnghae (星海) drew 300 grams of his own blood and performed a blood transfusion (輪血) into the dying Sŏhae's body. Besides him, there were two non-relatives (非*人) who also transfused blood. He received 800 grams of his comrades' blood, and yet, he still departed in the end.

On July 11th, the day of the funeral, those who rushed to his house and tearfully bid farewell (泣送), standing near (呎尺) the pitiful coffin (棺), included:

Yi Sŭngman (李承萬) and numerous other literary figures gathered like clouds (雲集). Such a gathering of poets and novelists in one place (一堂) had not been seen in recent times.

Among those who carried Sŏhae's coffin from the room to the hearse (靈柩車) on the main street were Sŏnghae, P'ain (파인 - likely Kim Tongin's other pen name?), and others from his six close friends (六友) during his lifetime. The funeral inscription (영정) covering the coffin was written by Mr. Yi Pyŏnggi (李秉岐), and at the burial site (葬地), after burying the coffin and placing concrete (콩그리드) over it, the one who wrote the final seven characters
"The Coffin of Sŏhae, Ch'oe Hak-song" (曙海 崔鶴松之柩)
was Mr. Kim Unjŏng (金雲汀). He departed for Pungmangsan (北邙山) amidst the sorrow and regret of many people.

Those who don't know how to escape the summer heat (還署) even when the warmth arrives are likely only the poor scholars (文士) in their humble rooms (가난방). Among them, only one, Mr. Yi Ŭnsang (李殷相), pen name Nosan (鷺山), received an invitation (招請) from a certain organization (團體) in Kangnŭng (江陵), Kangwŏn Province (江原道), and is said to be going on an exploration (踏査) of the Eight Scenic Views of Kwandong (關東八景). And there is another, Mr. Hyŏn Chin'gŏn (玄鎭健), pen name P'ingho (憑虛); he received a generous payment (厚幣) from the Tonga Ilbo Company (동아일보사) and set out to find the 4,000-year-old ruins (遺跡) of our ancestor Tan'gun (檀君 한배님) below Mt. T'aebaek (太白山下).

In Tokyo alone, the cornerstones (諸礎石) forming the literary world include "Waseda Literature" (早稻田문학), "Mita Literature" (三田문학), "Shinchō" (新潮), "Bungaku Jidai" (문학시대), "Shishin" (詩神), and the literary columns of "Kaizō" (改造) and "Chūō Kōron" (中央公論). Even in China, several pure literary magazines like "Fiction Monthly" (小說月報) and "Creation" (創造) can be counted. Yet, uniquely in Chosŏn (Korea), Pang Ch'unhae (方春海) and Nam Uhun's (南又薰) "Chosŏn Literary Barrier[?]" (朝鮮文塀‡ - likely a typo for 文壇, Literary World) has collapsed, the Proletarian faction's "Chosŏn Literary Arts" (朝鮮文藝) has also collapsed, Yang Muae's (梁無涯) "Literary Arts Forum" (문예公論) from P'yŏngyang (平壤) has also been suspended, and now even Pak Yongch'ŏl (朴龍喆) and Yi Hayun's (異河潤) "Literary Arts Monthly" (문예월간), which alone had maintained its lifeline (命脈) until recently, is rumored to be suspending publication (停刊說). Is it that there are no people in the literary world, or are the times unfavorable (시세가 불리하든가)?<104>

**Summary**

This article from August 1932 reports on recent events in the Korean literary world. It prominently features the recent death of novelist Ch'oe Sŏhae at age 32, detailing the outpouring of support from fellow writers, including blood transfusions and a large gathering at his funeral attended by many major literary figures like Yi Kwangsu, Kim Tongin, and Yi Pyŏnggi. The article contrasts this communal mourning with the ongoing activities of writers Yi Ŭnsang and Hyŏn Chin'gŏn, who are traveling for research/exploration. It concludes on a somber note, lamenting the successive closure or suspension of nearly all Korean literary magazines, contrasting this situation with the vibrant publishing scenes in Tokyo and China, and questioning the future of the Korean literary field. For historians, the article vividly illustrates the close-knit yet struggling nature of the colonial Korean literary community, showcasing solidarity in grief alongside the harsh economic realities reflected in the collapse of literary journals. Hyŏn Chin'gŏn's mission to find Tan'gun relics, funded by a newspaper, also points to the intertwining of nationalism, historical consciousness, and media activity during this period.

文壇雜話
曙海의 죽엄
소설계의 重鎭 崔曙海가 서울 醫專病院에서 32세를 一期로 長逝하엿는데 그 위급의 報가 한 번 전하자 文壇 諸씨의 吊問이 踏至하엿슴은 그가 얼마나 友人들에게 앗기고 미듬을 밧엇든 증거인가 알 것이다.

더구나 臨終이 갓가웟슬 때 星海는 자기의 피 3백구람을 빼내어 죽어가는 曙海의 몸에 輪血까지 하엿다. 輪血한 분은 이 밧게 非*人으로 두 분이 잇섯다. 그는 동무의 피를 8백구람이나 먹고 그러고도 끗끗내 가버리엇다.

李承萬 其外 諸文人이 雲集하엿섯다. 이러케 詩人 소설가가 一堂에 모여보기는 근래에 업섯든 일이다.

曙海의 棺을 방안에서 큰길가의 靈柩車에까지 안고 나간 이에 星海 파인 등 生前의 六友가 잇섯고 棺 우에 덥는 영정은 李秉岐씨가 썻고 그리고 葬地에 가서 棺을 뭇고 그우 콩그리드 한곳에
「曙海 崔鶴松之柩」
라고 최후의 일곱자를 쓴 분에 金雲汀씨가 잇섯다. 그는 만흔 사람의 슬퍼하고 앗가워 하는 속에서 北邙山으로 갓든 것이다.

더위가 와도 還署갈 줄 모르는 이는 가난방인 文士들 뿐이리라. 이 가운데서 오직 한 분 鷺山 李殷相씨가 江原道 江陵 엇던 團體의 招請을 밧고 關東八景의 踏査로 가리라든가 그리고 또 한 분에 憑虛 玄鎭健씨가 잇는데 氏는 동아일보사로부터 厚幣를 밧고 太白山下의 4천년전 檀君 한배님 遺跡찻기로 나섯다.

東京만 하여도 文壇을 형성한 諸礎石으로 「早稻田문학」 「三田문학」 「新潮」 문학시대」 「詩神」과 「改造」 「中央公論」의 文藝欄 등이 잇고 中國만하여도 「小說月報」 「創造」 등 數種의 순 문예잡지를 헤이고 잇는데 유독 조선만은 方春海, 南又薰의 「朝鮮文塀‡」이  쓰러지고 푸로派의 「朝鮮文藝」마저 쓰러지고 平壤서 나오는 梁無涯의 「문예公論」 亦 중단되고 그리고 오직 최근까지 命脈을 지탕하여 오는 朴龍喆
異河潤씨 등의 「문예월간」 조차 停刊說을 전한다.
