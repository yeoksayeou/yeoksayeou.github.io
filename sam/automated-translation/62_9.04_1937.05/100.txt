Article Title: 5월의 牧笛 (May's Shepherd's Flute)
Author: 岸曙 (An Sŏ)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

May's Shepherd's Flute
						An Sŏ

Blue mountains, blue sky
Welcome May
The wind blowing through the grassy field
Is also cool
Flowing, embracing the sand
By the clear stream
A cow, still chewing its cud
Looks at the distant mountains <15>

**Summary**

This short poem by An Sŏ depicts a peaceful pastoral scene in May. It describes the blue sky, green mountains, a cool breeze in a field, a clear stream, and a cow gazing at distant mountains while chewing its cud. The poem evokes a sense of tranquility and the simple beauty of nature. From a historical perspective, the poem offers a glimpse into the non-political literary expressions of the time. Its idyllic focus on nature, devoid of overt commentary on the colonial situation, might reflect a personal escape, a common poetic trope, or perhaps a subtle form of passive resistance through upholding traditional aesthetic values during a period of external control.
