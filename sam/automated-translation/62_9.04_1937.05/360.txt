해바라기(2)
						장덕조
前回梗槪 (Previous Outline)=Not long after getting married, the young, beautiful, sensitive Inae, who could perhaps receive "Norma Shearer-class" sentiment (正感), went to the cinema with her husband. There, she had an association (連想) from a certain scene on the screen, wondering if her husband might have another lover. When she voiced her doubt (懷疑), this fantasy seemed to reveal itself realistically (現實的) in various ways, causing her to suffer anguish (煩惱) all by herself due to this imagination.

Returning home, Inae simply could not calm her heart.
One suspicion led to another, bringing forth various other doubts. As her husband smiled, taking off his Western suit, his handsome forehead, sturdy physique, skin overflowing with a vigorous spirit, and even the cleanly shaven blueish stubble—none of it looked ordinary. Without even taking off her scarf or overcoat, she crouched by the warmest part of the floor, her knees trembling uncontrollably.
"Father (압바아 - term of endearment for husband)!"
Finally, as if unable to hold back any longer,
"That woman earlier... the kisaeng named Wŏrhwa we met in front of the theater!"
"Yes?"
As she looked up and asked, tears welled in her eyes.
"How long have you known her?"
"No matter what, she's not just some woman you met once or twice at a public occasion (公席) like a banquet, as you said, Father."
"Hmph, starting again? Are you jealous?"
It wasn't the time to argue about whether she was jealous or not. She was asking out of genuine worry, but her husband's ordinary reply seemed to contain not a shred of sympathy (同情). Suddenly, anger flared up again, and she turned her eyes away. She looked up at the electric light; the cover on the blue shade—she remembered that too. One night when her husband had also returned late, she had tried everything to forget her worries, untying and retying it, retying and untying it again, trying to distract herself, finally finishing it only near dawn. Thinking of this, the tears she had desperately held back poured down onto her knees like a sudden shower breaking through the clouds.
"Why are you crying? What did I do to make you act like this?"
Her expectation that he would hold her and console her was shattered; instead, her husband jumped up.
"Making groundless suspicions and throwing a tantrum. After living together for three or four years, can't you trust your own husband?"
"So, you're taking her side? Even about my crying?"
Muttering as if to herself, Inae looked up at her husband with hysterical eyes.
"Honestly, what's this fuss in the middle of the night? I thought something was strange since the theater earlier, and now you finally show your true colors, acting like a senile old woman..."
"Hmph, you don't even try to understand how I feel, just counterattacking with 'hysteria' this and 'hysteria' that whenever you get a chance. Someone like you, Father, will never know my true feelings, never!"
Tears welled up again.
"It's no wonder I can't trust a husband who fails to understand (理解) his own wife's heart like this."
"So what do you want me to do?"
"It's pitiful, me clinging to you while hearing unpleasant and pleasant words. Where in the world these days would you find a woman who receives such contempt from her own husband yet devotes all her sincerity?"
Inae threw herself onto the bedding the maid had laid out and began to sob, her shoulders shaking.
"Seriously, why are you like this?"
He stared blankly at her form beside him, then, with the peculiar habit of his tone becoming softer the angrier he got,
"Think about it. Tonight, was I in the wrong, or were you, Inae? Hmm? It's not to boast, but when a husband treats you well, you should appreciate it. How can this be considered proper behavior?"
Indeed, Inae was not unaware that her home (家庭) was peaceful (平穩) and her husband treated her well.
However, she was experiencing unbearable agony due to her husband's recent lukewarm attitude, neither hot nor cold.
As a reaction (反動) to having once thought there could be no happier woman in the world than herself, she now felt sadness at every single one of her husband's actions and words, even insignificant ones. Without reason, the feeling surged that there could be no woman more unhappy and miserable than she.
It felt as though a vaguely terrifying misfortune (悲運) lay unfolded in her future (前途).
The dreadful fights between wives and concubines she had read about in novels or heard in the gossip of neighborhood wives, the astonishing skill (技巧) of certain kinds of women (어떤 종류 게집), the defeat (敗北), grief (悲歎), despair (落望), and suicide (自殺) of the wronged first wife—all these seemed to reflect her own situation.
"Relationships between men and women (男女), especially the marital relationship (夫婦關係), don't work out perfectly like solving an arithmetic (算術) problem in mathematics (數學),"
She remembered something Yŏnghŭi had said once.
"Alright, stop that and let's try to sleep. It's already twelve o'clock!"
He put his arm around her waist to lift her up, forcibly tilted Inae's face towards him, bringing his lips close. She pushed his hand away.
"I don't want to, I don't want to. I don't want to receive charity (適線) given reluctantly."
Her two eyes, still welling with tears, were as clear as drops of water.
"What on earth are you doing this for? I need to at least know the reason."
"You know perfectly well."
"Hmm? That kisaeng from earlier? Is all this commotion just because of that trivial matter?"
"A trivial matter? It might be ordinary for an expert like you, Father, but to me, it's absolutely not a trivial matter!"
"Hmph. Damn it. With a wife like this, how can I relax? How can I succeed in life?"
The husband clicked his tongue and then once more,
"Damn it."
"Fine, fine! I'll go begging if I get kicked out, that's why I'm sticking to this damn house even while receiving this abuse!"
"This damn house?"<52>
"Why ask like you don't know?"
"Do as you please. Acting like you're hysterical (히스테리걸린 것). 'This fellow' (이눔)? Did you think your husband was your house servant?"
"Stop talking. You're the one treating me like a kitchen maid..."
"Eek! Crazy woman. I tried to listen patiently, but now all sorts of nonsense are coming out!"
Truly, since their marriage, they had never had such a huge fight.
"The woman who can't leave for fear of begging? No need to wait for you to leave. I'll go!"
The husband gathered the Western suit he had taken off and started putting it back on.
"I'll go find an understanding and gentle woman, remarry, and live happily. You should know that and handle things accordingly."
He noisily opened the sliding door, left it ajar, and just dashed outside.
The sound of him stepping down into the yard, the sound of the gate opening—as her husband's footsteps grew fainter, Inae felt she couldn't just let him go.
"Father!"
Calling out plaintively, she ran out onto the wooden porch. One sock off, she jumped down into the yard; the chilling sensation that gave her goosebumps must have meant it was snowing.
"Where is he going on this snowy night? I really messed things up by being unnecessarily neurotic."
Resentment and sadness forgotten for a moment, she stepped outside the open gate. Just turning the corner of alleyway was a black overcoat with its collar turned up—it was clearly her husband.
"Honey, where are you going? They say thieves are around these days, how am I supposed to sleep alone, scared?"
Muttering under her breath, she was about to chase after him when the maid (어멈), who must have followed her out, still heavy-lidded with sleep in her puffy eyes,
"Miss, what's wrong? Oh my, you're barefoot! You'll catch a cold!"
grabbed her by the waist and pulled her back inside.
"Aren't you embarrassed in front of the neighbors?"
Turning her head again,
"Ah, youth is truly wonderful."
Even finding the maid's overly stuffed trousers annoying and hateful, Inae lacked the strength to push her away and buried her face in the maid's chest, weeping.

A Man's Heart

"What time is it anyway?"
As the man started to raise his upper body as if to look at the desk clock (좌종) on the table, the woman lying beside him in the same spot, smoking a cigarette, wrapped one arm around his neck, pulling him back down.
"Don't worry about the time again,"
she cooed playfully. She was Wŏrhwa, less a kisaeng and more what you'd call an intimate friend (은근자), not particularly well-regarded.
"I told you not to worry. I'll let you go promptly at eight o'clock, yes sir. It's still dawn, the streetcars aren't even running yet, are they?"
"Who said I was leaving? I just wondered what time it was."
"Hmph, last night you came looking for me completely drunk, just boasting about your wife (부인 「노로깨(惚氣)」)..."
"Yes, I did."
As he replied, he truly began to worry about his wife.
"Inae, who gets scared and can't sleep even if I come home just a little late... how much she must have suffered all night."
Her clear eyes, her breath fragrant as narcissus, her soft hair... Last night it was snowing lightly, and the wind was terribly cold. His wife, who felt the cold so much, how much hardship must she have endured—. Lying face down, chin propped on the kisaeng house's long pillow (長枕), he thought of his wife with intense longing.
"Having grown up without siblings (兄弟), she relies on me like a husband, like an older brother, like a father, acting childishly dependent... I was unnecessarily harsh."
His wife, clear as the autumn sky. A wife who trusts only him. A warm wife, a cute wife, a wife like a flower, possessing beautiful emotions and strange old stories.
"Like a peony (모란)? No, like a plum blossom (매화)? That's not it either. Right. A sunflower (해바래기). A sunflower-like wife. A wife who trusts, respects, loves, and relies on me like the sun. That's right. Inae was a sunflower-like woman."
An abundant affection he had never experienced before surged into his heart like a rising tide.
"I hope nothing happened. With her quick temper..."
He grew tired of everything and just wanted to see his wife. Just as the woman lit a cigarette for him, without even taking a puff, he placed it in the nearby ashtray.
"I have to go."
He abruptly jumped up from his spot.
"I said I'd take you home at eight, why are you doing this?"
Pushing away the clinging woman,
"Something urgent came up."
"Urgent? You mean you miss your lady (아씨). Mrs. Yi Sang, whom I saw in front of the theater last night, was truly beautiful (고흐시든데요)."
Last night, they went to see "Romeo and Juliet." The pointless fight started after meeting Wŏrhwa in front of the theater, leading him to storm out of the house. How and where he got drunk, ending up seeking out Wŏrhwa—whom, in Inae's words, he truly "didn't have even a dog's fart's worth (개방귀만큼도) of interest (關心) in"—around three in the morning, probably had its own psychological (心理的) reasoning (理致).
However, deciding this wasn't the time to discuss (論議) such eloquent psychoanalysis (精神分析學), he ignored the woman trying to stop him and simply prepared to leave.
"I hope nothing happened. I wonder if she got angry and went off to her parents' home (親庭)? Didn't die..."
His mind raced so fast he couldn't even think beyond that.
"Please be safe, my sunflower-like wife."
He truly couldn't help but find himself strange.
That his wife, who until yesterday seemed so ordinary and monotonous, could become so precious, lovely, and endearing after just one night spent away... Finding his own feelings strange, he hastily called for a car.
This husband of Inae had indeed grown up in miserable circumstances from childhood (어렷슬), graduating from a College of Commerce (상과대학) through self-support, and thus had a truly orderly and consequently very cold-hearted disposition. Once he entered society and secured a position at some commercial company (영리회사), this trait intensified. While terribly afraid of imposing on others, he was by no means someone who enjoyed treating others either.
The only (唯一) emotional life (情緖生活) of his entire life <53> was during his so-called engagement courtship period when he first met Inae. Drawn by the lively charm of the maiden inexperienced in worldly affairs (셰고), he went with her to listen to music and spoke of literature. One year, on the day he received his bonus (賞與金), he met a friend on the streetcar. He paid ten *jŏn* and got change back, yet didn't pay for his friend's ticket. From this incident, his nickname "eternal self-supporting student" gained more effect (効果), and his reputation (評判) among friends was not good. His recent frequent nights out were also similar; having accidentally become friendly with Yŏnghŭi's husband, they frequented the "streets of gallantry" (협사의 거리) a few times, and he couldn't just sit back receiving hospitality (대접만 밧고 물러 안저 있을 수는 없었든 것이니). In other words, everything stemmed from resolute calculation (확호한 분별로부터 출발한 결과).
Therefore, it was no wonder he himself found it strange that such tender emotions found their way (차자옴을) into the heart of such a person.
"Inae, Inae, my fragrant wife."
Like someone consciously falling into a dream, he leaned against the cushion (쿳숀) and closed his eyes. 次號完 (Continued in next issue) <54>

**Summary**

The story continues exploring the marital jealousy and conflict between Inae and her husband. After a fight sparked by encountering a kisaeng (Wŏrhwa), the husband storms out into the snowy night. Inae, distraught, tries to follow but is stopped by the maid. The husband, meanwhile, ends up drunk at Wŏrhwa's place. However, morning brings regret and a sudden, intense longing for Inae, whom he now perceives as precious and terms his "sunflower." The narrative reveals the husband's cold, calculating past shaped by hardship and contrasts it with his newfound surge of affection, leaving the resolution for the next issue.

From a historical perspective, the story offers glimpses into urban middle-class life in colonial Korea during the 1930s: leisure activities like cinema ("Romeo and Juliet"), the presence of kisaeng houses as part of male social life, common marital anxieties, and references to Western culture (Norma Shearer). The husband's background as a self-supporting graduate of a College of Commerce reflects the changing social structures and aspirations. The detailed emotional landscape and psychological focus might interest historians studying changing gender roles, marriage dynamics, and the expression of modernity in colonial literature.

「생각을 해 봐요. 오늘 저녁일은 내가 잘못하엿나 인애가 잘못햇나 응. 내 자랑은 아니지만 남편이 잘 해주면 그걸 알아 주어야지 이래서야 어데 인사가 되엿소」

막연히 놀라운 비운이 자기 전도에 전개되여 있는 것 같은 생각이 나는 것이다.
소설에서 읽은 혹은 동리 여편네들의 잡담에서 들은 처첩간의 무서운 싸홈이 어떤 종류 게집들의 놀라운 기교 억울한 본처의 패북(敗北) 비탄 락망 자살 이같은 것이 바로 자기 모양이 되여 비최여 왓다.
「남녀의 관게 더욱이 부부관계란 수학의 산술 풀려가듯이 꼭 마자 떠러지는 게 아니어든」
언젠가 영희가 하든 말이 생각난다.
「자 그러지 말고 좀 자 보지. 벌서 열 두시구면!」
허리를 껴안아 일으키며 무리하게 인애의 얼골을 처들어 입술을 가까히 가저오는 남편의 손을 뿌리치고
「실혀 실혀. 마지못해 베푸는 적선 밧기 실혀요」
아즉도 눈물이 샘솟는 두 눈은 방울가치 맑다.
「대체 뭣 때문에 이러는 거야. 까닭이나 알아야지」
「알면서 뭘 그래」
「응 아까 기생, 겨우 그까짓 일 때문에 이 소동야?」
「그까짓 일이라구? 압바같은 상습자에게는 예사겠지만 내겐 도모지 그까짓 일이 아닌걸?」
「흥. 비러먹을 것. 예편네가 저모양이니 원 마음을 노흘 수 있나. 출세를 할 수가 있나」
남편은 끌끌 혀를 채고 나서 다시 한 번
「비러먹을 것」
「그럼 그럼 쫏겨나면 비러먹겠기에 이 구박을 밧으면서두 이눔의 집에 달라 붓허 있지」
「이눔의 집이라니」<52>
「웨 몰라서 물어?」
「마음대로 해. 히스테리걸린 것 같으니라구. 이눔이라니 사내를 제집 종놈으로 알앗나봐」
「말 말어. 제가 되려 사람을 밥어멈으로 알면서.....」
「엑기 미친년. 참고 들을려니 원 별 지랄이 다 나오지 안나」
정말 혼인한 이래 이러케까지 크게 벌어젓든 싸홈은 없었다.
「비러먹을까봐 못 나가는 년. 나가기 기다릴 것 없다. 내가 갈테야」
남편은 벗어노앗든 양복을 다시 주섬주섬 주어 입으며
「나두 리해있고 상냥한 녀자헌테 새장가들어 잘 살테니 너도 그리 알고 잘 조처해」
소리내여 방문을 열어 잿긴체 그냥 박그로 뛰여 나갓다.
뜰을 내려서는 기척 대문 여는 소리 남편의 발자최가 점점 멀어가자 인애는 암만해도 그냥 내버려둘 수 없을 것 같핫다.
「압바아」
애닯게 부르며 뛰여 나가는 마루 양말은 한 짝을 버슨 그대로 마당에 뛰여 내리니 선듯선듯 소름이 끼치게 해주는 촉감은 아마 눈이 오는 모양이다.
「이 눈오는 밤을 저이는 어대를 가려노. 정말 괜히 신경질을 부려서 일을 랑패식혀노쿠」

「여보, 어딀 가우. 요새 도적은 댕긴다는데 나 혼자 무서워 어떠케 자라구?」
입 속으로 부르지즈며 그 뒤를 쫏츠려는데 어느새 딸아 나왓는지 어멈이 부숭부숭한 눈에 아즉도 잠을 실고

「동리 창피하시지 안아요」

그 미여지게 솜을 노흔 바지바람까지 야속하고 밉다고 생각하면서도 인애는 뿌리질 기력이 없이 어멈의 가슴에 얼골을 뭇고 울엇다.

사나희 마음

「대체 몃 시나 되엿노」
책상 우의 좌종을 보려는 듯이 상반신을 이르키는 사내의 목을 한 손으로 껴안아 뉘이며
「또 시간 염려 말아요」
하고 아양을 떠는 것은 여태까지 한 자리 속에서 담배를 피우며 업데여 있든 기생이라느니보담 소위 은근자에 가까운 그러케까지 잘 불리우지 못하는 월화라는 게집이었다.
「걱정 마시라니까. 여덟 점만 땅 치거든 예에하고 노하드릴테니요. 아즉은 전차도 없는 새벽 아녜요?」
「누가 가겠댓나? 그저 몃신가 햇지」
「흥, 어제 저녁에는 아주 약주가 곤죽이 되여 차저와서 부인 「노로깨(惚氣)」만 하시드니...」
「응, 그랫서」
그는 이러케 대답하면서 정말 안해의 일이 걱정이 되기 시작하엿다.
「내가 조곰만 늣게 들어가도 무서워서 자지 못하는 인애가 밤새 얼마나 애를 썻슬고」
그 맑은 눈 소소같이 향기로운 입김 부드러운 머리칼, 어적게 저녁은 눈이 뿌리고 바람이 몹씨 찻겠다. 추위를 그처럼 타는 안해가 얼마나 고생을 햇슬고-. 기생방의 장침(長枕)우에 턱을 고이고 업데여 그립게 그립게 안해를 생각하엿다.
「형제도 없이 자라난 것이 되여 나를 남편처럼 오래비처럼 아버지처럼 녁여 어리광을 부리는 걸 괜히 내가 넘우 심햇지」
가을 하늘같이 맑은 안해, 자기 하나를 밋고 사는 안해 따뜻한 안해 귀여운 안해 아름다운 정서와 기이한 옛 이약이를 가진 꼿과 같은 안해.
「모란같은? 아니, 매화같은? 그것도 아니다. 올치. 해바래기. 해바래기같은 안해다. 나를 태양같이 밋고 존경하고 사랑하고 의지하는 안해다. 그러타. 인애는 해바래기같은 계집이었다」
그의 마음 속에는 여태까지 체험해 보지 못하엿든 풍부한 애정이 밀물처럼 밀려왓다.
「무슨 일이나 내지 안앗는지 모르겠다. 그 급한 성미에...」
모든 일이 다 실코 안해가 보고 십헛다. 마침 게집이부처 올리는 담배조차 한 목음 빨 겨를도 없이 엽헤 있는 재트리에 언즈며
「가 보아야겠군」
후닥닥 자리에서 뛰여 일어낫다.
「여덟 시만 되면 모셔다 드리겠다는데 웨 이러세요」
매달리는 게집을 뿌리치고

그러나 지금은 이러케 유창한 정신 분석학을 론의하고 있을 때가 아니라하며 말리는 게집에게는 상관할 것 없이 그냥 도라갈 준비를 하엿다.

어적게까지도 그처럼 범연하고 천편일률적이든 안해가 단 한 번의 외박으로 이처럼 아깝고 귀엽고 애틋할 줄이야...... 그는 자기 마음을 이상하다하면서도 급한 김에 자동차를 불넛다.
대체 이 인애의 남편은 어렷슬 때부터 비참한 환경속에 자라나 고학으로 상과대학을 나왓다느니만큼 실로 규측 정연한 따라서 몹씨 박정한 성미엿다. 일단 사회에 나와서 어떤 영리회사에 취직이 작정되자 그같은 성질은 더욱 심해가며 남에게 신셰를 끼치는 일을 끔직이도 무서워하는 한편 다른 사람을 대접하는 것도 결코 즐기는 배가 아니었다.
인애를 처음 알게된 소위 약혼교제시절에 셰고를 모르는 처녀의 발날한 매력에 끌리워 갓치 음악을 들으러 댕기고 문학을 입에 올려보앗든 것이 일생의<53> 유일한 정서생활이오 어느해 상여금을 밧든 날 전차 속에서 한 친구를 맛낫슬 때 십전을 내고 돈을 거슬러 밧으면서 오히려 그 친구의 차표를 찍어주지 안앗다는 일로부터 「만년 고학생의 별명은 더욱 효과를 나타내여 친구 사이에도 평판은 조치 못한 터이다. 요사히 밤노리가 자즌 것도 역시 마찬가지인데 그것은 우연히 친해진 영희의 남편과 함께 몃 번 협사의 거리에 드나들자 대접만 밧고 물러 안저 있을 수는 없었든 것이니 말하자면 모든 것이 확호한 분별로부터 출발한 결과었다.
그럼으로 이같은 사람의 마음 속에 그러틋 애틋한 정서가 차자옴을 보고 스스로 이상히 생각함도 결코 무리가 아니다.
「인애, 인애, 향기로운 내 안해」
