Posin'gak Semi-Monthly Chronicle
						By Ch'wigong
□
"Koreans don't strike Koreans" (朝鮮人不打朝鮮人) is a belief I have upheld until now. Let's not find fault with fellow Koreans; let's pretend not to see their mistakes and pretend not to hear their errant words. To express this belief more firmly, I have not been lazy in highly evaluating reasonable individuals, nor have I hesitated to praise decent endeavors as if they were splendid and unique achievements.

For the past nine years, while running this magazine, I have tried immensely hard to uphold this belief in my own way.

However, recently, this belief has begun to waver. "What's the point of cherishing them like that? Shouldn't we perhaps strike them sometimes?" (朝鮮人或打朝鮮人 - Koreans might strike Koreans)

Then, when seeing some figures posing as notables and patriots (名士, 志士然) hiding ten or a hundred evil deeds behind a single good deed, I sometimes rise up indignantly and become agitated, thinking, "Let's strike them all!" (朝鮮人必打朝鮮人 - Koreans must strike Koreans)

Not strike?
Perhaps strike?
Must strike?
These three possibilities are a persistent question that has not left my mind lately.

□
Looking at the financial statements of Kyŏngjŏn (京電), Chiksan (殖産), or Sint'ak (信託), they report net profits of hundreds of thousands of yen even after paying a semi-annual dividend of 12 percent.

Also, individuals like Mr. Noguchi (野口氏) of Hŭngnam Nitrogen (興南窒素) or Mr. Kashii (香椎氏) of Kyŏngnam Fishery (慶南漁場) report huge profits year after year. These companies and individuals are all making large profits largely by dealing with the resources of the Peninsula (半島) and the Korean people. Here, we reflect. Have these people ever shown a sense of gratitude towards Korean society? While there might have been individual instances, there has been no clear societal expression of it thus far.

When British and American people (英米國人) come to places like Beijing (北京), Nanjing (南京), or Shanghai (上海) to open shops, they first establish social facilities like Tsinghua University (淸華大學) or Tonghwa Hospital (同華病院) before seeking monetary gain. Even if, on the other hand, their conduct has unseemly aspects, there is perhaps a reason one might find points to appreciate [lit: points to be angry at, likely ironic or meaning 'mitigating factors'].

In the past on the Peninsula (半島), there were gentlemen merchants like Mr. Ōkura (大創氏) who established Sŏllin Commercial School (善隣商業), but nowadays there seem to be no figures of Ōkura's caliber. Though schools are so lacking that the academic world in April turns into a tragedy, they cannot even think of building just one school amidst this scene. This indifference is truly profound.

□
When we consider Mr. Pang Ŭngmo (方應謨氏), who says he would donate half if he had just 400,000 or 500,000 yen, and his 500,000 yen newspaper company; Mr. Wŏn Yunsu's (元胤洙氏) 500,000 yen high/middle school (高普); Mr. Han Inbo's (韓仁輔氏) 700,000 yen for Sungsil College (崇專); Mr. Cho Pyŏnggap's (趙炳甲氏) 60,000 yen for three professional schools (三專門學校); Mr. Kim Hongnyang's (金鴻亮氏) 600,000 yen high/middle school (高普); Mr. Yi Ch'unsŏp's (李春燮氏) 300,000 yen for Sungsil Middle School (崇中); Mr. Han Yangho's (韓亮鎬氏) 200,000 yen girls' high school (女高); and especially Mr. Yi Chongman's (李鍾萬氏) enormous plans—we find in these people who possess money here a certain noble humanity that transcends the commonly termed capitalistic mindset of capitalist society.

Kashii (香椎), Noguchi (野口), Kyŏngjŏn (京電), Chiksan Bank (殖銀)—facing these facts, would they lack the magnanimity to give it a thought?

□
It seems a song might flow like this: "The talented scholar (선비) loses his place to sit and stand, the beautiful lady (佳人) is sold for money and buried in mud." However, the day will come again when scholars who "speak well," "write well," and have "noble intentions" will be called upon. The beautiful lady, too, will have a day when she can freely perch on the branch she wished to embrace. And it feels like that day will come quickly.

□
Somehow, I used to have a habit of approaching women's writing with reservation [lit: folding one arm], but reading Ms. Mo Yunsuk's (毛允淑氏) "Ren's Elegy" (렌의 哀歌) this time, I felt apprehensive awe. It is a writing of heartbreak, lamenting the emptiness in the heart after sending a beloved person far away, yet there is no hatred or curse here; instead of the ugliness of lamentation, there is the beauty of solace (悌噓). It brings to mind that noble passage from Kunikida Doppo's (國木田獨步) poem, "At midnight, kneeling down, I pray to God, may happiness be upon the body of him who left me," and makes one find here the pure emotion Tennyson (테니슨) expressed calling "SisterHelen" [likely referencing a poem with similar themes of sorrow and forgiveness, or perhaps a misunderstanding of a Tennyson work]. There were previously writings of similar lament, such as Ch'unwŏn's (春園) "Memorial for a Beloved Child" (愛兒祭文) and Kyŏngsan's (䳱山) "Impermanence" (無常), but I believe this piece will be evaluated even more highly.

"Ren's Elegy" is a most beautiful prose poem that sings of the joy of the weeper. <5>

**Summary**

The author, Ch'wigong, reflects on his long-held belief that Koreans should not criticize each other ("Koreans don't strike Koreans"). However, witnessing hypocrisy among some elites and the lack of social contribution from profitable Japanese companies and individuals in Korea (contrasted with Korean philanthropists) makes him question this stance, contemplating whether Koreans *should* criticize or even "strike" fellow Koreans when necessary. He laments the state of society where talent and beauty are devalued by money but holds hope for the future. Finally, he highly praises Mo Yunsuk's poem "Ren's Elegy" for its emotional depth and lack of bitterness, placing it above works by established male writers like Yi Kwangsu (Ch'unwŏn) and Hŏ Kyŏngsan (Kyŏngsan).

From a historical perspective, this article offers insight into the internal debates and social commentary within Korean society under Japanese rule. The author's shifting perspective on criticizing fellow Koreans reflects the complex pressures and disillusionment of the colonial period. The specific critique of Japanese enterprises (Kyŏngjŏn, Chiksan/Shokusan Ginkō, Noguchi's Hŭngnam plant, Kashii's fishery) versus the praise for Korean educational philanthropists highlights Korean resentment over economic exploitation and a desire for national self-strengthening through education and social responsibility. The commentary on literature, particularly the elevation of a female poet's work, is also noteworthy for the period.

「朝鮮人不打朝鮮人」이란말은 지금까지 내가 직혀온 信念이다 朝鮮사람끼리는 서로헐지말자 잘못이 있어도 못본체 잘못하는말이있어도 못드른체 하고지내자 하였다 그리고 좀더 굿셰게 이 信念을 表하기위하여 원만한 人物이면 크게 評價하기를 게을리지안엇고 웬만하 事業이면 또한 훌륭하고 唯一한 功績처럼 讚揚하기를 마지안엇다
過去 九個年동안 내가 이 雜誌를 하여오는사이에 내은 이 信念을 직혀오는라고 무한애써왓다
그러나 最近에 와서 이 信念에 動搖가 생겨진다 「그러케 앗겨서 무얼하누 或時은 따려야 하지안나」(朝鮮人或打朝鮮人)
이러다가 名士, 志士然하는 一部人士가 한가지의 善行뒤에 숨어 열가지 百가지의 惡行을 하는 것을 볼때에 憤然히 이러나
「모도 때리자」(朝鮮人必打朝鮮人)하고 激하여 지는때도 있다
不打?
或打?
必打?
이셰가지는 요지간 나의 머리를 떠나지안은 宿題다
□
京電이나 殖産, 信託의 決算表를 보면 一割二分의 半期配當을하고도 數十萬圓式純利益을 내고 있다
또 興南窒素의 野口氏나 慶南漁場의 香椎氏 기튼분도 年年巨利를 보고 있다 이 會社나 個人들은모다 半島의 資源과 朝鮮사람을 大部分 相對로큰利益을 내고있는 것이다 여기에서 吾人은 생각한다 이분들은 일즉 朝鮮社會에 對하야 感謝의 念을 가저주었든고 지금까지 個人的으론 있었을지나 社會的으로 렷한 表示가 업서왓다
英米國人은 北京南京, 上海等地에 와서 店鋪를 버릴때에몬저 淸華大學이고 同華病院이고 하는 社會施設부터하여노코 貨幣增殖을한다 비록 反面에 素行이 조치못한 點이있다해도 或은 怒할 點이 있는 것이 까닭이다
半島에도 예전에는 大創氏가튼 紳商이잇서 善隣商業도 만들고 하더니 요지음은 大倉級人物도 없는 듯 그러케도 學校가없어 四月의 學府는 悲劇으로 化하건만 이 光景에 學校하나 만드러 내노흘생각들도 못한다 不感症도 여간 不感症이아니다
□
지금 조선사람들은 四五十萬圓만 생겨도 그 半分을 社會에 내놋는다는 方應謨氏의 五十萬圓新聞社, 元胤洙氏의 五十萬圓 高普, 韓仁輔氏의 七十萬圓 崇專, 趙炳甲氏의 六萬圓三專門學校, 金鴻亮氏의 六十萬圓 高普, 李春燮氏의 三十萬圓 崇中, 韓亮鎬氏의 二十萬圓 女高, 더구나 李鍾萬氏의 巨大한 計劃等에 想倒할 때 이곳돈가진분은 資本主義社會에서 通稱하는 資本家的觀念을 떠나서 人道的 엇든 崇高한 人格을 우리들은 차저진다.
香椎, 野口와 京電, 殖銀 이 事實압헤 一考할 雅量업슬손가

女人의 글이라면엇전지 한팔을접고 보려는 버릇이 있든지음 이번 毛允淑氏의 「렌의 哀歌」를 읽고 悚懼한 생각이낫다 愛慕하든분을 멀리보내고 가슴의 空虛에 悲歎하는 失戀의 글이언만 여기에는 憎惡 咀呪가없고 悲歎의 醜, 대신에 悌噓의 美가있다「夜半三更 무릅 고 하나님께 긔도드리노라 날버리고간 그이몸에 幸福이 있기를」하는 國木田獨步의 그놉흔 詩의 一節을 聯想케하고 또테니슨의 哀歌「天上의 누나여」를 부르든 그 純된 감정을 여기에서 찻게 한다 가튼 悲歎의 글로 前日 春園의 「愛兒祭文」이 있었고 䳱山의 「無常」이 있었거니와 이 一文은 그보다더놉히 評價되어 올흘것이락 밋는다
