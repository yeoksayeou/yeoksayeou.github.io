**Body Text Translation:**
As they say, "Flowers float along the stream filling the valley" (滿溪流水 泛花來); the fine season of early summer has arrived. On this fine occasion of returning warmth (一陽來復), we pray that your lofty integrity and deep aspirations will be further polished, becoming a "pure influence not just for one generation" (一世淸風) but for "a hundred generations" (百世淸風).

We deeply apologize that, due to various circumstances leading to editing in haste (蒼卒之間), the previous early April issue could not include even one-tenth of the manuscripts we had considered. Despite this, we offer our profound thanks for your devoted readership, which necessitated even a second printing.

Now, conventionally, it was asserted that magazines were made while sitting down, but from now on, we wish to create a magazine made "with our feet." That is, rather than relying on manuscripts gathered on a desk, we intend to actually go to "the field" (現地), gather "living" materials seen with our own eyes and heard with our own ears, and make something dynamic.

Also, previously, the emphasis was placed on movable type, meaning text, but henceforth, we will strive to increase pictures, that is, pictorials, as much as possible to create a magazine viewed with the eyes. "Today's Civilization" (今日의 文明) is already a "Civilization of Radio Waves" (電波의 문명); at a time when other places catch facts from the entire world via radio waves, should the media world (언론계) of the peninsula (半島) remain stuck in the old ways (舊態依然)? <54>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the May 1937 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine greets readers at the start of summer, thanks them for their support of the previous rushed issue, and outlines two major shifts in editorial policy. The editors state their intention to move from passive, desk-based editing to active, on-site reporting ("making with feet") and to significantly increase the use of photographs and illustrations ("making a magazine seen with the eyes"). The piece concludes by invoking the "Civilization of Radio Waves" and questioning whether the Korean media should remain stagnant compared to global trends. For historians, the article reveals the magazine's conscious effort to modernize its journalistic practices during the colonial period, embracing visual media and emphasizing firsthand reporting. It reflects an awareness of global media trends (like radio) and a desire for the Korean press ("언론계 of the 반도") to keep pace, hinting at aspirations for progress and dynamism within the constraints of the era.

告愛讀者諸賢之書
滿溪流水 泛花來라 하야 初夏의 조흔 철이 다 닥처 왓슴니다. 一陽來復한 이 조흔 때에 여러분의 놉흔 절개, 깁흔 뜻, 더욱 닥가저서 「一世淸風」 아니 「百世淸風」이 되소서 하고 빕니다.

지난번 4월 상순호는 여러 가지로 蒼卒之間에 편집하엿든 탓으로 생각한 원고의 10분 1도 못 收載하엿슴에 불구하고, 그처럼 애독하여 주시어 재판까지 나가게 되엇슴을 깁히 사례합니다.

그런데 종래에는 잡지라 하면 흔히 안저서 만드는 것으로 주장하엿스나, 금후는 「발」로 만드는 잡지를 만들고 십슴니다. 즉 책상 우에 모히는 원고보다 실지로 「현지」에 이르러 눈으로 보고 귀로 드른 「산」재료를 모아 생동하는 것으로 만들려 함니다.
또 한 가지 예전에는 활자를 즉 글을 위주로 하엿스나 압흐로는 그림 즉 화보를 될수록 늘리어 눈으로 보는 잡지를 만들기 노력하겟슴니다. 「今日의 文明」은 벌서 「電波의 문명」으로 남의 곳에서는 전세계의 사실을 전파로 캣취하는 이때에 언제까지든 반도의 언론계도 구태의연하여야 쓰겟습니까. <54>
