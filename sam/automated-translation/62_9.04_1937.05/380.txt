Selection of Famous Poems

The Road to Paektusan (백두산 가는 길)

Suju (樹州)

If this isn't Ch'ŏllyŏng (鐵嶺), why is it so arduous?
Ch'ŏllyŏng is the sorrowful pass one crosses weeping after parting with a loved one,
But Kapryŏng (甲嶺) is the hurried pass one crosses to see a loved one.

Lying by Ch'ŏnji (天池)

Descending the steep slope, climbing sheer cliffs and peaks,
Lying by the indigo pond (神潭), using my arm as a pillow,
Having committed the sin of merely sitting, my heart pounds.
10th day of the 7th month, Kyŏngo year (庚午 7월 10일)

Usuyŏng (右水營)

Yi Pyŏnggi (李秉岐)

Do you know where this place is, O boatman?
Where have the Turtle Ships (거북선) gone, which carried the navy and soared?
Only indifferent fishing boats come and go.

As the evening sun shines on the fine stones of Okmaesan (玉梅山),
And crows fly into the old fortress of Usuyŏng,
From somewhere, the plaintive sounds of a fisherman's flute (漁笛數聲) are heard.

The Crown Prince's Tomb (太子墓)

Yi Ŭnsang (李殷相)

Having lost the place where you are, what glory could I hope for?
Even if a lifetime were a thousand years, could there be even one laugh?
Yet you departed embracing a great ambition, so I am endlessly sorrowful.

Why did you abandon the work you intended to accomplish,
And become a handful of earth in the desolate, empty mountains?
The dew clinging to the tips of grass blades, I wonder if it is tears of resentment (怨淚).

The snow piled up all winter might melt in the spring sun,
But when, in which autumn, will this summer's overgrown grass be cut?
Indifferent mountain beasts gather in herds and have trampled it down.

A traveler folds his hands, patches the turf, and cuts the grass;
Someone who doesn't know the meaning asks, "What is this?"
Tears block my reply, and I stand unable to speak.

Hometown (고향)

Chŏng Chiyong (鄭芝溶)

Though I have returned to my hometown, my hometown,
Is it not the hometown I longed for?

The mountain pheasant incubates its eggs,
And the cuckoo sings in its season, yet.

My heart does not settle in its hometown,
But wanders like a cloud to a distant harbor.

Today too, I climb the mountaintop alone.
A white dot of a flower smiles kindly.

The sound of the grass flute I blew in childhood is not heard,
And my parched lips taste only bitterness.
Though I have returned to my hometown, my hometown,
Only the sky I longed for is high and blue. <54>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of five poems by four different poets (Suju, Yi Pyŏnggi, Yi Ŭnsang, Chŏng Chiyong) published in May 1937. The poems explore themes of longing, historical reflection, loss, and displacement. Suju reflects on the arduous journey possibly towards Paektusan, contrasting it with other passes. Yi Pyŏnggi evokes the past glory of Admiral Yi Sunsin's naval base at Usuyŏng, now quiet and melancholic. Yi Ŭnsang laments at a forgotten Crown Prince's tomb, reflecting on unfulfilled ambitions and neglect. Chŏng Chiyong expresses a sense of alienation upon returning to his hometown, finding it changed or perhaps himself changed, unable to reconnect with his past. For historians, these poems offer glimpses into the sentiments of Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. Yi Pyŏnggi's poem subtly references past national strength (Turtle Ships), Yi Ŭnsang's touches on lost royalty and neglect, and Chŏng Chiyong's expresses a profound sense of dislocation, potentially reflecting the broader cultural and national displacement felt under Japanese rule. Suju's poem, while focused on landscape, carries an undertone of struggle and weariness.

名詩抄
백두산 가는 길

樹州

鐵嶺 아니어든 어이 이리 힘드는가-
鐵嶺은 님 여희고 울며 넘는 설흔 재나
甲嶺은 님 뵈려 넘는 밧븐 고개이여니

天池가에 누어

가파른 비탈나려 峭峯削壁 올을하고
쪽빗가튼 神潭가에 팔벼개로 누었으니
안진 죄 지으야하야 가슴자로 뛰더라
庚午 7월 10일

右水營

李秉岐

이곳이 어데멘지 아는다 저 사공아
水軍실고 드날치든 거북선 어이가고
무심한 漁舟子들만 오락가락 하더라

玉梅山 고운 돌에 석양이 비최이고
右水營 낡은 성에 가마귀 날어들 제
어데서 漁笛數聲은 구슬푸게 들리는고

太子墓

李殷相

게신 곳 일헛스니 무슨 영광 바라리오
일생이 천년인들 우슴 한 번 있으리만
크신 뜻 품고 가시니 못내 설워하노라

일우려 하시든 일 어이다 버리시고
적막 공산에 줌 흙이 되오신고
풀끗헤 매친 이슬은 怨淚인가 하노라

겨으내 싸히든 눈은 봄볏헤나 녹으려니
이 여름 욱어진 풀은 어늬 가을에 베올것고
무심한 山줌생들은 떼를조차 헐엇더라

나그네 손을 것고 떼부치며 풀을 베니
뜻 모른 어떤 이는 웬일이냐 뭇는구야
눈물이 대답을 막아 말 못하고 섯노라

고향

鄭芝溶

고향에 고향에 돌아와도
그리던 고향은 아니러뇨.

산꽁이 알을 품고
뻐꾹이 제철에 울건만.

마음은 제고향 진허지 안코
머언 항구로 떠도는 구름.

오늘도 메끗에 홀로 오르니.
힌 점 꼿이 인정스레 웃고.

어린 시절에 불던 풀피리 소리 아니 나고
메마른 입술에 쓰디 쓰다.
고향에 고향에 돌아와도
그리던 하늘만이 높푸르구나. <54>
