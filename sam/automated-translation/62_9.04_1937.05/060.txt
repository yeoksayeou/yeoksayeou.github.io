For the Development of the One Million Compatriots in Manchuria, Our Letter Presented to Premier Zhang Who Came to Chōsen
Let Us Not Discriminate

Chūsuwon (Central Advisory Council) Councilor Choi Rin (崔麟)

I first met Premier Zhang Jinghui (張景惠) during my previous inspection tour of Manchuria. He has a cheerful disposition and an unmovable toughness that silently reveals his military background, yet there is also something benevolent about him that attracts the hearts of those he interacts with.

Koreans migrating to Manchuria are not being driven out; rather, they are seeking a place for new development in order to live better lives. Therefore, those departing should leave with soaring hopes and joy, and those sending them off should likewise send them with laughter and songs of praise, as they are going to build new Korean villages in a new land and become the founders of that new world. However, those departing always leave with tears, and those sending them off also do so with tears and sighs. I believe this is partly due to misguided, erroneous delusions.
It is with these sentiments that we are now having our compatriots relocate to Manchukuo. I once again emphasize to Premier Zhang not to forget our intentions. Furthermore, Manchukuo is exerting all its strength to realize East Asian peace and the complete development of the five races based on the spirit of Five Races in Harmony (五族協和). Therefore, I believe Manchuria will be the place for the future development of Koreans.
Manchukuo Premier Zhang Jinghui advocates for Japan-Manchuria unity (日滿一體) for the sake of East Asian peace. As Koreans should be given the same duties and rights, it would indeed be deplorable if there were any ethnic discrimination. I hope that he will administer affairs so that such things never happen.
Also, deeply considering the geographical and historical relationship between Chōsen and Manchuria, I ask that you foster ethnic harmony so that the Koreans there can develop freely in a peaceful environment.

Several Points for Improving Policies Towards Koreans

Sŏnman T'akshik Hoesa (Chōsen-Manchuria Development Company) Counselor Kim Tong-jin (金東進)

The advance of Koreans into Manchuria has occurred over the past 70 to 80 years, braving extraordinary persecution and immense difficulties, reaching a population of over one million today. Now that the protection and assistance of the Manchukuo government and the Chōsen Government-General reach the compatriots in Manchuria, and the Sŏnman T'akshik Hoesa has been established for the migration and settlement stabilization of Koreans, I believe their advance and development will become even more vigorous. The innate skill in agriculture, one could say, is a special privilege granted to Koreans by heaven. I hope that the rulers of Manchukuo will follow the principles of Heaven (天理) by guiding, protecting, and assisting Koreans, allowing them to exercise their heavenly mandate (天職) in the development of Manchukuo, thereby manifesting the ideal of Harmony Among Peoples (諸民族協和). I wish to state the following few points:
1. Beyond the Korean migration promotion areas in Jiandao (間島) Province and Dongbiandao (東邊道), the recent designation of primary settlement areas spanning the provinces of Fengtian (奉天), Jilin (吉林), Binjiang (濱江), Longjiang (龍江), and Xing'an Nan (興安南) is indeed a matter of shared celebration (同慶에 不勘). Observing the future distribution status and advance trends of Koreans, I hope for the selection of secondary and tertiary settlement zones.
2. We acknowledge the special privileges granted to Japanese mainlanders in migration to Manchukuo, believing this is the price paid in blood for the two great past campaigns and the Manchurian Incident. However, Koreans also made considerable sacrifices before and after the Incident, and furthermore, the development of Manchuria's agricultural land is undeniably the crystallization of Korean blood and sweat (血汗). Therefore, regarding the selection of migration sites, I hope the Japanese mainlanders will show the magnanimity to yield half the seats (半座) to Koreans.
3. Considering Manchukuo's ideal of Harmony Among Peoples, although there may be not a few disaffected or subversive individuals among the Koreans currently in Manchuria, I believe it is the founding spirit of Manchukuo to accommodate and guide them. It seems an undeniable fact that some local officials harbor a kind of animosity, citing reasons like "Koreans, borrowing Japan's authority (威), look down on the local Manchurian populace," leading to strained relations between the two sides. While such tendencies certainly exist, isn't the poor sentiment of the local Manchurian populace towards Koreans rather due to viewing Koreans as precursors of Japanese imperial authority's advance into Manchuria during the times when anti-Japanese sentiment in the Three Eastern Provinces was fierce? Therefore, I think it is necessary to gauge the emotional expressions of the Manchurian natives towards Chōsen and improve the policies towards Koreans accordingly.

Agreeing with Minister Shinoda's Argument

Yongbo Hapmyŏng Hoesa (Yongbo Partnership Company) President Min Kyu-sik (閔奎植)

Minister Shinoda (篠田) of the Office of the Yi Royal Household (李王職), speaking on the theory of the Korean people's northward advance, acknowledged their outstanding skills in land reclamation in South and North Manchuria over the past 30 to 40 years. He spoke of the achievements established by Korean farmers economically and culturally in various places despite the lack of official protection or financial support, strongly arguing that for the future reclamation of the Manchurian and Mongolian wilderness, the Korean people should be placed at the forefront. Furthermore, Mr. Shinoda advanced his argument, lamenting the poor results where, despite the South Manchuria Railway Company (満鐵) focusing so much on immigration to South Manchuria for the past 30 years, the number barely reached 400,000 to 500,000, and despite the Oriental Development Company (東拓) focusing so much on Korean immigration, the number today does not exceed one million. He stated that many Korean farmers should be sent to the vast lands within Manchukuo in the future. I agree with his words. I also know that knowledgeable people and politicians share this sentiment. Nevertheless, looking at the recent situation, restricting the areas for Korean immigrants under the name of immigration control is utterly unreasonable.
Today, the disposition of Korean farmers is most suitable for being pioneers reclaiming virgin land. Cast aside all prejudices and let the Korean immigrants reclaim this wilderness freely to the best of their abilities. After our efforts have completed the reclamation and pioneering, it would not be impossible for Japanese mainlanders or other peoples among the five races to come and live and cooperate together.
Premier Zhang, I hope you deeply understand this fact, never adopt unjust policies in the future, and quickly abolish the current restrictions.

Immigration Issues and Others

"Manzhou" (滿洲) Chief Editor Kim Sam-min (金三民)

The visit of Manchukuo Prime Minister Zhang Jinghui to Chōsen this time is believed to be highly significant for the friendship between the Korean and Manchurian peoples, and We welcomed him wholeheartedly. However, I deeply regret that due to unavoidable circumstances, I did not have the honor of meeting and talking with him.
Nevertheless, as someone who wishes to express several hopes to the Manchukuo authorities whenever the opportunity arises, I will state the following few hopes in response to your magazine's inquiry.

1. Korean Compatriots Before the Manchurian Incident<10>

The situation of Korean compatriots in Manchuria changed completely with the Manchurian Incident as a turning point. The declarations at the founding of Manchukuo about the Five Races in Harmony and building a Paradise of Kingly Way (王道樂土) remain in Our memory (excerpt-original text)... Thus, everyone compares the time before and after the Manchurian Incident. Here, I would like to briefly describe the situation of Korean compatriots in Manchuria before the incident.
(1) Freedom of residence and friendship between the two peoples (omitted-original text)
(2) Attitude towards bandit damage (omitted-original text)
(3) Low tenant fees
Before the Manchurian Incident, i.e., during the era of free migration, except for parts of Dongbiandao, Jiandao, and areas along the South Manchurian Railway line, the common tenant fee throughout Manchuria was around 30%, meaning a ratio of 3 for the landlord and 7 for the tenant was typical. However, in recent years, not only in the so-called secure farming villages but also in most other areas, it has become averaged out at half, i.e., 50%.

1. The Position of Koreans After the Founding of Manchukuo

There are also several advantages for Koreans in Manchuria after the founding of Manchukuo, as listed below.
(1) Benefits from financial institutions
Even before the Manchurian Incident, under the auspices of the Tōa Kangyō Kaisha (東亞勸業會社 - East Asia Industrial Promotion Company) and the Chōsen Government-General, institutions like financial cooperatives and finance companies existed mainly along the South Manchurian Railway line. However, residents in the interior (奧地) lacked such institutions, causing severe difficulties in obtaining agricultural funds, and the disadvantages and suffering in rural areas due to high-interest payments were immense (宏大). In recent years, financial institutions have appeared in various places, playing a significant role and providing benefits for rural development, which is, above all, a joyous fact.
(2) Group settlements and their advantages
In recent years, the need for defense against bandits (匪賊) has become a prerequisite issue, leading to the emergence of group settlements (集團部落) in various places. These group settlements are absolutely necessary for children's education, sanitation, and other cultural facilities. Numerous group villages have appeared in various parts of Jiandao, and besides those, secure farming villages managed by the Sŏnman T'akshik Hoesa are also becoming group villages, which I believe is very significant.
(3) Employment situation for Koreans
Until the Manchurian Incident, it was extremely rare for Koreans to be employed in Chinese government offices. However, in recent years, several hundred Koreans, albeit in lower ranks, have been employed by Manchukuo. It is said there are over ten at the Chokuninkan (奏任官 - Officials Appointed by the Emperor) level and 40-50 at the Hanninkan (判任官 - Junior Officials) level (estimated, as it was around 30 two years ago). If one includes the patrolmen in Jiandao earning 6 to over 10 yen per month, the total would exceed a thousand. However, this employment mostly involves lower-ranking officials necessary for ruling Koreans, and their numbers are extremely small, so there is little room to see it as a compassionate favor (恩典) towards Koreans. It is merely worth counting as one point if enumerating improvements, however slight, compared to before the Manchurian Incident.

3. Recent Issues Causing Great Harm to Compatriots in Manchuria

While the aforementioned issues can be considered advantages for Koreans in Manchuria, disadvantages, i.e., points adversely affecting Korean development, have become commonplace (비일비재) recently. To avoid tediousness, I will introduce only one or two important points below and earnestly hope for their rapid improvement on the occasion of Premier Zhang's visit to Chōsen.
(1) Issue of restricted residential areas
In the vast expanse of Manchuria, restricting Korean residential areas to parts of Dongbiandao, i.e., Andong (安東) Province, and the Jiandao region, and furthermore, forcing even Koreans who have lived stable lives for many years in the interior of Jilin Province to move into these restricted areas under the threat of various measures if they do not relocate within a set period – the fundamental purpose of this policy is beyond Our speculation. However, if it stems from considerations of national defense along the Soviet-Manchurian border or from public security measures, as is rumored, why can't Koreans be allowed to reside even in central areas like Jilin Province, Fengtian Province, Sanjiang (三江) Province, and Binjiang Province? The already designated restricted areas of Jiandao Province and parts of Andong Province are, from the following points, undeniably the most disadvantageous areas for Koreans.
(a) The part of Andong Province, Dongbiandao, although adjacent to Chōsen, lacks railway facilities, making transportation inconvenient and consequently keeping agricultural prices low.
(b) The interior of Dongbiandao has the poorest public security.
(c) Jiandao and Dongbiandao as a whole have had numerous Korean residents since long ago, making land prices and tenant fees the highest.
(d) Since most arable land in Jiandao is dry fields (田), it is unsuitable for rice cultivation (稻作), and the value of its produce is low (少).
(e) The Dongbiandao area has many mountains and forests (山岳과 삼림) and high elevation (해발), resulting in a poorer climate than the Jilin, Fengtian, and Sanjiang areas.
From the points above, the designated restricted areas can be seen as most disadvantageous for Koreans. However, the reason many migrated there previously was due to its proximity during the era before railways, when transportation was difficult. Residents from P'yŏngan and Hamgyŏng provinces freely crossed the border on foot, carrying belongings (男負女戴). Due to connections with earlier migrants (先移者), the population increased unnoticed (부지불식중), reaching a saturation point today, becoming densely populated.
(2) Issue of travel permits in border areas
This issue, recently announced and implemented, causes immense inconvenience not only for free migrants but also for general travelers, as reported frequently in various newspapers (各 紙上). This is said to be a source of great suffering, especially for residents near Jiandao.

Conclusion

Our hopes for the Manchukuo authorities are numerous, but the two issues detailed above are the most urgent and critical. We hope for their improvement as quickly as possible, even if just one day sooner. Regarding the restriction of residential areas, even if it cannot be completely abolished, we hope that freedom of residence will be granted at least in areas within Fengtian, Jilin, Sanjiang, and Binjiang Provinces where many Koreans have resided since long ago.
Last month, We discussed the gravity of this residential area restriction issue with Governor-General Minami (南). Former Governor-General Ugaki (宇垣) had actively opposed it, preventing its realization. We expressed that its implementation during Governor-General Minami's tenure was deeply regrettable and caused much dissatisfaction among the Korean populace. Governor-General Minami responded that this was by no means approved by the Government-General, but rather an issue debated since Governor-General Ugaki's time that had recently come to fruition, and that the Manchukuo authorities had done this. From this perspective as well, we can only hope that His Excellency Premier Zhang, commemorating his visit to Chōsen (渡鮮), will show special sympathy and consideration towards abolishing or relaxing the restriction on residential areas, which is the root of Korean suffering.

Opening Doors Politically and Economically

Chosŏn San'gŭm Chohap (Korean Gold Producers' Association) Director Yi Sŏng-hwan (李晟煥)

Reporter: Did you meet Manchukuo Premier Zhang during his recent visit to Chōsen?
Yi: I was unable to meet him.<11>
Q: I heard you met and talked with him. Is that not true?
A: Well, speculation is free, so there's nothing to say. I did not have the opportunity or time for that.
Q: Then, do you have no impressions regarding Premier Zhang's visit to Chōsen this time?
A: How could I not? A flood of emotions wells up.
Q: Please share a part of those impressions.
A: It would become too lengthy if I were to talk about it, so let's stop for today.
Q: Then, do you have any message for Premier Zhang who visited Chōsen this time, in other words, any requests?
A: There are heaps of issues, both fundamental and immediate. But what use would listing them all be? Let me just make a couple of requests regarding pressing immediate issues for now. One is to abolish the restriction on Korean immigration zones. Currently, preventing Korean immigrants from entering areas outside Andong Province, Jiandao Province, and four counties within Fengtian Province, and also preventing them from owning land there, contradicts the spirit of Chōsen-Manchuria as one (鮮滿一如). The matter is to strive towards opening the gates of restriction quickly and rather move towards a policy of extending the Jiandao and Dongbiandao areas.
Another is to grant the Korean ethnicity equal opportunities in politics, economy, culture, industry, education, etc., based on the spirit of Five Races in Harmony. It is crucial to thoroughly recognize that the Korean ethnicity is an indispensable component in the foundation of Manchukuo and to show the utmost kindness in nurturing the Koreans in Manchuria from a long-term perspective and a broad national viewpoint.
Q: And do you have anything else to say?
A: Let's leave it at that, as I am busy.

Equal Appointment of Officials and Military Duty

Lawyer Sin T'ae-ak (辛泰嶽)

Although I intended to meet Mr. Zhang Jinghui when he was in Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul), I was unable to see him due to various matters, so I cannot speak of his impression.

Five Races in Harmony was the objective and spirit of Manchukuo's founding. Especially since you, sir, always emphasize Chōsen-Manchuria as one and Five Races in Harmony, I cannot ask you to give special preferential treatment to Koreans in Manchuria, and doing so might not be proper etiquette (禮). My only request to you, sir, is based on the spirit of Five Races in Harmony: I hope you grant Koreans the same rights and duties given to Manchurians or Japanese mainlanders equally. That is, please give exactly the same rights regarding land ownership, appointment of officials, military duty, and everything else. That would be right, and I trust you will do so.

Hoping for Co-education up to Elementary School

Chosŏn Sangŏp Ŭnhaeng (Chōsen Commercial Bank) President Pak Yŏng-ch'ŏl (朴榮喆)

Premier Zhang, on this occasion of your visit, one word I wish to say is, please guide and support (指導 扶液) the one million compatriots in Manchuria according to the spirit of Five Races in Harmony, so there is no discord. Recently, listening to some officials coming from Manchukuo, they say things like, "Koreans are argumentative and only complain" (朝鮮人は 理窟が 多くて, 不平ばかり 云ふて), suggesting they are difficult to harmonize with. However, that is likely because Koreans trust Manchukuo and are just saying a few things about matters within the family; it probably isn't expressing complaints from the heart. Now that even elementary schools are said to have Manchu children and the children of Korean farmers educated together (共學), since elementary students are already living and studying together (同居同學) like this, I hope the restrictions on their residence will be eased as much as possible to give Korean farmers the opportunity for free development.

One or Two Immigration Issues

"P'ip'an" (批判 - Criticism) Magazine Chief Editor Song Pong-u (宋奉瑀)

To meet Manchukuo Premier Zhang Jinghui, one would first need to wear formal attire (예복), which I do not possess. Although I thought I should try my best to meet him, somehow I ended up not meeting him.
What I would like to request from Mr. Zhang Jinghui is... Manchukuo was founded with the goal of Five Races in Harmony and always promotes it. Therefore, it should grant the same duties and the same rights to the five races, and I believe it will do so. However, restricting the area for Korean immigrants to Jiandao Province and parts of Dongbiandao since last September, hindering the free development of Koreans, is unjust both for the Korean people and for the development of Manchukuo, I think. Therefore, instead of applying discriminatory policies unique only to Koreans, I ask that they be granted the exact same rights and duties based on the spirit of Five Races in Harmony.

Let Them Develop According to the Struggle for Existence

Chosŏn Saengmyŏng Pohŏm Hoesa (Chōsen Life Insurance Company) President Han Sang-yong (韓相龍)

As extraterritoriality within Manchukuo will be abolished starting this coming July, please take care to ensure that inconveniences do not arise in the lives of Korean people. Specifically, travel permits, which were not required previously, will become necessary, creating procedures for the entry and exit of farmers and general travelers. Please manage this carefully so as not to hinder the northward advance of Korean farmers.
Also, although it might be done out of various administrative necessities, please relax the policy that restricts Korean immigrants to farming only within certain designated areas as a result of immigration control. Regarding Korean farmers who possess genius (천재) in reclaiming paddy fields (水田) and pioneering forests (산립개척), I hope you will allow them to expand their existence wherever possible according to the laws of the struggle for existence that arise in life.

Considering the Effectiveness

Maeil Sinbo Editor-in-Chief Yu Kwang-ryŏl (柳光烈)

I first met Mr. Zhang Jinghui at the reception welcoming him previously. His portly build and broad chest appeared strong, yet he seemed quite benevolent.
Even if I were to make any request to Mr. Zhang Jinghui, it wouldn't have any effect, and therefore, there is nothing specific I need to request anew.

Let Them Develop Freely

Former Sidae Ilbo President An Hŭi-je (安熙濟)

It has already been over 30 years since Koreans began advancing into Manchuria, and during that time, the number who have flowed into North and South Manchuria has reached one million. Most of those Koreans could not make a living in Chōsen and thus crossed into Manchuria aimlessly (漫然渡滿) without even minimal farming capital (農資), seeking to survive. They received no protection whatsoever, only various forms of persecution, and could not live in peace (安靜). They groaned under indescribable hardships such as plunder by bandits (匪賊), unreasonable exploitation by landlords, coercion by communist forces (共産軍), etc. Only after the new Manchukuo was established with Five Races in Harmony as its fundamental spirit did they finally find new hope and peace. They had been developing with new vigor throughout North and South Manchuria, but last September, the area for Korean immigrants was restricted to Jiandao Province and Dongbiandao Province (東邊省 - *Note: likely means Dongbiandao, part of Andong Province*), once again blocking the free development of Korean farmers. How regrettable this is! In Manchukuo, which aims for the equal development of the Five Races, restricting only Koreans to reside in Jiandao Province and Dongbiandao, where transportation is inconvenient, the land is poor (좃치 못하고), and land prices (地價) are expensive (빗싼), is unjust not only concerning the interests of Koreans but also for the true development of Manchukuo itself, I believe. Premier Zhang Jinghui of Manchukuo - based on the fundamental spirit of Five Races in Harmony, please grant Koreans the same duties and rights as other peoples, allowing them to develop freely in a free environment.<20>

**Summary**

This article compiles pleas from prominent Korean figures (businessmen, intellectuals, officials) addressed to Manchukuo Premier Zhang Jinghui during his 1937 visit to Chōsen. The central theme is the demand for fair treatment of the over one million Korean immigrants in Manchukuo, aligning with Manchukuo's proclaimed ideal of "Five Races in Harmony." The most consistently raised grievance is the recent policy restricting Korean settlement to specific, less desirable areas like Jiandao and Dongbiandao, hindering their agricultural and economic development. Contributors argue for the abolition or relaxation of these restrictions, equal rights (land ownership, government employment, military service), and recognition of Koreans' contributions to Manchukuo's development, especially in agriculture. Some express hope in Manchukuo's potential while others contrast the current situation unfavorably with the pre-Manchukuo era regarding tenant fees and freedom of movement, despite acknowledging some improvements like financial institutions and security measures (group settlements). The article reveals the anxieties and aspirations of the Korean elite regarding the fate of their compatriots under Japanese-controlled Manchukuo, highlighting the tension between the rhetoric of pan-Asian harmony and the reality of discriminatory policies. Historians might find valuable the specific grievances articulated (restricted zones, travel permits, perceived discrimination compared to Japanese), the justifications used (Korean agricultural prowess, historical contributions, sacrifices), the appeals to Manchukuo's founding ideals, and the nuanced views on the benefits and drawbacks of Manchukuo compared to the pre-Incident era. The mention of discussions with Japanese Governor-Generals (Ugaki, Minami) about these policies is also significant.

잡지명	삼천리 제9권 제4호
발행일	1937년 05월 01일
기사제목	在滿 백만 동포의 발전을 위하야, 조선에 온 張총리에 呈하는 我等의 書
기사형태	논설

在滿 백만 동포의 발전을 위하야, 조선에 온 張총리에 呈하는 我等의 書
차별대우를 하지 맙시다

中樞院 參議崔麟

내가 총리 張景惠씨를 처음 맛난 것은 전번 滿洲시찰시엿는데 씨는 氣象이 쾌활하고 움즉일 수 없이 억세인 것이 무언중에 군인 출신임을 알려주면서 그러면서도 어대인가 인자한 성품을 보여 상대자의 마음을 끄을게 하든 것이외다.

조선인은 滿洲로 이주하야 가는 것은 쪽겨가는 것이 아니라 보담 더 잘 살기 위하야 새로운 발전지를 차저가는 것이외다. 그러니 떠나는 자 洋洋한 희망과 깁붐으로 떠날 것이요 보내는 자는 역시, 새 땅에 새로운 조선촌을 건설하고 그 새로운 천지의 始祖가 될 그들을 보내는 만치 우숨과 찬양가로 보내여야 할 것이엇만 언제나 가는 자 눈물로 떠나고 보내는 이 역시 눈물과 한숨으로 보내니 이는 일부의 그릇된 착오적 망상인가 함니다.
우리는 지금 이러한 감정으로 동포를 滿洲國에 작고 移居케 함니다. 이 우리 뜻을 있지 말기를 張총리에게 더 한 번 말슴해 둠니다. 그리고 滿洲國은 5族協和의 정신에 의하야 동양 평화와 5민족의 완전한 발전을 실현함에 가진 힘을 다 하고 있으니 조선인의 장차 발전할 곳은 滿洲일가 함니다.
滿洲國 총리 張景惠씨는 동양 평화를 위하야 日滿一體를 역설하니 조선인도 같은 의무와 권리를 줄 것으로 거기에 조곰도 민족 차별을 두어서는 실로 통한할 바인즉 결코 그런 일이 없도록 施政하여 주기를 바람니다.
또 조선과 滿洲의 지리와 역사적 관계를 심심히 고려하야 민족적 감정을 융화식혀 그곳에 있는 조선인이 평화스러운 가온대 자유롭게 발전하도록 하야주시옵기를 부탁하나의다.

對朝鮮人 정책의 개선 數點

鮮滿拓植會社參事 金東進

조선인의 滿洲 진출은 과거 7, 8십년간 비상한 박해와 막심한 곤란이 있음을 무릅쓰고 진출하야 于今에 백여만으로 헤이게 되었읍니다. 이제 滿洲國 정부, 조선 총독부의 보호와 원조가 在滿 동포에 밋치고 또 조선인의 이주와 그 誘導안정의 사업을 위하야 鮮滿拓植會社가 설립되엿스매 그 진출 발전은 더욱 왕성할 줄 압니다. 조선인의 선천적이라고도 할 농경의 技倆은 한울이 조선인에게 부여한 특권이라 滿洲國 위정자는 天理에 순응하야 조선인을 지도 보호 원조하야써 滿洲國 개발에 그 天職을 발휘케 하고 이로써 諸民族協和의 이상을 顯現케 함을 바라고저 左記 몃 가지를 말슴들이고저 합니다.
1. 間島省, 東邊道의 조선인 이주 장려지 외에 今般 새로히 奉天, 吉林, 濱江, 龍江, 興安南의 각 성에 亘하야 제1차적 집결지역으로 지정된 것은 실로 同慶에 不勘하는 바이외다. 장차 조선인의 분포 상태와 진출의 형세를 살피어 제2차적, 제3차적의 집결 구역의 선정이 있기를 바람니다.
2. 滿洲國 이주에 일본 내지인에 부여하는 특권을 우리는 시인하는 바이니 이는 과거 兩大戰役과 滿洲事變의 유혈의 代價이라고 밋기 때문이외다. 그러나 조선인도 事變 전후엔 상당한 희생을 바치었으며 더욱 滿洲의 농경지 개발은 실로 조선인의 血汗의 結晶임을 부인할 수 없으니 이주지 선정에 대하야 일본 내지인은 조선인에게 半座를 양여할 아량을 보혀주기를 바람니다.
3. 만주국의 諸民族協和의 이상으로 보아 현하 在滿 조선인중에 불온, 불순한 사람도 不少할 것이나 此를 용납 선도함이 滿洲國의 건국, 정신인가합니다. 지방관 중엔 「조선인은 일본의 威를 假하야 滿洲地元民을 멸시한다.」는 이유로 일종의 증오심을 抱懷하기 때문에 피차 석연치 못한 관계에 있는 것이 不誣할 사실인가합니다. 물론 이런 경향이 없지 안치만은 그보다도 滿洲地元民의 對조선인 감정이 양호치 못함은 과거 東三省의 排日思想이 치열할 시에 조선인을 日本國威의 만주 진출의 선구자이라고 간주한 까닭에 있지 안은가 합니다. 그러니 滿洲 본토인의 조선에 대한 감정 발로를 程度하야 對조선인 정책을 개선할 필요가 있다고 생각합니다.

篠田장관의 논거에 동감

永保合名會社社長 閔奎植

篠田 李王職 장관이 조선민족북진론을 말하되 과거 3, 4십년래 南北滿洲에서 토지개간에서 그 탁월한 기술을 인정하엿고 관헌의 보호나 재정의 후원이 없었음에도 불구하고 각처에서 경제적, 문화적으로 조선 농민의 扶植한 공적을 말하야, 진실로 금후 滿蒙황야의 개척에 있어선 조선민족을 先進시킬것이라고 역설하엿스며 또 篠田씨는 그 논지를 進하야 일본 내지인은 그 天與의 기질과 鄕土愛好性癖 때문에 과거 30년간에 亘하야 滿鐵에서 南滿이민에 그러케도 주력하엿건만 그 수 불과 4, 50만이요, 東拓이 조선 이민에 그러케도 주력하엿슴에도 불구하고 금일 백만을 넘지 못하는 不成績을 통한하면서, 금후 滿洲國 내의 너른 토지에는 조선 농민을 많이 보내라고 말하엿습니다. 나는 씨의 말에 동감함니다. 또한 識者나 爲政家가 이에 同感이할 줄 암니다. 그러함에도 불구하고 최근의 현상을 보건대 이민 통제의 일홈 아래 조선 이민에 대하야 그 지역을 제한한다한 즉, 이러케 불합리한 일이 없을 줄 암니다.
금일 조선 농민의 소질은 처녀지대를 개척할 역군되기에 가장 적합함니다. 모든 편견을, 버리고, 조선 이민으로 하여곰 제 마음대로 제 힘껏 이 황야를 개척하게 내버려두서요. 그래서 우리 힘으로 개간 개척이 완성된 뒤 일본 내지인이든 5族 중의 타민족이 와서 가치 협동하여 살어도 불가함이 없을 것이외다.
張총리께서는 이 사실을 깁히 이해하여 금후 결코 부당한 정책을 취하지 마시며, 현재의 제한을 하로 급히 철폐하여 주시기를 바라나이다.

이민문제 기타

「滿洲」主筆 金三民

금번 滿洲國總理大臣 張景惠씨의 조선 내방은 鮮滿 양민족의 친선상에 매우 의의있는 바로 밋고 吾人은 충심으로 환영한 바이었으나 부득이한 사정으로 拜談의 영광을 엇지 못하엿슴을 유감히 想覺함니다.
그러나 滿洲國 당국자에 대하야는 기회만 있으면 幾個의 희망은 말하고저 하는 자임으로 귀사의 무르시는 바에 대하야 다음과 같은 약간의 희망을 말삼드림니다.

1. 滿洲事變 전과 조선동포<10>

滿洲事變을 계기로 하야 在滿 조선동포의 사정은 일변하엿는대 滿洲 건국 당초의 5族協和, 王道樂土의 건설이란 선언은 吾人의 기억에 남어 있으나(중략-원문) 그리하야 만인이 모두 滿洲事變 전후을 대조하여 봄니다. 이에 간단히 사변 전의 在滿 조선동포의 사정을 말삼드리고저 함니다.
(1) 거주의 자유와 兩族의 친선(略-원문)
(2) 匪賊被害態度(略-원문)
(3) 소작료의 저렴
만주사변 전 즉 자유이주시대에 있어서는 東邊道의 일부, 間島及南滿鐵道沿線의 일부 지대를 제외하고는 全滿的으로 공통하야 소작료는 3할 내외 즉 지주 3 소작인 7의 비율이 보통이 있읍니다. 그러나 근년에 至하야는 소위 안전 농촌은 물론이요 其外도 대부분이 절반 즉 5할 평균화케 되엿습니다.

1. 滿洲 건국 후의 조선인의 입장

滿洲 건국 후의 在滿조선인의 유리한 점도 左의 幾個가 있읍니다.
(1) 금융기관의 혜택
滿洲事變 전에도 東亞勸業會社와 조선총독부의 후원하에서 주로 滿鐵沿線에 금융조합이니 금융회란 기관이 있기는 하엿스나 奧地 거주자에는 이런 기관에 결여되여서 農資金에 곤란이 막심하엿고, 고리 지불에 농촌의 불리와 고통이 宏大하엿는대 근년은 각지에 금융기관이 출현되여 농촌 발전상에 중대한 역할과 이익을 주고 있는 것이 무엇보담도 환희할 사실임니다.
(2) 集團部落과 유리점
근년에 至하야 匪賊 방비의 필요가 선결문제로 集團部落이 각지에 출현되는 중이나 아동 교육상, 위생적 기타 문화적 시설상에 이 集團部落은 절대 필요한 것인대 間島의 각처에는 다수한 집단촌이 출현되고 其外에도 鮮滿拓植會社 경영의 안전 농촌 등은 집단촌화하여 감으로 매우 의의 있는 일이라고 밋습니다.
(3) 조선인의 취직상황
滿洲事變 전까지는 조선인의 중국 관공서 취직은 극히 희귀한 일이었으나, 근년에 至하야 비록 하급이나마 幾百名의 조선인이 滿洲國에 채용되엿슴니다. 奏任級 십 수인 判任級 4, 50인(2년 전까지는 30명 내외이엇는대 이 4, 50명이란 것은 추정에 불과함)이 되엿다하며 間島의 月俸 6원 내지 십 수원의 순사까지를 다 계산한다면 천 명 이상에 달할 것임니다. 그러나, 이 취직은 조선인 통치상에 필요한 대부분의 하급관리에 불과하며 그 수도 극소함으로 조선인에 동정한 恩典이라고 볼 여지도 없고 이에 특필할 재료도 되지 아니하나 滿洲事變 전에 대비하야 다소라도 호전되엿다는 점으로 구구히 열거한다면 그 一에 算할 것 뿐임니다.

3. 최근의 在滿동포와 大打擊 문제

叙上한 諸문제를 在滿 조선인에 유리한 점이라 하면 이하에는 불리한 점 즉 輓近에 至하야 조선인 발전상에 악영향을 주는 점이 비일비재라 하겟스나 繁을 피하기 위하야 1, 2의 중요한 점만을 左에 소개하야 張총리의 조선 내방을 계기로 하야 此의 급속적 개선을 切望함니다.
(1) 거주지역 국한문제
광활한 滿洲에서 조선인의 거주구역으로 東邊道 즉 安東省의 일부와 間島지방에 국한하야 금후의 이민은 자유이민이나 집단 이민은 물론이요 吉林省 오지에서 다년 안정한 생활을 하여온 조선인까지를 이 제한지역 내로 이주를 강제하고 일정한 기한 내에 이주치 아니하는 자는 여러 가지 수단 방법으로 있지 못하기 한다하니 이 정책의 근본 목적이 那邊에 있는가는 吾人의 추측을 許치 아니하나 세간에 전하는 바와 갓치 蘇滿 국경의 국방적 견지 또는 치안 유지책에서 出한 것이라 하면 엇지 중앙지대인 吉林省, 奉天省, 三江省 濱江省같은 지대까지라도 조선인의 거주를 許치 못할 것인가. 이미 지정 제한된 間島省 及 安東省의 일부는 左의 諸點으로 보와서 가장 조선인에 불리한 지대라 아니할 수 없읍니다.
(가) 安東省의 일부란 東邊道 일대는 조선에 접근되엿다 하나 철도의 便이 없어서 교통이 불편하고 따라서 농산물가가 저렴한 것.
(나) 東邊道의 오지는 가장 치안이 불완전한 것.
(다) 間島 東邊道 전체가 종전부터 조선인의 거주민이 數多하야 地價와 소작료가 제일 고귀한 것.
(라) 間島는 경지의 대부분이 田임으로 稻作에 不適하며 그 생산물의 가치가 少한 것.
(마) 東邊道 일대는 산악과 삼림이 多하고 해발이 高하야 吉林省 奉天省 三江省 일대보다 기후가 조치 못한 것.
이상의 諸點으로 보와서 이미 국한된 지대는 조선인에 가장한 불이익 지대라 볼 수 있으나 종전부터 다수가 이주케 된 이유는 철도가 나기 전 즉 교통 불편한 시대에 있어서 인접지인 관계상 평안도 함경도 지방의 주민이 자유로 男負女戴하야 도보 월경 이주한 것이매 先移者의 인연으로 부지불식중에 증가되여 今日은 포화상태에 至할 만큼 조밀케 된 것임니다.
(2) 국경지대 여행 증명문제
輓近에 발표 실시중의 문제인대 자유 이주민은 물론이요 일반 여행자에 불편이 막심하다는 것은 각 紙上에 屢報되는 바이며 此는 특히 間島 부근의 주민에 일대 고통의 거리라 함니다.

結言

吾人의 滿洲國 당국자에 대한 희망은 허다하다 하겟스나 상술의 兩問題가 가장 긴급한 중대문제임으로 1일이라도 급속히 개선하되 거주구역 제한문제는 全然히 철폐치는 못한다 할지라도 奉天, 吉林, 三江 濱江 諸省 중 특히 종전부터 조선인이 다수 거주하는 지대만은 거주의 자유를 주기를 바람니다.
月前에 吾人이 南총독에 대하야 이 거주구역 제한문제의 중대성을 논하고 宇垣 前총독은 此를 적극 반대하엿슴으로 실현되지 아니하엿는대 南총독 시대에 入하야 此의 실현을 보게된 것은 매우 유감되는 일이라고 조선민중간에는 많은 불만이 있다는 말을 하엿더니 南총독은 이것은 결코 총독부에서 찬성한 바가 아니요 宇垣 총독시대부터 문제되여 오든 것이 근일에 와서 실현된 것인대 滿洲國 당국자가 이와 갓치 한 것이라 하엿스니 이 견지에서 보드래도 張총리 각하는 渡鮮을 기념으로 하야 이 조선인 고통의 근본이 되는 거주지역 제한의 철폐 又는 완화에 특별한 동정과 고려가 있기를 바랄 뿐임니다.
