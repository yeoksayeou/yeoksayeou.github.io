原稿料에 厚한 朝鮮
						草兵丁
이것은 逆說이 아니고 事實

Recently, Mr. Yi Sŏn-gŭn (李瑄根), the executive director of Hansŏng Books (漢城圖書), returned from observing the state of publishing culture in the Tokyo area and related the secret story that "Korean manuscript fees are more expensive than Tokyo's."

According to Mr. Yi, while the manuscript fees for newspapers and magazines are uncertain, he thoroughly investigated the actual situation regarding the publication of standalone books. What we might know is that famous writers like Kikuchi Kan (菊池寬) and Yamamoto Yūzō (山本有三) seem to receive royalties of 30-40%, but the reality is different. Those at the level of Kikuchi, Tanizaki (谷崎), and Yamamoto barely receive 10%. Other second-rate writers get 8%, and third-rate writers commonly receive 5%.

However, looking at the situation in Chosŏn (朝鮮), the highest peak, Ch'unwŏn (春園), generally gets 20%. Even first-rate writers like Kim Yŏng (箕永), Ansŏ (岸曙), T'aejun (泰俊), and Sangsŏp (想涉) receive over 10%, and besides them, 10% is generally the market rate. How well-treated Korean writers are! (Forgive me, I had to say it for the sake of emphasis!)

Does this mean that because Tokyo has enormous sales figures, the lump sum received from royalties is also large? No. Even there, no matter how famous a work is, the first printing is merely about 1,000 copies, and among those, a run of 500 copies is common. In Chosŏn, however, a print run means no less than 1,000 copies. Isn't this clear? This is not meant in the slightest to suggest lowering royalties in the Korean market. (Ch'obyŏngjŏng 草兵丁) <50>

**Summary**

The article, written by "Ch'obyŏngjŏng," reports on findings by Yi Sŏn-gŭn of Hansŏng Books, comparing manuscript royalties for book publishing in Chosŏn (Korea) and Tokyo in 1937. Contrary to potential assumptions, it claims Korean writers receive significantly higher royalty rates (10-20%) compared to their Japanese counterparts (5-10%), even top authors like Kikuchi Kan. Furthermore, initial print runs in Korea are typically 1,000 copies, whereas in Tokyo, they are often only 500-1,000, even for famous works. The author emphasizes this isn't a call to lower Korean rates but highlights the comparatively favorable financial treatment of Korean authors. For historians, this piece offers an interesting glimpse into the colonial publishing economy, suggesting Korean writers, despite political constraints, held a certain economic standing or bargaining power within their market compared to writers in the metropole, potentially due to market structure or publisher practices specific to colonial Korea. It also names prominent Korean authors of the period and provides specific figures for comparison.

이것은 逆說이 아니고 事實

漢城圖書專務 李瑄根 씨 日間 東京方面의 출판문화 형편을 시찰하고 도라와서 「朝鮮 稿料가 東京보다 너 빗싸다」는 秘事를 말햇다.

씨의 말에 의하면 신문 잡지의 원고료는 未詳하나 단행본 출판에 있어서는 그 진상을 철저히 究明한 바 우리가 알기엔 菊池寬 山本有三 等 저명한 文人 것은 3, 4割의 印稅을 내는 드시 알드시 실상은 不然하여 菊池, 谷崎, 山本級이 겨우 1割, 그밧게 2류는 8分, 3류는 보통 5分가 通例이라고.

그런데 朝鮮 형편을 보면 最高峰의 春園 에 大槪 2割, 箕永
岸曙
泰俊
想涉等 1류에 至하여도 1割 이상, 그박에도 대개 1割은 시세다. 이 얼마나 朝鮮文人은 厚遇(語勢上 부득이 그랫노라 용서하라!)을 입는고

그렇다고 東京은 판매 부수가 엄청나게 많으니 印稅 밧는 一時金도 많다고 보는가. 否라 거기서도 아모리 유명한 것이라도 초판은 겨우 千部 정도, 個中에는 5백부 한판이 보통이라는데 조선은 한판이라면 천부 이하는 없다. 이로 알 바 아닌가. 이러타고 조선시장서 印稅를 나추자는 뜻은 千萬秋毫도 없노라.(草兵丁) <50>
