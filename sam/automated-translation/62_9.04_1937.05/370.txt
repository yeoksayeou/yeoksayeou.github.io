**Body Text Translation:**
The Hamhŭng Envoy's "Im Kkŏkchŏng," When Will Pyŏkch'o's Idle Days End?

Did he go on an errand to Paektu Mountain again? Did he go to the plum blossom field to relieve himself after a meal? If not that, did he enter the deep, remote mountains (九重深山) to plot a righteous uprising (擧義謀事)? Like a Hamhŭng envoy, "Im Kkŏkchŏng" has gone without a trace.

Dumas, who wrote "The Count of Monte Cristo"; Tolstoy, who wrote "War and Peace"; Hugo, who wrote "Les Misérables"; Tōson Shimazaki (島崎藤村) of "Before the Dawn" (菩薩峙와 「날이 밝기 전」); Kaisan Nakazato (中里介山) – although none of these works may have been completed in one breath (一氣呵成), the discontinuity was not as severe as with "Im Kkŏkchŏng."

The writer Pyŏkch'o (碧初) must feel a sense of responsibility and duty. Has he forgotten that the incompletion of an artwork is "better left unstarted" (不如不始作)?

Moreover, the discerning readership hopes for the completion of Im Kkŏkchŏng for these three reasons. We ask this "in the name of the people." Namely:
1. Im Kkŏkchŏng is a more realistic figure than Hŏ Saeng (許生). His life story, passed down through unofficial histories (야사), matches the righteous giant (의인 거인) the masses sought. Therefore, this character and his deeds greatly attract the interest of the era's public sentiment.
2. In his past works, one can find remarkably pure Korean sentiment (조선 정조) and vocabulary, allowing the writer Pyŏkch'o's bloodline (혈관) to realistically connect directly to the reader's veins (혈맥).
3. It is the first great work in the Korean literary world that is long and has a grand story.<53>

**Summary**

This short essay expresses frustration over the long hiatus in the serialization of the novel *Im Kkŏkchŏng* by Pyŏkch'o (Hong Myŏnghŭi). The author compares the delay unfavorably to the completion of major world literary works and urges Pyŏkch'o to finish the novel out of responsibility. Three reasons are given for its importance: Im Kkŏkchŏng's status as a realistic folk hero aligning with public desire, the work's embodiment of pure Korean sentiment and vocabulary, and its significance as a major, large-scale Korean literary achievement.

From a historical perspective, the article reveals the high cultural value placed on *Im Kkŏkchŏng* during the colonial period as a significant national literary project. The emphasis on "pure Korean sentiment and vocabulary" and the character as a "righteous giant the masses sought" highlights the novel's role in fostering a sense of Korean identity and reflecting popular aspirations under Japanese rule. The plea "in the name of the people" underscores the collective anticipation and the perceived national importance of completing this work.

함흥차사의 「林巨正」, 碧初의 閑日月 언제 끗치나
또 백두산으로 심부름갓나 매화밧헤 식후 볼일 보러 갓나. 그러치 안으면 九重深山에 擧義謀事로 드러갓나. 「林巨正」은 함흥차사갓치 一去 무소식이다.

「몬테, 크리스도 백작」을 쓴 뚜-마 「전쟁과 평화」를 쓴 톨스토이 「레미제라뿔」을 쓴 유-고- 菩薩峙와 「날이 밝기 전」의 島崎藤村, 中里介山 모다 一氣呵成으로 된 것은 아닐지나 「林巨正」가치 불연속선이 심하지는 안햇다.

작가 碧初 는 책임과 의무를 늣겨야 할 것이다. 예술품의 미완성이란 「不如不始作」임을 이젓는가.

더구나 林巨正은 이 셰 가지 이유로 기어히 끗내주기를 뜻있는 독서층은 희망한다. 이를 「민중의 일홈」으로 청한다. 즉
1 林巨正은 「許生」보다 더 실재적 인물이요. 야사로 전해오는 그 일대기가 대중이 구하든 의인 거인에 합치한 것 그러니까 이 인물과 그 행적은 시대 인심의 흥미를 끄으는 바 큰 것
2 과거의 그 작품에서 놀납게 순수한 조선 정조와 어휘를 차즐 수 있어 작가 碧初의 혈관이 그대로 읽는 이의 혈맥에 현실적으로 통해지는 것
3 길고 스토리 큰 조선문단 최초의 대작인 것<53>
