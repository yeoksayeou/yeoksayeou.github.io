Do You Know?, Research Lab on the Reality of Chosŏn
5 Thousand Factories
Do you know how many factories there are in Chosŏn that produce any kind of manufactured goods, whether they are rice mills, rubber factories, or factories making luxury items like clothing, textiles, or gold, silver, and precious jewelry? According to a recent survey, the numbers are:

11 Million Won Goes Up in Smoke
Just as the song "Guya guya dambaeguya" [a folk tune about tobacco] flows along nicely, there is simply no place, here or there, without the smoke of people smoking tobacco. And so, between drinking alcohol and smoking tobacco, our pockets empty day by day. Now, as for how much tobacco was sold by the Government-General's Tobacco Monopoly Bureau from April to July this year, a period of about 4 months, it is said to be a total of 11,553,000 Won. How much money is just flying away like coal and charcoal!... <26>

**Summary**

This short news piece from 1930 reports two statistics about colonial Korea (Chosŏn). First, it lists the number of factories by province, totaling 5,342, highlighting a significant concentration in Kyŏnggi-do and Kyŏngsangnam-do. Second, it states that tobacco sales by the Government-General's Monopoly Bureau amounted to over 11.5 million Won in just four months (April-July 1930), lamenting this expenditure. For historians, this article provides specific quantitative data on the distribution of early industrialization across Korea and the significant revenue generated (and consumer spending involved) by the state-controlled tobacco monopoly during the colonial period. It reflects contemporary awareness of economic conditions and perhaps a subtle critique of consumer spending habits under colonial rule.
