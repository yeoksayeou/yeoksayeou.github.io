Gratitude and Apology

They say cold snow has already flown near Paektu Mountain, and in these times when the price of rice is low and money is scarce, I wonder how everyone on the Peninsula is faring. As I sit here making this magazine, which is only sent out after receiving payment, I cannot suppress a feeling of apprehension. Isn't the price of the book, 20 chŏn, the cost of one *toe* of rice? Far from adding to your resources, I wonder if I am instead diminishing them by one *toe* each—I intend only to pour all my energy into striving to create a magazine worth reading for you all. What can be done about my inherent lack of talent, but would my sincerity lag behind others?

The next issue is for the last month of the Year of Kyŏngo (1930). With the intention of providing a final summation of this year, we will publish the next issue on December 1st with substantial content.

As I am about to put down my pen, newspaper extras announcing that a subjugation order (討伐令) has been issued in Taiwan (臺灣) are circulating clamorously throughout Seoul City. In any case, the world is gradually growing tense. (P'ain)<70>

**Summary**

The editor, writing under the pen name P'ain, expresses gratitude to readers while apologizing for the magazine's cost during a time of economic hardship (low rice prices, scarce money). He acknowledges that the 20 chŏn price equals a measure of rice and pledges to compensate with sincere effort and quality content, despite his perceived lack of talent. He announces the upcoming December issue, which will summarize the year 1930, and concludes by noting the tense world situation, specifically mentioning news of a "subjugation order" in Taiwan circulating in Seoul. For historians, the piece offers insight into the economic struggles faced by Koreans and the press under colonial rule, the editor's direct address and sense of responsibility towards readers, and a contemporary, albeit brief, Korean perspective on events elsewhere in the Japanese empire, such as the likely reference to the Musha Incident in Taiwan, indicating an awareness of broader imperial developments.

感謝와 謝罪
白頭山近傍에는 벌서 찬눈이 날렷다는데 쌀갑은 싸지고 돈은 귀하여진 이 때에 半島의 여러분은 엇더게 지내시는지 돈을 밧고야 비로소 보내드리는 이 雜誌를 만들고 안젓는 저는 한갓 悚懼한 마음을 禁할길이 업슴니다. 冊갑 20錢이면 쌀 한 되가 아님니까. 보태여 드리지는 못한대도 도로혀 한되씩 축내드리는 가 함애-오직 精力을 다하여 여러분이 보실만한 雜誌를 만들어 드리기에 애쓸가 함니다. 원악 업는 才操야 엇저리까만은 精誠이야 남에게 뒤지리까.

來號는 庚午年의 마즈막 달이외다. 今年을 總決算하는 意味에서 充實한 內容을 가지고 12月1日에 次號 發刊하겟나이다.

붓을 떼려하는데 臺灣에는 討伐令이 내렷다는 號外가 서울 市內에 요란히 돔니다. 아무튼 世上은 漸漸 緊張하여 감니다. (파인)＜70＞
