New Rising China and the Military Academy
Chu Yo-han

When Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石), a pale-faced scholar under Sun Wen (孫文), established the Huangpu Military Academy (黃浦軍官學校) in Guangdong (廣東) and was teaching military drills to fewer than a thousand middle school students, the various warlords of China scoffed at it. Even senior military figures within the Kuomintang (KMT) faction paid no attention, thinking, "What could someone like Chiang Kai-shek possibly achieve?"

Seeing that the seeds of the Central Direct Command Army, which today has conquered the land from the Pearl River (珠江) in the south to the Yellow River (黃河) in the north, were sown there, even those warlords who had sneered must feel the passage of time. The student army that became the terror of the Northern Army in the early days of the Northern Expedition consisted of the first and second graduating classes of the Huangpu Military Academy, and the elite troops of renowned generals of the Direct Command Army today, such as Liu Chih (劉峙) and Ku Chu-t'ung (顧祝同), also saw their genesis (胚胎) during this period.

After the Northern Expedition was half completed and the Nationalist Government moved to Nanjing (南京), the Huangpu Military Academy also relocated to Nanjing and became the Central Military Academy (中央軍官學校). On an autumn day in 1928, as the setting sun cast its yellow light onto the dusty streets of the Jinling City (金陵城 - Nanjing), I had the opportunity to visit the Central Military Academy and the Training Division (敎導團) located at Taiping Bridge (太平橋). The Training Division selected experienced officers from the first and second classes of the Military Academy who had combat experience and retrained them.

As there was a student of Chosŏn (朝鮮) origin among the enrolled students, I first sought him out. After giving my name card at the gate and stating my purpose (來意), they gave me a form and asked me to write my address, name, the person I wished to meet, and the purpose of my visit. Dozens of visitors were all doing the same, sitting in a large office-cum-waiting room. Visiting hours were from 5:00 PM to 5:30 PM (?), and there were still about 20 minutes left until 5:00 PM. I passed the dull time reading a Chinese newspaper and looking at the things hanging on the wall: the Premier's last instructions (總理遺訓), the Map of National Humiliation (國耻地圖), <7> the Outline for Propaganda on the National Day (國慶日 宣傳大綱), and the duty roster for the officer of the watch (値星官).

After a short while, two soldiers carrying bugles walked to the center of the training ground, stood with the red setting sun behind them, and played a tune, "Ta-ta-ta-ta." It seemed to be the signal that class time was over and visiting hours had begun. In the middle of the opposite training ground was a platform, and on the platform was a background made of brick (鍊瓦). A glass window was visible, and inside, a portrait of Sun Wen (孫文遣像) and his last testament were enshrined. From behind it, a group of cadet officers emerged, most of them heading towards the duty room where we were sitting.

The visitors rushed to find the people they came to see, and a great commotion arose as they tried to share many feelings in a short time. If there had been a beauty with bobbed hair and a short skirt, it would have made for a very dramatic scene, but unfortunately, there was no such scene that day.

No matter how long I waited, the student I came to see did not appear. Ten minutes, twenty minutes, twenty-five minutes passed, and visiting hours were almost over, yet there was no sign of him. Thinking I might have missed him, I was preparing to leave when the loud sound of horse hooves (馬蹄聲) came from outside the main gate. I looked out and saw a troop of cavalry returning from field exercises.

A troop of spirited officers (精悍한 士官) in khaki uniforms, mounted on sturdy-looking (屈强하게 생긴) Chinese military horses that were not particularly tall, entered the middle of the training ground in perfect formation, their hooves clattering. Standing alone, watching their procession illuminated by the setting sun, my mind drifted away from reality into the realm of dreams. As I conjured visions of them commanding the deserts and plains of the Central Plains (中原), though my body was in Nanjing, my mind wandered far and wide.

After performing several routine ceremonial drills, they dismissed. Grooms (馬丁) led the horses around the training ground several times before taking them into the stables (馬廐). Lost in observation, I finally returned to reality and was about to turn back when the Chosŏn student I knew called out, chasing after me from behind. He said he had just returned from outdoor exercises with the cavalry, saw my name card in his room, and rushed over. His appearance, in a dust-covered uniform with his leggings still on, also seemed deeply moving.

Since it was already late that day, we agreed to meet the next day (翌日). I returned the following morning and toured the Training Division and the Military Academy under the guidance of Officer Hong (洪某氏), the officer of the watch. I couldn't help but feel grateful to them for guiding a newspaper reporter from Chosŏn so kindly and courteously (丁嚀하게).

At that time, the Central Military Academy was directly under the Military Affairs Commission of the Nationalist Government; now it seems likely under the Directorate General of Military Training. Chiang Kai-shek still held the position of Commandant (校長). Although Chiang Kai-shek held titles like Commander-in-Chief of the National Revolutionary Army and Chairman of the Nationalist Government, it is said he never used phrases like "This Commander" or "This Chairman" when speaking. He usually referred to himself as "This humble person" (卑人) or "I" (我). However, when speaking at the Military Academy, it was reportedly his habit to always refer to himself as "This Commandant" (本敎長).

Except for the large auditorium under construction, the Military Academy consisted entirely of buildings from the old Warlord Era (督軍時代). A two-story brick building (鍊瓦屋) stood at the entrance, serving as offices, classrooms, and lecture halls. From there, dormitories flanked a central training ground, and opposite them, the large auditorium was under construction. It has likely been completed by now, presenting a magnificent view (偉觀).

Next to the lecture hall was the dining hall. At the front of the dining hall, as per custom, were the Blue Sky with a White Sun flag (靑天白日旗, i.e., the military flag) and Premier Sun's portrait (遺像) and last will (遺囑). Pointing to a seat below them, the guide said:
"This seat is Commandant Chiang's. Although the Commandant is busy (分망하시지마는) with state affairs (國務), he usually comes here for breakfast, eats the same meal (飯食) as the students, and gives instructions. Also, once a week, he comes in the evening to share a meal with the students."
In this single explanation, I felt I had discovered the reason why Chiang Kai-shek's direct command army was strong.

Currently, the 6th and 7th classes were being trained, and it was said that over 8,000 individuals had graduated so far.
The training period was only one year, an unavoidable measure due to the urgent needs of the revolutionary army. Therefore, the Training Division (敎導團) was established to select those with practical combat experience for retraining.
Originally, there were many Russian instructors, but none remained now, and Japanese officers had never been invited. He explained that there were recent plans to hire many German officers.

What particularly caught my eye were the subjects included in the curriculum, such as "Three Principles of the People" (三民主義), "History of Chinese Diplomacy" (中國 外交史), <8> and "Explanation of Party Principles" (黨義 釋解), which heavily incorporated KMT political theory. Also noteworthy was that each company (中隊) had a separate sign reading "Kuomintang Capital Special Municipality Party Headquarters Military Special Branch No. X District No. X Section" (國民黨首都特別市黨部軍隊特別支部第何區第何區分部), and relatedly, the fact that KMT party (黨部) activities were naturally progressing among the officer cadets.
Party activities within the military—wasn't this the very characteristic that qualified them as revolutionary soldiers, distinct from ordinary soldiers?
They were, on the one hand, loyal revolutionary soldiers, and on the other, loyal party members, sending representatives to various levels of Party Congresses (黨部大會) and becoming representatives themselves, responsible for the Party's operation. I realized that calling them "Armed Comrades" (武裝同志) was a very meaningful designation.

After finishing the tour and heading out, I saw many officer candidates who had finished their classes out on the training ground. Some were chatting, some playing football, some on the horizontal bar, and some playing tennis, appearing no different from other university or middle school students. The only difference was that they wore yellow uniforms.
The dormitories had double-decker beds, with 8 to 16 people per room. Rifles were neatly cleaned and arranged at the head of the beds.
"Comrade Chiang (江同志)!"
It seemed one student was calling his friend. It appeared that even among soldiers, they addressed each other as "Comrade" (同志).

Saying thank you and leaving, I saw a tall American-educated student, who was a secretary at the Shanghai YMCA, entering, and the sentry (派守兵) saluted him. When I asked, I was told he was temporarily serving as an instructor for "Sociology" (社會學).
Pondering the "Relationship between Soldiers and Sociology" (軍人과 社會學의 關係) in my mind, I walked slowly beneath the wall of the Military Academy, on which political cartoons were drawn on one side. <9>

Labor-Farmer Russia's "Pioner"
Kim Se-yong

1
A troop of 30 to 50 boys, led by drummers and buglers, marches in neat single file columns, spaced one meter apart, down a wide paved road. They are all around 10 years old, with cheeks like apples (林樆) and arms and legs whose golden hues shine in the sun. They wear short black pants ('반쯔') above the knee, green shirts ('쌰쯔') with red neckties ('넥타이'), and bear the "Pioner" (비오낼) insignia on their chests—the red youth army of Labor-Farmer Russia (勞農露西亞). Filling their chests with the May air, the powerful sound of the bugles and the "Pioner Song" of the boys and girls, though simple in melody, is a triumphant anthem (凱歌) of truth, symbolizing the life of newly rising Russia while simultaneously heralding the dawn of a new morning for humanity. On festive days like May Day ('메-데이') or the October Revolution Anniversary, hundreds of tractors ('트락타-') overflow with laughing, chattering, singing Pioners waving red flags, proclaiming their happiness and sending encouragement (건투) and blessings to the proletarian youth of other countries. Hundreds of thousands of Pioners march in disciplined steps in their uniforms, singing, amidst the cheers (환호) of their fathers and mothers. Their laughter is the genuine laughter of humanity that has attained true freedom for the first time, and their lively actions and ideas signify the future of humanity to come. These "Pioners" can be seen in their beautiful, brave figures (勇姿) in any city or village of Soviet Russia. As a force carrying the future of Russia on their shoulders, they are the "strength" and organization that Russia boasts most among the various systems it prides itself on since the revolution. The organization and growth (生長) of the "Pioner" are viewed with jealousy (猜忌) and near-terror by the world's capitalist nations.

When the leaders of this country, standing atop the Lenin ('레-린') Mausoleum, sound the military call "Is the preparation sufficient?" towards the Pioner troops marching through the Red Square of the Kremlin ('크례무린') palace, they respond in unison with a sound that shakes the thick fortress walls, "Vsye gotovy" ('훗세, 가또후' - Preparation is complete). What is the preparation they claim to have completed?
It is not self-congratulation for their fortunate circumstances, but rather the preparation to faithfully adhere to Ilyich's (이-릿취 - Lenin's) teachings (견언), defend the Soviet Union (소뷔엣트同盟), and fight bravely for the world's proletarian youth.
Therefore, while other ruling nations level their customary accusations that they are indoctrinating youth with militarism and military training, these "Pioners" possess cultural demands exceeding those of the Boy Scouts ('보-이스카우트') in capitalist countries. Furthermore, they are fundamentally different (相違) from the Boy Scouts in that their fundamental spirit is egalitarian freedom and happiness, and their communist collective life and social consciousness are shown as a path towards forming the common happiness of humanity. Thus, criticisms of them being provocative, militaristic, etc., are merely jealousy (猜忌) transformed from a kind of admiration.
This "Pioner" movement aims to make the broad masses of proletarian youth understand their class position and struggle, educate them in the spirit of communism, cultivate creative, voluntary activity through diverse organizational forms and lively methods, mobilize (동원) them for class struggle, and organize the foundation for the future ideal society. Since the Russian Revolution, it has also been organized in other capitalist countries where class struggle is developing. Especially in China since the Wuhan Government (武漢政府), India, Germany (獨逸), Belgium (百耳義), and North America (北美), it has developed astonishingly. Recently in Japan, Labor-Farmer Youth Groups (勞農少年團) have been organized in Tokyo, Saitama Prefecture (埼玉縣), Akita Prefecture (秋田縣), and Yamanashi Prefecture (山梨縣), courageously struggling alongside adult workers and peasants using tactics like picketing ('비게틩'), distributing leaflets ('삐타 뿌리는 것'), demonstrations ('示威運動'), fundraising sales ('義捐行商'), and reporting ('례포-트'). This "Pioner" movement is a great force of young people making leaps forward under the control of the International Proletarian Youth League in Moscow (莫斯科) and the guidance of the Communist Youth Leagues in each country.
Hereinafter, I wish to briefly sketch (點描) the Russian "Pioner" and their life. <10>

2
"Pioner" (비오낼) is translated as Pioneer Youth Army or Labor-Farmer Youth Army (勞農少年軍). Its members are children of workers, peasants, and other laboring classes (動勞계급), aged from 8 to 17. There are three systems: organization within schools, organization outside schools by autonomous bodies, or direct state organization outside schools.

Children under 8 up to age 3 are called "Oktyabryata" ('옥자부리아-다' - Children of October) and form the red children's corps. Those from 8 to 17 belong to the "Pioner" as mentioned before. From 16 to 23, they are "Komsomol" ('꼼소몰' - Communist Youth members). There are age regulations, and while current statistics are unknown, in 1928:
Oktyabryata - 300,000
Pioner - Over 3,000,000
Komsomol - Over 1,400,000
They formed a pyramid-like (피라밋트) organization with the Communist Party at the apex.

The "Pioner" can be considered an intermediate vanguard (전도대) structure (체제), situated above the youth masses and XX masses at the lower levels, and below the Party, organized from elite elements of workers and peasants, and the Youth League, which encompasses more members than the Communist Party.
To select the best party members from the Youth League, effort must be invested in training the Pioner layer, which forms the foundation of the Youth League. It is natural to strive for scientific instruction in communist ideology and social functions for today's youth to create the members of the next generation's communist society.
The Pioner organization is extremely multifaceted. Factories, schools, clubs (구락부), and residential areas serve as organizational units, which are further organized into detachments (분대) called "Detachment Number X." To satisfy the specific missions and desires of all Pioners, horizontal reorganization occurs through "groups" ('그룹-뿌') and sections—for example, newspaper selling sections, youth sports, armed sports (Red Spear Corps - 赤槍團, Red Hawk Corps - 赤鷹團), travel clubs, Pioner orchestras (오-게스츄라團), Pioner choirs, etc.—under the unified leadership of the Communist Youth League and the Party. These special organizations <11> have specialists in various sports, music, etc., and many leaders are female Youth League members.
Each Pioner group has its own hall and club. Inside the hall, there is always a "Red Corner" ('렛트, 고-너-' - Krasny Ugolok) featuring portraits of Lenin ('레-닌') and other revolutionaries at the center, along with wall newspapers and posters ('포스타'). A committee is organized through voting, with departments for propaganda, reconnaissance, education, inspection, etc. Members have the right to recall committee members, making it a completely autonomous body. School Pioners are first organized around the sons and daughters of Communist Party and Youth League members, then admit children of the working class as much as possible. Children of NEPmen ('냅부만') are admitted only after strict examination or excluded altogether. Pioner committee members send delegates to the school committee to assert their rights regarding school self-governance. Currently, in any Russian elementary school, this Pioner organization is central to the students' self-governing body.
Furthermore, within the Communist Youth League, there is a Pioner bureau serving as the overall leadership body for all Pioners within its structure. This includes a special section for training Pioner leaders. The Bureau mobilizes adult workers, mothers, specialists in various fields, and members of revolutionary mass organizations for Pioner activities and work.
All Pioner groups maintain close daily contact, effectively implementing the "smychka" ('스미-츠카' - link/alliance) system. Each publishes materials—Pioner newspapers, magazines, etc.—and undertakes various artistic and recreational activities based on collective decisions of meetings. They expand their scope of activity among the populace through organizations like the Red Aid Society, International Workers' Aid, trade unions, mass sports groups, etc. Meanwhile, their organizational tasks include: broad propaganda about youth life and rights within the Soviet Union and socialist construction; solidarity between the youth of the Soviet Union and the proletarian youth of other countries; propaganda for joint struggle experience and constant preparation for the defense of the Soviet Union. One can imagine the important position these tasks occupy in the Soviet state and the great responsibility they hold for the future direction. Russian authorities concentrate maximum effort on the development of this Pioner movement as progressive builders of all rights and systems acquired through the revolution, further fostering the pride (矜誇) they hold as builders of the new society. They provide unsparing assistance for collective work and autonomous activities, and cultivate a lively interest in their surroundings and Soviet society.
From only 10,000 members at its founding in 1922, the organization grew to 300,000 in 1924, jumped to 2.5 million in 1926, and reached over 3.3 million in 1928. This year, it will surely surpass 4 million.
Moreover, to foster close international connections among Pioners, a conference of Pioner organizations from around the world is held annually in Moscow (莫斯科). Last year, the 4th Red Youth World Congress convened, attended not only by Pioner representatives from various nationalities within the Soviet Union but also delegates from Europe, America, China, and India. Various important resolutions were adopted, including:
One, mobilizing proletarian and working youth for economic and XX struggles.
For example, adopting common slogans ('슬로-간') for struggles like the anti-corporal punishment struggle, the struggle for warm meals (飯食) and clothing, etc.

3
The Pioner education policy aims to temper the strength to endure any hardship (勞苦), cultivate habits of collective social life and pure moral character (情操), emphasizing clean living, exercise, outdoor life, and social service. In these aspects, it resembles the Boy Scouts ('보-이스카우트') of ruling nations. However, its educational purpose lies in political and social education, with leaders fostering education and spirit, fundamentally aiming for the active mobilization of boys and girls in the formation of communist society.
For these sensitive and pure youths, a new world of ideas opens up even with a single XX song. Textbooks and fairy tales idealize the lives of working people. Coal mining, agriculture, and mechanical industries set against backgrounds of primeval forests, deep mountain valleys, vast plains, and factories become objects of a new "romance" ('로-만쓰') for them.
Conceiving the world as a single entity and thinking of all the world's workers as one body—this "solidarity" ('소리나리티-') is, above all, the foundation of their spirit.
The basic educational divisions are Cosmological Education, Biological Education, and Social Labor Education, broadly divided into three. Relationships between land and humans, animals/plants and humans, and human productive labor and social organization/function are theorized using the most advanced scientific methods, such as the experimental "natural method" ('네추랄, 메조-드'), without requiring textbooks or note-taking.
Various handicrafts (手功 工藝品) are also made through cooperative labor (協同的 勞作) as much as possible under the guidance of expert technicians, aiming to foster understanding of collective labor and spirit. <12>
In summer, detachments (分隊) of urban Pioners set up camp ('캼푸') and go to the countryside or mountains, establishing "smychka" ('스미-츠카') with the local Pioners there. Rural Pioners come to the city to gain new training and understanding before returning. Exploration teams and surveying teams go to designated areas for natural history exploration and geographical surveying. Propaganda teams visit Kolkhozes ('골호즈') and Sovkhozes ('솝호즈') to investigate their performance while simultaneously propagandizing Soviet policies and Pioner activities to the peasants.
Every city has a communist party hall, and Pioner-owned resort areas and sanatoriums welcome Pioners under any circumstances. Travel groups journey to the Caucasus ('까푸까-스') or Crimea ('크리미야'). Their activities are characterized by meticulous planning and agility, like the military. Various tasks, facility maintenance, meeting procedures, etc., are all handled autonomously. Reports on completed tasks are submitted to the committee for collective discussion.
Fundraising and propaganda for the International XX Relief Society (國際XX者 救濟會) and Workers' Aid Society are their special projects, achieving results exceeding those of the Communist Party. During the great British coal strike years ago, Pioners spearheaded efforts where schoolchildren took to the streets, collected large donations, and sent them to the striking workers. Currently, they are sending messages of encouragement in the name of the joint Pioner organizations to the bravely fighting Chinese Youth Army.
Particularly noteworthy is the Esperanto ('에스페란트') movement among Pioners and elementary school children. Schools teach Esperanto as a regular subject, and Pioners separately organize Esperanto study groups, contributing to international proletarian youth communication and understanding of "internationalism" ('인터내쇼나리즘') through it.
Furthermore, each Pioner group has leaders of the country like Lenin ('레-닌') and Stalin ('스타린') as honorary chairmen. Maxim Gorky ('막심, 골키-') served for many years as the honorary commander of the Ukrainian Pioners.

4
From the fragmentary introduction above, we can discern through their lives and discipline the communist spirit of the Pioners and their brilliant growth, and discover the prototype of humans in an ideal society. At the Pioner initiation ceremony, the new members (新粒子) take the following oath before everyone:
"I pledge upon joining, to be loyal to the working class, to work diligently myself, and I solemnly swear to obey the teachings of Ilyich ('이-릿취' - Lenin), the laws and customs of the Pioners, and to further strengthen our organization."
After this oath, the officiant says:
"Comrade, you are a Pioner of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics. Prepare for the struggle for the workers!" To which the new member replies fiercely, raising their left hand vertically above their head:
"'Vsye gotov'" ('으웃세, 가돕' - Everything is prepared).
The salute, raising the right hand with fingers pressed tightly together above the head, is used instead of a handshake ('握手代') as a daily greeting when Pioners meet. The five fingers symbolize the five continents (五大洲) where Pioners are fighting for freedom, and raising them above the head signifies that defending the oppressed peoples of the five continents is the highest duty of Soviet Russia.
Why was the handshake abolished? It is said that Lenin ('레-닌') considered handshaking a waste of time and an unhygienic practice that spreads disease.
The Laws of the Pioners serve as their code of conduct, strictly observed. While there might be specific clauses depending on urban (都會) or rural settings, the common mottos (표어) are as follows:
One, Be loyal to the cause of the working class and the commands of Ilyich ('이-릿취').
One, The path forward for the proletariat lies in steel-like discipline.
One, Pioners are the younger brothers and sisters of Komsomols ('꼼소몰') and Communists ('꼼무니스트').
One, The proletarian youth of the world share the same blood ('피') as us; let us not spare our lives for them.
One, Avoid smoking, drinking, and foul language (冒瀆語).
One, Be kind to useful animals; show no mercy towards locusts (蝗), hamsters (甘日鼠), marmots (몰못드), etc.
One, Study diligently. Those who do not love books are not Pioners.
One, Do not stoop when sitting, standing, or walking. Otherwise, you will look like a bent-over old person, and your heart will not function properly.
One, One person serves many, many serve one.
One, Pioners protect their own health and the health of others. Be honest with strong perseverance. Wake up early in the morning, wash yourself, and do exercises. <13>
One, Pioners value their own time and the time of others. Do your tasks quickly and accurately.
One, Pioners remember the effectiveness of collective labor.
One, Pioners are defenders of the Soviet Republic and standard-bearers of socialist culture. etc., etc.
Pioner life, of course, is not segregated by gender but separated by age and physical size. Superstition, fear, and religion are thoroughly eradicated from their consciousness.
If a Pioner misses a meeting and the reason given is fear of the dark night, they receive a stern rebuke from the leader:
"In foreign countries, Pioners are thrown into prison and abused, but our Pioners are not afraid of the dark." Furthermore, parents are not considered physical burdens (荊棘); rather, elders are understood to have bad habits and prejudices (偏見) because they grew up under the old drinking X property system (음주X産制度). Instead of criticizing them, it is considered the duty of young Leninists ('레니니스트') to interact with and guide them carefully and kindly.
Occasionally, Pioners hold political discussion meetings to debate communist theory and current Soviet issues. The content of the debates is theoretical, and the sharpness of argument (論峰) is enough to make scholars blush and leave their seats.
First, a judge and topic are chosen by member vote, and the debate begins in order. For example:
One, What is Communism ('꼼뮤니즘')?
Agreement is reached on the answer: A society without war, made up of free working people where everything is free.
One, Which classes do Pioners not support?
Those who exploit the labor of others—thieves, NEPmen ('냅푸만').
Those unable to answer the above questions are considered violators of the Pioner constitution, referred to a high court selected by the meeting, and receive appropriate punishment after the prosecutor's indictment and the judge's ruling.
Punishments—for example, those who secretly prompt or receive prompting during a debate receive the fearsome sentence of being a Pioner who has betrayed the Soviet Republic Federation and the world working class, or are punished by being made to stand by the window or suspended from attending meetings for two or more days.

5
From their organization in 1923, Russian Pioners served as beacons in all social spheres, monitoring and reporting domestic counter-revolution, fulfilling their duty as the "eyes" of policy. After the implementation of the New Economic Policy, when rural kulaks regained power, sought unjust profits, murdered rural correspondents, and opposed the establishment of poor peasant mutual aid associations, the Pioners most bravely and accurately investigated these matters, reported them to the center, helped the poor peasants, and worked towards rural collectivization. Since 1926, their struggle has primarily unfolded in the cultural sphere and the realm of socialist economic construction. The Pioner Palace in Moscow (莫斯科) records the results of these struggles through various statistics.
The approximately 4 million Pioners nationwide form the arteries of the newly rising Russia. They are the new cells of the Soviet people and, simultaneously, the true prototypes of free human beings, creating history.
In this sense, the Soviet authorities provide faithful guidance and absolute guarantees for the Pioner movement, sparing no sacrifice. As young communists and young citizens, their priority in life is fully secured. (End) <14>

German Revival and Youth Group Activities
From Berlin, by the Northern European Scholar (北歐學人)

I have now come to Berlin (伯林) via Paris (巴里). The German people, who were once subjected to economic and food blockades by the powers, and on top of being a defeated nation (全敗國), faced starvation (飢餓) so severe it was truly hard to witness (瀕하엿든), are now, upon observation, reviving (復興) at an astonishing speed. I will now record the state of revival (復活의 자태) here, based on what I have seen and heard while traveling around Berlin, its surrounding villages, and cities.

Firstly, what surprises me is the patriotism (愛國心) of the German youth. They are performing all sorts of social service (奉仕) with the sole intention of saving their dying fatherland. For example, the youth group members in the city of Dresden ('드레스든') work two hours longer than before. They wake up an hour earlier in the morning to work and work an hour longer in the evening, thus performing an extra two hours of voluntary labor daily. As a result of this movement, in which 74,000 male youth group members and 32,000 female members participated (totaling 106,000), conducted for about 4 years and 8 months, they were able to earn a huge sum (巨金) of 113,000 Marks. With this money, they founded a scientific research institute, named it the "German Male and Female Youth Group Scientific Research Institute" (獨逸 男女靑年團 科學硏究所), and are conducting research on science necessary for general household and social life. Besides this, women have organized and are active in groups like the "Electricity Saving Women's Youth Group" (電氣節約 女子靑年團) or the "Eight-Inch Society" ('八寸會'). The Electricity Saving Women's Youth Group involves members patrolling the city streets daily. If there are negligent residents who leave streetlights on from the night before during the daytime (白晝), they investigate and, if found, immediately turn them off (消燈). In addition, they wake up early in the morning and turn off unnecessary lights. The aim is to save even one 'volt' (볼드) of electricity and use that power for industrial production. And the aforementioned "Eight-Inch Society" ('八寸會') refers to an economic movement initiated by housewives who rose up (궐기하여) against the previously very long skirt length (치마기럭지) of German women, deeming it uneconomical, proposing to shorten skirts to eight inches. As a result, there were 1.8 million participants, and it is said that savings of about 100,000 Marks per year were possible. What astonishing unity and voluntary effort! Such examples exist in every city, and indeed, these admirable spirits reside in the hearts of German men and women, young and old.

That the entire nation is making all sorts of material efforts is something you, readers back home, can well imagine, so I will conclude this point here. More importantly, however, a profoundly significant social movement, which we must pay close attention to, is spreading like wildfire (燎原의 불길) in Germany (獨逸). It is the "Wandervogel" ('완다-, 포, 겔') movement. Simply put, it is a spiritual movement combined with physical exercise. Germans, whenever they have free time—or rather, they set aside specific time for recreation according to their fixed working hours—go to the mountains, to the water, to the fields. By basking in the sunlight and befriending the mountains and water, they cultivate the great 'magnanimous spirit' (浩然의 氣) of nature and foster a carefree, bold, and untethered disposition and character. Led by the local youth, men and women, young and old—sometimes entire families, sometimes whole villages—pack food and go into the high mountains for several days of camping (野營). The aim is to acquire an iron-like physique simultaneously with nurturing an iron-like spirit.

If things proceed this way, besides the fragrance of mountains and rivers, there will be many opportunities to visit famous sites and historical relics (名勝舊蹟), allowing people to cherish (懷할 수 잇고) the sentiments of ancestors who contributed to the nation and society. Furthermore, it creates many opportunities for individuals on the same path of movement to meet each other. <15> They constantly recite (日夜銘誦함니다) the ten great slogans:
"Bask in the sunlight! Nurture the magnanimous spirit! Befriend nature! Sing folk songs! Rediscover legends! Know the geography of the fatherland! Awaken the soul growing in the fatherland's soil! Cooperate! Unite! The nation is one German Nation. The people are one German People."
Thus, under slogans like these, the spirit of reviving Germany fills the mountains and rivers of Prussia (普露西山川). For the eternal existence of their people—no, even more so, to rise again from the tragic situation of a ruined nation that almost lost its ethnic identity due to the Allied attacks—they are grounding all movements in science, art, economy, and politics in this "Revival of the German Soul" (獨逸魂의 復興). As a result of the youth, both male and female, implementing these systematic and powerful movements at the forefront, social critics are already pointing out:
1. Showing the goal of building a unified Germany.
2. Dealing effectively with the ideological national crisis.
3. Striving for the restoration of social, moral, and orderly life.
4. Nurturing national unity.
5. Promoting national consciousness.
6. Emphasizing respect for duty and propagating each person's obligations.
7. Increasing social wealth through temperance and thrift.
8. Working diligently and making efforts.

Considering this, some might criticize (評去할) this movement—in other words, this movement to elevate national sentiment and ideas—as reactionary (反動的). However, such people do not understand Germany's current position. Economically and spiritually, they suffered truly fatal injuries from the Great War. Just as small nations need ethnic cooperation, they are undertaking a movement to raise the strength of their own people to a certain level; thus, I believe there is no basis for calling it reactionary.
In any case, the spirit (意氣) of the newly rising German state and the youth standing at the forefront of all its movements was magnificent. They will surely revive again. I believe there is much for us to observe, study, and learn from them. I will write more details later. <16>

**Summary**

This multi-part article from the November 1930 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* explores youth movements and national rebuilding efforts in three different global contexts: Nationalist China, Soviet Russia, and Weimar Germany. Chu Yo-han describes his visit to the KMT's Central Military Academy in Nanjing, emphasizing its role under Chiang Kai-shek in forging a politically indoctrinated, unified military force. Kim Se-yong details the Soviet Pioner youth organization, highlighting its structure, communist ideology, training methods (contrasted favorably with Boy Scouts), internationalist outlook, and importance as a pillar of the Soviet state, fostering future communists. The "Berlin Scholar" describes Germany's post-WWI revival efforts driven by youth patriotism, including voluntary labor, savings campaigns, and the influential *Wandervogel* movement promoting physical fitness, connection to nature, national spirit, and unity ("Revival of the German Soul").

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, these articles are particularly interesting. They reflect a Korean intellectual interest in models of national mobilization, military strength, and youth indoctrination from diverse political systems (Nationalist, Communist, post-WWI Nationalist/Revivalist) during a period of Japanese colonial rule. The focus on military academies (China), mass youth organizations (USSR), and national spiritual revival movements (Germany) suggests a search for potential strategies or sources of inspiration for Korean national strengthening or resistance, even if presented neutrally. The mention of a Korean student at the Chinese military academy hints at transnational connections relevant to the Korean independence movement.

在校生 중에 朝鮮出身 학생이 잇슴으로 우선 그를 차젓다. 문깐에서 명함을 주고 來意를 通하니까 서식을 주면서 주소 씨명, 면회자, 용무 등을 쓰라고 한다. 수십명 방문객이 다 그리하고 커다란 문방 겸 대합실에 안저 잇섯다. 면회시간이 오후 5시부터 5시 반까지(?)인데 아직 5시까지는 20여분이 나마 잇섯다. 나는 심심한 시간을 中國新聞과 壁上에 걸린 總理遺訓이며, 國耻地圖며,＜7＞ 國慶日 宣傳大綱이며 値星官(當直士官)順序表 등을 보고 잇섯다.

면회하러 온 사람들은 일일히 자기의 목적하는 사람을 차저 짧은 시간에 만흔 회포를 푸노라고 대소동이 일러낫다. 斷髮短裳의 미인쯤 하나 잇섯스면 매우 극적 씬이 되엿스련만 이날은 그런 씬이 부행이 업섯다.
내가 보러간 학생은 아모리 기다려도 나오지를 안햇다. 10분 20분, 25분, 면회시간이 거진 다 되엿는 데도 아모 소식이 업다. 아마 헷거름인가 하고 돌아올 준비를 하는데 대문 박게서 馬蹄聲이 요란하기에 내다 본 즉 一隊의 기병이 교외연습에서 돌아오는 광경이엿다.
키는 크지 안흐나 屈强하게 생긴 中國軍馬에 「카키」제복을 입은 精悍한 士官 一隊가 一絲整然하게 말발굽을 굴려 운동장 한 가운데로 들어간다. 석양에 비치인 그 행진을 홀로 서서 바라다 보는 기자의 마음은 어느듯 현실을 떠나서 몽상의 나라에 헤메엿다. 中原의 사막과 초원을 縱橫驅使하는 환상을 뇌에 그릴 때에 기자의 몸은 비록 南京에 잇스나 마음은 별별방면으로 달어낫다.
幾次例의 분례식을 행한 뒤에 散隊가 되고 馬丁들이 말을 운동장에서 여러 바퀴씩 돌리다가 馬廐로 들어간다. 정신 일코 서서 보든 기자는 비로소 현실로 돌아와 발길을 돌리려는데 知人인 朝鮮學生이 뒤쪼차 오면서 부른다. 그는 방금 기마대로 야외연습 나갓다가 들어와서 나의 명함이 방에 잇는 것을 보고 뛰여 왓다고 한다. 먼지무든 군복에 각반도 벗지 못한 그의 모양도 감회깁게 보이는 것이엿다.
그 날은 이미 느젓슴으로 翌日을 約하고 돌아왓다가 翌朝에 다시 가서 敎導團과 軍官學校를 値星官 洪某氏의 지도로 일일히 구경햇다. 朝鮮의 一 신문기자를 반갑게 丁嚀하게 안내해 주는 그들을 고맙다 아니할 수 업섯다.
中央軍官學校는 그때는 國民政府 軍事委員會 직속으로 현재는 아마 訓練統監府 직속이 된 듯하다. 蔣介石이 그때까지도 여전히 교장으로 잇섯다. 蔣介石이 비록 國民革命軍 總司令이니 國民政府 主席이니 하는 칭호를 가졋지마는 그가 연설할 때에 「本司令이」라든가 「本主席」이라는 말을 쓴 일이 업다고 한다. 대개 「卑人」이라든가 「我」라고 한다. 그러나 軍官學校에서 연설할 때는 반드시 「本敎長」이라 자칭하는 것이 그의 버릇이라고.
軍官學校는 신축중에 잇는 대강당을 제하고는 전부 예전 督軍時代에 잇든 건물로 입구에는 2층 鍊瓦屋이 가로막혓스니 사무실과, 교실, 강당 등으로 되고 그리로부터 운동장을 가운데로 두고 좌우로 기숙사가 잇스며 마즌 편에는 대강당이 新造中이엿다. 아마 지금은 벌서 낙성되여 偉觀을 정하고 잇슬 것이다.
강당 여페 식당이 잇는데 식당 정면에는 예에 의하야 靑天白日旗(卽軍旗)와 孫總理의 遺像 遺囑이 잇다. 그 미테 一席을 가르키면서 안내자가 말하기를
「이 자리는 蔣교장의 좌석입니다. 교장께서는 國務가 분망하시지마는 대개 조반만은 여기와서 학생들과 가튼 飯食을 가티 잡수시고 훈화를 하십니다. 또 일주일에 한 번식은 저녁에 오서서 학생과 飯食을 난호십니다.」
이 한 마듸 설명속에 蔣介石 직계군이 웨 강한 이유를 발견한 듯 햇다.
현재 양성되는 것은 제6기 及 제7기생인데 지금까지에 양성되여 나아간 사람이 8,000여명이라고 한다.
양성기간이 단 1년박게 안되는 것은 혁명군의 수요가 급함으로 부득기한 일이오 그러므로 敎導團이라는 것을 두어 실전에 경험잇는 사람을 선발하야 재훈련을 한다는 것이다.
본래 露國 교원이 만핫스나 지금은 업고 日本 사관도 초빙한 일이 업다 한다. 최근에는 獨逸 사관을 만히 고빙할 계획이라고 설명한다.
가장 기자의 눈에 띄운 것은 과목중에 「三民主義」「中國 外交史」＜8＞「黨義 釋解」 등의 國民黨 정치이론이 만히 포함된 것이오. 또 각 中隊마다 「國民黨首都特別市黨部軍隊特別支部第何區第何區分部」라는 문패가 따로히 부터 잇는 것과 딸아서 黨部運動이 사관생도 중에서 자연적으로 진행되는 사실이엿다.
군대 내의 黨部 운동―이것이야말로 그들을 보통 군인과 달리 혁명군으로서 자격주는 특색이 아닌가 생각햇다.
그들은 일방면 충실한 혁명 군인인 동시에 타방면에 잇서서는 충실한 당員으로 각급 黨部大會에 대표자를 보내며 대표자가 되여 黨의 운전을 책임지는 것이다. 그들을 「武裝同志」라 부르는 것이 매우 의의잇는 칭호인 줄을 깨달엇다.
구경을 다하고 나오다가 보니까 만흔 사관 후보생들이 학과를 마치고 운동장에 나왓는데 혹담화도 하고 혹 축구도 하고 혹 철봉도 하고 혹 정구도 하는 것이 다른 대학이나 중학 학생과 별다른 것이 업는 것 가탯다. 다른 것은 오즉 황색의 「유니폼」을 입은 것 뿐이엿다.
기숙사에는 2층식 침대로 되여 매 방에 8인 내지 16인이 잇고 총들은 말끔이 닥거서 침대머리에 정렬되여 잇다.
「江同志!」
하고 한 학생이 그의 친구를 불르는 모양이다. 군인간에도 서로 불을 때에 「同志」로서 찻는모양이엿다.
고맙다고 인사하고 나오는데 보니까 上海 靑年會 幹事로 잇는 키 큰 留美學生이 들어오는데 派守兵이 경례를 부친다. 물어보니까 그 사람은 임시로 「社會學」 담임선생으로 와 잇다고 한다.
나는 「軍人과 社會學의 關係」라는 문제를 심중에 풀어 보면서 정치만화를 1면에 그린 軍官學校 담장 미트로 터벅터벅 걸엇다.＜9＞

勞農露西亞의 「비오낼」
金世鎔

1
고수와 喇叭手를 선두로 한 一隊 30명-내지 50명의 소년이 一米突의 간격을 둔 一列縱隊로 정연히 열을 지여서 넓은 포도로 행진한다. 이들은 모다 10세 내외의 소년으로 林樆과 가튼볼과 황금의 손들이 태양에 빗나는 팔과 다리를 가젓스며 무릅 우에 짤막하게 입은 깜은 『반쯔』와 녹색 『쌰쯔』에 붉은 『넥타이』를 매고 가슴에 『비오낼』 휘장를 부친 勞農露西亞 적색 소년군이다. 한 가슴 가득이 5월 공기를 마시고 힘차게 부는 喇叭소래와 소년 소녀의 『비오낼놀애』는 음률이 단순하나 신흥 로서아의 생명을 상징하는 동시 인류의 새로운 아츰의 여명을 재촉하는 진리의 凱歌이다. 『메-데이』나 10월 혁명기념일가튼 축제일에는 數百臺의 『트락타-』에 웃고 재잭이고 노래하는 비오낼들이 늠치게 하고 적기를 흔들면서 자기의 행복을 부르짓고 다른 나라의 무산소년에게 건투와 축복을 보내며 幾十萬의 『비오낼』은 제복에 긴장한 보조로 노래 부르면서 아버지와 어머니의 환호 사이로 행군한다. 이들이 웃는 우슴은 처음으로 참다운 자유를 가진 인류의 거짓업는 우슴이며 그들의 潑剌한 동작과 관념은 장차 올 인류의 미래를 표징하는 것이다. 이 『비오낼』은 소뷔엣트, 로서아의 어떠한 都鄙에서도 그 아름다운 勇姿를 보게 되며 로서아의 장래를 두 억게에 지고 나아가는 한 세력으로 혁명 후, 로서아가 자랑할 여러 가지 제도 중에도 가장 자랑하는 『힘』과 조직이 이것이며 세계 자본주의 국가에게 猜忌와 공포에 갓가운 눈으로 보는 것이 『비오낼』의 조직과 生長이다.
『크례무린』 궁전의 붉은 광장을 행진하는 『비오낼』군을 향하야 『레-린』 묘탑 우에서 이 나라의 지도자들이 『준비는 어떠한가』의 軍號를 부르면 이들은 두터운 성벽을 진동하는 소래로 일제이 『훗세, 가또후』(준비는 다하엿다)라는 對號로 답한다. 이들이 다하엿다는 준비는 무엇일가.
행복한 경우에 잇는 자기을 視福함이 아니고 『이-릿취』의 견언에 충실하야 소뷔엣트同盟을 수호할 것과 세계의 무산소년을 위하야 용감이 싸우겟다는 준비를 말함이다.
그럼으로 다른 지배 국가에서는 소년에게 군국주의와 군사적 훈련을 한다고 예에 의한 비방을 하나 이 『비오낼』은 자본국가의 『보-이스카우트』보다 이상의 문화적 요구를 가지는 동시 평등적인 자유와 행복을 근본정신으로 하는 점에서 또 이들의 공산주의적 집단생활과 사회의식이 인류의 공동되는 행복을 형성하는 道程으로 보여줌에 잇서 보-이스카우트와 근본적으로 相違되며 도전적 군국적이니 하는 비평이 일종의 찬미에서 변태된 猜忌에 불과한 것이다.
광범한 無産 소년 대중으로 하여금 그들의 계급적 지위와 투쟁을 이해케 하고 공산주의 정신으로 교육하는 동시 다양적인 조직형태와 발랄한 방법으로 창조적 자발적 활동성을 함양하야 계급투쟁에 동원하고 장래할 이상사회의 기반을 조직한다는 이 『비오낼』은 로서아革命 이후 계급투쟁이 발전하고 잇는 다른 자본주의국가에서도 조직되여 잇다. 특히, 武漢政府 이래의 中國과 印度, 獨逸, 百耳義 北米 등에는 놀날만치 발달하여 잇고 최근 日本에서도 東京 埼玉縣 秋田縣, 밋 山梨縣 등에 勞農少年團이 조직되여 성인 노동자와 농민과 함께 『비게틩』과 『삐타 뿌리는 것』 『示威運動』 『義捐行商』 『례포-트』 등 전술로 용감히 투쟁하고 잇스니 이 『비오낼』운동은 莫斯科의 國際無産少年同盟의 통제와 각국 공산청년동맹의 지도아래 비약적 발달을 하여가는 위대한 젊은 청년의 세력이다.
이하 로서아 『비오낼』과 그의 생활을 간단하게 點描코저 한다.＜10＞

2
『비오낼』은 先驅少年軍 혹은 勞農少年軍이라 譯稱되여 8세로부터 17세까지의 노동자 농민 기타 動勞계급 자녀를 성원으로 하고 학교 내에 조직되는 것과 자치기관 혹은 직접 국가에서 학교 외에 조직하는 3계통이 잇다.

8세 이하 3세까지는 『옥자부리아-다』(10월의 아들)라고 하야 적색 유년군이 되여 잇고 8세이상 17세까지가 前記한 바와 가티 『비오낼』에 속하고 自 16세 至 23세는 『꼼소몰』(공산청년 회원)으로서 연령상 규정이 잇는데 현재의 통계는 알 수 업스나 1928년에
옥자부리아-나-30萬
비오낼- 300餘萬
꼼소몰 140餘萬
에 달하야 공산당을 정점으로 한 『피라밋트』적 조직이 되엿섯다.

『비오낼』은 노동자 농민 중 정예분자로 조직된 당과 공산당보다 수로서 일층 만은 동맹원을 포괄한 청년동맹을 우으로 하고 유년 대중 及 XX的 대중을 하부구층으로 한 중간적 전도대의 체제라 할 수 잇다.
最良한 당원을 청년동맹에서 선발할랴면 청년동맹의 소지가 되는 비오낼층의 훈련에 노력하여야 할 것이며 다음 세대의 공산사회의 구성원을 만들기 위하야 현대의 소년에게 공산주의적 관념과 사회적 기능의 과학적 교전에 힘쓰는 것은 당연할 일일 것이다.
비오낼의 조직은 극히 다면성을 가지여서 공장, 학교, 구락부, 거주 구성별을 조직 단위로 하고 다시 이것을 분대로 편성하야 제 멧 분대라고 부르며 비오낼 전원의 특수한 임무와 욕망을 만족시키기 위하야 『그룹-뿌』와 부문-예하면 신문 賣子부문 소년 스포-쯔, 무장 스포-쯔(赤槍團-赤鷹團) 여행 구락부 비오낼 오-게스츄라團, 비오낼 합창대등으로 횡단적 재조직을 한 후 공산청년동맹과 당이 통일적 지도를 하고 잇다. 그리고 이들 특수조직에는＜11＞ 각종 스포-츠와 음악 등의 전문가가 잇스며 지도자 중에는 여자청년동맹원도 만타.
각 비오낼은 자기의 회관과 구락부를 가지고 잇스며 회장 내에는 반드시 『렛트, 고-너-』 적색의 벽를 만들어 레-닌 기타 혁명가의 초상을 중심하야 벽신문과 포스타를 부치고 투표수에 의한 위원회를 조직하야 선전, 정찰, 교양, 검사 등 각 부를 두엇스며 위원에 대하야는 회원의 파면권이 잇서서 완전한 자치기관이 되여 잇다. 교내 비오낼은 먼저 공산당 及 청년동맹의 아들과 누이동생들을 중심으로 조직한 후 될 수 잇는대로 노동계급의 자녀만을 입회시키고 『냅부만』의 아들딸은 엄격한 시험으로 입회를 許하던지 그러치 아느면 제외하며 비오낼 위원은 학교 위원회에 대의원을 보내여 교내 자치에 관한 권이를 주장하니 현재 로서아의 어느 소학교든지 생도들의 자치기관으로 반드시 이 비오낼이 중심이 되여 잇다.
그리고 공산청년동맹 내에는 비오낼이 잇서 구성 내의 각 비오낼의 전체적 지도기관으로 되어 잇스며 이 중에는 비오낼 지도자 양성의 특수부문이 잇고 局에서는 각종 영성의 전문가 성인 노동자와 어머니 밋 혁명적 대중 조직의 성원을 비오낼 활동과 전업에 동원하고 잇다.
각 비오낼은 일상 긴밀한 연락을 하야 『스미-츠카』제도가 효과적으로 실행되며 각자 출판물-비오낼 신문, 잡지등을 발행하며 여러 가지 예술적 오락적 활동을 회의의 집단적 결의로 행하며 적색 구제회, 국제노동 구제회, 노동조합, 대중적 스포-츠단 등 민중 사이에 활동범위를 확대하는 한편 소뷔엣트 동맹내의 소년생활과 권리, 사회주의적 건설의 광범한 선전, 소뷔엣트同盟과 다른 諸國의 무산소년과의 단결, 소뷔엣트同盟 방어를 위한 공동투쟁의 경험과 부단한 준비를 위한 선전 등? 이를 조직의 임무이다. 이 임무는 소뷔엣트 국가에 얼마나 중요한 지위를 점령하고 압날의 방향을 위하야 위대한 책임을 가지고 잇는 것을 상상할 수 잇다. 로서아 당국자는 혁명으로서 획득한 모-든 권리와 제도의 진보적 건설자로서 이 비오낼의 발전에 최대한 힘을 집중하야 새로운 사회의 건설자로서 그들이 가지는 矜誇를 더욱 고취하고, 단체적 작업과 자치활동에 액기지 안는 원조를 하며 주위와 소비엣트 사회에 대하여서는 발랄한 흥미를 가지게 한다.
1922년 結團 당시 1만명에 불과하던 것이 1924년에는 30十萬, 1926년 일약 250만, 1928년에는 330여만으로 증가하니 금년에는 400만을 훨신 지날 것이다.
그리고 비오낼의 국제적 연결을 친밀이 하기 위하야 세계 각국의 비오낼 연합회의가 매년 莫斯科에서 개최되는 바 작연에는 제4회째 적색소년 세계대의가 열리어 소뷔엣트 내, 각 민족 비오낼 대표는 물론, 歐米, 中國, 印度의 대표자가 參集하야 여러 가지 중요결의를 하엿는데 그 중

예하면, 태형 及 반대투쟁, 따뜻한 飯食과 의복을 위한 투쟁 등의 공동 슬로-간을 채택하엿다한다.

로서아 비오낼은 1923년 조직 당시부터 모-든 사회적 방면의 등대로쎠 국내 반혁명을 감시보고하야 정책의 『눈』으로써 임무를 다하엿스니 신경제정책 실시 이후 농촌 부농이 세력을 만회하야 부정한 이익을 탐하고 농촌 통신비을 학살한 후 빈농의 공제조합설치을 반대할 때 가장 용감하게 정확하게 이것을 탐사하야 중앙으로 보고하고 빈농을 도아서 농촌협동화에 노력하엿스며 1926년 이후로는 그들 투쟁은 주로 문화적 방면과 사회주의적 경제건설 방면으로 전개하여 가고 잇다. 莫斯科 비오낼 궁전에는 이들 투쟁의 실적이 여러가지 통계로 기록되여 잇다.
전국 400여만의 비오낼은 신흥 로서아의 동맥이 되어 잇스며 소뷔엣트 국민의 새로운 세포인 동시 자유한 인간의 참다운 원형으로써 역사를 창조하고 잇다.
이런 의미에서 소뷔엣트 당국자의 비오낼 운동에 대한 충실한 지도와 절대한 보장는 모-든 희생을 앗기지 아느며 젊은 공산주의와 어린 국민으로써 생활상 우선권 완전이 보장되여 잇다.(完)＜14＞

獨逸復興과 靑年團活動
伯林에서 北歐學人
나는 지금 巴里을 거처 伯林으로 왓슴니다. 한때는 열국의 경제적 봉쇄와 식량봉쇄를 당하야 전패국인 그 우에다가 실로 눈을 뜨고 바라볼 수 업는 기아에 頻하엿든 獨逸 국민이 오늘에 이르러보면 놀라운 속도로 부흥하여가고 잇는 중에 잇소이다. 나는 지금 伯林을 중심삼고 그 부근 농촌과 도회 등지를 도라다니면서 보고 드른 이곳 부활의 자태를 기록하기로 하겟나이다.
첫재 나를 놀래이게 하는 것은 獨逸 청년들의 애국심이외다. 그네들은 瀕死의 조국을 球하랴는 일념이 온갓 사회적 봉사를 다하고 잇더이다. 예를 들면 『드레스든』市의 청년단원은 종래보다 노동을 두 시간씩 더함니다. 아츰에 한점동안을 몬저 이러나 일하고 저녁에 또 한시간을 더 일하여 매일 2시간식 봉사적 노동을 더하는 운동을 이르켯는 바 그 운동에 靑年團員 7만 4천명 女子團員 3만 2천명과 합계 10만 6천명이 참가하야 약 4년 8개월간 실시한 결과 실로 11만 3千馬克의 거금을 버을게 되엿다 함니다. 이 돈을 가지고 그네는 과학연구소를 창립하여 노코 『獨逸 男女靑年團 科學硏究所』라 명명한 뒤 일반 가정과 사회생활에 필요한 과학연구를 하고 잇더이다. 이 박게 여자들이면 또 『電氣節約 女子靑年團』이나 『八寸會』가튼 것을 조직하여 활동하고 잇더이다. 電氣節約女子靑年團이라 하면 도시의 거리거리에 혹 等閑한 주민이 잇서서
白晝에 夜來의 街燈을 그냥 켜둔 집이 잇슴니다. 女子 單元들은 매일 시가로 도라다니며 이것을 조사하여 만일 전등켠 곳이 잇스면 곳 소등하며 이 박게도 새벽 일즉히 이러나서 無用한 전등은 꺼버림니다. 비록 一『볼드』의 전력이라도 경제하여 그 전력을 공업생산력 방면에 쓰자함이외다. 그러고 앗가 말한 『八寸會』라 함은 이前에 獨逸 女人의 치마기럭지가 대단히 길엇는바 이리해서는 不經濟라 하여 가정부인들이 궐기하여 치마기럭지를 八寸까지 하자는 경제운동을 이루켯슴니다. 그 결과 180만명의 참가자가 잇섯고 1년에 약 10만원의 절약이 가능하엿다하니 얼마나 놀라운 단결과 봉사적 노력임니까. 이러한 예는 각 도시에마다 잇고 獨逸인의 남녀노소의 가슴마다 잇는 실로 부러운 기개들이엿소이다.
이이러케 물질적으로 전국민적으로 온갓 노력을 하고 잇는 것은 本國게신 여러분께서도 능히 상상할 일임으로 이헤 끈치거니와 이보다도 獨逸에는 우리들로 매우 주목하지 안으면 안될 심히 중요한 사회적 운동이 요원의 불길과 가치 펴지고 잇슴니다. 그것은 『완다-, 포, 겔』운동이란 것이외다. 이것은 쉽게 말하면 체육운동을 겸한 정신운동이니 獨逸 사람들은 틈만 잇스면 아니 노동시간이 일정한 모양으로 휴양할 시간을 일정하게 지어 가지고 산으로 감이외다, 물로 감이외다, 들로 감이외다. 그래서 日光을 쪼이고 산과 물을 친함으로부터 대자연의 浩然의 氣를 크게 길느고 悠悠 豪放 不縣하는 기분과 성격을 길느는 것이외다. 그네들은 그 地方靑年을 先頭로 男女老少 엇든 때는 一家庭이 엇든 때는 한 洞里 사람이 모다 식량을 싸가지고 놉흔 산으로 드러가서 數日의 야영을 하고 옴니다. 철과 가튼 정신을 養함과 동시에 철과 가튼 육체를 엇자함이외다.
이러케 된다면 山川의 향기 이외에 또한 名勝舊蹟을 차즐 기회가 만허서 국가사회에 공적잇든 古人의 정을 懷할 수 잇고 彼此 동일 운동선에선 甲乙이 相會함 기회를 만히 지을 것이로소이다. 그네는＜15＞ 「햇빗을 쪼여라! 浩然의 氣를 養해라! 자연에 친해라. 민요를 노래불너라! 전설을 도로 차저라! 조국의 지리를 알어라. 조국의 흙속에서 자라나는 혼을 깨달어라! 협력하여라. 단결하여라! 국가는 한낫 獨逸國. 민족은 한낫 獨逸民族.」

1, 統一獨逸의 건설의 목표를 보인 것

6, 직무를 존중히 하고 각 人에 의무를 보급식힌 것
7, 절제근검으로 사회적 부를 증진한 것
8, 근면 노력한 것
를 지적하고 잇슴니다.
