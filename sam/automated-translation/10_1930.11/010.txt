Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

鐵과 血
						金東煥

We must vow, when drawing up the "program" (푸로그람) for our respective lives, to allocate time multiplied by that spent on love, food, and study to "a certain life of sacrifice unseen by sunlight" (日光 못보는 그엇든 희생생활).

We must realize that our palms, still possessing the "beauty of youth," will one day become calloused (산적) from gripping fire-heated iron pillars, and that our sleek (윤택잇는) legs will also grow weary from being bound (매우기) in chains (사슬), and we must study in advance how to endure that time.

We must prepare to cross the bridge laid (노힌) before (압헤) us, by all means (긔어코), even while knowing it may be rotten. If it collapses midway, shouldn't we just fall (떠러저) into the river and swim (헤염처) out? And let us promise not to cheer until we have completely crossed that bridge.

Iron and Blood (鐵과 血) is the code (부호) that promises us glory.<1>

**Summary**

The article exhorts readers to dedicate themselves to a life of extreme sacrifice and endurance, foregoing personal comforts and pleasures for a greater, albeit hidden ("unseen by sunlight"), cause. Using metaphors of handling hot iron, being chained, and crossing a rotten bridge, the author urges preemptive mental preparation for hardship and perseverance without premature celebration. The final phrase, "Iron and Blood," directly signals that glory can only be achieved through struggle and suffering. For historians, this piece offers insight into the intense, almost militaristic, mindset fostered among some Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. The call for hidden sacrifice and the stark "Iron and Blood" metaphor could reflect nationalist sentiments advocating for determined resistance or preparation for future conflict, expressed necessarily through veiled language under censorship.

鐵과 血
						金東煥
우리는 제각기 자기 일생의 「푸로그람」을 짤 때에 연애와 식사와 수학 등에 쓰는 시간에 배되는 세월을 「日光 못보는 그엇든 희생생활」에 집어너키를 맹세하여야 한다.

우리는 아직 「靑春의 美」를 가진 우리의 손바닥이 어느 날은 불에 익은 쇠기둥 쥐기에 산적이 될 것과, 또 윤택잇는 다리도 사슬 매우기에 지치울 것을 깨닷고 미리 그 때를 참는 공부를 하여야 한다.

우리는 우리 압헤 노힌 교량이 비록 썩은 것인 줄 알면서라도 긔어코 건널 차림을 해야 한다. 중도에 문허지면 강에 떠러저 헤염처 나갈 뿐이 아니냐. 그러고 그 다리를 다 건널 때까지 결코 환성을 치지 말기로 약속하자.

鐵과 血은 우리에게 영광을 약속하는 부호다.＜1＞
