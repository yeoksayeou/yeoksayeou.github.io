浦口의 夜半 (Midnight at the Port)
						岸曙
連자즌 어야데얀 닷 감는 소리
잔노코 아리랑엔 밤도 깁헛네.
아리랑 열두고갠 興이 넘어도
실은 離別 이 고개 난 못넘겟네.

바람가치 뜬몸에 오가는 봄을
술잔 잡고 노래로 울어보냇네.
물결딸아 千里길 드나는 그대
가량이면 이대로 닛고나 주게.

오락가락 無心타 故鄕의 꿈아
떠나 十年 이날에 내가 알 것가.
떠나 十年 이날에 내가 알 것가.
쓸데업시 지친 몸 졸으지 말고
난 바다의 님의 잠 깨와나 보리.<29>

**Translation**

Midnight at the Port (浦口의 夜半)
						An Sŏ (岸曙)

The frequent sounds of "ŏyadĕya" as anchors are weighed,
Amidst the lingering Arirang, the night has deepened.
Though Arirang’s twelve passes overflow with excitement (興),
Truly, this pass of separation (離別), I cannot cross.

Like the wind, a floating body, I’ve wept away passing springs,
Holding a wine cup, singing songs.
You, who come and go a thousand leagues (千里) following the waves,
If you must leave, then just forget me like this.

Coming and going, indifferent, oh dream of my hometown (故鄕),
Ten years (十年) after leaving, what would I know today?
Don't let this uselessly weary body doze off,
I shall try waking my beloved who sleeps on the sea.<29>

**Summary**

The poem "Midnight at the Port" by An Sŏ depicts the melancholic atmosphere of a port late at night. The speaker hears sailors' chants and the song Arirang, contrasting the song's lively tune with their own sorrow over an impending or past separation. They reflect on a transient life, comparing themselves to the wind and lamenting years away from home, while expressing a longing for a loved one associated with the sea. From a historical perspective, the poem subtly evokes themes common in colonial Korea: displacement, wandering, separation, and a sense of loss or rootlessness felt by individuals, perhaps reflecting the broader social conditions and anxieties of the era under Japanese rule. The imagery of the port and the sea could symbolize both escape and uncertainty.
