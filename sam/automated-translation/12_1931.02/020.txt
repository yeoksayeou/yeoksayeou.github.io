Publication Date: 1931년 02월 01일
Article Title: 『엇는 것』과 『주는 것』 (Getting' and 'Giving')
Author: 金東煥
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellany)

'Getting' and 'Giving'
						Kim Tong-hwan

It seems the world's heart (心臟) is truly rotting away. Just as a person collapses when their lungs rot, today's world seems to be naturally facing a crisis due to the increase of its own internal contradictions (矛盾). This can be understood even by looking at Chiang Kai-shek's speech, which exposed Britain's (英國) greatest anxieties, and the views (所論) of renowned British, German, Italian, and French (英獨伊佛) politicians who spoke of the collapse of all of Europe (全歐州). In any case, it appears true that the capitalist powers (資本主義 列國) have certainly lost their health and fallen into crisis (危機). In a society where only "taking" and "being taken from" are in opposition (對立), it should hardly be considered strange (怪異치 안타) that crises, wars, and starvation (飢餓) will only repeat intermittently (斷續的).

Now, spring has come. The spring where all powers grow, transform (轉身), and leap (飛躍) has come. The spring where pressure (壓力) grows larger and repulsive force (反撥力) also strengthens has come. As soon as the grass shoots sprout from the ground after Ipch'un (立春 - Start of Spring), a distant London (倫敦) telegram reported the threatening desperate shouts (威嚇的 絶呌) even from the seemingly castrated (去勢한 격이든) Indian (印度) delegates and predicted many troubles ahead (多事할 것). The murderous (살인적) recession (불경기) in New York's (紐育) financial district (金融街) globally reports bank collapses (銀行倒潰) and a sharp increase (激增) in the unemployed (失業者). In Manchuria and Mongolia (滿蒙), the rivalry (角逐) among numerous nations is said to be intensifying daily (日甚하다) due to the influx of capital forces between hypothetical enemy countries (想定敵國間). When considering that, conversely, the increase in opposing forces (反撥적 세력) will also be intense (激甚), only the confrontational existence (對峙的 존재) of force against force will startle our retinas (綱膜) [eyes] from now on (압흐로). At this time, wouldn't the intention (用意) of the oppressed one (눌니우는 者) be in saying, "Everything is not given, but gotten!"?
—(Kim Tong-hwan)— <1>

**Summary**

Kim Tong-hwan reflects on the global crises of 1931, characterizing the capitalist world as decaying from internal contradictions, exemplified by political anxieties in Europe, economic depression originating in New York, and imperialist rivalries in Manchuria/Mongolia. He contrasts this decay with the vital energy of spring, suggesting that this season also intensifies opposing forces – the pressure of oppression and the growing resistance against it. The article concludes that the oppressed understand that things are not passively received ("given") but must be actively seized ("gotten"). From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its explicit critique of global capitalism and imperialism ("taking" vs. "being taken from") and its framing of international tensions (Britain/India, Manchuria/Mongolia) through this lens. It subtly aligns the situation of the oppressed globally with Korea's own situation under Japanese rule, using the metaphor of growing "repulsive force" in spring to hint at rising resistance, framed in the universal language of class or national struggle ("Everything is not given, but gotten!").

『엇는 것』과 『주는 것』
						金東煥
세계의 心臟은 확실히 썩어 가는가 보다. 사람도 肺가 썩으면 꺽구러 지드시 오늘의 세계는 자체내의 矛盾의 增大 때문에 저절로 危機에 직면하고 잇는 것갓다. 이 말은 英國의 最大不安을 說破하든 蔣介石의 연설과 全歐州 破綻를 말하든 英獨伊佛 著名 政治家의 所論으로 보아도 알 일이다. 엇잿든 資本主義 列國은 확실히 건강을 일코 危機에 빠진 것만은 사실인 듯, 「빼앗느냐」와 「빼앗기느냐」가 오직 對立되고 잇는 사회에서는 언졔든지 이와가치「危機」와 「戰爭」과「飢餓」가 斷續的으로 거듭할 뿐임이 오히려 怪異치 안타 하리라.

이제 봄이 왓다. 모든 힘이 成長, 轉身, 飛躍하는 봄이 왓다. 壓力도 커지고 反撥力도 켜지는 봄이 왓다. 立春 지난 땅 속에서 잔디 움이 솟기 밧부게 멀니 倫敦電報는 去勢한 격이든 印度대표 조차 威嚇的 絶呌와 今後외 多事할 것을 전하엿고 紐育金融街의 살인적 불경기는 세계적으로 銀行倒潰, 失業者 激增을 전하며 滿蒙에는 想定敵國間의 資本勢力流入 때문에 다수 국가의 角逐이 日甚하다 한다. 그 반면에 反撥적 세력의 증대도 激甚할 것을 생각할 때에 오직 力과 力의 對峙的 존재만히 압흐로 우리 綱膜을 놀내게 할 뿐이리라. 이 때에 잇서 눌니우는 者의 用意는「모든 것은 주는 것이 아니라 엇는 것이다!」함에 잇지 아닐가.
―(金東煥)― <1>
