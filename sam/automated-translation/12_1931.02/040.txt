Is the Korean Movement Cooperation or Confrontation? A Critique of the Shinganhoe "Dissolution Movement"
From the Magazine...... The Shinganhoe (新幹會), since its founding five years ago, has become Korea's largest association, boasting 150 branches and 40,000 members. However, since last winter, there have been new views regarding the "Shingan Movement," which have finally led to the proposal of the "Dissolution Theory," a fact widely reported by recent newspapers. At this time, how should we receive and critique this movement? As the outcome of this issue will greatly influence the future development of our movement, we decided to hear the calm and fair opinions of various figures in society, including the current authorities of the Shinganhoe. For further reference, the following individuals requested for opinions by our magazine had not replied by the editing date:
Sin Sŏgu, Yun Ch'iho, Yang Ponggŭn, Chŏng Unyŏng, Chu Yohan, Yi Chongnin, Yu Chint'ae, Chŏng Chongmyŏng, Han'guk Chong, Yi Chongho, Hong Sŏngha, Ch'oe Rin, Yi Tonhwa, Kim Kijŏn, Pak Wan, Paek Kwansu, Yi Kwanggu, Kang Sanghŭi. Hong Kimun, Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng, Yi Chŏkhyo, Kim Hyŏk, Han Pyŏngnak, Song Chinu, Yi Yŏsŏng, Sin Hŭng'u, Yi Sŏn'gŭn, Yu Ŏkkyŏm, O Kŭngsŏn, Pak Hŭido, Hong Chŭngsik, Kil Sŏnju, Yi Hangbal, Iwŏn, Wŏnsan, Pusan, various Shinganhoe branches.

2.  Critique of the past Shingan movement and opinions regarding the future<6>

Let's Avoid the Scattering of Concentrated Strength

Kim Pyŏngno

It is true that the Shinganhoe dissolution theory has been proposed by one or two branches. If viewed solely from the perspective and theory of class struggle, it might not be without fundamental significance. However, the Shinganhoe, by its original nature and organization, was formed as a cooperative line (協同X線) encompassing various strata and factions, not possessing a unique, creative guiding program (指圖綱領). Therefore, the Shingan movement, based on the cooperative significance of the organization itself, can only have a maximum [struggle] role possible up to a certain stage, and cannot become the exclusive camp of any single class, nor should it obstruct class-based movements.

If the Shingan movement cannot directly become a class struggle, asserting that cooperation lacks meaning, or harboring anxieties (杞憂) that the stronger the Shingan movement becomes, the more class consciousness will be erased, is nothing but a misunderstanding (誤謬) of the Shingan movement's stage strategy (階段X略) and cooperative spirit. In the current situation where the entire movement is suffocated (窒息), questioning the significance and existence of the Shingan movement by leaning excessively (偏依) on the theory of class struggle is merely an act of destroying (毁棄) an established camp and scattering even the concentrated strength. If theory becomes divided without realizing this, I fear it will eventually transform into divisive action (分化作用), reducing the strength of our movement. If we have established strength and it is being misused or misguided (誤用妄導), the key is to guide and utilize (善導運用) it well, not to scatter it. I believe we must clearly recognize that the basis of our cooperative significance lies not in focusing (置中) on a singular consciousness or ultimate theory, but in representing the immediate rights and interests (當面 權益) of the nation, cooperatively unfolding (協力 展開) the maximum possible [struggle] to aim for the achievement of a certain stage. (Note: The author is currently the Chairman of the Shinganhoe Central Committee)

Giving Advantage to the Fisherman is Absolutely Forbidden

Cho Mansik

One. The Shinganhoe dissolution movement, considering the current situation in Korea, should absolutely be rejected.
Reason: Those advocating dissolution seem to view the current Shinganhoe merely through a kind of class consciousness, using arguments like "it's a petit-bourgeois (小뿔조아) collective," "it lacks active struggle," or "it suppresses the fighting spirit of the worker-peasant masses," without looking at our situation.
However, I believe it is necessary to consider the following points:
1.  The Shinganhoe should not be approached with class-based or factional consciousness.
2.  We must mutually understand (互相諒知) that, considering the situation in Korea, there should be discretion (酌量) regarding the pace of struggle, and refrain from reckless destruction (無謀한 破壞) and rupture (決裂).
3.  When a party itself or a fraternal organization makes mistakes, stagnates (沈滯), or commits other improprieties, we should perform surgery or apply the whip (鞭韃), but causing friction (軋轢) among ourselves to give advantage to the fisherman (漁人에게 利를 주게 함) is absolutely forbidden (大禁物).
Two. My view on the past "Shingan movement" is as follows. It is true that initial public expectations for the Shinganhoe were very high. That's why, despite some struggle activities during this period, it has come to be seen as lacking active struggle. However, one could say that the past few years were inevitably a period of propaganda, guidance, or training. Looking at the history of any movement, East or West, ancient or modern, achieving significant results in a short period is extremely rare. If so, it's acceptable to view the Shinganhoe as still being on that kind of path (途程).<7> Therefore, let us further concentrate our strength through even firmer (鞏固) unity, like a certain bird that, after three years of not flying or singing (不飛不鳴), then flies to the heavens (衝天飛) and sings to startle people (驚人鳴).
(Note: The author is currently the Chairman of the P'yŏngyang Shinganhoe Branch)

Absolutely Oppose Dissolution - Considering the Korean Situation -

An Chaehong

One. I believe that the dissolution movement proposed by some should be rejected by me, and also by the general public.
First, the Shinganhoe aims to become the left-wing camp representing orthodox nationalist thought (民族主義的 正統思想). Only when it gradually yet rapidly proceeds through its formation process, reaching a stage where its rise (昻揚) and consolidation grow together, larger and higher, along with the changing (轉換) times, can it fulfill its historical "role." Whether the circumstances of the time then cause it to sharply change direction, or whether it undergoes a so-called developmental dissolution due to the formation of a second new entity, is a matter for that time. However, to already talk about dissolution during its difficult formation process is truly nothing more than defeatism (敗北主義).
Second, they say they intend to expand and strengthen labor unions (勞働組合) and peasant unions (農民組合) as the main bases of struggle for workers and peasants, withdrawing and concentrating them there to achieve [struggle-ization?] (XX化) through their class-based formation. However, if the situation is such that the Shinganhoe itself cannot expect the "growth of the masses and development of [struggle?]," then the labor and peasant unions will also surely find it difficult to achieve [struggle-ization?] and expansion/strengthening. Of course, since they are separate sectoral unions, they might be somewhat different from the Shinganhoe's tendency towards unwieldy size (尨大), but one cannot have entirely different, special expectations for the expansion and strengthening of labor and peasant unions as [struggle] alliances (XX同盟) compared to the Shinganhoe movement. Therefore, I cannot approve of dissolving the Shinganhoe for the sake of returning to the unions.
Third, regarding the growth of nationalism, I cannot agree with the dissolution argument that anticipates "many sacrifices" and prematurely worries about the "final double cross (最後의 二重X)," citing the example of the Chinese Kuomintang (國民黨). Since the late Qing (前淸), over 40 years, the Xingzhonghui (興中會) went through the Tongmenghui (同盟會) era, initially growing from feudalistic exclusionary xenophobia like "Overthrow the Manchus, Revive the Han" (滅滿興漢), colored by the conquering consciousness of "Five Races Under One Union" (五族共和), and developing on the foundation of a nationally-based capitalist clique (民族的 資本閥) that could emerge under a certain independent sovereignty, capable of organizing and controlling a newly sharp self-defense army. The heroes of China's party-rule-ism (黨治主義) are entirely different in circumstance from the so-called petit-bourgeois (小뿔) nationalist group in Korea, which is completely dissimilar. Thus, a dissolution theory based on such "premature worry" is too hasty (早計).
Fourth, the left-wing petit-bourgeois layer (小뿔르層) in Korea and other related intellectuals and petit-citizens should abandon their perpetually weak and irresolute (柔弱 無定見) attitude and, with a self-determining (自決的) stance, firmly hold onto (固守) this nationalist body themselves, proceeding along the cooperative path in the most planned (企劃的) manner.
Two. I will refrain from lengthy critique or thoughts on the past and future.
First, regarding its past formation, it was entirely a social gathering-like formation (社交的 結成), which was an unavoidable circumstance and perhaps a major weakness (缺陷) for the Shinganhoe. However, I see it as something unavoidable in the initial stages under circumstances like those in Korea. At that time, we intended, by whatever means possible—even if not through strong [political?] methods—to awaken consciousness through cultural or social mobilization (문화적 혹은 社交的 動員) of members and provide lessons gained through common action (共同動作), thus proceeding with extremely gradual (緩漫) but definite training. We hoped that this would provide valuable experiences, large or small, gradually leading to upliftment, expansion, and eventually a day of leaping forward (飛躍). Proposing the item of plain clothes and short hair (色衣斷髮) as the last among the 5 or 6 minimum action program items, as a minimum starting point for member action and mass contact, was part of that. (This condition has changed now, though). However,<8> looking back now, the past stagnation (沈滯) was not so much because the Shinganhoe initiated any imposing actions, but rather, the meddling attitude of its constituent elements (構成分子) prematurely created grounds for major obstacles (障害), which was one misstep. And more than that, the force of reaction (反動) was simply too strong.
Second, regarding future development, I do not suddenly expect great things. Setting aside emergency advances corresponding to immediate situations (當面當面의 情勢對應的의 非常進出), I still think it's good to continue going through a certain training process, even if generally through methods of cultural and social mobilization. The reason is that "'Existence' can grow and hope for a future leap, but 'Non-existence' ultimately remains non-existence, and 'Dispersion' can never magically bring about concentrated control." We should think about the "program" (푸로그람) considering a certain time—period.
Third, if the Shinganhoe itself maintains a good spirit and moves forward, I see no harm (無妨) in engaging in momentary strategic cooperation (政略的 협동) with external organizations (外他의 단체).
(Note: The author is currently the Vice President of the Chosŏn Ilbo)

It Absolutely Must Be Dissolved =A Dialogue Between A & B on a Certain Day=

An Pyŏngju

A: Hey! The Shinganhoe dissolution theory is now arising nationwide. Let's discuss this too!
B: The Shinganhoe dissolution theory is just a smuggled import (密輸入) of the Japanese Rōnōtō (勞農黨) dissolution theory, so what's the point of chirping about applying the Rōnōtō dissolution theory to the Shinganhoe?
A: Man! Why must the Shinganhoe dissolution theory be a smuggled import of the Rōnōtō dissolution theory? That's the same blind, stubborn thinking (頑固思想) of nationalist conceptualists (民族觀念論者) as the Chosŏn Ilbo editorial that views the Shinganhoe dissolution theory as international extensionism (國際延長主義). It's true that the Rōnōtō dissolution theory provided some reference material for the Shinganhoe dissolution theory, but how could it be a blind, literal translation? Haven't we been advocating the Shinganhoe uselessness theory (無用論) since last spring?
B: Then what's the difference between the Rōnōtō dissolution theory and the Shinganhoe dissolution theory?
A: The Rōnōtō dissolution theory is the ultimate self-abnegation (終局的 自己揚棄) in the development of a legal political party, but the Shinganhoe dissolution theory is not the ultimate abnegation of the cooperative line (協同X線); rather, because the body itself has organizational flaws, it's about dissolving this non-organizational body (非組織體) as a matter of course.
B: What? Is the Shinganhoe a non-organizational organization? Tell me the reason.
A: What kind of group is the Shinganhoe, anyway? It's nothing more than the [party] organization (X黨조직) of the petit bourgeoisie (小뿔조아지-) regarding Korea. First, it's a centrally-controlled [party] organization (中央集權的 X黨조직); second, it's merely the political representative (政治的 代辯者) of the Korean petit bourgeoisie. Consequently, it's currently the opposition party (反對黨派) of the [Communist Party?] (공XX) against the national [bourgeoisie?] class (國民XXXX계급). Therefore, the task of the [Communist Party?] regarding this group is to thoroughly isolate this opposition party from the entire allied masses (全同盟的 大衆) (peasants and petit-citizen masses).
B: No. That theory is truly a dissolutionist (解黨派) theory. The Shinganhoe at least arose from mass demand, didn't it? And isn't it growing based on mass demand? The current stage regarding Korea is (Omission - original text) nationalists and [socialists?] (XX主義者) are entirely in agreement on aiming for national [liberation] (民族XX) as the immediate goal. Therefore, we should completely wash away the meaningless confrontations of the past and achieve grand unity (大同團結) between national [liberationists?] (民族XX者) and [socialist] theorists (XX주의者). Isn't the Shinganhoe the necessary intermediary form (媒介形態) for this national unified cooperative [front/line] (民族單一協同X선)?
A: Haha! You call my theory dissolutionist! Your theory is the theory of a naive dissolutionist (解黨主義者). Why? Because the theory of a "national cooperative [front/line]" (民族協同X선) lacking any class-based guiding spirit (階級的 指導精神) ultimately cannot escape the error of conceptualist dissolutionism (觀念論的 解黨主義). Of course, the Shinganhoe grew from mass demand, but you must also know that it will disappear (消滅) due to mass demand. The distinctive basis of the Shinganhoe dissolution theory, in summary, is the growth of the masses (대중의 成長). Isn't the cheer of the worker-peasant masses trying to break the ropes (捕繩) of the pseudo (似而非的) cooperative [front/line] party (協同X선黨) and escape for a step forward <9> ultimately nothing but the Shinganhoe dissolution theory?
B: So, is it wrong to view the Shinganhoe as a "national cooperative [front/line]"?
A: That's right. The Shinganhoe is not a "national cooperative [front/line]" but merely a kind of pseudo "national-conceptual mixed-rice party" (民族觀念的 雜炊黨).
B: I don't understand what you're saying. Then critique the organizational flaws of the "national cooperative [front/line]" more concretely.
A: In the first past stage, we called the Shinganhoe the intermediary form of a "national single [party]" (民族單一X黨), but it was the most naive dissolutionism (幼稚한 解黨主義), nothing more, for the entire nation (全民族) (Three lines omitted - original text). In the next stage, we called the Shinganhoe the intermediary organizational body of a "national cooperative [front/line] party" (民族協同X선당), but it was undoubtedly a dissolutionist view similar to the initial so-called "national single [party]" theory, lacking class-based guiding spirit and vaguely suggesting that all national class strata should unconditionally participate in a common party. Next, the Shinganhoe was called the intermediary body of a "national cooperative [front/line]" (民族的 協同X선), but still, the leader of the national [revolution?] (國民XX), not XXX, could not control it and it still contained remnants of populism (人民主義的 殘滓)...
Can such a [struggle] (X爭) lacking any class-based guiding spirit truly exist? Such a thing can never exist on this earth. No matter how elementary the [struggle] for immediate demands, in a class society, it can only move realistically under a certain class-based guiding spirit.
B: Your theory is ultimately nothing but the sleep-talking (잠고대) of someone intoxicated with red ideology (赤色中毒者). If you follow your theory, you'll end up reaching the "separation-combination theory" (分離結合論) of the Fukumoto sect (福本宗派). Didn't we already completely liquidate the "separation-combination theory" in 1926-7 and grasp the Shinganhoe through a leap from unionist economic [struggle] (組合主義的 經濟XX) to full-front political [struggle] (全線的 政治XX)? Since there isn't an insurmountable Great Wall (萬里長城) separating national [liberation?] (民族XX) and [class struggle?] (XXXX), I think a [party-like] organization for cooperative [struggle] (協同XX) with the national petit bourgeoisie (民族的 小뿔조아지-) to some extent is absolutely necessary, even if temporary, at the current stage in Korea.
A: Haha! Calling me intoxicated with red ideology! Where did you learn that phrase? Then do you think the national bourgeoisie will really come to the forefront of the [struggle?] (XX) and fight alongside us? December 1928, spring (Omission - original text) If we analyze this national capitalist class (民族資産게급) further, there's national reformism (民族改良主義) (autonomist faction - 自治派) and left-wing nationalism (左翼民族主義), but these are ultimately twins on the reformist track of the same national [liberation?] movement (國民XX운동). The true nature of the former is obvious, so no need to say more, but the true nature of the latter, the left-wing nationalists, is also ridiculous (可笑). Their opposition to the autonomist faction is merely conceptual integrity-ism (觀念的 節介主義), and in reality, their hatred of [Communism?] (共XXX) is the same as that of the [Imperialists?] (帝XX의 者), so the only path they can realistically walk is an anti-[revolutionary?] role (反XX적 역할), nothing else. Therefore, we must thoroughly isolate this opposition faction with such reactionary tendencies (反動的 소질) from the worker-peasant strata (勞農層), while striving to organizationally capture the surging high tide (澎湃한 高潮) of the masses' [struggle] desire (大衆的 X爭慾) under our political influence. Why should we need to play the role of chief midwife (産姿役) to gather such petit-bourgeois opposition factions and establish the Shinganhoe?
B: That sounds plausible. But I think it's just a kind of left-wing infantilism (左翼小兒病) that doesn't know Leninist principles (레닌主義的 原則). (Omission - original text) Didn't Lenin say we should even support the big bourgeoisie against the reactionary aspirations of the petit bourgeoisie?
A: You really seem to be someone who ingested Leninism raw without digesting (消化) it and got choked. You're the one who sees only one side of Leninist principles and fails to see the other. Lenin said that the task of social democrats (社會民主主義者) is, while pointing out the commonality (共通性) between workers and other anti-government [?] groups (反政X的 집단), to always separate the workers from them, explain that the commonality is temporary or conditional, and emphasize (高調) the class independence (독립성) of the proletariat to the extent that they can oppose today's allies tomorrow. Some might tell us, "Such instructions weaken the [fighters?]" (X士를 弱하게 함이라), but we reply, "Such instructions are precisely what strengthens the [fighters?]" (X士를 强하게 함이라). That's right. Only those who fight representing the true interests of a specific class are truly strong [fighters?]. Also, the petit bourgeoisie is inherently dualistic (二重性的)... (Omission - original text) Well, isn't that right? <10> So, is this left-wing infantilism?
B: That's right. Then are you denying even the "Leninist principle that the proletarian movement must engage in cooperative [struggle] (協同XX) with [oppressed?] nations" (XXX민족)?
A: There's no reason to fundamentally deny that. But that cooperation is cooperation up to a certain stage, never eternal cooperation or eternal surrender (投降). Isn't it because this point was not well understood and applied that the Chinese [Communist?] Party (支那XX黨) was instead conquered (征服) by the Kuomintang (國民黨)? Also, in the Indian national movement (印度國民運動), unless it goes through a fierce [struggle] (激烈한 XX) against Gandhiism's (깐듸主義) non-violent preaching (非X力說敎), it will inevitably face the fate (運命) of failure.
B: That sounds plausible. So, are you saying the Shinganhoe should be dismantled (解體)?
A: That's right. It must be resolutely dismantled.
B: If we dismantle the Shinganhoe, shouldn't there be a more complete organization than the Shinganhoe to transfer (移舍식혀 가다) the masses within the Shinganhoe? If we just dissolve the Shinganhoe under circumstances where that's not possible, isn't that dissolving the [struggle] power (XX力量) of the masses, which would be suicidal (自殺的) action?
A: That's right. That's the problem. Of course, since the Shinganhoe is a kind of shackle (桎梏) hindering the growth of the masses, as long as it's being dissolved, the preconditions for one step forward in [struggle] (XX의 一步前進을 위한 前提적 조건) must be clearly presented. Otherwise, taking non-combative, dispersive actions like merely dissolving one branch, as the Shinganhoe Iwŏn branch did, is truly utterly laughable (笑止千萬) political play (政治的 遊戱).
B: Then what would truly be the precondition signifying one step forward?
A: The dissolution of the Shinganhoe, of course, aims to drive out the petit bourgeoisie within it and further expand and strengthen the [struggle] power (XX力) of the proletariat. Therefore, for the expansion of the [struggle] (XX) of the worker-peasant masses, it lies in (Omission - original text) establishing [something], and outwardly, in the expansion and strengthening (擴大强化) of labor unions and peasant unions.
B: If the Shinganhoe is completely dissolved, what about the issue of national cooperative [struggle/front] (民族的 協同XX)?
A: You still don't quite understand what I'm saying! National cooperative [struggle/front] doesn't necessarily have to be developed only through the Shinganhoe. Isn't it possible to discover the conditions for joint [struggle] (共同XX條件) with the radical petit bourgeoisie, etc., from the transformation (轉化) of the daily partial [struggles] (日常部分XX) of the worker-peasant masses into the overall [struggle] (全體XX), thereby acquiring them under the influence of one's own class? To put it more concretely, regarding the issue of national cooperative [struggle/front], the proletarian organizations should become the leader (盟主), involving groups like Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎), Christianity (基督敎), the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (衡平社), and other petit-bourgeois groups, unfolding brave [struggle] (勇敢한 XX), thereby absorbing (吸收) the radical petit bourgeoisie under our class influence.
B: ......
A: Do you have any further objections (異議)? Go ahead.
B: No particular objections. Then how should the Shinganhoe be dissolved (解消方法)?
A: Dissolution method? Right. That also needs to be clearly determined (究定). Regarding the dissolution policy (解消方針), it hasn't been presented (提示) nationwide yet, only raised partially, with resolutions from the Kyŏngsŏng branch, Pusan branch, Iwŏn branch, and Wŏnsan branch. Let's critique these strictly!
(a) Regarding the opposition theory of the Kyŏngsŏng branch, the reason for opposition lies in the fact that the right to act on behalf of the general assembly (全體大會代行權) was not exercised carefully. Thus, it's ultimately just a twin of the pro-dissolution faction that approves the right to act.
(b) Regarding the Pusan branch dissolution theory, seeing the conclusion to submit (上程) a dissolution proposal (解消案) to the general assembly next February, it ultimately seems to seek the dissolution method within the Shinganhoe itself. However, imagining that the dissolution of the Shinganhoe can be decided by a single famous resolution or discussion within the Shinganhoe, rather than seeking it through bold and unparalleled frontline [struggle] (前線的 XX) led by the proletariat outside the Shinganhoe, is a kind of conceptualist daydream (觀念論的 夢想).
(c) Regarding the differentiation theory (分化論) and dissolution theory of the Iwŏn branch: First, the differentiation theory suggests differentiating (分化식히다) workers into labor unions, peasants into peasant unions, and the petit-citizens into the Shinganhoe. However, the solution to the problem lies not in reciting this principle, but only in mobilizing and organizing (動員組織식히다) workers and peasants into actual [struggle] (實際的 XX) through the expansion and strengthening of class-based labor and peasant unions, which can actually differentiate the various class strata.
And looking at the claims of the dissolutionists (解體論者), they argue that although the Shinganhoe is composed (構成되엿스나) of workers, peasants, and petit-citizens, it is under the leadership (領導下) of petit-bourgeois reformists (改良主義者),<11> making concrete practical [struggle] (具體的 實踐XX) by each class impossible, thus it's acceptable to dismantle it. However, unless their deceptive policies (欺瞞政策) are exposed (曝露) through practical [struggle], this is ultimately just a kind of radical petit-bourgeois mischief (急進主義的 小뿔조아지-的 惡戱).
(d) As for the Wŏnsan branch resolution, even looking at the following slogan (술로간), it doesn't advocate dissolution but rather opposes it. And why they say "Let's actively participate in the cooperative [front/line]" (協同X선에 積極的 참가하자) is too ambiguous and hazy (瞹眛朦朧). The resolution is (Omission - original text)
Regarding the dissolution of the Shinganhoe, it should generally be either for or against; there cannot be neutrality (中立) that is neither for nor against, neither red nor white. Therefore, we boldly (大胆히) advocate for the resolute dissolution of the Shinganhoe. The dissolution method should not be satisfied with dissolving just one branch alone, but should organize a dissolution league (解消同盟) and achieve developmental dissolution (발전적 解消) through nationwide mass struggle (全鮮的 大衆的 鬪爭). Now that this dissolution theory arises from the masses' weariness (厭症), no matter how much they try not to dissolve the Shinganhoe, it will be the same as having actually dissolved it.
B: That's right. Then I agree too.
A: Well then! Let's shout "Long live the dissolution of the Shinganhoe!"
(The author is Chairman of the Inspection Committee of the Ch'ŏngnyŏn Ch'ongdongmaeng P'yŏngnam Provincial Alliance & an executive of the Anju Shinganhoe Branch)

Just Make It Active and Lively

Yi Kwangsu

One. Those who advocate for the dissolution of the Shinganhoe probably have reasons that outsiders (局外人) cannot meddle (아랑곳 하다) with. However, if there are no other underlying (裏面) reasons apart from the two surfaced reasons of "Shinganhoe's inactivity" (新幹會無爲) and "paralysis of fighting spirit" (鬪爭性痲痺), then dissolution is meaningless. Why? Because wouldn't it be fine if those advocating dissolution simply made the Shinganhoe active (有爲하게) and its fighting spirit lively (潑랄하게)?
Two. I believe there were no particularly memorable achievements (업적) in the past Shingan movement. However, rather than saying there were none, perhaps it was that there couldn't be any. Talk of Dissolution is a Perceptual Error

Im Wŏn'gŭn

For a while, the quiet political theory scene in Korea seems to have been stirred considerably by the "Shinganhoe Dissolution Issue," and it is presumed that this issue will likely continue to progress for some time. However, I do not yet intend to exaggerate or place great importance (誇大 중요시하려 아니한다) on this issue. This is because, although over a month has passed since this issue emerged, the dissolutionist side's thorough theory and statement regarding "dissolution" remain incomplete (불성립되여 잇는 까닭이다).
Looking at the main points of theory from the Iwŏn and Pusan branches that carried out dissolution resolutions, as reported in the papers:
1. The Shinganhoe is a petit-bourgeois (小뿌루조아지) group.
2. The Shinganhoe annihilates (말살한다) the fighting power of the worker-peasant masses.
Seeing these two points, I dismiss the Shinganhoe dissolution theory based on these alone. The reasons are as follows:
1. Firstly, the Shinganhoe is not such a purely (純全?한) petit-bourgeois group as this statement suggests; the majority of its constituent members are propertyless petit-citizens and the worker-peasant strata (노농층). Of course, it's also true that a large number of intellectuals (인테리) and the so-called petit-bourgeois layer are mixed in (혼재하여 잇는), and it's also true to a certain percentage that these petit-bourgeois elements currently hold the leadership roles (지도역할) in the Shingan movement. However, I do not believe in the firmness (확고성) of their leadership indefinitely, nor do I feel the danger of the worker-peasant strata being absorbed (被吸收性) by their petit-bourgeois guiding spirit. This is firstly because the foundational potential power (地盤的 잠재세력) of Korea's petit bourgeoisie, whose capitalist development is still extremely immature (幼稚의 域) compared to that of foreign countries,<16> is much weaker (미약한 것) than in other countries. Secondly, among the Korean worker-peasant masses, genuine trust in the native petit bourgeoisie is already scarce (희박하다). Thirdly, the worker-peasant strata currently included (포섭되여 잇는) in the Shinganhoe are already a propertyless populace with semi-training and semi-class consciousness (半訓練과 半계급의식), and only organizational and systemic training within their own class, along with its further expansion and strengthening, awaits them in the future.
Furthermore, the reason for the Shinganhoe's existence is by no means permanent (영구성); it came into being unnaturally (기형적으로), forced by objective conditions, in the political process of a small, weak nation (小弱民族). Therefore, the Shinganhoe inevitably carried the fate of dissolution (해소운명) from its birth; it is merely a matter of timing (時期問題). It will happen when the objective political situation (객관적 정세) in Korea changes, correspondingly clarifying the divisions in the class movement line (階級運動線), and consequently, the [struggle] power (XX力) of the worker-peasant masses themselves strengthens to the point where they feel no need for the kind of all-encompassing grand unity (두루뭉술이的 대동단결) like the unified pan-heavenly cooperative [front/line] (통일천하적 대협동X線) represented by the current Shinganhoe's existence. Based on the above factors, even if I were to accept the statement "The Shinganhoe is a petit-bourgeois group," one of the reasons for dissolution, I reject the suitability of its theoretical basis.
2. Secondly, the claim that "The Shinganhoe annihilates the [struggle] power (XX力) of the worker-peasant masses" is, frankly speaking, either the ignorant remark (客言) of so-called "left-wing infantilists" (左翼小兒病者) who don't know the objective circumstances in Korea today, or a perceptual error (인식착오) of a kind of conceptualist. By using such harsh words (酷言), I am by no means trying to defend or support (옹호지지하려는 것이 아니다) the Shinganhoe. It is merely because I wish to suppress the emergence of dissolution theories inappropriate for the time (시기에 부적당한), and because I detest the traditional bad habit (在來弊風) in the Korean movement line of casually importing others' things directly (直譯輸入하려는). (This doesn't mean I disregard legitimate theory.) Thinking about it, the recent talk about Shinganhoe dissolution in Korea somehow feels like it's pointlessly imitating (모방하는 것 가튼 느낌) others, following the model of the Japanese Rōnōtō dissolution talk. I really dislike hearing the term "Fukumotoism" (福本이즘), which was popular in Korea several years ago. (This is a digression, so let's drop it.)
The idea that the Shinganhoe, being a petit-bourgeois group, annihilates or at least diminishes (위축시킨다는 것) the [struggle] power of the worker-peasant masses sounds plausible at first listen (一聞), but it is utterly ignorant of Korea's objective circumstances. If that were true, what indeed were the historical achievements (역사적 X蹟) of the two major camps, the "Labor Federation (勞總)" and "Peasant Federation (農總)," which were outside the Shinganhoe, that motley crew (어중이 떠중이) coalition (합동체)? And what are they doing today?
Due to limited space (제한된 지면), I cannot write at length. In short, the point is simply to further organize and [struggle-ize?] (조직XX化하여 가면서) one's own camp (自我의 진영) as the worker-peasant masses outside the Shinganhoe, and dispatch well-trained masses from various fields into the Shinganhoe, the cooperative body encompassing all strata of Korea. Then, entering the interior, they, along with the distinct forces (自X勢力) existing behind them, will be able to exert their historical mission even better. If that happens, their [struggle] power (XX力) will not be diminished or weakened at all. Only when faced with such a [revolutionary?] process (XX的 과정) can one speak of Shinganhoe dissolution, and I believe the inevitable path (필연적 과정) for the current Korean proletariat (朝鮮푸로레타리아) to tread must surely be this way. What is the plan after tearing down (헐어 바리면) a house propped up (벗틔여 노흔) by force (仰制로) overnight (하로 아침에) without any future calculation (今後 타산도 업시)?
With these few words (멧말로), I reject the dissolutionists' second reason. I will await future developments (후기를 미루면서) on this issue. With these few words, though they may be inadequate for your question. (Note: The author is currently engaged in social movements)

**Summary**

This 1931 Samch'ŏlli article presents a survey of opinions from prominent Korean intellectuals, activists, and journalists on the controversial proposal to dissolve the Shinganhoe (New Korea Society). The Shinganhoe was a major united front organization aiming to unify nationalist and socialist factions against Japanese colonial rule. Respondents are sharply divided. Proponents of dissolution, often with socialist leanings (like An Pyŏngju, Song Pong'u, Yi Chuyŏn, Kim Kyŏngjae), argue the Shinganhoe is dominated by ineffective petit-bourgeois leadership, fails to engage in active struggle, hinders genuine class-based movements (especially labor and peasant unions), and reflects outdated strategies. Opponents (like Kim Pyŏngno, Cho Mansik, An Chaehong, Yi Kwangsu, Kwŏn Tongjin, Han Yong'un) stress the absolute necessity of national unity in the face of severe colonial oppression, warn against dispersing concentrated nationalist energy, argue the Shinganhoe's flaws should be internally reformed rather than leading to dissolution, and fear that premature dissolution would play into the hands of Japanese authorities or lead to factionalism without a viable alternative structure. The debate reveals deep ideological fissures concerning the path forward for the Korean movement – prioritizing national unity versus class struggle – under the constraints of Japanese colonialism.

Historians would find this article particularly valuable for several reasons. It provides a direct window into the intense ideological debates fracturing the Korean independence movement in the early 1930s, specifically the conflict between overarching nationalism and emerging socialist/communist class analysis influenced by international trends (e.g., Comintern policies, Japanese leftist debates like the Rōnōtō dissolution). The participation of major figures from various political, religious, and social sectors (journalism, law, education, Ch'ŏndogyo, Buddhism, social activism) offers a broad snapshot of elite opinion. Furthermore, the frequent use of "XX" for censored terms starkly illustrates the repressive political atmosphere under Japanese rule, forcing activists to use coded language when discussing sensitive topics like "struggle," "revolution," "party," "imperialism," or "communism."

朝鮮運動은 協同乎 對立乎, 新幹會「解消運動」批判
本社側......新幹會는 5년 전에 창립이래 현재 150支會와 4만 회원을 擁하고 잇는 朝鮮 최대의 結社가 되어 잇슴니다. 그런데 작년 겨을부터「新幹運動」에 대한 새로운 견해가 잇서서 그것이 드듸어「解消論」의 提唱에까지 이르고 만 것은 昨今의 新聞紙가 盛히 보도하고 잇는 사실이외다. 이때에 우리는 이 운동을 엇더케 밧어드리어 批判할 것인가? 압날의 우리 운동을 전개식히는데 잇서서 이 문제의 歸趨는 만흔 영향을 끼칠 것임으로 新幹會의 現 當局者를 위시하야 사회 각 방면 인사의 냉정하고도 공정한 의견을 듯기로 하엿슴니다. 더욱 참고로 本社側에서 의견을 請한 분 중에 編輯日字까지 回示가 업슨 분은 如左함니다.

金炳魯

新幹會解消論이 1,2支會에서 제창된 것은 사실입니다. 이것이 階級運動的 견지와 이론으로서만 본다면 근본적 의의에 잇서서는 근거가 업는 것이 안니겟지오만은 元來 新幹會로 말하면 그 조직의 본질과 또 자체에 잇서서 各層 各派를 통한 協同X線을 형성한 것일 뿐임으로 독특한 창조적 指圖綱領를 가진 것이 안님이다. 그럼으로 新幹運動은 조직자체의 협동의의에 기준하야 어느 階段까지 가능한 최대한도의 XX役割을 가질 뿐이오, 엇던 계급의 專用陣營이 될 수 업는 동시에 계급적 운동을 阻碍할 理도 업는 것입니다.

만일 新幹運動이 직면으로 계급투쟁화되지 못한다면 협동의 意義가 업다던지 또는 新幹運動이 强大化될사록 계급적 운동의 의식이 抹殺된다는 杞憂를 갓는 것은 新幹運動의 階段X略과 협동정신에 대한 誤謬밧게 될 것 업는 것입니다. 現下 全的 운동이 窒息된 情勢에 잇서서 계급투쟁의 이론에 偏依하야 新幹運動의 의의와 존재를 의심한다면 이는 결성된 陣營을 毁棄하며 집중된 역량까지 潰散하는 행동밧게 업는 것임니다. 만일 여긔에 覺悟함이 업시 이론이 分立된다면 결국 分化작용에 轉化되여 우리 운동의 역량을 감소함에 이를 것을 우려하는 바임니다. 우리에게 결성된 역량이 잇고 그 역량을 誤用妄導하는 바 잇다면 要는 그것을 善導運用함에 잇는 것이오 역량의 潰散에 잇슬 것이 안임니다. 우리의 협동의의는 單一的 의식과 극치적 이론에 置中함보다 민족의 當面 權益을 대표하야 가능한 최대한도의 XX을 協力 展開하야 어느 階段의 달성을 期함에 근거가 잇는 것을 명백히 인식하여야 할 것인  줄 암니다.(註=氏는 현재 新幹會 中央委員長)

漁人에 利를 줌은 大禁物

曺晩植

一. 新幹會의 解消運動은 現下 朝鮮情勢로 보아서 절대로 拒斥할 바입니다.
理由
解消를 主唱하는 이들은 현재의 新幹會는「小뿔조아的 집단이니」「적극적 투쟁이 업느니」또는「勞農大衆의 투쟁욕을 抹殺식히느니」等等의 論으로 우리의 情勢는 보지 아니하고 다만 일종의 계급적 의식으로만 보는 모양인 듯 합니다.
그러나 나는 以下의 몃가지를 생각할 필요가 잇다고 봅니다.
1. 新幹會를 계급적 派別的의 의식으로 當하지 말 것.
2. 우리는 朝鮮의 情勢를 보아 투쟁에 잇서 緩急의 酌量이 잇스리라는 것을 互相諒知하고 無謀한 破壞와 決裂을 일삼지 말 것.
3. 造黨自體나 友誼團體가 或 過誤 沈滯 其他 失當한 일이 잇슬 때에는 우리는 手術 又는 鞭韃을 줄지언정 피차 軋轢하야 漁人에게 利를 주게 함은 大禁物인 것.
二. 과거의「新幹運動에 대하야 나의 所見은 이러합니다. 新幹會에 대한 一般의 처음 企待가 퍽 컷든 것은 사실이다. 그러기 때문에 這間多少의 투쟁사실이 不無햇슴에 불구하고 적극적 투쟁이 업섯다고 보게까지 된 줄로 안다. 그러나 말하자면 과거 數年이야말로 宣傳指導 又는 훈련의 시대로 볼 수 박게 업다. 東西古今을 통하야 어떤 運動史를 보든지 短期間에 막대한 성적을 낸 것은 극히 드문 것이 안인가. 그러하다고 하면 新幹會는 아직<7> 그마마한 途程에 잇다고 보아도 좃타. 그러함으로 우리는 더욱 鞏固한 통일로 과연 역량을 집중하야 3년을 不飛不鳴하다가 衝天飛, 驚人鳴한다든 어떤 새와 가티 하도록 하자.
(註=氏는 현재 平壤新幹支會의 委員長)

解消絶對反對-朝鮮情勢로 보아서-

安在鴻

一. 일부에서 提唱되는 解消運動은 나로서 拒斥하고 또一般으로서도 拒斥할 것이라고 밋습니다.
첫재 新幹會는 民族主義的 正統思想을 대표하는 左翼陣營되기를 목표로 徐徐히 또급속히 그 結成過程을 걸어 나아가서 그 昻揚과 결성이 아울너 크고 놉허지는 시기에까지 간 때에 轉換되는 時局과 한 가지 비로소 그 역사적「할 노릇」을 다할 수 잇게 될 것입니다. 그때에 時代情勢가 그로 하야금 급각도로 방향을 전환케 하든지 혹은 제2의 新結成의 때문에 소위 발전적인 解消를 한다는 것 따위는 그때 문제입니다. 그러나 지금 그 多難한 結成過程에서 벌서 解消부터 한다는 것은 그야말로 敗北主義에 지나지 안습니다.
둘재 勞働者 農民들의 투쟁의 本營으로서의 勞働組合 農民組合 등을 擴大强化시키고 勞働者 農民들을 거기로 撤退 集中시키어 그의 계급적 결성에 의한 XX化을 하려고 하는 것이라고 하지마는 新幹會 그것이『大衆의 成長과 XX의 발전을 期할 수 업는 情勢이라 하면 勞働者 農民의 組合도 반듯이 XX化와 擴大强化를 가저오기 어려울 줄 압니다. 아무렴 각 부문의 갓튼 한 結束이니 만큼 新幹會의 尨大해서 몸 주체못하는 경향과는 다소 다르다고 할 터이나 그러나 XX同盟으로서의 勞働農民組合의 擴大强化라는 것을 新幹會運動과는 全혀 다르게 特殊한 기대를 할 수 업습니다. 그럼으로 조합으로 돌아가기 때문에의 新幹解消를 찬성할 수 업습니다.
