A Collection of Jokes

The Judge's Masterful Questioning

Woman: "I am truly pure and innocent (淸廉潔白)."
Judge: "But you perfectly match the description of the culprit. Young, beautiful, like a noblewoman, like a woman destined for greatness, and moreover, seemingly educated..."
Woman: "Your Honor! Then I confess. I am truly the culprit."

Son and Father

Pyŏngnam was caught secretly taking 10 Wŏn from his father's safe (金庫).
Father: "You don't need to resort to stealing like that. When I die, won't all my property go to you anyway?"
Pyŏngnam: "In that case, just subtract 10 Wŏn when you give it to me then. It's the same difference (마천가지)."

Poison Test

A: "Isn't there a way to know if this mushroom has poison or not?"
B: "What's the big deal? Just eat the mushroom first and wait patiently for a while. See if your stomach hurts or not."

Waiting Room

A passenger, displaying anger: "Don't the trains here ever arrive on time?"
Rural Station Attendant (停車場驛夫): "That's why there's a waiting room (待合室). If they came on time, would we need a waiting room just for cats to nap in..."

Reasons for Roundness

On an exam asking for "Three reasons why the Earth is round":
1. Because the teacher said it is round.
2. Because it is written in the book that the Earth is round.
3. Because my older brother who goes to middle school also said it is round.<60>

**Summary**

This article presents four short humorous anecdotes or jokes typical of the era. They include a courtroom exchange where flattery leads to a confession, a witty retort from a son caught stealing, a darkly humorous suggestion for testing mushroom toxicity, and a cynical justification for train delays and waiting rooms. These jokes offer a glimpse into the humor and everyday social interactions of 1930s Korea. For historians, they provide insight into common themes (authority, family dynamics, money, daily inconveniences) and the style of popular humor during the colonial period, reflecting perhaps a blend of traditional wit and modern situations.

笑話集
裁判長의 名質問

婦人 「저는 정말 淸廉潔白해요.」
裁判長 「그러나 너는 범인의 人相書와 꼭 갓다. 젊고 어엽부고 貴婦人갓고 將來 크게 될 女子갓고 그러고 더구나 學識이 잇는 것 갓고...」
婦人 「判事님! 그러면 自白하지요. 제가 정말 犯人이람니다.」

아들과 아버지

丙南이가 아버지 金庫에서 남몰래 10圓을 끄내다가 들킷다.
父 「그러케 도적질까지 아니하여도 내가 죽으면 내 財産은 모다 네게 갈것이 아니야.」
丙南 「그러거든 그때 10圓만 除하고 주서요. 마천가지니까.」

有毒試驗

甲 「이 송이에 毒이 잇고업는 것을 아는 方法이 업슬가.」
乙 「그까짓것 송이를 몬저 먹고 한참 참어봐라. 배가 압흔가 아니압흔가고.」

待合室

乘客이 怒氣를 띄고 「여기 汽車는 언제나 제시간에 마처 오는일이 업지 안으냐?」
시골停車場驛夫 「그러기에 待合室이 잇지. 제시간에 오면 고양이 낫잠자라고 待合室을 두어...」

동근 理由

「地球가 둥근 理由 세가지를 들나」는 試驗答案에
1, 先生님이 둥글다고 하엿슴니다.
2, 冊에 地球는 둥근것이라고 씨어 잇슴니다.
3, 中學校에 다니는 언이도 둥글다고 하엿슴니다.<60>
