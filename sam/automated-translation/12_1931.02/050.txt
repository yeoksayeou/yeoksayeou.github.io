Lighting up a cigarette

A *toe* of rice costs 16 *chŏn*. It's still more expensive than dirt. Because it's expensive*, the Landlord (地主) has become arrogant. The absentee landlord is even more arrogant.

The Tonga Ilbo's (東亞日報) "Establishment of Reading Week" is a timely event. However, its pages are ancient-looking. Get rid of the moth-eaten paper (조히) and moss-covered (이태) characters. The sky is wide, the era is advancing... the words used are narrow, and the "History of the Three Kingdoms" (三國史) is already rotten...

All—education to "vocational education!" Right, soon enough, a chicken farm will be spread out at Kwanghwamun intersection.

The rumor of an indirect tax through a "beer monopoly," upon meeting opposition from the Tōkyō and Ōsaka Capitalist Cliques (資本閥), was immediately replaced by the emergence of a "takju monopoly" rumor. Ginseng, Salt (鹽), and then takju, beer, soy sauce, sesame salt (깨소곰), chili powder (고초가루), air... the resources are still limitless.

Seoul's (서울) T'apdong Park (塔洞公園) Octagonal Pavilion (八角堂) is left to the wind and rain (風雨). After the wind and rain, goblins (독갑); after the goblins, ghost stories (괴담)... Alas (鳴呼)! Are our descendants born with the fate (宿命) of hearing ghost stories? Let those with intention (뜻잇는 이) quickly repair it. <19>

**Summary**

This short piece offers satirical commentary on various aspects of Korean society under Japanese rule in 1931. The author laments the high price of rice favoring landlords, criticizes the Tonga Ilbo newspaper for being outdated despite new initiatives, mocks the push for purely vocational education, exposes the cynical expansion of potential government monopolies and indirect taxes (from beer to takju and everyday items), and deplores the neglect of cultural sites like the Octagonal Pavilion in T'apdong Park, fearing the loss of heritage. For historians, the article provides valuable glimpses into public sentiment regarding economic hardship (rice prices, landlordism), media critique, educational policy debates, colonial taxation strategies (especially the shift from beer to takju monopoly discussions reflecting resistance and adaptation), and concerns about cultural preservation during the colonial period. The specific mention of potential taxes on items like chili powder and air highlights the perceived rapaciousness of the colonial administration.

담배 한 대 피어 물고
쌀갑 한되에 16錢. 아직은 흙갑보다 빗사다. 빗삽*까지 地主는 코이 놉흐젓다. 부재지주는 더욱 놉흐젓다.

東亞日報의 「讀書週間創設」은 時宜을 어든 일. 그러나 지면에 고색창연. 좀먹은 조히 이태(苔) 낀 문자를 가리어 버려라. 하늘은 넓고 시대는 나아가고... 말사쓰는 쪽고 「三國史」는 이미 썩고...

모든―교육을 「실업교육!에」 올치 이제 얼마 아니잇서 광화문 네거리에 양계장이 설판.

간접조세인 「맥주전매」설이 東京, 大阪 資本閥의 반대를 맛나자 그 뒤로 즉시 「탁주전매」설이 대신 대두, 인삼, 鹽, 그러고 탁주, 맥주, 간장, 깨소곰, 고초가루, 공기... 아직도 자원은 무진장.

서울 塔洞公園 八角堂은 풍우에 일임, 풍우 뒤엔 독갑, 독갑 뒤엔 괴담... 鳴呼 우리 자손은 괴담드를 숙명을 가지고 나오든가 어서 뜻잇는 이어 改修합시다. <19>
