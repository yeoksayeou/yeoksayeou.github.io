Article Title: 佛國寺의 冬栢꼿 (Camellia Flowers of Bulguksa Temple)
Author: 파인 (P'ain)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The Camellia Flowers of Bulguksa Temple
						P'ain

In the snowy courtyard of Bulguksa Temple
Camellia flowers bloom, bloom
Laughing, tiring,
Becoming fruit, they fall.

The flower blooming amidst the snow's glare
Is the spirit of this people,
With the fire burning in living plants and trees (生草木)
It is the flame in this heart.
- P'ain - <Cover>

**Summary**

This short poem by P'ain depicts camellia flowers blooming stubbornly in the snow at Bulguksa Temple. The flowers cycle through blooming, tiring, becoming fruit, and falling, symbolizing resilience. The poet explicitly links the flower blooming in the harsh winter landscape to the enduring spirit (넉) of the Korean people and the passionate fire within their hearts, fueled by nature itself. From a colonial history perspective, the poem is interesting for its subtle yet potent nationalist sentiment. By connecting the resilient camellia in a historically significant Korean location (Bulguksa) to the "spirit of this people," it evokes a sense of national endurance and inner strength despite the harsh conditions (implicitly, the colonial rule symbolized by the snowy winter).

佛國寺의 冬栢꼿
						파인
佛國寺 눈 마당에
동백꼿 피어, 피어
웃다가 지치다가
열매되어 떨어진다.

눈바래속에 피는 꼿은
이 백성의 넉일레라,
生草木의 붓는 불로
이 가슴의 불길이라.
- 파인 - <표지>
