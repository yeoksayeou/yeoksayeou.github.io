Article Title: 豊年이 왓구나 (A Good Harvest Has Come)
Author: 파인
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A Good Harvest Has Come
						P'ain

Seeds were sown in the March-April gentle rain,
Grain is harvested in the September-October wind.
Ehe ehe ya, a good harvest has come.
For us farmers, a good harvest has come.

Who brought us this good harvest?
Heaven doesn't know, nor did the earth.
Ehe ehe ya, who would give it?
My own forearms, my own sweat created it.

As the Eighth Month Ch'usŏk approaches,
Shall we butcher a cow, or shall we pound rice cakes?
Ehe ehe ya, a good harvest has come.
For the eighty households of this village, a good harvest has come.<31>

**Summary**

This poem celebrates the arrival of a bountiful harvest. It describes the cycle of planting in spring and harvesting in autumn, attributing the success not to divine intervention or nature alone, but emphatically to the hard labor and sweat of the farmers themselves. The poem concludes with anticipation for the Ch'usŏk festival, a time for communal celebration within the village. From a historical perspective, the poem offers a glimpse into rural Korean life in the early 1930s. Its strong emphasis on self-reliance and the value of farmers' labor ("My own forearms, my own sweat created it") might be seen by historians as a subtle expression of resilience and pride amidst the hardships of the colonial period, implicitly contrasting the farmers' agency with external dependencies or controls.

豊年이 왓구나
						파인
삼사월 단비에 씨갓을 뿌리어
구시월 바람에 곡식을 거둔다.
에헤 에헤 야, 풍년이 왓구나.
우리나 농부네 풍년이 왓구나.

풍년을 누구가 갓다 주든가
하늘은 모르고 땅도 아니데
에헤 에헤 야, 누가 주겟나
내 팔뚝 내땀이 만들어 내엇지.

八月 秋夕이 갓가워 오니
소나 잡을가, 떡이나 처볼가.
에헤 에헤 야, 풍년이 왓구나.
이동리 八十戶 풍년이 왓구나.<31>
