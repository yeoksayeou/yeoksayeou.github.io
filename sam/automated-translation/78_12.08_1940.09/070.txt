**Body Text Translation:**
My View of 'President Hitler'

Pak Sŏk-yun, Former Manchukuo Consul General stationed in Poland

A hero of singular genius.

Pak Ch'ang-hun, Doctor of Medicine

Judging by Lombroso's book "Genius and Insanity," he seems half-mad, half-genius, but the conclusion is that he is an outstanding person.

Sakong Hwan

President Hitler is a genius hero who is now conquering the European world and attempting to renew the hearts and minds of the world. Seeing his consistent pursuit of hegemony (覇道一貫) and exaggeration of bold spirit, it would not be an overstatement to say he surpasses the great Napoleon.

Yi Chong-man, Founder of Taedong Mining College

President Hitler is the hero of today. However, the existence of today's Hitler is a gift of the great unity of the German people. As long as progress can be expected from the world being in flux, Germany's victory will surely spur a transition to a new order. <39>

Yi Chŏng-sŏp, Kyŏngsŏng Central Broadcasting Station

I don't know if he can be called a hero, but whether he is a politician or not is difficult to assert without seeing the progress of the future European new order construction movement. Things like visiting Napoleon's tomb to pay respects after capturing Paris can be seen as a masterpiece from a human perspective.

Yi Chŏng-jae, Director at Yŏngbo Hapmyŏng Hoesa

President Hitler is a hero and a man of spirit born of the 20th century. When he conducted the signing ceremony for the subjugation of France in the sleeping car, the long-cherished desire of the Germanic people, displayed in the Forest of Compiegne, not only we of the friendly nation Japan, but surely everyone must have felt exhilaration. He will certainly achieve final victory, and I pray it will be so.

Yi Kŭk-ro, Chosŏn Language Society (Doctor of German Philosophy)

A forceful person.

Sin T'ae-ok, Lawyer

Although I think there might be room for various criticisms regarding his character as a human being, he is an unprecedented hero produced by the German nation; furthermore, he is truly a god-like man of genius who creates his own foundation, base, and even stirs the winds and clouds. Thinking about it, his future will be eventful.

Kim Tong-jin, Counsellor at Sŏnman Colonization Company

Until the "Hitler Jugend" becomes the backbone of Germany, it might be premature to speak of the stability of "Hitler's Germany." What does his pilgrimage to Napoleon's tomb right after entering Paris suggest? It is difficult for an ordinary person to guess what lies behind that gate. <40>

Hong Yang-myŏng, Editor-in-Chief at Mansŏn Ilbo

The fact that figures who followed conventional paths, gaining seniority and climbing the ladder step-by-step, cannot accomplish great things is a practical lesson given by the epoch-making success today of Mr. Hitler, who was a corporal 21 years ago—a truly thrilling event! Moreover, his fanatical passion and speedy drive speak to him being a genius among geniuses.

Chŏng Sŏk-t'ae, Doctor of German Medicine

One. To the German people, rather than President Hitler, he will be recognized as a savior who resurrected the German nation from the land of the dead.
Two. He becomes a leader providing the motive for self-awareness to find a way forward for the world's poor nations (Japan, Germany, Italy) and oppressed countries (India, China, South America, etc.), allowing us to see a bright world reformation, and making Britain, France, the US, etc., receive punishment for the sins they committed—he seems to be a rare, exhilarating great man of modern history.

Chŏng Kyo-wŏn, Managing Director of the National Spiritual Mobilization Chosŏn League

Based on his actions up to today, I do not hesitate to call Mr. Hitler one of the great men of the modern era. Whether looking at his spirit of sacrifice or his executive ability... However, whether he will truly become a great man in history or not remains to be seen in the future. If he merely ends with a vain, hegemonic victory, he will be nothing more than a prominent figure of his time; if he can capably complete his proclaimed thousand-year project with justice, he will truly become Europe's foremost great man in history.

Yi Kwang-su, Former Chairman of the Chosŏn Literary Association

Around Shōwa 5 (1930), I translated and published a part of Hitler's "Mein Kampf" (in the Tōkō Sōsho series). And I pointed out that this totalitarianism (全體主義) would surely sweep the world of the future. The word 全體主義 in Chinese characters <41> was first used by me in "Tōkō" (東光). At that time, the word "Fascio" existed, but the word 全體主義 did not.
In the movie "Festival of the Nations" (民族의 祭典), there were several snaps of President Hitler watching the Olympic Games. There were scenes of him clenching both fists and fretting when German athletes were in tight spots, and rejoicing when German athletes won. Seeing his demeanor as a human being like that made me happy.
He is a person who has no family, no pleasures, and makes patriotism his life.

Ham Sang-hun, Editor-in-Chief at Chosŏn Ilbo

Having risen from a corporal through struggle to attain his current position, there is no doubt he is a hero. The mission of the Führer, who has emerged bearing the thousand-year destiny of the German nation, is heavy and great. I look forward to the day he succeeds in breaking the world dominance of the Anglo-Saxon race. However, if, in dealing with the Anglo-Saxons, he uses Germanness, and instead of capital, uses violence to oppress numerous weak and small nations, the people of the world will once again be unable to live in peace.

Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak, President of Taech'ang Mining Company

Hoping he doesn't become a second Napoleon?

Yi Chong-nin, Leader of T'iandoism (天道敎)

A hero with two sides, wisdom and foolishness.

Yi Sŏng-hwan, Director at Taedong Mining Company

An energetic man of the emergency type; once the Great War ends and perhaps a European federation is formed, he will be an unnecessary figure for the German populace.

Kim Tong-hwan, President of Samch'ŏlli Company <42>
(Continued on page 141)
More than anything, he knows how to write with his own hand, speaks his mind without a secretary's draft; whereas the path to becoming a hero often involves deification by hiding one's rather unattractive face from the crowd as much as possible, he enjoys appearing before the crowd too much; he doesn't wear a bulletproof vest out of fear of the people, nor does he sit in a car fitted with bulletproof glass windows; he has served time in prison; before becoming Commander-in-Chief, he carried a rifle as a corporal and went to the battlefield; he has not married for the sake of the country; I have not heard of beautiful women and fine wine in his residence—thus, he gives the impression of a great man, a truthful man.—(End)— <141>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* compiles opinions from various prominent Korean figures on Adolf Hitler. The views are diverse, ranging from admiration for Hitler as a "genius," "hero," and "savior" of Germany who challenges the existing world order dominated by Britain and France, to more cautious or critical assessments questioning his methods, long-term impact, and comparing him (sometimes unfavorably) to Napoleon. Several commentators express exhilaration at Germany's victories, seeing them as paving the way for a "new order," while others worry about replacing one form of dominance (Anglo-Saxon) with another (German). For historians of colonial Korea, this article is revealing as it shows a spectrum of elite Korean opinion during the height of Japanese imperialism and the early stages of WWII. It demonstrates how some Koreans aligned themselves ideologically with the Axis powers, viewing Hitler's rise as a positive force against Western imperialism, while others, even amidst praise, expressed reservations about the nature of his power and the potential consequences for weaker nations, reflecting the complex political positioning of Koreans under colonial rule. Yi Kwang-su's claim to have coined the Korean term for "totalitarianism" (全體主義) is also noteworthy.

세찬 사람.
