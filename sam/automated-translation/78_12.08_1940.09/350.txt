"Namsadang" (Male Itinerant Performer)
						No Ch'ŏnmyŏng

I am a man who puts powder (粉) on his face
And lets down his braided hair like samttan.

Evenings when the chorach’i in conical hats (草笠) and vests (快子)
Blow their nallari horns,
I don a crimson skirt and become Hyangdani.

Thus, borrowing a wide yard somewhere in the marketplace,
Inside a cloth tent (布帳) where the lamp (람프) light is turned up high,
My male voice (男聲) is utterly humiliated. <194>

In that village passed beyond the mountain,
There was even a fine maiden (處女)
For whom I wanted to buy a silver ring,

But maiden, for whom the next day brings departure,
I had the blood of a gypsy (집시).

Tomorrow, into which village (洞里) shall we enter again?

Following behind the mule (노새)
Carrying our props (道具),
Shaking off the dew from the wild strawberries
As we set out on the road at dawn,

Like the sound of the nallari gathering spectators,
Sorrow and joy bloom intermingled. <195>
<194-195>

**Summary**

This poem by No Ch'ŏnmyŏng describes the life and inner turmoil of a male itinerant performer (Namsadang) in Korea. The speaker details the process of cross-dressing for performance, putting on makeup and women's clothes to become "Hyangdani," while feeling the humiliation of suppressing his male voice. He reflects on a nomadic life, recalling a missed connection with a village maiden and accepting his transient "gypsy blood." The poem concludes with the bittersweet feeling of setting out at dawn for the next performance, where sorrow and joy mix like the sound of the nallari horn. For historians, the poem offers insight into the lives of marginalized performers during the colonial period, touching on themes of gender identity, performance, rootlessness, and the complex emotions tied to a traditional way of life facing uncertain times. The use of specific cultural terms (Namsadang, Hyangdani, nallari, etc.) and the depiction of itinerant life provide a glimpse into Korean folk culture and social structures of the era.

「男사당」

나는 얼굴에 粉을 하고
삼딴같이 머리를 따 네리는 사나이

초립에 쾌자를 걸친 조라치들이
날나리를 부는 저녁이면
다홍치마를 둘르고 나는 香丹이가 된다

이리하야 장터어늬, 넓운 마당을 빌어
람프 불을 도둔 布帳 속에선
내 男聲이 十分 屈辱되다＜194＞

山 넘어 지나온 저 村엔
銀반지를 사주고 싶은
곻은 處女도 있었것만

다음 날이면 떠남을 가저 오는
處女야 나는 집시의 피였다

내일은 또 어늬 洞里로 들어간다냐

우리들의 道具를 실은
노새의 뒤를 따라
山딸기의 이슬을 떨며
길에 오르는 새벽은

구경군을 뫃으는 날나리 소리처럼
슬픔과 기쁨이 섞여 핀다.＜195＞
