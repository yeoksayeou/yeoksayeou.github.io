Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제8호
Publication Date: 1940년 09월 01일
Article Title: 베를렌의 탄식 (Verlaine's Lament)
Author: 주요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I
The choked sobbing sound of a violin on an autumn day
In that monotonous melody, how can the heart not ache?
As the wind scatters the fallen leaves, I suffer even more.

II
Scattering this way and that, are the fallen leaves my own fate?<191>
A pale face, deep sighs, there is no way to stop them.
What sound of bells tears the heart and makes it weep so?<192>
<191-192>

**Summary**

This article is a Korean poem by Chu Yohan, presented as "Verlaine's Lament," likely an adaptation or translation of Paul Verlaine's famous "Chanson d'automne." The poem evokes a deep sense of autumnal melancholy and personal despair, using imagery of violin sounds, falling leaves, pale faces, and tolling bells to convey a feeling of helplessness and sorrow. From a historical perspective, the publication of a translation of a melancholic French symbolist poem by a prominent Korean intellectual in 1940, amidst escalating wartime mobilization and cultural suppression under Japanese rule, is noteworthy. It reflects the persistence of Western literary influence and potentially offered an outlet for expressing sentiments of ennui or sadness, albeit indirectly through the guise of translation, which might have been tolerated more easily than original expressions of dissent or despair.
