Konoe Guard and Korean Clothing

In August of Meiji 33 (1900), Prince Konoe Atsumaro, father of Prince Konoe Fumimaro, was stopping in Kyŏngsŏng on his way back from an inspection tour (那視支察). One day, while sightseeing in Kyŏngsŏng city, he boarded a streetcar at Chongno 3-ch'ŏm and was heading towards Ch'ŏngnyangni via Tongdaemun when he saw a lovely child playing by the roadside. Because the child had rouge (연지) dabbed on its face, Prince Atsumaro asked his attendant, Mr. Kikuchi Kenjō,

"Is that child a girl?"

"No, it's a boy," Mr. Kikuchi replied.

Then, not long after, this time they saw a little girl playing. Pointing to that child, Prince Atsumaro asked,

"Is that one also a boy?"

"No, that child is a girl," he answered.

"They are truly lovely children. Just like we were in our own childhood. Especially their clothes are very nice. I'd like to get clothes like that for our Fumimaro to wear," Prince Atsumaro said, highly praising the Korean clothes (朝鮮 옷). After returning home, he arranged through people at the Imperial Household Ministry (宮內省) to have a set of Korean children's clothes made.

In later years (後年), when Mr. Kikuchi told this story to Prince Fumimaro, Prince Fumimaro said,

"Ah, now that you mention it, I remember wearing Korean clothes (朝鮮 의복) when I was nine years old," recalling the deeply felt old memory. <178>

**Summary**

This short reminiscence recounts an anecdote from 1900 when Prince Konoe Atsumaro, visiting Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), was charmed by the appearance of Korean children and their traditional clothing. Mistaking a boy wearing rouge for a girl, he admired the children's attire so much that upon returning to Japan, he had a set of Korean clothes made for his young son, Fumimaro (who would later become Prime Minister of Japan). Years later, Fumimaro recalled wearing these clothes as a child. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it portrays a prominent Japanese figure expressing aesthetic appreciation for an aspect of Korean culture during the early period preceding full annexation and more intense assimilation policies. Published in 1940, amidst wartime mobilization and heightened assimilation pressure, this anecdote might serve to humanize Prime Minister Konoe Fumimaro or subtly suggest a longer, perhaps more benign, history of Japanese interest in Korea, contrasting with the harsher realities of the time.

近衛兵과 朝鮮衣服
明治33년(1900년) 8월에, 那視支察로부터 歸途 중, 京城에 들린 近衛文麿公의 부친 近衛篤麿公이 어떤날 京城 시내를 구경하려고, 鍾路三丁目에서 전차를 타고 동대문을 지나 청량리 쪽으로 간 일이 있었는데 그때 길가에서 놀고 있는 어여쁜 아이가 있었다. 얼굴에 연지를 찍었기 때문에 篤麿公이,
『저 애는 계집앤가요?』
하고, 수행인 菊池謙讓씨더러 물으니,
『아니요, 사내입니다.」
하고, 菊池씨는 대답하였다. 그러자 얼마가지 않어서 이번엔 한 계집애가 놀고 있었는데, 그 애를 보고 篤麿公이,
『저 애도 사내애요?」
하고, 묻기에,
『안요, 그 애는 계집애얘요.』
하고 대답했더니,
『실로 예쁜 애들이로군. 우리 어렷을 때와 조금도 다름이 없군 그래. 특히 그 옷이 매우 좋소. 저런 옷을 우리 文麿에게도 가져다 입히고 싶소.』
하고, 朝鮮 옷을 격찬한 후, 고향에 돌아가서 宮內省 사람들을 周旋시켜서 朝鮮 아이 옷을 한벌 만들어 자*일이 있다.
後年, 이 일을 菊池씨가 文麿公더러 이야기하니, 文麿公은,
『참, 그렇게 말하니 내가 아홉살 때 朝鮮 의복을 입은 기억이 나요.』
하고 감회깊은 옛 기억을 말하였다. ＜178＞
