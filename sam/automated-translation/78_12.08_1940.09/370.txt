**Body Text Translation**

Youth
						Pa In

*Haedanghwa (Rugosa Rose)*

Intensely living, intensely blooming Haedanghwa
Haedanghwa that makes a man's heart flutter twofold
Before blooming, it makes one worry it might bloom where I cannot see,
And after blooming, it makes one anxious it might wither where I can see—.

*Like a Spring*

Though the clear water of the mountain valley flows on unconcerned <198>
Willows by the riverbank gain strength and rise one after another, flowers too reveal their faces
Fish flash their white bellies, and even the wind sings like holy water,
Thus, like a spring, even if you simply pass by unconcerned
This street will instantly brighten, fill with the laughter of youth
And I too, stopping my whistling, will gladly descend from the mountain.

*One Holding a Kŏmun'go*

Lingering still, deep in the night, is perhaps to fall asleep as if lulled by the sound of the kŏmun'go,
Pricking up ears early at dawn is perhaps to awaken gently to the sound of the kŏmun'go,
Sleeping and waking is a body dependent on your hand gestures
So what use is there in urging the setting sun and rising moon?
O one holding the kŏmun'go, please come quickly, on this night of anguish (懊惱) where I cannot sleep.

*Lost and Hidden*

Sent away and turning back, like a cloud in the sky, <199>
When will the wind meet and scatter us?
When will we become rain, divided East and West?
Therefore, thinking after sending you away, between those who love
There is nothing to lose or hide by saying there is a next day.

*Greek Woman*

Cutting my ring finger with a reed leaf
I scatter red blood to the sky,
This gushing blood of a young body, this hot breath
There is no one on this street to receive it
Thinking of a distant Greek Woman (希臘女人), today too I scatter it vainly to the sky.

*To the One I Love—*

Soaring and soaring again
It saw the blue sky, <200>
With the taste of kicking free from the narrow nest
That eagle flies well.
If only we too, while living in this world, could have had such a moment once.

*Stringless Zither (無絃琴)*

What does "Fig" (無花果) mean? Such a sad name exists in the world.
What tune is the "Stringless Zither" (無絃琴)? Such a sad tune one endures listening to.
But compared to this lonely heart
That sinks back down with no way to reach, what could be sadder?

*As If the Sky Has Intent*

On this desolate day with no reply despite waiting
I climb the mountain, take off my hat to the sky, and wave it,
As if the sky has intent
My heart becomes lighter, <201>

*Tomorrow*

By the old well near the gourd-vine-covered wall
Floating white gourd flowers in a water jar, carrying them one after another
That charm is too much to take in all at once
So today, from a distance, I gaze only at the lily-like charm of the back view and intend to savor the cool corners of the eyes tomorrow,
This frustrating joy of leaving something for tomorrow and waiting for the night.

*Jar of Fire*

Pouring oil (지름) into a jar, placing it atop the Castle Gate (城門)
Lighting a fire there, letting it burn until dawn
Placing that jar in the center, I wish I could circle it all night long,
Forgetting all thoughts, all memories, until the oil is all consumed
Circling and circling, like a moth drawn to the flame, circling the fire, making small turns <202>
Perhaps for just this night, I might escape from that voice, that image that torments me each evening.

*An Excerpt from a Certain Man's "Ballroom Notebook" (舞踏會의 手帖)*

1.
Like Dietrich of "Morocco" (「모록코」의 데-드릿히), the night of Passion and Impression opened upon our first meeting
As if weeping, as if yearning, poured into the rhythm of the "Italian Garden" (「伊太利의 庭園」) that unfolds heartbreakingly, this stooped heart
We even turned off the candles and lit that night of Stars and Moon with talk wrapped in the "Sorrow of Life" (「人生의哀切」)
She read Pushkin's poetry collection in English translation, was chased by police (포吏) during her girls' school days
A body like Anna Pavlova's (안나바울릿치) in "The Dying Swan" (「白鳥의死」) embodying beauty, transformed into a dancer (舞姬)
Standing tall and pure like a single narcissus blooming in the mire (泥濘), though perhaps a kisaeng (妓女)
Moreover, regretting her full bloom, she bloomed only halfway, and even that for less than three days, a body that came into my heart
Though I dare not search for where she has gone now, when the night of Stars and Moon of early summer comes
I put on the "Italian Garden" record (레코-드) and cannot help but long for the "Sappho of the East" (「東方의 삿포」).

2. <203>

With hands pitiful and soft like Sonia's in "Crime and Punishment" (「罪와 罰」)
Saying, "Like heaven's whip (챗죽), I will correct the wrongs of this world myself (스사로),"
Sometimes employing violent means, sometimes wielding eccentric theories
You who gently watched over "my season of twenty years," O pure-hearted one,
If not for you, this body, dragging its broken wings,
Would have already entered and been locked away in the Trappist Monastery (트라비스트 修道院) of Hokkaidō (北海島) or Mushanokōji's (武者小路) "Atarashiki-mura" (「新らしさ村」).
Leaving behind a sad letter saying she couldn't become a wife "because she loved too much, truly too much"
She was recuperating her wounded heart at a Seminary (神學院) by the southern seaside
When one morning, like a single haedanghwa blown about by a storm, she withered too quickly, too regrettably.
Later, like the student Raskolnikov (라스콥) confessing, this body, having vowed to follow heaven's will,
When it sought your doorstep, you were already lying in the Coastal Cemetery (海岸墓地).
O blue sea, white seagulls, was the season of youth thus desolate (虛荒튼고)? Whose sin (罪) shall we call this now? Ah—

3.
Like Sappho (삽포) of Greece (「끼리샤」), unable to control too much Talent and Passionate Fire <204>
Entrusting red blood, hot breath to the colored brush (彩筆), she sought "Hwahongmun" (「火虹門」) and "Manhugung" (「萬后宮」)
Now, having entered the world of Nirvana (寂滅), she has become a nun (尼僧) in long robes with a shaved head.
That evening, when you came with a long letter and much travel money, asking to go to Persia (「波斯」)
If only I had joined the Camel Caravan (駱駝隊商) treading the white sand dunes
And you had become a Southern Woman of the Harem (할레무) with a water jar under banana leaves
And we had lived burning red-hot even for just seven days' life under the Equator (赤道)
Wouldn't there not have been Buddhist chanting (佛門讀經) today? This shabby man
Who still wanders the marketplace, sometimes (各今) bows towards the evening bell of the mountain temple (山寺) in recognition of the impermanence of all things (諸行無常).

4.
Like Kitty (「켓티」), frail and yet bright, who cries if not laughing, sings if not loving—
When wearing a red chŏgori, like a red sparrow (紅雀), when wearing a blue skirt, like a swallow (제비)
Sometimes exceedingly gentle, like a virtuous lady (淑女) from Elementary School "Biographies of Exemplary Women" (小學, 烈女傳)
But turning back, indulging in reading Baudelaire's (「뽀-드렐」) "Cat" (「猫」) and "Owl" (「부헝새」), and tilting a wine cup
An unceasing spring spewing flames of Passion always through a small body (軆內)
An unfading bouquet holding Color and high Fragrance, always wafting near and far <205>
Decorating the streets of youth beautifully, O Red Sparrow, little Red Sparrow
Shaking the flowering trees on every branch, yet pretending not to know and flying away quickly, O Red Sparrow
Like the sixteen-day moon (十六夜月), exceedingly bright when present, but leaving not even a shadow behind after departing.
Looking back, it's already been over ten years; Tokyo (東京) of the time you were there was my "Heidelberg" (「하이델벨히」).

5.
Like Beatrice (뻬아드릿히) leaning on the railing of a Florence (「후로-렌스」) stone bridge, wearing a long, untied robe
When you walked quietly in wooden shoes (木靴) past the Haechi statues in front of Kwanghwamun (光化門) at noon
A white dove resting on your shoulder, light willow branches brushing your hair
As if about to fall, as if about to bend, fluttering in an air of Peace and Stillness (靜蜜)
This body stood watching from afar (먼-발추에), offering a few verses of poor songs
Offering up the songs of "Chŏnghanjip" (「情恨集」), "Simduch'o" (「心頭抄」), and "Dream of the First Autumn Night" (「첬 가을 밤의 꿈」)
Like a friend on the banks of the Rhine (라인江畔), I too wanted to experience the sad Heart (心情) of Werther (벨델)
Now, I intend to extinguish the fire in the Incense Burner (香爐) of Illusion and cross over to the "River of Forgetfulness" (「忘却의 江」).
-From the Lyric Poetry Collection "Susimga" (抒情詩集「愁心歌」) <206>

**Summary**

This collection of poems, titled "Youth," explores themes of intense emotion, fleeting beauty, love, longing, loss, and existential reflection associated with youth. The poet uses vivid imagery, often drawn from nature (haedanghwa, spring water, eagle, gourd flowers) and classical/literary references (Greek woman, kŏmun'go, Sappho, Dietrich, Pavlova, Sonia, Raskolnikov, Beatrice, Werther), to capture moments of passion, anxiety, sorrow, and contemplative yearning. The final section, "An Excerpt from a Certain Man's 'Ballroom Notebook'," presents vignettes of past loves, each framed by a literary or cultural comparison, reflecting on the ephemeral nature of youthful relationships and the lingering impact of memory and regret.

From a historical perspective, the poems offer glimpses into the cultural milieu of 1940s Korea under Japanese rule. The frequent references to Western and Japanese literature, film, and places (Greece, Italy, Morocco, Pushkin, Dietrich, Pavlova, Hokkaidō, Mushanokōji, Baudelaire, Heidelberg, Florence, Rhine, Werther) alongside Korean settings (Kwanghwamun) and traditional elements (kŏmun'go, haedanghwa) reflect the complex cultural landscape of the educated urban class during this period. The underlying tones of yearning, loss, frustrated passion ("scatter red blood to the sky... no one on this street to receive it"), and reflections on impermanence ("諸行無常") might subtly resonate with the broader sense of constraint or displacement felt during the colonial era, though expressed through personal, lyrical means rather than overt political commentary. The mention of being chased by "police" (포吏) in a woman's backstory provides a rare, direct, albeit small, reference to the colonial state apparatus.

사내 심정을 두 벌로 설레이는 海棠花
피기 전엔 내 안 보는데서 피어 버릴가 근심시키고,
피고 난 뒤는 또 나보는데서 저버릴가 조마조마케 하는-.

샘물인양
山谷깐 맑은 물이 저는 무심히 지내건만＜198＞
연달아 강가 실버들이 기운 어더 이러서고, 꽃도 얼골 펴며
고기는 힌 배 번득이고, 바름마저 성수나 노래하거니,
그러케 샘물인양, 그저 무심히 지내주신대도
이 거리는 금시에 환-해지고, 젊은이 우슴에 차오리며
저도 휘파람 거더치고, 깁버 산에서 내려오리다.

거문고 안은 이
밤깁히 아직 서성거림은 거문고 소리에 자자들듯 잠드러 볼가고,
새벽 일즉이 귀 기우림도 거문고 소리에 살포시 깨여 볼가고,
