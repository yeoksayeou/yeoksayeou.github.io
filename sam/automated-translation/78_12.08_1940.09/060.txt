**Body Text Translation:**
My View of 'Prime Minister Konoe'
Yi Chong-nin, Leader of Ch'ŏndogyo

A fine gentleman, free to advance or retreat.

Ch'oe Ch'ang-hak, President of Taech'ang Mining Co., Ltd.

I am confident that the Konoe Maru (近衛丸), embodying 'one body, one heart' (一體一心), will push through the tempests of the century and complete the great task of constructing the New East Asia (新東亞建設).

Chŏng Kyo-wŏn, Standing Director of the National Spiritual Mobilization Chosŏn League

It is inappropriate in any sense to offer commentary at this time on Mr. Konoe, who took office as Prime Minister only a few days ago. Therefore, I will refrain from giving 'My View' of him.

Chŏng Sŏk-t'ae, Doctor of German Medicine

1. While advocating for an emergency period (非常時), there seems to be no figure with the confidence to step forward, even temporarily, besides Prime Minister Konoe to liquidate the corrupt political parties of the past and the detrimental plutocrats (財閥) and establish fundamental national policies.
2. Since the China Incident (支那事變) occurred during his previous term as Prime Minister, it seems Prime Minister Konoe, having returned to office, is resolved to finally reap the seeds he sowed this time, and this is also what is hoped for.

Pak Sŏk-yun, Former Consul-General of Manchukuo stationed in Poland

The hope of all peoples of East Asia. <35>

Yi Kwang-su, Former Chairman of the Chosŏn Writers' Association

Seeing Prime Minister Konoe's photograph in the newspaper, my wife commented that his features looked Chosŏn-like (朝鮮的).
We also remarked that Duke Konoe is probably the most fortunate person in the political world.
While everyone knows Duke Konoe, it seems few know his view of life, or broadly speaking, his thought. This might be why he is a great statesman.
It will be 30 years until Duke Konoe turns 80, and these 30 years will be the most crucial period for our Japan (我日本). Perhaps the central figure of this period will be Duke Konoe. After fulfilling this duty, I hope he enters a leisurely retirement and lives as long as Saionji (西園寺).

Yi Chong-man, President of Taedong Mining Co., Ltd.

Establishing the new political structure (新政治體制) is the role solely entrusted to Prime Minister Konoe. Putting aside tomorrow's tasks for tomorrow, he is at least the most suitable person to take charge of today's political situation.

Yi Kŭk-no, Chosŏn Language Society (Doctor of German Philosophy)

A powerful person.

Ham Sang-hun, Editor-in-Chief of the Chosŏn Ilbo

He is not merely a simple nobleman raised in peaceful circumstances. However, neither did he fight his way to his current position through the struggles and hardships experienced by past great revolutionaries and statesmen. Therefore, while it might be fine when the military, political parties, and all other forces support him as they do now, will he be able to overcome serious difficulties without trouble when faced with them? Given his wide-ranging contacts and non-rigid thinking, might it not be beneficial for both him and the state to place him in the position of a political critic at an opportune moment? <36>

Kim Tong-jin, Councilor of the Sŏnman Colonization Company

Recently, there have been many Prime Ministers in our Japan (我日本) who were unknown quantities, but Duke Konoe is a first-rate figure belonging to the known quantities, thus commanding great trust from the people. The Duke is currently in a situation of 'smooth sailing with favorable winds' (順風掛帆), but in pushing forward the politics of the new structure (新體制), a time requiring self-reflection will surely arrive. How he overcomes this period will likely determine his political life.

Sin T'ae-ak, Lawyer

As a descendant of a distinguished Japanese family (日本의 名門後裔) whose rank has reached the pinnacle of human peerage (人爵), what personal avarice could he possibly possess? However, merely lacking greed is not the sole condition for being a statesman; I wish he had a bit more drive (迫力) regarding his work. His emergence as the final maintainer of Japan's old order (舊秩序) and a darling of the era (時代的 寵兒) is truly interesting when juxtaposed with the Empire's current situation in a transitional period (變轉期).

Yi Chŏng-jae, Executive Director of Yŏngbo Limited Partnership Company

I consider him the foremost statesman who can command the utmost trust and following from the people, who yearn for the realization of strong policies dedicated to resolving the China Incident (支那事變) and building the New East Asia (新東亞建設).

Pak Ch'ang-hun, Doctor of Medicine

He is truly a clean, ambition-free, splendid person. While those called heroes possess strengths surpassing others, they also carry many weaknesses others do not; Prime Minister Konoe is different, being a person with few weaknesses, someone who can be supported as a well-rounded, remarkable central figure.

Sakong Hwan <37>

Prime Minister Konoe (近衛首相) is a giant of character who will bring East Asia close (近衛東亞) and harmonize afar with the Western regions (遠和西域). Judging by the fact that not only the 100 million citizens but also the nations of the world praise unceasingly the re-establishment of the Konoe Cabinet, he is clearly a practitioner of the Imperial Way spirit (皇道精神), distinctly different from Chancellor Hitler's exercise of the 'Way of the Hegemon' (覇道行使).

Hong Yang-myŏng, Editor-in-Chief of the Mansŏn Ilbo

Although sitting in Shinkyō (新京), I cannot know the details of recent political affairs in Tokyo (東京), but seeing how Duke Konoe's emergence has been desired in unison by the military, officials, and civilians (軍官民) each time the cabinet changes, it is clear that Duke Konoe is the greatest 'hope' (홉) of the entire nation today. To venture an opinion now about the Duke, who stands on the testing ground as the Great Prime Minister (大宰相) bathed in the spotlight of national reorganization, would rather be impolite; ultimately, time will evaluate his qualifications as a leader.

Yi Chŏng-sŏp, Kyŏngsŏng Central Broadcasting Station

Regarding the idea of having a 'one country, one party' system (1國1黨主義) and appointing the party leader as Prime Minister, I read in the newspaper Duke Konoe's opinion that such a system resembles the past 'Daimyo' (大名) system, evoking the Shogunate (幕府), and thus is incompatible with Japan's national polity (國體). Seeing this, I trust that if he proceeds with such a spirit, the movement to establish the new structure will succeed without major errors. The ability to critically observe one's own actions is, I believe, a strength rather than a weakness.

Yi Sŏng-hwan, Executive Director of Taedong Mining Co., Ltd.

A transitional figure playing the role of laying the groundwork (地均工事) for the Shōwa Restoration (昭和維新).

Kim Tong-hwan, President of Samch'ŏlli Company

That he reportedly walked down the main street near his Kamakura (鎌倉) villa whistling popular songs, that he placed his son as a reporter for page 3 of the newspaper (新聞3面記者), that he sleeps in late in the morning and takes naps <38> every day, etc.—seeing how he tries to shed the skin of formality and standards of the court nobility (公卿家) and live as a bare human being, I think he is a good person. Whether he succeeds as a statesman depends solely on the extent to which he incorporates the intentions of the healthy national strata—the working class (勤勞階級) and returning soldiers (歸還將兵)—in organizing the current new structure (新體制). <39>

**Summary**

This 1940 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* compiles opinions from various prominent Korean figures (and a few others connected to the region) on the newly reappointed Japanese Prime Minister, Konoe Fumimaro. The views range from strong endorsements hoping Konoe can lead Japan and East Asia through the "emergency period" and establish a "New Order," to more cautious assessments questioning his ability to handle severe challenges or suggesting alternative roles. Several commentators link his return to the need to conclude the Sino-Japanese War he started in his first term. The overall tone reflects the pressures and expectations of the colonial period, with many expressing faith (or feigned faith) in Japanese leadership, while a few offer subtle critiques or focus on Konoe's personality.

For historians, this article is valuable as it showcases the diverse reactions within the Korean elite to Japanese political developments during a critical wartime period. It reveals the language of collaboration and accommodation used by some, the cautious or critical stances taken by others (often veiled), and the pervasive influence of Japanese imperial ideology (e.g., "New Order in East Asia," "Imperial Way"). Yi Kwang-su's remark about Konoe's "Korean-like" appearance and Kim Tong-hwan's focus on incorporating the working class and returning soldiers offer particularly interesting glimpses into contemporary perceptions and concerns under colonial rule. The range of professions represented (business leaders, intellectuals, journalists, officials, doctors, lawyers) provides a cross-section of elite opinion.

一. 非常時를 主唱하면서도 在來의 부패한 政黨과 百害의 재벌을 청산하여 기본국책을 확립하는데 近衛首相이 안이고서는 당분간이라도 出馬할만한 자신이 있는 인물은 없어보인다.
二. 전번 首相時 支那事變이 발생하였드니만큼 再出馬케 된 近衛首相으로서는 응당 자기가 뿌린 種子를 今般 기여코 성과를 거둘 결심이 있어 보이고, 또 바라는 바이다.

東亞全民族의 希望.＜35＞

近衛公爵은 아마 政界에 가장 팔자좋은 이러라고도 우리는 평하였다.
近衛公爵은 아모도 모르는 사람이 없지마는 그의 인생관, 汎博히 말하면 그 사상을 아는 이는 적은 모양이다. 그것은 公이 위대한 정치가인 所以일는지 모른다.
近衛公爵이 80歲가 되랴면 이제부터 30년인데, 이 30년이 我日本의 가장 중대한 시기일 것이오, 아마 이 시기의 중심인물은 近衛公일 것이다. 이 임무를 완수한 뒤에 한 閒養生活에 들어 西園寺만큼 사시기를 바란다.
