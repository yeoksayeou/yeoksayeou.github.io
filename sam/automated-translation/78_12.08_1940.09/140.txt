The Great Achievements of Dr. Wani
Duke Ichijō Sanetaka, President of the Economic Alliance

There is no need to discuss anew how truly great the contributions Dr. Wani (王仁博士) bestowed upon Japanese culture were. The achievements of the Doctor, who long ago, over a thousand years ago during the Paekche (百濟) era, already crossed the sea to introduce the Analects (論語) and the Thousand Character Classic (千字文) to Japan, thereby causing a new sprout of Japanese culture to emerge, are indeed considered great and brilliant.

When I think that even today, our ability to use Chinese characters and speak of the ways of Confucius and Mencius (孔孟), such as in "Our body, hair, and skin are received from our parents" (身體髮膚受之父母), was also given to us by Dr. Wani, I am deeply moved. Recalling the events of ancient times from this perspective, we cannot help but hold much love (愛) and respect (尊敬) for Chosŏn (朝鮮). (82)

At the same time, I believe that the people of Chosŏn should also hold a deeper understanding and intimacy (親愛) towards the Mainland (內地 - i.e., Japan).

It is more than natural that we, the descendants of today, should follow the intimate relationship (水魚의 交 - lit. relationship of water and fish) for which our ancestors (祖先) took the initiative (先鞭) long ago.

To erect a memorial statue (紀念像) for Dr. Wani now, I feel, is quite belated, but on the other hand, it is the most joyous thing that even today there are not a few people who cherish the memory of the late Doctor's historical remains (遺蹟), and that this number will increase more and more from now on (今後).

Taking this as an opportunity, the cultural exchange (文化交流) between the Mainland and Chosŏn will become even closer (緊密), and furthermore, it will greatly contribute (貢) to the construction of the New East Asia (新東亞).

While celebrating the achievements of Dr. Wani, I also hope for the further vigorous rising (奮起) of the youths (諸子) from Chosŏn. -(End)- (83)
<82-83>

**Summary**

Duke Ichijō Sanetaka, a Japanese nobleman, praises the ancient Korean scholar Wani (王仁) for introducing Confucian texts like the Analects and Thousand Character Classic to Japan, crediting him as a foundational figure for Japanese culture and literacy. He uses this shared history to argue that both Japanese and Koreans should feel mutual affection and respect, urging Koreans specifically to develop deeper understanding and intimacy towards Japan (Naeji). He expresses joy at the plan to erect a statue for Wani, hoping it will further strengthen cultural ties and contribute to the construction of a "New East Asia." From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it exemplifies Japanese colonial rhetoric using shared cultural heritage (Wani) to promote contemporary political aims: fostering closer ties (under Japanese hegemony) and mobilizing support for the "New East Asia," a concept linked to Japan's imperial ambitions (often associated with the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere). The paternalistic tone and the call for Koreans to feel "intimacy" towards the colonizer ("Naeji") while referencing ancient history are particularly revealing of the period's propaganda efforts.

王仁博士의 偉績
						經濟聯盟總裁 公爵 一條實孝
王仁博士가 日本문화에 끼친 바 공적이 얼마나 위대하였든가는 다시금 새삼스럽게 논의할 것이 없소이다. 박사는 그 녯날 1천여년전 百濟時代에 벌서 멀리 바다를 건너「論語」와「千字文」을 日本에 소개하여 日本문화에 새로운 싹을 낳게한 공적은 실로 크며 또한 빛난 일로 생각합니다.
오늘날 우리가 漢字를 사용하여「身體髮膚受之父母」와 如히 孔孟의 道들 말하게 된 것도 王仁博士의 주신 것이라 생각할 때에 감개깊습니다. 이런 점으로 上古時代의 일을 회상할 때마다 우리들은 朝鮮에 많은 愛와 尊敬을 가지지 않어서는 (82) 안될 것입니다.
그와 동시에 朝鮮의 여러분도 또한 內地에 대하여 좀 더 깊은 이해와 親愛를 가질 바이라고 생각합니다.
그 옛날- 우리들의 祖先이 先鞭을 지은 水魚의 交를 현대의 우리 자손들이 답습하는 것은 당연 이상의 당연한 일로 생각합니다.
지금에 와서 王仁博士의 紀念像를 세운다고 하는 것은 생각건대 퍽으나 뒤늦인 感이 많읍니다만은 그 반면에 今日 아직도 故박사의 遺蹟을 思慕하는 사람이 적지 않고, 그것이 今後 더욱더욱 증가하리라함이 무엇보다 기뿐 일이외다.
이것을 계기로 하여 內地와 朝鮮과의 문화적 교류가 일층 긴밀케 되고 나아가서 新東亞를 건설하는 우에도 크게 貢이 될 것이외다.
王仁博士의 공적을 讚賀함과 동시에 朝鮮출신의 청년諸子에 일층의 奮起를 바라는 바이외다. -(끝)- (83)
