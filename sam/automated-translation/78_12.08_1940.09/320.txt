Magazine Title: 삼천리 제12권 제8호
Publication Date: 1940년 09월 01일
Article Title: 세레나데, 림피안타 (Serenade, Limpianta)
Author: 주요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I
Sitting in a Seoul café,
Listening to a song of Spain,
I suddenly recall
From the economic delegation
That visited last year,
The dark-eyed woman of Peru.

II <190>

Though I don’t know the meaning, the familiar Spanish serenade
Has a beautiful harmony of vowels and consonants.
Like a Korean melody, it stirs the heart.

III
Are the streets where the maidens of Madrid, Spain live
Like the tune of Serenade, Limpianta?
Winding sorrowfully yet affectionately, knowing no end. <191>

**Summary**

This poem by Chu Yohan reflects on hearing a Spanish serenade in a Seoul café. The music evokes a memory of a Peruvian woman encountered during an economic delegation's visit the previous year. The speaker finds the Spanish melody, though its meaning is unknown, emotionally resonant and similar in its heart-stirring quality to Korean tunes, imagining the winding streets of Madrid mirroring the song's character. From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting as it showcases the presence of international culture (Spanish music, a Peruvian delegation) within colonial Seoul in 1940, suggesting a degree of cosmopolitan interaction and cultural consumption despite the political situation. It highlights personal reflection amidst broader geopolitical contexts.

세레나데, 림피안타
						주요한
一
서울의 다방에 안자
에스반야의 노래를 드르니
지난해 들렷던
경제사절 일행 중의
페루의 눈 깜안 여인이
문득 생각 나노라

二＜190＞

뜻 모르되 귀에 익은 에스반야의 세레나데는
모음과 자음의 어울림이 곱기도 하다
조선말 선율과도 가태 가슴 뭉클하여라

三
에스반야의 마드리드의 처녀들 사는 거리거리는
세레나데, 림피안타의 곡조가튼 거리거릴까
섧고도 다정스리 굽으러저 끝간 데를 모르리＜191＞
