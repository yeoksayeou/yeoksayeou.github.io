Article Title: 當面利益의 獲得運動과 그 批判 (The Movement for Achieving Immediate Interests and its Critique)

The Movement for Achieving Immediate Interests and its Critique
The Immediate Task Regarding the National Cooperative [Front/Line - censored]
Yi Hangbal

Foreword
Before entering the main discussion, the author wishes for everyone to understand that this short essay is being written under circumstances where it is impossible to clearly express the fundamental problems of the national movement (a situation faced by the entire Korean press).
I intend only to investigate two or three characteristics of the historical process of the national movement and, from this, define some of the organizational tasks for our immediate actions. Regarding the problem of the cooperative [front/line - censored], despite not having many documents, most of the published discussions have ended only with exploring fundamental theoretical issues, so one cannot help but always feel the lack of concrete methodology. Therefore, the author intends to strive to discover the points of conclusion for general and concrete problems as much as possible, rather than debating fundamental theoretical issues.

I. Two or Three Characteristics of the Historical Process
What is a nation (民族)?
A nation is not merely an aggregation of a race or a single type, but a historically accumulated<7> enduring commonality of humans unified by commonalities such as language, territory, economic life, and culture, as well as traditional psychology manifested through these. (For details, refer to Stalin's "The National Question")
If this definition is correct, then a nation is something formed at a certain stage according to the laws of change in the historical process, and it can also be defined as something that inevitably dissolves after passing through a certain historical process. Didn't a phase of past history make the formation of the American nation possible, while practically extinguishing the Jewish nation?
Therefore, the developmental process of capitalism is simultaneously the process of national *birth and the process of national dissolution. However, the formation and extinction of nations like this do not proceed smoothly like flowers blooming in the spring breeze but are formed or extinguished only through antagonistic [struggle - censored] relations between conquerors and the conquered, ruling classes and ruled classes. Seen from this perspective, the statement "All human history is the history of class [struggle - censored]" cannot but be true.
The national movement is an inevitable historical product originating from this truth. Then, what indeed are the characteristics of the Korean movement arising from this historical backdrop?
First, the current stage is a process of bourgeois democracy [revolution - censored] and, at the same time, because the [agent of change - censored] is not the indigenous bourgeoisie but foreign capitalist [imperialism - censored], it is a pan-national movement.
Second, in Korea, the power of the indigenous bourgeoisie is extremely weak, making the broad peasant stratum the decisive factor in the entire movement. (However, one must not forget that the [strategic - censored] subjective force is always the proletariat.)
Third, the intensification of the instability of "Third Period" capitalism sharpens the [struggle's - censored] upsurge of the worldwide working class and colonial peoples, and the various influences reaching Korea have come to have a direct correlative relationship with this.
Fourth, the various experiences of the colonial movements in [Soviet Union - abbreviated/censored] and China, India, etc., provide no small lesson for the [revolution - censored].
Fifth, due to the class-based [strengthening/maturing - abbreviated/censored] of the Japanese proletariat, direct cooperation with the Korean proletariat is possible.
Sixth, the nationalist political forces in Korea are currently weak, and the material foundation of the indigenous bourgeoisie and absolute autocratic forces to foster them is too immature.
Seventh, the [revolutionary - censored] potential of the Korean populace abroad can become a [force - censored] to be organized into future [units - censored], [reserves - censored], etc.

II. The Cooperative [Front/Line - censored] Process
Lenin said that all colonial national [liberation - censored] movements are [incomplete/insufficient - abbreviated/censored]. Then, what was the [strategic - censored] basic theory of the 1905 Russian movement process? The outline of the [strategic - censored] definition at that time was as follows:
1. Subjective force... Proletariat
1. Object of struggle... Tsarist [Autocracy - censored] and feudal remnant forces.
1. Primary allied force... Broad peasantry (not just the poor peasants).
1. Main direction of forces... Neutralization of the newly emerging bourgeoisie forces.
1. Disposition of forces... Formation of the worker-peasant alliance.
(Refer to Stalin's "Foundations of Leninism" - The author writes from memory, so there may be some parts that differ slightly from the original text, but I am confident it is generally correct.)
If we link these [strategic - censored] theories with the characteristics that cannot but exist (refer to "Two or Three Characteristics of the Historical Process"), analyze and critique them scientifically, and define them, this will become a [revolutionary - censored] strategy and tactics.
Then, will such fundamental [tactics - censored] necessitate the temporal continuation of a cooperative [front - censored] that allies class with class in the [revolution - censored]?
As long as it is true that the object of the [revolution - censored] is [imperialist - censored] [rule - censored] and feudal remnant forces, the temporal cooperation of anti-[imperialist - censored] elements and anti-feudal elements becomes inevitably possible in order to [overthrow - censored] it. Herein lies the theoretical basis necessitating the national cooperative [front/line - censored]. However, the limitation that the scope of this class alliance should be formed only through the organizational force called the Shinganhoe (新幹會) would be unacceptable; in the past, attempting to [conduct - censored] all cooperative [struggles - censored] necessarily through the Shinganhoe was a decisive error regarding cooperative [struggles - censored]. The recent commencement of slightly undervaluing the activities of the Shinganhoe, etc., might be due to an awakening regarding this.
Then, the final remaining problem would be the issue of cooperative organization. Regarding this problem, rather than creating an original definition, the author would like to introduce to the readers Stalin's<3> various writings that sharply analyzed and critiqued the Chinese national [revolutionary - censored] movement from a Leninist perspective. I urge you to refer to Stalin's "Leninism and the National Question" and "On the Chinese Revolution," etc.

III. How Should the Cooperative Organization Be?
In the process of the national movement, cooperative organization is not necessarily sustained or formed only by a relative balance between classes. If one subjectively recognizes this as correct, then attempts at cooperative organization will inevitably, as in the past, lead to the connection being maintained through diplomacy and personal sentiment.
Therefore, cooperative organization, rather, can only be formed, [seized - censored], and led by one class – the proletariat – through its own class's [revolutionary - censored] activities, and by expanding and strengthening its own class formation. Such organizational activity can enable the monopolization of [hegemony - censored] by one class, and this method of [struggle - censored] will be able to guarantee the advancement of the [revolution - censored] by one step.
The possibility of developing the national cooperative [front/line - censored] can only be determined, firstly, by clarifying that the Korean movement is an [anti-imperialist - censored], anti-feudal national [revolutionary - censored] movement; secondly, by establishing the [hegemony - censored] of the proletariat over the peasant stratum; and thirdly, by absorbing the experiences of the [Russian Revolution - abbreviated/censored] and various colonial [revolutionary - censored] movements.

Conclusion
In Korea, over 80% of the entire population is occupied by peasants. This is the historical fact that makes the peasantry the decisive element of the Korean movement. The Korean movement will only be possible by correctly resolving the land problem. Without attempting to resolve the land problem, breaking through the current [strategic - censored] process is unthinkable, even in dreams. Without raising issues that satisfy the demands of the peasants, the [mobilization - censored] of the peasants is impossible.
Firstly, the reconstruction of peasant unions (農民組合). Secondly, from the formation of peasant associations (農民協會, as organizations for the middle peasant stratum) and peasant committees (農民委員會, as joint bodies of tenant and owner-cultivator farmers), etc., to the organization of worker-peasant councils (農勞協議會), etc., the promotion of peasant activity under the leadership of the proletariat must be attempted. Such organizational activity is the primary activity of the worker-peasant alliance, and at the same time, the expansion and strengthening of this activity is the organizational activity that signifies progress towards a cooperative [front/line - censored]. Will not all these [tasks - censored] become the immediate tasks for the national cooperative [front/line - censored]? The end.
-(March 13, 1931)-

Interpretation of the Title "Immediate Interests"
Kim Seyong

I. "Immediate interests" are partial interests relative to the overall interests, the minimum program relative to the maximum program, and simultaneously, a processional [struggle - censored] goal containing daily, realistic, concrete conditions as its content.
In this sense, immediate interests possess the constructive content and significance<4> of the entire process of obtaining and achieving the highest interests and maximum program of a class or a nation, and become the basis for defining the [strategy - censored] or [tactics - censored] of a given period.
However, this is an abstract, broad interpretation of immediate interests. For a proposition, especially one like "immediate interests" which has a realistic character and has traditionally carried various interpretations, a concrete, inductive interpretation of "what are immediate interests?" is necessary, rather than just "what is the meaning of immediate interests?"
Extracting immediate interests from the actual living conditions and demands of the masses requires a strict principled standard, and the issues of how to act for this purpose – the method of [struggle - censored] – and how to link immediate interests with overall interests are even more crucial.
Originally, since immediate interests are the daily interests of the masses demanded within the existing system and social conditions, and their scope is limited by the temporal restriction of "immediate," they tend to be reformist and opportunistic. And they necessarily presuppose the expectation of possibility. Alternatively, all the various phenomena expressing the discontent and deficiencies of life that appear at a certain time, the discontent and deficiencies themselves, become objects for the sake of immediate interests, and therefore do not necessarily possess legality and reformism, and some things can be included in the [struggle - censored] "slogan" even while anticipating impossibility.
II. In extracting, organizing, and expressing the above realistic daily demands, regardless of whether they are legal or illegal, one must have certain principles. Recently, we frequently see examples of promoting unprincipled "immediate interests first-ism" under the pretext of the masses' daily struggles, calling it "immediate interests."
Especially, reformist right-wing nationalists and conceptual left-wing nationalists inherently differ in their principles, systems, policies, and [tactics - censored], thus their "immediate interests" would differ, and the limits of those interests would naturally be different. However, within the socialist – class movement – there appears a strong tendency towards legalistic reformism due to errors in analyzing the immediate situation.
"If legalism is pursuing what is possible under certain circumstances,"
"Is [revolutionary-ism - censored] pursuing what is impossible under certain circumstances?" - This is the doubt held by the immediate-interests-first-ists or reformist legalists, and their attack on scientific [revolutionary - censored] socialists.
Of course, there are cases where the immediate interests of democrats or reformists coincide with those of [revolutionary - censored] socialists. However, the maximum demand of the former is the minimum demand of the latter. Even if there are common immediate interests for the entire people of a subjugated nation in the anti-[imperialist - censored] struggle, differences in class and recognition based on principles will lead to differences in what is grasped and emphasized among the various immediate interests. That is, the immediate interests demanded by workers, peasants, and other petit-bourgeoisie will differ from those held by landlords and bourgeoisie. Common immediate interests detached from class interests, living conditions, and class principles are merely abstract goals.
Even if there are points where immediate interests coincide, differences in fundamental principles and programs will lead to fundamentally different methods in the means of [struggle - censored]. Depending on these methods of [struggle - censored], they are divided into [revolutionary - censored], reactionary, reformist, etc. Such examples can be seen plenty, not only in foreign countries but also in Korea. The means of [struggle - censored] determines the movement and its effect, gives purpose-consciousness to the organization and fighting spirit of the masses, and further undertakes the task of connecting and transforming immediate interests into overall interests – the highest goal. For example, there are several tendencies: from petitioning (*願), [appeal - censored] movements to round table conferences; from demanding legal compromise permits to imperial federation or territorial division; avoiding political [revolutionary - censored] movements and aiming for economic strength enhancement of the entire nation as one, leading to industrial independence. The political, class, and categorical scope (*圍) of these are beyond question, but their attitudes towards immediate interests and methods of acquisition will be in opposition to others.
III. If "immediate interests" can be interpreted as "immediate [struggle - censored] goals," then in a place like Korea, everything that affects daily life and all demands that realistically cannot but be made without justification would become immediate [struggle - censored] goals for immediate interests. Among these, some are immediate interests but possess continuity, some are urgent matters of the moment with temporal absoluteness, and there might also be specific local immediate interests, unique immediate interests of each class mass, common immediate interests across classes, or instances where one leading class represents the immediate interests of another class in a [strategic - censored] sense. Besides these, there is also the issue of immediate interests within the [revolutionary - censored] movement itself, which becomes an immediate interest – an immediate [struggle - censored] goal – by exposing the true nature of non-[revolutionary - censored] reformism and attacking reactionary groups cooperating with [imperialism - censored]. In short,<5> immediate interests are concrete problems possessing numerous constraints.
In essence, immediate interests can be said to be all the rights and conditions that a class or the popular masses demand from the immediate realistic living conditions, without which daily survival activities cannot be carried out, and without obtaining which effective struggle cannot be waged.
We have countless immediate interests that must be possessed in politics, economy, society, and culture. To enumerate these and present the methods of [struggle - censored] for them is the request of Samch'ŏlli magazine, but due to space constraints and other reasons, it is impossible. Therefore, I will postpone it for a later installment and limit myself here to writing a part of my view on the interpretation of the title "Immediate Interests."
In fact, if "immediate interests" is synonymous with "immediate struggle 'slogan'," then listing items has no meaning whatsoever. This should not be reflected in writing but must become the living cry – the concrete, practical action – of the people's lives.

Immediate Tasks Regarding Partial Demands
Yun Hyŏngsik

I. The problem concerning the unified [front - censored] tactics of the proletariat class becomes the problem of determining concrete slogans for partial demands. The determination of slogans for these partial demands, unlike the program concerning the strategic basic [revolutionary - censored] goals of a class, must be adapted to the mood of the masses regarding specific concrete situations at a specific moment. Therefore, the slogan presenting partial demand conditions for the acquisition of immediate interests must be composed of demand conditions related to the masses' immediate lives, within a scope they can understand, and indicate the direction of mass popularization.
However, one should not hesitate, being bound only by the current political circumstances, wondering whether realization is possible or not. If one raises all partial demand conditions within the scope allowed by the current circumstances, this would be a demand of reality-following-ism (現實追隨主義的 要求), merely an opportunistic demand. Therefore, the slogan for partial demands must always be presented to the extent that it does not abandon the defense of fundamental demands. Partial demands must be able to educate the [revolutionary - censored] spirit that can induce the full-scale advance of the masses, and must become technical slogans capable of exposing the deceptive policies of the [opposing - censored] class.
II. Then, deciding what the slogan for immediate partial [demands - censored] should be in the process of the national cooperative [front/line - censored] is one of the important themes pending in our historical process, a problem that must necessarily exist for us who do not [negate - censored] the cooperative [front/line - censored]. Although this problem needs to be resolved most urgently, a correct definition for it has not yet been found. The fact that the Shinganhoe (新幹會), which boasted as if it represented the greatest interests of the entire nation and possessed the highest authority, and firmly promised to concentrate the total strength of the entire nation, has not yet presented an action program, ultimately led to the complete loss<6> of the masses' trust in the Shinganhoe, bringing the advanced worker-peasant masses' heightened [revolutionary - censored] power to the point of trying to shatter the reactionary wall called Shinganhoe. Of course, this alone is not the entire reason for dissolving the Shinganhoe, but the fact that the Shinganhoe lacked an action program – a slogan for concrete partial demands – proves that the Shinganhoe was like Buddha, a static object.
However, even if the Shinganhoe is dissolved, the cooperative [front/line - censored] will not disappear for some time. Therefore, the question of what are the common demands concerning the immediate interests of the entire [nation - abbreviated] and what slogan expresses them still remains. If partial demands are recognized by the masses as if they were the final goal of a class movement, this too will inevitably risk falling into trade-unionism, so the need for a clear definition regarding this must never be forgotten.
III. Fundamentally common demands for the entire nation cannot exist in principle. The common interests of the entire nation are merely demand conditions that become temporally common, presented adapted to the mood of the masses for the sake of [tactical - censored] cooperation regarding economic or political interests, only to the extent that the unique class activities expand at a specific moment. The peasant stratum forms an alliance under the leadership of the proletariat. The cooperation of left-wing nationalist groups under the consultative organization of workers and peasants is not achieved through the deceptive diplomacy of the proletariat. The former can only be established when it is recognized that achieving the [ultimate - censored] goal of the proletariat fully accomplishes the demands of the peasants; the latter can only be established to the extent that the demands of the worker-peasant masses are temporally common with those of the left-wing nationalists, etc. Attempting to mechanically link these will ultimately not enable the activity of the masses with the masses but will end merely in a conspiracy of leaders. Also, no matter how splendid slogans, dressed up with clever phrases devised through study at a desk or from books, are scattered before the masses, as long as they have no correlative relationship whatsoever with the actual lives of the masses, it is impossible to mobilize the masses under these slogans.
Therefore, slogans for partial demands must necessarily be concrete [revolutionary - censored], [struggle-oriented - censored] conditions that are expressions of the people's lives, and at the same time, must be partial expressions of fundamental problems.
IV. Then, what are the immediate slogans for partial demands to be presented before the people at the beginning of 1931? This must be extracted from the expressions of various situations where the lives of the entire populace are being mercilessly [crushed/exploited - censored] by the economic crisis of the Third Period, while on the other hand, the people's lives are being [impoverished - censored]. The temporal demand conditions of the urban petit bourgeoisie feeling the insecurity of life, the army of unemployed wandering to sell their labor power, peasants who have left the land, workers inevitably subjected to the forced increase of labor power due to industrial rationalization, etc., become the national partial demand conditions. Briefly listing a part of these below:
1. Give work to the workers!
1. Guarantee the livelihood of the unemployed!
1. Establishment of minimum wage & payment of unemployment benefits!
1. Guarantee land for the working peasants!
1. Freedom of speech, press, assembly, and association for all people, etc.
(Of course, slogans for partial demands will be countless. Therefore, I will not attempt to write them all.)
<2-7>

**Summary**

This collection of three short essays from the April 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine delves into the concept of "immediate interests" (當面利益) within the context of the Korean national movement under Japanese rule. Yi Hangbal analyzes the historical formation of nations and class struggle, outlining the specific characteristics of the Korean situation (weak bourgeoisie, strong peasantry, influence of global events and other colonial movements). He argues for a national cooperative front led by the proletariat, allied with the peasantry, while critiquing past reliance on organizations like the Shinganhoe. Kim Seyong defines "immediate interests" as practical, daily demands that form part of the larger struggle, cautioning against unprincipled reformism and emphasizing the need for class-based analysis even when interests seem common. Yun Hyŏngsik discusses the tactical importance of formulating concrete slogans based on partial demands derived from the masses' immediate suffering (unemployment, land issues, lack of freedoms), arguing these must educate and mobilize the masses towards fundamental goals without falling into mere reformism or leader-driven conspiracies, again criticizing the Shinganhoe's lack of practical action. The pieces collectively reflect the intense theoretical and strategic debates among Korean socialists/nationalists influenced by Marxism-Leninism (specifically citing Lenin and Stalin), focusing on class leadership, the worker-peasant alliance, the land question, and the tactical use of immediate demands to advance revolutionary goals in a colonial setting.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into the ideological landscape of the Korean left in the early 1930s. The explicit references to Lenin, Stalin, the "Third Period" of capitalism (a Comintern concept), and the Chinese revolution demonstrate the strong influence of international communism. The internal critique of the Shinganhoe reveals the fractures and strategic disagreements within the broader nationalist movement. Furthermore, the emphasis on the peasantry as the "decisive element" and the centrality of the land question highlight key strategic considerations for revolution in agrarian colonial Korea. The censored words also indirectly point to the sensitive political nature of discussing imperialism, class struggle, revolution, and specific socialist strategies under Japanese surveillance.

當面利益의 獲得運動과 그 批判
民族協同X線에 對한 當面任務
李恒發

前言
本論에 들어가기 전에 筆者은 먼저 이 小論이 民族運動의 根本的 諸問題를 明確히 表示할수업는 環境(이는 朝鮮言論界에서 全體的으로 當하고 잇는 事情이지만)에 當하야 執筆하게 되는 것을 諸君이 諒解하여 주기를 바라는 바이다.
다만 民族運動의 歷史的 過程에 잇서서의 2,3의 特質를 究明하고 이에서 當面의 우리의 行動에 대한 組織的 任務의 若干을 規定하는데에 끗치고저 한다. 協同的 XX에 대한 問題에 잇서서는 그리 만흔 文獻를 갓지 못하얏슴에 不拘하고 임의 發表된 諸論의 大槪는 그 內容이 原則的 諸問題를 探究하는데만 끗치고 말엇슴으로 具體的 方法論에 대한 缺乏를 언제나 늣기지 안을수가 업섯다. 그럼으로 筆者는 原則諸問題에 대한 論議보다도 可及的 一般的 具體的 諸問題의 歸結點을 發見하기에 努力하고저 한다.

一. 歷史的 過程의 2,3特質
民族이란 무엇인가.
民族이란 것은 單純한 人種 또는 一個의 種類만의 結集體가 아니라 言語, 領土, 經濟生活과 밋 文化의 共通性으로써 顯現되여잇는 傳統的 心理등의 共通性에 의하야 統一된 人間의 歷史的 으로 積築된＜7＞ 永續性잇는 共通性이다. (詳言은 스탈닌의 民族問題參照)
萬一에 이 規定이 틀임업다면 民族이란 것은 歷史過程의 變化法則에 의하야 一定의 階段에 成生되는 것이며 또한 一定한 歷史過程을 지나서는 必然的 으로 解消되는 것이라고 規定할수 잇는 것이다. 過去歷史의 一端은 아메리카民族의 形成을 可能케 하엿스며 猶太의 民族을 事實上 消滅식히지 아니하엿는가?
그럼으로 資本主義의 發達過程은 民族의 *生過程인 同時에 民族의 解消過程도 되는 것이다. 그러나 이와갓치 民族의 生成과 消滅은 결코 春風에 發花와 갓치 順調롭게 되는 것이 아니라 征伐者와 被征伐者 支配階級과 被支配階級間의 對立XX關係에서만 成生 혹은 消滅되는 것이다. 이로써 본다면 「人間의 모든 歷史는 階級XX의 歷史이다」라고 한 말이 眞理가 안일수 업는 것이다.
民族運動이란 이러한 眞理에 起因한 必然的 歷史的 産物이다. 그러면 이러한 歷史的 前景에서 提起되는 朝鮮運動의 特質은 果然 무엇일가?

一. 當面利益이란 全體利益에 대한 部分利益이며 最大綱領에 대한 最少綱領인 同時 日常的 現實的, 具體條件을 內容으로 한 過程的 X爭目標이다.
