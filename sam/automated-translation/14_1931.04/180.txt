Impressions of ML Faction Figures
						An Pyŏngju (安炳珠)

The figures of the ML faction, who for a time thoroughly startled the eyes and ears of the world—they are still alive in this world. They are certainly alive on the earth like us. However, they cannot see the light of day as we do. Though their bodies are thus restricted, their spirits are said to constantly soar through space.
Even today, when news of them is still uncertain, looking back once more at their private lives and impressions from bygone days would also be a 100 percent interesting affair.

Ch'oe Ikhan (崔益翰)
He was from Ulchin (蔚珍), Kangwŏn Province (江原道), truly a talent (才X) that Kangwŏn Province could call an X-product. As a renowned X for his generation in Chinese classics (漢學), he was one of the figures who caused a stir among the Confucian scholars (儒林客) in ceremonial robes (道袍) by publishing, along with a certain Kwŏn (權某), a scathing essay titled "A Blow to the Head of the Imposter Ming Man" (假明人頭上加一棒) in the Tong-A Ilbo (東亞日報). When I also first met him in Tōkyō (東京), I saw him as nothing more than a polite "teacher" (샘님). But he later easily changed direction, quit Waseda University (早稻田大學), and specialized in Marxist studies (맑쓰學說), resulting in few who could match him in debates and writing. He could indeed be called a person reborn many times: first as a disciple of Confucius (孔子), then expelled from there to become a disciple of Ōyama (Ikuo) (大山郁夫) of Waseda, and then again leaping (?) to become a disciple of Fukumoto (Kazuo) (福本和夫)! It was apostasy, truly 100 percent apostasy. That's how sharp a brilliant talent he was in grasping the aspects of the times. He tried infinitely hard to shed the ingrained nature of a mellow Chinese classics scholar. However, there was just one aspect of the Chinese scholar's nature he could not shed: when walking, his true colors as a teacher remained. So, when we friends deliberately put Ch'oe at the front and walked together, teasing him, he could instantly correct his walking style, but later it would revert. <55> As he also had an affable personality, he enjoyed innocent jokes. No matter what kind of joke was made, he would respond to a joke with a joke. Therefore, once, in response to an essay Ch'oe wrote, I wrote an essay titled "(How Do Empiricist Armchair Factions Reject the Student Movement?)" (經驗主義的 書齋派는 學生運動을 엇더케 拒否하는가) in (Chosŏn chi Kwang) (朝鮮之光), and used the word (tamaba) (타마바), meaning Chinese language, which earned his serious anger.

Pak Ch'ŏn (朴泉)
(Also known as Pak Chagap, 朴自甲) He was from Onsŏng (穩城), Hamgyŏngbuk Province (咸北), and a most passionate (熱X的) man. Once he decided on something, he possessed a strong will to plunge right in without regard for fire or water. When he was active near the Russian (露西亞) border, during the time of the frantic factional strife that reached the point of aiming guns at each other, known as the so-called (Irkutsk Faction) (일크스크派) versus the (Shanghai Faction) (上海派), the fact that a scar remains where a bullet from an adversary pierced his thigh (大腿) allows one to guess his courage (膽力). Also, considering the fact that when the clash between the Chosŏn Labor Federation (朝鮮勞總) and the Sangaehoe (相愛會) occurred in Tōkyō, he suffered a head injury from a short sword (短刀) requiring two weeks of treatment, one can see he was someone unafraid of swords or spears (劍山刀水). He was truly a man of knowledge and action, a practitioner. There was a maxim he always powerfully advocated: (Text omitted in original) must exist. How powerful these words truly are. Therefore, even after fasting for 3 or 4 days, not only did he not show signs of hunger to others, but he also carried out every task assigned to him without fail. However, he was not merely a brute (匹夫) who advocated only (strength) (力). He also possessed the charm of being skilled in diplomacy (外交); within a few days of first meeting a friend, he would invariably form a brotherly friendship. Not only was he skilled in socializing with both old and new acquaintances, but he was also proficient in international diplomacy, handling negotiations with Japanese organizations (日本團體) and others himself. Thus, I too jokingly called him (Pak Ch'ŏn, the Japan-Korea liaison) (朴泉은 日鮮係員) at the time.

Han Rim (韓林)
He was the son of a renowned wealthy family (富豪) in Hamhŭng (咸興) and a respected bachelor's degree holder who finished the main course at Waseda University. Yet, just as Xenin (X닌이) [likely Lenin], though the son of nobility, cast off the glorious crown of a lawyer and became a friend to the workers, this scholar Han Rim (翰林學士) also trampled the scholar's cap and became a leader of the working class (勞働者 階級), an intellectual (인테리켄챠). Though his body was that of a petite bourgeoisie (小뿔) intellectual, his personality was open-minded, possessing the demeanor of a worker. While the ML Party (ML黨), true to its name, consisted entirely of orthodox disciples of Marx and Lenin (맑쓰 레닌), Han Rim possessed outstanding knowledge among them. It was said that among our comrades at the time, Han Rim was the only one who had read Das Kapital (資本論) entirely in the original text.

Song Ch'angnyŏm (宋昌濂)
Though said to be from Kangso (江西), P'yŏngannam Province (平南), he is described as a solitary figure with no family or home (無依無家). When he first appeared in Tōkyō <56>, he was a worker who didn't know much about the world's affairs. But within just one year, after passing through the labor union (勞働組合), when he emerged on the class front (게급線), he was a theorist (理論家) rarely seen in the world. Not only was he skilled in debate (舌辨), but he was also a person who maintained the most audacious and calm attitude amidst overflowing crowds. Consequently, it was no coincidence that he held the nickname (Chairman Song) (宋議長), as he alone used to take charge as the temporary chairman (臨時議長) at various organizational meetings held in Tōkyō at the time.

Yi Ujŏk (李友狄)
He was from Chinju (晋州), Kyŏngsangnam Province (慶南). As a youth who had studied only up to the 4th grade of primary school (小學校), once he went to Tōkyō, he became the youngest among the Marxist disciples (맑쓰學徒). Wielding a sharp pen (銳利한 筆鋒), he not only struck down all bourgeois scholars (뿔죠아學者流) but also became a central figure who couldn't be absent from the social movement front (社會運動線上) for even a moment, developing (theoretical struggles) (理論鬪爭) against opposing factions as the foremost vanguard of the Fukumoto sect (福本宗) at the time. His narrow forehead (面顙) didn't show that type, but his sharp (X哳한) mind and nimble planning (機敏한 作X計劃) showed the makings of a politician (政治家). Although he initially garnered considerable popularity in the political activity sphere (政治的 活動領域), it wasn't long before—though not quite a downfall—he ceased to be a significant figure, making one realize how accelerated the tempo of the times is.

Kang Ch'ŏl (姜徹)
(Also known as Kang Pyŏngch'ang, 姜炳昌) Born into a stubborn (頑固) family in Chinju, Kyŏngsangnam Province, he attended Japan University (日本大學) but dropped out midway. Though his name was Kang Ch'ŏl (Strong X) (剛X), his nature (性品) was gentle and steadfast like an ox (소)牛, possessing the qualities suited for planning (도X할) work. He was also tight-lipped (입이 무거워서) and spoke little, but the occasional words he did utter were X-filled. With a lumberjack-like (지려낀 목수패) appearance and a face like a pumpkin gourd (풍부처갓혼), his first impression seemed quite blunt, but upon getting to know him, one could find a savory human warmth (人情味) somewhere. Since he also had training in Chinese classics (漢學), he easily took up the brush for political essays (政治論文), making many contributions (功績) to the world of writing (著述界).

Pak Kyun (朴均)
(Also known as Pak Tŭkhyŏn, 朴得鉉) Born in Kwaksan (郭山), P'yŏnganbuk Province (平北), he and his younger brother Pak Tŭngnyong (朴得龍) were renowned (X聲이 놉하섯다) as socialist brothers. Recently, when I was greeting a certain university student from Chŏngju (定州), I asked, "(Since you are in Chŏngju, do you know Pak Kyun?)" He replied that although he didn't know him personally, there was no one in Chŏngju who didn't know Pak Kyun's name, showing a clear sign of expressing the utmost X-respect (X意). This makes it easy to guess how much of a X-star (X星) Pak Kyun was for Chŏngju. Born as the child of poor tenant farmers (貧寒한 小作人), he experienced all sorts of mistreatment (X待) from bourgeois society (뿔죠아社會), emerging as a truly strong person tempered by a hundred X-trials (百X磨練). Once, when he came as a special dispatch to the Kwansŏ Social Activists Conference (關西社會運動者大會) held in Anju (安州), commissioned (囑託) by the Tōkyō-based Chosŏn Kwansŏ Social Activists Friendship Association (在東京朝鮮關西社會運動者親睦會), Pak Kyun, being 100 percent popular, was approached by XX [likely police/authorities] attempting to manipulate (弄絡) him. They secretly sent a sum of money (金一封) to Pak, but he became greatly angered (大怒) and rejected it. <57>

In Chŏngsik (印貞植)
From Yonggang (龍岡), P'yŏngannam Province, born into a quite well-off (裕足한) family, he was a formidable person who kicked aside the school gate just three months before graduation from Hōsei University (法政大學) in Tōkyō. That he attended a bourgeois university (뿔죠아大學) for three years was not because he attached any hope to it, but merely to receive school expenses (學資) from his parents and elder brother (父兄)—this is proven by In's own confession. His rough face, curly hair, and receding hairline earned him the nickname "Xenin" (X닌) [likely Lenin]. His hearty laugh (껄껄 웃는 넙털우슴) and understanding, gentle heart ensured that comrades who became close to him would never leave.

Kim Kyerim (金桂林)

He was from Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道), a young man with handsome features (眉目이 秀麗한). He possessed a beauty surpassing women, a rare beauty among men (男中絶色) capable of making women's hearts ache. Nevertheless, perhaps he lacked luck with women (女福), as once he asked for an introduction to a woman. I introduced him to a female friend of mine, but as soon as the bud of love sprouted, the mirror broke (破鏡) [relationship ended], causing him considerable heartache—this too is one of his (episodes) (에비소트). Being from Hamgyŏng Province, he had the nature of a mud-fighting dog (泥田鬪狗), possessing the spirit (氣) to not let go of something once engaged until a result was achieved.

To Chŏngho (都正浩)
A handsome man (美男子) with clear features (眉目淸秀) born in Kyŏngsŏng (京城) [Seoul], his special trait was his pleasant diplomatic nature (外交性). I first met him when he was a machine worker (機械職工) at the Tongsongsa Printing House (同聲社印刷所) in Tōkyō, and I last bid farewell to him at his private residence (私宅) in Kyŏngsŏng. He has already become a departed guest of the underworld (地下의 不歸客) [i.e., he has died]. (Following individuals to be continued in the next issue) <58>

**Summary**

This article by An Pyŏngju is a personal memoir offering impressions of various figures associated with the "ML Faction" (Marxist-Leninist) active in the Korean socialist movement during the Japanese colonial period, likely around the late 1920s and early 1930s. The author recounts anecdotes about their origins, personalities, intellectual journeys (like converting to Marxism), activities (in Tōkyō, Russia, Korea), hardships (imprisonment, poverty, factional conflict), and unique traits, painting vivid portraits of individuals like the scholar-turned-revolutionary Ch'oe Ikhan, the resolute activist Pak Ch'ŏn, the intellectual Han Rim, the orator Song Ch'angnyŏm, and others. For historians, the article provides valuable firsthand character sketches and insights into the personal lives, networks, and ideological commitments of key figures in the often clandestine and persecuted early Korean communist/socialist movement, revealing details about their backgrounds, internal dynamics, and experiences under Japanese rule. The mention of specific events, publications, and factional disputes offers glimpses into the complex landscape of colonial-era Korean activism.

ML系 人物 印象記
						安炳珠
한동안 世上의 耳目을 놀랠대로 놀내이든 ML系의 人物들 그들은 아즉도 이 世上에 사라잇다. 그들은 確實히 우리와 갓치 땅우에 사라잇다. 그러나 우리와 갓치 白日을 보지는 못한다. 그들의 身體는 이러케 制限되여 잇지마는 그들의 精神은 노상 空間을 飛翔하고 잇단다.
아직도 그들의 消息이 茫然한 오늘에 잇서서 지나간 時節에 대한 그들의 私生活 밋 그 印象을 다시 한 번 들추어 보는 것도 또한 興味 100파센의 일이리라.

崔益翰
