Earthen Jar Project
						Kim Tong-hwan (金東煥)
(The Light of Spring Fills Heaven and Earth (天地))
The light of spring has filled the heavens and earth. It seems to be a sign that all creation (萬象) is reviving. At this time, I wish to shoulder an earthenware jar, visit the shelters of my brothers in all thirteen provinces, bite my ring finger (無名指), receive a drop of blood from each, and pour it into this jar. Even if it's only one or two drops of blood, when it drips from the fingertips of ten thousand, a hundred thousand people, will the jar not fill? Will the large jar not fill? If it doesn't fill, I will wander day and night until it does; I will roam the mountains and waters, weeping and calling out.

××
And so, let us place this jar filled with blood on a crossroads where ten thousand people pass by morning and evening. Then, even if we are fighting each other, let us think, "Ah, yes! Your blood and my blood are in that jar," and lower our raised fists. Let us calmly lower our glaring eyes. Also, when we see someone naked and destitute, let us say, "You and I are companions from the same jar," soothe them if they weep, support them if they are weak, and thus, let us all live together like beads strung on Buddhist prayer beads (百八念珠), like links in an iron chain. Let us not be scattered. Now is not the time to dissolve our patriotic spirit. It is a time when no one, whatever their ideology, can refuse to defend that ideology. Fundamental theory and action develop and are perfected only through absorbing partial, immediate theory and action—that is, through the complete resolution of the national question.

××
In the sky and land of April (四月), the light of spring is truly ripening. At this time, comrades, shall we not begin our Earthen Jar Project? <1>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Tong-hwan, uses the metaphor of filling an earthenware jar with drops of blood collected from every Korean in the thirteen provinces. This collective blood symbolizes unity and shared identity. He urges Koreans to place this jar in a public square as a reminder of their kinship, encouraging them to cease internal conflict, help one another, and remain united like beads on a string or links in a chain. He emphasizes that now is the time for solidarity and focusing on the "national question," suggesting that resolving this overarching issue is fundamental to progress, regardless of individual ideologies. For historians, this article is a poignant expression of nationalist sentiment and a call for unity among Koreans during the Japanese colonial period. The metaphorical "Earthen Jar Project" highlights the deep desire for solidarity and collective identity in the face of external oppression and internal divisions. The language reflects the era's style and the urgency felt towards national cohesion.

항아리 工事
						金東煥
(天地에 春光遍滿)
천지에 봄비치 가득 찻슴니다. 만상이 부활하는 조짐인가봄니다. 이에나는 오지항아리 한 개를 질머메고 십삼도방방곳곳의 형제들품막을 차저가서 無名指를 물어 피한방울씩 밧어서 이 항아리속에부어너코십소이다. 하고치, 두고치의 핏방울이라도 그것이 만사람, 십만사람의 손락테서 흘너러질 항아린들 못차오리, 독아린들 못오리? 못차면 차질지 밤을새어가며 도라다니지오 산과물을 울며 불며 도라다니지요.
××
그래서 가득찬 이피항아리를 우리는 아츰저녁 만사람이 지나다니는 네길거리에 노옵시다. 그래서 서로 싸우다가도 「올치, 네피, 내피다저항아리속에 잇거니」하고 들엇든주먹을 내립시다. 부릅든두눈을 고요히 내리김옵시다.  헐벗은 이를보거든 「너도 나도 가튼 항아리속동무」라하고 임르치고 울거든달내고 약하거든 부축하여주고, 이래서 百八念珠에인 구술알지, 쇠살슬의 한고리가티 우리들은 다 가티사십시오. 흐터지지를 맙시다. 지금은 애즉심을 해소식힐가 아님니다. 엇든사상을 가진이라도 그사상의 옹호를 거부하지못할임니다. 원측적리론과 행동은 부분적, 당면적의리론급행동을 섭취는데서즉민족문제의 완전한에서 비로소 발전, 완성되는것이외다.
××
四月의 하늘에는 정말봄빗치 무르녹고 잇슴니다. 이에 동모여 우리 항아리 工事를 시작하지 안으렴니.＜1＞
