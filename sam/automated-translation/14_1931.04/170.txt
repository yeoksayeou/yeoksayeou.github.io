**Body Text Translation:**
Pak Yŏl's Lover's Final Testament, Sent from Prison to a Certain Person
						Kaneko Fumiko (金子文子)

From that high window, three *cha* tall, truly spring-like, bright sunlight slants clearly across the three *tatami* mats, and the fragmented view of the distant blue sky lets my tired eyes rest. Without thinking, just gazing far off at the blue sky, memories of days spent talking endlessly with many comrades and various households in the suburbs, and thoughts of going to Kebi K'aera with Chodae, who is now already gone to the next world, surface as romantic recollections quite difficult to forget.

At that moment, the distinct, heavy sound of knocking on the wooden plank door calls me back from my wandering in the world of fantasy. "Number 49!" It's the voice of the chief guard, who always calls kindly.

I received a letter. Needless to say, it was the one written after meeting you the day before yesterday, detailing various things about the items sent in (差入物) because of me. Until I finished reading it, the guard was watching my hand turning the pages and my eyes following the characters.

"Today is really a fine day, like a spring day."

Because the weather was so fine, I was suddenly tempted by this fine weather, wishing I could take a slow walk even just in the yard, think of nothing, and lie down to relax on a patch of grass in a corner of the yard.

Yes. The truly beautiful world of Nature is the world most demanded first by me, no, by all who are confined; it is a place of rest, sustenance, and a place to freely launder one's heart.

The yard is full of weeds. So, I received permission to go out with that guard to pull weeds. <51>
Squatting in the garden shade, pulling small weeds
The prison's reality is utterly quiet.

It is truly quiet. Sparrows chirp on the roof. Again, various thoughts weigh upon my heart.

Pulling hard on a nameless weed entwined round my finger
it faintly cried "I want to live!"

My fingertips tremble so much I can barely grasp the weed. Somehow, I feel sad. But then again,

Stamping its feet, struggling not to be pulled
Its form is hateful, and sad.

So, I yank out the weeds vigorously again. It feels somewhat refreshing. But then,

Bending down, looking through my legs, not seeing people
Wanting to see the state of the world upside down.

I try such peculiar antics too.
That day was Sunday – a day off from labor (免役). Five or six female prisoners, guarded by two guards, are pulling weeds. It’s also a form of recreation.

Day off work, the young female prisoner's tied-up
*Ginkgo*-leaf hairstyle today is not disheveled.

The souls of those fallen to the guillotine
become the azalea's red gaze in the garden.

Two or three days ago, when I went to court for the first time in a while, I saw azaleas of the same color blooming by the gate of a magnificent mansion-like house near Yotsuya Mitsuke (四谷見附).

In the bourgeois garden, azaleas are blooming
the color of proletarian blood.

Flowers fall, flowers fall, but by the guillotine
falling, flowers bloom - ah, the XX person!

Carrying a basket, standing by the roadside at night
the young woman is now in prison.

Striving to live, striving to live, jostling together
The clamor of the fleeting world (婆娑), heard as someone else's affair.

Here(?) the young prisoner is just alone
lonely, sitting motionless
-Moonlight-

Tonight again, through the high window
the black bars are reflected(?) on my sleeping face-

The prison night is a tomb; literally, a deathbed. All my consciousness rests in this tomb. But when morning comes, again,

Morning and evening, consciousness returns to this corpse
The iron bars are visible, dark and bright.

My soul, do you wish for immortality?
Confined, in this body in prison.

Shedding the bonds called flesh, my soul
imagining a form taking revenge.

Afternoon of February 27th
There's notice that His Excellency the preliminary judge from the District Court will be inspecting the prison today. Humph, just stay completely still… (Omitted - Original Text)
As I wrote earlier, it's a big problem if three books arrive at once! My already unwell head might shatter. If only they could come in, please send even one novel by a so-called recently popular author.
By the time this letter reaches your hands, I'll have arranged for the return of "Civilization's Salvation" and "Uprising," so you don't need to hurry too much with the book to send in their place.
This morning, another detention renewal decision document arrived. Quite a pile has accumulated. Since this is property that only I can possess, I can share a portion with you as a gift.
The above was written after dinner on the 27th – while counting the sounds of melting snow dripping outside the window - <52>

Memory of September 1st
Today is September 1st – On the day of the Earthquake (震災), we were on the *tatami* mats spread out on the grass in that house's yard, eating cooked rice while talking about something unintelligible. It was you, XX brother, XX Mr./Ms., and P and me. – A middle school student stood far off listening to us.
Below the embankment, a woman was playing the Taishōgoto (大正琴). Far across the wide, wide field of grass, a red fire was burning. The moon was changing like the sun at sunset – ah, it was a chilling evening. But somehow, it feels like a very distant event from long ago emerging from a dream.
This morning, I encountered very good things two or three times. One was,

Removing the windowpane, capturing the obi's form
The morning a young female prisoner goes to court.

This relates to the "Matter of Petition" entrusted earlier, for which there was a summons from the court, allowing me to breathe the air of the outside world (娑婆) after a long time. The second thing was,

Wrapped in a padded kimono fragrant with indigo
Pitying my own worthless self, all alone.

With a guard, in the crowded corridor, XX's
Comrade, suddenly met - prison twilight.

Regarding my declaration:
(Omitted before - Original Text) I have acted and lived as a human being. And my relationships with many comrades must have been established on the foundation of the fact that I am human. You too should see me as having the qualification of a single human being. And isn't it only by seeing me that way that we can be true comrades? That is, isn't only solidarity based on a view of equality truly a free, personal solidarity?
Of course, between P (Reporter's Note: Refers to her lover Pak Yŏl) and me, there were other relations besides being comrades. Therefore, I now have no other thought than thinking of P as a comrade.
Furthermore, among those standing on the same X line, what need is there for a view of sexual distinction? Unless viewed as an object of sexual desire, what need is there for the special qualification of being a woman or a man? Are we not the same humans? What more is necessary beyond that?
Regarding sex, I possess only extremely disordered thoughts. Regarding direct sexual action, I am unconditional. However, at the same time, when I stand up as an individual human being, that is, when I stand up as a rebel, I declare that all matters related to sex, animals living under the qualification of being male – such things hold not even the value of a single damaged straw sandal before me.
What I seek now is neither man nor woman. Only human.
I exist as a human being. Based on the above reasons, I refuse to be seen as a female "possessing a very weak nature." - And at the same time, I also refuse the various favors premised upon that.
The slave who serves, calling the other party master; the master who pities, making the other party a slave – I reject both. Solidarity based on the view of equality in individual value and rights, that alone, only that, do I affirm as the proper relationship between humans. And I now declare again loudly that I seek a part of my relations with others only on that foundation.

Final Letter
Here is the very bottom of Hell – I feel the pressure as if being dragged down into a pit thousands of feet underground, and now I stand at the final point from which there is no escape. – I truly gaze upon myself as such, today of all days.
That I caused much worry and concern for a long time has now all become unforgettable memories and gratitude.
However, this time, this time the truly final day has come. Now, I will likely never again expose this unsightly body (身體) to the court; <53> and at the same time, sadly and lonely, there will probably be no chance to meet you, who are full of vitality and light.
Now is farewell. Saying this makes me feel terribly melancholic, but if this is truly the last, the very final letter, I might really cry. However, as far as I know, as far as can be predicted, this letter will be the final one.

Thinking of the groups of young people
gathering aimlessly on summer nights, I too want to go.

White collar, short sleeves, disheveled hair
a friend who greatly resembled me.

Fulfilling the last wishes, fulfilling the last wishes of a friend now gone
watching, thinking, the word "friend".

Wandering with a friend, seeking work
the stone pavements of Ginza in summer.

Flesh and spirit, two hearts in conflict
Waiting for a long time, my current self.

Everything is recollection – accompanied by sorrowful sentiment, I leave it behind in this world.
Speaking of myself, truly for a long time (for 3 years), it wasn't that I didn't anticipate such a day would come. Therefore, because of this day, how many times did I think firm, strong resolve and determination were necessary, and I have been striving to accept the moment that must come, truly easily, nonchalantly, as if falling asleep, if possible.
(Omitted in the middle - Original Text) I am also merely an idealist; I have not given up on myself today, nor do I think this is good enough for the wider world. Nor do I have the energy again to sing the "song of the dragged-along" of the world.
Although I don't think it's fine as it is, instead, I do not agonize over what to do about it either. Then, do I prostrate myself before something called Fate? Such bold, activist-like, heroic elements are not hidden within my body.
This fountain pen and the record of my "Victory of Death" will be the only mementos I send to you. Sometimes, please remember the fact that at one time, such a defiant human, an unwomanly human, existed. And that that human had a long, deep relationship with you. – If you wish to remember me and fill my lonely heart at some time, please place a branch of an evergreen tree (常盤나무), soaring straight up, before my gravestone.
I generally do not like flowers or grasses that bloom only to wither quickly. Though not beautiful and not eye-catching, I truly love the life-force of the evergreen tree, which remains forever green, stretching towards the blue sky.
Then, believing in the day I shall return like the new shoots of the evergreen tree stretching vigorously towards mid-heaven, I have decided to write this final letter to you.
I pray for your happy days to come. Then, truly, forever and ever, farewell.

Translator's Note - This is the farewell testament written from prison by Pak Yŏl's lover, Kaneko Fumiko, addressed to her comrade, a certain Mr./Ms., as she sat on the brink of her end. The original text is much longer, spanning several pages, but due to various circumstances, not all could be included; only the above has been presented here. It is the record of a female prisoner facing the death penalty – deeply concealed thoughts, perhaps worthy of careful reading. Furthermore, the original text was in Japanese, which has been translated, but it seemed best to leave the poems as they are, so they have been left untouched. Also, the details of the Pak Yŏl and Kaneko Fumiko incident and their love relationship are presumed to be well-known, so they are not repeated here. <54>

**Summary**

This article presents a poignant final letter from Kaneko Fumiko, a Japanese anarchist and partner of Korean anarchist Pak Yŏl, written from prison before her death (though published later in 1931). She reflects on her confinement, the beauty of nature contrasted with her imprisonment, memories of her past life and comrades (including Pak Yŏl, referred to as 'P'), and asserts her anarchist ideals of human equality, rejecting gender distinctions and societal hierarchies. Interspersed with melancholic Japanese *waka* poems, the letter conveys her resignation to her fate while maintaining her ideological convictions, requesting to be remembered not with transient flowers but with an evergreen branch symbolizing enduring life. For historians, the letter offers a rare, personal glimpse into the mindset of a radical figure involved in a major political case during Japan's colonial rule over Korea. It highlights the intersection of anarchism, anti-colonial sentiment (implied through Pak Yŏl's context and her critique of the 'bourgeois'), gender politics (her insistence on being seen as 'human' rather than 'woman'), and the psychological toll of imprisonment under the Japanese imperial system. Her reflections on the Great Kantō Earthquake and the reference to Yotsuya Mitsuke provide specific historical context.

9月 1日의 追憶
오늘은 9월1일- 震災가 잇슨 그날은 그집의 그 잔듸밧헤서 깔아논 「다다미」우에서 무엔지 알 수 압는 말을 하면서 쌀을 끄리어 먹고 잇섯다. 당신과 XX兄과 XX氏 그리고 P와 나엇지요.- 우리 겻헤는 中學生이 먼-히 서서 듯고 잇섯지요.
방축아래에서 女子가 大正琴을 타고 잇섯소. 너르나 너른 풀밧 저쪽에는 빨간불이 붓고 잇섯소. 달이 저녁놀 때의 太陽과 가치 변하면서- 아, 소름이 끼치는 저녁이엇지요. 그러나 엇전지 아조 먼- 녯날일이 꿈속에서 기어나오는듯하오.
오늘은 午前中 두 번이나 세 번이나 아조 조흔 일을 맛낫소. 그 한나는
窓硝子外して 寫す帶のさま
若き女囚の出廷の朝

나의 宣言으로 하여
(前略 - 원문) 나는 사람으로서 行爲하고 生活하여 온 터이다. 그러고 내가 人間인 事實의 基礎우에서 만흔 둥무들과의 交涉도 成立하고 잇섯슬 것이다. 당신들도 나를 한個의 人間의 資格우에서 보아줄 일이다. 그러고 그러케 보는데서만 眞實한 同志가 아닐가. 즉 平等觀의 우에선 結束 뿐만이 眞實로 自由스러운 人格的의 結束이 아닐가.
그야 P(記者註 愛人朴烈을 가르침)와 나 사이에는 同志로서의 그박게 交涉도 잇섯다. 그래서 나는 지금 同志로서의 P를 생각하는 以外에 딴생각이 업다.
또한 同一X線上에선 者사이에 무슨 性的 差別觀의 必要가 잇슬가. 性慾의 對象으로 보지안는 限에 女子라거나 男子라거나 하는 特殊한 資格이 무슨 必要가 잇슬가. 가튼 사람이 아닌가. 그 以上 무슨 必要가 잇슬가.
나는 「쎅스」에 대하여는 至極히 秩序업는 생각박게 가지지 못하엿다. 性的 直接行動에 대하여는 無條件이다. 그러치만 그와 同時에 내가 一個의 人間으로서 이러날 때 즉 反抗者로서 이러날 때 性에 대한 모든일 男子인 資格밋헤서 살고 잇는 動物. 그러한 것은 나의 압헤서는 한커테의 破損된 메투리짝만한갑도 가지고 잇지 안음을 宣言한다.
지금 내가 求하고 잇는 것은 男子도 아니다. 女子도 아니다. 人間 뿐이다.
나는 人間으로서 生存하고 잇다. 나는 以上의 理由에 基하야 「아조 軟弱한 性을 가진」 女性으로써 보여지는 것을 拒絶한다.- 그와 同時에 그의 前提밋헤선 여러 가지 恩惠를 또한 拒絶한다.
相對方을 上前이라고 하여 奉仕하는 奴隷, 相對方을 奴隷로 하여 불상해하는 主人, 이 두가지를 모다 나는 排斥한다. 個人의 價値와 權利에 잇서서 平等觀의 우에 선 結束 그것만이 다만 그것만을 人間相互間의 正當한 關係라하여 나는 肯定한다. 그리하여 나와 他人과의 交涉의 一部를 그 基礎우에서만 求하는 일을 나는 지금 다시 소리놉히 宣言한다.

最後의 便紙
여기는 地獄의 맨 밋바닥- 地下 여려 千尺의 坑內에 끄을려 드러가고 잇는듯한 威壓을 밧는 생각과 이제는 엇절 수 업는 最後의 一點에 서고 잇다. - 그러한 내 自身을 오늘이란 오놀 진실로 凝視함니다.
오랫둥안 여러 가지로 근심걱정을 만히 끼친 일이 이제는 모다 잇기 어러운 追憶이요 感謝이엇슴니다.
그러나 이번이란 이번은 정말 最後의 날이 왓슴니다. 이제는 다시 두 번 法廷에 이- 보기 흉한 身體를 내 번지는 일이 업슬 터이요＜53＞ 그와 同時에 슬푸고 또 외롭게도 元氣스럽고 光明에 찬 당신들과 맛날 機會도 잇지 안을걸요.
이제는 離別임니다. 이러케 말하면 몹시 憂鬱하여지나 그러나 이것이 정말 마즈막 最後의 最後의 便紙라고 하면 나는 정말 울고 말 것이요. 그러치만 내가 아는 程度에 잇서 豫斷되는 程度에 잇서 이 便紙은 이것이 最後가 될 것임니다.

모든 것은 追憶- 아슬푸러한 感傷을 짝하여 이세상에 남기고 감니다.
내 自身에 대하여 말하면 정말 오랫동안 (3년간이나) 이러한 날이 올 것을 豫斷치 못한 것은 아니엇슴니다. 그러기에 이날 때문에 몃 번이나 굿고 굿센 覺悟와 決心이 必要하다고 생각되어 될 수 잇스면 진실로 쉽사리 아모라치도 안케 자는드시 그와야할 瞬間을 甘受하려고 힘쓰고 잇섯슴니다.
(中略 - 원문) 나는 亦是 一介의 理想主義者로 오늘의 내 自身을 斷念하여 버린 것도 아니고 널니 世上에 대하여서도 이것으로 조타고는 생각지 안슴니다. 또다시 나는 세상의 「끄을너가는 者의 노래」를 부를 긔운도 나지 안슴니다.
이대로 조타는 것이라고는 생각지 안으나 그 代身 이것을 엇더케 하겟다고도 煩憫도 아니함니다. 그러면 運命이란 것 압헤 업듸어 비는가 하면 그토록 大膽한 志士的 , 英雄的의 分子는 나의 몸속에 숨어들어 잇지 안슴니다.
이 萬年筆과 나의 「死의 勝利」의 記錄이 당신에 보내는 唯一의 遺品이 될 것임니다. 때로는 어느 한때 이러케 不逞한 一人間이 女性답지못한 人間이 存在하여 잇섯다. 그래서 그 人間이 당신들과 오랫동안 깁픈 交際를 하여왓다. - 고 하는 事實을 생각하여 주십시요. 만일 당신이 나를 追憶하여 주어서 엇든 때의 寂寞한 나의 마음을 채이어줄 생각이 게시든 나의 비석압페 훨작 올니 소슨 常盤나무의 한 가지를 노아주세요.
나는 피어서는 곳 말라버리는 꼿이나 풀을 大體로 조와하지 안슴니다. 아름답지는 안고 사람의 눈을 끄을지 안치만 언제까지든지 푸르靑靑하게 蒼空에 향하여 뻐더오르는 常盤나무의 새엄을 나는 정말 사랑함니다.
그러면 새로 버더 올나갈 常盤나무의 새엄 中天에 향하야 긔운잇게 펴지어 오르는 새엄 그러면 다시 도라올 날을 밋고 나는 당신에게 最後의 便紙를 쓰기로 한바외다.
당신에의 幸福한 날이 오기를 빔니다. 그러면 정말 영원히 영원히 잘 잇스시오.
