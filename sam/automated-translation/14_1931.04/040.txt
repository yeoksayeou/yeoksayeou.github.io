Article Title: 解散된 臺灣民衆黨 (The Dissolved Taiwanese People's Party)
Author: 河野密
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

The Dissolved Taiwanese People's Party
						Kōno Mitsu

On February 28th last, the Taiwanese People's Party (臺灣 民衆黨), Taiwan's only political party, was ordered to dissolve its association. Subsequently, the Chief of the Police Affairs Bureau gave the reason for its prohibition as follows:
"The Government-General of Taiwan (臺灣 總督府) previously ordered the dissolution of the Taiwanese People's Party's predecessor, the Taiwanese People's Party (臺灣 民黨), on June 3, Shōwa 2 (1927). Moderate elements among the leaders of that party became central figures, softening the platform and policies, while simultaneously declaring that their purpose was not a nationalist movement. On July 10, Shōwa 2, they changed the name to the Taiwanese People's Party and formed the association. Not long after, however, the party's leadership was seized by radical elements. They persistently adhered to the principles of the former Taiwanese People's Party, and their movement increasingly turned towards extremism, recklessly adopting anti-mother country and anti-government attitudes, ultimately leading moderate elements to successively withdraw. Although the authorities constantly urged good guidance, they dared show no reflection. This time, they have again revised the platform and policies, revealing their intended path. Observing the party's essence through the People's Party's past actions and the newly revised platform and policies, it is clear that the consistent guiding spirit lies, in short, in absolute opposition to the Governor-General's rule and in national self-determination. As such, recognizing the existence of an association that obstructs harmony between the Interior [Japan] and Taiwan (內臺融和) and subsequently contravenes the grand policy for ruling this island (本島 統治의 大方針) is unacceptable. While freedom of association should originally be respected, it is a matter of course that strict legal regulations must be invoked against groups that harm peace and order (安寧秩序). This is the reason for prohibiting the association, the Taiwanese People's Party." -(Taiwan Daily News (臺灣日日新聞), February 19)-

Based on this, the circumstances leading to the prohibition of the Taiwanese People's Party association have become roughly clear. The crux was the revision of its platform and the change in its policies. If so, were the revised platform and policies truly, as the Chief of the Police Affairs Bureau stated, clearly "based on absolute opposition to the Governor-General's rule and national self-determination," and did they "obstruct harmony between the Interior and Taiwan and subsequently contravene the grand policy for ruling this island"?

The draft revision of the platform submitted by the Central Executive Committee headquarters to the 5th All-Island Congress of the Taiwanese People's Party is as follows:
1. Strive to win political XX for laborers, farmers, propertyless citizens, and all oppressed XX peoples.
2. Protect the daily interests of laborers, farmers, propertyless citizens, and all oppressed peoples.
3. Strive for the organizational expansion of laborers, farmers, propertyless citizens, and all oppressed peoples.

Compared to the former platform, which was:
1. Establish people-based politics.
2. Construct a rational economic organization.
3. Rectify defects in the social system.
one can say it has become much more concrete and clearly shows a proletarian stance. However, one cannot immediately say that this platform fosters "ethnic animosity (民族的 反感)." It merely signifies the removal of the admixtures of petty-bourgeois ideology (小市民的 「이데오르기」) that the Taiwanese People's Party previously held and the pursuit of purity as a proletarian party. <20>
This is something necessitated by the evolution of the times and is not a problem faced solely by the Taiwanese People's Party. Yet, to immediately judge a platform set at the same level as, or even below, the platforms advocated by proletarian parties in the Interior [mainland Japan] as "anti-Governor-General," "anti-government," and "nationalistic" seems excessively hypersensitive (甚히 神經過敏한 일).

Following the platform revision, there was a major revision of policies, and their content also became much more concrete. That is, whereas the previous policies consisted merely of 9 items for political policy, 7 items for economic policy, and 3 items for social policy, the new draft policies reached a large number: 28 items for political policy, 36 items for economic policy, and 8 items for social policy. As can be understood from this, it is a fact that the new policies delve into the minute and exhaustive details (微에 入하고 細를 極하야), drafted in direct response to the actual conditions of Taiwanese governance.

It is also true that there are items such as "Oppose the XX policy of imperialism (反對帝國主義之XX政策)," "Oppose imperialist wars of X peoples X capitalists (反對X民衆 X資本家的 帝國主義 戰爭)," "Ethnic discriminatory treatment must be completely abolished (民族的 差別待遇 須一切撤廢)," "Oppose all that hinders the free development of the indigenous peoples/tribes (反對一切阻害蕃人民族的 自由發展)," etc.

However, these concrete policies are also policies already put forth by legal proletarian parties in the Interior and are by no means exclusive possessions of the Taiwanese People's Party. Furthermore, it is clear from the phrasing and the arrangement of items that when drafting the policy revisions, they sought their model entirely in the policies of proletarian parties in the Interior. Is it not natural for the Taiwanese People's Party, which advocates for being a proletarian party just like those in the Interior, to try to advance to that point? And is it not also extremely natural to think that what is permitted for proletarian parties in the Interior should also be permitted for a legal political party of the colonial populace?

However, even conceding a thousand steps, if there were indeed elements in the Taiwanese People's Party's policy revisions deemed inappropriate for Taiwanese governance, why was their deletion not ordered before commanding the dissolution of the association? Would it not have been far from too late to order the dissolution of the association if they refused to comply after being ordered to delete the items deemed inappropriate? The Taiwanese People's Party is a political party aiming for legal existence. It is a party thinking of the XX of the Taiwanese masses as a proletariat. To snatch away the life of the association from behind with the reasons of being "anti-Governor-General" and "nationalistic" is, I think, an incorrect measure. Isn't this very approach to governance the reason that causes the Taiwanese people to internally accumulate grievances and dissatisfaction, thinking "Oppose the Governor-General's bureaucratic rule"?

The Taiwanese People's Party previously had strong tendencies towards being the main body of a moderate (徹溫的인) "nationalistic" movement. However, the subsequent development of the movement gradually took on stronger colors of a proletarian party style. This is a significant reason why internal confrontation arose between the left and right wings of the party, centering on the issue of platform revision and policy changes.

This internal party conflict led to the formation and split of the Taiwan Local Autonomy League (臺灣 地方自治聯盟) starting last summer, sending figures like Lin Hsien-tʻang (林獻堂), a great senior figure in the Taiwan movement, as well as Yang Chao-chia (楊肇嘉), Hung Yüan-huang (洪元煌), Tsʻai Pʻei-huo (蔡培火), etc., to the Local Autonomy League. In response, the Taiwanese People's Party side, centered around figures like Pʻeng Hua-shui (彭滑水), Li Yu-san (李友三), Chʻen Chʻi-chʻang (陳其昌), Lu Ping-ting (盧丙丁), Hsü Ping-ting (許丙丁), Hsü Hu (許胡), Pai Chʻeng-chih (白成枝), Hsieh Chʻun-mu (謝春木), etc., gradually thickened its proletarian colors, both in confrontation with the Local Autonomy League and in opposition to the right-wing, non-legal Cultural Association (文化協會). One can say that this time's platform and revised policy changes were the guidelines to settle accounts with its past and clearly mark the starting point for a new movement. Consequently, this platform revision and policy renewal were the first step towards rebirth (更生) for the Taiwanese People's Party and a life-or-death issue (死活의 問題) to resolve the contradictions contained within it since its founding. Yet, to face the misfortune of party prohibition precisely at such a moment, just as they were about to begin, must be a deep-seated regret (骨髓에 사모치는 遺憾) for the party leaders. For us too, observing from afar, the dissolution of a fellow party in a colony with which we had established contact cannot but make us feel a loneliness akin to parting with an acquaintance through death.

Nevertheless, for the Government-General to interpret the Taiwanese People's Party's proletarian progress as an immediate change in guiding spirit and proceed to order its prohibition is something that cannot but be called extremely short-sighted (甚히 短見). As long as the Taiwanese People's Party also aims for the expansion of the political freedom of the general populace and advocates for the protection of the economic interests of the colonial masses, its becoming a party of the propertyless masses (無産大衆) is an inevitable destiny. This is not due to the ideology of two or three leaders, but because the current objective situation commands it. Therefore, there was absolutely no need to panic and immediately apply pressure just because the Taiwanese People's Party gradually came to possess clearer proletarian party colors. If there is any rationality to the forceful suppression of the Taiwanese People's Party this time, it would only be the prohibition notice (禁制의 門碑) saying:
"Proletarian parties are not welcome in the colonies."
However, this is merely extremely anachronistic (時代錯誤). It is also called an extremely sad thing. (Remainder omitted - original text) <21>

**Summary**

The article, written by Japanese socialist Kōno Mitsu, critiques the Japanese Government-General of Taiwan's forced dissolution of the Taiwanese People's Party in February 1931. Kōno argues the official justification – that the party's revised, more proletarian platform was "anti-Japanese," "anti-government," and aimed at "national self-determination" – is unfounded hypersensitivity. He points out that the party's platform was similar to, or even less radical than, legal proletarian parties in mainland Japan, and its shift leftward was a natural evolution reflecting objective conditions and an attempt to resolve internal ideological contradictions after splitting with more moderate nationalist factions (like the Taiwan Local Autonomy League). Kōno condemns the dissolution as short-sighted, hypocritical (as similar platforms were allowed in Japan), and ultimately revealing a colonial policy intended to suppress proletarian movements ("Proletarian parties are not welcome in the colonies"), which he believes only fuels anti-colonial sentiment. For historians of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it provides a contemporary analysis of Japanese colonial suppression tactics in another colony (Taiwan), highlighting parallels in dealing with nationalist and leftist movements. Kōno's perspective as a Japanese socialist criticizing his own government's imperialism offers insight into the transnational connections and shared concerns among anti-colonial movements and leftist critics within the Japanese empire.

解散된 臺灣民衆黨
						河野密
지난 2월28일에 臺灣 唯一의 政黨인 臺灣 民衆黨은 結社의 解散을 당하고 뒤이어 警務局長은 그 禁止의 理由로
「臺灣 總督府는 昭和 2년 6월 3일 臺灣 民衆黨의 前身인 臺灣 民黨의 結社禁止 處分을 하엿는데 同黨 幹部 中의 穩健分子가 中心이 되어 網領과 政策을 緩和함과 同時에 民族運動을 目的하는 것이 아니라는 뜻을 宣言하고 昭和 2년 7월 10일 名稱을 臺灣 民衆黨이라고 改稱하고 結社를 組織함에 이르럿다. 그 뒤 얼마잇지 아니하야 黨의 指導權은 急進分子가 把握하는 바 되고 依然히 몬저의 臺灣 民黨의 主義를 墨守하고 그 運動이 날로 矯激에 향하야 함부로 反母國, 反官的 態度에 나옴으로 끗끗내 穩健分子는 前後하야 脫退함에 至하엿다. 當局으로서는 恒常 善導를 催促하엿스나 敢히 反省하는 바 업고 今番 다시 網領, 政策을 改修하여 그 나아갈 바를 보이엇다. 民衆黨 從來의 行動과 今回 改修된 網領政策을 통하야 黨의 本質을 察할 때 一貫한 指導精神은 요컨대 總督政治에 대한 絶對反對와 民族自決에 잇는 것은 明瞭하다. 如斯히 하야 內臺融和를 阻害하고 延하야 本島 統治의 大方針에 背反하는 結社의 存立을 認定하는 일은 되지안는 바이다. 원악 結社의 自由는 尊重할 바이나 安寧秩序를 害하는 團體에 대하여는 嚴正한 法規의 發動을 기다릴 것은 勿論이다. 이것이 結社 臺灣 民衆黨을 禁止하는 所以이다.)               -(臺灣日日新聞 2월 19일)-
이에 의하야 臺灣 民衆黨의 結社가 禁止됨에 이른 經緯는 大略 明瞭하여젓다. 要는 그 網領의 改修와 政策의 變更에 잇섯든 것이다. 그런면 改修된 網領과 政策과는 果然 警務局長이 말하는 바와가티 「總督政治에 대한 絶對反對와 民族自決에 잇섯든 것이」 明瞭하고 그러고 「內臺의 融和를 阻害하고 延하야 本島 統治의 大方針에 背反」하는 것일가?
臺灣 民衆黨의 제5차 全島大會에 提案된 本部中央執行委員會提出의 網領改修 草案은
1. 爭得勞働者農民無産市民及一切被XX民衆之政治的XX
2. 擁護勞働者農民無産市民一切被壓迫民衆之日當的利益
3. 努力勞働者農民無産市民及一切被壓迫民衆之組織擴大化
라고 하는 바이다. 이것을 前者의 網領이든
1. 確立民本政治
2. 建設合理的 經濟的 組織
3. 改除社會制度之缺陷
에 比하면 훨신 具體性을 뛰어 왓고 無産階級的 立場을 明示하여 왓다고 할 수 잇다. 그러나 이 網領을 가지고 卽時 「民族的 反感」을 助長한다고는 할 수 업술 것이다. 다만 臺灣 民衆黨의 從來 가지고 잇든 小市民的 「이데오르기」의 夾雜物을 除去하고 無産階級的 政黨으로서의 純一性을 求하고 잇다고 함에 不過할 것이다.＜20＞
이것은 時代의 進化가 식힌 것으로 單只 臺灣 民衆黨만이 逢着한 問題가 아닌 것이다. 그런데 內地의 無産政黨이 揭하고 잇는 網領과 가튼 水準 혹은 그 以下의 網領을 세워가진 것을 直時 「反總督政治」 「反官的」 「民族的」이라고 斷定하는 것은 甚히 神經過敏한 일이 아닐가.
網領의 改修에 따라 政策에 大改修가 잇고 그 內容도 훨신 具體的이 되어 왓다. 즉 以前의 政策이 政治政策 9項目, 經濟政策 7項目, 社會政策 3項目에 不過함에 反하야 新政策草案은 政治政策 28項目, 經濟政策 36項目, 社會政策 8項目의 多數에 及하여 잇다. 이에 의하여 知得되는 바와 가티 新政策은 微에 入하고 細를 極하야 臺灣 統治의 實相에 卽하야 立案된 것은 事實이다.
또 「反對帝國主義之XX政策」 「反對X民衆 X資本家的 帝國主義 戰爭」 「民族的 差別待遇 須一切撤廢」 「反對一切阻害蕃人民族的 自由發展」 等等의 項目이 잇는 것도 事實이다.
그러나 此等의 具體的 政策도 內地의 合法的 無産政黨의 이미 걸고 잇는 政策으로써 결코 臺灣 民衆黨의 專有物이 •„니다.
더욱 그 政策의 改修 草案을 할 지음에 잇서 專혀 그 範을 內地의 無産政黨의 政策에 求하고 잇는 것은 行文사이에 또는 項目의 排列 上으로 보아 明瞭하다. 內地의 無産政黨이 걸고 잇는 以上 가튼 無産政黨을 標傍하는 臺灣 民衆黨이 거기까지 進出하려고 함은 當然치 아니한가. 그러고 또 內地의 無産政黨에게 許諾되어 잇는 以上에는 그것을 殖民地 民衆의 合法的 政黨에도 許諾될 것이라고 生覺함도 또한 極히 當然하지 안을가.
