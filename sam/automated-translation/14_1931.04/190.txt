Slang Among New Women

有望 (Yumang) - Meaning an unmarried person. (Meaning that since she hasn't married yet, she can still get married, thus she is "promising" [有望 - yumang]).

푸른새 (P'urŭnsae) - Meaning happiness. (Because Maeterlinck's fairy tale play "The Blue Bird" symbolizes happiness).

陸軍 (Yukkkun) - Meaning men. (Because women are called 海軍 (Haegun - Navy), this is used as its counterpart).

졔비 (Chebi) - Meaning a younger lover (male). (When a woman takes a man younger than herself as a lover, that man is called a 'swallow' [제비 - chebi] - What does it mean? You all know already).

H - Meaning husband. (That is, because Husband in English means 男便 (namp'yŏn - husband), only the initial letter is taken and used. Calling it up to Hus, as "haesŭ," also has the same meaning).

M - Meaning menstruation. (This also takes the initial letter from the English 'Monthly-water').

衛生婦人 (Wisaeng Puin) - Meaning a woman whose body is healthy but whose head is extremely lacking.

기럭이의 便에 (Kirŏgiŭi P'yŏne) - Meaning a letter [便紙 - p'yŏnji]. (In the *Hànshū Sū Wǔ Zhuàn* [漢書蘇武傳], it says "When Emperor Zhao [昭帝] ascended the throne, the Xiongnu [匈奴] made peace with Han [漢], and Han sought Wu [Sū Wǔ] and others, (omitted - original text) etcetera etcetera") [referring to the story where Su Wu sent a message tied to a goose leg].

海棠花 (Haedanghwa) - Red means "I love you." White means "I express my respect to you with a pure heart."

洗禮 (Serye) - Refers to a man losing his virginity [童貞 - tongjŏng] or a woman losing her chastity [處女性 - ch'ŏnyŏsŏng]. <58>

**Summary**

This article from the April 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine lists and explains popular slang terms used among "New Women" in Korea at the time. The terms cover topics like marital status (有望 - promising/unmarried), relationships (졔비 - younger lover, H - husband), biology (M - menstruation), insults (衛生婦人 - healthy but dim-witted woman), and coded messages (海棠花 - flowering quince colors for love/respect). Some terms are derived from Western influences (literature like Maeterlinck's "Blue Bird," English words like "Husband," "Monthly-water"), while others draw on classical Chinese references (기럭이 - goose/letter, referencing Sū Wǔ) or create new Korean dichotomies (陸軍/海軍 - Army/Navy for Men/Women). The article provides a fascinating snapshot of linguistic innovation and changing social dynamics, particularly regarding women's roles, relationships, and awareness of Western culture during the colonial period. Historians might find the blend of traditional references, neologisms, and English loanwords particularly interesting, reflecting the complex cultural milieu and the evolving identity of the modern Korean woman in the 1930s.

졔비- 年下의 情夫란 뜻(녀자가 저보다 나어린 男子를 愛人으로 가질 때 그 사내를 제비라고- 무슨 뜻의냐고? 다 아시면서)

H- 男便이란 뜻(卽 英語에 Husband가 男便임으로 그 머리글자만 따다가 쓰는 것이다. 或 Hus까지 불너 「해스」라 하여도 가튼 意味)

M- 月經을 意味한다(이것도 英語에 Monthly-water의 머리글자를 떼어다가 쓰는 것)

衛生婦人- 몸은 튼튼하나 머리가 至極히 모자라는 女子의 뜻.

기럭이의 便에- 便紙라는 뜻(漢書蘇武傳에 「昭帝立, 匈奴與漢和親, 漢求武等, (略 - 원문) 云云

海棠花- 赤色은 「나는 당신을 사랑함니다」 白色은 「純潔한 마음을 가지고 당신에게 敬意를 表함니다」

洗禮- 男子가 童貞을 일코 女子가 處女性을 일는 일을 말한다.＜58＞
