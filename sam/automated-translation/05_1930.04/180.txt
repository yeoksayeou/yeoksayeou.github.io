Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The Boatman's Wife
						Pa'in (Poem), Sŏgyŏng (Illustration)

The waves that lightly strike
The bare hind legs

Are they waves from ten *ri* away?
Are they waves from a hundred *ri* away?

Ten *ri* away is the net my beloved cast
A hundred *ri* away is the boat my beloved rides

These waves lightly striking the bare hind legs
Are they perhaps a gift sent by my beloved? <51>

**Summary**

This short poem depicts a boatman's wife standing by the water, feeling the waves touch her legs ("bare hind legs"). She muses about the origin of the waves, associating the distances of ten *ri* and a hundred *ri* with her husband's fishing net and boat, respectively. She wonders if the waves themselves are a message or gift from her distant beloved. From a historical perspective, the poem offers insight into the emotional landscape of ordinary Koreans during the colonial period, highlighting themes of waiting, longing, and the connection to loved ones separated by distance, possibly due to economic necessities like fishing far from home. The simple language and focus on personal feeling provide a glimpse into everyday life and sentimentality.
