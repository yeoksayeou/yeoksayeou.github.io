**Body Text Translation**
Peninsula's Heroine, A Sad History of Queen Min
						Top'o Kŏsa (道袍居士)
I. Conflict with the Taewŏn'gun (大院君)
Queen Min (閔氏), the royal consort of His Majesty Yi Hŭi (李熙陛下) of Korea (韓國), was truly a peerless beauty (絶世의 美人) rarely seen in ancient or modern times. Among the mountains and rivers (山河) of the Eight Provinces (八道), there was said to be none who could claim to surpass her in appearance (容色); she was truly a woman of rare beauty (美貌). On top of that, she possessed the talent (才略) of deep wisdom and farsighted planning (深智遠謀), such that even if she were active in the first-rate diplomatic circles (第一流의 外交界) of Europe (歐羅巴), she would decidedly never be found lacking (不足) – such was the skill (手腕) she possessed. The Korean Foreign Minister (韓國外部大臣) at the time, Kim Yun-sik (金允植氏), was a famous figure known as a scholar (學者) of his generation (一代); he is said to have praised her at length (長久嘆稱) to a certain foreign dignitary (外國 某大官), saying,
"Queen Min's brilliance (聰明), talent (才略), and erudition (學識) are truly not inferior (遜色) in comparison to the world's first-rate diplomats (世界 第一流의 外交家)."
Indeed, the power (勢力) of the Queen Consort within the Korean royal court (韓國宮廷) was tremendous, so much so that the court officials (群臣) under the King's sole command (王宮一手) were anxious (汲汲하엿스며) not to go against the Queen Consort's wishes. Even the envoys (使臣) of the powerful great nations (强大列國) had no time to spare from watching her expression (얼골빗) as she held all political power (一切의 政治의 權力).
However, amidst this, there was one person whom even the Queen Consort herself could not control: the King's own father, the Taewŏn'gun (大院君), who had suppressed the Catholics (天主敎徒) and valiantly repelled (擊破하엿든) the French fleet (佛蘭西 艦隊) at Inch'ŏn (仁川) harbor. Queen Min (閔后) tried everything to bring him over to her side, sending money and goods (金品) and employing all sorts of methods (別別手段), but the bold and decisive (豪邁果敢) Taewŏn'gun would never fall into her snare (籠絡). At this point, as Yuan Shikai (袁世凱) arrived in Inch'ŏn (仁川) leading the Beiyang Fleet (北洋艦隊), the Queen Consort sent a secret message (密告) to Yuan, arranging it to look like an invitation to a Qing warship (淸國軍艦), and had the Taewŏn'gun imprisoned (監禁) just like that and sent to be confined (幽閉) in Baodingfu (保定府), Tianjin (天津), it is said. (Middle section omitted from original text) <28>

II. Scattered Flowers at Midnight (夜半에 落花浪藉)
In the last third of September, Meiji (明治) 28 [1895], Japanese Army Lieutenant General Miura Gorō (三浦梧樓) <28> was urgently appointed as the Minister to Korea (韓國公使), entered the palace (宮中에 叅內하여), and presented his credentials (信任狀). Then, just two days later, the Queen Consort's envoy (使者) presented Minister Miura with two conditions, demanding immediate approval (卽時 承認):
One, the immediate disbandment (卽時 解散) of the Korean Training Corps (韓國訓練隊), which the Japanese Garrison (日本守備隊) was guiding (指導).
One, allowing Min Yŏng-jun (閔泳駿), a relative (一族) of Queen Min who was in exile (亡命中) in Shanghai (上海), to return to the country (歸國) and be appointed (登用) to the cabinet (內閣).
These were fatal conditions (致命的 條件) for Japan (日本). However, Minister Miura surprisingly approved them immediately (卽時 承認); it seems the Minister already had an extraordinary resolve (非常한 決心) in mind at this time.
Then, an urgent messenger rushed to Adachi Kenzō (安澾藏) (the current Home Minister [內務大臣]), who was the president of the private Hansŏng Ilbo (漢城日報社長). On that evening, as the autumn rain drizzled down (가을비가 추눅히 내리는), the curtain of an untimely, great historical tragedy (歷史的 大悲劇의 幕) was already beginning to fall upon the capital city (서울의 天地); how many knew of this?
In early October of that year, many Japanese rōnin (日本人 壯士), led by Adachi, gathered on the second floor of the Hansŏng Ilbo office (漢城日報社 2層) in Yongsan (龍山). That night, at 12:30 AM, the aforementioned 48 rōnin (前記壯士 48人), each wearing a Japanese sword (日本刀) at their side (엽헤), formed groups of two or three and secretly converged on the Taewŏn'gun's residence (大院君의 邸宅) in Kongdŏk-ri (孔德里) outside the city (市外). As the late moon of the 20th day of the lunar calendar (陰曆 스무날의 느진달) hung at one edge of the sky (하늘 한 끗에) bearing some gloomy premonition (陰散한 豫感), the aforementioned group of rōnin, joined midway by a contingent of Korean strongmen (韓國壯士) led by Yi Chu-hoe (李周會), reached the Taewŏn'gun's residence. They locked up the captain of the guard (警衛隊長) and dozens of others, who had been previously placed there by Queen Min, in a warehouse (倉庫에 監禁하여 버리고). They placed the 78-year-old Taewŏn'gun (老大院君) (by then, he had already escaped Baodingfu and returned) in a palanquin (驕子), which the rōnin carried. They departed the residence, joined forces with the Training Corps (訓練隊) midway, passed through Sŏdaemun (西大門), and arrived before Kwanghwamun (光化압헤). Occasionally, dogs barked (개가 컹컹 지섯스나) at the sound of footsteps (발자최 소리), but that too soon faded. They immediately set off (卽時 바를 때고), climbed the walls of the royal palace (왕궁의 성벽), and after several clashes (衡突) with the Royal Palace Guard soldiers (王宮守衛兵士), reached the front (前面) of Kwŏnjŏngjŏn Hall (勸政殿). They came right before the Kŏnch'ŏng Palace (乾淸殿) in Kyŏngbok Palace (景福宮), where the King (國王) resided (게신). At that time, Royal Guard Commander Hong Kye-hun (侍衛隊長 洪啓薰) died in battle (戰死), and many officers and soldiers (將卒) also died (죽엇스며) (Remainder entirely omitted)... Thus, a major incident (大事變) erupted (突發); this event shocked the entire world (全世界), and the Japanese government (日本政府) immediately dismissed (卽時 罷免) Minister Miura and had the Japanese residents in the capital withdraw from Korea (在京日本人을 退韓식히엇든 것이외다). (Remainder omitted) (Because free description [自由記述] by our own hands is impossible [不可能], this text is a translated excerpt [譯載] from the writing of Mr. Ono [小野氏], a Japanese person, published recently = Photo is Queen Min [閔后]) <29>
<28-29>

**Summary**

This 1930 article portrays Queen Min as a beautiful and politically brilliant figure whose power rivaled foreign diplomats but who faced insurmountable opposition from the Taewŏn'gun. It describes her attempts to sideline him, including his exile to China. The second part details the arrival of Japanese Minister Miura Gorō, Queen Min's demands (disbanding the Training Corps, appointing Min Yŏng-jun), Miura's suspicious compliance, and the subsequent mobilization of Japanese rōnin and Korean collaborators who abducted the Taewŏn'gun and stormed Kyŏngbok Palace, leading to the assassination (though the killing itself is omitted). The article explicitly states it's a translation from a Japanese source (Mr. Ono) because "free description by our own hands is impossible," implying colonial censorship.

For historians, this piece is interesting for several reasons: 1) It shows a Korean perspective from 1930, albeit filtered through a Japanese source and likely self-censorship, acknowledging Queen Min's capabilities while framing her death as a tragedy. 2) The explicit mention of relying on a Japanese text due to inability to write freely highlights the constraints on historical discourse under Japanese rule. 3) The details provided about the Eulmi Incident (Miura's role, Adachi Kenzō's involvement, the mobilization of rōnin, the forced involvement of the Taewŏn'gun) reflect a specific narrative available or permissible at the time. 4) The use of older Korean language and orthography provides linguistic data.

半島의 女傑, 閔后哀史
						道袍居士
一. 大院君과 抗爭
韓國의 李熙陛下의 王妃閔氏는 실로 古今이 뵙기 드문 絶世의 美人이엇슴니다. 八道의 山河 中에 그 容色에 잇서서 그이보다 낫다고 나설 이가 업다 하리만치 실로 드문 美貌을 가진 분이엇슴니다. 그 우에 深智遠謀의 才略을 가지엇슴으로 歐羅巴 第一流의 外交界에 活躍을 식힌다 하여도 決코 不足을 取하지 안을 그런 手腕을 가지고 잇섯든 터이외다. 當時의 韓國外部大臣 金允植氏는 一代의 學者라 有名하든 인물인 바 氏는 外國 某大官에게 對하야
「閔后의 聰明과 才略과 學識은 실로 世界 第一流의 外交家에 比하여 決코 遜色이 업다」고 長久嘆稱하엿다 함니다.
실로 韓國宮廷에 잇는 王妃의 勢力은 굉장하여서 王宮一手의 群臣들은 王妃의 뜻에 어그러지지 말기에 汲汲하엿스며 一切의 政治의 權力을 쥔 王妃에 對하야 强大列國의 모든 使臣들까지 그의 얼골빗을 엿보기에 틈이 업섯슬 지경이다.
그러지만 이 속에서 王妃自身으로도 엇절 수 업는 人物 한 분이 잇섯스니 그는 天主敎徒를 虐殺하고 佛蘭西 艦隊를 仁川바다에서 훌용히 擊破하엿든 現王의 實父大院君 그 분이엇드람니다. 閔后는 엇더케 하든지 이 분을 자긔편에 너흐려고 金品도 보내고 또 別別手段을 다 썻건만 豪邁果敢한 大院君은 決코 그 籠絡에 빠지지 안트람니다. 이에 王妃는 袁世凱가 北洋艦隊를 끄을고 仁川에 來航하엿슴으로 密告를 袁에게 보내여 淸國軍艦에 招待하는 양으로 하고 大院君을 그양 監禁하여 天津保定府에 보내어 幽閉식혓드라 함니다.(中略-원문)＜28＞

二. 夜半에 落花浪藉
明治 28年 9月 下旬 日本의 陸軍中將 三浦梧樓는 韓國公使로＜28＞ 急遽赴任하여 宮中에 叅內하여 信任狀을 捧呈하엿슴니다. 그런 뒤 바로 이틀만에 王妃의 使者는 三浦公使에게 두가지 條件을 주면서 卽時 承認을 求하엿스니
一, 日本守備隊가 指導하고 잇는 韓國訓練隊를 卽時 解散하여 버릴 일
