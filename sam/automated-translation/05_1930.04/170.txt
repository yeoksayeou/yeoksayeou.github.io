**Body Text Translation**
Song of the Boatman
						P'ain (Song), Sŏgyŏng (Illustration)

The Great Wall too, if we decide to build it, we could build it
Even if just one or two people decide to build it, we could build it
Eya deheya, we'll surely build it
If there's just dedication, what couldn't we build?

The vast blue waves too, if we decide to go, we could go
Deciding to go in a day or two, we could go
Eya deheya, we'll surely go
If there's just dedication, where couldn't we go?

Clouds floating on the sea will go with the wind
Even rough waves will die down with rowing
Eya deheya, what cannot be done?
Let's put strength in our arms and row!<49>

**Summary**

This poem, presented as a boatman's work song, uses metaphors of monumental tasks—building the Great Wall and crossing the vast ocean—to assert that any challenge can be overcome through sincere dedication ("정성") and strenuous effort ("팔둑에 힘주어 놀질하자"). It conveys a strong message of perseverance and the power of human will. From the perspective of colonial Korean history, the poem's emphasis on overcoming immense difficulties through determination and hard work, published in 1930, could be interpreted by historians as a subtle reflection of the Korean people's spirit and resilience under Japanese rule, even if framed within the context of a seemingly simple folk song.
