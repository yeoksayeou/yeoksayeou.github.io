現代美人觀, 文學과 美人
						廉相涉
(Modern View of Beauty, Literature and Beauty
						Yŏm Sangsŏp)

I cannot know what the characteristics of modern beauty are, but in any case, the so-called oriental beauty is likely non-modern. Rather than emotional, intellectual; rather than static, dynamic; rather than ladylike, coquettish (코-켓트); rather than being described as elegant or graceful (端麗), it is in the jazzy (짜즈적) aspect that modern beauty might be glimpsed. In short, the Ch'un'aengmu (Spring Nightingale Dance, 春鶯舞) is classical, and the "Charleston" (찰스톤) is modern. The standard for beauty will likely follow this.

Then, what specifically are all the elements of modern beauty? This is hard to encounter easily, so even drawing an outline is difficult, but above all, the eyes must be sensual. While her development should be voluptuous (豊艶), her physique must be solid (완실). *This is because the beauty of frailty (孱弱) is non-modern. Furthermore, her movements should be extremely rhythmical (리드미컬), producing not just the beauty of the body's lines (線美) but a symphonic effect of subtle lines through her entire movement, radiating a rich hue and possessing a captivating beauty (眩惑). And while further displaying the beauty of her limbs (手足), her bearing (擧止) should be masculine. Besides these, things that indirectly enhance beauty lie in voice, knowledge, and taste, so to become a modern beauty, one must know at least the basics of vocal music, freely wield modern buzzwords and academic/technical terms, and possess common knowledge and some skill not only in areas like film, music, art, and literature, but also in billiards (玉突), golf (꼴프), and other sports (스포-쓰).

Next, what kind of beauty do I depict in my novels? Since I do not write novels of "romanticism" (로맨틔슴), my "heroine" (히-로인) is not necessarily limited to depicting only beauties. However, if I were to depict a beauty, since I write modern stories, there would be many cases where I follow the standards mentioned above, but more than that, I focus primarily on character <44> so it is natural that the "type" (타입) changes from time to time to match the character. However, in present-day Chosŏn (朝鮮), no matter how much of a "modern girl" (모-던, 껄) she may be, due to social circumstances or the influence of old customs (舊慣), there are many times when it is difficult to fully depict a "modern girl." Not only that, but although women who would not be ashamed to be presented as modern beauties occasionally exist in reality, and it is not impossible to treat them as novel characters, their cultivation, even just in terms of common sense, is still too shallow (淺劣) to become modern beauties or "modern girls," so in some cases, they end up being characters only slightly better than those from a drinking house (색주가). In any case, whether modern or not, beauty, that is, beauty of mind and spirit (心性美), surprisingly influences the beautiful appearance (美態), so to become a modern beauty, one must cultivate a modern person's disposition, mood, and sentiment (情操).
<End>

美人의 絶種
沈熏
(Extinction of Beauties
Sim Hun)

I am a person lacking not only amorous fortune (艶福) but even the fortune of sight (眼福), so let alone in my lifetime (生時), I have never met a beauty equipped with all conditions even in my dreams. I do not think there could have existed, nor is there any reason for there to exist, a peerless beauty like a jewel (寶玉) without a single flaw (徵瑕) and with neither ear missing, throughout heaven and earth, past and present, east and west.

That too differs according to each person's age (年期); in adolescence, it is the norm for any woman to appear as a beauty, while in old age, no matter how exquisite the beauty (절색), she will appear as nothing but a completely useless object. When one is acting immaturely, one seems to indulge (탐닉) only in the beauty of appearance or physique, but after gaining some sense (지각), it seems one demands, above all, the beauty of a trustworthy (미덤성) and virtuous (현숙한) "heart" (마음). This "heart" beauty (「마」美) must be what led the poet "Goethe" (꾀-테), who had loved much, to say, "Woman eternally leads mankind forward." <45>
(From page fifty-five)
If I must speak of a model of modern beauty (現代的美人), that is, a "modern girl" (모-던) in a good sense, she must be an excellent "cook" (쿡) whose hands drip with the taste of Ajinomoto (味の素) when she enters the kitchen (부억); a skilled seamstress (針母) when she holds a needle; a mother (오마니) when looking after children; a "mannequin girl" (마네킨) when she changes into new clothes and goes out; a "stick girl" (스틕 - perhaps 'stylish girl' or companion) when going for a walk as a companion; she must understand "cinema" (키네마), know how to watch dance (舞踊), and upon returning and entering the bedroom, become a "revue girl" (레뷰-) herself. However, that is all an unreasonable demand (無理의 注文); just as long as she is pleasing to my eye in some aspect, for us "proletariat" (풀레타리아), it should be enough to be satisfied if her body is sturdy and possesses healthy beauty (健康美). This is because a man's desire (慾心) is truly limitless (限定이업는 것이니요).<13>
<13, 44-45>

**Summary**

Yŏm Sangsŏp defines the "modern beauty" of 1930s Korea as diverging from traditional "oriental" ideals, favoring intellect, dynamism, a "jazzy" and somewhat masculine demeanor, sensuality, and broad knowledge including Western arts and sports. He notes the difficulty of finding or depicting such women fully formed in colonial Chosŏn due to social constraints and insufficient "cultivation," emphasizing that inner character (心性美) remains crucial. Sim Hun, in contrast, expresses skepticism about finding "perfect" beauties, suggesting perceptions change with age and experience. He prioritizes inner virtue ("heart beauty") and outlines an almost superhuman ideal "modern girl" skilled in cooking, sewing, childcare, fashion, and companionship, ultimately concluding that for the "proletariat," a healthy physique (健康美) is a more realistic and sufficient standard, acknowledging the limitlessness of male desire. These articles are interesting to historians as they reveal the complex negotiation of modernity, Western influence ("jazz," "Charleston," sports, cinema), and traditional gender roles in colonial Korea. They highlight the anxieties and evolving expectations surrounding women's appearances, education, capabilities, and character, reflecting both elite intellectual discourse (Yŏm) and perhaps a more grounded, class-conscious perspective (Sim's mention of the proletariat and focus on health/practical skills).

현대미인의 특색이 무엇인지는 알수업스나 엇잿든 소위 동양적 미인은 非현대적일 것이다. 정서적보다는 이지적이오 정적인보다는 동적이오 숙녀적임보다는 코-켓트하고 아담하거나 端麗하다느니 보다는 짜즈적인 점에서 현대미를 엿볼 것이다. 요컨대 春鶯舞는 고전적이오 「찰스톤」은 현대적이다. 미인의 표준도 여기에 따를 것이다.
그러면 구체적으로 현대미인의 모든 요소는 엇더한 것인가? 이것은 좀처럼 맛나보지 못하나니 輪廓이나마 그리기가 어려우나 무엇보다도 눈은 육감적이여야 할 것이오. 발육이 豊艶하면서도 체격이 완실하여야 할 것이다. *弱의 미는 비현대적이기 때문이다. 그러고 동작이 극단적으로 「리드이캘」하야 육체의 線美뿐만 아니라 동작 전체가 미묘한 線의 교향악적 효과를 내이고 농후한 색조를 放射하야 眩惑의 미를 가초야 할 것이다. 그리고 수족의 미를 더욱 발휘하면서도 擧止는 남성적이어야 할 것이다. 그 외에 미를 간접적 효과잇게 하는 것은 음성과 지식과 취미에 잇는 고로 현대미인이 되랴면 성악의 「가갸」라도 알어야할 알 현대적 유행어와 학술상술어를 자유자재로 조종하고 영화, 음악, 미술, 문학 등등 방면은 毋論이오 玉突, 꼴프 기타 스포-쓰 방면에도 상식과 약간의 기술을 가저야 할 것이다.

그 다음에 내가 소설에 그리는 미인은 엇더한 것인가? 나는 「로맨틔슴」의 소설을 쓰지 안으니까 「히-로인」은 반드시 미인만을 그림에 한하지 안치만은 미인을 그린다면 현대물을 쓰느니만치 如上의 표준으로 쓰는 경우가 만켓지만은 그보다도 성격을 주로＜44＞ 보는 고로 그 「타입」에 대하야는 성격에 마추어나감으로 수시로 변해가는 것도 당연한 일이라 하겟다. 그러나 現下의 조선에 잇서서는 아모리 「모-던, 껄」이라 하야도 사회사정이라든지 舊慣의 영향이라든지 하는 관계로 충분한 「모-던, 껄」을 그리기가 어려운 때도 만타. 그뿐아니라 간혹 현대미인으로 내세워도 부끄럽지 안흔 여성이 실재하기도하고 또 소설인물로 취급할 수도 업지 안치만은 아즉 그들의 교양이 상식으로만도 현대미인 혹은 「모-던, 껄」이 되기에는 아즉 淺劣하기 때문에 엇던 경우에는 색주가보담 조곰 나흔 인물이 되고마는 경우가 만타. 엇잿든 현대적이든 아니든 미인이라는 것은 心性美라는 것은 의외로 그 美態에 영향을 주는 것임으로 현대미인이 되랴면 현대인다운 심성과 기분과 정조를 길러야 할 것이다.
