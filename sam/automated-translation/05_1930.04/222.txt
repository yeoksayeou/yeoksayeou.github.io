**Summary**

This document is a public notice from the magazine 'Samch'ŏlli', published on April 1, 1930, announcing the establishment of twenty new branch offices across various provinces of Korea. For each branch, it lists the location (province, county/city, town/village/district) and the name of the branch manager. This notice (part 2, indicating more followed) demonstrates the magazine's expanding distribution network and reach throughout colonial Korea during this period. Historians might find this list valuable for mapping the geographical spread of popular Korean media and identifying local figures involved in its dissemination, providing insight into communication networks and regional cultural engagement under Japanese rule. The use of Japanese administrative terms like '町' (chō/jŏng) in addresses like Kimch'ŏn, Yŏsu, Chŏnju, and Taejŏn also reflects the colonial context.
