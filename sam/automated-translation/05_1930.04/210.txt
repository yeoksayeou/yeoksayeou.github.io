Article Title: 갈메기 (Seagull)

Article Type: 시 (Poem)

갈메기
						파인(謠), 석영(畵)

저녁 바다로
떳다 잠겻다
갈메기 한둘은
어듸로 가느냐

사공은 잠자고
배만 남으니
꿈이나 실어다
배에 부을가

밤이 밝으면
고기 잘잡혀
논 사고 집 사는
그런 꿈 실으려...＜57＞

Seagull
						P'ain (Poem), Sŏgyŏng (Illustration)

To the evening sea
Floating, then sinking
One or two seagulls
Where are you going?

The boatman is asleep
Only the boat remains
Shall I load a dream
And pour it onto the boat?

When the night brightens
Fish are caught well
Buying fields and buying a house
To load such a dream... <57>

**Summary**

This short poem depicts seagulls over the evening sea and a sleeping boatman. The speaker contemplates loading the empty boat with a dream – specifically, a dream of prosperity where fish are caught abundantly, allowing the boatman to buy land and a house. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period (1930), subtly reflects the common person's aspiration for basic economic security and a better life (owning land and a home) amidst the hardships of the time. The simple, almost naive, dream contrasts with the likely difficult reality faced by many Koreans, making it potentially interesting to historians studying the socio-economic conditions and desires of ordinary people during that era.
