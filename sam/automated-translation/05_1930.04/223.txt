**Summary**

This article is a company announcement (社告) listing newly established sales outlets for the *Samch'ŏlli* magazine across various locations in Korea in April 1930. It provides the address or location and the name of the business (often a bookstore or trading company) or the individual responsible for sales at each new point. The list covers provinces from Hamgyŏng in the north to Kyŏngsang and Chŏlla in the south, indicating the magazine's expanding distribution network. For historians, this brief notice provides valuable insight into the logistical aspects of print media dissemination in colonial Korea, showcasing the types of businesses (local bookstores, trading companies, named halls like 'Munu Tang') involved and the geographic reach attempted by a popular Korean-language magazine. The presence of Japanese-style administrative/address terms like 'Ponjŏng' (Honmachi) and 'Samjŏngmok' (3-chōme) in Taegu also reflects the contemporary colonial context.
