**Summary**

This article presents a table comparing the number of upper and lower house representatives, the total number of eligible voters, and the number of voters per 1,000 people across thirteen countries in 1930. The countries include major powers like the USA, UK, France, Germany, Italy, and Japan, as well as several other European nations. Germany shows the highest rate of suffrage per capita, while Japan's rate is among the lowest listed. For historians studying Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it provides contemporary comparative data on suffrage and representation during a period when Koreans themselves lacked these rights within the Japanese empire. The relatively low suffrage rate listed for Japan itself (202 per 1000) compared to several European nations (especially Germany at 620) provides context for the political landscape of the colonizing power and highlights the limited nature of democracy even within Japan at the time, let alone its colonies.
