**Summary**

This document is a company announcement (社告) from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* (삼천리), dated April 1, 1930, listing the establishment of fourteen new branch offices (支社). For each branch, it provides the name, location (address), and the name of the responsible person. The branches are geographically dispersed across the Korean peninsula, including locations in North P'yŏngan, South Kyŏngsang, North Kando (Manchuria), South Chŏlla, Kangwŏn, Kaesŏng, North Ch'ungch'ŏng (implied by Yŏngdong), Pusan, and Hwanghae provinces. Historians might find this list valuable as it maps the expanding distribution network and influence of a major Korean cultural magazine during the colonial period. The inclusion of a branch in North Kando (Yongjŏng) highlights the magazine's reach into Korean communities outside the peninsula, and the specific addresses provide details about local infrastructure and businesses (like bookstores Sŏngmun Kwan and Sungnam Sŏgwan, and Ch'ŏnmyŏng Tang) involved in distribution. The list also documents the names of individuals involved in cultural dissemination at a local level across Korea and Manchuria.
