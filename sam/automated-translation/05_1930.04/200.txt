Magazine Title: 삼천리 제5호
Publication Date: 1930년 04월 01일
Article Title: 지게꾼 (A-frame Carrier)
Author: 파인(謠), 석영(畵) (P'ain (Poem), Sŏgyŏng (Illustration))
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I am an A-frame carrier
An A-frame carrier who carries loads
I carry cabbage and pumpkins
I also carry ginseng and deer antlers

I am an A-frame carrier
An A-frame carrier who carries loads
I carry the tears of wife and children
I also carry the sorrow of the people

I am an A-frame carrier
An A-frame carrier who carries loads
Carrying away the useless world
Loading up and bringing in a good world
I am an A-frame carrier! An A-frame carrier who carries loads <55>

**Summary**

This poem portrays the life and burdens of a *jigekkun*, a traditional Korean A-frame carrier. The carrier transports not only physical goods, ranging from common vegetables to valuable medicinal herbs, but also carries the intangible weight of his family's tears and the collective sorrow ("서름") of the people. The poem culminates in a powerful image of the *jigekkun* actively discarding the current "useless world" and bringing in a "good world," transforming him into a symbol of resilience and hope for a better future. For historians, the poem offers insight into the hardships faced by the common Korean populace during the colonial period (1930s). The *jigekkun* symbolizes the burdened nation, carrying both daily struggles and the collective suffering ("백성의 서름") under oppression, while also embodying the aspiration for national liberation and a new society ("조흔 세상"). The contrast between mundane loads and profound national burdens is particularly striking.
