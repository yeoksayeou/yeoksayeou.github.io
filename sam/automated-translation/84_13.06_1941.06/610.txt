Selections of German Nazi Poetry
                        Translator: Kim Sŏnbu

Preface

The development of Nazi poetry progressed in parallel with the growth of the Nazi party led by Führer Hitler, and can be seen as having started with the famous poet Eckart, whose work is recorded at the end of Hitler's book "Mein Kampf". Indeed, the voices of the German people cried out from the battlefields swept by gunpowder smoke during World War I, the screams of nameless German soldiers facing the crisis of life and death! All of this can be seen as the first birth cry of Nazi poetry.

Although Nazi literature has only appeared on the surface of German literary history for a short time, just as the Nazi party realized the political unification of Germany, Nazi literature also took its rightful place and has reached the present day. Its spirit is exceedingly grand and sound, its lines are bold, and its terminology is simple and plain. In any case, in various senses, Nazi poetry can be said to be the most full of power and light among the literatures of the world's various countries.

The few pieces translated here are all selected translations from the "Nazi Poetry Collection" recently published in Tokyo. Of course, as this is a secondary translation from the national language (Japanese), it is nothing more than a mechanical translation, merely changing the kana particles scattered among the Chinese characters into Korean. The national language translation was by Mr. Jinbo Kōtarō. - All the above from the preface and original text of the Nazi Poetry Collection -

To the Führer
Marten, Simon

1. To all of us who suffer<274>
You have given Peace
Someday far in the future, surrounded
by the youngest grandchildren

As I speak your name
Recalling Heaven's grace and all the deeds
that illuminated that path of yours,
quietly reminiscing

Truly joyfully I shall tell, praise, and reveal
your image to all our grandchildren;
Then surely, the bells of unending
gratitude shall ring out, dong-dong.

2. We always dreamed, and around that dream
The blood of the brave leaped high, the age-old dream
around which

For many thousands of years, unchanged
That thousand-year dream, leaving behind the history of "Suffering and Wandering"

Finally found its realizer
Oh dream, dream speaking of Volk and Land, Blood and Fate
That dream now sought the one to realize it
Ah, finally, you appeared in this world

As if suddenly appearing from a corner of Heaven
And so you created this strong People and State.

To the Comrade Fallen in Battle
Heinrich, Frank

Without the slightest fear, when you
Charged towards the cannon fire
- like a flaring torch -
Moreover, though you were attached
to an unnamed military unit
With only the heart to serve the Fatherland
Only obediently following Duty and Orders; <275>
When death cut short
your lives
- Beneath the dew-covered arrowroot vines
When your limbs lay abandoned
God took your souls.
Your deeds,
matched to the quiet song of Sacrifice,
Sleep deeply within the eternal soul of this Volk.

Song of the Comrade
Menzel

Friend sworn to this flag banner,
You and I are intertwined.
Where the flag shines, comrade,
There is Germany.

Beneath this glittering flag,
There are comrades who meet each other.
For those who look up at the flag from beneath it,
There is harmony and laughter.

Thus, there is not one without a homeland,
Not one living meaninglessly without hope,
Those sworn only look to the flag,
Thus heading towards a cheerful and vigorous "Life."

Gather, You
Menzel

We shall fight for Germany
Say yes, you!
The way is made
Why do you still hesitate?

Brothers call to you
Gather, you!
Only here will you gain freedom <276>
Will you endure being counted among the ruined and broken?

Hear the beating of your pulsating heart in your chest
It beats high just like ours
What are you still thinking about?
Stand up! Cry out, "Here!"

Postscript: "The Position of German Literature and Poetry"

The aspect where the Nazi literary spirit shows its greatest vitality is none other than the realm of poetry. Starting with the greatest vanguard of Nazi art, the poet of "Deutschland, erwache!" ('Germany, Awake!'), Dietrich Eckart, a poet who must be specially noted is the author of "Die Fahne hoch" ('Raise the Flag'), Horst Wessel, whose song is invariably sung whenever the German national anthem is sung today. Also, Nazi literary history has elevated the names of poets hardly considered problematic until now as pioneers: Georg Stammler and Rudolf Paulsen. The deep religious character of the songs of struggle for the Volk flows torrentially in Heinrich Anacker (1901), author of poetry collections such as "Die Trommel" ('The Drum,' 1931) and "Der Aufbau" ('The Construction,' 1933, though the original text says 喇叭 'Trumpet'). Following him, a poet to note is von Schirach (1907) of "Die Fahne der Verfolgten" ('The Flag of the Persecuted,' 1933). He also held an important position within the party. These two poets have the great merit of being the first to bring out poetic forms and emotions, long confined within the castle of the "I," for the sake of "We." Therefore, their poems are not meant to be murmured softly in solitude but must be recited aloud cheerfully together. The poet Ludwig Friedrich Bartel, of the collection "Dem Vaterland" ('To the Fatherland'), is also counted among the outstanding poets of modern Germany. The poet of the famous "Deutschland im Aufbruch" ('Germany Awakening'), Herbert Böhme (1907), is a party member born in East Germany. However, among these young poets, perhaps the most highly evaluated is the youngest, Gerhard Schumann (1911). In his collections "Der Weg zum Ganzen" ('The Path to the Whole,' 1933), "Fahne und Stern" ('Flag and Star,' 1937), "Dienst an" ('Serve,' 1937, likely miscopied title, maybe "Wir aber sind das Korn" or similar), the contemplative spirit of old Swabia, which produced Schiller, Mörike, and Hölderlin, is combined with the resolute will of the warrior. His fundamental life-feeling that defines human existence lies, to borrow his words, in the phrase: "Slave of the Führer, simultaneously protector, avenger. Among them, like them, the Reich fiercely grows (帝國은 猛烈히 生育한다)." The factory worker Kelsch, along with Rellstab, is also a presence difficult to ignore. Among the Stormtroopers (SA), a poet whose future is particularly anticipated by Goebbels is Nierentz. Let me quote one of his lines here:
- "Where the world's final graveyard proves Death and Deed, there <277> comrade! Your monument shall stand."
Thus, the factory workers and peasants, who in Germany of the previous era were rather antagonistic, intend to attend a joint festival in the Nazi century.
The poetry of Oppeln-Bronikowski offers a good example of this. Hereafter, I will briefly list the names of poets currently active:
Baumann, Heinicke, Jünemann, Schölkendorph, Schwarz, Wesenberg, Menzel who contributed many songs to the Stormtroopers, Zorn, Krupka, etc.
Contemplative poetry on German destiny and history can be seen in Brunck ("Deutsches Schicksals-Psalmbuch" - 'German Book of Destiny Psalms'), Lüdtke ("Deutschland - Erde und Schicksal" - 'Germany - Earth and Destiny'), Brockmeier ("Ewiges Deutschland" - 'Eternal Germany'), etc. Poets who sang of the "Working People," besides Kelsch, include Bregger, Krautewig, Viehprecht, Engelke-Petscholt, and others. Finally, let me note two important anthologies.
One is the government-edited "Neues Dichterbuch" ('New Book of Poets,' 1934, original text says 新學文學年鑑 'New Academic Literature Yearbook'), and the other is Herbert Böhme's compilation "Rufe in das Reich" ('Calls into the Reich,' 1934).
All Nazi poets stand under the motto expressed in the words of Hanns Johst: "The true poet is national. With all body and soul, with all speech, the poet belongs to the national body, to the holy body." -End- <279>

**Summary**

This 1941 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* presents selected translations of Nazi German poetry. The preface explains that these are secondary translations, rendered into Korean by Kim Sŏnbu from a Japanese collection translated by Jinbo Kōtarō. It praises Nazi poetry for its "grand," "sound," "bold," and "simple" nature, claiming it emerged from the spirit of WWI soldiers and paralleled the Nazi party's rise. The translated poems include works dedicated to Hitler, fallen soldiers, and Nazi camaraderie. A postscript discusses prominent Nazi poets like Eckart, Horst Wessel, Anacker, Schirach, and Schumann, highlighting themes of national unity, struggle, sacrifice, and the Führer cult, framing Nazi poetry as the true "national" voice of Germany, citing Hanns Johst.

For historians of colonial Korea, this article is highly revealing. It demonstrates the direct transmission of Axis (specifically Nazi German) ideology and cultural products into colonial Korea via Japanese mediation during the height of WWII. The positive framing of Nazi poetry, emphasizing its "strength," "vitality," and "national spirit," aligns with the militaristic and totalitarian atmosphere promoted by the Japanese colonial government at the time. The publication itself, in a mainstream Korean magazine, indicates the extent to which such propaganda permeated Korean society under Japanese rule, attempting to foster admiration for Axis powers and their ideologies. The explicit mention of translation from Japanese underscores the cultural and political conduits connecting Nazi Germany, Imperial Japan, and colonial Korea.

序言
나치스詩의 전개는 총통 히틀러에게 인솔된 나치스당의 발전과 평행하여 발달된 것으로 히틀러의 著書 「나의 鬪爭」의 卷末에 기록된 유명한 시인 「엑카-트」로부터 시작되었다고 볼 수 있다. 실로 제1차 세계대전 당시, 硝煙이 휩쓰는 전장에서 부르짗든 독일민족의 소리, 생사의 위기에 서서 소리처 웨치든 독일 무명전사의 절규! 이 모든 것이 나치스詩의 최초의 産聲이라고 볼 것이다.
나치스문학이 독일문학사의 표면에 나타나기는 아직 日淺하지만 나치스당이 독일의 정치적 통일을 실현함에 따라서 또한 나치스문학도 정통적의 座를 차지하여 오늘에 이른 것이니, 그 정신은 심히 웅대하고 건전하며 선이 굵고 용어도 單純平明하다. 엇잿든 여러 가지 의미에서 나치스詩는 세계 여러 나라의 문학가운데 가장 힘과 빛에 차고있다 할 것이다.
여기에 譯出하는 몇 편은 모다 동경서 최근 발행된 「나치스詩集」에서 抄譯한 것으로 물론 국어에서 重譯하는 것이기에 모다 한문사이에 點在한 假名吐를 조선말로 고친 정도로 機械적 번역에 不外하다. 國語譯은 神保光太郞氏 譯이였다. -이상 모다 ナチス詩集 序文 及 원문에서-

附記 「獨逸文學과 詩의 地位」
나치스 문학정신이 가장 활발한 면을 示하고 있는 것은 다른 데 있지 않고 詩의 영역에 있어서다. 나치스 예술의 최대의 선봉 「깨여라 獨逸」의 시인, 데이·트릿히·엣카-트를 비롯해서 特記하지 않을 수 없는 시인은 금일 독일국가가 불리우는 때면 반드시 함께 불리우는 「旗발을 들라」의 작자, 홀쓰트·뷧세르다. 또 나치스문학사는 거반 금일까지 문제시하지 않든 시인의 이름을 선구자로서 올려놓았다. 게올크·슈탐라-와 루돌프·파울젠이다. 민족을 위해서의 항쟁의 깊은 종교적 資性의 歌聲은 「軍鼓」(1931) 「喇叭」(1933) 등의 시집을 쓴 하인릿히·아낫카-(1901)에 奔流해 있다. 그를 다음하여 주의할 시인은 「쫏끼는 者의 旗」(1933)의 폰시랏하(1907)이다. 그는 당내부에서도 요직에 있었다. 이 두 사람의 시인은 오랫동안 「나」의 城內에 있을 시적 형식과 감정을 「우리들」때문에 처음으로 내여놓은 점에 있어서 큰 공적이 있다. 따라서 그들의 시는 獨居하여 微吟할 것이 아니고 다같이 명랑히 高吟하지 않으면 않뎀œ다. 시집 「祖國에」의 시인 르트빗히·푸리-드릿히·발델도 역시 현대독일의 우수한 시인에 세운다. 유명한 「밝어가는 獨逸」의 시인 헬바-트-베-메(1907)는 동부독일에 난 당원이다. 그러나 이들 젊은 시인중에서 가장 높이 평가되여 있기는 최년소의 껠하르트·슈-만(1911)일 것이다. 그 시집 「全體에의 길」(1933) 「旗와 星」(1937) 「奉仕하라」(1937)등에는 씨라-·메리케·헬다-린을 낳은 고풍의 슈봐-벤의 瞑想적 정신이 덧없는 전사의 의지와 결합되여 있다. 그의 인간존재를 근본적으로 규정하는 생활감정은 그의 말을 빈다면 「總統의 奴隷, 동시에 보호자, 復讐者. 그들 가운데 그들과 같이 제국은 맹렬히 생육한닊�」의 一句에 있으리라. 공장노동자께라-도 역시 렐슈와 함께 등한이하기 어려운 존재다. 돌격대원 사이에서 껫벨스에서 특히 장래가 촉망되는 시인은 니-렌쯔다. 여기에 그의 말을 하나 인용하자.
-「세계 최후의 墓地가 死와 행위를 증명하는 곳 이래야＜277＞ 동무야! 君의 기념비는 설 것이다.」
이렇게 一時代 이전의 독일에서는 되려 반목하든 공장노동자와 농민은 나치스세기에 있어서는 합동의 제전에 臨려고 한다.
오폔벨오詩는 이에 好箇의 例證이다. 이하 간단히, 현재 활약하고 있는 시인의 이름을 들어보겠다.
보-만, 하이니께, 유-네만, 쉘컌돌푸, 슈발츠, 베-젠벨크, 돌격대에 많은 노래를 보낸 멘췔, 쵸룬, 크르-푸카 등이다.
독일운명과 역사에 觀照詩는 부른크(「獨逸의 運命詩篇」) 륫트케(「獨逸-土와 運命」) 부록크마이아- (「久遠의 獨逸」) 등에서 볼 수 있다. 「일하는 民族」을 노래한 시인은 켈슈를 除하고는 부레-까, 크라우떼비스, 뷔-푸레히트, 엔껠케펫춀트, 등이 있다. 최후로 두 개의 중요한 안트로기-를 記하여 두자.
하나는 정부에서 편집하는 「新學文學年鑑」(1934)고, 또 다른 하나는 헬바-트·베-메의 編纂하는 「帝國을 求하는 소리」(1934)다.
나치스 시인은 모다 「眞正한 詩人은 民族的이다. 심신을 다하여 言說을 다하여 시인은 국민신체에 聖體에 속한다.」(한쓰·요-스트)라는 언어의 表議下에 서 있다. -끝-＜279＞
