Article Title: 世界巨頭의 映畵觀 (Views of World Leaders on Film)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Article/Essay)

**Views of World Leaders on Film**

1. Foreign Minister Matsuoka (Japan)
I believe that film will play an even more important role from now on. Film is the best way to make foreign countries understand Japan's affairs (日本의 일). Film is better than a million words or writings. Film enters the human mind more quickly than any elegant writing or things heard by ear. Therefore, one could say that film influences the fate of the nation.

2. President Hitler (Germany)
The victory of an ideology is hastened the more propaganda is conducted on a large scale among all people. (Among propaganda methods, film is the fastest.)

3. Prime Minister Mussolini (Italy)
Film is the strongest weapon today.

4. Lenin (Russia)
For us, the most important art is film.<195>

**Summary**

This 1941 article compiles statements from prominent world leaders – Matsuoka (Japan), Hitler (Germany), Mussolini (Italy), and Lenin (Russia) – emphasizing the power and importance of film. They view cinema primarily as a highly effective tool for propaganda, national promotion, ideological dissemination, and wielding influence, deeming it faster and more potent than traditional writing or speech. For historians of colonial Korea, this article is significant as it was published during the height of Japanese wartime mobilization. It reflects the Axis powers' focus on film propaganda and intriguingly includes Lenin, perhaps to underscore the universal recognition of cinema's power, even by a communist figure, thereby legitimizing its use for state purposes in the eyes of the colonial Korean readership.

世界巨頭의 映畵觀
1, 松岡外務大臣 (日本)
영화는 이제로부터 더욱 중요한 역할은 하리라고 생각한다. 日本의 일을 外國에 解離시키는 데는 영화가 제일이다. 百万言의 말과 글보다 영화가 제일이다. 어떠한 高雅한 문장이라든가, 또는 귀로 듣는 것보다도 영화가 제일 빠르게 인간의 머리에 들어간다. 그럼으로 영화는 국운을 좌우한다고도 말할 수 있다.
2, 힛틀러 總統 (獨逸)
사상의 승리는 선전이 모든 인간 사이에 대규모로 이루어지면 그만치 빨라진다.(선전 중에는 영화가 제일 빠르다.)
3, 뭇소리니 首相 (伊太利)
영화는 오늘날 최강의 무기다.
4, 레-닌 (露西亞)
우리들에게 있어서 가장 중요한 예술은 영화다.＜195＞
