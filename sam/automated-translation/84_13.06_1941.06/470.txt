Films Awarded the Ministry of Education Prize for Showa 15 [1940]
5 Dramatic Films
3 Cultural Films

The works for the Showa 15 [1940] Ministry of Education Award (文部大臣賞), the highest honor in the film world based on Film Law No. 10 (映畵法 第10號), were undergoing selection (詮衡) by the Ministry of Education Film Improvement Committee (文部省 映畵改善委員會). The prize money is 3,000 Yen (圓) each, and the following works have been decided.

I. Dramatic Films (劇映畫)
Tōichi Hassei (?) 『Kojima no Haru (小島の春)』 Directed by Toyota Shirō (豐田四郞)
Tōhō Eiga (東寶映畵) 『Moyuru Ōzora (燃ゆる大空)』 Directed by Abe Yutaka (阿部豊)
Nikkatsu (日活) 『Kaze no Matasaburō (風の又三郞)』 Directed by Shima Kōji (島耕二)
Shōchiku (松竹) 『Nishizumi Senshachō-den (西住戰車長傳)』 Directed by Yoshimura Kōzaburō (吉村公三郞)
Shōchiku Tokusaku (松竹特作) 『Naniwa Onna (浪花女)』 Directed by Mizoguchi Kenji (溝口健二)

II. Cultural Films (文化映畵)
Riken Kagaku Eiga (理硏科學映畵) 『Aru Hi no Higata (或る日の干潟)』
Ōmi/Tōnichi (?) (大海·東日) 『Sumiyaku Hito-bito (炭燒く人々)』
Shōchiku Bunka Eiga (松竹文化映畵) 『Ujō (鵜匠)』<225>

**Summary**

This article from the June 1941 issue of the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* lists the recipients of the Japanese Ministry of Education Award (Monbudaijin-shō) for films produced in Showa 15 (1940). It names five dramatic films and three cultural films, along with their directors and production companies, noting that each award carried a prize of 3,000 Yen. The selection was made by the Ministry of Education Film Improvement Committee under the authority of Film Law No. 10. For historians, this piece is interesting as it demonstrates the reach of Japanese imperial cultural policy into colonial Korea, publicizing prestigious Japanese awards in a Korean publication. It highlights which films, likely aligning with national policy aims (e.g., war films like *Moyuru Ōzora* and *Nishizumi Senshachō-den*, or culturally significant pieces), were being promoted by the Japanese state during this period of heightened wartime mobilization and assimilation policy.
