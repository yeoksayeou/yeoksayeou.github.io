First Round of Film Personnel Registrants, 58 Film Professionals Registered

Following the mainland (內地), the Korean Film Ordinance (朝鮮映畵令) was put into effect starting August 1, Shōwa 15 (1940). According to its provisions, all film-related personnel engaged in film production, such as directors, actors, or cinematographers, were required to register. A six-month grace period was given until January 31st last, mandating registration for everyone to strive for the vigorous construction of national culture under the new system. The six-month deadline concluded on the past 31st. The number of registrants by the 31st deadline was 58. All of these individuals underwent a skills review by the Skills Examination Committee (技能審査委員會), composed of 12 officials and civilians, and further passed a background check (身元調査) conducted by the relevant police station before being registered. However, there are still about 60 film personnel who have not yet registered. It is said that these remaining individuals should register promptly, as it is not too late even now. Henceforth, any unregistered film personnel undertaking or engaging in film production will be considered to be committing an illegal act and will be subject to severe punishment. Looking at the list of the 58 film professionals who have completed registration, there are 9 directors, including Mr. Yi Kyu-hwan (李圭煥) of "Nagnae" (The Wanderer) and Mr. Ch'oe In-gyu (崔寅奎) of "Chip ŏmnŭn ch'ŏnsa" (Angels Without Homes); 42 actors, including Mr. Sim Yŏng (沈影) and Ms. Mun Ye-bong (文藝峯); and 7 cinematographers, including Mr. Yang Se-ung (梁世雄). **Summary**

This 1941 article from 'Samch'ŏlli' magazine reports on the initial registration of Korean film personnel under the Japanese colonial government's Korean Film Ordinance, enacted in August 1940. It states that 58 individuals (9 directors, 42 actors, 7 cinematographers) completed registration by the January 31st deadline after passing skills reviews and police background checks. The article lists all 58 registrants by name (in Hanja) and age. It also warns that approximately 60 unregistered film personnel must register immediately or face severe punishment for engaging in film production illegally, emphasizing the move towards a "new system" for "national culture."

For historians, this article is significant as it documents a concrete step in the Japanese colonial administration's increasing control over Korean cultural production, specifically the film industry, during the wartime mobilization period. The mandatory registration, skills assessment, and background checks highlight the surveillance and regulation imposed. The list of names provides valuable data on prominent film figures active (and officially sanctioned) at this specific historical moment, offering insights into the composition and state of the Korean film world under tightening colonial rule. The language emphasizing a "new system" and "national culture" reflects the colonial rhetoric of assimilation and mobilization.

제1기 영화인 등록자, 영화인 58명 등록
昭和 15년 8월 1일부터 내지에 따러 실시를 본 조선영화령(朝鮮映畵令)에 조치 영화제작에 종사하는 감독 배우, 또는 촬영자 중 영화 관게자는 누구나 전부 등록을 하기로 되었든 것인데 지난 1월 31일로서 6개월 간의 유예기간을 주어 일제히 등록케 하고 새로운 체제 아래 국민 문화의 힘찬 건설을 꾀하기로 되었든 바 지난 31일로서 6개월의 기한이 끝났다. 31일 마감가지의 등록자 수효는 58명인데 이들은 모다 관민 12명으로 조직된 기능심사위원회(技能審査委員會)에서 기능의 심사틀 받고 다시 소관 경찰서에서 신원조사를 거처 등록된 것이다. 그러나 아직도 등록되지 않은 영화인이 60여 명이나 되는데 남어지 이들은 지금이라도 늦지 않음으로 곧 등록하도록 하여야 한다고 한다. 지금부터 등록치 않은 영화인으로서 영화 제작에 착수한다거나 행동하는 자는 위법 행위로서 엄벌에 처한다고 한다. 이제 등록을 마친 58명 영화인의 명부를 보면 감독으로는 『나그내』의 이규환(李圭煥)씨 『집 없는 천사』의 최인규(崔寅奎)씨를 비롯하야 9명 연기자는 심영(沈影) 문예봉(文藝峯)씨 등 42명 촬영자는 양세웅(梁世雄)씨 등 7명인데 그들의 씨명을 보면 다음과 같다.
