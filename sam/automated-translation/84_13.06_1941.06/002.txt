Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Reflection/Editorial Postscript)

The Works I Anticipate

Regarding the kind of works I seek for this upcoming novel competition sponsored by the Total Mobilization League, I have no particular categories prepared. While I hope for novels that are healthy, cheerful, interesting, and imbued with the spirit of total mobilization, I wish to reject those that seem like mere dressings-up of the Total Mobilization League's practical outlines. Artistic fragrance is, of course, necessary, but I wonder if becoming too constrained is advisable. Rather than contrived, sycophantic works, I hope for the spirit of total mobilization, flowing naturally, to be grasped and expressed within an engaging narrative.

Calling Widely for Novelists - League's Prize Competition Guidelines

The newly launched Cultural Department of the National Total Mobilization Korean League has decided to offer prizes for novels that achieve cultural support [for the war effort] and express the spirit of total mobilization. The competition regulations were announced on the 2nd. With the first prize being the Governor-General's Award, this is anticipated as the first step in the Cultural Department's proactive efforts.
【Subject Matter】 Works expressing the spirit of total mobilization.
【Length】 Within 50 pages of 400-character manuscript paper (However, attach a synopsis of no more than 2 pages).
【Prizes】 First Prize (1 winner): Governor-General's Award, 200 Yen; Second Prize (1 winner): 100 Yen Prize; Third Prize (2 winners): 50 Yen Prize each.
【Judges】 Keijō Imperial University Professor Karashima Takashi (辛島驍), Keijō Nippō Arts Department Head Terada Ei (寺田瑛), Posŏng College Faculty of Law and Literature Head Yu Chin-o (兪鎭午).
【Deadline】 July 15th of this year.
【Announcement】 Late September of this year.
(Submission Address) National Total Mobilization Korean League Cultural Department, 9 Namibeikura-chō, Keijō-fu.
【Eligibility】 Limited to residents of the Peninsula (半島).
1. Works must be in the National Language (國文 - i.e., Japanese).
1. The copyright of selected works, as well as all rights including film adaptation, dramatization, broadcasting, translation, and others, shall belong to the National Total Mobilization Korean League.
1. Submitted manuscripts will not be returned under any circumstances.
1. No inquiries regarding this competition will be answered.
1. Do not write the submitter's address or name on the submitted work; list it only on the synopsis sheet. <33>

**Summary**

This article announces a novel competition sponsored by the Cultural Department of the National Total Mobilization Korean League in 1941. Judge Terada Ei expresses his hope for submissions that are artistically sound, engaging, and naturally embody the "total mobilization spirit" rather than being overt propaganda. The competition guidelines specify the theme (total mobilization spirit), length, prizes (including the Governor-General's Award), judges (Karashima Takashi, Terada Ei, Yu Chin-o), deadline, and submission details, including the crucial requirement that works be written in Japanese and that all rights belong to the League. For historians, this article is interesting as it clearly illustrates the Japanese colonial regime's strategy of cultural mobilization during wartime, attempting to co-opt literary arts and intellectuals (including prominent Koreans like Yu Chin-o) to promote state ideology and support for the war effort under the guise of a cultural prize. The terms dictating the transfer of all rights highlight the control exerted by the sponsoring organization.

期待する作品

私は今度の總力聯盟募集の小說に對して、どのやうな作品を要求するかといふことに就ては、格別範疇の用意はありません。健康で明朗で面白い小說であり、總力精神の盛り上つてゐるものであつてほしいとは思ひますが、總力聯盟の實踐大綱に衣を着せたやうなものは排斥したいと思ひます藝術的香氣も勿論必要ですが、あまり窮屈になるのは如何かと考へます。とつてつけたやうな阿諛的作品でなく自然に流露する總力精神が、興味のうちに會得表現されてほしいと思つて居ります。

小說家を廣く呼ぶ-聯盟の懸賞募集要領

新しく發足した國民總力朝鮮聯盟文化部では文化翼贊の實を擧げ總力精神を表はした小說を懸賞募集することになり、二日その募集規程を發表したが一等には總裁賞が贈られるすで、文化部の積極的動きの第一步として期待される。

【枚數】四百字詰原稿用紙五十枚以內 (但二枚以內の梗槪を附すること)
【賞】一等一名總裁賞金二〇〇、圓二等一名賞金一〇〇圓、三等二名賞金五〇圓宛
