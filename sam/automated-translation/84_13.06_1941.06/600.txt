Hwarang and P'ungnyu
						Yu Cha-hu

Hwarang (花郞) is also known as P'ungwŏlchu (風月主). This began 1,365 years ago in the 37th year of King Chinhŭng of Silla, the year Pyŏngsin (丙申) (576 AD), when beautiful women were selected to first serve as Wŏnhwa (源花). However, due to rivalry and jealousy between the two Wŏnhwa leaders, Chunjŏng (俊貞) and Nammo (南毛), Chunjŏng murdered Nammo. Consequently, the Wŏnhwa group, comprising about 300 beautiful women, was disbanded. In their place, Hwarang were newly appointed. Hwarang referred to young boys with handsome appearances who were elected and given the title P'ungwŏlchu. They gathered good warriors (善士) as their followers (徒象), encouraged filial piety, fraternity, loyalty, and trustworthiness (孝悌忠信), and nurtured and selected wise and capable individuals. Kim Tae-mun's (金大問) *Hwarang Segi* (花郞世記 - Chronicle of the Hwarang) states, "Wise advisors and loyal retainers emerged from this, and good generals and brave soldiers arose from this." Hyeron Chŏn (奚論傳) says, "Rather than live without righteousness (我), it is better to die with righteousness." Yusan Chŏn (遺山傳) asks, "When a warrior faces the military, how can he dare flee north (i.e., desert)?" Hyŏngna Chŏn (亨那傳) states, "A man should certainly die in battle; how can he lie on a sickbed and die at the hands of his family?" Kŏmgun Chŏn (劍君傳) says, "If it is not righteous, even a profit of a thousand gold pieces does not move the heart." Pining Chŏn (丕寧傳) states, "Today, I die for the country above and for my confidant below." Looking at these very real records, one can understand better than anything else what the Way of the Hwarang (花郞道) of Silla was; its military spirit was extremely intense. However, in cultivating this fine spirit, ideology, and principle in ordinary times, they trained by "wandering and playing among mountains and waters, going everywhere no matter how far" (遊娛山水 無遠不至). That is, while freely running, playing, singing, and making music, amidst their mutual enjoyment and pleasure (相悅互樂), they observed people's character (邪止), diligently selected the wise, and promoted the capable. This enjoyment, pleasure, singing, and music (娛悅歌樂) were all subjects for testing, honing filial piety, fraternity, loyalty, and trustworthiness, and mutually polishing righteousness and morality (道義). The spirit of Silla's Hwarang Way is truly the highest peak among our points of pride, and it is something I wish could be revived at any time to become our Way of Joy (樂道).

Just as the ancient saying goes, "The hides of a thousand sheep are not worth the hide of a single tiger," no matter how many myriad *p'ungnyu* tunes there may be called this or that, I believe there is no *p'ungnyu* that can surpass this Silla Hwarang *p'ungnyu*. I believe it would not be too difficult to concretely revive this Silla Hwarang Way's music (花郞道樂). That is, one could take the melodies of that era as the main element and create various lively and magnificent performances and plays (노름노리) suitable for the new system (新體制). <270> However, what is truly difficult is the performance and play of dedicating one's life to the nation (一死報國) with the sincere heart (誠心) of selecting the wise and capable while mutually polishing righteousness and morality through filial piety, fraternity, loyalty, and trustworthiness. But if there is truly a leader who can properly cultivate this *p'ungnyu* of the Silla Hwarang Way, I believe that wise advisors, loyal retainers, good generals, and brave soldiers will pour forth endlessly, saying "Rather than live without righteousness (義), it is better to die with righteousness," and many great figures dedicated to the nation (求國人物), willing to go through fire and water (赴湯蹈火), will emerge.

This is what I believe without a doubt. The reason is simple. We have inherited the great blood (血) of the Silla people in our veins, which is why I state this so emphatically. The world turmoil has now entered its most intense phase (方酣之狀). Without the spirit of the Silla Hwarang Way, we will not be able to acquire the new life of the New Order in East Asia (大東亞의 新機軸). Therefore, I wish for this Hwarang *p'ungnyu* to be revived and the righteous spirit (義氣) of the Hwarang Way's dedication of one life for the country (爲國一死) to be uplifted. This was my intention whenever I spoke of sending off volunteer soldiers (지원병), urging them to display the spirit of the Silla Hwarang.

Classification of Our Ancient Folk Performing Arts

Regarding the folk arts of our ancient customs – songs, dances, music, and performance pieces (演藝品) – it goes without saying that a large part occupied a purely native breeding ground (溫床) with its unique characteristics, where seeds sprouted, blossomed, and grew within our native soil. However, among them, not a few elements flowed back and forth, rising and falling, between court music (團樂) and folk music (鄕土樂), thus developing through mutual exchange. Furthermore, through interactions like diplomatic missions (交聘) or trade (通商), foreign music (外來樂) was partially imported and assimilated, adding some flavor to the warp and weft (經緯組織) of our folk music. The fact that our folk music successfully passed through and integrated these three major and minor streams of exchange (三交流) is because our ancestors in the past digested them well and succeeded in unifying them into our own (我有化). Now, if we delve into the numerous forms of entertainment and games that have reached this stage (升堂入室), clearing away the multitude of forms, and examine them broadly by roughly organizing and extracting their comparable and common patterns and lines (比類와 通性의 典局型線), we can discern the development of the following classifications:

1.  Fatigue Relief Principle (疲勞慰安主義): Mainly in the area of singing and music.
2.  Emotional Harmony Principle (感情融和主義): Mainly in the area of folk songs, music, and entertainment.
3.  Evil Aversion and Prayer Principle (厭勝祈禳主義): Mainly in the area of prayers, *narye* (儺禮 - exorcism rites), Ch'ŏyong dance (處容歌舞), lion dance (獅子舞), mask dances, songs, and preventive ceremonies.
4.  Literary Veneration and Exegesis Principle (尙文訓詁主義): Areas like civil service exam composition contests (白日場), poetry gatherings (詩會), village wine-drinking rites (鄕飮酒禮), etc.
5.  Martial Veneration and National Unity Principle (崇武單國主義): Areas like Go (圍碁 - Baduk), Korean chess (將棊), archery (弓術), horseback riding (馳馬), mounted archery (騎射), stick fighting games (擊棒戰), stone throwing fights (擲石戰), spear throwing games (射槍戱), polo (打毬), sword dance (劍舞), hunting (出獵), etc.
6.  Gambling and Competition Ideology (賭運勝負思想): Gambling games area.
7.  Faith and Joy Principle (信仰喜悅主義): Areas like chanting sutras for blessings (福田誦經), P'algwanhoe (八關法 - Buddhist festival), Yŏnggo (迎鼓 - Puyŏ harvest festival), Much'ŏn (舞天 - Ye harvest festival), Ch'ŏn'gun (天君 <271> - Mahan ritual leader), Tongmaeng (東盟 - Koguryŏ harvest festival), Kyoch'ŏn (郊天 - sacrifice to Heaven), Yehwaak (豫和樂), Paech'ŏnsaŏk (拜天射抑 - praying to heaven, archery suppression?), Yŏndŭnghoe (燃燈會 - Lantern Festival), Yŏraegok (如來曲 - Buddha music), Pŏpko (法鼓 - Dharma drum), Kungmuak (國巫樂 - state shaman music) (superstition), etc.
8.  Industry Promotion Principle (産業獎勵主義): Areas like Kabaehoe So-gok (嘉俳會蘇曲 - Silla weaving contest song), Kaeboksinje (開福神祭 - rite for god of fortune), Kinyŏnje (祈年祭 - rite for good harvest), Hasŏlje (賀雪祭 - rite celebrating snow), Kiuje (祈雨祭 - rain-making rite), P'ungnyŏnsong (豊年頌 - ode to a bountiful year), Kŏndŭkhwajŏk (建纛禾積 - setting up banners and stacking grain?), *Panga T'aryŏng* (방아打令 - milling song), Kyŏngjiktoga (畊織圖歌 - songs of farming and weaving), etc.
9.  Satire and Moral Encouragement Principle (諷剃勸懲主義): Areas like floats with mountain scenes (山車花隊), Sokhong Kamu (續紅歌舞 - red continuation song and dance?), Taeisin Kwanbi Kamu (大臣舘婢歌舞 - song and dance of minister's housemaid?), etc.
10. National Character Purification Principle (國格淨化主義): Areas like Silla Hwarang (新羅花郞), Hoemaengje (會盟祭 - alliance oath ceremony), etc.
Besides these, one could branch out further if classifying publicly, but generally, development and growth occurred over long periods within such categories.

Generally, among the folk music groups listed above, Puyŏ's Yŏnggo (迎鼓), Ye-Maek's Much'ŏn (舞天), Mahan's Ch'ŏn'gun (天君), Koguryŏ's Tongmaeng Hoe (東盟會) and Myŏngju Kok (溟州曲), Silla's Kabaehoe So-gok (嘉俳會蘇曲), Hwarang P'ungnyu (花郞風流), Sambul Mudŭkhŭi (三佛無得戱), Ch'ŏyong Kamu (處容歌舞), Hwangch'angnang Kŏmmu (黃倡郞劍舞), Hyangak Oji (鄕樂五枝), Kyeŭm (禊飮), P'agurak (抛毬樂), Koryŏ's K'una (驅儺 - exorcism), Chosŏn's Pongch'ang Yugyŏktae (棒槍遊擊隊 - stick and spear guerrilla unit?), Kunghŭi (弓戱), Sŭnggangnori (僧角觝), Hyangŭm Chuŭi (鄕飮酒儀 - village drinking ritual), Chŏllyŏk Yu (田獵遊 - field hunting outing), Ch'ŏllyŏphoe (川獵會), P'ungnyŏnsong (豊年頌), Hiusong (喜雨頌), Kinyŏnje (祈年祭), Nongga Ch'angak (農歌唱樂 - farmer's song music), Kugi Nongga (九妓農歌), Sesohoe Pyŏnjŏn (洗鋤會邊戰 - hoe washing meeting border fight?), Insŭng (引繩 - tug-of-war?), Kŏndŭkhwajŏk (建纛禾積), Ch'ŏngch'un Kyŏngnohoe (靑春敬老會), T'aep'yŏnggo (太平鼓), Ch'unch'ŏnhŭi (鞦韆戱 - swinging game), Yudu Yŏn (流頭宴), Pŏmchuyuhoe (泛舟遊會 - boating outing meeting), Kukp'unghoe (菊楓會 - chrysanthemum maple meeting?), Yŏngyanghoe (迎陽會), Changgu Hŭi (藏鉤戱), Kyŏngjikka Hŭi (畊織歌戱 - farming/weaving song game) – I wish these could be researched, practiced, and revived. These are generally related to Faith, Edification (信仰敎化), Martial Promotion (揚武), and Industry Encouragement (興業), and I believe they would bring great benefit (稗益) to revitalizing people's hearts and uplifting the spirit. Although none are without merit, below I will chatter about a few that I often dream of reviving while closing my book and sitting in contemplation during my usual reading, as my earnest plea for their revival first. <273>

**Summary**

The author, Yu Cha-hu, argues passionately for the revival of Silla's Hwarang spirit (花郞道, Hwarangdo) and its associated *p'ungnyu* (風流), emphasizing its core values of loyalty, righteousness, martial prowess, and self-sacrifice for the nation (一死報國). He contrasts this ideal with other forms of traditional Korean folk arts (鄕土樂), which he categorizes by purpose (e.g., relaxation, social harmony, ritual, martial arts, industry promotion). He lists numerous historical examples of Korean performing arts, rituals, and games from ancient times through the Chosŏn Dynasty, urging the revival of specific traditions he deems beneficial for spiritual and national uplift.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is significant for its attempt to leverage Korean tradition, specifically the militaristic and disciplined aspects of the Hwarang, to resonate with the contemporary wartime atmosphere of 1941. The author explicitly links the revival of Hwarang spirit to achieving the "new life of the New Order in East Asia" and mentions his encouragement for volunteer soldiers (지원병) to embody this spirit. This framing suggests an effort to align Korean historical identity with the goals of the Japanese Empire, potentially as a form of collaborationist rhetoric or a way to preserve Korean cultural elements by presenting them as compatible with imperial objectives. Historians might find the explicit connection between Hwarang revival, the "New Order," and volunteer soldiers particularly revealing of how some Korean intellectuals navigated or participated in the ideological currents of the late colonial period.

花郞과 風流
						柳子厚
花郞은 一名 風月主니 이것은 新羅 眞興王 37년 丙申(西 576) 거금 1,365년 전에 미녀를 간택하여 源花를 始奉하였다가 源花의 兩頭領인 俊貞과 南毛 사이에 爭娟相妬로 俊貞이 南毛를 살해하니 300여명의 美人인 源花黨은 해산되고 그 대신으로 곳 花奉을 更奉하니 花郞이란 童男의 容儀가 단정한 者로 選擧하여 風月主라 號하고 善士를 網羅하여 徒象을 삼아 孝悌忠信을 勸勵하여 賢能人士를 養擇하던 것이다. 金大問의 花郞世記에 賢佐忠臣이 從此而秀하고 良將勇卒이 由是而生이라 한 것이며 奚論傳에 與其無我而生인댄 不若有我而死이라 한 것이며 遺山傳에 士當軍無退는 豈敢奔北乎아한 것이라든가 亨那傳에 丈夫-固當兵死라 豈可臥狀席死家人之手乎아한 것이라든지 劍君傳에 苟非其義면 千金之利-不勳心이라 한 것이라든지 丕寧傳에 吾-今日에 上爲國家요 下爲知己死라 한 實實在在의 기록들을 보면 新羅의 花郞道라 하는 것은 무엇보다 잘 알 수 있는 것으로 군국의 사상이 대단 강열하든 것이다. 그런데 그 好箇의 정신과 사상과 主義를 평소에 수련함에 있어서는 遊娛山水 無遠不至하여 가면서 즉 마음대로 뛰고 놀며 노래하며 음악하면서 그 相悅互樂하는데 가운데에서 사람에 邪止을 보와 擇賢擧能에 근면하였으니 娛悅歌樂이 이것이 다- 孝悌忠信을 砥礪하며 道義를 相磨하는 試驗의 과목이었든 것이었었다. 이 신라의 화랑도의 정신은 實노 우리의 자랑꺼리 가운데에 최고봉으로서 이것마는 언제든지 부활하여 우리의 樂道로 하였으면 하는 바이다.
千孤의 皮가 一虎의 皮만 못하다는 古語와 같이 萬千의 풍류가락이 무엇이니 무엇이니 하여도 이 신라의 花郞풍류를 超駕할 풍류는 없을 줄노 안다. 이 신라의 花郞道樂을 구체적으로 부활함에는 그다지 어렵지 아니한 줄노 안다. 즉 그 시대의 가락을 주로 삼아 신체제에 맛도록 활발웅장하게 여러 가지로 노름노리를 하면 될 것이다.＜270＞ 그러나 참으로 어려운 것은 孝悌忠信으로 道義를 相磨하면서 擇賢選能하는 誠心으로 一死報國의 노름노리가 이것이다. 그러나 참으로 지도자가 있어 이 신라의 화랑도의 풍류를 잘 교화하면 賢佐忠臣과 良將勇卒이 무수히 쏟아저 나와 與具無義而生인댄 不若有義而死라 하고 赴湯蹈火의 위대한 求國人物이 만히 나올 줄노 믿는 바이다.
이것은 나의 確信不疑하는 바이다. 그 이유는 단순하다. 우리는 신라인의 그 위대한 피(血)를 우리의 혈관에 승계하여 있으므로 斷言을 割下하는 바이다. 지금 세계동란이 方酣之狀에 드러왔다. 우리에게는 신라의 화랑도의 정신이 없이는 大東亞의 新機軸의 신생활을 획득할 수 없을 것이다. 그럼으로 이 화랑풍류을 부활하여 화랑도의 爲國一死의 義氣를 昻揚하였으면 하는 바이다. 나는 지원병을 보내는 말에는 언제든지 신라 화랑의 정신을 발휘하라 하는 것이 이 뜻이었었다.

우리 古俗의 演藝의 分類
우리 古俗에 있어서 향토적 예술인 歌舞와 음악과 演藝品은 이것이 물론 아주 우리의 향토에서 그 고유한 특색이 있는 純然한 溫床 가운데서 種苗發芽하여 開花成長된 것이 많은 부분을 점령하고 있었음은 말할 것도 없거니와 그 중에는 간혹 궁중의 團樂의 부분에서 향토악으로 향토악의 부분에서 궁중의 국악으로 이와같이 交流昇降하여 出入發達된 것이 적지 아니하며 또는 때로 交聘이니 通商이니 하는 왕래의 관계로 외래악이 부분적으로 수입동화되어 우리의 鄕土樂을 經緯組織하는데 다소 加味를 가져온 것도 없지 아니하였다. 이와 같이 우리의 향토악이 三交流의 大中小인 滿集을 잘 通過會成하였음은 과거의 향토의 우리들이 그것을 잘 소화하여 我有化의 統一成功하였던 까닭이다. 지금 이 우리의 향토악이 그와 같은 階段을 발바가지고 升堂入室된 허다한 오락과 유희의 群像을 헤치고 들어가서 그 比類와 通性의 典局型線을 槪整抽列하여 대량적으로 관찰하여 보면 左와 如한 분류의 발달을 指示하고 있음을 알 수가 있는 바이다.
