**Body Text Translation:**
Precious Document: Answering the President of the United States.
						German President Hitler
1
The President of the United States sent me a telegram. Its unique content, as already noted in the newspapers, states Mr. Roosevelt’s opinion that it is a clear fact that hundreds of millions of people throughout the world are constantly in fear that war might break out again, and perhaps even multiple wars. Mr. Roosevelt said that he represents this view, as do the citizens of the United States, and indeed all the peoples of the entire Western Hemisphere.
Regarding this, the first thing I would like to say is that it is natural that such fear of war has permeated humanity since ancient times.
For example, since the Versailles Peace Treaty of 1919 until 1938, war broke out 14 times. However, Germany did not participate in any of these wars. But the nations of the "Western Hemisphere" that President Roosevelt speaks of did participate in them. Furthermore, during one period, 26 violent interventions and sanctions accompanied by bloody tragedies occurred. Germany did not participate in these even once. However, the United States of America has carried out military interventions six times since 1918. Soviet Russia has engaged in war and military actions involving violence ten times since 1918. Germany had no relation whatsoever to these cases, nor did it cause such incidents.
Therefore, I think it is wrong to say that Germany is responsible for the fact that the peoples of Europe and beyond Europe fear war and are trembling that a real war might break out right now. Such fear of war has arisen solely because of the licentious, falsehood-filled, vile agitation of the newspapers. They distribute extremely unpleasant pamphlets concerning foreign leaders, create artificial panic, and ultimately cause an unruly situation, like fearing that planets might collide. I believe that if the responsible governments themselves, or their official organs, adopted the most humble attitude regarding relations between peoples, and especially regarding the internal affairs of other nations, pretending to love truth, the danger of war breaking out would disappear, and the calm state we all desire would arrive.

2
Mr. Roosevelt stated in his telegram that even if a Great War breaks out on another continent, it will have significant effects during the war and for generations afterward.

Reply
Regarding this point, no one pays more attention than the German people. Although American constitutional scholars, historians, and history professors confirm that Germany, like any other nation, bears no responsibility for the outbreak of the World War, the German people were burdened with heavy loads by the Treaty of Versailles peace treaty that could not be resolved even after a hundred years. However, unless the whole world is intentionally drawn into planned disputes through the conclusion of favorably adjusted treaty obligations, I do not think that every war will result in dragging the entire surrounding world, that is, the whole world, into destruction. Because the world has experienced incessant wars for hundreds of years in the past and will continue to do so in the future—as I confirmed at the beginning of this reply—even in the last few decades, if Mr. Roosevelt's opinion were correct, humanity would have to suffer the sum total of all the effects received from such wars for several million more years.

3
Mr. Roosevelt said that he once told me he hoped political, economic, and social problems would be adjusted by peaceful means, without resorting to force of arms.

Reply
I have always held this view from the beginning. And as history has revealed, I have adjusted necessary political, economic, and social problems without using force.
However, precisely this peaceful adjustment became extremely difficult due to the agitation of politicians and journalists who had absolutely no relation whatsoever to the problems.

4
Mr. Roosevelt said, "The tide of the times" has now brought back the use of force. If such threats continue, a large part of the world will fall into ruin.

Reply
As far as Germany knows, we have never heard that other nations are pressured by such threats. Most days, democratic newspapers carry fabricated articles about such threats. Every day, articles appear claiming Germany is mobilizing, landing troops, and plundering even nations with which we live peacefully and maintain friendly relations. <120>

5
Furthermore, Mr. Roosevelt said that if war breaks out, victorious nations, defeated nations, and neutral nations will all suffer hardships.

Reply
As a politician, I have held this conviction for 20 years, but at that time, responsible politicians in the United States generally did not hold the same view as me when participating in the Great War and at the end of the Great War.

6
Mr. Roosevelt said that it is the duty of the leaders of great nations to save peoples from threatening anxiety.

Reply
If this statement is to be correct, then it is careless for national leaders who possess such rights not to suppress newspapers that provoke war and rescue the world from warlike anxiety. I think it deserves legal sanction as well. Furthermore, what I cannot understand is why these responsible leaders do not try to foster diplomatic relations between nations but instead make diplomatic relations difficult and attempt to destroy them through actions like recalling ambassadors without any reason.

7
Mr. Roosevelt said that three independent nations in Europe and one independent nation in Africa have lost their existence.

Reply
I do not know which countries are meant by these three nations in Europe, but if it refers to the territories returned to Germany, I must call the President's attention to a historical error. These peoples did not lose their independent existence in Europe now; rather, they lost their independent existence already in 1918 when the shining promises were broken, causing them to leave their community, and independence was forced upon them as unwilling nations, making them subordinate to countries they disliked at that time.
Furthermore, the statement regarding one African nation that lost its freedom is a misunderstanding. It is not just one nation in Africa that lost its freedom; almost all the inhabitants who have lived on this continent since ancient times have lost their freedom, succumbing to the violence of other nations. Moroccans, Tunisians (쮸니스人), other Arabs (아라비야人), and Black people (黑人) have all fallen under foreign violence. On their swords and bombs, it was certainly not engraved "Made in Germany (獨逸製)" but "Made by Democracy (데모크라시-製)".

8
Mr. Roosevelt said that although he thinks it is surely not true, there are rumors of plans to attack the remaining independent nations <121> as well.

Reply
If you let me speak, such groundless insinuations harm the security and peace of the world. In other words, they cause small nations to become fearful or nervous. And if Mr. Roosevelt says this knowing the actual facts, he should name the nations threatened by attack and their attackers. Then, this simple explanation would extinguish those repetitive accusations.

9
Mr. Roosevelt said that unless a rational method to prevent incidents from occurring is discovered, it is clear that the world's current state is heading down the path to ruin. And furthermore, he vouched that I and the German people do not want war, but if this is true, there should be no need to start a war.
I reply:
What I cannot but affirm again now is,
First, I did not start the war.
Second, I have long made statements hating war and especially hating warmongers.
Third, for what reason on earth must I start a war? Even I absolutely cannot understand it.
I would be grateful if Mr. Roosevelt would provide an explanation regarding this point.

10
In Mr. Roosevelt's opinion, except in cases where the reason for self-defense is clear, it is unacceptable to the nations of the world that any one government should possess the right or coercive power to use violence or bring about the results of war against other nations.

Reply
I believe this view is the view of all rational human beings. Therefore, it was customary for both participants in any war to cite the same clear reason of self-defense, and Mr. Roosevelt is also participating. Such institutions (likely refers to a War Cause Investigation Committee - Reader's Note) probably cannot provide a just solution to this problem.
Furthermore, there is no room for doubt that America did not participate in the Great War for clear self-defense.
An investigation committee established by President Roosevelt himself investigated the reasons for America's participation in the Great War and concluded that this participation was essentially carried out from a purely capitalistic viewpoint. Nevertheless, no practical results have been obtained since then. Therefore, what we hope is that at least the United States of North America (北米合衆國) will itself demonstrate this precious principle for the future and intends to start a war against any country only when the fact of clear self-defense actually occurs.

11
Mr. Roosevelt further stated that his making such a loud noise is not out of greed, weakness, or fear, but out of love for humanity.

Reply
If America's powerful cry of humanitarian love had been issued at the most appropriate time, and if practical value had been given to this cry, it would have at least prevented the establishment of a treaty like the Versailles Memorandum (벨사유覺書), which became the source of the greatest human destruction in history.

To the Soldiers of the Wehrmacht on the Eastern Front
By my command, you marched on September 1st to defend our country against Polish (波蘭) aggression.
You have fulfilled the duties imposed upon you with exemplary coordination between the Army, Navy, and Air Force.
You fought energetically and bravely.
Today, I had the opportunity to greet our troops who occupied the armed city of Warsaw (왈소). With this day, the final battle, conveying the finest essence of the German soldier, has ended.
Together with the German people, I am proud to express my gratitude to you. Thanks to you, in whom we place unwavering trust, the nation has been reunited with the Wehrmacht and its commanders. The deeds of the fallen soldiers who sacrificed themselves for Germany in this battle, like the 2,000,000 victims of the World War, are deeply engraved in our hearts. Under the fluttering flags filled with pride and joy throughout German territory, we strengthen our unity once more and retie the straps of our helmets.
The time has come to show your faith in Germany and your spirit that fears no one.
(October 5, 1939, Berlin (伯林))

To the Soldiers on the Western Front
Soldiers of the Western Front!
The time that will decide the final destiny of the German people has come. For 300 years, the aim of the power-holders in Britain (英國) and France (佛蘭西) was allegedly to consolidate Europe, but in reality, it became an obstacle. To achieve this, they first tried to weaken Germany and make it powerless. France alone <123> declared war on Germany 31 times in two centuries.
For the past several decades, the world rulers of Britain have devised every means to obstruct German unification and have tried to prevent the supply of living necessities required for the livelihood of 80 million people.
Britain and France have currently implemented their policy wherever they go, regardless of the form of government ruling Germany. They have always confronted the German people. Today, people in responsible positions in Britain and France are spreading this objective with large leaflets (삐라). Divide Germany, it must be split into many small countries. By doing so, the German Empire loses political power. The German people will not be able to secure the right to exist on this earth.
For these reasons, they rejected all my peace proposals and declared war on September 3rd last year.
The German people bear absolutely no hatred or enmity towards the British and French peoples. But today, the German people stand at a watershed between survival and destruction.
A few weeks ago, the elite units of our Army crushed the Polish enemy, who could be called the vanguard of Britain and France, thereby removing the danger in the East.
However, Britain and France decided to attack Germany from the North. Since April 9th, the German Wehrmacht suppressed this enemy attempt before it could happen.
But now, the dreadful crisis we have anticipated for several months has arrived. Britain and France intend to carry out a major turning operation (一大轉回作作戰) through the Netherlands (和蘭) and Belgium (白耳義) in the Southeast of Europe to advance into the Ruhr region (루-루 지방).
Soldiers of the Western Front!
Therefore, now is truly the time for you to rise.
The battle starting today will decide the fate of the German nation for the next thousand years.
Now is the time to fulfill your duty.
The German people offer you their blessings.
(May 10, 1940, Berlin (伯林)) <124>

**Summary**

This article presents Adolf Hitler's point-by-point rebuttal to criticisms or concerns raised by U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt regarding the escalating war situation in 1941. Hitler denies German responsibility for the prevailing fear of war, blaming Allied actions after World War I (specifically the Treaty of Versailles) and media "agitation." He defends Germany's recent actions as rectifying historical injustices and characterizes Germany as a victim acting in self-defense, while accusing the Allied powers and the U.S. of hypocrisy and warmongering based on economic interests. The article concludes with two appended messages from Hitler to German soldiers on the Eastern (1939) and Western (1940) fronts, justifying the invasions of Poland and France/Benelux as necessary defensive actions against Allied encirclement and historical antagonism.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the publication of this lengthy defense by Hitler in a prominent Korean magazine like *Samch'ŏlli* is highly significant. It demonstrates the extent of Japanese control over information and the dissemination of Axis propaganda within colonial Korea. Publishing Hitler's justifications served Japan's interests by portraying the Axis powers (including Japan's ally, Germany) in a favorable light, legitimizing their aggressive actions, and fostering anti-Allied sentiment among the Korean populace, aligning them with the Axis worldview during a critical phase of World War II. Historians would find this article valuable as evidence of the ideological content Koreans were exposed to under Japanese rule and the efforts to shape public opinion in favor of the Axis cause.

貴重文獻 米國大統領에 答하노라.
						獨逸總統 힛틀러
1
米國大統領은 나에게 전보를 보내었읍니다. 그 독창적 내용은 이미 신문지상에 적히운 바와 같거니와 『루-즈벨트』氏 의견에 의하면 전세계 수억의 사람들이 다시 戰爭이 일지나 않을가, 그것도 여러 갈래의 전쟁이 일지나 않을가 하야 不絶히 恐怖속에 있다 함은 명백한 사실이라 합니다. 『루-즈벨트』氏가 대표하고, 合衆國의 國民이 그러하고 全西半球의 모든 국민들이 모다 그러하리라고 氏는 말했읍니다.
여기에 대해서 먼저 말슴 들이고저 하는 것은 그런 전쟁의 공포가 바로 옛날부터 인류사이에 차고 있었다는 것은 당연하다는 것입니다.
예를 들면 1919년의 벨사유講和 이래 1938년까지 14번이나 전쟁이 일어났읍니다. 그러나 獨逸은 이런 전쟁에는 한 번도 참가하지 않었지만. 大統領 루스벨트氏가 말하는 「西半球」의 諸國은 여기에 참가하였읍니다. 그러나 더욱이 한 시기에 26회의 暴力的인 干涉과 流血의 慘事를 따른 制裁가 일어났읍니다. 여기에도 獨逸은 한 번도 參加하지 않었읍니다. 그러나 아메리카 合衆國은 1918년 이래 여섯번이나 軍事的 干涉을 했읍니다. 쏘비엣트·로시아는 1918년 이래 열번이나 暴力이 따르는 전쟁과 군사적 행동을 했습니다. 獨逸은 이런 경우에도 何等 關係를 하지 않고 그런 사건에 원인될 일도 없었읍니다.
그리하여 나는 구라파 及 구라파 이외의 諸國民이 전쟁을 무서워하고 지금 곧 정말 전쟁이 일어나지 않나하고 떨고 있는 데까지는 獨逸의 책임이라고 하는 것은 잘못된 것이라고 생각합니다. 이와 같이 전쟁에 대한 공포가 일어난 것은 오직 新聞의 放縱한 허위로 채운 비열한 煽動 때문입니다. 저들은 외국의 首腦者에 관한 불쾌가 극한 팜푸렛을 배포하고 人工的의 恐慌을 일으키고, 결국에는 遊星이 부듸치지 않을가하고 공포하는 것처럼 無軌道한 사태를 일으키게 됩니다. 나는 책임있는 정부 자신이 또는 그의 발표기관이 諸國民 상호의 관계 及 특히 他國民의 內部的으로 생긴 일에 관하여 가장 겸허한 태도를 가지고 진실을 사랑하는 것처럼 한다면 전쟁이 일어날 위험은 소실되고 我等 모두가 바라고 있는 平靜한 상태가 오리라고 생각하고 있습니다.

2
루스벨트氏는 그 전보 중에 大戰爭은 다시 다른 대륙에서 일어나도 그 전쟁기간 중, 또오는 그 후 몇 세대에까지 중요한 영향을 및으리라고 말했읍니다.

答
