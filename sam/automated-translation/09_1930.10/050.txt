**Sin'gan Movement - 40,000 Members, Strongest in Hamgyŏng North & South**
Kim Hangyu (金恒圭), Central Committee Member, Sin'ganhoe (新幹會)

Our Sin'ganhoe currently has over 40,100 dedicated members and 139 branches across the peninsula. Additionally, there are 35 branches in the process of being organized, so it seems likely that the total membership will soon reach 43,000-44,000, and the number of branches will reach 150-160, indicating prosperous development. Given that this remarkable strength has been built in just three or four years since its founding in February 1927, one can understand how profoundly the Sin'gan spirit and its movement have stirred the hearts of the people of this land.

Looking at the distribution of the Sin'gan movement's strength, Kyŏngsang North and South Provinces and Hamgyŏng North and South Provinces are the most robust and active. For instance, Kyŏngsang South Province alone has 25 branches, with figures like Mr. Kim Tongp'il (金東弼) of Yŏngju (榮州), Mr. Yi Unho (李雲鎬) of Andong (安東), and Messrs. Ch'oe Yundong (崔允東) and Yu Yŏnsul (柳淵述) of Taegu (대구) all being influential leaders in their respective regions. Turning to Hamgyŏng Province, there are 24 branches in places like Wŏnsan (元山), Anbyŏn (安邊), Hamhŭng (咸興), Iwŏn (利原), Tanch'ŏn (端川), Pukch'ŏng (北靑), Hongwŏn (洪原), Chŏngp'yŏng (定平), Kilchu (吉州), Hoeryŏng (會寧), Kyŏngsŏng (鏡城), Tŏkwŏn (德原), Najin (羅南), Ch'ŏngjin (淸津), Sŏngjin (城津), and others. Among these, the most astonishing<26> is the Ŏchu (漁朱) branch within the Kyŏngsŏng (鏡城) branch in Hamgyŏng North Province. Most farmers and merchants/industrialists there have joined the branch, bringing the total membership to over 1,500, making it comparable in size to the Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) branch. Mr. Yi Towŏn (李圖遠) and others are showing steadfast effort there.

Next would be Chŏlla North and South Provinces. Chŏlla South has excellent leaders in Mokp'o (木浦), Changsŏng (長城), Kurye (求禮), Hamp'yŏng (咸平), Yŏngam (靈岩), Kwangyang (光陽), and is progressing well. In Chŏlla North, Chŏnju (全州) is the most active, followed by Namwŏn (南原). In P'yŏngan South Province, the P'yŏngyang (平壤), Chinnamp'o (鎭南浦), and Anju (安州) branches are prominent. In P'yŏngyang, Mr. Cho Mansik (曺晩植) and several others are very active.

The regions where the Sin'ganhoe movement is somewhat less broad, deep, and large would be P'yŏngan North, Ch'ungch'ŏng North/South, Hwanghae, and Kangwŏn Provinces. In P'yŏngan North, the Sŏnch'ŏn (宣川) branch, boasting 600 members with Mr. Yi Yonggil (李龍吉) and others actively working, is noteworthy, but others like Yongch'ŏn (龍川), Ŭiju (義州), and Pakch'ŏn (博川) can only be described as inactive. In Ch'ungch'ŏng South, the Hongsŏng (洪城) branch is the most consistent, where Messrs. Kim Yŏnjin (金淵鎭) and Yun Taeyŏng (尹大榮) are busy. Although there are branches in Changhowŏn (長湖院) and Tangjin (唐津), they are still minor. In Ch'ungch'ŏng North, the Ch'ŏngju (淸州) branch is influential. In Hwanghae Province, there is a branch in Chaeryŏng (載寧), but it too pales in comparison to other provinces, and there are no others, making this province perhaps the least active for the Sin'gan movement. In Kangwŏn Province, the Kangnŭng (江陵) branch is the strongest, while the Kosŏng (高城) and Wŏnju (原州) branches are not particularly flourishing.

Finally, regarding Kyŏnggi Province, Seoul is the foremost, boasting over 1,600 members. Besides Seoul, there are branches in the cities of Inch'ŏn (仁川), Suwŏn (水原), Kwangju (廣州), Kaesŏng (開城), and Kanghwa (江華).

However, since specific circumstances differ in each region, this imbalance is currently unavoidable, but I believe they will all become robust before long. This is because people in every region are gradually coming to understand our movement correctly, and because various signs lead us to believe that many influential pioneers will emerge.

Looking at the occupations of the 40,000-plus members, farmers constitute 50%, merchants 20%, laborers 20%, and the remaining 10% consist of religious workers, newspaper reporters, teachers, lawyers, doctors, unemployed individuals, etc.

Financially, large sums of money have not yet been gathered, but membership fees are accumulating considerably year by year. Lastly, the responsible persons for each organ of the headquarters are: Central Executive Committee Chairman Hŏ Hŏn (許憲); General Affairs, Hwang Sanggyu (黃尙圭); Finance, Kim Pyŏngno (金炳魯); Education, Pak Hŭido (朴熙道); Publishing, Yi Chongnin (李鍾麟); Liaison, Sŏ Chŏnghŭi (徐廷禧); Investigation, Yi Ch'unsŏk (李春塾); Organization, Kim Hangyu (金恒圭); Propaganda, Yi Kwanyong (李灌鎔), and others. The total number of Central Executive Committee members is around 40. Currently, the number of individuals related to the Sin'gan movement across all of Korea who are imprisoned or detained, starting with Hŏ Hŏn, is over 140.

**Farmer Movement - 20,000 United League Members**
Han Pyŏngnak (韓炳洛), Central Committee Member, Chosŏn Nongmin Ch'ongdongmaeng (朝鮮農民總同盟 - Korean Farmer General Alliance)

Thirty farmers' associations, organized at the county (郡), city (府), and island (島) levels scattered throughout the 13 provinces, have come together to form the current Farmer General Alliance (Nongch'ong - 農總), with the number of affiliated members reaching a substantial total of 22,041. That we have achieved such strength within several years (*數年*) of founding indicates how powerfully the farmer movement in Korea is developing.

Something to note especially is that our General Alliance, which previously organized through Provincial Federations (聯合會), Associations (組合), and Branch Units (支部班), has changed its policy based on various practical experiences in the movement. Henceforth, it is possible to separately organize what are called District Committees (地區委員會) between one farmers' association and another, taking geographical circumstances into account. Another change is that whereas previously our farmers' association members were composed primarily of tenant farmers, agricultural laborers, and owner-farmers who also engage in tenant farming,<27> from now on, the inevitably declining class of owner-farmers will also be included. There are also points regarding economic and political struggles, but as these are very serious issues, I will defer them to another occasion.

Our Farmer General Alliance, embracing 30 farmers' associations and over 22,000 members, should rightly be astonishingly vigorous in its activities. However, as you know, like our allies Noch'ong (勞總 - Labor General Alliance) and Ch'ŏngch'ong (靑總 - Youth General Alliance), we are subject to a complete ban on assemblies, so from an external perspective, there might seem to be some inactivity. In reality, even Executive Committee meetings can barely be held, only in the form of informal gatherings (懇談會). But this is due to objective circumstances...

The farmer movement is developing well in all provinces. Now, I will conclude by listing the key figures at the headquarters.

**Ch'ŏndogyo Movement - One Million Followers, One Million Won in Assets**
Ch'oe Rin (崔麟), Ch'ŏndogyo宗理院 (Chongniwŏn - Central Administrative Institute)

Follower Count: One Million
Our Ch'ŏndogyo currently boasts one million followers. This definition of "follower" is quite different from how other religions count their adherents; it is very strict, referring to those who not only follow the doctrines daily but also unfailingly offer a spoonful of "Sincerity Rice" (誠米) morning and evening. Regarding the local Chongniwŏn, there are County Chongniwŏn (郡宗理院) in counties and Township Chongniwŏn (面宗理院) in townships. The number of County Chongniwŏn is approximately 400, and if Township Chongniwŏn are included, the total far exceeds 1,000.
This is just within Korea; overseas, Chongniwŏn have also been established by compatriots residing in places like Tokyo (東京), London (倫敦), Paris (巴里), the United States (米州), Cuba (「큐-바」), and Hawaii (布哇). Moreover, as the number of people embracing the truth of Ch'ŏndogyo grows daily, not only is the follower count increasing, but the foundation of the organization itself is becoming ever more solid.
Besides this (*이 밖에*), the faction commonly known as the Old Faction (舊派), associated with Mr. Pak Inho (朴寅浩), has approximately 100,000 followers, and we have consistently advocated for reunification whenever possible. The division into New Faction (新派) and Old Faction did not arise from orthodox versus unorthodox interpretations of Ch'ŏndogyo doctrine, but rather from the issue of the main leader (敎主). While we advocate for a council system (合議制), the aforementioned Pak Inho faction insists on elevating Mr. Pak, similar to how Master Son Ŭiam (孫義庵) was revered. However, we hope for a day when an amicable resolution to this issue can be reached.<30>

Strongest in P'yŏngan North/South, Hamgyŏng South
Ch'ŏndogyo's assets are not as vast as rumored. There isn't much land or forest; it mainly consists of buildings and other properties. Setting aside the assets of local Chongniwŏn, the headquarters' assets alone are likely worth about 1 million won. There isn't much cash, and the headquarters' buildings and other properties are held under the joint name of the New and Old factions.
Lastly, currently, Ch'ŏndogyo is strongest in P'yŏngan North Province, P'yŏngan South Province, and Hamgyŏng South Province. The followers in these three provinces number over 300,000, accounting for one-third of the total. Hamgyŏng North Province, which had flourished in the past but entered a period of stagnation in recent years, is currently reviving. Kyŏngsang North and South Provinces have a strong presence of the Old Faction. The other provinces are neither particularly thriving nor particularly declining. Kyŏnggi Province cannot be said to be very strong either.
"Sincerity Rice" (誠米) refers to the practice where all one million followers collect one spoonful (*한숫갈씩*) each time they prepare their meals, morning and evening. Those eating potatoes offer potatoes, those eating millet rice offer millet. This is collected for a month, brought to the local Chongniwŏn, converted to cash, half of which is used locally and the other half sent to the headquarters.

**Christian Movement - 1,000 Schools, 30 Hospitals**
Sin Hŭngu (申興兩), General Secretary, Chosŏn Chung'ang Kidokkyo Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoe (朝鮮中央基督敎靑年會 - Korean Central YMCA)

Currently, there are three main denominations in Korea: South Methodist (南監理), North Methodist (北監理), and Presbyterian (長老敎). The total number of Christians, in a strict sense, is around 300,000. The number of churches (寺院 - *used here generically for places of worship*) is over 1,900, and the number of evangelists (布數者 - *likely 布敎者, propagator/evangelist*) reaches a large figure of 2,872.
Since gaining the freedom to propagate Christianity in the 21st year of King Kojong's reign (李太王 21年 - circa 1884), although there might have been some fluctuations in the number of adherents over the decades, it has consistently remained around 300,000 or more. Christianity is known to be strongest in P'yŏngan North and South Provinces, and it is spreading widely and flourishing daily in other provinces as well. The value of church buildings across Korea would likely exceed several million won.

Cultural enterprises run by Christians primarily include schools and hospitals. Specifically, there are 4 specialized colleges (專門學校), 3 boys' higher common schools (高等普通學校), 5 girls' higher common schools (女子高等普通學校), 28 primary schools (普通學校), 230 other middle and elementary schools, 110 kindergartens (幼稚園), 710 Sunday schools, short courses, traditional village schools (書堂), and lecture institutes (講院), and one special school for the blind and mute (盲啞學校). Altogether, hundreds of thousands of students are being educated by Christian hands. Additionally, for medical institutions, starting with Severance Hospital (세브란쓰병원), there are 24 hospitals across the country, and leper hospitals (癩病院) have been established in Yŏju (麗州), Talsŏng (達城), and Tongnae (東萊) to strive for the relief of the sick and injured. Furthermore, mission funds come annually from the United States (美 本國) and Canada (「캐나다」), but since these are specific to each denomination, the exact total amount is not readily known.

**Buddhist Movement - Twenty Million Won in Land and Buildings** (*Note: Title says 30M, body text 20M*)
Kwŏn Sangno (權相老), Chosŏn Pulgyo Chung'ang Kyomuwŏn (朝鮮佛敎 中央敎務院 - Central Council of Korean Buddhism)

Assets: Thirty Million Won (*Note: Text body clarifies this as 20 million based on a prior survey*)
The last time we accurately surveyed the current strength of our Buddhism was four years ago.<31> According to the calculations at that time, the total number of temples (寺院) in all of Korea was 1,363. Also, the market value of all possessions held by those temples, including Buddha halls (法堂) and other buildings, land, forests, implements, etc., reached approximately 20 million won, as I recall. Considering that Buddhism was the center of national faith during the Silla and Koryŏ periods and, for a time, the source of all kinds of culture in society, but declined due to various reasons upon entering the Chosŏn Dynasty period, the number of Buddhist temples must have decreased significantly, and Buddhist assets likely diminished as well. Therefore, when imagining the number of temples and the assets owned during the peak of Buddhism in the Three Kingdoms period, one cannot help but feel one's blood boil.

Kyŏngsang North/South Strongest for Buddhism
So, how many Buddhist adherents are there? Unlike Ch'ŏndogyo or Christianity, Buddhism cannot accurately count its adherents. Why is that? While Christians or Ch'ŏndogyo followers come to their places of worship on set days and can be clearly registered on membership lists, Buddhism is not like that. Many people maintain their faith and perform Buddhist rituals at home, following traditions passed down for thousands of years. Perhaps it's "All beings have Buddha-nature" (萬人皆有佛性); regardless, when facing any significant event, good or bad fortune, or emergency, many people turn to Buddha. I believe this signifies that they are all Buddhists in a broad sense. Therefore, one could say that anyone in Korea who is not a Christian, Ch'ŏndogyo follower, or adherent of another specific religion is essentially a follower of our Buddhism. Now, surveying the number of adherents in a very narrow sense—those who regularly come to the Buddha halls—we count:

Cultural activities undertaken by the Buddhist community include founding and operating the Buddhist Specialized School (佛敎專門學校) with a 1 million won foundation, Seoul's Posŏng Higher Common School (普成高等普通學校), and elementary schools such as:

**Summary**

This 1930 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine provides a statistical overview of major Korean social, political, and religious organizations during the Japanese colonial period. Representatives from Sin'ganhoe, the Farmer General Alliance (Nongch'ong), Labor General Alliance (Noch'ong), Youth General Alliance (Ch'ŏngch'ong), Kŭnuhoe (women's organization), Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, and Buddhism report on their respective membership numbers, branch distribution, regional strengths, leadership structures, finances, and activities. Common themes include significant membership (tens or hundreds of thousands, even millions claimed by Ch'ŏndogyo), strong regional variations (Hamgyŏng and Kyŏngsang provinces often mentioned as active centers), and severe suppression by Japanese authorities, particularly impacting the nationalist and leftist groups (Sin'ganhoe, Nongch'ong, Noch'ong, Ch'ŏngch'ong) through assembly bans and arrests of leaders. Religious groups (Ch'ŏndogyo, Christianity, Buddhism) highlight their follower counts, property holdings, organizational structures, and contributions to education and social welfare. The most revealing aspects for historians are the quantified data on organizational strength and distribution just before the major crackdown of the 1930s, the explicit mention of widespread arrests and suppression hindering activities, and the self-reported demographics and regional disparities within each movement, offering a snapshot of civil society under colonial rule.

新幹運動-會員四萬 咸南北 最盛
新幹會 中央委員 金恒圭

우리 新幹會는 반도에 현재 40,100여명의 충실한 회원과 139개소의 支會를 가지고 잇슴니다. 이밧게 조직 진행 중에 잇는 지회가 35개소나 잇스니 不遠에 그 회원 총수가 4萬 3,4千을 헤일 것이요 또한 지회 수효도 백5,60을 헤이* 될 盛運에 이를 모양이외다. 이것이 1927년 2월 창립 이래의 일이니 겨우 3,4년 동안에 이러틋 놀라운 세력을 문치게 된 것을 보면 新幹精神과 그 운동이 엇더케나 이 땅 사람의 마음을 흔들어 오는 것임을 알 것이외다.
그런데 新幹運動의 세력의 분포상황을 보면 慶尙南北道와 咸鏡南北道가 제일 충실하고 또한 활발함니다. 즉 慶南 一道에만 25개의 지회가 잇는데 榮州의 金東弼씨, 安東의 李雲鎬씨, 대구의 崔允東, 柳淵述씨 등은 모다 그 지방의 유력한 지도자들이며 또한 咸鏡道로 보면 元山, 安邊, 咸興, 利原, 端川, 北靑, 洪原, 定平, 吉州, 會寧, 鏡城, 德原, 羅南, 淸津, 城津 기타 등지에 24개소가 잇는데 그 중에도 가장 驚嘆할＜26＞ 곳은 咸北 鏡城支會 안의 漁朱支會외다. 그곳의 농민, 상공업자들은 대개 그 지회에 입회하여서 총수가 1,500여명을 헤이고 잇스니 회원수에 잇서서 京城支會와 伯仲하고 잇는 텨임니다. 그곳에는 李圖遠씨 등이 꾸준한 노력을 보이고 잇슴니다.
그러고는 全羅南北이겟지요. 全南은 木浦, 長城, 求禮, 咸平, 靈岩, 光陽 등 다 훌융한 지도자를 가지고 잘 하여 나감니다. 全北은 全最盛이고 그 다음이 南原이겟지요. 平南은 平壤, 鎭南浦, 安州 等 支會가 有數하지요. 平壤은 曺晩植씨와 그 외 여러분들이 만히 활약하나이다.
그러고 다소 신간회운동이 넓고 깁고 크지 못한 곳은 平北, 忠南北, 黃海, 江原 등이라 하겟지요. 平北으로 말하면 宣川支會가 600명의 회원을 擁하고 李龍吉씨 등이 활약하여 주어서 크게 볼 것이 잇스나 그박게 龍川, 義州, 博川 등은 활발치 못하다 할박게 업고 忠南을 말하면 洪城 1個所가 가장 꾸준한 데 거기에는 金淵鎭, 尹大榮氏 등이 분주하고 잇슴니다. 그 외에도 長湖院이나 唐津에 잇기는 하나 아직은 미미하다 할박게 없고 忠北으로 말하면 淸州 1個所가 有力하고 黃海로 말하면 載寧에 잇스나 그 亦 他道에 비하야 손색 잇고 그 박게는 업서서 아마 新幹運動이 제일 不振한 곳이 此道일가 함니다. 江原道는 江陵支會가 가장 强大하고 高城, 原州 等 支會는 그리 시원하다 할 것이 업겟슴니다.
나중에 京畿道로 말하면 서울이 제일이어서 회원수 1,600여명을 擁하고 잇는 이이며 그박게는 仁川, 水原, 廣州, 開城, 江華 여러 도시에 잇지요.
그러나 지방마다 특수 정세가 다르니까 부득이 아직은 이와 가치 균형을 이럿스나 未久에 모다 충실하여지리라고 밋슴니다. 그것은 각지에 마다 우리 운동을 차츰 차츰 정당하게 인식하는 이가 만허지는 까닭과 유력한 선구자들이 만히 뛰이나올 것을 여러 가지 점으로 밋게 되는 까닭이외다.
그러고 40,000여명의 회원들의 직업을 보면 농민이 100分之50, 상인이 仝20, 노동자 仝20, 기타는 敎役者, 신문기자, 교사, 변호사, 의사, 무직자 등일 것이외다.
재정은 큰돈이 모여진 것은 아직 업스나 그 회비가 年年히 상당히 되어 가며 끗흐로 본부의 각 機關 責任者 氏名을 적으면 중앙집행위원장 許憲, 서무에 黃尙圭, 財政에 金炳魯, 교육에 朴熙道, 출판에 李鍾麟, 연락에 徐廷禧, 조사에 李春塾, 조직에 金恒圭, 선전에 李灌鎔 等 諸氏요 中央委行委員의 총수는 40여 명이외다. 그러고 全 朝鮮의 新幹운동 관계자로 방금 투옥 혹 구금자 수가 許憲 이하 140여 명 올시다.
