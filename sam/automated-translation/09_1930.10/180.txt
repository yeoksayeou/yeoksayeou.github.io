China's New Literature
						Yang Paek-hwa (梁白華)
Brother Pa In (巴人兄)!
It has already been a long time since you asked me by phone to cite points that Korea (朝鮮) should learn from Chinese literature, and I am truly sorry for not being able to write it for you by the deadline (期日). However, wishing to fulfill that promise, I belatedly take up my pen now, only to realize for the first time (비롯오) how preposterous (孟浪한) the topic is. What should we learn from Chinese literature? As for me (나로는), I know this is a question that allows no room for thought. What is there to learn? Not only has Chinese literature already long been an element of our own native literature, but also the similarity of its character shapes (字形), the closeness of its character sounds (字音), and furthermore, the ease in terms of application and assimilation (運用同化上), as well as the resulting natural harmony (水乳自然) in its combination, are matters of course. Therefore, even in this sense, I cannot clearly bring out specific points to learn.

Brother Pa In (巴人兄)
Not only that, but originally, the essence (본질) of literature is the same in any country. Therefore, if there is someone who says that the literature of a certain country has things to learn, while the literature of another country is worthless (못쓴다), this is the talk of someone who does not know what literature is. The question here is whether I might have misheard your phone call. If so, what is closest to this issue? Perhaps it is its characteristics. Meaning, does the literature of any given country have certain characteristics? For instance, just as Russian (露西亞) literature is said to be melancholic and profound, and French (佛蘭西) literature bright and clear, perhaps you were asking me to write about the characteristics of Chinese literature. I intend to speak under this personal judgment (독단).

Brother Pa In (巴人兄)
Generally, the characteristics of Chinese literature are as follows.
In terms of purpose, it is practical (실용).
In terms of spirit, it is grand (웅대).
In terms of temperament (性情), it is tragic (비장).
In terms of essence (본질), it is pure and unified (純一).<70>
In terms of form, it is concise (간결).
In terms of word formation (造語), it is symmetrical (對待).
In terms of flavor (風味), it has pictorial charm (畵趣).
It would be good to add explanations for each of these eight points (特點), but the writer currently does not have the time. Apart from this (이박게), if I were to mention its flaws, perhaps it is that its thoughts are not meticulous (치밀치 못하고) and its emotions are not subtle (미묘치 못한 것). However, for 3,000 years, great writers and outstanding poets (文豪詩傑) have appeared continuously generation after generation (代로), the grand works and masterpieces (雄篇大作) transmitted (傳할 者) are by no means (결코) few, and also in its enduring lineage (命脈), its intricate structure (결구의 錯綜함), its skillful rhetoric (辭令의 교묘함), and its rich vocabulary (語彙의 富膽함), I believe there is no literature in any country in the world that can rival (필적할) it.

Brother Pa In (巴人兄)
Our future literature, along with scholarship, must become world-class. That is, selecting the essence (粹) and gathering the materials (材) from all times and places, East and West, digesting them and fusing them to create a new approach (新機軸), is what writers (操觚) should rightly (맛당히) aspire to. And as mentioned above, Chinese literature has deep ties (舊緣) with us, unlike the entirely different literary language of Western literature (泰西文學), where the traces (흔적) of transplantation (옴기어) are clearly evident (歷然한). Therefore, while digesting (咀嚼하는) the new Western literature, isn't there also a need to appreciate (翫味할) Chinese literature with which we have old ties? I believe there is a need to promote (高吹할) practical literature concurrently with promoting (拮揚하는) aesthetic (美藝的) literature.<71>
<70-71>

**Summary**

Yang Paek-hwa argues that while Chinese literature's essence is universal, its specific characteristics include practicality, grandeur, tragic sentiment, purity, conciseness, symmetrical phrasing, and pictorial charm. Despite acknowledging flaws like a lack of meticulous thought and subtle emotion, he praises its long, unbroken tradition of great writers and rich works. Yang advocates for future Korean literature to become world-class by synthesizing global influences, urging contemporaries not only to digest modern Western literature but also to re-appreciate the historically intertwined and practically oriented Chinese literature, balancing aesthetic pursuits with practical ones. From a historical perspective, this article reveals the complex cultural positioning of Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. It shows a desire to modernize Korean literature by engaging with global (Western) trends while simultaneously reassessing and valuing the deep-rooted historical connection with China, suggesting a path that navigates between tradition and modernity, East and West, and aestheticism and pragmatism. Historians might find the explicit call to learn from both Chinese and Western sources, and the characterization of Chinese literature's strengths and weaknesses from a 1930s Korean perspective, particularly insightful regarding cultural identity formation under colonialism.

						梁白華
巴人兄!
중국문학에 대하야 조선에서 배울 점을 들라고 전화로 부탁하신지는 벌서 오래것만은 그 期日에 써들이지 못한 것은 참으로 죄송함니다. 그러나 그 약속을 이행코저 뒤느러지게 인제야 붓을 들고보니 과제가 孟浪한 줄을 비롯오 깨닷겟슴니다. 중국문학에서 우리가 무엇을 배울가? 나로는 이는 생각할 여지도 업는 문제인 줄로 암니다. 배울 것이 무엇인가요? 중국의 문학은 이미 오랜동안 우리 고유문학의 원소가 되얏슬 뿐 아니라 그 字形이 가튼 것이라든지 字音의 近似한 것이라든지 또는 運用同化上에 잇서 용이함은 물론이요 그 결과에 잇서 水乳自然의 화합성이 잇는 것임니다. 그런즉 이 의미에 잇서서도 명확히 그 배울 점을 들어낼 수는 업슴니다.

巴人兄
그뿐아니라 원래 문학이라는 것은 어느나라의 것이나 그 본질은 다가튼 것임니다. 그럼으로 어떤 나라의 문학은 배울 것이 잇다 어떤 나라의 문학은 못쓴다 하고 말하는 사람이 잇다하면 이것은 문학이 무엇인 줄을 모르고서 하는 사람의 말임니다. 이에 문제는 형의 전화를 내가 잘 못드럿지나 아니하얏나 하는데 잇슴니다. 만일 그러타 하면 이 문제에 가장 가까운 것이 무엇이냐? 아마도 특색인가 함니다. 말하면 아모 나라의 문학은 무슨 특색이 잇다. 즉 露西亞의 문학은 憂鬱沈庸하고 佛蘭西의 문학은 明快淸麗하다 듯이 중국문학은 또 어떠타 하는 그 특색을 써 달라는 것이 아닌가 함니다. 나는 이러한 독단 아래 말하려 함니다.

파인형
대체로 중국문학의 특색을 든다면 알에와 갓슴니다.
목적으로 말하면 실용임니다.
기상으로 말하면 웅대함니다.
性情으로 말하면 비장함니다.
본질로 말하면 純一함니다.＜70＞
형식으로 말하면 간결함니다.
造語로 말하면 對待임니다.
風味로 말하면 畵趣임니다.
이 여덜가지 特點을 일일히 설명을 加하얏스면 조켓스나 필자는 지금 그러한 시간이 업슴니다. 다만 이박게 그 결점을 든다면 사상이 치밀치 못하고 정감이 미묘치 못한 것인가 함니다. 그러나 3,000年來로 文豪詩傑이 代로 끈히지 아니하고 雄篇大作의 傳할 者 결코 적지 아니하며 또 그 命脈의 悠遠長久함이라든지 그 결구의 錯綜함이라든지 그 辭令의 교묘함이라든지 그 語彙의 富膽함이라든지 세계 어느 나라 이와 필적할 문학이 업슬 줄로 암니다.

巴人兄
우리의 장래문학은 학술과 한가지로 세계적이 되지 아니하면 안됨니다. 즉 고금동서의 粹를 採하고 材를 蒐하야 이를 소화하고 이를 융합하야 다시 한 新機軸을 내는 것은 操觚의 맛당히 理想할 배임니다. 그리고 중국문학은 우에 말한 바와 가티 우리와 인연이 깁허 全然 문학언어를 달니한 泰西文學을 옴기어 흔적이 歷然한 것에 비할 것이 아닌즉 이제 새로운 서양문학을 咀嚼하는 동시에 또 舊緣잇는 중국문학을 翫味할 필요가 업슬까요? 美藝的 문학을 拮揚하는 동시에 실용적 문학을 高吹할 필요가 잇는 줄로 생각함니다.＜71＞
