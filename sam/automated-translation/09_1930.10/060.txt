Chŏng Yagyong and Famous Products

Chŏng Yagyong and Famous Products

In the "Mongmin Simsŏ" (牧民心書) written by the great scholar Master Yagyong (若鏞선생), the following are written as famous products (名産物):
From Kanggyŏng (江景) in P'yŏngan Province (平安道), ginseng and marten fur (蔘貂); from Sunch'ang (淳昌) in Chŏlla Province (全羅道), fans (扇); from Tamyang (潭陽) in Chŏlla Province, colored chests (綵箱); from Tongnae (東萊) in Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙道), smoking paraphernalia (煙具); from Kyŏngju (慶州) in Kyŏngsang Province, crystal (水晶); from Haeju (海州) in Hwanghae Province (黃海道), inksticks (墨); from Namp'o (藍浦) in Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸道), inkstones (硯); from Kyŏngsŏng (鏡城) in Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道), cloth (布); from Kaesŏng (開城) in Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道), ginseng (人參); from Chŏnju (全州) in Chŏlla Province, fans (扇); from Wiwŏn (渭原) in P'yŏngan Province, inkstones (硯); from Chongsŏng (鍾城) in Hamgyŏng Province, inkstones (硯).

Great Scholars and Eminent Sages

Great Scholars (碩儒) and Eminent Sages (巨賢) referred to in Confucianism:
Song Siyŏl (宋時烈) (Uam, 尤庵), Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn (崔致遠) (Haeun, 海雲), Chŏng Mongju (鄭夢周) (P'oŭn, 圃隱), Cho Kwangjo (趙光租) (Chŏngam, 靜庵), Yi Hwang (李滉) (T'oegye, 退溪), Yi I (李珥) (Yulgok, 栗谷), Kim Chip (金集) (Sindokchae, 愼獨齋), and various other gentlemen (其他 諸氏). <32>

**Summary**

This short article presents two lists related to Korean tradition and history. The first list, attributed to the renowned Chosŏn scholar Chŏng Yagyong's work "Mongmin Simsŏ," details famous regional products from various provinces of Korea, such as ginseng, fans, inkstones, and cloth. The second list names several prominent Confucian scholars revered as "Great Scholars and Eminent Sages," including figures like Song Siyŏl, Yi Hwang, and Yi I. For historians, the article is interesting as it reflects a continued interest in and promotion of traditional Korean culture (regional specialties) and intellectual heritage (Confucian scholars) during the Japanese colonial period, published in a popular magazine like Samch'ŏlli. It showcases an effort to remember and catalogue elements of Korean identity.
