**Body Text Translation:**
Thanks and Apology

○ We are grateful that the brethren of the Peninsula (半島兄弟) love this journal so much. From now on, we intend to publish monthly and also enrich the content to repay even a fraction of your patronage.

○ An Chaehong's "Paektusan Journey" (白頭山行), Yi Kwangsu's "The Scent of Life" (人生의 香氣), Chŏkku's "Our Poems," and Ansŏ's "Four Poems for Diversion" (消遺四首) were all typeset, but due to page limitations, they have been pushed to the next issue.

○ Yi Kwangsu's "Wife of a Revolutionary" (革命家의안해) and Pak Yŏnghŭi's "Collection of Novels and Criticism" (小說, 評論集) are currently on sale amidst fervent acclaim. Please read them often. Bad books are not sold by our company. (End)

**Summary**

This editorial note expresses gratitude to readers for their support of the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* and announces the intention to publish monthly with improved content. It apologizes for delaying the publication of several planned articles by prominent writers like An Chaehong and Yi Kwangsu due to space constraints. It also promotes recently published books by Yi Kwangsu and Pak Yŏnghŭi, assuring readers of their quality. From a historical perspective, this brief note reveals the operational challenges (page limits forcing content delays) faced by Korean magazines during the colonial period, while also highlighting the active literary scene, featuring major figures like Yi Kwangsu and An Chaehong, and the magazine's role in promoting their works. The mention of "Peninsula brethren" underscores a sense of national identity among the readership.
