劇場創立과 新劇運動
						洪海星

In our Chosŏn, advocates for the New Theater Movement (新劇運動家), seeking to establish dramatic arts, appeared long ago. With the mission to contribute to a New Culture (新文化) for the masses, dreaming of a happy future, they walked the path of pioneers (開拓者) or martyrs (受難者). If that era when they sowed the noble seeds is called the First Period, we are now in a period of stagnation and decline in the current process of growing new sprouts from the First Period to the Second Period. The general public tolerates plays of an inferior (依級) taste as the standard for theater, showing no concern or reflection (省察), failing to explore (探求) the reality of our lives. The theater, which should be a human sanctuary (聖所), is turning into a matchmaking place (仲媒所) for lewd men and women (淫男淫女), and in the current theatrical world (現劇界), which is merely a place for simple entertainment (娛樂), this new organization has been formed, despite its meager strength (微力), to overcome this crisis (危機), establish our genuine New Theater, and possess an expressive organ (表現的 機關) for our lives (生洛 - likely typo for 生活), one that we can claim as our own troupe.<71> Preparations (準備) were rushed from July to mid-August. The preparation involved first gathering comrades (同志). Comrades for our troupe began to gather (集合), discussions took place almost daily (每日), and after repeated deliberation (熟議), our troupe was finally established (創立). Mr. Yi Ki-se (李基世), a public figure (公人) in the Chosŏn theater world, serves as Advisor (顧問), along with Yun Paeng-nam (尹白南), a veteran (宿將) of the theater world, Mr. Pak Sŭng-hŭi (朴勝喜), a newcomer who was a leader (指導者) of the theater troupe T'owŏrhoe (劇團 土月會), and my respected friends (畏友) Kim Ŭl-han (金乙漢) and Yi Sang-hwa (李相和) as fellow members (同人). We, the fellow members, share the same responsibility and duty (義務) for the artistic direction (藝術上 全責任) and management (經營維持) of this troupe. Initially, we will recruit first-term trainees (硏究生) and provide them with foundational education (基礎的 敎養). By cultivating theater professionals (劇場人) who will be the fundamental foundation (根本的 土臺), cultivating individuals of sound character (健全 人格者) suitable for our social structure (社會形態), the multifaceted functions (多角的 機能) of dramatic art, and the demands of the times (時代的 要望)—theater professionals who can enlighten (敎化) the masses (民衆) with new vitality (生活力)—and by establishing a new era (新紀元) in the creation of the dramatic art we must possess, with new spirit and skill (技術), we aim to make our movement the mainstream (主流) in the Chosŏn theater world, establish a complete theater troupe, and through our original plays (創作劇), promote reflection (省察) on society (社會), cultivate the beauty (美) of our lives, and cry out once again for the forgotten humanity (人間性). Such an endeavor, which can be called an expression (表現) of the Korean national character (朝鮮民族性), is the unified intent (意圖) of us fellow members. We will advance in the creation of stage art (舞臺藝術創造), shaking hands (握手) with unknown comrades (未知 同志) and the new masses ahead. We ask for much guidance (指導) and support (後援) for our endeavor.
September 4, 1930<72>

**Summary**

Hong Hae-sŏng announces the formation of a new theater troupe dedicated to reviving the "New Theater Movement" in Korea, which he feels has entered a period of stagnation. He laments the low standards of current theater, viewing it merely as entertainment and even morally debased. The new troupe, comprised of notable figures like Yi Ki-se, Yun Paeng-nam, Pak Sŭng-hŭi, Kim Ŭl-han, and Yi Sang-hwa, aims to establish a "genuine" Korean theater through training new actors, creating original plays reflecting Korean life and society, and promoting a higher artistic and moral standard, ultimately expressing the "Korean national character." From a historical perspective, the article vividly illustrates the cultural aspirations and anxieties of Korean intellectuals under Japanese colonial rule. Their desire to create a "genuine" national theater, distinct from both traditional forms and perceived decadent popular entertainment, reflects a drive to assert cultural identity and modernity amidst suppression. The emphasis on cultivating "sound character," societal reflection, and national expression highlights the role intellectuals envisioned for art in shaping a modern Korean consciousness.
