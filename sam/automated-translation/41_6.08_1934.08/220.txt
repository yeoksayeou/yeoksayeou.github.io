Impression of Mr. Bose
						Hong Yangmyŏng

"Rash Behari Bose" came to Seoul. As an exiled Indian, he is as widely known in Tōkyō and Chosŏn as Mr. Pura Taep'ŭ (푸라대프). Leaving aside the question of what connection he currently has with India's national movement, or whether his Pan-Asianism is generally appropriate or not, the fact that he was once a leader-level figure in the Indian national movement and a leader of the Social Republican faction, also called the left wing of the Swaraj Party (스와라즈 黨), was something I had heard previously from Mr. Tek Singh (덱,싱), the Secretary-General of the Eastern Oppressed Peoples' Association (東方被壓迫民族同盟) in Nanjing (南京), China. Also, from the articles he frequently published in the magazine "Tōyō" (東洋) published in Tōkyō and his personal magazine "Pan-Asianism" (『Pan-Asiaism』), I could see that his knowledge of the political, economic, and social problems of his homeland, India, was quite deep. Therefore, while he was staying in Seoul, I had some curiosity, hoping to meet him at least once if possible to hear about the recent situation in India. Amidst this, several newspaper executives happened to arrange an evening banquet for him, and through this opportunity, I was able to associate with him for a couple of hours. Although there are many things I heard and saw regarding India, I will set those aside and write down just a little of my first impression to repay my literary debt.

Born in Bengal (뱅골) state, where Western civilization is most widespread in India, Mr. Bose possessed a thoroughly modern cheerfulness. Also, as someone with experience working as an official,<128> he gave the impression of trying to dominate others somehow. This was especially accentuated by his piercing eyes and large, corpulent frame. Having lived in exile in Japan for 18 years, during which time he even married a Japanese woman (whom he recently lost), his Japanese was freely spoken, not inferior to his fluent English. His open-hearted appearance and the forcefulness of his speech, flowing like a torrent, gave the feeling of a person living by the impulse to try anything, whether it will succeed or fail, rather than that of a reclusive patriot (!). The people he associated with in Tōkyō seemed to be mostly figures like Tōyama Mitsuru (頭山滿) and Okada Ryōhei (岡田良平), ronin outside of government, individuals with ultranationalist tendencies promoting Pan-Asianism. Their influence seems to have naturally caused Mr. Bose to shed much of his Indianness and increasingly adopt "Japanese" ways. His gestures, his grandiloquent pronouncements, gave the impression that power, wealth, and all worldly existence were beneath his notice. And he emphasized that the manifestation of "Love," the culmination of spiritualism, should be the keynote of all movements.
This seemed somewhat incongruous with his rather materialistic demeanor, which resembled someone whose main business was fighting social evils in the streets rather than the scholarly appearance of one expounding the profound philosophy of India. His views on the Indian question were also extremely moderate compared to what I had expected or what he expressed in writing. I don't know if the recent attitude of the Indian National Congress (印度國民會議) – abandoning the non-violent non-cooperation movement and being satisfied with nominal self-government under the Indian Constitution – has extended even to this Indian independence activist in overseas exile, but the truth about this radical revolutionary, who escaped a search network with a 5,000 peso bounty related to a violent conspiracy against British authorities and sought refuge under the Japanese flag, is naturally something one cannot know just from his words. Also, considering the circumstances under which he came to Japan, if we see the motive for him throwing himself into the Pan-Asian movement as originating from the Indians' "sense of hatred towards the rule of the white British," then it can also be seen as a natural path for them. <129>

He stated that the purpose of his current trip was to attend the founding ceremony of the Xinjing (新京) branch of the Pan-Asian Peoples' Alliance (汎亞細亞民族主義聯盟) as a representative of the headquarters and also to inspect Manchukuo (滿洲國). Considering that his "patron," the Pan-Asianist Yasuoka Masahiro (安岡正篤), head of the Kinkei Gakuin (金雞學院), is a significant satellite in some sector of Japanese power, the scope of his activities might be larger than thought. He certainly had the air of a hero in his ability to drink well and talk well. At the same time, this seemed to have stripped away from him the profound Indian quality that forms a harmonious character through integrity, perseverance, and a self-sacrificing spirit. Whether he will become a shining presence in India's future or end up merely as a contracted representative for gatherings like the Pan-Asian Peoples' Congress – whichever it may be, it is all an unknown variable. <130>

**Summary**

The author, Hong Yangmyŏng, recounts his meeting with the exiled Indian revolutionary Rash Behari Bose during Bose's visit to Seoul in 1934. Hong observes Bose's modern, forceful personality and fluency in Japanese, noting his association with Japanese Pan-Asianist figures like Tōyama Mitsuru and Yasuoka Masahiro. While acknowledging Bose's past role in the Indian independence movement, Hong expresses skepticism about Bose's seemingly moderated views and his shift towards Japanese-influenced Pan-Asianism, questioning whether Bose retains his revolutionary integrity or has become influenced by his patrons and circumstances. The author finds a disconnect between Bose's proclaimed spiritual ideals ("Love") and his seemingly pragmatic, almost materialistic demeanor, ultimately concluding that Bose's future significance remains uncertain. For historians, this article offers a valuable Korean perspective on a prominent figure connected to both Indian independence and Japanese expansionist ideologies during the colonial period. It highlights the complex web of alliances, influences, and ideological shifts among Asian nationalists and intellectuals under Japanese imperialism, revealing Korean skepticism towards Japanese-led Pan-Asianism even when observing figures fighting Western colonialism.

보-스氏 印象
						洪陽明
『라스, 버하리, 뽀-스』가 서울에 왓다. 亡命 印度人으로서 東京과 朝鮮에 널니 알녀저 잇는 點에서 『푸라대프』氏와 함께 雙壁이다. 그가 現在 印度의 民族運動과 엇더한 關聯을 가지고 잇는지, 또는 그의 凡亞細亞主義가 一般的으로 妥當한 것인지 아닌지는 論外로 하고 그가 過去에 잇서 印度 民族運動의 首領級의 一人으로써 『스와라즈』黨의 左派라고도 할 社會共和派의 指導者엿든 點은 일즉히 中國 南京에 잇는 東方被壓迫民族同盟
(Easternoimaimasress a imaeoimales association)의 幹事長 『덱,싱』君에게서 들은 바 잇섯고 또 그가 東京에서 發行하는 雜誌 『東洋』 等과 그이 個人雜誌 『imaan-Asiaism』上에 자조 發表하는 글을 보면 그 祖國 印度의 政治經濟社會 諸問題에 대한 造詣가 相當히 깁혼 것을 알 수 잇섯슴으로 나는 그가 서울에 滯留하는 동안될 수 잇스면 한 번 만나보아 印度의 最近事情이나 들어보앗스면 하는 好寄心을 若干 가지고 잇섯다. 그러든 中에 新聞社의 幹部 數人이 그를 爲한 一夕의 宴을 베풀게되엿슴으로 이 機會에 나는 두어 時間동안 그와 交際할 機會를 엇덧든 것이다. 印度에 대한 여러 가지 보고 드른 말도 잇지마는 그것은 고만두기로 하고 그의 初印象이나 조곰 적어 書債을 갑흘가한다.

印度에서 가장 西歐文明이 普及된 『뱅골』州 産인, 뽀-스氏는 어데까지 近代人的인 明朗性을 가지고 잇섯다. 또한 그는<128> 官吏로 다니든 經歷이 잇는 사람임으로 어데인지 남을 威壓하려 함과 갓흔 印象을 주엇다. 그것은 특히 그이 炯炯한 눈 肥大한 巨軀가 그것을 더 한層 또렷하게 하엿다. 그는 日本에도 亡命하여온 지가 18년이나 되고 그 동안 日本 婦人과 結婚까지 하엿다가 最近喪配하엿슴으로 日本말은 그의 流暢한 英語에 比하야 遜色이 업게 自由自在하엿다. 磊落한 風貌, 談論風發하는 그의 語勢는 隱遁的인 志士(!) 보담도 될 일이나될 일이나 무엇이나 해 보려 하는 衝動에서 사는 人物이란 感을 주엇다. 그가 東京에서 交際하는 사람들은 대개가 頭山滿 岡田良平 갓흔 在野 浪人들 汎亞細亞主義를 내걸고 잇는 國粹的 傾向이 잇는 人物들이 대부분인 모양으로 이들의 感化는 自然 뽀-스氏에게 印度的인 것을 만히 遞感하여가고 『日本的 』인 걸로 만히 遞增한 듯 하엿다. 그의 『제스츄어』 그의 風發하는 大言牡談은 權力과 財力과 모든 現世的 存在가 眼中에 업는 것과 갓흔 印象을 주엇다. 그리고 그는 精神主義의 極致인 『愛』의 발휘가 모든 運動의 基調가 된다는 것을 强調하얏다.
幽玄한 印度의 哲理를 說하는 學者的 風貌보담도 巷間의 社會惡과 鬪爭함을 本領으로 함과 갓흔 物質的으로 보이는 그의 風格에 잘 맛지 안는 듯한 感을 주엇다. 그의 印度問題에 대한 見解도 내가 이러하리라고 생각한 것 또 그가 文字로 나태내는 것보담도 極히 穩和한 것이엿다. 印度 憲法이 名目上의 自治에 不過하지마는 이에 滿足하야 非軍事 非協動運動을 抛棄한 印度國民會議의 最近의 態度가 이 海外 亡命 中의 印度獨立運動者에까지 波及되엿는지 모르지마는 英國官憲에 대한 暴力的 陰謀에 關聯하야 5千『페소』의 懸賞附搜素網을 버서나 日章旗 아래로 亡命하여온 이 急速的 革命者의 問題가 眞實로 엇더한 거인지는 勿論 그의 말로써마는 모를 이리라. 또 그가 日本에 왓는 環境을 생각하면 그로서 汎亞細亞運動에 몸을 던지게 된 動機를 印度人의 『白人인 英國의 統治에 대한 僧惡感』에서 제來된 것이다고 보면 그들로써는 또한 自然스러운 길이라고도 볼 수 잇다. <129>

그의 今日의 旅行目的은 汎亞細亞民族主義聯盟 新京支部 發會式에 本部를 代表하야 出席하고 또 滿洲國을 視察함에 잇다고 하엿다. 그의 『페트론』인 大亞細亞主義者 金雞學院長 安岡正篤氏가 日本의 엇더한 部門의 勢力에 잇서 적지 안은 惑星인 點에서 볼 때 그의 活動의 範圍는 생각한 것보담 클넌지도 모른다. 그는 잘 『飮』하고 잘 談하는 點에서 확실히 豪傑의 風이 잇섯다. 이것은 同時에 그에게서 節操와 堅忍과 犧牲的 精神으로 渾然한 人格을 이르는 幽玄한 印度的인 것을 빼서가고만 듯하얏다. 그가 印度의 將來에 빗나는 存在가 될지 亞細亞民族大會 等 會合의 請負的 代表에 끄치고 말지 그 어느 쪽일지는 이는 다-未知數이다. <130>
