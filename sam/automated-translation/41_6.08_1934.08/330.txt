Life Bulletin Board
Clean People Movement (淸族運動)

The Clean People Movement (淸族運動) is about washing away the dirty stains of our people. Starting from the most trivial things:
"Let's not spit saliva on the street."
"Let's not pick our teeth in front of others."
"Let's always keep our mouths closed. Let's not leave them hanging open slack-jawed (헤-)."
"Let's speak softly. Except in large gatherings."
"We talk too much. Let's say only the two or three necessary words instead of ten."
Crying out like this, a certain person concerned about the nation and lamenting the times (憂族慨世家) let out a long sigh.<144>

**Summary**

This short piece introduces the "Clean People Movement" (淸族運動), advocating for Koreans to improve public etiquette by abandoning habits like spitting, public teeth-picking, speaking loudly, and excessive talking. It frames these behavioral changes as a way to "wash away the dirty stains" of the nation, concluding with the image of a concerned patriot sighing over these issues. For historians, this article offers a glimpse into the social anxieties and self-critique within Korean society during the colonial period. It reflects a desire for modernization and national improvement, potentially internalizing colonial criticisms about Korean customs while simultaneously expressing a form of national consciousness focused on collective self-improvement and public image.

인생 게시판
淸族運動

淸族運動이란 우리 민족의 더러운 때를 시처버리자는 것이다. 가장 사소한 데서부터 말하면 「길거리에 춤을 뱃지 마자.」
「이쑤시개질을 남의 압헤서 하지 말자.」
「입을 늘 담을자. 헤-하고 버리지 말자.」
「말을 나즉나즉하게 하자. 만히 모힌 자리 이외에는.」
「우리는 말이 너무 만타. 열 마듸 할 것을 꼭 필요한 말만 두세 마듸 하자.」
이러케 외치면서 엇든 憂族慨世家는 긴-한숨짓다.<144>
