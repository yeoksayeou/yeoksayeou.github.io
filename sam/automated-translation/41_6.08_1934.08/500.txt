Gratitude and Apology

It is now the height of summer (삼복). We trust that you, our readers, are striving to build strong bodies like iron pillars by visiting scenic spots amidst green mountains and clear waters. It truly seems right that Westerners, after working diligently for a whole year without looking back, quietly recuperate during the summer season alone. Please take sufficient rest during this summer, and starting from autumn, return to your workplaces with new energy and a fresh spirit.

Postscript: Due to necessity among our company's staff, this issue has ended at about two hundred pages, but starting from the next issue (來號), we will restore it again to a large volume (大冊) of about three hundred pages.

Thanks to the passionate support (熱愛) you all showed, the previous July issue went into a second printing (再版). Thank you very much. We hope you will continue to guide "Samch'ŏlli" (三千里) steadfastly.<200>

**Summary**

This editorial note expresses gratitude to readers for their support, particularly for the success of the previous issue which required a reprint. It apologizes for the current issue's reduced page count due to staff needs but promises a return to the usual larger size next month. The note also encourages readers to rest well during the summer, drawing a parallel with Western vacation habits, and return refreshed in the autumn. From a historical perspective, the note offers a glimpse into the publishing operations and reader engagement of a Korean magazine during the colonial period. The mention of Western vacation practices and the practical concerns about printing volume and sales reflect the cultural influences and economic realities of the time.

感謝와 謝罪
지금은 삼복 녀름이외다. 여러분은 靑山綠水의 명승처를 따라 무쇠기둥가튼 튼튼한 몸을 만들기에 노력하시는 줄 아옵니다. 서양사람은 일년동안 눈도 거들떠보지안코 부즈런히 일한 다음 여름 한철만은 고요히 휴양하는 것이 과연 오른 일로 보임니다. 어서 이 녀름동안 충분한 휴양을 하시고, 가을철부터 새긔운 새정신으로 일터로 도라와줍소셔

追告 이번호는 본사 사원들도 필요상 이백여頁에 끈첫사오나 來號부터는 다시 3백여頁 大冊으로 복구하겟나이다.

지난 7월호는 여러분께서 熱愛하여 주신 덕으로 재판까지 나갓슴니다. 감사하옵니다. 더욱더 꾸준히 「三千里」를 지도하여 주시기를 바라나이다.<200>
