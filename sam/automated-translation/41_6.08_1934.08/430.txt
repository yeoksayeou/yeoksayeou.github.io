食膳春色 (Sik Sŏn Ch'un Saek - Spring Colors on the Dining Table)

Fresh and crisp, the plump minari (water dropwort)
When chewed gently, its fragrance is high
Wrapping the green stems slowly
And skewering a strand of shredded red pepper askew
The small dining table is filled with the colors of spring

Splitting the mimyŏng clam in half, shell and all
Its soft, warm flesh looks so tempting
Two or three strands of fresh green thin onions
On top, vinegared red pepper paste – the taste of spring

Bracken, the curled young bracken fiddleheads
Plain, pure and white (淸白), that's all they are
If only these had existed even in their time
They could have saved Boyi and Shuqi (伯夷叔齊)

The slightly bitter mountain greens, another taste of spring
A village maiden (村시악시) with a basket (보구니) comes clearly to mind
Which young woman from what place dug these up, I wonder?
After eating, I felt grateful anew<180>

**Summary**

This poem, titled "Spring Colors on the Dining Table," describes the simple beauty and taste of various spring foods served on a Korean dining table. It evokes sensory details of fresh minari (water dropwort), clams with vinegared pepper paste, plain bracken fiddleheads, and slightly bitter mountain greens. The poet contemplates the origins of the food, imagining the village woman who gathered the greens, and feels gratitude. From a historical perspective, the poem offers a glimpse into the appreciation of traditional Korean cuisine and the connection to nature during the colonial period. The focus on simple, everyday Korean life and food, along with the reference to classical Chinese figures known for their integrity (Boyi and Shuqi), might subtly reflect a quiet cultural pride or an escape into pastoral aesthetics amidst the political realities of the time.

月灘詩篇
食膳春色

미라니 싱싱하다 살진 미나리
가많이 씹으면 향긔도 높네
푸른 줄기를 슬슬 감어서
실고초 한입 비슥 꼬지니
소반우에 봄빗 가득찻더라

미명조개 껍질째 반을 갈르니
부드러운 훈살 탐탐도하다
새파란 세파 두서너입
그 우에 초고초장 봄맛이러라

고사리 꼬부라진 어린 고사리
淡淡타 淸白 그것 뿐일레
철마저 이것 잇섯드라면
伯夷叔齊도 살려주는걸

쌉살한 山나물 또 봄맛인데
보구니 낀 村시악시 눈에 선하이
어느 곳 어떤處子 캐신 것인고
먹고나 새삼스레 고마웁더라<180>
