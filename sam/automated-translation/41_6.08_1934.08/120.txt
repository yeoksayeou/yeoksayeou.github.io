Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

A View on the Future of Hwasin Chain Stores, The Rise and Fall of the Peninsula's Commercial World and Hwasin's Crucial Battle

Hwasin (和信), which stands prominently at the Chongno intersection in Seoul as a department store managed by a Korean person, has recently announced a plan to establish Hwasin Chain Stores (和信連鎖店) to expand its commercial sphere nationwide across Chosŏn. Specifically, the plan is to establish approximately one thousand chain stores covering all regions of Chosŏn, unifying the distribution channels for daily necessities under Hwasin's control. This aims to reform the existing incomplete distribution system, facilitate the smooth circulation of goods, fully leverage the capabilities of the Hwasin Purchasing Department (和信仕入部) to rationalize product procurement for the benefit of the chain stores, and especially establish a guidance department to supervise each chain store in utilizing the latest sales techniques, thereby demonstrating the power of scientific management. It is observed that once this plan is completed, it will likely bring many benefits to general consumers, as the distribution of daily necessities will become smoother and goods will be provided at low prices by eliminating the profits of intermediary wholesalers. Furthermore, it may become a force for promoting the declining Chosŏn commercial sector and could serve as an institution to guide and train Chosŏn merchants who are unaware of how to break free from old customs.

Selecting Key Market Locations to Expand and Strengthen the Sales Network

So, what kind of plan does it have? Let's examine that first. Currently, the primary market for the vast majority of the populace for the distribution of daily necessities in Chosŏn can be considered the market (장) that gathers once every five days. <74> According to statistics from Showa 7 (1932), there were 1,481 market locations across Chosŏn. Excluding sparsely populated areas with completely inconvenient transportation, about one thousand locations are to be recruited and classified into four grades to form a large-scale Chosŏn-wide "chain." The current plan is as follows:
Grade 1: 68 locations
Grade 2: 352
Grade 3: 169
Grade 4: 413
In addition, 19 major metropolitan locations will be treated specially, totaling 1,023 locations. The plan is to establish one chain store in each of the 1,000+ candidate locations. Hwasin will first help with the circulation of funds, second, promote the rationalization of purchasing (仕入), and third, strive to improve sales methods, aiming to make the chain stores victors in commerce. The specific methods are:
One. Chain Store Operators do not need to provide a security deposit or engage in cash transactions but provide collateral to establish a mortgage (根抵當), receive supply of goods and capital financing, and engage in special long-term bill transactions with 150-day deferred payment (延拂).
One. Make reputable manufacturers at the place of origin (原産地) exclusive factories for Hwasin (和信專屬工場), or establish directly managed Hwasin factories (和信直營工場), thereby lowering production costs, eliminating intermediary profits, and improving product credibility based on the large-volume demand of one thousand stores and product standardization, resolving the procurement difficulties of each store.
One. Establish product distribution centers (商品配給所) in major port cities across Chosŏn and in Osaka to ensure smooth and rapid product distribution, and circulate goods by transferring unsold items from Region A to Region B to eliminate stagnant inventory (滯貨).
One. Establish a model store (模範商店) in Kyŏngsŏng and hold regular sample markets (代見本市) to facilitate convenient product purchasing and promote the exchange of commercial knowledge.
One. Establish a Guidance Department (指導部) at Hwasin Headquarters, dispatch guidance personnel to each store once every two months, issue a monthly report (月報), organize Osaka-Tokyo observation groups (大阪東京視察團), etc., to provide unified guidance and improvement of sales techniques, and strive to enlighten store owners' commercial knowledge.
Besides this, various departments related to Hwasin's planning (計劃) and investigation (調査) will refer to reports from chain store owners and guidance personnel to scientifically research and investigate product demand conditions, trend changes, and other matters related to store management. <75> The results will be used to determine the principles and direction for chain store management. To exert the power of low-cost purchasing (廉價仕入), each chain store will be encouraged to practice the principle of small profits and quick returns (박리다매주의), while also implementing fixed-price sales (정찰판매) to break down the bad customs of local markets that have yet to be reformed. By doing so, they aim to transfer all the aspects of today's Hwasin department store directly to the chain stores, securing a firm position in the sales market while providing consumers with cheap goods and beneficial service (써비쓰).
Such a plan is considered feasible due to Hwasin's unique position (독특한 지위) in the Chosŏn commercial world today and the special circumstances of the Chosŏn sales market, which is still developing. It can be called a uniquely Chosŏn plan. Hwasin is said to have been secretly conducting investigations into the economic situation and purchasing power of various markets and cities for over ten months already.
If this plan is realized, it is expected to become a major economic unit in Chosŏn, serving as a medium for regional development and an institution for training merchants, playing a significant role in building a commercial Chosŏn. Thus, general society is said to have high expectations.

Hwasin Chain Stores are a "Voluntary Chain"

A chain store refers to a retail store (산매상점) where multiple stores are unified under one central headquarters, engaging in unified purchasing (사입), sales (판매), and service (奉仕) to achieve large-volume purchasing and sales. It signifies that many stores are linked together like a chain (鏁). Its origin is in the United States, hence the English term "chain store." It is said that in the American retail sector, chain stores have developed significantly, accounting for over 12% (일활이분) of total sales.
In Berlin (백림) too, since small and medium-sized merchants and manufacturers fell into difficulties due to sluggish business, a movement arose to save them through joint purchasing (共同仕入), leading to the planning and development of the "voluntary chain," that is, the optional chain store (任意련쇄점) or free chain store (自由련쇄점). The current Hwasin Chain Store can also be seen as a type of this free chain store.
The difference between a free chain store and a regular chain store is that in a regular chain store, all the chain stores are entirely unified under and controlled by the central headquarters. In a free chain store, each store operates with independent accounting (*과 responsibility is missing, likely 계산과 책임 'accounting and responsibility') and is subject to the "chain's" control only for certain specific products. Examples of such free chain stores in Japan include the Tokyo Western Goods Alliance (東京洋品聯盟), Tokyo Stationery Chain (東京文具連鎖), Morinaga (森永) Belt Line, Shiseido (資生堂) Chain, etc. The development of free chain stores is seen as a measure to promote small and medium-sized merchants and manufacturers, drawing public attention as a new phenomenon in the commercial world.
Looking at the currently planned Hwasin Chain Store, there is no head office-branch relationship (본지점관게) <76> between Hwasin and each chain store. Each chain store operates with independent accounting, and close control is exercised only concerning:
One. Implementation of Joint Purchasing (共同仕入)
One. Improvement and Advancement of Sales Methods (販賣法)
and a few other conditions. Therefore, it should be called a type of free chain store. Consequently, while some new stores might be established, it is anticipated that many existing general merchandise stores will convert into Hwasin Chain Stores. It would not be an exaggeration to say that they might come to nearly monopolize the still underdeveloped goods distribution system in Chosŏn. It can be said that the responsibility of Hwasin Chain Stores towards urban general consumers is significant, and it is anticipated that in the future, the management rights of a chain store might be transferred and acquired with a premium (푸레미엄) attached, like a type of vested right (리권).

Leader Pak Hŭngsik's Character - What is the Secret Plan for the Second Stage?

Mr. Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植), the president of Hwasin, who is embarking on the enormous plan to establish over 1,000 chain stores across Chosŏn to provide daily necessities at low prices and build a Hwasin kingdom, can be called a businessman who has passed the test among Koreans in present-day Chosŏn. As the manager of three companies – Hwasin Co., Ltd. (株式會社和信), Sŏnil Chimul Co., Ltd. (鮮一紙物株式會社), and Sŏn'gwang Printing Co., Ltd. (鮮光印刷株式會社) – he enjoys deep trust both domestically and internationally (내외로부터) due to the skill he displays extensively and his humble, unpretentious personality.

After Mr. Pak acquired the Hwasin store, which was under the management of Mr. Sin T'aehwa (신태화씨), he poured his lifelong experience and skill into it. Through reorganizing it into a stock company, thoroughly reforming business policies, establishing an Osaka purchasing department (大板仕入部), acquiring the adjacent Tong'a Department Store (동아백화점), and relentlessly pioneering and expanding the business without pause, he has made Hwasin the sole Korean-managed department store in Kyŏngsŏng today, turning it into one of Kyŏngsŏng's famous landmarks (京城名物). This is still remembered as an astonishing fact in the peninsula's commercial world.
Besides Hwasin Department Store, Sŏnil Chimul Co., Ltd., managed by Pak Hŭngsik, is thriving with annual sales exceeding 3 million won, its sales network completed throughout Chosŏn and Manchuria. All newspaper companies in Chosŏn and Manchuria are said to purchase their newsprint from Sŏnil Company. Its sister company (자매회사), Sŏn'gwang Printing Co., Ltd., handles printing for various government offices (관청) and numerous regular publications (定긔刊物) in Chosŏn, showing excellent performance.
Seeing the smooth progress of these three major companies, Mr. Pak Hŭngsik's business acumen can be considered to have already passed the testing period. It is rumored that after seeing the completion of the Hwasin Chain Store project, he is formulating a secret plan (비책) for a second stage of activity. Mr. Pak's business leaps can be said to bring surprise and wonder each time to those looking towards the Chosŏn of tomorrow (明日의 朝鮮).<77>

"Hwasin's Success or Failure is a Matter of National Honor" - Remarks by Hwasin President Pak Hŭngsik

Upon announcing the Hwasin Chain Store plan, Hwasin President Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植) stated the following:
"In today's Chosŏn society, I believe the mission of Hwasin Department Store is not only very large but also has double and triple significance. While Hwasin must make its due contribution to the original mission of a department store – facilitating the smooth distribution of daily necessities and popularizing the benefits of civilization (文明의 리긔) among the masses – we take a step further. We believe it is also the responsibility of our Hwasin, walking at the forefront (첨단을 것는) of the Chosŏn commercial world, to awaken Chosŏn merchants, who are still mired in old habits and ignorant of scientific management methods, to the demands of the new era.
Moreover, among the Korean-owned stores in greater Kyŏngsŏng today, Hwasin is the one most noticed by people both inside and outside (내외인사). Therefore, whether Hwasin succeeds or fails is acknowledged by ourselves and others as the practical touchstone (試金石) proving whether Koreans are good at business or not. For this reason, the 300 employees of Hwasin are striving daily with sincerity and effort, with an apprehensive heart (송구한 마음), to make Hwasin better. In this sense, to make Hwasin truly 'Chosŏn's Hwasin' in name and reality, we have announced the plan to establish one thousand chain stores as a new project for the new year. I am confident that on the day this plan is completed, exemplary stores based on the latest scientific management methods will rise up in every nook and cranny of the 13 provinces (13도 방방곡곡), aiding local commerce and rural development (農村振興). Furthermore, through the provision of low-cost daily necessities resulting from the joint enterprise of one thousand stores and refined customer service (顧客奉仕), it will be great gospel (福音) to the consuming public."

A Study of Hwasin

From Gold and Silver Merchant Forty Years Ago to Today's Major Department Store, A Million-Won Company Towering in Chongno; Rumors of Secret Plans for New Building Construction

What history does Hwasin Department Store, which aims to seize hegemony in the commercial world at once by establishing one thousand chain stores across Chosŏn, possess? Hwasin, today one of Kyŏngsŏng's five major department stores and the only one managed by Korean hands, occupying a four-story Western-style building (四層洋屋) at the Chongno intersection (鐘路네거리), the center of Kyŏngsŏng's Korean district, and possessing a stately presence, traces its origins back over 40 years to a small Korean-run specialty shop dealing in gold, silver, and art objects (金銀美術品). Thirteen years ago, in 1924 (error in original, 1924 is 10 years before 1934, not 13; likely meant 1921 or calculation error), it expanded its business <78> to become a department store selling daily general merchandise; this was the first department store in Chosŏn under Korean management.
However, managing a department store at the cutting edge of a new era was not an easy task, and Hwasin's performance as a store did not meet expectations. Therefore, in September 1931, its organization was changed to a stock company. Mr. Pak Hŭngsik assumed the position of president, and Mr. Yi Kiyon (李基衍), who was the managing director (專務取締役) of Sŏnil Chimul Co., Ltd. managed by Mr. Pak Hŭngsik, took office as Hwasin's managing director. They began reforming the store, focusing all efforts on reorganizing the internal structure, remodeling the store, establishing the Osaka purchasing department, and training employees. As a result, business greatly improved, showing new vitality.
However, the following year, the large Tong'a Department Store appeared in an adjacent building, initiating intense competition, leading to a fierce battle (불꼿치 일어나는 백열전) that lasted for half a year. Victory eventually went to Hwasin, which acquired Tong'a Department Store under favorable conditions, making Hwasin the undisputed sole department store in northern Kyŏngsŏng in both name and reality. Since then, Hwasin connected its East and West wings (東西兩館) with a bridge (부릿지), improved the first-floor interior facilities, and strove for the perfection (盡善) of service (써비쓰). As a result, business further flourished, and today it has secured a firm position not just as Kyŏngsŏng's Hwasin but as Chosŏn's Hwasin.
Hwasin's rapid development thus far is astonishing. Now, its attempt to expand its commercial influence nationwide through the Hwasin Chain Store plan is a grand scheme (壯快한 計劃) that no one but Hwasin could imitate. According to rumors, following the chain store plan, there is an expansion plan being developed in utmost secrecy to construct a new Hwasin Building (和信삘딩) to create the foremost department store on this land, matching in both name and reality.

Organization to be Completed by July, Operations to Start from October

Hwasin, having laid out the plan to establish one thousand chain stores, has added over 50 employees and is proceeding with preparations day and night. Formal recruitment for chain stores is set to begin around the 15th of this month, with the organization to be fully completed by the end of July. Business operations are expected to commence in various locations by October at the latest. <79>

**Summary**

This 1934 article details the ambitious plan by Hwasin Department Store, led by entrepreneur Pak Hŭngsik, to establish a nationwide network of over 1,000 chain stores across colonial Korea (Chosŏn). The goal was to modernize the distribution of daily goods, lower consumer prices by rationalizing purchasing and eliminating middlemen, and provide guidance in modern business practices ("scientific management") to local Korean merchants. Structured as a "voluntary chain," individual stores would remain independent but cooperate under Hwasin's central purchasing and guidance system. The project was presented not just as a business expansion but as a crucial endeavor for the advancement of Korean commerce and even a matter of "national honor." For historians, this article offers a fascinating glimpse into Korean entrepreneurship and nationalist aspirations under Japanese rule. It highlights the adoption of modern capitalist structures (department stores, chain stores, stock companies), the challenges faced by Korean businesses, the strategic thinking involved in competing (implicitly, with Japanese capital), and the framing of economic activity as vital to national progress and identity during the colonial period. The sheer scale of the 1,000-store plan itself is noteworthy.

서울에서 조선사람 경영인 백화점으로 종로 네거리에 그 자태를 보이고 잇는 화신(和信)에서는 이번에 그 상권의 범위를 전조선적으로 확장하기 위하야 和信連鎖店을 설치할 게획을 발표하엿다. 즉 전조선 각 지방을 망라하야 약 일천여개소의 련쇄점을 설치하고 일용백화의 배급긔관을 화신의 손으로 통일하야 재래의 불완전한 배급게통을 개혁하야 물화류통을원활하게 하는 동시에 화신仕入部의 긔능을 백퍼센트로 발휘시켜 련쇄점의 리익을 위하야 상픔의 사입을 합리화하며 특히 지도부를 두어 각 련쇄점으로 하여금 최신식 판매술을 리용하도록 감독하야 과학적 경영의 위력을 발휘하리라고 한다. 이 게획이 완성되는 날에는 일반소비자로 보아서 일용픔의 배급이 원활해지고 또 중간도매업자의 리익을 업시하야 모든 상픔을 렴가로 제공하는 까닭으로 리익 밧는 점이 만흘 것 가치 관측된다. 그 뿐 아니라 쇠잔한 조선 상업게를 진흥시키는 힘이 될넌지 모르며 또 낡은 습관에서 버서날 줄을 모르는조선상인을 지도 훈련하는 긔관이 될 것가치 보인다.

市場樞要地를 選定 販賣綱을 擴大 强化

요. 이외에 특별히 취급을 하게 된 대도회지 19개소를 합하야 1,023개소라. 이상 1,000여 후보지에 각 각 련쇄점한 상점식을 설치하고 화신으로부터 첫재는 자금의 륭통을 도우며 둘재로는 사입(仕入)의 합리화를 도모하며 셋재로는 판매방법의 개선에 노력하야 련쇄점으로하여금 상업의 승리자가 되게하려는 것이라 한다. 그 구체적 방법으로는
