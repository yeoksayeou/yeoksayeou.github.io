Temporary Hire

A certain man, after catching sight of a nobleman's (華族) daughter over a stone wall, fell head over heels in love with her. After much thought, he wrote a long, long... love letter, tied it to a stone, and tossed it into the young lady's garden.
The next day, a reply was placed atop the stone wall. It read, "Starting tonight, for one hundred days, come to the front of our house every night and draw a line on this stone wall. If you do so, I will grant your wish." Overjoyed, the man went every night from that day on, whether it rained, the wind blew, or it snowed.
On the ninety-ninth night, he appeared in front of the house as usual, drew a line on the stone wall, and as he was turning back, the house's servant appeared and said, "The young mistress has spoken. She praises your effort in coming every night all this time and says she will forgive one night, so please come inside right away."
At that moment, the man stammered, "W-well, no, no," reluctant to follow right away.
"Why is that?"
"Actually, I just received thirty *chŏn* a day to come here every night and draw the line. The real young master (도렌님) only came on the first night!"

Tremendous Confidence

Sukcha, Ŭnhŭi, and Okyŏp, three old school friends, met at the Chongno intersection. They decided to go up to a department store and have a glass of soda water each.
"Well then, who's paying?"
"Let's do this. How about the one among the three of us who has the most handsome husband pays?"
At that moment, the three women cried out simultaneously,
"Oh no, that won't do. Then I'll be the one paying."

Crucial Moment

"I've decided not to drink unless it's a considerable occasion."
"A considerable occasion?"
"When I want to drink."<138>

Truth

Oksun: "Hey. Could it really be true? The Bible tells the story of Adam and Eve. Did Adam truly love Eve?"
Kyŏngsik: "Of course, he did. It was a time when there were no other women to court."<139>
<137-139>

**Summary**

This article presents four short jokes from 1934. The first tells of a man hired to court a noblewoman by proxy. The second features three women who each believe their husband is the most handsome. The third defines a "crucial moment" for drinking as simply wanting a drink. The fourth humorously suggests Adam loved Eve only because there were no other options. From a historical perspective, the jokes offer glimpses into social dynamics and attitudes of the time, such as class distinctions (nobility and hired help), evolving gender roles and perceptions (women meeting publicly, discussing husbands' looks), everyday leisure (soda water at department stores), and common knowledge (Bible stories). The first joke, involving a "temporary hire" for courtship and the mention of a "nobleman" (華族), subtly reflects the social hierarchy and perhaps the economic struggles that might lead someone to take such a job.

笑話
臨時雇

엇든 華族의 따님을 돌각담 너머 한번 보고나서는 죽을 드시 반하여 버린 한 남자, 생각다 못하여, 길고긴... 연애편지를 써서 돌에 싸서 그 색시 뜰압헤 뿌렷다.
그랫더니 그 이튼날 돌각담 우에 회답 한 장이 노엇는데 「오늘 저녁으로부터 백일 동안 밤마다, 우리집 압해 와서 이 돌각담 우에 금을 긋고 가시오. 그러면 뜻대로 하리다」 하는 것이잇다. 그 남자는 깁버서 그날 밤부터 밤마다 다니엇다, 비오거나, 바람불거나, 눈오거나.
그래서 아흔 아홉재 밤에 여전히 그 압헤 나타나 돌각담에 금을 긋고 도라질세, 그집 하인이 나타나서 「아씨께서 말슴하섯서요, 그동안 밤마다 와 주시는 은공을 치하하시면서, 하로밤쯤은 탕감하여 드릴 터이니 어서 드러오시라고오」
그때 그 사내는 「저-아니-아니구」 하면서 얼는 그 뒤를 따르려 하지 안는다.
「엇재서 그래요?」
「실상은 저는 하로 삼십전씩 박고 그저 밤마다 와서 금만 끄엇서요. 정작 도렌님은 첫날밤만 오섯구요!」

굉장한 自信

淑子, 銀姬, 玉葉의 녯날의 세 학교 동무가 鍾路 네거리에서 맛낫다. 그래서 백화점에 올너가 소-다水 한 잔씩 먹기로 하엿다.
『그럼 돈은 누가 내나?』
『이러케 하지. 셋 중에 제일 美男子를 남편으로 가진 분이 낼 내기할가.』
그때 세 여자 동시에 소리 잇서 曰
『에그, 그건 안돼. 그럼 내가 내게요.』
