Overseas Masterpiece Poetry Collection
Five Translated Poems

Translated by Yi Hwa-yun

Little Things

-James Stephens-

Running away, startled in fear,
Little things that die in Silence and Despair.

Fighting and losing, upon the Earth,
Little things that fall upon the sea and the Air.

All little things startled by the trap,
Mouse, rabbit, hear our prayer (긔돌).

As we forgive the sins (죌) we committed against ourselves,
Lamb, sparrow, and also rabbit,

Forgive all our sins,
Oh little ones, present everywhere.

-James Stephens-

Suddenly, a cry of pain is heard!<182>
A rabbit is caught in a trap.
Now that sound comes again,
But I cannot tell where it comes from.

But I cannot tell from where
The cry calling for rescue comes.
Screaming upon the startled air,
Making everything afraid,

Making everything afraid,
Wrinkling its small face (낫흘),
When it cries out for rescue once more,
I cannot find that place.

Where its ankle is caught in the trap,
That place I cannot find.
Little thing, oh little thing, little thing!
I search here and there.

To a Curlew

W.B. Yeats

Oh Curlew, cry no more high in the sky,
But cry only from the western sea.
Because your cry makes me long for the two eyes dark with passion
And the long, heavy hair that trembled and scattered over my breast,
There is endless sorrow in that weeping wind.

A Drinking Song

W.B. Yeats

Wine enters through the mouth,
And love enters through the eyes;
That is all the Truth we shall know
Before we grow old and die.
I lift the glass to my mouth,
I look at you, and I sigh.

He Wishes for the Cloths of Heaven

W.B. Yeats

Had I the heavens’ embroidered cloths,
Enwrought with golden and silver light,
The blue and the dim and the dark cloths
Of night and light and the half-light,<183>
I would spread the cloths under your feet:
But I, being poor, have only my dreams;
I have spread my dreams under your feet;
Tread softly because you tread on my dreams.
Translator's Note - Although one or two of these have already been published, as I prepared the translation collection "失香의 花園" (Garden of Lost Fragrance), I revised them all, so I present them together thinking they may be no different from new translations.

The Search for Truth

Translated by Kim Kwang-sŏp

Crossing the blue shadow where blue light seeps through,
The lamp light shines down upon me like "Truth,"
Warming the Books with many joyful Rays,
Refusing the succession of the encroaching night.
But when I search for "Truth," this Woman stealthily flees,

Dwelling among the brown bees in the deep mountain valley (골채기),
Among the shadowy Caves on a scorching hot day,
In those rough Dwellings.

Then I am a traveler (客) lost on the continent of "Doubt,"
Standing in the night of "Doubt,"
Seeking a Signal of the Gospel like a Prophet's Altar.
That Signal which guided the "Children" returning with heads bowed silently,
Which moved across the "Red Sea" (亦海) like a cloud,
Which split the night of Egypt with a pillar of fire.

Epitaph

Y. French, D. Batchelor

She lies covered by the star-strewn sky.
She was once the wonder and joy of the World.
April was but an Imitator of her two eyes.
Now she is the dead Mystery of the night.

Autumn Song

Edgar Poxhall
Now this Gloomy time
Recites the sad soul of bygone days.
Brown pinecones fall endlessly,
The grass has withered and died.
The dew-covered Earth shows wheel tracks.
Cry, nightingale!
Come, rain clad in the mantle of the Gray wind.<184>
My heart is dark, unable to find
A joyful Time to attach my feelings to.
What to do? Even the crows gather sorrowfully,
Lamenting the Season that has gone.
The Weather Vane is buried in the dense fog.
Cry, nightingale!
Come, rain clad in the mantle of the Gray wind.

The Rock (Prose Poem)

By Turgenev Translated by Ham Tae-hun
Have you seen the old gray rock by the seashore?
On a shining spring day, at high tide, have you seen how the lively Waves from all directions strike towards the rock, Dance Playfully (戱舞하며), Court it (拘愛하야), showering its moss-covered head like scattering expensive (갑빛 싼) Pearls about to break?
The rock remains the same rock, but a Bright (明朗한) light flows from its entire Gloomy Exterior (外面全體).
That light proves the old days when the Molten (溶解된) Granite (花崗石) had just begun to harden and was Burning (타고잇든) on one side with firelight.
Thus, the heart of a young Woman of bygone days Assaulted (襲擊하여) my old heart from all directions—due to this gentle Contact (接觸), my Heart (心臟) is already dyed with the old, worn-out light—with the Traces (痕跡) of past flames.
The waves receded (써나갓다), but the light has not yet faded (낡지않엇다).
Even though a rough person, if only they could dry up (말리워나 스면)!
(July, 1879)<185>

**Summary**

This article presents Korean translations of several poems by Western authors: James Stephens, W.B. Yeats, Y. French & D. Batchelor, an "Edgar Poxhall," and a prose poem by Turgenev. The translations were done by Yi Hwa-yun, Kim Kwang-sŏp, and Ham Tae-hun. The themes range from empathy for small creatures and reflections on mortality and love (Yeats, Stephens) to melancholy (Poxhall, Yeats' "Curlew"), the search for truth (Kim Kwang-sŏp's translation), and memories evoked by nature (Turgenev). Yi Hwa-yun notes some poems were revised for a forthcoming collection. For historians studying colonial Korea, this collection showcases the active engagement with and introduction of Western literature through translation, reflecting efforts by Korean intellectuals to broaden cultural horizons despite colonial constraints. The specific choice of poems (often dealing with sensitivity, loss, longing, or introspection) and the act of translation itself can be seen as subtle forms of cultural assertion and intellectual activity during a period of limited expression.

작은 것

-제임스. 스틔븐스-

다라나며 무서워 놀내이면서
沈黙과 絶望속에 죽는 작은것

싸호며 敗하면서 大地우에와
바다와 大氣우에 떠러지는 작은것

덧에 치어 놀내인 모든 작은것
생쥐여 토끼여 우리 긔돌(祈禱) 드르라

우리가 우리게 진 죌(罪) 사해줌가치
어린양아 참새야 그리고 또 토끼

우리 죄를 모도다 사하여주렴
업는 곳 업시 잇는 작은놈들아



-제임스. 스틔븐스-

들니느니 별안간 고통의 웨침!<182>
덧속에 토끼하나 걸닌 것이다
이제다시 그 소리 들녀는 오나
어듸서 나는 겐지 알 수 업노라

그러치만 어듸서 구원 어드려
부르는 소리인지 알 수 업노라
놀내인 대기우에 소리지르며
무엇이나 모도다 두렵게 하며

무엇이나 모도다 두렵게 하며
작은 낫흘 우이로 찝흐리면서
다시 한번 구원을 부르지즐때
그곳을 나는 찻지 못하겟노라

그 발목이 덧속에 끼여 잇는곳
그곳을 나는 찻지 못하겟노라
작은 것아 작은 것 작은 것이어!
이곳저곳 다니며 나는 찻노라

투르계-넵흐 作  咸大勳 譯
그대는 바다가에 잇는 늙은 灰色빛 바위를 보앗는가?
빛나는 봄날 滿朝된 때에 四方으로 生生한 波浪이 바위돌을 向하여 따리고 戱舞하며 拘愛하야 갑빛 싼 眞珠가 되여 깨처질 뜻이 그의 이끼덥힌 머리에 사르르 퍼붓는 양을 보앗는가?
바위는 依然히 같은 바위지만 그 陰鬱한 外面全體에는 明朗한 빛이 흘러나온다.
그빛은 溶解된 花崗石이 겨우 굳어지기 始作하야 一面 불빛에 타고잇든 그 면 옛날을 證明하고 잇는 것이다
이와같이 나의 늙은 마음을 지나간 날 젊은 女子의 마음에 四方으로 襲擊하여-이 보드러운 接觸때문에 내 心臟은 벌서 옛날 낡어 빠진 빛으로- 지난날의 불꽃 痕跡으로 染色되엿다
문결은 써나갓다 그렇나 빛은 아즉 낡지않엇다
거치른 사람인망정 말리워나 스면!
