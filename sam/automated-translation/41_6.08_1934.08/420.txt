Anseo's Poetry Collection
Samsugapsan

I long to see Samsugapsan, where is Samsugapsan?
Samsugapsan is distant, ah, a place where only layers upon layers of white clouds gather.

I long to go to Samsugapsan, I cannot go to Samsugapsan,
The road to Samsugapsan is long, ah, three days by boat, three days by water, the road is long.

Where is Samsugapsan? I cannot go to Samsugapsan,
Never returning, never returning, this heart, ah, if it were a bird, it would fly, fly away.

Samsugapsan, my hometown, I cannot go, I cannot go,
Coming and going, indifferent, ah, a dream of going because I long for Samsugapsan.

Samsugapsan, the long, long road, I long to go, I cannot go,
Never returning, never returning, this heart, ah, this feeling of not being able to go to Samsugapsan.

Old Mountain Fortress

Longing for the old mountain fortress, I went up,
The fortress had crumbled, and grass had grown.
In the thatched hut, indifferent, a flock of sparrows,
As if enjoying themselves, they just chirp and sing.

What happened to this once splendid mountain fortress?
Trying to find old traces, there is no way to find them,
Only the spring light is the same as before,
This mountain fortress makes my heart desolate.

Building a fortress in the heart, setting up a thatched hut,
Hoping perhaps to serve my lord was my wish,
But as the harsh worldly winds swirl around,
My lord has gone, and today, even the house leans.

Sŏgwan Girl

Indifferently, in the spring breeze, flowers bloom,
And vainly, in that breeze, they fall.
Isn't it sorrowful, Sŏgwan girl?
Shall I say pitiful, young lady?

Today too, on Nŭngnado, the willow catkins
Drift alone on the water and flow away.<178>
Isn't it sorrowful, Sŏgwan girl?
Shall I say pitiful, young lady?

It's not just flowers that are easy to fall,
This bright green youth is also but a moment.
Isn't it sorrowful, Sŏgwan girl?
Shall I say pitiful, young lady?

Sigh

It is the water of the Taedong River in P'yŏngyang flowing out,
Though I try hard not to think of it, I miss it.
Again, this feeling becomes lonely,
This heart, all alone, pounds heavily.

Shall I say hateful? If I do, it is hateful,
If I think of longing, I long again.
Whether awake or in dreams, there's no way to forget,
In this unjust feeling, I weep.

Even the bird flying in the sky has wings,
Doesn't it circle the mountain when coming and going?
Unforgettable, resentful, my insides are wounded,
This heart, all alone, breaks apart.

Welcoming Spring

Chosŏn's very center, Sŏul's Samgaksan Mountain,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! To the south is Chongnamsan Mountain.
On the highest peak, green leaves play with the wind,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! Let's go welcome spring.

As the fluttering spring breeze swirls around,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! Flower blossoms bloom.
Blossom by blossom, the flowers smile light pink,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! Let's go see the flowers.

Blossom by blossom, the flowers are my heart's blossoms,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! They play with butterflies.
The heart's blossoms, too, dance in light pink,
Hurrah, wonderful, Jihwaja! Let's gather the heart's blossoms.

Tobacco Song

In the wide field, bright green tobacco leaves,
Fluttering, fluttering in the wind, they welcomed me,
But today, their bodies are dead and enter the fire,
Thinking this way, this body becomes tobacco.

Packing it in a pipe and smoking, only black smoke appears,
Puffing, puffing, it drifts in the empty air and disappears,
Who would know the traces of this life?
Thinking this way, this body becomes tobacco.

Friends, one by one, all leave,
Thick love, indifferent, cools easily,
But the brightly burning tobacco fire does not cool,
Thinking this way, this body becomes tobacco.<179>

**Summary**

This collection of six poems by "Anseo" expresses themes of longing, loss, the passage of time, and existential reflection. The poems touch on nostalgia for a lost hometown (Samsugapsan), the ruins of past glory ("Old Mountain Fortress"), the fleeting nature of youth and beauty ("Sŏgwan Girl"), persistent sorrow and longing ("Sigh"), a brief moment of patriotic joy in spring ("Welcoming Spring"), and a metaphor of life as ephemeral as tobacco smoke ("Tobacco Song"). For historians, the poems offer insight into personal sentiments during the colonial period, possibly reflecting displacement or restricted movement ("Samsugapsan"), a sense of lost heritage ("Old Mountain Fortress"), and perhaps subtle expressions of national identity through references to specific Korean locales like P'yŏngyang, Sŏul, Samgaksan, and Namsan, framed within the broader context of "Chosŏn". The melancholy tone prevalent in most poems might also reflect the general atmosphere of the era under colonial rule.

岸曙詩集
三水甲山

三水甲山 보고지고 三水甲山 어듸매냐,
三水甲山 아득타아하 三은 첩첩 흰구름만 싸인 곳

三水甲山 가고지고 三水甲山 내 못가네,
三水甲山 길 멀다 아하 배로 사흘 물로 사흘길 멀다.

三水甲山 어듸메냐, 三水甲山 내못가네,
不歸不歸 이내맘 아하새드라면 날아날아가련만.

山水甲山 내 故鄕을 내 못가네, 내 못가네,
오락가락 無心타 아하 삼水甲山 그립다고 가는 꿈

三水甲山 먼먼길을 가고지고 내못가네
不歸不歸 이내 맘 아하 三水甲山 내못가는 이 心思

옛山城

옛山城이 그리워 올나갓노라
山城은 문허지고 풀이 자랏네
草堂엔 無心타 참새의 무리
興이라고 지죄죄 노래만하네.

호화롭든 이 山城 어인 일인고
옛자최는 차즈랴 차즐길 업고
한갓되이 봄빗만 예와 가튼가
이山城은 나의 맘 쓸쓸도 하이.

맘우에 城을 싸코 草堂세운것
幸여님을 모실가 所願이드니
거츤世上 바람이 휘도는 통에
님은 가고 이날엔 집도기우네.

西關아가씨

無心타 봄바람에 꼿은 폇다가
헛되이 그바람에 지고맘니다
시럽지 안을가요 西關아가씨
가엽다 말을할가 졀므신아씨.

오늘도 綾羅島라 버들개지는
물우를 혼자 돌다 흘너감니다<178>
서럽지 안을 가요 西關아가씨
가엽다 말을 할가 졀므신 아씨.

떨니기 쉬운 것은 꼿만 아니요
새팔한 이 靑春도 잠간입니다
서럽지 안을가요 西關아가씨
가엽다 말을 할가 졀므신 아씨.

嘆息

平壤에 大同江물 나간 것이라
생각을 애에말가 해도 그리워
다시금 요 心思가 외롭길내로
이가슴 혼자로서 쾅쾅침니다.

얄밉다 말을 할가 하니 얄밉고
그립다 생각하니 다시 그리워
生時랴 꿈 속에랴 니즐길 업서
어굴한 요心思에 내가 움니다.

空中을 나는 새도 깃을 둿길레
오갈 제 山을싸고 돌지 안트냐
못니저 원수라고 속이 상킬래
이 가슴 혼자로서 부서댑니다.

봄마지

朝鮮이라 한복판 서울 三角山
얼사죠타 지화자 南은 終南山.
上上峯에 파란닙 바람과 논다
얼사죠타 지화자 봄마중가자.

하늘하늘 봄바람 휘오는 통에
얼시조타 지화자 꼿송이 피네.
송이송이 꼿송이 연분홍 웃네
얼사죠타 지호자 꼿구경 가자.

송이송이 꼿송인 이내 맘송이
얼사죠타 지화자 나비와 노네
맘송이도 연분홍 춤을 추거니
얼사죠타 지화자 맘송이 줍자.

담배노래

넓은들에 새팔한 담배입사귀
바람에 나풀나풀 반겨들더니
이날엔 몸이 죽어 불속에 드네
이리생각 이 몸은 담배 되노라.

대에 담아 피우니 검은煙氣뿐
무력무럭 虛空을 돌다지거니
이 인생의 자최를 뉘랴알것가
이리생각 이 몸은 담배되노라.

親故들은 하나둘 모다 떠나고
덥은 사랑 無心타 쉬이 식으나
이글이글 담배불 안 식는것을
이리생각 이 몸은 담배 되노라.<179>
