**Body Text Translation:**
Idle Talk Room

Among literary figures, those who have not yet visited Mount Kŭmgang are as follows. They keep intending to go but haven't managed it.
Chu Yohan, Pak P'aryang, Kim P'albong, Pak Yŏnghŭi, Yi T'aejun, Kim Tonghwan

On the contrary, those who have already visited are Yi Kwangsu, Yang Paekhwa, Kim Ansŏ, Yi Ŭnsang, Kim Tongin, Chŏng Inbo, Yi Pyŏnggi, Yi Yunjae<154>

**Summary**

This short piece lists prominent Korean writers of the 1930s, dividing them into two groups: those who have yet to visit the famous Mount Kŭmgang despite wanting to, and those who have already made the trip. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it names key literary figures of the colonial period and references Mount Kŭmgang, a site of significant cultural and national importance. It subtly reflects the status and perhaps the leisure or means available to these intellectuals, making it a minor but potentially useful data point for social and cultural historians studying the era.
