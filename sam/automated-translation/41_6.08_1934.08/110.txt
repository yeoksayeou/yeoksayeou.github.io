**Body Text Translation:**
Addressing the Young Followers of Cheondogyo
						Kim Kyŏng-jae
I.

The article "Cheondogyo at the Crossroads of Rise and Fall" published in the July issue of *Samch'ŏlli* could not be written fully due to circumstances. However, even that crippled piece of writing seems to have caused a considerable reaction among Cheondogyo followers, as the issue is burning up here and there in the form of resolutions or warnings, and thus, a purification effect is beginning to sprout within the new faction of Cheondogyo.
At this time, faced with the reality that the Cheondogyo issue and the followers of the new Cheondogyo faction are wandering in a maze, unsure whether to turn left or right due to unexpected [censored], I keenly felt the need for another word of critique.

It was in the 11th year of King Ch'ŏlchong's reign, 74 years ago from now, that Ch'oe Che-u proclaimed the doctrines of Tonghak (東學). At that time, the political corruption in Chosŏn had reached its peak; from the royal family down to the petty functionaries in local government offices, they were destroying the lives of the populace through lewd, cruel, unjust, and barbaric methods. Confucianism (儒敎), which had traditionally been the pillar of the Chosŏn people's thought, ethics, morality, and customs, had fallen into degeneracy, leaving the populace spiritually and socially adrift, not knowing where to turn.

Whenever a social fact surfaces, there is a cause for its occurrence, a driving force, and thus it manifests as a result. Therefore, the cause of the emergence of Tonghak must also be sought in the social conditions of that time.
"Religion has at its base the process of material production and reflects the relations of domination and subordination of the secular world<66>. Accordingly, the religion of each era must be examined to determine from what material source it grew."

The social conditions for the emergence of Tonghak can be viewed from two aspects: one is the domestic condition, and the other is international influence.
After Hŭngsŏn Taewŏn'gun seized political power, the lives of the peasants faced even more severe [censored]. While stubbornly rejecting the waves of capitalist civilization and holding up the policy of seclusion (鏁國) as his sole banner, he forced the masses under the feudal domain and sought to enhance the dignity of the royal family by rebuilding Kyŏngbokkung Palace (景福宮). The result was the raising of various tax rates and the intensification of the people's corvée labor (夫役), leading the rural villages to further [censored]. The circulation of the Tangbaekchŏn (當百錢) coin, worth less than 1/20th of its face value, brought about a major collapse (破綻) in the economic structure.

At that time, neighboring China (中國) was suffering from the fierce invasion (侵畧) of capitalist powers such as Britain (英), Russia (露), France (佛), and America (米), its national strength (國勢) weakening day by day. In various parts of Southern China, the rebellion (叛亂) of Hong Xiuquan had arisen, and thus the 4,000-year-old great nation (老大國) of Qing China (淸國) faced a crisis like a lamp in the wind (風前燈火). In Japan (日本), the Meiji Restoration (明治維新) was carried out, and the spirit (氣魄) of a newly rising nation seemed to soar to the heavens (衝天).

These circumstances and general trends led to a fierce confrontation (對立) between the enlightenment (開化) and conservative (守舊) factions politically in Chosŏn. Subsequently, with the Kapsin Coup (甲申政變) of 1885 as the final event, the anti-feudal movement of Kim Ok-kyun and others met the fate (運命) of utter defeat. Chosŏn's capitalism thus forever lost the opportunity for sound and normal development, but the aforementioned internal and external situations made the Chosŏn people of that time yearn (憧憬) for the pioneering of some new path.

The established religions (旣成宗敎) at that time were Confucianism (儒敎) and Buddhism (佛敎), and Catholicism (天主敎) had entered in the 7th year of King Chŏngjo's reign with its tablet (位牌) of the Trinity. Confucianism had become the so-called "great foundation for governing the country" (治國의 大本) of the Yi Dynasty and served as the theoretical vanguard for [censored] the common people, so the populace was unlikely to turn to it for refuge (歸依). Buddhism (佛敎), due to its numerous sins (罪惡) in the late Koryŏ period serving as the warp and the continuous [censored] policies of the Yi Chosŏn government serving as the weft, was completely expelled (驅逐) to remote mountain areas. Catholicism (天主敎)<67>, though imported auspiciously as the vanguard of foreign culture, suffered greatly (受難) on its path of development due to the Taewŏn'gun's stubborn seclusion policy (鏁國政策). Under these circumstances, Ch'oe Che-u emerged, seeking to discover a new, unknown refuge (未知數의 새롭은 歸依處). This became the condition for the emergence of Tonghak.

Therefore, Ch'oe Che-u's role (役割) was none other than this. He avidly read (耽讀) books of Confucianism, Buddhism, Taoism, etc., found the points of agreement (合致點) among the three teachings therein, and used them as his own reference (參考). At the same time, to confront (對峙) the so-called Western Learning (西學), Catholicism, he put up the name Tonghak. On one hand, he aimed to gather the populace and create the strength and opportunity to [censored] the ruling class (支配階級), and on the other hand, he sought to reveal (發露) hatred (憎惡心) towards [censored] nations to escape government [censored], and also tried to overcome the national crisis that was worsening day by day.
Thus, in his Proclamation Text (布德文), he stated, "As the ancient way declined, Western Religion took advantage of this to proselytize, and the nation's destiny has become perilous (危殆) because of it. I have received a divine oracle (神託) from the Supreme Being and possess a divine talisman (神符)." He claimed that Western Religion was a religion that seized nations and destroyed the people, asserting that the only path to survival (生途) for the Chosŏn populace lay in Tonghak. However, Ch'oe Che-u was executed by decapitation (斬刑) at the Taegu execution ground on March 10, 1864, at the age of 41, under the charge (罪目) of "misleading the people with heretical ways" (左道惑民). Ch'oe Che-u was the son of Ch'oe Ok, a poor scholar (寒士) from Kyŏngju. From childhood, he aspired to enter government service (官界進出), but the political situation (政情) at the time involved four-color factional strife (朋黨) above, vying to monopolize (獨占) political power, while below, the ranks of petty officials (吏胥) specialized (專業) in bribery (收賂) and plunder (掠奪). Thus, entering government service was by no means easy for an unknown poor scholar. Therefore, he changed his initial aspiration and devoted himself (潛心) to studying the Way at the foot of Kumi Mountain, which must have greatly influenced Che-u's intellectual inclination. Later, Ch'oe Hae-wŏl inherited Che-u's will and engaged in proselytizing while moving from place to place, gaining considerable influence throughout the Southern Chosŏn (南鮮) region. Consequently, Hae-wŏl appealed (呼訴) to the government several times to gain legality (合法性) for Tonghak, but failed. When the Tonghak Rebellion occurred in 1895 [error in text, should be 1894], he fled east and west to avoid the calamity (禍亂), but was eventually captured (被捉) and met his end, also at the execution ground, in 1899 [error in text, likely 1898] at the age of 72. Tonghak, having suffered irreparable calamity (慘禍) due to the Tonghak Rebellion, gradually saw its influence disappear like clouds (雲消) in the Southern Chosŏn region. Son Ŭi-am became active in the Northwestern Chosŏn (西北鮮) region,<68> focusing on recruiting new followers. At one time, he formed a major force, popularly said to have 3 million followers. Later, Son Ŭi-am participated (加坦) in [-] and contracted an illness (得病) in [x], was released on bail (保釋) and received treatment, but eventually passed away.
Thus, the past of Cheondogyo was indeed a history of suffering (受難), and it is also true that the leaders of Cheondogyo were always pioneers (先驅者) of the populace.

We know that for a thing to be formed (生成), there must be sufficient soil (培土) for its formation, and the environment must be suitable. Therefore, the fact that Cheondogyo arose and grew in Chosŏn, achieving such development in just seventy years, means that it conformed (順應) to the demands of the Chosŏn populace at the time.
Religion, regardless of what it is, uses the realistic suffering (苦難) of this world (現世) as its basis, exploiting the poverty and sickness of the populace to plot its own development. Therefore, the fact that Ch'oe Suun [Ch'oe Che-u's courtesy name] advocated Suun-ism in response to the political situation of Chosŏn at that time, and that it developed and grew to such an extent, particularly centered around the poor and lowly classes, in an extremely short period, means that there is a unique characteristic of Chosŏn that cannot be discussed uniformly, which we believe. Here, no one can deny the fact that in the Tonghak Rebellion and other events influenced by Cheondogyo, a large number of peasants participated. Despite mobilizing such a large number of peasants, they failed to lead the masses effectively. Instead, they utilized the peasants' ideological tendency, rooted in a spirit of reliance (依憑精神) on natural forces, to create an opportunity (契機) for expanding the influence (勢力擴充) of their own sect. In that era, ignorant of the capitalist economic structure and class consciousness, they could not help but follow that path.
Even in the advanced nations (先進諸國) of Europe (歐羅巴), we see that during the transition from the Middle Ages (中世) to modern times (近世), most peasant [censored] were generally disguised (扮裝) as religious sects. This was because, in the early medieval period, people knew no other form of ideology besides religion and theology (神學).

Thus, Cheondogyo, which emerged riding the tide of historical inevitability (必然), was believed to be a faithful apostle (使徒) to historical progress (進展). The life of Cheondogyo also lay therein. At a time when the benefits (惠澤) of the so-called Industrial Revolution (産業革命) were still a distant (遙遠) future (將來) for us, and even medieval commerce and industry, the first stage of the capitalist system, had not yet sprouted (發芽),<69> it was a natural course (當然途程) to be captured by religious [censored] thought without possessing a scientific ideology. Since then, various situations in Chosŏn have been changing with lightning speed (電光石火的), and efforts by Cheondogyo followers to adapt (順應) accordingly were also visible. However, time is creating (創造) history. This ceaseless historical progress, before we knew it...
"Time, which does not stop (停止), ultimately establishes enormous differences (相異) between the forms (形態) that existed in past eras, the forms that exist now, and the forms that will exist in future centuries."
During the lifetime of the late Yang Han-muk, he tried to embrace the nihilistic (虛無主義的) theories of Laozi (老子) and Zhuangzi (莊子) to expound (祖述) Suun-ism (水雲主義). Later, Yi Ton-hwa (夜雷 李敦化) attempted to expound Suun-ism, the traditional amalgam of Confucianism, Buddhism, and Taoism, while also referencing (參考) some (若干) ideas from Russell (럿셀), Bakunin (바구닝), etc., and adding (添附) Marx's (맑스) economic theory.
However, the progress of Special Chosŏn did not lag in tempo. And



Seventy-four years after Ch'oe Suun proclaimed Tonghak, the organizational changes (組織變遷) of Cheondogyo during that period can be broadly divided into three phases. The first phase is the period when it existed as a secret society (秘密團體) with Ch'oe Suun and Hae-wŏl as leaders (敎主). The second phase is from after Pyŏngo [1906], when leader Son Ŭi-am transformed the previous underground organization into a legal entity, until the emergence (擡頭) of the class movement in Chosŏn after Kimi [1919]. The third phase can be considered from when Ch'oe Rin became the leader of Cheondogyo until today.
Cheondogyo, having existed as a secret organization for a long time, came to possess a unique (獨特한) organizational form: it was formed centered around a certain natural person (自然人). For example, if an individual surnamed Pak taught Tonghak to both Mr. Kim and Mr. Yi, they would always become subordinate (從屬) to Mr. Pak. If Mr. Kim or Mr. Yi then recruited 10 followers or 100 followers in the same way, those followers would be subordinate to the aforementioned Kim or Yi, while also having a subordinate relationship to Pak.<70> The Jŏpju system (接主制度) of the Hae-wŏl era, the P'odŏksa (布德師) of the Ŭi-am era, and more recently, the Tojŏngje (道正制), etc., though the names changed several times, the basic organizational method remained the same. Therefore, Cheondogyo is always centralized (中央集權的) and thus requires a dictatorial (獨裁的) figure (人格者). Consequently, the direction (動向) of Cheondogyo depends on the will (意思) and actions of that dictator. However, for the past 70 years, during both the Tonghak and Cheondogyo periods, the leaders generally understood (諒察) the will of the followers and did not engage in political actions (政治行爲) contrary (背馳) to it. Those who guided political maneuvering (政治工作) contrary to it were eventually excluded (排除) and came to natural extinction (自然消滅). That was extremely fortunate.
Thus, having superhuman (超人格的) faith (信仰) towards the leader, [one line deleted by NIKH] obedience (服從) and implementation (實行) has been the consistent phenomenon and custom of the followers.

The origin (起源) of religion is said to have begun with nature worship (自然崇拜). When our knowledge was extremely primitive (幼稚), we went to the mountains, saw trees too large to fathom, feared them, and worshipped (崇拜) them. We went to rivers and seas deeper than measure, and there too, fear (恐佈心) arose (發作), leading us to worship them. Later, changes in the mode of material production (物質生産樣式) accordingly changed the form (形態) of religion: the object of faith changed from polytheism (多神) to pantheism (汎神), and then to monotheism (一神).
It was extremely unpleasant (不快), as if we humans were being greatly insulted (侮辱), to hear that humans are descendants of apes (猿猩). However, the development of biology (生物學) and archaeology (考古學) has shown conclusive proof (確證) that humans are descendants of apes, based on numerous excavated artifacts (發堀物) and fossilized remains (遺骨化石) of primitive humans. Thus, the existence of God is being conquered (征服) through various mutual conflicts (相互衝突) among concepts like Deism (理神), Fetishism (物神), Personal God (人格神), Incarnation (現身), etc., alongside the development of science. However, Cheondogyo, with its Innaech'ŏn-ism (人乃天主義 - Man is Heaven doctrine), differs from traditional religions in acknowledging (承認) Darwin's (따윈) theory of evolution (進化論). Yet, in today's world where it has become common knowledge (常識的) that the driving force (動因) of social systems, whether false (虛僞) or true and good (眞善), depends on the contradiction (矛盾) between the development of productive forces (生産力) and the relations of production (生産關係), and that human consciousness (意思) is determined (決定) by human social existence (存在),<71> attempting to find the cause of changes (變x) in social systems in "human nature" (사람性) [one line deleted by NIKH]. Next, we who know the details (經緯) of Cheondogyo's emergence and the inner workings (內面) of its subsequent development, hearing that "all things (萬事) will be achieved (成就) in the 70th year of P'odŏk [proselytization]" [one line deleted by NIKH], fostered the thought of seeking government posts (拜官思想). [censored] Thus, they have used the method of [censored] appointing various local magistrates and governors (守令方伯) [censored]. For Cheondogyo to exist today, there must have been considerable financial (財政的) expenditure (消耗), and that finance came purely from the [censored] of those [censored] followers. Therefore, the development of [censored] in Chosŏn's rural [censored] bears partial responsibility. From a Cheondogyo standpoint, the development of Cheondogyo is the product (産物) of the absolute sacrifice (犧牲) and contribution (功獻) of its followers. Then, what did those followers hope for as they obediently followed (維維順應)?

Now, in discussing Cheondogyo, what concerns us most greatly is that Innaech'ŏn-ism is based upon an eclectic (折衷主義的) philosophical system. The economic confrontation (經濟的對立) between classes (階級) inevitably (必然的) forms two opposing philosophical systems: the worldview (世界觀) of dialectical materialism (唯物辯證法) and various idealistic (觀念論的) philosophical thoughts. However, constantly wavering (動搖) up and down between these two major classes, Innaech'ŏn-ism is neither idealism (唯心) nor materialism (唯物); Cheondogyo itself embraces (包容) both matter and mind within itself.

II.

After "Cheondogyo at the Crossroads of Rise and Fall" was published in the previous issue of *Samch'ŏlli*, I received various criticisms (論難) from Cheondogyo followers, both in writing and face-to-face. These can be broadly classified (區分) into the following three types: First, a request to distinguish between the individual (個人) and the group (集團), pleading earnestly (情曲) not to attack the entire Cheondogyo organization (機關) based on the [censored] of individuals. Second, remarks like, "That was a gratifying (痛快) exposure (暴露). But how could you, a non-believer (非敎人), be so detailed (詳細) about the internal affairs (內情)?" Third, threats (威脅) and intimidation (恐갈), such as, "You are an enemy of the Chosŏn populace. Don't ever feel safe." However, when I retorted, asking where it differed (相違) from the facts, there was no answer (對答) on that point. While we cannot intentionally (故意로) reject (拒否) or deny (否認) its past existential value, given that Cheondogyo was formed (生成) from the tide of historical inevitability (必然)<72> and that there was suitable soil (培土) and environment (環境) for its existence until today – that soil and environment being the recent history of Chosŏn – nevertheless, all things undergo a process of change without resting even for a moment. The progressing society of Chosŏn (중략-원문) [original text omitted] already does not overconfidently trust (過信) this historicist basis, and a type of fascist (파시즘) philosophy that cries out for adventurous (冒險的) heroism (英雄主義) [one line deleted by NIKH]. Martin Luther (마-친 루터) was a reformer (改革者) of Christianity (基督敎). In the past feudal era, currency (貨幣), which was able to grow by its own strength (自力) in rebellion (叛逆) against it, began its struggle (抗爭) against the large landowners (大地主). At that time, the personal representatives (人格的 代表者) of currency were the merchants, and the representatives of the large landowners were the feudal lords (封建諸候). Thus, the battle between currency and landowners was that between the Old Religion (舊敎 - Catholicism) and the New Religion (新敎 - Protestantism). Therefore, Luther's Religious Reformation (宗敎改革) was a revolution (革命) from a land-based economy (土地經濟) to a money economy (貨幣經濟). We can understand why Luther receives respect (尊敬) comparable to Jesus as a religious reformer in capitalist society.

It has only been 70 years since Cheondogyo emerged in Chosŏn, an extremely short moment in time. However, there is a vast difference (天壤의 間隔) between the time when Tonghak emerged and today. At that time, compared to Western Europe, we had not escaped the medieval domain (領域). Therefore, it was natural that Cheondogyo, representing the money economy, received acclaim (渴采 - likely 喝采) for struggling (抗爭) against the representatives of the landowners, namely feudal Confucianism (儒敎).<73>

**Summary**

Kim Kyŏng-jae argues that while Tonghak (later Cheondogyo) arose necessarily from the oppressive social, political, and spiritual conditions of late 19th-century Chosŏn, providing a needed refuge for the suffering populace, it now faces a critical juncture. He critiques its historical development, noting its centralized, dictatorial leadership structure and reliance on follower sacrifices. Philosophically, he positions its core doctrine, Innaech'ŏn ("Man is Heaven"), as an unstable eclecticism caught between materialism and idealism. He expresses concern about the direction of the "new faction" and urges young followers to critically assess the religion's relevance in a rapidly changing Chosŏn, drawing parallels with Luther's Reformation as a socio-economic shift rather than purely religious, suggesting Cheondogyo's initial success was tied to representing a nascent money economy against feudalism. The article is interesting to historians for its contemporary (1934) critical perspective on Cheondogyo from outside the faith, its analysis linking religious movements to socio-economic conditions (using Marxist-influenced language like "material production," "class," "contradiction"), and its references to internal factionalism ("purification action") and leadership issues within Cheondogyo during the colonial period. The explicit mention of censorship and deleted lines also provides insight into the constraints on publishing under Japanese rule.

天道敎 靑年徒衆에게 與함
						金璟載
一.

三千里 7月號에 揭載된「興亡岐路에 선 天道敎」는 事情으로 充分히 쓰지 못햇다. 그러나 그 절눔바리의 一文일 망정 그가 天道敎徒에게는 不少한 反響이 有하엿든 듯하야 여기, 저기에서 決議, 或은 警告等의 形式으로 問題가 燃燒되여서 그리하야 新派天道敎 안에서는 淨化作用이 發芽하고 잇다.
나는 이때에 잇서 天道敎問題와 新派天道敎徒가 不意의 xx으로 하야 左할가 右할가 迷路에서 彷徨하고 잇는 그 現實에 直面하야 이에 다시 一言의 批判이 必要함을 切實히 늣겻다.
崔濟愚가 東學의 敎理를 開宣한 것이 哲宗11年이니 지금부터 74年前의 일이다. 그 當時의 朝鮮은 政治의 腐敗가 極致에 達하야 우흐로는 王室에서, 地方官衛의 不級吏輩에 이르기까지 淫虐無道한 野彎的의 方法으로 民衆의 生活을 파괴하고 이섯고 從來에 朝鮮人의 思想, 倫理,  道德, 習俗에 柱礎가 되여 잇든 儒敎는 그가 墮落하야 民衆은 精神的으로나 社會的으로나 歸依할 바를 몰랏다.
무릇 한個의 社會的 事實이 表面化하는 거긔에는 그의 發生의 原因이 잇고, 動力이 잇고, 그리하야 그의 結果로  顯現하는 것이다. 그럼으로 東學發生의 原因도 亦是 그 當時의 社會的 條件에서 探求하지 안을 수 업다.
「宗敎는 그基底에 잇서 物質的 生産過程을 가지는 것이며 俗世의 支配隷屬의 關係를 反映하는 <66>것이다. 따라서 各 時代의 宗敎는 그가 엇더한 物質的 根源으로부터 成長하엿는가를 檢討하여야 한다」
東學發生의 社會的條件은 그를 두 가지 方面으로 볼 수 잇으니 하나는 國內的 條件이요 다른 하나는 國際的 影響이다.
興宣大院君이 政權을 잡은 이후의 農民의 生活은 一層 深刻한 xx을 招致하엿나니 그는 資本主義文明의 波濤를  頑强히 拒否하면서  鏁國의 政策을 唯一의 看板으로 하야 大衆으로하야금 封建的의 領域下에 xx식키며 景福宮의 再建을 實行하야 王室의 威儀를 發揚코저 햇다. 그의 結果는 各種의 稅率을 올니고 人民의 夫役을 xx케되니 이리하야 農村은 一層 xx에 至하고 實價의 1/20도 못되는 當百錢의 流通은 經濟機搆에 一大破綻을 招致코야 마럿다.
그 때에 隣邦中國에는 英 露 佛 米等의 資本主義列國의 猛烈한 侵畧을 바다서 國勢가 날로 疲廢하고 잇고 南中國 各地에서는 洪秀全의 叛亂이 起하야 그리하야 4,000年의 老大國인 淸國은 風前燈火와 갓흔 危機에 直面하고 잇으며 日本에는 明治維新을 斷行하야 新興의 氣魄이 衝天하는 듯 십헛다.
이러한 環境과 大勢는 朝鮮에 잇서 政治的으로는 開化, 守舊兩派의 激烈한 對立을 보게 되엿고 그 뒤 1885年의 甲申政變을 最後로 金玉均等의 反封建運動이 一敗塗地의 運命에 至하니 朝鮮의 資本主義는 그만 健全, 그리고 正常한 發展의 機會를 永遠히 失하엿으나 그러나 前記한 바의 內外의 情勢는 當時의 朝鮮民族으로 하야금 무엇인지 새롭운 進路의 開拓을 憧憬하야 마지 안엇다.
그 때의 旣成宗敎로는 儒敎와 佛敎가 이섯으며 그러고 三位一體의 位牌를 갓고 正祖7年에 天主敎가 드러왓다. 儒敎는 李朝의 소위「治國의 大本」이 되여 그는 庶民xx의 理論的 先鋒이 되여 이섯으니 民衆의 歸依가 그에 向할 리가 업섯고 佛敎는 高麗末葉에 幾多의 罪惡이 經이 되고 李氏 朝鮮政府의 繼續不止하는 xx政策이 緯가 되야서 그는 完全히 山間奧地에로 驅逐되고 마럿으며 天主敎는<67> 外來文化의 急先鋒으로 못처럼 輸入되엿건만 大院君의 頑强한  鏁國政策으로 하야 그가 發展의 經路에 넘우도 受難이 컷다. 이러한 情勢下에서 未知數의 새롭은 歸依處를 發見코저 崔濟愚가 登場하얏으니 그가 東學發生의 條件이 된다.



그럼으로 崔濟愚의 役割은 다른 것이 아니엿다. 그는 儒敎, 佛敎, 道敎等의 書籍을 耽讀하야 거기에서 三敎의 合致點을 發見하야 그것을 自己의 叅考로하는 同時에 所謂 西學, 天主敎와 對峙하기 爲하야 東學이란 일홈을 내어 걸고 한편으로는 民衆을 끄으러 支配階級에 xx할 힘과 機會를 造成하며 다른 한편으로는 x民族에 대한 憎惡心을 發露식키여 政府의 xx에서 버서나고저 하얏고 또는 하로 하로 기우러지고 잇는 國難을 打開하려고 햇다.
그리하야 그는 布德文에 가로대 「古道가 衰아니 西敎가 이에 乘하야 布敎하고 國運이 이로 말미암아 危殆하니 내 上帝의 神託을 바다 神符를 가젓다」하엿고 西敎는 나라를 빼앗고 百姓을 滅亡하는 敎이라고 하야 朝鮮民衆은 東學에 依하야서만 生途가 잇다고 햇다. 그러나 崔濟愚는 左道惑民이란 罪目으로 1864年 3月 10日에 41歲를 一期로 大邱刑臺에서 斬刑을 밧엇다. 崔濟愚는 慶州의 一寒士 崔영의 아들로서 幼時부터 官界進出에 뜻을 두엇으나 當時의政情이 우에는 4色의 朋黨이 잇서 서로 政權을 獨占코저 다트로 아레로는 吏胥의 무리 收賂와 掠奪을 專業으로 하고 잇으니 一個의 無名寒士의 官界進出은 결코 容易한 일이 아니엿다. 그래서 그는 初志를 變改하야 龜尾山下에서 道學에 潛心하얏다고 하니 그가 濟愚의 思想傾向에 크게 影響하얏을 것이다. 그後 崔海月이 濟愚의 뜻을 이어서 各處로 轉轉하면서 布敎에 從事하야 南鮮 一帶에서 相當한 勢力을 갓게 되엿다. 이에 海月은 東學의 合法性을엇으려고 여러번 政府에 呼訴하얏으나 未遂하고 1895年에 東學亂이 起하니 그는 禍亂을 避하야 몸을 東西에 달니엿으나 그만 被捉되어 1899年에 72歲에 亦是 刑場에서 最後를 맛치엿다. 東學亂으로 하야 餘地업는 慘禍를 밧은 東學은 그가 南鮮地方에서 漸次로 그의 勢力이 雲消되고 孫義庵은 西北鮮地方에 活動하면서<68> 새로히 敎徒獲得에 注力하야 한 때는 俗稱 300萬의 敎徒라고 하리만큼 一大勢力을 形成하얏으며 그後 孫義庵은 -에 加坦하얏다가 x에서 得病하야 保釋하고 治療햇으나 그만 最後를 지엇다.
이럿케 天道敎의 過去는 그가 受難의 歷史 그대로 이엿으며 그리고 그들 天道敎 領導者는 언제나 民衆의 先驅者가 되얏든 것도 事實이엿다.
한 개의 事物의 生成에는 그에는 그가 生成하기에 足한 培土가 잇고 環境이 適應햇다는 것을 우리는 알고 잇다. 그럼으로 天道敎가 朝鮮에서 生하고 長하야 그가 이제 겨우 七十年에 그만한 發展이 이섯다는 것은 그가 當時의 朝鮮民衆의 要求에 順應햇다는 것이다.
宗敎는 그가 何者임을 莫論하고 現世의 現實的 苦難을 根거로 해가지고 民衆의 가난과 病弱을 利用해가며 自己發展을 圖謀한다. 그럼으로 崔水雲이 當時의 朝鮮의 政情에 임한 바 이잇서 水雲主義를 唱導하엿고 그가 極히 쩌를 時間에 特히 貧賤階級을 中心으로 하고 그만큼한 發展이 이섯고 成長이 이섯다는 것은 거긔에는 一律的으로 論키 어려운 朝鮮의 特殊性이 잇다는 것을 우리는 밋는 者이다. 여기에서 東學亂이나 其他에 잇서 天道敎的 影響에 의하야 多數한 農民이 그에 가담한 사실을 누구나 可히써 부인하지 못할 것이요. 그럿케 다수의 농민이 거긔에 동원되엿음에 不拘하고 그들 大衆을 誘導하지 못하고 農民의 思想的 傾向이 自然力에 對한 依憑精神에 근거되여 잇음을 그것을 利用하야 自己宗派의 勢力擴充의 契機로 만드럿다는 것은 資本主義의 經濟組織을 모루고 階級意識을 모루는 그 時代에 잇서 그저 그 길을 밟지 안을 수 업섯다.
그를 歐羅巴의 先進諸國에서 보아도 中世로부터 近世에 이르는 동안에 모든 農民xx은 그가 大槪는 宗派로 扮裝되여 이섯나니 그는 中世史 前期에 잇서 그 때의 사람들은 宗敎와 神學 以外에는 다른 形態의 이데오르기-를 알지 못하든 까닭이엿다.
이럿케 歷史的 必然의 潮流를 타고 나온 天道敎는 그가 歷史의 進展에 잇서 그에 充實한使徒가 될 것으로 밋엇다. 또한 天道敎의 生命도 거긔에 잇는 것이엇다. 이른바 産業革命의惠澤도 우리에게는 아즉 遙遠한 將來의 일이요 資本主義制度의 第一段階인 中世紀的의 商工業까지도 그가 發芽하지 못하고 잇는<69> 그 때에 잇서 科學的 이데오로기-를 가지지 못하고 宗敎的x想에 붓들니게 되는 것은 그는 當然途程일 것이요. 그 후 朝鮮의 여러 가지의 情勢가 電光石火的으로 變하고 잇으니 그들의 天道敎徒도 그에 順應코저 하는 努力이 뵈이기도 햇다. 그러나 時間은 歷史늘 創造하고 잇다. 이럿케 쉬일 줄 모루는 歷史의 進展은 어는 듯
「停止하지 안는 時間은 結局에 잇서 以前에 지나간 時代에 存在하든 形態와 지금 存在하는 形態와 將來할 世紀에 存在할 形態와의 間에 巨大한 相異를 設하게 되는 것이다.
故梁漢黙의 生時에는 그는 老子와 莊子等의 虛無主義的의 理論을 마저드리어 水雲主義를 祖述하려고 하얏으며 그 후에 와서 夜雷李敦化는 럿셀, 바구닝-等의 思想도 若干 參考하면서 맑스의 經濟理論도 添附하야 從來의 儒彿仙三敎의 綜合物인 水雲主義을 祖述하려고 햇다.

崔水雲이 東學을 開宣하야 74年 그 間의 天道敎의 組織變遷을 그는 대개 三期로 分할 수잇을니지 第一期는 崔水雲, 海月을 敎主로 하야 秘密團體로 存在하든 時期이요. 第二期는 丙午以後 孫義奄敎主가 從來의 地下組織을 合法的의 團體로 表現식키든 그때로부터 己未以後 朝鮮에 階級運動이 擡頭될 때 까지이고, 第三期는 崔麟 이 天道敎의 領導者가 되야서부터 오날까지로 이러케 나누워 볼 수가 잇을 것이다.
