**Taedonggang Aegok**
						Pak Sŭng-gŏl

Sunset on the Taedong River

Earnestly,
with all its passion, the setting sun, like the yearning face of a loved one, has drooped, lacking energy.

Like the pitiful heart of a lover
reluctantly letting go
of the beloved they do not wish to release, its remaining light,
that apple-color (林檎빗) burning goldenly with passion,
has let down its hair into the winding waters of the Taedong River.

Sound of Laundry Bats

Echoing off Ch'ŏngnyubyŏk (淸流壁) and rising to the very edge of the sky,
the sound of young wives' laundry bats.

Their hearts, burying their youth in the sound of the laundry bats,
find this day's sunset intensely sorrowful.<68>

Moonlit Night on the P'aegang River

The waves of the P'aegang River (浿江)
where the moon's reflection dances.

The waves dance,
embracing the moon's reflection.

Upon the waves where the moon's reflection dances,
a small boat glides silently.

Upon that gliding small boat,
my lonely shadow reflects on the river water.

The wound thrown by one who flowed away
stings even more in the moonlight.

Oh, heart-aching old memories,
all of you, come back to life.<69>

**Summary**

This three-part poem by Pak Sŭng-gŏl uses the scenery of the Taedong River (also called P'aegang) in P'yŏngyang – sunset, the sound of laundry, and a moonlit night – to evoke feelings of sorrow, melancholy, and longing. The speaker observes the fading light, the sounds of daily life imbued with sadness, and reflects on personal loneliness and past heartbreak under the moonlight. From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting as it reflects a lyrical, perhaps subdued, expression of sorrow (aegok, 哀曲) common in Korean literature during the colonial period. While not overtly political, the pervasive sense of sadness tied to iconic Korean scenery like the Taedong River might resonate with the broader national sentiment of loss and suppressed feelings under Japanese rule.

大同江哀曲
						朴承杰
大同江의 落日

정성스럽게
열정을 다하야 내려 쪼이든 태양은 사랑하는 사람의 애타는 얼굴과도 가티 기운업시 푹 푸러젓다.

노아주고 십지안은 연인을
마지못하야 노아주고가는
애처로운 연인의 마음과도 가튼 그 남은 빗
정열에 누엿 누엿타는 그 林檎빗은
大同江 구비저 흐르는 물에 머리를 프러 흐트렷다.

빨내ㅅ소리

淸流壁을 울니고 하날 저-끗까지 올나가는
젊은 아낙네의 빨내하는 방망이ㅅ소리

빨내하는 방망이ㅅ소리에 청춘을 무어버리는 그들의 마음은
이날의 落日이 몹시도 스러워라.<68>

浿江의 달밤

달그림자 춤추는
浿江의 물결.

물결은 춤춘다
달그림자 안고.

달그림자 춤추는 물결우에
고요히 미츠러지는 적은 배한척

미츠러지는 저근 배우에
외로운 내 그림자 강물에 빗치인다.

흘너간 이의 던진 상처는
달빗에 더 쓰리고나.

가슴 압흔 녯 기억아
모조리 사러나지럼.<69>
