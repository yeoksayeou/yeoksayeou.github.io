Magazine Title: 삼천리 제4권 제9호
Publication Date: 1932-09-01
Article Title: 三千里 全體會議, 朝鮮의 政治的 將來를 悲觀乎·樂觀乎 -아울너 文化的·經濟的으로-半島의 現狀과 今后 十年의 觀測 (Samch'ŏlli General Meeting: Pessimism or Optimism Regarding Korea's Political Future? -Also Culturally and Economically- Observation of the Peninsula's Current State and the Next Ten Years)
Author: Multiple respondents (listed below)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Hŏ Hŏn

I am certainly optimistic about the future of Korea.
The reasons, briefly stated, are as follows. (3 lines omitted from original text)
Therefore, the future growth and development of Korea depend on the activities of Koreans. Politically (3 lines omitted from original text), economically, it is possible to realize a Korean-centric economic policy, and culturally, by striving for the improvement and development of Koreans, it is possible to stand on equal footing with the Japanese. Thus, in this regard, I believe we must simply advance the wheels of history even one step further through our united cooperation.

Song Chin-u

Korea's current situation does not permit optimism. However, I am not pessimistic either.
Why is this? Because optimism or pessimism depends on the struggle and effort of the Korean people themselves.

Sŏ Sang-il

While Korea, as one link in the world chain, will naturally have its internal situation determined by international trends, accurately perceiving the state of Korea as it trends over the next 10 years is not an easy matter without basing it on considerable specific arguments; it seems too broad a topic.
One. Politically, it is imaginable that considerable changes will occur in the international political situation with the new Manchurian-Mongolian problem (滿蒙 問題) as a fuse. Whether the repercussions are large or small, the impact on Korea, both direct and indirect, will have a decisive effect on the expectations and concerns of Koreans manifested in Korea's political movements. Furthermore, regardless of whether such changes in the international situation occur, the daily rising political consciousness of Koreans holds the necessity that some influential popular political movement will arise in the near future to determine the course of the people's lives.
Two. Economically, since Korea is already a colony, all statistical figures define the economic life of Koreans almost fatefully with excessive clarity. However, this depends solely on the resolution of political problems. Depending on the bold determination of patriots or leaders who worry about Korea first, there might be a glimmer of hope for revival. Also, since Korea is one link in the global economic chain, and currently all capitalist nations worldwide are shifting direction from deflation (대푸래숀) to inflation (인푸래숀), if capitalist economies fortunately enter a period of stability, even temporarily, will some vitality be seen in various labor markets? In any case, the economic revival of Koreans lies solely in the cooperative movement (협동조합운동); looking at future trends, this movement alone will show rapid development.
Three. Culturally, improvement and development cannot be expected unless food and clothing are sufficient first. In other words, only when economic life is stabilized will a hundred flowers bloom luxuriantly. Culture is the desire of the spirit; where would Koreans, wandering in material ruin, find the leisure to permit spiritual desires? However, Korea is neither a frigid nor a torrid zone. It is a temperate peninsula, one of the world's cultural wellsprings, and a cultured people (문화민중) with a half-myriad-year history. Is not the genius that Koreans can boast most proudly to the world their artistic nature (예술성)? Material deprivation due to unnatural systems cannot possibly obliterate (抹殺) the creativity of a people with a deep history. The more extreme the environmental distress, the higher the spiritual creation will become.<3> For these brief reasons, I say Korea's current situation is optimistic.

Kim Yak-su

Originally, it is difficult to set a deadline in advance for the results of comprehensive movements like politics, economy, and culture. Why? Because even within the same stage, depending on artificial preparations, 10 or 20 years can turn out this way or that way, so saying one cannot know in detail is the honest thing to say. However, generally speaking, the results of such movements—while subjectively being those of purposeful, conscious effort—are objectively those of inevitability. Therefore, concerning all Korean movements, I decidedly adopt historical optimism (역사적 樂觀主義) without the slightest hesitation.

Yu Ŏk-kyŏm

I am optimistic about all matters. This is because I believe pessimism is a great enemy that negates life itself. I will let you know I am an optimist numerically and respond briefly to your questions as follows.
One. Politically, Two. Economically, regarding the current state of Korea or Korea in the next 10 years, I regretfully have no choice but to answer "I do not know" (不知). The reason is that I lack specialized knowledge in these areas. Three. Culturally, regarding the current state of Korea or Korea in the next 10 years, I am generally optimistic—although I know there will be differences depending on the observer's subjectivity. The reasons for my optimism are (a) because, looking at our past culture, I am convinced our people possess talents (才質) that are by no means inferior to others, and (b) because not only are there many educated people who will become the central force of culture and individuals dedicated to that work, but statistics also show that the number of educated people and workers is increasing year by year.

Kim Ch'ang-je

This question is too important to be answered so simply. However, unable to refuse your esteemed intention in asking, I express my respects with a few words.
Who would be optimistic about Korea's current situation? Especially politically and economically, it would not be an exaggeration to say there is nothing. Only culturally speaking, are we not a historical people with a profound culture of half a millennium? Therefore, I think the path to Korea's revival (更生) lies solely in this cultural power. For example, looking at China, although political power was taken by foreign tribes era after era throughout its thousands of years of history, the foreign tribes ended up being assimilated (同化) into the great Chinese culture. Thus, China resolutely remains the China of the Chinese people, doesn't it? Similarly, I strongly argue that Korea's lifeblood lies only here. And I am originally an idealist optimist. Thus, whatever the reality, I am absolutely not pessimistic about Korea's future. If space and opportunity permitted, I would elaborate on my thoughts regarding this issue.

Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi

I am moved by your sincerity in continuing your vigorous efforts without rest despite the harsh summer heat, and I further congratulate the development of your esteemed magazine. One. The three main pillars—politics, economy, culture—that make up the current state of Korea are indeed base and miserable. This can be seen by looking at the inner aspects of life generally in cities or rural villages. If there were actual political practice (政治의 實擧), economic harmony, and cultural development, the people's lives would not actually be like the current state. Therefore, regarding Korea's current situation, there is no room for pessimism about what might happen. Just as pleasure follows suffering, I believe that henceforth, along with the awakening (자각) of the masses, the rulers too<4> will develop a practical resolve. Mutual awakening (覺醒) is inevitable, and once awakened, there is only optimism!

Yŏ Un-hyŏng

Having traveled abroad for many years and not having had the time to study the current political structure since returning to Korea, I will refrain from making political observations. However, looking economically, one cannot help but be pessimistic. This is because current Koreans need special protective policies (보호적 정책), but compared to people in other areas, they are rather—left standing; thus, I know that economically, the future cannot be viewed with optimism. In the current situation of Koreans, there would be no chance of winning even if free competition (자유경쟁) were allowed, so if there are—policies on top of that, how much worse would it be?
However, looking culturally, there is some optimism. Although culture as a whole is under the influence (영향밋헤) of other forces, the popularization of culture and the elevation of cultural standards are gradually becoming more vigorous day by day, and I believe our culture will greatly improve in the future.

Yi Wŏn-hyŏk

Regarding whether the state of Korea in the next 10 years is optimistic or pessimistic politically, economically, and culturally, I know it is an extremely difficult question. Politically, as we are in a position where we cannot speak freely, I cannot even suggest a personal opinion by presenting reasons and conclusions. Economically, due to the global depression, all sorts of issues like peasant relief measures and relief measures for small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists are pending (懸案), making noise through the pages of newspapers and magazines, but I know it is a separate issue for Koreans. As people who are suffering total bankruptcy (破産), even if economic vitality arrives globally, is there any hope that Korean merchants, industrialists, and the peasant class will receive its benefits? This is a problem of breaking through the current situation for those on that stage, but although it might be somewhat different from the economic problem, since the term economic problem generally has a broad meaning, the question of economic optimism or pessimism is not relevant to us. Culturally, comparing the past 10 years, hasn't there truly been rapid progress, giving a feeling of a different era (隔世의 感)? Extrapolating from this, I think there will similarly be progress with a vast difference (雲霄의 差) in the next 10 years. Although all our problems are difficult, culturally, we can carve out and achieve things even in this reality by ourselves. Not only is it possible, but it is also a necessity for self-defense (自衛上), so I definitely think optimistically.

Song Pong-u

There is no reason to hold the despair of pessimism. However, one cannot just face everything, including the immediate situation, with unwavering optimism either. No matter how much the political, economic, and cultural situation of Korea feels like it has entered a dead end, since this is one stage in the dialectical process (변증법적 과정) of movement, one can predict that there will be progress to the next stage. However, even with such a scientific prediction, if there is no action to push history forward while just optimistically waiting for it to come, it will not arrive.

Kim Yŏ-sik

Thinking about Korea in the next 10 years, there will be much to do politically, economically, or culturally<5>. However, under a fixed guiding spirit (주의정신) and purpose, with the condition that the majority of the populace is trained and united (훈련 또한 결합), and each person strives and struggles to do their best, I am optimistic.

Yu Chin-hŭi

Because the question is extremely abstract, the answer can only be abstract.
The current situation is extreme X [極X]. And from a national perspective, the three points of the question speak of the total X [總X].
However, this extreme X situation always becomes the driving force of social evolution (進化). Now, from the bottom (밋창) of the extreme X situation, the wheels of evolution are turning, and the song of joy (歡歌) for revival rises.
10 years later? At that time, the logic of this question will become clear enough that magazine publishers will consider it a shame (치욕) to go around asking such questions.

Im Wŏn-gŭn

Our reality, facing economic difficulties, gives an undeniable sense of distress (窘塞), making it too difficult to be optimistic about its superstructure (上部 建築), the political future. However, the various trends in the global political situation (政局) changing moment by moment (especially concerning the Far East), along with the various tendencies of domestic political currents (潮流), both tangible and intangible, which nevertheless seek renewal and ceaselessly demand a better reality, will surely provide us with material for optimism in the near future.
Of course, the content of the term "political" itself will cover many branches, and "future" too will inherently have differences in distance, near and far. However, the history of human society is not crafted by the small mind of any single hero, nor is its form changed by the military force of a single troop unit. One must know that there is always the immovable mass power (덩어리 힘) of the populace, and only their common demand for something (썸딍) accompanies its own course. So, what are the Koreans of today all demanding, and what are they striving to obtain? Because there is the pain of reality, felt as dissatisfaction, there is effort and struggle to eliminate that pain, and where there is effort and struggle, I think it is an inevitable principle that there will surely be a corresponding reward (報酬).

Myŏng Che-se

Greetings [拜夏].
I pray for the daily increasing prosperity of your company.
Regarding your inquiry:
Politically, economically, and culturally, I am pessimistic about Korea's current situation. Also, for the next 10 years, although there might be some cultural improvement, I do not see anything to be optimistic about politically or economically unless special circumstances arise. However, I do not wish to state the reasons.

Pak Ch'ang-hun

One. Politically, while perhaps not reaching optimism, I am definitely not pessimistic. Won't Korea in the next 10 years be even more so? (Reason omitted - original text) Two. Economically, I am currently pessimistic. If things continue this way, I fear it will be truly serious after 10 years. (Reason omitted - original text) Three. Culturally, it's just so-so. I know there will be some progress in 10 years. However, our effort is an essential condition.

Yŏm Sang-sŏp

I believe there's no basis for optimism, but neither is there reason to be pessimistic. "When cornered, a way opens up" (窮하면 통하는 것); since this opening is a course that arrives at some juncture<6>, if we continuously cultivate the ability to ride the momentum (乘勢할만한 능력) upon that chance (찬쓰), we will not lose hope even in a situation that can only be viewed pessimistically, and I believe an optimistic day will come. (However, the word 'chance' here should not be called opportunism (기회주의).)

Kim Kyŏng-jae

I view the Korea problem as something that will be resolved along with the entire Far East problem. Currently, all global situations give the feeling of the eve of a storm (폭풍우 전야). Accordingly, a great XX [一大XX] is demanded in the near future. Therefore, rather than talking about optimism or pessimism, isn't preparation urgent?

Kim Pyŏng-no

Judging by the current domestic and international situation, looking ahead 10 years, everyone anticipates some kind of change globally—politically, economically, or culturally. In our reality, could there be a scene inspiring greater pessimism? Nevertheless, I believe that at this extremity (窮極), if there is global change, it will give us optimistic hope.

Chŏng Se-kwŏn

Greetings [拜夏]. Regarding your question, for Korea's current situation or Korea in the next 10 years to be optimistic, it must proceed based on technical effort (기술적 노력) economically; politically and culturally, it is utterly impossible.
It is optimism. Technical effort follows and grows, and consciousness also grows. From there comes entrepreneurial power, from entrepreneurial power comes the economy; this economy will never decline permanently.

Yi Chŏng-sŏp

One. Politics: As things stand, it is pessimistic. There are probably not many nations as weak (薄弱) in political insight as the Korean people. One cannot hear or see any clear political view (政見)—a political view capable of sweeping up the majority of the 20 million people and making them into one bundle (一束). On top of that, the Korean temperament (氣質) is like wax under the hot sun (炎天下의 臘), completely limp, lacking courageous fighting spirit and self-sacrifice. For that reason, present-day Korea has become a windless zone of those living dissipated lives without purpose (醉生夢死者). At such times, figures must emerge. Koreans must possess the generosity (雅量) to understand that national leaders (領導者) are not made by the leaders themselves but are made by society. On the day heroes emerge, political views are established, and fighting spirit spreads, it will be optimistic.
Two. Economy: Can the nation's political movement cease forever? Three. Culture: The destruction of a people's economy and culture, aside from special natural disasters (天變地災), would be extremely difficult, wouldn't it?

Ŏ Ku-sŏn

Your question—whether Korea in the next 10 years is optimistic or pessimistic politically, economically, and culturally—seems to be the same as asking, in other words, given that our Korea is in a special situation regarding all the above points, to what extent can Korea's XX power [XX세력] mature (성숙) within the next 10 years, or in what direction will it advance? Discussing optimism and pessimism here seems somewhat lacking in appropriateness, but generally, one can be optimistic. For this, we must first strengthen our XX power [XX力量], but because all objective situations (客觀的 정세) and internal conditions (內在的 조건) (flood of pioneer groups, fascisization [洪水 파쏘화], etc.) following the path of collapse in the third period (제3기) guide us (the working class [勤勞계급]) to no other path (他途) than XXXX... After passing through such adversity (逆境), there might also be favorable circumstances (順境)... (Continued on page 41)<7>

Mun Il-p'yŏng

If Korea's current situation continues as it is, one cannot help but be pessimistic about everything—politically, economically, and culturally. However, it seems that pressing internal and external circumstances will surely bring about some kind of change within the next 10 years, breaking the deadlock.

Yi In

If the root and trunk (根幹) are damaged, it goes without saying that the tree cannot flourish, but doesn't it manage to survive arduously (辛酸하게 벗틔어 가다가) through rain, dew, and nature's energy, sometimes with the root and trunk reviving (更生), and sometimes withering away completely (고사하기도)? Our situation is just like a tree with a damaged root and trunk, but there's no need to be pessimistic about it. Since I came of age, worrying that if things continue like this, Korea (abbreviated in original) would not last 10 years, it has already been several decades. Isn't it a fact that while nothing—political, economic, cultural—has developed or improved, Koreans as a people are still alive? Seeing this, it seems just like a damaged tree precariously (곤드래만드래 하면서) yet arduously maintaining its life. While there is nothing remarkable about anything in Korea, and what meets the eye is only material for pessimism, it is an undeniable fact that amidst the arduous maintenance of life, the consciousness of life/existence (生의 의식) strongly exists implicitly. This consciousness/will itself is truly precious. Just as a tree's life does not die and endures through rain, dew, and nature's great atmosphere, the life of a person or a people, if only the consciousness of life is alive, the surrounding environment naturally fosters (조장케도 하지오) that life. Synthesizing the globally swirling economic crisis, the conflict between Japan and China centered on the Manchuria problem, and the emergence of complex relationships among various countries centered on the Chinese market, Korea, existing within that context (於中에 分在한), receiving the education of its surrounding environment, will, for the next 10 years at least, progress remarkably (괄목할만큼) in various fields like politics, economy, and culture, leading to more than full optimism. The next 10 years hold significant meaning for us.

Sin T'ae-ak

I am optimistic.
1. Politically, the world currently coincides (際會하얏나니) with a time poised for great change (動하랴는 때); if we just skillfully adapt to the waves (물ㅅ결) of the changing world, I believe Korea will have brightness politically after 10 years.
2. Economically, it is true that the current situation is in a state of ruin, but this will share the fate of politics, so Korea after 10 years becomes optimistic.
3. Culturally, judging by the current Hangeul promotion (한글장려), literacy campaign (문맹퇴치운동), and other various movements, future Korea is optimistic.

Kim Hang-gyu

Currently, politically, economically, and culturally, Korea is not just pessimistic but in a state of despair; this is no exaggeration. However, as Korea is a part of the world, I know the world current will change precipitously (급전직하격으로). Then, moving from pessimism to despair, and if change comes from despair, it is an immovable principle of human society that it changes towards the path of optimism. I keenly feel the relevance of the old saying, "When pushed to the extreme, change occurs; when change occurs, a way through is found" (窮則變하고 變則通), and at the same time, I assert that whether it takes 10 or 20 years, the flexibility (신축) of time depends entirely on the total effort of Koreans, keenly aware that Korea is in the process (도정) of transformation.

Yang Pong-gŭn

I am optimistic about Korea's current situation politically, economically, or culturally. The reason, you ask? It is because we cannot view all things only by their current state. Since we do not stop at seeing all things in their current state but view them in an evolutionary process (진화과정), the political and economic XX [XX] and cultural stagnation of the Korean people today appear only as the agony of labor pains (진통). The result of labor pains can be the birth of a noble child (貴童子). (End)<41>

**Summary**

This 1932 survey from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine captures diverse perspectives among prominent Korean intellectuals and figures on Korea's future over the next decade under Japanese colonial rule. While acknowledging the dire political and economic realities, leading to pessimism for some (Myŏng Che-se, Yŏ Un-hyŏng on economy), a significant number express optimism, often conditional. Optimism stems from faith in the Korean people's inherent cultural strength and history (Kim Ch'ang-je, Yu Ŏk-kyŏm), the potential for self-strengthening efforts like the cooperative movement or cultural/literacy campaigns (Sŏ Sang-il, Sin T'ae-ak), the belief in historical progress or dialectical change (Kim Yak-su, Song Pong-u), and anticipation of favorable shifts in the international situation, particularly concerning Manchuria and global power dynamics (Sŏ Sang-il, Im Wŏn-gŭn, Kim Pyŏng-no, Yi In). Others maintain a cautious stance, tying the future directly to the Korean people's own efforts and awakening (Song Chin-u, Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Yŏm Sang-sŏp). The responses reveal a complex mix of despair, resilience, and strategic thinking among Koreans navigating colonial oppression, highlighting debates on whether cultural preservation, economic self-help, political awakening, or external geopolitical changes held the key to a better future. Historians would find valuable insights into the range of intellectual responses to colonialism, the perceived avenues for national survival and advancement, and the specific hopes and anxieties tied to the geopolitical context of the early 1930s, including the Manchurian Incident's impact and global economic shifts. The varying emphasis on internal agency versus external factors is particularly revealing.

三千里 全體會議, 朝鮮의 政治的 將來를 悲觀乎·樂觀乎 -아울너 文化的·經濟的으로-半島의 現狀과 今后 十年의 觀測
許憲

今後의 朝鮮을 물론 樂觀함니다.
그 이유를 간단히 記述하면 다음과 갓슴니다. (3行略원문)
그러면 今後의 朝鮮의 성장 발달은 朝鮮人의 활동여하에 의하야 정치적으로 (3行略원문) 경제적으로 朝鮮人 본위의 경제정책을 실현식힐 수도 잇스며 문화적으로 朝鮮人의 향상 발전을 圖하야 日本人과 대등된 지위에 立할 수도 잇슬것임니다. 그럼으로 나는 이 점에 잇서서 吾人의 一心협력으로써 다만 一步라도 역사적 車輪을 운전하여 나갈 것 뿐이라고 봄니다.

宋鎭禹

朝鮮의 현상은 樂觀을 不許한다. 그러하나 비관도 아니한다.
何故오 云하면 낙관 비관은 朝鮮 민족 자체의 奮鬪 노력여하에 달렷슴으로.

徐相日

朝鮮도 세계 연쇄의 一環인 입장에서 국제적 動向이 朝鮮의 내부 狀勢를 결정할 것은 물론이어니와 今後 10년을 一期로 하야 趨移될 朝鮮의 현상을 정확히 인식함에는 상당한 各論에 근거치 안코는 용이한 문제가 아닌 것만큼 너무나 汎論인 것갓다.
一. 정치적으로는 새로운 滿蒙 문제를 導火線으로하여 국제정국에 상당한 변화가 이러날 것은 상상에 가능한 일이다. 그 파문이 크거나 작거나 朝鮮에 밋치는 영향은 직접과 간접을 물론하고 朝鮮의 정치운동에 나타나는 朝鮮人의 기대와 관심에 절대한 효과를 줄 것이다. 또는 그러한 국제정세의 변화가 잇거나 업거나를 불문할지라도 朝鮮人의 날날이 昻揚하여가는 정치의식은 未久의 장래에 무슨 유력한 민중의 정치운동이 이러나서 이 민중의 생활 歸趨를 결정하여 줄 필연성을 가지고 잇다 한다.
二. 경제적으로는 朝鮮이 임이 식민지인 것만큼 모든 통계 數字가 너무나 명확하게도 朝鮮人의 경제생활를 거의 운명적으로 규정하여잇다. 그러나 이것은 오로지 정치문제 해결에만 의존하여 잇는 것이니 몬저 朝鮮을 걱정하는 志士나 지도자의 과감한 결의여하를 따라서는 起死回生의 曙光도 업지아니할 것이요 朝鮮은 또한 세계적 경제 紐帶의 一環 연쇄인 것만큼 방금 전세계 자본주의 제국은 대푸래숀에서 인푸래숀으로 그 방향을 전환한 此際인 즉 다행히 자본주의 경제가 일시적이나마 안정기에 드러온다면 각 노동시장에는 幾分의 활기가 보일는지? 하여간 朝鮮人의 경제적 부활은 오즉 협동조합운동에만 잇슬 것이니 이 운동만은 今後의 趨勢로 보아서는 長足의 발전을 보일 것이다.
三. 문화적으로는 첫제 衣食이 足한 後가 아니면 그 향상 발전을 기대할 수 업다. 다시 말하면 경제적 생활안정이 되여야 百花가 爛熳할 것이다. 문화는 곳 정신의 욕구이니 물질의 파멸에 헤매이는 朝鮮人의게 정신의 욕구를 許할 餘暇가 어대 잇슬가 할 것이다. 그러나 朝鮮은 寒帶도 아니요 熱帶도 아니다. 세계의 문화적 發源地의 하나인 溫帶半島이며 반만년의 역사를 가저온 문화민중이다. 朝鮮人이 세계인의게 가장 자랑할 수 잇는 가진 바 천재는 오즉 예술성이 아닐가한다. 제도의 不自然으로 인한 물질의 핍박이 결코 역사깁흔 민중의 창조성을 抹殺할 수 업다. 환경의 困迫이 극하면 극할사록 정신의 창조은 놉하질 것이다.<3> 以上의 간단한 이유에서 朝鮮의 현상은 樂觀이라한다.

金若水

원래로 정치, 경제, 문화 등 이러한 전반적 운동의 성과문제에 대하야 미리 기한을 부치기는 곤란한 것이다. 웨 그러야하면 동일한 階段에서도 인위적 준비여하에 의하야 10년, 20년가튼 것은 이리도 되고 저리도 되는 까닭이니 그것은 자세히 알 수 업다고 말하는 것이 정직한 말일 것이다. 그러나 대체로 말하면 그러한 운동의 성과는―주관적 자체로는 목적적 의식적 노력적의 그것이면서 객관적으로는 필연적의 그것이다. 그러므로 나는 일절의 朝鮮 운동에 就하야 조금도 주저치안코 단연한 역사적 樂觀主義를 取한다.

兪億兼

나는 일절 사물에 대하야 樂觀을 함니다. 비관은 인생자체를 부정하는 大敵인 줄 밋기 때문임니다. 數字로 낙관자임을 알니어 드리고 貴問에 대하야 아래와 갓치 간단히 응답함니다.
一. 정치적으로 二. 경제적으로 본 朝鮮의 현상又는 今後 10년의 朝鮮에 대하야는 未安하지오마는 「不知」라고 답할 수밧게 업슴니다. 然故는 이 방면에 전문적 素養이 업는 까닭임니다. 三. 문화적으로 본 朝鮮의 현상 又는 今後 10년의 朝鮮에 대하야는 나는 일반적으로 낙관함니다.―아마 觀察子의 주관에 따라 相異가 잇슬 줄은 암니다마는―낙관하는 然故은 (가) 우리의 과거 문화로 보아 우리 민족이 남에 비하야 결코 지지아니하는 才質을 具備하엿다. 확신하는 까닭임과 (나) 문화의 중심세력이 될 유식자와 그 사업에 헌신하는 人士가 만히 잇슬뿐 아니라 또 통계로 보아 年年히 유식자와 사업가가 증가하여감을 알기 때문임니다.

金昶濟

이 문제는 너무 중대하니만큼 그러케 간단히 대답할 수는 업음니다. 그러나 무르신 尊意을 저바리지 못하야 두어 마대로 謝禮를 표하나이다.
朝鮮의 현상을 누가 낙관하겟읍니까? 더구나 정치적, 경제적으로는 皆無라 하여도 과언이 아닐가 하나이다. 오직 문화적으로 말하면 반만년의 근심한 문화를 가진 역사적 민족이 아님니까. 고로 나는 朝鮮 更生의 道는 다만 이 문화력에 잇다고 생각함니다. 가령 中國으로 보아도 수천년의 역사를 통하야 시대 시대의 外族에게 정권을 빼앗김이 잇엇지마는 원악 위대한 중국문화에 外族까지 同化되고 말엇읍니다. 그리하야 의연히 中國은 中國人의 中國이 되고 잇지안습니까? 마찬가지로 朝鮮의 생명도 오직 여기 잇다는 것을 力說함니다. 그리고 나는 원래 理想的으로는 낙관자이올시다. 그리하야 현실은 엇잿던지 朝鮮의 장래에 대하야 절대로 비관은 하지 안슴니다. 만일 紙面과 기회가 허락하면 이 문제로 懷抱를 詳述하여 볼가요.

徐廷禧

暑炎이 孔酷함에 不抱하시고 健鬪하심을 쉬여쥬지 안이하시는 誠意를 감격하오며 더욱 貴誌의 발전을 축하하나이다. 一. 조선 현상을 만드러놋코 잇는 정치, 경제, 문화 三大綱領은 과연 底劣 참담함니다. 이것은 도시나 농촌에 잇써 일반적으로 생활 裏面을 보아서 알 수 잇음니다. 정치의 實擧와 경제의 조화와 문화의 발달이 잇고서는 민중생활이 현상과 갓흔 것은 실제상 업는 것이올시다. 이럼으로 조선 현상에 대하야는 무엇이 엇더하겟다고 비관은 가질 餘地가 잇을 수가 업읍니다. 苦를 지내면 樂이 잇는 것과 갓치 압흐로는 대중의 자각과 함께 지배자들도<4> 실제상 각오가 생기리라고 함니다. 피차 覺醒은 될 수밧게 업고 覺醒되면 낙관뿐!

呂運亨

나는 해외만을 多年 도라다녓고 또 朝鮮에 드러와서도 現行 정치기구에 대하야 아직 연구하여 볼 時日을 가지지 못하엿든 관계로 정치적으로 모든 관찰을 하기를 피하겟슴니다마는 경제적으로 볼 때에는 비관아니할 수 업슴니다. 그것은 현재의 조선 사람들에게는 특별한 보호적 정책이 서주어야 하겟는데 어느 방면 사람들에 비하여 오히려―서잇게 되니까 이러고는 경제적으로 그 前途가 낙관이 되지못할 줄 암니다. 현재의 조선 사람 경우는 자유경쟁을 식혀준대도 勝算이 업슬터인데 그 우에―정책이 잇다면 더구나 엇더케 되겟슴니까.
그러치만 문화적으로 볼 때에는 다소 낙관됨니다. 비록 전반적으로 문화는 딴세력의 영향밋헤 잇다할지라도 작고 날로 熾盛하여가는 문화의 보편화 문화수준의 고도화가 되어 장래로 우리의 문화는 크게 향상될 것을 밋어짐니다.

李源赫
