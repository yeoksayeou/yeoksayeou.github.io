Intellectual Waitress Sad History: Why Become a Waitress After Finishing Girls' Higher Common School? Café Crown, Café Angel, Café Paradise, Café Pacific

Café! The café is a place where alcohol, women, and sensationalism lie dormant. Under red lights, blue lights, and dim chandeliers, the sound of footsteps, the rustling of clothes, tobacco smoke, the smell of alcohol, the loudly flowing jazz <72> to which young men and women dance, fluttering laughter, and excited faces! They frolic joyfully as if they have forgotten all the pains and bitterness of life.
It seems like a place where only happy people gather, with no tears, no sorrow, no worries.
In the heavy drifting air, even as the night deepens, the sound of lips touching continues along with the splendid excitement indefinitely.
Young men! And lovely waitresses embrace each other, saying "Drink up, eat up," looking at each other with drunken red faces and eyes barely managing to stay open.

What kind of people are those who seek out such places as cafés, and what kind of people are the waitresses who greet and deal with them?
There may be people who go for the purpose of socializing, and people who go to dispel a moment's melancholy, but mostly it is the children of wealthy people going to obtain women and alcohol.
Why? Why curse café waitresses as lowly? Borrowing the words of some, they even call café waitresses XXX women. Looking at the surface, who wouldn't say so?
Becoming mere commodities for the bourgeoisie, they put on all sorts of affected coyness, and with the money they wrest away, they only know how to buy expensive clothes and cosmetics—who would call them respectable professional women?
Their debts increase, and they have thrown their own bodies and souls into a mud pit. Their lives are too miserable! The café is filled with the light and dark shades of women and Eros.

However, to blindly curse café waitresses as wicked women or lowly women is nothing but a lack of awareness.
Among them, there are stalwarts who possess a commendable work ethic and thoroughly fight against the contradictory capitalist society, and there are also pitiful heroines of tears who, pressed by life's difficulties, endure all sorts of violence from numerous men.
Looking at their surface lives, who would say they have worries? But one must know that behind the splendor, there are always tears and tragedy hidden—
Now, I shall introduce several waitresses. Why did they become waitresses, deemed lowly by the world?

Crown Chapter
The Sad Tale of Miss Ch'oe Tasun (崔多純孃), Who Parted Tearfully with Her Lover in a Northern Port

Café Crown—From the tall Western-style building standing loftily across from Yumi Hall (優美館), the waltz tune <73> flowing out from the loudly spinning record must have dizzied the heads of passersby.
Though run by poor Koreans, it doesn't look particularly gloomy, and it seems there are quite a few waitresses. According to the stories of customers frequenting this place, there is said to be a lovely nineteen-year-old woman named Ch'oe Tasun (崔多純), who holds a very tearful secret story. Born in Ch'ungch'ŏngnamdo (忠淸南道), she parted from her beloved mother at age nine, and unable to endure the abuse and painful beatings under her unkind stepmother, the young Tasun, not yet ten, left home and fled far away to strange lands.
Who besides parents could guide Tasun completely? Like a water weed floating on water, today east, tomorrow west, the young wanderer drifted here and there with no place to rely on.
As time flowed, autumns passed, and springs came several times, the young Tasun matured, her body grew bigger, and by the time she came to know the opposite sex, she had joined the theater troupe of Hyŏn Sŏngwan (玄聖完). Thus, she toured as far as Hamgyŏngbukto (咸鏡북도). At that time, when they were in Unggi, the troupe faced severe economic difficulties, forcing them to stay there for a long time. It was then she became acquainted with a certain young man, Kim X-pong (金X鳳), a youth who intended to sacrifice himself for the XX movement in the region.
Kim was the only son of a widowed mother, had attended XX Higher Common School (XX高等普通學校) but dropped out midway due to the XX incident, and was engrossed only in social sciences. His acquaintance with Miss Ch'oe reportedly began the day before when he went to see a play performance with a comrade.
However, Miss Ch'oe already had a husband, so she couldn't possibly be free. Kim, unaware of her circumstances, met Miss Ch'oe and pleaded with her to become his comrade and his beloved wife. For some reason, although she had a husband, after meeting Kim, her affection for her husband (not a husband joined by understanding) subtly turned away, and only the degree of her passionate feelings for Kim grew day by day. Though young, Miss Ch'oe's heart was filled only with secret anguish and the growing flames of love.
Kim's comrades came to Miss Ch'oe and firmly requested several times, "Comrade Tasun, please sacrifice for Comrade Kim." The young Miss Ch'oe, while fearing her husband's sharp eyes, desperately thought about how to escape his fearsome grasp.
However, had she known the world a bit more, been a bit stronger, and aged a bit more, she might have run to her beloved Kim. But Miss Ch'oe, merely seventeen, lacked such strong judgment, so she couldn't heed Kim's burning desire or her comrades' urgings, and followed her disliked husband as far as Sŏngjin Port (城津港).
But how can one find peace of mind or achieve success after leaving a truly beloved person?
Even during the week-long performance, her heart was only with Kim in the north, so Miss Ch'oe had no choice but to send him a letter.
Kim, who had been burning with anxiety after sending Miss Ch'oe away, immediately rushed to Sŏngjin upon receiving her letter. In Sŏngjin, there was a comrade of Kim named Pang Kyusŏng (方奎星), <74> and he stayed at his place.
However, somehow the news that Kim had come to Sŏngjin quickly spread throughout the theater troupe, making it difficult for Miss Ch'oe, like being trapped in a cage, even to see the sky outside.
But the young Miss Ch'oe, wanting to meet Kim somehow, secretly wandered the night streets searching for Pang Kyusŏng's house. Kim, appearing in the room of Mr. Pang's house she finally found, was drunk. But his mind must have been clear, as he greeted Miss Ch'oe gladly upon seeing her and firmly shouted, "Let's go together," they say.
Miss Ch'oe also firmly pledged to go together and stealthily made preparations, but her husband's eyes were too sharp, noticing Miss Ch'oe's actions without missing a thing. Escape seemed distant for the pitiful Miss Ch'oe.
The evening of departure arrived. Miss Ch'oe, who intended to take the Northbound Train (北行車) with her beloved Kim, ended up taking the Southbound Train (南行車) following her disliked husband. It was an evening when the snow that had been falling on the winter night quietly ceased, and the moonlight shone brightly upon the snow.
Miss Ch'oe's theater troupe members came out to Sŏngjin Station (城津驛).
Knowing Miss Ch'oe was leaving, Kim and Mr. and Mrs. Pang, along with several other comrades, also went to the station, but Kim had to hide and couldn't even meet the gaze of his beloved Miss Ch'oe.
When Mr. Pang and other comrades urged her, "Comrade Ch'oe, you can't possibly leave," she replied that she would jump off when the train departed. But her husband and other troupe members surrounded her, preventing her from making even the slightest move.
So, what could she do? Kim's heart, waiting with the thought that she would get off when the train left, must have felt indescribably suffocated, and his vision must have gone pitch black.
Sometime after that, when Miss Ch'oe was in unfortunate circumstances in Wŏnsan (元山), Kim tried to take her away, but even then, things didn't go as planned. Ultimately, Miss Ch'oe today reminisces about the past in Café Crown, located in a corner of Sŏul. Though she interacts with several men each evening, laughing, talking, singing, and dancing, the old memories deeply embedded in her heart will likely never fade.
Miss Ch'oe's final wish, her greatest hope, is to meet Kim again, who is far away in the northern land.
She has sent letters to Kim several times, but now there is no reply, she said sadly. Hearing from his comrades, Kim is said to be in the state of XX. Miss Tasun, who spends her time meaninglessly dealing with customers, deserves sympathy, but Kim's situation, repeating the records of the old days in XX and longing for her, is also pitiful.
Miss Ch'oe says she will go to Kim someday. Though her body has been completely trampled upon, her hope is to work together with Kim for the sake of the contradictory society.

Angel Chapter
Intellectual Waitress Kim Chŏlcha (金節子孃), Studying Foreign Languages; The Constancy of Mr. Yi X's Sister, Aecha (愛子양).

Gentlemen who used to frequent Café Angel <75> will likely have already discovered the heroine of this story.
A slender figure, a luscious face, intelligent and determined eyes, a gentle and clear voice—any man who stepped foot into the café would undoubtedly be captivated by her.
She is twenty-one years old, born in Hamhŭng (咸興), Hamgyŏngnamdo (咸鏡南道). Even when she was back home, her looks stood out, so she experienced baseless criticism more than once or twice. You would know even if I didn't write it here, but Chŏlcha of Café Angel X Group is famous as an intellectual waitress. Anyone who has spoken with her once would nod, saying, "Ah, yes." She is the protagonist of a sadder and more pitiful story than anyone else.
Though only twenty-one, she is a person who has experienced all the ups and downs the world has to offer, leaving none behind.
Her appearance is adorned just like other waitresses, but in her behavior and thoughts, there is likely no one to match her.
Her usual words are:
"I am not the least bit ashamed of being a waitress itself. There might be other jobs, but in my current situation, this job is the most suitable compared to others. When I no longer need money, I will try doing what I want to do." This is how she talks to customers who understand her.
She has good friends. They sleep together, eat together, and work together in the same place. Her friend, Miss "Yuriko" (유리꼬), is also said to possess considerable awareness.
When they return from the café, they become part of a wholesome family and also students. They currently attend a foreign language school.
She always regrets the days when she ran around thoughtlessly in the past and nurtures her strength for the future.
Although deeper sorrow and grief than others are hidden within her, Miss Chŏlcha, who has grasped what the world is like, is said to clearly distinguish between laughter and tears.

Mr. Yi X (李X氏), currently appointed as an Army XX in Manchukuo (滿洲國)—there are likely several people in the capital who knew him in the past.
His younger sister also seems to have had many changes. At one time, it was said she would become a movie actress, but these days she has become a waitress at Café Angel.
After experiencing the Russian (로서아) winds and returning, it was said she was the wife of a certain tycoon outside Sŏdaemun (西大門) and even had a child, but now she is single, carrying herself as a 'Miss' without the child.
There were many stories leading up to her being called to the café, but if she now clears away her past mistakes and advances as a professional woman, it would be all the more joyful. Currently, under the name Aecha (愛子?), she is said to be diligently striving <76> at Café Angel.

Paradise Chapter
Miss Kim Yŏngnan (金鈴蘭) and Kim Songgŭm (金松錦), Who Became Waitresses Due to Life's Hardships

The night has begun once again. Inside the large, modern-style building in the back alley behind Umi Hall (優美館), today too—men and women keeping step to the foxtrot, whiskey, tobacco, fruit, cake, the stifling, oppressive presence of women, charm, jealousy, and songs, more songs. Dozens of waitresses, always the same, have adorned themselves with all their might and skill.
They applied thick powder, drew their eyebrows black, and dabbed on rouge. That is their life and their ultimate weapon.
A round face and a suitably tall figure—the character starring here, Miss Chŏn Kibong (全奇峰孃), is also a pitiful slave to "money," spending her days there.
Her age is said to be 20, not even that old, but likely no one knew she was the heroine of the strong Lily of the Valley (ススラン/鈴蘭), single-handedly feeding several family members.
In her childhood, she was a disciple of Mr. Cho Ch'ŏrho (趙喆鎬), the commander-in-chief of the Boy Scouts (少年軍), a chivalrous child who traveled as far as Pukkando (北間島) to collect famine relief funds. Miss Chŏn Kibong was famous for her excellent dancing and rumored to be good at acting.
She spent five long years in the theater world (劇界), but since the Korean theater scene was poor, not a single penny of payment returned to her.
Her family is extremely poor. Her mother's back became bent, and her young siblings' cries, begging for food, only grew louder day by day.
She jumped out of the theater world to help her pitiful mother and save her hungry, crying siblings, but
she couldn't find a job sufficient to sustain the lives of several family members. Because of the food—the mouths—of her several family members, Miss Chŏn ended up stepping into the life of a waitress.
Miss Chŏn, who hadn't grasped a professional consciousness and only perceived waitressing as a lowly thing, saw herself as base. She also completely cut off ties with friends she knew from the old days. If she met a friend or acquaintance on the street, she would run away as if to the ends of the earth.
She dreaded the café nights the most—the nights when the five-colored lights were lit, the records spun, and men flocked in.
After being embraced by unpleasant customers and suffering all sorts of humiliation, the faces of her siblings waiting at home for her return and her mother, staying awake late and crying, appeared and disappeared like a broken film strip. <77> She compared her innocent days on stage, receiving applause from many, with her current self. And then she wept. Day by day, she came to realize the harshness and contradictions of the world.
Thus, as Miss Chŏn came to understand waitressing and simultaneously understand herself, her aspiration is said to be to strive forward shamelessly as a professional woman in the future.

After struggling entirely in the hands of men until three in the morning, where is the resting place for those returning waitresses? Those with homes in Sŏul find their own houses, but those who came from afar seek out desolate rented rooms—empty rooms—and the rest throw their bodies into the gloomy rooms on the upper floor of the café.
Their bodies are tired, so if they fall asleep, they might forget everything, but when that sleep breaks—how lonely and how desolate must it be.
Those who frequented Paradise Hall (樂園會館) would have seen the bob-haired young lady—Miss Kim Songgŭm (金松錦)—with a suitably tall figure, a very fortunate-looking and pretty face. Her hometown is Hoeryŏng (會寧) in Hamgyŏngbukto (咸鏡북도), and two years ago, at the age of sixteen, she came to Sŏul and frequented the theater market and various opera troupes.
Why did she, a girl of eighteen, end up entering a café?
Her father is not present, and there were her elderly widowed mother and two older brothers (옵바), but her older brothers are said to have passed away. As things turned out, the young girl realized that she had to make a living herself and threw herself into the theater world, but it was vastly different from the theater world she had imagined. Thus, Miss Kim had drifted into the café.
How much more painful must be the heart of the mother waiting far away for the money her young daughter sends, but Miss Kim, experiencing all the world's hardships as a lonely traveler in a foreign land a thousand miles away from her mother, also deserves considerable sympathy.
She is the owner of a voice more beautiful than a nightingale's.
Had she, with her good looks and beautiful voice, not been born in Korea, she would likely have become a beloved and splendid figure. She says the thing she fears most is being tormented by drunkards.

Young Miss Kim says she now understands the world.
Even when she has reason to cry, she knows how to cry inwardly, and even when she dislikes laughing, she knows how to laugh. She came to understand herself, having to wear a smile even while shedding tears.
Her greatest objective was to learn more music. Even now, if there were a place to entrust her mother, she would study music—<78>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine profiles several "intellectual waitresses" working in prominent Sŏul cafés like Crown, Angel, and Paradise. While acknowledging the societal view of waitresses as morally compromised figures catering to wealthy men, the author argues for sympathy, portraying them as often educated women (graduates of girls' higher common schools) forced into the profession by extreme poverty, family obligations, or tragic personal histories involving lost love or escape from difficult situations. The article details the specific struggles of Ch'oe Tasun, Kim Chŏlcha, Mr. Yi X's sister "Aecha," Chŏn Kibong (also called Sŭsŭran/Yŏngnan), and Kim Songgŭm, emphasizing their inner turmoil, resilience, and aspirations despite the degrading aspects of their work.

From a historical perspective, the article offers a fascinating glimpse into the social dynamics and economic hardships faced by educated Korean women during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the limited employment opportunities available, forcing even those with schooling into precarious service jobs often associated with sexual commodification. The mention of political activities (XX movement, XX incident, Manchukuo Army) subtly interwoven with personal tragedies points to the broader political context influencing individual lives. Historians could find the piece valuable for its nuanced portrayal of female labor, class divisions, urban modernity (café culture), and the complex intersection of personal lives with colonial realities and nascent political consciousness/censorship (represented by 'XX').

카페! 카페는 술과 게집 그리고 엽기가 잠재하여잇는 곳이다. 붉은 등불 파란 등불 밝지 못한 샨데리아 아래에 발자최 소리와 옷자락이 부버지는 소리 담배 연긔 술의 냄새 요란하게 흐르는 짜즈에<72> 맛차서 춤추는 젊은 남자와 녀자 파득파득 떠는 우슴소리와 흥분된 얼골! 그들은 인생의 괴롬과 쓰라림을 모조리 이저 버린 듯이 즐거웁게 뛰논다.
거긔엔 눈물도 업고 슯홈도 업고 고민도 업는 행복한 인간들만 모히는 장소가티 보인다.
무겁게 떠도는 공긔속에서 밤이 느저도 입술의 접촉하는 소리와 한가지로 화려한 흥분은 어느때까지든지 게속된다.
젊은 남자! 그리고 어엽분 웨트레쓰는 서로 얼사안고 「마서라 먹어라」하며 술취한 붉은 얼골에 겨우 여지는 눈으로 서로서로 처다본다.

대체 카페란 그 곳을 찻는 者들은 엇던 者들이며 그들을 마저 상대해주는 웨트레쓰들은 엇더한 者들인가.

여러 식구의 생명을 구할만한 직업을 엇지를 못햇섯다. 여러 식구의 밥―입 그것 때문에 全孃은 워트레쓰 생활에 발을 듸려놋케 되엿다.
직업의식은 파악치 못하고 다만 원트레쓰란 것이 천한 물건이라고 인식햇든 全양은 자긔 자체를 비열하게 보앗다. 그리고 넷날의 알엇든 동무들과도 발을 똑 끈헛섯다. 길에서 동무나 아는 사람을 맛나면 천리나 만리나 도망하엿섯다.
그는 카페-의 밤 오색등불이 켜지고 레코-드가 도라가는 밤 남자들이 몰녀드는 카페-의 밤이 제일 무서웟다.
추한 손에게 얼싸 안커서 가진 모욕을 당한 뒤 제 집에서 자긔의 도라오기만 기다리는 동생과 늣도록 잠 안자고 울고 게신 어머님의 얼골이 끈허진 필님처럼 낫타낫다 살아진다.<77> 그는 무대에서 여러 사람의 환영밧든 천진한 시대와 오늘의 자긔를 대조해 보앗다. 그리고는 울엇다. 세상의 악착함과 모순을 하로 잇틀 깨달엇섯다.
그리하야 全양은 워트레쓰란 것을 리해하는 동시에 자긔자체를 리해하게되자 압으로는 붓그럼업시 한 직업부인으로서 맹진하겟다는 것이 그의 그의 포부라고한다.

밤 세시까지 순전히 남자들 손에서 부댁기다가 도라가는 그들 워트레쓰의 안식처가 어듸냐 서울에 집잇는 사람은 자긔의 집을 차저간다만은 멀니서 떠나온 사람들은 쓸쓸한 셋방―빈방을 차저가고 남어지는 카페-우 층-음산한 방에 몸을 던진다.
피곤한 몸이라 잠이 들면 모-든 것을 이저버리겟지만 그 잠이 깨여지는 때―얼마나 외로우며 얼마나 쓸쓸할 것인가.
락원회관에 드나든 사람은 키가 알맛게 크고 얼골이 몹시 복성스럽고 어업부게 생긴 단발양―金松錦양을 보앗을 것이다. 그는 고향을 함경북도 회령(會寧)에 두고 2년전 열여섯살 적에 서울에 와서 연극시장과 여러 가극단에 단엿다고.
그는 나희 열여덟살 소녀로 웨 카페-까지 드러오게 되엿든가?
아버지는 안게시고 늙으신 홀어머니와 옵바 두 분이 이섯는데 그의 옵바는 도라가섯다고 그러케되니 나어린 소녀의 생각에도 자긔가 생활을 하지안으면 안된다는 것을 깨닷고 극게에 몸을 던젓섯다만은 그가 생각햇든 극게와는 너무나 엄청낫든 것이다. 그리하야 金양은 카페-까지 굴너왓섯든 것이다.
멀니서 어린 딸의 돈 보내주는 것을 기다리는 어머님의 가삼인들 여북하리만은 어머님을 떠나서 천리타향의 외로운 객으로 세상에 가진 파란을 맛보는 金양도 어지간이 동정할비이다.
그는 꾀꼬리보다도 더 아름다운 목소리의 주인공이다.
인물 잘나고 소리곱은 그가 조선에 태여나지 안엇드면 귀엽고 훌늉한 인물이 되엿섯슬 것이다. 그가 제일 무서워하는 것이 주정뱅이들에게 괴롬밧는 때란다.

어린 金양도 세상을 다-알엇단다.
그는 울 일이 잇서도 속으로 울 줄 알고 웃기시려도 웃을 줄 안다. 눈물을 흘니면서도 우슴띄어야하는 자긔의 자신을 리해하게 되엿섯다.
