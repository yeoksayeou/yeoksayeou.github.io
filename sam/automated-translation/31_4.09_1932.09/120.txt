Secret Detective Bureau
Salary Investigation Team = First Team Dispatch Report

In this world, there are many incidents and individuals shrouded in a thick fog of secrecy. This is especially severe in a society like ours, where not everything has found its order yet, and where not all individuals stand in their respective posts. Our company has now established a Secret Detective Bureau and, after the strictest investigation, will expose and introduce the internal affairs of all these areas.
As the first phase plan, we will first handle one of three topics each month:
1. Salary investigation across various sectors of society
2. Investigation of the assets of the wealthy throughout all of Chosŏn (全朝鮮)
3. The amount of gold foreign companies take from Chosŏn.

Foreword

In the Government-General or its direct subordinate public offices, there is such a thing as a staff register (職員錄), where it is clearly numerically investigated and recorded how much annual salary everyone receives, from the newly appointed Governor-General down to the hired employees (雇員). Thus, people in the world can roughly guess that person's income.
Just because one can guess doesn't mean anyone intends to extort money from that person, nor does it mean criticizing whether the salary is small or large. Still, because one knows the level of income, one can know that person's standard of living and ascertain whether they are living an honest life corresponding to their station. This is, on one hand, an interesting <42> and important matter.
However, the income of gentlemen (諸氏) in various public institutions formed amongst ourselves has not been made known to the world. Believing there would be various benefits if this were known, this Detective Bureau has decided to tackle this salary stratum as its first task. However, no matter how fair an investigation we conduct, since we are trying to press for facts that others try to hide, sometimes sixth-sense-like conjectures may arise, and also, there might be points deliberately obscured out of consideration for the impact their announcement might cause, even when known fairly. We ask for your understanding on that point.

A Team = Banking and Company Sector

No matter who you name as the banking kings of the peninsula (半島), they are the two individuals, Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl (朴榮喆) and Mr. Min Tae-sik (閔大植). Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl is the Managing Director (取締役頭取) of the Sangŏp Bank (商業銀行) with a capital of 9 million won, a giant in one part of the financial kingdom, and Mr. Min Tae-sik is the President (頭取) of the Tongil Bank (東一銀行) with a capital of 4 million won, almost dividing the realm between them and waging a great struggle for supremacy.
So, how does the comparison of these two gentlemen's annual salaries turn out? Living (捿息하느니만치) in the kingdom of gold, one imagines their compensation would be tremendously large, but according to what I have discovered, Mr. Pak of Sangŏp Bank's official annual salary received from the bank amounts only to about 3,600 won. This includes the executive allowances given at each settlement period, so all told, he receives about 300 won per month.
However, since Mr. Pak is the President (頭取), his social engagements are frequent. The enormous entertainment expenses—said to be about 10,000 won annually!—are, of course, all paid by the bank, and also the large travel expenses when he occasionally goes to inspect regional branches—these are temporary, special incomes.
While his legitimate income received from the bank like this is 3,600 won annually, this is only a fraction of Mr. Pak's total income. He holds 6,700 shares (each share 50 won, half paid-up) in Sangŏp Bank alone, and also holds 3,970 shares in his father, Mr. Pak Ki-sun's (朴基順), name, yielding very large dividends. Additionally, there is income from privately owned fertile land (沃土) throughout Chŏlla Province (全南) and income from other stock certificates (株券)—however, since the purpose here is not to investigate Mr. Pak's assets but only his income as the Bank President (頭取), we stop here.
Then how much does Mr. Min Tae-sik receive? Mr. Min also has a huge total income, backed by the entire fortune of Chosŏn's landed millionaire, Min Yŏng-hwi (閔泳徽), but since this is outside the scope here, it is all excluded from discussion. Only, how much is the annual salary received from <43> Tongil Bank? He is also neck and neck with Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl, with an annual income of 3,500-3,600 won, about 300 won per month.
Tongil Bank is renowned for having numerous branches and sub-branches throughout every nook and cranny of Chosŏn. Starting with the branches in Seoul at Tongdaemun, Namdaemun, Sŏdaemun, Kwanhun-dong, its branches extend to over ten locations including Kunsan, Wŏnsan, Hamhŭng, Kimhwa, Ch’ŏnan, Yesan, Hongsŏng, etc. Thus, President Min Tae-sik's branch visits are frequent, and it goes without saying that he has much extra income. The dividends from the 1,071 shares of Tongil Bank stock held in his name are also substantial.
Looking at the department heads (과장급) of Tongil Bank, secretary Mr. Im Kŭng-sun (任兢淳) receives 120 won; General Affairs Manager Yun [*]p'yŏng (尹*炳), Business Manager O Kŏn-yŏng (吳建泳), Inspection Manager Kim Sŏng-kwŏn (金星權), and the branch managers in various locations receive roughly 100 to 140-150 won per month.
And the general bank employees' salaries are said to start at over 50 won for graduates of higher commercial schools, and 40 won is the starting salary for graduates of Class A commercial schools (甲種 상업학교).
Then, how much is the income of the members of the Haedong Bank (海東銀行), which is advancing into the financial world with the force of one warrior matching a thousand (一騎當千)?
However, the income of the executives at Haedong Bank is entirely different in nature from other banks. Executive Director Kim Nyŏn-su (金년洙), Directors Kim Chae-su (金在洙) and Yi Chi-song (李之松), Manager Mun Sang-cha (文尙字), Acting Manager Ch’oe Sŏn-mun (崔善文), and others at Haedong Bank have no fixed monthly salary. No, putting it that way is misleading. It's not that they have no salary at all; they do, but it is extremely small. Therefore, it amounts only to a kind of transportation expense (車馬費).
In reality, the executives working at this bank do their work with the spirit that, since it's their own bank, what need is there for compensation? Even if it's their own bank, how could such a spirit arise without income from other sources for living expenses? Fortunately, it is said that these gentlemen have considerable income from their abundant private assets.
Thus, they are not in a position where they need to count the days until the twenty-first of the month [payday?] for the bank's compensation, but in any case, their spirit of not receiving an annual salary beyond transportation expenses is something to be admired.
Besides these, there are investigations into the incomes of executives at Hansŏng Bank (漢城銀行), and regionally, Tongnae Bank (東萊銀行) in Tongnae, Puksŏn Sangŏp Bank (北鮮商業은행) in Hamhŭng, Kyŏngil Bank (慶一은행) in Taegu, Honam Bank (湖南은행) in Kwangju, Miryang Bank (密陽은행) in Miryang, etc. However, since the amounts are roughly slightly less than the standard set by Mr. Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl of Sangŏp Bank and Mr. Min Tae-sik of Tongil Bank, we leave it to everyone's conjecture and move our pen to the newspaper company sector.

B Team = Newspaper Company Sector

Speaking of newspaper companies, the Chosun Ilbo (朝鮮日報) is currently under suspension, and the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報) also closed its doors long ago <44>, so among private newspapers, the Dong-A Ilbo (東亞日報) stands alone loftily (兀立), and the Maeil Sinbo (每日申報) stands firm as the government mouthpiece (御用紙). Although the newspaper world is quite quiet, the existence of the figures carrying the burden of that reporting institution, which makes a hundred thousand readers cry and laugh day and night, is significant. So, how much compensation do those individuals receive? First, Mr. Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹), the president of Dong-A Ilbo, which has a capital of 300,000 won and now reports profits, even if only a few thousand won, at each semi-annual closing, receives a monthly salary of 250 won. Besides that, banquet expenses amounting to several thousand won, for instance, for inviting businessmen from Japan or inviting high officials from the Government-General at year-end/New Year, are all paid by the company. The next highest-ranking person after President Song is Business Director Yang Wŏn-mo (梁源模), who receives 170 won. Logically, based on seniority (席順), Editorial Director Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) should receive more, but Ch’unwŏn (春園) joined the company later than Mr. Yang, so he ranks below Mr. Yang. Yi Kwang-su receives 150 won, 20 won less than him, and Acting Editorial Director Sŏl Ŭi-sik (薜義植) receives 100 won.
Apart from these, Political Editor Kuk Ki-yŏl (鞠琪烈), Economics Editor Kim U-p'yŏng (金佑坪), Society Editor Hyŏn Chin-gŏn (玄鎭建), Provincial Editor Pak Ch’an-hŭi (朴찬熙), and Mr. Ham Sang-hun (咸尙勳) of the editorial board all receive between 80 and 85 won. Only among the department heads, Research Editor Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿) is at the highest peak, receiving 90 won. The various department heads in the Business Bureau are also comparable to this. Among regular reporters, Mr. Kim Tong-jin (金東進) is the highest at 80 won. Messrs. Sin T'ae-ik (申泰翊), Kim Tu-baek (金두白), Kim Chang-hwan (金章煥), Pak Kŭm (朴錦), female reporter Ch'oe Ŭi-sun (崔義順), Im Pong-sun (任鳳淳), Sŏ Hang-sŏk (徐恒錫), Ch'oe Yong-hwan (崔容煥), Yi Kil-yong (李吉用), and others all receive over 60 won, up to around 75 won. Mr. Chu Yo-han (朱耀翰), a commissioned writer for the editorial board, receives 50 won, and at the lowest level, there is someone in the collation department (整理部) receiving 40 won. Dong-A Ilbo's payday is the 26th of every month, and they always pay on this date. In the newspaper sector, where delayed salary payments are common due to managing lean households with meager finances, the fact that Dong-A Ilbo's salaries are paid without discount is a unique feature. Being a wealthy household, it should naturally be different, but while we're at it, let's elaborate a bit more and add a digression (客談). Even if appointed as a county magistrate (郡守) in the Government-General, the starting salary for a high official (高等官) of the 7th or 8th rank is only about 90 won, so a newspaper department head receives the salary of a clumsy (섯투른) county magistrate. Also, one has to serve 7-8 years as a county clerk (郡書記) or police inspector (警部) to reach the 6th or 7th sub-official rank (判任官), but a regular reporter's salary can be said to be superior to that of a county clerk or police inspector with 7-8 years of service. If we talk about the 150-160 won of the two directors, this could be said to surpass the level of a Provincial Councilor (道 事務官) or Advisor (參與官). And the president's 250 won corresponds to the starting salary of a Provincial Governor (道知事) wearing three gold stripes. Gentlemen, how do you feel? Haha (呵呵).
I hear that the standard starting monthly salary for reporters at this newspaper is 70 won for university graduates and 50 won for vocational school graduates, and it increases by about 5 won each year. Also, there is an annual bonus (賞與), which is about 100%, meaning roughly one month's <45> salary.
Then, what about the government organ Maeil Sinbo? The president is a Japanese named Matsuoka (松岡), so we exclude him from discussion. The vice president is Mr. Pak Sŏk-yun (朴錫胤). For a while, rumors of his transfer were frequent—going as a vice-consul to the Kando (間島) consulate, or as the head of the Minsaengdan (民生團)—but he still sits firmly in the vice president position, with a monthly salary of 200 won? Below him is Mr. Yi Ik-sang (李益相), pen name Sŏnghae (星海), known as a man of letters, as Editorial Director, receiving 150 won. Society Editor Chŏng In-ik (鄭寅翼), Economics Editor Chu Yun (朱潤), Mr. Ko Yŏng-han (高永翰) of the Political Department, etc., receive from 60 won up to 90 won. The regular reporters (諸氏) are said to receive roughly 50 to 80 won. Besides this, Maeil Sinbo is generous with bonuses, given twice a year, generally about one month's salary each time (upper and lower half-year).
The late Sŏhae (曙海), Mr. Ch'oe Hak-song's (崔鶴松), salary was also said to be about 70 won.
Then, what were the salaries of the president and other executives of Chosun Ilbo and Chungang Ilbo? Since it is awkward to disclose the internal affairs of newspapers currently closed (閉門), this detective is withholding the announcement. And in the future, on the day the newspapers reopen (開門) and the lineup of new executives is announced, we intend to accurately investigate their salaries and complete the investigation of this newspaper sector.

C Team = Educator Sector

Even among educators, there are various types. The compensation received by university professors or vocational school professors is vastly different from that received by secondary school teachers. And even among secondary schools, government schools (官立學校), private schools (私立學校), and schools managed by Westerners all differ in their [*illegible character]s.
Also, the number of schools is too large, and the number of teachers reaches a multitude of 400-500, making it impossible to record each one here. Therefore, we will record one or two examples to provide a general standard. First, let's look at secondary schools. Among them, Paichai Academy (배재학당), which has the longest history, is a school managed by Westerners. Its principal, Mr. Appenzeller (아펜설라), receives a monthly salary of 500 won. Below him, the academic dean (敎務主任), Mr. Sin Pong-jo (辛鳳祚), receives 150 won. And the teachers for geography (地歷), mathematics (數理), law and economics (法制 經濟), and physical education (體操) start from 70 won and generally reach up to 120-130 won. Moreover, at Paichai Academy, the annual subsidy of 28,000 won from the American Mission (米國 미슌) recently decreased to 20,000 won, which is said to have even affected the teachers' salaries.
Since Paichai Academy is like this, other secondary schools under Western control like Kyŏngsin School (경신학교), Ewha Academy (이화학당), and Paehwa Girls' High School (배화여자고보) generally follow that standard without error. For schools managed purely by Koreans, Chungang High School (중앙고보) principal Hyŏn Sang-ch'un (玄相充) receives 150 won?, Posŏng High School (보성고보) principal <46> Yi Hŏn-sŏng (李惃性) receives 150 won, Tongdŏk Girls' High School (동덕여자고학교) principal Cho Tong-sik (趙東植) receives 150 won, Hwimun High School (휘문고보) principal Yi Yun-jae (李潤在) and the principal of Yangjŏng High School (양정고보) are all around that level. For girls' schools, Sukmyŏng (숙명) and Chinmyŏng (진명) have slight differences, but generally fit the standard written above.
Chungdong School (중동학교) principal Ch'oe Kyu-dong's (崔奎東) salary is exactly the same as Academic Dean An Il-yŏng's (安一英) at 160 won? Generally, principals' salaries have small increments. If they get promoted or if the school's finances become ample (有餘), it might jump by several tens of won at once, but academic deans generally receive 120 to 150 won, and the teachers below them rise from 70 won up to 130 won.
However, a general standard is being established for determining salary amounts. In the past, due to a shortage of qualified secondary school teachers, any graduate from a Higher Normal School (高師) could command 140-150 won, and schools almost competed to hire them. But now, due to the flood of qualified individuals (有資格者), the starting salary level is also dropping low like the Han River's (漢江) water level in July-August.
The starting salary for a qualified person who finished vocational school in Chosŏn and passed the secondary school teacher examination is 75 won; graduates from Hiroshima or Tokyo Higher Normal Schools (廣島나 東京 고등사범) get 85 won; and graduates with bachelor's degrees from Tokyo Imperial University (東京帝大) or Keijō Imperial University (京城帝大) get 95 won, with variations of about 5 won depending on the school.
Also, just a few years ago, any decent school routinely gave a year-end bonus, but recently, due to financial reasons, many schools have completely eliminated it. If given, it's usually 100%, i.e., one month's salary; some give 50% or 30%. Even so, it's better than nothing, perhaps.

Regarding vocational schools (專門學校), there are only six in Chosŏn altogether: Posŏng Vocational School (보성전문), Buddhist Vocational School (불교전문), P'yŏngyang Sungsil (평양숭실), Chŏnghŭi Vocational School (정희전문), Severance Medical College (세부란쓰醫專), and Ewha Womans Vocational School (이화여자전문). Speaking of Posŏng Vocational School, associated with Mr. Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙), Principal Kim receives almost no compensation due to various honorary positions (譽職). Academic Dean Ch'oe Tu-sŏn (崔斗善) receives 150 won, and professors like Mr. Hong Sŏng-ha (洪性夏) receive 100 won? Commissioned lecturers (囑託 敎諭) like Yi Kwang-gu (李寬求) and Yu Chin-o (兪鎭午) are paid based on hours, reportedly X won per hour.
At Chŏnghŭi Vocational School, Dean Yu Ŏk-kyŏm (兪億兼) receives 200 won, and the various professors there—Paek Nam-un (白南雲), Chŏng In-bo (鄭寅普), Chŏng In-sŏp (鄭寅燮), Yi Yun-jae (李允宰), etc.—all receive around 100 won. Other vocational schools are likely comparable to this, without significant error.

We will reinvestigate and report in more detail when the opportunity arises later. Additionally, investigations into individuals in society, Christian, Buddhist, and Ch'ŏndogyo circles have been conducted but are deferred to the next issue due to space constraints. Lastly, please understand and provide much assistance to the activities of our team members working in various fields. <47>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine launches a "Secret Detective Bureau" series, aiming to expose hidden facts about Korean society under Japanese rule. Its first report focuses on salaries across different sectors: banking, newspapers, and education. It details the official salaries and some additional income sources (like expense accounts and dividends) of prominent figures like bank presidents Pak Yŏng-ch’ŏl and Min Tae-sik, newspaper executives Song Chin-u and Yi Kwang-su, and school principals. It also provides salary ranges for lower-level employees and teachers, comparing them to government official salaries and noting differences between institutions (e.g., private vs. public, Korean-run vs. Western-run). The article acknowledges potential inaccuracies due to secrecy and promises further investigations.

For historians of colonial Korea, this article is particularly valuable. It offers concrete, albeit potentially estimated, figures on the economic realities and compensation structures within key Korean institutions during a period of significant social change and colonial pressure. It sheds light on the relative status and income levels of elites in finance, media, and education, revealing both the considerable wealth of some (bankers) and the varying, sometimes precarious, financial situations in fields like journalism and private education. The comparison with government salaries provides context for the socio-economic positioning of these professions within the colonial hierarchy. The magazine's stated goal of transparency and investigation, even with caveats, reflects a contemporary interest in understanding the inner workings and disparities within Korean society itself.

월급 조사隊=第一隊 출동報告

이 세상에는 비밀의 濃霧속에 잠긴 사건이나 인물이 만히 잇슴니다, 더군다나 우리 사회와 갓치 모든 것이 아직 질서를 잡지 못하고 또 모든 인물들이 제각기 부서에 서지 못한 곳에서는 더욱 尤甚함니다. 본사는 이제 비밀 탐정국을 두어 가장 엄밀한 조사을 거친 뒤 이 모든 방면의 내부 사정을 폭로 소개하겟슴니다.

첫머리

총독부나 직속 관공서에는 직원錄이란 것이 잇서서 신임의 총독 이하 雇員들에게 이르기까지 어느 사람은 연봉 얼마를 먹는다. 또 어느 사람은 월급 얼마씩 밧는다고 분명하게 수자적으로 조사 기록되어 잇서서 세상사람들은 그 사람의 수입을 대강 짐작하게된다.
짐작하게 된다고 누가 그 사람더러 돈비력지를 하자는 뜻도 또는 월급이 적다만타고 시비하자는 뜻도 안이지만 그래도 그 수입 정도를 알기때문에 그 사람의 생활정도를 알 수 잇서서 그가 정직한―分에 상응하는 생활을 하고 잇나 엇저나함을 잘 알게된다. 이것은 한편으로 흥미도 잇는<42> 重要한 일이다.
그런데 우리네들 사이에서 이루어진 여러 가지 공적 기관에 잇는 諸氏의 수입은 세상에 알니워진 바가 업다. 만일 아라노코 보면 여러 가지로 유익한 경우가 *을 뜻하여 本탐정국은 제1착수로 이 월급층을 건드리기로 한 것이다. 그러치만 아모리 공명한 조사를 한다 할지라도 남이 작고 숨기려는 사실를 채근하려하는 관계로 간혹 제6감적 추측이 생기겟고 또한 엇든 것은 공명히 알면서도 그 발표로 말미아마 영향되는 점을 고려하고서 일부러 흐리어 놋는 점도 잇겟다. 그 점은 양해하여 주기를 바란다.

A班= 은행회사방면

半島의 은행王은 누구누구 하여도 朴榮喆씨와 閔大植씨의 두분이다. 朴榮喆씨는 자본금900만원의 商業은행의 取締 役頭取로 금융왕국의 一方의 巨雄이요 또 閔大植씨는 자본금400만원의 東一은행의 頭取로써 천하를 양분하다십히하고 大쟁패전을 치르고 잇다.
그러면 이 두 분의 연봉비교는 엇더케 되는고? 황금국왕에서 捿息하느니만치 그 보수는 굉장히 만흘 것 갓치 상상되지만, 내가 탐지한 바에 의하면 商業은행 朴씨는 정식으로 은행에서 밧는 연봉이 3,600원 정도에 끈친다. 이것은 결산기마다 잇는 중역 수당까지 합산한 것으로 이럭저럭 월 300원 쯔음 밧는다할 것이다.
그러치만 朴씨는 頭取이니만치 그 교제가 빈번하다. 거액의! 年 약 만원이란 말이 잇다―교제비는 물론 모다 은행 지출이요 또 각금 지방의 지점 시찰로 갈 때의 거액의 여비 등―이런 것은 임시임시의 특별 수입이다.
이와 가치 은행에서 밧는 정당 수입이 年 3,600원은 되지만 이것은 朴씨의 수입의 몃분지1에 불과하다, 氏에게는 商業은행에만 6,700주의 주(매주 50원 반액불입)를 가지고 잇고 又 그 아버지 朴基順씨 명의로 3,970주를 가지고 잇서 그 배당이 대단히 크다. 또 이 밧게 全南 일대의 사유 沃土로부터의 수입 기타 株券 등으로 드러오는 수입―그러나 이 자리는 朴씨의 재산조사가 목적이 아니고 다만 은행 頭取로서의 수입을 조사함에 목적이 잇섯슴으로 이에 끈친다.
그러면 閔大植씨는 얼마나 밧는고! 閔씨 亦 朝鮮의 토지갑부 閔泳徽씨의 全재산을 배경으로 하느니만치 제반 총수입이 거대한 바 잇지만은 여기서는 범위 외의 일임으로 모다 논외로 하고 오직 東一은행으로부터<43> 밧는 연봉이 얼마나 되는고. 氏 亦 朴榮喆씨와 막상막하하여 年收 3천5,6백원 月收 300원 정도에 잇다.
東一은행은 全朝鮮 방방곡곡에 지점과 출장소 만키로 有數하다. 서울에 동대문 남대문 서대문 寬勳洞의 각 지점을 위시하여 群山 元山 咸興 金化 天安 禮山 洪城 등 10여처에 가지가 버더 잇슴으로 頭取 閔大植씨의 지점 출장도 빈번하야 그 예외 수입이 만히 잇슬 것은 물론 氏의 명의로 가지고 잇는 東一은행株 1,071주에서 나오는 배당이 또한 거액이 잇다.
東一은행의 각 과장급을 들추어보면 비서역의 任兢淳씨 120원 서무과장 尹*炳 업무과장 吳建泳 검사과장 金星權 及 各地 지점장의 월급은 대략 백원에서 백 4,50원을 밧고 잇다.
그러고 일반 행원의 월급은 고등 상업출신은 초임급 50원 이상이요 甲種 상업학교출신은 40원이 초임급이라한다.
그러면 新進氣銳하여 一騎當千의 勢로 財界에 약진하는 海東은행의 여러분에 수입은 얼마나 되는고?
그런데 海東은행 중역의 수입은 다른 은행과 그 성질을 전혀 달니한다. 海東은행의 전무 取締役 金년洙씨 이하 取締役 金在洙 同 李之松씨나 지배인 文尙字 지배인 대리 崔善文 등 諸씨는 일정한 월급이라고 업다. 아니 그러케 말하면 어폐가 잇다. 월급이 전무한 것은 아니요 잇기는 잇되 극히 소액이다. 그러기에 그것은 일종 車馬費 정도에 불과하다.
실상 이 은행에 근무하시는 중역 諸氏는 자기의 은행인데 보수는 무슨 보수랴 하는 기개로 일을 보신다. 아무리 자기 은행이라 하여도 딴 곳으로 의식의 수입이 업시야 감히 엇더케 그러한 기개인들 나리요만은 다행히 諸氏에게는 풍부한 사유재산으로부터 엇는 수입이 상당한 바가 잇다한다.
그래서 은행의 보수를 스무하롯날 되기를 손곱아 기다릴 필요업는 처지이지만 아무튼 車馬費 정도박게 연봉을 아니 밧는 그 기개에는 경탄할 노릇이다.
이 밧게 漢城은행이나 또 지방으로 東萊의 東萊은행 咸興의 北鮮商業은행 大邱의 慶一은행 光州의 湖南은행 密陽의 密陽은행 등의 諸중역 수입의 조사가 잇스나 대략 商業은행의 朴榮喆씨 東一은행의 閔大植씨 표준에서 조곰 감하여 낸 금액이기 여러분의 추측에 맛기고 붓을 신문사 방면으로 옴긴다.

신문사라 하여도 지금은 朝鮮日報가 정간중에 잇고 中央日報마저 문을 다친지 己久함으로 <44> 민간 신문으로는 東亞日報가 외로히 兀立하고 그리고 御用紙로서 每日申報가 버티고 잇서서 신문계가 자못 한산한 중에 잇지만은, 그러터래도 日日夜夜 십만 독자를 울리고 웃기고 하는 그 보도기관의 세력들을 떠메고 잇는 인사들의 존재는 크다. 그러면 그 분들은 얼마씩의 보수를 밧고 지내는가. 위선 30만원의 자본을 가지고 이제는 상하반기의 결산때마다 다만 수천원식이라도 이익을 보고 잇다는 東亞日報의 사장 宋鎭禹씨는 월급 250원이고, 그 박게 가령 日本서 나온 실업가를 초대한다든가 연말년시에 총독부 大官들을 초대한다든가 하는 연회비 수천원은 모조리 社에서 내어준다. 宋사장에 다음가는 고급자로는 영업국장 梁源模씨가 잇는데 원래 席順으로 말하면 편집국장 李光洙씨가 더 밧어야 올켓지만 春園은 梁씨보다 입사한 年祖가 엿터서 梁씨의 하위에 잇다. 梁源模씨의 월급은 170원, 李光洙씨는 그보다 20원이 적은 150원 그리고 편집국장 대리로 잇는 薜義植씨가 100원을 밧는다.
