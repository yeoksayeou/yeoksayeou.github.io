Volunteer Soldier's Marching Song
Ch'unwŏn

One
On this day we send you off shouting Mansei!
The path you take as a soldier of His Majesty the Emperor.
To protect our nation, Japan,
You go, a volunteer soldier, receiving this awe-inspiring command.

Two
How gallant and clean is your appearance!
Reliable and strong like Mount T'aesan!
A brave soldier of the Imperial Army, born of my hometown,
A great honor offered to His Majesty the Emperor.

Three
Home front service and matters behind are our duty,
May loyalty and courage be with you wherever you go!
Go forth! Let us meet again on the triumphant return day!
Wave the Flag of the Rising Sun all around, shout Mansei! <171>

Let's Go, Let's Go Quickly
Chu Yo-han

Let's go, let's go quickly
Crossing mountains, crossing waters,
To the hill of hope,
Let's seek it out.
Ninanī nanina
The wind dances,
The sky is clear.
Open your heart!
The whole world
Is my home.

Let's go, let's go quickly
Shouldering a backpack,
Towards the highest peak,
Let's climb up.
Ninanī nanina
The birds sing,
The woods are deep.
A grass cushion, a stone pillow,
The whole world
Is my home.

Let's go, let's go quickly
Crossing the sea,
Towards the sun-rising harbor,
Let's head there.
Ninanī nanina
In the young one's heart,
Blood always leaps.
Let the waves leap too!
The whole world
Is my home. <172>

Mother's Heart
Yang Chu-dong

One
Forgetting all the pain of childbirth,
The heart that strives day and night in raising,
Changing wet places for dry ones,
Toiling until hands and feet are worn.
What under heaven can be called vast?
A mother's sacrifice is boundless.

Two
In childhood, holding, carrying on the back, soothing,
Grown up, leaning on the door, the waiting heart,
Worried lest the child fall ill or go astray,
On her once-fair brow, wrinkles are full.
What on earth can be called high?
A mother's devotion is utmost.

Three
In people's hearts are all kinds of wishes,
In a mother's heart, only one,
Giving her whole life unstintingly for her children,
The heart that offers, carving flesh and bone.
What among humans can be called holy?
A mother's love is endless.

Autumn Harvest
Yi Sŏ-gu

One
Golden-hued vast fields, overgrown with grain,
The harvest season of autumn is a joyful time.
Keeping rhythm with the merry harvest song (적양가),
Ŏnŏllŏl sangsadiya, let's do the harvesting.

Two <173>
The women who weeded, their toil was great,
The old man who guarded the water channels, venerable indeed.
The grain raised by the whole family, the whole village,
Ŏnŏllŏl sangsadiya, let's do the harvesting.

Three
The grain concluded with young strength and beads of sweat,
Becomes the provisions for the great family of New East Asia (신동아).
Farming is the great foundation of the world, a day to rejoice,
Ŏnŏllŏl sangsadiya, let's do the harvesting.

Moon Viewing
Pak Chong-hwa

Today is the day of the great full moon, let's go moon viewing,
Letting down silk ribbons, let's go moon viewing.
Binding torches and lighting fires, we go moon viewing,
Climbing the back hill, we go moon viewing.
On the mid-slope of Moonrise Peak (월출봉), the moon rises,
Buoyantly, beyond the clouds, the moon smiles.
On the top branches of the green pine, a silver tray hangs!
Moon, oh moon, receive my bow, nineteen-year-old moon spirit.

The sound of the village troupe's gong is also delightful under the moonlight,
In ten thousand homes, every household plays yut dice.
The heart prays, respectfully laying down the torch,
May the fields and paddies have a bountiful harvest this year.

Patriotism Day Song
Ch'unwŏn

One
On the first morning of every month, early,
The people of the whole village gather fully,
Receiving the sunbeams rising in the Eastern Sky on their bodies, <174>
They offer distant worship towards where His Majesty the Emperor resides.

Two
A silent prayer (묵도) offered with solemnly bowed heads,
For the guardian spirits of the nation (護國英靈) who sacrifice their bodies in the Holy War (聖戰),
For the Imperial Army soldiers fighting on water and land,
A day to appreciate their toil and great achievements.

Three
We are subjects of Great Japan (大日本)!
Eternally believing in and loving Japan and Korea as One Body (內鮮一體),
Let us proclaim the great spirit of the Imperial Way (皇道),
A day we gather myriad mouths to pledge together.

Four
The village is one house, all one family,
Helping each other in joyful and sad times,
Becoming the foundation of a total mobilization national defense state,
Our duty is great, so great, our Town/Village Association (町(洞里)會).

Green Sky
Yi Sŏ-gu

One
The sky is blue, like crystal,
Like ground jade, it is beautiful.
The sunbeams rising in that bright sky,
Are our hope, our vanguard.

Two
The sky where white clouds drift in clusters,
Is missed like a mother's embrace.
Where the shining evening stars twinkle,
Our beautiful dreams dwell.

Three
Looking at that sky, hope surges,
A heart burning with hope is like sunbeams. <175>
Leading young strength and high ideals,
Our green sky, we are deeply moved.

Spring News
Yi Ha-yun

All night long, the rain whispered affectionately,
Melting the remaining snow on the front and back mountains.
When young sprouts flutter on the grassy field,
White clouds spread their wings and fly lightly.

Carrying dreams, swallows come from Kangnam (江南),
At the eaves' edge, the heat haze shimmers, the path seems long.
Even the bird, lonely in the cage through the cold winter,
Yearning for the blue sky, cries and cries again.

The flute sound in the heart overflows with hope,
When flowers bloom, bees and butterflies, along the stream,
Every morning, may a new face be beautifully reflected.

Morning of Hope
Ch'unwŏn

Night has dawned, the morning of Hope!
The sunbeams rising in the eastern sky,
Everyone receive them, receive them fully,
And raising your voices, shout Mansei!

Arise! Receiving the command (吩咐)
Of our Emperor, one hundred million hearts as one,
Throughout the vast Heaven and Earth, under the principle of Eight Corners of the World Under One Roof (八紘一宇),
To build a new World.

Continent of twenty thousand li, Great Ocean of one hundred thousand li,
Of the Great Co-Prosperity Sphere (大共榮圈) of Great Asia (大亞細亞),
Where our Flag of the Rising Sun (日章旗) flies, <176>
Is the land where descendants for ten thousand generations will enjoy blessings.

Mountain and Water
Kim Sang-yong

The song of new birds in the clear morning is beautiful!
One drunk on flower fragrance gets soaked in the grass color (草色).
From the mountain top that even clouds rest to cross,
I look down upon the vast fields of ten thousand li.

From whose sorrow do the waves break?
It's distant, the water touches the sky.
Seeing the boats returning to the Port (浦口),
I yearn for the hometown of my heart.

Letting sorrows flow away on the waves,
I will climb the mountain and cleanse my heart with spiritual energy (靈氣).
The Life carefully nurtured by Heaven and Earth,
I intend to cherish and live cleanly.

Village Where the Cuckoo Cries
Kim Tong-hwan

One
Where the cuckoo's cry is heard deep in the Green Mountains (靑山),
Let's build a clean house and settle in.
Nilliriya, we young farmers,
Plowing the field (밧갈고) and sowing seeds, let's live this life.

Two
Among the woods, the water mill turns by the stream,
The shaggy dog chases chickens and runs out to the field.
Nilliriya, our young ladies,
Raising silkworms and weaving hemp, let's live joyfully.

Three
If it doesn't finish crying this year, it cries next year,
If it can't play enough on this mountain, it goes to that mountain to play.
Nilliriya, in our village in the Green Mountains,
The cuckoo comes well every year and cries. <177>

How Nice is Our Home
Mo Yun-suk

It's nice, so nice, oh how nice!
Can there be a place as good as my home?
On a chilly early winter night,
The warm lower part of our room's heated floor is nice.
In the scorching heat of May and June, under the blazing sun (볓불),
The main floor hall where the wind blows through freely is also nice.

It's nice, so nice, oh how nice!
Can there be a place as good as my home?
Swallows on the clothesline, sunny spring sunlight,
The rhythm of the cutting board knife in the kitchen is also nice.
On an evening when fluffy snow piles up deep,
The swish-swish sound of the broom is refreshing too.

It's nice, so nice, oh how nice!
Can there be a place as good as my home?
The baby sibling taking first steps, playing "round and round",
Playing "pat-a-cake" and "no-no", their cute acts are nice.
Tasty morsels, for you and me,
Our grandmother's stories (애기) are interesting too.

Coming Spring, Going Spring
Chu Yo-han

Spring sky, far away
Beyond the layers of clouds,
Skylark, what task takes you
Higher and higher?
Missing the spring of home,
Missing the spring of home,
The flock of swallows coming from Kangnam (江南),
Will they come today?
Will they come tomorrow?
To see if they are coming,
I climb up. <178>

Like silk thread unraveling,
An endless song,
Oriole, what task makes you
Sing all day long (하로종일)?
Missing the spring breeze,
Missing the spring breeze,
The young lady out for a stroll,
Has spring already come?
Is spring already going?
To let her know, to let her know (알래알래),
This is the song I offer.

Across the field, over the hill,
Riding the heat haze,
Kite, what task makes you
Circle the mountain peak?
Missing the spring of home,
Missing the spring of home,
The young man leaving on a long journey,
The great ambition once set,
The high, high hope,
Do not forget, do not forget,
I circle to remind you.

Evening Star
No Ch'ŏn-myŏng

One
Who scattered jewels in the sky?
Small jewels, large jewels, so beautiful.
Gathering around a bonfire, eating corn,
There were nights counting the stars in the sky.

Two
One star appears, two stars appear,
In the paddy fields, the cuckoo cries mournfully,
As the cornstalks rustle in the wind,
Gazing at the Milky Way, sleep drifted far away (머러저). <179>

Three
The sound of the water mill—it's been long since I heard it—
The night sky full of stars in my hometown, a night I long for.
Putting fireflies in pumpkin flower lanterns,
Are the children these days also counting stars?

When the Rugosa Roses Bloom
Kim Dong-hwan

Spring March too fades before long,
As azaleas and apricot blossoms also fade,
On the sandy beach by the river in front of my hometown,
Bright red, bright red Rugosa roses (海棠花) bloom.

When they bloom and smile, I was indifferent, but
Leaving home and thinking of them, I miss them terribly.
My hometown where seabirds fly and waves crash,
Even a dozen times a day, I visit in dreams.
This year too, after I left, in my hometown,
Receiving the wind and rain gently, did the Rugosa roses bloom?
I cannot forget, cannot forget, when the flower season comes,
My longed-for hometown, I truly cannot forget.

Sea
Im Hak-su

In the morning, golden sunbeams,
Ten million waves each leap and play.
In the evening, flocks of seagulls,
At the cliff's edge, the Storm (暴風) rages.

At midday, flocks of white Sheep (羊),
Distantly cross the Silver Line (銀線) and come, and come again.
As night falls, countless flowers,
Above my head, scatter brilliantly.

Ah, whether it rains or shines, <180>
In the shade of that blue island, the heart-rending Waves (波濤),
If I jump splash into them,
Among the grasses, Coral (珊瑚) and Pearls (眞珠) are intertwined (얼켜있다).

Hometown House
Chu Yo-han

One
On the thatched roof,
Gourds hang open,
On the grain stack,
Flocks of sparrows descend.
Missing my hometown house,
Where the castor oil plants bear fruit,
The shaggy dog barking,
My mother, whom I saw in dreams daily.
Ah, today too, in dreams,
I see you.

Two
On the thatched roof,
Peppers are drying,
Steaming mugwort rice cakes,
Welcoming autumn.
Missing my hometown house,
The yellow cow in the cowshed
Lies down with a full belly,
My younger sibling who brought in fodder.
Ah, today too, in dreams,
I long for you.

Three
On the thatched roof,
Crows cry,
In the elm tree,
Magpies build nests.
Missing my hometown house,
Goose, oh duck,
Don't be shy of me,
My hometown I left for ten years.
Ah, today too, in dreams,
Let's return (도라잔다). <181>

Snowy Night
Kim An-sŏ

Snow falls, snow falls.
The dark blue sky threatened to clear, but
White snowflakes, from early evening,
Flutter down, flutter down.
One flake, two flakes, all night long.

Snow falls, snow falls.
Saying this night is endlessly dark (검다고),
White snow upon the sleeping earth,
Pours down (내려쌥니다), pours down (내려쌥니다).
One flake, two flakes, all night long.

Snow falls, snow falls.
Though fated to melt in the sunlight,
Catching the moonlight coldly (차차), just gently,
It falls at night, falls at night.
Two flakes, one flake, all night long.

Our Joyful Home
Kim Tong-hwan

Shall we build our house on the white sandy riverbank in front?
No, no, the seagulls might mistakenly come calling.
Shall we build our house in the willow field behind the back fence?
No, no, the orioles will come.
Our, our three siblings' nest (보금자리),
Let's build it prettily on the grass-green embankment (방축).
Cows in the front yard, bees in the backyard,
Let's also plant plenty of forsythia and azaleas.

Buckwheat planted in early spring blooms in summer,
Barley sown in summer is harvested (거더지니) in autumn.
Laughter (우슴) in every house, grain in every field,
Throughout the year, our house's fortune is great.
Our, our three siblings' life,
When the sun rises early in the morning, we go out to the field.
In the evening, singing, we return (도라를와서),
Joyfully, happily, we spend the time. <182>

The Morning Sun is Rising
Yi Ha-yun

The eastern sky brightens, the siren (싸이렌) wails.
Gathering our clear minds, let us arise (이러들나세).
The beating heart as the red sun rises,
Is the hasty preparation amid hope.

Receiving the golden sunbeams, with all sincerity,
The palace worship (궁성요배), the single will of the myriad people.
To serve diligently at the workplace, to offer our bodies,
I too joyfully leave the house.

The black clouds retreat, the East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere (동아공영권).
The sun always rises in the East,
Bearing the heavy burden of world peace,
Let's greet (마지하자) the new morning, the siren wails.

Let Us Live
Chu Yo-han

Adapted from "Ch'ŏngsan Pyŏlgok" (靑山別曲), said to be a work from the Koryŏ Dynasty, included in the "Akhak Kwebom" (樂章歌詞)

One
Let us live, let us live,
Let us live in the Green Mountains (靑山),
Eating wild grapes (며루) and vine fruits (다레),
Let us live in the Green Mountains.
Bird, cry!
If you cry,
I cry too.
Ariari arŭrida
In the Green Mountains, in the Green Mountains, let us live.

Two
Let us live, let us live,
Let us live by the sea,
Eating fish and clams,
Let us live by the sea.
The day is unfamiliar,
When night comes,
What shall I do?
Ariari arŭrida
By the sea, by the sea, let us live.

Appendix: The several dozen songs above were all commissioned by the Korean Broadcasting Association (朝鮮放送協會) from gentlemen of the poetry world to provide good songs for general households, and these are all the ballads (謠曲) that have been broadcast since last autumn. <185>
<171-185>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of "New Songs" (Sin'gayo), essentially poems intended for singing, composed by prominent Korean poets in 1941. Commissioned by the Korean Broadcasting Association, the poems cover a range of themes, from pastoral scenes, family life, and nature appreciation to overtly political and pro-war mobilization pieces. Several poems explicitly promote Japanese imperialism, praising volunteer soldiers, the Emperor, the "Holy War," the concept of "Naesŏn Ilch'e" (Japan and Korea as One Body), and the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

For historians, this collection is significant as it starkly reveals the cultural mobilization efforts during the late colonial period. It showcases how leading Korean literary figures were enlisted (whether willingly or coerced) to produce propaganda supporting Japan's war effort and imperial ideology, blending nationalistic rhetoric with traditional poetic forms and themes. The juxtaposition of propagandistic verses with poems on nature and home life within the same collection highlights the complex and often pressured environment faced by intellectuals under colonial rule.
