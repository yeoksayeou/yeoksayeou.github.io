**Body Text Translation:**
Images of Beauties Gathering at Seoul Beauty Salons, A Half-Hour Scene at Han-Ch'ŏng Beauty Salon
						By Kim P'al-lyŏn

A single rickshaw came speeding like an arrow down the Tabang-gol alley and arrived before the main gate (正門) of the Han-Ch'ŏng Building on the Chongno intersection, where many flower wreaths (花環) were placed.
Getting out is the first-class famous kisaeng (一流名妓) Miss XXX, who boasts of having finished Tokyo Women's University, being fluent in English, and having formally studied music (音樂) and literature (文學). She sweeps her slender body, wrapped in a jade-colored, flower-patterned, fine silk skirt, and vanishes into the third floor of the building. It is now 10 in the morning. It seems she slept late <120> after attending some banquet last night and has come rushing in to get her makeup done, perhaps because there is a Nodol ferry boat song gathering today. After a while, Mrs. M, the wife of a bank executive, a "New Woman" (新女性) known for her musical talent since her early days studying abroad in Tokyo, a female teacher from some school, and about four or five other "New Women" hurriedly disappear onto the third floor. They are all entering the beauty salon.

I followed them up to see the newly opened Han-Ch'ŏng Beauty Salon (韓靑美容院). As soon as I stepped off the elevator and entered the corridor (廊下) paved with white tiles (白다일), the fragrant scent of face powder and rouge (脂粉) pierced my nose.
When I knocked on the door (아), a young girl asked me to come in. The room I entered and sat in was a reception room (應接室) furnished in Western style (洋風). On the left side hung a large, luxurious full-length mirror (體鏡), and on one wall stood an embroidered screen depicting flowers and plants (花草屛風). Landscape paintings also hung there, and there was a statue of Venus (비-너스 型像). On the large table in the middle sat a flower vase (花甁) with brightly blooming Crape Myrtles (百日紅), and illustrated magazines (畵報冊) were placed there to look at while waiting.

Just then, hearing a sound like undressing (解裳소리) from the other side, I looked over and saw a three-tiered wardrobe (장농) inlaid with classic-style mother-of-pearl (古典式 자개). There, the beautiful women (가인) who had just come up were putting down their handbags and some were changing their skirts. Hearing the explanation, I learned this was the preparation room, with lockers (衣寵) for five people, where each person receives a key (열대) and keeps her own belongings.

Next to it is a very large, spacious, and bright (明朗) Western-style room (洋室), which is the actual place where the beautification happens. Just then, three young women, the chief stylists (主壬技師), were busy trying to transform three young ladies (아씨) into peerless beauties (絕世美人) like Yang Guifei. Above their heads hung aluminum contraptions resembling windmills (風車) or sun lamps (太陽燈); these are apparently the so-called "hair drying machines" (머리 말니는 긔게). Although the Mitsukoshi Beauty Department (三越美容部) also has such machines, it takes 15 minutes to dry hair once, but the machines here, being expensive (said to be 500 won <121> each), supposedly remove all moisture (水分) in just 5 minutes.

In one corner, someone is applying powder to a nose bridge; in another, thick rouge (연지) is being dabbed onto lips (입설); and in another, a curling iron (아이론) is being applied to hair. This fragrant scent enveloping the room – indeed, the room is enveloped in skin and flower powder (花粉).
The young beauticians are all said to have honed their skills at first-rate specialty beauty salons in Tokyo.

The ladies (淑女) who gather at this establishment truly belong to various strata, from female teachers (女敎員), female office workers (女事務員), young ladies (令孃), and madams (令夫人級) to waitresses (女給) and kisaeng (妓生層) – women from all sorts of cutting-edge (尖端) classes are said to gather here.
This Beauty Salon (美容院) is managed by Mrs. O (吳氏), the wife (夫人) of Mr. Yi Chŏng-p'a (李鼎坡氏). Previously, Ms. O Yŏp-chu (吳葉舟女士) operated a beauty salon on the third floor of the Hwasin Department Store (和信百貨店), but after the fire (火災), there were no beauty salons run by Koreans in Seoul. Now, having secured a good location in the Han-Ch'ŏng Building, she is newly managing this one.
Is it because this Beauty Salon was established that many women who previously directed their steps towards Chōjiya (丁子屋) or the Mitsukoshi Beauty Salon (三越美容所) are flocking to Han-Ch'ŏng? <122>

**Summary**

This 1935 article by Kim P'al-lyŏn describes a visit to the newly opened Han-Ch'ŏng Beauty Salon in Seoul's Han-Ch'ŏng Building. The author details the modern, Western-style decor, the advanced equipment like a fast hair dryer imported from Japan, and the various beauty treatments offered. The salon caters to a diverse clientele of modern Korean women, including professionals, educated "New Women," wives of prominent men, and even kisaeng, reflecting changing social norms and the rise of beauty culture. It notes this Korean-owned business fills a void and competes directly with established Japanese salons like Mitsukoshi and Chōjiya. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the modernization of Seoul, the emergence of Korean consumer culture and entrepreneurship under colonial rule, the social phenomenon of the "New Woman," and the competition between Korean and Japanese businesses in the service sector.

서울 美容院에 모히는 美人群像, 韓靑美容院의 半時間風光
						金八蓮
인력거 한대가 다방골 골목으로 쏜살가치 달려오더니 종로네거리 韓靑삘딩 正門앞 花環 만흔 곳압헤 와 다인다.
나리는 이는 동경 녀자대학을 마처 영어 잘하고 음악과 문학을 본격적으로 안다고 떠드는 일류명긔 000로다. 그는 옥색 꼿문의 노은 비단치마로 휘휘감은 간얄픈 몸을 휘우처 삘딩 3층으로 자최를 감춘다. 지금이 아츰 10시. 어제밤 어느 연회에 갓다가 늣잠을 <120>자고 이러나서 오늘 또 노돌뱃노리나 잇는상 십허 허둥지둥 화장하려옴이다. 그런뒤 한참만에 모 은행의 중역부인으로 일즉 동경류학시대에 음악 잘하기로 일홈을 날니든 신녀성 모씨, 어느 학교의 녀교사 등등 4, 5인의 신녀성들이 창황히 3츰으로 사라진다. 모다 미용원으로 드러감이다.
나는 그 뒤를 따라 이번 새로 되엇다는 韓靑美容院 구경으로 올너것다. 벌서 「에레베타」에서 내려 白다일로 깐 랑하에 드러서자 향그러운 脂粉 내음새가 코를 찌른다.
또 아를 녹크하니 젊은 소녀가 드러오십사고 한다. 내가 드러가 안즌 방은 洋風으로 차린 應接室이라 왼편쪽에 크다란 호화로운 體鏡이 걸니어 잇고 한편벽쪽에는  수노은 花草屛風이 처잇다. 풍경화도 걸니어 잇고 비-너스 型像도 잇다. 가운데 큰 데-불에는 빨가케핀 百日紅을 꼬즌 花甁이 노혓고 쉬는 사이에 보라고 畵報冊도 노여 잇다.
그럴때에 저쪽에서 무엇인가 解裳소리 나기에 바라보니 古典式 자개를 물린 3층 장농이 노혓는데 그 쪽에서 지금 올너 오든 가인들이 핸드백을 두고 혹은 치마도 가라입는다. 설명을 듯고 보니 여기가 준비실로 다섯사람의 옷을 둘 衣寵이 잇서 여기서 열대를 마터 가지고 제것을 제가 간직하는 제도요.
그리고 그 다음은 아조 크고 넓고 明朗한 긔분이 도는 洋室인데 여기가 정작 美容하는 곳이다. 마츰 주임긔사인 젊은 녀자 셋이 세 아씨들을 양귀비 가튼 절세미인으로 만들고저 분주한 중이다. 머리 우로는 風車통 갓기도 하고 太陽燈갓기도 한 알미늄통이 내려덥헛는데 이것이 이른바 「머리 말니는 긔게」라 한다. 三越美容部에도 이런 긔게는 잇기는 하나 한번 말니자면 15분 동안이나 걸니나 여기 긔게는 갑이 빗싸느니 만치(500원 짜리라 한다)<121> 단 5분간이면 水分이 전혀 빠진다고 한다.
한쪽에서는 콧등에 분을 발너주는가 하면 한쪽에선 입설에 진한 연지를 찍어주고 한쪽에선 머리에 「아이론」을 걸고 잇다. 방안을 휩싸고 도는 향그러운 이 내음새 실로 방안은 살결과 花粉에 싸여잇는 것이다.
젊은 미용사들은 모다 동경에서 일류급 전문미용소에서 수완을 닥고 왓다고 한다.
이 집에 모히는 숙녀(淑女)는 실로 여러층으로서 女敎員 여사무원 令孃 令夫人級으로부터 女給 妓生층에까지 온갓 첨단 계급의 여성들이 모여든다고 한다.
이 美容院은 李鼎坡氏 夫人 吳氏가 經營하는 것으로 녜전에는 吳葉舟女士가 和信百貨店 3층에서 美容院을 하엿스나 火災后 서울에는 조선사람 측으로 하는 미용원이 업더니 이번에 韓靑삘딩의 조흔 장소를 어더 새로 경영하고 잇는 중이라 한다.
이 美容院이 설입되기 때문에 종래 丁子屋이나 三越美容所로 발을 옴기든 녀성들이 만히 韓靑으로 몰닌다든가. <122>
