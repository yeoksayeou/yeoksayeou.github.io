**Body Text Translation:**
All-Korea Store Owners United: The Hwasin Chain Store Owners' Association

This past July, over 170 Hwasin Chain Store owners who gathered in Seoul founded the Chain Store Owners' Association for the purpose of protecting their business interests.

Amidst public attention, the Hwasin chain stores have already greeted their second year. At the sample fair held this time on the upper floor of the chain store division in Myŏngch'ijŏng, contracts worth approximately 700,000 won were reportedly secured. Following this, the Hwasin head office's policy is to add about 200 more chain stores by this autumn. Thus, they will be doing business with 300-400 stores in the future, meaning transaction contract amounts will further surge, and the number of chain store owners will also further increase.

Considering this future prospect, the founding of this Chain Store Owners' Association, encompassing chain store owners from all of Korea in a mutually connected relationship with the head office, holds extremely profound significance. Reporting now on the circumstances before and after its establishment, on July 27th at 4:30 PM, several dozen Hwasin store owners from various regions who had come up to Seoul gathered at the "Marubiru" (마루삘) in Myŏngch'ijŏng, Seoul.

◎ Let's nurture and support Hwasin as our complete joint purchasing organization.
◎ Let's recognize that the responsibility of the initial (first-term) chain store owners is increasingly heavy and great.
◎ Let's not leave bad practices for the second and third-term applicant chain stores.
◎ Lack of control is tying one's own hands. It is self-destruction.

Under these slogans, first, a resolution was made to rationalize Hwasin as a joint business entity. Next, noting that the rise and fall of Hwasin does not end with just the rise and fall of Hwasin alone but now actually has great economic repercussions throughout all of Korea, a resolution regarding self-awareness was made to fulfill our duties with this point in mind. Following this, based on the experiences of the already recruited first-term applicant chain stores, various resolutions were made, such as not leaving bad practices when recruiting the second and third terms in the future, and stating that "Lack of control is tying one's own hands," urging work under unified control without disorder. <126>

But how did this Chain Store Owners' Association initially come about?
1. Lack of uniformity in fixed-price sales (定價販賣)
2. Disorder in purchasing (仕入)
3. Lack of understanding of "Hwasin"
4. Lack of sense of duty
5. Loss of external trust in the chain network arising from lack of control

As existing local chain stores keenly felt these five harms, they realized they could conduct business safely only by eliminating these harms. Thus, initially, Mr. Sŏ Hyŏnjong (徐鷴鍾), the owner of the Hamnam Hongwŏn chain store within the Wŏnsan Distribution Center's jurisdiction, and Mr. Kim Chŏngbong (金珽鳳), the owner of the Chuil chain store, took the initiative and sent out an appeal (檄) to several dozen locations within the Wŏnsan distribution center's jurisdiction. Namely:

A Letter Regarding the Founding Principles of the Association of Chain Store Owners within the Wŏnsan Distribution Center Jurisdiction

Respectfully addressed,
Amidst the current peak of summer heat, we pray for your well-being and prosperity in business.
Regarding recent matters, glancing at the situation of chain stores in various places lately, it seems inconsistencies in management policies are arising due to various causes. The initial conviction is gradually scattering, losing pace and displaying various forms. Some even treat "trust" (信) with "doubt" (疑), forgetting the long-term grand plan and seeming constrained by temporary conveniences. Although there may be many reasons for this, we believe the main cause lies in the "long-standing business customs" (積年의 商習). Traditionally, our suppliers (仕入先) inevitably had interests contrary to ours, leading to the suspicion: Isn't Hwasin, our supplier, also the same? We believe this doubt gives rise to all sorts of harmful effects.
Moreover, at this time, the very beginning of the business and the first such plan in the East, while such twists and turns might be considered a natural process, do we not know that the causes of failure and setbacks in major affairs often start from small causes?
For our long-term great benefit over a hundred years, we must devise, with our own hands, a plan to eliminate all the harms that have arisen during the short period of a few months and firmly promise the future. While Hwasin and the chain stores are fundamentally one body and mind, since the Hwasin head office is not a chain store, and the chain stores are likewise not the Hwasin head office, their standpoints naturally differ slightly. Consequently, a corresponding distance arises, perhaps preventing the full execution of "all commands" and "all guidance." We believe it is likely true that if chain stores consult, decide, and encourage each other, even on challenging issues, the effectiveness will surely be multiplied.
From this, we feel the need for an organization where chain store owners, who are in the same position in name, form, and substance, can consult each other without reservation.
The reason we propose the establishment of the Wŏnsan Distribution Center Affiliated Store Owners' Association first, as a matter of sequence, with the goal of forming an All-Korea Chain Store Owners' Organization, lies herein.
The fourth day of the third sample fair, the 28th, is the admission day for chain store owners within the main distribution center's jurisdiction. Therefore, it would be most convenient to gather together on the following day, day or night, for concrete discussions. <127>
We hereby notify you in advance with these few words, seeking your endorsement.
July 20, Shōwa 10 (1935)
Chuil Chain Store Owner Kim Chŏngbong (金珽鳳)
Hongwŏn Chain Store Owner Sŏ Hyŏnjong (徐鷴鍾) 白

After the organization was completed, there was a banquet hosted by the President of Hwasin Headquarters, Mr. Pak Hŭngsik (朴興植), and Division Head Chu (朱課長) at the Kyŏngsŏng Hotel. In any case, the future direction of this All-Korea Store Owners' Association, seeking to protect business rights, draws considerable attention. <128>

**Summary**

This 1935 article reports on the formation of the Hwasin Chain Store Owners' Association in Seoul. Driven by issues like inconsistent pricing, purchasing problems, lack of understanding of the Hwasin model, poor sense of duty among owners, and resulting loss of public trust, store owners, initially led by those from the Wŏnsan region, organized to protect their collective interests and work more effectively with the Hwasin head office. The article details the founding meeting, its resolutions, the rationale outlined in an initial appeal letter, and the elected leadership structure, concluding that the association's future actions will be closely watched. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it showcases Korean capitalist enterprise (Hwasin Department Store and its chain) attempting modern business organization techniques during the colonial period. It highlights the challenges faced by early Korean chain stores, the tension between centralized control (Hwasin HQ) and franchisee autonomy, and the emergence of collective action among Korean merchants to navigate the complexities of the colonial economy and modernize their practices, reflecting a degree of indigenous economic development and organizational effort despite the overarching colonial structure.

全鮮 店主團結한 和信連鎖店主會
지난 7월에 서울에 모힌 和信連鎖店主 170여 氏는 상업상으로 자긔네의 리익을 옹호할 목적으로 連鎖店主會를 창입하엿다.
和信연쇄점은 세상사람의 주목하는 속에서 이미 第2年을 마지하여 이번 명치정 연쇄점課 樓上에서 열닌 見本市에는 약 70만원어치의 계약고를 보앗다 하며 이 뒤로 화신본점의 방침이 금년 가을 안에 약 200점의 연쇄점을 더 증설 하리라 한즉 금후는 3,400점을 상대로 장사할 터임으로 취인상 계약고 등이 더욱 격증될 것이고 또한 연쇄점주들 수효도 더욱 증가될 것이다.
이러한 장래일까지 관측할 때에 이번에 전조선의 연쇄점주를 망라하야 본점과는 서로 련낙된 관계에서 창립된 이번 연쇄점주회는 비상히 이미 깁다 할 것이다. 이제 창립 전후 모양을 상보하건대 7월 27일 오후 4시 반부터 서울 명치정에 잇는 「마루삘」에 서울에 올너온 각지 화신점주 수십명이 모히여
◎ 和信을 우리의 完全한 共同仕入機關으로 育成支持하자.
◎ 初創期(第一期)連鎖店主의 責任이 一層重且大함을 認識하자.
◎ 第二期 第三期應幕連鎖店에 惡風을 남기지 말자.
◎ 不統制는 自繩自縛이다. 自滅이다.
