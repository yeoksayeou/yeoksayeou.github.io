A Story About Houses
						Yi T'aejun

These days in Sŏngbukdong and Hyehwadong, all they do is build houses. Even last autumn, from Posŏng Higher Common School to the bus terminus, there were hardly any houses besides Hyehwa Ordinary School. I used to walk seeing the deep green of cabbage patches, but this autumn, Western-style houses and Korean houses are mixed haphazardly, forming streets with almost no empty space. In Sŏngbukdong too, wherever the terrain is even, there isn't a bit of vacant land. So these days, if you step out of the house just a little, you can't help but see them, and the newly built houses constantly catch your eye.

Because they constantly catch my eye like this, even though I know nothing, I've sometimes had several impressions regarding architecture.

Generally, looking at how Koreans build houses, they lack too much of a tasteful life. They build bright red brick walls, which absolutely do not match a Korean tile-roofed house, piling them up past the eaves. And they seem to enjoy shutting themselves inside that prison.

Also, they apply an oily finish to perfectly good timber, making it look shiny and sticky like a sweaty face. Shall we say it's like putting on the toe-covers of socks even before they wear out? I don't know what kind of epidemic makes people like spending money on that sticky-looking, shiny-looking oil finish. It's like children getting hold of 'enogu' (paint) and carelessly painting it on appropriate and inappropriate places. That kind of oil, needlessly imported at a low price, is ruining all Korean buildings.<263>
They say it was like that in Tokyo, Kyoto, and other places during the Meiji era. When 'penki' (paint) was first imported, there was a trend of indiscriminately applying it everywhere on Japanese domestic buildings, attracted only by the fact that it prevented rotting and could produce color, even though it didn't suit them. It's said that foreigners came and questioned why Japan was conquering its proud, unique architectural culture with 'penki' like that, which prompted efforts among intellectuals to quickly curb that 'penki' trend. It truly is time for our Korean architecture to reflect on this trend of brick walls, oil finishes, and paint jobs. Glass windows are convenient, but if one is spending a large sum of money to build, is there really a need to be so enthusiastic about glass windows to the point of killing the character of a Korean building? A building is an entity that expresses the culture, taste, and entire personality of the person living within it. That's why John Ruskin said, "Good architecture is not the result of the contractor's skill, but of the character of the owner of the building."

Last spring, I had the chance to view the former villa of the Taewŏn'gun outside Ch'angŭimun Gate. Originally built by a certain Prime Minister Kim of that time, it later came into the possession of the Taewŏn'gun. The first strange thing was why a house built with such fine timber, without skimping like that, was built with such narrow partitions, like the houses of today's house merchants. Upon inquiry, the reason was not that people back then were shorter, nor that they skimped on timber, but simply stemmed from the owner's humility, thinking 'this much is sufficient.' It was the modesty of the owner, who, even when building a villa, would not indulge in a high and spacious mansion.

In this way, Korean buildings have long had a deep connection with personality. But today, let alone the level of personality, how much has even taste degenerated? Piling up those towering red walls, and still not trusting, as if thinking the whole world is thieves, they break glass bottles and embed them – one truly cannot help but feel sorry for the owner of such a house.
They talk of distressing measures (苦憫策), distressing measures, but could there be such a distressing measure, such a distressing life (苦憫生活)!
Even if it's someone else's house, if you see a house built with refined taste, you feel affection for the owner and want to seek them out.
If only there were some such beautiful houses on the streets I always walk, how much lighter my steps would be.<264>

**Summary**

Yi T'aejun laments the decline of aesthetic taste and traditional values in contemporary Korean house construction in areas like Sŏngbukdong and Hyehwadong. He criticizes the adoption of jarring elements like bright red brick walls, shiny oil finishes, paint ('enogu', 'penki'), and excessive glass windows, arguing they clash with traditional Korean architecture and reflect a lack of taste and character. He contrasts this with the past, citing the Taewŏn'gun's former villa as an example where architectural choices reflected the owner's humility and personality. He deplores the prison-like walls topped with broken glass, seeing them as symbols of a fearful and aesthetically impoverished modern life.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects a Korean intellectual's critique of modernization and Western/Japanese influence during the colonial period. Yi T'aejun frames the architectural changes not just aesthetically, but morally, linking modern styles to a loss of traditional virtues like humility ('겸손') and personality ('인격'). His specific mention of Japanese trends (Meiji era 'penki' use) and imported materials ('에노구', '뼁기') highlights the complex cultural adaptations and anxieties of the time. Historians might find this piece valuable for understanding colonial-era perspectives on cultural identity, modernization, and the built environment.

집이야기
						李泰俊
요즘 城北洞과 惠化洞엔 짓느니 집이다. 작년 가을만해도 普成高普에서부터 빠쓰종점까지 惠化普通學校外에는 별로 집이 업섯다. 짐장 배추밧이 시퍼런 것을 보고 다녓는데 올가을엔 洋館, 조선집들이 제멋대로 석기여 거이 空地업는 거리를 이루엇다. 城北洞도 지형의 고르기 만한데는 공터라고는 조곰도 업다. 그래서 요즘은 조곰만 집을 나서도 안보려야 안볼 수 업고 새로 짓는 집들이 작고 눈에 띄이는 것이다.
그러케 작고 눈에 띄이는 것이니 아모것도 몰으면서도 멧가지 건축에 관한 감상을 가저보군 하엿다.

대체로 조선사람들은 집 짓는 것을 보아도 취미생활이 너머 업다. 조선 개와집엔 결코  어울리지 안는 싯뻘건 벽돌담을 쌋퇴 추녀끗 올려쌋는다. 그리고 스사로 그 감옥 속에 드러안기를 즐긴다.
또 멀정한 材木에 땀흐른 얼굴처럼 번질번질하고 끈적끈적해 보히는 기름칠들을 한다. 해여지기도 전에 보선볼부터 미리 바더 신는 격이라 할가. 그 끈적끈적해 뵈고 버질번질해 뵈는 기름칠을 돈드려가며 조와하는 것은 무슨 流行病인지 모르겟다. 아이들에게 「에노구」가 생기면 된데 안된데 함부로 칠해논는 것이다. 그런 기름이 공연히 安價로 수입되여가지고 조선건물을 모다 망처논는 것이다.<263>
明治 때에 東京, 京都 등지에서 그랫다한다. 「뼁키」를 처음 수입해 가지고 일본 內地建物에 당치 안은 것을 썩지 안는다는 점과 色彩를 내일 수가 잇다는 점에만 끄을리여 된데 안된데 막 칠하는 것이 유행이 되엿다. 그것을 외국인이 와보고 日本의 자랑인 일본 고유한 건축문화를 왜 저러케 「뼁기」로 征服해 버리느냐고 의문을 일으키여 識者間에서 곳 그 「뼁기」思潮를 막기에 노력하엿다는 말이 잇거니와 우리 조선건축들이야말로 벽돌담, 기름칠, 뼁기칠 思潮에서 좀 반성해야 될 시기다. 유리창도 편리하기는 하지만 큰돈을 드려 지을 바에는 조선건물로서의 면목을 죽여가면서까지 유리창에 열광할 필요는 업지 안을가. 건물은 그 속에 사는 사람의 교양, 취미, 모-든 人格을 다 표현하는 존재인 것이다. 그리기에 쫀, 러스킨은 「훌융한 건축은 請負業者의 기술로 되는 것이 아니라 그 建築物 주인의 인격으로 되는 것이라」하엿다.

지난 봄에 彰義門 밧에 잇는 전大院君의 별장을 구경한 일이 잇다. 워낙은 金某라는 當時宰相이 지은 것인데 뒤에 大院君이 가진 것이라는데 첫재 이상한 것은 그러케 조흔 材木으로 그것케 앳기는 것이 업시 짓는 집을 왜 요즘 집장수들의 집처럼 간사를 좁게 지엇나하는 것이엿다. 알고보니 그 까닭은 그 때 사람들이라고 키가 더 작엇든 것도 아니요, 材木을 애겻슴도 아니요 다만 주인의 이만하면 족하다는 謙讓에서 나온 것이엿다. 별장을 지을망정 高臺廣室에 거드러거리지는 안켓다는 그 주인의 겸손이엿다.
이러케 朝鮮建物은 옛날부러 인격과의 交涉이 깁헛든 것이다. 그러나 오늘엔 인격의 정도는 커넝 취미로 보아도 얼마나 타락하엿는가. 그 길길이 뻘건담을 쌋코 그래도 못미더워서 세상을 다 도적으로 아는지 유리병을 깨트려 박은 것이라 참말 그 집 주인을 위해 딱해하지 안을 수 업는 것이다.
苦憫策苦憫策하니 그런 苦憫策, 苦憫生活이 잇는가!
남우 집이라도 노푼 취미로 지은 집을 보면 그 집 주인을 차저보고 십게 정이 드는 것이다.
늘 지나다니는 거리에 그런 아름다운 집들이 좀 잇섯스면 얼마나 거름이 가뜬 가뜬 할가.<264>
