Is Japan the Power of the East?
						By Tagore of India

At a time when we in Asia were under self-hypnosis, believing there was no way to break through the current situation, Japan awoke from that dream, cast off the inactivity of several centuries, and took giant steps to reach the highest indicators of the modern era. This event awakened the nations living within whatever geographical boundaries we may have from their deluded dreams.

We had forgotten that in Asia, several great kingdoms were once built, where philosophy, science, art, and literature splendidly developed, and all the world's great religions had their cradle there. Consequently, the climate of Asia particularly showed mental inactivity and caused people's progressive abilities to wither. The fact that we in the East held the torch of culture during the past centuries when the West slept in darkness sweeps away the evidence that we were lazy.

Then, the darkness of night covered the entire East. As if the flow of time had suddenly stopped, Asia ceased thinking of taking in any new nourishment and tried to live only in its own past. To live only in the past actually means living by consuming one's own body as sustenance. That stillness was like death, and thus Asia's great voice fell silent.

At such a time, when Japan one morning cast off the barriers of old customs overnight and emerged full of spirit, the whole world watched with eyes of astonishment. This was truly accomplished in such an incredibly short period that it wasn't like slowly changing into new clothes or gradually building a new house. Japan showed the power of confident maturity, and at the same time, showed the freshness and infinite potential of new life. Consequently, it raised anxious thoughts: Was this merely a simple prank of history, a trick of time, like a soap bubble, perfect in color and shape but empty inside?

However, Japan proved that this sudden proclamation of its power was not a short-lived wonder, nor was it an accidental product pulled up from a chaotic abyss only to be swept away into the sea of oblivion the next moment.

Indeed, Japan is a country both old and new at the same time. Japan received the heritage of ancient culture from the East – enabling it to regain its truth, wealth, and strength.

In a word, modern Japan emerged from the eternally imperishable East, like a lotus flower elegantly and beautifully blooming while firmly rooted in the deep pond from which it came.

Thus, Japan, a descendant of the ancient East, also bravely demanded all the gifts of the modern era for its own sake. Japan showed its daring spirit by smashing the constraints of habit – the lazy mind that seeks safety through diligence, locks, and keys. In this way, Japan came into contact with the living age and undertook the responsibilities of modern civilization with astonishing ****** talent.

If it were merely a reproduction of the West, one could not have great expectations. This is because there are several significant problems that Western civilization has presented to the world but has not yet completely solved. The struggles between individual and state, labor and capital, man and woman; the conflict between material desire and human spiritual life; the conflict between the organized selfishness of nations and the high ideals of humanity; the ugly complexities inseparable from the huge organizations of commerce and the state – all these must be harmonized in ways previously unimaginable.

I cannot help but think of the old days when the entirety of East Asia, from Burma (緬甸) to Japan, and India were bound by deep friendship between nations. Our relations were not selfish, and there was no need for arms.

The relationship between the two was not one of selfishness, nor was it the kind of plunder where they rummaged through each other's pockets. Ideals were exchanged, and the highest love was given and received. Differences in language and customs did not hinder the closeness of hearts.<86>
National pride and physical or spiritual consciousness did not harm our relationship. Our respective literatures and arts were well combined, like flowers blooming under the sunlight. Thus, various peoples with different lands, languages, and histories were united in humanity's most sublime love. Do we not see in these times, filled with peace and goodwill, that the essence of the Japanese people possessed an immortal fragrance for its own sake? Do we not see that because of that fragrance, new life is attached to their bodies so they may live long?

We Indians are now troubled by our own problems. They are the problems of the world in miniature. India, unfortunately, has a vast territory, and the races living there are also diverse. India is several countries crammed into a single geographical container. It is the exact opposite of Europe (歐羅巴), which is one [civilization] divided into several countries. Thus, Europe has utilized many strengths simultaneously while developing its culture as a single force. In contrast, India has suffered due to its [need for] unity.

However, the political civilization growing in the soil of Europe, spreading across the world like proliferating weeds, has exclusion as its foundation. This political civilization not only constantly tries not to get close to foreigners but seems rather intent on their extermination. This advisor [political civilization] is greedy and cruel, living off the resources of other peoples, and trying to swallow their entire future. Political civilization always fears the success and fame of other nations and sees it as dangerous. Thus, it attempts to obstruct the budding of anything great outside its own domain. In any case, among the workers who broke through all these barriers and emerged onto the world stage, Japan was the first nation to appear from the East. It injected hope into the heart of all Asia. Asia now keenly feels that the day has come to prove its own life by producing living works, and that it must never passively hibernate or fall into fear or flattery, merely imitating the West. For this reason, we thank Japan and hope that it will not forget the mission of the East that it must fulfill. Japan must inject the sap of humanity back into the center of modern civilization. Japan must not be suffocated, pressed down by poisonous weeds. At this time when the Eastern world is in disarray, I cannot help but think once again of the relationship between India and Japan.<87>

**Summary**

Tagore expresses admiration for Japan's rapid modernization, viewing it as an awakening for Asia from centuries of stagnation and a source of hope. However, he strongly cautions Japan against simply mimicking the West, particularly its materialism, aggressive nationalism, and inherent tendency towards conflict and exclusion ("political civilization"). He urges Japan to retain its unique Eastern spirit, drawing strength from its ancient cultural roots while engaging with modernity. Recalling a past era of peaceful, mutually enriching cultural exchange between India, Japan, and other Asian nations, Tagore hopes Japan will fulfill a higher, humane mission for the East by harmonizing modern advancements with spiritual values, rather than succumbing to the "poisonous weeds" of Western imperialism and selfishness.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is significant. Published in a Korean magazine in 1935, under Japanese rule, Tagore's words offer a complex perspective. While praising Japan's rise, his critique of Western (and implicitly, potentially Japanese) imperialism, materialism, and nationalism, coupled with his call for an Eastern spiritual path, could subtly resonate with Korean intellectuals seeking alternatives to both Western dominance and Japanese colonial assimilation. Historians might find it interesting how this nuanced view from a respected non-Western figure was presented to a Korean audience, potentially offering a framework for critiquing power and envisioning a distinct Asian modernity, even within the constraints of colonial censorship.

그런데 歐羅巴의 흙에서 자라서 마치 繁茂하는 잡초와 가치 全世界에 버더저 나가는 政治的 文明은 排他를 그 基幹으로 하고 잇다. 이 政治的 文明은 恒常 外國人을 갓가히 하려하지 안을 뿐더러 도로혀 그의 絶滅되기에 注意를 붓는 것과 갓다. 이 顧問은 貧慾殘忍하여 他의 人民의 資源에 의하여 생활하고 그네들은 全將來를 삼키어 버리려한 政治的 文明은 항상 他民族이 成功하여 有名하게 되는 것을 두려워하고 그것을 危險하게 본다. 그리하야 自己의 領域이외에 잇는 偉大한 모든 것의 萌芽를 妨害하려고 試하려 한다. 이것은 참지 못할 일이다.(中略-원문)
엇잿든 이 모든 障壁을 깨트리고 世界에 뛰어나온 일꾼 중에 日本은 東洋에서 가장 몬저 나타난 國家다. 그는 全亞細亞의 마음에 希望을 注入식혓다. 亞細亞는 지금 生命 잇는 事業을 産出함으로써 自己의 生命을 實證할 날이 온 것으로 결코 受動的으로 蟄伏하거나 恐怖나 阿諛에 빠지어 한갓 西洋을 模倣하여서는 안될 것을 痛感한다. 이 때문에 우리는 이 日本에 감사드리며 日本이 다할 동양의 사명을 잇지말기를 희망다. 日本은 現代文明의 中心에 다시 人道의 汁液을 注入하지 안으면 안된다. 日本은 有毒한 잡초에 눌니워 窒息하여서는 안된다. 東洋天地가 어즈러워진 이때 다시금 印度와 日本의 關係를 생각하지 안을 수 업다.<87>
