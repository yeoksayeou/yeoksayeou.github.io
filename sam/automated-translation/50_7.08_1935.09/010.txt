上海事變에 中國軍人 六千戰死 (6,000 Chinese Soldiers Killed in the Shanghai Incident)

The damage from the Shanghai Incident, which began on January 29th, Shōwa 7 (1932), is greater than we imagine.
According to a Chinese announcement on March 14th of the same year, the number of Chinese military casualties from January 29th to March 3rd was 5,000.
At that time, according to the Shanghai Evening Post, the Chinese forces engaged in the battle were the 60th, 61st, and 78th Divisions of the 19th Route Army, and the 87th and 88th Divisions of the 5th Route Army, totaling five divisions.
The first three divisions were Guangdong Army (廣東軍),
The latter two divisions were Jiangsu (江蘇) and Zhejiang (淅江) armies. Among them,
The 88th Division suffered the most damage,
with one-third of its total personnel, or 3,334 casualties.
The 87th Division had 2,000 deaths,
And the three divisions of the 19th Route Army had 3,700 deaths.
Meanwhile, the estimated damages suffered by the Chinese populace
are inconsistent: 1.5 billion won (announced by the National Government Statistics Bureau on March 20th),
or 600 million won (United Press dispatch from Shanghai on March 20th),
or 66 million dollars (report received by the US Department of Commerce on March 11th).
However, according to the Shanghai Social Affairs Bureau announcement on March 16th,
the number of damaged Chinese houses was 160,000,<18>
deaths were 6,800,
injured were 2,000,
and missing were 10,400.

The losses of the Japanese army, according to the Army Ministry's announcement on March 15th of the same year, were
243 killed in action,
1,515 wounded in action.
Navy losses (as of March 6th of the same year) were
145 killed in action,
460 wounded or sick.
Meanwhile, government expenditures since the occurrence of the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents
amounted to approximately 150 million won.
The decline in trade with China
is said to have exceeded approximately 100 million won, and furthermore, the devastating blow to small and medium-sized commerce and industry among Japanese people within Japan is said to have been immense.

「中央」雜誌 休刊 事情 (Circumstances of "Chungang" Magazine's Suspension)

"Chungang" (中央), the house magazine of the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報), had been losing four to five hundred won each month. Due to a change in the company's policy, it has been decided to suspend publication starting this autumn, in September, leaving behind a shining history of over a year. The suspension of this magazine, which was very active in our desolate media world, is deeply regretted by many. Not only "Chungang" magazine, but in the past, there were few organizations involved in cultural enterprises, especially monthly magazines, that did not suffer financial losses. An easy example is "Tonggwang" (東光); according to Chu Yohan (朱耀翰), who was the chief editor, it lost about 3,000 won in just over a year. Going back further, Ch'oe Yuktang (崔六堂) is said to have sacrificed over 10,000 won for the "Ch'ŏngch'un" (靑春) magazine and Kwangmunhoe (光文會) publications. Do the numerous readers of the world even recall that a single magazine, a single newspaper, is produced through the sacrifice of such precious money and the tears of its managers? The hardship and sacrifice of cultural institution managers are truly known only by those who know.

동아일보의 증축기공 (Groundbreaking for Tong-A Ilbo's Expansion)

The Tong-A Ilbo, as planned, is set to break ground for a major expansion doubling the size of its building. They are currently busy drawing up the blueprints. The total construction cost is over 100,000 won, with completion scheduled for next spring in March. The expansion involves constructing a new building about one and a half times the size of the current office building to its south. Then, after combining the new and old buildings, another floor will be added on top, turning the current 3-story building into a 4-story one. This construction cost will reportedly be covered by savings and other means, without issuing additional stock. Thus, next spring, within a distance of 2 *chō* (町), the two great buildings, the 4-story Tong-A and the 5-story Chosŏn, will stand facing each other, boasting of their premises.

**Summary**

This "Black Chamber" news section from the September 1935 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine covers a wide range of topics. It details casualties and costs of the 1932 Shanghai Incident for both China and Japan, notes the impending release of nationalist figure An Tosal, lists prominent members of the pro-Japanese Simuhoe group (including many Ch'ŏndogyo figures), compares newspaper circulations in Korea, Japan, and the US, provides statistics on religious adherents in Korea (showing Ch'ŏndogyo's decline and Christianity's rise), discusses the financial struggles of Korean magazines like *Chungang* and *Tonggwang*, reports on the Tong-A Ilbo's building expansion, updates on personnel movements (Yi Kwangsu, Yang Kŭnhwan, etc.), lists faculty at Yonhŭi Chŏnmun School, highlights business news like Hwasin Department Store's success and expansion plans, mentions economist Paek Namun's past connection to Kaizō-sha president Yamamoto Sanehiko, contrasts Korean actor pay with high Hollywood salaries, relays rumors of Japanese tycoon Kobayashi Ichizō planning a large entertainment complex in Seoul, presents stark statistics on low Korean homeownership in Seoul, announces the founding of a Korean Chamber of Commerce in Tokyo, and compares high land prices in Tokyo with those in Seoul.

From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable snapshots of colonial Korean society in 1935. The listing of Simuhoe members reveals collaboration patterns among Korean elites, particularly the significant involvement of Ch'ŏndogyo figures. The discussion of magazine closures underscores the economic fragility of Korean cultural enterprises under colonial rule. The comparison of newspaper circulation and land prices highlights Korea's economic position relative to Japan and the US, while the low homeownership rate in Seoul points to the economic marginalization of Koreans in their own capital. The rumors surrounding Kobayashi Ichizō's potential venture reflect increasing Japanese capital penetration into Korean leisure industries.

上海事變에 中國軍人 六千戰死

昭和 7년 1월 29일에 戰端을 開始한 상해사변의 손해는 우리의 상상이상으로 크다.
동년 3월 14일 중국측의 발표에 의하면 同1月 29일 이래 3월 3일까지의 중국군이 입은 死傷者의 수는 5,000인이라 한다.
그때 상해 이브닝 포스트지에 의하면 전투에 종사한 중국군은 第19路군의 第60師, 61師, 78師, 第5路軍의 第87師, 88師
이상의 5個師團이엇다고 한다.
前 三師는 廣東軍,
後 二師는 江蘇, 淅江軍인데 그 중에서도,
第88師가 입은 손해가 가장 많엇스니
전원의 삼분지일, 즉 3,334명의 사상자를 내엿고,
第87師는 2,000명의 死者,

中央日報의 機關雜誌 「中央」은 그동안 매월 사오백원의 缺損을 보아 왓다는데 同社 方針의 變更 때문에 1년 有餘의 빗나는 역사를 남기고 今秋 9월부터 休刊하기로 되엇다. 寂寞한 우리 言論界에 잇서 만흔 활약을 하든 同雜誌의 休刊은 다수인사가 모다 哀惜하게 아는 바어니와 비단 「中央」잡지 뿐 아니라 과거에 잇서 문화사업 그 중에도 월간 잡지을 경영하든 분으로 경제적으로 손실을 아니본 機關이 드믈다. 손쉬운 例로 「東光」은 同誌 主간斡이든 朱耀翰氏의 말을 드러도 1년 有餘의 사이에 약 3천원의 缺損을 보앗다하며, 좀 더 올너가 崔六堂은「靑春」잡지와 「光文會*書發行」으로 약 만 餘원을 희생하엿다 전한다. 世의 多*한 讀者는 一冊의 雜誌, 一枚의 新聞이 이러케 앗가운 돈의 희생과 경영자의 눈물 속에서 되어 나가는 것을 想起나하여 주는지요, 文化機關經營者의 苦心과 犧牲은 실로 아는 사람이나 알진저.

서울 땅갑과 東京 땅갑

요지간은 여름철이 되야 모든 것이 閑散期인 까닭에 土地賣買도 만치 못하지만 그러나 금년 봄까지도 서울 종로 네거리 가튼 가장 繁華한 곳에 잇는 垈地도 만히 팔렷다. 그 때 시세는 最上이 매평 600원 내지 750원 정도 엇는데 이제 東京의 지가는 얼마나 되는가함을 조사하여 보건대 「丸ノ內」의 東京驛前이 매평 1,675원 그 다음이 日本橋通으로 1,321원, 3等의 新宿驛前 1,150원 이라 하며 各處의 平均地價는 室町이 888원, 京橋가 876원, 新宿이 862원, 丸ノ內에서 日比谷附近이 853원, 學生들이 만히 잇는 神田區 뒷골목도 매평 300원이라 하며 더구나 말하든 까리에 한가지 더 부처둘 것은 東京은 3층 이상의 洋屋數가 2,211棟이라 한다. <24>
