Chapter 2: Medicaments for Digestive System Diseases

Section 2: Stomachics (Digestive Aids)

These agents are medicines intended to promote or regulate gastric function when there are so-called gastric symptoms such as loss of appetite, pressure/heaviness in the stomach area, acid reflux (heartburn), noisy stomach (rumbling), nausea, fatigue, etc., by inducing changes in the stomach's functions (digestion, secretion, motility, absorption). The types are as follows:
(1) Bitter Agents: Most are plant-based medicines that promote gastric digestion or secretion/motility to increase appetite. Gentiana preparations, Gentian root (龍膽), Senburi Bitter Tincture (センブリ苦味丁幾), Colombo preparations, Condurango preparations, Quinine (キニネ), Nux Vomica preparations (ホミカ劑), Cinchona bark preparations (樛皮劑), Creosote preparations (クレオソ-ト劑), Rhubarb preparations (大董劑), etc., belong to this category.
(2) Pungent Agents: These agents stimulate the mucous membranes of the nose, oral cavity, and gastrointestinal tract, reflexively promoting digestive fluid secretion while also changing the permeability of the stomach wall cells. Pepper (胡椒), Mustard (芥子), Mint (薄荷), Ginger (生薑), Nutmeg (肉豆蒄), Vanillin/Vanilla (ワニルク), Clove (丁香), Anise/Carum (アニスカ-ルム), Wasabi (山葵), etc., belong to this category.
(3) Acid Agents: These agents promote secretion (hormone action) to increase the secretion of digestive enzymes in the duodenum. Dilute Hydrochloric Acid (稀鹽酸), Phosphoric Acid (燐酸), Citric Acid (拘櫞酸), Tartaric Acid (洒石酸), Acetic Acid (醋酸), Nitric Acid (硝酸), Malic Acid (林檎酸), etc., belong to this category. <219>
(4) Antacids: For hyperacidity, Atropine preparations (アトロビン劑) that reduce gastric acid secretion, and Alkali salts (アルカリ鹽類), Bismuth preparations (蒼鉛劑), Aluminum preparations (アルミュウム劑), etc., that neutralize excessive stomach acid belong to this category.
(5) Digestive Enzymes: These agents supplement deficiencies in digestive enzymes. Diastase (ヂアスタ-ゼ), Pepsin (べブシンパンク), Sort?, etc., belong to this category.
(6) Anti-fermentative Agents: These agents are used when abnormal fermentation occurs in the stomach. Resorcinol (レゾルモン), Creosote (クレオソ-ト), Salol (ザロ-ル), Mint (薄荷), etc., are examples.

**Summary**

This article, part of a series on health in the magazine Samch'ŏlli, is authored by Dr. Pak Ch'anghun, a prominent medical figure in colonial Korea. It focuses on "Stomachics" (健胃劑), medicines for digestive ailments. The text first categorizes these remedies into six types: bitter agents, pungent agents, acid agents, antacids, digestive enzymes, and anti-fermentatives, listing examples (often Japanese pharmaceutical names or ingredients) for each. It then provides 25 detailed prescription examples, specifying ingredients (including botanical extracts, chemicals like sodium bicarbonate and bismuth salts, enzymes like diastase and pepsin, and brand-name products like Biofermin), dosages, preparation methods (tinctures, powders, decoctions, syrups mixed with distilled water), administration instructions (e.g., before/after meals), and target conditions (e.g., indigestion, gastric catarrh, loss of appetite, hyperacidity, gastric/duodenal ulcers, abnormal fermentation).

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it showcases the practical application of Western and Japanese pharmacology in Korea during the 1930s, disseminated through a popular magazine. It reflects the blending of traditional botanical ingredients (like gentian, rhubarb, cinnamon) with modern chemical compounds and manufactured pharmaceuticals (including Japanese brand names). The author's credentials and the detailed prescriptions suggest an effort to educate the public or possibly provide guidance for practitioners beyond basic folk remedies, indicating the increasing influence and availability of modern medical treatments, albeit filtered through the colonial context and Japanese influence (evident in terminology and product names). The inclusion of opium derivatives (Pantopon/Narcopon) and potentially hazardous substances like Creosote and Resorcinol in popular prescriptions is also noteworthy regarding historical medical practices.

第二章 消化器病에 對한 藥劑

第二節 健胃劑

本劑는 胃의 기능(消化, 合泌, 運動, 吸收)에 변화를 야기하야 식욕부진, 胃部壓重, 呑酸, 嘈囃, 惡心, 倦怠 등의 所爲 胃症狀이 有한 時에 胃機能의 亢進 又는 調整을 圖謀하는 藥劑니 種類는 如左하다.
(一) 苦味劑의 대부분이 植物性藥劑니 胃의 消化 又는 分泌運動을 促進하야 食慾을 增進식히는 것이니 げンチアナ劑, 龍膽, センブリ苦味丁幾, コロンボ劑, コンヂュランコ劑, キニネ, ホミカ劑, 樛皮劑, クレオソ-ト劑, 大董劑, 등이 此에 屬함.
(二) 苛味劑, 鼻, 口腔, 胃腸粘膜을 剌戟하야 反射的으로 消化液分泌을 亢進하는 동시에 胃壁細脂의 滲透性을 變化식히는 藥劑니 胡椒, 芥子, 薄荷, 生薑, 肉豆蒄, ワニルク, 丁香, アニスカ-ルム, 山葵, 등이 此에 속함.
(三) 酸劑分泌을 亢進하야 (홀몬) 作用으로 十二指腸의 消化酵素分泌를 增進식히는 藥劑니 稀鹽酸, 燐酸, 拘櫞酸, 洒石酸, 醋酸, 硝酸, 林檎酸, 등이 此에 屬함. <219>
(四) 制酸劑 胃酸過多에 대하야 胃酸分泌를 減少 식히는 アトロビン劑의 過多한 胃酸을 中和식히는 アルカリ鹽類, 蒼鉛劑, アルミュウム劑, 등이 此에 屬한다.
(五) 消化酵素, 消化酵素의 不足을 補充하는 藥劑니 ヂアスタ-ゼ. べブシンパンク, ソ-ト, 등이 이에 속한다.
(六)制酵劑 胃內에 異常酵素가 出現하는 時에 使用하는 藥劑니 レゾルモン, クレオソ-ト, ザロ-ル, 薄荷等이다.
