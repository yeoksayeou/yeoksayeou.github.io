Heard Tidings/Anecdotes

Ms. Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng (丁七星) and the Kayagŭm

Could it be 7 or 8 years ago already? Ewha Womans Professional School's (梨花女子專門學校) Music Department earnestly requested (懇請) Ms. Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng to come and teach the kayagŭm as a professor. However, at that time, Ms. Chŏng disliked touching the kayagŭm strings, having just plunged into social activism, so she refused (拒絶).

Ms. Chang Tŏkcho (張德祚) and "Teacher"

The female writer (女流作家) Ms. Chang Tŏkcho always calls her husband (夫君) "Sŏnsaengnim, Sŏnsaengnim!" (先生님, 先生님!) regardless of whether guests are present. While there are various terms for a husband, this is one of the new examples.

Ms. Ch'oe Chŏngwŏn's (崔貞嫄) Devotion

Alongside Mr. Chang Hyŏkchu's (張赫宙) letter of recommendation (推薦辭), the emerging writer Ms. Ch'oe Chŏngwŏn, who is gaining literary fame by publishing the medium-length story "Naktong River" (洛東江), followed her husband Yi Kapki (李甲基) to Chŏnju (全州) when he was incarcerated in Chŏnju Prison (全州刑務所), and is devotedly bringing in privately prepared meals (差入) morning and evening.

Ms. U Pongun's (禹鳳雲) Subsequent Path

Ms. U Pongun, who used to work as a newspaper subscription solicitor (新聞勸誘員) for the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), recently left the company and took up residence at the Women's Zen Academy (女子禪學院) in Ankuk-tong (安國洞). Because of this, rumors that the lady might become a Buddhist nun (爲僧設) circulated for a while, but whether this is true or not is still unknown. <77>

**Summary**

This article presents four short news items about prominent Korean women in 1935. It notes Chŏng Ch'ilsŏng's past refusal of a kayagŭm professorship at Ewha due to her focus on social activism, Chang Tŏkcho's modern habit of calling her husband "Sŏnsaengnim," Ch'oe Chŏngwŏn's dedication in supporting her imprisoned husband Yi Kapki in Chŏnju, and recent speculation about U Pongun potentially becoming a nun after leaving her job at the Tonga Ilbo and moving to a Zen academy. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable glimpses into the diverse lives and public personas of educated and active Korean women during the colonial era. It highlights their involvement in fields like music/education (Chŏng, Ewha), literature (Chang Tŏkcho, Ch'oe Chŏngwŏn), journalism/work (U Pongun, Tonga Ilbo), and their navigation of social expectations, political realities (imprisonment of Yi Kapki), and personal choices (activism vs. arts, devotion to spouse, potential religious path).

드른 風月記
丁七星 女士와 가야금

벌서 7, 8년 전이 될가. 梨花女子專門學校 音樂科에서 가야금 敎授를 할터이니 丁七星女士에게 와서 敎授하여 달나고 懇請하엿다. 그랫스나 그 당시의 丁女士는 가야금줄 만지기 시려 사회운동에 뛰어 나온 즉후인지라 拒絶하엿든 것이다.

張德祚女士와 先生

女流作家 張德祚 여사는 그 夫君을 부를 때에 그 座席에 손님 有無를 不問하고 늘 「先生님, 先生님!」한다. 夫君에 대한 용어로는 여러 가지 잇지만 이는 新例의 一.

崔貞嫄 女士의 精誠

張赫宙씨의 推薦辭에 아울너 「洛東江의 中篇을 발표하야 文名을 놉히고 잇는 新進 崔貞嫄女史는 夫君 李甲基가 全州刑務所에 잇게 되자 全州로 따라가서 朝夕私食을 差入하기에 熱中하는 중.

禹鳳雲 女士의 그 뒤

東亞日報를 다니며 新聞勸誘員 노릇을 하든 禹鳳雲女史는 日前에 그 社를 그만두고 安國洞 女子禪學院에 居處를 정하고 잇섯는데 이 때문에 한동안 女士의 爲僧設까지 流布되엇스나 그 眞否는 아직도 모르겟다. <77>
