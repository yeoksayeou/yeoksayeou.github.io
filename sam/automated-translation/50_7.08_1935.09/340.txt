Recording Scene of Ms. Chŏng Hun-mo at the Okeh Recording Room

Recently, what attracts the public's popularity, no matter what, are movies and records. Wondering what the process of recording these records looked like, I happened to receive an invitation asking if I wouldn't come to watch a recording session in Seoul, as an engineer had specially made a business trip from Tokyo, bringing equipment worth tens of thousands of won. Guided by Mr. Yi Ch'ŏl, the branch manager of the Okeh Company, and Kim Nŭng-in, the head of the Arts and Literature Department, I went to the recording room in Myŏngch'i-chŏng.

Just then, Ms. Chŏng Hun-mo, who possesses the highest peak of popularity as a tenor on this land, was in the middle of recording "Ave Maria." I thought it fortunate that since I had come to observe anyway, I got to see such a fine scene. Record recording involves the tenor standing in front of something like a microphone found in a broadcasting station and singing solo, while three or four steps behind her, Professor Pak Kyŏng-ho of Ewha Womans College (梨專敎授) sat playing the accompaniment on a large piano.
On this day, Ms. Chŏng Hun-mo, dressed simply in a white *chŏgori* and black *ch'ima*, stood before the microphone and quietly opened her crimson lips (朱唇), singing
"Ave Maria." The song flowed purely and beautifully, without a hint of artificiality. It reminded me of how Mr. Yi Kwang-su once told a story<200> that while sitting at Sorimsa Temple near Se'gŏmjŏng Pavilion, such a fluid song flowed from a restaurant below that he wondered if it was recorded by some famous Westerner, followed the sound, and discovered it was the voice of Ms. Chŏng Hun-mo; indeed, the lady's voice is a treasure we can all boast of. "Ave Maria," befitting a sacred song, finished the first verse with a pious and solemn voice and moved onto the second verse.

Holy Mother Maria, mother of the lonely one
Protector of the thirty
From your shining throne (寶座)
Hear my prayer (祈禱)
When sorrow follows
And violent rulers (暴惡스런 님검들)
Inflict pain

Holy Mother Maria, Maria, Maria
When we face hardship (患難)
When we die
Give us
Patience (忍耐力)
And grant us your help, Amen.

Waiting until it was all finished, I entered the adjacent machine room and was astonished to hear what had just been recorded instantly copied onto a wax master (蠟版) and played back exactly as it was. In this machine room, a specialized engineer sat, adjusting the sound flowing in from the recording room and recording it onto the master disc. Here, I listened with satisfaction to the engineer's diagrammatic explanation (圖解), drawn painstakingly on a blackboard, and returned, having grasped the marvelous truth of the gramophone.
I heard that there are still no gramophone factories that can be established in Chosŏn, and perhaps in the future, if things go well, a recording studio (吹込所) might be set up by some company.<201>

**Summary**

The article describes a visit to an Okeh Company recording session in Seoul (Myŏngch'i-chŏng) in 1935. The author witnesses the renowned singer Chŏng Hun-mo recording "Ave Maria," accompanied by pianist Pak Kyŏng-ho. The piece details the recording setup (microphone, piano placement), Chŏng's performance and vocal quality (praised even by Yi Kwang-su), and the technical process involving a Japanese engineer operating equipment brought from Tokyo to create a wax master disc. The article concludes by noting the absence of gramophone factories in Korea and the possibility of establishing a recording studio in the future. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the early Korean recording industry under Japanese rule, highlighting the reliance on Japanese technology and personnel (Okeh Company, Tokyo engineer), the process of cultural production (recording popular singers like Chŏng Hun-mo), the interaction of Korean artists and intellectuals (Chŏng Hun-mo, Pak Kyŏng-ho, Yi Kwang-su), and the nascent state of technological development in colonial Korea regarding audio recording and manufacturing.

鄭勳模女史의 레코-드 吹込光景, 오-케 吹入室에서
근래에 대중의 인긔를 끄을고 잇는 것은 무엇 무엇하여도 영화와 레코-트라. 이 레-코-드를 취입하는 모양은 엇더한가 하고 궁금하게 알든 지음 하로는 東京서 일부러 수만원 짜리의 긔계를 가지고 技師가 出張나와서  서울서 吹込을 하니 와서 구경치 안켓느냐는 초청를 밧고 오-케會社 支店長 李哲氏와 文藝部長金陵人의 안내로 明治町 吹込室로 가보다.

마츰 이 땅의 테-너로서 가장 최고봉의 인기를 가지고 잇는 鄭勳模 女史가 「아베마리아」를 「吹込」하는 중이라. 긔왕 구경을 올 바에는 조흔 장면을 보게, 되엇슴을 유쾌하게 생각하엿다. 레코-드 吹込은 마치 放送局에 잇는 마이크로폰 가튼 것 압헤 서서 테-너가 독창을 하고 그로 서너 거름 뒤에 크다란 피아노를 노코, 梨專敎授 朴慶浩氏가 안저 반주를 하고 잇섯다.
이날 鄭勳模女史는 힌저고리 거문치마로 수수하게 차리고 마이크로폰 압헤 서서 고요히 朱唇을 여러
「아베마리아」하고 부른다. 그 노래가 조곰도 지어 하는대가 업시 순순히 아름답게 흐른다. 언젠가, 李光洙氏가 洗劍亭 少林寺에 안젓느라니<200> 저 아래 料亭으로부터 하도 流麗한 노래가 흐르기에 서양 어느 유명한 이가 취입한 것인가고 소리나는 곳을 따라와 드르니 鄭勳模氏의 소리더라고 이약이 하든 모양으로 실로 女史의 목청은 우리들이 모다 자랑할 만한」 보배로운 것이다. 「아베, 마리아」는 聖歌답게 敬虔嚴肅한 목청으로 제일절이 끗나고 제이절로 올머간다.

나는 다 끗나기를 기다려 그 엽 기계실에 드러가 지금 취입한 것이 금시에 蠟版에 올나 고대로 복사되어 나오는 것을 듯고 놀낫다. 이 기계실에 專門技師가 안저 취입실에서 흘너드러오는 소리를 조절하여 원판에 녹음하는 것이다. 나는 여기에서 흑판에 일일히 써가며 설명하여 주는 이 기사의 圖解를 만족하게 드러가며, 축음긔의 오묘한 진리를 깨닷고 도라나왓다.
드른 즉 조선에는 축음긔 공장이 아직 설 수 업고, 압흐로 혹 잘되면 吹込所 쯤은 어느 회사의 손으로든지 설듯하다 한다.<201>
