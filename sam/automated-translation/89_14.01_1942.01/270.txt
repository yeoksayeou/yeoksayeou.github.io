**Body Text Translation:**

War Poem
Imperial Edict Declaring War - December 8, Showa 16
Yi Kwang-su (Kōyama Mitsurō)

A sound above the clouds
Heaven and earth rumble and shake
The Great Edict (大詔) declaring war
Strike the unrighteous America and Britain!
Who foster disaster and chaos in East Asia
And seek to fulfill their misguided ambition to dominate the East
Strike the unrighteous America and Britain! Thus commands
Our Emperor's great order
One hundred million subjects respond "Yes!" and bow their heads
Mountains and seas tremble.

How could this be Our wish?<138>
Our Emperor lamented thus
Thinking of Asia's trampled eight hundred million souls
This Imperial Edict declaring war was bestowed
For two hundred years—two hundred years—for that long duration
Entangled in England's chains, wailing
India—Burma—Malaya—China (支邦)—the South Seas
To save the eight hundred million people
This gracious Command (吩咐) bestowed upon us, the one hundred million subjects.

Just one word, "Yes!"
At sunrise over Pearl Harbor, the roaring sound of propellers
Our sons of Japan have received well, magnificently
Our Emperor's will.

Listen, twenty-four million of the Peninsula!
To you and to me, equally
The great Command (吩咐) of war declaration has been bestowed
"Strike the unrighteous America and Britain!" ordered He
The brave Imperial soldiers who died at Pearl Harbor, Kuantan sea,
Like them, you and I too
Shall we not offer these lives to our Emperor?
-End-<139>

Islands of Hawaii
Chu Yo-han (Matsumura Kōichi)

Blue waves gentle, boasting spring skies year-round
Paradise of the Pacific, Hawaii Archipelago, lovely islands
On December eighth, when blood and fire rained down upon you like rain
You must have seen the banner of East Asian Liberation fly and the sword of Justice flash
On this day, the enemy warships sunk, starting with the flagship "Arizona"
"Oklahoma" and "West Virginia," and the aircraft carrier "Enterprise"
Four more wrecked beyond use, famous Pearl Harbor became a wretched place of corpses (屍體)
You must have seen the arrogant American nation's Asia Fleet become paralyzed on the spot.

"On this day, our aircraft losses were thirty-eight planes."
Though the Navy Ministry's (海軍省) announcement is brief
Behind the great victory on the first day of the glorious Great East Asia War
The band of brave warriors who answered the call of the Fatherland with their lives
Precious, treasured sacrifices, their unknown names will shine for a thousand years.

This is the blossoming of the Imperial spirit (皇國精神) passed down through three thousand years of history
This is the appearance of the invincible power of our Navy, polished and nurtured for over three thousand years
This is the explosion of East Asia's indignation (憤滿), having endured the unendurable for eight months<140>
Once setting out, never expecting to return again
Offering one's body to die for the Emperor and save the nation
Did you understand this great spirit, unparalleled in the world?
"If I go away to sea, I shall be a corpse washed up.
If I go away to the mountain, I shall be a corpse in the grass." (うみゆかば みつ゛くがばね 山めがば 草むすがぱね - *umi yukaba mizuku kabane, yama yukaba kusa musu kabane*)

Ah, what shatters and overturns warships of copper and iron?
Is it gunpowder? Is it bombs? Is it torpedoes (魚雷)? No, no
It was the soft flesh, blood, and bones of men who know no fear
It was the fiery spirit of youth.

Ah, thirty-eight sea hawks of the ocean that do not return
And with them, the petals of the military nation scattered in the blue sky (蒼空) of the Pacific
You are truly the heroes of the century!
With hot tears and trembling gratitude, we praise (讚揚) you
For the great victory on the first day of the Great East Asia War
We will eternally sing of your noble merits (功勞) established
On the lovely islands of Hawaii, in the middle of the Pacific
The day when Paradise returns again is not far off
Then, we shall build a tower piercing the heavens for you
All the races of the world will prostrate themselves beneath it and cry out
"Behold, here sleeps the mother who birthed a new era," they will call out
They will sing and rejoice
Their song and rejoicing will never cease.

But now, the waves of the Pacific boil daily.
Beneath which wave do the shattered wings sleep?
Sleep, shattered silver wings, sleep peacefully
One hundred million brethren are behind you
One billion yellow races are behind you
Upon the altar of your glorious lives
We will become a single mass of fire and offer our bodies
Until the day the final victory is won
Until the day East Asia's freedom is found
We will follow in your footsteps.
-End-<141>

To the Young Men
No Ch'ŏn-myŏng

The street, quiet yesterday,
Today, even the footsteps of passersby are heavy
Under the frosty December moon
Even in a single newspaper extra (號外) distributed
Citizens tense with worry for the nation—

Against old Britain
Which has suffered humiliation (屈辱) for generations
Towards that mixed-blood child (混血兒) America
The Empire has finally issued a declaration of war (宣戰)
For justice, for the construction of Great East Asia
We have raced the chariot of fire <142>
There were young men who threw off their uniforms (制服), left the beloved university campus (校庭)
And went out to the battlefield (싸홈터)
For the Fatherland, for the eternal peace of humanity
The red heart blooming like a rose in their young breasts

Even if we have to spit sand, we must win
The misery (悽慘함) of a nation defeated in battle
Do you not know it?
Do you not see it?
Even if we have to spit sand, we must win

Brother! Young men of this land!
Asia's urgent morning has come
Arise!
To where the roaring bugle sound (나발소리) is heard
My young men!
Will you not run forth like true men?
My young men!
Will you not run forth like true men?
-End-<143>

One Hundred Million Rifles Advance
Paeksan Ch'ŏngsu (Kim Tong-hwan)

1
One hundred million rifles (銃) advance
One hundred million rifles advance
Pushing through rough waves and fierce winds
One hundred million rifles, carried on the shoulders (억개) of one hundred million people, advance forward (압흐로).

Who can block these rifle points?
Who can withstand these rifle butts?
No one in the Pacific, no one in the Atlantic (太西洋)
No one in Asia, no one in North America (北米州)

Ah, to the sacred battlefield
One hundred million rifles
Carried on the shoulders of the old, on the shoulders of the young
Dash forth to the battlefield, reeking of gunpowder, to the battlefield.

2
One hundred million rifles advance
One hundred million rifles advance
Pushing through high mountains and deep seas
One hundred million rifles, carried on the shoulders of one hundred million people, advance forward, forward.

Where these rifle points reach is Pearl Harbor (眞珠灣), Borneo, below the Equator (赤道)
Where this rifle sound is heard is the Philippines (比律賓), Hawaii (布哇), the ears of the people of India
They aim to pierce the hearts of the powerful enemies, Britain and America; are those rifle butts five hundred million? Or one billion?<144>

Ah, how magnificent! Among them, our young men ride airplanes
Fly through the sky, board submarines and swim through thousand-fathom waters
Will they embrace torpedoes (魚雷)? Will they drive tanks?
The time has come, the time has come! The day for the youth of Asia to rise has come.

3
One hundred million rifles advance
One hundred million rifles advance
Pushing through harsh encirclement (包圍) and fierce oppression (壓迫)
One hundred million rifles, carried on the shoulders of one hundred million people, advance little by little.

Now, even in the coffee fields of South America
Even in the sugar plantations (砂糖農園) of Manila, there is only gunpowder and the smell of blood
But on the day the powerful enemies Britain and America retreat, birds will sing again
Wives will pick mulberry leaves, and children will tend cattle

To greet that day of peace not far off
Let us quickly share bread and smoke cigarettes together
Becoming one body, one mind, let us rush yelling toward the battlefield
Where the banner of victory flies, toward the battlefield.

4
Japan! Japan! My Fatherland (祖國) Japan!
Mother! Mother! Mother of Asia, Japan!
Hungry children are starving, naked children are cold
Asking for milk, asking for clothes, one billion children cry, they cry

They know your house floor is small, but trusting your heart is wide and large
Now they spread their arms, shouting to be embraced, they shout
Though their weak bodies have been defiled without choice
Their souls (靈魂) are still clean, and their blood is pure (純潔).

Ah, in the mountains and rivers of old Asia
Now the sound of young life is heard, vigorously heard
In our long-lonely hearts too
The trumpet sound of the dawning new century is heard, is heard resoundingly.
-End-<145>

**Summary**

This collection features four poems published in the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* immediately after Japan's attack on Pearl Harbor and declaration of war against the US and UK. Written by prominent Korean intellectuals Yi Kwang-su, Chu Yo-han, No Ch'ŏn-myŏng, and Kim Tong-hwan (some using Japanese names), the poems fervently support Japan's war effort. They praise the Emperor's decision, glorify the Japanese military's initial victories (especially Pearl Harbor), laud the sacrifices of soldiers, demonize the Allied powers (US and UK), and call upon Koreans ("Peninsula's twenty-four million," "Young men of this land") to join the fight for the "Great East Asia War" and Japan, portrayed as the liberator and mother of Asia. For historians, these poems are stark examples of pro-Japanese collaborationist literature produced by leading Korean cultural figures during the late colonial period. They reveal the immense pressure and ideological mobilization efforts under Japanese rule, showcasing how the narrative of "liberating Asia" was deployed to rally support for Japan's imperial ambitions, even among the colonized population, and the complex, often tragic positions intellectuals found themselves in.

戰爭詩
宣戰大詔 - 昭和 16年 12月 8日
李光洙(香山光郞)

구름우에 소리 있어
하늘과 땅이 드르르 울리다
宣戰하시는 大詔
無道한 米國과 英國을 쳐라
東亞의 禍亂을 助長하고
東洋制覇의 非望을 채우랴는
無道한 米國과 英國을 쳐라 합시는
우리 임금의 크신 분부
億萬백성이 예하고 고개를 숙이니
山과 바다가 떨다.

이 어찌 朕의 뜻이랴＜138＞
우리 임금은 이렇게 恨嘆하시다
아세아의 짓밟힌 八億生靈을
혜오시매 나리신 宣戰大詔샷다
二百年- 二百年- 기나긴 동안
앵굴의 쇠사슬에 얼켜서 慟哭하는
印度의- 緬甸의- 馬來의- 支邦의- 南洋의
八億蒼生을 건지시라고
우리들 一億臣民에게 내리신 거륵하신 吩咐샷다.

「네」 하는 한 소리뿐
眞珠灣 해뜰력헤 우렁찬 푸로펠라소리

우리 임금의 뜻을 받자왔다.

들으라 半島의 二千四百萬아
네게도 내게도 꼭 같이

하와이의 섬들아
朱耀翰(松村紘一)

푸른 물결 잔잔하고 사철에 봄하늘 자랑하는
太平洋의 樂園 하와이 群島 어여쁜 섬들아

깨어저서 다시 못쓰게 된 자도 네隻, 이름 좋은 眞珠灣은 비참한 屍體가 되고
황포한 아메리카나라의 亞細亞 艦隊는 앉은자리에서 半身不隨의 病身이 됨을 네 보았으리라.

「이날에 우리 飛行機의 損害는 三十八機」
海軍省의 發表는 비록 簡單하지마는
光輝있는 大東亞戰爭 첫날 大勝利 뒤에
祖國의 부르심을 生命으로 報答한 勇士의 무리
귀하고 보배스런 犧牲, 이름 모를 그들의 이름 千歲에 빛나리.

이는 곧 三千年 歷史에 나려온 皇國精神의 꽃핌이며
이는 곧 三千餘年 닦고, 길러온 우리 海軍의 無敵의 힘이 나타남이며

한번 나가면 다시 돌아오기를 기약치 아니하고
내 몸을 죽여서 임금께 바치고 나라를 구하자는
世界에 둘도 없는 偉大한 精神을 네가 알았드냐
「うみゆかば みつ゛ ぐがばね
山めがば 草むすがぱね」

아아 銅鐵의 軍艦을 깨트리고 뒤엎는 것은
火藥이드냐, 暴彈이드냐, 魚雷드냐, 아니다  아니다
그것은 두렴을 모르는 사람의 연한 살과 피와 뼈였다
젊은이의 불붙은 精神이였다.

아아 돌아오지 않는 바다의 솔개 38機여
그와 함께 太平洋蒼空에 흐터진 軍國의 꽃떨기여
그대야말로 世紀의 英雄!
우리는 뜨거운 눈물과 떨리는 感謝로서 그대를 讚揚하리
大東亞戰爭, 첫날의 큰 勝利에
세워진 그대의 거륵한 功勞를 永遠히 노래하리
太平洋의 한북판 하와이의 어여쁜 섬들에
또다시 樂園이 돌아올 날도 머지 아니하다
그때에 우리는 그대를 위해서 하늘을 찌르는 塔을 세우리
世界의 모든 人種들은 그 아래 엎디여 부르짖으리니
「보라 여기 새로운 時代를 나으신 어머니께서 잠자신다」부르리니
그들은 노래하고 즐겨하리니
그들의 노래와 즐김은 끊일 날이 없을 것이다.

그러나 지금은 太平洋의 물결이 날마다 끓고 있다.
그 어느 물결밑에 깨어진 날개들은 잠자고 있느냐
잠자라, 깨여진 은빛의 날개들아, 平安히 잠자라
一億의 同胞가 네 뒤에 있다
十億의 黃色人種이 네 뒤에 있다
그대들의 榮光스런 生命의 祭壇에
한 덩어리 불이 되어 우리 몸을 바칠 것이다
最後의 勝利를 얻는 날까지
東亞의 自由를 찾는 날까지
우리는 그대들의 자최를 따라갈 것이다.
-끝-＜141＞
