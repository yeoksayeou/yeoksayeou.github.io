**Mass Migration to Mudanjiang**
Sometime ago, Mr. Yamamoto Sanehiko, president of the Kaizōsha (改造社) in Tōkyō, published an account in a certain magazine of his meeting with Governor-General Ugaki. According to him, the Governor-General stated,
"In the future, we plan to send a large number of Korean immigrants to the vicinity of Mudanjiang (牧丹江) on the banks of the Heilongjiang River in Manchukuo (滿洲國)."
Governor-General Ugaki, taking industrial policy as his motto, has already implemented various policies such as Northern Korea Development (北鮮開拓), South Cotton North Sheep (南綿北羊), Gold Production Policy (産金政策), Promotion of Agriculture, Mountain, and Fishing Villages (農山漁村振興), and Self-Reliance (自力更生). Now, embarking on the Manchurian immigration plan, the Mudanjiang area has historically been known as an agricultural region due to its fertile land and suitable climate. If only the rampant banditry (土匪) can be eradicated and public security work completed, a safe and abundant large agricultural area will be realized here, becoming a paradise for numerous immigrants.

**Impending War Clouds in Europe and Japan's Proactive Measures**
An article recently published in the Ōsaka Mainichi (大版 每日) is noteworthy, so it is recorded here.
"Dark clouds increasingly hang low over the entire European and African continents across the Mediterranean Sea.
These days, the issue between Italy (伊太利) and Ethiopia (에치오피아) is about to reach the point of war.
Italian airplanes are bombing Ethiopian people and livestock.
Seeing Ethiopia's peril, British (英國) public opinion has suddenly erupted, moving towards hardline diplomacy like blockading the Suez Canal (스에즈 運河).
However, Italy, with the backing of France (佛蘭西), opposes Britain and acts defiantly.<18>
Due to this relationship, unusual dark clouds gather between Britain and Italy, and there is a sense of tension flowing, on the verge of war.
If France assists Italy in this situation, it is clear that Germany (獨逸), which has maintained silence and observed the situation until now, will side with Britain.
If this happens, Russia (러사아) is also unlikely to remain idle and just sit back.
Might Europe plunge into a 'Second World War' once again?
At such a time, what kind of demands is Japan (日本) making towards China (中國)?
A proactive policy is being pursued, including things like:
1. Japan shall employ Japanese administrative, military, and other advisors throughout all provinces of China.
2. Railroads shall be constructed comprehensively from central to southern China.
At such a time, a pro-Japanese atmosphere is suddenly boiling up in China.
The United States (米國) is merely watching the world situation deeply and maintaining silence.
For this proactive policy towards China, Japan will carry out a major expansion of its Army and Navy. Also, the Yen exchange rate (圓爲替) will experience a second plunge. If this happens, Finance Minister Takahashi (高橋藏相) will certainly be forced to retreat, and the Okada cabinet (罔田內閣) will yield its place to a newly formed, even stronger cabinet. If that occurs, an Ugaki cabinet will be formed.
Furthermore, a young financier with an understanding of currency depreciation phenomena will be appointed as the Minister of Finance.
In this way, Japan will make its second leap forward across politics, economy, and finance.
Only time will prove all these catastrophic global events.

**A Day in the Life of An Tosan**
About 40 *ri* into the mountains from P'yŏngyang (平壤), there is an old temple village (廢寺村) where a temple used to stand. This temple was built by the grandmother of Ms. Sin Sŏngnyŏsa (信聖女史), the Kwansŏ (關西) heroine who was formerly the President* of the Kŭn'uhoe (槿友會). Tosan (島山) went there last summer for recuperation. Ch'unwŏn (春園) and several others accompanied him. Here is what we heard about how Tosan spent his days:
In the morning, he wakes up so early that no one in the household has ever seen him get up. He washes his face far away by the clear water of a mountain stream bend.
For meals, he eats many vegetables. If young people play Go (將碁), finding it boring, he comes nearby. Unable to directly tell them to stop, he calls out loudly to a player, "So-and-so, come here," and then talks about worldly affairs.
(*Original text has *槿友委圓會/*長, likely a typo for 槿友會委員長 - Kŭn'uhoe President/Chairperson)

**Scene from the Taiwan Local Autonomy Alliance All-Island Convention**
Ahead of the implementation of the Local Autonomy System on October 1st and the inaugural election on November 22nd, the 3rd All-Island Convention of the Taiwan Local Autonomy Alliance (臺灣地方自治聯盟), the only political association permitted for Taiwanese islanders, was held on August 17th at 3 PM in the Taichū City (臺中市) Public Hall. Attending were 150 delegates from branches across the island and 150 general participants, totaling 300 people. The venue was held under the strict surveillance of 50 uniformed and plainclothes police officers from the Taichū Prefecture Police Department (臺中州警務部) and Taichū Police Station (臺中署).
At the scheduled time, Mr. Ch'ae (蔡氏) gave the opening remarks, followed by a report on the movement's progress by Alliance Standing Director Yang Chao-chia (楊肇嘉氏). Then, Alliance Advisor Lin Hsien-t'ang (林謙堂氏) delivered congratulatory remarks.
From Tōkyō (東京), congratulatory messages from House of Peers member Sakatani (阪谷貴族院議員), Mr. Abe Isoo (安部磯雄氏), and others were read aloud, and congratulatory telegrams from Nagai Ryūtarō (永井柳太郞), Metropolitan Police Superintendent-General Oguri (小栗警視總監), and others were received.
With Yang Chao-chia presiding as chairman, the agenda proceeded. Agenda item No. 1, concerning the reform of the Autonomy Alliance organization, aimed to expand the Alliance's single goal of "establishing local autonomy" to include general political, economic, and social movements in its platform. However, it led to a confrontation between the status quo and reform factions, continuing with fierce debate for an hour and a half. Amidst great clamor, no conclusion was reached, and the item was ultimately shelved. Agenda item No. 3, regarding whether to officially endorse candidates for city council members and town/village council members, was prohibited from being presented by the authorities. After proceeding with the agenda on five other items, the convention adjourned at 8 PM the same day.

**Great Chinese Politicians and Wealth**
China (中國) has many large plutocrats. It is already known worldwide that there are many great capitalists in financial institutions and industrial fields. However, statistics from a recent investigation by a certain organization reveal that China's top politicians also possess considerable wealth.<20>
Moreover, someone like Yan Xishan (閻錫山), while entrenched in Taiyuan (太原), reportedly does not own much private land, nor does he deposit funds in Chinese banks. According to hearsay, he deposits his entire fortune in foreign banks, amounting to about 100 million yuan. Even if not entirely believable, it shows he is a man of great wealth.

**Chang Tŏksu's Return Scheduled for December**
Mr. Chang Tŏksu (張德秀氏), Vice President of the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), who has long been traveling through Europe and America (歐米), was rumored to be returning several times. This time, however, it is said he will truly set foot on his longed-for native soil this winter in December.

**Dr. Shin's Review Magazine Postponed**
A rumor circulated some time ago that Dr. Shin Hŭng'u (申興雨博士) would launch a review magazine. According to the doctor, it has been postponed for the time being due to various circumstances.

**Hyesŏk, Iryŏp Together in the Same Temple for 2 Months**
The painter Ms. Na Hyesŏk (羅蕙錫 女史), who had returned to her hometown in Suwŏn (水原故山) and ceased painting, recently traveled extensively through the Honam region's mountains and rivers (湖嶺山川) seeking painting subjects. It is said she met the female poet Ms. Kim Iryŏp (金一葉女士), who had shaved her head to become a nun (削髮爲僧) at some temple in Ch'ungnam (忠南), and stayed together in the nun's quarters (僧房) for about two months before returning.

**Chŏng Chongmyŏng's Trip to Tongnae**
Ms. Chŏng Chongmyŏng (鄭鍾鳴 女士), former leader of the Kŭn'uhoe (槿友會), who was recently released after serving a four-year sentence, stayed in Seoul (서울) for a while. To recover her health, she recently went down to Tongnae (東萊). There, she plans to stroll around Haeundae (海雲臺) and other places to recuperate, and when the autumn winds blow gently, she intends to reappear in Hanyang (漢陽) with her cheerful and talkative face.

**Our Proud Historic Sites**
Historic sites (古蹟) are a heritage from our ancestors. Recently, the fervor for preserving and cherishing historic sites seems to be gradually increasing on the Peninsula (半島). With September 10th designated as Historic Site Protection Day (古蹟愛護日), public interest in this matter is growing daily.
On this occasion, let us once again identify the most important historic sites hidden here and there in Kyŏngsŏng (京城) and its suburbs.
Among the oldest are Tonhwamun (敦化門) in Waryongdong (臥龍洞), southwest of Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮), and Ch'angŭimun (彰義門) towards Ch'ŏng'undong (淸雲洞)—these are from 531 years ago.<21> Sajiktan (社稷壇) in Sajikdong (社稜洞 - likely Sajikdong) is from 539 years ago, and Munmyo (文廟) in Sungindong (崇三洞 - area name?) is an ancient site from 538 years ago.

**Recent Trend in Mining Claim Applications**
The number of mining claim applications (鑛區 出願數) during the past August was 620, bringing the cumulative total since January this year to 7,552. While this statistic shows an increase of over 2,500 compared to the same period last year, the number of recent applications since June this year shows a trend of sharp decline. The Government-General Mining Bureau (總督府 鑛山當局) views this with regret (遺憾視), considering it a result of hesitation (悛巡) in investment from mainland Japanese capital (日本內地資本) due to the rampancy of dishonest brokers (不正뿌로커).

**Pak Indŏk and the Panama Canal**
Ms. Pak Indŏk (朴仁德氏) has received an invitation from the US headquarters to attend the World Christian Conference (世界基督敎人大會), held once every four years under the name "Student Volunteer Movement for Foreign Missions" (米國學生宜學義勇團), which will take place in Indianapolis, Indiana (米國) from December 28th this year to January 2nd next year, as a Korean representative. Thus, the lady will depart for beloved America (米洲) after a long time. A few days before her departure, the lady mentioned to a friend, "On this trip, the thing I am most eagerly and impatiently anticipating is passing through the Panama Canal (파나마 운하)." The friend didn't understand the meaning and asked again. The lady said,
"If I just pass through the Panama Canal this time, it means I will have passed between all the continents across the so-called six continents (6大洲). When I traveled around the world for 36 months before, I passed through the strait at Constantinople (큰스탠치노-불) [Istanbul], the boundary between Asia (亞細亞洲) and Europe (歐羅巴洲), and it was quite delightful. The European side had fertile mountains and rivers with lush trees, but this Asian side had mountains without a single tree. However, when coming back, why did I long so much for the mountains and rivers of Asia? After that, I passed through the Suez Canal (스에즈 운하), so I also passed the boundary between Africa (阿弗利加洲) and Europe. And since I crossed the Pacific Ocean (太平洋), it means I passed between America (米洲) and Asia too. Passing between continents like this truly refreshes the mind. So, if I pass through the Panama Canal this time, I will pass between South America (南米洲) and North America (北美洲), which means I will have passed all the boundaries of the six continents. This is the most delightful and joyous thing for me." etc.<24>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from "Samch'ŏlli" magazine covers a variety of news snippets under the heading "The Korean Black Chamber." Topics include Japan's plans to settle Koreans in Mudanjiang (Manchuria) as part of colonial development policy, the tense international situation with Italy/Ethiopia potentially sparking a wider European war and Japan's simultaneous assertive policy towards China, gossip about prominent figures like An Ch'angho (An Tosan), Na Hyesŏk, Kim Iryŏp, and Chŏng Chongmyŏng, business news like the Hwashin acquisition of P'yŏngan Department Store, reports on Taiwanese political movements, lists of wealthy Chinese politicians and famous Pen Club members, personnel changes involving Korean intellectuals and journalists, a list of historic sites in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), updates on mining claims, and an anecdote about Pak Indŏk's upcoming trip to America focusing on her excitement about traversing the Panama Canal. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into Japanese colonial policy (Manchurian migration, economic development slogans), the perception of international affairs from Korea, the social activities and movements of Korean intellectuals and elites (including travel, affiliations, and personal struggles), urban development (Tongyang Theater), colonial economic activity (department stores, mining), and the state of cultural preservation awareness (historic sites list). The mention of specific figures moving between Korea, Japan, Manchuria, China, and the West also highlights the interconnectedness and mobility (or lack thereof) within the Japanese empire and beyond.

東洋劇場開館은
서울 西大門에다가 신축 중인 극장(東明극장)은 그 費 만원으로 2층 赤煉瓦 洋舘인 바 벌서 그 事가 몹시 진보되야, 今冬부터는 開館하게 되리라 한다. 이 극장은 靑年實業家 洪淳彦氏가 經營하는 것으로 氏로 말하면 舞姬 裴龜子女史의 남편이라. 준공된 뒤에는 裴龜子의 무용도 상연하게 되리라 한다.

펜俱樂部멤버-記
세계의 代表的 文筆家를 두합하야 펜 俱樂部가 조직되어 잇는데 그 멘버-中 중요한 이는 H·G·웰스(英), 로만·로-란, 포-ㄹ·바레이(佛), G·하웁트만, 토-마스·만(獨), E·A·로빈손(米), 막씸·고-리키-, P·메리지콥흐스키-(露), R·타고-ㄹ(伊), 그리고 東洋人으로는 中國의 胡適 陳立夫 등이다.

最近의 人事
滿洲國 陸軍中佐이든 李亮氏는 이번에 滿洲國을 그만두고 北中 天津에 와서 활동한다고.
金岸曙씨는 漢城圖書를 나온 뒤 京城放送局의 囑託으로 就任.
閔丙徹氏는 批判社에 入社.
毛允淑女士는 身病으로 咸興에 내려가 筆硯을 갓가히 하며 고요히 精養中.
異河潤氏는 京城放送局을 그만두고 컬럼비아 레코-드會社 文藝部長에＜22＞ 就任.
崔貞熙女史는 映畵時代社에 入社.
金箕範氏는 朝鮮日報總務部長을 辭하더니 和信에 入社하리란 說이 잇다.
朱耀翰氏는 和信연쇄점 用務를 띄고 今秋에 東京 大阪으로 出張할 듯하다고 한다.

改造社 入社한 安漠氏
한동안 「캅프」에서 左益 文藝理論를 전개하였든 일이 있으며, 早稻田大學 露文學科를 今春에 졸업한 安漠氏의 최근 소식에 대하여서는 지난 9월에 「半島의 舞姬」를 撮影次로 入京하였든 氏의 안해 崔承喜女史에게로 물었드니 얼마전에 安漠氏는 「改造社」에 社員으로 入社하였다고.

金玉均先生이 오늘까지 사라게섯더면 올에 74세가 되섯스리라고.

鑛區 出願數의 最近傾向
지난 8월 중의 鑛區 出願數는 620件으로서 本年 1월 以降의 누계가 7,552件이다. 이 통계는 전년 同期에 비교하면 2,500餘 件의 증가를 보이고 잇스나 今年 6월 以降 최근의 出願件數는 격감되고 잇는 경향이다. 이것은 한편에 잇서서 不正뿌로커의 발호에 의한 日本內地資本의 投下가 悛巡됨에 이른 결과이라고 總督府 鑛山當局에서는 遺憾視하고 잇다.

朴仁德氏와 파나마運河
朴仁德씨는 금년 12월 28일붙어 明年 정월 2일까지 인듸아나州(米國) 인듸아나·풀리스에서 열니는 「米國學生宜學義勇團」이라는 명칭을 가지고 4년마다 1次씩 열니는 世界基督敎人大會에 조선대표로써 출석하도록 米國本社로 붙어 초대장이 와서, 女史는 오랫간만에 다시금 그리운 米洲를 향하야 떠나리라는데, 출발을 맻날을 압둔 女史는 하루 엇떤 友人의게 「나는이번 길에 가장 기대를 가지고 초조하게 기대리는 것은 파나마 운하 통과할 때 일 것이다」라는 말을 하엿다. 그 友人은 그 뜻을 몰나 다시 물엇드니 女史는 말하되,
「나는 이번 파나마 운하까지만 지나면 소위 6大洲에서 대륙과 대륙 사히를 다-통과하여본는 섬이지요. 전에 36개월간을 세계 일주할 때에 亞細亞洲와 歐羅巴洲의 경계선인 「큰스탠치노-불」이의 海峽을 지나보았는데 퍽 유쾌하드군요. 저쪽 歐洲쪽은 山川에 기름끼 돌고 나무도 무성한데 이쪽 亞洲는 산에 나무 하나 없드군요. 그러나 다시 도라올 때에는 亞洲의 山川이 웨 그렇게도 그리워요. 그후에 스에즈 운하를 지낫스니, 阿弗利加洲와 歐羅巴洲의 경계선도 지나게 되었고, 또 太平洋을 건넛스니 米洲와 亞洲 사히도 지난 섬으로, 이렇게 대륙과 대륙 사히를 지날때면 참으로 마음이 상쾌하드군요. 그런데 이번 파나마운하만 통과하면 南米洲와 北米洲 사히를 지나게 되니, 이렇게 되면 6大洲의 경계선을 모조리 지나보는 섬이지요. 나는 이것이 제일 유쾌하고 깁뿐일이야요.」云云.＜24＞
