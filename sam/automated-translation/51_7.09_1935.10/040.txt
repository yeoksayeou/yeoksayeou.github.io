**Summary**

This 1935 article from the Korean magazine *Samch'ŏlli* provides a comparative overview of the military strength of major world powers: Italy, France, the United Kingdom, the United States, and Russia (Soviet Union). It details troop numbers (regular, colonial, reserve, volunteer, police/gendarmerie), organizational structures (divisions, corps, brigades), and equipment levels, specifically aircraft and tanks, for each nation. The final sections offer a direct comparison of air force sizes and tank numbers across these powers plus Japan and Germany. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects the dissemination of international geopolitical and military information within colonized Korea, likely curated or permitted by Japanese authorities. The focus on the military buildup of major powers highlights the tense international atmosphere preceding World War II and implicitly positions the Japanese Empire (mentioned briefly in the final comparisons) within this global power dynamic.
