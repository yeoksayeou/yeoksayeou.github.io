발행일	1935년 10월 01일
기사제목	길손 (Wayfarer)
필자	麗水
기사형태	시 (Poem)

길손
						麗水

길손- 그는 한 코스모포리탄
아모도 그의 故國을 아는 이 업다
大空을 날르는 『새』의 自由로운 마음
그의 발길은 아모데나 거칠것이 업다.

길손- 그는 한 니힐리스트
그의 슲은옷자락이 바람에 나붓긴다
쓰듸쓴 過去여 탐탁할것업는 現在여
그는 將來의 『꿈』마저 물우에 떠보낸다.

길손- 그는 한 樂天主義者
더 일흘것은업고 어들것만잇는 그다
안해와 명예와 안락은
그가 버림으로서 다시 엇는재산이리라.

길손- 그대는 쓰듸쓴 입맛을 다신다
길손- 그대는 슲은大空의 自由로운 『새』다.＜244＞

***

Wayfarer
						Yŏsu

Wayfarer—he is a cosmopolitan
Nobody knows his homeland (故國)
The free heart of a “bird” flying the vast sky (大空)
His footsteps face no obstacles anywhere.

Wayfarer—he is a nihilist
His sad coattails flutter in the wind
O bitter past (過去), O unsatisfactory present (現在)
He even lets his dreams (『꿈』) of the future (將來) float away on the water.

Wayfarer—he is an optimist (樂天主義者)
He has nothing more to lose, only things to gain
Wife and honor and comfort
Are assets gained again by his having abandoned them.

Wayfarer—you smack your bitter lips
Wayfarer—you are a sad, free “bird” of the vast sky (大空).<244>

**Summary**

This poem portrays the "Wayfarer" (길손) as a paradoxical figure embodying freedom, detachment, and a unique form of optimism. He is depicted as a rootless cosmopolitan, a nihilist discarding past, present, and future, yet also an optimist who finds gain in loss. The poem evokes a sense of melancholic freedom. For historians studying colonial Korea, this poem might be of interest as it potentially reflects the complex psychological states of individuals during a period of national loss and restricted identity. The themes of rootlessness, nihilism contrasted with a forced optimism, and the embrace of detachment could mirror the experiences or sentiments of some Koreans navigating life under colonial rule, seeking freedom in detachment when external freedoms were curtailed.
