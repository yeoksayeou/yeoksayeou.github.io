Act 3
Characters
Jing Ke
Yu Ji
Prince Dan
Eunuch
Ju Wu
Setting - A room in the palace.
Jing Ke and Yu Ji enter together.
Jing Ke: In the palace, the days and months fly like arrows,
Yu Ji: Turning again to the time of frost and snow,
Jing Ke: A beautiful woman steals away a hero's courage,
Yu Ji: Only a hero deserves a beautiful woman's pity! (Sound of wind)
Jing Ke: What's that sound of wind outside?
Yu Ji: It's the falling leaves in the wind.
Jing Ke: Time flies like an arrow, it's already autumn!
Yu Ji: Soon, snow will block the Qin Pass, making it impossible to go.
Jing Ke: That's nothing...
Yu Ji: By the way, sir, when are you going to the state of Qin?
Jing Ke: That...
Yu Ji: Yes?
Jing Ke: Why do you ask about matters of state?
Yu Ji: Everyone wants to hear about matters of state.
Jing Ke: Yu Ji. Play the geomungo (거문고) again for me, let me listen some more.
Yu Ji: (Hesitating somewhat) Yes. (She pulls the geomungo closer and plays.)
Jing Ke: Ah, it's a difficult thing for a man born into this world to receive the care of a beautiful woman.<273> In this land of warmth and tenderness (溫柔鄕), one forgets about growing old!
Yu Ji: If one grows old, does the world and the state still exist?
Jing Ke: Of course, the world and the state still exist.
(Yu Ji gives a cold smile and pushes the geomungo aside.)
Jing Ke: Why are you doing that? Are you angry?
Yu Ji: (Lowers her head, silent, gently plucking the geomungo strings with her hand. Jing Ke approaches and takes her hand.)
Jing Ke: A wondrous hand. A fine hand!
Yu Ji: Do you like my hand, sir?
Jing Ke: Anyone with fine hands is endearing.
(Yu Ji stands up, takes Jing Ke's sword, and cuts her left hand. Jing Ke grabs hold of her. Yu Ji faints. Jing Ke is greatly alarmed.)
Jing Ke: Yu Ji, are you alright! (Quickly tears his clothing hem and binds Yu Ji's hand) Yu Ji has regained consciousness!
Yu Ji: (Singing) In an instant, the pain before me is unbearable.
Ah, ah, unable to do anything else, I made this decision.
You tell me, a woman shouldn't ask about state affairs, but you clearly, seeing gain (利祿), forget righteousness (義), intending not to leave.
Perhaps the matter of stabbing the King of Qin is just empty talk.
Is it not that your great ambition has diminished due to coveting glory and splendor (榮華)?
I regarded you as an extraordinary man (奇男子),
But you regard me merely as a lowly person!
Jing Ke: (Singing) Yu Ji. Your words are too excessive,
Is it not that you too have misjudged me?
Stabbing the King of Qin to death is no easy task; it must not be done carelessly.
Yu Ji. Killing the King of Qin is not such an easy task. A meticulous plan must be made. I must pretend to be an envoy, go offer land, and request peace (和親) to be able to get close to the King of Qin. Also, there is a general named Fan Yuqi (樊於期) in Qin; he committed a crime against the King of Qin, and the King wants to capture and behead him. This man has now fled and is staying in the state of Yan. If his head could be cut off and presented to the King of Qin, the King would surely be pleased. When the King sees the severed head, it would not be difficult to stab him then. But now, the map is not yet ready, and Fan Yuqi has not died, so how can I possibly go? For you to say I see gain and forget righteousness, how can this not be vexing? Yu Ji, I love you. I resent you!
Yu Ji: Lord Jing Ke! Whether you love me or resent me,<274> I love you completely.
If you enter Qin and succeed in the assassination, you will surely not return alive. Since you cannot live, I too have only death once. And if, because you love me, you mess things up and become a laughingstock to the world, then too I will only have death once. Since I must die once anyway, why not die by the blade of a hero (壯士), die at the hands of my lover, while I am still young and my face is fair? Jing Ke, since you love me, please kill me!
Jing Ke: (Hesitates for a long time. Yu Ji looks up, grasping Jing Ke's sword. Jing Ke draws the sword, then sheathes it again, embraces Yu Ji, and kisses her.)
Jing Ke: Yu Ji. I love you! (Draws the sword and stabs Yu Ji, killing her. He stands holding the sword, silent for a while.)
(Prince Dan and Ju Wu enter. Ju Wu holds a map (地圖) and is greatly shocked seeing the body with one eye, but Prince Dan signals him not to make a commotion.)
Dan: Sir Jing Ke, the map is ready.
(Jing Ke receives the map. A dagger (匕首) is hidden within the scroll.)
Jing Ke: I intend to take one person with me this time. Is there such a person anywhere?
Dan: There is a man named Qin Wuyang (秦舞揚). He is young but courageous and strong. You will see him shortly.
Jing Ke: Then it is settled.
Ju: Sir Jing Ke. Why did Yu Ji die?
Jing Ke: That child, you mean? She died because of the King of Qin. Ah, because of the King of Qin, Tian Guang (田光) committed suicide, and because of the King of Qin, Yu Ji lost her life. So how can I not go quickly, follow that man (the King), kill him, and resolve the public anger (公憤) of the world! Only, Fan Yuqi is someone the King of Qin is currently offering a reward for his capture. I too have long thought of getting his head to present to the King of Qin, but Your Highness could not bear it. However, things having reached this point, we have no choice but to borrow that man's head. Your Highness, please prepare the travel arrangements. I will set off tomorrow. (Jing Ke exits. Prince Dan and Ju Wu look at each other.)
Dan: Ah, (Singing) Jing Ke is a man of courage (肝膽),
Ju: (Singing) Great success is imminent (指顧問).
Dan: (Singing) Seeing Yu Ji, my heart is truly grieved (心實慘),
Ju: (Singing) A beauty's death weighs heavier than Mount Tai (重泰山)!
(The two look down at Yu Ji) -Curtain-<275>

Act 4
Setting - Yishui River (易水).
Yan Dan (燕丹), leading his officials dressed in white mourning clothes, bids farewell to Lord Jing at the bank of the Yishui River. Qin Wuyang (秦舞陽), holding the human head and the map, follows Jing Ke.
Crowd: This is the Yishui River.
Dan: Bring wine. - Sir Jing Ke, Sir Wuyang, let this cup of wine be our farewell. Today, sending you off from the banks of the Yishui, we await your return after achieving great success (大功), bringing life (生色) back to the Yishui.
Jing: Your Highness, what words are these? If one does not have the resolve to surely die, how can one accomplish a great task (大事)? If I go this time, I will not return alive.
-Qin Wuyang, come here.
Qin Wuyang: Yes!
Jing: Think carefully. Are you afraid of going to the Qin palace this time? If you are afraid, you don't absolutely have to go.
Qin Wuyang: Although I am young, my courage (膽) is big enough to cover the heavens. Entering the paltry Qin palace will be like walking into an empty place (無人之境). I will definitely go and meet King Ying Zheng (嬴政) of Qin. - Sir, please do not look down on me too much!
Jing: Then it is decided! Since you have such boldness (胆量), let us go together. Bring the horses!
Soldier: Yes! (Leads the horses over)
(Jing Ke mounts his horse and slowly departs)
Jing Ke: (Singing) The wind sighs, sighing; the Yishui River is cold (風蕭蕭兮易水寒).
A brave warrior sets out, never to return (壯士一去兮不復還)!
Crowd: (Chorus) The wind sighs, sighing; the Yishui River is cold.
A brave warrior sets out, alas, to return (壯士一去兮復還)! [Note: The crowd's line likely contains a typo, repeating '不復還' (never to return) is standard. '復還' (to return) contradicts Jing Ke's line and intent.]
Jing Ke: (Sings in Yu mode (羽聲)) The wind sighs, sighing; the Yishui River is cold.
A brave warrior sets out, never to return!
Crowd: The wind sighs, sighing; the Yishui River is cold.
A brave warrior sets out, never to return!
(Jing Ke exits. The crowd watches him go into the distance.)
Dan: Truly a brave warrior (壯士)!
Jing [error, should be Dan or Crowd]: Truly a brave warrior! -(Curtain)-

Scene 1
Characters

Qin General Meng Jia
Jing Ke
Two Banner Guards (旗牌)
Qin Wuyang
Meng: (Entering and speaking) My authority fills the world (聲威滿天下). Mighty armies garrison the nine provinces (雄兵鎭九州). I am the Qin General Meng Jia (蒙嘉). His Majesty raised an army to attack and destroy the state of Yan. Yan, knowing its guilt, immediately sent an envoy to cede land, request peace (和親), and present a map. However, people from Yan are very treacherous (奸詐), so when I see the envoy, I must investigate him thoroughly. Come here. Bring in the envoy!
Banner Guard: The General's order! Bring in the envoy from Yan! (Four soldiers push Jing Ke in.)
Jing Ke: (Singing) Under the tent, the general's command; he knows not Jing Ke's fury (憤). I just swallow the fury in my heart, put on a smiling face, and intend to see the General.
Qin Wuyang, you must keep your heart calm, do not tremble in fright under any circumstances (千萬).
Come here. Come here. Let us enter the tent together. (Enters the tent)
Appearing before the General (將軍台前).
(Speaking) Greetings to the General.
Meng: Are you the envoy from Yan?
Jing: That is correct.
Meng: Seeing the commander (本帥), why do you not kneel?
Jing: An envoy has arrived with ceremony (禮); the General should rightly come out of the tent to greet him. Yet the General himself commits a discourtesy (失禮) and instead blames the envoy. What kind of reasoning (道理) is that?
Meng: You are an envoy of a small and weak state (小國弱國); how dare you dispute ceremony with a great state (大國)? Enough talk. Drag him over here. Let the commander take a closer look!
(A soldier pushes Jing Ke from behind.)
Meng: A bold fellow. What kind of envoy are you? You clearly came as an assassin (刺客)! What else could it be?
(Wuyang is greatly startled. Jing Ke signals him to stop.)
Jing: Ha ha, the General calls me an assassin. What evidence (證據) do you have?
Meng: You carry a weapon (凶器) on your body, and still say you are not an assassin?
Jing: What weapon?
Meng: Let me search you.
Jing: Search me!<277>
(Soldiers search him.)
Meng: What is inside the box (箱子)?
Jing: This is the head of Fan Yuqi (樊於期). Fan Yuqi committed a crime against His Majesty. His Majesty wished to capture and behead him, offering a reward of a thousand gold pieces (千金) for his head. Unexpectedly, this man fled to our humble country (敝國). The ruler of our humble country immediately had him killed and sent me to present his head to His Majesty.
Meng: Open it! (Opens and looks) The Yan envoy lies!
Jing: What lie?
Meng: Unfold that map!
(Wuyang becomes very frightened)
Jing: (Calmly unfolds the scroll halfway.) General, please look!
Meng: Ah, so that's how it was. Then speak.
Jing: (Quietly rolls up the scroll and bows his head) General, Yan and Qin are brother nations. This time, I have received the mission to come and offer half of the entire country to Qin, seeking eternal peace. The General should rightly treat me with courtesy (禮). Who would have thought I would be treated like this? Perhaps the feudal lords (諸侯) of the world will feel alarmed and not come to pay tribute!
Meng: Envoy! Earlier, I was testing whether there was any treachery (奸詐). I was discourteous (失禮). I apologize. Please, sit down!
(They laugh together) Ha ha ha!
Jing: Not at all (千萬), ha ha ha!
Meng: I was very discourteous earlier, please forgive me!
Jing: That is part of the General's duty (職責), perfectly appropriate (宜當). How could it be discourteous? By the way, when might I be able to see His Majesty?
Meng: His Majesty heard that Yan is ceding land and was extremely pleased, wishing to meet the envoy in person. It would be best for the envoy (大夫) to return to the guesthouse (館驛) for now and await the imperial decree (傳旨).
Jing: Then I shall take my leave!
Meng: Very well then!
Jing: This is truly "With iron shoulders bearing heavy responsibility (自有鐵肩擔重任), relying entirely on courage and strategy to handle perilous situations (全憑膽略處危難)." (Exits)
Meng: I must report to His Majesty.

(Jing Ke speaks while rising and pointing to the map. As the map fully unfolds, the dagger (匕首) is revealed. The King of Qin jumps up startled. Jing Ke grabs his sleeve. The sleeve tears. King Qin: How dare you come to stab me?
Jing: How could there be only one Jing Ke in the world? Death is before my eyes, yet you still don't realize it. Truly laughable! Ha ha ha.
King Qin: Bring someone with some strength (力量). You don't count. Ha ha ha.
Jing: Why do you laugh?
King Qin: I laugh because the state of Yan, with its millions of people divided by millions of minds, entrusted the great matter of the state's rise or fall (興亡大計) to a single assassin. Ha ha.
Jing: I did not come solely for the sake of Yan, but for the sake of the world, to eliminate tyranny (暴虐)!
King Qin: In Qin, superiors and subordinates are of one mind (上下가 한마음). Even if I die once, it doesn't matter. Jing Ke, do you still have courage?
Jing: Forget heavy wounds; even if my bones are ground to powder (粉骨碎身), my heroic ambition (雄心) will remain forever, and my courage will endure eternally!
King Qin: Fine. I will grant your wish! (Orders the dagger brought and gives it to him. Jing Ke takes the dagger in his hand.)
Jing: Ying Zheng (嬴政)! Are you telling me to commit suicide (自殺)? I am no coward (懦夫); how could I commit suicide? I still have breath left; I intend to fight. Watch out for my blade! (Throws the dagger at the King of Qin. The King, enraged, raises his sword, kills him, then stands holding the sword, trembling.)
King Qin [error, likely should be Official]: Your Majesty, please rest. Your Majesty was startled. It is our fault, your servants' (臣等). -
Your Majesty usually kills people without the slightest change of heart (動心), why are you like this today?
All Officials [error, likely should be King Qin]: What do you know? In all matters under Heaven (天下萬事), there is nothing that cannot be accomplished if someone resolves to die trying. If there were just a few more men like Jing Ke, the world would no longer be at peace (便安)! (Curtain)<280>

**Summary**

Acts 3-5 of the play depict the final preparations and execution of Jing Ke's assassination attempt on the King of Qin. Act 3 shows Jing Ke's internal conflict between his affection for the courtesan Yu Ji and his deadly mission, culminating in him killing her at her request to remove any lingering attachments. Prince Dan provides the map hiding the dagger and the nervous young warrior Qin Wuyang as an assistant. Act 4 portrays the solemn farewell ceremony at the Yishui River, where Jing Ke sings his famous lament, fully aware he will not return. Act 5 shows Jing Ke and Qin Wuyang navigating the Qin court, first bluffing their way past General Meng Jia, then entering the presence of the King of Qin. During the presentation of the map, the assassination attempt unfolds, but fails due to Qin Wuyang's fear, the King's quick reaction, and court intervention. Jing Ke is mortally wounded but defiant to the end, ultimately killed by the King himself, who reflects uneasily on the power of such desperate courage.

From a historical perspective, the publication of this translated Chinese play in 1935 Korea is interesting. It presents a classic story of resistance against a powerful, unifying state (Qin), which might have resonated subtly with Korean audiences under Japanese rule. The themes of sacrifice for one's state (or a greater cause), the tension between personal feelings and duty, and the portrayal of a determined individual challenging overwhelming power could be interpreted in the context of Korean nationalism and anti-colonial sentiment, even if presented through a historical Chinese narrative. The translation itself, by Yang Paekhwa, signifies cultural exchange between China and Korea during this period.

음악을 알외면서 開幕, 臺後에 또 細樂이 잇다. 太醫가 막 診脈하고나서 廂下에서 藥을 끄낸다.
太醫. 大王洪福이라 上天保佑하사 이만하온 微恙은 藥이 아니라도 나옵시지마는 다만 족음 補하옵실 것을 쓰시옵는 것도 無妨하옵니다.
(藥을 가저 王에게 들인다. 王이 太醫에게 먼저 맛보게 하고 그런뒤에 족음 마신다. 蒙嘉가 올라온다.)
秦王. 드르니 燕國使臣이 왓다는 구나. 어째와 報하지를 앗느냐? (將軍이 급히 아프로 나온다.)
蒙. 臣이 殿閣모통이에서 伺侯한지가 오래오나 傳敎를 밧잡지 못하와 감히 殿上에 올으지 못하얏사옵니다. 大王께 알외옵니다. 燕國使臣荊軻와 副使秦舞揚이 地圖를 밧치라 왓사옵니다.
秦王. 그 樊於期머리는?
蒙. 그것도 밧치러 왓사옵니다.
秦王. 사신을 들어오라해라.
蒙. 네의!
秦王. 가만히 잇거라!
蒙. -
秦王. 使臣의 몸을 뒤저보앗느냐?
蒙. 다 뒤저보앗사옵니다.
秦王. 가 불러라!
蒙. 大王의 傳敎시다. 燕使를 올라오랍신다!
(荊軻와 舞揚이 올라온다. 서로 보며 神氣를 짓는다. 秦王이 殿上에서 가만히 傳令하니 衆臣이 모다 알아듯고 荊軻가 막 殿上에 올으자 衆臣이 크게 외친다.)
衆. 燕使가 詐가 잇다!
(舞揚이 이 소리를 듯고 놀라 주춤한다. 荊軻는 從容自若히)
荊. 臣荊軻는 謁見이요!
衆. 燕使는 詐가 잇다!
(荊軻가 舞揚을 본다.)
荊. 小國의 신하가 大國의 威嚴을 본 일이 업사와 우숩사옵니다!
(荊軻가 舞揚의 손에서 箱子를 바다 秦王에게 밧친다. 秦王이 侍臣을 식히어 열어보고 가저가게하니 荊軻가 또 地圖를 밧친다.)
荊. 이것은 燕國의 地圖이옵니다. 무릇 그림에 잇는 땅은 모다 大王의 所有가 되는 것이옵니다. 다만 大王께 바라옵기는＜279＞ 小國의 太子가 大體를 아옵지 못하와 偶然히 大王께 得罪하온 것을 諒察하옵시와 誅戮을 加하옵지 아니하옵시면 感恩無窮하옵겟사옵니다!
(秦王이 그림을 펴며 웃는다.)
秦王. 너희 나라의 太子가 능히 제 분수를 안다면야 寡人이 어찌 諒察하지 안흘理가 잇느냐?
荊. 地圖우에 한 곳 똑똑히 그리지 아니한 곳이 잇사옵니다. 臣이 집겟사옵니다.
(荊軻가 일변 말하면서 일변 일어나서 그림을 가르친다. 그림이 다 펴지자 匕首가 뵈힌다. 秦王이 놀라 일어난다. 荊軻가 그 소매를 잡는다. 소매가 끈허진다. 秦王이 칼을 빼나 나오지 아니하야 기둥을 안코 돈다. 荊軻가 딸흔다. 太醫가 藥箱子로 荊軻를 친다. 秦舞揚은 놀라 꼼작을 못한다.)
臣甲. (趙高) 大王은 빨리 칼을 등으로 돌라빼십시요!
(混戰한 결과, 荊軻는 重傷을 입어 손이 끈허지고 다리를 傷하야 기둥을 의지하야 딱 섯다. 衛士가 秦舞揚을 죽이다.
秦王. 네가 焉敢히 와 찔으는가?
荊. 天下에 어찌 荊軻 한 사람 뿐이랴? 죽엄이 눈압헤 잇거늘 네 그래도 아지 못하니 참으로 우수운 일이다! 허 허 허.
秦王. 좀 力量이 잇는 자를 오래라. 너는 셈 안된다. 허허허.
荊. 너 웨 웃느냐?
秦王. 내 燕나라가 千萬사람이 千萬마음으로 나뉘어 국가의 興亡大計를 一個刺客에게 부탁한 것을 웃는다. 허 허.
荊. 내 전혀 燕國을 위하야 온 것이 아니라 天下를 위하야 暴虐을 업새자는 것이다!
秦王. 秦나라는 上下가 한마음이니 내가 한번 죽는 대도 無妨하다. 荊軻야. 너 그래도 용기가 잇느냐?
荊. 重傷은 말고 粉骨碎身할지라도 雄心은 永히 잇고 용기는 길이 남아잇다!
秦王. 오냐. 네 원대로 해주마! (匕首를 가저오라하야 이를 준다. 荊軻가 칼바더 손에 들엇다.)
荊. 嬴政아. 네가 나더러 自殺하라느냐? 내가 懦夫가 아니니 어찌 自殺을 한단 말이냐? 내 아즉 숨이 남앗스니 싸호랸다. 너 칼 봐라! (칼을 가저 秦王에게 던진다. 秦王이 怒하야 칼을 들어 이를 죽이고 칼을 집고 떤다.)
秦王. 大王은 쉬십시요. 大王이 놀라섯사옵니다, 臣等의 죄이옵니다.-
大王께서 平日에는 사람을 죽이시는데 족음도 動心을 아니하옵시더니 오날은 어찌하야 저와 가티 하오십니까?
衆臣. 너희가 무어를 알랴, 天下萬事는 단지 사람이 죽으랴고 만들면 못할 것이 업는 것이다. 荊軻가튼 자가 더 몃 사람만 잇스면 天下는 고만 便安치 못할 것이다! (幕)＜280＞
