**Body Text Translation:**
Autumn Literature

Song of Harvest

Now I must leave here
No, no, I cannot leave
Until that violin sounds
To the Hungarian, wine
To the Slovak, brandy
To the German, beer
Offer it, offer it
Even if leaving, I must leave

This song is sung by the "Gypsy" (집시) tribe in Southern Europe (南歐) after the harvest is all finished.<217>

**Summary**

This article presents a short poem titled "Song of Harvest," attributed to Gypsy people in Southern Europe singing after the harvest season. The poem expresses a reluctance to leave a place but an acceptance of the necessity, marked by the offering of different alcoholic drinks (wine, brandy, beer) to various European nationalities (Hungarians, Slovaks, Germans) before departure, seemingly waiting for a violin signal. From a historical perspective, the introduction of a European folk song (specifically Gypsy) in a Korean magazine during the colonial period is interesting. It might reflect a broader cultural curiosity about Europe or potentially resonate with themes of wandering, displacement, and the bittersweet nature of endings, which could have held subtle parallels for contemporary Korean readers, even if presented simply as foreign folklore.

淸秋文藝
收獲의 노래
이제는 여기서 떠나야하지
아니 아니 떠나지는 못하겟네
저기 바요링이 울니기까지는
항가리 사람에게는 포도주를

독일사람에게는 삐루를
갓다바치고 갓다바치고
떠나드래도 떠나야지요

이 노래는 收獲이 다 끗난 뒤 南歐에서 「집시」族들이 부르는 것이다.＜217＞
