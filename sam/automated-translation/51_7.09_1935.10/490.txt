Article Title: 잔이나 듭소서 (Raise a Glass)
Author: 金岸曙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Raise a Glass
						Kim Ansŏ

A man's heart
Is hard to trust <240>
A woman's love
Changes easily, they say.
People yesterday and today,
Turning away,
All sigh and sigh,
Lamenting their sorrows.

The untrustworthy man
Is terribly missed,
And the easily changing love
Is cruel indeed,
On this evening today
When wind and rain stir,
People raise their glasses
And sing. <241>
<240-241>

**Summary**

The poem expresses a melancholic view of love, lamenting the untrustworthy nature of men's hearts and the fickle nature of women's affection. Despite this awareness, the speaker notes the persistent longing for the unreliable lover and the pain caused by changeable love. Ultimately, people seek solace from these sorrows by drinking and singing together, especially on a stormy evening. From a historical perspective, this poem, published in 1935 during the Japanese colonial period, reflects a focus on personal emotion and perhaps escapism. While not overtly political, the expression of universal human sorrows like heartbreak might have resonated with readers experiencing broader societal hardships and restrictions, offering a shared sense of melancholy or a temporary distraction.

잔이나 듭소서
						金岸曙
사나히의 마음은
밋기어렵고＜240＞
애女子의 사랑은
變키쉽다고.
어제오늘 사람들
똘아서면서
모도다 탄식탄식
설어를 하네.

밋지못할 사내가
못내 그립고
變키쉬운 사랑이
야속도하야,
바람비 뒤설네는
오늘 이저녁
사람들 잔을들고
노래하거니. ＜241＞
