기사제목	人氣歌手의 藝術·私生活·戀愛 (Art, Private Life, and Love of Popular Singers)

**Art, Private Life, and Love of Popular Singers**

**Mr. Ko Poksu, Who Aspires to Build a Music School**

The sweltering summer, when the mercury in Seoul hovered around 100 degrees, has somehow vanished far to the south, and the mournful cry of the crickets in the morning and evening must sound even sadder to a single person living alone.
Now, as autumn deepens, the sky is high without a single cloud, the moon is bright on this long autumn night, and somewhere, the faint sound of a sad song (梨園哀曲) quietly pierces the heart of the heartbroken.
This singing voice is clearly a passage from a song sung by Mr. Ko Poksu, the popular singer on the streets of Seoul these days.
As I listened intently with fluttering ears, it was the sound of a record faintly leaking from under the deep pink curtain of the room across the way, beyond the neighbor's wall.
On this long autumn night, if I could become the spirit of a cricket, gently lift that pink curtain, and peek inside,<98> in a corner of that cozy room, I would surely discover the radiant figure of a lovely maiden trembling with a sorrowful tune!
Lost in these rambling thoughts of autumn melancholy, I turned past the Ch'ŏndogyo Church (天道敎堂) protruding from a back alley of Chongno and entered the inn where Mr. Ko Poksu was staying.

**A Music School of 300,000 Won**

Me: When did you start learning music?
Ko: Before I entered Pusan Commercial School (釜山實業學校). After graduating from Tongnae Normal School (東萊普通學校), I first began learning music from the wife of the principal of Ilsin School (一新學校), Mrs. Tae Kyo (a Westerner).
Me: How long did you learn from that lady?
Ko: Probably about two years.
Me: And did you not attend a music school after that?
Ko: I couldn't attend what you'd call a music school. Even now, I always regret not being able to attend a music school. I still want to learn more, but it's not working out that way. That's why I'm always thinking I'd like to establish a music school in Korea (朝鮮). Truly, there are many unknown music lovers in Korea with abundant musical talent and promising futures. I believe that if we gather such people and skillfully nurture their innate talents, world-class musicians will emerge.
Me: So, is your future ideal to establish a music school?
Ko: Yes, that's right. If I had about 300,000 won, I would definitely like to establish a music school. Also, educational projects are good, and I would like to do social work too.

**Prefers a Kindergarten Teacher Most**

Me: Are you married?
Ko: Yes, I already am. It was when I was 21. She's a woman eight years older than me.
Me: But you're not living together?
Ko: My wife is currently ill and has gone down to her hometown.
Me: Then you must be very lonely.
Ko: Yes, I suppose I am lonely. Haha…
Me: Now, or perhaps in the future, what kind of person would you like to have as a lover?
Ko: A kindergarten teacher is best. It would be nice if she had some musical talent and knew methods of child education. And she should be someone who can console me, who is wrapped in loneliness and solitude.

**Selection in Columbia Competition Was First Start**

Me: When did you become a popular singer?
Ko: That was the year, I forget which, when Columbia Records held a music competition. By some good fortune, I was selected. After that, I had a contract <99> with Columbia Records where they would pay 40 won for recording both sides, A and B, of a record. But later, due to personal reasons, I quit that place.
Me: When did you join O.K. [Records]?
Ko: That was last May.
Me: So, your fame grew so much in just one year. How many records have you recorded so far?
Ko: Probably about 50 discs.
Me: What recordings do you feel most confident about so far?
Ko: I don't really have confidence in any. Among them, ones that might be considered slightly better are "Yi Wŏn Ae Kok" (梨園哀曲 - Sad Song of the Pear Garden), "T'ahyangsari" (타향사리 - Living Away From Home), "Hwip'aram" (휘파람 - Whistle), "Oeroun Kkum" (외로운 꿈 - Lonely Dream), and such.
Me: What was the happiest moment during your time as a singer?
Ko: The happiest time is when I test a recording after making it, and also when, in a quiet and scenic place like the Han River (漢江) or the outskirts of the city, some unknown young people sing the songs I recorded. That feels good.
Me: What path will you take in the future?
Ko: For the sake of art, I will devote my life to this field I am responsible for.
After this conversation, imagining him leaning against the cold window of his guest room on an autumn night, alone, vaguely picturing his unknown lover (?) from that country in his mind, his young heart yearning endlessly, I shook his hand.
In which country might the kindergarten teacher he longs for be? Please, listen carefully for the mournful tune of "Hwip'aram" drifting from a corner of the guest house!
Hoping that the grand ideal of Mr. Ko Poksu, the popular man of the streets, will be realized even a day sooner, I turned and emerged onto the back alley of Chongno, deepening amidst the crossing neon signs.<100>

**Chŏn Ok, Like a Flowering Quince Dozing in the Spring Breeze and Gentle Rain**

While the scenery of the East Sea, where the fragrant scent of the flowering quince drifts on the breeze and two or three white seagulls rise and dip beyond Myŏngsa Simni's silken sea, would be lovely,
The women of Hamhŭng (咸興) town, who frolic cheerfully and smile brightly on the fertile and vast green fields of the "Hamhŭng Plain" (咸興平野), perhaps imbued with the spirit of the mystical Mt. Pallyong (盤龍山) and the silk-like Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River (淸川江), have seen countless beautiful blossoms bloom and fade, bloom and fade, continuously from ancient times until now.
Twenty-four years ago from now, within the fence of a poor house in this town, a lovely flowering quince blossom sprouted. This single blossom grew beautifully, whether the spring grass was green on the slopes of Mt. Pallyong, or the moonlight shimmered on the waters of the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River, or the snowstorm whirling over Mt. Mach'ŏn (摩天嶺) covered the vast expanse of the Hamhŭng Plain, growing spotlessly clean—her name is Chŏn Ok (全玉).
Her face, born with the spirit of the northern peaks, washed and rinsed by the blue waves of the East Sea.
With those sparkling eyes! Since childhood, how many times did she pick various grasses in spring, and on moonlit autumn nights, lean against the railing of Mansae Bridge (萬歲橋), singing in a lovely voice like a nightingale?
Once captivating the hearts of countless young men and women on the silver screen, and for a time on the stage,<101> she has now turned her heels towards being a star of records, her popularity still deeply stirring the hearts of the peninsula's youth, producing melodies that roll like jade.
It has already been ten singers that this reporter has sought out among the popular singers of the peninsula. On this day, I decided to visit Ms. Chŏn Ok, who has built a love nest with Mr. Kang Hongsik (姜弘植) in a neat and charming Western-style house just outside Seoul's East Gate.
The myriad colorful flowers and plants planted in front of the garden exude a fragrant scent carried by the gentle, cool breeze.
Guided by a young girl, I entered the reception room decorated in a modern style.
Seeing her in the house, one could tell she was a modest housewife, her attire simple and neat.
Like white waves surging over red lips, her jade-like white teeth appeared, and a slight smile rippled across her cheeks. Leaning against the flower-patterned mat she brought out, I began asking about her love life, art, and private life.

**From Dancer to Singer**

Me: When did you first appear on stage?
Chŏn: It was probably quite a long time ago. I think it was when I was 15.
Me: Then, at the young age of 15, right when you should have been studying, how did you end up stepping onto the stage?
Chŏn: Well, the circumstances were like that back then.
Me: What kind of circumstances were they?
Chŏn: What's the use of talking about that? After graduating from elementary school at 15, I had no choice but to just stay at home. Because, unlike others, I didn't have money, I couldn't study even if I wanted to. So, after spending a year just staying at home, my parents must have secretly arranged for me to get married. Even with my young heart back then, I was resentful, and the thought of it was so frightening that I just wanted to leave home. Just then, a certain "film crew" (촤령단) happened to come to Hamhŭng to shoot. I, who always loved singing and always had the desire to make large audiences of hundreds of thousands cry and laugh before many people, secretly joined as an actress without my parents knowing. My hopes back then were truly immense. In my young girl's heart back then, the ideal of becoming an artist renowned throughout the world filled my narrow chest. Naturally, the marriage issue fizzled out and disappeared.
Me: That was your first start as a film actress, then.<102>
Chŏn: That's right.
Me: When did you start as this popular singer?
Chŏn: It must be about 4 years now. Since I was 21.
Me: Even while living as a singer now, don't you miss your former stage life?
Chŏn: How could I not miss it? Although I suffered a lot and sometimes shed many tears, I deeply miss that stage life back then. Especially the times when Mr. Kang (姜) and I were counterparts in the same troupe, passing each other, becoming affectionate towards each other, shedding tears and pledging our love—even now, when I reminisce about those times, sometimes a smile naturally spreads across my lips without me realizing it.
Me: When you were living the stage life, what was the happiest moment?
Chŏn: The most exhilarating time was stepping onto the stage in the freezing winter with snow blowing, my cheeks freezing and feet so cold I had to stomp them, and receiving applause from a full house.
Me: Don't you get that kind of exhilarating feeling as a singer now?
Chŏn: You can't experience scenes like those felt on stage again. However, when the records I've sung are played on the streets by young people, I still feel happy.
Me: Which company were you with initially?
Chŏn: It was Polydor Records (포리돌 회사). Then last year, I moved to Victor Records (빅타-회사), recorded maybe twice, and then went back to Polydor after all.

**A Life Dedicated to Art**

Me: Do you have any thoughts of returning to the stage again?
Chŏn: Well, I want to return, but how can it happen just like that? If a good opportunity comes, of course, I'll go back on stage once more.
Me: How many records have you recorded to date?
Chŏn: I don't remember exactly, but it must be around 100 doubles (meaning 100 records, with A/B sides).
Me: You must have earned quite a lot of money too.
Chŏn: What's the use of earning a lot? Since I spend a lot, it all just gets spent on living expenses.
Me: Are you planning to continue walking the path of art indefinitely?
Chŏn: Well, the last day will probably be when my hair turns white and I return to the netherworld.
Me: When singers or actors go out on the streets, they usually seem to dress up, but Ms. Chŏn Ok, don't you want to wear fancy clothes?
Chŏn: Well, when appearing on stage or in front of the camera, it can't be helped, but when I'm just at home, is there really a need to do so?

**My Private Life**

Me: Since both of you are in the same artistic field, your life together must be quite interesting.<103>
Chŏn: Yes, that's right. He also records as a singer, so we share the same profession. Because of that, we understand and help each other in a special way, truly building a very harmonious home.
Me: Where do you find enjoyment in your home life?
Chŏn: Placing the little one in the middle and watching their cute antics while we burst into laughter is the best. Next, I enjoy playing the organ (오루강 - organ) to match his singing. Hohoho.
Me: What kind of reading do you enjoy?
Chŏn: In my spare time, I read novels, and among Korean magazines, I enjoy reading "Sin Kajŏng" (新家庭 - New Home) and "Samch'ŏlli" (三千里).
Me: As for food, what do you...
Chŏn: Oh my, what strange questions... Hohoho. I like kimchi more than meat.
While we talked back and forth, the sun setting in the west passed over the peak of Mt. Kŭmhwa (金華山), casting a red glow that painted the west-facing glass window of this room red. I abruptly stood up, came out into the garden, and hoping that the fragrance of this eternally beautiful singer, this thousand-year artist's home, would linger on and on, I disappeared into the crowd on the twilight avenue stained red by the evening glow.

**Summary**

The article features interviews with two popular Korean singers of 1935, Ko Poksu and Chŏn Ok. Ko Poksu discusses his musical beginnings, his ambition to build a 300,000 won music school in Korea to nurture talent, his marriage to an older woman currently away due to illness, and his ideal partner being a kindergarten teacher. He reflects on his start via a Columbia Records competition, his popular songs like "T'ahyangsari," and finding joy in hearing strangers sing his songs. Chŏn Ok recounts running away from an arranged marriage at 15 to join a film crew, her transition from stage/screen actress to a Polydor/Victor recording artist, her happy marriage to fellow artist Kang Hongsik, and her simple domestic joys like playing the organ and reading magazines. Both express lifelong dedication to their art.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into the aspirations and lifestyles of popular entertainers in colonial Korea. Ko Poksu's dream of a Korean music school highlights a desire for national cultural development amidst colonial rule. The mention of specific record labels (Columbia, O.K., Polydor, Victor) and song titles maps the landscape of the burgeoning recording industry. Chŏn Ok's journey from avoiding arranged marriage to becoming an actress and singer reflects changing social norms and opportunities for women, while their domestic lives reveal a blend of traditional values (kimchi preference) and modern aspirations (artistic careers, modern home). The apology concerning Yi Nanyŏng and T'aep'yŏng Company also provides a glimpse into journalistic practices and the entertainment scene of the time.

人氣歌手의 藝術·私生活·戀愛
音樂學校建設에 뜻을 둔 高福壽氏
서울 장안 水銀柱가 100度에서 오르내리든 무더운 여름도 어느듯 남쪽으로 멀니 사라저 버리고, 아침저녁 귀뜨람이의 처량이 우는 소리 홀로 있는 독신자에게 더욱 구슬프게만 들여올 것이다.
인제 가을도 바야흐로 깁히저 가는 이즈음, 하늘은 구름 한점 없이 높을대로 높고, 달은 밝아 秋夜長인데, 어데서인가, 고요히 「梨園哀曲」의 가느다란 노래 소리가 단장인의 가슴을 더욱 저리게 들여오고 있다.
이 노래 소리는 분명히, 요지음 서울거리의 人氣歌手 高福壽씨가 부르는 노래 꼭조의 한 구절이다.
나는 가만히 설네이든 귀를 기우리노라니 담 넘어 엽집의 건넌방 그윽한 연분홍 카-뎅 밑으로 간간히 새여 나오는 레코-드의 소리다.
秋夜長 긴긴 밤인데, 내 만약 귀뜨람이의 넋이 되어 그 연분홍 카-뎅을 슬며-시 들치고 엿서볼 수 있다면,＜98＞ 그윽한 그 방 한편 구석에서는, 哀調에 떠는 어엽분 처녀의 영롱한 자태를 발견하리라!
이러틋 秋思의 두서없는 생각에 잠기면서, 鍾路의 뒷골목에 삐죽하게 솟은 天道敎堂 엽길을 돌아, 高福壽씨가 있는 旅舘집 문안으로 들어섯다.

콜놈비아 콩쿨 入選이 첫 出發로

高. 바로 작년 5월임니다.
나. 그럼 1년이란 동안에 그렇게 명성이 놉하젓소이다 그려. 입때까지 몃판이나 吹込하엿나요.
高. 아마 한 50枚쯤 될걸요.
나. 지금까지 가장 자신잇게 吹込되엿다고 생각하는 것은 무엇무엇인가요.
高. 자신이란게 없슴니다. 그 중에서 조곰 낫다고 할 만한 것으로 「梨園哀曲」과 「타향사리」 「휘파람」 「외로운 꿈」 등이라고 하겟지요.
나. 그렇게 가수로 내려오는 동안 제일 깁뿐 때는 언제엿서요.
高. 제일 깁뿐 때는 吹込하여서 테스트한 때이고, 또 漢江이나, 교외의 조용하고 경치 조흔데서, 어떤 이름 모를 젊은이들이 내가 吹込한 노래를 부를 때가 조트군요.
나. 장차는 어떤 길로 나가겟서요.
高. 좀더 예술을 위하야, 나의 마튼 이 方面에 몸을 기우려 일생을 받치겟서요.
이러한 대화가 잇슨 후, 가을밤, 외로히 客舍의 寒窓에 기대여 앉어 물끄럼이 저 나라 未知의 愛人(?)을 머릿속에 그려보며, 하염없는 젊은이의 가슴을 애태우고 있을 그를 머리속에 그려보며 나는 그에게 손목을 나누웟다.
어느 나라에 그가 그리워하는 유치원의 보모가 잇슴인지, 청컨댄 그대는 客舍一隅에서 「휘파람」의 구슲은 노래곡조 들여오는 것을 조심성잇게 들어볼 지어다!
거리의 人氣男兒 高福壽씨의 그 크다란 이상이 하루라도 속히 실현되기를 바래면서 네온, 싸인의 交叉속에 깁허저 가는 종로의 뒷골목길을 돌아저 나왓다.＜100＞

東風細雨에 조으는 海棠花格인 全玉
해당화 향그러운 내음새가 바람에 쌔워 풍겨지며, 明沙十里 저편 명주바다 우으로는 흰 갈매기 두, 서너 마리 떳다 잠겻다하는 東海바다의 풍경도 좋으련만,
기름찌고 넓다란 「咸興平野」 푸른 들팔에서 쾌활하게 날뛰며, 명랑하게 우슴짓는 咸興고을의 녀자들은 영그러운 「반룡산」과 비단껼같은 「淸川江」의 정긔를 타고낫슴인지, 예로 붙어 이제까지 수 많은 아름다운 꽃송이들이 피였다가는 사라지고 피였다가는 사라지기를 수없이 게속하고 있다.
지금으로붙어 24년 전, 이 고을 한 가난한 집 울타리 안에서 에업븐 海棠花 한 송이가 도다낫다. 이 꽃 한 송이가 고이 자라, 반룡산 언덕에 봄풀이 푸를 때나, 청천강 물 우에 달빛이 너훌거릴 때나, 「마천령」으로 휘모라 넘는 눈보래가 함흥평야 넓다란 벌판을 뒤덥흘 때나 티끌하나 뭇지 않고 곱다랗게 자랏스니, 그의 이름  全玉이다.
北國 준령의 영긔를 타고난 그 얼골모습, 東海蒼波에 씻기우고 씻기워 젓다.
그 반짝이는 두 눈동자!를 가진 그는 어린 시절붙어, 봄이면 가진 잔디풀을 뜨으며, 달 밝은 가을 밤이면 「만세교」란간에 긔대여 서서, 꾀꼴새같은 귀여운 목소리로 노래 부르기를 그 멫 번이였든고?
한 때는 銀幕우에서 또 한동안은 舞臺우에서＜101＞ 수 많은 젊은 남녀의 가슴을 흔들어 놓든 그는, 이제 다시 레코-드의 花形으로 발굼치를 돌였스니, 그의 인기는 아즉 半島의 젊은이들의 가슴에 깁히 사모칠, 玉같이 구으는 음률을 자아내고 있다.
기자가 지금까지 半島의 인기가수를 찾기 벌서 10氏이였다. 이러한 오늘에야, 서울 장안 東大門 밖으로 샛틋하고 아담하게 꾸며진 양옥으로 姜弘植씨와 사랑의 보금자리를 일우워 지내는 全玉씨를 찾기로 하였다.
뜰 앞으로 심어저 있는 千紫萬紅의 各色花草는 가비얍게 불어오는 시원한 바람결을 타고 향그러운 내음새를 풍겨준다.
어린 소녀의게 안내를 받이 모단풍으로 꾸며저 있는 응접실로 들어섰다.
집안에서 보는 그는 한 가정의 얌전한 주부임을 알만큼 그 몸차림이 간결하고 말쑥하게 뵈였다.
불근 입설우으로 흰 파도가 처드러오드시 玉같이 하이얀 잇발이 나타나며 그의 두 뺨에는 미소가 약간 물결친다. 이여 내여놓는 꽃 노흔 돗자리에 몸을 기대이고 그의 연애, 예술, 사생활에 대하야 뭇기를 시작하였다.
