Samch'ŏlli "Wall Newspaper" No. 2, Korean Cadres within the Government-General

The Korean cadres within the Government-General of Korea are as follows, from which one can clearly see in which areas the supreme governing body employs Korean officials.

Governor's Secretariat Cadre: 15 persons total, Korean: None
Home Affairs Bureau Cadre: 34 persons total, Korean: 1
Finance Bureau Cadre: 18 persons total, Korean: None
Industrial Promotion Bureau Cadre: 33 persons total, Korean: 1
Judicial Bureau Cadre: 3 persons total, Korean: None
Educational Affairs Bureau Cadre: 10 persons total, Korean: 3
Police Affairs Bureau Cadre: 15 persons total, Korean: None
Forestry Bureau Cadre: 11 persons total, Korean: None
Land Improvement Department Cadre: 16 persons total, Korean: None
Total: 155 persons total, 5 <7>

**Summary**

This news brief from the September 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine lists the number of Korean officials holding cadre (幹部) positions within the various bureaus of the Japanese Government-General of Korea. It reveals that out of 155 total cadre positions across major departments, only 5 were held by Koreans, predominantly in the Educational Affairs Bureau (3), with one each in Home Affairs and Industrial Promotion. Historians might find this data valuable as a stark, quantitative illustration of the limited extent to which Koreans were integrated into the higher levels of the colonial administration, particularly outside of culturally-focused areas like education, highlighting the structural limitations placed on Korean participation in governance during this period.
