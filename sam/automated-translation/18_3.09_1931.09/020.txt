Article Title: 朝鮮新文化建設 三個年計劃 (Three-Year Plan for the Construction of a New Korean Culture)
Author: 三千里社主幹 (Samch'ŏlli Company Editor), 安在鴻 (An Chae-hong), 宋鎭禹 (Song Chin-u), 朱耀燮 (Chu Yo-sŏp)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Argument)

Three-Year Plan for the Construction of a New Korean Culture

Within the scope permitted by current conditions, and under the constraint of a fixed timeframe, couldn't we, with the united effort of everyone, armed with fiery enthusiasm and iron-like discipline, achieve the New Culture we must possess in a "blitzkrieg" manner? The fragmented experiments in various fields over the past few years seem to promise success in this regard.

Now, even within what we call New Culture, there are various sectors such as National Health, Social Movements, Artistic Activities, etc., but this time we have decided to pour all our energy solely into breaking down illiteracy, which is the prerequisite for all movements, and to compile and publish a 3-year plan for it. In the future, our discussions should naturally extend to all sectors of culture, and furthermore, in the next issue, we plan to continue publishing a 3-Year Plan for New Economic Construction centered on the Consumer Cooperative and Cooperative Union movements. We hope that those concerned about the future of Korea will follow this with sincere discussion and implementation. (Samch'ŏlli Company Editor) <2>

14 Million Illiterate People and the Mass Culture Movement

Chosŏn Ilbo and Literacy Dissemination

An Chae-hong, Chosŏn Ilbo Company
The Chosŏn Ilbo aims to closely interact with the masses, focusing on the dissemination of common knowledge and the promotion of public health. It goes without saying that a media organization should not be detached from the masses, and as the promotion of public health has barely taken its first steps, let's not speak prematurely. Instead, I'd like to talk a little about this Literacy Dissemination Movement (文字普及運動), which the Chosŏn Ilbo Company has been pursuing as a continuous project for three years now. Literacy dissemination is the first step in the enlightenment of mass culture, and defining it as a project would be appropriate, but that's not a major issue.

However, I view the Literacy Dissemination Movement as a major undertaking related to the Chosŏn Ilbo's company policy of "action without fanfare" (不言實行). Whenever we implement this project, we issue large company announcements, promote it extensively through articles, advocate for it in editorials, offer specific reward formats to the team members (班員), and even hold grand consolation and award ceremonies, making it a massive publicity event altogether. I know there might be immediate rebuttals asking how this can be "action without fanfare." However, those are merely the administrative procedures for the project's progress; they do not involve discussing or promoting its essence. Simply put,
Knowledge is power
Learn to live!
As expressed in these short slogans, we believe that apart from emphasizing the desire to know and learn, no elaborate promotion is necessary. There are 17 million illiterate people in Korea. Excluding approximately 2 million children below school age and perhaps 1 million elderly individuals and middle-aged or older women who find it difficult to receive new education, about 14 million illiterate people yearn for the light of education. Looking at this through statistical figures, from the primary education institutions:

Korean Population: 20,437,219
Number of Common Schools (普通學校): 1,710
Population per School: 11,952+
Number of Common School Students (Public & Private): 487,878
Population per Student: 42
Total School-Age Children in Korea: 2,450,000
Enrollment Rate per 100 Children: 19.9%

That is, compared to the total number of school-age children, the enrollment rate is less than 20%, meaning nearly 2 million children receive no education at all. According to recently published figures, the total number of Sŏdang (書堂 - traditional village schools) in Korea is 11,469, with 162,247 students. Even adding these to the common school student count results in only 740,630 <3> students. Moreover, many students in Sŏdang education do not learn Hangŭl, so it cannot be considered truly modern mass education.
And even if the number of common schools increases from now on, the surplus of school-age children grows considerably each year, making this kind of instructional education (講習敎育) very necessary.

This Literacy Dissemination Movement first began three years ago, from July 1929. At that time, we prepared one sheet of a lithographed "Hangŭl Primer" (한글원본), team member certificates, and self-certification forms for those who had learned Hangŭl. After promoting it for just over 10 days:
1. Team Member Applicants: 409 people
1. Hangŭl Primers Distributed: 40,000 sheets
were distributed.
In the autumn, based only on reported results:
1. Team Members: 91 people
1. Hangŭl Learners: Approx. 3,000 people
Last year, 1930, there were some preparations made in advance. The "Hangŭl Primer" was made into a relatively detailed textbook of about 20 lessons, published as a standalone book with publication permission. When we recruited team members:
1. Team Member Applicants: Over 900 people
1. Participating Schools (Large, Medium, Small): 46 schools
1. Hangŭl Primers: Approx. 90,000 copies
In the autumn, the reported results were:
1. Team Members: 161 people
1. Hangŭl Learners: 10,567 people
Looking at this, it might not seem like a huge achievement, but for instance, at a certain girls' high school very close to the Chosŏn Ilbo office in the city, many students participated and achieved excellent results individually, yet they didn't submit any reports, saying, "Well, I taught Hangŭl out of automatic enthusiasm for national service, so I don't want to submit a detailed report as if seeking an award." While this is a beautiful sentiment, it's also a great pity from another perspective because, despite breaking down illiteracy, the lack of proper statistical surveys is quite unfortunate. However, based on regular correspondence and other circumstances, including the results of many unreported team members, we estimate that 30,000 illiterate people were educated over the two years. Regardless, this year, the response from schools in Seoul and the provinces has been much better than before; this is not something we boast about in advance, but the mobilization of Literacy Dissemination team members is being reported daily in the Chosŏn Ilbo. This year, we anticipate the reported number of learners to reach thirty-some thousand. Thus, this movement, more than just the reported results, is gaining a very deep understanding among youth circles, and from now on, it will develop with increasing efficiency (所事半功倍 - achieving twice the result with half the effort).

In the future, we plan to continue this on a large scale for another 7 years. During this time, we will not limit ourselves to the simple education of reading and writing Hangŭl but will gradually improve the methods and content step by step. However, in accordance with the principle of "action without fanfare," we will not make empty boasts beforehand. We always tell our team members, "For now, be satisfied with just teaching reading and writing," and proceed accordingly. We are confident that good results will appear within a total of 10 years. <4>

300,000 People in 3 Years

Song Chin-u, Tong-a Ilbo Company
You probably know the general outline through several reports in the Tong-a Ilbo about how the Tong-a Ilbo established a plan to eradicate the countless illiterate people scattered across Korea as part of the movement for constructing a new culture, and as a strategy for its implementation, mobilized male and female students to organize and dispatch Literacy Dissemination Teams (文字普及班).

Now, speaking of the results, since this was the first year of its inception, all preparations were not complete. Additionally, due to various circumstances, the plan was announced only a few days before the summer vacation, leaving no time to make it widely known, so we couldn't achieve the full effect. Nevertheless, the Enlightenment Teams (啓蒙隊) dispatched throughout Korea numbered about one hundred, and through the classes held up to today (August 17th, noon), the number of illiterate masses reached through instruction has been calculated at over 10,700. Here are one or two examples:

P'yŏngan Pukto, Sŏnch'ŏn
Started July 23rd, Ended August 12th
Students: 278 (182 women)

Chŏlla Namdo, Tamyang County, Nam-myŏn, Oedong-ni
August 5th - 18th
Students: 80 men

Ch'ungch'ŏng Namdo, Chinch'ŏn County, Manoe-myŏn, Kwanghyenŭng-ni
July 29th - August 20th
Students: 128 (46 women)

Hwanghae Province, Anak Town
August 2nd - 16th
Students: 263 (152 women)

Hamgyŏng Namdo/Pukto, Pukch'ŏng County, Chungsi-ri
August 3rd - August 20th <5>
Students: 52

Hamgyŏng Namdo, Yŏnghŭng County, Ho'u-ri
August 1st - August 30th
Students: 243 (120 women)

Generally, they accommodate one or two hundred people each, and the classes (講習會) are held with the fervent support of local community leaders.
Considering that there are still two weeks left of summer vacation, and there are many places from which we haven't yet received news of classes, we believe that by the end of the planned period, we will show truly numerous achievements in enlightening the illiterate.
Since the distribution of literacy primers (文字臺本) has already reached over 30,000 copies.

300,000 People in 3 Years

However, this would be an exceptional figure for the first year, and when we anticipate a normal year state again, we feel our determination strengthening.
That is, I think like this. From the second year, next year, we believe that the number of students who understand our company's true intention and lend their strength to this movement will increase dramatically, and the enthusiasm of the illiterate masses for absorbing knowledge will rise with tremendous force. Therefore, we believe that 1,000 Enlightenment Teams will be organized and dispatched annually, and consequently, assuming each Enlightenment Team acquires an average of 100 people from the illiterate masses, the total number will easily reach 100,000.

Furthermore, this should not only utilize the agricultural off-season of July and August but also the winter period when farmers sit at home twisting straw ropes.
Besides this, the movement for the unification and dissemination of "Hangŭl," specifically the spelling system (綴字法), which is being carried out concurrently with the literacy enlightenment movement, is also progressing nationwide under the fervent support of the members of the Korean Language Society (朝鮮語學會).

But how large is the illiterate population currently in all of Korea? Statistics on this would have been good to have already, but it seems even the ruling authorities have yet to conduct a survey, so we have no choice but to resort to estimation. If the total population within Korea is 20 million, about 20% might be literate, meaning the remaining 16 million are unable to read a single character (目不識丁字). Among them, if we exclude the elderly who have already entered senescence, infants, and women in very remote villages from whom social activity cannot possibly be expected – taking them as half the total – the actual number we should target for illiteracy eradication would reach 8 million.

Now, if our company's practical movement, planning for 100,000 people per year, were to achieve its intended goal, proceeding as is would require 80 years. However, who would deny that the enlightenment of the illiterate will leap forward geometrically, as those who learn teach others, and those who learn from them teach yet others?
We intend to believe that this movement will be completed within 10 years, but first, we know that at least 300,000 people will be enlightened in the upcoming 3 years. This is a figure we are confident about.

Expenses Amount to 1,000 Won Annually

Generally, the shameful slogan "Eradicate Illiteracy" (文盲打破) can only be found in Russia when looking back through history. Civilized countries implemented compulsory education systems long ago, ensuring children receive standard education from a young age, so there's no reason for illiteracy among their entire populations. Aren't Britain, France, America, Japan, Germany, etc., all good examples? Instead, illiteracy exists in undeveloped countries or among barbaric peoples where the level of civilization is low and political systems have not reached a high degree.

Eradicating illiteracy is the most preliminary and fundamental movement for all cultural construction. Especially in a place like Korea, where illiteracy occupies 80% of the entire population, how can this be neglected even for a single day?
Initially, our company intended to launch this movement on a large scale four years ago, commemorating the 10th anniversary of our newspaper's founding, but it was halted due to special circumstances related to certain quarters. Today, we have changed the format and initiated the enlightenment movement starting this year.

Lastly, the expenses incurred for this movement so far amount to about 1,000 Won annually. We intend to increase this amount in the future. <6>

800,000 Won Expense for 3 Years

Chu Yo-sŏp

Aiming for 5 Million People

The illiteracy eradication project (文盲退治事業) has seen much effort from the private sector in the meantime. The Chosŏn Ilbo Company's Literacy Dissemination Teams and the Christian Summer Children's Bible Schools have been active for many years, and starting this year, the Tong-a Ilbo Company has also begun its Enlightenment Team movement. These are commendable efforts.

Now, as I take up my pen to lay out a concrete 3-year plan for this, the first thought that naturally comes to mind is the desire for a total mobilization of all these already active institutions. Perhaps that is the easiest and most feasible path.
I believe that if the illiteracy eradication project is implemented systematically for just 3 years, it will be possible to educate approximately 5 million illiterate people.
If it continues like that, it might be possible to make illiteracy disappear in Korea within 10 years.

So, how can 5 million illiterate people be eradicated? First, let's look at the summer temporary schools.
According to the results of the Chosŏn Ilbo Company's Literacy Dissemination Teams, those involved say surpassing 50,000 people annually will be easy in the future. However, since the Tong-a Ilbo also started this year, let's assume the numbers will be somewhat divided between the two, and each newspaper company should be able to surpass about 40,000 people.

And looking at the Summer Children's Bible Schools run annually by Christian groups, the total student count in 1930 was 68,000, of which the number of uneducated children (i.e., children unable to attend school) was 15,400. Therefore, if Christian groups put just a little more effort into accommodating illiterate children rather than currently enrolled students, I think it would be easy to surpass about 30,000 people.

Besides these, another organization in Korea that could cooperate in this great movement is Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎). If Ch'ŏndogyo also utilizes its local propagation centers (布敎所) and mobilizes all its youth groups, handling about 30,000 people would likely be manageable.

Then, we arrive at the numbers shown in the table below:
Tong-a Ilbo: 40,000 people <7>
Chosŏn Ilbo: 40,000 people
Christianity: 30,000 people
Ch'ŏndogyo: 30,000 people
Total: 140,000 people

The above calculation is only for summer schools. However, if we are to undertake the illiteracy eradication project on a large scale, we cannot help but utilize the winter vacation as well. In fact, rural areas have slightly more free time during the winter than in the summer. But during winter, the cold climate means the issue of space will always be a headache.

However, if each organization works during the winter vacation with the same enthusiasm as they do during the summer, I think it would be manageable to accommodate about half the number achieved in the summer. If so, that number would be about 70,000 people.
Thus, the number of children these four major organizations can accommodate in one year reaches 210,000. If this continues for 3 years, the total reaches 630,000.

Next, night schools (夜學) can be established utilizing the buildings of each common school. For a concrete plan regarding this, please refer to my article "Open Night Schools for the Proletariat" (無産夜學을 열나) in the August issue of the monthly magazine Hyesŏng (慧星) (P. 40). In any case, in cities, each school building can accommodate 100% of its capacity for night schools.
However, in rural areas, since attending night school from distant places is impossible, let's assume perfection cannot be expected and postulate that only about half of the accommodation capacity can be met on average. Then, the current total enrollment in Korean common schools is 700,000. Half of that is 350,000. This means that if night schools were opened in Korea now, using only existing school buildings, at least 350,000 people could be accommodated.

Now, since the purpose of these night schools is purely illiteracy eradication, there is no need to continue them for more than 2 months. Two months of night school should be sufficient to provide instruction equivalent to one month of temporary school.
Then, if the school buildings are used for only 8 months a year, it can be divided into 4 terms of 2 months each. Since 350,000 people can be accommodated per term, 4 terms would mean 1.4 million people. If this continues for 3 years, it reaches 4.2 million people. 4.2 million plus 630,000 totals 4.83 million, which is roughly 5 million.

Expenses and Curriculum

Of course, expenses will be borne by each organization themselves, as is currently done. And the minor costs arising in each locality will also be covered locally, as is currently the practice.
The subjects will also be similar to the current ones. Since teaching Hangŭl and literacy is the foundation, that will be the focus. If religious organizations wish to teach their own doctrines, it's better to tacitly permit it without actively stopping them. Since the costs will be entirely borne by that denomination, no objection can be raised there.

Central Committee

To promote the movements of each organization and facilitate mutual assistance, a Central Committee composed of representatives from each organization will be necessary. It seems ideal if a Central Committee were organized with 11 members: 2 representatives elected from each of the 4 major organizations, 2 representatives representing the Hyanggyo (鄕校 - local Confucian schools) (the reason for this will be explained later), plus 2 representatives from society elected by the representative meeting.

Of course, it is best to limit the authority of this Central Committee, much like the Central Government's Bureau of Education in the United States of North America, to have no decision-making or real power, but only to facilitate mutual understanding of each organization's movement and compile statistics, etc. In other words, this Central Committee should suffice as a body for mutual communication and consultation, not a decision-making body. This way, work can proceed smoothly without conflict.
However, this Central Committee should specifically be the body responsible for the publishing enterprise, which would be a joint project of the four major organizations. Now, I will outline a plan for this publishing enterprise.

Publishing Enterprise

The fundamental purpose of eradicating illiteracy is to enable the masses to read. If so, after teaching the ability to read, it is naturally imperative <8> to supply reading materials. However, in the current situation in Korea, these reading materials must be specially published and supplied free of charge.
(Those wishing to read a more detailed discussion on this issue should refer to my article "A Consideration Regarding the Illiteracy Eradication Movement" (文盲退治運動에 대한 一考察) in the July-August combined issue of the monthly magazine Sinsaeng (新生) (P. 6)).

Here is a budget estimate for a simple monthly magazine to be supplied free of charge to those who attend the temporary schools.
It seems ideal to publish a simple monthly magazine, quarto size (4,6版4切). Calculating the cost:

Editing Fee: 1,000 won per year
Office Expenses: 2,000 won per year
Printing Costs, First Year: 127,000 won
Breakdown:
Printing Cost: 1,500,000 copies: 1,000 won (?) *[Note: This figure seems erroneous, likely a typo in the original or requires context. The total suggests a much higher cost per copy or refers to something else.]*
Paper Cost: 9,000 reams: 36,000 won
Postage (Mailing Cost): 90,000 won
Total: 130,000 won

So, it costs about 130,000 won in the first year. Then, in the second and third years, as the circulation increases, the printing, paper, and mailing costs will increase proportionally. Thus, to publish 3 million copies in the second year would cost 270,000 won, and to publish 5 million copies in the third year would cost about 400,000 won.

Financial Resources

So where does that money come from? This is the most difficult problem. Of course, during the first 3-year plan period, these reading materials must be supplied entirely free of charge. Naturally, if a second 3-year plan is made, from that point on, we would plan to charge a subscription fee and sell it, starting with the graduates from the first year of the initial plan. In other words, give it away for free for 3 years, and starting from the 4th year, have people pay a fee to subscribe. We can reasonably anticipate that a publication endeared over 3 years would attract many subscribers, provided the fee is not excessive.

So now, where will the 800,000 won needed for the 3 years come from? After searching here and there for financial resources, this writer stopped before the Hyanggyo assets (鄕校財産). Isn't the income from Korean Hyanggyo the most suitable money to use for such an enterprise?
According to statistics from the 2nd year of Shōwa (1927), the annual income from Hyanggyo assets was 597,625 won. An annual income of 600,000 won from Hyanggyo assets!

If this were used for the expenses of the illiteracy eradication project, wouldn't even Confucius, residing in the underworld, dance with joy? Furthermore, looking at the breakdown of expenditures, 201,440 won among them is accounted for as "Cultural Enrichment Expenses" (敎化費). (For details, refer to my article "Aiming for Compulsory Education" (義務敎育을 目標로) published in the Tong-a Ilbo in April). What greater cultural enrichment project could there be than illiteracy eradication using this "Cultural Enrichment Expense"?

If the Hyanggyo asset income could provide this annual amount of 200,000 won (one-third of the total annual Hyanggyo asset income) for just 3 years for this great illiteracy eradication project, the majority of the publishing costs would be solved. The remaining 200,000 won could likely be covered by donations, various subsidies, etc.

If the Hyanggyo income provides this level of assistance, then naturally, as mentioned above, Confucian representatives (儒林代表) should be included as members of the Central Committee. If the Confucian scholars (儒林) of Korea today show the courage to do this, we would gladly not hesitate to forgive their past wrongdoings.

The time has come! (時乎來哉)
Is there such a person? (有其人乎?)

**Summary**

This 1931 article from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine outlines a "Three-Year Plan for New Korean Culture Construction," focusing initially and urgently on eradicating illiteracy. It includes contributions from the Chosŏn Ilbo and Tong-a Ilbo detailing their ongoing literacy campaigns, which utilized primers, student volunteers ("Enlightenment Teams"), and summer/winter vacations, reaching tens of thousands annually despite challenges. Writer Chu Yo-sŏp proposes a vastly expanded, coordinated 3-year plan aiming to teach 5 million people by mobilizing newspapers, Christian groups, Ch'ŏndogyo, establishing nationwide night schools in existing buildings, and creating a central committee. He controversially suggests funding a free mass-circulation magazine for new literates using substantial income from traditional Hyanggyo (local Confucian school) assets, appealing to Confucian leaders to participate in this modern national project.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for revealing the intense focus on literacy as a cornerstone of national self-strengthening and cultural development among Korean intellectuals and media outlets during the colonial period. It showcases practical strategies (volunteer mobilization, targeted campaigns, use of existing infrastructure) adopted under restrictive conditions. The detailed statistics, budget proposals (including the specific, large sum eyed from Hyanggyo funds), the explicit comparison with "civilized" nations vs. Korea's 80% illiteracy rate, and the call for cooperation among diverse groups (press, religious organizations, potentially traditional Confucian structures) provide valuable insights into the social dynamics, aspirations, and perceived obstacles within colonial Korean society. The tension between traditional resources (Hyanggyo assets) and modern nationalist goals (mass literacy) is particularly noteworthy.

발행일	1931년 09월 01일
기사제목	朝鮮新文化建設 三個年計劃
기사형태	논설

朝鮮新文化建設 三個年計劃
우리들이 가저야 할 新文化를 現實條件이 許諾하는 範圍內에서, 또 一定한 時間上의 制約밋헤서 全員이 總出하야 불과 가튼 熱誠과 鐵과 가튼 統制를 武器삼고 「突擊的」으로 이루어 노을 수 업슬가? 最近 數3年來의 諸方面의 斷片的 試驗은 이에 대한 成功을 約束하는 것갓다.
이졔 新文化라 하여도 그속에는 民族保健이라든가 社會運動, 藝術行動 等 여러 部門이 잇지만은 一切運動의 先行條件이 되는 文盲打破에만 이번에 全力을 너허 그 3個年의 計劃을 編制, 發表키로 하엿다. 압흐로 우리의 議論이 應當 文化의 全般部門에 미치겟거니와 더욱 來號에는 消費組合, 協同組合 運動을 中心삼은 新經濟建設3個年計劃을 繼續 發表하려하니 明日의 朝鮮에 關心을 가지는 諸氏 이에 대한 眞摯한 討議와 實行이 뒤대어 잇서주기를 바라노라. (三千里社主幹) <2>

그런데 文字普及運動을 나는 不言實行으로 하는 朝鮮日報의 社是에 관한 一大事業이라고 봄니다. 무어 그 事業을 實行할적마다. 社告를 크게 내고 記事로 푸품하게 宣傳하고 社說로 鼓吹하고 또 班員에게는 一定한 懸賞形式으로 하야 盛大한 慰安과 授賞式까지 하니 온통으로 大大的 宣傳거리가 되는 것이라. 어떠케 不言實行이냐고 당장 辨駁이 잇슬줄 아나 그러나 그런 것은 다만 그 事業進行의 事務的 順序이오 그 本質을 論核하고 宣揚하는 것은 아님니다. 쉽게 말하자면

3年間에 30萬名

그러나 이것은 第1年의 例外數字이겟고 다시 우리는 平年狀態를 豫想하여 볼 때 우리의 決心이 더욱 구더짐을 늣겨짐니다.
卽 나는 이러케 생각함니다. 第2年인 明年부터는 우리 社의 眞意를 아러주는 學生諸君이 더욱 만허지어서 이 運動에 힘을 빌니어 줄 이가 激增할 것과 文盲群衆의 智識吸收熱이 非常한 勢로 昻揚될 것을 밋음으로 每年 1,000隊의 啓蒙隊가 編制되어서 出發될줄 알며 따라서 그 啓蒙隊가 每一隊에서 獲得하는 文盲大衆이 平均 百名式으로 보아 그 總數10만명에는 넉넉히 이를 것을 밋슴니다.
또한 이것은 7, 8月의 農閑期만을 利用할 것이 안이라 겨울, 農民들이 집세기를 틀면서 집에 안져잇슬 그 時期도 利用하겟슴니다.
이밧게 또한 文字啓蒙運動과 竝行的으로 實行하고 잇는 「한글」 卽 綴字法의 統一普及을 위한 運動도 朝鮮語學會諸氏의 熱熱한 後援下에 全朝鮮的으로 進行되고 잇슴니다.
그런대 全朝鮮에 現在 얼마나한 文盲群이 잇는가 하면 이에 대한 統計는 벌서 잇서 조흘것이로되 統治當局의 調査조차 아직 업는 모양이기에 不得已 推定의 길을 취할밧게 업슴니다. 朝鮮內 人民의 總數를 2千萬名이라 하면 그중 2割이 文字解讀者일것이요 그밧게 천6백만명이 目不識丁字群일 것임니다. 그중에도 이미 老衰期에 入한 老嫗老爺과 乳兒及社會的活動을 到底히 期待할 수 업는 아조 深村의 婦女들을 全部 그 半數로 하야 除去할진대 吾人이 文盲打破의 對象을 삼어 조흘 實數가 8백만명은 達할 것임니다.
이제 年10만명을 計劃하는 本社의 實地運動이 所期의 目的을 達하자면 現狀대로 進行되어 80년을 要할 것이나 그러나 文盲의 啓蒙은 배운 사람이 또 가르치고 또 그 배우사람이 또 가르치고 하여 幾何級數的으로 飛躍하리라고 누가 아니하오리까.
吾人은 10년 以內에 이 運動을 完成될 것을 미드려 하거니와 爲先 最近3個年에도 30만명은 깨오질 줄 암니다. 이것은 確信하는 數字외다.

經費는 每年千圓들어

大體 「文盲打破」란 恥辱的 標語는 歷史를 뒤저볼지라도 露西亞밧게 업슴니다. 文明한 나라는 벌서부터 義務敎育制度가 實施되어 어릴때부터 成規의 敎育을 밧게 되니까 그나라 全國民中에 文盲이 잇슬 까닭이 업슴니다. 英, 佛, 米, 日, 獨 等이 모다 조흔 例가 아님니까. 그 代身 人文程度가 낫고 政治的 施設이 高度에 이르지 못한 未開國이나 野蠻民族 사이엔 文盲이 잇슴니다.
文盲을 打破한다 함은 一切文化建設이 가장 初步的이자 基本的 運動이외다. 더구나 朝鮮가치 文盲이 全人口의 8割을 占한 곳에서 엇더케 이것을 하로라도 等閑視할 것이리까.
最初 本社는 4년전 本報創刊10週年紀念 때에 이 運動을 大大的으로 이르키려 하엿슴니다만은 某方面에 대한 特殊事情으로 中止하엿든 것이엇스나 今日은 形式을 고치어 啓蒙運動을 今年부터 이르킨 것이외다.
끗흐로 只今까지 이 運動을 위하야 支出한 經費는 每年 千圓可量이외다. 압흐로는 더 增額식히려고 함니다. <6>

500만名을 目標로

文盲退治事業은 그間 民間側으로부터 만흔 努力을 해왓다. 朝鮮日報社의 文字普及班과 基督敎의 夏期兒童聖經學校가 여러해 전부터 만흔 活動을 해왓고 今年부터는 東亞日報社에서도 啓蒙隊運動을 시작햇다. 고마운 일들이다.
이제 여기 관한 3個年計劃을 具體的으로 짜보려고 붓을 들고 안즈니 自然 첫생각이 임이 活動하는 이 모-든 기관들의 總動員을 바라게 된다. 아마 그것이 가쟝 쉽고 또 可能한 方途인가 십다.
文盲退治事業을 3個年만 計劃的으로 實施하면 約5백만명의 文盲을 깨칠수가 잇다고 나는 밋는다.
萬一 그대로만 게속한다면 10년以內에 朝鮮에는 文盲이 업서지게 만들 수가 잇슬 것이다.
그러면 엇더케 5백만의 文盲을 退治할 수가 잇슬가? 몬져 夏期臨時學校로부터 보자.
朝鮮日報社 文字普及班의 成績으로 보면 압흐로는 每年 5만명 突破는 容易하리라구 當務者가 말한다. 그런대 今年부터는 東亞日報도 시작햇스니까 數가 兩處로 좀 갈릴 예정잡고 一新聞社에서 約4만명식은 突破할 수 잇슬 것이다.
그리고 基督敎에서 每年 實行하는 夏期兒童聖經學校로 보면 1930년 生徒總計가 6만8천명인데 그즁 無學兒童(곳 學校에 단니지 못하는 兒童)數가 1만5천4백명이엇다. 그럼으로 基督敎에서는 在學生들 보다도 文盲兒童들을 收容하기에 조곰만 더 努力하면 約3만명을 突破하기 容易하리라구 생각한다.
그外 朝鮮서 이 偉大한 運動에 協力을 할만한 團體로 天道敎가 잇다. 天道敎에서도 各地方 布敎所를 利用하고 各靑年團이 總動員하면 3만명쯤 다루기에는 無難할 것이다.

以上은 夏期學校만 算한 것이다. 그런대 文盲退治事業을 크게 이르킨다면 우리는 冬期休暇까지를 利用하지 안을수 업다. 도로혀 農村에서는 夏期보다도 冬期에 時間의 餘裕가 좀더 잇다. 그러나 冬期에는 氣候가 寒冷한 關係로 늘 場所問題가 頭痛거리가 될 것이다.
그러나 冬期休暇에도 亦是 各機關에서 夏期에 活動하는 것과 가튼 熱誠으로 活動하면 夏期成績의 約半數 가량을 收容하기 無難할줄로 생각한다. 그럿타고 하면 그 數가 約7만명이 된다.
그리하야 이 4大機關에서 1년동안에 收容할 수 잇는 兒童數가 21만명에 達한다. 그것이 3년을 게속하면 都合 63만명에 達한다.
그 다음에는 各 普通學校舍를 利用하여 夜學을 세울수가 잇다. 거기 대한 具體案은 月刊雜誌 慧星 8월호의 拙論 「無産夜學을 열나」(P, 40)을 참조하기 바란다. 하여튼 夜學으로 都市에서는 各校舍가 收容할 수 잇는 百%를 收容할 수 잇다.
그러나 農村에 잇서서는 夜學에는 遠地方에서 通學하기 不能한 關係上 完全을 期할수 업다고 잡아서 平均으로 收容量의 約半數만을 收容할 수 잇다고 假定하자. 그러면 現朝鮮普通學校 在學總數가 70만명이다. 거기 半數면 35만명이다. 곳 지금 朝鮮서 夜學을 열면 現存校舍만을 利用해가지고도 最少로 35만명은 收容할 수가 잇다는 말이다.
그런데 이제 이 夜學을 純全히 文盲退治로써 目的하는 것인 故로 2個月 以上을 더할 必要가 업다. 夜學 2個月만 가졋스면 臨時學校 1個月과 同程度의 敎授를 넉넉히 할 수 잇슬 것이다.
그러면 校舍를 1年中 8個月間만 쓰기로 하면 2個月式 4期로 分할 수 잇다. 一期에 35만명式 收容할 수 잇스니까 4期면 1백4십만명이 된다. 그럿케 3년을 게속하면 4백2십만명에 達한다. 4백2십만명과 63만명이면 總計 4백8십3만명이니 約5백만명되는 세음이다.

經費와 學科

勿論 經費는 지금 모양으로 各機關에서 自擔하도록 할 것이다. 그리고 各地方에서 생기는 約干의 費用 亦是 現在 모양으로 該地方에서 充當하기로 할 것이다.
各科目 亦是 現在와 비슷할 것이다. 한글을 가르치고 文字를 가르치는 것이 本位니까 그것이 中心이 될터이고 或 宗敎機關에서 自派의 敎理를 가르치려 하면 구타여 막을 필요 업시 黙認함이 좃타. 그 費用은 全部 그 敎派에서 負擔할 것이니까 거기 異論을 세울수 업는 것이다.

中央委員

各 機關運動을 促進하고 相互扶助하기 위하여는 各 機關의 代表로 組織된 中央委員會가 必要할 것이다. 4大機關에서 各 2人式 選出한 代表들과 鄕校를 代表하는 代表者 2人과 (그 理由는 뒤에 說明하겟다) 그 代表會議에서 選出하는 社會側代表 2人을 加하여 合11人으로 中央委員會를 組織햇스면 理想的일가 한다.
勿論 이 中央委員會는 맛치 北米合衆國 中央政府敎育局 비슷하게 아모런 決議權이나 實權이 업시 오직 各 機關運動을 互相 理解식히고 統計를 作成하는 일 等으로 그 權限을 制限하는 것이 조타. 다시 말하자면 이 中央委員會는 決議機關이 아니고 오직 서로 意思相通하며 相議하는 機關으로써 足한 것이다. 그러케 하여야 衡突이 업시 일이 잘되어 갈수 잇는 것이다.
그러나 이 中央委員會에서는 特別히 四大機關의 聯合事業으로 될 出版事業을 맛하하는 機關이 되어야 하겟다. 이제 이 出版事業에 대한 案을 꿈이려 한다.

出版事業

文盲을 退治하려는 根本目的은 곳 大衆에게 讀書를 식히기 위함이다. 그러할 것 가트면 讀書할 能力을 가르처준 후에는 讀書할<8> 資料를 供給해주는 것이 맛당히 잇서야 할 事實이다. 그런데 아직 現朝鮮에 잇서서는 이 讀書物을 特히 出版하여 無料로 供給하여야할 處地에 잇다.
(이 問題에 대한 더 詳細한 論을 읽고저 하는 이는 月刊雜誌 新生7, 8月合號 拙論 「文盲退治運動에 대한 一考察」(P, 6)을 참조하기 바란다)
臨時學校을 다녀가는 사람들에게 無料로 供給할 月刊簡易雜誌 豫算을 하나 꾸며보면 이러하다.
月刊으로 하고 4, 6版4切 簡易雜誌을 發刊햇스면 理想일가 십다. 그 費用을 計算해보면.
編輯費 年   천원
事務費 年  2천원
印刷費 第一年  12만7천원
內譯
印刷代  150萬部   천원
紙代     9千連    3만6천원
郵費(發送費)       9만원
計                13만원也
곳 第1年에 約13만원이 든다. 그리고 第2年, 第3年에는 發行部數가 더 增加될 것인 故로 따라서 印刷代, 紙代, 發送費 等이 그 比例로 增加될 것이다. 그래서 第2年에 3백만부 出版하러면 27만원이 들겟고 第3年에 5백만부를 出版하려면 約40만원이 든다.
財源

그러면 그돈은 어데서 엇나? 이것이 가장 어려운 問題이다. 勿論 第1, 3個年計劃 동안에는 이 讀物을 全部 無料로 供給하지 안으면 아니 되겟다. 勿論 第2, 3個年計劃을 한다면 그때부터는 第1計劃, 初年卒業生들로부터는 誌代를 밧고 팔도록 計劃할 것이다. 다시 말하자면 3년동안은 無料로 주고 第4年째부터는 誌代를 내고 사보도록 하는 것이다. 3년동안 情드린 刊行物이면 誌代가 過度하지 안은 限에서 多數가 사보려고 하리라구 豫想하여도 크게 망발은 아니될줄 안다.
그러면 이제 다시 3個年間 쓸 80만원 돈이 어데서 생길가? 筆者는 여기저기 그 財源을 求해보다가 鄕校財産압에 와서 멈츳섯다. 朝鮮鄕校收入이야말로 가장 이러한 事業을 위하여 쓰기에 適合한 돈이 아닐가?
昭和2年度 統計로 보면 鄕校財産收入이 年5십9만7천6백2십5원이엇다. 鄕校財産年收入 60만원!
이것을 가지고 文盲退治事業費에 쓴다고 하면 그야말로 地下에 게신 孔子님도 깁버서 춤을 춤것이 아닌가? 더욱 그 歲出種別로 보아도 그중 20만1천4백4십원이 「敎化費」라는 것으로 計上되어 잇다. (詳細는 4월중 東亞日報所載 拙論』 義務敎育을 目標로」를 참조)이 「敎化費」로 文盲退治보다 더 큰 敎化事業이 어데 또 잇을가?
鄕校財産收入에서 이 「敎化費」 年額20만원(鄕校財産總收入의 3分之1)을 3년간만 이 偉大한 文盲退治事業을 위해서 提供해 준다면 出版費의 大部分이 해결된다. 그 남어지 20만원은 寄附金, 諸補助金 등으로 充當할 수 잇슬 것이다.
鄕校收入에서 이만한 助力을 준다고 하면 勿論 우에도 말한 것처럼 儒林代表로써 中央委員會의 一員을 둘 것이다. 萬一 朝鮮儒林들로 오늘날에 이 일을 할만한 勇氣를 뵈인대면 우리는 달갑게 그들의 過去罪惡을 용서해 주기를 주저하지 아느려한다.
