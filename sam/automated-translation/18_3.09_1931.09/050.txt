**Summary**

This article from the September 1931 issue of *Samch'ŏlli* magazine lists the major shareholders of three Korean banks: Tongil Bank, Haedong Bank, and Honam Bank. It provides details on their total capital, paid-in capital, the value per share, and the names of individuals holding significant numbers of shares, along with the bank presidents and a managing director. For historians, this article offers valuable insight into the structure of Korean-owned capital and the economic elite during the Japanese colonial period. It identifies key figures involved in banking and finance, their relative investment power, and the concentration of ownership, which is significant for understanding indigenous economic activities and social stratification under colonial rule.
