Samch'ŏlli "Wall Newspaper" No. 7
Major Shareholders of the Tong-a Ilbosa

The Tong-a Ilbosa (東亞日報社), a stock company (paid-in capital 350,000 won) with a 7-year history and 50,000 readers, is having its foundation laid by the hands of the major shareholders listed below.
Total Shares: 14,000 (50 won per share)
Including: Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) 3,000, Kim Sangyŏn (金相硏) 1,442
Kim Jaesu (金在洙) 1,000, Yi Ajoo (李娥珠) 1,000
Kim Yŏnsu (金䄵洙) 836, Ko Hyŏnnam (高賢男) 610
Im Chŏnghyŏp (林正燁) 530, Kim Sanggyŏng (金相卿) 550
Minor shareholders from within and outside: 189 persons
Furthermore, the executives are: Managing Director & President Song Chinu (宋鎭禹), Vice President Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), Director Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙), Managers Yang Wŏnmo (梁源模), Im Chŏnghyŏp (林正燁), Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), Kim Yongmu (金用茂), Auditors Chang Hyŏnsik (張鉉軾), Hyŏn Sangtae (玄相台)

Major Shareholders of Hansŏng Toso Chusik Hoesa

Looking at the major shareholders of the Korean-managed Hansŏng Toso Company (漢城圖書會社), which also has a 10-year history in book publishing and printing, they are as follows.
Total Shares: 6,000 Capital Stock: 300,000 won
(Paid-in capital 90,000 won, 1 share 50 won)
Including: Yi Chongjun (李鍾駿) 1,161, Yi Pongha (李鳳夏) 1,000
Han Kyusang (韓奎相) 1,200, Yi Ch'angik (李昌翼) 500 <39>

**Summary**

This article lists the major shareholders and key executives of two prominent Korean-owned companies in 1931: the newspaper company Tong-a Ilbosa (Dong-A Ilbo) and the publishing/printing company Hansŏng Toso Chusik Hoesa (Hansong Book Company). It details the number of shares held by principal investors and names the individuals in leadership positions for both organizations, highlighting their financial structure (total shares, capital stock, paid-in capital). For historians, this piece provides valuable insight into the ownership structure and key figures associated with significant Korean cultural and media enterprises during the Japanese colonial period. It reveals the concentration of ownership among a relatively small group of wealthy Koreans and lists individuals, some of whom played complex roles in both nationalistic and collaborative contexts later on.
