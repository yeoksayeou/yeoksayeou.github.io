**Body Text Translation:**

Forgetting the Child

An American couple, Mr. Johnson and Mrs. Mary, set out on a trip with the intention of driving across America. However, after three days, while eating lunch somewhere in the mountains, they forgot their six-year-old eldest son right there and left. The next day, while washing their feet at a streamside, they also forgot their four-year-old second son, whom they had seated on the sand, and left. Discovering this later, they were greatly surprised and are searching, but it's uncertain where they went; they are truly a very forgetful couple. Well, as long as one just takes the wife along, children can be born limitlessly!!<20>

**Summary**

This short article recounts an anecdote about an American couple, Mr. Johnson and Mrs. Mary, who supposedly forgot their six-year-old son in the mountains and their four-year-old son at a streamside on consecutive days during a cross-country trip. The article ends with a cynical comment about their forgetfulness and the replaceability of children. For historians, this piece might offer a glimpse into how Americans were perceived or portrayed (in this case, as incredibly absent-minded) in popular Korean media during the colonial period. The somewhat dismissive and possibly exaggerated tone, along with the cynical closing remark, could reflect underlying cultural attitudes or serve simply as amusing, albeit dark, filler content for the magazine.

아해를 이저버리어
미국사람 미스터 존손과 미세쓰 메리-는 부부인데 자동차타고 미국일주할 작정으로 려행을 떠낫다. 그러다가 사흘만에 엇든 산속에서 점심을 먹고 다시 려행을 떠날 때에 여섯 살먹은 맛아들을 그냥 그 자리에 이저버리고 떠낫고 그 잇흔날 시내까에서 발을 씻다가 또 네 살 먹은 둘재아들을 모래우에 안처노은 채 이저바리고 떠낫다. 그것을 뒤에 발견하고 크게 놀나 수색 중이나 아직 어데 갓는지 막연, 잘도 이즘이 만흔 부부이다. 따는 녀편네 하나만 더리고 다니면 아해야 얼마든지 나을수 잇스니까!!<20>
