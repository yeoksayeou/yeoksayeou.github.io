○Company Notice= The papers written by Mr. Yi Hangbal (李恒發) under the title "Proletarian Kult," by Someone (某氏) under the title "Active Plan for the Social Movement," and by Someone (某氏) under the title "Korean Reality and the Theater and Film Movement" could not be published due to unavoidable circumstances. <4>

**Summary**

This brief notice from the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* informs readers that three planned articles, dealing with proletarian culture ("Kult"), social movements, and the state of Korean theater and film, could not be published due to unspecified "unavoidable circumstances." The mention of topics like "Proletarian Kult" and "Social Movement" is significant, reflecting the intellectual and political currents of the time, particularly the influence of leftist thought in colonial Korea. For historians, this notice hints at potential censorship or self-censorship regarding sensitive political or social topics under Japanese colonial rule, as "unavoidable circumstances" was often a euphemism for external pressure or the risks associated with publishing such content.
