Autumn of Solitude (and 2 others)
						P'ain

Autumn of Solitude

Autumn moon leans against the poplar pillar
that has sent its leaves flying.
Sparse branches, branches, branches
Tonight, will a bird whistle from down there<85>
From somewhere, the damp smell of a tomb drifts.
Ah, how lonely.
If only I could go to the distant mountains to gather herbs—
If only I could go and never return—<86>

Waiting

Opening the window,
The night sky is pitch black.
As if someone might come,
I press my ear to the threshold and stay awake all night,
Listening to the sound of sorrow that stops and starts.<86>

Sup'yo Bridge at Twilight

When the evening glow sinks onto Sup'yo Bridge,
Appearing quietly on the stone railing, from who knows where,
Letting her shadow flow onto the water, she stands,
then disappears again, that woman.

Is she waiting for her departed husband?
Is it sorrow for a dead young child?
Dry paulownia leaves on the water
float and sink, float and sink— one by one.

Tonight, even the rain falls,
Has she come again, she who is like my sorrowful elder sister? <86>
<85-86>

**Summary**

This work consists of three short poems by P'ain (Kim Tong-hwan) expressing themes of loneliness, longing, and melancholy. "Autumn of Solitude" uses imagery of a bare poplar tree, moonlight, and the scent of graves to evoke deep loneliness and a desire for escape. "Waiting" portrays anxious anticipation through a sleepless night, listening for someone who may never arrive. "Sup'yo Bridge at Twilight" presents a poignant image of a mysterious, sorrowful woman appearing and disappearing at the bridge, symbolizing lingering grief or memory. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in 1931, reflect a common lyrical focus on personal sorrow and introspection during the Japanese colonial period. While not explicitly political, historians might find the pervasive sense of melancholy, yearning, and the use of a specific Korean landmark (Sup'yo Bridge) significant as subtle expressions of the era's atmosphere and perhaps a quiet assertion of Korean identity and feeling amidst oppression.

孤獨의 가을

나무입 날려 보낸 포푸라 기둥에
가을 달 기대어 쉰다.
엉성한 가지, 가지, 가지
오늘 밤은 휘바람 불며 저 아래서 새일가<85>
어듸서 늬늬한 무덤 냄새 흘너 온다.
에그, 외로워라.
나는 먼-산에 藥 캐러나 갓스면一
가서 영영 오지나 말엇스면一<86>

기다림

창을 여니
밤 하늘 까마타
누가 올이 잇는 듯하여
문턱에 귀 기우리고 이 한밤 새이노라
끈첫다 잇는 스르럼 소리 드르면서<86>

黃昏의 水標橋

수표교에 저녁 놀이 잠길 때
어데서 왓는지 고요히 돌 란간에 나타나
물 우에 제 그림자 흘니고 섯다간
다시 사라저 버리는 저 女人네

가신 남편 기둘 님인가
죽은 어런 아히 서려함인가
물 우에 마른 오동입
하나 둘 떳다 잠겻다 떳다 잠겻다一.

오늘 밤은 비조차 내리는데
그이 또 왓는가, 슬품 만흔 내 맛누이가튼 그이가 <86>
