Publication Date: 1931년 09월 01일
Article Title: 가을밤 한울우에 (On an Autumn Night Sky)
Author: 麗水 (Risu)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

On an Autumn Night Sky
						Risu

Above the dark blue autumn night sky
Seeing the stars shining brightly
About "Nature" and "Life"
I have had times of deep, deep thought
But truly, for me
It was an "Eternal Riddle."

Sitting in a lonely room on a rainy night
Listening to the sound of raindrops falling without sorrow
I was once again lost in deep thought
But what could be done? It was still
An "Eternal Riddle."

Then one day arrived when
I shouted, "It is Science!"
Truly, I banged the desk and shouted.
"This, having Science like this,
I have suffered in vain"—I said.

I stopped sighing and
Whistled. However,
The "Nature" and "Life" before me
Remained as Unknown Variables—. <82>

I shouted once again
"Only through the power of Scientific Development!"
A lonely echo arose
In my room submerged in solitude
But the "Nature" and "Life" before me
Remained as a "Great Mystery"—.

After that, I finally realized
That it was too great a "Single Mystery"
One extremely small bug
One extremely small blade of grass
One extremely small stone
And within one extremely small seed
I saw the "Great Mystery" hiding.

Only then, in a loud voice
I sang of the hills
I sang of the streams
Of the rain, the wind, the birds, the flowers
The sea, the trees, the woods
The various forms of the "Great Mystery"
I sang.

Now, the season when crickets chirp before the garden
I shall make the small cricket my friend
And sing together with it
Of Eternal Nature
And also of brief Life—.
1931.8.21. <83>

**Summary**

The poem by Risu describes a personal journey of contemplation about the fundamental nature of existence ("Nature" and "Life"). Initially baffled and viewing them as an "Eternal Riddle," the speaker turns to "Science" with great hope, believing it holds the answers. However, science fails to resolve the profound mystery. Ultimately, the speaker recognizes the immense "Great Mystery" inherent not just in the grand cosmos but in the smallest elements of nature – an insect, a blade of grass, a stone, a seed. This realization leads to acceptance and a desire to sing about all aspects of this mystery, finding companionship even in a cricket to appreciate both eternal nature and transient human life. For historians, the poem offers insight into the intellectual grappling with modernity (specifically the promise and limits of science) within colonial Korea. The shift from seeking definitive answers through science to embracing inherent mystery reflects a complex response to changing worldviews during a period of external pressure and internal reflection. The emphasis on nature and individual contemplation might also represent a subtle turning inward, away from intractable socio-political realities.

가을밤 한울우에
						麗水
검풀은 가을 밤 한울 우에
총총히 빗나는 별들을 보고
나는 『自然』과 『人生』에 대하여
깁히 깁히 생각하여 본 일이 잇서노라
그러나 그것은 진실로 나에게 잇서서
『永遠한 수수꺽기』이엇노라

비오는 밤 외로운 방 안에 안저
시름도 업는 락수물 소리를 드르면서
나는 또 다시 깁흔 생각에 잠기엿섯노라
그러나 엇지하랴. 그것은 역시
『永遠한 수수꺽기』이엇노라

그 어느 날에 니르러
나는 『科學이다!』 하고 소리첫노라.
진실로 책상을 치며 소리첫노라.
『이것을, 이가치 科學이 잇는 것을
나는 헛되히 고생하엿노라』一고.

나는 한숨을 그치고
휘파람을 불엇노라. 그러나
나의 압헤 잇는 『自然』과 『人生』은
未知數 그대로 남어 잇는 것을一.<82>

나는 또다시 소리첫노라
『오직 科學發展의 힘으로!』
쓸쓸한 反響이 孤獨에 잠긴
나의 방에 니럿섯노라
그러나 나의 압헤 잇는 『自然』과 『人生』은
『커다란 神秘』 그대로 남어 잇는 것을一.

그후에 이르러 나는 비로소
너머나 큰 『한개의 神秘』인 것을 알엇노라
지극히 적은 벌네 하나
지극히 적은 풀닙 하나
지극히 적은 돌뎅이 하나
그리고 지극히 적은 씨앗 한알 속에
숨어 잇는 『커다란 神秘』를 보앗노라

나는 그때에 비로소 큰 목소리로
언덕을 노래하엿노라
시내를 노래하엿노라
비를, 바람을, 새들을, 꼿들을
바다를, 나무를, 숩풀을
『커다란 神秘』의 가지가지 모양을
노래하엿노라

이제 뜰 압헤 귀뜨람이 우는 시절
나는 적은 귀뜨람이 벗을 삼어
그와 함께 노래하리라
悠久한 自然을
또는 짧은 人生을一.
1931.8.21. <83>
