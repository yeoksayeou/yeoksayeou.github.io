交叉點 (Crossroads)

Please tell me the age of Yŏ Unhyŏng, who was arrested in Shanghai and is now serving time.
Proprietor of Tae Street, P'yŏngyang

The elder brother's age is forty-six this year.
Respectfully, Younger Brother Yŏ Unhong.

Young and Vigorous or Old and Declining?

Hello there, Crossroads Standard-Bearer!
Regarding certain individuals in Chosŏn society - for example, Messrs. Kim Pyŏngno,
Ch'oe Rin,
Hŏ Hŏn,
An Chaehong,
Hong Myŏnghŭi,
Yi Kwangsu,
Song Chinu,
Sin Sŏgu,
Han Yongun, etc. - are they generally young and promising (少壯有爲) figures or old and inactive (老衰無爲) figures? Please tell me their ages at least.
Pak Il Tŏk, Taegu

Whether they are promising (有爲) or inactive (無爲) is something we'll know by watching what happens next. There are cases where someone promising today becomes inactive tomorrow, and someone inactive today becomes promising tomorrow. However, as for whether they are young and vigorous (少壯) or old and declining (老衰), you might be able to tell by looking at their ages, so we have investigated and recorded them here.
Mr. Kim Pyŏngno 45 years old
Mr. Ch'oe Rin 48 years old <51>
Mr. Hŏ Hŏn 41 years old
Mr. An Chaehong 41 years old
Mr. Hong Myŏnghŭi 44 years old
Mr. Yi Kwangsu 40 years old
Mr. Song Chinu 42 years old
Mr. Sin Sŏgu 38 years old
Mr. Han Yongun 51 years old
Reply from a Samch'ŏlli Company reporter.

To Mr. Yi Ŭnsang

Regarding your work, "Collected Tales of Korean History" (朝鮮史話集), are you not publishing volumes for eras other than the Three Kingdoms Period? Are you currently writing them? If you are writing, around when will they be published?
Song Pŏm, Ŭiju.

In September

(Reply) Besides the Three Kingdoms Period volume, there are two more volumes: Koryŏ and Yi Dynasty (李朝). They will be published sequentially. The middle volume, the Koryŏ篇 (Koryŏ Period), will come out in September.
Respectfully, Yi Ŭnsang.

Who is the Fighter?

Crossroads Standard-Bearer?
Among the current Chosŏn fighters (鬪士), which gentleman
has the most real power and is also worthy of admiration? "Excluding Mr. An Ch'angho."
Hong Haedŏk, Sinŭiju Border

The Leader of an Organization

Even if we say Chosŏn fighters (朝鮮人鬪士), they exist in various fields - for example, politics, economy, culture, social movements <52> - so it's a bit vague to specify. However, it seems what you are mainly asking about pertains to social movements or the national movement, and in that case, it would probably be the leaders of the relevant organizations.
Samch'ŏlli Reporter, Akhoa (岳火).

To Mr. An Chaehong

Sir, how old are you this spring and autumn (春秋)?
Yi Hyŏk Se, Cheju Island

Yes. I am 41 years old.
An Chaehong

Will You Dance Again?

To Ms. Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi
I am one of those who admire your dance (舞踊). I heard a rumor that since you became a housewife after getting married this spring, you will no longer appear on stage. Is that true?
Please, as much as possible, do not let that precious art wither away within just one household, but come out into the world again like in the old days.
Will you really come out on stage again?
Please tell me.
Pak Chŏng Ho, Ch'ŏnan Town Center

Will Perform Again in September

Thank you for asking. Appearing on stage and dancing is my life; how could I <53> stop as long as I am alive? For now, I will hold my third performance sometime in the coming September. Please offer much guidance.
Respectfully, Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi, Chŏksŏn-dong, Sŏul.

How Much are Police Expenses?

To the Esteemed Crossroads Standard-Bearer (三千里交叉點旗手 足下).
Looking at the recent India Special Issue of Samch'ŏlli, it says that while India spends only 100 million rupees on education expenses (敎育費), it spends 600 million rupees on military expenses (軍事費). By the way, how much is Chosŏn's annual police expenditure (警察費)?
Pak Yang Hoe, Andong County (安東縣)

21 Million Won

Yes. We understand your query. The total police expenditure (警察費總額) budgeted in the Government-General's budget for the next fiscal year is 21.4 million won. Of this, the salaries (俸給) for Police Superintendents (警視), Inspectors (警部), Policemen (巡査), etc., account for more than half, at 13.8 million won. Furthermore, the secret funds (機密費) expended for the arrest (檢擧) in cases related to the Higher Police (高等警察) involving Communists (共産黨) or Nationalists (民族主義者) amount to 324,000 won. Additionally, the detention facility expenses (留置場費) spent to detain suspects (被疑者) in detention centers (留置場) are said to reach 570,000 won.
(Reply from a Samch'ŏlli Reporter). <54>

**Summary**

This article is a Q&A column from the September 1931 issue of Samch'ŏlli magazine, responding to reader inquiries. It covers the age of imprisoned nationalist Yŏ Unhyŏng, the ages of prominent Korean figures like Kim Pyŏngno and Yi Kwangsu (contrasting "young and vigorous" vs. "old and declining"), the publication plans for historian Yi Ŭnsang's history series, a question about identifying the most influential "fighter" (answered vaguely as leaders of organizations), confirmation of dancer Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's plans to return to the stage despite marriage, and a breakdown of the colonial police budget in Chosŏn, highlighting significant spending on salaries, secret funds for political policing (Higher Police targeting communists and nationalists), and detention costs. The column provides a snapshot of public interest in prominent figures, cultural activities, and the workings of the colonial administration. For historians, it offers valuable data points on the perceived status of key individuals in 1931, the public discourse around national and social movements, cultural life under colonialism (Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi), and especially the significant financial resources dedicated by the Government-General to policing and political surveillance, quantifying aspects of colonial control.
