Seoul Decameron
"Ero" Northward Advance

The temple of "ero," the cafe, is gradually moving up to Pukch'on (North Village). Mijinjwa (美人座), which charges thirty-five *chŏn* for a cup of "mikŭsekki" (믹서기 - likely milkshake or mixed drink), and Salon Arirang, which employs thirty to fifty beauties (미히), are both reportedly striving to extend their branches to the Jongno thoroughfare, shouldering significant capital. Besides these, Minado (미나도) and P'aradaissŭ (파라다이쓰) are also diligently pushing into Pukch'on, centered around T'apkol Park. Frivolous Chosŏn youths will likely offer their meager salaries there, and immature sons of rural landlords (만석꾼) will probably offer up plots of land. This is truly a profoundly significant "beauty trap" (미인게)! Be vigilant.

Drawers (즈로-쓰) Thief

On a public holiday in early August, several hundred beautiful mermaids (人魚 - i.e., women) came out to bathe at the Han River swimming beach. At that time, a certain young man stole a newlywed bride's "drawers" (즈-로쓰 - likely bloomers or undergarments) and ended up heading to the detention center. Is it the fault of the "drawers," or the "drawers" of the fault?<54>

**Summary**

This article consists of two short, unrelated vignettes reflecting on contemporary Seoul life in 1931. The first piece discusses the northward expansion of cafes, associated with Western "ero" (eroticism/modernity), into the traditionally elite Pukch'on area near Jongno, expressing concern that young men will waste their money and resources on the female employees there. The second briefly reports an incident where a man was arrested for stealing a woman's undergarment ("drawers") at the Han River public swimming beach. From a historical perspective, the article reveals anxieties about modernization, Western cultural influences (cafes, "ero," public swimming), changing gender roles and social spaces, and consumerism in colonial Seoul. The contrast drawn between the "north village" (Pukch'on) and the cafe culture moving into it, along with the beach incident involving women's attire, highlights social tensions and transformations during the period.

서울 떼,까메,롱
「에로」北進

「에로」의 神殿 카페-가 점점 북촌으로 올마온다. 「미크세끼」한잔에 삼십오전씩 밧는 미인좌「美人座」와 삼오십명의 미히를 거느리고 잇는 사론 아리랑도 큼직한 자본을 질머메고 종로통으로 그 가지을 벌저 노으려 얘쓴다하며 그밧게 「미나도」요 「파라다이쓰」요 모다 탑골공원을 중심삼고 북촌진출에 여렴이 업다. 경박한 조선청년은 월급푼을 밧어서 그에다 바치겟고 철업는 시골만석꾼의 자손도 땅마직이나 그에 바칠 것이다. 이것은 실로 의의심장한 「미인게」로구나. 정신차려라.

「즈로-쓰」도적

팔월초생의 어느 공일날 한강 해수욕장에는 수백명의 아름다운 인어(人魚)가 나와서 목욕하엿다. 그때 엇던 청년이 세색시의 「즈-로쓰」를 훔첫다가 류치장으로 행차햇다. 「즈로-쓰」의 죄냐 죄의 「즈로-쓰」냐.<54>
