**Summary**

This article, presented as a "Wall Newspaper" brief, details the financial status and major shareholders of three regional banks in the Kyŏngsang province area (specifically Taegu and Tongnae) as of September 1931: the Kyŏngnam Hapdong Bank, the Tongnae Bank, and the Kyŏngil Bank. It lists their total capital, paid-in capital, total shares, the names of significant shareholders along with their share counts, and the head of each bank's board of directors. Historians might find this data valuable for understanding the structure of Korean-owned banking and capital accumulation in the provinces during the colonial period. The presence of both Korean and Japanese major shareholders (like Sugihara Chōtarō and Kokushi Mamoru) within these primarily Korean enterprises offers insight into the economic interactions and power dynamics of the era.
