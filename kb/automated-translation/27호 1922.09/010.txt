山河動色
(Mountains and Rivers Change Color)

"With a three-foot sword, I swear to Heaven; mountains and rivers tremble (三尺誓天 山河動色)" – this is the self-inscription our forefather, Yi Ch'ungmugong (李忠武公), carved onto the face of his sword (劒面). Indeed, where a person's single-minded determination (斷斷一念) moves with the ultimate power of resolve (決然) most solemnly (嚴肅), mountains and rivers truly change their color, and even metal and stone (金石) lose their hardness (剛). That numerous righteous men and ardent patriots (義人烈士) before us have already demonstrated this example (例), there should be no room for doubt (懷疑) now.

While it can be said that alternating tension and relaxation (一張一弛) is indeed the constant way of civil and military affairs (文武의 常道) and there is nothing particularly wrong with it, our people's spirit (人氣) concerning various spheres (各 方面) lately has been too inactive (不活潑) and too depressed (銷沈). If someone were to question (質) our current state (今日 現狀) today and ask, "Where now is the spirit of bygone days? (當年意氣 今安在오)", even the most fluent sophist (多辯兒) of the current trend, possessing ten mouths and a hundred tongues (百舌), would not be able to answer so easily.

Of course, there must be various causes (原因) and circumstances (事情) behind this. Those causes and circumstances were likely, to a certain extent, unavoidable and inescapable.

However, brothers! Haven't we now passed over a ridge? Haven't we weathered a storm (風波)? In the meantime, new developments (經緯) must have arisen, and new preparations (準備) must have been made.

The time is coming. The autumn wind (金風) blows along the banks of the Yŏlsu River (洌水). The opportunity arrives. The returning geese (歸鴈) fly over Paeksan Mountain (白山). I know not, but perhaps this is the joyous time for men of high志士男兒)? Comrades! Sweep away (一掃) the mood (氣分) of longstanding silence and melancholy (沈寂憂鬱), and with a new vow that makes mountains and rivers change color (山河動色), let us each leap forward (躍進) in the direction we intend to pursue. <1>

**Summary**

This editorial from the September 1922 issue of *Kaebyŏk* uses the famous inscription of Admiral Yi Sun-sin ("Mountains and rivers change color") as a starting point to lament the current state of inactivity and depression among Koreans. The author acknowledges potential reasons for this lethargy but argues that the time for despondency is over. Invoking autumnal imagery (wind, returning geese, Paektu mountain) as signs of change and opportunity, the editorial passionately calls upon readers ("brothers," "comrades") to cast off their melancholy and renew their determination, urging them to vigorously advance ("leap forward") in their respective endeavors with a spirit powerful enough to move nature itself. For historians, this piece is significant as it reflects the mood among Korean nationalists a few years after the March 1st Movement. It captures the attempt to overcome the post-uprising slump and reignite activism and resolve under colonial rule, using powerful historical and natural symbolism to inspire action without explicitly stating anti-colonial goals, likely due to censorship. The call to "leap forward" in "various spheres" suggests a turn towards cultural nationalism, self-strengthening, or other forms of resistance possible within the constraints of the time.

山河動色
「三尺誓天에 山河動色」이라 함은 우리의 先人 李忠武公의 그 劒面에 文노흔 自銘이거니와 사람의 斷斷一念이 最後의 決然의 力을 가지고 가장 嚴肅하게 움즉이는 그곳에는 實로 山河라도 그 色을 動하는 것이며 金石이라도 그 剛을 失하는 것이라. 이는 우리에 先하야 幾多의 義人烈士가 일즉이 그 例를 展하엿섯나니 只今에 다시 懷疑할 것은 업스리다.
一張一弛는 實로 文武의 常道이라 새삼스럽게 非라 할 것은 업다 할지라도 近來의 各 方面에 對한 우리의 人氣는 넘우나 不活潑하엿스며 넘우나 銷沈하엿나니 試하야 우리의 今日 現狀을 質하는 이 잇서 「當年意氣 今安在오」한다 하면 비록 열입에 百혀(舌)를 가진 流行의 多辯兒이라 할지라도 그려케 쉬웁게는 對答치 못하리라.
勿論 여기에는 여러 가지의 原因이 잇고 事情이 잇슬 것이다. 그 原因 그 事情은 想必 어느 程度 까지에는 업슬 수도 업고 또 避할 수 업는 것이엇스리라.
그러나 兄弟여 이제는 그만하여도 한 고개를 넘은 셈이 아니며 한 風波를 치른 셈이 아닌가. 其間에는 다시 새로운 經緯가 나섯슬 것이오 새로운 準備도 이뤄젓슬 것이라.
때가 옴이어. 金風이 洌水ㅅ가를 불고 機가 이름이어. 歸鴈이 白山 우를 날도다. 아지 못하거니와 志士男兒의 樂樂할 때는 이 때인가 하노니. 동무여 從來의 沈寂憂鬱의 氣分을 一掃하고 山河動色의 새로운 誓願으로써 다 各其 하고저하는 바의 方面에 躍進함이 잇슬지어다.＜1＞
