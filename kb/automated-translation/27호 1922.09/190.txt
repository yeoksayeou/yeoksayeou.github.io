Article Title: 不滅의 象徵(對話) (Symbol of Immortality (Dialogue))
Author: 林蘆月
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Symbol of Immortality
						Im No-wŏl
-(Characters) Poet. Demon. God,-

"In all abandon, delusion, misery, and deep mystery, I hear a sorrowful discord. Thus, my fervent desire strives to find a new beauty between the destruction and creation of heaven and earth.
I am a wandering child, floating from the finite to the infinite, from eternity to the刹那 (moment).
The time when one discovers the tragic meaning in all phenomena is the time when one is most faithful to oneself.
The ideal of the perfect is the imperfect."

(Poet) Oh, oh, dear Demon, you hold all the secrets of the universe. I know that at the end of the path (奈路) you silently dream upon, all wonders, unknowable sorrows, and bewitchment (蠱惑) lie hidden.
When the constant fluctuation of my imagination strives always to seek the ultimate secret, my burning lips, drawing red lines upon the gray space, ask you thus:
"What is the symbol of eternal immortality? Oh fairy, what is the art that can make the spirit and flesh of humankind soar (翶翔) to the extremity of power? Who possesses the materials to supply the making of new art? Oh, Demon, I am convinced the world will surely become the possession of dreamers, dissenters, the defeated, and the abnormal, just as you desire. And when humans, seeing your bewitching beauty for the first time, which they had never known, faint away, there will be a time when God drinks cocaine, casting you amorous glances (秋波) courteously. Oh fairy, when scientists discover new stars and inform us of strange <35> creatures and the abnormal laws of nature, and when artists perform miracles of style through new impulses and suggestions, your beauty will increasingly make time immortal and space new.
Oh, my only queen. What is absolute, infinite pleasure? Who knows the magic to stir up a dense fog of wondrous legends between humans and nature, to detach (淤離) the crude instinct from life, love, and desire?
Who has the power to purify (醇化) humanity's consciousness of life through some mysterious sentiment? And the power to relieve all the burdens of life? Oh Demon God, if it were you, you could lighten all my anguish (懊惱)... Oh Witch, are you now breathing clouds and treading on the river? And it seems the blue starflowers are each tied to your hair, swaying in the wind. But none of the scenery around you looks natural. The red flower clusters floating on the river, the white wind illuminating the sky, all seem like artificial nature. When I look around you, strange and miserable thoughts arise. I cannot tell what will happen next, or what has just happened. But when I gaze at that flower, larger than a parasol, when I see the grass blades piercing the clouds, I realize an unbearable bewitchment (蠱惑). Moreover, when I see the water's dark color, when I think of meeting you secretly across that water—oh, a shiver runs through me.
Are you now catching red flower clusters from the river with a red net (綱)? Doesn't red blood flow through that net?
But my only Witch. Your lips, redder than pomegranates, burn my chest. Your eyes, darker than black pearls, make my heart wander. Oh desolate fairy, my fervent desire lies only in meeting you secretly, but when I embrace your body, whiter and colder than ivory, how many times will I slip and be unable to attain my desperate wish?

(Witch) Poet, the only eternally immortal lover is I alone. In a single moment (刹那), I can see billions of centuries. And to make the essence of sensation immortal, I can set all things in motion. For the permanence of beauty, I can stop the extension of time. And for the infinity of life, I can destroy the completion of creation.

(Poet) Oh, miserable and radical beauty. If it is you, though it may be discord, you can praise my delusion (妄想). <36>

(Witch) Poet, I will teach you the pleasure of absolute nobility. Unlike God, I am not a hypocrite who lends (貸與) cheap (價廉) pleasures. God uses vain pleasures like satisfaction and happiness to dim human eyes and primarily aims to stimulate the inheritance and instincts of the crude race, but in truth, nothing God created—be it the sun, living beings, wind and rain, or any other law—was created for human pleasure. He only deceives you for the sake of destiny, his sole magic.
Of course, let's say you attain some degree of happiness. However, from that moment on, you cannot help but repay that happiness with severe pain and sorrow. Herein lies your misfortune. Moreover, not only do you lack the slightest knowledge or desire regarding pain and sorrow, but pain is too naked towards you, so it is natural that your vanity feels no inspiration towards this new beauty. But I will teach you the pleasure of absolute immortality... Towards the ugliness you once hated and avoided, or towards the calamity you feared and detested, I can savor hedonistic amusement, even transcending life and power. This is the most miserable and ecstatic symphony of skeletons, sulfuric acid, flesh, and starving spirits, and at the same time, it is when all fantasies and futures display a reality greater than reality, and all the ugly suffering of realization manifests a state of intoxication (酣醉境地) beyond imagination.
Oh, my only friend, your yearning for me is truly the soul's plea offered to the infinite world.

(Poet) Demon, when I long for you, my blood vessels boil (沸騰) from the flesh, and my soul cries out as if burning to death (燒死).

(Witch) But death and despair are a new liberation.

(Poet) Isn't death the forgetting (妄却) of all things?

(Witch) To forget all phenomena means to create all phenomena anew. It means seeing and hearing things completely different from what you now see, hear, and discern with your senses. Death is for you the highest, supreme pleasure, and at the same time, a solemn and grave style.
Life is for you most abstract and non-spiritual, whereas death is dramatic and, simultaneously, conceptualized reality.
When I reign over you with the exquisite fragrance of ethereal morphine, you can forget all ugliness and vanity and go only to the world you longed for without knowing it your whole life, to the world you yearned for without possessing it your whole life. Thus, death is entirely the originality of self-fantasy and, at the same time, rejects the impure elements of absolute objectivity. <37> Oh, Death, making the finite infinite, the mundane profound, making all things tragic, oh Death. Poet, sing high the hymn (讚美歌) of Death. Then the heavenly palace of art will shine forever, shrouded in sorrow.

(Poet) Oh sinful woman of lamentation, your eyebrows (眉墨) are like venomous snakes.

(Witch) But lamentation is the ultimate aesthetic experience, and sin (罪) is the power that profoundly expresses my will. And anyone living in a criminal psychology can truly return to their own self.

(Poet) Every time I hear your disguised words, I feel like I'm seeing a terrifying dream.

(Witch) A dream is dramatized reality. And disguise has a reality greater than what you have yet experienced. Can't you tell by how it always exaggerates your feelings?

(Poet) Demon, my heart is wrapped in fear. But I want to taste the poison spewing from your lips. If only that could be called love. Oh, cruel lover. Every time I hear your poisonous words, I realize the intense intoxication like fighting poison with poison, and the painful pleasure of convulsions (痙攣). Dear Queen, I offer all my vigor entirely to you. My soul has long labored to taste the fragrance of your poisonous flesh. Moreover, I know your flesh is immortal pleasure.

(Witch) Lewd Poet! If your passion rots and becomes maggots (蛆蟲), and you still endure the anguish and sorrow... Lover, I will embrace you.

(Poet) For you, I am willing to offer my passion and happiness to eternal anguish (懊惱).

(Witch) To soar (翶翔) into the higher, infinite world of symbols, one must tread the path of anguish. Lover! Satisfaction and joy are the ideals of the incomplete, while sorrow and anguish are the ideals of the complete.

(Poet) Oh, Demon of sorrow. All beauty is desolate. Amidst the late autumn void, or amidst destruction, storms, and the blue light of the setting sun, I will meet you secretly.

(Witch) Lover, I shall prepare much poison for you. And to make pain and mystery intimate, I will cure your disease of boredom (倦怠) with lewdness and intoxication...

(Poet) Oh, highest, worst Witch, my flesh has suffocated in an embrace that feels like melting away. Your poison spreads through my entire body, making my heart-sea boil. My youth rings the alarm bell of a shipwreck, and my music spends sorrowful years. <38>
When you command me to see the "Symbol of Immortality," my five senses (五管) blur, and I only see a mottled space. Then, when the impulse of a strange life arises in my confused brain, groundless regret (悔恨), joy, and the consciousness of lewdness flowed past me, bewitching like moonlight on a crisp autumn night.
At that time, stars scattered like sparks in the void, and a light green wind flowed diagonally through space like willow threads, when you and your sisters, holding youth in your arms, called to me, saying "Poet!" Oh, I don't know if this was merely the experience of a single moment (一刹那), but Witch, the agitation of passion extended my life to the gates of Hell (地獄門).
Seeing your beauty at this time... I thought this must surely be the palace of immortal expression and symbolism where noble mystery lies hidden. Truly, your beauty is the ideal style that we poets, through dreams, have painstakingly sought a thousand times over, through solemn centuries from past to future. How much universal time has been wasted because of this, and how much has our exhausted strength, searching in dark corners, dried up our creative power? Consequently, how much has it dampened the value of all human actions, the brilliance of ideals, and the high tide of life? But through your great suggestion, a new world has now appeared before me.
Oh, may there be eternal feasts and bewitchment in your palace.

(Witch) You are dreaming. Poet, your eyes see through eternity, your mouth reads the sermon of sin, your hands sculpt evil. Oh, fulfilled artist, in the marble steps you are now treading, there hides a maiden yearning like you. And around you are the tones of many Tosca's awaiting the sound of weeping.

(Poet) Oh, in your spirit hall, moonlight seems to dance upon the waves. As maidens fall into the water one by one, the sea seems to reach high tide.

(Witch) You are seeing the legend of sorrow. Your eyes see the state of a boat in the rapids, your mouth speaks that the secret of death is greater than the secret of life, your hands point to a distant uninhabited island. But in my domain where you are, many maidens with flower wreaths and pearls wish to adorn your youth. Do not the pearls take the form of a bride, and the flowers become years, wanting to meet you?

(Poet) Oh, in your spirit place, a bewitching bride is spying on my virginity. <39> And the years want to steal our youth.

(Witch) You seem to fear destiny. But in my domain, destiny becomes naked, hoping to meet secretly with victims. Look at my companion passing by you now. Doesn't she wish to offer you her beautiful naked body, like a poppy flower?

(Poet) Oh, Witch more beautiful than a poppy flower. Why do you have no jealousy? I love only you.

(Witch) You should love diverse beauty. I hope you live in my domain always with a humble heart. Here, everything is equal and fraternal love.

(Poet) That group of witches seems to have cried. Their eyelids are like violets soaked by a storm, their breath like a炽熱 (fervent) censer.

(Witch) They cry, agonize, despair, and suffer for their own pleasure.

(Poet) They seem dressed in crimson evening gowns. Their heads are like black mushrooms (茸), their hands and feet like lilies.

(Witch) They prefer poppy-like red blood from their milk-white bodies.

(Poet) Their voices are full of danger and poison, yet sadder than Chopin's mournful pieces.

(Witch) Isn't verbal poison more charming and influential than virtue? Poison is nobler than virtue because poison psychologically opens up your mind entirely, whereas virtue barely stimulates the passive capacity of preconceptions.

(Poet) That group of witches seems to be quietly thinking about something. Otherwise, why would they be in that posture? It's like a scene from a fairy tale play trying to symbolically express the mystery of life. Isn't their breath like the sigh of a heartbroken lover slumped over a piano, and don't their eyes show the thrill of a wanderer awakened to a new wonder? Oh, before their eyes, it seems strange plants, a purple sea, golden fragrant powder, orange moonlight like a beehive, and red lamplike swarms of bees appear like a mirage. Otherwise, why would they be in that posture? I want to know. Their white foreheads, like evening primroses, look up (仰) at the void as if receiving some suggestion. If I were to secretly meet them on a mysterious staircase. Oh, my energy, active amidst pleasure, <40> might go into a frenzy and try to open their fragile, clean breasts, though I cannot know—oh, no. Listening to their whispers (囁語), I might calmly melt my tense feelings and receive, like them, an ineffably subtle vibration beyond sorrow. But beauty that deviates from the norm. What if you are a lewd witch who scorns my spirit and covets my flesh? Oh, anything is fine. As long as you don't arouse the boredom (倦怠) of fear, what does it matter if you are a lewd woman, a maiden, or a sorceress?

(Witch) Poet, come closer here. That group of witches is in that posture to gain new sorrow, pain, and hypnosis. You must absolutely not disturb their pleasure. They inflict cruel revenge on those who interfere with their pleasure.

(Poet) Demon, my heart is sorrowful.

(Witch) Oh, the night deepens and is quiet. My domain is wrapped in blue light, and the time for hypnosis has come. Poet, I will console you with a final embrace. Parting is sad, but your fantasy is almost at its final act.

(Poet) Your peach-colored cheeks have somehow turned like blue crystal. Oh, the stars have changed like fireflies, and the clouds have become pale blue small waterfalls. I feel as though I have returned from the infinite to mystery, from mystery to eternity.

(Witch) Oh, your fantasy is almost complete. Poet, the sorrow of parting is cherished like a fragrant autumn sunset.

(Poet) Oh, oh, dear Witch.

(Poet) Having parted from the Demon, I am like a reed boat adrift in a storm. My heart-sea is soaked in sorrow and tears. But if, by luck (僥倖), the black clouds and winds were to wrap my boat and guide me just once more beyond those scattered celestial bodies, oh, I would hoist high the sail of my fantasy and wander through the atmosphere, searching for my lover, but nature is foolish (愚頓) and shows me no miracle. Oh, incapable nature, I will exert the passion of my creation and force a miracle upon you. Behold! When my pitiful despair becomes enraged and wails amidst the lightning flashes, new hope raises a red flag before the Heavenly Palace Gate (天宮門) and commands me to advance against the current... Oh, Demon, I can meet you secretly again. ...

(God) ! Deluded (망녕된) Poet. Why do you not respect me? <41> Did I not place Nature, that great laborer, for you, giving you health, food, and pleasure?

(Poet) Nature, to me, is mute (啞), deaf (聾), and blind.

(God) !!

(Poet) God, I see your dignity (尊嚴) only in your demonic existence. Even amidst hypocrisy and cunning, I can discover your demonic sincerity. Oh, ambitious God, you have greatly tormented humans until now. Humans have finally come to realize this. And your vanity has become completely bankrupt because of this.
Your ambition to possess the Witch through honor and authority has come to nothing, like bubbles on water.

(God) !!!

(Poet) God, your joy and peace will also not last long, so quickly regret (悔恨) your past wrongs and weep with me.

(God) !!!

(Poet) Do you still dream of the vanity of Heaven (天國)? Do you not know that even good people and your son Jesus have already grown weary (倦怠) of peace, satisfaction, and joy?
When we decorated the Gates of Hell (地獄門) dazzlingly and extinguished the fires with tears, Jesus came to us secretly and consoled us with words of thanks and praise.

(God) Ah—that certainly never happened. Poet—please, say no more from now on!

(Poet) I am by no means delivering a sentence of judgment (審判) upon you. Nor am I seeking revenge because you disturbed our pleasure. I am merely awaiting your repentance.

(God) !!!

(Poet) Oh, oh, can you not hear those footsteps? It seems someone is coming.

(God) ?!!

(Poet) Oh, can you not hear that whisper (囁語)? It seems someone is calling me.

(God) !!!

(Poet) Look how the air has turned a fragrant (馥郁) peach color. Poppy flowers fall to the ground, one by one. A gift for whom?

(God) !!!

(Poet) God! It is nothing to be so frightened about. Surely, good fortune will come to me from this.

(God) !!!!

(Poet) It seems to be the Witch coming to report to me the pleasures of Hell.

(God) Oh!!

(Witch) Beloved Poet, what is your wish, seeking to meet me secretly in such a hazy atmosphere?

(God) Oh—oh—Witch!..

(Poet) Demon, God has fainted.

(Witch) Poet, what concern is God's fainting to us?

(Poet) Oh—Dear Demon.
...(In the July rains)...<42>
<35-42>

**Summary**

This dramatic dialogue features a Poet enthralled by a Demon (also called Witch/Fairy), rejecting traditional notions of beauty and morality. The Demon advocates embracing suffering, ugliness, sin, and death as the true path to profound aesthetic experience, absolute pleasure, and immortality, contrasting this with the "cheap," hypocritical happiness offered by a conventional God. The Poet is drawn into the Demon's world, experiencing intense, often painful, ecstasy and visions. The play culminates with the arrival of the Demon causing God, depicted as fearful and diminished, to faint, leaving the Poet and Demon united. From a historical perspective, the work reflects the influence of Western decadent and symbolist literature (e.g., Baudelaire) on Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. Its radical rejection of conventional morality and divinity (represented by God), and the embrace of intense, dark, and transgressive experiences offered by the Demon, could be interpreted as an artistic form of rebellion against imposed norms or an exploration of existential angst and alternative value systems within the restrictive context of Japanese rule in 1922. The questioning of established authority and the search for a "new beauty" resonate with the intellectual ferment of the era.

不滅의 象徵
						林蘆月
-(人物) 詩人. 惡魔. 神,-

「나는 모든 暴棄와, 妄想과, 悽慘과, 深秘속에서, 한 哀然한 亂調를 듯는다. 그래서 나의 熱望은 天地壞滅과 創造새에 새로운 美를 엇고저 애쓴다.
나는 有限에서. 無限에, 永遠에서. 刹那에, 浮遊하는 放浪兒일다.
모든 事象에 對하야 悲劇的 意味를 發見한때는 自己에게 忠實을 다-한때이다.
完全한 者의 理想은 不完全한 것일다.」

(詩人) 오-오- 親愛하는 惡魔여 너는 宇宙의 모든 秘密을 다-가지고 잇고나. 네가 黙黙히 夢想하는 奈路의 끗에는 모든 驚異와 알지못할 설음과. 蠱惑이 숨어잇는 줄을 안다.
想像力의 不斷한 나의 動搖가 恒常 究極의 秘密을 찾고저 애쓸때 나의 타는 입슐은 灰色空間에 붉은 線條를 그려가면서 네게 이러케 뭇는다.
「永久不滅의 象徵은 무엇이냐? 妖精이어 人類의 靈肉을 힘의 極度까지 翶翔시킬 術은 무엇이냐. 새로운 藝術의 製作을 供給할 材料는 누가 가지고 잇느냐? 오- 惡魔여 世界는 將次 너의 熱望대로 期必코 夢想家와 異論者와 戰敗者와 變態者의 所有가 될줄로 確信한다. 그리고 人間은 아즉것 몰랏섯든 너의 妖艶한 美貌를 처음으로 보고 氣絶할때 神은 고까잉을 마시고 네한테 慇懃히 秋波를 보낼 때가 잇스리라. 妖精이어. 科學者가 새로운 별(星)을 發見하야 奇怪한＜35＞ 生物과 自然의 變態的法則을 알려주며. 藝術家가 새로운 衝動과 暗示에 의하야 樣式의 奇蹟을 行할때 너의 美貌는 더욱 더욱 時間을 不滅케하며 空間을 새롭게 할 것이다.
오-나의 唯一한 女王이어. 絶對無窮의 快樂은 무엇이냐. 人間과 自然 새에 不思議할 傳說의 濃霧를 일으켜 粗雜한 本能을 生活과 戀愛와 慾望에서 淤離시킬 魔術은 누가 아느냐?
人類의 生活意識을 어떤 神秘한 情調에 依하야 醇化시킬 힘은 누구에게 잇는고, 그리고 生에 對한 모든 重荷를 解除시킬 힘은? 魔神이어 그대일 것 가튼면 나의 모든 懊惱를 가볍게 할 수가 잇것마는...  - 魔女여 너는 方今 구름을 呼吸하고 江을 밟노나. 그리고 푸른별꼿은 一一이 네머리털억에 매어달려 바람에 動搖가 되는가 십다. 그러나 네 周圍에 잇는 모든 風光이 하나도 自然가티는 아니보여. 江우를 흘러가는 붉은 꼿송이도, 天空을 비추는 흰 바람도, 다- 人工的自然인가 십다. 나는 네 周圍를 볼때에 凄慘하고 異狀한 생각이 난다. 將次 무슨 일이 생길는지. 또는 方今 무슨 일이 생기고 난 뒤인지 알 수가 업고나. 그러나 나는 저-파라솔 보다도 더 큰 꼿을 바라볼 때에 풀닙이 구름을 꿰여 올라간 것을 볼때에. 참을수업는 蠱惑을 깨닷는다. 더구나 물빗이 시컴한 것을 볼때에 그-물 건너서 너와 密會할 것을 생각할때에 오-솔음이 끼친다.
方今 너는 붉은 그물(綱)을 가지고 붉은 꼿송이를 江에서 건지노나. 그 그물새로 붉은 피가 흔르지 안느냐.
