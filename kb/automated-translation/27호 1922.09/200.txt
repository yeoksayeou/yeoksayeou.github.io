Magazine Title: 개벽 제27호
Publication Date: 1922년 09월 01일
Article Title: 옛날의 노래여 (Song of Olden Days)
Author: 金明淳
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

옛날의 노래여
						金明淳

一
고요한 옛날의 노래여 그는...
내 어머니 입에서 울어나서
가장 神妙하게 사라지는 音響이러라
어미니의 노래여 사랑의 嘆息이어

Song of olden days...
It emerged weeping from my mother's lips,
The most mysteriously vanishing sound.
O song of my mother, sigh of love.

二
「타방 타방 타방네야 너 어대를 울며 가니
내 어머니 몸진 곳에 젓먹으러 울며 간다」
이는 내 어머니의 가리키신 長恨歌이나
물결이는 말못미테 이것만 알겟노라

"T'abang, t'abang, t'abang-neya, where do you go weeping?
I go weeping to suckle at the place where my mother birthed me."
This is the *Changhanka* (長恨歌 - Song of Everlasting Sorrow) my mother taught,
Beneath the rippling, unspoken words, this much I understand.

三
黃昏을 울리는 呻吟은 施律만 술질 듯 애탈 때
젓꼿빗으르 열린 들길에는 微風쪼차 설어라＜43＞
옛날에 날 사랑하시든 내 어머니를
큰 사랑을 세상에서 일흔 설음이니,

When the moaning (呻吟) that makes the dusk (黃昏) weep seems to reel only with melody, consumed with grief,
On the field path opened to the color of milk flowers, sorrow follows even the gentle breeze (微風).<43>
My mother who loved me in the old days—
It is the sorrow of having lost that great love in the world.

四
오래인 노래여 내게 옛말슴을 들리사
어린이의 설음 속에 引導하소서
不老草로 繡노흔 綠衣를 닙히소서
그러면 나는 萬年靑의 빨간 열매 가트리다

O ancient song, let me hear the old words,
Guide me amid the sorrow of a child.
Clothe me in green garments (綠衣) embroidered (繡) with the herb of immortality (不老草).
Then I shall be like the red fruit of the Rohdea japonica (萬年靑).

五
말을 니즌 노래여 音響만 남아서...
길다한 곳에 레-데江이 홀릅듸까
오- 그러면 그는 나를 淨化해 줄 것이오
웃음빗을 모은 神秘의 거울이 되리다

O song that has forgotten words, only sound remaining...
Does the River Lethe flow in that distant place?
Oh- then it shall purify (淨化) me,
And become a mirror of mystery (神秘) gathering the hues of laughter.

六
無言歌여 다만 音響이어 나를 이끌어
그대의 말슴 사라진 곳에 저 젓꼿 빗길에
내 어머니 몸진 곳에 山을 넘고 물을 건너라
옛날의 노래여 사라지는 音響이어.
-(七, 十日)- ＜44＞
<43-44>

Song without words (無言歌), only sound, lead me
To the place where your words vanished, on that milk-flower-colored path,
To the place where my mother birthed me, crossing mountains and water.
O song of olden days, vanishing sound.
-(July, 10th)- <44>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Myŏng-sun expresses a deep yearning for the poet's deceased mother, evoked through the memory of her song. The song, remembered primarily as a sound or melody whose words are fading, represents lost love, sorrow, and a connection to the past. The poet recalls a specific lullaby taught by her mother and seeks guidance and purification through this remembered sound, imagining a journey back to her origins and finding solace in mythical elements like the herb of immortality and the River Lethe. From a historical perspective, the poem's intense focus on personal loss, memory, and the melancholic beauty of fading traditions (represented by the mother's song) could resonate with the broader sense of cultural displacement and yearning for connection felt by Koreans during the colonial period. While deeply personal, the themes of sorrow, memory, and the search for solace through connection to maternal origins might offer historians insight into the affective landscape of individuals navigating that era.
