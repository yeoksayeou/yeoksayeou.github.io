黃浦海岸
						岸曙
Tears

Tears—
More than the stars fading
in the brightening night sky,
in their faint, diminishing light,
rather, on your colorless face,
still without color,
around your two eyes,
barely visible, gathering and flowing—
those teardrops,
I, far away on the road of a foreign land (異域),
gazing up now at the stars
in the midsummer night sky,
righteously, longingly, am thinking of them.

Balsam Flower＜31＞

Crimson, crimson flower,
dyed with blood-water,
because of a profound, boundless,
dark love, they say.
On your colorless face,
a smile playing,
you stand with your head bowed,
Oh, crimson, crimson
flower of longing (相思), Balsam!
Today, too, one day
has imperceptibly passed, and it is evening.

Body of a Coconut Palm
On the coconut tree's—
on my body too, spring flowers bloom,
Oh, but the body is a coconut palm,
where are those flowers?
When the days pass and they ripen,
when the days pass, ripen, and fall,
the sea is boundlessly wide.＜32＞

Passing Spring
Oh, young heart,
In the May night sky, fading stars,
On a passing spring evening, falling, scattering flowers,
Oh, what to do about them.

Oh, young heart,
Together with the passing spring flowers,
the heart of youth disappearing unbeknownst to others.
Oh, what to do about it.

Homesickness
Even the swallow, flying high in the sky (空中),
follows the season of its time, seeking its old nest (녯깃),
On the Hwangp'o (黃浦) coast where boat sounds are leisurely heard,
where flowers bloom in the grassy fields and pine pollen flies in the pine groves—
my village under the blue sky, my old house,
in the spring season, how could I possibly forget?

Hwangp'o Sea
Passing the long, long-waisted road＜33＞
Enter the large loop that becomes a single valley,
And then, climbing step by step, breathlessly,
up the tall pass where the moon-rock sits perched,
Ah, the white sea, how wide it is!
This is my hometown (故鄕), the Hwangp'o sea.

Sigh
Saying he's a detestable fellow,
resolving firmly
not even to think of him in dreams,
suddenly, the scene of that day appears.

Truly, those times passed so well.
Saying he's someone not even in my heart,
believing he's all forgotten,
no, no, while laughing,
suddenly, that person's face appears.

Truly, there is no way to forget.＜34＞
＜31-34＞

**Summary**

This collection of seven short poems by An Sŏ, titled "Hwangp'o Coast," explores themes of longing, memory, nostalgia, and the passage of time. The poems express a sense of displacement ("foreign land"), homesickness for a place associated with the Hwangp'o coast (likely the Huangpu River area in China, suggesting the author might be living abroad), sorrow over fleeting youth and spring, and the persistent nature of memory, particularly concerning a past relationship or person. The imagery often contrasts the beauty of nature (stars, flowers, sea) with inner turmoil or sadness. For historians, the poems offer a glimpse into the personal sentiments and possible experiences of a Korean intellectual or writer during the early colonial period (1922). The references to a "foreign land," homesickness ("思鄕"), and the specific mention of "Hwangp'o" could reflect the increasing movement of Koreans abroad, including to China, during this era, possibly due to political or economic reasons, and the complex emotions associated with such displacement.

눈물
밝아가는 밤한울에
희미하여가는 여튼빗의
슬어저가는 별보다도,
오히려 아즉 빗갈업는
그대의 얼굴의,
두 눈가에
보일 듯 말듯키 고여 흐르는
그 눈물의 방울을
나는 멀게도 異域의 길가에서
只今 盛한 녀름의
밤한울의 별을 울얼으며,
의롭게도 그립게 생각하고 잇노라.

鳳仙꼿＜31＞

새빨간 새빨간
피물을 들인 꼿이어,
그윽하게도 가이업는
어둡은 사랑때문이라고.
빗갈업는 얼굴에
微笑를 띄우고
너는 머리를 숙이고 잇서라,
오오 새빨간 새빨
相思의 꼿, 鳳仙이여!
하로의 오늘도
어느덧 넘어가고 저녁이러라.

椰子의 몸
椰子나무의
내몸에도 봄의 꼿은 피어라,
오오 그러나 몸은 椰子,
그 꼿은 어대랴,
날이지내 익어질때,
날이지내 익어서 떨어지면
바다는 限도 업시 넓어라.＜32＞

가는 봄
어린 맘아,
五月의 밤 한울에는 슬어저가는 별,
가는 봄 저녁에는 떨어저 흐터지는 꼿,
오오 그것을 어쪄랴.

어린 맘아,
가는 봄꼿과 함끠,
남모르게 업서지는 靑春의 맘.
오오 그것을 어쪄랴.

思鄕
한울 空中 놉게도 나는 제비의 몸으로도
한때의 철을 딸하, 녯깃을 차저오거든
한가하게도 배소리가 들리는 黃浦의 海岸,
잔듸밧엔 꼿이 피고 솔밧에 松花가 나는
프른 한울알에의 넷마을, 낡은 내집을
때의 봄철, 내가 어 니즐가보냐.

黃浦바다
기나긴 긴허리의 길을 지내서는＜33＞
외마디의 골작이 되는 큰 고리로 들어라,
그러고는 우뚝 안즌 놉흔 嶺의 달바위재를
한거름 한거름 숨차게 올라서면,
하-얀 바다, 넓기도 하여라,
이는 나의 故鄕, 黃浦의 바다.

歎息
밉쌀스럽은 녀석이라며,

굿게 決心하는 사이에

正말로 그때는 잘도 지내서.
