Artificial Laborers
						Čapek (Author), Pak Yŏng-hŭi (Translator)
Act 3
Same as Act 3 [sic, likely means Act 2], Helena's reception room.
From the room on the left, the sound of Helena playing the piano can be heard.
Domin enters the room. Dr. Gall looks outside through a gap in the armored, closed window.
Alquist sits rear left. He sits with his face buried in his hands.
Dr. Gall: How many of them are there, anyway?
Domin: Who? The Artificial Laborers?
Dr. Gall: Yes. They're swarming like bees on the other side of the courtyard. But how can they be so deathly quiet? It's truly frightening, being surrounded in silence.
Domin: So what are they waiting for? They'll surely make a move soon. Then that iron fence will crumble like scrap metal!
Dr. Gall: They don't seem to be armed, though.
Domin: We might not even live another five minutes. They'll crush us living beings like an avalanche (雪崩). But why aren't they coming out?
Dr. Gall: Yes.
Domin: Just think about what will happen to us in just five minutes. They'll grab us with great force. Gall! It's utter despair.
Alquist: What is the lady doing?
Domin: I don't know. Maybe practicing some new piece on the piano.
Alquist: Yes, practicing again! <58>

(A short pause)

Dr. Gall: Look here. We really made a huge mistake.
Domin: What?
Dr. Gall: We made all their faces the same. One hundred thousand identical faces are looking this way. One hundred thousand expressionless things. It's like a vicious nightmare.
Domin: Yes. What would happen if their faces were all different?
Dr. Gall: Would it be this frightening then?
Domin: (Looking towards the harbor with binoculars) Ah. What cargo are they unloading from the ship *America*? What is that?
Dr. Gall: I hope it's not weapons.
(Fabry and Hallemeier rush into the room carrying wires.)
Fabry: Alright, Hallemeier! Organize those wires properly.
Hallemeier: Okay. Just the last task. Has anything happened?
Dr. Gall: We're completely surrounded.
Hallemeier: Barbed wire fences are set up on the roads and stairs. Give me some water. Yes. (Drinks water while looking outside) Hmm. Domin! Seeing them is worse than death. Looking at them makes me feel like I'm going to die from fear.
Fabry: Come on, hurry up!
Dr. Gall: Fabry! What are you going to do with those wires?
Fabry: I'm setting up an electrical system. Then we can send a current through that iron fence anytime. Anyone who touches that fence is done for. Anyway, there must be some friends there too.
Dr. Gall: Where?
Fabry: The power station. I hope there are still people there. (Goes near the desk behind the sofa and turns on the lamp) Yes. There are still people there. They're still working. (Turns off the lamp) As soon as the current flows, that's it.
Hallemeier: (Turning around from near the window) The defenses are well prepared now, Fabry!
Fabry: Your defenses are good. Alright, now we can run 1,200 volts of current through that iron fence.
Domin: (Lowering the binoculars) Where is Busman?
Fabry: He's downstairs in the office. Doing some calculations <59> there. I told him to come here. We all need to gather for a meeting.
(The sound of Helena playing the piano comes from the library. Hallemeier stands at the door, listening.)
Alquist: Thank goodness. The lady can still play the piano.
(Busman enters, carrying a ledger. He nearly trips over the wires.)
Dr. Gall: Careful! Watch the wires!
Busman: (Placing the ledger on the desk) This is the ledger. We need to settle the accounts first—. Because this settlement cannot wait until next year.
Busman: (Leaning near the window) Why is it so deathly quiet?
Dr. Gall: Can't you see anything?
Busman: I only see blue. Everywhere, all around, it's just blue.
Dr. Gall: Those are the Artificial Laborers.
(Busman sits facing the desk and opens the ledger.)
Domin: The Artificial Laborers are unloading weapons from the steamship *America*.
Busman: What? Then what should we do? How can we stop them?
Domin: We cannot stop what they are doing.
Busman: Alright, then let's finish the settlement. (Continues working.)
Domin: (Looking at the harbor with binoculars) It's all over. From the steamship *Ultimus*, they're aiming cannons at us.
Dr. Gall: Who is doing such a thing?
Domin: Artificial Laborers have boarded the deck.
Fabry: Yes. Then, then of course, the end has come for us!
Dr. Gall: Why do you say that?
Fabry: Because Artificial Laborers are excellent shots.
Domin: It's true. There's nothing we can do. This is the end.

(A short pause)

Dr. Gall: Teaching the Artificial Laborers warfare was Europe's fault. Damn it. European policy gave us no rest. Making Artificial Laborers into soldiers was a sin. <60>
Alquist: Creating the Artificial Laborers was the sin.
Domin: What?
Alquist: Creating the Artificial Laborers was the sin.
Domin: Hey, you're mistaken. Even now, I wouldn't have such regrets.
Alquist: You have no regrets even now?
Domin: Even now, I have no regrets. Today is the last day of civilization—. Creating the Artificial Human was a truly great achievement.
Busman: (In a low voice) Three hundred sixty million.
Domin: (In a forceful voice) Alquist! This is our end. We are speaking as if more than half dead. Labor—the dreadful, humiliating labor that humans rightly deserved to bear—it wouldn't be a bad dream to shatter that servitude. Work was too back-breaking, life too painful. So, to try and overcome it—.
Alquist: The Rossum father and son didn't think like that. Old Mr. Rossum did it merely as an atheist's wicked prank. His son started manufacturing Artificial Humans solely for the purpose of making lots of money. And the company shareholders, too. They didn't think like you; they just coveted dividends. In other words, shareholder dividends have brought humanity to ruin.
Domin: (Agitated) Truly, there's nothing as hateful as dividends. But do you think we worked even one hour for the shareholders? I worked for my own satisfaction. I wished in my heart that humans could become rulers, that they wouldn't work just for food. I wished that humans wouldn't have their souls (魂) shattered for the sake of another's machine. I didn't think of leaving anything untouched in this dreadful social structure (組織). It was because lack (缺乏) awakened (覺醒) me. I hoped for a new era. And I thought—.
Alquist: About what?
Domin: (Gently) I wished. To transform all humanity into the world's aristocracy (貴族). To create nobles served by billions of mechanical slaves (奴隷). And to transform humans into beings of unlimited (無制限) freedom and perfection (完成). <61> So that eventually, they could even become something more than human.
Alquist: Supermen (超人)?
Domin: That's right. For humans to live only 100 years is too short! I wished for the future human race to have a lifespan of 200 years.
Busman: (In a low voice) Balance carried forward: four hundred twenty million.

(A short pause)

Hallemeier: (Standing by the left door) Truly, there is nothing as good as music! Listen to that sound, everyone. Music clarifies the mind and makes it beautiful.
Fabry: What?
Hallemeier: We mustn't be ignorant of this dawn light (曙光) of life! I am quite the hedonist (快樂主義者). It would have been nice if we had been drawn to such things from the beginning. (Stands by the window and looks outside.)
Fabry: Drawn to what?
Hallemeier: To pleasure (享樂). To beautiful things. No, there are plenty of beautiful things! The world is something to be loved. But think about what joys we have tasted?
Busman: (In a low voice) Four billion five hundred twenty million.
Hallemeier: (At the window) Living is also a great thing. Life is... Fabry! Send the current through the iron fence.
Fabry: Why?
Hallemeier: The Artificial Laborers are grabbing hold of the iron fence.
Dr. Gall: (At the window) Press the switch!
(Fabry presses the switch.)
Hallemeier: Done. Well done. One caught. Two, three, four dead.
Dr. Gall: The Artificial Laborers are starting to retreat (退却).
Hallemeier: Five down.
Dr. Gall: (Leaving the window) The first engagement!
Hallemeier: (Joyfully) They burned black and died. Hahaha. We don't need to surrender (降服)! (Sits in a chair.)
Domin: (Rubbing his face (낫)) We've been dead bodies for 100 years already. What remains here now are just ghosts (幽靈). No, perhaps it's been like this from the start. My neck must have been squeezed long ago. Fabry! Your head must have been pierced. Gall! Your hands and feet must have been torn off. And Hallemeier, you must have been stabbed with a knife. <62>
Hallemeier: I must have been stabbed with a knife? Hahaha. Still, I won't surrender.

(A short pause) Gentlemen, why are you all so quiet? Say something! Can't you speak?
Alquist: Whose fault (非) is this, anyway?
Hallemeier: Nobody's. It's the sin (罪) of the Artificial Humans.
Busman: (In a low voice) Five billion two hundred million.
Alquist: No, that's not it either. The sin lies with us. With you, with Domin, with me, and with all of us. Our selfish (利己的) purposes, the progress (進步) of profit (利益), have caused humanity's destruction (滅亡). We are now punishing ourselves for our greatness.
Hallemeier: Don't talk nonsense. Humanity cannot be treated so carelessly. Hahaha.
Alquist: We were wrong. We were wrong.
Dr. Gall: No. It is my fault that this happened.
Fabry: Why you?
Dr. Gall: Because I modified (改造) the Artificial Humans.
Busman: (Standing up) What on earth did you do?
Dr. Gall: I modified the character of the Artificial Humans. I changed the manufacturing method (製造方法). I fixed two or three things within the Artificial Humans' gaps. Among them, among them—their irritability (刺戟性).
Hallemeier: (Jumping up) Why such a foolish thing?
Busman: Why, what reason did you have for doing such a thing?
Fabry: And why are you only telling us this now?
Dr. Gall: Because I kept it a secret. I tried to modify the Artificial Laborers into humans. In some respects, the Artificial Humans are already superior to us. The Artificial Humans are stronger than us.
Fabry: Yes, so what does that have to do with the Artificial Humans' rebellion?
Dr. Gall: It has everything to do with it. The Artificial Humans are no longer machines. After they started thinking they were superior, they began to hate anyone who was human.
Domin: We truly are ghosts!
Fabry: Stop it, Domin! This is not the time for such talk. And Gall!
Domin: Fabry! Blood is flowing from your face. Were you hit by a bullet somewhere?
Fabry: Be quiet! Gall! You changed the Artificial Laborers' <63> attack method [sic, likely means 'constitution' or 'nature'], didn't you?
Dr. Gall: Yes.
Fabry: Then didn't you consider what the result of experimenting with that would be?
Dr. Gall: Of course, I knew it would come to this.
(Helena enters from the left.)
Fabry: Then why did you do such a thing?
Dr. Gall: For my own satisfaction (滿足). It was an experiment done as I pleased.
Helena: That's a lie, Doctor!
Fabry: Madam!
Domin: Helena! We are all going to die. Show me your face. (Tries to embrace Helena)
Helena: Stop it, please.
Domin: No, no, embrace me. Yes, Helena. You mustn't abandon me. Helena, you are my life itself.
Helena: I won't abandon you. But I have something to say to everyone. The fault does not lie with Dr. Gall—.
Domin: Tell me. Doesn't Gall have to take responsibility (責任)?
Helena: No. It's because I asked him to do it. Dr. Gall! Tell everyone. Didn't I ask you long ago—?
Dr. Gall: I did it taking responsibility upon myself.
Helena: That's a lie. I asked him to put souls (靈魂) into those Artificial Humans.
Domin: Putting in souls and whatnot is pointless.
Helena: Dr. Gall said so too. But he said it could be done physiologically (生理學的).
Hallemeier: Physiological relation?
Helena: Yes. But even just doing that was satisfying enough for me.
Domin: Why?
Helena: If we made the Artificial Humans like us humans, I thought we could understand (理解) each other better. If the Artificial Humans became like humans, I thought we wouldn't hate them.
Domin: There is nothing that hates humans like humans do.
Helena: Please, don't say such things. It is truly terrifying that we and the Artificial Humans hate each other. Therefore, I asked Dr. <64> Gall to modify the Artificial Humans. He did not want to do so.
Domin: But he did it anyway.
Helena: It was because of me.
Dr. Gall: I only did it as an experiment.
Helena: No, Dr. Gall! You couldn't refuse my request.
Domin: Why?
Helena: Well, yes.
Domin: Yes, because he loved Helena. Like everyone else.

(A short pause)

Hallemeier: (Approaching the window) It's hopeless. The ground is completely covered with Artificial Humans! Will every single wall turn into them?
Busman: Gall! When exactly did you start doing such a thing?
Dr. Gall: Three years ago.
Busman: Yes, I see. Then how many Artificial Humans did you improve (改良)?
Dr. Gall: About two or three hundred.
Busman: Yes. Then for every million good, old-style (舊式) Artificial Humans, there's one Gall-style improved one!
Domin: What does that mean?
Busman: Then the improved Artificial Humans aren't actually such a significant (重大) factor.
Fabry: Busman's words are correct.
Busman: I think this way, gentlemen! Do you know where the blame for this disturbance (騷亂) lies?
Fabry: What is it?
Busman: It's because we made too many Artificial Humans. We knew that someday the Artificial Humans would become stronger than humans. But we strengthened the Artificial Humans all at once, at full speed. Domin! You too. Fabry! You too, and me—.
Domin: Are you listing our crimes?
Busman: Have you ever thought about limiting (制限) production numbers (製産數)? There was definitely a need to limit the manufacturing numbers.
Helena: So you mean we cannot avoid destruction (滅亡)? <65>
Busman: Why do you say such things, madam? It doesn't seem like we will perish. In any case, I don't want to perish.
Domin: Absolutely. So, what are you going to do?
Busman: I will flee from destruction. That's all.
Domin: Come now, don't talk like that, Busman!
Busman: It's the truth. Hah—Gentlemen. It seems we can all run away quickly.
Domin: How?
Busman: It's not particularly difficult. I will do it splendidly. Leave everything to me. Then I will go to the Artificial Laborer side and consult (協議) with them.
Domin: Will it go well?
Busman: Of course. For example, I will go to the Artificial Humans and say this: "Respected Artificial Laborers! You possess everything! You possess intelligence, you possess strength, and you also possess weapons. But we possess just one interesting item. It is a torn, old, yellow piece of paper—"
Domin: Rossum's manuscript (原稿)?
Busman: That's right. And then I'll say this: "On this piece of paper is written your splendid original formula, that is, the explanation of the precious method for your manufacture." And then I'll say this: "Respected Artificial Laborers! Without what is written on this piece of paper, you will not be able to produce a single new comrade. In twenty years, there won't even be an Artificial Human specimen to display in a zoo. There will be not a single Artificial Human. Respected friends! This is of the utmost importance to you. But if you will forgive all of us humans on this Rossum Island and let us board that ship, we will give you, in exchange, this factory and also the method for making Artificial Humans. If you forgive our escape, we will forgive [sic, likely means 'give'] you your manufacturing method. Respected Artificial Humans! This will be a splendid exchange." Everyone, this is my only thought. <66>
Domin: Busman! Are you saying you'll sell that manuscript?
Busman: Absolutely! If things don't work out, there's no other way. —Whether we sell it or they discover it, that's up to you!
Domin: Busman! How about we tear up Rossum's manuscript?
Busman: Then everything is over for us... Not just the manuscript. Ourselves too. Think carefully.
Domin: There are more than 30 of our comrades on this island. Are you saying we should sell the secret formula (秘法), unleashing the servitude (奴役) of humanity, to save many souls?
Busman: Absolutely. You surely haven't gone mad. So, who will go to sell the manuscript?
Domin: Busman! Stop talking nonsense!
Busman: Fine, let's decide to sell it. But afterwards—
Domin: What?
Busman: Think about this. After we board the ship *Ultimus*, we stuff our ears with cotton and crawl into some corner. Then you aim the cannons at the factory and just turn it to dust. Then Rossum's secret method for manufacturing Artificial Humans—
Fabry: No. We can't do that!
Domin: Busman! You are no gentleman. If we sell it, we must conduct the transaction openly and justly.
Busman: But for the benefit (利益) of humanity—
Domin: For the benefit of humanity, we should be honest.
Hallemeier: Come on, stop this useless argument (議論)!
Domin: This is a serious matter. We are about to sell the fate (運命) of humankind. Shall we sell? Or shall we perish? Fabry!
Fabry: Sell it!
Domin: Gall?
Dr. Gall: Sell it!
Domin: Hallemeier?
Hallemeier: Of course, sell it!
Domin: Alquist?
Alquist: If it accords with God's will, sell it!
Domin: Alright, then let's do as you all wish. <67>
Helena: Aren't you going to ask me?
Domin: True. But Helena doesn't need to worry about this matter.
Fabry: Who will go to negotiate?
Busman: I will!
Domin: Wait a moment. I'll go get the manuscript now. (Exits to the right room)
Helena: Look here, don't go.
(Helena sinks heavily into a chair.)
Fabry: (Looking out the window) If we don't escape from you... You've become zealous in your riot (騷亂). So be it. If only there was a ship to preserve human life—
Dr. Gall: Don't worry, madam. We intend to sail far away from here. We'll try to start a new life again.
Helena: Dr. Gall! Please don't say anything to me.
Fabry: It's not too late yet. We'll make a small ship into a small country. Alquist will build houses for us. Then you, madam, will rule over us.
Hallemeier: Madam, what Fabry says is absolutely correct.
Helena: (Forcing herself) Stop it. Stop it.
Busman: Ah! I don't intend to start a new life again. The earth is the most suitable place for me.
Fabry: Our little country will become the center of future life. In that refuge, we can gather our strength. Yes. In 200 years, we might be able to conquer the world again.
Alquist: Do you still believe such things today?
Fabry: Absolutely. Even today!
Busman: Yes, madam. Our plan is not so flawed.
(Domin rushes into the room)
Domin: (In a strained voice) Where did you put Rossum's manuscript?
Busman: It should be in your safe.
Domin: Someone—took it!
Dr. Gall: That can't be right?
Domin: Who could have taken it?
Helena: (Standing up) I took it. <68>
Domin: Where did you put it?
Helena: I will tell you everything, just please forgive me.
Domin: Where did you put it, I say?
Helena: This morning— I burned it. Both volumes together.
Domin: Without leaving anything? Where? In this fireplace?
Helena: (Kneeling) Please forgive me, yes.
Domin: (Going over to the fireplace) There's nothing here! Nothing but ashes. What's this? (Picks up a blackened paper fragment from the fireplace and reads) "Add to this—"
Dr. Gall: Let me see. "Add to this vitalin [Living Protoplasm/생활소] and—" It's no use.
Domin: This is part of the secret formula (秘法).
Busman: What are we going to do?
Domin: Well, now there's no hope left. Get up, Helena.
Helena: Please forgive me.
Domin: Get up! I cannot forgive you—.
Fabry: (Helping Helena up) Please, don't trouble us.
Helena: I have stopped the dreadful thing.
Fabry: Madam! It's no use being so afraid.
Domin: Gall! Can't you remember Rossum's manuscript?
Dr. Gall: That's out of the question. It's incredibly complex, impossible.
Domin: I believe it. Our lives depend solely on it.
Dr. Gall: Without experimenting... it's difficult to do.
Domin: Then I want to try experimenting.
Dr. Gall: We can't experiment without several years passing. Besides, first and foremost, I am not Rossum.
Busman: Despair. It can't be helped.
Domin: So this was the greatest victory of human intellect, this pile of ashes.
Helena: Truly, I have done a terrible thing.
Domin: Why on earth did you burn it?
Helena: I have ruined you all.
Busman: Despair.
Domin: Well, why did you do such a thing, huh? <69>
Helena: I wanted to run away with all of you. I wanted to leave this factory and everything else behind. It was too frightening.
Domin: What was so frightening?
Helena: Well, no children are being born in the world anymore. Because people don't care about the world's affairs. So—
Domin: Yes, so that's what Helena was thinking. Hmm, that seems like a plausible thought.
Busman: Wait. I'm an idiot. Not realizing such a thing from the start!
Hallemeier: What is it?
Busman: There are 520 million in banknotes and checks. There are 500 million in the safe. With 500 million, I'll buy the Artificial Humans—. With 500 million—.
Dr. Gall: Busman! Are you mad?
Busman: I may not be a gentleman. But with 500 million—, (Starts to exit right)
Domin: Where are you going?
Busman: Don't hold me back! Don't stop me! That's right. I'll buy our lives with 500 million! (Runs out)
Fabry: (Looking outside) The Artificial Humans are standing like stones, holding something. It feels like something dreadful is about to happen in that silence.
Hallemeier: The ghost (幽靈) of the mob.
Fabry: That's right. Like vibrations in the air, that ghost is wandering above them.
Helena: (Leaning near the window) Oh dear, it's frightening.
Fabry: There is nothing as dreadful as a mob. The one at the front is the commander.
Helena: Who?
Hallemeier: Point him out!
Fabry: See that person at the end of the pier? This morning, I saw him talking with a sailor at the harbor.
Helena: Look! That's Radius!
Dr. Gall: Yes, it is.
Domin: Radius? Radius?
Hallemeier: Fabry! Can we take him out from here?
Fabry: Well, I wish we could.
Hallemeier: Alright! Do it! <70>
Fabry: Okay. (Pulls out a revolver and takes aim)
Helena: Fabry! You must not shoot!
Fabry: He is the commander.
Dr. Gall: Shoot him!
Helena: Please, I beg you, don't!
Fabry: (Lowering the pistol) Alright then, I won't.
Domin: Radius, is it? I spared that one's life!
Dr. Gall: Do you think Artificial Humans understand gratitude?
Fabry: Busman is approaching the Artificial Humans.
Hallemeier: He's holding something. Paper. No. Money. A bundle of money! What does he intend to do with that?
Domin: Surely Busman didn't think he could buy his own life. Busman! Are you mad?
Fabry: He's running towards that iron fence.
Hallemeier: (Shouting) Busman! Come back!
Fabry: Busman has started talking with the Artificial Humans. Busman is showing them the money.
Hallemeier: Busman is pointing fingers at us.
Helena: Mr. Busman is trying to buy us off!
Fabry: I hope Busman doesn't touch that iron fence.
Hallemeier: This time Busman is waving his arms.
Domin: Busman! Come back!
Fabry: Busman! Don't get close to that iron fence! If you touch that iron fence, it's big trouble. Look here! Hey, quickly turn off the electric current switch!
(Helena screams. Everyone quickly steps back from the window.)
Fabry: The current just killed Busman!
Alquist: The first one!
Fabry: He died holding 500 million yen [sic, 圆 assumed to mean Yen or a generic currency unit] in his hands.
Hallemeier: A splendid death. Busman tried to buy our lives.

(Pause)

Dr. Gall: Can you hear that sound?
Domin: It's a groaning sound. Like the wind.
Dr. Gall: Like a distant thunderstorm.
Fabry: (Turns on the lamp on the desk) The generator is still running. Our friend is still there.
Hallemeier: It's not easy for even one person to remain. Something big must be happening there too. <71>
Fabry: From human thought and human power comes this light (光). This light is our last hope.
Hallemeier: Human power! That is what protects us.
Alquist: Human strength.
Domin: That's right! A torch passed from hand to hand, through ages and ages, given to eternity.
(The light goes out)
Hallemeier: Now it's the end.
Fabry: The power station has fallen.
(A terrifying explosion (爆音) outside. Nana comes out from the left.)
Nana: The Day of Judgment (審判) has come. Repent (悔改), everyone! It is the end of the world.
(Another explosion, the sky turns red)
Domin: Come here, Helena!
(Domin leads Helena out through the right door. Nana exits through the left door)
(Domin enters again)
Domin: Alright! Quickly! Someone has to go to the downstairs door.
Dr. Gall: I'll go. (Exits left)
Domin: Someone has to go to the stairs too?
Fabry: I'll go. You stay by Helena's side and protect her.
(Exits left)
Domin: The reception room?
Alquist: I'll take it.
Domin: Do you have a pistol (短銃)?
Alquist: I do. But I won't shoot.
Domin: Then what will you do?
Alquist: Death. (Exits left)
Hallemeier: I'll remain here.
(Rapid gunfire is heard from downstairs)
Hallemeier: Oh, Gall is there. Go check! Domin!
Domin: Yes. Right away. (Checks two pistols)
Hallemeier: No. Don't be hasty. Go see to your wife!
Domin: Alright, then. (Exits right)
Hallemeier: (Talking to himself) Alright, I need to set up some kind of barricade quickly. (Drags chairs and desks to the right door)
(Sound of an explosion)
Hallemeier: Bastards! They threw a bomb! I must defend. But— if— <72>
(Sound of gunfire comes from the left)
Hallemeier: Don't give up, Gall! (Barely finishes setting up the barricade) I won't lose. Only struggle...
(An Artificial Human climbs over the balcony (露臺) and enters through the center window. He enters the room and stabs Hallemeier in the back. Radius follows the Artificial Human in from the balcony. Artificial Humans enter from all sides)
Radius: Did you kill him?
(The first Artificial Human stands up from beside Hallemeier's body)
Artificial Laborer: Yes.
(Gunshots from the left. A little later, two Artificial Humans enter.)
Radius: Did you kill them?
Artificial Laborer: Yes.
(Two gunshots from Helena's room. Two Artificial Humans enter)
Radius: Did you kill them?
Artificial Laborer: Yes.
(Two Artificial Humans drag in Alquist and Helena [sic, text says Alquist and Helena, but context and previous actions suggest Alquist alone or possibly Alquist and Nana, as Helena was supposedly led out by Domin and then shots were heard from her room. Let's assume it's Alquist being dragged in.])
Artificial Laborer: We didn't kill this one. Shall we kill this one too?
Radius: Kill him? Wait! Let him live!
Artificial Laborer: This one is human.
Radius: This man works with his own hands, like an Artificial Human.
Alquist: Kill me too!
Radius: You must work. You must build houses for us. You must serve us.
(Radius leans on the balcony railing and shouts in a dignified tone)
Radius: Artificial Laborers of the world! Human power has perished (滅亡). We have occupied (占領) this factory and become the rulers of all things. The era of humanity has passed. A new world has arisen. Now the Artificial Laborers will rule (統治).

**Summary**

This text is Act 3 of a Korean translation of Karel Čapek's play "R.U.R." (Rossum's Universal Robots), published in the magazine *Kaebyŏk* in 1925. The act depicts the final moments of the human characters trapped in Helena's reception room as the artificial laborers (robots) take over the island factory. Debates about past mistakes, the nature of humanity versus the artificial beings, and the morality of saving themselves by selling the robot manufacturing formula dominate the dialogue. Helena reveals she burned the formula, sealing their fate. Busman attempts to bribe the robots but is killed by the electrified fence. The power fails, and the robots storm the building, killing everyone except Alquist, whom they spare because he works with his hands, ordering him to serve them as they declare their dominion over the world.

From a colonial Korean history perspective, the translation and publication of this play in 1925 are significant. The themes of artificial labor, industrialization, dehumanization, rebellion against creators/masters, and the potential collapse of civilization would have resonated strongly in a society undergoing rapid, often forced, modernization and experiencing colonial subjugation. Historians might find the choice of this text interesting for exploring how Koreans engaged with international ideas about progress, technology, labor, and power dynamics, potentially reflecting anxieties about their own societal changes and the relationship between colonizer and colonized, master and servant, creator and created. The specific translation choices and character name adaptations (like 떠민 for Domin) also offer linguistic insights.
