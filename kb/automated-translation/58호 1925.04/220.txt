Article Title: 온 저잣 사람이 (All the People in the Marketplace)
Author: 趙明熙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

All the People in the Marketplace
						Cho Myŏnghŭi

Even if all the people in the marketplace wish to befriend me,
Truly, I do not desire it.
Only the friend who comes with silence,
Please, come find me soon.

Even if all the people in the world say they love me,
Truly, I do not desire it.
Only the beloved one who comes with silence,
Please, come find me soon.

And so, let our world be locked in silence.
Only the painful heart listening intently amidst the silence...<42>

**Summary**

This poem by Cho Myŏnghŭi expresses a strong preference for quiet companionship over widespread, potentially superficial, affection from "all the people in the marketplace" or "all the people in the world." The speaker explicitly rejects these outward shows of friendship or love, instead yearning for a single individual—a "friend" or "beloved"—who approaches with "silence." The poem concludes by envisioning a shared world enclosed in silence, where only the "painful heart" communicates through intense listening. For historians, this poem, published in the influential magazine *Kaebyŏk* during the colonial era, might reflect a weariness with the noise and perhaps insincerity of public life, possibly hinting at a desire for deeper, unspoken understanding or solidarity ("painful heart") among individuals facing shared hardship or alienation in a suppressed society.

온 저잣 사람이
						趙明熙
온 저자 사람이 다 나를 사괴랴 하야도,
진실로 나는 원치를 아니하오
다만 침묵을 가지고 오는 벗님만이,
어서 나를 차저 오소서.
온 세상 사람이 다 나를 사랑한다 하야도,
참으로 나는 원치를 아니하오
다만 침묵을 가지고 오는 님만이,
어서 나를 차저 오소서.

그리하야 우리의 세게는 침묵으로 잠급시다
다만 압푼 마음만이 침국 가운대 귀기우리며...＜42＞
