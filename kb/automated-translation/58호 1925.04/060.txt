Magazine Title: 개벽 제58호
Publication Date: 1925년 04월 01일
Article Title: 모든 父兄을 代하야 普通學校訓導諸氏에게 (To All Elementary School Teachers, On Behalf of All Parents)
Author: 朴達成
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

To all you teachers (先生님) serving as elementary school instructors (普通學校 訓導).

A certain Westerner once said, "The world will eventually fall into the hands of educators (敎育家)." Borrowing this phrase and applying it to Korea (朝鮮), we cannot help but say, "Korea will eventually fall into the hands of teachers." To summarize further, one could say, "Whether Korea fares well or poorly depends on the hands of the elementary school instructors (普通學校 訓導 諸氏)." Why single out just elementary school instructors? But given the current situation in Korea, isn't it true that elementary school instructors bear the greatest responsibility and are actually doing the most work?

To all you teachers, elementary school instructors who have embarked on the proper path of national education for all children. Korea holds such great expectations for you teachers, and you teachers bear such great responsibility. In other words, it is acceptable to say that Korea's level of civilization (文野) and Korea's rise and fall (興替) depend on the hands of teachers. Your responsibility, teachers, is this grave, and the work you do is this noble. This fact is known by everyone in Korea, young and old, men and women (朝鮮의 老少男女), without needing further tedious explanation. However,

Esteemed teachers. Do you yourselves truly possess such a sense of responsibility (責任感) and carry out your work with that much self-confidence (自信)?

Does the profound thought, "I am a worker bearing the crucial responsibility of reviving Korea," reside in your minds, teachers? Of course, such a sense of responsibility must be there – but I suspect it might not be so, which is truly regrettable.

Teachers, everyone. Although I cannot know for sure, aren't there perhaps individuals like this among you? <28>
I mean those who are merely devoted to the profession (職業), thinking, "There's nothing else to do, so I might as well do this." Or are there perhaps genuine breadwinners, thinking, "There's nothing to eat, so for the sake of eating..."? And furthermore, aren't there also those who do it for honor (名譽) or status (行勢), thinking, "What greater glory is there than receiving respectful treatment from all parents and being called 'teacher' by all the children?" Wasn't it just a few years ago that many did it for the allure of the gold-rimmed hat (金軆帽子) and the black copper sword (烏銅칼)? "What greater status could there be, rubbing shoulders with county office employees (郡廳職員), flashing swords alongside police station officers (警察署員), and looking down on township office employees (面職員)! It's a great honor for our family," wasn't this the joy and pride proclaimed not just by the instructor himself but by his entire family and clan (門中), as if someone from their lineage had just passed the highest state examination (大科) the day before? Aren't there such individuals among you teachers? I don't know for certain, but perhaps there are such people?

Elementary school instructors, esteemed teachers. When you finish your breakfast, gather your book bundles, and set off towards the school, what thoughts do you entertain? Do you perhaps force your reluctant steps, thinking, "It's time to earn my keep, gotta go again!"? As you walk through alleys and streets, when those innocent (天眞爛漫) boys and girls (男女少年) carrying their book bundles see you, teacher, and bow their heads politely in greeting, what thoughts do you have? Do you merely put on the utmost arrogance, thinking, "Hmph! I am the teacher!" while just glancing sideways or nodding your head? Or do you smile warmly and say, "Ah, lovely, our boys and girls! Did you sleep well? Are you going to school?" Do you earnestly grasp their wrists? Thinking about it, it seems you might just be puffing yourself up with airs (거드름), thinking, "Ahem. I am the teacher. Yup! Of course, they should greet me!" Perhaps this is a misunderstanding, though!

Teachers, everyone. When you take the book, chalk, and cane (채죽) and stand on the teaching platform, what thoughts do you have? Calculating your salary and the time, do you perhaps think, "Let's get through another hour. That's several tens of *chŏn* (or several *won*)"? Do you only think about money and how to pass the time quickly? During teaching (敎授), if some unruly students (學生) don't listen well or, unable to contain their lively (潑剌) spirits, perhaps cause mischief, do you not perhaps curse them harshly, "Damn brat, you rascal!" or strike them with curses or blows? Do you not perhaps vent your frustrations uselessly, thinking, "My livelihood (口腹 literally mouth and stomach) is my enemy, making me do this job"? According to secret reports (密告) from young pupils, there are teachers like that. Being young, they can certainly be tiresome often – but if this is truly the case, we are deeply saddened. – (Hereafter, pages 30-31 omitted – original text) <29>

**Summary**

Pak Tal-sŏng, writing on behalf of parents, addresses elementary school teachers in colonial Korea, emphasizing their critical role in shaping the nation's future. He expresses deep concern that many teachers lack a true sense of mission and responsibility. He criticizes those who view teaching merely as a job of last resort, a means of survival, a path to social status (comparing themselves favorably to local officials), or an opportunity to act self-important towards students. He questions their motivations and attitudes, urging them to reflect on whether they approach their duties with dedication or merely as a chore to earn a salary, potentially treating students harshly out of frustration. Historically, this article offers insight into the perceived attitudes and motivations within the colonial education system's teaching staff. It highlights parental expectations and anxieties about education's role in national destiny under Japanese rule and provides a contemporary critique of the professionalism and commitment among some Korean teachers working within that system, including mentions of uniforms (hats, swords) and comparisons to other colonial administrative personnel.

모든 父兄을 代하야 普通學校訓導諸氏에게
						朴達成
普通學校 訓導 되시는 여러분 先生님.
서양의 엇던 사람은 「세계는 장차 敎育家의 손에 도라가리라」고 말햇슴니다. 이 말을 비러 朝鮮에 利用한다 하면 「朝鮮은 장차 선생님들 손에 도라가리라」라고 안이할 수 업슴니다. 더 要約해 말하면 「朝鮮의 잘 되고 못됨은 普通學校 訓導 諸氏의 손에 달녓다」라고 할 수 잇슴니다. 하필 普通學校 訓導 뿐이겟슴닛가만은 現下의 朝鮮 형편으로는 普通學校 訓導 諸氏가 그 책임을 가장 만히 가젓고 또 실제에서 그 일을 가장 만히 하고 잇지 안슴닛가?.
모든 어린이 즉 國民敎育의 正路에 나선 普通學校 訓導되시는 여러분 선생님. 朝鮮은 생생님들에게 그 만큼 크게 기대하고 잇스며 선생님들은 그만큼 큰 책임을 가지고 잇슴니다. 즉 말하면 朝鮮의 文野, 朝鮮의 興替가 선생님들 손에 달녓다해도 可함니다. 선생님들의 책임은 이러케 중대하고 선생님들의 하시는 일은 이러케 高貴함니다. 이 말은 더- 呶呶할 것도 업시 朝鮮의 老少男女가 다 가티 알고 잇는 사실이외다. 그러한데
여러분 선생님. 선생님들 자신이 과연 그만한 責任感을 가지고 그만큼 自信잇는 일을 하시며 지내심닛가?
「나는 朝鮮을 살닐 그만큼 중대한 책임을 가진 일군이다」라는 深重한 생각이 선생님네 머리ㅅ 속에 게심닛가? 無論- 그러한 책임감이 게시겟지오 - 만은 의심컨대 그러치 안은 듯도 십흐니 이것이 얼마나 섭섭한 일임닛가?.

선생님 여러분. 몰으긴 하지만 선생님 중에 - 이런 이가 게시지 안슴닛가?.＜28＞
「할 일이 업스니 그거라도 해야지」하는 직업에 뿐 팔녀다니시는 이 말슴이외다. 또는 「먹을 것이 업스니 먹기 위해서라도...」하는 정말 밥버리ㅅ군이 게시지 안슴닛가?. 그리고 또는  「모든 학부형에게 貴여운 대접을 밧고 모든 어린이에게 선생님 소리를 듯게 되니 이런 영광이 어대 잇느냐」하는 名譽 又는 行勢거리로 하는 이도 게시지 안슴닛가?. 맷 해 前 일이지요만은 金軆帽子와 烏銅칼 멋에 한 이도 만치 안으섯슴닛가?. 郡廳職員과 억개를 가티 하고 警察署員과 칼을 가티 번적거리고 面職員가튼 것은 눈 아레로 깔보고 지내니 이런 行勢가 어대 잇스랴! 우리 집안 큰 영광이다』라고 訓導되신 自身 왼 가족 왼 門中까지 바로 前날에 大科나 나신 門中가티 떠드러 내는 깃븜이요 자랑이 안이엿슴닛가. 선생님들 중에 이런 이가 게시지 안슴닛가?. 분명히는 몰으지만 그런 이가 혹- 게시지오?.
普通學校 訓導 여러분 선생님. 아츰을 잡수시고 책보를 거두어 가지시고 학교를 향해 거름을 옴기실 때 엇더한 생각을 가지심닛가?. 「밥버리 시간이 되야스니 또 가야지!」하고 실흔 거름을 억지로 옴겨 놋치나 안슴닛가?. 골목과 골목 巨里와 巨里에서 그- 天眞爛漫의 책보 낀 男女少年들이 선생님을 뵈올 때 곱게 곱게 머리를 숙여 인사할 때 선생님들은 엇던 생각을 가지심닛가?. 「나는 선생님이닛가. 에헴!」하고 가장 거만을 피우면서 눈만 흴긋하거나 고개만 끗덕하지 안슴닛가?. 「아- 사랑흡다. 우리 少年少女야! 잘 잣느냐? 학교에 가늬?」하고 반가히 우서줌닛가?. 살뜰히 손목을 잡아줌닛가?. 생각컨대 「에헴. 나는 선생님이닛가. 암! 그럿치 인사해야지!」하고 거드름만 빠는 듯 십슴니다. 혹- 오해인지는 몰으시요만은!.
선생님 여러분. 책과 분필과 채죽을 들고 강단에 올나 설 때는 엇던 생각을 가지심닛가?. 월급과 시간을 따저 가지고 「또- 한 시간 해보자. 돈이 멧 拾錢(혹은 멧 圓)이다」하고 돈 생각 시간 速키 보낼 생각이나 안이 하심닛가?. 敎授 중에 혹- 節업는 學生들이 말을 잘 안이 듯거나 潑剌-한 기분을 익이지 못하야 혹- 작란을 칠 때면 「에익 망할 자식 요놈아!」하고 독살스런 욕이나 매나 때리지 안슴닛가?. 「口腹이 원수가 되야 이 노릇을 한다」하고 공연한 성푸리나 하지 안슴닛가?. 어린 學童들의 密告를 드르면 그런 선생님도 게시다 함니다. 어린 것들이라 無論 성가실 때도 만켓지요 - 만은 실상으로 그러시다 하면 우리는 퍽- 섭섭함니다. - (이하 30 31頁 삭제- 원문)＜29＞
