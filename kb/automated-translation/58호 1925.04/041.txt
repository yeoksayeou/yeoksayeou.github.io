**Summary**

This article from the April 1st, 1925 issue of Kaebyŏk magazine compiles several company notices received during March regarding the magazine's distribution network. It announces the reopening of the Inch'ŏn branch, the establishment of a new branch in Sŏngjin, staff appointments at the Pyŏktong sub-branch, details about sales outlets in Chinju and Hyesan (including locations and managers), and significant personnel changes (including a new branch head) at the Hamnam (South Hamgyŏng) branch office. For historians, this piece offers valuable insight into the operational expansion and organizational structure of a prominent Korean cultural and nationalist magazine during the Japanese colonial period. It maps the magazine's geographic reach and identifies key individuals involved in its dissemination across various Korean regions, demonstrating its efforts to maintain and grow its presence despite colonial constraints.
