필자	ST
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellany)

感傷語의 一二
						ST
Joseon people seem incomplete. They seem eternally incomplete. They seem like hopelessly desperate, incomplete beings with no room for further development and no visible sprout for further growth. It seems the people are destined to perish someday without a sound or trace, just going on like this, "becoming and then crumbling." I don't know how unsightly, hateful, and also pitiful and wretched these people are. Should one cry, should one shout, should one beat them until blood pours out, or should one soothe and coax them until sorrowful tears block the chest? Or will they just tire, fall over, and die, with nothing having any effect? Who could know?
Groaning people of Joseon, from where will the light come before you? When the morning sun, the glittering sun, rises upon the earth and shines quietly upon your heads, is not the energy that should be your joy, your light, your solid strength, pulling your lives forward, forever laughing? Is not even that generous sun quietly struggling and sneering?

Whether the day is dark or dawning, what are you thinking and doing? When you can clearly observe your own form, plotting small tricks like a rat and being petty like a jealous girl, what do you think? O weak, slow, and helpless people.
What light could there be in a place like a gloomy, shady, dim cave where you pinch, scratch, and fight each other? When I see you crazily dancing in your seed-fight (시앗싸움 - possibly factional strife), lacking the eyes and reason to distinguish friend from foe, and showing no strength to grow, how can I describe it? The final evil play of the death throes—ah, these people, you can't even do that briskly.<44>

The world today is groaning together. It groans chronically, struggles chronically. It is the civilized disease of capitalism. It is a disease inseparable from "human life" since "capital" conquered humanity. Since capital occupied the human heart, human life has been pathological.
People do not live life, nor do they yearn for ideals grown from life, and thus human action is not for the development of existence, nor a process of the pain or ecstasy of completion, however pathological he may be; rather, he lives and yearns to complete capital, and thus it is a pathological extension of existence. Thus, a battle has arisen in the world's heart: between solidification, stagnation, and creation, growth. Here, chronic symptoms have occurred. It is the fight between capital and humans. It is the fight between decadence and development.
The 20th century is the age of capitalist civilization. Civilization shines and its life develops upon a mountain of corpses piled with machines and slaves. The glory of capital and the subjugation of life arise here.
From country to country, in large markets, in remote mountain villages, wherever two-legged beasts struggle, where is this disease and this fight absent? It is the fight of social classes, the fight of humanity and capital.
Culture, civilization, wealth—all are said to be unable to exist apart from human life. Right. That is the truth. It is a self-evident principle. That humans are the subject, creating and destroying everything—this is the principle of existence.
However, in today's world, all these self-evident principles and truths are heard only as nonsensical sleep-talking, or else the chanting of monks, or the sentimental words floated on the fervor of idealists. Where humans, life, should be the subject, capital has become the subject. It is not humans controlling and growing their own lives; rather, capital is the subject, controlling human life instead. Just as philosophy, morality, and art, when they develop for their own sake apart from life, rebel against life and cause decadence, so too when capital acts for its own completion apart from humanity, humanity becomes sick and groans. On one side, decadence arises, and on the other, resistance arises. History is said to be propelled this way. But<45> people are sad. They live repeating this fact. Thus they groan. They fight. And so, it is said, the vigorous light of the future comes. People seem to bear the fate of having to endlessly repeat this destined, miserable path. Should one be optimistic and glad, seeing the traces of evolution and progress from an evolutionary perspective, or should one realize that humans are nothing more than this and cry? One does not know. Humans are sorrowful.
Time passes. People move their steps toward the light of the future. They move one step, two steps, painful steps, deeply branding blood-soaked tracks, seemingly indelible for future eternity, onto the earth, onto humanity's heart. Where to!

Let's go to reality! Let's go to reality!
I mutter like sleep-talking. Before that sound came out, I chanted sweet and sad words like sleep-talking prayers. Whatever my thoughts, whatever the worries of deep-thinking sentimentalists, the world, and humanity, proceeds quietly, listlessly, without glancing sideways at others or listening to the voices of feverish people, sometimes swiftly like an arrow, sometimes crawling like a cow. Thinking endlessly like this is, after all, head-aching.
Let's go to reality! Let's go to reality!
One must go. Unless one weeps in a human graveyard, dances with phantoms in a rainbow, or kneels like a religious fanatic for a fasting prayer, a destiny whips me from behind my back that I must see this reality, whether my chest feels tight, my head aches, whether it's hateful or beautiful.
Let's go to reality! Let's go to reality!
Though it is a scene of carnage (修羅場) where all classes, discontent, the battles of falsehood, all sorts of ugliness, and vampires run rampant, it seems people still cannot leave this. Holding onto life, holding onto society, looking out at history, however wrongly everything may have turned out, it seems something shouts from behind humanity's back that people must think well and poorly within it, be dragged by its currents, find life, fix things, destroy, and build anew.<46>
The world groans. It groans in reality. Yet, the world is still reality, and that reality, though mixed with groaning and vigor, degeneration and development, beauty and ugliness, good and evil, seems to be pulled forward by the light of the future. Here lies the sorrow and agony of reality, and also the joy and deep emotion of reality.
Now the world fights. Nation against nation, this class against that class, this individual against that individual, and even neighbors within the same class kill and tear at each other. The enemy's prison and the neighbor's trap lie with open mouths.
—Humanity of the world, falling to the enemy, starving, deceived and dying by individual envy, jealousy, and hypocrisy, and also falling by the hands of neighbors! This is neither misery nor misfortune, neither something to rejoice over nor happiness; it cannot be described as anything. Only, reality is thus disordered, painful, bizarre, and hateful. But, on the other hand, when one sees vigorous comrades joining hands and fighting, stepping forward for the world, reality also seems moving, joyful, and full of ecstasy and light.
Who can sever ties with this reality? One must walk together with the people of the world. In the transcendent, shining world of a single pessimistic philosopher, sentimental poet, or humanitarian idealist, there is no humanity. There is poetry, his philosophy, there is Jesus (야소), there is Schopenhauer (소페하우에-르), there is Tolstoy (톨스토이), and besides them, there is the world fantasized in the yearning and sleep-talking of all such poets, philosophers, and religious figures.
Let's return to reality; there, humanity now groans. It fights for the light.
Comrades! Will you not cry with the people, run with them, rejoice, and fight? Let's go to the world of reality; one must have the wisdom to face reality squarely. And one must understand that reality. One must know what the vanguard of that reality is. The fight and dynamism are there. What is coming? Well then—comrade.

(10 lines blank in original)

X-kun!
I read your letter with pleasure.
While I respect your sincerity, I cannot agree with your understanding of neutrality and your actual attitude towards OOOO. Seen this way, your position and mine are not the same. There is a difference in direction, and also a difference in distance. Currently, there are three of us. These are three groups with the same goal, principles, and policies. In truth, they should become one, but they each hold their own banner and stand separately. They stand not on the work, but on people. Therefore, this is not a division of three kinds of work, but a division of people, and the division of people is ultimately a fight for leadership. Thus, the current situation is not one of fighting and campaigning based on some common interest arising from social work, but a fight where neighbors envy, slander, are jealous of, and scheme against each other to expand their own factional power. What should have been ideology, policy, and work aimed at fighting a common enemy has been overturned into a divisive fight over common ideology, policy, and work. Who can see this as progressing rightly, let alone praise it as correct, or even feel sympathy? It seems your so-called neutrality arose from this. It seems you waited for the self-righteous *kun* (군 - term for 'you' or 'group') who stand alone cleanly without involving themselves in 1, 2, or 3.
X-kun
Division is by no means a correct thing. As long as 1, 2, and 3 each defend their own position and view the others as enemies, it is certainly not right, and the work cannot be done. It ultimately amounts to nothing but the disgraceful spectacle of fratricidal conflict that invites the enemy's ridicule and satisfaction. But unfortunately, this is the current situation. It is also the work. It is something that cannot be helped by the strength of one or two people; whether the world ridicules or criticizes, their reality flows on regardless. This so-called actual fact, transcending good and evil, right and wrong, proceeds. Despite history and currently witnessed facts proving that such examples have destroyed nations, it flows on just the same. Then where is the way to save this? Is it the neutrality you advocate? No. No. Absolutely not. On the contrary, it only results in further promoting the divisive situation. Neutrality, in the end, is also a faction. Your attitude, *kun*, of supposedly being able to walk the right path from a third-party position, judging and correcting good/evil, right/wrong from an impartial standpoint without joining 1, 2, or 3—that becomes self-righteous, divisive, and inevitably factional, betraying the original advocacy. This is not because your self-righteousness implies a lack of sincerity, but because the actual state of affairs allows for nothing else. Human opinions and emotions, in such cases, are inevitably dragged along by the current of the situation. From here, you *kun* must either cling to a self-righteous attitude and flee from the workplace, or else create a 4th group, or failing that, inevitably dissolve into one of 1, 2, or 3. This is how our work proceeds now. At least, as long as we are all doing the work of OOO O, it seems there is no other way. But this is painful and difficult work.
X-kun
Who would call this situation right? Let's assume it is determined that there is no other way for things to go. But who would call it right? My personal feeling is that I would rather harbor desperate sorrow about the work and flee from the workplace than be swept into that desolate scene of fratricidal conflict with hands stained by the blood of curses. It does not seem like a path that saves humanity, or at least us; it seems like the longer it continues, the more it becomes a path to destruction. At least, my personal feeling is so. If one thinks that is right, one should go into the mountains. When everyone is staggering, how can someone who knows better stagger along with them without any plan or reflection? Impossible. Impossible. I repeat this thought endlessly and suffer. All human sorrow and the conflict and baseness of reality lie here. For months, I have thought and suffered in exactly the same way, but the answer is always the same. However, without looking back at all my intentions and feelings, reality flows on just the same. Frighteningly staggering and sneering.
X-kun!
Can we not escape from this harsh, dirty, frightening path? Can we not turn back for the better? Isn't this what we all have striven, worried, and worked for?
So I thought this way. If one despairs over this harsh workplace and flees, he is hardly worth talking about, but if people have the sincerity to grasp this situation and work to make it better somehow, to gather strength and become firm, then they will always<49> strive to somehow bring these three or four together into one. That is the crucial point determining whether the work succeeds or fails. To do such work, whatever happens to one or two individuals, my position was one of neutrality, or to borrow your words, a buffer zone (緩衝地帶), to create a place where we could somehow embrace each other, reconcile, and harmonize. (Of course, the meanings of this word "neutrality" and the term "buffer zone" are vastly different. But one must not forget that it was, to the last, a means and position for unification). Therefore, if that work becomes hopeless, as I have repeated several times above, one either flees, adopts a self-righteous attitude—which means flight and division—or jumps into 1, 2, or 3. One jumps into that frightening place where one must grip one's own brother's throat with one's own hands. In that place, even if there is minor individual betrayal, sacrifice, and immediate disadvantage and discord, it seems one must discard them like worn-out shoes for the sake of the greater work, for the sake of today's greater benefit. This seems to be what our current reality is patting us on the back for. The cursed hand... may there be eternal pain and sorrow. It's frightening. Something dreadful seems to threaten me. A frightful suggestion. An omen. Everyone is covered in blood, grinding their teeth and trembling. An ominous wind blows incessantly. But that fight is brief. Let us prepare for the moment when we grab each other's wrists and run to the street of the great fight.
OOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO The reality, the market above markets, is thus grotesque and frightening.
Comrade, will you not go? To that battleground (修羅場) of reality? Whether it turns out well or poorly, is not that place waiting for us? Who is the enemy? Is it their blood-stained left hand? O dull, foolish, wicked multitude, who is your enemy?

Spring has come. The weather, where white snow swirled even into March and harsh winds cut people's flesh like knives, has quietly subsided, and the spring that had to come has finally arrived.
No matter how madly the wind shakes heaven and earth, seemingly blocking people's noses and eyes to suffocate them, within it, an undeniable harmony and warmth seep into people's hearts.
Sunlight, freedom, and healthy spirit seem to dance and whisper upon the world. O lonely human heart. Spring has come.<50>

**Summary**

The author, "ST," expresses profound despair and criticism towards the Korean people ("Joseon people"), viewing them as "eternally incomplete" and trapped in petty internal conflicts ("seed-fight"). This despair is linked to a broader critique of global capitalism, described as a "civilized disease" that subjugates human life and fosters conflict. Despite the bleakness of reality—characterized by division, strife, and the dominance of capital—the author insists on facing it ("Let's go to reality!"). The latter part addresses "X-kun," lamenting the factionalism (three groups that should be one) within a movement (likely socialist or nationalist) and rejecting "neutrality" as ineffective or even counterproductive, ultimately urging commitment to the difficult struggle within reality, even if it means painful compromises and internal conflict ("fratricidal conflict"). The piece ends on a slightly hopeful note, welcoming the arrival of spring as a symbol of potential renewal.

From a historical perspective, the article vividly portrays the deep disillusionment and frustration felt by some Korean intellectuals during the colonial period (1925). It reflects the complex interplay of national despair ("incomplete Joseon people"), the influence of global ideologies (sharp critique of capitalism, hinting at socialist leanings), and the crippling internal divisions that plagued Korean nationalist and social movements. Historians might find the raw emotional expression of colonial angst, the specific critique of capitalist modernity's impact, and the candid discussion of factionalism (the "three groups," the critique of "neutrality," the reference to "OOOO") particularly revealing about the intellectual and political currents of the time.

感傷語의 一二
						ST
조선 사람들은 未成品인 것 갓다. 영원한 未成品인 것 갓다. 더 발전될 여지도 업고 더 生長할 싹도 안 보이는 救할 수 업는 절망적 未成品인 것 갓다. 백성들은 이대로 가다가, 이대로 「되다가 찝으러지다가」 언젠지 소리도 자최도 업시 멸망될 운명을 타고난 종족들인 것 갓다. 엇더케 보기 실코 밉고, 또 불상하고 가련한 백성들인지 모르겟다. 울어야 조홀지, 웨처야 조흘지, 또 피가 쏘다지도록 때려주어야 조흘지, 서른 눈물이 가슴을 막히도록 어르만지고 달내여야 조흘지 이것도 저것도 아무 效力이 업시 지처 넘어저 죽어 버리려는지, 누가 그것을 알 것이냐.
신음하는 조선 백성들아, 너희들 압헤 光明은 어데로부터 올 것이냐. 아침 날이, 번득거리는 태양이, 지상에 떠 너희들의 머리 우에 가만히 빗날 제, 그것을 깃븜으로, 光明으로, 또 健實한 힘이 될, 너희들의 생활을 끄러나가게 하는 에네르기는 영원히 웃고 잇지 아니하냐. 저 너그러운 태양조차 가만히 버둥거리며 비웃고 잇지 아니하냐.

날이 어둡거나 새거나 너희들이 생각하고, 일히고 잇는 것이 무엇이냐. 쥐도 적도 가티 적은 꾀부리고 시긔만 게집애가튼 암상피는 네 꼴을 네 자신이 똑바로 살필 수 잇슬 때, 엇더케 생각이 되느냐. 이 힘 업고, 느리고 하잘 수 업는 백성들아.
음침하고 그늘진 으슴프러한 동굴가튼 속에서 서로 꼬집고 쥐어뜻고 싸우는 곳에 무슨 빗이 잇슬가 보냐. 적과 벗을 분별할 만한 눈도 理智도 업고, 成長할 힘도 잘 보이지 안는, 김이 빠진 허우적거리는 너희들의 시앗싸움에 밋처 날뛰는 꼴을 볼 때, 그것을 무엇이라고 형용하랴. 斷末魔的 최후의 惡戱-아아 이 백성들아 그나마 시원스럽게 하지도＜44＞ 못하는구나.

지금 세계는 다가티 신음하고 잇다. 만성적으로 신음하고, 만성적으로 허우적거리고 잇다. 자본주의의 文明病이다. 「資本」이 사람을 정복한 이후의 「사람의 生活」에서 떼어 버릴 수 업는 病이다. 資本이 사람의 심장을 점령한 이후의 사람의 생활은 病的이엇다.
사람들은 생활을 하지 안코, 또 생활에서 자라난 理想을 동경하지 안코, 그래서 사람의 행동은 그가 病的인 만치 生存을 發展하기 위하야, 또 완성의 고통이나 희열의 科程이 아니라, 그는 資本을 완성하기 위하야 생활하고 동경하고 그래서 생존의 病的 延長이다. 이리하야 세계의 심장? 固化 침체와 창조, 성장과의 싸홈이 일어낫다. 여긔서 만성적 病候가 발생하엿다. 자본과 인간의 싸움이다. 퇴폐와 발전의 싸움이다.
20세기는 자본문명의 시대다. 기계와 노예가 堆積한 屍山 우에 문명이 빗나고 그의 생활이 발전한다. 자본의 광영도 생활의 예속도 여긔서 일어난다.
나라와 나라에서, 큰 저자에서, 산간 벽촌에서, 두발 달린 즘생이 허우적거리는 곳에, 이 병과 이 싸움이 업는 곳이 어데냐. 사회상의 계급의 싸홈이나, 인류와 자본의 싸움이다.
