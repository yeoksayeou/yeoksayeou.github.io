**Body Text Translation:**
Myriad Figures of the Kyŏngsŏng Education World
						By Physiognomist

My goodness—it's already April this year, and I haven't done a single physiognomy reading; how boring. No matter how busy things are, let's start again. This time, let's specifically look at figures in the education world within Kyŏngsŏng [Seoul]. Among them, let's push aside the various common schools and girls' schools for the next opportunity and significantly narrow it down to just the teachers at boys' schools of middle school level and higher. Ah—March and April are truly the prime months for school teachers. Due to graduation ceremonies and graduates' thank-you parties, they get some bags of sweets and platters of food; they might even pawn something to wear thick-salaried old-fashioned attire; they wield some authority during graduation and entrance exams; and because they collect exam fees, entrance fees, and tuition in advance, their pockets are plump. Doing a physiognomy reading at such a time is refreshing and interesting even for this Physiognomist, who receives no fee for it. Well then, which school shall we start with? What order could there possibly be? Let's just look at them as we encounter them.

Posŏng College (普成專門學校) - The principal, Mr. Yu Sŏng-jun (兪星濬), is so short and small that even the bright eyes of this Physiognomist cannot see him well without a microscope. Perhaps because he belongs to the cave-dweller faction (穴者派), he insists on wearing a scarf made only of fox fur, a cave-dwelling animal. Also, many different scents emanate from him: the scent of the Government-General (總督府) from his resume, the scent of the Bible (聖經) from his breast pocket, the scent of a Confucian scholar (儒生) from his gaiters (行纏), and the stale, rotten (outdated, 時代遲) scent from his speech. And although his temporary job is principal, his lifelong profession seems to be that of a graduation ceremony congratulatory speech master. Every year during graduation season, he diligently attends every single school without fail and routinely gives a guest congratulatory speech. It would be nice if he at least said something novel, but he takes one hackneyed, incomprehensible phrase and, like an ignorant person from the countryside reading the same oily ritual prayer (祝文) year after year, repeats it over and over, receiving苦笑 (bitter smiles) from the general public, which is not something a trend-following, clever teacher should do. And the dean, Mr. Sŏ Sang-hwan (徐相懽), perhaps understood the character for "Paragraph One" (第一項) in the law lecture notes to mean "Number One" (第一), as he always brandishes his long neck well. When lecturing, he pads out half the time with the sound "So-re-de" [Japanese: それで - "And so"], snorts three times with every word, and his speech is said to be unintelligible, whether Korean or Japanese. Originally, it's common for any teacher<32> to be criticized by students, but when he was acting principal before Principal Yu arrived, students specifically gave him the mocking name "Crafty Tanuki Statue Illusion" (巧裝大狸鼠像幻 - a pun on his name). Shh—enough about him, let's look at Professor Kim Yŏng-ju (金泳柱). He is a legal scholar and simultaneously an iron bar gymnast. He lectures well, and he also does iron bar gymnastics well. This seems to be because, while studying law, he keenly felt the saying "The law is far, but the fist is near" (法遠拳近). Also, when lecturing, "Yōsuruni" [Japanese: 要するに - "In short"] is his trademark phrase, and his drooping lower lip is also a characteristic. Next, Mr. Hong Sŏng-ha (洪性夏) often says irresponsibly, when a student asks a question, that they will understand it well if they go look at the lecture notes, and he sometimes threatens them with point deductions as if treating college students like elementary schoolers. His ideology seems to have a socialist tint, yet they say he communicates well with the capitalist director, Mr. Kim Ki-t'ae (金琪邰). Yikes—so much time has already passed. Let's stop looking at Posŏng College and head straight over to Posŏng High School (普成高等普通學校).

The principal, Mr. Chŏng Tae-hyŏn (鄭大鉉), has been at the school for over 10 years, yet like a new bride (新嫁娘), he is always very shy, and perhaps because he has some deep sorrow, the character for "river" (川字 - frown lines) constantly appears between his eyebrows. The way he tilts his head when speaking and covers his mouth with his hand when laughing would make even someone who dislikes him see him as endearing (貴염성). But he smokes so much tobacco that his fingers are stained thick with nicotine residue, looking at a glance like the hands of a maiden who has dyed her nails with balsam flowers (鳳仙花). Though his exterior is so feminine, his interior is masculine. Even if the heavens fall, he will surely do and say everything he must. While student strikes (同盟休學) and staff conflicts often occur at various schools in Kyŏngsŏng, the relative lack of such turmoil at Posŏng School, though other reasons may exist, cannot but be attributed largely to Mr. Chŏng's meticulousness (仔詳周密). Dean Komatsuzaki (小松崎) - His surname is Komatsu ("Small Pine"), but he possesses three longs: long face (面長), long body (身長), and long speech (言長 - criticized for speaking too long to students), so "Chōshō" ("Long Pine") seems appropriate. He is quite stubborn (固執性), but he is praised among staff and students for being silent, reserved (沈黙寡言), diligent, and frugal (勤實儉素). Mr. Ch'oe Myŏng-hwan (崔鳴煥) - Having been at the school for many years, he also has many nicknames. Because his face is pockmarked, some call him "Katabang" (가다방 - pockmarked face); while teaching zoology, he earned the titles "Parapara" (빠라빠라) or "Kangaroo" (강가루); and because he loves alcohol (愛酒), he is called "Alcohol Teacher." Watching him clasp his hands together and continuously shake his body while standing during class allows anyone to guess his impatient nature, and his stubbornness is no less than Mr. Komatsuzaki's. Enough about him, let's look at Mr. Yi Hŭi-sang (李熙祥), who is nicknamed "Kongnamul" (콩나물 - soybean sprout). Seeing him walk down the street in shiny red shoes, yellow glasses, brandishing a short cane (短杖), he looks like a contemporary "멋쟁이" (dandy), yet when he occasionally flares up at students saying, "Hey—what<33> kind of ugly behavior is this?" he also seems rather unstylish (無멋쟁이). However, he is loyal and diligent (忠實勤勉), and in terms of physical education skills, he would likely hate to be ranked anything but among the top few physical education teachers in Kyŏngsŏng schools. No, it's no exaggeration to say he is the pioneer (先導者) of recreational gymnastics (遊戱軆操) in Kyŏngsŏng. Also, Mr. Yu Hŭi-jang (柳熙章) is also a physical education teacher; while Mr. Yi gets the nickname "Kongnamul," he gets the nickname Noktunamul (綠豆나물 - mung bean sprout). But in reality, he is not weak like a mung bean sprout but a man of strong bones (强骨). Seeing him command during gym class with his deep-set eyes wide open, he appears very imposing (威嚴). And he receives much welcome for being kind and attentive (親切叮嚀) to students. Mr. Chang *-jin (張*震) truly has the air of an educator in both appearance and behavior. However, carrying a bulky briefcase with his old suit makes him resemble a collection agent (收金員) for a Japanese moneylender (高利貸金業者), and seeing him, having been the central figure as the dean of Hwimun School, now sitting in a teacher's chair in a corner of the office at Posŏng School, his bleakness (蕭條無色) looks like that of a remarried widow (後嫁 온 寡婦). Oops—I almost forgot again. There's also the school's resident fixture (터주대감), the plump Mr. Cho Nam-hŭi (趙男熙). Although briefly introduced during last year's Kyŏngsŏng Figure Physiognomy, he was truly quite plump. Though his body is sluggish (鈍), his hands are nimble, writing calligraphy well, and his voice is like a yanggeum mallet (양금채 - dulcimer mallet), so he sings well too. During calligraphy class, he fills the time entirely with jokes (익살), causing students to laugh so hard their hands tremble, making it difficult to practice calligraphy, and sometimes their sides ache unbearably. Let's leave Posŏng School and leap over to the end of Kye-dong (桂洞) to look at Chungang High School (中央高等普通學校).

The principal, Mr. Hyŏn Sang-yun (玄相允), true to the "Hyŏn" (玄 - dark, mysterious) in the cosmic phrase *Ch'ŏnji Hyŏnhwang* (天地玄黃 - Heaven and Earth are dark and yellow), has a characteristically sallow face. Because his speech, physique, and behavior are all like those of a classical licentiate (生員님), he has the title "Licentiate Principal." Still, his disposition is gentle (溫和), and he has studied his subjects extensively, so he is greatly welcomed by students. We called Posŏng's Mr. Komatsuzaki "Three Longs"; this Mr. Hyŏn has a long face (面長), long body (身長), long legs (脚長), long fingers (指長), and additionally holds the position of principal (校長), so we could call him "Five Longs." Among these, his fingers are especially long, and it's a topic of conversation among students that during class, he casually holds the chalk box (白墨匣) and attendance book in one hand while teaching. He seems to be shuffling his long legs around lately, intending to study abroad in Germany (獨逸), and since he has a capitalist school owner (校主님) behind him, it's possible this might materialize. Mr. Yi Chung-hwa (李重華) - He has the nickname "Chŏlgut'ong" (절구통 - mortar). Whatever he says, he can't speak without adding the word "nosang" (노상 - always, constantly). Also, his face always bears a smiling expression. And during morning assembly, he uses codes well: tapping the table means he wants a student to give him a pencil; pointing at someone and slightly tilting his finger upwards with a faint smile means they should stand up. Also, standing with his legs spread wide (쩍 버리고) on the athletic field platform during morning assembly is another characteristic. Mr. Na Wŏn-jŏng (羅元鼎), with the surname Na (羅 - silk gauze), is also<34> fine and pretty like silk (비단결). The way he half-removes his hat and smiles broadly when greeting, or the way he walks with his upper body bobbing (깝신깝신), is truly endearing (귀염성스럽다). Fortunately, he's at a boys' school; if he were at a girls' school, who knows what kind of problems might have arisen. Just as the docile goat (염생이), among animals, smokes tobacco well, perhaps gentle people also smoke well, as Mr. Na also smokes quite a lot. Even during class time, as soon as the bell rings, he rushes out without finishing the courtesy (禮) and just smokes until the bell rings for the next class. Just as Principal Chŏng of Posŏng School has nicotine poisoning (煙草中毒) on his fingers, Mr. Na's poisoning extends from his lips to his gums (齒齦 - 니몸), which have turned from yellow to completely black. Also, he frequently uses the phrase "meticulous and thorough" (綿密周到) towards students. Mr. Pak Hae-ton (Pak Hae-ton) seems to be trying hard to look stylish, constantly changing suits, perhaps because he receives a lot of secret affection. And Mr. Cho Ch'ŏl-ho (趙喆鎬) is so dark and fierce-looking that students call him "Tiger" (타이거) or "Sŭtchangsa" (숫장사 - virile man/strongman). Seeing him walking around Kye-dong in the yellowish uniform of the Boy Scouts (少年軍) carrying a leather whip (가죽챗즉), he also resembles a night soil cart driver (衛生馬車夫). Perhaps because he is a former soldier (軍人出身) with an adventurous spirit (冒險性), if a student makes even a small mistake, he hits them indiscriminately; even in severe cold or heat (隆冬盛暑), he makes them exercise with their inner shirts removed; and even on days with heavy snow and cold weather, he routinely makes them take off their socks, which students absolutely detest (질색을 한다). Recently, however, it's said he might be abandoning militaristic rule (武斷) and trying to respect culture (文化), as his violence has somewhat decreased (减下되야다). Mr. Kwŏn Tŏk-kyu (權悳奎) has a pockmarked face, earning him the nickname "Orange" (오렌지 - 蜜柑), and because he often goes to beef soup restaurants (설넝탕집) alone and frequents "standing hotels" [likely pubs/bars] (立字호텔), he has the elegant titles "Solo Soup Teacher" (獨湯先生) or "Secret Drinking Teacher" (密買飮先生). Although short, his brain (腦) is good, having researched history and the Korean language extensively. His mouth is also well-developed, being good at criticism (批評) and witty remarks (才談). In any case, he is much welcomed by students as an interesting (滋味스러운) teacher. Ah—let's stop with Chungang School and head over to Hwimun High School (徽文高等普通學校).

To qualify for the principalship of this school, does one need not only a teacher's license (敎員免許狀) but also a smallpox god license (痘神면허장)? The former principal, Mr. Im Kyŏng-jae (任璟宰), was pockmarked, and the new principal, Mr. Kim Hyŏng-bae (金亨培), is also pockmarked. While Mr. Im had a long face (面長), Mr. Kim has a short face (面短). He looks like he would be quick-tempered (急性), but he speaks haltingly (뜸북뜸북), earning him the nickname "Ttŭmbuk'i" (뜸북이 - Bittern bird, known for its slow call) since his Posŏng School days. Just as his speech is halting, his walk is also halting (뜸북뜸북하게), perhaps explaining why he is always late for work, garnering criticism as the "tardy principal" (지각교장). Dean Yi Yun-ju (李潤柱) - Although he hasn't been at this school long, he spent many years on the teaching platform (敎壇) at Posŏng and Songdo High (松高). His bulging forehead, prominent nose, and strong buck teeth (옥니) exude a chilly breeze even when seen in May or June. Just like his appearance, his personality is also fierce and harsh (强辣), earning him the title "Cholera" (虎列刺) at Posŏng School previously, and now at Hwimun<35> the nickname "Kkaltakwi" (깔다귀 - biting midge). However, as much as his personality is strong, everything he does is systematic (規模的), and because he is strict (嚴切) with students, they are all afraid of him. And Mr. Yi Il (李一), pen name Tongwŏn (東園), has more than one characteristic (不一). He often wears long boots (長靴) on clear days (晴明), wears a winter hat (防寒帽) even on hot days—always one that looks like a dried, split black ox testicle—and his overcoat invariably has something dangling (껑거리) attached to the back. When facing students, he constantly chatters (쨍알쨍알), wrinkling his thin, ungenerous face, yet at other times, he also smiles sweetly. A talented man is often amorous (才子多情), and having much domestic bliss (家庭 滋味), he customarily goes for a walk with his wife (同夫人) on Sundays when the weather is warm. How could Tongwŏn's Peach (東園桃) let Yi Il's Spring (李一時春 - pun on his name meaning 'one spring') pass ordinarily? Mr. Yi Sang-jun (李尙俊) is the roving singing teacher (돌님唱歌敎師) for Posŏng and Hwimun. No—back when Kyŏngsŏng didn't even know what new music (新音樂) was, he, along with Mr. Kim In-sik (金仁湜), was one of the Twin Jades (雙璧) of the Kyŏngsŏng music scene. Whether the progress of the times is fortunate or unfortunate for him, it is difficult indeed to compete with emerging musicians knowing only a few tunes on the reed organ (풍금) or cornet (코렛). He still maintains his reputation (聲譽) based on his experience (履歷), but it is questionable whether he can preserve his current reputation if he does not diligently pursue new studies. Anyway, he is a man of much musical talent (才). Besides new-style songs (新式唱歌), he also sings Korean songs (朝鮮 노래) well. Seeing him, with his "Negro"-like physique, strap on a long drum (長鼓통) and perform Nollyang (놀냥) or Yangsando (양산도), with shoulder and foot movements (억개짓, 발짓), all the beauties of the entertainment world (花柳界) lose their minds. The rumor that he is unusually blessed with female favor (艶福) might be due to his singing. Also, Mr. Kim Yŏng-gŏn (金永建) teaches English. When students ask this way, he answers this way, and when they ask that way, he answers that way, just like writing the character "曰" (said) on deerskin (鹿皮 - meaning inflexible/rote), hence he is called the "American-made Deerskin Teacher" (米國製 鹿皮先生). Ah—what need is there to say such things. Let's stop now and go to Kyŏngsin School (儆新學校).

Although this school was established by the Christian church (基督敎會), it smells too much of the church. Also, these days, having become a designated school (指定學校), they even boast in newspaper advertisements, writing in large letters "Designated by the Government-General" (總督府指定), and the ever-flattering (諂 잘하는) principal, Mr. Kun Ye-bin (君芮彬), brags even at the graduation ceremony that they became a designated school thanks to the grace (恩德) of His Excellency the Governor-General (總督府 閣下). Anyway, among the teachers at that school, there are many characteristics. Mr. Kun Ye-bin looks as if he fell while climbing to Heaven (天堂) and injured himself, lacking a lower jaw with only a pointy nose; he is always good at playing the fool (익살). Messrs. Chang Chi-yŏng (張志暎) and Ch'ŏn Yun-sŏk (千潤錫), quite inappropriately (千不當 萬不當), have no blood relation, yet their parted hair (南北머리), receding foreheads (버서진 이마), appearance (貌色), way of speaking (語態), and even height are so similar that they are called the "Twin Teachers" (雙童先生) at the school. New students often cannot distinguish between the two teachers and frequently say things meant for Mr. Chang to Mr. Ch'ŏn. That's one story, but the physical education teacher, Mr. Ko (高씨), is called Mr. Crow (烏先生) because his face is as black as a crow (가마귀). Coincidentally, there is a library teacher named Mr. O Il-yŏng (吳一英), so<36> when students call out "Mr. O!" (吳先生님!), the gym teacher sometimes gets angry (努한다), thinking they are mocking him. Since Mr. O's name came up, his eye-blinking, frequent snorting, dark glasses (黑眼鏡), and short cane (短杖) are also characteristics. Let's leave Kyŏngsin and, like birds of a feather (草綠同色 - green grass, same color), look at Paechae High School (培材高等普通學校).

The position of Academic Dean (敎務主任) at this school seems as coveted as the Prime Minister's seat, as there is constant infighting (자리다툼) over it, leading to severe discord (反目) among the staff, leaving little leisure for physiognomy reading. However, as the saying goes, "Whatever the host's illness, the ritual must be performed" (主人의 병이야 엇더하던지 할 굿은 다 한다), so whether they fight or not, how can I not do my physiognomy reading? Academic Dean Kang Mae (姜邁) pretends (체) a lot. He pretends to know much old learning (舊學問), pretends to know much new learning (新學問), pretends to socialize (交際) (especially trying to connect with students - 學生聯絡), pretends to wield authority (權利), pretends to believe in Christianity (기독교), pretends to revere Confucianism (유교), and pretends to write (글). The recent turmoil (風波) among the staff at Paechae seems to have originated from these various pretenses (체) of his. However, talking about him while he's in the midst of trouble would be like fanning the flames of a fire (火災에 키질), so let's stop and look at others. Mr. Yu Chin-ŏk (兪鎭億) seems to resemble the neighbor Mr. Kim (뒷집 金道令), clearing his throat frequently (잔기침을 잘 하고), exclusively using the Japanese words "tsumari" (쓰마리 - つまり, in short) and "kara ne" (까라네 - からね, because), and perhaps because he spends his salary only feeding his children (子女) and not himself, his children's faces are all plump (豊厚), while his face gets progressively pointier (뾰죽해저서), resembling a completely eaten corncob (옥수수대) (because his face is particularly pockmarked and long). Also, Mr. Kim Chin-ho (金鎭浩) constantly says "Ittamatta" (잇담맛다 - ?) and has the title "Jesus Goblin" (예수 독갑이). Mr. Mun Se-yŏng (文世榮) has bulging eyes and is called "Toad Teacher" (두검선생). Mr. Ch'oe Tŭng-man (崔登萬) is plump and called "Ch'oe Mireuk" (崔彌勒 - Ch'oe Maitreya). Mr. Kim Sŏng-ho (金成鎬) has a long neck, earning the nickname "Waegari" (왜갈이 - grey heron). And Mr. Yi Wan-sik (李完植) has the elegant title "Love Doctor" (戀愛博士). Shh—talking recklessly might cause problems. Let's stop here and head straight out Namdaemun Gate to Yangjŏng High School (養正高等普通學校).

This school indeed has a quiet location (閑寂) and fresh air, which is very good (좃타만은), but many strange odors (異臭) emanate from it. Mr. Ŏm Chu-ik (嚴柱益), who occupies the principal's chair like an unmovable ancestral tablet (不遷之位) due to his connection with Queen Eom (嚴妃), has a medium build (不短不長), wears his Western suit stylishly, and grooms his handlebar mustache (八字髥). His imposing posture (堂堂한 자세) seems the best among the principals of Kyŏngsŏng's private middle schools. However, one cannot judge the taste of a wild apricot (개살구) just by its appearance. Mr. An Chong-wŏn (安鍾元), no matter how you look at him, is a gentle, old-fashioned scholar (꽁생원). Like Principal Ŏm, Mr. An also occupies the dean's (學監) chair at the same school as an unmovable fixture (不遷之位), but his fame as a calligrapher (筆家) is higher than his reputation as an educator. In any case, he is an indispensable meritorious figure (功勞者) at the school. Besides them, there are several other teachers, but time is pressing, so let's stop. There are also the First and Second Higher Common Schools (第一 第二高普), Chosŏn, and other governmental/public schools (官公立學校), but the Japanese smell (日臭) is too strong, giving me a headache (골이 압푸닛가), so let's skip those too. Although Yŏnhŭi College (延禧專門學校) is near Kyŏngsŏng, it's still Koyang (高陽) territory, so I'll plan to look at it along with provincial educators next time and lay down my brush (擱筆). <37>

**Summary**

This satirical 1925 article, written under the pseudonym "Physiognomist," critiques prominent male educators in Seoul's (Kyŏngsŏng) private secondary schools and colleges (Posŏng, Chungang, Hwimun, Kyŏngsin, Paechae, Yangjŏng). Using the conceit of reading faces and characteristics, the author comments gossip-style on principals' and teachers' appearances, mannerisms, perceived personalities (stubborn, gentle, harsh, vain), teaching styles, rumored habits (smoking, drinking, womanizing), competence, and sometimes their relationships with school administrators, capitalist backers, or Japanese authorities. The piece offers a candid, albeit biased and mocking, snapshot of the colonial-era Korean education scene, focusing on personal flaws and institutional dynamics.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its frank portrayal of Korean educators navigating the colonial system. It reveals internal conflicts within schools, differing pedagogical approaches, the social status and anxieties of teachers, the influence of Japanese language and personnel (like Komatsuzaki), the perceived sycophancy towards the Government-General by some (like Kun Ye-bin of Kyŏngsin), and the underlying tension between traditional Korean values and modern/Western/Japanese influences in education. The casual mentions of political leanings (socialism, Confucianism), connections to power (Government-General, Queen Eom's family), and the economic struggles implied (pawning items) provide valuable social commentary for historians studying this period.

千態萬狀의 京城 敎育界 人物
						觀相者
이애- 금년은 벌서 4월이 되도록 觀相을 한번도 안이 보닛가 너무 심심하구나.
아모리 일이 밧부더라도 또 다시 시작해 보자. 이번에는 특히 京城內 敎育界의 인물을 보자-. 其中에도 각 普通學校와 女學校는 다음 기회로 밀고 헐신 추려서 中等 이상의 남자학교 선생님들만 보자. 압다- 3,4 兩월이야말로 참 학교 선생님들의 생일텬하라는 달이다. 졸업식이니 졸업생 謝恩會니 하는 바람에 과자 봉지, 요리접시도 좀 생기고 전당포 신세를 지여서라도 厚祿古套도 입어 보며 졸업시험, 입학시험에 權利도 약간 부리고 受驗料, 입학금 月謝金을 先捧하는 까닭에 주머니도 통통하다. 이런 시기에 觀相을 보는 것은 비록 觀相債를 안이 밧는 本觀相者라도 마음이 후련하고 또한 흥미가 잇다. 그러면 엇던 학교부터 본단 말이냐. 그것이야 무슨 순서가 잇겟니. 닥치는 대로 막 보자.
普成專門學校- 校長 兪星濬씨는 身軆가 엇지 短小한 지 현미경을 대기 전에는 아모리 밝은 觀相者의 눈이라도 잘 볼 수가 업다. 穴者派 선생이 되야 그러한지 목도리도 꼭 穴居동물인 여호의 가죽으로만 하것다. 또 그에게서는 여러 가지 냄새가 만히 나는데 이력서에는 總督府냄새 懷中에는 聖經냄새, 行纏에는 儒生냄새, 言論에는 묵고 썩은(時代遲) 냄새가 난다. 그리고 임시직업이 校長이지만은 終身專業은 卒業式 祝辭장인 것 갓다. 매년 졸업식 때면 한 학교도 빼놋치 안코 열심으로 쫏차 다니며 의례 來賓祝辭를 한다. 그나마 신기한 말이나 하얏스면 조켓지만은 케케묵은 要領不得의 한 가지 말을 가지고 마치 산골 무식한 사람이 祝文에 기름을 절궈서 해마다 읽덧시 重言復演하야 일반의 苦笑를 밧는 것은 追勢 잘 하고 꾀 만흔 선생의 취할 바가 안이다. 그리고 學監 徐相懽씨는 法律講義錄에 第一項이라는 文字를 목긴 것이 第一이라는 의미로 알엇던지 긴 목을 항상 잘 휘두른다. 敎授할 때에는 「ソレデ」소리로 半볼충을 하는데 말 한마듸에 코방귀는 세번식이나 뀌고 또 말소리도 朝鮮말인지 日本말인지 알 수 업다고 한다. 원래 아모 선생이라도＜32＞ 학생에게 비평듯기야 例事이지만은 그는 兪校長 오기 전 臨時校長代理로 잇슬 때에 학생들에게 특히 「巧裝大狸鼠像幻」이라고 嘲名을 어덧다 한다. 쉬- 그의 말은 구만해 두고 敎授 金泳柱씨를 좀 보자. 그는 法學家인 동시에 鐵棒運動家이다. 강의도 잘하거니외 鐵棒運動도 또한 잘한다. 이것은 아마 氏가 법학을 연구하면서도 法遠拳近이라는 말을 절실이 늣긴 까닭인 것 갓다. 그리고 敎授할 때는 「要スルニ」가 단골文字요 下唇 축 처진 것도 특징이다. 그 다음에 洪性夏씨는 학생이 질문을 하면 가서 講義錄보면 잘 알 것이라고 무책임한 말을 곳잘 하고 專門學校 학생을 小學生 취급하듯이 减点한다는 威脅을 각금한단다. 그의 主義는 社會主義 색채가 좀 잇는 듯 한데 資本家 理事인 金琪邰씨와 氣脉을 잘 통한다고요. 잇크- 시간이 벌서 만히 갓구나- 普成專門學校는 구만 보고 그 길로 普成高等普通學校를 또 좀 보자. 校長 鄭大鉉씨는 그 학교에 벌서 10여 년이나 잇섯것만은 新嫁娘 모양으로 항상 수집은 편이 만코 또 무슨 愁心이 그리 잇는지 兩 미간에 川字가 일상 나타난다. 말할 때에 머리를 빼두름하는 것과 우슬 때에 손을 입에 대는 태도는 아모리 미워하는 사람이라도 貴염성 잇게 볼 것이다. 그런데 담배는 엇지 잘 먹는지 손까락에 담배진이
