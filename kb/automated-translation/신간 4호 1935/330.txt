Modern Utopia!
Modern Utopia!

Recently, because Upton Sinclair (■통·씽크레어) made a political advance, his name has suddenly become prominent. In California (칼리포니아), USA (米國), there is a secret organization (秘密團體) called the Utopian Society (유-토피안·쏘싸이에틔). According to the calculations of Mr. Creel (클리-루 氏), who contested the seat of a representative (代義士) with Sinclair, its membership is said to be 500,000, and apparently, the membership is rapidly increasing (붓적붓적 느러가고 잇다한다).

What this Society (■會) advocates is this: Production will be for use, not for profit (利益을 위해서가 아니고 使用을 위해서의 生産). The livelihood of all people (萬人) will be guaranteed. Citizens will only work from age twenty-five to forty-five (廿五歲로부터 四十五歲지만), and after that, they can retire (隱退) with their livelihood guaranteed. The poor will become comfortable, and the comfortable will become even more comfortable on top of that (그 우에 더 넉넉해지고)—thus realizing a true paradise (眞實의 ■樂世界).

When we think about it, it is, of course, nothing more than an empty thought (空思), but seeing how they handle this with such a sincere attitude there, one cannot help but say that America (米國) is indeed somewhat different from other places. <86>

**Summary**

This article reports on the rise of the Utopian Society in California, USA, spurred by Upton Sinclair's recent political activities. The society, reportedly boasting 500,000 members and growing, advocates for a system of production for use, not profit, guaranteeing everyone's livelihood with mandatory work only between ages 25 and 45, aiming for a "paradise" where the poor become comfortable and the comfortable even more so. The author expresses skepticism, calling it an "empty thought," yet marvels at the seriousness with which Americans are pursuing it, suggesting America's distinct character. For historians, this piece reflects Korean intellectual interest in alternative socio-economic models and utopian ideals during the colonial period, possibly as an indirect critique of their own circumstances or fascination with different societal experiments happening abroad, particularly in America. The author's blend of fascination and skepticism towards this American phenomenon is also noteworthy.

모던·유-토피여
最近 ■통·씽크레어를 政治的 進出을 식힌 까닭에 갑작이 그 일홈이 높아진 米國 칼리포니아에 유-토피안·쏘싸이에틔라는 秘密團體가 잇다 씽크레어와 代義士의 椅子를 다툰 클리-루 氏의 計算에 依하면 그 會員은 五十萬이나 된다고 하며 그리고 붓적붓적 會員이 느러가고 잇다한다. 이 ■會의 主張하는 것은 ─ 利益을 위해서가 아니고 使用을 위해서의 生産을 하게 되여잇고 萬人의 生活은 保證되어 國民은 廿五歲로부터 四十五歲지만 일을 하고 그 後부터는 生活의 保證을 받어 隱退할 수가 잇다. 가난한 자는 넉넉해지고 넉넉한 자는 그 우에 더 넉넉해지고 ─ 이렇게 眞實의 ■樂世界를 實現하자는 것이다. 우리들이 생각해보면 勿論 空思에 지나지 안치만 그곳에선 이것을 너무나 眞實한 態度로서 取扱하는 것을 보면 果然 米國은 다른 곳보다 좀 다르다고 아니할 수 없다.＜86＞
