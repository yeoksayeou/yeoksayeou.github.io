Magazine Title: 개벽
Publication Date: 1935-03-01
Article Title: 戀書를 태우며 『첫아츰 옛 愛人이엇든 太陽에게 주는 絶綠狀』 (Burning Love Letters 『A Letter of Severance Given to the Sun, My First Morning's Old Lover』)
Author: 金海剛
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I
Every year on the first morning,
With a devoted heart, I knelt and prayed
—May a beautiful new son be born.—
Red hope shone like the sky in this small breast.

And so, my youthful prime lit candles to greet you,
And my young chastity was torn and soaked for you, <28>
But saying the breath settling on my shy skirt-folds was tedious,
Didn't you kick me aside and run away?

Love is already a broken zither;
Even if I tune the strings, the booming sounds of past seasons are unlikely to resonate,
Yet, how often did my frail arms flail in the void,
Trying to grasp your shining garments in the morning?

Oh, now you are a heartless sun.
My beloved lord whom I called out to in old days.
Into this pitiful girl's naive little heart,
You cast such a large wound of heartbreak and left.

II
Today is again the first morning of the new year.
Your soft fingers
Gently tapping on the lattice of my house window—
What thoughts are you sowing now, trying to make buried affections sprout again? <29>

『Leading the young choir of the new day,
I've driven my chariot, carrying the shining morning.
My lovely bride! Come out in fine makeup and greet me.』
Are your red lips trying to whisper sweet nothings into my ear again?

You, who left saying you disliked me,
Crossing over snow-covered roofs
To stop again before my house's small window—
Is it that you still can't forget me? Or is it toying with me?

Oh, hateful sun! (My lord!)
Didn't you steal my song back then?
Though beautiful melodies overflow in the streets,
Are you trying to mourn my lost, unfindable song?

III
I hate to see your red face now. <30>
Seeing you only makes the wound in my heart grow larger, so gather your busy steps and go find your new lovers who await you.

Cross over those imposing, high windows,
Part the green curtains,
Go find your new lovers, adorned in the happy red dream of the first morning,
Who will gladly welcome you.

There lies the blessing of the new year, there lies the laughter of the first morning,
And so, a grand feast will be held with you as the guest of honor;
Amidst the billowing sleeves of your lovers' garments, soaked in beautiful fragrance,
Bury your face, drunk on light pink joy.

I will not be deceived by you anymore.
I only prepare the promises to bestow
For my young sisters.
And so, I shall burn the old pages of the past this morning. <31>
<28-31>

**Summary**

The poem is a dramatic monologue addressed to the Sun, personified as a former lover who betrayed the speaker's youthful devotion and hope. On a New Year's morning, the speaker rejects the Sun's attempt at reconciliation, expressing bitterness over past heartbreak and the loss of innocence ("my song"). Declaring the Sun hateful, the speaker resolves to no longer be deceived and instead focuses on preparing promises for "young sisters," symbolizing a turn towards nurturing a new generation or future, concluding by burning the "old pages" of the past. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted allegorically. The powerful, radiant Sun, once a source of hope ("red hope," "new son") but now a source of disillusionment and pain, might symbolize failed promises or perhaps even the Japanese empire itself, whose symbol was the rising sun. The speaker's rejection and turn towards the "young sisters" could represent a nascent desire for a self-determined future for the Korean people, focusing on internal strength and the next generation rather than looking outward to a deceitful power.

戀書를 태우며 『첫아츰 옛 愛人이엇든 太陽에게 주는 絶綠狀』
						金海剛
一
해마다 첫아침이면
정성스리 이 무릅 꿀코 비는 마음
-아름다운 새아들을 誕生하여지이다.-
붉은 希望이 하늘처럼 이 적은 가슴에 빛낫더니라.

그리하야 내 젊은 靑春은 너를 마지려 촉불을 달켯고
내 어린 절개는 너를 위하야 찟겨 젓거늘 ＜28＞
수접은 치마폭에 서리는 숨ㅅ결이 지긋하다고
너는 나를 차 버리고 다라나지 안햇느냐.

이미 사랑이 깨여진 거문고라
줄을 골라본들 지난 時節의 우렁찻든 音響이 퉁겨날리 없지만두
그러나 아츰이면 빛나는 네 옷자락을 움켜잡으려
얼마나 연약한 팔이 虛空을 휘저엇느냐.

오오 이제는 無情한 太陽이다.
옛날에 불러보든 나의 사랑튼 郞君아.
이 가엾은 게집아이의 철없는 적은 가슴에
너는 그다지도 커다란 失戀의 상처를 던저주고 갓더니라.

二
오늘은 또 다시 새해의 첫 아침.
네 보드라운 손ㅅ가락들이
내 집 창살을 살그만이 두다림은
네 또 무슨 생각을 뿌려 파무친 未練을 움트게 하려함이냐. ＜29＞

『새 날의 어린 合唱隊를 선두로
수레를 몰아 빛나는 아침을 실어왓노라.
내 어엽븐 색씨! 고흔 丹粧으로 나와 나를 마지라』
네 붉은 입술은 아름다운 속삭임을 다시 내 귀에 뿌려주려 함이냐.

나를 싫다고 가버리든 네가
눈덮인 집웅들을 넘어
다시 내 집 들창 앞에 걸음을 멈춤은
그래도 나를 못이즘이냐. 그렇지 않으면 戱弄함이냐?

오오 미여운 태양이여! (郞君이여!)
그때에 너는 내 노래를 빼아서 가지 안햇느냐.
아름다운 曲調가 거리거리에 넘처 흘러도
차즐 수 없는 이저버린 내 노래를 너는 吊喪하려 함이냐.

三
네 붉은 낯짝이 이제는 보기도 싫다. ＜30＞
너를 보면 내 가슴의 상처만이 커지기만 할 뿐여서 네 밧븐 걸음을 거두어 너를 기다리고 잇는 네 새로운 戀人들을 차저 가거라.

번듯한 그 높은 들窓을 넘어
綠色 커-텐을 헤치고
첫 아츰의 幸福스러운 붉은 꿈을 丹粧하고 잇는
너를 반겨마질 네 새로운 戀人들을 차저 가거라.

거기엔 새해의 축복이 잇고 거기엔 첫 아츰의 우슴이 잇고
그리하야 너를 主賓으로 성대한 잔채가 벌어지리니
아름다운 香氣에 저진 네 戀人들의 옷자락 활개속에
연분홍빛 깃쁨에 醉한 네 얼굴을 파묻어 버리라.

이제는 더 네에게 속지 않으리라.
내 어린 姉妹들을 위하야
베푸러줄 約束을 준비할 뿐이다.
그리하야 옛날의 낡은 책장을 이 아침에 태워바리리라. ＜31＞
＜28-31＞
