Student Conduct Final Tally
Deviant behaviors among middle school and elementary school students during December totaled 1,212 cases, showing a trend of gradual increase<78>, a noteworthy phenomenon at a time when the issue of student conduct has become a topic of discussion.
The most frequent offense was entering motion picture theaters, with 550 cases (elementary school 98, middle school 452). Next was improper attire with 398 cases, all involving male middle school students.
There were 43 cases of tardiness, also mostly middle school students (38 cases), including some female middle school students.
Next were 22 cases of entering cafes, restaurants, and teahouses, which included 6 female students. Eating street food accounted for 30 cases, skipping meals for 12 cases, and incidents of insulting women or beating younger students. Broken down by elementary/middle school and gender:

**Summary**

This article from the March 1935 issue of *Kaebyŏk* presents various statistics related to colonial Korea. It covers January tobacco sales figures by region, a significant increase in mining applications in 1934 compared to 1933, the allocation of 8 million *wŏn* for the third poverty relief project detailing specific public works, a breakdown of student misconduct cases in December (with movie-going and improper attire being most common), and extensive data on film censorship in 1934, including figures by production company and genre, noting a sharp rise attributed to new film control measures. The section on provincial illiteracy rates is mentioned but lacks data. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable quantitative insights into the colonial economy (resource extraction via mining, state monopolies like tobacco), social control mechanisms (student discipline monitoring, extensive film censorship under new regulations), and state-led infrastructure development presented as "relief." The detailed breakdown of relief funds and the specific categories of student offenses and censored film genres provide concrete data points reflecting the administration's priorities and societal trends during this period.
