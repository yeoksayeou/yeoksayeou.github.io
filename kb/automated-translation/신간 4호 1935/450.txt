[Summary of the previous installment = King Sejong's eldest son, the Crown Prince, was weak and lacking in both mind and body. Though destined to be the king of this country, he had too many shortcomings.
The second son, Grand Prince Chinpyŏng, Yu (*presumably 瑈, later King Sejo), was, on the other hand, too excessive. As he aged, his personality became even stronger, resembling that of a king (王者) rather than a prime minister. It was the bearing of one who stands above ten thousand and commands them, not one who stands beneath another. King Sejong constantly worried about this contrast.
It happened one evening during the grand feast held at Kyŏnghoeru Pavilion to celebrate the 30th year of the king's reign. A dog that the king usually cherished suddenly dashed into the banquet hall. The Crown Prince ordered a eunuch to chase it away. At that moment, Yu, who was nearby, interjected, saying, "Since it is a dog His Majesty loves, wouldn't it be alright to leave it be?" Though Yu spoke without any deep intention, the Crown Prince became angry, causing a small emotional clash between the Crown Prince and Yu. The king, observing this from the sidelines, began to harbor a new worry from that night onwards.
After that, the king had the Crown Prince act as regent and stepped back himself.
However, because the Crown Prince was weak-minded, he was constantly overshadowed by Yu. Being overshadowed, he would get angry and inwardly disliked, envied, and suspected Yu greatly.] <65>

Seven
Discord between brothers—.
The discord between the Crown Prince and Suyang.
Suyang didn't seem to blame his elder brother, the Crown Prince, much for the scorn, but the more Suyang didn't blame him, the more the Crown Prince seemed to scorn Suyang, as if to say, "Look at this."
Observing the attitudes of these two sons, the king sometimes shuddered inwardly. Even a tiny worm has a fifth of its spirit—no matter how broad-minded Suyang was towards his elder brother, the Crown Prince, and how magnanimously he endured all the scorn, what if this scorn went too far and reached an unbearable point?
Gradually entering his later years, the king—who in his prime had viewed the future somewhat optimistically, thinking things would somehow work out—found that worry about this issue never left him.
Sometimes he would summon Suyang and tell him to be forever loyal to his elder brother, the Crown Prince. Suyang would laugh cheerfully and say,
"As a subject of this country, how could I dare betray the sovereign?"
Thus, he would openly express his feelings to his father. And the king's keen insight knew there was no falsehood in Suyang's words.
However, when he summoned the Crown Prince and said the exact same words he had said to Suyang, how did the Crown Prince react to his father's words? The Crown Prince's face always turned bright red. Then, looking away slightly, he would say,
"As for me, how could I view my younger brother... but Suyang's attitude..."
And thus, he would reveal his suspicions about Suyang. <66>
It was truly a distressing matter.
If the king were to emphasize to the Crown Prince that he should not suspect Suyang, the weak-minded Crown Prince might bow and agree on the surface, but inwardly, his hatred for Suyang would grow in inverse proportion. And who could predict what unfortunate consequences that hatred might bring in the future?
Gradually entering his later years, the fatigue from having been engrossed in too many achievements during his prime suddenly emerged, and the king's health weakened day by day. Since ascending the throne after the three preceding generations—T'aejo, Chŏngjong, T'aejong—the exhaustion from the effort expended to stabilize this half-formed (半造) nation suddenly surfaced in his later years. Seeing his own health, worse this month than the last, worse today than yesterday, the emotional discord between the Crown Prince and Suyang weighed even more heavily on his mind.
Now just over 50—in some ways still considered middle-aged—but having personally directed and encouraged so many achievements, and also having faced delicate family issues, such as the matter with his elder brother Grand Prince Yangnyŏng in the previous generation, and the issue between the Crown Prince and Suyang in his own, the mental strain was immense. Thus, the age of 50, which is middle age for an ordinary person, was old age for the king.
Although he had ostensibly entrusted the affairs of state to the Crown Prince and retired to rest peacefully at the residence of Grand Prince Yŏngŭng, this was solely motivated by the inner desire to let the weak Crown Prince observe state affairs while he was still alive, to guide him personally, holding his hand, and try to make him kingly material. The trouble was actually greater than when he directly handled state affairs himself. Due to such things, the king's health deteriorated further, and as his health worsened, his worries grew proportionally.
Amidst this, the only person who still offered a glimmer of hope was his eldest brother, Grand Prince Yangnyŏng. Now the elder among the royal relatives, not only highest in rank but also a sage (賢人) rarely found in ancient or modern times, Yangnyŏng's continued good health <67> provided one source of hope. Yangnyŏng's good guidance and direction were a single hope. Even if the Crown Prince ascended to the throne in the future, as long as Yangnyŏng, the elder of the royal family, remained alive, the weak-minded Crown Prince would not be able to act freely against Yangnyŏng's wishes. With Yangnyŏng's supervision, even if the Crown Prince and Suyang could not achieve harmony, they might endure without reaching bloodshed.
Thus, in his later years, the king sought out his elder brother Yangnyŏng more often to entrust the future to him.

Eight
One day in the winter of the Kisa year (己巳年, 1449).
That day too, the king was conversing with Yangnyŏng at the house of Grand Prince Yŏngŭng (琰), where the king was currently residing as a detached palace (別宮).
"Brother."
Although publicly their relationship was that of sovereign and subject, the king always treated Yangnyŏng with the utmost respect due an elder brother in private matters.
"A lion is said to kill its cub if it seems incapable of fulfilling the role of a lion, but humans cannot do that. It is truly pitiful."
Yangnyŏng bowed his head to this lament from the king, which he heard frequently whenever they met, remained silent for a while, and then finally replied.—
"Your Majesty. It is something that cannot be helped. It seems an era is coming when the qilin sleeps and the lynx (시라손이) dances..."
"Yes. It seems that you, brother, must look after that lynx and guide it so that it does not dance too fiercely." <68>
I believe not. These eyes of Yangnyŏng have yet to misjudge a person, and as far as Yangnyŏng has seen, Yu is not a petty person constrained by small positions or temples. Forgive my audacious words, but he is a figure who can stand shoulder-to-shoulder with Your Majesty. He is not one to resent being treated lightly, nor one to become arrogant if treated importantly; whatever treatment he receives, he is a person who will steadily proceed according to his own beliefs. What should be guarded against is Yong (龍) - generally, literary types (文學之士) tend to be petty and cunning, pursuing profit (利) inwardly while feigning righteousness (義) outwardly. After Your Majesty's time, with Yu present, this dynasty (社稷) will be like a bedrock (磐石), but without Yu, Yong is a fearsome figure. As Your Majesty must have seen, a person's skill in paduk (바득, Go) reveals their character—the Crown Prince's paduk focuses on defense (防備), and if, by chance, the defense is completed before the enemy launches an attack (攻勢), he barely holds on. But if the enemy attacks before this side's defense is finished, the Crown Prince's paduk ends in total annihilation (全滅). Yu's paduk focuses on attack—while attacking, he defends his own formation (陣形) point by point, giving the enemy no opportunity (機會) to attack, and in the process, annihilates the enemy. Yong's paduk relies solely on sneaky tricks (속임ㅅ수), deceiving the eye to gain tactical advantage (긔리)—the paduk of these three men speaks to their respective characters. In terms of skill level (手), the Crown Prince's paduk appears strongest, but when they play a full game, he has never won by his own skill. Sometimes Yu intentionally lets him win, and when Yu loses, the Crown Prince taunts him with venomous words, and when he loses to Yu, his expression changes (顔色), and he insists they play again. These behaviors are indeed not very pleasant (향그럽지 못한)—"
"It is a pitiful matter."
"Truly a pitiful matter."

Nine
Indeed, it was pitiful. <71>
Such an incident occurred just the other day.
When the king was lying down, feeling unwell with a cold (感氣), the Crown Prince came to inquire after his health. Yu also came to inquire.
At that time, another small clash occurred.
After inquiring about the king's health, the two brothers, the Crown Prince and Yu, withdrew to the main hall for casual conversation (閑談). While talking, Yu was scribbling some characters on a piece of paper (종이ㅅ조각).
Watching this doodling, the Crown Prince suddenly turned pale.—
"What is this?"
On the paper was a phrase from the *Xiaoxue* (小學, Elementary Learning):
"舅姑若使介婦, 母敢敵耦於家婦" (If the parents-in-law employ the daughter-in-law, the mother dare not be a match for the daughter-in-law.)
In this phrase, the character "耦" (ou) was written as "偶" (ou). The Crown Prince pointed this out.
"What's wrong?"
"The radical (변) of the character 'u' is wrong."
"Isn't it the character for 'pair' (짝우ㅅ자)?"
"It is the character for 'pair,' but it's not written with the person radical (人偏); it's written with the plow radical (耒偏)."
"The plow radical? If it's the plow radical, wouldn't it mean something like plowing a field or doing something to a field? Like 'plow-u' (밭갈우) or 'hoe-u' (후치질할우)..."
"Aargh!"
The Crown Prince's face turned pale, and his lips trembled.— <72>
"Born into the royal family (王家), what use is such ignorance (無識)? The character '耦' originally means 'mattock-u' (따비우), but here it also serves as the character for 'pair' (짝우ㅅ자). Not even knowing that, such!"
"'耦' originally means mattock (따비 - I don't know what a 'ttabi' is, though) 'ttabi-u', and if there's a separate character for 'pair-u', wasn't it a mistake (실수) to use the 'ttabi-u' character when the 'pair-u' character should be used? Wouldn't using the 'pair-u' (偶) character be correct (正當)?"
Yu asked this retortively, still smiling cheerfully. At this, the Crown Prince's mouth trembled as if dumbfounded.—
"Agh, ignorant fool! The ancient sages (聖賢)..."
"Perhaps the sages were ignorant like me and wrote the wrong character (誤字)."
—The Crown Prince sprang up from his seat (자리). But Yu just kept smiling cheerfully.
This laughable comedy—the king, lying in the main chamber (正寢) and hearing this argument (다툼), sighed unintentionally.
In truth, Yu couldn't accurately recite even a few passages from Mencius (孟子) if asked. As long as the meaning (뜻) was conveyed, he was the type of person who wouldn't hesitate (긔탄함이 없을) to change individual characters (글짜 개개).
The Crown Prince was the type who (as Yu put it) insisted on sticking exactly to the original text (원문대로 고대로), even if it meant using a different character that the ancient sages might have mistakenly written out of ignorance, regardless of whether the meaning was conveyed.
This broad-minded second son and this meticulous eldest son.
"Ah, why were their orders reversed?"
"Brother. I have a request. If, unfortunately, I should pass away first, I entrust the Crown Prince's (동궁) future (장내) to you. The Crown Prince is inherently weak-minded and likely won't be able to defy his uncle (숙부님), even if he is the sovereign. Please watch over him carefully (감시하서서) and ensure he does not fall into discord (불화) with Yu. For a younger brother to entrust his successor (후사) to his elder brother is backwards, but given the current situation, <73> it seems I must make this request."
To this earnest plea from the king, Yangnyŏng bowed and agreed.

Ten
The Kisa year passed, and the Kyŏngo year (庚午年, 1450) arrived.
The king's health, which had declined alarmingly since the autumn of the Kisa year, worsened further as the Kyŏngo year began.
His excursion to Kŭnjŏngjŏn Hall early in the month to receive New Year's greetings from the court officials (朝臣) became the cause (볼미) of his taking to his sickbed (병석). What was initially thought to be a frequent common cold gradually became serious (침중하여 갔다).
One day (around the middle of the month), Yu came to Grand Prince Yŏngŭng's residence to inquire after his father's health.
"Your Majesty (상감마마). How are you feeling?"
As Yu knelt at the head of the dim room, where screens were drawn in layers because the king felt extremely cold, the king turned his emaciated (수척한) face towards Yu and simply gazed at him intently.
Yu saw his father's emaciated face. The gaunt visage (면영) of the wise ruler (명군) who, as the master of this nation for over 30 years, had freely commanded numerous soaring and crawling officials.
Yu remained prostrate, lifting his head only slightly, looking into his father's eyes. The king too, without a word, just looked back into the eyes of this outstanding son.
Their eyes met. Eyes that seemed expressionless yet held meaning.
"Yu." <74>
Only after a while did the king finally speak.
"Yes..."
"My illness has penetrated to the marrow (골수)."
"..."
"I fear I may not rise again."
"Heaven forbid—"
The king interrupted the words Yu was about to continue.—
"No, answer me truthfully (진정으로). Do you really think there will be a time when I rise again?"
Yu felt a chill in his heart. Seeing his father like this, it didn't seem likely he would ever rise again. Out of filial desire, he wanted to believe the impossible was possible and hoped for a one-in-a-million chance (만에 일의 요행), but thinking with cold reason (리성), he had no choice but to see this illness as the final one. This illness of his father, who had finally collapsed due to excessive exertion (과도한 노력), would likely be the illness that ended his entire life (전생애). Although his age (춘추) suggested he still had time, in terms of achievements (업적)—accomplishments that others might not complete even over several generations, which this father of his finished in just 30 years—it seemed likely that he would end his life concurrently with the conclusion (일단락) of those achievements.
Yu could not answer his father's question. Without waiting for Yu's reply, the king continued.—
"Yu. It's strange."
"?"
The king paused. He closed his eyes. Beneath the thin eyelids, one could see his eyeballs rolling here and there. <75>
"What is it?"
When Yu asked again, the king opened his eyes slightly. In the king's eyes appeared a pitiful (가련한) expression, seeking sympathy (동정), never seen before.—
"I wish to lie ill in your house."
"?"
What did he mean? Yu did not immediately understand the meaning.
"Your house—the rear hall (후당) of your house—"
Yu was startled. Tears (눈물) suddenly welled up, covering his eyes.
Four years ago—the place where the queen consort had passed away (승하). As his illness grew serious, the king suddenly seemed to long for that room.
The queen consort (mother of the Crown Prince, Yu, Yong, etc.) had also left the palace when her illness became critical and breathed her last in the rear hall of Yu's house. Now, lying in the house of his most beloved youngest son, as his illness worsened, he suddenly seemed to long for the room where the queen consort had passed away four years prior.
Yu bowed his head deeply (푹 숙으렸다) to hide the tears welling in his eyes.
"It depends on Your Majesty's decision, but the weather is so cold, I worry how it might affect your condition (환후)..."
"No, it's just talk. What's the point of going?"
The conversation broke off.
Yu remained silent, his head bowed low. A thousand thoughts raced through his mind. Now a middle-aged man approaching 40, mature in thought (사상) and physique (골격), Yu was a strong-willed person who had forgotten <76> sentimental feelings (애상적 긔분). He was magnanimous (호활) enough to dismiss most anger-provoking matters with a laugh like an elder (長者) and barely feel most sentimental things, but this self-deprecating (자조) plea from his father struck him to the core (가슴이 쿡 찔렸다).
To sympathize with his father's current state of mind (심경), he should, of course, bring him to his own house.
But if he brought his father to his house, how would the Crown Prince—who (already looked at him strangely in private matters, attaching peculiar 외누리) think?
His father, unlikely to recover. If he were to pass away (승하) in the rear hall of his own house, it might satisfy his father's heart, but the rift (새) between the Crown Prince and himself would widen further.
After his father's passing, the Crown Prince would naturally be the king of this country. —However, as Yu well knew, he was too weak a king, a scholar-type (샌님) who could absolutely not manage a country without his (Yu's) assistance (보필).
If the rift between the Crown Prince and himself widened and he were sidelined (바지고 보면), there would be no one to support the country during the Crown Prince's era.
From this perspective (견해 아래서), it was difficult to heed his father's pitiful wish (뜻). His father seemed to have guessed as much and withdrawn the suggestion with self-mockery, but to consider his father's withdrawal fortunate and ignore the issue felt desolate (쓸쓸하였다) to his conscience (량심상).
—My poor father.
After bowing his head for a while, Yu lifted it slightly.—
"Your Majesty. The weather (일긔) is so cold, moving (거동합시기가) seems a bit difficult."
"It was just a passing remark. Don't mind it." <77>
Another brief silence.
"Yu."
"Yes?"
"Don't mind it."
"Yes."
"You know the reason why I cannot go."
"Yes..."
"Besides that, I have something to tell you. Come closer."
"Yes..."
Yu moved closer on his knees (무릅거름으로 밧삭).
"The Crown Prince visited earlier, so he won't come again today. I have something to tell you."
"Yes..."
The king closed his eyes. His thin lips trembled several times.
"You understand my intentions (뜻), don't you?"
"Yes..."
"I don't know if I'll have another chance to speak with you quietly like this before my death (림종전). So, on this occasion today, I intend to tell you everything that's been on my mind. It's essentially my last will (유언)."
"Yes..." <78>
"You know what your position (립장) will be after my passing (천추만세후에), don't you?"
"Yes..."
"The Crown Prince is a weak person. Because he is weak, he is suspicious. You must support him (붓들어야 한다). Even if the Crown Prince dislikes you because he is weak, you must not blame him but serve him with utmost loyalty (충성)."
"Yes. I always think that way."
"Right. I know that too. Because I know your heart and trust you, I make this request. Understand this. Even if he hates you, you are someone who has no right to resent him."
"I have known that for a long time (일즉부터)."
"Also, because he is weak, the dynasty (사직) itself is endangered (위트럽다) if you do not support him."
"I know that too."
"No matter how much the Crown Prince scorns (괄시) you—even if he orders your death—you cannot defy him (거역)."
Yu looked up.—
"Your Majesty. I will certainly never blame the Crown Prince for his scorn. However, if he tries to distance me, I will resist (거역). If he orders my death, I will oppose (항거) it. If I am sent far away or if I die, who will protect (두호히리까) this dynasty?"
"Alright. I misspoke. Since I trust your loyalty (충심) and your strength (힘), assist the Crown Prince to the best of your ability."
"Yes, I too have long—"
He cut off his words. <79>
"So?"
"Yes..."
"Go on, speak."
Yu stammered.—
"Yes. Uh—it is disloyal and unfilial (불충불효) to ask such things of Your Majesty while you are still alive (생존해 겝신)—"
"I will not hold it against you."
"When the Crown Prince ascends the throne (등극하시게 되오면), I have occasionally thought that I would respectfully escort the Crown Prince to Kangnyŏngjŏn Hall (강녕전) and guard the veranda (툇마루) of Sajŏngjŏn Hall (사정전) myself."
The king opened his eyes wide and looked up at his son. A slight gleam appeared in his weak and listless eyes.
"I trust you. I trust you."
"I will repay you with utmost sincerity (지성껏)."
"One more thing. The Crown Prince's health is not good. It might be excessive to think this far, but in the event of misfortune befalling the Crown Prince, I entrust the future (장내) of the Crown Prince's son (세손) to you today as well."
"Yes. If my lifespan (수) allows, I will fulfill the role of the Duke of Zhou (주공) for generations (대대로), for however many generations it takes."
"What if the Crown Prince scorns you to the point where it's unbearable for you?"
"Even then, I would endure it."
"If you endure and endure and endure, and still cannot bear it?"
"I will consult with my uncle (숙부, Yangnyŏng)." <80>
"Ah—"
—My son, the king swallowed hard the words that had risen to his throat.
"Your uncle is a wise man (현인). If you encounter difficult matters, go to your uncle. With your wisdom (지혜) and strength (힘), you should be able to overcome most difficulties, and with your patience (참을성), you should navigate most situations smoothly. However, when you face truly unbearable times, consult your uncle. The Crown Prince too will not be able to defy his uncle's words."
"That is my thought, but if I firmly engrave the single character for patience (忍 'in') in my heart, I think I can manage without troubling my uncle."
"Yes. If ten times of enduring doesn't work, endure twenty times; if twenty times doesn't work, endure thirty times! Endure, endure. Now I too can close my eyes in peace."

Eleven
That day, the king was reluctant to send this son away, so he had him eat dinner in his own bedchamber and let him go only at night.
After bidding his father farewell at night and leaving the detached palace, Yu wondered whether to go straight home but then decided to visit his uncle, Yangnyŏng. He hadn't visited his uncle since the latter returned from a hunting trip two or three days ago.
Seated in his palanquin (남여), Yu did not open his eyes once on the way to his uncle's house. While his attendants (구종 별배놈들), proud of escorting the imposing Prince Suyang, shouted loudly and cleared the path, the master of the procession, Yu, sat with a heavy heart, eyes closed, lost in thought.
His father's words from earlier—though they were things he himself often contemplated and weren't particularly bothersome in themselves—weighed heavily on his mind when he considered that <81> all those words might constitute his father's final instructions (유탁).
How thin his cheeks, once so full, had looked earlier. It wasn't just due to illness. He must have become so gaunt from the constant worry about the troublesome issue between the Crown Prince and himself.
—If only the order had been reversed...
Ah. Though his father showed no sign of such words, Yu knew well that such thoughts resided in his father's heart too.
—If only the order had been reversed. Why was he born later?
—Since the order was as it was, if only his elder brother, the Crown Prince, had at least been a broad-minded person like his father's elder brother, Yangnyŏng, things might have been a little better. Why was he born such a small-minded, narrow, and weak person?
... I am the current Crown Prince and the future king, so my position is my position, but since my strength is lacking, you must help me. Why couldn't he be straightforward like that?
He himself had no other intentions (타이) and only served him as an elder brother and Crown Prince. Why did the Crown Prince view him with suspicion, avoid him, shun him, and try to distance him?
Up until now, his father was still alive and below his father, so there wasn't much to complain about. But if his father passed away and the Crown Prince ascended the throne, then open hatred would surely be poured upon him. He intended to endure everything and remain loyal to his brother-king until the very end, but what if that loyalty was not accepted? What if the king used the power he held to distance him?
It would surely happen. It was certain to happen, a pitiful situation.
To resist that would be to resist the sovereign. But to submit to it would also not be loyalty. To obey the king's wrongful <82> actions unquestioningly also goes against the duty of a subject (인신).
What should he do?
It was a difficult position (립장).
"Woof woof woof woof"
Startled by a loud clamor, he came to his senses. The procession had already entered the main gate of his uncle Yangnyŏng's house. The hunting dogs of the house ran out, barking loudly.
Yu alighted from the palanquin.
"You dogs!"
Yu shouted at the dogs barking fiercely around them, while the attendants nervously backed away.
While the servants of the house ran out to calm the dogs, Yu entered through the middle gate (중대문).

Twelve
"Uncle. His Majesty's condition (환후) is not ordinary."
"Oh? Are you just coming from the detached palace?"
"Yes..."
Uncle and nephew sat facing each other.
Yangnyŏng gazed intently at his nephew's face.
"Worry doesn't suit your face. Smooth it out." <83>
Yu gave a wry smile (고소하였다).
"Uncle, you are fortunate indeed to always be free from worries."
"What worries could I have? My status is Royal Elder Brother (王兄), Buddhist Brother (佛兄)..."
"Perhaps, but I am a Royal Prince (王子), and soon to be a Royal Younger Brother (王弟), yet I have many worries."
Yu lowered his head despondently (적적한 듯이).
"Don't worry so much. The Crown Prince, though weak-minded, is not a wicked person. It's said that even when a tiger has many cubs, one among them is bound to be a lynx (시라손이). It's just a bit problematic that the lynx turned out to be the eldest..."
Yangnyŏng laughed cheerfully.
"Well then. How about trying some deer meat?"
"I don't want any."
"What's wrong? You declining deer meat?"
Yu lowered his head. A sigh threatened to escape his lips.
"Uncle. I envy your state of mind (심경)."
"I am the King's Brother, you are the King's Brother-to-be. What's the difference between you and me? It all depends on one's mindset."
"Is that so? Your role as the King's Brother might just involve relaxing comfortably and going hunting, but I fear my role as the King's Brother won't be like that."
Yangnyŏng stared intently at his nephew's face for a moment.
"Hey. You think I don't know what's on your mind? I know everything. But what can be done? It's your unfortunate fate (팔자), what can you do about it?" <84>

**Summary**

This excerpt from Kim Tongin's historical novel "Giant" depicts the growing tension between King Sejong's two sons: the weak-willed Crown Prince (later King Munjong) and the capable, ambitious Grand Prince Suyang (later King Sejo). King Sejong, ailing in his later years, worries deeply about the future of the dynasty due to the Crown Prince's inadequacy and his suspicion of Suyang. Sejong entrusts Suyang's guidance to his elder brother, Grand Prince Yangnyŏng. In a poignant scene, the dying king asks Suyang to remain loyal to the Crown Prince, even anticipating future conflict, and extracts a promise from Suyang to protect the throne and the young Crown Prince's son if necessary. Suyang later visits Yangnyŏng, expressing his anxieties about his difficult future role under his weak and suspicious brother.

From a colonial history perspective, this piece is interesting as it reflects a continued engagement with pre-colonial Korean history and figures during the Japanese occupation. Publishing a historical novel centered on powerful Joseon Dynasty figures like Sejong and Sejo in 1935 could be seen as a subtle assertion of national identity and historical continuity. The focus on internal conflict and succession struggles within the royal family might also implicitly comment on leadership and stability, potentially resonating with contemporary anxieties under colonial rule, though any direct political allegory is carefully avoided.

中篇 巨人
						金東仁
「前號의 梗槪= 세종대왕의 맛아드님 세자는 그 마음으로던 몸으로던 약하고 부족하엿다. 장내의 이 나라의 왕이 될 귀한 몸이나 너무도 부족한 점이 많엇다.
둘재아드님 진평대군 유(*)는 또한 그 사람이 너무 과하엿다. 나이가 드러가면서 더욱 그 성격이 억세어가서 그것은 재상ㅅ가음이 아니고 왕자(王者)ㅅ가음이엇다. 만인의 우에 서서 만인을 지휘할 가음이지 남의 아래 설 가음이 아니엇다. 세종대왕은 이 대조(對照)를 늘 근심하엿다.
그것은 왕의 즉위 30년 축연이 경희루에서 굉장이 열린 날 저녁이엇다. 평상때 왕이 사랑하는 개한마리가 잇섯는 데 그날 그 개가 돌연 연석에 뛰처들엇다. 그것을 세자가 환관에게 명하여 내려쪼츠라 하엿다. 그때에 유가 겨테잇다가 상감이 사랑하시든 개니 그냥 두어도 괜찬치 안습니까하고 말렷다. 유는 별로이 기픈 뜻이 업시 한 말인대도 불구하고 세자는 성을 내엇기 때문에 세자와 유의 사이에 조고만 감정상의 충돌이 생겻다. 이것을 방관한 왕은 그날 밤붙어 새로운 근심을 하게 되엇다.
그 후 왕은 세자로 하여금 섭정케하고 당신은 물러안젓다.
그러나 세자는 마음이 나약하기 때문에 유에게 늘 눌리우고 눌리우면서는 역정을 내고 내심 매우 유를 실허하고 투기하고 의심하는 것이엇다. ＜65＞

七
동긔간의 불화-.
세자와 수양과의 새의 불화.
수양은 그다지 형 세자의 괄시를 탓하지 않는 모양이나- 수양이 탓하지 않으면 않느니만치 세자는 더욱더 이것 보아라는 듯이 수양을 괄시하였다.
이 두 아드님의 태도를 가만히 관찰할 때에 왕은 때때로 속으로 몸서리까지 첬다. 한치의 벌레에도 5분의 결ㅅ긔는 있는 것- 아모리 수양이 활달한 눈으로 형 세자를 대하고 모든 괄시를 관대히 참아온다 하나 이 괄시가 도를 넘처서 참을 수 없는 정도까지 이르면 어쩌나.
차차 만년(晩年)에 들은 왕- 장년시대에는 그래도 장내에 어떠케던 되겠지 이만치 락관의 눈으로 보아왔지만 만년에 들면서부터는 어제던 여기 대한 근심이 왕에게서 떠나 본 적이 없었다.
때때로 수양을 불러서 장내 영구히 형 세자께 충실하라고 하면 수양은 쾌활히 웃으면서
「신이 이 나라 백성인 이상에야 어찌 감히 군왕을 배반하리까」
하여 자긔의 마음을 숨김없이 아버님앞에 피력하고 하였다. 그러고 왕의 투철한 안목은 결코 이 수양의 말에 거짓이 없음을 안다.
그러나 세자를 불러서 수양에게 한 말과 꼭 같은 말을 하면 세자는 이 부왕의 말에 어떤 태도로 대하고 하나? 세자는 언제던 얼굴을 바-ㄹ가케 변하였다. 그런 뒤에는 좀 외면을 하며
「신이야 동생을 어떠케 보리까만 수양의 눈치가...」
하여 수양에게 대한 자긔의 의심을 나타내이고 하였다. ＜66＞
진실로 괴로운 일이었다.
여기서 만약 왕이 세자에게 수양을 의심치말기를 강조하면 심약한 세자는 표면으로는 절하여 이를 승복할지나 내심으로는 그 반비례로 더욱 수양을 미워할 것이다. 그러고 그 미움은 장차 어떤 불길한 결과를 낳을넌지 예측도 못할 것이다.
차차 만년에 들면서 장년시대에 너무도 많은 업적에 골몰하였든 피곤이 한꺼번에 나서 왕의 몸은 나날이 쇠약하여 갔다. 태조, 정종, 태종 - 전 삼대의 뒤를 이어서 등극한 이래 이 반조(半造)의 국가를 고정시키노라고 쓴 그 노력의 피곤이 만년에 들면서 갑자기 나서 이 달이 전달보다 못하고 오늘이 어제보다 못한 당신의 건강을 볼 때에 세자와 수양과의 새의 감정상 불합이 더욱 마음에 걸렀다.
지금 50을 조금 넘은 - 어떠케 보자면 아직도 장년이랄 수가 있으나 너무도 많은 업적을 몸소 지휘하고 장려하고 또한 가정적으로도 선대에는 형 양녕대군과의 새의 뗄니케잍한 문제이며 당신의 대에는 세자와 수양과의 문제등으로 심노가 많었드니만치 보통ㅅ사람의 장년인 50이 왕에게 있어서는 만년이었다.
지금은 표면으로는 나라의 정사를 들어서 세자에게 맡기고 당신은 은거한 몸으로 한가히 영응대군의 사택에서 쉬인다하나 이것은 오로지 약한 세자로 하여곰 당신이 아직 생존한 동안 정사를 견학시키고 당신이 스스로 세자의 손을 잡고 지도하며 세자를 국왕ㅅ가음이 되도룩 만들어보려는 내심에서 나온 것이지 번거러움은 당신이 직접 정사를 볼 때보다도 도로혀 더 하였다. 이런 일 등 때문에 왕의 건강은 더욱 상하고 건강이 상하면 상하느니만치 근심이 더욱 컸다.
이러한 가운데서 단지 한사람 그래도 희망을 붙여두는 사람은 당신의 백형 양녕대군이었다. 지금 왕족중의 어런이오. 행렬로도 가장 위-ㄹ뿐 아니라 고금에 다시 찾기 힘들은 이 현인(賢人)인 양녕이 그냥 건강히 있거니 ＜67＞ 양녕의 좋은 지휘와 지도가 한 가지의 희망이 되기는 하였다. 장내에 세자가 국왕의 지위에 등극을 하는 날이라도 왕족의 어런으로 양녕만 그냥 생존했으면 심약한 세자는 양녕을 거역하여서까지 자유로운 행동은 못할 것이고 양녕의 감시만 있으면 (비록 세자와 수양과가 화목까지는 못한다 할지라도) 류혈지극까지는 보지 않고 견디어 날 것 같다.
이리하여 왕은 만년에 더욱 형 양녕을 가까히 찾어서 장내를 당부하였다.

八
기사년(己巳年) 겨울 어떤 날.
그 날도 왕은 왕이 지금 별궁으로 거처하고 있는 영응대군 염(永膺大君 琰)의 집에서 양녕과 마조 이야기를 하고 있었다.
「형님」
비록 공(公)으로는 양녕과 군신지간이라 하나 왕은 언제던 양녕에게 대하여 사분의 형 대접을 깐땃이 하였다.
「사자는 제 새끼가 사자ㅅ구실을 감당할 것 같지 못하면 죽여 버린다 하지만 사람은 그러치도 못하고 참으로 딱하오이다.」
만나는 때마다 늘 듣는 이 왕의 하소연에 대하여 양녕은 머리를 숙이고 잠자코 한참을 있다가야 비로소 대답하였다.-
「전하. 헐 수 없는 일이올시다. 기린은 잠자고 시라손이가 춤추는 시대가 올 모양이니...」
「네. 그 시라손이를 형님께서 돌보서서 과히 숭한 춤이나 안추도록 지도해 주어야 할가 봅니다.」 ＜68＞
아닌가 하옵니다. 이 양녕의 눈이 아직것 사람을 헐 본 일이 없사온데 양녕이 본 바 유는 작은 지위나 절에 구애될 소인이 아니옵고 무엄한 말슴이오나 전하와 넉넉히 어깨를 비길만 한 인물이옵니다. 소홀히 여긴다고 그런 것을 나므렴할 인물도 아니요 중히 여긴다고 거만해질 인물도 아니요 아모런 대접을 할지라도 제가 믿는 바 대로 꾸준히 해 나갈 인물이옵니다. 경게할 것은 용 - 대체 문학지사라는 것은 좀ㅅ되고 간특하고 속으로는 리를 취하고 겉으로는 의(義)를 가식하는 것 - 전하 천추만세지후에는 유있으니 이 사직도 반석과 같겠지만 유만 없으면 용은 무서운 인물이옵니다. 전하도 보섰겠지만 바득의 수는 그 사람의 성격을 나타내는 것-세자의 바득은 방비를 위주하는 것으로서 방비만 하다가 요행 적이 공세를 취하기 전에 방비가 다 되면 그렁그렁 견디거니와 이 편의 방비가 끝나기 전에 적이 공세를 취하면 세자의 바득은 전멸이 되옵니다. 유의 바득은 공격을 위주하는 것 - 공세을 취하는 중에 한점 한점 자긔의 진형도 지켜서 적이 공세를 취할 긔회를 안주고 그러는 중에 적을 전멸시키는 것 - 용의 바득은 살살 속임ㅅ수만 뚤러서 눈을 속여서 긔리를 취하려는 것 - 이 세 사람의 바득은 각기 그 사람됨을 말하는 것이옵니다. 수로 보아서 세자의 바득이 가장 센 모양인데 대국을 하면 한 때도 제수로 이겨본 적이 없읍니다. 때때로는 유가 짐짓 저주는 일이 있는데 유가 지면 독이 있는 말로 유를 아유하고 유에게 지면 안색을 변해 가지고 또 두자고 강청하는 모양등은 과연 그다지 향그럽지 못한 일-」
「딱한 일이올시다」
「참 딱한 일이옵니다」

九
과연 딱하였다. ＜71＞
일전에도 이런 일이 있었다.
왕이 감긔로서 몸이 좀 불편해 누어있을 때에 세자가 문병을 왔다. 유도 문병을 왔다.
그때 또 조그만 충돌이 있었다.
문병을 한 뒤에 세자와 유, 이 형제가 대청에 물러나가서 한담을 할 때에 유는 이야기를 하면서 종이ㅅ조각에 무슨 글을 그저기고 있었다.
이 장난을 들여다 보다가 세자가 문득 질색하였다.-
「이게 무에란 말인가」
조히에는 소학의 한 구절
「舅姑若使介婦, 母敢敵耦於家婦」
라는 글이 적히어 있었는데 그 가운데 「耦」자가 「偶」자로 되였다. 세자는 그것을 지적하였다.
「웨요?」
「우ㅅ자의 변이 틀렸네」
「짝우ㅅ자 아닙니까」
「짝우자는 짝우ㅅ자지만 人변에 쓴 자가 아니오 耒변에 쓴 자라네」
「耒변입니까? 耒변이라면 무슨-밭을 간다던가 밭을 어떠컨다는가 하는 자가 아닐가요? 「밭갈우」라든가 「후치질할우」라든가...」
「에익!」
세자는 얼굴이 창백해지면서 입술은 파들파들 떨었다.- ＜72＞
「왕가에 태여나서 그러틋 무식해서 무엇에 쓴단 말인가? 「耦」자는 본시는 띠비우ㅅ자지만 여기서는 짝우ㅅ자도 되는 자야. 그런 것도 모른단 그런!」
「耦가 본시 따비(따비가 뭐인지는 모르겠읍니다만) 따비우ㅅ자고 짝우ㅅ자가 따로히 있으면 짝우ㅅ자를 쓸 경우에 따비우ㅅ자를 쓴 것이 실수가 아닐가요? 짝우(偶)자를 쓰는 것이 정당치 않을가요?」
유는 그냥 벙글벙글 웃으면서 이러케 반문하였다. 거기 대해서 세자는 기가 막히는 듯이 입을 떨었다.-
「에이 무식한! 옛날 성현이...」
「성현도 아마 저같이 무식해서 오자(誤字)를 쓰신 모양입지오」
-세자는 자리를 떨치고 일어났다. 그러나 유는 그냥 벙글벙글 웃고만 있었다.
이 웃지 못할 희극-정침에 누워서 이 다툼을 들은 왕은 뜻하지 않고 한숨을 쉬었다.

十一
그 날 왕은 이 아들을 돌려 보내기가 싫어서 당신의 침전에서 저녁까지 먹여서 밤에야 놓아주었다.
밤에야 부왕께 하직움„ 하고 별궁을 나선 유는 그냥 집으로 돌아갈가 하다가 다시 생각을 돌이켜서 삼촌 양녕을 찾기로 하였다. 사냥을 나갔다가 二三일전에 돌아 왔다는 삼촌을 유는 돌아온 이래 찾지 못하였든 것이다.
남여에 몸을 잠그고 삼촌의 집으로 가는 동안 유는 한번도 눈을 떠 보지 않었다. 서슬이 푸르른 왕자 수양대군의 행차라고 구종 별배놈들은 의긔가 양양하여 우렁차게 호령을 하며 길을 달렸지만 행차의 주인 유는 무거운 마음으로 눈을 감고 생각에 잠겼다.
부왕의 아까의 말- 그것은 자긔도 늘 생각하는 배로 그것이 새삼스러히 마음에 걸리는 배는 아니었지만 아까의 ＜81＞ 모든 그 말이 모도 부왕의 유탁(遺託)인가 생각하면 저절로 마음이 무거워젔다.
이전에 그러틋 풍부하든 뺨이 아까보니 얼마나 야위였드냐. 그것은 단지 병때문이 아니다. 마음에 늘 걸리는 동궁과 자긔와의 문제가 근심되여 그러틋 야위였을 것이다.

-순서만 바뀌어 났드면. 웨 자긔가 뒤에 났느냐.
-순서가 이러케 된 이상에는 하다 못해 자긔의 형 세자가 부왕의 형 양녕과 같이 활달한 인물로라도 되였으면 그래도 좀 나을 것을 웨 그다지도 마음이 작고 좁고 약한 인물로 태여났나.
... 나는 현재의 동궁이오 장내의 국왕이니 내 자리는 자리려니와 내 힘이 모자라는 껏 네가 도와다고. 웨 이러케 솔직이 나오지 못하는가.
자긔는 아모 타이(他意)가 없고 오로지 형으로서 또는 동궁으로서 섬기거늘 웨 그러케 자긔를 의심의 눈으로 보고 꺼리고 피하고 멀리하려 하는가.
지금껏은 아직 부왕이 생존해 게시고 부왕의 아레이니 그다지 탓할 것도 없거니와 만약 부왕 승하하시고 동궁이 즉위하면 그때는 자긔에게 대하여 노골적 증오가 부어질 것이다. 자긔는 모든 일을 다 참고 그때의 형왕께 끝끝내 충성되려 하지만 그 충성을 받지 않으려면 어쩌나. 왕의 잡은 바 권력으로서 자긔를 멀리 하려면 어쩌나.
필시 그럴 것이다. 필시 그럴 것이라 딱한 일이었다.
거기 항거하는 것은 군왕에게 항거하는 것이다. 그러나 거기 또한 승복하면 이는 충성이 아니다. 왕의 그릇된 ＜82＞ 일을 그대로 복종하는 것도 인신의 도리에 어그러진 일이다.
어쩌나.
괴로운 립장이었다.
「컹컹컹컹」
와르르 요란한 소리에 펄덕 정신을 차리니 행차는 어느덧 삼촌 양녕의 집 대문안에 들어섰다. 이집 사냥개들이 우렁차게 짖으면서 뛰여나왔다.
유는 남여에서 나렸다.
「이 개!」
둘러서서 짖어내는 개에게 별배들은 겁이 나서 비슬비슬 피하는 것을 유는 호령하였다.
이 댁 하인들이 달려 나와서 개를 진정시킬 동안 유는 중대문 안으로 들어섰다.

十二
「숙부님. 상감마마 환후가 심상치 않습디다」
「응 지금 별궁에서 오는 길이냐?」
「네... 』
마조 앉은 숙질.
양녕은 조카의 얼굴을 물끄럼이 들여다 보았다.
「네 얼굴은 근심하면 어울리지 않는 얼굴이다. 펴라」 ＜83＞
유는 고소하였다.
「숙부님은 언제던 참 근심이 없으서서 다행이올시다」
「내게 무슨 근심이 있겠느냐. 신분이 왕형(王兄)이고 불형(佛兄)이고...」
「그러치만 저는 왕자이오. 인제 왕제가 될 신분이라도 근심이 많습니다」
유는 적적한 듯이 머리를 숙였다.
「그다시 근심 말어라. 동궁도 마음이 약할 뿐이지 악인은 아니다. 호랑이도 새끼를 많이 낳으면 한 머리는 시라손이가 있는 법이니라. 시라손이가 맏이가 된 것이 좀 탈이지만... 」
양녕은 쾌활히 웃었다.
「자. 노루고기나 좀 먹어보련?」
「싫습니다.」
「네가 노루고기를 싫다니 웬일이냐?」
유는 머리를 숙였다. 한숨이 입에서 새이려 하였다.
「숙부님. 숙부님의 심경이 부럽습니다.」
「나는 왕형 너는 왕제 네나 내나 다를게 뭐이 있느냐? 마음 하나 먹기에 달렸지」
「그럴가요? 숙부님의 왕형은 편히 놀으시고 사냥이나 다니시면 그뿐이겠읍지만 제 왕제 노릇은 그러치 못할가 하는데요」
양녕은 잠시 뚫어저라 하고 조카의 얼굴을 바라보았다.
「야. 난들 네 마음을 웨 모르겠느냐. 다 안다. 알지만 헐 수 없지 않으냐. 네 팔자 고약해서 그런걸 어저겠냐」 ＜84＞
(85면 원본 낙장 - 국편)
