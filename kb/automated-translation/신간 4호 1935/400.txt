Song to Greet the Night
						Sin Sŏkchŏng

When bidding farewell to twilight (黃昏)
And welcoming the night

Those deep forests (森林) held a solemn concert (音樂會) by means of the little mountain birds (山ㅅ새) to bid farewell (餞別) to the twilight. That must be why my little pigeons, who were crossing over the woods, haven't returned yet—
they must still be watching the concert.

When bidding farewell to twilight
And welcoming the night

The old mountain, after long meditation (冥想), has quietly closed its eyes with sleep that comes visiting, one hand supporting its chin. Therefore, don't those clouds, which so love a leisurely journey through the blue sky, now seek the homeland (故國) they had forgotten?

When bidding farewell to twilight
And welcoming the night <24>

The quiet lake (湖水) used the white water birds as a handkerchief for farewell (餞別), and with mist as soft as white silk, it has wiped clean the path for the night to come.
Soon, from the surface of that lake, won't the sound be heard of the brilliant "night's" evening gown (夜會服), dragged by its footsteps, brushing against the mist?

When bidding farewell to twilight
And welcoming the night

At my bedroom (寢室) window, the night breeze that occasionally crosses the Yellow Sea (黃海) whispers chilly "sea stories," and the distant sky watches over the quiet stars' blue dreams... Now the forest (森林) must be watching over the soft breath (숨ㅅ결) of those precious mountain birds.

When bidding farewell to twilight
And welcoming the night

It is time, sitting in my bedroom full of the scent of seaweed (미억), having lit a white candle (燭ㅅ불), to begin the solemn meditation (冥想) of contemplating (思索) my life (人生).
O Night—until this quiet daily task (靜安한 나의 日課) of mine is finished, will you forever watch over my soft breath? -(From the poetry collection 『Sanhollim ŭi Kongjak (珊瑚林의 孔雀)』)- <25>
<24-25>

**Summary**

This poem by Sin Sŏkchŏng describes the transition from twilight to night through vivid personification of nature. The forest holds a concert, the mountain meditates and sleeps, the lake prepares the way with mist, and the night arrives like a figure in an evening gown. The final stanzas shift to the speaker's personal space—a bedroom filled with the scent of the sea—where they intend to engage in quiet contemplation by candlelight, asking the night to gently watch over them. The poem showcases a lyrical and introspective style, focusing on the beauty and tranquility of nature as a backdrop for personal reflection. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, avoids overt political themes, instead finding refuge in nature and inner thoughts. Historians might find interest in this aesthetic focus as a form of subtle resistance or escapism, the use of older Korean orthography and vocabulary, and the reference to the poet's collection *Sanhollim ŭi Kongjak*, placing it within the literary currents of the 1930s.

밤을 맞이하는 노래
						辛夕汀
黃昏을 餞別하고
밤을 迎接할 때

저-깊은 森林들은 작은 山ㅅ새들로 하여곰 黃昏을 餞別하기 위하야 거륵한 音樂會를 열엇다. 그러길래 숲을 넘어가든 나의 어린 비들기들이 돌아오지 안는 것은
아직도 音樂會의 구경이 끝나지 않은게지요.

黃昏을 餞別하고
밤을 迎接할 때

늙은 山은 오랜 冥想끝에 찾어오는 잠으로 하여곰 한 손으로 턱을 고인채 고요히 눈을 감엇슴니다. 그럼으로 푸른 하울의 한가한 旅行을 무척 좋와하는 저 구름들도 시방 잊엇든 故國을 찾어가지 않즙니까?

黃昏을 餞別하고
밤을 迎接할 때 ＜24＞

고요한 湖水는 힌 물ㅅ새들로 하여곰 餞別의 손수건을 삼엇스며 힌 명쥬처럼 부드러운 안개로 하여곰 밤이 오는 길을 닥거 놓왓습니다.
이윽고 저 湖面에서는 발ㅅ길에 끌고 오는 찬란한 「밤」의 夜會服이 안개에 스치는 소리가 들려오지 앉겟습니까?

黃昏을 餞別하고
밤을 迎接할 때

내 寢室의 창문에는 이따금 黃海를 건너온 밤바람이 속새이는 「바다의 이야기」가 치웁고, 머-ㄴ 한울은 조용한 별들의 푸른 꿈을 지키고... 지곰 森林은 고 갸륵한 山ㅅ새들의 보드러운 숨ㅅ결을 지키겟습니다.

黃昏을 餞別하고
밤을 迎接할 때

미억 내음새 가득한 침실에 힌 燭ㅅ불을 켜고 앉어 내 人生을 思索하는 거륵한 冥想을 비롯할 때입니다.
밤이여-이 靜安한 나의 日課가 끝날대까지 당신은 언제까지나 보드러운 내 숨ㅅ결을 지켜주겟습니까?. -(詩集 『珊瑚林의 孔雀』에서)- ＜25＞
＜24-25＞
