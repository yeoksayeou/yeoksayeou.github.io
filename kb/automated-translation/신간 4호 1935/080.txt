**Body Text Translation**

Female Journalist Portraits

Setting aside newspapers from olden times, even among the three private newspapers established anew after the March First Movement (己未), their age has already reached 14 or 15 years. Thus, the number of journalists who have come and gone within them must surely number in the several hundreds. However, as this society is organized with men as the standard, newspaper companies are also male-centric. Consequently, almost all journalists are men. Even if there are female journalists, they are truly like "a single red dot in a lush green bush" (萬綠叢中一點紅); a company typically employs no more than one as a "flower arrangement journalist" (花草記者). Furthermore, due to environmental factors, there are few female journalists themselves who have worked continuously for more than a year, and accordingly, there are also few whose activities have yielded notable results. However, since this issue is a newspaper special feature, just as newspaper companies hire female journalists for show, we will also write about the portraits of female journalists here for show. But the material is too scarce, so it is doubtful whether it will make much of a show.

Hŏ Chŏng-suk (許貞琡)

She is the first-generation female journalist of the Tonga Ilbo Company (東亞日報社). Although Kaebyŏk Company (開闢社) was the first among general newspapers and magazines after the March First Movement to initially hire a female journalist (the first female journalist being Kim Kyŏng-suk, 金慶淑), this Ms. Hŏ of the Tonga Ilbo was the first female journalist for a newspaper. As everyone knows well, she is the beloved daughter (愛孃) of Mr. Hŏ Hŏn (許憲). At that time, Mr. Hŏ Hŏn served as the principal of Posŏng College (普專校長) and also held an executive position at the Tonga Ilbo Company, thus having deep ties with the company. Because of this, Ms. Hŏ Chŏng-suk's husband at the time, Mr. Im Wŏn-gŭn (林元根), joined the company, and following him, Ms. Hŏ Chŏng-suk also joined. Back then, women finding employment was quite rare, and among those, newspaper journalists received treatment like uncrowned kings. Seeing that young couple, dubbed the "Mandarin Duck Journalists" (鴛鴦記者), walking shoulder-to-shoulder and matching steps through the alleys of Hwa-dong (花洞), not only people on the street but also colleagues within the same company greatly admired (欽羨) them and praised their happiness. How much she contributed through the newspaper pages is not well remembered now, but in terms of her knowledge and skill, she certainly did not yield the top spot (一頭を讓ち) to male journalists. This can be sufficiently inferred from her competence demonstrated later when she transferred to Kaebyŏk Company and took charge of editing Sin Yŏsŏng (新女性), writing articles, editing, and even proofreading the printing herself to produce a respectable magazine. Furthermore, seeing how she published a "Bobbed Hair Issue" (斷髮號) in Sin Yŏsŏng, advocating for bobbed hair among women in general while simultaneously taking the lead herself, bravely cutting her hair along with Chu Se-juk (朱世竹) and certain other <70> women of the Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe (女性同友會 - Women's Comrade Association), shows that her principles (主義) and execution ability were considerably strong. However, she was perhaps better described as a female fighter (女流 鬪士) than a female journalist. Thus, even when at Tonga or Kaebyŏk, she was more faithful to the Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe and Kŭnuhoe (槿友會 - Rose of Sharon Friendship Association; *assuming 槿友會 for 槿劏�會*) than to her duties as a journalist. She spent less of her salary on her private life and used much of it for comrades or group activities. Afterwards, having become the subject of numerous rumors and experiencing many hardships, she left the path of journalism and the women's movement, opening a sunlight therapy clinic—something neither she nor others had ever dreamed of—and currently lives a sweet, no, a warm life under the sunlight with Mr. Song Pong-u (宋奉瑀). Rumor has it that her father Mr. Hŏ Hŏn's gold mining business is very promising. If he becomes a nouveau riche (成金家) like Mr. Pang Ŭng-mo (方應模) and manages to establish the long-cherished grand newspaper for the people of Hamgyŏng Province (咸慶道), perhaps Ms. Chŏng-suk will re-enter the newspaper world and become an old female journalist.

Hŏ Yŏng-suk (許英肅)

Seeing her name, people who don't know might easily assume she has a sisterly relationship with Ms. Hŏ Chŏng-suk. However, in reality, they were unrelated Hŏs (不知許人之許) to each other (though they surely knew each other). Following Ms. Hŏ Chŏng-suk, she entered the Tonga Ilbo Company with her husband Mr. Yi Kwang-su (李光洙) as the second "Mandarin Duck Journalists." Although she too would not lag behind other female journalists in terms of ability or activity, because Ch'unwŏn's [Yi Kwang-su's pen name] status was established and his reputation was already high, the editorial department did not assign her tasks freely, and she herself did not engage in such activities actively. Therefore, as she told a certain female journalist, she couldn't perform the role of a female journalist properly. Moreover, even when she occasionally wrote good articles and fine prose, many colleagues within the company suspected that she hadn't written them herself, but that Ch'unwŏn had ghostwritten (借作) them for her. However, that is entirely mistaken. She is someone who writes well when she puts her mind to it. It's unknown if it's true, but the story that during her time as a journalist, she went to P'yŏngyang (平壤), called for famous kisaeng, and enjoyed a boat ride (船遊) on the Taedong River (大洞江) was considered a very bold amusement. Also, when Ch'unwŏn was frequently absent due to illness, President Song (宋社長), half-jokingly, said, "Hey, Ms. Hŏ Yŏng-suk— Ch'unwŏn is sick again today. We really need to separate you two for the company's work to go well..." This remark remains a topic of conversation among the staff to this day. However, she too resigned before long. <71>

Yi Hyŏn-gyŏng (李賢卿)

With her elegant height, sparkling platinum teeth (白金齒), and possessing the characteristic shrewdness and quick grasp of Suwon (水原) people famous for understanding things well, she was adept at making a good impression on interview subjects while handling assignments swiftly. However, being the lover of Mr. An Kwang-ch'ŏn (安光泉) and an executive of the Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe, she treated journalism as a side job (副業) and social activism as her main occupation (本業). Therefore, she didn't produce results befitting a journalist, just passing the days, until she escaped across the border following Mr. An. During her time as a journalist, there aren't any particular stories about her, but incidents from her student days in Tokyo (東京)—like causing a commotion with an attempted suicide due to a passionate same-sex love affair with the late female doctor Yi Tŏk-yo (李德耀), and the time on the train to Suwon when she saw a man in strange clothes (異服) behaving insultingly towards a country woman, became enraged (大憤慨), bravely rushed over, and slapped his cheek—are still talked about among her female comrades.

* Ŭi-sun (*義順) (*First Hanja missing/illegible*)

This person originally worked at Kaebyŏk Company before moving to Tonga. She is famous for her curly hair, and her physique is petite and graceful. She is sociable and has a pleasant speaking voice, making a good impression on those she interacts with. The female doctor Yi Tŏk-yo, known for her same-sex attractions, was said to be so captivated by her voice over the phone that, despite not being particularly close, she would call her more than ten times a day just wanting to hear her voice, and when visited, would deliberately keep her for hours—an anecdote tells. When she was married to Mr. Chin Kŭm-sŏng (秦金星), because they were both small and gentle, they earned the nickname "chick couple" (병아리 부부) among their peers. Her pen name, Paegwŏl (白月 - White Moon), is said to have been chosen to correspond with Mr. Chin's pen name, Kŭmsŏng (金星 - Venus/Gold Star). She wrote children's stories, novels, and sometimes poetry, so some magazines occasionally promoted her as a female writer (女流 文人). However, she was a translation journalist (飜譯記者), not a creative one (創作記者); a desk journalist (卓上記者), not a field reporter (다리기자). Nevertheless, being naturally multi-talented, she never performed the role of a female journalist in a way that would bring shame compared to others. Should we call it "beauty meets misfortune" (佳人薄命)? Or perhaps, as her pen name Paegwŏl suggests, "one fullness, one decline" (一滿一戱)? Her enjoyable life with Mr. Chin experienced a breakdown (破綻), and her health was also constantly poor (不健), so she could not continue her life as a journalist either. Her recent life circumstances are? Curious. <72>

Kim T'an-sil (金彈實)

Ms. Kim Myŏng-sun (金明淳), pen name T'an-sil, is more famous as a female poet (女流 詩人) than a female journalist. Being a poet, she was a temperamental woman, causing considerable headaches for people in the society section when she was at Maeil Sinbo (每申). If her article was edited, she would immediately tear up the manuscript, cry, and sometimes even threaten to throw herself into the Han River (漢江) in a commotion. Among the most frightening stories is one where an office boy brought her coffee, and she flew into a rage (怒氣), dismissed him, and declared, "What an ignorant fool! Don't you know it's harmful to drink coffee during menstruation (月中 行事)?" Another involved her tearing pages (뒤지) out of books while reading at the library and receiving a sharp scolding (꾸지람) from the librarian. Other stories are omitted (略).

Ch'oe Ŭn-hŭi (崔恩喜)

She was the first female journalist at the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) and, among all past and present female journalists across various companies, the one who was most active and achieved the most results. What actions she took during her life as a journalist are detailed in her memoir (回想記), which could be called her autobiography (自敍傳), published in this very issue, so there is no need to repeat them here. Her writing talent (才筆), active capability (活腕), and strong legs (健脚) are something that even capable male journalists could hardly aspire to (仰望). As she had many connections with the Ch'unwŏn couple [Yi Kwang-su and Hŏ Yŏng-suk], her handwriting (글시) also resembled Ch'unwŏn's style, and just as Ch'unwŏn's wife used the pen name Ch'ungye (春溪 - Spring Stream), she chose the pen name Ch'ugye (秋溪 - Autumn Stream). Although an autumn stream is chilly and clear, she, contrary to her pen name, does not try to live only such a chilly and clear life. She aims to become an autumn stream where many fallen leaves gather, and goldfish (金붕어) dance and play in deep pools. This can be roughly guessed from the fact that she has many friends of both sexes, has a beloved daughter and son (愛女愛子) despite living as a single woman, does not live in great hardship despite lacking fundamental financial resources (資力), and even engages in stamp dealing, something others cannot do. Other remarks are omitted (略), and finally, we wish her well for her future.

Yun Sŏng-sang (尹聖相)

This person served as a journalist for both the Chosŏn (朝鮮) and Chungang (中央) newspapers. Her name sounds more dignified than a man's name, and especially since the pronunciation is Sŭngsang (like 'Your Excellency' 丞相), she earned the nickname "Your Excellency Sŭngsang" (승상폐하) among colleagues. Being very dignified and taciturn, even the troublesome, mischievous, and flighty young male journalists reportedly couldn't easily joke around with her. When she was at Chungang, the troublemaker Sim Hun (心薰) supposedly sat facing Ms. Yun, deliberately called the office boy saying, "Hey, bring the newspapers! Have the Hōchi (報知) and Yorozu Chōhō (萬朝報) arrived?" seeking out newspapers with strange sounds [referencing Japanese newspaper names to potentially tease or provoke], making even the men nearby blush (얼골이 붉어젓지만은), but Ms. Yun pretended not to hear (聽而不聞), maintained a poker face (시침을 딱떼고), and when entering the printing plant (工場), even when the workers <73> customarily used all sorts of vulgar 'talk' (『변』 말) to tease female journalists, she likewise pretended not to know. Furthermore, she had considerable opinions (主見) of her own; if there was some disagreement with An Sŏg-yŏng (安夕影), she would invariably say in her Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡道) dialect, "Teacher (先生), why are you like that? Let's talk," and drag him to the reception room for a debate (論難). Her personality is like that, and her academic background and writing skills are all considerable. Among these, her physique is excellent; when she walked down the street, everyone would glance at her at least once or follow her. Some mischievous young men, wanting to admire the beauty of her backline (背線美) as she walked, would deliberately stand at the Kyŏnji-dong (堅志洞) intersection around her quitting time just to follow her. When she lived in the back alleys of Che-dong (齋洞) near the P'a T'ang bathhouse (巴湯), there were times she would go to the bathhouse or the Taedong Sanghoe (大東商會) wearing only her 'nemaki' (네마기 - nightgown/robe), and it's said that some people deliberately watched the Che-dong back alleys just to see this sight. The incident where she received a sudden marriage proposal from some sex maniac (色情狂) at the Tongan streetcar stop, caused a stir by going to the East Police Station (東署), and suddenly gained the reputation of a noble lady (貴婦人) in the gossip columns (꼬십) of the newspaper she worked for, was also thanks to (恩澤) that fine physique. However, she worked as a journalist for a living, definitely not because she enjoyed it. Therefore, as soon as her husband, Mr. Chŏn (全군), received his parents' inheritance, she quit the company, built a modern 'culture house' (文化住宅) in Sajik-dong (社稷洞), and lives a different life than before. It's said that if anyone asks her to work as a female journalist again, she dislikes the idea (실타) and vehemently shakes her head.

Hwang Sin-dŏk (黃信德)

She is a journalist considerably senior to Ms. Yun, having worked continuously at the Sidae Ilbo (時代日報) and the Chungoe Ilbo (中外日報). Being a university graduate and a feminist theorist (女流主義者), her knowledge and theoretical grounding were superior in many aspects compared to other female journalists, or even ordinary male journalists. However, while she could be highly regarded as a journalist who analyzed theories and did translations at her desk (卓上), she couldn't dig up actual live stories (活記事) or uncover hidden news like Ms. Ch'oe Ŭn-hŭi. As she herself said, she was a journalist who reluctantly held the job for 'bread' (빵), not one who pursued it with interest (興味). During her time as a journalist, she didn't leave behind any memorable accomplishments, either for herself or for others. Among things to mention, perhaps the most significant memorable event is that, due to her work as a journalist, she formed a happy union (佳緣) with Mr. Im Pong-sun (任鳳淳), who was younger than her. At the time, she told her close younger female relative (親知孃), "Hey—I've encountered a big problem working at the newspaper company. It's all despair..." raising curiosity about what she meant. Later, it was discovered <74> that the reason was that, as an older unmarried woman (老處女), she had conceived Mr. Im Pong-sun's beloved child (愛兒). It might have been despair then, but now it seems like optimism. Being mature (老熟), she loves and cherishes (얼우만지다) Mr. Im as a husband, sometimes seemingly like a younger brother, continuing an enjoyable life with children born (有子生女). Recently, rumors say their household finances have improved (살림도 바시락 소리가 나서), and they even bought considerable farmland before the New Year (歲前). How could Ms. Hwang (黃) not have gold (黃金)?!

Kim Mal-bong (金末峰)

She was also a journalist at the Chungoe Ilbo. While other female journalists generally paid attention to their appearance, her characteristic was to go around with disheveled hair (머리가 흐덥은) and shabby clothes (의복도 허름하게), looking like a woman gathering wild greens in the mountains or a servant living in someone else's outer quarters (行廊사리). When she was having a hard time because she couldn't get her salary properly from the Chungoe Ilbo, seeing her carrying small bundles of firewood (푼거리 나무) and bags of rice (봉지 쌀) barefoot through the Kan-dong (諫洞 - *assuming this is the intended location*) alley where she lived, no one would likely have thought she was a Chungoe female journalist. There are no other particular stories about her, but the fact that she announced publicly she was going to study abroad in Europe or America (歐米留學), resigned, returned to her hometown, married a Mr. Yi (李某씨), and later only sent marriage announcements to her acquaintances became a topic of gossip (이약이 거리) for a while. Currently, she is said to be in business with her husband in Pusan (釜山).

Kim Wŏn-ju (金源珠)

She also started at Kaebyŏk Company and moved to Maeil Sinbo (每申). Because she has a pleasant appearance (복스럽게 생기다) and is taciturn, she gives a very favorable impression to others. She apparently wrote visitor's articles (訪問記事) quite well and also wrote essays (隨筆) considerably, so whenever various newspapers and magazines wrote about female writers (女流 文人), she was routinely included, often appearing actively in photographs. After leaving Maeil Sinbo, there were many rumors for a while that she was going to Paris (巴里) or Shanghai (上海) with her husband. Instead, she went to Tokyo, gave birth to a precious son (玉童子), returned, and is living a prosperous and enjoyable life in Kye-dong (桂洞). Some woman was heard saying that she is the only one among female journalists who achieved personal success. Whether it's truly success is unknown, but she is still living well, and a certain extra edition reporter (號外記者) relayed that she has already 'edited' her second child (第2子) and will 'publish' it soon.

Besides these, there are Pak Sŭng-ho (朴承浩) of Tonga, No Ch'ŏn-myŏng (盧天命) of Chungang, and Kim O-nam (金午男) of the former Chosŏn, but their tenures were all short, leaving no material to write about, so we postpone this to a later opportunity and lay down the pen (擱筆). <75>

**Summary**

This 1935 article from *Kaebyŏk* magazine profiles several early female journalists in colonial Korea, primarily associated with major newspapers like Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, and Chungang Ilbo. The author provides brief biographical sketches, often focusing on their appearances, personalities, personal lives (marriages, relationships, children), connections to prominent men (fathers, husbands), and perceived successes or failures, rather than solely their professional achievements. While acknowledging some talent and activity, the overall tone reflects the patriarchal attitudes of the time, expressing skepticism about their commitment and impact as journalists, sometimes framing their work as secondary to social activism or personal matters.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the nascent stage of female participation in the Korean press during the colonial period. It highlights the significant social hurdles women faced in professional fields, the tendency to view them through the lens of their male connections or personal lives, and the societal fascination mixed with judgment surrounding their unconventional roles. The mention of specific organizations like Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe and Kŭnuhoe, activities like advocating for bobbed hair, and the journalists' involvement (or perceived involvement) in social movements offer glimpses into women's activism and changing social norms under Japanese rule. The gossipy tone and focus on personal details, while biased, inadvertently preserve social history and contemporary perceptions of these pioneering women.

이는 東亞日報社의 제1세 여기자이다. 己未후 일반 신문 잡지계를 통처서 여기자를 처음으로 채용하기는 開闢社엿지만은 (최초 여기자 金慶淑) 신문으로서의 여기자는 東亞日報의 이 許씨가 처음이다. 이는 누구나 다 잘 아는 바와 같이 許憲씨의 愛孃으로 당시 許憲씨가 普專校長으로 또 東亞社의 중역을 겸한이 만치 그 社와 관계가 깊엇던 까닭에 당시 許貞琡씨의 부군 林元根군이 입사하게 되고 뒤를 따러 許貞琡씨가 또한 입사하게 되였다. 그때만 하여도 여자의 취직이 퍽 듬을던 때 그 중에도 신문기자라면 무관 제왕의 대우를 바들 그때에 그들 젊은 부부가 鴛鴦記者란 雅號를 들으며 억개를 가지런이 하고 발을 맛추어 花洞 골목으로 드나드는 것을 보면 가두의 사람들은 물론이고 같은 社의 사람들도 퍽이나 欽羨하고 그들의 행복을 찬미하였다. 그가 지면으로 얼마마한 활동을 하엿던지 그것은 지금에 잘 기억이 나지 안치만은 그의 지식으로나 수완으로나 결코 남성기자에 一頭를 讓치 안이하엿다. 그것은 그 뒤에 그가 開闢社로 전근하야 신여성 편집책임을 맡어 볼 때에 자기의 손으로 논문을 쓰고 편집을 하고 인쇄 교정까지 하야서 잡지를 상당하게 내 노흔 것으로 보와 그 실력을 족히 짐작하겟고 新女性에 斷髮號를 내여 일반 여성의 단발을 주창하는 동시 자기가 솔선하야 女性同友會의 朱世竹 外 某某＜70＞ 여성과 같이 용감하게 단발한 것을 보면 그의 主義 또는 실행력이 상당히 강한 것을 또한 알 수 있다. 그러나 그는 여기자라는 이보다 여류 투사란 것이 올켓다. 그리하야 東亞나 開闢에 있을 때에도 그 기자의 직무보다도 女性同友會와 槿劏�會에 더 충실하였다. 월급은 자기 사생활에 쓰는 것이 적고 동지 또는 단체활동에 만히 充用하얏다. 그 뒤 허다한 풍문의 주인공이 되고 만흔 고초를 맛본 그는 기자생활 또는 여성 운동의 선을 떠나서 자기로나 또는 남으로나 꿈도 꾸지 못하던 태양광선 치료소를 내고 현재에는 宋奉瑀군과 같이 달큼한 생활 안이 태양광선하에서 땃뜻한 생활을 한다. 풍문에 드르면 그의 父君 許憲씨의 금광업이 매우 유망하다니 그가 方應模씨와 같은 成金家되여 宿年의 포부인 咸慶道人의 大新聞을 경영하게 된다면 貞琡씨는 다시 신문계에 出脚하야 老여기자가 될는지 알 수 없다.

이는 朝鮮日報 최초 여기자로 各社 역대 여기자중 제일 활동을 만히 하고 제일 성적을 만히 내인 분이다. 그가 기자 생활중 어떠한 행동을 하였던 것은 그 자서전이라고도 할만한 그의 회상기가 본지 이번 호에 발표 되였은즉 여긔에 새삼스럽게 말치 안커니와 그의 才筆과 活腕, 健脚은 여간한 남기자로는 仰望도 못할 것이다. 그는 春園 부부와 연고가 많엇더니 만치 글시도 春園 필법이 많고 春園 부인이 春溪의 號를 가진 對로 秋溪라 號를 지엿다. 가을 개울은 쌀쌀하고도 깨끗하지만은 이는 號와는 딴판으로 그러케 쌀쌀하고 깨끗한 생활만은 하랴고 하지 안는다. 秋溪 중에도 낙엽이 만히 모이고 금붕어가 금실금실 춤을 추고 노는 웅덩이 진 秋溪가 되랴고 한다. 그가 남녀간 친구가 만코 독신생활을 하면서도 愛女愛子가 잇고 근본 資力이 없으면서도 그리 곤란한 생활을 하지 안코 남이 못하는 우표장사까지 하는 것을 보와도 대강 짐작할 수 잇다. 다른 말은 略하고 끝으로 그의 장래를 위하야 축복한다.

尹聖相
이는 朝鮮, 中央 兩社의 기자를 역임한 분이다. 이름이 남성의 이름보다 더 점잔케 그 중에도 音이 승상이여서 사원간에 『승상폐하」라는 별명을 드른 그는 퍽 점잔코도 침묵한 까닭에 말성만코 찟코 까불기 잘하는 젊은 남기자들도 그에 대하야는 농담 한 번을 잘 못하였다 한다. 그가 中央에 있을 때에 말성쟁이 心薰군이 尹씨와 마주 안저서 일부러 급사를 부르며 얘 신문 가저 오너라 報知, 萬朝報 왓니 하고 음이 괴상스러운 신문을 차저서 압헤 잇는 남자들도 얼골이 붉어젓지만은 尹씨는 聽而不聞한 척하고 시침을 딱떼고 잇섯고 공장에를 드러가면 직공들이＜73＞ 의례 여기자를 놀리너라 가진 흉악한 『변』 말을 써도 역시 모른 척하고 또 자기의 主見이 상당이 있어서 安夕影과 무슨 불합한 일이 있으면 咸鏡道 사투리로 의례 『선생(先生) 엇지 그러우 좀 봅세』하고 응접실로 끌고 가서 논난을 한다. 그는 성격도 그러하거니와 학력, 필력이 다 상당하다. 그 중에 체격이 조와 거리에 나서면 누구나 한 번식 치여다 보고 또는 뒤를 다른다. 어떤 짓구진 청년은 그의 거러 가는 背線美를 구경하기 위하야 그가 퇴사할 시간이 되면 일부러 堅志洞 네거리에 가서 섯다가 뒤를 따르기도 하고 그가 齋洞 巴湯 뒷골목에 살 때에는 『네마기』만 입고 湯이나 大東商會에 다니는 일이 잇섯기 때문에 그 광경을 보기 위하야 일부러 齋洞 뒷골목을 직히고 있던 사람도 있엇다고 한다. 그가 통안 전차정류장에서 어떤 색정광에게 도중 결혼 청구를 밧고 東署에 가서 문제를 이르키고 자기 다니던 신문 꼬십에 一躍 귀부인 소리를 듯게 된 것도 그 조흔 그 체격의 恩澤이였다. 그러나 그는 생활을 위하야 기자 노릇을 하였지 결코 취미에 마자서 한 것은 안이엿기 때문에 그 남편 全군이 부모의 유산을 바드며부터 社를 그만두고 나아가서 社稷洞에 문화주택을 짓고 전과 다른 생활을 하며 누가 여기자 노릇을 다시 하라면 실타고 머리를 홰홰 내두른다고 한다.

黃信德
이는 尹씨보다 훨신 선진의 기자로 時代日報 中外日報를 계속 근무하였다. 그는 대학 출신이요 여류주의자이엿더니 만치 학식과 이론이 다른 여기자 안이 보통의 남기자보다 우월한 점이 만타. 그러나 그는 탁상에서 이론을 캐고 번역을 하는 기자로는 한 목을 크게 보겟지만은 崔恩喜씨 모양으로 실제 活記事를 끄집어내고 숨은 기사를 들추어내지는 못하얏다. 자기의 말과 같이 『빵』을 위하야 직업을 억지로 다닌 기자요 흥미를 부처 다닌 기자는 안이다. 그는 기자 생활을 하던 중에는 자기로나 남으로나 무슨 기억될 만한 일을 남겨 노지 못하얏다. 그 중에 말하자면 기자 생활을 하던 관계로 자기보다 年少의 任鳳淳군과 佳緣을 맷게된 것이 그 중 큰 기억되는 일일 것이다. 당시에 그는 자기의 親知孃을 보고 『얘- 나는 신문사 다니다가 큰 일을 만낫다 비관뿐이다. ...』라고 하야 그것이 무슨 소리인가 하얏더니 야종에 알고 보니＜74＞ 노처녀로서 任鳳淳군의 愛兒를 배엿던 까닭이였다. 그때는 비관이엿는지 모르나 지금은 낙관일 듯 그는 노숙하니 만치 任군을 남편 엇지 보면 동생같이 사랑하고 얼우만지고 하야 有子生女에 자미잇는 생활을 계속하고 근래에는 살림도 바시락 소리가 나서 歲前에는 상당한 농토까지 삿다는 풍문이 잇다. 黃씨로 엇지 황금이 없을 수 잇나!.

金末峰
이도 中外日報의 기자엿다. 다른 여기자들은 대개 모양을 보지만은 그는 산골에서 나물캐는 여자나 남의 행낭사리하는 여자 모양으로 머리가 흐덥은 하고 의복도 허름하게 차리고 다닌 것이 그 특색이다. 中外日報에서 월급을 잘 타지 못하야 곤란한 생활을 할 때에 푼거리 나무와 봉지 쌀을 사 가지고 맨발로 자기 잇던 諫洞 골목으로 가는 것을 보면 누구나 中外의 여기자로는 생각지 못하얏슬 것이다. 그는 다른 이약이 거리는 없지만은 歐米留學을 한다고 일반에게 선전하고 퇴사 귀향한 후 李某씨와 결혼하고 추후 그 친지에게 결혼 통고만을 한 것이 한때 이약이 거리가 되얏다. 지금은 부산에서 부군과 같이 상업을 한다고.

金源珠
이도 開闢社에 출신하야 每申으로 간 분이다. 사람이 복스럽게 생기고 침묵한 까닭에 남에게 호감을 만히 준다. 방문기사도 상당히 쓰려니와 수필 같은 것도 상당히 썻기 때문에 각 신문 잡지에서 여류 문인 이약이를 쓰게 되면 의례로 그도 한 목을 주고 사진으로 각금 활동을 한다. 그는 每申을 나온 후 부군과 같이 巴里를 가너니 上海를 가너니 하고 한참 소문이 만터니 東京에 가서 옥동자를 나 가지고 도라와 桂洞에서 풍족하고 자미잇는 생활을 하고 잇다. 여기자로서 개인 성공한 사람은 그 한 사람이라고 어떤 여성은 말하는 것을 드럿다. 그가 과연 성공일지 모르나 아즉까지 잘 살고 벌서 第2子를 편집하야 미구에 발행한다고 某 號外記者는 전언.
