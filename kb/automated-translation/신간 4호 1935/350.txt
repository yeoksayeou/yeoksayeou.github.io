**Summary**

This article presents a collection of four short, humorous or cynical anecdotes reflecting aspects of urban life and social commentary in 1930s Korea. The stories touch on the clever exploitation of installment plan terms, a dark joke involving suicide and borrowing money for travel to Manchuria, a cynical observation about the unchanging nature of a fashionable woman's husband, and the irony of achieving a desired marriage leading to sleeplessness due to worry. From a historical perspective, the piece is interesting for its portrayal of modern consumerism (pianos, installment plans), contemporary social interactions and humor, and references to specific locations like Chongno and Manchuria, the latter possibly hinting at migration or desperation during the colonial period. The mix of everyday concerns and darker humor provides a glimpse into the mindset and social fabric of the time.
