Secret Stories of New Weapons

One indication can be seen in the land, sea, and air force exercises conducted in various European countries last year. Among the large and small nations of Europe, there was almost no country possessing what could be called an army that did not conduct exercises last year. Furthermore, observing these exercises, it seems as if all countries conspired together to significantly enlarge the forces of the [obscured] army or attacking forces, while the defending forces were made [obscured]. This is for no other reason than [obscured] to stimulate the [obscured] public's interest in national defense and spur on the fulfillment of military preparations.

Looking at the approach of various countries' exercises conducted last year, one can generally imagine what aspects of training each country is focusing on, and consequently, what form a future war might take if it were to break out.

Be that as it may, let's introduce two or three new weapons that have recently become hot topics in European military circles and are presumed to play a great role in future warfare.

First is the "Silent Airplane" (無音飛行機). A silent airplane, suitable for nighttime air raids, could appear over an enemy [obscured] city without sound and drop bombs. From the perspective of those being attacked, this is truly terrifying. Experts from the British Air Ministry have reportedly already witnessed a demonstration of such a silent airplane, stating that no sound could be heard at all from a distance of 2,500 feet.

Next is the "Flying Tank" (飛行탕크). That is, an airplane carrying field artillery with a large caliber and high rate of fire. Some might say, "Firing field artillery from an airplane is absurd," but it is said that in fact, a 75mm rapid-fire cannon has been accurately fired sufficiently <80> from a certain French airplane.

Third is the "Construction of High Towers" (高塔建設) for airplane landings. Although this has not yet been realized in physical form, French engineers assert its possibility. What exactly is this high tower? According to French engineers, it would be 6,000 feet high (about three times the famous Eiffel Tower). Platforms would be built at various heights on the tower, allowing fighter planes to land there. And during wartime, fighter planes would be housed on these platforms, ready to launch immediately towards the enemy [obscured] upon orders. With conventional ground hangars, even if an enemy suddenly appeared, there was the inconvenience of first having to ascend to a certain altitude to begin combat. However, if such [obscured] towers are realized, it is said this inconvenience could be [obscured] eliminated.

**Summary**

The article discusses rising military tensions in Europe, evidenced by large-scale military exercises emphasizing offensive capabilities. It then introduces speculative "new weapons": silent airplanes for stealth bombing, "flying tanks" mounting large cannons, and colossal towers for rapid aircraft deployment. Shifting focus, it praises the American "Zephyr" streamlined train for its record-breaking speed (Denver to Chicago) and remarkable fuel efficiency, presenting it as a symbol of railway resurgence against cars and planes. The article reflects a contemporary fascination with military and transportation technology during a period of global uncertainty. For historians, it showcases the awareness of international technological advancements (both military and civilian) within colonial Korea in 1935, juxtaposing European war anxieties with American progress, while implicitly omitting the specific geopolitical context of Korea under Japanese rule.
