Article Title: 四月의 世相, 3월 21일부터 4월 20일까지 (The State of the World in April, from March 21st to April 20th)
Author: 一記者
Article Type: 소식 (News/Dispatch)

The State of the World in April, from March 21st to April 20th
						By One Reporter

March 21st
In Shanghai, the Chosŏn Youth Alliance (朝鮮靑年同盟會) was initiated (under the banner of the independence movement, aiming for national unity and sacrificial struggle for the completion of national independence). The Chinese government announced the details of the Sino-Soviet negotiations to various regions, asserting the absolute invalidity of Mr. Wang Tingzheng (王廷正氏)'s signing, while communicating the intent for renewed Sino-Soviet talks. The Soviet representative, Mr. Karakhan (카라한), reported to the Soviet government the intention for renewed Sino-Soviet talks based on China's wishes. The Irish army fired upon British troops landing at Queenstown (크언스타운), causing over ten casualties and resulting in political instability between Britain and Ireland. The U.S. House of Representatives passed the naval budget bill and simultaneously requested President Coolidge (쿨리지) to negotiate the reopening of the Arms Limitation Conference. The French Parliament passed the Army Reorganization Bill.

March 22nd
The striking Ochiai (落合) Rice Mill workers in Kunsan (群山), angered by the owner's stubbornness, formed a group of 40 and attacked the factory (they resumed work the next day through mutual understanding). Ideologically inclined women in Yongjŏng Village (龍井村), Kando (間島), organized the Bobbed Hair Alliance (斷髮同盟) (date unspecified). In Nagano (長野), Japan, 23 communist party members were arrested. In London (倫敦), England, over 40,000 tram drivers, conductors, and bus drivers initiated a general strike, paralyzing transportation. The Australian National Congress advocated opposition to Britain's cessation of the Singapore base. The Indian Muslim Conference resolved to establish an Islamic government in Turkey. The French Senate and Chamber of Deputies passed the financial reform bill.

March 23rd
In Kyŏngsŏng (京城), the Municipal Council (府協議員會) convened, primarily discussing the abolition of discrimination between north and south villages regarding electricity supply from the city. At the Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇場) in Kyŏngsŏng, a major altercation occurred between promoter Hwang Wŏn-gyun (黃元均) and building owner Yazawa (矢澤) over the refusal to open the theater. In Gunma Prefecture (群馬縣), Japan, the Kantō Suphyŏngsa (關東 水平社) Convention was held, resolving three major articles denouncing insulting treatment: 1. Thoroughly denounce any instance where insulting intent is expressed towards us using traditional names or new symbols. 1. Abolish all educational movements that disregard the spirit of the liberation movement. 1. Reject all insulting improvement measures and charitable facilities from the government and others, striving for their complete abolition. China's Mr. Gu Weijun (顧維均) obtained approval from his home government and proposed renewed Sino-Soviet negotiations to Mr. Karakhan. Greece declared the abolition of the monarchy effective March 25th. Russia appointed Mr. Rakovsky (리곱스키) as its representative to Britain.

March 24th
The 12 organizations (Youth Association, Farmers' Assembly, Proletariat Alliance, etc.) in Suncheon (順天) rose up in anger against police violation of human rights and held a Police Denunciation Rally (警察聲討大會). A fire broke out on Chŏlyŏng Island (絶影島), Pusan (釜山), destroying over 40 houses. The Citizens' Assembly in Fengtian (奉天) distributed anti-Japanese propaganda leaflets with six points: 1. Recovery of jurisdiction over Chinese residents in the Kwantung Leased Territory. 1. Recovery of the Antung-Mukden Railway (安奉鐵道). 1. Withdrawal of Japanese post offices from areas near the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵). 1. Withdrawal of Japanese police substations in Fengtian city. 1. Demand for cessation of Japanese mail transport between Changchun (長春) and Harbin (哈爾賓). 1. Abrogation of the Sino-Japanese joint management contract for the Jilin-Changchun Railway (吉長鐵道). The Association for Supporting the Recovery of Liaodong (遼東) in the Three Eastern Provinces decided on and distributed a six-point recovery plan: 1. Send telegrams throughout the country to promote the recovery. 1. Issue a warning to the Japanese people. 1. Dispatch a petition group to the State Council. 1. Organize lecture groups to promote the boycott of Japanese goods. 1. Dispatch representatives to each province to establish contact. 1. Cooperate with various organizations in Beijing (北京) to exert maximum effort in the recovery movement.

March 25th
A fierce battle occurred between Independence Army forces and police in Sinch'ŏn (新川), Wiwŏn County (渭原郡). In Kyŏngsŏng, the Federation of Various Faction Volunteers (各派有志聯盟會) emerged from the union of 11 organizations promoting Japanese-Korean harmony, including the National Association (國民協會) and the Sangaehoe (相愛會). The resident officer in Maegok (梅谷), Yŏngdŏng County (永同郡), unreasonably beat about 10 tenant farmers involved in a tenant rights dispute. The Japanese cabinet decided<44> to reinstate tariff reductions due to the earthquake disaster. The issue of compensation for the Changsha (長沙) incident resurfaced between Japan and China. The Soviet representative retorted to China that renewed negotiations were impossible unless formal diplomatic relations were restored first.

March 26th
A large fire broke out in Zōshigaya (雜司谷), Tokyo (東京), burning down over 600 houses. Student groups in Fuzhou (福州), China, held large-scale anti-Japanese demonstrations. The French cabinet resigned en masse. The British House of Commons rejected the amended naval base bill. Denmark recognized the Soviet Labour-Farmer government.

March 27th
The Calligraphy and Painting Association (書畵協會) held its 4th exhibition in Kyŏngsŏng. The Funabashi Public Common School (舟橋公立普通學校) in Kyŏngsŏng city burned down. A fire also broke out in the Shanghai Building (上海「삘딍」) on Nanjing Road (南京町), Shanghai, causing damages estimated at 60 million taels. France's Mr. Poincaré (포앙카레) accepted reappointment as Prime Minister. The U.S. Senate Immigration Committee approved the new immigration bill (completely deleting the Japanese immigration clause). In Muan (務安), a disturbance occurred where the family of a landlord named Mun Chae-ch'ŏl (文在喆) violently assaulted executives of the tenant farmers' association.

March 28th
Several hundred farmers fromŏmtae Island (嚴泰島), Muan County (務安郡), attacked the house of landlord Mun Chae-ch'ŏl. About 40 tenant farmers from Sŏch'ŏn Township (西川面), Taedong County (大同郡), angered by the landlord's unreasonable actions, rushed to the county office to petition. The Korean Provisional Government Assembly (朝鮮議政院) in Shanghai resolved to extend its session by one month due to circumstances. France reorganized its cabinet with Mr. Poincaré again as Prime Minister. In the U.S. state of Dakota (다꼬다), the vote for the next presidential election showed Mr. Coolidge with the most votes at over 50,300, followed by Mr. La Follette (플랫트) and Mr. Johnson (똔손), each with over 30,000 points, it is said.

March 29th
Ch'oe Sŏn-myŏng (崔善鳴), representative of Koreans in the Kansai (關西) region of Japan, headed to Tokyo intending to petition various ministers regarding the cold treatment by Japanese police reality. Katō Takaaki (加藤高明), president of the Kenseikai (H本憲政會), was hit by an air rifle shot by someone while giving a speech at the party's Gunma branch. The proposal submitted to the U.S. Congress for reopening the disarmament conference was rejected. The new French cabinet agreed to follow the foreign policy of the previous cabinet. Sun Yat-sen's (孫文) army and Chen Jiongming's (陳炯明) army began combat.

March 30th
Paek Se-ch'ae (白世彩), a strange figure who deceived the world and deluded the people, calling himself Master Cheon'gong (天空大師) and tricking many foolish people in the capital and countryside, was arrested by the Tongdaemun Police Station and transferred to the prosecutor's office. A reply denying the facts arrived at the Methodist Youth Association from American missionary Welch (웰치), the subject of the Korea defamation issue. Chinese parliamentarians and student groups held a joint meeting demanding the government remove the Soviet representative.

March 31st
Japan's Privy Council deliberated on the universal suffrage issue. Song Pyŏng-ch'ŏn (宋秉天) and Kang Kyu-jin (康圭鎭), involved in the Wŏnsan (元山) March First commemorative independence declaration distribution incident, were sentenced to one year. Over 7,000 British construction workers went on a general strike demanding a 2 pence per hour increase. The Chinese representative to Russia argued to his home country the disadvantage of unconditional recognition of Russia. The H刊新聞時代日報 (likely 日刊新聞時代日報, Daily News Shidai Nippo) was launched. The Pyŏllyang Farmers' Assembly (別良農民大會) in Suncheon County held a general meeting and passed resolutions on issues such as the Oriental Development Company (東拓) and tenant farming transfers (移作).

April 1st
China sent a conciliatory reply to Russia. Russia, seeking to check the Beijing government, dispatched a representative to shake hands with Sun Yat-sen. In Shizuoka (靜岡), Japan, an ideologue named Takashima Sanji (高島三次) was arrested, simultaneously uncovering a plot of high treason. The Korean Labor Soldiers' General Association (韓國勞兵總會) held a meeting in Shanghai. About a thousand members of the Samjin Labor Mutual Aid Society (三鎭勞働共濟會) in Ch'angwŏn County (昌原郡) grandly celebrated the first anniversary of the society's founding.

April 2nd
Kim To-sim (金道深), who had entered the country to raise funds for military academy purposes, received a 3-year sentence. The previously divided 30 head temples of Korean Buddhism (朝鮮佛敎) reconciled amicably; simultaneously, the management of Posŏng College (普成高等學校) was finalized (Tonggwang School (東光學校) merged into Posŏng). Members of the so-called Federation of Various Faction Volunteers, Kim Myŏng-jun (金明濬), Pak Ch'un-gŭm (朴春琴), Ch'ae Ki-du (蔡基斗), etc., assaulted Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹), Kim Sŏng-su (金性洙), etc., at Sikto-wŏn (食道園). Former Australian Prime Minister Hughes (휴쓰) strongly advocated the need for the U.S. and Australia to cooperate in excluding East Asians.

April 3rd
The April issue of Kaebyŏk (開벽) was confiscated again. A motion of no confidence against Mr. Gu Weijun was submitted to the Chinese parliament. The planned Romanian conference concerning Russia broke down due to discord<45> between the two countries.

April 4th
The lecture team of the Kyŏngsŏng Sin'hŭng Youth Association (京城新興靑年會), which had been touring the southwest lines, returned to the capital. The Japanese government rejected China's demand regarding damages to Chinese nationals during the earthquake disaster.

April 5th
Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) grandly held its 64th Ch'ŏnil Anniversary ceremony. The Chosŏn Youth General Alliance Preparatory Committee (朝鮮靑年總同盟準備會) dispatched investigators to the provinces to prepare for the alliance's founding. The Opium Conference hosted by the League of Nations concluded. The Soviet representative rejected China's request to continue negotiations. Wu Peifu (吳佩孚) declared his intention to subjugate Sun Yat-sen. Students Kim Kyŏng-bok (金景福), Kim Chae-gon (金在坤), and three others from Sungsil Middle School (崇實中學校) in P'yŏngyang (平壤) were arrested on suspicion of arson against the school. In the Gompal (곰플) region of India's Northwest Province, cotton spinning workers went on strike over wages, leading to a major battle between police and workers with many casualties. French Prime Minister Poincaré summoned the German ambassador for stern talks regarding the mining issue.

April 6th
In P'yŏngyang, a tragic incident occurred where a police car ran over and killed a Korean girl. The National Fascist Party (國粹黨) won the Italian general election. The Soviet representative announced the absolute non-issuance of entry visas to nationals of countries not recognizing Soviet Russia. The 2nd Temperance Convention was held in Okayama Prefecture (岡山縣), Japan, resolving 10 articles on the temperance issue. The International Communist Party in Vladivostok (海港) decided to hold the Far East Communist Party Congress in Shanghai in late May (representatives from Korea, Japan, and other countries to attend). Members of the 3rd Philippine Independence Petition Mission, including Messrs. Quezon (예술) and Osmeña (오스메나시), departed for the United States.

April 7th
Students of Chŏnju Higher Common School (全州高等普通學校) went on a collective school strike due to failed demands for curriculum expansion. Various labor organizations in Japan held a preparatory meeting at the Mita Alliance Headquarters (三田同盟本部) for the May 1st "May Day" demonstration procession. About 50 workers from the Anju (安州) sluice gate construction project went on strike due to wage dissatisfaction. An earthquake occurred in the Ongjin (瓮津) region. The cabinet of the British dominion Union of South Africa resigned en masse. Suphyŏngsa conventions were held in Aichi (愛知縣) and Gifu (岐阜), Japan. In Changchun, Japanese consular police officer Kuniyoshi Seihō (國吉精保) was shot dead by members of an independence group. The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs issued a notice to France not to interfere in Sino-Soviet negotiations.

April 8th
In Harbin (哈爾賓), Korean Independence Party (朝鮮獨立黨) members Yu Ki-dong (柳基東), Wang Kuk (王國), and one other fought against Japanese police and Chinese troops; outnumbered and outmatched, all three died in battle. The disputed Sungsil Middle School in P'yŏngyang reopened. Representative McDuffie (머크들리지) from California delivered a lengthy anti-Japanese speech. The International Council of Women (國際婦人聯盟會), which convenes every two years, decided to hold its meeting from May 1st to 7th. The British House of Commons rejected the government's proposed Rent Act (借家法案) (intended to prevent landlords from evicting unemployed tenants). A major disturbance occurred in the Turkestan region of Russia, and the Labour-Farmer government dispatched a large army. The revolutionary turmoil in Central America continued, producing numerous casualties. Tensions worsened between Germany and France over the Ruhr (루얼) and Rhine issues. In Kanggye (江界), three common school students were arrested for singing an Independence Army song.

April 9th
A counter-revolution erupted in the Kiev (케푸) region of Russia; the government conducted large-scale arrests nationwide, ordering executions or imprisonment. Chinese International Communist Party members in the Beijing-Tianjin (天津) area decided to hold an International Communist Party Congress in Tianjin on May 10th, National Humiliation Day (國耻紀念日). An Myŏng-gŭn (安明根), who had been imprisoned for 15 years for the attempted assassination of Governor-General Terauchi (寺內), was released from Kyŏngsŏng Prison. A preparatory committee for launching a public rally in Kyŏngsŏng regarding the issue of the so-called Federation of Various Faction Volunteers was formed. The Yi Royal Family (李王家) discovered the loss of the royal seals of Emperors Tŏkchong (德睿兩朝 -德宗 and 睿宗) from within the Chŏngmyo Shrine hall (宗廟殿內).

April 10th
Yi Hyŏn-jae (李賢在), successor to Yi Yong-ik (李容翊), filed a lawsuit demanding 790,000 won against the Yi Royal Family, the Japanese Government, and the Dai-Ichi Bank (第一銀行) at the Tokyo District Court. The peace treaty between Britain and Turkey was passed. The trade treaty between the Netherlands and Czechoslovakia also passed the Dutch lower house. Russia newly promulgated university regulations under the banner of practical education for workers and peasants. Preliminary Japan-Soviet negotiations broke down as Russia strongly rejected Japan's demands; simultaneously, the release of Japanese nationals detained in Vladivostok became hopeless. A Farmers' Assembly was held in Songgwang (松光), Suncheon.

April 11th
An important meeting of the diplomatic corps of various countries was held in Beijing. An Imperial Economic Conference was held at the Japanese Prime Minister's official residence. An explosion at the Fushun Coal Mine (撫順炭鑛) instantly killed 10 workers. Female independence activist Kim Sin-ae (金信愛) from Kosan Township (高山面), Kanggye, received a 3-year suspended sentence from the Kanggye Branch Court.<46>

April 12th
The Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (衡平社) moved its headquarters to 143 Toryŏm-dong (都染洞), Kyŏngsŏng. The controversial Japanese immigration exclusion bill passed the U.S. House of Representatives by a vote of 320 to 71. The military reapportionment bill submitted to the U.S. Senate was rejected. It was agreed to adopt the experts' report for all Allied reparations issues.

April 13th
In Chŏnju, a citizens' rally was held due to the transfer of the Tax Supervisory Bureau to Kwangju (光州), resolving seven articles plus market closure and turning off lights (撤市廢燈). The British Bankers' Association submitted five conditions regarding Anglo-Soviet negotiations: 1. Russia must recognize public and private debts. 2. Return private property to foreigners. 3. Recognize the sanctity of private contracts and guarantee private property against future state confiscation. 4. Allow foreign private entrepreneurs to negotiate private enterprises in Russia without government interference. 5. Cease propaganda against foreign systems within Russia.

April 14th
Young lawyers in Japan organized the Sacred Election League (神聖選擧聯盟). Chosŏn Boy Scouts (朝鮮少年軍) representatives Cho Ch'ŏl-ho (趙喆鎬) and Kim Chu-ho (金周鎬) departed for Beijing to attend the Far East Boy Scout Jamboree held there. Germany held a Federal Chancellor's Conference centering on discussions of the reparations issue. The Jilin Provincial Assembly (吉林省 議會), angered by the arrogant and high-handed methods of Soviet representative Karakhan, resolved to demand the government request a replacement Soviet representative if Mr. "Ka" (카) did not show remorse. The U.S. Senate rejected the Gentlemen's Agreement with Japan. The German government approved the reparations experts' report as originally drafted. The results of the Greek national referendum showed a landslide victory for the Republican Party.

April 15th
The All-Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Congress (全朝鮮勞農大會), which had been under preparation, convened at the Chŏngno Central YMCA Hall (鍾路 中央基督敎靑年會舘) in Kyŏngsŏng with over 80 representatives from 82 organizations. The Japanese immigration exclusion bill also passed the U.S. Senate; simultaneously, senators fiercely argued that the continuation of the Gentlemen's Agreement with Japan was an infringement upon U.S. sovereignty. The U.S. Democratic Party Convention residing in the Philippines resolved for Philippine independence and submitted a proposal to the U.S. government. The long-discussed Chosŏn Imperial University (朝鮮帝國大學) was decided upon by the Japanese Cabinet as Kyŏngsŏng Imperial University (京城帝國大學) according to the Imperial University Ordinance, following the proposal of the Government-General.

April 16th
The 2nd Congress of the Chosŏn Labor General Alliance (朝鮮勞働總同盟) was held at the Citizens' Hall (市民舘) in Osaka (大阪), Japan; the meeting was dispersed by Japanese police during proceedings, and executives were simultaneously arrested or beaten. The Labor-Farmer Congress held in Kyŏngsŏng agreed to merge with two organizations, the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance (南鮮勞農同盟) and the Kyŏngsŏng Labor Federation (京城의 노동연맹), electing negotiation committee members to organize the Chosŏn Labor-Farmer General Alliance (朝鮮勞農總同盟). Britain ratified the Treaty of Lausanne (로잔누) with Turkey. The National Assembly in Ankara (앙코교라) ratified the German-Turkish Friendship Treaty.

April 17th
The Chosŏn Labor-Farmer General Alliance was initiated through the merger of the three major organizations: the Labor-Farmer Congress, the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance, and the Labor Federation. Britain, the U.S., and Italy lodged protests with China regarding the beating and injury of British, American, and Italian nationals by Chinese army soldiers recently. Numerous Russians entered Guangdong (廣東), China, and busily engaged in radical propaganda. The Allied Reparations Commission unanimously approved the report of the reparations expert committee. The U.S. Senate passed a special immigration bill allowing a minimum of 100 skilled agricultural laborers per country. Regarding the passage of the new U.S. immigration bill, Italy, suffering the same negative impact as Japan, had its ambassador protest to the U.S., but to no avail.

April 18th
The 1st Far East International Boy Scout Jamboree was held in Beijing, China. Regarding the U.S. exclusion of Japanese immigrants, Japan held a cabinet meeting to discuss the policy towards the U.S. The leader of the Philippine independence movement, Philippine Senate President Quezon (망케손), along with six assembly members, arrived in Japan. The founding general meeting of the Labor-Farmer General Alliance was held. It is reported that the Korean Independence Army in North Manchuria (北滿) has been completely unified and has begun organizing volunteer forces. Greece completed its transition to a republic through a general referendum of the entire nation, with Mr. Koundouriotis (콘두라치스) assuming office as provisional president. Minister Yoshizawa (芳澤公使) in China proposed the reopening of preliminary negotiations to the Soviet representative.

April 19th
A conference of princes (王候會議) was held in Mongolia (蒙古) aiming to break away from Russia. Chinese people in Harbin, greatly angered by the killing of three Korean Independence Party members, held a joint meeting and presented a strong protest to the Japanese consul through envoys (date unspecified). △ Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) and Wu Peifu confronted each other centered on the struggle for Mongolia. Japan's Seiyūkai (政友) and Kenseikai (憲政) parties held meetings regarding the U.S. issue, resolving to demand reconsideration from the United States.

April 20th
The Chosŏn Labor-Farmer General Alliance, which had convened at Kwangmudae (光武臺) in Kyŏngsŏng, concluded its agenda and adjourned. It then opened a provisional Labor-Farmer Assembly (勞農臨時大會) to discuss labor issues, tenant farming issues, the Oriental Development Company issue, the Tong-A Paper issue, and the Federation of Various Faction Volunteers issue, but was dispersed by police mid-discussion. Subsequently, a group of 600-700 formed ranks, sang labor songs loudly, and conducted a demonstration march, leading to the arrest of about 10 people before dispersing. About 70 female workers at the Chosŏn Silk Reeling Factory (朝鮮製絲工場) in Taegu (大邱) went on a general strike demanding shorter working hours and wage issues.<47>

**Summary**

This article chronicles significant global and local events from March 21 to April 20, 1924. It covers diverse topics including Korean independence activities in Shanghai and Manchuria, labor strikes and organizing efforts in Korea and Japan (like the formation of the Chosŏn Labor-Farmer General Alliance), social movements like the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (Paekchŏng rights) and women's groups, international diplomacy involving China, Russia, Japan, the US, and European powers (especially concerning treaties, reparations, and immigration), political shifts in various countries (Greece, France, Italy), and incidents like fires, arrests, and conflicts. For historians of colonial Korea, the article is valuable for its contemporary reporting on the persistent independence movement abroad, the burgeoning labor and tenant farmer movements facing suppression, internal social divisions (like the pro-Japanese vs. nationalist clashes), the activities of social equality groups like Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, and the direct impact of international events, particularly US immigration policy and Japanese-Chinese tensions, on the Korean populace and diaspora.

四月의 世相, 3월 21일부터 4월 20일까지
						一記者
3월 21일
上海에는 朝鮮靑年同盟會가 發起 (독립운동의 旗幟下에서 민족적으로 일치단결, 민족적 독립의 완성을 위하야 희생적 분투의 강령으로)되고 中정부는 각지에 中露 교섭의 顚末을 표명하야 王廷正氏의 調印은 절대무효를 주장하는 동시에 中露 再交涉을 通達하고 露代表 「카라한」씨는 中國의 희망에 의하야 露정부에 中露 再交涉의 旨를 보고하고 愛蘭軍隊는 英國軍隊가 「크언스타운」에 상륙함을 射擊하야 十數名의 死傷者를 出케 한 바 英愛間 정치의 不穩을 招하고 米國 대의원은 해군 예산안을 통과함과 동시에 대통령 「쿨리지」씨에게 軍備制限會議 再開를 교섭하고 佛國議會는 陸軍改編案을 통과하다.

22일
동맹파업중이든 群山의 落合精米職工은 주인의 고집에 분개하야 40명 作隊로 공장을 습격 翌日 쌍방의 理解로 復業)하고 間島 龍井村의 主義泒 女子들은 斷髮同盟을 조직하고(日字 未詳) 日本 長野에서는 공산당원 23명이 검거되고 英 倫敦市에서는 電車 運轉手 及 車掌 자동차 운전수 등 4만 여명이 동맹파업을 起하야 교통이 杜絶되고 濠洲國民大會는 英國이 新嘉坡根據 중지에 대한 반대를 주장하고 印度 回敎徒會議에서는 土耳其에 回敎政府 수립을 可決하고 佛國 上下院은 財政 개혁안을 통과하다.

23일
