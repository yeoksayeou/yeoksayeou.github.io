Facing the Falling Rain
						海狸 (Haeri)
If winter is death, then spring is life. Now it is spring. The season of life. New sprouts (芽) shoot from the dead stumps, and lovely flowers have bloomed on the withered branches. Moreover, a drizzling (霏霏) spring rain (春雨), barely visible, hastens life. When this spring passes, summer, autumn, and winter will follow in turn. Nature does not deceive people. However, people deceive. They also deceive things.
-I am twenty-one years old- Facing this spring, I am welcoming spring for the twenty-first time. Ah! Then even a tree planted on the mountain (山) is used as timber (材木) after 10 years. But what have I done in the past 20 years? Also, what have I felt? Have I only eaten my fill and played as I pleased thanks to my grandfather? No. Embracing my grandfather's warm affection, I undertook the task of studying far from home (之東之西 負笈). However, there is nothing I have thoroughly completed. Just as it is said of Confucius (孔子), "He learned broadly but did not become famous for any specialty (博學而無所成名)," I truly have learned much but succeeded in nothing (多學而無所成功). Though I read Mencius (孟子), I failed to "curb human desires and preserve heavenly principles (遏人慾存天理)"; though I read the Great Learning (大學), I did not master "investigating things and extending knowledge (格物致知)"; though I read the Analects (論語), I did not learn from Confucius (孔子); though I learned arithmetic, algebra, and geometry, I did not become proficient (通曉) in mathematics; though I learned physics and chemistry, I was not thorough in science (理學); though I learned history and geography, it was also the same. People deceive people. Fathers deceive sons, and sons deceive fathers; teachers deceive students, and students deceive teachers. Mencius (孟子) said, "If superiors and inferiors compete for profit, the state is endangered (上下交征利而國危矣)," so how much more so when superiors and inferiors deceive each other (上下交相欺)! Human life advances toward destruction day by day. Yesterday's benefactor (恩人) turns into today's enemy. The dog one raised bites one's heel.
-A line of poetry comes to mind. "People forming ties need gold; if gold is scarce, the ties aren't deep. Even if promises are made for a moment, from then on, the heart remains that of a passerby on the road (世人結交須黃金, 黃金不多交不深, 縱令然諾暫相許, 從是悠悠行路心)." That's right. "People forming ties need gold." Look at Messrs. Kim and Yi, who were sons of rich families until yesterday. During autumn moons and spring breezes (秋月春風), flower mornings and moonlit nights (花朝月夕), when they reveled with beauties in red skirts (紅裙美人), weren't all the loafers of Changan (長安乾達) and certain gentlemen their friends? But today, when their wealth is gone and hands are empty (財盡手空), who are their friends? Friends from those days are like Wu and Chu (吳楚) today. It has long been said that the people's heart is Heaven's heart (人心이 天心). But Heaven's heart (天心) has changed. Heaven's heart is growing shallower.<135> It is becoming formalized. Even if many people dislike something, it is ignored, and only the words of one or two scoundrels are listened to and called the national will (國是) or the people's will (民是). Gathering just two scoundrels, they call it some meeting (會); gathering just three, they call it some grand convention (大會), and newspapers beat the drums, and society makes a fuss. Even if one is involved with just one beggar-like magazine or one wastepaper-like newspaper, that scoundrel's single word is called public opinion (輿論), and that scoundrel's actions are called the symbol (象徵) of society.
Laozi (老子) said, "Being arises from non-being (無生有)." Then non-being (無) is superior to being (有). But now, contrary to this, non-being is dominated by being. The poor (貧者) are dominated by the rich (富者); the lowly (賤者) are dominated by the noble (貴者); the weak (弱者) are dominated by the strong (强者). But how can they only be dominated? They flatter obsequiously (阿諛苟容), shrug their shoulders and force smiles (挾肩謟笑). Thus, they try to slavishly exert themselves like dogs and horses (犬馬의 勞). They use all sorts of means. Ah! I have seen it. During the past twenty-one years, I have seen such things. I have also learned and felt them. But even standing at this boundary line of twenty-one years, I still see and hear. I am still seeing and hearing now. My body is in the countryside. In the mountains. Only the sounds of birds, the wind, and water reach my ears. But I hear. I hear the clamor of those who, day and night without distinction, scurry around busily like flies (蠅營狥苟) to exert their dog-and-horse labor (?) for people, to exhaust their petty loyalty (?) for people. I am in the mountain valley. What I see are only woodcutters (樵軍), mountains (山岳). Moreover, the day is now cloudy. It is raining. Clouds fill all directions. Heaven and earth are dense [with fog/clouds]. But I see. Passing mountains and crossing waters, I see that distant place. I see them wandering. Now, at the end of a whole day of wandering, they gather with their wives (게집) and children, sitting around broken earthenware bowl fragments (뚝배기 조각) with a few lumps of cold rice, eating mindlessly.
-A bird cries. A sparrow cries. A mountain bird also cries. But a child also cries. It struggles and cries because its mother's breast (젓/乳) yields no milk when it's hungry. And an adult cries too. Cries from hunger. Cries from having no home. But they laugh joyfully (喜喜樂樂), saying the lump of cold rice they just ate filled their stomachs (창자). And they are happy as if they've acquired a palace, having obtained a little space under the floor of someone else's outhouse (뒤간 마루 밋). No, they think they are satisfied with that. Someone called them. And asked. Why is the sound of wailing (哭聲) so rampant (狼藉)? they ask. But they say it's not wailing but cheers of joy. They are perhaps some karmic retribution (業冤) of ours. The other day, they sold our house. They sold it off as they pleased without even asking us, the owners. But we vacated the house without a word. And feeling sorry for them, we left them alone. But now, they intend to sell off our wives and children (妻子) again. They intend to sell off our souls (靈). Thus, they laugh at us who weep.<136> They intend to sell us off under some pretext (稱託).
-Cloud banks rise around the mountain peaks (山峯) visible over there. Cloud banks rise in my heart too. Clouds of anger (憤怒) rise, clouds of hatred (憎惡) rise, and clouds of agony (煩悶) and anguish (懊惱) rise. And following them, clouds of longing (憧憬) rise. Suddenly, I think of you (君), who are in Seoul (서울). I miss seeing him. He and I were ones who sought to fight together against reality for our ideals (理想). We were ones who intended to share sorrow and joy together. But worldly affairs (世事) are impermanent (無常). Worldly affairs are impermanent!! The circling black hand (黑手) of flowing fate seized my body. Thus, I came to be in the South (南), while he remains in the North (北). We truly became "a white heron flying east, a swallow flying west (東飛白鷺西飛燕)." However, though blue mountains (靑山) block and green waters (綠水) lie between him and me, our wills (志) and spirits (氣) face each other and always flutter. This is what he used to say to me. "Let's build upon Seoul (京城) as our foundation and, with the world as our background, unfold our ideals"— these are his words. But I am now in the South, and he is now in the North. That is because both he and I are 'proletarians' (푸로레타리아). Because we are powerless, worthless 'proletarians.' Thus, fleeing the red dust (紅塵), no, chased by the red dust, I left behind beloved Hanyang (漢陽). But he must still be struggling right now. He must be fighting against the swarm of demons (魍魎群).
- Flames flare up. Flames flare up in my heart. I am now facing the falling rain in the darkness. My clothes are soaked (저젓다). But the flames in my heart burn as before (如前). Flames of longing (憧憬) flare up. I miss my father (아버님) far away, I miss you (君), I miss the bright light (빗). I miss the wide fields (들). I miss vast Russia (廣漠한 露西亞). I miss windswept Siberia (시베랴). I miss the shores of the South Seas (南國) dense with palm leaves (椰子닙). I want to go. I want to seek the other shore (彼岸) of longing. But I cannot go there. My feet are stuck (부텃고), my body is bound (매여젓다). I want to go there, but my surroundings (주위) do not permit it, and my body cannot go. But I will go. My soul (靈) will go towards that distant place. But I will stay a little longer before going. I will go after seeing what becomes of the final path (末路) of the greedy demons (貪婪의 魍魎들). I will go after seeing what becomes of them when all the wealth (財物) in this world burns away.
The house is on fire. The fire has enveloped the entire house. It covered the red roof (집웅), but the flames are now crawling under the eaves (簷下). But the swallow (제비) that built its nest under the eaves does not know this, and the mother and chicks enjoy each other (질긴다). They will all burn to death while I watch now. People say the swallow is a beneficial bird (益鳥). But it commits many evils (積惡) against countless insects for the sake of humans. Yet, when winter comes and they leave, people tear down (헐어버린다) their nests. It is truly a case of "when the cunning hare dies, the hunting dog is cooked (狡兎死走狗烹)." But when the cunning hare (狡兔) dies, in the end (那終), the hunting dog (走狗) also dies. Now, the cunning hares are not yet all gone (盡치 안엇다). Thus, they throw even bone fragments (뼉다귀ㅅ個) to their hunting dogs. Ah! But foolish hunting dogs! In the end, the one who will wring (끈흘) your necks will be your current master. (End/了) <137>

**Summary**

Written in 1924 by an author using the pen name Haeri (Sea Otter), this melancholic essay reflects the disillusionment of a 21-year-old intellectual. He laments his lack of achievement despite extensive traditional and modern education, contrasting it with the deceit and hypocrisy he observes in society—from personal relationships to public affairs. He feels alienated, identifying himself and a friend as powerless "proletarians" separated by circumstances. Residing in the countryside, he observes the suffering of the poor but also their seeming acceptance of hardship, contrasting it with the arrogance and eventual doom he predicts for the powerful and wealthy, using the metaphor of hunting dogs eventually being discarded by their masters. The piece is significant for its raw expression of youthful angst and social critique during the colonial period. Historians might find interest in the early use of the term "proletariat" (푸로레타리아) to describe personal powerlessness, the critique of social hierarchy and hypocrisy potentially reflecting colonial realities, and the blend of traditional East Asian philosophical references (Confucius, Mencius, Laozi, Chinese poetry) with modern existential concerns.

나리는 비를 마지며
						海狸
겨울을 죽움Œ이라 하면 봄은 삶이다. 지금은 봄이다. 삶의 시절이다. 죽엇든 등걸에 새싹(芽)이 돗고 시들엇던 가지에 보기 조흔 꼿이 피렷다. 더구나 霏霏한 春雨가 삶을 재촉하며 보일 듯 말 듯이 내린다. 이 봄이 가면 또 다시 여름, 가을 겨울들이 차레로 올 것이다. 자연은 사람을 속이지 안는다. 그러나 사람을 속인다. 또한 물건을 속인다.
-내 나히 스물한살- 이 봄을 맛는 나는 스물한번재의 봄마지를 하는 것이다. 아아! 그러면 山에 심은 나무도 10년이면 材木으로 쓰이는 것이다. 그러나 나는 지난 20년 동안에 한 것이 무엇인가? 또한 늣긴 것이 무엇인가? 한아버님 덕분에 배불니 먹고 맘대로 놀앗슬뿐인가? 아니다. 한아버님의 뜨거운 애정을 담복 안고 之東之西에 負笈의 業을 닥것슴이다. 그러나 나에게는 무엇 하나도 끗까지 철저한 것은 업다. 마치 孔子는 博學而無所成名이라 함과 가티 나야말로 多學而無所成功이다. 孟子를 닑엇스나 遏人慾存天理를 하지 못하엿스며 大學을 닑엇스나 格物致知에 通達치 못하엿스며 論語를 닑엇스나 孔子를 學得치 못하엿스며 算術이나 代數, 幾何를 배웟스나 數學에 通曉치 못하엿스며 物理化學을 배웟스나 理學에 철저치 못하엿스며 歷史, 地理를 배웟스나 그 역시 그러하다. 사람들은 사람을 속인다. 아비가 자식을 속이며 자식은 아비를 속이며 선생은 학생을 속이고 학생은 또 선생을 속인다.孟子는 上下交征利而國危矣라 하엿거든 하물며 上下交相欺이랴! 인생은 하루가 하루보다 점점 파멸을 향하여 전진하는 것이다. 昨日의 恩人이 今日의 원수로 化한다. 저 기른 강아지가 발뒤굼치를 무는 것이다.
-詩 한 句가 생각난다. 世人結交須黃金, 黃金不多交不深, 縱令然諾暫相許, 從是悠悠行路心이란 詩가 생각난다. 그럿타. 世人結交須黃金이라 昨日까지의 富家子이든 金某 李某하는 그들을 보면 秋月春風과 花朝月夕에 紅裙美人으로 興淸대든 그 때는 長安乾達과 某某紳士가 모다 그의 친구가 안이엿섯나? 그러나 財盡手空한 오늘에 그의 친구가 누구인가. 당시의 親友는 今日의 吳楚이다. 전부터 人心이 天心이라 한다. 그러나 天心은 변하엿다. 天心은 점점＜135＞ 얇아간다. 형식화하여 간다. 만흔 사람이 실타 하여도 그것은 도라보지도 안코 한 두 놈의 말만 듯고 國是니 民是이니 한다. 두 놈만 모아 노코 무슨 會이니 세 놈만 모아 노코 무슨 大會이니 하여 신문지가 風을 치고 사회란 것이 떠든다. 그지가튼 잡지 하나만 하여도 휴지가튼 신문 하나만 관계하여도 그 놈의 말 한 마듸를 輿論이라 하고 그 놈의 행동을 사회의 象徵이라 한다.
