**Body Text Translation:**
The Day Before the Wedding
						Hwaewŏl (懷月)
One
"Ah, it's heavy!" sighing listlessly but with a light breath, Yŏngsun passed in front of the E Girls' School in C-dong, her two hands holding some large bundle. On Yŏngsun's forehead, neither wide nor narrow but pleasing to the eye, beads of sweat appeared sparsely, not unpleasant to see. Her two cheeks were faintly flushed as if intoxicated by unseen thoughts. It was unknown when she had last combed her hair, but half of her front hair covered her right cheek and ear. After carrying the heavy object with a pained expression for some time, she couldn't help but put the bundle down on the ground before reaching the end of the long wall of the E school. Then, taking out a handkerchief from her sleeve that was just beginning to show dirt, she listlessly wiped the sweat from her forehead.
"Whew—it's heavy—" she murmured, gazing blankly at the deep marks the string used to tie the bundle had left on her fingers. Then she raised her head again and glanced around once, as if checking if there were any people on the street. But at that moment, there was no one on that road.
As if relieved, Yŏngsun let out a breath and wiped the sweat from her entire face. If Yŏngsun were to do as her tired body wished, she would, disregarding decorum, just stretch out her legs right there on the ground and sit down; that would feel more refreshing than anything else. But instead, Yŏngsun decided to hurry home, go comfortably into her room, stretch out her legs and arms freely, and sleep to her heart's content. Just as she was about to pick up the heavy bundle again, she suddenly heard a pleasant singing voice from somewhere.<144>
When heading toward that heaven
Across the calm sea
The Lord gives me a fair wind
Truly, I am thankful.

This singing voice, as if echoing from the blue sky, as if emerging from the tangled tree branches, roused Yŏngsun's weary heart. Unconsciously, it seemed as if the many past people, the present suffering she was receiving, and some future hope, in this one moment, revealed something to Yŏngsun with a strange power. Yŏngsun looked up toward the source of the singing. It came floating out from within the E Girls' School classroom, accompanied by the sound of a piano. Yŏngsun's ears and eyes, rather than just looking at the red classroom, seemed to forget everything and stood blankly, as if contemplating some unknowable great misfortune of the past. Whenever Yŏngsun passed this way, she always looked with feelings of both love and resentment, thinking of all the old days as she went by. But today, she had passed by almost unconsciously, as if not even knowing whether this road led to the E school or not. However, upon hearing this singing voice, Yŏngsun began to stir again, like a wave encountering spring.
On the left bank, the green leaves of the White Poplar trees, half or more emerged with new buds, were beautifully spread out like a thin green canopy, and on the right bank, fully bloomed flowers stood in clusters, crimson and colorful, as if smiling and sighing. The school building, though old, seemed extraordinary, reflecting the clear sky like glass and the flowing blue light like the wind, having met the spring. Especially in Yŏngsun's highly sensitive heart, it stirred uncontrollably.
Yŏngsun stood there blankly, oblivious even to people passing by, as if she had discovered something marvelous. Girl students wearing pink jeogori and cloud-like white, billowing skirts came out one by one, sitting here and there on the warm, sunny lawn, whispering, poking each other, snickering, and laughing. <145> Among them, a girl who looked seventeen or perhaps older was seen gently hugging the girl next to her with all her might, stretching like a cat basking in the sun, and quietly lying half-prone on the lawn as if unable to bear something. In Yŏngsun's tired eyes, it was as if she had suddenly found light again; she knew neither how to go nor how to come back. She couldn't find words to say nor thoughts to think. Just a year ago, when passing this school, Yŏngsun didn't know how many tears she had shed from her clear eyes. But now, instead of tears, something like the experience of a very old person made Yŏngsun scoff. Previously, when passing by this school, tears had flowed from thoughts of all her friends, thoughts of wishing she had studied more, thoughts of love she once had. But the Yŏngsun of now was not so simple as to cry. Yet, it wasn't that she had no tears either. It was just that the tears then burst out from her eyes, while the tears now flowed within her heart.
Yŏngsun picked up the heavy bundle again, dividing it between her two hands. And she walked forward.
"Heavy bundle!"—it contained everything that constituted Yŏngsun's final preparations. It was all the things necessary for Yŏngsun to get married.
Yŏngsun was to leave for her marriage tomorrow morning on the ten o'clock express train.
"I am a woman about to be married," she thought, and when she looked back at those students, it felt as if there was some great abyss between herself and them. Those students were lying on the bank on the far side of the abyss, while she seemed to be sinking step by step into that abyss. The words "She is getting married" made her heart feel desolate, like someone leaving for some unknown country. She compared herself and those students again. At that moment, Yŏngsun's heart silently crumbled beyond words. A sorrow she couldn't name clutched at Yŏngsun's chest.
Her body and mind were weary, each step appearing listless. Early in the morning, when Yŏngsun received thirty won in an envelope from her father, she had been a very strong woman. An unyielding woman. Before receiving the money, she had asked her father in a strong voice, "What money is this?"
"What do you mean, what money?" her father had looked back at Yŏngsun.<146>
"Why are you giving this to me?" Yŏngsun's face had flushed not so much with embarrassment as with anger.
"Ah—well, didn't Mr. Sangho say anything to you?"
"Who is Sangho?"
"Ah, what an impertinent thing you are! Don't you know the name of the man who will be your husband?" Her father chuckled.
"Yes, I am impertinent!"—and she plopped down right there on the edge of the wooden floor.
"Well, aren't you something. Here I am, your father, running around without even eating properly, arranging things, borrowing money we don't have, adding money we do have... things are finally working out, and you have to act annoyed? Huh—" and he threw the envelope containing the thirty won onto Yŏngsun's skirt where she sat. From the fallen envelope, a ten-won note stuck out halfway. And on the envelope was written, "To Miss Kim Yŏngsun." To Yŏngsun, this envelope falling onto her skirt felt like some unknowable misfortune falling upon her; her body shivered, and goosebumps ran down her spine. And it felt as if the dirty hand of some man was wantonly playing with her noble self, her clean self, her virtuous self; her heart grew resentful, and her body trembled. Yŏngsun's lovely hands moved as if to tear the envelope lying on her skirt into pieces. Her face flushed, and her chest heaved. But in that instant, the clever Yŏngsun quickly observed her father's expression. Anxiety and melancholy together clouded her father's face.
"I'll trouble father's heart. I'll tear it up later when father isn't looking!" she thought, suppressing the flowery vital energy of youth that rose like fire. Instead, hot tears began to well up in Yŏngsun's clear eyes. As the tears gathered heavily within her eyelids, about to fall, Yŏngsun quickly bowed her head.
The teardrops inevitably fell onto Yŏngsun's skirt. Yŏngsun stared blankly at her skirt.
Still, the ten-won note stuck out halfway. Upon that paper, more than ten teardrops had already fallen, wetting one corner.<147>
Yŏngsun, who had been sitting absently, inevitably picked up the envelope with her own hand. The thought, "If Yŏngch'ŏl comes in, it will be troublesome again!" made Yŏngsun pick up the envelope. Yŏngch'ŏl was Yŏngsun's immediate younger brother (a boy). When the news that Yŏngsun's marriage had been decided reached Yŏngch'ŏl, his heart felt desolate, as if he had lost something. All of Yŏngch'ŏl's difficult inner problems and his agonizing worries had, until now, been comforted and resolved by his beloved sister's hopeful guidance. But when he heard she was getting married, Yŏngch'ŏl's heart stirred uncontrollably. And so, whenever he saw his sister, he felt an unprecedented urge to mock her. It wasn't out of malice, nor joy, nor envy. It was simply an impulse welling up from within Yŏngch'ŏl's heart that couldn't be resisted.
"Sister is getting married, they say! Your face looks so much better!" he would always tease his sister. In fact, Yŏngsun was not unaware that her face had become terribly pale recently. The more Yŏngsun knew this, the more Yŏngch'ŏl wanted to say such things. Every time she heard such words, Yŏngsun felt like dying. More than anything, the most unpleasant sound was Yŏngch'ŏl's mocking voice. Thus, suspecting that Yŏngch'ŏl would mock her again if he came in now and saw the money lying on her skirt, she inevitably picked it up.
Making sure no one was looking, as her father had gone outside and her mother was in her room, she quickly picked up the money. Then she looked down again at the spot on her skirt where the money had lain. On the skirt, traces of mending where holes had been patched with unsuitable old cloth were visible. Even if it was a skirt worn at home, there weren't just one or two such mended spots; there were enough worn-out places to be embarrassing if others saw. When she occasionally went out, a plain cotton skirt and plain cotton jeogori made Yŏngsun happy. Of course, Yŏngsun never even thought of things like silk clothes, but still, sometimes she wished she could wear such silk clothes too. "How would my heart feel if I wore silk clothes?" she had thought sometimes. Especially when she was young and attending school, she didn't know better, but as she grew older, seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, along with all sorts of splendid fantasies, concerns about wearing nice and good-looking clothes also occupied a corner of her mind. But her poor household <148> and poor father did not permit her such things. Now, in Yŏngsun's heart, this worn-out dress and the money in the envelope were painfully conflicting. Without even considering the impending marriage, the suffering from lack of money, the frustration of not being able to buy things due to lack of money, the sorrow of not being able to go places due to lack of money—all the accumulated anger and sorrow converged onto the money contained in this envelope. Without needing to ask where the money came from, the money now in her hand held the power to buy anything, as Yŏngsun well knew.
Unconsciously, she stood up abruptly. And went into her own room. She put on the clothes she cherished and didn't usually wear, as if having made some resolution, picking out whatever she could. As she was about to go outside, she saw a letter on her desk.
It was addressed "To Miss Kim Yŏngsun." Yŏngsun picked it up and opened it.
=Introduction omitted= The marriage we were thinking about is proceeding according to plan, which cannot but be thanks to Miss Yŏngsun's generous virtue. However, I worry that the timing has become too rushed and might cause Miss Yŏngsun anxiety. In the meantime, not knowing if there is anything you might need to buy, I am sending a small sum of 30 won, so please feel free to buy whatever you wish. -Conclusion omitted-
Yŏngsun finished reading the letter. And then her heart began to tremble again. She threw the money she held onto the floor. She herself slumped down. Until this moment, the money she had intended to use to buy things, she had thought of only as money itself; she hadn't considered in the slightest that it was sent by Sangho. She had tried to forget that thought. But the letter, unexpectedly arriving as if the owner of the money was saying, "This is money I am giving, so think of me when you use it," tormented Yŏngsun once again.
The interpretation "A marriage forced by money" tormented Yŏngsun. This was the third letter Yŏngsun had received from Sangho, and the second time she had received money. However, she had not used the previous money and had sent it back to Sangho.<149>
Yŏngsun briefly thought about how she had come to know Sangho.—
Three months ago, Yŏngsun had gone to a concert with a friend. At that time, when it ended and she was returning home, it was night and she couldn't see well, but a certain man had followed Yŏngsun all the way to her house. However, since this was not the first or second time such a thing had happened to Yŏngsun, she took it as usual. Then one day,
Yŏngsun received a letter addressed "To Miss Kim." Of course, the content of the letter was "I want to love you," "Please love me." And at the end of the letter, the name "Yi Sangho" was written neatly. But Yŏngsun, for whom this was not the first time, didn't even blink. Sangho must have waited futilely for a reply. Then about half a month passed. One day, a relaxed-looking woman came to Yŏngsun's house without any particular business and, while chatting idly with Yŏngsun's mother about this and that, pointed at Yŏngsun and asked,
"Who is that young lady (시약씨)?"
"Yes, she is my daughter."
"My, she's certainly good-looking."
"..."
"How old is she?"
"Nineteen."
"Does she attend school?"
"No, she finished higher elementary school and attended middle school for a while, but then quit."
"Oh my! Why?"
Yŏngsun's mother was somewhat at a loss for an answer. She really didn't want to say, "She quit because we didn't have money." So she quickly made an excuse,
"She has a bit of an illness."
"Yes? Illness? What kind of illness...?" <150>
"Well, it's not a particular illness, her body is just weak."
"Ah—yes—well, that sort of thing..."
Up to this point, they had talked loudly enough for Yŏngsun to hear, but suddenly the old woman went into the room. Then she whispered something close to her ear. This was indescribably dirty, disgusting, and unpleasant for Yŏngsun to witness. So Yŏngsun went outside. After that, the old woman visited frequently. Well over two months later, Yŏngsun's father one evening said to Yŏngsun,
"Hey, you know that man Sangho, right?"
"..."
"Really, that man is sharp and clever. He looks manly too... He was quite polite. I didn't know he was like that..." By the time he was muttering this to himself, shaking Yŏngsun's ears, he had already received a large sum of three hundred won from Sangho for their poor household.
If Yŏngsun or Yŏngch'ŏl had known what was happening, the siblings might have held hands and gone out to beg on the streets. But they lacked the worldly experience to realize that what they had been eating for the past month was already the product of injustice.
After that, her father and mother urged Yŏngsun every day. But Yŏngsun neither answered nor fainted. She didn't make an issue of it at all.
However, the wedding date they had set was tomorrow.
Yŏngsun thought about this. And she looked fearfully at the money she had thrown on the floor. She felt indescribably ashamed that she had put on new clothes intending to go out and buy things with that money. If someone nearby had said, "You are a dirty girl," Yŏngsun would likely have collapsed on the spot and shed tears of blood. She felt indignant and resentful, as if her chastity had been violated. She took off her skirt again. As she was about to take off her jeogori, Yŏngsun <151> saw a letter lying barely visible under the desk. Yŏngsun quickly picked it up. And checked the back of the letter.
The welcome name "Pak Hyesun" was written there. So she quickly opened it.

Why haven't you come for so long? I waited every day. How troubled must your heart be these days? Although I know all of your heart, I too am not a free person, so although my heart is always sad and desolate about the world, I haven't been able to visit you even once. However, I want to create an opportunity for us to talk for a long time about the words and thoughts in our hearts, even if just for a month. In two more months, I plan to go somewhere quiet and scenic for sightseeing, like a trip, so please wait until then. At that time, I intend to bring Yŏngch'ŏl along too. Then, how beautiful the poetic inspiration that will arise for Mr. Yŏngch'ŏl! ...Omission...
Meanwhile, this evening there is a concert by the famous Mr. XX, so please be sure to come. I will definitely be waiting. -Conclusion omitted-

Yŏngsun's heart, which had been filled with anguish and sorrow, completely dissolved upon reading this. The beautiful affection, the lovely handwriting of Hyesun, was exactly what simmered in Yŏngsun's chest. Yŏngsun had always received Hyesun's generous kindness. Not only spiritually but also materially, she had received much help. However, as Yŏngsun gradually matured, she began to think about others' circumstances rather than blindly loving. After that, she didn't meet Hyesun often. Her desire to see her was as immense as mountains and as vast as the sea, but the pain of constantly relying on others was boundless. The thought, "Someday, when I have money, I will give gifts to Hyesun and pay for outings myself," always prevented Yŏngsun's empty pockets from allowing her to visit Hyesun. The offer to even arrange a trip this summer was endlessly wonderful and appreciated, but relying on others again was a major issue. Anyway, the thought of leaving home, leaving the painful home, and wandering endlessly here and there became stronger the more Yŏngsun suffered. But, she had no "money." Thinking this, she looked down again at the money <152> thrown on the floor. More than anything, going to the concert tonight was also no small worry. It wasn't just once or twice that she had experienced how listening to music when distressed was refreshingly pleasant. Especially on a painful day like today, having a concert felt like it was meant for Yŏngsun; the desire to definitely attend was a more pressing concern, setting aside all other sufferings.
"Music! Music! There lies the scattered spirit of my sad soul!" When Yŏngsun had said this sometime before, Yŏngch'ŏl had replied,
"Poetry! Poetry! In it lies the living cry (叫聲) of joy where the human soul calls out!" This exchange between the siblings stirred Yŏngsun again. But she and her brother had no "money."
Thinking this, she looked again at the money thrown on the floor. If only she had that, she could first buy what she desperately needed, and also buy admission tickets with her own money, proudly give one to Hyesun without feeling indebted, and she and Yŏngch'ŏl could also relieve their troubled hearts—this thought arose like fire. By now, it was already striking eleven o'clock. The warm spring sunlight seemed to smile as it covered the blooming flowers. The wind, gentle as lamb's wool, tormented Yŏngsun even more. The blue sky, painfully clear to her troubled heart, was utterly spotless. Somehow, Yŏngsun had to go outside. But she couldn't leave the money envelope just lying there. She had to reach some kind of clear resolution. At that moment, in Yŏngsun's mind, rather than the hateful and dirty connection between Sangho and the money, the most urgent cry of her heart regarding the relationship between herself and the money became the dominant thought.
Eventually, she even had thoughts like this:
"If I don't use this money and throw it away, it won't go back to Sangho to demonstrate my pure heart; rather, Father will gladly take it and spend it. Then, no matter what I do, I cannot prove my innocence in this current situation..."
Thinking this, Yŏngsun, without seeking any further reason, picked up the money envelope lying on the floor again.<153>
And she retied the ribbon of her jeogori and put back on the skirt she had taken off. Then Yŏngsun stepped outside her house gate. First, the place called "Honmachi" (本町) came to mind without need for thought.
And so, wandering here and there, she became absorbed and excited buying things—stationery, ink, handkerchiefs, socks, magazines, books, cosmetics, notebooks... buying whatever she could. When she bought one thing, she wanted to buy another; when she bought that, she wanted this. Spending money also seemed to require great effort, and leaving only 3 won in her pocket, she looked very tired from buying everything. When she saw that only 3 won remained after buying things, Yŏngsun herself was startled. Absently, she had almost missed the chance to go to the concert. Realizing she couldn't buy anything more, Yŏngsun let out a weary sigh, "Hoo—" weakly, and started on the road back home. Carrying these various things herself seemed very awkward. Not only did it look awkward, but she herself seemed aware of it, occasionally looking back down the road and glancing sideways as she walked. Since there were many people on the main street, fearing she might run into someone she knew, she deliberately turned into an alley called C-dong. Halfway down the road, Yŏngsun realized she was surprisingly more tired than before.
Cold sweat beaded on her forehead, her shoulders felt strangely heavy, and her legs had no strength at all. She felt like collapsing right there. Especially when passing the E school and looking at the girls filled with joy, her heart felt indescribably weary. This day felt like a week long to Yŏngsun, and the work she had done so far felt like forced mechanical labor performed unwillingly, leaving her exhausted. Looking down at the things she had bought, she felt bewildered, as if she didn't know why she had bought such items.
"Why did I buy these things?" Yŏngsun wondered as she walked, wanting to throw them away on the street, but even that couldn't be done without an inner battle. It wouldn't be a lie, for the moment, to say that all her hopes were contained within these items.
Then, would Yŏngsun's hopes disappear along with these items if they were gone? No. Yŏngsun could gain greater freedom precisely when all these items were gone.<154>

Two
Returning home, Yŏngsun quickly went into her room, afraid Yŏngch'ŏl might see. She didn't have the courage to unpack the things. "I must have been crazy. Why did I buy those?" As she sat absently, Yŏngch'ŏl opened the door without knocking, which he never usually did. Yŏngsun was startled for no reason. Seeming apologetic for startling his sister by opening the door unexpectedly, Yŏngch'ŏl smiled faintly. And
"Sister!"
Yŏngsun looked up anew.
"Sister!" Yŏngch'ŏl smiled.
Yŏngsun looked at her brother, looking as if she might cry any moment.
"Sister!" When he called again, his voice was somewhat stern.
"Yes!" It was very weak and lacked strength.
"I won't blame you, sister, for any of this. I am just trying to think about how we can live more freely, like human beings. Our lives can never just be about comfort and laughter. Facing such anxieties and pains constantly is our life now. It is said that the wisest person here is the one who can best manage this suffering.
Sister! There's absolutely no need for all these things to trouble your heart!" Then again,
"Come on! Let's see what's here, let's unpack it," Yŏngch'ŏl rushed forward and began to untie the bundle.
These words shook Yŏngsun, who was lost in fatigue and shame, as if in a faint. For Yŏngsun, who didn't know what to think, it sounded like some great truth. At this moment, she realized how lacking her thinking was compared to Yŏngch'ŏl's. However, Yŏngsun's thoughts were not without reason. If Yŏngch'ŏl's truth utilized heroic or perhaps compromising means to conceive of a different life, Yŏngsun's truth could aptly be described as superhuman and flawless, considering life as a whole. <155> However, while Yŏngsun's "superhuman" aspect might be considered noble if it were a theory, it was not without weaknesses when it came to practice. In other words, suffering while trying to practice it was Yŏngsun's weak point.
He untied the bundle completely. Then Yŏngch'ŏl examined each item one by one. At that moment, Yŏngsun's troubled heart, associated with those items, calmed down somewhat. And sitting closer to Yŏngch'ŏl,
"Hey, this ink, notebook, and this magazine are for you to have, and those are for me..." As she pointed with her hand, she suddenly remembered the terms they used when playing together as children. The memory of the words they used when dividing pencils and notebooks they got when they both attended school saddened Yŏngsun's heart.
But now, the time for happily dancing while holding items to share like when they were little had passed. Even if the feelings and terms were the same, their thoughts about the items were different. Especially when holding the items, it was the way of adults dividing things to become thoughtful about something.
"I still have a notebook and ink,"
When Yŏngch'ŏl replied, Yŏngsun felt troubled again. Yŏngsun tried to force the interpretation that his meaning was, "I won't take such dirty things." And again, Yŏngsun said in a disappointed voice,
"I don't want to have them either."
"Why?"
"If you find them dirty and don't want them, then I find them dirty too," she said, her face flushing.
Yŏngch'ŏl was about to say, "Then why did you buy them?" but to comfort Yŏngsun's heart, he said,
"If I didn't have any, I'd use them, but I really do have some. Sister, you don't have any, so you need to use them—" At this, tears inexplicably welled up in Yŏngsun's eyes. She simply bowed her head. And instead of saying anything, clean teardrops began to fall onto the opened notebook. Yŏngch'ŏl's heart was also indescribably shaken. Sadness doesn't arise from any logical reason. It simply arises from the heart when needed. Accordingly, sympathy also has no reason. A tragic fact <156> shakes human sentiment, regardless of whether it's good or bad. Unseen tears welled up in Yŏngch'ŏl's heart too. But instead of expressing anything verbally, he just stood up abruptly and went outside. This was the first time Yŏngsun had shed such tears in such a situation with her brother, but speaking truly, it wasn't the only time.—
Early on, Yŏngch'ŏl attended P school and Yŏngsun attended E school. Yŏngch'ŏl liked poetry and Yŏngsun liked music, so in the evenings, under the moonlight or starlight, they would read poetry and also sing songs. They had a poetic life that adults couldn't experience. Sometime, when autumn came, one quiet evening, a heart-wrenching evening, they cried for no reason while reading poetry. Both felt embarrassed; the sister went back to her room, and the brother also went back to his. The next day, they were embarrassed to look at each other.
Yŏngch'ŏl was embarrassed, fearing Yŏngsun might ask, "Why did you cry yesterday?" and Yŏngsun was afraid Yŏngch'ŏl might ask, "Sister, why did you cry?" But these tears now, before asking "Why did you cry?", they both knew well that they had to cry first. Especially since Yŏngsun had casually looked at Yŏngch'ŏl's diary yesterday—

'She intends to leave.—
The hand that picked lilies, my sister's hand that touched the wound on my chest, follows the soundless water in the shaded valley of life, 떠나가련다 (intends to drift away endlessly).
Alone, at every twilight, I get drunk on contemplation I don't even understand. But life is not only this.'

Remembering seeing such passages, she immediately recalled the song heard from E school:

'When heading toward that heaven
Across the calm sea...'<157>
This song came to mind together. At that time, her heart was troubled, but an indescribably clear poetic thought rose like a cloud within her heart. Yŏngsun's tears at this moment were precisely the moment of recovery of a beautiful mind speaking the energy of poetry. This moment, this moment of shedding tears, was again Yŏngsun's most beautiful realm of poetry, difficult to attain again. Yŏngsun once more recalled a line of poetry received from a certain man a year ago.—

Toward the night-country where the heart rests,
I follow your bright light like the moon.
Clouds cover the moon, darkening my country,
But in my heart, there is a moon that never disappears.
... ... ...
There was also a man who tried to love with the passion that wrote such poetry. When Yŏngsun thought that the "moon that never disappears" in that man's heart was her, how much she could taste the beauty of womanhood herself through that poem. She cherished that poem. But she did not love him. Thinking of that man again now, the thought "If I had loved him back then..." also occurred sometimes. But she had never met him again after that. However, although these tears now and those tears then were similar, they were actually quite different.
The tears then were tears of "longing for life," while the tears now were like those of a soldier on the front line longing for home. They were unavoidable tears, something that must pass through suffering.
Yŏngsun, who had been in distress for some time, seemed to have had some thought herself; she gathered the scattered items again and swept the room. Yŏngch'ŏl, who had gone outside earlier, came back into Yŏngsun's room.
"Sister?"
"Yes!" Yŏngsun looked up, seemingly resentful.<158>
"Here, look at this!" he unfolded the newspaper he held and showed her.
"What?"
"The concert!"
"I knew about it too."
"How?"
Yŏngsun showed him the letter from Hyesun. Then, as their eyes met again, they couldn't help but smile.
"Do you have money?"
"Of course, I do."
"Where did you get money..."
"I sold some books..."
At that moment, Yŏngsun, without a word, picked up the wallet lying beside her and gave it to Yŏngch'ŏl. Then, looking at Yŏngch'ŏl as if about to say something,
"Open that wallet!"
"Why?" As Yŏngch'ŏl opened the wallet, it wasn't unexpected, but still, there were 3 won in the wallet which usually contained nothing. Before Yŏngch'ŏl could ask where the money came from, he looked again at the items laid out before him.
Quickly, the thought "The remaining money!" came to his mind. Yŏngch'ŏl was happy to be able to go to a concert after such a long time, however it happened. Unlike when he was asked to take the notebook or ink earlier, using this money didn't feel awkward.
"So, you mean we should go to the concert with this money?"
"Do as you please!"
"Alright then, whatever!" Yŏngch'ŏl put it in his pocket. He took it in preparation for buying the admission tickets.<159>
"Hey, girl!" a voice called from the inner room.
It was unmistakably Yŏngsun's mother's voice.
"Yes?"
"Come over here a moment!"
Yŏngsun hated facing her mother and father. In Yŏngsun's heart, she hated their base actions, even if they lacked money. She hated how they had shamelessly accepted 300 won using her as collateral. So, since finding out recently, she especially disliked seeing them.
"Why is it?"
"Ah, just come over!"
"Just tell me!"
"Ah, just come here, I said!" she shouted.
Having no choice, Yŏngsun crossed over to the inner room.
Her mother had laid out *sambal* trousers, a skirt, another jeogori, and various other items. Looking up at Yŏngsun, she gestured with her hand as if Yŏngsun were very dear,
"Come here—this way."
"Just tell me what you want!" she snapped.
"Why are you so cold, girl? Don't be like that to others..."
"..."
"Here, I had the clothes you'll wear when you leave tomorrow made, try them on to see if they fit!"
"You had them made according to measurements, so they should fit, right?"
"Still, don't be like that, just try them on!" <160>
Having no choice, Yŏngsun took the clothes and went back across. She didn't want to wear them. But since there was no reason not to try them on, she playfully put on the new clothes. Putting on new clothes, especially silk ones, made her realize anew how heavy her old clothes had felt. And she didn't know how light and pleasant these new clothes felt. The soft, snug clothes felt like a kind-hearted person's disposition; once she put them on, she didn't even feel like taking them off. The old clothes she had taken off, dirty old clothes, heavy old clothes, suddenly felt unpleasant to wear. And when Yŏngsun looked in the mirror, although her face was a bit thin, she looked quite confident with the new style of dress. Suddenly, wearing those clothes, the desire to go to the concert flared up like fire. "If only I could have gone to the concert wearing clothes like these..." she thought, feeling a renewed aversion to her old clothes.
"Girl, let me see!" her mother called. Yŏngsun went without complaint.
"Wow, they made them really well. How can they fit so perfectly! Walk around a bit!"
Yŏngsun took a few steps.
"They fit perfectly!"
"..."
"Since you have them on, go visit your aunt!"
Yŏngsun had been thinking of doing so anyway, and since it was also her mother's order, she picked up her new shoes and parasol without a second word, putting one on and holding the other.
No matter how you looked at it, she seemed to possess the dignity to become quite a beauty. On the street, there was no one who didn't look at her. Light clothes, shoes pleasant for walking, a stylish parasol; even standing still, she felt as if she were naturally walking. When her spirits were high for a moment, the thought "If only I could meet someone I know at a time like this, I could even speak cleverly..." was not absent. Her long suffering in poverty felt newly painful. "How did I live like that!" she asked herself, so much so that her past life felt meaningless.<161>
She arrived safely at her maternal aunt's house.
But entering felt newly embarrassing.
If she were someone who regularly wore such clothes, it wouldn't be an issue, but suddenly dressing luxuriously for no reason felt shameful. However, having come this far, she couldn't just not go in, so she had no choice but to enter.

"Yŏngsun, is that you? Why haven't you visited in so long?"
Her aunt asked, looking at Yŏngsun with strange eyes.
"There were various things going on."
"So, is your mother well?"
"Yes."
And then there was no particular other conversation. After a little while, her male cousin came in, saying,
"Ah! Sister! It's been a long time since you came!"
"I had some things to do..."
"I wondered who this stylish person (하이카라) was who came, because the shoes are excellent—"
"..." Inside, she felt truly ashamed. If she was going to be this speechless, she wanted to quickly return home.
"So, how much did they cost?"
"I don't know."
"The price must be considerable."
Her cousin's wife rushed out from the kitchen, <162>
"That umbrella is quite nice too!" she said, looking at her own plain black umbrella.
"You'd like something like this, wouldn't you?" he said, looking at his wife and smiling.
Leaving her work unfinished, Yŏngsun's cousin's wife rushed into the room. She wanted to admire the clothes. Before the cousin's wife could ask, the aunt, already sensing the situation, asked,

"Are you getting married! Going without any announcement?"
"Yes," Yŏngsun replied, her face flushing as if she might cry.
"Where to?"
"I'm going to the countryside," she said, and regretted saying such a thing without thinking. It wasn't Yŏngsun getting married, but rather her family marrying her off.
"When?"
"Tomorrow morning at ten, I think they said?"
"Are you going by train?"
"I think so," she said, forcing a hollow laugh she didn't feel. Her female cousin-in-law, sitting beside her, lifted Yŏngsun's skirt to look at the clothes underneath.
"Did the groom's family make this too?"
"Yes!" Yŏngsun pushed it out towards the admiring onlooker. And touching it herself, she said,
"It's not the best, but it's sturdy *sampan* (삼판)!"
The cousin's wife touched it again and again, seemingly envious. Her male cousin came in.
"Sister, where are you going to?"
"..." <163>
"Huh?"
"To the countryside—"
"So, they're rich, right?"
"Well, that's what they say, but who knows?"
"Rich!" he muttered repeatedly, as if giving some kind of hint.
At those words, Yŏngsun felt indescribably ashamed. Hearing them, she realized how much her own heart was becoming assimilated.
She realized how much shameful talk and action her heart had engaged in without knowing. The more she thought, the more she couldn't sit there, her face burning. She stood up immediately.
"Why, are you leaving?"
"Yes, I have somewhere else to go."
"Are you leaving so abruptly? Have dinner with us before you go!"
"No, thank you—" Yŏngsun insisted on standing up and went outside.

She returned to her own house. During that time, Yŏngsun's heart had become quite weary. Going into her room and seeing the old clothes she had taken off, she didn't know how glad she felt. For some reason, when wearing those clothes, everyone seemed accepting, but wearing these new clothes, it felt like everyone was mocking her. As if exhausted, still dressed, she stretched out her legs and lay down quietly, seemingly lost in thought.
The clock hanging in the dim corner of the wall struck five. The drawn-out melody of a spring flute heard from afar was, note by note, Yŏngsun's sigh.
When Yŏngsun thought again of the "moon that never disappears," she knew that she herself was now a moon veiled by clouds.<164>
"I will become the queen of the moon that eternally never disappears," she nodded to herself.
It was half past seven when she finally awoke. Yŏngsun had dozed off for a short while.
"The concert time is seven—" Yŏngch'ŏl rushed in.
But Yŏngsun had no intention of going to the concert. She seemed to be thinking of something greater.
That night, Yŏngsun, Yŏngch'ŏl, and Hyesun, these three young people, instead of going to the concert, stayed at Hyesun's house for a long time, discussing something heartfelt, crying together.

Early the next morning, her father went around searching for Yŏngsun. But he couldn't find her even after ten o'clock. In Yŏngsun's room, the bundle of items bought in Honmachi, the wallet with 3 won still inside, and the new clothes, taken off and neatly folded, were all left just as they were.
But the old clothes were gone. Yŏngch'ŏl also did not return. Her father had no choice but to send the following telegram to the groom.
"Bride ill, departure postponed."
-The End- <165>

**Summary**

Set the day before her arranged marriage, the story follows Yŏngsun, a young, educated woman from a poor family. Forced into marriage by her parents who accepted money from the groom (Yi Sangho), Yŏngsun grapples with feelings of shame, resentment, and lost dreams, particularly her desire for continued education and a life chosen freely. She briefly succumbs to the allure of material comfort bought with the groom's money but ultimately rejects the marriage. The story ends with her disappearance on the wedding day, along with her like-minded brother Yŏngch'ŏl, choosing an uncertain future over a compromised present, after a heartfelt discussion with her supportive friend Hyesun.

From a historical perspective, the story vividly portrays the economic hardships faced by many Koreans during the colonial period, where poverty could force families into difficult choices like arranging marriages for financial reasons. It highlights the emerging consciousness of educated young women ("New Women") like Yŏngsun, who valued personal freedom, education, and romantic love over traditional obligations, showcasing the conflict between modern aspirations and societal/familial pressures. The mention of specific locations like Honmachi (a Japanese name for a district in Seoul) and the overall themes of disillusionment and seeking freedom subtly reflect the broader context of life under colonial rule.

왼쪽 언덕으로는 새싹이 반이나 너무 나온 白楊木의 푸른 입이 열분 초록 포쟝을 친 듯이 아름답게 펼처서 잇고 오른 언덕에는 滿開된 꼿들이 울긋불긋 웃는 듯이 하느기고 뭉치뭉치 서서 잇다. 봄을 만난 敎舍는 비록 오래는 되엿슬지라도  유리가티 맑은 한울의 바람가티 흐르는 푸른 빗헤 反射가 되여서 보기에 凡常치 안엇다. 더욱이 영순의 感受性이 빠른 마음에는 견댈 수 업시 마음이 흔들렷다.
영순은 무슨 신기한 것이나 차저낸 듯이 겻테 사람 가는 것도 관계하지 안이하고 우두머니 바라다보고 잇다. 분홍 저고리에 구름빗가티 희고도 뭉글거리는 치마를 입는 녀학생들이 한아한아 나오드니 땃듯한 양지바른 잔듸 우에 여기저기 안저서 소근거리기도 하고 서로 꾹꾹 찌르고 씩씩거리고 웃는 계집애도 잇섯다.＜145＞ 그 즁에는 나이가 열일굽이나 혹 너머 보이는 계집애 한아가 엽헤 계집애를 은근히 자기 힘것 꼭 끼드니 해빗 쪼이는 고양이처럼 기지게를 키며 무엇에 견댈 수 업는 것처럼 잔듸밧 우에서 고요히 반즘 업드린 것도 보인다. 영순의 피로한 눈에는 문득 또 다시 광명을 어든 것처럼 갈 줄도 모르고 올 줄도 몰낫다. 말할 것도 찻지 못하고 생각할 것도 엇지 못하엿다. 일년 젼만 해도 이 학교를 지내갈 때에  영순은 그 얼마나 그의 맑은 눈속에서 눈물을 뿜앗는지 몰낫다. 그러나 지금 영순의 눈속에는 눈물이 나는 대신으로 나희 만흔 늙은이의 경험가튼 것이 영순을 비웃게 하였다. 젼일에 이 학교압흘 지낼 때에 영순은 모든 동무의 생각, 쟈기가 더 좀 공부를 햇스면 하는 생각, 사랑이 잇든 생각, 이 모든 것으로써 눈물이 흘넛섯다. 그러나 지금의 영순은 눈물이 날 만치 그러케 단순하지는 안엇다. 그러타구 또 눈물이 업는 것도 안이엿다. 다만 그때 눈물은 눈 밧그로 터저나온 것이고 지금 눈물은 마음속으로 흘느는 것이엿다.

「웨 이것을 나를 주세요?」하고 영순의 얼굴은 붓그럼이라는 것보다도 분로한 빗츠로 붉어젓섯다.
「아-니, 상호씨가 너에게 아모 말도 업섯니?」

「누님은 시집을 간다지! 얼굴이 다 조화젓서요!」하고 늘 자기 누님을 조롱하엿다. 사실 영순의 얼굴이 요사이로 몹시 파리한 것을 영순이가 모르는 것은 안이엿섯다. 영순이가 그런 줄을 알사록 더 그런 말을 하구 십헛다. 그런 소리를 드를 때마다 영순의 마음은 죽고만 십헛다. 무엇보다도 듯기 실흔 소리는 영철이의 조롱하는 소리엿다. 그리해서 지금도 영철이가 드려오다가 자기 치마 우에 노인 돈을 볼 것이면 또 조롱을 하겟슴을 의심하고 할 수 업시 집엇다.
누가 볼까 보아서 아버지도 밧게를 나아가고 어머니도 방안에 계신 틈을 타서 얼는 그 돈을 집엇다. 그리고 다시 그 돈 노엿든 치마 우를 나려다 보앗다. 그 치마 우에는 해여진 구멍을 당치도 안은 헌 헝겁으로 기인 자죽이 보인다. 아모리 집에서 입는 치마라도 이러케 긴 자죽이 한 군데 두 군데가 안이고 남이 보면 붓그려울 만치 해여진 데가 만엇다. 모처럼 밧게를 나아가게 되면 옥양목치마 옥양목저고리가 영순을 즐거웁게 하엿다. 물론 영순은 비단옷 가튼 것은 생각지도 안이 하엿지만은 그래도 간혹 나도 저런 비단옷을 입엇스면 하엿다. 「비단 옷을 입으면 마음이 엇대지나?」하고 생각한 일도 잇섯다. 더욱이 나이가 어려서 학교에 단일 때에는 오히려 몰낫으나 나이도 점점 열일곱, 여덜, 아홉까지 될 동안에는 모든 화려한 空想과 한 가지 입기 조코 보기 죠흔 옷 걱정도 한 구석을 차지하엿섯다. 그러나 업는 살림＜148＞ 업는 아버지는 그에게 그런 것을 허락지 안엇다. 지금 영순의 마음속에는 이 해여진 옷과 봉투 속에 든 돈이 괴롭게 싸호고 잇다. 얼마 안이 되여서 결혼이라는 것을 생각할 여지업시, 돈 업시 괴롭게 고생하든 것, 돈 업서 못 사든 화푸리, 돈 업서 갈 데를 못 가든 슬품 싸이고 싸엿든 화란 화, 슬품이란 서름이 한 가지 이 봉투 속에 느어 잇는 돈으로 集合되엿섯다. 그 돈이 어듸서 온 것을 무러볼 여지도 업시 손 안에 잇게 된 그 돈은 모든 것을 사게 할 만한 권리를 가진 것을 영순이도 잘 알엇섯다.

「이야, 이 잉크, 노트, 하고 이 雜誌는 네가 가질 것이고 저것은 내가 가질 것이다... 」하고 손으로 가라칠 때에는 별안간 어렷슬 때에 영철이와 가티 놀 때의 쓰든 用語가 생각낫다. 둘이 다 학교에 단일 때에 연필과 공책이 생기면 서로 논아 가질 때에 쓰든 말의 기역 영순의 마음을 슬푸게 하엿다.
그러나 이제는 어렷슬 때처럼 논아 가질 물건을 들고 즐거히 춤추든 때는 다 지내갓다. 마음은 갓고 用語는 갓다 할지라도 그 물건에 대해서 생각하는 것이 다르다. 더욱이 물건을 손에 들 때에는 무엇인지 생각하게 되는 것이 어른들의 물건을 난흐는 법이엿다.
「나는 아즉것 노트도 잇고, 잉크도 잇서요」하고

「나도 갓기가 실타」
「웨?」
「네가 더러워서 갓기 실으면 나도 더러워 실타」하고는 얼굴이 붉어젓다.
「그런데 웨 사 가지고 왓서요?」하는 말을 하려다가 영철은 영순의 마음을 위로하기 위해서
「업스면 쓰지만 참말로 잇서요. 누님은 업쓰니까 쓸 필요가 잇서요-」할 때에 영순은 까닭도 업시 눈에서 눈물이 핑 돌앗다. 머리를 그냥 숙으렷다. 그리고 아모 말도 업는 대신에 깨끗한 눈물 방울이 펴서 노은 노트에 떠러지기 시작한다. 영철이도 말할 수 업시 마음이 흔들렷다. 마음이 슬퍼지는 것은 무슨 理致로 되는 것이 안이다. 다만 마음에서 필요한 때에 일어나는 것이다. 따러서 同情에도 理由가 업는 것이다. 悲憺한 사실이＜156＞ 조코 낫분 것을 물론하고 사람의 人情을 흔드는 것이다. 영쳘이 마음속에도 브이지 안는 눈물이 고엿다. 그러나 무어라고 말로 발표하는 대신으로 그냥 벌덕 일어나서 밧그로 나아갓다. 영순이는 자기 아우와 이와 가튼 자리에서 이와 가튼 눈물이 처음은 처음이나 진뎡한 뜻으로 말하면 이번뿐이 안이엿다.-
일즉이 영쳘이는 P학교에를 단이고 영순이는 E학교를 단일 때이엿다. 영철이는 詩를 조화하고 영순이는 음악을 조화하여서 저녁이면 달빗 아래서 혹은 별빗 아래서 詩를 닑고 또한 노래를 불넛다. 어른은 체험할 수 업는 詩的 생활이 저들에게 잇섯다. 어느 때인지 가을이 되엿슬 때에 고요한 저녁 마음 압흔 저녁에 저희들이 詩를 닑다가 까닭업시 운 일이 잇다. 둘이 다 붓그러워서 누님은 자기 방으로 동생도 자기 방으로 도라갓다. 그 잇튼날은 둘리 서로 보기가 붓그러웟다.

『그는 가려는도다.-
百合을 따든 손, 내 가슴의 傷處를 만저 주든 누님의 손은 生의 그늘진 山골에 자최 업는 물소리를 따라 끗업시 떠나가련다.
나는 홀로 黃昏마다 나도 몰을 黙想에 醉한다. 그러나 이것뿐이 生은 안이다.』

「시골로 가요」하고는 자기도 몰으게 그런 말 한 것이 後悔가 낫다. 영순이가 시집가는 것은 안이라 자기 집이서 시집을 보내는 턱이엿다.
「언제?」
「래일 아츰 열시라든시지요?」
「車로 가니?」
「그럿타는지요」하고는 마음에 업는 헛된 우숨을 픽- 우섯다. 엽헤 안젓든 사촌 오라비댁은 영순의 치마를 들치고 속에 입는 옷을 보앗다.
「이것도 신랑집이서 햇서요?」
「응!」하고 구경하는 사람에게로 숙 내여 밀엇다. 그리고 자기도 그것을 만지면서
「조치는 안어, 그러나 튼튼한 삼판이야!」
오라비댁은 부러운 듯이 만지고 또 만진다. 사촌 동생이 드러왓다.
「누님 어듸로 가게 되엿소?」
「... 」＜163＞
「응?」
「시골로-」
「그래 부자라지?」
「무얼 말에는 그러지만 몰나?」
「부자!」하고 무슨 暗示를 주는 것처럼 거푸 중얼대엿다.
그 말에 영순은 말할 수도 업시 붓그러웟다. 그 말을 들을 때에 자기의 마음이 얼마나 同化되여 가는 것을 깨닷게 되엿다.
알지 못하는 새이에 자기에 마음이 얼마나 붓그러운 말과 일을 하게 된 것을 알엇다. 생각할사록 그 자리에 안젓슬 수가 업서 얼굴이 홧홧하엿다. 곳 이러섯다.
「웨, 가런?」
「네, 다른 데도 갈 데가 잇서요」
「이러케 섭섭하게 간단 말이냐. 저녁이나 가티 먹고 가지!」
「안이예요-」하고, 영순은 기여히 일어서서 밧그로 나왓다.

자기 집으로 도라왓다. 영순은 그 동안에 마음은 퍽 피로하엿다. 자기 방에 들어가서 버서버린 헌 옷을 볼 때에 얼마나 반가웟는지 몰낫다. 엇전 일인지 그 옷을 입으면 모든 사람이  마저서 주나 이 새 옷을 입고서는 사람마다 조소하는 것갓햇다. 피곤한 듯이 옷 입은 채 다리를 벗고 무엇을 생각을 하는지 고요히 드러누엇다.
침침한 벽 구퉁이에 걸닌 시계는 다섯時를 친다. 멀리 들이는 봄 피리의 느러진 곡조는 마듸마듸 영순의 한숨이엿다.
「업서지지 안는 달」을 다시 영순이가 생각할 때에 지금 자기는 구름에 가린 달인 줄을 알엇다.＜164＞
「나는 영원히 업서지지 안는 달의 女王이 되리라」하고 자기 혼자 고개를 끄덕이엿다.
일곱시 반이야 비로소 깨엿다. 영순은 잠간 동안 졸앗든 것이다.
「음악회의 시간은 일곱신대-」하고 영철이가 뛰여 들어왓다.
그러나 영순이는 음악회에 가려구는 안이하엿다. 그보다 더 큰 무엇을 생각하는 모양이다.
그날 밤에 영순과 영철과 혜순, 이 세 사람의 젊은 청년들은 음악회에를 가는 대신에 혜순의 집이서 무슨 이야긴지 오랫동안 서로 울면서 마음의 吐議가 잇섯다.

그 이튼날 아참 일즉이 그의 아버지는 영순이를 차지려 단이엿다. 그러나 열시가 넘도록 찻지 못하엿다. 영순의 방에는 本町에서 사온 물건 뭉치와, 지갑 속에 3원을 넌 채로. 새 옷은 버서서 잘 개여 논 것이 그대로 다 그냥 잇섯다.
그러나 헌 옷은 업섯다. 영철이도 도라오지 안엇다. 그의 아버지는 하는 수 업시 아래와 가튼 電報를 신랑에게로 노앗다.
「신부 신병, 출발 연기」라고 하엿다.
