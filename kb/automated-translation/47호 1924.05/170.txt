Youth
						Translated by Kim Ŏk

Alas, oh youth, someday you will abandon me,
Holding love in both hands, you will abandon me.
Though I cry wrapped in sorrow, you will abandon me,
Thus, even your shadow will not remain with me.

With shadow in the mouth, wrapped in cries in the eyes,
Still, yet, I will strive to call your name.
So as not to hear me wailing so,
Quickly will death place my half-dead heart in its palm.

Beautiful and sorrowful, boundless love,
This parting from you, whom I longed for with so deep a heart,
That I can still walk this perilous path where even the shadow of footprints
Cannot be reflected, is that not perhaps a falsehood?<141>

Alas, youth, now stolen by "Joy" and "Love,"
That you and your dreams and tears and smiles and roses
Would ever become hateful again... Ah, boundless,
Was I not the one who only wished for and longed for just that... .<142>

**Summary**

The poem is a melancholic lament about the inevitable loss of youth, personified as a lover who departs despite the speaker's sorrow and pleas. The speaker reflects on the pain of this departure and the bittersweet memories associated with youth – love, dreams, tears, smiles, and beauty (symbolized by roses). The poem questions the ability to endure life after youth's departure, suggesting it might be a "falsehood." It concludes with a poignant reflection on how the very things cherished in youth, once lost, were all the speaker ever truly desired. From a historical perspective, this poem, translated by the prominent literary figure Kim Ŏk during the Japanese colonial period, captures a sense of loss, transience, and perhaps subdued despair that characterized the era for many Korean intellectuals. The intense focus on personal, lyrical sorrow over the passage of youth could be seen by historians as reflecting broader anxieties or a turning inward amidst the constraints of colonial rule, where overt expressions of national sorrow might be suppressed.

절믐
						金億 譯
애닯아라, 오오 절믐이여, 언제 한 番은 그대가 나를 바리리,
두 손으로 사랑을 밧들고 그대는 나를 바리리.
설음에 싸여 내가 운다도 그대는 나를 바리리,
이리하야 내게는 그대의 그림자좃차 잇지 안으리.

입에는 그림자, 눈에는 부르짓즘에 싸여,
아직도 오히려, 나는 그대의 이름을 애써 불으리.
이리도 울부짓는 나를 다시는 듯지도 안으랴고
빨으게도 나의 半死엣 心臟을 죽음은 손바닥에 노흐리.

아름답고도 설음 만흔 가이 업는 사랑이여,
이리도 깁흔 맘으로 그대를 그립어 하든 나의 이발이
발자최의 그림자좃차 빗최이지 못한 險한 길을
아직도 걸을 수 잇을 이람은 것즛이나 아닌가?＜141＞

애닯아라, 절믐이여, 지금 「깃븜」과 「사랑」에게 빼앗기여
그대와 그대의 꿈과 눈물과 미소와 薔薇가
다시는 밉게 되리라고야... 아, 가이 업서라
다만 그것만을 바래고 그립어 하든 내가 아니엿든가...  .＜142＞
