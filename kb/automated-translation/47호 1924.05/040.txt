Publication Date: 1924년 05월 01일
Article Title: 詩村建設의 第一義 (The Primary Meaning of Building a Poetry Village)
Author: 許竹齋 (Hŏ Chukchae)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Essay)

The Primary Meaning of Building a Poetry Village
						By Hŏ Chukchae

All civilization of this era is merely a boast of money. Therefore, the key to the era's civilization is also only grasped in the hands of the rich. Ah! Does the civilization of the era truly end with just boasting about money? This represents the inner thoughts of the multitude of poor people sighing on the brink of death (死線), and it is the focal point of what is deeply lamented (恫*). However, wealth (富) will not always continue its comfort on its pedestal of pride (驕臺), nor will poverty (貧) always endure hardship on its path of humiliation (凌路). The universe is a treasure house (富庫) of inexhaustible riches. Therefore, this earth fundamentally supports the possibility of gradually becoming a paradise (樂園化)—no, a Poetry Village (詩村化)—along with the realization of the "common." However, it is from the futile conflict on the path of status (姓路) that the original truth centered on humanity was forgotten. As individual desire (慾釗) and societal corrupt customs (弊風) caused conscience to be lost (喪) and Heaven's mandate (天命) to be harmed (損), the inexhaustible treasure house became severely divided. Thus, the original Poetry Village was sealed off (沒閉). Although the treasure house and the Poetry Village are the common property of humanity, the key (鍵) to the treasure house was allowed into the devil's possession (所弄), so opening the flower gate (花閂) of the Poetry Village has become entirely uncertain (茫然). Consequently, humanity came to lament (歆歎) beside the wall of the treasure house and wander (방황) outside the gate of the Poetry Village. Accordingly, money (金錢) came into use, and names (姓名) came to be distinguished. Thus, pride (驕) appeared (頭現) among humans. From the time money was manufactured, deceit (姦溪) was washed in exploitation (欺汗), and from the time names were established, the dust of power (勢塵) was shaken (振*) in the wind of resentment (怨風). Clouds of luxury (奢雲) arose spontaneously from ponds of wine and forests of meat (酒池肉林), and struggles for power (權拳) were uniquely tested in petty disputes (蠅頭蝸角). As the volume of money and names emerged and disappeared in this sea of fire (火海), waves of jealousy (嫉猜), fraud (邪欺), robbery (强盜), embezzlement (橫領), adultery (姦淫), murder (殺人), and strife (爭鬪) arose layer upon layer (層生疊出). These things gradually became the motivation (動機) to establish (設) a Poetry Village on this earth. Seeing such forms (꼴) in the phenomena (現象)
of this confusing world (塵間), one cannot help but cry out for even
No Money, No Name-ism (無金錢 無姓名主義).
Using money is nothing more than (不外) a representation of trust (信代) for exchanging between haves and have-nots (有無相通), and distinguishing names is a target (목표) for pointing out duties (의무), so its benevolence (仁德) should be esteemed (是尙). In other words, the correct understanding (正解) would be that money was established to express the amount (多少) of labor (勞) in human society, and names, the length (長短) of merit (功). However, contrary to this (是に反하야), the way of humanity (人道) has become increasingly desolate (索莫). Those who accumulate much (多聚) money are called rich (富), and those who blindly rush forward (盲進狂奔) on the path of desire's pit (慾壑의 傾路) solely for their own profit (榮利) are called clever figures. This is the common view (通觀) of the modern era. However, the observation of those whose insight (主見) is extraordinary (超凡) attaches a different label (別號) to wealth to avoid this. Even those who occupy the abundant wealth (饒富) of Yidun (猗頓) or the gold mines (金穴) of the untitled wealthy (素封) are pointed out (指目) and condemned (唾罵) as treacherous rich (姦富) if they are as avaricious (吝) in spending as they are keen (走) on profit (利), deceiving (欺) people and coveting (貪) wealth (財). Look! Within our grounds (터뎐) seen in the modern era, not only do they fail to foresee resignedly (儚然히) the ebb and flow (漲落) of various trends (風潮) and make it their life's great work (大事業) just to fill their own bellies (飽腹)—no, just their single bodies (一身)—but in that very appearance (꼴아지), what is so great that they are self-important and self-deceiving (自尊自瞞), struggling (끌먹대고) only to expand (擴拓) their own path of status (姓路)? How is this not just deceiving the general trend (大勢)? While they readily look up the ancient sages' teachings (垂訓) such as "To be rich is to lack benevolence" (爲富不仁), "When wealth is gathered, the people scatter; when wealth is scattered, the people gather" (財聚則民散하고 財散則民聚), and "Wealth and goods are tools for inviting disaster" (財貨는 取禍之具), they merely retail (小賣) their own arrogance (矜肆) just to hear the title (稱號) of 'rich'. For that arrogance to secretly import (輸入) tears (淚) of resentment (怨恨) from the poor (貧), what a sinister calculation (酬酌) it is for the construction of the Poetry Village! Money is the root of pride (驕根) and the ladder of resentment (寃梯); pride is the mother (母) of all evil (萬惡), and resentment is the tears (淚) of the myriad people (千姓). Just seeing such sights, one cannot help but advocate No Money, No Name-ism—no, even No Money, No Name-ism. This world (日下) is surely not just the world of the rich. If the myriad people are to be submerged (沈淪) for the sake of a portion (一部) of humanity living well, as long as the truth determined by Heaven (天定) is not eternally cancelled (取消), it is a fixed rule (定則) that those myriad people will not permit (許與) it. A cornered dog (困狗) bites (咬) people, an exhausted horse (疲馬) defies (凌) the whip (箠), and a desperate bird (窮鳥) enters (入) one's bosom (懷); why do those under the name (名稱) of 'rich,' who possess ears, eyes (耳目), and hearts (心臟), not deeply consider (淵量) the trend (趨向) of the times and reconsider (再思)? When the poor meet the rich, they needlessly sell (賣) a demeanor (行色) of servility (비굴); the rich, unable to grasp the situation (속 못 차리는), only try to sell their own joy (喜悅). Ah! The joy of the rich is the flower (꼿) of the poor's resentment. In other words, the laughter (笑) of the rich (富) is the tears (淚) of the poor (貧). Wouldn't it be consistent to weep together (가티) when weeping and laugh together when laughing? On the single road (一路) of this transient inn (逆旅), where could such unofficial, wicked mischief (작난) exist? Ah, you who love only to hear the name of rich (富名)! Have you ever imagined (상상) why the poor do not spare their respect (尊視) for those bearing the name of rich? Is it out of flattery (謟) for the imposing air (威氣) of your faces (면목)? No—isn't it because of the money rusting (녹 슬는) in your hands? But the wealth of a gutless young master (無膓公子) is unknown. <18>
No, the wealth of the crab-like (蟹的), heartless (沒情), dazed rich man (벙벅중이의 富者) remains cold (冷然). If there were rich people who thoroughly understood for what purpose (何에 在) it is necessary for a human being to accumulate much (多聚) money, I declare (斷言) that our lives (살님) would not have become so wretched (볼픔업시). Why do we live in the modern era (現代)? It is to resurrect (부활) the initial Poetry Village—no, to restore (還元) the human standard (사람 本位), to save the era. Why do you seek to destroy (破滅) the life that will flow eternally for the sake of money that will rot away (썩어질)? Ah, the key to paradise (樂園) is hidden in the grass of status (姓草) containing the dew of money (錢露). Ah! Until the initial paradise is discovered (발견)—no, until the Poetry Village is built upon this earth (地上)—just as one cannot avoid treading through (踏破) the grass of status stained with the dew of money, it is the reason why one cannot help but demand (要求) the money seen from the circuit (回路) of Poetry Village construction. Abstracting from the phenomenon (現象) as it is, money has merely become
The marker (標目) that divides (分界) the noble and base (貴賤) of human life.
Money is a fluid entity (流動物); it does not stagnate (止滯) for the comfort (安逸) of a single individual (一個). The period during which it stagnates is precisely the time during which the rich person who temporarily holds (맛허 잇는) the money is expected to undertake worthy (떳덧한) projects (事業). Failing to use wealth and ultimately making it only useful to others is just like water that collected (괴이엿섯지마는) in my pond (웅덩이) but, lamenting (恨) the inability to use it well, remained stagnant (잠기고 잇서서) until the smell (내암새) of decaying life arose, unable to move (움찍을 못하다가), then fortunately found a channel (물길을 엇어) and collected in another pond, becoming a spiritual spring (靈泉) for the many thirsty (渴者), it is the same (一般). It is a fact (사실) that the life of a rich person who does not know this meaning (의미) will result in many defeats (敗北) on the line (線上) of the people's (민중) cultural development (문화 발전). However, if there is another person like Zigong Fu (子貢富 - wealthy like Zigong), it is also a fact that they will become eternally fortunate (행운아). Otherwise, money will harm (反害) that rich person and finally take (奪) even their life before departing. Is that all? It will publicly sell (公賣) insult (侮辱) even to all the descendants (後裔) of that wealth! How can this not be something for the rich person themselves to reconsider thrice (三思)? Gentlemen (諸君)! Listen to the cries of lament (哀怨聲) shouted by the many poor (貧) in your surroundings (환경). Perhaps because a veil of flattery (謟幔) lies between (隔在), that sound may not be heard (들니지 아닐는지도), but the money you hold (맛허 잇는) is always sensing things beforehand (미리 눈치를 채이고) and preparing (준비) to scatter (四散). That a wind of greed (釗風) blows within those cries of resentment (怨聲) is because of that money, so when money itself eventually takes out its anger (憤풀이) on you, it will be because of that very wealth you prioritize (第一條로 重寶視) that even your life <19> will be taken away (빼앗기고 마는 것이리라). Look! How can money merely serve as the marker (標目) dividing the noble and base? People judge (評) the grade (甲乙) of character (품격) by the possession of money; the rock of error (誤巖) is ten thousand fathoms high (萬丈). The *Jang엄 Won결 Gye* (莊嚴芫�偈 - likely 'Solemn Verse') states, "Contentment (知足) is the greatest wealth (第一富)"; the *Yugyo Gyeong* (遺敎經 - Sutra of bequeathed teachings) states, "One who knows not contentment (不知足者), though rich (雖富), is rich yet poor (富而貧)"; the *Shuoyuan* (說苑) states, "Wealth lies in knowing contentment (富在知足), nobility lies in seeking retirement (貴在求退)." How could that evidence (證) deceive (欺) us (吾人)? It is because one is wealthy yet fails to perceive (自覺) that contentment (足) that one persistently lives with the nickname (별명) of "money-grubbing slave" (守錢虜). Is this not a shameful name (陋稱)? Therefore, at a gathering (會場) where all the slaves (奴) are assembled (모힌),
The money-grubbing slave is the foremost slave (守錢奴가 一等 奴).
One cannot deny that the worldly suffering (塵間苦) we face is less a gift (선물) of modern civilization and more a curse (明呪) of the modern rich, to the extent that the life of the poor is indeed a solitary existence akin to "near death" (죽음가음). In other words, it is because of those foremost slaves that the lives of the many poor are wretched (참담하다).

In Han (漢), Ma Yuan (馬援) possessed several thousand head (數千 頭) of cattle, horses, and sheep (牛馬羊) and ten thousand *hú* (萬斛) of grain (穀). He lamented (歎), saying, "Generally, when accumulating wealth and property (凡殖貨財), what is valued is the ability to bestow and relieve (貴能施賑); otherwise (否則), one is a money-grubbing slave (守錢虜)." *Ryo* (虜) can be taken as synonymous with *no* (奴 - slave). Buddhist scriptures (佛經) speak of "hungry ghosts possessing wealth" (有財餓鬼). How could this evidence (其證) be mistaken (誤錯) regarding the poverty of this era? The evidence (有證) from Zigong Fu (子貢 富) states, "Poor yet without flattery (貧而無謟), rich yet without arrogance (富而無驕)"; and the response (有答) from Mr. Kong of Lu (魯 孔氏 - Confucius) states, "Poor yet joyful (貧而樂), rich yet fond of propriety (富而好禮)." As the meaning (義) is illuminated layer by layer (層照), "rich and thus fond of propriety" (富卽好禮) becomes mutually dependent (相俟) with "rich and fond of learning" (富而好學). Ah! You rich with copper eyes (銅目) and iron brains (鐵腦)! Transform and harmonize with the times (於變하며 時雍하야), shed the old shell (舊殼) of the money-grubbing slave, quickly leave behind the money standard (돈 本位) and return to the human standard (사람 本位). Let's live together (함끠 어울니어). Let's stop acting as harsh grain merchants (苛酷한 穀物商) from now on. And let's weep together (가티) with those who weep. Let's laugh together (가티) with those who laugh. Let's understand well the outcry (아우성 소리) saying, "Let's build the Poetry Village together," realize it, and prepare a worthy banner (名旌거리) – this, I think, is the work of the peerlessly (無雙) wise (智者). Why, possessing considerable wealth (富), do you intend to live under the target (목표) of "money-grubbing slave," feeding off (긁어먹고) the gaunt cheekbones (야윈 광댓뼈) of the many poor, and end (맛치우고) a lifetime amidst the pointing fingers of hatred (미움의 손가락질)? Why become a hazy object of the era (時代의 希迷物) and an outwardly sound invalid (멀정한 病身)? Ah! You *algyŏng* (臬獍 - mythical beast symbolizing wickedness) of society! You *odu* (螯蠹 - destructive pests) of human life! You poverty-eating insects (食貧蟲) of the farming villages! Ah! You goblin-like (魑魍的) grain merchants! That the pivot (樞) of cultural development precisely awaits (苦待) your awakening (覺悟) is clearly proven (確證) by present reality (현실) and history (역사). More than anything, from our standpoint (처지), your awakening is the urgent task (急務). Instead of curtailing (節畧) your unnecessary expenses (冗費)—no, interrupting (中折) the expenditures you consume in the world of drinking and women (酒色界)—quickly
Establish educational institutions (敎育機關을 建設함이 잇스라)!
Let's quickly build educational institutions right where you have established your position (위치). Education is a loud cry (高喊) for everyone to live together. Because there are too many losers (劣敗者) in this world (塵間), education is advocated to cultivate (冶成) everyone into winners (優勝者)—no, with the aim of everyone living well together. The root (本) is established because of the branches (末). We certainly do not buy (買入) anxiety (煩悶) and suffering (고통) because of money. That fellow who says he lives for money is actually worse than money itself. Why did we come to require money? Rather than saying a peculiar creature (別種物) called the money-grubbing slave came to lie hidden (雌伏) among humans, it means that because of that something called the branch (末), we seek that something called the root (本). Therefore, for this era, I want to repeat (반복) the ancient example (例) of "Virtue the root, wealth the branch" (德本財末). The initial need (要) for wealth (財) is solely (不外) for the ultimate purpose of establishing (樹) virtue (德). If one has wealth but ultimately fails to establish virtue, this is dead wealth (死富). If one wishes to attain (取) life (生)—no, eternal life—one must rule over many lives with formless (無形) assets, that is to say, with benevolence (仁德). This is truly the work of the wise (智者). All civilization of the era is nothing but the handiwork (手品) of the wise; how could it be merely captured (捕虜) momentarily by the boast of money? If you wish to become an eternal property owner, not a hasty (*急) one, you must quickly build schools in the cities (都會) and villages (鄕村). Nurturing people (人을 양육하는 것) is precisely the shortcut (捷徑) to becoming a worthy person of character (인격자) myself. One must understand (양해) the pitiable (可憐) state in which our contemporary youth wander (徜徉) regarding their future (前途). The thread of money, the clue to the path of life (生路) tied (매인) in your hands (手中), stubbornly refuses to remain solely yours. If you have come to your senses (정신을 차린), quickly find a way to manage (變通) it before that thread's clue shifts and gets untied (옴겨 풀니기 전에)—no, before it aimlessly moves elsewhere (딴 군대로 덧업시 옴겨 가기 전에). If you wish to utilize (활용) temporary assets (一時의 有産) to create an eternal vessel of life (永遠의 生命器), first invest in and assist (投助) the educational world (교육계). If you have the intellect (두뇌) to imagine (想像) the indignation (憤) of Bao Jiao (鮑焦), who proclaimed (宣傳) millennia of resentment (千古의 怨恨) to humanity from upon the Luo River (洛水), do not bury (埋葬) yourself upon the spit (가래침) of humanity by neglecting (忽諸에 附함으로써) the essence (骨子) of these words. Humans are higher animals (高等動物) transcending beasts (禽獸) inasmuch as they do not live by "bread" (빵) alone. Money is the shield (방패) of the flesh (肉), and knowledge is the news (소식) of eternal life (永生). Humans require (要) money to open up (개척) the path (經路) of knowledge, and they require knowledge to build (건설) the Poetry Village (詩村), the forest (숩) of eternal life's truth. Ah! Key (쇠여) to the era's development! <21> If you have awakened (覺悟), establish educational institutions! And please, for goodness sake,
Do not solely engage in acting like an inhuman landlord (非人間的 地主)!
Throughout Korea (槿域), the problem between landlords and tenant farmers (소작인) has become extremely complex, just as the count (算表) of tenants groaning (呻唫) with the harsh winds and cold mists (酸風寒烟) occupies the vast majority (最多數). The ingenious plan (妙案) to correctly or incorrectly answer that difficult question (難題) can only be made by the landlord. According to recent surveys (調査), within Chosŏn's (朝鮮) total area (총면적) of over 20.3 million *chŏngbo* (町步), forests and fields (임야) constitute over 16 million *chŏngbo*, grasslands and uncultivated land (草生未墾地) over 70,000 *chŏngbo*, and the rest is purely cultivated land (純耕作地), within which paddy fields (畓) are over 1.54 million *chŏngbo* and dry fields (田) over 2.78 million *chŏngbo*. The farming population (경작인구) consists of 2,653,776 Korean (朝鮮人) households (戶) with 14,074,198 people (人), and 12,110 Japanese (日本人) households with 40,175 people. If the total population (人口 總合) of 14,114,373 people is divided equally (平分), it amounts to merely one *tanbo* (一段步) per person. Three-quarters (4분의 3) of the entire population are poor people (貧民); who holds the key (열쇠) to their relief (救濟)? Do you even know how much the pitiful life of the poor (貧民사리), who have nowhere to appeal (호소) even if beaten (마저) to death, shoots glares (눈총을 射하는가) towards you? Although humans certainly do not live for "bread" (빵) alone, it is a fact that failing to prepare "bread" first causes a great hindrance (大碍) to building character (인격). Thus, if there is anyone who thoroughly observes (철저히 관찰하는 者) our current situation (現象), just as one landlord lives because of many tenant farmers, because of one landlord, many tenant farmers have no time (겨를) to live with spirit (氣를 펴고). Even more than education, until the "bread" problem is completely resolved, the masses (뭇 민중) cannot be settled in peace (安堵에 徙치 못할 것인 것만콤). In our situation, landlords must surely engage in deep thought (깁흔 사고) and find answers (해답). What causes people's unrestrained (跅弛) behavior and violent (椎剽) actions (事爲)? As members of the same human race, why can they not be magnanimous and outstanding (濶達俶儻), but instead harbor (含) resentment piercing the heavens (徹天의 恨) and become slave-like (隷性化)? What is the precise reason (理由) for this? If not caused by poverty (貧한 所致), it must be the poisoning (茶毒) by the money-grubbing slave (守錢奴) who treads upon (蹈伏) this world (塵間) under false pretenses (假). Seeing the cruel and virtueless (殘忍薄德) landlords, rotten for ages (千古朽), one cannot help but cry out for the destruction of this humanity. Why do they try to stir (風動) this forest of suffering (苦林) with such a foul smell (내암새) that deceives the surface (皮相)? Seeing the cohort (또래) of the era,
Wealth (富) is about to be regarded as rot (腐). <22>
While the landlord's granary (창고) overflows with grain (百穀盈盈), singing praises (謳歌) of peace and bumper harvests (歲和年豊), the tenant farmers cannot avoid starvation on the roads (道路에서 莩餓를 不免). They hurry (奔走) to the western fields (西疇) and toil (犇汨) in the southern acres (南畝), unable to assert (伸張) their human rights (인권) or establish (확립) their character (품격). Ah! "Great wealth is great thievery (大富는 大盜), small wealth is small thievery (小富는 小盜)" must be a saying referring to the rot (腐) of the modern era. Who tests (試) their knives (刀) at the gates of the rich (富門)? Who hangs (懸) chime stones (磬) on the walls of their rooms (室壁)? Who sells (賣) resentment (怨恨) for a thousand years (千載) and creates (作) a temporary evil soul (一時의 惡魂)? It must be because the food problem (食문제) itself has not been clearly resolved first. Then, if there is just plenty to eat, does that mean one has fulfilled their human duty? No, it is not like that. How could all (모도) wealth be rot? There are those who only know how to take in (收入) but, being stingy and mean (然瓜吝劣), do not know how to utilize (활용) it; this is akin (一般) to taking in food through the esophagus (食道) but failing to digest (消化) it, leading to blockage (秘塞) and the decay (부패) of the five organs (五臟). Look at the Dead Sea (死海) in West Asia (西亞). Why did it come to be cursed (咀呪視) as the Dead Sea? Is it not said (일음이 아니뇨) that it is because the four rivers (四水) only converge (집중) there, and its outflow path (流路) has no passage (變通이 업슴)? Will the stubborn heart pillar (强執한 心柱) be broken (欺摧) just because the era rejects (排斥) those who speak sharply (骨鮫히), straightforwardly (峭直히), and meticulously (覶縷)? We must sincerely (眞덥게) know it is the duty (의무) of this human life that we cannot stop crying out (절규) until true justice (眞正) arrives. If those with志 (志者) do not give each other hints (귀띄임) for the sake of life (生) and duty (의무), everyone resonates (共鳴) that this earth (地上) will turn into hell (地獄化). As beings of the same human status (人位), why should some esteem (是尙) extravagance (豪强) in flowery gates and cloud pavilions (花門雲亭) with brocade clothes and jade meals (錦衣玉饌), while others cling (猶執) only to poverty and hardship (彫窶) in shabby huts and snail-shell houses (蓬戶蝸屋) with paper rags and coarse soup (紙鶉藜羹)? If Heaven's decree (天定) were so, I would never believe (信) in creation (造化). Alas (於乎)! O humanity! For the sake of the dung-tasters (嘗糞의 徒 - base flatterers) in this world (區宇), do not attach yourself to the ridicule (笑) of the small bird (斥鷃) or the mockery (譏) of the mountain-carrier (負山). As the great transformation (洪化) of the world situation (世界大勢) spreads culture (문화) with the force of wind sweeping and water following (風靡水從의 勢), why do you suddenly (奄然히) dominate (跋扈) the era, hesitate and hide alone (猶預孤隱) under the fence of evil desires (邪慾의 籬下), and intend to self-destruct (自滅) your life through futile (브즈럽은) hidden calculations (暗算)? If you spit (唾) towards Heaven (天), where will the shame (羞) fall (落)? Why can you not remember (根覺) your tadpole days (올창이쩍) and make it your best policy (상책) to scorn (蔑視) the poor? Was not wealth (富) produced (産出) because of the poor (貧)? Why do you not know why things like 'self' (自家) and 'civilization' (문명) were established (성립) and how they came about (出來), but only stir up (風起) the dirty smell (더럽은 내암새) of decay (腐敗), making life difficult only for the poor? You who boast (自誇) only of wealth while deceiving and selling (欺賣) the poor! When the poor recover (회복) their fundamental rights (근본적 권리), where will those flames (火焰) spread (亘張)? The time for your heads to turn (회전) is gradually approaching (조촘조촘 오고야 말려 하도다). This era will definitely not tolerate (容與) only your unconscious stance (無意識한 그 입장) indefinitely; even if the Jingwei bird (精衛) tried to fill the sea (塡海), would it be possible (가능)? Can mere wealth (如干의 富) intoxicate (渾醉) the all-encompassing civilization of the spirit? Ah, ah! You detestable and abominable (可憎可惡) rotten bags (腐帒)! Do not wait (待) for the later example (後例) of small greed leading to great loss (小貪大失) to repent (懺悔), but support (扶) the tenant farmers and reach a worthy understanding (떳듯한 諒解).
And from now on, do not live for the rotten bag (腐帒)! The flames (炎) of the poor's ominous voices (不祥스럽은 音聲) scorch the heavens (天을 鍋煼하도다). The day when Heaven grows angry (怒) and takes its revenge (憤풀이) on you is not far off. Ah! Wealth (富)! Pioneer the path (行路) of coexistence and co-prosperity (共存共榮) with the poor (貧). I assert that all affairs (일거리) of this world (世間) are due to wealth, and thus distinctly (鼎然히)
Five types of wealth (五富) are mixed up (뒤석겨).
There are five types of wealth, clearly divided (儼然히 五派로 分立), namely: First (一曰), the Fraudulent Group (騙欺沠); Second (二曰), the Land-Holding Group (守地派); Third (三曰), the Vice-Purchasing Group (買淫派); Fourth (四曰), the Power-Abusing Group (弄權派); Fifth (五曰), the Dutiful Group (義務派). This lists them according to the order (次第) of their paths (經路). The Fraudulent Group tests (試) the ignorant masses (愚氓) of the era and, through strange and cunning (怪妙的) means, steals (盜得) money instantly, affecting (事) an air (風度) of disguise (假面); the enrichment (致富) of swindlers (挾雜輩) is this type. The Land-Holding Group is also called the Miserly Group (吝嗇派). They make it their life's great work (大事業) solely to pass down (繼承) their ancestral property (祖業) but are oblivious (沒覺) to the times. They are seemingly virtuous but malicious (似德而凶). To give an example (一例): when the old grain in their granary has rotted (陳穀殘腐) to the point where it is unpleasant (언짠코) for them to eat and unsightly (볼성 사나웁게) even to offer as off-colored goods (色거리라도 내여놋키가), under the placard of praise (美榜) of supposedly giving to the starving people (饑民) as if possessing some benevolence (仁德), they call (嘯) their tenant farmers and distribute (配與) a few *mal* each for free. After thus gaining temporary fame (好名) and perhaps fortunately getting an uncalled-for stone inscription (碑文) erected, the following year (翌年) during the land assessment (看坪 때), they levy excessive rent (賭租를 例外로 濫俸) to certainly make up for it (보충을 기어히 하고야 마는) – this is hidden wealth (陰富). Then, the Vice-Purchasing Group consists of those who get rich (致富) through unexpected windfalls (意外의 橫財) midway between the Fraudulent Group and the Land-Holding Group. The Power-Abusing Group embodies (軆) the luxury (奢) of Solomon (솔로몬) and blatantly tramples (蹂躙) the human rights of the poor. However, these types belong to the stream (流) of those whose steam of arrogance (驕의 김) cools before they merely lose their wealth in vain, end up empty-handed (맨손만 쥐고) in their own generation (當代), glare at their own downfall (敗亡), and return (歸) to poor alleys (窮巷). However, the Dutiful Group represents the wealth of the considerably educated class (상당한 지식계급의 富). They insist on a thorough insight (洞見) into the era and encourage (獎勵) education, seeking to become human beings themselves (자기 스사로부터가 사람이 되고저 함에서), washing away (滌洗) the dust (塵) of the shameful name (陋稱) "money-grubbing slave" and the criticism (비난) "rotten bag," striving forward with determination (發憤勇進). Ah! Let me ask (뭇거라), how many (몃 個) rich people know their duty (의무)? If there is much consideration (參考) and deep understanding (淵諒) here, it will be a fortune (幸) for the poor, beginning (始) with the speaker (言者)... .
Amidst this commotion (惹端) of clamoring that there is no farming profit (農利가 업다고), and furthermore, regarding tenant farmers, amidst this chaos (뒤집박적 속) where even the price of soy sauce (醬價) is insufficient (부족) come the autumn settlement (秋算에 及하야), they still cannot find (尋) another path (他路), hesitate (어물정거리며), endure (참어가며), and continue living as farm slaves (農奴 노릇을 하고 지내는) <24> – that pitiable (可矜) life, from where is its price of sin (罪價) destined to be called forth (喚出하고야 말게만 되여젓는지)? Ah! Wealth (富), do you know? The only reason they continue tenant farming is to secure the capital for begging food from others (남에게 엇어먹고 사는 밋천), that is, treating it like a "debt" (債務가음). Forcing themselves to endure nausea (嘔逆질을 억지로 참어가면서), bearing the curse (咀呪) given by modern civilization, just acting like dazed fools (멀정한 벙벅중이 노릇을 하여가면서), dreaming (꿈꾸면서) of a distant line (一線) of happiness, though shameful (昌皮하나마), they continue their secretive, discontented lives (꿍꿍이 노릇만 하고 지내는 것일다).
Regarding these various tenant farmers (小作人에게 對하야),
the landlord must display a special perspective (特見을 연출하여야 하겟다). Regarding what is described below as an example of one hair from nine oxen (九牛一毛의 例 - i.e., a small part), I cry out (절규) for sound new establishments (건전한 新設) to replace the abandonment (집어치우는) of existing evil practices (在來의 惡弊). The general outline (大槪) of the points (條目) is as follows:
1. Instead of placing that figure (物形) called *Saŭm* (舍音 - manager/agent?), whether called a small landlord (小地主) or perhaps a fake landlord (假地主), whatever fits best, in a corner (區席) of the farming village and making tenant farmers cater to the dirty ways of other landlords, abolish (撤廢) such things. Instead, establish (창립) an Agricultural Promotion Society (勸農會) on the farm based on a system of mutual aid (相護의 제도) among tenant farmers. Through that society (會), attempt (試) harmony (원만) between landlord and tenant, and execute (집행) affairs using an impartial, public case method (不偏的 公案式). Accordingly, foster good customs (美俗을 釀成) by allowing self-governance (自治) even over the general corrupt practices (一般 弊習) of that farming village.
2. Towards diligent farmers (勸農者에게 향하야), besides providing higher rent shares (소작료를 더 厚하게 하는 외에), especially bestow (施與) annual prizes (年例의 賞品) to serve as a precedent (前鑑) for lazy farmers (惰農者), ensuring as much as possible (가급적으로) that no land (토지) remains uncultivated (荒蕪함이 보이지 안토록).
3. Mutually agree (互相協約) on a policy until the fields (田畓) eventually become the property (소유화하기까지) of the tenant farmers. Absolutely abolish (절대로 취소) the land assessment system (看坪制). Instead, excluding extraordinary circumstances like natural disasters (天災地變의 異兆를 제외하고), draft a plan (立案) for a fixed rent (的定 賭租) based on (據) 30% (3割) of the average year's output (平年 所出). The land tax (地稅) should be borne by the landlord (지주가 自擔하기로). Budgeting (예산) for 10 years, allow tenants to take possession (配當自占) through an appropriate land price equal division method (相當한 地價平分式). Nowadays, one can create a riddle (수수꺽기): "What only knows how to go up and doesn't know how to come down?" The immediate answer (뻑뻑이) would be: "The tenant rent according to the autumn assessment (秋筆) under the current land inspection (看坪上) of Chosŏn's rich." How can this not be lamentable, infuriating, painful, and detestable (可怪 可忿 可痛 可憎)?
4. The paddy field registration practice (畓案例) must be quickly corrected (速히 訂正). While vividly imagining (幡然히 想像하면서도) how debtors (借務者) deceived creditors (債權者) and reported false paddy registrations (假畓案을 呼한) <25> in the former dark and careless (暗黑無周到) era, implementing the false (假) as true (眞) is surely a deceitful act of covering one's ears while ringing a bell (掩耳搖鈴의 엉큼한 짓거리)? For the sake of the era's atmosphere (風氣), it should be settled according to the actual acreage (坪數대로만 풀어갈 것이로다).
5. The practice of "half-interest" (半長利例 - likely 50% interest) and especially the "doubling-interest system" (곱長制例) in farming households (農家) must be abolished (撤去). Disguising the practices (行色) of Korean-style usury (朝鮮的 高利業), unheard of in the world (세계에서 들니지도 안는), how about instead using the current regulation (現行規定) of the 30% rate (3割例)? This is also one of the crucial (大切) issues for farming households. Is it not a fact that as usury expands (高利業이 확장될스록), more and more poor people (貧民) will be produced (産出)? As this fog (迷霧) lifts and clears away, the Poetry Village will gradually open (조촘조촘 열니울 것이로다). Regarding the above five points (右 5個條), start (몬뎌 버롯하야) with what aligns with your heart, then sequentially (鱗次로) reform (개조하며), destroy (破壞식히며), and maintain (지속함이 잇스라). And let's build the ideal paradise, the Poetry Village, on this earth (地上).

The Method of Building the Poetry Village (詩村의 建設方)
What does this refer to? As long as the noun 'wealth' (富) exists (現出) in this world (塵間), a perfectly pure (圓潔) paradise cannot be built on our earth. To whatever extent possible, land should be equally divided (平分) according to the ancient well-field system (古代的 井田式). Diligence (勤勉) should be prioritized (主), methods of encouragement (獎勵方) established (建), and the education of descendants entrusted (附) to a unified national institution (國家 同一機關). Everyone should be unable to obtain (得) three meals (食) a day (1日) without laboring (노동). The *Discourses on Salt and Iron* (鹽鐵論) state, "A cornered rat bites a cat" (窮鼠가 齧猫라); the era well proves (증명) this analogy. As you all may judge abstractly (抽象으로 판정할 바와 갓치), I am a proletarian (無産階級者). However, I absolutely do not wish to act like a member of the propertied class (有産階級者). Even if I were to become one (된다손을 칠지라도), I fear (恐) becoming hideous (醜惡化). No, just as much as one is propertied (有産), one buys the resentful souls (怨魂을 買入) of the propertyless (無産); how could one live humanely (사람다웁게) like that? Just as the carnal desires (肉慾) of the propertied class always lament insufficiency (不足을 恨하는 것 만콤), the spiritual quality (靈格) of the propertyless (無産者) always yearns (憧憬) for the eternal, ideal national wealth (理想의 國富), being self-sufficient (自足), self-reliant (自大), self-progressing (自進), and self-creating (自造). Since ancient times (自古), those known as the enlightened (達覺) have well repented (懺悔) that things like benevolence (仁), righteousness (義), or love (愛) cannot coexist (붓허 잇슬 수가 업슴) with wealth (富)—no, with the evil rich (惡富) seen in reality. Therefore, Christ (基督聖) taught (訓을 垂하되), "It is harder for a rich man to enter (入) the kingdom of heaven than for a camel to go through the eye of a needle (針門)." This world is nothing special (別件). No matter how much one lives for one's greedy desires (壑慾), there is no special solution (別數는 업다). If one continues to live just like that, it would be better (나흘) to die early (일즉 死去). However, human life is the realization (실현) of spirit-flesh harmony (靈肉交化) possessing the potential (가능성) to build a Poetry Village on this earth; how <26> can one merely calculate the passing years (1年去 1年來) by counting how many times one laughs (笑) and weeps (哭), sit idly (무심히 안저서) waiting only for white hair (白髮), and boast only of old age (高齡)? Ah! From now on, let's somehow try to live a life with a solution (별수가 잇는 생활을 하여보잊�). Let's rejoice together (가티 즐기자) with the many poor who yearn (愛想) for the construction of the Poetry Village. Let's not just make a banner (名旌거리만 작만하지 말자) out of the rotten bag (腐帒) called "money-grubbing slave" (守錢虜).

In the countryside villages (野村), when harvest time comes and work is busy (일이 한참 밧불 때), they say, "Work is tight" (일이 째인다) and "It's chaos because there are no workers" (일군이 업서 야단이라). However, I say it's not the workers that are tight, but the money (돈이 째이는 것). If there were money, what would be tight? Humans came to use money (錢) simply to exchange (交用) valuable things. Money itself is certainly not valuable. It is merely using something base (賤) for the sake of something valuable. Base money (賤用할만한 錢) came to be used by humans to create valuable work (귀한 사업). To see money only as valuable and guard (직힘) it, thereby viewing one's own life as baser (賤視) than money—how is this not self-destruction (自滅) for the sake of the greater self (大我)? The farming village is the story (이약) of those who conquer nature, and the weeds (잡초) in the fields (田畓) are the laughter (웃음) of the poor people's lives. Ah! Let's make weeds our laughter. Let's make them our work (일거리). And let's build a clean nation (깻긋한 나라) without selfish desire (私慾) on this earth. Whether the Poetry Village is built quickly or slowly (늣게) depends (步武는 ... 如何에 잇다) on your awakening (覺醒). Now, from this moment on, let's just not value money more highly (貴視) than people. As much as possible, let's utilize it generously (넉닉히 활용하야감에서) and build a truly valuable Village of Life (生命村). Let this be the Method of Building the Poetry Village (詩村의 建設方).

These words were first chattered (呶呶) on July 20th of the year the rooster crowed at dawn (닭이 홰를 치던 해 - 1921 or 1922?) in my home village (本村) called "Meorime" (머리메). However, as human feeling (늣김) knows no end (긋침이 업는지라), upon encountering the spring of the Peninsula's (半島) paradise (樂園), where nature's spring hills (東山) open and myriad fragrances (芳香) nod their heads (머리를 달삭거리는), when the eternal Poetry Village comes to mind (聯想), based again on an old diary (묵은 日記), I will conclude for now (于先 이만 긋치고 마노라) with these first greetings (첫 人事) addressed solely to the wealthy of Chosŏn (朝鮮 富) regarding the primary meaning (第一義) of building the Poetry Village. <27>

**Summary**

Hŏ Chukchae argues vehemently against the materialism and wealth inequality plaguing contemporary Korean society, viewing money worship as the root of human suffering and social ills. He contrasts the arrogant, exploitative rich ("money-grubbing slaves," "rotten bags") with the suffering poor, particularly tenant farmers. Hŏ advocates for the creation of a "Poetry Village" (詩村), an egalitarian utopia based on shared resources (like the ancient well-field system), universal labor, and prioritizing human virtue (仁德) over wealth. He calls upon the wealthy to awaken to their social responsibility, cease exploitative practices (especially harsh landlording and usury), and invest in education and cooperative systems like Agricultural Promotion Societies as the first step towards realizing this ideal community. The article passionately critiques the current social structure and calls for fundamental reform rooted in humanism and shared prosperity.

For historians, this article is significant as a powerful expression of socialist or utopian ideals blended with traditional East Asian ethical concepts (Confucian virtues, Buddhist ideas of suffering and detachment) within the context of colonial Korea (1924). It vividly portrays the perceived injustices of the colonial economic system, particularly the plight of tenant farmers under exploitative landlords (both Korean and potentially Japanese, though Japanese landlords aren't explicitly singled out as the main target here). The call for "No Money, No Name-ism," the critique of wealth accumulation, the emphasis on communal living and shared labor, and the specific proposals for land reform (fixed rent, path to ownership, abolishing assessment systems) reflect contemporary anxieties and radical intellectual currents responding to colonial capitalism and social stratification. The author's explicit identification as a "proletarian" and his direct address to the "Wealthy of Chosŏn" highlight the class consciousness and confrontational tone present in some intellectual discourse of the period.

詩村建設의 第一義
						許竹齋
시대의 모든 문명은 돈 자랑뿐이다. 때문에 시대문명의 열쇠도 부자의 손에 움키여 잇슬 뿐이다. 아! 과연 시대의 문명은 돈 자랑에서만 그치우고 말을 뿐인가. 이것이 死線에서 한숨 짓는 뭇 貧民들의 속색임을 대표하야 甚히 恫*되는 바의 焦點일다. 그러나 富는 驕臺에서 언제나 그대로만 안락을 지속할 바가 아니며 貧은 凌路에서 언제나 그대로만 고초를 堪忍할 바가 아니리라. 우주는 無盡藏의 富庫일다. 그래서 이 지구도 딸어서 「콤몬」의 실현과 더불어 즘짓 樂園化할-아니 詩村化할 가능성을 근본부터 支持한 것일다. 그러나 부질업십 姓路에서 葛藤대는 그것에서부터 당초의 사람 본위의 진리를 망각케 된 것이라. 개인의 慾釗과 사회의 弊風이 양심을 喪하고 天命을 損함에서 無盡藏의 富庫는 頗히 分立이 되고 말엇다. 그래서 당초의 詩村은 沒閉가 되고 말엇다. 富庫와 詩村은 인생의 공통적 소유물임에 불구하고 富庫의 鍵을 악마의 所弄에 許케 되엇스니 詩村의 花閂을 闢하기는 아즉것 茫然케 되여진 것이다. 於是에 인생은 富庫塀邊에서 歆歎하면서 詩村門外에서 방황케 되엿다. 딸어서 金錢도 사용케 되엿스며 성명도 稱別케 되엿다. 그래서 驕라는 것이 인간에 頭現케 되엿다. 金錢을 제조할 때부터 姦溪에서 欺汗을 洗濯케 되엿스며 성명을 작정할 때부터 怨風에서 勢塵을 振*케 되엿다. 酒池肉林에서 奢雲을 自起케 되엿스며 蠅頭蝸角에서 權拳도 獨試케 되엿다. 金錢과 성명의 容積이 이 火海에서 出沒케 되면서부터 嫉猜, 邪欺, 强盜, 橫領, 姦淫, 殺人, 爭鬪의 뭇 風波가 層生疊出케 되엿다. 이런 것들이 즘짓 이 지구상에다가 詩村을 設케 하는 動機가 되고 말엇다. 現象에서 그런 꼴을 보아서는
無金錢 無姓名主義
까지 절규한나고도 아닐 수가 업스리만콤 雜紜한 塵間이라 하겟다. 金錢을 사용하기는 有無를 相通하자는 信代에＜17＞ 不外하며 姓名을 稱別하기는 의무를 指點하자는 목표임에서 그 仁德을 是尙함이 엇슬 것일다. 말하자면 金錢은 勞의 多少, 姓名은 功의 長短을 인간사회에 표현식히기 위하야 성립된 것이라 함이 正解일 것이다. 그러나 是에 反하야 人道는 작구작구 索莫케 되엿다. 金錢을 多聚한 者를 富라 하고 자기 혼자의 榮利만을 위하야 慾壑의 傾路에서 盲進狂奔하는 者를 똑똑한 인물이라 한다. 이것은 현대의 通觀읽 것일다. 그러나 主見이 超凡하다는 者의 관찰은 그러치 안키 위하야 富에도 別號를 附한다. 猗頓의 饒富와 素封의 金穴을 占據한 者라도 利에 走하는 것만콤 用에는 吝하야 人을 欺하고 財를 貪하는 者를 姦富라고 指目하야 唾罵한다. 보라. 현대에서 보이는 우리네의 터뎐안에서 뭇 風潮의 漲落을 儚然히 각오치 못하고 自家만을-아니 자기의 一身만을 飽腹하자는 그것으로써 일생의 대사업을 作할 뿐 不是라 그 꼴아지에서도 무엇이 잘낫다고 自尊自瞞하며 自家의 姓路만을 擴拓하려고 끌먹대고 잇슴은 엇지 이 大勢를 欺함이 아니다 뿐이랴. 「爲富不仁」이니 「財聚則民散하고 財散則民聚」이니 「財貨는 取禍之具」이니 하는 등 녯사람의 垂訓은 잘 차저보면서도 富라는 칭호를 듯기만 위하야 스스로의 矜肆를 小賣하나니 그 矜肆로써 貧에서 怨恨의 淚를 비밀히 輸入함이야말로 詩村의 건설을 위하야 얼마나 엉큼한 酬酌이다 뿐이랴! 金錢은 驕根이며 寃梯이니 驕는 萬惡의 母이며 寃은 千姓의 淚일다. 오즉 그런 꼴들을 보아서는 無金錢 無姓名主義-아니 無金錢 無姓名主義까지를 主唱하지 아닐 수도 업다 . 이 日下는 아마도 富라는 그네만의 세상은 결코 아닐 것일다. 一部의 인생 잘 살자고 千姓이 沈淪이 되고말을 것일진댄 天定의 진리가 永히 取消가 되고 말기까지는 千姓 그들이 許與치 아니할 것만이은 定則일다. 困狗는 人을 咬하고 疲馬는 箠를 凌하고 窮鳥는 懷에 入하나니 富라는 명칭 下에서 耳目과 심장을 가즌 그네들은 엇지 시대의 趨向을 淵量하야 再思함이 업는가? 貧民이 부자를 대하면 부즈럽시 비굴한 行色을 賣하나니 속 못 차리는 부자 그는 스스로의 喜悅만 팔고 말려 하도다. 아! 부자의 喜悅은 빈민의 怨恨의 꼿일다. 다시 말하면 富의 笑는 貧의 淚라 함일다. 울을 때에 가티 울고 웃을 때에 가티 웃서봄이 한갈지지 안을가? 逆旅의 一路서 엇지 그런 非公式的인 못된 작난이 어대 잇슭가? 아! 富名만 듯기 됴와하는 그네들이여! 무엇 때문에 貧民 그네가 富名을 띄인 그네들에게 尊視를 애끼지 아니하는지를 상상함이 잇는가? 그대네의 면목의 威氣에서 謟하는 것이일가-아니라 그대네의 손에서 녹이 슬는 金錢 그것 때문이 안일가? 그러나 無膓公子의 富는 몰으고 지내도다.＜18＞
아니 蟹的 富인 沒情스럽은 벙벅중이의 富者는 依然히 冷然하도다. 人이 되고서 金錢을 多聚하는 필요가 正히 何에 在한가를 철저히 이해할만한 富가 잇다하면 우리네의 살님이 이다지 볼픔업시 되여지지는 아니 하엿스리라고 나는 斷言한다. 웨 現代에서 사느냐고 하면 최초의 詩村을 부활식히기 위하야-아니 사람 本位를 還元식히기 위하야 시대를 살니기 위하야 사는 그것일다. 엇지 그대네는 썩어질 金錢만을 위하야 영원히 흘을 생명을 破滅식히려 하나뇨? 아 樂園의 열쇠가 錢露가 담긴 姓草 속에 감초여 잇도다. 아! 최초의 낙원을 발견식히기까지는- 아니 詩村을 이 지상에다가 건설하기까지는 錢露가 슬닌 姓草를 踏破치 아닐 수가 업는 것처럼 詩村 건설의 回路에서부터 보이는 金錢 그것부터를 요구치 아닐 수 업는 까닭일다. 現象대로만 抽象하야 본다하면 金錢 그것은 오즉
人生의 貴賤을 分界하는 標目
만 되고 말엇도다. 金錢은 流動物이라 언제나 一個의 安逸을 위하야 止滯하지 아니하나니 止滯가 되는 동안은 곳 金錢을 잠간 맛허 잇는 그 부자의 떳덧한 사업을 기대하는 그 동안이리라. 재물을 쓰지 못하고 맛츰내 남의 소용만 만들고 마는 것은 맛치 흘으는 물이 나의 웅덩이에 괴이엿섯지마는 그 물을 잘 이용할 줄을 몰으고 잇슴을 恨하는 그 물은 꼭 잠기고 잇서서 생명의 썩은 내암새가 나기까지 움찍을 못하다가 다행히 물길을 엇어 딴 웅덩이에 괴이어서 뭇 渴者들에게 靈泉이 되는 것과 一般이다. 이런 의미를 몰으는 부자의 일생은 민중의 문화 발전의 線上에서 만흔 敗北에 歸하고 말을 것은 사실일 것일다. 그러나 子貢富와 갓흔 또 엇더한 사람이 잇다 하면 영원히 행운아가 되고야 말 것도 사실일 것이다. 그러치 안흐면 金錢은 그 富를 反害하야 맛츰내 그 생명까지를 奪하고야 이에 떠날 것일다. 그다 뿐이랴! 그 모든 富의 後裔에서까지 侮辱을 公賣할 것이니 이 엇지 富의 자신을 위하야 三思할 바가 아니랴! 諸君아! 諸君의 환경에서 뭇 貧들이 불으짓는 哀怨聲을 들으라. 아마도 謟幔이 그 중간에서 隔在가 되엿기 때문에 그 소리가 들니자 아닐는지도 몰으겟지만은 그러나 그대네가 맛허 잇는 그 金錢은 언제나 미리 눈치를 채이고 四散하기를 준비하고 잇는 것일다. 그 怨聲 속에서 釗風이 불게 됨은 金錢 그것 까닭임에서 金錢 스스로가 즘짓 그대네를 향하야 憤풀이를 할 때에는 그대네가 第一條로 重寶視하는 재물 그것 때문에 그 생명까지＜19＞ 빼앗기고 마는 것이리라. 보라! 金錢으로써 엇지 貴와 賤만을 分界하는 標을 木만 만들고 말을 뿐이랴? 人은 金錢의 소유로써 품격의 甲乙을 評하나니 誤巖이 萬丈이로다. 莊嚴芫�偈에 曰호대 「知足이 第一富라」하며 遺敎經에 曰호대 「不知足者는 雖富나 富而貧이라」하며 說苑에는 「富在知足이오 貴在求退라」 하엿도다. 그 證이 엇지 吾人을 欺함이 잇스랴! 富하고도 그 足함을 自覺치 못함에서 꾸준히 守錢虜라는 별명을 가지고 지내게 되는 것이로다. 엇지 陋稱이 아니리오? 때문에 奴라는 奴가 모조리 모힌 會場에서는
守錢奴가 一等 奴
라고 아닐 수가 업도다. 우리가 당하는 塵間苦는 時代文明의 선물이라고 함보다도 時代富의 明呪이라고 아닐 수가 업스리만콤 貧의 생활여야말로 「죽음가음」의 외사리엇도다. 다시 말하면 一等 奴라는 그네 때문에 뭇 貧들의 생활이 참담하다 함이라.

小作人에게 對하야
지주는 特見을 연출하여야 하겟다. 이 아래에 九牛一毛의 例로써 記述한 바를 위하야 그 在來의 惡弊를 집어치우는 그 대신에 건전한 新設이 잇스라고 나는 절규한다. 그 條目의 大槪는 이러하다.
1. 小地主라고 할는지 假地主라고 할는지 무에라고 하엿스면 꼭 알마즐지 몰으는 舍音이라는 그 物形을 농촌 區席에다가 두고 소작인에게 딴 지주의 더럽은 꼴을 이바지하지 말고 그런 것들을 撤廢하는 그 대신에 농장에다가 소작인 相護의 제도로써 勸農會를 창립하고 그 會에서 지주와 소작인간에 원만을 試하며 不偏的 公案式으로써 사업을 집행함에 딸어 그 농촌의 일반 弊習까지 自治케 함으로 美俗을 釀成케 할 것일다.
2. 勸農者에게 향하야는 소작료를 더 厚하게 하는 외에 특히 年例의 賞品을 施與함으로써 惰農者에게 前鑑이 되게 하야 가급적으로 토지치고는 어데나 荒蕪함이 보이지 안토록 하여야 할 것이다.
3. 田畓이 장차, 소작인이라는 그네의 소유화하기까지 방침을 互相協約하되 看坪制는 절대로 취소하고 그 대신에 天災地變의 異兆를 제외하고는 平年 所出에 據한 3割의 例로써 的定 賭租로 立案하고 地稅는 지주가 自擔하기로 하되 10년을 예산하야 相當한 地價平分式으로 配當自占케 할 것이다. 현대에서 일종의 「수수꺽기」를 만들어낼 수가 잇나니 「올나갈 줄만 알고 나려갈 줄은 몰으는 것이 무엇이냐?」하면 뻑뻑이 「현대 朝鮮부의 看坪上 秋筆의 소작료라.」고 대답할 것이로다. 엇지 可怪 可忿 可痛 可憎한 바가 아니랴!
4. 畓案例를 속히 訂正식히여야 하겟다. 그 전에 暗黑無周到한 시대에 借務者들이 債權者들을 欺하고 假畓案을 呼한＜25＞ 그대로를 幡然히 想像하면서도 假를 以하야 眞으로 實施하는 것은 엇지 掩耳搖鈴의 엉큼한 짓거리가 아니!랴 시대의 風氣를 위하야 坪數대로만 풀어갈 것이로다.
5. 農家의 「半長利」例와 더구나 「곱長制」例를 撤去하여야 하겟다. 세계에서 들니지도 안는 오즉 朝鮮的 高利業의 行色을 變裝하고는 그 대신에 現行規定 그대로 3割例를 쓰는 것이 엇더할가? 이것도 農家를 위하야 大切한 문제의 하나이다. 高利業이 확장될스록 貧民만 작구작구 産出될 것은 사실이 아니뇨? 이런 迷霧가 들고들고 것치여가면서 詩村은 조촘조촘 열니울 것이로다. 右 5個條를 위하야 마음에 符合되는 대로에서부터 몬뎌 버롯하야 鱗次로 개조하며 破壞식히며 지속함이 잇스라. 그리고 이 地上에다가 理想의 낙원인 詩村을 건설하여보자.
詩村의 建設方
은 무엇을 이름인가? 富라는 명사가 이 塵間에서 現出되는 그 동안에는 우리의 이 地上에는 圓潔한 낙원이 건설되지 못할 것일다. 어데까지던지 古代的 井田式으로 토지를 平分식힘을 딸어 勤勉을 主하야 獎勵方을 建하고 자손 교육에 대하야는 국가 同一機關에 附케 하고 누구나 노동치 안코는 1日 3回의 食을 得치 못하게 하여야 하리라. 鹽鐵論에 「窮鼠가 齧猫라」하엿스니 시대가 그 비유를 잘 증명하고 잇다. 나는 여러분들이 抽象으로 판정할 바와 갓치 無産階級者이다. 그러나 有産階級者 노릇하기를 절대로 바라지 아니 한다. 만일 된다손을 칠지라도 醜惡化할가 恐함이다.=아니 有産인 그만콤 無産에서 怨魂을 買入하리니 엇지 그리고서야 사람다웁게 살 수가 잇스랴! 유산계급자의 肉慾은 늘 부족을 恨하는 것 만콤 無産者의 靈格은 언제나 영원의 理想의 國富를 憧憬하며 自足 自大 自進 自造하도다. 自古로 達覺이라는 그네들치고는 富=아니 현실에서 보이는 惡富들로 더불어는 仁이라는 것이나 義라는 것이나 愛라는 것들이 붓허 잇슬 수가 업슴을 잘 懺悔한 것일다. 그래서 基督聖이 訓을 垂하되 「부자가 천국에 入하기가 낙타가 針門을 通하기보다도 오히려 難하다.」 하엿다. 이 세상은 別件이 아닐다. 아모리 자기의 壑慾을 위하여서 산다 하여도 別數는 업다. 만일 그대로만 살아간다고 할 것이면 차라리 일즉 死去하고마는 것이 나흘 것이다. 그러나 인생 그것은 이 세상에다가 詩村을 건설하고야 말을 가능성을 有한 靈肉交化의 실현임에서 엇지＜26＞ 몃번 笑하고 몃번 哭하는 것으로써 1年去 1年來를 계산하며 무심히 안저서 白髮만 待하고 高齡만 自誇할 뿐이랴! 아! 지금부터는 아모쪼록이면 별수가 잇는 생활을 하여보잊�. 詩村의 건설을 위하야 愛想하는 뭇 貧으로 더블어 가티 즐기자. 守錢虜라는 腐帒로써 名旌거리만 작만하지 말자.
野村에서 추수할 때가 되야 일이 한참 밧불 때에는 「일이 째인다」고 「일군이 업서 야단이라」고 한다. 그러나 일군이 째이는 것이 아니라 돈이 째이는 것이라고 나는 말한다. 돈만 잇스면 째일 무엇이 잇스랴! 人이 錢을 사용케 됨은 귀한 것을 交用하자 함에 不外한 것일다. 錢 그것이 귀한 것은 결코 아니다. 귀한 무엇을 위하야 賤한 것으로써 활용하는 것뿐일다. 귀한 사업을 창조하기 위하야 賤用할만한 錢을 인간에서 사용케 된 것일다. 돈을 귀하다고만 보고 직힘에서 자기의 생명을 돈보다도 오히려 賤視함은 엇지 大我를 위하야 自滅함이 아니리오. 농촌은 자연 정복하는 者의 이약이며 田畓의 잡초는 貧民의 생명의 웃음일다. 아! 잡초로써 웃음을 삼자. 일거리를 삼자. 그리고 이 地上에다가 私慾이 업는 깻긋한 나라를 건설하자. 詩村이 바로 건설이 되고 늣게 건설이 되는 그 步武는 그대네의 覺醒 如何에 잇다고 하노라. 자 지금부터는 돈을 사람보다 더 貴視만 하지 말자. 될 수 잇는 데까지는 넉닉히 활용하야감에서 참으로 귀한 生命村을 건설하고 보자. 이것으로써 詩村의 建設方을 만들자.
