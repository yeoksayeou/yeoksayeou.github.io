Magazine Title: 개벽 제70호
Publication Date: 1926년 06월 01일
Article Title: 美展第五回短評 (Short Critique of the 5th Fine Arts Exhibition)
Author: 金復鎭
Article Type: 문예평론 (Literary/Art Criticism)

Short Critique of the 5th Fine Arts Exhibition
						Kim Pokchin

"Scales and Shells" (鱗介) by Mr. Yi Yongu
Overall, it is difficult to understand how this artist has come to this. Truly, he merely repeats the strategy of Sun Wu, advancing one step and retreating one step. Last year's "Scent of Earth" (土薰) was certainly not a complete work either, but one could see points of effort and intention. However, in "Scales and Shells," the colors are faded, the lines are empty and wild, and the composition is disordered, forcing the viewer to search for the painting within the painting. Yet, even if something is dead, should it be abused like this? In not showing any particular progress, one could say he is a painter of a different kind.

"Spring Warmth" (春暖) by Mr. Kim Kyŏngwŏn
I don't know if it's a common fault in this person's works, but it is a joyful thing that the extremely bad tendency towards realistic depiction (寫形癖) is somewhat lessened this time. In the *Hibiscus mutabilis* flowers, the white and purple varieties lack refined taste, and the technique in the leaf parts, due to a "family tradition" taste (家傳의 趣味), I see as a complete failure. There is an improvement in composition, but I wish he hadn't gotten caught up in the details.

"Sleep" (睡), "Spring Warmth" (春暖) by Mr. Yi Hanbok <104>

Seeing this person's various works submitted to perhaps this spring's Association Exhibition (協展), I wanted to weigh his brain matter. Such an artist can only be acknowledged for his existence as a specimen of vulgarization, mediocrity, and decline. Also, imitation itself is not necessarily bad, but for it to reach this extent, one cannot help but ponder. Whether the painting is successful or not is a separate issue, but even considering the honor of the government-academic school (官學派), wouldn't it be wise to withdraw it if withdrawal were possible?

"Spring Dawn in a Mountain Valley" (谿山春曉), "Green Mountains, White Water" (靑山白水) by Mr. Hŏ Paengnyŏn
They say truth flows. Before evaluating such paintings, the reviewer himself has too many thoughts, which is therefore one of the regrettable things for this artist.

"Early Winter" (初冬), "Layered Peaks" (疊嶂) by Mr. Yi Sangbŏm
I see him walking down a dead-end alley with quiet steps. Without rumination, leap, or dream, one could see it as merely repeating a habit (癖) ingrained in his hands. However, since one cannot find anything better than this in the Eastern Painting section anyway, his status is elevated, but "Early Winter" is an unfinished piece, and in "Layered Peaks," I wish the *sungnok* (宿綠 - a deep green pigment) of the front and back mountain ranges were distinct. Patching things up with a single stone pedestal or obscure thick fog is not a path we should gladly take.

"Old Temple Welcoming Spring" (古寺迎春) by Mr. No Suhyŏn
One gets a glimpse of a composition that can only be seen in Eastern painting. The season seems a bit early for an old temple welcoming spring, but the meandering stream and the protrusion of strange rocks give force to the picture plane, which could easily fall into monotony. It is appreciated that he showed more talent in sparing ink (惜墨) on the right mountainside and its surroundings.

"Spring Boudoir" (春閨) by Mr. Kim Kwŏnsu <105>

Generally, the hair of a Korean woman is not something that can be drawn so easily. Moreover, for this person to handle it readily, I know the burden is quite heavy. Also, I believe the profile standing pose is something to reconsider. One should draw whatever one pleases. However—I remember someone saying that duty follows freedom.

"Spring Light" (春光) by Mr. Yi Yŏngil
Carried away by brush inspiration (筆興), he ultimately lost vitality. Whether it's self-seduction or whatever, trying to extend last year's success as is would be laughable, and because there is no sense of depth, it makes passersby stop in their tracks.

"Autumn in a Secluded Valley" (幽谷의 秋) and "Barbarian's Dawn Journey" (野人曉行) are excluded. (End of Eastern Painting Section)

"Balsam and Younger Sister" (鳳仙花와 妹) by Mr. Yi Chint'ae
How could it be without flaws, but it is a delightful work. The artist is said to be still young in age. I can only hope he practices self-discipline. If asked to point out the most delightful aspect, it is that the cute girl's head is quite large. So why is it delightful? Because both the artist and the model are young, and that feeling is richly expressed.

"Spring Light" (春光) by Mr. Son Yongju
One must proceed on the left side. Even if it's just the beginning.

"Landscape of Ahyŏn-ri" (阿峴里의 風景) by Mr. Kim Chunghyŏn
Compared to last year's, the brushwork is praised as more skilled, but for some reason, the emptiness in the heart grows larger. <106>

"Still Life on Table" (卓上靜物), "Still Life" (靜物) by Ms. Paek Namsun
In color and shadow, in composition, in artistic intent, there are far too many people standing on the same line in the Chosŏn Art Exhibition (鮮展). Isn't the entire sentiment of a quirky artist not to follow others but rather not to allow others to follow them?

"Flower" (花) by Mr. Yi Haesŏn
"Early Spring" (初春) by Mr. Pak Myŏngjo
"Landscape" (風景) by Mr. Kim Yonghae
"Landscape" (風景) by Mr. Sin Yŏnggyun
"Landscape" (風景) by Mr. Ch'oe Seyŏng
"Still Life" (靜物) by Mr. Ham Sŏkkwan
"Suburbs in Spring" (春의 郊外) by Mr. Pak Sangjin
"Landscape" (風景) by Mr. Yu Chinha
"Still Life" (靜物) by Mr. Kim Yŏngsŏn
The impression is faint. The artist must bear full responsibility for this for the theory to hold.

"Southern Korean Girl" (南鮮少女) by Mr. Kwŏn Kuhyŏn
Let me just say that he treads a path prone to decline.

"China Town" (支那町), "Tianhou Palace" (天后宮) by Ms. Na Hyesŏk
She writes a long self-defense in the newspaper. From this, one can read that she lacks confidence in her work. As fellow female painters go, she appears more weighty than Ms. Paek Namsun, but in the lack of compelling realism (迫眞力), I believe it's hard to know the "weight of the tripod" (鼎의 輕重 - i.e., judge the relative merit). "China Town" <107> has no particular flaws nor things to request; rather than saying it's safe, it can be called a powerless work, and "Tianhou Palace," in its conception, I know to be womanly. I had heard that if early uterine disease (子宮病) were as painful as a toothache, one wouldn't know how many women's lives could be saved. Reading the newspaper evoked this memory, and let me just state that, beyond the right or wrong of the painting's clumsiness, I hold goodwill towards her for still gripping the brush.

"My Nest in Early Spring" (初春의 吾巢), "Still Life" (靜物), "Landscape of Kyŏngsŏng" (京城의 風景) by Mr. Yi Sŭngman
Each piece is scattered, evaporated, only the remains that have lost life adhere to the canvas. Looking first at the still life, it just tiresomely fatigues the viewer. The cause is the unfortunate combination of nature's diversity and the artist's pathological nervous excitement. In "My Nest in Early Spring" and "Landscape of Kyŏngsŏng" too, only the decadent taste and emotional extremities are visible, so what I request of the artist is to abandon such tastes and walk a path of purity. Let's see reality.

"Goldfish Bowl" (金魚鉢), "Cloudy Day" (曇日) by Mr. Chang Yunch'ŏn
I saw it as seeing once again the path Mr. Son Ilbong walked.

"A Certain Village of Freedom" (自由의 某村), "Chair" (椅子), "Musical Instrument" (樂器) by Mr. Kang Sinho
These are said to be works to keep a distance from. I nod at the boldness in natural modification, perhaps the taste is too noble, but I am discouraged by the signs of premature aging (早老). "Landscape" (風景 - likely referring to "A Certain Village of Freedom") must be an inferior work, and moreover, the expression of the village houses and trees is seen as childish. In "Chair," there is room for consideration regarding distortion (삐-루병 - perhaps referring to perspective issues or a 'beer disease' metaphor?), and in "Musical Instrument," I remember the mandolin possessed the most sense of reality. As works without any side effects, they draw the most attention in the venue.

"Self-Portrait in Winter" (冬에 自畵像) by Mr. Chang Sŏkhu <108>

Perhaps because all effort was concentrated solely on the effect of color, the drawing (素描) appears unstable.

"Fruit and Candlestick" (果物과 燭臺), "Still Life" (靜物) by Mr. Yi Ch'anghyŏn
I first offer praise for the refinement of color tones, but if the mystification of the objects is repeated once more, through meditation and intoxication, I have much dissatisfaction with his resulting self-alienation (遊離化).

"Snowscape" (雪景), "Landscape" (風景), "Still Life" (靜物) by Mr. Son Ilbong
I think he has achieved mastery in technique (技巧). Still, there are childish parts here and there, and concluding this is related to his age probably wouldn't be reckless talk. "Snowscape" is a completely failed work; "Landscape" has a rich sense of nature, but I wish the relationship between the figure's height and the telephone pole had been handled more kindly, and it's regrettable, shall I say, that balance was lost due to the white surrounding garment (白色周衣).

"Miss H on a Spring Day" (春日의 H孃), "Early Spring" (早春) by Mr. Song Pyŏngdon
Among figure paintings, especially those of Korean people, this was the most interesting. That doesn't mean there are no flaws, of course. For example, in "Miss H on a Spring Day," there's dissatisfaction with the shadows on the cheeks of the face and the plain yellow *jeogori* (저고리), and "Early Spring" is a work that will provoke much antipathy. In the artwork, the foolish plan (妄計) of attempting beautification using only color catches the eye. Let's see the specific characteristics of nature. And its interaction with human life—.

"Goldfish and Still Life" (金魚와 靜物) by Mr. Kim Ch'angsŏp
Compared to last year's, perhaps due to the climate, the goldfish look lively. Overall, perhaps because the affinity between objects is lacking, there is no unity—to put it shortly, the road is still long before it becomes a painting. Let me add one more word. Even beginners know that organizing everything under some main subject is more effective in painting than simply joining parts together. <109>

"Tableware" (食器) by Mr. Kim Panghun
The tableware lacks stability, the background color (빽빗 - likely 'background light/color') is underdeveloped, and these flaws cancel out with the fact that the artist is an elementary school student, leaving nothing behind.

"A Certain Landscape of Sopa" (小坡의 엇던 風景) by Mr. Chŏn Pongnae
I saw it as better than his sculpture. If he takes one more step forward, who knows how it will turn out; rather than being interesting, there is much danger.

"Landscape" (風景) by Mr. Kwŏn Myŏngdŏk
It cannot be helped. I will just write down that before writing the critique, comparing the elegant pen name 'Mukkiin' (黙祈人 - Silent Prayer Person) with the work was unpleasant.

(End of Western Painting Section)
"Head of W" (W의 首) by Mr. Chang Kinam
Overall, there is dignity (氣禀) and a focused gaze (凝視), but the lack of pressure (壓力) is lamentable.

"Face" (顔) by Mr. Yang Hŭimun
The expression is stiff, caught up in details, fails to become a single mass (塊), and lacks weight.

"P'yŏngyang Woman" (平壤婦人) by Mr. An Kyuŭng
Emphasizing mood (氣分偏重) in sculpture is a dangerous path. Let's just see it as it is and leave it as it is.

(End of Sculpture Section)
Unless they are my father's enemy, what grudge could there be? I simply look at your paintings and write about the paintings. If a sales contract falls through because of this critique, I give my word that I will take some responsibility and strive to mediate. <110>

**Summary**

This article is a sharp, often scathing critique by Kim Pokchin of works displayed at the 5th Chosŏn Art Exhibition (referred to as Mijŏn or Sŏnjŏn) in 1926. Kim reviews numerous paintings (Eastern and Western styles) and a few sculptures, offering blunt assessments of technique, composition, color, and artistic merit. He praises few, finding fault with many for lacking originality, depth, or technical skill, sometimes resorting to personal or metaphorical attacks. The critique covers established and emerging artists, including female painters Na Hyesŏk and Paek Namsun. Historically, this text provides a valuable window into the colonial Korean art scene, the critical standards of the time (influenced by both tradition and Western modernism), and the reception of the government-sponsored Chosŏn Art Exhibition. The direct, harsh tone and the mention of specific artists and trends (like imitation, "government-academic school," differing styles between Eastern and Western painting) offer insights into the artistic debates and power dynamics within the art world under Japanese rule. The mention of Na Hyesŏk's newspaper defense and the critic's final disclaimer about sales contracts also hint at the public and commercial aspects of art during this period.

美展第五回短評
						金復鎭
『鱗介』  李用雨氏作 대체로 이 作家는 엇더케 이와 가티 되는지 알 수 업다. 그야말로 一進一退의 孫吳의 兵法을 反覆할 따름이다. 昨年에 『土薰』도 물론 完成된 作品이라는 것은 안이지만은 그래도 노력한 점 하랴던 곳을 보앗섯다. 그런데 『鱗介』에 잇서서는 그 色彩가 바래고 線條가 虛荒하고 配置가 亂雜하여서 보는 사람으로 하야곰 그림속에 그림을 찻게 할 뿐이다. 그런데 아모리 죽은 것이라도 이다지 虐待할 것이야 업슬가 하야 엇더마한 進境을 보이지 안는 이 만콤 類다른 畵家이라고 할 수 잇다.

『春暖』 김경원氏作
이 사람의 作品에 通弊라고 할는지 모르나 極惡스러운 寫形癖이 이번 것에는 좀 적다는 것이 한 깃븐 일이다. 楊貴妃꼿에 잇서서 白, 紫 兩種은 고흔 맛이 부족하고 葉部의 手法은 家傳의 趣味로 하야 全然히 실패하엿다고 본다. 구도에 一進化를 보이나 그보다는 細部에 붓잡히지 말엇스면 한다.
『睡』 『春暖』 李漢福氏作＜104＞

今春協展이던가의 出品된 이분의 諸作을 보고 그 腦漿의 輕重을 달어보고 십헛섯다. 이런 作家는 俗化, 凡化, 墜落의 標本으로나 그 存在를 是認하여 줄 수 밧게 업다. 또 模倣 그것이 낫부다는 것은 아니다마는 이러케까지 된다는 것은 좀 考念을 아니할 수 업다. 그림이 되고 안된 것은 별개 문제로 하고 官學派의 名譽로 보더래도 撤回식힐 수만 잇다면 撤回식힌다는 것이 賢明한 일이 아닐가 한다.

『谿山春曉』 『靑山白水』 許百鍊氏作
眞理는 流動된다고들 한다. 이런 그림을 評價하기 전에 評者自身의 想念할 바 일이 넘어나 만흠으로 하야 그럼으로 이것이 이분을 위하야 섭섭한 일의 하나이 일 것이다.

『初冬』 『疊嶂』 李象範氏作
靜寂한 步調로 막다른 골목을 것는다고 본다. 反芻도 飛躍도 夢想도 아모것도 업시 다만 손에 익은 버릇(癖)을 되푸리 한다고도 볼 수 잇다. 그러나 엇재던 東洋畵部에 잇서서 이보다 더 낫다는 것을 찻지 못하는 것만큼 그 地位를 놉힐 뿐이나 『初冬』은 未完成品이고 『疊嶂』에서는 前後의 山脈의 宿綠을 分明하엿스면 한다. 石臺一基나 矇昧한 濃霧로 彌縫한다는 것은 우리의 질겨 取할 길이 안닐가 한다.

『古寺迎春』 盧壽鉉氏作
東洋畵에서 밧게 보지 못할 構想을 엿보게 한다. 古寺의 迎春으로 난 時節이 족곰 일는 듯 하다마는 그러나 迂回된 溪流 奇岩의 突起 이로하야 單調에 빠지기 쉬운 畵面에 迫力을 주엇스며 右便山麓과 그 주위에 잇서 더 만히 惜墨의 才技를 보이난 것은 고마운 일인 것이다.

『春閨』 金權洙氏作＜105＞
