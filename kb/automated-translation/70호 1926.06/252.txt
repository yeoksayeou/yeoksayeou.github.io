**Summary**

This text is the colophon or publication information for issue number 70 of the Korean magazine Kaebyŏk (開闢), published on June 1, 1926. It lists the editor (Yi Tonhwa), publisher (Yi Tusŏng), printer (Min Yŏngsun), printing company (Taedong Printing Co., Ltd.), and the publishing house (Kaebyŏksa), along with their respective addresses in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul). It also includes the telephone number and postal transfer account number for the publishing house. For historians, this colophon provides valuable concrete details about the individuals, businesses, and locations involved in Korean publishing during the Japanese colonial period, mapping out a part of the intellectual and commercial landscape of 1920s Seoul. The use of Japanese era names (Taishō) and Japanese-style addresses (like Ch'ŏngsu-ch'ŏng/清水町) also reflects the political context.
