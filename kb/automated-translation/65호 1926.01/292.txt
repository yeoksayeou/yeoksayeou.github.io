◎Advertisement Corrections
There were omissions or misprints in the advertisements published in *Kaebyŏk* No. 64 (Ŭlch'uk, December issue). We hereby correct them below and apologize that our proofreading was not thorough.

**Summary**

This article is an editorial notice from the magazine *Kaebyŏk* correcting errors found in advertisements published in its previous issue (No. 64, December 1925). The corrections cover various details, including phone numbers, names of individuals and businesses, addresses (moving locations between counties), and job titles within the magazine's own branch network announcements. The errors occurred in congratulatory ads celebrating the magazine's continued publication and the development or establishment of its branches in Kowŏn, Chejin, and P'yŏnggang. For historians, this notice provides specific details about businesses (pharmacy, hospital, brewery), individuals, and media organizations (Chosŏn Ilbo branch) operating and advertising in colonial Korea in late 1925. It also sheds light on the geographic reach of *Kaebyŏk* magazine's distribution network (branches/sub-branches) in areas like Kowŏn, Yŏnghŭng, Chŏngp'yŏng, Chejin (likely Wonsan area), and P'yŏnggang, and illustrates the practical challenges of print publication, such as proofreading errors.
