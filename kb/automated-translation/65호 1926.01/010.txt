Looking Around Both Outside and Inside the Borders

It is the New Year. It is New Year's Day. Today is the day when another year of time, the new year, is embraced before us.
Through last month's issue of this magazine, we have already briefly discussed the transformations of Chosŏn and the world over the past year, but what shape will things take this coming year? First, how will the major global situation unfold, and amidst that, what will be the state of Chosŏn? As I take up my brush and face my desk, my thoughts reach this point, and my heart cannot remain calm.

World affairs, and especially the changes in the times, can only be anticipated for the future by extrapolating from the present. Looking at the current state of affairs both inside and outside the borders (境內 境外), what do we see?

The masses of China, commonly called the "Hidden Dragon of the East" (東方의 潛龍), have begun to stir once again. With one cry, the central government (中央政*) has become revolutionized, and with a second cry, the Three Eastern Provinces (東三省) have fallen into chaos. Yesterday, General Kuo (郭장군) was leading Liaodong troops (率遼東兵) eastward, and today, the Japanese Empire (日帝國) dispatches its army and navy westward. Amidst this, a country like Russia (露西亞) provides covert assistance (暗助) on one hand while lodging protests on the other, causing observers to recall both the Sino-Japanese and Russo-Japanese wars. Furthermore, Britain and America (英米 兩國) watch covetously. The winds and clouds gathering in the East are indeed in no ordinary state. Of course, just because the situation is like this does not mean arrows and stones will immediately fly across the Yalu River (鴨江) or the Genkai Sea (玄海) will turn into mulberry fields (桑田), but even thinking deeply and repeatedly, isn't it a fact that this deserves our general attention and scrutiny?

Turning around to examine the internal situation (境內 內情), what do we find? Rather than trying to assimilate the Korean people or what have you, the Government-General of Korea (朝鮮總督府), which established a new ironclad plan for ruling Chosŏn since Taishō 8 [1919], decided that the best policy would be to completely manage them <2> in practical terms—that is, economically and culturally. During that time, due to the depression in the financial world—or rather, more than just a simple financial depression, being absorbed in maintaining public order within the borders (境內) and manufacturing the signboard of "Cultural Rule" (文政)—they had not fully achieved their desires. However, recently, as Japanese trade barely regained its balance and the financial world seemed somewhat revived, and now that some six or seven years have passed since the Kimi [1919] year, with various things becoming slightly plausible, they intend to execute the original plan conceived when Governor-General Saitō (齊蕂總督) arrived. What is that? It is the so-called "Industry First Policy" (産業第一主義) politics. It involves initiating large-scale enterprises and construction projects such as increasing rice production (産米增殖), expanding railways (鐵道擴張), and managing forestry and rivers (治山治水), while inducing many entrepreneurs (企業家) and property owners (有産家) from mainland Japan to effectively Japanize Chosŏn in practice. Look! Despite implementing policies of austerity upon austerity in Japan itself, such as budget cuts (豫筭 緊縮), non-issuance of bonds (非募債), and tax system adjustments (稅制整理), when it comes to Chosŏn, they have confirmed (though not yet passed by the Diet) an unprecedentedly enormous budget (豫筭) of 180 million won to be steadily implemented starting from the new fiscal year. Once this policy was confirmed, vitality revived among Japanese entrepreneurs. Places like the Oriental Development Company (東拓) are even revising their organizational structure in preparation, and other various visible and invisible Japanese financial cliques (日人側 財閥) are waiting expectantly, rubbing their hands with spit. So, what will be the result of this? From the perspective of the pure Korean populace (純 조선인 민중), what will this result be...?

There is no need to say much more here on this occasion for celebrating the New Year's blessings (新年 景福). More than anything, we who are Koreans must observe carefully and think carefully. Therefore, we must advance even more bravely and boldly than in any previous time. "Place them in a deadly spot and they will survive" (陷之死地而後生). There is not one iota of reason to shrink back from today's difficult circumstances (難境). Since the Great War, the direction of history has been set; there is only one thing to do: to share our destiny with the proletarian and powerless masses of the world (無産 無權의 天下 大衆).<3>

**Summary**

This 1926 editorial from *Kaebyŏk* magazine reflects on the precarious situation of Korea under Japanese rule at the start of the new year. It contrasts the volatile international scene, particularly the turmoil in China involving major powers, with the domestic situation where Japan's colonial government is intensifying its economic exploitation of Korea under the guise of development ("Industry First Policy"). The author warns that policies like increased budgets for infrastructure and rice production, funded even while Japan practices austerity at home, primarily serve to attract Japanese capital and further Japanize Korea, ultimately harming the native Korean population. The editorial concludes with a call for Koreans to remain vigilant, courageous, and align themselves with the global struggle of the oppressed masses. For historians, the article offers a contemporary critical perspective on the economic dimensions of Japanese colonial policy in the mid-1920s, specifically the "Industry First Policy" and its perceived impact on Koreans. It also highlights the awareness among Korean intellectuals of international power dynamics and their connection to Korea's fate, alongside an emerging socialist or class-conscious viewpoint ("proletarian and powerless masses").

境外境內를 돌아 보면서
新年이다. 元旦이다. 새해 1년이라는 세월이 또 한번 우리의 압헤 안겨지는 오늘이다.
우리는 일즉히 本誌 前달號를 통하야, 작년 1년의 조선 及 세계의 變態를 略略 說去 한 바 잇섯거니와 올해 1년에는 또 엇지될 모양인가. 第1 세계의 大局은 엇지 되며 中에도 조선의 형편은 엇지 될 것인가. 붓을 들고 書案을 대하야 생각이 이에 밋츠매 마음이 스스로 平然치 못한 바 잇다.
세상 일이란 더욱이 시국의 변천이란 현재를 미루어 장래를 想察 할 수 밧게 업는 것인데 境內 境外의 현재 형태를 본다 하면 또 엇던가.
世稱 東方의 潛龍이라는 중국의 민중은 또 한번 動하기를 시작하야 一呼에 中央政*이 革命化 하고 再呼에 東三省이 혼란화하야 어제는 郭장군이 率遼東兵而東하는가 하면 오늘은 日帝國이 出陸海軍而西하며 그 중에도 露西亞와 가튼 나라는 한편으로는 暗助 또 한편으로는 항의를 提出하야 보는 사람에 의하야는 넷날 日淸 日露의 兩戰役을 아울너 聯想케 함이 잇스며 게다가 英米 兩國은 호시탐탐하야 동방의 風雲은 사실로 尋常치 안은 상태에 잇다. 물론 형세가 이러타 하야 당장으로 鴨江에 矢石이 날고 玄海가 桑田이 된다는 것은 아니지만 주리고 또 주려 생각한다 할지라도 우리 일반의 응시 주시할 處가 되는 것 까지는 사실이 아니겟느냐.
도리켜 境內 內情을 살펴 보면 엇더한가. 조선 사람을 동화시키느니 엇저느니 하는 것 보다도 아조 事實上으로＜2＞ 다시 말하면 經濟的으로 문화적으로 經略해 버리고 마는 것이 상책이겟다 하야 大正 8년 以降 조선 통치의 新鐵案을 세운 조선총독부에서는 그 間 財界의 불황 단순한 財界의 불황이라 하기 보다도 境內의 치안유지와 文政의 看板 製造에 汨沒하야 12분 所欲을 未遂하다가 근래 일본 무역이 겨우 其 균형을 어더 財界가 자못 회생하는 듯 하고 己未 이후 어언 6, 7년을 지내여 이것 저것이 약간 그럴 듯 하게 된 오늘에 와서는 齊蕂總督 渡來 時의 腹案을 그대로 실행하려는 것이다. 그것이 무엇이냐 하면 소위 산업 제일主義의 정치란 것이 그것이며 産米增殖, 鐵道擴張, 治山治水 등의 대기업 대공사를 니르키되 일본 內地의 만흔 기업가와 有産家를 誘致 하야 사실상으로 조선을 일본화하자는 그것이다. 보라 일본 자체에서까지 예산 緊縮이니 非募債니 稅制 정리니 하야 절약 又 절약의 방침을 실행 함에 불구하고 至若 朝鮮 하야는 1억 8천만원이라는 曾前 未有의 尨大한 豫筭을 확정 (아직 議會까지에 통과한 것은 아니나) 하야 新年度 부터 착착 진행하려는 것을. 이 方策이 한 번 확정되매 일본인 기업자 간에는 活氣가 蘇生하야 저 東拓과 가튼 데에서는 職制까지 고치고 준비 중이며 기타 有象 無象의 日人側 재벌은 손에 침을 바다 가면서 等 待하고 잇다. 그러면 이 결과는 무엇이냐. 純 조선인 민중 側으로 볼 때의 이 결과는 무엇이것느냐...
구태여 新年 景福을 恭祝할 이 자리에 잇서 여러 가지 말을 할 것은 업다. 무엇보다도 조선 사람된 우리는 잘 살피여야 할 것이며 잘 생각하여야 할 것이다. 그래서 어느 시절보다도 더 一層 용감하고 대담하게 나아가야 할 것이다. 陷之死地而後生 이라. 一毫라도 今日의 難境에 窮縮할 것은 업다. 一自 大戰 이후 역사의 방향은 정하여 졋나니 오즉 無産 無權의 天下 대중과 더불어 運路를 가티 할 一事가 잇슬 뿐이다.＜3＞
