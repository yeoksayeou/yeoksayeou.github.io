餘言 (Concluding Remarks)

People of the world speak much of our recklessness. Out of 10 issues published in a year, eight were confiscated, one was suspended, and only two passed without incident—countless people rebuke us, asking where else such reckless behavior can be found. Thinking about how the authorities' intense pressure and coercion are so immense that they don't even need to speak twice, our recklessness is not entirely non-existent.

To tell the truth, editing this New Year's issue was done with as much caution as nursing the gravely ill only son of three generations. Reflecting on the past year and considering that the future will also be adverse, the tip of the editing brush does not move as freely as the heart desires. There's no need to invoke fatalism, but because illness has been so frequent, that too is a source of concern.

The past is the past, and the future is the future. There is nothing left but to exert our sincerity for the sake of the future, and above all, to our comrades throughout the world!
How were the accomplishments of the past year, and what plans are there for the new year? As we stand on the same line, it should be the same for both them and us, yet how earnest is the feeling of longing!

Chung* [China] is, without need for further words about peaceful New Year greetings, amidst tumultuous fighting just like us. Let's not view it as a fire across the shore, according to the saying "neighbors are friends." Furthermore, isn't the fall of the warlords and the rise of the "Reds" (赤色) even more interesting? Ponder the piece, "The Great Upheaval in China That I Witnessed," Part 1.

This year is Pyŏnginnyŏn (丙寅年). The commonly called "Year of the Tiger"! It would be good to read the piece "A Tiger's Lament in the Zoo" (動物園**虎狼이의 신세타령) meaningfully. "Six Views of the Mountain Lord" (山君六面觀) is also said to vividly present stories concerning humans in the Year of the Tiger.

What distinguishes this New Year's issue above all is the creative writing section. Wŏl (月), P'inghŏ (憑虛), Ip'ong (入峰), Sŏnghae (星海), Sŏhae (曙海), and Kiyŏng (箕永) all took up their pens. The valiant generals of the literary world have formed ranks simultaneously (䶀€時). The first creative works of the first year, spanning over 90 pages front and back! What a treasure this is! While expressing thanks to all the writers, we introduce them widely to our comrades throughout the world.

Finally, one more word. It is a request to understand and appreciate that, for the time being, there are circumstances we cannot speak of. How about briefly setting aside the question of the content's nature? It is already decided! Knowing it might be painful/bitter (씀‘해할 줄은 알면서도) ― I add this one word. <92>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the January 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* reflects on the immense challenges faced by the magazine under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting frequent confiscations and suspensions due to censorship. The editor expresses anxiety about the future but reaffirms commitment, calls for solidarity among comrades, briefly touches upon the political turmoil in China, and proudly introduces the creative writing contributions for the New Year issue, featuring prominent writers of the time. The piece concludes with a veiled plea for readers' understanding regarding unspoken difficulties, likely related to censorship or operational constraints. For historians, the article offers a stark glimpse into the repressive media environment of 1920s colonial Korea, the resilience of Korean intellectuals and publishers facing constant government pressure, their awareness of international events (like in China), and the coded language used to navigate censorship. The mention of specific writers and the sheer volume of suppression (8 confiscations, 1 suspension out of 10 issues) are particularly revealing data points.

餘言
세상 사람은 우리의 無謀를 만히 말한다. 1년 10個號 발행에 押取가 여덜번 停止가 한 번 無事通過는 게오 두 번― 이런 無謀읫즛이 어듸 잇느냐고 우리를 無數히 責망한다. 당국의 强壓高壓은 넘우 엄청나 그런지 말을 두번도 안한다 생각하면 우리의 無謀도 업는바는 안이다.

실상말이지 이번 新年號 編輯은 三代獨子 重病看護하듯이 여간 조심이 안이엿다. 과거 1년을 생각하고 장래 亦 逆境임을 생각하니 編輯하는 붓끗티 마음대로 안도라 간다. 運命說까지 붓칠것은 업지만 하도 병이 자즌지라 그 亦 念慮가 업지 못하다.

과거는 과거요 미래는 미래다. 오직 장래를 위하야 誠意를 盡할 밧게 업고 대관졀 滿天下同志諸君?
과거 1년의 업적은 엇더하며 새해 1년은 엇더한 經綸이 잇는가 同一線에선 우리인지라 彼我가 일반이겟지만 그래도 사모하는 마음이 간졀하구려!

中*온 過歲安寧을 더 말할 여지도 업시 저와 가티 퉁탕퉁탕 싸우는 중에 잇다. 「이웃은 벗」이라고 彼岸의 火로는 보아 주지 말자. 더구나 軍閥의 沒落「赤色의 加勢가 더욱 흥미잇지 안느냐 『내가 본 중국의 大動亂』 1편을 음미하라

금년은 丙寅年이다. 俗所謂 호랑이해!」다 「動物園**虎狼이의 신세타령」 1편을 뜻잇게 보면 조켓다 「山君六面觀」도 丙寅年 人間으로 의한 니약이거리는 歷歷하리라 한다.

이번 新年號에는 무엇보다 創作篇이 특색이다. *月, 憑虛, 入峰, 星海, 曙海, 箕永이 모다 집필 햇섯다. 文壇 驍將이 䶀€時에 陣을 成하얏다. 前後 90餘*의 첫해 쳣 창작! 얼마나한 보배냐! 執筆諸君에게 謝하는 동시― 널니 天下同志에게 소개한다.

최후로 한 마듸 더 한다. 당분간은 우리에게 말못할 사정이 잇는 것을 짐작 해아러 달나는 그것이다. 내용여하를 잠간 不問에 付해주면 엇더한가 발서다! 씀‘해할 줄은 알면서도― 한 마듸한다. ＜92＞
