Company Announcement / New Branch and Sub-branch Offices Established in December

**Summary**

This company announcement from the January 1, 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine details the establishment of a new main branch office in Kwangju covering the entire Chŏnnam (South Chŏlla) province, the restructuring of the Sŏnch’ŏn branch office (covering Sŏnch’ŏn, Kwaksan, Ch’ŏlsan), and the creation of a new sub-branch office in Anbyŏn. It lists the names and roles of the staff for each location, highlighting the magazine's efforts to expand its network for news gathering, reader recruitment, and advertising sales across different regions of Korea. For historians, this provides concrete evidence of the organizational structure and regional expansion strategy of a major Korean cultural magazine during the colonial period. It shows the geographic reach Kaebyok aimed for, encompassing both southern (Chŏnnam) and northern (Sŏnch’ŏn, Anbyŏn in modern North Korea) regions, and lists local personnel involved in its operations.
