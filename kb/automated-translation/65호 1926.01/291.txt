Company Announcement / Branches (支社) and Sub-branches (分社) Newly Established or Reestablished in December
◎ Ch'ungju (忠州) Branch Newly Established

◎ Taegu (大邱) Branch Reestablished
Location: 51 Kyŏngjŏng 2-ch'ome (京町 二丁目 五一), Taegu Prefecture (大邱府)

=Staff=

Advisor: Pak Chae-sŏn (朴載善)

**Summary**

This company announcement from the January 1, 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine details the establishment or reestablishment of three branches/sub-branches in December 1925: a new branch in Ch'ungju, a reestablished branch in Taegu, and a new sub-branch in Mun'ch'ŏn. It lists the location, operational area, and staff (including roles like branch head, reporters, general affairs, advisors, clerks, and bill collectors) for each new office. For historians, this notice provides valuable insight into the organizational structure and expanding reach of a significant Korean magazine during the colonial period. It demonstrates the magazine's efforts to establish a formal distribution and reporting network across different regions of Korea, listing specific personnel and their roles, which helps map the media landscape and potential intellectual networks of the time.
