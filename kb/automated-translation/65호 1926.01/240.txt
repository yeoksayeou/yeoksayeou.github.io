Article Title: 事件! (Incident!)
Author: 懷月
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Fiction)

Incident!
						Hoewŏl (懷月)

1

In the bruised purple sky, the cold wind howled and raced around. The weather, which had been growing colder since morning, did not let up in the afternoon and grew even colder.
P and Y, reporters for the H Newspaper Company, finished their work and stepped out of the newspaper office door, intending to go home. The cold wind, scattering small snowflakes, rushed wildly here and there.
P and Y walked about thirty steps together without saying a word. There, they met another friend named R. Thus, the three of them walked on in silence.
For P and Y, it was a daily occurrence, but come afternoon, everything felt tedious, and their hearts grew unbearably lonely. Especially on a day like today, after working so much, when lunch had been nothing more than two slices of bread and a cup of tea. The hungrier they were, the less interest they had in anything, and the hungrier they were, the colder they felt.
Walking silently, P thought to himself.
—Isn't there anyone who'll buy me a drink today?— He endured it for a while, but finally complained to the friends walking beside him.
"On a cold day like this, it would be nice to have a drink."
"Well now, what you said has quite some meaning." <11>
Y replied with a laugh.
"You guys deserve to be punished today. Didn't you drink quite a bit the other day? Others couldn't even last an hour, but you drank until dawn like this, so aren't you being punished?" P retorted.
"That's true, indeed!" Y said, sounding somewhat ashamed.
Once they left the office, they disliked going straight home. From four o'clock onwards, they were busy seeking their pleasures. They wandered from bar to bar, from lover's house to lover's house. If there was nothing else to do, they had to pass the time by facing each other, engaging in idle chat and jokes.
They, like youths who have lost the hopes common to young people, or unlike others who internally strive with personal responsibility towards Korean society or its small duties, lacked even those unavoidable aspects of youth. They only degenerated further into despair and discouragement. But today, they suddenly felt suffocated, as if they had nowhere to go.
R posed the question again.
"Well then, shall we budget for just about an hour of drinking today?"
However, P and Y were reluctant to spend their own money on drinks. This was because, at the end of the month, it would be deducted from their salaries as if it were some extra money being wasted. Therefore, they always hesitated in such situations. It was because they were used to drinking on someone else's dime. R, sensing this, slyly said,
"What are you people hesitating for? At most, it'll be two or three *wŏn*, won't it?"
P and Y, walking while shivering in the cold, were tempted by his words. As they talked, they reached the front of the DH Building (建築物).
The DH Building soared grandly into the grey sky where snowflakes drifted. P looked up at the building. Previously, the building had seemed imposing and majestic to him. Although he passed by this building every day, today for some reason it seemed <12> to leave a strange impression on him. Consequently, at that moment, a certain desire, far too fantastical, arose in his mind.
"Hey, Y. Wouldn't it be truly splendid if this building were our newspaper company?"
"Exactly. If I owned a building like that and ran a newspaper, I'd become the president and easily earn over a hundred *wŏn* a month just lying down. Hahaha."
"Hey, you!" P exclaimed.
"What's there to worry about? I'll make you the head of the society section. Isn't that enough? Then you'll earn over a hundred *wŏn* too."
R, who was beside them, chimed in again.
"Eh, you senseless people!"
"Right. You were left out. Since you're talkative and good at socializing, I'll make you the head of the advertising department." Everyone laughed.
"I don't want it. I won't run a newspaper or magazine that gets confiscated day and night. Getting permits, getting confiscated, getting arrested... no, I don't want it," R refused.
"Then what is your wish?"
"If that building were mine, I'd sell it."
R replied again.
"Then what would you do with the money?"
"I'd take it and run off to Russia (아라사)."
Everyone laughed again.
R, straightening his expression, said, <13>
"Alright, alright. Seeing you folks talk nonsense, it seems your stomachs are quite empty. Let's drop all that idle chatter and reconsider the initial problem."
"You mean the drinking problem?"
"Of course, that's the most important thing. What use is other empty talk?"
P, as if suddenly finding courage, said,
"Alright! Let's go."
And so, the three men, shivering in the cold, entered a tavern in the C district.

2

Just one or two drinks were enough to make them thoroughly drunk. Drinking on an empty, skinny stomach that had only had two slices of bread for lunch, it was natural they would get drunk. In truth, their drinking wasn't because they particularly liked alcohol. Especially for P and Y, though they drank when offered, they couldn't endure the pain and depression until the alcohol wore off.
So why did they drink?
They shared the agony common among Korean youth. Seeing themselves being destroyed day by day, whether by personal life, social environment, or national or class oppression, and realizing they themselves were heading towards ruin, they tried drinking instead of resisting.
They did so under the pretext that, at least while drinking, they could forget everything amidst the suffering. But how could young Koreans ever forget their resistance, even in their dreams?
Thus, whenever P drank, his drunken rambling became:
"Is this a time for us young fellows to be drinking like this? Haha, alcohol! Alcohol! Why do we aggravate our tormented nerves further with alcohol? Alcohol clouds everything. But that resolute will <14> of ours, which we must uphold, should not be clouded even for a moment by alcohol. Haha! But alcohol! Alcohol! Let's drink!" This became his drunken habit.
Another reason P started drinking was after joining the newspaper company. Whether asked to handle someone else's article request or when his articles weren't published—(of course, he refused many times at first)—he found himself having to attend gatherings where drinking was unavoidable. Fundamentally, he should have quit the newspaper company. However, in Korea, even being a newspaper reporter was considered a high-level profession. Even PhDs returning from abroad were welcomed primarily by newspaper companies. Therefore, P had no choice but to compromise with the evil aspects created by the modern social structure. Thus, out of habit, he began to drink.
However, whenever they drank, they always complained to each other about themselves.
P put down the glass he was holding. With a solemn attitude, he looked at his two friends beside him and spoke.
"Huh! Suddenly, a sad thought comes to me."
"Sad?" the two friends asked in unison. For a moment, all three reflected fragmentarily on themselves.
"Man! What's the use of thinking such thoughts?" R tried to comfort him.
"No, really. I feel like someone in a deep pit right now. Like a dog scavenging for bones (骨), I wander around picking up incidents that arise in a society where decay, malice, oppression, and exploitation worsen daily, doing so too objectively. I feel so distant from this society."
"What can we do? We have to live!" R added immediately after.
"Well. P, when you came from Shanghai (상해), weren't you also considered a promising figure in the social movement?"
Y exclaimed. At that moment, P suddenly recalled something from the past. More than anything, he remembered H's words: "If you stay in Seoul too long, you'll end up not being able to work. It's a place where people become too compromising. When you're pressed down from one side, and your own life is extremely <15> poor, typically, either extreme rebellious movements arise, or people become extremely compromising. You'll see."
"That's right. H, too, probably made many compromises before finally awakening and running off to XX to cultivate himself..." he thought inwardly. And he lamented that he himself, having left society and the masses, had dedicated everything merely to gathering material for articles that appeared on the surface.
"Indeed. The change from when I came from Shanghai to now, holding this glass and drinking together like this with you guys, is truly astonishing, even to myself."
Y became agitated at these words.
"No. We won't live like this forever. No. We shouldn't. Therefore, we have absolutely no need to be discouraged." Extending his hand and counting on his fingers, he said,
"You're twenty-five now, right? One, two, three... hmm, don't worry. You're still a young man. You can do anything. Right now, for us, truly, no mountain should seem daunting, no great sea fearsome. Why despair? Why hesitate? There are many things for us to do!"
P was also stirred by these words.
"Right! What you say seems true."
"Come on! Stop talking like that and just drink," R said again, trying to break the tense mood.
Just then, a thin, soft voice sounded from outside the door. It sounded a second time. The words were:
"Excuse me. Yes? Excuse me."
The group's eyes widened. But among them, R seemed to have already guessed, muttering, "Here comes trouble again!" and then called out,
"Yes, who is it?"
"Yes... Excuse me," she said, opening the door and entering. <16>
She was a woman. She looked to be around twenty-two or twenty-three years old. Her hair was done up like a female student's, her clothes were slightly soiled, she carried a large bundle (봇짐) in her right hand, and a parasol and a bouquet of flowers (꼿) in her left hand. She entered and sat down with a solemn demeanor, appearing extremely careful.
The group was silent. Among them, Y asked,
"Um, who might you be?"
"Yes, well, I am a woman who makes a living day by day by carrying these goods around and selling them to gentlemen like you. Please buy something," she said, unwrapping the large bundle with hands chapped from the cold. Inside were socks, soap, cigarettes, matches (성양), cosmetics, handkerchiefs, face powder (밀), and combs (빗). There were also stationery and envelopes.
"Buy these socks, and buy this comb too!" the woman urged, her face softening into a charming smile.
"What can we do? We don't have money right now. Please come again next time, and we'll buy from you," R said.
"What kind of talk is that? Gentlemen like you saying you have no money? Please don't be like that."
Saying this, she picked up a pair of socks and pressed them into Y's hand, her face holding a gentle smile. "Buy just one pair," she pleaded. Then, the woman reached under the socks and grasped Y's hand. Her face suddenly flushed slightly as she murmured intimately,
"Yes, just buy one pair. Yes. You have to be high-collar (하이칼라) for women to follow you, right? Yes?" At that moment, Y was startled. At any other time, he might have jokingly grabbed her hand back, but tonight, in his agitated state, he couldn't allow such dissolute behavior. He forcefully pulled his hand away, exclaiming,
"Women following me? Hey! Really!"
Y was angry.
"What— A gentleman like that probably likes women even more. Just pretending to be proper on the outside— Let me see! Haha!"
Y didn't know what to do. He felt both furious and utterly dumbfounded. Then, the woman again <17> laid out some stationery.
"Here! You must buy this too. So you can write love letters (련애 편지) to kisaeng."
"What? Kisaeng?" Y shouted.
"That gentleman has been so stiff since earlier. When you go to a kisaeng house tipsy like this, you'll be adored. Oh, right, you can't just go empty-handed," she said, taking out the bouquet of flowers she had brought in.
"Buy these flowers and take them with you. Yes? They are fragrant. How could a butterfly seeing flowers just pass by?"
Y couldn't bear it any longer.
"Hey, really, this woman doesn't know manners—"
"What does a woman know? Just a thing that smiles and plays in front of men—"
"What? Don't you know this is the era of women's liberation—" Y yelled.
"Liberation? You need to eat too, right?" she retorted, her tone shifting back to her true feelings. She had flirted like this with many men, but in this place, facing such sternness, she suddenly felt a kind of sorrow herself.
"I don't do this because I want to," she said with a sigh.
At this moment, P felt as if a sharp knife had suddenly pierced his chest.
"So, selling things like this, about how much do you sell in one night?" P asked.
"If I do well, I can sell about ten *wŏn* worth," she replied, her face flushing. At that moment, P quickly understood. He realized she spent her nights selling goods using the same base and lewd methods she had just tried on Y.
"Since when have you been doing this?"
"I started last year."
"With so many jobs available, why do you do this business, going around taverns?" <18>
The woman sighed again.
"Since you gentlemen are so proper, let me tell you my story. Please listen," she began again, her demeanor becoming composed.
"My family was originally poor. So I worked at the XX Rice Mill (XX정미소). But the owner there always gave me strange smiles and behaved insidiously towards me. Then one day... yes, it was the tenth day of the eleventh lunar month (동지달). Suddenly, the factory was in an uproar. All the factory girls' (공녀) eyes widened, their hands went limp, and they looked at each other, not knowing what to do. It was nothing else but that thirty *wŏn* of the office money, which had been there in the morning, had disappeared. So the office staff searched everywhere but couldn't find it. Then finally, they bluntly told us,
'When you leave in the evening, everyone come to the office first.' So who would dare disobey? If we didn't listen to the owner's words, our lifeline would be cut off from the next day. So in the evening, everyone gathered at the office without leaving. And then, can you believe such a thing happened? They stripped everyone naked, women (녀편내), girls, everyone!" As she spoke, a look of fear and shame crossed her face.
"If they were Koreans, maybe it would be different. But the owner is Japanese (일인), so just imagine when he gets angry. How could it be? But since it was something they wanted to do, how could they just let it be? They herded everyone back into the factory and called them one by one into the office to strip. So what could we do? Then, when it was my turn, they didn't strip me in the office. Instead, that man said, 'You need to be investigated thoroughly, so I'll do it last,' and made me wait for several hours. So, finally, I had no choice but to face it. Of course, at that time, he said all sorts of things. Promising good clothes, lots of money, saying I could live without working... But I refused, crying. Then that man said, 'If you don't comply, I'll report to the police that you stole the thirty *wŏn*. Now, listen to me!' At that time, I was foolish and ignorant." Then she sighed suddenly. <19>
"Oh dear, I've said too much. Without knowing shame—"
The three men were burning with an overwhelming flame of indignation.
"No. Please continue. Surely there will be a day to avenge your wrongs?" P urged her on.
"But all his words were lies. Moreover, he kicked me out of the factory. I don't know the exact reason, but thinking back now, it seems he was afraid I might speak ill of him in the factory. After that, I didn't go to any factory. It seemed all factory owners were unspeakably malicious. But what use would it be? Instead of going to the factory, I went from this man to that man—" she said, her voice trembling with agitation.
The group was all agitated. And the alcohol had worn off sometime during her story.
"And so?"
"So, after trying everything, I finally came up with this plan. Because I'm a woman rather than a man, I get harassed a bit, but it's better for selling things—"
As soon as these words fell, P felt a dreadful thought. He didn't think this woman, driven to such degradation by the struggle for survival, was the only one. He thought about how the lives of all men and women in the propertyless class were being violated like this. Before he entered the newspaper company, his own younger siblings (동생들) had worked in factories. "Ah, how dreadful," he cried out inwardly. Even now, P's youngest half-sister (끗헤 이모(異母) - interpreted as youngest half-sister/step-relative, original term noted) was enduring abuse in a rubber factory. And it wasn't just P's relative. In the land of Korea, which was declining and weakening day by day, he thought of the many sisters (누의), younger siblings, friends, and parents who had no choice but to live such slave-like lives. And perhaps he himself might face such a life in the future.
"Ah! The dreadful future of Korea! The terrifying present of Korea!" he cried out inwardly, clenching his fists. Ice-cold sweat uncontrollably welled up in his palms. <20>
"I didn't even realize things were this bad. I have degenerated during this time. How far I have strayed from this society and the masses!" he cried out alone. Y, standing beside him, his face turned pale, said,
"That's right. The bourgeoisie (쁘르지와) must be exterminated..."
"Just thinking about them makes my skin crawl," the woman replied roughly.
"They use the wealth they amassed unjustly to oppress the poor, the powerless, the rightless, and treat them like slaves. That's why they must be eliminated!"
"That's absolutely true," the woman continued.
"If we just took away all the money from those bastards who think they can commit any evil deed as long as they have money—"
"It's not just the money, their fundamental nature is like that—"
Y and P exchanged words. The woman stood up from her seat.
"I feel relieved. Talking with you all and listening has truly refreshed me. Well, I'll see you again next time," she said, standing up.
"No. Since you came in anyway, let's buy at least one pair of socks," P said, putting out thirty *jŏn*. Having sold the socks, the woman disappeared outside again.

3

After sending the woman off, the three men were greatly agitated. They thought about how shameful their lives had been up until then. As people in such miserable circumstances themselves, and as individuals who had intended to work for the masses, they regretted how unfaithful their lives had been during that time. Therefore, each of them gained new strength and resolve right there. And they stopped drinking alcohol. Their hearts felt heavy and pounded, making them reluctant even to speak to each other. <21>
They were just about to step outside.
Suddenly, the single shriek of a woman fainting was heard. A cry filled with all despair and sorrow echoed dismally along with the wind on the cold, snowy night.
The three men stood still.
The sound came from the inner room. Consequently, the voice unmistakably belonged to the woman who had entered earlier.
"That's her voice, isn't it?" asked the agitated P.
"Indeed. It seems she's being beaten by some guy?" R asked in turn.
"Look there— He's hitting her hard—"
"Let's go in!"
And so, they simultaneously opened the door to the inner room. In the middle, a drinking table was set, and three or four men in Western suits were seated. One of them was pinning down the woman who sold goods and was mercilessly hitting her. Then, seeing the group's sudden entrance, the man stood up. The woman saw P and Y, rushed towards them, and suddenly burst into loud tears.
The agitated P simply punched the man who was getting up.
"Aigo!" The drunken man collapsed immediately. A man sitting nearby stood up and mumbled,
"That bitch stole that man's wallet in here, that's why—"
"What?"
"Theft (절도), you mean?"
"Theft or whatever, why are you hitting her? Why are you hitting a weak woman?"
"Because it's theft (절도), we hit her." <22>
"You bastard, you assert it's theft just with your mouth. Do you know what crime hitting a person is?"
During this time, blood flowed from the woman's nose. Seeing the blood, the drunkards seemed to get scared and slipped out one by one. The young woman who had collapsed remained lying there, not getting up. The three men stanched her nosebleed with a cloth, then simply came outside. They called the owner, arranged for the woman to sleep in that room, and then went back outside.
On the main street of Chongno, where traces of people had almost vanished, white snow lay clean. P, Y, and R, the three men, walked, treading on the white snow, keenly feeling the XX that must surely come to us, heedlessly forgetting even the cold.
In the hearts of the three men, the same burning flame of agitated, fervent passion blazed.
However, their agitation was not a momentary excitement. It was an excitement that would never die down! A passion preparing until the XX occurs.

4

The next day, P wrote the following articles in his newspaper's society section:
— An article conveying 'The cruel tyranny of factory owners created by bourgeois (쁘르즈와) society and the underside of the miserable lives of lamb-like female factory workers (녀직공)' and
— An article with the meaning of 'The depraved lives of fallen female factory workers and the beastly (즘승) cruel amusement (악희) of bourgeois youth,' along with the 'romance' (로맨스) of the female peddler (녀행상인) from the previous evening.
However, that day's newspaper was immediately confiscated (압수) and could not be published.
For P, the incident that occurred the previous night had stimulated his weary nerves. He now sought to grasp the truth of a new radiance from below, from the abyss.
Struggle! A new struggle had begun for him. -The End- <23>

**Summary**

Disillusioned journalists P, Y, and R encounter a female peddler in a bar who recounts her exploitation in a Japanese-owned factory and subsequent fall into a precarious life selling goods through flirtation. The encounter reignites their social conscience. Later, they rescue her from assault by men accusing her of theft. P writes articles exposing the harsh realities of factory exploitation and bourgeois depravity, based on her story. The articles are promptly censored (압수) by authorities. The story concludes with P energized, recognizing the need for struggle against the oppressive system. Historically, the story offers a raw glimpse into Korean colonial society: the poverty and lack of options forcing women into exploitative factory work or degrading means of survival, the brutality of colonial capitalism (represented by the Japanese factory owner and indifferent bourgeois youth), the stifling censorship faced by critical media, and the awakening political consciousness and desire for resistance among intellectuals grappling with personal disillusionment and societal injustice.

1

잔뜩 질린 보라빗 한울에는 추운 바람이 소리를 치며 휘도라 단인다. 아참 부터 점점 추어들어 오든 날은 오후에도 풀리지 안코 더 추엇다.
H신문사의 기자로 잇는 P와 Y는 자긔들의 할 일을 다 맛치고 집으로 가령으로 신문사 문에를 나왓다. 추운 바람은 적은 눈발을 던지며 이리 저리로 밋처서 휘도라 단인다.
P와 Y는 서로 아모 말도 업시 한 삼십 보 쯤이나 거러서 갓다. 그 곳에서 R이라는 친구를 또 맛낫다. 이러 해서 세 사람은 또 말 업시 거러서 간다.
P와 Y는 날마다 그러케 당하는 일이지만 오후가 되면 모든 것이 귀찬코 마음이 지아할 수 업시 외로웟다. 더구나 오늘처럼 일을 많이 한 데다가 점심이라구는 팡 두 조각에 차 한 잔밖에 멋은 것이 업섯다. 배가 곱흘수록 만사에 뜻이 업시 되며 배가 곱흘수록 춥기도 더 추운 것이엿섯다.
P는 말 업시 거러서 오면서 속으로 이러케 생각을 하엿다.
-오날은 술도 좀 사줄 녀석이 업나-하면서 참고 오다가 기여코 엽헤 오는 친구들에게 하소연을 하엿다.
『이러케 추운 날 한 잔 햇스면 좃켓다』
『글세 자네 한 소리가 매우 뜻 잇는 소릴세』＜11＞
하고 Y가 웃으면서 대답하엿다.
『자네들 오날은 벌 바더 싸지. 그적게들 좀 먹엇나. 남들은 한 시간도 멀한데 이런 새벽까지 먹엇스니 벌 밧지 안햇나?』 하고 P가 대답하엿다.
『하기는 참 그래! 하고 Y가 좀 붓그러운 듯이 말하엿다.
그들은 사에서 나오면 곳 집으로 도라 가기가 실혓다. 그들은 넉점부터는 그들의 행락을 구하기에 밧벗다. 술집에서 술집으로 애인의 집에서 애인의 집으로 휘도라 단이엿섯다. 만일 그것 저것 업는 날에는 서로 얼굴을 맛보고 안저서 잡담과 희롱으로라도 그 시간을 대신하여야 하엿섯다.
그들은 청년들이 흔이 가지고 잇는 희망 혹은 다른 사람들 모양으로 조선 사회에 대한 개인 뎍 체임 혹은 그 적은 의무로써 내뎍으로 얘쓰는 그의 노력과 모든 청년긔에서 피치 못할 것 희망을 상실한 쟈 모양으로 실망과 락담에서 오즉 퇴폐하여 가기만 하엿섯다. 그러나 그들이 오날은 별안간 갈 곳이 업는 것 가티 가슴이 답답하엿다.
R는 다시 문뎨를 내엿다.
『그러면 오날은 한 시간쯤만 먹을 예산을 하지』
그러나 P나 Y는 일부러 자기 돈을 내여서 먹기는 실혓다. 그것은 월말이 되면 새삼스럽게 딴 돈이나 업새는 것 모양으로 월급에서 제하게 되는 까닭이엿다. 그럼으로 이러케 되는 경우에 그들은 늘 주저 하엿다. 그것은 늘 남의 돈으로만 먹엇든 까닭이다. 그 눈치를 알어 채인 R는 능갈치게
『이 사람들 무얼 그리 주저하나. 깃것해야 이 삼원이면 될 것이 안인가?』
추이에 떨면서 거러가는 P와 Y는 그 소리에 솔깃하엿다. 그리면서 그들은 DH삘딩 (建築物) 압까지 이르럿다.
DH삘딍은 눈발 날리는 회색 한울 우로 엄쟝하게 소사 잇다. P는 그 삘딍을 치여다 보앗다. 그는 전에 입시 그 삘딍이 엄쟝하여 보이고 위염스러히 보엿다. 날마다 이 집 압흐로 단이엿스나 변으로 오날은 그에게＜12＞ 이상스러운 인상을 던저 준 듯 십다. 따라서 그는 그 순간에 엇더한 욕심이 너무도 공상뎍으로 그의 마음에서 일어낫다.
『여보게 Y군. 이 집이 우리 신문사라면 참 휼융 하겟나.』
『글세 말일세. 만일 내가 저런 집을 가지고 신문을 한다면 나는 사쟝이 되여서 한 달에 누어서 백여원 월급을 따시거든. 허허허』
『에, 이 사람!』 하고 P가 말하엿다.
『이 사람 걱정할 것 무엇잇나? 내 자네랑은 사회부쟝을 식히지. 그러면 그만이 안인가? 그러면 자네도 백여원은 먹네』
엽헤 잇든 R이 또 참예를 하엿다.
『에, 주책 업는 사람들!』
『올치. 자네가 빠젓네 그려. 자네는 수다해서 교제를 잘 할 터이니 광고부쟝이나 한아 식히지, 일동은 웃엇다.
『나는 실혀. 밤낫 압수만 당하는 신문 잡지는 안이 하겟다. 허가 맛고, 압수당하고 잡혀 가고 예 나는 실흐이』 하고 R이 거졀 하엿다.
『그럼 무엇이 소원인가?』
『저 집이 만일 내 집이라면 나는 팔어 먹겟네』
하고 R이 다시 대답하엿다.
『그럼 그 돈을 가지고는 무엇을 할텐가?』
『가지고 아라사로 돌고 빼겟네』
일동은 또 웃엇다.
R는 다시 얼굴 빗을 바르게 하고＜13＞
『자 자네들 헛소리 하는 것을 보니 암만해도 속이 좀 비인 모양일세. 그까진 잡담은 다 집어 치우고 인제는 처음의 문뎨를 다시 좀 생각하세』
『먹는 문뎨 말인가?』
『암 그것이 뎨일이지. 다른 공론이야 무슨 소용 잇겟나?』
P는 변안간 무슨 용긔를 낸 듯이
『그래! 가자』
이러케 해서 세 사람은 추음에 떨면서 C동내외 주점으로 들어갓다.

2

한 잔 두 잔 뿐으로도 그들을 흠뻑 취하게 하엿다. 점심이라구 팡 두 쪽만을 먹은 빼빼 말는 속에다가 술을 먹엇스니 취할 것은 사실이다. 그들은 사실 술을 먹는다는 것이 결코 술이 조화서 먹는 것은 안이엿다. 더욱이 P와 Y는 술을 권하면 먹기는 하나 그 술이 깰 때까지 고통과 우울로 견대지 못한다.
그런데 술을 왜 먹을까?
그들에게는 조선청년들이 흔이 당하는 고민이 또한 잇섯다. 그것은 개인의 생활로나 사회뎍 환경으로나 민족 혹은 게급뎍 압박으로나 하로 하로 파멸을 당하고 잇는 것을 보면서 또한 자긔 자신도 파멸을 당해 가는데 그들은 반항하는 대신에 술을 먹어 보앗다.
술을 먹엇든 때만은 괴로운 중에 모든 것은 니저 버린다는 핑계로 그리 하엿섯다. 그러나 엇지 조선의 젊은 사람으로써 꿈엔들 그들의 반항을 이저 버리겟느냐?

『나는 그래도 이러케까지는 몰낫다. 나는 그 동안 타락 하엿섯구나. 이 사회와 민즁과 퍽도 멀엇섯구사!』 하고 그는 홀로 부르지젓다. 엽헤 잇든 Y는 얼굴이 노래지면서
『그럿타. 쁘르지와를 박멸하여야 한다...』
『나는 그 생각만 해도 지긋지긋 해요』 하고 그 녀자는 어림치고 대답을 하엿다.
『그들은 불의로 모흔 재산을 가지고 가난한 모든 사람 힘 업는 사람 권리 업는 사람을 압박하고 종으로 부리는 것이야. 그리니깐 그것을 업새야 할 것이란 말이야」
『참말로 그래요』 하고 그 녀자가 말을 니엿다.
『돈만 잇스면 가진 낫분 짓을 다해도 관계 업다는 놈들의 돈을 다 빠어서 버리면-』
『돈뿐이 안이라 그 놈들은 근성이 그 모양이니깐-』
하고 Y와 P는 서로 주고 맛엇다. 그 녀자는 그 자리에서 일어낫다.
『마음이 시원함니다. 여러분과 이야기를 하고 또 드르니 참 시원함니다. 자 요 다음에 또 보옵겟슴니다』 하고 그는 일어낫다.
『안이요. 이왕 드러 왓스니 양발이나 한나 삽시다』 하고 그는 삼십전을 내노앗다. 그리해서 양발을 판 그 녀자는 다시 밧그로 사라젓다.

3

녀자를 내여 보낸 세 사람은 한것 흥분하엿다. 그들은 그들의 이때 것 나려 온 생활이 얼마나 붓그러웟든 것을 생각하엿다. 이와 가티 비참한 사람들인 자긔로서. 또한 민즁을 위해서 일하겟다든 자긔들이 그 동안은 얼마나 불성질한 생활을 하엿든 것을 스스로 후회 하엿다. 그럼으로 그들은 다 각각 그 자리에서 새로운 힘과 결심을 어덧다. 그러고 그들은 술먹기도 그만 두엇다. 마음이 답답하고 가슴이 벌떡임으로 서로 말하기도 실혓다. ＜21＞
그들이 막 밧그로 나오려 할 때이엿다.
별안간 녀자의 긔졀하는 외마듸 소리가 들렷다. 모든 졀망과 비애의 부르지짐이 눈 오는 추운 밤 바람과 한 가지 침울하게 울이엿다.
세 사람은 가만히 섯다.
그 소리는 안방으로 부터 나왓다. 따러서 그 목소리는 틀림 업시 아까 드러왓든 녀자인 것이 분명하엿다.
『그 녀자의 목소리이지?』 하고 흥분된 P가 물엇다.
『글세. 웬 놈에게 매를 맛는 모양이지?』 하고 R가 또 물엇다.
『저것 보아 막 따리는데-』
『자 드러가 보세!』
그리해서 그들은 일제히 안방문을 열엇다. 한 가운데에는 술상이 놋혓고 양복 입은 산아희가 세너시나 안젓다. 그 즁에 한 사람은 틀림 업는 그 물건 팔러단이는 녀자를 깔고 안저서 막 따려 준다. 그리다가 이 일행의 들입함을 보고 그 자는 일어낫다. 그 녀자는 P와 Y를 보고는 달려 들면서 문득 큰 소리로 울엇다.
흥분된 P는 일어나는 놈을 그냥 주먹으로 갈겻다.
『아이코!』 하고 술 취한 그는 그냥 쓰러졋다. 엽해 안젓든 자가 일어나면서
『그 년이 이 자리에서 그 사람의 지갑을 훔첫스니깐 그럿치-』 하고 말을 어믈거렷다.
『무엇?』
『절도란 말야?』
『절도니 엇지란 말야? 너희들 사람은 웨 따리니? 약한 녀자를 웨 따리니』
『절도니깐 따리지』＜22＞
『이놈아 졀도는 네 입으로만 도졀지. 사람을 따리는 것은 너희 무슨 죄인 줄 아니?』
그럴 동안에 그 녀자의 코로서는 피가 흘너 나리엿다. 그 피 나오는 것을 본 그 주정군이 들은 무슨 겁이 낫든지 하나 둘 다 빠저 나갓다. 그 자리에 쓰러진 그 젊은 녀자는 다시 일어나지 안코 그냥 그 자리에 누어 잇섯다. 세 사람은 코피를 못 나게 헝겁으로 틀어 막고 그들은 그냥 박그로 나왓다. 그리고 쥬인을 불러서 그 녀자를 그 방에서 자게 하도록 하고 그들은 다시 박그로 나왓다.
사람의 자최가 거의 끈어진 종로 큰 길에는 흰 눈이 깨끗하게 깔엿다. P와 Y와 R 이 세 사람은 흰 눈을 밟으면서 우리에게 반시드 와야 할 XX을 절실이 늣기면서 스름 업시 추이도 니저 버리고 거러서 간다.
그 세 사람 마음에는 한 가지로 흥분 된 뜨거운 정렬의 불길이 타고 잇섯다.
그러나 그들의 흥분은 일시뎍 흥분은 안이엿다. 어느 때든지 꺼지지 안는 흥분! XX이 일어날 때까지 준비하고 잇는 열정이엿다.

4

그 이튼날 P는 그의 신문 사회란에 이러한 기사를 썻다.
-『쁘르즈와 사회가 생기게 한 공장주의 잔인 횡폭과 어린 양 가튼 녀직공의 참담한 생활 이면』이라는 뜻의 기사와
『타락한 녀직공의 생활과 즘승 가튼 쁘르즈와 청년들의 악희』라는 의미의 기사를 어제 저녁 녀행상인(女行商人)의 로맨스와 아울러 내여 버렷다.
그러나 그 날 신문은 세상에 나오지 못하고 곳 압수 되고 말엇다.
P에게는 어제 밤에 생긴 한 사건이 그의 피로 하엿든 신경을 자극하여 주엇다. 그는 이제 아래로 부터 구렁텅이로 부터 새로운 광명의 진리를 잡으려 하엿다.
