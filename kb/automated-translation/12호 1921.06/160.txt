Article Title: 流星 (Shooting Star / News Flashes)
Author: Not Specified
Article Type: 소식 (News/Tidbits)

According to the accounts of a certain landlord's agent (마름), the annual income of Mr. Min Yŏnghwi (閔泳徽) of Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) is said to be 250,000 *sŏk* in white rice alone. Besides this, there must be income from miscellaneous grains. Also, there must be a magnificent accumulation of cash. Truly, it could be said to be the first private fortune of its kind since the beginning of Korean history. The soil cultivated by a diseased social system, if this much, is quite a large sum. An annual income of 250,000 *sŏk* of white rice! Isn't that indeed a frightening statement? Even if this were divided among 25 people, each would not fail to be one of the extremely wealthy.

Last year, when the foundation issue of Hwimun High Common School (徽文高普), managed by Mr. Min, arose, causing staff and students to shed tears and stir up trouble, the sole plea of the student representative negotiator to Mr. Min was reportedly, "Your Excellency, Your Excellency, please grant just three days' worth of your current income. That would make Hwimun High Common School splendid forever." However, that three days' worth has not yet been granted.

According to police authorities of the Government-General (總督府), the number of magazine titles subscribed to within Korea, including those published in Japan and Korea, is about 600 types. The total number of subscribers is approximately 160,000 Japanese and approximately 70,000 Koreans. Calculating this against the total population residing in Korea, it means that among Japanese, one out of two people subscribes to a newspaper or magazine, while among Koreans, barely one out of 200 subscribes. Thus, they say the reading power of Koreans is still that weak. Of course, the accuracy of these statistics is questionable. Also, even if it were so, it is common for Koreans, especially in rural areas (地方), for one village or one group to circulate and read a single newspaper or magazine. Therefore, it's unlikely that only one in 200 people reads, but nevertheless, it is an undeniable fact that the reading enthusiasm of Koreans is lower than that of Japanese.

The average burden of one police officer (巡査) currently in Korea is calculated as follows: in terms of area, one Japanese police officer is responsible for an area of 1.77 square *ri* (方里). In terms of households, a Japanese officer handles 313 households, while a Korean officer handles 369 households. And in terms of population per officer, a Japanese officer covers 1645 people, while a Korean officer covers 2102 people. There is an old saying that if one's territory is 100 square *ri*, it is enough to rule as a king. Today, if the area per Japanese police officer is 1.77 square *ri*, it doesn't quite reach 100 square *ri*, but it amounts to about 20 square *ri*. Therefore, although it falls a bit short of playing king, it seems they play the role of a tiger (범 - implies tyranny or dominance) within that area. (If you go to the countryside).

Whenever one visits Yu Sŏngjun (兪星濬) at his residence, he is practicing calligraphy (習字). Whenever one meets Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙), he is twisting string (노끈 꼬기). Mr. Yu says, "This calligraphy practice is indeed a very good thing. It removes all anxieties (煩惱)." Mr. Kim says, "Twisting string is quite enjoyable (滋味). And it makes me smoke less." Indeed, it must be so. Even if temporary, passing time idly (無聊度過) is a disgrace for a gentleman (紳士).

According to someone who recently returned, in Pukkan-do (北間島 - North Jiandao), one can stay for a month for 30 *chŏn* to 1 *wŏn* 50 *chŏn* per *mal* of millet. Even today, when prices have fallen somewhat, it does not fail to be a different world (別世界).

Yi Kŭn-yang (李根양), the former county magistrate of Puryŏng (富寧) and Pukch'ŏng (北靑), and current representative of the Korean people in Hunchun (琿春), stopped by Ch'ŏngjin (淸津) in Hamgyŏng Pukto (咸北) on his way back from sightseeing in Japan around the 5th of this month. At that time, there happened to be a lecture meeting sponsored by the citizens of Sŏngjin (城津 - Ch'ŏngjin), and he, dead drunk (泥醉), barged into the meeting venue, flailing his arms and shouting violent words—no, mad words (狂語)—such as "イフタイキミタナハナンスルカ..." (Japanese gibberish), causing an indescribable disturbance (질알). Not only that, but he stayed there day and night, getting perpetually drunk, making rounds of kisaeng houses (妓家), and exhibiting all sorts of indescribable disgraceful behavior (醜態). Investigating his true colors reveals that he is said to be a promoted member of the former Ilchinhoe (一進會). Of course, among many people, there may be drunkards (酒狂者). There may also be those with personalities uglier than a dog's. However, if the representative of the Hunchun Korean people, who traveled even to Japan on business, is such a person, what will become of the dignity (體面) of the Hunchun compatriots—no, the Korean people (朝鮮民族)? Primarily, it is deplorable (寒心) for the Hunchun compatriots.<90>

**Summary**

This article from the June 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* compiles several short news items reflecting contemporary Korean society under Japanese rule. Topics include the staggering wealth of Min Yŏnghwi contrasted with the financial needs of Hwimun School, statistics highlighting the disparity in magazine readership between Japanese and Koreans, comparisons of the workload between Japanese and Korean police officers, anecdotes about the hobbies of prominent figures Yu Sŏngjun and Kim Sŏngsu, the remarkably low cost of living in Pukkan-do (Jiandao), and a scathing report on the drunken misconduct of Yi Kŭn-yang, a former official and representative of Koreans in Hunchun, identified as a former Ilchinhoe member. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the socio-economic stratification, colonial administrative practices and statistics (even if questioned by the author), cultural life, conditions in diaspora communities like Jiandao, and strong nationalist criticism directed towards collaborators like Yi Kŭn-yang, reflecting the tensions and realities of the colonial period.

流星
某都「마름」의 精算에 依하면 京城 閔泳徽氏의 年 收入은 白米로만 25萬石이라한다. 이 外에 雜穀의 收入이 有할지오. 또 宏壯한 現金의 積置가 有할 것이다. 實로 朝鮮歷史의 잇서온 後 처음 잇는 私富이라 하겟다. 病的 社會制의 지어노흔 흙도 이만하면 꽤꽤 큰 세음이다. 白米 25萬石의 年收! 果然 무서운 말이 안인가. 此를 25人이 分有한다 할지라도 巨富의 1人 됨을 不失할 것이다. 昨年 中 閔氏의 經營하는 徽文高普의 財團問題가 起하야 職員과 生徒가 눈물을 흘리며 惹端이 날 때에 學生側 代表의 交涉員의 閔氏에 對한 唯一 懇願은 「大監님이시어 大監님 現在 收入의 3日 分만을 許施하여 주십시오. 그것이면 徽文高普는 令媛히 훌늉하겟나이다.」 하얏다 한다. 그러나 그 3日分은 아직까지 許與되지 아니하얏다. 總督府 警務 當局者의 말에 依하면 朝鮮 內에서 購讀되는 雜誌 數는 日本 及 朝鮮 發行의 것을 通하야 約 6百種이 有하며 此購讀者의 總 人員數는 日本人이 約 16萬人, 朝鮮人이 約 7萬人인데 此를 在鮮 全 人口數에 對算하면 日本人은 2人 中에 1種, 朝鮮人에는 2百人 中에 겨우 1種의 新聞雜誌를 購讀하는 세음인 바 朝鮮人의 讀書力은 아직까지 그만큼 貧弱하다」한다. 勿論 이 統計의 正確與否가 疑問이오. 또 그러타 할지라도 우리 朝鮮人은 地方 가튼대서는 1新聞이나 1雜誌를 1村 或은 1團體가 輪覽함이 普通인 바 2百人 中에 1人 임과 如한 事는 無하겟지마는 如何間 朝鮮人의 讀書熱이 日本人보다 低한 것은 不可 誣의 事實. 現 朝鮮에 있는 巡査 1人의 平均 負担은 面積上으로 日本 巡査 1人이 1方里 77의 區域을 마탓고 또 戶數로는 日人巡査가 313戶 鮮人 巡査가 369戶이며 又 1人當 人口는 日人 巡査가 1645人  鮮人 巡査가 2102人이라는 計算이다. 녯 사람의 말에 方이 百里면 足히 써 王하리라는 말이 잇거니와 今日 日人 巡査 1人當 區域이 1方里 77이면 方百里는 채 못되나 方 20里는 되는 세음이다. 따라 王노릇 까지는 조곰 못하나 그 區域으로써 범 노릇은 하는 樣이다. (地方을 가보면) 兪星濬氏를 宅으로 차즈면 차를때마다 習字하며 잇고 金性洙氏를 對하면 對할때마다 노끈 꼬기이다. 兪氏는 가르되 「이 習字야 말로 퍽 조흔 일이지오. 一切의 煩惱를 除합니다.」 金氏는 가르되 「노끈 꼬는 것 퍽 滋味가 나요. 그리고 담배를 덜 먹게 됩니다.」라고 한다. 果然 하겟다 비록 一時 일지라도 無聊度過는 紳士의 恥辱. 最近 돌아온 이의 말을 들으면 北間島에서는 조쌀 한 말에 30錢-1圓 50錢이면 逸個月을 宿泊할 수 잇다 한다. 비록 얼마콤 物價가 떠러진 今日이라 할지라도 別世界됨을 不失. 前 富寧郡守北靑郡守요, 現琿春朝鮮民族 代表라는 李根양은 去 5日中 日本을 구경하고 돌아오는 途中 咸北淸津을 들럿다. 그때 마츰 城津市民主催의 講演會가 有하엿는데 그는 泥醉의 몸으로써 會席에 亂入하야 팔을 버리고 소리를 질너가며 「イフタイキミタナハナンスルカ...」하는 等의 暴語-아니 狂語를 連發하야 말할 수 업는 질알을 하얏스며 뿐만 아니라 그는 그 곳에 日夜長醉로 妓家를 巡圓하며 께끈하야 말 할 수 업는 別別 醜態를 演하얏는데 그의 本色을 探하면 前一進會員의 昇級者라는 말이다. 勿論 만흔 사람中에는 酒狂者도 잇슬지오. 개보다 더 醜한 人性을 가진 者도 잇을 것이다. 그러나 적어도 日本까지 出張한 琿春朝鮮民族의 代表者가 그따위 사람이라하면 琿春同胞-아니 朝鮮民族의 體面은 어찌될가. 第一 琿春同胞를 爲하야 寒心＜90＞
