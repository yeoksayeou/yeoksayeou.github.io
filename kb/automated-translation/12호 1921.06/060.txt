**Body Text Translation:**
Such a Great Person Also Exists.

It is generally known that the prosperity (隆盛) of ancient Greek (古代 希臘) Sparta (스파타) was based entirely (專혀) on King Lycurgus's (王族 리커꺼쓰) diligent enforcement (法則勵行) of the laws. According to tradition (傳), Lycurgus, about to implement (方場實行코저 하는) the laws he intended, promulgated them, summoned the people (國人), and explained, "Although this law (此法) is quite (자못) strict (嚴勵하야) and may be difficult to practice (行키 難할지나), I (余) will now travel around various countries (諸國을 巡歷하야) and inspect (視察한 後) their legal systems (法制). Upon my return (歸國하야), I will immediately (곳) revise it. Though it may be difficult (어려운대로), just observe (遵守) and do not violate (勿犯) this (此) until the time of my return (余의 歸國하는 때까지만)." Then, he finally (디듸어) hid himself (身을 隱하야) and never appeared again (다시 出現치 아니하얏다).

His thought was that if the laws were implemented (實行하면), the nation (國家) would surely (반듯이) become strong and prosperous (强盛할 터인데). Worrying (慮하야) that the people (人民) might oppose (反對할가) before it could be fully established (成行하기도 前에), he used this strategy (此策을 用하고). It is said that he himself (自己는) alone (혼자) entered the mountains (山中에 入하야) and starved to death (餓死하얏다 한다). Indeed (果然), what a noble deed it is! (어떠케 거룩한 일인가.)<27>

**Summary**

This article recounts the story of Lycurgus, the legendary lawgiver of ancient Sparta. To ensure his strict laws, which he believed essential for Sparta's prosperity, would be followed without immediate opposition, Lycurgus made his people promise to obey them until his return from a journey. He then deliberately disappeared and starved himself to death, sacrificing his life so the laws would remain in effect permanently, thereby guaranteeing Sparta's strength. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it presents a foreign historical anecdote emphasizing extreme self-sacrifice for the perceived good of the state or collective. Historians might find it noteworthy as an example of the moralistic or didactic narratives prevalent in publications of the era, potentially used implicitly to inspire dedication or sacrifice among Koreans for a greater cause, albeit framed through a classical Western example rather than a Korean one.

이런 훌륭한 이도 잇도다.
古代 希臘 「스파타」의 隆盛이 專혀 王族 「리커꺼쓰」의 法則勵行에 基함인 것은 一般이 知하는 바이라. 그런데 傳에 依하면 「리커꺼쓰」는 方場實行코저 하는 그 法令을 頒布하여 國人을 召하야 說明하되 此法이 자못 嚴勵하야 行키 難할지나 余가 自今諸國을 巡歷하야 諸國의 法制를 視察한 後 歸國하야 곳 改正할 것이니 어려운대로 余의 歸國하는 때까지만 此를 遵守勿犯하라 하고 그는 디듸어 身을 隱하야 다시 出現치 아니하얏다. 그의 생각에는 그 法則을 實行하면 國家는 반듯이 强盛할 터인데 成行하기도 前에 人民이 反對할가 慮하야 此策을 用하고 自己는 혼자 山中에 入하야 餓死하얏다 한다. 果然 어떠케 거룩한 일인가.＜27＞
