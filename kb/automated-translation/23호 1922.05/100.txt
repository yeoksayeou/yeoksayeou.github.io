「If I Were the Creator」 and others
						Kim Sŏksong
These days, for some reason
I've grown weary of the whole world
To people who cannot live without eating
Why was conscience (廉恥) given?
I curse even the Creator (造物主)

If I were the Creator (造物主)
I would bow before all creation (萬物)
Apologize (謝過) for all faults
Then smash the universe (宇宙)
And kill myself (自殺) as well.

Life's Conflict (生命의 葛藤)
Love (愛情), Jealousy (嫉妬)... <12>
Health (健康), Illness (病弱)...
Wealth (富裕), Poverty (貧困)...
Freedom (自由), Restraint (拘束)...
These all sow the seeds of conflict (葛藤)
On the ground of life (生命)
They are the sole malicious guides (指路者)
That lead (引導) me to the grave (墓地)

Oh! But my young soul!
Remember (記憶) this
When things like these disappear entirely
That is when you yourself have already disappeared.

A Nursing Infant's Sleeping Face
Smiling gleefully
Pouting as if to cry
Suckling softly at the breast
Wrinkle-free and smooth
With cheeks like apples (林檎)
A nursing infant's sleeping face <13>

Ah! My lovely baby
Whenever I see your face
I am always surprised
At the unknowable growth
Changing day by day
And then, with pointless thoughts
My mind is captivated

How did that happen
- A person giving birth to a person -
Who can know this creation (造化)?
Drinking only watery milk
It's strange (怪常) how it grows
And before you know it can even turn over (업치는 줄도 모르는 동안에)
How it crawls in no time
Is even more amazing (한칭 더 놀라운 일이다)

Putting that aside
Why was it born?
No, did it want to be born?
What will it do while alive? <14>
What will it do when it grows up?

After it grows up, too
It will fight with people to live
Cry over duties and responsibilities
Knowingly lie
To speak of its view on life (人生觀)
And assert its philosophy (哲學), probably
And thus, in the end (最後)
It will become the protagonist
Who builds one new grave
Just as its ancestors (祖先) did

Ah! Nursing baby!
My beloved child!
Link in the chain of humanity (人類의 連鎖)
Ancestor (祖先) of the future (未來)
Who knows nothing now
O nursing infant's sleeping face!
From your mother's womb
The moment you first came into the world <15>
You already received a death sentence (死刑宣告)

Who among those who know life
Does not know death?
The shouting of life
Is the prelude (序曲) to death

But, breathing softly without words
O nursing infant's sleeping face!
What is life,
And what then is death? <16>

On a Morning with Driving Snow
On a morning with driving snow
Startled awake by the wind
I opened the door for no reason

Under the eaves of the opposite room
Two magpies
Were huddled <16>
Startled by the sound of my door opening
One flew off first
Into the driving snow
Where one couldn't distinguish [even] close distances (咫尺)

As if I had committed a great sin (罪)
I closed the door without a sound
And waited by the crack in the door
For the flown magpie to return
But, but
Even the remaining one
Cawed "Kkak!" resentfully
And flew off somewhere

The wind blows as if mad
The driving snow whips the cheek
Feeling as if I committed (犯) a great sin (罪)
I went down into the yard
And looked (삷혓스나) for where the magpies went
But there wasn't even a shadow anymore <17>
<12-17>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by Kim Sŏksong expresses profound existential weariness and despair. The poet critiques the Creator for a flawed world, contemplates the inherent conflicts within life (love/jealousy, health/illness, etc.), observes the bittersweet growth of an infant from innocence towards inevitable adult suffering and death, and feels guilt over disturbing the natural world. The poems convey a deep sense of disillusionment with life's cycle and meaning. From a historical perspective, these poems, written in 1922, starkly reflect the pessimism and angst likely felt by many Korean intellectuals under Japanese colonial rule. The questioning of existence, the focus on suffering, and the critique of a seemingly unjust order could be interpreted as indirect commentary on the oppressive political and social reality of the time, making it a valuable text for understanding the psychological climate of colonial Korea.
