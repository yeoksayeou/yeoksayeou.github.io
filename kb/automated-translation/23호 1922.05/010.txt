Masses, Exercise Self-Restraint

1. If the phrase "When pushed to the extreme, a way through appears" (窮則通) points to one aspect of the natural law, then the phrase "Poverty leaves nothing undone" (窮無所不爲) may also be an explanation that penetrates the greater part of the subtleties of human sentiment. Regarding what kind of dead-end street we have now encountered, observations will naturally differ depending on the observer. However, it is an almost indefensible fact that matters often go against our intentions, plans mostly do not follow their intended course, and thus, as the weak, we may feel heartbreak, or as petty people, we may overstep (濫). Therefore, we say: do not whine recklessly like a suckling infant, nor compromise makeshiftly like a defeated fighter, but only take the proper path (正經大道) we ought to follow, loving and respecting ourselves.
Did not Kong Chungni (孔仲尼) of old once say? "If wealth and honor could be sought and obtained, even if it meant holding the whip [as a lowly groom], I would also do it. But if they cannot be obtained, I shall follow what I love."

2. If one loves one's own children too much, one might perhaps feel jealousy and hatred towards the children of others; if one laments one's own deficiencies too much, one might perhaps feel envy towards the prosperity of others. Thus, in the reverse side of the tendency to solely pursue the rights of our nation and solely strive for the protection of our spirit, we cannot know what kind of ills might arise again. Therefore, we say: do not presume to disregard others' things just because you protect your own, nor presume to destroy others' constructions just because you recognize your own deficiencies, but only proceed soundly and purely with a human heart (人間된 心步).

3. The masses (民眾) themselves are not inherently wise, and this seems especially true for our masses; yet the problems laid before us were complex and grave, difficult for anyone, no matter how wise, to readily interpret. Thus, considering by ethnicity, our people, Anglo-Americans (英米國사람), Russians (露西亞사람), Japanese (日本國사람); considering by ideology, nationalists (國粹主義者), socialists (社會主義者) (of various kinds), proponents of harmony (融和主義者), federalists (聯邦主義者); to speak more concretely, new figures such as politicians (爲政者), missionaries (宣敎者), newspaper and magazine publishers (新聞雜誌業者), those who sell their names and words (賣名賣言業者), as well as old figures (等人) (all those who position themselves as interpreters of the problem) have descended upon the heads of the masses in a manner of total mobilization. Leading this way and enticing that way, sometimes with profit (利), sometimes with authority (威), sometimes with principle (道), sometimes with righteousness (義), they have made the minds of the masses daily more complex and bewildered (眩慌). Moreover, the rampant arguments (橫議) and policy discussions (策論) of the false patriots (假志士) and false ideologues (假主義者輩) that have recently sprung up like inedible mushrooms in early March (첫 三月의 버섯) further serve to confuse the direction of the masses. Our subtle intention (微意) in urging the masses anew to exercise self-restraint also lies herein.

4. The desire to speak frankly about this and that truly feels like a fire wanting to ignite. However, as we are today, we cannot say more than this. Our only hope is that the wise reader (賢明한 讀者) will infer the other three corners from the one corner presented (一隅로써 三隅에 反함이 잇스라함). <3>

**Summary**

This 1922 editorial from *Kaebyŏk* magazine urges the Korean masses (민중) to exercise caution, self-respect, and clear thinking amidst difficult times and confusing influences. The author warns against acting out of desperation like infants or defeated fighters, and against narrow-mindedness or destructive envy born from focusing solely on one's own suffering or national rights. The piece highlights the complexity of the issues facing Koreans and criticizes various figures—politicians, missionaries, journalists, ideologues (nationalists, socialists, etc.), both foreign and domestic, old and new—for bewildering the public. It specifically condemns newly emerged "false patriots" and "false ideologues," urging the masses to maintain prudence and follow a righteous path instead of being easily swayed. The author concludes cryptically, implying constraints prevent further elaboration but hoping discerning readers understand the full message.

For historians, this article offers insight into the intellectual climate of 1920s colonial Korea. It reflects the anxieties of Korean intellectuals navigating the complex landscape of burgeoning ideologies (nationalism, socialism, etc.), foreign influences (Japanese, Western, Russian), and internal divisions within the Korean society and independence movement. The call for "self-restraint" (자중) and the warning against "false patriots" (가지사) might suggest concerns about factionalism, radical actions deemed counterproductive, or perhaps a subtle critique of certain emerging leaders or movements within the nationalist sphere. The text showcases the use of classical allusions (Confucius, Chinese idioms) alongside commentary on contemporary political actors and ideologies, characteristic of the period's intellectual discourse.

잡지명	개벽 제23호
발행일	1922년 05월 01일
기사제목	民衆이어 自重하라
기사형태	논설

民衆이어 自重하라
1. 「窮則通이란 語句가 自然의 理法의 一面을 指摘한 形喩라 하면, 「窮無所不爲」란 語句도 또한 人情의 機微의 大部를 透察한 說明이 될지라. 只今 우리의 다닥친 골목이 그 어떠한 압길을 가진 것이냐 함에 對하야는 보는 者에 依하야 그 觀察을 달리할 것은 勿論이나, 如何間 일이 만히 뜻한 바에 어기고 謀計가 主로 豫定한 바에 들지 아니하야 弱者로서 或 傷心할 수도 잇고 小人으로서 이에 넘칠(濫) 수도 잇게 된 것은 거의 辯護치 못할 事實이라. 이에 써 우리는 말하노니 젓빠는 어린애와 가티 함부로 보채지 말며 敗餘의 鬪手와 가티 苟且하게 妥協치 말고 오즉 우리의 나아갈 바 正經大道를 取하야 自愛하며 自重하라.
옛날의 孔仲尼는 일즉이 일러두지 아니하얏는가. 「富貴를 만일 求하야 어들진대 비록 채쭉을 잡는 일이라도 내가 또한 하려니와 만일 그러치 못할진대 나의 조하하는 바에 쪼츠리라」고.

2. 自己집 兒孩를 넘우 사랑하면 남의 집 兒孩를 或 嫉憎할 수도 잇스며 自己의 不足을 넘우 傷嘆하면 남들의 興隆을 或 妬忌할 수도 잇나니, 오로지 우리 民族의 權利뿐을 是圖하고 우리 精神의 護持뿐을 是務하는 그 傾向의 反面에는 또다시 어떠한 病弊를 惹起할는지도 모를 바이라. 이에서 우리는 말하노니 나의 것을 護持한다 하야 남의 것을 함부로 無視코저 말며 나의 不足을 認識하얏다 하야 남의 建設을 함부로 破壞코저 말고 오즉 人間된 心步로써 健實하게 또 純然하게 나아 감이 잇슬 것 뿐이라 한다.

3. 民衆이란 그 元軆가 聰明치 못하며 또 우리의 民衆에 잇서 別로 甚함과 갓거늘 우리네의 압헤 노힌 問題는 누구의 聰明으로도 문득 解釋하기 어려운 複雜하고 또 重大한 것이엇다. 이에 써 人族別로 치면 우리 사람, 英米國사람, 露西亞사람, 日本國사람, 主義者로 치면 國粹主義者 社會主義者(各種의) 融和主義者, 聯邦主義者, 右를 더 좀 具軆的으로 말하면 爲政者 宣敎者 新聞雜誌業者 賣名賣言業者가튼 新人. 가튼 舊人 等(다-가티 그 問題의 解釋者로 自處하는 사람)이 總動員格으로 民衆의 머리 우에 臨하야, 或은 利와 或은 威로 或은 道와 或은 義로써 이리 이끌며 저리 꾀이어써 民衆의 머리를 날로 複雜, 眩慌하게 되엇스며, 더욱 近日에 이르러 첫 三月의 버섯(먹지도 못할 것)과 가티 생겨나는 假志士 假主義者輩의 橫議와 策論은 加一層 民衆의 向方을 眩惑케 함이 잇나니 우리가 새삼스럽게 民衆의 自重을 促하는 微意-또한 여긔에 잇도다.

4. 이것 저것을 露骨的으로 說去하고 십흔 생각이 實로 불이 일어나고저 함과 갓도다. 그러나 今日의 우리로서는 보다 以上의 말을 할 수가 업도다. 다못 바라는 바는 賢明한 讀者의 一隅로써 三隅에 反함이 잇스라함 뿐이다.＜3＞
