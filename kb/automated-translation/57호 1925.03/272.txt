**Summary**

This text is the colophon or masthead for issue 57 of the Korean magazine Kaebyŏk (開闢), published on March 1, 1925. It lists the fixed price, printing and publication dates (using the Japanese Taishō era), the names and addresses of the editor (Yi Tonhwa), publisher (Yi Tusŏng), and printer (Min Yŏngsun), as well as the printing company (Taedong Inswae Chusik Hoesa) and publishing house (Kaebyŏksa) with their addresses, phone number, and postal transfer account number in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul). For historians, this colophon provides valuable concrete details about the Korean publishing industry during the Japanese colonial period. It identifies key figures and businesses involved in producing influential journals like Kaebyŏk, maps their locations within colonial Seoul (including Japanese place names like Ch'ŏngsu-ch'ŏng), and documents the logistical infrastructure (phone, postal transfer) supporting their operations.
