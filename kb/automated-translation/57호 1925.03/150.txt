Author: 八峰 (Palbong)

Brief Comments on Current Affairs
						Palbong

1. Women's Liberation Movement
I believe there are no comrades who do not know what tenant disputes are or what labor disputes are. That is how much these issues have become talked about in the world, the reality of Chosŏn today.
Why do tenant disputes arise? Why do labor disputes arise? To put it simply in one word, it is because the interests of the tenant farmer, who gets land to farm, and the interests of the landowner, who lends the land and receives rent, are different. It is also because the interests of the laborer, who is hired receiving daily or monthly wages, and the interests of the factory owner capitalist, who employs dozens or hundreds of such laborers, are different. The interests of tenants and laborers must be protected by the tenants and laborers themselves, and because the interests of capitalists and landowners are protected by the capitalists and landowners, a great conflict between people cannot help but arise here. The modern era, the world today, stands solely on relations of interest. And this has become an independent view of ethics. The beacon fire of the fourth class movement and social reform being clamored about throughout the world today truly originates from this new view of ethics.

What is the women's liberation movement? Just as tenants and laborers protect their own interests, it is one of the human movements whose starting point lies in the idea that women themselves should protect and acquire their own interests. Then, what are women's interests? Women's interests are nothing other than taking women, who have fallen into a state of slavery in a male-dominated society, and forming an independent society of women. Truly, look at the history of humankind up to this point. Is there even a trace anywhere of women forming an independent society as women themselves?<71> This proves the fact that women have never even been able to live an independent life as women themselves. Politics, law, economics, scholarship, literature and art, philosophy – is there even just one among all the superstructures of human society that was created for us women? Setting that aside, has today's society ever even given us women the right to speak, to express even a single word of opinion regarding these things? Everything, from one to ten, all things exist and operate only for those men. It goes without saying that there is a need to rescue and liberate women themselves from under the tyranny of men.

That is right. The women's liberation movement is an inevitable course of human life that must occur at some point. And it is also true that this movement is rooted in the interests of women. In the society of Chosŏn people, who lack any political or social power worth mentioning, how much interest could women possibly have, or lack? If there are any "new women" who would shrink back, implicitly believing the words, "Just manage the household well and take good care of the home," I say without hesitation: You should be ashamed of the slave mentality in your spirit!

Thus came into being the Chosŏn Women's Liberation General Alliance (朝鮮女性解放總同盟), which held its inaugural general meeting on February 8th. We new women have also awakened. It is not that there were no women's gatherings called such-and-such society before, but compared to the previous ones which were gatherings of so-called ladies with a certain kind of propertied class coloration, or gatherings of women steeped in rotten thought who came forth embracing the spirit of Jesus Christ, this is the first time for a gathering of women possessing proletarian training, tactics, and education. Does the emergence of this kind of gathering, which advocates class struggle and passionately cries for women's liberation, not signify the general awakening of us women?

However, I say this. If the fourth class movement, which is currently calling for social reform and is the vanguard embarking on that task, proceeds solely focused on the struggle for their interests from beginning to end, then after that reform is achieved, there will inevitably be another great reform. Similarly, if those among us called women's movement activists <72> carry out the movement solely for the purpose of acquiring women's interests and status from beginning to end, it is unfortunate. Our movement will be considered a failure. What is the reason? It is because it stops at being a superficial movement merely seeking to seize interests, and can never touch upon the human being itself. A liberation movement aimed only at liberation from men or from artificial systems and organizations will stop at the halfway point of humanity's goal.

What kind of being is a person? What kind of being is a woman? It must be a human movement that addresses such questions, striking at the foundation of life that arises from the depths of the heart's soul. That is right. The Women's Liberation Movement is not just a movement for women. It is merely a part of the movement for all humanity.

2. 4,000 Cases of Compulsory Execution
There was a newspaper report stating that the number of cases where citizens of Kyŏngsŏng-bu (Seoul) faced compulsory execution during the past year reached over four thousand. This is the fact that 4,000 people borrowed money from others, could not repay it, received visits from court execution officers (執行吏), and had their household assets and furniture (什物) seized. If this was the situation that occurred within Kyŏngsŏng-bu during the past year, how many people throughout all of Chosŏn must have suffered such executions? Truly, the number must be vast, and consequently, the misery of the people who suffered such calamities can easily be inferred.

Asking why they came to face execution is a pointless question. Why? Because it is an all too easily understood fact.

However, the point worthy of attention now is that the people subjected to compulsory execution are Chosŏn people, and the people carrying out the execution are Japanese people. Thus, the people being driven out day by day, that is, the people having their land taken, their houses taken, even their hand dishes taken, and standing up empty-handed, driven out from their own homeland, are Chosŏn people. The reason why the number of Japanese people coming into Chosŏn increases year by year, and in their place, Chosŏn people are driven out from within Chosŏn land year by year, is none other than such cases of compulsory execution being the cause and reason.<73>

Then why borrow money that cannot be repaid and leads to execution? Or if circumstances make borrowing unavoidable, why borrow from Japanese people instead of Chosŏn people? I know there may be people who would say such things. But, pitiful brethren, this fact is an unavoidable fate intertwined with the tears of the Chosŏn people. There is a "general trend" (大勢) that makes borrowing money absolutely necessary. There is a "system" (組織) that naturally makes it so that if one wants to borrow money, one inevitably has to go to a Japanese person to borrow. This is the necessary relationship between a conquered society and a conquering society.

However, what I want to speak about here is not to explain that general trend (大勢) more thoroughly or to dissect (解剖) that system (組織) as mentioned above, but rather to point out the most pitiful and hateful type of person among those who borrow money and face execution.

What kind of people are they? They are dissolute men and women swayed by vanity. They are men who borrow money when they don't need to, just because they want to ride in automobiles, want to take kisaeng to restaurants, want to wear Western-style suits (학구라이), want to adorn themselves, want to fool around making noise. And behind the scenes, women adrift in vanity manipulate these men like well-inflated balloons of the same kind. This is the most pitiful and infuriating type.

"Women rule!" This statement holds truth to a certain extent. Indeed, from one perspective, women can be seen as ruling male society from behind men's backs. When I see the fact that women, who possess this precious mission and inherent position, produce pitiful men, I cannot suppress my pathetic thoughts. And this sin is an evil deed that women and men must bear together. Please reflect deeply in order to support even one side of the collapsing Chosŏn society and raise it up. Then the result will not only be the ability to grasp one falling pillar, but it can also become the driving force capable of holding up and raising the entire collapsing house.

3. 88 Suicides
According to a report published by the Kyŏngsŏng-bu investigation <74>, the number of suicide cases within Kyŏngsŏng-bu alone during the past year was eighty-eight. It seems quite a lot of people were tired of living. If statistics were compiled for all of Chosŏn, the number would surely be vast.

If one is tired of living, there is no choice but to die, that much is obvious, but why were they tired of living? I wish to speak starting from here.

Now, if we classify suicides, there would be types such as pessimistic suicide, world-weary suicide, and suicide due to hardship of living. However, even for those who committed suicide out of pessimism or world-weariness, it can be asserted that 80-90% of their motives were based on hardship of living. At the same time, it can also be said that 85 or 86 out of the 88 suicides took their own lives due to hardship of living.

Then, what is this fearsome hardship of living? This is too easy to understand. Namely, this hardship of living, a peculiar product of modern commercial and industrial civilization aimed at mass production, is a fact that stands sternly present rather than a theory, so I will not explain it. And thus, this hardship of living becomes the motive for the frightening act of suicide.

Of course, suicide can be a gratifying act in certain cases. It is far more heroic than being murdered by another (他殺). However, suicide, generally speaking, especially from the viewpoint of social life, cannot avoid being an extremely cowardly act. Even if there is an obstacle on the path of life such that one cannot endure without dying, do not die! Gather the courage to take your own life and the decisiveness to abandon attachment to the world, and go charge head-on against the enemy in front, "that obstacle on the path of life"! If the hardship of living borne on your back obstructs your path of life, combine the courage, decisiveness, and capacity for action that would allow you to take your own life and abandon attachment, and assault the obstacle in question, the hardship of living! This is the repulsive force of life.

That's right! The repulsive force of life!... If you step on an earthworm, it squirms. That is the repulsion of the earthworm's life arising because its body is harmed (阻害) under a person's foot. If even an earthworm has the power to repel, how could a human not have it? Life is a flowing waterfall. If there is something blocking that waterfall,<75> the waterfall collides with that blocking obstacle, surges up once, becomes rapids, forms a torrent, and flows down with even greater force than before. This is the law (理法) of nature. And this law (理法), for humans, becomes the philosophy of life.

That's right! Hardship of living is a peculiarity of modern life, and the repulsion of life is the philosophy of living. One cannot live modern life without this repulsive force of life.

4. Peasant Movement and Labor Education
Chosŏn is, industrially, one of the most underdeveloped places in the world. There are no products worth mentioning as such. Consequently, there are no means of production either. In the global capital war, it cannot possibly even be an issue. Chosŏn's original position is that of an agricultural country.

Because Chosŏn's original position is in "agriculture," the important production here lies in the five grains (五穀). And consequently, the important people here are those who farm. The reason is that the foundation (긔초) of social life cannot be established apart from "production relations." That is, people live because they eat, wear clothes, and use things.

Therefore, the important populace of this land called Chosŏn is the peasantry. Consequently, any kind of movement that arises here, whatever its nature, must absolutely not be a movement detached from the "peasants" here. Among the 20 million population of Chosŏn, 17 million are peasants; the populace here cannot initiate any other movement excluding this vast majority (最大多數) of 17 million.

Therefore, all culture must start with the peasantry as its standard. And that peasant-standard movement cannot succeed without removing the ignorance of the peasants.

The urgent task (急務), the necessity, of peasant education and worker education truly lies here. And it goes without saying that the institutions for such enlightenment (敎化) need to be interconnected and organized.

In this sense, the fact that the "All-Chosŏn Labor Educators Conference" (全鮮勞働敎育者大會) has been initiated and will convene soon is something for which we are deeply grateful for the sake of the peasants, for Chosŏn, and for our future.<76>
<71-76>

**Summary**

This editorial by "Palbong" in the March 1925 issue of *Kaebyŏk* discusses several pressing social issues in colonial Korea. It champions the women's liberation movement, framing it alongside tenant and labor disputes as a necessary struggle for self-interest against oppression, while cautioning against focusing solely on interests without addressing deeper human values. The author laments the high number of compulsory property executions, disproportionately affecting Koreans indebted to Japanese, linking it to colonial economic exploitation but also criticizing Koreans who fall into debt through vanity. High suicide rates are attributed primarily to economic hardship ("hardship of living"), urging readers to channel despair into active resistance ("repulsion of life") against obstacles rather than succumb. Finally, it stresses the centrality of the peasantry (85% of the population) to any meaningful social movement in agricultural Chosŏn and welcomes the upcoming All-Chosŏn Labor Educators Conference as crucial for peasant and worker education. Historically, the article offers a valuable glimpse into the intersection of burgeoning socialist/nationalist thought, feminist awakening ("New Woman"), critiques of colonial economic structures, and the focus on peasant mobilization in 1920s Korea. The explicit linking of economic hardship, Japanese exploitation, and social problems like debt and suicide, alongside calls for class-based and gender-based liberation movements rooted in education, reflects key intellectual currents of the period.

時事小評
						八峰
1. 女性解放運動
小作爭議가 무엇인지 勞働爭議가 무엇인지 모르시는 동무는 업슬 줄로 생각함니다. 그만콤 이 문뎨에 대하야서는 세상에 떠들리우게 된 오늘의 조선의 현실이올시다.
소작쟁의는 무슨 까닭으로 이러남닛가? 로동쟁의는 무슨 까닭으로 이러남닛가? 그것은 간단하게 말 한 마듸로 말하야 버리면 땅을 어더가지고 농사를 짓는 소작인의 리익과 땅을 빌리어 주고 도조를 밧고 잇는 디주와의 리익이 서로 다른 까닭이며, 일급이나 혹은 월급을 바다가며 고용을 하는 로동자의 리익과 이와 가튼 로동자를 수십 명 수백 명식 사용하고 잇는 공장의 주인 자본자의 리익이 서로 다른 까닭임니다. 소작인과 로동자의 리익은 소작인과 로동자들 자신이 옹호하여야만 할 것이며 자본가와 디주의 리익은 자본가와 디주가 옹호하는 까닭으로 이곳에서 사람과 사람사이에 커드란 갈등이 생기지 아니할 수 업게 됨니다. 현대는 오늘날의 세게는 오로지 리해관게 우에 섯슴니다. 그리고 이것은 한 개의 독립한 倫理觀이 되엇슴니다. 오늘날의 온 세게에 떠들리우고 잇는 제사게급의 운동 사회개조의 봉화불은 진실로 이 새로운 倫理觀에서 출발한 것임니다.
녀성해방운동은 무엇이냐? 그것은 소작인과 로동자의 리익으로 소작인과 로동자 자신이 옹호함과 가티 녀성 자신의 리익을 녀성 자신들의 옹호하고 획득하자하는 생각에 그 출발뎜을 둔 인간운동 중의 그 하나임니다. 그러면 녀셩의 리익이란는 것은 무엇이냐? 녀셩의 리익은 다른 것이 아니다. 남자젼제의 사회에서 노예의 상태에 떠러저 잇는 녀성자신을 빼어 내어다가 한 개의 독립한 녀성의 사회를 이루는 것이다. 참말로 이때까지의 인류의 녁사를 보십시요. 어느 곳에 녀성이 녀성 자신으로 독립한 사회를 형성하엿든 흔적이나마 잇슴닛가.＜71＞ 이것은 녀성이 녀성 자신으로 독립한 생활을 하여보지도 못하엿다는 사실을 반증하는 것임니다. 政治가, 法律이, 經濟가, 學術이 文藝가, 哲學이 사람의 사회의 온갓 上部構造가 다만 하나이라도 조흐니 우리들 녀성을 위하야서 지여진 것이 잇슴닛가? 그것은 고사하고 다만 이러한 것에 대하야 다만 한 마듸의 意見을 발표할 만한 發言權이나마 오늘날의 사회는 우리들 녀성에게 주어본 일이 잇슴닛가? 모든 것이 하나로부터 열까지가 왼갓 것이 저들 남자를 위하여서의 것으로만 존재하고 활동함니다. 녀성 자신을 남자의 젼횡 아래에서 구원하여다가 해방할 필요가 잇슴은 두 말을 할 것도 업는 바의 일임니다.
그럿슴니다. 녀성해방운동은 반드시 언제이든지 한번 잇서야만 할 인생생활의 필연의 經路임니다. 그리고 이 운동은 녀성의 리해관게에 그 뿌리를 둔 것임도 사실임니다. 온갓 정치뎍 사회뎍 다만 오즉지 안은 귄력이나마도 업는 조선 사람의 사회에서 녀성의 리익이 잇스면 얼마나 잇고 업스면 얼마나 업겟느냐. 살님이나 잘하고 집안이나 잘 다사려라-는 말에 고지듯고서 주저안질 신녀성이 잇슬진댄 저는 주저하지 안코 말함니다. 당신은 당신의 정신에 奴隷根性이 잇슴을 붓그러워 하십시오!
이와 가티 하야 생긴 것이 지난 2月 8日에 創立總會를 한 朝鮮女性解放總同盟임니다. 우리들 신녀성도 자각하엿슴니다. 무슨 會니 무슨 會니 하는 녀자들의 모임이 이때까지 하나도 업섯든 것은 아니지마는 전에 되얏든 것들이 엇던 종류의 有産階級的 色彩를 가진 所謂 淑女들의 모힘이거나 또는 예수 크리스트의 졍신을 꿰여차고 나선 썩어문드러진 思想에 물저진 녀자들의 모힘임에 대항하야 無産階級的 訓鍊과 戰術과 敎養을 가진 녀자들의 모힘으로는 이번이 처음임니다. 階級鬪爭를 鼓吹하고 女性解放을 血叫하는 이러한 종류의 모힘이 이러낫슴은 確實히 우리들 녀성의 일반뎍 자각을 의미하는 것이 아니고 무엇이겟슴닛가.
