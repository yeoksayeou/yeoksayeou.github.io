발행일	1925년 03월 01일
기사제목	狂亂 (Frenzy)
필자	星海
기사형태	소설 (Novel/Fiction)

狂亂
						星海
Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! The Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn stream that pierces through the center of Kyŏngsŏng from east to west!
What a beautiful name, Ch'ŏnggye (Clear Stream). But this well-named Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn is not a Clear Stream, but a Muddy Stream. A Filthy Stream. Seeing that turbid water flowing long and dingy like a dirty string through the middle of black and reddish mud-sand flats, who could possibly call it a Clear Stream!?
The well-named Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn is the path that receives all the filthy water dirtied by the 300,000 souls of Kyŏngsŏng. All the grime water of the thoroughly cunning city dwellers flows into it. Feces, urine, rotten fish water, rotten vegetable water, rotten grain water—all the filth discarded by people as dirty flows into it. Yet, the city dwellers still call this Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn. The city dwellers, whose nerves have become as sensitive as can be, still call it Ch'ŏnggye. Calling it Ch'ŏnggye, they feel no contradiction, no disharmony.
- murmuring thus, Yŏngsun walked along the northern bank of Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn. It was sunset on an early spring day. The sun, hanging about midway on the line connecting the peaks of Mongmyŏksan and Inwangsan, still brightly illuminated the northern bank of Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn. The wind that blew over the stream from time to time still carried a mix of winter's chill. However, the light reflected from the walls of the houses lining the bank held a spring-like warmth. The people coming and going along this bank all seemed to covet the warmth of the sunlit side, as very few were seen walking on the shaded side of the stream. It seemed only those with specific business on the south bank walked there. And at the mouths of the ditches and drains pouring filth into the stream, the icicles that had frozen throughout the three winter months had not yet melted. It <35> looked as if they were mocking, sticking out their tongues with mouths agape, asking, "Is it still a Clear Stream even when all imaginable filth flows into it?"

Right! It's still Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! It's a Clear Stream in another sense. Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn for the sake of cleaning the capital city. The water when it springs from the crevices between the rocks in the valleys of Paegak, Mongmyŏk, and Inwang—that water, of course, was known. It was not water mixed with this kind of grime. Its purity from when it seeped out from between rocks or from under tree roots was already lost. Lost for the sake of cleaning Kyŏngsŏng. Yet, even carrying all the filth, it flows on without any complaint. - he murmured again. The setting sun, as if wanting to give away all its warm rays at once without any lingering attachment, seemed hasty.

Yŏngsun soon reached Kwansugyo Bridge. The vermilion color of Ch'angdŏkkung's Tonhwamun Gate, visible straight ahead through the opening beyond the bridge, appeared even more vivid, slantingly receiving the faint rays of the setting sun. On the new road, straight as if drawn with an inked line, human shadows appeared sparsely, flickering white. He turned north again and headed onto the Chongno street where the trams ran.

Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! What a beautiful name, Ch'ŏnggye! Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn before these 300,000 ogre-like people, before the churchmen who are the incarnations of falsehood, gathered towards Samgaksan! Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn some 500 years ago! That was the clear water flowing as it emerged from the crevices of Pukak's rocks, as it hid beneath the roots of Mongmyŏk's trees, as it bathed the grassy turf of Inwang, winding through the white sand, between paddy ditches, under field banks, letting baby fish play—the Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn that flowed! Ah! How beautiful Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn must have been!
- he murmured again.

He turned again towards Tongdaemun and walked. Only the dark outline of the city gate stood tall at the end of the broad, gently curving main road. Trams rolled by, clanging their bells, drawing lines. Cars sped away, sputtering, spewing gasoline fumes and dust. Ox carts and horse carts rolled by, clattering. The panting sound of a man pulling a rickshaw could be heard.

Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! The well-named Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! The Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn that flows only with grime water, despite being called Clear Stream!
But who would say that the mighty Han'gang river, into which this grimy Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn flows, is turbid? Again, who could call the vast ocean into which the Han'gang flows <36> a filthy pond?

Right! Before the great – before the absolutely great – there is neither filth nor purity. Neither turbidity nor clarity. Neither evil nor good. When returning to the absolutely great, one cannot bring sin or evil. Nor can one bring goodness. Only its intrinsic nature is accepted by the absolutely great. Just as that vast ocean... that great sea... that great river... does not consciously distinguish between clear and turbid – he murmured.

A gentleman in an overcoat with a fur scarf pulled up deep around his neck passes by. A beggar with a straw mat piece draped over his shoulder like a cape sits crouched in the sun. A powdered beauty in a silk durumagi coat, emitting the scent of perfume, walks slowly by. An old woman with a bundle on her head crosses the tram tracks in front of an oncoming tram with hurried steps.

Yŏngsun reached the Chongno Sach'ome tram intersection. He turned again towards Ch'anggyŏngwŏn. The brick wall of the Monopoly Tobacco Factory looked like a prison. The smell of tobacco and oil stung his nose. Pale-faced workers carrying lunch boxes came out onto the street.

He arrived in front of the Government-General Hospital (Ch'ongdokpu Ŭiwŏn). There seemed to be a smell of medicine. The groans of patients seemed audible. A group of young nurses returning home descended the slope inside the hospital grounds lined with poplar trees. From within Ch'anggyŏngwŏn, the drawn-out cry of a crane could be heard. And even the sound of flapping wings could be heard. It felt as though the moans of all the animals in cages and enclosures were audible. A dark aura seemed to emanate from the pine forest behind the hospital.

Yŏngsun stopped walking. His head was spinning violently. His vision went dark. He felt dizzy.

He asked as if someone who could answer his questions stood beside him. But he only asked in his mind and answered in his mind.

"Where are you going now?"
"To the inn..."
"To do what?.." <37>
"To eat... and sleep..."
"What kind of food..."
"Food bought with money... tasteless food, maybe leftover dregs (텍찍기) or something..."
"And sleep?..."
"A single room... a room painted with bedbug blood! And a room as cold as ice..."
"What's the point of eating?"
"To not die..."
"What's the point of sleeping?..."
"Just... because I'm sleepy -"
"What's the point of not dying?..."
"To live..."
"What's the point of living?..."
"Just... I don't know..."

Yŏngsun started walking again. "Just... everyone... just trying to live!" he mumbled. He stopped again. The sun still shone brightly on the Ch'anggyŏngwŏn palace wall. The reflected light dazzled his eyes, making his head feel like it was spinning wildly.

Like this, like a fever patient, changing between hot and cold every second and minute, what was swirling in Yŏngsun's mind?

He asked again. But no one outside of Yŏngsun's mind knew of this dialogue.

"Then, are you satisfied just living, eating that tasteless food, maybe leftover dregs or something?"
"No! I want something more delicious. The pure taste of nobility – I want to eat the ambrosia of the world."
"Are you satisfied with a single room painted with bedbug blood?..." <38>
"No. I want a house like the Epang Palace. I want high terraces and vast halls. I want a dragon palace like in fairy tales, built with amber foundation stones and coral pillars."
"Do you always desire solitude?"
"No. I want all the beauties of the world before me. Yang Kwei-fei... Cleopatra..."
"Are you content being the unknown Yŏngsun forever?"
"No! I hate being this worthless Yŏngsun. Like Muhammad, valiantly! Like Buddha, compassionately! Like Jesus, tragically... I want such a name!"

The sound of loud laughter reached Yŏngsun's mind's ear. He took a few more steps. The dialogue began again.

"What are you eating now? And where are you sleeping?"
"The same as I said before! Food no different than leftover dregs! A single room painted with bedbug blood! That's it."
"Who are you with?"
"I am alone. If there is anything else... anything I desire... it's the beauty in the oil painting hanging on the wall... with her voluptuous body..."
"Who knows your name?"
"The inn owner, colleagues at work, a few acquaintances – besides them, no one knows my name."
"How do you make a living?"
"By earning... receiving a salary."
"How much is the salary?..."
"Barely enough to get cold rice meals for a month – or to give a night's rest to a tired body."

Yŏngsun thought. How miserable is his reality – and how contradictory...
He saw delicacies from mountains and seas. Golden palaces and jade pavilions shimmered. The coquettish faces of the world's beauties appeared. History <39> appeared. Portraits of great men appeared. Hymns were heard. Buddhist chants were heard.

All this was illusion to Yŏngsun. A hallucination he saw almost pathologically – convulsively. And then, appearing even more clearly before his eyes, was the money filling the safe he handled frequently every day. Bundles of paper currency. He imagined scattering it at the Chongno intersection. It flew here and there, scattered by the wind. Passersby all rushed towards it. Old people, young people, beggars, the rich, gentlemen, students, beauties, laborers...

Yŏngsun seemed to be wandering in a different kind of country than before. He seemed almost unaware of where his feet were planted. He felt this much confusion in his head. He shouted again.

"Cast away your shallow conscience! Sever your lukewarm desire for life! And bury that conscience and desire for life deep and hard at the sunny edge of the back hill! And let's play just once!" he...

He turned his feet, abandoning the tranquility of the street in front of Ch'anggyŏngwŏn, and headed back towards the heat and noise of Chongno thoroughfare.

Where was Yŏngsun heading, running almost convulsively in such a chaotic state? And for what purpose? The cry of the crane from the Ch'anggyŏngwŏn pond sounded long. The gradually lengthening early spring sun had already hidden its face in the western sky.

The place Yŏngsun rushed towards so convulsively, excitedly, unconsciously, was the company where he worked. That company was run by a private individual. Yŏngsun was one of the company's faithful employees. But why did Yŏngsun, who was returning to his inn, turn back towards the company? Moreover, unconsciously... convulsively... why? - Yŏngsun's actions, performed a few hours later, eloquently showed the reason for turning back.

Yŏngsun appeared at the Chongno intersection late at night. The long hand of the clock on the tower of the Chongno Police Station pointed to X (10). The short hand pointed to IX (9). Yŏngsun was not alone; he was walking towards Namdaemun Street with 2-3 colleagues, chatting loudly about something. Yŏngsun, walking and talking loudly, looked extremely excited.

"Hey, what's gotten into you tonight?" <40> a friend's voice was heard asking.
"What do you mean, what's gotten into me? A man has his moments... Yŏngsun, I am no longer the Yŏngsun of yesterday..."
came the excited reply from Yŏngsun.
"Truly incomprehensible! The sun will probably rise in the west tomorrow!"
another person's voice was heard.
"Why?"
was Yŏngsun's excited retort.
"Where are we going?..."
"Let's go to S Kwan (S館) and have a round..."
Chatting like this, Yŏngsun's group almost occupied one side of the Namdaemun Street sidewalk.

They entered the S Kwan restaurant.

Yŏngsun, appearing under the dazzling electric lights of the restaurant, was truly an unrecognizable Yŏngsun compared to before. He was new from head to toe. Even his complexion had turned pale. He no longer looked like the Yŏngsun who used to wear a worn-out, stained Western suit, sit slumped on a chair in front of his desk, fiddle with an abacus, and scribble in ledgers. He didn't look like the man who used to bow his head saying "Yes, yes" in front of the executives. A jeweled ring glittered on his finger. A watch chain like a metal cord hung on his chest. His neck, deeply wrapped in a fur-lined overcoat, looked menacing like a bulldog's head. Beneath the hem of the new overcoat, patent leather shoes gleamed.

They found a particularly quiet room and entered.

The eyes of the restaurant boys glittered strangely. Even the boys seemed unable to easily see customers like Yŏngsun and his companions, dressed entirely in new things from head to toe – dressed only in such expensive items. <41>

Kisaeng were called.
Food arrived.
They got drunk.
Conversation began.
Everyone except Yŏngsun regarded all this with suspicion.

Yŏngsun was drunk. Everything he saw spun before his eyes.

He spoke with a slurred tongue. Towards the kisaeng –
"Hey! What do you girls like best? In the world, I mean... the very..."
A kisaeng holding a bottle to pour alcohol – the one who seemed the most modest among them – smiled faintly and said,
"What's the use of knowing what we like? Just have your drink..."
How did this sound to the heavily intoxicated Yŏngsun? That soft voice... Yŏngsun gulped down the drink in one breath, let out a hot sigh, and began lecturing the kisaeng anew.
"What did you say your name was... What Hongnyŏn? Oh right! Nanhyang? And what did that girl say her name was? Right! Oksŏm? Hmm..."
"You're drunk..."
"What do you like best?"
"What's the use of knowing that?"
"Shall I say it – Money? Money?"
"Do you dislike money, sir?"
"Right! Right! Who dislikes money? Within the scope of what you know... that is." <42>
"Please stop talking like that!"
"Stop?.... Ha.... ha... Hey girls! If you had lots of money too, you would have been called Miss So-and-so of such-and-such house... or... Madam So-and-so, wife of Mr. So-and-so, that's what people would say! It's money, right? Money, right? As long as you have money, that's all that matters?..."
"Please stop! Why are you being so crude?"
"Crude? Talking about money?"

Yŏngsun put his hand into his suit pocket. The hand that went in came out trembling slightly. A wad of paper currency was clutched in it. He slapped the table once with the wad and said,
"This is money! Take this money... hmm... There's a folk song like that – The capable but wretched fellow – the wretched but capable fellow... The capable wench, the wretched wench – the wretched wench, the capable wench! How frightening this saying is! How it exposes the depravity of human life!" he shouted in a declamatory tone. The room fell silent.

"You're too drunk,"
one friend said. But his voice was also slurred.

Yŏngsun thrust the wad of currency towards where the kisaeng were.
"Here! Take this! This thing you trust the most. No. This thing the whole world bows down to -" he shouted.
But no one picked it up. Seeing this, Yŏngsun shouted again.
"So, you still seem to have a conscience that prevents you from taking unjust gains beyond your labor! But in this world..."
"Hey, hey, you're drunk! Drunk... What is this! There's a limit to insulting people!" a friend said.

Yŏngsun laughed, "Ha... ha..." and shouted. <43>
"Do you people even have a concept of insult? Do you have the senses to feel insulted? It's a miracle! The insult I speak of? What kind of money is honor? Do you know what property is? What did Plato (프라톤) say?"
"Is this man really drunk? Where on earth did you get so much money?..."
"No need to say it! I stole it, stole it!"

How great a shock must Yŏngsun's words "stole it" have given them? And how easily must it have resolved their earlier questions?
The drunkenness they had felt instantly evaporated, and their minds cleared.

Yŏngsun shouted again.
"Don't be alarmed! My friends! What does a diamond ring have to do with me? Look at this finger! Look at this finger calloused from flicking abacus beads! And is a platinum watch and gold chain even appropriate for me? For me, who barely earns enough for meals each month – and for me, who sweats blood every day, these clothes are unreasonably excessive... I knew that, and doesn't everyone else know it too? But as a result of stealing – as a result of property coming into my hands – I have tasted, if only for a moment, this extravagant life, this luxurious pleasure that the rich bastards of this world enjoy! This kind of pleasure! This luxury – I experienced the depraved life of humanity as a result of stolen property! However, the more skillful the means and methods of stealing, the longer one can enjoy such pleasure and luxury! My means were not subtle! My method was wrong! Therefore, I will probably only be able to enjoy this small pleasure and luxury for just one night! Understand? Hmm... I might be thrown into a prison darker than this night, even tonight! Of course, it would be fitting to go in! Since I willingly rejoiced in this depravity of humanity? But it's a bit perplexing that those whose methods and means are skillful remain as they are! Ha... ha... Hey kisaeng! Pick up that money! Without a word... Hey, do you know what my name is, flowing through the middle of the capital? It's Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn! The Ch'ŏnggye you see in the sijo poems you sing! Even if sewage flows, it's Ch'ŏnggye! Even if filthy sludge flows, it's Ch'ŏnggye! No one calls Ch'ŏnggye <44> Takkyе (Muddy Stream) or *kyе (Filthy Stream) just because sludge or sewage flows in it. The world is all like that! You refuse the money I give you because I said it was stolen!"

Yŏngsun spoke at length up to this point. Then he let out a hot breath. Everyone in the room just stared at Yŏngsun's face, as if they didn't understand what was happening.

Yŏngsun shouted again.
"Right! The money possessed by the rich! People will regard it as different from the money I have now and will all bow and accept it. No. They will wish to bow and receive it! Ha... ha..."

A grim atmosphere swirled in the room. Everyone's complexion had clearly changed. They regarded everything that happened tonight as unfortunate. They wished they could vomit out what they had eaten and drunk if possible. One among them said, "I'm leaving..." and staggered out. Yŏngsun let him go without a word. Another person left in the same way. The kisaeng also left one by one, two by two. What remained was Yŏngsun and the messy table (浪籍한 孟盤). Yŏngsun muttered to himself.

"Ah! You who are tightly bound by something! Stop it! Even the Takkyе (Muddy Stream) that is Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn becomes clear when it enters the Han'gang, and appears clearer when it enters the Great Sea. Before Great Nature – before the absolutely great – there is neither good nor evil! What I did today may undoubtedly be a sin! But Great Nature – the absolutely great will forgive it! When it reaches that point, it will be purified... Ah! I don't know..."

A boy came in. Yŏngsun grabbed a handful of money as it came to hand and gave it to him.

He staggered out of S Kwan.

The night was already deep. The cold wind whipped past, striking his ears. He came out onto the sidewalk next to the tram tracks. The neatly lined streetlights stood quietly guarding the cold night.

Beside the staggering Yŏngsun, something dark, hunched over, followed him, holding out a hand. <45>
"Sir! Please give me a penny! I can't sleep at the Salvation Army because I don't have 5 chon. Please spare a penny, sir! Uheung..." A sound came, whether crying or shivering, it was unclear. It was a beggar with his back covered by a piece of sackcloth. Behind this beggar, another dark shadow followed.

Yŏngsun stopped. And spoke in a hungry voice.
"So you're a beggar! Among the worthless animals, the most worthless beggar? The most docile beggar? The most cowardly beggar? What's the point of begging like that? Don't you know how much warm food there is in this world? Don't you know how many warm places to sleep there are? Even without begging like this, there are plenty of ways to get food and sleep... yet-"

At that moment, someone grabbed Yŏngsun's arm firmly, saying "Hey! Where are you going?". Yŏngsun turned his eyes from the beggar to the one grabbing his arm.

"Where are you going? Come over here! There's something to investigate..."

Who could this speaker be? Needless to say, it was an official (관헌) who had been tailing him due to his suspicious appearance and behavior (行色 不審). Yŏngsun already knew.

He thrust one hand into his pocket. What did the hand thrust inside grasp? He had no weapon (寸鐵). The hand pulled sharply out of the pocket held a wad of banknotes. He threw it into the air.

"And shouted. Beggar! Pick that up! And abandon your lukewarm desire for life! To save corrupt humanity, depraved humanity, one must abandon that! You corrupt one! You depraved one! Pick that up! Quick..."

The wad of paper currency hit a utility pole with a thud and scattered down with a rustle. The fallen notes fluttered in the cold wind.

"Quick, pick it up! Pick it up! That money, the only thing that can save you!"

But the beggar just stared up blankly at the glittering eyes of the official, standing there trembling.

The official's whistle blew, wh-ee... whirrr. The money tumbled and rustled in the wind. (End)
- Night of Feb. 16th -<46>

**Summary**

The story "Frenzy" (狂亂) follows Yŏngsun, a disillusioned white-collar worker in colonial Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), on a single evening. Initially contemplating the polluted Ch'ŏnggyech'ŏn stream, symbolic of the city's moral decay beneath a veneer of modernity, Yŏngsun feels trapped by his meager existence and yearns for wealth, power, and pleasure. In a sudden, "convulsive" act, he embezzles a large sum of money from his company. He then indulges in a night of lavish spending with colleagues, shocking them with his transformation and cynical pronouncements about money, morality, and the hypocrisy of society. His frenzy culminates in him throwing the stolen money into the street, urging a beggar to take it, before being apprehended by an official. The story ends on his arrest, leaving his ultimate fate ambiguous but implying imprisonment.

From a historical perspective, the story vividly portrays the social stratification and psychological pressures within colonial Korean society. Yŏngsun's alienation, his critique of the disparity between the ideal (the name "Clear Stream") and the squalid reality, and his desperate act fueled by a desire to escape his powerless position reflect the anxieties and potential moral compromises faced by educated Koreans under Japanese rule. The stark contrast between the wealthy elite (implied) and the poverty of beggars, alongside the presence of Japanese colonial infrastructure like the Government-General Hospital and the police, provides a snapshot of urban life and power dynamics in 1920s Kyŏngsŏng. Historians might find the depiction of class consciousness, the critique of capitalism intertwined with colonial modernity, and the protagonist's existential crisis particularly revealing.
