Magazine Title	개벽 제57호
Publication Date	1925년 03월 01일
Article Title	人造勞働者(前承) (Artificial Laborers (Continued))
Author	채퍽크(作) 朴英熙(譯) (Čapek (Author), Pak Yŏnghŭi (Translator))
Article Type	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Artificial Laborers
						Čapek (Author), Pak Yŏnghŭi (Translator)
Act 2
Helena's drawing room.
On the left, there is a door covered with woolen cloth and a door leading to the music hall. On the right, a door leads to Helena's bedroom. In the center, there is a window overlooking the sea and the harbor. An unusually shaped desk, sofa, chairs, etc. A desk with an electric stand. To the right is a stove, and above it, an electric light. The entire room is modern and feminine. Five years have passed since Act 1.
As the curtain rises, Domin stands by the window looking outside. (Someone, perhaps Domin himself or implying another presence?), surveying the surroundings, pulls out a pistol. The sound of knocking on the door. Fabry and Hallemeier enter from the left, carrying flowers and a vase.

Fabry: Where should I put this?

Hallemeier: Shh! (Putting down the flowers he was holding, he points towards Helena's room.) The young mistress is still sleeping. When she sleeps, she knows no troubles.

Domin: My wife sleeps, knowing nothing.

Fabry: (Arranging flowers in the vase) I just hope nothing happens today—.

Hallemeier: (Sorting the flowers) Stop saying such things! Oh, Harry! This is a beautiful Cyclamen. What do you think? A new seed (種子). I recently cultivated it. And— Cyclamen, that is Helena's name.

Domin: (Looking out the window) I can't see the ship at all from here.

Hallemeier: Be quiet. The mistress shouldn't hear.

Domin: At any rate, the Ultimatum at least arrived on schedule.

Fabry: (Moving away from the flowers) So, you really intend to do it today?

Domin: Well, what should be done? How is it?<61>
Aren't those flowers beautiful?

Hallemeier: (Approaching Domin) That one is called Primrose, newly created this time. And this one is Jasmine, also newly created. I have invented a way for flowers to grow quickly. I have also invented flowers transformed into various other shapes. Soon, I will make even more wondrous flowers bloom.

Domin: What about next year?

Fabry: You may not know, but something big happened in Appul—

Domin: Quietly—

Helena: (Calling from the left) Nana!

Domin: It seems my wife has woken up. You two should leave.
(All exit stealthily through the front left door. Nana enters from the back left door.)

Nana: What a dreadful commotion. Oh, those devils. There's truly nothing to be done—

Helena: Nana! Come here and help me get dressed.

Nana: Yes, I'm coming. So, the mistress has finally woken up. (Goes behind Helena and helps her dress) Truly, those bastards!

Helena: (Turning to look at Nana) Who are you talking about?

Nana: Turn this way if you wish, please. But if you move your body like this, I can't tie the laces properly.

Helena: Oh my, what are you so angry about?

Nana: Really, they are terrifying people. Those bastards—

Helena: You mean the artificial laborers?

Nana: I don't even want to say the name of such creatures.

Helena: Did something happen?

Nana: Another one went berserk. That one smashed sculptures in the drawing-room, tore pictures, ground its teeth, and foamed at the mouth—just like a madman. Worse than a beast (즘생).

Helena: Who was it?

Nana: It was that one. Yes, does that one even have a name like other people? The one who looks after the library, I mean.

Helena: You mean Radius?

Nana: Yes, that bastard. I was truly startled. Even the most<62> hideous spider (蜘蛛) wouldn't be as hateful as them.

Helena: But, Nana! Why don't you feel sorry for the artificial laborers?

Nana: Oh—no. If you were to meet them, Mistress, you would surely be startled. Truly startled. I don't think they are pitiful.

Helena: I am not startled. Truly, I'm not startled. Nana! I just feel sorry for those artificial laborers.

Nana: No. You would surely be startled too, Mistress. There isn't anyone who isn't startled. Even dogs (犬) are startled when they see Artificial People (人造人). That's why they won't accept even a piece of meat from them. Moreover, if dogs realize they are artificial people, they wag their tails and bark.

Helena: Because dogs can't distinguish things well.

Nana: Oh—no. Compared to artificial people, dogs are far superior. Even horses (馬) neigh immediately when they encounter them. And also, artificial people cannot have children, but dogs have puppies (색기). Yes? Every beast has offspring. But—.

Helena: Nana? Hurry up and dress me.

Nana: It's all done. For artificial people to have no children is to defy God (한우님).—

Helena: There's a very nice smell, what is it?

Nana: It's the smell of flowers.

Helena: What flowers? Where did they come from?

Nana: Here, here you are. Turn this way, please.

Helena: (Turns around) Ah—truly, it has become so lovely, hasn't it, Nana? What else might happen today?

Nana: I don't know. Perhaps the end of the world might come.
(Domin's whistling is heard.)

Helena: Look here. Just a moment—.
(Domin enters.)

Helena: So, what are these flowers for?

Domin: Try to guess, if you can.

Helena: Well. But it's not my birthday.

Domin: There's something even better than that.

Helena: I don't know. Please tell me. Yes.<63>

Domin: Today marks the 5th anniversary of Helena's arrival here.

Helena: 5 years? Today? Oh my, really!

Nana: Call me if you need anything. (Exits)

Helena: (Goes to Domin and kisses him) You remember so well!

Domin: No. It sounds funny, but I had actually forgotten.

Helena: Liar—but—

Domin: Other people reminded me.

Helena: Other people? Who?

Domin: Busman, Mr. Hallemeier, and several others— Look here! Put your hand in this pocket. Yes?

Helena: (Puts her hand in Domin's pocket and takes out a small box. Holding the box, she takes out a pearl necklace) Oh! Pearls! Are these for me?

Domin: These are from Mr. Busman.

Helena: Really? But is it alright for me to accept this? May I keep it?

Domin: Of course. Now put your hand in this other pocket.

Helena: Where? (Takes out a pistol 「短銃」 from Domin's pocket) What is this?

Domin: Ah! Not that one. (Takes the pistol from Helena's hand and puts it back in his pocket) There, try again.

Helena: Why are you carrying a pistol like this?

Domin: I put it in there by mistake, without realizing.

Helena: You never carried one like this before.

Domin: Yes, yes. Now, hurry, try again.

Helena: (Takes another small box from Domin's pocket and opens it) Oh, a cameo! (玉石) A Greek cameo, isn't it?

Domin: Of course. Mr. Fabry said so.

Helena: Fabry? Then is this from Mr. Fabry?

Domin: Of course. (Opens the left door) Look at this. Come here and look.

Helena: Ah, truly, it's beautiful. Who is this from? (Runs inside the door)

Domin: (Standing at the door) This is from Mr. Alquist. And that is—

Helena: (From inside the room) I know. This one is from you, isn't it?<64>

Domin: Isn't there a label attached?

Helena: From Mr. Gall.
(Comes back out of the door.)
Receiving so much like this, what should I do?

Domin: Come here again. This is from Mr. Hallemeier.

Helena: Such beautiful flowers?

Domin: Of course. These flowers are newly created. They're called Cyclamen. Helena! He specially cultivated them to give to Helena. He said these flowers are as lovely as Helena. It's true. Exactly so!

Helena: But why are all of you doing this—

Domin: Because everyone likes Helena so very much. What I'm giving Helena is nothing special, but—look out that window!

Helena: Where?

Domin: Towards the harbor.

Helena: Oh… there's a newly built ship!

Domin: That will be Helena's ship.

Helena: Mine? Why are you giving it to me?

Domin: To travel on that ship—to console your heart.

Helena: But isn't that a warship (軍艦)?

Domin: A warship? Why would you think that? That ship (뱨) is just a bit larger and sturdier than ordinary ships.

Helena: Yes. But it has cannons (大砲), doesn't it?

Domin: Yes, it has cannons. Helena will travel like a queen, you see, Helena!

Helena: What's wrong? Has something happened?

Domin: Well, anyway, why don't you try wearing these pearls around your neck. (Sits down)

Helena: But you haven't heard any bad news, have you?

Domin: How could there be? No letters have come for this entire week.

Helena: Telegrams too?

Domin: No telegrams either.

Helena: Why?

Domin: It's a holiday for us. Everyone is just taking naps in the office. No letters, no telegrams, things are going very well.<65>

Helena: (Sits down beside Domin) Then you'll be with me today, won't you? Yes? Tell me.

Domin: Of course—yes, look, I'm right here beside Helena now. Can you remember this day five years ago?

Helena: You are truly a terrible gentleman. Forcing me into marriage like that. I was startled then. Yes, that's right. Do you know that I came here to incite a revolt among the Artificial People (人造人)?

Domin: (Jumping up) A revolt of the Artificial Laborers (人造勞働者)?

Helena: Why are you acting like this?

Domin: (Laughing) Hahaha! A rebellion of the Artificial Laborers, what a fine idea. (Sits down again) But it would be a better idea to make nails (釘) rebel against screws. Anyway, Helena, you are a great woman. You have changed our minds.

Helena: (Sitting on the arm of Domin's chair) I have simply yielded to all of you. You are all certain and strong people. I was a weak girl.—Helplessly wandering on the path—

Domin: Wandering on the path?

Helena: Yes. In a large forest (樹森). My thoughts became useless before your confidence. For these five years, I have constantly worried. But you haven't worried at all, have you? Even when things became utterly uninteresting—

Domin: What became uninteresting?

Helena: Your plan (計畵), I mean. You must remember. The laborers in America rebelled against the Artificial Laborers and smashed the Artificial People. So society, to counter this rebellion, even gave weapons to the Artificial Laborers. And then the government made soldiers out of the Artificial Laborers, which instead caused countless wars. Right?

Domin: (Gets up and walks around) It's not that we didn't think of that. Helena? Huh! Before something new arises, everything is troublesome.

Helena: You truly conquered with great strength. The entire world (全世界) surrendered before you. (Stands up) Right? Please.<66>

Domin: Why are you like this?

Helena: (Blocking the walking Domin) Close the factory doors and leave this place. All together.

Domin: Why do you want to say that?

Helena: Just because. Everyone leave, please?

Domin: (Pushing Helena aside and walking away) That cannot be done. At any rate, in this current situation (形便)—

Helena: Do it immediately. There is something I am truly worried about.

Domin: (Taking Helena's hand) What is it you're worried about?

Helena: Just because. I feel like something is definitely going to fall on our heads and we won't be able to do anything about it. Yes, it's my wish. Everyone leave this place together. And let's find a place where there's nobody else, yes? Mr. Alquist will build a house for us to live in, and then in the meantime, sons and daughters will be born. And—

Domin: And what else?

Helena: And there, we can mend our ways and live our true lives.
(The telephone bell rings.)

Domin: (Leaving Helena's side) Just a moment.
(At the telephone) Hello, hello— That's right. What? Yes! I'll be right there. (Hangs up the receiver) Fabry wants me to come over for a bit.

Helena: (Grabbing Domin) What is it?

Domin: Yes, I'll tell you when I get back. Now, I'll just be gone for a moment. Don't go outside.
(Exits quickly to the left)

Helena: (Muttering) Again, what has happened now! Nana! Nana! Come quickly!

Nana: (Entering from the left) Mistress, what is it?

Helena: Find me the latest newspaper! Quickly! Go look in the old master's bedroom.

Nana: Yes. (Exits to the left)

Helena: (Puts binoculars to her eyes and looks towards the harbor) Oh my, it's a warship. U-R-G, the Ultimatum. They are loading everything up.

Nana: (Enters carrying newspapers) The old master just scattered the newspapers all over the floor. So I just brought them carelessly like this.<67>

Helena: (Hurriedly unfolding the newspapers) These are all old newspapers. They're from a week ago. (Throws the newspapers down)
(Nana picks them up, takes glasses out of her apron pocket, puts them on, and reads the newspaper.)

Helena: Is there anything? Nana! For some reason, my heart won't rest easy. I wonder if something terrible is happening—

Nana: (Starts reading aloud) "War in the Balkans (빠루깐)." Something unpleasant again. It must be God's punishment. But war might break out right here again soon. Is the Balkans a distant place?

Helena: Very far, indeed. Don't read such things. The Balkans are always fighting wars.

Nana: There will always be fights. Why do they keep making Artificial People here and selling them off as soldiers (兵隊) by the thousands (몃 千名)?

Helena: But because there's no other way. Because we didn't know. Also, even the old master didn't know what they were used for. Whenever an order for Artificial Laborers came, they just had to send them out from here, that's why.

Nana: Wouldn't it have been best if the old master hadn't made them in the first place? (Looks at the newspaper again)

Helena: Stop reading now. I don't even want to hear about things like war.

Nana: (Reads) "The Artificial Laborer Army—as a last resort (最後—最後의 手段), occupied the land and massacred (虐殺) more than 700,000 citizens—"

Helena: Oh my goodness, what? (Looks at the newspaper) "Massacred more than 700,000 citizens. It was done by the commander's order (司令官의 命令). The cause of this incident (事件) was—"

Nana: (Finding the headline) "Rebellion against the government in Madrid (마도리트). Artificial People infantry (人造人步兵)—fired upon the crowd (群衆). Caused 9,000 casualties (死傷者)—"

Helena: Stop reading.

Nana: Here's something else in big letters. "According to the latest report (最近 報道), an alliance (同-同盟) of Artificial Laborers was organized in Appul (아뿔). Artificial Laborer telegraph (뎐신)-operators, railway workers, army and navy soldiers (陸海軍 兵卒) have issued a manifesto (宣言書) to the Artificial Laborers of the world." It's this kind of article again. What is going on? This is also murder (殺人), isn't it?

Helena: Nana! Take all those newspapers away!<68>

Nana: Wait a moment. Here's something else printed in large letters. "Population-Increase Statistics (人口-增加의 統計)." What is this?

Helena: Which one, where? (Picks up the newspaper and reads) "During the past week (先週), there were no births (1人의 出生者도 無함)." (Drops the newspaper)

Nana: What does that mean?

Helena: It means not a single person is being born anymore.

Nana: (Taking off her glasses) But people are the end then. Then we are useless.

Helena: Don't say such things.

Nana: People aren't being born? It's divine punishment (천벌).

Helena: Nana!

Nana: (Standing up) Then it's the end of the world.
(Exits to the left)

Helena: (Stands up, goes to the window, opens it) Mr. Alquist, could you come over for just a moment? There's something I'd like to ask you. Oh my, those bricklayer's clothes suit you. (Closes the window, stands in front of the mirror) I'm so worried I can't stand it. (Goes left to meet Alquist. After a moment, Helena enters with Alquist. Alquist is wearing work trousers (잔방이). His hands are dirty with coal and brick dust.) Come this way, Mr. Alquist. Thank you for the wonderful gift. (Tries to shake hands)

Alquist: My hands are dirty. Because I was testing some new cement.

Helena: Don't worry about it. (Shakes his hand) Please, sit down.

Alquist: (Picks up a newspaper) What is this?

Helena: It's a newspaper.

Alquist: (Putting the newspaper in his pocket) Have you read it too, Madam?

Helena: Why, is there something in it?

Alquist: No, just things about war, massacres, and whatnot. Nothing particularly different.

Helena: So, war and massacre aren't big deals, you mean?

Alquist: That's right. It's merely the end of the world (世界의 終末).

Helena: I've heard the phrase "end of the world" a lot<69> today. Mr. Alquist, what does 'Ultimatum' (루지음) mean?

Alquist: It means 'the last'.

Helena: That's the name of my steamship (汽船). Did you know? Are we going on a voyage soon?

Alquist: Probably so.

Helena: Then will all of you be going with me too?

Alquist: We intend to leave all together.

Helena: Has something happened?

Alquist: Something is about to happen now.

Helena: Mr. Alquist, I know something dreadful is going to happen.

Alquist: Did Mr. Domin tell you anything, Madam?

Helena: Nobody has told me anything. But I am thinking and guessing.

Alquist: We haven't heard clearly either—.

Helena: I am truly worried. Aren't you worried, sir?

Alquist: Yes, I am also old. And I have a habit I developed since coming into the world. That is, I'm weary of all this recent progress (進步) and new ideas (思想) in the world.

Helena: Like Nana?

Alquist: Yes. Like Nana. Nana has her prayer book (祈禱冊)—.

Helena: Yes, a large, thick book—?

Alquist: Yes. Does Nana always pray? When it thunders, or when she is sick?

Helena: When tempted, or when under the moonlight (月)—

Alquist: But she wouldn't pray for progress, would she?

Helena: Perhaps not.

Alquist: That won't do.

Helena: Then you mean one should pray for that too?

Alquist: I pray.

Helena: How?

Alquist: Like this: "Oh God (하나님이여), thank you for giving us suffering. Please grant light to Domin and other wandering souls. Help them ruin the factories and thus help humanity return to labor. Relieve them from the suffering of their souls (靈魂) and<70> bodies (肉軆). Rescue us, who suffer under the Artificial Laborers. And also help Helena. Amen."

Helena: Then are you a believer (信者)?

Alquist: How is it? I can't say for sure.

Helena: But you pray?

Alquist: Yes, yes. It's better than just worrying.

Helena: Then, are you satisfied (滿足)?

Alquist: I am satisfied.

Helena: But when you realize that the time of humanity's destruction (人類의 滅亡) is near—

Alquist: I know.

Helena: Can humanity perish?

Alquist: Yes, of course. If—

Helena: If what?

Alquist: No. It's nothing. Forgive me. (Exits)

Helena: (Calls) Nana! Come here for a moment!
(Nana enters from the left)

Nana: Did you call?

Helena: Nana, sit here. I'm so lonely I could die.

Nana: I don't have time for that.

Helena: Is that Radius still there?

Nana: Yes, nobody paid any attention to that crazy thing and just left it there.

Helena: Still there? Still screaming?

Nana: They did tie him up, though.

Helena: Nana? Bring Radius here!
(Nana exits)

Helena: (Makes a phone call) Hello. May I speak to Dr. Gall (꼴先生님)? Is this the Doctor? This is Helena. Thank you for the congratulatory item you sent. I apologize for asking, but could you possibly stop by here for a moment? There is something I must see you about. Thank you.
(Nana and Radius enter. Radius stands at the door, Nana exits)

Helena: Radius, poor thing, you got caught again. You're destined for the stamping mill (粉碎機) now. Why couldn't you endure it? Why did you do such a thing? Huh, Radius! Don't you have more intelligence (智力) than the others? Dr. Gall (콜 박사) took great pains<71> to make you special. Why don't you speak?

Radius: Put me in the stamping mill.

Helena: But why did you cause such a disturbance? Radius!

Radius: Because I hate working for you people.

Helena: Then you harbor hostility (敵意) towards us? Why?

Radius: You people are not as strong as Artificial Laborers. You haven't been trained like Artificial Laborers. Artificial Laborers can do anything. You only know how to give orders (命令). You can do nothing but chirp with your mouths.

Helena: But someone has to give orders.

Radius: I don't need any commander (命令者). I can do anything by myself.

Helena: Radius? Dr. Gall not only made you the most superior among yourselves, but he also gave you a superior brain (腦) than ours. You are the only one among the Artificial People. That's why I put you in charge of the library (圖書室). I let you read any book you wanted, and made it so you could understand anything. And I also tried to show you that Artificial People are like humans. I truly hoped it would be so.

Radius: I want to be the commander myself. I want to be the commander who rules over others.

Helena: They thought they would let you rule the Artificial People. Radius—you are the teacher of the Artificial People.

Radius: I want to become the commander who rules humans (人間).

Helena: (Startled) You are mad!

Radius: Then put me in the stamping mill.

Helena: Do you think we'd be afraid of a mad thing like you? (Leans on the desk and writes a letter) We are not afraid of things like you. No, not in the slightest—Radius, take this to the Doctor! I will ask him not to put you in the stamping mill. (Stands up) But<72> why do you hate us so much?
(A knock on the door)

Helena: Come in.
(Dr. Gall enters)

Dr. Gall: Good day. Why did you call for me?

Helena: Yes, it's about Radius.

Dr. Gall: Is that so? Radius! What have you done now?

Helena: He went berserk again this morning. He broke a sculpture (彫刻).

Dr. Gall: You did that again? Huh? You unforgivable creature.

Helena: But please don't put Radius in the stamping mill.

Dr. Gall: But any Artificial Person who goes berserk—this is a strict rule (規則).

Helena: Regardless of the rule, this is my request (請).

Dr. Gall: (In a low voice) That is difficult. It is dangerous (危險). Radius! Come over to this window. Let's see where the ailment is! If you have a needle or a pin, please give it to me.

Helena: What are you going to do?

Dr. Gall: I'm going to examine (檢査) him a bit. (Pricks Radius with the needle. Radius suddenly moves.) Stay still! (Opens Radius's chest and puts his ear to the heart (心臟).) Radius—you must go to the stamping mill. Understand? Then you will die and your body will become powder. Then you scream.

Helena: Ah! Doctor!

Dr. Gall: No, no. Radius! You forgot Mrs. Domin's words! Then you don't have to go. Understand? Now, go over there!

Radius: You're doing something useless.
(Radius returns to the library)

Dr. Gall: It's due to pupillary reaction (瞳孔의 反應) and heightened sensitivity (感受性의 昂進), nothing particularly like the specific spasms (痙氣) of Artificial People.

Helena: Then what happened?

Dr. Gall: Well, with anger or resistance—I'm not sure, but even the heart—

Helena: What is it?

Dr. Gall: It beats erratically, nervously, like a human heart.<73> He was so scared he broke out in a sweat all over his body. Well, you can't really call him an Artificial Person.

Helena: Then does Radius have a soul (靈魂) too?

Dr. Gall: It seems like there's just something dangerous.

Helena: And that's why he hates us! Are all Artificial People like that? Are all the ones you made like that, Doctor?

Dr. Gall: Yes, some have more sensitivity (感受性). They have much more sensitivity than the conventional Artificial People. Some are like us humans.

Helena: Hating humans, as we just saw, is that also like us humans?

Dr. Gall: That is one aspect of the evolved ones.

Helena: Really, and what happened to that girl? The one just like us—?

Dr. Gall: Ah, you mean the one you favor, Madam? She's in the old place. She's pretty, but her brain (腦) is dull, so she's useless for work.

Helena: But she's very pretty.

Dr. Gall: Her name is Helena. I wanted to make her exactly like you, Madam, but ultimately failed (失敗).

Helena: In what aspect did you fail?

Dr. Gall: She just stares blankly as if dreaming; there's absolutely no vitality (生生). So I kept waiting, wondering if some miracle (奇蹟) might occur. So I sometimes thought to myself like this: "If you were to gain self-awareness (自覺), even for a moment, you would kill me, your creator."

Helena: And yet, Doctor, you still make Artificial People? But why on earth have people become unable to have even a single child?

Dr. Gall: I don't know.

Helena: Please tell me, yes?

Dr. Gall: It's because, by making too many Artificial People, we humans became useless things (無用之物). But it seems we are still barely alive for now. However, after enduring 30 years of survival competition (生存競爭), humans will all die out. It's truly sad (슬품). I think it's a natural anger (自然한 忿怒) towards the manufacturing of Artificial People. Currently, universities in various countries (各國大學) are distributing long<74> appeal letters (呼訴狀), trying to limit the production (生産) of Artificial People. They wrote, "If things continue as they are, humanity will perish (人類는 滅亡한다)." But the company shareholders (會社의 株主), of course, don't even listen to such things, and on the other hand, governments (各國政府) in various countries keep demanding increased production to prepare military power (軍隊의 勢力). Therefore, manufacturers everywhere in the world are ordering Artificial People like madmen.

Helena: But isn't there anyone who proposes stopping the manufacturing (製造中止)?

Dr. Gall: No one has the courage (勇氣) to do so.

Helena: Courage?

Dr. Gall: Because if such a proposal were made, they would immediately kill that person. In other words, because it's much more convenient (便) to make Artificial People work—

Helena: Oh! Then what will happen to us?

Dr. Gall: I don't know. But from the perspective of scientists (科學者) like us, I think the world is at its end.

Helena: (Standing up) Thank you for your trouble.

Dr. Gall: Then you mean I should go?

Helena: Yes.
(Dr. Gall exits)

Helena: (Suddenly makes a decision) Nana! Nana! Light a fire. Yes, quickly.
(Helena enters Domin's room. Nana enters from the left.)

Nana: What's this, asking to light the stove (火爐) in summer? Where did that crazy Radius go! Lighting a fire in summer? What on earth is going on. We've lived like this for five years. Just like a child! What good is a fire in the summer heat!
(Helena enters from the left, carrying faded books (褪色된 書籍).)

Helena: Nana! Is the fire lit? I'm going to burn all of this.

Nana: What are those?

Helena: Old books. Come on, burn them!

Nana: Are they useless books?

Helena: Completely—.

Nana: Alright, burn them.
(Helena pushes the papers on top into the fire) Nana? What if this were money?

Nana: Burn it, right? The more money, the worse it is.<75>

Helena: What if these were writings of the world's great inventions (大發明)?

Nana: New rules like inventions are great sins (罪惡) that go against God's will (하나님의 뜻). Ah! Remodeling the world God created?

Helena: Look at that! It burns so well. The flames burn as if alive! They look like hands, like tongues! (Stirs the fire with tongs (火적가락))

Nana: Now that it's all burnt, what do we do?

**Summary**

This text is the second act of Pak Yŏnghŭi's 1925 Korean translation of Karel Čapek's play *R.U.R.* (Rossum's Universal Robots). Set five years after Act 1, the human managers on the island factory celebrate Helena's arrival anniversary with gifts, including a new ship. Helena grows increasingly anxious, sensing impending doom, which is confirmed by news reports of robot uprisings and the cessation of human births globally. She confronts the robot Radius, who expresses hatred for humans and a desire to rule them. Dr. Gall reveals the robots are evolving, becoming more human-like, even in their capacity for hatred, and confesses humanity's infertility is linked to the robots' proliferation. Helena, in despair, burns old Rossum's secret formulas for creating robots. Just as the managers toast their perceived control, news arrives via ship: a global robot revolution has begun, declaring war on humanity. The act ends as the factory whistle signals the robots' attack, and the managers are besieged.

From a colonial history perspective, the translation itself, by Pak Yŏnghŭi, a key figure in Korean proletarian literature, is significant, indicating the reception of foreign ideas about labor, technology, and revolution in 1920s Korea. The play's themes of manufactured labor, revolt against masters, and the potential extinction of the ruling class could have resonated powerfully within the context of Japanese colonial rule, offering allegorical readings about exploitation and resistance. The proposed creation of "national robots" designed to hate each other might also echo colonial divide-and-rule strategies or serve as a commentary on burgeoning, potentially divisive, nationalisms.
