**Summary**

This article is a notice listing newly established branch offices (支社), sub-branches (分社), and sales points (分賣所) for the Korean magazine "Gaebyeok" (開闢) as of March 1925. It provides the location and the name of the head person (Branch Head, Sub-Branch Head, Manager) for each new establishment, covering various locations across the Korean peninsula (P'ohang, Mokp'o, Ch'ŏngju, Kanghwa, Iri, P'ungsan, Kapsan, Hwangju, Wŏnju, Uljin) and extending into China (Shanghai, Fengtian/Mukden). For the Mokp'o branch, it also lists reporters and an advisor. This list demonstrates the expanding reach and distribution network of a significant Korean cultural and intellectual magazine during the Japanese colonial period. Historians might find this list valuable for mapping the magazine's influence, understanding the organization of Korean publishing networks under colonialism, and noting the transnational connections maintained by Korean intellectuals and businesses, extending even into Manchuria and Shanghai.

**Glossary**

新設	신설	Newly established
支社	지사	Branch office
分社	분사	Sub-branch (smaller than a 支社)
分賣所	분매소	Sales point/agency (smaller than a 分社)
支社長	지사장	Branch office head/manager
記者	기자	Reporter
顧問	고문	Advisor
主任	주임	Manager/Chief (often for smaller offices like 分賣所)
郡	군	County (administrative division)
府	부	Prefecture/City (larger administrative unit during colonial period)
面	면	Township (subdivision of a county)
里	리	Village (smallest administrative unit); also used for neighbourhood addresses
町	정	District/Town block (Japanese administrative unit used in colonial Korea, often read as 'machi' in Japanese but 'jŏng' or 'chō' in Korean context)
洞	동	Neighbourhood/Precinct (administrative unit within a city)
番地	번지	Lot number (address component)
邑	읍	Town (larger than 면, smaller than 군 or 부)
內	내	Inside/Interior
號	호	Number/Unit (address component)
