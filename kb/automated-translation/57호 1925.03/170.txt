Social Scene, From January 7th to the 31st

January 7th
Germany lodged a strong protest against the Allied Powers' ultimatum delaying the withdrawal of troops from Cologne.
The Italian government applied high-pressure policies against the anti-Nationalist faction; the anti-Nationalist faction launched a major demonstration movement, shouting loudly for the burial of the misruler Mussolini and "Long live Worker-Peasant Russia!"
The Allied finance ministers' conference convened in Paris.
Since the 6th, a large-scale riot by Communist Party members erupted in Paris.

8th
Kim Chi-hyŏp, who received a life sentence for the Ŭiyŏltan (Righteous Brotherhood) incident and was appealing while in Tokyo City's Ichigaya Prison, began a hunger strike on the 5th, outraged by his illegal confinement.
The Egyptian government approved the payment of expenses for its military expansion plan.
Sin To-hong, commander of the 2nd Regiment of Korean Independence, died in battle after clashing with Japanese police officers in Yŏngil-hyŏn, Mundŏk Island.

9th
Italy's anti-government party exposed the vices of the current government and issued an anti-government declaration.
U.S. House Representative Britten proposed holding a Pacific Caucasian Conference.
China's Li Yüan-hung and K'ang Yu-wei refused to participate in the government's Rehabilitation Conference.
At the finance conference, the United States agreed to receive reparations payments over 24 years.

10th
Across the river from Wiwŏn, a battle occurred between the Independence Army and police officers, resulting in 3 Independence Army soldiers killed and 1 police officer wounded.
In Japan's Yamaguchi Prefecture, a major brawl broke out between the Nationalist Party and the Suiheisha.
U.S. Secretary of State Hughes resigned.

11th
In Shanghai, war erupted between the forces of Military Governor Chang Yün-ming and Garrison Commander Kung Pang-tuo.

12th
Germany's cabinet formation had been problematic, but the Lutter cabinet, led by the former finance minister, was formed.
The new U.S. Secretary of State appointed was Kellogg, the former ambassador to Britain.
In China, a train on the Peking-Hankow line derailed on the Yellow River railway bridge, causing a disaster with over 300 passenger casualties.

13th
In Germany, a train collision between Berlin and Cologne resulted in over 100 casualties.
The consular corps stationed in Shanghai held a rehabilitation meeting due to the resurgence of warfare.

14th
The Allied finance ministers' conference being held in Paris concluded.
The ministers of the four nations Britain, America, France, and Italy in Beijing, citing significant damage from the Shanghai conflict, held a rehabilitation meeting, demanding the swift withdrawal of the Military Governor and discussing other post-conflict measures.

15th
This day being the 6th anniversary of the martyrdom of Germany's Karl Liebknecht and Rosa Luxemburg, leaders of the world proletarian youth movement, commemorative memorial ceremonies were widely held among proletarian youth in various countries.
The appeal trial for P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl, leader of the Ŭisŏngdan (Righteous Accomplishment Group), opened at the P'yŏngyang Appellate Court.
China held a cabinet meeting and decided on four items: 1. Abolition of the Shanghai and Hunan arsenals. 1. Abolition of the Songhu Military Governorship. 1. No stationing of troops in Shanghai permanently. 1. Appointment of Sun Yüeh as commander-in-chief of the punitive expedition against Henan, Shaanxi, and Gansu provinces.

16th
Fengtian's Chang Tso-lin convened a military conference of the Three Eastern Provinces to discuss current affairs.
U.S. President Coolidge decided to convene a second disarmament conference.
The Italian lower house passed a vote of confidence in Mussolini by a majority of 303 votes to 30.

18th
U.S. Senate committee chairman Borah initiated a movement to nullify the financial agreement.
Russian Commissar for Army and Navy Trotsky formally resigned due to a vote of no confidence from the Communist Party Central Committee.
Mongolia formally organized a communist government.

19th
A large fire broke out at Imundang bookstore in Ankuk-tong 4-ka, Kyŏngsŏng, causing over thirty thousand won in damages.
Economic talks began between Germany and France.
France launched strong opposition to Russia's movement to nullify public bond redemption.
The diplomatic corps in Beijing held a council meeting on war prevention countermeasures.
The International Opium Conference, which had been adjourned, reconvened in Geneva.

20th
The Russo-Japanese Agreement, under negotiation for many years, was formally signed in Beijing between Japanese representative Yoshizawa and Russian representative Karakhan.
In Myŏngnye-ri, Hanam-myŏn, Miryang County, a large fire broke out, burning down over 13 private houses.
Koreans residing in Osaka organized the Sedō Dōmei (Labor Alliance), announcing its inauguration along with its principles and platform.<80>

21st
This day being the 1st anniversary of the death of Lenin, the giant of the proletarian revolution in Worker-Peasant Russia, commemorative memorials were widely held among ideologists in various countries.
Inside Pusan Harbor, a motorboat exploded and sank, resulting in 8 or 9 casualties.
The U.S. Senate approved the convening of the second disarmament conference.
Im Il-gŭn, a supervisor in the tramway division of the Kyŏngsŏng Electric Company, embezzled over ten thousand won from the joint savings of about 500 crew members and fled, causing great turmoil between the crew and the company.

22nd
P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl, leader of the Ŭisŏngdan, whose appeal was pending in P'yŏngyang, was sentenced to 7 years, same as the first trial verdict.
Taegu police arrested about 10 youths gathered for a Lenin memorial commemoration.
The German parliament passed a vote of confidence in the administrative policy of the new Lutter cabinet.
China signed the League of Nations agreement regarding railway management by foreign powers in China.

23rd
The Italian Senate signed the Geneva Peace Protocol (on the condition that it does not apply to colonies).
Germany issued an exchange of notes regarding the Franco-German trade agreement.
In Unch'ŏn-dong, Songjang-myŏn, Ŭiju County, the Independence Army captured Ch'oe Il-hwan, Kim Sam-nyong, and one other person. Across the river from Pyŏktong, a battle occurred between the Independence Army and police, resulting in one Independence Army soldier killed.

24th
A military coup occurred in Chile, the government was overturned, and Prime Minister Altamirano resigned.
The Prussian cabinet also resigned en masse due to a vote of no confidence from the parliament.

25th
In Tokyo, Japan, about 500 university students attempting to hold a demonstration against military education clashed with about 400 police officers, resulting in a major brawl.
A large fire broke out in Kita-Senju, under the jurisdiction of Tokyo Prefecture, burning down about 350 houses and causing numerous casualties.
Kunsan police arrested popular movement activists So Yong-gwan, Kim Yŏng-hwi, and Kim T'ak, confiscating numerous documents. Russia declared a boycott alliance against Britain.

26th
China's Sun Yat-sen suffered from a severe illness, liver disease, and fell into critical condition, with reports even suggesting his death after surgery.

27th
The entire staff of the Kyŏngsŏng Electric Company's tramway division united and presented 13 demands to the company.
On the river in Ch'ŏngsu-ri, Kwangp'yŏng-myŏn, Ŭiju County, another battle occurred between the Independence Army and police, resulting in one Independence Army soldier killed.
In Tokyo, Japan, the House of Peers Reform Implementation Committee resolved to hold a national rally. China's Sun Yat-sen, who had been in critical condition, regained consciousness.
The entire workforce of the P'yŏngan Rubber Factory in P'yŏngyang went on strike over issues of treatment and wage increases.

28th
China's Ch'i Hsieh-yüan went into exile in Japan with his family.
The Romanian government lodged a protest with Japan regarding the clauses concerning Bessarabia in the Russo-Japanese Treaty.
In Sariwŏn, Hwanghae Province, a social gathering of popular movement activists from the entire province was held, resolving 11 points regarding the labor-farmer movement.
In Kwangju, Chŏnnam, about 40 ideologists from various areas gathered to hold a "Welcoming Spring" social gathering.
A large fire broke out in Mokp'o, causing over one hundred thousand won in damages.
Russia lifted foreign currency restrictions.
French Premier Herriot, commenting on the Russo-Japanese Agreement, advocated for a European alliance.

29th
The Japanese Diet fell into great disorder due to attacks from the Seiyū Hontō party.
The Guangdong government in China decided to relocate its political committee to Beijing.
French ambassador to Russia Krasin returned to Russia regarding the establishment of a Russo-French bank.
China's Lu Yung-hsiang occupied Shanghai.
The Allied ambassadors' conference deliberated on whether German factories were manufacturing munitions.

30th
The Shirane volcano crater lake in Gunma Prefecture, Japan, had a major eruption.
France and Belgium began withdrawing troops from Dortmund and Wesel in Germany.
50 households of tenant farmers of the Oriental Development Company (東拓) in Sain-myŏn, Pongsan County, faced forced execution by the Sariwŏn branch of the Oriental Development Company.

31st
The struggle between Kyŏngsŏng Electric (京電) and its crew had continued for many days; the Kyŏngsŏng Electric side approved 9 out of the 13 demands made by the crew.
Diplomatic relations between Greece and Turkey were severed due to the incident where Turkish authorities expelled the Greek Patriarch from Constantinople.

February 1st
The Kyŏngsŏng Library, which had been closed, reopened.
China convened the National Rehabilitation Conference with about 110 national representatives.
Worker-Peasant Russia issued a special amnesty order for all counter-revolutionaries.

2nd
The Greek government issued a mobilization order against Turkey over the expulsion of the Greek Patriarch.
The French lower house passed the bill to abolish the ambassadorship to the Vatican by a large majority.
China's Nationalist Party (Kuomintang) refused to participate in the ruling government's Rehabilitation Conference, and simultaneously, the Nationalist Party's political committee relocated to Beijing, selecting committee members Li Tsung-shih (李宗石), Wu Ch'ao-shu (伍朝樞), Wang Chao-ming (王兆銘), and Yü Yu-jen (于能辰 - likely typo for 于右任). Ahmed Zogu (아헌드소고氏) was elected President of Albania by unanimous consent of the parliament.

3rd
Turkey also decided to take military action against Greece.
In the Japanese Diet, a major brawl broke out between members of the Seiyū Hontō party and ruling party members over the issue of compulsory education expenses, resulting in many injured Diet members, and the compulsory education bill was rejected.
U.S. Secretary of State Hughes issued a reply stating that the United States would not participate in the Paris reparations agreement.
The committee of the Opium Conference in Geneva rejected the U.S. proposal for the complete abolition of opium smoking within 15 years.
In Kunsan, about 100 workers marched en masse to the police station demanding the release of arrested members of the Popular Movement Association.

4th
Japan's Minister of Justice, Yokota Sennosuke, passed away.
Postal service between Japan and Russia, which had been suspended for years, was restored.
The Guangdong Army in China launched a general offensive to eliminate Ch'en Chiung-ming's forces.
In Chogu-dong, Kwansŏ-myŏn, Kusŏng County, two Independence Army soldiers were killed by police.

5th
In Pungnyul-myŏn, Chaeryŏng County, a major clash broke out between 300 tenant farmers of the Oriental Development Company and the company's bailiffs (집달리).
Paektang Hyŏn Ch'ae, famous in the Korean literary world, passed away.
Across the river from Huch'ang, 4 or 5 Independence Army soldiers were killed after engaging in battle with police.

6th
The conflict between the Oriental Development Company tenant farmers and bailiffs in Pungnyul-myŏn, Chaeryŏng County, escalated further, with Japanese even firing guns at the tenant farmers.
The U.S. delegation withdrew from the International Opium Conference.

7th
Following the death of Japan's Justice Minister Yokota, Ogawa Heikichi was newly appointed as Justice Minister.
China also withdrew from the International Opium Conference.<79>

**Summary**

This news digest covers international and domestic events from January 7th to February 7th, 1925. Internationally, it reports on post-WWI tensions (German protests, reparations), political instability and shifts (Italy, Germany, US, Russia, China, Chile, Albania), the signing of the Russo-Japanese Agreement, disarmament and opium control conferences, and regional conflicts (China, Greece-Turkey). Domestically within Korea, it highlights continued independence movement activities (clashes in Wiwŏn, Ŭiju, Pyŏktong, Huch'ang; arrests and trials like Kim Chi-hyŏp and P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl), growing social unrest including labor actions (Osaka Korean workers' union, Kyŏngsŏng Electric strike, P'yŏngan Rubber Factory strike) and tenant farmer disputes (Chaeryŏng, Pongsan), alongside local news like fires and the death of writer Hyŏn Ch'ae. For historians, this article is valuable as it chronicles specific instances of Korean resistance and independence efforts (names, dates, locations), documents the rise of organized labor and agrarian movements under colonial rule, and places these events within the broader context of global political and social change in the mid-1920s, reflecting the interconnectedness of Korea's situation with world affairs.

연합국 대사 회의에서는 獨逸 공장의 군수제조 興否를 심의하다.

同 三十日
日本 群馬縣 白根山川이 대폭발되고.
佛白 兩國은 獨逸 「돌스던」 及 「웨젤」에서 철병을 시작하고.
鳳山郡 舍人面 東拓 소작인 50戶는 沙里院 東拓支店에게 강제집행을 당하다.

同 三十一日
京電과 승무원간의 항쟁은 多日持續하든 바 京電측으로서는 승무원의 요구 13개조 중 9개조는 승인하고.
希臘 土耳其間에는 土官憲이 希大司敎를 君士垣丁에서 放逐한 事로 국교가 단절되다.

二月 一日
폐관되얏든 京城 도서관이 개관되고.
中國은 110여명의 국민 대표로 國民善後會議를 開하고.
勞農露國은 반혁명자 全都에게 특사령을 발하다.

同 二日
희랍정부는 土耳其가 希大司敎放追 事件에 대하야 동원령을 下하고.
佛國 하원은 다수로 羅馬法王廳의 대사 폐지안을 가결하고.
中國의 國民黨은 집정 정부의 善後會議에 참가를 거절하고 동시에 國民黨 정치위원회는 北京에 이전하고 위원 李宗石 伍朝樞 王兆銘 于能辰 4氏 선정하다. 「알비니아」 대통령은 의원의 만장일치로 「아헌드소고」氏가 選與되다.

同 三日
土耳其도 희랍에 대하야 군사행동을 취하기로 결정하고.
日本 의회는 政友本黨 의원 及 與黨 의원간에 의무교육비 문제로 대격투가 起하야 의원의 부상자를 多出하고 의무교육*案은 否決*고.
米 국무경 「휴스」氏는 巴里 배상협정에 米國은 참가치 안는다고 회답을 發하고.
「제네바」의 아편회의위원회는 米國 제안의 15연간 阿片飮全廢를 부결하고.
群山에서는 노동자 100여명이 民衆運動社員撫擧放還運動을 大擧하야 경찰에 作隊殺到하다.

同 四日
日本의 사법대신 橫田千之助氏 死去하고.
年年 정체되얏든 日露間의 우편이 회복되고.
中國 廣東軍은 陳炯明軍을 일소키 위하야 총공격을 개시하고.
龜城郡 舘西面 造丘洞에서는 독립군 2명이 경관에게 피살되다.

同 五日
載寧郡 北栗面 東拓 소작인 300명과 東拓 집달리간에 일대 격투가 起하고.
朝鮮 著述界에 名하든 白堂 玄采氏 死去하고.
厚昌對岸에서 독립군 4,5명이 경관과 교전하다가 피살되다.

同 六日
載寧郡 北栗面 東拓 소작인 대 집달리간 爭亂은 더욱 확대되야 日本人은 소작인에게 放銃까지하고.
米國 대표는 국제아편회의에 탈퇴하다.

同 七日
日本의 橫田法相 死去한 代에 小川平吉氏가 法相으로 신임되고.
中國도 국제아편회의에서 탈퇴하다.＜79＞
