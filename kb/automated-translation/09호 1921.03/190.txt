**Body Text Translation**
The secret of education lies in discovering the method to spend time wisely and cleverly, and the purpose of education is to make children become individuals of self-respect and self-governance.
Herbert Spencer

Do you consider merely eating and sleeping the way of a gentleman and lady? Do you think that one who eats, then sleeps, and upon waking sits idly, has a more pleasant heart than one who works steadily? I ask again. What benefit do comfort and laziness give us? Is it beneficial for maintaining health? Is it beneficial for achieving wealth? Is it beneficial for preserving the family? Or is it beneficial for success in academics? If examined closely, are there not a hundred losses and not a single gain?
The Greek Sage Socrates<100>

**Summary**

The article presents two short passages, attributed to Herbert Spencer and Socrates, emphasizing the value of productive use of time and diligence over idleness. Spencer's quote focuses on education's role in fostering self-reliance and wise time management. The second passage, attributed to Socrates, directly questions the value of a leisurely life of eating and sleeping, arguing it brings no benefit compared to steady work, whether for health, wealth, family, or learning. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects the promotion of Western enlightenment ideals (self-reliance, diligence, education) in early 1920s Korea. Publishing such content in *Kaebyŏk*, a prominent intellectual magazine, suggests an effort to inspire Koreans towards self-improvement and modernization, possibly as a form of cultural resistance or nation-building under restrictive colonial conditions.
