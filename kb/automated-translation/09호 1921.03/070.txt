Failure! What on earth is failure? It means losing when trying to win! Isn't this a common occurrence in the world? No, isn't it a stepping stone for great undertakings? Through it, we gain experience that even ten thousand volumes of books cannot teach, and it enables us to lay down completely sound plans for the future. What kind of fool would despair upon encountering this failure? Failure is a test through which Heaven intends to bestow happiness upon us, and truly an opportunity in life to redouble one's efforts.
- British Great Statesman Disraeli

I address these words to scholars and other researchers of the world: Whatever problem you have seized upon, think it over deeply for a long time. Then the problem will naturally be solved, and finally, a kind of electric light will, in an instant, vibrate your brain and ignite the heat of stimulation. The time when a person's genius is best displayed is precisely that moment.
- Discoverer of Universal Gravitation, Newton

A petty person yields when encountering misfortune, but a great person transcends it.
- American Literary Master Irving <44>

**Summary**

This untitled piece compiles three quotes from Western figures (Disraeli, Newton, Irving) focusing on the themes of overcoming failure, the power of deep contemplation for breakthroughs, and the resilience of great individuals in the face of adversity. Published on March 1st, 1921, the second anniversary of the March 1st Independence Movement, the article likely served as an indirect encouragement to Koreans under Japanese colonial rule. Historians might find it interesting for its timing and its implicit message of perseverance, intellectual endeavor, and maintaining dignity despite setbacks, reflecting the nationalist sentiments and aspirations for eventual success prevalent during that period.

기사제목	[무제]
기사형태	잡저

失敗! 失敗라는 것이 大體 무엇이냐. 이기려다가 진 것! 이 世間의 常事가 아니냐 아니 大事業의 階梯가 아니냐. 吾人은 그로 말미암아 萬卷의 書도 能히 가르치지 못하는 經驗을 엇는 것이며 또 能히 將來 萬全의 策을 세우게 되는 것이라. 어떤 痴漢이 이 失敗를 當하야 失望할 者이냐? 失敗는 天이 吾人에게 幸福을 降코저 하는 試驗으로서 實로 人生의 發奮一層할 機이라.
英國 大政治가 띠스러리

말을 世上의 學者 其他 硏究家에 부티 노니 이는 무슨 問題이던지 한 가지를 붓잡앗거던 기리 熟思하라. 그러면 問題는 自然히 解釋하게 되는 것이며 듸디어 一種의 電光인 瞬時間에 너의 頭腦를 震動하야 刺戟熱을 起하리라. 或 人의 天才가 가장 잘 發秀되는 때는 正히 그 때이니라.
萬有引力의 發見者 뉴-톤

小人은 不幸을 遭하면 屈하나 大人은 此를 超越한다.
米國 文豪 아-빙끄＜44＞
