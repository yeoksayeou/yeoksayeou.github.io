From the Editorial Office

Whether there are special facts or not, offering a few words of courtesy to you, our readers, upon completing the editing is not something that cannot be done, but disliking the mere listing of conventional words without substance in every issue, we have left this column blank for some time.

However, in this issue, there are matters we cannot help but inform you about, so although it may seem sudden, we write these few words; we would be most fortunate if you do not regard them as the usual conventional phrases.

Even if we do not confess it, you, our readers, will surely acknowledge it first: we are ones lacking in both wisdom (智) and ability (能). However, it is us who resolve that, even with this insufficient wisdom and ability, if you do not abandon us, we will exert our heart's blood (心血) to the very end.

Yet, what we have felt most deeply since the very first day we began this work is that the self-awareness (自覺) of our brethren (兄弟) has already taken another step forward from the top of the pole (竿頭), and now demands the method (方法) of "how must it be done" rather than the encouragement (激勵) of "it must be done." Feeling this, we have strived, as much as possible, even in a single character or phrase, to avoid mere superficiality and shell-like emptiness (虛) and pursue substance (實) with blood and bone, reaching this present day.

Everyone, what is the meaning of this? Though we always strive, looking back after completing the work, it is still unsatisfactory. We, who feel this keenly, in order to realize our conventional principle (主義) even more thoroughly (徹底히), have now adopted the policy (方針) that concerning any kind of problem (如何한 問題), the content must be limited to that formed by the intention (意思) of someone possessing special cultivation (特別 素養) regarding that field. We dare to announce this, so please monitor (監視) how this policy will first be realized (實現) starting from the next issue (來號).

One more thing to mention is that the definition of poetry supplemented in the margin (餘白) of this journal's November issue became a catalyst (導火線), initiating a type of literary world criticism (文壇論評). We, who always considered the lack of criticism (無批判) in our lives regrettable (遺憾), welcomed this with a joyful heart. However, unexpectedly, among the manuscripts (原稿) that arrived for this month's issue, there was a regrettable (遺憾) tendency for the tone (論調) to depart from the true spirit (本精神) of criticism and lean towards the problem of personal feelings (個人의 感情問題). Therefore, we have decided not to publish (揭載) the two manuscripts sent by Mr. Hwang Sŏgu (黃錫禹 氏): "Clod Literary World (土塊文壇)" and "Strict Distinction Between New Style Poetry and Free Verse (新體詩와 自由詩의 嚴正한 區別)," nor the continuation of Mr. Hyŏn Ch'ŏl's (玄哲氏) "So-called Poetic Form and Nebulous Style (所謂 詩形과 朦朧體)."

Regarding this, what we ask you, our readers, and the few individuals directly related to this issue to reconsider is that our doing this now is not intended to block the path of criticism (批評) henceforth, but is nothing other than (不外함) an effort to make sacred (神聖케) the sound of criticism first heard in our society. The one thing is that criticism which transcends private feelings (私的 感情) and solely seeks to purify (淨化) our lives is always welcome. <118>

**Summary**

This editorial note from *Kaebyŏk* magazine outlines a shift in editorial policy towards more practical, expert-driven content, responding to a perceived reader demand for "how-to" methods rather than simple encouragement. It also addresses a burgeoning literary debate sparked by a previous issue, explaining the rejection of several manuscripts on poetry criticism. The editors state these submissions deviated into personal attacks rather than objective critique, emphasizing their commitment to fostering "sacred" criticism focused on societal purification, while still welcoming critiques that transcend personal feelings. For historians, this piece reveals the intellectual currents of early colonial Korea, showing a move towards pragmatic solutions and grappling with the nature and boundaries of public critique and literary debate within the constraints of the period. The explicit mention of wanting "blood and bone" substance over superficiality reflects a desire for meaningful content relevant to the Korean situation.

編輯室로부터
特別한 事實이야 잇던 업던 編輯을 마치는 同時에 두어 마디의 禮辭를 讀者 여려분에게 올림도 그러케 못할 일은 아니오나 別로 씨도 업는 例套의 말을 遂號羅列하기가 실혀 그 동안은 이 欄을 비엇나이다.
그러하얏사오나 이번 號에는 여러분에게 아니 告할 수 업는 것이 잇사와 새삼스러우나 두어 말슴을 적사오니 普通 쓰는 例套의 말이라 看做치 안흐시면 幸甚이겟나이다.
우리가 告白치 아니하야도 여러분께서 먼저 認定하실 것이어니와 저희는 智와 能이 한께 不足한 者들이 오며 다못이 不足한 智와 能으로나마 여러분이 버리지 안흐시면 最後까지 心血을 盡하겟다 함이 저희들 이외다.
그런데 저희가 이를 始作하던 첫 날부터 가장 깁게 느낀 것은 우리 兄弟의 自覺은 어느덧 竿頭에 一步를 更進하야 이제는『하여야 되리라』激勵보다『어떠케 하여야 되겟느냐』하는 方法을 要求하는 그것이라. 이를 느낀 저희는 비록 一字一句이라도 될 수 잇는대로 입뿐이요, 깍지뿐인 虛를 避하고 避잇고 뼈잇는 實을 懋하야써 今日에 至하얏나이다.
여러분, 이것이 웬일입니까요. 늘 하노라 하여도 하여노코 보면 亦是 不滿합니다. 이것을 압흐게 느끼는 저희는 從來의 主義를 一層 徹底히 實現키 爲하야 自今은 如何한 問題이던지 그에 對한 것은 반듯이 그 便에 對한 特別 素養을 有한 이의 意思로서 成한 것에 限하는 方針을 取하게 되온 바 敢히 此를 發表하오니 이 方針이 爲先 來號부터 어떠케 實現되는가를 여러분이 監視하야 주십시오.
다음 한 가지 말슴하올 것은 本誌 11月號 餘白에 補充한 詩의 定義가 導火線이 되어 一種 文壇論評이 開始되매 每樣 우리 生活의 無批判을 遺憾으로 생각하던 우리는 깃거운 마음으로써 此를 迎하얏더니 意外로 今月號에 來着한 原稿 中에는 그 論調가 批判의 本精神을 떠나서 個人의 感情問題에 傾하는 嫌이 有함으로 遺憾이나마 黃錫禹 氏가 보내 온 「土塊文壇」 及「新體詩와 自由詩의 嚴正한 區別」의 二稿와 玄哲씨의「所謂 詩形과 朦朧體」의 續稿는 揭載치 아니하기로 하엿나이다.
이에 對하야 讀者 여러분이나 또는 該問題에 直接 關係된 몃 분으로서 다시 생각하여 주셔야 될 것은 우리가 只今 이리함은 이제부터 批評의 길을 막고저 함이 아니라 우리 社會에 처음 들리는 批評의 소리를 神聖케 하자 함에 不外함인 바 私的 感情을 超越하야 오즉 우리의 生活을 淨化코저 하는 批評은 언제던지 歡迎한다는 一事이외다.＜118＞
