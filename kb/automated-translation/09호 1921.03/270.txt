Current Postal Rates Table

As the scale of our livelihoods grows, so does the amount of mail, thus we should also have common knowledge regarding postal matters. Even if one trusts the Postal Law, the postal handlers cannot be trusted. The number of undelivered mail items is countless, and even for mail that is delivered, there are cases where Third-Class Mail is handled as Fourth-Class (even in Kyŏngsŏng), and cases where Third-Class Mail is handled as Parcel Post (in various regions). When encountering such situations, we think we must also have common knowledge about postal matters.<131>

**Summary**

This short piece from 1921 argues that Koreans need to be knowledgeable about postal regulations because postal workers are unreliable. It cites instances of mail being lost or misclassified (resulting in incorrect charges) in both the capital (Kyŏngsŏng) and other regions. From a historical perspective, the article offers a glimpse into the everyday frustrations and distrust citizens felt towards the colonial-era postal service, highlighting perceived incompetence or potentially discriminatory practices within a basic state function.
