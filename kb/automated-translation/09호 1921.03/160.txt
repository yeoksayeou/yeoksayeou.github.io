Regarding the inquiry from reader Mr. Yi Ŭng-ryong and Mr. Im Sŏng-bong of the Chosŏn Infantry Unit,

The lodging place of aviator Mr. An Ch'ang-nam is:
Morishima-kan (守島舘), 23 Furuishiba (古石場), Fukagawa-ku (深川區), Tōkyō (東京), Japan. The Oguri (小栗) Airfield (which has recently become a Flying School) is the Oguri Flying School (小栗飛行學校) at the Fukagawa Susaki (深川洲崎) reclaimed land in Tōkyō. <91>

**Summary**

This short notice provides the residential address and the location of the flying school (formerly airfield) associated with the Korean aviator An Ch'ang-nam in Tōkyō, Japan. The information is given in response to inquiries from two readers, Yi Ŭng-ryong and Im Sŏng-bong (affiliated with the Chosŏn Infantry Unit). For historians, this piece highlights the public interest in pioneering Korean figures like An Ch'ang-nam during the colonial period, even necessitating the publication of his contact/location details. It also points to the fact that prominent Koreans sought training and opportunities in Japan itself, and shows the magazine's role in connecting readers with such figures.
