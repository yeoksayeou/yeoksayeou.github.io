기사제목	婦人敎育 問題로 動議하야, -一般人士의 可決을 促함- (Moving on the Issue of Women's Education, -Urging the Approval of the General Public-)
필자	朴思稷
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Argumentative Essay)

**Translation**

Moving on the Issue of Women's Education, -Urging the Approval of the General Public-
						Pak Sajik

One

In issue No. 4 of this magazine, under the title "Views on the Liberation of Korean Women by Various Notables," many esteemed teachers indeed cried out passionately about the issue of women's education. However, when treating a patient, the method of administering medicine depends on the severity of the illness. If the illness is mild, one or two doses of medicine might suffice, but if the illness is severe, even if hundreds or thousands of doses are given, treatment must continue until the illness is cured. Similarly, the issue of our Korean women's education is truly lamentable. The illness has persisted for so long that it has become a chronic disease (痼疾). Therefore, until this chronic disease is completely eradicated and our women's society can lift its head even slightly from the ground and rise, the question of medicine will not disappear, no matter the day or time. In other words, our Korean women's education is utterly lacking. That is, until women's education is implemented, and even after implementation, until it spreads to every corner and village (面面洞洞), we must, treating it as handling old business, move on this issue a thousand times, nay, ten thousand times. Confident in this, though my words may lack order, I intend to speak from now on without the slightest hesitation.

Two

The term "women's education" (婦人敎育) is truly broad in meaning. Whether a young child, a youth, or an elder, without any restriction regarding age, as long as they are women, within the possible scope, <35> they should all be treated as students and taught. To speak the plain truth, our society still has not escaped the realm of a deformed society (畸形的 社會), no, a widower society (鰥夫的 社會), and at the same time, our society has become quite desolate and uninteresting.

Look. What makes Mount Kŭmgang (金剛山) renowned for its beautiful scenery? It is because its twelve thousand peaks rise high and low in verdant splendor, and from within, the waters of Manp'oktong Falls (萬瀑洞瀑布水) gush out, flowing clear and pure, painting the beauty of nature—this is why people exclaim, "Kŭmgangsan! Kŭmgangsan!" If the waters of Manp'oktong Falls did not exist, what would make the twelve thousand peaks so beautiful?

Regarding our human society, let us follow the saying "mountains are yang, waters are yin" (山陽水陰) and say men are mountains and women are waters. If so, a society composed only of men would be like the twelve thousand peaks without the waters of Manp'oktong Falls; what beauty could there be? Therefore, perhaps a third party, due to my incomplete explanation, might ask, "What do you mean our society has no women?" But in any human society (人族社會), the cosmetic is surely the flower of culture. To put it another way, only a world where the flowers of culture bloom resplendently (燦爛하게) can be a beautiful human world.

Now, let us lift our eyes again and look from the perspective of our culture. If we look, our culture is surely just masculine culture, lacking affection, stiff, originating from the minds of men. Even if it progresses to its fullest, it would be like the twelve thousand peaks of Mount Kŭmgang standing tall and green alone. That culture born from the minds of women, full of affection and warmth! That culture depicting infinite emotion (無限한 感情)!! That feminine culture (女性的 文化) like the waters of Manp'oktong Falls, embracing the twelve thousand peaks with gentle arms and flowing clear and pure—it would be absent.

On this point, I curse without hesitation that our society, no, our culture, is deformed (畸形的), desolate, and uninteresting.

Three

However, as we continually cry out (絶呌하며서) to build a new culture (新文化), how can we not strive for the construction of a complete and well-rounded (雙全 圓滿한) culture? To strive for it, we must inevitably begin by first tackling women's education. Only then will a culture adorned with the harmony (어룰로) of clear waterfalls and green twelve thousand peaks, like the real Mount Kŭmgang complete with both (具全한 實在의 金剛山), appear in our society. Only when that day arrives <36> will our society achieve a concrete world of culture.

The above was stated in terms of culture, but how could it apply only to culture? Wherever one looks at the census records (人口調査表) of Chosŏn (朝鮮), there isn't much difference between the number of men and women. According to the census table from September last year, there were 8,159,659 women (men numbered 8,695,630) within Chosŏn proper (內地). Can we then afford to disregard (警視할 수 잇겟습니까) such a large number, over eight million of our compatriots (同胞)? Ah—I truly feel regret. When we stand here now and look back (回顧) at our past history (過去 歷史), we cannot help but feel we have been blinding ourselves (自手眼盲). Towards these more than eight million compatriots, how could we have restricted (束縛하엿스며) their freedom so much, and how could we have disregarded (無視하얏는가) their personhood (人格) so much? I believe this is the greatest among the reasons why our Chosŏn nation (朝鮮 民族) has become weakened (衰弱하여진) culturally and in various other aspects as it is today. When we sit in a quiet room (從容한 房), each closing our eyes tightly, and trace back from our mothers to our grandmothers, from our grandmothers to their mothers, and their grandmothers' grandmothers, contemplating (推察하면) the state of their lives (生活하시던 狀態), we who love freedom (自由) and respect personhood (人格) cannot help but feel guilt (罪) and shed tears that well up spontaneously. Ah, our mothers! Our grandmothers!! For what sin were they confined within those inner quarters (深閨) for their entire long lives (百年平生長歲月), unable to see the bright, bright sky of freedom and the warm sunlight (太陽의 빗), ending their lives in indefinite confinement (無期禁錮로 終身하얏나뇨)?

Last summer, I took a night train (夜車) and arrived at a certain provincial city (地方 市街). When I got off at the station (停車場), it was exactly 8:30 AM. While strolling around, partly for a walk and partly to see the city, I unfortunately saw a woman of some house sitting before a mirror (面鏡압헤), busy shaping her forehead and eyebrows. That morning, I saw roughly three such women. At that time, tears suddenly welled up (피-ㅇ돌며서) in my eyes, and unusual emotions surged within me.

Ah—those women of other countries are now busy demanding suffrage (參政權 要求)!
Ah—those women of other countries are now busy striving for the expansion of life (生의 擴充)!
But our Chosŏn women are still, in a dream of Muryeong Dowon (武陵桃園; Peach Blossom Spring, an idyllic utopia), busy shaping their foreheads and eyebrows to become beloved playthings (玩弄物) for beastly lustful (獸慾的) men... Thus I couldn't help but sigh deeply (歎息하기를 마지 안핫스며) for their future (將來), and also couldn't help but weep deeply (涕泣하기를 마지 안핫습니다) for the future of our society.

Whose fault (罪) is all this then? Is it the fault of us men <37>? Is it the fault of those women themselves? Since the pot is already broken, let's not argue about it (破甑이라 莫論하거니와), but however it happened, if we look in terms of knowledge (知識) and personhood (人格), we men are also not perfect, but when it comes to the world of women (婦人界), it was completely empty. From the past until the present, however you look at our Chosŏn society, except for the physical body (肉), it was deformed (畸形的) and widower-like (鰥夫的). Alas—"A bird flies high with two wings, a cart travels far on two wheels" (鳥雙翼而高飛, 車兩輪而遠去)—this phrase seems to bitterly satirize (諷刺한 글句인가 십흡니다) our Chosŏn society.

Four

The unit of society, as you all know, is each one of us individuals (個人). Therefore, the civilization (文明) of our individuals is precisely the civilization of society. To put it another way, the place where civilized individuals combine (結合) and live is precisely a civilized society. Looking back at Chosŏn society from this perspective makes it even more lamentable. Our Chosŏn nation's 20 million includes women in the total count (總數), meaning our society is one united (團結한) by combining men and women. If we, at any time, do not strive for women's education, what will become of our society's civilization?

Just as the sun (太陽), though bright, cannot illuminate the Eastern and Western Hemispheres simultaneously, by the same principle (同一의 理), even if only we men become enlightened, perhaps we can illuminate one side (一面), the surface (表面), of our society. However, for the inner side (裡面) of our society to also become bright, I believe it is impossible (不可能) until women's education is thoroughly widespread (徹底히 普及되기 前에는).

Having realized (自覺한) this today, we must proceed steadily (着着 進行) with women's education as quickly as possible (하루바삐). Although the methods (方法) and institutions (機關) for this education are not yet concrete (具體的), how about doing it this way for now?

Our individual mothers and our individual wives have already passed school age (學齡), so let's establish something like temporary lecture centers (臨時講習所 비슷하게 設하여) and teach them, starting the curriculum (科程) first with "a ya, ŏ yŏ, o yo, u yu, ŭ i, a" (아야, 어여, 오요, 우유, 으이,아; the basic Hangul vowels). Let the scope for recruiting students (講習生 募集 範圍) be just each person's own neighborhood (近隣). To be more specific (仔細히 말할진대), if our closest neighbors (最近隣) are five households (五戶), then five households, if ten households (十戶), then ten households—limit it to the women within that area. As for the location (塲所), let's use the largest house's sarangbang (舍廊; outer room typically used by men) among those five or ten households, hang up a blackboard (漆板) or something similar, and teach them there.

Our individual young sisters (누이) and our individual young daughters (딸) are currently of school age (學齡), so let's quickly, quickly send them to school (學校). However, currently, the number of schools in Chosŏn is small. So, even if we have to follow the rules of the old village schools (書堂 規例), let's establish children's educational institutions (兒童敎育機關) in every village and district (面面洞洞). The curriculum (科程) and methods (方式) must be similar to those of regular schools (普通學校). To be specific, let's not follow the old-style sŏdang system (舊式書堂制)—that is, the place that used to be a hunchback factory (곱장이 製造所)—but absolutely make it modern (時代的으로). It is a fact (事實) that we Chosŏn people are poor (貧寒한). However, even if we have to cry and lament, even if we have to become servants (종노릇) to others, I strongly argue (力說하오) that we must educate our young sisters and young daughters.<38>

**Summary**

Pak Sajik argues passionately for the urgent necessity of women's education in early 1920s Korea. He contends that Korean society is "deformed" and incomplete, like Mount Kŭmgang without its waterfalls, because it lacks the contribution of educated women. He laments the historical confinement and lack of freedom for Korean women, contrasting their situation unfavorably with women elsewhere seeking suffrage. Pak proposes practical, immediate steps: establishing basic literacy classes for adult women in neighborhood homes and sending young girls to school, even if it means creating makeshift modern schools in every village despite poverty. The article reveals the profound sense of crisis among some intellectuals regarding the exclusion of women from modern life and education during the colonial period, viewing it as a primary cause of national weakness. Historians would find the stark description of women's limited lives, the explicit linking of women's education to national cultural development and societal 'wholeness', and the practical, grassroots educational proposals particularly interesting for understanding early Korean feminist thought and nationalist responses to colonial modernity.

一
本誌 第4號에 記載한 諸名士의 朝鮮女子解放觀이라는 題下에서도 女子敎育問題로 여러분 先生이 痛切히 부르짓지 아님은 아니올시다. 그러나 病者에 對하야 藥쓰는 法이 그 病勢의 輕重을 딸아서 쓰는 것입니다. 만약 그 病勢가 輕할진대 藥1,2帖에 그칠 것이지마는 만약 그 病勢가 重하고보면 藥 數百帖으로 乃至 幾千帖을 나리울지라도 그 病이 快差할 時까지로 限定할 수밧재 업습니다. 그와가티 우리 朝鮮女子의 敎育問題로 말하면 眞實로 寒心하올시다. 病든 지가 하두 오래서 아조 痼疾이 되엇슴니다. 그러니까 우리는 何日何時까지라도 그 痼疾이 永永 拔根되어 우리 女子社會가 족으만치나마 머리를 땅에서 떠이고 일어나는 것을 보고야 藥問題가 업서질 것입니다. 다시 말하면 우리 朝鮮女子敎育은 넘우도 空이니 卽 女子敎育이 實施되기까지 實施되어가지고도 저 面面洞洞에 普及되기까지는 舊事件處理 삼아 우리가 千番이라도 動議하여야 되겟스며 萬番이라도 動議하여야 되겟다 自信하고 비록 順序업슨 말이나마 족음도 廚躇치 아니하고 只今부터 말코저하는 바이올시다.

二
