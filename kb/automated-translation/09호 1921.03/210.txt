社會諷刺 銀파리
						목성 記

The world has become modernized, and now even behind the fly follows a sword-wearing Yangban. It seems the Silver Fly, too, has now gained the title of "Rebellious Fly" and added at least one protective policeman. In a world ruled by the sword-wearer, ah, danger! Danger!!... But a promise is a promise, so this time I shall go to Miss Kim, who brandishes her single life as a shield, like a Westerner seeking Justice. Anyone hearing this Miss Kim's story must first know that she is the owner of beauty that burns the hearts of countless young men, a New Woman who graduated from a certain girls' school in Seoul and has even been abroad.

Fly: "Madam, are you alone? Where did the young lady go out?"
Madam: "Some Youth Association or Chapel, they said, asked her to give a speech. So she went there."
Fly: "Hahaa, the elder young lady?! Asked her to give a speech? What kind of speech?"
Madam: "How would I know what kind of speech. She says so, so I suppose it's true..."
Fly: "Where did the younger young lady go? Did the younger young lady also go to give a speech?"
Madam: "That child went to some Office, she said it's outside Tongdaemun. She went to handle business there. She said the business won't get done if she doesn't go. She said she must go without being late."
Fly: "The elder young lady goes to give speeches, and the younger young lady goes to handle business. They are quite remarkable." <107>
Madam: "One shouldn't look down on them just because they're daughters. Since they graduated like that and participate in society... If only their father were alive, how pleased he would have been... That's the one thing that makes me sad."
Fly: "Of course. In today's world, even girls must be educated. What need is there to say it? But it would be good if they learned properly, but studying-schmudying, wandering around, and like your young ladies, day and night with vagrants... no, no, I mean... By the way, the elder young lady is well past marriageable age, isn't she getting married?"
Madam: "Without a son, I rely only on those two, and I can't just leave her past her age, so I tried to find a match, and already several rich men from the countryside have proposed, but what can I do if she herself says she won't go."
Fly: "So you'll just listen to the young lady and let her remain a maiden, no, just, just leave her be?"
Madam: "She says she'll live her whole life as a maiden and grow old like that. She says, although we are mother and daughter, once married, she becomes an outsider, so she can't leave her lone mother behind, and besides, if she were with parents-in-law and a husband, there's so much social work to do, she wouldn't be able to do any of it. So she says she'll just live alone, participating in society, and won't leave her mother. Her sentiment is admirable, isn't it?"
Fly: "And so, because there's so much of that social work, the nightly comings and goings happen every single night, and on days she doesn't go out, many gentlemen come to visit?"
Madam: "Well, I tried to stop her at first, too, but she said that unlike ignorant women like us who stayed stuck in a room doing needlework and cooking in the kitchen in the old style, it's customary for any New Woman nowadays to do so. She said because there's much social work, her comings and goings naturally become frequent, and also those who visit to discuss things are all famous gentlemen in society. And they also say that the world now is different from before, and women too learn much and work without losing to men. She said only in the uncivilized past did women practice avoidance of outside men and wear head coverings pulled down tight when going out on the street, but not anymore. And she asked what would be the point of studying if one were just stuck in a room instead of participating in society and working like that. It's just that ignorant people needlessly say this and that and badmouth her..." <108>
Fly: "It's pitiful! Because the parent is ignorant, whatever the daughter does, they just believe it, calling it 'New Style, New Style,' thus ruining their precious daughter. Is going out at night the New Style? Is wandering around without staying put at home the New Style? Goodness, the elder young lady is coming. Carrying a kangkangi, no, a violin..."

Today too, several vagrant youths must have followed her right up to the main gate. Miss Kim, who speaks of the single life, where did she go and what speech did she give, returning carrying a violin? Did she greet her mother or not? She went straight into her own room.
...(A short while later)...

Fly: "Young Lady, where have you been?"
Kim: "Why!? I just went to a friend's house."
Fly: "Did you go to your friend's house to give a speech?"
Kim: "Speech? Why, did someone say I gave a speech? Oh oh, Madam must have said that. When I went out earlier, I told her that on purpose, that I was going to give a speech..."
Fly: "Then what did you do at your friend's house? Did you give a speech on the single life?"
Kim: "N-no, I went to Hwanggŭmchŏng with my friend and got photos taken. We each took a solo portrait and one together..."
Fly: "Have all those many photos run out already? Taking solo portraits again... Who are you having a photo-marriage with this time?"
Kim: "When did I say I'm getting married? I'm going to live a single life, aren't I?"
Fly: "There comes the single life again. Exactly for what reason are you living a single life? Is it truly out of a filial heart, because you cannot bear to leave your mother alone? Or is it an extreme principle of respecting individuality too much...?"
Kim: "..."
Fly: "Or else, are you planning to grow old single for the sake of faith based on some doctrine? Is that it? Huh? Is it Christianity? Buddhism? Huh?"
Kim: "You're annoying. Stop nagging."
Fly: "What do you mean, nagging? Ah, you believe in all religions, don't you? On the Buddha's Birthday, you're a Buddhist disciple; on Christmas Day, a Christian disciple; and on days when many people gather at the Catholic Church <109> you're a Catholic disciple... Right? Still, among them, Buddhism is probably the closest, isn't it? Since you often go out to Ch'ŏngnyangsa Temple even late at night."
Kim: "Why are you raising your voice and making a fuss like this? So they can hear in the main room..."
Fly: "Still trying to deceive your poor widowed mother to the very end... What? Some Youth Association asked you to give a speech? You deceive her well! A maiden goes out at night because she has much social work? What kind of work is that, work? Claiming your mother doesn't know the affairs of the changing world, you insist only on 'New Style, New Style,' and wander about fluttering, to the theater at night, to the streets by day—is that your so-called work? In such a state, why do you talk of a single life? Is it because there's no man who will take you...?! Is it because there's no person like your ideal type?! Are all the men who visit the house famous gentlemen? How could they not be? Famous vagrant youths from the 'Register of Scoundrels'! Several guys visit every day, claiming to teach music or discuss some association affairs, deceiving your old mother while whispering in the next room. At night, claiming to perform at a concert or give a speech, you deceive her and go off together to the theater. Is that all? Your younger sister, too, is she not a so-called social work expert no less than her older sister, with many male acquaintances and unforgivable... No, and do you tell your mother about each other's doings? Fearing that, you pretend not to know what you know, and occasionally when you meet eyes, you exchange shameful smiles, right? While doing so, the older sister in her room, the younger sister in her room... And you call yourselves human? Still call yourselves New Women? Your innocent old mother sits happy, thinking you participate in society thanks to her educating you. As for you, if you were married and had a regular husband, you wouldn't be able to conduct that wonderful, high-income business-work, would you? For you, your single life is a clever stratagem for business development! That harmonium (風琴) that X, still attending school, bought for you after deceiving their parents, what does that signify? And that gold watch (金時計) on your arm, sent by O, famous as a vagrant, after getting caught stealing, what does that speak of? The mantle (만또) you acquired this winter, that didn't just appear out of thin air either, did it? To hide all these deeds, worried someone might suspect your chastity (貞操), the shield you put up is that <110> single life, isn't it? As your so-called social work develops and your acquaintances multiply, having reached a point where you absolutely cannot serve one man as a husband, the words that come out are 'single life,' right?"
Kim: "..."
Fly: "Why no answer? Your eloquence, which flowed like water before your mother, where has it gone? Huh?
Ah, O holy single person! Afraid that three or four grooms might rush at you on your wedding day, a single-lifer who decided to live a single life, outwardly an infinitely pure single-lifer, secretly agonizing single life! A single person sacrificing herself for social work, a single person sacrificing herself for her old mother, you truly admirable single person...
Oops, what is this? Trying to grab me? Do you think I'll get caught entangled in your hands like some dazed vagrant? If my words are lies and you are a genuine single-lifer, truly a person who will take female purity to the grave, why is there a book called 'Contraception Methods' (避妊法), bought in Japan, in your dresser drawer? Huh? Still a single-lifer? A single-lifer who wants to know contraception methods—you are famous! Ahh,..." <111>

**Summary**

This satirical piece from 1921, presented as a dialogue between a sharp-tongued critic ("Fly") and a "New Woman" (Miss Kim), critiques the perceived hypocrisy of modern women embracing new freedoms. Miss Kim publicly claims to pursue a single life out of filial piety and dedication to social work, but the "Fly" accuses her of using this as a cover for promiscuous behavior, deception of her naive mother, and accumulating possessions through questionable means, ultimately pointing to a book on contraception in her possession as proof against her claims of purity and noble singlehood. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects the intense social anxieties and debates surrounding the changing roles of women ("New Women") in early colonial Korea. It showcases conservative backlash against female education, social participation, and perceived Western influences, portraying the "New Woman" not as liberated but as morally corrupt and deceitful, highlighting the tensions between tradition and modernity, and the difficulties women faced in navigating new social landscapes under colonial rule.

마님『내야 아들도 업시 저이 둘만 밋고 사는데 저이를 過年하도록 내바려 둘 수도 업서서 婚處를 求하려구도 하고 벌서 시골 富者들이 몃 번이나 求婚을 하건마는 제가 안간다는 걸 어떠케 하니.』
파리『그래 아가씨 말만 듯고 그대로 處女로 아니, 그냥, 그냥, 내버려 두실 터입니까.』
마님『永-그냥 處女로 그냥 늙겟다구 한단다. 암만 母女間이라도 出嫁하면 外人인즉 홀로 계신 어머니를 버리고 갈 수도 업거니와 시父母와 男便과 가잇스면 社會相 事務도 만흔데 그 일을 하나도 못하게 되겟스니까. 그냥 저 혼자 저대로 社會에 出入이나 하고 어머니를 떠나지 안코 산다구 한단다. 마음이야 奇特하지.』
파리『그래서 그 社會上 事務가 만하서 밤이 깁도록 쏘대기가 每日每夜요, 出入하지 안는 날이면 차저오는 紳士가 만쿠면요.』
