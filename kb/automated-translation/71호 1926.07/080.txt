Advertisement Correction
						From the Gaebyeok Company Advertising Department

Regarding the congratulations for development published in the May issue (No. 69) of this magazine, the name of Mr. "Pak Kwan-ch'ae" (朴琯釆) of Sambong-dong, Nam-myŏn, Sŏnch'ŏn-gun was mistakenly printed as "Pak Kwan-ch'ae" (朴琯采). We hereby correct this and offer our apologies.

From the Gaebyeok Company Advertising Department <78>

**Summary**

This article is a brief notice from the advertising department of *Gaebyeok* magazine correcting a misprint in a previous issue (No. 69, May 1926). The name of Mr. Pak Kwan-ch'ae from Sŏnch'ŏn-gun was printed with an incorrect Hanja character (采 instead of 釆), and the magazine apologizes for the error. For historians, this notice, though mundane, confirms the publication details of *Gaebyeok*, illustrates standard publishing practices like issuing corrections, and provides a specific name and location (Sŏnch'ŏn-gun) linked to the magazine's readership or contributors in 1926, potentially offering a small data point for social or regional history during the colonial period. The attention paid to correcting even a subtle Hanja difference in a name might also be noted.
