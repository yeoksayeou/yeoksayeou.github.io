Company Notice

It is truly delightful that the manuscripts for Mr. Kim Satkat's (金笠) poetry, prose, and other biographical materials, which our company's literary department began soliciting several months ago, have piled up like a mountain thanks to the ardent support of our readers. As submissions are still continuously arriving without cease, we are unavoidably extending the editing deadline by one month. We request that those who wish to submit please send their manuscripts promptly. <8>

**Summary**

This is a company notice from the magazine *Kaebyŏk* announcing an extension for submissions related to the poet Kim Sakkat (金笠). Due to the enthusiastic response from readers resulting in a large volume of manuscripts, the deadline has been pushed back by one month. For historians, this notice highlights the active public interest in traditional Korean literary figures during the colonial period, suggesting a desire to engage with and preserve national culture. The magazine's role in facilitating this cultural activity is also evident.
