기사제목	漫畵에 나타난 新興文壇의 文士 (Literary Figures of the Emerging Literary Circle Appearing in Cartoons)
필자	安碩柱
기사형태	만화 (Cartoon)

From the Editor
As for the initial plan, it was to depict all the literary figures through cartoons, but due to space limitations, it was utterly difficult to do all at once. Also, concerned about adding further fatigue to Mr. Sŏgyŏng's already very tired brush, we have decided to introduce the people of the emerging literary circle first, dividing it into the first installment. Among them, it is also regrettable that figures such as Mr. Songyŏng and others could not be drawn, unavoidably, because the artist has neither seen their faces nor possesses photographs. However, we intend to try and create another opportunity next time.

**Summary**

This editor's note explains the context for a cartoon feature in the magazine *Kaebyŏk*. The original intent was to caricature all contemporary literary figures, but due to limited page space and concern for the cartoonist's (An Sŏkchu, referred to by his pen name Sŏgyŏng) workload, the feature was limited to writers associated with the "Emerging Literary Circle" (Sinhŭng Mundan) as a first installment. The editor expresses regret that some figures, like Songyŏng, couldn't be included due to the artist's lack of familiarity with their appearance or absence of photos, promising a future installment. For historians, this piece offers insight into the editorial processes and constraints (space, artist availability, source material like photos) of a prominent colonial-era Korean magazine. It also highlights the formation and recognition of specific literary groups like the Sinhŭng Mundan and the use of cartoons as a medium for representing cultural figures.
