餘墨 (Residual Ink / Afterthoughts)

As previously announced, this issue is the enlarged edition commemorating the 6th anniversary of this magazine's founding. Initially, we planned to make the contents unprecedentedly grand to bring great satisfaction to our readers.

However, as the saying goes, "Good things face many obstacles" (好事多魔). During the editing process, the entire staff was detained for many days under suspicion of involvement in a certain major incident. Upon release, all planned work had unfortunately come to nothing.

Among these, the most regrettable incident was the loss of many precious manuscripts (玉稿) submitted by comrades from within the country and overseas (海內海外) during the so-called Great Search (大搜索). Although it may not be our fault, we offer our apologies to the writers.

Nevertheless, with the spirit of "The river flows, but the rocks do not turn" (江流石不轉), we renewed our commitment to our principles (主義) and compiled this issue. Considering the short timeframe, the content has become relatively substantial.

In this issue, Mr. Yi Sun-t'ak's lengthy thesis "Chosŏn Population Statistics (朝鮮人口統計)," Mr. An Chae-hong's "General Overview of 500 Years of Chosŏn (朝鮮500年總祥)," and Ch'ŏng'o's "Scholar Purges and Factional Strife (士禍及黨爭)" are worth reading once for reference or research. Also, Yi Ya-roe's tale "To Chŏk (盜跖)" will bring hearty laughter under the green shade of long summer days. Besides these, a special feature in literature is the introduction to foreign literature by several dozen distinguished figures from home and abroad. In any case, we believe this issue is worth reading once, both in quality and quantity.

What use is there in boasting about good crops on confiscated farmland? However, last month's "Grand View of 500 Years of Chosŏn" issue (朝鮮500大觀號) truly received a great welcome from readers all over the world (滿天下). But, only a few days after publication, it was confiscated by the authorities (당국). Subsequently, we issued an extra, which was also confiscated, and then even the extra's extra was confiscated, making it three consecutive confiscations (連3차). Due to this, the timing was delayed, and the editing for the July issue became hectic, so regrettably, we had no choice but to halt the publication of the third extra. There is no issue for those who have already seen it, but we apologize (未安을 謝하다) to the readers who could not.

Mr. Pak Tal-sŏng, despite limping (절늠절늠) on a leg injured during his detention (구금), has continuously traveled to the Western Korea (西鮮) region on business for our Headquarters Expansion Preparatory Committee (本社 擴張 旣成會). Although he has received much sympathy and support before, we earnestly request (仰囑) our compatriots in Western Korea to show even more special sympathy and support this time. <97>

**Summary**

This editorial afterword from *Kaebyŏk* magazine's July 1926 issue apologizes to readers for the diminished content of the 6th-anniversary edition. The editors explain that the staff's recent arrest and detention related to an unspecified "major incident," along with the confiscation and loss of submitted manuscripts during a "Great Search," severely disrupted their plans. Despite these setbacks, they highlight key articles in the current issue and lament the repeated confiscation of their previous special issue and subsequent extras by colonial authorities. The piece concludes with an appeal for support for a staff member undertaking fundraising activities despite a recent injury sustained during detention. For historians, this text vividly illustrates the constant threat of censorship and suppression faced by Korean publications under Japanese rule, the resilience of journalists and intellectuals who continued their work despite arrests and confiscations, and the specific mechanisms of control (like searches, seizures of issues/extras, and detention of staff). The mention of a "major incident" and the repeated confiscations point to the politically sensitive nature of the magazine's content.

餘墨
豫告한 바와가티 이번 號는 本誌創刊 제6주년기념 增大號이다. 당초에 예정하기는 내용을 아주 空前宏大하게 하야 독자 여러분에게 대만족을 들이랴고 하얏다.

그러나 好事多魔라고 編輯臨時에 사원 전부가 某 중대사건에 관련되엿다는 혐의로 多日間 피검이 되엿다가 나오니 예정한 일이 모도다 실패에 歸하게 되엿다.

其中에도 제일 통석한 일은 소위 大搜索을 하는 중에 海內海外의 여러 동지로부터 投寄한 玉稿를 만히 분실한 그것이다. 비록 우리의 과실은 안이라할지나 필자 여러분에게 미안한 말슴을 드리다.

그러나 江流石不轉으로 우리는 우리 主義의 정신을 더욱 가다듬어 이번 호를 편성한 까닭에 短時日의 일로 보와서는 비교적 내용이 충실하게 되였다.

이번 호에 이순탁씨의 「朝鮮人口統計」의 장편논문과 安在鴻氏의 「朝鮮500年總祥」과 靑吾君의 「士禍及黨爭」은 參考로나 硏究로나 한번 읽을만하고 또 李夜雷의 「盜跖」說話는 長夏綠陰에 破顔大笑가 되겟스며 其外의 문예로는 內外 數十 名士의 외국문학소개가 특색이라 하겟다. 엇지하엿던 이번 호는 質로나 量으로나 한번 읽을 필요가 잇다고 생각한다.

빼앗긴 농토에 곡물 잘된 것을 자랑한들 무슨 소용이 잇스랴마는 前月分 朝鮮500大觀號는 참으로 滿天下 독자의 대환영을 바덧섯다. 그러나 발행한지 수일만에 당국의 압수를 當하고 繼하야 號外를 발행하다가 또 압수를 당하고 其後 호외의 호외까지 連3차 압수를 당하게 되엿다. 그리고보니 시기도 느저지고 7월호 편집도 분망하야 제3차 호외발행은 유감이나마 부득이 중지하게 되엿다. 기왕 보신 이는 문제가 업거니와 밋처 보시지 못한 독자 여러분에게 未安을 謝한다.

朴達成君은 구금 중에 닷친 다리를 절늠절늠 절면서도 본사 擴張 旣成會의 용무를 가지고 계속하야 西鮮 방면으로 출장하게되엿다. 기왕에도 만흔 동정과 애호를 바덧지마는 이번에 더욱 특별한 동정과 애호가 잇기를 西鮮 同胞에게 仰囑한다.＜97＞
