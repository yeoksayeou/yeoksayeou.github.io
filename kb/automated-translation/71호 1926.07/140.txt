京城雜話
						尖口生

An amendment to the Chosŏn Temple Ordinance (朝鮮寺刹令) is expected to be announced soon, and one characteristic feature is said to be the recognition of monks eating meat and taking wives (食肉娶妻). Since many monks were already eating meat, taking wives, drinking alcohol, and even keeping concubines before the legal recognition, it seems it would have been good if this ordinance had been instructed to be implemented even before its promulgation.

The so-called Poch'ŏnkyo Kyŏngsŏng Chinjŏngwŏn (普天敎京城眞正院) in Chaidong, downtown, was mortgaged to a Japanese woman named Iizuka for 15,000 wŏn. As there was no way to repay the debt, the creditor recently carried out enforcement and even sealed the gates. In an era when even the True Son of Heaven (眞天子) met his demise, it is perhaps not surprising that the detached palace of a False Son of Heaven (假天子) would be sealed.

The Land Improvement Stock Company (土地改良株式會社), the Second Tōtaku (第二東拓), is gradually becoming a reality, recruiting stocks from Koreans as well. O Hŭiwŏn, a wealthy man from P'yŏngan North Province residing in Kyŏngsŏng, is said to have applied for the most shares, 1,300. Although it's like giving away your boat and then begging for passage, a directorship in that company is probably a reserved seat for him!

Mizusawa Masakazu, a large landowner in Japan's Hyōgo Prefecture, was tormented by tenant disputes, went mad, stabbed his parents and two other family members, and then committed suicide. It is a fixed rule that riches bring much death (富則多死), but if all the capitalists in this world had such thorough agony as this landowner, all the movements cried out for by the propertyless would be well realized without effort. Seen this way, Mizusawa's madness might better be called thorough awareness rather than madness.

Regarding the student manse incident on the day of the Insan (因山) procession, June 10th, the issue of responsibility has arisen within the Government-General authorities. Some say it's the result of the Education Bureau's misguided educational policy, while others claim it's a reaction to the Police Bureau's overly strict security measures, leading to much debate back and forth. This, so to speak, is like a beggar weaving his own sack.

Speaking of the Police Bureau, according to their announcement, in some regions on the day of the Insan, the old Korean flag (구한국기) was hoisted because the Japanese flag was not yet prepared. While they're at it, why not say that the students shouting "Chosŏn OO Manse" did so as a substitute (代唱) because they didn't yet know the word "Japan"?

At the Ponch'ŏng Police Station, during the interrogation of someone recently arrested, they asked peculiar questions like, "What relationship did you have with Inspector Miwa of Chŏngno Station, and what did you do together?" It seems that station considers Mr. Miwa some kind of dangerous figure too.

Hyŏn Hŭiu'n of the Actors' School used the stage name Hyŏn Ch'ŏl for several years, but recently he is said to have changed his name again to Hyŏn Munjongch'ŏl. As long as the life of art is long, who would argue about an artist's name being long? However, for a Korean, not Japanese, a four-character name is quite a masterpiece.

The June issue of Sinmin (新民) contained many articles attributed to authors without their knowledge, drawing much criticism from those named. Elder Sŏkch'on Yun Yonggu eventually raised the issue, even advertising in the newspaper to demand the cancellation of the title "Annals of Sunjong" (純宗實記), and Yi Kakkchong also published an apology. Thus, there is talk that Sinmin is not Sinmin (New People) but Kimin (欺民 - Cheating the People). <110>

Kŭnhwa School (槿花學校), facing financial difficulties, sent Principal Ms. Kim Mirisa all the way to Japan with a letter of introduction obtained from some high official in the Government-General, but ultimately failed to achieve good results and returned empty-handed. Recently, they have gained the support of the controversial actress Yi Wŏrhwa, Ch'oe Sŏnghae, and their group, holding a joint opera performance (歌劇會) with students. Enthusiasm for education is enthusiasm, but isn't there a need to think things over a bit more?

Chŏn Sŏnguk and others, cadres of the People's National Funeral Commemoration Group (府民國葬奉悼團), received money intended for over 2,400 members of the pallbearer group (輿士軍) – 15 wŏn per person (5 wŏn for clothing, 10 wŏn wages). However, they gave only 7 wŏn each (2 wŏn for clothing, 5 wŏn wages) to the pallbearers. They planned to hide and divide the remaining large sum of nearly 20,000 wŏn amongst themselves, but ultimately Chŏn Sŏnguk allegedly took it all alone and didn't give even a penny to the others, leading to infighting and the exposure of their disgraceful conduct. Dogs fighting over bones at a house of mourning (喪家狗爭骨) is an old saying, but where else could such despicable wickedness as theirs exist? Better to say nothing.

A certain Christian pastor Kim, famous for strictly receiving 30 wŏn per wedding ceremony he officiates, told someone that it was fortunate no one from his church was arrested (檢束) before or after the recent state funeral. He certainly has the qualifications to go to the Government-General and request a special bonus.

The Kyŏngsŏng Municipal Office is expanding roads, and this time they say they will specifically make the residents who benefit from the new roads bear the cost. Methods of exploitation seem to be gradually evolving.

The Kyŏngsŏng Weather Observatory (京城測候所) predicted that if high pressure broke, welcome rain (甘雨) would come by June 21st or 22nd. Perhaps because the high pressure hasn't broken yet, the drought has become even more severe since the 21st and 22nd. Since a weather observatory isn't originally a god-like prophet, it's not expected to be absolutely correct in its predictions, but if the Kyŏngsŏng Weather Observatory were as good at getting the weather right [as it is at getting it wrong], there would be no worry about being mistaken.

A former Hanil Bank employee named Pak Sangsŏl, due to his dissolute life, experienced domestic discord and daily beat his wife, even gouging out her eye before expelling her to her family home. Subsequently, he kicked his own elderly mother to death, causing a great incident that destroys ethics and corrupts morals (滅倫敗常). Such articles occasionally appear in Japanese newspapers, but this is the first time such a major incident has appeared in Korean newspapers. Should this also be called a result of the changing times?

Regarding the June 6th incident, the simultaneous arrest (檢束) of the Cho Wŏnsuk siblings from the Women's Comrades Association (女性同友會) could be called "Two red dots amid a thicket of green" (萬綠叢中雙点紅).

Also, regarding the June 10th incident, while Kyŏngsŏng University student Yi Ch'ŏnjin being a ringleader is an issue, the fact that Ch'oe Chemin, the son of an official in the Korean Government-General's Police Bureau, was also a ringleader is truly a peculiar fact. However, it is fortunate that there isn't a second Hwang Ok. <111>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of short news items and gossip from Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul) in mid-1926, written with a satirical and critical tone by "Sharp-Tongued Person" (尖口生). Topics include the impending legalization of monks marrying and eating meat, the financial collapse of a Poch'ŏnkyo religious center, involvement of Korean elites in Japanese-led land development, a shocking murder-suicide by a Japanese landowner contrasted with the potential efficacy of proletarian movements, bureaucratic infighting over responsibility for the June 10th Manse protests, police actions and alleged biases, name changes of cultural figures, accusations of plagiarism against the magazine *Sinmin*, questionable fundraising methods by a girls' school, corruption among organizers of Emperor Sunjong's funeral, a pastor boasting about his congregation avoiding arrests, new methods of taxation for road expansion, failed weather predictions, a horrific domestic violence case ending in matricide, and notes on arrests related to recent political incidents, including the noteworthy involvement of an official's son. For historians, the piece offers valuable glimpses into colonial society: the tension between tradition and modernization (Buddhist reform), the rise and fall of new religious movements, Korean participation in colonial economic structures, commentary on social stratification and unrest (landowner suicide, June 10th Movement), critiques of colonial administration and policing, cultural happenings, everyday corruption, and the underlying political dissent simmering beneath the surface, often expressed through coded language or gossip. The commentary on the June 10th incident and the mention of specific individuals involved provide insight into the event's immediate aftermath and public perception.

시내 齊洞에 잇는 소위 普天敎京城眞正院은 飯塚이라는 日女에게 15,000원에 저당하엿더니 辦償의 道가 전무하야 일전에는 債主가 집행을 하고 門戶까지도 봉쇄를 하얏다고 한다. 眞天子도 파멸을 당하는 이 시대에 假天子의 별궁이 봉쇄당하기도 容或無怪이지.

第二東拓인 土地改良株式會社는 점차 실현되야 조선인게도 株를 모집하는데 平北 富豪로 京城에 존재하는 吳熙源군이 최상으로 1,300株를 응모하얏다 한다. 배주고 배속 비러먹는 격이지만 同會社의 중역 한아는 아마 뀌여노흔 당상일걸!

日本 兵庫縣의 대지주인 水澤政一은 소작쟁의로 煩悶 發狂되야 자기의 부모 외 가족 2인을 刺傷하고 자기까지 자살하얏다. 富則多死는 원래 定則이지만은 이 세상의 모든 자본주가 이 지주 가튼 철저한 번민이 잇슬 것 가트면 무산자의 절규하는 모든 운동은 不勞하고도 잘 실현이 될 것이다. 그리고 보면 水澤의 發狂은 發狂이라 하는 이보다 철저한 각오라 함이 可할 것이다.

6월 10일 因山 당일에 학생의 만세사건으로 총독부 당국에서는 책임문제가 니러나서 일부에서는 學務 當局이 교육방침을 그릇한 결과라 하고 또 일부에서는 警務 當局이 경계를 너무 엄중히 한 반동이라 하야 갑론을박이 매우 만흔 모양이다. 이것이 말하자면 거렁이의 제 자루 짓는 격이라고나 할가.

警務局 말이 낫스니 말이지 同局의 발표에 의하면 因山 당일에 모모의 지방에서는 일본국기를 아즉 미비함으로 구한국기를 게양하얏다고 하얏다. 이왕이면 학생들의 조선OO만세 부른 것도 학생이 아즉 일본이라는 말을 몰나서 조선OO만세도 代唱하얏다고 하는 것이 何如.

本町警察署에서는 이번에 검거된 엇던 사람을 심문하는데 네가 鍾路署 三輪警部와 평소에 엇던 관계가 잇스며 무슨 일을 서로 하엿느냐고 진기한 심문을 하더라니 그 署에서는 三輪君도 무슨 위험인물로 인정하는 모양인가.

俳優學校의 玄僖運군은 예명을 玄哲로 하야 몃해 전부터 행세하더니 근일에는 다시 玄門宗哲이라고 變名하얏다 한다. 예술의 생명이 長한 이상에야 예술가의 성명이 長한들 누가 그의 論難하리오마는 일본인이 안인 조선인으로는 四字姓名도 대걸작이다.

新民 6월호는 본인도 不知하는 記名記事가 만하서 記名된 이의 비난이 만터니 石村 尹用求노인은 그여히 문제를 이르키여 「純宗實記」라는 題號取消을 신문상에까지 광고하고 李覺鍾군도 사과 광고를 또 하얏다. 그리하야 新民은 新民이 안이고 欺民이라는 평이 잇다.＜110＞

槿花學校에서는 經費困難으로 총독부 모 고관의 소개장을 어더가지고 교장 金美理士씨가 일본까지 갓다가 결국 好成績을 엇지 못하고 空歸하더니 근일에는 또 문제의 여배우 李月華, 崔星海 등 일파의 후원을 得하야 학생과 共同的 歌劇會를 開하얏다. 교육에 열심도 열심이지마는 좀더 한 번 생각할 필요가 업슬가.

府民國葬奉悼團의 간부인 全聖旭 외 모모는 輿士軍二千四百餘名에게 每人 15원식(피복비 5圓 貸金 10圓井) 줄 돈을 타 가지고 輿士軍에게는 僅히 7원식만 주고 (피복비 2원 貸金 5원) 其餘額 근 20,000원의 거금을 隱匿分食하랴다가 결국 全聖旭이 단독 沒喫하고 타인은 一分도 주지 안이함으로 피차 爭鬪가 생겨서 추태를 폭로하게 되엿다 한다. 喪家狗爭骨은 自來잇는 말이지만은 彼輩 가튼 추잡한 죄악이 또 어듸 잇스랴 寧欲無言

혼례식 주례 한 번에 일금 30원식 꼭꼭 바더 먹기로 유명한 기독교 목사 김모는 엇던 사람에게 대하야 말하기를 이번 國葬 前後에 자기 교회에서 한 사람도 檢束이 아니된 것은 다행이라고 하더라니 총독부에 가서 특별상여금을 청구할 자격이 잇군.

京城府에서는 도로를 확장하는데 금번에는 특별히 신도로로 受益하는 주민에게 그 비용을 부담케 한다고 한다. 착취의 방법도 점차 진화하는 모양이지.
