A Bundle of Small Talk
						JSU Saeng

Border Security and the Great Wall. Emperor Qin Shi Huang, in order to defend against the invasion of the northern barbarians (北奴), gathered the people of the realm (天下) to build the Great Wall (萬里城) on the border, seeking to monopolize eternal glory for himself. Ultimately, however, he fell in the second generation, becoming fodder for a rustic scholar's verse: "Not knowing disaster arose from within one's own walls (蕭墻內), futilely was built the ten-thousand-li wall to defend against the Hu (胡)." The wise men of the Land of the Rising Sun (日之丸國 - Japan), who used to ridicule this, are now dispatching numerous police officers to the three-thousand-li border along the Changbaek Mountain range to defend against the invasion of radical thought (過激思想) from the continent. They have deployed numerous armed officers at intervals of one li or even half a li. Whether it's the Great Wall built of stone and earth or the Great Wall built of humans and animals, though there may be a difference in era, it seems they are equally foolish policies (愚策).

The Meaning of Cultural Rule Adopted by the Japanese. The Cultural Rule (文化政治) spoken of by Koreans and the Cultural Rule spoken of by Japanese seem to have different meanings. What is the Cultural Rule spoken of by Japanese residing in Chosŏn? The three Japanese representatives, Ōishi Suekichi, Mizuno Iwao, and Shiki Shintarō, selected by the resolution of the so-called All-Korea Chamber of Commerce Federation (全鮮商議聯合會), recently went to Tokyo stating they would report Chosŏn's situation to the central government. While visiting the Prime Minister and other important figures, they publicized Chosŏn's situation in various ways. Among these, the strangest publicity was the call to thoroughly implement the so-called Culture. That is to say, they stated, "To thoroughly implement Cultural Rule in Chosŏn, at least two million mainlanders (內地人 - Japanese) must be brought to Chosŏn to fundamentally ensure the enrichment of economic power." If this kind of Cultural Rule were to be completed, assuming 50,000 Japanese immigrate to Chosŏn each year, it would take approximately 40 years for the full two million to immigrate. Then, 40 years from now, we might see the complete fruits of Cultural Rule. Koreans who will suffer the conflict of interests from such a Cultural Rule, take great caution, great vigilance!!

Who is Noda Heijirō? A certain Count (伯爵 某氏), a great politician of Chosŏn famous for seeking private profit through public actions, perhaps wishing to avoid public attention regarding his own movements,<116> frequently appears and disappears on the Pusan-Shimonoseki Ferry (關釜連絡船), even changing his name. The said gentleman, trying to evade public scrutiny with the changed name "Noda Heijirō" (野田平次郞), which was absent from the passenger list, was unfortunately caught by a newspaper reporter and delivered an imposing political statement... "The issue of political participation rights (叅政權) for Koreans is important, but more than anything, Koreans must have the readiness (覺悟) to bear the duty of conscription (徵兵)... and above all else, the reform of the Central Council (中樞院) is the most urgent priority." ... .

Who Created the So-Called Disloyal Koreans? A student researching disloyal Koreans (不逞鮮人) says that if a portion of Koreans are to be called disloyal Koreans, then all Japanese must inevitably be the originators (元祖) who produced them. Furthermore, if some form of Japan-Korea harmony (日鮮親和) were realized, all Koreans would end up becoming disloyal Koreans. The reason given is that many of today's so-called disloyal Koreans were assimilated into Japanese culture, assimilated into the Japanese personality, and directly modeled themselves after the Japanese archetype and Japanese spirit while residing in mainland Japan or Chosŏn.

The Origin of the Name Kando. Historically, Kando (間島) is part of ancient Chosŏn; culturally, Kando is a settlement of modern Koreans. However, it seems rare for people to have deeply researched the etymological origin (言源出處) of the name Kando. According to the explanation of Dr. Shinoda (篠田法學博士), Doctor of Law and current Governor of South P'yŏngan Province, who obtained his doctorate with a thesis on Kando, there are two theories regarding the origin of the name Kando. One theory is that when Koreans living near the border in the past advanced north seeking places to migrate, they first called the vast new land north of the Tumen River (豆滿江以北) "K'ant'o" (墾土 - cultivated land), which later underwent phonetic corruption (音相訛) into "Kando." Another theory is that there is a small island (小島) in the middle of the Tumen River north of Chongsŏng in North Hamgyŏng Province. Because its location occupied the center of the river, after this island was newly pioneered (開拓), it was called "Kando" (間島 - island in between). Later, as people gradually advanced onto the continent by crossing the river via this island, even today, the land north of this island came to be collectively called Kando. Regardless of which theory is correct, it is clearly beyond doubt that the name Kando was first uttered by Koreans.<117>

**Summary**

This collection of short opinion pieces from 1922 critiques Japanese colonial policies and attitudes. The author likens Japan's border control against "radical thought" to the folly of the Great Wall, warns against the Japanese version of "Cultural Rule" which involves mass Japanese immigration to Korea, exposes a high-ranking Korean collaborator traveling incognito, argues that Japanese influence itself created the so-called "disloyal Koreans," and discusses the Korean origins of the name "Kando." The article reveals contemporary Korean intellectual dissent against Japanese rule, particularly highlighting skepticism towards the "Cultural Policy," concerns about demographic change, awareness of collaboration, and assertions of Korean historical/cultural claims over border regions like Kando. Historians might find the specific criticisms of the Cultural Policy (mass immigration), the anecdote about the collaborating Count, and the discussion on the Kando naming origins particularly interesting for understanding Korean perspectives and resistance narratives during this period.

國境警備와 萬里長城. 秦始皇帝는 北奴의 侵入을 防禦키 爲하야 天下의 民을 召集하야 邊境에 萬里城을 築하고 萬世의 榮華를 自專하려다가 畢竟은 2世에 自盡하야 「不知禍起蕭墻內. 虛築防胡萬里城」이라는 野士의 一片詩材꺼리가 되고 마는 것을 嘲笑하든 日之丸國의 賢人輩은 大陸國境으로부터 侵入하는 過激思想를 防禦키 爲하야 長白山沿流國境 三千里 邊에 多數의 警官을 派送하야 一里 或은 半里를 間隔하야 多數의 武裝官을 配置하엿다. 石士로 築한 萬里長城이나 人畜으로 築한 萬里長城이나 時代的 差異는 잇다 할지나 그 愚策되기는 一般일 듯하다. 日本人의 採用하는 文化政治의 意味. 朝鮮사람이 말하는 文化政治, 日本사람이 말하는 文化政治는 各其 意味가 同一치 아니한 듯하다. 朝鮮에 在住하는 日本사람이 말하는 文化政治는 어떠한 것인가? 所謂 全鮮商議聯合會決議로 選出된 日本人 代表者 大石季吉, 水野巖, 志歧信太郞 三人은 朝鮮事情을 中央政府에 陳述한다고 向者에 東京에 往하야 總理大臣 以下 各重要人物을 歷訪하면서 여러 가지로 朝鮮의 事情을 宣傳하얏는데 그 中에서 가장 奇怪한 宣傳은 所謂 文化를 徹底히 施行하라는 宣傳이다. 即 말하되 『朝鮮의 文化政治를 徹底히 하자면 적어도 二百萬人의 內地人을 朝鮮에 招來하야 根本的으로 經濟力의 充實을 期하여야만 한다』고 萬一 이러한 意味의 文化政治가 完成되자면 每年 五萬人의 日本人이 朝鮮에 移住한다 하고 二百萬人이 全部 移住되자면 約 40年을 要할 것이다. 그러면 自今 40年 後에는 完全한 文化政治의 實果를 見케 될지니 이러한 文化政治의 利害衝突을 受할 朝鮮人은 大注意 大警戒!!
野田平次郞은 誰?. 公的 行爲로 私的 利益을 圖取키에 有名한 朝鮮의 大政治家 伯爵 某氏는 自己出動에＜116＞ 對한 世人의 注視를 回避코저 그리하엿던지 自己의 姓名까지 變하여 가지고 關釜連絡船에 자조 出沒한다. 乘客名簿에 간데 업는 「野田平次郞」의 改名으로 世人의 注視를 回避하려 하든 同氏는 不幸히 新聞記者에 붓들려 가지고 堂堂한 政治談을 吐하되...「朝鮮人의 叅政權問題가 重하니 모다도, 『朝鮮人은 徵兵應募의 義務를 負擔할 覺悟가 有하야만 하겟고... 또 무엇보다도 中樞院 改革이 最急先務라고』... . 所謂 不逞鮮人을 製造한 사람은 누구?. 不逞鮮人硏究生은 말하되 一部의 朝鮮人을 不逞鮮人이라 할진대 全部의 日本人은 不逞鮮人을 製出한 元祖가 되지 안흐면 아니되겟고 萬一 어떤 意味의 日鮮親和가 實現되면 朝鮮人은 全部 不逞鮮人이 되고 말리라 한다. 그 理由로는 今日의 所謂 不逞鮮人의 多數는 日本內地 或은 朝鮮에 在하야 日本人의 文化에 同化되어 日本人의 人格에 同化되어 日本人의 典型과 日本人의 氣像을 그대로 模範한 者가 多한 까닭이라 한다. 間島라는 名稱의 出處. 歷史上으로 間島는 古代朝鮮의 一部이요, 文化上으로 間島는 現代朝鮮人의 移植地이다. 그러나 間島라는 言源出處에 對하야 此를 깁히 硏究한 사람이 稀한 듯하다. 間島에 關한 論文으로 博士의 學位를 得한 現平安南道 道知事 篠田法學博士의 說明에 依하면 間島라는 名稱 出處에 對하야는 二說이 有한데 一은 往昔國境附近에 住居하는 朝鮮人이 北으로 進하야 移住地를 求探할 때에 豆滿江以北의 廣濶한 新地를 「墾土」라 始稱하얏는데 此가 「間島」로 音相訛하엿다 하고 또 一說은 咸鏡北道鍾城北部 豆滿江中央에 一小島가 有한데 그 位置가 江의 中央을 占하얏슴으로 此 島를 새로 開拓한 후 稱하여 「間島」라 하얏고, 그 後 此 島를 經由하야 江越便 大陸에 漸進케 된 今日에 在하야서도, 此島以北의 地를 總히 間島라 稱케 되엇다 한다. 何의 說을 물론하고 間島라는 名稱이 最初부터 朝鮮人이 呼出한 것은 分明 無疑하다.＜117＞
