꿈자리 (Dream Spot/Sleeping Place)
						金素月 (Kim So-wŏl)

Oh, my beloved? Everywhere I went, didn't you lay out this place for me? It must be so. I know for certain it must be you. Because everywhere I go, I always live within this place you have spread out, I knew beforehand that it was you.

Oh, my beloved! This place you have laid out is serene and cozy like the clear bottom of a pond. Won't the long path, like a soft sand floor breathing softly (흠싹 흠싹 숨치우는), always guide my lonely and powerless footsteps toward you, my longed-for beloved? But, my beloved! The night will be dark. The cold wind will blow, too. Though the rooster has crowed, the shining dawn will likely not yet come. Oh, my beloved, who gives strength to my body! Please hold me, lonely and powerless, tightly in your arms and let me sleep forever in your trustworthy embrace.

This place you have laid out is lonely and desolate, but when I sleep and play and think only of you within this place, all fear disappears, and all suffering is forgotten.

Then, beloved! I will live in this place for the rest of my life.

Oh, my beloved! You laid out this place everywhere for me, wanting me to linger in this world even one day longer. Taking pity on the traces of my homeless and solitary self, you prepared this humble place everywhere as my possession. And you also firmly held onto the thin thread of my life and let me live my lonely life safely within this place, free from danger.

Oh, then my beloved! Please let me stay within this place until the very end. With your own hands, let me sleep quietly in your strong and trustworthy<57> embrace, and with your own hands, please bury me within this place as well.

깁흔 구멍 (Deep Hole)

Looking back after those times have passed, all the memories that follow might seem meaningless, but it is certainly not so.

Well now, look here! When did you slip into my heart? No matter how I try, I cannot figure it out.

Oh! My lover!

Looking now, I suppose all my diligence, patience, and endurance until now, even the smallest pains, must have all come from a heart (心誠) devoted to you. How could it possibly be worthless?

Oh! My lover!

But I already know all about what mischievous (소삽스럽은) things you were doing while hiding in my heart during that time. Even if it has come to the point where I cannot live even for a moment apart from you in the future, that must be the reason.

There must be hateful people too! What's so good about being happy after drilling such a large, darkish, deep hole in someone else's peaceful heart?

Oh! My lover!

You must know the depth (深淺) of the hole your hands have made. Then, day after day, please blow ceaselessly into my small heart your breath, more precious than the scent of flower dew hidden in a thicket hazy like a spring morning, so that the hole may be filled to the brim without any space left. <58>
<57-58>

**Summary**

These two short prose pieces by Kim So-wŏl express themes of intense longing, devotion, and existential dependence on a beloved figure ("Nim" or "Lover"). In "Dream Spot," the speaker finds solace and safety only in the places prepared by the beloved, pleading for eternal rest in their embrace. In "Deep Hole," the speaker reflects on how the lover has entered their heart, making separation impossible, and describes this love as a deep, perhaps painful, void that only the lover's presence can fill. From a historical perspective, the pieces, written in 1922 during Japanese colonial rule, are interesting for their intense emotional vulnerability and focus on an intimate, almost suffocating dependency. Historians might find the ambiguity of the "beloved" significant—whether a person, a divine entity, or perhaps a veiled symbol for a lost homeland or ideal—reflecting the complex emotions and constrained expressions of the era. The somewhat archaic language and spelling also provide linguistic insights.
