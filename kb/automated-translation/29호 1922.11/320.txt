落葉 (외 3편)
						金石松

Fallen Leaves (and 3 others)
						Kim Sŏksong (金石松)

When I strolled in the garden early in the morning,
A thin, worm-eaten willow leaf
Flew powerlessly and before my feet
Lay helplessly, saying,
"He who is trampled by the kicks of Fate (運命),
Whoever you are, look upon my form!
Life (生) is merely Death's (死) forerunner.
What power does Life (生)—all by itself—have?
Do not rejoice that it is morning now,
Before long (未久), the fearsome night will swallow you,
And eternal death will dance."

Looking down at the pitiful willow leaf,
"You who speak of the philosophy of Life (生) and Death (死),
If you know it is your Fate (運命)
To bloom in spring and fall in autumn, that's enough.
Why do you spitefully say such things
To my heart that stubbornly wants to live?"

Ah! Falling leaves! My settling heart!

Eternal Parting (永遠한 離別)

The old nanny who looked after the child, fearing the cold winter,
Was to return to her home.
It was a morning when frost lay in the roof tiles and the wind's edge was cold. <54>
Her face, wrinkled like a Japanese pumpkin (倭호박),
Was filled with a look of sorrow, tears even welling up.

Seeing her emerge clutching her bundle,
The child, unknowing, said, "Mommy! Carry me..."

She, nearly 70,
Parting (作別) from her two-year-old friend,
Said, "Baby! When it's warm next spring..."
As warm tears flowed, in her eyes,
A graveyard (墓地) must have dimly appeared.

Ah! The sorrow of eternal parting (永遠한 離別)!
What in the world can compare to this?

The Way of People (사람의 常事)

A certain friend (親舊) said to me,
If a person possesses anything for too long,
No matter how precious, they tire of it.
An ideal lover, the child (子息) one bore,
Valuable clothes, a splendid system (制度),
It is the way to tire of everything.

I asked him again,
"Still, isn't the desire for possession (所有慾)
Always constant?
Even a millionaire loves money."

"No. That is not the work of people.
It is the work of the system (制度) they tire of, which enjoys it.
Those who desire a new system (制度),
They even tire of that possessive desire (所有慾) rooted in attachment (愛着)."

Ah! Is this the way of people (常事)?
The natural course of Life (生命)?

White-cheeked Tit (白頰鳥)

In the autumn of my nineteenth year,
When I suffered from love,
Every morning, every morning,
Coming to my window sill and crying <55>
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

Holding a red leaf in your beak,
Seeking my window again,
Did you come to console
Me, suffering like back then?
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

But that sickness back then,
In a fragrant flower bed (꼿밧),
The butterfly's play seeking honey,
You too must have suffered your fill.
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

My heart that burned fiercely,
Only ashes remain starkly,
The wind blowing this way and that,
Will it sweep away even the ashes?
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

Before the young soul (魂) could grow,
It became sick with the poison of livelihood,
On the face like a wild flower (외꼿),
Only the cheekbones are stark.
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

Is the leaf held in your beak
Medicine (藥) to cure my illness?
Saying I am better, my illness,
Even for a moment, quickly, quickly...
White-cheeked tit, affectionate bird!

—The "White-cheeked Tit (白頰鳥)" was written as an experiment, whether to call it folk song style (民謠體) or children's song style (童謠體)—<56>

**Summary**

This collection comprises four poems by Kim Sŏksong. "Fallen Leaves" contemplates mortality and the defiance of life against the inevitability of death. "Eternal Parting" portrays the deep sorrow of a final farewell between an elderly nanny and a young child. "The Way of People" discusses the human tendency to tire of possessions and even systems, driven by a constant desire for novelty. "White-cheeked Tit" expresses longing and suffering, possibly from lost love or hardship, using the recurring image of a bird from the past, written experimentally in a folk or children's song style. From a historical perspective, these poems, written in 1922 during the Japanese colonial period, reflect common themes of melancholy, contemplation of life's hardships, and the passage of time found in literature of that era. The mention of "livelihood poisoning" (살림中毒 病) in "White-cheeked Tit" might subtly hint at the economic or social pressures of the time, while the philosophical musings and focus on personal sorrow offer insight into individual sentiments amidst broader national circumstances.

落葉 (외 3편)
						金石松
아츰에 일즉이 뜰에 거니를 적에
얇고 버레먹은 버들입 하나가
힘업시 날리어 내발압헤
풀업시 업드리며, 하는 말이
運命의 발길에 짓밟히는 者는
누구든지 나의 꼴을 보라!
生은 死의 압잡이 뿐이오.
生- 저 혼자서야 무슨 힘이 잇스랴.
지금이 아츰이라고 너의는 질기지말아
未久에 무서운 밤은 너의를 삼키고
永遠한 죽엄이 춤을 추리라.

가련한 버들입을 나는 나려다보며
生과 死의 哲學을 말하는 그대여
봄에 피이고 가을에 지는 것이
그대의 運命인줄 알면 고만이지
억지로 살겟다는 나의 가슴에
심술굿게 그런 말은 웨하는가.

아! 떨어지는 나무닙! 나려안는 나의 가슴!

永遠한 離別

아이보든 한멈이 치운 겨울을 두려워하야
저의 집으로 돌아가게 된 것은
기와고랑에 서리치고 바람끗차든 어느 아츰이다. ＜54＞
倭호박가티 줄음잡힌 그의 얼굴에
섭섭한 빗이 가득하야 눈물조차 그렁그렁하며

보퉁이를 끼고 나오는 꼴을 보고
아이는 철업시 「엄마! 업어...」

70에 갓가운 그가
두살된 어린 친구를 作別하고
「아가! 來年 봄에 따뜻하면...」하며
더운 눈물 흘리는 그의 눈에는
어렴풋이 墓地가 보엿슬 것이다.

아! 永遠한 離別의 슬픔!
世上에 무엇이 이에 짝하랴.

사람의 常事

어느 親舊가 내게 말하기를
사람은 무엇이나 오래가지면
아모리 보배라도 실혀진다.
理想의 愛人도, 제가 나흔 子息도,
갑진 옷이고, 훌륭한 制度이고,
모든 것이 다 실혀지는 법이다.

내가 다시 그에게 뭇기를
그래도 所有慾이라는 것은
언제든지 한결가티 잇지안나
百萬長者라도 돈은 조하하니

아니다. 그것은 사람의 짓이 아니다.
실혀하는 制度가 질겨하는 짓이오.
새로운 制度를 願하는 그들은
그 愛着만흔 所有慾까지 실혀한다.

아! 이것이 사람의 常事요.
生命의 當然한 進路인가.

白頰鳥

열아홉살 가을철에
사랑으로 알흘적에
아츰마다 아츰마다
내窓머리 와서울든 ＜55＞
白頰鳥야 情든 새야

붉은닙을 입에물고
나의窓을 다시차저
그때가티 알는나를
慰勞코저 네가왓나
白頰鳥야 情든새야

그러치만 그때病은
香氣나는 꼿밧속에
꿀을찻든 나비작난
너도실컷 알앗스리
白頰鳥야 情든새야

싸게타든 나의가슴
재만앙상 남아잇고
갈팡질팡 부는바람
재나마도 쓸어가랴
白頰鳥야 情든새야

어린魂은 크기전에
살림中毒 病이되고
외꼿가튼 얼굴에는
광대뻬만 앙상하다
白頰鳥야 情든새야

너의입에 물은닙이
나의병을 고칠藥가
나허다고 나의병을
一時라도 어셔어서
白頰鳥야 情든새야
