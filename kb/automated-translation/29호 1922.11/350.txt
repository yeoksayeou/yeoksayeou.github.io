기사형태	사고·편집후기 (Notice / Editorial Postscript)

編輯局의 이야기

今 11月號에는 10月號에 豫告한 그 方針に 依하야 時事에 關한 問題를 主로 編入하게 되엇다. 그러나 이번 號 뿐에는 時事 及 實事에 關한 極大體의 意識을 認得케 하기에 努力하엿다.

그런데 이 때에 잇서 우리가 確信잇게 생각하는 것은 우리가 時事를 쓴다 하야 日刊의 新聞紙나 또는 週刊의 雜誌 等과 性質을 混同캐할 것이 아니요. 月刊雜誌로의 鄭重한 特性을 固守하야 이것저것을 닥치는대로 報道하는 一種의 雜書가 되지 아니케 하고 最近 一個月間에 起한 內外의 諸問題이며 이에 伴하는 一般 社會의 思想 及 傾向을 充分히 看取, 考究, 批評, 紹介하야 讀者 一般이 本誌를 讀함으로 因하야 實世間의 現狀 及 傾向에 對한 系統的 知識을 어더 무엇을 考究하는 데에나 또는 무엇을 續行創始하는 데에 對한 唯一한 師友가 되게 하기를 期하는 바이오니 本誌에 特色의 一이 여긔에 잇다 하야도 過言이 아닐 것입니다.

今號에 發表 施行하게 된 月 1回의 時局講座와 社友制度의 設定은 이 眞實로 朝鮮民族의 精神上 物質上 明日作戰에 對한 最善의 準備를 急히 하려는 心慮에서 流露된 計劃인바, 決코 생각잇는 讀者 同志의 歡迎을 博할 것을 信하오며 아울러 그리함이 잇기를 期望하는 바입니다.

最後로 一言할 것은 本社는 每樣 內外兄弟의 玉稿를 辱受하는 惠顧를 입는바 一一히 答謝치 못함을 恐悚하여 하는 바이오며 다 못한 말삼으로써 請할 것은 그 記事의 內容에 잇서서는 그 계신 곳의 特殊한 社會問題 或은 現像에 對한 考究, 視察, 紹介, 論評이거나 그러치 아니하면 自己가 專門으로 硏究하는 特殊問題에 對한 意思發表에 限하게 하여 주시되 한번 주신 原稿의 處分に 對하여는 다시 그 뒤를 믓지 아니할 覺悟를 가저주십시요. 이러치 아니하시면 彼此間에 때로 섭섭하게 되는 일도 잇는 까닭입니다.

本號에 連載할 重要都市의 巡禮記는 本社에 今後 計劃과 相須하야 반다시 어떠한 形式으로써 나타날 것이며 오래동안 連載하야 讀者의 最後까지의 忍耐에 訴하는 文藝部의 諸繼續品은 來月號卽年末號로써 大體로 끗을 막게 되겟습니다.＜68＞

Story from the Editorial Office

In this November issue, according to the policy announced in the October issue, we have mainly included matters concerning current affairs (時事). However, in this issue particularly, we have strived to enable the recognition of the general consciousness regarding current affairs and practical matters.

However, what we confidently think at this time is that just because we write about current affairs, we should not confuse our nature with that of daily newspapers or weekly magazines. We must adhere firmly to the serious characteristics of a monthly magazine, ensuring it does not become a kind of miscellany (雜書) that reports haphazardly on this and that. Instead, by sufficiently observing, studying, criticizing, and introducing the various internal and external issues that have arisen during the recent one-month period, along with the accompanying thoughts and trends of general society, we expect that general readers, by reading this magazine (本誌), will gain systematic knowledge of the actual world's current situation and trends, thereby making it a unique teacher and friend (師友) for contemplating anything or for continuing or initiating anything. It would not be an exaggeration to say that one of the special characteristics of this magazine lies herein.

The establishment of the monthly Current Affairs Lecture (時局講座) and the Supporter System (社友制度), announced and implemented in this issue, are plans truly stemming from the concern to urgently make the best preparations for the Korean nation's (朝鮮民族) spiritual and material 'operation for tomorrow' (明日作戰). We believe they will surely receive a broad welcome from thoughtful reader-comrades (讀者 同志), and we hope it will be so.

Lastly, one word is that our company (本社) receives the favor of precious manuscripts (玉稿) from internal and external brethren (內外兄弟) regularly, and we are apprehensive that we cannot thank each one individually. As a request through unfinished words, regarding the content of articles, please limit them to studies, observations, introductions, or critiques of special social problems or phenomena in your respective locations, or otherwise, to expressions of opinion on special issues you are professionally researching. However, please be prepared not to inquire again about the disposition of a manuscript once submitted. Otherwise, there may sometimes be regrettable matters between us (彼此間).

The Pilgrimage Record of Important Cities (重要都市의 巡禮記) to be serialized in this issue will surely appear in some form, in conjunction with the future plans of our company. The various continuing works in the Literary Section (文藝部), which have been serialized for a long time appealing to the readers' utmost patience, will largely conclude with next month's issue, the year-end issue (年末號).<68>

**Summary**

This editorial from the November 1922 issue of *Kaebyŏk* outlines the magazine's shift towards focusing on current affairs, distinguishing its role from daily newspapers by providing deeper analysis and systematic knowledge of recent events, societal thoughts, and trends over the past month. It aims to be a "teacher and friend" guiding readers in understanding the world and taking action. The editors announce a new monthly "Current Affairs Lecture" and a "Supporter System" as initiatives to prepare the Korean nation spiritually and materially for the future ("operation for tomorrow"). They also provide guidelines for contributors, requesting focused articles on specific social issues or specialized research, and asking contributors not to inquire about submitted manuscripts' status to avoid potential disappointments. Finally, it mentions a planned series on "Pilgrimage Record of Important Cities" and the conclusion of long-running literary serials in the upcoming year-end issue. From a historical perspective, the editorial reveals the magazine's ambition to actively shape Korean public consciousness during a period of colonial rule, framing its journalistic and organizational activities (like the lecture series) as preparations for national advancement. The explicit mention of the "Korean nation" (朝鮮民族) and the somewhat militaristic phrase "operation for tomorrow" (明日作戰) hint at underlying nationalist sentiments and a desire for collective progress, while the pragmatic instructions to contributors shed light on the practical challenges of periodical publishing at the time.
