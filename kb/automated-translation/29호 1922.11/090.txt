발행일	1922년 11월 01일
기사제목	「開闢」之動中靜觀 (A Quiet View Amidst the Movements of "Kaebyŏk")
필자	一記者 (One Reporter)
기사형태	소식 (News/Reports)

「開闢」之動中靜觀
						一記者
又復土地開懇會社 (The Land Reclamation Company Again)

The issue of the Chosŏn Land Improvement Company (朝鮮土地改良會社), which has already been proposed several times and rejected several times, is being submitted again to the current assembly, and this time it appears certain to be established. This means the Government-General authorities have become the manipulators, inducing capitalists from Japan and within Chosŏn to establish it with a capital of 20 million yen, of which the Government-General will undertake 10 million yen, having already earmarked 2.5 million yen (one-fourth) in next year's budget. Whether this company will be established or not will be known after some time passes, but if it is realized, is its purpose truly to feed the Korean people, or is it a scheme to supply food for the Japanese? Or perhaps it is a plan to give Koreans the leftover grains (糟糠) and Japanese the polished rice (精米)? We must wait and see, but it would be fortunate if the last speculation does not prove to be the most likely.

流行の調査と裏面 (Investigation of Trends and the Inside Story)

According to a telegram from Tokyo, the Japanese government, based on the proposal of the Director-General of the Colonial Bureau (拓殖局長官), has ordered renowned religious scholars to conduct a fundamental investigation regarding Chosŏn. It also states that the reason many Koreans today lack a nationalistic concept is the result of Confucianism (儒敎), Chosŏn's traditional central religion, not respecting the ideology of loyalty to the monarch and patriotism in its morality. Therefore, the investigation of Chosŏn's religions is deemed the most necessary task for the future improvement and progress of Chosŏn's governance, and the current cabinet will conduct a fundamental investigation of Chosŏn's religions in the future. Thinking about this, whether the investigation will cover all of Chosŏn or be limited only to religious issues, its scope is still unclear, but it seems certain that an investigation will be conducted. From this, we can infer that (1) it is true that the central government or the Chosŏn Government-General intends to adopt a more thorough policy regarding the Chosŏn issue than before, and (2) particularly by discussing the fundamental investigation of religious issues, perhaps they are planning a strategy to instill (吹廳) the belief in Japanese Shinto (日本神道) among Koreans, aiming to fundamentally permeate (淶洽) the Korean mindset with Japan's Imperial Way (皇道). In any case, it is advisable to watch future actions closely. <42>

總督府にも調査課 (A Research Division in the Government-General Too)

With the revision of the regulations for the division of duties among Chosŏn Government-General officials dated the 13th, the establishment of a new Research Division in the Government-General was announced. The affairs of this division are:
1. Matters concerning the investigation and supervision of statistics.
2. Matters concerning the investigation and introduction of internal and external situations.
3. Matters concerning various reports and printed materials.
Thus, while abolishing the former Statistics Division, its duties are taken over by the Research Division, and special emphasis is placed on matters concerning the investigation and introduction of internal and external situations. Is this also already in accordance with the inner intentions (內意) of the central government? In any case, talk of investigation and such has been a frequently used magic potion by the rulers of Japan and Chosŏn in recent years; it's reassuring to view this merely as another tactic to embellish (文飾) cultural rule.

齋藤總督の辭任如何 (What About Governor-General Saitō's Resignation?)

Along with Governor-General Saitō's recent trip to Japan, rumors spread about his promotion to Fleet Admiral (元師) and his resignation as Governor-General. Saitō himself and other related authorities absolutely denied this. Baron Saitō, who served as Minister of the Navy when the current Prime Minister Katō was Vice Minister of the Navy, might feel, looking back from today, a sense of the changing fortunes (一龍一猪 - one dragon, one pig). Even for Baron Saitō, who received the Imperial command (大命), his current position might not be so agreeable. Therefore, before this trip to Japan, Governor-General Saitō was fishing leisurely by the water and strolling idly through various parts of the city, exuding an extremely relaxed mood. Also, at the time of his departure from Kyŏngsŏng, the fact that he made a special personal visit to Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮) suggests, like the common psychology of anyone having made a new resolution, that he was displaying a final leisureliness. Perhaps he will indeed become a successful departee.

高等工業の紛紏と三山氏 (The Dispute at the Higher Industrial School and Mr. Mikami)

The faculty non-confidence strike at Kyŏngsŏng Higher Industrial School (京城 高等工業學校) seems to have reached a temporary resolution as of the 23rd due to mediation by the school's graduates. However, looking at the reasons listed by the striking students for their non-confidence in Principal Mikami, they cite incidents such as inevitable quarrels (喧譁) with the principal when faculty members resign, and a lack of respect towards the Imperial Household (皇室) and Imperial Family (皇族). They also list similar points divided into several clauses, not without a touch of childishness. In any case, judging by these reasons, it can be recognized that Principal Mikami's character is quite arrogant (傲慢), largely ignoring the intentions of faculty and students, managing school affairs dictatorially and high-handedly, and completely lacking communication between students and faculty. Furthermore, although Mr. Mikami explained to a certain newspaper reporter that the alleged assault (歐打) on a teacher was groundless, <43> it is said to be true that he has intimidated teachers, even directly pushing them away with his hands. The truth is, reportedly, the faculty distrusts him more than the students do. Being distrusted by both faculty and students, clinging to the principal's chair is a very unpleasant affair. Mr. Mikami's issue too seems to be just a matter of saving face and a matter of time.

培花女學校も 不穩乎 (Is P’aehwa Women's School Also Unstable?)

Is distrust of teachers and student strikes becoming a trend again? Prior to Kyŏngsŏng Higher Industrial School, there was the strike at the same industrial school [likely referring to an earlier incident or another school], and around the same time, there was a magnificent student resistance at the nearby Chinjŏngnyŏ School (貞信女學校). Now, has that spark flown westward? There are frequent rumors of discussions among students at Kyŏngsŏng P’aehwa Women's School (京城 培花女學校) plotting the ousting (退校) of both male and female teachers at the school, along with anonymous letters regarding this. The writer also has investigated this matter but refrains from revealing it prematurely out of respect for the school's honor. However, those in authority at the school should pay attention beforehand (未然). Furthermore, a general word to the authorities of schools in Kyŏngsŏng and the provinces: as a reaction to the severe student strikes of a while ago, there is a sense that the faculty's control (團束) over students has become too excessive, unaccompanied by a corresponding display of sincerity towards the students. It is said that this is one thing the faculty side should reflect on first.

勞働共濟會の 解軆と 그 影響 (The Dissolution of the Labor Mutual Aid Society and Its Impact)

The Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Society (朝鮮勞動共濟會) has been dissolved (解軆), and a Labor Alliance (勞働同盟), comprising actual labor organizations from Kyŏngsŏng and the provinces as its members, has been newly organized, promulgating its platform (綱領) and declaration. With this, the issue of the Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Society, which had fought and fought for years with insults (辱說), knives (食刀 - possibly literal or figurative violence), and iron fists (鐵拳), and issued two statements, has reached a conclusion for now. Whether the fight will reappear in another organization or form is unknown, but under the name of the Labor Mutual Aid Society, it is now hopeless. Of course, those who fight must have had circumstances and reasons compelling them to do so. However, from the perspective of an outside third party, this event allows us to infer that (1) in today's society, the gentleman class (紳士級) or the "new gentleman class" (新士級 - those who think clenching fists, wearing short jackets, and shouting loudly makes them seem like manual laborers) who talk about social improvement or reform were either merely immersed in the theory, i.e., the conceptual life, of labor issues, or else preoccupied with discussing other problems, lacking any practical effort worthy of gaining the trust of the actual working class; (2) the general populace—especially those companions engrossed (汨沒) in daily toil (努務)—has already attained a certain degree of self-awareness (自覺) and no longer relies solely on the words (口吻) of some theorist or propagandist; (3) this issue <44> will soon spread to people in the provinces, and henceforth, provincial people will place much less trust in the actions of those factions in Kyŏngsŏng who were saying this or that. Generally, social movements progress in this manner, and the public's social consciousness becomes clearer through such processes.

一種の 示威行列 (A Kind of Demonstration Procession)

Despite the unprecedented recession in Kyŏngsŏng due to various rumors of price drops, consumption cutbacks, and financial tightening, the Kyŏngsŏng Shrine Festival (京城神社大祭 - obviously an annual festival for the Japanese side) held on the 16th and 17th showed greater splendor than last year. Processions of eaves lanterns (軒燈), portable lanterns (行燈), floats (山車), and stalls (屋臺) densely passed through Chongno Street, the Korean commercial district. It is a noteworthy phenomenon that, apart from the main participants in the procession, the people pulling ropes and singing were almost all Koreans dressed in Japanese shop attire. Also unusual is that this year, unlike before, they paraded through the Korean district with a particularly grand procession, overwhelming (壓過) Chongno. It wouldn't be wrong to see this as a kind of demonstration by the Japanese populace in Kyŏngsŏng. When we see such processions, we keenly feel that movement itself is power, and that national vitality (元氣) should be stimulated by frequent popular movements. However, looking at our Korean side, what is the situation? Are we not the ones who cannot even enjoy the New Year's holiday (換歲) properly due to the competition between the solar and lunar calendars (陽陰曆)? This is something to reflect upon repeatedly.

天下に 焉有如此慘事 (How Could Such a Tragic Event Exist in the World?)

Major Hara (原少佐), the victim of the infamous Vladivostok weapons disposal issue, was referred (迴送) to a court-martial, received the expected sentence of one and a half years in prison with suspension of execution (執行猶豫), and became temporarily free. However, his beloved wife (艾妻) Chiyoko (千代子), after hearing the news about her husband, harbored eternal resentment (怨恨) and buried herself in the midst of the Hyŏnhae Strait (玄海灘 - Genkai Sea). Major Hara's message from prison, "Don't worry about me at all, just live peacefully," had no one left to hear it. Hara, upon first hearing this news while incarcerated, lamented, "Chiyoko, my beloved wife whom I longed for day and night—why couldn't you wait until my trial was over?" Even he, prepared for anything, couldn't eat or sleep. Now released from prison and free, he plans to go with his wife's younger brother and older brother to a certain point in the Hyŏnhae Strait where his beloved wife threw herself in, intending to search for her body (屍體) for a week. Ah, how pitiful even just hearing about it! How sad. What sin did Chiyoko commit, and what sin did Major Hara commit? In this day and age where militarism (軍國主義) and the principle of armed security (武裝保安主義) still prevail, how could the world's <45> sorrowful people be limited to just this Major and his wife? Alas! It is something a truly human heart cannot do, nor news it can bear to hear.

鍾路市形の一變 (A Change in the Appearance of Chongno Market)

The main street of Chongno (鍾路) has quickly been neatly reconstructed, but most of the shops on the south side of the street have suddenly fallen into a state of disarray (鼠竄鱉入). Amidst this, from barely visible shops sunk far below the roadside embankment, the clanging sound of auction (競賣) bells, like a funeral dirge (吊聲) for Kyŏngsŏng's bankruptcy, is unbearable to hear. Those buildings must be repaired somehow; it's agonizingly impossible otherwise.

八千萬人の血液 (The Blood of Eighty Million People)

A scholar named "Chesson" (?) says, "Each individual's blood (血液) retains the blood of at least twenty million men and women over a thousand years." Needless to say about the prehistoric era, even calculating based on our history of four thousand years since the beginning of recorded history, each individual among us retains the blood of eighty million men and women of our nation. The meaning of compatriots and siblings likely originates and is established here.
However, the world has too many useless schemers (弄策者), which is the problem. Despite this body possessing the blood of eighty million people, they seek separate blood ties (血緣), establishing lineage record offices (譜所) for each surname and engaging in meddling (挾雜). Perhaps due to the sudden money shortage (錢荒), the lineage record office movement has recently revived. Yi clan (李氏) and Pak clan (朴氏) lineage offices have arisen; the former has installed Prince Yi Kang (李堈公 殿下) as its president, and the latter has installed Mr. Pak Yŏng-hyo (朴泳孝氏), intending to command the entire Yi and Pak clans. Alas! Are they truly seeking blood, or are they perhaps seeking gold (金)?

阿片君, 烟竹君, 鐵拳君 (Mr. Opium, Mr. Pipe, Mr. Iron Fist)

Though perhaps biased, a certain Japanese person evaluated the people of the three East Asian countries like this. He said, "If you think of the East as one village, there is Mr. Opium (阿片君), who, despite having an extremely vast garden, never lays a hand on it, but idly lies down in a spacious room, smokes opium (阿片), gambles (賭博), and makes a living by getting into debt (借金取得). Next door is Mr. Pipe (烟竹君), living in a dilapidated house with crumbling walls and rooms, without even provisions (柴糧) for the day, listlessly holding a long tobacco pipe (長大の 烟竹) despite his anemic and weakened appearance. And next door to him is Mr. Iron Fist (鐵拳君), in a cramped house where ten people sit squeezed into a three-mat room; these impatient individuals jostle each other here and there, readily brandishing their fists (銖拳を 揮動) at the slightest provocation." He <46> then continued, "If the meaning of Japanese-Korean assimilation (日鮮の 同化) is assimilation into the Japanese personality, it has already been perfectly manifested in one segment. The archetype is the disgruntled Korean (不平 朝鮮人). Why? Because today's disgruntled Koreans have adopted the Japanese personality wholesale, and their spirit is identical to that of the Japanese." Setting aside the rightness or wrongness of the argument, it is presented here as perhaps an issue worth considering.

鮮滿連絡鐵道 (Korea-Manchuria Link Railway)

Recently, due to the rapid increase in Japanese residents centered around Kyŏnghŭng (慶興) and Sŏngjin (城津) in northern Chosŏn, and the development of their industries, there has been discussion since last year about constructing a railway between Wŏnsan (元山) and Hunch'un (琿春). It is said that the government side has also informally agreed to guarantee an annual dividend (配當) of 8% when the company starts operations, and they are now commencing the route survey. Originally, some Japanese circles complained that due to the lack of convenient transportation between northern Chosŏn and northern Manchuria, Chosŏn's wealth flows backward (逆流), concentrating entirely in Osaka, Japan, and even the funds of special banks (特殊銀行) or special companies meant to guide and encourage the development of Korea and Manchuria flow backward to Osaka. Thus, the construction of this railway will likely be realized. Ultimately, if Japanese economic power thus penetrates (侵入) into northern Manchuria, it will surely affect the lives of the Koreans who have retreated (敗退) there. If they suffer another defeat (負敗) there, where will they go next?

直接運動の反響 (The Repercussions of Direct Action)

In the editorial (卷頭言) of a certain issue of this magazine, we emphasized the power of direct action (直接運動), urging (慫慂) that the thoughts buried in our brothers' hearts and the words smeared on their lips all emerge as facts through the method of direct action. Where there's smoke, there's fire. The Labor Mutual Aid Society in Chinju (晋州) already began tackling the tenant farmer (小作人) problem directly through action, and recently, the General Assembly of Free Laborers in Kyŏngsŏng resolved to abolish (毁廢) the Porter Control Office (擔軍取締所), which had long been an institution of double exploitation (二重搾取) for porters (擔軍). The momentum for direct action in this area is growing daily. The emergence of such movements certainly has considerable repercussions. To cite an example, in Chinju, we already see the landowners' association resolving to "protect tenant farmers." The key issue lies in initiating direct action. Theory has already become outdated delirious talk (譫語). How about those who speak of cultural movements and social reform first pouring all their efforts into this area? <47>

**Summary**

This article from the November 1922 issue of *Kaebyŏk* presents a series of news snippets and commentary ("A Quiet View Amidst Movements") by "One Reporter." It covers diverse topics reflecting colonial Chosŏn: Japanese economic projects like the proposed Land Improvement Company and the Wŏnsan-Hunch'un railway, aimed at resource extraction and facilitating Japanese expansion into Manchuria; government surveillance and control attempts, including investigations into Korean society (especially religion, potentially to promote Shinto) and the creation of a Research Division; social issues like student unrest against Japanese school authorities (Kyŏngsŏng Higher Industrial, P’aehwa Women's School), the internal conflicts and reorganization within the labor movement (dissolution of the Labor Mutual Aid Society, rise of the Labor Alliance), and the visible assertion of Japanese presence through events like the Kyŏngsŏng Shrine Festival parade in Korean areas; poignant personal tragedies resulting from Japanese policies (the Hara suicide case); and observations on social trends like lineage societies and the growing call for "direct action" among Koreans, particularly concerning labor and tenant issues.

From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the multifaceted realities of Korea under Japanese rule in the early 1920s, a period often characterized by the so-called "Cultural Rule" policy. It highlights the tension between Japanese assimilationist goals (promoting Shinto, investigating Korean "character") and economic exploitation (land company, railway) versus growing Korean social consciousness and resistance, manifested in student strikes, labor organizing, and calls for direct action against exploitation (e.g., tenant issues, porter exploitation). The reporter's critical, often cynical, tone towards Japanese initiatives and commentary on Korean societal issues (like lineage societies or divisions within movements) provides a contemporary Korean perspective on the complexities and challenges of the era. The mention of specific events like the Hara case, school strikes, and labor reorganizations provides concrete data points for historians studying social movements, colonial policy implementation, and everyday life during this period.

「開闢」之動中靜觀

又復土地開懇會社

벌서 數回를 提出되어 數回의 否決을 當한 例의 朝鮮土地改良會社는 今次의 議會에 다시 提出하는데 이번에는 確實히 成立을 見할 貌樣이라 한다. 이는 即 總督府當局이 이에 對한 操縱者가 되어 日本 及 朝鮮內에 在한 民間의 資本家를 誘致하야 期成하되 資本金 二千萬圓으로 하야 一千萬圓을 總督府가 引受하고 明年度 叡算에 爲先 二百五十萬圓 (四分之一)을 繰上하엿다. 이 會社의 成立與否는 아즉도 얼마의 時日을 지내인 然後에 알 일이나 이것이 만일 實現된다 한면 그 目的이 眞實로 朝鮮 사람에게 살 밥을 먹이기 爲함인가 아니 日本사람의 살 밥을 補給할 策인가. 그러치 아느면 朝鮮 사람에게 糟糠을 주고 日本사람에게 精米를 줄 料量인가. 두고 보아야 알 것이어니와 最後의 推測이 가장 有力한 것이 되지 아느면 幸.

流行의 調査와 裏面

總督府에도 調査課

13日付 朝鮮總督府事務官分掌規程의 改正으로 總督府調査課의 新說을 發布하엿다. 이 課의 事務는
1. 統計의 調査 及 監督에 關한 事項
2. 內外事情의 調査 及 紹介에 關한 事項
3. 各種 報告 及 印刷物에 關한 事項
이라 하야 從來의 統計課를 廢止하는 代에 그 課의 事務를 調査課에서 引行케하고 別로 內外事情의 調査 及 紹介에 關한 事項에 重을 置하게 되엇다. 이것도 벌서 中央政府의 內意에 則함인가. 如何間 調査 云云은 年來의 日本 及 朝鮮의 爲政者를 通하야 頻用되는 妙劑, 이것도 文化政治를 文飾하는 一策이라고만 보면 安心.

勞働共濟會의 解軆와 그 影響

朝鮮勞動共濟會가 解體되고 京城 及 地方의 實際 勞働團體를 그 成員으로 한 勞働同盟이 새로 組織되고 그 綱領과 宣言을 아울러 頒布하엿다. 이로써 해를 두고 辱說로 食刀로 또 鐵拳으로 싸우고 또 싸우며 二次의 聲明書를 發表하든 朝鮮 勞働共濟會의 問題도 爲先 이로써 一段落을 告하엿다. 그 싸움이 또 或 다른 團體에서 다른 形式으로 나타날지 모르나 勞働共濟會의 名義로서는 이제는 絶望이다. 勿論 싸움을 하는 사람은 반듯이 그러치 아니치 못할 境遇와 階梯가 잇는 것을 말할 것이다. 그러나 在外의 第三者로 보아 이번의 그 事實로 因하야 (1) 今日의 社會에 잇서 社會의 改善 或은 革新을 말하는 紳士級 또는 新士級(주먹 부루쥐고 동저고리 입고 목소리만 크게하면 肉體勞働者가 된 樣으로 認함과 如한 그 級)이 勞働問題의 理論 即 觀念生活에 뿐 沈溺하얏거나 不然하면 그보다도 다른 問題의 論議에 熱中하고 實際의 勞働者級의 信任을 바들만한 何等의 實地 努力이 업섯든 것과 (2) 一般의 民衆-特히 나날이 努務에 汨沒하는 그 동무들도 벌서 어느 程度까지의 自覺을 得하야 어떤 理論家나 宣傳家의 口吻 뿐을 依賴하지 아니하게 된 것 (3) 이 問題는＜44＞ 곳 地方의 사람사람에게 傳하야 今後의 地方人은 一層 京城 內에서 이러니 저러니 하던 側의 行動을 만히 信任치 아니할 事 等을 推察할 수가 잇겟다. 大槪 社會의 運動이란 이렁저렁하야 進步되는 것이며 또 民衆의 對社會意識이란 이렁저렁하야 明瞭하게 되는 것이겟지.

一種의 示威行列

物價 落下, 消費節約, 金融緊縮 等 여러가지 喧傳으로 因하야 京城의 市况은 例에 업는 不況임에 不拘하고 16,7日에 擧行된 京城神社大祭(勿論 日本人側의 年中行祭)는 昨年 以上의 盛況을 呈하야 軒燈, 行燈, 山車, 屋臺 等의 行列이 朝鮮人 市街되는 鍾路通을 삑뻑하게 지나갓다. 그 行列의 主要人員을 除한 以外의 줄끌고 노래하는 사람은 거의 日本商店服 입은 朝鮮 사람으로 이뤄진 것이 注意할 現狀이며 그 前에는 朝鮮人 市街를 그러케 通行하지 아니하던 것을 今年부터는 別로 宏盛한 行列을 지며 鍾路를 壓過하는 것이 또한 異常한 일이다. 一種의 在京 日本人民衆示威로 보아도 틀리지는 아니 하겟다. 우리는 이러한 行列을 볼 때에 別로 運動은 勢力인 것을 實感하며 國民의 元氣는 모름즉이 民衆運動의 頻頻으로써 鼓吹될 것을 느끼게 된다. 그러나 우리 朝鮮의 사람便을 돌아보면 어떠한가. 換歲의 名節 한때도 陽陰曆의 競爭으로 因하야 한번 快하게 즐기지 못하는 이 우리가 아닌가 再三 思究할 일이라 한다.

八千萬人의 血液

「체-손」이란 學者는 말하되 「各 個人의 血液 中에 少하야도 一千年의 間에 二千萬人의  男女의 血液을 留存한다.」는 말이다. 有史 以前은 말할 것이 업시 有史 以後의 우리 歷史의 四千年으로써 計算할지라도 우리 個人의 血液 中에는 우리 國族男女 八千人의 血液을 存留할지니 同胞兄弟의 意義도 여긔에서 비롯오 成立될 것이다.
그러나 世上에는 쓸대업는 弄策者가 만하서 탈이다. 이 한사람에게 八千萬人의 血液을 保有하는 이몸에도 不拘하고 거긔에 따로 血緣을 찻는다 하야 姓姓이 譜所를 내어서 한창 挾雜질을 하더니 돈이 갑작히 錢荒함에 인함인지 近來에 또다시 譜所運動이 起하야 李氏譜所, 朴氏譜所가 起하야 前者는 李堈公 殿下를 總裁로 推戴하고 後者는 朴泳孝씨를 推戴하야 李·朴의 全族에 令코저한다. 嗚呼라. 그들이 正말로 血을 찻고저 함인가. 或은 金을 찻고저 함인가?

그럴는지는 모르나 日本人 某씨는 東洋의 三國人을 評하야 이러케 말하엿다. 가르되 「東洋이란 것은 한 村이라 하고 생각하면 非常히 廣大한 庭園을 가지고 잇스면서도 거긔에 손 한번을 대이지 아니하고 넓다란 房안에 그대로 閒臥하야 阿片빨고 賭博만하야 借金取得을 爲業하는 阿片君이 잇고 그 이웃에는 璧도 헐고 房도 떨어진 零落한 집안에서 그날을 지내일 柴糧의 準備도 업시 貧血衰弱의 그 貌樣에 長大의 烟竹을 떨고 잇는 烟竹君이 잇고 또 그 이웃에는 三疊房안에 十人이나 曲坐하게 된 그 狹小한 집에 性急한 그 者돌이 이리저리 어울어저 動輒則銖拳을 揮動하는 鐵拳君이 잇다」 그는＜46＞ 다시 말을 굴리어 「만일 日鮮의 同化라는 의미가 日本人의 人格에 同化라 하는 意味이면 일즉이 一部分에서 完全히 顯現되어잇다. 그 典型은 即 不平 朝鮮人이다. 何故오하면 今日의 不平 朝鮮人은 日本人의 人格을 그대로 取移하야 그 氣象이 日本人의 그것에 同한 故이라」하엿다. 論의 是非는 別問題로 하고 如何間 한번 考究할 問題인가 하야 이에 指示한다.

鮮滿連絡鐵道

近來 北朝鮮의 慶興, 城津 等을 中心한 日本人의 居住者가 激增하고 且其 生産業이 發展됨에 依하야 昨年來로부터 元山 琿春間의 鐵道敷設議가 有하던 中 政府側도 亦是 會社開業의 時는 年 八朱의 配當을 保証하기로 內約이 有하야 이제 線路 調査의 役에 著手한다 한다. 元來 一部의 日本人 間에서는 朝鮮 北滿間에 交通의 便이 不開하야 朝鮮의 富는 逆流하야 모다 日本의 大阪으로 集注되고 鮮滿의 開發을 指導獎勵할 만한 特殊銀行 或은 特殊會社의 行金이나 資金까지도 大阪으로 逆流하게 됨을 不平하던 中인即 此 鐵道의 敷設은 勿論 實現될 可望이 有할 것이다. 結局 그리하야 北滿의 方面으로 日本人의 經濟的 勢力이 侵入하면 그곳에 敗退한 朝鮮人의 生活에도 또 影響이 미츨 터이겟지. 만일 거긔에서 또 한차례 負敗한다한면 그 다음으로 갈 곳은 어대?

直接運動의 反響

우리는 本誌 어느 號의 卷頭言에서 直接運動의 威力을 高調하야 兄弟들의 마음 속에 잠긴 생각과 입술 끄테 발리운 말슴들이 다-가티 直接運動이란 方式을 通하야 事實로써 들어나게 함이 잇기를 慫慂하엿다. 烟氣가 자즈면 불이 일어나는 法이라. 일즉히 晋州의 勞働共濟會에서 事實로 小作人 問題의 直接解決에 着手하엿고 最近 京城의 自由勞働者大會에는 從來 擔軍의 二重搾取機關이던 擔軍取締所의 毁廢를 決議하야 이 方面에 對한 直接運動의 氣勢는 날로 增長됨이 잇다. 이러한 運動의 勃起는 그 反響이 果然 不少하야 一例를 擧하면 晋州에는 벌서 地主會의「小作人, 保護」라는 決議를 보게 되엇다. 요커대 問題는 直接運動의 開始에 잇다. 理論은 벌서 時代에 뒤떨어진 譫語가 되엇다. 文化運動을 말하고 社會革新을 말하는 이는 먼저 이 方面에 全力을 注함이 如何.＜47＞
