Song of Dreams (and 2 others)
						岸曙

Bright sunlight shines on the crow's wings
Blown by the wind, above the drying golden grass (金잔듸),
The hired hand's song, sung by a woodcutter on the empty mountain (山),
Along with the sound of the rain of falling leaves descending without end,
Slants across the wide fields awaiting the autumn harvest (秋收).

Now it is autumn, and in autumn, it is noon (正午),
Ah, ah, my dear, with a low voice, lovely even to hear,
Carefully sing your "Song of Dreams."

Loss (喪失)

In the autumn's
Clear sky,
A single piece of black cloud,
As if something terrible has happened,
Floats, then vanishes,
Vanishes, then floats again.

In the calm sea of the heart,
In the calm very center,
Something of unknown name,
As if something has happened,
So sorrowfully, all alone,
Is making faint ripples.<52>

Autumn

Only autumn,
Like the thought of the departed old love,
Seeps intimately into the heart.

Though now the eyes hold a strange smile,
That face, pale beyond measure,
And those white, sickly, frail fingers,
Even those are all forgotten, and what remains
Is only the sweet thought, impossible to forget.

That thought, impossible to forget,
As if chasing a vanished, finished old dream,
Pours into the complaining heart
The sharp, white light of "regret,"
And the dimly gathering "loneliness."

Only autumn,
Like the old thought of the departed one,
Unforgettably seeps into the heart.
― 1922. 9. 24 ―
At Sinŭiju, Returning Place (於新義州旋地)<53>
<52-53>

**Summary**

This work consists of three poems by An Sŏ (岸曙), all centered around the theme of autumn. "Song of Dreams" sets a scene of autumn noon, urging a loved one to sing. "Loss" uses the imagery of a solitary cloud and ripples on a calm sea to convey a sense of internal disquiet and unnamed sorrow. "Autumn" delves deeper into melancholy, comparing the season's pervasive feeling to the lingering, sweet-sad memory of a deceased loved one, marked by regret and loneliness. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in *Kaebyŏk* during the Japanese colonial period, reflect a common mood of introspection, loss, and melancholy. While personal, the themes of fading memories, enduring sorrow, and loneliness could resonate with the collective feelings of Koreans living under occupation, offering a subtle expression of grief and longing in an era of censorship. The specific mention of Sinŭiju dates the final poem precisely.

꿈의 노래 (외 2편)
						岸曙
밝은 해볏은 말라가는 金잔듸우의
바람에 불리우는 가마귀의 날래에 빗나며,
뷔인 山에서 나무軍이 부르는 머슴노래는
끈힙업시 내리는 落葉의 비소리와 함께
秋收를 기다리는 넓은 들에도 빗겨울어라.

只今은 가을 가을에도 때는 正午,
아, 아 그대여, 듯기조차 곱은 나즌 목소리로,
操心스럽게 그대의 「꿈의 노래」를 부르라.

喪失

가을의
새맑안 한울에
한조각의 검은 구름이
무슨 큰일이나 난듯이
떳다가는 슬어지고
슬어젓다가는 뜨고는 한다.

고요한 맘바다의
고요한 한복판에는
이름 모를 무엇이
무슨 일이나 난듯이
구슬프게도 다만 혼자서
잔물살을 내이며 잇다.＜52＞

가을

그저 가을만은
돌아가신 옛님의 생각처럼
살틀하게 가슴속에 숨여들어라.

只今이야 야릇하게도 웃음을 띈 눈이나
핼금하게 파리한 가이도 업든 그 얼굴과,
하야케도 病的의 연약한 손가락이나마
그나마 다 닛기워지고, 남은 것이란
살틀하게도 닛지못할 달금한 생각뿐.

살틀하게도 못니즐 그 생각만은
업서저 다한 옛 꿈을 쫏는듯이도
날카롭은 「뉘우츰」의 하얀빗과,
어득하게도 모여드는 「외롭음」을
하소연한 맘속에 부어너흘뿐.

그저 가을만은
가신 님의 옛 생각처럼,
못잇게도 가슴속에 숨여들어라.
― 1922. 9. 24 ―
於新義州旋地＜53＞
