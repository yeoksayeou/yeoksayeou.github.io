Views of Various Notables on the Liberation of Korean Women

Women's liberation has been increasingly discussed in recent times. However, the actual liberation of women has not yet been realized. Is there, then, opposition to women's liberation? Not necessarily. Rather, it seems that our general populace still lacks a thorough awareness of this issue.
Should we liberate women? If so, what should be the scope of this liberation, and what steps must be taken to achieve it? Our company now seeks to present the opinions of various notable figures on this matter, urging brothers and sisters to contribute to the resolution and implementation of this issue. <28>

**First, Solving the Education Problem is Urgent**
Chang Ŭngjin (張膺震), Professor, Hwimun High Ordinary School (徽文高等普通學校)

It is truly a matter for endless celebration that the women's issue on our peninsula has recently begun to attract public attention. Originally, since our human society was founded on the union of both sexes, men and women, they should mutually complement each other's strengths and weaknesses, allow each to display their unique talents, and share and carry out labor most suitable to their respective bodies and minds. Only after both sexes achieve perfect harmony can a family or a society see complete development, and consequently, human happiness will increase.
As you know, our Korea (朝鮮) has long been a famous country of male supremacy and female inferiority (男尊女卑). Women were men's playthings and appendages. Women were merely machines for producing children and dolls for preparing clothes and food. Women's personhood was disregarded, and education was not provided. Consequently, it is no exaggeration to say that women's position in the family, and especially in society, was entirely void. Thus, assuming Korea's population is sixteen million, if about half, the women, are not included as citizens possessing independent personhood, isn't this one of the main reasons why Korean culture has declined to its current state? We have managed to live like this until now, but facing the new era to come, with such one-sided (隻輸的) life, no matter how hard men may work, we will not be able to avoid becoming stragglers of civilization. So, what should be done about women in the future? Of course, women must be liberated from men's bondage. They must be freed from the life of confinement within the inner chambers (內房 蟄居生活). They must be educated just like men. At the very least, as human beings, men and women must have equal rights. Like the women of Europe and America (歐米), they should eventually attain suffrage as well.
Often, listening to public commentary, it is said that the so-called modern women (新式 女子), that is, women who have received modern education, upon learning even a tiny bit of knowledge, immediately act as if they have become great heroines (女丈夫), their attitude becomes arrogant. Especially concerning cooking clothes and food, which are most important duties for women, not only do they not know how to do them, but they consider the meticulousness of domestic chores (家庭的 業務) a shame for modern women, mistakenly so. Some even go so far as to advocate for a life of independent self-support and celibacy as if it were an honor. It is said that educating women in this way will ultimately destroy our families and cause our society to decline. Indeed, there is some truth <29> in this, but I believe this is merely a temporary phenomenon arising as a reaction during a transitional period. That is, in a women's society hitherto completely devoid of education, when one first learns some new knowledge, it is an unavoidable consequence of the situation that she considers herself the most outstanding woman compared to other women around her. However, when general education for women spreads and improves, and she sees numerous other women who have acquired more knowledge than herself, her psychology will naturally become humble, and at the same time, it is the inevitable course of reason that she will become aware of what a woman's duties are.
Another point requiring attention here is the severe misunderstanding that equates women's liberation with women disregarding household affairs, neglecting childcare, taking over men's businesses, or performing the same tasks as men to achieve equality. Of course, it seems there are not a few women in Europe and America recently who are passionate about political issues, labor issues, and all sorts of other social problems, and it is said to be a fact that the number engaged in operating trams, policing, postal delivery, and various other forms of physical labor has markedly increased after the Great War. However, this is a special phenomenon caused by the shortage of men during the war, and it is questionable whether this truly brings happiness to women themselves or is necessary for social development. Since our families and society are formed by the union of both sexes, it is natural that men perform labor suitable to their bodies, and women perform labor most suitable to theirs. It goes without saying that managing affairs divisionally will yield greater effects on cultural development. Furthermore, it is an undeniable fact that men, being physically strong with powerful arms, are suited for external work and physical labor, while women, conversely, being weaker in mind and body, are most suited for internal labor. Moreover, women have the prenatal promise of bearing and raising children; the complete fulfillment of this duty by women can be considered a fundamental condition of our human society. As long as the current family system is not destroyed, production and child-rearing are women's exclusive duties. At the same time, for convenience, women should also take charge of preparing clothes and food, manage household affairs, and allow men to be active outside to secure the means of living. There should be absolutely no distinction of high or low status based on the type of work. I believe that only when the two achieve such harmony can we expect a happy life.
What, then, is the essential point of women's liberation? First, men's and women's human rights must be viewed as absolutely equal without discrimination. In other <30> words, the attitude men have held towards women until now must be completely changed, giving them equal status and treatment. If men demand chastity from women, it should be natural for women to demand the same from men. If a man's remarriage is considered natural, a woman's remarriage should also be natural. Women are by no means appendages of men. Women, like men, are human beings of the opposite sex possessing complete personhood. The discriminatory and enslaving view men have held towards women must be abolished. This is just according to humanitarian principles, conforms to the ideals of humankind, and is the natural course of heavenly reason (天理). Regarding the tasks shared by men and women, it is acceptable to engage in the work most suited to their respective physiological and psychological states. I believe that we should not establish a discriminatory view between men and women based on the difference in tasks, such as domestic labor versus external labor, or mental labor versus physical labor. Thus, when equal sexes unite through mutual, equal love, they will form a harmonious family. However, regarding the practical realization of this, in our Korean women's society (and men's society is still like this too), where the level of culture is as immature as it is today, no matter how loudly women's liberation is proclaimed and shouted, it will have not even half a point of effect. Unless men and women first possess the corresponding thought, consciousness, and substance, women's liberation will forever remain merely empty theory and hollow discussion. Therefore, for those who sympathize with the issue of women's liberation and have sincerity, isn't the most urgent task to first strive to solve the problem of women's education?

**Restraining Women is Merely an Artificial Bad Custom**
Yun Ik-sŏn (尹益善), Former Principal of Posŏng College (普成專門學校)

When the Great Ruler, the Great Truth of the Universe (宇宙의 大主宰 大眞理), brought forth all phenomena in the universe, it also brought forth a type of animal called humankind. However, when the Great Ruler, the Great Truth, brought forth humankind, while creating billions of individuals from the same spirit and same matter, it distinguished between male and female as a great natural law for the propagation of humankind, giving differences in their material structure, but in terms of spirit, sensation, movement, and all other functions of life, there is no point of difference whatsoever between man and woman.
Therefore, throughout the human world, those who live lives suitable for human existence – that is, possessing the equal rights granted by the Creator and the equivalent freedom established and protected by the Great Truth, in other words, <31> those peoples who live in accordance with the great natural law of the universe, never stepping beyond or falling short of this law, always conforming to it – have, since primitive times, been free from the man-made bad custom where men restrain women and women submit to men. The status of men and the status of women are on a horizontal line, without a hair's breadth of difference, as we witness in our own time.
Nevertheless, in our Korea (我 朝鮮), although the ancient history is unclear and difficult to discuss, from the middle ages to the present day, disregarding the heavenly grace given by the Great Ruler, the Great Truth – namely, equal rights and equivalent freedom, or in other words, the great law of nature – women have lived for several thousand years without freedom or rights, in a state like servants under men's command. This truly violates the great law of nature and disregards humanitarian justice. Alas! We descendants who have inherited the sacred teachings of Tan'gun (檀君), the great divine man of our Eastern beginnings, under his virtuous influence, teachings of heavenly reason and humaneness based on effortless action (無爲化的 聖訓), how could we abandon these sacred teachings and adopt the principle of male supremacy and female inferiority (男尊女卑主義), which violates natural law and goes against humanitarian justice? Reflecting on this, one cannot help but feel that we have invited the wrath of the Great Ruler and incurred the severe reproof of the Great Truth.
Then, what use is regret that our ancestors lived lives not thoroughly based on the Great Law of the Great Truth, passing this down to the present day? Having greatly awakened to the fact that such inequality between men and women is inconsistent with human life, should we continue this system or abolish it and strive for a sacred life suitable to the laws of nature? If the latter is considered correct, then without a moment's delay, without omitting a single person among the twenty million people, let us awaken to heavenly reason (天理), practice the way of humanity (人道), respect heavenly reason, not disregard the way of humanity. Let women be like men, and men like women, pursuing the same knowledge and the same cultivation, and plan for a sacred life under mutual respectful treatment. This I hope for.

**Liberation is Not an Absolute Urgency**
Yu Pyŏngmin (劉秉敏), Professor, Posŏng High Ordinary School (普成高等普通學校)

My research and experience concerning the world of Korean women are truly shallow, but I will offer a word on this topic.
I sincerely approve of the recent progress in education and activity within the general female community. However, when it comes to the two characters for "liberation" (解放), it is necessary to add research upon research and attention upon attention. This is precisely a matter of degree and family. If complete liberation were to occur now, questions would arise regarding their actual capabilities and duties, needing no further discussion. It would be better to first further <32> spread complete education to women; liberation is not an absolute urgency, I say.

**Today is the Era of Preparation for Liberation**
Talk by Mr. Han Kyu-sŏl (韓圭卨氏), Kang Sŏk (江石)

This issue, in my shallow view, cannot be definitively stated, but it seems appropriate for Korean women to be liberated in accordance with social development. Women in England and America (彼 英米) have even obtained suffrage and participate in the legislature, the highest organ of the state, not to mention recent rumors that one has even become a presidential candidate. However, if you ask whether women should be liberated in current Korean society, it is still a question of degree. Those women in Europe and America (彼歐米 女子) lack not the slightest difference from men in terms of education, insight, or other capabilities. It is under such circumstances that women are liberated there. To say we should liberate women just because others do, without considering the surrounding circumstances and the level of development, seems somewhat misguided. In the current situation, liberation might be worse than non-liberation. If Korean women are to be liberated, women's education must first be promoted as preparation.

**Liberate Within a Suitable Scope**
Talk by Mr. Yu Kŭn (柳瑾氏), Editorial Supervisor, Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報)

Women's liberation is said to be a demand of the times. Even if we try not to liberate women, we cannot avoid it due to modern trends (現代 思潮). However, whether Korean women should be absolutely liberated like those in various European and American countries (彼 歐米 各國), given suffrage, allowed to exercise all rights and fulfill duties, is a separate issue. Their level of knowledge has not yet advanced like theirs, nor do they have the same capabilities. Currently, Korean women lack complete ability and thorough awareness. They are still in a period of cultivation (修養時代). If Korean women, who are in this period of cultivation, are absolutely liberated and made to step out into complex society like men, it seems there will be considerable failure. Therefore, liberating Korean women within a suitable scope, in accordance with the progress of knowledge, seems to be the best policy.

**Bondage is Sin**
Chŏng Tae-hyŏn (鄭大鉉), Principal, Posŏng High Ordinary School (普成高等普通學校)

Regardless of gender, bondage is sin (罪惡), and liberation is truth (眞理). Do not fall into sin, but yearn for the truth. <33>

**Do Not Harm the Unique Virtues of Korean Women**
Yi Kyu-bang (李奎昉), Secretary, Korean Education Association (朝鮮敎育會)

The ideology of closing the country and expelling foreigners (鎖國攘夷) has changed to opening the doors and welcoming foreigners (開門迎洋); the processions where outriders cleared the way (前驅後陪) shouting for people to step aside have changed to two-horse carriages or automobiles; travel that involved riding palanquins or horses, covering a hundred ri (里) a day, exhausting both people and horses, has changed to trains, steamships, or airplanes; urgent messages sent by relay riders (把撥) changing horses and whipping them on (換乘加鞭) have changed to telegraph and telephone. Everything is in an era of change and transformation.
Therefore, who would not agree with or approve of giving freedom and happiness to women, who for long years and deep ages have endured the harshest restraints, knowing nothing of the world's breadth beyond the deep inner quarters (深閨) and narrow courtyards (狹庭), and knowing nothing of learning beyond needlework (針繕) and cooking (炊爨)? Especially when there are countries where women have gained suffrage, how can one oppose mere liberation now?
However, regardless of old customs or new practices, one must discard the shortcomings and adopt the strengths, harmonize what is others' with what is one's own, and then fully digest it to truly achieve the substance of transformation. If one only attends to form and does not cultivate substance, not only will it disgrace the "freedom gained with such effort," but it might even reach the point of harming the "good customs and beautiful virtues of women, which are Korea's distinct characteristic" (朝鮮의 特色인 女子의 良風美德). How can one not be fearful? I pray fervently that the women's world, with a cautious attitude and sound thinking, will reap good results.

**Some Sacrifice is Unavoidable**
Ko Wŏn-hun (高元勳), Principal, Posŏng Law and Commerce School (普成法律商業學校)

Liberation means existence; closure is accompanied by extinction. To manifest the fundamental meaning of things, one must be prepared for some sacrifice; to find eternal truth, one must endure transitional obstacles. I am convinced there will be no negative arguments regarding this question, and merely offer a word in response to those arguing it is premature.

**I Add the Word "However" (但·但)**
Yi Pyŏng-jo (李秉祚), President, Munhŭng Company (文興社長)

Regarding the issue of women's liberation, of course, the marching tune is playing. However, when the two words "Korea" (朝鮮) are appended to "women's liberation," the path of the pen heads towards myriad valleys and thousand peaks. <34>
How should Korean women be liberated? What will happen after liberation? In these end times – in this chaotic society... won't it just reduce the population like in France (佛蘭西)? Won't theories destroying chastity (貞操 破壞論) arise under the guise of free love (自由 戀愛) or sacred spiritual love? All sorts of anxieties pour forth.
However, the opposite of liberation is bondage (束縛). All that constitutes bondage, which makes our society shed tears like beads, the illogical contradiction where the destructive power lies not in others but potentially within ourselves (我) – this must be burned away quickly.
But, if we compare women's liberation to a tree, what I advocate for liberating is neither the masculinized woman, like a utility pole (電線木), nor the woman who only serves as a guard, a sewing machine frame, or a production device (生産器) in the home, like a potted plant (盆에 栽培한 花草). I only cry out for liberation that allows the natural tree – the womanly endowed individuality – to flourish.
Yet, I add the word "however" (但) again to attach conditions. Knowing firmly that women must be liberated and preparing firmly for liberation, each family should begin the liberation. First, men must stop regarding women as subordinate appendages. They must cast off rotten morals and old empty formalities, abolish the accursed rules of separation between inner and outer spheres (內外法), and liberate education. Then, when women become self-aware and blow the trumpet for democracy (떼모크라시), men must beat the drums in accompaniment. This, I am confident, is how to embark on the path of liberation. I don't know what others think...

**First, Respect Women's Personhood**
Ch'oe Tu-sŏn (崔斗善), Principal, Chungang School (中央學校)

One day, a reporter from the magazine "Kaebyŏk" (開闢) visited me to ask for my opinion regarding the liberation of Korean women. As I have not yet sufficiently studied this issue and consequently do not hold a thoroughly settled view, I have nothing opinion-worthy to say. However, I wish to speak a fragment of my thoughts on the issue as they come to me on the spot.
Whether it is the liberation of women, the liberation of slaves, or any liberation, in a word, liberating a person means recognizing and respecting their personhood (人格). Apart from this recognition and respect for personhood, liberation has no meaning. Now, considering the liberation of women, its spirit and meaning lie in respecting women's personhood; it is precisely the liberation of personhood. It means correcting the previous contempt for women's personhood <35> and acknowledging and respecting their personhood as no different from men's. Because their personhood was not respected, they were not given the same education as men, not accorded equal treatment, and not granted appropriate freedom. Therefore, true liberation lies in respecting their personhood, and all movements for liberation must necessarily be based on this respect for personhood. Apart from this respect for personhood, the liberation of women has no meaning. Since the principle is already thus, the practical methods of implementing it should also follow this purport. To enable the cultivation of their personhood, education no less than that given to men must be provided. To aid the expression of their personhood, opportunities and treatment equal to men's must be given; otherwise, it will not do. However, one thing that cannot be forgotten on the side is the necessity for women's own self-awareness and reflection. No matter how much social systems and institutions respect women's personhood and provide appropriate education and treatment, if women's own cultivation is insufficient and they do not possess the personhood worthy of receiving appropriate treatment, it will ultimately be no different from not providing appropriate education and treatment. 諸 名士의 朝鮮 女子解放觀
女子解放은 近日을 期하야 一層 盛傳합니다. 그러나 事實上의 女子解放은 아즉것 그러케 實現을 見치 못하나이다. 그러면 女子解放에 異議를 揷하는가? 반듯이 그런 것도 안이외다. 말하자면 우리 一般은 아즉까지 이 問題에 對하야 徹底한 自覺이 업는 듯 합니다.
우리는 女子를 解放하여야 可한가? 可하다 하면 그 解放의 範圍는 如何하며 又 此를 實現함에는 如何한 階段을 踐하여야 할가. 本社는 이제 此에 對한 諸 名士의 意見을 叩揭하야 兄弟姊妹와 더불어 本 問題의 解決 及 實行을 促코저 하나이다.＜28＞

먼저 敎育問題를 解決함이 急務
徽文高等普通學校敎授 張膺震

우리 半島의 女子問題가 近來 漸次 世人의 注意를 喚起하게 됨은 實로 慶賀不已할 者입니다. 元來 우리의 人類社會가 男女 兩性의 結合으로써 그 基礎를 作한 것인즉 長短을 彼此에 서로 補充하며 特長은 이것을 各히 發揮케 하고 各自 身體와 精神에 가장 適合한 勞働을 分擔 遂行하야 兩性이 完全한 調和를 得한 然後에 一家庭이나 一社會가 비롯오 完全한 發達을 見할 것이오 人類의 幸福은 딸아서 增進하야질 것입니다.
우리 朝鮮은 아시는 바와가티 自來로 有名한 男尊女卑의 國이엇습니다. 女子는 男子의 玩弄物이오 附屬物이엇습니다. 女子는 單只 兒孩를 生産하는 機械요 衣服 飮食을 調理하는 人形이엇습니다. 女子에는 人格을 無視하엿고 敎育을 施치 아니 하엿습니다. 딸아서 家庭이나 特히 社會上에 女子의 地位라는 것은 全혀 空이라고 하야도 過言이 아니엇지오. 그리하야 全朝鮮의 人口를 一千 六百萬으로 假定하면 其中 約 半數되는 女子는 이것을 獨立의 人格을 有한 國民에 編入치 안앗슨즉 이것이 朝鮮의 文化가 今日과 가티 頹敗된 原因 中의 主要한 一이 아니될가 합니다. 그러케나마 至今까지는 살아 왓습니다만은 今後로 오는 新時代에 處하야서는 이와 가튼 隻輸的 生活로는 男子가 아무리 勤勞한다 할지라도 文明의 落伍者 됨을 免할 수 업슬 것입니다. 그러면 今後의 女子는 엇더케 하여야 可하겟습닛가. 勿論 女子를 男子의 束縛에서 解放하여야지오. 內房 蟄居生活에서 開放을 하여야지오. 男子와 同樣으로 敎育을 施하여야지오. 적어도 사람으로서는 男女가 同等의 權을 가지게 되어야지오. 歐米의 婦人들과 가티 將來에는 參政權도 엇도록 되어야지오.
往往 世人의 評論하는 바를 들으면 近來 所謂 新式 女子 即 新敎育을 바닷다는 女子를 보면 如干 쥐꼬리마큼의 學問을 배우고 보면 곳 女丈夫가 된 듯이 態度가 驕慢하야지고 特히 女子의 職分으로 가장 重要한 衣服 飮食 等의 調理와 가튼 것은 이것을 知치 못할 뿐만 아니라 家庭的 業務에 齷齪함을 들우여 新女子의 羞耻로 誤解하고 甚하면 一平生을 自立自營으로 獨身生活을 한다고 이것을 名譽와 가티 主唱하는 女子도 往往 不無하니 이와 가티 敎育하면 畢竟 女子敎育은 우리의 家庭을 破滅하며 우리의 社會를 衰退케 하고 말 것이라 합니다. 이것도 果然 一理가 업지는＜29＞ 안습니다만은 그러나 이것은 過渡時代의 反動으로 일어나는 一時的 現象에 不過한 것인 줄 생각합니다. 卽 至今까지 敎育이 全無한 女子社會中에서 自己가 처음으로 若干의 新學問을 배우고 본 즉 周圍의 他 女子에 比較하야 自己가 가장 傑出한 女子인 줄로 自認할 것은 事勢의 不得已한 것이지오. 그러나 一般 女子의 敎育이 普及向上하야 自己보다 以上의 學問을 修得한 他女子가 無數히 多함을 볼 때에는 그 心理가 自然히 謙遜하야지는 同時에 女子의 職分이 무엇인지를 自覺하게 될 것은 必然의 理勢라고 생각합니다.
