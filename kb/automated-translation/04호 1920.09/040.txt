Critiquing Traditional Filial Piety and Declaring the Future Parent-Child Relationship
						Myohyangsanin

We are those engrossed in establishing a new aspect in both material and spiritual matters. In doing so, we cannot leisurely determine the order but must simply tackle and implement things as they come, as we think of them. The humble intention in choosing the above issue now, whether it succeeds or not, and wanting to cry out, lies solely in this: that this issue of the value of filial piety rises to the public's lips, and the parent-child relationship is renewed even one day sooner.

What is Filial Piety (Hyodo)?
What is filial piety? Considering the teachings of the various elders like Confucius, Zengzi, Mencius, Zhu Xi, etc., who are the main proponents of ethics and morality:
A filial son is one who skillfully carries on the will of others and skillfully describes the affairs of others.
The body, hair, and skin are received from one's parents; daring not to damage them <17> is the beginning of filial piety. Establishing oneself and raising one's name to bring glory to one's parents is the end of filial piety.
Parents gave birth to one completely; only if the child returns completely can it be called filial piety.
A gentleman leaves nothing unrevered, but revering the self is the greatest. The self is a branch of the parents; how could one dare not revere it?
A filial son serves his parents: in daily life, he shows utmost respect; when they are ill, he shows utmost concern; in supporting them, he brings them utmost joy; in funeral matters, he shows utmost grief.
What the parents revere, the child also reveres; what the parents love, the child also loves. This should be so even down to dogs and horses.
There should be no deviation in any matter. Also, if the child transfers the sentiment with which parents worry about their child's illness to their parents, it can be called filial piety.
If parents are alive, one should not travel far; if one travels, one must have a definite destination.
Only if, for three years, one does not change from the way of the father can it be called filial piety. If parents have any faults, one should remonstrate with a harmonious air, pleasant expression, and gentle voice; if they absolutely do not listen, one should weep and follow them.
Even if a son thinks his wife is extremely suitable, if his parents are not pleased, he should discard his wife. Even if a son recognizes his wife as unsuitable, if the parents say she is acceptable, he cannot distance himself from his wife.
Nourishing the parents' will rather than their mouths and bodies (口軆) can be said to understand filial piety.

If one were to collect all records concerning filial piety from ancient sages and worthies, there would truly be no limit, but even just looking at what is excerpted above, one can sufficiently infer what the nature of filial piety is and how heavily they emphasized it.
According to the above, filial piety refers to the conduct and sincerity with which a child best cares for the parents' mind/will (心志) and mouth/body (口軆). It is not just ordinary care of the mind/will, but the *best* care that constitutes filial piety. Therefore, even if the parents' mind/will or the actions arising from that mind/will violate social morality, there is no choice but to weep and follow. This applies not only during their lifetime but even after death, for three years one must not change from their ways. Furthermore, what the parents love and revere, the child must necessarily love and revere; what the parents desire, the child must necessarily provide; what the parents enjoy, the child must necessarily <18> enjoy. In a word, one who is a child cannot have their own mind/will apart from that of the parents; if even slightly one has something separate from the parents' mind/will, this is not good nourishment of the parents' mind/will and at the same time cannot be called filial piety.
Simultaneously with nourishing the mind/will thus, one must also nourish their mouth/body in the best way for it to be filial piety. Entering the kitchen to prepare delicacies, going to their hall to inquire about their well-being, fanning their pillow in summer, and warming their bedding with one's body in winter are matters of course. Regardless of any circumstances concerning oneself or other family members, one cannot but first seek firewood and grain for the parents, one cannot but prepare their living quarters. If unfortunately the parents pass away, one must reach the extreme of grief in the funeral and ancestral rites, offering various delicacies to pursue their memory from afar. In short, one who is a child cannot have their own mouth/body apart from the parents' mouth/body; if even for a moment one recognizes having a mouth/body separate from the parents' mouth/body, this is not good nourishment of the parents' mouth/body and at the same time cannot be called filial piety.
Only by nourishing the parents' mind/will and mouth/body as above can it first be called filial piety, but if, due to unavoidable circumstances and situations beyond one's control, one is in fact unable to nourish the parents well, if one merely possessed the utmost sincerity to nourish them well, they were still recognized as a filial child.

The Position of Filial Piety in Traditional Morality
In traditional morality, filial piety was made the root of all conduct and the source of ethics and morality. Seeing the phrase "Words must praise loyalty and filial piety (忠孝)," it seems loyalty is placed first among the two paths of loyalty and filial piety, but this is merely for linguistic convenience. In reality, filial piety was considered the center of all virtuous conduct. Seeing phrases like "Because serving parents (事親) is filial piety, loyalty can be transferred to the sovereign (君)" and "Of the categories under the five punishments (五刑), there are three thousand, but no crime is greater than unfilial piety," one can glimpse the situation on that side. Also, examining the Confucian classics, there are no specific writings dedicated to loyalty to the king and love for the country, but the existence of a specific classic titled "Classic of Filial Piety" (孝經) regarding filial piety allows us to infer how much emphasis was placed on 'Filial Piety.'
Thus, while making filial piety the main tenet of all ethics and morality, Confucianism recognized absolute sanctity in the parents, the object of that filial piety. Even if parents displayed extreme cruelty and wickedness towards their child, this was not considered the parents' fault but stemmed entirely from the child's lack of filial sincerity. Therefore, it was said that there are no wrong parents under heaven. Not only that, but generally for us humans, apart from having the two individuals called 'my parents,' there should be no other people, and apart from the <19> act of serving parents, there should be no other acts, thus subordinating the entire existence of human life to the single clause of serving parents. This can be sufficiently proven by Mencius's words to a certain disciple. A disciple asked Mencius, "If Shun became the Son of Heaven and Gao Yao became the Minister of Justice, and Gusu (瞽瞍, Shun's father) killed a person, what should be done?" "Gao Yao should merely handle it according to the law." "Then what should Shun do?" "Shun should regard the empire like a worn-out shoe (弊履). He should secretly carry his father on his back and flee to the seashore." This means that regarding the parent's actions, unjustified killing is not a crime, and to serve the parent, even the position of the Son of Heaven, who saves the people of the world, must be discarded like a worn-out shoe. Furthermore, Confucianism, wanting to make the ethical order between parent and child even more stringent, established 'The father is the guide for the son' (父爲子綱) as one of the Three Bonds (三綱), placed 'There is closeness between parent and child' (父子有親) at the head of the Five Relationships (五倫), and declared this the Heavenly Relationship (天倫), sacred and inviolable.
In essence, 'Be filial to your parents' became an absolute command of traditional morality towards us, and that filial piety became the highest morality existing in this world. Then, for what reason do we bear the responsibility of having to be filial to our parents?
Father, you gave me life; Mother, you nurtured me. Wishing to repay their virtue, it is as boundless as the heavens. (Book of Songs, Liao E chapter)
Simply put, the reason a child must be filial to the parents is exhausted in the few lines above. That is, it lies in comforting and repaying the toil (劬勞) of 'giving me life and nurturing me' (生我鞠我). An old poem asks, if even the crow chick (烏子) knows filial regurgitation (反哺), and a human does not know filial piety, are they not worse than a bird? This too allows us to gauge that the fundamental meaning of filial piety lies in repaying gratitude towards parents.

Critique of Traditional Filial Piety
Up to this point, we have described the meaning of filial piety and subsequently briefly explained how important the position occupied by filial piety was in traditional morality and upon what the basis of filial piety rested. This has been purely a description based on the literal meanings found in Confucian classics, presented objectively as they are, and not the writer's subjective commentary. Now, I turn my pen to discuss the value of filial piety as seen from the perspective of modern thought and proceed to declare the future relationship between parent and child.
To say 'Be filial to parents' implies that the child should share the same fate as the parents, and at the same time, filial piety presupposes the existence of the family system. For, when we were in primitive times, <20> wandering aimlessly in mountains and fields, solely gathering natural fruits to sustain our lives, there was naturally nothing recognizable as a family. Later, with the advent of children born from the joyous union of man and woman, a mother-child lineage naturally existed, but the designation of father-child was still absent. Subsequently, progressing from the nomadic era to the agricultural era, the need for group power (羣聚力) involving more than one person arose for the struggle and acquisition of food. At this point (於是乎), the father-child relationship was added to the mother-child one, forming a family (家). The father, being comparatively older and stronger, became the head of the family, the patriarch (家長). General authority concerning internal and external affairs was exercised by the father, and the power of the family entirely belonged to that patriarch. The children inevitably came to revere and serve (敬事) their father. This is the initial establishment (始設) of the family system and the root cause (根因) of filial piety. This family system, after passing through the chieftain era, still largely exists today with the establishment of the state. Particularly in Confucian morality—nay, Eastern (東洋) morality—which reveres ancient times, adheres to inherited laws, and takes the past era as its sole ideal, the sanctity of this family system is recognized, and its maintenance and expansion are guaranteed by the way of filial piety and fraternal duty (孝悌). I recognize that the family system, a kind of relic (遺物) of primitive life systems—that is, the large family system (大家族制度) which strongly includes not just the couple and the young children born from them, but also parents, siblings, nephews, and nieces—has no special meaning today when social order is complete. At the same time, I first state that the way of filial piety and fraternal duty, which guarantees the maintenance and expansion of said system, also does not hold significant meaning for us.

Parents are always a kind of old friend who precedes the child; their thoughts will invariably lag one step behind the new ideas of the time. The bulk of the legacy (遺産) they pass down is nothing but the form of customs (因習) they adhered to, or things that are anti-historical. To drive the general populace to absolutely look up to them, describe their will, and carry on their affairs—that is, to be filial to parents (as in the traditional way)—means to inherit their thoughts and legacy as they are. The result is only knowing the existence of the predecessors' inherited laws, without imagining that the constructions of successors could be greater; only looking up to the dregs (餘瀝) of seniors, forgetting that one's own originality (獨刱) is more precious. This cultivates the bad habits (惡癖) of conservatism and dependence. On the one hand, it fosters the arbitrariness (恣橫) of kinship power (親族權) and gerontocracy (老人閥), stripping freedom of speech (言論의 自由) from the home, suppressing intellectual originality from society, ultimately leading to nothing but the creation of a state of incoherence and disintegration (支離滅裂) through conservatism and regression (守舊退嬰). Of course, respecting the model (儀範) of parents and carrying on their inherited will (遺志) may, depending on the time and circumstances (境遇), not only be permissible but sometimes absolutely necessary. For example, King Wu (武王) and the Duke of Zhou (周公) succeeding to the inherited work and will of their ancestors since Ji Ji (姬稷) to replace King Zhou of Yin (殷紂), possess the world, and reform cultural institutions—even without Confucius, one would praise their great deeds. However, such cases should be limited to instances where the model and inherited will are suitable (適) for <21> descendants (後昆) to follow. Simply forcing compliance unconditionally just because it belongs to the parents will only provoke (惹起) the vicious abuses (弊端) noted above. To say that one can only be called filial if, regardless of the good or evil of the matter, one does not change from the father's way for three years (三年) - no matter how I think about it, I cannot comprehend (了解) its meaning. Therefore, from the standpoint that it leads the general populace towards conservatism and regression, I reject (排) the 'succession-first principle' (繼述 第一主義) of traditional filial piety (that is, the idea of 'skillfully carrying on the will of others, skillfully describing the affairs of others').

To say 'Be filial to parents' means recognizing the existence of the parents' mind/will and mouth/body, while denying the existence of one's own mind/will and mouth/body. If parents are alive, one should first not travel far (遠遊), but if one travels, one must have a definite destination. And whatever the parents do themselves or command their child to do, whether bitter or sweet, must be followed and revered (遵仰). For example (例), even if parents are harsh (惡), one must toil (勞) and not resent (怨). If it is something the parents love, even if it does not suit one's own taste (性味), one must follow it (襲行). Regarding one's wife, too, one must decide on divorce (離緣) or non-divorce based on whether the parents deem her suitable or unsuitable. For all other matters related to the parents, not to mention major and minor actions (大小事行) concerning oneself (身上), one cannot but depend entirely on the parents' direction (左右). Not only that, but if the parents die, one suddenly becomes a sinner (罪人) between heaven and earth, mourning (居喪) for three years, during which time one must abstain (謝絶) from all self-care (自奉) and activity (活動). Here (玆), if someone (惑人) were to be under parental care (侍下) until about age fifty, and then subsequently encounter the parents' funeral and observe the mourning rites for three years, even if they lived sixty years, there would not be a single day of their own life (自己의 生) in that entire life. Alas (嗚呼라)! If there is no one born human who does not have parents, and if those who have parents—all of them (擧皆)—live only for their parents, meaning they do not possess their own lives, what will become of this world? It will truly bring about a tragic state (慘狀) where there are fathers and sons, but no individuals (人). Here, from the standpoint of disregarding the existence of the individual, I reject the 'parent-first principle' (爲親 第一主義) of traditional filial piety.

To say 'Be filial to parents' not only denies the child's self-care but also extends (延) to preventing the child from fully fulfilling the way of raising (養育) their own children. In other words (更言), while filial piety taught the child to act for the parent (親), it did not teach the parent to act for the child. Simultaneously, the widespread propagation (盛傳) of filial piety has proclaimed to the world at large that it doesn't matter how one treats one's children. According to the teachings (所敎) of filial piety, as long as a person has nourished their own parents well, that's enough. One's own children are nothing compared to the parents. Even if the children <22> weep (泣) from hunger (飢) or cry (啼) from cold (寒), as long as the parents' mouths and bodies are kept full and warm (飽暖), that's enough. In ancient times (古昔), there was a case where a child was buried alive (生埋) for the sake of the parents, and the government of the time rewarded this greatly, calling it the pinnacle of filial piety. Even now, if such a person existed, the world would likely praise their heaven-reaching great filial piety (出天大孝).
Thinking deeply (그윽히), rather than placing importance on supporting parents, who are merely members of the past's account (過去 帳中), we should focus all our efforts on raising children, who shape the future and the future beyond that, to first hope for the advancement (增進) of worldly happiness (幸福). Constantly saying 'parents, parents' and not thinking (想到) about the children's future, causing the general populace to forget (忘却) the issue of children, and causing contempt (輕蔑 - following DB주석 001 correction from 輕滅) for the sense of responsibility (責任觀念) towards them, is truly an unfortunate matter (不祥事) for the world. Of course, in the ancient Confucian past, while teaching children the duty (義務) of filial piety, it was not that the responsibility (責) of upbringing (敎養) for parents was unrecognized. However, traditional morality, which made the father the guide (綱) for the child and prioritized acting for the parents (爲先) as human virtue (美德), saw the voice of filial piety gradually grow louder (漸大) with the passage of time (推移), while the thought of upbringing weakened daily (日弛). Eventually, only filial piety remained as the one-sided duty (片務的 義務) of the child, while issues like children's upbringing were left entirely to the parents' discretion (隨意). Consequently, the world knew how to attack (攻擊) a child's lack of filial piety towards parents but did not know how to refute (論駁) parents' lack of upbringing towards their children. Here, for the sake of making our future ever more brilliant (燦爛), layer by layer (層一層), I reject the 'looking-back-first principle' (回顧 第一主義) of traditional filial piety, which leans solely towards the parents.

"Parents gave birth to one whole (全而生之); only if the child returns it whole (全而歸之) can it be called filial piety (孝)." "The body, hair, and skin (身體髮膚) - daring not to damage them is the beginning of filial piety (孝之始也)." Filial piety demands, first of all, the offering (獻上) of one's spirit and flesh (靈肉) from every child. Once that spirit and flesh are offered to the parents, it commands (命) that one can no longer dispose (處分) of that body oneself. When Zeng Shen (曾參, Confucius's high disciple/曾子) was critically ill (篤) and about to change mats (簣を易코저 - i.e., die), he left these words to his disciples (門徒): "Reveal (啓) my feet; reveal my hands. (Half a line deleted below - National Institute of Korean History) Now I am exempted (免)." His meaning was, "Thus I leave this world with my hands and feet intact; now I am exempted from being unfilial to my parents." Alas (嗚呼라)! To avoid being unfilial to his parents, he spent his life trembling with fear (戰戰又兢兢), as if approaching a deep abyss (深淵), as if treading on thin ice (薄冰), and then found his final peace of mind (安心) with the single phrase, "Now I am exempted." One might call it respectable (얌전하다) if one wished, but is not that life of trembling fear pitiful (可憐)? When we are born into this complex (複雜) world and wish to pass through one lifetime, whether successfully or not, especially if we wish to live by our own will (意志), there will be times when we enter and exit the boundary of life and death (死生의 境), and occasions when we traverse between axes (斧<23>鉞 - fig. danger/punishment) will not be absent. How can one possibly just tremble with fear, worrying (是慮) about damaging the body? Moreover, the child's body, having already (旣) been completely bestowed (附與) upon them by the parents, the child possesses the right (權利)—nay, the duty (義務)—within the bounds of not contravening (不悖) justice and humanity (正義人道), to freely utilize (運用) or dispose of that body. Why especially (何特) should it only be filial piety if the body is returned whole (全而歸之) to the parents? What reason (理) is there for that (何有)? Never mind the body; perhaps (或) if parents gave their beloved child a sum of money (金額), say several Won (幾圓) or several tens of Won (幾十圓), for their use (所用), and later that child returned the money exactly as received, without spending even one cent (一分), how disappointed (섭섭할가) would the parents feel? It is in this sense that I insert an objection (異議를 揷) to the 'return-whole-first principle' (全而歸之 第一主義) of traditional filial piety.

In the various points (諸項) above, I have critiqued the content (內容) of our traditional filial piety almost (殆) to the point where there is no intact skin (完膚가 無). However, I am not disregarding the great way (大道) of filial piety itself. Rejecting the traditional 'succession-first principle' does not mean unreasonably (無理) opposing the parents' inherited work and will. Rejecting the traditional 'parent-first principle' does not mean the child bears no responsibility (不負) even if the parents fall into a ditch (溝壑). Rejecting the traditional 'return-whole-first principle' does not mean it is acceptable to recklessly (함부로) damage the body (遺軆) received from parents. The only things being criticized (非難) are that aspect of not recognizing anything else exists besides one's parents or serving one's parents—that is, the 'first principle' aspect—and the resulting abuses (弊端) that arise (從生) from it. In other words (更言), it means inheriting the parents' will and work, but choosing (擇) based on the good or evil (善惡) of that work and will, and whether that work and will suit (適否 如何) the child's own interests and abilities (趣味技能). It means acting for the parents, but recognizing that the child themself and their own children also exist (更有). It means keeping the body whole, but not being confined (拘囚) by a trembling-ism (戰兢主義) just because it is the parents' legacy body. In a word (一言으로 蔽), it means that besides knowing oneself is a part (分身) of the parents, one must also know that the self is one human being, living simultaneously in the capacity (身分) of a child and in the capacity of a human being.
Until today (今日), we have just said "filial piety, filial piety," recognizing only its sanctity and not examining (不察) its dark side (暗面), thereby managing to calmly (恬然) overlook our own errors (自過). But if we consider the dark side produced by it, that is, its latter-day evils (末弊), through the lens (眼) of critique (批判), we cannot overlook (看過) it as it is, even for a moment. Try thinking (試思)! The 'parent-first principle' of traditional filial piety unknowingly (不知中) brewed (釀出) the omnipotence of parental authority (親權萬能), leading parents to regard their children as a type of property (所有物). Regarding all matters concerning the children's lives, the parents made arbitrary decisions (獨斷 - following DB주석 002 correction from 獨壇). Thus, even major life issues (大問題) like marriage (結婚) were arbitrarily arranged (恣行) by the parents of both families (兩家). Making a son take a wife was not for the son's own household (살림) but to satisfy the labor needs (勞役) of the 'family' or <24> their own pleasure (悅樂). Therefore, the general age of marriage (結婚期) became unreasonably (無理) early; 'marrying at thirty' (三十而有室) became 'marrying at ten' (單十而有室 - hyperbole). In contrast to their own sons marrying so early, they forced cohabitation (同居) upon daughters-in-law (며느리) taken at the suitable age (適齡) of sixteen or seventeen (十六七歲). Extreme cases (甚한 者) involved selling off (放賣) their daughters for their own livelihood (生計). Even currently (現在) among the so-called Yangban class (兩班階級), there are those who use their daughters as a stepping stone (段階) to curry favor (阿附) with powerful houses (權門). And regarding children's education (子女敎育), which by custom (依例) is the parents' responsibility (責任), they say whether or not to educate is also at their own discretion (任意). For the sake of affection for the children at their knee (膝下의 昵情) or other conveniences (便宜) of their own, they do not provide (不施) or refuse (不肯) appropriate (相當) education. Furthermore (尙), claiming children are their property, they act arbitrarily (恣行) with love and hate (愛憎), unaware of their own child's faults and others' merits. Extreme cases treat their children however they please, excessively resorting to private punishments (私刑) like scolding, humiliation, beating, and hitting (叱辱毆打). If one were to list examples (例を擧) one by one (一一), there would be no end. Parental abuse of power (濫權) is like this (如斯), yet children dare not (敢) utter (唱) a single word of objection (異議). If they raise a banner of revolt (反旗を擧), the world immediately attaches the criminal charge (罪名) of 'unfilial piety' and does not permit (不許) them to stand (立) in society. Not only that, but if anyone is moved (感) by this and criticizes the content of traditional morality or its latter-day evils by mouth (口) or perhaps by pen (筆), they are immediately called someone who does not know the ethical principles (倫綱) or perhaps an evil scoundrel (惡漢) without ancestors or father (無祖無父), and a portion (一部) of the stubborn, ignorant, and idle crowd (固陋無事輩) recklessly attacks (덤비). How can social reform (改革) be expected (期) under these circumstances?

The Future Parent-Child Relationship
Then what (如如何) should be done? The utmost sincerity (至情) of a child towards their parent, arising from the Heavenly Relationship (天倫), should rather (寧) be encouraged (助長), certainly not stopped (沮止). Yet, what traditional filial piety recognized cannot possibly be followed (襲用). Then what should be done? There is no other way but for us to propose (提唱) a New Filial Piety-ism (新孝道主義) from the standpoint (見地) of contemporary thought (時代思想) and thereby renew (一新) the future parent-child relationship. Brothers (兄弟)! Allow me to state (陳述) my humble opinion (管見) on this, just the main points (要領).
One, The degree (程度) of a child's filial piety towards a parent should be limited (限) to the dictates (攸命) of that emotion flowing out naturally (天然), and there should be no far-fetched association (牽强附會) of other meanings.
One, Between parent and child, discard the looking-back-ism (回顧主義) of traditional morality where the child acts for the father, and that father acts for his father. Instead, adopt the idealism (理想主義) of present-day morality where the father acts for the child, and that child acts for their child.
One, Parents should recognize that their children are not objects in their palm (掌中物) but complete (完全) human beings. Children should also remember (記) that besides being someone else's child, they are independent (獨立) human beings.
One, Upon giving birth to a child, parents must bear (必負) the duty (義務) to fully <25> raise and teach (기르고 가르칠) that child. In circumstances (境遇) where they cannot fulfill (履行) that duty, they should rather seek (講) ways to avoid (回避) the birth (産出) of children. Also, parents, before they become old and frail (老衰), should possess assets (資産), however small (小), to support themselves (自身을 養), so as not to demand (要求) support (扶養) from their children. If they cannot do so (그러치 못하면), it is because the child would then be unable to fulfill (盡) their responsibility (責) towards their own child.

**Summary**

The author, Myohyangsanin, argues that traditional Confucian filial piety (Hyodo) is an outdated relic of the large family system, hindering social progress, suppressing individual will and potential, and prioritizing past obligations (serving parents) over future development (raising children). The article critiques specific tenets like unquestioning obedience, inheriting parental ways regardless of merit, prioritizing parents' needs over children's or one's own, and the excessive focus on preserving the body intact. It highlights negative consequences such as the abuse of parental authority (親權萬能), forced early marriages, neglect of children's education, and the stifling of social critique under the charge of 'unfilial piety'. The author proposes a "New Filial Piety-ism" based on natural affection, mutual responsibility shifting focus towards the future (parents responsible for raising children well, children caring for parents if needed but parents striving for self-sufficiency), and recognizing children as independent individuals with their own lives to live.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this 1920 article is highly significant. It represents a bold intellectual challenge to the core Confucian values that structured traditional Korean society and were often reinforced by Japanese colonial rule to maintain order. The call for individualism, future-orientation, and reform of the family system reflects the strong desire for modernization and social change among Korean intellectuals following the March 1st Movement. Historians would find this article valuable for understanding the intellectual debates, social critiques, evolving family dynamics, and the tension between tradition and modernity during a pivotal period in colonial Korea.

잡지명	개벽 제4호
발행일	1920년 09월 25일
기사제목	從來의 孝道를 批判하야써 今後의 父子關係를 聲言함
필자	妙香山人
기사형태	논설

從來의 孝道를 批判하야써 今後의 父子關係를 聲言함
						妙香山人
우리는 物質上으로나 精神上으로나 한가지 新面目의 樹立에 熱中하는 者들이다. 그리함에는 閑暇히 先後를 차즐 수가 업고 그저 닥치는 대로 생각되는 대로 着手하고 實行할 것 뿐이다. 只今 右의 問題를 選하야 되나 못되나 부르지져 보고저 함도 微意는 오즉 이러함에 在할 뿐이니 이 孝道의 價値問題가 一般의 口에 上하야 一日이라도 速히 父子의 關係가 維新되엇스면.

孝道라 함은 무엇이냐?
孝道라 함은 무엇이냐. 倫理道德의 本宗인 孔子 曾子 孟子 朱子等 여러 어룬의 가르킨 말삼을 考하면
孝子는 善히 人의 志를 繼하며 善히 人의 事를 述하는 者니라.
身體髮膚는 父母에게서 受한 것이라 敢히 毁傷치 안이함이＜17＞ 孝의 始요 身을 立하며 名을 揚하야써 父母를 顯케 함이 孝의 終이니라.
父母ㅣ 全하게 生하얏스니 子ㅣ 亦 全하게 歸하야사 可히 孝라 謂할지니라.
君子ㅣ 敬치 암음이 업스나 身을 敬함이 最大함이 되나니라. 身은 親의 枝이니 敢히 敬치 안이 할 줄이 잇스랴.
孝子는 親을 事하되 平居에는 그 敬을 致하며 病患이어던 그 憂를 致하며 奉養에는 그 樂을 致하며 喪事이어던 그 哀를 致하나니라.
父母의 所敬을 子亦敬하며 父母의 所愛를 子亦愛할지니 犬馬에 至하야도 亦然할지니라.
凡事에 違함이 無하며 又는 父母가 其 子의 疾을 憂하는 그 心情을 子息이 그 父母에게 移하면 可히 孝라 謂할지니라.
父母ㅣ 계시거던 遠遊치 말며 遊하면 반듯이 方을 有할지니라.
三年을 父의 道에 改함이 無하야사 可히 孝라 謂할지니라. 父母에게 만일 過失이 有하거던 和氣怡色柔聲而諫하되 一向聽許치 안이하면 號泣하며 隨할지니라.
子ㅣ 甚히 그 妻를 宜하다 思할지라도 父母ㅣ悅치 안이하면 그 妻를 棄할지오. 子ㅣ 그 妻를 不宜라 認할지라도 父母 曰 可라 하거던 그 妻를 踈치 못할지니라.
父母의 그 口軆를 養함보다 父母의 그 志를 養함은 可히 孝를 知한다 云할지니라.

從古 聖賢의 孝道에 關한 記錄을 蒐輯코저 하면 實로 限이 업거니와 右에 摘記한 것 뿐으로 見하야도 孝道라는 것이 그 性質이 如何한 것이며 또 그네가 如何히 그에 置重하얏는가를 可히 推知할 것이다.
右에 依하면 孝道라 함은 子息이 그 父母에게 對하야 그의 心志와 口軆를 最善히 養하는 그 行과 又는 그 誠을 云함이라. 그 心志를 如干 通常的으로 養함이 안이오 最善히 養하여야 孝가 되는지라 故로 設或 父母의 心志 或은 그 心志로 從生하는 行爲가 社會道德에 違背함이 有할지라도 號泣하며 隨할 外에 無하며 이리함에는 生前뿐이 안이라 死後일지라도 三年은 그의 道에 改함이 無하여야 할지며 又 進하야 父母의 愛敬하는 바는 그 子가 必히 愛敬하여야 할 것이며 父母의 欲求하는 바를 그 子가 반듯이 致하여야 할 것이며 父母의 悅樂하는 바를 그 子가 반듯이 悅樂하여야＜18＞ 할 것이라. 一言으로 蔽하면 人의 子된 者는 그 父母의 心志를 有한 외에 更히 自己로의 心志를 有치 못할 것이니 少毫이나마 父母의 心志 以外에 自己의 것을 別有하면 是는 父母의 心志를 善養함이 안인 同時에 孝道라 云치 못할 것이라
心志를 그리 養함과 同時에 그의 口軆를 亦 最善히 養하여야 孝가 되는지라 廚에 入하야 甘旨를 具하고 堂에 上하야 起居를 問하며 夏이면 그 枕에 扇하며 冬이면 身으로써 그 被를 溫함은 勿論이오 自己自身 又는 其他 家族을 爲하야 如何한 事情이 有한 限이라도 그 親을 爲하야 爲先 柴糧을 求치 안이할 수 無하며 居室을 備치 안이할 수 無하며 不幸 父母가 沒하면 그 喪祭에 哀痛을 極히 하며 庶羞를 薦하야 그 遠을 追치 안이치 못할 지니 約言하면 人의 子된 者는 그 父母의 口軆를 有한 外에 更히 自己의 口軆를 有치 못할지니 一時이나마 父母의 口軆 以外에 自己의 口軆가 別有함을 認하면 是는 父母의 口軆를 善養함이 안인 同時에 孝道라 云치 못할 것이라.
如右히 父母의 心志와 口軆를 善養하야사 始로 孝라 謂할지나 만일 不可避의 事情과 엇지할 수 업는 形便으로 因하야 事實上 그 父母를 善養하기 不能한 時는 그를 善養할 至誠만 有하얏스면 亦 孝子이라 認하얏도다.

從來道德에 在한 孝道의 位置
從來 道德에 在하야는 孝로써 萬行의 根本을 作하얏스며 倫理道德의 源泉을 삼앗도다. 言必稱 忠孝이라 함을 見하면 忠孝의 二道中 忠으로써 그 先에 置함과 如하나 此는 言便을 取함에 不過함이오 實際는 孝로써 總道行의 中心이라 하얏나니 『事親이 孝故로 忠可移於君』이라 하며 又曰 『五刑之屬이 三千이로되 而罪莫大於不孝라』함을 見하면 那邊의 消息을 可窺할지며 又 儒家의 經傳을 考하면 忠君愛國에 關한 것이라. 特히 著述한 것은 無하나 孝에 關하야는 『孝經』이라는 經籍이 特有함으로써 推하야도 如何히 『孝』에 置重한 것을 可知하겟도다.
如斯히 孝로써 總 倫理道德의 本宗을 삼는 同時에 儒家에서는 그 孝의 對像되는 親에게 對하야 絶對의 神聖을 認하얏나니 設或 父母가 그 子에게 如何히 頑惡을 逞할지라도 是는 그 父母의 非가 안이오 專혀 그 子의 孝誠 不足에 由함이라. 故로 天下에 올치 안이한 父母는 업다 하얏스며 그 뿐만 안이라 大體 우리 人生에게는 自己의 父母이라는 二人이 有한 外에 更히 他人이 有치 못할 것이며 父母를 事하는＜19＞ 그 行이 有한 外에 更히 他行이 有치 못할것이라 하야 人生의 全 存在를 事親의 一節에 繫屬케 하얏다. 是는 孟子의 某 弟子에게 與한 言으로써 足証할지니 或 弟子가 孟子다러 『舜이 天子되고  皐陶ㅣ 士되엇는데 瞽瞍(舜의 父)ㅣ 人을 殺하얏거든 엇지 하리잇가』 『皐陶는 法에 依하야 治할 뿐이니라』 『그러면 舜은 如何히 하리잇가』 『舜은 天下를 視함을 弊履와 가티 할지니 그윽히 그 父를 負하고 海濱으로 逃하시리라』 云云하얏다. 곳 그 親의 所爲에 在하야는 無故殺人이 罪惡이 안이며 그 親을 事하기 爲하야는 天下의 生民을 濟度하는 天子의 그 地位도 弊履와 如히 去치 안이치 못하리라 함이다. 尙儒家에서는 父子의 倫序를 一層 緊重히 하고저 『父爲子綱』이라 하야 三綱의 一을 삼고 父子有親이라 하야 五倫의 首에 置하얏고 此를 天倫이라하야 神聖不可侵을 宣言하얏도다.
要컨대 父母에게 孝하라 함은 從來 道德의 吾人에게 對한 絶對命令이 되엇스며 그 孝道는 이 世間에 在한 最高 道德이 되엇도다. 그러면 何故로 吾人은 父母에게 對하야 不可不孝의 責을 負하는가.
父兮生我하시고 母兮鞠我하시니 欲報其德인대 昊天岡極이로다.(詩經蓼莪篇)
簡單히 말하면 子息의 父母에게 孝하여야 된다는 理由는 右의 數句에 盡하얏다 하겟다. 即 生我鞠我의 그 劬勞를 慰하며 報함에 在한 것이다. 烏子도 反哺를 知하거든 人이오 그 孝를 知치 못하면 鳥만 不如함이 안인가 하는 古詩를 見하야도 孝의 根本 義가 父母에게 對한 報恩에 在함을 斟酌할 것이다.

從來 孝道에 對한 批判
우리는 여긔까지 孝의 意義를 述하고 繼하야 從來 道德에 在한 孝道의 占한 位置가 如何히 重要함과 밋 孝의 根據가 何에 在함인가를 畧說하얏다. 이것은 專혀 儒家 經傳 中에 現한 辭意에 依하야 客觀 그대로 敍述함이오 記者의 主觀的 論評은 안이다. 이제부터 筆을 轉하야 現代 思想上으로 見한 孝道의 價値 如何를 論하고 進하야 父子間의 今後關係를 聲言코저하노라.
父母에게 孝하라 함은 子된 者가 그 父母와 運命을 共히 할 것을 意味함인 同時에 孝道는 家族制度의 存在를 前提로 한다 할지니 盖 吾人이 原始時代에 在하야 定處업시 山野로 彷徨하며 오즉 天然의 果物을 取하야 그 生을 營할 時는 勿論 家族이라 認할 것이 無하얏다. 그後 男女의 交歡으로＜20＞ 子女가 生함에 及하야는 自然 母子의 系統은 有하얏스나 父子의 稱은 尙無하던 바 其後 遊牧時代로부터 農耕時代에 進하여 食物의 爭奪取得上 一人 以上의 羣聚力을 必要하게 되매 於是乎 母子의 外에 父子의 關係를 添하야 一家를 成하며 比較的 年長力 强한 父되는 者는 그 家의 長 卽 家長이 되며 對內 對外에 對한 一般 權威를 그 父가 代行하며 一家의 權勢는 全혀 그 家長에게 歸屬하고 그 子女되는 者는 스스로 그 父를 敬事치 안이치 못하게 되엇나니 是가 卽 家族制度의 始設이며 孝道의 根因이라. 此 家族制度는 其後 酋長時代를 經하고 國家의 建立을 見한 今日에 在하야도 大槪 存在하여 特히 古昔을 祖述하고 遺法을 仍襲하야 過去時代로써 唯一한 理想을 삼는 儒家道德-안이 東洋 道德에 在하야는 此 家族制度의 神聖을 認하고 孝悌의 道로써 此의 維持 擴充을 保障하얏나니 余는 原始生活制의 一種 遺物인 家族制度-即 單히 夫妻 及 夫妻로부터 生하는 어린 子女에 不限하고 父母弟姪까지 强히 包含한 大家族制度-가 社會의 秩序完成한 今日에 在하야 別般 意味가 無함을 認하는 同時에 該 制度의 維持 擴充을 保障하는 孝悌의 道가 亦 우리에게 重大 意義를 不有하다 함을 爲先 말하야 둔다.

父母는 언제던지 그 子에 先하는 一種 舊의 友로 그 思想은 當時의 新思想보다 每樣 一步를 後할지며 그의 傳하는 遺産의 大槪는 自己의 固守하던 因習의 形式이 안이면 反時代的의 것 뿐일지니 一般을 驅하야 絶對로 그를 仰하며 그의 志를 述하고 그의 事를 繼하라 함은 即 父母에게 孝(從來와 如히)하라 함은 그 思想 그 遺産을 그대로 仍襲하라 함이니 그 結果는 오즉 先人의 遺法이 有함을 知하는 外에 後人의 建設이 更大할 것을 不想하며 先輩의 餘瀝을 是仰하는 外에 自家의 獨刱이 更貴함을 忘却하야 保守, 依賴의 惡癖을 養하게 되며 一面으로 親族權, 老人閥의 恣橫을 馴致하야 言論의 自由는 家庭으로부터 剝奪되어 思想의 獨刱은 社會로부터 沮抑되어 結局은 守舊退嬰으로 一種 支離滅裂의 現狀을 做出할 外에 無할 것이다. 勿論 父母의 儀範을 景仰하고 遺志를 繼述함이 時와 境遇에 依하야는 不可함이 되지 안이 할 뿐 안이라 반듯이 然하여야 할 것도 有할지니 武王 周公이 姬稷以來 列祖宗의 遺業과 遺志를 繼述하야 殷紂를 代하야 天下를 有하고 文物制度를 改修함과 如함은 孔子가 안이라도 그의 偉行을 稱揚할 것이라. 그러나 如此함은 그 儀範과 遺志가 後昆의 仍襲키에＜21＞ 適한 事에 限할 것이오 그저 父母의 것이라 하야 無條件으로 奉行을 强함과 如함은 右記와 如한 凶惡한 弊端을 惹起할 而已라. 事의 善惡을 勿論하고 三年을 父의 道에 改함이 無하야사 可히 孝라 謂한다 함은 如何히 思하야도 그 意義를 了解할 수가 업도다. 於是乎 余는 一般 人生을 守舊退嬰에 導하는 點에서 從來 孝道의 繼述 第一主義(卽 善繼人之志 善述人之事이라는 그것)를 排하노라.

父母에게 孝하라 함은 父母의 心志와 口軆가 有함을 認하는 外에 更히 自己의 心志와 口軆를 勿有하라 함이다. 父母가 계시거든 爲先 遠遊치 못하되 遊하면 必히 方을 有하여야 할지며 그리고 父母의 스스로 行하는 바 又는 그 子에게 對하야 命하는 바의 것은 쓰나 다나 此를 遵仰하여야 할지니 例하면 父母가 惡할지라도 勞하고 怨치 말며 父母의 愛하는 바여든 自己 性味에 不適하야도 그대로 襲行하여야 할 것이며 그 妻에 對하야도 父母의 宜하다 或 不宜하다 함을 依하야 離緣 不離緣을 決하여야 할 것이며 其他 父母에게 關한 것은 勿論이오 自己의 身上에 關한 大小事行에 對하야 全혀 父母의 左右에 依치 안이치 못할 것이며 뿐만 안이라 만일 父母가 死하면 문득 天地間 罪人의 몸이 되어 居喪 三年하되 그 間에는 一切의 自奉과 活動을 謝絶하여야 할지니 玆에 惑人이 有하야 約 五十 歲까지 侍下의 몸이 되고 그 後間 三年으로 父母의 喪을 當하야 居喪의 禮를 奉한다 하면 그는 六十年의 一生을 살앗다 할지라도 그 一生中 自己의 生은 一日도 無할 것이다 嗚呼라 人生되고 그 父母를 不有한 者 -無할지며 父母를 有한 者-擧皆 그 親을 爲하야 살을 뿐이라 하면 即 自己의 生을 不有하게 된다하면 이 世上의 꼴은 무엇이 될 것인가. 眞所謂 父와 子는 有하나 人은 無하게 되는 慘狀을 致할 것이니 玆에 余는 一般 個人의 存在를 無視라는 點에서 從來 孝道의 爲親 第一主義를 排하노라.

父母에게 孝하라 함은 子된 自身의 自奉을 否認함은 勿論이오 延하야 子된 그 사람의 子에게 對한 養育의 道를 盡치 못하게 함이다. 更言하면 孝道라 함은 子가 그 親을 爲함을 敎하얏스나 親이 그 子를 爲함을 不敎한 同時에 孝道의 盛傳은 世間 一般에게 向하야 子女에게 對한 것은 아모러케 하야도 無關하다 함을 宣傳함이다. 孝道의 所敎에 從하면 사람은 그저 自己 父母만 善養 하얏스면 그만이다. 自己의 子女는 父母에 比하면 아모 것도 안이다. 子女는 設或 飢에 ＜22＞ 泣하며 寒에 啼할지라도 父母의 口軆만 飽暖케 하얏스면 그만이다. 古昔에 父母를 爲하야 그 子를 生埋한 事가 有하얏거늘 當時의 政府는 此를 孝의 至라 하야 크게 賞與하얏다. 只今이라도 如許한 人이 有하면 世間은 亦 그의 出天大孝를 稱揚할 것이다.
