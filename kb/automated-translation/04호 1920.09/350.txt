讀者交情欄, 우리 開闢讀者에게
Reader Exchange Column, To Our Kaebyŏk Readers

億千萬載에 地球가 創造되고 人類가 始生한 後로 死死生生이 億億千千 이로대 多幸이 우리는 同一한 時代에 同一한 地球上에 生命을 共存하엿고 其中에도 同一히 朝鮮 사람이 되엇스며 同一히 우리 開闢을 供覽하게 되엇스니 엇지 적은 緣分이라 하리요. 이에 우리 開闢雜誌의 編輯 當務者들은 共存 共覽의 幸運을 祝福하기 爲하야 開闢 第5號로부터 紙面의 一部를 公開하야 讀者와 編輯者, 讀者와 讀者 間에 交誼을 圓滿이 하며 友情을 敦篤케 하고 互扶相助로 世運에 共進코자 하노니 願컨대 讀者 諸位는 此 欄을 利用하시옵소서.
For countless eons since the Earth was created and humankind first came into being, the cycles of death and rebirth have numbered in the hundreds of millions. Yet, fortunately, we coexist in the same era on the same Earth, and among us, we have become the same Chosŏn people, and have come to peruse our *Kaebyŏk* together. How could this be called a small affinity? Therefore, we, the editorial staff of *Kaebyŏk* Magazine, in order to celebrate the good fortune of our coexistence and shared readership, intend, starting from *Kaebyŏk* Issue 5, to open up a portion of our pages to bring fulfillment to the exchange between readers and editors, and between readers themselves, to strengthen friendships, and to advance together with the trends of the times through mutual aid and support. We hope that all you readers will make use of this column.

投稿의 範圍는. 本誌에 對한 希望, 讀後感想, 學術知識에 對한 問答, 人物紹介, 短評感想文, 誌友通信 等.
Scope of Submissions: Hopes for this magazine, reading impressions, questions and answers regarding academic knowledge, introductions of individuals, short critiques or impression pieces, correspondence among magazine friends, etc.

投稿字數는. 1,000字 以內.
Submission Length: Within 1,000 characters.

氏名과 住所는. 誌上에는 郡名과 匿名은 無妨하나 本社에까지는 氏名住所의 內報를 要함.＜134＞
Name and Address: Publishing the county name and an anonymous name in the magazine is acceptable, but notification of the full name and address to our office is required.<134>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from *Kaebyŏk* magazine announces the creation of a new "Reader Exchange Column" starting with the next issue (No. 5). It emphasizes the shared destiny of the readers as Koreans living in the same era and reading the same magazine. The column aims to foster communication and friendship between readers, editors, and fellow readers through submissions like opinions on the magazine, reviews, Q&A, introductions, short critiques, and letters, with a goal of mutual support and collective progress. Historians might find this article interesting as it explicitly fosters a sense of shared Korean identity ("同一히 朝鮮 사람이 되엇스며") and community ("互扶相助로 世運에 共進코자") through the medium of a cultural magazine during the Japanese colonial period. It reflects an effort to build connections and potentially national consciousness within the constraints of the time.
