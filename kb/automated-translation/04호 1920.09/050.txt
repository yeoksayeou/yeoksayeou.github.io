Autumn
						Ch'unsŏng (春城)
One
Oh light and cool Autumn!
Your fresh, clear, beautiful song
Riding the white horse of a crystal stream
From the distant land beyond the great sea
Ah! Comes so welcomely, indeed.
To drive out the red-eyed heat
And create a pleasant world of Lapis Lazuli.

Two
Ah! How welcome you are.
When your clear sound once softly, softly
Like the affectionate smiles of young girls
Gently shakes the blue sky, the white void,
The fearsome air transforms into a first-day bride,
The blackened earth changes into the morning sky,
Lightness that flutters its wings everywhere,
Coolness of new spring water in every place,
Ah! On your breast, I wish to build a golden monument
And hold a peaceful banquet of praise.

Three
Ah! Dear sir/madam, open your eyes and look!
Rustling, rustling among the paulownia trees,
The clear praise sung for you.
Upon the wide garden's green platter,
Apples (林檎), Grapes (葡萄), Yellow Pears (黃梨), and various others,
All the gifts (贐物) spread out vibrantly colored.
Upon the golden grass blades' cups,
Poured drop by drop, the silver new liquor (Dew - 露).
Ah! This is the sincere feast prepared
By benevolent Nature for you.

Four
Ah! Cool Autumn!
With your brand new, white, beautiful skirt,
Please wrap my young heart completely.
Beneath your skirt,
Listening to the flock of wild geese calling under the moonlight,
To their eternal, sorrowful appeal,
With the interesting companions (subjects - 學科) brought from the school window,
I intend to sleep in a dream of longing.
1920.8.20. <27>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unsŏng, published in the September 1920 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine, personifies autumn as a welcome visitor bringing coolness, clarity, and beauty after the oppressive heat of summer. The poet uses vivid imagery of nature – crystal streams, blue skies, colorful fruits, morning dew, and wild geese – to celebrate autumn's arrival and the peace it offers. The speaker expresses a desire to find solace and rest within autumn's embrace, dreaming with the "companions" (lessons) from school. From a colonial history perspective, the poem is interesting as an expression of appreciation for natural beauty and a longing for peace and refuge, potentially reflecting a desire for escape or tranquility amidst the often harsh realities of Japanese colonial rule. The use of purely Korean natural imagery and the Korean language itself (albeit with older spellings) in a public CULTURAL journal like *Kaebyŏk* can be seen as a subtle assertion of Korean identity and sensibility during this period.

가을
						春城
一
가비엽고 서늘한 가을님이어!
당신의 새 맑은 고흔 노래가
水晶줄기의 흰 말을 타고
큰 바다 저 便 먼 나라로부터
아! 반가히 오십니다 그려
붉은 눈 뜬 더를 몰아내고
살기 조흔 琉璃世界 맨드시랴고요.

二
아! 반갑소이다.
님의 맑은 소리가 한 번 솔솔
少女떼의 情다운 微笑와 가티
푸른 한울 흰 空中 가만히 흔들 때에
무섭던 空氣는 첫날 新婦로 化하고
검어튼 땅은 아츰 한울로 變하야
간 곳마다 날개치는 가비어움
곳곳마다 새 샘물의 서늘함
아! 님의 가슴에 黃金碑를 세우고
讚美의 平和宴을 베풀고저 합니다.

三
아! 님이어 눈 떠 보셔요
梧桐나무 사이에서 살랑살랑
님을 위해 부르는 맑은 讚美를
넓은 동산 푸른 소반 우에
林檎·葡萄·黃梨 等 여러가지
시뻙어케 벌여 잇는 모든 贐物을
黃金가튼 풀입 잔우에
방울방울 부어 놋는 銀빗 새술(露)을
아! 이것은 님을 爲해 베푸는
어진 自然의 精誠스러운 잔채올시다.

四
아! 서늘한 가을님이어
님의 새 하얀 고흔 치마로
나의 어린가슴 흠뻑 싸주셔요
나는 당신의 치마 아래서
달빗 아래 소리하는 기럭이 떼에
永遠의 애처러운 呼訴를 들으며
學窓에서 가져 온 滋味잇는 同侔(學科)로
憧憬의 꿈 속에 자랴 합니다.
1920.8.20. ＜27＞
