**Body Text Translation:**
One should not interfere in sound discussions (議論) with biased thoughts; if one interferes once, one will inflict shame upon all generations (萬世上). One should not set foot into a treacherous house or a pit that knows only profit (利); if one sets foot once, one will bear the blame for an entire lifetime (平生).<90>

**Summary**

This short, untitled piece offers moral admonishments. It advises against engaging in proper discourse with prejudice, warning of long-lasting shame. It also cautions against associating with treacherous people or situations driven solely by profit, predicting lifelong regret for such entanglement. From a historical perspective, this aphoristic writing from 1920 reflects the ethical concerns and didactic tone common in Korean intellectual circles during the early colonial period, perhaps subtly commenting on the moral compromises perceived in society.
