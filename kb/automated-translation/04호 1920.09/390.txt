필자	玄哲
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

脚本 隔夜
                        玄哲
Moscow River bank. A small chapel on the road.
To the right, by the roadside, a half-ruined small chapel built next to a dry well.
Five or six white poplar trees with sparse branches stand there. The winding river can be seen.
Across the river, the distant view is entirely of golden grain fields ripe for harvest. Churches, houses, windmills, etc., fill the early autumn scenery of a Russian village.
The atmosphere is that of after a great thunderstorm, but the sky is not yet clear, and a light rain continues. Occasional distant thunder and lightning.
Inside the small chapel, Elena is talking knee-to-knee with Kach'a, the beggar girl who came looking for her in the previous scene.
Elena: (Leaning halfway out and looking outside) I still don't know when the rain will ever stop. What should I do?
Kach'a: (Her eyes are open, but she stares blankly with unseeing eyes) Miss, don't go. If you leave, I'll be lonely by myself.
Elena: But I have to go see off someone who is going far away now. What can I do about the rain? — What are you eating?
Kach'a: (Showing the black bread she was eating) This. (And holds it to her mouth.)<143>
Elena: Why are you like this? Did you get that from your auntie?
Kach'a: No. What would auntie give me? She even takes the money I have. If I hide it, she hits me. I got hit yesterday too. (Shows her arm.)
Elena: Oh my! Your arm is swollen like this. You poor thing.
Kach'a: Miss, they say it's comfortable when you go before God (한울님), right? Is that really true? I wish I could go before God soon too.
Elena: Don't say such things.
Kach'a: Still, if I go before God, you won't come anymore, will you, Miss? Still, come visit.
Elena: I will. I'll come see you.
Kach'a: Really? If you come, I'll be truly grateful. — Ah, I'm going now. If I'm late, I'll get scolded by auntie. (Suddenly adopting a subservient attitude) Miss, give me something. Give me some money, please.
Elena: (Feeling her pockets) What should I do? I came out in such a hurry I didn't bring anything.
Kach'a: Still, please give me something.
Elena: I don't have anything, what can I do? Here, take this at least. (Gives her handkerchief)
Kach'a: What's this, maybe a handkerchief (手巾)? Oh, it's nice. It's probably red, right? It's wet. Like this. Miss, did you cry again? What makes you so sad? Your mother doesn't hit you, does she, Miss?
Elena: Why would she hit me? The young lady—it's because of someone else's affairs—the young lady is worried about the person she likes best.
Kach'a: The young lady is always worrying about other people's affairs. Who is the person you like? A man or a woman?
Elena: It's a man.
Kach'a: A man? Father, uncle—no. Ah, a new groom (섀실랑). You mean your new groom, Miss? Are you getting married, Miss?
Elena: What is this child saying?
Kach'a: That's right. You're getting married. Then you won't go to the temple, Miss? You said you'd go to the temple and become a nun, that must have been a lie.
Elena: Mm-hmm. The young lady intends to go somewhere farther than a temple, a more bustling place, and do something there.
Kach'a: Really? Are you really going far away? Then what about me?<144> I'll go to God. I should just go.
(The dim moon shines.)
Ah, the sun is out, the sun has come out. I'm going.
Elena: Keep your wits about you and go carefully.
Kach'a: (Her voice fading as she leaves, singing)
Where does the blind pigeon go?
Over mountains, over fields, just keeps going
Does it think there's another world (世上) unknown to others?
Does it think there's a high and clear heaven?
Elena: (Stands for a while watching her go) Ah, I must go too. Why am I just standing here blankly? (Busily puts on her coat and prepares to leave.)
(Insarop passes by her side, dressed for travel, carrying a small bag, head bowed low, hurrying past Elena without noticing her.)
Elena: (Suddenly thinking it might be Insarop, mutters to herself) Insarop, Insarop—(Expression as if the voice won't come out of her throat) Yes, it's definitely Insarop. Dimitri Nikanovich— Where are you going? Where are you going? Don't go. Stay there. (Shouts desperately.) Dimitri Nikanovich—(Chasing after him)
Insarop: (Barely hearing, turns around and stops short.) Who is it—(Only then realizing it's Elena) Ah, Miss Elena? I wondered who it was.
Elena: (Wordlessly goes towards the chapel and sits down slumped on the steps.)
Insarop: (Following Elena, smacks his lips.) Were you here, Miss Elena? (Not knowing what to do, just looks around in all directions.)
Elena: (With a composed attitude, stares intently at Insarop's face.) Were you on your way to our house?
Insarop: Uh—uh, no, I was on my way somewhere else.
Elena: Is that so? But you promised (約條) to come to our house today at one o'clock, didn't you? I've been waiting until now. Waiting and waiting, unable to wait any longer...
Insarop: It's too—
Elena: You seem to be traveling somewhere urgently. Where are you going? I heard from Mr. Bersenev.
Insarop: Did Bersenev say that? Where did he say I was going?<145>
Elena: Where indeed? He told me to ask you. Because you would surely meet once before leaving. I thought you would at least say goodbye before you left.
Insarop: However, it couldn't happen that way—
Elena: Yes, it couldn't happen that way— Truly, you intended to leave without a word. The thought that you might just leave without a word has indeed come true. After becoming so close and trusting each other— You were just going to leave without saying where you were going or even that you were leaving... you were just going to go. If I hadn't met you here by chance (偶然이) right now, I might never have seen you again. If I hadn't met you now, I might never have seen you ever again.... Mr. Insarop? Are you truly so faithless (無信)?
Insarop: (Sitting down beside Elena and looking at her) Miss Elena, please forgive (容恕) me. Even without you saying such things, this heart is already filled with matters many times more stifling. The decision I made to come this far was not such an easy one either. Please try to understand a little.
Elena: No. I cannot understand. What good would understanding do?— For what reason did you intend to travel? People sometimes have unavoidable circumstances when they travel, but it wasn't like that. What is it that makes you leave without any concern (掛念), tormenting a friend like this? Between such close friends (親舊間), can you just leave like this? Do you remember what we said while walking together one morning, the day after we went to the lakeside (湖水邊)? That we would always help each other with close friendship (友情).—
Insarop: Yes, that happened.
Elena: At that time, when I said I liked roses (薔薇꼿), you said you liked roses too, and you picked two white roses blooming there, saying this was a token (情表) of our friendship, and we each pinned one to our chests before parting, remember?—
Insarop: I haven't forgotten that.
Elena: I kept that flower hidden away to remember it later (日後). The flower has dried, and its form (形狀) remains, but are we two not to remain as we were, and instead part ways?
Insarop: It seems it will be so. Now, we cannot just call ourselves friends.<146>
Elena: Oh! Then we can't call ourselves friends?
Insarop: That's right. Now, we cannot just call ourselves friends. That's why I couldn't help but leave this place.
Elena: (With a disappointed attitude) Why is that?
Insarop: The reason (理由) why? Please don't ask me to say more beyond this. To speak further beyond this is painful (苦痛). I cannot say more.
Elena: You cannot say more? Before (以前), you used to talk about everything without any distance (間隔) between us.—
Insarop: That's true. But back then, there was no need (必要) for any particular words. But now—
Elena: What about now?—
Insarop: Well, now—no, I must go. If fate allows, I will see you again (뵈옵지요).
Elena: (Calmly and coolly (沈着 冷靜이)) Go, go then. Then, as a memento (紀念) of our meeting here—(Extends her hand.)
Insarop: (Starts to extend his hand, then stops again) No, that cannot be either.
Elena: That cannot be either?
Insarop: That's right, that's right. I am going. (Hurriedly (慌忙이) descends from the chapel.)
Elena: (Standing up) (Without composure) Dimitri Nikanovich, wait just a moment (暫間). Just for one minute (分). Dimitri, you fear (겁을 내시지요) me, don't you? But I do not fear you. I have many times more courage (勇氣) than you... I do indeed— Ah, I must speak, what if I do? Do you know why I came here?— Do you know where I was intending to go like this?
Insarop: Well.
Elena: I was on my way to find you. To wherever you are.
Insarop: Eh, you were coming to me?—
Elena: Yes, that's right. Without you, I don't feel like I'm alive even for a moment. If you go far away—if you leave for some unknown distant journey—how will I manage? Do you know who is left behind? A father who isn't gentle and is very stubborn, a mother whose chest feels like it will burst day and night (밤낫) from heart disease (心臟病), a pot-bellied, dull old man (우왈 老人) like a toad.— The imprudent Shubin—<147>
Insarop: Isn't Bersenev there?
Elena: Bersenev? Bersenev is a good person too. Kind (親切), sincere (眞實), a fine gentleman (紳士). But there's something lacking (不足), some deficiency. He lacks passion (熱氣) and has a slow (느릿느릿한) nature (性質).— (Hiding her face) Ah, Dimitri Nikanovich, somehow, somehow, you—want to hear me say it, that I love you, that I love you. That I love you, I love you— Ah, now I've said it all— (Buries her face in her own chest.)
Insarop: Elena! (Calls her name and reaches out his hand without thinking.)
Elena: (Firmly grasps the man's outstretched hand, lifts her head and presses her face against the man's chest.) Dimitri, I have never known greater happiness (깃분 일) than this.
Insarop: (Holding the woman tightly) Elena. I too have never known greater happiness than here.
(A long, short silence filled with emotion continues.)
Insarop: (Gently releases the body he was holding, raises his arm and lightly rests it on Elena's shoulder, both go to the chapel steps and sit side by side) Elena, thinking about it, it feels like a dream. I didn't know it would happen so quickly (速히).
Elena: That's right. That it would be so quick—. But you were trying to run away because you were scared of me, weren't you?
Insarop: Yes, that's right.— But even now, that thought still remains with me. If I turn away right now, won't I immediately regret it? Won't there be times when I think it would have been better (조흘 것) to have stopped?
Elena: Why do you say such things? I have been yours for a long time already. Outside of you, outside of the country of Bulgaria, I have never thought about or worried about anything else. Stop it. Such talk—
Insarop: But our marriage (結婚) is not like worldly marriages, advancing towards the happiness (幸福) of a comfortable (安樂) and peaceful (平和) home (家庭). Not only that, but do you know it is rather (돌우) the first step into a gate of hardship (苦楚) and adversity (窮厄)?
Elena: I have already considered (斟酌) that.
Insarop: Then, from now on, are you prepared to walk with me on any path of destiny (運命), no matter how dreadful and frightening it may be?
Elena: Of course, I will go with you. Wherever it may be, I'll go with you. Even to the ends of the Earth (地球), if I can go with you, I will go.<148>
Insarop: But don't you feel that you are forcing yourself, deceiving yourself, even though you know it is dangerous (危險)? You know that if we marry, our parents (父母님) will not give their permission (許諾), don't you?
Elena: I am not deceiving myself in the least. I have considered everything.
Insarop: Not only that. You also know that I am poor, almost in the position of a beggar, don't you?
Elena: I know.
Insarop: And also that I am not a Russian, and that I cannot stay long in Russia, and consequently, you too will have no choice but to cast off all bonds (覇絆 - likely 羈絆 'bonds') to your homeland, family, ancestors, and such things.
Elena: I know. I know. That Russia is not my homeland (故鄕) either.
Insarop: There is more. There is the most important thing. I am currently experiencing things different from ordinary people. I—no, not just me, but my comrades (同志) not only do all sorts of dangerous things, but also suffer all kinds of oppression (壓迫) and unspeakable disgrace (汚辱), and besides that—
Elena: Yes, I know. I know everything. How could I not know that? I know it all. It's just that I love you—
Insarop: No, not just that, but more than anything, you must abandon the life you have continued for 20 years until today, and just by yourself, go to an unfamiliar foreign land (山설고 물선 他鄕), mix with unknown people, perhaps, depending on the circumstances (境遇), even engage in painful labor (勞働) together—
Elena: (Quickly covers the man's mouth with her hand) Stop now, please. I know all about those things. For me, loving you is my entirety (全軆). Nothing else again—
Insarop: Thank you, Elena.— (Takes the woman's hand with gratitude (感謝) and gives her a kiss (接吻) filled with passion (熱情). The woman, with a gentle (溫順) demeanor, dazedly looks up towards the distance (遠方), letting the man do as he pleases. Unending kisses and embraces (抱擁)—. Suddenly, as if remembering something, the woman hastily buries her face in the man's chest with a bashful attitude.)
Insarop: (Looking into the woman's face while holding her with warm affection) Elena. My beloved wife (안해)—
(Long, long kisses and embraces)
Elena: (Pushing him away and standing up) Don't do this. Don't<149> do this. (With a happy expression (깃거운 빗), tidies her disheveled (허터러진) hair with her hands) You've messed up my hair like this.
Insarop: (Also stands up and paces back and forth) Elena, neither of us will ever forget this happy (幸福) moment today, for as long as we live.
Elena: Of course. How could we ever forget? But I wonder if this moment isn't the end (最後) of our happiness.
Insarop: However, when I think about it, in our future (前途), terrible pain awaits us both, along with happiness.
Elena: Is that so? Happiness and pain will bind the two of us even more tightly together. Am I not your wife?
Insarop: And because of that, the absence of joys like the family life (家庭生活) that two people live together—
Elena: Do you call that absence a pain?
Insarop: That pain is truly (實로) right before my eyes. Elena, the day I must leave this country is not far off.
Elena: Is that so? Then are you going to your homeland?
Insarop: That's right. The great opportunity (機會) our comrades have long awaited, enduring humiliation, has finally arrived now. Between this country, Russia, and Turkey (土耳其), the long-standing conflict (葛藤) has reached a point where this time (이番), they absolutely must fight. The Russian army (軍隊) is occupying (占領) the Principality of Bulgaria (불가리아 公國). The Turkish government (政府) has apparently submitted protests (抗議) several times demanding troop withdrawal (撤兵), but it seems they don't even blink an eye. Not only that, but at the border (國境) on the Crimean Peninsula (크리미야 半島), they are daily reinforcing (增員) the garrison troops (守備兵) of the Sevastopol Fortress (세빠스트포올 要塞), preparing for any eventuality (萬一). The time (時機) has come to overthrow the tyrannical (暴虐) Turkey and repay the enmity (怨讎) built up over many years of fighting. The time has come to restore (恢復) the independence (獨立) of our homeland and re-establish the great Bulgarian Empire (불가리아 大帝國) as before. Letters saying "Rise up, rise up"—come daily from comrades (同志者) in the homeland.— However, thinking deeply (깁히), I wonder if now is truly the time, or if it isn't still too early (일르지).— But the movement (運動) of our comrades progresses (進就) day by day. Success lies with heaven (成事는 在天), but planning lies with man (謀事는 在人), so whether we win or lose, we must try until we have tried. Whatever happens is fate (天命).
Elena: Then when do you intend to go?
Insarop: If things turn out that way, I cannot hesitate (猶豫) even for a moment.<150> If I dawdle, the path is likely to be blocked.
Elena: You will take me with you, won't you?— I suddenly want to see the country called Bulgaria.
Insarop: Thank you. We must go together. But a woman cannot go to war (戰爭), can she?
Elena: But if I were to stay here alone, I would rather die.
Insarop: I understand. It feels as though I cannot refuse anything you say.
Elena: Then please take me with you. If you just give your permission (許諾), I would go right now (이양이라도). I don't even want to go back home.
Insarop: Even if it's right now, there must be preparations (準備). We need travel documents (旅行券) and travel funds (路費). If possible, I would like to go after obtaining permission (許可) from both parents (兩親). I don't want us to go like fugitives (도망군이).
Elena: (Suddenly becomes dispirited) Will father give his permission? Travel documents and funds (路資)— I have travel funds. I have about (可量) 80 rubles.
Insarop: That's not enough (不足), but it's good (조흡니다) that you have some.
Elena: But I can arrange for more. I'll borrow it. I'll speak to Mother, or maybe speaking to Mother isn't good (조치 못할는지)— Then I'll sell my watch (時計) too.— I'll sell my earrings (귀ㅅ고리) too.— Bracelets (팔ㅅ지) too— Lace (레에쓰) too—.
Insarop: Thank you for saying so. (Gradually shows a gloomy (陰鬱), pained (苦痛) expression (表情))
Elena: (With a joyful look (喜色)) Traveling together would be wonderful, wouldn't it? (Suddenly notices the man's expression (氣色)) Why are you like that? What's wrong? Are you unwell (편챤으십닛가)? Did my excessive chattering bother (거슬리습닛가) you?
Insarop: No. It's alright (괜챤으니). Just leave me be. I got too excited (興奮) earlier, my blood rushed.
Elena: Your face has become pale (해쓱하여졋습니다). What should I do? Isn't it some illness (病患)?
Insarop: Yes. A little—
Elena: It's an illness? I had no idea at all.—
Insarop: It's not such a serious (大端) illness.
Elena: But for your precious (貴重) body, with such a great undertaking (事業) ahead— Since when has it been like this? Since yesterday? The day before yesterday? Since before (以前)?<151>
Insarop: It has been like this since before.— Elena, forgive (容恕) me. My illness is internal (안읫 病). It is an incurable, lifelong illness (終身病). (Presses his chest, speaks in a grief-stricken (沈痛) voice) Elena, I have lung disease (肺病) in my body.
Elena: Lung disease?—
Insarop: Don't be alarmed. It manifested (發表) itself recently (近者). I too worried for a time (一時). You said I was running away out of fear (劫). The reason I was afraid and ran away was that I feared my illness for your sake (爲해서). Even if I don't die on the battlefield (戰場), my future path (압길) is already numbered (헤아리고 잇습니다). How long can it be? But drawn by your loving, strong (굿센) heart, even though my heart knew it was wrong (안된 줄 알면서도)—
Elena: (In a sorrowful (悲愴) voice) Stop saying such things. Stop it. I know everything you are going to say. I am not a coward (겁쟁이) like you. Fearing illness, fearing death (죽음), can one love (戀愛)?—(With a hysterical (히쓰테리的) laugh) Haha, isn't it a joyful (질거운) thing to die together anytime?— But why must we die? Surely, we don't have a fate (運命) where we absolutely must die, do we? Whether long or short (짤으던지), whatever hardship (苦生) we face, let's live as long as we can live. We are still so young (젊쟌습닛가), aren't we— You, how old were you? Twenty-six (스물여섯)—.
Insarop: Yes, twenty-six.
Elena: I am twenty (스무살).— We are both surprisingly young. There's no reason for two such young people (양주 - likely 'people') to die so quickly (速히), is there?— Make up your mind firmly (튼튼이 잡수셔요). We will never (決코) die.
Insarop: Thank you. Elena. You are like a lamp (燈불) to me walking a dark night road (밤길). Like an angel (天使).
Elena: The terrible storm (暴風雨) that raged between us has already passed. From now on (일로부터), a stable (安靜), quiet (閒寂), and firm (堅固) life (生活) will begin between us.— Before the shrine (殿閣) of the Madonna (마든나님), please ask her to bestow her blessing (祝福) upon your beloved wife. (Kneels (跪坐))
Insarop: (Stands up quietly, places his hand on Elena's head) A blessing for my wife (나의 안해를 爲하여 祝福을) —
Elena: Ah, you, you are my—(Hugs Insarop's knees (무릅)).— ...(Curtain (幕))... <152>

**Summary**

In this scene from Hyeon Cheol's play "The Night Before," set by the Moscow River, Elena confronts Dimitri Insarop, a Bulgarian revolutionary she loves, who is planning to leave Russia secretly. After an emotional exchange where Elena confesses her love and determination to follow him anywhere despite the dangers and hardships (including his newly revealed tuberculosis and revolutionary activities aiming for Bulgarian independence from Turkey), Insarop accepts her love. They pledge to face their uncertain future together, acknowledging both the potential happiness and the immense suffering ahead, culminating in a request for a blessing before a Madonna shrine.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this 1920 play is interesting. Its publication in 'Gaebyeok' (Creation/The Beginning), a significant magazine during the cultural nationalist movement, and its adaptation of (or inspiration by) a Russian story (likely Turgenev's "On the Eve") reflects the period's engagement with foreign literature and ideas. The themes of national liberation (Bulgarian independence from Turkey), self-sacrifice for a cause, and the tension between personal love and patriotic duty would have resonated deeply with Korean intellectuals and readers living under Japanese colonial rule, offering a parallel or allegorical exploration of their own aspirations and struggles for independence. The use of older Korean orthography and vocabulary also provides linguistic data from that era.

모스코河岸. 道路의 小禮拜堂
右便은 路傍, 枯井 엽헤 지은 半壞의 小禮拜當
疎枝의 白楊樹 5, 6株서 잇고. 迂曲한 河水가 보인다.
河水의 越便으로 그 遠景이 一面으로 成熟한 金色의 穀物田. 會堂, 人家, 風車 等 露西亞 鄕村의 初秋 風景이 充滿하엿다.
大雷雨의 後景인 氣分과 아즉도 晴天이 안이고 細雨는 繼續되다 間間 멀리 들니는 雷聲, 電光.
小禮拜堂中에서 에레나 前場에 차자왓던 乞食小女 가아챠와 무릅을 맛대이고 이악이를 하고 잇다.
에레나. (몸을 반쯤 내밀고 밧그로 보면서) 아즉도 비가 언제나 끄칠지는 모르겟다. 이를 엇져면 조와.
가아챠. (눈은 떳스나 보지는 못하는 눈을 또렷또렷 살피며) 아가씨 가지 마셔요. 가고 나면 나 혼자 심심해요.
에레나. 그러치만은 나는 只今 멀리 가는 사람을 餞送하고 와야 하겟다. 비가 와서 엇더케 하나. ―너는 무얼 그리 먹니.
가아챠. (멱던 黑방을 보이며) 이것. (하고 입에 데민다.)＜143＞
에레나. 웨이러니. 그것 너이 아지머니에게 어덧니.
가아챠. 안이. 아지머니가 무얼 주어. 내가 가지고 잇는 돈도 뺏는데. 감춰두면 때린다눔. 어저께도 마졋지. (팔을 내어 보인다.)
에레나. 애그! 팔이 이처럼 부엇구나. 가엽시.
가아챠. 아가씨 한울님 압헤 가면 便타지? 정말 그래요. 나도 어서 한울님 압헤 갓스면.
에레나. 그런 말은 안이한다.
가아챠. 그래도 내가 한울님 압헤 가면 가아씨는 인제 안이 오겟지. 그래도 와요.
에레나. 가지. 너 보러 가지.
가아챠. 정말. 와주시면 참 고맙지요. ― 아아 나는 가요. 느지면 아지머니에게 말 드러요. (急作이 賤婢한 態度로) 아가씨, 나 무어 좀 주어요. 돈이라도 좀 주어요.
에레나. (주머니를 추어보더니) 이를 엇쟤. 急히 나온다고 아모 것도 가지고 나오지 안이 헷다.
가아챠. 그래도 무엇 좀 주어요.
에레나. 아모 것도 가지지 안이햇는데 엇더케. 아나 이거라도 주지. (手巾을 준다)
가아챠. 이것 무엇이야, 아마 手巾이지. 애그 조와라. 아마 붉은 것이지. 저졋내. 이처럼. 아가씨, 또 울엇세요. 무엇이 그처럼 설읍닛가요. 마님은 아가씨를 때리지 안이하지?
에레나. 맛기는 우에 맛니. 아가씨는 남의 일로― 아가씨의 第一 조와하는 사람의 일로 걱정이 되엇서 그러탄다.
가아챠. 아가씨는 一生 남의 일로 걱정만 하고 잇지. 조와하는 사람이 누구야. 사나이 녀편내.
에레나. 사나이란다.
가아챠. 사나이라니? 아버지 아저씨―안이지. 아아 섀실랑. 아가씨 섀신랑말이지. 아가씨 시집가오.
에레나. 이 에가 무슨 말을 해.
가아챠. 그러타. 시집가시는군. 그러면 아가씨 절에는 안가시우. 절에 가서 중 된다더니 거짓말이로구려.
에레나. 으응. 아가씨는 절보다 더 먼, 더 繁華한 곳으로 가서 무슨 일을 하랴고 한다.
가아챠. 참말. 정말 먼 대로 가셔요. 그러면 나는 엇더케＜144＞ 하나. 나는 한울님에게 가요. 그만 가야지.
(으스름 달이 비친다)
아아, 해가  낫다, 해가 나왓군. 갑니다.
에레나. 精神 차려서 잘가거라.
가아챠. (가는 목소리로 가면서)
압 못 보는 저 비듥이 어대로 가나
山 넘고 들 넘어서 가기만 하면
남 모르는 딴 누리(世上)가 잇슬 줄 아나
놉고도 맑은 한울 잇슬 줄 아나
에레나. (한참 서서 가는 것을 보더니) 아아 나도 가야지. 웨 이리 정신업시 섯기만 하고 잇나. (부산이 外套를 입고 가랴고 한다.)
(엽흐로 지나가는 인사롭, 旅行하는 차림에 적은 가방을 들고 고개를 푹 숙이고 急히 에레나 압흐로 모르고 지나간다.)
에레나. (별안간 인사롭인가 생각이 들어 혼자말로) 인사롭, 인사롭―(목에서 소리가 나오지 안는 表情) 그러타 꼭 인사롭이다. 디미트리 니가노윗치― 어대로 가오. 어대로 가요. 가지 마셔요. 거긔 잇셔요.(絶叫한다.) 디미트리 니가노윗치―(조차가며)

에레나. (아모말 업시 禮拜堂 쪽으로 가서 層層臺에 걸터 안는다.)
인사롭. (에레나 뒤를 딸아오면서 입맛을 다신다.) 에레나氏가 거긔 잇섯던가요. (엇졀 줄 몰라 四方을 두런두런 삷히기만 한다 .)
에레나. (鎭靜한 態度로 인사롭의 얼골을 뚤어지도록 본다.) 우리집에 가시던 길인가.
인사롭. 에―에 안이요, 다, 다른대 가는 길이야오.
에레나. 그레서요. 그러나 오늘 한 點에 우리집으로 오신다고 約條한 일이 잇지요, 나는 只今껏 기다렷서요. 기다리다 기다리다 못해서...
인사롭. 넘우나―
에레나. 어대로 急히 旅行하시는 模樣갓습니다. 어대로 가십닛가. 벨세넵氏에게 드럿지요.
인사롭. 벨세넵이 그리고합듸가. 어대로 간다고 해요.＜145＞
에레나. 어대인지요. 그것은 날더러 무러보라고 합듸다. 아마 떠나시기 前에 한번 맛날 것이니까요. 아모래도 떠나시는 人事야 하고 가실줄 알앗지요.
인사롭. 그러나, 그러케 못되엇지요―
에레나. 예에, 그러케 못되엇셔요― 정말 아모말 업시 가시랴고 하엿습니다 그려. 或時 아모말 업시 가지나 안이할가 생각한 것이 정말 그러케 되엇습니다. 모처럼 親해서 서로 밋고 지내던 터에― 어대 가시면서 가신단 말도 업고 가시는 곳도 일러주지 안이하고...이양 가시랴고 하셧습니다 그려. 只今 이자리에서 偶然이 맛나지 안이하엿더면 아주 못 볼 번하엿습니다 그려. 只今 맛나지 못하엿더면 永遠이 못볼는지도 모를 것입니다 그려.... 인사롭氏? 그처럼 사람이 無信합닛가.

에레나. 안이요. 생각해 드릴 수 업지요. 생각해 드려서는 무엇해요.― 무슨 일로 旅行은 하시랴고 하셧던가요. 사람이 가다가 不得已한 事情이 잇기도 하겟지만은. 그런 일도 안이고, 이처럼 친구의 속을 태우고 조금도 掛念업시 가실 일이 무엇인가요. 모처럼 親한 親舊間에 그냥 가실 수가 잇습닛가. 언제인지 湖水邊에 놀라갓던 그 이튼날 아츰에, 둘이 散步하면서 한 말이 잇섯지요. 우리들은 언제던지 서로 親密한 友情으로 서로 도와가자고.―
인사롭. 그런 일이 잇섯지요.
에레나. 그럴 적에 내가 薔薇꼿을 조와한다고 한 즉 당신의 말이 나도 薔薇꼿을 조와한다고 거긔 피어잇는 흰 薔薇꼿 두 송이를 꺽거 이것은 우리둘이 親舊된 情表라고 하고 하나式 가슴에 꼿고 허여진 일이 잇섯지요?―
인사롭. 그것을 잇지 안이햇서요.
에레나. 그 때 그 꼿은 日後에 記念하자고 그양 감추어 두엇지요, 그 꼿은 말랏지만 그 形狀은 그냥 잇지만은 우리둘은 그대로 잇지 안코 혀여지게 됩닛가?
인사롭. 그러케 되겟지요. 인제는 親舊라고만 할 수 업지요.＜146＞
에레나. 에에! 그르면 우리들은 親舊라고 할 수 업서요.
인사롭. 그럿습니다. 인제는 親舊라고만 할 수 업습니다. 그러니까 이곳을 떠나지 안을 수 업게 되엇습니다.

인사롭. (손을 내밀다가 다시 멈추며) 안이요, 그것도 될 수 업서요.

인사롭. 글세요.
