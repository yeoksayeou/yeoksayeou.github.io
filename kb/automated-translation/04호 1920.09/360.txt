Seventh is Background.
As mentioned in the Outline of the Novel, the nature of the background warrants another discussion. Background refers to the place and time where the events and characters in a novel operate. Describing the place and time where an event occurred is the responsibility of the background. Therefore, when analyzing the background, one must pay attention to several points: First, whether it is depicted clearly and pictorially, or described vaguely in a hazy state. Second, whether the background is described in minute detail, or only the essential points of the outline are arranged. Third, whether a common, everyday place is represented, or whether the unique mountains, rivers, or scenery characteristic of a certain region, namely Local Color (地方的 色彩), is employed. Originally, the fundamental purpose of the background is to bring the characters and events within the work to life. Thus, if the background merely exists without any deep connection to the characters and events and serves no useful function, or if it does have some relationship, to what extent does it vitalize the characters and events? Closely examining this is the necessary focus when studying the background. Therefore, if the background lacks a close relationship with the characters and events, it can be called a useless burden (無用의 長物). Consequently, backgrounds that vitalize characters and events come in two styles: often, the background proceeds along parallel lines similar to the characters and events, or sometimes it functions entirely oppositely, achieving only the effect of contrast. For example, when describing something like war, expressing a storm, which cannot specifically be designated as background, or when a father and son part, choosing a gloomy scene of continuous rain (霖雨霏霏) as the background. Conversely, when describing a tragic situation, a splendid place or time might be chosen, and when recounting a bright and upright event, a gloomy and stagnant background might be used. Whatever background is used, one must deeply investigate how much it vitalizes <135> the characters and events. And next is

Eighth, Classification.
One must try classifying. If one were to classify novels meticulously based on their nature, the number would be limitless, but broadly, they can be divided into two types. The first is the novel of human sentiment (人情小說), and the second can be called the romance novel (傳奇小說). Romance novels are characterized by events in the work being mysterious (不可思議), often things that do not happen in our actual human society, and the main characters also possess personalities not commonly seen. Furthermore, as novels, interest (興味) is prioritized above all else. However, in novels of human sentiment, there are no mysterious points in the events, the characters' personalities are ordinary ones we usually see, and moreover, as novels, the focus is placed on vitalizing character just as much as providing interest. Distinguishing according to these standards, one must determine whether the novel I am reading is a novel of human sentiment or a romance novel. And

Ninth is Dialogue.
What is it like? Generally, among literary works, novels and plays (戱曲) contain the most dialogue. The skillfulness or clumsiness of the dialogue can be considered a major gateway for evaluating the merit of novels and plays. First, are there unnecessary parts in the dialogue, places that could be omitted without issue? In other words, is every single word and phrase related to character and connected to the development of events? Or are there useless phrases that could be removed? Judging this allows one to determine the quality of the dialogue, of course. Also, one must see whether the tone of the dialogue (語調) is appropriate for the character. Does a lower-class character use the tone of a middle-class character, or a middle-class character use the tone of an upper-class character? In other words, do the speaker and the tone match their social status and circumstances? If an unsuitable tone is used, even if the words are golden rules (金條玉科), it cannot be approved. Another point not to be forgotten is whether the dialogue is too long or concise, whether there is dialect (方言) or not, and if there is dialect, whether it is necessary for the situation. It is certainly worth paying attention to that. Next,

Tenth is Style (作體).
Style is the nature of the entire novel; in other words, just as apples (苹果), pears (梨實), <136> or tangerines (柑子) have the same general shape and form as fruit, but when tasted, their flavors differ, so too do numerous novels have a distinct flavor when read. Even if two people write about the same facts, the flavor will naturally differ according to their writing style. Similarly, some writers have a calm (穩靜) style, some have an ingenious (奇拔) style, some have a humorous (詼諧) style, and some have a tragic (悲痛) style, each possessing a distinct characteristic flavor. When reading a novel, one should savor and ponder (玩味咀嚼) it well to consider what kind of writing style, or *jakche* (作體), it possesses. At this point is

Eleventh, Comparative Study.
After reading a novel, another thing to study is comparative analysis. Comparing and studying two works of the same kind is by no means a fruitless endeavor. The reader should not blindly follow (盲從) the author's fame (盛名) or others' empty praise (虛讃), being swayed without understanding (無理解), independent viewpoint (無主觀), or reason (無理由) just because others say so-and-so. Instead, one must find the true value of the novel based on one's own reasoning and perspective, under adequate understanding. With this, the main points of the method for studying novels should be clear, so I will now lay down my pen (擱筆). In the next issue (次號), as promised (約束), I intend to discuss the outline of drama (戱曲) and briefly explain (畧說) the position and circumstances of all the theater troupes (劇團) in Joseon (朝鮮), whether they are called New School (新派) or Old School (舊派). -End- <137>

**Summary**

This article, a continuation of a series on how to study novels, outlines key analytical points: examining the background's clarity, detail, local color, and its function in vitalizing characters and events (either through parallelism or contrast); classifying novels into 'novels of human sentiment' (realistic) or 'romance novels' (adventure/fantastical); evaluating dialogue for necessity, relevance, character appropriateness (social class), length, and use of dialect; identifying the overall writing style or 'flavor' (e.g., calm, witty, humorous, tragic); and finally, conducting comparative studies with an independent critical perspective, avoiding blind acceptance of fame or praise. The author concludes by promising a future article on drama, including the status of 'New School' and 'Old School' theater troupes in Korea.

From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it reflects the burgeoning field of modern literary criticism in early colonial Korea (1920). Published in the influential magazine *Gaebyeok*, it shows intellectuals actively engaging with and defining methodologies for analyzing literature, adapting Western concepts like 'local color' and genre classification (realism vs. romance) for a Korean context. The emphasis on independent critical thinking ("自己의 理由와 自己의 主見") against "盛名" (fame) or "虛讃" (empty praise) subtly advocates for intellectual autonomy. The planned discussion on contemporary Korean theater (신파/구파) further highlights the engagement with modern cultural forms developing under colonial rule.

第七은 背景
이다. 背景의 如何한 것은 小說槪要에도 말하엿거니와 또 한번 다시 말하면 小說中의 事件과 人物의 活動하는 場所와 時間을 가르키는 것이 背景이다. 엇더한 場所에서 엇더한 時日에 事件이 發生되엇다는 것을 敍述하는 것이 背景의 責任이다. 그러면 背景을 讀破하는데는 第一은 明瞭이 繪畵的으로 되엇는지 或은 朦朧한 狀態로 曖昧하게 記述하엿는지 第二는 背景이 細微하게 敍述되엇는지 或은 그 大要의 急所만 排置하엿는지 第三은 世間 普通의 場所가 表現되엇는지 或은 엇더한 地方의 特色되는 山川이나 風景 卽 地方的 色彩(Local Colol)를 採用하엿는지 이러한 點을 注意하지 아니치 못할 것이니 元來 背景의 根本 目的은 作中의 人物과 事件을 躍動함에 잇는 것이니 다못 背景이 記載해 잇슬 뿐으로 人物과 事件에 무슨 깁흔 關係가 업고 아모 效用이 업게 되엇는지 또는 多少의 關係가 잇다 하면 엇더한 程度까지 人物과 事件을 躍動케 하는지 이것을 細密이 監察하는 것이 背景을 硏究하는 必要한 焦點이다. 이러함으로 背景이 萬一 人物과 事件에 가까운 關係가 업스면 無用의 長物이라 할 수 잇는 것이다. 까닭에 人物과 事件을 躍動케 하는 背景은 두가지 樣式이 잇는 것이니 往往이 背景도 人物과 事件에 類似한 平行線으로 進就하는 수도 잇고 或은 全혀 反對로 對照의 效用만 取得하는 일도 잇다. 一例를 들면 戰爭가튼 것을 敍述할 적에 特히 背景으로 稱定치 못한 暴風雨를 表現하는 것이나 또는 父子가 離別할 때에 背景으로 霖雨霏霏한 陰鬱할 景色을 取하는 일이 잇고 또는 正反對로 悲境을 敍述할 때에 華麗한 場所나 時日을 取하는 일도 잇고 光明正大한 事件을 陳述할 때에 陰鬱沈滯한 背景을 使用하는 일도 잇는 것이다. 엇더한 背景을 쓰던지 그 人物과 그 事件을＜135＞ 얼마나 躍動케 하는지 그것을 깁히 究察치 아니하지 못할 것이다. 그리고 다음에는

第十은 作體이다
作體는 小說 全體의 性質이니 다시 말하면 苹果나 梨實이나＜136＞ 柑子가 그 果實된 模樣과 形狀이 同一하나 비롯오 이것을 입에 물 때에는 그 風味가 各各 다른 것과 가티 數多의 小說도 이것을 읽어 보면 一種의 風味가 다른 것이다. 두 사람이 各各 同一한 事實을 쓰더래도 그 作風에 딸아 風味가 다를 것은 勿論이다. 이와 가티 엇더한 作家는 穩靜한 作風을 가졋고 엇더한 作家는 奇拔한 作風을 가졋스며 엇더한 作家는 詼諧적 作風을 가졋고 엇더한 作家는 悲痛한 作風을 가져 그 各各 同一치 아니한 特味가 잇는 것이다. 一篇의 小說을 讀破할 때에 玩味咀嚼을 善爲하여 엇더한 作風 即 作體를 가졋는지 이것도 생각해둘 것이다. 이 際는
第十一 比較硏究이다
小說을 讀破한 後에 다시 한번 硏究해 볼 것은 比較硏究이다. 同種의 兩者를 比較하여 硏究하는 것도 決코 無爲의 일이 아니다. 讀者는 作者의 盛名이나 他人의 虛讃에 盲從하여 無理解와 無主觀 無理由로 그저 남이 엇더하다 하면 그대로 牽引될 것이 아니라 自己의 理由와 自己의 主見으로 相當한 理解下에서 그 小說의 眞正한 價値를 차져내어야 하겟도다. 小說硏究法은 이만하면 그 大旨는 알겟기에 이제 擱筆하고 次 號에는 約束한 것과 가티 戱曲의 槪要를 論及하여 所謂 朝鮮에 新派이니 舊派이니 하는 모든 劇團이 엇더한 地位와 處地에 잇는 것까지 畧說하고자 하다.-完-＜137＞
