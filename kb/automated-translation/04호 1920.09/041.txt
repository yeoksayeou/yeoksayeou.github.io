What Kind of Rule is This?

I have a peculiar habit of enjoying the company of young children more than others, finding supreme joy (無上의 悅樂) in laughing and talking with them. This happened when I was staying at Chŏngyangsa (正陽寺) Temple on Kŭmgangsan (金剛山) Mountain. A group had come from a nearby village to hold a Buddhist service (齋). As one or two days passed, a six-year-old girl from that group and I became inseparable friends (莫逆의 親友). Whenever she saw me, she would throw down the spoon (匙) she was holding and follow me, and whenever I saw her, I would forget everything else and was just happy.

One day, as usual, I went up to the Hyŏlsŏngnu Pavilion (歇醒樓) of the temple and was chatting and laughing with the girl when her mother came up. At this, I immediately went down from the pavilion and moved away to the front of the Dharma hall (法堂) on the other side. Seeing this, the girl shouted, "Why are you going like that?" and called out repeatedly, "Come here and play again! Play! Play!" But I couldn't go. As long as the mother of my dear girl was there, I could not go. I could only silently watch the girl's eager anticipation (苦待하는 樣).

Seeing that I wouldn't come even when called, the girl ran towards me, grabbed my hand, and pulled, saying, "Let's go over there and play. Why aren't you going? What reason is there not to go?" But I couldn't go. Still, I couldn't go. The girl pleaded again, "Please go quickly," looking up at my lips, as if demanding an answer. So I said, "I cannot go. It is the rule (法) that I cannot go if another person is there."

The girl seemed unexpectedly surprised. "The rule that you can't go if a person is there! Where is such a rule (法)? Come on, let's play together."

"Where is such a rule (法)?" That question from an innocent child (赤子)! What answer could I possibly give? I couldn't answer. I could never answer.

Ah, gentlemen of society (社會諸公)! I am but a small child. What kind of rule is that? Who decided on that rule? Could such a rule possibly exist in this wide world? Please give me a definite answer (確實한 解答). Are men and women not all the same people?<26>
<17-26>

**Summary**

The author recounts an experience at Chŏngyangsa temple where his close bond with a six-year-old girl is interrupted by the arrival of her mother. Due to unspoken social rules regarding the separation of unrelated men and women (*naeoe*), the author feels compelled to distance himself, despite the child's pleas. The child's innocent question, "Where is such a rule?", highlights the perceived absurdity and unnaturalness of this social constraint. The author concludes by adopting the child's perspective, directly questioning society about this "rule" and affirming the equality of men and women. For historians, this article offers a poignant glimpse into the strict gender segregation norms (*naeoe*) prevalent in early 20th-century Korea and the burgeoning critical consciousness questioning such traditional constraints, framed through the powerful device of a child's innocence challenging ingrained social customs. The setting within a Buddhist temple adds another layer, contrasting spiritual ideals with restrictive social practices.

이것이 무슨 法입닛가
나는 남달리 幼少年과의 交際를 질기며 그네로 더블어 웃고 말함을 無上의 悅樂으로 아는 癖이 잇다. 金剛山 正陽寺에 安居할 時의 事이다. 該寺 近村으로서 齋 올리려 온 一行이 有한데 一日二日 지내는 間에 그 一行中 今年 六歲의 女兒 一人은 나와 莫逆의 親友가 되엇다. 그래서 그는 날만 보면 잡앗던 술가락(匙)을 던지고 딸아 나서며 나는 그만 對하면 이것저것을 다 잇고 그저 조왓섯다. 一日은 例와 가티 該寺 歇醒樓에 올라 그 女兒와 가티 談笑하는 中 그 女兒의 母親이 登樓함으로 나는 그만 그 樓를 下하야 저편 法堂 門압까지 몸을 避하얏다. 이것을 본 女兒는 『웨 그러케 가느냐고』 소리를 지르며 이리와 또 노라요! 노라요! 하며 連하야 부른다. 그러나 나는 갈 수가 업다. 나의 親愛하는 그 女兒의 그 母親이 잇기까지는 나는 갈 수가 업다. 다못 그 女兒의 苦待하는 樣을 黙然히 볼 뿐이다. 불러도 오지 안음을 본 女兒도 다름박질로 나를 向하야 오더니 내 손을 잡아 이끌며 『저리 놀러가서요. 웨 안이 가요 안이 갈 것이 무엇이예요?』 한다. 그러나 나는 갈 수가 업다. 如前히 갈 수가 업다. 女兒는 또 다시 『어서가셔요』 하며 나의 입술을 쳐다본다. 그러면 對答이라도 하라 함이다. 그래서 『나는 못간다. 거긔에 다른 사람이 잇스면 나는 못가는 法이다』 하얏다. 女兒는 意外에 놀라는 듯이 『사람이 잇스면 못가는 法! 그럴 法이 어대 잇셔요? 어서 가티 놀아요』 한다. 「그런 法이 어대 잇셔요?」 하는 赤子의 그 무름! 무엇이라 對答하여야 可할가. 나는 對答할 수가 업섯다. 永永히 對答할 수가 업섯다.
아아 社會諸公! 나는 어린 兒이외다. 그 法이 무슨 法입닛가. 누가 定한 法입닛가. 世上 天下에 그런 法도 잇슬가요? 나에게 確實한 解答을 주시오. 男子와 女子는 다 가튼 사람이 안이라 합닛가.＜26＞
