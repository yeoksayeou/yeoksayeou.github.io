讀者交情欄 (Reader Exchange Column)

To Mr. Ch'o Ki-yŏng of Hagomyŏn, Myŏngch'ŏn County! - I will answer your question regarding the Publishing Law.

(Question 1) Article 3 of the Publishing Law states, “When publishing documents or drawings, two bound copies shall be attached and submitted to the Ministry of Home Affairs three days prior to the date of issuance, excluding the number of days required for arrival.” Regarding this, are there no procedures such as attaching a separate manuscript and requiring permission, but simply submitting the two bound copies to the Governor-General after publication?

(Answer) What you are asking about is the Publishing Law applied to Japanese people. For Japanese people, publishing without permission was allowed, but Koreans were not permitted to use this provision; there is a separate Publishing Law specifically for Koreans.
Article 2 of the Publishing Law applicable only to Koreans states, “When intending to publish documents or drawings, the author or their successor and the publisher shall jointly seal and attach the manuscript, applying for permission to the Minister of the Interior via the local governor (in Hansŏng Prefecture, this shall be the Police Superintendent General).” Thus, of course, you must attach the manuscript and obtain permission. In the same article, Hansŏng can be considered Kyŏngsŏng, the local governor as the provincial governor, and the Minister of the Interior as the Governor-General.

(Question 2) In Article 5 of the Publishing Law, does the term "publication notification" refer to the notification mentioned in Article 3?

(Answer) Of course, that is correct. However, that provision is not used for us Korean people.

(Question 3) In Article 2 of the Subscription Publishing Law, should the term "competent local government office" be considered the Provincial Office?

(Answer) That is correct.

(Question 4) In Article 4 of the Subscription Publishing Law, how should the security deposit payment form be made? And is the subscription price limited to the price per copy or the total price of the publication?

(Answer) Please refer to a collection of legal forms (書式大全) for the security deposit payment form format, or if it is not available there, inquire at a scrivener's office (代書所). Also, the subscription price would be considered the price per copy.

(Question 5) Your question is unclear, so I cannot answer it.

(Question 6) What is the format for the request form for the refund of the security deposit related to the Subscription Publishing Law?

(Answer) Please inquire about that at the relevant government office. As it is not a commonly used form, few people know about it.

Mullim (文林 - Forest of Literature)

To the People of Kaebyŏk, from Yu Nyŏn-mu, Wŏnju

The creation (開闢) from Ja, Cha, Chuk; the sun hangs mid-sky, specters gradually flee; light shines on border gardens, bearing no small responsibility.
Emulating the strength and compliance of heaven and earth; spring returns to secluded valleys, plants and trees vie anew; awakening the Tan(gun) tribe, shouldering a momentous instrument.
Hee!
Two years since founding, long out in the world; quickly looking up in reverence, let it spread without a moment's delay. The fresh civilization of the 20th century, then absorb its essence; encompassing past and present, riding the ancestral wave.
Ten issues published, why so slow in progress? Greeting with folded hands, surely hoping for eternal existence for ten thousand years. The ingrained habits of four thousand years of history, then root them out; considering East and West, taking the good <87>
Selecting the essence, with righteous pen denouncing the treacherous and monstrous, losing their souls; appearing and disappearing in the sea of delusion, encountering a compassionate vessel (慈航).
Discarding the negative? Brilliant writings arrayed in the Forest of Literature (文林), opening the eyes of the foolish; wandering the dark crossroads (昏衢), obtaining a bright candle (明燭).

And seeking the way, treating the groaning with the electric needle of the world of speech and thought.
And guiding the way, shattering the snoring with the thunderbolt hammer of the academic and literary arts department.

The sickness of the bowels (病膓), darkly pondering the civilization/barbarity of society; who but you can reform and innovate?
The stubborn brain (頑腦), everything depends on your rise or fall; enlightenment and guidance rely solely on you.

Arouse fervent zeal, spread the great bird's wings and fly far upwards; with full spirit and sincerity.
Industriously strive, stretch the thoroughbred's legs and advance briskly; with a single-minded purpose fixed.

Truly without end, daring to express heartfelt loyalty, I humbly hope
Hold fast and do not change; please publish this in the remaining space.

Ah, My Dear! By Kim Yong-gwang (女史 - Female Writer)

Seeing off my dear who is going to study abroad, right there
Ah! I called out, but my dear
Who departs for the vast sea of learning (萬里學海)!
Be a healthy man, the master of the laurel wreath.
Ah! My dear, who leaves without looking back, in my heart
There will be an infinite drought.
The steps of my dear walking briskly, those steps
Before them, the light of glory shall shine.
Ah! If only I were a man
I would have climbed the mountain of learning (學山) like that.
Ah! If only I were born in the West
I would have floated on the sea of learning (學海) like that?
Ah! Outside this gate, there may be many schools
All schools might be eagerly awaiting me
But this body remains only here...
Ah! My dear! Please take pity, think of me
This body that is degenerating
May it at least be
A solace for sorrow.
Night of March 28th, 4254 (1921), in my prison-like boudoir (監房閨中).

Boudoir Lament (閨中嘆) By Chu Ha-sun (女史 - Female Writer)

Our family, hemiplegic (半身不遂)
This body too is one of the disabled (不具者).
In this world bustling with life
What will become of our family?
No, what will become of this body?
The daily gain is only forgetfulness
With what shall I teach them in the future,
Those children in their tender state (水態)?
Does this petty drudgery (小小한 이 雜役) become teaching material for children's instruction?
Is this disabled life (不具의 이 生活) the joy of human life?
Good teaching materials for children's instruction and
Good doctors and medicine for this disabled person
Must be plentiful where my dear is!!
Ah! This body remains only in this place
Dreaming amidst the red dust (紅塵)
Groaning in disability (不具에 呻吟하노나)!!
Among humans, "Do not be born a woman's body (莫作婦人身)"
"A lifetime of sorrow and joy depends on others (一生 苦樂을 依他人)" they said
It was said about me.
Dawn of March 28th, 4254 (1921), on a night deep with sky, in my detention-place boudoir (留置場이 閨中).

Recent Feelings (近感) By Yi In-jong, Kangnŭng

Reading "As the first step of rebirth and reform (新生改良), I propose [reform of] Korean clothing, food, and housing," I could not help but leap for joy, and at the same time, I consider it a unique pleasure to have the opportunity to briefly state the long-held ambition of a frog in a well (井蛙) and the narrow view of a one-eyed person (隻眼). (This is by no means a criticism of the original article; referring to the previous issue is advisable.)
Following the editor's note in the Reader Exchange Column, I wish to state my humble thoughts straightforwardly.

(A) Regarding Clothing Reform

(a) Men's clothing should all be gray or black, but it seems best for the collar (襟) and socks (襪) to be white. Black clothing is praised unanimously, but domestic dyes are completely nonexistent. If we take the gray color from bamboo leaves and use it as a substitute dye, wouldn't it kill two birds with one stone? (However, formal attire is an exception.)
For 500 years, Buddhist monks and the like have been treated with contempt, but now that we realize this is a vile custom and evil practice, imitating them might not be objectionable. (Your humble servant is not a monk who has returned to secular life.) <88>
(b) Wide trousers (筒袴) seem to have aesthetic drawbacks in appearance; it might be better to make the lower part narrower than before and sew on a single lapel (單衽).
(c) Abolish the chogori tie-string pouch (胴着 - chŏgori?) entirely and instead create a pocket (物置*) on the upper front part of the trousers.
(d) It is an undeniable fact that the amount spent on the heads of 20 million people in one year reaches a colossal sum. Prohibit all kinds of hats like wheat-leaf hats (麥葉子), fedoras (中折帽), and others, and make a Korean traditional hat (朝鮮笠) in the shape of a straight-brimmed straw hat (一文字麥槀 帽形) to wear instead. This might be good. However, hats like the island-made ones we produce (吾輩製作島打帽) are exceptions. (I will leave this to the research of hat makers and omit further details.) It is not only wrong to blindly imitate foreign systems of clothing, food, and housing. If the nation's unique characteristics disappear, we will also gradually fall into the plight of livelihood difficulties, which is deplorable.
(e) For women's clothing, white top and gray (or black) bottom seems best.

(B) Regarding the Housing System (假屋制度)

(a) Your 2nd, 4th, and 8th architectural reform proposals seem to set the standard for the middle class and above; they don't seem universal. Of course, regarding the desire for housing, even a castle in the air (氣蜃樓) would not be unwelcome...
(b) Make windows slightly wider (limited to rural village reform).
(c) Building warehouses slightly farther away?
(d) Demolish stables next to the kitchen and build them slightly farther away (limited to rural village reform).
(e) Build houses slightly higher than before.
Addendum: Regarding foodstuffs and the housing system, Mr. Pak Tal-sŏng's opinions have many plausible points, but realistically, if one observes the situation in rural villages, they have fallen into a pitiful state currently and view this world with pessimism. How could the poor without constant means (無恒産) find the leisure to create a paradise? Who would not prefer rich food and delicacies (膏粱珍味)? In the article on foodstuffs, the complexity of food and drink is rightly mentioned, but asking "Why not eat meat porridge?" (何不食肉米) and distinguishing between rich and poor – does this not conversely encourage vanity and luxury (浮華奢侈)? The urgent task at hand is to banish the demon of extravagance (侈魔) and cultivate practicality (實方), which is crucial. Gladly accepting coarse clothes and simple food (粗衣蔬食) is not something I dare ask for, but it is certainly what I desire. (With the premise of eating well and dressing warmly several years later, enduring this hardship and that hardship...)

Boarding the Ferry (渡船에 登하야) By Yi Myŏng-ho, Ich'ŏn

Accompanied by 4 or 5 comrades (同志), after a walk, I boarded the Sŏnghojin Ferry (星湖津 渡船), upstream on the Imjin River (臨津江). The flat water surface, stimulated by fierce winds, became blue waves (碧波) and white waves (白浪), striking enough to shake the boat's hull. Entrusting myself to this boat, I could not help but recall passing through this river one month ago and marvel at the constant cycle (循環) of Heaven's Way (天道). At that time, the vast ice covering the entire water surface was as clear as glass (琉璃), yet people and animals could cross over it without any fear of danger, as solid as an iron fortress (鐵甕城). Twelve days later, when I happened to pass this river again, the ice had melted for about one *kan* (間 - unit of length) from the ferry landing, allowing passage on foot barely, but passage for horses and livestock was absolutely impossible. Today, arriving for the third time, the solid ice has completely dissolved, making it impossible to find even a remaining fragment, and only vast blue waves (滄波萬頃) spread boundlessly before my eyes. How could someone who only understood the solidity of the ice that people and animals crossed a month ago have predicted that ferry boats would navigate these vast blue waves today? It was the warm spring sun, the gentle breeze, the great nature of the universe, the great miracle of the Creator (化翁) that could melt the solid ice and form the natural state of today. Ah— today's society— the constraints diverging from morality, and today's world— the bonds of domination (覇絆) resulting from tyranny— I imagine a time will come when, under the great trend of justice and humanity, they will be eradicated and liberated, just like the solid ice melted in the warm spring day, and a bright world (光明한 天地) will appear. As I was thinking this, the boat had already arrived at the ferry landing.

The Rich Do Not Enter the Gate of Heaven (富는 天國의 門에 不入함) By Chu Chae-nam, Hwasun

The New Testament, Gospel of Matthew, Chapter 19, Verse 24 says: <90>
"Again I tell you, it is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle (針孔) than for someone who is rich to enter the kingdom of God (天國)." Whenever we read this, we cannot help but clap our hands with satisfaction. The "eye of a needle" refers to the name of a small gate in Jerusalem; a camel carrying luggage cannot pass through this small gate called the "eye of a needle." It is said that it is harder for a rich person to enter Heaven than for a camel to pass through the small gate called the "Eye of a Needle," meaning that the rich cannot enter Heaven.
We do not intend to use the tone of yon pastor (牧師) to speak of Heaven or talk about God's judgment (審判). However, what the pastor calls Heaven is not in Hell or the underworld; it is right here in the present world. If a person embodies the tenets of religion and can practice universal love (博愛), their surroundings will always become a land of much fortune and blessing. Therefore, even amidst utter desolation, one can still enjoy the bright heavenly sun; even if death presses closely like a ghastly shadow covering both eyes, one can still be peaceful and calm. When we look at this theory of Heaven and the creed of religion, we realize there is truly truth beyond words. That is, our success must be sought within a realm of happiness; in other words, the laurel crown (桂冠) of success can undoubtedly only be obtained after entering the gate of Heaven.
A Western proverb praises success, saying, "What is permanently good is success." We too, of course, do not desire success that is not permanently good. Then, permanently good success lies in firmly upholding morality (道德). Another Western proverb says, "There has never been a great person who did not firmly uphold morality," and also, "Morality allows us to bear our greatness, glory, and happiness." Thus, we should be convinced of the great and mighty power of morality. Therefore, only a person of virtue (有德君子) will know the way to success. A person of virtue is what religious figures call God's beloved child (神의 赤字), qualified to enter the gate of Heaven. Therefore, the laurel crown of success will only flourish after entering the gate of Heaven.
What is stated above is truly not empty talk from a desk. In essence, the gate of Heaven is not solely the possession of Christians; it is a gateway of life that politicians, soldiers, scholars, and businessmen can all pass through. However, the Gospel of Matthew says it is harder for the rich to enter the gate of Heaven than for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle. Does this then mean it is a definitive judgment that the rich cannot enter the gate of Heaven? Or is it the conclusion that the rich cannot obtain the laurel crown of success? No! How could life permit such illogicality?
Then what did the Gospel of Matthew mean? It refers to the embodiments of selfish desire (私利私慾), the modern rich who would even attempt to take others' lives for the sake of their own property, that is, the cruel and heartless P'als in human form (人面獸心), not the wealthy who possess moral sense (道念). However, if people believe in the omnipotence of gold (黃金萬能), make acquiring wealth the sole purpose of life, and are willing to throw themselves into fire with gunpowder to obtain wealth, how could such types ever enter the gate of Heaven, nay, even glimpse its threshold?
Therefore, that the rich cannot enter the gate of Heaven is an immutable truth for all ages. However, in modern times, the power of money contributes not insignificantly to the prosperity of society and the happiness of individuals. If the wealthy could cultivate moral sense and study the way to use wealth virtuously, then no matter how firmly locked the lintel of Heaven's gate might be, it would open naturally without effort. (End)

**Summary**

This "Reader Exchange Column" from the May 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine features a diverse mix of content. It begins with the editor answering reader questions, notably clarifying the discriminatory dual publishing laws under Japanese colonial rule, where Koreans faced stricter censorship (requiring pre-publication manuscript approval) compared to Japanese residents. The column also includes advice on literature, reader-submitted poems and prose reflecting themes of national identity, personal struggles (especially women's confined roles), aspirations for progress, reflections on nature and societal change, and philosophical musings on wealth and morality based on Christian scripture. It concludes with a list of contemporary Korean magazines, their formats, page counts, and prices.

From a historical perspective, the article is highly revealing. The explicit discussion of separate, discriminatory publishing laws for Koreans versus Japanese highlights the legal mechanisms of colonial control and censorship aimed at suppressing Korean expression. The readers' contributions, particularly the poems by women expressing frustration with societal constraints ("prison-like boudoir," "disabled life," "depend on others") and the essay advocating for practical lifestyle reforms (clothing, housing) while critiquing blind imitation of foreign ways, offer valuable insights into the social consciousness, cultural anxieties, and aspirations for modernity and self-determination among Koreans during this period. The list of magazines provides a snapshot of the burgeoning, yet heavily regulated, Korean print media landscape in the early 1920s.

明川郡 下古面 楚基英君!- 무로신 出版法에 對하야는 答하겟습니다.
(1問) 出版法 第 3條에 『文書 圖畵을 出版한 時는 發行의 日로부터 到達할만한 日數를 除한 3日前에 製本 2部를 添付하야 內務省에 屆出할 事』라 하엿스니 此에 對하야는 별로히 原稿를 添付하야 許可를 要하는 等 手續이 無하고 但只 出版한 後 該 製本 2部만 添付하야 總督에게 屆出만 하는지요?
(答) 君의 무로신 바는 日本人에게 適用하는 出版法입니다. 日本人에게는 許可업시 出版을 하도록 하엿지마는 朝鮮人에게는 그러한 것을 使用치 못하게 하고 따로이 朝鮮人만 使用하는 出版法이 잇습니다.
朝鮮人만 適用하는 出版法 第 2條에 『文書 圖畫를 出版하랴고 할 時는 著作者 又는 그 相續者 及 發行者가 連印하고 稿本을 添附하여 地方長官 (漢城府에서는 警視總監으로 함)을 經由하야 內部大臣에게 許可를 申請할 事』라고 하엇스니 勿論 稿本을 添附하야 許可를 어더야 하는 것이올시다. 同 條文에 漢城은 京城이고 地方長官은 道長官이며 內部大臣은 總督으로 看做할 수가 잇습니다.
(2問) 出版法 第 5條 中 出版屆라는 것은 第 3條의 屆出을 謂한 것이온지요?
(答) 勿論 그런 것이올시다. 그러나 그것은 우리 朝鮮 사람에게는 使用치 아니하는 것이올시다.
(3問) 豫約出版法 第 2條 中 管轄 地方官廳이라는 것은 道廳으로 看做할 것이온지요.
(答) 그러합니다.
(4問) 豫約出版法 第 4條 中 保證金 納付書는 如何히 하며 豫約定價는 一部 代價에 限한 것이온지 出版物 總數 代價에 限한 것이온지요.
(答) 保證金 納付書式은 書式大全을 보시던지 거긔에 萬一 업거던 代書所에 問議하여 보서요. 또 豫約定價는 一部에 對한 定價로 看做하겟습니다.
(5問) 은 問意가 分明치 못하야 答치 못하나이다.
(6問) 豫約出版法에 對한 保證金의 還付請求書式은 如何하온지요?
(答) 그것은 所管 官廳에 問議하여 보시기 바랍니다. 常用하는 書式이 아닌 故로 아는 사람이 別로 업습니다.

渡船에 登하야 伊川 李命浩

同志 4, 5人을 伴하야 散步의 餘에 臨津江의 上流인 星湖津 渡船에 登하엿다. 平坦한 水面은 狂風의 刺激함을 受하야 碧波도 되고 白浪도 되여 船體가 動搖할만치 突擊을 한다. 此 船上에 一身을 寄한 余는 只今부터 1個月 前에 此 江을 通過하던 事를 回想하고 天道의 循環이 有常함을 感歎치 아니치 못하엿다. 其時 水面의 全部에 亙巨한 結氷은 淸淨하기 琉璃와 如하나 人畜이 其上에 通行하여도 何等 危險의 慮가 頓無하야 鐵甕城과 가티 鞏固하엿섯다. 其後 12日을 經하야 再次 此 江을 通過케 되엿슬 시는 渡船場부터 約 1間假量이나 解氷되여 步行으로 겨우 通過는 하엿스나 馬畜의 通行은 絶對 不能이엇섯다. 今日 第 3次 來到에는 鞏固하든 結氷이 一切 溶解되어 一片의 殘在도 覓得하기 不能하고 滄波萬頃이 眼前에 浩蕩할 뿐이다. 1個月 前에 人畜이 通行하던 結氷의 鞏固함만 본 了解로서야 어찌 今日의 滄波萬頃에 渡船의 通行이 될 줄을 豫想하엿스리요. 그것은 載陽한 春日溫暖한 和風, 宇宙의 大自然, 化翁의 大神妙로써 結氷의 鞏固한 것을 能히 溶解하야 天然한 今日의 現狀을 形成하엿섯다. 아- 今日의 社會- 道德에 어그러진 制裁와 今日의 世界- 橫暴로 말미어 된 覇絆은 正義와 人道의 大勢下에서 溫和한 春日에 鞏固하던 結氷이 溶解됨과 가티 撲滅되고 解脫되어 光明한 天地가 出顯할 時期가 잇슬 줄을 想像하는 中에 船體는 벌서 渡船場에 到着하엿다.

富는 天國의 門에 不入함 和順 朱在南

新約全書 馬太傳 第19章 第24節에 曰＜90＞
『다시 爾曹에게 말하노니 駱駝가 바늘구멍(針孔)으로 나가는 것이 富者가 天國의 門에 入하기보다 易하다.』 하엿스니 吾人은 此를 讀할때마다 拍掌快哉함을 不禁하도다. 『바늘구멍』이라함은 『예루살넴』에 잇는 小門의 名稱이니 짐을 실꼬 가는 駱駝는 이 『바늘구멍』이라하는 小門을 通過하기 不可能하도다. 富者가 天國에 入함은 駱駝가 『針孔』이란 小門을 通過하기보다 難하다 하엿스니 卽 富者는 天國에 入함을 不得한다는 意味로다.
吾人은 저-牧師의 口調를 用하야 天國을 說하며 神의 審判을 말코자 함은 아니올시다. 그러나 牧師의 所謂 天國이라 함은 地獄이나 黃泉에 잇는 것이 아니요. 곳 現世에 잇습니다. 人이 宗敎의 佀條를 奉軆하야 能히 博愛의 實를 擧할진대 그-周圍는 恒常 多幸多福한 天地로 될 것이올시다. 그래서 四顧暗慘한 중에 잇슬지라도 오히려 熙熙한 天日을 樂할지며 死는 愴然히 逼迫하야 慘憺한 黑影으로 我의 雙眼을 掩할지라도 오히려 安心平然할 것이올시다. 吾人은 이 天國說을 보며 宗敎의 信條를 볼때에 實로 言外의 眞理엇슴을 알겟습니다. 卽 吾人의 成功은 반듯이 幸福場裏에서 求得할지니 換言하면 成功의 桂冠은 卽 天國의 門에 入한 然後에야 비롯오 可得하게 됨을 無疑합니다.
西諺에 成功을 嘆美하야 曰 『永久히 善한 것은 即 成功이다.』하엿스니 吾人도 勿論 永久히 善치 아니한 成功은 渴望치 아니합니다. 그리면 永久히 善한 成功은 곳 道德을 堅守함에 在하니 西諺에 又日 『일즉 道德을 堅守치 안하고 偉大한 人物된 者업다』 하엇스며 又 曰『道德은 吾人의 偉大, 光榮, 幸福을 擔케 한다.』 하엿스니 道德의 힘 偉且大함을 確信할지로다. 그럼으로 有德君子라야 오즉 成功의 道理를 알 것이니 有德君子라함은 即 宗敎家의 所謂 天國의 門에 入할만한 資格을 가진 神의 赤字올시다. 그럼으로 成功의 桂冠은 天國의 門을 入한 後라야 비롯오 亨通할 것이올시다.
以上 所述은 實로 桌上의 空談이 아니올시다. 要컨대 天國의 門은 獨히 基督敎徒의 所有뿐 아니라 政治家나 軍人이나 學者나 實業家나 一齊히 通過할 수 잇는 人生의 關門이올시다. 그런데 馬太傳에 富者가 天國의 門에 入하기는 駱駝의 針孔에 通過하기보다 有難하다 하엿스니 그러면 卽 富者는 天國의 門에 入함을 不得한다는 斷案일가. 或은 富者는 成功의 月桂冠을 得할 수 업다는 論斷인가. 否*人生에 어찌 이러한 沒論理를 許하엿겟슴니까.
그러면 馬太傳에는 何를 謂하엿슬가. 曰 私利私慾의 權化, 自己의 財産을 爲하야는 他人의 生命까지라도 奪코자하는 現代의 富者 卽 慘忍酷薄한 人面獸心의 徒를 指함이요. 道念을 有한 富豪를 總稱함은 아니올시다. 그러나 人이 만일 黃金萬能을 信하야 一是致富로서 人生의 歸趣로하며 富를 得하기 爲하야는 火藥을 품고 장작불에라도 投身自入하는 徒輩들 어찌 天國의 門에 入할 수, 아니 門前에도 비처 볼 수 업슬 것이올시다.
그럼으로 富는 天國의 門에 入함을 不得한다함은 萬世 不可動할 眞理올시다. 그러나 現代에는 金力이 能히 社會의 繁榮과 個人의 幸福에 及하는 배 不尠라 하겟습니다. 萬若 富한 者가 能히 道念을 修養하야써 富를 善用할 道를 講究할진대 天國의 門楣는 如何히 堅鎖하엿슬지라도 無爲而化로 自開할진저 (完)
