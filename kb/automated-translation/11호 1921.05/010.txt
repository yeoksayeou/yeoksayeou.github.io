Opening Words: Pleading with the Esteemed Intellectuals

Anyone with ears must have heard. Anyone with eyes must have seen. Anyone with feelings must have felt it. The atmosphere of our society has now truly lost its tense spirit and, like a bow shot and unstrung, barely preserves its pitiful existence amidst a lack of vitality and inspiration. Even the patriots and intellectuals (志士識者) of former days (昔日), who used to clench their fists and rack their brains at the slightest thought, are now reluctant to unfold their own statesmanship (經綸), busying themselves seeking lazy conformity following the dust motes of the commonplace cart. The general populace, their vitality diminished by some period of exhaustion and their inspiration cooled by the daily pronouncements (聲言), show a tendency, like frozen (凍) people standing hoping for warmth (陽氣), to dislike moving from the start. The observers (鑑察) and outsiders (外人) who were watching with ten or twenty eyes opened at once now freely offer the ridicule, "You folks are just so-so too," and the straightforward and innocent youth (靑年) spread voices of resentment (怨嗟) everywhere, saying, "The words chattered and the deeds done by these so-called intellectuals (所謂이 좀 識者) are nothing to speak of." Alas! Where now can the dignity (體面) of the Korean people (朝鮮民族) be found, and where now can the ever-warm spring of inspiration (感激) be drawn (汲)?

The old ones are still not awakened, and the young comrades are not yet established (그루(株)가 서지 못하얏도다). There are only the patriots and intellectuals of the middle class (中間階級의 志士識者). The initiators (發起者) of all currently situated projects (施設), the prime movers (主動者) of all pronouncements, the producers (做出者) of all issues (問題) – these middle-class patriots and intellectuals, they are all there is. They alone must once again become the creators (作成者) of a new atmosphere (新空氣), the source (源泉) of new inspiration (新感激). The seed once sown cannot but be reaped by the hand of the one who sowed it again. If they cannot do this, that is, if they themselves make noise saying 'we will do, we will do' but there is no final result (乃終), and if the final disgrace (最終의 汚辱) falls upon the entire Korean people (朝鮮民族 全體), how could you esteemed ones (諸位) possibly endure (忍爲) it? Of course, there would be no reason (理由) for that, surely.

Summer is an unusual (異常한) season (時候). Even the clenched fist may open, and even the stuck glue (?) may melt away. The hearts of the populace (人衆) may find it easier to become lax (解弛). The progress (進展) of any effort (努力) may become particularly difficult. Is this not precisely the time (時) for those with aspiration (有志兒) to truly rouse their spirit once (勵氣一番)? As a final word (最後의 一言), I plead (歎願) only with you, the esteemed intellectuals (識者諸位):

Esteemed ones (諸位)! Reconsider (再思) your slogan (標語)! The construction (建設) of a New Culture (新文化), and thereby the promotion of happiness (幸福增進) for the 20 million brothers (2千萬 兄弟).

Esteemed ones (諸位)! Reconsider (再思) your position (地位)! That of being the initiators (發起者) of all projects (施設), the prime movers (主動者) of all pronouncements (聲言), the producers (做出者) of all issues (問題).<1>

**Summary**

This editorial from the May 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* laments the pervasive apathy and lack of vitality gripping Korean society two years after the March 1st Movement. The author criticizes the intellectual class (志士識者), particularly the "middle class" intellectuals who initiated past movements and pronouncements, for losing their initial fervor, becoming complacent, and failing to lead. The editorial urges these intellectuals to remember their responsibility and their stated goals—building a "New Culture" (新文化) and promoting the happiness of the Korean people—and to reignite their efforts before disillusionment completely sets in, warning that failure reflects poorly on the entire nation. From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as it reflects the mood of stagnation and disillusionment that followed the initial burst of nationalist energy from the March 1st Movement. It highlights the internal pressures and criticisms faced by the nationalist intellectual leadership in the early 1920s, pointing to the challenges of sustaining momentum under colonial rule and managing public expectations.

누구나 귀를 가젓거던 들엇슬지요. 눈을 가젓거던 보앗슬지요. 情이 잇섯거던 느꼇슬 것이다. 우리 社會의 空氣는 이제야말로 緊張한 氣分을 일코 쏘고 노흔 활굴과 가디 無活氣 無感激한 속에서 애처로운 存在를 僅保할 뿐이다. 생각남아엔 줌억위를 쥐고 말끄테 괴를 무티던 昔日의 志士識者도 이제는 自己의 經綸 모이기를 꺼리고 平凡한 수레의 微塵을 딸아 怠惰한 一致를 求하기에 奔走하며 一般人衆은 얼마둥안의 疲弊에 元氣가 줄고 나날이 울리는 聲言에 感激이 식어 마디 陽氣바라고 섯는 언(凍)사람가디 當初 움즉어리기를 실혀하는 傾向이 업지 안토다. 열 스물의 눈을 한꺼번에 뜨고 기다리던 鑑察의 外人들은 『너이들도 그저 그러코나」하는 嘲笑을 함부로 안기며 直情無邪인 靑年들은 『所謂이 좀 識者라는 이의 지껄인다는 말이며 한다는 일은 떠들 수가 업다.』하야 怨嗟의 聲을 도처에 뿌리도다. 嗚呼라 朝鮮民族의 體面을 이제 어느 곳에 차즈며 늘 따수한 感激의 샘을 이제 어느 곳에서 기를(汲)가.
묵은 사람은 오히려 깨지 못하고 젊은 동무는 아즉 그루(株)가 서지 못하얏도다. 다 못 中間階級의 志士識者가 잇슬 뿐이다. 現下에 노힌 모든 施設의 發起者, 모든 聲言의 主動者, 모든 問題에 做出者인 中間階級의 志士識者, 그 사람이 잇슬뿐이다. 그 사람이 다시금 新空氣의 作成者 新感激의 源泉의 되는 것뿐이다. 한 번 뿌린 씨는 다시 뿌린 그 사람의 손으로 거두지 아니치 못할 것이라. 만일 그를 못한다 하면 即 한다 한다 떠들기는 自己가 하고 그 乃終이 없다는 最終의 汚辱은 朝鮮民族 全體에게 돌린다 하면 이 어찌 諸位의 忍爲할 바이랴. 勿論 그럴 理由가 업것지요.
여름이란 異常한 時候이라 쥐엇던 줌억위도 뚤리고 부텃던 뚤도 녹아지도다. 人衆의 마음은 더 한 층 解弛키 쉬울지요. 모로든 努力은 그 進展이 別로 어려울지도 모르나니 有志兒의 正히 勵氣一番하야 可할 時인가 하노라. 最後의 一言으로써 識者諸位에게만 歎願하노니
