The True State of Our Society and Its Trends
Although the topic is titled "The True State of Our Society and Its Trends," what we have written here is not the complete picture of today's Chosŏn society as it is. To speak truthfully, it is merely a collection of fragments gathered within the scope that our situation fortunately allows us to write, or as far as our thoughts reach. Therefore, there are things here that we might be able to conceive but lack the freedom to publish, and many things we could not write because our thoughts did not reach them. In other words, it is but a single slice of the cross-section of the current societal reality. Moreover, whether showing such things will benefit or harm our welfare, we do not know. We simply wish to lay bare our things, good or bad, before all people, nakedly, and only under our thorough awakening, strive to do our best through individual effort. (Among this manuscript, "The Beauty and Ugliness Reflected in Society's Mirror" is based on inquiries made on April 20th. "What Do These Recent Facts Signify?" compiles facts that occurred between April 1st and April 20th.)

The Beauty and Ugliness Reflected in Society's Mirror
Looking at the Religious World: 1, Individual faith has become twice as strong compared to before.
2, The competitive missionary zeal of each religion is truly experiencing unprecedented prosperity.
3, The traditional salvation-focused faith principle (Christianity standard) is gradually delving into the truth of religion and shifting towards the practical reality of human life.
4, Whereas past concepts of faith were objective, they are now subjective, and along with this, the fulfillment of duties towards the group has become earnest.
5, The majority of the new youth seem to have realized that all the diverse elements of human life reside in religion.
=(From a talk with Mr. Sin Hŭngu (申興雨), General Secretary of the Central YMCA)=
A, Observing the current state of the general religious world within Chosŏn, one must deeply admire the fact that sincere faith, regardless of whether it is misguided or orthodox, is manifesting favorably through each religious sect. <69> However, it is a clear fact that new concepts and new inclinations regarding that faith are emerging today. This is the phenomenon of moving away from otherworldly faith towards self-faith. In other words, it is believed to be a general psychology seeking to depart from ritualistic, dependent faith and return to self-deification,歸宿 (returning) to the great universe, the great truth.
B, It is a notable fact that believers in Cheondogyo (天道敎) are increasing daily (a 30% increase compared to last year), and it is also true that the inclination of the general public mind is largely turning towards this religion. Speculating on the psychology behind this: (1) It can be said to be a natural inclination arising from the activation of our innate morality and social nature; it stems from the self-awareness that Chosŏn people must also become new people of the world, seeking to move from the old to the new, that is, from fragmented darkness towards light, and also from the understanding that society itself cannot achieve harmonious unity without morality at any time. (2) It is thought that Cheondogyo's doctrines are extremely modern, and at the same time, the construction of future culture, filled with the joy of creation and emotion, cannot but stand upon the faith of Innaecheon (人乃天 - Man is Heaven) principles.
=(From a talk with Mr. Chŏng Kwangjo (鄭廣朝), Head of the Great宗司 (Main Religious Affairs Office?) of Cheondogyo)=

Looking at Lecturers: 1, The emotional, impulsive emptiness of audiences in the past has become comparatively calmer, and simultaneously, lectures that are referential and research-oriented are welcomed.
2, Among the audience, one sees people with topknots (sangt'u), those belonging to the stubborn old school, and a large number of middle-aged and older people attending, who were not seen before.
3, Whereas in the past, people were unwilling to attend even free lectures, now, despite admission fees, crowds twice as large as before gather.
4, The audience's applause and welcome are mainly directed at words signifying the breaking down of class barriers, union, solidarity, etc.
=(From a talk with Mr. Kang Mae (姜邁), Student Supervisor at Paejae High Common School)=

Trends in Youth Groups: 1, Each group increasingly realizes the necessity of unity, and concurrently, the power of solidarity strengthens.
2, They intend to embark on practical action rather than empty talk. For instance, at the recent joint general meeting, branch associations from various regions that participated sent their respective proposal drafts, requesting the implementation of their own plans.
3, Various groups have earnestly expressed the need for regional lecture tours, that is, the necessity of encouragement and development.
=(From Mr. O Sanggŭn (吳祥根), Chairman of the Chosŏn Youth Association Federation)<70>

New Phenomena in the Women's World: 1, Looking first at our Education Society (敎育會), whereas enrollment was merely 40-50 students in previous years, this spring suddenly saw 150-160 applicants.
2, Relatively more 'anbang ladies' (안방아씨), meaning ladies from noble or wealthy families, are enrolling compared to before. That is, previously there were no applicants over 30, but now there are even elderly women over 40, up to 50 years old.
3, Whereas women in the past only desired to be able to write and read simple letters in Eonmun (諺文 - Hangul) or novels, now they have taken a step further, possessing the idea of family improvement, thinking that our households too should strive for fulfillment like others.
4, The trend of women from the 'flower and willow world' (花柳界 - entertainment/geisha district) gradually returning to the world of learning is conspicuous.
5, Until recently, women relied on men for enrollment procedures, but now women themselves are properly handling their own enrollment procedures.
=(From the talk of Ms. Yi Ŭnnyang (李銀孃), Head of Education Department, Chosŏn Women's Education Society)=

The World of Lawsuits and Book Sales: 1, In the recent world of litigation, fraud cases are the most numerous. This is perhaps because as the world becomes more civilized, our cunning (means) increases, and as cunning increases, the sense of morality weakens. Furthermore, there are many lawsuits related to acquiring or expanding rights in various fields.
2, Recently, book sales have decreased compared to before due to financial hardship, but the sale of textbooks has conversely increased dramatically. Regarding regional orders, orders for previously numerous items like the Seven Classics (七書) or other old books have completely diminished, replaced by numerous orders for newly published various magazines. (Based on the words of people in the respective fields)

The Education World and the Student World: First, looking at what appears in the Education World:
a, Public sympathy towards the education world is converging; notable examples include the recent establishment of the foundation for Posŏng Law and Commerce School (普成 法律商業學校), the donation of a house by Mr. Han Kyusŏl (韓圭卨) for use by the Education Society (敎育會), and the establishment of new schools and training centers in various regions. Particularly, the local populace is inclined towards the promotion of education, such that it's said establishing a school is easy if someone just initiates it.
b, Not stopping at merely impulsively and ambitiously aiming for the establishment of new schools, there is progress towards researching and implementing improvements to the school system and enrichment of its content. Even though it's the same <71> surge of educational fervor, there is a distinct difference that cannot be spoken of in the same breath as the events of the Yung-hui (隆熙) era of old Korea.
c, The shortage of teachers is a problem that cannot be overlooked in today's education world, and the call for the establishment of normal schools is particularly loud.

Next, looking at the Student World:
1, The general student psychology shows a trend of becoming calmer than before, while simultaneously striving harder in studies and valuing good conduct.
2, There are no students dropping out mid-term.
3, The number of students failing when advancing from the 1st to the 2nd year increases annually. This is because students enter middle school right after graduating from common school, and the difference between the 4th-grade common school textbooks and the 1st-year middle school textbooks is quite large, making it difficult for common school graduates to handle the middle school level curriculum.
4, The number of students working their way through school (苦學生 - kosaengsaeng) increases daily, and society's sympathy for them also deepens progressively. The recent organization of the Society for Supporting Students Working Through School (苦學生 後援會) serves as one example. Among these students, there are often individuals who continue their studies diligently even while skipping meals for 1 or 2 days. This seems to stem from the acute realization that the mental suffering from lack of education is far greater than the physical suffering from hunger. (The above is based on talks with Mr. Yu Chint'ae (愈鎭泰), Executive Director of the Chosŏn Education Society, and Mr. Chang Ŭngjin (張膺震), Vice-Principal of Hwimun High Common School)

Seen from the Tram and Changgyeongwon: First, regarding the tram:
1, Passenger psychology has improved considerably compared to last year; self-control is abundant, and acts such as men yielding seats to women, and young people to the elderly and children, show that the degree of kindness has intensified compared to before.
2, There are more male passengers than female passengers.
3, The increase in passenger numbers was exceptional last year. Looking just at the Electric Company's revenue, compared to the same period last year, an increase of over one-third is seen, with the highest single-day revenue reaching the huge sum of 6,080 won. (Above from tram staff talk)

Next, regarding Changgyeongwon (昌慶苑):
a, On ordinary days, there are 2,000 to 3,000 visitors; on Sundays, 6,000 to 7,000 visitors (recent standard).
b, There are more female visitors than male visitors.
c, The psychology of today's visitors is mainly focused on studying artifacts and animals; the number of people entering for this purpose exceeds half. Besides them, those visiting meaninglessly are mostly people who came to the capital from the provinces, and other elderly or young people.
d, In essence, the number of visitors is greater than the same period last year, and observing their <72> manner of viewing, the vast majority show a serious attitude of exploration and research; in various aspects, it is utterly incomparable to previous days.
=(Above based on the talk of Changgyeongwon staff)=

Social Trends Appearing in Commerce: 1, The sale of all textiles shows considerable prosperity compared to last year, as prices have stabilized.
2, Regional orders were also very sluggish last year due to famine, but this year the results are relatively favorable.
3, The main items sold are particularly cotton fabrics, while the sale of high-priced silks (綢緞) and similar goods shows a tendency to decrease slightly compared to before.
=(Above based on the talk of the General Affairs Head at Tongyang Mulsan (東洋物産))=

a, Sales of student supplies increased by about 30% compared to before (last year), cosmetics sales by about 50% or more, and umbrellas and Western clothing accessories by about 30% or more. This seems mainly due to seasonal factors.
b, Previously, Japanese goods were generally welcomed, but now there is a tendency to demand Chosŏn goods or products from China or Europe. (Above is the response from Tonga Women's Commercial Association (東亞婦人商會))

The sale of gold and silver items has decreased by about 4/5 compared to before. Among the items sold somewhat are silver products. Also, the sale of gold half-rings (金半指 - kŭm panji), which were once sold briskly, has almost ceased. Instead, there are occasional demands for silver half-rings (銀半指 - ŭn panji). Anyway, recently there's a trend of moving away from gold and favoring silver. (Talk from a gold and silver merchant)

A, Orders for catered food (料理 - yori) are less than half of the same period last year. Simultaneously, it has become customary to order take-out (外上 - oesang?) and pay almost none of the cost. Also, if there are any orders, they are mostly for social gatherings of people prominent in the business world or other societal fields.
B, Previously, there were many food orders from idle young men (浮浪靑年 - purang ch'ŏngnyŏn), creating a prosperous scene, but now such people are nowhere to be seen.
(Talk from a catering business owner)

Chosŏn as Reflected in Japanese Eyes
Thoughts and Crimes of Chosŏn People: A certain Japanese resident in Chosŏn wrote roughly the following meaning under the title "Curious Lawsuits and Crimes" in a recent work. The correctness of the observation is a separate issue; for us, it is something to refer to anyway. There are quite a lot of unusual lawsuits in Chosŏn. As a result of years of misrule, the land system is confused, leading to many land-related lawsuits, among which those concerning gravesites, namely sansong (山訟), are even more numerous. This arises from the belief in feng shui (風水), selecting so-called auspicious sites (明堂), and the superstition that the prosperity or decline of a family and the fortune or misfortune of descendants depend entirely on the quality of the gravesite, thus prioritizing tombs above all else. Among lawsuits concerning personal affairs, divorce lawsuits filed by wives against husbands are not few, which is quite curious in the East. This might be due to the recent self-awareness of women's status, but also (1) disharmony between couples caused by the past abuse of early marriage, (2) whereas in the past, a divorced woman had no other path but to remain a widow for life or become a concubine, recently remarriage is not viewed so lowly, (3) Chosŏn women, who solely rely on men's wealth, seeking to gain their own comfort by remarrying into another family, seem to be the causes. Also, regarding criminal matters, there is the abduction of widows; this occurs because widowers and bachelors belonging to the propertyless class find formal marriage expensive, and unmarried men face social contempt. Also, since ancient times, there has been a custom considering remarriage a disgrace for women, and due to the abuse of early marriage, young widows are not few. Therefore, it arises from seeking satisfaction for this unnatural human condition through the barbaric form of abduction, and society tends not to view it as such a bad thing. And the most amusing thing is among document forgery crimes, forging and selling edicts of the old Korean Emperor. Also, among cruel crimes are wives murdering their husbands; dozens of such cases are counted each year. This damages the harmony between couples as a result of early marriage, subsequently promotes adultery, and is an unavoidable phenomenon under a legal system that forbids divorce for life. Chosŏn women are unexpectedly bold when committing such crimes; perhaps society's evil customs have driven them to such extremes. <74>

Recent Status of Chosŏn Associations and Groups: The Ōsaka Mainichi Shinbun (大阪 每日新聞) West Chosŏn Edition dated April 1st reported the following about various associations and groups in Chosŏn under the title above: "Chosŏn's associations and groups, sprouting like bamboo shoots after rain in recent years, now number over 2,000 across the entire peninsula. However, the current state of such groups is one of listlessness and stagnation. (After listing 4 or 5 examples) The biggest reasons for this are said to be (1) lack of money, (2) lack of enthusiasm and character among leaders, causing internal strife, (3) interference from the authorities." So it was stated. While the correctness of the observation may be set aside, it seems like a stone from another mountain (i.e., something to learn from).

General Thoughts of Chosŏn People: A certain official of the Government-General (總督府) spoke as follows under the title "Chosŏn's Current Situation, Difficult to be Optimistic About": "Chosŏn's current situation seems calm for now, but it is difficult to be optimistic about it. Having tried throwing bombs, killing people, and establishing a so-called provisional government in the past, the results mostly ended in failure, and they were unable to achieve their desired goals. Thus, they merely think it better to carefully await the arrival of the right time and aim for success then. Their deeply ingrained independence ideology has by no means vanished from their minds. Therefore, they are merely absorbing more culture and accumulating economic power, waiting for a later opportunity. Hence, their current state of calm can be called a preparatory latent period for taking action in the future. At least, the pioneer class capable of guiding the new thought of the Korean people thinks this way, I believe. The thoughts of the Chosŏn government authorities also seem to align with mine in planning the cultural enlightenment and industrial development of Chosŏn, but of course, differences will arise when it comes to the destination point. That is, when their cultivation of strength reaches the ideal stage and the time for independence nearly arrives, will they again launch an independence movement and desperately strive to achieve their original aspiration, or will they utilize the cultivated strength to realize a grander purpose, a great hope and ideal for humanity, beyond independence? Regarding the question of which they will choose, we authorities must expend extremely careful consideration and urgently prepare for the future." Etc., etc., he stated. The correctness of his observation is a separate issue; it is sufficient just to show that they observe things this way.

What Do These Recent Facts Signify?
Establishment of a 5 Million Won Industrial Company: As a sister company to the Chosŏn Forest Railway Company (朝鮮森林鐵道會社), the Chosŏn Industrial Co., Ltd. (朝鮮工業株式會社), which had been planning "pulp" (필푸 - presumably pulp) industry around the time of the Forest Railway's establishment last year, had temporarily halted due to financial turmoil. However, as prices for various machinery, materials, land, and labor <75> have fallen, it has been decided to establish it again. Therefore, Mr. Hasegawa Tarōkichi (長谷 川太郞吉), Managing Director of the Forest Railway, came to the capital and consulted with the authorities regarding the matter of acquiring forest land. The company initially planned to start with a capital of 10 million won, but since various prices have plummeted, the capital was reduced to 5 million won. They plan to establish it in May and start construction in June.

Plan to Establish Chosŏn Sericulture School: The Chosŏn Sericulture Silk Association (朝鮮蠶業絲會), which had been preparing for establishment since last year, has seen progress in the meantime. In March, Mr. Ariga Mitsutoyo (有賀光豐), head of the Industrial Bank (殖産銀行), was appointed as president, and Messrs. Han Ch'angsu (韓昌洙) and Tomita Gisaku (富田儀作) as vice presidents. They discussed this fiscal year's business plan. As the association's first project, they are currently planning to establish a Chosŏn Sericulture School (朝鮮蠶業學校) and conduct instruction via correspondence courses.
Chosŏn mulberry field area: 23,180 chōbu (町步 - unit of area) (as of 1918). Cocoon production: 122,350 sŏk (石 - unit of volume/weight) (1919). Silk reeling production: 35,057 kwan (貫 - unit of weight) (fiscal year 1919).

Establishment of Research Society for the Affairs of Koreans Abroad: The so-called Research Society for the Affairs of Koreans Abroad (在外鮮人事情硏究會), intended to study the circumstances of Koreans in foreign countries, held its first meeting in Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) on April 2nd. First, after opening remarks by Mr. Kikuchi Kenjō (菊地謙讓) and introductions by Mr. Ushimaru (牛丸), Mr. Ushimaru gave an inspection report on the situation of Koreans residing in Manchuria, Mongolia, and Siberia. Then, there were speeches by several gentlemen including Messrs. Kanemaru (金丸), Watanabe (渡邊), Yi Kakkchong (李覺鍾), and the Suzuki (鈴本) branch manager of Tōtaku (東拓 - Oriental Development Company). About 30 Japanese and Koreans attended the meeting.

Handshake Between Japanese Residents in Kyŏngsŏng and Confucian Scholars: In recent years, the handshake between a portion of Confucian scholars from the capital and provinces and influential Japanese residents in Kyŏngsŏng has drawn considerable societal attention. On the 5th of last month, a Confucian scholars' gathering was held at the Kyŏngsŏng Hotel, hosted by four individuals: Ayugai Fusanojin (鮎貝房之進), Kikuchi Kenjō (菊地謙讓), Sase Yasutetsu (佐瀨態鐵), and Ōgaki Takeo (大垣丈夫). From various organizations such as Taedong Samunhoe (大東斯文會), Yugyo Chinhŭnghoe (儒敎振興會 - Confucianism Promotion Society), Yugyo Taesŏnghoe (儒敎大成會 - Confucianism Great Achievement Society), Yugyo Taedonghoe (儒敎大同會 - Confucianism Great Unity Society), etc., Messrs. Chŏng Manjo (鄭萬朝), Ŏ Yundŭk (魚允迪), Sin Myŏnhyu (申冕休), Chŏng Pongjo (鄭鳳朝), Sŏ Sanghun (徐相勛), Sim Chongsuk (沈鍾舜), Yi Haejo (李海朝), Yi Sŭnguk (李承旭), Ku Ch'ansŏ (具瓚書), Cho Pyŏnggŏn (趙秉揵), Song Chihŏn (宋之憲), Hong Hŭi (洪熹), Yun Hŭigu (尹喜求), Pak Yang (朴彛陽), Yi Pŏmch'ŏl (李範喆), Pak Hangno (朴學魯), etc., attended. After welcoming remarks by Mr. Ayugai and a speech concerning the translation of classics and holding lecture meetings, a banquet commenced. In response to Mr. Ōgaki's speech of hope, the eldest, Mr. Sin Myŏnhyu, gave a reply, and the meeting adjourned at 6:30 PM.

This Time, an Education Reform Society is Organized: Although there are currently 1,800 schools and 12,000 sŏdang (書堂 - traditional village schools) in Chosŏn, the number of students they accommodate is merely 210,000. This is insufficient to quench the blazing thirst for education among our 20 million brethren. During the admission period last spring, various schools faced great difficulties due to applicants, managing the situation temporarily by rejecting about 9/10ths of the applicants. To research and resolve this pressing education problem, the Education Society (敎育會) already existed among the populace. Now, initiated by young volunteers, the Education Reform Society (敎育改選會) was organized on April <76> 6th. Its objectives are (1) to reform the education system to suit the times, (2) to refresh and expand educational institutions. The committee members to implement this are Cho Chonggu (趙鍾九), Kim Sanghang (金相恒), Chang Tŏksu (張德秀), Kim Myŏngsik (金明植), Kim Chongbŏm (金鍾範), Kim Saguk (金思國), O Sanggŭn (吳祥根), Chang Inhwan (張仁煥), Yang Chihwan (梁址煥), Yŏ Pyŏngsŏp (呂炳燮), Pak Sajik (朴思稷).

New Experiment of Civic Academic Lectures: For the vast majority of brethren who could not receive formal schooling, or for those engrossed in their duties, there is no other way to inject practical knowledge than adopting a system of public lectures. Now, the Kyŏngsŏng Central YMCA (京城中央基督靑年會), possessing the means to do so, sponsored by its Boys' Department, is conducting the first phase of Civic Academic Lectures (市民學術講演) from April 8th to June 3rd, and the results are said to be very favorable.

Dubious Joint Meeting of Peddler Groups: Among the various peddler groups (褓負商團 - Pobusangdan), known as "possessors of shadows" (陰影의 所有者), three organizations - the Sangmu Hyŏphoe (商務協會 - Commerce Association), Sangmu Siryŏptan (商務實業團 - Commerce Industrial Group), and Sangmu Yŏn'guhoe (商務硏究會 - Commerce Research Society) - held a joint inaugural general meeting on April 10th and performed a merger. While the internal details are unknown, according to Mr. Chŏng Minhwa (鄭民和), head of the Sangmu Hyŏphoe, this merger is aimed at dispelling the public misunderstanding that such groups are nests of miscellaneous riffraff. Within the association, a grand structure of 6 departments and 12 sections (even if only in name) has been established, giving the appearance of having organized some kind of government. (The 6 departments are General Affairs, Accounting, Commerce, Public Works, Education, Communications). What the future of this group holds indeed.

The Kungmin Kongjinhoe Has Emerged.
The Kungmin Hyŏphoe (國民協會 - National Association), which was gasping for breath following the assassination of Mr. Min Wŏnsik (閔元植), experienced internal strife over the presidency and was in turmoil. Then, key figures of the association, Messrs. Chŏng Pyŏngjo (鄭炳朝), Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) from P'yŏngyang, and about 40 others, separately initiated the Kungmin Kongjinhoe (國民共進會 - National Mutual Advancement Society) and split off from the Kungmin Hyŏphoe. It was not a conflict of principles but a division caused by struggles for position and fame; the principles of this association, which split off, are naturally the same as those of the Kungmin Hyŏphoe.

New Activities of the Japan Industrial Policy Association: The Chosŏn Branch of the Japan Industrial Policy Association (日本工政會 - Nihon Kōseikai), composed of industrialists and engineers, held a council meeting on the afternoon of April 11th at its office on the upper floor of the Oriental Development Company (東洋拓植會社). Mr. Kawada Naoji (河田直治) and over 10 others attended, discussing this fiscal year's budget, internal rules for member qualifications, holding the regular meeting on May 3rd, etc. They decided to establish an investigation committee to research labor issues and other matters, and further discussed coordinating with the Government-General Industrial Investigation Committee (總督府 産業 調査會). The fervent activity of Japanese individuals in Chosŏn's industrial sector in recent years is indeed remarkable. <77>

Talk of Establishing a Hanja Specialized School: The Chosŏn Yugyo Taedonghoe (朝鮮儒敎大同會), managed by Messrs. Sŏng Kiu (成岐運) and Ch'oe Yŏngnyŏn (崔永年), is reportedly discussing establishing a Hanja Specialized School (漢文專門學校) in Kyŏngsŏng. The plan is for a 3-year main course, accommodating 300 students in the first intake, and also establishing a 1-year preparatory course for 100 students. Their intention might be to promote Hanja (漢文 - Classical Chinese) study just like in the old days and thereby obstruct the establishment of new culture, but public opinion questions whether they possess the organizational capacity to realize such a plan.

Realization of the All-Korea Christian Youth Federation: Since last year, various youth groups within Christian churches across Korea (Mallyŏnhoe 勉勵會, Epworth League 엡웟, Kongnyŏhoe 共勵會, Kidokhoe 基督會, etc.) have been conducting a unification movement to carry out more organized activities in the future. On April 21st and 22nd, they held the founding general meeting at the Central Methodist Episcopal Church Hall in Kongp'yŏng-dong, Chongno, and saw the establishment of the Federation (聯合會). The aims of the federation, besides promoting and implementing the three educations of Wisdom, Virtue, and Physicality (智, 德, 體), are (1) the advancement of the Christian church on the peninsula, (2) propagating the truth of God ideally. Tenant Farmers' Association in Pongsan, Hwanghae: Mr. Kang Ch'ango (康昌梧) and other volunteers in Sariwŏn, Pongsan County, organized the County Tenant Farmers' Association (小作農會) some months ago. Its substance includes preventing landlords and farm supervisors from unconditionally altering tenant farmers' tenancy, promoting agriculture, and other matters. On April 15th, they gathered over 100 tenant farmers in Hwayang-ri, Yŏngch'ŏn-myŏn, in the county, held a major lecture necessary for agriculture, with speeches by Association President Kang Ch'ango, Sŏsŏn Shikch'ŏl (西鮮殖 鐵 - West Chosŏn Development Railway?) Director Yu Hunhyŏp (劉壎燮), Paek Namkyu (白南珪), Kim Kiyŏng (金基英), and County Magistrate Ch'oe T'aehyŏn (崔台鉉). The association plans to advance further to become the Tenant Farmers' Association for all of Hwanghae Province.

Rural Improvement Societies in Anju and Chŏngju Counties: Initiated by volunteers Kim Yongbŏm (金容範) and Yi Yunsu (李允洙) in Onsang-ri, Un'gok-myŏn, Anju County, South P'yŏngan Province, the Rural Improvement Friendship Society (農村改良 親睦會) was organized and continues its tireless activities. Most of the 40-plus households in the village have become members. Current activities include popular lectures every Saturday and joint subscriptions to newspapers and magazines. Furthermore, in Iŏn-myŏn, Chŏngju County, North P'yŏngan Province, initiated by Mr. Kim Kongsŏn (金公善) and several dozen others, a Rural Improvement Society (農村 改善會) was organized and is actively proceeding. The rural improvement movement in North and South P'yŏngan Provinces is said to be worthy of close attention.

Guardian Spirit of the Homeland, Mr. Min Yŏngjik: The pottery clay in the area of Kŭmsŏ-myŏn, Sanch'ŏng County, South Kyŏngsang Province, was found upon analysis to be superior in quality to the kaolin clay of Hamgyŏng Province. About 800,000 pyeong (坪 - unit of area) in the eastern part of the township had previously fallen under the occupation of the Tokyo Pottery Company (東京陶器會社). <78> However, since the pottery clay is still inexhaustible, Mr. Min Yŏngjik (閔泳直) of Tŭk-ri in the same township realized the promising and urgent need for improved ceramics in Chosŏn. About three years ago, he invested approximately 4,000 won to build a test kiln, painstakingly analyzed the clay, experimented with producing test ceramics, achieved satisfactory results, and is now building a commercial kiln. The provincial office also decided to encourage this by providing an annual subsidy of 1,000 won for five years. Mr. Min plans to invest all his assets, manage independently for about five years, and once the products are comparable to foreign goods without inferiority, transform it into a company organization to contribute greatly to the Chosŏn ceramics industry. Mr. Min's dedicated efforts are truly pioneering in this field.

Currency Circulation in April: According to information heard about currency circulation within Chosŏn around mid-April, the total amount was 102,927,744 won, consisting of 8,403,522 won in hard currency, 2,334,105 won in small-denomination paper money, and 92,197,002 won in Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行) notes. Compared to the same period last year, this shows a total decrease of 46,415,050 won. <79>

**Summary**

This 1921 article from *Gaebyeok* magazine provides a fragmented yet detailed snapshot of Korean society under Japanese rule. It compiles observations from various figures (religious leaders, educators, businessmen) and news reports covering religion (increased fervor, Cheondogyo growth), public lectures, youth organizations, women's rising educational aspirations, legal trends (fraud, land/grave disputes, divorce), education challenges (teacher shortages, curriculum gaps), student hardships, changing urban life (trams, parks), commercial shifts (textiles vs. luxury goods, preference for non-Japanese items), social issues reflected in newspapers, Japanese analyses of Korean society (perceived traits, independence thought, organizational weaknesses), new economic ventures (industry, sericulture), evolving Korean-Japanese interactions, and grassroots movements like tenant farmer associations and rural improvement societies. The article presents these "facts" without overt editorializing but implicitly calls for self-awareness and effort ("徹底한 覺悟미테서 各各의 努力의 最善을 다하자는 것 뿐이다"). For historians, the piece offers valuable insights into the social fabric of early colonial Korea, revealing the complex interplay of tradition and modernity, the tangible impacts of colonial policy, anxieties about social change, the persistence of Korean cultural practices (like *sansong*), nascent consumer nationalism, and the varied responses of Koreans—from seeking education and economic development to organizing for social improvement and maintaining underlying independence sentiments, even as observed (and perhaps misinterpreted) by Japanese officials. The inclusion of direct quotes and specific data (like newspaper statistics and currency figures) adds to its documentary value.

問題를 『社會의 實相과 그 推移라』하얏스나 우리는 여긔에 적은 이것으로써 곳 今日 朝鮮社會의 實相의 그대로라 함이 아니다. 事實대로 말하면 우리의 處地에서 多幸히 적을 수 잇는 範圍內에서 또는 우리 생각의 미치는 限에서 斷片的으로 주어 모은 것 뿐이다. 그러니까 여긔에는 우리가 能히 생각하는 것일지라도 發表할 自由가 업서 못한 것도 잇고 또 우리의 생각이 미치지 못하야 쓰지 못한 것도 만흐다. 다시 말하면 現 社會 實相의 斷面의 一片뿐이다. 그러고 이러한 것을 구태여 보이는 것이 우리의 福利增進에 利가 될는지 害가 될는지 그도 모르겟도다. 다못 우리는 조커나 언찌안커나 우리의 것을 萬人의 압헤 赤裸裸히 들어 내노코 오즉 우리의 徹底한 覺悟미테서 各各의 努力의 最善을 다하자는 것 뿐이다. (이 原稿 中 『社會의 거울에 나타나난 姸醜』는 4月 20日 探問에 의한 것이요. 『最近의 이 事實은 무엇을  말함인가.』 한 것은 4月 1日 至仝 20日間에 起한 事實을 모은 것이다.

日本人의 눈에 비취운 朝鮮
朝鮮人의 思想 及 犯罪 朝鮮 在住 日本人 某의 近著中에 『珍奇한 訴訟 及 犯罪』란 題下에 略 이런 意味의 글을 썻다. 觀察의 正否는 別問題요. 우리로서는 如何間 한 번 參考할 것이라 한다, 朝鮮에는 異常한 訴訟이 퍽 만타. 多年 惡政의 結果로 土地制度가 混亂되어 土地에 關한 訴訟이 多하며 그 中에는 墳墓에 關한 것, 即 山訟이 更多하다. 是는 風水의 說을 信하야 所謂 明堂의 地를 擇하며 一家의 榮枯와 子孫의 禍福은 全혀 墓地의 善惡에 잇다는 迷信이 有하야 무엇보다 墳墓을 爲하는 대서 起하는 것이다. 人事에 關한 訴訟으로는 妻로서 夫에 對한 離婚訴訟이 不少한 바 東洋에서는 퍽 珍奇한 일이다. 이것은 近來 婦女子의 地位 自覺에도 因함일지나 (1) 從來 早婚의 弊로 夫婦間의 不和를 致한 것 (2) 昔者에는 一朝 離婚한 者는 終世토록 寡居 或은 妾이 될 外에 他道가 無하얏스나 近時는 再婚을 그러케 賤視치 아니하는 것 (3) 오즉 男子의 富를 依賴하는 朝鮮의 女子는 다시 他家로 嫁하야 自身의 豪閑을 得코저 하는 等이 그 原因인 듯하며 또 刑事에 關한 것은 寡婦의 强奪이니 此는 無産級에 屬한 鰥夫 及 總角은 定式 結婚을 行코저 하면 費用이 多하며 且 未婚男子는 社會의 侮蔑을 受하는 關係로써 그리함이며 또 自古로 再婚을 女子의 恥辱이라하는 習慣이 잇고 또 早婚의 弊로 年少寡婦도 不少한 故로 自然 掠奪이란 野蠻的 形式으로써 此 不自然한 人情의 滿足을 求코저 함에서 生함인 바 社會도 그를 그러케 惡事로 보지 안는 傾向이 잇다. 그리고 가장 웃으은  것은 文書僞造犯 中에 舊韓國皇帝의 勅書를 僞造하야 파는 것이며 또 殘酷한 犯罪로는 妻가 其 本夫를 殺害하는 것이니 此는 每年 數十件을 筭한다. 此는 早婚의 結果로 夫婦間의 圓滿을 傷하고 디듸어 姦通의 風을 助長하며 또 終世 離婚을 不許하는 法制下에서는 不可免의 現象이라. 朝鮮의 女子는 이러한 犯罪를 行하는 境遇에는 豫想外로 大膽하나니 盖社會의 惡習慣이 그러케 激成시킨 것 일지라.』＜74＞

朝鮮結社團體의 近況 4月 1日 附 大阪 每日新聞 西鮮版은 右記題下에 朝鮮의 諸 結社團體에 이러케 報하얏다. 『數年來 雨後竹筍가티 朝鮮의 結社團體는 이제 全鮮으로 2千餘에 上하얏다. 그런데 그러한 團體의 今日 現狀은 萎靡不振中에 잇다. (4, 5의 實例를 擧하고서) 그런데 그리되는 最大原因은 (1) 돈이 업는 故 (2) 主宰者의 無熱誠 無人格으로 內訌을 惹起하는 故 (3)當局의 干涉이 有한 故이라 할 수 잇다.』 云하엿다. 其 觀察의 正否는 姑捨할지라도 他山의 石은 될듯 하다.

朝鮮人의 一般的 思想 總督府 事務官 某氏는 『樂觀이 難한 朝鮮의 現狀』이라고 下와 如히 말하엿다. 「朝鮮의 現狀은 目下 靜穩한 듯하나 그러나 그것은 樂觀키 難한 것이니 曾前에 彼等이 爆彈을 投하고 人을 殺하며 假政府라는 것을 造設하야 본 結果가 거의 失敗에 歸하고 希望하던 目的을 達키 不能하얏슴으로써 鄭重히 時機의 到來를 期待하야 成事를 期하는 것이 爲好라 思悟할 뿐이며 決코 彼等의 腦首에 深刻된 獨立思想은 消滅된 것이 아니다. 그래서 그들은 더욱 文化를 吸收하고 經濟力을 蓄養하야 後機를 期待함에 不過하다. 故로 彼等의 現在의 靜穩狀態는 將來에 事를 擧코저 하는 前提的 潛在期라 云하겟다. 적어도 鮮人의 新思想을 司配할 만한 先覺者 階級은 이와가티 생각하고 잇다고 나는 생각하는 바이라. 朝鮮政務當局者의 생각도 朝鮮의 文化의 啓發産業의 發達을 計하는 點에는 余와 一致한 듯 하나 그러나 그 到達點에 至하야 差異가 生할 것은 勿論이다. 即 彼等의 實力涵養이 理想域에 到達하야 거의 獨立할 時代가 到來하면 彼等이 再次 獨立의 運動을 起하야 그 素志를 貫徹하기에 必死 極力할는지 或은 獨立보다도 一層 更大한 目的으로 人類의 大希望 大理想을 活現코저 그 涵養한 實力을 活用할는지 何를 選하리라는 問題에 對하야 余等 當局者는 極히 愼重히 考慮를 費하야 將來에 處할 覺悟를 切要한다.』 云云하엿다. 그의 觀察의 正否는 別 問題요. 다못 그네는 이러케 觀察한다는 것을 보임으로써 足하다하다.
