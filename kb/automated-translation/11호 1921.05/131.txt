**Body Text Translation:**
Great Poll / Who Is the Greatest Contributor to the Development of the Korean Nation?

Measuring the place where we live, its area is merely fourteen thousand-odd square *ri*; counting our beloved brethren, their number does not exceed twenty million. It is not necessarily that it must be large or numerous, but when one's thoughts reach this point, who can suppress a feeling akin to loneliness? Let us speak without deception. If, for us today, there were limitless power and an inexhaustible sense of inspiration, someone who could provide eternal help or support, how fortunate would that truly be? However, let us try looking around today's globe. What is it like? Who is there who delves into our reality and holds warm feelings? Even if someone were to hold them momentarily, who would express those feelings through deeds, sacrificing their own possessions for the sake of our great life—in other words, who would provide support through actual deeds? We write this, asserting that there is no one. Then, are we unable to escape eternal loneliness? Are we unable to find a supporter in the true sense? Alas! Even a tiny bird has its mate; how could we, twenty million people, lack our companion? There is! There is! Certainly, there is! Who could it be? The four thousand years of history is it, and the many great, outstanding figures who forged the heart and soul of that history are precisely it. Unbeknownst, their spirits, possessing overflowing affection and resolute will, surely circle our land now. The only regrettable thing is that each of us is like wood and stone, unable to connect with their spiritual inspiration, unable to enter their embrace. We have felt this, and now, borrowing the format of a "Great Figures Poll," we intend to seek out and introduce the great figures from all fields who made the greatest contribution to the development of Chosŏn over the past four thousand years, thereby daring to create supporters in the true sense for our twenty million brethren. Comrades! If you share our sentiments, no, if you are Korean brethren, strive together to bring this endeavor of our company to fruition.

□ To those who will vote
One, Select one great figure you recognize as having made the greatest contribution in each of the ten fields: Thought, Politics, Military Affairs, Literature, Art, Religion, Science, Industry, Education, and Social Improvement (i.e., write down who for Thought, who for Politics, who for others, etc.) and send it to our company via postcard.
One, The great figures to be voted for should be those who appeared in the four thousand years of Korean history, but do not write down those currently living.
One, This poll does not inquire whether you are a reader of this magazine or not.
One, On the front of the voting postcard, be sure to write in red ink: "Kaebyŏk Company, Editorial Department, Great Figures Poll Section" (開闢社 編輯部 偉入投票係).
One, The voting period is from May 1st to June 10th, and the results of the vote will be published in the July issue of this magazine.
One, The great figures from each field who receive the most points in the vote, that is, the ten individuals, will be introduced in greatest detail, one person per issue, starting with the August issue of this magazine.<85>

**Summary**

This editorial from the May 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine announces a reader poll to identify the greatest contributors to the development of the Korean nation across ten fields (thought, politics, military, literature, art, religion, science, industry, education, social improvement) over the past 4,000 years. Lamenting a perceived lack of external support, the editorial posits that Korea's true strength and inspiration lie within its own history and the great figures who shaped it. The poll aims to reconnect contemporary Koreans with these historical figures, presenting them as "true supporters" for the nation's future. For historians, this article is significant as it reflects the burgeoning cultural nationalism and search for indigenous sources of pride and inspiration in early colonial Korea, shortly after the March 1st Movement. The categories chosen also reveal contemporary understandings of national development and the desired qualities of historical heroes.
