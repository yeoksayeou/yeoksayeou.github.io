自由通情 (Free Correspondence)

People of Yongsan, let us fight to the very end!
Yong Gongseol (龍公舌 - Mouthpiece of Yongsan?)

On this day when the fervor for entering school is swelling, while other schools within Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (Seoul) are adding extensions or constructing new buildings to accommodate as many applicants as possible, Yongsan Public Normal School doesn't even dream of extensions or new construction. Furthermore, it limits new admissions for the boys' department first year to 60 students and recruits no students at all for the girls' department first year. While other schools are newly establishing girls' departments they didn't have before, Yongsan Public School is said to be eliminating the girls' department it already had. What on earth is the reason for this? Are the authorities, who advocate for impartiality (一視同仁), discriminating so severely in education? Don't the residents of Yongsan pay school fees just like others? Do we have the obligation to pay school fees but not the right to enroll our children? Can we, the residents of Yongsan, sweetly accept such unfair discriminatory treatment and just sit idly by? Therefore, the Yongsan Public Normal School Supporters' Association held an emergency meeting on the 14th, and as a result, decided to negotiate with the Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture authorities. They selected Representative Committee Member Kim Ch'ang-du and three others to inquire about the reason from the Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture authorities. The Prefectural Governor (府尹) used the lack of classrooms as an excuse, and Director of Internal Affairs T'aekch'on (澤村 內務部長) stated that instead of eliminating the girls' department in Yongsan, it was decided to newly recruit students in Hyoch'ang. When the representative committee members mentioned that there is a usable classroom (a warehouse that could be used as a classroom with minor repairs) and that young girls cannot commute to Hyoch'ang, the authorities, perhaps having no response to that, eventually cited a lack of budget. Because the authorities' words were so absurd, the representative committee members returned disheartened and immediately initiated a Yongsan Residents' Assembly. At the Yongsan Residents' Assembly held on the 24th at Yongsan Public Normal School, the following resolutions were made.

Resolution Draft
1. Limiting the number of newly enrolling children for the 1924 academic year (Taishō 13) at Yongsan Public Normal School to 60 for the boys' department first year and not recruiting any for the girls' department first year is deemed as disregarding education in Yongsan.
2. We aim for an increase in the recruitment of boys' department first-year children and the revival of the girls' department first year.
3. The method for implementing the above shall be entirely entrusted to five executive committee members according to the chairman's instructions.
The above is resolved.
February 24, Taishō 13 (1924) Yongsan Residents' Assembly
Chairman Min Yŏng-sun
Secretary Yi Hang-nae
Resident Representatives Pak Hyŏng-bae
Kim Ŭn-hyŏn
Pak Tong-gu
Based on this resolution, Executive Committee Members U Chong-hyŏn, P'yo Sang-hyŏp, Kim Ch'ang-du, Yi Sun-yŏng, and Yun U-yŏng (5 individuals) were designated to negotiate with the Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture authorities. While we trust that the executive committee will, of course, fulfill their responsibility by exerting their utmost effort on behalf of all us residents, we residents must possess the resolve to fight with consistent enthusiasm until the day the resolutions of this assembly are implemented as decided.

How is the "Historical Study of Korean Art" (朝鮮 美術의 史的考察) coming along?
Tongmo Ilsaeng (東慕一生)

Greetings. I hope for the health of everyone at your esteemed company and the increasing development of the beloved "Kaebyŏk" (開闢). The "Historical Study of Korean Art" (造船美術의 史的 考察 - *Translator note: Uses 造船 (Chosŏn), older way to write 朝鮮 (Chosŏn)*), which was serialized starting from your esteemed company's issue 22, has remained unfinished since May last year, issue 35, and we have been unable to see its conclusion until now. What is the reason for this? Please let me know whether it will be continued or not.

Editor: We are very sorry to have to tell you this upon your inquiry. It was unavoidably discontinued due to the writer's circumstances. We cannot yet determine whether it will be continued or not. <109>

Why did "Social Aspects of the Past Year" (最近1년의 社會相) stop midway?
Student from Sunch'ang Tamyang (淳昌 潭陽生)

(Excerpt) I read the January issue with great interest. Thank you very much. One thing I'd like to ask is, why did the article in the January issue, "Social Aspects of the Past Year (during the recent year)," only cover up to June and not continue? Synthesizing the recent movements in various fields of our society provides a comprehensive overview and likely offers considerable benefit to the reader. Please continue it through December if possible. Also,
Regarding the critique of the inside story of Mimun Kobo (微文高普) in this issue, I sympathize with it for the sake of our education sector, for Mr. Min's (閔氏) personal enterprise, and also for the many entrepreneurs who will emerge in Chosŏn's future. However, I feel there is a need for a bit more advice towards Principal Im (任氏) of the same school. To ask him to act like a principal.

Editor: We intended to continue it in the January and February issues if possible, but as both became enlarged issues, it was pushed back until now due to space constraints. Now, too much time has passed, making it impossible to bring up old matters anew. There is a Social Diary (社會日誌) recorded in each issue, so you will have to refer to that instead. Henceforth, we will put more effort into covering the social aspects that arise each month.

The Past and Present of Brothers in Beijing (在北京 兄弟)
Student K in Beijing (在北京 K生)

Shall I relay, at least roughly, the news of our brothers residing in Beijing? Since the year is still young and there are no remarkable conclusions to our endeavors yet, I'll postpone this year's news for the future and first inform you of the news of our brothers in Beijing up to the end of last year.
From the perspective of life's endless desires, one cannot say that we were particularly happy last year, but neither can one say we were particularly unhappy. On the social side, the establishment of new organizations like the Korean Club (韓人俱樂部) and the Christian Youth Association (基督敎靑年會 - YMCA); the great success of Korean support during China's May 7th Commemoration Day (五七紀念日), allowing for a grand propagation of our principles and assertions; the investigation and announcement of the actual situation of our compatriots (同胞) during the Japanese earthquake; the Student Association's (留學生會) annual March 1st Commemoration Meeting (三一祝賀會), National Humiliation Day (國恥紀念), National Foundation Day (開天紀念), and various other lectures and discussion meetings held; especially the grand event like the Spring Athletic Meet (春期 大運動)—these vividly demonstrate our vitality. Furthermore, Chang Cha-il's (張子一 君) outstanding graduation from the English Department of Peking University (北大); Sŏ Wal-bo's (徐曰甫 君) excellent completion of studies at the Peking Aviation School (北京航空學校); Pak Ki-bok's (朴基福 君) graduation from the Leather Department of Yenching University (燕大); all these are valuable achievements of our brothers last year. And one more thing is the increase in the number of international students, that is, the rapid improvement in our brothers' passion for education and learning. The number, which was merely 30-40 until the year before last, doubled last year, reaching 80-90. Given the circumstances, this is enough to boast that our brothers in Beijing are doing quite well, isn't it? I will inform you of this year's news later.

Editor: Thank you. Please inform us often. The brothers here in the homeland (內地) are very curious to hear news of the brothers abroad (外地). Especially when there are new developments, please be sure to let us know. <110>

**Summary**

This "Free Correspondence" section from the March 1924 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine presents several reader contributions and editorial responses. The main piece details the discriminatory actions of Japanese colonial authorities in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) against the Yongsan Public Normal School, specifically limiting new male students and eliminating the girls' department, citing spurious reasons like lack of classrooms or budget. It reports on the Yongsan residents' protest meeting and their resolve to fight this decision. Other sections include reader inquiries about the discontinuation of serialized articles on Korean art history and recent social trends, with editorial apologies citing writer issues and space constraints. The final piece is a positive report from a Korean student in Beijing, highlighting the establishment of Korean organizations (Korean Club, YMCA), participation in Chinese events, academic achievements of students at major universities (Peking University, Yenching University, Peking Aviation School), and a significant increase in the number of Korean students studying there. From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into Korean resistance against colonial educational policies, the active engagement of readers with intellectual magazines like *Kaebyŏk*, and the burgeoning activities and academic pursuits of the Korean student diaspora in China during the 1920s, reflecting a growing nationalist consciousness and desire for education both at home and abroad. The mention of specific events like the May 7th Commemoration in China and the response to the Great Kanto Earthquake provides context for Korean activities within broader East Asian events.

龍山人士여 最後까지 奮鬪하자
龍公舌
入學熱이 팽창한 오늘날에 京城府內의 다른 학교에 대하야는 校舍를 增築, 또는 新築을 해 가면서 入學志願者를 될 수 잇는 대로 수용하고 오죽 龍山公立 普通學校에는 校舍의 增築新築은 꿈도 꾸지 안코 新入學 兒童도 男子部 1學年은 60명에 한하고 女子部 1학년은 全然 募集치 아니한다. 다른 學校에는 업던 女子部를 新設도 하면서 龍山公立學校에는 잇던 女子部를 업샌다 한다. 이게 대체 무슨 이유이냐. 一視同仁을 표방하는 當局者로서 교육에 대한 차별을 이러케 심하게 하느냐. 龍山 住民도 남과 가티 學校費를 내지 안느냐. 學校費 낼 의무는 잇서도 子女 入學시킬 권리는 업단 말이냐 우리 龍山 住民은 이러한 不當한 차별 대우를 달게 밧고 거저 안졋슬 수야 잇느냐. 그래서 龍山公立普通學校後援會에서는 去 14日 緊急會議를 한 結果 京城府 當局者와 교섭해 볼 작정으로 代表委員 金昌斗외 3인을 꼽아서 京城府 當局者에게 그 이유를 질문하엿다. 府尹은 敎室不足으로 핑게하고 澤村 內務部長은 龍山에 女子部를 업새는 대신에 孝昌에서 새로 모집하기로 되엿다 한다. 代表委員의 말이 교실도 쓸만한 것이 (倉庫를 약간 수리만 하면 한 교실로 쓸만함)잇다. 孝昌에는 어린 女子가 통학할 수 업다 한 즉 當局者는 그 말에는 대답할 말이 업든지 나종에는 豫算不足이라 한다. 當局者의 말이 넘우도 어이업스니까 代表委員은 無聊히 돌아와서 곳 龍山住民大會를 發起하엿다. 去 24일에 龍山公立 普通學校에 열린 龍山住民大會에서는 左와 如한 결의가 잇섯다.

決議案
1. 龍山公立 普通學校 大正 13년도 新히 入學할 아동은 男子部 1학년 60명에 止하고 女子部 1학년을 全然 募集치 안는 일은 龍山의 교육을 無視하는 자로 認함.
2. 男子部 1학년 兒童增募 並히 女子部 1학년의 부활을 期함.
3. 右實行方法은 議長의 指示에 의하야 5명의 實行委員에게 一任함.

朝鮮 美術의 史的考察은 엇더케 되섯슴닛가
