思想의 歸趨와 運動의 方向
						O民

To critique Chosŏn's thought fairly, the writer wishes to forget that he is a Chosŏn person. This is because the argument "it will become so" is easily tilted towards the subjective desire "I wish it would become so." Also, I wish to abandon my own views and individual assertions. Rather than "I think this way," stating "truth exists that way" is the fairest. Therefore, while writing, the author will strive to maintain this attitude and seek this truth as much as possible.

Chosŏn's world of thought underwent a major transformation spurred by the March First Movement. Before that, the main current of Chosŏn thought was political liberalism, that is, independence-ism. When the Chosŏn Political Protection Treaty was concluded, our seniors implicitly pointed to the *History of Vietnam's Ruin* (越南亡國史) and secretly discussed the *Record of Poland's Fall* (波蘭滅亡記). Also, saying that Chosŏn's absolutist politics caused its ruin, they promoted Royalist Patriotism (勤王愛國主義) aiming for a constitutional monarchy. Thinking about it now, it seems laughable (可笑), but at that time, it was a political ideology with implicit influence. Afterwards, political changes advanced another step, and when Japan announced the annexation of Chosŏn, the Chosŏn monarch naturally became XX. Consequently, royalist agitation suddenly faded, and except for a very small number of former officials, *panjok* (班族, traditional yangban lineage), and stubborn Confucian scholars (頑儒), the general youth became strongly colored by democratic patriotism. Thus, they sought to cultivate national pride by studying Chosŏn history, which was on the verge of being erased (抹滅). Also, because they thought the highest ideal of modern politics was a republican representative system (共和代議制), when the so-called patriots (志士) were busy at home and abroad, even while their whole bodies were paralyzed by local fever, regarding the future ideal political system of Chosŏn, there was almost no disagreement, converging on this.<41> The Chosŏn authorities, unaware that this was a political view that naturally emerged in Chosŏn, worried that American missionaries (米國宣敎師) were fostering an American spirit in Chosŏn students in churches and schools, leading to the dramatic act of bringing in the Japanese Congregational Church (日本耶蘇人 組合敎會). After the famous Sŏnch'ŏn Incident (宣川事件) at that time, as a reaction, many young people confided their frustrations and resentment to God through prayer under the stained-glass windows of Gothic architecture, while the military surveillance on the ground did not permit a single step forward. Not long after, the Great War in Europe broke out, and as its scope gradually expanded, the cruelty of militarism reached its peak. Only when the end came did people worldwide begin to realize, somewhat, the horrors of war and the evils of conquest. Thus, at the Paris Peace Conference (巴里講和談判席), the slogan "National Self-Determination" (民族自決) proposed by the late Mr. Wilson (월손氏) seemed to open a new path for the weak nations and colonial peoples of the world, leading to uprisings of the conquered peoples in both the East and West. Chosŏn, not falling behind in this rank, launched an independence movement empty-handed (赤手); this is the March First Movement, still vivid in our memory.

Nations possessing colonies and conquered peoples were too XX to liberate the weak and too XX to establish justice. Thus, the seemingly promising independence of the Philippines (比律賓) returned to being mere paper waste according to documents, the opportune revolution in India (印度) was silenced by violent oppression, and Chosŏn's March First Movement also retreated from reality, hibernating in ideals. At this time, a strange giant appeared in the Arctic, and the hungry ghosts (餓鬼) of the dark night ran rampant in Chosŏn. These two causes exerted enormous influence on the Chosŏn intellectual world. The giant from the north refers to the Communist Party of Russia (露國); this giant, formed by the blood of 100 million serfs (農奴) and the strength of 1 million soldiers, grew and lay dormant for hundreds of years, then, seeing its chance, suddenly emerged. When it concluded a separate peace treaty with Germany (獨逸), which the Russian Empire (露帝國) had targeted as an enemy (仇讎) and challenged, the surprise, fear, and anger of the Allied powers truly reached their peak. They tried to push it into difficulties economically and militarily, but not only did it calmly continue to exist, but its foundation grew stronger day by day. Seeing this, people realized it was not like the Paris Commune (巴里 콤뮨), which appeared briefly and ceased, but that its essence was communist socialism (共産社會主義) based on Marx's economic thought (맑스經濟思想). Consequently, the revolutionary proletariat (無産階級) worldwide became even more firmly convinced of future victory. Meanwhile, the hungry ghosts that appeared in Chosŏn were the livelihood difficulties of all Chosŏn people. Chosŏn had barely sustained itself with a portion of its natural products, namely grains, but fell into a state where it was completely unable to produce manufactured goods (製造品). As life gradually modernized, the burden <42> of public levies (公課) increased dramatically, and daily expenses swelled, inevitably leading to agony in poverty. Poverty reached its extreme, and 90% of the Chosŏn people groan on the starvation line (飢渴線).

Today, no matter how beautifully a nation's colonial policy is adorned with fine words (美辭), its essence is to XX the XX of the colony. Recently, the British, using a cunning interpretation (巧詐), stopped calling India a colony and referred to it as the Indian Empire because the British Emperor also held a coronation ceremony in India. However, the substance cannot possibly change. The interests of the minority British are always prioritized, and the survival of the majority Indians is not considered. As evidence, the import duties on Indian yarn and cotton cloth (線系 及 線布) were abolished, forcing over 220 Indian spinning factories (紡績工場) to inevitably close, making their supply permanently dependent on Lancashire in the British homeland. Other industries possible to produce in large quantities in India, such as hemp production (製麻), paper making (製紙), and silk weaving (織絹業), met the same fate. According to an investigation by the Englishman Lord Curzon (커슨 卿), famous as an apologist for British politics, the average annual income of an Indian peasant was 13 *wŏn* 30 *chŏn* per person. Indians with so little leeway suffered the tragedy of 5.22 million starving to death (餓死) during the great famine of 1877-78. The lesson is not far off (殷鑑不遠). Does not XX's XXXX, which completely XX Britain's policy towards India, cause the XX people not to XX in the XX XX? XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX They always cite numbers to exaggerate Chosŏn's development, but that is entirely the economic development and profit of the Japanese in Chosŏn. Isn't the livelihood of the Chosŏn people inversely proportional to this, racing towards destitution (窮境)? The reports of those numbers mostly refer to the economic development of the Japanese in Chosŏn. As the scope of Chosŏn people's production rather shrinks progressively, the standard of living falls extremely low, and all of Chosŏn is truly turning into a den of hungry ghosts (餓鬼窟). The average production value per Chosŏn person is said to be 5 *wŏn* 75 *chŏn* per year, or 48 *chŏn* per month. If Indians cried out that they reached the point of starvation (飢渴点) with an income of 13 *wŏn*, then Chosŏn people can only be said to stand on the line of hungry ghosts (餓鬼線). Furthermore, there is likely no place where the so-called collapse of the middle class (中産者) has happened as rapidly as in Chosŏn. Skilled XXXX has been added to the withered Chosŏn economy, so its defeat is an inevitable destiny (定數). Land is being consolidated by large landowners at an extraordinary speed, and owner-farmers are daily becoming tenant farmers (小作農). Those who manage to eat today are merely facing tomorrow's fate while cutting their own flesh to fill their bellies (割肉充腹).

The meaning of national XX is too weakly described by mere political sentiments like historical stains (汚損) or ethnic humiliation (恥辱). <43> Now, the true meaning of XX lies in making the conquered people serve as XX for XXXX economically on the spot. Although Chosŏn people were not unaware of economic deprivation during the March First Movement, there was a clear preparatory consciousness to focus on production after gaining political liberation. However, as that ideal XX XX, the Chosŏn people came to stand facing darkness again. The economic situation, pressing relentlessly and accelerating without a moment's pause, makes it a natural trend for the Chosŏn people to seek a ray of light (一條光明) towards the X side. Here, the evil of politics lies not within politics itself, but XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX. Since Marx (맑스), a German (Jew) (獨逸(猶太)人), was the one who systematically and theoretically completed these crucial matters, Chosŏn's intellectual class and social activists competitively began to study Marx's systematic XXXX-ism. Some say this trend was instigated by foreign propaganda influencing Chosŏn people, but that is a superficial observation (皮相). In reality, it is an ideology born from the society's instinct to explore a new path (새 活路) as an inevitable demand arising from Chosŏn's economic ruin. It's just that Marx's doctrine indicated a method for organized movement (組織的 運動力法), and XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX. Thus born, Chosŏn's socialist economic thought, though its history is short (日淺), charges forward vigorously in direct proportion to the economic distress, becoming an important issue in the Chosŏn intellectual world.

Chosŏn socialists, raising their first voice, shouted for the dissolution of the nation (民族 解體). Under the current economic system, they say, the relationship of friend and foe depends more on wealth or poverty than on ethnic affinity, identifying enemy soldiers within one's own side and welcoming rebel forces from the enemy's midst. Witnessing a small number of rich Chosŏn people making the majority poor labor, enjoying a life of pleasure without moving their own hands and feet, using the peasants' sweat and blood (汗血), the propertyless intellectuals (無産有識者) first took hostile actions against the Chosŏn rich, beginning the militant trials of the propertyless. Thus, tenant farmers in various regions united and showed signs of resisting landlords, demanding reductions in tenant fees (小作料). Secondly, based on the Communist Manifesto's declaration "Workers of the world, unite!" (萬國 勞動者는 단결하라), the Chosŏn proletariat tries to approach and ally with the Japanese proletariat to challenge their common enemies, the Chosŏn rich and Japanese capitalists. The meaning is roughly <44> this: "The 'national unity' (민족일치) promoted by the old-era Chosŏn patriots (朝鮮志士) – for the two classes of rich and poor with conflicting interests within Chosŏn to unite and confront all Japanese people, who also have conflicting interests under the same logic in Japan – is a major contradiction. Rather, the Japanese proletariat not only cannot participate in acquiring rights and interests in Chosŏn but are also being exploited (搾取) by capitalists within Japan. Let's seek new brothers in Japan, unite, and regard all those belonging to the capitalist class as enemies." Also, the movement to promote Chosŏn products (朝鮮 物産獎勵) that spontaneously arose in various places since the year before last, while affirmable in the sense of rejecting foreign goods for the sake of Chosŏn industry under independence-ism (commonly called nationalism, though nationalism hasn't formed), ultimately only results in losses for the proletariat due to high prices. Therefore, they opposed it, calling it a selfish movement of Chosŏn capitalists and the middle class. In this way, the scope of political consciousness in the Chosŏn intellectual world has shrunk, the new domain of economic consciousness has expanded, and the traces of its movement's significance becoming globalized, not confined (局限) to Chosŏn, are evident (歷然).

Chosŏn's thought has certainly become globalized, and it seeks its principles in Marx's scientific doctrine. However, if we now examine Chosŏn society with an extremely cool, objective attitude, we cannot help but despair again, even according to Marx's doctrine. Why? Because when Marx completed his doctrine, he obtained his materials from the economic conditions of Europe where capitalism had developed smoothly, and established his evidence and theory based on that; it was not theorized based on the economic relations of a society under modern colonial policy, that is, economic relations where the productive forces have ample room for development but are suffering distorted suffocation (窒息) due to political strategies. Of course, Marx's general laws are truths universally applicable to any society, so there's no reason Chosŏn would escape this. However, overlooking the fact that Chosŏn's economy inevitably bears a special characteristic due to artificial policies means losing the secret key (秘鍵) to revolution. According to the gist of Marx's materialist law of cause and effect, after the emergence of a capitalist economic organization in human society which had lived communally for long ages, based on the relationship of the strong plundering and the weak being exploited, politically passing through feudalism where industry developed locally, and economically passing through the industrial revolution where industry developed divisionally, the more the capitalists' exploitation increases, the more the workers' consciousness and unity expand. As production develops, contradictions arise within the current economic system, and simultaneously, the workers' revolutionary spirit intensifies, thus the downfall of the capitalist class <45> approaches, and the victory of the proletariat class becomes inevitable. However, none of Chosŏn society's production relations show any trace of having passed through such a smooth path. In the past, it did not politically go through feudalism, economically lacked the opportunity for an industrial revolution, and currently, while there is ample room for further production development, it is halted due to policy XX. Therefore, some Chosŏn thinkers say, "Chosŏn must inevitably leap over the stage of industrial development and XXXXXXXXXXXXX." That's right. At this point, even if we didn't want to leap over it, there's nothing else we can do. But then, should we strive to develop Chosŏn's industry like Europe's, and tolerate capitalism while implementing it? Thinkers aiming to XX XX do not affirm this. However, one cannot overlook the vast essential difference in the content of the XX-subject (proletariat) because one has passed through the stage of industrial development and the other has leaped over it. European workers are skilled in tactics, their unity is solid, and they also possess superior production intelligence and all the advantageous conditions of socialized industry. Therefore, if they just seize the means of production, the rest of the system can advance with the force of splitting bamboo (破竹之勢). However, in Chosŏn, industry has unknowingly shrunk (零縮), means of living have dried up (枯渴), the populace is driven to the starvation line (飢渴線), and fallen into poverty without any training; XX element has all the disadvantageous flaws. Even if one affirms the makeshift subjective excuse, "Facing the construction of a new society, we will promote production," one cannot rest assured. The Chosŏn proletariat, being in a more desperate plight (窮境) than any other proletariat in the world, is not in an active position for the great XX, unprecedented in human history, of fundamentally transforming the world economic structure. Human physiological survival demands food without interruption even for a single day; isn't the economic revolution idealized by socialism rather too slow (遲緩)? It demands greater urgency. Also, according to Marx's opinion, "If a revolution occurs in a society where production development is lacking, its productive capacity either declines or it perishes as is." Even if we deliberately omit the word "perish" (滅亡) here for the sake of Chosŏn people's subjective pride, if Chosŏn's production declines further than now, the result will reach an unimaginably tragic state (慘境). This is by no means arguing to XX XX to preserve the stagnant productive forces as they are, but rather stating that Chosŏn, viewed economically, is in a uniquely miserable situation even among all the world's colonies. Regarding this point, Chosŏn socialists must pay close attention and find the correct path for their direction, otherwise, when the world proletariat <46> rejoices in the new society, the Chosŏn people will perish nameless.

Towards the author who has made such a judgment, some might angrily shout, "You counter-revolutionary! Are you saying we should obey any irrationality until production develops, hesitate at XX fearing production decline, and self-destruct in a state where production is impossible?" To that, the author would reply, "Faithful disciple of Marx! Your revolution is too global, your assertion too economic, thus there is rather a danger of long obedience to irrationality, and rather indifference towards the extinction (泯滅) of Chosŏn." Before propagandizing to the still untrained and ignorant Chosŏn proletariat as if a communist society will be realized tomorrow, make them think today about what is the driving force destroying Chosŏn. Within the scope of social XX, the production relations of that society serve as a guide, indicating sometimes an economic revolution, sometimes judging it to be political XX. Those who study Marx's theory as economic scholarship can be left alone to become world citizens absorbed in European revolutionary theory, but if one intends to conduct social movements in Chosŏn, one must at least start from the specificity of Chosŏn society, connected to the past and present through complex causal relationships. The recitation of propagandistic single phrases expressing the universality of XX has extremely weak effect. Considering the current state of Russia (露國), although their highest ideal is a complete communist system, they are currently adopting a policy of concentrating capital in the state, striving to further socialize and scientize the insufficiently developed production relations from the old society, and then shed the empty shell (虛殼) of the state. The Russian Revolution was generally more political than economic. If Germany (獨逸), whose production relations are more advanced, were to start a revolution right now, the economic part would likely be larger than the political part. **Summary**

This 1924 article by "O Min" analyzes the shift in Korean thought following the failed March First Movement, from nationalist independence aspirations towards socialist ideologies inspired by Marx. The author attributes this shift primarily to the extreme economic hardship and perceived exploitation under Japanese colonial rule, which he details with comparisons to British India and stark statistics about Korean poverty. However, the author critically argues that directly applying European Marxist theory, born from industrialized capitalist societies, is problematic for Korea, whose production is underdeveloped and artificially suppressed by colonial policy. He warns that a premature revolution, focused solely on international proletarian solidarity or abstract economic ideals without considering Korea's specific conditions (lack of industrial base, peasant majority), could lead to further production decline and national ruin. The author advocates for a more pragmatic approach, urging socialists to focus first on awakening and uniting the Korean peasantry, potentially cooperating with certain nationalist elements ("old comrades"), and tailoring revolutionary strategy to Korea's unique socio-economic reality rather than blindly following international models or focusing solely on class struggle within Korea.

Historians would find this article valuable for its insight into the intellectual ferment and strategic debates among Korean thinkers during the colonial period, particularly the tension between nationalism and emerging socialism. It provides a contemporary perspective on how Marxist ideas were being adapted (or debated regarding adaptation) to a non-Western, colonial context. The detailed description of perceived economic devastation, the critique of applying foreign ideologies wholesale, and the call for a focus on peasant mobilization offer significant insights into the complexities of Korean anti-colonial thought and the socio-economic conditions shaping it. The heavy censorship (XX) also reveals the constraints under which such discussions took place.

조선의 사상계는 三一運動을 기회삼어 일대 전환하얏다. 그전의 朝鮮사상의 主潮에는 정치적 자유주의 즉 독립주의이엇섯다. 朝鮮政治保護條約이 성립되얏슬 때에 우리의 선배는, 은근히 越南亡國史를 가리치며 波蘭滅亡記를 密談케 하얏고 또, 朝鮮의 전제정치가 멸망을 因致하얏다 하야 군주입헌정체를 목표삼은 勤王愛國主義를 고조하얏슴을 지금 생각하야보면 可笑할 일이나 그 당시에는 은연히 세력잇든 정치사상이엿섯다. 其後 정치의 변동이 更히 一步를 進하야 日本이 朝鮮을 병합하얏다고 발표하게 되매 朝鮮의 군주가 자연히 XX되얏슴으로써 勤王煽動은 졸지에 퇴색되야 극소수의 전관료와 班族, 頑儒를 除한 외에는 일반 청년은 민주적 애국의 색채가 농후하게 되얏다. 그리하야 抹滅되랴든 朝鮮歷史를 강구하야 민족적 자부심을 배양하랴 하얏고 또 근대 정치의 최고이상이 共和代議制인 줄 思惟한 까닭에 소위 志士들이 국내외에 분주할 때에 지방열병에 전신이 마비되면서도, 장래 朝鮮의 政體의 이상에 관하얀, 거의 이론업시 此에 歸一하얏슴으로,＜41＞ 朝鮮당국에서는 그것이 朝鮮에서 자연히 발견된 政見인 줄 모르고, 米國宣敎師가, 교회에서, 학교에서 朝鮮학생에게 米國정신을 양성한다하야, 걱정끗헤 日本耶蘇인 조합교회를 載來하는 활극까지 잇섯다. 당시에 유명하얏던 宣川事件이 잇슨 후 그 반동으로 다수의 청년은 꼬틕건축의 색유리창 밋헤서 그 억울과 원한을 祈禱를 통하야 상제에게 告하얏고, 지상의 軍式監視는 一步의 진행을 용납치 아니하얏다. 얼마 아니되야서 歐洲의 대전이 돌발되야 그의 범위가 점차 확대됨을 따러서 軍國의 殘虐이 극악에 달한 후 終局을 告할 際에 비로소, 온 세계사람은 전쟁의 慘害와 정복의 죄악을 족음, 깨닷게 되얏다. 그래서 巴里講和談判席上에서 故人 월손氏의 제창한 바 民族自決이란 표제가, 전세계의 약소국민과 식민지 사람에게 신활로를 개척하는 듯 하야 동서양에서 피정복자의 동란이 일어낫다. 그 班列에 빠지지 아니하는 朝鮮은 赤手며 독립운동을 일으켯스니 이것이 곳 우리에게 기억이 선명한 三一運動이다.
