To Brother Nagao-taek Again
						Ch'unp'a
From a Seoul Mouse to a Country Mouse (2)
Brother Nagao-taek. Like the habits of those human bastards, you too sit there wearing a hat of falsehood, just snatching up and swallowing any letter that arrives. How is it that there's been no word for over a month since I sent my letter? Have you been massacred (虐殺) by the human bastards like the young master house mouse at our place? Did the mouse plague (Cholera - 虎列刺) strike, leading to a mass funeral? Why won't you tell me the news from the countryside that I so earnestly want to know?
But brother, don't be too angry. We shouldn't get flared up over trivial matters like fingernails or burst out laughing "haehae-" over things as small as a needle's point, like those human bastards do. No matter how much we bear the name 'mouse' and crawl around under the ceilings or floors of human houses, don't we still hold the high and dignified responsibility of overseeing the human world? Isn't that right? Therefore, we must exercise self-restraint in all things. Yes, brother.
Brother. The doings of human bastards are truly unpredictable. This way and that, back and forth, they seem to have no fixed standard. Seen one way, they seem pitiful; seen another, detestable (可憎); and seen yet another way, they seem plausible. But brother.
As you know, brother, I speak of the year Kiminyŏn (己未年), just six years ago. You surely remember clearly what the Chosŏn human bastards were doing then, right? Declaring independence, seeking autonomy, shouting "Manse!" Didn't men and women, young and old, cause a huge commotion throughout the capital and countryside (京鄕)? Yet today, barely four or five years later <95>, they seem to have quieted down as if nothing happened. Though they were indeed suppressed quite severely, perhaps their energy was just that limited. It seems like they just made noise once on a passing breeze without any definite calculation (成算). We mice held a meeting about tying a bell on the cat's neck and danced merrily, but when it came to "Who could possibly take responsibility?", we all just stared wide-eyed, wagged our tails, and ran away – such things happened to us too. Setting that aside (姑捨), just look at this.
Saying things like Socialism, Communism, Anarchism, World Familism, they push aside issues like independence or the nation as outdated (時代遲) (perhaps because they can't achieve them?), thrusting them behind their heels. If any bastard mentions such things, they sneer at them as rotten or foul-smelling, or else launch attacks (攻撲) against them.
Brother. Just look at this. A while ago, a certain human bastard published a magazine called OOO, advocating for National Unity for quite some time, but after facing a major attack (大攻撲) from some bastards, he seemed to shrink away (?). Recently, something called OOOO has been making noise about Great Unity (大同團結) or political something-or-other, but bastards who were in Tokyo and bastards living in Chinju all joined in with harsh questioning, causing such a headache that they just seem to be sighing deeply, "Hoo-."
Brother. Quietly observing the actions of human bastards reveals many different layers (層節). It seems their heads ache too. One bastard talks of independence, another of Communism, another of OO; they run this way and that. Ten, a hundred bastards pull at one bundle from all directions, ultimately tearing it to shreds. Truly a spectacle (可觀).
But what's even funnier is that there are bastards who, completely exhausted (氣盡脉盡), plunge headfirst into the pit of despair and disappointment and collapse for good. And then there are bastards who follow behind them, wailing "Aigo! Jigo!" with reddened eyes, sniffling profusely. Among them, those with some remaining energy (餘氣) casually step on the backs of the fallen and pass by, spitting contemptuously. The cruelty among human bastards seems considerable. There are also those who try to stop them from doing so, but the bastards wearing red armbands (빩안 테 두룬 놈들) fiercely block their path. Anyway, those bastards are quite ferocious (獰惡). No matter which rope some bastard pulls or where they run off to, no matter how secretly they try to flee, they are surely caught and held. Those fellows discover things like ghosts <96>. We mice boast quite a bit about our detective work (探偵) and are reputed to be famous, but those fellows are quite good too. Recently, there have been trials for the Ŭiyŏltan Incident (義烈團事件) and whatnot... but let's stop talking about such things.

Brother Nagao-taek. The Chosŏn human bastards celebrate New Year's twice, by the solar calendar (陽曆) and the lunar calendar (陰曆). Is it the same for the country folk? Perhaps even more so in the countryside. Anyway, habits seem to be fearsome things. They say they won't do it, yet they still do. Because they celebrate New Year's twice, our mouse society becomes rather abundant... Indeed, on the Solar New Year, we got to nibble heartily on rice bowls at the houses of Western-clothed folk, and this Lunar New Year, we received rice-cake soup morning and evening at the houses of top-knotted folk. Today is the Great Full Moon of the first month, so thanks to the human bastards, we get to eat quite a lot of delicious, sweet things like chestnuts (栗) and jujubes. But brother.
No matter how I look at it, the Chosŏn human bastards seem to live by being deceived by other bastards. With eyes wide open, they just get tricked and fall for it.
The Japanese bastards and Chinese bastards are quite cunning. They take and take from the Chosŏn bastards until they even cleanly snatch away the snot-nosed money of little children. Setting other things aside (姑捨), just the money they took for children's toys during this Lunar New Year and the Great Full Moon probably amounts to several thousand, maybe tens of thousands of won.
If you quietly go out onto the main street and sit picking up and chewing on fallen jujubes or chestnuts, amidst the flashing red and blue five-colored paper, they buy all sorts of nameless toys – it's truly a spectacle (可觀). Things like rubber balls, horses, cats, dolls, cars.
Things like tin plates, trumpets (喇叭), harmonicas, flutes (洞簫). Things like paper cards (딱지), Hwatu cards (花鬪), made from dung paper (馬糞紙). They buy all sorts of strange and bizarre things, lining up one after another, and the ones who made them are entirely Japanese bastards. Seeing the Japanese bastards taking things like this, the inwardly treacherous (內凶) Chinese bastards, who jump at the sight of money, think, "Hey- this looks profitable. Chosŏn bastards are easy prey, let me get a piece too. Why should only the Japanese eat?" And so, one by one, they sit down on the street and start luring the children, which is also a spectacle (亦 可觀).
Never mind hawthorn berries or sugar-coated beans; they sell those so-called cap guns (딱총) or firecracker paper tubes (海花紙筩) – those red things filled with gunpowder in the middle with a string attached. In the blink of an eye <97>, the snot-nosed copper coins of the children flow into those bastards' pockets by the bucketful.
Brother. No matter how you look at it, Chosŏn bastards are soft (물금니다). They are not ferocious (獰惡하지 못하외다). They are easily deceived. They readily give things away to others. They whine about having nothing to eat or wear, yet they seem to have money to offer to the Japanese and Chinese bastards. Saying they look pitiful one moment and detestable the next refers to this. Let's stop talking about this too.

Brother. Last New Year (年初), the human bastards seemed quite lively and confrontational, but this New Year, they seem extremely desolate. Last year, there was a fistfight at Kugilgwan (國一館), a bomb exploded on Chongno (鍾路), a person collapsed near Namdaemun (남대문), and gunfire echoed faintly in Hyoje-dong (孝悌洞). But perhaps that energy has also been exhausted (氣脉이 盡하얏는지), as this year has become silent as a house after a failed shaman ritual (寂然無聞).
Recently, there seems to be quite a commotion with talks of holding a Labor-Farmer Rally (勞農大會), forming a Youth General Alliance (靑年總同盟), and the emergence of the Emerging Youth Association (新興靑年會). But we'll see how it turns out; what will happen this time? Are the actions of humans (사들의 하는 줏) untrustworthy? Anyway, I'll keep watching and let you know more later.

Brother. Word just came that Uncle Blue-Fur (靑毛宅아졋씨) has been massacred again. It seems while crossing from the wall of the Separate Palace (別宮) directly to the restaurant opposite, he fell under the shoe-clad feet of quick-eyed children and died with his belly burst. Ai, truly, there are many heartbreaking incidents.
Brother. Let's put aside all this and that, and let us too form a General Alliance (總同盟) and adopt a counter-strategy (復歸策) against the human bastards. As the first step, let's enter their houses and persistently gnaw holes everywhere – floor, walls, ceiling – so smoke leaks out and throws them into disarray. Let's gnaw holes in the roof so rain constantly leaks in. Let's dig under the crock stand so the jars topple over... Let's keep doing this, causing destruction (破壤), so those bastards cannot live in this world. Perhaps you'll laugh, saying it's like hitting a rock with an egg (以卵擊石), but just as they have their saying "The masses can overcome heaven" (人衆勝天), is there a law that says "A multitude of mice cannot overcome humans" (鼠衆勝人)? I await your reply (回意) and merely pose the question for now. Please think hard and send me your ideas (考案). That's all for now- <98>
<95-98>

**Summary**

Writing as a "Seoul mouse" to a "country mouse," the author Ch'unp'a uses allegory to critique the state of Korean society under Japanese rule in 1924. The narrator laments the fading spirit of the 1919 independence movement, criticizing the subsequent ideological fragmentation (socialism, communism, anarchism vs. nationalism), infighting, and perceived weakness ("softness," easily deceived) of Koreans. The article highlights the economic exploitation by Japanese and Chinese merchants, especially targeting children during holidays. It concludes with a satirical call for mice (representing the oppressed Koreans) to unite and wage a campaign of sabotage against humans (the oppressors and perhaps the complacent Koreans themselves). The piece is historically interesting for its candid, albeit allegorical, portrayal of disillusionment following the 3.1 Movement, the rise of new ideologies, internal conflicts within the nationalist/socialist camps, awareness of economic vulnerability, and mentions of specific contemporary events like the Ŭiyŏltan trials and political organizing efforts (Labor-Farmer Rally, Youth Alliances). It offers a contemporary, critical perspective on the challenges and divisions facing Korean society in the mid-1920s.

다시 長尾宅兄님에게
						春坡
서울쥐로 시골쥐에게(2)
長尾宅형님. 형님도 사람놈들의 하는 버릇과 가티 허위의 감투를 쓰고 안저 남의 片紙가 가게 되면 낭큼 낭큼 집어 생키기요. 편지 한지가 달이 넘도록 엇지하야 아무런 소식이 업슴니까. 우리집 생쥐 샌님과 가티 사람놈들에게 虐殺을 당하섯소. 쥐통(虎列刺)이 도라가 무리 葬事가 나섯소. 내가 그러케도 알들히 알고 십허하는 시골 소식을 엇지면 그러케도 안알켜주오.
그러나 형님 과히 노여워하지는 마십시요. 사람놈들과 가티 손톱만한 일에도 샛득샛득 노염을 내거나 바눌 끗만한 일에도 해해- 하고 웃어 버릇해서는 안됨니다. 우리가 아무리 쥐라는 이름을 쓰고 사람놈들의 집 천정이나 마루 밋흐로 기여다닌다 할지라도 그래도 사람놈들의 세상을 감독하는 놉고 점잔은 책임을 가지지 안엇서요. 그럿치요. 그러니까 우리는 모든 것에 자중하여야 됨니다. 네 형님.
형님. 사람놈들의 하는 짓이란 과연 대종 잡을 수 업슴니다. 이러쿵 저러쿵 요랫다 조랫다 일정한 표준이 업나 보아요. 엇더케 보면 불상도 하고 엇더케 보면 可憎도 하고 또 엇더케  보면 그럴 듯도 해요. 그런데 형님.
형님도 아시다십히 지나간 바로 6년 전 己未年 말슴이외다. 그때 朝鮮 사람놈들의 하든 일을 역역히 기억하시겟지요? 독립을 하느니 자주를 하느니 선언을 하며 만세를 부르며 京鄕이 떠나가게 남녀노소가 두루 법석을 치디 안엇섯요? 그리든 놈들이 불과 4, 5년이 ＜95＞ 못된 今日에 와서는 시시나 한 듯 하구려. 원체 눌니우기도 어지간히 눌녓지만. 그놈들의 기력도 아마 그 뿐인가봐요. 아모런 成算이 업시 지나가는 바람결에 한번 떠드른 것 갓타요. 우리 쥐들은 고양이 목에 방울 매여 달자는 회의를 하고 당실당실 춤을 추다가 급기야 『누가 능히 그 책임자가 될』가 함에서 서로 눈이 똥글하야 꼬리를 흔들며 다- 도망하든 그런 일도 이섯지만요. 그는 姑捨하고 글세 이것 좀 보셔요.
社會主義니 共産主義니 無國家主義니 世界一家主義니 하야 독립문제니 민족문제니 그 따위는 발서 時代遲라고 (할 수가 업스닛가 그런지?) 발꿈치 뒤로 미러 놋코 엇던 놈이 그런 소리만 해도 썩어진 놈이니 냄새 나는 작자니 하야 픽픽 웃거나 그러치 안으면 攻撲을 막하는 구려.
형님. 글세 이것 좀 봐요. 얼마 전에 엇던 사람놈 한아가 OOO이란 잡지를 하면서 民族一致란 것을 한참을 주장하다가 엇던 놈들에게 大攻撲을 바다 그만 쑥으러지는 듯하더니(?) 근일에 와서는 OOOO이란 것이 大同團結이니 정치적 무어니 하고 떠들다가 동경가 잇던 놈 晋州 사는 놈 모다 드러부터 혹독한 질문을 하는 바람에 그만 머리ㅅ살이 압흔지 「후-」하고 長太息만 부르짓나봐요.
형님. 가만이 서 사람놈들의 하는 짓을 엿보노라면 別別層節이 만하요. 그놈들도 머리ㅅ살이 압흔가봐요. 엇던 놈은 독립을 하느니 엇던 놈은 共産을 하느니 엇던 놈은 00을 하느니 서로 이리 달아나고 져리 달아나고 보잭이 하나를 열놈 100놈이 四面八方으로 잡아 끄으니 결국은 산산히 찟기고 마는구려. 참 可觀이외다.
그런데 더욱 우스은 것은 가다가 氣盡脉盡하야 낙심 절망의 웅텅이에 코틀 박고 영영 꺽구러지는 놈이 잇는데 그놈의 뒷통수에 따라부터 애고지고 눈이 빩애지도록 콜작콜작 우는 놈도 잇슴니다. 그 중에 좀 餘氣가 잇다는 놈은 그러케 넘어진 놈의 등살을 함부로 발고 지내가며 침을 탁탁 밧는 놈도 잇슴니다. 사람놈 사람놈끼리의 학대가 여간이 안인가봐요. 그리고 그리지 말나고 말니는 놈도 잇는데 빩안 테 두룬 놈들은 지독하게도 그 놈들의 길을 막슴니다. 엇잿든 그 놈들은 꽤 獰惡해요. 엇던 놈이 엇던 줄을 끌고 어대로 달아나든지 아무리 비밀히 달아난다 해도 꼭꼭 붓드러 놋는구려. 고놈들 참 귀신가티도 ＜96＞ 발견을 해요. 우리가 探偵으로는 꽤 壯談도 하고 유명하다는 평도 듯지만 고놈들도 꽤 잘 하든데요. 近日에도 義烈團事件이니 무어니 하고 공판을 하는 일이 잇지만... 그 까짓 말은 그만하고.

長尾宅 형님. 朝鮮 사람놈들은 陽曆이니 陰曆이니 하고 설을 두 번이나 쇄요. 시골 놈들도 그럿치요? 아마 시골은 더 하리이다. 엇잿든 습관이란 무서운 것인가 봐요. 그놈들도 그러지 안는다고 하면서 그래도 그리는구려. 그 놈들이 설 두번 쇠는 바람에 우리네 쥐들 사회는 좀 풍성풍성하지만... 안인게 안이라 만졋번 陽曆설날은 양복쟁이 집에서 쌀밥그릇이나 톡톡이 어더 먹엇고 이번 陰曆설에는 상투쟁이 집에서 떡국을 아츰저녁으로 어더 먹엇는데 오늘은 正月大보름날이라 하야 사람놈들의 덕분에 밤(栗)과 대추 등 고소하고 달큼한 것을 꽤 만히 어더 먹게 됨니다. 그런데 형님.
