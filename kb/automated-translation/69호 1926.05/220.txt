Author: 編輯者 (Editor)
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Announcement/Editor's Note)

We are grateful for the contribution to this magazine (本誌) from Mr. Kurihara (栗原), who recently entered the capital (入京) and then returned to Tokyo (東京). We would like to briefly introduce Mr. Kurihara: he is a social activist (社會運動者) (a Japanese anarchist) and not an artist (藝術家) or critic (評論家). However, as a social activist, he came to Chosŏn (朝鮮), observed the conditions (形便) of this land, and wrote these impressions (感想) or expectations (期待) with an eye toward art. It is not incorrect to say that he wrote this piece from the standpoint (立脚地) of an anarchist. Therefore, the publication (登載) of this piece is not something for which the editor personally bears responsibility (責任); we state, from the position (處地) of the magazine editor, that it is merely an introduction of his thoughts through the medium of a magazine (雜誌). <111>
-Editor (編輯者)-

**Summary**

This editor's note introduces a contribution from a Japanese anarchist, Mr. Kurihara, who recently visited Korea (Chosŏn). The editor clarifies that Kurihara is a social activist, not an artist or critic, and wrote his piece based on observations of Korea from an anarchist perspective. Crucially, the editor distances the magazine from Kurihara's views, stating that the publication is merely an introduction of his thoughts and does not imply endorsement, absolving the editor of personal responsibility for the content. For historians, this note is interesting as it reveals the complex dynamics of publishing potentially sensitive material (an anarchist's view on colonial Korea) in a Korean magazine under Japanese rule. The editor's careful disclaimer highlights the constraints and risks faced by Korean publishers during this period, while also showing a willingness to introduce diverse, potentially critical viewpoints, albeit cautiously.
