Is It Moving from Introduction to Main Body?

When speaking of the Governor-General's rule, one naturally divides it into the periods before and after Kimi [1919]. Consequently, just as speaking of the period before Kimi evokes Terauchi-style military rule (武斷政治), speaking of the period after Kimi evokes Saitō-style Cultural Policy (文化政策). That the preceding military rule was replaced by a Cultural Policy! This single phrase was indeed the sole slogan and signboard of the post-Kimi Government-General of Chosŏn, both internally towards the Korean people and externally in the eyes and ears of foreigners, and in reality, it was something irreplaceable.

Under the signboard of Cultural Policy, although various items were listed, first, respecting the general will of the people and giving considerable thought to speech, assembly, and association was quite central. Following that, educational reform, industrial development, and other various things were mentioned. However, what is the state of the Government-General's rule today, seven years after advocating such a Cultural Policy? Using the maintenance of public order as a pretext, they first expanded security organizations (for Chosŏn's administrative regions of 12 Bu, 2 Do, 218 Gun, and 2,503 Myeon, the security organs primarily consist of 250 police stations and 2,487 police substations). Next, they extensively repaired government-run railways, private railways, harbors large and small, and other first, second, and third-class roads, completing a land and sea transportation network. On top of that, they invited Japanese capitalists of both old and new types to directly take charge of managing (經略) Chosŏn, while completely forbidding any mention of this by the Chosŏn populace, thereby suppressing speech, assembly, and association today. Look. The authorities' policies, growing more severe moment by moment, this year compared to last year, this month compared to the previous month, have become increasingly overt (露骨化) and unrestrained (無忌憚化) since last autumn. They continuously subject newspapers and magazines, the sole eyes and ears of the Chosŏn masses, to seizure (押牧) and suspension of publication. Regarding all other assemblies and associations,<2> if they possess even the slightest socialist coloring in their nature, they are absolutely forbidden. It has gone so far that even members belonging to a certain organization are prevented from assembling for academic lectures. Taking another step further, they have reached the strange state of censoring the manuscripts even for children's story meetings (童話會) hosted by youth groups.

Thus, the Chosŏn masses today are fundamentally facing the destruction of their livelihood due to the economic exploitation by them [the Japanese], while simultaneously being unable to discuss even a single word regarding countermeasures. Just like during the 5th and 6th years of Taishō [1916-1917], Chosŏn's sun and moon descend into gloom and sorrow. Only the capitalist-style Governor-General's rule, which opened its curtain on September 2nd of Kimi [1919], shedding its shell of 'culture' and 'assimilation,' moves from the introduction to the main body, and from the main body, again, into the conclusion.

Regarding the situation in Chosŏn, which deepens daily like this, we have no freedom to express any subjective opinions. At the same time, we can only point out a part of the unfolding situation as it is. At the end of these words, however, there is just one thing to add: Those so-called ruling authorities point to us, who hold the pen like this, or to the activists (主義者) engaged in social movements, calling them disturbers of public peace or inciters of ethnic and class consciousness between Koreans and Japanese, and apply all sorts of pressure (逼迫). However, in reality (其實), we are not disturbers of any public peace, nor are we inciters of ethnic or class consciousness. We are merely pointing out, a little ahead of time, a part of the situation or aspect of Chosŏn that emerges as a consequence of the ruling authorities—who serve as clerks and guards (隨護員) for the Japanese financial cliques (日本資閥)—implementing the policies they wish to pursue first. It is just like a meteorologist (氣象學者) examining the atmospheric pressure relationships here and there to foresee and forecast the coming weather. If someone were here to say to the meteorologist, "Hey you, weather forecasts saying 'tomorrow will be a storm' or whatnot are altogether dangerous words. Get rid of them all," how could that be an appropriate act? Furthermore, even if one could stop the weather forecast, could one stop the weather change itself? Alas! (吁).<3>

**Summary**

This 1926 editorial from the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* argues that the Japanese Government-General's "Cultural Policy," implemented after the 1919 March First Movement, is a deceptive facade. The author contends that beneath the surface of reforms, the colonial administration is intensifying its control and economic exploitation, evidenced by expanding police presence, developing infrastructure primarily for Japanese benefit, inviting Japanese capitalists, and severely suppressing Korean freedom of speech, assembly, and association, particularly targeting any socialist-leaning groups. The author compares the tightening control to the earlier, overt military rule and states the current phase is the "main body" of colonial exploitation, no longer masked by the "introduction" of cultural rhetoric. For historians, the article offers a contemporary critique of the "Cultural Rule" period, highlighting Korean perceptions of its hypocrisy, the specific mechanisms of control (police expansion, censorship, suppression of groups), the focus on economic exploitation benefiting Japanese capital, and the comparison drawn to the preceding military rule era. The author's use of the weather forecast metaphor to defend their critical reporting as merely observing reality, not inciting unrest, is also revealing about the constraints and dangers faced by Korean intellectuals and journalists.

序論으로부터 本論에 드는 格이냐
總督政治를 말하려면 스스로 己未以前과 己未以後를 갈나서 보게 되며 따라서 己未以前을 말할 때에 寺內式의 무단정치를 연상함과도 가티 己未以後를 말할 때에 齌蕂式의 文化政策을 聯想하게 된다. 在來의 武斷政治에 代하되 文化政策으로써 하엿다는 것! 이 한 말이야말로 안으로 朝鮮사람에 대해서나 밧그로 外國人耳目에 대해서나 己未以後朝鮮總督府로서의 唯一한 標榜이엿스며 看板이엿는 同時에, 實로 무엇으로써도 밧굴 수 업는 것이엿다.
文化政治라는 그 看板下에는 勿論 여러가지를 列記하여섯지만 먼저 一般의 民意를 尊重하야 言論, 集會, 結社에 대하야는 相當히 考慮한다는 것이 꽤 主要한 것이엿스며 그 다음으로 敎育의 改新, 産業의 發展 其他 무엇 무엇을 云謂하엿던 것이다. 그런데 이러한 文化政治를 標榜한 以後, 於焉 7年을 지낸 오늘의 總督府政治는 엇더한가. 治安維持를 憑藉하야 먼저 警備機關을 擴張하고 (朝鮮行政區域 12府, 2島, 218郡 2,503面에 대하야 警備機關은 爲先 警察署가 250, 駐在所가 2,487個所이다), 다음으로 官設鐵道, 私設鐵道, 大小의 港灣, 其他 1, 2, 3等의 道路를 大修하야, 海陸의 交通網을 完成하고 그 우에 在來種新來種의 日本人資本家들을 招致하야, 直接으로 朝鮮을 經略하는 任에 當케하되 이에 대한 朝鮮民衆의 云謂를 一切禁斷키 爲하야 言論, 集會, 結社를 그대로 壓迫하는 오늘이다. 보라. 今年이 昨年보다 今月이 前月보다 刻一刻甚해 가는 當局의 政策은 昨秋以來로 益益露骨化하며 無忌憚化하야 朝鮮大衆의 唯一한 眼目인 新聞, 雜誌를 連續的으로 押牧, 停刊하며, 其他의 모든 集會와 結社에 대하야도＜2＞ 그 性質이 多少나마 社會主義的 色彩를 帶한 者이면 絶對로 禁斷하야, 甚하야는 엇던 團軆의 그 소속 인원까지 集會하야 學術講演하는 것까지를 못하게 하며 또 한거름 나아가서는 少年團體의 主催인 童話會까지에도 그 原稿를 檢閱하는 奇狀을 致하얏다.
이리하야 오늘의 朝鮮大衆은 根本的으로 저들의 經濟的經略에 因한 生活이 破滅에 遭遇하는 同時에, 이에 대한 對策問題에 대하야는 한마듸의 論議도 하지 못하고 마치 大正 5, 6年의 그때와 가티 朝鮮의 日月은 暗憺沈痛한 속으로 들고 己未 9月 2日로써 開幕된 資本式 總督政治만이 文化니 融化니 하던 깍댁이를 벗고 序論으로부터 本論에 本論으로부터 또다시 結論에 드러가는 것이다.
우리는 이러케 나날히 深化해 가는 朝鮮의 時局에 對하야 아모러한 主觀의 意思를 發表할 自由가 업는 同時에 다못 展開되여가는 局面의 一部를 그대로 指摘할 뿐이거니와 이 말을 하는 끗헤 잇서 오즉 한마듸 말을 添付할 것은 所謂 爲政當局에 잇는 그들은 이와가티 붓대를 잡고 잇는 우리들이나 또는 社會運動을 하고 잇는 主義者를 가르켜 治安을 紊亂하는 者이니 鮮日人間의 民族感, 階級感을 鼓吹하는 者이니하며 가진 逼迫을 加하나 그러나 其實 우리는 何等의 治安을 紊亂하는 者도 아니며 民族感階級感을 鼓吹하는 者도 아니오 다못 日本資閥의 事務員되고 隨護員되는 爲政當局者가 自己하고 십흔 政策을 先行함으로 해서 나타나는 朝鮮의 局面 乃至 側面의 一部를 조곰 먼저 指摘함에 不過하다. 마치 氣像學者가 이곳저곳의 氣壓關係를 살피여 그 當來할 天氣를 豫見豫報함에 不過함과 가튼 것이다. 여긔에 엇던 者가 잇서 氣象學者다려 「이놈아 來日은 暴風雨니 무엇이니 하는 天氣豫報가 도모지 危險한 말이다. 모다 거더치여라」하면 그엇지 得當한 行爲이며 또 天氣豫報 그것은 못하게 할 수 잇다 할지라도 天氣變化 그것을 못하게 할 수 잇슬가. 吁.＜3＞
