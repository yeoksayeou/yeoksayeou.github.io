Greek Love Song
						(Translator) Powŏlsaeng

One who has not tried love, one whose love is in vain,
Cannot feel the anguish, nor taste the pity,
Of endless rejection, contemptuous eyes (瞳子),
Of the fleeting flashes that linger in a scornful glance of angry love.

Light of my life! Those lips and eyes that do not smile,
Ah, what change is this?
My loving little bird (小鳥)! My beautiful friend!
Have you changed, or have you grown weary (厭) of me?

My flowing blood vessels, and my maddened heart,
Amidst silence and sorrow (悲痛), I barely sustain them,
And while my heart tears apart, your heart,
Will likely leap with joy, without the slightest anguish (些少의 苦悶).

Ah, you, fearlessly sprinkle fierce poison (激毒) upon me!
You cannot slaughter me more severely than you do now,
I was born to resent my birthday (生日),
And love only extends slaughter like this.<97>

**Summary**

This article is a Korean translation of a poem, presented as a "Greek Love Song" from Lord Byron's collection, translated by someone with the pen name Powŏlsaeng (步月生). The poem expresses the intense pain, despair, and bitterness of unrequited or rejected love. The speaker laments the coldness of their beloved, the torment of seeing their indifference or joy while the speaker suffers, and resigns themselves to the idea that love itself is a form of destruction, making them curse the day they were born. From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as an early example of Western Romantic poetry being translated and published in Korea during the colonial period. It highlights the introduction of Western literary themes, particularly intense individual emotion and romantic suffering, into the Korean cultural sphere. The translator's identity (Powŏlsaeng) and the choice of Byron might also be subjects of interest for literary historians studying cultural exchange under Japanese rule.

希臘의 戀愛歌
						(譯)步月生
戀愛를 해보지 못한 사람, 헛되이 戀愛하는 사람은,
하염업는 拒絶, 蔑視하는 瞳子,
성낸 戀愛의 一瞥間에 멈으른 뻔적어림들의,
苦悶도 感得치 못하고 또한 哀憐도 맛보지 못한다.

나의 生命의 빗! 그 웃음치지 아니하는 입슐과 눈은,
아아 무슨 變化이냐,
戀愛하는 나의 작은 새(小鳥)! 나의 아름다운 벗!
그대는 變하엿나 또는 실혀(厭)젓나.

흐르는 나의 피줄과, 미친 나의 마음을,
沈黙과 悲痛 中에서 나는 僅僅이 支撑한다,
그리고 나의 가슴이 찌저져가는데, 그대의 가슴은,
些少의 苦悶도 업시 깃버 뛰놀리라.

아아 그대, 두려움 업시 激毒을 내게 뿌려라!
그대는 只今보다 더 甚히 나를 虐殺하지는 못하리라,
나는 나의 生日을 怨望하기 위하야 낫다,
그리고 戀愛는 이와 가티 殺害를 뻐칠 뿐이다.＜97＞
