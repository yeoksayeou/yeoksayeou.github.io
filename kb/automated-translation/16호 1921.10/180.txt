蜾蠃의 聲 -航陸漢이여-
						辛槿圃

맑고 먼(遠)
牛乳빗가튼 여름날 大氣속
微風에 흔들리는 柔枝에
惶忙히 몰아뜨던
허리(腰部) 잘눅(細)한
작고도 큰(力)蜾蠃는
奔走히도, 돌아든 (入)다
난간 안(軒內) 天井의
椽木 한 엽(一隅邊)가에.

그래 한참 後
樂譜에 마친가튼
淸雅한 曲調의 노래가
間斷업시 들린다, 그곳에서
노그만한 나의 귀에.
나는 보앗다(發見)
그 *薄한 나래(屢)를 저으면서
온갓 才幹을 다하야
土甁가튼 흙덩이(土塊)의 끗
조그만 구녕에 머리를 向하고
한곳에 떠날면서
간간이 우는(鳴)
부질업는 즛을

나는 꼿臺를 지엇다.
겻헤잇는 器物을 모아
그리고 그를 붓잡자 하얏다
또한 그 泥窟을 바스랴고
긴 棍棒을 가초아 쥐엇다.
그러나 그는
때의 急함과
形便의 危殆함을 모르는지
如前히 운다.
그 步調로 『간간이』

아-愚痴한 蜾嬴여?
類族이 판다른 남의 남을
化하야 自己가튼
異怪한 變軆로
맨들어 날리랴는
불상한 蜾蠃여

設使 그것이 뜻가티 된다 하더래도
-計劃은 成功되더래도-
動物인 同班의 怨呌와
그의 絶對慾求인 生의 갈릴 때의 勞憫
-또한 嘗膽할 決心이
『果然 어떠할가』함을 생각하라

그리고 깨달으라
무서운 나의 손과
구든 棍棒이
너의 뒤를 따라
將次 義의 憤을 披瀝하랴 함을.

아-말하라! 宣布하라!!
眞理가! 原則이
너도? 너의 智操로도!
螟蛉이 아니면 能치 못함을
絶對로!
나에게, 아니 一般衆生에게.
(8月 中旬 沙口私堂에서)＜103＞

Voice of the Mud Dauber - O Wanderer!
						Sin Kŭn-p'o

Clear and distant (遠)
In the milk-colored summer day's atmosphere (大氣)
On a tender branch (柔枝) swaying in the gentle breeze (微風)
Hurriedly (惶忙히) driving in
The thin-waisted (腰部 細)
Small yet strong (力) mud dauber (蜾蠃)
Busily (奔走히), enters (入).
Inside the railing (軒內), on the ceiling (天井)
To a corner side (一隅邊) of a rafter (椽木).

Then, after a while
Like it’s 맞춰matched to a musical score (樂譜)
A song of elegant (淸雅) melody (曲調)
Is heard without pause (間斷업시), from that place
To my smallish ear.
I saw (發見)
While beating those *thin wings (薄 屢)
Using all its talent (才幹)
Facing its head towards the small hole
At the end of the earthenware pot (土甁)-like lump of earth (土塊)
Hovering in one place
Occasionally crying out (鳴)
Doing seemingly useless things.

I made a stand.
Gathering the objects (器物) nearby
And intended to seize it
Also, to smash its mud cave (泥窟)
I prepared and grasped a long club (棍棒).
However, it
Does it not know the urgency (急함) of the time
And the peril (危殆함) of the situation (形便)?
It cries as before (如前).
At that tempo (步調) 『Occasionally』

Ah—Foolish (愚痴) mud dauber (蜾嬴)?
Trying to transform (化) another's kin, of a different kind (類族),
Into a strange transformed body (異怪한 變軆)
Like itself (自己)
To make it fly
Pitiful mud dauber (蜾蠃).

Even if that were to happen as intended
—Even if the plan (計劃) were to succeed (成功)—
Think about the resentment (怨呌) of its fellow creature (動物 同班)
And the anguish (勞憫) when its absolute desire (絶對慾求) for life (生) is severed
—Also, the determination (決心) to endure bitterness (嘗膽)
『Indeed (果然), what would that be like?』

And realize
That my fearsome hand
And the firm (구든) club (棍棒)
Follow behind you
Intending to soon (將次) unleash righteous (義) anger (憤).

Ah—Speak! Declare!!
That Truth (眞理)! Principle (原則)!
Even you? Even with your wisdom and integrity (智操)!
Cannot be achieved unless it is a *mingling* (螟蛉, adopted larva)
Absolutely (絶對로)!
To me, no, to all sentient beings (一般衆生).
(Mid-August, at Sagusa Tang (沙口私堂)) <103>

***

**Summary**

The poem describes the speaker observing a mud dauber wasp diligently building its nest and capturing prey. Initially annoyed and planning to destroy the wasp and its nest, the speaker reflects on the wasp's actions, framing its instinctive behavior of paralyzing and provisioning its nest with other insects (symbolized by *mingling*, 螟蛉) as a foolish and cruel attempt to forcibly "transform" another being into its own kind. The poem concludes with a warning to the wasp that its actions, violating "Truth" and "Principle," will inevitably provoke righteous anger and destruction. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem is highly significant. Written in 1921, shortly after the suppression of the March 1st Movement, the mud dauber wasp (蜾蠃) serves as a clear metaphor for Imperial Japan, and its prey (螟蛉) represents Korea. The wasp's attempt to "transform" the prey reflects Japan's coercive assimilation policies (同化政策). The speaker's shift from observation to condemnation and the threat of "righteous anger" symbolizes Korean resistance and resentment against colonial oppression, suggesting that forced assimilation is unnatural, cruel, and ultimately doomed to fail. Historians would find the poem's use of traditional East Asian metaphor (蜾蠃/螟蛉) to critique Japanese colonialism and express nascent nationalist sentiment particularly interesting.
