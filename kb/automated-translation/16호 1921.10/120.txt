智之端
(The Beginning of Wisdom)

Dr. W.E.B. Du Bois (떼유보이쓰) of Britain, famous as a defender of Black rights, declared forcefully at the Pan-African Congress held in London last September that distinguishing humanity by skin or hair is the height of foolishness. He wrote, "Those who engage in selfish rhetoric such as saying the development of a certain race is impossible or undesirable are truly villains poisoning the world. It is a fact that the world's humanity is composed of various races, but the differences between those races are far less than their similarities. Despite this, it is an undeniable shame of the world that relations and mutual respect between large groups of humanity have hitherto been determined primarily by the standard of the degree to which one race makes another subordinate and tributary." Thus, he attacked Britain for granting self-government to white colonies within the same colonial territory while denying it to Blacks and other people of color. Indeed, a refreshing outcry. And the fact that it is not the outcry of a Black person or other person of color, but the outcry of a white person himself, adds a further layer of interest.

However, while some people are campaigning to abolish even the distinctions that once existed between colored and non-colored people like this, it is deplorable that, on the other hand, there are those passionately engaged in trying to create new distinctions (differences) among the same race. It is generally known that Mr. Yun Tŏgyŏng (尹德榮), who became the central figure in the so-called Munch'ambong Incident (문참봉事件), received summons from the prosecutor's office several times, and barely obtained the fortune of non-prosecution, has filed a lawsuit against the *Tonga Ilbo* (東亞日報) for defamation, which remains unresolved to this day. Perhaps predicting that the outcome of the lawsuit would not be satisfactory, he reportedly again campaigned to the authorities for the *Tonga Ilbo*'s closure, adding the reason, besides damaging his reputation, that it had an "extreme anti-Japanese coloring." So, campaigning for newspaper closures has recently become a kind of trend in our Chosŏn. It was already four years ago. When Yi Kwangsu's (李光洙) "New Life Theory" (新生活論) was serialized in the *Maeil Sinbo* (每日申報), there was a movement by the faction of the late Yŏ Kyuhyŏng (呂圭亨) to shut down the *Maeil* for allegedly slandering Confucian ethics and morals. Last year, when Kwŏn Tŏkkyu's (權悳奎) article "A Blow Upon the Head of the Fake 'Ming' Person" (假明人頭上에 一棒) was published in the *Tonga Ilbo*, there was also a movement by the old Confucian faction to shut down the *Tonga*. Since the matters are hardly comparable, there's little point in discussing them further, but if those people really want to campaign like that, before undertaking such incomparable campaigns, they should first conduct the most beneficial self-head-remodeling campaign. And if they cannot do that, the best policy would be to rather conduct a campaign for rest towards the populace. Alas.

According to missionary Mr. Smith (스미쓰), who returned to his post after visiting his homeland for the first time in 7 years, the use of machinery in America has become even more prevalent since the Great War. Most households use dust absorbers (塵埃吸取器) to substitute for cleaning rooms; this device, about 1 foot 5 inches square, is connected to an electric current and passed around the room, powerfully absorbing dust for a complete cleaning. Another interesting item is the washing machine (洗濯器); laundry and soapy water (非陋水) are put into the machine, an electric current is applied, and the machine washes by itself. American women living amidst such blessings of knowledge are said to be mainly absorbed in the study of political issues today. Hearing such words and thinking of our own housewives – that is, how they break up their entire day just cooking morning and evening meals and mending clothes, unable to do even one thing properly – is suffocating. If we do not first devise measures to simplify the lifestyle of the home, then women's activities and liberation are all just useless empty talk.

There is a line in an old poem, "Springtime of Great Peace Under Heaven" (天下太平春), and also a common saying, "All is Peaceful" (萬事太平). Both phrases often came to our lips, but unexpectedly today, there are the Pan-Pacific Arms Limitation Conference, the Pan-Pacific Education Conference, and the Pan-Pacific Press Conference, leading everyone to speak of the Pacific (太平). However, although the dates these conferences are held differ, the point that they speak of peace and advocate cooperation is the same. Does this mean that, indeed, all affairs in the world are heading towards peace? At the Pan-Pacific Education Conference, held from August 11th and concluding on the 19th, Sin Hŭngu (申興雨) from among our Chosŏn people attended and spoke about the current state of education in Chosŏn. Kim Tongsong (金東成) attended the Pan-Pacific Press Conference, which opened on October 11th in the capital of the US territory of Hawaii (布哇). What both these gentlemen have already done and will do now at those conferences – conferences composed of representatives from over 20 countries – is something we can only entrust to their usual conduct and their capacity for action. However, what we, the general public, desire is that regardless of what organization or institution they belong to, when expressing the general situation or observations of Chosŏn at those meetings, they should not adopt a partial (偏頗) attitude as merely a member of a certain group or institution, but rather maintain the extremely fair attitude and extremely well-rounded observation of 'I am just one Chosŏn person' from beginning to end. (It is said that when Mr. Sin Hŭngu recently introduced the Chosŏn situation to the world as a Chosŏn delegate at the Pan-Pacific Education Conference, he stated, 'For education, the Chosŏn Education Association is organized; for religion, Christianity is developing to some extent; for newspapers, there are the *Tonga Ilbo* and *Chosŏn Ilbo* (朝鮮日報); and for magazines, there is *Asŏng* (我聲).' If this report is true, did he not know that there are other religions besides Christianity in the land of Chosŏn today, or did he know but chose not to mention them? Did he not know there were other newspapers besides *Tonga* and *Chosŏn*, or did he know but chose not to mention them? Did he not know there were other magazines besides *Asŏng*, or did he know but chose not to mention them? If he did not know, how could his usual observation be so crude (粗莽)? And if he knew but did not mention them, how could his mindset be so narrow-minded (偏狹)? The reporter deeply feels sorry for Mr. Sin himself and grieves that the various aspects of the Chosŏn situation introduced to foreigners through his words and actions must have been largely incomplete.) <83> Saying this is not because we, the general public, distrust them, but simply because our expectations for them are so high.

There is an old saying, "Good deeds don't leave the house, bad deeds travel a thousand leagues" (好事不出門, 惡事聞千里). Indeed, people's affairs, especially the unfavorable ones, always catch others' eyes more easily and are transmitted more readily than the good ones. This has been particularly true regarding the introduction of Chosŏn affairs abroad. The first unfavorable impression was given during the past Kwangmu (光武) era when the government dispatched envoys to various countries, who met foreigners still wearing topknots and carrying long tobacco pipes. Next, postcards printed by foreigners and circulated widely further damaged the dignity (軆面) of the Chosŏn people. Consequently, foreigners in various countries think of Chosŏn people merely as a kind of savage who carries a long pipe, wears a topknot, and lives barefoot. The task of attending, and preparing to attend, gatherings of such foreigners as one Chosŏn person is undoubtedly not light. Hence, we offer these words. (It is quite regrettable that this issue is included in such a miscellaneous section, but this resulted from manuscript deadlines; readers, please forgive.)

The Tripitaka Translation Society (三藏譯會), recently managed by Mr. Paek Sanggyu (白相奎), is truly undertaking a proper endeavor, and when the work is completed, its contribution to the clarification (闡明) of Chosŏn Buddhism will be significant. As is generally known, the relationship between Buddhism and Chosŏn culture is so deep, and although the number of Buddhist scriptures is said to count eighty thousand, there has yet been no translation into our language. Therefore, the general public has lacked the convenience to study them, and consequently, the teachings of Buddhism have not become very common. Considering this, the idea of Mr. Paek, who manages the society, should indeed be called beautiful.

Although it might not seem like a noteworthy article these days, the reports written about lecture groups, touring theater troupes (巡劇團), etc., as they toured the provinces last summer were truly spectacular to read. It was always described as a great success, always having given a tremendous impression to a full house. Of course, it's not impossible that troupe after troupe enjoyed great success everywhere. However, according to what we actually witnessed, it was not necessarily so. Why not write factually and truthfully? Wouldn't that serve as a crucial reference for the audience and the performers themselves? This is not limited to the provinces; it is also the case in Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul). Although this may seem like a small matter, it greatly fosters an insincere atmosphere in society and greatly damages the general public's trust in lectures. Even if temporary success was achieved this year through such means, what will happen from next year if people no longer trust the articles, or rather, such propaganda? Honesty is the best policy. It is said that in all things, one should only prioritize sincerity.

It was the 8th of this month. The alumni association of Tongdŏk Girls' School (同德女學校) in Kyŏngsŏng held a ceremony in the schoolyard to commemorate over 10 years of continuous service by the school principal, Mr. Cho Tong-sik (趙東植), and school manager, Mr. Sŏ Pyŏnghun (徐丙薰). At that time, in Mr. Cho's response speech regarding the presentation of commemorative gifts, there was a rather interesting remark. He said, "I once set my mind on women's education and, long ago, around the first year of Yunghŭi (隆熙元年 - 1907), I hung a sign reading 'Tongwŏn Girls' Night School' (東媛女塾) at my private residence inside and out, and tried implementing women's education (night school). Among the students, who numbered over 20, the attendance pattern was normal before the 15th or 16th of each month, but abnormally decreased thereafter. Investigating the reason, it was said that before the 15th or 16th, there was moonlight, allowing even women to travel, but afterwards, it was impossible. Also, at that time, when girls came to school, they necessarily required the accompaniment of a female servant (女下人) from their household. The phrase 'Yesterday, the servant was not at home, so I was absent' was heard almost daily." This was the state of women's education in Chosŏn 10 years ago. Not only that, when Mr. Cho first joined Tongdŏk School (in the 2nd year of Yunghŭi - 1908), the total number of students was merely 30, and the total monthly expenses were only ₩17. But what about today, 10 years later? There are over 300 students, and the total monthly expenses are over ₩730. At a time when the general public had not yet come to understand women's education, the devotion of Messrs. Cho and Sŏ, who held onto that feeble and stagnant (萎微不振) school day after day for over ten years (星霜), is truly worthy of endless admiration. Although Chungang (中央) and Posŏng (普成) schools also had devoted individuals who served continuously for over 10 years like this, Messrs. Cho and Sŏ of today's Tongdŏk School are said to have a special distinction as benefactors of women's education. <84>

**Summary**

This editorial from 1921 covers diverse topics reflecting contemporary Korean concerns. It begins by citing W.E.B. Du Bois's critique of racial discrimination, contrasting it with internal Korean conflicts, specifically mentioning Yun Tŏgyŏng's dispute with the *Tonga Ilbo* and past conservative attempts to censor newspapers critical of tradition. It juxtaposes the perceived liberation of American women through technology with the drudgery of Korean housewives, advocating for domestic reform. The piece discusses the hope and anxiety surrounding Korean representation at Pan-Pacific conferences, urging delegates like Sin Hŭngu and Kim Tongsong towards impartiality while criticizing Sin for allegedly presenting a narrow view of Korean society. It praises Paek Sanggyu's Buddhist translation project, critiques exaggerated reporting on cultural events, and concludes by celebrating the long service of educators Cho Tong-sik and Sŏ Pyŏnghun at Tongdŏk Girls' School, highlighting the progress in women's education over a decade.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the intellectual currents and social tensions in colonial Korea. Historians might find interest in: 1) The awareness of international anti-colonial/anti-racist discourse (Du Bois). 2) The internal fault lines within Korean society, particularly the conflict between modernizing nationalists (associated with newspapers like *Tonga Ilbo*) and conservative Confucian elements, as well as intra-elite disputes (Yun Tŏgyŏng). 3) The anxieties about national representation and image on the international stage, reflecting a desire for recognition and critique of perceived misrepresentations. 4) The commentary on women's roles, contrasting Western modernity with Korean domestic life, linking household efficiency to women's liberation. 5) The concrete details about the struggles and growth of early modern education, especially for women.

智之端
黑人의 權利擁護者로 有名한 英國의 「떼유보이쓰」博士는 지난 9月 中 倫敦에 열린 汎亞弗利加會議席上에서 喝破하되 皮膚나 毛髮로써 人類를 區別함은 미욱의 極이라 하얏다. 그는 써하되 「或 人種의 發達은 不可能, 或은 조치 못한 일이라 함과 가튼 利己的 言辭를 弄하는 者는 實로 世間을 荼毒하는 惡漢이라 世界의 人類가 여러 種族으로 이루어진 것은 事實이나 그 種族의 差異는 그 類似에 밋지 못함이 멀도다. 이러함에 不拘하고 從來 人類의 大集團間의 關係와 밋 그 相互間의 尊敬이 主로 一人種이 他人種을 隷屬朝貢케 하는 程度를 標準하야서 決定된 것은 世界의 恥辱이라 하지 안흘 수가 업다」하야써 今日 英國이 同一한 殖民地內에서도 白人種殖民地에는 自治를 許與함에 不拘하고 黑人 其他 有色人에게 對하야 自治를 不許함을 攻擧하얏다. 果然 痛快한 絶叫이다. 그리고 黑人이나 其他 有色人 中 누구의 絶叫가 아니요 白人 自己의 絶叫인 點에서 一層 妙味가 잇다. 그런데 어떤 사람은 이와 가티 有無色人間에 일즉잇던 區別까지 撤廢하기를 運動함에 不拘하고 또 어떠한 한 便에서는 가튼 人種間에서 새로운 區別(差異)을 짓지 못하야 熱中하는 者가 업지 안흐니 可憎. 所謂 문참봉事件의 中心人이 되어 數次 檢事局의 喚問을 밧고 艱辛히 不些訴와 幸을 어든 尹德榮氏는 東亞日報를 名譽毁損으로 訴訟을 提起하야써 今日까지 結末을 보지 못한 것은 一般이 다 아는 바어니와 그 提訴한 結果가 시원치 못할 것을 豫測함이엇던지 그는 다시 東亞日報의 廢刊을 當局에 運動하얏다는데 그 理由는 自己의 名譽를 毁損한 外에 極端의 排日色彩가 잇다는 한 가지를 加添하엿섯다는 말이다. 그러니 말이지 新聞廢刊의 運動은 近來로 우리 朝鮮에 一種의 流行거리가 되엇섯다. 벌서 4年前일이다. 李光洙詩의 新生活論이 每日申報紙上에 連載되엇슬 때에 儒敎의 倫理道德을 中傷하얏다 하야 故呂圭亨氏 一流의 每日에 對한 廢刊運動이 잇섯고 昨年 權悳奎氏의 『假明人頭上에 一棒』이란 글이 東亞紙上에 揭載되엇슬 때에 亦是 舊儒一流의 東亞에 對한 廢刊運動이 잇섯다. 일이 일갓지 아니한 지라 足히 믈어써 말할 것이 업거니와 만일 그네들로서 運動이 그러케 하고 십거던 일도 일갓지 아니한 그 따위 運動에 先하야 가장 有實益할 自己머리 改造運動을 行할 것이며 만일 그를 못하겟거던 차라리 居諸에게 向하야 安息運動을 行하는 것이 第一良策일 것이다.吁. 7個年만에 故國을 찻고 다시 歸任한 宣敎師 『스미쓰』氏의 말에 依하면 大戰以後 美國의 機械利用은 一層 盛行하는데 第一多數家庭에서는 塵埃吸取器를 使用하야써 房內의 掃除에 代케 하는 바 此器는 約1尺5寸角의 大를 有한 者로서 거긔에 電流를 通하야 房內를 一周하면 盛히 塵埃를 吸取하야써 完全하게 掃除가 되며 또 한가지 滋味잇는 것은 洗濯器인데 그 器中에 洗濯物과 非陋水를 너코 電流를 通하면 器械 저 혼자 洗濯을 行하게 되는데 이와 가티 知識의 恩惠 속에서 사는 美國婦人들은 主로 政治問題의 硏究에 沒頭하는 今日이라 한다. 이러한 말을 듯고 우리네의 家庭婦人 卽 아츰 저녁밥 짓기와 옷가지나 뀌여 대노라고 왼 終日의 時間을 破碎하고 한가지 일이라도 모지게 하지는 못하는 그것을 생각하면 氣가 막힌다. 우리는 第一로 家庭의 生活方式을 簡單히 할 策을 講치 아니하면 女子의 活動이고 解放이고 다 無用의 空談뿐이다. 古詩에 『天下太平春』이란 말이 잇고 또 俗語에 『萬事太平』이라는 말이 잇서 이 두말이 다 우리의 口頭에 잘 오르는 바이더니 不意今日에는 汎太平洋軍備制限會議, 汎太平洋敎育會議, 汎太平洋新聞記者會議가 잇서 사람마다 太平을 말하게 되도다. 그런데 이 會議가 열리는 날字의 先後는 잇스나 그 平和를 말하고 協調를 主張하는 바 點은 한가지이니 이로써 果然 世界萬事가 太平이 될 貌樣인가. 지난 8月11日에 開催되어 19日로써 終了된 汎太平洋敎育會에는 우리 朝鮮 사람 中으로서 申興雨氏가 出席하야 朝鮮의 敎育現狀을 말하얏스며 10月11日부터 美領布哇首府에서 열린 汎太平洋新聞記者大會에는 金東成氏가 出席하얏다. 右兩氏가 그 會議-20餘箇國의 代表者로서 成한 그 會議-에 臨하야 이미 어떠하얏스며 또 이제
