Love on the Other Shore
						Powŏlsaeng

Is it because of a dreadful karmic bond (因緣)?
Is it like this due to past life's retribution (業冤)?
The place where my heart goes without reason (空然),
Leaves only messy and scattered traces.

Without giving me the chance to give up (斷念) by never seeing you again,
Nor holding me fully to my heart's content day after day,
What do you intend to do with me, unknowing one?
I have needlessly fallen into a whirlpool (盤渦) of illusion (幻弄).

I know you are not a virgin (處女),
Even the great gravity (引力) of the Earth (大地) that binds you,
Even that from which you cannot escape,
Knowing this, the love that went to you has no strength to help.

I do not intend to trample (蹂躪)
All that is beautiful about you,
Nor do I intend to make you my possession (所有),
I simply cannot restrain (抑制) the impulse (衝動) of emotion (情).

Though warm affection (情) flows in my heart,
To whom that flowing is directed (向),
Even I do not know that,
I only know it flows because it flows.

Do not ask for what reason
Your love comes to me,
Even I do not know that reason (理由),
And you have no right (權利) to ask it.

Just as it flows,
Just as it is directed (向), <96>
Just as it collides,
I can only watch.

The twitching of your lips,
Seems unable to speak, fearing others' ears,
The movement of your pupils (瞳子),
Seems unable to offer a slight smile (笑), fearing others' eyes.

When the fine hairs on your forehead cover your eyes,
And long sleeves cover your jade hands (玉手),
It seems like you are boasting to me
That you have hidden the seed of such love.

Even if there is affection (情), do not say
That the absence of words means there is no affection,
Merely with our physical eyes,
We cannot know the count (數爻) nor measure the amount (斗量).

You must simply not direct your intention (向意) toward me,
What I love is not aimed (目的) at you,
Only on that path, unknown even to me,
I merely follow (斟酌) where my affection (情) goes. <97>
<96-97>

**Summary**

This poem by Powŏlsaeng describes a passionate yet unattainable love, possibly directed towards someone unavailable ("not a virgin," bound by "Earth's gravity"). The speaker feels drawn by an uncontrollable emotion ("情"), not seeking possession but simply following the feeling's flow. The beloved appears constrained by societal judgment ("fearing others' ears," "fearing others' eyes"). The poem emphasizes the mysterious, perhaps fated nature of this love, acknowledging its complexity and the inability to fully grasp or control it, positioning it metaphorically on "the other shore" (彼岸), suggesting transcendence or distance. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the intense emotional expression found in early colonial Korean literature. Historians might find interest in its exploration of restricted love, the potential use of metaphor (the beloved's constraints, the "other shore") to navigate societal or even political limitations, and the emphasis on inner emotional turmoil (情) characteristic of the period's romantic sensibilities.

彼岸의 사랑
						步月生
몹슬 因緣이 잇서 그러한가,
前生에 業冤으로 이러한가,
空然이 마음가는 그곳이,
어지럽고 散亂한 자최를 남겨둘 뿐이다.

아주 못보아 斷念하는 機會도 주지 안코,
날이 맛도록 마음에 차게 나를 잡지도 아니하니,
네 모름이 나를 어찌코저 하는가,
내 空然이 幻弄의 盤渦에 들엇도다.

나는 안다 네가 處女의 몸이 아닌 줄,
너를 붓들어 맨 大地의 큰 引力까지도,
벗어나지 못할 그것까지도,
알면서 네게 간 사랑은 거들 힘이 업다.

나는 네게 모든 너의 아름다운 것을,
蹂躪하랴고 하는 것도 아니요,
所有로 맨들랴고 하는 것도 아니다,
다만 情의 衝動하는 그것을 抑制치 못할 뿐이다.

따뜻한 情이 가슴에 흐르지마는,
그 흐르는 것이 누구를 向하는지,
그것은 나도 몰은다,
흐르니까 흐르는 줄만 안다.

무슨 까닭에 너의 사랑이,
내게로 오느냐고 뭇지를 말아,
그것은 나도 그 理由를 몰으고,
너도 그것을 물을 權利가 업다.

오즉 흐르는 그대로,
向하는 그대로,＜96＞
부드치는 그대로,
보고만 잇슬 뿐이다.

너의 입슐의 달삭어리는 것이,
남의 귀를 두려워 말을 못하는 것 갓고,
너의 瞳子 움즉이는 것이,
남의 눈을 두려워서 잔 웃음(笑)을 치지 못하는 것 갓다.

압이마 잔털이 눈을 덥고,
긴소매가 玉手를 덥흘 때에,
내게는 이러한 사랑의 씨를,
감추엇다고 자랑하는 것 갓다.

情이 잇서도 말이 업다고,
그것을 情업는 것이라고 하지 말아,
오즉 우리의 肉眼으로는,
數爻도 알 수 업고 斗量도 할 수 업는 것이다.

너는 오즉 내게 向意를 말아라,
나의 사랑하는 바는 너를 目的한 것은 아니다,
다만 나도 알지 못하는 그 길로,
情가는 그것을 斟酌할 뿐이다. ＜97＞
