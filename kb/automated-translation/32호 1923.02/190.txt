**Body Text Translation**
The Tomb of Lord Yi of Chuyŏn

In this busy world, a peculiar matter became an issue and remained in contention for several years before finally being resolved a short while ago. A peculiar matter! What on earth was that matter? It was the Kŭmgok stele erection problem (金谷建碑問題), no, the problem of the single character "前" (chŏn, former). There is an old saying that "one character is worth a thousand pieces of gold" (一字直千金), and indeed, the character "前" at the heart of this recent problem was worth more than ten thousand pieces of gold, let alone a thousand.

As is generally known, this problem of erecting the stele at Kŭmgok arose years ago when the remains of Empress Myŏngsŏng (明成皇后) of Hongnŭng (洪陵) were jointly interred (祔葬) at Kŭmgok, concurrently with the relocation and rebuilding (移建) of the Hongnŭng stele there. Specifically, on the front of the Hongnŭng stele, the characters "大韓" (Taehan, Great Han) were written in a separate line on the right, and then, after skipping a line, "明成皇后" (Empress Myŏngsŏng) was written. However, when relocating and rebuilding that stele from Hongnŭng to Kŭmgok, it was unavoidable to write something concerning Emperor Kojong. Therefore, at that time, they intended to engrave the five characters "高宗太皇帝" (Kojong T’aehwangje, Emperor Kojong the Great) and erect the stele. But the Office of the Yi Royal Household (李王職, Iwangjik) (those who convey the intentions of the Japanese Imperial Household Ministry (宮內省)) insisted that unless the single character "前" was placed above these characters, they absolutely could not permit it. Consequently, the stele could not be erected and was left lying down until last winter. Then, in December of last year, the issue arose when Mr. Ko Yŏng-gŭn (高永根), the tomb keeper (陵叅奉, Nŭngch’ambong) at Kŭmgok, secretly engraved "高宗太皇帝" and erected the stele. Ultimately, the matter concluded according to Mr. Ko's assertion; that was the beginning and end of the problem. To speak of this issue again, what now remains is the question of the responsibility of the Head of the Iwangjik, Mr. Yi Chae-gŭk (李載克).

That is, why was a problem that could ultimately be resolved so easily dragged out until today? Frankly speaking, if the Head of the Iwangjik had possessed even a little more sincerity and conviction (主見) regarding this issue, it would have been resolved long ago. But unfortunately, Head Yi Chae-gŭk lacked these. His thoughts were likely consumed with how he could curry favor (承順) with them [the Japanese] even slightly more, to maintain the position he once obtained until his death. Even more base (賤陋) than this is the despicable behavior (鄙行) of the faction of Yi Yun-jong (李胤鍾) (who is certainly no advocate of egalitarianism), who are anxious that they might not do enough (猶恐不及) to defend Head Yi Chae-gŭk. He brazenly declared at a meeting of the Ihwa Hakhoe (李花學會) that since the Great Emperor (太皇帝) was the Lord of OO (OO之主), there was no need for debate, and calling it "The Tomb of Lord Yi of Chuyŏn" (珠淵李公之墓) was most appropriate. Hearing this, several opponents immediately beat and insulted (歐辱) him. He then reported it to the police station, leading to the arrest and imprisonment (拘囚) of Yi Chong-jin (李鍾秦) and several others from the opposing side. Is this not truly shameful conduct that should not be heard by others? <80>

**Summary**

The article reports on the resolution of a long-standing controversy regarding the inscription on Emperor Gojong's tombstone at Kŭmgok. The Japanese-controlled Office of the Yi Royal Household (Iwangjik) insisted on adding the character "前" (Former) before "Emperor Gojong," diminishing his title, while Korean resistance opposed this. The stalemate was broken when the tomb keeper, Ko Yŏng-gŭn, secretly inscribed the title without "前" and erected the stele. The article criticizes the Iwangjik head, Yi Chae-gŭk, for lacking conviction and delaying the resolution, and condemns Yi Yun-jong for suggesting the even more diminutive title "Tomb of Lord Yi of Chuyŏn" and reporting dissenters to the police. Historically, this incident reveals the subtle but persistent ways Korean figures navigated and resisted colonial pressure regarding symbols of national sovereignty and royal legitimacy, even years after annexation. The conflict over a single character highlights the intense symbolic weight attached to the former monarchy and the deep divisions within the Korean elite regarding collaboration and resistance.

珠淵李公之墓
이 분주한 세상에 別異한 일이 다ㅡ문제가 되야 여러 해를 두고 相持하여 오다가 얼마 전에 비로소 해결된 일이 잇다. 別異한 일! 그 일이 대체 무슨 일이냐? 金谷建碑問題, 아니 「前」 1字의 문제 그것이다. 一字直千金이라는 녯말이 잇거니와 금번이 문제의 중심된 「前」의 1字는 천금은 말고 만금의 價도 더 되는 셈이엿다.
일반이 아는 바와 가티 이 金谷의 建碑問題는 年前 洪陵 明成皇后의 遺骸를 金谷으로 祔葬함과 共히 洪陵의 碑를 移建하는 거긔에서 생긴 것이다. 즉 洪陵碑의 정면에는 바른편 딴줄노 「大韓」이라 쓰고 그 다음 한줄을 띄어 노코 明成皇后라 써섯다. 그런대 그 碑를 洪陵으로 金谷에 移建함에는 고종황제에 관한 것을 쓰지 안흘 수가 업섯다. 그래서 그 당시에 곳 「高宗太皇帝」의 5字를 색여써 그 碑를 세우려 하엿스나 李王職(일본 宮內省의 의사를 전달하는 者)에서는 「前」 1字를 그 우에 冠하야 색이지 아느면 도저히 許할 수가 업다하야 그 碑를 세우지 못하고 그대로 뉘여두어 작년 겨울까지에 니르럿던 바 작년 12월 중에 金谷의 陵叅奉 高永根씨가 그윽히 「高宗太皇帝」라 색이여써 그 碑를 세운 데에서 문제가 생겻다가 결국 고씨의 주장대로 되고 말은 것이 그 문제의 始終이엿다. 이제 이 문제를 다시 말하자면 이제 남은 것은 李王職 長官 李載克씨의 책임문제이다.
즉 필경은 그러케 쉽게 해결되는 문제를 엇지하야 오늘날까지 끌어오게 하엿는가 하는 그것이다. 사실 말이지 李王職 장관이 족음만이라도 더 이 문제에 대한 성의와 主見을 가졋스면 이 문제는 벌서 해결되얏슬 것이다. 그러나 불행 李載克 장관은 그것이 업섯다. 엇더케 하면 저들의 뜻을 족음이라도 더 承順하야 한 번 어든 자리를 죽기 전까지 유지할가 한 것이 그의 생각이엿슬 것이다. 이보다도 더 賤陋한 일은 李載克 장관을 옹호하기에 猶恐不及하는 李胤鍾(평민주의자도 아닌 것은 물론) 일파의 鄙行이니 그는 일즉히 李花學會의 會席에서 揚言하되 太皇帝는 OO之主이니 이러니 저러니 할 것이 업고 「珠淵李公之墓」라 함이 最可하다 하엿다. 그래서 이 말 들은 반대의 몃 사람은 당장에 그를 歐辱하엿더니 그는 곳 경찰서에 고소하야 반대파의 李鍾秦 외 몃 사람을 拘囚하여섯다고. 실로 타인에게 들리지 못할 陋行이 아닐가.＜80＞
