Publication Date: 1923년 02월 01일
Article Title: 먼저 農民부터 解放하자 (Let's Liberate the Farmers First)
Author: 李晟煥
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Argumentative Essay)

Let's Liberate the Farmers First
						Yi Sŏnghwan (李晟煥)

To the Editor.
Have not our farmers, from the eternal past and even unto the present, been hearing the collective term "those mere peasants" from society, while silently enduring treatment like beasts regarding all matters? Moreover, in recent times, haven't the aftereffects of the industrial revolution, the capitalist trends, invaded even the corners of our farming villages, and isn't the so-called annexation of land (토지겸병) worsening day by day? Consequently, even landlords residing in the farming villages think of the generally poor and weak farmers (貧弱農民) like cattle and horses (牛馬), like slaves (노예), exploiting them until their blood (혈액) runs dry, and working them until their flesh (膚肉) wastes away. Even if farmers break their bones and squeeze out their sweat, they gain nothing in their entire lives. Is it merely that they gain nothing? On the contrary, year by year (年一年), they only accumulate debt (借金). Are not these farmers, caught in the giant hand of debt, ultimately reporting tragedies of bankruptcy and wandering (流離)? Social misfortunes (不祥事) and national pities (可憐事) endlessly expose their shameful state (추태) from this. Despite this, society says: "Farming is the foundation of the nation. Farmers are obedient. Farmers are honest. Farmers are moderate. Rural life is peaceful. There is no occupation as stable as farming." Indeed, how splendid are these words of praise (稱言)? And how beautiful? However, the reality is entirely the opposite of these verbal praises. Society does not know that farmers have nothing to eat, nothing to wear, nothing to live on. People (세인) do not know that the farmer's entire life is truly a meaningless existence, whether for oneself or for society. This is a sad and painful thing for the farmers. The writer (기자), too, trembles and feels pain in response to this reflection, shedding sorrowful (애닯은) tears himself.<33> Thus, this sorrow of ours cannot but turn into resentment (원한). Ah, what a cold society this is! Ah, what tyrannical (橫暴) propertied people (有産者) they are! Since they and our farmers are all human beings, why can they not exist together (存하지) and prosper together (榮하지)? Our farmers cannot always blindly obey (맹종) the unreasonable customs (無理한 인습) and tyrannical systems (橫暴한 제도) of current society like this. Our farmers will surely live only after [resisting?] the society that seizes (탈취) our interests with our weapon of justice (定義) [and destroying?] the fortress (牢城) of the propertied class (有産家), built firmly with our flesh and blood (혈육), with our weapon of truth (진리). Therefore, we must first find our true heaven-sent rights (천부의 권리). This aspiration (열망) and demand are boiling over in the farmers' hearts. Thus, the writer (記者) has recorded this piece (一篇) representing the psychology (심리) of 14 million farmers. Henceforth (금후), I earnestly desire (切望) that a major organization (일대기관) representing and acting on such farmer psychology will emerge centrally, and I am confident that such an organization will surely exist in the not-too-distant tomorrow (不遠한 明日). Although this piece (一篇) is incomplete (不備한), I hope it may serve as a stimulant (興奮劑) for comrades (同志者) striving to achieve such things, and consequently, I hope it may contribute (裨益됨이) even in the slightest (毫末이라도) to the construction of Korean culture.

Today's Cultural Movement Neglects the Root for the Branches (捨本取末)
It has already been half a decade since the cultural movement (文化運動) arose in our society. Accordingly, the issues of construction are not few, and the outcries (절규) for liberation have not ceased after just one or two. Political issues, religious issues, educational issues, or women's liberation issues, workers' liberation issues, youth liberation issues, literary liberation issues, etc.—indeed, those with principles (主義), assertions (主張), convictions (定見), and beliefs (신념) regarding these have each established their schools of thought (문호), deepened their characteristics (색채), researched them theoretically (學理的), and acted upon them practically (實地的). The trend has reached a point today where it seems to have advanced our society considerably towards a valuable domain (境域). However, when one closely examines (叩察하며) its other side (이면) and dissects its interior (내부), one finds that it has sometimes been propagandistically agitated (선동) and trendily followed blindly (맹종), resulting, on the contrary, in not bearing fruit (結하지) and not achieving merit (奏치)—such cases are also not absent. Our society's cultural movement is thus enveloped in a dense fog (五里霧中). Now, in newly advocating this farmer liberation movement, I first looked back (回顧하야) and questioned (質하며), hesitated (躊躇하야) and thought (思하얏노라), whether this movement would directly gain vitality and whether this advocacy would immediately yield a great harvest (수확).<34> However, each time I thought it over thousands of times (千思萬度), I realized even more that I could not help but cry out (절규) about this issue. It is not because I am someone whose theoretical (學理的) research is particularly profound, nor because I am someone whose strategic maneuvering (작전) in movements is skillful (교묘한). Nor is it because I am a reckless activist (盲動者) of trendy thought, nor because I am a social invalid (社會病者) of modern ideology. It is simply because of the issue of bread. It is because it is a cry of anguish (哀聲) from suffering that cannot be endured without shouting. It is precisely because it is a pressing (切迫한) life-or-death issue. Therefore, this outcry of mine for farmer liberation is by no means a manipulation of writing (舞文) or a test of distorted writing (曲筆). It is merely a cry of love for humanity (인륊�애—*note typo*) emanating from the undisguised sorrow of farmer oppression (압박) and the groans (신음) of suffering. Thus, it aims to break away (脫却하며) from this groaning, to be liberated from this oppression, and to let our Chosŏn farmers also taste what this so-called cultural life, frequently sung and repeatedly cried out for (頻唱累呌) recently, is like. However ignorant (무지식하고) and however incompetent (무능력한) we may say the farmers are, even so (그러타고), farmers cannot be non-human, non-nationals, or non-members (일원) of society. As long as farmers are human, they too must possess the demand and aspiration (열망) to live by and enjoy a life of meaning (意義) and leisure (여유).
How could they be unequal (갓지) to anyone else or fall behind others in this regard? Even if we concede one step (일보를 讓하야) and say that a life at the very bottom (밋바닥) of poverty (貧乏) is the fixed fate (定한 운명) for farmers, could it be that where negative energy (陰電) reaches its extreme (極되는), there is no slight stirring (미동) of positive energy (陽氣)? Farmers too must be liberated from this gloomy life (陰慘한 생애) and, even if just a little, become human beings worthy of being called human, advancing towards a free and bright (광명) life. If, by any chance, today's farmers are left neglected (방임한다) as they are, alas! (오호!) The farmers (who constitute 80% of our entire nation) will be unable to maintain (維持할) or prolong (延長할) their lives. No matter how much blood and sweat farmers shed, no matter how hard they work (勞作하야도) to the point of breaking their bones, all they will obtain (엇는) is a pale (창백) face (顔貌) that has lost its color, an emaciated (앙상한) body (軆骸) consisting only of bone marrow (骨髓), plus compounded debt (새끼 借金), and ignorant (無識丁) children (子弟). Thus, the current stagnation (沉澱) will have absolutely no prospect (餘望) of rising again (浮揚할), no matter when (何日何時), and finally (드듸여), only the tragedy of ruin (파멸) and destruction (멸망) will impend upon us (迫到하리로다). Isn't it something to tremble (전율할) at just thinking about it? Therefore, I shout loudly (高聲大呌). No matter what issue, what principle is said to be necessary and urgent (긴급하다), before all else, cut the iron chains (鐵鎖) hung around the farmers' necks, lift and remove the bedrock (盤石) pressing down on the farmers' bodies. Thus, let the farmers break away (脫却하야) from their miserable plight (慘境) and proceed towards a future (前途) of revival (부활).<35> Otherwise, social reform (사회개조) will find it difficult (難하고) to avoid the harm (폐해) of neglecting the root for the branches (捨本取末), and the cultural movement will not escape the failure (실수) of being empty fame without substance (虛名無實). By whatever method, by whatever principle, first break through (타개하며) the inside of this scene of fire (火場) of farmer annihilation (夷滅), first dissect (해부하야) the inner workings (내막) of this den of demons (魔窟) trampling (蹂躪) our compatriots, and at the very least, plan a place of peaceful settlement (안주의 地) for the farmers and build a house (宅) of existence and prosperity (存榮) for the nation.
Then, what is the way (道) to that revival? This is indeed an unparalleled great issue for our Chosŏn, and at the same time, it is an issue that we, having greeted the new year, must combine our new strength (合擧하야) and strive (분투치) to resolve. Therefore, regarding this, I cannot but brandish (振舞치) a new Yongquan sword (龍泉釰—*metaphor for sharp argument*), and I cannot but sing loudly (高唱하지) a powerful march (행진곡). This is the "Outcry for Farmer Liberation." I dare to state (진술하야) my humble opinion (愚見) and urgently propose it (動議한다) to society in general.

The Sorrow of Farmers Placed in Adversity (逆境)

They are equally elements (分子) forming society, equally citizens (市民) constituting the nation, but who is the foundation (근본)? And who also (亦) established (立한) the base (基礎)? There is none other than the farmer engaged in agriculture. Starting from the crucial foodstuffs (식량품) without which we cannot live, not only the materials for clothing (의복) and housing (住屋), but indeed, even all the raw materials (원료) for commerce and industry (상³µ업—*note typo*), there is nothing that can exist without depending (기다리지) on agricultural production, and nothing that can live (生할) without the farmers' blood and sweat. Truly, our farmers can be said to be the masters (주인) holding the key (열쇠/鍵) to the final secret (최후비밀) of human existence. That is, farmers hold in their hands (수중) the key (열쇠/鍵) to the storehouse (창고) of clothing, food, and shelter—the basic elements (기본요소) of our human life. However developed modern commerce and industry may be, however much modern civilization (문물) may be called a civilization centered on commerce and industry, one must know that behind today's commerce and industry lie agriculture, the farming village, and the farmer. Whether it be labor, capital, raw materials, or human resources, there is nothing that does not rely on the farmers' strength and is supplied from the farming villages. If one forgets (망각한다) such a vital and important (大切且重) background, it is a huge contradiction (모순). Therefore, things like commerce and industry will only reveal the value of their existence after agricultural management (농업경영) develops and the existence of farmers becomes sound (건전한). Now, conceding one step (일보를 讓하야), let's assume (가정하자) that our Chosŏn suddenly transforms (변천되야) into a nation founded on commerce and industry (商工業立國), and agriculture disappears (跡을 絶하게) in a single day (一朝에). Even so, this immutable key to life will still (의연히) be held in the hands (수중) of farmers in some country on earth (지구상).<36> This is a truth that no person (如何한 人이나) or god (如何한 神도) can dare deny (부인치). How much more so for our Chosŏn, which cannot escape being a Chosŏn founded on agriculture (農業立國), a Chosŏn based on farmers, both in the known (旣知) past and even in the unknown (미지) future! Confident that the words above are immutable truth, I believe, based on this, that agriculture should be revered (推尊하여야) in the most important position among all production industries, and farmers should be given (與하여야) the most respected treatment among the general populace. I even deny the sequential idiom (順序的 熟語) of the so-called Scholar-Farmer-Artisan-Merchant (士農工商) from the old days (昔日). I would rather advocate for changing the order/name of positions (位置更稱) of Farmer and Scholar. Reason (事理) and principle (道體) are thus, but what is the actual position and factual treatment of Chosŏn farmers today? Is it viewed with respect (尊重視)? Or viewed with abuse (虐待視)? Ah, to my view (소견) and consideration (고찰), regrettably (유감이나), only facts deeply contradictory to truth are encountered, and only situations greatly violating (위반) nature are discovered.
Look!! Among abuses, the highest abuse, and among contempts (輕侮), unparalleled contempt—who is it that is pressed down (눌이우고), trampled upon (밝히우고), and kicked (채움을), enduring it silently with scorn (笑受黙當)? Is it not truly the farmer class? Bureaucrats (官吏輩) oppress farmers, claiming to be the bureaucratic class (官吏階級); the knowledgeable types (유식쟁이) insult farmers, claiming to be the knowledgeable class; city dwellers (도회인) look down on (천시하며) farmers for living in the city; merchants and industrialists (상공업자) despise (멸시하며) farmers while pretending to be gentlemen (신사 체); educators (교육자) show contempt (輕侮) for farmers, claiming to be teachers (선생); the rich abuse (학대) farmers because they have money. Even those things called teachers (훈장) in village schools (서당), who live together with farmers day and night (日夕), ignore (무시하나니) farmers just because they are farmers. To give one example (일례), when they teach (교훈할) children (아동), they invariably say (言必稱曰), "You must study hard. Only then can you live respectably (행세한다). Otherwise, you can do nothing but the wretched work of a farmer (農軍)." Ah! Is this not a mentality that recognizes farmers as lacking personality (인격)? Is this not ridicule and abuse (嘲罵가) that looks down on (下視하는) farmers as slaves (노예)?!
In this way, the general public (세인) and various classes have an ambiguous (애매하야) understanding of agriculture, not knowing (不知한다) the greatness (위대) of agriculture. Accordingly, they misunderstand (오해하야) their duty (임무) towards farmers and forget (망각하고) the achievements (공적) of farming. How unfortunate! How much indignation (분개) must farmers feel about this abuse, and what must be their sorrow (비애) standing in this adversity (역경)? Truly, it is not something that a person with reason (이성) can bear not to pay attention to (頓着치), nor something that a person with feelings (감정) can just view calmly (悠然視하고) and let pass. There must surely be some sighs (한숨) about this sorrow, and there cannot but be some complaints (불평) about this abuse. Farmers too will look after their own position (입장) and examine what their standpoint (立脚地 如何) is.<37>

Farmers are Not Slaves (奴隷), Nor Machines (機械)
Thus abused (학대) and despised (경멸) by society in general, wandering (방황하는) in adversity (역경), what is the farmer's position (입장)? Groaning (신음하는) in misfortune (비운), what are the farmer's circumstances (처지)? Naturally, their bodies (육체) and minds (정신), their work (업무) and actions (동작), become tamed (馴하며) into slaves, transformed (化한다) into machines. The so-called knowledgeable class (유식계급) (including officials) and the propertied class (유산계급) position themselves (자처하야) above the farmer class with all sorts of monstrous (怪惡한) behaviors—despotic (專制的), arbitrary (武斷的), tyrannical (橫暴的), exploitative (搾取的), oppressive (壓迫的), contemptuous (輕蔑的)—and rule (御하되) the general farmer class either through extreme dependence (극단적 依從主義) or active blind obedience (적극적 맹종주의). The farmer class exists not for the farmers themselves, but entirely (專혀) for the world of the knowledgeable class and the propertied class; in a sense, they exist as a sacrificial class (희생계급). It is clear that they are a type of slave, whose life and death (殺活) and existence itself (생존의 여부) are entrusted (일임한) to those classes. Meekly (唯唯然히) submitting to their power (權), unavoidably (부득불) obeying their orders (令), they cannot express any opinion (의사표시) or possess any consciousness of life (생활의식) beyond that. In a sense, they are worse than wood and stone (목석), inferior (劣하다) to beasts (금수). Thus, their innate (천부적) mental (정신) abilities (능력) seem to have been slaughtered (학살된) under the iron fist (鐵權), degenerating (퇴화하며) into a low-intelligence class (低腦階級), and their natural (自然性) desire for knowledge (지식) seems to have been castrated (거세한) as if by a sharp instrument (銳勺先—*meaning unclear*), forming (形出한) an ignorant class (무식계급). In this way, the knowledgeable and propertied classes, taking advantage (濫乘하야) of the weakness of the poor and ignorant farmer class, gradually deepen the roots of their absolute right to make farmers blindly obey (맹종시킬), while the farmer class itself has almost developed a habit of absolute duty to obey them. Thus, they believe this is a virtue (미덕) and solely trust that this is morality (道義). Rulers (위정자) call farmers who obey well even under coercion (강요) "good people" (양민), and propertied people call farmers who obey well even under unrighteous tyranny (無道한 橫暴) "true people" (眞人). They permanently seize (탈취하며) the freedom (자유) of the farmer class and do not grant (不與하나니) opportunities for education (교육슝�—*note typo*), thinking this is a golden rule (金科玉條) indispensable even for a moment (須臾도) for creating obedient slaves suited (副意되는) to their own exploitation (使役) and despotism (專制). Due to these circumstances, among the 14 million farmers in our country, excluding a very small number of landlord-capitalists (지주자본가), the vast majority (多大數) of farmers, not only in the past but even more so today, are not treated as human beings. The farmer's labor—no, the farmer himself—is treated (취급되야) in the same way (마챤가지로) as a single (一挺) hoe (호미), alongside a single (一挺) sickle (낫), just like a single (一頭) farm ox (農牛), regarded merely as a farming tool (農具) and livestock (우마), like a machine (기계), and receives not even a bit (족음도) of human treatment.<38> Consider this: the farmer is nothing more than a machine that produces grains (穀物), vegetables (菜類), textiles (織物), and timber (材木). Does the farmer gain security of life by diligently digging the earth? Does the farmer preserve physical health by struggling with nature? Does the farmer have any enjoyment in life despite excessive labor? Does the farmer see any profit in the profession despite doing the work of a serf (농노)?
Look! Farmers produce rice (미곡) with their own hands (自手로). But they cannot eat it themselves. They just load it up to go to the city. Farmers also produce silk cocoons, thread, and fabric (繭絲紬) in their own homes (自家에서). But they cannot wear it themselves. They just offer (밧칠) it to the propertied class (有産者). Do they eat or wear what's left after loading and offering? They cannot. It is sucked away (빨리우고) along with heavy taxes on goods (重한 物稅) and precious blood taxes (貴한 血稅). And the city absorbs (흡수하며) and utilizes (이용하야) this, constructing the so-called city-centered civilization and developing the commerce-and-industry-centered culture as much as it does. Looking at this too, today's Chosŏn farmer can only be seen as a mere machine (일개의 기계). I want to call them "sacrificial machines" (犧牲機) for the construction of social culture. They can be seen just like a bread (빵) maker and seller. Others eat all the very sweet (다듸 단) bread, while they are in the situation (세음이다) of licking the bottom (밋을 할타먹는) of the pot (가마) where the bread was steamed (쩌내인). What a sorrowful (悲哀한) fact this is! All these unfortunate events (不祥事) thoroughly turn our farmers into slaves and machines.
Then, will farmers indeed remain slaves forever (영영), continuously (내내) become machines? Are farmers truly unintelligent people (低腦者), incompetent people (무능자), unaware people (無自覺者)? Will they just drink, eat, be dragged (끌리우고), and be loaded (실리울) like cattle and horses (牛馬), and finish (맛치고) their lives like that? Will this tragic (비장한) phenomenon (현상), ah, this cruel (참혹한) situation (경우), continue forever? Compatriots (동포여)!! Listen. Farmers are also our compatriots. They are human. Speaking more broadly, farmers are also living beings (생물) possessing life. They seek food when hungry (배곱흔), desire (欲하며) drink when thirsty (목마른), seek cool places when hot, warm places when cold (치운), comfortable work (氣樂한 일) away from excessive labor, beautiful clothes and delicious food (美衣美食) instead of coarse clothes and food (粗衣粗食), freedom (자유) from being mechanical (기계적), and equality (평등) from being slavish (노예적). This is the manifestation (발현) of the natural instinct (自然性) that no living being can lack, and the symbol of the beauty of human feeling (人情美) that no human can be without. From this arise the evolution and selection (진화도태) of living beings, and comes (來하는) the advancement and development of humankind. Look! Aren't people of other classes (타계급) and other occupations (타직업) progressing rapidly (長足의) and with extraordinary (非常의) influence (세력), constantly (작구작구) advancing without falling behind this principle (원리원칙)? Then, there should not be even one Buddha (一佛인들) among the Buddhas and gods (佛神) of the entire universe (三千世界) who would approve (찬성할) the reason (이유) why farmers alone (유독) should continue the lifestyle of the primitive era (원시시대), becoming slaves and machines of the knowledgeable and propertied classes.<39> Farmers are also dignified (堂堂 한) human beings, definitely not slaves, nor machines. They are possessors (소유자) of splendid (훌륭한) character (인격). They are possessors (소지자) of sound (건전한) physique (체격). At the same time, they are clearly the preservers (保持者) of the country of Chosŏn. And farmers too have the spirit (기운) to break the silence (沈黙), the strength (筋力) to penetrate burial (埋沒), and also possess the courage (용감) to break the earth's crust (地殼). How could they imitate (본바드랴) the actions (所爲) of that low-ability person (저능아) who performed disgracefully on the banks of the Wu River (烏江邊—*allusion to Xiang Yu*)? The temporizing (姑息) of holding shame and enduring disgrace (抱羞忍耻) cannot but end, and the ambition (抱負) to stage a comeback (捲土重來) cannot but begin its fulfillment (履行).
Think about it. Do farmers have anything other than coarse clothing (粗衣), coarse food (粗食), fields (野), mountains (山), farming tools (農具), thatched cottages (茅屋), daily (日日) excessive (과격한) labor, and the fatigue (피로) and anguish (고민) resulting from it? Also, are there any others besides farmers who are innocent (천진스럽고도 무구하며) and natural (자연스럽고도 무죄한)? Despite this, whose fault (罪責) is it that they have become slaves and machines like today? Are the farmers themselves the culprits (罪責者)? No. The fault lies with society in general. Politicians (정치가도), educators (교육가도), religious figures (종교가도), moralists (도학가도), merchants and industrialists (상공업자도)—all, regarding (就하야는) this culpability (罪責), should fear heaven themselves and face (접허할) the gods (神明) with their conscience (양심).
Ah, sinless (죄업는) farmers! Ah, faultless (허물업는) farmers! It is sad (슬푸고) that they became slaves abused (학대밧는) without cause (無端스럽게), and pitiful (애츠럽다) that they became machines that completely (여지 업시) lost (일흔) their freedom. For anyone equipped (구비한) with reason (이성) and emotion (감정), the indignant grief and lamentation (悲憤憾慨) over this must truly be immeasurable and unbearable (無量難堪).

Chosŏn Farmers are Clearly the Pillars (棟樑) of Chosŏn
So, we have clearly understood that farmers are not slaves, nor machines. But, is it satisfactory (만족하다) for the farmers' situation, and sufficient (충분하다) for the farmers' treatment, just to fluctuate (왕래하거나) at the boundary marker (境界標) between being and not being slaves, or to move up and down (上下하는) at the standard level (標準度) of being or not being machines? No. It is not so. Farmers are clearly the root and stem (根蔕者) and the pillars (棟樑者) of Chosŏn.
Consider this. Our Chosŏn, over the past several decades, has been pushed back (물리우고) and torn apart (띄끼우되) by numerous foreigners (幾多外人), blown about (날리우고) and washed (씨끼우며) by sad winds and miserable rain (悲風慘雨), yet despite all that, each time, hasn't the cohesive (결정체적) movement known as the Chosŏn nation, though meager (빈약은), still maintained its form (모양)? One must recognize this as the unseen blessing (蔭德) of the farmers' strong (강건한) bodies and upright (강직한) spirits, standing unshakably and immovably (不搖不動) like a huge rock (巨巖) supporting (支持함과 가티) its presence (勢) majestically (屹然히) in the midst of a turbulent muddy stream (滔滔然한 濁流), stubbornly defending (墨守하고) the land (토지) handed down (傳來) from their ancestors (祖先).<40> Taking one step further, even when thinking about the pride (자랑거리) of our 5,000-year (반만년) history that we always use as a background (배경), the growth of that great national vitality (원기) truly radiated (煥發하얏다) from the farming villages, the collection (집합) of farmers. It is because there were farmers possessing both (雙健) a robust (强壯한) body forged (鍜鍊된) in agricultural labor (노동) and a sound (건전한) spirit selected (도태된) through struggle with nature, that the history of our Chosŏn has added pages (頁數). The devotee (사도) who fulfills the mission (사명) of nationalism even in silence (沈黙) is solely the farmer, and the loyal people (忠民) conscious (의식하는) of the patriotic concept (애국관념) without acting wildly (날뛰지 아니하여도) are only the farmers. Ah, "When the family is poor (家ㅡ貧함에), one thinks of a wise wife (賢妻); when the country is in chaos (國이 亂함에), one longs for (思한다) a good minister (良相)." This may seem like a poem of reactive (변태적) demand, but I know it is a proper matter unavoidable in our human world. From this, observing (관찰하며) the current state (현상) of our Chosŏn and thinking about (想到할) the future, I cannot help but pray (기도하야 마지 아니 한다) ever more for the soundness (건전) of the Chosŏn farmer spirit. Why should it be only from such a one-sided (單偏된) emotional argument (감정론적) of nationalism? Even turning to the Rousseau-style (룻소ㅡ류) naturalistic (자연주의적) praise of farmers (農民 讚禮) and considering it, one cannot help but value (존중히) the clean (청결) farming village, the sacred (신성) farming village, the natural (자연) farming village, the place of human production (인간생산소), rather than praising (賞讚하기보다는) the opposite: the filthy (불결) city, the sinful (죄악) city, the corrupt (부패) city, the place of human consumption (인간소비소). Consequently (딸하), one cannot help but value highly (귀중히 녁이지) the sound (건전한) farmers engaged (종사하는) here, rather than praising (讚禮하기보다는) the unsound (불건전한) class, the merchants and industrialists, residing (거주하는) there. However, sitting here today where we must not fall behind (뒤저서는) culture and must admire (찬미하자) civilization, it is not my real intention (본의) to deny the development of commerce and industry or to obstruct (장애하고자) the progress of the city. Commerce, industry, and the city should be planned for (期圖하여야) to develop as much as possible. But we must have countermeasures (대책) against their harmful effects (폐해). That is to say, we must seek (구하여야) a self-defensive (자위적) method (방도) for our country. There, we must firmly seek and establish (求立하야) countermeasures sufficient (足할) to supplement (補하며) the shortcomings (短) of commerce and industry and remedy (濟함에) the vices (蔽) of the city. Through these, we must recover (회복하며) the strength lost (상실되는) for the sake of commerce and industry and produce (생산하지 아니면 아니된다) what is consumed (소비) or diverted (유용되는) by the city. Indeed, such things cannot be found anywhere else but by seeking them in the farming villages and depending (기다리지) on the farmers. Herein, the significance (의의) of farmer respect, truly like adding flowers to brocade (금상첨화격인), proves (증명하고) its immense importance (九鼎의 重).
Let's liberate them quickly! Let's quickly liberate the farmer class, which bears (自負한) such a great mission (사명) for our country. Let's carry out (단행하야) a thorough (徹底的) liberation from now on, based on the understanding that farmers are the pillars (棟樑) of Chosŏn, and grant (부여하쟈) them rational (합리적) treatment (대우).
Postscript (附記): Regarding how to liberate (엇떠케 해방하며) and how to grant (엇더케 부여할가), I will change the topic (題를 改하야) and discuss it further (更論하리라).<41>

**Summary**

This 1923 editorial by Yi Sŏnghwan passionately argues for the urgent liberation of Korean farmers, whom he identifies as the foundational pillar (棟樑) and 80% majority of the Chosŏn nation. He decries the systemic exploitation and contempt farmers face from all societal classes—officials, intellectuals, city dwellers, capitalists, even rural teachers—who treat them not as humans but as ignorant slaves or production machines (기계). Yi contrasts the farmers' essential role in providing life's necessities and preserving national spirit with their miserable reality of poverty, debt, and lack of rights or opportunities, warning of national ruin (파멸) if this continues. He criticizes contemporary cultural movements for neglecting this fundamental issue ("neglecting the root for the branches" 捨本取末) and calls for immediate, thorough liberation and rational treatment, seeing farmers as the key to national strength and counteracting the negative aspects of modernization and urban growth. From a historical perspective, this article is significant for its strong articulation of peasant consciousness and grievance during the colonial period. It highlights the severe socio-economic stratification, the impact of encroaching capitalism on rural Korea (land annexation 토지겸병, debt), the prevalent class-based discrimination, and the rising nationalist sentiment linking the fate of the farmers directly to the survival and identity of the Korean nation (민족). Historians might find the explicit critique of other contemporary social/cultural movements for ignoring the peasant issue, the framing of farmers as the 'true' Koreans preserving tradition against urban/modern corruption (influenced by nativist or possibly Rousseauian ideas), and the call for a dedicated organization to represent farmer interests particularly revealing of the intellectual currents and social tensions of the early 1920s.

먼저 農民부터 解放하자
						李晟煥
編者 足下.
우리 농민은 영원한 과거에 잇서서 또 현재에 잇서까지 사회로부터 「그까진 농부들」이라는 總稱을 드러오면서 凡於事에 대하야 禽獸同樣의 冷遇를 黙受하고 잇지 안앗엇습니까. 더욱 근래로는 산업혁명의 여파인 자본주의적 세태가 우리 농촌 구석까지 侵襲한 바 되야 소위 토지겸병은 나날이 심해가지 아니함니까. 그리하야 한 가지로 농촌에 거주하는 지주들까지도 일반 貧弱農民을 牛馬와 가티, 노예와 가티 생각하고, 그 혈액이 고갈하도록 착취하며, 그 膚肉이 衰耗하도록 使役하나니 농민을 뻬를 부스며, 땀을 짜내여도 일생에 엇는 바난 아모 것도 업습니다. 엇는 바가 업슬 뿐이겟습니까? 도리혀 年一年으로 借金만 둘너지고 잇습니다. 이 借金의 巨手에 붓잡힌 농민들은 결국 파산, 流離의 참사를 보고하고 잇지 아니합니까. 사회적 不祥事와 민족적 可憐事는 이에서 그 추태를 한업시 노출하고 잇습니다. 이러하건마는 사회에서는 말합니다. 「農은 國의 근본이라. 농민은 從順하다. 농민은 정직하다. 농민은 온건하다. 농촌 생활은 安閑하다. 농사처럼 안정한 業은 업다. 」라고. 이러한 稱言이야말로 과연 얼마나 훌륭합니까. 또 얼마나 아름다움니까. 그러나 사실은 이 口舌的 稱言과 죄ㅡ다 반대이외다. 먹을 것도, 입을 것도, 살 것도 업는 농민인 줄 사회는 아지를 못합니다. 농민의 일생은 자기 일신을 위하야서나 또는 사회를 위하야서나 실로 의미업는 생활을 하고 잇난 줄을 세인은 아지 못하고 잇습니다. 이것이 농민의게 대하야는 슯흐고, 압흔 일이외다. 기자도 이로 생기는 반성에 몸이 떨니고 마음이 압하 애닯은 눈물을 스사로＜33＞ 흘니고 잇습니다. 그러하야 우리들의 이 비애는 원한이 되지 안닐 수 업습니다. 아ㅡ얼 마나 냉정한 사회입까. 아ㅡ얼마나 橫暴스러운 有産者들임니까. 그네들이나 우리 농민이나 다가티 사람인 이상에 어찌하야 가티 存하지 못하며, 가티 榮하지 못함니까. 우리 농민은 이와 가튼 현 사회의 無理한 인습과, 橫暴한 제도에 언제든지 맹종할 수는 업습니다. 우리 농민은 당연히 우리의 이익을 탈취하는 사회를 우리의 정의의 00로서 0000하고야 살겟습니다. 또 우리의 혈육으로써 강고하게 축성한 有産家의 牢城을 우리의 진리의 00로서 0000하고야만 되겟습니다. 그럼에는 우리의 진실한 천부의 권리부터 차저야 되겟습니다. 이 열망과 요구는 농민의 가슴 속에서 끌어 넘치고 잇습니다. 그래서 記者는 이 一篇을 1,400만의 농민심리를 대표하야 기록하얏습니다. 금후로는 이러한 농민의 심리를 대표 활동할 만한 일대기관이 중앙에 생기기를 切望하며, 또 不遠한 明日에 반듯이 그런 기관이 잇슬 줄을 자신하고 잇습니다. 이 一便이 비록 不備한 것이나 이러한 일을 期成하려 하는 同志者의 興奮劑로나 供함이 될가 하오며, 딸라 朝鮮 文化建設에 대하야 毫末이라도 裨益됨이 잇슬가 바라는 바이외다.

捨本取末의 今日의 文化運動
우리 사회에 문화운동이 이러난 지도 벌서 반 10년으로 헬 수 잇다. 따라 건설의 문제도 적지 아니하며, 해방의 절규도 1, 2에 끈치지 아니하얏다. 정치문제, 종교문제, 교육문제 또는 여자해방문제, 노동자해방문제, 소년해방문제, 문학해방문제 등 실로 이에 대한 主義와, 주장과, 定見과, 신념을 가진 자 각각 그 문호를 세우고, 그 색채를 농후하게 하야 學理的으로도 연구되며, 實地的으로도 활동되야 그 경향은 금일에 이르러 우리 사회로 하야금 가치 잇는 境域으로 향상 진보케 한 觀이 적지 아니하다. 그러나 그 이면을 叩察하며, 그 내부를 해부할 때에 혹은 선전적으로 선동도 되며, 유행적으로 맹종도 되야 도리혀 그 實을 結하지 못하고, 그 功을 奏치 못한 자ㅡ또한 업지 아니하다. 우리 사회의 문화운동이 이와 가티 五里霧中예 쌔워잇거늘 이제 다시 이 농민해방운동을 제창함에 먼저 하야 나는 이 운동이 직접으로 활기를 패며, 이 제창이 당장에 수확이 만흘가를 回顧하야 質하며, 躊躇하야 思하얏노라.＜34＞ 그러나 나는 이러케 千思萬度할 때마다 더욱 이 문제를 절규하지 아니할 수 업슴을 깨다랏다. 나는 별로 學理的 연구가 심오한 자임으로 그러함이 아니오 특히 운동상 작전이 교묘한 자임으로 그러함도 아니다. 또 유행적 사상의 盲動者임으로 그러함도 아니며, 현대적 주의의 社會病者임으로 그러함도 아니다, 다만 밥 문제로써 그러하다. 부르짓지 안코는 견댈 수 입는 고민의 哀聲임으로써 그러하다. 곳 生이냐? 死이냐?의 切迫한 생명의 문제임으로써 그러하다. 그럼으로 나의 이 농민해방의 절규는 결코 舞文의 회롱이 아니오, 曲筆의 시험이 아니다. 단지 거짓업는 농민의 압박의 비애와, 고민의 신음으로부터 발하는 인륊�애의 絶叫聲일 뿐이다. 그리하야 이 신음으로부터 脫却하며, 이 압박으로부터 해방하야 近時에 頻唱累呌하는 소위 문화생활의 맛이 어떠함을 우리 조선농민들도 맛보게 하자 함이다. 如何히 무지식하고 또 如何히 무능력한 농민이라고 하자. 그러타고 농민은 사람이 아니거나, 민족이 아니거나, 사회의 일원이 안될 수는 업다. 농민도 인간인 이상에 意義잇는 바의 생활과, 여유잇는 바의 향락이라면, 그것으로 살림하려 하며, 그것으로 질기고자 하는 요구와 열망을 가졋슬 것이다.
이 점이야 어찌 누구만 갓지 못하거나, 남보다 뒤질 수 잇슬랴. 설사 일보를 讓하야 貧乏의 외 밋바닥 생활이 농민의게 취하야는 定한 운명이라 하자, 그러나 陰電이 極되는 곳에 어찌 陽氣의 미동이 업슬가 보냐. 농민들도 이 陰慘한 생애부터 해방되야 조곰이라도 인간으로서 인간다운 인간이 되야 자유롭고, 광명잇는 생활로 향상하지 아니면 안될 것이다. 만일이라도 금일의 농민을 이대로 방임한다 하면, 오호! 농민(우리 전민족의 8할되는)은 그 생명을 유지할 수도 延長할 수도 업게 될 것이다. 농민은 암만 피와 땀을 흘이며, 뼈가 불너지도록 勞作하야도 엇는 것은 다만 혈색을 이러바린 창백의 顔貌와, 骨髓만 앙상한 軆骸와, 밋 借金의 새끼 借金과, 無識丁의 子弟 뿐일 것이다. 그리하야 현재의 沉澱은 何日何時가 되든지 다시 浮揚할 餘望이 絶하야 드듸여 우리예게는 파멸, 멸망의 참사 뿐이 迫到하리로다. 생각만 하여도 전율할 일이 안닌가. 그래서 나는 高聲大呌한다. 무슨 문제, 무슨 주의가 모다 필요하며 모다 긴급하다 할지라도 그것보다도 먼저먼저 이 농민의 목에 걸인 鐵鎖를 끈허바리며, 이 농민의 몸 우에 눌이운 盤石을 드러치우라. 그리하야 농민으로 하야금 비애의 慘境으로부터 脫却하야 부활의＜35＞ 前途에도 나아가게 하라. 그러치 아니하고는 사회개조도 捨本取末의 폐해를 면키 難하고 문화운동도 虛名無實의 실수를 피치 못할 것이니, 여하한 방법으로든지, 여하한 도리로든지 먼저 농민 夷滅의 이 火場부터 그 이면을 타개하며, 먼저 동포를 蹂躝의 이 魔窟부터 그 내막을 해부하야, 적어도 농민을 위하야 안주의 地를 계획하며, 민족을 위하야 存榮의 宅을 건설치 아니면 안되겟다.
그러면 그 부활의 道는 如何한가? 이것이 실로 우리 조선에 잇서 다시 업슬 큰 문제인 동시에 새해를 마즌 우리들이 새 힘을 合擧하야 해결에 분투치 아니면 안될 문제이다. 그럼으로 나는 이에 대하야 새로운 龍泉釰을 振舞치 아니할 수 업스며, 힘잇는 행진곡을 高唱하지 아니할 수 업다. 이 곳 「농민해방의 절규」이다. 감히 愚見을 진술하야 일반사회에 긴급히 動議한다.

逆境에 立힌 農民의 悲哀

朝鮮 農民은 分明한 朝鮮의 棟樑이다
그러면 농민은 노예가 아니며, 기계가 아닌 줄은 확실히 아랏다. 그럿타고, 노예가 되며 안되는 境界標에서 왕래하거나, 또 기계가 되며 못되는 標準度에서 上下하는 그것으로써 농민의 경우에는 만족하다 하며, 농민의 대우에는 충분하다고 할가? 아니다. 그러치 아니하다. 농민은 분명한 조선의 根蔕者이며 棟樑者이다.
생각할지라. 우리 조선이 과거 수십년 동안에 幾多外人에 물리우고, 띄끼우되, 또 悲風慘雨에 날리우고, 씨끼우며, 그리면서도 그럴 때마다, 오히려 조선 민족이라는 결정체적 운동만은 비록 빈약은 할 망정 그래도 모양은 그대로 내이고 잇지 아니한가. 이것은 저 巨巖이 滔滔然한 濁流 중에도 屹然히 자립하야＜40＞ 그 勢를 支持함과 가티 不搖不動하는 농민의 강건한 신체와, 강직한 정신을 가지고, 祖先 傳來의 토지를 墨守하고 잇는 蔭德인 줄로 알지 아니하면 안되겟다. 또 한거름 더 나아가서 우리가 매양 배경으로 하는 바 반만년 역사의 자랑거리에 就하야 생각하야 보아도 그 위대한 민족적 원기의 성장은 실로 농민의 집합인 농촌으로부터 煥發하얏다. 농업 노동에 鍜鍊된 强壯한 신체와, 밋 자연계와 분투하야 도태된 건전한 정신, 이 雙健을 겸한 농민이 잇섯슴으로부터 우리 조선의 역사는 頁數를 加하야 왓다. 沈黙 중에도 민족주의의 사명을 다하는 사도는 오직 농민이오, 또 날뛰지 아니하여도 애국관념을 의식하는 忠民은 다만 농민이다. 아ㅡ家ㅡ貧함에 賢妻를 생각하며, 國이 亂함에 良相을 思한다. 이는 일종의 변태적 요구의 詩인 듯 하지마는 우리 사람 사는 세상에는 면치 못할 떳떳한 일인 줄 안다. 나는 이에서 우리 조선의 현상을 관찰하며, 또 장래를 想到할 때에 더욱 더욱 조선적 농민정신의 건전을 기도하야 마지 아니 한다. 어찌 다만 이와 가튼 민족주의에 單偏된 감정론적 뿐으로만 그러하리오! 룻소ㅡ류의 자연주의적 農民 讚禮에 돌아가서 생각하야 본다 하드라도 불결의 도회, 죄악의 도회, 부패의 도회, 인간소비소인 도회를 賞讚하기보다는 이와 반대의 처지에 잇는 청결의 농촌, 신성의 농촌, 자연의 농촌, 인 간생산소인 농촌을 존중히 하지 아니할 수 업다. 딸하 거긔에 거주하는 불건전한 계급인 상공업자를 讚禮하기보다는 여긔에 종사하는 건전한 농민을 귀중히 녁이지 아니할 수 업다. 그러타고 문화에 뒤저서는 아니되며, 문명을 찬미하자 아니면 안될 금일 이 자리에 안저서 상공업 발달을 부인하거나 도회의 발전을 장애하고자 함은 나의 본의가 아니다. 상공업과, 도회는 발전할대로 발달될대로 그것을 期圖하여야 된다. 그러되 그 폐해에 대하야 대책이 업서서는 안되겟다. 즉 우리 나라의 자위적 방도를 구하여야 되리라는 말이다. 거긔에는 상공업의 短을 補하며, 도회의 蔽를 濟함에 足할 만한 대책을 견고히 求立하야 그것으로써 상공업을 위하야 상실되는 세력을 회복하며 도회에 의하야 유용되는 소비를 생산하지 아니면 아니된다. 그런대 이와 가튼 것은 실로 농촌에 求하며 농민에 기다리지 아니 하고는 다시 업다. 이에서 참으로 금상첨화격인 농민 존중의 의의는 더욱 九鼎의 重을 증명하고 잇다.
어서 바삐 해방하자! 이럿틋이 우리 나라에 위대한 사명을 自負한 농민계급을 어서 바삐 해방하자. 그리하야 이제부터는 농민은 조선의 棟樑이라는 이해 하에서 徹底的 해방을 단행하야 합리적 대우를 부여하쟈.
