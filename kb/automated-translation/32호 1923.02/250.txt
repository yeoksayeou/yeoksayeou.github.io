One Year
						Yang Chu-dong

It was a summer, long, long ago—

One day, I heard the sound of a cicada crying for the first time,
And drawn by that lovely voice,
I ran up the back hill in one stride.
But when I got close to the tree where the cicada was crying,
The cicada abruptly stopped its song,
And flew off somewhere, not even showing its figure.

On the way down from the back hill,
I saw a small dog.
The dog stared at me blankly for a moment,
Then vanished, going off between those pine trees.

When I returned to the village, my friends asked what I had gone to do,<28>
When I told them I had gone up the back hill, they seemed to mock me,
Saying, "So, how many cicadas did you catch?"
And then, a little later, I heard a child ask,
"Hey, has anyone seen our dog?"

For some reason, I suddenly felt sorrowful then.
So, secretly, I cried my heart out alone.

Ah, that was surely one summer long ago...

Fantasy
Countryside,
Farmhouse,
Inner room,
Castor oil lamp,
Wife,
-Sent back for not bearing a child-
Sigh,

...<29>

Tears,
Old maid,
-Pitiful youth-
Electric light,
Inner chamber (內室),
Tile-roofed house,
City,

...

Fantasy!

...

Them,
Me... .<30>

Song of the Beloved
The clear song of my beloved one
Is always soaking in my heart.

Even if I stand outside the gate all day listening,
The song my beloved one used to sing
Lingers in my ears until the sun sets and dusk falls.
It lingers in my ears until night comes and I fall asleep.

To the gently swaying melody,
My sleep becomes just that deep.
Even lying alone in a lonely (孤寂) bed,
My sleep becomes cozy and deep.

Then, if I wake from sleep, the beloved's song
Is lost entirely, without a trace.
As soon as I hear it, the beloved's song
Is forgotten entirely, without a trace.<31>

Old Story.
When the quietly dark night comes,
When the night comes with a dim lamplight,
Lonely and painfully, all alone,
I weep, soaked in endless tears.

My own self, too, once knew no tears,
And spent time in a small world,
Back then, the old stories (넷이악이) of days past,
I just memorized them without knowing sorrow.

But after my beloved one left,
After completely abandoning me and leaving,
All the things I used to have in former days
Have disappeared, one by one.

However, that one thing, memorized back then,
Only the old story remains.
The old story that comes to mind every night
Makes my body weep in vain.<32>

**Summary**

This collection features four poems by Yang Chu-dong. "One Year" recalls a childhood memory of disillusionment and unexplained sorrow related to a cicada and a lost dog. "Fantasy" presents stark, contrasting images of rural hardship (a barren wife, castor oil lamp) and urban modernity (electric light, tile house), hinting at social commentary or personal alienation. "Song of the Beloved" expresses deep longing for a lost love whose song brings comfort in sleep but vanishes upon waking. "Old Story" describes enduring sorrow after being abandoned by a beloved, where only a memorized old tale remains, now evoking tears instead of naive recitation. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in the influential magazine *Kaebyŏk* during Japanese colonial rule, offer insight into the personal emotional landscape of a Korean intellectual. While not overtly political, the pervasive themes of loss, sorrow, memory, and the stark contrast between traditional suffering and inaccessible modernity ('Fantasy') could subtly reflect the broader sense of cultural displacement, individual angst, or longing experienced under colonialism.

어느 해
						梁柱東
머-ㄴ, 먼, 어느 햇여름이다-

어떤 날, 나는 매암이 우는 소리를 처음 듯고,
그 아릿다운 목소리에 끄을려,
한거름에 뒷동산으로 뛰여올라 갓섯다.
그러나 내가 매암이 우는 나무 엽헤 갓가이 갓슬 때에
매암이는 노래를 뚝 끄치고,
어대로 날어갓는지, 맵시조차 보이지 안핫다.

뒷동산에서 나려 오는 길에,
나는 족으마한 개 한 마리를 보앗다.
개는 잠간 나를 물그럼이 건너다 보더니,
자취 업시, 저-편 솔나무 사이로 가고 말앗다.

마을에 돌아오니까, 동무 아이들이 무엇하러 갓섯느냐고 뭇기로,＜28＞
내가 뒷동산에 올라갓든 말을 한즉, 그애들은 나를 비웃는 듯이
「그래, 매암이 몃 마리나 잡앗느냐?」하얏다.
그리고, 족음 잇다가 어떤 아이가
「너의들, 누구, 우리 개 못 보앗늬?」 하는 말을 나는 들엇다.

어쪈 일인지 나는 그때 갑작이 설어젓다.
그래서, 남모르게 혼자 실컷 울엇다.

아아, 그것은 분명히 어느 해 여름이거니...

님의 노래
그립은 우리님의 맑은 노래는
언제나 내 가슴에 저저 잇서요.

긴 날을 문밧게서 서서 들어도
그립은 우리님의 부르든 노래는
해지고 저므도록 귀에 들려요.
밤들고 잠드도록 귀에 들려요.

고히도 흔들리는 노래가락에
내 잠은 그만이나 깁히 들어요.
孤寂한 잠자리에 홀로 누어도
내 잠은 포스근히 깁히 들어요.

그러다 자다깨면 님의 노래는
하나도 남김업시 일허 버려요.
들으면 듯는대로 님의 노래는
하나도 남김업시 닛고 말아요.＜31＞

녯 이악이.
고요하게도 어둡은 밤이 오면은
어스러한 등불에 밤이 오면은
외롭게도 괴롭게 다만 혼자로
하염업는 눈물에 저는 웁니다.

제 한몸도 예전엔 눈물 모르고,
족으마한 세상을 보냇습니다,
그 때엔 지낸 날의 넷이악이도
다못 설음 모르고 외왓습니다.

그런데 우리님이 가신 뒤로는
아주도 저를 버리고 가신 뒤로는
전날에 제게 잇든 모든 것들이
가지가지 업서지고 말앗습니다.

그러나 그, 한때에 외와두엇든
넷이악이 뿐만은 남앗습니다.
밤마다 생각나는 넷이악이는
부질업시 제 몸을 울려줍니다.＜32＞
