P'yŏngyang Random Notes
						By a Reporter at the P'yŏngan Namdo Branch

In P'yŏngyang, there are many various associations (會). Among them, a distinctive feature of recent times, which let out its first cry (呱呱) starting last spring, was a unified project of the P'yŏngyang women's community. Called the "Taedong Women's Commercial Association" (大同婦人商會), it opened for business last year. The opening was nothing special: retailing sundry goods and cosmetics on Nammun T'ongsin Road. The sales routes were mostly opened towards delinquent youths, and the purchasing of goods was limited to the P'yŏngyang Hirota Store (平壤廣田商店), drawing considerable ridicule (批笑) from onlookers and boiling resentment from the general shareholders. At this, the association's managing director (專務理事), Miss Kim Kyŏngsuk (金敬淑), roused herself and immediately went on a business trip to Ōsaka City (大阪市) to arrange a special contract for direct import of goods (商品直輸入特約) while also inspecting Japan (兼事兼事). As a result, in the autumn, they displayed Ōsaka-style sundry goods they had procured. However, amid frequent storms (風雷), the internal affairs of the association seemed to be spewing various troublesome rumors (煩多스러운 風說), and a somewhat romantic struggle (romance-的 爭鬪) began on one side. Because of this, interest in business progress waned, and unrealistic plans (公衆樓閣) began to occupy the minds of the office workers, ultimately leading to a final, shameful sealing (恥事한 封鎖). Shareholders from rural areas, thrifty women who had gathered funds from various places, packed their travel expenses and entered P'yŏngyang Castle to hold a final general meeting last winter. Whether it was the curtain call for mourning (追悼) or dissolution (解散), the scene where each person called out only for what they could retrieve—"Give us the Sŏnch'ŏn (宣川) shares back to Sŏnch'ŏn!" "Set aside the Ŭiju (義州) portion for Ŭiju!" "From Anju (安州), it was a total of 200 shares!"—is said to have been incredibly tragic. Now, has the association declared permanent closure (永遠한 閉業), or is it temporarily closed (臨時閉業)? If only they would post a clear notice on the front door of the association... to ease the minds of those who want to know!

If you go out to the provinces, so-called 'hoddeok newspaper reporters' (호떡 신문기자) are common. This term refers to those who carry around name cards identifying them as reporters for some newspaper branch, but who just hang around places like where hoddeok (a type of pancake) is baked, supposedly gathering news. It was around November of last year. A young man named Pak (박모) in P'yŏngyang, having temporarily succumbed to temptation, found himself in a situation detrimental to his reputation. A newspaper reporter, also named Pak (박모), wrote down the details of this incident on manuscript paper, went to the Pak in question, and said that if he gave him 50 won, he would bury the story secretly. At that time, Pak<76> reportedly refused the 50 won. A few days later, an article allegedly appeared on page 3 of the OO Daily News (OO일보) under the headline "Beauty Trap Fraud Gambling" (美人計詐欺賭博), exaggerating the facts several times over. There is also a person named Chŏng Ch'angdŏk (鄭昌德), who allegedly operates under the signboard of the OO Daily News P'yŏngan Namdo Branch (OO일보 平南支局) reporter, employing threats (威脅) or foolish intimidation (愚脅) like: "Is it better for your story to be published or not? If you give me 50 won, I'll sweep it under the rug just like I did recently with So-and-so's case after taking 30 won." Whether he is called by any name, this cannot solely be considered Mr. Chŏng's misfortune. It is the negligence (不察) of the OO Daily News P'yŏngan Namdo Branch managers, namely the Branch Chief or the communications supervisor; however, the presence or absence of scrutiny (察不察) by the branch management is also not the main point. This brings disgrace (失色) upon the OO Daily News company itself. The fault lies heavily in handing out titles like Branch Chief or whatnot, like a special agency (特約店) for some store, just for receiving a security deposit (保證金) of a few hundred won (但). A newspaper company, whether large or small, should be the activity of the most intellectual class, maintaining organic (有機體) connections (連絡) with the entire society. Do not treat the selection of a Branch Manager, who acts as the master of your institution's (機關) local presence, merely as a profit-making (營利) venture based on a security deposit of a few hundred won. This is my advice to the OO Daily News company. To prevent even reporters from other newspapers from suffering unjust harm, I hope they will select individuals who are somewhat upright, even if they are just country reporters. Taking this opportunity, I wish to add a couple more things: not just the OO Daily News, but other newspaper authorities too, for the sake of P'yŏngyang, even just to show respect to P'yŏngyang, should not put forward such 'hoddeok reporters'. Furthermore, considering the reputation of their own companies, they should handle such matters with special care. How great is the sin against the journalism world (操弧界) of selling one's signboard to scoundrels (惡德行事者) engaged in blackmail (公喝) or threats (脅迫) within that world?
(What more is there to add about these heartbreaking true stories?)<77>

**Summary**

This article from 1923 presents "random notes" from P'yŏngyang. The first part details the rise and rapid, somewhat scandalous, fall of the Taedong Women's Commercial Association, a business venture started by P'yŏngyang women that failed due to mismanagement, internal conflicts, and poor business decisions, ending with rural shareholders demanding their investments back. The second part sharply criticizes the unethical practices of some local newspaper reporters, labeled "hoddeok reporters," citing specific instances of blackmail allegedly involving reporters from the OO Daily News P'yŏngan Namdo Branch. The author blames the newspaper headquarters for prioritizing profit (security deposits) over competence and ethics when appointing branch representatives, thus damaging the reputation of journalism itself. For historians, the article offers insight into early Korean women's economic activities and their challenges, the state of provincial journalism during the colonial period, including corruption and the tension between central newspaper offices and their local branches, and social commentary on P'yŏngyang life in the early 1920s. The specific examples of alleged blackmail provide a glimpse into the power dynamics and ethical issues within the nascent Korean press under Japanese rule.

平壤에는, 여러 가지 會가 만타, 중에도 근래의 특색으로 지난 해 일흔 봄부터 呱呱의 소리를 내인 것은, 平壤婦女界의 단합적 한가지 사업이엿다. 各曰 「大同婦人商會」라 하고, 지난 해부터 개업을 하엿다. 개업은, 별 것이 아니라. 南門 通新路에서, 잡화, 화장품 소매, 그것이엿다. 販路는, 대개 부랑청년에게로 열리우게 되고, 物品輸買는, 平壤廣田商店에 한하게 되여, 자못 사방 인사의 批笑와, 一般株主의 원성이 물 끌릇 하엿다. 그러자 同商會 전무이사, 金敬淑 양아 분발하야 즉시, 상품직수입특약을 할 겸, 일본시찰 兼事兼事하야 大阪市에 출장하엿든 결과로, 秋期에는 大阪식 잡화를 재간 것 진열하엿셧더니, 風雷가 자즌, 同商會의 내부는, 여러 가지 煩多스러운 風說을 自吐하는 모양이엇스며 그 일면의 로맨쓰적 爭鬪가 시작되엿다. 그 때문에, 사업진행에 뜻이 업고 공중누각이 사무원들의 머리살에 오르내리기 시작하다가, 그만 최종의 恥事한 봉쇄를 마졋다. 四處에서 鳩聚하여 내인 농촌의 株主, 근검주의적 부녀들이 노자를 싸가지고, 평양성을 향하여 들어와셔 지난 겨울에 최종의 총회를 열엇다. 추도의 幕인지, 해산의 幕인지, 「宣川목은 우리 宣川으로 주시요.」 「義州치는, 義州치대로, 내여 노흐시요.」 「安州서는 합이 200株이엿소.」 등 차자갈 것만, 졔각금 불리는 광경이 넘우도, 비참하엿다고 한다. 인졔는, 同商會가, 영원한 폐업을, 선고하엿는지, 임시폐업을 하엿는지, 속 시언히 商會 門前에, 광고라도, 써부쳐 주엇스면 ... 알고십흔 사람의 마음이나 노케!

지방지방에 나가보면 소위 호떡 신문기자라는 것이 흔하다. 그것은, 무슨무슨 신문지국의 기자라는, 명함을 가지고 단니면서, 호떡 굽는 데로나, 다니면서, 사건탐방한다는 의미이다. 작년 11월 경의 일이다. 平壤의 박모라는 청년이, 일시 유혹에 빠저서, 명예상 불이익하게된 어떤 사실을 원고지에 젹어가지고 當者인 박모에게 가서, 돈 50원을 주면, 암암리에 무더 바리겟다고 한 신문기자 박모가 잇섯다. 그 때에 박모는＜76＞ 50원을 거절하엿다는대 수일 후에, OO일보 제 3면에, 「美人計詐欺賭博」이라는 題下에 사실의 몃 배를 늘여서 게재된 일이 잇섯다고 한다. OO일보 平南支局의 기자라는 간판을 내여노코, 「그대의 사실을 내이는 것이 조흐냐, 안내이는 것이 조흐냐. 돈 50원을 주면 요젼에 阿某의 사건을 30원 바다먹고, 쓸어처버린 實例과 하가지로 스러처버리지요.」라는 威脅인지, 愚脅인지를 피우든, 그 者는, 鄭昌德이라고 부른다고 한다. 아무개라고 부르건, 이는 鄭군의 불행이라고만 할 수 업는 일이다.
