Publication Date: 1923년 02월 01일
Article Title: 1月 中의 朝鮮과 世界 (Korea and the World in January)
Article Type: 소식 (News)

**1月 中의 朝線과 世界**
(Korea and the World in January)
From December 18, Imsul year, to January 22, Kyehae year

**Trial of Two Men Surnamed Paek and Kŭm** The trial of Paek Tae-jin regarding the *Sinch'ŏnji* (New World) writing incident was held at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, and on the same day, the trial of Kŭm To-wŏn, leader of the Poaptan group, opened at the Kyŏngsŏng Appeals Court. (December 18)

**Haeju Fire** A large fire broke out on Namdaemun Street within Haeju city, destroying over 10 houses. (Ibid.)

**Sino-Russian Talks Break Down** Due to the Russian Communist Party's propaganda promoting aggression in Mongolia and along the Chinese Eastern Railway, Sino-Russian talks have reached a desperate state. (Ibid.)

**Murdering Policeman Commits Suicide** Policeman Hirashima (平島) stationed at the Tŏksan-myŏn substation in P'yŏngwŏn County, for unknown reasons, shot and killed passerby Kim T'ae-p'yŏng (from Anju) without cause, and then committed suicide with his own gun. (19th)

**Masan Fire** A large fire started at the Masan Rice Mill in Wŏn-chō, Masan-bu, destroying over 600 sŏk of unhulled rice. (Ibid.)

**Laborers Buried Alive** At the construction site of the Kyushu Hydroelectric Company in Ōno County, Ōita Prefecture, Japan, a tunnel collapsed, burying over 10 people alive; it is reported that many were Korean laborers.

**British Ultimatum to Turkey** British Foreign Secretary Lord Curzon (「케손」경) delivered an ultimatum to Turkey, stating that if the Allied proposals are not accepted, the Near East Conference will collapse. (Ibid.)

**Martial Law in Poland** Issued by order of the new Prime Minister, Mr. Sikorski (「시콜쓰키」氏), for maintaining public order. (Ibid.)

**Return of Messrs. Kim and Yi** Messrs. Kim Yun-su and Yi Chŏng-pŏm, who had attended the Pan-Pacific Commercial Conference, returned to Korea. (20th)

**Straits Issue Resolved** As Turkey accepted the Allied proposal, the Straits question has been settled. (Ibid.)

**International Conference Participation Ruptured** The issue of Korean lawyers' participation in the international lawyers' conference opening in Manila, Philippines, on January 10th ultimately broke down due to the Prosecutor General's insistence that they participate under Japanese names.

**Mr. Yi Chae-gŭk Questioned** Regarding the issue of erecting a monument at Hongnŭng, the Ihwa Society (이화학회) questioned the Minister of the Office of the Yi Household (李王職). (Ibid.)

**Eleven Drown** A ferryboat capsized on the Naktong River in Hyŏnp'ung-myŏn, Talsŏng County, drowning 11 passengers.

**Tenant Farmers Rise Up** Over 1,000 tenant farmers in Sŏ-myŏn, Sunch'ŏn County, angered by the tyranny of landlords, declared a five-point resolution to the landlords. (To divide the harvest equally after deducting public charges and land tax; to refuse unpaid labor service; for the landlord to bear the costs of natural disasters; and two other points) (Ibid.)

**Poland's New President** Left-wing candidate Mr. Wojciechowski (「보-세코쓰키」씨) was elected. (Ibid.)

**Tonghŭng School Support Association** Concurrent with the permission for fundraising for Tonghŭng Middle School in Kando, various prominent figures in Kyŏngsŏng organized a support association to aid the school. (22nd)

**Russia Refuses Foreign Warships** The Labor-Peasant Russian Government refused entry of foreign warships into the Baltic Sea.

**India Communization Plan** The Indian Communist Party in Berlin announced a concrete communization plan (Abolition of the landlord system, nationalization of public utility enterprises, establishment of labor councils, construction of a federal republic). (Ibid.)

**Mr. Paek Yun-hwa Threatened** Mr. Paek Yun-hwa (a district court judge) in Mugyo-dong, Kyŏngsŏng City, was threatened by a pistol gang. (23rd)

**Two Robbers in Kwach'ŏn** Two robbers attacked five passersby on Namhae Mountain in Kwach'ŏn, Sihŭng County, robbing them of 26 won in cash before fleeing. (Ibid.)

**Agreement on Universal Suffrage** Japan's political parties Kenseikai (憲政會), Kakushin Club (革新俱樂部), Kōshin Club (庚申俱樂部), and other opposition parties reached an agreement on universal suffrage. (Ibid.)

**Ch'ŏndogyo Commemoration Day** The Ch'ŏndogyo Church held a ceremony at its church building to commemorate the succession of Son Pyŏng-hŭi (孫義菴), the third leader of the religion. (24th)

**Writers' Association Organized** Organized by Yi Nae-hun, Yŏm Sang-sŏp, O Sang-sun, Hwang Sŏk-u, and other writers. (Ibid.)

**Foreign Post Offices Abolished** Japan, Britain, the US, France, etc., decided to completely abolish their foreign post offices in China by December 31st. (Ibid.)

**Pan-Russian Unification** The Labor-Peasant Government passed the All-Russia Unification Plan. (Ibid.)

Paek Tae-jin was sentenced to 6 months for the crime of disturbing the state constitution (朝憲紊亂罪). (25th)

**Rejection of British Currency Passed** The Indian legislature passed a bill rejecting British currency. (Ibid.)

**National Self-Determination Association** The Shanghai Council in China organized and proclaimed the Chinese National Self-Determination Association as a movement to prevent international joint administration. (Ibid.)

**Major Socialism Trial** The trial of Pak Hŭi-do and Kim Myŏng-sik in the *Sinsaenghwal* (New Life) case, the first socialism trial in Korea, opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court. (26th)

**New Korean Independence State** It is reported that Koreans residing near Siberia constructed a New Korean Independence State in Khabarovsk (「하바라수코」), establishing six departments: Administration, General Affairs, Finance, Judiciary, Army, and Staff.<81> (Ibid.)

**Armed Independence Group in Pyŏktong** Eight members shot and killed Ch'oe Hak-hak, a wealthy man in the county, and simultaneously set fire to the house of the public school principal.

**Robber Sŏng Mun-ho** Arrested after struggling with a policeman near Sulla-gol, Kyŏngsŏng. (Ibid.)

**Kŭm To-wŏn Sentenced to Death** Kŭm To-wŏn, leader of the Poaptan group, who had shot a detective in Kyŏngsŏng city and subsequently spent a year in prison, was sentenced to death by the Kyŏngsŏng Appeals Court. (27th)

**National Representatives' Meeting** A preliminary meeting of the Korean National Representatives' Meeting opened in Shanghai. (Ibid.)

**Chaos in Parliament** The Chinese Chamber of Councillors turned into pandemonium due to conflicting opinions when Premier Chang's bill was introduced. (Ibid.)

**Turkish Participation Refused** Turkey was refused participation by the committee on the protection of minority races. (Ibid.)

**New Muslim Leader** Mr. "Tansal" (「탄살」씨) gained recognition as the leader from the Muslim Leaders' Conference. (Ibid.)

**Disarmament Manifesto to the World** The All-Russian Soviet Congress decided to issue a disarmament manifesto to the entire world. (Ibid.)

Japan's House of Peers and House of Representatives opened. (28th)

**Volunteer Corps Arrested Again** Over 10 members of the Independence Support Volunteer Corps were arrested again in Kyŏngbuk province. (Ibid.)

**Mr. Lenin** Received approval from the All-Russian Central Executive Committee to concurrently serve as Premier. (Ibid.)

The Government-General announced the budget draft for Fiscal Year 12 (1923). (29th)

**Robbers in Yangju** Over 10 robbers appeared in Sinhyŏn-ri, Kuri-myŏn, Yangju, attacking 6 passersby. (Ibid.)

**Kyŏngsŏng Fire** A large fire broke out at the public market in Meiji-chō (明治町), completely burning down over 20 shops. (30th)

**Communization Propaganda** 29 Russians were ordered to leave Wŏnsan for Vladivostok (海蔘威). (Ibid.)

**Wuzhou Falls** Yunnan forces captured Wuzhou (梧州), and Chen Jiongming's (陳炯明) situation became desperate. (Ibid.)

**Anglo-French Disagreement** Britain declared strong opposition to France's military measures due to disagreement over German reparations. (Ibid.)

**Shanghai Fire** A large fire broke out in a Chinese district, destroying over 1,200 houses, and 30 bodies were discovered. (31st)

**New Year's Cards from OO Party** Sealed New Year's cards inscribed with "Republican New Year" (共和新年) and "Long Live Communism" (共産主義萬歲) were scattered throughout Kyŏngsŏng city. (1st Jan)

Namdaemun Station was renamed Kyŏngsŏng Station. (Ibid.)

**Two Men from Pistol Gang** Entered the house of Yi Sŏk-ku in Changgyo-dong, Kyŏngsŏng city, demanding funds for the military.

**Sun Yat-sen (孫文總理)** Representing the Kuomintang in Shanghai, proclaimed the principle of national self-determination. (Ibid.)

**India Refuses Burden** India decided to refuse all burdens from the British administration henceforth. (Ibid.)

**Express Train Derails** An engine derailed near Taegu, but there was no significant damage. (2nd)

**President Li Expresses New Policies** Policies such as abolishing the military governor (Tuchun) system, implementing troop reductions, settling foreign debts, and unifying North and South were announced to each province. (Ibid.)

**Allied Premiers' Conference** Held in Paris. (Ibid.)

**National Representatives' Meeting** Formally opened in Shanghai. (3rd)

**1,000 Bandits** Caused a great disturbance at Nanyuan Shàngzi in Manchuria. (Ibid.)

**Joint Friendship Meeting Ruptured** The annual New Year's Joint Friendship Meeting of various social leaders in Kyŏngsŏng, held at the Kugilgwan hall, was ultimately dispersed midway due to obstruction by the Proletarian Youth Faction. (4th)

**China's Zhang Shao Zeng Cabinet** Formally announced.

**Chen Jiongming Defeated** Yunnan forces advanced on Guangdong (廣東) along three routes. (Ibid.)

**Independence Army Clashes Three Times** Clashed with police three times in Ch'osan, injuring one policeman. (Ibid.)

**Paris Conference Ruptures** Ultimately broke down due to Anglo-French conflict. (Ibid.)

**Six Needy Students** Six needy students, including Kim Chae-su and Yi Chong-ha, were taken into custody at Chongno Police Station centering around the Kugilgwan disturbance. (5th)

**P'yŏngyang Labor Federation** Searched by the local police station on suspicion of secret documents. (Ibid.)

**Mr. Yi Sŭng-hun** Went to Tokyo, Japan, for an educational inspection trip. (Ibid.)

**Britain Isolated** France, Italy, and Belgium (白國) raised objections to Britain's German reparations plan. (Ibid.)

**Shoemakers Resume Work** Striking Western-style shoemakers in Kyŏngsŏng finally resumed work at the original wages. (6th)

**Tenant Farmers' Rally** The tenant farmer movement against landlords in Sunch'ŏn intensified; the county office hosted a meeting, presenting 9 proposals and convening 50 tenant representatives to seek a resolution. (Ibid.)

**Turkish Representative** Strongly insisted on the abolition of Allied extraterritorial rights.<82> (Ibid.)

**New US Proposal** A proposal arose from the US Senate suggesting Britain and France cede the West Indies and Bermuda (「바-무다」島) as compensation for war debts. (7th)

**German Government Statement** The German government issued a statement rejecting the Allied demands for German reparations as excessive. (7th)

***Sinsaenghwal* Publication Banned** The *Sinsaenghwal* magazine was banned, and its staff faced a second trial. (8th)

**French Army Marches into Ruhr Region** The French army advanced into the Ruhr (「루엘」) region, and as a countermeasure, the Polish army also mobilized. (Ibid.)

**Dr. Hulbert (「덜스트」博士) Returns Home** Dr. Hulbert, who had been in charge of a department at Severance Hospital for many years, departed for the US with his wife due to illness. (9th) (*Translator's note: "덜스트" likely refers to Dr. O. R. Avison or someone associated with Severance, but "Hulbert" is a guess based on phonetics and prominence; the name is ambiguous.*)

**Society for the Promotion of Korean Products** Initiated by Messrs. Yu Chin-t'ae, Chŏng Kwan-sik, Kim Su, and Yi P'an-nŭng.

**Yi P'an-nŭng** Life imprisonment demanded by the prosecution following a mental evaluation.

**Tokyo Fire** A large fire broke out in Shibuya (澁谷), Tokyo, destroying about 350 houses. (Ibid.)

**Ch'ŏndogyo Proclaims Prayer Period** The church proclaimed a 21-day prayer period for the spiritual training (煉性工夫) of all believers. (10th)

**Robbery at Tosŏn Hermitage** Four robbers appeared at Tosŏn-am (道詵菴), threatened the head monk, and fled after robbing 7 wŏn in cash.

**Troubled Situation in South China** Chang Ching (?) (長慶) was captured by allied forces, and *** fled to Jingzhou (荊州). (10th) (*Translator's note: Names unclear*)

**China's Premier Chang** Announced unification policies to provincial leaders and southern figures like Sun Yat-sen (孫文) and Cen Chunxuan (岑春煊). (Ibid.)

**French Army Closes in on Essen** Greatly angered by Germany's non-delivery of coal, the French army closed in on Essen (「엣셴」). (Ibid.)

**Another Large Fire in Kyŏngsŏng** A large fire broke out at a Chinese shop in Kwansu-dong, destroying goods worth 50,000 wŏn and 2 buildings. (11th)

**Major Police Activity** Believing an assassination group had infiltrated Kyŏngsŏng city, the South and North Police Stations launched major activities.

**Sun Yat-sen** Decided to enter Guangdong; Luo Wengan (羅文幹) was released as innocent. (11th)

**French Army Occupies Ruhr** The French army occupied the Ruhr (「루엘」), and all of Germany held large protest movements. Simultaneously, Germany ordered the withdrawal of the French and Belgian ambassadors and declared refusal of reparations. (Ibid.)

**Former Greek King Dies** Mr. Constantine (「코스탄친」씨), the former King of Greece, passed away in Italy. (Ibid.)

**Bomb Thrown at Chongno Police Station** Around 8:10 PM, a bomb was thrown at the west window of the police station, breaking two windows and causing moderate to severe injuries to 7 passersby, creating a major incident in Kyŏngsŏng city. (12th)

**Ŭiju Incident** Nine independence soldiers appeared in Ŭiju, clashed with police, and then fled. (Ibid.)

**In China** Bills for abolishing military governors (廢督案) and reducing troops (裁兵案) were passed. (Ibid.)

Due to the issue of unconfirmed admission qualifications, students from Kyŏngsŏng First Higher Common School, along with students from six major higher schools in Kyŏngsŏng, held a large joint protest. (13th)

**Tax Refusal** Residents of Pukch'ŏn-myŏn, Hadong County, formed a league to refuse payment of national and supplementary taxes in protest against the local officials. (Ibid.)

**No Reply to German Protest** Germany protested the French occupation of the Ruhr (「루엘」) to the Allies, but the Allies did not send a reply. (Ibid.)

**Fairy Tale Drama Festival** The first fairy tale drama festival in Korea, organized by the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Association, opened at the Ch'ŏndogyo Church. (14th)

**Twenty Independence Soldiers** Attacked the Sŏt'ae Police Substation in Wiwŏn. (15th)

**Meeting on Tuition and Dorm Fees** A meeting of representatives from 8 influential organizations in Kyŏngsŏng city was held regarding the issue of lowering monthly tuition (月謝金) and dormitory fees (寄宿料). (Ibid.)

**French Army Violence** During the occupation of Bochum (「보콤」), French soldiers shot Germans dead because they loudly sang the national anthem. (Ibid.)

***Sinsaenghwal* Case** Verdict delivered in the *Sinsaenghwal* case; several political offenders were arrested at Tongdaemun Police Station. (16th)

**Kim Ik-sang's Younger Brother** OO, the younger brother of Kim Ik-sang, was arrested in Shimonoseki (下關) on suspicion of plotting the Governor-General's assassination. (Ibid.)

**Policeman Shot Dead in Kyŏngsŏng City** In the early hours of the 17th, Detective Tamura (田村) of Chongno Police Station was shot dead on OO Street in the city, and Inspector Imase (今賴) of the same station and Assistant Inspector Umeda (梅田) of Tongdaemun Police Station suffered serious injuries in a major incident.

Governor-General Saitō (齊蘇總督) traveled to Tokyo; a general meeting of Buddhist abbots opened within the Government-General building. (18th)

**In China** A clash occurred between student groups and police in front of the parliament building during a session, resulting in many serious and minor injuries.

**Greece and Turkey** Clashed in the Thrace (「스래-스」) region, resulting in some casualties. (19th)

**Hungary** Experienced bandit unrest, resulting in the killing of 20 peasants.

**Manchuria** It is reported that an independence group shot and killed over 10 pro-Japanese Koreans in the Haijing (海井) area along the Chinese Eastern Railway. (20th)

**Hongnŭng Monument Issue** It is reported that notification came from the Imperial Household Ministry (宮內省) granting permission as requested by the Office of the Yi Household (李王職).

**Miners Strike in Ruhr** Miners went on a sympathy strike in the Ruhr (「루엘」) coal mines occupied by the French army.

**Policeman's Killer Killed** OOO, the culprit who shot the policeman on the 17th, engaged in a prolonged firefight with a squad of 80 police officers in Hyojedong, Kyŏngsŏng city, in the dawn of the 22nd, and was finally killed by police bullets; simultaneously, an inspector from Tongdaemun Police Station was also seriously injured.<83>

**Summary**

This news digest covers events in Korea and internationally from mid-December 1922 to late January 1923. Domestically, it reports numerous fires, accidents, robberies, and social unrest, including tenant farmer protests, student demonstrations over school fees and qualifications, and labor strikes. Significant political and independence-related events include trials of activists (Paek Tae-jin, Kŭm To-wŏn, Sinsaenghwal socialists), the formation of a "New Korean Independence State" in Siberia, activities of armed independence groups (clashes in Pyŏktong, Ch'osan, Wiwŏn; attacks in Manchuria), the bombing of Chongno Police Station, the subsequent killing of a suspect after a shootout, and arrests related to assassination plots and independence support groups. Internationally, it covers the breakdown of Sino-Russian talks, the Anglo-Turkish crisis resolution, the French occupation of the Ruhr and German resistance, political changes in Poland and China, Soviet developments (Lenin's position, unification plan, disarmament call), and events in India (communization plans, rejection of British currency).

For historians, this article is valuable as a contemporary snapshot of the turbulent socio-political climate in colonial Korea. It documents the pervasiveness of Korean resistance (from legal challenges and protests to armed struggle and bombings), the harsh colonial response (trials, arrests, suppression), economic hardship (fires, strikes, fee protests), and the connection of the Korean situation to broader international events like post-WWI diplomacy, the rise of communism, and anti-colonial movements elsewhere (China, India). The reporting on the National Representatives' Meeting in Shanghai and the "New Korean Independence State" highlights the diaspora's efforts in the independence movement.
