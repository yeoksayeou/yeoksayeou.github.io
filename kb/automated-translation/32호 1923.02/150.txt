Shanghai Jottings
						Chang Tŏksan

They say this place is Sanghae. The London of the East.
What gives it such a name?
The ships entering and leaving the enormous wharf,
Lined up tail-to-tail like teeth in a comb.
Those splendid buildings on Nankingno Road!
Those sturdy iron houses along the Hwangp'ot'an Bund path,
They say are either banks or companies.
Ah—I realize this is the foremost trading port in the East.

Trams and cars run day and night,
Rickshaws and carriages never cease.
Lights flash on telephones and telegraphs,
The sound of typewriters and abacuses hurts the ears.
It seems as though the population of 2 million has all rushed out,
It seems there is not one person
Taking a nap or playing Go.
This is truly a living city.

All sorts of races gather, boasting their strength.
By financial power, by physical strength, and then by military might,
The struggle for existence surges like a tide.
The saying "the weak are prey to the strong" still applies in this late era.
I ask, who is the master of this land?
Ah—the old landlord politely bows with clasped hands (揖)
And has offered up the whole thing to the yellow-haired devils (黃毛鬼).
I realize who the master of this land is.

The greenery in Sanghae's six great parks is lush,
The verdant hues look as if they might drip down at any moment.
Beautiful flowers of unknown names,
A strange fragrance (香嗅) stings the nose.
This too is the playground of the yellow-haired devils.
Within my own borrowed land,
If I enter wearing my clothes, I am thrown out.
Ah—blood wells in the chest of the old, frail landlord.

The required attire for entering the park is Western clothes.
Even Eastern clothes (Japanese clothes) are refused entry.
The influence of Western clothes—is truly great.
But Korean clothes are freely allowed anywhere.
Judging by this, our clothes also have wide acceptance
And occupy a considerable influence.
No, no, it seems clear that we "Kkoriin" (Koreans) too
Have become world figures.

Indian police like Red Turban rebels,
Annam (Vietnamese) police<74> wearing green conical hats,
Guiding the way on the bustling city roads.
Raising and lowering batons in their left hands,
It seems their duty is only that.
Their menacingly braided (編) mustaches,
Their hideously twisted topknots,
Just looking at them makes one shudder.

Indian police are forbidden to cut their hair short,
The deeds of the Englishmen (英人) are truly strange (怪心).
Are they not trying to make antiques out of their police
Just to look at them and laugh?
Anglo-Saxons stick with Anglo-Saxons,
Latins and Slavs also stick together.
Each commands their own slaves (奴).
The masters of this playground are clearly them.

Enviable! The activity of Chinese women.
Beautiful! The Boy and Girl Scouts (童子童女軍).
Seeking financial independence,
There are many businesses run by women's hands.
To devote themselves to national and social affairs,
Women's associations, formed by cutting their hair in alliance.
Boy and Girl Scouts aiming to correct public morals.
The dawn of this country will come from them.

Chinese young women with stylishly cut front hair (압머리),
Beauties with rouge on lips and cheeks,
When they go out after dinner,
They stream out in carriages and rickshaws.
Like fairies wandering the Twelve Peaks of Wushan (巫山12峰),
The beauties of Sohangju (蘇抗洲 - Suzhou/Hangzhou) have all gathered here.
But a fearsome poison mixed in the fragrance—
Just thinking of it makes my skin crawl.

On the jade-plate-like tennis courts,
The forearms of Western women (洋女) hitting the ball over with rackets,
Adorned with jewel rings or gold bracelets (금팔도리).
Picking up dropped balls seems their routine task.
The state (景狀) of the children of this land's master is miserable,
The army of "Wangboch'ŏ" (rickshaw pullers - 인력거) running, dripping with sweat,
Yet still kicked from behind for not going fast enough!
Ah—this world is unfair, the feeling of this land. (End)
- May 8, 1922, at the P'ŭranssŭ Kongwŏn (French Park) -<75>

**Summary**

The author, Chang Tŏksan, describes his impressions of Shanghai in 1922. He portrays it as a bustling, modern metropolis, the "London of the East," dominated by Western economic and military power ("yellow-haired devils"). He observes the stark contrasts: the dynamism and wealth alongside foreign control, the exclusion felt by Chinese ("the old landlord") even in public parks, and the exploitation of laborers like rickshaw pullers. He notes the international presence, including Indian and Vietnamese police serving foreign interests. He also observes the emerging role of Chinese women and youth movements with hope, while expressing caution about the city's allure. The author reflects on Korea's position, noting Korean attire is surprisingly accepted, perhaps ironically suggesting Koreans ("Kkoriin") have gained some "world figure" status, though the context implies a complex feeling about identity and influence in this imperial playground. For historians, the article offers a valuable Korean colonial-era perspective on Shanghai, highlighting the complex interplay of colonialism, modernization, nationalism, and racial hierarchies in the treaty port city. It reveals Korean consciousness of its own status relative to both China and Western/Japanese imperial powers, and the observations on social dynamics (foreign dominance, Chinese resistance/change, labor exploitation) provide a contemporary snapshot of Shanghai's atmosphere.

이곳은 상해란다. 동양의 런돈.
그 무엇 가르쳐 일홈함인가.
굉장한 부두의 출입하는 배(舟)
꼬리를 맛무러 빗살 박히듯
南京路의 화려한 져 건물들라
黃浦灘길이 튼튼한 져 쇠집들은
은행이 아니면 회사라 한다.
아ㅡ동양 제일 무역항 이로 알괴라.

전차와 자동차 밤낫 달니고
인력거 마차는 끈일 새 업다.
전화와 전보기에 불이 생기고
타자기 주판 소래 귀가 압흐다.
200만 인구가 다 떠러난 듯
낫잠 자고 바둑 두는 者
하나 업는 듯
이것이 과연 산 도회이다.
각색 인종 모혀드러 제 힘 자랑해
金力으로 腕力으로 그 다음 武力
물밀 듯 하도다 생존경쟁이
弱肉强食時代遲의 말 아직 적용해
뭇노라 이 땅 주인 그 누구인가?
아ㅡ늙은 집주인 공손히 손 揖하고서
왼통 드러 밧쳣도다 黃毛鬼의 게
알괴라 이 땅 주인 그 누구임을.

上海 6대 공원 녹음이 푸러
蒼翠가 방금 떠러질 듯 한다.
일홈 몰을 아름답운 꼿
이샹한 香嗅가 코를 찌른다.
이것도 黃毛鬼들 노름판이라.
내가 빌닌 내 땅 안에셔
내 옷 입고 드러가면 追出을 당해
아ㅡ가슴에 피고인다 노쇠한 집주인.

공원의 출입예복 양복이란다.
동양복(일본복)도 거절을 당해
양복의 그 勢ㅡ과연 크도다.
그러나 고려복은 어듸나 자유.
이로보아 우리 옷도 公認이 넓고
어지간한 세력을 차지하엿다.
아니아니 우리네 「꼬리」인들도
세계젹 인물됨이 분명하도다.

紅巾賊 가튼 인도의 巡査
청삿갓 쓴 안남의 巡捕＜74＞
번잡한 도회길 인도하노라.
왼손에 방망이를 들엇다 노핫다
그네들 임무는 그것 뿐인 듯
험상스럽게 따아(編) 넘긴 수염
흉물스럽게 틀어부친 그 상투
보기만 하여도 진저리가 난다.

인도의 순사는 단발을 금해
英人의 하는 즛 怪心도 하다.
그들는 순사로 골동품 만들어
보고서 웃고저 함이 아닌가!
앵글노 색손은 앵글노 색손끼리
라틴과 슬라브 또한 저끼리
제각금 제 종(奴)을 잡아 부린다.
이 노름판 주인은 분명 그네들.

부럽다. 중국의 여자의 활동.
아름답다. 童子童女軍.
생계의 독립을 엇고저 하야
여자의 손으로 사업이 만타
國事와 사회에 몸바치려
머리깍고 동맹한 여자동맹단.
풍기를 矯正코저 男女童子軍.
이나리 曙光은 그들이 된다.

맵시나게 압머리 자른 중국의 春女
입술과 뺨에 연지미인들
저녁을 먹고 나서게 되면
마차로 인력거로 쓸어나온다.
巫山12峰에 仙女가 논일 듯
蘇抗洲 미인들은 다 모혀드럿다.
그러나 향기에 석긴 무서운 독
생각만 하야도 소름끼친다.

玉盤 가튼 테니쓰 코ㅡ트에
락켓들어 처 넘우는 洋女의 팔뚝
보석 반지 아니면 금팔도리라.
떨어진 뽈 집어주기 일과 삼는 것.
이 땅 주인 아이들의 景狀이 참혹
비지땀을 흘려가며 달리는 「왕보쳐」
(인력거)軍
그래도 못 간다고 꽁문이를 차!
아ㅡ不分平한 세상이다 이 땅의 늣김.(끗)
