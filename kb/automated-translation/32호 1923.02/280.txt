Fifty Folk Songs of Cheju Island, Call-and-Response Songs of Women Grinding Millstones
						Kang Pongok

Everyone knows that folk songs are the expressed flowers of a nation's character. Therefore, there is no need to speak anew about the value of folk songs now. The only regrettable thing is that the songs of our people seem as if they have been abandoned in a vast wilderness. These are songs sung by the women of Cheju Island (濟州島). With explicit, simple lyrics, they are truly the sorrows overflowing from the wellspring of the heart, stemming from our people's hunger for affection and yearning for love. They are the sacred, beautiful infants of raw, rustic humanity. The rhythm, bowing before mediocrity, repeating before solemn form—it is the beauty of sentiment sung with a primitive melody, a pure humanity without obsequiousness or cowardice, a simple plea, a flawless confession. Among the many folk songs of Cheju Island—songs sung by women grinding millstones (磨石), songs sung by diving women (潛娥 [海女]), songs sung while weaving yangdae (making Korean traditional hat frames), while netting manggŏn (網巾 - traditional headband), songs sung by farmers while weeding, and other song-like melodies such as the herdsman's calls and fisherman's songs—these are sung when women grind millstones. Regardless of whether there are two, three, or four people, one person leads the song, and the next person follows, singing a chorus. Just as Shelley said, "Our sweetest songs are those that tell of saddest thought," this melody is the mournful tune of a cicada (蟬) weeping in the autumn shade. Due to the archaic tone and abundant dialect, there are many meanings I cannot yet comprehend due to my limited historical scalpel <39>, and I can only ask for the bright understanding of various elders. Accordingly, I intend to gather and present some Korean folk songs in the future. People, people, come close and kiss the songs of our nation.

Ihŏdo (離虛島) is an island in the legends of Cheju Island people. It is said one can reach it by sailboat in 4 or 5 days southwest of Cheju Island. However, no one has ever returned from there. The island is said to be flat land like the horizon in the middle of the sea, always shrouded in clouds and mist, spring throughout the four seasons, a fairyland far removed from the world, an ideal paradise yearned for by the people of Cheju Island.

**Summary**

This article by Kang Pongok introduces 50 folk songs from Cheju Island, specifically focusing on the call-and-response songs sung by women while grinding millstones in 1923. The author frames these songs not just as simple tunes but as profound expressions of the Korean people's innermost feelings – their sorrows, yearnings for love, and unadorned humanity. Kang emphasizes their raw, sentimental beauty, contrasting them with formal artistic conventions. The introduction also provides cultural context, explaining the legend of Ihŏdo (a utopian island) and mentioning other types of Cheju folk songs. The songs themselves cover a wide range of themes pertinent to women's lives: hardship, longing for absent loved ones, family relationships (including difficult in-laws and co-wives), social commentary (comparing Seoul and Cheju), reflections on fate and death, and connections to the natural world.

For historians, this article offers valuable insights into early 20th-century Cheju culture, particularly the lives and perspectives of women. The songs serve as primary sources reflecting social structures, economic conditions (references to magistrates, officials, poverty in Seoul), folk beliefs (Ihŏdo, fate, afterlife customs), linguistic features (Cheju dialect, older Korean orthography), and the emotional landscape of Koreans under colonial rule, albeit expressed through personal and traditional themes rather than overt political statements. The detailed descriptions and explanations accompanying the songs enhance their value as cultural and historical records.

민요는 그 국민성의 표현된 꼿이라 함은 누구나 다 아는 바이외다. 딸하 민요의 가치가 어떠하다 함은 이제 새삼스럽게 말할 필요가 업겟습니다. 다못 한스러운 것은 우리 민족의 노래는 넘우나 황야에 버리워진 것 가튼 그것이외다. 이것은 濟州島의 主張女子들이 부르는 노래올시다. 노골적 단조로운 「리리크」로써 참으로 우리 민족이 인정에 줄이고 사랑의 동경에 심정의 샘(泉)이 넘처나는 설음이올시다. 적나라인 粗野의 인간의 신성한 美의 赤子입니다. 「리즘」은 凡庸의 압헤 구을느고 장엄한 형식압헤 번복하야 무어라는 추종업고 怯懦업는 순결한 인간성, 소박한 哀訴, 흠업는 고백이 원시적 선율로써 노래한 「센틔몐탈」의 美입니다. 濟州島의 민요에 여자들이 맷돌(磨石)을 갈 때에 부르는 노래와 潛娥(海女)들이 부르는 노래와 양대(朝鮮笠子를 역는 것)역글 때, 망건(網巾) 뜰 때에 부르는 노래, 농부들이 김맬 때 부르는 노래, 기타 가곡적 「메로데이」로 부르는 목동의 소리, 어부의 소리들이 만흔 중에 이것은 여자들이 맷돌을 갈 때에 두 사람이나 3, 4인을 물론하고 한 사람이 노래를 주면 그 다음 사람이 딸하서 「코-라스」를 부르는 것이올시다. 「세-레」가 「가장 설음에서 넘처난 노래가 가장 아릿다운 詩歌라」는 말과 가티 이 곡조는 秋蔭에서 嗚咽하는 매암(蟬)의 哀調입니다. 古體的 語調와 방언이 만흠으로 역사적 메스력이＜39＞ 적은 나에게는 아즉 이해치 못할 語意가 만흔 것은 다만 여러 어른의 밝으신 이해를 비는 바이며 딸하 후일에 조선민요의 약간을 모아 드릴가 합니다. 사람사람이어 우리 민족의 노래에 갓가이 와서 「키스」하시오.
