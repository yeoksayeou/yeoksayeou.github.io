기사제목	多事한 癸亥 京城 一月을 들어, (시골 게신 M兄에게 부치노라 1月 22日) (Recounting the Eventful January in Kyŏngsŏng of the Kyehae Year, (Sent to Brother M in the Countryside, January 22nd))
필자	春坡 (Ch'unp'a)
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

Recounting the Eventful January in Kyŏngsŏng of the Kyehae Year,
						Ch'unp'a

M hyŏng. I have just returned after seeing off SP-kun, who is heading to Tokyo, at Kyŏngsŏng Station. The road was so muddy that the back hem of my turumagi got terribly dirty. People are whispering here and there, though whether it's true or just a rumor, that somewhere in the city, a certain police squad shot a certain criminal dead.

M hyŏng. Did it rain where you are, hyŏngnim? In Seoul, it started raining last night and rained all day yesterday. The weather hasn't cleared up today either. The muddy, slushy roads and the dripping rainwater are just like the July monsoon season, and the trickling snowmelt and the softly thawing ice definitely feel like spring. While not entirely unprecedented, this is the first time I've seen so much rain during the Taehan period (Great Cold). And the weather is so foul; a yellow fog (黃霧) presses down heavily, making it hard to think straight. Like fish that have eaten boiled water chestnut (가래), everyone just shakes their heads and vents their ill temper.

M hyŏng. For us, managing our difficult lives, both internally and externally, the way we get by must be the same, whether one is in the countryside like you or in Seoul. There's nothing particularly to boast about, nothing particularly to champion, right? Since it's the beginning of the year, perhaps there might be something to boast about, but for me, there's nothing worth mentioning. I just keep scribbling these dull and murky words as they are. Trying to express these vague, spineless, seedless, rambling thoughts makes my heart pound and my fingertips tingle, and there are times I just want to fling this brush away. And the thought of going down to the countryside like you, hyŏngnim, to find solace for my mind and spirit amidst green waters and blue mountains, and to secure physical sustenance through tilling my own land – I don't know how many times a day this thought crosses my mind.<62>

M hyŏng. Is this perhaps the talk of a loser? If so, should I become like those who take a step further north, shouldering guns and swords, engaging recklessly in destruction ahead and construction behind? It's not that I haven't thought about it, but talk without action is just empty bluster, so I will just introduce the matters I intended to inform you about.

M hyŏng. It's mutual sentiment, isn't it, that those in the countryside want to know news from Seoul, and those in Seoul long for news from the countryside? Indeed, I truly miss news from the countryside. I am anxious because I don't know how everyone is getting by. I often feel a cowardly sympathy, wondering: Are they perhaps starving? Have they perhaps left? Are they perhaps being insulted by some scoundrel? And when I hear from some news source about the vigorous emergence of movements most crucial to our people's lives right now, such as the Temperance and Anti-Smoking Movement (禁酒斷煙問題), the Self-Production and Self-Sufficiency Movement (自作自給問題), and the Tenant Farmers vs. Landlords Movement (小作人對地主問題), there have been times when I rejoiced and jumped for joy, thinking, "Ah, a new movement is rising among us! Ah, the path to self-reliance (自活) is opening for us!"

M hyŏng. Just as I do this for the sake of those in the countryside, you must be waiting anxiously, your eyes growing heavy, for news from Seoul, right? Though I cannot perceive other matters, I deeply understand this sentiment. Therefore, for those in the countryside, I intend to introduce, at least roughly, the state of affairs in Seoul at the beginning of the Kyehae year. Perhaps you have already heard through newspapers or rumors...

M hyŏng. The beginning of the Kyehae year seems exceptionally eventful, unlike the usual. Since January is when the plans for the entire year converge, and also because it is the Kyehae year, marking the start of a new sexagenary cycle after the previous sixty years have passed, it is natural for various sectors of society to be busy. Whether for this reason or that, it is unusually eventful. Leaving aside the wider world, our Chosŏn is certainly eventful. In Shanghai, the National Representatives' Conference is being held; in Manchuria, the unification movement of the independence army is arising; within Chosŏn proper, peasant movements and domestic product movements are occurring; and within the minds of individuals, various conflicting ideas—communism, socialism, independence, self-strengthening, etc.—are entangled, creating a mood where stability and self-possession are impossible, and it seems like any trigger could lead to rash actions, as if we are on the verge of a major upheaval (大事將起).

M hyŏng. This is a separate issue. It seems I've strayed from what I intended to talk about. I'll get straight to the point and dive right into the Kyŏngsŏng news.

M hyŏng. January 1st. Being the first day of the new year, various sectors of society were extraordinarily busy. There was much ado about paying New Year's visits, drinking Toso-ju (屠蘇酒),<63> receiving New Year's cards, and so on. But the day was so cold and the wind so fierce that everyone had ice hanging from their noses and tumbled around like wind-blown gourds. A young girl fetching noodles stood crying at the gate, and a young boy going to pay New Year's respects collapsed while opening the door. It was a day of severe cold, rare in recent years, yet houses were still busy receiving New Year's cards. One unusual thing was the New Year's card from the CC Party (CC당), titled "Konghwa Sinnnyŏn" (共化新年 - Communized New Year?) and ending with "Long Live CC-ism" (CC주의 만세). Recipients, with strange feelings, repeatedly exclaimed, "Ah, Konghwa Sinnnyŏn!" "Ah, Long Live CC-ism!" "The CC Party!" "Hey, look at this!" wondering anxiously who sent it and from where. And the police authorities, with wide eyes, actively worked to catch the culprits. Also on that day, at the house of Yi Sŏkku (李鍚九) in Changgyo-dong (長橋町), Kyŏngsŏng, a certain group armed with pistols demanded funds, causing a major commotion.

From the 1st onwards, the atmosphere in Kyŏngsŏng, already showing unusual signs, became increasingly strange day by day, with unprecedented incidents occurring frequently, making people uneasy.

January 4th. As is customary each year, various prominent figures of Kyŏngsŏng held a joint fellowship meeting at the Kuktonggwan (國一舘) restaurant. About 80 people, including religious leaders, educators, and businessmen, gathered and opened the meeting. Just as they were about to get comfortable, six or seven young men suddenly rushed in and caused a major disruption. "We don't know the purpose of your New Year fellowship meeting, but there are venues like the youth hall or the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall. Why must you hold it in a restaurant where prostitutes and profligates gather? Also, among you, there may be some with respectable status, but there seem to be so-called educators as well. You suspend us if we fail to pay even one month's tuition fee (月謝金) on time, so where do you get the money to gather in a restaurant like this?" they shouted loudly, fists gesturing, demanding, "Disperse!" Then, somehow alerted, police officers rushed in and stopped the young men. Thus, the fellowship meeting ended abortively amidst the turmoil, and those few young men were taken to the Chongno Police Station (鍾路署).

Once this incident became known, people shook their heads and murmured various things: "Ah, scary..." "Can't even go to restaurants freely anymore..." "Is this deterioration or improvement?" "Are they the propertyless (無産者) or poor students (苦學生)?" and so on.

A few days later, the Literary Society (文人會) also held its New Year meeting at Kuktonggwan. They thought, "Surely such a thing won't happen at our meeting?" "But who knows?" "Anyway, we are also propertyless," they mused.<64> Gathering their last bit of courage, half-fearful and half-anxious, they held the meeting. As drinks went back and forth, cup after cup, and just as the table was getting lively, two young men suddenly entered and shouted, "You bastards, is this a Rose Village (薔薇村) or a Utopia (이상향)? We haven't even had a single meal..." and threw a plate of chapchae. Then, apparently deciding to flee quickly, they bolted. In the end, one of them was caught by a member of the Literary Society and reportedly received quite a beating.

Thus, the confrontations centered around restaurants, pitting the propertyless against the propertied—or perhaps the porridge faction (죽먹파) against the rice faction (입파)—occurred three or four times in total. (People from Sinsaenghwalsa (新生活社) also experienced it once.) As the social atmosphere became like this, discussions and debates erupted everywhere in the city. Eventually, the Society for Helping Poor Students (苦學生같돕회), which was under the most suspicion, had 8 or 9 students arrested and their homes searched, leading to daily commotion between civilians and the police.

As the city's atmosphere grew increasingly tense day by day, alarming reports kept coming in about the infiltration of certain groups or the arrival of certain factions in the capital. Consequently, the police, plagued by headaches, worked tirelessly day and night, investigating in all directions.

Amidst this, on the 8th, the trial for the Sinsaenghwal Incident (新生活事件) was held, resulting in demands for sentences of 3 or 2 years, and simultaneously, *Sinsaenghwal* magazine was banned, causing an uproar in the publishing world and further unsettling the general intellectual sphere. As things tightened daily like this, the 12th arrived. On this day, there happened to be two major lecture meetings. The Chasakhoe (自作會) held one at the Chongno Youth Hall (鍾路靑年會舘), and the Seoul Youth Association (서울청년회) held one at the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall (천도교당). With two major lectures occurring on the same day at the same time, the crowds hesitated, unsure whether to go to the Youth Hall or the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall. However, among the speakers for the Seoul Youth Association was the controversial Miss Kwŏn Aera (權愛羅), who was scheduled to speak on the controversial topic of free love. Drawn by curiosity, the crowd packed the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall to bursting.

Under the title "The Cry of the Proletariat" (無産者의 절규), speaker Mr. Chang Ch'aegŭk (張彩極) was about to discuss the evils of the private property system when the police officers present ordered him to stop. The crowd erupted, shouting, "Where is the reason for this baseless interruption?" "Explain the reason! (理由ヲ說レ理由 - Riyū o setsu[mei] seyo?)", causing a huge commotion. Despite the uproar, the police remained unmoved. However, they must have been somewhat afraid, as they had stationed 7 or 8 uniformed policemen in ambush at the back of the hall.

Next, the controversial Miss Kwŏn Aera took the stage. The crowd erupted again with shouts of "Ugh!"<65> There were demands for her to sing "Nanbongga" (난봉가), calls for her to speak later, demands to hear the reason for the interruption before discussing free love, and all sorts of harsh remarks were hurled. Notably, the Kim Mirisa (金美利史) faction demanded the right to speak before Miss Kwŏn, leading to fists flying everywhere, feet stomping here and there, turning the place into utter chaos.

Thus, as one newspaper put it, "Love may be free, but speech is unfree," and after a long period of fighting, the lecture meeting dispersed amidst the turmoil.

M hyŏng. This was the first time I had ever witnessed such a peculiar scene at a lecture meeting. The crowd's jeering was expected, but the physical fights among the crowd itself, Miss Kwŏn's free love topic being a new phenomenon, and the uproar from the women's side were also record-breaking. It was truly a spectacle. If only you could have seen it, hyŏngnim... And

M hyŏng. The current mood of our people has indeed become astonishingly sharp and biting. The lethargic disposition, where one wouldn't even flinch if poked hard, has vanished somewhere, replaced by a boiling fervor amidst a mood of angry waves (怒濤). And the courage to simply smash through and advance according to one's own assertions, without any room for talk of morality, order, or forgiveness, truly confirms the spirit of the living. Speaking recklessly, might one call it deterioration (惡化)? Indeed, given our situation of suffering upon suffering, both internally and externally, it's inevitable that extreme thoughts and extreme actions are exposed.

M hyŏng. It was this day. Precisely this day, around 8:10 PM. While the Youth Hall was abuzz with talk of self-sufficiency (自作), and the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall was chaotic with fistfights over the cry of the proletariat and free love, creating a very tense atmosphere, an explosion suddenly occurred at the Chongno Police Station—something entirely unexpected—shaking the whole of Kyŏngsŏng.

It was the very next day. Bells rang continuously in the city, and newspaper extras spread everywhere. "Bomb Thrown at Chongno Station Last Night at 8 o'clock!" "7 Passersby Seriously or Lightly Injured!" "Chongno Station Suffers Damage to 2 Window Panes!!" Such alarming news, printed in large type (初號活字), flew around.

M hyŏng. What must the mood in the city have been like? One can easily imagine just by guessing. Through word of mouth (口口相傳), even infants and the elderly (乳兒病老) were buzzing, "Ah, an explosion!" "Ah, at Chongno Station!" causing a stir everywhere.

Consequently, the police authorities worked frantically to arrest the culprit, while the public watched thirstily to see how things would unfold. It was as if martial law had been declared; search parties lined the streets and alleys like ropes, and by 8 PM, the Chongno thoroughfare became deserted. Anyone slightly suspicious was subjected to body searches 4 or 5 times a night.<66> Thus, amid fear, astonishment, and anticipation, one dreary day passed after another. Then, in the early morning of the 17th, a major incident occurred where a policeman was shot dead on a street in the city (시내 00通), greatly shaking the capital once again. Newspaper extras circulated repeatedly: "Chongno Station Detective Tamura Killed!" "Inspector Imase Seriously Injured!" "Assistant Inspector Umeda Lightly Injured!" "Culprit Escaped!" – such alarming reports spread rapidly.

Now, the capital was truly enveloped in fear. The tense atmosphere in the city would not ease even slightly until the culprit was apprehended. The Chongno Station, having been hit once, bore a dark and mournful appearance (愴然); patrolmen stood guard around it, while the body of the martyred policeman reportedly lay horizontally in the upstairs meeting room. It was said that a full mobilization had occurred.

M hyŏng. Earlier, I mentioned there was talk of a certain criminal being shot dead somewhere in the city. Indeed. As soon as the bells rang, newspaper extras came flying.

M hyŏng. Don't be alarmed. The culprit of the policeman's shooting on the 17th, that audacious and valiant culprit, after a long firefight from 2:30 AM to 7:30 AM today, the 22nd, in Hyojedong (孝悌洞) number 73 (spanning 3 houses), fiercely fought with the search party and was finally shot dead by police bullets. Whether it was a wicked death (惡死) or a good death (善死) is a separate issue, but he was indeed a person of extraordinary valor and audacity. After committing the act on the 17th, he immediately crossed Namsan (南山) and hid in the vicinity of Wangsimni (往十里), eventually taking refuge in Hyojedong. In the dead of night (夜 三更時), armed with two pistols, he fought single-handedly against about 80 policemen across three houses, dodging, resisting, and fighting fiercely like a roaring tiger, until finally, cornered (勢窮), his spirit flew away at the muzzle of a gun. Truly a rare hero (稀罕의 勇者). It's said he died clutching a pistol tightly in each hand, fingers still on the triggers until the very last moment. During this great drama, Inspector Kurita (栗田) of the Tongdaemun Police Station (東大門署) was seriously injured, and an elderly neighbor, Mr. XXX, was also seriously injured. The culprit's name, address, and background are still under a publication ban. His body has been handed over to his family.

M hyŏng. My heart is pounding. My whole body is trembling. "Why does the world allow such ruthless things to happen? Ah, God (한우님)!" I throw down my brush. I simply don't have the courage to write anymore.

M hyŏng. Although I've put down my brush, there are still a few more things I must write. That event is that event, but why should the matters between us brothers be sacrificed?

M hyŏng. Another major event has occurred in Seoul, namely the issue concerning the eligibility of graduates from various higher common schools (고등보통학교) for admission to upper-level schools. When the new education ordinance was announced last year,<67> it was clearly stated that graduates of Chosŏn's higher common schools would have confirmed eligibility (確有) for admission to various high schools and normal schools in Japan. Despite this, as the actual academic semester approaches this spring (今春), the authorities announced that this is still undecided (미확정). Consequently, students from various higher common schools became greatly infuriated, decrying the deceitful education, untrustworthy education, manipulative education. They submitted petitions to the school authorities and strongly insisted that if a satisfactory response is not received within a reasonable period, they will resolutely stage a collective withdrawal from school (동맹퇴학). At this, not only the school authorities but also the Government-General authorities were wide-eyed. They rushed negotiators to the Ministry of Education (文部省) on one hand, while attempting appeasement tactics (慰撫之策) on the students on the other, reaching the peak of a major crisis. How this incident will conclude remains uncertain. Although a notification from the Ministry of Education has arrived (來하얏스나), it is reportedly still vague (要領不得), and the student community remains prepared to follow through with their initial resolution (初志貫徹). And

M hyŏng. It might not seem like a major phenomenon, but in fact, there is something else that could be called a major phenomenon. It's the so-called *kenmai pan* (ケンマイパン - brown rice bread/buns). Until the year before last, the "Kalsudop mandu" (갈돕만두) made by poor students (苦學生) were quite popular in Kyŏngsŏng city, but for some reason, they have vanished without a trace. Now, this *kenmai pan* has become quite fashionable. On every street and alley, the cries of "*Kenmai pan*" are so loud my ears hurt unbearably. It's bread made by Japanese, but it's popular throughout the Korean market. It's staggering to think how much money is spent on it daily. This is one new phenomenon. There is another new phenomenon, and this one is truly a profoundly significant new phenomenon arising from our self-awareness and demands. That is, the popularity of cotton turumagi (무명 周衣 - cotton outer robes?). Besides the Chasakhoe, the Society for the Promotion of Korean Products (朝鮮物産獎勵會) has also been formed, but anyway, cotton turumagi are becoming very popular. It's changing so rapidly to cotton clothes that those wearing Western suits or silk clothes feel ashamed to go out.

M hyŏng. It's a good phenomenon, isn't it? Is it the same in the countryside? And hyŏngnim, what is the price of rice in your area? In Seoul, white rice (백미) is usually 12 wŏn (up to 13 wŏn) per kama (sack). And a bag of coarse rice (小米) is typically 10 wŏn, while red beans (赤豆) are 2 wŏn 50 chŏn per tu (斗 - measure). Thus, rice prices are significantly cheaper compared to previous years, but other goods remain stubbornly expensive.

M hyŏng. It seems I've gone on too long, so I'll cut it short. I'll leave the rest for later and just wish for your good health, hyŏngnim.<68>

**Summary**

This article, written as a letter in January 1923 by "Ch'unp'a" in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) to his brother "M" in the countryside, vividly portrays a tense and eventful month in the colonial capital. It details social unrest, including youth protests against elite gatherings in restaurants, the circulation of mysterious "CC Party" New Year cards, and the trial related to the banned *Sinsaenghwal* magazine. The atmosphere intensifies with a chaotic lecture meeting featuring Kwŏn Aera discussing free love, followed by the bombing of the Chongno Police Station, a harsh police crackdown, the killing of a detective, and the eventual dramatic shootout and death of the suspected bomber Kim Sang-ok (though unnamed due to reporting restrictions at the time). The letter also touches upon student protests against unclear university admission policies and notes emerging social trends like the popularity of Japanese-made *kenmai pan* and, significantly, the growing preference for Korean-made cotton clothing (turumagi) spurred by self-sufficiency movements. For historians, this piece offers a rich, contemporary ground-level view of the volatile atmosphere in 1920s colonial Seoul, capturing the blend of political tension (nationalist/socialist undercurrents), social conflict (youth vs. elites, generational clashes), colonial oppression (police actions, censorship), economic conditions (rice prices vs. goods), and burgeoning cultural nationalism (promoting Korean products). The detailed accounts of specific incidents like the restaurant disruptions, the Kwŏn Aera lecture chaos, and the Chongno bombing aftermath provide valuable insights into the anxieties, resistance, and daily realities of Koreans under Japanese rule.

M형. 형님 게신 곳도 비가 왓섯는지요? 서울은 어제ㅅ 밤부터 비가 오기 시작하야 어제 종일 왓슴니다. 오늘도 날은 개지 안엇슴니다. 길이 질퍽질퍽하고 落水ㅅ물이 주룩주룩함은 꼭 7월 장마때 갓고 눈 녹인 물이 졸졸하고 어름이 푹석푹석 풀림은 확실히 봄날 갓슴니다. 例에 업는 바는 아니지만 大寒 시절에 이처럼 비가 만히 옴은 처음 보는 바임니다. 그리고 일기는 엇지나 사나운지 黃霧가 꽉 내리눌너 정신차릴 수 업슴니다. 가래(楸)탕 먹은 고기 모양으로 모다 머리를 휘휘 내두루며 惡氣만 토할 뿐이외다.
M형. 안으로 밧그로 쓸이고 압흔 살림을 하는 우리의 처지로서는 싀골 게신 이나 서울 잇는 사람이나 그ㅡ지나는 바가 一樣이겟지요? 별로 자랑할 것도 업고 별로 표방할 것도 업겟지요. 정초이니까 혹ㅡ엇더한 자랑거리가 잇는 지도 몰으나 나 뿐은 아모 무어라 할 것이 업슴니다. 그ㅡ미즈 그러하고 흐리럽럽한 글을 그대로 꼬부작 꼬부작할 뿐이외다. 아모 뼈가 업고 씨가 드지 안흔 그ㅡ두루뭉수리 생각을 발표하랴니까 가슴이 조마조마하고 손끗치 자릿자릿하야 그만 붓대를 휙ㅡ내여던지고 십흔 때가 업지 못함니다. 그리고 형님과 가티 시골로 내려가 綠水 靑山에서 心神의 위안이나 밧고 自耕自耘에서 육체의 보장이나 엇고 십흔 생각´ 하루도 몃번인지 몰으겟슴니다.＜62＞
M형. 이것이 패배자의 수작일넌지요. 만약 그럿타ㅡ하면 누구누구와 가티 北으로 한거름 더ㅡ놉히 뛰여 총을 메고 釰을 두루며 압흐로 破壤 뒤로 건설 두루 덤벙거리는 산애가 될까요? 생각이 업는 바는 아니지만 實地 업는 수작은 한 虛風旋이 즛이니까 다만 내가 알켜 들이랴든 그 일만 소개하겟슴니다.
M형. 시골 게셔서는 서울 소식이 알고 십고 서울 잇서서는 시골 소식이 그리운 것은 兩兩常情이지요. 아닌게 아니라 나는 시골 소식이 퍽 그립소이다. 다ㅡ들 엇더케 지나시는지 아지 못하야 궁금하외다. 혹ㅡ굼지나 아니 하시나 혹ㅡ떠나시지나 안으시나 엇던 놈에게 모욕이나 아니 당하시나! 하는 비겁적 동정이 만히 생김니다. 그리고 엇던 소식통으로부터 禁酒斷煙問題, 自作自給問題, 小作人對地主問題 가튼 現下 吾族의 최절실한 生의 운동이 만히 일어남을 들을 때는 아ㅡ우리에게도 새로운 운동이 일어나는구나! 아ㅡ우리에게도 自活의 途가 열리는구나 하고 무한히 깁뻐 날뛴 젹도 업지 못함니다.
M형. 내가 시골 게신 이의 소식을 위하야 이리 함과 가티 형님은 서울 소식을 위하야 그처럼 눈이 감기도록 안탁갑게 기다리시지요? 내ㅡ다른 무엇은 洞察치 못하나 그 사정은 이윽히 아옵니다. 이에 시골 게신 이들을 위하야 계해 정초에 대한 서울의 景氣를 대략이나마 소개하려 함니다. 혹ㅡ신문지나 傳說로도 들엇섯겟지만...
M형. 계해년 정초는 例에 업시 別로 多事한 듯 하외다. 일년의 計가 총집한 1월이니까 또는 六甲이 다 지나고 새로 上甲을 마즈려는 계해년이니까 사회 각 방면이 多事할 것은 사실임니다. 그래 그런지 저래 그런지 別로 多事하외다. 널리 세계는 그만두고 우리 조선은 확실히 多事함니다. 上海에는 국민대표회의가 열리고 滿洲에는 독립군의 통일운동이 일어나고 조선 내지에는 농민운동 국산운동이 일어나고 각 개인 머리 속에는 무슨 주의 무슨 사상 즉 공산이냐 사회냐 독립이냐 실력이냐 등 別樣別種의 생각이 엉크러저 안정 또 자위할 수 업는 기분 중에 무슨 動械만 생기면 猪進的 행동이 생길 듯 아조 大事將起의 秋에 당한 듯 하외다.
M형. 이것은 별문제임니다. 내가 말하랴든 데서 확대가 된 듯 함니다. 단도직입으로 京城 記事에 쑥 들어가고 말겟슴니다.
M형. 1월 1일이외다. 새해 첫날이니까 사회 각 방면이 무쌍 분주하얏슴니다. 세배를 하느니 屠蘇酒를 먹느니＜63＞ 연하장을 밧느니 두루 야단들인데 그날은 날이 엇지나 치운지 바람이 엇지나 지독한지 사람마다 코ㅅ끗헤 어름을 달고 바람에 삐친 발랑갑이 모양으로 대굴대굴 굴러단녓슴니다. 국수 사오든 소녀는 대문 간에서 울고 섯고 세배가든 소년은 문을 열다가 주저 안젓슴니다. 이러케 近年에 듬은 혹한의 1일인데 그래도 집집에는 연하장 밧기에 분주하얏슴니다. 한 가지 例에 업는 것은 연하장 중에 『共化新年』이라 題하고 『CC주의 만세』를 尾書한 CC당의 연하장이엿슴니다. 밧는 이마다 이상한 기분으로써 『아 共化新年』 『아 CC주의 만세』 『CC당』 『야ㅡ이것 보아!』하고 몃번식 겁허 외이며 어대셔 누가 하얏는지 몰나 궁금하게 지내엿슴니다. 그리고 경찰측에서는 눈이 휘등그래서 범인을 잡으려고 크게 활동을 하엿슴니다. 그리고 그날 京城 市內 長橋町 李鍚九의 집에는 권총 가진 某團이 군자금을 청구한 事가 잇서서 큰 소동이 잇섯슴니다.
1일부터 이러케 別 景氣를 띄인 京城 市內는 날이 갈사록 차츰 別에 別字를 加하야 例에 업는 일이 작구 발생되여 인심이 뒤숭숭해졋슴니다.
1월 4일이외다. 例年의 事로 京城 市內 각 사회유지들이 시내 國一舘이란 요리점에서 聯合懇親會를 개최하엿슴니다. 종교가, 교육가, 실업가 두루 80여 명이나 모여 會를 開하고 장차 간담이 나오려 할 때에 엇던 청년 6, 7명이 갑작이 달려들어 대풍파를 야기하얏슴니다. 『당신』들의 新年懇親會의 목적이 那邊에 在함은 모르나 會場으로써는 청년회관도 잇고 천도교당도 잇는데 하필 淫女蕩子가 모야 노는 요리집에 열 것이 무엇이며 또 여러분 중에는 상당한 신분이 잇는 이도 잇겟지만 소위 교육가라 하는 자도 잇는 듯함니다. 우리는 月謝金 한달치만 기일 내에 못 내이면 정학을 시키면서 당신들은 무슨 돈이 잇서서 이러케 요리집에 모혓느냐』 손길이 들먹하며 『해산하라』고 大呼를 하얏슴니다. 그러자 엇더케 알엇는지 경관이 달려들어 그 청년들을 저지하얏슴니다. 이러케 되야 懇親會는 풍파 중에 流産되고 그 청년 몃 사람은 鍾路署에 引致되얏슴니다.
이 사실이 한번 세상에 전하게 되매 사람사람들은 해를 내여두루며 『아ㅡ무서워...』 『요리집에도 이제는 마음대로 못 가겟다....』 『이것이 惡化냐 善化냐?』 『無産者냐 苦學生이냐』 등 별별 소리가 만하 젓슴니다.
그리한 지 몃츨 뒤에 文人會에서 또 신년회를 國一舘에서 열엿슴니다. 『우리 會에는 그러한 일이 업겟지? 『그러나 알 수 잇나』 『엇제든 우리도 無産者들이니까』하야＜64＞ 半懼半徨하면서 최후의 勇力으로 會를 열고 酒盃가 왓다갓다 하야 一盃復一盃로 장차 요리상이 뻔뻔할 즘에 갑작이 두 청년이 쑥 들어서서 『이 자식들아 이것이 薔薇村이냐 이상향이냐 우리는 밥 한끼도 못 먹은 처지인데...』 하고 잡채 접시를 메다 쳣습니다. 그리고는 귀떨어지면 다음 줍자 하고 走字를 대엿다 합니다. 결국ㅡ한 사람은 문인 회원의 손에 붓잡혀 뼘개나 되게 어더 마졋다 함니다.
이리하야 요리점을 중심한 無産 대 有産 아니 죽먹파 대 입파의 풍파는 凡 3, 4차엿습니다.(新生活社便의 사람도 한 번 당함) 사회의 공기가 이러케 됨에 시내 도처에는 왈가왈부의 평이 왁자해졋습니다. 급기야에는 가장 혐의를 밧든 苦學生같돕회에서는 8, 9명 학생이 被捉이 되며 또 가택수색을 당하며 하야 민간 대 경관 간에는 날마다 부산스럽게 지내엿습니다.
이러케 시내의 공기는 日益緊張되야 가는 판인데 무슨 團 잠입이니 무슨 派 입경이니 무서운 경보가 작구 전합니다. 그래서 경찰계는 두통이 나리만치 식끄럽게 주야를 繼하야 각 방면으로 활동을 하얏습니다.
그리하든 차인데 8일에는 新生活事件의 공판이 열리어 3년 혹 2년의 刑의 요구가 잇스며 동시에 신생활 발행금지를 당하며 하야 언론계가 와그르 떠들며 일반 사상계가 更 일층 동요가 되얏습니다. 이러케 이러케 날마다 죄여오는 판인데 12일을 당하엿습니다. 이날은 마츰 2대 강연회가 잇섯습니다. 自作會에서는 鍾路靑年會舘에 서울청년회에서는 천도교당에 同日 同時에 2대 강연이 有하야 청년회로 갈가 천도교당으로 갈가 군중은 발길이 갈팡질팡하얏습니다. 그런데 서울청년회의 演士 중에는 문제의 權愛羅 양이 문제의 연애자유를 말하게 되엿습니다. 호기심에 끌닌 군중은 천도교당이 터져라고 드리밀녓습니다.
『無産者의 절규』 라는 題下에서 演士 張彩極군이 사유재산제도의 폐해를 말하려 하는 차에 臨席하얏든 경관은 중지를 시켯습니다. 『羣衆은 이유업는 중지가 어대 잇느냐』고 『理由ヲ說レ理由』하고 큰 야단이 낫습니다. 야단을 수업시 하나 경관측은 꼼작도 아니 하얏습니다. 그래도 무섭기는 확실이 무서웟는지 正服 巡査가 7, 8명을 會場 뒤에 매복을 시켯섯습니다.
이번은 문제의 權愛羅 양이 단상에 나섯습니다. 羣衆 측에서는 「으악」하고 또 야단이엿습니다. 難捧歌를 하라느니 잇다가 나오라느니 연애자유 전에 중지이유를＜65＞ 듯는다느니 별별 險口가 막 쏘다 졋슴니다. 그 중에 특히 金美利史一派는 권양이 말하기 전 자기네에게 言權을 달나하야 주먹이 사방에서 불근거리며 발길이 여긔저긔셔 꽝꽝거리며 일대 난장판이 되얏습니다.
이리하야 모 신문의 말과 가티 『연애는 자유나 말은 부자유』가 되야 장시간 싸움만 하다가 강연회는 풍파 중에 散會하고 마럿습니다.
M형. 나는 강연 유사이래 처음으로 이러한 別景氣를 보앗습니다. 羣衆의 야지는 母論이거니와 군중끼리의 격투까지 잇섯고 권양의 연애자유도, 新현상이거니와 여자측의 대야단도 신기록이엿슴니다. 참 장관이엿슴니다. 형님이 좀 보섯드면...그리고
M형. 現下 우리 민족의 기분은 과연 놀낼 만하게 맵고 쌀쌀하게 되얏슴니다. 누가 꾹 찔너도 움즉하지도 아니하리 만치 늘큰하든 기분은 어대로 다라낫는지 그만 怒濤의 기분 중에서 펄펄 뛰게 되얏슴니다. 그리고 도덕이니 질서니 또는 용서니 그 따윗 말은 할 여지도 업시 그냥 自家의 주장대로 막 부셔대고 나가는 용기는 과연 生者의 기분을 확증합듸다. 함부로 말하면 惡化라고 할넌지요? 안인게 아니라 안으로 밧그로 고통 又 고통을 당하는 우리의 처지니까 극단의 생각과 극단의 행동이 폭로될 밧게 업는 事勢이지요.
