The Situation of Compatriots in North Manchuria
						Pak Saeng from Kirin, September 12

The situation of our compatriots in North Manchuria is truly behind a dark curtain (黑幕). Recently, there seems to be some chatter about culture, industry, and education, and phenomena such as alliances aimed at cultural movements are forming here and there; it appears to be a degree of self-awareness. Also, following the breakdown of the National Representatives Conference (國代會), under the unavoidable responsibility for the extreme disappointment felt both internally and externally, efforts are underway to first unify South and North Manchuria (南北滿) for the sake of overseas unity. It seems likely that the leaders (主腦者) of various organizations will meet before long (不遠間) to discuss some sort of plan (方針). However, as our society has too many layers/divisions (層折), there are indeed doubts as to whether it might not just become a second Representatives Conference. Nevertheless, depending on the opportunity, it also seems likely to produce a second something that is not so meaningless.

Hyŏng [Elder brother/friend], would you consider coming to see Manchuria sometime? There is a great deal of work to be done. We only lament the lack of capable people (인물). If there is no aid (援助) whatsoever from the homeland (內地), the future (前途) of the compatriots residing in Manchuria (在滿同胞) holds nothing but bitter tears (痛哭).

Editor's Note: Brother Pak, I apologize for publishing this part (一幕) of your private letter (私信).<97>

**Summary**

This article is a personal letter from Pak Saeng in Kirin (North Manchuria) describing the situation of Korean immigrants there in 1923. He notes fledgling cultural and organizational activities but highlights the deep disappointment following the failure of the National Representatives Conference (Kuktaehoe). Efforts towards unifying Koreans in South and North Manchuria are ongoing, though skepticism remains due to internal divisions. Pak Saeng laments the lack of capable individuals and resources, stressing the community's dependence on aid from Korea (Naeji) for its survival and future prospects. For historians, the text offers a glimpse into the political fragmentation, aspirations for unity, emerging cultural consciousness, and sense of vulnerability among the Korean diaspora in Manchuria during the early colonial period, shortly after a significant but failed attempt at political consolidation (the Kuktaehoe). The reliance on 'Naeji' and the mention of specific political efforts provide valuable context for understanding diaspora politics and identity formation.

北滿同胞의 情形
						九, 一二 吉林에서 朴生
北滿同胞의 情形 이는 넘우 黑幕임니다. 근일 와서 비롯오 문화니 실업이니 교육이니 하고 떠드는 모양인데 여긔저긔서 문화운동을 目的한 단결들이 성립될 현상, 多少의 自覺인 듯 십흡니다. 그리고 國代會가 破裂된 후 내외의 인심이 극히 실망임을 면키 불능한 責任下에 해외통일 爲先 南北滿의 통일을 위하야 역시 奔走하는 판임니다. 아마 不遠間 각 단체의 主腦者들의 會合이 되야 何等 方針을 협의케 될 듯 하외다. 그러나 우리사회는 넘우 層折이 만으닛가 또한 第二 代表會나 안닐가 하야 의문도 업지는 안음니다. 그러나 기회에 따라서 그리 무의미한 것이 아닌 第二의 무엇을 산출할 듯도 십슴니다.
형은 한번 滿洲구경 아니 오시렵닛가. 일꺼리 매우 만음니다. 오직 인물이 업슴을 한탄할 뿐임니다. 만일 內地로부터 何等 원조가 업다하면 在滿동포의 前途는 한갓 痛哭할 외에 아모것도 업슴니다.
編者曰 朴兄이여 이러케 私信의 一幕을 공개하여서 미안함니다.＜97＞
