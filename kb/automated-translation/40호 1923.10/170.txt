Renewal of the Labor Congress (Reportedly Planning to Hold a Labor Federation Convention)

The Labor Congress (勞動大會) was the very first labor organization established in Chosŏn, a historic congress that once had numerous members and over thirty branches. It is true that during the four or five years that have passed, there were times when it merely maintained its signboard without any particular achievements, and it is also true that reforms involving insincere executives occurred several times. However, on the other hand, it is also true that various efforts were made, such as organizing various craft unions (職工組合), creating free laborers' unions (自由勞動者組合), sweeping away those self-unaware idlers (遊民) who sought to use labor organizations for movements seeking fame and profit (名利運動), and attempting various resistance movements against the exploiting class (搾取階級). Recently, it is said that the same association has revised its rules, newly selected executives, and is ardently campaigning to make its activities even more solid, especially by deciding to hold an All-Korea Labor Federation Convention (全鮮勞動團大會). Now, for reference, the newly appointed executive committee members of the said association are shown as follows:
Kim Wan'gyu, Kim Sŏnggyu (Standing), Yi Kyohong, Yi Pyŏng'ŭi (Standing), O Sangjun, Kim Hyŏnje, Ku Yŏnsuk, An Chong'yŏng (Standing), Sŏ Chŏnggi (Standing), Sŏ Mansŏk, Ch'oe Chinman, Kang U (Standing).<105>

**Summary**

This news brief reports on the revitalization efforts of the Labor Congress (노동대회), described as Korea's first major labor organization. Despite periods of inactivity and internal issues with leadership ("insincere executives"), the article notes its past efforts in organizing craft and free laborers' unions and resisting exploitation. The organization is now reforming its rules, appointing new leadership (listing twelve names, five designated as 'Standing'), and planning to hold an All-Korea Labor Federation Convention to strengthen its activities. For historians, this article offers a glimpse into the early, complex development of the organized labor movement in colonial Korea, highlighting both its initial significance, internal struggles ("insincere executives," "idlers seeking fame and profit"), efforts at consolidation (All-Korea Convention), and its explicit framing of activities as resistance against the "exploiting class." The list of new executives provides concrete names associated with this movement in 1923.

노동대회의 更新 (勞動團大會를 開催하리라고)
勞動大會는 朝鮮에서 맨 처음으로 생겻던 노동단체로서 다수의 회원과 三十餘의 支會를 두엇던 한 역사잇는 대회이엿섯다. 四五星霜을 經來하는 동안에 특별한 실적이 업시 간판만 유지한 때가 잇던 것도 사실이오 不誠意의 幹部改革이 數次에 至함도 사실이엿다. 그러나 또 한 면에는 各 職工組合을 조직하며 又는 自由勞動者組合을 만들어 自覺업는 遊民이 名利運動으로 노동단체를 이용하고저 하는 자를 掃蕩하며 搾取階級에 대한 반항운동을 試한 여러 가지의 노력이 잇섯던 것도 사실이다. 최근에 至하야 同會에서는 회칙을 改하며 간부를 新選하야 운동을 一層 堅實히 하되 특히 全鮮勞動團大會를 개최하기로 하야 열렬히 운동하는 중이라 한다. 이제 참고로써 同會 신임 집행위원을 示하면
金完圭, 金成奎(常任), 李敎鴻, 李丙儀(常任), 吳尙俊, 金賢濟, 具然淑, 安鍾英(常任), 徐廷基(常任), 徐萬石, 崔晋萬, 姜禹(常任).＜105＞
