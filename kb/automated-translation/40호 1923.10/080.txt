Magazine Title: 개벽 제40호
Publication Date: 1923년 10월 01일
Article Title: 間島 (Gando)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

It is said that the number of our brethren within North Kando (北間島), which encompasses the four counties of Yŏn'gil (延吉), Wangch'ŏng (旺淸), Honch'un (渾春), and Hwaryong (和龍) in China's Jilin Province (吉林省), amounts to over five hundred thousand.

Among these areas, Yongjŏng City (龍井市), which has the nickname "the Seoul of North Kando," has over fifteen thousand brethren residing there, T'udu-gu (頭道溝) has over four thousand, and Kukcha-gae (局子街) has over two thousand brethren residing, giving the impression that a second Chosŏn (朝鮮) has been formed. The first thing I want to talk about regarding Kando is education. Setting aside (却說) the old stories about this for now, school education in Kando, which suffered a fatal blow along with Japan's (日本) military dispatch to Kando, has flourished again since the Kyŏngsin year (1920), reaching a prosperous state last year with over 150 elementary and middle schools within Kando. However, the actual financial power (金力) was often unable to support them afterwards, leading to the regrettable situation of schools being quickly established and quickly abolished (速成速廢). Nevertheless, among them, the schools Yŏngsin (永新), Tonghŭng (東興), Sajin (思眞), Taesŏng (大成), Myŏngsin (明新), Chungang (中央), Myŏngdong (明東), Kusan (邱山), and Tongyang (東洋) are all teaching subjects roughly equivalent to middle school level and achieving considerable results. Particularly, places like Tonghŭng Middle School (東興中學校) currently have over nine hundred students enrolled and six affiliated elementary schools, achieving a grand and prosperous state.

As you may know, regarding the Sino-Japanese joint venture Tumen Light Railway (圖們輕鐵) (approximately 270 ri in distance), stretching from Hoeryŏng (會寧) in Hamgyŏng Pukto (咸北) to Ch'ŏnbosan (天寶山) in China, the section between Hoeryŏng and Sangsambong (上三峯) via Kyŏngsŏng (鏡城) has already been opened, and the section from Sangsambong to Kando's Yongjŏng City will be opened from October 1st of this year, making transportation in Kando even more convenient. Consequently, the development of Kando will likely attract particular attention. Furthermore, in Yongjŏng City, there has long been an electric company managed by the Chinese side, and the supply of electricity is expected to be realized before long. Thus, the development of Kando compared to a year ago is truly worthy of special notice (刮目할). Don't be surprised: this year alone, the number of households added in Yongjŏng City has reached 1,600.

It's unpleasant to even mention, but at the very beginning (劈頭) of this year, one of our brethren residing here, Mr. Ch'oe Ch'angho (崔昌浩), was shot dead by Chinese officials (官憲), and last July, another one of our people was shot dead by a Japanese policeman. Regarding the former incident, residents' meetings were held and various countermeasures were discussed (講하엿스나), but no resolution was reached. Regarding the latter incident, nothing could be done at all. It was utterly impossible. Such is the fate of us who lack a political background. We can only look up at the heavens and weep (噓唏할 뿐이외다).<93>

**Summary**

This 1923 news report describes the growing Korean community in North Gando (Jiandao), China, numbering over 500,000, particularly in cities like Yongjŏng. It highlights the resurgence of Korean education with over 150 schools, despite financial struggles, mentioning prominent institutions like Tonghŭng Middle School. The article notes infrastructure development, like the upcoming Tumen Light Railway extension and potential electricity supply in Yongjŏng, contributing to rapid growth evidenced by 1,600 new households. However, it starkly contrasts this progress with the precarious situation of Koreans, recounting the recent killings of two Koreans, one by Chinese officials and another by a Japanese policeman, emphasizing the community's powerlessness due to lack of political backing. For historians, this article offers a valuable glimpse into the Korean diaspora during the colonial period, showcasing community building efforts (especially in education), the impact of Japanese infrastructure projects extending into Manchuria, and the vulnerability and dangers faced by Koreans living outside the peninsula under competing Chinese and Japanese authorities.

間島
中國 吉林省의 延吉, 旺淸, 渾春, 和龍의 四縣을 包括한 北間島내의 우리형제는 其 數-五十萬이상을 算한다 함니다.
中에도 北間島의 셔울이란 稱이 잇는 龍井市에는 약 一萬五千이상의 형제가 居留하며, 頭道溝에는 四千이상, 局子街에는 二千이상의 형제가 거주하야 바로 第二의 朝鮮을 형성한 감이 업지 안음니다. 間島에 잇서 제일몬저 이약이하고 십흔 것은 교육에 대한 일이외다. 이에 대한 녓 니약이에는 이제 却說로 말할 것도 업고, 日本의 間島出兵과 共히 일대 치명상을 당햇던 間島의 학교교육은, 庚辛年 以降으로 다시 蔚興하야, 작년쯤에는 間島내의 小中學校가 百五十餘個所에 달하는 盛況을 呈햇슴니다. 그러나 역시 실제의 金力이 만히 그 후를 支撑지 못하야, 速成速廢의 유감이 업지 못하나, 그러한 중에서도 永新, 東興, 思眞, 大成, 明新, 中央, 明東, 邱山, 東洋의 諸학교는, 다 各히 중등정도의 학과를 敎授하야, 상당한 성적을 거두며 잇슴니다. 특히 東興中學校와 가튼 곳은 현재 受業生이 九百餘名에 부속소학교가 六個所에 達하야 宏壯한 盛況을 일우엇슴니다.
여러분도 아시려니와, 咸北 會寧으로부터 中國의 天寶山까지에 니르는, 中日合營의 圖們輕鐵(약 二百七十里의 間)은, 會寧 至鏡城 上三峯의 間은 벌서 관통되엿고, 上三峯으로부터 間圖 龍井市까지의 分은 今十月 一日부터 관통되는 바, 間島의 교통은 일층 便宜하게 됨니다. 이에 딸라 間島의 開拓이 별로 사람의 주의를 惹起할 것이외다. 그러니 말이지 龍井市에는, 일즉히 中國人側 經營의 電氣회사가 잇는바, 電氣의 공급도 멀지안하 실현될 것이외다. 이럭저럭하야 間島의 발전은 일년전에 비하야 실로 刮目할 가치가 잇는바, 놀나지 마시오. 금년 중 龍井市에만 增築한 家戶가 一千六百戶에 達햇슴니다.
말하기도 실습니다마는. 今年 劈頭에는 이 곳에 在留하는 우리 형제 중, 崔昌浩씨가 中國人 官憲에게 총살되고, 지난 七月中에는 우리사람 중 또 한 분이 日本人巡査에게 총살되엿슴니다. 前者에 잇서는 주민대회를 열고 여러 가지로 대책을 講하엿스나 何等의 要領을 못엇고 말엇스며, 後者에 잇서는 이도 저도 못햇슴니다. 도저히 할 수가 업섯슴니다. 정치적 배경을 가지지 못한 우리일은 이러함니다. 다못 한울을 울얼어 噓唏할 뿐이외다.＜93＞
