餘墨
△ The world of the past month of September was truly a troublesome world. Needless to say how the earthquake that occurred in Japan (日本) at the beginning of the month made the Japanese people, or the Korean (朝鮮) people, tremble, but the various events such as revolutionary movements and armed conflicts that arose in Europe (歐洲) also stirred the nerves of the world's people. Firstly, regarding the incident where the Italian (伊太利) border commissioner Tellini (치리니) was assassinated in Greece (希臘), it is fortunate that the conflict between Italy and Greece ultimately did not escalate into a major affair due to Greece's apology. However, in Romania (羅馬尼), a plan to establish a Romanian Communist government on September 17th was exposed, leading to dreadful clashes between officials and civilians, and it is unknown when it will end. In Spain (西班牙), the former Minister of War, General Primo de Rivera (리버라), initiated a great upheaval, overthrew the current government, and became Prime Minister himself, emulating Mussolini's (무쏘리) style in Italy (第二伊太利), which is also a spectacle. On the 21st, riots suddenly broke out in Bulgaria (勃牙利) in the Balkans (巴彌幹), and while the Communist Party and the Agrarian Party are in communication, Serbia (塞爾比) is concentrating troops on the Bulgarian border, making the situation in the Balkans urgent. Italy's Prime Minister Mussolini, famous for his stubbornness, has appointed a Military Governor to Fiume (휴매), designated as a free state by the Treaty of Rapallo (라파로條約), thus stirring up trouble on calm waters today. First of all, it is fortunate that the meeting between the British and French (英佛) Prime Ministers took place in Paris (巴里), and its result is said to be satisfactory. However, the internal turmoil in Germany (獨逸) is growing ever more severe, and it seems the majority of the populace desires a fundamental revolution to be carried out swiftly, even if just for a day.

◇ Since President Li's (黎總統) escape from Beijing (北京), the political world in China (中國) has reached an extreme state of disorder. Under the manipulation of the Zhili clique (直隷派), a Regency Cabinet (攝政內閣) was established, passing day by day. Amidst this, General Cao Kun's (曹錩將軍) movement to ascend to the presidency seems to have made significant progress recently. Accordingly, the conflict arising between Cao Kun and Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) will be something worth watching from now on.

◇ Turning back to take a glance at the situation within Korea (朝鮮), after the earthquake turmoil passed, the nerves of the Government-General (總督府) authorities became extremely agitated. While exercising the utmost authority in controlling speech, assembly, association, and other activities, Messrs. Park Soon-ho (朴淳鎬) and Yoo Byung-gi (兪炳璣) of *Sinch'ŏnji* (新天地) have already become imprisoned persons, and several people related to the Seoul Youth Association (셔 geochemical울靑年會) and the Labor Federation (勞働聯盟會) have also been arrested early on. Is this all? The extreme caution of banks regarding loans is causing extraordinary hardship in the general economic world, and the various kinds of destitution arising from this are beyond description. Let's first glance this much and see what tomorrow, and the day after tomorrow, brings.＜161＞

**Summary**

This article provides a news digest covering international and domestic events in September 1923. Internationally, it highlights widespread political instability: the Italy-Greece Corfu incident, revolution scares in Romania, a coup in Spain, tensions in the Balkans involving Bulgaria and Serbia, Mussolini's provocative appointment regarding Fiume, relative optimism about Anglo-French talks, ongoing turmoil in Germany, and the power struggle in China following President Li's flight. Domestically in Korea, it reports on the heightened state of alert and suppression by the Japanese Government-General following the Great Kanto Earthquake, including the arrest of magazine staff (Park Soon-ho, Yoo Byung-gi) and youth/labor activists, and severe economic hardship exacerbated by restrictive bank lending. For historians, the article is revealing as it documents the immediate repressive atmosphere in colonial Korea after the Kanto earthquake, explicitly linking the disaster to increased surveillance and arrests of Koreans involved in nationalist or social movements (*Sinch'ŏnji*, Seoul Youth Association, Labor Federation). It also reflects the interconnectedness of global events and their perception within the colony, alongside the specific economic pressures faced by Koreans.

餘墨
△지나간 九月 한 달의 세상은, 졍말로 말셩스리운 세상어엿다. 月初에 일어난 日本의 震災가 엇더케 日本 사람을, 또는 朝鮮 사람을 떨게 하엿넌가는 말할 것도 업거니와, 歐洲에서 일어난 혁명운동, 交戰 등의 여러 가지 일은, 역시 세상 사람의 신경을 들추어 주엇다. 第一伊太利國境委員長 「치리니」가 希臘에서 암살된 事로 인하야, 伊, 希양국의 교전이, 希國側의 陳謝로 인해서 결국 大事에 不至한 것은 다행하나, 羅馬尼에는 9月 17日로써 羅馬尼共産政府를 수립하리라는 계획이 폭로되야, 끔즉한 官民간의 충돌이 생기여, 그 끗이 언제 날지 모르며, 西班牙에는 전 육군대신이던 「리버라」장군이 一大動亂을 起하야, 현 정부를 顚覆하고, 스스로 首相이 되야, 第二伊太利에 무쏘리式을 自取함도 壯觀이며, 21日에는 巴彌幹의 勃牙利國에 폭동이 돌발하야, 共産黨과 農民黨이 氣脉을 相通하는 중, 塞爾比國은 勃牙利 國境에 군대를 집중하야, 巴爾幹의 風雲을 急케 하며, 제 고집 세우기로 유명한 伊太利의 무쏘리 수상은, 「라파로」條約에 의하야, 자유국으로 作定된 「휴매」에 陸軍都督을 임명하야, 平地의 風波를 惹起하는 今日이다. 爲先 다행타 할 것는, 英佛首相의 회견이 其 巴里에서 행하얏는데, 其 結果가 만족하다는 말이다. 그러나 獨逸국내의 騷擾는 去去益甚하야, 다수 민중은 근본적 혁명이 一日이라도 속행되기를 바라는 모양이다.

◇一自黎總統 北京脫出 以後의 中國政界는 일대 紊亂을 極하야, 直隷派의 操縱下에서 攝政內閣이 樹立되야, 一日 一日을 經來하든 중 曹錩將軍의 大統領昇格運動은, 近日 顯著히 進捗되는 樣이다. 이에 따라서 생기는 曹錩 張作霖間의 충돌이 이제부터 볼만한 일이겟다.

◇돌이켜 朝鮮內의 情景을 一瞥하면, 震亂一過後의 總督府 當局의 신경은 극도로 興奮되야, 언론, 집회, 결사 기타의 取締에 극도의 權能을 발휘하는 중, 新天地의 同人 朴淳鎬, 兪炳璣 兩君은 벌서 監禁 중의 사람이 되엇스며, 셔울靑年會
勞働聯盟會의 관계자 중 여러 사람도, 일즉히 拘囚된 몸이 되엇다. 이 뿐이랴, 은행의 극도의 貸出警戒는 일반 경제계에 비상한 困窮을 與하야, 이로써 생기는 가지로의 窮狀은 이로 말할 수가 업다. 爲先 이만큼 一瞥하고 明日 또 明日을 보기로 하쟈.＜161＞
