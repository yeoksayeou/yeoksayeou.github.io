Brief Comments on the Financial World

Min Yŏnghwi (閔泳徽) and Kim Sŏngsu (金性洙) are the two great chaebols (財閥) of Chosŏn (朝鮮). While Mr. Min has the Tongil Bank (東一銀行), Mr. Kim has the Haedong Bank (海東銀行); while Mr. Min has Hwimun High School (徽文高普), Mr. Kim has Chungang High School and Posung College (中央高普普專); and while Mr. Min has Chosŏn Silk Weaving (朝鮮絹織), Mr. Kim has Kyŏngsŏng Spinning and Weaving (京城紡織). However, because Mr. Min lacks a newspaper like Mr. Kim's Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), he has always been at a disadvantage. Recently, the Tongil Bank has also faced many issues (問題 多事), leading even to the resignation of its president, Mr. Min Taesik (閔大植). Henceforth, it seems that in the financial world of Chosŏn, Mr. Kim stands virtually unrivalled.

◇

Following the passing of Mr. Yu Ch'ihyŏng (兪致衡), the Managing Director (常務取締役) of Han'ŭn (漢銀 - likely Hansŏng Bank), his successor is said to be either Mr. Im Hŏn'gyŏng (林憲慶) or Mr. Chang Usik (張友植). One could say: if not Im, then Chang (非林則張).

◇

The Government-General's (總督府) budget for the next fiscal year shows revenues in the regular section of 239,800,992 yen, an increase of 33,518,340 yen compared to this fiscal year's 206,282,652 yen. The provisional section shows 51,711,591 yen, a decrease of 610,155 yen compared to this fiscal year's 52,321,749 yen. Consequently, the total revenue (總收入) is 291,512,583 yen, an increase of 32,908,185 yen compared to this fiscal year's 258,604,398 yen. (Remainder omitted) <127>

**Summary**

This article briefly covers three news items from the Korean financial world in late 1934. First, it compares the business empires of Min Yŏnghwi and Kim Sŏngsu, noting Kim's growing dominance due to his ownership of the Tonga Ilbo newspaper and recent troubles at Min's Tongil Bank leading to its president's resignation. Second, it reports on the potential successors for the deceased managing director of Hansŏng Bank. Third, it details the increase in the Government-General of Chosŏn's budget for the upcoming fiscal year, breaking down the changes in regular and provisional revenues. For historians, this piece offers a glimpse into the competition between prominent Korean capitalists (Min Yŏnghwi and Kim Sŏngsu) operating within the colonial structure, highlighting the importance of media ownership (Tonga Ilbo) alongside traditional industries. It also quantifies the colonial government's expanding financial scale.
