Blue Page PARANG PAGE, Several Jokes

Regular Demand

The landlord suddenly got angry and said, "…Honestly, don't you have any shame, making me come by every day to demand the rent?" "Is that so? Then how about this? Just come every Tuesday…?"

Excellent Answer

While an officer was leading soldiers on a night march, he pointed out the direction and said, "Now do you understand? If you understand, I'll start asking questions..... If the Big Dipper is directly in front of you, what is behind your back (背)?"
"Yes, sir. My backpack (背囊) is there."

Automobile Convenience

A judge looked at a young child and asked, "If your father and mother separate, who will you go with?" "Well, I'll go with the side that has the car."<115>

**Summary**

This article presents three short jokes or humorous anecdotes. The first involves a tenant cleverly negotiating the frequency of rent collection demands. The second is a wordplay joke where a soldier misunderstands an officer's question about directions during a night march. The third depicts a child pragmatically choosing which parent to live with after a separation based on who owns a car. From a historical perspective, these jokes offer glimpses into everyday concerns and societal elements of 1930s Korea. The rent joke hints at economic pressures, while the final joke points to the growing significance and status associated with automobiles during that era. The humor relies on simple situational irony and wordplay.

定期催促

집 주인이 화를 더럭 내면서 「....글세 당신도 렴치가 잇지 날마다 집세를 재촉하러 단이게 한단말요」 「그럿슴닛가? 그러면 이럿케 하면 으떠슴닛가? 매주 화요일에만 오시면....?」

傑答

將校가 兵卒을 인솔하고 야간 행군을 할 때 방향을 가르치면서 「인제는 아럿나. 아랏스면 지금부터 무를 터이야..... 너이들은 정면에 북두칠성이 잇스면 등(背) 뒤에는 무엇이 잇서?」
「네 배랑(背)이 잇슴니다」

自働車便
