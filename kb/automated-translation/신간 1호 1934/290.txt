One Hundred People, One Hundred Stories

Lieutenant Colonel Yi Ryang of the Manchukuo Army stayed at the Chosŏn Hotel during his return to Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul). Thinking of the days he used to lodge at the XXX Labor Lodging House, he must feel deeply moved (感慨無量).

Kim X-in recently suffered the loss of his mother, and Yu Chin-o has now lost his father. This autumn is truly a time of mourning for novelists (小說家 蒙喪之秋).

Yi Ki-yŏng, famous for his emaciated physique (瘦瘠), earning nicknames like "Professor of Thinness University" or "Malnourski" (말너스키), and whose slender wrists slipped in and out of handcuffs (명꽁이 時計 - slang for 手錠 'handcuffs') while imprisoned, becoming a topic of discussion among the guards, has been arrested (檢擧) again in connection with a certain incident. Though his body may be confined (不自由), his wrists seem always free.

Literary critic Paek Ch'ŏl's name, containing 'iron' (鐵), was unlucky (不吉), leading him to 'sightsee iron bars' (鐵窓 - prison), or so a friend joked (戱言). But perhaps, since his surname is Paek (白 - white/clear), he might also be acquitted/released without charge (白放).

Kim Tong-hwan seems to regard Na Hye-sŏk as an angel or fairy (天使仙女), repeatedly milking her story like chewing on crab bones (뼉다구를 게먹드시), reheating it two or three times (再湯三湯) in every issue of Samch'ŏlli Magazine (三千里誌). At this rate, even after Ms. Na's death (死後), Mr. Kim Tong-hwan will likely lay claim to her white bones (白骨).

Samch'ŏlli Magazine (三千里誌) published a detailed account of the expenses for the memorial service (追悼會) for Sŏhae Ch'oe Hak-song. If only they had also published a detailed accounting of the condolence money (賻儀金) expenditures from the funeral, the public rumors would likely die down.

Some people are curious about the outcome of Pak Hŭi-do's defamation (名譽毁損) lawsuit against the Chungang Ilbo and occasionally ask how it was resolved (歸決). However, inquiries at a certain place (某處) reveal that the matter (事宜) is still pending at the prosecutor's office (檢事局) and has not reached any conclusion (何等 段落).

Minse An Chae-hong, whose whereabouts had been unknown (杳然) since his release from prison (出監), made a public reappearance (街頭進出) via posters announcing his review (檢閱) of the *Collected Works of Chŏng Tasan* (丁茶山遺集) published by Sin Chosŏn Sa. More recently, he has been traveling (巡廻) around the Yŏsu area searching for the historic sites (遺跡) of Yi Ch'ungmu-gong (Admiral Yi Sun-sin) and publishing his travelogue serially in the Chosŏn Ilbo. The autumn wind rises, the wild goose flies south (秋風起兮鴻南飛).

In Ch'ŏndogyo, there are four 'Rin's (麟): namely Ch'oe Rin, Yi Chong-nin, Im Rin, and Sŏl Rin. However, they are not the four 'Lenins' (네린) of Russia (露西亞), but four Korean 'Rin's.

If one were to compile a "Biographies of Figures" (人物列傳) section for a study called "Numerical Chosŏn Research" (數字朝鮮硏究), the following people must certainly be included (入參): Yi Il, Kim Il-sŏn, Kim Il-dae, Yu Il-sŏn, O Il-yŏng, Ko Il-ch'ŏng, Kim Il-yŏp, Yi Pyŏng-sam, O Sam-ju, Yi Ch'un-sam, Yi Kun-o, Kwŏn O-sŏl, Ch'oe Yuk-tang, Pak Ch'il-yang, Pak P'ar-yang, Kim P'al-bong, Kim Yŏng-p'al, Kim Yang-p'al (Ch'ŏndogyo), Kim Chin-gu, Cho Wan-gu, Cho X-gu, Ch'a Sip-paek, Hyŏn Paek-un, Ch'ŏn Il-ch'ŏng (female official, 女官), Ch'ŏn XX, Ch'ae Man-sik, Ch'oe X-man, Ch'a Man-jae, Yi Sŭng-man (Syngman Rhee), Han Man-hae (Han Yong-un?), XX Un, Yu Man-gyŏm, Yu Ŏk-kyŏm, etc.

If a fortune teller based on the Four Pillars (四柱쟁이) were to select figures based on the Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches (天干地支), they might pick: Yi Kap-sŏng, Yi Kap-ki, Kim Kap-sun, Yi Kap-su, Yi Ŭl, Yi Ŭr-u (X牛 likely 乙雨), Kim Ŭl-han, Yi Pyŏng-sam, Yi Pyŏng-X, Min Pyŏng-X, X Pyŏng-ju, Chŏng Hong-gyo, Chŏng Ch'il-sŏng, Chŏn Mu-gil, Pang Mu-gil, Kim Ki-sun (female teacher, 女敎員), Min Kyŏng-hwi, Kim Kyŏng-sŏng (Ch'ŏndogyo), Sin Il-yong, Sin Sŏk-chŏng, Sin T'ae-ik, Sin Hŭng-u, Sin Chŏng-ŏn, Sin Myŏng-gyun, Sin Albert, No Cha-yŏng, Yu Chin-o, Yu In-ik, Chŏng In-bo, Yi X-chin, etc., etc.

If Yi Yŏ-sŏng had been born during the Imjin (壬辰) War period, he would surely have been treated considerably well, assumed to be a brother of the Ming generals (明將) Li Rusong (李如松), Li Rubai (李如栢), Li Rumei (李如梅), Li Ruzhang (李如樟), Li Ruwu (李如梧), Li Ruzhen (李如楨), etc. And even if Yun Ch'i-ho did not set foot (投足) in the official world (官界), if he were in Kyŏngsŏng, he would be the Kyŏngsŏng Prefect (京城府尹), and if in Kaesŏng, the Kaesŏng Prefect (開城府尹), so his official fortune (官福) is abundant everywhere (到處多).

Among the various 'shadows' (諸影) Sŏgyŏng, Songyŏng, Muyŏng, Yuyŏng, Songyŏng was XX [obscured, likely arrested/died], leaving three cold shadows (寒三影) on the autumn moonlit rivers and mountains (秋月江山). Among the 'steps' (步) Ubo, Hoengbo, Xbo, Ilbo, Ubo ('Ox Step') unfortunately fell first (不幸 先倒), so what will become of the 'infantry formation' (步兵陣) in the literary world (文壇) henceforth?

Kim Ŭl-han was reportedly confined to his sickbed (病席) for a long time, but recently he has recovered considerably (快復), is going out somewhat, and also had a precious son (玉童子). For Mr. Kim, who experienced two joys (兩喜) simultaneously with the blowing of the autumn wind (金風一吹), we offer this notice (一視).

A few years ago, after Hyŏn Chin-gŏn moved outside Ch'angŭimun Gate (彰義門 外) and established his 'Apple Orchard Study' (능금밧 文閣), Yi Ki-yŏng subsequently (隨X) moved nearby. Recently, Yi Kwang-su is said to be building a new 'culture house' (文化住宅) near Sorim Temple (小林寺). It seems Se'gŏmjŏng (Pavilion for Washing Swords, 洗劍亭) will henceforth turn into Se'yŏnjŏng (Pavilion for Washing Inkstones, 洗硯亭).

It's quite a set of three contrasts (好個의 三對照) that Yu Chin-t'ae, who is illiterate (目不識丁), is in charge of the Chosŏn Education Association (朝鮮敎育會); Yu Ŏk-kyŏm, who acts without distinguishing right from wrong (是不XX - likely 是非不辨), serves as Vice President of the Chosŏn Sports Association (朝鮮體育會); and Yi In, who wears Western clothes (身着洋服), serves as President of the Association for the Promotion of Korean Products (朝鮮物産獎勵會).

Cho Man-sik's durumagi coat (두루액이) isn't short, his lower legs are long. Pak Hŭi-do's eyes aren't small, his face is large. Son Chŏng-gyu X's son isn't late, his grandson is late. Ch'oe Ŭn-hŭi X(?) seeing a son-in-law isn't late, seeing a husband was late.<117>

**Summary**

This article is a compilation of short news items and gossip concerning various Korean public figures in late 1934, primarily writers, intellectuals, and community leaders. It covers personal events like births and deaths in families (Kim X-in, Yu Chin-o, Kim Ŭl-han), arrests and legal troubles (Yi Ki-yŏng, Paek Ch'ŏl, Pak Hŭi-do), travels and publications (An Chae-hong, Kim Tong-hwan), organizational activities (Ch'ŏndogyo, Chosŏn Education/Sports/Product Promotion Associations), and movements within the literary scene (relocations near Se'gŏmjŏng). Many items involve wordplay based on the figures' names. For colonial history, this piece offers insights into the social milieu of the Korean elite, showing their interconnectedness, their engagement with both traditional and modern culture, and the background presence of Japanese surveillance and control (evidenced by arrests and discussion of prison). The mention of figures like An Chae-hong (nationalist activist), Yi Kwang-su (prominent writer, later collaborator), Yi Sŭng-man (future president), and Cho Man-sik (nationalist leader) alongside literary gossip provides a snapshot of the complex landscape of Korean society under colonial rule.

百人百話
만주국 육군 中佐 李亮씨는 歸京 중 조선호텔에서 체류하얏다. 전날 XXX勞働宿泊所에서 留宿하던 일을 생각하면 씨도 感慨無量할 듯.

金X仁씨는 저번에 母親喪을 당하니 兪鎭午씨는 요새 또 父親喪을 당하엿다. 今秋는 小說家 蒙喪之.

몸이 瘦瘠하기로 유명하야 瘦身大學교수니 말너스키니하는 別名을 듯고 收監 중에도 가느다런 손목이 명꽁이 時計(手錠의 별칭) 속으로 들낙달낙하야 看守들의 이약이거리가 되던 李箕永은 某사건으로 또 檢擧되엿다. 몸은 비록 不自由나마 손목만은 항시 自由일 듯.

文藝評論家 白鐵은 이름이 不吉하야 鐵窓구경을 하게 되엿다. 어떤 친구가 戱言하는 것을 드럿다. 그러나 그 대신 姓이 白이닛가 白放될 수도 잇겟지.
