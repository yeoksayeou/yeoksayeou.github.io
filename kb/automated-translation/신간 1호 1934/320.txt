**Summary**

This article consists of captions for a caricature exhibition featured in the magazine "Kaebyŏk". It lists the names of prominent Korean figures depicted in two panels (122 and 123), arranged from right to left as displayed in the caricatures. The list includes politicians, cultural figures, educators, and activists, noting those who were deceased at the time of publication. For historians, this piece offers insight into which individuals were considered noteworthy public figures in colonial Korea in 1934, significant enough to be included in such an exhibition, and provides a record of their contemporary recognition across various sectors of society.
