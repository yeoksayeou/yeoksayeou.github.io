Magazine Title: 개벽 신간 제1호
Publication Date: 1934년 11월 01일
Article Title: 첫사랑 (First Love)
Author: 金起林
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A square Desk (冊床).
On the white Wall, a crooked painting of “Cézanne.”

Struck by the white fingers turning old Pages,
Suddenly breathing, the withered Rugosa Rose (海棠花).
A Lake of evaporated Fragrance (香氣).
(At the seaside)
A red smile watched the two people's playfulness.

The white Fossil (化石) of white hope, the white Skeleton (骸骨) of white longing, the white Mummy (미이라) of white waiting.
The two eyes and two eyes that mocked the Lighthouse (燈台) on the Mountain (山上), sucked dry by the bitter sea wind,
Saw off the Departure (出航) of Steamships (汽船) sliding across the round sea.

Today
In the dark sea of my heart
Having left behind a white Lighthouse (燈台)
- O hand that lit the fire <30>
- O breath that put out the fire

Suddenly, the Steam Whistle (汽笛) of the bee swarm shaking the window bars.
The dream, set adrift on a boat upon the sea, seems to be returning again.

I must prepare to welcome it.
A watch face coated with whispers
A Fountain Pen (萬年筆) weary of Talkativeness (多辨).
A soiled Map (地圖).
These things
I spread out on the white Tablecloth (테-블 크로트) of my Memory (記憶).

Now, I must flee
Ran-ah-
Until I return
Tidy up the Room (房) a bit. <31>

**Summary**

This modernist poem by Kim Ki-rim, titled "First Love," uses a series of fragmented images and sensory details to evoke the persistent memory of a past love. Objects like a desk, a Cézanne painting, a withered rose, a watch, pen, and map are juxtaposed with feelings of longing and the haunting return of dreams associated with the sea, a lighthouse, and steamships. The speaker feels overwhelmed by these returning memories and expresses a desire to flee, asking someone named "Ran" to tidy the room, symbolizing an attempt to impose order on the intrusive past. From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting for its clear adoption of Western modernist techniques (fragmentation, juxtaposition, inclusion of Western names like Cézanne) within Korean poetry of the colonial era. It reflects the intellectual and artistic currents of the time, showing engagement with international culture alongside personal introspection. The use of specific objects like steamships and maps could subtly point to the changing modern landscape under colonial rule, while the poem's primary focus on subjective experience offers a contrast to more overtly political literature of the period.

오늘
어두운 나의 마음의 바다에
힌 燈台를 남기고 간
