Hard Is the Road (Contemporary-style Folk Song)
						Kim An-sŏ

A thousand *li* to the Western Province (西道), I have come this far away,
Wandering about, is this body like drifting grass?
My hometown is Chinju (晉州) in Yŏngnam (嶺南),
Leaving the East, turning West, I have come to the North.

A flower blooming alone on a deep mountain slope,
Flutters as it will in the blowing wind.
But this flower that bloomed in my youthful body of eighteen,
Withers helplessly in the bitterness of worldly suffering (世苦).

In this body lamenting the fleeting passage of years,
Why is parting so frequent? There is no place to rest my heart.
Even when I joyfully sing Arirang Arari,
The floating sorrow in this one body never ceases. <26>

Like the haze on the distant mountain, in the blue river water,
The bright sun and green mountains seem to embrace the deep island.
My pleading dream follows the lark,
Wandering alone to the distant edge of the sky.

On a fine spring day with green grass and red flowers,
Though I raise a cup with my beloved and sing songs,
My heart drifts with the passing spring, the falling flowers;
If this face of mine grows gaunt, my beloved too will leave.

The full moon soon embraces the western mountain,
Merry with Arirang, I am also drunk with wine.
Arirang Arari has twelve passes,
But I cannot cross the single pass of my beloved's heart.

As the water that swirled daily enters the river,
Welcome, *ŏyadŏya*, the boat comes in.
The stream water flows to reach the sea, they say;
Indifferently, my beloved deceived me.

Because the seawater keeps its faith twice a day,
I believed the boatman to be my beloved. <27>
But why is it that the departed love shows no sign of returning,
And only the seawater comes in 찾아서 time as before?

Welcome return, the fifth day of the fifth month, Tano,
The old day past when I played with my beloved.
Clear sky, green mountains, blue river water,
In this joyful land, where has my beloved gone?

In the garden, the sound of the swing, the sky laughs,
On the sandbank, the shouts of the wrestlers;
Gathering and dispersing, this joyous day.
Yet, this heart is simply lonely.

Untrustworthy is a man; the promises we whispered
Are like a sandcastle by the ebbing and flowing sea.
Beloved memories drifting like a dream,
There is utterly no way to resolve them. Is it my fault?

Autumn night is desolate by the Taedong River (大同江),
The long-drawn sound of the flute (笛) makes the sky weep.
Coming and going indifferently on the lonely dream path,
The falling leaves tremble, how desolate. <28>

Cricket, what are you crying about?
Do you think you won't sit crying over tomorrow's fate?
Is the dream I fell into, resting on my arm as a pillow, reckless (孟浪)?
Stirring up old village memories only hurts the heart.

Sorrowful, the snowstorm, the swirling wind,
Looking out, the ice on the river is white.
Though burning thoughts blaze within me,
Is there any way to make them known? This lonely heart.

Looking all around, this body is drifting grass.
There is no beloved to lean on, no place to trust.
Raising a cup, Arirang is my solace,
The kŏmun'go weeps for this body, I alone.

The thousand-*li* road to the Western Province (西道) is long, why did I come?
Untrustworthy is my beloved, what am I to do?
That the Taedong River (大同江) water is clear and good for living,
I was deceived by such whispering, floating words.
...(End)... <29>

**Summary**

This poem, titled "Hard Is the Road (Contemporary-style Folk Song)," expresses the deep sorrow, loneliness, and sense of displacement experienced by a speaker (likely female) who has traveled far from her home in Chinju (Yŏngnam) to the north (implied Sŏdo/P'yŏngan region). She laments her wandering existence, lost youth, frequent partings, and betrayal by a lover, comparing herself to drifting grass and a withering flower. The poem weaves in elements of the folk song "Arirang" to underscore her enduring sadness despite attempts at finding solace. For historians, the poem offers insight into personal experiences of migration and hardship, potentially reflecting the social conditions and emotional toll on individuals during the colonial period. The blending of traditional themes (like "Haengnonan"), folk elements ("Arirang," Tano festival), and personal lament provides a poignant snapshot of individual struggles against a backdrop of displacement and disillusionment, possibly linked to economic or social pressures compelling internal migration.

行路難(時體歌謠)
						金岸曙
西道千里 먼 곳을 내가 왓노라
떠도나니 이 身勢 뜬풀 이랄가.
내 故鄕은 嶺南에 晉州 드니만
東에 지고 西 돌아 北을 왓노라.

깁흔 山 山기슭에 홀로 핀 꼿은
부는 바람 제멋에 나붓기건만.
열여듧 절믄 몸에 피인 이 꼿은
속절 업시 지누나 쓸인 世苦에.

가는 歲月 덧업다 탄식는 몸에
離別은 웨 자즌가 맘 둘 곳 업네.
아리랑 아라리를 興에 불너도
이 한몸엔 뜬 시름 끈일 날 업네.＜26＞

먼 山에 아즈랑이 프른 江물엔
白日靑山이 깁섬을 감도는 휜 듯.
하소연한 내 꿈은 종달새 딸아
저 멀니 하늘 끗을 외로이 도네.

프른 풀에 붉은 꼿 고흔 봄날을
님과 함꾀 잔들고 노래 불으나
가는 봄 지는 꼿에 이 맘 흘이네,
이 내 얼골 여위면 님도 가려니.

보름달도 어느덧 西山에 안고
아리랑에 興겨워 술도 醉햇네.
아리랑 아라리는 열두나 고개
외고개 님의 맘을 내 못 넘노라.

날마다 휘도든 물 江으로 들제
반갑다 어야데야 배 들어오네.
개울물은 흘너서 바다 간다고,
無心타 우리님이 나를 속엿네.

바닷물 하로 두 때 信이 잇길래
뱃사람을 님이라 내 미덧드니,＜27＞
어인일가 간 님은 올 줄 몰으고
예대로 바닷물만 때 차자 드네.

돌아오니 반갑다. 五月 端午는
지내간 그 옛날에 님과 놀든 날,
맑은 하늘 프른 山 프른 江물의
즐겁은 이 江山에 님 어디간고.

동산엔 그네 소리 하늘이 웃고
모래밧엔 웨치는 씨름꾼들의,
모혀들고 뒤나는 깃븐 이 하로.
그런 것을 이 맘은 그저 외롤뿐.

못미들건 산애라 말잔 맹서도
드나는 물 바다까 모래 城이요,
꿈결가치 떠도는 그립은 회포,
풀길은 바이 업네, 내 잘못이냐.

가을 밤은 쓸쓸타 大同江까의
길게 뽑는 젓(笛) 소리 하늘이 울고
오락가락 無心타 외론 꿈길에
떨니나니 입사귀 쓸쓸할네라.＜28＞

귀들암이 무어라 네가 우느냐
來日 목숨 운다고 안 질가보냐,
팔벼개에 부친 꿈 孟浪하다랴,
뒤타나니 옛 마을 맘이 傷할 뿐.

설을세라 눈보래 휘도는 바람
바라보니 하얏타 江 우의 어름,
덥은 생각 내 속에 불타기로니
알닐길야 잇는가, 외로운 이 맘.

두르두르 살펴도 이 몸음 뜬 풀.
의지 가지 님이라 미들 곳 업네.
잔들고 아리랑은 이내 속풀이
거문고는 이 身勢 나 혼자 우네.

西道千里 길 멀다 나 웨 왓든고
못미들건 님이라 어이 하리요.
大同江 물이 맑고 살기 조타고
소삭이는 뜬 말에 나 속앗노라.
...(끗)...＜29＞
