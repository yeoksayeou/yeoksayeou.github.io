Kaebyŏk New Publication No. 1
Prepayment Required

▼ Magazine orders must be accompanied by prepayment.
▼ For sending money, using postal transfer (振替) is most convenient.
▼ When sending postage stamps (郵票), please be sure to add an extra ten percent (一割).
▼ Also, please try to send stamps (切手) of two *chŏn* or less.

**Summary**

This text is an announcement from the publisher of the newly relaunched *Kaebyŏk* magazine, issue number 1, dated November 1, 1934. It outlines the procedures for ordering the magazine, emphasizing that prepayment (先金) is mandatory. It recommends using postal transfer (振替) for convenience but allows payment via postage stamps (郵票), provided a 10% surcharge is included, preferably using stamps valued at 2 *chŏn* or less. The notice also includes standard publication details like printing/issue dates, editor/publisher, printer, and addresses. For historians, this notice provides insight into the practical aspects of magazine distribution and financial transactions in colonial Korea, including the requirement for prepayment, the common use of postal transfers, and the specific conditions for paying with stamps, possibly reflecting currency availability or postal regulations. The explicit mention of Shōwa era dating alongside Korean names and addresses highlights the administrative reality of the colonial period.
