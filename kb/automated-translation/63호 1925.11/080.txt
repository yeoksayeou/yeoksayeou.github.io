Social Diary During Suspension
From July 15th

【July 15th】 In the Kyŏngnam Ch'angwŏn and Kimhae regions, disasters involving casualties to people and livestock, and damage to farmland, are continuously occurring due to major floods. Korean compatriots residing in Kyoto, Japan, founded the ideological group Pukchihoe (北志會).
Japanese Ambassador to Russia, Mr. Tanaka, met with Mr. Kalinin and presented his credentials.
A group of 1,500 Peking students was transported to Zhangjiakou (張家口) to receive military training from Mr. Feng Yuxiang.
Chinese laborers in Singapore went on a general strike.

【July 16th】 A major flood also occurred in the Hwanghae Province Sariwŏn region.
The British cabinet discussed the China problem.
The U.S. government issued a notice to various nations regarding the abolition of extraterritoriality in China. The ambassadors of Japan, the U.S., and France visited the British Foreign Secretary to discuss the China problem.

【July 17th】 An unprecedented great flood occurred along the Han River in Kyŏngsŏng, causing great turmoil inside and outside the city.
The French army began an attack on the Rif army. An anti-British rally centered on Chinese people occurred in Vienna.

【July 18th】 The flood in Kyŏngsŏng continued to overflow, and the entire Han River area was devastated. (Water level increase 42 ch'ŏk) <35>

【July 19th】 The great flood in Kyŏngsŏng gradually subsided.
Inmates at the Taegu Prison caused a disturbance over the issue of rice.
A formation ceremony for a proletarian party took place in Japan.

【July 20th】 In Sŏnch'ŏn, group fights occurred due to drought.
American middle school teacher Mr. John Scopes was sentenced to a fine of one hundred dollars for the crime of lecturing on Darwin's theory of evolution.

【July 21st】 There was a disturbance in Hamhŭng city due to the appearance of eight armed individuals.

【July 23rd】 In Tongmak, outside Kyŏngsŏng city, a Christian church collapsed, causing further hardship for flood refugees.
Textile workers in Qingdao went on strike again.
The Portuguese cabinet resigned en masse.
The Dutch cabinet was formed by Finance Minister Echigo (エチコ).
130,000 British workers went on a general strike over wage cuts; simultaneously, coal miners also went on strike.

【July 24th】 A deliverymen's general strike occurred in Shanghai, China.

【July 25th】 At the Han River pedestrian bridge reconstruction site in Kyŏngsŏng, over 1,000 laborers caused a major disturbance due to dissatisfaction with treatment. The Shanghai Federation of Commerce and Industry demanded three items from Chief Executive Duan (段執政): punishment of the Shanghai military command, opening of various sealed institutions in Shanghai, and repeal of martial law.
The Chinese Minister of Justice cabled the British Labour Party, stating, "People of the 20th century cannot endure foreign oppression." <36>

【July 26th】 The California State Senate in the U.S. rejected a resolution opposing the theory of evolution.

【July 27th】 The preliminary examination of Chŏng U-dal and others involved in the Red Flag Incident concluded.

【July 28th】 The political situation in Japan became chaotic.
Coal miners in the Saar region of Germany declared a general strike.
Britain planned to build warships.

【July 29th】 A preferential tariff law was implemented in Korea as well. Over three hundred laborers involved in reclaiming land from the sea surface in Wŏnsan went on strike over working hours.

【July 30th】 Laborers at the Han River pedestrian bridge went on a general strike.
The U.S. President planned a second Washington Conference.
The Korean Labor Party Committee...
Mr. Chŏn Il drowned in Songp'a while rescuing flood victims.

【August 1st】 The August issue of this magazine (Kaebyŏk) was confiscated and simultaneously ordered to suspend publication.
The Katō cabinet in Japan resigned en masse.
The Peking government issued an order to prevent Bolshevization.
A fight between armed men and detectives occurred on the streets of Kyŏngsŏng.
40 compatriots were shot dead by the Red Army in Russian territory.

【August 2nd】 In Japan, Katō Shishaku (Viscount Katō) was ordered to form a cabinet again, and simultaneously, a Kenseikai (Constitutional Government Association) single-party cabinet was established.
A clash between British and Chinese people occurred in Nanjing.
Civil war broke out in Tibet.
The trial for the case of a policeman from the Sep'o substation in P'yŏnggang County shooting a civilian dead was held in Kyŏngsŏng.
The Hanyang Youth Alliance was formed **** by the Kyŏngsŏng **** Union. <37>

【August 3rd】 A large fight between Koreans and Chinese occurred in Yongsan, Kyŏngsŏng.
At the Han River pedestrian bridge, over 1,000 laborers caused a major disturbance over wage issues.
In Fujiwara, Japan, over 3,000 citizens surrounded and attacked the police station.

【August 4th】 China's Zhang Zuolin issued an order to close schools run by Koreans.
Yi Wan-yong ultimately paid increased school fees.
The British situation regarding Guangdong became even more perilous.
Three students drowned in the Han River at Sŏbinggo.

【August 5th】 A conspiracy to revive the Great Qing Empire arose in China.
Compatriots residing in Hawaii started a movement to expel Vice-Consul Yang Chae-ha.

【August 6th】 A battle occurred between a Korean armed group and Chinese police on the opposite bank of Pyŏkch'ŏn (碧澺對岸).
A street battle occurred between striking miners and police in Britain.

【August 7th】 Ten British warships hurried to China.
American coal miners went on a general strike.
The Guangdong government decided to implement labor-farmer policies.

【August 8th】 A tidal wave occurred in the Chŏngju region.
Japan planned to transfer three brigades to Korea.
A Ku Klux Klan convention with over 50,000 members took place in the U.S.

【August 9th】 Striking workers at the Taedong Printing Company in Kyŏngsŏng resolved to stage a hunger strike (lit. starvation alliance).
Both houses of the British Parliament passed the coal mine subsidy bill. <38>
In Berlin, Germany, the 6th-anniversary ceremony of the 80 large Communist councils was held.
A major disturbance occurred between farmers and the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (Equality Society) in *ch'ŏn County.

【August 10th】 A conference regarding the organization of a proletarian party opened in Osaka, Japan.
A Labor Alliance was formed in Kyŏngsŏng.
Over 1,000 farmers in *ch'ŏn County again attacked the Wip'yŏng branch of the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, causing a major disturbance.

【August 11th】 A disturbance occurred in *song-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, due to the appearance of a gun-wielding criminal.
A clash occurred between police and textile workers in Tianjin, resulting in several workers being killed.

【August 12th】 Korea's unprecedented great flood struck the northwest region, putting the areas north of P'yŏngyang and towards Hamhŭng in danger.
A disturbance occurred in Japan due to the eruption of Mt. Shirane.
In Sariwŏn, over a thousand households were flooded due to the flood.
One member of an armed group was killed by police in Wiwŏn.

【August 13th】 Kim Chi-sŏp, the main culprit in the Nijūbashi Bridge bombing in Japan, appealed again.
An armed group attacked a raft boat on the Yalu River.
Mr. Kim Man-hwa, leader of an armed group in Manchuria, issued a protest letter to the Chinese authorities.

【August 14th】 At the Ch'ŏndogyo Church in Kyŏngsŏng, a conflict between the old and new factions arose when the Earth Day commemoration was held.
The French army suffered a major defeat by the Syrian revolutionary army.

【August 15th】 In Yŏnbyŏn (延邊), an armed group shot and killed former policeman Sin **.
The Japanese-*** North Sakhalin conference <39> was held in Moscow.
An incident of assault by Japanese people against Koreans occurred in ** 생기*.

【August 16th】 A lecture by Japanese socialists Mr. Nakanishi and others at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall was halted due to an attack by the Kuksusahoe (National Essence Society).
A conference for the Jewish national independence movement was being held in Vienna.
The Kyŏngsŏng Youth Federation was initiated within the Kyŏngsŏng Youth Federation Hall.

【August 17th】 A temperance demonstration movement by over 1,000 farmers occurred in Muju town center.

【August 18th】 A tragedy occurred in An'yŏng-myŏn, P'ungsan County, where 23 people drowned.
Shanghai postal employees went on strike over wage issues.
China notified various countries about holding a customs conference on October 26th.
An armed group abducted two district chiefs (區長) in Unsan County.

【August 19th】 A fierce battle occurred between an armed group and police in Pongsan, Unsan, resulting in 2-3 casualties on each side.
British trade unions resolved on a general strike to oppose wage reductions.
The Chairman of the U.S. Senate Committee asserted that peace in Asia is hopeless without a change in policy towards China.

【August 20th】 A flood struck Hamhŭng County again.
The Kyŏnggi Province Police Department summoned various ideological groups within Kyŏngsŏng to warn them about the Public Order Maintenance Law.
Judgment was rendered for 27 individuals, including Sakai Toshihiko and Sano Manabu, in the Japanese Communist Party incident; the heavy sentence was 10 months' imprisonment. <40>
The Moroccan independence army rejected peace talks.
The Canton (束?) government prohibited the entry and exit of British ships under the pretext of rights infringement.

【August 21st】 France, Britain, and Italy reached an agreement to approve the security treaty proposed by Germany.

【August 22nd】 A major disturbance between Koreans and Japanese occurred in Kyŏngsŏng city due to unfair police handling of affairs.

【August 23rd】 The strike by French bank employees in Shanghai expanded.

【August 25th】 Russia protested the construction of the Tao-Ch'i railway (逃齊鐵道).

【August 26th】 The response regarding the Franco-German security treaty was exchanged.
Britain demanded that China prohibit economic boycotts.
A secret agreement was made between Director Mitsuya (三矢局長) and the Governor of Dongbiandao (東邊道尹) regarding the crackdown on armed Koreans.

【August 27th】 The British in Hong Kong issued an ultimatum to Guangdong.
Britain reserved judgment on, and Japan did not participate in, the peace protocol. Russia established a plan for relocation to the Far East.

【August 28th】 A major storm occurred in the Kantō region of Japan.
The Chinese National Assembly decided to adjourn for one month.
France decided on a 62-year payment * for British ** repayment.
A Soviet airplane passed over P'yŏngyang.
China demanded that Japan remove its post offices along the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵) line. <41>

【August 29th】 Security was tight ahead of the anniversary of the annexation of Korea by Japan.
The Syrian revolutionary army defeated the French army.
A railway workers' strike occurred in Australia.
A sailors' strike occurred at the Cape of Good Hope.

【August 30th】 The communist faction in Guangdong seized real power. The Peking government issued an order to prevent communism.
Kyushu Imperial University in Japan burned down.

【August 31st】 Yi Chong-il, one of the 33 signatories of the March First Movement, passed away.
An armed group of over 10 members attacked the police substation in Yangsan-myŏn, Sakchu County.
Mr. Liang Hongjie (梁鴻階), leader of the anti-communist faction in Guangdong, was assassinated by Mr. Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) of the communist faction.
The trial of Kim Pyŏng-hyŏn and others, accused of punishing pro-Japanese collaborators, began.

【September 1st】 The trial of Chŏng Chae-dal and others in Korea's first Communist Party incident opened.
The Syrian revolutionary army occupied As-Suwayda.
A worldwide Jewish Congress was held in London.
In Hyŏnp'ung, Talsŏng County, over ten farmers attacked members of the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa.

【September 2nd】 A commerce treaty was concluded between China and Austria.
An explosion occurred at the Chŏngbaek Coal Mine in South P'yŏngan, burying 5 miners alive. <42>

【September 3rd】 [No entry for Sep 3rd, text skips to Sep 4th]

【September 4th】 Due to the appearance of armed groups in the Hŭich'ŏn and Kanggye regions, everywhere is insecure.

【September 5th】 At the Anglo-Turkish negotiation meeting on the Mosul issue, the Turkish representative opposed the British mandate over Mosul.
The Rif army shelled the allied fleet.
Soviet Consul General to Kyŏngsŏng, Mr. Shadbanov (샤드바놉), and his party arrived in Kyŏngsŏng.

【September 6th】 Security was tight as it was International Youth Day.
China launched a movement demanding the abolition of extraterritoriality and the return of leased territories from various nations.

【September 7th】 Another major disaster occurred due to heavy rain in the Kyŏngnam area (especially Miryang) (simultaneously, a church and a train overturned).
The 6th General Assembly of the League of Nations opened in Geneva. The Allied Foreign Ministers' Conference approved inviting Germany to discuss the European security issue.

【September 8th】 The Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper was ordered to suspend publication, and simultaneously, writer Sin Il-yong, publisher Kim Tong-sŏng, and printer Kim Hyŏng-wŏn were sued.
Japan rejected China's demand to remove post offices along the South Manchuria Railway line, calling it outside the district.
Three people drowned due to a ferry capsizing on the Han River.

【September 9th】 The diplomatic corps in Peking held a preliminary meeting regarding the customs conference.
Doctor of Oriental Philosophy Paek Sŏng-uk returned from Germany.
A major flood also occurred along the Yellow River <43> in China, causing 2,000 drowning deaths and washing away 10,000* homes.
A nationalist secret society was discovered in Germany, and arrest warrants were issued.

【September 10th】 Furuta, the attempted assassin of Japanese General **, was sentenced to death.
The French representative to the League of Nations proposed holding an international economic conference.
U.S. foreign claims were announced to be $12,080,000,000 (28 debtor nations).

【September 11th】 The Scrutiny Committee of the Japanese Privy Council passed a bill prohibiting the trafficking of women and children.
A major storm also occurred in the Nagoya region of Japan, causing over 20,000 households to be flooded.
There were disturbances due to armed groups in the Wiwŏn and Kŭmsan regions.
The Chinese representative to the League of Nations gave a speech regarding the abolition of unequal treaties.

【September 12th】 Chŏng Chae-su and Yi Chae-bok of the Communist Party incident were each sentenced to 3 years in prison.
Chinese streetcar employees in Shanghai went on strike over wage issues.
The Ambassador to France communicated that Soviet Russia would also join the unification of the International Metric System.

【September 13th】 The Australian Parliament passed a bill abolishing the death penalty.

【September 14th】 A storm struck Taiwan, causing the tragic situation of over 10,000 flooded households.
The legation corps in Peking agreed to a 10% increase in import tax, similar to relief funds, to be implemented entirely after the customs conference. <44>

【September 15th】 France sent reinforcements to Indochina due to suspicion of an uprising.
The British Colonial Secretary at the League of Nations protested to Turkey regarding the expulsion of Christians from the Mosul region.
In P'yŏngyang, the appeal trial of Yang Sŭng-u (sentenced to death in the first trial), commander of the Pyŏkch'ang Volunteer Corps, opened.

【September 16th】 A conference of European small and weak nations was decided to be held in Geneva in October.
26 German parliamentarians departed for the Washington International Parliamentary Conference.
In Japan, 26 people were arrested at the memorial service for the late Ōsugi Sakae.
30,000 Indian textile workers went on strike over wage issues.
The U.S. Secretary of State declared the refusal of entry to a British Communist MP.

【September 17th】 Japan's Gotō Shinpei and others launched a company for Siberian development.
A large celebration ceremony for the withdrawal of troops from the Ruhr was held盛行 in Germany.
The sailors' strike in Australia expanded.

【September 18th】 Both the House of Peers and the House of Representatives buildings in Japan burned down completely.
The General Labor Union was sealed off in Songjiang (松江), China.

【September 19th】 Italy arrested 15,000 Communist Party members nationwide.

【September 20th】 The residence of Duke Tokugawa, president of the Japanese House of Peers, burned down completely.
Vienna police arrested a conspiracy group planning the assassination of high-ranking Labor-Farmer officials. (Continued in the next issue) <45>

**Summary**

This article is a chronological compilation of news briefs ("Social Diary") from July 15th to September 20th, 1925, covering a period when the magazine *Kaebyŏk* itself was suspended. It reports on a wide range of events, including major floods in Korea (Kyŏngsŏng, Ch'angwŏn, Kimhae, Sariwŏn, Hamhŭng) and China, labor strikes (Korea, China, UK, US, Germany, Australia, Cape of Good Hope), political developments (formation of parties in Japan, communist trials in Korea and Japan, government changes in Portugal and Netherlands), international relations (China's push against unequal treaties and extraterritoriality, tensions between Britain/Japan and China, Franco-German treaty), and armed activities (Korean armed groups in Korea and Manchuria, Rif War, Syrian Revolt). From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the diary is revealing for its documentation of natural disasters impacting Koreans, numerous labor strikes reflecting worker discontent, the suppression of the press (*Kaebyŏk*, *Chosŏn Ilbo*), the ongoing activities and suppression of Korean independence fighters (armed groups, arrests like the Red Flag Incident and Communist Party cases), social tensions (Korean-Chinese fights, farmer conflicts with the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa), and Japanese colonial policies (preferential tariffs, troop movements, crackdowns on ideological groups, agreements with China to suppress Korean fighters). Historians might find the sheer breadth of reported events, linking local Korean issues with global happenings, particularly interesting, showcasing the interconnectedness of the world in 1925 and the specific struggles faced within colonial Korea.

잡지명	개벽 제63호
발행일	1925년 11월 01일
기사제목	停刊 中의 社會日誌
기사형태	소식

停刊 中의 社會日誌
7月15日부터
【仝7月15日】慶南 昌原 及 金海地方에는 大洪水로 因하야 人畜 及 田畓의 慘害가 續出되고 日本京都 在留朝鮮同胞는 思想단체 北志會를 創立하고
駐露 日本大使 田中氏는 「카리닌」氏와 會見하고 親任狀을 捧呈하고
北京學生團 1千5百 名은 馮玉祥 氏에게 軍事敎育을 受하기 위하야 張家口로 輸送되고
新嘉坡의 중국노동자는 同盟罷業하다.
【仝16日】黃海道 沙里院 地方도 大洪水가 出하고
英國內閣은 中國問題를 討議하고
米國政府는 列國에 대하야 중국에 대한 治外法權 撤廢의 通牒을 發하고 日 米 佛 3國 大使는 英 外相을 訪하아 中國問題를 討議하다.
【仝17日】京城漢江一帶에는 未曾有의 大洪水가 出하야 市內 外가 大變亂을 當하고
佛國軍은 「리프」軍 攻擊을 開始하고 維也納에는 中國人 中心의 排英大會가 起하다.
【仝18日】京城의 洪水는 계속 汎濫하야 漢江一帶는 全滅되다.(增水42尺) ＜35＞
【仝19日】京城 大洪水는 其勢 漸減되고
大邱刑務所 囚徒는 밥 問題로 騷動을 起하고
日本에는 無産政黨 結黨式이 有하다.
【仝20日】宣川에는 旱災로 因하야 作黨格鬪가 起하고
米國 中學敎諭「존스코프」氏는 「따윈」의 進化論을 講義한 罪로 百弗의 罰金言渡를 當하다.
【仝21日】咸興部에 武裝團 八名이 出現한 騷動이 有하다.
【仝23日】 京城 市外 東幕에서는 耶穌敎堂이 崩壞되야 水災避難民이 重亂을 當하고
靑島紡績織工은 再次罷業하고
葡萄牙內閣은 總辭職하고
和蘭內閣은 藏相 「에치코」氏가 組織하고
英國職工 13萬은 減給問題로 同盟罷業하고 同時 炭坑夫도 罷業하다.
【仝24日】 中國上海에 配達夫 同盟罷業이 起하다.
【仝25日】京城 漢江人道橋 修築場에서는 千 餘 名 人夫가 待遇不平으로 大騷動을 起하고 上海商工聯盟會는 段執政에게 「上海軍司令處罰, 上海封鎖 各機廳 開放, 戒嚴令撤廢」등 3個條 要求하고
中國司法總長은 英國노동당에게 「20世紀人은 外國壓迫을 甘受할 수 업다」고 打電하다.＜36＞
【仝26日】米國 加州上院에서 進化論 反對決議案을 否決하다.
【仝27日】赤旗團事件의 鄭右達 등의 예심이 종결되다.
【仝28日】日本政局은 混亂化되고
