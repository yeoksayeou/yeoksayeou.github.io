잡지명	개벽 第63號
발행일	1925년 11월 01일
기사제목	當面하야 오는 五十一議會와 日本政局의 將來, 旣成政黨의 懊惱, 無産者의 擡頭 (The Coming 51st Diet and the Future of Japanese Politics, the Anguish of Established Parties, the Rise of the Proletariat)
필자	洪承耈
기사형태	소식 (News/Report)

Among Japan's successive prime ministers, Hara Takashi, known as a man of sharp ability and leader of the Seiyūkai party which controlled an absolute majority in the House of Representatives at the time, whose authority was illustrious, met a miserable end at the hands of a single insignificant youth at Tokyo Station. Since then, Japan's political situation has been shaky and unstable. Moreover, among the relics of the old Meiji era, the Genrō elders, who served as tutors to the Emperor and controlled the real political power, have withered away one by one. Especially with the death of Yamagata Aritomo, nicknamed Japan's Pope for being the supreme commander of the military clique (軍閥) and bureaucratic faction (官僚派), the tide of the political world shifted secretly and silently, even leading to the emergence of the party cabinets they loathed like snakes and scorpions. Consider this: since Hara Takashi's assassination, haven't Japanese cabinets shown short-lived records like Takahashi Korekiyo's 7 months, Yamamoto Gonnohyōe's 4 months, and Kiyoura Keigo's 5 months? Isn't this, more than anything, proof that the central force of the political world is bidding farewell to the old and welcoming the new? And isn't the fact that the current Prime Minister Katō Takaaki has managed to maintain a lifespan of over a year telling of recent news? However, the current cabinet, bowing its head (叩頭) under the pressure of the demands of the era, managed to enact the Universal Suffrage Bill, albeit insufficiently. It thus expanded the right to political participation, previously open only to a portion of the propertied class under numerous restrictions, to a new mass of several tens of millions of proletarians. Seeing that even the stubborn old men of the House of Peers, who until last year viewed proponents of universal suffrage as traitors, and the Seiyūkai, which had dedicated itself to obstructing universal suffrage, could not help but agree to it, one cannot but marvel at the power of the changing times. Thus, the legal center of Japan has departed from the hands of the few privileged classes and the capitalist established political parties, and sooner or later, we are set to see the rise of proletarian parties. The restoration of diplomatic relations with Red Russia<28> and the enactment of the Universal Suffrage Law (though both may be incomplete) are acknowledged first as Japan's two major achievements in foreign and domestic policy this year. However, because it was an expansion of suffrage they were originally reluctant to grant, and because they fear the loss of their old power due to the rise of new parties, the established political parties are trying in every way to avoid the swift implementation of the new election law. Not only that, but they seem busy trying to buy the favor of those enfranchised by the new election law using baits such as tax system reorganization incorporating social policy, revitalization measures for exhausted rural villages, or expansion of educational institutions and projects like railways, rivers, and harbors. Therefore, last year was a time when frustration and anguish reached their peak for Japan's established political parties. The Seiyūkai, having broken ties with the Kenseikai and been expelled from the coalition cabinet, seeks to avoid a head-on clash as much as possible until the Seihon Gōdō (政本合同 - Merger of Seiyūkai and Seiyūhontō) is achieved. The Kenkyūkai in the Upper House and the Seiyūhontō in the Lower House, which previously had an irreconcilable relationship with the Kenseikai, are now showing favor towards the current Kenseikai-supported cabinet under pretexts like common policy or power sharing, even showing signs of passive support. This is all nothing but a scheme (魂膽) to prevent the early appearance of proletarian class Diet members who are expected to emerge anew from a dissolution of the Diet. If the 51st Diet were to be dissolved this time, it is a foregone conclusion that differently colored members would appear in the 52nd Diet, however much they dislike seeing it. However, if there is no dissolution, the lifespan of the current Diet members will be maintained until Taishō 17 (1928). If, during this time, the established parties engage in opportunistic alliances (野合) and makeshift compromises (姑息한 妥協), political power will not shift elsewhere for two or three years. That is, they share a common fear of the new enemy and are suppressing their brimming fighting spirit. Therefore, barring any sudden, unexpected changes, the observation that the next Diet session will likely conclude without dissolution is not far off the mark (正鵠). Even if the Seihon Gōdō, which the Kenseikai most fears and detests, materializes, this is only unpleasant for the Kenseikai; since political power would smoothly remain with the established parties representing the capitalist cliques (資本의 閥), there would be no change in the central force of the political world. This clarifies the reason why the Kenseikai, which should naturally dissolve the Diet, contrary to its verbal grandstanding (大言狀語), sends flirtatious glances (秋波) to the Seiyūhontō and fawns (媚謟) upon the Kenkyūkai. While the established parties, intuitively sensing their fate, strive to take preventive measures, the focus of interest lies entirely on what kind of preparations are underway in the bloc known as Japan's proletariat. Looking at the declaration and platform of the Proletarian Party, which recently held its inauguration ceremony in Osaka, although they are not unimpressive, their actual strength is still too weak to mention. Besides this, proletarian and other left-leaning groups are too numerous to count (枚擧하기에 不遑하지마는), but due to differences in ideology or emotional discord, they not only lack connection and unity but also view each other with jealousy and hostility, suffering from internal strife (自中之亂) with no peaceful days (寧日). Thus, there is a sense that the road ahead is long and distant (前途遼遠) before they become a new force in the political world. Furthermore, considering that the Japanese national character has not yet escaped the realm of subservience to the powerful (事大主義), and is consequently governed by a psychology of bowing to the strong and being arrogant towards the weak, and on the other hand, the influence of the Kokusuikai and other conservative and reactionary right-wing groups, which can be seen as auxiliary defenders of the established parties, is still not to be dismissed (侮視치 못할 것), even if the next Diet were dissolved, the new forces would likely not increase rapidly. Viewed thus, it seems certain that the 51st Diet, which has the air of "the wind fills the tower signaling the coming mountain rain" (山雨欲來風滿樓), will end uneventfully, and we likely won't witness the interesting political battles we wish to see. However, the arrow (矢) is already on the bowstring (弦上). It must be released sometime (何時에든지 發치 안으면 己치 안을 것이니); Japan's political situation is bound to become eventful (多事하랴) from now on. In the meantime, the party hacks (黨人者流) poisoned by lust for power (權勢慾) and greed (漁利慹) will be busy performing farces (魄劇), hardly having enough days (維日不足할 것이오). It is guaranteed that the old men (老漢) of the Kaei and Ansei eras (嘉永安政時代) will shed copious tears of nostalgia (感舊의 悲淚가 縱橫할). When will the world be settled? (October 30th manuscript) <30>

**Summary**

This 1925 article by Hong Sŭng-gu analyzes the unstable Japanese political landscape following Prime Minister Hara Takashi's assassination. It notes the succession of short-lived cabinets, the waning influence of the Genrō, and the grudging passage of the Universal Suffrage Bill under the Katō Takaaki cabinet. The author highlights the established parties' (Seiyūkai, Kenseikai, Seiyūhontō) deep anxiety about the newly enfranchised proletariat and their maneuvering to delay elections and prevent the rise of proletarian parties. While acknowledging the emergence of proletarian political groups, the article points out their internal divisions and weakness, predicting that the upcoming 51st Diet will likely avoid dissolution, thus postponing a major political shift, though tensions remain high for the future. For historians of Korean colonial history, this article offers a valuable contemporary Korean intellectual's perspective on the internal political dynamics and social tensions within Imperial Japan. It reveals a keen observation of the colonizer's vulnerabilities, the power struggles among its elite, the fear generated by social change (universal suffrage and proletarian movements), and a somewhat skeptical assessment of both the established order and the nascent progressive forces, anticipating future turmoil.

當面하야 오는 五十一議會와 日本政局의 將來, 旣成政黨의 懊惱, 無産者의 擡頭
						洪承耈
日本의 歷代總理大臣 中에 辣腕家의 稱이 잇고 當時 衆議院에서 절대다수를 制하는 政友會의 黨首로 威權이 赫赫하던 原敬이가 渺小한 一個 靑年의 手에 의하야 東京驛頭에서 비참한 최후를 遂한 이래로 日本의 政局은 搖搖不定하얏고 더욱이 帝王의 師로 任하야 정치의 實權을 左右하고 잇든, 明治舊代의 遺物인 元老輩가, 次第로 淍謝한 중에 특히 軍閥과 官僚派의 最高統帥者로 日本의 羅馬法皇이라는 綽名이 잇는, 山懸有朋의 死로써 政界의 機運은 暗遷黙移하야, 그들의 蛇蝎가티 厭惡하는 政黨內閣의 출현까지 보게 되얏다. 觀하라 原敬의 橫死후로 日本內閣은 高橋是淸의 7개월, 山本權兵衛의 4개월, 淸浦奎吾의 5개월이라는 短命的 記錄을 보지 아니 하얏는가. 이것이 무엇보다도 政界의 중심세력이 送舊迎新하야가는 예증이 아니며 僅히 現 首相 加藤高明에게 의하야 1년이상의 壽命을 保하는 것이 這間의 소식을 語하는 바이다. 그런대 現內閣은 時代의 要求라는 壓力下에 叩頭하야 불충분하나마도 普通選擧法案을 성립식히여 從來 幾多의 制限下의 僅히 一部 有産者에게 개방하얏든 政治參與의 權利를 新히 一千 數百 萬이나 되는 無産大衆에까지 擴張식혓다. 작년까지도 普選論者를 反逆視하든, 貴族院의 頑老들과 또 普選阻止에 專力하야 오든 政友會가 이에 찬성치 아니치 못한 것을 보면 時代變化의 위력이란 것을 驚嘆치 안을 수가 업다. 그리하야 日本의 法治的 中心點은 少數의 特權階級과 밋 資本主義的 旣成政黨의 手를 떠나서 早晩間 無産政黨의 勃興을 보게 되얏다. 赤色 露國과의 國交恢復과＜28＞ 普選法의 成立(두가지가 비록 不徹底하나마) 今年 日本의 外交는 內政上에 二大收穫인 것을 몬저 承認하야둔다. 그런대 원래가 附與하기 실흔 參政權이 擴張이기 때문에 또 新政黨의 成興으로 自家의 舊勢力 失墜를 畏忌하는 까닭에 旣成政黨들은 百方으로 新選擧法의 속히 實施되기를 피하랴 한다. 그뿐 아니라 社會政策을 加味한 稅制整理이니 혹은 疲罷한 農村振興策이니 또는 敎育機關의 확장과 鐵道, 河川, 港灣 등 事業을 香餌로 삼아 新選擧法에 의한 有權者들의 환심을 買하기에 汲汲하는 貌樣이다. 그럼으로 昨年은 日本旣成政黨에 取하야 煩悶과 懊惱가 절정에 달한 때이다. 憲政會와 絶綠하고 聯立內閣으로부터 遂出된 政友會가 政本合同이 成就되기 전에는 正面突擊을 될 수 잇는데까지 피하랴하고 종래 憲政會와 不共戴天의 關係에 잇든 上院의 硏究會와 下院의 政友本黨이 혹은 政策의 공통이니 혹은 政權의 分配이니 하는 口實下에 憲政會의 支持하는 現 內閣에게 호의를 보히여 消極的으로 援助할 형세까지 보히는 것은 이것이 모다 議會의 解散으로부터 新히 擡頭하랴는 無産階級議員의 速現을 阻止하랴는 魂膽에 不外한 것이다. 만일 금번에 第51回 議會를 解散한다 하면 아모리 보기 실혀도 第52回 議會에는 異色議員이 出現할 것이 旣定의 사실이다. 그러나 해산만 업스면 現 議員들의 壽命은 大正 17년까지는 維持될 것이오. 此間에 旣成政黨들이 苟且한 野合과 姑息한 妥協을 행한다하면 2,3년간은 政權이 다른대로 가지 안을 것이다. 즉 그들은 공동의 新敵을 두려워하는 점에 공통하야 滿滿한 戰鬪心을 抑止하고 잇다. 그럼으로 急激한 의외의 변화가 업는 한에 次期議會는 解散이 업슬것으로 보는 것이 正鵠을 失치 안은 觀測이다. 假使 憲政會의 第一 畏厭하는 政本合同이 성립된다 하야도 이것은 憲政會가 실흘 뿐이오 政權은 슝연히 資本의 閥의 대표자인 旣成政黨이 가짐으로 政界의 中心勢力에는 變動이 업는 때문임으로 당연히 解散을 行할 憲政會가 口頭의 大言狀語에는 반대로 政友本黨에게 秋波를 보내며 硏究會에게 媚謟하는 이유가 명백하야 진다. 이와 가티 旣成政黨들이 自家의 運命을 直覺하고 예방에 노력하는 他面에
