**Summary**

This article from the November 1, 1925 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine provides a list of major media organizations operating in Seoul (Keijō) at the time. It details the key leadership and editorial staff for four daily newspapers: Tonga Ilbo, Sidae Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, and the government-affiliated Maeil Sinbo. It also mentions the bi-weekly *Chosŏn chi Kwang* and two monthly magazines, *Sinmin* (associated with the Government-General) and *Sisa P'yŏngnon* (linked to the pro-Japanese Kungmin Hyŏphoe and the late Min Wŏnsik). For historians, this piece offers a snapshot of the media landscape in colonial Korea during the mid-1920s, revealing the prominent figures associated with each publication, including notable nationalist figures and writers alongside those connected to the colonial administration or pro-Japanese groups. The mention of recent personnel changes at the *Chosŏn Ilbo* and the explicit links of *Sinmin* and *Sisa P'yŏngnon* to the Government-General and pro-Japanese organizations respectively are particularly interesting.
