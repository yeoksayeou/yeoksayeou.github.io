謝告

In our circumstances of struggling for life amidst harsh winds and cold frost, what special joy or sorrow could we feel about suspension versus resumption of publication? Nevertheless, we had hoped to enjoy this resumed issue with you all just as it was, but it has been cruelly confiscated (押收) yet again. Though unsatisfactory, we present it to you as an extra issue. (Upper volume's sections 9 through 13 and lower volume's sections 1 through 16 deleted). <9>

**Summary**

This short notice from the editors of the magazine *Kaebyŏk* (개벽) expresses resignation and frustration over the repeated confiscation of their publication by authorities. Despite their difficult circumstances ("harsh winds and cold frost"), they had hoped to release the resumed issue (No. 63) but it was seized, forcing them to publish an altered version with significant sections deleted. For historians, this notice starkly illustrates the severe censorship and suppression faced by Korean publications under Japanese colonial rule in the 1920s, highlighting the constant struggle for freedom of expression and the methods used by colonial authorities to control information.
