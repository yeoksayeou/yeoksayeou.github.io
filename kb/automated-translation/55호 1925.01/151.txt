New Spring Readers' Literature Grand Recruitment
□Era of Literature for the Common People□ / To popularize literary taste generally and welcome new writers of the new era

The time has come when literature, rather than merely having great power (權力) to purify human emotion or sentiment, takes a firm basis (根據) in human life and exerts a great influence on the improvement of thought (思想向上) and the development of life (生活啓發). Therefore, literature should not be a diversionary tool (轉用的 手段) for comforting the anguish (苦憫) of a segment or portion of the wise (智者) but must become the common literature of the entire populace (全民衆) or all humankind (全人類). However, for us currently, what is called literature, apart from being the work of a portion of the intellectual class (智識階級), is generally not even known how to be loved or understood (理解). Stemming from this, our *Kaebyŏk* (開闢), while sacrificing page space (紙面) as much as possible, will open its pages starting from the February issue to generally promote the literary taste (文學的 趣味) of our readers (記者 諸氏 - *Note: 記者 likely refers to readers/contributors here, not just journalists*) and thereby hone the creative talent (創作的 才能) of our readers, in order to welcome the new writers (新進作家) that Chosŏn (朝鮮) seeks. Do not miss this opportunity (機會) and please send in your manuscripts (原稿).

Types: Novel (小說), Drama (戱曲), Poetry (詩), Short Piece (小品)
Regulations: Within 250 lines of 23 characters each.
Deadline: Within the 10th of each month.

Note
Please write only in Korean script (朝鮮文) as much as possible, use few Chinese characters (漢字), and also refrain from messy handwriting (亂書). Also, please use punctuation (句節) as much as possible. Manuscripts will absolutely not be returned (返還). <98>

**Summary**

This announcement from the January 1, 1925 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine calls for reader submissions for a new literature section. It argues literature should be for everyone ("common people"), influencing thought and life, not just an elite pastime. *Kaebyŏk* is opening its pages to encourage readers' literary taste and creative skills, seeking to discover new writers for Korea ("Chosŏn"). The notice provides submission details (genres, length, deadline) and requests manuscripts primarily in Korean script (Hangul) with minimal Hanja and clear punctuation. For historians, this piece is interesting as it explicitly advocates for a popular, vernacular Korean literature during the colonial period, reflecting cultural nationalism and the desire to broaden literary participation beyond the educated elite. The emphasis on minimizing Hanja and using Korean script is a notable aspect of the burgeoning modern Korean literary movement.

新春讀者文藝大募集
□庶民的 文學時代□/文學的 趣味를 一般으로 普及하게 하며 新時代의 新進作家를 歡迎하기 爲하야
文藝라는 것이 人生의 感情 혹은 情緖를 純化시키는 데에, 큰 權力을 가젓다는 것 보다도, 文藝는 人生生活에 確平한 根據를 두어가지고, 思想向上과 生活啓發에도 多大한 影響을 주는 時代는 왓다. 그런故로 文學은 智者들의 苦憫을 慰勞하려는 一部 혹 一圃의 轉用的 手段이 안이라 全民衆 는 全人類의 普通的 文藝가 되여야겟다. 그러나 우리에게는 現今 文藝라하는 것은 智識階級 一部의 짓인 外에는 一般으로는 文藝을 사랑할 줄도 몰으고, 理解할줄도 몰낫다 이에 비롯해서 우리 開闢은 할 수 잇는대로 紙面을 犧牲하면서도, 一般으로 記者 諸氏의 文學的 趣味를 增進케 하며 러서 讀者 諸氏의 創作的 才能을 熟練케 함으로써 朝鮮이 찾는 新進作家를 歡迎하기 爲해서 二月號부터 紙面을 公開하겟으니 機會를 일치말고 原稿를 보내주시요.
