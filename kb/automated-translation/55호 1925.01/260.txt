Five Fragments
						Yi Sang-hwa (李相和)
Crimson Sound - Preface to "Crimson Sound" -

From the dark night, swallowing this century,
Dreaming of darkness again, the dozing night of Chosŏn-
Into this night, like a lump of oblivion,
Sunlight cannot shine,
And the word of God (한우님) sounds like the grumbling of a full stomach.

Night even in the daytime - Night even at nighttime -
The wretched spirit, squeezed out from the darkness of that night,
Does not even know the name "necklace of Light,"
Like a drunken blind man walking a lo-ng road,
On the staggering footsteps, blood flows! <62>

The Most Sorrowful Prayer-Wish - Seeing the Kando Immigrants

Ah, they go, they go, they are chased away.
To Kando (間島) and the Liaodong (遼東) plains within this haze,
Clutching their starving lives, they are chased away.
Even if they eat mud for rice, even if they endure the sun's rays,
If only they could have anything, they could bind themselves to sweet sleep-
Oh Creator who made humans, quickly,
Rather take away these starving lives!

Ah, we live, we live, we live drunk.
To Seoul and the countryside within self-abandonment,
Lest our bruised lives depart, we live drunk.
Embracing a silent stone in the dark night,
If we cried tears of blood, our sorrow might be eased-
Oh Creator who made humans, quickly,
Rather kill these drunken lives and throw them away!
- From "The Stolen Soul" (빼앗긴 魂) -

Night in a Poor Village
Through the hole in the paper window <63>
Lined up, dozing,
The flickering oil lamp-
Like an old eyeball shunning the sunlight,
It suffers, suffers outside the world.

Ah, my heart,
Is this how humans
Yearn for Light-
Reaching the front of the straw-mat door that lacks even a wall,
Hearing it, weeping, it turned around.
- From "The Gift of Escape" (遁竄의 선물) -

Ridicule
Even covering oneself
With thick blankets piled up,
Still cold,
On this winter night,
Treading the frozen road,
The itinerant peddler, the bundle merchant,
Going over the pass to the village <64>
To see the market,
Muttering,
The gasping breath,
Ah-
Looking at me,
It passes by, mocking me.
- Ibid -

Mother's Smile
Until the day ends,
Wandering everywhere-
Even with a weary body,
Even with a withered heart,
In the dark kitchen,
My mother cooking rice,
Her gentle smile, smiling at me.

When I was young and suckled <65>
Upon her lap,
Holding me gently,
Not even knowing old age,
That smile, I think I still
See it, and so
A little of the loneliness
Disappears, and from there
A subtle joy finally comes.
- From "Mute Song" (벙어리노래) -

**Summary**

This collection, "Five Fragments," by Yi Sang-hwa presents a series of bleak and poignant vignettes reflecting the suffering and despair of Korean life under Japanese colonial rule in the 1920s. The poems depict a pervasive darkness ("Night even in the daytime"), the desperate emigration of starving people to Manchuria (Kando and Liaodong), the crushing poverty of rural villages, the feeling of being mocked even by the hardship of others, and a final, fragile solace found in a mother's enduring smile. Historians would find the poems valuable for their raw portrayal of the psychological and material conditions of colonial Korea, particularly the theme of forced migration ("Kando Immigrants"), the sense of national despair and hopelessness ("Crimson Sound," "The Most Sorrowful Prayer-Wish"), and the stark depictions of poverty ("Night in a Poor Village," "Ridicule"). The language itself, with its older spellings, also provides linguistic insights into the period.

緋音-『緋音』의 序詞-

이 世紀를 물고 너흐는, 어둔 밤에서
다시 어둠을 꿈꾸노라 조우는 조선의 밤-
忘却뭉텅이가튼, 이 밤 속으론
해쌀이 비초여 오지도 못하고
한우님의 말슴이, 배부른 군소리로 들리노라

나제도 밤-밤에도 밤-
그 밤의 어둠에서 씀여난, 뒤직이가튼 신령은,
光明의 목거지란 일홈도 모르고
술 취한 장님이 머-ㄴ 길을 가듯
비틀거리는 자욱엔, 피물이 흐른다!＜62＞

가장 悲痛한 祈慾-間島移民을 보고

아, 가도다, 가도다, 쪼처가도다.
이즘 속에 잇는 間島와 遼東벌로
주린 목숨 움켜쥐고, 쪼처가도다
진흙을 밥으로, 햇채를 마서도
마구나, 가젓드면, 단잠은 얽맬 것을-
사람을 만든 검아, 하로일즉
차라로 주린 목숨 빼서 가거라!

아, 사노라 사노라, 취해 사노라
自暴 속에 잇는 서울과 시골로
멍든 목숨 행여, 갈가, 취해 사노라
어둔 밤 말업는 돍을 안고서
피울음을 울드면, 셜음은 풀릴 것을-
사람을 만든 검아, 하로일즉
차라로 취한 목숨, 죽여 바리라!
-『빼앗긴 魂』 가온데서-
