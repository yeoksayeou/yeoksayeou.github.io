千態萬相 (Myriad Aspects)

November 16th
China's Zhang Zuolin (張作霖), Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥), and Lu Yongxiang (盧永祥) held a conference together with Duan Qirui (段祺瑞) to discuss issues of cabinet reorganization, army unification, and the Yangtze River.
In Tokyo, Japan, over 50 members of a leftist group held an important meeting and discussed causing a major political disturbance using the opening of the 50th Diet session.

November 17th
China's Mr. Sun Wen (孫文) entered Shanghai from Guangdong.
The newly appointed US Ambassador to Japan, Mr. Bancroft, entered Tokyo.
The conference for the prohibition of harmful drugs, hosted by the League of Nations, opened in Geneva with the participation of 48 countries.
Over 40 influential figures in Beijing, China, organized the National Salvation Association (國民求治會).
Opposite Chasŏng in North P'yŏngan Province, 2 members of the Independence Army were killed by police officers.
Italian Lower House member Mr. Giolitti and former Prime Minister Mr. Orlando proposed opposition to the government's bill for suppressing speech and revising the constitution.

November 18th
Independence Party Cheonma Unit (天摩隊) leader Ch'oe Sihŭng (崔時興) was again sentenced to death at the P'yŏngyang Review Court.
Messrs. Qi Xieyuan (齊爕元), Sun Chuanfang (孫傳芳), and Wu Peifu (吳佩孚) of China's Yangtze Alliance Faction (長江聯盟泒) declared the establishment of a military government in Wuchang.
The Czechoslovak Republic decided to recognize Russia early in the new year.
Mr. Ramek became the new Prime Minister of Austria.

November 19th
Hong Kyŏngsik (洪敬植) and his group, members of the Ŭitongbu (統義府) Kyŏngsŏng Branch, were transferred to the Prosecutor's Office.
As a result of a conference, China's four giants including Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) decided on the subjugation of Jiangsu by Lu Yongxiang (盧永祥), Hu Jingyi (胡景翼), and Sun Yue (孫岳).
Mr. Sun Wen (孫文) narrowly escaped an assassination attempt (gunshot, 銃聲) by a ruffian in Shanghai but was unharmed.
Mr. Wu Peifu (吳佩孚) arrived in Dingzhou (定州) and established a general headquarters.

November 20th
A large fire broke out at the P'ungguk Flour Mill and Saitō Rice Mill in Kyŏngchŏng, Yongsan, Kyŏngsŏng, causing damages of about 1 million Yen.
The executive committee meeting of the All-Russia Labor-Farmer Union passed a resolution impeaching Naval Commissar Trotsky (트르츠키), stating that Mr. Trotsky's published history of the Bolshevik revolution criticized Lenin (레닌).
At the Chinnamp'o Commercial School, 50 students were suspended under the pretext of being leftists.
The American Federation of Labor convention declared the maintenance of its anti-Japanese stance.
The British Governor of Sudan and Commander-in-Chief of the Egyptian Army, Sir Lee Stack, was attacked and killed by an Egyptian nationalist.

November 21st
British Foreign Secretary Sir Austen Chamberlain refused to ratify the Anglo-Russian treaty.
In Cairo, Egypt, 36 suspects were arrested for the assassination of the British Governor Stack.
The British Foreign Secretary declared the truth about the Zinoviev (지노비에프) letter, which caused Anglo-Russian problems, to the Russian representative Rakovsky (라코우스커).
In Tokyo, Japan, incidents occurred including the arrest of Shibata (柴田), who secretly sent seditious documents to the army nationwide, the discovery of the body of Nanba Daisuke (難波大助), and the arrest of suspects in the Imperial Garden (御苑內) explosives incident.

November 22nd
While a train was running near Yongsan Station in Kyŏngsŏng, gunpowder belonging to a passenger (乘客) exploded, resulting in 7 serious and minor injuries.
Mr. Sun Wen (孫文) departed Shanghai for Japan.
The Domingos Santos cabinet was formed in Portugal (*萄牙).
The Turkish Ismet (이스멋트) cabinet resigned, and the Fethi Bey (*지베) cabinet was formed.
China's Provisional Chief Executive Duan (段執政) and Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥) entered Beijing. Britain demanded 500,000 pounds compensation, an apology, and the prohibition of all future political demonstrations from the Egyptian government regarding the assassination of the Governor of Sudan.
Students from various universities and colleges in Japan launched a large-scale movement opposing military education nationwide.

November 23rd
Robbers Kim Pyŏngsan (金炳山), Yi Ch'angsik (李昌植), and others, totaling 4, who were rampant in Yongsan, Kyŏngsŏng, were arrested by the Yongsan police.
Mr. Sun Wen (孫文) entered Kobe via Nagasaki.
Egypt agreed to Britain's demands regarding compensation, punishment of culprits, and apology, but rejected those concerning the permanent status of foreign advisors and military issues.
The P'unggi Tenant Farmers' Union resolved to denounce oppressive landlords.

November 24th
Negotiations for an Anglo-German trade treaty took place.
China's Mr. Duan Qirui (段祺瑞) formally assumed the office of Provisional Chief Executive and announced the five articles of the Chief Executive's powers (1. Concurrently hold the position of Marshal of the Army and Navy 2. Right to appoint and dismiss civil and military officials and convene State Council meetings 3. Directly supervise all ministries, placing two ministers in each ministry, one for departmental affairs and one to attend State Council meetings 4. Convene the State Council to resolve issues of the National Assembly and Constitution 5. Convene the State Council within a short period, with resolutions executed by the Chief Executive).
The Egyptian government handed over 500,000 pounds in compensation money to the British representative. The Zaghloul (자클) cabinet resigned, and the Ziwar Pasha (지왈파사) cabinet was formed.

November 25th
China's cabinet was formed by order of the Provisional Chief Executive (Provisional Chief Executive and Premier Duan Qirui (段祺瑞), Foreign Affairs Tang Shaoyi (唐紹儀), Internal Affairs Xi Xinlan (襲心湛), Finance Li Sihao (李思悎), Army Wu Guangxin (吳光新), Navy Lin Jianzhang (林建章), Justice Zhang Shijian (章士劍), Education Wang Jiuling (王九齡), Agriculture and Commerce Yang Shukan (柳庶堪), Communications Ye Gongchuo (葉恭綽)).
In Kyŏngsŏng, over 40 ideologues gathered and organized the Pukp'unghoe (北風會 - North Wind Society).<117>
In Egypt, martial law was declared, and 500 British troops landed in Alexandria.

November 26th
China's Mr. Tang Shaoyi (唐紹儀) refused to accept the appointment as Minister of Foreign Affairs.
The Russian representative for the Franco-Russian trade negotiations committee, Mr. Klyshko (쿨리친), arrived in Paris.
The diplomatic corps of various countries stationed in China reached a consensus to recognize the new Chinese government as the de facto government.
Egypt's protest against Britain was rejected by the League of Nations.

November 27th
Near Hanp'o Station on the Kyŏngŭi Line, a train collision occurred, resulting in over 20 serious and minor injuries.
A coal mine (炭坑) explosion occurred, resulting in over 20 casualties.
Important points of the Anglo-German trade treaty were agreed upon.
Over 1,500 tenant farmers in Anp'yŏng-myŏn, Ŭisŏng-gun, resolved to fight to the death against unreasonable landlords.

November 28th
The diplomatic corps of various countries in Beijing visited Provisional Chief Executive Duan (段執政) all at once.
Russia issued a diplomatic note in response to the British official communication, denying the facts and stating it could not suppress the activities of the Third International.
China's Provisional Chief Executive Duan (段執行) invited the ministers of various countries.
In Khartoum, Egypt, British and Egyptian soldiers clashed, resulting in numerous casualties on both sides.
Kim Haesan (金海山), leader of the Daedonghoe (大同會) active in the independence movement in the Dunhua (敦化) area of Jilin, was arrested by the Japanese consulate in Kando (間島).
The Opium Conference being held in Geneva stalled due to opposition from the Indian representative to the US proposal.
The trial of Kim Yongbok (金龍福) and others from the Independence Group (獨立團) in Ch'ŏngsŏngjin (淸城鎭), Ŭiju, opened at the Sinŭiju District Court.

November 30th
Representatives of the Oriental Development Company (東拓) tenant farmers in Sain-myŏn, Pongsan-gun, Messrs. Yi Wŏnik (李源翊), O T'aekyong (吳宅龍), and Chang Yongjin (張龍鎭), went up to Kyŏngsŏng to petition (陳情次) the ODC branch (支店) and the Governor-General for Political Affairs.
China's Sun Wen (孫文), staying in Kobe, Japan, departed (出發) for Tianjin.
Representatives from 11 provinces including Jiangsu and Hubei in China gathered at the Guangxi Hall (廣西會舘) and resolved on self-governance for each province.
Over 40 female workers at the P'yŏngyang Rice Selection Mill initiated a strike over wage reduction issues.

December 1st
As a result of administrative restructuring by the Government-General of Chōsen - Korean governors Yi Kyuwan (李圭完), Yun Kapyŏng (尹甲炳), and Wŏn Ŭngsang (元應常) resigned, and Sŏk Chinhyŏng (石鎭衡), Chang Hŏnsik (張憲植), and Pak Yŏngch'ŏl (朴榮喆) became new governors.

December 2nd
Former President of China, Cao Kun (曹銀), attempted suicide by poison but failed.
The Military Governor of Jiangxi province, Fang Benren (方本仁), declared independence.
Mr. Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) came to Tianjin from Beijing.
Mr. Lloyd George was elected leader of the British Liberal Party in the House of Commons.
The Chinese government decided on the organization of a national assembly. On the same day, the government declared its opposition to the theory proposed by foreign powers of applying the Dawes Plan to China.
A major fire broke out in Hongch'ŏn town, completely burning down 9 houses.

December 3rd
Former Prime Minister MacDonald was elected leader of the British Labour Party in the House of Commons.
The committees for the Russo-German treaty exchanged draft agreements.
The International Opium Conference approved the Japan-Britain agreement.
Armed independence fighters appeared in areas like Hŭich'ŏn and Ch'osan, cutting telephone lines and taking 160 Yen in cash.
Mr. Chŏng Ilsa (鄭一史), who had specialized in X-rays while studying in the US for 16 years, returned to Korea.

December 4th
A large fire broke out in Honmachi (本町), Kyŏngsŏng, causing about 30,000 Yen in damages and killing one elderly woman by burning.
Mr. Sun Wen (孫文) arrived in Tianjin. A fire also broke out in Wonsan, burning down 7-8 houses.
Labor-Farmer (Soviet) Ambassador to France, Krasin (크라신), arrived in Paris.
A major fire broke out in Sŏktae-ri, Hakil-myŏn (學一面), T'ongch'ŏn-gun, completely burning down over 40 houses.

December 5th
Japanese student groups launched a major protest movement against the Ministry of Education, citing the wrongfulness of military education and the prohibition of speeches.
Egyptian elementary school student groups and 130 representatives launched a large-scale anti-British movement.
Spain approved the abolition of the military government.
The Estonian military court sentenced 17 Communist Party members among 300 rebels to death.
A major fire broke out in Kaŭm-myŏn, Ŭisŏng-gun, completely burning down over 90 houses.

December 6th
A large fire broke out at the Saitō Rice Mill in Manseki-chō (萬石町), Inch'ŏn, burning down 8-9 neighboring houses and causing over 30,000 Yen in damages.
Various ideologues in Kyŏngsŏng organized the Socialist League (社會主義同盟).
Italian Prime Minister Mussolini (뭇소리니) gave a major speech defending himself in the Senate, and a vote of confidence passed.
As a prerequisite for troop reduction supervision (裁兵督辦), Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) resigned from his post as Inspector General of the Three Eastern Provinces (東三省巡閱使). In Sacramento, USA, an anti-Japanese movement arose with a new bill, forming a boycott league against Japanese agricultural products.
French police conducted large-scale arrests of Communist Party members nationwide.
Mr. Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) sent a telegram nationwide halting military actions.

December 7th
The Yongsan Police Station in Kyŏngsŏng arrested 5 robbers who had recently been appearing frequently in the vicinity of Kyŏngsŏng.
Over 100 cotton mill workers in Masan went on strike over wage issues and submitted 10 demands.
The French Minister of the Interior issued deportation orders for 60 foreign Communist Party members.

December 8th
The Government-General of Chōsen announced its decision to move the South Kyŏngsang Provincial Office (慶南道廳) to Pusan starting April 1, 1925, prompting a large opposition movement from the citizens of Chinju.
Philippine Governor-General Wood refused the Philippine Independence Bill.
The general election in Germany resulted in the Social Democratic Party gaining dominance.
In Changbai County (長白縣), 5 members of the Independence Army were arrested by Japanese police while trying to cross the river.<118>

**Summary**

This news digest from the January 1925 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine covers international and domestic events from mid-November to early December 1924. International news focuses heavily on political instability and power shifts in China (involving figures like Zhang Zuolin, Feng Yuxiang, Duan Qirui, Sun Wen, Wu Peifu), Anglo-Egyptian tensions following the assassination of Sir Lee Stack, European politics (Italy, Czechoslovakia, Austria, UK, Germany, France, Russia), and international conferences (League of Nations drug conference, Opium Conference). Domestically within Korea, it reports on the activities and suppression of the independence movement (arrests, killings, trials of figures like Ch'oe Sihŭng, Hong Kyŏngsik, Kim Haesan, Kim Yongbok), social issues like tenant farmer disputes (P'unggi, Ŭisŏng, Pongsan), labor strikes (P'yŏngyang, Masan), student activism (Chinnamp'o school suspensions), fires (Kyŏngsŏng, Hongch'ŏn, Wonsan, T'ongch'ŏn, Ŭisŏng, Inch'ŏn), crimes (robberies in Yongsan), and administrative changes by the Japanese Government-General (reshuffling of Korean governors, relocation of the South Kyŏngsang provincial office).

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is significant for its documentation of continued Korean independence activities both within Korea (Chasŏng, Hŭich'ŏn, Ch'osan) and in Manchuria (Kando/Jiandao, Changbai), alongside the Japanese colonial authorities' suppression efforts (arrests, killings, trials). The mention of specific groups like the Cheonma Unit and Ŭitongbu, and individuals like Ch'oe Sihŭng and Kim Haesan, provides glimpses into the organized resistance. Additionally, reports on tenant farmer protests against landlords and the Oriental Development Company, labor strikes, the formation of socialist groups (Pukp'unghoe, Socialist League), and student issues highlight the growing social unrest and ideological movements within colonial Korea. The administrative changes, particularly the relocation of the provincial capital causing local protest, also reflect the dynamics of colonial governance.
