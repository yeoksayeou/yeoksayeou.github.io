南信北通 (News from South and North)
Anju Communication
Ilgwansaeng (一貫生)

If someone were to ask about the state of thought among the youth of Anju, one could undoubtedly answer that the youth of Anju are those possessing a certain ideology characteristic of a transitional period, like new sprouts in winter who have yet to savor what social movements truly are. However, even among the youth who embraced certain past ideologies, a tendency towards gradual change has recently become quite apparent. Now, let me show the classification of Anju youth by ideology as of the end of the past Kapcha year (1924), roughly as follows:

One. A certain number of ideological youth.
One. Approximately 60 masquerading-non-ideological youth (like a portion of certain youth association members, those who make noise about fasting for some cause).
One. Approximately 10 masquerading youth (those who outwardly speak of public benefit or some ideology but inwardly advocate for their own full stomachs and some kind of accommodation).
One. Approximately 15 fawning youth (those who hastily initiate welcomes for certain distinguished guests or farewells for certain heads of criminal affairs departments through advertisements or circulars at the slightest provocation; those who become attendants to the propertied class, fearing only that they might not protect them enough).
One. Approximately 10 name-seeking youth (those involved as something in some preparatory committee or as promoters of some company, such that a foot-long name card cannot list all their titles; those who consequently end up with a dragon's head but a snake's tail).
One. Approximately 5 bat-like youth (those who appear as sympathizers of the officials when near the government and as sympathizers of the people when among the populace).
One. Approximately 20 youth living as if dreaming or dead (those dreaming of making a fortune overnight without labor; those indulging in amusement with prostitutes left and right; those who think high-interest moneylending is all there is; those craving office, campaigning headlong hoping to become some council member).
One. Approximately 80 unaffiliated youth (youth and laborers who have yet to keenly taste the flavor of humanity).

Then who will bring the light of rebirth to the ideological world of Anju youth? Undoubtedly, we must place our hopes in the ideological youth or the unaffiliated youth. On the occasion of the new Ŭlch'uk year (1925), I earnestly hope that the buds of new life among Anju youth will quickly sprout.

Wŏnsan Short Critique
Kim Ch'un'gang (金春岡)

Originally, the Wŏnsan prefectural authorities have been so cold towards what they call Wŏnsan-ri, where Koreans reside, that there is hardly any need to speak of it anew now. However, regarding the prefectural administration towards Wŏnsan Koreans, although various facilities like land reclamation from the sea, a clinic charging actual costs, road issues, and others currently exist, the disparity between Korean residential areas and Japanese residential areas is too severe to be overlooked. Do the prefectural authorities know that the voices of criticism and attack from the general prefectural populace are loud? I request, if you hear such complaints, how about showing even a little sign of being moved?

It is also necessary to upgrade the Commercial School in Wŏnsan from the current 3-year system to a 5-year system. Although one might say this is not directly related to Wŏnsan since it is a Provincial School, the majority of entering students are children of Wŏnsan residents. That is, Wŏnsan is where the interests are closely connected. Therefore, this goal of upgrading should also be achieved through the efforts of the Wŏnsan populace. It is indeed an immediate issue for Wŏnsan that cannot be neglected.

This may seem like old news, but it's so ridiculous that I'll jot down a few words. A certain gentleman, Mr. OOO, said to be a teacher at a certain high school in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), came to his wife's family home in Wŏnsan during this summer vacation. When his wife's family, despite their difficult circumstances, bought and sent a block of ice because their granddaughter's husband had arrived, that gentleman supposedly refused to eat it, asking if he should eat something sent by laborers. Hehe, this gentleman is truly a *yangban* (aristocrat).

Regarding the inadvisability of relocating the clinic charging actual costs, Messrs. Kim X-sik of the Wŏnsan Youth Association, XX Su of the Labor Association, and others spoke to Prefectural Governor Niwa.<119> Niwa's response was reportedly that it was unavoidable and done so only for the time being. It is difficult to guess how long "for the time being" will last. Rather, in this critic's opinion, entrusting the management to a public organization like the Wŏnsan Youth Association seems like it would be a good policy for the prefecture and would reduce the populace's complaints.

It is utterly shameful that in Wŏnsan, where there is a population of over twenty thousand consisting solely of us Koreans, there isn't a single school of higher level. We should educate our own children with our own hands, without expecting anything from anyone else. How about the twenty thousand brethren uniting as one body and striving to establish one school of higher level?

The Wŏnsan Labor Association established a simple eatery last winter for fellow laborers exposed to the wind and sleeping under the dew. Now that they've settled accounts this year, there was reportedly some loss, leading a few people to criticize them. Since it was established for those who labor, isn't it the usual state of affairs that it wouldn't turn a profit? Also, since the association already established this facility, despite the losses, they decided to improve its scale compared to before and focus even more on kindness and diligence. This is indeed a thankful enterprise for the general fellow laborers.

Kaesŏng's Current State from an Ideological Perspective
Kim Hyŏnggŭn (金亨根)

If one were to evaluate Kaesŏng, which hears itself called a somewhat safe zone economically, from an ideological perspective, it's regrettable but one can only say it is 'zero'. Ideological life, needless to say, gives life-giving sweet rain to us who are drying up day by day; in other words, it combines the individual psychologies of all people and shows the target direction and path forward.

So, what is the state of Kaesŏng's ideological world? Kaesŏng, first awakened from its deep slumber by the sounds of "Manse!" (Hurrah!) 7 years ago, seemed poised to create some ideological waves, organizing social movement bodies and pretending to do this and that, making quite a commotion. But what could be done when Kaesŏng couldn't shed its smelly conservatism and the individualistic thinking arising from petty merchant-ism? Thus, the grand plans for 100 years, proclaimed with soaring spirits, all returned to emptiness. Recently, it has reached a state where, without any guiding shore, it drifts aimlessly like a rudderless, compassless ship on a vast, boundless ocean, tossed about by the blowing wind and crashing waves – this is the current ideological world of Kaesŏng. In this great society of Kaesŏng, where everything wanders in a midday nap, stagnating and further stagnating, there isn't a single ideologue or person of character capable of leadership. Even the Youth Association is utterly powerless, serving as nothing more than an after-meal chat room for the bourgeoisie, a worn-out chess board. Is this not Kaesŏng's lamentable point, something those with blood cannot calmly overlook? As the ideological world is thus 'zero', the rampancy (*) of various organizations is severe, and demons freely appear and disappear on the great road to suicide. Is this not a Kaesŏng of walking corpses? Because everything is like this, it has become excluded from all movements. Even Korea's special police, with their extremely sensitive nerves, have attached the term "Special Safe Zone" to it. Eventually, even the worthy and unworthy sorts from religious organizations have started bragging (*)!

Ah! Among the over 100,000 masses of Kaesŏng, you young people with red courage and some thought! Let's rise up! Let's stir up a new spirit in the new year! The abundant future of the new Korea and the new world to come rest solely with the youth. And let us not be mere talkers without action. You people with money! Let's stop playing the role of pseudo-gentlemen wearing false masks with just a few pennies! Let's also donate money that is nominally contributed! And starting this year, let's shed our rotten masks and live a truly human life! Reckless behavior (*) is not the duty of life. Young people! And people with purpose! Arise! Let's move! Thus, this year, let's accomplish all the long-pending tasks, build a proper Youth Hall to use as a public meeting place, and avoid smelling the dirty stench of Western friends. Let's also revive the various associations with a fresh new spirit and become a solid, single bloc! This, I believe, is the only way to revive and awaken the ideologically dying Kaesŏng. <120>

**Summary**

These three dispatches from Anju, Wŏnsan, and Kaesŏng in early 1925 paint a picture of Korean society under Japanese rule, focusing on the state of youth ideology and social issues. The Anju piece categorizes local youth by their perceived ideological engagement, ranging from committed activists to opportunists and the apathetic, expressing hope for genuine change from the dedicated or unaffiliated groups. The Wŏnsan critique highlights administrative neglect by Japanese authorities towards Korean residents, particularly the disparity in facilities compared to Japanese areas, and calls for upgrading the local commercial school and establishing a Korean-funded higher-level school. It also includes anecdotes criticizing elitism and commends the Labor Association's efforts. The Kaesŏng piece laments the city's ideological stagnation, conservatism, and lack of leadership, contrasting its economic stability with intellectual apathy, and issues a fervent call for youth and intellectuals to awaken, unite, shed pretense, and actively build a better future, including constructing a Youth Hall.

Historians might find these articles particularly interesting for their contemporary social commentary. The detailed classification of youth types in Anju offers insight into the perceived spectrum of political and social consciousness among young Koreans. The Wŏnsan piece provides direct evidence of Korean grievances against discriminatory colonial administration and highlights community efforts towards self-reliance in education and social welfare (like the Labor Association's eatery). The Kaesŏng article reflects the frustration of intellectuals with perceived apathy and conservatism in some segments of Korean society, even within a context of broader national consciousness (referencing the 1919 movement), and underscores the internal calls for revitalization and action directed at the Korean community itself. The explicit mention of the "Special Safe Zone" designation by police in Kaesŏng is also revealing.

元來 元山府當局者는 朝鮮人 居住하는 彼所稱 元山里에 對하야는 넘우도 冷情하니까 至今 새삼스럽게 말할 必要도 업거니와 元山 朝鮮人에 對한 府行政은 現在 海面埋立, 實費 診察所, 道路問題를 비롯하야 其他 諸般施設이 잇다하나 朝鮮人 居住地, 日本人居住地의 差別이 넘우도 甚함은 黙過치 못하겟다. 府 當局者야 一般府民의 批難攻擊의 소리가 놉흔 즐을 아는가? 請컨대 이러한 不平을 聞하거던 죠곰이라도 動하는 빗을 뵈임이 엇덜가?

元山에 잇는 商業學校를 現行의 3年制를 5年制度에 昇格함도 必要하다. 此는 道立學校이기 때문에 直接 元山의 關係가 업다해도 過言이 안인 듯 하나 그 入學生徒는 大部分 元山 在住者의 子弟이다. 卽 利害關係가 密接으로 잇슴은 元山 그곳이다. 그러면 이것도 元山府民의 努力에 依하야 昇格의 目的을 達成하여야 하겟다. 卽 元山의 當面의 問題로 閑却치 못할 한 問題이다.

좀 舊聞인 것 갓지마는 넘우도 웃으운 일이니까 두어 字 그적그려 보자. 京城 某 高等學校에 敎諭로 잇다든가 하는 OOO兩班은 今年 夏期休暇에 元山 妻家에 왓셧는데 妻外家에서 外孫女婿가 왓다고 困難한 形便에 어름(氷) 한 덩이를 사셔 보냇더니 그 外孫女婿 兩班이 勞動者가 보낸 것도 어더 먹느냐고 絶對로 먹지를 안핫다든가. 허허 이 兩班이야말로 참말 兩班인 걸.

實費 診察所 移轉의 不可를 元山靑年會 金*植 勞動會 **洙 等 諸氏가 丹羽府尹에게 말햇더*＜119＞ 羽此의 所答이 不得已 當分間만 그리 하엿노라 하얏다나. 그 當分間이란 것은 또 언졔까지나 될나는지 推測키 難하다 차라리 短評者의 마음가태서는 元山靑年會 가튼 公共團軆에 委任經營을 하엿스면 府의 得策도 되고 府民의 不平도 减할 듯 한걸.

우리 朝鮮人뿐 으로만 二萬餘民衆이 잇는 元山에 高等程度의 學校 하나가 업다함은 넘우도 羞恥스러운 것이다. 우리는 누구에게 바랄 것 업시 우리子弟의 敎育은 우리의 손으로 함이 宜當하다. 二萬兄弟가 一軆로 奮鬪하야 高等程度의 學校 하나를 設함이 엇덜넌지.

元山勞動會에서는 風찬露宿하는 勞動同務를 爲하야 昨年 冬브터 筒易食堂을 設하엿든 바 今年에 와셔 決算을 하고 보니 多少의 損害가 잇섯다고 少數의 批難을 加하는 이들이 잇다나. 勞動하는 이를 爲하야 設한 것이니까 利益을 브지 못할 것은 依例의 茶飯事가 안일가. 또 그 會에서는 旣是 設하엿든 機關임으로 損害는 잇다할지라도 從前보다 規模도 刷新하고 더욱 親切叶嚀을 主로 하기로 하엿다. 지참 一般勞働동무를 爲하야 感謝한 事業이다.
