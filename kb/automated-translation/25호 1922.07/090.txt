A Warning to the Landowners of Southern Korea
						Kim Honggi in Chicago

Recently, looking at articles in newspapers and magazines published in Sŏul, it is said that landowners in various regions of Southern Korea (南鮮) are very busy worrying instead of working, saying that there are no workers in the farming villages and that much farmland (田畓) will soon lie fallow. The current worries of the landowners are the worries of the European gentry (兩班) of the 18th century.

About 150 years ago, in various European countries—especially England—the gentry lived well, eating fine food and wearing fine clothes, just by collecting rent (賭租). But as the so-called Industrial Revolution began, the workers who farmed and paid tribute all fled to the cities or ports to work in factories or start their own businesses. Seeing that this was thousands, tens of thousands of times better than farming for the gentry, receiving the treatment of cattle and horses, and barely surviving (救命圖生), they did not return to the farming villages. Thus, these gentry grew frantic with worry as their farmland lay fallow. Similarly, workers in the farming villages of various regions in Southern Korea now work themselves to death all year round farming, only to give two-thirds of the produce to the landowner. They live on the remaining one-third, and by the following spring, they have nothing to eat and suffer terribly, barely managing to stay alive. Who would enjoy this? If one is caught between a rock and a hard place (進退維谷) with nowhere to go, one might have no choice but to endure this harsh life in the farming village until death, unable to end one's own wretched existence. But if there is somewhere else to go, who wouldn't leave? Over across the Korea Strait (朝鮮海峽), the Japanese gentry (兩班) are diligently developing commerce and industry, thus requiring many workers. Since the wages (雇價) of Japanese workers are relatively higher than those of our workers, using Korean workers, except for those skilled in industry, is beneficial in two ways. First, by using Korean workers, they can pay lower wages, thus manufacturing goods <81> cheaply and selling them at high prices. Second, Japanese workers unskilled in industry can immigrate to Korea, buy the fallow farmland cheaply to farm, or engage in other labor, earning two or three times more than they could in Japan. Who would refuse such benefits? Therefore, Japanese industrialists entice the workers living and working in the farming villages of Southern Korea, barely surviving, with all sorts of sweet talk, taking them to Japan as immigrants by the hundreds and thousands each year. Consequently, Korean farmland inevitably lies fallow, and as this farmland lies fallow, the livelihoods of the Japanese workers coming into Korea will become prosperous.

In this way, we become hunchbacks attacked from front and back (안팟 밧곱사둥이), and our stature gradually shrinks. Before long, the Peninsula (半島) will become a land of dwarves. Yet, the so-called landowners just sit holding their long pipes, smoking tobacco, merely worrying without researching any remedial measures (善後의 方針). And the so-called capitalists, in this crucial opportunity, should be making money beget money, thereby promoting the welfare of society as a whole. Instead, they stuff their pockets full of money and play the blind fortune-teller's gambling game (판수노름), unable to see what's right before their eyes. It is no wonder (怪異할 것이 업습니다) that our society lags behind others by almost 200 years.

If this current situation is left as it is without artificial improvement (人爲的 改善), even if that land does not entirely become workplaces for Japanese workers, the agricultural system of the Peninsula will change according to natural law (天然律). Such natural change (텬연변경) requires a long time. Therefore, change will happen one way or another, but if we do not utilize natural selection (天然 淘汰) through human intervention (人爲的으로), our society will endure infinite hardship during the period of this change.

If, after the disturbances of the Kapsin year (甲申年, 1884), our predecessors (先輩) had stared intently (흡뜨고) and added human effort (人爲) following the tendency of our society's natural evolution (天然進化의 傾向), our society would not have suffered such unspeakable hardship (곤난) for the past half-century, nor would we be facing the situation we face today. I worry (염여합니다) that our agriculture might become like this.

From Chicago, United States of North America <82>

**Summary**

The author, writing from Chicago in 1922, warns Korean landowners in Southern Korea (Namsŏn) about the impending crisis caused by farm laborers migrating to Japan for industrial work due to harsh conditions and low pay, mirroring Europe's 18th-century Industrial Revolution aftermath. He criticizes the landowners' passivity and Korean capitalists' inaction, contrasting it with Japanese industrialists actively recruiting Korean labor and benefiting from cheap wages and potentially acquiring fallow Korean land for Japanese settlers. He fears Korea's decline ("land of dwarves") and urges proactive, "artificial" intervention guided by "natural selection" principles to manage this socio-economic shift, lamenting past failures to modernize after the Gapsin Coup (1884). For historians, this article offers valuable insight into colonial labor dynamics, the push factors driving Korean emigration (tenant exploitation), the pull factors from Japan (industrial jobs), the awareness among Korean intellectuals abroad of these transnational issues, and the critique of the Korean elite's perceived failure to adapt, drawing parallels with Western historical processes. The framing of Japanese exploitation alongside Korean elite inaction provides a nuanced perspective on the causes of Korea's socio-economic vulnerability under colonialism.

南鮮 農主에게 警告하노라
						在市俄古 金鴻機
근일에 서울서 발행하는 新聞과 雜誌의 記事를 본즉 南鮮 各地方 農村에 일군이 업서 將次 묵을 田畓이 만켓다고 農主들이 일하기 대신에 걱정하기에 매우 밧부다 합니다. 農主들의 지금 걱정은 18世紀 유롭 各國 兩班들의 걱정입니다.
지금으로부터 150餘 年 前에 유롭 各國- 特別히 英國 兩班들이 賭租만 바다가지고 好衣好食하며 지내다가 所謂 産業革命이 始作되면서 農事지어 바치던 일군들이 다 逃亡하여 都城이나 港口로 가서 工場에서 일하거나 或 스스로 商業에 着手하거나 하고 본즉 兩班을 爲하여 農事지어 바치고 牛馬의 待遇를 바드며 救命圖生하는 이 보담 千倍 만배 나흠으로 다시 農村으로 돌아가지 아니하엿습니다. 그리 하야 이 兩班들은 뎐답을 무키며 걱정하기에 열이 낫섯슴니다. 지금 南鮮 각디방 農村일군들이 죽기를 한하고 일년동안 농사지어서 그 농산의 3分의 2를 農主에게 바치고 그 남어지 1分을 가지고 살다가 그 이듬해 봄에는 먹을 것이 업서 고생고생으로 죽지 못하여 生命을 부지하니 누가 이것을 즐겨하겟습니가. 進退維谷으로 올데 갈데 업스면 할 수 업시 모진 목숨을 스스로 끈흘 수 업서 그대로 그 農村에서 사는 날까지 살려니와 만일 갈 데만 잇스면 누가 아니 가겟습니가. 이에 朝鮮海峽 저편에 日本 량반들이 상업과 공업을 힘써 발달시킴으로 일군을 만이 요구하나 日本일군들의 雇價는 비교덕 우리 일군들의 雇價보다 빗쌈으로 공업에 한숙한 일군 外에는 朝鮮일군을 쓰는 것이 두 가지로 리익이 잇습니다. 첫재 朝鮮일군을 쓰면 雇價를 헐하게 주니까 물건을＜81＞ 헐가로 제조하여 귀한 갑을 밧고 팔 터이며 둘재 工業에 한숙지 못한 日本일군들은 조선으로 移民하여 저 묵을 뎐답을 헐가로 買收하여 농사를 짓게 하거나 다른 사역에 조사하여 그이들이 일본안에서 버는 것보다 2,3倍를 더 벌게 할 터입니다. 이런 리익을 누가 마다겟습니까. 그래서 일본 공업가들이 남선 각 디방 농촌에서 참아 죽지 못하여 살고 일하는 일군들을 만단으로 꾀여 일년에 몃 백명 몃 천명식 移民하여 일본으로 데려간즉 조선사람의 뎐답은 묵울 수 밧게 업고 이 뎐답들이 묵으면 일본으로서 조선에 들어오는 일군들이 생애는 풍족하여질 터입니다.
이러케 우리는 안팟 밧곱사둥이가 되여 키가 점점 졸아집니다. 얼마 되지 아니하여 半島가 난장이 나라이 될 터이지만 소위 농주들은 장죽에 담배나 부처 물고 안저 걱정만 하고 善後의 方針을 연구 아니하며 소위 자본가들은 이런 절대한 긔회에 돈으로 하여금 돈을 벌게 하며 그리함으로 전반사회의 복리를 增進할 터인데 돈을 잔득 주먼이에 올가 집어넛코 눈뜨고 못 보는 판수노름만 하니 우리 사회가 남에게 거의 200년을 뒤진 것이 怪異할 것이 업습니다.
지금 이 현상을 이대로 두고 人爲的 改善을 하지 아니하면 설혹 저 토디가 일본일군들의 일거리가 다 되지 안터라도 天然律에 의지하여 半島 농업제도가 변경될 터이오. 이런 텬연변경은 장구한 시간을 요구합니다. 그럼으로 어찌 하엿던지 변경은 되는데 人爲的으로 天然 淘汰를 리용치 아니한즉 이 변경이 되는 그 동안 우리 사회가 무한한 곤난을 당할 터입니다.
갑신년 전장이후에 우리의 先輩들이 눈을 흡뜨고 우리 사회의 天然進化의 傾向을 딸아 人爲를 더 하엿더면 우리 사회가 말할 수 업는 곤난을 지난 半世紀 동안 그러케 밧지도 안엇슬 터이오. 또 오늘날 만나는 상태도 업섯슬 것입니다. 우리의 농업이 이와 가티 될가 염여합니다.
북미 합중국 쉬카고에서＜82＞
