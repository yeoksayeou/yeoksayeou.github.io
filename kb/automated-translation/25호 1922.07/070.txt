To those who read this special issue

Confiscation! That dreadful news, enough to make one shudder as if struck by malaria even in a dream, has fallen upon this magazine's July issue. Since our first publication until January of this year, this has happened without fail once every three months, so it is not something newly surprising, but merely thinking about how long this situation will continue makes our blood boil uncontrollably. Now, from the main July issue, the sections said to touch upon so-called taboos—namely
Advocacy for Rural Reform ― Yi Sŏnghwan - Pages 19 to 36
The Problem of Laborers in Chosŏn ― Yi Chŭnghŭi - Pages 60 to 67 top section
Observations of Red Russia ― Yŏn'gyŏng Kwagŏk - Pages 72 to 80
—a total of 33 pages have been deleted, and additionally, Anatole France's masterpiece "Thais" (湖水의 女王 - Queen of the Lake) has been translated and appended to the literary section as a supplement.

Brothers, our hearts are sore. It is agonizing. For us, for whom nothing goes as wished even without this… Ah, there is nothing more to say. Even if the bow breaks and the arrows run out, we must only advance.

Brothers, can you read this, understanding our hardships?

Kaebyŏksa<60>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from *Kaebyŏk* magazine informs readers that the July 1922 issue (No. 25) was confiscated by authorities due to "taboo" content. Three specific articles dealing with rural reform, labor issues in Korea (Chosŏn), and observations of Soviet Russia were removed, totaling 33 pages. To compensate, a translated work by Anatole France was added. The editors express deep frustration and sorrow over the repeated censorship but affirm their determination to persevere despite the difficulties. For historians, this text vividly illustrates the harsh reality of Japanese colonial censorship targeting discussions of social reform, labor conditions, and alternative political systems like communism (implied by "Red Russia"). The specific articles cited as problematic offer direct insight into the topics considered subversive by the colonial government in the early 1920s.

本 臨時號를 읽은 이에게
押收! 꿈에 들어도 瘧疾이 떨어질 만한 그 險惡한 消息이 本誌의 7月號 우에 떨어젓습니다. 刱刊以後로 今年 1月에 니르기까지 3個月 마다에 반듯이 한 번式 當한 일이라 새삼스럽게 놀라이는 바도 아니나 다못 이 꼴이 어느 때까지이나 繼續될 것인가를 생각할 때에 피의 줄이 스스로 뜀을 깨닷지 못하겟습니다. 이제 7月號의 本號 中에서 所謂 忌諱에 觸한다는 部分-即
農村改革의 提唱 ― 李晟煥- 19頁至36頁
朝鮮의 勞働者 問題 ― 李烝熙- 60頁至67頁上段
赤色露西亞의 見聞記 ― 燕京過客- 72頁至80頁
의 計 33頁를 削除하고 更히 아니톨·후란스의 名著인 「湖水의 女王」을 譯出하야 附錄의 文藝篇에 添附하엿습니다.
兄弟여 우리의 마음은 압프외다. 괴롭소이다. 그러치 안하도 모든 것이 如意치 안흔 우리로… 아아 더 말할 것이 업습니다. 비록 弓이 折하고 矢가 盡할지라도 나아갈 것 뿐이외다.
兄弟여 우리의 苦辛을 諒하야써 읽어 줄 수가 잇겟습니까.
開闢社＜60＞
