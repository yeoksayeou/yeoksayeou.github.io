Publication Date: 1922년 07월 10일
Article Title: 四日間 (Four Days)
Author: 까-신 (Garsin)
想涉 (Translator: Sangsŏp)
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

Four Days

By Garsin, Translated by Sangsŏp

Vsevolod Mikhailovich Garsin (1855-1888) was one of Russia's great writers. He initially studied at a mining school and enlisted in the army in 1877. It was during his deployment to Turkey that he gained the material for his first masterpiece, this "Four Days." Later, in 1880, he temporarily went mad and received treatment, recovering after two years. He then served as a secretary for a railway conference, married a female doctor, and resumed his literary life. However, in 1888, his madness recurred, and he committed suicide. (Translator's Note)

I haven't forgotten it. At that time, our side was fleeing through the forest. Pushing aside the small branches falling amidst the whizzing bullets, trying to weave through the hawthorn bushes, the bullets became increasingly intense. Something red flickered on the other side of the forest. Sidorov, an eternally young soldier from the 1st Company, stumbled into our line. "Why are you doing that?" I fleetingly wondered amidst the chaos. Just then, in the blink of an eye, that fellow suddenly flopped down, his bewildered eyes wide open, staring blankly at my face, blood gushing from his mouth... Ah, I can see it clearly. That's not the only thing I see clearly. At that moment, I casually noticed we had almost exited the forest. Among the lushly growing hawthorn bushes stood a stout man, about nine *ch'ŏk* tall. Though I was thin and weak, I charged at him with a shout. There was a clicking sound, and something about this big – that's how I thought of it then – flew in, and my ears rang. Realizing he was startled, by the time I regained my senses, the enemy soldier <4> let out a cry and got caught in the dense hawthorn bushes. He could have turned back if he wanted to, but in his excessive fear, he placed one foot into the thorny tangle of branches and struggled anxiously. With a single strike (一擊), I knocked his rifle away and ruthlessly thrust my bayonet. Hearing him make a strange sound, not quite a beast's bark (吠) nor a groan, I fled. Our side was yelling and shouting, caught between fleeing and firing. As we broke out of the forest into the open field, just as I thought I had fired perhaps two or three shots, suddenly the shouts grew even louder, and everyone scattered and ran. While everyone else fled, I... I was still in that same spot. Eh? I thought. Even stranger, suddenly all sounds ceased as if everything had died. There were no shouts, no gunshots; all sounds vanished, and only something blue appeared before me, which I suppose must have been the sky. Shortly after, even that blue became blurry...

Hey, this is strange. It seems I'm lying face down. The only thing blocking my view is a patch of ground the size of my palm. A few blades of small grass, an ant crawling downwards along one of them, and a handful of debris, perhaps remnants of last year's dried grass – this was the small world within my sight then. I saw it with my left eye, as my right eye was pressed against something hard like a tree branch, and my head felt heavy, making it quite uncomfortable. Even trying to move my body slightly, it was strangely impossible to budge even a little. I remained like that for a while. Nothing could be heard except the chirping of crickets and the buzzing of bees (蜂). After a moment of struggle, I managed to pull out my right hand, which was trapped beneath my body. Using both arms to support myself, I tried to get up, but putting aside other things, a sharp pain, like being drilled with an awl, shot from my knee through my chest and head, and I collapsed again. Once more, it was like midnight, utterly disoriented.

When I suddenly awoke, stars twinkled in the dim sky. Eh? This isn't inside a tent. How <5> did I end up here? I tried to move my body, and the pain in my legs was bone-chilling.
It seemed I was indeed wounded, but how serious was it? Was it just a scratch? I felt the wound area; both sides were soaked in blood. Touching it hurt more, but the pain was incessant, like a decaying tooth (齒), throbbing constantly, feeling as if my intestines were being scraped out. My ears rang, and my head felt heavy. It was clear enough that both legs were wounded, but what remained unclear was why I had been left like this. Though I thought it unlikely, could it be that our side had been defeated? First, I tried to retrace my memory, recalling the events of that time. What was initially hazy became clearer: no, no matter what, we couldn't have been defeated, I reassured myself. Why? Because, although not entirely clear, I had just collapsed. Perhaps while many were running away, I alone couldn't flee. I only remembered seeing something blue in front of me, but anyway, I fell in this field (田) on a small hill. Just as the short captain had pointed here and shouted, "Occupy! Occupy!" if we occupied this place, then we weren't defeated. So why wasn't I looked after? This is a wide-open place, impossible not to be seen, and surely I wasn't the only one rolling around here like this. The enemy's firing was so fierce! Right, let's see, I need to turn my head and look around. Fortunately, since waking up earlier and seeing the ant crawling down the small grass blade, I hadn't lain face down again after trying to get up and falling flat, so it was easier to move as I pleased. So, alright, these stars (星) too.

I tried hard to get up, but because both feet were injured, I couldn't manage it easily. After realizing it was useless and collapsing back down 2 or 3 times, I finally managed to raise myself halfway, but ah, the pain! Tears welled up involuntarily.
Looking up, I saw a corner of the dim sky, one large star with three or four smaller ones twinkling around it, and dark shapes sticking up all around. These were hawthorn bushes. I was inside the bushes. Truly <6> abandoned and fled from...
Thinking this, a chill ran down my spine, and my hair stood on end. But wait, if I was shot in the middle of the field, it's strange that I'm inside these bushes. If I think about it, it's clear that the wound hurt so much that I crawled here unconsciously. But still, it's strange that I could crawl this far then, but now I can't move at all. Or perhaps I was only wounded in one place when hit, and after crawling here, I was shot again?

The pale, broad moonlight, like countless spots (斑点), shimmered across the ground. The light of the large stars dimmed, and the small ones vanished altogether. Oh, the moon is rising from the tide. If this were home, ah, wouldn't that be nice... A strange voice sounded. It seemed like someone groaning... No, it really was groaning. Could there be someone nearby in the same miserable state, perhaps with injured feet or a bullet lodged in their gut, abandoned? The groaning sound was clearly close, but there seemed to be no one nearby. Ah, goodness, what sound is that? It's me, me, I'm groaning. Such a faint, pitiful sound I'm making. Does it hurt that much? Of course, it must hurt, but my head is just throbbing now, so I probably don't realize it. Let's lie down again and dream. It's good to sleep, good to sleep... But what if this is the end... Eh, does it matter!

As I tried to sleep, the pale white moonlight, like thin silk or spread seamlessly, illuminated the temporary sleeping spot. About 5 steps ahead, something large and dark was visible. Bathed in moonlight, reflections glittered here and there on its surface – perhaps buttons or the gleam of a weapon? Huh? Is this a corpse? Or a wounded person?
It doesn't matter either way. I'm going to sleep. ...
No, no, no matter how I think about it, it can't be. Our side hasn't gone anywhere. They must be here. They must have chased off the enemy and are guarding this place. But why can't I hear any talking <7> or the sound of sparks from a campfire (화투불)? What silence is this? No, it must be because I'm exhausted that I can't hear anything. In reality, our side must be here.
"Help! Help!"
A hoarse sound, barely human, ripped from my throat, but there was no response. The loud cry pierced the night air, echoing all around, and then silence returned. Only the crickets chirped ceaselessly, and the round moon shone down melancholically. If that thing over there were a wounded person, hearing this shout, surely they would know. Then it must be a corpse. One of ours? An enemy officer? Eh, pointless! What difference does it make? And again, my heavy eyelids closed in sleep.

I've been awake for a while, lying with my eyes still closed because the sunlight filtering through my closed eyelids suggests it would be blinding if I opened them, which I dislike. But thinking about it, perhaps it's better to remain perfectly still like this. Yesterday... I'm sure it was yesterday, a full day and night have passed since I was wounded. If two days, three days pass like this, I'll die. What is this? Death is one. If it's come to this, it's better to stay still. If I can't move my body, then not moving is fine too. If only my brain function could stop along with it, that would be great, but I can't do anything about that. Endless thoughts scatter in various directions, the anguish is intense, and my mind has no empty space, but it doesn't matter. If I endure this for a short while, soon I'll bid farewell to this floating world, remembered only by a few lines in a newspaper: Our army suffered few casualties, several wounded, volunteer Ivanov killed in action. No, will my name even appear? Just "1 soldier killed," that's probably it. One soldier! Ah, like that dog (犬).

Past events rise vividly before my eyes. This was long ago... In fact, it's not just this that feels like the distant past. My entire life, everything up until I hurt my leg and fell here, feels like a very long time ago... One day, walking down the street, a crowd had gathered tightly. I stopped idly and looked. <8> A pitiful, sad sight: a white object covered in blood, everyone standing stiffly, staring blankly. What was it? It turned out to be a cute puppy, hit by a horse-drawn tram, dying just like my body now. Then, from somewhere, a guard (守直軍) appeared, pushed through the onlookers, tightly grasped the puppy by the scruff of its neck, and carried it off somewhere.
And so the onlookers dispersed. Will anyone take me away too? No. I'll just die like this. But thinking about it, life is interesting. The day that dog (犬) met its misfortune was a happy day for me. Even walking felt somehow intoxicating. There was a reason for it too. Eh, thinking of the past is bitter. What's the use of thinking? Past happiness. Present suffering... Suffering is unavoidable anyway, but I don't want to be tormented by nostalgia. Recalling the past naturally leads to comparing it with my current situation, and the sorrowful thoughts are worse than the wound.

But it's getting quite hot. The sun is beating down. Opening my eyes, I saw the same hawthorn bushes and the same sky, only now it was daylight. Oh! The person beside me. Ah, it's an enemy corpse. What a powerfully built man. Wait a minute. I think I've seen him somewhere before. Yes, it's him...
The man I struck down is lying right before my eyes, his legs stretched out stiffly. What grudge did I have to lay hands on this man?
He's already completely covered in blood and stretched out, but to drag such a person onto the battlefield, the god of fate is cruel too. What kind of person was he, anyway? Perhaps, like me, he has an elderly mother, who probably stands forlornly at the door of a cramped shack every evening, gazing at the distant sky, wondering if her lifeline, her conscripted son, isn't returning, if she can't see the face of her beloved child, waiting endlessly day after day.
But what about my fate? I'm exactly the same... Ah, I envy this man. His fate is better. He hears nothing, feels no pain from his wounds, doesn't reminisce about the past, nor does he know the value of life. <9> Indeed, the bayonet pierced right through the center of his heart. Right, right, this big hole in his uniform, the blood around the hole! Whose work is this? It's all my doing, the one saying this now.

Ah, it shouldn't have come to this. I didn't come to the war out of malice. If I recovered, yes, I might kill, but somehow, that thought had completely slipped my mind. I was only concerned about how to face the incoming bullets, and stepping forward, I became almost a target for them.
And so, this state. Eh, eh, what a worthless fellow! I was a fool. But from the perspective of this unfortunate Egyptian farmer (埃及農夫) (though wearing an Egyptian uniform), he is even more innocent. Packed onto a ship like rice balls, until escorted to Constantinople (콘스탄티노-블), he probably hadn't even heard rumors about the situation in Russia (露西亞) or Bulgaria (勃加里亞). He just came because he was told to. If he dared open his mouth to complain, forget the sorrow of being flogged (笞刑); he might taste the lead pellets of some general's six-shooter (六穴鉋). From Stamboul (스탐부) to Ruse (루시추-크), enduring hardship through a long march, he encountered our army's attack and fought defensively. But our side is famous for being reckless troops who disregard consequences. Whether facing British-patented Peabody rifles (피-뽀듸-) or Martini-Henrys (만틔니-), they fear nothing and advance, advance. Only then did terror strike him, and as he tried to flee, some little guy from somewhere – a small man who, in normal times, could be settled instantly with just one of the black fists he carried – ran up, raised his bayonet, and thrust it into his chest... Isn't he completely guiltless?

I am equally dead, but what sin have I committed? For what sin do I suffer this thirst (燥渴症) that feels like my throat is drying up? Thirsty! Do you know what it means to be thirsty? When passing through Romania (루-마니아), under the dreadful heat (炎天), who knows how many hundreds or tens of times we marched 10 *ri* daily, but even then, it wasn't like this. Ah, if only someone would come. That's it! This man's large water canteen. There must be water in it! But I have to go get it. It will probably hurt. Eh, what does it matter, let's try!

And so I crawled out. Dragging my legs, moving my immobile body with weakened hands. The distance to the corpse is not even one *kan* (間 - approx. 6 feet), <10> but sometimes one *kan* is... not farther, but more strenuous than 10 *ri*. However, there's nothing to do but crawl. My throat feels hot, like it's burning. Although I think it would be easier to end things quickly by not drinking water, still, drawn by the thought of "what if..."
I crawl. Each time my legs move against the ground, the pain is unbearable. Groaning sounds "eung-eung," soon turning into whimpers, but I don't yield and keep crawling. Finally, I arrived. Egeu! The canteen – indeed, there's water! – and it's full! About half the canteen remains. Egeu, what joy! With this much, at least I won't suffer from lack of water – I won't struggle until death. Ah! Ah!
"Somehow, I am saved by your virtue," I thought, supporting myself on one arm, starting to untie the canteen strap. But suddenly, I lost my balance and found myself draped over the chest of my now benefactor's corpse. Indeed, the smell of the corpse (屍臭) hit my nose sharply.

Drank it, drank it! The water was quite lukewarm but not spoiled, and moreover, there was plenty. My life would likely hold on for another 2 or 3 days. That thing, in the *Physiology Primer Miscellany* (生理須知雜錄), it said that if one has water, even without food, a person can survive for over a week. And there was a story about a man who starved to death; because he kept drinking water, he didn't die for a long time.
So what does that mean? Dragging this out for another 5 or 6 days, what use is that? Our side is gone, the enemy has fled. If there's no path nearby where people pass, death is certain anyway. If it just prolongs three days of suffering into a week, wouldn't it be better to end it quickly? Beside the corpse, there's a rifle. Moreover, it looks like a fine British-made one. If I just endure the slight effort of reaching out my hand, it will be settled quickly. Looks like it wasn't completely unloaded; bullets are rolling around plentifully inside.
Well then – should I die – or wait? Wait for what? To be rescued? To die? To wait for the enemy to come and try to skin my wounded leg? Rather than that, better to do it decisively with my own hands...
No, that's not right. I shouldn't despair like this. Shouldn't I try to live as long as I can? What, if this <11> gets noticed, I'll be rescued. Maybe the bone wasn't hit. If so, I'll definitely recover. I'll return home, see Mother, and Ma, Ma, meet Maria...
Ah, I don't want them to know about this back home. I'd rather have them think I died decisively, but that's not right either. If by chance it becomes known that I suffered for three or four days...
My eyes are dizzy. My energy is completely depleted from moving to the neighboring spot. On top of that, this dreadful smell – I must look terribly pale!... If things continue like this tomorrow and the day after – eh, I'm already sick of it. Even now, I don't want to stay like this for a moment longer, but being this exhausted, I can't move back even if I want to. I need to rest a bit and return to where I was before! Fortunately, the wind is at my back, so the smell blows forward.

Completely drained of strength. The sun beats down on my hands and face. There's nothing to cover myself with. If only night would come quickly. This way, then, it's the second night.
As I thought this, my thoughts became increasingly jumbled and incoherent, and I drifted into sleep.

Waking up after what felt like a long sleep, it was already night. But nothing had changed. The wound hurt, and beside me lay the familiar large figure, still as death. This man weighs heavily on my mind. But even he, leaving behind all that was cherished and loved, crossing mountains and rivers for 300 *ri*, came to this Bulgarian desolate place, starving, freezing, suffering in the heat – isn't this somehow a dream? Is it worth suffering such agony just for having ended this unlucky fellow's life? Besides this killing, what benefit have I brought to the war?
Murderer, a great sinner who committed murder... Who is that monster? Ah, it's astounding, it's me!
Right, right, when I decided to enlist, Mother and Maria didn't stop me, but they cried. Was there any way their tears could enter my eyes, blinded as I was by fantasies? The thought that my decision alone would cause worry and anxiety for my mother and family didn't occur to me then. Only now, facing this, have my eyes finally opened.
Eh, eh, revisiting it now is useless. The past cannot be made the present, can it? <12>

But the way my family acted then was quite amusing. "What? Foolish son! Blindly rushing about in confusion!" - they said things like that. Where did such words come from? How could such malicious words emerge from mouths speaking of patriotism or martyrdom for the nation's difficulties? In their eyes, wasn't I a patriot? Wasn't I sacrificing myself for the nation's hardship? However, despite that, they called me a fool. Anyway, I went to Kishinev (키시뇨-프), shouldered my pack and other gear, and set out with thousands of comrades. Among them, those who volunteered like me were perhaps only 4 or 5; most were the type who would rather stay home if possible. Yet, quite remarkably, they marched alongside us, the so-called suicide squad, crossing mountains and rivers. Once battle commenced, they fought well without giving ground, perhaps even showing more skill. Of course, if permitted, they might throw everything down and run back home, but anyway, they fulfilled their duties well.

As the morning breeze blew gently, the hawthorn leaves rustled, and a bird, drunk with sleep, flew out. The stars were no longer visible. The sky, dark until now, brightened to a whitish hue, and clouds like scattered soft feathers (羽毛) spread densely, while the gray morning mist lifted higher and higher into the sky. This makes it the... what should I call it? Neither life nor death, perhaps the third day of death agony (死苦)?

The third day... How many more days will I suffer? It won't be long now. I'm so exhausted! In this state, even if I wanted to leave the side of the corpse, I might not be able to now. Soon I too will become like this, lying with slumped shoulders, but neither of us will feel heart-wrenching sorrow.
Anyway, I will drink plenty of water. Three times a day: morning, noon, and evening.

The sun rises! A large, disc-like shape, appearing blotchy black through the hawthorn branches, intersected vertically and horizontally, red like streaks of blood. It will likely be hot again today. That aside, neighbor – what are you planning to become? Even now, your form is gruesome to behold. Truly a gruesome form. Your hair is falling out, your naturally dark skin tone <13> has turned bluish with a jaundiced tint, the swollen facial skin is stretched tight, splitting behind the ears, where maggots squirm. Your legs are swollen like those with edema (水腫), bulging flesh peeking out hugely from the folds of the puttees. Your whole body is bloated, like a calf. What will become of this after baking in the sun all day today? I can't bear being so close. Whether my bones become relics (舍利 - uncrushed and undecayed remaining bones after cremation) or not, I must move away. But can I? True, I can lift my hand, open the canteen, and drink water, but can I move this heavy, immobile body? Eigu, whether I can or not, I have to move somehow. Even if just a little bit, perhaps half a step per hour.

So now I began the move, spending the entire morning on it. The wound hurt, but how could I succumb to such a thing? It felt like I had forgotten the mindset of my healthy days; it didn't even come to mind, and somehow I seemed numb to the pain. Crawling about one *kan*, collapsing and dragging myself, I finally managed to return to where I had lain before. But having breathed fresh air for only a moment, the wind changed direction, now blowing directly towards me, the stench turning my stomach. My empty stomach cramped as if spasming, and my insides felt wretchedly overturned. The foul, decaying air blew fiercely, gust after gust.
Utterly exhausted, I finally wept.

Completely drained, panting, I lay almost unconscious. Hmm... No, was my mind wandering, hearing things? It seems to me... Oh, it really is human voices. The sound of hooves and human voices. I almost shouted out, but wait a moment. What if it's the enemy? What then? On top of this agony, I might face a situation that made my hair stand on end just reading about it in the newspapers. For one thing, they might skin me alive; they might roast my wounded leg over a fire – things like that are mild. Those fellows are experts at such things, devising clever tortures; who knows what they might do! If I fall into their hands and am tortured to death for sport, it would be far better to just die here like this! However, what if, by chance, this is our <14> side? Eh, damn these hawthorn bushes acting like cursed obstacles! Why did they have to grow so densely like a wall, damn it? Blocked by this, I can't see anything. But oh, joy! There is just one spot, a gap between branches like a window, offering a view down to a distant cliff. At the bottom of that cliff, there's definitely a small stream (川); before the war, people probably fished there. Looking closer, yes, I can see the large sandstone slab placed as a makeshift bridge. They'll probably cross this. The sound of talking is already inaudible. I couldn't understand which language they were speaking anyway, but has my hearing gone now too? Hey you guys, if this is our side... If I shout from here, surely they couldn't fail to hear, right? Better that than rashly rushing out and falling into the hands of tiger-like mercenaries (日傭兵). They've almost passed now, what am I dawdling for? My heart anxious as if each moment were a thousand years (一刻千秋). The stench of the corpse hasn't lessened at all, but I had forgotten even that, to this extent.

Suddenly, on the bridge, our cavalry appeared. Blue uniforms, red stripes, glistening bayonet tips; about 50 riders in total (路50騎). At the head of the troop, an officer with a long black beard sat astride a magnificent horse, proceeding forward. As soon as the entire unit crossed the bridge, the officer, still mounted, quickly looked back and shouted in a booming voice,
"Gallop—!"
"Hey! Wait, wait! Please save me!"
My hoarse voice was drowned out by the sound of galloping hooves, the clatter of sabers (環刀), and loud chatter – ah, ah, they didn't hear.
Eh! So heartless! My strength instantly drained, I collapsed flat on the ground, sobbing uncontrollably. The canteen overturned. From it, the water – but at that moment, my life, the thread of my life, no, no, the elixir meant to extend my deadline for death – gurgled out. By the time I came to my senses, less than half a cup remained; most of it had soaked into the parched ground, which seemed to make a thirsty sound.
Facing this devastating situation, my disappointment and despair were indescribable. Eyes half-closed, I lay as if <15> dead. The wind kept changing direction, sometimes bringing fresh air, sometimes wafting the familiar stench. By this day, the neighbor had become a truly ghastly sight, the horror beyond description by pen and paper (紙筆). Glancing over to check the situation, I startled. The face was already gone. Bones had fallen away and disappeared. The cheekbones, grotesquely grinning as if frozen mid-laugh, presented a loathsome, tragic sight. Although I had handled skeletons and made human skull specimens, I had never seen anything like this before. Seeing this skeleton dressed in a uniform, only the buttons gleaming, I shuddered involuntarily and sighed inwardly. "Ah, war – this is it. This is its reality," I thought.

Still, the day remained overcast yet muggy, the sun beating down, making my hands and face tingle. I drank the little remaining water, leaving not a drop. Parched with thirst, intending only to moisten my lips, I gulped it all down without thinking. Ah, when those cavalrymen passed right by, why didn't I shout and call out? Even if they had been the enemy, it would have been better to call out. What's the worst? Only 1 or 2 hours of torture, compared to lying here, rolling around in agony for who knows how many more days. At times like this, thoughts of my mother come to mind. If she knew this, she would surely tear out her white hair, beat her head against the wall, curse the day I was born as an unlucky day (厄日), and condemn this world for inventing things like war to torment people's children.
But there's no way Mother or Maria could know, even by rumor (風便), that I am dying in such agony. Ah, I can't see Mother anymore, nor meet my fiancée Maria. Eh, so cruel! Thinking this, my chest felt tight...
Eh, that white puppy again. The guard (守直軍) was cruel too. He smashed its head against the wall and threw it into the trash bin. It was still breathing, so it suffered for a whole day afterwards, but compared to that dog, I am far more wretched. I have suffered continuously for exactly three days. Tomorrow is the fourth, then the fifth, sixth... Where is Death (死神)? Come! Take me away quickly! But Death doesn't come, nor does it take me away. Baked by this dreadful sun, <16> without a drop of water to drink, no way to forget the burning thirst in my throat. Furthermore, saturated with the stench of the dead man, my own body feels like it's starting to decay. The master of that smell has now completely dissolved, and countless maggots, falling off piece by piece, swarm and squirm nearby. After they devour everything, leaving only the master's bones and clothing, then it will be my turn here. I too will become like this.

That day ended, and night passed. Nothing changed; morning came, still the same situation. Another day wasted.
The hawthorn branches swayed, rustling, the sound of leaves rubbing together like secretive whispers.
"See, you'll die. Die. Die," they whispered in my ear. Then, from beside my ear, a friendly face,
"Can't see anymore, can't see, can't see."
"Of course, you can't see, being here," a loud voice said, from somewhere nearby...
Trembling, I snapped awake. Sergeant Yakolev (야-코우레프) of the company, his kind, darkish eyes peering intently through the hawthorn bushes towards me.
"Bring the stretcher! There are still two more. This one's an enemy too!" he said.
"No need for the stretcher. Don't bury me. I'm alive!" I wanted to say, but only a stifled groan escaped my parched lips.
"Yat! Is this one alive? It's Ivanov (이와-노프)! Come here, come quickly! Ivanov is alive! Get the Surgeon General, the Surgeon General!"
In an instant, water, vodka (燒酒), and something else were poured into my mouth, but after that, I lost consciousness again.

The stretcher (擔架) swayed rhythmically. It felt pleasant, almost lulling me back to sleep. Just as I thought I was awake, I lost consciousness again. After the bandages were applied, the pain in the wound subsided, making me feel indescribably comfortable, as if every joint in my bones was loosening. <17>
"Halt! Lower! Change bearers! Ready! Lift!" The commands came from our medical unit's chief medic (看護長), Pyotr Ivanovich (표-톨·이와-누이취). Four tall young men carried me on their shoulders. Turning my eyes towards him (the chief medic), I first saw his shoulder, then his long, sparse lower beard, and finally his head.
"Chief Medic, sir!" I called out in a small voice.
"What is it?" he asked, leaning forward slightly.
"What did the Surgeon General say? Is there no chance I'll survive?"
"What nonsense! How could that be! There's no damage to the bone; you'll definitely recover. You were lucky. You could say you picked up a life! Neither bone nor artery was touched. How did you survive for these 3 days and nights? What did you eat?"
"I didn't eat anything."
"Did you drink water?"
"The enemy's canteen... Chief Medic, sir, I can't talk right now. A little later..."
"Of course, of course. Sleep now, sleep—"
Again, I fell into a dream, completely unaware. When I awoke, I was here, in the divisional field hospital (假病舍). At my bedside were a military doctor and a nurse. Besides them, I saw the familiar face of a famous doctor from Petersburg (彼得堡), bending over my wounded leg. The doctor, his hands covered in blood, did something briefly to my leg, then turned towards me.
"Sir, you've saved your life! Rest assured now. I'm taking one leg, but what's one leg like this? It's no big deal. Can you speak now?"
Now I could. And so I told my story. ...(End)...<18>

**Summary**

The article is a Korean translation of Vsevolod Garsin's short story "Four Days." It recounts the harrowing experience of a Russian soldier, Ivanov, who lies severely wounded on a battlefield for four days after the fighting has ceased. He is near the corpse of an Egyptian soldier he killed in the battle. During these four days, he endures intense physical pain, extreme thirst, the horror of the decaying corpse beside him, and profound psychological torment, reflecting on the senselessness of war, his act of killing, his past life, and his longing for home and loved ones. He narrowly misses rescue once but is eventually found alive by his comrades and taken to a field hospital, where a doctor informs him he will live, though his leg must be amputated.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the publication of this translated Russian anti-war story in a prominent Korean magazine like *Kaebyŏk* (개벽) in 1922 is significant. It suggests an interest among Korean intellectuals in international literature that explored themes of suffering, humanity, and the brutality of conflict, potentially resonating with their own experiences under oppressive rule. Publishing a story focusing on the individual soldier's trauma, especially one where the protagonist questions his own actions and the nature of war itself, could subtly critique militarism and imperialism without directly challenging Japanese authority. It reflects the complex cultural and intellectual currents flowing through colonial Korea, where translated foreign literature served as a window to the world and a medium for expressing nuanced perspectives.

四日間

까-신 作   想涉 驛

Fsewolod Mihailovitch Garsin (1855-1888)은 露西亞 文豪의 1人이니, 처음에 鑛業學校에 修業하고, 1877年에 軍隊에 入하야, 土耳其에 出戰하얏슬제, 彼의 第一傑作인 이 「四日間」의 題材를 得한 바이라 하며, 其後 1880年에 一時 發狂하야 加療한 結果, 2年만에 快復된 後, 鐵道會議의 書記가 되엿든 事도 잇고, 一女醫와 結婚하야, 다시 文學生活을 繼續하얏스나, 1888年에 狂症이 再發하야 自殺하얏다 한다. (譯者)

이저버리지도 안핫다. 그때 우리便은 森林 속으로 다라나고 잇섯다. 식식하는 彈丸사이로 떠러저 오는 족으만 柯枝들을 잡아헤치면서, 아가위나무 사이로 요리조리 뚤어 나가랴니까, 彈丸은 漸漸 甚하야지고, 森林 저便에는 무엇인지 빨간 것이 힐근힐근 보이엿다. 第1中隊의 시-드로프라는 노상 젊은 兵丁이, 이쪽 戰線에 뒤석겨 들어오기에, 「왜 그리누?」하며, 忽忙한 中에도 얼핏 그런 생각을 하야보앗다. 그리려니까 瞥眼間에 厥者가, 펄떡 엉덩방아를 찌으면서, 허둥지둥하는 눈을 뚱그러케 뜨고 물ㅅ금 내 얼굴을 보든 그 입에서는 피가 콸콸콸... 아-, 눈에 선하다. 눈에서 선한 것은 그것만 아니다, 그때 無心쿠 본즉 森林을 거진 다- 빠저 나가서 蒼蒼히 茂盛한 아가위나무 사이에 9尺가튼 뚱뚱한 놈이 잇섯다. 나는 瘦瘠하고 虛弱하긴 하얏지만, 악소리를 치며 뛰여 뎀비자 짹걱하는 소리가 나며 무엇인지 이마마한 것이, - 그때는 그러케 생각하얏지만 - 휙 날아들어 오고, 귀가 잉하얏다. 노핫구나 하며 精神을 차릴 때에는, 敵兵 놈은＜4＞ 엑소리를 치며, 叢叢 들어선 아가위 나무에 걸녀 버렷다. 돌아갈랴면 돌아갈 수 잇는 것을 넘우 怯결에, 뾰죽뾰죽한 柯枝 틈박운이에다가 한발을 들여노코, 焦燥히 애를 쓰는 것을 單 한매(一擊)에, 銃을, 두득여 떠러트리게 하야노코, 함부루 鎗끗으로 푸욱 찌르니까 짐숭이, 짓(吠)는 것도 아니요 끙긍대이는 것도 아닌, 異常한 소리를 치는 것을 드리며, 다라낫다. 우리便은 으악으악 高喊을 치며, 逃亡을 하랴 銃을 노으랴 하는 한참곱이 나도 벌판으로 森林을 빠저나와서, 정녕 두세방이나 노앗다고 생각할제, 瞥眼間에 으악하는 喊聲이 한層 더 노피 들니며, 一齊히 뛰여 다라나갓다. 모두 다라나는 판에, 나는 그... 아까 그 자리에 잇섯다. 에-ㅅ? 하며 생각하야 보앗다. 그보다도 더 異常한 것은 忽然히 萬籟가 쥐죽은 듯이, 高喊소리도 업거니와, 銃소리도 들니우지 안코, 소리란 소리는 다- 스러저 버리고 단지 무엇인지 압히 퍼-럿타고 생각한 것은, 아마 한울이엇든 貌樣이엇다. 얼마 안되여서 그 프른 것조차 어룽어룽하게 되어버렷다...

얘 이것 別일이다. 암만해도 업드러저 잇는 貌樣인데, 눈을 가리는 것이라구는, 單只 손바닥만한 지면뿐. 족으만 풀이 몃 닙사귀하고, 그 中 한 닙사귀를 딸하서, 걱구루 기여 나려오는 개미하고, 昨年에 남은 말은 풀의 紀念인듯한 쓰레기 한줌 쯤 되는 것, - 이것이 그때의ㅅ 眼中의 小天地이엇다. 그것을 외쪽 눈으로 보기 때문에, - 한便 눈은 무엇인지 단단한 나무가지 가튼 것에 눌니우고, 게다가 또 머리가 언치랴고 하는 故로 如干 거북하지 안타. 몸을 좀 움즉어리랴도, 異常도하게 조금이나 꼼짝할 수 잇서야지. 그대로 暫間 지냇다. 굿드래미가 우는 소리하고 벌(蜂)이 붕붕하는 소리 外에는 아모 것도 들니지 안는다. 조금 잇다가 한바탕 苦悶을 하고 나서 몸 알에에 깔니엇든 오른손을 겨오 빼여가지고, 두 팔쭉지로 몸을 밧치면서 니러나랴 하야섯지만, 다른 것은 姑捨하고, 송긋으로 비비는 것가티, 압흔 病이 무릅에서 가슴과 머리로, 꾀뚜르는 것처럼 치미러 올라서, 나는 다시 걱구러젓다. 또 우밤中 가티 前後不覺이다.
훌적 눈이 깨여보니까, 컴컴스그레한 한울에, 별이 반짝반짝 보이엇다. 에-? 이건 天幕안은 안인데, 엇재＜5＞ 이런데에 나왓는가 하고, 뭄을 움즉여 본즉, 足痛이 뼈가 저릴 地境이다.
果然 負傷하기는 한 貌樣이나, 何如間 重傷인가 조금 벡겨지기만 하얏나하며, 傷處를 만적만적하야 보니까, 左右가 다- 흥근한 피. 만적어리면 더 압흐지만, 그 압흔 것이 버레먹은 니(齒)ㅅ 속 가티 쉴 새 업시 재릿재릿 쑤시우며, 배창자를 훌터내는 것 가타야서, 귀에서 소리가 나고, 머리가 묵업엇다. 두 다리에 負傷한 것은, 이만하면 흐릿하게라도 알겟스나, 그래도 알 수 업는 것은, 엇재 이대로 두엇는가? 설마 그럴理는 업스리라고 생각하얏지만, 萬一 엇쪄면 우리便이 敗北하지는 안엇는가? 하며, 爲先 記憶을 밟아가며, 當時의ㅅ 일을 생각을 하야보니까, 처음에 흐리멍덩하는 것이, 나종에는 아니다 아모리 하야도 敗北는 하지 안엇다고 安心하얏다. 왜 그런고 하니, 勿論 分明치는 아느나, 나는 고만 넘어저 버렷다. 아마 여럿이 뛰여 다라나는데, 나 혼자만은 다라날 수가 업시, 무엇인지 압헤 푸른 것이 보이드라는 記憶만은 잇지만 何如間 조고마한 山 우에 잇는 이 밧(田)에서 넘어진 것이다. 여기를 가르치면서, 작딸막한 大隊長이 占領 占領이라고 부루지지든 것대로 이곳을 占領하얏다고 하고 보면, 敗北한 것은 아니다. 그러면 엇째서, 내 뒤ㅅ갈망을 하야 주지 안엇는가. 여기는 四方이 툭터진 훤한 곳이라, 보이지 안을 理도 업슬 터이요, 더구나 여기서 이 貌樣으로 듸굴고 잇는 것은 나만일 것도 아니겟지. 敵軍의 射擊이, 그처럼 猛烈하얏스니까! 응, 엇의, 머리를 돌려서, 近處의 光景을 좀 보아야. 그러케 하기에는, 마츰 아까 깨여서, 例의 조고만 풀닙에서 걱구로 기여나려오든 개아미를 보앗슬 때에, 니러나랴고 하다가, 반듯이 넘어진 後에, 다시 업듸리진 안엇기 때문에, 마음대로 하기가 便하다. 그래서 올치, 이 별(星)도.
겨오 니러나랴고 하야보앗스나, 엇쨰던 두발을 다- 傷하얏기 때문에, 좀처럼 니러날 수가 업섯다. 암만 하야도 所用업다고, 벌떡 잡바지기를 2, 3次나 하고 나서, 인제는 겨우 半身쯤 니러낫스나, 아, 그 아프기란, 無心中間에 눈물이 핑 돌앗다.
그리고 본즉 머리 우는 컴컴스그레한 한울의 一角, 커-단 별 한아에 조곰아한 것이 서넛 반짝반짝하고, 周圍에는 무엇인지 껌언 것이 쭉쭉 솟아잇다. 이것은 아가위의 灌木. 나는 灌木속에 잇다. 그야말로＜6＞ 내버리고 다라난 것...
그러케 생각하니까 읏슥하며 머리끄치 쭙볏하얏다. 그러나 가만 잇거라, 밧 가운데서 銃을 마젓다 하기로서니 이 灌木안에 잇는 것은 怪常하다. 하고 볼 地境가트면 이것은 닷친 것이 넘어 압하서, 精神업시 이리로 기여드러온 것이 分明하나, 그러타 하야도 그때는 여기까지 기여드러올 수가 잇섯고, 只今은 꼼짝도 할 수 업는 것이 異常한 일, 或은 마젓슬 때는 한곳만 負傷하엿섯는데, 여기에 기여드러온 뒤에, 또 한방을 마젓는가?
푸르죽죽한 넓은 달비치, 數업시 얼넉(斑点)가티, 땅 위를 얼진얼진 기우면셔, 큰 별빗은 열버지고, 적은 것은 아조 스러저버렷다. 오, 달이 潮水에서 나오는구나. 이것이 집이엿슬 지경이면. 아- 참 좀 조앗슬가... 하는, 異常한 목소리가 난다. 마치 누가 呻吟하는 듯한,... 아니  참 呻吟하는 것이다. 누가 똑가티 발이 傷하얏거나 그러치 안으면, 배창쥐에 彈丸이 백여가지고 내버려진 悲慘한 꼴을 當한 사람이 그 近處에 잇지는 아는가. 新音하는 소리는 歷歷히 갓가히 나는데, 近處에 사람은 잇슬 것 갓지도 안타 앗, 에이쿠, 무슨 소리야, 내, 내, 이 내가, 끙끙대는 것이다. 가느듸가는 딱하기도 한 소리가 나는구나. 그러케도 압흘가. 勿論 압흐기야 하겟지만, 머리가 인젠, 다만 띙하기 때문에, 그래서 몰으는게로군. 또  들어 누어서 꿈이나 꾸자. 잘 일이다 잘 일이다... 그러나 萬一 이대로 고만일 디경이면... 에잇, 相關잇나!
자랴고 하니까 蒼白色의 月光이, 얇은 緋緞이나, 비인틈업시 까라노은 것가티, 한 때의 잠자리를 비치우고, 5步쯤 압헤 무엇인지 검은 커-다란 것이 보인다. 月光을 뒤집어 쓰기 때문에, 自邊의 여기저기서 反射하는 것이 보이는 것은 단추던지 武器의 光彩인가 보다. 응? 이것이 屍體인가. 그러치 안으면 負傷者인가.
무엇이거나 相關업다. 나는 자겟다. ...

해가 뜬다! 커-다란 장盤가튼, 것이 껌옷껌읏하게 보이는 아가위나무 가지에, 縱橫으로 끈허지며, 피ㅅ줄기가티 붉게. 오늘도 아마 더웁겟다. 그는 그러커니와, 이웃에- 너는 참 어떠케 될 作定이냐? 只今에도 보기도 慘酷한 그 形象. 참 정말 慘酷한 形象. 머리털은 漸漸 빠져 떨어지고, 元바탕이 검은 皮膚빗은,＜13＞ 프르러진데다가 黃疸빗까지 끼우고, 부은 얼굴의 가죽은 켱기어서 귀ㅅ뒤가 터지고, 거긔에 구덱이가 움질움질하며, 다리는 水腫다리처럼 부어 올라서, 脚絆이 접힌 틈으로 흠을흠을한 살(肉)이, 커-다라케 삐쥭히 나오고, 全身이 붓풀어 올라서, 마치 송아지갓다. 오늘 하로 벼테 쪼이면 이것이 참 어떠케 되랴는가 이러케 부터 잇서서는 견딀수 업다. 뼈가 舍利(碎치안코 朽치안하, 保存하는, 火葬 後의 殘骨)가 되든지 何如間 떠나지 안으면 아니 되겟다.- 그러나 될가? 事實 손도 들 수 잇고, 水桶도 열 수 잇고, 물도 먹기는 먹엇지만, 이 묵업은 꼼작할 수 업는 몸을 움즉일 수는? 에이구, 되거나 말거나 어떠튼 움즉이지 안흘수 업다. 設使 조금式이라도, 한 時間에 設或 半步式이라도.
그래서 인제는 移徒를 始作하야, 이걸로 하로 아츰을, 全數히 消費하얏다. 傷한 것도 압흐지만, 아모리 하기로 그만 일에 屈할 수야 잇나. 인젠 健康한 때의ㅅ 마음은 이저버린 것 가타서, 아조 생각도 나지안코 어쪈 細音인지 압흔데 저저버린 貌讓이엇다. 한間 통쯤 되는대를 하로아츰 걸리어서, 안저 뭉개면서 아까 누엇든 곳으로 겨오 차저 오긴 왓스나, 그러고보니 新鮮한 空氣를 呼吸해 본 것은 霎時間이요, 바람이 變하야, 이번에는 正面으로 이리로 불어와서, 냄새가 가슴을 뒤집어 놋는다. 속비인 胃臟은 痙孿이 일어남 듯이 오글어지고, 臟腑는 뒤집힌 것 가티 괴롭다. 냄새나는 썩은 空氣가,  심사 사납게 훅훅하며 불려온다.
폭삭 시진하다 못하야, 나는 마츰내 울엇다.
아조 탈진이 되어서 헉하며 거의 人事不省으로 누어 잇섯다. 응, ... 아니 마음이 浮虛해서 ,헛들은게 아닌가? 암만해도 나에게는... 오- 亦是 사람의 소리다. 말굽소리와 사람소리. 까땍하면 소리를 지르랴다가, 暫間 참어라, 萬一 敵일지경 가트면, 그땐 어떠캐? 이 苦悶에 게다가 또, 한層더 떠드러논 新聞에서 보기만 하고도, 머리털이 으쏙으쏙하는 듯한 境遇를, 當할지도 모른다. 一手, 生껍질도 벗기우겟지 負傷한 다리를 불에 굽기도 할 터이지- 그만쯤 한 일은 약과다. 이런 일에는 一等가게 생각도 잘하야 내이는 그놈들의 짓이라, 무슨 짓을 할지 알 수 잇나! 그놈들의 손에 걸리어서, 놀리며 죽이는 것을, 바들 地境이면 그보다도 여긔서 이러케 죽어버리는 것이 차라리 낫지! 그러키는 하지만, 萬一에 偶然히, 이것이 우리＜14＞便일 地境이면? 에이, 아가위나무란 놈이 망할 희살을 놀아서! 무엇하랴고 이러케 비인틈 업시, 담모양으로 빌얼먹게 生長하엿누? 이것에 막히어서 아모것도 보이지 안는다. 그러나 반거워라. 單 한군대가, 窓가티 가지틈으로 멀리 낭떨어지가, 내려다보이는 곳이 잇다. 저 낭떨어지에는, 分明히 족으마한 내(川)가 잇서서, 戰爭前에 그물을 먹은 법하지. 그리고 보니, 올치 저긔 다리 代身에 걸어논, 큰 砂巖石의 板石도 보인다. 아마 이것을 건널테지. 벌서 이악이 소리도 들리지 안는다. 어떤 나라 말로 이악이를 하던지, 족음도 알아듯지를 못하얏지만,귀까지 인제는 먹엇는가. 너희들, 얘, 이것이 우리便일 지경이면, ... 여긔서 소리를 치면, 제서도 설마 듯지 못할理는 업겟지. 섯불리 서들다가, 호랑이가튼 日傭兵의 손에 걸리우는 것보다, 그便이 낫다. 인젠 거진 지나게 되엇는데, 무엇을 꿈을꿈을하고 잇는가 하고, 一刻千秋의 躁急한 마음. 屍臭는 족음도 엷어진 것은 아니지만, 그것까지 이저버렷든 이만큼.
