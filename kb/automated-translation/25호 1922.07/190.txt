Staying Temporarily in a Mountain Home
						Kim Sŏksong

Driven by the waves of the world, my body was chased into the mountains,
Is the slow bell from the monk's room the cry of my spirit?
Others, not knowing my heart, say I went seeking mountains and water.

Who was it that said life is a sea of suffering (苦海)?
It's the ugly cry of a weakling like me,
Ah, let it be, I shall burn my young heart just so much.

My beloved, sitting beside me, says to me,
"Look here, my love, my love, how much road lies ahead?"
Let it be, let's see how it turns out, whether wind or rain.<129>

Casual Musings Under the Moon

The thin light of the waning moon shines on my window,
My young body, unable to sleep, is 더욱이 (even more) pained.
I ask, that moon over there, is it sentient (有情) or insentient (無情)?

What should shine on my window is the willow by the garden,
Who can understand what is knotted in this heart?
Rather, I shall hide it completely, unknown to anyone.

While the world all sleeps, only the moon and I are awake,
Quietly in my garden, the two of us play,
Aha! I realize, beginning this night, Heaven above, Heaven below, only I am uniquely idle (天上天下 唯我獨閑).

To the Poet

One who writes short pieces is a poet of the current style (時體),<129>
Writing laments for a beloved is the vernacular poetry (언문풍월) of today.
Let what others say flow past your ears.

The old traces (녯자최) are faint, so the way to search is distant,
There is no new path, so there is no way to follow.
You who wanders alone, it seems to be you.

Though you don't know where to go, it's a path that must be walked,
If you just go that way, a true path will emerge.
Let it be, the words of the world cannot be taken seriously.

A May Morning

On a May morning when the beach roses (海棠花) are red,
Dewdrops sparkling on the flower petals
Whisper in a quiet voice,
Poet! Recite the May morning!
Just as you see it, in this place, at this moment (瞬間).

The sun, like a young person's face,
When it shines upon the Earth
Where the lake of humanity flows,
The young shoots (어린엄) of grass and trees
Grow rustlingly (벗석벗석) on this May morning!

The cuckoo (뻑국이) sings on the back hill,
The skylark (장기대장) goes *irajjo-jjo* in the front field,
The bullfrog (부룩지) goes *umme* on the paddy ridge,
Ah! All this symphony (交響樂)
Sings of youth (靑春) on this May morning!

The universe (宇宙) has already put on new clothes,
The Great Spirit (大靈) holds a new basket (새광이) in hand,
Upon the new Earth, like virgin land (處女地),
It sows the seeds of new life (新生).
Ah! Limitlessly (限업시) sacred (거륵한) May morning!

They Are Happy
(The Sorrow of an Iconoclast (偶像破壞論者))

Right next to our house<130>
There is a temple called Chijangam (地藏菴).
To that hermitage, every evening,
A woman who knows nothing comes,
Bows before the Buddha,
And prays for the fulfillment of her wishes (所願成就 發願).

Whenever we gather,
Mocking and ridiculing
That woman's foolishness
Is our daily routine (日課) each evening.

However, they are happy.
For them, that is pleasure.
That knowing nothing,
That cookie-cutter life.

I, who until now mocked them
And spoke of iconoclasm (偶像破壞),
Hearing the sound of the metal gong (쇠북소리) struck right by my ear,
As if startled into new senses (새얼), looked left and right (左右를 삷혓다).

Before my eyes
Are my wife and children (子息),
In the next room
Are my younger sibling and grandmother (한멈).
And also...

Ah! I realized. I cried!
That these are all idols (偶像) I created (創造한).
Ah! I clearly realized.<131>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by Kim Sŏksong explores themes of alienation, loneliness, the role of the poet, the beauty of nature, and a complex self-reflection on belief and attachment. The speaker feels like an outcast ("山家에 寓居하야"), finds solace only with the moon ("月下偶吟"), ponders the uncertain path of a modern poet ("詩人에게"), celebrates the vibrant life force of spring ("五月 아츰"), and finally, after scorning the simple faith of a neighboring woman, experiences a painful epiphany, recognizing his own family and responsibilities as self-created "idols" ("그들은 幸福이다"). For historians, these poems vividly portray the intellectual and emotional struggles of a Korean individual in the early colonial period (1922). They reveal a tension between disillusionment with society and tradition, an embrace of nature's vitality, and the dawning, complex awareness of modern ideas like iconoclasm colliding with inescapable personal bonds and duties, offering a window into the era's psychological landscape.

僧房의 느진鍾은 내 靈의 울음인가
남들은 속도 모르고 山水차저 갓다 하데

人生은 苦海라고 누구가 일럿던고

두어라 꼬라지보자 바람일지 비일지＜129＞

月下偶吟

下弦달 여윈 빗이 내窓에 비추이니
잠못든 어린 몸이 더욱이 괴로워라
뭇노니 저긔저 달은 有情인가 無情인가

내窓에 비췰 것은 뜰압헤 버드나무
이맘에 매친 것은 누구라 알어 줄고
차라리 아모도 몰래 끗끗들이 감춰두리

세상이 모다자되 달님과 나만 깨어
가만히 나의 뜰에 둘이서 노니르니
알괘라 이 밤에 비롯오 天上天下 唯我獨閑

녯자최 흐렷스니 차즐길 아득하고
새길이 업섯스니 예을바 바이몰라

갈바는 모른대도 가기는 가야할 길
그길 곳 가고보면 참길도 나서리라
두어라 世上의 말은 대종할 수 업느니
