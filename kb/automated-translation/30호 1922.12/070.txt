平壤의 生産方面 (Production Aspects of P'yŏngyang)

Japanese people refer to P'yŏngyang in P'yŏngan-namdo Province as the Osaka of Chosŏn. Thinking of it that way, they are steadily planning for economic development. Anyone going to P'yŏngyang who looks across the Taedong River will be astonished by the unusual development on the opposite bank. However, that is purely the practical result of the economic activities of the Japanese side. But what about the economic activities on the Chosŏn people's side? There is nothing really worth mentioning. Yet, isn't this quite a curious thing? The purely Chosŏn-managed sock manufacturing industry (洋襪製造業) has achieved unusual development, with production value for just the first half of this year already reaching 655,500 Won. Its sales channels have also expanded greatly, to the point where there is almost no place throughout Chosŏn where one cannot see P'yŏngyang socks. Indeed, it is a curious development of a curious business. Whether it's one or two, it's good. Promoting our economic revitalization, one thing or two things at a time, is the first priority for now. Alas! Who will rescue the P'yŏngyang of Chosŏn from the Osaka of Japan? <33>

**Summary**

The article describes P'yŏngyang being called the "Osaka of Chosŏn" by the Japanese, who are actively developing the area across the Taedong River. It contrasts this with the lack of significant economic activity by Koreans, except for the surprisingly successful, purely Korean-run sock manufacturing industry, whose products are sold throughout Korea. The author laments the general economic disparity but finds hope in this single successful Korean enterprise, urging for more such efforts to revitalize the Korean economy and figuratively "rescue" P'yŏngyang from Japanese economic dominance. For historians, this piece offers insight into the colonial economic landscape, highlighting the significant Japanese economic presence, the perceived weakness of Korean capital, and a specific example of a successful Korean indigenous industry (sock manufacturing) thriving despite the colonial context, reflecting both economic realities and nationalist aspirations in 1922 Korea.

平壤의 生産方面
日本사람들은 平南의 平壤을 稱하야 朝鮮의 大阪이라 한다. 그마큼 생각하고 그들은 着着으로 經濟的 發展을 圖한다. 平壤을 가는 사람으로 한번 大同江의 越便을 본다 하면 對岸의 異常한 발전에 愕然하리라. 그런데 그것은 純 日人側의 經濟的 活動의 實跡이다. 그러나 朝鮮人側의 經濟的 活動은 如何한가. 별로 이것이라 할 것이 업다. 그런데 자못 妙한 일이 아니리요. 純 朝鮮人 經營의 洋襪製造業이 異常의 발전을 致하야 今年 上半期의 生産高뿐이 旣히 육십오만 오천오백원에 達하얏스며 그 販路도 非常히 擴張되야 朝鮮 到處에서 거의 平壤 洋襪을 보지 못하는 곳이 업게 되엿다. 과연 묘한 사업의 묘한 발전이다. 하나이라도 조코 둘이라도 좃타. 한가지식 두가지식 우리의 經濟的 振作을 圖하는 것이 爲先 第一이다. 嗚呼라 朝鮮의 平壤을 日本의 大阪으로부터 救出할 이가 누구?＜33＞
