Feelings Upon Joining the Company
						Yi Ch'oehwan, P'yŏngnam Branch Office

The cold air has already invaded every place where we live. The time has come when, facing a solitary lamp beneath the window, it is naturally difficult to suppress a lingering affection for *Gaebyeok* (開闢), which holds the center of the peninsula's cultural world, as one's sole companion. At such a time, for me, who was no more than one element engrossed in leisurely reading, to have come to bear partial responsibility for the Gaebyeok Company in P'yŏngyang is not so much an honor as it is, first and foremost, that some heavy, some immense sense of responsibility inevitably surges up.

On the one hand, I realize that the concern of how I, insignificant and lacking insight, can fulfill this solemn mission and also this faithful servitude for our P'yŏngyang society, and from what direction, has come to always occupy a corner of my thoughts.

But on the other hand, considering that *Gaebyeok* serves as a fuse (도화선) and that the fervor (熱力) of you, the readers, is contained within it, if I too devote my meager sincerity and devotion (誠心精趣), then the collective energy (氣力) connecting you, *Gaebyeok*, and myself will surely be able to extend something, to pierce something, within our society. And at the same time, I realize a yearning (憧憬), directed toward nature and society, earnestly crying out that perhaps in our P'yŏngyang too, some influence and darkness, whether small or large, might not be absent. First of all, as a greeting upon joining the company, I pray for the health of the editorial gentlemen (諸先生) and all you readers. <88>

**Summary**

Yi Ch'oehwan, newly responsible for the P'yŏngyang branch of *Gaebyeok* magazine, expresses his mixed feelings of honor and burdensome responsibility. He feels inadequate but hopes that by combining his sincere efforts with the magazine's influence ("fuse") and the readers' enthusiasm ("fervor"), they can collectively make a positive impact on P'yŏngyang society, reducing its "darkness" and extending its "reach" or influence. He concludes with well wishes for the editors and readers. From a historical perspective, the article reveals the sense of cultural mission associated with nationalist publications like *Gaebyeok* during the colonial period. Yi's language, expressing both humility and determination to contribute to societal improvement ("reduce darkness") in P'yŏngyang, reflects the aspirations and anxieties of Korean intellectuals seeking to foster cultural development and enlightenment under Japanese rule. The mention of *Gaebyeok* as a "fuse" and the readers' "fervor" hints at the potential for cultural work to have broader social or even political implications.

入社의 感
						平南支社 李最煥
寒氣는 벌서 우리의 사는 곳곳마다 침입하엿습니다. 窓下에 孤燈을 대하야, 반도 문화계에 중추를 잡은 『開闢』을 유일한 벗으로 삼아, 그 戀戀함을 자연 금하기 어려워 할 때가 왔습니다. 이때를 당하야 閒讀에 열중하는 一分子밧게 안되던 내가 개벽사에 대한 평양의 일부 책임을 負하게 되엿슴은, 영광이라느니 보다도 먼저 그 무슨 무거온 그 무슨 큼직하다는 책임관념이 용소슴하기를 마지 안습니다.
일변으로 생각하면, 미미한 또 識見업는 나로서 적어도 우리 평양사회를 위하야 신중한 이 사명과 또한 충실한 이 종노릇을 어떠한 방면으로부터 실현할까 하는 掛念이 나의 이상의 한 모퉁이를 늘 차지하게 되엇다고 깨닷지마는
또 한편으로 생각하야 개벽이 도화선이 잇스며 여러분 독자 諸氏의 熱力이 포함되어 잇스니 그 중에서 나 역시 미미한 誠心精趣를 다 기우릴진대 여러분과 개벽과, 나와 연결체 氣力이 응당, 우리 사회에 무엇이나 뻣치고, 찌를 수 잇슬 것이라 합니다. 그리고 그러는 동시에는, 우리 평양에도, 무엇이나 미츰과 어듬이 적으나 크나 업지안흘까 하는, 憧憬이 자연을 향하야, 사회를 향하야 간절히 울어 담을 깨딸읍니다. 爲先 入社의 人事로 편집 諸先生과 아울러 독자 여러분의 건강을 비나이다.＜88＞
