The Authorities' Suppression of the Press and the Public's Heightened Opinion,

On the morning of November 21st, the Kyŏngsŏng District Prosecutors' Office summoned and interrogated Paek Tae-jin (白大鎭), the chief editor of the monthly magazine *Sinch'ŏnji* (新天地), and its printer Chang Chae-hyŏp (張在洽). Immediately afterwards, arrest warrants were issued, and they were sent to Sŏdaemun Prison. Then, on the 20th [likely meant 22nd or subsequent day, as 20th precedes 21st], they also summoned and interrogated Pak Hŭi-do (朴熙道), editor and publisher of the weekly magazine *Sinsaenghwal* (新生活), and its printer No Ki-jŏng (魯基禎). Arrest warrants were similarly issued, and they were sent to Sŏdaemun Prison. Furthermore, on the 25th, three reporters from Sinsaenghwal Company—Kim Myŏng-sik (金明植), Yu Chin-hŭi (兪鎭熙), and Sin Il-yong (辛日鎔)—were summoned and imprisoned. In addition, the residences of those involved were searched, and printing machinery was confiscated (差押), making it feel as though a violent storm had suddenly descended upon Chosŏn's press circles. As news of such grim events spread throughout the capital and countryside (京鄕), public opinion became agitated and enraged, reaching an uncontrollable state. Amidst this, prominent figures in our press and legal circles separately sought concrete countermeasures while also moving to defend the press in general. After several joint discussions, on the night of the 27th, they finally reached the following resolution and declared that the legal and press circles would cooperate and strive for freedom of the press.

Resolution
Regarding the authorities' handling of the "writing incidents" (筆禍事件) involving Sinch'ŏnji Company and Sinsaenghwal Company related to press regulation, we recognize the measures taken as excessively harsh. We pledge to cooperate and strive to defend the freedom of the press.
Attendees at the time this resolution was made were:
From the legal circle: Ch'oe Chin (崔鎭), Pak Sŭng-bin (朴勝彬), Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Kim Ch'an-yŏng (金瓚泳), Pyŏn Yŏng-man (卞榮晩)
From the press circle: Yŏm Sang-sŏp (廉尙燮) of Tongmyŏngsa (東明社), Kim Wŏn-byŏk (金元璧) of Sinsaenghwal Company (新生活社), O Sang-ŭn (吳尙殷) of Sinch'ŏnji Company (新天地社), Nam T'ae-hŭi (南泰熙) of Sisap'yŏngnonsa (時事評論社), Ch'oe Kuk-hyŏn (崔國鉉) of Chosŏn Ilbosa (朝鮮日報社), Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹) of Tonga Ilbosa (東亞日報社), Yi Chae-hyŏn (李在賢) of Kaebyŏksa (開闢社) <90>

**Summary**

In November 1922, Japanese colonial authorities in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) arrested editors, publishers, printers, and reporters from the magazines *Sinch'ŏnji* and *Sinsaenghwal*, imprisoning them in Sŏdaemun Prison and confiscating printing equipment. This crackdown provoked widespread public anger. In response, prominent figures from the Korean legal and press circles held joint meetings and passed a resolution condemning the authorities' actions as excessively harsh and pledging to cooperate in defending the freedom of the press. This article is historically significant as it documents a specific instance of Japanese suppression of the Korean press during the supposed 'Cultural Rule' period and shows a unified, organized response by Korean intellectuals (lawyers and journalists) demanding press freedom. Historians might find the specific names of arrested individuals and the attendees of the joint resolution meeting particularly valuable for understanding the network of resistance among the Korean elite.

當局의 言論壓迫과 民衆의 輿論激昻,
京城地方檢査局에서는 11월 21일 朝에 월간잡지 新天地의 主幹 白大鎭氏 同印刷人 張在洽氏를 引致 取調한 후 곳 拘引狀을 發하야 西大門監獄으로 압송하고 越 20일에는 又 復 週刊雜誌 新生活의 編輯 兼 發行人 朴熙道氏와 同印刷人 魯基禎氏를 引致取調한 후 역시 拘引狀을 發하야 西大門監獄으로 압송하고 후 25일에는 更히 新生活社의 기자되는 金明植, 兪鎭熙, 辛日鎔의 3氏를 引致 囚監하는 이외에 관계자의 가택을 수색하며 인쇄기계를 差押하야 朝鮮의 언론계에는 暴風烈雨가 일시에 來迫하는 감이 有하엿다. 如許한 살풍경의 소식이 한번 京鄕의 天地에 전하매 일반의 여론은 爲하야 激昻하며 爲하야 분노하야 그 저지할 바를 不知하는 중 우리의 언론계와 법조계의 有志는 별로 이에 대한 구체적 대책을 講究하는 동시에 進하야 일반 언론의 옹호를 행하고저 數次로 聯合討議한 結果 去 27일 夜에는 遂히 如下한 결의를 행하고 언론의 자유를 위하야 법족계와 언론계가 협동 노력할 事를 聲明하엿다.

決議文
言論取締에 속한 新天地社와 밋 新生活社의 筆禍事件에 대한 당국의 처치는 太히 가혹함으로 認하며 吾輩는 언론의 자유를 옹호하기 위하야 협동노력함을 期함.
