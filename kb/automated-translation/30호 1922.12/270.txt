Magazine Title: 개벽 제30호
Publication Date: 1922년 12월 01일
Article Title: 月光(對話) (Moonlight (Dialogue))
Author: 林蘆月
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Moonlight
						Im No-wŏl
(Characters) Dream Woman (夢女)
Ghost (亡靈)
Skeleton (骸骨)

Dream Woman: Oh, mysterious Moonlight, enveloping all the customs of reality! You are different from the majestic Aurora, the Dawn God (曉神), and different too from the clear foam-light (淸泡빗) of the blue sea (碧海) that repels the white Extreme North Mountain (極北山). In your cast shadow lies elegant spring affection (春情), in your scent lies an indescribably subtle sentiment, and in your flowing whisper (囁語) hides the mournful sorrow (哀然) of Debussy.
In the long past or the distant future, who could possibly know your tragic, sorrowful beauty (悽悲한 美)? But when my senses (感官) are exceedingly sharp (銳敏) and my concepts (觀念) ecstatic (황홀), embracing the winding path between infinity (無限) and the moment (刹那), thus hearing the most difficult-to-understand modulating sounds (變調的의 音響) and seeing the hitherto unseen fine, scattered blue light (微細한 亂射光), then I knew you thus.
Oh—moonlight like dragonfly eyes!

Ghost: Sorceress! Your words symbolize the content and form of an endlessly lofty world. Are not your eyes like the black wheat (黑麥) illuminating the vast, lonely horizon (寞寞한 地平線)?

Skeleton: Oh—your cheeks are like red pearls (紅眞珠) tied to an evening dress (夜會服), your hands and feet like mint flowers (薄花) touched by the autumn sun, and your fragrance is noble like fresh medicinal herbs (풋약초)! Your starlit eyes, as if combing the dawn sky! It is truly pitiful to leave you in the human world.<50>

Dream Woman: Oh! In life, you were my lover-comrade (戀人同志). After parting from you, how many sorrowful days I spent! But if I can see you even like this, in the deep night... if I can see your lovely face, seemingly carved from ivory (象牙)... what difference is there between then and now? ... And Ghost—ah, you loved me dearly in life. But I could not repay your love... After parting from me, what world did you wander (彷徨)?

Ghost: Comrade—let us quickly guide that sorceress to our realm of magic (魔術境)!

Skeleton: Sorceress! I have seen a world even stranger than you described! After parting from you, I went to a world of brilliant phosphorescence (燐光). When the fragrant scent of rotten flesh (腐肉) sensually and intensely bewitched (蠱惑) me, a melting fantasy (空想) like a fairy tale (伽噺) unfolded within the phosphorescence. Unconsciously, drawn by an irresistible (不可抗的) mysterious power, I entered a realm of pleasure (逸樂境) that felt like melting away blissfully. How could I possibly describe all the sights I saw then? Would it be right to call it the licentious dream (放逸한 꿈) of the hibiscus (槿花) blooming in the boundless expanse (無限際涯)? Or the sight of the four-season flowers (四節花) decorating the riverbank, blooming and blooming again? A world completely transcending the vulgar emotions (卑俗한 감정) clung to in the human world, such as love, charity, and sacrifice. A world where hatred (憎惡) and grief (悲痛) sometimes spring from noble emotions, giving the dizzying pleasure (쾌감) of a poisonous delight (毒樂味)—how could I possibly describe all its miracles (奇蹟)? And the sight of all light rays bending into curves (曲線), or a world that is only night... where the faces of the inhabitants all possess a stimulating (刺戟性) charm (魅力), their lips (입살) extremely white, their complexions (顔色) changing moment by moment, where intentions are expressed through facial expressions rather than language, and happiness chooses its object with complete freedom (自由自在), utterly detached from the constraints of conscience—how can I explain the content of this world in detail? Oh—Lover! Tonight, let us become thoroughly drunk, and before the excitement cools, let us go together to our world!

Dream Woman: Oh—the reed leaves (갈닙히) rustle in the wind. A crab (게) pushes aside the reed flowers (갈꼿) and crawls lingeringly (어정어정) past me. My love—I like it here. Meeting you here every night brings me boundless joy.<51>

Ghost: Sorceress! After parting from you, I hesitated (躊躇躊躇) on a dark path, like an abnormal psychologist (變態心理學者) struggling to interpret the words of a mental patient (精神病者). How can I describe my loneliness (寂寞) and sorrow then? Confused by unknowable surroundings and unsure footing, floundering in the dim (朦朧) atmosphere, a lovely female devil (女데뷜) passed by me and said, "Oh—Ghost—the truth you seek lies in the forest of yonder profound realm (幽玄境); follow me." So I went with her.
My love! How astonished you would be if I told you all the sights of the demon world (魔界) I saw! But it is utterly impossible to express this with language. It is difficult to express supernatural (超自然的) secrets with vulgar (卑俗) language... But if I force myself to translate this into words... Firstly, it is the bizarre, pleasure-seeking life of the female demons (女惡魔). Their hearts are always sorrowful like the pale white winter moon (靑白한 冬月), extreme (矯激) like peacocks embroidering the blue sky (碧空), and free like a September storm (暴風), so their meaning of happiness is vastly different from ours of the past. For example, they comfort pain with pain, and sorrow with sorrow. If one comrade grieves, others come and tell tragic legends, showing a tendency to live tragically to the utmost. And since they have absolutely no concept of time or space, spring, summer, autumn, and winter sometimes flow together through a single moment (一刹那). Thus, I saw them living in a state of intense intoxication (陶醉境) where they lacked even the capacity to distinguish between beauty and ugliness.
To understand their mysterious ways, I gazed intently (熟視) at the postures (姿勢) of the female demons for a long time.
However, rather than finding their secrets, as my contemplation of beauty was swept away by a whirlwind (旋風) of joy (歡喜), the revelation (啓示) of lightning-like happiness springing from within myself struggled to connect (結紐) moment-to-moment impressions. Such climactic (絶頂) sorrow disturbed the peace of my soul (魂). I thought surely the subtle vibrations (波動) of their mysterious magic had transformed my perception. No, at that time, I probably didn't even have the leisure for such reflection... Like a pilgrim (巡禮者) seen in fairy tale plays, I couldn't help but search everywhere for the lost mystery... "Find the lost moment (刹那) again. If not,<52> then grasp the future moment tightly. Be convinced that eternity is the repetition of moments, and infinity begins from the tragedy of the moment..."
At this time, to experience the superiority (優越性) of the moment concerning beauty, I gathered my courage and rushed into the coven of witches (魔女團). And I grabbed one of the most beautiful female demons and embraced (포옹) her tightly. Whether it was the result of her soft nakedness, when her breasts (젓가슴) were buried in my embrace, I was ecstatic (황홀). Then, seeing her eyes and lips gradually becoming like yours, Sorceress— "When I deeply realized that my lifelong desires were being reenacted in such an incomprehensible (不可解) demon world"— Ah! The female demon held in my arms vanished without a trace, and I fainted right there on the spot. Oh—Lover! Your nipples (젓뚝겅) are like rain-soaked (비마진) white roses (백장미)! Come quickly with me into the demon world. I cannot even begin to describe your eye sockets like the shade of poppies (양구비꼿) or your breath like the scent of ginseng (人蔘).

Dream Woman: Oh—your words are too absurd!... Ghost—do you hear the mournful tone hidden in the reed leaves (갈닙헤)? Do you see the pale whiteness (해득해득한) of the reed flowers (갈꼿) in the blue light (靑光)? Oh! I like it here. Bathing here every night with that Moonlight brings me boundless joy.

Ghost: Sorceress! I love you. The world we have seen has beauty beyond natural value (自然的 價値). There, the moon you love rests merely atop a cactus (仙人掌) a hundred feet high. Thus, one can shoot it down to the ground with an ivory bow and arrow (象牙의 활살(矢)).

Dream Woman: Ghost—does your land not have conversation?

Ghost: But it has music. When numerous maidens sing, there are times when flowing water and fleeing time (星霜) listen ecstatically to the song and turn back their steps. Therefore, they know nothing of aging and only sing praises (謳歌) of infinite youth.

Dream Woman: Does your land not have clothes?

Ghost: What need is there for jackets and skirts? They love plants dearly, so they merely cover their bodies lightly with flower petals like a bouquet (花束). And since there are absolutely no animals there, no matter how much one wanders through lush (茂盛) forests and flower beds, there are no disgusting insects.<53>

Dream Woman: Oh! Every time I gaze at those reed flowers (갈꼿), I feel desolate (凄凉). But when I see something whiter than gourd flowers (박꼿) and paler than cherry blossoms (앵도꼿), my mind becomes distant.

Skeleton: Sorceress! That you meet us every night is surely because you have responded (感應) to the mysterious power that dedicates us to eternal beauty. Those like you who possess beauty beyond human value (人間的 價値), or have received a psychologically unusual influence (心理上 異樣의 感化), deserve the privilege of living an astonishing life. Come—for your own happiness... drink this magic wine (魔酒) and follow us... I truly love you.

Dream Woman: What happens if I drink the magic wine?

Skeleton: The nerves become thrillingly dizzy, and the fibers (纖維) melt away.

Dream Woman: My love! I still like it here. When I hear the sobbing (欷歔) of those trembling reed leaves (갈닙), what would my heart feel?

Skeleton: Oh—my world is more ecstatic (황홀) and more beautiful than here! Stars constantly waver (動搖), and their brilliance (光彩) glitters finely. And watching the kiss of blue and red, the embrace of yellow and white, the whisper of pink and black, and the alternation (交替) of curve and line, I felt as if living in a strange poetic radiance (詩的 光輝). But I realized this vague world was not one for me to conquer, but a place where my heart would be stolen... No matter how bizarre the conception (授胎) of life this world received, or even if the authority of existence craved only pleasure (逸藥) and luxury (華奢)... But what charm is hidden in this phosphorescent world that knows my heart like the flattering pleasure (媚樂) of a demon?
... "Oh—Beauty, the great revealer (啓示者) of the universe! You have planted magnificent flowers never before seen in the desolate demon world (荒凉한 魔界). All life first began through your miracle. If you did not exist, how meaningless and shallow (淺薄) our existence would have been?" ... When my frenzied (熱狂) consciousness of solemnity (莊嚴) and wonder (驚異) tried to reach the supreme (至高) radiance of beauty, I wept. My love, at this moment, I realized the infinite religious ecstasy (無限法悅) of the consciousness of sorrow.

Dream Woman: I still love you now.

Skeleton: Then hurry, drink this magic wine and bid farewell to your soul (魂).<54> And follow us.

Dream Woman: Oh—the taste is so fragrant! This liquor must surely be immortal wine (仙酒). My heart feels exceedingly light, and an indescribable fragrance of first love arises. And my nipples (젓뚝겅) tingle without limit. Oh—my love! Please hold me just once.

Ghost: Oh—my love!

Dream Woman: My eyes become bright enough to pierce the moonless night. Oh—beyond those stars, passing above them, it seems I can see even an endlessly distant place.

Skeleton: Ghost—look at the bewitching corpse (屍骸) of that madwoman! How plump her hands and feet are, like portulaca leaves (채송화닙)!

Ghost: My love! Love is neither a plaything (飜弄物) of the spirit nor a slave of emotion; it is merely sensing the rhythm (律動) of passion one previously hid (隱惹하엿든 - perhaps 'harbored') towards a tranquil form. Love is by no means a spiritual exchange (靈的 交換) nor an emotional accommodation (感情上 融通). These are merely commercial trade (商業的 貿易). Therefore, when one feels profound love, it is when the lover's red lips (赤唇) become mute regarding one's own anguish (苦惱), and when their heart closes its door to our wandering soul (魂). This is because the purpose of love lies in enjoying (享樂) suffering and silence.

Skeleton: Oh—your words are truth! Therefore, if there is any lawless impulse (不逞한 妄動) in a lovely form, or if the wrinkles of a cracked emotion (龜裂的의 감정) appear, this can be called the destruction of beauty. Look at the endlessly serene corpse (屍骸) of that sorceress. There flows only the supreme, highest supernatural life (超自然的 生命) that has transcended (脫化) spirit and emotion. For this reason, a corpse is a distinguished artwork (特秀한 예술품).

Ghost: Endlessly quietly sleeping Sorceress! What is your silence a symbol of? Isn't all the beauty and sorrow of the world yours? The countless years (星霜) disappearing into the waving, flowing sounds do not wish to greet any other bride (花嫁) but you. The legends of all ages will strive to unravel the lineage of your mysterious beauty. But when your eternal silence is cooled again and again by the poet's passion, and still <55> your silence inspires even greater incomprehensible passion in the poet, the poet's dream, dizzy (眩暈) from the lightning-like spiritual fire (靈火), will collapse merely from his own passion (激情) and bewilderment (困惑). Thus you will create the future world and adorn (潤飾) the ages. Oh—thus you will rule life. Sorceress, I embrace you. The red powder (紅粉) on your cheek touches my trembling lips (入脣). I think it is poppy pollen brushed by a butterfly's wing (나뷔짓). I am drunk. But I still remember your secret. Love, be forever new!

Skeleton: Love and freedom exist only in the land of death. I can love you without any resistance or obstruction. Sorceress! I think of you like a lost precious jewel (寶玉) from long ago. But I threw it away into an unseen, distant land. You are not of this world now; I think of you like a beautiful princess (美姬) in a distant land. And so, as night passes and day goes, as months pass and years come, you will just live on in that distant land. But you and I remember "tonight." Oh—we remember only "tonight." Night comes and day goes, months pass and years come, but the happiness of "tonight" cannot exist again. You should long for (思慕) and sorrow over "tonight" alone. You are eternal youth that will not age beyond "tonight."
Centuries later, perhaps when a poet cries out... "My lady! I love you. Why do you not answer?"... your answer will still be "tonight"...
Various poets, seeking to know the great mystery concerning you, their imaginations will constantly waver (動搖), traversing from the future to the past. But searching through all the centuries, there will be no special time called "tonight."
They will eventually collapse, breathless from their own sad longing (哀望) and passion. Thus you will conquer and rule the world to come.
Oh—how noble! May the grand and solemn (壯重) "time" be forever immortal (不滅)!
...(November 1922)...<56>

**Summary**

This symbolist play, "Moonlight," features Dream Woman (夢女) conversing with a Ghost (亡靈) and a Skeleton (骸骨), former lovers. They describe fantastical, decadent afterlives – a phosphorescent realm of sensual decay and a demonic world of inverted values and intense, timeless experience – urging her to join them by drinking magic wine. Initially hesitant, preferring the moonlit earthly realm, Dream Woman eventually succumbs, drinks the wine, experiences ecstatic visions, and dies. The Ghost and Skeleton then philosophize over her beautiful corpse, praising its static perfection as the ultimate art form, embodying a love beyond spirit and emotion, rooted in silence, suffering, and the eternal memory of "tonight."

From a historical perspective, the play is interesting for its intense aestheticism and potential escapism during a period of Japanese colonial rule in Korea. Written in 1922 for the influential magazine *Kaebyŏk* (개벽), its focus on decadent beauty, death, supernatural realms, and complex psychological states, possibly influenced by Western Symbolism/Decadence (Debussy is mentioned), contrasts sharply with overtly nationalistic or realist literature. Historians might find its exploration of themes like the rejection of mundane reality, the equation of beauty with death and silence, and the yearning for transcendent, perhaps amoral, experiences revealing of certain intellectual or artistic currents seeking refuge or alternative forms of expression under oppressive political conditions. The ornate language and specific cultural references (like the mention of Korean flowers) embedded within a universalizing, symbolic framework are also noteworthy.

月光
						林蘆月
(인물) 夢女
亡靈
骸骨

夢女. 모든 현실의 풍속을 덥허싸는 신비한 月光이여. 너는 장엄한 오-로과의 曉神과도 다르고 또는 하얀 極北山을 물리치는 碧海의 淸泡빗과도 다르다. 네가 비취인 그림자에는 高雅한 春情이 잇고 네 냄새에는 형용키 어려운 미묘한 정감이 잇고 네가 가는 囁語에는 데뷜의 哀然한 설음소리가 숨어잇다.
오랜 과거에나 또는 먼 장래에 누가 能히 너의 悽悲한 美를 알겟느냐. 그러나 나의 感官이 몹시 銳敏하고 觀念이 황홀하야 無限과 刹那새에 잇는 九曲九折의 길을 감쌀 때에 그래서 가장 了解키 어려운 變調的의 音響을 듯게 되며 아즉까지 업섯든 청색의 微細한 亂射光을 볼때에 나는 너를 이러트시 알엇다.
오 -짬자리눈 가튼 月光이여.
亡靈. 妖女여. 그대의 말은 한업시 고상한 세계의 內容과 形을 象徵한다. 그대의 눈은 마치 寞寞한 地平線을 빗날리는 黑麥과 갓지가 안는가.
骸骨. 오- 그대의 兩頰은 夜會服에 매여달린 紅眞珠와 갓고 手足은 가을 볏을 부닷는 薄花와 갓고 그대의 향기는 풋약초와 가티 고상하고나. 새벽 한울을 빗질하는 듯한 그대의 별눈이여 암만해도 그대를 人間世界에 두기는 애처럽다.＜50＞
夢女. 오! 그대가 生前에는 나와 戀人同志엿섯다. 그대를 이별하고 나는 얼마나 설은 날을 보냇는고. 그러나 이러케 깁흔 밤에과도 그대를 볼 것가트면... 象牙로 조각한 듯한 어엽뿐 그대의 얼굴을 볼것 가트면 넷날이나 지금이나 무엇이 다르겟느냐. ... 그리고 亡靈 아- 너는 生前에 나를 퍽 사랑하엿다. 그러나 나는 그대의 사랑을 갑지 못하여서... 그대는 나를 이별하고 어떠한 세계를 彷徨하엿는고?.
亡靈. 동무여- 어서 저 妖女를 우리의 魔術境으로 引導하자!

骸骨. 사랑과 자유는 죽엄의 나라에서만 잇는 법일다. 나는 아모러한 저항과 阻隔이 업시 그대를 사랑할 수가 잇다. 妖女여. 나는 그대를 녯날의 일헛든 寶玉가티 생각한다. 그러나 나는 그것을 뵈이지도 안는 먼-나라에 던저 버렷다. 그대는 지금 이세상 사람이 아니어 나는 그대를 먼-나라에 잇는 美姬와 가티 생각한다. 그래서 밤이 가고 낫이 가고 달이 지나고 해가 오되 그대는 그냥 먼나라에서 살 것일다. 그러나 그대와 나는 「오늘밤」을 기억한다. 오- 「오늘 밤」만을 기억한다. 밤이 오고 낫이 가고 달이 지나고 해가 오되 「오늘 밤」의 행복은 잇슬 수가 업다. 그대는 「오늘 밤」만을 思慕하고 설어하여라. 그대는 「오늘 밤」 이외에는 더-늙지도 안흘 영원한 청춘일다.
몃세기를 경과하여 설혹 시인이...「님이이여. 나는 그대를 사랑합니다. 왜 대답이 업서요」... 하고 부르지질 적에 그대의 대답은 역시 「오늘 밤」... 하고 말 것이다.
여러 시인들은 그대에 대한 큰 神秘를 알고저 저희들의 상상은 미래에서 과거를 통하야 不斷히 動搖가 될 것일다. 그러나 모든 世紀를 다-차저 보아도 「오늘 밤」이란 특별한 때는 업슬 것일다.
저희들은 마츰내 자기혼자의 哀望과 情熱때문에 숨차서 너머질 것이다. 그대는 이와가티 後日의 세상을 정복하고 지배할 것일다.
오- 고귀하다. 壯重한 「때」는 영원히 不滅할지어다.
