Magazine Title: 개벽 제30호
Publication Date: 1922년 12월 01일
Article Title: 隨聞隨見 (As Heard and Seen)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 소식 (News/Tidbits)

Wich'ang O Sech'ang (葦滄 吳世昌氏) is said to have resolutely given up alcohol and tobacco, which he used to enjoy so much regularly, ever since he came out of prison once. Anyone who meets Mr. O recognizes that his character is distinct from others, making it difficult to easily gauge his intentions. However, nobody could have imagined that his decisiveness would be exercised towards alcohol and tobacco, which were treasures of his life. As they say, "seeing one spot, you know the whole leopard" (一斑을 보아 全豹를 안다); inferring from this one act, one can sufficiently imagine how meaningfully Mr. O's future life will unfold.

Among our Korean people (朝鮮人), who possess intellectual brilliance far superior to both the Chinese and the Japanese, there must surely be individuals here and there who have achieved mastery solely through self-study and self-learning (獨修獨學), without needing the help of any teacher or friend. In Kyŏngsŏng, our capital, there must be quite a few such devoted scholars (篤學). Mr. Yi Wŏnch'ang (李源昌氏), currently at the American Consulate (美國 領事舘), and Mr. Pyŏn Yŏngt'ae (卞榮泰氏), at the Chungang Higher Common School (中央高等普通學校), are undoubtedly notable examples among such successful self-taught individuals. That is, the unique mastery of English achieved through self-study by both Mr. Yi and Mr. Pyŏn is publicly acknowledged by all, and it is said that Mr. Pyŏn, in particular, has profound attainment (造詣) in high-level English composition, which ordinary English literature scholars cannot hope to reach (企及).

The recent explosion of judicial power (司法權) regarding *Sin Ch'ŏnji* (New World/Heaven and Earth; 新天地) and *Sin Saenghwal* (New Life; 新生活) is a major threat (威嚇) and insult (侮蔑) to the press world of Korea (朝鮮). Public sentiment possesses indignation (抗奮) towards this, and who knows in what form they might express a part of their pent-up feelings (鬱念). However, a formal discussion concerning this issue will be detailed under a different topic. For now, in this section, one small thing to mention first is that when the Prosecutor's Office issued an arrest warrant (拘引狀) and detained (拘囚) Mr. Pak Hŭido (朴熙道氏) of *Sin Saenghwal*, they made him walk bound with ropes from the courthouse in Chongno (鍾路) to Sŏdaemun (西大門) prison in broad daylight on a main road (白晝大道). Even if Mr. Pak himself, receiving such treatment, could only endure whatever was inflicted upon him, how could the actions of the Prosecutor's Office be so excessively harsh (苛甚)? Do the officials (兩班) at Wae Sŏngdae (倭城臺 - likely referring to the Government-General) consider the old sayings, "Stopping the mouths of the people is harder than stopping a river" (防民之口, 甚於防川), and "If water is dammed and bursts, it will surely injure many" (水壅而潰, 傷人必多), or not?

Doctor Paek Injē (白麟濟君), working at the Government-General Hospital (總督府 醫院), together with a certain Japanese doctor there, examined the blood serum (血淸) of Koreans and Japanese, scientifically proving once again that the racial bloodlines (人族的 血統) of Koreans (朝鮮人) and Japanese (日本人) are distinctly different (判異). It is truly a joyous thing that such trends in academic research are growing increasingly vigorous among us day by day. <83>

**Summary**

This collection of tidbits covers four topics: the notable abstinence from alcohol and tobacco by independence activist O Sech'ang after his imprisonment; praise for the English proficiency achieved through self-study by Yi Wŏnch'ang and Pyŏn Yŏngt'ae; sharp criticism of the harsh public arrest and transfer of Pak Hŭido (associated with *Sin Saenghwal* magazine) amidst a crackdown on publications (*Sin Ch'ŏnji*, *Sin Saenghwal*), framing it as suppression of the press by Japanese authorities; and the mention of scientific research by Dr. Paek Injē demonstrating distinct racial bloodlines between Koreans and Japanese. For historians, the article offers glimpses into the resilience and intellectual pursuits of Koreans under colonial rule (self-improvement, academic research), the overt suppression of Korean media by the Japanese Government-General, and the undercurrent of nationalist sentiment reflected both in the critique of colonial authority and the interest in scientific studies emphasizing Korean distinctiveness.

隨聞隨見
葦滄 吳世昌氏는 한번 감옥을 나온 이후로 平時에 그러케 질기던 酒草를 斷然히 禁絶하얏다 한다. 氏의 사람됨이 다른이로 더불어 스스로 다름이 잇서 容易히 그의 意中을 尺度하기 어려운 것은 氏를 대하는 누구라도 認하는 바이어니와 氏의 果斷이 평생의 愛物이던 酒草에 향하야 발휘될 줄은 누구라도 생각치 못한 바이엇슬 것이다. 一斑을 보아 全豹를 안다고 此의 一事를 推하야써 足히 氏의 생활의 今後가 얼마나 의의잇게 전개할 것을 상상할 수가 잇다.

두뇌의 명석하기로는 中國人보다도 日本人보다도 월등하게 나음이 잇는 우리 朝鮮人에게는 何等 師友의 도움을 빌을 것도 업시 獨修獨學으로써 능히 그 章을 成한 人士도 處處에 不無할 것이다. 우리의 首府인 京城에는 그러한 篤學의 人이 또 얼마라도 잇슬 것이다. 지금 美國 領事舘에 잇는 李源昌氏와 中央高等普通學校에 잇는 卞榮泰氏와 如한 이는 그러한 독학 성공자 중의 著例됨을 不失하겟다. 즉 李卞 兩氏의 독학에 成한 영어의 독특은 일반이 공인하는 바어니와 특히 卞氏는 高級的의 영어작문에 造詣가 深하야 여간한 영문학자의 企及을 不許한다는 말이다.

新天地, 新生活에 대한 今回 司法權의 폭발은 是 朝鮮의 언론계에 대한 一代의 威嚇이오 侮蔑인 바 일반의 인심은 此에 대한 抗奮을 소유하야 장차 何等의 형식으로써 그 鬱念한 감정의 일부를 表白할는지도 모르거니와 이 문제에 관한 정식의 논의는 題를 改하야 具論하기로 하고 本題에서 爲先 족으마하게 한 말할 것은 檢査局으로부터 拘引狀을 發하야 新生活의 朴熙道氏를 拘囚할 際에 白晝大道에 氏를 鍾路의 法院으로부터 西大門 감옥까지 줄을 지워 徒步로 拉致케 하엿다는 그것이다. 그와 가튼 취급을 밧는 氏 자신으로는 이것이던 저것이던 오즉 당해지는 것을 당한다 할지라도 檢査局의, 所爲로서야 어찌 그러케까지 苛甚할 수가 잇스리요. 防民之口, 甚於防川, 水壅而潰, 傷人必多의 古語를 倭城臺의 兩班들이어 생각하는가 못하는가.

總督府 醫院에 在勤하는 의사 白麟濟君은 그곳의 日本人 의사 某氏와 共히 朝鮮人 日本人의 血淸을 검사하야 朝鮮人과 日本人의 人族的 血統이 判異한 것을 다시곰 과학적으로 증명하엿다. 이와 가튼 학술적 연구의 경향이 우리의 새에 날로 왕성하여 가는 것은 사실로 깃거운 일이다.＜83＞
