기사제목	우리의 社會에 새로히 비치우려하는 經濟思想과 經濟運動의 發現如何 (How Economic Thought and Economic Movements are Newly Appearing in Our Society)
필자	鮮于全
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

How Economic Thought and Economic Movements are Newly Appearing in Our Society
						Sŏnu Chŏn

Recently, looking at the pages of newspapers and magazines published in our Korean society, one sees discussions regarding practical matters rather than theory, and issues of livelihood and economy rather than ideological problems. It can be said that this certainly shows the beginnings of Korean society progressing in a substantial direction. Regarding the underlying reasons why the so-called economic problem, which was not considered overly important in former days, has come to be discussed today in newspapers and magazines representing the thoughts of all people, and why the need to cultivate material power is being discussed regardless of village or city, I believe there must have been special causes different from those existing in other societies and among other peoples. If we were to cite these special causes, the first would seem to be political reasons, and the second, reasons related to livelihood. Generally, regardless of the force, if it becomes a mass movement and arises as a kind of social force, when some opposing obstacle confronts its advance to defend against it, that force inevitably changes its form and attitude to evade the confrontation with the obstacle, but its inner nature never changes. This is a principle common to all things and difficult to deny. For example, <14> if the force of flowing water following its original path strikes some embankment, there is absolutely no reason for it to flow backward; it will change the direction of its original path and proceed by finding a new course to the left or right of the embankment. That is to say, although the direction and form of the water's force may have changed, the water, as water, will flow downstream. If we apply such physical principles to observe the various forces in society, we can affirmatively acknowledge phenomena quite close to reality in an empirical way.

That is, the social force of Koreans, which arose politically, suffered a truly excessive blow of confrontation at the very moment it emerged from its source and was about to enter its course. Although its direct forward thrust was temporarily halted, that force, unable to change into a reverse flow, seeks to take a new path by changing its form and attitude. That is, facing the left of the confrontation, it tries to advance fiercely in propagating new doctrines and new ideas; facing the right of the confrontation, abandoning the narrow path of small-scale politics, it takes the broad road of large-scale economics, diverging from the point of collision with the confrontation, attempting to take a new path to the left and preparing for advance to the right.

It is difficult to deny based on facts that such phenomena have been exposed in recent Korean society. As for the question of how far these two major forces will advance in the future, it is difficult to predict definitively as social transformations are limitless. However, while these forces are progressing and developing, opposing forces attempting to counter them will surely arise frequently, leading to considerable conflict and mutual constraint between the two. Furthermore, among the divergent factions originating from the same source, there will inevitably be much conflict until the two sides can reconcile doctrinally and actively. What must be noted in this situation is, among those divergent factions from the same source, under the notion of being of the same origin and same kind, not allowing disharmony in the coordination of forces due to minor ideological clashes and conflicts over partial interests. The absolutely necessary condition for carefully observing this caution is that the concept of nationality must be embraced by each individual. <15>

In any case, it is a fact that the emergence of economic thought and economic movements in recent Korean society is partly due to political reasons. Acknowledging this, let us discuss concretely the content of the other aspect, the reasons related to livelihood. To discuss these reasons, one must inevitably discuss them together with the specific issue of the livelihood problem of Koreans. The study of the Korean livelihood problem, as discussed continuously in the February, March, April, and June issues of this magazine this year, shows that the living standard of Koreans is excessively low compared to the living standards of people in civilized countries. Therefore, the question of how to improve the living standard of Koreans is the most critical and paramount issue for Korean society. It has already been argued, and it is also my persistent view, that improving the living standard of Koreans will have a great effect through the self-awakening efforts of each Korean individual, and there is no need to wait for the protection and policies of others. It seems that this self-awakening effort has become an important cause for the rise of economic thought among Koreans today.

The meaning of "self-awakening effort" includes not only the self-awareness and effort to maintain one's economic position against the economic oppression of other ethnic groups, but also the desire for livelihood (the force driving improvement in living standards) – how to raise one's own living standard to advance to the same level as other ethnic groups. Explaining these two mental efforts separately: one is the selection process of economic life arising from ethnic power competition, and the other is the pressure of living difficulties arising from the improvement of living standards. It is clear that both, regardless of which, have become causes (livelihood-related causes) for the rise of economic thought among Koreans. Since we Koreans all know the factual content of these two aspects, I will avoid propagandistic explanations and limit myself to merely pointing out two or three issues regarding these two causes that must be given the most attention and studied with careful deliberation.

Regarding the first cause, the question is by what means can one maintain one's economic position against other ethnic groups? <16> Next, if there is a best means, how to utilize that means most effectively and virtuously in terms of skill becomes an important research problem for the first cause. Regarding the second cause, this is an additional explanation for the circulating theory that the recent rise of economic thought among Koreans is solely due to living difficulties. The term "living difficulties" in this theory seems not to mean living difficulties based on the "standard of living" or "level of living," but rather living difficulties based solely on the conditions related to clothing, food, and shelter.

Of course, it is a fact that many Koreans today cry out about living difficulties, and it seems that economic thought and economic movements have arisen because of this. However, if you ask the destitute who are actually experiencing living difficulties about the cause compelling them, they only answer that they try to work but have no place to work, seek employment but find no jobs, thus suffering hardship in maintaining their livelihood. They do not answer that they suffer living difficulties because economic thought in Korean society is underdeveloped. Also, it is a fact that among the middle class, whose clothing is sufficient to protect against invasion [of cold/elements], food sufficient to fill the stomach, and housing sufficient to protect against the elements, many complain of living difficulties. At the same time, conversely, it is also a fact that among this type of person experiencing living difficulties, economic skills and economic knowledge are quite cultivated. If that is the case, then mere living difficulties (living difficulties related to clothing, food, and shelter) cannot be the cause that gives rise to economic thought. Simultaneously, it creates the contradictory fact that the class with developed economic thought conversely suffers from living difficulties, making it possible to say that the spread and cultivation of economic thought is actually harmful and ineffective for national life. Furthermore, looking at the national level, it is also a fact that countries with developed economic thought have many citizens experiencing living difficulties, while countries with immature economic thought have relatively eased living difficulties among their citizens.

However, that living difficulties give rise to economic thought and economic movements is recognized as fact <17> when considering the meaning of living difficulties and supplying other subsidiary conditions. That is to say, as long as the economic structure possesses capitalistic characteristics, this fact can be seen when the people's standard of living improves. Then, regarding the content of the cause whereby Koreans' living difficulties have given rise to economic thought, has Korea's economic structure indeed acquired capitalistic characteristics, and is the living standard of Koreans trying to improve? To one, I would say yes, and to the other, I would say so. Why? Because although commercial and industrial capital economy, as seen abroad, has not developed in Korea in the past and up to the present, capital economy related to land has developed to some extent and is trying to develop further. Thus, the immature economic structure, centered on land, possessed capitalistic characteristics. Recently, the living standard of Koreans has suddenly improved, and regardless of the internal substance, externally they try to aspire to the same forms as foreigners. These two kinds of facts have certainly brought about living difficulties for Koreans, causing the majority class to give rise to the latest economic thought and the latest economic movements. As long as these movements and thoughts continue, one cannot but anticipate that various social waves and difficulties will arise.

As stated above, the reasons for the rise of economic thought and economic movements among Koreans have been indicated as, on one hand, the selective action of political forces and ethnic power competition, and on the other hand, the improvement of the living standard of Koreans. Finally, what must be advised to the public who intend to carry out economic movements or preach economic thought to the general populace is:

Absolutely do not mix economic movements with other movements. Research on economic problems should, as far as possible, be based on economic principles and conducted within an independent scope. It is extremely necessary to research and investigate the currently emerging economic problems of Korea based on reality and make them widely known to the public. To elaborate further, for instance, when initiating an economic movement, attempting to achieve political results through economic means, thereby using the economic movement as a tool for political movement, does not conform to the fundamental purpose of the economic movement. <18>

Why? Because even regarding the same issue, there might be points to criticize politically but none economically. Simultaneously, there are not a few cases where something cannot be praised as an economic issue but must be welcomed as a political issue. Also, looking at this from a legal perspective, there are cases where an incident constitutes an illegal act politically but causes no legal conflict economically. Simultaneously, there are not a few cases where an incident that can hardly avoid legal conflict economically receives no legal constraints politically. Therefore, political movements have the purpose of political movements, and economic movements have the purpose of economic movements. They certainly do not possess the nature to be mixed. Of course, there may be cases where, depending on the situation, an economic movement is initiated simultaneously with a political movement. Even in this case, the political movement should proceed as a political movement, and the economic movement should proceed as an economic movement. I believe the two movements should never be merged into a single entity and proceed together. Next, regarding the research of economic problems, since economics as a science is essentially distinct from other sciences, the principles of other sciences cannot be arbitrarily applied to economic research, nor can the principles of economics be unreasonably cited in the research of other sciences. Therefore, whether researching economics academically or studying it strategically in connection with practical problems, it is appropriate to completely separate it from the principles of other sciences and conduct research and discussion under the inherent principles of economics itself. Consequently, regarding any issue from the perspective of academic theory, inconveniences often arise where political or other scientific theories can criticize and attack it, but economic theory must affirm and approve it. The fact that, among adherents of the same faction, same doctrine, and same spirit, when discussing and critiquing the same issue from the standpoint of their respective independent sciences, their subjective views differ, leading to conflicts in speech and disagreements in assertions, is because economics and other sciences possess fundamentally different contents. In any case, for those who wish to study economics exclusively, <19> it is appropriate to research and discuss economics within its independent scope.

We possess the hope and interest to dissect and research the various problems in Korean society economically. Therefore, when stating the above two cautionary personal views regarding economic movements and economic research, it would be most interesting and desirable to discuss them citing various actual economic problems occurring in Korea as examples. However, regarding the question of what kind of negotiation and connection exists between the cautionary personal views of the first two cases and the various actual economic problems of Korea, it is not only difficult to speak of it solely, but it is also difficult to discuss the various current economic problems of Korea using the same method of argument. Therefore, it seems more convenient to discuss these numerous economic problems that we are witnessing under separate topics later, based on the aforementioned cautionary personal views. Thus, examples for reference regarding the cautions on economic movements and economic research are not mentioned here. The third point of the final cautionary advice is:

Research the economic problems currently occurring in Korean society, especially those deeply related to Koreans, based on reality and make them widely known to the public. Recently, due to living difficulties or ethnic relations, although various sectors of society desperately cry out that creating an economic position for Koreans is extremely necessary, when it comes to actual research and investigation, there is nothing worth seeing. Even among intellectuals who advocate that economic problems are the most urgent issue, there is relative indifference and neglect towards economic problems concerning Korea. Is there not a complete absence of institutions and reports that actually research and investigate issues such as the livelihood problems of Koreans, land problems, agricultural labor problems, customs and land tax problems, and other general financial problems concerning Korea, making them widely known to the general Korean populace?

Not only that, the psychology of Koreans, rich in changeability, is prone to change easily overnight. When difficulties arise, they cry out, but if the difficulties ease even slightly, they immediately cool down and become complacent and indifferent. Just as political fervor, boiling like a burning fire, cools down instantly, <20> it is difficult to guarantee whether this momentarily aroused economic demand might immediately cool down tomorrow due to some palliative measures or inducements to compromise. While there is no need to question the ignorant masses, it is truly regrettable that those who have a comparative social status and can be called the intellectual class are so ignorant about such important economic problems. Establishing a nation's industrial and economic policies requires fundamentally discussing their appropriateness and academically explaining the principles of economics, which cannot be done except by specialized researchers of economic knowledge. However, regarding the current practical economic problems that directly involve the interests of the general public themselves, each person must have common-sense knowledge. For example, while we bear various taxes, which are the main source of government political expenses, we are not only ignorant about how many types of taxes we bear, the standards for their assessed values, and the determination of tax rates, but we do not even try to know. When the notice of the amount to be paid arrives, we do not even try to investigate the details of the tax items and the standards of the tax rates, but simply set it aside with indifference. For that reason, the provincial office or the government, established to promote the convenience of the provincial citizens or the entire nation, sometimes becomes indifferent to the people's condition and drifts into the abuse of misappropriating political expenses, a malpractice that often occurs. However, the people, lacking a critical perspective on such budget revenues and expenditures, fail to realize that their own human incompetence and lack of financial insight caused such abuses to arise, and only criticize the government's wrongdoings. Isn't the fact that Koreans try to commit to ignorance and forgetfulness regarding the state of national finances, revenues and expenditures, and the burden rate on citizens – matters that civilized citizens of other countries, rich in self-governing spirit, study most carefully and seek to know in detail – an explanation of the incompetence of Koreans' economic knowledge?

The reason why Koreans are so indifferent and negligent regarding government financial revenues, expenditures, and the content of tax obligations is certainly not because they trust the government excessively. Nor is it due to harboring opposition to the government's policy direction and intending to have no dealings with the government. <21> If it were due to excessive trust in the government, this would be a major cause hindering the development of the people's autonomous spirit due to excessive reliance on the government. In this regard, it is imperative to awaken the self-awareness of the people as much as possible. And if it were due to harboring resentment towards the government, doing so while actually fulfilling the duty of tax payment would be a contradiction in principle. Then, where does the main reason lie? It lies in the immaturity of Koreans' economic thought and autonomous spirit.

Recently, reports are often heard from the authorities that the tax payment records of Koreans in various regions are generally poor, and among them, the poor performance is more pronounced among the propertied class than the propertyless. The reason, of course, must be heavily attributed to the economic recession, but another reason is also largely due to the immaturity of the general Korean populace's autonomous spirit and the lack of research perspective on economic problems. In this sense, the cultivation of Koreans' economic thought and autonomous spirit becomes an important issue not just for the economic development of Korean society, but also for administrative matters.

The above merely points out one example of the extremely negligent attitude of Koreans towards financial and economic problems directly concerning them. There are many other actual economic problems that Koreans must voluntarily research and investigate, but I will leave these for a later date and put down my pen.

**Summary**

Sŏnu Chŏn observes the recent rise of economic discourse and movements in 1922 Korean society. He attributes this shift away from purely political concerns to two main factors: the suppression of political movements, causing national energy to redirect towards economics (like water finding a new path around an obstacle), and issues of livelihood. He distinguishes between basic survival needs and the desire for an improved "standard of living" comparable to others, arguing the latter, coupled with nascent land-based capitalism, fuels economic thought despite potential hardships. Sŏnu cautions against conflating economic and political goals or methods, advocating for independent economic study based on economic principles, and laments the Korean public's general ignorance and indifference towards practical economic matters like taxation and government finance, urging self-awakening and research. From a historical perspective, the article vividly illustrates the redirection of nationalist energies under colonial constraints, shifting from overt political resistance towards economic self-strengthening and cultural/livelihood improvement as alternative forms of assertion. Historians might find the explicit separation of political and economic spheres, and the critique of public apathy towards fiscal matters under colonial rule, particularly revealing about the complex strategies and internal debates within the Korean nationalist movement during this period.

우리의 社會에 새로히 비치우려하는 經濟思想과 經濟運動의 發現如何
						鮮于全
近來 우리 朝鮮人 社會에서 出版되는 신문지나 잡지 면에도 이론보다도 實地를, 思想問題보다도 生活問題 經濟問題에 관한 議論이 揭載되는 것을 볼진댄 확실히 조선인 사회가 着實한 방면으로 진행하여 가는 緖端이 뵈인다고 可言할 수가 有하다. 前日에 在하야 과히 중대시되지 아니하엿든 소위 경제문제가 今日 萬人의 사상을 대표하는 신문지나 잡지 상에 論議하게 되며 村巷과 都市를 물론하고 物質的 勢力을 培養할 필요가 有하다. 云云하게 된 그 裏面의 원인에 대하야는 필히 他社會, 他國民 間에 有한바 그 원인과 동일치 아니한 특수한 것이 在하엿다 思하엿노니. 그 특수한 원인이라는 것을 擧하야 말할지면 第一은 政治的 原因 그것이 되겟고 第二는 生活上 原因 그것이 될 듯하다. 대체 如何한 세력을 물론하고 그것이 군중화하야 일종 사회적 세력으로 발생한 者이면 그 勢力進向에 대하야 此를 防禦코저 하는 무슨 相對的 障物이 前衝할 時에는 그 세력은 반듯이 形으로 變하고 태도로 變하야 그 障物의 前衝을 回避하려 하나 결코 그 內容性質은 變치 아니하나니 이것은 萬物에 向하야 通存한 원칙이라 부정키 難할지니 一例로＜14＞ 말하면 原路를 順行하는 流水의 세력이 어떤 堤防을 衝當하면 필히 退流할 理는 萬無하야 原路의 방향을 變하여 가지고 堤防의 좌측으로나 혹은 우측으로 新方面의 進路하야 진행할지니 즉 말하자면 그 水勢의 방향과 형태는 變하엿슬지나 水는 水대로 下流할 것이다. 이러한 物理的 原則을 社會的 諸勢力에 引合하야 관찰하면 얼마쯤 사실에 가까은 현상을 實証的으로 긍정할 수 有하다.
즉 정치적으로 발생하엿든 朝鮮人의 사회적 세력은 源을 發하야 境路에 入하려 할 그 때에 사실 過大한 前衝의 痛打를 당하야 直進突出은 일시 停止하얏스나 流의 逆退에 변화키 불능한 그 세력은 形式으로 變化하고 태도로 변화하야 新面의 進路를 取코저 하나니 즉 前衝의 左面에 향하야는 新主義와 新사상의 宣傳에 猛進하려 하는, 前衝의 右面에 향하야는 小範의 政治的 狹路를 棄하고 大範의 經濟的 廣路를 取하야 前衝의 衝突點을 分歧하여 가지고 좌측으로 新路를 取하려 하며 우측으로 前進을 준비하려 한다
최근의 조선사회에 이러한 현상이 露出되어 잇는 것은 사실에 의하야 否認키 難할지니 今後 此의 二大勢力이 어대까지 진전될가 한 문제에 대하야는 社會的 變態가 無常한 限에 在하야 斷測키 難하나 如何間 此等 勢力이 進行發展되는 그동안에 此를 對抗하랴는 相對的 勢力도 필히 多生하야 兩者間에 衝突과 相制도 頗히 多할 것이고, 동일한 세력에서 分歧된 同源分派間에서 兩者가 主義的으로 活動的으로 和合할때까지 相衝될 事가 必히 多할지로다. 此 경우에 의하야 다만 주의할 것은 그 세력의 同源分派者間에 在하야 同源同種이라는 관념 하에서 少한 主義의 相衝과 分部的 利害의 충돌로 勢力調合의 不順을 生치 아니케 할 것 그것이니 此 注意를 愼守키에 절대로 要한 조건은 民族的 觀念이라는 것이 各自에 包有되어만 하겟다는 그것이다.＜15＞
如何間 최근 朝鮮人 社會의 經濟思想과 經濟運動의 발생이 일부 政治的 原因에 在한 것은 사실일지니 此를 認證하면서 他面 生活上 原因의 내용에 대하야 如何한가하는 此의 원인을 具體的으로 의논하자면 특수의 論題를 持有한 조선인의 생활문제와 幷하야 論치 안흐면 아니될 터인데 朝鮮人의 생활문제의 연구는 本誌 本年度 出版 2월, 3월, 4월, 6월 各 號에 連續하야 論한바와 가티 朝鮮人의 生活標準은 文明한 나라 사람의 生活標準에 비하야 過히 低劣함으로 如何히 하야 朝鮮人의 生活程度를 向上케 할가 한 문제는 조선사회를 위하야 가장 切重하고 至大한 문제이다. 朝鮮人의 생활정도를 향상케 함에는 朝鮮人 各自의 自醒的 努力에 의하야 그 효과- 大할 것이고 他人의 保護와 政策을 待할 必要無하다는 것을 旣論하엿고 또는 此가 余의 持論인데, 此의 自醒的 努力이 今日 朝鮮人의 經濟的 思想을 興起케 한 중요한 원인이 된 듯하다.
自醒的 努力이라는 의미에는 他民族의 經濟的 壓迫을 대항하야 자기의 經濟的 地位를 保持하겟다는 自醒과 努力은 물론, 기타 如何히 자기의 生活標準을 向上하야 他의 民族과 동일한 정도에 進할가 하는 生活慾(生活程度向進力)을 包括한 말이다. 此 兩個의 정신적 노력을 구별하야 설명하면 一은 民族的 勢力競爭으로부터 生하는 經濟生活의 淘汰, 二는 生活程度의 향상으로부터 生하는 生活難의 乏迫인데 何者를 물론하고 朝鮮人의 經濟思想을 興起케 한 원인 (生活上 원인)이 된 것은 명백하다. 此 兩面의 원인의 내용사실에 대하야는 朝鮮人된 우리는 누구나 다 알 바임으로 宣傳的 說明은 피하고 兩個原因에 대하야 가장 注意되고 愼深히 연구치 아니치 못할 二三의 문제를 다만 擧示함에 止할 터인데.
즉 第一原因에  대하야 他民族에 대항하야 자기의 經濟的 地位를 如何한 수단에 의하야 保持키 능할가 한 문제,＜16＞ 其次에는 만일 最善의 수단이 有하다 하면 그 수단을 가장 유효하고 善良하게 運用할 技能方面의 如何가 第一의 원인의 연구문제로 중요한 者가 되겟고 第二의 원인에 대하야는 近來 朝鮮人의 經濟思想의 興起는 그 원인이 단독히 생활난에만 在하다는 遊說에 대한 加條的 說明인데 此說의 生活難이라는 것은 「生活의 標準」 「生活의 程度」를 기초로 하야 言出한 생활난의 의미가 아니오. 다만 衣食住에 관한 생활의 조건을 본위로 하야 言出한 生活難의 의미인 듯하다.
물론 今日의 朝鮮人의 다수가 생활난에 哀呌하는 것은 사실이겟고 此로 인하야 經濟思想과 經濟運動이 興起된 듯도 하다. 그러나 사실 생활난을 당한 赤貧者에게 그 생활난은 迫當하는 원인을 問한 즉 彼는 노력하려 하나 노력할 곳이 無하고 직업을 구하려 하나 직업이 無하야 生活維持에 고난을 당한다 答할 뿐이오. 朝鮮人 社會의 經濟思想이 발달되지 못하엿슴으로 자기가 생활난을 당한다고는 答치 아니하며 또 衣로는 防侵하기에 可足하고 食으로는 充腹하기에 可足하고 住로는 防氣하기에 可足한 中流階級者 중에 생활난을 呌하는 者 多한 것이 사실인 동시에 此에 반대로 此種類의 生活難者 중에는 經濟的 技能과 經濟的 知識이 頗히 薰養되어 잇는 것도 사실이다. 만일 그럿타 하면 單獨한 生活難(衣食住的 生活難)이 經濟思想을 興起케 하는 원인이 되지 못하는 동시에 經濟思想이 발달된 階級者가 돌이어 生活難을 迫受하는 반대적 사실을 生케 하야 經濟思想의 普及과 薰養은 돌이어 國民生活에 有害無效한 者이라고 하여도 可하게 된다. 또 國을 標準하여 볼지라도 經濟思想이 발달된 國에 국민의 생활난이 多하고 經濟思想이 幼穉한 國에 국민의 생활난이 緩和되어 잇는 것도 사실이다.
그러나 생활난이 經濟思想과 經濟運動을 興起케 하는 것은 생활난의 의미 如何와 他의 附屬的 條件을 供하야 사실로＜17＞ 인식되는 바이니 즉 말하면 經濟機關이 資本的 特色을 持有한 限에 在하야 국민의 생활의 표준이 향상할 때에 此의 사실을 見키 能하다. 그러면 朝鮮人의 생활난이 經濟的 思想을 起興케 한 그 原因內容에 대하야 과연 朝鮮의 經濟機關이 資本的 特色을 持有하얏스며 朝鮮人의 生活標準이 향상하려 하느냐 하면 一에 曰 是라 하겟고 一에 曰 然타 할지니 何故오 하면 朝鮮에는 過去 及 現在까지는 외국에서 實見하는 바와 如한 商工業的 資本經濟는 발달되지 못하얏스나 토지에 관한 資本經濟는 어느 정도까지 발달되엇고 또 발달되려 하야 幼穉한 經濟機關은 토지를 중심으로 하야 資本的 特色을 有하엿스며 最近의 朝鮮人의 生活程度는 俄然히 향상하야 내용으로는 如何하엿던지 外樣으로는 外人과 동일한 형식을 期하려 한다. 此二種의 사실은 확실히 朝鮮人의 생활난을 招來하야 多數階級으로 하여곰 最新의 經濟思想과 最新의 經濟運動을 起興하얏나니 此 운동과 사상이 계속되는 동안에는 사회적으로 多種의 波難이 生할 것을 豫期치 아니치 못하겟다 하노라.
이상과 如히 朝鮮人의 經濟思想과 經濟運動의 起興케 된 이유를 一面 정치세력의 淘汰的 作用과 他面民族의 勢力競爭, 朝鮮人의 生活標準의 향상으로써 그 원인을 摘示하얏는데 최후 經濟運動을 실행하거나 又는 經濟思想을 일반 군중에게 告吹하려 하는 일반에게 設告치 아니치 못할 것은
經濟運動을 절대로 他의 운동과 混合치 아니할 것, 경제문제의 연구는 어대까지던지 경제 原理原則에 準하야 독립한 범위 내에서 연구할 것, 現發하는 朝鮮의 경제문제를 실지에 의하야 硏究 調査하야 일반에게 普知케 할 것이 極히 필요타 하노니 此를 다시 縷說的으로 말하면 가령 經濟運動을 起할 際에 경제적 운동으로 정치적 결과를 得코저 하야 경제운동을 정치운동의 수단으로 이용코저 하는 것은 경제운동의 根本意에 不合한다.＜18＞
何故오 하면 동일한 문제에 대하야서도 정치적으로는 비난할 点이 有하나 경제적으로는 비난키 不能한 者 有할 동시에 경제문제로는 可贊키 不能하나 政治問題로는 歡迎치 아니치 못할 경우도 少치 아니함으로써라. 또 此를 법률상으로 觀할지라도 정치상으로는 불법행위를 構成하는 사건이 경제상으로는 아모 法律的 抵觸을 致치 아니하는 경우가 有할 동시에 경제적으로는 법률상 抵觸을 避키 難한 사건이 정치적으로는 아모 法律的 拘束을 受치 아니하는 경우가 少치 아니하다. 故로 정치운동은 정치운동의 本意를 有하엿고 경제운동은 경제운동의 本意를 有하엿다. 결코 그 운동을 混合할 성질을 有치 아니하엿다 하노라. 물론 경우에 의하야 經濟運動을 起하는 동시에 政治運動을 幷하야 起하는 경우-有하다 할지나 此 경우에도 政治運動은 政治運動으로 진행하여야 可하고 經濟運動은 經濟運動으로 진행하여야 可할 것이고 결코 兩者의 운동을 同一體에 幷合하야 진행할 것은 아닌 줄로 思하노라. 其次 經濟問題의 연구에 在하야서도 경제라는 科學이 本質的으로 他의 과학과 分立된 이상에 他科學의 원리 원칙을 경제 연구에 임의 적용키 불능하며 경제학의 원리 원칙을 타 과학 연구에 무리 인용키는 불능할지니 고로 경제라는 과학을 학문적으로 연구할 경우에나 사실 문제에 引合하야 정책적으로 연구할 경우에나 타의 과학의 원칙과 원리에서는 전연 分限하야 經濟學 本持의 原理 原則 下에서 硏究 論議하는 것이 可하다. 故로 學文上 理論으로 如何한 문제에 대하야 政治的 其他의 科學的 理論으로는 此를 비난하고 공격키 能하나 經濟學的 理論으로는 此를 肯定 是認치 아니치 못할 不便이 往往 生한다. 同一派 同一主義, 粊�神者 間에 在하야 어떠한 동일한 문제에 대하야 각기 독립한 과학의 見地에서 此를 論하고 비평할 때에 各히 그 主觀的 見解가 相異하야 言論에 衝突이 生하고 주장이 相合치 못하는 것은 經濟科學과 타의 과학이 本質的으로 相異한 내용을 持有하엿슴으로써라. 如何間 경제라는 과학을 專意的으로＜19＞ 연구코저 하는 者에게는 經濟學을 독립한 범위 내에서 硏究 論議하는 것이 可타 하노라.
