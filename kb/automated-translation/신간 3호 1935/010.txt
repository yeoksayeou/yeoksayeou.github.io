Article Title: 開闢 제2권·제1호 卷頭言 (Kaebyŏk Vol. 2, No. 1 Foreword)
Author: N/A
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Essay)

Sending off the old year and welcoming the new (송구영신) has occurred thousands, tens of thousands of times over the course of eternal (悠久) time, so people try to hold new hopes, new happiness, and new aspirations (抱負) whenever a New Year arrives. However, this is also an extension of past life and adding spurs (拍車) to present life. It is common sentiment (常情) to wish for future happiness even if the past was unhappy, and to wish for future success even if one failed in the past, so there is likely none who would stop this. But this year, departing from this general rule (常則), people are dwelling in maintaining the status quo (현상유지) rather than hope, in fear (恐怖) rather than happiness, and wandering lost in complex doubt (懷疑) rather than firm will.

This is likely a piece of the dark shadow (暗影) cast by the recent unstable trend of the world political situation, and the reason is that the New Year was greeted just like that, without even the prospect (餘望) of this dark shadow being resolved. It is also because the New Year was greeted just as it is, without the dawn light (曙光) of the poverty (빈궁) of mass life being alleviated (緩和) in sight. Far from resolution (解消), the anxiety remains as it is, and people clamor that it is a crisis. However, this too is not today's problem, nor yesterday's story. It is not something to make a fuss about anew now. While people's moods are dazzled (眩亂) by this particularly exaggerated momentum, the truth is that it brews (釀造) a crisis of the mind, and when that develops negatively, one gives up in despair (자포자기), loses mental balance, and enters the pit of downfall (轉落) oneself. How can we not be wary (警戒) of this!

While the New Year comes, and the New Year goes, the Earth grows old and feeble (老衰), history fades in color (褪色), yesterday's newness (新) has already aged, and yesterday's justice becomes like waste material (廢物) today. The shift (移變) in the world's trends is unparalleled (無雙), and social standards are ambiguous (曖昧), making it difficult to establish a center for life, causing anxiety. However, when winter goes, spring comes; when spring comes, sprouts emerge; old people have young descendants (後裔); and old trees (古木) have new sprouts (新芽) from their roots (根蒂). Thus, there will be a new birth (新生) for aged history. The New Year should, by all means, try to nurture these new sprouts. <1>

**Summary**

This 1935 New Year's editorial from *Kaebyŏk* magazine contrasts the traditional optimism of the holiday with the prevailing mood of anxiety and fear gripping society. It attributes this gloom to the unresolved global political instability and persistent economic hardship ("mass poverty"). The author warns against succumbing to despair ("crisis of the mind"), which leads to self-destruction. Despite the decay of old norms and the ambiguity of the present, the editorial finds hope in the cyclical nature of life and history, urging readers to nurture the "new sprouts" of regeneration (新生) that inevitably emerge, just as spring follows winter. From a colonial history perspective, the article is revealing in its palpable sense of unease and hardship under Japanese rule in the mid-1930s, reflecting the difficult socio-economic conditions and the anxieties fueled by international tensions (likely referencing Japan's increasing militarism and expansionism). The call for finding hope in "new birth" amidst decay, while ostensibly universal, could carry subtle undertones of longing for national renewal or change within the constrained environment of colonial censorship. Historians might find the candid portrayal of widespread fear, poverty, and psychological distress, alongside the cautious yet persistent hope for future regeneration, particularly interesting.

開闢 제2권·제1호 卷頭言
송구영신이 悠久한 시간 우에서 그 몇 천 번 몇 만 번을 지나 봣으니 사람들은 新年이 올 때마다 새로운 희망 새로운 행복 새로운 抱負를 갖이려고 한다. 그러니 이것은 역시 과거 생활의 연장이며 현재 생활에 拍車를 가하는 것이니 과거는 불행하엿으나 장래 행복코자하며 과거는 성취하지 못하엿으나 장래는 성공하고저 하는 것은 常情이니 막을 者가 없으려니와. 今年에는 이 常則에서 버서나서 사람들은 희망보다도 현상유지에, 행복보다도 恐怖에 ,견고한 의지보다도, 복잡한 懷疑속에서 迷路하면서 잇다.

이것은 작금 세계 정국의 불안한 동향이 더저주는 한 조각의 暗影이려니와, 이 暗影은 해소될 餘望조차 없이 그대로 新年을 맞은 까닭이며, 대중생활의 빈궁이 緩和될 曙光이 보이지 않고 그냥 그대로 신년을 맞어진 까닭이다. 解消는 커냥 불안이 그대로 남어서 사람들은 위기라고 떠드러댄다. 그러나 이것도 오늘의 문제가 아니며 어적게의 이야기가 아니다. 지금 새삼스러히 떠들 것이 못되는 것이니, 이러케 特히 과장된 기세로 사람의 기분을 眩亂케 하는 동안에 그 實은 마음의 위기를 釀造하야 그것이 소극적으로 발전할 때에는 자포자기하며 마음의 균형을 상실하야 스스로 轉落의 구렁텅이로 드러가게 되나니, 이엇지 경계하지 않을 것이랴!

新年이 오며, 또 新年이 가는 동안에 지구는 老衰하여지며, 역사는 褪色하여저서 昨日의 新이 임이 늙어지며, 昨日의 정의가 今日에 廢物처럼 되여, 世間의 그 추세의 移變이 無雙하며 그 사회적 표준이 曖昧하야 생활의 중심을 세우지 못하고 초조하나, 겨을이 가면 봄이 오고 봄이 오면 싹이 나오고 늙은이는 젊은 後裔가 있으며 古木은 根蒂의 新芽가 나는 것이니, 노쇠한 역사의 新生이 있을 것이다. 신년은 모름즉이 이 新芽를 북도더 볼 것이다.＜1＞
