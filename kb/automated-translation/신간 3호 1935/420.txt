Editorial Notes

Although this year marks the full 15th anniversary of our company's founding, the Kaebyŏk New Publication has only reached its third issue with this New Year edition. It feels as though a 70-year-old man is greeting the New Year holding his beloved 3-year-old child, and thus joy and sorrow mingle.

However, in the spirit of "Although Zhou is an old state, its mandate is new," although our company was founded long ago, it certainly does not age and decline like a natural person following its years; rather, the longer its history, the more its foundation develops. Therefore, greeting the New Year brings even greater joy and sorrow.

This New Year issue, in particular, has been specially edited with contributions from esteemed figures in relevant fields, aiming to thoroughly explain or provide outlooks on the global situation of this year, which the whole world is clamoring is a crisis. The content is not only rich but also deeply interesting. We hope you all will rejoice together and cherish it.

Starting especially from the New Year, we intend to publish many works by new talents in the literature and arts section. We hope each of you will send us your fine, strenuous works as if this were your own platform, and on the one hand, strive further for the Korean literary world, while on the other hand, become closer to our company, pointing out its faults and lending your support to its strengths.

In this way, Kaebyŏk will become, in name and reality, an organ of the public, and thereby make at least a small contribution to Korean culture. —(Cha, Pak, Yi)—

We strove to release this issue before the year's end, but due to various unavoidable circumstances, it could not proceed as scheduled, and we cannot but apologize to our readers that we are only now managing to put it out. However, as the issues repeat—February, March!—we will put them out a little earlier, so we ask for your patronage, doubled from before.
From the Kaebyŏk Editorial Bureau <115>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the January 1935 issue of Kaebyŏk reflects on the magazine's 15-year history juxtaposed with the 'New Publication' series being only three issues old, comparing it to an old man with a young child. Despite its age, the editors assert the magazine is renewing itself, particularly with a New Year issue focused on analyzing the perceived "world crisis." They call for contributions, especially from new writers, aiming to make Kaebyŏk a true public platform contributing to Korean culture. The piece concludes with an apology for the issue's delayed publication, promising future timeliness. From a historical perspective, the piece is interesting for its expression of Korean cultural aspirations and national consciousness ("Korean literary world," "Korean culture") under Japanese colonial rule. The reference to a "world crisis" in 1935 clearly points to the global anxieties of the time (Depression, rising militarism), and the magazine's intent to address it reflects an engagement with international affairs from a Korean perspective. The blend of classical references ("Zhou...") and modern concerns is also notable.

編輯餘墨
본사의 창립은 금년이 벌서 만15주년이 되였으나 신간 개벽은 이 신년호까지 겨우 3호에 불과하니 맛치 70의 노인이 3세 愛兒를 안고 신년맞이하는 것과 같어서 희비가 交集한다.

그러나 周雖舊邦에 其命維新格으로 본사가 창립된 지는 이미 오래지만은 결코 자연인과 같이 연령을 따라서 老衰하는 것이 안이오 역사가 오라면 오랠사록 기초가 더욱 발전되는 것이니 신년을 맞일 때에보다 悲喜가 일층 더 만타.

이번 신년호는 특히 왼 세상이 위기라고 떠드는 금년의 세계정세를 유감없이 해설 혹은 전망코자 하야 斯界名士의 총집필로 특별 편집을 하였다. 내용의 풍부는 물론 흥미 또한 津津하다. 여러분도 갓티 깃버하며 애호하기를 바란다.

문예란도 특히 신년부터는 新進의 작품을 많이 실어보고저 하니 각각 나의 機關이나 다름없이 좋은 力作을 보내주며 조선 문단을 위해서 더욱 노력하는 一便 본사와 밀접하여저서 그 잘못을 책하여 주며 그 장점을 한가지 援助하여 주기를 바란다.

이리해서 開闢은 名實한가지 대중의 機關이 되며 그러므로 조선 문화上에 적은 功獻이라도 잇게 할 것이다. ―(車·朴·李)―

이번 호를 되도록 연말안에 내여노랴고 노력햇스나 여러 가지 부득이한 사정으로 예정대로 되지 못하고 이제야 겨우 내놋케 된 것을 讀者諸氏께 謝해마지 안는 바이다. 그리나 2월 3월! 號를 거듭할사록 좀더 일즉이 내노을 것이니 여러분은 倍前愛護해 주시기를 바랍니다.
開闢編輯局白＜115＞
