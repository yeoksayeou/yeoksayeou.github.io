To the Sad Musician of Misfortune
						Mo Yun-suk

By the banks of History's river, flowing down,
Dreadful groans drift,
And at the entrance of the crumpled castle gate,
The lingering sound of wailing remains in the empty air;
Yet Time, swallowing its heavy weeping,
Gave birth to a new day and departed.

When a new day stirs upon the heap of earth covering Life,<26>
Even a long slumber will vanish beneath the earth.
Oh, Sad Musician of Misfortune, soaked in tears!
Do not grieve the black stain on your blood-stained garments.
Do not the hoofbeats carrying courage and action,
Bearing the relentless future, come galloping?

Ah, cease the mournful songs,
Burn your sad tunes that linger in every alley.
For the sake of a greater life, that Life was forgotten;
What is the pain of wounded footsteps?
Oh, you who glared at the altar of Fate!
Let us hear a new piece of music in this Port of Agony.<27>

**Summary**

The poem "To the Sad Musician of Misfortune" by Mo Yun-suk addresses a figure burdened by past sorrows and national suffering ("Misfortune," "wailing," "blood-stained garments"). It urges this figure (and implicitly, the Korean people) to stop lamenting the painful past ("mournful songs," "sad tunes"), symbolized by the "Sad Musician," and to embrace a future filled with hope, courage, and action ("new day," "hoofbeats," "new piece of music"). The poem calls for forgetting past sacrifices ("that Life was forgotten") for the sake of a "greater life" and overcoming the present "Port of Agony." For historians, this poem reflects the complex emotional landscape of colonial Korea: the acknowledgement of deep suffering and historical trauma alongside a strong nationalist desire for renewal, resilience, and a break from the perceived victimhood narrative, urging active engagement with the future despite present hardship. The call to "burn" the sad songs and create "new music" is a powerful metaphor for cultural and national revitalization.

悲運의 樂師에게
						毛允淑
주우려 흐르는 歷史의 江까에
두려운 신음소리 떠돌고
찌푸러진 城門 어구에
통곡의 여음이 허공에 남엇거늘
歲月은 무거운 울음을 삼킨 채
새 날을 나어 놓고 가버렷거니

生命을 덮은 흙덤이에 새 날이 움직이면＜26＞
오랜 수민도 따 밑에 사러지려니
눈물에 저진 悲運의 樂師여!
피 무든 옷자락에 黑印을 슬퍼마라
勇氣와 活躍을 실은 말굽소리가
줄기찬 앞날을 싫고 뛰여 오지 안느냐

아아 서른 노래를 끈치고
골목마다 서리운 네 悲曲을 불살느라
큰 삶을 위해 그 生命을 닞엇거니
상한 발자욱의 앞음이 무어랴?
運命의 제단을 눈 흘긴 者여!
이 苦惱의 港口에 새 樂曲을 들려주라.＜27＞
