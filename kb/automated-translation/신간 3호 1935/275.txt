Fun Science: Human Body Electricity Theory / Soon, Human Electric Lamps Will Appear

It has been said for some time that a considerable amount of electricity flows within our human bodies. It is one of the most famous stories how Mr. Dubois-Reymond (듀보아레몬) once imagined the phenomenon of electricity related to nerves, causing a great sensation in the world of psychology.

However, although this imaginative theory was considered a very new doctrine at the time and was quite an interesting topic, it has already become a commonplace story today.

Anyone who has studied even a little physiology knows that when nerves are excited and begin to act, an electric current with a potential of 0.02 to 0.03 volts constantly flows through the body. According to Dr. Turrel's (도렐) experiments, the electric current in a person in a state of fainting is actually 30 volts, which is surprising.

In reality, it is entirely unimaginable that there are 30 volts of electric current<102> inside the human body, but Dr. Turrel claims this is a fact and recently presented this research to the physiological community.

×

If we briefly consider the issue of electric current in animals other than humans, the electric catfish (鯰/멕이) is said to have a current of 35-40 volts, and the electric eel (鰻/뱀장어) can generate a high current of 300 volts. It is astonishing that this current alone can kill a healthy horse.

×

Mr. Helman (헬만) theoretically asserts the generation of this human body electricity, but this is merely a theory derived from a kind of phenomenon of biological alteration (生體變質現像).

Today, with research into biological radioactivity (生體放射能) flourishing, it is said that Mr. Helman's theory of bioelectricity must be thoroughly revised.

The German physician Dr. Virchow (월효), who advocated the theory of cellular pathology (細胞病理學說) and developed modern medicine as it is today, already held a viewpoint on this bioelectric current early on.
It seems that the idea that one of the foundations of normal and abnormal cellular medicine depends on the correctness of some kind of magnetism flowing within it dates from this time.

×

Famous sexologists like Havelock Ellis (하마록스 에리스) have often explained the sexual relationship between men and women by analogizing it to a form of physical energy (物理學的 에네르기). In the works of writers like Moll (물-), who gained popularity for a time with "love physics" (戀愛物理學), we find many research papers related to such things. Among the most interesting is the presentation of statistics that even quantify female shame or virginity using some physical parameter (物理的 파르메-타).
What is called physical therapy (物療) in the world seems to be a new research school within medical physics.

×

Considering this and that, it is true that research into human body electricity is gradually uncovering new discoveries. First, the American biologist, physicist, and zoologist Dr. Cawin (카윈) is said to have invented a gas lamp (互斯燈) that does not require matches.
The detailed research announcement will be made public soon. The main point will likely be a method of igniting the lamp's gas using the human body's electric current.
This can truly be called the most cutting-edge application of human electricity.

×

Finally, I want to briefly introduce another seemingly unbelievable invention that has emerged from this research on human body electricity. It is the story of Mr. Albert Szepessy (알밧다 쎄폐시-), a radio engineer living in Budapest (부다베스토), the capital of Hungary (항가리). He is still young—and a diligent researcher.
The year before last, he married a young and beautiful wife, but unfortunately, she recently passed away.
That wife was famous in the cosmetics world as the inventor of a luminous cream (夜光『크림』). But now the wife is dead and gone—what he thought of here was using the body's electric current to light an electric lamp. Placing this precious invention, now nearing completion, before him, he spoke with a face full of emotion as follows:
『This is a precious gift for which I got a hint from the luminous cream my wife invented. For her dear sake, I will definitely complete it.』 In any case, this, as a human electric lamp, is a curious invention and at the same time, a valuable study attracting attention in various senses. -(End)-<103>

**Summary**

This 1935 article discusses the concept of electricity within the human body, referencing early researchers like Dubois-Reymond, physiological findings (0.02-0.03V normally, 30V in fainting according to 'Dr. Turrel'), and comparisons to electric fish (catfish 35-40V, eel 300V). It mentions theories by 'Helman' and Virchow's cellular pathology linking health to bio-magnetism. The article touches on sexologists like Havelock Ellis and Moll applying physical energy concepts to human relationships. It highlights two recent inventions purportedly utilizing human electricity: one by 'Dr. Cawin' to ignite a gas lamp without matches, and another by Hungarian engineer Albert Szepessy, inspired by his late wife's luminous cream, aiming to create a 'human electric lamp'. The article presents these developments with enthusiasm for scientific progress.

For historians, this article reveals the keen interest in Western science and technology, particularly sensational or 'cutting-edge' ideas, within colonial Korea during the 1930s. The specific examples, like the slightly dubious claims about 30V currents or the 'human electric lamp', reflect popular science reporting trends of the era, mixing factual information with speculative or exaggerated accounts. The mention of specific (though sometimes vaguely identified) Western scientists and concepts like cellular pathology, sexology ('love physics'), and electrotherapy indicates the channels through which such knowledge was disseminated. The Szepessy story adds a sentimental, human-interest angle typical of popular journalism.

趣味科學 人體電氣論/未久에 人間電燈이 生긴다
우리들 人體內에는 相當한 分量의 電氣가 流轉하고 잇다는 것은 以前브터말해오는 事實이다. 『듀보아레몬』氏는 일즉이 神經에 依한 電氣現像이라는 것을 想像하야 心理學界에 一大『쎄세-슌』을 이르킨 것은 가장 有名한 이야기의 하나이다.
그러나 이 想像的理論도 當時는 非常한 新學說이라하야 꽤 興味잇는 問題엿스나 現今에와서는 발서 平凡한 이야기가되고 말엇다.
神經이 興奮되서 動作을 開始할 境遇에는 電位○, ○二乃至○○三『볼드』의 電流가 恒常 身體에 流轉하고 잇는 것은 多少라도 生理學을 배흔사람쯤이면곳 알수잇는 것이다. 『도렐』博士의 實驗에 依하면 失神狀態의 人間의 隣間의 電流는 實로 三○『볼드』나된다하니 놀라운일이다.
實際로 人間의 體內에 電流＜102＞가 三○『볼드』나잇다는 것은 全혀 想像할수업는일이나 『도렐』博士는 이것을 事實이라하야 最近生理學界에 이 硏究를 發表하엿다.
×
人間以外의 動物에 關해서 電流問題를 잠간 考察한다면 電氣生體鯰(멕이)은 三五-四○『볼드』의 電流를 가지고 잇스며  電氣鰻(뱀장어)은 三○○『볼드』의 만혼 電流를 發生 할 수가 잇다한다. 이 電流만가지면 健壯한 말(馬)한필을 죽일수잇다니 놀라운 일이다.
×
『헬만』氏는 이 人體電氣의 發生을 理論的으로 主張은하나 이는 一種 生體變質現像으로부터 잇러나온 理論에 不過하다.
生體放射能의 硏究가 盛히되여진 今日『헬만』氏의 生體電氣의 理論은 充分히 곳치지안으면 안될것이라한다.
細胞病理學說을 主唱하야 今日의 醫學을 如斯히 發達식힌 獨逸醫師『월효』博士는 일즉브터 이 生體電流에 關해서는 임의 一見識을 가지고 잇다.
즉 細胞正當 異常醫學基礎의 하나는 이것에 流轉하고 잇는바의 어 磁氣의 正否에 依하는 거와갓치 생각해온것도 이 브터인모양이다.
×
有名한 性醫學者『하마록스』『에리스』等도 男女의 性的關係를 한個의 物理學的『에네르기』에 假想해서 說明한일도 적지안타 戀愛物理學으로 一代에 人氣를 날리든 『물-』等의 著書에도 多分히 이런것等에 關係한 硏究論文을 우리들은 發見한다. 그中에서 가장 興味잇는 것으로는 女性의 羞恥心이나 處女性을 어 物理的『파르메-타』로 計量한 統計지 提示한 것이다.
世上에서 말하는 物療라는 것은 相異한 醫學物理의한個의 새로운 硏究學派라고 보여지는 것이다.
×
이런 것 저런 것으로 미루어 보아 人體電氣의 硏究는 차츰 새로운 發見을 차저내고잇는 것이 事實이다. 于先 米國의 生物學者요 物理學者요 動物學者인 『카윈』博士는 석냥이 不必要한 互斯燈을 發明하엿다 한다.
그 仔細한 硏究發表는 머지안어 公開할 것이다 그 要點은 人體의 電流를 利用하야 燈用互斯에 點火하는 方法일 것이다
이런 것은 正히 가장 尖端的인 人間電氣의 應用이라고 할수잇슬 것이다.
×
흐로 한 거짓말가튼 發明이 이 人體電氣硏究에 생겨진 것을 簡短히 紹介하려한다 그것은 『항가리』의 首都『부다베스토』에 사는 『라듸오』技師『알밧다 쎄폐시-』君의 이야기인데 그는 아즉도 나 젊은-그리고 篤實한 硏究家이다.
再昨年에 亦是젊고 아름다운 婦人을 안해도 마젓섯스나 不幸히 그가 最近에 死亡하엿다.
그런데 그 婦人은 夜光『크림』의 發明者로 化粧品界에 有名한 存在이엿섯다. 그러나 지금은 죽고업는 夫人-여기에서 생각해낸 것이 人體의 電流를 利用하야 電燈을 켜는 것이다 거의 完成에 갓가운이 貴重한 發明品을 압해다 놋코 그는 感激에 찬얼골로 아래와갓치말하엿다.
『이것은 나의안해가 發明한 夜光『크림』에서 『힌트』를어든 貴여운 선물로 그리운 그를 爲해서라도 기어코 完成하려합니다』하여간이것은 人間電燈으로서 珍奇한 發明인 同時에 한 여러 가지 意味로 注目되는 貴重한 硏究이다 -()-＜103＞
