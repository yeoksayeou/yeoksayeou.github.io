Look at This (Part 1)

Among the many goods we import from foreign countries (外國) and use, in just one year (Taishō 7 [1918]):
The amount of money flowing out for the import of envelopes (封套) is 130,793 Yen (圓)
The money escaping for the import of pencils (鉛筆) is 56,285 Yen
The import value of Western-style umbrellas (박쥐洋傘) is 207,470 Yen
The amount of money escaping for matchboxes (셩냥갑) is 311,842 Yen
The money escaping for Western paper (洋紙) is 1,889,775 Yen
The money flowing out for the import of fruits (果實類) is 1,163,195 Yen
The import value of alcoholic beverages (酒類) is 1,999,022 Yen<18>

**Summary**

This article highlights the significant amount of money leaving Korea due to the import of common consumer goods from foreign countries in the year 1918 (Taishō 7). It lists specific figures in Yen for items like envelopes, pencils, umbrellas, matches, paper, fruit, and alcohol, emphasizing the economic drain associated with relying on these imported products. For historians, this provides a snapshot of Korea's consumption patterns and economic dependency during the early colonial period, quantifying the outflow of capital for everyday items and reflecting concerns about national economic self-sufficiency.
