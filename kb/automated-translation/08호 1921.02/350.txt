**Summary**

This document is a complete table of contents for the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* (開闢), covering the issues published from June to December 1920 (Inaugural Issue to Issue No. 6). It lists the titles of articles, essays, poems, novels, and other contributions published during this period, reflecting a wide range of topics including Korean social issues, modernization, nationalism, international thought (democracy, socialism, individualism), cultural critique (family system, filial piety, traditional customs), education reform, philosophy, literature, and historical figures. For historians, this table of contents provides a valuable snapshot of the intellectual ferment and socio-political concerns prevalent among Korean intellectuals and activists in the immediate aftermath of the March 1st Movement (1919). The emphasis on "opening" (Kaebyŏk), self-awakening, social reform, engagement with global ideas, and critical examination of tradition reveals the aspirations and challenges faced by Koreans under Japanese colonial rule during this pivotal "cultural rule" period.
