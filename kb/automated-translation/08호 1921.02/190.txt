Voice of Society
						One Reporter

Presently in our society, no, our world, there are quite a lot of clamoring voices. Big voices, small voices, welcome voices, sad voices, fighting voices, reconciling voices – they are too numerous to count. All these voices reach our ears daily through the many newspapers published and contemporary rumors, with little missed. However, while all these voices can be listened to casually, half-heard, they can also be listened to with some significance attached. To speak truthfully, paying attention and dedicating one's mind (Note: the original uses 붓다, meaning 'to pour' or 'to dedicate') to such voices, especially the small, barely audible ones, is the first step toward building a meaningful life. Yet, such small voices (things that truly warrant attention) are rarely taken to heart. For example, even when reading a newspaper, the eye naturally goes to the huge headlines printed in size 1 type, but passes over the tiny headlines in size 4 or 5 type. Feeling this, we have gathered these small voices from newspapers, magazines, or rumors you may have already perused, like carefully saved pennies (淨財), and offer them through this column. If you, readers, do not spit them out (唾棄) for being small, you will gain something, however little it may be.

Forty-one Pitiful Children, Destitute for Food, Head to Japan

On the night of December 19th last year, awaiting the connecting ferry departing Pusan Harbor, there was a group of 41 young siblings, around 14 or 15 years old. Due to the hardship in their own homes, all the children's faces were extremely pale, and their bodies were emaciated (瘦瘠), resembling at first glance children housed in an orphanage. They had all been recruited as workers for the glass factory (琉璃工場) in Tokyo City owned by a Japanese man, Mr. Takase Seijirō (高淸次郞君), and were heading to Tokyo. Among them were young children under 13 or 14 years old. The employment period they were sold into was a full three years, and their wage was 60 *sen* per day.
Reporter's Comment: Had these children been born to parents in another country, they would be coddled on their warm parents' laps (膝下) and engrossed in their studies. But unable to do so, these young and weak children have become bodies swallowing tears in the harsh winds of a foreign land (異域). It is the irresponsibility of parents who only know how to give birth but cannot provide the strength to live, and it is also our industrial world that failed to establish even one factory where they could work for their own keep. <80>

Eighteen-Year-Old Maiden Commits Suicide by Train

On January 2nd, near the Choch'iwŏn Station on the Kyŏngbu Line, a girl about 18 years old was run over and killed by a train. Investigating the cause, it was found that her parents, due to their own financial difficulties, had arranged to sell their daughter into prostitution (色酒妓) for 250 *won*. The girl, despairing of her future, resorted to suicide as the final measure.
Reporter's Comment: What heartbreaking news this is. In our world, how many are there who end up like this, dying tragically, beyond just this girl? Even if they do not go as far as suicide, how many endure the suffering and humiliation, gritting their teeth? And what about the harm inflicted upon other girls and boys from good families by the final revenge (revenge born from the feeling of 'since it has come to this, I will go to the extreme') of such women?

Commendable Educational Zeal of Mr. Chang

Mr. Chang Ho-jŏng (張鎬井氏) of Ch'ojŏng-ri, Sŏha-myŏn, Anak County, Hwanghae Province, lamenting (慨嘆) the lack of educational institutions in the said township around Taishō 5 (1916), established the Ch'angsŏ School (昌西學校) with a large sum of 3,000 *won*. As the number of students surged dramatically in recent years, the school building became cramped. Thus, he donated another thousand *pyeong* of land for an expansion and, starting from the beginning of the New Year (新年 劈頭), added a girls' department, opening the path to schooling for village girls who had never dreamed of it before.
Reporter's Comment: This is truly a splendid deed. From Mr. Chang's own perspective, doing so is genuinely for his own sake and for the sake of his descendants. (Reporter's comment abbreviated hereafter)

Women's Youth Association Organized in Miryang

Progressive female leaders within the town of Miryang County, South Kyŏngsang Province (慶南), organized a Women's Youth Association towards the end of last year. They promptly established a night school, attracting over 100 students. The association's president, Ms. Ko Wŏn-sŏp (高遠涉女史), working diligently for the local women's community with a sincere and passionate heart (一毫의 거짓 업는 뜨거운 마음), has earned the sympathy (同情) of local figures, receiving donations (義捐金) exceeding 600 *won*.

France's Population Growth Policy

France (佛蘭西), obsessed with population growth, seems to be adopting quite unusual measures. The council of the Seine (세누) Prefecture in that country has reportedly decided to pay an annuity of 350 francs (法式 - likely 'francs') to the third child and subsequent children, irrespective (勿論) of whether they are legitimate (嫡出生兒) or illegitimate (私生兒). The financial resources for this are to be secured by levying taxes on single persons.

Recent Labor Society in Kyŏngsŏng

Looking at the movement of factory workers in Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) city between June and September last year, when the financial slump (財界의 不況) reached its peak: The current number of factories in the city is 503, with 13,687 currently employed workers. Due to the financial slump, 154 factories reduced operations or closed, laying off 2,272 workers. Furthermore, due to the financial slump, labor funds were reduced by a maximum of 10% and a minimum of 5%. Among these, the Textile Cooperative Association (織物同業組合) side was particularly severe (尤甚). In the two months from June to September last year, factories suspending operations (操業) included Yi Chŏng-gyu (李正珪氏) and 45 others, while those completely closing down included Pak Yŏng-wŏn (朴泳遠氏) and 24 others. Since these textile factories employed many female workers, the slump in this industry caused extreme hardship for women engaged in domestic work. Moreover, most of the laid-off workers mentioned above transferred to other jobs, but some did not. Additionally, many companies that were in the process of being established since last year were dissolved (多部 解散), creating so-called "higher-class unemployed" (高等遊民). Currently, the number of such people drifting (浮游) in Kyŏngsŏng is calculated at 2,450. (Based on investigation and announcement by Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture)

World's First Female Speaker of the House in America

The person who recently assumed the position of Speaker of the Columbia (콜롬비야 - likely District of Columbia/US Congress) Assembly in the United States (美國) is a woman named Mrs. Smith (「스미쓰」婦人). For a woman to become the head of the legislative body like this is a first in the world. <81>

Heartbreaking Grudge-Death of a Maiden

A maiden named Paek Kŭm-saeng (白金生) of Chungan-dong, Chinju-myŏn, Chinju County, aged 18 this year, lost her parents early (早失父母) and was raised (收養) by her foster father (義父), Yi Ch'an-gyŏng (李贊敬). In August last year, she was arranged to be married (出嫁) to Chŏng Hwa-yŏng (鄭華永) of Hadong County. Chŏng Hwa-yŏng already had a wife but, relying (恃하고) on his wealth (富饒), took another wife (有妻又取妻). For some reason, after returning home just three days after the wedding, he completely ignored her. Paek sent several letters asking him to take her back, but received no reply. Despairing of her future, she eventually (듸디어) developed a psychosomatic illness (心病) with no hope of recovery. Seeing this, her foster father Mr. Yi, finding her situation pitiful (可矜), sent her in a palanquin (가마) to the groom Chŏng's house. Paek, barely preserving her life (艱辛히 生命을 保하야), managed to meet her husband. With tear-filled eyes (눈물 먹음은 눈으로), she silently stared at her husband for a while, then passed away two hours later.

Japanese Policeman Extorts Marquis Yi Wan-yong

Since the outbreak of the Manse Movement two years ago, Marquis Yi Wan-yong (李完用侯) has constantly been watched (엿보는 바되어) by anti-Japanese Koreans, always feeling uneasy and endangered. Despite this, he was recently promoted to Marquis, pledged anew his efforts towards Japan-Korea harmony (日鮮融和), and felt some measure of comfort. However, on December 26th last year, an unusual disturbance occurred at Marquis Yi's residence. Around 5 PM that day, a Japanese policeman in full uniform visited Marquis Yi, claiming he had official business to discuss. The heir, Yi Hang-gu (李恒九), met him instead (代身). The policeman brazenly stated, "Although I have no prior acquaintance with Mr. Yi, I am currently unable to endure my financial hardship. Lend me 2,000 *won* for now. However, if this matter leaks (綻露) to the public, I will be immediately dismissed from my post, and there will also be danger to Marquis Yi's person for revealing this incident. Acknowledge this and prepare 2,000 *won* by 4 PM tomorrow, the 27th. I will return at that time..." Having received (바든) this threat (威脅), Mr. Yi was extremely frightened, agreed to the demand, and sent him away. At this time, a great commotion arose at Yi's residence, and the incident was reported (申告) to the relevant police station. The station investigated and found that there indeed was such an officer within their ranks, who was promptly dismissed as a disciplinary measure. The next day, at the appointed time, the policeman again insisted strongly (强謠) on meeting Marquis Yi and demanded the fulfillment of yesterday's agreement. When the station chief, who was guarding the scene, tried to restrain (制止코저) him, he retorted, "Until yesterday I was your subordinate, but today I am a private citizen, so there's no need for me to tolerate your interference (干涉)!" and insisted only on the fulfillment of the contract, so he was forcibly dragged away.

Foul-Smelling Bomb at Berlin's New City Council

Germany's (獨逸) Berlin (伯林) City's new council had been under threat from Communist Party members since its first meeting. On January 6th, a bomb emitting a foul odor (惡臭) was thrown from the public gallery (公衆傍聽席), forcing the council to adjourn as they could not continue the meeting due to the stench.

Labor University Established in Frankfurt, Germany

In the city of Frankfurt (후랑코포트市), Germany (獨逸), a university for laborers is set to be established. The purpose is to have talented laborers study labor issues.

Commendable Act by Haeju Students

There are over 70 young people from Haeju (海州) currently studying at various schools in Kyŏngsŏng. They organized the Haeju Cultivation Youth Association (海州修養靑年會) to aid their self-cultivation and fellowship. When they returned home during the winter vacation late last year, they held a play for two days to help poor youths from Haeju who could not afford to study, raising over 600 *won*. With this amount, they first secured lodging in Kyŏngsŏng for needy students from their hometown.

Major Conflicting Trends in the Recent World

Professor Marey(?) (「마레」敎授 - likely H. R. Mill or similar figure from the Royal Geographical Society, as context suggests), the new president of the UK (英國) Geographical Association (地理學協會), said the following in his inaugural address: "Philosophers of old considered the universe as one great city (一大市邑) of God and man, with the Earth <82> as its material center and humanity as its moral center. However, for Britain and other powers today to reject other peoples like beasts is a fall so degenerate that our more enlightened ancestors would be shocked if they knew. (Omitted section) Here, there is something that appears as a critical issue recently, namely, which of the two great conflicting trends, legacies left by the Great European War, will achieve victory? One is the trend of strong nations arbitrarily committing economic plunder (經濟的 掠奪을 恣行하는 傾向) against weak nations and their territories. The second is the trend of regarding the Earth as a great city and acknowledging worldwide significance and humanity's duty towards fellow humans simply as people. Which of these two will achieve victory is the great question for the future world," and so on.

Korean Buddhism Reform Plan Resolved

Around the tenth of January, the Thirty Head Temples (三十本山) of Korean Buddhism held a general meeting of representatives from each head temple at Kakhwang Temple (覺皇寺) in Kyŏngsŏng and resolved the following five reform measures:
1.  Fundamentally reform Korean Buddhism from its roots.
2.  At the same time, rename the current Federation (聯合會) to the Korean Buddhist Central Administration Office (朝鮮宗務院).
3.  As the location of the current Office of the Federation of Thirty Head Temples (三十本山聯合事務所) is unsuitable for the activities of Korean Buddhism, acquire a suitable place for 180,000 *won* and construct the Central Administration Office building.
4.  Fundamentally reform the current Central Buddhist Seminary (中央學林), teach subjects appropriate for the times to reach the level of a Higher Common School, and rename it Buddhist Professional School (佛敎專門學校).
5.  Previously, the management policy for propagation by each head temple defined specific regions; unify this for propagation efforts.
Besides the above, there were several pending proposals (保留案). This coincides (相等하야) with the movement last year by Yi Hoe-kwang (李晦光氏), the abbot of Haeinsa Temple, to merge Korean Buddhism with Japan's Rinzai sect (臨濟宗) and his new independent propagation center (in Sŏdaemun-jŏng, Kyŏngsŏng), making it a noteworthy situation in the Buddhist world.

Japanese Common Sense Also Questionable

Japan has compulsory education for the general populace, and since the Meiji Restoration, they have worn the clothes of civilization, so one might think they are reasonably knowledgeable, but reality seems otherwise (樣이다). Some years ago, when Japan recruited 3,000 policemen, a common sense test asked what '**만소**' (likely マンショ, mansion/apartment, or perhaps related to 万歳, Banzai/Manse?) was. I heard that among the 3,000 applicants, only a few could answer, and many answered it was a type of caustic soda (苛性曹達). More recently, at the Yongsan (龍山) Artillery Regiment in Kyŏngsŏng, something similar to a common sense test was conducted for new recruits. The questions covered the most common topics like Emperor Jimmu (神武天皇), General Nogi (乃木將軍), the Russo-Japanese War (日露戰爭), etc. – eight questions in total. Seeing that only a few answered them correctly reveals the extent of their mental deficiencies (精神上 缺乏 如何도 推此可知이다). Perhaps Japanese people also need to focus a bit more on their own self-cultivation (自體의 修養)?

India's Harvest Last Winter Was Disastrous

India (印度), known as the paradise of the world for farming two or three times a year, unfortunately seems unable to avoid a bad harvest due to drought (旱魃) during last winter's farming season.

Alcohol Civilization Trying to Invade the East

A rather famous archaeologist in Italy (伊太利) recently published an essay stating that Italians' annual alcohol consumption reaches 16 billion lire ("리라"). Citing the 'American Prohibition Fact' as an example of alcohol's evils, he continued, saying that since America became a prohibition country, it seeks to export its alcoholic beverages to the East, encouraging drinking by selling them cheaply. Therefore, all peoples of the East should consider how America and Austria(?) (墺國 - could be Australia or Austria, context slightly favors Austria due to European perspective, but America exporting its surplus due to prohibition points more towards US origin alcohol) were poisoned (鴆毒) by that alcohol civilization and be especially wary. <83>

Sunday Entertainment Ban Bill Proposed

In the Near East(?) United States (近東 米國地方 - unusual phrasing, likely means Eastern USA), a movement for the realization of the so-called Blue Sunday Law is underway. Recently, a Blue Law bill (Sunday Entertainment Ban) submitted to the Tennessee (데네시) state legislature was particularly harsh. It proposed banning not only baseball and other games (遊戱) on Sunday, but also train operations, newspaper publication, all other business activities, and even church sermons and marital kissing (夫婦의 接吻). But here's another amusing thing: even violators of the Blue Law on Sunday would be entirely exempted from punishment execution on that day.

Divination Results Announced at the Peers' Club

On January 13th, the Japanese diviner Mr. Kodama Donshō (兒玉呑象씨), through the arrangement (斡旋) of Marquis Komura (小村侯 - son of the late Komura Jutarō 小村壽太郞), announced this year's divination results (易占) at the Peers' Club (華族會館). The gist was: "Regarding the year's affairs (年事), Japan will have abundance, China mediocrity, Britain and France scarcity. In foreign relations, Japan-UK and Japan-France will be friendly; Japan-Germany shows the form of a secret talk between two people; Japan-US and Japan-Russia will be somewhat uninteresting. In Germany, a famous person will meet a disastrous end (凶變). In Japan, the two elder statesmen Yamagata (山縣) and Matsukata (松方), and the three ministers Uchida (內田), Tanaka (田中), and Motoda (元田) will suffer illness or misfortune (病厄 又는 不安). The Diet will be uneventful," he reportedly said. Superstition cannot be absent from any nation, but that this was publicly announced at the Peers' Club is truly off-putting.

French Tiger Premier's Tiger Hunt

Mr. Clemenceau (「끌레만소」氏), the former French Premier known as the Tiger Premier for his masterful maneuvering as one of the Big Four at the Peace Conference, has recently been hunting tigers as a guest of the Maharajah Scindia(?) (싱띠아 太守), ruler of a protectorate in India. He reportedly shot two large tigers (well, they're having fun...).

Noteworthy Nationalist Sentiments in Various Countries

Many French (佛國) Lower House members have stated they will submit a resolution next year to expel (放逐) resident foreigners who do not possess a fixed livelihood and to levy heavy taxes on wealthy foreigners. Also, in the United States (米國), laws of a similar nature aimed at excluding foreigners (mainly East Asians - 東洋人) have been promulgated. Claiming justice and humanity, the nationalistic consciousness of various countries seems to be gradually strengthening, doesn't it?

Koreans Judging Koreans: Characters over Pictures

"Koreans Judging Koreans (the actual text says 流石은 鮮人 - 'As expected of Koreans'/'Truly Koreans'): Characters over Pictures" - I saw this interesting (滋味잇는) headline in the Manchuria-Korea edition (滿鮮版) of a certain Japanese newspaper published in Osaka (大阪) (mid-January). The headline being unusual, I read the article's content. It stated that at the Haeju Public Normal School (海州公立普通學校) in Hwanghae Province, student works were displayed from the 4th to the 6th, 9 AM to 4 PM daily, for public viewing. Among the 450 visitors (來觀者) over the three days, many Koreans were criticizing the quality of calligraphy (習字), while seeming to neglect (閑却) things like drawings (圖畵). This was likely true (事實). But must they write headlines twisting things (빗꼬아 가며) like that to feel satisfied? This is still tolerable. Whenever Japanese people write about anything related to Koreans, eight or nine times out of ten, they adopt this tone. For example, "Koreans also have beautiful customs," or "Koreans also have etiquette," or "Impressive for a Korean." I do not particularly wish to criticize (非難코저 함이 아니나) the attitude of the Japanese, but can the Japanese really mock Koreans to such an extent? What if the Japanese themselves think about things before 50 years ago? Until then, weren't Koreans, in many ways (如何間), more advanced than the Japanese? Weren't they also benefactors?

**Summary**

This article, "Voice of Society," from the March 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine, presents a collection of news snippets reflecting social conditions and events primarily in colonial Korea, alongside some international news. It highlights grim realities like child labor export, poverty-driven suicide, unemployment, and exploitation, often with critical commentary from the reporter on societal failures and parental irresponsibility. Contrasting these are positive stories of educational philanthropy, the formation of women's youth groups, and student initiatives. International items cover diverse topics from French population policy and German labor issues to American politics and social laws, and Japanese societal observations. The piece concludes with a self-deprecating critique of Japanese condescension towards Koreans and an appeal to readers for contributions.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for its raw portrayal of the social distress in early 1920s colonial Korea – poverty forcing child labor migration and suicides, economic hardship impacting workers, especially women, and the struggles faced by ordinary people. The reporter's explicit commentary provides insight into contemporary critical thought regarding social responsibility, the failures of the colonial economy, and nascent national consciousness. Historians might find the juxtaposition of local suffering with international events, the direct critique of Japanese attitudes ("Koreans Judging Koreans"), and the call for reader participation in documenting social realities particularly revealing about the intellectual climate and social concerns of the time, as reflected in a prominent nationalist magazine like *Kaebyŏk*.

社會의 聲
						一記者
現下의 우리 社會, 아니 우리 世界에는 떠드는 소리 퍽 만습니다. 큰 소리, 작은 소리, 반가운 소리, 슬픈 소리, 싸우는 소리, 和親하는 소리 이기어 헤일 수 업스리 만콤 만습니다. 이 모든 소리는 每日 出刊되는 諸多新聞紙이며 當時의 傳說에 依하야 別로 漏落되는 것 업시 우리의 귀에 들어오나이다. 그런데 그러한 모든 소리는 우리가 웃읍게 그저 귀ㅅ결로 들으랴면 들을 수가 잇는 同時에 얼마큼 意義를 부쳐 가지고 들을 수도 잇는 것이외다. 事實대로 말하면 그러한 소리, 其中에도 들릴락 말락한 작은 소리에 귀를 기울이고 마음을 붓(注)는 것이 意義 잇는 生活을 짓는 第一步라 할 것 이외다. 그러나 그러한 작은 소리(實狀 注意하여야 할 일)는 좀처럼 귀에 담지 아니합니다. 假令 新聞을 본다 하야도 初號一號活字로 表示한 커다라한 題에는 스스로 눈이 가나 四號五號의 족으마한 題에는 그저 넘어가게 됩니다. 이제 우리는 여긔에 感함이 有하야 여러분이 일즉 考閱하신 新聞雜誌 또는 傳說 中에서 족으만한 소리를 淨財가티 하야 本欄을 通하야 提供하오니 여러분께서 적다고 唾棄 아니하시면 적으나마 무엇이던지 어듬이 잇스리이다.

四十一名의 可憐한 兒童 먹기에 窮하야 日本으로

昨年 12月 19日 夜, 釜山港 出帆 聯絡船을 기다리는 14歲 15歲 假量의 어린 兄弟 41名의 一隊가 잇섯다. 그 아이들은 모다 自己 집 살림의 困難으로 얼굴빗은 甚히 蒼白하고 全身은 瘦瘠하야 一見에 마치 孤兒院에 收容된 兒童과 가탓다. 그런데 그들은 모다 東京市에서 琉璃製造業하는 日本人 高淸次郞君의 琉璃工場에 職工으로 募集되어 東京으로 向하는 것이엇는데 그 中에는 13, 4歲 未滿의 幼兒도 잇섯스며 雇傭으로 팔려가는 期間은 滿 3個年이며 雇金은 日給 60錢이엇섯다.
記者 曰, 다른 나라 다른 父兄에게 태어날 아이엿스면 따수한 父母의 膝下에서 어리광하며 工夫가 한참일 터인데 그러치 못하야 어리고 弱한 그들이 異域의 거친 바람에 눈물 먹음는 몸이 되도다. 다 만낫는 것이 壯한 줄만 알고 살아갈 힘을 주지 못하는 父母의 無責任도 無責任이거니와 그들의 제 밥 먹고 일할 工場 하나를 일으켜 주지 못한 우리의 産業界도 産業界이다.＜80＞

(中略) 玆에 最近에 重大問題로 보이는 것이 有하니 即 歐洲大戰의 남긴 遺産에 依하야 상긴 二大相反傾向이 有한 中 何者가 勝利를 할가 함이다. 卽 其一은 强國이 弱小國과 밋 그 領土에 對하야 經濟的 掠奪을 恣行하는 傾向이요 其二는 地球로써 大市邑으로 見做하고 世界的 意議 並 人類가 오즉 사람으로의 同胞에 對한 義務를 承認하는 傾向인 바 此 二者 中 何者가 勝利를 占할가 함은 今後 世界의 大問題이라 云云.

朝鮮佛敎革新案決議

朝鮮佛敎 30本山에서는 一月旬頃에 京城 覺皇寺에서 三十本山 各 代表總會를 開하고 左의 五個 革新條案을 決議하얏다.
一. 朝鮮佛敎를 根本으로부터 革新할 일.
二. 同時에 現在의 聯合會를 朝鮮宗務院이라 改稱할 일.
三. 現在 三十本山聯合事務所는 朝鮮佛敎의 活動上 場所가 適當치 못한 즉 適當한 곳을 18萬圓으로 買收하야 宗務院을 建築할 일.
四. 現在佛敎 中央學林을 根本的으로 改革하야 時代에 適當한 것을 敎授하여 高等普通學校程度로 하야 그 名稱을 佛敎專門學校라 改稱할 일.
五. 從來에는 各 本山에서 布敎하는 經營方針이 區域을 定하얏스나 이것을 統一的으로 布敎케 할 일.
右外에도 몃 가지 保留案이 잇섯다는데 此는 昨年 朝鮮佛敎를 日本의 臨濟宗과 合코저 運動하던 海印寺 住持 李晦光氏의 獨立布敎所 新建設(京城 西大門町에)과 相等하야 佛敎界에 注意할 만한 現狀이다.

日本人의 常識도 말 아니다

日本에는 一般 人民에게 對한 義務敎育이 有하고 且 明治維新 以來 文明의 衣를 被하야 그네들은 그래도 웬 만하리라 思하얏스나 實際를 보면 그러치도 못한 樣이다. 先年 日本에서 3千人의 巡査를 募集할 時에 常識 試驗으로 「**만소」라는 것이 무엇이냐 하얏는데 解答者가 그 3千人 中 幾人이 못 되고 苛性曹達의 一種이라 答한 者가 多數라는 말을 들엇더니 先者 京城 龍山 砲兵聯隊에서 初年兵에 對한 常識試驗 비슷한 것을 行하얏는데 그 問題는, 神武天皇, 乃木將軍, 日露戰爭 等의 最通俗的의 8問題이엇는데 그것을 正解한 자가 幾人이 못되엇다 함을 見하면 그네의 精神上 缺乏 如何도 推此可知이다. 日本人된 者도 爲先 自體의 修養에 좀 注力할 必要가 잇지 안흘가.

東洋을 襲코저 하는 酒精文明

伊太利에 꽤 有名한 考古學者의 一人은 近者 一論文을 公表하야 伊太利人의 每年 飮酒 消費額이 160億 「리라」에 達한다 述하고 酒의 罪惡의 例로 「米國禁酒事實을 擧하고 繼하야 米國은 禁酒國이 된 뒤로 自國의 酒類를 東洋에 輸出하야 飮酒를 奬勵코저 廉價로 賣却코저 한 즉 東洋의 全 民族은 米國及墺國이 그 酒精文明에 鴆毒된 것이 如何한 것을 思하고 特히 警戒함이 有하라 하얏다.＜83＞

日曜娛樂禁止法案提出

近東 米國地方에는 所謂 靑色日曜法案實現運動이 行한다는데 最近 「데네시」州議會에 提出되엇던 靑色法案(日曜娛樂禁止案)은 其中에 苛酷하얏다. 即 日曜日에는 野球 其他 遊戱는 勿論 列車의 運轉, 新聞紙의 發行, 其他 一切 營業을 始하야 敎會의 說敎夫婦의 接吻까지 禁하자는 것이엇다. 그런데 여긔에 또 한가지 웃으운 것은 日曜日에는 비록 靑色法案違反者이라도 全部 形의 執行을 免除하야 준다는 것이다.

華族會舘에서 易占發表

日本의 易占者는 兒玉呑象씨는 1月 13日 小村侯(故小村壽太郞之子)의 斡旋으로 華族會館에서 本年度의 易占을 發表하얏는데 封에 曰「年事는 日本은 豊, 中國은 中, 英佛은 凶이요 外交로 말하면 日英日佛은 親善, 日獨은 二人의 密談의 形, 日米日露는 좀 자미 업엇스며 獨逸에는 名士에게 凶變이 잇고 日本에는 山縣, 松方의 二元老, 內田, 田中, 元田의 三大臣에게는 病厄 又는 不安이엇슬 터이요 議會는 無事하리라」하얏다 한다. 迷信이란 何民族에게도 업지는 못하나 이것이 華族會館에서 公公然히 發布까지 하얏다는 것은 참 情떼는 말이다.

鮮人이 鮮人, 繪보다도 字

「鮮人이 鮮人(實際의 本文은 流石은 鮮人)繪보다 字이라」는 滋味잇는 題目을 大阪發行 某日本 新聞 滿鮮版(一月旬)에서 보앗다. 問題가 異常하야 記事內容을 본 즉 黃海道 海州公立普通學校에서 4日 至 6日間 每日 午前 九時至 午後 4時까지 同校 內에 生徒의 成績品을 陳列하고 一般의 觀覽에 供하얏는데 3日間의 來觀者 450名 中 鮮人은 習字의 良否를 批評하는 者가 多하고 圖畵와 가튼 것은 자못 閑却하는 感이 有하얏다 云한 것이다. 事實은 勿論 事實이엇슬 것이다. 그러나 그러케 빗꼬아 가며 題目을 쓰어야 마음에 快할가. 이것은 오히려 可하다. 全體로 日本人에 關한 무엇에 붓을 들면 十中八九는 반듯이 이러한 투를 쓴다. 卽「 朝鮮人에게도 美風이 잇다. 或은 朝鮮人에게도 禮義가 잇다. 又는 朝鮮人으로는 壯하다」하는 等이다. 구타여 日本人의 態度를 非難코저 함이 아니나 日本人으로서야 그러케가지 朝鮮人을 비웃을 수가 잇슬가. 日本 自己네라고 自今 50年 以前의 일을 생각하면 엇덜가. 그때까지는 如何間 朝鮮人은 日本인 보다의 先進者가 아니엇는가 또 恩人이 아니엇는가.
