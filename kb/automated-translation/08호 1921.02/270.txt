I am but an ignorant man from a mountain village, one who has not even read a single newspaper or magazine devotedly. Saying "I too am a person," I just went along, thinking "if others do this, perhaps it is so," hearing this and that, seeing this writing and that writing. Then, sometime ago, someone called "Gaebyeok" from Gaebyeoksa in Kyŏngsŏng, disregarding the long distance, came to this inconvenient place looking for this ignorant person. Setting aside various things, I asked for an introduction, how they came to be here with such trouble, and where they came from. They said, "The people of Chosŏn are sleeping so frustratingly, so I am traveling around to wake them up as best as I know how, and there is nowhere I haven't been." "Then, please teach me as well, and I will follow even one ten-thousandth of your guidance. Please tell me." Among the many truly interesting things they said, hearing about Innaech'ŏn-ism (人乃天主義), the Woman Question (婦人問題), the Labor Question (勞動問題), and the Construction of a New Korean Culture (朝鮮新文化建設), even an ignorant person's spirit naturally becomes refreshed (洒落), and my heart is simply uncontrollably joyful. Feeling regretful that such a person had not come sooner, I asked, "Then, do the people at your company all write something like a magazine?" "Yes, there is. It's none other than the magazine that everyone says is splendid, sharing the same name." "It is published once a month, and you will understand when you see its contents." Thank you very much. I hope you will become ever healthier and teach much for the sake of our youth, for the sake of Chosŏn, no, for the sake of the world. From that day on, I ordered and read it, and indeed, there is much worth reading. Even this ignorant person finds it very interesting now, so I hope all you readers will read it with an even newer spirit. And for those brothers across the four seas who unfortunately have not yet seen it, even if you skip one meal out of the three daily meals, please order it from afar, read it with interest, reform what needs reforming, build what needs building, and with the responsible method of pioneers, pass it on to the general brothers and sisters who have been sleeping until now. At the same time, I add a word to welcome and introduce the birth of the Gaebyeok magazine... (From the village of Sŏngji-dong, Tongsin-myŏn, Unsan Kim Ŭi-jun (雲山金儀俊))...

Elm Tree Shade (Continued from previous issue)
Kaesŏng Kyebong

On the white sand dune dazzling under the scattered light beams,
Leaving one or two footprints,
Entering the shade of the thickly grown elm tree,
A seven-colored rainbow powder hangs across the blue sky.
Golden carp dance upon the brilliant water surface.

Do the eternal elm tree and the meandering waves
Not speak of memories of bygone days?
When the wind carrying the scent of flowers swept the water's surface,
Ah, here, pressing a soft cheek and singing,
Thoughts of the dear girl I played with in childhood.

The cicada's song resonates beautifully through the woods,
When the clear river wind raises silver waves,
Her lovely voice seems clear and her cheek seems to touch mine,
Ah, singing, looking up at the 'orange'-colored sky,
Where is the girl, my childhood playmate...

Ah, holding this single lovely sweet-briar rose (海棠花),
In the girl's innocent, crystal-palace-like heart,
I want to place this flower, bury my head in its faint fragrance,
Suppressing my unbearably longing heart,
I wander here and there, crying out in sorrow,
Ah, where has the girl gone?

Grass, smother my chest!
Elm tree, press down on my back!
Waves, wash through my heart!
Ah, rainbow, drink this liquid of love!

Only the fallen sweet-briar blossom trembles its hand in the clear water,
Flowing on the water like a seagull clothed in white clouds.
Ah, lovely girl who should receive this flower,
Where are you?

Ah, the lovely girl must be in a bolted inner room,
Blocked by the wall of 'naeoe' (內外, separation of sexes).
Here where green shade flows, the time we played together,
The blue waves that embraced us,
The affectionate little birds that blessed us,
The elm shade that sheltered us,
Where have they gone? These are not them now.

Ah, endlessly sorrowful!
The sweet-briar color blooming on your cheeks,
When can I face it with the innocence of childhood again?
When will the flowing river of that time pass before me again?
Sorrowful, your heart like when you came out then...
The distant church bell we heard together rings, ddeng ddeng
Where has the girl I played with gone!
Only the sound of the lonely flute is heard. <107>

Towards Gaebyeok-gun
Ŭnam Ch'o Ki-yŏng from Myŏngch'ŏn County

Gaebyeok-gun, advancing into perilous territory, embracing boundless ambition! Why was your appearance in this world so delayed? I wish to ask the reason for that delay. No, I can guess the reason myself. I wish that, having entered the world, you will not retreat but advance bravely. Be healthy, be healthy. I only wish eternal health for you, Gaebyeok-gun.
Ah, Gaebyeok-gun! Is not your duty 'Gaebyeok' (Creation/Opening)? Since you bear such a duty heavily on both shoulders, engage in spiritual activity for the work of Gaebyeok even one day sooner. In my view, the land of the peninsula seems dark. When opening up the darkness, if you do not firmly grasp a sharp axe and a good knife with both hands, it will be nothing more than a plan on paper. Prepare without hindrance.
Ah, Gaebyeok-gun! I have waited long for you. You are truly my good companion and beneficial friend, so I wish to interact with you daily, associate with you hourly, and failing that, form a permanent friendship. As you know, my first friend is you, and next are the Seoul and *Kwang magazines, but it is you with whom I wish to form a permanent friendship. Therefore, I even give warm introductions to the local gentlemen, encouraging them to associate with you. Moreover, since you sent me a letter with such warm affection back then, I now express my gratitude with this, and at the same time, send your travel expenses via postal transfer. Please do not mind the long journey of 1,500 li, do not delay month after month, and I hope you will come. Also, I hope you will be even healthier in the New Year of Sinyu (辛酉).

Two Sijo Poems
P'yŏngyang Hwang Kyŏng-hwan

Sorrow
Things that involve sorrow, I put them aside here and there,
Only the sorrow-less things, I picked them out and did them,
But others, even if I starve and wear rags, pretend not to see.

Worry
To live is worry, to live well is worry,
Having become human among all creation, can such worries be absent?
In the world, if you want to see true worry, see non-worry (無걱정).

Fantasy
Unch'ŏnsaeng

On the night of August 15th, gradually enveloped in silence,
Under the clear moonlight, alone, leaning against (?)
A poplar tree, I weakly embraced it with both arms.
The cool wind blowing directly through the moonlight brightly risen in the eastern sky,
I tried to gather handfuls, armfuls of it.
My heart felt refreshed.

The unseen infinite curtain, passing one day, two days,
Layer by layer, piling up daily,
With no resonance to the angel's voice, no hearing of God's word,
Not knowing the way forward, groping in the pitch-black darkness ahead,
Under the bright, bright moon, I wished to become clearly revealed.

I gazed at it in one place. That distinctly bright moon.
And I also compared it. How large was its size?
"If I spread my arms and embraced it, it would surely be one armful,"
Closing my eyes, I quietly imagined.
Going out to the East Coast, before it rises into the sky,
Stretching out one tiptoe, spreading both arms wide, with eyes sharpened,
As it rises from the sea and brushes against my arms,
If I swiftly embraced it tightly, I too would soar into the sky.<108>
Closing my eyes, I quietly imagined.

Soaring high into the sky together with that moon,
The beloved child nestled in its mother's bosom,
Those dreaming sweet dreams in a cozy place,
Those sighing long sighs in this painful world,
Together with "Time" flowing infinitely, infinitely,
Human life advancing silently, I too would watch,
Closing my eyes, I quietly imagined.

Ah, this fantasy!
I absently shook the "poplar" I had embraced with both arms.
Only awakening them from their deep slumber in the nest,
The sparrows fluttered away, hududuk hududuk. (End)

Crossing Manse Bridge
Hamhŭng Han Kyŏng-sik

Gazing blankly at the snowy color of dusk faintly visible on the distant mountains, my mind randomly troubled by endless thoughts, shedding tears of sorrow for a couple of nights, I now happen to cross Manse Bridge.
No, I cannot help but call this my happiness too. The rays of the lingering sunset that stretched over the western mountains also gradually fade, hiding their traces in the mountain shadows, and the dark blue curtain of dusk surrounds all directions. The so-called severe winter wind, occasionally blowing across the formidable Manse Bridge, strikes my face, indescribable by mouth or brush, and flies on, skimming the frozen surface of the Sŏngch'ŏn stream visible before me. I looked once more at the snow-covered mountains winding and lurking across the northern region. They too evoke thoughts of past sorrow and dreams of a hazy future. Especially when I think of Mr. Yi (李君), who fought against the harsh winds and bitter cold in the Siberian (西伯利亞) cold zone, only to become food for the crows and magpies (烏鵲) of Siberia, my heart truly stings, my head aches, and I cannot suppress the sadness and pain. Ah, Mr. Yi! For your parents, for your siblings, for OO, you fought against all those hardships in a foreign land, only to finally tread the sorrowful path.
Muttering this forcefully to myself, I could no longer hold back the tears that welled up again.
Indeed, Mr. Yi was a genius. You always told me, "Strive! Struggle!" Ah, you must have said it with some profound meaning, but this foolish fellow did not understand its meaning well. Right, but now I understand its meaning well. Oh, and so, Mr. Yi ultimately sacrificed his assets and entire body to striving and struggling. Truly, you were a brilliant Mr. Yi. However, our Chosŏn society was too young and weak, unable to use such a genius, leaving him neglected. I shouted again, "Strive! Struggle!" clenching both fists tightly. And I didn't realize my head naturally felt heavy. Indeed, I think deeply, deeply of you. Ah, but Mr. Yi, where are you now?
While doing this, I had already crossed the bridge. The cold I had tried to forget for a moment was felt once again. (Night of 1921, 1, 3, From a corner of Hŭngdo-ri)

**Summary**

This section, "Readers' Correspondence Column" (讀者交情欄), from issue 8 of the magazine Gaebyeok (開闢), presents a collection of letters, poems, and short prose pieces from readers across Korea in the early 1920s. Readers express deep gratitude for the magazine, viewing it as a vital source of enlightenment and a catalyst for building a "new Korean culture." They share personal experiences, request information (like how to obtain the Chinese magazine "Hsin Ch'ing-nien"), submit literary works for critique and publication, and encourage others to read Gaebyeok. The letters reflect a strong desire for knowledge, social progress (mentioning topics like women's rights and labor issues), and national consciousness, portraying the magazine as a crucial forum for intellectual exchange and community building during a period of darkness.

From a historical perspective, this column is valuable as it provides direct insight into the thoughts, aspirations, and literacy levels of ordinary Koreans during the early colonial period. The readers' engagement with intellectual currents (like the Chinese literary revolution, Innaech'ŏn-ism), their discussions of social issues, the widespread desire for "enlightenment" and "civilization," and the sense of shared identity and purpose fostered by the magazine highlight the burgeoning nationalist and modernist sentiments circulating despite colonial constraints. The geographical diversity of the contributors (P'yŏngyang, Munch'ŏn, Sŏngch'ŏn, Yangsan, Kyŏmi-p'o, Hamhŭng, etc.) also indicates the magazine's reach.

萬歲橋를 건너며서
咸興韓景植

遠山에 히음히 보이는 黃昏의 雪色을 精神업시 바라보며 끗업는 생각에 空然히 마음이 쓸려서 두어밤을 悲哀의 눈물을 흘리며 나는 지금 萬歲橋를 건너게 되엇다.
아니 이것도 나의 幸福이라 하지 안흘 수 업다. 西山에 뻐첫던 殘照의 光線도 차츰차츰 사라저서 山그림자에 자최를 감추고 黃昏의 검푸른 幕이 四方을 에워 쌋는다. 잇다금 잇다금 萬歲의 畏橋를 빗겨부는 所謂 嚴冬寒風은 口筆로도 形容하야 말할 수 업는 이 나의 얼굴을 한 번 치고는 저 眼前에 얼어 보이는 成川水面을 씨츠며 날아간다. 나는 北方一帶에 굽을굽을 潛伏한 雪山을 또 한 번 바라 보앗다. 그는 亦是 過去의 悲哀와 未來渺然한 꿈을 聯想케 한다. 더욱 저 西伯利亞寒帶에서 모진 바람과 모진 추위에 싸우다가 그만 西伯利亞의 烏鵲의 밥이 된 李君을 생각할 때에는 참으로 가슴이 쏘고 머리가 압흐며 슯흐고 苦痛함을 抑制할 수 업다. 아아 李君이어 君은 父母를 爲하고 兄弟를 爲하고 OO을 爲하야 異境에서 그 모진 모든 苦痛과 奮鬪하시다가 마츰 最終의 슯흔 길을 밟게 되엇도다.
나는 이처럼 혼자 입속말로 힘잇게 부르짓고 다시는 다지는 눈물을 禁할 수 업섯다.
과연 李君은 天才이엇섯다. 君은 恒常 나에게「努力하라 奮鬪하라」고 말하엿다. 아아 君은 무슨 絶大한 意味로 한 말이엇것마는 미련한 이 놈은 그 뜻을 잘 理解치 못하엿다. 올타 그러나 至今은 그 뜻을 잘알겟다. 오오 그리하야 李君은 마츰내 資産과 全肉身까지 努力과 奮鬪에 犧牲하엿도다. 果然 聰明한 李君이엇다. 그러나 우리 이 朝鮮社會가 넘우 어리고 弱하야 그런 天才를 쓰지 못하고 내버려 두엇다. 나는 다시「努力하라 奮鬪하라」부르지즈며 두 주먹을 붉근 쥐엇다. 그리고 머리가 自然이 묵어워짐을 깨닷지 못하얏다. 果然 나는 君을 깁히깁히 생각한다. 아아 그러나 李君이어 君은 지금 어대 잇는가.
나는 이러는 사이에 벌석 다리는 다 건너게 되엇다. 暫時間 이저버리려는 추위는 또 다시 느끼게 되엇다.(1921,1,3,夜興島里 一隅에서)
