Article Title: 죽음의 美 (The Beauty of Death)

The Beauty of Death
						Kim Sŏksong

One
Modern people! Ah, affectionate modern people!
Comrades with human consciences free of dust (塵)!
Remember October 25, 1920!
Within your hearts, what day this is.

Though there are many days to remember in the 5,000 years since human history began,
Remember especially October 25, 1920!
And also remember June 28, 1919.
Within your hearts, what day this is.


Two
Far, far away towards the setting sun,
Where the Atlantic Ocean (大西洋) and the Arctic Ocean (北冰洋) meet,
The people of Ireland (아일랜드)! Who have persistently continued
A seven-hundred-year blood-fight for freedom (自由)!

"Give us freedom (自有) or give us death,"
As if moved exactly by the appeal of those few,
Their finally launching a "hunger strike"
Is the greatest wonder (最大의 驚異), immeasurable by human intellect (智力)!


Three
The Mayor (市長) of Cork (콜크), a city (都市) in the south (南方) -
That MacSwiney (맥스위니) and 10 others were captured on August 12th
For the crime (?) of being leaders (頭領) of the Republican Army (共和軍),<100>
Is already an old page in humanity's record book (備忘錄).

Their bodies were transported under guard (護送) to England (英蘭),
And as soon as they were thrown into the sinister iron bars (鐵窓) of Brixton (뿌릭스튼),
That they again resolutely carried out (決行) a "hunger strike"
Still whispers in our memory (記憶).


Four
But on October 25th, at 5:40 AM (午前)!
Mayor (市長) MacSwiney (맥스위니) of Cork (콜크) finally
Passed away (殞命) in prison (獄中) after 74 days of fasting (絶食) -
This heroic (悲壯) news (報道) violently struck the eardrums (鼓膜) of humankind.

For the last (最后) 36 hours (時間), he lost consciousness (意識),
Not recognizing even his father (父親) watching over his beloved son's (愛兒) noble (高潔) deathbed (臨終),
Nor his loving younger brother,
And quietly disappeared with the dawn star, they say.


Five
When his death was once heard in the world,
Not only every single person, but even the aggressors (侵略主義者) who killed him,
There was none who was not dumbfounded (失魂落魄)
As if struck by a thunderbolt (霹靂) out of a clear blue sky (靑天白日).
All the citizens (市民) of Ireland (아일랜드) wore mourning clothes (喪服),
The Pope (法王) of Rome (로-마) held a long (長時間) silent prayer (黙禱),
It created great ripples (波紋) in the political world (政界) of the United States (米國),
And humankind deeply engaged in self-reflection (反省) and repentance (懺悔).


Six
The executor (實行者) of the hunger strike pact (絶食同盟), MacSwiney (맥스위니),
His body draped in pure white (純白) vestments (法衣),
Through the warm arrangements (周旋) of close friends (知己) from his homeland (故國),
In the Sinn Féin (신핀) party members' (黨員) cemetery (墓地) outside Cork (콜크) city,
With arms linked alongside his comrades (同志) from life (生前),
Came to sing of freedom (自由) forever (永遠).
However, until the final moment (瞬間) his remains (遺骸)
Were buried three feet underground (地下),
Two armored cars (裝甲自働車) carrying loaded (裝彈) machine guns (機關銃)
And six motor trucks (自働貨車) filled with soldiers (兵丁)
Controlled even the atmosphere (空氣) of the ceremony site (式場).


Seven
Thus the spirit (精靈) of patriotism disappeared forever,
Radiating (放射) great (偉大) rays of light (光線) like the setting sun (落日),
Quietly holding its heavy burden (業).
But the brilliant (燦然) radiance (光輝) of the multitude of stars (群星)<101>
Soon solemnly (莊嚴히) adorned (粧飾) the cosmos (宇宙).

In the week (一週間) after the Mayor's death (死後),
The demands (要求) of the Irish people (愛蘭人) for freedom (自由) reached 95 times,
Two million MacSwineys (맥스위니) were revived (甦生),
And in the withered chests of countless disenfranchised (失權者),
The blood (血液) of bright new life (生) began to boil.


Eight
Executors of the hunger strike (絶食)—before MacSwiney (맥스위니),
It's not that Bo Yi (伯夷) and Shu Qi (叔齊) did not exist,
But anyway, the hunger strike pact (絶食同盟) is a new act (新行爲) of new humanity (新人間).
Though the tongues of cunning (狡滑) militarists (軍國主義者)
Scoff at this death as utilitarian (功利的),
Life (人生) sometimes makes life (生) out of death (死).
Living (生活) is art (藝術), and action (行爲) is creation (創作).
Beauty (美) and utility (功利) seem to conflict (背馳), yet they do not.


Nine
Ah, what a solemn (莊嚴) death!
O tragic (悲壯) appeal of the oppressed (壓迫) masses!
His outcry (絶呌) in silence (沈黙) shall echo (反嚮) throughout the world (全世界)!
His most beloved woman (女性) is already in the Americas (米洲),
Following his voice, crying out.

To be trampled (蹂躙) for justice (正義) is glory (光榮).
The resentment (憤怨) of the weak (弱者), which seemed capable of piercing (뚤흘 듯하던) even an iron plate (鐵板),
Once again seems poised to grind down and drink the whole world,
And the courage (勇氣) dreaming of victory (勝利) seems capable of moving (*) even Mount T'ae (泰山)!
Ah! Sublime (崇高) death!
Ah! October 25, 1920!


Ten
The day holy (聖潔) spirits shed blood
To eliminate the very noun (名詞) "the weak (弱者)."
June 28, 1919—
Remember it together with the day the blood price (血價) of the weak was sealed (細音한 날),
Forever (永遠) and ever (永遠)!
Modern people! Stripped bare (밝아 벗은) modern people!

(Thirty days after the Mayor's death (沒後), in a corner of the East (東方一隅))<102>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏksong commemorates the death of Terence MacSwiney, the Lord Mayor of Cork, who died from a hunger strike on October 25, 1920, protesting British rule in Ireland. Kim portrays MacSwiney's death not as a failure but as a "beautiful," "sublime" sacrifice that serves as a powerful act of resistance and creation. The poem contrasts MacSwiney's spiritual victory and the subsequent revival of the Irish struggle ("Two million MacSwineys were revived") with the brutality of the oppressors (British militarists). It explicitly urges contemporary Koreans ("Modern people") to remember MacSwiney's death alongside June 28, 1919 (Treaty of Versailles signing), framing these events as crucial moments in the global struggle of the "weak" against oppression.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem is highly significant. It demonstrates how Korean intellectuals and nationalists actively consumed news of international anti-colonial movements and drew direct parallels between Ireland's fight against Britain and Korea's struggle against Japan. By celebrating MacSwiney's hunger strike as a heroic and effective form of resistance ("New act of new humanity"), the poem implicitly encourages similar resolve and sacrifice among Koreans. The linking of MacSwiney's death with the date of the Treaty of Versailles highlights the disillusionment with international diplomacy (which failed to grant Korea independence despite Wilsonian promises) and underscores the belief that liberation required direct action and sacrifice by the oppressed themselves. Historians would find the poem valuable for understanding the transnational consciousness within the Korean independence movement, the adoption and interpretation of global resistance tactics like the hunger strike, and the use of poetry as a tool for political mobilization and inspiration in colonial Korea.

죽음의 美
						金石松
一
現代人아! 아 情다운 現代人들아!
티(塵)도 업는 人間의 良心을 가진 동무들아!
記憶하여라 1920年 10月 25日!
이날이 무슨 날임을 너의 마음속에

人類의 歷史가 잇슨 後 5千年, 記憶할 날이 만치만
特別히 記憶하여라, 1920年 10月 25日!
그리고 또 다시 1919年의 6月 28日을
이 날이 무슨 날임을 너의 마음 속에


二
멀리 멀리 해지는 쪽으로
大西洋과 北冰洋이 合水되는 곳에
自由를 爲하야 七百年의 피싸움을
끈기잇게 이어 오는 「아일랜드」의 民族!

「自有를 다구치 안흐면 죽음을」하는
몃 사람의 부르지즘 고대로 옴긴 듯이
마츰내「헝거스트라익」을 일으킴은
人間의 智力으로는 헤일 수 업는 最大의 驚異!


三
南方의 一都市「콜크」의 市長-
「맥스위니-」等 11名이 8月 12日에
共和軍의 頭領이란 罪(?)로 잡히어 감은＜100＞
벌서 人類의 備忘錄 中에 묵은 페이지다.

그들의 몸은 英蘭으로 護送되어
「뿌릭스튼」의 陰險한 鐵窓 속에 던지우자 마자
「헝거스트라익」을 또다시 決行함도
우리의 記憶이 아즉껏 속살거린다.


四
그러나 10月 25日 午前 5時 40分!
「콜크」市長「맥스위니-」는 마츰내
絶食 74日만에 獄中에서 殞命하얏다는
悲壯한 報道는 人間의 鼓膜을 激烈히 울리엇다.

最后의 36時間은 意識을 일코
愛兒의 高潔한 臨終을 보삷히는
그의 父親과 사랑하는 아우도 몰라 보고
고요히 새벽별과 한께 사라젓다 하는


五
그의 죽음이 한 번 世上에 들리매
사람이란 사람은 勿論, 그를 죽인 侵略主義者까지
靑天白日에 霹靂이 나린 듯
失魂落魄아니한 者가 업스며
온「아일랜드」의 市民은 喪服을 입고
「로-마」의 法王은 長時間의 黙禱을 하얏고
米國政界엔 큰 波紋을 그리게 되고
人類는 그윽히 反省과 懺悔를 하게 되엇다.


六
絶食同盟의 實行者「맥스위니-」는
純白한 法衣로 몸을 가리고
故國의 知己의 따뜻한 周旋으로
「콜크」市外「신핀」黨員의 墓地에
生前의 同志와 팔을 겻고
永遠히 自由를 노래하게 되엇다.
그러나 그의 遺骸가 三尺의 地下에
마즈막 무티는 瞬間까지도
裝彈機關銃을 실흔 裝甲自働車 二臺와
兵丁을 가득 실흔 自働貨車 六臺가
式場의 空氣까지 團束하얏다.


七
이리하야 愛國의 精靈은 永遠히 사라젓다
마치 落日과 가티 偉大한 光線을 放射하며
묵어운 업을 담으른 채로 고요히
그러나 群星의 燦然한 光輝는＜101＞
未久에 宇宙를 莊嚴히 粧飾하얏다.

市長의 死後 一週間에
愛蘭人의 自由를 要求함은 95次
二百萬의 「맥스위니-」는 甦生하얏고
無數한 失權者의 말른 가슴엔
새ㅅ밝안 生의 血液이 끌케 되엇다.


八
絶食을 實行한 者-「맥스위니-」前에도
伯夷의 叔齊가 업슴은 아니나
어쨰던 絶食同盟은 新 人間의 新 行爲이다.
狡滑한 軍國主義者의 혀로는
이네의 죽음을 功利的이라 嘲笑하나
人生은 이따금 死로써 生을 삼기도 한다.
生活은 藝術이요 行爲는 創作이다
美와 功利는 背馳되는 듯하고도 아니다.


九
아아 莊嚴한 죽음이어!
