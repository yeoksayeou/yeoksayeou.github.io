南信北通 (News from South and North)
The Current State of Border Farming Villages

Pyŏktong Branch, A Reporter

Seen from any perspective, the most pitiful situation in the regions is, above all, that of the mountain farming villages. The living conditions of our compatriots are truly cruel and pitiable, making it difficult to describe. The fact that they are now wandering in famine due to last year's poor harvest is a misfortune shared throughout Chosŏn, but it seems there are compatriots who, having barely survived since last winter shedding tears of sorrow because they had nothing to eat, are now trying to preserve their lives this spring with grass roots and tree bark. Truly, by whose power can they be saved? Amidst this, the particular suffering unique to these border farming villages is, as reported daily in the newspapers, the collection of military funds by those OOO people. And also, the crackdown on 'disloyal Koreans' (不逞鮮人取締). The sorrow and resentment of enduring such double and triple trampling (蹂躪) are truly beyond the capacity of the brush to record one by one. Compatriots facing this unexpected calamity find it impossible to live in the mountain farming villages, and the number of migrants flocking suddenly to the cities of those regions is countless.

Also, among these issues, the pain of ignorance is the most severe. There is no place to learn even if one wants to, and no one to listen to, so most seem to fall into the pit of superstition (迷信), lacking the courage to overcome the adversity of fear and trembling, seemingly filled only with the atmosphere of an era of polytheism. Why has our life fallen into such a miserable state?
How can this not be a cause for lament in the farming villages? Like Mr. Ch'unp'a's words published in the May issue of Kaebyŏk, I hope that a special "Farming Village Section" (農村欄) will be established to provide even a small convenience to the farmers.

Kanghwa Dispatch

Kanghwa, A Reporting Student

△ Several individuals known as elders (長老) of the Kanghwa Chamdu Presbyterian Church (江華 蠶頭耶蘇敎會) have resolved absolutely not to lend the church hall to the general public. Under the pretext of it being a "sacred church," they invariably refuse to lend it even for lectures or debates organized by the local youth association. If there were another suitable location, we wouldn't insist on borrowing it, but since it is the only meeting place, it greatly hinders youth activities and various other aspects in the Kanghwa region. One reason for their resolution is that during an amateur play (for famine relief) hosted by the Kanghwa Central Youth Association on a day around January of this year (solar calendar), the comedy "A Blind Man Reading Scriptures" performed inside the sacred church was deemed tasteless. Another reason is that during a lecture hosted by the Kanghwa Mumyŏng Alliance (江華無名同盟) this spring, Messrs. Pak Ilbyŏng <32> and Chŏng Chongmyŏng mentioned that among Christians (who preach love and such with their mouths but practice all sorts of hypocrisy in reality), which was interpreted as calling the religion hypocritical. Most of these so-called elders are old men in their 70s and 80s. It is laughable that they, having no understanding of religion and merely advocating theories of heaven and hell, cause such hindrances like the lack of a meeting place due to their superstitious actions regarding religion and the stubbornness of these useless elders. We vehemently denounce how detrimental this is to society.

△ In Yangdo-myŏn, Kanghwa County, the campaign for the position of township head (面長 - myŏnjang) seems quite fierce. After the previous township head, Min T'aehŏn, died last month, there are reportedly over 20 people currently maneuvering in various ways to become the next township head. Whether they campaign for the position or not is irrelevant, but many of these so-called campaigners are individuals with some money. Not only are there those with specialized knowledge engaging in base actions to become township head, but among them is a certain Kwŏn OO, who was previously ousted as township head years ago due to opposition from the township residents following an incident of attempted rape of a young woman from someone's house. Now, he is said to be the most likely candidate again. A laughable anecdote among this is that even the township office servant (面小使 - myŏnso sa), a certain Kye, is one of the campaigners. It's said some started campaigning upon hearing Min might be ill even before he died, and others came to the county seat (府內) the day after he died to campaign.
One might dismiss this merely as something to laugh about, but no matter how rampant the running dogs (走狗) are in this world, shouldn't they realize how dirty their actions are? Utterly ridiculous!

P'yŏngyang Ramblings

Recently, the society sections of various newspapers carry numerous articles about sons hitting fathers, younger brothers insulting older brothers, and other such acts that destroy ethics and defy norms (滅倫悖常). While this is undoubtedly the ultimate destruction caused by the so-called almighty power of wealth (財産萬能), such heinous examples seem to exist even in the 'embroidered rivers and mountains' (錦繡江山) of P'yŏngyang.
In our P'yŏngyang, as anyone might have heard, Mr. Kim U-yŏng (金牛盈 - pseudonym) seems so possessed by that thing called money that upon forming intimacy with money, he severed all ties of father-son and brotherly affection. On one occasion, he refused even his father's request for a bowl of water, leading to a kitchen knife fight (食刀戰). Next, trying to seize his own younger brother's house, he had the house mortgaged under another person's name, lent money against it, and when payment wasn't made by the deadline, had it auctioned off and acquired it for half price. When this was discovered, it led to a second fight between the brothers. Like brother, like brother; U-yŏng's younger brother, U-t'u (牛鬪 - pseudonym), lent a few roof tiles (盖瓦) to his own father, <33> and when they weren't returned within the agreed period, acting like a brat, he went to his father's house and stripped off all the roof tiles, causing a third brawl (鬪鬨).
Under today's system (制度), it is true that money is valuable to a certain extent, and it is also true that because of it, human nature fundamentally becomes base (野卑化) and vicious (凶惡化). However, for animals more hideous than beasts (禽獸) to emerge in the land of P'yŏngyang, called 'embroidered rivers and mountains,' what misfortune is this? It is truly something to blush (赤面) for Kisŏng society (箕城社會 - another name for P'yŏngyang), and simultaneously, a matter for the general awakening (警醒) of the propertied class who seem to know nothing but money.
(P'yŏngyang, A Reporter)

Chinju Miscellany

Chinju Student

Late last month, Chinju Agricultural School (晋州農校) and Kyŏngnam Public Normal School (慶南公立師範學校) held their entrance exams. An interesting issue arose: both school authorities reportedly absolutely forbade admission specifically to last year's graduates of common schools (普校 - pot'ong hakkyo) (limited to Chinju residents). The reason given was that these graduates, in the year leading up to this spring, did not engage in self-cultivation (修養) but instead professionally frequented taverns (酒店) and red-light districts (花柳界) day and night, thus having the disposition of vagrants (浮浪者) and being deemed unqualified. How dreadful are these words! Parents scrape together money from east and west, borrowing bits and pieces to cover tuition fees and other school supplies. They seek out whatever they can find, enduring rough clothes and coarse food themselves, just so their children won't fall behind others. They pour their hearts out like a single day over the long six years, yet even achieving that much hope and expectation would be hard to satisfy. But without a shred of reward, failing to meet the parents' expectations and hopes, and furthermore bringing disgrace (侮辱) upon their parents through their post-graduation activities – which parent does not love their child, but seeing them worsen through study, would they gladly continue their education?
Parents are parents, but first and foremost, your future, which stretches for ten thousand li (萬里), is concerning. It may seem like a small matter (小事), but how much disappointment has this incident caused the parents? Will your future (前途) not face even greater disasters? And the hindrance to those who come after you is also great. Who knows what fierce winds might arise during the higher common school entrance exams? Gentlemen! Will you have fierce self-reflection (猛然 自省)?!

Kowŏn County, Full of Distrust

A Reporter

They say there are many stones on the riverbanks in the city and many stars in the empty heavens, and in Kowŏn County (高原郡), there is much distrust (不信任). Distrust of the township head, distrust of the district head (區長 - kujang), distrust of the county magistrate (郡守 - kunsu) – what on earth is going on? Is it due to the insincerity (無誠意) of the authorities' administration? Or is it because the consciousness (覺醒) of the populace has advanced (進就)? Whatever the reason, <34> the administrative world of this county undeniably feels like an isolated army amidst enemies on all sides (四面楚歌). Investigating the details, the distrust incident concerning the Hap'al-myŏn (下鉢面) township head has resulted in the replacement of the old head with a new one, so there's no need to discuss it further. However, Kim Ki-sŏn, the district head of Namhŭng-ri in the same township, faced distrust due to the fact that he embezzled the payment for straw ropes (索綯 - sakto) that the village people had deposited with the township. While such actions are not impossible for a district head holding peripheral power under the current Governor-General's rule, what is regrettable is that the county and township authorities still protect and retain such a district head. Putting that aside, what about the distrust directed at County Magistrate Kim Hyŏngt'ae (金衡泰)? According to the resolution passed at the Kowŏn County Mass Meeting on April 30th: First, regarding the plan by the Kowŏn Confucian Association (高原 儒林慕聖會) to establish a higher course (高等科) lecture program, despite having adequate resources and qualified teachers, it was not approved (認可). The reason given was that the provincial authorities deemed it 'premature' (時期尚早). However, numerous counties within the province have had higher courses approved, so why should it be premature specifically for Kowŏn? This is attributed solely to the County Magistrate's lack of sincerity (無誠意) and responsibility (無責任). Second, concerning the County Agricultural Association (郡農會), although all farming households and landowners in the county are members, with total annual membership fee revenue reaching nearly 10,000 won, in the 4-5 years since its establishment, it has undertaken no projects of practical benefit to the people. Last year, its sole project was establishing sericulture and weaving training centers, but even that was abolished this year citing budget shortages. Furthermore, the association's officers consist of the County Magistrate down to the chief clerk and township heads, prohibiting member intervention, and the budget and settlement of accounts are not made public. All this suggests the problem lies not with the Agricultural Association itself, but with the mismanagement by its officers, namely the County Magistrate who serves as the head of the County Agricultural Association. Additionally, during the tenure of the former County Magistrate Yu Hongsun, when the province was implementing a five-year compulsory promotion of industry (强制勸業), a mulberry sapling nursery (桑苗圃) was established in the county based on a self-sufficiency policy to avoid purchasing from other counties. However, after the current County Magistrate took office, he purchased and distributed 160,000 mulberry saplings from the Provincial Office, causing losses to the already established nursery. We shall see what the outcome will be in the next issue. Another splendid anecdote worth laughing about: Due to the non-approval of the higher course lecture program, during questioning by certain concerned individuals, the County Magistrate, seizing upon someone's slip of the tongue, became furious and said, "If I govern well now, will they erect a monument to good governance (善政碑) for me in this town? If I get transferred, will any resident even come out to the train station to see me off?" He then reportedly ordered the abolition of even the already approved elementary course (初等科). Truly a spectacle! Like the words of a woman from the red-light district (花柳界), it's like saying, 'Since getting a virtuous woman monument is out of the question, let's at least satisfy my dear lover's desires' – meaning, since erecting a good governance monument is hopeless, let's just obstruct the county residents' happiness. Does one govern well only if a monument is erected, <35> and not govern well if it isn't? Was it even possible to govern well? Then why couldn't he declare beforehand, right upon taking office, that he would govern well only if a good governance monument was erected? Truly material for a good laugh (一笑之資).
The Forgetful (健忘症) Chief Clerk. Mr. Fujibayashi (藤林君), the Chief Clerk of this county, has such a good memory (精神 - sarcastic) that he apparently forgets today what he said yesterday. As mentioned before, when the County Magistrate ordered the return of the permit for the elementary school located at the Hyanggyo (鄕校 - local Confucian school), the founders Sŏ Tŏkku, Kim Yongsu, and Cho Chunghŭi went together to return it. At that time, Mr. Fujibayashi and the county official in charge of academic affairs, Yun T'aebyŏng, were present. When told the reason for returning the permit, Mr. Fujibayashi said he understood well. However, when parents of the elementary school students, faced with the sudden order to abolish the elementary course, rushed to the county office demanding reasons, Mr. Fujibayashi replied that he didn't even know the elementary school permit had been returned, suggesting that Sŏ Tŏkku and Cho Chunghŭi alone must have returned the elementary course permit out of resentment (野心) because the higher course lecture program wasn't approved. This provoked the emotions of the parents, who were unaware of the internal facts, leading them to furiously insult Messrs. Sŏ and Cho endlessly. Ultimately, when Messrs. Sŏ and Cho questioned the county authorities, despite the County Magistrate and County Official Yun testifying that the three individuals Kim Yongsu, Sŏ Tŏkku, and Cho Chunghŭi had returned it and explained the facts to Mr. Fujibayashi, he maintained that he knew absolutely nothing about it, speaking irresponsibly and absent-mindedly. If his mind (精神) is so poor, shouldn't he go back to farming? What is a Chief Clerk for? Wasn't Mr. Fujibayashi's 'aiding King Jie in his tyranny' (助桀爲虐 - assisting an evildoer) or his crime of 'issuing orders to the feudal lords under the emperor's name' (挾天子令諸侯 - acting presumptuously using authority) partly responsible for the County Magistrate facing distrust? One final word: as the abolition order for the already established and permitted elementary course was issued, over 60 parents, angered by this, each contributed 20 won as basic funds. Additionally, concerned individuals Kim Nakryong, Kim Yongsu, Kim Yusu, and Kim Tusu each made voluntary donations, totaling 1,600 won. They plan to raise another 400 won locally to reach a capital of 2,000 won and take a step further by establishing a kindergarten (幼稚學院). The children who faced forced dissolution are jumping and leaping with joy, saying, "Even if the authorities forcibly dissolve it, our parents and local patrons will establish institutions for us to play and learn." Looking at this inside story, the county authorities merely end up being obstructors of the education provided nationally.

Short Essay on Kando

Kando Branch

Kando (間島) – as we all know, refers to the cold region of the Three Eastern Provinces beyond the Chosŏn border to the north. It is the Manchurian plain, a vast wilderness of four thousand li, with fertile soil and abundant grain, bordered by Siberia to the east, Heilongjiang Province to the north, and facing Beijing to the west. And it is a place with deep ties (因緣) to us since the time of Koguryŏ. This is evident in history, <36> so I will not attempt to argue it further.
Left desolate and overgrown for several hundred years, with occasional Chinese residents, our compatriots began pioneering (開拓) it again 4-5 years ago. Now, everywhere is nothing but jade soil and golden estates (無非玉土金庄). Whether in cities or rural villages, one million compatriots are scattered throughout, and it has now earned the name 'Second Chosŏn'.
Ah, parents and siblings residing in the homeland! Do you view the lives of us Kando compatriots as comfortable or miserable? Of course, since the land here is rich and grain is cheap, many probably assume we live comfortably with abundant food and clothing. However, we are all a bright red mass of proletarians (無産大群) who were chased by lack of knowledge and driven out by material hardship, abandoning our warm embroidered rivers and mountains, parting from beloved relatives and old friends, swallowing tears of blood and harboring resentment as we crossed over.
We barely survive, preserving our remaining lives by digging the earth and laboring. But as we focus wholeheartedly on newly building a collapsed society and strive diligently to cultivate our lagging knowledge, there is scarcely a day when we can escape the suffering of mind and body.
To inform our parents and siblings in the homeland about the recent situation of the Kando compatriots, we have dispatched 3-4 members of our branch office on a survey across the entire breadth of Manchuria. Since the goal is to traverse thoroughly from the east to Sŏryŏng (西領 - Western Territory?) and north to Heilongjiang, it seems likely to take at least a month. In the meantime, anticipating your impatience and curiosity, we intend to introduce the nearby areas first, albeit incompletely.
Let's start by recording the geography. After getting off the train at Sangsambong in Hamgyŏng North Province and crossing the Tumen River, vast fertile land appears, with our settlements (部落) densely packed (櫛比) on both sides. Traveling 30 li by rail, you reach a town called Namyangp'yŏng (南陽坪). There are over 100 households (族居), a population of 500-600, and numerous schools. There isn't a village without a school, and in places known as towns, it seems like every other building is a school. Continuing another 30 li, you arrive at the large city of Yongjŏng Village (龍井村 - Longjing), laid out (排設) on a wide plain. Our people (吾族) number over 3,000 households, with a population of 16,400. People from various nations gather here, products are abundant, and social institutions are concentrated, making it the central place of the entire Kando region. The thousand-li long Haelan River (海蘭江 - Hailan River) surrounds it, Mao'er Mountain (帽兒山) is its Blue Dragon, and Ilssong Pavilion (一松亭) is its White Tiger. Setting Yongjŏng as the center point, traveling 40 li north leads to the large town of Kukchagae (局子街 - Júzǐjiē), where our people number 700 households with a population of 3,500, and the boundless plains are immeasurably vast. 國境農村의 現狀

碧潼支社 一記者

엇던 方面으로 보던지 地方에 잇서서는 무엇보다도 山間農村의 慘狀이다. 우리 同胞의 生活狀態는 참으로 慘酷 불상하야 實로 엇더타 形容하기 어렵다. 昨年 凶作으로 因하야 至今 飢饉에 헤매이는 事實은 全 朝鮮的으로 다-가티 悲運에 陷한 바이지만은 昨冬브터 먹을 것이 업서 悲哀의 눈물을 뿌리며 僅僅히 지내오다가 이 봄쳘을 當하야는 草根木皮로써 生命을 保存코자 하는 同胞도 잇는 모양이다. 참말 누구의 힘으로써 救濟하려는가? 그런 中에도 이 國境農村에 限하야 더욱 만흔 苦痛은 新聞紙 上에 連日報道되난 바와가티 OOO그네들의 軍資金募集이다. 그러고 또는 不逞鮮人取締 그것이다. 이가티 二重三重으로 蹂躪을 當하는 悲哀와 鬱憤은 실로 붓을 들어 일일히 記錄할 수가 업다. 이 不意의 變을 當한 同胞들은 山間農村에서는 살 수가 업서 該地方都會로 不時에 몰녀드는 移徙군은 數를 들어 헤아릴 수가 업다.
또한 其中에도 無知의 苦痛이 第一 甚하야 배울내야 배울 곳도 업고 들을내야 들녀 줄 사람이 업서서 大槪는 迷信의 窟에 빠저서 그저 恐怖와 戰慄의 逆境을 超越할 勇氣가 업시 多神時代의 氣分만 넘치는 모양이다. 우리의 生活은 엇지하야 이러한 慘境에 빠졋쓸가?
이 엇지 農村의 痛歎할 바 아니랴. 나는 개벽 5月號에 揭載된 春坡先生의 말슴과가티 「農村欄」을 特別히 두어서 얼마라도 農民에게 便宜를 주기를 바란다.

江華片信

江華 一 報告生

△江華 蠶頭耶蘇敎會의 長老란 몃몃 사람은 絶對로 同會堂을 一般社會에 빌려주지 안키를 決議하고 「神聖한 禮拜堂에서」란 口實로 當地靑年會의 講演會나 或은 討論會에 對하야도 無可奈何로 빌리지 안는다. 萬一 다른 場所가 合當한 곳이 잇스면 구태여 빌리라고도 아니할 것이지만은 集會所는 但只 그곳 하나인 故로 江華地方은 靑年事業이나 여러 가지 方面으로 큰 妨害가 만타. 그들의 決議한 理由는 今年 陽曆 1月頃 어느날 江華 中央靑年會 主催 素人劇(饑饉救濟) 當時에 神聖한 禮拜堂 內에서 喜劇 「盲人의 經文 읽는 것」이 滋味업다는 것이 그 理由의 하나이오 또는 今春에 江華無名同盟에서 主催한 講演 時에 朴一秉, ＜32＞鄭鍾鳴氏가 말하는 中에 야소敎 信者 中에 (입으로는 사랑과 무엇을 부르면서 實際로는 모든 虛僞를 한다)한 그것이 宗敎를 虛僞라고 하엿다는 理由인 바 대개 그 所謂 長老란 者들은 70, 80된 老人들이다. 宗敎에 對한 아모 理解도 업고 한갓 天堂 地獄說만 主唱하는 그들도 可笑이지만은 그 者들로 因하야 集會所도 업고 또는 여러 가지 妨害가 만흐니 그 宗敎에 對한 迷信的 行動과 廢物인 長老의 固執이 얼마나 이 社會에 害毒인가를 吾人은 痛罵한다.
△江華郡 良道面에서는 面長運動이 매우 猛烈한 모양이다. 지난달에 그곳 面長 閔泰憲이가 죽은 後 方今 百方으로 面長되기를 周旋하는 者가 20餘名이나 된다고 한다. 面長運動을 하거나 말거나 아모 關係가 업겟지만은 그 所謂 面長運動者들은 돈푼도 잇는 者가 만흔 터이오 그 中에 專門學識이 잇는 者로서 卑陋한 行動으로 面長이 되고저 하는 者가 잇슬 뿐 아니라 그 中에 權OO이란 者는 年前에 當地面長으로 잇다가 남의 집 處女를 强奸未遂한 일로 面民의 反對로 떠러진 그 者인데 今番에 다시 第一 有力하다 하며 그 中에 可笑한 일은 面小使 桂某까지 面長運動者 中 1人이라한다. 閔面長이 죽기도 前에 알는다는 말을 듯고 運動한 者도 잇고 죽은 지 그 이튼날에 府內에 와서 運動한 者도 잇섯다 한다.
이것을 한갓 우슴에 붓치고 말 것이지만은 아모리 走狗의 橫行하는 世上이지만은 그 者들의 行動이 얼마나 더러운 것을 알 것이 안인가. 可笑千萬-.

平壤漫話

요좀 各新聞의 社會欄에는 아들이 아비를 따렷느니 아우가 兄을 辱하엿느니 하는 等 滅倫悖常의 記事가 얼마라도 난다. 이것이 財産萬能 所謂 第一主義의 究竟의 破壞임은 勿論이거니와 그와가튼 怪惡한 例는 錦繡江山 平壤에도 잇는 모양이다.

尖口生

鍾峴天主敎會는 歐洲大戰以後로 本國의 送金이 끈어저서 經費가 困難함으로 敎堂을 50萬圓에 賣却하랴고 某 日本人會社와 交涉한다는 說이 잇더니 그것도 如意히 되지 못하고 近頃에는 더욱 困難에 빠저서 엇지 할 수 업는 中 佛國 가로아 領事夫人의 努力으로 5月 23, 4 兩日同敎會 內에 大慈善市를 開하고 三越吳服店 기타 유력 상점과 交涉하야 특별 廉賣를 행하고 또 각 模擬店을 開하야 信者 以外 一般人까지 敎堂 內部의 自由觀覽을 許하고 그 賣品代幾分을 敎會에 寄附하게 하얏다. 그 敎의 篤信者가 아니면 自來觀覽을 不許하던 所謂 信聖의 淨域도 金錢이 困難한 데야 엇지하랴. 거록하신 天主시여-돈을 좀 주십소사-아멘.

無識하고도 文字 잘 쓰는 咸南道知事 金寬鉉君은 近來 그 사랑하던 夫人이 죽엇는데 엇던 친구를 對하야 하는 말이 中年에 內艱喪 當하는 것이 참 困難하다고 하야 一時 笑話거리가 되엿다고 한다. 納稅를 아니하면 斯文亂賊이라고 하는 金氏로서 妻喪을 內艱喪이라고 하는 것도 容或無怪.

培材高等普通學校長 阿篇薛羅氏와 敎務主任 姜邁氏는 西大門警察署를 訪問하고 同校盟休事件에 努力 만히한 것을 致謝하얏다. 不幸이 學生들이 監獄은 안니갓기에 그만이지 萬一 監獄에까지 갓더면 兩君은 裁判所 刑務所에까지 致謝하러 디이네라고 발바당이 달을 번 하엿다.

問題多端하던 宋秉畯遺産은 그의 忘僕인 芮宗錫, 白完* 兩君이 마타 整理하던 中 全財産을 31萬圓에 賣却하야 負價를 淸帳하고 其餘 몃 萬圓은 개장메(狗肉) 논우드시 諸子群妾과 其他關係者의게 分配하엿는데 其中에는 踈薄妾으로 世評이 만턴 白樂子와 50萬圓事件으로 爭訟하던 李容九의 子 李顯奎까지 叅與하엿다. 그런데 宋氏의 諸子間에는 遺産分配로 暗鬪가 닐어나 잘못하면 兄弟相訟이 생길는지도 모르겟다 한다. 訟事問題로 火病이 나서 죽은 宋氏는 地下에서도 火病이나 또 나지 안을는지.
