**Body Text Translation:**
The Death of a Young Idealist
						By P'al Pong

Comrades always used to speak like that about him. And though I also thought that way, truthfully, the thought that he would die – moreover, that he would commit suicide – was something I had never even dreamed of. At the same time, his death is a regrettable and sad event for us that can never come again. Truly, we never even dreamed he would die in such a manner.

He drank lye water, vomited blood from his eyes, nose, and mouth, his limbs twisted, and in a gruesome, indescribable state, he departed this world. Comrades tell me the sight of his death was too terrifying to behold. However, as I happened not to be here at the time, I did not witness the scene of his death in that dim corner room of the inn. – A year has already passed since then.

After his death, my comrades who were always close to him went through his meager belongings and pulled out a bundle of papers he had scribbled on himself in his daily life. It seems there were many kinds of writings there, but wanting to know why he died, or how he had lived, what kind of heart and soul he possessed until his death, I obtained only his diary from my comrades. Thinking that besides his diary, there could be no better way to understand what kind of "thoughts" he had, or how he struggled in this world trying to put those thoughts into practice, I took his diary and looked through it whenever I had time.

However, I truly don't know for sure whether he was an idealist, or if not, something similar. <16> But because there are occasional passages in his diary where he refers to himself as an idealist, and sometimes it comes out sounding exactly like the habit of a pure idealist, I have no choice but to call him an idealist, just as he called himself.

Now, I will select the most novelistic parts of his diary and lay them before the readers' eyes.

July 22nd, Cloudy after Rain
It’s been a long time since I wrote in my diary. Starting today, let me write down and observe how dirty and ugly this self called 'I' is. Let me write down even the smallest feelings in my heart without omitting a single one. Above all, lying to myself is a bad thing. Forcibly creating and embellishing things was the attitude of past people towards aesthetics... Our aesthetic sense lies in the expression of our ugliness. I await the day my diary becomes my art.—
Last night, I talked too late and couldn't return home, ultimately spending the night at Sang-su's house, then came home early in the morning. However, when I stepped inside our gate, I found myself confronted by a sad fact.—The reason was that I heard from my younger sister that Mother had suffered from vomiting and diarrhea during the night, nearly died, and barely survived.

In fact, Mother's health has been declining since the year before last, so this year, though she is only fifty, she looks as old as a woman past sixty. On top of that, having vomited and had diarrhea five or six times during the night, her appearance was beyond words.

All day today, I was busy nursing Mother, who lay in bed, delirious and talking nonsense. According to the doctor, her fever is very high. Indeed, I saw the thermometer myself; it had reached 39 degrees.

The thought "If only Father were here at a time like this—" occurred to me, but I couldn't send a telegram asking Father, who is currently in the countryside, to come up. So, I dismissed the thought and resolved that somehow, with my own strength—no, with the strength of myself, my younger sister, and my sister-in-law—we must manage Mother's illness. But how could my younger sister, still a child,<17> properly nurse the sick? My sister-in-law, sweating profusely on this hot day, hasn't left Mother's bedside. Sister-in-law, who usually received such dislike from Mother, played the role of a devoted daughter-in-law firmly today. However, I definitely could not feel any goodwill towards my sister-in-law. Yes, I state it plainly. Ah, how much I think ill of my sister-in-law?... But there's no need for me to painstakingly write down bad things about my sister-in-law in my diary. Of course not. The reason my older brother treats his wife coldly is also because he dislikes her in this sense. My brother is currently in Japan (日本). He went to Osaka (大阪) and there's talk he's now in Tokyo (東京), but there's been no news for several months already. My brother is a thorough wanderer. A 'Korean Éireann'. To put it badly, a vagrant. A 'korosŭkki' (고로쓰기 - likely slang for vagrant/loafer). But I like my brother. Because he and I have much in common.

After the sun set and the electric lights came on, the night air after the rain was terribly muggy. Mother, having barely slept all day, only let out nonsensical talk intermittently without rest. As night fell, even in her weakened state of mind, the inability to sleep must have been agonizing. She looked up at me, sitting by her head and gently fanning her, and whispered in a mosquito-like voice,
—Hey... even for a moment... please... let me... sleep a bit...
muttering weakly. Truly, I didn't know what I should do. At that moment, I recalled writing something like this in my diary sometime before.
—Mother is a goblin (독가비). My mother who gave birth to me is destined for ruin because of her "anachronism." Ah! That which must disappear should be left to disappear!
I remember writing such words now. Anyway, it's true that Mother is a pitiful person, like a fading shadow. This morning, and even now, I saw a skull in Mother's face, I saw a skull! The eyeballs sunken in the wrinkled face of Mother lying on the bed are like those of a puppy pecked out by crows. — Ah, it seems Mother's life won't last much longer either. Flies buzz "Wheh-..." flying around the room. From beneath the wall, the "k'ŭrŏrŏng... k'ŭrŭrŭ..." sound of earthworms stirring arises. The windless night air is heavy like settled kimchi water <18>, stuffy, and the air tonight, holding moisture due to the recent strange weather, weighs down one's heart indescribably. In the room filled to the brim with sticky air, the fearsome demon of illness, tormenting Mother and trying to devour her, sits solemnly, solemnly!—Mother seemed unable to fall asleep no matter what...

Seeing her finally seem to drift off, I have now returned to my room. There are no thoughts in my head. I am just writing in my diary like this, listlessly.

July 25th, Extreme Heat
Yesterday, I started writing in my diary and then tore it up. No matter how I thought about it, it wasn't just that there was no need to write a diary, but rather, because I had been simmering inwardly all day yesterday, I simply didn't have the inner composure to endure and continue writing.

"Ch'oe Tŏk-ho (崔德浩)!"

Yes, Ch'oe Tŏk-ho is my name. People call me Ch'oe Tŏk-ho. I am a young man born in the year my father turned 30, the prime of his vigorous middle age. Since my father is now 54, my age is 24. So why is this "I," this "Ch'oe Tŏk-ho," a 24-year-old young man, such a good-for-nothing (빈충마지냐)?

Truly, the me called Ch'oe Tŏk-ho is utterly good-for-nothing! Today again, I heard from Mother, "What's the point of all your studying up till now... Spending all that money on studying has to be useful, but it's not... Aren't you old enough? A grown man... It's exasperating. You good-for-nothing thing... Get out of here." To confess the truth, I hated hearing those words, "good-for-nothing thing," more than anything.

But what can be done when being a good-for-nothing is the truth? Didn't I, until last year, rely on my father, already half a century old, requesting money he didn't have, wandering here and there to study? And then last year, didn't I inevitably return home and continue until today without—or unable to—earn a living? Father is now in the countryside, his hair turning white trying to earn an income that amounts to only seventy or eighty won a month, while I do nothing, just loafing around and eating, don't I? But this person called 'I' only thinks big thoughts. Beautiful, noble, shining big thoughts! Within me <19> is the ideal that things "should not be allowed to remain this way." That is my ideal. I demand a world according to my ideal. The reason I haven't done anything until now is because this present reality clashes with my ideal. That's why, without knowing the reason, Mother calls me a good-for-nothing. This is the reason I hate hearing those words. And the reason I call myself a "good-for-nothing thing" is because while demanding a world according to my ideal in this reality, I am well aware of my own cowardice in not being able to practice it thoroughly. So, whichever way I roll, the fact is I cannot escape being a good-for-nothing man. Yes!

Then, am I the only good-for-nothing man like myself in this world? No. I see many people just like me, so to speak, of the same kind. I know many good-for-nothing Korean youths just like me.

Writing this much, my heart, which until just now was unbearably tight without the slightest leeway, has loosened somewhat. I wonder what day of the lunar month it is today; the moon is bright. A cool breeze washes over my hot forehead. The summer evening mood is thick even here in Nuhadong (樓下洞), this poor village reminiscent of Peking's (北京城) slums—a strange phrase, perhaps. I don't want to write any more than this. Let me just look at the moon. Let me gaze at the moon and think of the person I miss. Wait a minute. It's already been over ten days since a letter came from her!

July 26th, Extreme Heat, Brief Downpour
Like a starving puppy, I wandered around all day. I sought out every person I knew, without missing a single one. Just like a starving puppy scavenger looking for food in trash heaps and manure piles, I wandered around all day. But not a single one, yes, not a single one! offered me a job. In the end, I even went to a personnel consultation office (人事相談所) and asked them to point me to a place where this body could earn a living. If only I had a means, I am a person willing to do any kind of work. In my current state (狀熊), I am not in a position to look for a job suitable for my qualifications.

If only I could have done it in Seoul, it would have been better, but unfortunately, since moving to the countryside this spring, the money coming from Father amounts to only thirty or forty won. Mother, trying to be frugal with this small amount of money, <20> ages rapidly. Therefore, now is the urgent time when I must find some kind of work. Since the day after Mother recovered her senses from her bout of vomiting and diarrhea, she has continuously worried, fretted, regretted, lamented, and sighed, so I resolved that I must absolutely do some kind of work. To allow my younger sister to continue attending school, to relieve Mother even a little from the pressure of needing even a single penny, to lessen Father's worries and anxieties even slightly, and for myself to escape from this constrained existence to some extent, I am a defeated person willing even to become a machine. I am a miserable loser who even has the mindset to endure becoming a rich man's "dog." But not a single person looked at me and said, "I'll give you some money, come work for me." No one was willing to buy this person called 'I' who was trying to sell his own body.

Having no choice, after six in the evening, staggering along, hungry, dragging my tired legs, I returned home. My sister and sister-in-law were out somewhere, and only Mother was home. Seeing me enter, she smiled brightly as if meeting someone she was glad to see, stopped her sewing, and asked,
—Where have you been wandering around to only come back now? You must be hungry. Have you had anything to eat?
I took off my hat, wiped my sweat, then went into the kitchen, drank a bowl of water, and sat down again on the wooden floor (maru). Mother asked again,
—Did you find a monthly salaried position today? Did anyone say they'd hire you?
My chest suddenly felt tight.
—Hire me for what... They aren't even recruiting streetcar conductors... This is a big problem. I should ask someone I know differently and try to become a policeman (순사)...
Hearing my words, Mother, in a disheartened voice mixed with a sigh, looked at the distant mountains and said, as if to herself,
—That's right, you have to do XX [likely referring to being a policeman, possibly censored or self-censored] to survive...
These words sounded to me less like half-formed words and more like a sigh released in sadness or fatigue. <21> I didn't know what I could say to comfort Mother.

However, truthfully, although I spoke such words, in reality, I have not even the slightest intention of becoming a policeman. I do have the thought of becoming a "dog" out of necessity to survive, but even among types of dogs, I don't want to become a "hunting dog" (산양개 - likely metaphor for police/collaborator). Even if the distance between a guard dog (番犬) and a hunting dog (獵犬) is not far, even if it's a difference of "fifty steps versus a hundred steps" (五十步百步), I cannot tolerate it. Becoming a dog doesn't matter. But to become their direct subordinate and play the role of a hunting dog—my conscience is too red for that! Yes, the color of blood is too vivid!

Think about it. A twenty-four-year-old, having wandered here and there supposedly studying, comes back to Chosŏn to play the role of a hunting dog—does that make sense?... When matters have reached this point, it's no longer a question of ideals or spirit, but primarily a question of whether one has a conscience or not. Therefore, I absolutely cannot play the role of a hunting dog.

Then, what "means" (버리) do I have? What I can do is write. Even working as a copyist (寫字生) would be a suitable occupation for me.
Yet, no one offers even that to me.

July 29th, Extreme Heat, Rain at Night
The events of the past 2 or 3 days are recorded in the diary up to yesterday, but truly, things cannot continue like this. I absolutely cannot endure it.

Once it has come to this, there's no other way. Either I yield, or reality yields to me; anyway, there's no choice but to fight bravely. I know well the flaws of today. I alone know them well. I see all the injustice and all the servility before my eyes. The only path laid before me is the path to clear away this injustice and servility, just that one path. I have already, long ago, declared war in my heart against the filthy reality of today's Chosŏn. I say it again, I will fight against this until I die. There is no other path for me.

July 30th, Rain Continues <22>
I thought, I thought anew, that the cause of all misfortune lies in today's capitalist system of production (資本主義制生産組織) and the militarism (軍國主義) that accompanies it. This land is a colony (植民地). Ponder that word, colony—isn't it a mystical word? Isn't it a detestable word?

Now, the ultimate destination of my discontent and indignation is only one place. I hate everything. Cars have started running up Namsan (南山). I hate Namsan. I hate Namsan so much I can't stand it.

Fighting with Mother every day—I can't bear it anymore. Anyway, it's a pitiful life. Mother is a gray specter (亡靈)—yes, a specter. "When the sun rises, the specters diminish" (太陽出而亡靈減)... is it? But how could Mother be the only specter! My heart aches.

A letter came from the person I miss after a long time. She is my wife!—But why does her heart seem to waver back and forth in the letter's content, what could be the reason? Is she perhaps beginning to be disappointed in me! Or perhaps! Perhaps?

August 3rd (Truly unbearably hot.)
Are people beautiful? — I thought this while walking on the road spewing out hot steam. It was because on the way back from Sang-su's house, I suddenly thought of the person I love.

Even if not all people are beautiful, human affection (정) is beautiful. Therefore, a person with beautiful affection should be a beautiful person. Yes, people must be beautiful. Why should I doubt the person I love? Am I not loving that woman like this? Let's discard the doubting heart, let's discard the doubting heart...

October 20th, Clear Weather
This diary entry should actually have been written last night, but I am writing it now. It is now 3:20 AM on the 21st. I couldn't sleep a wink on the night of the 19th, and on the night of the 20th, lying in bed, I tossed and turned with my eyes open until past three in the morning, unable to sleep at all. My head is utterly confused right now. Ah, give me rest, give me rest!

October 21st, Clear Weather, Cool <23>
That I love that woman—is a fact known well enough by everyone among my friends, and even among my friends' friends. At the same time, that she loves me is also a fact known by those who should know.

Ah! What is a person? What is affection (정)? What is suffering? What is love? I realize now as if I have misunderstood everything completely. At the same time, I realized that everything is "unknowable." I am an unfortunate person. A puppy leashed by a 40-won monthly salary. A twenty-four-year-old puppy. On top of that, I have been cast out by the person I love!

— Omitted Portion — (Author)

I came to know that woman 4 months ago from now. At that time, I was a "hunter of fantasies" (空想의 산양꾼). I loved that woman from the very first glance! At that moment, my soul and her soul seemed to merge.

I am not a poet. But my soul at that time heard a kind of mysterious whisper of fate. And so, I felt a kind of pressure, as if I was forced by some unknown power to receive that whisper, and I received it into my own heart. While doing so, it grew within my heart.

There is no need for me to tediously lay out the course of my love affair here. Or rather, it would be something my current state of mind absolutely could not undertake. Let me speak simply – I followed a path not much different from ordinary common relationships between men and women, and began to experience what is commonly called love in the world. Ah! It was my first love! How much itchy "happy feeling" that love brought me! Oh, allow me to waste just one more word on this deeply memorable love affair of mine – allow me to add the words that my world of recollection is indeed a surreal, hazy world like a fog-enshrouded, distant world, like a rainbow mixed with pink, blue, and purple, clothed in a mystery that a third party cannot possibly feel!

Ah, what should I say? What more should I write down in my diary? When all human passion and effort <24> turn into bubbles and dry up, truly, is there not only bleak despair – yes, the despair that arises after being pushed off and falling from the elastic line called life?

A few days ago, I replied to Yŏng-ae (英愛)—yes, that woman's name is Yŏng-ae—like this.

"... Miss Yŏng-ae! I cannot force you 'not to do so,' however you dispose of your own self, Miss Yŏng-ae. Because that is Miss Yŏng-ae's freedom. However, I cannot remain silent now without saying anything. This is because my current state of mind has received a huge blow from you.

I do not wish to ask now for the reason why you say you cannot love me. Fine! You will gather all sorts of gentle words to explain that reason! However, even without hearing the reason explicitly from you, I can well guess it. No, not only guess, but I think even words heard directly from your lips would not be a more certain answer than this!

Well then, let me state that reason. Let me state my guess, which has more certainty than hearing it directly from you. Above all, you did not love me. And though you now say you are abandoning me and loving Ch'ŏl-su (哲洙)—forgive me—I dare say it. This time too, you are not loving Ch'ŏl-su. Do you love Ch'ŏl-su? Even if your answer to this question is 'Yes,' this person, Ch'oe Tŏk-ho, who has experienced receiving what you call love, knows it more certainly than you yourself do. You do not truly love Ch'ŏl-su. Of course, Ch'ŏl-su has a lot of money. He's good-looking. He's a talented man (秀才) who graduated—albeit midway—from university after eight years... .

Oops, I have no need to slander my friend. I am by no means trying to speak with thorns attached.

However, when I see that your motive for loving me and your motive now for abandoning me and loving Ch'ŏl-su are exactly the same, I truly cannot help the thorns rising in my mouth. <25>

Initially, I pleaded for your love. Once, twice, three times..... I wrote letters to you. Finally, you said you were impressed—no, moved—by my passion, and told me you truly loved me, didn't you? And now, though you speak as if for the same reason as you did to me then, the reality is that you are loving Ch'ŏl-su out of a completely different motive, far removed from how you treated me then—namely, moved by money (金錢). That's truly how it is.

That's right. Money is capable of everything. It gives birth to everything. Since the world today is one where money even gives birth to people, what more is there to say? Money even creates ghosts (귀신). It's even creating the vengeful spirit (怨靈) of a jilted man! Truly, compared to the phenomenon where money is creating even vengeful spirits, the fact that your heart has changed is perhaps not such a doubtful matter! This good-for-nothing me is a 'dog' leashed by a 40-won monthly salary, dragged around by life. A 'dog' dragged around by commands other than my own! My being jilted is perhaps fitting!

What more can I say to you now? Though you are five years younger than me, you are cleverer than I, so I even know how my words will sound in your ears. Ch'ŏl-su is a friend with whom I spent 2 or 3 years closely, if you can call it that. Towards that person, I truly don't want to open my mouth. And I do not plead with you again! I am now sitting quietly before the great 'catastrophe' descending upon myself, and will just try to face it steadfastly for a while!

Well then, beloved person whom I will never approach again! 『Adieu! Adieu!』"

Ah, it's painful. Ah, what kind of crazy act is this! Why did I write down such scraps of writing here again! Yŏng-ae! Yŏng-ae! That woman, with her pretty and clever-looking face, is also arrogant and like a venomous woman (毒婦), a cute and hateful modern-style courtesan type (現代式娼婦型) of woman! But though I say I have given up, haven't I still not detached myself from that woman with my whole heart and soul? Isn't that so?

Ah—what should I do?

October 23rd, Rainy Weather <26>

Since I hadn't gone into the office for 2 or 3 days, today I had no choice but to go in again. Everything proceeded as usual, fixed tasks in fixed places. I, too, sat with a completely empty (휑덩그럿케 텡뷔인) head on my shoulders, like a machine, like an artificial human (人造人間), running a brush pen over paper. I am not a person. I am a 'pen.' A 'pen'!

'Life - Misfortune - Reality - Misery - Justice - Reform - Current State - Yŏng-ae - Happiness'... I wrote such words on the paper, then tore it up, wrote again, then tore it up, until I left for home at five in the afternoon. At home, there's a big fuss because they are moving tomorrow. Mother, taking my sister-in-law and younger sister, is determinedly leaving for the countryside where Father is staying tomorrow morning. They say that while I was at the office today, they gathered only the necessary things and took them out to the train station (停車場). My younger sister was sad about transferring to a countryside school.

Like a sleepwalker (夢遊病者), I sat and stood before my desk until late at night, and now I write this diary. I don't know what I've written. What should I do? Truly, what work should I do to live in this world?

After the family moves out, I decided to find lodging (下宿) near Sang-su's house, but should I perhaps just go to the countryside too? That would likely be a blow to Father! Whether painful or bitter, I have no choice but to stay here!

Today, some crazy fellow friend came looking for me specifically to talk about news of Yŏng-ae. I made the excuse that I didn't have time and sent him away right at the office door. Is love such a fleeting thing!

Ah, I truly don't know what I should do. Why is my heart so anxious (焦燥)! My heart is anxious and confused, what should I do, ah, what should I do! (Unfinished - Concludes Next Issue)

**Summary**

This excerpt from a fictional story presents diary entries of a disillusioned young Korean man, Ch'oe Tŏk-ho, in 1925. He grapples with unemployment, family pressure (especially from his ailing mother), his own perceived uselessness despite harboring "idealistic" desires for societal change, and profound heartbreak after being rejected by his lover, Yŏng-ae, apparently for a wealthier friend, Ch'ŏl-su. The diary reveals his internal turmoil, self-loathing mixed with a sense of righteousness, contempt for the societal conditions (implied colonial context), refusal to take a job like a policeman (seen as collaboration), and growing despair, culminating in suicidal ideation hinted at by the story's title and the narrator's opening remarks about Ch'oe's actual suicide.

For historians, the text offers a poignant glimpse into the psychological struggles of educated Korean youth during the colonial period. Ch'oe Tŏk-ho embodies the frustration of intellectuals unable to find meaningful work or enact their ideals within the constraints of colonial society ("This land is a colony... isn't it a detestable word?"). His description of himself as a "good-for-nothing" (빈충마지) despite his education, the economic hardship forcing family dependence, the painful choice between survival and collaboration (refusing to be a "hunting dog" or 순사), and the overwhelming sense of personal and societal despair reflect common themes of alienation and resistance (both active and passive) experienced under Japanese rule. The specific mention of Namsan, recently accessible by car (likely symbolizing Japanese development/presence), becoming an object of hatred further underscores the anti-colonial sentiment.

젊은 理想主義者의 死
						八峰
동모들도 늘 그와 가티 말하든 터이요. 또한 나도 그와 가티 생각하든 터이지마는 참말로 그가 죽으리라는 -더구나 자살하리라는 생각은 꿈에도 생각해본 적이 업는 동시에, 그가 죽은 것은 우리에게는 다시 두 번 차저 올 수 업는 앗가운 일이며 슬픈 일이라고 생각한다. 참말이지 우리는 그가 그 모양으로 죽으리라는 생각은 꿈에도 안이하엿섯스닛가.
그는 양재물을 먹고서 눈으로 코로 입으로 피를 토하고 사지가 뒤틀려 가지고서 보기에도 악착스럽게 무에라 말할 수 업는 형상으로 이 세상을 떠나버렷다. 동모들은 나에게 그의 죽은 모양은 도저히 눈으로는 보지 못할 무서운 것이엿다고 말한다. 그러나 나는 맛침 그때 이곳에 업섯슴으로 그가 려관집 한 모통이 어둠침침한 방에서 죽어버린 모양을 보지는 못하엿섯다. -그것은 벌서 일년젼이 되고 마랏다.
그가 죽은 뒤에 그와 항상 갓갑게 지내든 나의 동모들은 그의 손세간을 뒤여서 그가 평시에 자긔의 손으로 끄적어려 노앗든 조희ㅅ뭉턱이를 끄집어 내엿섯다. 거긔에는 여러 가지 종류의 글이 만헛든 모양이나 나는 그 사람이 엇지 하여서 죽엇는지 그 원인에 대해서 또는 그 사람이 엇더케 살어왓는지 죽을때까지 엇더한 마음과 혼을 가지고 지내여왓는지 그것이 알고 십허서 나의 동모들로부터 그의 일긔만 어더다 노핫섯다. 그가 엇더한 「생각」을 가지고 잇섯스며 또는 엇더케 그 생각을 실행할녀고 이 세샹에서 헙위적어리며 살어왓는가-하는 그와 가튼 것을 알기에는 그 사람의 일긔 외에는 더 훌륭한 것이 업스리라는 생각으로 나는 그의 일긔를 갓다가 틈잇는대로 뒤적어려 보앗섯다.
그러나 나는 그 사람이 정말로 리상주의자이엇섯는지 혹은 그것은 안이드래도 그와 비슷한 것인지 그것은 확실히＜16＞ 모른다. 그러나 그의 일긔에는 자긔가 자긔를 가르처서 리상주의자라고 불는 대문이 간혹 잇고 그것이 어느 때는 순전한 리상주의자의 입버릇가티 나아오는 때가 잇는 까닭으로 나는 하는 수 업시 이 사람을 자긔가 스스로 자긔를 지목하야 불느든 거와 가티 리상주의자라고 하여 둔다.
지금 그의 일긔를 가장 소설뎍으로 추리여 내여 가지고 독자들의 눈압헤 페여 놋기로 한다.

7월 22일 雨後雲
오래간만에야 일긔를 쓰게 되엿다. 오늘부터 어듸좀 나라는 이 몸이 얼마나 더러웁고 추한가 적어두고서 살피여보자. 마음속의 조고마한 늣김까지도 하나도 빼여놋치 말고 적어노코보자. 무엇보다도 내가 나에게 거짓말을 한다는 것은 낫븐 일이다. 억지로 지여서 맨들고 꾸미여 놋는다는 것은 지나간 사람들의 美學에 대한 태도이엇스닛가... 우리의 美意識은 우리의 醜의 表現에 있다. 나는 나의 일긔가 나의 예술이 되기를 기다린다.-
어제 저녁에 너머 늣게까지 이야기하느라고 집으로 도라오지 못하고 급긔야 相洙의 집에서 밤을 새운뒤에 아츰에 일즉이 집으로 도라왓섯다. 그러나 나는 우리집 대문안에 드러섯슬 때 한 개의 슬픈 사실압헤 가로막히여서 잇는 내 자신을 그곳에서 발견하얏섯다.-그 까닭은 어머니가 밤동안에 토사곽란을 맛나서 죽을 뻔하다가 겨오 사라낫다는 말을 나의 누의 동생으로부터 드럿든 까닭이다.
사실, 어머니는 재작년부터 몸이 쇠약하야저서 금년에 불과 오십밧게는 안되는 부인이 한갑 지난 로인과 가티 늙어보히는 것이다. 그런데다가 밤 사이에 오륙차를 토사한 까닭에 그 모양은 더욱 말이 아니엇섯다.
오늘 하로동안 나는 정신을 일코 자리에 누어서 헷소리만 하는 어머니를 간호하여 주기에 밧벗섯다. 의사의 말을 듯건대 熱이 대단히 만타한다. 사실 나도 체온계를 보앗지만은 39도에까지 올나왓섯다.
「이런때 아버지라도 게시엇스면-」하는 생각이 잇섯스나 지금 싀골가 게신 아버지를 올너오시라고 뎐보를 노을 수도 업고 하야서 그런 생각은 집어치우고 엇더케하든지 나 혼자의 힘으로 아니 나와 내 누의동생과 내 형수만의 힘으로 어머니의 병환을 붓잡어 노아야만 하겟다는 생각을 하엿다. 그러나 내 동생이라는 애가 무슨 제법 철이 나서＜17＞ 병간호를 할 수 잇스랴. 형수는 이 더운 날에 땀을 흘니여가며 어머니가 누으신 방에서 떠나지를 안을뿐이다. 평소에 그러케 어머니에게 미움을 밧든 형수가 오늘은 효부노릇을 단단히 하엿다. 그러나 나는 결단코 나의 형수에게 호감을 갓지 못하엿다. 그러타, 단언한다. 아아 얼마나 나는 나의 형수를 조치 못하게 생각하고 잇느냐?... 그러나 나는 나의 일긔에다 결단코 나의 형수에 대한 낫븐 말을 애써서 적어둘 필요는 업다. 물론 업다. 형님이 그의 안해를 소박하는 것도 이와 가튼 의미에서 그를 미워하는 까닭이다. 형님은 지금 日本에 가잇다. 大阪으로 가잇다가 지금은 東京으로 갓다는 말도 잇스나 소식이 업는지 발서 몃 달이 된다. 형님은 철저한 방랑자다. 『코레안 애-란』이다. 낫브게 말하면 부랑자다. 『고로쓰기』다, 그러나 나는 형님을 조와한다. 그와 나와는 공명하는 점이 만흔 까닭이다.
해가 넘어가고 뎐등불이 켜진 뒤로 비오고 난 밤 공긔는 몹시도 무더웟다. 어머니는 거지반 왼종일 한 잠도 못 자고서 공연한 햇소리만 잇다금식 쉬일 사이 업시 내놋트니 밤이 되닛가 업는 정신에나마 잠자지 못하는 것이 몹시도 괴로웟든지 머리맛헤 안저서 부채질을 슬금슬금하여 주든 나를 치어다 보며 모긔소리와 가튼 목소리로
-얘 잠간이라도... 조흐니... 나 좀... 자게 해 다고...
하며 힘업시 중얼거린다. 참말로 나는 엇더케 햇스면 조흘는지를 몰랏섯다.그때에 나는 그 언제인가 나의 일긔에다 이와 가티 쓴 일이 잇는 것을 생각하얏다.
-어머니는 독가비다. 나를 나어준 나의 어머니는 당신의 「아나크로니슴」때문으로 滅亡하게 되엿다. 아!사러지지 아니하면 아니될 것은 사러저 버리도록 내버려두는 수 밧게는 업고나!
이와 가튼 말을 쓴 것을 나는 지금 記憶하고 잇다. 아모러튼 어머니는 사라저 가는 얇은 그림자와 가튼 불상한 사람인 것은 사실이다. 나는 오늘 아츰에도 지금도 어머니의 얼골에서 해골을 보앗다, 해골을 보앗다! 자리우에 두러누은 주름쌀 만흔 어머니의 얼골에 파먹고 잇는 눈알맹이는 까마귀가 파먹은 강아지의 그것과 갓다. - 아아 어머니의 목숨도 인저는 오래가지 못하게 되엿나 브다. 파리는 「왜-... 」하면서 방안으로 날러다닌다. 담밋헤서는 지렁이의 「끄러렁... 끄르르... 」하는 니알는 소리가 일어난다. 바람도 업는 밤의 공긔는 가러안진 초찟기 ＜18＞모양으로 틉틉하고 더구나 요지음의 괴상한 일기로 인해서 濕氣까지 바더가지고 잇는 오늘밤의 공긔는 무에라 말할 수 업시 사람의 마음을 무거웁게 덥허눌는다. 끈적끈적한 공긔가 하나 가득히 차잇는 방안에는 어머니를 괴롭게 굴고 잡아먹을려고 드는 무서운 病魔가 儼然히 儼然히 안저 잇는 것이다!-어머니는 아모리해도 잠이 들지 못하는 모양이엇다...
겨오 잠이 든 듯 십흔 모양을 보고서 나는 지금 나의 방으로 도라왓다. 대가리 속에는 아모 생각도 업다. 그냥 아모럿캐 나이 모양으로 일기를 쓰고 잇쓸 뿐이다.
7월 25일 酷暑
어제는 일기를 쓰다가 찌저버렷다. 아모리 생각하여도 일긔를 쓸 필요가 업다느니보다도 어제 왼종일 속을 부글부글 끄리든 나로서는 도뎌히 참고 견듸면서 일긔를 쓰고 잇슬만한 마음속의 너그러움이 업섯든 까닭이다.
『崔德浩』!
그러타 『崔德浩』는 내 일홈이다. 여러 사람은 나를 부를 때 崔德浩라고 불른다. 나는 나의 아버지의 나희가 가쟝 旺盛한 장년기인 30세되든 해에 생기여 난 청년이다. 지금 나의 아버지의 나희가 54세이닛가 나의 나희는 24세다. 그런데 24세의 청년인 이 「나」라는 「崔德浩」가 엇지 이와 가티 빈충마지냐?
참말로 崔德浩라는 나는 빈충맛기짝이 업다! 오늘도 어머니로부터 『너 이때껏 공부햇다는 것이 다 무어냐... 돈만 처드려가며 공부한 것이 쓸데가 잇서야지 아니하니... 나희나 적으냐? 장정이... 긔막힌다. 빈충마진 것... 저리 가거라』하는 소리를 드럿다. 나는 사실을 고백하면 이 『빈충마진 것』이라는 말이 무엇보다도 듯기 실혓다.
그러나 빈충마진 것이 사실인데야 엇지하랴. 나는 작년까지도 半百이나 된 아버지의 신세를 저가면서 업다는 돈을 청하여 가며 이곳저곳으로 공부하러 다니지 안이하얏느냐? 그리고는 작년에 이르러서 하는 수 업시 집으로 도라와서 오늘날까지 아모 밥버리도 하지 안코 또는 못하고 내려오지 아니하엿느냐?아버지는 지금 싀골서 한달에 칠팔십원 밧게는 되지 안는 수입을 엇기에 밥버리하기에 머리털이 희여가거든 나는 아모 일도 못하고 놀고 먹고 잇지 아니하냐? 그러나 나라는 이 백성은 커드란 생각만 하고 잇다. 곱고 거륵하고 빗나는 커드란 생각을 하고 잇다! 나에게는＜19＞ 모든 것을 『이러케 되여 잇게 하여서는 아니 된다』는 理想이 잇다. 그것은 나의 理想이다. 나는 나의 理想대로의 세계를 요구한다. 내가 지금껏 아모일도 하지 안코 잇는 것은 지금 이 현실이 나의 理想과 背馳되는 까닭이다. 그런 것을, 그런 것을 까닭도 몰르고 어머니는 나다려 빈충마진 것이라고 한다. 이것이 내가 그 말을 듯기 실혀하는 이유이다, 그리고 또 내가 자신을 가라처 「빈충마진 것」이라고 하는 이유는 내가 나의 理想대로의 세계를 이 현실에서 요구하면서 그것을 徹底히 力行하지 못하는 내 자신의 無勇을 내가 잘 알고 잇는 까닭이다. 그러면 나는 엇던 편으로 궁글든지 빈충마진 사내인 것은 면치 못하는 것이 사실이다. 그러타!
그러면 나 가튼 빈충마진 사내는 이 세상에 나 하나밧게 업슬까? 아니다. 나는 나와 똑가튼 말하자면 同種類의 사람들을 만히 본다. 나와 똑가튼 빈충마진 朝鮮靑年을 나는 만히 안다.
이만큼 써 오닛가 악가까지도 조고만한 餘裕도 업시 뻑뻑하기 한량업든 마음이 얼마쯤 풀니여젓다. 오늘은 음력으로 며칠이나 되는지 달이 밝다. 선선한 바람이 더운 이마를 씨서준다. 끈적끈적하는- 좀 이상스러운 말이지마는- 北京城의 貧民村인 이곳 樓下洞에도 녀름 저녁의 기분은 濃厚하다. 나는 이 이상 더 쓰기가 실타. 달이나 보자. 달이나 치어다 보다가 그리운 사람의 생각이나 하여 보자. 가만 잇거라. 그에게서 편지가 오지 안은지가 벌서 열흘이 넘엇고나!

나는 주린 강아지 모양으로 왼종일 도러다녀 보앗다. 알만한 사람은 하나도 빼여 놋치 안코서 차저 다녀보앗다. 맛치 주린 강아지 색기가 쓰러기통으로 두엄탕으로 먹을 것을 차지러 도러 다니는 것과 똑가튼 모양으로 왼종일 도러 다녀 보앗다. 그러나 한 놈도, 그러타 한 놈도! 나에게 밥버리를 맛기여 주는 놈은 업섯다. 나종에는 人事相談所에까지 가서 이 몸하나 밥버리 할 곳을 지시하여 달나는 청을 하여 보앗다. 나도 버리만 잇스면 무슨 일이든지 할 사람이다. 지금의 나의 狀熊로는 내 資格에 맛는 직업을 구하고 잇슬 형편이 못된다.

아아 - 나는 엇더케 하면 조흔가?
10월 23일 雨天＜26＞
(27·28면 원본 낙장 - 국편)

그동안 2,3일 사무소에 드러가지 못하엿섯든 고로 오늘은 하는수 업시 또다시 드러갓섯다. 모든 것은 전과 갓치 정해노은 자리에서 정해 노은 일이 운행되엿섯다. 휑덩그럿케 텡뷔인 대 다리를 목아지 우에 이고 안진 나도 기계모양으로 人造人間모양으로 조희우에다 붓대를 달니고 잇섯다. 나는 사람이 아니다. 『펜』이다. 『펜』이다!
『人生 - 不幸 - 現實 - 悲慘 - 正義 - 改革 - 現想 - 英愛 - 幸福』...  이따위 글자를 조희우에다 써보다가는 찌저버리고 또 써보다가는 찟다가 오후 다섯시에 집으로 나왓다. 집에서는 내일 移徙를 간다고 야단법석이다. 형수와 누의동생을 다리고서 어머니는 긔여코 아버지가 가 게신 싀골로 내일 아츰에 떠난다. 짐은 오늘 내가 사무소에 잇는 동안에 필요한 것만은 모다 주서다가 停車場에 내다 노앗다고 한다. 누의동생은 싀골 학교로 전학한다고 섭섭해하얏다.
나는 夢遊病者모양으로 밤이 깁도록 책상압헤 안젓다 섯다하다가 지금 이 일기를 쓴다. 무슨 소리를 써노앗는지 몰른다. 나는 엇더케 할까? 참말로 엇더케 무슨 일을 하면 세상을 사러가나?
집에서 이사를 간 뒤에 나는 相洙의 집근처에다 下宿을 정하기로 하얏지마는 기왕에 나도 싀골로 가 버릴까? 그러면 아버지에게 打擊이 되렷다! 다나 쓰나 이 곳에 잇슬 수 밧게!
