**Summary**

This article is a company announcement from the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk*, detailing personnel changes, relocations, and the establishment of new branches, sub-branches, and distribution points during May 1925. It lists the names and positions of staff at various locations, including Kanggye, Hŭnggyŏng County (in Fengtian Province, Manchuria), Kando (also in Manchuria), Naju, T'ongyŏng, Hoengsŏng, and Pyŏktong County. For historians, this piece provides valuable insight into the organizational structure and expanding reach of a prominent Korean cultural and intellectual magazine during the colonial period. The presence of branches in Manchuria (Hŭnggyŏng, Kando) highlights the magazine's engagement with the Korean diaspora, while the detailed staff lists offer potential avenues for researching individuals involved in Korean media and nationalist movements at the time. The inclusion of a "Female Reporter" (女記者) is also noteworthy for the period.
