Publication Date: 1925년 06월 01일

Article Type: 소식 (News)

Social Scene, From April 17th to May 14th

April 17th: In France, the Painlevé cabinet was formed, and its members were formally announced.
The fellowship meeting of the Chosŏn Journalists' Conference opened at the Ch'ŏndogyo Sangch'unwŏn outside Tongdaemun, presenting a grand spectacle.
A rebellion broke out in Sichuan, China, and Yao Zhen's army attacked Liu Chengxun's army.
At the Sveti Nedelya Cathedral in Bulgaria, during the funeral for General Georgiev, a member of the country's parliament assassinated on the 14th, a bomb suddenly exploded. 20 women, 10 children, 6 generals, and 20 high officials met tragic deaths. The government declared martial law throughout the country that day.

18th: Off the coast of Tientsin, China, great wind and waves arose, causing three steamships to collide and suffer damage.
17 Japanese stonemasons working on the new construction of Kyŏngsŏng City Hall went on a collective strike over wage increases and the issue of restricting the hiring of Chinese stonemasons.
A revolution broke out in Lisbon, the capital of Portugal, resulting in street fighting.
A large fire broke out in Chŏngp'yŏng-ŭp, Hamnam Province, completely burning down over 100 houses.
In Kadŏk-ri, P'arwŏn-myŏn, Nyŏngbyŏn-gun, an independence group clashed with police forces, and Patrolman Tamura was shot (被丸) and seriously injured.

19th: Around 8 PM that night, over 300 delegates from the People's Movement Conference, which had been ordered to dissolve, waved red flags (赤旗) in Chongno, shouted "Long live the proletariat!" (無産者萬歲), and caused a major disturbance for a time before many were arrested. Photojournalists from the Sidae and Chosŏn newspapers were also unreasonably beaten by police officers.
The Chinese National Assembly enacted and passed 30 articles of the Assembly regulations. Chang Tso-lin ordered Wang Yongjiang, Governor of Fengtien Province, to strictly prohibit the lending of paddy fields (水田貸與) to Koreans.

20th: A large fire broke out on Kayasan Mountain in Sŏsan-gun, Ch'ungnam Province, burning down over 200 chōbo (町步) of forest.
Due to heavy snowstorms in the Such'ŏng Grodekovo area of Russian territory, over 200 resident compatriots met tragic deaths.
A forest fire also broke out on the mountain behind Pŏphŭngsa Temple in Kongp'yŏng-myŏn, P'yŏngwŏn-gun, burning down 300,000 large trees.
In Khamatang, Russian territory, resident Koreans clashed with revolutionary police forces, resulting in the arrest of over 80 Koreans.
In Pyŏktong-gun, P'yŏngbuk Province, about 10 armed members of an independence group attacked a Japanese merchant ship, seizing a large amount of money and goods, and abducted one patrolman who was aboard.

21st: Bulgarian government forces and Communist Party forces battled, with casualties on both sides reaching 4,000. The Bulgarian government also executed 500 Communist Party members.

22nd: The Conference of Ambassadors of the Allied Nations permitted Bulgaria to increase its militia by 7,000 members by May 30th.
In Kisan-ri, Tong-myŏn, P'och'ŏn-gun, 11 members of Yi Wan'gyu's family ate poisonous herbs (毒草) and were poisoned; 2 died tragically, and the others are in critical condition.
Japan promulgated the Peace Preservation Law via the official gazette.

24th: Regarding the issue of excessive levies by the An'gang Water Utilization Association (安康水利組合 負担金) in Kyŏngju-gun, 13 representatives of over 1,000 landowners submitted a petition (陳情) to the Government-General (總督府).
Workers at the Nisshin Spinning Company (日淸紡織會社) in Tsingtao went on a collective strike, causing cotton yarn market prices to soar unexpectedly.
The trial of Yang Sŭng'u, leader of the Pyŏkch'ang Righteous Volunteers Group (碧昌義勇團), which had been repeatedly postponed since last October, opened at Courtroom No. 2 of the P'yŏngyang Appellate Court (覆審法院).
The All-Chosŏn Hyŏngp'yŏng Conference opened within the Sich'ŏn Kyodang (侍天敎堂).
At Koyang [*Island name unclear] Public Normal School (公立普通學校), a Japanese instructor (訓導) named Ishimoto beat a student, causing him to collapse (卒倒). Several hundred parents (學父兄) surrounded the school, causing a major disturbance, which was calmed by police intervention.

25th: The U.S. Navy's large-scale naval exercises, simulating Japan as a hypothetical enemy (假想敵), began in Hawaii.
Bulgarian President Manoel Domecq García tendered his resignation, taking responsibility for the outbreak of the revolutionary movement in his country, but it was rejected (不受理) by the parliament.

26th: In Kitatama District, under the jurisdiction of Tokyo Prefecture, a Japanese railway construction foreman (什長) named Kawarai (?) (川笑) embezzled and absconded with over 500 yen of wages belonging to Korean laborers. Due to extreme hardship, about 20 compatriots, accompanied by their wives and children, attempted railway suicide but were prevented by police.
Yangp'yŏng Police Station Assistant Inspector Haji Chūsaku, while drunk (乘醉), shot and killed Kim Ch'anje, the head of Mansan-myŏn in the same county.
A second major whirlwind (旋風) struck the northeastern region of the United States, causing damage amounting to 200 million yen. <41>
In Puk-myŏn, Kaech'ŏn-gun, P'yŏngnam Province, an independence group engaged in a fierce battle with police forces for 30 minutes before 2 members were captured (被擒).

27th: General Hindenburg, leader of the German National People's Party (國權黨 巨首), was elected President with 650 votes [Note: This count seems incorrect, Hindenburg won the second round with 14.6 million votes, not 650].
The P'yŏngyang sock workers, who had been on a collective strike for many days, resolved their issues through the mediation (調停) of the P'yŏngyang Prefect (府尹) and returned to work all at once starting today.

28th: Heavy snow fell in Ch'ŏngju, causing great damage to agricultural crops.
Authorities from the Chinese Police Bureau in Antung County (安東縣) shot and killed 2 Koreans on suspicion of smuggling "white salt/opium?" (白監 密輸入).

30th: 208 workers from P'yŏngyang noodle houses (麵屋) went on a collective strike.

May 1st: Children's Day commemorative celebrations were held with great success throughout Seoul and rural areas (京鄕 各地). Especially in Kyŏngsŏng, a parade (旗行列) of 5,000-6,000 boys and girls presented an unprecedented spectacle.
About 10 armed members of an independence group set fire to and attacked the workshop of the Huch'ang Forestry Station (厚昌營林廠) in P'yŏngbuk Province.
A large fire occurred at Kyoto Imperial University in Japan, burning down research rooms and the library (書庫); damages reached approximately 100,000 yen. A group of 16 students from Nanking, China, on a sightseeing tour (觀光團), arrived in Kyŏngsŏng.
Sun Yat-sen's hometown, Xiangshan County (香山縣), was renamed Zhongshan County (中山縣), and the worker-peasant system (勞農制度) was implemented, marking a first (嚆矢) in China. The Alliance for the Exclusion of Orientals (東洋人排斥 期成同盟會) convened in Canada and resolved to exclude all Orientals from the region.

2nd: The All-China Labor and Peasant Conferences opened in Kwangtung (Guangdong).

3rd: Kang Ingnyok, who had attacked the Nam-myŏn police substation (駐在所) in Sŏnch'ŏn-gun, P'yŏngbuk Province, was arrested in Tokyo, Japan.
Poland held its Independence Day celebration (獨立祭).

4th: In Ibaraki Prefecture, Japan, over 60 resident compatriots engaged in a large brawl (大格鬪) with over 300 Japanese laborers (土工), resulting in serious and minor injuries on both sides.

7th: In Peking, China, over 600-700 students, enraged (激憤) over the issue of improper handling of Boxer Indemnity funds (團匪陪償金), attacked the private residence (私邸) of the Minister of Education. They clashed with police (巡警), resulting in numerous serious injuries on both sides.
At the All-Chosŏn Women's Oratorical Contest held at the P'yŏngyang Namsanhyŏn Chapel, sponsored by the P'yŏngyang YWCA (女子基督敎靑年會), speaker Miss Yi Ŭndŏk (a student at Sunghyŏn Girls' School) was detained (檢束) at the P'yŏngyang Police Station that night for allegedly making subversive remarks.
It was announced by Imperial Ordinance (勅令) that Japan's Peace Preservation Law would be enforced in Chosŏn (Korea), Taiwan (대만), Karafuto (*太), the Kwantung Leased Territory (關東州), and the South Seas Mandate (南洋群島) starting from the 12th. A major storm hit Ibaraki Prefecture, Japan, destroying over 100 fishing boats, and the whereabouts (行衛) of over 300 people are unknown.
At a cotton factory in Shanghai, 5,000 workers went on a collective strike.
National Humiliation Day (國恥紀念) processions were held grandly throughout various parts of China.

8th: The Florida State Legislature in the U.S. passed a bill prohibiting land ownership (土地所有 禁止案) by Japanese people.
The Warsaw Police Department in Poland conducted a large-scale roundup of Communist Party members.
The All-Russian Soviet Congress opened in Moscow.

9th: The Worker-Peasant government (勞農政府 - Soviet government) supplied 1,500 tons of oil to the Kwangtung (Guangdong) government.
The International Industrial Conference opened in Rome, the capital of Italy.

10th: A large fire broke out in Yuzawa-machi, Ogachi-gun, Akita Prefecture, Japan, completely burning down over 400 houses.
At the Chongso-myŏn police substation (駐在所) in Kanggye-gun, Japanese Patrolman Kunitomi Kahō (?) (國富嘉穗) shot and killed the eldest daughter of Ch'oe Yŏngsil of Sinsŏng-dong in the same township.
At the Sariwŏn power plant (발전소), a Japanese operator confined 15 young women who had come for sightseeing.

11th: In the Paikp'aja area of Kirin Province (Jilin Province), China, the Rènyìjūn (仁義軍) bandit group clashed fiercely with government troops, resulting in over 600 casualties on both sides.

12th: The Peace Preservation Law came into effect in Chosŏn today.
In the vicinity of [*Hai unclear] in Honan Province (Henan Province), China, a bandit group of over 1,000 invaded, burning down 32 villages.

13th: Near the coast of Yŏnghŭng, a fishing boat capsized, and 4 fishermen drowned (*死).
A large fire occurred in Saitama Prefecture, Japan, burning down 800 buildings, with damages reaching 5 million yen.

14th: The Chairman of the People's Commissars of Worker-Peasant Russia (Soviet Russia), Rykov, declared to Turkey the repudiation of Tsarist-era debts and the renunciation (抛棄) of claims to them.
The same country (Soviet Russia) recognized the former Turkish territories of Menistan [?] and Uzbekistan as independent republics.
Students from Peking University mobilized en masse, waving flags repudiating the Twenty-One Demands, and conducted a large demonstration (示威運動). <42>

**Summary**

This article provides a chronological news digest covering international and domestic events from April 17th to May 14th, 1925. International news includes political changes in France and Bulgaria, conflicts in China, revolution in Portugal, diplomatic moves by Soviet Russia, US naval exercises targeting Japan, and anti-Asian sentiment in Canada and the US. Domestic Korean news highlights resistance activities (journalist meetings, independence group clashes, proletarian protests, strikes by various workers), colonial suppression (arrests, beatings, implementation of the Peace Preservation Law), social issues (student beatings by Japanese teachers, land disputes, accidents), and natural disasters (fires, storms). The period saw significant labor unrest, ongoing independence movement actions, and a tightening of Japanese colonial control.

For historians studying Korean colonial history, this article is valuable as it juxtaposes international events with the situation in Korea, offering a snapshot of the global context. It clearly documents various forms of Korean resistance, from organized political action and armed struggle to labor strikes and spontaneous protests, showing the breadth of opposition to Japanese rule. Crucially, it records the extension of the oppressive Peace Preservation Law to Korea and other territories, a key moment in the intensification of colonial control and suppression of dissent. The mention of specific incidents like the Chosŏn Journalists' Conference, the Hyŏngp'yŏng Conference, clashes involving independence groups, numerous strikes, and the arrest of an orator for "subversive remarks" provides concrete examples of social and political dynamics during this period.
