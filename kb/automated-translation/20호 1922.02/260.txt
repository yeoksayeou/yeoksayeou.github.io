Article Title: 가을(외 2편) (Autumn (and 2 other poems))
Author: 億生 (Oksaeng)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Autumn
						Oksaeng
Autumn (Creative Work)
Yesterday,
The lovely flower buds of early spring
Upon your joy-filled rosy cheeks
Played, releasing the fragrance (香氣) of smiles,

Today,
The desolate falling leaves (落葉) of autumn
Upon your trembling, fading heart
Scatter as if empty, shattering young dreams.

Apple (林檎)
Apples (林檎) are bright red, yes,
And peaches (복송아) are also bright red, yes,
Apples (林檎) have white flesh inside, yes,
And peaches have red flesh inside, yes.

Here are an apple (林檎) and a peach (복송아)
Both ripened to a bright red.
Yes, your heart, ripened bright red like an apple (林檎),
And my heart, ripened bright red like a peach.

You are the apple (林檎), I am the peach,
Let us seek the soul (魂) of love formed by the two of us together.

Memory (記憶)
It is so, Life (人生) is memory (記憶), memory is remaining ash (殘灰).
Useless past dreams come now (只今)
Seizing this frost-covered body of mine
Tormenting me so, hurting me so.

It is so. Embracing this body of mine now (只今)
Faintly and seemingly cautiously
That dream of bygone days
Sobs coquettishly.

Then I declare=
Life (人生) is surely, dreams are of oblivion (忘却)
That which collapses without a trace.
As autumn falls, winter comes, and even the year changes.<62>

**Summary**

This publication presents three short poems by Oksaeng (億生): "Autumn," "Apple," and "Memory." "Autumn" contrasts the joy of spring with the melancholy of autumn, symbolizing fading youth or happiness. "Apple" uses the different flesh colors of a red apple and a red peach to represent two distinct individuals whose hearts have ripened in love, seeking a unified soul. "Memory" portrays memory as painful "remaining ash" from past dreams, ultimately concluding that life and dreams fade into oblivion with the passage of time. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in 1922 during the Japanese colonial period, offer a glimpse into the lyrical sensibilities of the time. While not overtly political, the themes of transience, love, and the burden of memory might reflect the underlying anxieties or emotional landscape of Koreans navigating a period of significant cultural and political change. The use of older Korean spellings and Hanja provides linguistic data from the era.

가을
						億生
가을(創作)
어제는
아릿답게도 첫 봄의 꼿봉오리가
너의 悅樂 가득한 쟝미의 빰 우에
웃음의 香氣를 노흐며 뛰놀앗스나,

오늘은
쓸쓸하게도 지는 가을의 落葉이
너의 떨며 아득이는 가슴의 우에
어린 꿈을 깨치며 비인듯 흐터지어라.

林檎
林檎은 새빨하지요,
그리고 복송아도 새빨하지요,
林檎은 속 빗이 희지요,
그리고 복송아는 속 빗이 붉지요.

여긔 林檎과 복송아가
다 가티 새빨하게 닉은 것이 잇습니다.
녜 林檎과 가티 새빨하게 닉은 그대의 맘,
그러고 복송아 가티 새빨하게 닉은 나의 맘.

그대는 林檎, 나는 복송아,
둘이 함끠 이뤄진 사랑의 魂을 차자 봅시다.

記憶
그러하다, 人生은 記憶, 記憶은 殘灰.
쓸대도 업는 지내간 꿈은 只今 와서
나의 불 서리운 이 몸을 붓잡고
이리도 괴롭히며, 이리도 압하라.

그러하다. 只今 나의 이 몸을 붓 안고
희미하고도 삼가는 듯 하게도
저, 지내간 녯날의 그 꿈은
아양스럽게도 흐득여 울어라.

그러면 나는 이르노니=
人生은 꾹, 꿈은 忘却의
슬어저 자최 업슬 그것이라고.
가을 지고, 겨을 와서 해 조차 바꾸는 때의.＜62＞
