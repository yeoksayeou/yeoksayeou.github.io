**Summary**

This article serves as a notice announcing the establishment of several new branch offices (支社) and a branch bureau (支局) for the magazine *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) across Korea in early 1922. It lists the names, locations, jurisdictions (covering numerous counties and provinces like P'yŏngan, Hwanghae, Hamgyŏng, Kyŏngsang, Chŏlla, and Kyŏnggi), and staff members for offices in P'yŏngyang, Chinju, Chaeryŏng, Hamnam (likely Hamhŭng area), Kaesŏng, Sŏngch'ŏn, and Sunch'ŏn. The notice instructs readers to direct future correspondence, subscriptions, and payments to their local branch. From a historical perspective, this demonstrates the expanding organizational network and reach of influential Korean cultural and nationalist publications like *Kaebyŏk* during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights efforts to establish a nationwide distribution and operational structure, indicating a growing readership and the magazine's significance in disseminating ideas despite colonial constraints. The detailed listing of jurisdictions and staff provides insight into regional engagement and the personnel involved in cultural movements of the time.
