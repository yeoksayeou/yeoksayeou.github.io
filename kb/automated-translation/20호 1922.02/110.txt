Views on Marriage by Literary Figures

The Love of Husband and Wife
Sisters may part from sisters, brothers may part from brothers, and fathers may part from sons, but a virtuous wife will never part from the husband she has chosen.
Irish Playwright Sheridan Knowles (1784-1862)

The Mother of the World
Marriage is the mother of the world. It preserves kingdoms, fills cities, fills churches, and also fills Heaven.
English Writer Jeremy Taylor (1613-1667)

A Wise Wife's Sympathy
When there is worry on the head and worry in the heart, its difficulty to bear is such that even shackles would keep a distance of three *sa*. However, what greatly alleviates this is truly the wife's sympathy.
English Author William Davenport Adams (1828-1891)

A Righteous Man's Wise Wife
When a good woman and a righteous man marry, it is like giving a metal club to a powerful spirit (鬼神에게 金棒을 주는 것이다). Indeed, I do not hesitate to make special note of this truth.
English Woman Writer Catherine Hamilton (1730-1803)

Good Fortune and Bad Fortune
A person unfortunate in marriage cannot also be called a person of good fortune.
French Classical Scholar Anne Dacier (1651-1722)<71>

**Summary**

This article compiles quotes from various Western literary figures and thinkers concerning marriage. The recurring themes emphasize the indissoluble bond between a virtuous wife and her chosen husband, the fundamental role of marriage in sustaining society and even Heaven, the solace a wife's sympathy provides amidst hardship, the formidable strength resulting from the union of a good woman and a righteous man, and the idea that marital fortune reflects overall life fortune. For historians, this piece offers insight into the perspectives on marriage being introduced or discussed among Korean intellectuals in the early 1920s. It highlights a focus on Western ideals of companionate marriage, virtue, and its societal importance, potentially contrasting with or complementing traditional Korean views during a period of significant social change under colonial rule. The selection of exclusively Western sources is also noteworthy.

文人의 結婚觀
夫婦의 사랑
姊妹는 姊妹와 떨어지고 兄弟는 兄弟와 떨어지며 父子는 父子와 떨어지더래도 有德한 안해는 決코 그 選定한 男便과 떨어지는 일이 업다.
愛蘭劇家 세리단·노올쓰(1784-1862)

世界의 母
結婚은 世界의 어머니다. 王國을 保全하며 都會를 채우고 敎會를 채우며 또 天國을 채우는 것이다.
英國의 作家 제레미·데에라아(1613-1667)

賢妻의 同情
머리에 근심이 잇고 마음에 근심이 잇서 그 견대기 어려운 것이 桎梏도 3舍를 避한다. 그러나 크게 이것을 가엽게 하는 것은 참으로 그 안해의 同情이다.
英國의 著述家 데뿌엔뽈트·아담스(1828-1891)

義人의 賢妻
착한 女子와 正當한 男子가 結婚하면 그것은 鬼神에게 金棒을 주는 것이다. 이야말로 自己의 眞理로 特筆함을 辭讓치 아니한다.
英國의 女作家 께엘·하밀톤(1730-1803)

好運과 不運
結婚에 不運의 사람은 또한 好運의 사람이라고 할 수가 업다.
佛國古文學者 데에시에에(1651-1722)＜71＞
