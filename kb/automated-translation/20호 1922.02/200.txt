The Rooster Crows
						Sowŏl

The rooster crows, cock-a-doodle-doo.
The rooster crows. When it crows cock-a-doodle-doo,
Reaching out in vain, my two arms are pushed away.
The long night, long enough to burn my heart......
After waking from the dream, sleep does not come, no matter how I close my eyes.

Up there, the green grass hill, the straight, deep island.
Last evening at the Namp'o boat wharf where we parted.
Holding my body, tossing and turning as I lie down,
Gently, tenderly, longing comes as I close my eyes.

No matter how I look,
The bright lamp is dim.
When I close my eyes, within them is a field of red reeds.
When the fog, formed on the sand, rises above the water

At the Taedong River ferry crossing, the sun rises.

The Day I Dreamt

Outside, snow, let the snow come.
Quietly, below the window, let the moonlight enter.
That Woman (女子) who came riding the twilight,
Came into my dream's embrace and nestled there.

My pillow was soaked through with tears.
Just like that, that Woman went away.
Only a quiet star, a single shadow
Peeped through the window crack.

At Chemulp'o

Night
Falling asleep alone is truly lonely.
Longing pierces my heart.
Like this, steadily,
It seems I might even forget your face altogether.

The sun has already set and dusk has fallen,<18>
In the drizzling rain, the night lingers,
The sea wind is just cold.
This place is called Chemulp'o (濟物浦) in Inch'ŏn (仁川).

Just lying quietly and listening,
Just lying quietly and listening,
The spring tide rolling in whitely,
Blocks my sight and I just sob.

Dawn

At the edge of the pond where fallen leaves (落葉) hide their feet,
The towering tree shadows,
Float dimly upon the water,
I stand alone. Still, still
The eastern sky is still dark.
Even for heavenly beings (天人), tears of love become clouds,
Has the lonely dream pillow become blurred?
My beloved, but, but,
Let the waves come washing in. Redly, redly,
The clouds standing in the west, treading the sky.
The half-moon (半달) stays awake in mid-heaven (中天).

My House

At the foot of the mountain, fallen onto the field,
Behind the shore of the wide sea,
I shall build my house,
Placing a large road before it again.
Those people passing by on the road,
Each separated, walking the road alone.
When the day darkens at the white rapids' edge,
I will stand at the door sill (門 깐) and wait.
As the dawn bird cries and night's shade lingers,
The world whitely, or quietly
From the sparkling coming morning,
Watching the passing travelers attentively,
Is it you? Is it you? <19>

**Summary**

This collection features five poems by Kim Sowŏl, characterized by themes of longing, solitude, dreams, and the passage of time, often set against evocative natural backdrops like the Taedong River, Namp'o, and Chemulp'o. The speaker laments separation from a beloved, waits expectantly, and observes the melancholic beauty of landscapes at night, dawn, or during snowfall or rain. The poems convey deep personal emotions using simple language and imagery rooted in Korean scenery. From a historical perspective, these poems are significant examples of early modern Korean lyric poetry, showcasing Sowŏl's signature style blending traditional Korean sentiment (han) with modern forms. While not directly addressing colonial realities, the pervasive sense of longing, loss, and waiting, tied to specific Korean places, might offer historians insight into the emotional atmosphere of the period, reflecting perhaps a displaced yearning or the general sorrow felt during that time. The use of 1920s Korean language and spelling is also of linguistic interest.

大洞江 뱃나루에 해 도다 오네.

꿈 뀐 그넷날
박게는 눈, 눈이 와라.
고요히 窓 알에는 달비치 들어라.
어스름 타고서 오신 그 女子는
내 꿈의 품속으로 들어 와 안겨라.

나의 벼개는 눈물로 함빡히 저젓서라.
그만 그 女子는 가고 말앗서라.
다만 고요한 별, 그림자가 하나
窓 틈을 엿보아라.

濟物浦에서

새벽
落葉이 발을 숨는 못 물가에,
웃둑웃둑한 나무 그림자
물 우에 어섬푸러히 떠오르는데
나 혼자 섯노라. 아즉도 아즉도
동녁 한울은 어두워 오아라.
天人에도 사랑 눈물, 구룸 되어
외롭은 꿈의 벼개 흐렷는가.
나의 그대여, 그러나 그러나,
물질녀와라. 붉게도 붉게도,
한울 밟고 서녁에 섯는구룸.
半달은 中天에 지새여라.

내집
들 우에 떨어진 메의 기슭,
넓은 바다의 물가 뒤에,
나는 지으리, 나의 집을,
다시곰 큰 길을 압헤다 두고.
길로 지나가는 그 사람들은
제각곰 떨어저 혼자 길거리.
하얀 여울턱에 날은 저물 때,
나는 門 깐에 섯서 기다리리.
새벽새가 울고, 지새는 그늘로
세상은 희게, 또는 고요하게
번쩍이며 오는 아츰부텨
지나가는 길손을 눈녁여 보며,
