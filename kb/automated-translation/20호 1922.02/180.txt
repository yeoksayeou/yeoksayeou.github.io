Publication Date: 1922년 02월 08일
Article Title: 해빗 못보는 사람들 (People Who Cannot See the Sunlight)
Author: 金石松 (Kim Sŏksong)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

People Who Cannot See the Sunlight
						Kim Sŏksong

Sunlight is the light that shines
Into every nook and cranny of the world, without gaps.
Oh! But alas, alas,
People who cannot see the sunlight!
How unfortunate they must be.

Without even distinction between Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter,
Where the wind over the ice pierces the flesh,
Even in the North Pole where only white bears live,
Ah! The merciful sunlight
Shines into every corner.

Carrying the dawn upon their heads,
Treading on the evening moon's shadow,
Everyone bustling about busily—
The faces of pale friends.
Oh! Faces that cannot see the sunlight.

A suspicion suddenly arises in me.
Could you perhaps be prisoners?
Confined behind those iron bars, hands and feet bound,
Perhaps rebels against the Law?
Ah! My suspicion deepens further.

If I record you in your order:
Officials, the Rich, the Educated Class,
Merchants, Tenant Farmers, Laborers—.
If I record you as I please:
The awake ones, the sleeping ones, the working ones, the playing ones—.

Friends of all classes!
Why is it that you
So cruelly despise
The sunlight granted absolutely—
This unconcealed, masculine love?

Someone among you
Will likely resent me deeply for saying this.<8>
Saying, 「You so-called poet who knows nothing of the world,
Learn more about the world」.

「To live, one must eat,
To eat, one must work,
To work, one must be busy,
And if busy, naturally, like you,
There is no time for admiring the wind and moon」

「But Friend—look here.
When you return from the factory,
(The sky seems murky
And my head feels dizzy),
Didn't you tell me the other day?」

「(And I grow weary of my job,
Work doesn't feel right in my hands,
And sometimes the world feels so tiresome
That thoughts of just quitting it all
Also come to mind), you said」

「And my memory
Speaks your words again—.
(We don't even have a Sabbath!
Does Jesus only believe in the rich ones?
Aren't we all children of the same God......)」

「(Whether the morning sun is beautiful
Or the evening moon is bright,
It has absolutely nothing to do with us.
Should we rather pluck out the sun and moon
And bury them forever in the Pacific Ocean!)」

I always hear it.
Such anguished cries
From the mouths of all humankind,
And what is even more severe,
From the mouths of Koreans—from my own mouth.

Oh! Friends! You who cannot see the sunlight,
Friends cursed by the world!
What shall we do hereafter!
Shall we shatter the sun and moon!
Shall we create a new sun and moon!
(Night of January 16th)<9>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏksong laments the condition of people from all social strata—from officials to laborers—who are so consumed by the struggles of daily life and toil that they cannot perceive or appreciate the beauty of nature, symbolized by sunlight. It portrays their exhaustion, despair, and alienation, using a dialogue with a worker friend to illustrate the physical and mental toll. The poem culminates in a desperate cry, questioning whether to destroy the existing world (sun and moon) or create a new one. From a historical perspective, the poem powerfully reflects the harsh socio-economic realities and pervasive despair within Korean society under Japanese colonial rule in the early 1920s. Historians might find the poem's depiction of class-spanning suffering, the explicit mention of "Chosŏnin" (Koreans), the critique of labor conditions, and the final radical questioning indicative of the social consciousness, hardship, and nascent resistance simmering during that period.

해빗 못보는 사람들
						金石松
해빗은 누리의 구석구석에
뷘틈업시 비추는 해빗이다.
오! 그러나 그러나
해빗 못보는 사람들!
그들은 얼마나 不幸일가.

春夏秋冬이 分別조차 업이
어름위 바람이 살을 찌르는
힌 곰만사는 北極에도
아! 慈悲한 해빗은
구석구석에 비추인다.

새벽을 머리우에 이고
저녁달 그림자를 밟으며.
저마다 바뿐듯이 돌아다니는
새아햔 친구들의 얼굴들이다.
오! 해빗 못보는 얼굴들이어.

나는 疑心이 벌컥 난다.
그대들이 囚人이나 아닌가.
저 銕窓 속에서 손발까지 묵긴
法의 反逆者나 아닌가.
아! 나의 疑心은 더욱 깁허간다.

그대들의 次序대로 記錄하면
官吏, 富者, 有識階級,
商人, 小作人, 勞働者-.
나의 마음대로 記錄하면
깨인 놈, 자는 놈, 일하는 놈, 노는 놈-.

모든 階級의 친구들이어.
그대들은 어찌하야
絶對로 許諾하는 해빗-
숨김없는 男性的 사랑을-
그러케 모지게 실혀하는가.

그대들 中에 누구는
이러케 말하는 나를
살 깁히 원망할 것이다.＜8＞
「世上 철모르는 所謂 是認아
좀더 世上을 알으라」고.

「살기 위하야는 먹어야 하고
먹기 위하야는 일하여야 하고
일하랴면 밧부어야 하고
바뿌면 自然히 너의 가티
吟風咏月할 겨를은 업다」

「그러나 親舊-여보게.
자네가 工場에서 돌아갈 때에
(한울이 부우연한 것 갓고
머리가 어찔어찔하다)고
엇제인가 나다려 말하얏지」

「(그리고 職業에 실증이 나며
일이 손에 잡히지 안코
때때로 世上이 귀찬하서
그만 말아 버리리가 하는
생각도 난다)고 하얏지」

「그러고 나의 記憶은
또 자네의 말을 말하네-.
(우리에게는 主日도 업네!
예수도 돈 잇는 놈만밋나?
다가튼 한우님 子孫인데......)

「(우리는 아츰해가 고흐나
저녁에 달이 밝으나
도모지 상관이란 업네.
차라리 해와 달을 따다가
太平洋에 영장이나 할가!)」

나는 언제든지 듯는다.
이와 가튼 沈痛한 絶叫를
온 人類의 입으로부터
더욱이 甚한 것은
朝鮮人-나의 입으로부터

오! 친구여! 해빗 못보는
世上에 咀呪바든 친구들이어!
우리는 장차 어찌할거나!
해와 달을 깨치어 버릴가!
해와 달을 새로 만들가!
(1月 16夜)＜9＞
