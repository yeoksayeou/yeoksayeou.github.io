**Body Text Translation:**
The Foolish Fisherman
						Kim Nang'un

The Foolish Fisherman

One
Pitch black room
The wind whirls and strikes
The waves leap as if mad

The fisherman’s tiny boat
Shakes and shakes
As if it will soon capsize

He—
With a fearful heart, a pounding chest
Firmly grips the oar
And, with one mind, rows.

“Row! Row!
This is big trouble”
—Thus he cries out inwardly

The wind dashes
The waves leap
Roaring swoosh—swoosh
Crash! Crash!

Two
“I must be almost there...”
—Thinking this, he
Stopped the oar and looked around carefully
The shore where the boat could land—
Picturing it before his eyes.

All around
—Darkness <31>
—Gale
—Raging waves
He could not tell where he was

His body trembled
His spirit was obliterated
He
Was seized
By terror and anxiety

“Lord of Heaven (한우님), save me!”
—The fisherman prayed
—Upon the waves in the dark night

And then—
The foolish fisherman
Began to row again
The shore where the boat could land—
Picturing it in his thoughts

Three
Ah!
The wind is strong
And his strength is weak

Useless longing
Futile rowing
He grew exhausted

Blood forms on his two hands
Sweat flows over his whole body
A fire burns within his chest

The foolish fisherman
Still rows

The wind dashes
The waves leap
Roaring swoosh—swoosh
Crash! Crash!

The boat— <32>
Becomes a mere plaything
Sinking, floating
Flustered, rushed
Simply dragged here and there

—Towards the middle of the sea
—Into the darkness
—Onto the path of death

Ah!
The wind is strong
And his strength is weak. (End)

Song of the Wanderer

I am a wanderer, I am a floating weed
As the wind blows, as the waves strike
Flowing and flowing, blown and blown
I know not the place where I will finally land

My home is East, West, South, North
That path—
High mountains, deep waters, pitch-black wilderness
And that desolate field of dusk
That is perhaps my place of eternal dwelling

Rain, wind, frost, snow
Loneliness, tears, sighs, fatigue
Those things that do not leave me
Are my friends, my companions

I shall weep all alone
I shall laugh all alone
Whether you hear or not
I shall sing all alone

I am a wanderer, I am a floating weed
As the “unknowable hand” leads
I shall go, I shall go, the path without direction
I shall go, I shall go, the endless path
...(End)...<33>

Prayer

Lord of Heaven (한우님)?
I—
Fame, position, property,
Palaces with golden pillars,
Crowns
—I desire none of them

If I
Were to possess
Even one of those things
I too would
Become a captive
Of falsehood, greed, jealousy

Lord of Heaven!
I only pray
Let me—
Without applying powder and perfume
Without wearing a false mask
Honestly, truthfully
With red blood!
—Live just like that.
(End)
(Written Dec. 1921)<34>

**Summary**

The three poems by Kim Nang'un explore themes of struggle, alienation, and authenticity. "The Foolish Fisherman" depicts a desperate, perhaps futile, battle against overwhelming natural forces, symbolizing a struggle for survival or against insurmountable odds. "Song of the Wanderer" portrays a sense of rootlessness, displacement, and lonely endurance, embracing a life of wandering without a destination. "Prayer" expresses a rejection of worldly success and superficiality, pleading for a life lived with simple sincerity and truthfulness. From the perspective of Korean colonial history (published in 1922), these poems resonate deeply. The fisherman's plight could reflect the Korean people's struggle under Japanese rule, the wanderer's rootlessness could mirror the loss of national sovereignty and cultural identity, and the prayer for authenticity might represent a yearning for spiritual integrity amidst the pressures and corrupting influences of the colonial era. Historians might find the poems' expressions of powerlessness, existential wandering, and the desire for inner truth significant indicators of the psychological and spiritual landscape of colonized Korea.
