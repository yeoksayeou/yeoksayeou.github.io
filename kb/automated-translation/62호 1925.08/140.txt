Article Title: 社會日誌 (Social Diary)
Article Type: 소식 (News)

Social Diary
From June 16th to July 14th

June 16th
Korea △ The dispute between staff and students at Sup'o Chŏngmyŏng Girls' School (水浦貞明女學校) worsened, and 50 dormitory students were expelled. △ In Hŭngju-dong, Koktong-myŏn, Kanggye, one member of an armed group was killed by police. △ In Tŏgwŏn County, one recruiter for the Taedonghoe (大同會) raising military funds was arrested (被促). Overseas △ The joint association of various groups in Beijing resolved to hold a nationwide strike on June 25th. △ The coal miners' strike in Canada expanded.

June 17th
Korea △ Residents of Sŏhwa-myŏn and Haeun-myŏn in Yonggang County submitted a petition of opposition to the County and Provincial Offices regarding the establishment of a bombing squad base on the west coast of Yonggang County. Overseas △ Negotiations between the diplomatic corps of six nations and the Chinese side regarding the Shanghai Incident began. △ A new cabinet in Belgium was formed with Mr. Boré (?) (뽀레) as premier. △ The number of unemployed in Britain was announced as 1.3 million.

June 18th
Korea △ In Kyŏngsŏng, four robbers were arrested by the Chongno Police Station. △ A press corps was organized in Kaesŏng. △ <64> In Yanggu County, two members of an armed group were killed. Foreign △ Negotiations with the committee of six nations regarding the Shanghai Incident broke down due to disagreement. △ Various groups in Beijing postponed the general strike planned for the 25th to the 30th. △ The Guangdong Student Conference resolved to sever economic ties with Japan and Britain. △ Koreans also participated in the anti-Japanese and anti-British movement in Jilin.

June 19th
Korea △ In P'ungnyong-ri, Ŏrae-myŏn, Kanggye County, a fierce battle occurred between about 20 members of an armed group and police, resulting in the deaths of two members. Overseas △ The diplomatic corps of six nations notified the Chinese side in Beijing of the resumption of negotiations. △ Representatives from various sectors in Shanghai decided to halt the market strike (罷市). △ Chŏng 7 (鄭 7), a special envoy for the Chinese anti-foreign movement, was arrested in Yokohama, Japan.

June 20th
Korea △ The All-Chosŏn Tennis Tournament, hosted by the Kwansŏ Sports Association, was held in P'yŏngyang. △ Miryang Agricultural School (密陽農樂學校) burned down completely. △ In Changjin County, about 10 members of an armed group appeared, causing a disturbance.

June 21st
Korea △ P'yŏngyang Higher Normal School (平壤高等普通學校) burned down completely, causing 150,000 won in damages. △ In Iksan County, due to drought, a disturbance arose as farmers destroyed the workshop (場坊) of the Igwŏk Irrigation Association. Overseas △ 2,500 Chinese in the British concession of Guangdong went on a general strike. △ The 3rd International Labour Conference was held in Belgium, attended by over 700 representatives from 38 countries. <65> △ An anti-foreign movement also arose in Hunchun (畏春), leading to clashes between police and the public.

June 22nd
Korea △ On the eastern bank of the Yalu River in North P'yŏngan Province, two members of an armed group were killed by Japanese police. Overseas △ France began preparations for withdrawal from the Ruhr (루얼).

June 23rd
Korea △ In Changhŭng-dong, Changnim-myŏn, T'aech'ŏn County, a battle occurred between an armed group and police. Overseas △ The diplomatic discussion group in Beijing resolved on five points and submitted them to the Duan government. △ Chinese and British forces finally clashed in Guangdong, resulting in over 10 casualties.

June 24th
Overseas △ In Fukushima Prefecture, Japan, about 500 Korean laborers caused a major disturbance over unpaid wages. △ The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs submitted 13 demands to the Diplomatic Corps, including the lifting of martial law and the release of all arrested Chinese, simultaneously submitting a proposal for adjusting unequal treaties. △ In the Guangdong Sino-British conflict, the Guangdong side suffered 1,400 casualties. △ The Guangdong government demanded an apology from Britain.

June 25th
Korea △ The Women's Garden Party, hosted by the Women's Department of this magazine, was held at Sangch'unwŏn outside the East Gate. △ The track and field meet hosted by the Chosŏn Sports Association was held at the Hullyŏnwŏn training ground in Kyŏngsŏng. Overseas △ In Beijing, taking advantage of the Dragon Boat Festival (端年日), a large anti-foreign demonstration parade of over 200,000 people <66> took place. △ A revolution broke out in Greece, and the cabinet resigned en masse. △ The foreign diplomatic corps rejected the 13 points submitted by the Chinese side. △ An anti-foreign demonstration by student groups also occurred in Jiuzijie, Jiandao (間島局子街). △ Various countries in Shanghai decided to hold a national funeral for those who died in the Shanghai Incident.

June 26th
Korea △ In Pukchin, Unsan, two members of an armed group used a theatrical performance (腳戱會) to incite cheers for independence (萬歲). Overseas △ The Shanghai General Chamber of Commerce declared a permanent economic severance with Japan and Britain. △ Japan rejected China's submission for revising unequal treaties. △ Banks and shops in Shanghai opened all at once. △ The Guangdong student group issued an ultimatum to Britain, demanding a response regarding the cancellation of unequal treaties within 40 hours. △ A new cabinet in Greece was formed by Mr. Pangalos (팡가로스).

June 27th
Overseas △ A major fire broke out in Aomori Prefecture, Japan, burning down over 500 buildings including the station, theater, and banks. △ Three main culprits in the assassination plot against General Kada (禍田 大將) in Japan were sentenced to death. △ The diplomatic corps of various countries rejected China's submission regarding unequal treaties. △ Russia's Mr. Trotsky (토로츠키) was elected Chairman of the Supreme Economic Council.

June 28th
Overseas △ At the Japan-China Journalists' Conference held in Beijing, the absolute necessity of abolishing unequal treaties was resolved. <67>

June 29th
Korea △ Disturbances occurred in ** [Text Missing] and T'aech'ŏn due to the appearance of armed groups.

June 30th
Overseas △ In Beijing, a large demonstration expressing vengeance against Britain (雪恥的 大示感) reconvened. △ The British Labour Party submitted a motion of no confidence in the government, which was rejected. △ A large anti-British movement arose throughout India. △ At the commemoration meeting for the Shanghai Incident in China, attended by a large crowd of over 50,000, cries against imperialism were raised.

July 1st
Korea △ The Korean Lawyers' Association resolved on a motion to abolish discriminatory treatment between Japanese and Koreans. △ In Yangji-dong, Tongsan-myŏn, Chu-sŏng County (주城郡 - likely typo for Pukch'ŏng?), an armed group took military funds. Overseas △ A major typhoon hit the Philippine Islands, causing numerous casualties. △ The Chinese government announced its intention to convene a national assembly within one month. △ The United States began preparations for holding a conference on abolishing extraterritoriality for China.

July 2nd
Overseas △ Students and workers in Guangdong requested a declaration of war against Britain or organized death squads. △ Anglo-Russian conflict intensified, with Russia insisting it would not shy away from war. △ The Guangdong government abolished the General Headquarters and organized itself as a revolutionary government.

July 3rd
Overseas △ The Beijing government announced an ordinance to control anarchism <68> (1. Those propagating anarchism shall be sentenced to second-degree fixed-term imprisonment. 1. Those misleading the people and spreading rumors shall be sentenced to second-degree fixed-term imprisonment. 1. Those inciting riots for propaganda purposes shall be sentenced to first-degree fixed-term imprisonment. 1. Those receiving money to agitate shall be sentenced to third-degree fixed-term imprisonment. 1. Those who confess before being discovered shall have their sentences commuted (滅刑함).)

July 4th
Korea △ In Tongmak, Paengnam 7-ka, Koyang County, a fire started from a mosquito net (蚊帳), causing casualties in one family. △ In Ch'ŏlsan County, at Ch'aryŏngwan, four policemen were shot dead by an armed group. △ In Yangp'yŏng-dong, Taep'yŏng-myŏn, Pyŏk* County (碧*郡 - Name Incomplete), a clash occurred between an armed group and police, resulting in the injury and death of two policemen. Overseas △ A strong earthquake occurred in the Yonago region of Tottori Prefecture, Japan. △ European diplomatic circles opposed the US proposal for an international conference on China as premature. △ Representatives of the Beijing Vengeance Conference (雪恥대회) petitioned Feng Yuxiang (憑玉鮮) and Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) to prepare militarily. △ The British government demanded the extradition of the Indian revolutionary Mr. Praphulla (?) (푸라라트), who was staying in China calling for the liberation of Asian nations. △ British coal miners resolved on a major strike. △ In Kyushu, Japan, 5,000 coal miners also went on strike over wage issues. △ Disturbances occurred in various places during the US Independence Day celebrations, resulting in 1,800 casualties.

July 5th
Korea △ In Tongsin-myŏn, *San County (*山郡 - Name Incomplete), an armed group burned down the township office (面所). <69> △ In Nyŏngbyŏn, a battle occurred between an armed group and police, resulting in serious injury to a policeman. Overseas △ The World Peace Conference was held in London with representatives from 16 nations. △ Chinese textile mills halted operations due to power outages in Shanghai. △ The Dutch cabinet resigned en masse.

July 6th
Korea △ 400 students at Tongnae Higher Normal School went on strike demanding the expulsion of a teacher. △ In Yangch'aek, North P'yŏngan Province, a major battle occurred between an armed group and police. △ The armed group from Ch'aryŏngwan clashed with police in Kiju Pŏmhwa-myŏn (幾州凡華面). Overseas △ The Indian revolutionary Mr. Praphulla (?) (프라라프) issued a manifesto stating that Britain is the enemy of both China and India and calling for joint resistance. △ The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs submitted a protest to the Shanghai Consular Body.

July 7th
Overseas △ A strong earthquake occurred in the Nagoya region of Japan. △ The Kuomintang party in Guangdong issued a proclamation severing all relations with Britain. △ Mr. Hu Hanmin (胡漢民) of Guangdong resigned as Generalissimo following the establishment of the National Government. △ Franco-German trade negotiations broke down. △ 2,000 British troops arrived in Hong Kong. △ In Shanghai, 45,000 people became unemployed due to power shortages.

July 8th
Korea △ In Kangsŏ County, Korean briquette workers (鍊炭職工) went on strike together demanding wage increases (値上). Overseas △ Due to heavy rain in the Nagoya region of Japan, over 1,500 houses <70> were flooded. △ The Guangdong government resolved to implement an armed blockade when the opportunity arises. △ Guangdong students organized a student army. △ The French Foreign Minister attacked the imperialistic policy towards China in the Chamber of Deputies.

July 9th
Overseas △ The waterworks company in the foreign concessions of Shanghai suspended water supply to Chinese districts. △ The French Chamber of Deputies unanimously ratified the 8-hour workday. △ A boycott (不賣同盟) arose in Shantou (沙頭), China, and concurrently, seamen went on a general strike.

July 10th
Overseas △ Striking British coal miners rejected the proposal from the mine owners. △ The Beijing government sent an official document to the Guangdong government stating, "Let us jointly confront foreign powers with righteousness as our weapon," and dispatched a special envoy. △ Leading US officials advocated for the swift opening of an international conference on the China issue. △ In the US state of Tennessee (케네데 - likely typo for Tennessee), a trial began over the question "Did humanity evolve from apes or was it created by God?" (Scopes Trial).

July 11th
Overseas △ The Beijing diplomatic corps suspended meetings due to the withdrawal of the French minister. △ In Guangdong, a Strike Federation (罷工聯合會) was formed by people from China, Korea, Annam (Vietnam), India, and Taiwan. △ Regarding the Shanghai Incident, the Beijing government issued official documents to each province stating, "Maintain order, uphold justice; the government will not yield an inch."

July 12th
Korea △ Along the Han River in Kyŏngsŏng, <71> severe flooding occurred, causing the tragic devastation of over 7,000 households. Overseas △ The Kuomintang committee in Guangdong resolved to sever economic ties with Britain and France, stating, "Britain and France are responsible for the Shakee Incident (沙面事件)."

July 13th
Korea △ Along the Naktong River, disturbances occurred in various places due to heavy rain. △ In North and South Chŏlla Provinces, various areas were thrown into turmoil due to heavy rain. △ In P'ungsan-dong, Wiyŏn-myŏn, Unsan County, a fierce battle occurred between an armed group and police, resulting in serious injuries to 3 policemen and the deaths of 3 group members. Overseas △ The trial of Kim Chi-sŏp (金祉爕) for the Nijubashi Bridge bombing incident in Japan reopened in Tokyo. △ A revolution broke out in the South American republic of Ecuador, overthrowing the government.

July 14th
Korea △ Along the Naktong River in Kyŏngsang Province, unprecedented devastation occurred due to major flooding, with the number of affected residents reaching tens of thousands. △ The entire Kyŏngsŏng Municipal School Council resigned over the issue of Kyŏngsŏng City reducing school expenses for Yi Wan-yong (李完用). Overseas △ The British ship HMS Hermes (?) (헬매쓰號) sailed for China loaded with troops and aircraft. △ Japanese residents in Shanghai resolved to demand the cancellation of the 21 Demands. <72>

**Summary**

This "Social Diary" from *Kaebyŏk* magazine chronicles events in Korea and overseas from mid-June to mid-July 1925. Korean news highlights ongoing social tensions, such as school disputes and labor issues, alongside frequent, violent clashes between Korean armed resistance groups (무장단) and Japanese police across various northern provinces. Natural disasters, particularly severe flooding along the Han and Naktong rivers, also caused immense suffering. Overseas news is dominated by the aftermath of the Shanghai Incident (May 30th Movement), detailing widespread anti-imperialist protests, strikes, boycotts across China, diplomatic negotiations and breakdowns involving foreign powers (Britain, Japan, France, US, etc.), political instability within China (Beijing vs. Guangdong governments), and related labor unrest and political events in Japan, Britain, India, Russia, and elsewhere. The diary reflects a period of intense anti-colonial struggle within Korea and interconnected anti-imperialist fervor across East Asia, particularly in China.

For historians, this diary provides valuable snapshots of the persistent armed resistance against Japanese rule in Korea during the 1920s, often underreported in official sources. The frequent mentions of "무장단" activities (armed groups engaging police, raising funds, inciting crowds) indicate a significant level of organized opposition. Furthermore, the juxtaposition of Korean events with the major upheavals in China (Shanghai Incident, anti-British/Japanese movements, calls for treaty revisions) highlights the interconnectedness of anti-colonial and anti-imperialist movements in the region and how events in one country resonated in others, even featuring Korean participation in Chinese protests (e.g., Jilin, Guangdong Strike Federation). The diary also captures domestic issues like school conflicts, labor strikes (Korean workers in Japan and Korea), and the devastating impact of natural disasters under colonial administration.
