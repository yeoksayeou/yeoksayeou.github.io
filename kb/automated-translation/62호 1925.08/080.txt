Mr. Tan Chae Sin Ch'aeho, the Fixed Star of Nationalism..
						Pyŏn Yŏngno

When was it? It must have been 15 or 16 years ago already, when I was 12 or 13 years old. At that time, he was the chief editor of the 00 Newspaper Company and lived in Samch'ŏng-dong, while I lived in Maenghyŏn (present-day Kahoe-dong). As he was close with my elder brother (now deceased), he would come over to our house almost every day after leaving the newspaper office, or at least every other day. At my young age back then, how could I have known about the teacher's writing or character? Only his delicate constitution, his pale complexion, and especially his sparse upper mustache come faintly to mind today, after half of 30 years have passed.<39>

Since then, the teacher went over to China. After he went to China, there was naturally no opportunity to see his visage, and even news by letter became completely scarce (杳然). Occasionally, I would hear news (風便) of his well-being through hearsay, and whenever P'i Cho (碧初), Wi Tang (爲堂), or my elder brother sat together, they would discuss the teacher's personality and conduct so engagingly that even drowsy eyes would open. I heard that during that time, the teacher could not rest his body comfortably even for a single day, traveling east and west without a fixed destination (定往업시), and that he was preoccupied with writings such as National Poems (國詩) and Studies of Eastern History (東史考). I also heard that, having studied it intensely one winter, he became proficient in English and read books like Carlyle's "On Heroes, Hero-Worship, and the Heroic in History" and Gibbon's "The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire" (羅馬衰亡史) in the original language, even if his bookshelves gathered much tobacco ash (담배ㅅ진). There was even an anecdote that he once bought and wore pink women's underwear and entered a public bath (泳浴湯) — a place incomprehensible [for such attire] in society — suffering an embarrassment that shouldn't have been embarrassing.

To briefly mention the most notable points of the teacher's character and personality, it is his attitude of maintaining absolutely uncompromising principles (절대 비타협의 지조) steadfastly for over ten years without regret. Absolute non-compromise! That indeed might be the teacher's admirable strong point, and at the same time, perhaps a beautiful flaw. Just as someone said, "Genius is eternal suffering," the persistence of an uncompromising stance must surely be endless suffering. The state of the teacher's heart, enduring and experiencing such suffering for more than ten years into his declining years (己暮之年), truly remains beyond imagination.

Furthermore, the teacher has an incorrigible idiosyncrasy (癖) — almost to the point where it could be seen as an illness — <40> of not doing as others do. Isn't such an idiosyncrasy perhaps similar to the disposition of the current giant of the English literary world, Mr. G. K. Chesterton, who, if asked to work hard, plays, and if asked to play, insists on working hard?

The teacher writes poetry but is not a poet; he writes essays but is not a professional essayist; although he is versed in all sorts of studies on origins and explanatory texts (興考說文等), he is not one who devotes himself entirely to that path either. The teacher is, through and through, a historian (史家). Would it be disrespectful to the teacher to call him the Ranke of Chosŏn? In any case, in terms of his genius-like disposition, he is our most revered historian, bearing a resemblance [to Ranke]. The teacher's view of history is not systematic or scientific. However, his historical insight (史眼), like a torch (炬火), which wouldn't be an exaggeration to say penetrates the back of the page (眼光이 紙背를 뚤는다), will surely become the dissecting light (解剖光) for our history, which is dark and difficult to comprehend. Only concerning the teacher, precisely because it is not systematic, there is a super-systematic nobility (超조직적의 고귀함), and because it is unscientific, there is a super-scientific brilliant radiance (초과학적의 燦然한 光鋩). Lastly, in this world where two-legged beasts (二足獸) of petty talent and cunning (小器奸才輩) run rampant (跋扈), it is deeply admirable (밋브다) that the teacher, whose character and scholarship are both lofty and outstanding (高邁阜絶), exists.<41>

**Summary**

This article by Pyŏn Yŏngno is a personal recollection and assessment of the historian Sin Ch'aeho (Tan Chae). Pyŏn recalls Sin from his childhood, noting his physical appearance and later hearing about his difficult life in exile in China, his studies (including English), and anecdotes revealing his eccentricities. Pyŏn emphasizes Sin's defining trait: his absolute, uncompromising spirit, viewing it as both a strength and a flaw causing great personal suffering. He characterizes Sin primarily as a historian, not systematic or "scientific" by conventional standards, but possessing a uniquely penetrating, "torch-like" insight capable of illuminating Korea's obscured past. Pyŏn concludes by expressing deep admiration for Sin's profound character and scholarship in a world perceived as full of mediocrity.

For historians, this piece offers a valuable contemporary perspective on Sin Ch'aeho during the colonial period, written by another prominent intellectual. It provides personal insights into Sin's character, particularly his famous intransigence and eccentric habits, beyond his formal writings. The assessment of Sin's historical methodology as non-systematic but possessing "super-systematic" insight reflects contemporary views on his unique approach to nationalist historiography. The language itself, including the older spellings and vocabulary (like "밋브다" for admirable), is also of linguistic interest.

國粹主義의 恒星인 丹齊 申采浩 先生··
						卞榮魯
그언제이든가 벌서 15,6년 전이니 내가 12,3세때인가 한다. 그때 00신문사 주필로서 三淸洞에 사섯고 나는 孟峴(현재 嘉會洞)에 사럿섯다. 나의 舍伯(晩)과 친하신 관계로 罷社後면 거진 매일, 적어도 隔日로는 집에 놀너 오섯다. 그때 나희 어린 나로서야 엇지 선생의 문장이나 인격이야 알 수 잇섯스랴. 다만 纖弱하신 체질, 혈색조차 못한 얼골, 특히 빈약하신 웃수염만이 半30년 지난 오날에 아득히 생각날 뿐이다.＜39＞
이후로 선생은 中國에 건너가섯다. 中國에 건너가신 뒤로는 선생의 풍채에 접할 긔회는 물논이고 言信좃차 杳然하엿다. 잇다금식 風便으로 안부를 듯는 것과 碧初
爲堂 두 분이나 舍伯하고 안즈면 조름오는 눈이 뜨이도록 선생의 人物性行을 평할 뿐이다. 드르니 선생은 그간 하로동안 몸을 편히 쉬이지 못하시고 동서 定往업시 다니섯다는 것과 國詩, 東史考等 저작에 여념이 업섯단 말이며 어느 겨울에 연구하섯는지 영어를 精通하야 칼라일의 영웅숭배론, 끼본의 羅馬衰亡史 등 書籍을 책장에 담배ㅅ진은 만히 뭇치실망정 원어로 읽으신다는 것을, 桃色 여자내의 사서 입고 泳浴湯이란 이해업는 사회에 드러가섯다가 붓그럴 것 업는 망신을 하섯다는 일화까지 아울너 드럿다.
선생의 인물 성격중 특기할 점을 잠간 말할 것 가트면 그의 절대 비타협의 지조를 10년 이하로 가티 끗끗내 직히고 뉘우치지 안는 태도이시다. 절대 비타협! 그야말로 선생의 갸륵하신 장점인 동시에 아름다운 결점도 될가 한다. 누구의 말에 「천재는 영원한 고통이다.」 한 것과 가티 비타협적 지조의 지속이야말로 그지업는 고통이라 할 것이다. 그러한 고통을 己暮之年에 10여년을 더하도록 당하고 체험하시는 선생의 흉중이야말로 상상에 남는다.
그리고 선생에겐 남이 하는대로는 하지 안는 뜨더 고칠 수 업는-거진 병으로까지 보아도 상관업슬 癖이＜40＞ 게시다. 그러한 癖은 現今 영국 문단의 거장 지, 케, 췌스터론 군의 남이 에쓰라 하면 노라 하고 노라 하면 구지 에쓰라 하는 풍치와 비스름치나 안은가 한다.
선생이 시는 지으나 시인은 안이시고, 논문은 쓰시나 전문적 논문가도 안니시며, 모든 興考說文 등에도 통하시긴 하다하나 그러타고 그 길에 전념하시는 이도 아니다. 선생은 어대까지든지 史家이시다. 朝鮮의 랑케라면 선생께 무례나 안닐는지. 하여간 천재적 기분에 잇서서 방불한 가장 경모할 우리의 역사가이시다. 선생의 역사관은 조직적이거나 과학적의 것은 아니다. 그러나 眼光이 紙背를 뚤는다 하여도 과언이 안닐만한 선생의 炬火가튼 史眼이야말노 족히 어두어 가히 알 수 업는 우리 역사의 解剖光이 되실 것이다. 선생에 한하여서만은 조직적이 아니니만큼 超조직적의 고귀함이 잇스며, 비과학적이니만큼 초과학적의 燦然한 光鋩이 잇는 것이다. 마즈막으로 小器奸才輩의 二足獸가 跋扈하는 이 세상에 人格學識이 아울너 高邁阜絶하신 선생이 게심 저윽히 밋브다.＜41＞
