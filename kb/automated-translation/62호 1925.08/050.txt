Mr. Yi Tongnyŏng, a Magnanimous Elder..
						Han Kiak

It was ten years ago from now, in July, when I was heading to Siberia to study in Russia (露國), that I first met Elder Sŏgo Yi Tongnyŏng (石吾 李東寧) in Nikolsk-Ussuriysky (니코리스크). For nearly a year, I stayed attached to the same house, receiving much guidance and causing various troubles. The Elder was born in Mokch'ŏn (木川), South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸南道). He came early to Kyŏngsŏng (京城) to study at home, but moved by the overseas trends and the domestic situation, he began various movements together with Mr. Chŏn Tŏkki (全德基), who was a pastor at the Sangdong Methodist Church (尙洞 禮拜堂). What is especially memorable is the establishment of a middle school called the Youth Academy (靑年學院), based on 5 won sent by a young man named Kang Ch'ŏnmyŏng (姜天命) from Hawaii (布哇), which trained many fine young people scattered both inside and outside the country, and the organization of a group called the Youth Academic Friends Association (靑年學友會) by connecting with numerous comrades, the spirit of which remains even today.

As worldly affairs did not go as wished, in the winter of the Kyŏngsul year (庚戍年 - 1910), he moved with the five brothers of Mr. Sŏngje Yi Siyŏng (省齊 李始榮) to West Jiandao (西間島) and devoted himself to educational work (育英事業). Later, he crossed over to Vladivostok (海蔘威) and cooperated in various aspects with P'oje Yi Sangsŏl (溥齊 李相卨) in his activities. A funny story from that time is that because Elder P'oje's nature was upright (亢直) and strong-willed (剛勁), he would relentlessly scold any young man who came by if he saw a fault, without forgiveness. Elder Sŏgo, however, would kindly explain (曉喩) and appease (無魔) them afterwards, so the young people in that region used to say that P'oje was like a strict father (嚴父) and Sŏgo was like a gentle mother (慈母). This alone allows one to infer (酬酌) a glimpse of his character. His courteous yet solemn (沈重) demeanor, his upright yet magnanimous (渾厚) character, his brilliant gaze (眼光), and his resonant voice (音聲) give anyone the feeling of familiarity as if they've known him forever, while simultaneously making them feel an unknowing sense of pressure (威壓). He is known for being very stubborn and often giving blunt rebukes, but it is his characteristic trait that he possesses leadership ability (統御力) and gives a favorable impression to anyone he meets even once.

Not long after hearing the news of Mr. Chŏn Tŏkki's passing (作故), Elder P'oje also fell ill (成病) due to anguish (心火) and became a guest who would not return (不歸의 客) in a foreign land ten thousand li away. One can imagine the Elder's feelings (心懷) at that time without needing words. Afterwards, he spent lonely years in sorrowful indignation (悲憤), and then before the March First Movement (三一運動) of the Kimi year (1919), he came from Russian territory (俄領) to Shanghai (上海) together with Mr. Sŏngje Yi Siyŏng.<34> At that time, I was in Tokyo (동경) and went to Shanghai, and meeting him again to speak of our long-pent-up feelings (阻懷) seems like yesterday, but I don't know how many times the indifferent spring and autumn (春秋) have already changed. It has been a long time since he has not been in good health due to over a decade of hardships (風霜), but I hear that his mind and body have become even weaker (衰弱) in the last 2-3 years. As his illness cannot be taken lightly (等閑히), it is said that due to the strong urging (强勸) of his comrades, he is seeking a quiet place for recuperation (療養), but has not yet recovered (快復). I have seen him in my dreams (夢寐間) more than once or twice, but even news (音信) is cut off, and now I do not even know his address. The Elder is 57 years old this year; his elderly mother and other family members live in Wŏnsan (元山), and his eldest son, Ŭisik (義植)君, is studying at Yamagata High School (山形高等學校) in Japan (일본). Finding it difficult to refuse repeated requests (屢次 부탁), I write these few words merely to fulfill my duty (塞責), and from afar, I pray for the Elder's recovery (快差).

**Summary**

This 1925 article by Han Kiak is a personal tribute to Yi Tongnyŏng (Sŏgo), a prominent figure in the Korean independence movement. Han recounts meeting Yi in Nikolsk-Ussuriysky a decade earlier and describes Yi's character as magnanimous, respected, yet firm, contrasting him with the stricter Yi Sangsŏl ("strict father" vs. "gentle mother"). The author outlines Yi's key activities: co-founding the Ch'ŏngnyŏn Hagwŏn school in Seoul, moving to West Jiandao and Vladivostok for educational and independence work with Yi Siyŏng and Yi Sangsŏl, and eventually arriving in Shanghai. The article concludes with Han expressing concern over Yi's declining health and his current whereabouts being unknown, while mentioning his family's location. From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for its contemporary portrayal of Yi Tongnyŏng's personality, his network (Chŏn Tŏkki, Yi Siyŏng, Yi Sangsŏl), and his movements across key centers of the independence movement (Seoul, West Jiandao, Vladivostok, Shanghai) during a critical period. It offers a glimpse into the personal connections and hardships faced by exiled activists in 1925.

寬厚長者의 李東寧氏··
						韓基岳
石吾 李東寧 선생을 뵈옵기는 지금으로부터 10년전 7월에 내가 露國에 유학하려고 西伯利亞로 향하엿슬 때에 「니코리스크」에서 初對面하게 된 것임니다. 거진 1년동안이나 한집에 부처 잇스면서 만흔 지도를 밧고 여러 가지 괴로움을 끼처섯슴니다. 선생은 忠淸南道 木川 출생으로 일즉히 京城에 올나와 가정에서 수학하다가 해외 풍조와 국내 현상에 感한 바가 잇서 尙洞 禮拜堂에서 목사로 잇든 全德基씨와 갓치 여러 가지 운동에 착수하게 되엿는데 특히 기억할 것은 布哇가 잇는 姜天命이라는 청년이 보낸 돈 5원을 기초로 하야 靑年學院이라는 중학교를 설립하야 내외에 버려잇는 조흔 청년을 만히 양성한 것과 다수의 동지와 연결하야 靑年學友會라는 단체를 조직하야 금일까지 그 정신이 그저 남은 것이라 할 것임니다. 世事가 如意치 못함을 따라 庚戍年 겨울에 省齊 李始榮씨 5형제분과 가티 西間島로 이사하야 育英事業에 전력하다가 다시 海蔘威로 건너가 溥齊 李相卨와 가치 여러 가지 방면으로 협력하야 활동하게 되얏슴니다. 그때에 한 우순 말은 溥齊 선생의 천성은 亢直하고 剛勁하기 때문에 來往하는 청년의 과실만 보면 용서업시 모러대는데 石吾 선생은 그뒤로 잘 曉喩하고 무마하야 그 지방 청년들이 溥齊는 嚴父갓고 石吾는 慈母갓다고 하엿다 함니다. 이것만으로도 그 성격의 一斑을 酬酌한다 하려니와 慇懃하고도 沈重한 태도, 강직하고도 渾厚한 성격, 광채잇는 眼光, 우렁찬 음성, 누구에게든지 一面如舊의 感을 니르키는 동시에 不知中威壓을 늣기게 됨니다. 고집이 대단하고, 생핀잔 잘 주기도 유명하지마는 統御力이 잇서 한번 본 이에게는 호감을 주 는 것이 특색이라 할 것임니다. 全德基씨가 작고한 소식을 드른지 얼마 안되여서 溥齊선생도 心火로 成病하야 萬里異域에서 不歸의 客이 되니 그때 선생의 心懷이야 말치 안하도 알 것임니다. 其後 외로히 悲憤한 세월을 보내다가 기미년 三一運動이 잇기 전에 省齊 李始榮씨와 갓치 俄領에서 上海로 오시게＜34＞ 되얏슴니다. 그때 나는 동경에 잇다가 上海로 가게 되야 다시 맛나 阻懷를 말슴한 것이 어제 갓것마는 무심한 春秋는 발서 몃번이나 밧구엿는지 모르겟슴니다. 십수년 風霜으로 늘 건강치 못하신 것은 오래 전부터 일이어니와 2,3년 이래로 더욱 심신이 쇠약하섯다 함니다. 병세가 等閑히 할 수 업서 동지들의 强勸으로 한적한 곳을 차저 療養중이라 하오나 아즉도 快復치 못하섯다 함니다. 夢寐間에 뵈온 것은 한두번이 아니오나 音信조차 막키어 지금은 주소까지도 모르게 되얏슴니다. 선생은 금년 57세로 노친과 기타 가족은 元山에 사시고 그 장자 義植군은 일본 山形高等學校에 재학중임니다. 屢次 부탁을 저바리기 어려워 塞責으로 數字 적사오며 멀리 선생의 快差를 바람니다.
