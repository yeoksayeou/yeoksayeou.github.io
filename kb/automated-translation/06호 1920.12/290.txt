Article Title: 藝術과 道德의 關係 (The Relationship Between Art and Morality)
Author: Not mentioned
Article Type: 문예평론 (Literary Criticism)

There was an era when the purpose of art was sought in Beauty (美). No, even now, there are many such schools in literature. Since the sudden rise of Naturalism, it is said that what art seeks is Truth (眞). That is, the true reality (眞相) of human life and nature. Whether it is ugly (醜) or evil (惡), if it is true, it can be depicted without hesitation (忌憚 업시). For this reason, the dark side of human life, unknown to people until now, has been endlessly exposed (暴露). The era of defining literature as a slave (奴隷) to morality and intending to use it as a tool (器具) for promoting good and punishing evil (勸善懲惡) has already passed. If a moral scholar (道學者) heard this, they would say that modern literature is truly useless and harmful (無益有害), but literature by no means (決코) has a practical (實行的) purpose. Therefore, literature with a practical purpose, like educational novels (敎育小說) or religious novels (宗敎小說), cannot be called pure (純粹) art. We only (오즉) depict it "as it is" (如斯하다고); we do not admonish or reject by saying "do this" or "do not do this." Often (往往), those who view literature as a tool for morality and intend to reject (排斥) it if it does not conform (附合) to morality are people who do not know what literature is. Literature is not morality. It is not bound (拘束) by religion; it occupies (占領) a position one level higher (한層 더 놉흔 地位) than these. Literature can sometimes critique (批評) morality and critique religion, but morality and religion cannot critique literature. Literature – literature that aims for truth (참) – is a second Nature. Nature is absolute (絶對).<150>

**Summary**

The article argues that the primary goal of art, particularly literature following the rise of Naturalism, is the pursuit of Truth (眞), not Beauty (美) or moral instruction. It asserts art's autonomy, stating that literature should depict reality, including its ugly or dark aspects, without hesitation and free from the obligation of serving moral or religious ends (勸善懲惡). Works with practical aims, like educational or religious novels, are deemed impure art. Literature occupies a higher plane than morality or religion and can critique them, but not vice-versa. True literature, seeking truth, is presented as a 'second Nature', absolute in its own right. From a historical perspective, this 1920 article reflects the influx of Western literary theories like Naturalism into colonial Korea and the ensuing debate about the function of art. The strong assertion of art's independence from didactic moralism (a traditional function) is significant, signaling a shift towards modernism and potentially 'art for art's sake' amidst a complex environment where literature was also seen by some as a tool for national enlightenment or resistance. Historians might find this useful for understanding the evolving intellectual landscape and the tensions between aesthetic autonomy and social engagement in early colonial Korean literature.

藝術과 道德의 關係
藝術의 目的을 美에서 求한 時代도 잇섯다. 아니 지금도 文藝上 이러한 派가 만히 잇다. 自然主義가 勃興된 以來로 藝術의 求하는 바는 眞이라고 한다. 卽 人生 自然의 眞相이라고 한다. 醜이던지 惡이던지 그것이 참이면 忌憚 업시 描出할 수가 잇다. 이러한 까닭으로 只今까지 사람들이 몰랏던 人生 暗黑面은 끝일 것 업시 暴露되고 말앗다. 文學을 道德의 奴隷로 定하고 勸善懲惡의 器具로 쓰랴고 하는 時代는 이미 지낫다. 道學者가 이런 말을 들으면 現代文學은 진실로 無益有害한 것이라고 하겟지마는 文學은 決코 實行的의 目的를 가지고 잇는 것은 아니다. 이럼으로 敎育小說이나 宗敎小說과 가티 實行的 目的을 가진 文學은 純粹한 藝術이라고 할 수가 업다. 오즉 우리는 如斯하다고 描寫할 뿐이요 이러케 하라 이러케 하지말라고 懲勸하거나 拒絶하는 것은 아니다. 往往이 文學으로써 道德의 器具로 보고 道德에 附合치 아니하면 排斥하랴고 하는 사람은 文學이 무엇인지 모르는 사람이다. 文學은 道德이다. 宗敎에 拘束된 것이 아니고 이보다 한層 더 놉흔 地位를 占領한 것이다. 文學은 때때로 道德을 批評하고 宗敎를 批評할 수 잇지 마는 道德 宗敎로써는 文學을 批評할 수 업는 것이다. 文學-참-을 目的하는 文學은 第2의 自然이다. 自然은 絶對이다.＜150＞
