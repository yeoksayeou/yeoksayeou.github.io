A Western Dance Not Found in the West

It would be good if people like me admitted what they don’t know and what they can’t do, but there are many instances where people say what is not true is true and claim to know what they don’t know, often becoming a laughingstock to others. When a certain organization (團體) shamelessly performed in public a strange dance, supposedly Korea’s (朝鮮) only Western dance, which does not even exist in the West (西洋), incurring the ridicule of foreigners (外人), people of our knowledge class (知識 階級), knowing what dancing itself is, might forgive it. However, if foreigners who do not understand that organization were to see it, might they think our entire people are being shamed?<139>

**Summary**

The article criticizes a Korean group for publicly performing a supposedly "Western dance" that is actually unknown in the West. The author laments the tendency to pretend knowledge or skill one doesn't possess, arguing that such inaccurate imitations lead to ridicule by foreigners and potentially shame the entire Korean nation. For historians, this piece offers insight into early 20th-century Korean anxieties about cultural encounters with the West, concerns over national representation and reputation in the eyes of foreigners during the colonial period, and the self-perceived role of the "knowledge class" in judging cultural authenticity and public behavior.

西洋에 업는 西洋 춤
내 나쯤이나 모르는 것은 모른다고 하고 못하는 것은 못한다고 하엿스면 조흘 것인데 안닌 것을 그것이라고 하고 모르는 것을 안다고 하야 往往이 남에게 取笑하는 일이 만타. 엇던 團體에서 朝鮮 唯一인 西洋춤은 西洋에서도 업는 奇怪한 춤으로 公衆의 압헤 無廉히 公開하며 外人의 取笑를 當하니 우리 知識 階級의 사람들은 춤추는 自體가 엇더한 것인 줄 아니까 容恕하겟지마는 萬一 그 團體를 解釋치 못하는 外人이 보면 우리 사람의 全體가 羞恥를 當하는 줄 알른지?＜139＞
