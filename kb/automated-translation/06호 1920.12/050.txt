Demands of Contemporary Notables Regarding Local Youth Groups

Having painfully realized lately that, no matter what, one must find a way to live by one's own efforts, our society has recently come to witness several new phenomena never before seen or heard. Among these, local youth groups (各地의 靑年團體), being both "local" and "youth groups," draw much expectation from the general public while also attracting considerable attention. Therefore, what actions must be taken to meet these expectations, and what attitude should be adopted when facing the situation? This is something the youth groups themselves must thoroughly investigate, of course—and indeed, any person thinking about our future path must surely have some long-held opinions on this matter. Thus, under the aforementioned title, our company sought the esteemed views of various personages, daringly wishing to share joys and sorrows with our beloved brothers. We hope this will serve as a reference for the current youth groups, and we express our gratitude to all those who offered their valuable opinions amidst their busy schedules. (The order follows the arrival of submissions; due to space limitations, those received relatively later could not be included. We sincerely ask for your understanding.)

My Conditions of Hope
Chang Ŭngjin, Vice-Principal, Hwimun High School

The recent emergence of countless youth groups in various regions is a truly delightful phenomenon. For us, who have formed a society and lead social lives, if we wish to further develop and perfect this society we inhabit, we absolutely cannot achieve this through the isolated power of separated individuals. It requires no further elaborate discussion that effects can only be produced after combining forces through collective action and making efforts.

Therefore, isn't it a fact known to us all that even in civilized nations today, various large and small groups like youth associations, women's associations, or other societies are established in every town and village, planning for the development and improvement of their respective regions? In the past, it was a common failing for all countries to treat only the capital cities as the sole centers of culture, concentrating all cultural facilities and institutions exclusively in the cities while neglecting the provinces. However, recently, this trend <25> has gradually changed. As a result of keenly feeling the need for cities and provinces to develop in step with each other, great efforts are now being devoted to regional development. Therefore, speaking of various regional groups, it is also a natural tendency that there will be various differences depending on the businesses and objectives they pursue. However, in planning the improvement and sound development of a region, groups formed by robust youth, such as today's youth associations in Chosŏn (朝鮮), should become the backbone and leaders. They must take responsibility for developing local industries, enlightening knowledge, establishing a sound spirit, and guiding the people's hearts towards the realm of civilization.

However, do all the youth groups springing up in our various regions today truly realize this mission and responsibility and act under firm conviction? Or does the majority consist of those merely swept up by a temporary trend, thinking, "Since other regions are organizing youth associations, should we try it too?"—arising from just a vague idea? This is my great question and, on the other hand, something I cannot help but worry about. What was the reason that the fervor for schools, which once arose in every town and village, cooled down like dead ashes within just a few years? Also, looking at Kyŏngsŏng (京城, Seoul) since last year, various associations and groups sprouted like bamboo shoots after rain, making noise with prospectuses and whatnot. Yet today, less than a year later, it seems seven or eight out of ten have already vanished like bubbles, becoming silent and unheard of. What indeed is the cause behind this? I can only think that this all stemmed not from firm self-awareness and confidence, but merely from being swept along by the temporary trends of the times.

Therefore, if the youth groups emerging in various regions today end up achieving no results, repeating the past mistakes, and merely ending up existing in name only, it will not only cause the relaxation and disappointment of the regional populace, whose spirits were once roused, but also lead to the loss of credibility for the youth, making it impossible to undertake any joint projects in the future. How can this not be a matter of great concern? We often catch glimpses in the local correspondence sections of newspapers that regional youth associations have held lecture meetings or discussion forums. Of course, lecture meetings are necessary, and discussion forums are also needed to enlighten knowledge. However, I believe that unless they devise improvements for farming villages, establish a sound spirit among local youth, promote industry, or demonstrate and implement some tangible effect on the ground, no matter how much they discuss practical principles or debate abstract empty theories, there will be no benefit. Having attached infinite hopes for the future to today's local youth groups while harboring infinite concerns, I hereby list some conditions of hope for youth groups, jotted down as they come to mind without any particular order, <26> to offer as a reference.

Things Youth Groups Should Strive to Realize as the Center of Each Region:
One. Correct the general trend of luxury.
One. Resolutely abolish drinking and smoking.
One. Eradicate the habits of laziness and vagrancy among youth.
One. Destroy the base customs of vulgar rituals and superstitions in villages.
One. Improve all unhealthy customs and manners of the region.
One. Make the general populace enthusiastic about educating their children.
One. Establish labor night schools in every village to educate children unable to attend regular schools.
One. Establish newspaper and magazine reading rooms in each village or township (面, myŏn) for general perusal, and for villagers without the leisure or ability to read, have those who have read conduct evening talks in the sarangbang (舍廊, guest room) or, in summer, courtyard talks.
One. Ensure that healthy men and women engage in proper occupations like farming, industry, or commerce, making them realize that idleness and living off others are absolute sins and sources of shame in life.
One. Encourage side businesses (sericulture, poultry farming, beekeeping, pig farming, etc., and others suitable for the region).
One. Establish simple communal barbershops and communal bathhouses in each village (洞, tong; 村, ch'on), offering services to the public at cost.
One. Consolidate various existing traditional kye (契, mutual financing association) assets within the village to establish a foundation for common village property, managing relief for hardship and disaster, aid for the poor, communal sanitation facilities, and other beneficial projects.
One. Pay attention to agricultural improvement, tree planting, road maintenance, communal sanitation, etc.
One. Establish savings kye or similar organizations in each village to encourage general savings.
One. Encourage the use of domestically produced goods for clothing and daily necessities as much as possible to cultivate a spirit of diligence and frugality.
One. Foster among youth respect for propriety, righteousness, integrity, and shame (禮義廉耻), cultivate trustworthiness (信用), and nurture sound virtues.
One. Encourage children of reasonably wealthy families to study abroad as much as possible.
One. Help find employment for the unemployed.
One. Hold lecture meetings or discussion forums from time to time.
One. Encourage exercise for young students.
One. Practice mutual aid for the sick, the poor, widows, orphans, etc.
(There are countless more if I were to enumerate them, so I stop here.)

I wish to add one more word here regarding the question of what attitude and <28> actions the youth association should take when striving to realize the conditions listed above. Of course, since the youth association is not an entity holding any power, it cannot force the general public, nor can it issue commands. Therefore, it is utterly impossible to realize all the items listed above at once. If the members of the youth association encourage and exhort each other, first practicing these things diligently themselves to become exemplary figures in the region, and if the general youth come to consider membership in this youth association as the highest honor, then naturally, they will gain the trust of the elders and the community. As trust expands, the projects can also expand. Thus, projects should by no means be rushed. Like the missionary work of the Christian church, I believe effects will manifest according to the degree of each member's struggle, through constant sincerity and effort.
(November 9th night, recorded ramblingly while listening to the sound of falling leaves rustling outside the window)

Strive for Education in Practical Strength
Cho Tongshik, Principal, Tongdŏk Girls' School

Currently, the paramount need and demand of our people, summed up in one word, lies in advancing civilization. We must manifest our capabilities through this, develop our lives through this, and achieve a complete renewal of all aspects. There is no other way but this. However, because this righteous main path is so vast and profound, we tend to treat it casually, wasting time with endless armchair discussions and empty theories. How many have truly paved a path of genuine livelihood into the realm of civilization and taken firm steps forward on the line of civilization? Therefore, such empty talk ultimately becomes utterly empty and vague, possessing no meaning and no value, merely returning to bubbles. To whom can the knowledgeable lament this? Generally, people have their own intentions, ideals, and aspirations. To propagate these intentions, ideals, and aspirations to the public, there must be methods. One might wield the pen or expend words. However, to realize these methods and achieve their effects, it is most crucial first to organize groups with unified intentions and coordinated actions. Thus, regardless of the nation or ethnicity today, there are inevitably assemblies and associations. Yet, our country lacked this freedom for ten long years. But now, as the slumbering peninsula's landscape seeks to illuminate itself, various kinds of youth groups have recently arisen in different regions, increasing daily. Their purposes and objectives are all perfectly good and beautiful, predicting a successful beginning and end for the future, thus gaining the welcome of society. Consequently, the power of active encouragement among aspiring youth grows stronger day by day. This can be called a phase where our new life is being produced. Here, I offer my heartfelt congratulations without reservation and, while expressing admiration, I wish to add a word regarding my hopes. Gentlemen, you are pioneers with abundant knowledge and thorough research, not individuals who will only understand after awaiting this humble person's discourse. <28> Gentlemen, while stimulating your own vigorous progressive spirit and lively constructive courage, you must simultaneously teach common sense and worldly wisdom to our pitiful brothers and sisters who, being shallow in knowledge and limited in experience, have dull nerves and weak sensitivity, wandering in darkness. The duty of the enlightened is to guide and transform the unenlightened, raising a civilized atmosphere in all matters, opening convenient doorways, and if even one person lags behind other ethnic groups, feeling shame, researching day and night, and managing the way to surpass other groups. If the enlightened view the unenlightened with indifference, how can we anticipate the improvement of our people? While generally striving for spiritual progress, it is also a responsibility to investigate ways to refine and improve farmers' tools if they are crude. It is also a responsibility to delve into the principles of craftsmanship and innovate if artisans' tools are inadequate. Starting from the everyday practices of human life, concerning food, clothing, and shelter, if there are unseemly aspects, debating them in writing and discussing them in talks, aiming to reach a state of perfection, is also a responsibility. If the enlightened do not perform such tasks, who else can? This is the unavoidable responsibility of the knowledgeable pioneers.

Striving for the improvement and development mentioned above, the capable, active, and accomplished youth groups are unions of brave individuals carrying the burden of our people's advancement and heading towards a new world. How could they remain merely formal groups? Strive on, our general youth...

Increase the Fervor for Reading and Exercise
Chang Tobin, Seoul Editor (?) (서울主幹)

I demand an increase in the fervor for reading from our youth groups. Without reading, knowledge cannot increase. Books are undoubtedly our teachers. Encountering many books is the same as attending to many good teachers and listening to their teachings and discourses.

I also demand an increase in the fervor for exercise from our youth groups. Without exercise, the body will weaken. If the body weakens, eventually one dies. Moreover, if the body is weak and incapable of handling various tasks, even while alive, it is the same as being dead. The only way to avoid this calamity is exercise.

Furthermore, I demand that our youth refrain from drinking alcohol, smoking tobacco, and engaging in all unseemly behaviors. Instead, they should believe only in truth, understand that human happiness lies in living a good spiritual life, and practice this. I believe religious faith is the most crucial method for this. <29>

Strive to Cultivate Your Own Strength
Im Kyŏngjae, Principal, Hwimun High School

One. Do not rely on external power (他力); strive to cultivate your own strength (自力). All things in the universe are governed by power and are results of power. From the grand movements of celestial bodies down to the scattering of dust motes, everything is related to power, whether it be self-generated or external. The appearance of wind, rain, frost, and snow, the blooming of flowers and falling of grass, manifesting the phenomena of spring, summer, autumn, and winter—whether conscious or unconscious—is beyond our ability to fathom. In any case, we can only call it the dominion of a mysterious power of creation. Also, observing the action of power, there is a generative action, meaning force A generates force B, and force B in turn generates force C, continuing endlessly in this manner. Therefore, we humans are also born through a kind of power, possess our own inherent power, and through this inherent power, we also produce other powers.

Although there may be various kinds of human power, they can be broadly divided into two: external power and self-power. Examples of external power include the power of wealth, the power of position, the power of protection from parents and siblings, etc. Of course, these powers are not unnecessary for our lives, but they are not permanent and indestructible powers. The power of wealth will be exhausted with use; the power of position will fall depending on circumstances; the power of protection will disappear with time. They cannot be possessed for long. Look around the world, are there not often people who rely solely on ancestral inheritance, parental protection, or their own position, indulging in ease and entertainment, neglecting the cultivation of their own strength, only to suddenly become miserable beggars when their wealth runs out and protection ceases? Such powers are indeed unreliable because they are not self-power but external power. How can one rely on external power?

Therefore, what we need is permanent and indestructible self-power, namely the power of virtue, the power of knowledge, the power of the body, etc. These powers, when cultivated through appropriate methods, increase the more they are cultivated and trained. Moreover, the possession of these powers persists as long as the self exists and has no limit no matter how much they are used. In terms of safety, they will not burn even if put in fire, nor melt even if put in water; thieves cannot steal them, and those with authority cannot take them away. Furthermore, wealth that did not exist and positions that did not exist can all be obtained through this power. <30> Achieving great deeds unparalleled in the world is also possible through this power. The sages and heroes of ancient times were also results of this power. How can the cultivation of this power not be the fundamental meaning of human life? Indeed, this power is a noble, solid, and important treasure. Strive! To cultivate self-power.

Two. Unite with the power of love. A group lacking the power of unity is not a complete group. Consequently, it cannot accomplish its intended projects and will eventually dissolve meaninglessly. Therefore, when organizing a group, solid unity is essential, and to solidify unity requires adhering individual powers together solely through the power of love. This is why a group without love can never become a solid group. Love is the sole adhesive for group cohesion. Therefore, when individuals contribute their individual power to form a group, it should not be like contributing money to a bank or company through stocks or partnerships. One must not hesitate to contribute the entirety of one's owned individual power.

Just as individuals possess varying amounts of wealth, individual powers naturally differ. However, if one thinks, "If others contribute five units of power, it is sufficient for me to contribute only five, even if I possess more than five," one can never form a solid group. One must pool the entirety of one's owned power of virtue, knowledge, body, etc. If, when contributing power, individuals calculate ratios of power or compare superiority and inferiority, how can solid unity be achieved? And without solid unity, how can glorious success be attained? What cannot be broken by hitting with wood should be hit with stone, and what cannot be broken by hitting with stone should be hit with iron. Strive for solid unity. Unite to be as solid as iron.

Three. The mission of youth groups is grave. Whether fortunate or unfortunate, it cannot be definitively stated, but the burden on the youth in Chosŏn at this time, neither early nor late, is excessively heavy. In present-day Chosŏn, none of the myriad endeavors have progressed, and nothing has developed. If one insists there is development, it is likely all due to external power. Indeed, Chosŏn is too desolate. To brilliantly adorn this naked Chosŏn, which has nothing; to make impoverished Chosŏn affluent; to make ignorant Chosŏn wise; to bring happiness to Chosŏn groaning in suffering—all these piled-up tasks are the burden of contemporary Chosŏn youth. Did the era store up these tasks, or did the tasks await the contemporary youth? Truly, too much has accumulated. In any case, whether liked or disliked, it is an unavoidable burden. To whom can I pass my work? Have the youth groups in various regions not felt this and risen up vigorously, acting in concert without prior agreement, one call answered by a hundred echoes? <31> I earnestly hope, O youth groups of various regions! Strive to cultivate each individual's self-power, fully develop practical strength, and at the same time, strive to perfectly fulfill the duties of all the burdens mentioned above and build the new culture of Chosŏn.

Establishment of Ideals, Propagation of Principles
Chŏng Taehyŏn, Principal, Posŏng High School

Youth are the flowers of the era and the vital energy of society. The rise and fall of peoples, the flourishing and decline of cultures, the fortunes and misfortunes of humankind—all depend on their ideals and activities. This is an exceedingly self-evident truth.

Recently, like blooming flowers and flowing rivers, the ideals and objectives of the various youth groups vigorously rising in our Chosŏn can be simply stated: to break down old forms and build a new civilization, thereby fully realizing each person's innate rights and displaying the inherent vitality and prosperity of the people. Praying for their sound development, I respond to the inquiry of the *Kaebyŏk* reporter and, disregarding presumption, dare to present two points of request: first, the establishment of ideals, and second, the propagation of principles. I heard a Japanese politician say, "The ideal remains lofty as ever, but the time for implementation has not yet arrived." If I were to dissect and articulate his psychological content, it would be: the ideal has barely taken shape, but there is still no definite self-confidence. If the ideal is established and self-confidence is strong, what inability to implement could there be? Furthermore, to accomplish a task, it is needless to say that one needs to gain the trust of society and expand one's influence. Therefore, I believe the propagation of principles is the urgent task at present.

Be Sincere! Be Persistent!
Yu Pyŏngmin, Professor, Posŏng High School

Recently, the youth groups burgeoning in various regions are truly difficult to count, but speaking of them collectively, this signifies the revival of Chosŏn youth and symbolizes the revival of Chosŏn itself. However, youth, carrying the elderly before them and the young behind them, singly bearing the safety and danger of society upon themselves—their original significance is profound and their responsibility grave. How much more so for the youth of Chosŏn today, facing a difficult and busy era requiring creation and construction from nothing to something, from darkness to light? Therefore, the groups formed by gathering such youth also carry a profound and heavy mission, and just as their own mission is profound and heavy, society's expectations placed upon them are also immense. <32>

I, too, as a member of society, am one who holds expectations that cannot be betrayed. Countless tasks in the world await you all; wherever you touch with your hands or tread with your feet, what is not your work? To now pettily list item by item what I hope for from you, and point out piece by piece what I expect of you, is like trying to catch fish by lifting just one mesh of a large net. How could I enumerate them all? I wish only to provide you with the secret to bearing this heavy responsibility in a few words. First:

Practice Sincerity (實踐, Silch'ŏn / Ch'ungsil). That Mr. Yi's advocated Theory A and Mr. Kim's proclaimed Theory B largely share the same purport, yet the Association A formed today and the Group B established tomorrow differ in form, reveals the hidden shadows and internal cracks they cannot conceal. Vainly stealing empty reputation and hollow fame, finding fault over minor flaws to establish separate factions, or pointlessly brewing ill will and malice, relying on trifles to readily form cliques—these are entirely relics of the old era that inflict enormous harm on our future path. If their objective is to enhance the welfare of society and their assertion is to reform the spirit of the people, then they must wholeheartedly concentrate their energy on this. Generally, all our endeavors will bear beautiful leaves and blossoms from this "Sincerity First" (忠實第一, Ch'ungsil Cheil). Second:

Persist (持續, Chisok). Sandbanks built up by river currents and mirages created by cloud shadows are not reality. The sayings that even a tiny plot of land must be chosen on the safety of the continent, and even a small room must be built upon the ground, are all satirical remarks mocking momentary emergence and instantaneous destruction.

If the various groups currently burgeoning throughout Chosŏn are merely a temporary phenomenon, then they are no different from sandbanks and mirages. What benefit would they bring to our society and people? For one who lacks the final basketful of earth at the summit of a nine-fathom mountain, not only is the previous effort lost, but it leads to doubled or tripled disappointment. For one who takes another step forward from the top of a hundred-foot pole, not only is the work completed, but a hundredfold hope arrives. Generally, all our endeavors will bear beautiful fruit from this "Persistence First" (持續第一, Chisok Cheil).

Therefore, let us take these two great principles—Sincerity and Persistence—and grasp hold of the various groups sprouting on our peninsula, even if they are coincidental, temporary phenomena. Let us forge them into one body under the unified command of a great conscience, uniting the hundreds of thousands of Chosŏn youth to accomplish noble work for our society and people. Only then will the future of Chosŏn approach a hopeful state; only then will the grave mission you bear achieve complete fulfillment; and only then can we say that the earnest expectations reflected in our eyes have finally yielded perfect results.

Urge Cultural Development
Hwang Ŭidon, Professor, Posŏng High School

Gentlemen of the youth groups in various regions, sprouting like bamboo shoots after rain, following the world's trends! <33> I truly believe and trust that you possess the great self-awareness, born riding the tide of world reconstruction, to shoulder the responsibility for the reconstruction of Chosŏn. However, how do you foresee and plan to proceed along the path of reconstruction you will manage in the future? Of course, everyone must have their own envisioned awareness of good methods, but now, prompted by the inquiry from the *Kaebyŏk* reporter, I wish to present my humble opinion regarding what I expect from you.

If we intend to reconstruct our lives, we must first reflect upon and investigate what the elements of our lives are. The elements of our lives are not solely spiritual, as Eastern sages dreamed. Nor are they solely physical, as material scientists are intoxicated with. As Whitman proclaimed, the physical life on the reverse side of spiritual life cannot be ignored. As Eucken asserted, spiritual life must be built upon natural life.

The reconstruction movement in our society, where both spiritual and physical aspects of life have simultaneously degenerated, especially requires extending both hands. Therefore, my hopes for you, gentlemen, who are the vanguard of our life movement, also lie herein. Accordingly, I wish to present some specific items related to this to you.

In the aspect of physical life... the economic problem cannot but be the primary issue. Among these, the two great problems of production and consumption are currently agonizing the entire world and all humanity. The era of individual, solitary management has already passed, and the era of social, cooperative management has arrived. I hope that you, gentlemen, will swiftly establish or expand producers' cooperatives and consumers' cooperatives to rescue our dying compatriots.

In the aspect of spiritual life... it is a fixed rule that one cannot maintain modern life without understanding modern culture. To enable our society to universally understand modern culture, naturally, we must rely on what has now become a globally familiar term: "cultural propagation" (文化宣傳, munhwa sŏnjŏn)... Taking it a step further, I believe there is nowhere else to turn without the three essential great tools: the teacher's pointer, the journalist's pen, and the lecturer's podium. Therefore, even if they are imperfect in the founding era, I also hope that you, gentlemen, will steadily, calmly, uninterruptedly, and tirelessly pour efforts into promoting education, publishing magazines, and conducting lecture tours, thereby urging cultural development.
"That is all."

With That Same Spirit and Sincerity
From a Talk with Mr. Kang Sŏk, Han Kyusŏl (江石 韓圭卨氏 談)

Regarding this issue, I not only lack complete and sufficient research <34> and consequently do not hold thorough and accurate views, but also, being old and shallow in knowledge, I have nothing to present as a proper opinion or complete assertion concerning various social problems. However, compelled by the earnest request of the *Kaebyŏk* reporter, I will briefly state my feelings on this matter as they come to mind. Rather than discussing this or that about our society, the hope for the future flourishing of Chosŏn youth groups, requiring no thousand words or ten thousand phrases, lies solely, I believe, in these four characters: spirit (精神, chŏngsin) and sincerity (誠心, sŏngsim).

Make Everything Chosŏn-ized
Ch'oe Chonghwan, Editor, Kongu (工友主幹)

But before making such demands, what I want to demand first is that the various youth groups scattered in the regions place their spirit, their ideals, their foundation—everything—upon the two characters "Chosŏn" (朝鮮). Thorough Awareness as New People
Kim Chegwan

Step out the door. Come out quickly. Staying inside the room is a disgrace to you.
Quickly step out the door. Your path has unfolded.
If there is any error in recognizing your own value and your surroundings, the fierce waves of the times will bury your life forever.
Do not go against the times. However, do not blindly conform to the times either. Your duty is only to grasp the great spirit that guides the times and pioneer a new path for the general trend.
You! Young gentlemen! You are Chosŏn youth. Asian youth. World youth.—The creation of human society through love and its construction through beauty rests solely on your iron fist. Magnificent is your greatness... there is no object against which to measure it.
Burn away the entire history of humankind—the history of plunder. Creating the sacred history of eternal peace is your great task. With this premise, perhaps what is expected will become clear on its own.
I have nothing particular to demand from the youth groups. Nor do I intend to demand anything. Why would I not have demands? Execute the established principles and platform. Strive for industrial development. Cultivate the spirit of self-governance and self-sufficiency. Uphold the sense of responsibility to preserve the family and make the village prosper, thereby establishing the stability of collective life. *****None of these are unnecessary. However, in my conscience, I absolutely cannot hope for such things. To expect such things from those who lack the freedom—political, economic, or otherwise—absolutely necessary to realize these required items based on their own consciousness for their own sake, not only amounts to advertising the requester's own hypocrisy but ultimately instills only a dirty sense of obligation in some sense, offering no benefit beyond insulting their character. Furthermore, even if they have some leeway to realize such internal projects, rather than hoping for something from the group itself, expressing desires regarding the spirit of the youth who form the foundational elements of that group ultimately constitutes the overall demand for the group, I think.
Just as a magnificent building adorned like a golden pavilion (金樓鳳閣), if its foundation is built on sand, carries the decisive fate of unavoidable destruction before one's eyes, if <36> their youth group is not determined by the thorough ideals and firm convictions of the youth themselves, then the general expectations placed upon them will not only end in despair, but the harm manifested in unintended directions, corresponding to the greatness of the group's power, will also be immense.
Regardless of whether it concerns figures or systems, the old seeks to obliterate new life to protect the interests of the old, while the new seeks to punish the rampant arrogance of the old to pioneer the life of the new—this is where the struggle between new and old begins. Fortunately, as the ethics of the new-old struggle are clarified today, it is the only path for the new person to unhesitatingly ally with the new and treat the old as the enemy. Therefore, it is an undeniable fact that, regardless of the era or circumstances, pioneering a new path and building a new life is utterly impossible without the new people, the youth. Thus, if this view is correct, the new person, the youth, must, from the starting point where their impulse is expressed, possess a thorough awareness, to the extent of severing all ties with the old generation class—regardless of age or whether they are capitalists—and all opposing elements to the new person. However, in certain circumstances, the new and old sides, excluding special classes, can sometimes be companions. It is true that even during that time or currently, they shake hands and accompany each other on certain paths, but this should not be considered the norm. At the very least, the new person, the youth, must be the chủ (主, master/main subject).
However, among the new people, the youth, there are still instances where, caught up in outdated personal feelings (情實, chŏngsil) in certain situations, they fall into self-imposed binds without realizing it. Examining the main cause, it generally stems from feeling the weakness of tangible and intangible social power, such as financial strength or social status, leading them to fear and shrink from social praise or blame, ultimately denying even the value of their own inherent nature. No matter how much real life is organized for the benefit of a portion of the powerful, leaving the world abandoned to the monopoly of evil people, selling one's character to seek money, flattering capitalists, or abandoning one's principles due to lack of social status and tailing the old while feigning an aged face—this is absolutely not the path for youth emerging from darkness into light. The frivolity and rashness of youth can never compare to the steady and calm attitude of the old, just as the hesitation and indecision of the old can never match the brave advance and strong decisiveness of youth. If the distinctive features of these strengths and weaknesses were compromised and harmonized, it might be convenient in terms of practical affairs, but in reality, as long as the conflict of interests between the two natures and the subtle emotional impulses in social psychology are ultimately incompatible, one must <37> reject the attitude of the old, who cling to enjoying their remaining years, in order to embrace the new life.
That the vibrant spirit of youth shrinks back, intimidated by social praise and blame, certainly proves the monstrous power of the old class. However, their praise and blame, not based on social conscience, are not worth a penny; rather, the harm they inflict by misleading the future path of youth is immense. Just as the personal value of the capitalist class, if stripped of their wealth, would not far surpass that of straw figures or wooden statues, if the traditional social treatment enjoyed by the old class—the value of their existence—were halted, their character would also be little different from that of capitalists. Therefore, ultimately, instead of worshipping such empty-shell, cloud-like idols or compromising with false and insubstantial praise and blame, one must firmly uphold the profound sense of responsibility that new life absolutely cannot be pioneered without youth comrades. Displaying the courage to sever all old things hindering this with a single stroke is precisely the youthful spirit that today's youth cannot afford to lack. If, fortunately, the youth depart from this spirit and establish themselves on a foundation for action, I shall be content with this.
— 10—15 —

Become a Purely Popular Union
Muga (無我) Yu Chinhŭi

Until the human world reaches some "Utopia," human effort must forever be a history of liberation. Flowing broadly like a great river from liberation to liberation is the promise of human history. Pursuing ceaseless liberation is the expression of striving towards ceaseless freedom, ceaseless life, and ceaseless creation. Therefore, the history from today onward, embracing the true meaning of liberation, is not the history of aristocrats or bureaucrats. It is purely the history of the populace (民衆, minjung). Consequently, the human liberation movement from today onward, unless it is a purely collective action of the populace, will not yield even the slightest effect. Resolutely separating from all encroachments of financial cliques (財閥, chaebŏl), from all coercion of bureaucratic cliques (官閥, kwanbŏl), from all possessions and traditions, from all familial sentiments (家庭的 情調), from all parochial attachments (鄕士的 愛着)—the populace, centered as much as possible around the proletariat (無産階級, musan'gyegŭp)—must strive for social and national rebirth with a consciousness of common purpose. In this sense, writing down a few conditions of hope:

1. Transcend Government-Established Groups (官設團體). The current Chosŏn policy, being one of restraint and interventionism, seems likely to be meticulously applied even to youth groups. This is not just the writer's conjecture but also true in fact. Officials from local government offices <38> or teachers from common schools seem to become the guiding organs of these groups. Through guiding intervention and encouraging coercion, their actions appear merely obligatory and passive. The true nature of such government-established groups can already be imagined and evaluated. Regardless of the presence or absence of future effects, these groups absolutely cannot be permitted to exist, primarily because they castrate the vibrant energy of youth, obstruct the self-awareness needed to respond to the currents of the times, and promote a passive morality that commits self-suicide. Therefore, either take a step forward and transcend the current situation, or if transcendence is impossible by any means, then all should dissolve themselves. This is because a useless existence becomes the source of fearsome harm.

2. Swiftly Strive for Grand Unity (大同團結). The promise of human life is cooperation (協同, hyŏptong), and the true fulfillment of this promise lies in global cooperation. Thus, from fragmented and narrow village-level unity and regional unity, we feel the need for national unity. Without belaboring the reasons for this, first strive for the realization of a Chosŏn Youth Federation (朝鮮靑年團, Chosŏn Ch'ŏngnyŏndan) that can serve as an organ for expressing the pure will of the populace and participate in global cooperation.

3. Become a Branch of the World Youth Federation (世界靑年團). The reason for urging the integration of local groups and the realization of a Chosŏn Youth Federation is the necessity of keeping step with the international youth federation (萬國靑年團, Man'guk Ch'ŏngnyŏndan). Just as today's Italian Youth League, Polish Youth League, and German Youth League not only take collective action within Italy, Poland, and Germany respectively, but are in reality branches of the World Youth Federation—the Young Italy League demanding a nation-state for Italy, the Young Poland League demanding a nation-state for Poland, and the Young Germany League demanding a nation-state for Germany—uniting nationally means international cooperation.

If I were to make demands freely, they wouldn't stop at just two or three. However, given the current state, demands beyond this seem like unreasonable coercion. So, I will write more later as the situation progresses, and stop here for now.

Possess This Kind of Spirit
Yu Hongjong, Director, Hongje Hospital

My demands for the youth groups in various regions are as follows:
1. Spirit of Sacrifice (犧牲的 精神). Whether individual or group, without a spirit of sacrifice towards its ideals and objectives, the ideal cannot be realized, and the objective cannot be achieved.
2. Spirit of Unity (歸一的 精神). This spirit is an element of cooperative group life and is especially necessary in our society. Regarding the group's mission, <39> sacrifice personal views and emotions, unite wholeheartedly, possess a spirit of unity, and avoid useless factional strife.
3. Spirit of Diligence and Frugality (勤儉的 精神). Guard against vanity and ostentation, change the spirit of laziness, and be diligent and frugal. This is the mother of success.
4. Practical Strength (實力). Practical strength is the foundation of all endeavors. Individuals should cultivate their own practical strength, and groups should strive to substantiate the group's practical strength. Existing in name only but lacking substance is the root of failure.
5. Power of Endurance (忍耐力). Endurance is most necessary. When planning an action, if one acts on a momentary impulse without ideals, objectives, practical strength, or endurance, rising in the morning and abandoning by evening, it is difficult to accomplish anything. Also, do not seek quick success, but seek it through perseverance and endurance.
6. Fighting Power (奮鬪力). We lack fighting power. To accomplish a task, one cannot lack the fighting power to advance bravely without yielding, hundred times folding but never breaking. To lose heart after a small defeat or lose courage due to temporary circumstances can hardly be called the action of a true man. Comparing one's life with the realization of the ideal, if one does not fight with the determination to offer one's life as a sacrifice for realizing that ideal, the future will be tragic, and the gate of destiny will be closed. The fighting power of great figures who achieved success in the world is extraordinary to behold.
7. Promotion of Hygienic Thought (衛生思想の鼓吹). This is a hope. Originally, medicine is the precursor of civilization. This can be sufficiently proven by civilized nations. Among them, hygiene is directly related to life, so it must be the precursor of civilization. Strive to promote this hygienic thought, as it is directly related to the destiny of the people. Therefore, each group should establish a hygiene department and strive with the primary goal of disseminating hygienic thought and improving hygienic conditions. This is earnestly hoped for. The great contribution made by youth groups in various regions to epidemic prevention during the recent outbreak is cause for celebration for the Chosŏn hygiene community and has set a new record for it. I hope for even greater efforts in this area.
8. Public Spirit (共益心). This is an element of communal life. It means having altruism (利他心) alongside egoism (利己心). Our society lacks this public spirit. Evidence for this can be seen in the lack of assembly halls, libraries, sports grounds, hospitals, etc., built by the hands of Chosŏn people in places called cities. I fervently hope for the swift display of public spirit and exertion of effort—directly, tangibly, intangibly, spiritually, and materially—in public benefit projects.
9. Jealousy (猜忌心). This is a part of psychological function and cannot be absent. Chosŏn people have a pathologically strong sense of jealousy. This is a major obstacle to social cooperative life and a great thief <40> in collective endeavors. If there is someone superior to oneself, one should advance and learn, striving to become equal to that person's practical strength, and discard the bad habit of jealousy.
10. Spirit of Dependence (依賴心). Independence and self-respect are human instincts, yet dependence hinders these instincts and produces incapable people. From individuals to groups, existence is possible without dependence, while dependence leads to ruin. Cherish independence and self-respect, and discard the spirit of dependence.

The above are hopes and advice. While hoping these things for the groups themselves, it should be the fundamental and foundational task of the central group in each region to voluntarily guide the local brothers and sisters in cultivating this spirit. I dare to offer this one word of advice to all members of youth groups everywhere.

Demands for Youth Groups
Ch'oe Tuson

The continuous emergence of various youth groups in different regions recently is a welcome phenomenon, and it is undoubted that, because of this, the desolate and lethargic Chosŏn society will achieve prosperity and gain vitality. However, even for projects with righteous purposes and good objectives, depending on the people managing them, the methods used to operate them, and the attitude adopted to conduct them, results contrary to the purpose and objective can arise—this is the common principle of worldly affairs. Therefore, regional youth groups cannot escape this common principle. Those in charge and those forming the body must be cautious about the methods and attitudes. Now, to avoid complexity, I wish to express our demands for youth groups in a few articles:

One. Cultivate moral spirit among youth to help establish new morality.
Two. Foster a social and communal spirit.
Three. Disseminate a spirit of self-discipline and self-control.
Four. Create opportunities to promote the desire for new knowledge.
Five. Instill in youth a spirit that rejects vanity and reveres practicality.
Six. Ensure that younger members do not neglect the cultivation of individual practical strength by becoming engrossed in the formal activities of the group.
Seven. Ensure there is no harm from fostering regional, biased, exclusive—in short, misguided—local sentiments.
That is all.

Knowledge and Practical Strength
O T'aehwan, Governor, Koyang County

In our long-slumbering land of Chosŏn, for whatever reason, <41> youth groups, formed by young people in various regions opening their eyes wide, shouting out, and rising like swarms of bees, have indeed started to move, whether actively or passively. Since they must advance, regardless of whether quickly or slowly, and since the objectives to be pursued through advancement are numerous, what should come first and what later? The future prospects depend, I believe, on carefully considering this order of precedence and executing tasks step-by-step without the slightest error.

Among the issues discussed today—religion, education, industry, etc.—which endeavor is not the responsibility of you, the youth? However, realizing these requires considerable time. Therefore, I request a few conditions as urgent matters for now.

One. Encourage the purchase and reading of newspapers and magazines.
Daily knowledge is in newspapers.
Monthly knowledge is in magazines.
Let the knowledge our twenty million people gain daily from newspapers and monthly from magazines be equal to that of others.

One. Diligently conduct popular lectures.
In today's situation where institutions for social education are incomplete, the only method to directly influence and enlighten both the ignorant and knowledgeable classes is through lectures. Organize lecture teams and conduct lecture tours in every nook and cranny.
Let the knowledge our twenty million people gain from lectures be equal to that of others.

One. Encourage the fine custom of diligence and savings.
Work diligently and earnestly in whatever occupation, save as much as possible from the income earned, and accumulate savings with any surplus.
Let the practical strength our twenty million people gain from savings be equal to that of others.

One. Establish economic organizations.
Appropriately reform old-style marriage and funeral kye, school kye, farming kye, etc., and newly establish industrial cooperatives, purchasing cooperatives, consumer cooperatives, etc., in each city, county, township, and village (府郡面里, pu-kun-myŏn-ri). Take economic measures in general life as much as possible.
Let the practical strength our twenty million people gain economically be equal to that of others.

My demands above are ordinary words that others also say, without any special opinion. However, my thought is that only when our knowledge and practical strength are equal to others can we claim a share in the equality and freedom discussed as world issues, and only then will the proclaimed "equal treatment" (同等待遇, tongdae tae'u) by those in power not be empty words. As a result, I make demands with these commonplace conditions, but I wonder what the authorities of the youth groups in various regions think. <42>

Demand for Complete Groups under Earnest Awareness
O Sanggun

Various forms of youth groups have already emerged and organized like bamboo shoots after rain throughout Chosŏn. As humans who inherently and necessarily lead social lives, our youth, in particular, are figures in the prime of life, standing at the beginning of endeavors with the richest hopes for the future. When gazing at the majesty of Paektu Mountain (白山), [we feel] our spirit; when viewing the vastness of the Blue Sea (碧海), [we see] our ideals. Thus, the groups formed by youth striving for cultural improvement with such vigorous and flourishing power, the central axis of youth society, are precisely our youth groups.

However, the youth groups currently emerging in various regions of Chosŏn are as vigorous as newly gushing springs and as splendidly blossoming as myriad flowers. This prosperity must have been achieved under the self-awareness of aiming to bathe together in world culture according to the trends of the times, assisting the development of virtue, wisdom, and physique, and creating an opening for societal unity to seize opportunities for project promotion.

Thinking thus, while deeply congratulating the vigorous rise of such groups, I add a great self-awareness upon self-awareness: it is the primary task of the present time for the various scattered groups in different regions to unite uniformly and establish one grand federated body. This is indeed one of the demands. Firstly, the highest means of social life lies in uniting the un-united to form unity and combining small unities to form great unity; this is the way to gain superiority. Secondly, each group must possess common characteristics, acting with coordinated movements under the same spirit and principles with a unified pace. Thirdly, for the existence and development of society, it is necessary to mutually unite for mutual assistance and supplementation. Only by doing so can the achievements of the youth groups be completed.

From this standpoint, a preparatory committee for the federation of youth groups has emerged, and its purpose has long been announced to the world. Now, with the Youth Association Federation (靑年會聯合會, Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoe Yŏnhaphoe) about to be established, one of our demands is partially fulfilled. It could be said so.

However, the thought arises again: are all those various youth groups truly organized under earnest awareness, and are they truly complete groups? At the same time, another hope arises: namely, our earnest awareness regarding this era and our youth.

The culture of this era, formed over many years—others live in the electric age, the age of freedom, situated in the realm of civilization, possessing all sorts of happiness and beauty bestowed upon human life. Yet, we have not even reached the ordinary age, the age of translation (通譯時代), still unable to fully grasp what life has to offer, <43> still unable to fully obtain what life can grant. However, if one seeks to grasp, one can grasp; if one seeks to obtain, one can obtain. Earnestly realizing this, expending heart and effort, and advancing ceaselessly—it is a natural principle that there is nothing that cannot be grasped or obtained. Therefore, we consider the cultural movement our sole objective and organize youth groups now. This is the sole life-giving organ for cultural development, whether through displaying our inherent culture, importing world culture, or enlightening knowledge. As such a crucially important entity, a life-giving organ, if it lacks thorough and earnest awareness, sways back and forth like a slender willow in the wind, or follows blindly, merely establishing a name because others are doing it—how could our youth endure such a thing?

Moreover, youth are the flowers of society. Just as there is no fruit without flowers, a society without youth lacks substance in its endeavors. Perhaps society could be formed even without youth, like figs or pomegranates? Could it be that even with youth, formation is impossible? It seems difficult to call this an exception, but regarding youth and society, it seems absolutely not the case. Human society cannot be formed without youth.

Herein lies the greatest purpose and greatest task of human life, resting solely on the shoulders of our youth. How could a group formed by those in such a position lack thorough and earnest awareness? If a group is not organized under earnest awareness, it can hardly be called a complete group.

Of course, I believe the current youth groups in various regions are complete groups. However, my earnest wish is that each group reflects more deeply, does not deviate from the line, follows its determined track—that is, if engaged in the cultural movement, uses its full strength only for the tasks related to the cultural movement, its defined objective, and does not tread outside that line—thereby ensuring the group's longevity and striving diligently and practically in its endeavors. <44>

**Summary**

This 1920 *Kaebyŏk* survey compiles responses from various Korean intellectuals and public figures regarding the burgeoning youth group movement across colonial Chosŏn. The respondents express both hope and concern, universally emphasizing the need for these groups to move beyond fleeting enthusiasm and superficial activities. Key demands include cultivating self-reliance (자력양성), focusing on practical action (실천) over empty rhetoric, moral development, education (including literacy and modern knowledge), economic improvement (promoting industry, frugality, cooperatives), fostering unity while avoiding factionalism, and developing a strong national consciousness rooted in "Chosŏn." Several writers warn against repeating past failures where movements fizzled out, stressing the importance of sincerity (성심), persistence (지속), and a clear sense of mission for national revival and cultural development.

For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of elite Korean perspectives on social mobilization and nation-building strategies under colonial rule, just after the March 1st Movement. The emphasis on self-strengthening, cultural development, practical skills, and moral reform reflects both nationalist aspirations and the constraints of the colonial environment, while the anxieties about superficiality and dependence hint at the challenges facing these nascent organizations. The explicit calls to "Chosŏn-ize" imported ideas and the debate around interacting with "old" versus "new" forces (including capitalists and established figures) illuminate contemporary ideological currents and internal social dynamics.

各地 靑年團軆에 對한 現代名士의 要求
암만 하야도 自己의 것은 自己 스스로가 살 道理를 꾀하여야 하겠다고 압흐게 깨다른 近來의 우리 社會에는 前에 일즉 見聞치 못하던 新現狀을 얼마라도 見聞케 되엇다. 그런 中에도 이 新現狀의 一되는 各地의 靑年團體와 如함은 그 各地이라는 또는 靑年團體이라는 點에서 一般의 囑望이 多한 同時에 또 一面으로는 注意가 甚하다. 이러한지라 어떠한 일을 實踐하여가야사 그 期望에 副答하며 또한 어떠한 態度로써 局에 當하여야 可할가 함은 靑年團體된 自身으로서 專意討究하여야할 것은 勿論이며- 적어도 우리의 압길을 생각하는 人士로서는 여긔에 對한 夙素의 意見이 업지 못할 것이다. 이에 本社는 右題의 下에 여러 人士의 高見을 하야 敢히 親愛하는 兄弟와 더불러 喜憂를 난우고저 하노니 이것이 現下의 靑年團體를 爲하야 一種의 參考가 되엇스면 幸甚이라하며 아울러 여러 가지로 悾惚하신 中에 特히 高見을 주신 여러분에게 向하야 感謝의 意를 表하노라. (順序는 到着하는대로 하얏스며 딸아 紙面의 關係上 比較的 나중에 着한 分은 揭載치 못하얏사오니 깁히 諒恕하심을 바라나이다)

나의 希望條件
徽文高等普通學敎校監 張膺震

近來 各 地方에 無數한 靑年團體가 發生함은 甚히 可喜할 現象이올시다. 社會를 組織하고 社會的 生活을 營爲하게된 우리가 우리의 居住하는 이 社會를 더 一層 發達하게 하며 더 一層 完美하게 하랴면 우리가 分離된 個個人의 單獨力으로서는 到底히 어찌할 수 업고 어대까지던지 團體的 行動으로 力을 合하야 振作한 然後에 效果를 奏할 것은 다시 贅論을 得할 必要가 업슬 것입니다.
그럼으로 今日 文明 各國에서도 地方마다 靑年會니 婦人會니 무슨 團이니 하는 大小의 各團體가 坊坊村村이 設立되지 아닌 것이 업서 各其 地方의 發展을 計하며 改善을 圖함은 우리의 周知하는 事實이 아니옵니까. 曾往에는 各國이 모도 都會로써 唯一의 文化 中心地를 삼아 온갓 文名의 施設이나 機關의 設置를 都會에만 專力하고 地方에 踈忽히 하야 왓슴은 一般의 通弊이엇스나 近來에는 이 傾向이＜25＞ 漸次로 一變하야 都會나 地方이나 同一의 步調로 發達을 圖치 아니면 안될 것을 切實하게 感覺한 결과 地方 發展에 多大한 努力을 傾注하게 되엇습니다. 그런 즉 地方의 各 團體로 말하여도 그 經營하는 바 事業과 目的에 依하야 種種의 差異가 有할 것은 亦是 自然의 勢이겟스나 何如間 一地方의 改善을 圖하며 健全한 發達은 計劃함에 對하야는 其中에도 健實한 靑年으로 組織된 團體, 假令 말하면 今日 우리 朝鮮의 靑年會와 如한 것이 中堅이 되며 指導者가 되어 그 地方의 産業을 發達시키며 知識을 啓發하고 健實한 氣風을 樹立하며 人心을 文明의 域으로 向導하는 責任을 가져야 할 것입니다.
그러나 今日 우리 各 地方에 勃興되는 모든 靑年團體가 모도 이와가튼 使命과 責任을 自覺하고 確乎한 信念下에서 起動하는 것인지 或은 一時的 風潮에 끌리어 他地方에서 靑年會를 組織한다니 우리도 하야 볼가하는 다만 漠然한 생각으로 起한 것이 그 大部分을 占領치 아니하엿는지 이것이 余의 一大 疑問이오 一邊으로 憂盧를 不禁하는 바이올시다. 曾前에 坊坊村村이 일어낫던 學校熱이 數三年을 不過하야 死灰와 가티 冷却하고 만 것이 무슨 까닭이옵니까. 또 昨年來 京城으로 보아도 무슨 會니 무슨 團이니 雨後의 竹筍가티 發生하야 趣旨書니 무엇이니 하고 떼들던 것이 一年이 不過한 今日에 벌서 泡沫과 가티 消滅되어 寥寥無聞하게 된 것이 十中七八이 넘는 듯합니다. 이것이 果然 어떠한 原因에 基由한 것입니까. 나는 이것이 모도 確固한 自覺과 自信에서 出하엿던 것이 아니오 다만 一時的 時代 風潮에 끌리어 되엇던 것이라고 밧게 생각할 수 업습니다.

讀書熱과 運動熱이 增進하도록
서울主幹 張道斌

余는 우리 靑年團體에 對하야 讀書熱의 增進을 要求하노라. 讀書 아니하면 知識이 增進될 수 업스리라. 書籍은 無論 우리의 先生이니라. 書籍을 만히 接함은 곳 조 先生을 만히 모시고 敎訓講話를 듯는 것과 一般이니라.
또 余는 우리 靑年團體에 對하야 運動熱의 增進을 要求하노라. 運動 아니하면 身體가 衰弱하리라. 身體가 衰弱하면 나종에는 죽나니라. 또 身體가 衰弱하야 萬事에 堪能치 못하면 살아서도 죽음과 一般이니라. 이 禍를 免할 道는 오즉 運動이니라.
또 余는 우리 靑年團體에 對하야 靑年들이 술먹지 말며 담배 먹지 말며 온갓 不美한 行爲하지 말고 오즉 眞理를 信仰하야 人生의 幸福이 靈的 生活을 잘함에 잇슴을 알고 實踐하기를 要求하노라. 그 方法은 宗敎信仰이 第一 緊要하다고 思하노라.＜29＞

各自의 自力養成에 勉勵하라
徽文高等普通學校長 任璟宰

一. 他力을 信賴치말고 自力養成에 努力하라. 天地萬物은 모다 力의 支配오 力의 結果 아닌 것이 無하다 하겟도다. 크게는 天體의 運行으로부터 작게는 塵埃의 飛散까지라도 自力으로던지 他力으로던지 모다 力의 關係이다. 風雨霜雪이 有하며 花開草落이 有하야 春夏秋冬의 氣像을 現出함도 意識的인지 無意識的인지는 吾人의 窺知키 不能한 바이다. 何如間 神秘한 造化力의 支配라 謂할 밧게 업도다. 또 力의 作用을 觀察하면 生生하는 作用이 有하니 卽 甲力이 乙力을 生하고 乙力이 又 丙力을 生하야 이와가티 線延不絶하게 生生하는 것이다. 그럼으로 우리 人生도 一種의 力으로 生하야 自身의 力을 禀有하고 이 稟有한 自身의 力으로 又 他力을 産出하게 되는 것이다.
吾人 人生의 力에는 多樣의 別이 有할지나 此를 他力과 自力의 二로 大別할 수 잇는 것이니 卽 他力으로 말하면 財産力도 有하고 地位力도 有하고 父兄의 保護力 等도 有할 것이다. 勿論 此等의 力도 吾人生活에 對하야 必要치 아니한 것은 아니나 此等의 力은 恒久不滅의 力이 아니다. 財産의 力도 使用을 딸아 磬渴될 것이오 地位의 力도 境遇를 딸아 落下될 것이오 保護의 力도 時期를 딸아 消滅될 것이라 長久히 所有키 不能할지니. 보라 世間에는 祖先의 遺業이나 父兄의 保護이나 또는 自己의 地位만 信賴하고 安逸과 娛樂을 貪하야 自力養成에 等閑하다가 財産이 盡하고 保護가 絶하면 一旦에 悲慘한 丐乞兒를 作하고마는 者가 往往잇지 아니한가 此等의 力은 果然 信賴할 수 업는 力이니 이는 自力이 아니오 他力인 所以다. 어찌 他力을 信賴하랴.

一 飜覆之心, 物慾之心 두지 말며 虛言말며 內로 不良하고 外로 假飾치 말일을 實踐할 일
今我 靑年界의 活動諸氏-이 三章의 實踐으로써 이 社會의 內的 改造 即 總人類의 精神開闢을 實際로 實現途中에 獻身 確立하여야 實際의 目的을 達할 줄 압니다.
恒常 自己에게 要求함도 이 三章의 實踐이오 同侔에게 希望함도 이 三章의 實踐 一事올시다. 나는 敢히 開闢君을 依賴하야 이 三章을 말합니다.

모든 것을 朝鮮化케하라
工友主幹 崔宗煥

寂寞하던 山川에 猛虎聲 가티 우렁차게 부르짓는 各地 靑年團體에게 要求하는 것은 여러가지가 잇겟지요. 社會의 여러가지 風俗制度도 改良하며 敎育, 文藝, 實業 其他 모든 것을 다 奬勵 發展시키어 달라는 要求도 잇겟지요 마는 나는 이런 것을 要求하기 前에 먼저 要求할가 하는 것은 地方에 散在하여 잇는 여러 靑年團體들을 그의 精神, 그의 理想, 그의 基礎 모든 것을 朝鮮이라는 二字 우에 두며 세울 것이다. 어느 때 어느 일을 當하던지 우리는 朝鮮 靑年이며 此의 結社는 朝鮮의 團體라는 것을 忘却치 말며 設或 海外의 新思想, 新知識을 輸入하드래도 이것을 朝鮮化로 시키라. 그래서 여긔에 잇서 참으로 靑年團體인 그의 光彩가 發揮될 것이며 眞實로 그의 目的을 到達하리라는 것을 自信하고 이것을 들어 여러 地方 靑年團體에게 要求하는 바이외다.＜35＞

門에 나서거라. 急히 나오너라. 房속에 잇는 것은 君에게 恥辱이다.
急히 門을 나서거라. 君의 갈 길이 展開되엇다.
自己 自體의 價値와 그의 周圍를 認識함에 만일 錯誤함이 잇다하면 時代의 激浪은 君의 生命을 永遠히 埋葬하고 말 것이다.
時代에 逆行치 말아라. 그러타고 時代에 盲目的 順應도 말아라. 오즉 時代를 指導하는 大精神을 把持하고 大勢趨向의 新路를 開拓하는 것이 君의 할 일이다.
君아 靑年諸君아 朝鮮 靑年이다. 亞細亞 靑年이다. 世界 靑年이다.- 人類 生存 社會가 愛로써 創造되고 美로써 建設되는 것이 모다 君의 鐵拳에 잇슬 뿐이다. 壯하다 君의 偉性... 이것을 尺度할 아모 對物도 업다.
人類의 歷史- 掠奪의 歷史를 모조리 불살라 버려라. 永遠한 平和의 神聖한 歷史를 創造하는 것이 君의 大業이다. 이만한 前提를 가젓스면 아마 그 期待하는 바가 스스로 明確하야질 것이다.
靑年團體에 對하야 나는 別로히 要求할 것이 업다. 또 要求하랴고도 아니한다. 웨 업스랴. 旣定한 主義綱領을 實行하여라. 産業啓發에 努力하여라. 自治 自活의 精神을 涵養하여라. 一家를 保存하고 一村을 繁榮하게 하야써 集團生活의 安定을 確立하는 責任觀念을 堅持하여라. 하는 것이*****必要치 아니한 것이 없다마는. 나의 良心으로는 到底히 이와가튼 것을 希望할 수 업다. 自己들을 爲하는 自己의 意識으로써 그와가튼 必要項目을 實現함에 對하야 絶對로 아니가저서는 아니될 自由- 政治上 經濟上 또는 其他 方面의-를 가지지 못한 그들에게 如此한 것을 期待하는 것이 要求者 自己僞善을 廣告함에 그칠 뿐만 아니라 結局 어떠한 意味에서 더러운 義務觀念만을 鼓吹하게 되는 것이 그들의 人格을 侮辱하는 外에 아모 利益도 업슬 것이다. 또 그러한 內部的 事業을 實現함에 多少의 餘裕를 가젓다하더라도 團體 그것에 對하야 무엇을 希望하랴는 것보담 차라리 그 團體를 造成한 基礎要素인 靑年諸氏의 精神에 對하야 所望을 披露하는 것이 結局 그 團體에 對한 全般의 要求가 되는 줄로 생각한다.
金樓鳳閣의 美飾이 아모리 壯觀이라 할지라도 만일 그의 基礎가 沙上에 築成한 것이라하면 그 建物은 目前에 破壞를 避치 못할 決定的 運命을 질머진 것과 가티 만일＜36＞그의 靑年 團體가 靑年諸氏의 透徹한 理想과 確乎한 信念에서 決定되지 못하얏슬 것 가트면 그들에 對한 一般의 期待는 그만 絶望될 뿐아니라 團體力이 偉大함을 딸아서 期待치 아니하는 다른 方面으로 顯露되는 害毒이 또한 大多할 것이다.
人物이나 또는 制度를 不問하고 舊는 舊物의 利益을 擁護하기 爲하야 新生命을 默殺하며 新은 新物의 生命을 開拓하기 爲하야 舊物의 跋扈跳梁을 膺懲하랴는대서 新舊의 爭鬪가 開始된다. 다행히 新舊爭鬪의 倫理가 闡明된 오늘날이라 조금도 躊躇업시 新은 新物을 同志로 하고 舊物을 敵으로 하는 것이 오즉  新人의 取할 바이다. 그럼으로 어느 時代와 어떠한 境遇에서라도 新人인 靑年이 아니고는 新活路를 打開하고 新生活을 建設하는 것이 到底히 될 수 업는 것은 否定치 못할 事實이다. 그런즉 이와가튼 所見이 만일 正確하다 할것 가트면 모름지기 新人인 靑年은 그의 衝動이 表現되는 出發點에서부터 舊人階級 老少 또는 資本家를 勿論하고 新人의 모든 反對 物象과 一切의 交際를 斷切하리만큼 徹底한 覺悟가 업서서는 아니될 것이다. 그러나 어떠한 境遇에는 特殊階級을 除外한 新舊兩方이 或은 同伴者도 될 수 잇다. 그동안 또는 現在에도 어떠한 道程에서 握手 同伴하는것도 事實이지마는 이것을 常則으로 생각지 말고 적어도 어대까지던지 新人인 靑年이 主가 되지 아니하야서는 아니될 것이다.
그런데 新人인 靑年 諸氏 中에 아즉도 어떠한 境遇에는 그 陳腐한 情實에 걸려서 아지 못하는 동안에 自繩自縛에 陷하는 事實이 잇스니 그의 主因되는 바를 考察할 것 가트면 大槪 金力이나 社會的 地位와 가튼 有形無形의 社會的 實力이 貧弱함을 느낀 남아지에 自己에 對한 社會的 褒貶에 畏怯 退縮하야 結局 自己 本然性의 價値까지도 否認하는 까닭이다. 아모리 現實生活이 一部 强權者를 爲하야 組織되기 때문에 惡人輩의 壟斷에 맛겨버린 世上이라 하더라도 金錢을 求하랴고 自己人格을 賣하고 資本家에게 阿諂하거나 社會的 地位가 업다고 自己主義를 抛棄하고 舊物을 尾行하며서 老面을 假裝하는 것은 決코 暗黑에서 光明으로 나오는 靑年의 할 바가 아니다. 靑年의 輕浮妄動은 到底히 老人의 穩健沈着한 態度에 比치 못할 것이오 老人의 逡巡躊躇가 決코 靑年의 勇進强斷의 氣象에 及할 바가 아니니 이와가튼 一長一短의 特徵를 折衷融和할 것 가트면 事爲上 未賞不 利便할 수 잇겟스나 事實上 兩性의 利害衝突과 또는 社會的 心理上 微妙한 感情的 衝動이 到底히 相洽치 못하는 以上은 맛당히＜37＞ 新生命을 擁하기 爲하야 餘命享樂에 戀着하는 舊人의 態度를 排斥치 아니할 수 업다.
社會的 褒貶에 畏怯하야 靑年의 潑潑한 英氣가 退縮하는 것은 舊人階級의 勢力이 怪大한 것을 証明함은 勿論 社會的 良心에 根據치 못한 그의 褒貶은 一文의 價値도 업슬뿐 아니라 돌이어 靑年의 前途를 誤導하는 害毒이 莫大할 것이다. 資本家階級에서 만일 財物이라하는 것을 떼여노코보면 그들의 人格的 價値는 草人木像에서 얼마 超越치 못할 것과 가티 舊人階級이 享受하던 從來의 社會的 待遇―그의 存在의 價値―를 停止할 것 가트면 그들의 人格도 亦是 資本家와 五十步百步일 것이다. 그러니까 結局 그와가튼 空殼雲烟 가튼 偶像을 崇拜하거나 또는 虛僞無實한 褒貶에 妥協치 말고 어대까지던지 靑年同志가 아니면 絶對로 新生命을 開拓치 못하리라 하는 深切한 責任觀念을 堅持하고 이것을 阻害하는 모든 舊物을 一刀 兩斷할만한 勇敢을 發揮하는 것이 곳 오늘날 靑年의 아니가질 수 업는 靑年的 精神이라 할것이니 다행히 靑年諸氏가 이러한 精神에서 出發하야 事業基礎에 立할 것 가트면 나는 여긔에 滿足할 다름이다.

純然한 民衆의 團結이 되라
無我 兪鎭熙

人類의 世界가 어느 「유―르삐아」에 達하기까지는 人類의 努力은 永遠히 解放의 歷史가 아니면 아니될 것이다. 解放에서 解放으로 汪洋하게 長流하는 것이 人類史의 約束이니 不斷의 解放을 追進함이 不斷의 自由로 不斷의 生命으로 不斷의 創造로 向하여 努力하는 表現이다. 그러함으로 解放의 眞意를 抱負한 今日 以後의 歷史는 貴族이나 官僚의 歷史가 아니다. 純然한 民衆의 歷史이다. 딸아서 今日以後의 人類의 解放運動은 純然한 民衆의 團結的 動作이 아니면 毫末의 效果도 업슬 것이니 一切의 財閥의 跋扈에서 一切의 官閥의 威壓에서 一切의 所有와 傳統에서 一切의 家庭的 情調에서 一切의 鄕士적 愛着에서 斷然分離한― 아모쪼록 無産階級을 中心으로 한 ―民衆이 共同目的의 意識으로 社會的 乃至 國家的 新生을 圖하지 아니하면 아니될 것이다. 如斯한 意味로서 數條의 希望條件을 써보면.
1. 官設團體에서 超脫하라. 拘束主義인 干涉主義인 現朝鮮 政策은 靑年團體가튼 데도 用意가 周到할듯하다. 筆者의 推想뿐만 아니라 事實에서도 亦然하니 地方官廳의＜38＞ 役員이나 普通學校의 敎員가튼 이들이 團體의 指導機關이 되어가지고 指導的 干涉과 奬勵的 强制로서 그 動作이 모다 義務的 他動的에 不過한 듯하다. 如斯한 官設團體는 벌서 그 正體를 想像評價할 수가 잇스니 他日에 在한 效果의 有無는 莫論하고라도 爲先 潑剌한 靑年의 元氣를 去勢하는 點에서 時代思潮에 響應할 自覺을 阻止하는 點에서 自身을 自殺하는 消極道德을 鼓吹하는 點에서 如斯한 團體는 決코 存在를 容認할 수 업다. 그러함으로 一步를 進하여 現狀을 超脫해보던지 아무리하여도 超脫할 수 업거던 모다 스스로 瓦解하라 無益한 存在가 무서운 害毒의 源泉이 되는 까닭에.
2. 速히 大同團結을 圖하라. 人類生活의 約束은 協同이며 이 約束의 眞實한 履行은 世界的 協同에 잇스니 區區偏狹한 部落的 團結과 地方的 團結에서 다시 民族的 團結의 必要를 感하는 것이다. 이에서 理由를 呶呶할 것 업시 爲先 純然한 民衆意思의 表現機關으로 世界的 協同에 參加할만한 朝鮮靑年團의 實現을 圖하라.
3. 世界靑年團의 支部가 되라. 地方團體를 統合하야 朝鮮靑年團의 實現을 促함은 萬國靑年團과 同一한 步調를 取할 必要가 잇는 까닭이니 마치 今日의 이태리 靑年團과 波蘭靑年團과 獨逸靑年團들이 다만 이태리에서 波蘭에서 獨逸에서 團體的 行動을 取할뿐 아니라 實狀인즉 世界靑年團의 一支部로 이태리를 爲하야 民族國家를 要求하는 것이 靑年 이태리團이오 波蘭을 爲하야 民族國家를 要求하는 것이 靑年 波蘭團이요 獨逸을 爲하야 民族國家를 要求하는 것이 靑年獨逸團임과 가트니 民族的으로 團結한다함은 國際的 協同을 意味한 말이다.
마음대로 要求를 하자면 2,3에만 止치 안켓지마는 目下의 狀態가타서는 이 以上의 要求는 無理한 强請일 듯해서 남아지는 時局의 進展을 딸아 다시 써보기로 하고 爲先 이만.

이러한 精神을 가지시오
洪濟醫院長 劉洪鍾

各地 靑年團體에 對하야 나의 要求하는 바는 이러하다.
1. 犧牲的 精神. 個人이나 團體나 그 理想目的에 對하야는 犧牲的 精神이 업고는 그 理想을 實現하고 그 目的을 達하지 못하나니라.
2. 歸一的 精神. 此 精神은 協同團體生活의 要素가 되나니 우리 社會에는 더욱 必要하니 그 團體의 使命에 對하야는＜39＞ 私見과 感情을 犧牲케하고 合心一致하야 歸一的 精神을 가질것이오 無益한 黨爭을 避할지니라.
3. 勤儉的 精神. 浮虛外華를 戒하며 懶怠한 精神을 變하야 勤儉하게 할 것이니 此는 成功의 母가 되나니라.
4. 實力. 實力은 모든 事爲의 根本이 되나니 個人이면 各其 實力을 養成할 것이오 團體면 團體의 實力內容의 充實을 圖할 것이니 有名無實은 失敗의 根本이 되나니라.
5. 忍耐力. 忍耐力은 가장 必要하니 一事를 圖謀함에 一時的 動機로 그가온대 理想 目的 實力 忍耐力이 업시 朝에 起하야 夕에 棄하는 精神으로는 一事를 成키 難하고 또 功을 急한 대에서 求하지 말고 持久忍耐에서 求할지니라.
6. 奮鬪力. 우리는 奮鬪力이 적소. 事業을 成코저하면 百折不屈勇進退하는 奮鬪力이 업슬 수 업는 것이니 小敗를 보고 落心하며 一時의 事情으로 勇氣를 損케함은 丈夫의 事라 稱키 難하니 이 理想의 實現과 自己生命과 比較하야 生命을 그 理想實現에 對하야 犧牲에 供하겟다는 覺悟를 가지고 奮鬪치 아니하면 그 將來는 悲境이 올 것이오 運命의 門은 閉鎖될 것이니라. 世에 功을 成한 偉人들의 奮鬪力은 그 非常함을 볼수 잇다.
7. 衛生思想의 鼓吹. 此는 希望이니 元來 醫學은 文明의 先驅가 되나니 此는 文明各國으로 能히 證을 立하기 足하니 그中 衛生은 生命과 直接 關係를 가진 者라 반듯이 文明의 前驅가 되나니 이 衛生思想을 鼓吹하기를 努力할 것이니 이는 直接 民族運命에 關係잇슴으로써니라. 然則 各 團體에는 衛生部를 設하고 衛生思想의 普及 衛生狀態의 向上을 主眼으로 하야 努力하기를 懇切히 바라는 바요 今番 各地 處疫流行시 各地 靑年團體가 防疫에 큰功을 이루운 것은 朝鮮衛生界를 爲하야 慶賀하는 同時에 朝鮮衛生界에 新記錄을 준 것이니 더욱 이方面에 努力하기를 바라오.
8. 共益心. 共同生活의 要素가 되나니 利己心을 가진 同時에 利他心을 가지라 함이니 우리 社會에는 이 共益心이 缺乏하오. 그 證據는 各 都會라 하는 곳에 朝鮮人의 손으로 된 會館, 圖書館, 運動場, 病院가튼 것이 업슴을 보면 可히 알 수 잇다. 速히 共益心을 發揮하야 共益事業에 直接 有形無形 精神的 物質的으로 用力하기를 切望하오.
9. 猜忌心. 此는 心理作用의 一部라 업슬 수 업스니 朝鮮사람은 病的으로 猜忌心이 가장 强하니 此는 社會協同生活에 一大妨害가 되는 同時에 團體的 事業에 큰 盜賊이＜40＞ 되나니 勝己者―有하면 進하야 學할 것이오 그 사람의 實力과 가티 되기를 用力할 것이오 惡習인 猜忌心은 버릴 것이니라.
10. 依賴心. 獨立自尊은 人生의 本能이어늘 依賴心은 人生의 本能을 妨害하는 同時에 無能力者를 産케 하나니 此는 個人으로부터 團體에 이르기까지 依賴心이 업스면 存在할 것이오 依賴心이 잇스면 亡할 것이니라. 各其 獨立自尊心을 愛하고 依賴心을 버릴 것이니라.
以上은 希望이오 忠告라. 그 團體에 對하야 希望하는 同時에 各地에 中樞가 되는 團體가 自進하야 그 地方兄弟姉妹로 하여금 이 精神을 修養케함이 各 團體의 根本的 事業이오 基礎的 事業이 되리라하오. 敢히 一言으로 各地 靑年團體 諸位께 忠告하노라.

崔斗善

知識과 實力
高陽郡守 吳台煥

오래ㅅ동안 잠들어떤 우리 朝鮮天地에서도 무슨 動機로인지＜41＞ 各地 靑年이 눈을 번쩍 뜨고 소리를 웨치며서 벌(蜂)떼 가티 일어나는 靑年團體들이야 말로 自動被動을 勿論하고 이미 動하기는 動하엿스니 急進緩進은 勿論하고 進하여야 될 일다. 進하야 取할 目的物이 하나 둘이 아닌 以上에는 무엇을 先하며 무엇을 後할가 그 先하고 後할 것을 再三 생각하야 一毫의 差錯이 업시 順序的으로 着着 執行하여야 將來가 有望할 줄로 밋는 바이다.
今日 問題되는 宗敎·敎育·實業 等 모든 事業 어느 것이 諸君 靑年의 責任이 아니리오 마는 此를 實現함에는 相當한 時日을 要하겟는 故로 爲先 急한 事情으로 몃가지 條件을 要求하는 바이다.
一, 新聞과 雜誌를 購讀케 하라.
一日의 知識은 新聞에 잇다.
一月의 知識은 雜誌에 잇다.
우리 二千萬이 每日 新聞 上에서 어든 知識·每月 雜誌 上에서 어든 知識이 남과 갓게 하라.
一. 通俗講演을 勵行하라.
社會敎育의 機關이 完備치 못한 今日에 無識階級·有識階級을 網羅하야 直接感化시기는 方法은 오즉 講演이다. 講演隊를 組織하야 方方谷谷에 巡廻講演하야
우리 二千萬이 講演에서 어든 知識이 남과 갓게 하라.
一. 勤儉貯蓄의 美風을 奬勵하라.
무슨 職業에던지 誠實히 勉勵하야 그의 所得으로 될 수 잇는대로 節用하고 餘裕 잇는대로 貯蓄하야 우리 二千萬이 貯蓄에서 어든 實力이 남과 갓게 하라.
一. 經濟機關을 設하라.
舊式의 婚喪契, 學契, 農契 等 가튼 것도 相當히 改良하고 새로이 産業組合, 購買組合, 消費組合 가튼 것을 各 府郡面里에 設立하야 一般 生活 上에 아모쪼록 經濟를 取하야
우리 二千萬이 經濟 上에서 어든 實力이 남과 갓게 하라.
以上의 나의 要求는 別 意見 업고 남이 다 하는 普通의 말이다마는 나의 생각에는 우리의 知識과 實力이 남과 가튼 然後에야사 世界問題로 떠드는 平等自由라는 그 통에 한목을 차지하겟고 爲政者의 標榜하는 同等待遇라는 그 말이 헛말이 아니될 줄로 생각한 結果에 이러한 平凡한 條件으로 要求하는 바이나 各地 靑年團體 當局者의 생각이 어떠할는지＜42＞

切實한 覺悟 下에서 完全한 團軆되기를 要함
吳祥根

이미 朝鮮 各地의 여러 貌樣의 各 靑年團體가 雨後竹筍과 如히 興起組織되엇도다. 人類로서는 天性과 必要로 社會的 生活을 안일 수 업는 者인 中 우리 靑年은 더욱 吾人 一生 中 全盛時期의 人物이며 事業初頭에 立하야 前途의 希望이 最富한 身分이다. 白山의 巍巍를 仰할 時는 我의 氣槪를-碧海의 洋洋을 觀할 時는 我의 理想을-이리하야 方强方昌의 힘을 가지고써 文化向上에 努力하는 靑年이 組織한 靑年社會의 中軸인 團體가 卽 우리 靑年團體이다.
然한데 現今 朝鮮 各地의 興起한 靑年團體는 百泉의 新湧함과 如히 旺盛하며 萬花의 方發함과 如히 爛漫하다. 이는 時運을 딸아 世界文化에 共浴코자 無論德智體의 發達을 助하며 社會團結로 端緖를 作하야써 事業振擧을 占하랴는 自覺 下에서 이가튼 隆昌을 이루은 것일 것이다.
이리 思하야 이러한 團體가 蔚興함을 深祝하는 同時에 自覺의 上에 一大 自覺을 加하야 各地에 散在한 各 團體가 一致團結하야 一大 聯合團體를 成立함이 時下의 先務요 是果要望의 하나이라 한다. 一曰 社會生活의 最高 手段은 不團結를 合하야 團結을 作하며 小團結을 合하야 大團結을 作함에 在하니 是는 優勝을 得하는 所以요 二曰 各 團體는 반듯이 共通性質을 가저서 同一한 精神·主義 下에서 一致한 步調로 共通動作을 行함이 必要하고 三曰 社會 存立 發達 上에 必要로 서로 聯合하야 相助相補함이 可함이다. 이가티 하여야 靑年團體의 功業을 完成할 것이라 한다.
이러한 見地 下에서 靑年團體 聯合期成의 會도 生하고 趣旨도 世에 發表됨이 已久하여 今에는 靑年會聯合會가 將次 成立됨에 우리 要望의 하나는 幾分間 이루어진다. 可謂키도 하겟도다.
그러나 果然 저 各 靑年團體가 참말 모다 切實한 覺悟 下에서 組織되엇스며 참말 모다 完全한 團體인가 하는 一念이 更生하는 同時에 且尤 一望이 有하니 卽 우리는 此時代와 我靑年에 關한 切實한 覺悟이다.
이미 만흔 歲月을 經過하여 이루어진 이 時代의 文化·남들은 電機時代·自由時代·그리되어서 文明의 域에 處하야 人生에게 附與된 온갓 幸福·온갓의 美를 가추가추 가젓거늘 우리는 아즉도 普通時代 卽 通譯時代에도 못미쳐서 人生의 取할 것도 아즉것 盡取치 못하고 人生의＜43＞ 得할 것도 아즉 것 盡得치 못하엿다. 하나 取코자하면 取하고 得코저하면 得하는 것이다. 切實이 이를 覺悟하고 이에 心을 費하며 力을 用하야 進進不息하면 取·得치 못할 것이 無함은 天理其然한 것이다. 故로 우리가 文化運動을 唯一한 目的으로 思하고 今에 靑年團體를 組織함이니 是는 吾人 固有文化의 發揮로나 世界文化의 輸入으로나 知識啓發로나 어느 것으로던지 文化發展上 唯一의 生命機關이다. 生命機關되는 若是히 重且大한 處地로서 徹底하고 切實한 覺悟가 업시 風前細柳로 바람을 조차 이리저리 動搖되거나 附和雷動되어 남이 하니 나도 한다하여 名義만 擧케 되면 是豈我靑年의 忍爲할 바리오.
何况靑年은 社會의 花이다. 花가 無한 實이 無함과 如히 靑年이 無한 社會는 事業上 其實이 無하다. 無花果나 花石檑가 有함 가티 靑年이 빠지고도 或시 社會가 成立될른지! 靑年이 有코서 成立되지 못하는 수가 잇슬는지! 이는 例外라 言키 難할 듯 하나 靑年과 社會라 하면 決코 其然치 안타 하겟다. 人生社會는 靑年이 아니고는 成立할 수 업다.
이에 人生의 最大目的 最大事業은 우리 靑年의 雙肩에 專在하니라 若此한 身分으로 成立한 團體가 어찌 徹底하고 切實한 覺悟가 無하랴. 만일 切實한 覺悟 下에서 組織되지 안한 團體는 完全한 團體라 稱키 難할 것이다.
毋論 現今 各地 靑年團體는 完全한 團體인 줄로 思한다마는 余의 懇切이 願함은 各 團體가 更加熟考深思하야 線을 脫치 말고 定한 軓道로 卽 文化를 運動하거든 文化運動에 屬한 바 그 自體의 定한 目的의 事爲만 專力을 用하고 以外의 線을 蹈치 말아서 그 團體의 壽命이 長久토록 그 事爲上에 務實力行하는 것이라 하노라.＜44＞
