離鄕 (Leaving Home)
						金石松 (Kim Sŏksong)

一
Whenever, every time
Each time I leave
As if grasping my ankles
Begging me not to go
Oknyŏ, Ch'aeun! Oh! My affections

No matter how you spread your arms
“Don’t go! Live with me!”
Screaming until your throat splits
Seeing me off as far as the Nonsan corner
Ah! Heartless me, I always ended up leaving.

二
But today, for some reason
You are many times more than before
In a white-hot frenzy
And even I, who was such a cold-blooded child
Cannot bear... cannot bear it.

Half an hour before the train arrives
In my heart, which you know is nothing but bones
A force of anguish whose name I don't even know
When it dulled my spirit to the extreme
Ah! A frightening tragedy unfolded again.

三
When I stepped out of our house's main gate
Seeing me off to the edge of the barley field
Grandparents, parents... sister and younger brother too
“Go well... Go safely”
I hear their calls.

Rather than being grateful for their kindness
I hated their cold-heartedness instead.
Those whom I love the most
Alone in the empty house, the empty room
Making her feel and weep, that inhumanity

四
The already worn-out clothes of "Etiquette" (禮儀)
To me, the protagonist of new emotions
No matter how hard they try to dress me in them <114>
I, who grind my teeth (齒) at you
Am thrown into the fiercely burning fire of curse...

"Etiquette" (禮儀) that ignores human feeling (人情)!
"Logic" (論理) like patching a broken pot!
Destroying sacred humanity (人間性)
Sowing the seeds of ruin, "Old Morality" (舊道德)!
Ah! I truly curse them.

五
The train (列車), sometime soon
Leaving behind the familiar Kanggyŏng Plain (江景平野)
Cries out, "We've arrived at Nonsan!"
Those who arrived get off
Those who will depart get on.

My companions number fifty or sixty.
Southerners (南人), Northerners (北人), our people, other people...
Many women who look alike also boarded.
But my companion is nowhere to be seen.
Though the handbag I carried is here

六
Oh! Lover (愛人)! My companion!
Where did you go? Leaving me all alone
In the bitter storm of restraint (拘束)
In the pit of bone-melting temperance (節制)
In the endlessly lonely desert (沙漠)

Oh—I understand now.
In the terrifying prison (獄) called "marital home" (시집)
Becoming a prisoner (囚人) called "daughter-in-law" (며느리)
Losing all freedom (自由)
That you are serving your sentence (服役).

七
Ah! My loving wife!
I have already burned the old clothes of "Etiquette" (禮儀)
In the flames (火焰) of curse
And now again (though it may be pitiful)
I curse you—the daughter-in-law.

But you—my wife
In the old house (舊家) called timeworn "Morality" (道德)
Have sworn a righteous arson (放火)
Already a long time ago
Just like Noah's Flood (洪水) <115>

八
Humans (人生) are creative (創造的) animals (動物).
The complete expression (表現) of individuality (個性)
The full development (發達) of emotion (感情)
Herein lies creation.
Only then does the New World (新世界) unfold (展開).

Even "love," greater than anything else
We cannot do as we please
Cannot even get sleep and food
Life (人生) that has received the declaration of character bankruptcy
All that will rise will lift the torch of revolution (革命)

九
Piercing the pitch-black night
The train (火車) fleeing like skin
Just now crossing the Han River Railway Bridge (漢江鐵橋)
Informed me, half-asleep from fatigue (疲困).
With the loud clatter of the wheels

When something sharp like a needle point called "Seoul!"
Seemed to break my heart
In my head, multifaceted phantoms (幻影)
Like a revolving lantern (走馬燈)... spin... spin...
Until the welcome train whistle (汽笛) is heard

十
Namdaemun! Namdaemun!
Getting off the train with them, I
In the chilly wind that brushes past with a *swish*!
Come sharply to my new senses, cross the bridge
And move my feet toward the exit gate.
Inn lanterns (旅館提燈) form a dazzling procession

But even Seoul, so longed for
Though it embraces a population (人口) of 300,000
Why does it seem utterly empty!
Ah! Half a day lived without love!
What greater emptiness (空虛) could there be than this half-day!

Sixty-one, eleven, eleven, Written by Kim Sŏksong—
This is a record of my most recent (最近) feelings just as they are. I don't know if it's a poem, a song, or what, but the demand for the manuscript (原稿督促) was so urgent (急) that I couldn't get time (時間) for revision (推敲), which the author (作者)! considers extremely (甚) regrettable (遺憾). Also, "Oknyŏ, Ch'aeun" (玉女, 彩雲) in the fifth line of the first stanza are the names of mountains (山名) in my hometown (故鄕). <116>

**Summary**

The poem "離鄕" (Leaving Home) by Kim Sŏksong expresses the intense emotional turmoil of a man departing from his hometown and lover. He feels torn between his affection and the necessity of leaving, while fiercely criticizing the traditional social constraints, represented by "Etiquette" (禮儀) and "Old Morality" (舊道德), that oppress individual feelings and particularly confine his wife within the role of a "daughter-in-law" (며느리) in her "marital home" (시집), depicted as a prison. The narrator yearns for a "New World" built on individual expression, emotional freedom, and love, suggesting revolution against the old order. For historians, the poem is significant as a raw expression of modernist angst and rebellion against Confucian social norms in early colonial Korea. It highlights the conflict between traditional obligations and emerging desires for personal freedom and romantic love, and offers a critique of the oppressive aspects of the traditional family system, especially for women, hinting at early feminist consciousness.

白熱的의 發狂을 하고

「잘가거라... 安寧히 가시오」라고

오히려 그들의 薄情을 미워하얏다.
나의 가장 사랑하는 그로서
뷘집 뷘방안에 외로이

六

가업시 孤獨한 沙漠에
