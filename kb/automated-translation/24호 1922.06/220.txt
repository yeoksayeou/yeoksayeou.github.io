잡지명	개벽 제24호
발행일	1922년 06월 01일
기사제목	K兄에게 (To Brother K)
필자	一集生
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Essay)

K兄에게
						一集生
第一信
Brother K! In the flower-smiling mornings and moonlit evenings, embraced within the serene bosom of great Nature under the blue sky, singing of life and praising the natural world, or perhaps shouting by the flowing green water, "Thus it flows on, never ceasing!" — how much savor is there in this? Having turned your back on the hazy yellow dust and happily sought the realm of leisure, I imagine you must be full of darkness and pleasure, and I congratulate you on your happiness while, on the other hand, cannot help but quietly envy you!

Thinking back, it has already become a thing of the past. On the slopes of Mokmyŏk Mountain (木覓山), looking out at the blue waves of the Han River (漢水), breaking willow branches on Ch'ŏngnyang street (淸凉街頭)! In spring, wearing pads at the Hullyŏnwŏn (訓鍊院 training ground), and in winter, playing with skates on the ice! Was that the time of joy? Was that the springtime of happiness?
We dreamt of Napoleon's (나폴레온) great deeds, and sought to model ourselves on Su Dongpo's (蘇東坡) writing. But it wasn't that we set our minds on this under some doctrine or with a fixed view, was it? It was simply because they were objects of admiration in the world, that's why we did it.
But those were good times, weren't they? Why? Because back then, whether you call it an ideal or a delusion, there was something, some hope, that we wanted to achieve. And we didn't know how difficult the realization of that ideal was, nor did we know what the world was like; we only burned with the immediate cheerfulness and boiling hope.
But now, having been deceived once, having failed once, I have realized that what I thought before and the so-called real society are vastly different. And I have learned that success, the harvest, is not easily achieved through effort alone. Of course, how could I thoroughly understand what this world is like? That's why I agonize, why I worry. If I thoroughly understood and correctly interpreted this world, even if the contradictions of the present age reached their extreme, I wouldn't agonize, would I? I am confident only that this world is not suffering. However, what meets the eye <31> is only suffering, and what reaches the ear is only the resentful voice of discontent. Though I try not to sigh, sighs escape naturally, and though I try to laugh, tears flow – what can be done?

Brother K! When you abandoned the city, turned your back on so-called active society, and went into the mountains, I laughed and mourned for you. Having guessed your usual ideals, I wondered why you would take such action. But having faced it myself, I realized it was likely so, and I cannot help but sympathize. I have learned that the path for us to return to is none other than Nature, and the place for us to find our joy is also none other than Nature.

Brother K! Someone sang of his ideals and spirit, saying, "If a man at twenty has not brought peace to the nation, who in later generations will call him a great man?" Such a person might seek satisfaction that way, find happiness that way. Others may try to obtain money, and others status, each seeking their own satisfaction and happiness, trying to live as if alive.
Then how shall we realize our ideals? And what are those ideals? The words seem to stray, but human happiness likely lies in the subjective, doesn't it? The millionaire with vast sums is not the only happy one; the noble of ten thousand chariots is not the only human one. Even if one goes out gazing at the stars in the morning and returns home bathed in moonlight in the evening, limbs exhausted and ragged clothes barely covering the body, one can surely seek happiness within that, and I know one can think of it as happiness! The joy of life lies therein, and the essence of being human is likely found there!

Brother K! The essence of human life – I know it to be mysterious. There might be nothing that cannot be explained through reasoning. But I think there is no need to try. We know the brilliant sun is bright, and we are moved by its radiance. But if asked why it is so bright, science would surely explain it. However, that explanation is already within the sphere of influence of Nature. Is it not obvious that human life cannot exist beyond the sphere of influence of Nature? Therefore, without needing to borrow fussy words to explain the essence of human life, just as someone who knows nothing of science or anything else sees the bright rays of the sun and rejoices without agonizing to know its principles, if we are satisfied with just the human-like quality of human life, without needing to know its essence, we would be satisfied. <32>
Brother K! How shall we make life human-like? How shall we find satisfaction? A life of subjective unhappiness, which is not satisfaction, I know to be a life of falsehood.
One who wanders in a life of falsehood is called either shallow or foolish! Seen in this light, I do not consider suicide a sin. If one recognizes one's own life as false and one's situation as truly adverse, one should not continue living. A meaningful death is happier than a meaningless life. No, it is truth. Then shall we die? Acknowledging a life of falsehood and committing suicide is not a sin, but the act of suicide itself might be a sin. Therefore, I think we must make our lives such that they are not false, that they are satisfying, that they are happy. From here, the path forward for life emerges, and I know that everything begins!

Brother K! However, in our surroundings, no matter what we do, we cannot feel happiness! Not only that, but we keenly feel the sense of unhappiness, so are we destined to wander in falsehood and perish? Right now, it is falsehood! It is unhappiness! But the past was happiness. As I mentioned at the beginning, our former days were filled only with hope, and our hearts were cheerful! At that time, the happiness of that time was not due to the reality of that time, but rather due to anticipating the present or the future. However, once the winds of the world hit us, all irrationalities came into view, and all injustices were rampant, we felt discouragement and the sorrow of unhappiness. Therefore, just as the happiness of former days did not stem from the reality of that time, shall we try to live recognizing that today's happiness is not due to today's reality?
Otherwise, in the desolate fields, the day darkens, the moon is hidden by dark clouds, foul winds and ghostly sounds clamor from all directions, and fear and sorrow pile up – how could we sustain our fate even for a moment?

Brother K! Then, perhaps we can find some comfort in that way. That is, by anticipating what "will be" in the future, we can find happiness! However, just as the anticipation of the past realized the present, what will the realization of today's ideal be like?
Will people always live being deceived?!
As I mentioned earlier, what, generally, will our ideal be?! In former days, great heroes, great figures were the ideal, and trying to realize that ideal was happiness! <33>

Brother K! Regardless, while we were striving to realize that ideal, our hearts were joyful, and we also engaged in much of our best activity. Thus, much was built, and much was destroyed. And so, the past created the splendid pages of the past and was also not in vain. Thus, the past passed by as the past. That alone is happiness. I wonder if that is the essence and purpose of life. The purpose of life is not achieved by becoming an immortal hermit, nor is satisfaction found by obtaining something specific; rather, seeking and obtaining day by day, month by month, is the true essence! It is precious!
Be that as it Bmay, the ideal of today has changed. Today, simple targets like heroes or great men cannot satisfy our ideal! Should this be called progress?!
In any case, that it has changed is a fact, it is natural, and all the impurities of our current situation fail to move us at all. It was the utopia of the future, and what must be done to build this, I intend to write successively.
My rambling words jump back and forth and seem to become too lengthy. However, these are words squeezed from a troubled mind, mixed with some unnatural effort, but if time permits, I hope you will read them to the end.

第二信
Brother K! Generally, our unhappiness stems from keenly feeling discontent towards all the irrationalities of the modern age. What is called modern civilization increasingly forms evil as it progresses! Claiming to benefit humanity, is there anything that actually harms humanity as much as modern civilization? Try comparing the so-called dark ages when warships, torpedo boats, airplanes, telegraphs, cannons, gas – none of these existed – with the civilized present day! Don't you automatically get goosebumps?
The ancients called it a floating world (浮世). They called it a sea of suffering (苦海). However, this sea of suffering, this floating world, is definitely not the true nature of the world, nor is it an expression of truth. It is merely something formed by all people through evil deeds, and it is also just a name given by people or perhaps nothing more than the bitter words of a few! In other words, shall we call it tying oneself with one's own rope (自繩自縛)?! Having created it all themselves, they only resent heaven and curse the life of utmost goodness and beauty. Ah! Isn't the exposure of extreme foolishness too severe? People are increasingly driven into the pit of suffering like this. This is the civilization praised in the modern age! <34>

Brother K! Looking at those called cheerful fellows or heroic men in the world, they seem to dismiss worldly affairs with a laugh, viewing life as a momentary play. Thus, I wonder if many waste their lives drunk and dreaming. If they think that way and seek solace for themselves, it might be beneficial solace for them personally. However, it is like burning jade and stone together; I think it insults solemn life, noble life. Ah! How could mere comfort be the ideal and happiness of life? It may sound extreme, but for a body that in about 70 years will become white bones turned to dust, a mound of earth amidst the green mountains and clouds, if it is comfortable, how much comfort is that, and what meaning does it have? It is because we are alive, because it is life, that we know the body is precious, truly precious. Yet, for the sake of comforting such a body, people insult life and take actions that are insulting! They commit sins! If not for the flesh, there would be no theft, no war! Ah! Pitiful life! How long can it be sustained?! Harming that flesh for the sake of this flesh and openly boasting about it is called the best thing in the modern age.
Originally, unless the fundamental problems of this world are resolved, we cannot see the true dawn. Is it only this world? Wouldn't it be the same for any place, any time, any matter? Yet, everyone in the modern age seems not to think about such things at all! They just label life as dew on the grass or a spring dream, and let's say they find solace by looking down on life like that. Solace might be temporary, but the thing itself remains as it is, and the sins of humanity continue to exist, letting loose a terrible toxic flow upon all living beings. How much more tragic a fate will that poison inflict upon a life that is like dew on the grass? It merely insults life and brings no gain; rather, it will produce the result of regression! It's like seeing a sick person suffering here and, instead of thinking of removing the root cause of the illness to revive them, simply labeling them as sick, isolating them somewhere, and not looking after them. One might feel at ease by not witnessing the patient's agony immediately! But wouldn't that patient forever fail to taste the glory of recovery, leading to a tragic fate?!
Similarly, in the modern age, taking measures to seek solace through names alone, without thinking of reforming it, is truly advancing oneself into the pit of destruction. Moreover, for people like us to be pessimistic and disheartened is even more likely to invite our own negation and inflict terrible harm on the entire society. Therefore, I rise up resolutely, striving and agonizing to attain a firm awakening!

Brother K! It feels like I keep repeating myself, but then, how shall we reform everything in the modern age to build the <35> ideal village of the future? And thus, make the present and the future together happy, and bestow glory upon countless generations of descendants?!
Look! The world is all falsehood! All sin! Needless to say, there is almost nothing in the modern age that should not be destroyed! It may sound too bold, but isn't it the reality?
Everything modern is the crystallization of humanity's sins, and the majority of the past is a catalog of human tragedies!
Humans live by goodness and wisdom! Becoming progressively wiser is, of course, human evolution. However, if progress is made only in one aspect while another aspect regresses, then humanity is truly finished. Ah! Who dislikes becoming wiser! Who dislikes living luxuriously! But trying to attain personal wealth and status with the current way of thinking is not in the slightest different from a morphine addict (모루 히네中毒者) secretly killing themselves little by little for momentary pleasure!
Modern man is a cripple (病身) whose one leg has grown a bit longer, but the other leg is getting progressively shorter. That's why they constantly, no, always, stumble and fall, producing many sins. Yet, they still pride themselves on this and walk around. Ah! Isn't it truly pitiful?! Our essential need for reform lies here, the mission of destruction is urgent here, and the urgency of construction also lies here.

Brother K! How many days will a rootless tree sustain itself? How long will a spring whose source has dried up flow? Now the roots are about to rot! The water source is about to dry up! Ah! Our ancestors must have tried to prepare for us to live somehow! And modern people look back at antiquity and boast infinitely of modern civilization!
When we see all the modern things busily constructing the source of that tragic and miserable deluge, my own grief is momentarily forgotten. However, when I think that my grief arises from all those things, and that my present is unnatural because of all those things, I cannot again hold back the torrent of resentful tears!

Brother K! What are the horrific consequences of the struggle for existence truly like? There is likely nothing that obstructs endeavors and poisons humanity like competition. Some say progress arises from competition. Is that really so? That must be a statement based on past barbarism! How could true humans live by competition? <36> At the end of competition, evil principles like jealousy and exclusion inevitably arise. The result, for the individual, is the ruin of the individual; for the nation, the ruin of the nation; and ultimately, it attempts to encroach upon all humanity. Ah! Brother! Let us lose to others rather than defeat others. The 'victory of the defeated' gives us a terrifying lesson, not insignificantly! Losing to others definitely does not mean our regression!
When is the spring season when the wind is calm, the waves are still, a hundred flowers are in full bloom, and the gentle spring breeze brushes the collar? Where is the paradise of peace, where the warm energy fills the sky and smiles overflow on every face? A sword hidden within a smile (笑中에 懷劒) is the sole weapon of the victors in this world, and outward goodness with inner evil (表善裏惡) is the foremost strategy for navigating this world!

Brother K! When will this world ever be calm? When will we be able to live as if truly living? If never, wouldn't it be better to leave the realm of life early and head towards the lonely netherworld? Death transcends irrational life; doesn't it express a part of truth? Life is precious; otherwise, what is precious? What is savory? What is regrettable? Therefore, we do not live a meaningless life. We choose a meaningful death! Saying this about life and death again seems like repeating what I said earlier. Please forgive me!

Brother K! How shall we destroy all things modern? And how shall we build? How shall we improve fundamentally from the foundation up? We must be free. But that alone... We must also have money, but that alone too... We must also have knowledge, but that alone either... As I've been saying from the start, we require something fundamental that encompasses all these things. We must be good, we must be strong, but these too point to only one branch, one part among them. How can that requirement be fully satisfied? Think soundly, resolve firmly, and rise up. The hope for the recovery of the ailing human world is brilliant. It is merely covered by clouds. As I pleaded at the very beginning, by lying quietly in the deep shade of trees and entrusting all thoughts to the flowing water and the empty sky, I think we will find no gain! Is this neither life nor death, but the truth of the great universe! A derailed, deformed life?!
May everyone discover true life and taste genuine life by possessing all virtues and exerting full potential – I hope and pray for this. <37>

Brother K! The modern age is just a collection of contradictions and regressions! Even so, we cannot transcend the modern age, separate from it, and seek happiness! Isn't the modern age also not *not* a part of the expression of truth? It's just that it's not the right way, and the path has gone awry! Therefore, without necessarily immersing oneself in its vortex, how can one seek the satisfaction of the ideal?

Brother K! Whether a saint or a thief, whether someone who harms others to enrich themselves or someone who harms themselves for the sake of others, isn't it all for the sake of living? Isn't the aim to realize the purpose of life the same for all? Ah! How can people heading towards one goal have such different hearts, different paths, and ultimately even obtain different things? Rather than hating them, we should truly pity them! Indeed, a play enacted on a snail's horn (蝸牛角上)! Should we call it laughable? What should we call it?! With our limited words, with the words of the modern age, we cannot depict the true state of affairs.

Brother K! I apologize for troubling your clear sight too much with these incoherent and disjointed words. There will be a time later when I gather my thoughts again and marshal my ideas to speak further! Finally, I pray for your ever-increasing well-being and bless the future of our society as I put down my pen... (1921, 1, 9)... <38>
<31-38>

**Summary**

The author, writing to "Brother K" who has retreated to nature, expresses deep disillusionment with modern society, which he sees as full of falsehood, irrationality, suffering, and sin, despite its claims of civilization and progress. He contrasts this with a nostalgic view of past youthful idealism and finds solace only in nature or the possibility of future transformation. The author critiques competition, superficiality, and the pursuit of wealth and status, viewing modern life as inherently contradictory and harmful. He wrestles with existential questions of life, death, happiness, and the possibility of meaningful existence, suggesting that true life requires fundamental change and destruction of the current corrupt system, even contemplating suicide as a potentially more truthful act than living a false life, though ultimately calling for striving towards a genuine, fulfilling existence.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is highly revealing. Written in 1922, it reflects the profound angst and alienation experienced by Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule. The critique of "modern civilization" can be interpreted as a veiled critique of colonial modernity, which brought superficial changes but also immense suffering, exploitation (implied by critique of competition and focus on flesh/materialism), and a sense of lost authenticity. The yearning for nature and a "true life," coupled with the call for destruction and rebuilding, resonates with the nationalist and socialist ideas circulating at the time, expressing a deep desire for liberation from the current "false" reality, both spiritually and politically. Historians might find the blend of existential philosophy, social critique, and implicit nationalism particularly interesting as a snapshot of intellectual ferment during a period of intense oppression and searching for new paths.

K兄에게
						一集生
第一信
K兄! 꼿웃는 아츰 달밝은 저녁에 푸른 大空알에 그윽한 大自然의 품속에 안기어서, 生을 노래하고 天然을 讚美하며, 或은 흐르는 綠水가에 逝者如斯矣를 大呌하는 滋味 얼마나 되십니까. 濛濛한 黃塵을 등지고 快然히 閑界를 차저가신 형은 應當 어듬과 쾌함이 만흐실 줄 생각하고, 兄의 幸福을 祝賀하며 한편으로 가만히 부러워함을 마지안나이다!
생각하니 벌서 녯일로 化하고 말앗나이다. 木覓山언덕에 漢水의 滄波를 바라보며 淸凉街頭에 버들가지를 찍을때! 봄에는 訓鍊院에 빼트를 두루고 겨을에는 어름우세 스케트를 戱弄하든 때! 그 때가 快樂의 時期이엇든지요? 幸福의 봄철이엇든지요?
나폴레온의 偉擧도 夢想하야 브앗고 蘇東坡의 文章도 模範하랴 하얏나이다. 그러나 그것이 무슨 主義알에 一定한 主見에서 마음둔 것은 아니지요, 다만 덥허노코 그네들은 世上에서 欽仰의 관역이 된 것이니까 그랫든 것이지요.
그러나 그 때가 조키는 조핫든 때이지요. 왜 그러냐하면 그 때는 理想이라고 할지? 妄想이라고 할지? 어찌하얏든지 이러케하겟다는 무엇무엇 希望하는 것이 잇섯스니까요. 그리하고 그러케 그 理想의 實現이 어려운 줄도 몰랏섯스며 兼하야 世上이라는 것이 어떠한 것인지도 모르고 다만 當面의 快活과 끌른듯한 希望의 불만 탓섯스니까요.
그러나 只今은 한번 속앗는 故로, 失敗하얏는 故로, 前에 생각하는 바와 所謂 實社會라는 것과는 判異한 것인줄을 깨달앗나이다. 그러하고 努力만 가지고도 成功이라는 收穫이 容易치 안흔 것을 알앗나이다. 勿論 이 世上이 어떠한 것인줄을 어찌 透徹히야 알리까? 그러니까 煩悶하지요 苦心하지요. 徹底히 이 世上을 理解하고 正解하면 비록 現代의 矛盾이 極하기로 煩悶은 아니하겟지요. 다만 이 世上이 苦가 아닌 줄 自信합니다. 그러나 눈에 보이는＜31＞ 것이 苦뿐이요 귀에 들리는 것이 不平의 怨聲뿐입니다그려. 歎息을 아니하려하나 自然히 한숨이 나오고 웃으려하나 눈물이 흐르는 것을 어찌하겟습니까.
