Article Title: 오는 봄 (Coming Spring)

Coming Spring
						Sowŏl

Thinking that spring days will come,
Alone, I shall endure the long winter.
Today, I see on the white poplar's budding branch,
A white bird, unlike before, sits and cries.

Below the bank where snow has yet to melt,
Is it shadow, is it fog, is it haze?
The villages everywhere are motionless<39>
Peaceful beneath that distant sky.

On the willow with long, drooping branches,
A crow cries, looking out at the sea.
The sound of bells, coming from somewhere,
Is the tune of a young child going out.

Behold, the traveler of the season hesitates,
Without direction, knowing not where to set foot.
Be sorrowful, be endless, fall incessantly,
Your tears that flow without cease.

Each person's deep sorrow of loneliness
Becomes a hesitation, unable to come or go.
Ah, this unbearable parting, having lost my beloved,
It is a sorrow unable to find its place.

The news of spring, awaited to come,
Though it may sometimes make weary fingertips tremble,
The strands of hair hidden beneath the forest
Do not fall, but tangle around my feet.<40>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl expresses a deep sense of longing and melancholy while anticipating the arrival of spring. The speaker observes subtle signs of the changing season – a bird on a branch, melting snow, distant village sounds – but these are tinged with personal sorrow, loneliness, and the pain of separation ("이 離別"). The poem contrasts the external signs of approaching spring with the internal state of unresolved grief and hesitant waiting, where even the hope of spring brings a tremor rather than pure joy, and burdens (like tangled hair) remain. For historians studying the Korean colonial period, the poem, written in 1922, can be interpreted as reflecting the broader national sentiment. The "long winter" could symbolize the period of oppression, "spring" the hoped-for liberation or better future, and the pervasive sorrow and hesitation the difficult reality and uncertain path forward for Koreans under Japanese rule. The sense of stasis and unresolved grief speaks to the emotional landscape of the time.

오는 봄
						素月
봄날이 오리라고 생각하면서
나 혼자 긴 겨을을 지내보내라.
오늘 보니 白楊의 버든가지에
전에 업시 흰새가 안저 울어라.

눈이 아즉 안녹은 두던미테는
그늘이랴, 안개랴, 아즈랑이야,
마을들은 곳곳이 움직임업시＜39＞
저 먼한울알에서 平和로워라.

가지길게 늘어진 버드나무엔
바다를 바라보며 우는 가마귀,
어대로서 오는지 방울소리는
젊은 애기 나가는 曲調일러라.

보라, 때에 길손은 머뭇거리며
지향업시 갈 발의 곳을 몰라라.
설어라, 끗 업서라, 사뭇나려라,
낫븜업시 흐르는 그대의 눈물.

저마다 외로움의 깁흔 근심이
오도가도 못하는 망사림 되어,
아아 못할 이 離別, 님 올여이고
곳을 잡지 못하는 설음일러라.

오기를 기다리는 봄의 消息은
때로 여윈 손끄를 울릴지라도
수풀미테 숨겻는 머리낄들은
떨어지지도안코 발에 감겨라.＜40＞
＜39-40＞
