기사제목	公園의 밤 (Night in the Park)
필자	素月
기사형태	시 (Poem)

公園의 밤
						素月
白楊가지에 우는 電燈은 깁흔밤의 못물에
어렷하기도 하며 어득하기도 하여라,
어둡게 또는 소리업시 가늘게
줄줄의 버드나무에서는 비가 싸힐 때.

푸른 그늘은 나즌듯이 보이는 긴 닙알에로
마주 안저 고요히 내려깔리던 그 보들어운 눈껼!
인제, 검은내는 떠돌아올라 비구름이 되여라
아아 나는 우노라 『그 녯적의 내 사람!』＜39＞

Night in the Park
						Sowŏl
The electric lamp crying on the white poplar branch, in the pond water of deep night,
sometimes appears clear, sometimes dim,
darkly or soundlessly, finely
when rain gathers on the weeping willows.

The blue shade, onto the long leaves that seem low,
facing each other, seated, that soft gaze that calmly settled down!
Now, let the dark mist drift up and become rain clouds
Ah, I weep, "My person of that old time!"<39>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl depicts a somber, melancholic night scene in a park. The speaker observes an electric lamp reflecting dimly in a pond, weeping willows in the rain, and recalls a past encounter characterized by a "soft gaze." The poem concludes with an expression of sorrow and longing for a lost person from the past. From a historical perspective, the poem showcases early modern Korean lyrical sensibilities, blending imagery of modernity (electric lamp) with nature and personal sentiment. While not overtly political, its mood of sorrow and remembrance might resonate with the broader sense of loss and hardship experienced during the Japanese colonial period. Historians may find the specific linguistic features (older spellings and grammar) and the emotional tone representative of Korean literature in the early 1920s.
