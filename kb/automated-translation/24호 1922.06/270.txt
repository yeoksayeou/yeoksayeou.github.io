**Body Text Translation:**
Person Within the Heart
						Sowŏl

As if forgetting, as if seeing, as if always seen,
Yearning, truly yearning, so truly longed for,
In this heart of mine, in its unknowable depths,
I know that person who always resides there.

Even now, if only I could see them now,
That person of mine who would be incomparably dear,
It feels as though I have seen them not just once or twice;
That person, longed for since the moment I was born.

Though others may say it's impossible,
What can be done about what lies deep within?
Ah, that person of mine whose face I have never known,
That incomparably cherished person of mine...

Unable to forget me, unable to forget,
Their burning love becomes tears,
Taking this body of mine that hopes to finally meet—
That person of mine, burdened with a terrible affliction? <40>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl expresses an intense and melancholic yearning for an idealized figure dwelling deep within the speaker's heart. This "person" feels profoundly familiar, as if known forever, yet their face remains unseen, embodying a deep, perhaps spiritual or unattainable connection. Published in *Kaebyŏk* magazine in 1922, during a period of heightened Japanese colonial suppression following the 1919 March First Movement, the poem's themes of deep longing, internal suffering, and the "unknown but cherished" figure could be interpreted allegorically. Historians might find this poem significant for its reflection of the period's emotional atmosphere, potentially representing a veiled expression of national longing, suppressed identity, or the pain of separation under colonial rule, conveyed through personal lyrical expression.

밈에속읫 사람
						素月
니칠듯이 볼듯이 늘보던듯이
그렵기도 그려운 참말 그려운
이 나의 맘에 속에 속모를 곳에
늘 잇는 그 사람을 내가 압니다.

인제도 인제라도 보기만해도
다시업시 살틀할 그 내 사람은
한두번만 아니게 본듯하엿서
나쟈부터 그립은 그 사람이요.

남은 다 어림업다 이를지라도
속에 깁히 잇는 것, 어찌하는가.
하나 진작 낫모를 그 내 사람은
다시업시 알뜰한 그 내 사람은...

나를 못니저하여 못니저하여
애타는 그 사랑이 눈물이 되어,
한끗 맛나리 하는 내 몸을 가져
몹쓸음을 둔 사람, 그 나의 사람?＜40＞
