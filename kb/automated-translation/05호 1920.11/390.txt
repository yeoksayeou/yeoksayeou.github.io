Article Title: 犧牲花 (Sacrifice Flower)
Author: 憑虛 玄鎭健
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Short Story)

犧牲花
						憑虛 玄鎭健

一
Mother formed a lonely household with us, brother and sister (男妹), at the far end of Sajikkol.
Our father is said to have passed away in the autumn when I was three years old. Mother, often holding back tears, would talk about how Father served as a pastor (牧使), how his passionate eloquence (雄辯) moved sinful people to believe in God, how he devoted himself wholeheartedly to the church's work without the slightest regard for his own well-being. My older sister, four years my senior, would get teary-eyed in her clear, beautiful eyes whenever she heard this story. Not knowing any better, I shed tears not because of the story itself, but because seeing Mother and Sister cry made me sad.
Although the household wasn't wealthy, with few mouths to feed, we could avoid hunger and cold thanks to the harvest from the few plots of land Father had prepared while he was alive.
Perhaps due to Father's influence (感化), Mother sent both my sister and me to school.
Since it was already over ten years ago, there was much criticism (批評) about educating my sister. However, Mother, though perhaps not knowing exactly why female education was necessary, seemed to think it was a good thing to educate girls as well. <125>

二
This happened in the spring when my sister, a flower-like maiden of 18, advanced to the 4th year of the girls' division at OO School with top grades, and I advanced to the 2nd year of the same school.
The chill that reddened my hands and made my face blue was long gone. The sunlight was warm, and the edge of the wind was soft. New sprouts emerged in the grassy fields, and forsythia and azaleas had already embroidered (繡노앗다) the mountains and fields in red and yellow.
Before long, orioles sought their mates among the intertwined willows, and skylarks soared high in the hazy sky.
The few daphne trees planted in front of our yard were also in full bloom (爛漫), as if boasting they too had received the beautiful colors of the spring lord (春君).
As the brilliant morning sun scattered its dazzling golden hue through the faintly drowsing morning mist, Sister stood like a child, gazing at the daphne flowers, her hair fluttering in the gently breathing spring breeze. The piercing sunbeams shone on her eyes, and the dew-drenched blossoms brushed against Sister's cheek. Gently touching (만치며) the flower petals (花瓣) with her hand, she lowered her head to look into the flowers...
After watching Sister and the daphne flowers for a while, I checked the clock hanging in the room to see if it was time for school, and sure enough, the time was near. Hurriedly entering the room across the yard (건넌방), I packed my books (冊褓) and came out, saying, "Sister, let's hurry to school, it's almost time." "Oh, already!" Sister, startled by my words, turned around, rushed into the room across the yard, packed her books, and then sat there vacantly (茫然이) again.
"Let's hurry." I urged impatiently (燥急히 부리지젓다). Sister, startled once more, stood up.
Sister's behavior had been very strange (異常하엿다) lately. Even her studies, which she used to pursue so diligently, she would stop reading her books midway and often sit lost in thought (茫然이 自失하야), gazing vacantly (머ᅟᅵᆯ거니) at the distant mountains.— Since Sister slept with Mother in the main room but studied with me in the room across the yard, I had seen her sitting lost in thought several times.
That night, waking around one in the morning, I suddenly needed to use the outhouse (뒤). I quickly got up and went. Coming out after relieving myself, I saw a waning, dim half-moon (半달) hanging in mid-sky (中天). Looking up at the moon, I walked step by step into the middle of the yard.
The daphne <126> flowers in front of the yard were dimly illuminated by the faint moonlight, and something whitish (하야스럼한) was visible among the blossoms. Looking closely (仔細히), it was Sister, standing with her head buried among the flowers. Her white cotton jacket (玉洋木 겹저구리) caught my eye (띄임이라). Why was Sister standing there like that? In the middle of the night, when the whole world was wrapped in the warm sigh of spring and sleeping peacefully, why had she come out? My young heart pounded as I asked, "Sister, what are you doing there?" Startled by my voice, she flinched but gave no answer. Quietly approaching, I gently shook her shoulder. Her back heaved as if she were breathing rapidly. A faint, trembling sound of sobbing (鳴咽聲) could be heard, as if she were trying to suppress her tears. I moved closer and looked at Sister's face.
Her face, visible between her powder-like fair hands, was flushed (밝으레 하엿다). Wondering what was wrong, I tried hard to pull away the two hands covering her face. Her hands were wet (저셔 잇섯다). Tears streamed from Sister's eyes. Pearl-like tears fell drop by drop onto the daphne flowers. The daphne flowers, drinking these tears, seemed to weep faintly in the moonlight, which was like thin silk gauze. Sister's head was hot as a ball of fire. "Why did you come out instead of sleeping..." she said, pushing my hand away, her own hand seeming to tremble (떠는 듯하엿다). In a choked voice, I asked, "Sister, why are you crying? Huh?" and tears welled up in my own eyes too.
The scent of dew-drenched flowers gently caressed the heart like a love song, and the warm gentle breeze brushed the cheeks softly like blood burning with love (戀愛). On such a night, could a tender heart be without feeling? Could a flower-like heart be without sorrow (愁心)?
Not knowing any better, I said, "Sister, please go inside," and took her wrist. I could feel (感覺) her pulse (脈) beating irregularly. Sister wiped her tears and said, "You go in first, I'll be right in..."
"What on earth (大關節) is the matter? Are you unwell (便챤으셔요)?"
"No, I just feel restless for no reason (空然이)." Then, holding onto a daphne branch with one hand, she pressed her forehead against her arm and sobbed bitterly (흑흑 늑기여 운다).
The dim moonlight flowed hazily over the daphne tree, like the gaze of eyes weeping at a bitter farewell (離別).

三
Two days later, on a holiday (空日날), Sister and I went sightseeing at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑). <127>
Articles stating that the cherry blossoms (사구라 꼿) at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn were in full bloom had been appearing in the newspapers (新聞) for several days, and the weather (日氣) was pleasant, so sightseers gathered like clouds, covering the vast Imperial Garden (御苑) until it looked white. Indeed, the cherry blossoms were in full bloom, and the path leading from the Zoo (動物園) to the Botanical Garden (植物園) looked like ten thousand bolts of red brocade (萬段紅錦) unfurled on both sides.
"Kukchu (國柱, narrator's name), let's skip the Zoo and just sit on that lawn (잔디 밧헤) and enjoy the flowers to our heart's content?" Sister looked at me as if seeking approval. Remembering the peculiar smell near the animals, I readily agreed, "Let's do that."
We settled down (座定) under a pine tree in a secluded (慇懃한) spot on the lawn beside the path. The sight of white-clad visitors (遊客) passing under the bridge of flowers, which looked like a red sunset glow, appearing reddish in the flower's light, evoked an indescribable spring feeling (春興). Even young me realized the warm, soft joy of spring and turned to Sister with a smiling face, only to find her sighing softly, lowering her head, and picking a yellow flower blooming amidst the green grass to hold against her cheek. She frowned as if worried about something. Recalling the sight of Sister crying among the daphne flowers that night, I carefully (有心이) observed her actions.
Seeing the sorrowful expression (愁色) on Sister's face made her look quite pitiful, so I pondered what to say to capture her interest (興味), looking around.
By chance (偶然이), looking towards the Botanical Garden, I pointed there, shook Sister, and said, "Look over there." For some reason, Sister gave a start. With an expression common to someone awakened from a deep sleep, she looked where I was pointing. A student wearing our school uniform (校服) was coming down this way from there. He was the class president (級長) of the 4th year at our school. Sister stared vacantly (머ᅟᅵᆯ거니) for a moment, and it seemed their gazes (秋波) met. Sister lowered her head. I saw the area around Sister's ears turn slightly red (밝으레). Sister gripped my knee tightly and said, barely audible (艱辛이 귀에 들리리만큼), "What's there that you told me to look?"
"Ouch! Oww, that hurts. Why, don't you know him? He's the one who advanced to the 4th year in first place this time. They say he studies exceptionally well and has extraordinary talent (才操), plus he's so handsome, right?" I praised (稱贊) him effusively, beaming as if I were him. Sister smiled shyly and said, "Why would I not know him, having attended the same school for 4 years... Is that why you shook me and made such a fuss, just to look at him?" <128>
"Of course... By the way, yesterday I came out a bit earlier than you, right? When I got home, several of Mother's friends (親舊) were there, and they were praising you like crazy. 'How can someone be so good-looking and so talented? You've truly received many blessings (福).' Hearing that made me feel like dancing with joy. He may be impressive (壯하지만), but you're even more impressive (壯해요)." I praised him so much that I worried Sister might feel inferior, so I then boosted her up for a while. Sister blushed again and said, "You say the strangest things, who asked to hear praise from you."
While we were having this chat (수작), he had already passed by us, glancing sideways at Sister. Their gazes met once more. Sister's face suddenly turned tea-red (茶紅). Sister watched blankly as he gradually disappeared into the crowd (衆人總中). He was gone. As Sister's eyes turned this way, my own eyes, which had been stealthily watching her watch his departing figure, were caught. "Why are you staring (빤이) at someone else's face?" Sister's face reddened again. "Who's looking?" I said, smiling broadly.

四
The next morning, Sister applied a little powder (粉), which she rarely did, took off her clothes that weren't even dirty, and changed into new ones. "What's gotten into you today?" Mother asked suspiciously (疑訝). Sister hesitated, then like a child, snuggled into Mother's chest and said, "Don't I look particularly pretty today?" and laughed. With the laugh, a blush (紅潮) spread across Sister's face. Indeed, Sister looked prettier today. Leaning slightly against the doorframe, supporting herself lightly with both hands behind her back on the main room door, her smiling appearance was indescribably lovely. Her cheeks, like infant's milk (牛乳) tinted pink, seemed plump, and her rose-colored (薔薇꼿빗) lips parted slightly, revealing a glimpse of sparkling white teeth. Her eyebrows, like painted spring mountains (春山), seemed slightly arched upwards, and beneath them, eyes clear as autumn water (秋水) rippled with gentle laughter.
Mother looked at Sister and smiled, "When were you ever not pretty?"
"But what about today?" Sister asked back.
"Yes, you look even prettier today."
"Mother, really?" Sister beamed again. A happy expression wrapped in shyness (羞色) emerged. <129>
"You really look prettier today. How happy your father would have been if he could see you." As Mother said this, tears slowly welled up in her eyes. A cloud seemed to pass over Sister's beautifully radiant face as well. However, soon the cloud dissipated, and the light of joy and hope sparkled once again.
Sister, who had been looking blankly (悶惘이) at her weeping mother, said in a seemingly forced sad tone (슯흔 語調), "Mother, please don't hurt your heart."
"Child, it must be getting late. Don't worry about me, just hurry to school," Mother said, swallowing her tears.
We left, carrying our book bundles (冊褓).
As we entered the school gate (門턱), the bell (鐘소리) rang. We ran inside (다름박질). All the school students (生徒) had gathered. As soon as everyone had formed lines (行列) and counted off their numbers (番號), a clear, ringing voice was heard: "Attention (氣着), Bow (敬禮)! Total attendance (出席員數合) OO members!" It was the voice of the 4th-year class president. As soon as this ended, a similar command (號令) and report (報告) were heard from the girls' section. It was a sharp voice, like jade being struck (玉을 바으는 듯한). It was my sister's voice. For some reason, these two voices made my young heart pound that day.
The following Saturday, after school (下學), the 4th-year students announced there would be a school association (校友會) meeting, closed the school gate, stood guard, and prevented the carefree 1st and 2nd graders from leaving. When we entered the lecture hall (講堂) where we always gathered, the male students were already packed tightly on one side, and the female students on the other. As I sat down there, there was some commotion about something, and soon after, 4th-year students distributed small white pieces of paper (投票券), shouting, "These are ballots for the Education Department Secretary (智育部 幹事). Write one name per sheet." Yŏn-sŏk, sitting next to me, acting smart, asked, "Is it a signed ballot (有記名投票) or an unsigned ballot (無記名投票)?" "Of course, it's an unsigned ballot," replied the 4th-year student who had shouted earlier. From the other side, someone asked, "What's an unsigned ballot?" "You don't even know that and yet you try to go home every time there's a meeting! An unsigned ballot is one where the voter's (選擧者) name isn't written," the 4th-year student replied reproachfully, his expression serious (嚴肅). I unconsciously wrote down the 4th-year class president's name straight away. Ultimately, he was elected with the most votes for the boys' section, and my sister was elected with the most votes for the girls' section.
From then on, Sister frequently went out after dinner, saying she had a committee meeting (幹事會) or an Education Department committee meeting (智育部 幹事會), returning around nine or ten o'clock (點). Whenever she went to these meetings, she would look in the mirror, which she didn't usually do, smooth down stray hairs, and adjust her clothes' ties (옷고름). <130>
One night, Sister went out after dinner for an Education Department committee meeting, but she hadn't returned even by ten-thirty. Mother, worrying endlessly, said,
"Your sister still hasn't come back? The meeting should have ended long ago. Go check on her."
I put on my overcoat (두루막) and walked out of the house, heading from the far end of Sajik (社稷) gol towards Kwanghwamun Avenue (光化門通). It was a moonless late night at the end of May. The street was dimly lit with few electric lights and sparse pedestrians (行人), making me feel scared (무싀무싀한). To chase away the fearful thoughts, I stomped my feet loudly and ran (다름박질), counting "one, two." After running for a while, I was panting and sweating profusely. Taking off my hat to fan myself, I walked slowly. Not far ahead of me, a young man and woman were walking up this way together. It was a male student and a female student! It was him and Sister! My heart leaped (설렁하며), and a kind of curiosity (好奇心) arose. I quickly hid (隱身) behind the corner of someone's wall. Unaware I was hiding there, the two passed by, whispering something in English. Among their words, this phrase was the clearest (똑똑이) (I didn't know it then, but thinking back now, it was probably this): He said,
"Love is blind," and Sister laughed quietly, saying, "But, our love has eyes!" After they passed, I quietly followed them. In the darkness, Sister's white blouse (흰적삼) was quite conspicuous. As they passed under an electric light by someone's main gate (大門), I saw his right hand firmly holding Sister's left hand. For some reason, I smiled faintly (싱긋이). Worried they might look back, I muffled my footsteps, hugging the walls of houses, keeping quite a distance. As they neared our house, they stopped walking and seemed to shake hands (握手) again and again. They appeared reluctant (戀戀이) to part. After doing this for a while, he let go of her hand, whispered something else for a moment, and then turned back. Sister stood in front of our gate (門), watching him leave for a while. He passed by me again. His steps were hurried (허둥허둥). After he passed, I ran home. Entering the main gate, I heard Mother and Sister talking.
"Why are you so late? I was worried sick."
"Today, there were quite a few things to discuss..." Sister hesitated. <131>
"Where did the boy (그애) go? Didn't he come with you? Did you not see him on the way?" Mother asked.
"Where could he have gone... You should have met him on the way," Sister worried.
I opened the door to the main room, put on an innocent face (시침을 뚝 따고), and said with a grin, "Sister, you're back now." Mother exclaimed in surprise, "Why are your cheeks and clothes covered in dirt?"
"I was leaning against the wall.... No, it's nothing... just..." I looked at Sister and giggled (빙글빙글). Sister's face turned bright red (밝애젓다) again.

五
After that, on moonlit nights during the hot weather, Sister often went out, saying she was going somewhere with a friend (親舊). Since Sister always left me behind and went out alone, I don't know how many times she and he met in places overgrown with green grass or quiet under the moonlight, exchanging honey-sweet whispers of love.
Perhaps because Sister's comings and goings (出入) became frequent and her demeanor (氣色) seemed unusual (殊常), Mother told her, "From now on, whenever you go out, make sure you take your younger brother with you." After that, whenever Sister was home, she would get inexplicably irritated (짜증), and an endless sorrow (愁色) would envelop her lonely, beautiful face (寂寞한 花容). And sometimes she would complain of a headache, pull the covers (입울) over her head, and lie down.
One day, after we finished lunch (點心), Sister asked me, "Do you want to go for a walk (散步) with me in Namsan Park (南山公園)?" It was June, the height of summer (炎天), and the heat felt like it was steaming people. Thinking I could breathe some cool air there and wash my sweaty body in the verdant color (翠色) dripping from the lush (茂盛) trees and plants (草木), I immediately said, "Yes."
We took the electric tram (電車) from Kwanghwamun Avenue, passed through Chin'ogae (진고개), and climbed up to Namsan Park. On the hill over there, he was visible, sitting down and standing up as if tired (支離하다) of waiting. Sister suddenly turned, looked at me, and said, "Take this and go buy some snacks (菓子) at Chin'gogae! Okay?" giving me 20 chŏn (貳拾錢) in cash. I quickly headed out to Chin'gogae. I bought the snacks quickly and went back, but there was no trace of him or Sister. "Where could they have gone?" My heart pounded (팔닥어리엇섯다) as if Sister had gone somewhere dangerous. No matter how much I looked around (삷혀), they weren't there. I peered this way and that. After staring up at the verdant peak (頂上) of Namsan for a while, I started walking again. I walked for some time, but still couldn't see them. "Aigo, where did they disappear <132> to? They probably didn't come this way," I thought, turning back the way I came.
Retracing my steps seemed quite far. "Aigoo, I walked quite a bit during that time," I muttered to myself. Being annoyed (골딱지가 나니까) seemed to make it feel hotter. The air (大氣) felt like it was boiling (와글와글) in the sun's fire. Am I being boiled alive in this air? Sweat trickled down, and I panted for breath. When I took off my hat, steam rose from my head. Forcibly suppressing my bubbling ill temper (心術), I returned to the spot where he had stood earlier. "Where could they have gone? Let's look over there," I grumbled to myself and walked in the opposite direction from before. After walking for a while, they were still nowhere to be seen. The irritation I had suppressed finally exploded (爆發). I plopped down (털석) on the lawn and bawled (엉엉). After pulling at the grass and crying for a while, I felt ashamed and amused (웃으웟다) at how childish I was being. Wiping away the lingering tears, I chuckled once (희희) and started looking around (삷혀 보기) again.
Over there, under the shade of a pine tree, in a spot unlikely to catch people's eyes, I saw them sitting side by side. I rejoiced as if I had found a lost treasure (보배). "Sister! You're there!" I was about to shout and run over, but then thought, "Ah, let me eavesdrop a bit on what they're talking about," and quietly walked closer, just like that night I followed them. It was midday, so there were no other visitors (遊客), and not even a breeze stirred. The hot air was still (波動이 업다) like congealed oil. Even their quiet whispers, meant not to be overheard, reached my ears distinctly (낫나치).
"Of course, we must do that, but why haven't I been able to see you lately?" he said.
"Mother won't let me go out anywhere, you know? She says if I go out, I absolutely must take my younger brother with me. That's why I came with him today too." Then Sister smiled and continued, "Talking about other things, I forgot. You must have been so tired (支離하셧겟서요) of waiting."
"I waited for over an hour. I even thought maybe you couldn't come today either and was about to just leave."
"What? You were going to leave? Do I ever break a promise (約束)? I was in such a hurry, I didn't even know if the rice was going into my mouth or my nose when I ate lunch." They both laughed. I laughed too. I approached step by step, like a child trying to catch a butterfly sitting on a flower. Lost in their intimate conversation, the lovers didn't notice anyone approaching. I slipped quietly behind the pine tree where they sat. Their shoulders touched, and Sister's loose hair brushed against his cheek. Their eyes <133> and lips overflowed with affectionate laughter. Then, holding hands, they stared blankly (멀거니) at each other like people who had lost their minds (失心). I felt enveloped in the warm, fragrant (香氣로운) aura emanating (發散) from Sister's body. I rushed forward, exclaiming,
"Sister, you're here! I thought you'd gone somewhere... Aish, you really worried me!"
They were both startled (깜짝 놀라엿다). Seeing Sister's ramie blouse (모시 적삼) flutter (달싹달싹), I knew her heart was pounding.
He tried to feign composure (시침이를 뚝 따려 하엿스나), but there was no hiding the red flush spreading across his face due to the element of "shame" (부끄럼).
"Aigoo, I was so startled, wondering who it was," Sister said, stroking her pounding chest with one hand. Turning to him, Sister said,
"This child is my younger brother. He's still immature... Please treat him kindly." Then, looking at me and indicating him with her eyes, she said,
"From now on, call him Hyŏngnim (兄님, older brother)."
"Why should I call him Hyŏngnim?" I teased. Sister's face turned pale (샛밝애지며) as she glared at me.
"Why, are you angry, Sister? Alright then, I'll call him Hyŏngnim," I said, acting cute (어리광), "Hyŏngnim, Sister, have some snacks." I placed the snacks I was holding in front of them. Sister smiled brightly at me and said,
"We don't want any, so you go eat them over there by yourself (흔자). We'll call you when we're leaving..."
I didn't want to be a nuisance (妨害) for long either. Taking the snacks, I went and sat on the grass to eat, muttering to myself, "They treat me like a child, but I have twelve old men sitting in my belly..." and laughed.
The long, long sun was already setting over the Western Hills (西山). The green trees (綠樹) and fragrant grasses (芳草) illuminated by the sunset glow (落照) looked red, as if on fire.
During this time, I had become quite bored. I lay down on the grass, stretched, twisted my body, and sang songs (唱歌) whose tunes I didn't even know. Will they come now? Will they call now? I waited and waited (苦待苦待), but the shadows of the two did not appear quickly (얼른도 아니한다). What could they possibly have so much to talk about? Perhaps the talk between lovers is endless. Though tens of thousands of words must have already been spoken, they probably felt the sun set too quickly before they could say even a few more words.
The red light that shone on the grass and trees at the foot of Namsan gradually faded, and the evening dusk (暮色) crept in dimly (가믈가믈). The sunlight, chased away, climbed steadily up towards <134> the peak of Namsan (南山 頂上), finally huddling only at the very top.
As the dark blue evening light enveloped the base of Namsan, even the sunlight shining on the peak faded, and the red sunset glow in the sky dyed the white clouds red and yellow.
Unable to wait any longer, I stood up and went towards their spot. Startled by the darkening light, they too stood up. I stopped walking and stood stiffly, like a person carved from wood. Sister's worried, trembling voice struck my eardrums.
"Mr. K! If we just continue like this, thinking only of our present happiness, I fear our honey-sweet happiness might turn into bitter-like suffering (苦痛) in the end. We must each do exactly as we discussed earlier."
"Of course! We absolutely must... but as I said before, my family is so obstinate (頑固)..." His voice trembled.
My heart sank (선뜻하엿다). What had they said? What were they planning to do? I regretted (恨) not having overheard it. The two walked this way. Sister's eyes were slightly red (밝으레하엿다). Traces of tears were visible on her beautiful cheeks. My heart sank again, wondering what was wrong.

六
That night, even with my childish understanding, I thought of many things and couldn't sleep soundly. Every time I faintly woke up, I could hear Mother and Sister talking about something in the main room without interruption (間斷업시).
Around one in the morning, I woke up again and heard sobbing (훌쩍훌쩍) from the main room. Mother's voice, mixed with tears, could be heard.
"So that's why you've been listless (탈긔) and acting strangely (殊常) lately... Even if I consent (許諾), what will happen to your life (身世) if his family refuses... I'm upset (괘심하다) that you got engaged (約婚) to him behind your only mother's back. To think I raised you like gold and jade (金玉) without a father, only for this to happen! Just because I don't have a husband, you disregard me too, is that it..." Sister sobbed bitterly (흑흑 늑기며),
"Mother, I did wrong. I don't know what words (말삼) I should use to tell you (엿주어야)... Before we became close (親키도 前에), it was embarrassing to tell you... After we became close, I tried several times to tell you, but the words just wouldn't come out... Please understand. Even though you are my mother, I was embarrassed then. Now that we are engaged, my body and heart are burning, and I can no longer afford to be shy. <135> That's why I brazenly told you. As you said, Mother, there's no way he would forget me. No way he would abandon me. How could someone so affectionate (多情) do such a thing? Yesterday at the park, we swore firmly (盟誓). We agreed that if our respective parents listened after we told them, nothing could be better, but if not, we would run far, far away. Even if we were hungry and cold, even if we couldn't see our parents or siblings, we would be happy (幸福) as long as we were together. We vowed to gladly endure (달게 격겟다구요) all hardships (困難) and all suffering (苦痛). It's true, I feel like I would go mad without him. If you don't give your consent, Mother, I feel like I can't live in this world (갓쟌아요)." Sister spoke like water bursting through a dyke (防築) that had held back rising water (밀어 오는 물). A pure-hearted maiden (處女), having hidden the fire of love deep within her heart, trembling (戰戰兢兢) lest anyone find out, burning with anxiety alone, once she starts revealing (發說) it to someone close, pours out her innermost thoughts (所懷) fiercely (猛烈이).
My heart surged (울렁그리며) as I crossed over to the main room.
Sister was crying with her head buried in Mother's lap, and Mother was crying with her forehead pressed against Sister's back. I also stood there solemnly (悄然이) for a moment, then sat beside Mother. Shaking Mother, I said in a choked voice, "Mother, don't cry." As soon as I said this, my chest felt pierced (찌르르), and I couldn't stop the flowing tears. Mother swallowed her tears, shook Sister, and said, "Child, child, stop it now."
Sister cried even more sorrowfully.
"Child, it's embarrassing. Stop it. Alright, I'll grant your wish (願). Bring him here once." Mother finally yielded (동곳을 빼엇다). What did they mean by the saying, "Though a woman is weak, as a mother she is strong (女子 雖 弱 爲母則强)"?
Two days later, Sister brought him. His handsome face and polite demeanor (擧動) immediately won Mother's affection. "Truly a match for my daughter," she said. "My beloved daughter's life (平生) is entrusted (有託)," she declared. "Sweet dreams will be dreamt," she predicted. "Joyful days will come." Moreover, she remarked that his clear eyes and dark eyebrows closely resembled her daughter's. Watching Sister and him speak in English, Mother smiled without understanding the meaning. She dreamt of her daughter's delightful (滋味) future household and lovely grandchildren (外孫子).
From then on, the lovers, who had previously met furtively (쌀으기), avoiding others' eyes and ears (耳目), after long waits, could frequently meet (맛나), laugh, and enjoy themselves (질기게) at our house. <136>

七
One evening, he came to our house (왓섯다). Just then (마츰), Mother was out somewhere, leaving just Sister and me.
I rushed out, exclaiming, "Hyŏngnim, come in!" greeting him warmly (반갑게). Sister also greeted him happily (마즈며),
"Why haven't you come lately?"
"No, when did I last come?" he asked, forcing a smile (지어서 웃는다).
Sister slowly (스르르) closed her eyes as if thinking about something, then said,
"Today is the 10th of the 7th lunar month, and the 7th was a holiday (空日)... You came on the holiday and today is the first time since then, right?"
"Right, it's only been about three days."
"Three days! It felt like three years to me. Meeting once every three days?! That's too long! Way too long! Do you think three days is that short?" Sister looked at him as if searching for something.
"Coming once every three days is still impressive (壯하지요)," he said, smiling again.
"Impressive! Do you know how many times I looked out the gate (門 박글) during those three days? I worried about everything. Are you unwell? Did you get scolded (꾸중)..." Her voice took on a trembling tone (顫聲), and tears welled up (괴이어진다) in her eyes. "I was so anxious, wondering if some big problem had arisen regarding our situation! How much my heart ached (애간장을 태왓는지요)!" Tears brimmed and flowed down.
"No, no! Anyway (如何間), I apologize needlessly." He frowned (눈살을 찝흐리다가), then chuckled awkwardly (선웃음을 치며),
"Why do you cry at the drop of a hat like a child? Aren't you embarrassed in front of your little brother? (Suddenly changing his tone strangely) Anyway, I wanted (올라 카고) to come yesterday and the day before (아레도 올라 켓지마는), but every time I planned to come, a friend came to visit (차자와서), so how could I?"
Sister, who had been crying, started to smile. I laughed too.
He was from Taegu (大邱). His parents still lived in Taegu. He was staying (留宿) in Seoul at the house of his paternal cousin once removed (五寸堂叔). Since he had been in Seoul for five or six years already, he barely used dialect (사투리), but sometimes he would say strange things to make us laugh (웃키랴고).
"Trying to come (올라 카고), trying to go (갈라 카고)," I mimicked, rolling (뚤뚤) on the floor laughing. He put on an innocent face (시침이을 뚝 다고) and said, "Why are you laughing when someone else is talking? <137> How ridiculous (얄굿다)." Sister laughed so hard her face turned red, and clutching (훔처쥐고) her stomach, she said breathlessly,
"Stop it, stop making me laugh."
We laughed and enjoyed ourselves like this for a while, then Sister sent me on another errand (심부름 시켯다)— I can't remember what it was. Whenever he came, Sister would ask me to buy something or go somewhere, always trying to get rid of me (따돌렷다).
"Aigoo, Sister, why do you always try to get rid of me?" I grumbled (두덜두덜하면서) as I left the house. A half-moon hung slanted in the blue sky. It looked like a lonely boat (一葉扁舟) sailing away on a vast blue sea (萬頃蒼波).
Wanting to hear (듯고 십허서) what they were talking about while I was gone, I hurried back, but it took over an hour. Already accustomed (익숙하여) to eavesdropping, I entered the middle gate quietly and peeked (가만이 삷혀보니). The lovers had brought out a flat wooden bench (平床) under the moonlit daphne tree and were sitting side by side, whispering (소근그린다) something. I held my breath and strained my ears.
"Then what should we do (어쨰요)? Mother probably won't come up [to Seoul] easily... Why, isn't it possible to explain this situation in a letter (上書)?" Sister's anxious voice was heard.
"Well, I've written the letter several times... but I can't bring myself to send it (부티지를 못하겟서오)."
"What if, while procrastinating (此日彼日), they arrange (定해 노흐시면) your marriage to someone else (딴대)?"
"Even if they arrange it, I just won't go, that's all."
"Then won't it be difficult (어렵지 안해요)?"
"By the way, my cousin and his wife (五寸堂叔 內外분) seem to have noticed something... What? I think they probably have. So, perhaps there was some communication (通寄) home, because my grandfather (한아버지) is supposedly coming up soon (日間)."
"If he comes up, you mean you'll tell him (엿줍겟단 말슴이구려) everything?"
"Well, yes, but... my grandfather is truly a tiger-like elder (어룬)... Stubborn (頑固), stubborn, truly stubborn... I don't know what to do either. That's why I can't sleep well at night." He scratched his head and frowned (눈ㅅ살을 찡기더니), then continued,
"Today, Father sent another letter (下書), saying that the Kim Sŭngji (金承旨) family from Ulsan (蔚山) is coming to look you over (선보러) this time, so I should behave properly (行動을 端正이 하여라). <138> It's truly suffocating (氣 막힐 일이야요)." He sighed.
"We must tell your parents this situation as soon as possible (하로 바삐 엿줍지 안하면), or we'll be in big trouble," Sister said anxiously.
"No matter what scolding (如何한 꾸중) I receive, I will say I cannot get married! Don't worry at all," he said decisively (斷然이), lifting his head as if determined (決心).
The bright moon seemed unaware of the two anxious hearts, drifting here and there, scattering its clear light in the warm air. The daphne flowers looked redder, more beautiful. They seemed to say, "I have forgotten the worries and pains (憂愁苦惱) of this dusty world (塵世)."

八
The face of Sister who woke up the next day (이튼날) was pale (해쓱하엿다). Seeing her hair disheveled (허터질대로 허터진 것), one could easily tell (可히 알러라) she hadn't slept well the previous night, having turned her pillow (벼개) over many times (몃 번이나 고처 빈). Sister had tasted the bitter and astringent side of love for the first time! She had learned for the first time (비롯오) that trying to pick (꺽그랴면) the haedanghwa (rose) of happiness meant getting pricked by thorns.
A day passed, two days passed, and soon a week went by, but he, whom Sister waited and waited (苦待苦待) for, hoping he would come today with good news (好音), or tomorrow (來日) with joyful tidings (喜息), didn't show even the tip of his nose (코끗도 보이지 안는다). (Even when I went to school, I couldn't see him, and Sister, from this time, became mentally distraught (心思가 散亂하야) and couldn't go to school either.)
During this time, Sister was so consumed with anxiety that the lovely color faded (살아저가고) from her cheeks (兩頰), and the area around her eyes took on a bluish tinge (푸른氣) and sank. Her lips (입슐) became dry and dark (깜옷깜옷), and her arms hung limply (脈업시).
On the day the week was up, Sister, unable to bear it any longer, gave me a letter (便紙) and said, "Take this letter, give it to him if he's at that house (宅), and if you don't see him, bring it back."
Having followed him to his house once before, I knew it well (仔細히 알아두엇다). Entering the gate of that house, there were no servants in the outer quarters (行廊), and the door to the detached parlor (舍廊門) where he stayed was closed.
From inside, the angry voice of a robust (긔운찬) old man struck my ears.
"This brat, still a student, says he can't get married. A fine excuse, this disgraceful (괘심한) brat. I hear you've gotten yourself involved (어더 가지고) with some female student and are acting crazy (미쳐 <139> 날뛴다는 구나)!! What do you mean, 'No, it's not true'? You say you can't marry the bride your parents arranged because you're a student, but as a student, it's okay (관계챤으냐) to have a woman (게집)? This wicked brat! I would have let you marry after you finished school as you wished, but since you're already acting impatient as a young brat, getting involved with female students and what not, if I leave you alone, you'll ruin your life (身世를 亡치고) and disgrace the family name (家門을 더렵힐 터이야)! That's why we hurried to arrange the marriage (定婚) and even sent the wedding gifts (婚需), so what do you mean now by saying whether you'll go or not? Even for a young brat's thinking... That family is famous throughout Ulsan, they have wealth, and good lineage (兩班)... If we break off (退婚) this finalized marriage, is that bride (新婦) supposed to grow old as a widow while her husband lives (生寡婦)? Haven't you heard the saying, 'If one woman holds a grudge, frost will fall even in May (一婦含怨에 五月飛霜이란 말)'? You'd rather die than go? Heh heh, this rascal deserves to be beaten to death (撲殺할 놈)! If you want to cut ties (끈코) with your grandparents, parents, and all relatives (一家親戚), just try doing as you please!"
Hearing this, goosebumps (솔음) ran down my spine. On the other hand, I was utterly furious (憤하기 짝이 업섯다). I was deeply angered (切切이 憤하엿다) that my pure sister had suffered such insults (侮辱). I glared (홉뜨고) with my small eyes and clenched my fernbrake-like (고사리 가튼) fists, feeling like I would storm in and vent my anger.
"Heh heh, this despicable (괘씸한) brat! Aarrgh, I'm furious! Bring my coat (두루막내) there," the old man's booming voice sounded again. My liver and gallbladder felt chilled (肝膽이 서늘하엿다). It sounded like the old man was shuffling (찍찍 끄을고) this way in his shoes. A fit (症) of fear seized me, and I hastily ran (다름박질) out of the house.

九
That night, after Mother fell asleep, Sister quietly came over to my room and said, "Child, please tell me (이악이하여 다고 응?) everything you saw." As Sister said this, her face showed colors of distress (苦惱) and shame (羞愧). She felt embarrassed even asking her young brother about her lover! I kept my mouth shut and sat silently (默默히). I couldn't bring myself (참아) to tell that story.
"Why are you being ill-tempered (心術) again? Please just tell me. Since you brought the letter back (돌우 가지고 온), does it mean you didn't meet Hyŏngnim? Or did you meet him but couldn't deliver it (못 傳햇니)? Or perhaps (或은) something happened (무슨 일이 낫더냐)? Stop tormenting me (남의 속 고만 태고) and please just tell me. Isn't this the plea (請) of your pitiful (可憐한) sister?" Her voice was plaintive and desolate (哀婉凄凉). My young heart felt pierced (지르는 듯 하며), and tears overflowed. I felt sad (슯헛슴이라) that the honey-sweet future my sister, who treated me so affectionately, had dreamt of had turned to foam (물거품), and angry (이가 떨리엇다) that my pure sister had heard such dirty words from that old man, like "what kind of woman" (어떠타) or "got a woman" (게집을 햇다).
In a sorrowful and indignant (悲憤) tone, I recounted what I had heard at that house. Sister, listening intently (精神업시), collapsed weakly (긔운업시 쓸어지며) as soon as I finished speaking, <140> and the tears that had been welling up (괴엿던) since she started hearing the story streamed endlessly down her feverish (불ㅅ덩이 가튼) cheeks. "Sister! Sister!" I cried, clinging to her chest and weeping too.
At that moment, someone gently shook the main gate and called in a trembling voice,
"Miss S! Miss S! Are you sleeping?" Sister heard this voice and quickly stood up. A lover's voice is well-heard even at such times. Urgently trying to stifle the rising sobs with her lips, she hurried out.
The gate creaked open, and then her crying voice was heard, "Mr. K! Come in." I went out too. They clung to each other (서로 붓들고), tears falling chaotically (撩亂이). Sister, half-crying, half-speaking, managed to say, "I thought... I would never... see you... again." He too sobbed bitterly (흑흑 늑기며),
"It's all my fault."
"Because of me, you were severely scolded today, weren't you?"
"How did you know?"
Sister told him about me going to his house with the letter and what I had heard. Then, in a crying voice, she said, "Please come inside for a bit."
"No, tomorrow my grandfather is definitely planning to take me with him. I'm planning to run far, far away right now (至今 곳). That's why I came, just to say these few words."
Sister, as if doubting her own ears, asked,
"What? You're going far, far away?! Abandoning your parents, abandoning your siblings, you're going far away?! My life (身世) is already pitiful (불상하게). Why should I make you, whose future (前程) stretches boundlessly (九萬里), unhappy (不幸) too because of pitiful me? Please forget me forever (永永이) and marry according to your parents' words. Please be an affectionate partner for life (百年이 다 진토록 情다운 짝) with the one you marry. Have sons (아들 나코), have daughters (딸 나코)... Even if I sacrifice everything for you, isn't it enough if you become happy? Isn't your joy my joy? Isn't your happiness my happiness? Even while sighing and shedding tears, I will try to smile in the shadow of your happiness." From her passionate eyes, <141> tears flowed endlessly, blurring her lonely, beautiful face.
"Ah! To make Miss S unhappy with my own hands and seek happiness alone... Can there be happiness apart from love? When Miss S, who gives me happiness, is floundering in a sea of tears, can I look down from the peak of happiness and laugh alone? No! Without Miss S, I cannot enjoy happiness alone!"
"My misfortune (不幸) is of my own making (제 손으로 맨든 것임니다). But our situation today is neither your fault nor mine. It's that old, rotten custom (慣習) that made us this way! Still, I feel so sorry and heartbroken (엇더케 가엽고 애닯은지 몰라요) that I seem to have troubled (愁亂) your heart because of me! Would I then make you eternally unhappy on top of this (이 우헤 더)? If you become happy, I wouldn't regret dying today (아깝쟌아요)."
"That's unacceptable talk (안될 말슴입니다). The more I hear such words... I feel choked up! It's always the same words, so there's no need to talk at length. I'm leaving. Miss S! Please, farewell (부듸 安寧이)!" He wiped his flowing tears and, seemingly resolved (決心), turned to leave. "Mr. K!" she called in an anxious, trembling voice (안타가운 떠는 소리로), but overwhelming sobs choked her words (북바처 나오는 울음이 말을 막는다). He looked back once more and said, "Miss S! Please, farewell..." Finishing his words, he turned his reluctant feet, his heart remaining here while his body moved away...
I felt as if someone was slicing (싹싹 에이는 것 가탓다) my heart (心臟) with a knife.

十
After that, there was no news (消息) of where he went, and Sister began to fall ill (病들기 始作하야), groaning (신음신음), and as the days and months passed, her illness gradually deepened (漸漸 깁허온다).
Her face, once reddish like a dew-drenched lotus flower (蓮花), became pale (해쓱하엿다) like a pear blossom (梨花) seen through blue window glass (靑色窓鏡). Her flesh, once healthy-colored like a ripening apple (林檎), withered like a frost-bitten yellow leaf (黃葉). Her hazy (거슴치레한) eyes were reddened with white tears.
Then she withered away until she was unbearable to look at. She became like white bones (白骨) covered with thin white paper and soaked thoroughly (흠신) with water. Finally (마츰내), when the Han River (漢江) froze over (어름 얼고) and snow piled up (눈 쌔일제) on Namsan (南山), Sister left this world, which had given him sighs and tears.
Ah, love, fire of love! Because you seem soft and warm, unsuspecting youth treasure (강징난다) you like a jewel in their tender, soft hearts (心臟/心膓). Cruel you, you just set fire to that heart. A pillar of fire like a stone column rises uncontrollably. Jade skin (玉肌) burns away, rosy cheeks (紅顔) burn away, a brocade heart (錦心) burns away, embroidered feelings (繡膓) burn away! The candle flame (燭불) lit in the room suddenly (忽然이) went out; wondering why, I found the candle had already burned down! The tears wetting both cheeks suddenly dried; asking the reason (緣由 뭇잿더니), I found her breath had already stopped (숨이 벌서 끈쳣더라)! (End, written in October) <142>

**Summary**

This story, narrated by a young boy, recounts the tragic romance between his older sister, an educated "new woman," and a male student leader from their school. Their love blossoms amidst poetic descriptions of nature and burgeoning modern ideals, symbolized by their meeting at school events and shared English phrases. However, their relationship clashes violently with traditional Korean society, embodied by the male student's fiercely patriarchal grandfather who insists on an arranged marriage. Despite the lovers' vows and the mother's eventual, reluctant consent, the pressure from his family forces the young man to flee, leaving the sister heartbroken. She gradually wastes away from sorrow, dying young, a "sacrifice flower" to the conflict between modern love and entrenched customs.

The story vividly illustrates the tensions in colonial Korean society between emerging aspirations for individual choice in love and marriage, fueled by modern education, and the rigid constraints of traditional patriarchal family structures and arranged marriages. Historians might find the depiction of the "new woman" (educated, expressing agency in love), the specific anxieties around defying parental authority (especially the grandfather's role), the mention of English usage between lovers as a marker of modernity, and the ultimate tragic failure of this modern love against tradition particularly revealing of the social and cultural conflicts of the era. The story serves as a poignant commentary on the human cost of societal transition.

犧牲花
						憑虛 玄鎭健
一
어머님은 우리 男妹를 다리고 社稷골 막바지에서 쓸쓸한 家庭을 이루어 잇섯다.
우리 아버지는 내가 세 살 
멱던 가을에 돌아가셧다 한다. 어머님께서 時時로 눈물을 먹음고 아버지께서 牧使로 게시던 것이며 그 熱烈한 雄辯이 罪 만흔 사람을 感動시켜 한우님을 밋게 하던 것이며 自己 몸은 족음도 돌아보지 아니하고 敎會 일에 盡心竭力하던 것을 이악이하신다. 나보다 4年 마지인 누님은 이 말을 들을 적마다 그 맑고 고흔 눈에 눈물이 어리엇다. 철모르는 나는 그 이악이보다 어머님과 누님이 우는 것이 슯허서 눈물을 흘리엇다.
집안은 넉넉지는 아니하나마 만치 안흔 食口라 아버지 生前에 장만하여 주신 몃 섬직이나 秋收하는 것으로 飢寒은 免할 수 잇섯다.
아버지의 感化인지는 모르나 어머님은 우리 男妹를 學校에 단이게 하엿다. 
벌서 十餘年 前 일이라 누님 工夫시기는데 對하야 別別 批評이 다 만핫다. 그러나 어머님은 무슨 까닭에 女子敎育이 必要한 것인 줄은 모르셧겟지마는 아마 女子도 敎育시기는 것이 조흔  줄로 아신 것 갓다.＜125＞
二
누님은 18歲의 꼿가튼 處女로 OO學校 女子部 4年級에 優等成籍으로 進級되고 나도 그 學校 2年級에 進級되던 봄의 일이다.
나의 손을 붉게 하고 내 얼골을 푸르게 하던 치위는 업서진지 오래이다. 해ㅅ빗은 따뜻하고 바람 끗은 부들업다. 잔디 밧헤는 새싹이 도다나고 개나리와 진달래는 벌
서 山野를 붉고 누르게 繡노앗다.
어느덧 버드나무 얽힌 곳에 꾀고리는 벗을 찻고 아지랑이 熹微한 한울에 종달새는 놉히 떳다.
우리 집 뜰 압헤 심어둔 두어 나무 月桂花도 春君의 고흔 빗을 나도 바닷노라 하는 듯이 爛漫이 피엇섯다.
하로날 떠오르는 
鮮明한 해ㅅ빗이 어렴풋이 조으는 듯한 아츰안개에 煒煌한 金色을 허틀 적에 누님은 가늘게 숨쉬는 春風에 머리카락을 날리며 어리인 듯이 月桂花를 바라보고 섯다. 쏘아오는 해ㅅ발이 그의 눈을 비취니고 이슬 저즌 꼿송이가 누님의 뺨을 스친다. 손으로 가벼야이 花瓣을 
만치며 고개를 숙여 꼿을 드려다 본다...
나도 한참 누님과 月桂花를 바라보다가 學校에 갈 時間이나 아니 되엇나 하고 房에 걸린 時計를 보니 아니나 다를
가 벌서 時間이 다 되어간다. 急히 건넌방에 들어가 冊褓를 싸가지고 나오며 「누님 어서 學校에 가요, 벌서 時間이 다 되엇서요.」 「응, 벌서!」하고 누님은 내 말에 
놀라 돌아서더니 허둥허둥 건넌房에 들어가 冊褓를 싸더니 또 茫然이 안저잇다.
「어서가요.」 나는 燥急히 부리지젓다. 누님은 또 한 번 놀라 몸을 일으켯다.
요사이 누님의 하는 일이 매우 異常하엿다. 그 熱心히 하던 工夫도 冊을 보다가 말고 茫然이 自失하야 
먼 山만 
머ᅟᅵᆯ거니 바라보고 잇슬 적이 만핫다.— 누님이 잠은 어머님을 뫼시고 큰房에서 자되 工夫는 나를 다리고 건넌房에서 하엿슴으로 누님이 精神 일코 안즌 것을 여러 번 보앗다.
그날 밤 새로 한 時나 되어 잠을 깨니 갑작이 뒤가 보고 십헛다. 나는 急히 일어나 뒤간에 갓섯다. 뒤를 보고 나오니 이미 이즈러진 어스름 半달이 中天에 걸리어 잇다. 나는 달을 치어다보며 한거름 두거름 마당 가운대로 나왓다. 
뜰압＜126＞ 月桂花는 熹微한 달빗에 어슴푸레하게 비취이는데 꼿 사이로 하야스럼한 무엇이 보인다. 仔細히 보니 누님이 꼿에다 머리를 파뭇고 서 잇다. 그의 흰 玉洋木 겹저구리가 내 눈에 띄임이라. 웨 누님이 저긔 저리고 서잇나? 온 世上이 따뜻한 봄의 歎息에 싸이어 고요이 잠든 이 밤中에 무슨 까닭으로 나와 섯나? 나는 어린 가슴을 두근거리며 「누님 거긔서 무엇해요.」 내 소리에 깜짝 놀랏는지 몸을 흠칫하더니 아모 對答이 업다. 가만가만 갓가이 가서 어깨를 가볍게 흔들엇다. 숨을 急히 쉬는 지 등이 들먹들먹한다. 나오는 울음을 물어 멈추는지 가늘고 떨리는 鳴咽聲이 들린다. 나는 바싹 
대들어 누님의 얼골을 보앗다.
粉결 가튼 두 손 사이로 보이는 얼골은 밝으레 하엿다. 나는 웬일인가 하고 얼골 가린 두 손을 힘써 떠엿다. 두 손은 저셔 잇섯다. 누님의 두 눈으로 눈물이 흘러나린다. 구슬 가튼 눈물이 點點이 月桂花에 떨어진다. 月桂花는 그 눈물을 먹음어 엷은 明紬로 가린 듯한 달빗에 어렴풋이 우는 것 갓다. 누님의 머리는 불덩이가티 더웟다. 「웨 안자고 
나왓늬...」하며 내 손을 밀치는 그 손은 떠는 듯하엿다. 나는 목멘 소리로 「누님 웨 우서요? 네?」하고 내 눈에도 눈물이 핑 돌앗다.
이슬에 저즌 꼿香氣는 사랑의 노래와 가티 살근살근 가슴을 여의고 따뜻한 微風은 戀愛에 타는 피처럼 부들업게 뺨을 스쳐 지나간다. 이런 밤에 부들어운 창자에 늣김이 업스랴! 꼿다운 마음에 愁心이 업스랴!
철모르는 나는 「누님 어서 들어가셔요.」하고 누님의 손목을 이끌엇다. 脈이 종작업시 뛰는 것을 感覺하엿다. 누님은 눈물을 씻으며 「먼저 들어가거라, 나도 곳 들어갈 것이니...」하엿다.
「大關節 웬일이야요? 어대가 便챤으셔요.」
「아니 空然이 마음이 뒤숭숭하구나.」하더니 한 손으로 月桂花가지를 부여잡고 이마를 팔에다 대며 흑흑 늑기여 운다.
어스름 달빗은 쓰린 離別에 우는 눈의 視線가티 朦朧하게 月桂花나무 우에 흘러 잇다.
三
이틀 後 空日날 누님과 나는 昌慶苑 구경을 갓섯다.＜127＞
昌慶苑 사구라 꼿이 한창이란 記事가 數日 전부터 新聞에 揭載되고 日氣도 和暢함으로 구경군이 구름가티 모여들어 넓으나 넓은 御苑이 희도록 덥혀 잇다. 果然 사구라는 필 대로 피어 動物園에서 植物園 가는 길 兩便에는 萬段紅錦을 펼친 듯하다.
「國柱야 우리는 動物園은 그만두고 저 잔디 밧헤 안자 꼿 구경이나 실컷 하자?」누님은 贊成을 求하는 듯이 나를 드려다 보며 뭇는다. 나도 짐승 겨테 가니 야릇한 무슨 냄새가 나던 것을 생각하고 「그립시다.」라고 곳 贊成하엿다.
우리는 길엽 잔듸밧 慇懃한 便 소나무 미테 座定하엿다. 붉은 놀 가튼 꼿다리 미트로 지나가는 흰옷 입은 遊客들을 꼿 빗에 비치어 붉으스럼해 보이는 것이 말할 수 없는 春興을 자아낸다. 어린 나도 따뜻한 듯한 부들어운 듯한 봄의 깃븜을 깨달아 웃는 낫으로 누님을 도라보니 누님은 나즉이 한숨을 쉬며 고개를 숙이더니 푸른 풀 사이에 핀 누른 꼿을 하나 꺽거 뺨에다 대인다. 무슨 걱정이나 잇는 듯이 눈쌀을 찌프렷다. 나는 그날 밤에 누님이 月桂花 사이에서 울던 光景을 가슴에 그리면서 有心이 누님의 行動을 살피엇다.
누님이 얼골에 愁色을 띈 것이 퍽 애처로와서 무슨 이악이를 하야 누님의 興味를 끄을가 하고 곰곰 생각하며 이리저리 살피엇다.
偶然이 植物園 便을 바라보다가 그곳을 가르치고 누님을 흔들며 「저긔를 좀 보셔요.」하엿다. 웬일인지 누님은 깜짝 놀란다. 困한 잠을 깬 사람에게 흔
이 잇는 表情으로 내가 가르치는 곳을 바라본다. 거기서 우리 學校 校服을 입은 學生 하나이 이리로 나려온다. 그는 우리 學校 4年級 級長이엇다. 누님이 한참 머
ᅟᅵᆯ거니 바라보다가 두 秋波가 마조친 것 갓다. 누님은 고개를 숙이엇다. 나는 누님의 귀밋이 밝으레 해진 것을 보앗다. 누님이 내 무릅을 꼭 잡으며 「거긔 무엇이 잇다고 날다려 보라니.」 艱辛이 귀에 들리리만큼 말하엿다.
「아야! 아이고 압하요. 웨 저 이를 모르서요. 그이가요. 이번에 첫재로 4年級에 進級한 이야요. 工夫를 썩 잘하고 또 才操가 非凡하대요, 게다가 얼골이 저러케 잘 낫겟지요.」 나는 바로 내나 그런 듯이 깃버하면서 입에 침이 업시 稱
贊하엿다. 누님은 부끄럽게 웃으며 「웨 내가 그를 모른다듸 4年이나 한 學校에 단엿는데... 그래 그 사람 보라고 사람을 흔들고 야단을 햇니?」＜128＞
「그러면요... 그런데요, 어저께 내가 누님보다 
좀 일즉이 나왓
지요? 집에 오니까 어머님 親舊 몃 분이 오셧는데 누님 稱
贊이 야단입듸다. 『어쩌면 人物도 그다지 잘나고 才操도 그러케 조흘고 참 福 만히 바닷습니다.』라고요. 나는 그 말을 듯고 춤이라도 출 듯이 깃버하여서요, 저 사람도 壯하지만 누님은 더 壯해요」 나는 그 사람을 넘우 稱
贊하여 幸여나 누님이 그에게 질까 보아서 또 한참 누님을 추어 올렷다. 누님은 또 얼골을 붉히며 「너는 別소리를 다 하는구나, 누가 네게 稱贊 듯고 십다듸.」
우리가 이런 수작을 하는 틈에 그가 벌서 우리 압흘 지나가며 슬쩍 누님을 엿보앗다. 두 視線은 또 한 번 마조쳣다. 누님의 얼골은 갑작이 茶紅 빗을 띄엇다. 그가 衆人總中에 석기어 漸漸 멀어가는 樣을 누님이 물그럼이 바라본다. 그는 나가버렷다. 누님의 눈이 이리로 도는 바람에 그 사람의 뒤꼴을 보는 누님을 盜賊해 보던 내 눈이 잡히엇다. 「너는 남의 얼골을 웨 빤이 드려다 보니.」하고 누님의 얼골은 또다시 붉어젓다. 「보기는 누가 보아요.」하고 나는 빙그레 웃엇다.
四
그 이튼날 아츰에 누님은 좀처럼 바르지 안튼 粉을 若干 바르며 더럽지도 안한 옷을 벗고 새 옷을 갈아 입엇다. 「네가 오날은 웬일이냐.」하고 어머님이 疑訝하신다. 누님이 머뭇머뭇하더니 어린애 모양으로 어머님 가슴에 안치며
「제가 오날은 퍽 잘나 보이지요.」 하고 웃는다. 그 웃음과 한께 누님의 얼골에 紅潮가 퍼진다. 果然 오날은 누님이 더 어여뻐 보이엇다. 두 손으로 긔운업시 뒤로 큰 房門을 집고 비스듬이 門에다 몸을 半만 실려 웃난 양이 말할 수 업시 어여뻣다. 어리인 牛乳에 粉紅 물을 들인 듯한 두 뺨은 부풀어 오른 듯하고 薔薇꼿빗 가튼 입술이 방실 벌어지며 보일 듯 말 듯이 흰 이ㅅ발이 번적어린다. 春山을 그린 듯한
 눈섭은 살짝 우으로 치어 오른 듯하며 그 미테서 秋水가 맑은 눈이 웃음의 가는 물결을 친다.
어머님이 누님을 보고 웃으시며 「언제는 못낫듸.」
「그런데 오날은요?」 누님이 되질러 뭇는다.

南山 밋 풀과 나무에 빗나던 붉은 빗은 漸漸 거치고 暮色이 가믈가믈 처들어온다. 해ㅅ빗은 쫏기어 南山 頂上을
 向하야＜134＞ 자꾸 기어올라 가더니 南山 맨 꼭대기에 옴추리고 안젓슬 뿐이다.
검푸른 저믄 빗이 南山 미틀 에워싸자 頂上에 비치는 해ㅅ빗조차 슬어지고 저 便 한울에 붉은 놀이 흰 구름을 붉고 누러케 물들인다.
나는 참다 못하여 몸을 일이켜 그곳으로 갓다. 어두운 빗에 놀란는지 그들도 일어섯다. 나는 거름을 멈추고 나무로 깍가 세워 노흔 사람 모양으로 주춤 섯다. 누님의 걱정스러운 떨리는 소리가 나의 耳膜을 울림이라.
「K씨! 우리가 目前에 질거움만 多幸이 여겨 그냥 이리 지내다가는 우리의 꿀 가튼 幸福이 끄테는 소태 가튼 苦痛으로 變할 것 가태요, 우리 各各 꼭 아까 말한 것과 가타야 됨니다.」
「아무렴요! 꼭 그리 해야 될 터인데... 아까도 말햇지만 우리 집은 워낙 頑固라...」 그의 말 떨리엇다.
나는 가슴이 선뜻하엿다. 무슨 말을 하엿나? 무슨 일을 하랴는가? 엿듯지 못한 것이 恨이 되엇다. 둘은 이리로 거러 온다. 누님의 눈은 약간 밝으레하엿다. 그 고흔 뺨에 눈물 痕跡이 보이엇다. 나는 또 웬일인가 하고 가슴이 선뜻하엿다.
六
그날 밤에 나의 어린 소견에도 別別 생각을 다하고 씩씩이 잠도 잘 자지 못 하엿다. 내가 어렴풋이 잠을 깰 적마다 큰房에서 어머님과 누님이 무어라고 이악이하는 소리가 間斷업시 들리엇다.
새로 한 點이나 되어 내가 또 잠을 깨니 큰房에서 훌쩍훌쩍 우는 소리가 들린다. 우름 석긴 어머님의 말소리가 난다.
「그래 네가 요사이 늘 탈긔를 하고 行動이 殊常하더라... 나는 許諾한다 하더래도 만일 그 집에서 안된다면 네 身世가 어떠케 되니... 네가 다만 하나 잇는 어미 몰래 그 사람과 約婚한 것이 괘심하다. 아비 업시 너를 金玉가티 길러 내어 이런 일이 날줄이야! 男便 업다고 너까지 나를 업수이 여기는 게지...」 누님은 흑흑 늑기며
「어머님 잘못하얏습니다, 무어라고 말삼을 엿주어야 조흘지... 親키도 前에 말슴 엿주기도 부끄러운 일이고... 親한 뒤에 몃 번이나 말슴 엿주랴 하얏지만 입이 잘 떨어지지를 안핫서요... 들어주셔요. 암만 어머님이라도 그때는 부끄러웟서요. 인젠 서로 約婚까지 해노흐니 몸과 마음이 달아 부끄럼도 돌아볼 수 업게 
되엇서요.＜135＞ 
그래서 뻔뻔스럽게 엿준 것 이야요. 어머님 말슴가티 그가 저를 이질 理는 업서요. 버릴 理는 없어요. 그다지 多情한 그가 그럴 理가 잇다고요. 어제 公園에서 단단이 盟誓하엿습니다. 各各 父母님께 엿주어 들으시면 이우에 더 조흔 일이 업거니와 만일 그러치 안커든 멀리멀리 달아나겟다구요. 배가 곱흐고 옷이 차더래도 父母도 못 보고 兄弟도 못 보더래도 둘이 가티만 잇스면 幸福이라구요. 온갓 困難과 가진 苦痛을 달게 격
겟다구요. 정말 그래요, 저도 그 업스면 미칠 것 가타요. 어머님이 許諾을 아니하신다 할 것 가트면 저는 이 世上에 살아 잇슬 것 갓쟌아요.」 밀어 오는 물을 막앗던 防築을 문허버릴 때 물 미듯이 누님이 말하엿다. 혼이 純潔한 處女가 사랑의 불을 가슴 속에 깁히깁히 숨겨 두고 幸여나 남이 알가 보아서 戰戰兢兢하며 호을로 肝臟을 태우다가도 한 번 自己 親한 이에게 發說하기 始作하면 猛烈이 所懷를 베푸는 것이라.
나는 가슴을 울렁그리며 안房에 건너왓다.
누님은 어머님 무릅에 머리를 파뭇고 울며 어머님은 누님의 등에다 니마를 대고 운다. 나도 한참 悄然이 섯다가 어머님 겻헤 안잣다. 어머님을 흔들며 목멘 소래로 「어머님 우지마서요.」 이 말을 마치자 가삼이 찌르르 해지며 흘으는 눈물을 禁할 길이 업섯다. 어머님은 눈물을 삼키고 누님을 흔들며 「이애 이애 그만 끄쳐라.」
누님은 더 설게 운다.
「이애 남부끄럽다. 그만두어라. 오냐 네 願대로 하마. 그도 한 번 다리고 오너라.」 어머님은 그만 동곳을 빼엇다. 「女子가 雖 弱이나 爲母則强」이란 말은 어찌 생각하고 한 소리인고?
이틀 後 누님이 그를 다리고 왓다. 그의 곱상스러운 얼골과 얌전한 擧動이 當場 어머님의 사랑을 이끌엇다. 참 내 딸의 짝이라 하엿다. 愛女의 平生이 有託하다 하엿다. 단꿈이 꾸이리라 하엿다. 깃븐 날이 오리라 하엿다. 더구나 맑은 눈과 깜안 눈섭이 내 딸과 恰似하다 하엿다. 누님과 그가 英語로 말하는 양을 보고 뜻도 모르면서 웃으섯다. 滋味스러운 딸의 將來 家庭을 꿈꾸고 사랑스러운 外孫子를 꿈꾸엇다.
그 後부터는 남의 耳目을 避해 가며 몃 번이나 서로 마추어서 길게 기다려 가지고 쌀으기 만나던 愛人들은 자조로이 우리 집에서 맛나 웃고 질기게 되엇다.＜136＞
七
어떤 날 저녁에 그가 우리 집에 왓다. 그때 마츰 어머님은 어대 가시고 나와 누님과 단둘이 잇섯다.
나는 와락 내다르며 「兄님 오셔요.」 라고 반갑게 인사하엿다. 누님도 반가이 마즈며
「요사이는 웨 오시지 안하셔요.」
「아니 내가 언제 왓는데」하고 그는 지어서 웃는다.
누님은 눈을 스르르 감으며 무엇을 생각는 듯 하더니
「오날이 7月 初열흘이고 初7日이 空日이라... 空日날 오시고 오날 처음이지요?」
「그래요, 한 사흘밧게 더 되엇서요.」
「사흘! 저는 한 三年이나 된 듯 하엿서요, 사흘만에 한 번式 맛나?! 멀어요! 퍽 멀구 말구요! 사흘이 그다지 갓가운 것 갓습니까?」하고 누님은 무엇을 찾는 듯이 그를 바라본다.
「사흘만에 한 번式 와도 壯하지요.」하고 그는 또 웃는다.
「壯해요! 사흘 동안에 제가 몃 번이나 門 박글 내다보는지 아셔요, 저는 온갓 걱정을 다햇지요. 몸이나 便챤으신가 꾸중이나 뫼셧는가...」하고 목소리는 顫聲을 띄여가며 눈에는 눈물이 괴이어진다. 「저는 우리 일에 對하야 무슨 큰 걱정이나 생겻나 하고 얼마나 애간장을 태왓는지요!」하고는 눈물이 그렁그렁 넘쳐흐른다.
「아니야요! 如何間 罪업시 잘못하엿습니다.」하고 그는 눈살을 찝흐리다가 선웃음을 치며
「어린애 모양으로 걸핏하면 울기는 웨 울어요, 저 동생 부끄럽지 안하요. (갑작이 語調를 야릇하게 
變하며) 그런데 내가 어지도 올라 카고 아레도 올라 켓지마는 올라 칼 때마다 동무가 차자와서 올 수가 잇서야지.」
울던 누님이 웃음을 띄엇다. 나도 웃엇다.
그는 大邱 사람이다. 그의 父母는 아즉도 大邱에서 산다. 서울 잇는 五寸堂叔 집에 그는 留宿하고 잇다. 그는 서울 온 지가 벌서 5, 6년이 지내엇슴으로 사투리는 거의 안 쓰게 되엇스나 때때로 우리를 웃키랴고 야릇한 말을 하엿다.
「올라 카고 갈라 카고」 흉내를 내며 나는 房바닥에 뚤뚤 굴러가며 웃엇다. 그는 시침이을 뚝 다고 「남 
이악이하는데＜137＞  웃기는 와 웃소 가참 얄굿다.」 하엿다. 누님은 어떠케 웃엇는지 얼골이 붉어지고 배를 훔처쥐고 숨찬 소리로
「그만 두셔요, 그만 웃기셔요.」
한참동안 우리는 이러케 웃고 질기다가 나를 누님이 또 무슨 심부름을 시켯다- 무슨 심부름이던가 생각이 아니 난다. 그가 오기만 하면 누님이 무엇 좀 사오나라. 어대 좀 갓다 오너라 하고 늘 나를 따돌렷다.
「애그 누님도 웨 나를 늘 따돌려.」 두덜두덜하면서 집을 나왓다. 半달은 비스듬이 푸른 한울에 걸리어 잇다. 萬頃蒼波에 외로이 떠나가는 一葉扁舟와 가탓다.
나 업는 동안에 그들이 무슨 이악이를 하는지를 듯고 십허서 急히 오노라고 오는 것이 한 時間이나 넘어 걸리엇다. 나는 벌서 엿듯기에 익숙하여 사뿐 中門에 들어서며 가만이 삷혀보니 愛人들은 달 비치는 月桂花 나무 미테 平床을 내어 노코 나란이 안저서 무어라고 소근그린다. 나는 숨소래도 크게 아니 쉬고 귀를 기울엿다.
「그러면 어
쨰요? 어머님께서는 좀처럼 올라오시지 안을 것이고... 웨 그러면 上書로 이 事情을 못 아뢸 것이야 잇서요.」 누님의 애태는 소리가 들린다.
「글세요 몃 번이나 上書를 썻지만... 부티지를 못하겟서오.」
「만일 此日彼日하다가 딴대 婚姻을 
定해 노흐시면 어
쨰요?」
「定해 노하도 안가면 그만이지요.」
「그러면 어렵지 안해요?」
「그런데 五寸堂叔 內外분은 아마 이 눈치를 아시는 것 가타요... 네? 아마 그런 것 가타요, 그래서 집에 무슨 通寄가 잇섯는지 한아버지께서 日間 올라오신대요.」
「올라오시면 죄다 엿줍겟단 말슴이구려.」
「글세요, 그런데... 우리 한아버지는 참 호랑이 가튼 어룬이라... 頑固 頑固 참 頑固신데... 나도 어찌 할 줄을 모르겟서요, 그래서 밤에 잠이 잘 오지 안해요.」하고 머리를 극적극적하고 
눈ㅅ살을 찡기더니 또 말을 이어
「오날 또 아버지께서 下書하셧는데 이번 蔚山 金承旨 집에서 너를 선보러 간다니 行動을 端正이 하여라 하는＜138＞ 뜻입듸다. 참 氣 막힐 일이야요.」하고 한숨을 내쉰다.
「父母님께 하로 바삐 이 事情을 엿줍지 안하면 큰일나겟습니다 그려.」 누님의 안타가운 소리가 들린다.
「如何한 꾸중을 보시더래도 장가를 못 가겟다 할 터이야요! 족음도 걱정마셔요.」 그는 決心한 듯이 고개를 들며 斷然이 말하엿다.
밝은 달은 애태는 兩人의 가슴을 나는 몰라 하는 듯이 이저리로 저리로 미끌어저 가며 더운 空氣에 맑은 빗을 헛날린다. 月桂花는 더욱 붉고 더욱 곱다. 塵世의 憂愁苦惱를 나는 이졋노라 하는 것 가탓다.
八
그 이튼날 일어난 누님의 얼골은 해쓱하엿다. 머리카락이 허터질대로 허터진 것을 보아도 昨夜에 잠을 못 이루어 몃 번이나 벼개를 고처 빈 것을 可히 알러라. 누님이 사랑의 맛이 쓰고 떫은 것을 처음으로 맛보앗도다! 幸福의 海棠花를 꺽그
랴면 가시가 손 찌르는 줄 비롯오 알앗도다.
하로 가고 이틀 가고 어느덧 一週日이 지내엇건만 누님이 오날이나 와서 好音을 傳해줄가 來日이나 와서 喜息을 알려줄가 苦待苦待하는 그는 코끗도 보이지 안는다. (내가 學校에을 가도 그를 볼 수 업섯고 누님도 이때부터 心思가 散亂하야 學校에 못 갓섯다.)
이 동안에 누님은 어찌 애를 태웟던지 兩頰에 고흔 빗이 살아저가고 눈언저리는 푸른氣를 띄고 들어갓다. 입
슐은 깜옷깜옷 타들어 가고 두 팔은 脈업시 늘어젓다.
一週日 되던 날 누님은 생각다 못하여 便紙 한 張을 주며 「너 이 便紙 가지고 그 宅에서 그가 잇거던 傳하고 못 보거던 돌우 가지고 오너라.」 하엿다.
前日에 그를 딸아 한 번 그 집에 갓던 일이 잇슴으로 그 집을 仔細히 알아두엇다. 그 집 대문에 들어서니 行廊 사람도 업고 그가 잇던 舍廊門도 다치어 잇다.
안에서 
긔운찬 老人의 성난 말소리가 나의 귀를 울린다.
「이놈 아즉 學生이니 장가를 못가겟다. 핑계야 조티 이놈 괘심한 놈. 들으니 네가 어떤 女學生을 어더 가지고

나는 이 말을 들으니 솔음이 쭉 끼치엇다. 한 便으로는 憤하기 짝이 업섯다. 깨끗한 누님이 이다지 侮辱을 當한 것이 切切이 憤하엿다. 곳들어가 憤푸리나 할 듯이 작은 눈을 홉뜨고 고사리 가튼 손을 불끈 쥐엇다.
「허허 이놈 괘씸한 놈! 에이 火나. 거긔 내 두루막내.」하는 그 老人의 우렁찬 소리가 또 들린다. 나는 肝膽이 서늘하엿다. 그 老人이 신을 찍찍 끄을고 이리로 나오는 것 갓다. 나는 무서운 症이 나서 急히 다름박질하여 그 집을 나왓다.

그날 밤 어머님 잠드신 後 누님이 살짝 내게로 건너와서 「이

「웨 또 心術이 낫늬. 어서 이악이를 좀 하렴으나. 便紙를 돌우 가지고 온 것을 보니 兄님을 못 맛낫니? 맛나도 못 傳햇니? 或은 무슨 일이 낫더냐? 남의 속 고만 태고 어서 좀 이악이 하여다고. 可憐한 네 누이의 請이 아니냐.」 이 말소리는 哀婉凄凉하엿다. 나의 어린 가슴이 지르는 듯 하며 눈물이 넘쳐 나온다. 이다지 나에게 情다이 구는 누님이 가슴에 그리던 꿀 가튼 將來가 물거품에 돌아가고 만 것이 슯헛슴이라. 그리고 純潔한 우리 누님이 그 老人에게 「어떠타」던가. 「게집을 햇다」던가 하는 더러운 소리를 들은 것이 이가 떨리엇다.
나는 悲憤한 語調로 그 집에서 들은 것을 이악이하엿다. 精神업시 듯고 잇던 누님은 내 말이 끗나자 긔운업시 
쓸어지며＜140＞ 이 이악이 들을 적부터 괴엿던 눈물이

새업시 줄줄 흘러나린다. 「누님! 누님!」하고 나도 누님의 가슴에 안키며 울엇다.

「S씨! S씨! 주무셔요.」한다. 누님은 이 소리를 듯고 얼른 일어낫다. 愛人의 音聲은 이럴 때라도 잘 들리는 것이다. 나올 듯 나올 듯 하는 울음을 입슐로 꼭 다물어 막으려 急히 나갓다.

「아니야요, 明日은 한아버지께서 꼭 다리고 가실 모양이야요, 至今 곳 멀리멀리 다라나랴고 합니다. 그래서 이런 말이나 몃 마듸 할 양으로 왓서요.」
