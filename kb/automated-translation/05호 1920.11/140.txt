**Body Text Translation:**
Japan's Marquis Ōkuma Shigenobu (大隈重信侯) once contributed planning to the Meiji Restoration (明治維新), and thereafter made many contributions to the political (政界) and academic (學界) worlds, becoming an important figure in today's Japan, a fact generally acknowledged. It is said that since his youth, he idealized greatness (大) in everything, leading him to where he is today. Previously, when a delegation of American congressmen (米國議員團) entered Japan, he welcomed the group at the great study hall (大書院) of his Waseda residence (早稻田邸) and delivered welcoming remarks. During that speech, he brought up his usual topic of the character "great" (大字問題), saying: "The American people like greatness (大). I also like greatness (大). Therefore, my surname is also Ōkuma (大隈)." And so, the magazine he is associated with is also named Taikan (大觀).<76>

**Summary**

This short piece discusses Ōkuma Shigenobu, a prominent Japanese figure known for his role in the Meiji Restoration and his contributions to politics and academia. It highlights his lifelong fascination with the concept and the character "大" (great/large), recounting an anecdote where he pointed out the character in his own surname (Ōkuma 大隈) and the name of his magazine (Taikan 大觀) while addressing American congressmen. From a historical perspective, the article's publication in the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* shows the awareness and interest among Korean intellectuals regarding influential Japanese figures during the colonial period. It reflects the flow of information about the colonizer's society and could be interpreted in various ways by historians studying Korean perspectives on Japanese leadership and culture at the time.

日本의 大隈重信侯는 일즉이 明治維新에 策함이 有하고 其後 尙 政界 學界에 貢獻함이 多하야 今日의 日本에 重함이 된 것은 一般이 認하는 것이다. 그런데 그는 自幼少時로 무엇에던지 大를 理想하야 今日에 至하얏다. 先者 米國議員團 一行이 日本에 入하얏슬 時 그는 그 一行을 早稻田邸의 大書院으로 迎하야 歡迎辭를 述하얏는데 그 演說 中에도 例의 大字問題를 吐하얏스니 曰 「美國民은 大를 好하오. 余도 大를 好함니다. 故로 余의 姓도 大隈이지오.」하얏다. 그러니 말이지 그의 關係하는 雜誌도 其名이 大觀이다.＜76＞
