If You Are a Man, Play Football
						Kim Wŏnt'ae

Generally, it goes without saying nowadays that competitive sports, regardless of whether they are team-based or individual, yield benefits for physical education according to their respective strengths.

However, regarding expense, amount of exercise, and also educational effect, I believe there is nothing like Association Football.

In saying this, it is not that I have only played football and haven't tried other sports, thus being completely unable to consider others. I have probably tasted most of the sports currently popular in Chosŏn, and indeed, I still play tennis, volleyball (빨늬뽈), and basketball (빠쓰께ㅌ뽈). I also enjoy things like baseball, Judo, and swimming. However, I have not delved deeply into all the above from a specialized standpoint to conduct research on physical education from an impartial position. Nevertheless, as far as my observations are not mistaken, I am by no means merely highlighting the strengths of football alone. What I intend to speak about now is based on my own several years of experience and fair judgment; that is, it would not be inappropriate to consider it the true value that the sport of football itself professes.

So, what are the points that make it superior compared to other sports? There are many, but I will raise two or three main ones.

Any competition that has even a little interest inevitably requires sporting equipment. This is not to say there are no competitions without equipment, but they are extremely mundane and uninteresting, leading to quick boredom and the inability to continue permanently, thus utterly failing to exhibit the true value of what is called exercise.

Baseball and tennis receive tremendous welcome as interesting sports, and schools invest large sums to purchase their equipment, such that the majority of physical education expenses are consumed by these two types of sports. Even so, regarding their value in physical education, <104> I believe they are unsatisfactory.

However, with this Association-style football (A式 蹴球), if there is just one ball, a large number of people can enjoy pleasant exercise simultaneously, and even then, that one ball is not easily damaged. With just a little care, it can be used for a long period of at least 2 months, possibly even 3 months.

Furthermore, one ball can be bought for about 10 wŏn, so the setup is extremely simple, and concerning the low cost, I believe there is nothing superior to this sport first and foremost.

This sport allows a large number of people, 11 per side, i.e., 22 people (the number can be increased if both sides have an equal number), to use one ball and exercise simultaneously. Moreover, they can be intensely active mentally. However, looking at baseball players in winter, most of the attacking side wait wearing overcoats (“o-ba”), and they must always be expending mental energy needlessly; I believe this truly has little value as exercise.

Tennis is also no better than this; thus, football is likely the best for allowing a large number of people to exercise simultaneously.

Some say that competitive sports cause students to degenerate. However, I think this probably contains only one side of the truth. That is, various competitive sports have no time limit, and therefore, following one's interest, it is easy to forget the passage of time.

Unless one has a truly steadfast will, it is easy to become engrossed, consequently leading to some degree of negligence towards academics; I believe this is why competitive sports are rejected.

However, concerning this point, there is no need to worry at all with football. When starting a game, the match time is definitely set, and when that time arrives, it stops immediately. Even during practice, there are set dates and set times, so it will absolutely not interfere with academics, which are the main goal during schooling. It will achieve the true purpose of exercise.

It is a fact acknowledged by everyone that the physique of us Chosŏn people is considerably inferior compared to Europeans and Americans.

However, while height and weight might be unavoidable due to anthropological racial differences, the poor posture of the entire body, especially the insufficient development of the lower limbs compared to the upper body, is something whose cause must be considered and corrected.

Generally, Chosŏn people have had the custom since long ago of carrying children on their backs and squatting from a young age, and due to this custom, such underdeveloped legs are created. Because the legs are short, it's not very noticeable when sitting, but upon standing, some have the height of a child, and when wearing Western clothes, there are many <105> whose legs are so bent that their trousers (바지Trowsers) form sharp angles.

Also, the upper body is constantly bent forward, making our posture possibly the worst in the world.

If the bent legs and poor posture were merely unsightly in appearance, there might not seem a need to strive so hard to correct them, but this poses disadvantages in various aspects, so our youth must somehow try to fix this from now on. Everyone knows that the development of our legs is insufficient and also feels keenly the disadvantages related to this.

The main reason for this lack of development, as mentioned before, comes from the long-standing customs of carrying children on the back and squatting (屈坐) during childhood, but considered from another angle, it is also because there was no physical education method available to correct this aspect. However, football is truly the only sport with ample capacity to supplement this deficiency; the legs of those who play this sport are indeed complete and robust. Also, bent legs can be straightened, so if children were made to play this sport from a young age, the physique of us Chosŏn people would improve without us even realizing it.

Some say that football only exercises the legs and not the whole body, but this is a superficial observation by those with absolutely no experience in football, amounting to nothing more than a critique. Anyone who has even thought of kicking a ball once will undoubtedly realize that with every single kick (One-Kink), the arms and the entire body undergo tremendous exercise.

Seeing that those new to this sport distinctly feel fatigue not only in the legs but also in the shoulders, lower back, abdominal muscles, etc., one can infer how much of a full-body workout this sport provides. Thus, I believe this football has great value in terms of physical education.

Since this sport is team-based, the 11 members of one side must be interconnected and unified, just like the five fingers of a hand.

Of course, it is necessary for each individual to excel in their skills, but no matter how outstandingly skilled a person may be, if everyone acts according to their own whim, victory cannot even be contemplated.

When playing this match, observing the state of the team's coordination is how we determine victory or defeat, as is our daily experience. Even a team with one or two outstanding players cannot be predicted to win. Until one sees the cooperative coordination of the entire team, one cannot judge its superiority or inferiority. Because of this characteristic requiring cooperative, unified action, those who play football cultivate a strong cooperative spirit without even realizing it. That a cooperative spirit is necessary for social life needs no further argument now; I believe this spirit must be cultivated among students and youth who have not yet been swayed by social trends. <106>

Individual competitions like fencing (擊劍), Judo (柔道), and track/running (徒步) depend on the skill of the participant; thus, only the individualistic spirit of "just me alone" is fostered, which is the exact opposite of the spirit required for social and national life. If the ambition of today's youth and students were solely this individualistic kind, it would be greatly lamentable. To whatever extent possible, they must possess this football-like cooperative spirit.

Furthermore, it can cultivate the great virtue of disregarding oneself for the sake of the group. Striving for the whole while disregarding oneself, in a small sense, cultivates a spirit of chivalry and sacrifice, and in a larger sense, it can become the root of the great patriotic spirit, such as loyalty to the state and devotion to the country (盡忠報國). This provides spiritual education superior to lecturing on self-cultivation and ethics from a desk.

Actions must be swift; contending over split seconds will forge characteristics like quick-wittedness (明敏), speed (峻速), and decisiveness (敢斷). When attacking, bravely advancing and fiercely assaulting fosters qualities of initiative and progress (進取邁進). When defending, caution (操心), firmness (堅固), and steadiness (着實) form the foundation for the most crucial character traits in worldly affairs. It also becomes the origin of the beneficial spirit to resolutely carry out one's convictions even in the midst of a crowd (衆人稠座). Thus, the three great aspects of character—intellect (知), emotion (情), and will (意)—can be concretely cultivated.

This is why I firmly believe that the sport of football has significant effects on both the physical and spiritual aspects.

Especially for youth and students who are at the most critical stage for character development, I believe it will have a tremendous effect.

Thinking about why football, possessing such various strengths, has declined so much in our Chosŏn today is truly a bitterly regrettable matter. Indignant about this, we have recently organized a Football Team within the Physical Education Department of our Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Association (天道敎 靑年會 體育部), aiming to have youth and students like ourselves try this sport and thereby gain limitless benefits for mind and body. This might be the purpose and also the responsibility of us Children of Heaven (天兒).<107>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Wŏnt'ae, argues passionately that football (soccer) is the most beneficial sport for Korean youth. He highlights its low cost and simple equipment requirements compared to baseball or tennis, its ability to involve many participants simultaneously, and its structured time limits that prevent interference with studies. Physically, he claims it is a full-body workout uniquely suited to correcting perceived weaknesses in the Korean physique, particularly underdeveloped and bowed legs attributed to traditional child-rearing practices. Spiritually, Kim emphasizes football's power to cultivate crucial traits like teamwork, cooperation, self-sacrifice for the group, quick thinking, decisiveness, and patriotic spirit, contrasting this favorably with the individualism fostered by sports like judo or running. He laments the decline of football in Korea and promotes its revival through the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Association. From a historical perspective, the article is revealing for its explicit connection between sport, physical improvement of the Korean race (contrasted with Westerners), and the cultivation of specific character traits deemed necessary for national spirit and social cohesion during the Japanese colonial period. The concern over physique and the promotion of collective virtues reflect common nationalist sentiments and anxieties of the era.

그 다음에 또 團體를 爲하야 自己를 不顧하는 큰 德性을 養成할 수 잇스니 그 全體를 爲하야 自己를 不顧하고 盡力하는 것이 적게 말하면 義俠 犧牲的 精神을 養成하고 크게 말하면 盡忠報國 等에 愛國的 大精神의 根源이 될 것이니 冊床 우에서 修身과 倫理 等을 講釋하는 것보다 以上되는 精神的 敎育이 된다.
萬若 動作을 迅速하게 하지 아니하면 아니될 것이니 그 分秒를 다투는 것은 明敏·峻速·敢斷 等의 性格을 作할 것이요. 攻擊함에 當하야는 勇進猛襲하야 進取邁進의 性格을 與하며 防禦에 際하야서는 操心·堅固·着實 等 處世上 가장 緊要한 性格의 基礎가 되고 또한 衆人稠座 中에서라도 斷斷乎 하야 所信을 實行하는 有益한 精神의 起原이 되나니 知情意 3大 性格을 具體的으로 涵養할 수 잇는 것이다.
