Article Title: 六月의 世界 (The World in June)
Author: 一記者 (One Reporter)
Article Type: 소식 (News)

The World in June
						One Reporter
May 21st to June 20th

(May 21st) Kyŏngsŏng's Posŏng Primary School is renamed Kyesan Posŏng School.
In Kwanch'ŏl-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, a fire breaks out, and the motion picture theater Umi-gwan and 5 other places burn down.
In Kuryong-dong, Tong-myŏn, Ch'osan, a major tragedy occurs as several armed members of an independence group inflict gunshot wounds on 5 family members of Kim Hang-bong, as well as Kim Kyŏng-jun and Kim Yong-ik, over the issue of non-compliance with military funds demands.

(May 22nd) Due to the problem of laborers migrating to Japan concentrated in Pusan, Pusan citizen representatives An Hŭi-je and two others enter Kyŏngsŏng to negotiate with the Government-General.
In Hwaizumi-chō (和泉町), Kyŏngsŏng, a fire breaks out, completely burning down the Sinhŭng Rice Mill and Chosŏn Rice Mill, causing damages of over 100,000 wŏn.
At the P'yŏngyang District Court, the trial opens for Kim Il-bong, Ch'a Yŏng-p'il, and others involved in the Taehan Chayuhwoe (Korean Freedom Association) incident, who were arrested in Nyŏngbyŏn late last year.
Mr. Herriot, recommended as the Prime Minister of France, declares the demand for world peace.
The UK-US Prohibition Treaty is ratified, and similar treaties between the UK and Sweden, and the UK and Germany, are also signed.

(May 23rd) The inaugural meeting of the Women's Friendship Association (Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe) is held at the Ch'ŏndogyo Church in Kyŏngsŏng.
Robbers Yi Chŏng-o, Pak Su-wŏn, and Chang Ton-sang, who were imprisoned in Kimch'ŏn Prison, break out and escape.
The 13th Communist Party Congress is held in Moscow, Russia.
The Russo-Japanese conference proceeds smoothly and recesses to await instructions from both governments.
Japanese soldiers Lieutenant Colonel Minodzuma, Captain Matsui, and others detained in Vladivostok are released.
50 Chinese nationals in Mexico are massacred by bandits of that country.

(May 24th) A murder-robbery incident occurs in Pusanjin, and elderly woman Ch'oe Hyŏp-i is killed.
The Japanese House of Peers and House of Representatives pass a resolution on countermeasures against the US Anti-Japanese Bill.
The Japanese Consul stationed in Fengtian (Mukden) lodges a protest with Zhang Zuolin regarding the assassination attempt on Governor-General Saitō.
Korean Army Commander Kikuchi Shinnosuke makes insulting and violent remarks about Koreans to newspaper reporters in Shimonoseki.

(May 25th) The Kyŏngsŏng Bar Association holds a meeting and resolves on countermeasures regarding the Hŭich'ŏn Police Station Torture Incident (appointing a 3-member committee for fact-finding, demanding dismissal of responsible parties and punishment of torturers, etc.).
On P'anmang-nyŏng between Ŭiju and Sakchu, an automobile overturns, and 3 people are seriously injured.
Residents of Sinwŏn-dong, Haryu-myŏn, Chaeryŏng County hold a village assembly, draft a resolution regarding the improper disposal of the model forest by the county and township offices, and submit a protest to the county magistrate.

(May 26th) In Chiktong, Ch'osan County, 40 independence fighters briefly clash with a police search party, and 5 members of the search party suffer serious injuries.
Kim Chŏng-sŏk and about 10 others related to the corruption case of Chinanp'o's Kwangjesa company are sent to the prosecutor's office on charges of extortion, embezzlement, and fraud.
The US Anti-Japanese Bill, which was the focal point of Japan-US issues, is signed by President Coolidge.
The German Cabinet resigns en masse.
The King of Italy arrives in London for a visit to the UK.
Another large fire breaks out in Sinŭiju, completely burning 6 houses and causing damages of over 16,000 wŏn. Mr. Sŏ Sang-han, arrested in Tōkyō for planning to throw a bomb during the wedding ceremony of Crown Prince Yi (Yi Un) in Taishō 8 (1919), is released from Tōkyō's Ushigome Prison.

(May 27th) American New York Times reporter Mr. "Savel Simmonds" (likely Abel Simmons or similar) arrives in Kyŏngsŏng via India and China. Chinese nationals Wang Guogui and Fan Laozeng, perpetrators who murdered 3 people in Kaesŏng, are arrested in Kyŏngsŏng.
In Wŏn-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, 8 Korean children are taken into custody at the police station over the issue of picking up discarded Japanese fruit.
German President Ebert accepts the resignation of the cabinet.
Japanese Prime Minister Kiyoura holds a cabinet meeting and announces his resignation.
A murder incident occurs suddenly at Sŏgwangsa Temple in Anbyŏn.

(May 28th) Governor-General of Annam (French Indochina), Mr. Merlin, arrives in Kyŏngsŏng via Japan<103>.
The trial for Hamazaki Kiyoshi, captain of the Yamashiro Maru, responsible for the drowning deaths of 68 students who went to see a warship in Yongdangp'o, Haeju, opens at the Haeju Court Branch Office.
Off the coast of Wŏnsan, 4 Standard Oil Company oil tankers capsize, and 3,000 boxes of petroleum sink.
Regarding the fraud case involving Kim Sun-ch'ang, agent for Tōkyō Kyōdō Seishi (Silk Reeling Co.), that occurred in P'yŏngyang, about 100 victimized women rush to the P'yŏngyang Police Station demanding their money back.
A general strike occurs in Canton due to anti-government demonstrations.
The Japanese Cabinet decides on a protest note to the US.
Students of the 3rd and 4th years at Songdo High School in Kaesŏng again go on a collective school strike due to distrust of teachers.

(May 29th) Mr. Hong Ch'an-bo, a defendant tortured during the Hŭich'ŏn Incident, dies in P'yŏngyang Prison.
Mr. Zhang Zuolin in Fengtian holds consultations with key figures in the judicial system regarding the issue of recovering judicial power.
The Russian government sends internal instructions to Mr. Karakhan regarding the Russo-Japanese negotiations, advising caution on the issue of apologizing for the Nikolaevsk Incident.
An armed independence group appears at the Kim Kwang (gold mine) in Choak-tong, Kwanseo-myŏn, Kusŏng County, demanding 2,000 *nyang* weight (approx. 75 kg) of pure gold.

(May 30th) Indian poet Tagore, who was staying in China, departs for Japan.
French Prime Minister Poincaré threatens Germany, stating that if disarmament is not carried out, the Allied powers will continue military control of Germany.

(May 31st) Another strong earthquake occurs in Tōkyō, Japan (no damage).
An agreement between China and Russia is signed.
Japanese Ambassador to the US, Hanihara, submits a formal protest regarding the Anti-Japanese Immigration Act to US Secretary of State Hughes.

(June 1st) The 3rd Art Exhibition, hosted by the Government-General, opens in Kyŏngsŏng.
The Japanese Police Security Bureau decides to abolish restrictions on the migration of Korean laborers to Japan. Due to the anti-Japanese issue, Tōyama Mitsuru's faction forms the National Anti-American Congress in Japan.
Austrian Chancellor Seipel suffers serious injuries after being shot by an assassination plotter.
No Paeng-nin becomes the Chief of Military Staff of the Shanghai Korean Government.

(June 2nd) A group of 20 members of the Kyŏngsŏng Central YMCA's baseball team bound for Hawaii departs for Hawaii. The US bans the entry of Italian immigrants as well.
The Communist Party Congress held in Moscow, Russia, concludes, resolving on cooperation between the Communist Party and peasants.
Canada also proposes restrictions on Japanese immigration.

(June 3rd) The Kyŏngsŏng Labor-Farmer General Alliance and the Youth General Alliance hold an executive meeting to arouse public opinion regarding the authorities' suppression of speech and assembly.
In Mokp'o, a major disturbance is caused by over 400 tenant farmers from Amtae, Muan County, demanding the release of 30 tenant leaders detained due to the Amtae Tenant Dispute.
The French Senate passes a motion rejecting the current President Millerand by a majority vote.
German Chancellor Marx forms a cabinet with old members and formally accepts the Allied reparations plan.
The Romanian government is overthrown due to civil unrest in the region.
The Sun cabinet in China submits its resignation en masse.

(June 4th) A French round-the-world airplane passes through Kyŏngsŏng.
Saitō Tōshirō, Yamamoto Shinpei, and dozens of others hold a Farmers' Consultation Meeting at the Tōkyō Imperial Agricultural Association to organize a Farmers' Party in Japan.
In Sunch'ŏn County, peasants cause a major disturbance over the issue of the Oriental Development Company (Tōyō Takushoku Kaisha) revoking tenancy rights.
19 Japanese newspaper companies jointly issue a declaration regarding the US Anti-Japanese Bill.
In P'yŏngyang city, police are highly active day and night due to the unusual activities of unusual young men.

(June 5th) The German Constitutional Party's motion of no-confidence in the cabinet is rejected.
The Russian Democratic Party decides to implement a republican form of government in Mongolia.
US President Coolidge formally approves Lowden of the Republican Party as Vice President.
In Nam-myŏn, Hŭich'ŏn County, 4 armed independence fighters appear and exchange fire with the village head; there are no casualties on either side.

(June 6th) A Women's Garden Party hosted by the New Women's Department of Kaebyŏk-sa is held at Sangch'unwŏn outside Kyŏngsŏng's East Gate.<104> The Central Executive Committee of the Russian Communist Party changes the leadership from a 5-member body (Rykov, Kamenev, Litvinov, Spiridon?, Stalin) to a 7-member body (adding Tomsky and Trotsky).
An electric workers' strike occurs in London, UK, cutting off transportation.

(June 7th) The Kiyoura cabinet in Japan submits its resignation en masse.
Over 30 ideological groups and media organizations in Kyŏngsŏng gather at the Chosŏn Education Association, organize a committee to impeach the authorities' suppression of speech and assembly, pass a 2-point resolution (1. We shall actively resist the authorities' unreasonable suppression of speech and assembly with firm solidarity. 1. [sic] The method of resisting the suppression of speech and assembly shall be entrusted to the executive committee), and select 13 executive committee members (Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Han Sin-gyo, Yun Hong-nyŏl, An Chae-hong, Ch'a Sang-ch'an, Yi Chong-nin, Yi Chong-ch'ŏn, Yi In, Kim Pyŏng-no, Kim P'il-su, Sin Myŏng-gyun, Yi Pong-su, Kim Pong-guk).
In Fengtian (Mukden) city, 3 independence soldiers attack the house of pro-Japanese Korean Ch'oe Ch'ang-gyu, shoot 2 people dead, and also enter a Korean restaurant, taking 300 wŏn in cash.

(June 8th) In Naech'am, Oejung-dong, Kanggye County, over 10 armed independence fighters appear, shoot dead the couple Tamaki Seiichi (玉木靑), employees of the Shingiū (Sinŭiju) Tada Joint Stock Company, attack a logging work site in Chungch'am, shoot lumberjack Matsumi Kumajirō (松實熊二郞) dead, and on the same night, attack and set fire to the Nambong-dong Forestry Office work site in P'yŏngbuk-myŏn.
At a bicycle race event in Pukch'ŏng, a dispute over the competition arises, leading to the killing of Hamhŭng athlete No Sŏng-man.

(June 9th) The new Prime Minister of Japan is decided: Katō Takaaki, president of the Kenseikai party.
Mr. Yun Ch'i-hyŏng, born in Changjin, receives the title of Doctor of Medicine from the Faculty Council of Kyushu Imperial University in Japan.
In Changsa-dong, Namjŏng-myŏn, Yŏngdŏk County, 2 independence fighters are shot dead by police officers.
In Ōsaka, Japan, over 30,000 people hold an Anti-American Far East Peoples' Rally, denouncing the injustice of the United States.

(June 10th) At Kakhwangsa Temple in Kyŏngsŏng, there is a disturbance where Sangaehoe (Mutual Love Society) members Kim Chin-t'ae and Kim Kye-yong assault monk Kim Ku-ha.
A Conference of Provincial Governors from across Korea is held within the Government-General building.
The French Chamber of Deputies votes 329 to 214 to adjourn parliament, defeating the new cabinet.
The Shanghai Korean Provisional Government issues a proclamation domestically and internationally expressing the intent for great unity.

(June 11th) The investiture ceremony for the new Katō cabinet is held in Japan (Prime Minister Viscount Katō Takaaki, Home Affairs Wakatsuki Reijirō, Foreign Affairs Baron Shidehara Kijūrō, Finance Hamaguchi Osachi, Army Ugaki Kazushige, Navy Takarabe Takeshi, Justice Yokota Sennosuke, Education Okada Ryōhei, Agriculture & Commerce Takahashi Korekiyo, Communications Inukai Tsuyoshi, Railways Sengoku Mitsugu).
Han Se-ryŏl, a student at Kyŏngsŏng's Yangjŏng High School, is tragically killed when struck by a javelin thrown by a classmate.
A severe storm hits Kimch'ŏn, killing or injuring 28 people and washing away 67 houses.
Off the coast of Inch'ŏn, due to a storm, 3 sailboats capsize, resulting in 1 drowning death. The Russian government grants amnesty to the White Army.

(June 12th) In P'yŏngbuk-myŏn, Kanggye County, a clash occurs between 20 independence fighters and about 10 police officers, resulting in 1 police officer being shot dead. On the same day, in Sinhŭng-dong, Hwach'ang-myŏn, Wiwŏn County, 8 independence fighters appear, shoot 1 resident dead, and seriously injure 3 others.
The US warship USS Mississippi explodes, killing 38 instantly.
In Tŏksang-dong, Sŏ-myŏn, Pongch'ŏn County, 6 armed independence fighters appear and shoot dead the family of Kim I-ho (7 people total: 4 killed instantly, 3 seriously injured), and also shoot dead the district head and a member of the Self-Defense Corps in a major incident.
The Romanian government is overthrown by revolutionary forces.

(June 13th) All students of the 3rd and 4th years at Kyŏngsŏng's Hwimun High School again go on a collective school strike due to distrust of the principal and academic dean.
The new President of France is elected by votes of the Senators and Deputies: former Prime Minister and current President of the Senate, Mr. Doumergue. Simultaneously, the new President orders Mr. Herriot to form a cabinet.
General Dawes is elected as the Republican Party's Vice Presidential candidate by vote at the convention in the US.
The Italian Parliament adjourns due to the disappearance of Socialist deputy Mr. Matteotti.
A clash occurs between independence fighters and police in Naesan-dong, Sŏrim-myŏn, Ch'ŏlsan County.

(June 14th) The All-Korea Track and Field Meet, hosted by the Chosŏn Sports Association, is held in Kyŏngsŏng<105>.
At Kyŏngsŏng First High School as well, a dispute arises between students and the school due to distrust of teachers, resulting in over 100 students being suspended indefinitely.
The President of China releases the original text of the Sino-Russian agreement.
Citizens of Hamhŭng hold a citizens' rally to mediate the animosity between the two counties (Hamhŭng and Pukch'ŏng) that arose from the issue of the cyclist killed in Pukch'ŏng.
The new French cabinet is formed with Mr. Herriot as Prime Minister, Lanquest for Justice, Chautemps for Interior, and others.
In Simch'ŏn-myŏn, Sŏnch'ŏn County, 6 independence fighters appear and advocate for unity and cooperation.

(June 15th) In Kyŏngsŏng, Provincial Councilors, School Councilors, and Chamber of Commerce Councilors gather to hold a so-called Public Officials Conference regarding Korean autonomy and political participation issues.

(June 16th) In Tŏk-tong, Sŏt'ae-myŏn, Wiwŏn County, independence fighters and police clash, and 1 independence fighter is shot dead.
Kim Yong-bok, a provisional government special agent involved in last year's attack on the Ch'ŏngsŏngjin Police Substation in Ŭiju, is arrested in Sŏnch'ŏn by officers from the Sinŭiju station.
The 6th International Labour Conference of the League of Nations opens in Geneva, Switzerland.
The US delivers a firm response to the Japanese protest to the Japanese Ambassador.

(June 17th) In Sakchu and Kusŏng Counties, independence fighters appear and engage in battle with police; in Kwanseo-myŏn, Kusŏng, 1 independence fighter dies in battle.

(June 18th) A trade treaty is concluded between Russia and Denmark, and simultaneously Denmark formally recognizes Russia.
The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs lodges a protest with Japan regarding the infringement of fishing rights by Japanese nationals near Shandong.

(June 19th) Annam Governor-General Mr. Merlin, who arrived in Shanghai via Japan and Korea, is targeted with a bomb by Kwangboktan (Restoration Corps) member Phạm Hồng Thái at a welcome reception hosted by his countrymen, resulting in 3 immediate deaths and 7 serious injuries (the Governor-General is unharmed).
Nakada Tadashi (中正), working at the T'aech'ŏn branch of the Oriental Development Company, is shot dead by independence fighters in T'aech'ŏn Sŏ-myŏn. Diplomatic relations between the UK and Mexico are severed, and the Mexican Ministry of Foreign Affairs issues an order for the British representative, Mr. Cummins (캄 신스), to leave.
Chinese people in Shanghai hold an Anti-Japanese Citizens' Rally and send a resolution to the Japanese Katō cabinet, responsible for the 21 Demands, demanding the abolition of its aggressive policies.

(June 20th) The People's Rally against the Suppression of Speech and Assembly, planned to be held at the Ch'ŏndogyo Church in Kyŏngsŏng, is forcibly dispersed by police; simultaneously, executive committee members Yi Chong-nin, Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, and 2 others (4 total) are arrested.
The French Chamber of Deputies passes a vote of confidence in the new cabinet, 313 to 234 [Note: text says 234 to 313, likely reversed].
Near MacArthur (마카-사-) in the US, 2 Japanese individuals are murdered by Americans, causing relations between Japan and the US to worsen.<106>

**Summary**

This article chronicles daily events from May 21 to June 20, 1924, covering news from Korea, Japan, China, Russia, Europe, and the US. Significant Korean events include independence movement activities (armed clashes, assassinations of collaborators/Japanese, funding demands, arrests, trials), social issues (labor migration protests, tenant farmer disputes, school strikes against authorities/teachers), political suppression (police torture, crackdowns on assembly/speech, arrests of activists like Yi Chong-nin), and disasters (fires, storms). International news focuses on the fallout from the US Immigration Act excluding Japanese, Japanese and European political changes (cabinet resignations/formations in Japan, France, Germany), the signing of the Sino-Soviet agreement, ongoing Russo-Japanese talks, violence in China and Mexico, and diplomatic tensions.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is revealing. It highlights the persistent armed struggle by independence groups along the northern border and even within Korea, alongside Japanese counter-insurgency efforts. It also shows widespread social unrest stemming from colonial policies, including disputes with the Oriental Development Company, tenant farmer agitation (Amtae dispute), student activism (school boycotts), and protests against suppression of basic rights (speech, assembly). The documentation of specific incidents like the Hŭich'ŏn torture case and the Government-General's response, alongside the daily activities of Korean activists and organizations, provides valuable ground-level detail often missing in broader historical narratives. Historians would find the juxtaposition of local Korean resistance and social issues with major international developments (like the US Exclusion Act impacting Korean labor migration via Japan) particularly interesting.

六月의 世界
						一記者
5월 21일부터 6월 20일까지
(5월 21일) 京城의 普成初等學校는 桂山 普成學校로 改稱하고
京城 貫鐵洞에는 火災-起하야 활동사진관 優美館 기타 5處가 延燒되고
楚山 東面 龜龍洞에는 數名의 무장 독립단이 군자금 不應 문제로 金恒奉의 가족 5명과 金京俊
金龍翼 등을 銃傷한 大 참사가 起하다.
(仝 22일) 釜山에 집중한 渡日 노동자 문제로 하야 釜山 市民 대표 安熙濟 외 2氏는 총독부와 타협키 위하야 入京하고
京城 和泉町에는 火災-起하야 新興精米 及 朝鮮精米所가 盡燒되야 손해 10여만 원을 出하고
平壤지방법원에는 작년 말 寧邊에서 체포된 大韓自由會 사건의 金一鳳
車永弼 등의 공판이 開廷되고
佛國의 총리로 推薦된 「에리오」氏는 세계평화의 요구를 聲明하고
英米間 禁酒條約이 批準되고 瑞典 獨逸과의 英米間도 同樣으로 調印되다.
(仝 23일) 京城 天道敎堂에는 여성 同友會 發會式이 有하고
金泉刑務所에 수감 중이든 강도 李正五
朴水源
張燉相은 破獄 逃走하고
露國 莫斯科에는 제13회 共産黨大會가 개최되고
H露會議는 順調로 진행되야 兩國정부의 訓電을 待하기로 휴회하고
海蔘威에 구금 중이든 日本 군인 蓑妻小佐 松井 大尉 기타는 석방되고
墨國에 在한 中國人 50명은 同國 土匪에게 虐殺을 당하다.
(仝 24일) 釜山鎭에는 살인 강도사건이 生하야 노파 崔夾伊가 피살되고
日本 貴衆兩院은 米排日案에 대한 대책의 결의가 有하고
奉天駐在 日領事는 齋藤總督 襲擊사건으로 張作霖에게 항의를 提하고
朝鮮軍 司令官 菊池愼之助는 下關에서 신문기자를 대하야 朝鮮人 모욕적 暴言을 發하다.
(仝 25일) 京城에는 辯護士協會가 有하야 熙川警察署 拷問事件에 대한 대책을 결의하고(위원 3명으로 진상조사, 책임자 면직 고문자 처벌 등)
義州 朔州間 板幕領에서는 자동차가 轉覆되야 3명이 重傷하고
載寧郡 下柳面 新院洞民은 洞民大會를 開하야 郡面廳이 模範林 不當 처분에 대한 결의문을 작성하야 군수에게 항의를 提하다.
(仝 26일) 楚山郡 直洞에서는 40명의 독립단과 경찰의 수색대와 일시 간여나 接戰하야 수색대 5명이 重傷을 당하고
鎭南浦 廣濟社의 부정사건 관계자 金貞錫 외 10여 명은 공갈 횡령 사기죄로 검사국에 송치되고
日米문제의 極點이든 米의 排日法案은 대통령 쿨리지氏도 署名하고
獨逸 內閣은 總辭職하고
伊太利 황제는 英國을 방문차 倫敦에 着하고
新義州에는 大火가 又 起하야 6戶 全燒 16,000여 원의 損을 당하고 大正 8년 李王世子 혼례에 폭탄 투척의 계획을 하다가 東京에서 체포된 徐相漢氏는 東京牛谷刑務所로서 출옥하다.
(仝 27일) 米國 뉴욕타임쓰 신문기자 「새벨씨만드」는 印度 及 中國을 경유하야 入京하고 開城에서 3人을 死殺한 범인 中國人 王國桂 范老增은 京城에서 체포되고
京城 苑洞에는 내버린 日本人 果樹 拾取문제로 朝鮮아동 8명이 경찰서에 拘引을 당하고
獨 대통령 「에-베트」氏는 내각의 辭職을 聽許하고
日本의 淸浦 수상은 閣議를 開하고 辭職을 明言하고
安邊釋王寺에는 살인사건이 돌발하다.
(仝 28일) 安南 總督 「멜란」氏는 日本을＜103＞ 경유하야 京城에 入하고
海州 龍塘浦에서 軍艦 구경을 하기 위하야 학생 68명을 溺死케 한 책임자 山城丸 선장 濱崎淸에 대한 公判이 海州법원 支廳에 開하고
元山海 內에서는 스탄다회사 석유선 4隻이 轉覆되야 석유 3천 箱이 침몰되고
平壤에 生起인 東京共同製絲 대리점 金淳昌의 사기사건에 대하야 피해당한 여자 100여 명은 돈 차저 달나고 平壤警察署에 쇄도하고
廣東市에는 반정부 시위로 大同盟罷業이 起하고
日本 閣議에서는 對米 抗議書를 결정하고
開城 松都高普校 3, 4학년생은 교사 불신임으로 又 동맹휴교하다.
(仝 29일) 熙川事件 중의 고문당한 被告 洪贊甫氏는 平壤 감옥에서 사망하고
奉天의 張作霖氏는 재판권 회수문제로 재판계 관계 주역을 招하야 협의를 開하고
露國정부는 日露 교섭에 대하야 尼港事件 사죄문제를 주의하라고 「카라한」氏에게 內訓을 發하고
龜城郡 舘西面 造岳洞 金鑛에는 武裝 獨立團이 출현하야 순금 2천 兩重을 요구하다.
(仝 30일) 中國 滯在중이든 「타골」 印度 시인은 日本으로 출발하고
佛 수상 포앙카레氏는 獨逸에 대하야 군축을 실시치 안는 경우는 연합국이 獨逸의 군사관리를 계속한다고 위협하다.
(仝 31일) 日本 東京에는 又 復强震이 起하고(손해 無)
中國과 露國의 협정은 調印되고
駐米 日大使 埴原氏는 排日 移民法案에 대한 정식 항의를 米 國務卿 「휴쓰」氏에게 提하다.
(6월 1일) 京城에는 총독부 주최의 제3회 미술전람회가 개최되고
日本警保局에서는 朝鮮 勞働者渡制日에 대한 철폐를 결정하고 排日문제로 인하야 日本에는 頭山滿 一派의 國民對米大會가 성립되고
墺國 수상 「사이펠」氏는 엇던 암살 계획자의 탄환을 受하야 重傷을 負하고

(仝 2일) 京城中央基督敎靑年會의 포哇 遠征 야구단 一行 20명은 布哇를 향하야 출발하고 米國은 伊國 이민도 入國을 금지하고
