Report on the Suppression of Speech and Assembly
						By a Korean

It goes without saying that everyone knows the suppression of our speech and assembly by the Government-General authorities, who advocate the so-called Cultural Rule (文化政治), is severe. Particularly since the Tokyo earthquake disaster last autumn, the suppression has reached an even higher level, and our freedom has been persecuted without respite. Deeply feeling this, over 100 representatives from 31 organizations within Kyŏngsŏng gathered at the Korean Education Society (朝鮮敎育會) on June 7th to impeach the authorities' unjust measures, organizing the "Society for the Impeachment of Suppression of Speech and Assembly" (言論集會壓迫彈劾會). They passed resolutions stating: "1. We shall actively resist the authorities' unreasonable suppression of our speech and assembly through firm solidarity. 2. The methods of resistance against the suppression of speech and assembly shall be entirely entrusted to the executive committee." After selecting 13 executive committee members, they attempted to hold their first public event, a citizens' rally, at the Ch'ŏndogyo Church in Kyŏngun-dong on the 20th of the same month, but this too was suppressed and dispersed. Among the committee members, Messrs. Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi, Yi Chong-nin, Han Sin-gyo, and Sin Myŏng-gyun were detained for two days. While it is unknown how this issue will progress further, one can see from the attached list how the authorities have recently applied suppression to speech and assembly.

**Summary**

This article from July 1924 reports on the increasing suppression of freedom of speech and assembly in colonial Korea by the Japanese Government-General, particularly intensified after the Great Kanto Earthquake of 1923. It details the formation of a multi-organizational committee in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) to protest this suppression, the subsequent crackdown on their attempted rally, and the arrest of key members. The latter half provides itemized lists of articles and issues confiscated from major Korean newspapers (Tonga Ilbo, Choson Ilbo, Sidae Ilbo) and the magazine Kaebyŏk itself during the first half of 1924, citing the reasons for censorship, which often related to nationalism, communism, labor rights, anti-Japanese sentiment, or reporting on independence activists. For historians, this article is valuable as a contemporary account documenting the specific nature and extent of Japanese censorship during the "Cultural Rule" period, providing concrete examples of suppressed topics (e.g., Russian communism, Korean independence movement figures like Kim Sang-ok, the Ŭiyŏltan, labor issues, criticism of Japanese policy, international affairs involving Japan) and the resilience of Korean organizations in attempting to resist these restrictions. The lists of confiscated articles offer a snapshot of the discourse deemed dangerous by the colonial authorities.

言論集會壓迫報告
						조선인
所謂 文化政治를 표방하는 總督府 당국에서 우리의 言論集會에 대하야 압박이 심한 것은 다시 말 안이하야도 누구나 알 것이다. 특히 昨秋 東京 震災 이후로는 압박이 일층 高度에 達하야 우리의 자유는 餘地업시 迫害를 당하얏다. 이것을 痛切히 思한 京城 內 31개 단체의 대표자 100여인은 당국의 부당한 處置를 탄핵하기 위하야 6월 7일에 朝鮮敎育會 內에 會集하야 「言論集會壓迫彈劾會」를 조직하고 「1. 우리의 言論 及 集會에 대한 당국의 無理한 압박을 鞏固한 結束으로써 적극적 대항할 일, 2. 言論 及 集會의 압박에 대한 抗拒 방법은 실행위원에게 一任함」이란 결의문을 통과하고 실행위원 13인을 選定한 후 仝월 20일에 第一聲으로 慶雲洞 天道敎堂內에서 시민대회를 開하랴다가 이것도 또한 압박을 당하야 해산되고 위원 중 徐廷禧, 李鍾麟, 韓愼敎, 申明均 4氏는 2일간 檢束이 되얏섯다. 이제 그 문제가 또 엇지 진전될지 알 수 업스나 近間 當局에서 言論, 集會에 대하야 如何히 압박을 加한 것은 別表를 보면 알겟고
尙又 今年 1월부터 6월 20일까지에 압수된 신문잡지의 槪况을 거擧하면 左와 如하다.
