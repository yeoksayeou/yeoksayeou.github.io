朝鮮苦 (The Sufferings of Korea)
Korea in June - Independence Groups Move Within the Borders, Poor Farmers Move Outside

To summarize the unusual events that occurred within Korea from late May to late June, ever since Governor-General Saitō toured the border in May and received gunfire from the Independence Army on the opposite bank, the entry of independence forces into the territory has become even more severe. In places like Kanggye, Ch'osan, and Musan, there are hardly any days without exchanges of gunfire. According to people coming from there, the Independence Groups seemed rather quiet the year before last, but from last year into this summer, their momentum has greatly increased, and their backing appears very strong. At the end of May and beginning of June, there was an issue where several thousand Korean laborers were stranded in Pusan due to being unable to travel to Japan, with the Sangaehoe (相愛會 - Mutual Love Society) meddling in the situation, but this has temporarily subsided with the easing of restrictions on travel to Japan. Among events like the meager art exhibition hosted by the Bureau of Education (total submissions only 156) and various athletic meets, the track and field competition hosted by the Athletic Association was, in any case, a new test for the Korean sports world. On June 6th, the long-pending direct shipping route between Pusan and Shanghai was opened by the Chōsen Yūsen Kaisha (朝鮮郵船會社). While the authorities' pressure on Korean press and assembly had been notably severe since last year, entering this spring and summer, newspapers and magazines face confiscation daily and monthly. As for other gatherings, any meeting or lecture with even a slight coloration of social issues is absolutely banned. In response, several organizations in the city have formed a Committee to Denounce the Suppression of Speech and Assembly, which is currently active. Amidst quite fickle weather this year, a large hailstorm hit the Kimch'ŏn region on the 11th, causing casualties and significant crop damage, which is also a kind of strange event. Amidst student strike turmoil erupting everywhere from Kwangju High Common School, Kyŏngsŏng First High Common School, Hwimun School, Sinsŏng School, down to even normal schools, the attitude of school authorities seems, as if by agreement, more stubborn than before – perhaps this too is a reactionary trend? There were conferences of Provincial Governors and Police Chiefs, and an All-Korea Public Officials Conference (poorly attended) <14> where lectures on sincerity, harmony between Japan and Korea were once again reviewed. This time, when the old grain is gone and the new has not yet ripened, is the hardest time for the rural proletarian masses. Saying they survive by mixing red clay into porridge, the misery of the slash-and-burn farmers (*hwajŏnmin*), among the destitute with nowhere to turn, sometimes makes newspaper readers weep. Besides this, issues like the disputes within the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (衡平社), the entry of Chinese laborers, the meddling of Japanese merchants, and the ever-present problems of irrigation associations and tenant farmers became frequent topics of conversation.

The Reactionary Trend of School Authorities - A Consideration of the Popular Student Strikes (盟休)
Uprisings of student strikes (學生同盟休學) are erupting everywhere, with difficulties greater than the Japan-US problem lurking between students and authorities. The words of Mr. Katō Tsunetarō (加藤常次郞), the principal of the First High Common School, to the student representatives, which triggered the strike by the 4th-year students, revealed fully the mindset of school authorities backed by the modern power structure. That is, on June 3rd, when all the 4th-year students selected several representatives and petitioned Principal Katō to replace the Japanese language and chemistry teachers, the principal said, "While the students' situation is such, considering the teachers' side, it cannot be done." When the students asked, "Then are the students for the teachers, or are the teachers for the students?" Principal Katō, with a cold demeanor, retorted, "Indeed, the students are for the teachers. What are you going to do about it?" Thus, the students, swallowing their tears, resorted to the final measure of a strike. Now, how about that? Katō's words are the most honest confession. That's right. Today's schools are schools for the teachers. In other words, they are schools for the privileged class to which the teachers belong. They are schools meant to defend the existence of that class and devise its nurturing. If the discussion goes here, the explanation will become lengthy, so I will briefly end it here. However, we must first express a word of gratitude to Mr. Katō for stating the mindset of a modern powerful figure, a school authority, more boldly and honestly than anyone else. Furthermore, we can only praise Principal Katō's righteous act of visiting the police station and earnestly requesting the arrest of his own students.

At Sinsŏng School (信聖學校) in Sŏnch'ŏn, the 3rd-year students went on strike demanding the replacement of two American teachers (teaching geography and math) because they couldn't understand their explanations due to the teachers not knowing Korean. By the 16th, all were expelled. The students, in resentment, <15> went to the school principal and demanded, "Then give us back the admission guarantee deposit we paid when we enrolled," but he also stubbornly refused. Consequently, they resorted to direct action, damaging physics and chemistry lab equipment worth about 150 won, and 16 students were arrested by the local police. There had been a movement to upgrade this Sinsŏng School for some time, with some funds even arranged by some Korean teachers (part of the school authorities) and general parents outside the school. However, due to non-cooperation from the church side that backs the school (there must have been various reasons), it ultimately failed. The 5 or 6 teachers central to that movement resigned en masse last spring, so the school situation was unstable from the start. It is regrettable for the school that this incident has occurred now. However, since that school, too, exists not so much for the students but rather for a certain purpose related to spreading Christianity, it's likely they will proceed according to their established policy, whether the students find it bitter or sweet, or whether the student body shrinks to one or ten. However, what is regrettable not just for one school but for the entire church backing it – no, for the American missionaries – is that the fundraising by the Mission Headquarters of the Presbyterian Church in the U.S.A. (North) for missions in various places (spanning 27 countries) has in recent years fallen short of previous levels, eventually leading to a debt of 17 million won. This has forced the unavoidable measure of cutting mission expenses to various countries. As a result, the missionaries of the Northern Presbyterian Church sent to Korea also suffered a 20% salary cut starting last June. Perhaps the absolute refusal by the Sinsŏng School administration to replace the two American teachers was to help subsidize their living expenses in this way? If only the expelled students had foreseen this pitiable situation of the missionaries, they might have readily conceded the admission deposit, saying, "Just keep it." Things in the world always fail due to lack of honesty.

Another strike disturbance (盟騷動休) has occurred at Hwimun High Common School (徽文高等普校), and the issues this time are said to be considerably more serious than last year. It was this time last year. As the strike incident at the same school grew daily and public opinion was poised to swell, the school principal publicly announced, even through several newspapers at the time, that once this incident was resolved first, he would resign his position anyway. Now, looking at the main reasons for the renewed strike, the school authorities' breach of promise (食言問題) is one of them. Although we don't know the detailed circumstances, the dishonest and shameless attitude of the school authorities, trying to spit it out when it's bitter and swallow it when it's sweet, is extremely ugly no matter how favorably interpreted. <16> Furthermore, this time, they are flaunting a stubbornness towards students that anyone would find strange, saying, "Let's see who gives in first, you rascals or us." Does this not indicate a reactionary worsening of the school authorities? It's all quite unpleasant. Among these, another absurd thing is the issue of Principal Im's suppression of speech within the school, one of the conditions for this strike. That is, in a non-commercial magazine-like publication within the school, an article written by someone from the school with the meaning "The future belongs to us youth" was deleted under the reason of "disobedience to old morality." Though the incident is small, one cannot help but be surprised at such stubbornness in the minds of yesterday's intellectual class in today's Korea. Moreover, one cannot help but worry that this class's control (whether direct or indirect, spoken or unspoken, in their homes or in society) over new speech and new thought is more rigid than that of the Government-General's police authorities.
And internally, regarding the students involved in the strike, I have a word to say: (1) You currently attack the school authorities' lack of sincerity, inconsistency, being behind the times, despotism, and tyranny. But assuming you switch places and sit in their seats a few years from now, how well would you really do? – This is not said based on a prediction that you wouldn't do well. It's a suggestion to think thoroughly about this point at such a time and move on. (2) Things in the world are not fixed in a single day; they necessarily require time. In today's unfavorable environment, students—no, in our position—cannot act recklessly like trying to roast sweet potatoes over a bonfire. Thus, a certain degree of patient self-reflection is necessary. (3) Even with the same kind of strike, the fact that these incidents always occur right before the summer vacation, when the weather heats up every year, risks inviting suspicion from school authorities, fellow students' parents, and the general public. Seeing cartoons suggesting that "no-exam injections are the best prevention for student strikes" precisely conveys the news on this front. Whether such facts actually exist or not, it is, in any case, a point for students to deeply consider.

Exhibition of Society's Performance - Looking at the Statistics of All Prisoners in Korea Last Year
According to an investigation by the Prison Division of the Bureau of Justice at the end of May this year, the number of prisoners who received sentences and were incarcerated in the 29 main and branch prisons throughout Korea last year was: Japanese <17> 537, Koreans 8,298, Foreigners 143, totaling 8,970. Looking at the content of the crimes, theft leads with 3,207 cases, followed by fraud and embezzlement with 1,176, and then robbery. Special crimes like morphine and cocaine use increased compared to the previous year, while political crimes decreased to 100 (likely because Independence Group members soliciting military funds were all classified under robbery, fraud, or embezzlement). Observing the motives for crime, those due to financial gain numbered 2,929, poverty 728, and those due to illness have increased year by year (those due to habit numbered over 1,800). By age group, those between 30 and 40 were the most numerous, followed by those between 25 and 30, and then those between 20 and 25. In terms of sentence length, imprisonment for 6 months to less than 1 year numbered over 4,000, and 1 year to less than 3 years numbered over 2,000.
People, this is the world today. These are the products of today's world. Looking at the motives for crime, crimes due to financial gain, poverty, and illness are the most common. Looking at the age of criminals, able-bodied men between 30 and 40, an age when they should know the realities of the world, are the most numerous. Looking at the length of prison sentences, minor offenders sentenced to 6 months to 1 year are the most numerous – this is the world today. If we consider solving the economic problem here, how many criminals would likely remain? In the homes of the wealthy, grain rots, money rusts, clothes fade, and they engage in all sorts of debauchery day and night. Yet, for the propertyless people who struggle to survive day to day, there is no work even if they want it, and nothing remains even if they work. If those people quietly take even a single measure or a single penny of that rotting grain or money, it's theft; if they take it by force, it's robbery; if they take it through deception, it's embezzlement. What a truly well-made legal system! For whose sake? Are the people currently serving prison sentences the criminals, or are the propertied and powerful who forced these prisoners into committing theft and robbery the criminals? Truly, unless one is the god of creation (天地開闢), who could possibly know?

Twenty Million People as Goods for Sale at the Shop Counter - Words Beyond Words, Nothing More to Say
The Chōsen Bukkyō Taikai (朝鮮佛敎大會 - Korea Buddhist Association), run by a certain Japanese individual named Kobayashi, recently established a foundational juridical person (財團法人) to further propagate its existing Japan-Korea assimilationist doctrine. The promotional statement by Mr. Yi, the vice-chairman of the association, regarding this is truly spectacular. He said, "Christianity invests 3 million won annually in Korea to capture the spirit of the Korean people. To make the spirit of these 20 million Koreans return from Western-style Christianity to Japanese-style Buddhism, <18> we must secure at least 300,000 won annually from the main temples of various sects in Japan." So, does this mean the spirit of 20 million Koreans is sold to Western-made religion for 3 million won a year, and then sold again to Japanese-made Buddhism for 300,000 won a year, just being sold back and forth day and night? Words beyond words, no need to repeat them.

Increasing Imports of Manchurian Millet - What Does This Signify?
In April, the amount of Manchurian millet arriving within the jurisdiction of the P'yŏngyang Transport Office of the Kyŏngsŏng Railway Bureau was 4,335 tons, an approximate threefold increase compared to the same period last year. May saw about a 20% increase over April, and June is expected to increase by several tenths more than April. From January to May this year, the Manchurian millet arriving just at Sinanju Station reached 4,000 tons. This only refers to arrivals north of P'yŏngyang; if we calculate the Manchurian millet spread throughout Korea, the amount would be truly enormous. What is shocking amidst this is that the import volume of Manchurian millet increases year by year, regardless of the harvest conditions (豊凶) within Korea. The reasons for this increasing import are said to be twofold: (1) many of our people genuinely have nothing else to eat, and (2) although they might have rice to eat immediately, due to overall lack of means, they sell rice to buy and eat millet (粟). Whether eaten for this reason or that, is it not a solemn fact that Koreans are falling into an unspeakable predicament where they must buy more Manchurian millet each year?
I don't know what land improvement (토지개량) is, but the Government-General authorities talk about land improvement every year, investing huge budgets. This fiscal year's budget also includes 2.3 million won allocated for it initially. If we add the subsidies provided to Japanese-run irrigation associations in various places, the amount is indeed immense. However, what is the result of this yearly focus on increasing rice production? Is it anything other than feeding Japanese people rice (the so-called food policy of the Japanese Empire) and handing Koreans Manchurian millet? Alas! The Korean masses, unable to live in Korea, first drift to Manchuria, and then import Manchurian millet. Meanwhile, Japanese people immigrate to Korea and take away Korean rice. At that time, the proponents of the popular "cultivating real strength" (實力養成論) theory, who at least eat rice comfortably, say, "Even under these circumstances, one can survive if one just does well."
Look at these numbers. Regarding the consumption of agricultural products within Korea last year, rice consumption, compared to the total consumption of 11,344,962 *sŏk* (石) in 1921 (Taishō 10), or 6 *tu* 5 *sŭng* per person, <19> showed an increase in total consumption by 133,326 *sŏk*, but per capita consumption actually decreased to 6 *tu* 4 *sŭng* 4 *hop*. Millet consumption, compared to the total consumption of 5,950,922 *sŏk* in 1921, or 3 *tu* 4 *sŭng* 1 *hop* per person, increased to 6,242,225 *sŏk*, or 3 *tu* 5 *sŭng* 4 *hop* per person. These statistics include the nearly 400,000 Japanese residing in Korea, so they cannot be considered as clearly showing the consumption status of pure Koreans. However, it can be acknowledged that millet consumption increased while rice consumption decreased.

The More Cruel Fate of Slash-and-Burn Farmers (*Hwajŏnmin*) - Tenant Farmers on the Plains, *Hwajŏnmin* in the Mountains
Above, there was talk of Manchurian millet. However, those who substitute their diet even with Manchurian millet are, for the time being, managing to survive. But what about the propertyless masses wandering everywhere, cut off from ways to obtain even oat or potato porridge, let alone millet rice?
Regarding slash-and-burn farmers (*hwajŏnmin*, 火田民), there are likely more people who don't know about them than those who do. In today's society where the strong get stronger, the weak weaker, the rich richer, and the poor poorer, impoverished people unable to live on the plains, as a prelude to drifting to North Manchuria or Russian territory, first take a flintstone (燧石, commonly called *pusittol*) and just their bodies, go deep into the mountains, typically to high mountains or plateaus of 3,000 to 4,000 feet. There they cut down trees, set fires, and plant things like potatoes and oats, thereby sustaining their lives. In other words, until recently, they had no laws, no society, only living their own unique primitive life in the natural primeval forest with a single flintstone. However, in recent years, the administrative authorities, under the pretext of cultivating water sources, have forbidden all setting of fires in mountains and fields. Those whose entire lives were governed by a single flintstone now find themselves unable to go elsewhere due to lack of capital, and unable to continue as before because current laws forbid it. They are now in a situation where they must fold their hands and starve to death.
Some months ago, someone went to investigate the living conditions of *hwajŏnmin* in the Kanggye region of North P'yŏngan Province. Let me introduce one fact he reported upon returning:
(Going about 200 *ri* into the mountains from the plains where ordinary people live, into a valley with no paths for passage and no sign of human habitation, *hwajŏnmin* lived sparsely, one house every 5 *ri*, one every 10 *ri*. Finding one house, there was no one else there (perhaps gone to dig for roots), only an old man lying sick. When asked, "What illness makes you suffer so?" the sick man replied, "There's nothing to eat, but I just don't die. So I got some millet taffy (조ㅅ계 - 粟糖) and added Solomon's seal root (둥구레 - 黃精), made porridge, and ate it. Now my bowels are blocked, so I'm lying here." Hearing this, the visitor said, "Then, I have medicine for diarrhea. Take this and get well soon." At this, the old man shed tears and said, "I am grateful that you would cure such an illness, but if I take this medicine, recover, and sit up, what then? (Since there is nothing to eat)" and refused to accept the medicine.) <20>
This is roughly their situation. Yet, such *hwajŏnmin*, pushed to the brink of death, exist practically everywhere in Korea. Not only in North and South P'yŏngan and North and South Hamgyŏng provinces, but also in Kangwŏn, Hwanghae, and North Kyŏngsang provinces. Their households number no fewer than 100,000.
The Government-General authorities, on one hand, are constantly driving them out, and on the other, speak of their relief, claiming to alleviate the immediate crisis through resettlement onto plains and encouragement of side jobs. Of course, if there is land available for relocation to the plains, that would be best, but talk of encouraging side jobs is utterly useless. To thoroughly relieve them, one must fundamentally solve the social problem and wait for the construction of a new society that does not manufacture the propertyless on the plains. The best measure possible under the current system is to lease them their customary slash-and-burn fields (*yŏn'go hwajŏn*), restrict only new slash-and-burn activities, and otherwise leave them alone as before. To make a multitude of lives unlivable now for the sake of future issues like forest management (治山) or flood control (治水) seems an approach even the authorities should not take.
The tenant farmer problem on the plains, the slash-and-burn farmer problem in the mountains – these two problems are the most critical and most urgent issues in Korea today. Such problems must be resolved from the very root. (This *hwajŏn* issue will be addressed again in a more concrete article depending on circumstances.)

Noteworthy Japanese Youth Groups - What is the Recent Situation of Korean Youth Groups?
When we hear "Japanese youth groups" (日本人 青年團), we immediately think of the deaths and injuries of our compatriots during the Great Kantō Earthquake last September. Thinking of that event, we still don't know what to say. Immediately after the earthquake last year, Japanese residents in Korea, contemplating what their own kin had done and fearing some kind of reaction from Koreans, were like startled deer, attempting to organize things like self-defense corps (自衛團) and youth groups (靑年團). This wasn't in some remote region; it happened right here at the foot of Namsan in Seoul. But recently, perhaps due to the noticeable social activism from the Korean youth side, or perhaps due to an increase in the strength of the Japanese side, there is a trend among Japanese youth residing in Korea busily forming various kinds of associations like youth groups (靑年團), training groups (修養團), and purification groups (洗足團). Recently, youth groups were formed in Chongŭp and Yŏngil counties, which Japanese newspapers reported with large headlines. <21>
Of course, whatever gatherings they hold are for their own solidarity and development, and we harbor no particular resentment towards them for this. What comes to mind, however, is how much progress our Korean youth solidarity movement has made recently. Of course, last spring, there was the Youth General Alliance (靑年總同盟) movement, which promised to clarify its direction and invigorate its momentum. However, today, when the old-style youth associations are collapsing and the new-style ones have yet to mature, I wish to diagnose the health of youth associations in various regions, and at the same time, I especially wish for the further efforts of the Youth General Alliance committee members in Kyŏngsŏng. No matter what, the primary issue is the solidity of our foundation, the perfection of our cells.

Who Are the Fundamental Masses of Korea? - Thoughts on the Issue of Poor People Migrating to Japan
Putting aside earlier times, from January to August last year, the number of Korean laborers entering Japan averaged 10,492 per month. However, after the earthquake last September, the Japanese Ministry of Home Affairs almost completely banned the passage of Korean laborers. This became a major threat to the approximately 70,000 laborers who had temporarily returned due to the persecution during the earthquake and those newly seeking to go to Japan. For a time, an unstable situation arose, such as 3,000 laborers seeking passage converging on the Pusan docks. Then, on June 1st, the Japanese Ministry of Home Affairs lifted all the restrictions that had been in place, allowing free passage as before September last year. What is strange, however, is that this time, the Korean Government-General authorities issued directives to each province to restrict passage, as before, for those whose place of employment is unclear. While this is not, of course, a formal law or decree, it certainly possesses practical enforcement power greater than formal laws.
According to one Government-General official, "In the rural villages of various parts of Korea today, there is land and capital, but a shortage of labor is felt, and voices lamenting rural decline are loud. Despite this, insisting only on the lifting of restrictions is extremely regrettable," thus promoting the disadvantages of Korean laborers migrating to Japan. However, since the migration of Korean proletarians to Japan in recent years was not based on anyone's propaganda but was a forced necessity arising from their own miserable circumstances, it is clear that their decision to go or not go to Japan cannot now be swayed by any particular propaganda.
The speaker, as noted above, mentions that there is land and capital in Korean rural villages, but even if there is land and capital, what concern is it to those who possess nothing but their emaciated <22> bodies?
The Korean middle class, or the Japanese authorities, observing the propertyless farmers currently muddling through in the villages – meaning those tenanting a few plots of someone else's land or working as hired hands in others' homes – might lightly observe, "Whatever the case, they must be managing to live somehow, that's why they carry on like that." But in reality, their situation is not that they manage because they can, but rather because they have no other choice; in other words, they endure that much because they cannot simply die. If they had even a slightly better way to live, they would immediately tie their shoes and run towards that path. That is, the lives and feelings of the poor (*semin*) in our rural villages are in such an unstable state. Attempting to forcibly restrict their dispersal while affirming the current situation as it is, is exactly like trying to prohibit water from flowing downwards.
For those who cannot survive day to day in Korea, going first to Japan to eat rice or to North Manchuria to eat millet porridge is indeed a temporary measure to quench their thirst. For now, there is no other way. However, if the 99 out of 100 Korean masses are merely to end up with such a wandering fate – in other words, if our people merely adopt an attitude of indifference towards this – what will ultimately become of the fate of the Korean masses, and where will the roots of the "Korea" we speak of day and night attach themselves?
Those who receive blessings in the present – these are generally those who compromise with the present world, and furthermore, those who exploit the injustices of the present world to gain unjust profits. Therefore, looking at our Korea, the faction currently receiving blessings is not, in a strict sense, the Korean masses. The true Korean masses, the fundamental Korean masses, are those people who cannot eat or dress properly in today's society. In other words, they are those who go to Japan, North Manchuria, and the Russian territory, and even if they haven't gone, those who are tasting the same degree of misery as those who do go. Are there wealthy people among us? If so, they should pack their clothes and provisions as they are and bolster their vitality (if it's land, abandon it). Are there people with will among us? If so, they should naturally breathe together with them and infuse new air. Is the place they exist Japan proper, or North Manchuria? Then we should follow them there. Is the place where even more exist the various rural villages of Korea? Then we should participate there. Let's take everything and go to the poor (*semin*). Without looking up at the blessed ones in this world even slightly, let us descend solely towards the suffering people. Let's resolve to build a new order centered on them. <23>
It seems this is the only path for us to take. Although it feels like rambling at a crossroads, I've written it down as it came to mind.

Suppression of Speech and Assembly - If a Water Jar Breaks, Many People Will Surely Be Hurt
It was the 7th of last month. Initiated by the Korean Labor Federation (朝鮮勞働總同盟) and the Youth General Alliance (靑年總同盟), 25 organizations based in Seoul gathered to form the Committee to Denounce the Suppression of Speech and Assembly (言論集會 壓迫彈劾會). They resolved to actively resist the authorities' unreasonable pressure on speech and assembly with firm solidarity, selected 13 executive committee members, and are currently investigating the reality of the unreasonable suppression. They plan to subsequently hold a citizens' rally to urge the righteous uprising of the populace.
As we see it, the Korean Government-General authorities, somewhat imbued with a new atmosphere after the March First Movement (己未運動), perceived that the tendency of most Koreans within the territory at that time leaned towards the cultural movement, or in other words, the principle of cultivating real strength (實力養成主義). They assessed that there would be no significant changes in the near future. Also, considering it themselves, having newly taken charge replacing the old figures, they felt a need to devise something new for their own track record, and doing so would practically serve as a policy to appease the knowledgeable class among Koreans. Thus, they permitted several newspapers and magazines and allowed assembly and association to a certain extent, watching how things developed. However, the majority of Koreans soon realized how minimal or incomplete the results of the cultural movement and cultivation of real strength under these circumstances were, and resolved to take a new step once again. The social movement, which the authorities had until some point believed would not go beyond the propaganda of a certain vagrant faction, gained prominence almost generally since the year before last and last year. At this, the authorities abruptly changed their attitude and began employing active measures of coercion against it. This can be seen as a policy that materialized roughly between the winter of the year before last and July of last year. Amidst this progression, the Great Kantō Earthquake occurred last September. Setting aside the deaths and injuries of over 60,000 people, one-fourth of the national wealth was destroyed. Not only did people other than Japanese come to view Japan's prestige lightly, but the Japanese people themselves developed a stronger sense of self-deprecation regarding it. Subsequently, as American exclusion of Japanese became a reality, their nerves, especially those governing the colonies, became hypersensitive. Although the movement on the Korean side cannot be said to have gained momentum due to this, there was certainly no reason for it to diminish. Consequently, they <24> became truly red-eyed, wary of every single move of the Koreans, leading to the present day where they apply as much pressure as possible on speech, assembly, and association – the sole indicators of those moves. On the occasion of the birth of this Denunciation Committee, we have a word for both the authorities and the general public.
Government officials tend to think that changes in general thought or social unrest are caused by the propaganda of a few people or a few organizations. However, the current changes in the thoughts and actions of Koreans are due to the instigation of their own circumstances or the prompting of global trends – in other words, they are driven by the spirit of world transformation. This should rather be guided well (a phrase always used by the official side), but it cannot be suppressed unreasonably by force. If suppressed, "水甕而潰 傷人必多" (If a water jar breaks, many people will surely be hurt) – the water will eventually go where it must, and only people will be harmed. Therefore, even short-sighted and nervous authorities should acknowledge this sequence of events.
Next, looking at it from the perspective of the organizers of the Denunciation Committee, rather than aiming for some immediate result, their intention likely lies in reporting the reality of the oppression to the general public along with tears of blood, thereby raising public opinion, while simultaneously solidifying their own unity to establish the high principle of "威武不屈" (unyielding to power and force). If that is the case, instead of just presenting the realistically investigated, unadorned facts once at a citizens' rally or distributing them once in some document, given that there are 25 base organizations, there seems a need to thoroughly instill the meaning of this denunciation into each member of those organizations again and again. This is something the organizers likely thought of before us, but I mention it in passing. Finally, addressing the general public specifically:

For instance, when newspapers and magazines are confiscated daily or monthly, and assemblies are absolutely forbidden, as has been happening recently, the official side, or media outlets directly or indirectly run by the official side, twist this around and spread propaganda claiming that newspaper and magazine publishers do so for their business strategy, and speakers at assemblies actually use police warnings or eventual detention as self-advertisement – extremely vile slander.
Regarding this propaganda, anyone who is Korean, putting everything else aside, <25> can surely understand better than anyone the suffering endured by Koreans at the forefront of speech and assembly, simply by inferring from their own <25> circumstances and thoughts. Thus, there is no need for further explanation. However, it seems that some people, particularly in the provinces, who may not fully grasp the reality of the media world, seem to hold thoughts like this: "Since they are already publishing or assembling under that person's rule, they should try to go in two days the distance they could cover in one, or discuss in two sessions what could be done in one, even if it means taking longer, to avoid the recent kind of persecution..." They harbor such concerns.

Indeed, it is a concern that should be felt. However, those who worry about this should first consider this: "We who actually suffer such oppression have even greater concerns about that aspect than you do." Alas! When the awakening of the masses is still incomplete and the actions of those in power are as they are, how could we possibly act recklessly, losing moderation and rushing towards achievement? How could we do such things for our own business strategy? To put it plainly, what I want to request further from the general public is that the development of speech can never progress separately from the support of the masses. In this regard, I earnestly seek the direct support of the general public for the emergence of this Denunciation Committee. <26>

**Summary**

This article from the July 1924 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine paints a bleak picture of Korea under Japanese rule, titled "The Sufferings of Korea." It details increased border activity by independence groups, the dire economic situation forcing poor farmers and laborers into migration (to Manchuria, Japan) or extreme poverty (*hwajŏnmin* resorting to eating clay), and the rising cost of living reflected in increased millet consumption over rice. It reports on numerous student strikes against perceived unjust or incompetent school authorities (both Japanese and American missionary) and criticizes the schools' focus on institutional goals over student welfare. The piece strongly condemns the intensifying suppression of Korean speech and assembly by the colonial government, linking it to post-earthquake anxieties in Japan and US anti-Japanese sentiment. It uses crime statistics to highlight poverty as a root cause and criticizes attempts at forced assimilation through promoting Japanese Buddhism. The author calls for solidarity with the suffering masses (farmers, laborers, *hwajŏnmin*) as the true foundation of Korea and urges support for the newly formed committee resisting media suppression.

From a historical perspective, this article is valuable for its contemporary account of the multifaceted struggles faced by Koreans in 1924. It provides specific details on independence movement actions, the harsh realities of *hwajŏnmin* life, the dynamics of student resistance (including specific grievances and principal's quotes), the economic pressures pushing migration, and the tightening grip of censorship. Historians would find the direct commentary on colonial policies (land improvement resulting in rice export and millet import, suppression tactics), social issues (Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, Chinese labor), and the critique of both Japanese and missionary education systems particularly insightful for understanding the lived experience and burgeoning resistance movements of the period. The article's explicit identification of the impoverished rural and urban populace as the "fundamental masses" reflects the growing influence of socialist and nationalist thought.

學生同盟休學의 一揆는 도처에 蜂起하야 학생과 당국자의 간에는 日米문제 이상의 難關들이  伏在한 중 第一高普 4학년생의 同盟休를 誘致한 同校校長 加藤常次郞君의 학생대표에 대한 言辭는 현대의 권력조직을 배경으로 한 학교 당국자의 심정을 유감업시 폭로하엿다. 즉 6월 3일에 同校 4학년생 일동이 日語 及 化學 敎授의 兩人을 갈어 달나고 대표 몃명을 선출하야 加藤校長에게 진정한즉, 교장의 말이 「학생의 사정은 그러하나 선생便의 관계를 도라보면 그리할 수 업다」함으로 학생편에서는 「그러면 선생을 위하야의 학생임니까, 또는 학생을 위하야의 선생임니까」하매 加藤교장은 冷然한 作態로써 「그러치 선생을 위하야의 학생이다. 엇절 터이냐」하고 反問함으로, 학생측에서는 울음을 먹음고 盟休의 최후의 수단을 취햇다는 말이다. 자- 엇더냐, 加藤의 말이 가쟝 솔직한 고백이다. 그러타. 오늘의 학교는 선생을 위하야서의 학교이다. 다시 말하면 선생 그 級이 隷屬해 잇는 特權級族을 위하야서의 학교이다. 그 級族의 존재를 변호하고 그 級族의 長養을 꾀하기 위하야서의 학교이다. 말이 여긔에 오면 해설이 길어지겟슴으로 간단히 이에 끗치거니와 우리는 위선 근대식 유권자의 一인 校당국자의 심정을 누구보다도 대담 且 솔직히 言明해 준 加藤君에게 대하야 一言의 謝意를 표해 두며 더욱히 加藤 교장이 警署를 委訪하야 自校학생 拘引을 懇請한 義擧를 장하다 할뿐이다.

宣川信聖學校 3학년생이 지리와 수학을 교수하는 두 米人 교수(朝鮮語를 몰음으로 그 설명을 알어들을 수가 업는 故)를 갈어 달나고 동맹휴학을 햇는대 지난 16일까지 전부 퇴학 처분이 되엿슴으로 학생일동은 忿恨한 나마에＜15＞ 그 校 교장에게 몰녀 가 「그러면 입학할 때에 내인 입학보증금이나 도로 달나」한즉 그 亦 완강히 거절함으로 仍히 직접행동에 出하야 理化學 실험기구 150원 가량의 値를 파손하고 학생 16명이 그곳 경찰서에 검거되엿다고  한다. 이 信聖學校에는 언제부터 학교 昇格運動이 잇서 校당국자의 일부인 朝鮮人선생과 校外 일반 학부형측으로부터 얼마간의 기본금 주선까지 잇섯스나 이 학교의 배경이 된 敎會便의 不應酬(거긔에는 여러 가지 사정이 잇섯겟지)로 결국 실패에 歸하매 그 운동의 중심이 되던 敎員 5, 6인은 客春으로 連袂辭退한 일이 잇는 등 校情은 처음부터 불안함이 잇던 중, 이번에 이 일이 또 생기게 됨은 그 학교를 위하야 섭섭한 바이다. 그러나 그 학교-역시 학생을 위하야 존재한 것이라 하기보다, 基督敎 宣布를 위한 엇던 목적에 잇는 것이 사실인 이상, 학생이야 쓰다 하던지 달다 하던지 또는 單 한 명이 되던지 열 명이 되든지 旣定의 방침대로 진행할 것이겟지. 그러나 한 개의 학교보다도 그 학교의 배경이 된 全 敎會-아니 米人선교사측을 위하야 섭섭한 것은 米國 北長老敎會 宣敎本部의 각지(27개국에 亘한) 선교를 위한 醵金이 近年에 니르러는 만히 前과 갓지 못하야 결국 1,700만원이라는 債務를 지게 되야 각국에 대한 선교비를 削减하는 不得己에 至한 바 朝鮮에 派送된 北長老敎會의 선교사도 그 영향으로 작년 6월부터 종래의 봉급에서 2할 삭감을 당하엿다는 그것이다. 이번 信聖校 경 영자로부터 두 米人의 교사의 更迭을 絶對不許함도 그러케 그러케 하야 그 兩人의 생활비를 보조해 주자 함은 아닌지. 만일 퇴학된 학생들도 선교사편의 이러한 可憐을 豫知하엿더면 입학보증금쯤은 그대로 당신네 잡수시오 하고 순순히 양보하엿슬넌지도 몰을 것을. 세상일은 언제던지 솔직하지 못해서 실패하것다.

微文高等普校에 또 盟騷動休이 생겨 이번은 작년보다 문제가 자못 험악하다고 한다. 작년 이때이다. 同校의 盟休事件이 날로 확대되야, 사회의 여론이 자못 커지려 할 때에, 그 학교 교장으로부터 이 사건만 鮮決되면 자기는 여하간 그 자리를 사퇴하겟다 한 것은 당시의 몃종 신문지를 통하야서까지 公公然 발표된 바이다. 이제 또 盟休된 이유의 주요한 점을 보면 校당국의 食言問題가 그 하나인즉 그 간의 情實 如何는 우리가 모른다 할지라도 엇잿던 쓸 때엔 吐하려 하고 달 때엔 생키려 하는 校당국자의 不率直 無廉隅한 태도는 아모리 好意로써 해석할지라도 醜陋莫甚한 일이며＜16＞ 더욱히 금번에 잇서는 누가 드러도 이상하게 생각하게까지 對학생 태도의 완강을 標榜하야 「너-놈들이 지나 우리가 지나 해보려면 해보자」하는 것은 결국 校당국자의 反動的 惡化를 표시함은 아닌지, 모도가 滋味가 업스며 그 중에도 또 한 가지 맹낭한 것은 今回 盟休條件의 하나인 任校長의 校內 언론압박의 件이다. 즉 그 학교 내에 발행하는 非賣品의 雜誌 비슷한 것이 잇는대 거긔에 校中人의 누구가 쓴 「장래는 우리 청년의 것이다」하는 의미의 文은 「舊 道德에 대한 不順」이란 이유 밋헤서 삭제가 되엿다는 그것이다. 일이 비록 적으나 오늘 朝鮮에 잇는 昨日 지식계급의 머리가 그러탓 頑冥한 데에는 놀나지 아늘 수가 업스며 더욱 그 級 사람들의 새 언론 새 사상에 대한 取締(말하면 자기 가정에 잇서 사회에 잇서 직접 간접 또는 有言 無言의 중에서)가 總督府 警務當局 이상으로 頑冥한 것을 걱정하지 아늘 수가 업다.
그리고 내종으로 盟休에 관계된 학생 편에 대하야서 一言할 것은, (1) 그대들이 지금은 그와 가티 校당국자의 無誠意 不徹底 時代遲 專斷 橫暴를 공격하나 이제 몃 해 후 땅을 밧구어 그대네들이 그 자리에 드러 안는다 가정하면 그대들은 과연 얼마나 잘 하겟느냐 하는 그것-다. 잘 하지 못하리라는 豫斷을 내리고 십퍼하는 말이 아니라. 이러한 때에 거긔에 대한 생각을 한번 철저히 하고 넘어가라 하는 말이며 (2) 세상일이란 하루 아츰에 곳처지는 것이 아니오 반다시 時日이란 것을 要하는 것인즉 모든 환경이 이롭지 못한 오늘, 학생 아니 우리의 처지에 잇서 그저 장작불로 감로 굽도록 함부로 할 수가 업는 것인즉 어느 정도까지 隱忍自省의 노력이 필요하며 (3) 가튼 盟休이라도 그 사건이 每樣炎天이 되여 오는 夏季放學을 임시해서 생기는 것은 校당국자 及 學友父兄은 물론 일반의 致疑를 바들 위험이 잇다는 그것이다. 學生盟休의 예방에는 無試驗 注射가 제일이라는 漫畵를 보게 됨과 가틈은 正히 이 편의 소식을 전하는 것인 바, 실제에 그런 사실이 잇던 업던 여하간 학생들은 깁히 생각하여 볼 점의 하나라고 할 수 잇다.

朝鮮의 中産階級이나 또는 日本人 당국자는 현재의 농촌에서 꾸물거리고 지내는 無産農民 말하면 남의 땅 몃 마직이 소작하고 지내는 사람 또는 남의 집에서 머슴노릇을 하고 잇는 그 사람들이 爲先 그럭저럭 지내오닛가 「이러니 저러니 해도 자기들은 모다 살 수가 잇서서 그러케 지내오는 것이겟지」하는 輕輕한 관찰을 할는지 모르나 사실 그들의 사정을 말하면 지낼 형편이 되어서 지내는 것이 아니라 엇지엇지 할 수 업서서 말하면 죽지 못해서 그만큼 참어 지내는 것 뿐이라. 만일 그들에게 조곰이라도 그보다 낫게 지낼 도리가 잇다고만 하면 그들은 곳 그 길로 신발 매고 다라날 것이다. 즉 우리 농촌에는 細民의 생활은 심정은 이와 가튼 불안정한 상태에 잇는 바 오늘의 현상을 이대로 是認하면서 그들의 離散을 억지로 제한하려 함과 如함은 正히 水의 就下를 禁禦하려는 것과 一般일 것이다.
朝鮮 잇스면 朝夕을 못하는 그들이 爲先 日本으로 가서 쌀밥이라도 먹고 北滿으로 가서 조밥이라도 먹는 것 그 亦 一時의 解渴策이 아님이 아니다. 爲先 그밧게 수가 업다. 그러나 朝鮮민중의 100의 99인 져들이 뎌와 가티 漂泊의 운명에 終始할 뿐이라 하면 다시 말하면 우리의 사람사람이 여긔에 대해서 그져 방임하는 태도를 가질 뿐이라 하면 朝鮮민중의 운명이란 결국 엇지되며 우리가 밤낫으로 말하는 朝鮮이란 그것의 뿌리는 어대에 依接할 것인고.
현재에 福을 밧는 者 이 者는 대체로 現世와 타협하며 나아가 現世의 非理를 仍用하야 不當利益을 엇는 者들이다. 따라서 우리 朝鮮을 두고 볼지라도 현재에 복을 밧고 잇는 派는 嚴正한 의미에서 朝鮮의 민중이 아니다. 진정한 朝鮮의 민중은 朝鮮의 기본 민중은 오늘이 사회에서 못 먹고 못 입고 사러가는 그 사람들이다. 말하면 日本에 가고 北滿에 가고 露領에 가고 비록 가지는 아니하엿슬지라도 그 가는 사람과 가튼 정도의 비참을 갓추 맛보고 잇는 그들이다. 우리에게 돈이 잇는 사람이 잇느냐. 잇스면 잇는 그대로 그의 衣糧을 싸주어 그의 元氣를 보태줄 것이오(土地이면 抛棄하고) 뜻이 잇는 사람이 잇느냐. 잇스면 맛당히 그들로 더브러 호흡을 통하야 새로운 공기를 불어 줄 것 뿐이다. 그들이 잇는 곳이 日本 內地이냐 또는 北滿이냐. 그러면 거긔까지 따라갈 것이오. 그보다 더 만히 잇는 곳이 朝鮮의 각 농촌이냐 그러먼 거긔에 入叅할 것이다. 모든 것을 가지고 細民에게 가자. 조곰일지라도 이 세상에서 복밧는 놈을 처다 볼 것이 업시 오로지 苦痛밧는 사람을 향하야 내려서자. 그래서 그들을 本位로 한 새 판국을 짓기로 하쟈.＜23＞
우리의 취할 한 가지의 길은 이것일 뿐인가 한다. 만이 岐路에 드른 感이 잇스나 생각난 김에 그대로 써 노은 것뿐이다.

言論集會 壓迫- 水甕而潰 傷人必多
지나간 7일이다. 朝鮮勞働總同盟과 靑年總同盟의 發起로 서울에 잇는 25개의 단체가 會合하야 言論集會 壓迫彈劾會를 조직하고 언론 及 집회에 대한 당국의 無理한 압박에 대하야 鞏固한 결속으로써 적극적으로 抗拒할 件을 결의하고 실행위원 13인을 선정하야 方히 無理壓迫의 실상을 조사하며 니여써 시민대회를 열어 義烈잇는 민중의 奮起를 促하리라 한다.
우리의 눈으로써 보건대 己未運動 이후 적이 새로운 공기로써 조성된 朝鮮總督府 당국은 당시 境內에 잇는 朝鮮사람의 대부분 경향이 文化運動 달니 말하면 實力養成主義에 귀의하야 갓가운 장래에는 무슨 현저한 變異가 업슬 것을 看取하엿스며 또는 자기네가 스사로 생각할지라도 넷사람을 대신하야 새로히 板局을 만들고 안즌 이상 자기성적을 위하야서라도 전에 업든 무슨 새로운 일을 꾸밀 필요가 잇고 또 그러케 함이 실제로 朝鮮사람의 識者級을 懷柔하는 策이 되리라 하야 몃 種의 신문 잡지를 허가하여 어느 정도까지의 集會 結社를 許하야 그 되여 가는 바를 주시하고 잇든 바 朝鮮사람의 多數는 어느덧 이 현상 밋헤서의 文化運動 實力養成의 성과가 얼마나 微微 또는 불철저한 것을 깨닷고 다시 한번 새로운 걸음을 옴기기로 하매 어느 때까지의 당국은 一種 浮浪派流의 宣傳감에서 지내지 못하리라고 밋던 사회운동이 재작년 작년 이래에 거의 일반적으로 擡頭하게 된즉 당국은 於是乎 태도를 一變하야 이에 대한 적극적 威壓策을 쓰게 되엿스니 이것은 대개 재작년 冬으로브터 작년 7월까지의 間에 亘하야 구체화한 방침이라 볼 수가 잇다. 그래 오던 중 작년 9월의 大震災가 起하며 6만여의 인명의 死傷은 姑舍하고 全國富의 4분1이 燒蕩되메 日本人 이외의 사람이 日本의 威信을 輕視한다기보다 日本사람 자신이 한층 더 그에 대한 自癖의 감정을 가지게 되며 니여써 米國의 排日이 事實化하매 그들 특히 식민지를 지배하고 잇는 그들의 신경이 예민하게 된 중 朝鮮사람측의 운동이 역시 그로하야 기세가 을낫다고 할 수는 업스나 줄을 理는 만무한즉 이에써 그들은＜24＞ 정말 赤眼이 되야써 朝鮮사람의 一動一靜을 경계하게 되 며 그 動靜의 유일한 표시인 언론집회결사에 대하야 할 수 잇는 대로의 압박을 加하는 今日을 馴致하엿다. 그런대 우리는 이 彈劾會의 출생을 際하야 당국자 及 일반민중에게 一言할 것이 잇다.
관청 당국자는 일반 思想의 변동 혹은 사회의 動搖가 몃 사람 혹 몃 기관의 선전에 因함인 것과 가티 생각하나 지금 朝鮮사람의 思想 及 행위의 변동은 자기가 가진 환경의 敎唆 또는 세계 大勢의 使嗾 달니 말하면 세계 改變의 기운의 所使로 그러함인 바 이를 차라리 善導(官邊에 항상 쓰는 말)할지언정 無理로 抑遏할 수 업는 것이며 만일 抑遏한다 하면 水甕而潰 傷人必多로 물은 결국 갈대로 가고 사람만 傷할 것인즉 아모리 近視眼이오 신경질인 당국이라 할지라도 이런 經緯쯤은 是認할 필요가 잇슬지며
다음 彈劾會의 執事者편으로 보면 이번 이 일을 반다시 目前의 엇던 성과를 보기 위하야 햇다 하기보다도 일반 민중에게 압박의 실제가 如許한 것을 피눈물과 아울너 報明하야 세상의 여론을 놉게 하는 동시에 한 편으로 자체의 결속을 굿건히 하야 威武不屈의 高義를 수립하기 위함에 잇섯슬 듯 십다. 만일 그러타 할진대 그 實狀스럽게 조사한 꾸밈 업는 사실을 한번 시민대회에 훌날녀 보내거나 또는 엇던 文書로써 한번 撒布하고 말을 뿐이 아니라 25개라는 기본단체가 잇슨즉 그 단체들의 成員 각자에게 이 탄핵의 意義로 다시금다시금 철저케 할 필요가 잇슬가 한다. 이것은 우리에 先하야 執事者가 몬저 생각햇슬 일이나 말하는 참이니 하는 것이고 맨 내종으로 일반 민중에 專委해서 한 말 할 것은

가령 근래와 가티 신문 잡지 가튼 것이 連日 또는 連月해서 압수되고 집회가 절대로 不許되면 官邊 또는 官邊에 直營 혹은 間營하는 機關紙 가튼 것은 이를 뒤잡아 선전하되 신문 잡지와 가튼 것은 경영자가 그 經營策을 위하야 그리하며 집회의 演士는 警官의 주의나 또는 결국에 拘囚될 것을 도로혀 自家의 광고로 삼아 그리함이라는 極 野薄한 中傷의 선전을 하고 잇다.
이번 선전에 대하야는 적어도 朝鮮사람된 이는 다른 것은 다 그만 둔다 할지라도 각각 자기가 밧고 당하고 잇는＜25＞ 處地 心思로써 추측하야 언론 집회의 衝에 當한 朝鮮사람으로서의 당하는 바 苦衷이 如何할 것을 무엇보다 잘 알 것이니까 다시 辨說할 것이 업거니와 대개 지방에 엇서 言論界의 실정을 밋처 洞悉하지 못하는 일부의 사람은 생각권대 이런 생각쯤은 가지고 잇는 듯 십다. 즉 『旣히 저 사람 治下에서 刊行 또는 集會하는 것임을 각오하고 하는 이상 될 수 잇는 대로는 하루에 갈 길을 잇틀에 가고 한 번에 할 것을 두 번에 논하 할지라도 近來와 가튼 迫害는 아니밧도록 하엿스면 됴흘 것인대...』하는 우려를 가지고 잇다.

과연, 하여야 할 우려이다. 그러나 이것을 우려하는 이는 몬저 이것만은 생각해 주어야 할 것이 잇다. 즉 「실제로 그런 압박을 당하는 우리는 여러분보다 일층 더 그 편에 대한 우려를 가젓다」는 그것을 생각해 주어야 할 것이다. 슬프다. 민중의 覺醒이 오히려 철저치 못하고 執權者의 행사가 如今한데 우리가 엇지 생각업시 절도를 일코 事功을 급히 하려는 無謀에 出할 수가 잇슬가. 하믈며 이런 일을 自家 營業策으로 한다고 함이랴. 다 못한 말로써 더 일반에게 부탁할 것은 언론의 발전 그것이 결코 민중의 옹호를 떠나서 따로히 발전될 수 업다 하는 그것이다. 이 점에 잇서 이번 彈劾會의 출생에 대한 일반민중의 직접 옹호를 구하야 마지 안는 바이다.＜26＞
