天玄地黃 (Heaven is Black, Earth is Yellow)

A while ago, a certain Japanese wealthy merchant in Chinju, whose name was Ko something, became an issue across the Kyŏngnam area for cheating on his scales and stealing from the pockets of the common people. Now, a Japanese member of the Kunsan Rice Cooperative, colluding with a certain Japanese inspector from his own community, has shipped out substandard rice marked with false inspection seals, supposedly having a major impact on the rice trade throughout all of Chosŏn. ▲ Not only this, but recently a significant issue is said to have arisen centered around a certain brokerage house at the Inch'ŏn Rice and Bean Exchange. ▲ It seems only natural that those fellows who got stuck together through fraud (挾雜) will meet their downfall through fraud.

▲ They say the Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (京城府) decided to build its government office on the former site of the Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo Company on T'aep'yŏngt'ong, with the Prefecture bearing all costs for relocating the old building. ▲ However, the Prefecture forgot to include the removal of the concrete (콩크릿트) foundation work in the conditions for relocating the old house (舊家瓦), making it unavoidable to deal with this anew, ultimately leading to a budget shortfall. So, the bureaucrats cause the problem, and the citizens of the Prefecture bear the entire loss. How pitiful (寒心也)!

▲ Last month, at the Tamok Farm in Hamyŏl, Chŏnbuk province, they held a commendation ceremony, quite grandly, for 600 outstanding tenant farmers selected from the 1,800 tenants. With the farm owner's address and shouts of "Long live His Majesty the Emperor!" (聖壽萬歲), it truly exemplified the grand occasions of the current age. Playing around nicely on someone else's land with someone else's property.

▲ If Japanese-Russian recognition (日露承認) leads to mutual exchange (互交), various new phenomena will arise, but the most refreshing is the realization of the Eurasian railway connection (歐亞鐵道聯絡). According to railway authorities, if Japanese-Russian diplomatic relations (國交) are restored, the Eurasian railway connection will begin immediately. Starting from Pusan, it seems likely one or two direct trains per week will depart. If so, one could reach Moscow (莫斯科) in 7 days and Berlin (伯林) in 10 days. In any case, times are gradually getting better (됴와오는 셈).

▲ With the emergence of the Kato cabinet in Japan, it must have crossed everyone's mind at least once that there might be some new reshuffle among the major backers (大株主측) of the Government-General of Korea (朝鮮總督府). ▲ However, if the current situation facing the Japanese Empire allowed enough leeway to consider such colonial problems first, that would be truly splendid.

▲ Regarding the "Portraits of Kyŏngsŏng People" (京城人物百態) published in this magazine's previous Kyŏngsŏng Issue (京城號), there seems to be quite a lot of talk about it outside, this way and that. ▲ Well yes, it was written to become a topic of conversation, so how could there not be talk? However, if one interprets too deeply (깁게 解釋) and meticulously (천착스럽게) what was written as just a story (니악이로) to laugh over and pass by, that would be different from the writer's true intention (本意). Shh, shh.

▲ The various chapels of the Christian Church (基督敎會) in Kwangju city proper have decided to use the upcoming summer vacation (夏休) to educate the city's proletarian children (無産兒童). Indeed, it is a good (됴흔) new experiment. The only hope is that they approach it with a sincere heart (赤心) solely for the children's benefit, without adding the proselytizing flavor (傳道式 臭味) of making them seek God the Father from one to a hundred.

▲ Has the discord (不和) that arose between the citizens of Pukch'ŏng and Hamhŭng after an athlete was beaten to death (歐打 致死) now completely returned to a state of spring breeze and great harmony (春風 太和)? I don't know how different the people (人種) of Pukch'ŏng and Hamhŭng are, or how far apart (隔遠) the two places are, but engaging in a regional conflict (人種戰이나 地境戰) between them is just too regrettable (야속한 일이지). Regarding this incident, wouldn't the best approach (好방편) be for truly conscientious and compassionate people in Pukch'ŏng to voluntarily step forward and soothe the feelings of the other side, following the principle of "he who tied the knot should untie it" (結者解之)?

▲ Constable Yi (李某) at the Anju Ipsŏk-myŏn police substation (駐在所 巡査) reportedly beat an elderly woman (노파) (62) from the neighborhood severely (亂打無地) because she didn't fetch water for his house. Truly the act of a wicked (괴약한) fellow. But what is more wicked than this is that such wicked constables are rampant (橫行) not only there but everywhere (處處). <90>

**Summary**

This article compiles various news snippets from Korea in mid-1924. It covers incidents of corruption involving Japanese merchants and officials in Chinju and Kunsan, bureaucratic incompetence leading to budget issues in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), a large tenant farmer award ceremony laced with commentary on colonial power dynamics, speculation on international relations affecting Korea (Japan-Soviet ties and a potential Eurasian railway), political rumors surrounding the Japanese cabinet and the Governor-General, commentary on reader reactions to a previous magazine feature, a social initiative by churches in Kwangju to educate poor children, lingering regional tensions between Pukch'ŏng and Hamhŭng after a violent incident, and an instance of police brutality by a Japanese constable in Anju. The article often carries a critical or sarcastic tone towards the colonial authorities and Japanese actors, while also observing social conditions and hopes for change. For historians, this piece offers valuable insights into the everyday realities of colonial Korea: economic exploitation (rice trade fraud), administrative inefficiency and its impact on Koreans, the complex landlord-tenant relations under Japanese landowners, the local impact of international politics, social stratification (無産兒童), regional conflicts within Korean society, and the pervasive issue of police misconduct under the colonial regime. The commentary reveals a contemporary critical perspective on Japanese rule and its effects.

天玄地黃
얼마 전에는 晋州의 고 누구라 하던 日人 豪商이, 저울 눈을 속혀, 細民의 주머니를 도적질하야, 慶南 一帶의 문제가 되더니, 이번에는 群山米穀組合員인 日人 某가, 자기 동포의 검사원 某와 배가 마저서, 거즛 檢印을 찍은 不正米를 移出하야, 全朝鮮의 米穀 무역에 큰 영향을 끼첫다나. ▲이것만도 아니라, 요새에는 仁川 米豆取引所 某仲買店을 중심삼아, 무슨 중대 문제가 생겻다고． ▲본래 挾雜으로 맛붓튼 그놈들이 挾雜으로 패망할 것도 사실이겟지. ▲太平通 京城日報社의 跡에 京城府 廳舍를 짓게 하야, 그 舊가옥의 移轉料는 일체 府에서 擔當으로 하엿셧다자. ▲그런대, 府에서는 舊家瓦의 이전조건에, 그의 기초공사인 콩크릿트의 제거를 망각하야서 새로히 이를 처치하지 아늘 수 업는 바, 결국, 예산의 부족을 誘致하엿다나, 그러면 일은 관료가 져즈르고 損은 府民 전체가 보게 되엿지. 寒心也. ▲지난달이다. 全北 咸悅-多木농장에서는 소작인 1,800인 중에서 600인의 소작인 우량자 표창식을, 한번 氣것 盛旺히 하며, 농장주의 告辭며 聖壽萬歲의 呼唱이 실로 當世의 盛况을 盡하엿다고, 남의 땅에 와서 남의 것 가지고 잘들도 노네. ▲日露承認이 互交하면, 새로 생길 현상도 가지가지이겟거니와, 제일 통쾌한 것은 歐亞鐵道聯絡의 실현이다. 철도당국자의 말을 드르면, 日露國交가 회복되면, 곳 歐亞鐵道연락을 개시할 터인데, 釜山을 기점으로 하야, 1주 1회 내지 2회의 직통 열차를 발차할 듯 십흔대, 그리되면, 莫斯科까지 7일간, 伯林까지 10일간이면 得達한다고, 여하간 때는 점점 됴와오는 셈. ▲日本 加藤내각의 출현과 共히, 朝鮮總督府의 大株主측에도 무슨 새로운 교체가 잇스리라 하는 것은 사람사람이 한 번식 생각핊� 바이엿슬 것이다. ▲그러나 日本帝國의 지금 당한 형편이 이런 식민지문제를 몬져 생각하리만큼 여유가 잇스면, 정말 훌륭하게. ▲本紙 前月 京城號에 쓴 京城人物百態에 대해서, 밧게서는 이러니져러니하고 꽤 말들이 만흔 모양이지. ▲그럿치, 말거리가 되라고 쓴 것이 말이 업서야 될 수가 잇나. 그러나, 한번 웃고 지내가는 니악이로 쓴 것을, 너무 천착스럽게 깁게 鮮釋한다 하면, 그것을 쓴 사람의 本意와는 틀니지. 쉬쉬. ▲光州 읍내 基督敎會의 각 예배당에서는, 오는 夏休를 이용하야, 시내의 無産兒童을 교육하기로 되엿다고. 과연 됴흔 새 시험이다. 다못 바라는 것은, 하나으로부터 백까지 하나님 아버지를 찻게 하는 傳道式 臭味를 加하지 말고, 온젼히 그들을 위하는 赤心으로 만나아 가기를. ▲운동 선수를 歐打 致死케 하야, 北靑, 咸興의 시민간에 생긴 不和는, 이제는 아조 春風 太和를 復하엿는지. 北靑의 인종과 咸興의 인종이 얼마나 다르며, 또는 그 兩地의 새가 얼마나 隔遠한지는 모르지만 그 새에 人種戰이나 地境戰을 한대서는, 너무나 야속한 일이지. 이번 일에 잇서는 結者解之로 北靑에 잇는 진실로 뜻잇고 눈물잇는 사람이 자진하야써, 저편의 심정을 풀어줌이 제일 好방편이 아닐가. ▲安州 立石面 駐在所 巡査 李某는 근처 노파(62)가 자기 집 물을 길어 주지 안는다고 亂打無地하엿다고 한다. 과연 괴약한 놈의 행사이다. 그러나 이보다 더 괴악한 것은 그 따위 괴약한 巡査가 그곳만 아니라 處處에 橫行하는 그것이다.＜90＞
