Before the Field Report

Although I have said this once before, writing articles for the provincial special issues (道號記事) is truly more difficult than anything else. Whether I observed well or poorly, since I have already done what is called a field survey (답사), out of responsibility, I absolutely cannot avoid writing, but I am truly bewildered (어리 삥삥하다) as to what I should write. No matter how much I ponder, there is no path to follow other than that usual format. That is to say, there is no cleverer plan (策案) than making it an object of complaint.

Needless to say (毋論), the hopes of the readers or the gentlemen (諸氏) of the province in question (該道) must be many, but it cannot be helped.

Among the many people already waiting, saying, “This time it’s the Hamnam Province Special Issue (咸南道號)! So-and-so is responsible! Where and how has it been done? Let me see—” there will surely be those who, like a certain friend in Nanam during the Hamgyŏngbuk-do Special Issue (咸北道號時), are firmly growling (우르는), “If it even slightly displeases my eyes,” and those who, like certain friends in Inch’ŏn during the Kyŏnggi-do Special Issue (京畿道號時), are preparing a denunciation (성토), saying, “How could you do this to our group (모딈)? If you make the slightest mistake (깟닥하다가는)—,” and also those who, like the Heaven Faction (天堂派) members in P’yŏngyang, will first look for grounds for censure, asking, “Who dares— our sacred principles (主義)?” I anticipate (豫期) that harsh written criticism (筆棒), voiced denunciation (聲討), and reprimands (問責) will all come my way. I will accept them. They must surely be done, and I must accept them. I believe that real benefit (實益) will surely arise (떠러지리라) amidst that back-and-forth.

In reality (實上), it was true that when there wasn't a single word, this way or that way (이럿타 저럿타), from that province after a provincial special issue, without even a slight stir (꿈젹), it seemed to us like a dead society or a finished society, a society utterly worthless (보잘 것 업는), making us rather (도리여) worried.

Readers—needless to say (毋論), all will offer their esteemed critique (高評), but I especially request that the brothers of Hamnam send many post-reading impressions.

And one thing I feel apologetic about is that due to the circumstances (사정상) of our company (本社), we had to divide Hamhŭng North and Hamhŭng South into the November and December issues. From a certain perspective, one might perhaps say, “Perhaps it might be acceptable (猶或可矣).” It differs from our prior agreement (豫約). This is because the area is vast and the time is short (地廣日淺). Hamhŭng South will be reported by our company’s reporter, Mr. Cha Sangch’an (車相瓚君), in the December issue, but first, this reporter intends to fulfill his responsibility with a general overview (總觀) of Hamnam, followed by field reports (踏査記) of seven counties: Tanch’ŏn, Iwŏn, Pukch’ŏng, P’ungsan, Samsu, and Hongwŏn. (達成 Talsŏng) <78>

**Summary**

The author, Talsŏng, writes a preface to the upcoming field report on Hamgyŏngnam-do for the magazine *Kaebyŏk*. He expresses the difficulty of writing such reports and anticipates harsh criticism from readers, drawing parallels with negative reactions to previous special issues on other provinces like Hamgyŏngbuk-do and Kyŏnggi-do. However, he welcomes this feedback, arguing that silence from a region indicates a "dead society" and that constructive debate yields real benefits. He apologizes for splitting the Hamgyŏngnam-do coverage into two issues due to the vastness of the region and limited time, assigning the southern part to another reporter, Cha Sangch'an, while he covers Hamhŭng and the northern counties. This article is interesting for colonial history as it reveals the active intellectual engagement and critical readership across different Korean provinces in the 1920s, despite colonial rule. It also highlights the logistical challenges faced by publications like *Kaebyŏk* in covering the entire country and the author's explicit belief in the importance of public discourse and even dissent for societal vitality.

踏査記에 先하야
언제도 한 번 한 말이지만 道號記事란 과연 무엇보다 어려운 일이다. 잘 보앗거나 못보앗거나 이미 답사라고 하긴 한 일이닛가 책임상 안이 쓸 수는 도저히 업다만은 무어라고 써야될지 과연 어리 삥삥하다. 아무리 따저보아도 例의 그 형식밧게 더 밟을 길이 업다. 卽 불평의 대상물밧게 더 묘한 策案이 업다는 말이다.
독자 또는 該道 諸氏의 희망이야 毋論 만켓지만은 할 수 업는 일이다.
『이번은 咸南道號! 책임자는 아무개! 어듸 엇더케 해놋노? 어듸 좀-보자-』하고 발서부터 기다려주는 여러분 중에는 咸北道號時에 羅南의 엇던 친구가티 「만약 내 눈에 거슬니기만 하면은」하고 단단히 우르는 이도 잇슬터이요 京畿道號時 仁川의 엇던 친구들가티 「우리 모딈을 엇지고 엇지해-만약 깟닥하다가는」하고 성토를 준비하는 이도 잇슬 터이고 또 平壤의 天堂派들 가티 신성한 우리 主義를 누가 감히-」하야 문책거리부터 차자 내실 이도 잇슬 줄 안다. 筆棒, 聲討, 問責, 그거 다 드러올 것을 豫期한다. 밧는다. 반듯이 해야 되고 또 바더야 된다. 가고 오고 하는 그 새이에 實益은 반듯이 떠러지리라 한다.
實上 말이지 道號 뒤에 그 道로부터 이럿타 저럿타 말 한 마듸 업시 꿈젹 안이하는 것은 죽은 사회인지 다 된 사회인지 아모 보잘 것 업는 사회 가티 우리가 도리여 걱정 되여 보임이 사실이엿다.
讀者-毋論 다 가티 高評을 내리우겟지만 특히 咸南兄弟로서 독후감을 만히 보내기를 부탁한다.
그리고 한 가지 미안한 것은 本社의 사정상 咸興 以北, 咸興 以南을 11月, 12月로 分하게 된 그것이다. 엇던 의미에서 猶或可矣라고 할넌지도 몰으겟다. 豫約과는 틀렷슴이다. 地廣日淺한 까닭이다. 咸興 以南은 本社 記者 車相瓚君의 답사로 12月號에 발표하겟거니와 爲先 기자는 咸南總觀을 비롯하야 端川, 利原, 北靑, 豊山, 三水, 洪原 七郡의 踏査記로써 책임을 면하랸다.(達成)＜78＞
