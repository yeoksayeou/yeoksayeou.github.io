The Proletarian Army Gathering Under the Banner of the Black Emperor

Suddenly, it is already November! The Russian Revolution of seven years ago comes to mind. However, there is no freedom to speak of it. Our Proletarian Army is even more of an issue. They say snow is falling in the Northern Sky, and frost has descended in the south. Even in Sŏul, already every household worries about *kimjang*, and overcoats flutter on every street.

The time is precisely late autumn, early winter! The entire world is nearly drawn under the banner of the Black Emperor (黑帝). The season is certainly cold.

Those friends who have houses, food, and clothes = the haves, worry about *kimjang* out of concern for surviving, worry about overcoats, worry about coal, worry about window screens (房帳), worry about folding screens, worry about scarves, worry about gloves, and even worry about fur hand-warmers (털吐手) and padded socks.

But our friends who have no house, no food, no clothes = famously thin and gaunt friends, who sweated through the summer and have shivered since early autumn, what worries do our have-not friends endure?

Worries about houses and clothes are secondary; worries about rice and firewood are also secondary; wanting to die, yet finding it hard to get even a lump of lye (洋재물) and lacking even a bit of strength to go to the Han River – how are our truly stark-naked friends managing?

Ah, the plight of the have-nots is indeed pitiful!

Looking up at the yellow rice stacks, how much saliva do they swallow? Looking at the white cotton fields, how many times do they twist their bodies? Passing by large houses, how many sighs do they heave?

When they think, "There are certainly many things in the world, but only I have nothing!", how much do they cry out against the injustice of the world! Ah – the situation of the entire Proletarian Army, shivering and crying out as they face the cold season, appears vividly before my eyes! How will they pass this winter? <2>

All Proletarian Masses! It is better to die than to ever stand before the gate of a have, begging, "Please give a penny, please have sympathy, please give charity." Let us keenly realize that there is a distinct path to take instead of begging.

Sympathy, charity – these are words spoken among the haves as after-dinner talk. Where circumstances are not the same, there is never sympathy. So-called sympathy and charity are words used to distinguish between high and low, noble and base.

Just as there is no reason for the haves to sympathize with the have-nots, there is no condition for the have-nots to receive sympathy from the haves. If one has nothing, one must appeal to one's own strength to obtain something, and if appealing to one's own strength is insufficient, there is no other way but to die. Doing so is the natural duty of a human being.

Facing the cold season, without a house, without food, without clothes – thus looking up at the haves and hoping they might give even a penny or a measure, there are surely many brothers swallowing empty saliva, but one can never live like that. Even if life endures moment by moment, one can never truly live that way. There is a separate path to take other than begging for sympathy, charity, etc. To repeat once more:

Going to someone else's banquet (宴席), gazing at the sumptuous feast, and hoping for just one morsel, just one cup, swallowing saliva until the throat aches – that is the lowest of the low (賤夫中에도 又賤夫). Rather, the true hero among heroes is the one who rolls up his sleeves, clears his throat, boldly steps in, and says, "We may be strangers, but the food is familiar. Let's eat together."

Talk of face or shame – those are also words used in the society of the haves. The have-nots possess neither. For one struggling for survival, there is no leisure to distinguish between ugly weeds and pretty flowers. The day one tramples over everything indiscriminately is the day one lives.

No matter how afflicted Chosŏn is by the disease of famine (饑饉病), there is rice, there is firewood, there is cotton (木花), and there are houses. We think there is enough for the people of Chosŏn, both propertied and propertiless (有産無産), to eat and wear.

Here, we state these words:
"Let's not say we cannot live because there is no food or clothing, but let's say we cannot live because we are incompetent."

And we add these words: "The time is precisely the cold season; let the tactics (戰術) of all proletarians be tested at this time." <3>

**Summary**

This editorial laments the plight of the poor (the "Proletarian Army") facing the harsh winter in Korea, contrasting their lack of basic necessities with the comfortable worries of the wealthy. It vehemently rejects begging, charity, or relying on the sympathy of the rich, framing such actions as demeaning and futile. Instead, it urges the poor to rely on their own strength and implies a need for direct action, comparing the ideal response to boldly joining a feast uninvited rather than begging for scraps. The piece concludes by stating that resources exist in Chosŏn for everyone and calls the winter a time to test the "tactics" of the propertyless. For historians, this article is significant as it reflects the growing influence of socialist or class-conscious ideas in colonial Korea, likely spurred by events like the Russian Revolution mentioned at the outset. It showcases deep social discontent and a radical critique of inequality, moving beyond mere appeals for help towards advocating for self-reliance and potentially confrontational methods ("tactics") for survival and asserting rights, all under the watchful eye of Japanese colonial rule.

黑帝의 幕下에 모여드는 無産軍
어느듯 발서 11월! 칠년전 露西亞 혁명이 연상된다. 그러나 그는 말할 자유가 업다. 우리의 無産軍이 더욱 문제된다. 北天에는 눈이 날닌다 하며 남방에는 서러가 내렷다 한다. 서울만해도 발서 집집마다 짐장 걱정이요, 巨里마다 외투가 나붓기인다.
때는 정히 深秋요, 初冬! 왼世界는 거의 거의 黑帝幕下에 기여들려 한다. 때는 확실히 寒節인데.
집잇고 밥잇고 옷잇는=잇든 친구들은 살고 남은 걱정으로 짐장을 걱정하며 외투를 걱정하며 석탄을 걱정하며 房帳을 걱정하며 병풍을 걱정하며 목도리를 걱정하며 장갑을 걱정하며 심지어 털吐手 덧버선까지 걱정하는데.
우리 집업고 밥업고 옷업는=업기로 유명한 뺏뺏 마른 친구 녀름내 땀흘리고 初秋부터 불불떠는 우리 업는 친구들은 무엇을 걱정하고 지내는고?
집과 옷걱정도 둘재요, 쌀과 나무 걱정들도 둘재요, 죽을내야 洋재물 한뎅이 엇기가 어럽고 漢江갈 기력 한分이 업는 우리 정말 새빩안 친구들은 엇더케 지내는가 말이다.
아 업는 놈들의 신세 과연 가련하구나!
누른 벼멍석을 쳐다보고 침은 얼마나 생키고 지내며 하-얀 목화밧을 쳐다보며 몸을 멧번이나 비틀며 크다란 집을 지낼 때 한숨은 얼마나 지우는고?
『세상에 잇는 것도 만키는 만쿠나 마는 유독 나뿐 업구나!』할 때에 세상의 불공평을 얼마나 부르지지는고! 아-모든 無産軍의 寒節을 당하야 떨며 부르짓는 정황이 눈에 환하게 빗치는구나! 엇더케 이 겨울을 지날 것인가?＜2＞
모든 無産大衆아. 죽으면 죽엇지 결코 잇는 놈의 門前에서「한分 줍시요, 동정합시요, 慈善합시요.」하고 구걸하지는 마자. 구걸의 代로 별로히 취할 道가 잇는 것을 절실히 알어두자.
동정이니 자선이니 그는 잇는 놈끼리 食後餘談으로 하는 말이다. 가튼 처지가 안인 곳에 결코 동정이 업다. 尊卑 貴賤=그런 것을 가리기 위하야 쓰는 말이 소위 동정이요, 자선이라 한다.
잇는 놈들이 업는 놈들에게 동정할 이유도 업거니와 업는 놈으로서 잇는 놈에게 동정 바들 조건도 업는 것이다. 업스면 잇도록 자력에 訴할 뿐이며 자력에 訴하야 不及하면 죽는 수밧게 다시 도리가 업는 것이다. 그리하는 것이 인간의 당연한 일이다.
寒節을 當하야 집은 업고 먹을 것은 업고 입을 것은 업고-하니까 잇다는 놈들을 쳐다보고 한푼 한말이라도 주엇스면 하고 헛침만 생키는 형제가 母論 만흐리라마는 그러하고는 결코 못사는 법이다. 一時一時 생명은 運한다 할지라도 결코 못사는 법이다. 동정, 자선-등의 구걸보다 따로이 취할 도리가 잇다. 다시 한번 반복한다 하면
남의 宴席에 가서 진수성찬을 바라보고 한点 만이라도 한잔 만이라도 주엇스면=하고 목젓이 떨어지도록 침만 생키는 것은 賤夫中에도 又賤夫이다. 차라리 손 부르것고 에헴하고 쓱드러서서「사람은 初面이지만 음식은 舊面일세. 가티 먹세.」하는 놈이 대장부 중에도 대장부라는 말이다.
체면이니 염치이니 그런 말도 잇는 놈의 사회에서 하는 말이다. 업는 놈에게는 업다. 생명을 위하야 뛰는 놈에게는 미운풀 고은풀 언제 그것을 가릴 여가가 업는 것이다. 함부로 짓빩고 넘어서는 날이 사는 날이다.
朝鮮이 아무리 饑饉病에 걸녓다 해도 쌀도 잇고 나무도 잇고 木花도 잇고 집도 잇다. 有産無産間에 朝鮮 사람이 먹고 입을 것은 잇다고 생각한다.
여긔에서 우리는 이러한 말을 해둔다.
『먹을 것 입을 것이 업서서 못산다 하지 말고 저 못생긴 탓으로 못 산다고 하자』라고.
그리고 이런 말을 첨부해 둔다.『때는 正히 寒節인뎨 모든 無産者의 戰術은 이때로 한번 시험하리라』고.＜3＞
