自由通情 (Free Communication/Letters to the Editor)

Please establish a Readers' Column?
From Kŭnhyŏn Hagin in Sŏnch'ŏn

It is a definite trend these days that as new literature gradually buds in Chosŏn, the youth are engrossed in seeking out this new literature. Thus, even when one goes to places like bookstores, what they browse through are literary works.
The fact that the Tong-A Ilbo has a Monday column and is widely soliciting works from readers, and that the Chosŏn Ilbo, upon meeting a new owner and innovating its pages, has established an Arts and Sciences column and is also soliciting literary works from readers—these trends are undeniably welcome for understanding the psychology of our modern youth. However, among magazines, I only see "Chosŏn Mundan," which published its inaugural issue, solitarily seeking works from readers in the world.
Because of this, I wish to recommend that Kaebyŏk also have a Readers' Column. It is truly regrettable that Kaebyŏk, which publishes the largest number of copies in our magazine world, does not encourage literature from readers interested in the arts. I sincerely hope you will establish a Readers' Column! I wish to ask the Editor's opinion.

Editor: That is a very pertinent statement. Indeed, it has been a long-standing task for our company. We intend to certainly try implementing it starting from the New Year.

The Worry of Not Being Able to Keep Time
Yongch'ŏn, Yi Pyŏng-gwan

Mr. Yi Ton-hwa, how is your health and disposition? My worry is that I cannot keep to my schedule. I have set times for waking up in the morning, going to school, exercise, study, leaving school and returning home, reading newspapers and magazines at night, studying, and sleeping. I've posted the daytime schedule above my school desk and the nighttime schedule on my wall at home. But what can I do about not being able to follow the schedule? Perhaps because I have a habit of sleeping in, I am always later than the set time. Even if I occasionally wake up on time, breakfast is late, or visitors come. Consequently, my time for going to school gets delayed, and dealing with school affairs gets postponed. Every hour does not go according to the timetable. I cannot even accomplish one-third of the planned work. Even if I manage to handle all the planned tasks, previous tasks are piled up like a great mountain; isn't it pathetic that I cannot finish the planned work like this? I offer this one word of my worry to my respected Teacher Yi.

Editor: I also experienced the same situation as you. Observing time strictly is truly difficult. I think this way: initially, do not try to mix necessary and unnecessary times together and attempt to execute them all at once. Instead, select the most necessary, simple, and strictly defined times and try to adhere to them faithfully. I saw effectiveness by doing so. Initially, select and keep only simple and strictly defined times.

From the Brothers in Paris
We, who are far from our native land, having left behind relatives and old friends, have no place to inquire, discuss, and speak of our lonely anxieties and sorrowful situations in a foreign land with different customs and sentiments to find solace. The longing for our already broken homes and scattered kin grows more earnest day by day. Furthermore, as we are weak and lack backing, small and large sufferings, both physical and mental, never cease, and when we consider that this is also what all of us are experiencing together, we cannot hold back mournful sighs. We also realize that the greatest sins in this world are weakness and ignorance.
In this situation where we have nowhere to turn, we thought that even a small support, even an organization in name only, was necessary, and thus two years ago, we organized the Pari Chaeryuin Ch'inmokhoe (Paris Korean Residents' Friendship Association) <75>. Although it's called an association, the members do not exceed 20, and moreover, as these members are all powerless and unknowledgeable like Hangul itself, the current state of the Association or its future development are not yet materials worthy of mention. However, we consider it a great joy just to gather and see each other's faces, and if news received by A can be heard by C, and work learned by B can be known by D, this is what brings us great happiness.
Whenever we gather, the first thing we ask each other about is news from our homeland, but it is no exaggeration to say we are entirely ignorant (不知) of what events have already occurred in our society, which we left far away and haven't seen for a long time, and what new matters are progressing in what way. It goes without saying that it is a great shame for us, as members of society, not to know our own society, and it is also greatly lamentable that we become a defiling flaw (汚瑕) that harms the luster of a brilliant jewel (鮮麗한 寶玉).
To know our society, to console our loneliness, we wish to continuously read all newspapers and various magazines published in our home country. Particularly, we seek your company's sympathy and earnestly hope that you will **send** one copy of the magazine as it is published and donate it to our Association.
August 1, 1924 AD
Kim Yŏng-bae, President of the Pari Chaeryuin Ch'inmokhoe
Receiving Address: Paris P.O. Box 369
Reunion coreenne

Paris

Editor: This is a private letter. However, it is made public to widely inform about the news of our brethren residing in Paris. This letter, which left Paris on August 1st, arrived only after two months and 17 days, on October 17th. How keenly one feels the inconvenience of communication!

From the Banks of the Thames
Brother Kim! Having lived affectionately in Germany for three years, I came to England at the end of last month. I am lodging in a village on the banks of the Thames near London and learning English. This place is very quiet (開寂), and I have not met a single person from our country. As autumn deepens in the island nation and seeing people from all over the world gathered for the Empire Exhibition, which boasts the national prestige of the Great British Empire, I cannot help but feel deeply moved, recalling the natural disasters in our homeland. Early last month, I read the English section in the Tong-A Ilbo titled "Korean Students in Europe." Compared to when the reporter was in Germany, the number of students from our country in Central and Western Europe has decreased significantly. In Germany alone, there are fewer than 40. Most went to America, Russia, or France due to the rapid fluctuations in German prices, and some have returned home. There is a rumor that about 10 people are currently residing in England, but it seems there are only 5 or 6 students. I don't know the details about those in France, but there are 2 students in Switzerland and 4 in Italy, and 2 in Austria are all students. Additionally, it is said there is one person each in Poland and Romania. I gratefully receive Kaebyŏk and Sin Yŏsŏng every month.
September 11th, Pak Sŭng-ch'ŏl in England

Pusan Girls' Youth Association Founded
In Pusan, it had long been deeply regretted that there was no organization for children, regardless of gender. Recently, several like-minded friends gathered and held the inaugural meeting of the Pusan Yŏja Sonyŏnhoe (Pusan Girls' Youth Association) at the Pusan Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoegwan (Pusan Youth Hall) on September 7th. The main purpose of this association is said to be ideally fostering the individuality of modern women while simultaneously disseminating the true meaning of female education to the general public. The officers of this association, led by President Miss Kim Tu-yŏng, include advisors specially chosen from among parents or teachers to receive guidance on all affairs. <76>

**Summary**

This article compiles several letters sent to the editor of Kaebyŏk magazine in late 1924. The letters cover a request to start a readers' contribution column, personal struggles with time management, news and requests from Korean expatriates (students and residents) in Paris and London detailing their loneliness, desire for news from home, and organizational efforts, and finally, a notice about the founding of a Girls' Youth Association in Pusan aimed at female education and empowerment. The editor provides brief responses to some letters. For historians, these letters offer valuable glimpses into the intellectual life, daily concerns, diaspora experiences (loneliness, networking, financial struggles affecting study locations, slow communication), and early social organizing efforts (especially for women and youth) among Koreans during the colonial period. The mention of specific locations, numbers of students abroad, and the founding of local associations provides concrete data points about the Korean community's activities and mindset in the 1920s.

讀者欄을 두어주시요?
宣川에서 羣賢學人

朝鮮에서도 새 문학이 차차 엄이 돗음과 동시에 요새 청년들이 새 문학을 찻기에 열중함은 확실한 경향이 되여 잇슴니다. 그리하야 書舖갓흔 데를 가도 뒤적어리는 것이 문예서적이외다.
東亞日報가 月曜欄이 잇서 가지고 널니 독자의 작품을 모집하고 잇스며 朝鮮日報가 새 주인을 맛나 지면을 혁신함과 동시에 學藝欄을 설치하야 또한 문예작품을 독자에게 모집하는 경향은 요럿케도 현대 우리 청년의 心理를 알아주는 데는 안이 반길 줄이 업슴니다. 그러나 잡지로서는 창간호를 내인『朝鮮文壇』이 쓸쓸하게 혼자서 독자의 작품을 世間에 구할 뿐 밧게 보지 못하겟슴니다.
나는 이것들로 해서 開闢에게도 讀者欄이 이스소서 권하고 십슴니다. 우리의 잡지계에 第一部數를 만히 내이는 開闢에 잇서서 문예에 뜻 둔 독자의 문예를 장려하지 안는다 함은 넘우 섭섭한 일이외다. 바라보니 讀者欄을 두어 주십소서! 編者의 의견을 뭇고 십슴니다.

編者, 지당한 말슴이외다. 그야말로 本社로의 宿題엿슴니다. 新年부터는 期於히 실행해 보려 함니다.

時間을 못 직히는 걱정
龍川 李炳寬

李敦化 선생 氣候 건강하심니까. 저는 시간을 못 직히는 것이 걱정입니다. 아츰에 기상하는 시간, 등교하는 시간, 운동, 공부, 退校歸家하는 시간, 야간에 신문잡지 보는 시간, 공부하는 시간, 취침하는 시간 등을 다 정하야 晝間의 시간표는 학교책상 머리에 붓처 노앗고 夜間의 시간표는 자택벽면에 붓처놋치 안앗슴니까. 그러나 시간표 대로 시간을 못 직혀 나감을 엇지 함니까. 朝寢의 습관이 잇서 그런지 늘 정각보다 느저짐니다. 혹시 정각에 일어나면 朝飯이 느저지거나 또 방문오는 이가 잇슴니다. 그러니까 등교시간이 느저지지오, 敎務處理가 지체되지오. 每時 時間表대로 되지 안슴니다. 예정한 三分의 一도 일을 못함니다. 예정한 일을 다 처리하야 나가도 前事가 泰山가티 만이 싸여 잇는대 이처럼 예정한 일을 못 다 하니 한심한 일이 안입니까. 나의 경애하는 李선생의게 저의 걱정 한마듸를 올님니다.

編者. 나 亦 당신과 가튼 사정이엿슴니다. 시간 恪守란 참 힘드는 일이외다. 나는 이러케 생각함니다. 처음은 필요불필요의 시간을 한꺼번애 어울너 실항하려 하지말고 가장 필요한 간단한 시간 엄정한 시간을 가리여 착실히 직켜 보십시오. 나는 그리해서 효력을 보앗슴니다. 처음은 간단한 시간 엄정한 시간뿐 가리여 직히시오.

멀니 故土를 등지고 친척과 故友를 떠난 우리들은 人情과 풍속이 다른 남의 땅에서 외로운 근심과 설은 사정을 물어 의논하고 말하야 慰安을 어들 곳이 업사오매. 임의 문어진 넷집과 이리저리 흣터진 내 결레를 생각하는 마음이 날마다 간절하나이다. 더욱이나 우리들은 힘이 약하고 압이 업스매 육체상으로 정신상으로 적고 큰 고통이 떠날 날이 업사오며 이것이 또한 모든 우리들의 함께 밧는 것음을 생각하오매 애닯은 한숨을 금치 못하나이다. 이 세상에 제일 큰 죄가 약하고 무식한 것임을 아울너 깨닷겟나이다.

테임쓰 河畔에서
金兄! 삼년이나 정드리고 살든 독일을 떠나서 去月末에 영국으로 왓나이다. 倫敦 근교 테임쓰 강변촌가에 寄宿하면서 영어를 배호나이다. 이곳은 매우 開寂하며 本國人士는 한 분도 맛나지 못 하나이다. 島國에 秋色이 깁허짐과 大弗列顚帝國의 國威를 자랑하는 帝國박람회로 해서 世界人士가 다 모혀드는 것을 볼 적에 본국의 天災를 追想하고 감격이 업슬 수 업나이다. 去月 初旬 東亞日報의 歐洲에 朝鮮학생이란 영문란을 읽엇나이다. 기자가 독일에 잇슬 적보담 中西歐에 본국학생수가 만히 줄엇나이다. 독일만 해도 40명이 못 되나이다. 대개는 독일의 급격한 물가의 변동으로 米洲나, 露國이나, 佛國으로 갓스며, 혹은 귀국한 이도 잇나이다. 지금에 영국에 在留하는 이는 10여 명 된다는 傳言이 잇스나 학생은 5,6 명 되는 것 갓소이다. 佛國에 잇는 이는 자세히 몰으나 瑞西에 2명과 이태리에 4명과 墺地利에 2명은 전부가 학생이며, 其外에 波蘭과 羅馬尼亞에 각 1명이 잇다 하나이다. 매월 개벽과 新女性은 감사히 밧나이다.
