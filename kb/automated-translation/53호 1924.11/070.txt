Article Type: 소식 (News)

**Body Text Translation:**
Kapsa Autumn and the World Situation
September 13th to October 13th

September 13th
A P'yŏngyang Aviation Unit airplane crashed and disintegrated in P'yŏnggang, resulting in the immediate deaths of Ebine and Morikatsu.
The Chosŏn Ilbo, previously managed by Song Pyŏngjun, was handed over to Sin Sŏgu, Yi Sanghyŏp, Sin Kubŏm, etc.
At the Kyŏngsŏng Ch'ŏndogyo Church, a first-anniversary memorial service was held for compatriots massacred during last year's earthquake disaster in Japan. A similar memorial service held in Tokyo was dispersed, leading to a clash between the crowd and police.
The Fengtian Army occupied Chaoyang.

14th
In Wyoming State, USA, the "Kemmerer" coal mine exploded, tragically killing 50 miners.
In Yulho, Yanghwa-myŏn, Pukch'ŏng County, a major clash occurred between the wealthy Yun T'aeho and the Sŏngil School parents regarding the cancellation of a 10,000 won donation, resulting in police drawing swords and the arrest of dozens of villagers.

15th
In Tongmak-tong, Kwangp'yŏng-myŏn, Ŭiju County, 50-60 members of an independence group attacked a combined passenger car between Ŭiju and Sakchu, causing the immediate death of one woman and serious injuries to two Japanese individuals.
A major disturbance occurred at the home of Kim Chunwan in Mugyo-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, due to a break-in by robbers Kim Yŏngnan and Pong Sŭngsun.
Mr. Zhang Zuolin of Fengtian issued a final threatening notice to President Cao Kun, involving an airplane visit.
Japanese-Russian negotiations broke down once again.
In the mountains of Ando-dong, Isŏ-myŏn, Kanggye County, ginseng gatherers Chŏn Uryong, Pak Myŏngho, and Pang Chŏngok encountered an independence group; due to mistrust, one was killed and two were seriously injured.

16th
A major storm hit the Tokyo vicinity, flooding over 40,000 homes.
The Japanese Navy's Suzuki 1st and Katō 2nd fleets in the Pacific Ocean encountered severe storms and faced great danger.
On the Mokp'o coast, a large brawl erupted between the Maritime and Land Labor Union and the Wholesalers' Association over job competition, resulting in dozens of serious and minor injuries on both sides.

17th
Chinese President Cao Kun ordered the suppression of Zhang Zuolin.
In Hwanggeum-chŏng 4-chome, Kyŏngsŏng, a major disturbance occurred due to an attempted murder case involving Pak Yongsul (husband) against Kim Chŏngsuk (wife).

18th
Another strong earthquake occurred in the Tokyo region of Japan, causing a major commotion.
Regarding the long-standing Muan County Amtae tenant dispute case, Sŏ T'aejŏp, Sŏ Ch'angsŏk, etc., were sentenced to over 2 years imprisonment for charges of disturbance and injury.
Internal conflict arose within China's Zhejiang Army; Hangzhou declared independence, and Lu Yongxiang fled.
The Zhili-Fengtian War in China began.
The Five-Power Ministers residing in Shanghai lodged a strong protest with the Chinese government with four articles regarding self-defense concerning the turmoil in China.

19th
The Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals was organized in Kyŏngsŏng, initiated by resident foreign women.
The League of Nations Committee meeting in Geneva passed the disarmament protocol.
The 2nd and 3rd Committees of the League of Nations passed the cultural cooperation proposal submitted by France.
The United States declared absolute non-intervention regarding the turmoil in China.
A large fire occurred in Wakayama, Japan, causing damages of over 100,000 won.
The Consular Body of Japan, Britain, America, and France residing in Shanghai demanded the disarmament of both Zhejiang and Jiangsu forces near the Huangpu River.
In Sakashita Village, Gifu Prefecture, Japan, a major fight broke out between the Sangaehoe (Mutual Love Society) and the Uhwahoe (Friendship Society), both composed of Korean laborers from the Japan Electric Railway Company, resulting in many serious and minor injuries on both sides.

20th
Kim Yongwŏn (also known as Kim Kŭmgangsan) and O Sŏng, members of the Taedongdan group who had been arrested by the Kyŏngsŏng Tongdaemun Police Station, were transferred to the Prosecutor's Office.
The trial of Kim Chisŏp for the Nijūbashi Incident in Japan was indefinitely postponed due to Mr. Kim's illness.
The Chinese government requested permanent representation on the League of Nations council.

21st
Influential members of both the upper and lower houses of the Japanese Diet and a group of journalists decided on a peace recommendation proposal regarding the turmoil in China.
News arrived of the alleged assassination of Tang Jiyao, a distinguished figure in China's Third Revolution.
Paek Kwangun<41>, the assailant who attempted to assassinate Governor-General Saitō, was killed by the main faction of the Taehan T'ongŭibu in Ji'an County.

22nd
The All-Korea Tennis Tournament, hosted by the Chosŏn Sports Association, was held in Kyŏngsŏng.
Japan also declared absolute non-intervention regarding the turmoil in China.
The League of Nations established a regulation to allow citizens of each nation to participate in next year's Disarmament Conference.
China's Wu P'ei-fu held a military council in Beijing.
German Chancellor Marx declared Germany's intention to join the League of Nations.
Egyptian Prime Minister Zaghloul Pasha traveled to Britain to negotiate the Egypt issue.

23rd
The plenary session of the League of Nations also passed the international cultural cooperation proposal suggested by France.
In the sea off Kongho-ri, Koŭp-myŏn, Kohŭng County, a fishing boat capsized, resulting in the drowning deaths of 12 women.

24th
A severe storm and strong hail occurred near Kyŏngsŏng, causing significant damage to crops.
On Mt. Tŏkko in Hoengsŏng County, 60 men and women died tragically due to strong hail.
In Kyŏngsŏng, the Famine Relief Society was founded through the initiative of prominent social figures and various organizations.

25th
Britain, France, and Japan within the League of Nations resolved to aid Georgian independence.

26th
Germany issued an interrogative public notice to the representatives of each country on the League of Nations council regarding joining the League.
China negotiated a loan of 12 million yuan from various banking syndicates but was refused on suspicion of fueling the civil war.

27th
At the Kyŏngsŏng Central YMCA Hall, a lecture meeting reporting on the investigation into the inner workings of Poch'ŏn'gyo was held, passionately calling for the eradication of Poch'ŏn'gyo.
Japan's revised proposal submitted to the League of Nations meeting was withdrawn due to opposition from various representatives.
Japan held cabinet meetings daily regarding the issue of political consolidation between the two parties.
An independence group appeared in Kodang-dong, Kongsŏ-myŏn, Kanggye County, harming Messrs. Kye Yŏngp'al, Yun Chŏngha, and Yu Ch'angik. Aviator An Ch'angnam was threatened by members of the Japanese Anti-Communist Group (赤化防止團) due to rumors of his participation in China's Zhili-Fengtian War.

28th
In Inch'ŏn, printing workers went on a major strike over wage reduction issues.
A large fire broke out in Mokp'o.
In the Pongp'yŏng region of Nyŏngbyŏn County, a battle occurred between police and an independence group, resulting in the death of one independence fighter.
Irregular forces from Montenegro looted several villages in Albania; the Albanian army mobilized, while protesting to the Roman Government and the League of Nations.

29th
A revolutionary movement against Soviet Russia broke out in the Caucasus region.
The Fengtian Army occupied Jianping.
The League of Nations passed next year's budget of over 22.2 million gold francs.

30th
The League of Nations meeting held in Geneva reached an agreement.
The French government sent an encoded telegram to the French representative instructing them to translate and print the League of Nations disarmament conference protocol.
The arbitration committee, composed of representatives from 50 countries of the League of Nations, passed Japan's revised proposal.
The US Consul residing in Fengtian declared the inability to recognize the Soviet consul resulting from the Fengtian-Soviet agreement.
The Kyŏngsŏng Famine Relief Society held a committee meeting and resolved on eight articles, including soliciting sympathetic donations throughout Korea and reducing tenant fees.

October 1st
The Kyŏngsŏng Library was closed due to financial difficulties.
The trial of Namba Daisuke for the High Treason Incident opened at the Tokyo Supreme Court.
A tragedy occurred at the Han River Railway Bridge in Kyŏngsŏng, where a mother and her two children drowned due to life difficulties. (Mrs. Sin, 34, wife of Ŏm Munsŏp from Sogyeok-dong, and her sons Ki-sang (5) and Ki-in (2)).
The Sangju-Kimch'ŏn line of the Chosŏn Railway Co., Ltd. was opened.
German-French negotiations began, chaired by French Prime Minister Herriot.
In Wain-dong, Ch'osan County, a clash occurred between 4 policemen and 7 members of an independence group.

2nd
The League of Nations resolved to hold the International Disarmament Conference in Geneva in June 1925, and the session closed after passing the protocol including Japan's revised proposal.
The Chinese government submitted a strong protest to Japan regarding the Manchurian Railway's transport of armed Fengtian troops.
The World Peace Conference, hosted by the French Peace League<42>, opened in Berlin, Germany, chaired by Belgian Senator Henri La Fontaine.
The Ilsin High Common School in Poju (likely Jinju) was authorized.

3rd
The Chinese representative protested to the League of Nations council regarding the rejection of their country's proposal "to elect council members from the Orient by election as well."

4th
P'yŏngyang Airplane No. 乙7 of the 6th Battalion crashed after getting caught in electric wires during training; Lieutenant Suzuki died instantly, and Captain Hattori was seriously injured.
The acting French Minister declared to the Chinese government that a protest would be lodged if French citizens' interests regarding the Chinese Eastern Railway were harmed due to the Fengtian-Soviet agreement.
The Turkish Army shot down a British airplane passing through the Mosul Vilayet and arrested the spy and pilot.

5th
In Sunch'ŏn County, a farmers' assembly was held, resolving on seven articles: tenant fees within 40%, seed selection, dispatching observers during inspections, land tax and public charges to be borne by the landlord, guarantee of tenancy rights for 5 years, notification to tenants one month prior to eviction even with justifiable cause. A large lecture followed.

6th
Japanese-Russian negotiations broke down again.
In Kyŏngsŏng, the 4th-anniversary celebration of the Seoul Youth Association was dispersed by police, and Messrs. Chŏng Paek, Yi Sŏngt'ae, Yu Chinhŭi, and Sin Paegu were arrested.
Mr. Chŏng Kyuch'an, who had resided in Russian territory for many years, was arrested by officers from the Sŏdaemun Police Station on a train at Kyŏngsŏng Station.

7th
A major storm occurred in the Kyushu region of Japan, causing significant disasters such as disruption of telegraph lines and collapsed houses.
In Taeok-myŏn, Chinju County, a farmers' assembly was held, making resolutions regarding tenant fees, guarantee of tenancy rights, matters concerning the association, and relief for landless members.

8th
The Fengtian Army occupied Shanhaiguan.
The Danish Parliament passed a bill abolishing conscription. (Fortresses to be demolished, the army to be used as police, the navy for coastal patrol.)

9th
Canadian provinces protested the passage of Japan's revised proposal at the League of Nations. (Japan's action is seen as related to Canada's policy of closing its doors to Asian immigration.)
France's Society for the Study of Russian Affairs voted to recognize Soviet Russia.
Fengtian declared diplomatic independence, breaking away from the Beijing Government's jurisdiction.

10th
The British Parliament was dissolved.
The Korean Famine Relief Society held a committee meeting and sent out appeals for sympathy to over 3,000 organizations across Korea.
Messrs. Yi Sŏngt'ae, Yu Chinhŭi, and Sin Paegu, who were detained following the dispersal of the Seoul Youth Association anniversary ceremony, were transferred to the prosecutor's office.

11th
Sŏ Ch'angmu of Kilchu, who had been active in the independence movement in various ways in Russian territory and Chinese regions, appealed his 12-year sentence.
The trial of Kim Chisŏp for the Tokyo Nijūbashi Incident opened at the Tokyo District Court, where Mr. Kim vehemently denounced Japan's misrule (惡政).
A new National Essence Party (國粹黨) was formed in Britain. The Hamgyŏng Line was opened from Hamhŭng-T'oejo to Pukch'ŏng-Yanghwa, and from Tanch'ŏn to Kilchu. Mr. Wu P'ei-fu, leader of China's Zhili Clique, personally commanded troops in the Qinhuangdao area.

12th
The world-renowned writer Anatole France of France passed away at the old age of 81. In Inch'ŏn, over three thousand female rice selectors from 13 rice mills united to form the Rice Selecting Female Workers' Union.

13th
Kim Yongsuk and Ko Tŏksu, members of the Kando National Guard Department (國民警護部), who had specialized in assassinating Japanese spies and received the death sentence at the Ch'ŏngjin District Court, were sentenced to life imprisonment on appeal in Kyŏngsŏng. China's Zhejiang Army suffered a major defeat by Sun Chuanfang's Zhili forces; Zhejiang Army leaders Lu Yongxiang, He Fenglin, etc., fled to Japan. Simultaneously with the Zhejiang Army's defeat, volunteer corps from various countries were mobilized in Shanghai.<43>
<41-43>

**Summary**

This news digest covers a turbulent month (Sept-Oct 1924) across Korea and the world. Domestically, it reports accidents, crimes, the transfer of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper, social issues like tenant farmer protests (Muan, Sunch'ŏn, Chinju) and labor strikes (Mokp'o, Inch'ŏn), arrests and trials related to the independence movement (Ŭiju attack, arrests in Kyŏngsŏng, Kim Chisŏp trial, Sŏ Ch'angmu appeal, Kando activists' sentences reduced), and relief efforts for famine and natural disasters. Internationally, the focus is heavily on the escalating Zhili-Fengtian War in China, League of Nations activities (disarmament talks, German membership, Georgian aid, Chinese representation issues), stalled Japanese-Russian negotiations, political shifts in Europe (France/Germany, UK election, Denmark abolishes conscription), and regional instability (Caucasus, Balkans).

For historians of colonial Korea, the article is valuable for its juxtaposition of local Korean events with major international news, reflecting Korea's integration into global currents despite colonization. It highlights the persistent activities of the independence movement both within Korea and abroad (Manchuria, Russia), the colonial authorities' suppression efforts, and growing social unrest manifested in tenant and labor disputes. The mention of specific individuals involved in independence activities (Kim Chisŏp, Paek Kwangun, Sŏ Ch'angmu, Kim Yongsuk, Ko Tŏksu) and social movements (Sŏ T'aejŏp, Sŏ Ch'angsŏk) provides concrete data points. The Famine Relief Society's activities and the organization of female workers in Inch'ŏn also point to developing civil society initiatives and labor consciousness under colonial rule.

同 14일

奉承順의 突入사건으로 大騷動을 起하다.

朴明浩
