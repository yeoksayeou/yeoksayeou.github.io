Magazine Title: 개벽 제3호
Publication Date: 1920년 08월 25일
Article Title: 運命 (Destiny/Fate)
Author: None Provided
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

One who possesses Luck (運), even if they fall into the Nile River (나일江), will float back up to the surface (水面) holding a Pearl (眞珠) in their mouth. (Arabian Proverb (아라삐아 俚言))<100>

**Summary**

This short piece presents an Arabian proverb emphasizing the power of luck or fate (運). It suggests that a fortunate person will inevitably overcome adversity and even find unexpected fortune (a pearl) amidst misfortune (falling into the Nile). From a historical perspective, the use of a foreign proverb about fate in a Korean magazine during the colonial period might reflect contemporary interests in universal ideas or perhaps a subtle commentary on the perceived role of luck or destiny in the lives of Koreans under occupation, suggesting either resilience or resignation.
