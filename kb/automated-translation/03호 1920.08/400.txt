小說 幸福
						아ㄹ치바아새푸 (原作), 憑虛生 (譯)

After the prostitute Sashuga's nose fell off, her lovely and beautiful face became like a rotting seashell. Sashuga's life lost everything Sashuga herself proudly called life.

All that remained for her was only the ugly and dirty thing. Also, the bright daylight became an endless black night, and the moonless night, conversely, became a limitless white day.

Hunger and cold strangled her weak body. The body, like a half-dead dog or cat (猫), barely had the left and right breasts like field (田) furrows and the uneven joints like a public cemetery barely attached. She couldn't help but move from the main street to a desolate alley. And she was in the unfortunate situation of having to permit her body to the dirtiest, most wicked men. One very cold moonlit night, she came to the newly paved road, constructed just this fall. She went towards the edge of the urban area near the railroad tracks, where there were still no houses at all, only wasteland riddled with holes. Here, there was no sound, only the line of streetlights dimly shining, gently mixing with the blue moonlight. The bright, cold moonlight <130> flowed silently over the deathly still construction site.

Black shadows crawling out of the pits looked like ghosts crouching on the ground, and telegraph poles connected with wire (鈇絲) glistened in the moonlight, coated with white frost. The air was dry (乾燥氣), but the chill of the severe frost pierced deep into one's bones. One suspected the whole world might freeze in this cold, that one's whole body was being seared with red-hot iron. Because of this, it felt as if one's body was breaking apart, pieces of flesh falling off here and there on the skin, and the breath exhaled from the mouth came out like a cloud, taking on a blue tint before immediately seeming to freeze.

Sashuga hadn't earned a single penny in these five days. When she was kicked out of the place she had stayed five days ago, even the last remaining corset she had was taken then.

Her small, hunched breasts pressed together, her bent little body seemed to draw lines on the desolate stone-paved road shining in the moonlight, looking pitiful.

She thought that she was utterly alone in the whole world and would never be able to escape this desolate territory.

With each step, one step, two steps, her freezing feet gathered intense pain as time passed, second by second, minute by minute. The stinging pain felt like walking barefoot on a gravel field, bleeding.

Coming to the middle of this gloomy wasteland, Sashuga began to weep, deeply contemplating the terror of her meaningless existence. Tears welled up in her swollen eyes, which could barely move from the cold, flowed into the dark hole of the scar where her nose used to be, and froze. There was no one to see those tears. The moon shone as before, scattering its clear, pure, blue-tinged rays as before.

No one came. An indescribable feeling surged up ever more strongly, fiercely, unbearably. She thought that in such a state, a person would surely fall into beastly despair and scream like mad. She thought she would scream madly to the ends of the earth, to the very end of this world. But the person remained silent. Shivering convulsively, just grinding her teeth <131>.

Sashuga offered a prayer. "I want to die, I just want to die." But she soon stopped.

Just then, suddenly, the black shadow of a man appeared on the white road. The shadow approached quickly. The sound of crunching snow could be heard incessantly. She saw the moonlight glinting on the man's sheepskin collar. Sashuga recognized him as an employee of the factory at the end of this road.

Sashuga stood by the roadside and waited for the man. She alternately grasped her sleeves with both hands, buried her head between her shoulders, and stomped her feet. Her lips felt like they were made of rubber. And they moved very sluggishly, unnaturally. Sashuga was terribly worried that she might not even be able to utter a word.

"Hey there," she managed to make a barely audible sound.

The passing man turned his face slightly towards the woman, then suddenly turned his feet and strode quickly past. Sashuga ran with all her might. Chasing after the man, she said in a seemingly forced intimate tone,

"Hey... let's go together... really, yes... let's go right now... I'll show you something good, you'll laugh so hard your sides will hurt... yes, come on, let's go... yes, that would be nice, right? It's true. I'll really show you anything, so let's go. Yes, hey..."

The passing man paid no attention to the woman and strode quickly on. His unmoving face had eyes like glass, round like something inanimate.

Sashuga was already retreating before the man as if dancing, hunched her shoulders once more, made a desolate whining sound, and trembling violently in the cold,

"Hey. I'm like this right now... my body is clean... my place isn't far, let's go... come on, eh..." <132>

The moon hung high over the plain, and Sashuga's voice vibrated strangely weakly in the cold night air submerged in moonlight.

"Eh, come on, let's go-" Sashuga gasped, barely speaking, and almost fell over. But she still retreated before the man as before. "Yes, yes, if you dislike it... then give me 2 gunibenik (about 20 Cheon)... for bread... I've starved since this morning... give me... yes, then give me even one guribenik... eh? Hey..."

When the two reached a very deserted place, the man, without a word, came slightly closer to the woman, his strange, glass-like eyes still roundly reflecting the moonlight, like something inanimate.

Suddenly, a thought of absolute desperation pierced the woman's head.

"I'll do whatever you want... definitely... I'll show you a good sight. I'm quite good at thinking up such things... Shall I take off my top?... And sit on this snow?... I can endure it well for 5 minutes... Yes, you can look at your watch... Yes, definitely... I'll sit for 10 gobegaen (1 gobegaen is 1/10th of a gunibenik)... It'll be really funny, won't it? Yes, hey."

The man stopped dead. His glass-like eyes seemed to show some feeling arising. The man laughed with intermittent, choked sounds.

Sashuga also stood before the man. Shivering from the cold, she tried hard to laugh along. And she couldn't take her eyes off the man's hands and face for even a moment.

"Look here. What if I gave you 5 rubles instead of 10 gobegaen?" the man said, looking behind him.

Sashuga trembled with cold. She didn't believe the man's words. She remained silent.

"Here.... listen... you take off your clothes and stand there naked. Then I will strike you ten times. <133>
Half a [ruble] for each strike. Got it?" the man said, laughing silently with a shudder.

"How can I? It's too cold. It's a terrible thing to do," Sashuga said pleadingly. The fear of shock, severe hunger (餓), and anxiety pierced the woman's body nervously, convulsively.

"What? Such a thing... Instead, won't you get Five Rubles? Of course, it's because it's cold. Because it's painful."

"It will hurt terribly, if you hit me"... Sashuga mumbled hesitantly, as if resentful.

"Eh, what's what- 'it will hurt'- just endure it a bit, that's all. Then you will get the money, Five Rubles."

The man strode quickly away.

Sashuga trembled even more.

"Hey- then just give me five gobe[gaen], yes..."

The man strode further away.

Sashuga tried to grab the man's hand. The man raised his hand as if to strike her. And suddenly flew into a rage. The woman retreated.

"Hey... hey. I'll do it. Yes, hey," Sashuga pleaded.

The man stopped and looked back.

"Alright-" the man said simply through his teeth.

Sashuga stood uncertainly, then slowly began to take off her clothes. Her frozen fingers felt like they weren't hers. And for some reason, she couldn't tear her eyes away from the glass-like eyes of the man before her.

"Come on- hurry up... someone might come..." the man said again. <134>

The cold stabbed the naked Sashuga from all directions. The woman's breath caught. Suddenly, it felt as if red-hot iron was being poured all over her body.

It felt like her frozen skin was being cut into pieces.

"Hurry up and hit me," Sashuga mumbled, turning her back to the man. The woman's lower and upper teeth chattered violently against each other.

The woman stood naked before the man. Her small, thin body standing in the clean snow-covered night, immersed in moonlight and cold air, was indeed a peculiar sight.

"Alright-" the man said, panting, "Hey... if you just endure it... this is it. Five Rubles... If you can't endure it or scream, then hurry up and leave..."

"Just hit me, whatever... I'll endure it..." the woman's lips, seemingly about to fall off from the cold, mumbled. Her whole body trembled violently.

The man stepped aside, suddenly raised the cane he was carrying, and with all his strength, struck the woman's skinny back. The whip-like force pierced through the woman's frozen body to her brain. Everything around the woman seemed to merge into a single feeling of terrible pain. "Ah," a short, startled sound burst from Sashuga's lips.

Sashuga stumbled a few steps away, pressing hard on the spot she was hit with her pale, trembling hands.

"Move your hands... move your hands..." the man shouted, panting as he followed her.

Sashuga brought her forearms together. The second blow immediately gave the woman unbearable pain.

The woman screamed, fell, and put her hands on the ground. Again and again, whip-like, harsh blows fell on the woman's naked body.

"Nine," the low sound of counting fell from the man's lips. And again, a new sound like lightning <135> passed over the woman's flesh. Something seemed to burst. Like a frozen sweet potato bursting. Red blood spurted vigorously onto the white snow. The piled snow formed red holes where the blood fell. Her tightly clenched stomach shone in the moonlight. At this moment, her left breast struck the ground with a thud. The breast bounced up with a pathetic short sound.

"Get up," a voice sounded as if from far away. Sashuga was dazed for a moment, then quickly regained her senses. "Alright, get up, take this," a loud voice was heard. "Alright- I'm going."

Naked, Sashuga, supporting herself with trembling hands, convulsively gripped the ground and struggled to get up, staggering. Blood trickled down continuously. The feeling of cold had vanished for the woman. She only knew an unprecedented weakness in every joint. She realized her body ached as if torn apart.

The woman gently stroked the bruised, damp spots here and there as she got dressed. It took a long time to pick up and put on the frozen, tattered clothes.

On the moonlit, cold ground, the woman moved silently.

The man's black shadow had disappeared. After finishing dressing, she first looked at her fist. In her blood-stained palm, the 5 Ruble coin glittered like fire.

-Five Rubles- the woman thought. Suddenly, a great joy arose, as if her body had become light. She ran towards the city streets with nimble feet. She clutched the coin tightly in her hand. The jacket stuck tightly to the woman's body, causing great pain, but she paid no attention. The woman's existence was filled with the feeling of happiness... Food, warmth, safety, and besides, the fact that she had just been beaten half to death by someone else didn't even cross her mind anymore.

-Good. Truly good... Now I won't crawl like that anymore...- the woman rejoiced. And she entered a narrow alley. The bright lights of a night eatery already glittered before her... (End)...<136>

**Summary**

This translated story depicts the extreme desperation of Sashuga, a prostitute disfigured by disease and living in abject poverty during a harsh winter. Shunned and starving, she encounters a man on a desolate road. In a final act of desperation for money for food and warmth, she agrees to strip naked and allow him to beat her ten times with a cane in the freezing snow for five rubles. Despite the horrific pain and humiliation, the moment she receives the money, she is overcome with a feeling of immense happiness and relief, focusing solely on the prospect of food and shelter, the trauma momentarily forgotten as she heads towards an eatery.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as an early example (1920) of translating foreign (Russian) literature into Korean, published in the influential magazine *Kaebyŏk* (개벽). It reflects an engagement with international literary currents. The stark portrayal of suffering and the complex, perhaps shocking, depiction of "happiness" derived from enduring extreme degradation could have resonated with contemporary readers experiencing harsh social realities under colonial rule, or it might have served as a purely literary exploration of human psychology under duress. The translator's identity (P'yŏnghŏsaeng) and the specific linguistic choices (archaic spellings, vocabulary) are also valuable artifacts for linguistic and cultural history.

賣淫婦 사슈가의 코가 떠어진 뒤는 그 어엽브고 고운 얼골이 썩어가는 조개 껍질가티 되엇다. 사슈가의 생명은 사슈가가 스스로 生命이라고 자랑하는 모든 것을 일허바렷다.
그에게 남은 것은 다만 醜하고 더러운 그것뿐이요. 또 한가지는 밝은 낫빗이 끗업는 검은 밤이 되고 그믐밤은 도로혀 限업는 白晝가 될 따름이다.
飢寒은 그의 弱한 몸을 졸라맨다. 몸이라고 하는 것은 半쯤 죽어가는 개나 대이(猫)모양으로 겨우 밧(田)이랑가튼 左右의 가슴과 共同墓地가티 울퉁불퉁한 뼈마듸만 겨우 부터잇슬 뿐이다. 그는 큰길로부터 쓸쓸한 골목길로 옴기지 안을 수 업게 되엇다. 그리고 第一 더럽고 第一 고약한 男子에게 몸을 許諾하게 된 불상한 신세이다. 어느날 몹시 추운 달밤에 그는 올 가을에 처음으로 닥근 新作路에 왓다. 그 길은 鐵路길 近處 市街地 맨끗 아즉도 집이라고는 하나도 업고 다못 구녕만 숭숭뚤린 荒蕪地만 잇는 젓짝으로 갓다. 여긔서는 아모 소리도 업고 길가에켠 등불의 行列이 다만 히미이 비치어 푸른 달빗과 가티 은근이 석겨비칠 뿐이다. 밝고 찬 달빗흔＜130＞ 죽은 듯이 建築場우을 고요히 흘너 잇다.
구덩으로부터 기어나오는 거믄 그림자는 鬼神이 땅우에 웅쿠리고 안진 것 갓고 鈇絲로 이어 노은 電信기동은 힌서리을 마저 달빗헤 번적인다. 空燥氣는 乾하나 甚한 서리의 찬긔운은 사람의 뼈속을 숙숙 수신다. 이 추이에 싸이여 온 世界가 어러바리지나 아니할가 온 몸이 빨가케 단쇠로 찌지는 것이 안인가 疑心하게 되엇다. 이럼으로 사람의 몸은 부서저서 皮膚의 여게 저게 살점이 떠러지는 것 갓고 입으로 吐하는 숨은 구름가티 나와 푸른 빗을 띄고 올라가다가 곳 어러보이지 안는다.
사슈가는 이 다새동안에 한푼돈도 버어러 본 일이 업섯다. 그 닷세前에 머무러 잇던 住所에서 쫏겨 나올 때에 다맛 골샛드 하나 남을 것조차 그때 빼겻섯다.
그 쩍고도 우크린 左右가슴은 탄대 붓치고 굽프린 적은 몸은 달빗에 빗치는 쓸쓸한 돌깐 길우헤 금을 거리고 가여간는 것 갓다.
自己는 온 世界에 다맛이한모이 다시는 이 荒凉한 領地를 버서 나지 못할 줄 生覺하엿다.
한거름 두검 발자최띄는 대로 어러가는 발은 秒秒分分이 時間가는 대로 몹시 압홈이 모여던다. 그 쓸아리고 압흔 것이 피흘너는 맨발노 자갈밧을 거러가는 것 갓다.
이 음산한 荒蕪地 한복판에 와서 사슈가는 처음으로 自己의 意味업는 存在의 무서운 것을 곰곰 생각하고 울기 시작하엿다. 어러서 움적이지도 잘못하는 부은 눈에 눈물이 그렁그렁 넘치여 코대신 움석드러간 헌듸 자최의 어두운 구넝속에 흘러드러가 어럿다. 어느뉘 하나 그 눈물 본이는 업다. 달은 前과 가티 빗취고 前과 가티 맑고 맑은 푸른빗 석긴 光線을 헛텃다.
어느 뉘 하나 오는 이 업다. 엇다타 말할 수 업는 心思가 더욱 더욱 힘잇기 猛烈하기 치어빗처 올라와 견딀수 업다. 어러키되면 사람은 곳 野獸的 自暴自棄에 빠저 밋처 부르지질 것이라고 생갹하엿다. 이 地球끗까지 이 世上끗에 끗까지 밋처 부르지질 것이라고 하엿다. 그러나 사람은 默默히 잇다. 痙攣的으로 떨며서 니만 갈아 ＜131＞ 부치는 것이다.
사슈가는 祈禱을 올인다. 「나는 죽고 십허 다만 죽고 십허」 그러나 곳 근치엿다.
그리하자 不意에 힌길에 사나희의 거믄 그림자가 낫하낫다. 그 그림자는 急한 거름으로 이리 갓가이 온다. 쉬일새업시 바삭바삭 눈밟는 소리가 들인다. 그 사나희의 羊皮 읏깃에 달빗이 번적이는 것이 보인다. 사슈가는 그 사나희가 이길 끗헤 잇는 工場에 雇人인 줄 보아 알랏다.
사슈가는 길가에 서서 그 사나희을 기다렷다. 두 손으로 제 소매을 번갈라가며 잡이다리며 머리을 두 억개사이에 파뭇고 발로 땅을 굴이엿다. 입술은 고무로 맨던 것 갓햇다. 그리고 아주 둔하기 不自由롭기 움적일 뿐이엿다. 발셔 말한마듸 못하지나 아니할가 사슈가는 그것을 펵 근심하엿다.
