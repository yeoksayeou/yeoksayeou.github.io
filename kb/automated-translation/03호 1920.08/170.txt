Magazine Title: 개벽 (開闢) 제3호
Publication Date: 1920년 08월 25일
Article Title: 諧謔 (Humor/Jokes)
Author: 새봄 (Saebom - "New Spring")
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

諧謔
						새봄

내의 血統을 아노라!
나는 나의 血統을 아도다.
꺼질줄을 모르는 불길이로다.
늘 스스로 炎上하며
늘 나이가 燒盡하도다.
나의 잡는 모든 것 빗이 되미어
나의 버리는 모든 것 숫(炭)이 되도다.
그러타 나는 불길이로다.
꺼질 줄 모르는 불길이로다.

I know my Bloodline (血統)!
I know my Bloodline.
It is a flame that knows not how to die out.
Always blazing up by itself,
Always consuming my age.
Everything I grasp becomes light,
Everything I discard becomes charcoal (炭).
Yes, I am a flame.
A flame that knows not how to die out.

엇더케 내 별이 되리!
님 늘 그리는 님
나는 넘우 갓가히 그를 두고 십지 안타.
더-먼 곳에 더-놉흔 곳에 두어 보고저
그러치 안으면 엇더케 내 별(星)이 되리!.

How shall I become a star!
O beloved one, always longed for,
I do not wish to keep you too close.
I want to place you further away, higher up.
Otherwise, how shall I become a star (星)!

一切의 울음을 그치라
決코 丐乞일랑 말지라
그리고 一切의 울음을 그치라
取하라 神의 懇願이니 그저 取하라.

Stop all weeping!
Absolutely do not resort to begging (丐乞)!
And stop all weeping.
Take! It is God's earnest plea, so just take.

아즉도 어리다
녹(錆)도 必要하다.
銳利만으로는 아즉 不充分하도다.
그러치 못하면 언제던지 말성이다
『아즉도 어리다』고 『좀 不足하다』고
녹도 必要하다 당신은 너모 銳利해!.

Still young
Rust (錆) is also necessary.
Sharpness (銳利) alone is still insufficient.
If not so, it's always trouble.
Saying, "Still young," saying, "A bit lacking,"
Rust is also necessary. You are too sharp (銳利)!

그것 먹을 것 생겻네
平野에 나리지 마라. 蛇蝎이 덤비리라.
山巓에 오르지 마라. 虎狼이 여보리라.
『낫도 놉도 안은 居其中하야
얌전한 樓閣이 잇섯스면...』 이것이 世智
그러나 平凡의 巨魔는 그것 먹을 것 생겻다고.

Something to devour has appeared
Do not descend to the Plain (平野). Vipers and scorpions (蛇蝎) will attack.
Do not climb the Mountain Peak (山巓). Tigers and wolves (虎狼) will spy you.
"Neither low nor high, residing in between,
If only there were a modest Pavilion (樓閣)..." This is Worldly Wisdom (世智).
But the Giant Demon (巨魔) of the Ordinary sees something to devour has appeared.

嘲笑할 外에 업도다
나는 내 스스로의 집에 살도다.
엇던 사람에게 엇던 무엇을 模倣함 업소라. ＜78＞
제 主人한아도 변변히 못웃기는 저 大家들 나는 嘲笑할 外에 업도다.

Nothing but ridicule
I live in my own house.
There is no imitation (模倣) of any person or any thing. <78>
Those great masters (大家) who cannot even properly amuse their own Master (主人) - I have nothing but ridicule for them.

참 友人이 되기까지
同侔여 알겟지?
땅우의 모든 幸福은 싸음이 주는 것
『참 友人이 되기까지에는
火藥냄시가 第一이라고』
엇던 哲人은 일부러 내 귀에

Until becoming true Friends (友人)
Comrade (同侔), you understand, right?
All happiness on earth is given by struggle.
"Until becoming true Friends (友人),
The smell of gunpowder (火藥) is the best,"
Some Philosopher (哲人) deliberately whispered in my ear.

君子大路行
里程表의 左右엽헤
두 形狀 업듸엇스니
그름이 가르되 大路와 險路.
路畔사람, 저마다 손바닥처하는 말
『君子大路行』이지
十字街 등에 진 큰 어룬 홀로 소리처하는 말
좁은 門으로 들어가거라.
死亡으로 引導하는 門은  크고 그 길이 또 넓의되
生命으로 引導하는 門은  크고 그 길이 또 險하니라.

A Gentleman Walks the Main Road (君子大路行)
To the left and right of the Milestone (里程表)
Two forms were prostrate;
Their names indicated: Main Road (大路) and Treacherous Path (險路).
People by the roadside, each clapping their hands, saying,
"A Gentleman Walks the Main Road (君子大路行)," right?
A great elder bearing the Crossroad (十字街) on his back, shouting alone,
"Enter through the narrow Gate (門)!
For the Gate (門) that leads to Death (死亡) is large and its way is wide,
But the Gate (門) that leads to Life (生命) is small and its way is treacherous (險)."

헛배부른 滯症人
그는 貧乏兒이다. 無一物이다.
그러나 그는 빼앗긴 것이 안이라
모든 것을 제 스스로 抛棄한 것뿐이다.
그가 무엇을 介意할가나.
그는 벌서 新發見에 滋味가 한창
新傳說에 깃븜이 가득
그의 貧乏을 貧乏이라 嘲笑하기는
헛배 부른 滯症人의 헛소리일뿐.

The Indigestive Person (滯症人) Full of Hot Air
He is a Poor Child (貧乏兒). Has Nothing (無一物).
But he has not been robbed;
He has merely discarded (抛棄) everything himself.
What would he care (介意)?
He is already deeply relishing New Discoveries (新發見),
Full of joy in New Legends (新傳說).
To ridicule his Poverty (貧乏) as Poverty (貧乏)
Is merely the nonsense of an Indigestive Person (滯症人) full of hot air.

貴치 안은 讚美
甲 사람은 自己와 同等人으로부터 뿐 讚美를 밧는 것이다.
乙 그러타. 그대를 讚美하는 者는 同一語調로 그대에게 말하리라. 『그대는 꼭 나와 同等이야』라고? 讚美!, 과연 貴치 안은 것이다.

Praise (讚美) Not Precious
Person A: One only receives praise (讚美) from those equal (同等) to oneself.
Person B: That's right. The one who praises (讚美) you will say to you in the same tone (同一語調), "You are exactly my equal (同等)"? Praise (讚美)! Indeed, it is not precious.

내 同侔는 말하얏다.
『버릇이라 함은 사람의 先入見
善惡이라 함은 神의 先入見
神이라 함은 憧憬의 錯覺』
뿐이라고 내 同侔는 말하엿다...

My Comrade (同侔) said.
"Habit (버릇) is human Prejudice (先入見),
Good and Evil (善惡) are God's Prejudice (先入見),
God (神) is the Illusion (錯覺) of Longing (憧憬)."
That's all, my Comrade (同侔) said...

眞實은 무엇?
甲 내 것은 眞理이다. 그러나 네 것은...
乙 내 것이 眞實이다. 네 것은 確實히...
그러면 結局 사람의 眞實은 무엇?
누구나 反駁치 못하는 誤診일 뿐.＜79＞

What is Truth (眞實)?
Person A: Mine is Truth (眞理). But yours...
Person B: Mine is Reality (眞實). Yours is certainly...
Then, in the end, what is human Truth (眞實)?
Merely a Misdiagnosis (誤診) that no one can refute (反駁).<79>
<78-79>

**Summary**

This collection of short, aphoristic poems by "Saebom" explores themes of fierce self-identity, ambition, independence, the necessity of hardship, authenticity versus imitation, the nature of friendship forged in struggle, the choice between easy conformity and a difficult path to life, the value of self-chosen poverty over complacent "fullness," the emptiness of conventional praise, and a cynical view of habit, morality, God, and truth itself, concluding that human "truth" is merely an irrefutable misdiagnosis. The pieces are characterized by a strong, assertive voice, a rejection of convention, and paradoxical insights.

From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the intellectual ferment and burgeoning national consciousness of 1920s colonial Korea. The emphasis on a unique "bloodline," the rejection of imitation ("I live in my own house... no imitation"), the critique of established "masters," the valuing of struggle ("The smell of gunpowder is best" for friendship), and the call to choose the difficult path ("Enter through the narrow Gate") can be interpreted as veiled expressions of a desire for Korean self-determination and cultural authenticity in the face of colonial suppression and assimilation pressures. The questioning of established notions of truth and morality might also reflect the disillusionment and search for new values characteristic of the era.
