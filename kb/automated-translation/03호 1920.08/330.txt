**Body Text Translation:**
Past and Future
One must not think that today's World (世界) is something eternally unchanging. Unknowingly, the ways of the World (世態) are changing. For that reason, we must take the Past as our Weapon (武器) and plan for new Construction (建設) in the Future. (Goethe)

Time
Time is the most impartial Judge (裁判長). (Wills)

Pleasure
The most joyful Pleasure (快樂) lies on the other shore (彼岸) of Pain (苦痛) and Sorrow (悲哀); to obtain this, one must die in Pain and Sorrow and be resurrected (復活) in Pain and Sorrow. (Romain Rolland)

Victory
Soul (靈魂)! The price of your Victory (勝利) is truly painful. He who loses Everything (一切) gains Everything. Ah, what is lost is indeed Eternal (永遠) Possession (所有). (Ibsen)<114>

**Summary**

This untitled collection of aphorisms from Kaebyŏk magazine presents quotes emphasizing change, the judgment of time, finding joy through suffering, and achieving ultimate victory through loss. Attributed to Western thinkers like Goethe, Romain Rolland, and Ibsen, these themes likely resonated with Korean readers during the colonial period. For historians, this piece offers insight into the intellectual currents and the kind of philosophical encouragement sought by Koreans under Japanese rule. The focus on enduring hardship, embracing change, and finding paradoxical strength in loss suggests a message of resilience and hope for a future transformation, potentially reflecting nationalist aspirations veiled in universal philosophical terms.

기사제목	[무제]
기사형태	잡저

過去와 將來
오늘날 世界를 恒久不變의 者라고 생각하여서는 안이된다. 아지 못하게 世態는 變하여가는 것이다. 그 까닭에 우리는 過去를 武器삼고 將來 새 建設을 圖謀하지 아니하면 안이된다.(괴테)

時間
時間은 가장 公正한 裁判長이니라(윌쓰)

快樂
가장 즐거운 快樂은 苦痛과 悲哀의 彼岸에 잇나니 이것을 어드랴면 苦痛과 悲哀에서 죽고 苦痛과 悲哀에서 復活하여야 한다.(로만 로란)

勝利
靈魂아! 너의 苦痛을 참으로 勝利의 갑슨 괴롭다. 一切를 일허바리는 者는 一切를 엇는다. 아아 일허바린 것이 곳 永遠의 所有로다.(입쎈)＜114＞
