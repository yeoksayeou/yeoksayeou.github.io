잡지명	개벽 제3호
발행일	1920년 08월 25일
기사제목	吾人의 新紀元을 宣言하노라 (We Declare Our New Era)
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)

We Declare Our New Era

We all have the habit of boasting about the great length of our country's history and the antiquity of our family lineages. We consider this our extended life, looking back upon it to console ourselves, to satisfy our hunger, and to become prisoners of it.

The antiquity of history! Should we truly boast so much about it? Must we do so to feel pleasure? Length implies duration, but it also implies staleness. Staleness implies stagnation, and stagnation implies decay. If this is so, then the antiquity of our history, however little, signifies the stagnation and decay of our lives to that extent. Is stagnation and decay truly something for us to boast about? <2>

From our perspective, the inheritance passed down to us consists mostly of conventions, empty forms, memorial days, and ritual days. At the same time, if our history is ancient, it means we have inherited that much more of this legacy, and it confesses that our obsession and attachment to that legacy are correspondingly strong. Is possessing relatively more of that bothersome legacy and that sticky attachment truly something for us to boast about?

Without needing to point to any specific nation or people, the history of this thing called human life has indeed been long. It has been about 200,000 years since we acquired the name "humanity," and it is said to be about 5,000 years since the formation of states. Observing the sequence of the four seasons, even clinging to the same state for three months seems excessive, as spring, summer, autumn, and winter constantly alternate. Observing the breathing of animals, they cannot endure even a moment's pause, exchanging the old for the new every second and minute. Ah, 200,000 years of stagnation! 5,000 years of adherence to convention! What an astonishing accumulation of staleness. Even if there were some waves and revolutionary fears during that time...

Alas! Our history has been excessively stale, and our conventional habits have become excessively ingrained. Our calendars are filled with memorial days and ritual days, and every corner of the Earth is saturated with corpses and dregs. The well-named laws of our ancestors have cruelly stripped us of our complete selves, and contemptible dog-like individuals, standing under hateful egoism, openly pierce others' walls and ruthlessly trample on others' human rights without hesitation. Alas! The world today is breathing faintly. The air surrounding it is already extremely heavy and turbid, and the blood <3> nourishing it has already promised to stop circulating; it will suffocate sooner or later. Its filial sons and loyal wives pray for its longevity, wishing it ten thousand years, but in the gloomy atmosphere, its end will come.

However, the heart-wrenching dirge on the sunset mountain path, bidding farewell to the old era, vanished along with the setting sun. And one morning, after the rain stopped and the clouds rolled away, a fresh morning sun shone forth. Thunderous applause erupted on all sides, and the myriad masses of the world pledged to become the new legitimate children of the new world. Thus, we dare to declare the establishment of a new era and, at the same time, present the principles we stand for under this new era, hoping to share this feeling with our readers, our brothers and sisters, who are filled with new joy.

One. We Will Live Only as Human Beings

If there has been any effort in our past history until today, it has been merely to liberate us humans a bit more. In other words, it has been merely to make us a group that tries to live more as human beings. Consider this: although the eras and phrasing differ, which of the continuous cries or speculations of religious figures and philosophers was not an effort for human liberation (whether from idols, classes, the self, or other things)? Similarly, political revolutions and religious revolutions largely contained this essence. The 15th-century Renaissance is widely proclaimed as the central axis constituting all of today's civilization, but its reality was merely the movement of self-awakening <4> — escaping from the tyranny of established religious authority or convention, saying, "Now we too will live as humans," meaning, "I am not a slave to convention, nor a servant of God, but only a human being." Furthermore, what are called the new thoughts and new movements of the modern era may have two or three methods, but their essence uniformly lies in saying, "We are all humans, so let's live as fellow humans without distinctions."

Reflecting quietly, until today, we have often failed to live as human beings. To put it bluntly, we have lived as men and women, as masters and slaves, as rulers and subjects, as fathers and sons, as adults and children, as sages and commoners, as rich and poor, as spirit and flesh, as strong and weak, but we have not lived as humans—as complete human beings. Therefore, in this world, there are men and women, but no humans; there are strong and weak, but no humans; there are counterfeit versions of this and that, but genuine human character is extinct. This is the reason for calling for the great reconstruction of the world today and the rationale for proclaiming a new era for humanity. From now on, we must not live merely as men and women, not merely as masters and slaves, not merely as rulers-subjects and fathers-sons, not merely as adults-children, not merely as sages-commoners, not merely as spirit and flesh, not merely as rich-noble-strong-weak, but solely as pure human beings. All the cries today—called the world's new thought, new movement, reconstruction, creation—are merely means to realize living as human beings. We urgently proclaim this as the foremost principle of our future lives. <5>

One. We Must Remember Only the Fulfillment and Expansion of Life

Humans strive to recognize illusions beyond themselves; lower-level humans even draw upon water, fire, wood, and stone to make objects of their own faith. However, in this day when each of us has discovered our own great life, such things are no longer an issue. The only thing we should trust is our will to live—that is, the will to power (勢力意志). Just as all living things, whether plants or animals, ceaselessly yearn for the fulfillment of life, the expansion of life—that is, the expression of the will to power—this world consists only of the world of the will to power, that is, the world of instinct. Is human life itself truly good or evil? We do not know this. Also, is being born into human life truly fortunate or unfortunate? We wish to pursue this question. However, it is an indisputable fact that from the moment of birth, human life desires the fullness of its life and ceaselessly seeks the expansion of that life to the extent of its power. Therefore, we must simply rely on this instinct and faithfully respond to the demands of that life. Our bodies prefer constant, vigorous movement, whether bitter or sweet, over sluggish stillness; responding to this, we should act powerfully in this broad stage, this society full of tasks, even using our own corpses as steps for advancement. Our spirit refuses to be imprisoned by anything else, always desiring to soar freely like the great Peng bird covering ten thousand li; our entire will always seeks expansion beyond the current state; and our total emotion desires to run free, greatly abhorring becoming a person confined within etiquette or models. We must not obstruct this desire, <6> and as long as it does not betray the great righteousness of humanity, we should solely respond to it, contributing to the completion and expansion of life. Engaging in futile debates about pessimism, optimism, this world, or the next world within a lifespan of less than a hundred years is something we, standing under the new era, cannot tolerate.

One. We Must Remember That Everyone Is an Unfinished Product.

We are, first of all, unfinished products as human beings. If there is some stage beyond being human, we are naturally unfinished products concerning that too. Why? Because humans are not machines or minerals but are certainly superior examples of living beings, possessing something called life. That life possesses a kind of primitive impulse, and spurred and encouraged by that impulse, it has the disposition to evolve infinitely at all times. At the same time, no matter how holy a person among us humans may be, they are not a completed product who has already exhausted all that we can become or enjoy, but merely a relatively superior being. To speak pointedly, just as the Zhang San and Li Si (張三李四 - ordinary people) we regularly encounter are unfinished products, so too are Confucius (孔子), Shakyamuni (釋迦), Jesus (耶穌), and others whom we revere like gods also unfinished products. Being unfinished products, we all have imperfections and flaws. Because of this, there will be errors and deficiencies in everything discovered or established by us humans. That is, just as the teachings of sages are not absolute truths, the systems of the past are not necessarily unchangeable. We must view everyone, past and present, as a kind of unfinished peer. At the same time, we can harbor total doubt towards all rituals, models, and morals remaining in the present, and we can also completely retune everything according to our preferences and today's circumstances. Furthermore, since we are unfinished products, <7> today we will chew on the gospel of a new life superior to yesterday's, and tomorrow, superior to today's. If, by chance, we were to become finished products, having already reached a state of completion and no longer recognizing hope for the future, our life would already be extinguished; there could be no greater misfortune than this. Herein, we declare that human life is still an unfinished product. Without adding self-imposed rational criticisms like "Why...", we sweep away inertia such as "Because the gods are holy, the saints taught it, or because it has been done this way since ancient times!". We dare to shout that we possess the innate nature to infinitely reform and infinitely create.

One. Remember That Longevity (in our common saying) Is a Kind of Misfortune

We habitually say, "May you have long life, wealth, and many sons," and also, "Among the five blessings, longevity comes first," and, "A dead prime minister is worse off than a living stray dog." Thus, long life has become our greatest desire.
However, surveying the lifespans of all things in the world, inanimate, inorganic minerals have lifespans like heaven and earth—boundless. The lifespan of plants, one step evolved from minerals, comes next. The lifespan of animals, another step evolved from plants, follows that. And the lifespan of human life, transcending animals by another step, is limited to merely around a hundred years, leading ancients and moderns alike to chant, "Life to seventy is rare since ancient times." Seen this way, the lowest-ability things have the longest lifespans, and the highest-level things have the shortest lifespans. Judged by our sentiments, it seems generosity is shown to the unworthy, and stinginess to the worthy.

Perhaps the lifespan of a being refers to the period during which its current state is maintained in this world. In that case, the <8> length or shortness of a lifespan refers to the length or shortness of the period of maintaining the current state. However, maintaining the current state generally implies stagnation and regression for that thing. If the period of maintenance is long, damage will be incurred corresponding to that length; if it is short, profit will be gained corresponding to that shortness. But the fundamental way to change the current state is perhaps to completely overturn the present existence of a being and replace it with a distinctly new phenomenon, namely "death"; there is no other way. Therefore, the death of a living being is nothing but a means taken to further evolve that life. Thus, the length of a living being's lifespan is inversely proportional to its degree of evolution. In this sense, the shortening of our human lifespan to around a hundred years indicates our highest rate of evolution and is the reason why we stand at the highest peak of evolution, unattainable by general living beings. If, by some misfortune, humans from the primitive era had enjoyed their lives unchanged until today, they would have continued the savage and ugly state like the indigenous people of Taiwan (臺灣) or perhaps the natives of the African continent (亞弗利加洲); they would not have even glimpsed the brilliant civilization of the 20th century today.

We praise the shortening of our lifespan and the frequency of birth and death because the speed of evolution and progress increases due to this change. However, even if birth and death were repeated daily, without the effort for evolution and progress, that birth and death would be exactly the same as the birth and death of cattle and horses. The lifespan of cattle and horses is much shorter than ours, yet their form remains unchanged. Therefore, although our life may be short, the days of that life must be filled with the spirit of effort and progress, directly transmitting that momentum to our descendants, for only then can we say we have lived our lifespan completely. A lifespan otherwise <9> may be the lifespan of dogs and horses, but it is not the lifespan of humanity, endowed with reason. Looking at ordinary people in the world until today, they worry about the disappearance of their own lives, anxiously scrambling for mere existence, yet observing their reality, most not only fail to further extend the life they were innately endowed with but even damage the lifespan they received. For when we say we lived yesterday and live again today, it means that today's life has undergone some positive change from yesterday's life. If today's life has no change whatsoever from yesterday's life, it may be yesterday's life, but it is not today's life. If a lifetime is 70 years, then all 25,550 days of those 70 years must be like this to be said to have fully lived that lifespan. Yet, the common way of the world does not follow this path; today is like yesterday, and this year is like last year, merely ending one's life under the grace of passing time. This is truly living drunk and dying in a dream, a great blasphemy against life, and a villain on the path of human evolution. There is no need to question how things were before today. At this juncture, as the era becomes new, we must recognize that our lifespan's lack of length is a great convenience for evolution and progress, be grateful for it ourselves, and take another step forward to live the lifespan we have received completely, ensuring that we do not perform the farce of having our own memorial day within our life story. Shakespeare (쉑스피어) said, "Great men have but one death, not two." Does this not speak precisely of that matter?

One. Remember That the Earth (땅) Is Mother

This is not a particularly new saying; "Heaven and Earth are Parents" (天地父母) has been passed down since ancient times. Yet, for some reason lately, the words of God the Father <10> are widely transmitted, but the words of Mother Earth have completely vanished. God (하느님) refers to Spirit (神). Spirit cannot be imagined apart from human life itself, and human life cannot be imagined apart from its nurturing mother, the Earth. Ultimately, for us, there is only the nurturing Mother Earth. What is a living being, fundamentally? It is merely an embodiment of the soil (土). The account in Genesis that humans were made from dust (土) also signifies this. Some may seek a Father other than the Earth, citing the existence of a spirit (靈) besides the flesh (肉), but what is called spirit is merely the skillful condensation of the great atmosphere breathed by Mother Earth. Thus, however one thinks about it, we cannot acknowledge anything for us other than the pure Mother Earth.

Brothers and sisters, be loyal to the Earth! In former days, blaspheming the ancient sages or perhaps blaspheming the Heavenly Father was the greatest sin. However, today, when the ancient sages have become our peers, fellow unfinished products, and the existence of the Heavenly Father is difficult to acknowledge, we cannot help but remember that blaspheming the Earth is the greatest sin. Do not call this a forced interpretation. The great Earth that embraces us is a great crystallization of the great mystery of the universe. From a single blade of grass or tree in the fields to a single stone or grain of sand in the mountains and rivers, there is nothing that is not a materialized form or attire of that mystery, and humanity is the most prominent among them. Therefore, when we empty our minds and quiet our spirits, entering the bosom of the great Earth, it is exactly like an infant returning to its loving mother, feeling innocent joy and pleasure. At the same time, we directly touch the very heartstrings of the great cosmic mystery—the source of our own genesis—and taste a great purity like the purity of the very moment of birth. In this complex world, without entering such <11> meditative absorption (三昧 - sammae), even for a time, how could one possibly fulfill one's own mission? And upon entering her bosom, one unknowingly gains the most sincere realization that not only humanity but all living things in this world are born from the same womb of Mother Earth, and that they are truly our brothers and sisters. One cannot help but offer the prayer, "Mother, with the pure sincerity you first gave me, I will share sorrows and worries with my many siblings, divide joys and sorrows, and make our lives prosper together." Only by touching this realization can we become pure individuals loyal to ourselves, possess the great sincerity of sharing destiny with all humanity, and have the great realization of participating in the transformative nurturing of Heaven and Earth. Without achieving this, merely speaking of compatriots and siblings or extending love from kin to all things based solely on temporary documents and thoughts is falsehood upon falsehood—merely becoming charlatans who trifle with the world. In this sense, from now on, we urge everyone, above all else, to return to the nurturing bosom of the Great Earth to embody great purity, and we predict that the day is not far off when all humanity in this world will call out "Mother Earth."

One. We Reject the Supremacy of Mediocrity

Our lives hitherto have been characterized by an indescribable supremacy of mediocrity (平凡至上主義). We do not know what the "Mean" (中庸 - chungyong) truly is, but we always speak of upholding the great Way of the Mean, saying that both excess and deficiency are unacceptable methods. In all affairs, we have adopted the attitude of an utterly demure young lady. Among ways to die, dying wrapped in horsehide on the battlefield or rolling into a ditch were considered great misfortunes in life. Only dying while having one's eyes closed by the hands of women and children, even in a stinking sickroom, <12> was considered achieving a proper death. Thus, under any circumstances, the glory of dying away from home or being killed was denied, and what is called "dying peacefully after a long life" (考終命 - kojongmyong) was counted among the five blessings. When dressing, wear wide robes and long sleeves; when wearing shoes, wear gentleman's boots; when walking, take all day for a ten-li journey; furthermore, keep your mouth still and your feet heavy; and idling about in fine clothes and food was considered the greatest human ideal. Such sluggish appearances truly induced nausea in the world. Although these rustic customs have largely been removed in recent times, it is our great shame to recognize the fact that, due to ingrained habit, a part of them still remains. We declare urgently: mediocrity is the sole friend of destruction, and eliminating this mediocrity from one's own life is truly the beginning of all good.

One. We Rather Urge the Thoroughness of Extremism

Our life is either mediocrity or extremism; extremism or mediocrity. There is no room for an intermediate rate between these, and mediocrity and extremism absolutely cannot coexist. Therefore, for us who reject the supremacy of mediocrity, it is rather natural to revere extremism. There are often those in the world who dislike the radicalness of extremism—this is merely the talk of the ilk who bury themselves in mediocre adherence to the Mean. What worldly affair can be accomplished or improved apart from extremes? Look at the pendulum of a clock: because it swings to extremes on both sides, <13> its reaction causes the clock hands to turn. If that pendulum unfortunately maintained the great virtue of impartiality towards both sides, what would its fate be? Even the civilization we see today was forged through the clash and collision of numerous extreme phenomena over a long period in the past. Look at the great men called great men, the chosen people called chosen people—they all emerged from passionate actions that were extreme. It requires no elaborate examples to show that nothing great has ever come from mediocrity, like adding water to water. It is either life or death, existence or non-existence. For us, there is only this kind of principle.

In short, without asking "Why?", we stand at the beginning of the path of a new global creation (世界的 新開闢). We cannot avoid it even if we wish to, nor is there any reason to avoid it. For us, there is no history before today, no convention, no ritual days, no legends. There is only the first day of the new era, only the first self-awakening of the new human being, only the first step of the new life. Of course, it is not that there are no systems or legends inherited consciously or unconsciously from the people of yesterday. However, these can be our references, but not our main articles; they can be utilized by us, but cannot be our standards. Today, we ourselves must become the creators of good and evil, the establishers of all rituals and models. The task of the new person is truly not light. We must not merely proclaim this with our mouths but act upon it with our hands. Starting first from our immediate selves, extending vigorously to our villages, regions, and nation, we must strive together for the establishment of the new world. This is the first point that we, as people living today, must keenly recognize as our self-awakening.
Brothers and sisters! Throw open your respective windows wide and let in the air of freedom. And gather under the banner of the new era! <14>

**Summary**

This 1920 editorial from *Gaebyeok* magazine passionately declares a "New Era," urging readers to reject the dead weight of history, tradition, convention (因習), and inherited social roles (gender, class, status). It advocates for living authentically as "complete human beings," focusing on the dynamic fulfillment and expansion of life (生의 充實과 擴大), driven by instinct and a "will to power" (勢力意志). The author argues that humans are inherently "unfinished products" (未成品), destined for continuous evolution and creation, making past authorities (sages, institutions) relative, not absolute. Even longevity is reframed as potential stagnation, while change, even death, facilitates progress. Loyalty is redirected from abstract deities or past figures to "Mother Earth" (땅 어머니) as the source of life and universal kinship. The editorial concludes by vehemently rejecting mediocrity (平凡) and embracing thorough "extremism" (極端主義) as necessary for achieving meaningful change and creation in this new epoch. From a historical perspective, the article is significant for its radical modernist and potentially nationalist/anti-colonial stance in 1920s Korea. Its fierce critique of tradition, calls for self-awakening, emphasis on vitality and creation, and the declaration of a "New Era" breaking from the past resonate strongly with the desire for national renewal and liberation from perceived stagnation, which could be implicitly linked to the colonial condition, even without explicitly mentioning Japan. Historians might find the blend of vitalist philosophy (possibly influenced by Nietzsche - "will to power" is mentioned), anti-traditionalism, and the focus on collective self-reinvention particularly interesting for understanding Korean intellectual currents during this period.

吾人의 新紀元을 宣言하노라
吾人은 均히 自國 歷史의 長遠을 자랑하고 自家譜乘의 久遠을 자랑하는 癖을 有하얏스며 그것으로써 自己의 延長的 生命이라 하야 그를 回顧하면서 스스로 慰勞하며 스스로 療飢하며 스스로 拘囚되도다.
歷史의 久遠! 우리가 그러케 자랑하여야 果可할 것인가. 必히 그러하고사 始로 愉快를 感할 것인가. 長遠은 悠久를 云함이오 陳舊를 意味함이오 陳舊는 停滯를 意味함이오 停滯는 腐敗를 意味함이니 果然하면 吾人歷史의 悠久는 少하야도 吾人生活의 그만큼 停滯腐敗함을 表明함이니 停滯腐敗가 그러틋 吾人의 자랑일가.＜2＞
吾人으로써 見하면 우리에게 相續하는 遺産은 그 大部가 因習이며 骸形이며 紀念日이며 祭日뿐이라. 同時에 吾人의 歷史가 久遠하다 하면 久遠한 그것만큼 그 遺産을 相續함이 多하다 함이며 그 遺産에 대한 執念과 未練이 그만큼 强하다 함을 告白함이니 그 구지구지한 遺産을 그리고 그 축은축은한 執着念을 比較的 多有하얏다 함이 그러틋 吾人의 자랑일가.
구태 엇던 國家 或은 엇던 民族을 指摘할 것 업시 통틀어 人生이란 이것의 歷史는 實로 悠久하얏도다. 人類의 稱을 得하기 旣히 約 20萬年이엇스며 國家라는 것을 形式한 以後를 計하야도 約 5,000年이라 하도다. 四時의 序를 見하면 同一現狀의 3個月 執着도 오히려 過하다 하야 春夏秋冬이 不絶히 交迭을 行하며 動物의 呼吸을 見하면 瞬時의 渟滯를 오히려 不忍하야 秒秒分分에 新陳을 交換하거늘 아아 20萬年의 渟滯! 5,000年의 因循! 그 얼마나 可驚할 陳積이냐. 設或 그間의 多少 波瀾과 改革的 可恐이 不無하얏다 할지라도...
嗚呼라. 吾人의 歷史는 陳腐가 넘우 甚하얏스며 吾人의 因習은 成癖이 太히 過하얏도다. 우리의 曆日은 紀念日祭日로써 塡充하얏스며 地球의 구석구석은 尸骸와 糟粕으로써 飽滿되엇도다. 이름조흔 先人의 遺法은 至酷하게도 吾人의 全容을  剝奪하엿스며 可憎한 利己主義 下에 立한 狗犬輩는 公公然 남의 담을 뚜르며 忌憚업시 남의 人權을 蹂躪하도다. 嗚呼라 今日의 世界는 氣息이 奄奄하도다. 그를 圍한 空氣는 重濁이 旣極하얏스며 그를＜3＞ 養하는 血은 循環止를 旣約하얏나니 그는 早晩 窒死할 것이다. 그의 孝子忠婦는 그 壽의 萬歲萬萬歲를 祝하나 陰鬱한 零圍氣中에 그의 最後는 來할 것이다.

一. 땅(地)이 어머니임을 記하라
特히 새로운 말이 안이라 天地父母이라 함은 自古傳稱이거늘 近來 何故인지 하느님 아버지의 말은＜10＞ 盛傳하나 땅님 어머님의 말은 아조 跡을 斷하얏도다. 하느님이라 함은 卽 神을 云함이니 神은 人生이란 自體를 離하야 想像할 수 업스며 人生은 此의 慈母인 地를 離하야 想像할 수 업슨즉 結局 우리에게는 慈母의 땅이 有할 뿐이로다. 大體 生物이란 것이 무엇이냐. 一種土의 權化品에 不過한 것이니 創世記에 人은 土로써 人을 造하얏다 함도 此를 意味함이라. 或人은 肉의 外에 靈이 更有함을 以하야 地 以外의 或父를 探코자 하나 所謂 靈이라 함은 慈母인 地의 呼吸하는 大氣의 巧妙한 凝集에 不過함인즉 如何히 思하야도 吾人에게는 純實한 땅의 어머니가 有한 外에 更히 他가 有함은 不認하얏도다.
兄弟들아 地에 忠할지라. 昔日에 在하야는 先聖을 瀆함이 或은 天父를 瀆함이 最大 罪惡이엇다. 그러나 先聖은 同一한 未成品인 우리 友人이 되고 天父는 그 存在를 認키 難한 今日에 在하야는 地를 瀆함이 一大 罪惡임을 記치 안이치 못할 것이다. 牽强附會의 말이라 하지 말지어다. 우리를 抱擁하는 大地는 宇宙 大神秘의 一大 結晶體이니 저 原野의 一草一木으로부터 山河의 一拳石一沙粒에 至하기가지 어느 것이나 다ㅡ 그 神秘의 現實化한 一形狀 又는 一服裝 안임이 업스며 人類에 在하야는 其中의 尤然한 者이라. 故로 吾人이 心을 虛하고 氣를 靜하야 大地의 胸懷에 入하면 正히 幼兒가 慈母에 歸依함과 如하야 無邪氣한 喜와 快를 感하는 同時에 直히 大宇宙의 神秘 그 琴線ㅡ 自己化生의 그 源頭에 觸하야 出生 그 刹那의 그 純粹와 如한 大純粹를 맛볼지니 複雜한 이 世間에 在하야 時日이나마 이러한＜11＞ 三昧에 入함이 업고 엇지 自家의 使命을 盡遂키 可得하리오. 그리고 그의 胸懷에 入하면 不知不識中 人類는 勿論이오 이 世間 萬生은 均히 땅어머니의 一胎中 所産인 同時에 그네는 確實히 우리 兄弟이라는 最眞摯한 感念을 得하며 「어머니시어 저는 어머니의 처음 주신 그 純實로써 여러 兄弟와 憂戚을 共히 하며 喜悲를 相分하야 그 生을 共榮케 하겟나이다.」 하는 祈禱를 스스로 안이하지 못하게 되나니 이 感念에 觸하고사 吾人은 始로 自己에 忠하는 純人이 될 것이며 戰 人類와 運命을 共히 한다는 大至誠을 有하게 될 것이며 天地와 그 化育을 分한다는 大感念을 有하게 될 것이다. 此擧에 不出하고 오즉 一時의 文書와 思考에 依하야 同胞 兄弟를 말하고 親親及物을 말함과 如함은 僞의 僞ㅡ 오즉 世間을 弄하는 徒輩가 될 뿐 안이라 이 意味에서 今後의 吾人은 무엇무엇보다도 爲先 大地의 慈懷에 歸하야 大純粹를 體得하기를 慫慂하며 이 世界의 人類는 모다 『땅어머니』를 呼할 日이 不遠에 到來할 것을 미리 말하노라.

一. 平凡至上主義를 排除하노라
吾人의 從來 生活은 말할 수 업는 平凡至上主義이엇다. 中庸이란 것이 如何한 것인지 모르거니와 言必稱 中庸의 大道를 守한다 하야 過不及이 다 못쓰는 法이라고 凡於事爲에 더 할 수 업는 새아씨의 態를 執하얏스며 가튼 죽음이라도 或 馬革에 屍를 裏하고 或 溝壑에 身이 轉함과 如함은 一生의 大不幸이오 비록 구린내나는 病室에서나마 兒女子의 手에＜12＞ 눈을 감기우는 죽음이라야 始로 그 死를 得한 것이라 하야 如何한 境遇에서라도 客死 或 他殺의 榮光임을 不認하고 所謂 考終命을 五福의 一에 數하얏스며 衣를 着하면 廣衣長袖履를 着하면 君子靴, 步를 行하면 十里路程에 날저믈며 그리고 口容은 止하고 足容은 重하라 하며 遊衣遊食은 人間의 最大 理想이라 하는 等 그 느른느른한 꼴은 實로 世間의 嘔逆을 催하얏도다. 近日에 至하야 此等의 野風은 大槪 除去하얏스나 因習의 所致로 그 一部가 尙히 殘存함과 如한 事實을 認하게 됨은 우리의 一大恥辱이라. 吾人은 切言하노니 平凡은 唯一한 滅亡의 友인 同時에 此平凡을 自家의 生活로부터 排除함은 實로 萬善의 始이라 하노라.

一. 寧히 極端主義의 徹底를 慫慂하노라
吾人의 生活은 平凡이 안이면 極端 極端이 안이면 平凡으로 此間의 間位率은 容할 수 無한 同時에 平凡과 極端은 絶對 兩立치 못할 것이라. 故로 平凡主義를 排除하는 吾人으로 極端主義를 崇奉할 것은 寧히 當然한 事이라. 世間에는 往往 極端主義의 矯激을 嫌하는 者ㅡ 有하냐 是는 平凡한 中庸主義로 그 身을 自葬乃已하는 徒輩流의 말뿐이라. 世上萬事가 極端을 離하고 成就될 것은 何이냐 向上될 것은 何이냐 彼時計의 振子를 見하야도 兩方으로 極端의 偏依가 有한지라. 於是乎 그 反動을 因하야 時針의 廻轉을 策하나니 若其振子가 不幸이 兩方에 對한 不偏不倚의 大德을 守하얏더면 그의 運命은 如何할가. 今日의 이만한 文明을 見함도 過去 長侍日間의 幾多 極端的 現狀의 相衝相擊하는 間에 鑄成된 것이며 그 偉人이란 偉人,＜13＞ 選民이란 選民을 見하면 皆極端的이란 熱烈한 作爲에서 出함이니 水에 水를 加함과 如한 平凡한 中에서 出來한 것이 無함과 如함은 구타여 例를 煩할 것이 업는 것이다ㅡ 生이 안이면 死, 有가 안이면 無니라. 吾人에게는 오즉 이러한 主義가 有할 뿐이니라.

요컨대 우리는 『웨 그런가?』의 理由를 問할 것 업시 世界的 新開闢의 첫 路程에 立하얏도다. 避코자 하야도 避할 수 업스며 또한 피할 까닭도 업도다. 우리에게는 今日 以前의 歷史가 無하며 因習이 無하며 祭日이 無하며 傳說이 無하고 오즉 新紀元의 第一日이 有할 뿐이며 新人生의 첫 自覺이 有할 뿐이며 新生活의 第一步가 有할 뿐이로다. 勿論 昨日의 昨人으로부터  有意識 無意識的으로 引受한 그 제도 又 傳說이 無함은 안이나 그것은 우리의 參考가 될지언정 正條가 되지 못하며 우리의 利用이 될지언정 標準이 되지 못할 것이니 今日의 우리는 우리 스스로가 善惡의 創造者가 되며 一切 儀型의 制定者가 되어야 할 것이니 新人의 任務는 實로 輕하지 못하도다. 吾人은 오즉 口로서 此를 唱할 뿐 안이라 手로써 此를 行할 것이며 爲先 갓가운 自家自身으로부터 始하야 蔚然히 鄕黨邦國에 及하야 新世界의 樹立에 더블어 策함이 有할 것이니 이것이 今日 사람된 吾人으로의 自覺할 第一点임을 切認하노라.
兄弟들아 당신네 各自의 들창을 快히 열치고 自由의 空氣를 導入하라. 그리하야 新紀元의 旗幟 下에 集할지어다.＜14＞
