Success
Success is the crystallization of Effort.
Talent is another name for Patience. (Maupassant)

Do you admire the success of the great figures of old? Then first look at their history of tears and blood!! (Carlyle)

Do not desire Success hastily!! The warrior setting out prepares slowly. (Greek Proverb) <87>

**Summary**

This short article defines success as the result of effort and equates talent with patience, citing Maupassant. It urges readers admiring great figures to remember their struggles ("tears and blood"), referencing Carlyle. Finally, using a Greek proverb, it advises against hastiness, emphasizing slow and steady preparation. From a historical perspective, published in 1920s colonial Korea shortly after the March 1st Movement, the article's focus on individual effort, patience, and gradual preparation (rather than immediate results) might reflect a shift towards cultural nationalism or a pragmatic call for resilience and long-term strategy under difficult circumstances. The mention of "tears and blood" could implicitly reference contemporary national suffering.
