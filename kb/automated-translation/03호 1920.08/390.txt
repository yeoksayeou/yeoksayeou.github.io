Labor
Labor is Life, Thought is Light. (U-go)

Old Age
When I was young, I always spoke good words. But back then, no one paid attention to my words. Now I am an old man. And so, I always speak uninteresting words. But many people all pay attention to my words. (Samu'el)<129>

**Summary**

This short piece presents two aphorisms attributed to "U-go" (likely Hugo) and "Samu'el" (likely Samuel). The first equates labor with life and thought with light, emphasizing the fundamental importance of both physical work and intellectual activity. The second reflects on the irony of age: youth speaks valuable words ignored, while old age speaks uninteresting words that command attention. From a historical perspective, the emphasis on "Labor is Life" might reflect the social conditions and ideologies of the time, perhaps aligning with modernization efforts or nationalist sentiments emphasizing diligence. The reflection on age and authority offers insight into generational perspectives or societal respect for elders, even when their words lack youthful vitality, which could be particularly poignant in a rapidly changing colonial context.

老年
내가 젊은 때에는, 恒常 조흔 말만 하엿다. 그러나 그때는, 아모도 내 말예 귀를 기울이지 아니 하엿다. 只今은 老人이다. 그리하야, 恒常 滋味업는 말만 한다. 그러나 여러 사람은, 내 말에 모다 귀를 기울인다. (삼우엘)＜129＞
