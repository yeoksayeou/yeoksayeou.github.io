**Body Text Translation:**
Despair

"It cannot be done" are words found only in the dictionary of the weak. (Napoleon)

People live by hope. Even today's suffering (苦痛) and destitution (貧寒) are endured by thinking of tomorrow's (明日) joy (快樂) and success (成功). However, the day hope (希望) fades from me is the day my life (生命) fades away. (Emerson)

Never despair!! If only you have a burning will (意志) in your heart (가삼), there is a way (方法) to carry that will (意志) through (貫徹). (Johnson)<100>

**Summary**

This short piece compiles three quotes emphasizing the importance of hope and willpower in overcoming adversity. Citing Napoleon, Emerson, and Johnson, it argues that despair is a sign of weakness, hope is essential for life, and a strong will can always find a way to succeed. Published in 1920 under Japanese rule, the article's message of resilience against despair and suffering likely aimed to encourage Koreans enduring colonial oppression. Historians might find it significant as an example of morale-boosting literature promoting inner strength and perseverance within the constraints of colonial censorship.
