Magazine Title: 개벽 제3호
Publication Date: 1920년 08월 25일
Article Title: 음악 (Music)
Author: Unknown
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Music's infinite power is the shortest path to escape from the phenomena of falsehood and enter the inner world of the spirit. (Ttanunch'yo-)

Music is the sweet kiss of our life. (P'et'op'en Haeng*) <100>

*Translator's Note: "P'et'op'en Haeng" (페토펜幸) likely refers to Beethoven, with "Haeng" (幸) possibly indicating happiness or fortune, or it might be part of a name/pseudonym associated with the quote. "Ttanunch'yo-" (딴눈쵸-) is unclear, possibly a name, pseudonym, or citation marker.

**Summary**

This short piece praises music's profound ability to bypass superficial reality ("phenomena of falsehood") and provide direct access to the spiritual ("inner world of the spirit"), calling it the "sweet kiss of life." The references, though slightly obscure, likely point to Western figures or ideas (Beethoven). For historians, this article, published in the influential cultural magazine *Kaebyŏk* (개벽), illustrates the early adoption and Korean interpretation of Western aesthetic and philosophical concepts in the colonial period. The lofty, slightly romantic tone regarding music's spiritual power reflects the era's intellectual currents and the magazine's role in introducing modern thought.
