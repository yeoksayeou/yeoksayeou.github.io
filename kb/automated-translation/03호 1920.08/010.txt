권두시

壯丁 四五人이 잇서
도끼와 괭이와 메와 호믜를 各其 둘러멧다.
10代나 100代나 살아오던 그 故家를 헐기 始作한다.
全身의 힘을 다 주어 「낑」하고 벽을 따린다 또 한번 기둥을
따린다.
몬지가 일며 와르르하고 집이 半쯤 문어졋다. 이때 지나가던
老人 二三人이 잇서
그를 보고 큰 걱정을 내리운다.
「그런대로 그냥 두엇스면 하로이틀 한해두해 지긋지긋 살
아갈 것을 아ㅡ 저것이 무슨 즛일가? 아조 문어트럿스니...
아ㅡ 몬지!」하고
그 뒤를 몃 달을 지냇섯다.
落成式이라는 宴會에서 그 壯丁들은 술 만이 먹고
成功의 舞를 興致 잇게 춘다.
그때 그 老人들도 參席이 되엇다.
부끄러운 술잔을 들고 머리만 끄덕인다.＜1＞

Opening Poem

There were four or five young men (壯丁).
Each slung an axe, a pickaxe, a mallet, and a hoe over their shoulder.
They began to demolish the old family home, lived in for 10 or 100 generations.
Putting all their strength into it, "Heave!" they struck the wall, and again struck a pillar.
Dust rose, and with a crash, the house half collapsed. At this time, two or three old men (老人) passing by
saw this and became greatly worried.
"If they had just left it as it was, they could have lived on drearily for a day or two, a year or two... Ah— what is this act? They've completely torn it down...
Ah— the dust!" they said.

Several months passed after that.
At a feast called the completion ceremony (落成式), those young men drank much alcohol
and danced the dance of success with gusto.
At that time, those old men were also in attendance.
Holding their shameful cups of alcohol, they just nodded their heads. <1>

**Summary**

This allegorical poem depicts young men energetically demolishing an ancestral home, representing the destruction of the old ways or structures. Passing old men lament the destruction, preferring the continuity of the past, however dreary. Later, at a celebration marking the completion of something new (implied), the young men rejoice while the old men, now present, silently and perhaps shamefully accept the new reality. For historians, the poem offers insight into the generational conflict and the powerful urge for radical change and modernization felt by some Koreans in the early colonial period. It reflects a desire to break from the past, symbolized by the dilapidated ancestral home, and embrace a new future, even amidst the concerns of the older generation.
