Magazine Title: 개벽 제3호
Publication Date: 1920년 08월 25일
Article Title: 벗의 王國 (Kingdom of Friends)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary/Miscellaneous)

Brother, do not doubt me even a little. I have no particularly gloomy heart. It is merely a few earnest desires: how can I extend the work of my heart that I wish to do unto others, and how can I feel together the sorrows and joys of others? And I have no demands towards you, Brother. Some people say, "Use brothers to assist benevolence (仁)," claiming that if one is not a so-called beneficial friend (益友), one should not associate with them. (The conditions for being a beneficial friend are taught as: one must be upright (直), one must be trustworthy (諒), one must be well-informed (多聞)). And it is also taught, "Do not make friends with those not equal to oneself." Should the requirements for a person to form a friendship with another person be so strict and harsh? As long as a person is not a carpenter's guideline (繩墨), who can be so perfectly upright? As long as a person's composition is not only of a stainless (無垢) spirit but also includes the flesh, who can be so perfectly pure and good? And as long as a person is also not an encyclopedia (百科辭典), who can be so <121> well-informed? Furthermore, if one does not make friends with those not equal to oneself, I do not know what standard of 'self' is being used; if one considers oneself a wise person (賢人), then all below the wise person would not be one's friends. If one considers oneself a sage (聖人), then all people below the sage would not be one's friends, and if one considers oneself a divine person (神人), then even sages and wise persons below the divine person would not be one's friends. If one follows this kind of talk, before choosing good friends, one would first become a solitary man (獨夫) in the world. Becoming a solitary man in the world oneself might be acceptable, but solely for the purpose of assisting one's own benevolence, for the sake of one's own self-righteousness (獨善), to disregard the masses of the world—that state of mind (心事)—strange and base—if true benevolence, righteousness, morality, and virtue were to hear it, they would surely say, "Alas! What words are these?" I find these words—words that delve only into profit and loss—truly offensive (逆) to hear. I say again, I have no demands whatsoever regarding associating with you, Brother. It is fine if you are better than me, fine if you are lesser than me (though there likely isn't anyone lesser), fine if you are male, fine if you are female, fine if you are an elder, even better if you are younger, fine if you are a fellow countryman, and also fine if you are a foreigner. It is enough if you just love me without falsehood (거즛업시); it is enough if you just believe in me with substance (實地 잇시). Just like the affection a loving mother (愛母) has for her child, like the bond a wise elder brother (賢兄) has for his younger sibling (少弟). Thus, when we are apart, you miss me as if unbearable, and when we meet, you welcome me infinitely—that is enough. If it is so, whether I am joyful or sorrowful, I will seek that Brother. Seeking brings comfort (慰安). One gains new strength. One will come to feel new feelings not usually felt in ordinary times, and if so, the other's common words (凡語), like sand and gravel (砂礫), will sound to me like gold and jade (金玉). The other's ordinary every move and stillness will become an indispensable gift (賜物) to me. A hungry and parched heart can be healed (療) here; knotted and pent-up emotions (鬱憤 情緖) can be resolved here; a heavy and muddled mind (濁亂 頭腦) can be ordered here; and the brightly bursting light (光明) can be grasped (把得) here. This is not only what I myself perceive; the other's perception (感受) will also be the same (同然). If there are a hundred friends, a hundred; if a thousand, a thousand will perceive the same. If it has come to this, it is enough. Ah, mutually becoming friends and then—laughing, enjoying, kissing, embracing, gaining new strength, and feeling new feelings! Isn't this the pinnacle of friendship? May this kind of friendship weave through (經緯) this world, may the Kingdom of Friends quickly spread (버퍼지고), and I wish for it to be so, starting between me and you Brothers. <122>

**Summary**

The author passionately argues against the traditional, restrictive Confucian criteria for choosing friends, such as demanding friends be upright, trustworthy, knowledgeable, or superior to oneself. Instead, the author advocates for unconditional friendship based purely on mutual love, trust, and emotional connection, regardless of status, gender, age, or nationality. True friendship, in this view, offers profound emotional support, comfort, strength, new perspectives, and healing. The author yearns for this ideal, a "Kingdom of Friends," to spread throughout the world, starting with the reader. From a historical perspective, this article from 1920s colonial Korea is interesting as it reflects a potential shift away from rigid social hierarchies towards more modern, egalitarian, and emotionally centered values. It expresses a deep longing for human connection and solidarity, possibly as a response to the social constraints and hardships of the colonial period, emphasizing universal human feelings over prescribed social roles or utilitarian benefits in relationships.

벗의 王國
兄弟여 나를 조금이나 懷疑치 말지어다. 나는 別로 측측한 마음이 업소라. 다못 엇더케 하면 나의 하고 십흔 마음의 일을 남에게 베풀어 보고 남의 슯흠과 깃븜을 내가 더불어 가티 늣겨 볼가 몃가지 切願뿐이다. 그리고 나는 兄弟에게 對하야 要求하는 것이 업소라 엇던 사람들은 무엇 兄弟로써 仁을 輔한다 하야 所謂 益友가 안이면 더불어 벗하지 안는다. (그 益友되는 條件에 가르되 直한 者라야 諒한 者라야 多聞한 者라야)고 또 가르되 自己만 갓지 못한 者와 더불어 벗하지 말라하얏다고 사람이 사람으로 더불어 友誼를 結함에 要求個條가 그러케도 嚴且苛할가 사람이 繩墨이 안인 以上에 誰가 그러케 다 直한 者이며 사람의 組織이 無垢한 靈뿐 안이라 肉이 거긔에 加한 以上 誰가 그러케 다 純良한 者이며 사람이 亦是 百科辭典이 안인 以上 誰가 그러케-多聞한 者일가 ＜121＞ 또한 自己만 갓지 못한 者와 더불어 벗하지 안는다 하면 自己란 것이 如何한 程度에 잇는 自己를 標準함인지 모르거니와 自己가 賢人이라 하면  賢人 以下는 皆自己의 友가 안일지오. 자기가 聖人이라 하면 聖人 以下의 人은 皆自己의 友가 알일지며 自己가 神人이라하면 神人以下의 聖人賢人도 皆 自己의 友가 안일지라. 이 따위 말에 從하면 吾人은 善友를 擇하기에 先하야 먼저 自己가 天下의 獨夫가 될 것이다. 自己 一身이 天下의 獨夫가 됨은 오히려 可하거니와 오즉 自己의 仁을 輔키 爲하야 自己의 獨善을 爲하야 世間人衆을 無視하는 그 心事-怪하고 陋한 心事-實로 眞正한 仁義道德이 들으면 『惡라 是何言也오』할 것이다. 나는 이런 말-利害속으로만 쏙 빠지는 이런 말-정말 듯기에 逆하도다. 다시 말하노라 나는 兄弟와 더불어 交함에 對하야 아모 要求함이 업소라. 나보다 나아 조코 나보다 못하야(못할 사람도 업겟지만은) 조흐며 男子이야 조코 女子이야 조흐며 長者라도 조코 幼者이면 더 조흐며 內國사람이야 조코 外國사람이라도 또한 조타. 그저 나를 거즛업시 사랑만하야 주면 그만이며 實地 잇시 미더만 주면 그만이다. 마치 愛母가 그 子에게 對한 그 情가티 賢兄이 少弟에게 對한 그 誼가티. 그래서 떠나 잇슬 때면 못견댈 것 가티 그려주고 만날 때는 無限 반겨주면 그만이다. 그리하노라면 내가 깃븐 때에는 슯흘 때에나 그 兄弟를 찻게 될지오. 차즈면 慰安이 될지오. 새 힘을 엇게 될지오. 平時에 能히 느끼지 못하던 새 느낌을 느끼게 될 것이며 그리되면 저편의 砂礫가튼 凡語도 나에게는 金玉 그 音이 될 것이오. 져편의 尋常한 一動一靜도 나에게는 不可無의 賜物이 될 것이다. 주리고 클클한 心情을 여긔에서 可히 療하면 맷치고 鬱憤한 情緖를 여긔에서 可히 解하며 묵업고 濁亂한 頭腦를 어긔에서 可히 整하며 휜-하게 터져오는 光明을 여긔에서 可이 把得할 것이다. 이것은 오즉 나의 感受하는 것이 그러할 뿐안이라 저편의 感受가 또한 同然할 것이다. 벗이 百이면 百, 千이면 千이다 同然할 것이다. 이만하얏스면 그만이다. 아아 서로 벗이 되어 그리고-웃고 즐기고 입마치고 껴안으며 새 힘을 엇고 새 느낌을 느끼는 것! 이것이 交友의 極致가 안일가. 이런 交誼가 이 世間을 經緯하야 하로 바삐 벗의 王國을 버퍼지고 爲先 나와 兄弟들 사이에 그리하고 십허서.＜122＞
