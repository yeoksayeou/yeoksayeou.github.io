Magazine Title: 개벽 제3호
Publication Date: 1920년 08월 25일
Article Title: 삶의 물 (Water of Life)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

ㄱ
My spirit, my spirit
Whether in dark night or cloudy day, clear and new
Like a spring ceaselessly, softly, softly gushing forth

ㄴ
My heart, my heart
Whether autumn goes or spring comes, round and full
Like a pond limitlessly, deeply, deeply brimming

ㄷ
My vigor, my vigor
Over repeated rocks or broken gullies, firm and forceful
Like a waterfall willfully, crash! crash! pouring down

ㄹ
My devotion, my devotion
Though difficult and moreover painful, enduring and overcoming
Like a stream following its own path, gently, gently flowing

ㅁ
My bosom, my bosom
Whether coming from the sky or from the earth, large or small
Like the sea without complaint, full, full receiving <22>

**Summary**

This poem, "Water of Life," uses metaphors of different forms of water—spring, pond, waterfall, stream, and sea—to describe the desired qualities of the human spirit, heart, vigor, devotion, and capacity. Each stanza portrays resilience, depth, strength, perseverance, and acceptance, respectively, suggesting an ideal state of being that is adaptable, enduring, and embracing. From a historical perspective, published in *Gaebyeok* in 1920, shortly after the March 1st Movement and during the early, harsh phase of Japanese colonial rule, the poem can be interpreted as an expression of Korean national spirit and resilience. Historians might find the use of nature metaphors significant, reflecting a common literary trope used to subtly convey aspirations for inner strength, constancy, and the ability to endure hardship during a period of suppressed national identity.

삶의 물
ㄱ
나의 정신은 나의 정신은
어두운 밤에나 흐린 낫에나 맑고도 새롭게
거침업시 소ㅡㄹ 소ㅡㄹ 솟아나는 샘과 가티
ㄴ
나의 마음은 나의 마음은
가을이 가던지 봄이 오던지 둥글고 가득케
한정업시 충ㅡㅇ 충ㅡㅇ 깁허잇는 못과 가티
ㄷ
나의 긔운은 나의 긔운은
거듭한 바위나 끈킨 구렁에 굿세고 줄차게
제멋대로 콰ㅡㅇ 콰ㅡㅇ 쏘다지는 폭포가티
ㄹ
나의 정성은 나의 정성은
어렵고 더구나 괴롭지마는 참고도 이기어
제갈대로 주ㅡㄹ 주ㅡㄹ 흘러가는 내와 가티
ㅁ
나의 보ㅅ장은 나의 보ㅅ장은
하늘로 오던지 따로 오던지 크거나 작거나
투정업시 가득가득 받아두는 바다가티＜22＞
