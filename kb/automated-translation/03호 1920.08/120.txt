文豪의 玉條
						盧子泳

愛
Love

愛는 黃金의 크림이다.(꿰터)
Love is the cream of gold. (Goethe)

愛가 잇는 곳에는, 平和의 神 ´ 來臨한다.(랏셀)
Where there is love, the God of Peace descends. (Russell)

愛를 所有함은, 天下를 所有함보다 나흐니라.(하이네)
Possessing love is better than possessing the world. (Heine)

眞正한 愛의 길은, 平坦하지 아니하다.(쉑쓰피어ㅡ)
The path of true love is not smooth. (Shakespeare)

愛의 동산에는, 福이라는 꼿이 피고, 猜忌라는 동산에는, 가시나무가 茂盛한다.(떠ㅡ스터예ㅡ푸스키ㅡ)＜56＞
In the garden of love, the flower called Blessing blooms, and in the garden of jealousy, thorny bushes flourish. (Dostoevsky) <56>

**Summary**

This short piece compiles five aphorisms about love, attributed to prominent Western literary figures: Goethe, Russell, Heine, Shakespeare, and Dostoevsky. Each quote offers a perspective on the nature, value, and challenges of love, contrasting it with peace, worldly possessions, and jealousy. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it appeared in *Kaebyŏk*, a significant Korean intellectual magazine during the colonial period. It demonstrates the early introduction and translation of Western literary and philosophical thought into Korea, reflecting an engagement with global culture. The phonetic transcriptions of the Western authors' names also offer insight into early 20th-century Korean transliteration practices.
