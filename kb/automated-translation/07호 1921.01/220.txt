Harvest of the Gyeongsillyeon Year
						One Reporter
July
(Great Flood in 50 Years) The rain that began on the 2nd of this month continued for a week, and by the 8th, it finally caused the greatest flood in 50 years. The Han River rose by 36 ch'ŏk 9 ch'on (approx. 11.2 meters), causing 5,000 houses in Yongsan to be submerged. Furthermore, much flood damage occurred along the Han River banks, with losses reaching approximately 3 million won. Various newspaper companies in the city collected relief funds for the flood victims.
(48 People's Trial Begins) Responding to national sentiment and global trends of reorganization, the 33 representatives of the Korean nation who declared Korea's independence internally and externally on March 1st of last year and voluntarily entered imprisonment, along with 14 others directly related to this, including Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, spent about a year and a half going in and out of the District Court and High Court amid debates over whether it was a crime of internal disturbance or not. Eventually recognized as simple security offenders, the public trial for these 48 individuals finally began on the 12th at 9 AM in the special courtroom on the lower floor of the Kyŏngsŏng Railway Department building. At the very beginning of the trial, lawyer Ōkubo submitted a motion for non-acceptance, stating that "this case does not fall under any provision of current laws," attracting considerable public attention. However, the case hearing continued. On the second day, Ch'oe Rin and 8 others were interrogated; on the third day, Yi In-hwan and 5 others; on the fourth day, Kil Sŏn-chu and 40 others. On the fifth day, lawyer Hŏ Hŏn submitted a motion for non-acceptance of the public prosecution, forcing Presiding Judge Tachikawa to declare the court closed. On the sixth day, the suspension of the main trial was formally declared. According to the Code of Criminal Procedure, when a court receives a case transferred from another court, the ruling indicating the transfer must specify the receiving court. However, although this case was transferred from the High Court to the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, the ruling lacked the phrase specifying the Kyŏngsŏng District Court (though it was present in the statement of reasons <90>). Legally, the Kyŏngsŏng District Court lacked the qualification to receive the case. Lawyer Hŏ Hŏn, noting this point, finally submitted a protest for non-acceptance. The entire court, panic-stricken, declared the trial suspended. This incident became a major laughingstock, causing the judiciary's authority to plummet. Not long after, a major purge took place in the Chosŏn judiciary. The names of the 48 individuals are:
Son Pyŏng-hŭi, Ch'oe Rin, Kwŏn Tong-jin, O Se-ch'ang, Im Ye-hwan, Kwŏn Pyŏng-dŏk, Yi Chong-il, Na In-hyŏp, Hong Ki-jo, Kim Wan-gyu, Na Yong-hwan, Yi Chong-hun, Hong Pyŏng-gi, Pak Chun-sŭng, Yi Sŭng-hun, Pak Hŭi-do, Ch'oe Sŏng-mo, Sin Hong-sik, Yang Chŏn-baek, Yi Myŏng-yong, Kil Sŏn-chu, Yi Kap-sŏng, Kim Ch'ang-jun, Yi P'il-chu, O Hwa-yŏng, Pak Tong-wan, Chŏng Ch'un-su, Sin Sŏk-ku, Han Yong-un, Paek Sang-gyu, An Se-hwan, Im Kyu, Kim Chi-hwan, Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, Ham T'ae-yŏng, Song Chin-u, Chŏng No-sik, Hyŏn Sang-yun, Yi Kyŏng-sŏp, Han Pyŏng-ik, Kim Hong-gyu, Kim To-t'ae, Pak In-ho, No Hŏn-yong, Kim Se-hwan, Kang Ki-dŏk, Kim Wŏn-byŏk, Yu Yŏ-dae
(Traveling Lecture Group Disbanded) The Hak'uhoe, organized by over a thousand of our Tōkyō exchange students, planned to use the summer vacation to form a traveling lecture group and conduct a major cultural propaganda campaign within Chosŏn. Having secured the support of the Tonga Ilbo early on, they announced their intention to the public, generating great anticipation. On their way back home, they first held lectures in places like Pusan, Chinju, and Taegu, receiving an enthusiastic welcome from compatriots. The group entered Kyŏngsŏng on the 17th and held a lecture meeting at Tansŏngsa Theater the next afternoon, the 18th. Kim Chun-yŏn took the stage and delivered a passionate speech titled "The Reorganization of the World and Our Awakening" for about two hours. Following him, Kim To-yŏn attempted to say a few words on the topic "Regarding the Future of Chosŏn Industry" when the Kyŏnggi Province 3rd Department suddenly ordered the meeting dispersed and also commanded the lecture group itself to disband. However, there was no specific reason given; their overall message was deemed contrary to the spirit of Chosŏn governance.
(Sŏ Sang-han Trial) On the 20th, the Tōkyō District Court sentenced Sŏ Sang-han to four years of imprisonment. Prior to this, when the news of the wedding ceremony of Crown Prince Yi Wang and Princess Masako Nashimoto spread, Sŏ Sang-han, a young man from Taegu studying hard in Tōkyō, felt he could not stand idly by. He prepared bombs in advance, intending to throw them at the carriage carrying both royals on the wedding day, as well as at Governor-General Saitō and the Metropolitan Police Agency, aiming to eliminate the pretext of Japan-Korea harmony (日鮮融和). However, his plan was discovered beforehand, and after ongoing investigations, the trial was held this time.
(Plan to Attack Government-General) On the 29th, the investigative details concerning Kwak Chae-gi, Yun So-ryong, Yi Sŏng-u, and 4 others, who planned to bomb and destroy the Chōsen Sōtokufu, Tōyō Takushoku Kaisha, and Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo in one fell swoop, were announced <91>, further agitating the public mind.
(New Local System Promulgated) On the 29th, the new local system was promulgated by Government-General decree. Its outline was to establish Prefectural/Township Consultative Councils (府面協議會) in prefectures and townships, but only in prefectures and designated townships would council members be elected from among the people. Furthermore, School Councils were to be established in counties and islands, and Provincial Councils in each province, with several councilors appointed by the government to provide consultation on school expenses and local finances. However, the general immediate feeling was that these councils or consultative bodies under the new system were merely advisory bodies, not decision-making ones, and thus not fundamentally different from the previous Prefectural Council Member Councils or Provincial/County Councilor Councils.
(China's Factional Strife) Also, from the beginning of this month, a major conflict erupted in China between the Anhui clique (including the Anfu clique) and the Zhili clique, that is, between the Duan Qirui faction and the Wu Peifu faction. As the two sides met with arms, public opinion largely sided with Wu, and the Duan faction suffered repeated defeats. Nine members of the Duan faction, including Xu Shuzheng, hid in the Japanese legation, barely concluding the situation. Furthermore, in our Kyŏngsŏng, the Korean Youth Association United Preparatory Committee was initiated by O Sang-gŭn, Chang Tŏk-su, and over ten others, seeking to unite the youth groups emerging throughout Chosŏn. The Korean Student Congress conducted lecture tours in various regions. On the 30th, during a lecture hosted by the Korean People's Association, the speeches of speakers Pak Mu-byŏng, Im Chong-yŏn, and others became problematic, leading to the dissolution of the said association.

August
(Another Great Flood) On the 2nd, there was heavy rainfall again, causing the Han River to rise by 3-4 ch'ŏk (approx. 0.9-1.2 meters). Over 2,000 houses in Kyŏngsŏng city were flooded, and numerous houses collapsed and bridges were washed away.
(Verdict of Non-Acceptance of Public Prosecution) Regarding the controversial issue of the non-acceptance of public prosecution for the 48 individuals, the Kyŏngsŏng District Court delivered a verdict of non-acceptance on the 9th. Prosecutor Kyŏng appealed this decision to the Kyŏngsŏng Appellate Court. Along with this, the Suwŏn case involving Kim Hyŏn-muk and 26 others, the Sinŭiju case involving Pak Kyŏng-dŭk and several others, the Ansŏng case involving Ch'oe Ŭn-sik and 126 others, and the Ch'angwŏn case involving Pyŏn Sang-t'ae and 9 others all subsequently received verdicts of non-acceptance of public prosecution.
(California Anti-Japanese Bill Passed) Anti-Japanese sentiment in California has intensified further since last spring, and this time a concrete anti-Japanese bill was passed. Its main provisions included denying land ownership, lease rights, and all other land rights to Japanese ineligible for naturalization, and prohibiting Japanese from owning land under the names of several individuals.
(Great Disturbance in P'yŏngan North Province Announced) On the 19th, the Police Affairs Bureau announced the situation of the great disturbance that had arisen in the P'yŏngan North Province region since spring. According to this announcement, between March and July, police officers and other pro-Japanese collaborators <92> were killed over 20 times, many township officials resigned, and the general public unrest was indescribable.
On the 15th, independence party member Ch'oe Pyŏng-hyŏk was executed by firing squad.
(Egyptian Independence Recognized) On the 23rd, Great Britain conditionally recognized Egypt's independence.
(US Congressional Delegation Enters Kyŏngsŏng) On the 24th at 8 PM, a US congressional delegation of 47 members led by Mr. Smoot arrived in Kyŏngsŏng by train from Fengtian (Mukden). Prior to this, when news of the US delegation's visit to Chosŏn spread, the public planned a meaningful welcome, and preparations were swiftly made at various points along their route. When the group first entered China, the Provisional Government in Shanghai dispatched welcome envoys and took appropriate measures. Within Chosŏn, especially in Kyŏngsŏng, in coordination with Shanghai and the North Manchu military government, various actions were planned for the day of arrival. On the 22nd, several members of the Kwangboktan Military Camp Special Suicide Squad, including Kim Yŏng-ch'ŏl (born in Nyŏngbyŏn, P'yŏngan North Province), were arrested, and bombs were confiscated. Subsequently, Yang Ki-t'ak and several others were detained. Astonishing reports such as the arrest of the OO Group or the entry of the OO Squad continuously appeared in newspapers. From the 23rd onwards, various propaganda leaflets were distributed in the city. On the 24th, the weather also turned foul, with gloomy rain falling from the afternoon. Shops throughout the city closed in unison, and the streets were unusually crowded with pedestrians. The scene truly felt like "the wind fills the tower before the mountain rain comes." Around 6-7 PM, the crowd began heading towards Namdaemun, forming a sea of people from Namdaemun Street to Hwanggeumjŏng 4-chōme. As the delegation passed Taehanmun Gate by automobile, cries of "Manse!" suddenly erupted from the plaza in front of Taehanmun, and the crowd responded in kind. Pre-positioned police and military police frantically rode horses through the crowd to barely control the situation. The crowd then surged again towards Hwanggeumjŏng 4-chōme, shouting "Manse!" A large part of this group ran towards Chongno, continuously shouting "Manse!" until they reached the front of Umigwan Theater, where they were surrounded and dispersed by police. At this time, pitiful cries of "Someone is dying! Save us!" arose from everywhere, people were beaten and trampled, and the scene was tragic. Furthermore, despite strict security, demonstrations occurred at several stations the delegation passed through that day. Amidst this, the 24th passed, and the 25th arrived. The market closure continued, and the city's atmosphere was even more tense than the previous day, filled only with whispers everywhere. Prior to the US delegation's arrival, the Korean side in Kyŏngsŏng, initiated by over 200 prominent figures from various classes and organizations, had planned to host a reception for the delegation. On the morning of the 25th, this intention was communicated to the delegation, who readily agreed. The event was scheduled for 3:30 PM that day at the Chongno Young Men's Hall, and all preparations were made. However, the authorities, learning of this, became concerned about security in the city and immediately conducted <93> unofficial negotiations with the delegation. When the delegation conveyed that they could not attend the reception, the public was deeply disappointed, unsure what to do. Around 4 PM, Mr. Hulsmann, one of the delegates, suddenly appeared at the Young Men's Hall. With Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho interpreting, he delivered a courteous speech of apology. The crowd of about 400, who had gathered unexpectedly, shouted "Manse!" repeatedly. Learning of this, the Chongno Police Chief personally entered the hall, requested Mr. Hulsmann's departure, and prohibited everyone inside from leaving. Mr. Hulsmann stated that he could not leave alone if the others were not allowed to leave, so eventually everyone was allowed out. At this point, people trying to enter from outside the hall and those trying to leave from inside mingled together.
(The Delegation's Parting Words) The delegation departed Kyŏngsŏng suddenly on the night of the 25th, the second day of their visit, heading for Japan. Under the premise that they did not wish to express political opinions in this land, they left a message for their Korean brothers through the Korean-language newspapers, saying:
"A nation is a gathering of a society, and a society is a gathering of individuals. All success and happiness depend on the struggle and effort of each individual, and the foundation for such struggle inevitably lies in education. More than anything else, I leave only the words 'just study well and strive hard' for the people of Chosŏn."
(Cholera Invades the City) From around the end of this month, suspected cholera cases began appearing in Kyŏngsŏng city, becoming quite prevalent by the 9th of the next month. Also during this month, the trial for the famous Yŏnt'ongje (liaison system) case, which originated in Nanam, Hamgyŏng Province, opened at the Ch'ŏngjin Branch Office.

September
(Independence Group Invades P'yŏngan South Province) Since last spring, incursions by independence groups had been frequent in the P'yŏngan North and Hamgyŏng North Province areas, allowing little peace. Around late July, they moved south again, crossing the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River. Upon entering, they shot dead one Japanese policeman at the Anju Police Station. On August 3rd, they threw a bomb at the P'yŏngan South Provincial Office's 3rd Department. Subsequently, incidents of ambushing policemen continued, centered around P'yŏngyang, creating an indescribable state of chaos for a time. By early September, the fire gradually subsided, and the general outline of these events was published in various newspapers. Furthermore, on the 1st of this month, someone threw a bomb at the Sŏnch'ŏn Police Station.
(Provincial Governors' Conference Held) From the 1st to the 6th, a conference of provincial governors was held within the Chōsen Sōtokufu.
(Chosŏn Ilbo Publication Suspended) The Chosŏn Ilbo, which had published 116 issues since its inception and was growing daily, was subjected to an indefinite suspension of publication effective the 4th, on suspicion of radical rhetoric. <94>
(Forty-Eight People Appeal Trial) As previously mentioned, Prosecutor Kyŏng had appealed the Kyŏngsŏng District Court's verdict of non-acceptance of public prosecution. The appeal trial opened on the 20th at 10 AM at the Kyŏngsŏng Appellate Court's Chŏngdong Branch Office. The issue of whether to accept the public prosecution became contentious again, just as in the District Court. However, Presiding Judge Tsukahara turned a deaf ear and, with lightning speed, proceeded with the examination of facts. By the afternoon of the 24th, the factual examination for 47 individuals, excluding Son Pyŏng-hŭi, was completed. Although it was rumored that Son Pyŏng-hŭi's case would be tried separately, he also appeared in court on the 25th, and the factual examination continued. It was decided to set another date for the oral arguments by the various lawyers for the 48 individuals, and the court adjourned.
(Major Manse Movement in Wŏnsan) On the 23rd at 9 PM, a Manse movement suddenly erupted in Wŏnsan. The situation quickly became perilous, leading to a major clash with the police force. Over two hours of major commotion ensued, during which the police substation, the Chosŏn Industrial Promotion Bank, the Tōyō Takushoku Kaisha's two branches, and seven Japanese houses were attacked. The cause was that around 10 PM that night, about 20 students gathered at the Yongdong Public Ordinary School within Wŏnsan Prefecture and played a game simulating war between a foreign country and Korea. Declaring that Great Han (Korea) had won, they shouted "Manse!" The surrounding crowd joined in, immediately starting a street procession. The momentum grew rapidly, finally resulting in a major fight with police in front of the Changch'ŏndong police box before dispersing late at night. At this time, several Korean civilians died, and over ten suffered serious or minor injuries. From the next morning, a major crackdown occurred throughout the city, leading to the interrogation of over 600 people.
However, the period from July to September was indeed permeated with a sense of unease throughout Chosŏn. Concurrently, the third page of various newspapers reporting world news was continuously filled with tragic incidents like fights, fierce battles, shootings, arrests, OOOO, etc. Although somewhat regrettable for the purpose of this article, the reporter cannot bear to record such things, so this much is omitted.
(Tonga Ilbo Publication Suspended) The Tonga Ilbo, which had launched its first issue on April 1st and was developing day by day amidst public welcome, was ordered by the authorities on the 25th to indefinitely suspend publication. The reason cited was its alleged efforts to promote independence ideology by frequently using sarcasm and coded language.

October
On the 1st, a provisional census was conducted throughout Chosŏn. Furthermore, from this day, judicial officials of the Chōsen Sōtokufu began wearing specified legal attire.
(Hunch'un Incident Erupts) On the 2nd at 4 AM, over 300 mounted bandits attacked Hunch'un, immediately setting fire to the Japanese Consulate and Japanese homes. <95> They advanced further, killing 10 Japanese and injuring several dozen seriously or lightly. Subsequent incursions occurred multiple times. The problem escalated significantly because these were not purely mounted bandits, but included many anti-Japanese Koreans. Japan dispatched troops to Jiandao (Kando) and announced the reason for the troop deployment on the 8th.
(All-Chosŏn Industrial Conference) The All-Chosŏn Japanese Industrial Conference was held from the 10th for three days at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall. Over 120 representatives from various regions gathered, opening the conference in the presence of high-ranking officials from the Government-General. Proceedings were conducted in secret daily, attracting public attention. While the content is difficult to ascertain, it seemed to involve discussions on the drastic changes in Korean thought and attitudes, and the countermeasures for Japanese entrepreneurs and Japanese people in response. The representatives finally visited the authorities separately and made statements. Korean entrepreneurs, feeling aggrieved at being excluded from the conference, talked about holding an All-Chosŏn Industrial Conference composed purely of Korean representatives, but this did not materialize.
(Kwangboktan Members Arrested) On the 10th, Ch'oe Chi-gwan from Choak-tong, Kwansa-myŏn, Kuju County, P'yŏngan North Province, Kim Sun-ho and Son Pong-jun from Ogung-dong, same township, and three others, totaling 6 members of a Kwangboktan unit operating under the command of Korean Independence Army General Commander Cho Maeng-sŏn, were arrested during a search by the Sinŭiju Police Station, and numerous weapons were confiscated.
(British Coal Miners Riot) The great strike of all British coal miners worsened. Tens of thousands of miners, carrying red flags and singing revolutionary songs, rioted on the streets where important government offices were located, clashing with police forces and creating a major scene of carnage.
(Son Ŭi-am Released on Bail) Ch'ŏndogyo leader Son Ŭi-am (Son Pyŏng-hŭi), due to his chronic illness, was released on bail on the 22nd to receive treatment at his home outside Kyŏngsŏng's East Gate.
(Verdict Delivered for the Forty-Eight) The verdict for the 48 individuals whose factual examination had already concluded was delivered on the 30th at the Kyŏngsŏng Appellate Court's Chŏngdong Branch Office. Those sentenced to 3 years of imprisonment were:
Son Pyŏng-hŭi, Ch'oe Rin, Kwŏn Tong-jin, O Se-ch'ang, Yi Chong-il, Yi In-hwan, Ham T'ae-yŏng, Han Yong-un (8 people).
Those sentenced to 2 years and 6 months were:
Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, Yi Kap-sŏng, Kim Ch'ang-jun, O Hwa-yŏng (4 people).
Those sentenced to 2 years were:
Im Ye-hwan, Na In-hyŏp, Hong Ki-jo, Kim Wan-gyu, Na Yong-hwan, Yi Chong-hun, Hong Pyŏng-gi, Pak Chun-sŭng, Kwŏn Pyŏng-dŏk, Yang Chŏn-baek, Yi Myŏng-yong, Pak Hŭi-do, Ch'oe Sŏng-mo, Sin Hong-sik, Yi P'il-chu, Pak Tong-wan, Sin Sŏk-ku, Yu Yŏ-dae, Kang Ki-dŏk, Kim Wŏn-byŏk (20 people).
Those sentenced to 1 year and 6 months were:
Yi Kyŏng-sŏp, Chŏng Ch'un-su, Paek Sang-gyu (3 people).
For the above 37 defendants, 360 days of their respective pre-trial detention were credited towards their sentences.
Han Pyŏng-ik, Kim Hong-gyu were each sentenced to 1 year of imprisonment.
Pak In-ho, No Hŏn-yong, Song Chin-u, Hyŏn Sang-yun, Chŏng No-sik, Kim To-t'ae, Kil Sŏn-chu, Im Kyu, An Se-hwan, Kim Chi-hwan, Kim Se-hwan were each acquitted and released.
Among the confiscated items, 7 copies of the declaration, evidence No. 282, were confiscated. <96>

November
(New Law Regulating Executed Persons) On the 4th, the Chōsen Sōtokufu promulgated a new law concerning the regulation of executed persons, their graves, memorial rites, and portraits. Its content prohibited public funerals or memorial rites for those executed or those who died while serving life imprisonment or life confinement sentences. It also forbade the public display or praise of photographs, writings, or portraits of executed persons, and the holding of memorial services. This was primarily a preparation for the execution of Kang U-gyu.
(California Anti-Japanese Referendum Passes) The general referendum on anti-Japanese measures in California passed by a large majority, with the vote count being five to one.
(Chinese Students Oppose Troop Deployment) Since Japan dispatched troops to the North Manchuria region, Chinese territory, last month, the Beijing government has repeatedly protested, calling it a disregard for China's sovereignty. However, Japan insisted it was unavoidable for self-defense and has delayed until today. Recently, the All-China Student Federation has vehemently argued against the troop deployment, sending long telegrams to President Xu and Inspector General Zhang.
(New US President Elected) The US presidential election battle, which had been extremely fierce, ultimately resulted in the victory of the Republican candidate, Mr. Harding ("Hiding"). He is expected to take office, replacing the current President Wilson, around March of next spring.
(Kwangboktan Members Sentenced to Death) Pak Sang-jin from Kyŏngju and Kim Han-jong from Yesan, who had continuously harbored discontent since the Japan-Korea annexation, organized the Kwangboktan, and engaged in fundraising for military purposes, were confirmed for the death penalty by the High Court on the 4th for extortion and murder.
(Haeju Inspection Team Departs) About a week ago, an inspection team of over 20 members, formed by brethren residing in Sinhanch'on, Haeju (Vladivostok), arrived in Kyŏngsŏng and toured various places. They departed Kyŏngsŏng on the 8th to return to Sinhanch'on. Team leader Cho Yŏng-jin and one other person stayed in Kyŏngsŏng for several days due to some incident.
(Chosŏn Education Edict Revised) On the 11th, a part of the Chosŏn Education Edict was revised by Imperial Ordinance. The key points were: (1) Previously, the standard duration of study for ordinary schools was 4 years; this was changed to 6 years, with the option of 5 or 4 years depending on local circumstances. (2) Geography and history were added to ordinary schools with a 6-year curriculum. (3) A supplementary course (補習科) of up to 2 years could be newly established for graduates of higher ordinary schools. Graduates of this supplementary course would reportedly gain the qualification to formally take entrance exams for Japanese high schools or specialized schools in the future.
(Japanese Diet Convocation Date) An imperial rescript was issued via the official gazette on the 12th, convoking the 44th <98> session of the Japanese Diet for December 25th.
(First General Assembly of the League of Nations) The first General Assembly of the League of Nations, convened by US President Wilson according to Article 3 of the League Covenant, opened on the 15th in Geneva, the capital of Switzerland. Participating nations included Britain, France, Japan, Italy, etc., totaling 53 countries. At the opening, former Belgian Prime Minister Carton de Wiart ("Tte·rakŭroa") read President Wilson's convocation order for the first General Assembly. Swiss President Motta delivered a response speech combined with a welcome address to the representatives of the nations. Next, the election of the President took place, with former Belgian Foreign Minister Hymans ("Iman") being elected. The important issues for this meeting included the admission of certain countries to the League, amendments to the League Covenant, the mandate system issue, and the arms limitation issue, comparable to the Hague Peace Conferences of 1889 and 1907.
(Tait'ongdan Incident Trial) The trial for the 37 members of Chŏn Hyŏp's group, who had planned various measures to bring Prince Yi Kang-gong to Shanghai to boost the momentum for Korean independence and achieved some progress before failing midway and being arrested, opened on the 19th at 10 AM at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court. As generally recognized, this incident was the second attempt to declare Korean independence, following the declaration by Son Pyŏng-hŭi and 32 others on March 1, 1919 (Kiminyŏn). They sought comrades by encompassing all classes, printed declarations, and as a first step, aimed to enthrone Prince Yi Kang-gong, who had connections with the Korean masses, in Shanghai. The case was extremely extensive. The factual examination finally concluded on the 24th, followed by arguments from 2-3 lawyers. The verdict was announced to be delivered on December 7th.
(Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural Council Election) The first Kyŏngsŏng Prefectural Council election under the new local system was held on the 20th. The number of council members was 18 for the Japanese side and 12 for the Korean side. The elected members from the Korean side were Messrs. Ch'oe Han-u, Kim Sŏk-t'ae, Wŏn Tŏk-sang, Yi Kang-hyŏk, Han Ik-kyo, Kim Kyu-wŏn, Pang Kyu-hwan, Yi Se-hyŏn, Yu Pyŏng-p'il, Ye Chong-sŏk, Chŏn Sŏng-uk, and Sin Chang-hŭi.
(Kyŏngsŏng Library Established) As Kyŏngsŏng still lacked a library, thanks to the passionate efforts of Mr. Yun Ik-sŏn and several others since last September, a library was finally established. The opening ceremony was held inside Ch'wiunjeong Pavilion on the 27th.
(Mayor of Cork Finally Starves to Death) The Irish question grows increasingly perilous day by day, reaching a state of chaos where even the British Empire likely doesn't know what measures to take. Terence MacSwiney ("Makswiini"), the Mayor of Cork, one of the main figures in this turmoil, personally stood at the forefront, attempting armed resistance against British authority, leading to his arrest last August. <99> On August 26th, he received a two-year prison sentence from a court-martial. Declaring "Righteously, I will not eat British food," he began a hunger strike. Despite his fate drawing nearer daily, the British government merely watched passively. Thus, on the 25th of this month, the 74th day of his fast, he forever lost consciousness. This heroic death suddenly stirred the world's conscience. When the news spread, the entire land of Ireland was abruptly shrouded in mourning, as if a curtain of death had fallen. The general populace rushed to churches, praying for the mayor's soul amidst weeping. Newspapers worldwide unanimously took up their pens to mourn his death. Even the Paris newspaper "L'Écho de Paris," famous for not meddling in foreign internal affairs, reported his death reverberated through the world as the "tragic appeal of an oppressed people." Japanese newspapers like the Osaka Mainichi Shimbun also mourned his heroic death in editorials.
(Kang U-gyu Executed) Kang U-gyu, who had spent 180 grueling days behind the iron bars of the Chongno Detention Center since his death sentence was confirmed along with the dismissal of his appeal on May 27th, breathed his last on the execution platform at Sŏdaemun Prison on the 29th at 10:30 AM. Despite the day of his execution drawing near, he remained composed, engrossed in reading the Bible. At the moment he ascended the gallows, he left no particular last words, only a poem of several lines: "Compatriots, do not expect my appearance, Heaven bestows loyalty engraved on the bones and body. The traces of life and death are sought anew, Paradise has opened for the forest of righteous men." (同胞莫期我容貌, 天賜忠烈銘骨身. 死生踪跡方更尋, 樂園已開義士林.)

December
(Korean Youth Association Federation Established) Since recent times, youth associations have sprung up like bamboo shoots after rain throughout Chosŏn. Aiming to guide and unify these to fulfill their intended mission, numerous proponents led by O Sang-gŭn and Chang Tŏk-su initiated the Korean Youth Association United Preparatory Committee and worked towards establishing the federation. Their sincerity was not in vain, and the inaugural general meeting was held at the Chongno Young Men's Hall starting from the 1st of this month. After three days of meetings, the complete establishment of the association was achieved. At that time, 130 local youth associations had joined.
(Detective Shot Dead in Kyŏngsŏng) On the 4th, upon receiving a report that fundraisers for the Provisional Government's military funds had entered the house of Pyŏn Tŏk-yŏng in Unni-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, officers from the Chongno Police Station went to make arrests. **Summary**

This article chronicles events in Korea and internationally from July to December 1920. Key domestic events include major floods on the Han River, the lengthy and procedurally flawed trial of the 48 signatories of the March 1st Declaration, the suppression of student lectures and newspapers (Choson Ilbo, Tonga Ilbo), the visit of a US Congressional delegation which sparked demonstrations and further crackdowns, armed resistance activities by independence groups in northern provinces and attacks within cities like Wonsan and Kyŏngsŏng, the implementation of a revised (but still limited) local administrative system, the arrest and sentencing of members of resistance groups like Kwangboktan and Tait'ongdan, and the execution of independence activist Kang U-gyu. International news covers anti-Japanese sentiment in California, British recognition of Egyptian independence, the Hunch'un Incident leading to Japanese troop deployment in Manchuria, the Irish struggle exemplified by the death of Terence MacSwiney, unrest in India, and the establishment of the League of Nations and the Permanent Court of International Justice.

The article is particularly revealing about the tense atmosphere of colonial Korea in 1920. It highlights the persistent Korean resistance through various means – legal challenges, peaceful demonstrations (Manse protests, welcoming US delegation), cultural activities (lectures, youth groups), and armed struggle (bombings, attacks on police/officials). Simultaneously, it details the Japanese colonial authorities' responses: legal prosecution (often flawed), censorship and suspension of publications, violent suppression of protests, military/police actions against resistance groups, administrative reforms aimed at co-option, and capital punishment. Historians would find the detailed accounts of the 48 signatories' trial, the descriptions of public unrest surrounding the US delegation's visit, the reporting on armed resistance and its suppression (including specific names and locations), and the commentary on international independence movements (Ireland, India, Egypt) highly valuable for understanding the dynamics of Korean nationalism and Japanese colonial rule during this period.

庚申年의 거둠
						一記者
七月
(五十年來의 大洪水) 이 달 2日부터 始한 降雨는 1週間을 繼續하야 8日에 至하야는 듸디어 50年來 初有의 大洪水를 出하야 漢江은 36尺 9寸의 增水를 起하며 龍山에는 5千戶의 沉水가 有하고 더욱 漢江沿邊에는 幾多의 水害를 致하야 損害額이 約 300萬圓에 達하얏스며 市內 各 新聞社에서는 避害民을 爲하야 救濟金을 募集하얏다.
(48人 公判 開始) 民族으로의 感情과 世界로의 改造에 隨應하야 昨年 3月 1日, 朝鮮의 獨立을 內外에 宣言하고 自進하야 囹圄中의 몸이 된 朝鮮民族代表 33人과 及此에 直接 關聯된 崔南善 以外 14人은 內亂罪이니 非內亂罪이니 하며 地方法院·高等法院 出入에 約 1年有半의 時日을 費하던 中 結局 單純 保安犯으로 認定되어 듸디어 12日 午前 9時부터 京城鐵道部  下層特別法廷에서 右 48人에 對한 公判을 開始하얏는데  開廷 劈頭에 大久保辯護士의 「이 事件은 現行 法令 中 如何한 條文에도 當하지 아니한다는 不受理申立이 有하야 자못 一般의 注意를 惹하얏스나 事件의 審理는 繼續되어 第2日은 崔麟 外 8人을 審問하고 第3日은 李寅煥 外 5名, 第4日은 吉善宙 外 40人을 審問하얏스며 第5日에 至하야는 許憲辯護士의 公訴 不受理申立이 有하야 立川裁判長은 不得已 閉廷을 宣言하고 第6日에는 正式으로 本案公判 中止를 宣言하얏다. 刑事訴訟法에 依하면 或 法源이 他 一法院으로부터 移來한 事件을 受理함에는 그 事件의 移屬을 示한 主文 中에 반듯이 그 受理할 法院을 指定한 것이 有함을 要하게 되엇다. 그런데 이 事件은 高等法院으로부터 京城地方法院에 移屬된 事件임에 不拘하고 그 主文에 京城地方法院을 指定햇다는 文句가 無하얏는 바(理由書에는＜90＞ 잇엇스나) 京城地方法院은 法理上 그 事件을 受理할 資格이 無하얏다. 許憲 辯護士는 此에 着眼하야 듸디어 不受理의 抗議를 提出하매 裁判所 一同은 驚惶罔措하야 公判 中止를 宣言한 바이며 이 事件은 延하야 世間의 큰 웃음거리가 되어 司法의 權威가 地에 墮한 感이 有하매 그 後 얼마가 못되어 朝鮮司法界에 大淘汰가 行하얏섯다. 그런데 48人의 氏名을 示하면
孫秉熙, 崔麟, 權東鎭, 吳世昌, 林禮煥, 權秉悳, 李鍾一, 羅仁協, 洪基兆, 金完圭, 羅龍煥, 李鍾勳, 洪秉箕, 朴準承, 李昇薰, 朴熙道, 崔聖模, 申洪植, 梁甸伯, 李明龍, 吉善宙, 李甲成, 金昌俊, 李弼柱, 吳華英, 朴東完, 鄭春洙, 申錫九, 韓龍雲, 白相奎, 安世桓, 林圭, 金智煥, 崔南善, 咸台永, 宋鎭禹, 鄭魯湜, 玄相允, 李景燮, 韓秉益, 金弘奎, 金道泰, 朴寅浩, 盧憲容, 金世煥, 康基德, 金元璧, 劉如大
(巡回講演團 解散) 千有餘員의 우리 東京留學生으로 組織된 學友會에서는 夏期休暇를 利用하야써 巡回講演團을 組織하야 朝鮮 內地에 文化的 大宣傳을 行하기로 하고 일즉이 東亞日報의 後援을 得하야 其旨를 一般에게 發表하매 世間의 期待는 實로 多大하얏다. 歸國路次에 爲先 釜山, 晋州, 大邱 等地에서 講演을 行하야 同胞의 熱狂的 歡迎을 受하고 一行은 17日 京城에 入하야 翌 18日 午後 團成社에서 講演會를 開하고 金俊淵君이 登壇하야 「世界의 改造와 吾人의 覺悟」라는 題로 약 2時間에 亘하는 熱辯을 揮하고 繼하야  金度演君이 「朝鮮産業의 將來에 就하야」라는 題로 몃마디 말을 試하는 際에 京畿道 第3部로부터 突然히 解散을 行하고 該講演團에 對하야도 또한 解散을 命하얏다. 그런데 그의 理由는 特別指論한 것이 업섯고 그 말하는 全體가 朝鮮統治精神에 違反함이엇다.
(徐相漢公判) 20日에는 東京地方裁判所에서 徐相漢에게 對하야 四個年 懲役을 宣告하얏다. 是에 先하야 王世子殿下對方子女王殿下의 嘉禮日이 世間에 傳하매 東京에서 苦學하던 大邱出生 徐相漢이라는 靑年은 그것을 그대로 볼 수가 업다 하야 미리미리 爆彈을 準備하야가지고 嘉禮 當日을 期하야 兩 殿下의 同乘馬車와 又는 齋藤總督 及 警視廳에 擲彈하야써 日鮮融和의 口實을 滅絶코저 하다가 事前에 發覺되여 그間 審理를 繼하던 中 今回 公判을 行한 것이라.
(總督府 襲擊計劃) 29日은 朝鮮總督府, 東洋拓殖會社, 京城日報社에 爆彈을 投하야 一擧 破壞코저 하던 郭在驥, 尹小龍, 李成宇 外 4人에 對한 取調事實의 發表가＜91＞ 有하야 一般 人心은 더욱 恟恟하얏다.
(新地方制 發布) 29日은 總督府 制令으로 新地方制가 發布되엇는데 그 槪要는 府面에는 府面協議會를 置하게 하되 府及指定面에 限하야는 協議會의 協議員은 人民中으로서 選擧케 하고 尙且郡島에는 學敎評議會와 各 道에는 道評議會를 置하고 此에 幾人의 評議員을 官選하야 學校費 又는 地方費에 對한 諮問을 行하게 함이엇다. 그런데 此 新制度에 依할 協議會 又는 評議會도 議決機關이 아니요, 다만 諮問機關에 不過하는 點에서 從來의 府協議員會 又는 道郡叅事會와 別般 相異가 업섯다 함이 一般의 直感이어섯다.
(中國의 黨派戰) 그리고 이달 月初부터 中國에는 安徽派(幷安福派) 對 直隷派, 卽 段琪瑞派對 吳佩孚派의 大衝突이 有하야 兩派는 干戈로서 相見하게 되매 輿論은 大槪 吳氏 側으로 歸하며 段氏 側이 敗北를 重하야 段派 中 徐樹錚 以下 9名은 日本公使館에 身을 隱하고 겨우 局을 結하얏스며 尙且 우리 京城에서는 朝鮮 各地에서 起한 靑年團體를 聯合코저 하는 朝鮮靑年會聯合期成會가 吳祥根, 張德秀氏 以外 十數人에 依하야 發起되엇스며 朝鮮學生大會에서는 各地에 巡回講演을 行하얏스며 30日에는 朝鮮民團 主催 講演 中 演士 朴武炳, 林鐘淵氏 等의 言論이 問題가 되어 同民團이 解放되엇다.
八月
(又復 大洪水) 2日에는 又復 大降雨가 有하야 漢江의 增水가 3, 4尺에 達하고 京城市內의 浸水家屋이 2千餘 戶에 達하며 家屋의 崩壞, 橋梁의 流失이 頗多하얏다.
(公訴不受理 判決) 問題의 48人 公訴不受理 問題는 9日 京城地方法院으로부터 公訴不受理의 判決을 言渡하얏는데 境檢事는 此에 不服하고 京城覆審法院에 控訴하얏다. 此와 共히 金賢黙 外 26名의 水原事件과 朴擎得 以外 數人의 新義州事件과 崔殷植 外 126人의 安城事件과 卞相泰 外 9人의 昌原事件도 其後 一體로 公訴不受理의 言渡가 有하얏다.

(光復團 死刑) 日韓倂合 以來로 不絶히 不平을 품고 光復團을 組織하야 軍資金 募集에 從事하던 慶州 朴尙鎭 禮山 金漢鍾 兩人은 恐喝 殺人犯으로 4日에 高等法院에서 死刑으로 確定하얏다.
(海參威視察員 出發) 約 一周間 前에 海參威 新韓村에 居住하는 兄弟로부터 成한 視察團 一行 20餘 名이 京城에 入하야 各處를 見學하다가 8日 京城을 發하야 新韓村으로 歸하얏는데 團長 趙永晉 外 1人은 或 事故로 因하야 數日을 京城에 加留하얏다.
(朝鮮敎育會 改正) 11日 附勅令으로 朝鮮 敎育令 一部가 改正되엇는데 그 要旨는 (一) 從來 普通學校는 修業年限 4年을 本則으로 하던 바 此를 今番에 6年으로 改하고 地方의 情形에 依하야 5年 又는 4年으로 함을 得하게 된 것, (二) 普通學校의 修業年限이 6年으로 되는 普通學校에는 地理·歷史를 新加하는 것, (三) 高等普通學校 卒業生을 爲하야 2個年 以內의 補習科를 新設함을 得하게 된 것인데 此 補習科를 畢하면 將來 日本의 高等學校나 專門學校의 入學試驗에 正式으로 應할 資格을 엇게 될 것이라 하얏다.
(日本議會 召集期) 12月 25日로써 日本은 第 44回＜98＞ 義會를 召集키로 12日 官報로 詔書를 發布하얏다.
(第一回 國際聯盟總會) 國際聯盟規約 第3條에 依하는 第1回 聯盟總會는 米國 大統領 윌손氏의 召集에 依하야 15日부터 瑞西의 서울 「제네바」에서 開催되엇다. 參加國은 英, 佛, 日, 伊 等 53個國이엇는데 開會劈頭에 白耳義 前首相 「떼·라크로아」는 米國 大統領 「윌손」氏의 發한 聯盟 第1回 總會의 召集令을 朗讀하고 瑞西 大統領 「모타」는 答辭 兼하야 列國 代表에 對한 歡迎辭를 述하얏스며 次에 議長의 選擧가 有하얏는데 白耳義 前外相 「이만」氏가 當選되엇다. 그런데 今會議에 重要問題가 될 點은 某某國에 對한 聯盟加入의 許否, 聯盟規約 修正, 委任統治 問題·軍備制限 問題 等이 有한 바 1889年, 1907年의 海牙平和會議에 比할 만하얏다.
(大同團事件 公判) 李堈公殿下를 上海로 擁立하야 大韓獨立의 氣勢를 揚하고저 諸般計劃을 整하고 어느 點까지 實現을 見하다가 中途 失敗되어 檢擧되고 말은 全協一團 37人에 對한 公判은 19日 午前 10時부터 京城地方法院에 얼리엇다. 그런데 이 事件은 一般이 認함과 如히 己未 3月 1日 孫秉熙 以外 32人이 朝鮮獨立을 宣言함에 次하야 第2回로 朝鮮의 獨立을 宣言코저 一般階級을 網羅하야 同志者를 求하여 宣言書를 印刷하고 그리하는 第一着으로 朝鮮의 民衆과 因緣이 自有한 李堈公을 上海로 擁立하고저 함이엇는데 그 事件은 非常히 浩大하얏는 바 24日에 至하야 겨우 事實審問을 終了하고 2, 3辯護士의 辯論이 有하얏고 判決은 12月 7日에 言渡한다 하얏다.
(京城府協議 選擧) 新地方制度에 依하는 第1回 京城府協議 選擧는 20日에 行하얏는데 協議員數는 日本人 側으로 18名, 朝鮮人 側으로 12名이엇다. 朝鮮人 側 當選者는 崔翰宇, 金鍚泰, 元悳常, 李康爀, 韓翼敎, 金圭源, 方奎煥, 李世賢, 劉秉珌, 芮宗鍚, 全聖旭, 申章熙 諸氏이엇다.
(京城圖書館 設立) 京城에는 아즉것 圖書館의 設置가 無하던 中 去 9月 以降 尹益善氏 外 몃 분의 發憤彈誠으로 圖書館의 設立을 見하게 되어 27日 翠雲亭 內에서 開館式을 行하얏다.
(콜크市長 遂 餓死) 愛蘭問題는 日復日 險惡을 極하야 이제는 英帝國으로서도 實로 取할 策을 不知하리만큼 混沌하게 되엇다. 그런데 이 混亂의 主要 人物의 1인 愛蘭 콜크市長 막쉬이니氏는 市長으로서 挺身, 先頭에 立하야 英國의 政權에 武力反抗을 試하다가 去 8月에 逮捕한 바＜99＞ 되어 同月 26日 軍法會議審理로 二個年 禁錮를 受하매 氏는 義不食英食이라 하야 餓死同盟을 始하얏다. 그리하야 氏의 運命은 日로 迫함에 不拘하고 英政府는 그대로 泛視하고 말앗다. 그래서 絶食하기 74日 되는 이달 25日 永遠히 生의 意識을 失하얏는데 此 英雄的 死는 문득 世間의 良心을 沸騰케 하야 그 報가 世間에 傳하자 愛蘭 全土는 문득 死의 幕이 나림과 가티 憂愁에 包圍되며 一般 人民은 寺院에 奔詣하야 鳴咽 中에 市長의 冥福을 祈禱하얏스며 世界 新聞紙는 一齊히 筆을 擧하야 氏의 死去를 吊하얏는데 外國의 內事에 筆을 不染하기로 有名한 巴里 新聞 中 「에고·또·파리」紙까지 彼의 死로써 『壓迫된 民의 悲壯한 哀訴』로써 世界에 反響타 하얏스며 日本新聞 中 大阪每日新聞과 如함도 社說로써 그의 英雄的 死를 吊하앗다.
(姜宇奎 死刑執行) 去 5月 27日 上告棄却과 共히 死刑確定된 以來 180餘 日의 지긋지긋한 時間을 鐘路拘置監鐵窓下에서 지나이던 그는 29日 午前 10時 30分 西大門監獄 死刑臺에서 最後의 숨을 짓고 말앗다. 그는 刑臺에 오를 날이 今日 明日에 迫하얏슴에 不拘하고 依然히 聖經을 耽讀하며 泰然自若하얏스며 絞首臺에 오를 그때에는 別般 遺言도 無하고 다만 「同胞莫期我容貌, 天賜忠烈銘骨身. 死生踪跡方更尋, 樂園已開義士林.」의 數句詩를 남겻슬 뿐이엇다.

(京城에 刑事銃殺) 4日 京城內 雲泥洞 邊德永家에 假政府軍資金募集員이 入하얏다는 報를 聞하고 鍾路警察署로부터 出張檢擧 中 6穴砲의 發射가 有하야 朝鮮人 刑事 1名은 卽死하고 日本人 巡査 1名은 重傷을 當하얏는데 各地에 如許한 報가 續出하얏스나 京城에는 初有의 事變이엇는 바 一般은 자못 驚惶하얏다.

(印度形勢 險惡) 印度는 英國의 覊絆을 脫하려는 運動으로 『라홀』地方에서 『마하트칸티』氏를 首領으로 한 分離黨員이 反對投票者에게 暴行을 加하고 候補者를 襲擊하야 形勢가 益益 惡化하는 中이라는데 此에 對하야 日本外務省 歐米局 堀內第二課長은 語하되 歐洲大戰의 時 印度人은 戰線에 出征하얏스며 公債에 應하기도 하야 英國을 援助하얏스나 印度總督은 依然히 壓制政治를 施함으로 時代에 覺醒한 印度人은 1917年 印度大臣 『몬타큐』氏와 印度總督과 協議하야 作製한 印度 自治政策에 滿足치 아니하고 更히 一步를 進하야 印度의 獨立을 渴望하는 바이니 由來 印度人은 政治, 法律學에 興味를 抱하고 英國의 各 大學에서 熱心으로 硏究하는 中이며 多數는 成籍이 優秀하나 印度總督府에서는 何等 官職도 與치 아니함으로 辯護士가 되어 反英國運動을 繼續하는 바 英國은 恰似히 虎子를 養하야 野에 放함과 如한 者로 頗히 危險千萬이라 思하노라. 勞農政府는 將來 世界大革命의 導火線으로 벌서부터 『아푸카니쓰탄』 波斯 等에 過激派黨員을 特派하야 赤化運動을 巧妙히 行하는도다. 物質無盡藏인 印度를 失함은 英國으로는 大打擊인 故로 各種의 政策을 施하야 印度의 獨立을 防止하는 中이나 愛蘭 以上의 大問題로 思하며 『베싼트』 未亡人이 印度人에게 同情하야 反英運動에 從事함은 多少 興味잇는 對照라 하엿다. 云云(新聞紙의 發表에 依함).
