Article Title: 歲拜들입니다 (We Offer New Year's Bows)
Author: 一記者 (One Reporter)

歲拜들입니다
						一記者
From young boys and girls to respected parents—from students to teachers—from youth to the elderly—from tenant farmers to landlords—from factory workers to company presidents—from younger siblings to older brothers—from farmhands to masters
We offer New Year’s bows. I am Chungnam. I respectfully wish Father and Mother ten thousand blessings for the new year.
We offer New Year’s bows. I am Kyŏnghŭi. I respectfully wish Father and Mother ten thousand blessings for the new year.
We didn’t sleep all night last night and played instead. They say if you sleep on the last day of the twelfth lunar month, your eyebrows turn white? That wouldn't happen with the solar New Year, right? We played *yut* and *chogaetjil* (shell game?) and also had our fortunes told; we had so much fun. Early this morning, we paid our New Year's respects to Grandfather and Grandmother. Grandfather and Grandmother were very pleased and gave us 50 *chŏn* each, telling us to buy toys or snacks. They also said, "Aren't you happy to be one year older? You must grow up quickly to get married," showing us much love. And we sat on Grandfather and Grandmother's laps and deliciously ate the *yŏt* (taffy) Grandmother gave us. Then Grandfather asked, "Aren't you going to pay respects to your father and mother?" So we immediately got up and came to offer our New Year's bows to Father and Mother. I don't know how happy I am to be one year older. Father always used to say, "Those children must grow up quickly so I can send them to school to study," and was very worried that we weren't growing up fast enough. I am nine years old now, and Kyŏnghŭi is seven, so starting this year, I can go to school, right? Yes, Father? Starting the new year, I'm going to school? Yes, Mother? And from the new year, we won't play pranks anymore and will take care<46> of our clothes and shoes. And we won't fight or argue, but will love each other and spend our days smiling together. Grandfather told us that's how you become a good person, a *yangban* (nobleman). And Mother, why do you only praise Kyŏnghŭi as a good child, and why do you always scold and reprimand me, loving me less? Starting this year, will you love me like Kyŏnghŭi? Yes, Mother?
But Mother? I don't understand what Chungnam is saying. To me, it seems like you love Chungnam more. When you make clothes, you make Chungnam's clothes first, using good fabric... And when you serve rice, you only give Chungnam white rice... And you give me what Chungnam leaves uneaten... And you often scold me, saying "You darn girl!" And whenever you go somewhere, you only take Chungnam along, and if I even show a sign of wanting to go, you shout, "Where does a girl think she's going?"... I feel very resentful (忿해요), I often cried alone. Starting this year, please love me too, just like Chungnam says. Don't look down on me just because I'm a girl. And can I go to school starting this year too? Yes, Mother?
Well then, we will go pay our respects to Uncle and then go offer New Year's bows to the neighborhood elders. If we offer bows, we also get money...
We offer New Year's bows. I am Yŏngsik, a third-year student.
Principal? I am very grateful to greet the new year. Last year too, you loved me much and taught me much, and that grace is like the rivers and seas, but this year too, please love me much and teach me much. I am still a young person and an ignorant person. If you, Teacher, teach me well, perhaps I can become a good person. Upon facing the new year, I have no special impressions and no special plans. Only, embraced in Teacher's loving bosom, I wish to learn much and ask much, to come to know what I do not know, and to realize more of what I have idealized, thereby repaying Teacher's sincerity in teaching even to some extent, and fulfilling even one ten-thousandth (萬의 一) of what my parents hope for... These are my only thoughts. And
Teacher, starting this year, how about changing the curriculum somewhat to include more subjects of practical use (實地有用)? While no course may be unnecessary, for us Koreans (朝鮮人), focusing too much on subjects like mathematics (數理) seems inappropriate (不當). For us, who have little training (鍜鍊) of the brain and lack ways of research, it is often very<47> cumbersome. It seems the reason for our low health rate (健康率), with ailments like nervous breakdowns (神經衰弱) and brain diseases (腦病), likely lies largely there. And
Teacher, please prepare more equipment for chemistry and physics experiments (理化學實驗具) and specimens of animals and plants (動生物 標本) so that we can gain knowledge through actual observation and practice (實見 實習). Just hearing about things through words and seeing them only in writing, with descriptions of "like this, like that," makes things vague (어슴푸레), seeming plausible but not quite, making it hard to understand well. Also, even if we seem to understand it at the time, once that moment passes, it becomes the same material again (마챤가지 재료). Therefore, instruction through experiments or lectures using specimens seems more necessary than anything. And
Teacher, the number one issue at our school is the matter of the athletic field (運動場). Because the athletic field is inadequate, our school's athletic performance (成績) is very inferior (劣等) compared to other schools. That's why whenever we have an athletic competition (試合) with any school, we consistently suffer crushing defeats (慘敗). What greater shame (羞恥) could there be for the school's reputation (體面) or the students' reputation? Just look at the recent All-Korea (全鮮) Baseball Tournament (野球大會), what greater shame could there be? As a so-called leading school (屈指) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), no, as the school once said to be unique (唯一) in Korea (朝鮮) in terms of history and education (育英上), to be defeated (노흠을 當햇스니) by OO School... That day, we couldn't even finish our meals, and we were so resentful (忿한지) that we cried for three consecutive days (連 三日).
Principal, starting this year, please prepare (작만하셔서) an athletic field at our school and promote (鼓吹) enthusiasm for sports. Please also provide more sports equipment. If the school cannot do it, we students are thinking of raising funds through begging (乞錢) or selling our labor to prepare at least one athletic field this year. And
Teacher, why are there no organizations at our school that meet separately outside of class hours? It would be good if things like a student association (學友會) or alumni association (同窓會) were organized, or if not, at least discussion groups (討論會) or lecture societies (講演會) were organized, so that general students could gain much in knowledge exchange (知識交換), friendship and intimacy (友誼親睦), and other spirits of assembly (集會) and concepts of groups (團體).
Teacher, facing the new year, I intend to offer my mind and body to new directions (方面) with new feelings. Please discern (洞察) our intentions and love us more and teach us more. From here, I will go to offer New Year's greetings (換歲問安) to Teacher K (K先生).
We offer New Year's bows. I am a youth living in the neighborhood. I pray that your esteemed self's (尊位님) energy (氣力) will be even more vigorous (萬康) in the new year, and also wish for ten thousand blessings (萬福) within your household (宅內). Last year too, thanks to your esteemed self's thorough (周到) sincerity (誠意), the village affairs (洞中일) were handled well, and the village (洞中) passed the time in peace and comfort (安樂). This year too, please love the village even more and do many good<48> things. By the way, last year's project (事業) was establishing an improved village school (改良 書堂), but for this year's project, let's organize a farmers' association (農會) and strive for agricultural improvement (農事改良), industrial development (産業發達), etc., in our village (洞里). Such matters are not easy (容易치 아니합니다) unless someone like your esteemed self, who possesses all the real power (實力) in terms of age (年齡上), experience (經歷上), and credibility (信用上), becomes the central figure (主軸). For the happiness and improvement (幸福 向上) of this village and these residents (主民), I have the thought that it would be especially good if your esteemed self became the initiator (發起의 主人). Also, it seems our village is still mired in old customs (舊俗), with all the old-style evil practices (惡風) of coming-of-age ceremonies, weddings, funerals, and ancestral rites (冠婚喪祭 等) remaining as they are, and all the decadent customs (敗俗) like heterodox rituals (淫祀) and grave relocation (遷墓) still present. Let's fix these things quickly. Especially, let's have the villagers jointly (共同) subscribe (購讀) to several newspapers and magazines so that the general villagers can become somewhat aware of the new trends (新風潮) and gain some new knowledge (新知識). This too seems unlikely to be easy without your esteemed self's arrangement (周旋), so please give it special consideration (加念). Also, it seems there are some among the village youth who are indulging (侵犯) in drinking, womanizing, and gambling (酒色 雜技). Please call them individually (낫나치), persuade (曉喩) them with good words, and help them become decent human beings. I hear my own cousin (四寸 동생) has devoted himself to gambling (鬪錢) and suffered a loss (損失) of nearly one hundred won (近百圓). Where could such a sinner (罪人) be? What if that money had been used (업시하엿스면) for the village school or for the public good (公益) of the village? If there are those who do not listen even after your esteemed self persuades them, they are harmful elements (害毒物) to our village<49> and sinners (罪人) in human society (人世), so we cannot leave them be. It seems best to either sue them in court (法에 訴하던지) or else (不然하면) deprive them of their right of residence (居住權). And
Esteemed Sir, by the initiative of us youths, it has been decided to organize a Diligence and Frugality Association (勤儉契) to, on the one hand, work diligently (勞働) to make production (生産) abundant (豊足), and on the other hand, prioritize saving (節約) to enrich (潤澤) the economy (經濟界). Please provide special support (贊助) and protection (擁護) so that it succeeds well. These are generally my thoughts upon facing the New Year. From here, I will go to offer New Year's greetings (換歲 問安) to the village head (洞長) elder.
We offer New Year's bows. I am a tenant farmer (小作人). I respectfully wish the landlord (地主) elder ten thousand blessings for the new year and venture to ask one thing. Last year too, thanks to the landlord elder's kind consideration (厚顧) and favor (德澤), I managed to preserve my meager life (殘命). This year too, please take pity (愛恤) on this poor and cold body and open up a way of living (生活之方) for me. I hear that you are taking away the Samsŏnp'yŏng (三仙坪) paddy field and giving it to someone else. Is this true (事實)? If it is true, then my life is truly over.
Landlord elder! Even if I farmed lazily (懶農) and the harvest (收穫) was less compared to previous years (例年), please forgive (容恕) me especially just for this one year. This year, I will farm diligently as much as possible to ensure a large yield (所出) so that there is no loss (損害) to you, landlord elder. If the landlord elder does not consider my circumstances (事情), I will have no choice (不得已) but to wander and beg (流離求乞) with my old mother and young children. Landlord elder! Please, I only hope for your gracious (거륵하신) decision (處分). Never mind my own self (一身), please take care of me especially for the sake of my old mother and young children! And please select (擇하야) good seeds (種子) for me, and also pick out about ten ditch-diggers (가래ㅅ軍) to repair (修築) the ditch that was damaged (破壞) in last year's monsoon. Around March... Feeling very sorry, I brought a live pheasant (生雉) and passed it to your servant (下人). Please use it for a meal (食饌) sometime. I will go home this way and have decided (作定) to go into the city (城內) tomorrow to sell my labor (품을 팔기로). Please stay well (安寧히).
We offer New Year's bows. I am a factory worker (職工). I wish the company president (社長) elder blessings (慶福) for the new year and also pray for the great fortune (萬幸) of the company (社中). I am a fellow who sells his body (몸을 팔아 먹는 놈), so even though it's the New Year, I have no special feelings (別 感想). Only, if my body is healthy (健康) so I can work hard and receive much money to eat my fill and dress warmly, that would be satisfaction (滿足). However, President elder, the amount of labor (勞働率) is too much, making it impossible to manage myself (지접할 수 업스며?) or look after my family (家族). Please give us one day off (休暇) per week and also reduce (減해) the working hours (勞動時間) somewhat.<50> They say civilized countries (文明國) use an 8-hour system (8時間制), but we probably can't do that yet. And please raise the wages (雇金) by just 20% (2割) compared to the current situation (現狀). Prices are soaring (高騰) and I have many mouths to feed (食口), so it's utterly impossible (到底히) to survive. Coming at dawn (새벽) and leaving only after dark (어두어야), earning merely 50 or 60 *chŏn* (五六拾錢) — the situation (情況) of my many family members crying from hunger (배곱하), the scene (景況) of them shivering violently (으르르 떠는), I truly cannot bear to see it. With what can I console (慰勞) them? How can I fulfill the demands (要求) of many family members with just 50 or 60 *chŏn*?
President elder, please examine (삷히시고) the situation of us workers and consider (參酌) the public opinion (輿論) of the labor world (勞働界), and starting this year, please raise the wages somewhat and reduce the workload (勞働割) somewhat. This is not my personal (個人) selfish opinion (私意), but words representing the workers. Listen here (여봅시오).
President elder, the situation of the workers is indeed pitiable (可矜). We alone break our backs (뼈가 빠지도록) sweating, yet we are number one (第一이외다) in not eating well, not dressing well, and not living well. If you don't understand this situation, it's truly frustrating (抑鬱). Please, by all means (아모쪼록), discern our situation and grant at least some of our demands. Otherwise, who knows what kind of incident (變故) might occur... There's a proverb (俗談) that says, "Inviting a guest you could treat with a chicken, you end up treating them with a cow (닭 잡아 격글 손님을 소 잡아 격는다)." President elder, do you understand?
We offer New Year's bows. I am the much-felt younger brother whose wrist you warmly grasped, saying, "Ah, my dearest younger brother." I respectfully wish you, elder brother (兄님), ten thousand blessings and pray that you achieve all the things you have planned (期約) according to your wishes. My ideal (理想) for the new year is aimed at studying abroad (遊學). It seems I will leave my homeland (故國) in the not-too-distant future (將來) and experience the scenery (風景) of a foreign land (異國). Ah, my respected (敬愛) elder brother! Let us, you according to your will, elder brother, and I, the younger brother (小弟), according to my will, try to carry them out (實行) to the end. Ah, my respected elder brother! Please, please have great fortune (萬幸).
We offer New Year's bows. I am a farmhand (머슴군). I look up to and thank (仰謝) the master (主人) elder for his great virtue (厚德) and pray for even greater fortune (萬幸) in the new year.
Among my lack of particular feelings (所感), my only thought is that I wish you would feed me well, clothe me well, and also give me high wages (雇金), enabling my future (將來) to include getting married, having sons and daughters, and forming a satisfying (滿足) family just like you, Master.<51>
一記者<46-51>

**Summary**

This article from *Kaebyŏk* magazine presents a series of New Year's greetings (Sebae) from various individuals representing different social strata in colonial Korea to figures of authority or respect. Each greeting reveals the specific concerns, desires, and sometimes grievances of the speaker: children wanting love and schooling, a student requesting practical education and better school facilities, a youth advocating for village modernization and social reform, a tenant farmer pleading for leniency and security, a factory worker demanding better wages and working conditions, a younger brother expressing aspirations for studying abroad, and a farmhand wishing for basic necessities and the chance to build a family. The article uses the traditional custom of Sebae as a frame to explore contemporary social issues and hierarchies.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its portrayal of social tensions and aspirations during the colonial period. It highlights issues like gender inequality in families, dissatisfaction with the education system's focus and resources (especially compared to Japanese schools, implied by the baseball defeat), calls for rural modernization conflicting with old customs, the precariousness of tenant farming, the nascent labor consciousness demanding better conditions based on "civilized country" standards (8-hour day), and the personal ambition for overseas study as a path for advancement. The frank expression of complaints and demands, particularly from the student, youth, and factory worker, reflects the social ferment and growing awareness documented in publications like *Kaebyŏk*.

歲拜들입니다
						一記者
어린 男女로부터 어룬 父母에게—學生으로부터 先生에게—靑年으로부터 老年에게—小作人으로부터 地主에게—職工으로부터 會社長에게—아우로부터 兄님에게—머슴군으로부터 主人에게
歲拜 들입니다. 저는 中男이올시다. 아버님 어머니께 새해의 萬福을 삼가 비옵니다.
歲拜 들입니다. 저는 敬姬올시다. 아버님 어머님께 새해의 萬福을 삼가 여쭙나이다.
저의들은 어제 밤새도록 자지 아니 하고 놀앗습니다. 섯달 그믐날 잠을 자면 눈섭이 세인다나요? 陽曆설은 그러치 안켓지요? 저의들은 윳도 놀고 조개질도 하며 또 身數 보기도 하야 메우 질겁게 놀앗습니다. 저의들은 오늘 아적 일즉이 한아버지와 한머님께 歲拜를 들엿습니다. 한아버지와 한머님께서 매우 귀해 하시며 작난감을 사가지던지 菓子를 사먹던지 하라고 돈 五拾錢 式을 주셔요. 그리고 「너의들 한 살씩 더 먹으니까 깃브지? 어서 자라야 장가도 들고 시집도 가지.」 하시며 매우 사랑하셔요. 그리고 저의들은 한아버지와 한어머니의 무릅 우에 안겨서 한머니께서 주시는 엿을 맛잇게 먹엇서요. 그리다가 한아버지 말슴이 「아버지 어머니께는 歲拜 아니 들이니?」 하시길래 저의들은 곳 일어나 아버님 어머님께로 歲拜 왓습니다. 저의는 한 살씩 더 먹으니까 어떠케 깃븐지 모르겟서요. 아버님께서 항상 말슴하시기를 「저 애들이 어서 자라야 學校에 보내어 工夫를 시길 터인데」 하고 저의들이 어서 자라지 못함을 매우 걱정하셧지요. 저는 지금 아홉 살이고 敬姬는 지금 일곱 살이니까 저는 이 해부터는 學校에 갈 수 잇지요? 네 아버지? 새해부터는 學校에 가요? 녜, 어머니? 그리고 저의들은 새해부터는 작난도 아니 하고 옷과 신발도＜46＞ 들마추겟습니다. 그리고 싸흠질도 아니 하고 말다툼도 아니 하고 서로 사랑하며 서로 웃으며서 지내겟습니다. 그래야 조흔 사람이 된다고 兩班이 된다고 한아버지께서 말슴해주셔요. 그리고 어머니께서는 왜 敬姬만 조흔 애라고 稱贊을 하시고 저는 왜 걸핏하면 꾸짓고 나므라고 하야 들 사랑하셔요? 이 해부터는 저도 敬姬와 가티 사랑해 주셔요? 녜, 어머니?
그런데 어머니? 中男이 말을 나는 알 수 업습니다. 나 보기에는 中男이를 더 사랑하는 듯하던데요. 옷을 지으면 中男이 옷부터 지어주고 또 조흔 천을 들여서...그리고 밥을 담아도 中男이는 쌀밥만 담아주고...그리고 中男이가 먹다가 남은 것은 나를 주고...그리고 걸핏하면 「계집애년이」 하고 막 나므라시고 또 어대를 가던지 하면 中男이만 더불고 단이고 나는 가겟다는 氣色만 보여도 「계집애년이 어대를 가」하고 꾁 소리를 치시며...나는 매우 忿해요, 혼자 늘 울군 하얏습니다. 이 해부터는 中男이 말과 가티 저도 사랑해 주셔요. 계집애라고 업수이 여기지 말구요, 그리고 나도 이 해부터는 學校에 가요? 녜- 어머니?
자- 저의들은 三寸님 업헤 가서 歲拜하고 또 이웃집 어룬들께 歲拜 들이려 가겟습니다. 歲拜들이면 돈도 엇구요...
歲拜-들입니다. 저는 三年級 學生 英植이올시다.
校長先生님? 새해를 마지셔서 매우 고맙습니다. 昨年에도 저를 만히 사랑하시고 만히 가르켜 주셔서 그 恩惠ㅣ 河海갓지만 今年도 亦是 만히 사랑해 주시며 만히 가르켜 주셔요. 저는 아즉 어린 사람이며 모르는 사람이외다. 先生님께서 잘 가르켜 주시면 或 조흔 사람이 될 수 잇구요, 저는 새해를 當하야 別 感想이 업스며 別 作定이 업습니다. 다만 先生님의 사랑하시는 품에 안겨서 만히 배우고 만히 뭇고 하야 모르는 것을 알도록 하고 理想하던 바를 더만히 實現하야 先生님의 가르치시는 誠意를 어대까지 報答하려 하며 父母님의 바라시는 바를 萬의 一이라도... 저는 이러한 생각 뿐이외다. 그리고
선생님, 今年부터는 學課를 좀 變更하셔서 實地有用의 課目을 더 너헛스면 어떠실가 합니다. 어떤 課程이 必要치 아니 하겟습니까마는 우리 朝鮮人 程度로는 數理 가튼 것을 넘우 主體로 하심이 不當할 듯해요. 腦의 鍜鍊이 적고 硏究의 方이 들한 저의들에게는 매우＜47＞ 거북한 편이 만하요. 神經衰弱이니 腦病이니 하야 身體의 健康率이 우리에게 적음은 그 原因이 大槪 거긔 잇슬 듯해요, 그리고
先生님, 理化學實驗具·動生物 標本 等을 더 만히 準備하야 實見 實習의 知識을 엇게 해 주셔요. 말로만 듯고 글로만 보고 어떠타 어떠타 形容만 하니까 어슴푸레 한 것이 그럴 듯 말듯해서 잘 알 수 업고 또 그때는 그럴 듯이 알다가도 그때만 지내면 돌오 마챤가지 재료가 되구 말아요. 그러니까 實驗式 敎授나 標本式 聽講이 무엇보다 必要하겟서요. 그리고
先生님, 우리 學校의 第一 問題는 運動場 問題올시다. 運動場이 不完全하니까 우리 學校의 運動成績은 다른 學校에 對하야 매우 劣等이올시다. 그래서 어떤 學校와던지 運動 試合만 하게 되면 밤낫 慘敗를 當하군 하지요. 學校 體面으로나 學生 體面으로나 그런 큰 羞恥가 어대 잇겟습니까. 日前 全鮮 野球大會가튼 때로만 보아도 그런 羞恥가 어대 잇습니까. 所謂 京城 屈指의 學校로서, 아니 歷史上 育英上, 朝鮮 唯一이라던 學校로서 OO校에게 노흠을 當햇스니... 저의들은 그날 밥을 다 못 먹고 어찌 忿한지 連 三日이나 울엇습니다.
校長先生님, 今年부터는 우리 學校에서도 運動場을 작만하셔서 運動熱을 鼓吹시켜 주셔요. 運動機具도 더 만히 작만해 주시고 學校에서 만약 못하신다 하면 저의들이 乞錢을 하던지 몸을 팔던지 해서도 今年으로써 運動場 하나는 작만하려고 생각합니다. 그리고
先生님 우리 學校에서는 왜 學課時間 外에 따로히 모히는 機關이 업습니까? 學友會나 同窓會 가튼 것을 組織하던지 그러치 아니 하면 討論會·講演會 가튼 것이라도 組織하야 一般學生으로 하여곰 知識交換이나 友誼親睦이나 其他 集會의 精神 團體의 感念을 만히 엇도록 하섯스면 조켓서요.
先生님, 새해를 當한 저의는 새 感想을 가지고 새 方面으로 心身을 提供코저 하오니 저의들의 뜻을 洞察하시와 더욱 사랑하고 더욱 가리켜 주십시요. 이로부터 저는 K先生께로 換歲問安을 가겟습니다.
