For the Religious World
						Talk by Mr. Yi Sang-jae

Isn't there a saying by Confucius (孔子)? "If you lead the people by laws and regulate them by punishments, they will avoid punishment but have no sense of shame (道之以政하고 齊之以刑이면 民免而無恥)." Looking at the general level of people in the world today, although there may be some differences in civilization and variations in morality depending on the country or ethnicity, in a word, it could be said they barely reach the state of *myŏn i mu ch'i* (avoiding punishment but having no sense of shame). We humans, the lords of creation, must reach the dignity of *yu ch'i ch'a kyŏk* (having a sense of shame and arriving at correctness/goodness) in the world we live in, no matter what. Indeed, according to my observations, the lives of ordinary people today lack sufficient sincerity; typically, they engage in double or even triple lives characterized by suspicion, probing, and masks. Even those considered relatively decent often merely engage in simple actions directed only towards the outside. At the same time, what is called civilization today is entirely a business-like civilization biased towards one aspect. Since it is not a true spiritual civilization, this current world, while appearing plausible on the outside, is quite empty internally and will soon collapse. Therefore, what the world's populace today must undertake with all their might is to enrich the content of the common person's life, make it more sincere, thereby connecting all humanity to a single spiritual world, and by doing so, bestow new meaning and new value, that is, a new dignity, upon human life. In other words, it is to enable them to reach the state of *yu ch'i ch'a kyŏk*. This is not only an urgent and important duty right now but also something that cannot be achieved unless all humanity strives together. However, among the various positive phenomena that have occurred in our Chosŏn (朝鮮) since last year, the religious world has achieved remarkable development. Established religions such as Christianity (基督敎), Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎), and Buddhism (佛敎) have each gained many new followers. Furthermore, numerous new religions such as Ch'ŏngnimgyo (靑林敎), Cheugyo (濟愚敎), Samsŏngmugŭkkyo (三聖無極敎), and T'ongch'ŏn'gyo (統天敎) have arisen and are striving to proselytize. Although I do not know, nor need to know, the nature of their truth or content, it is nonetheless a splendidly positive phenomenon. Whatever name it bears, no religion would logically promote evil; it must surely promote good. Seeing the frequent emergence of religions directed towards this good <17> and the satisfactory expansion of religions that promote good, it shows that we Chosŏn people are indeed a people strong in goodness. And the Chosŏn people, strong in goodness, will surely soar in the coming world which seeks to enter spiritual civilization, a civilization of substance.

Thus, long ago in the era of Tan'gun (檀君), Chosŏn already lived a virtuous life of *yu ch'i ch'a kyŏk*, and this has become the national character of the Chosŏn people. Just as a single stream of living water temporarily reveals its force at the foot of a mountain, then hides again, passes through much sand and stone, and reappears at its source, the good heart (善心) that is our national character, established over 3,000 years ago, is revealing itself today. It has directly transferred to religion, the crystallization of all goodness, resulting in today's favorable situation. Around this time, discussions on arms reduction seem to be loudly proclaimed via Switzerland's capital (瑞西서울 - likely referring to Geneva). However, the various instruments like machine guns, cannons, and warships, which aim to save the world by killing people, will inevitably be destroyed someday. From the day after their destruction, the various religious organizations that seek to save the world by saving people will become the sole power. At the same time, in that era, having many such religious organizations will be equivalent to having much military power today. Therefore, in this sense, I think the expansion of the religious world in Chosŏn today is a more splendid thing than the preparation of machine guns, cannons, or warships by other foreign countries.

However, what I wish to request of the religious world and the general public for the New Year is this: do not proceed small-mindedly with small thoughts, but try to advance grandly with large hearts. And advance with absolute sincerity that cannot tolerate even the slightest falsehood. If someone intends to catch carp in the Han River (漢江) and prepares a net, but sizes it just to fit the body of a carp, will that person ever manage to catch a carp in their entire life? One must prepare a large net. Let us set our goal on the enrichment of all human life and advance grandly and sincerely. This is precisely preparing a large net. <18>
<17-18>

**Summary**

Yi Sang-jae argues that the contemporary world suffers from a lack of sincerity and true spiritual civilization, existing in a state Confucius described as merely avoiding punishment without shame (*myŏn i mu ch'i*). He contends that humanity must strive for a state of having shame and reaching correctness (*yu ch'i ch'a kyŏk*). Yi sees the flourishing of various religions (both established and new) in colonial Korea as a very positive sign, reflecting the inherent "goodness" (善心) of the Korean people, rooted in ancient traditions like that of Tan'gun. He contrasts this spiritual development favorably against the militarism (symbolized by arms reduction talks and weaponry) of other nations, suggesting that religious strength will be the true power of the future. He urges Koreans, particularly those in religious circles, to pursue this path with grand vision and absolute sincerity, likening it to preparing a large net to achieve a great purpose. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its articulation of cultural nationalism within the colonial context, framing religious growth as evidence of Korean spiritual superiority and a path towards future national prominence, distinct from Western/Japanese models of modernity focused on material or military power. It reflects attempts to find sources of national strength and identity under oppression, emphasizing spiritual and moral values.

宗敎界를 위하야
						李商在氏 談
孔子 말슴에 이런 말슴 잇지 안소「道之以政하고 齊之以刑이면 民免而無恥」이라고 今日 世界에 在한 一般 人民의 程度는 國家 國家 或은 民族 民族에 依하야 多少間 文明의 差와 道德의 異는 有하다 할지라도 一言으로 蔽하면 겨우 免而無恥라 하겟소. 萬物의 靈되는 우리 사람이 산다는 世界는 어떠케 할지라도 有恥且格의 그 品位에는 到達하여야 하오. 果然이지 現時 一般 世人의 生活은 余의 觀察에 依하면 十分의 純摯가 缺如하고 普通은 狐疑, 探索, 假面의 二重 乃至 三重 生活을 營하며 其中 相當하다 하는 者일지라도 오히려 簡單한 作用으로써 外部뿐 向하는 者에 不過하며 同時에 今日의 文明이라는 것도 全혀 一方面에 偏한 事業的 文明이오. 眞的 精神文明이 아닌바 現下의 이 世界는 外貌는 그럴 듯하나 內部는 자못 空인즉 不遠에 주저안고 말 것이요. 故로 今日 世界民衆이 全體의 力을 盡하야 반듯이 着手하여야 할 것은 一般人의 生活內容을 더 좀 充實케 하야 眞摯케 하야써 모든 人類를 唯一한 精神世界에 結付케 하며 그리함에 因하야 人類生活에 新意義와 新價値, 即 新品位를 附與할 그것이라 하겟소. 다시 말하면 有恥且格의 그 자리에 到達케 함이겟소. 이리함은 刻下의 緊急 且 重大한 義務인 同時에 世界人類가 共同하야 努力치 아니하면 不可한 것이요. 그런데 昨年來 우리 朝鮮에는 여러 가지 조흔 現狀이 多起한 中 宗敎界가 非常한 發展을 遂하야 從來의 宗敎 卽 基督敎∙天道敎∙佛敎와 如한 敎는 各各 幾多의 新敎徒를 得하얏스며 尙且 靑林敎∙濟愚敎∙三聖無極敎∙統天敎 등 幾多 新宗敎가 勃興하야 布敎에 盡力하는 等, 그 眞理의 如何와 內容의 如何는 余가 모르는 바요 또 알 것도 업스나 어찌 하얏던 훌륭히 조흔 現狀이라 하오. 如何한 名義를 가젓던지 宗敎치고 惡을 勸할 理는 업겟고 반듯이 善을 勸할 것인즉 이 善에＜17＞ 向하는 宗敎의 發生이 多하고 善을 勸하는 宗敎의 擴張이 如意함을 見하면 우리 朝鮮 사람이야말로 果然 善이 强한 民族이며 善에 强한 朝鮮 民族이야말로 精神文明, 內容文明에 들고저 하는 今後世界에 雄飛할 것이지요.
그러니 말이지 朝鮮은 멀리 檀君時代에 잇서서 이미 有恥且格의 道化的 生活을 하얏는 바 그것은 눌러 朝鮮 사람의 民族性이 되엇소. 彼一條의 活泉이 山미테 其勢를 暫現하고 다시 隱하야 幾多의 沙石을 通過하야서는 原頭에 다시 나타남과 가티 3,000年 以前에 일러진 우리 民族性인 善心이 今日에 至하야 發露되며 그것은 直히 諸善의 結晶體인 宗敎로 移하야써 今日의 好現狀을 致한 것이요. 이지음에도 軍備縮小論이 瑞西서울을 通하야 喧傳하는 樣이거니와 機關銃∙大砲∙軍艦 等 人을 殺하야써 世上을 救한다는 諸多 機具는 不期何日에 반듯이 破壞될 것이오. 그것이 破壞되는 翌日부터는 사람을 살리어써 世上을 살리고저 하는 諸多 宗敎團體가 唯一한 勢力이 될 것이요. 同時에 그때에 在하야는 그러한 宗敎團體를 만히 가진 便이 今日로 말하면 武力을 만히 가진 세음이 되겟소. 故로 余는 이 意味에서 今日 朝鮮에 宗敎界가 이러틋 擴張됨은 余는 思하되 彼 他邦國의 機關銃이나 大砲이나 軍艦 等을 準備함보다 더 휼륭한 일이라 하오.
그런대 余가 新年의 宗敎界 또는 一般에게 向하야 一言을 付託코저 하는 것은 족으마한 생각으로써 족으마하게 나아가지 말고 크다라한 마음으로써 크게 좀 나아가보라 함이며 그리고 秋毫의 虛僞를 敢히 容치 못하는 絶對의 純摯로써 나아가라 함이다. 漢江의 鯉魚를 잡는다고 그물을 뜨되 그 鯉魚의 體와 見樣하야 겨우 한코의 그물을 準備한다 하면 그 사람 自己 平生에 鯉魚 천신하야 본단 말이요. 큰 그물을 準備하여야 되지요. 우리는 目的을 全人類 生活의 充實이라는 그 點에 두고 크다라하게 純摯하게 나가 보자 하오. 이것이 即 큰 그물을 準備하는 것이요.＜18＞
