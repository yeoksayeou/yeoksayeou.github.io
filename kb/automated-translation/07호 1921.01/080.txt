Stand in the Position of the World-Self and Undertake All Endeavors—My Intuition Upon Greeting the New Year
						Yun Ik-sŏn

Greeting the New Year of 1921 and looking back upon the facts experienced over the past 40 years, there are indeed matters for pessimism and matters for optimism. Whether these facts arise from fate or from free will is a question, but observing them empirically as they are realized, they match like tally sticks (符節). Why is this so? Generally (盖), is it not a clearly recorded (昭載한) fact in history that actions taken while ignoring the truth of the universe and violating the laws of nature, regardless of whether the matter is large or small, not only result in the failure of the matter itself but also plunge one's own life into destruction? Conversely, for those who live under the truth of the universe and practice the laws of nature, regardless of the difficulty of the endeavor, undertaking it leads to possibility, and implementing it leads to success. Therefore, avoiding the former and pursuing the latter is the desire of our lives. To practice this, one must awaken to (覺悟하고) the truth of the universe and clearly observe (明察하시요) the laws of nature. The teachers who enable this awakening and clear observation are our inherent sincerity (誠心) and public fairness (公情). Matters managed with sincerity cannot be destroyed even by the might of Hsiang Yü (項羽), and matters implemented with public fairness can achieve success even in things that could not be succeeded at even with the wealth of Shih Ch'ung (石崇). This is what I have realized through experience. I wish, my fellow compatriots, that when undertaking endeavors upon facing the New Year, you leave the individual-self (個人我) and stand in the position of the world-self (世界我) to manage them, and upon reaching success, do not think of it as the honor of the individual-self but consider it (看做하야) the honor of the world-self, thereby demonstrating the true meaning (眞意義) of human life and achieving the ultimate purpose (究竟 目的) of the great universe. <22>

**Summary**

The author, Yun Ik-sŏn, reflects on past experiences and asserts that success comes from aligning actions with universal truth and natural law, guided by inherent sincerity (誠心) and public fairness (公情). He urges readers, upon the new year of 1921, to transcend the limited 'individual-self' (個人我) and operate from the perspective of the 'world-self' (世界我) in all endeavors, viewing success not as personal gain but as contributing to a larger, universal purpose. From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as it promotes a philosophical, almost spiritual approach focused on universal principles rather than overtly nationalistic or political ones during the colonial period. Historians might find the emphasis on the 'world-self' concept and the downplaying of individual or potentially national identity significant, possibly reflecting an intellectual strategy to find meaning and advocate for ethical action within the constraints of Japanese rule.

世界我의 位地에 立하야써, 온갓 事業에 下手하라―나의 迎新直感
						尹益善
1921年의 新歲를 迎하며 旣往 40年 來의 經驗上 事實을 回顧하니 果然 悲觀할 事도 有하고 樂觀할 事도 有하도다. 此 事實이 運命上으로 出來함인가 自由意志上으로 出來함인가 함은 一 疑問이나 實現하는 바에 從하야 經驗的으로 觀察하면 符節과 如히 合來하더라. 何者가 然한가. 盖 宇宙의 眞理를 無視하고 自然의 法則에 違背하야 하는 行爲는 大事小事를 勿論하고 事 自體가 失敗에 歸함은 勿論이요, 자기 身命 까지 滅亡에 陷케 함은 歷史上 昭載한 事實이 아닌가. 此와 反하야 宇宙의 眞理下에 生活하며 自然의 法則을 履行하는 者는 事業의 難易를 勿論하고 着手하면 可能이요 實行하면 成功이라. 그러면 前者를 避하고 後者에 就함은 吾 人生의 欲望이라. 此를 實行함에는 宇宙의 眞理를 覺悟하고 自然의 法則을 明察하시요. 此를 覺悟케 하고 此를 明察케 하는 師는 吾人 本有의 誠心과 公情이로다. 誠心으로써 經營하는 事는 項羽의 氣力으로도 打滅하기 不能하고 公情으로써 實行하는 事는 石崇의 富力으로도 成功치 못할 事를 成功함에 至하는도다. 此가 余의 經驗上 覺悟한 바니 願컨대 吾 同胞諸氏여 新年을 當하야 事業에 就할 때에 個人我를 離하야 世界我의 位地에 立하야 經營하야 成功할 때에 臨하야 個人我의 名譽로 思치 말고 世界我의 名譽로 看做하야 人生의 眞意義를 發揮하여 大宇宙로의 究竟 目的을 達할지어다.＜22＞
