讀者 交情欄 (Reader's Friendship Column)

To all readers under heaven, let us, the general readership, correct our spirits, unite our hearts, and celebrate! Let us pray! Let us think and pray for this Mr. Kaebyŏk (開闢君), for the esteemed gentlemen of the editorial office. Truly, upon requesting and seeing this Kaebyŏk magazine (開闢誌), I could not suppress feelings of joyous elation. As the proverb goes, "Give water to the thirsty and give rice to the hungry," I believe that for the youth of our peninsula (半島), a great wet nurse has truly been born.

Therefore, although a frail, clumsy (孱拙), and foolish person like myself need not say more, as you highly discerning gentlemen (紳士) will sufficiently grasp (斟酌), let us youth of the peninsula (半島), as much as possible, purchase and read (購覽) this revered and great Kaebyŏk magazine more and more, so that our knowledge (學識) may become as abundant as others', and our aspirations may be perfectly, loftily, and firmly cultivated. I pray this with all my heart and conclude.
(P'ungsan-gun [豊山郡], Pak Yong-ch'ae [朴龍綵])

Since Kaebyŏk Issue No. 4, I have had the privilege of being among the readers. Upon thoroughly reading the swiftly delivered copy of No. 4, every character, every phrase, without exception, pioneers the chaos, shatters the darkness, and allows the peninsula's universe (半島乾坤) – like the Great Ultimate (太極) appearing at the beginning of heaven and earth, like the dazzling sun wheel (晃晃日輪) emerging from Fusang (扶桑) – to freely engage in new activities of transformation (幻骨脫態) under the great light. This is a grand mission, a gospel! Reading it once, I danced with joy (手舞足蹈); reading it twice, I shouted in fervent delight (熱狂呌喜); reading it thrice, the forests, streams, and rocks seemed moved (山林泉石이 爲之動色이로다). Grand is Kaebyŏk! Exhilarating is Kaebyŏk! However, having been completely unaware of Kaebyŏk's birth from issues one to three, and only encountering it with issue four, I lament being behind the times and feel the shame of being a frog in a well (井蛙) regarding society. While hoping for Kaebyŏk's tolerance, I briefly state my impressions. If it can serve as a reference for the esteemed writers (執筆諸兄), I would consider it the highest honor.

Generally, when attacking a person's evil, one must first consider their capacity to endure it. When teaching goodness to a person, one must certainly consider their ability to follow it. Attacking too excessively provokes the stubborn man (頑夫) to draw his sword and glare (按劒視); teaching too loftily merely encourages the self-destructive tendencies (自暴心) of the vulgar person (俗子). Looking around Korean society (朝鮮社會), how many individuals have truly escaped the dens of stubborn and vulgar people? Furthermore, how many even possess the sprouts of escape? <119>

Therefore, in our peninsula's (半島) world of discourse, excessively harsh arguments and overly lofty words not only have little effect on the rapid awakening of the people's wisdom (民智), but rather, many will reach the point of drawing swords and self-destruction (按劒自暴). Thus, I earnestly hope that those who take up the pen for the development of the peninsula will certainly keep these stubborn and vulgar people as the standard, and take care to gradually correct their ingrained vulgar habits (固慣鄙習) with a writing style like a gentle breeze and beautiful sun (和風麗日的 筆法).

Myohyang Sanin's (妙香山人足下) critique of filial piety (孝道) and his discussion on the value of Chinese literature are indeed a thunderbolt hammer (霹靂椎) shattering Korea's imitative and pretentious (蹈襲假裝的) bad customs (陋習), and a lion's roar (獅子吼) proclaiming the foundation of new culture. However, while admiring the extreme earnestness (切摯) of his writing style, I cannot help but worry (未嘗不 憂慮) about how it will be understood (諒解) by the stubborn and vulgar people who occupy the vast majority of our society. Perhaps it's a groundless worry (杞憂).

If a stubborn person (頑夫) were to read this, they would surely claim it insults the ancient sages (先聖) and charge in with lofty and stern arguments as if to raise a great affair. This is what is called provoking the drawn sword and glare (按劒視). If a vulgar person (俗子), that is, one who only esteems superficial civilization (皮相文明) and lacks principles and substance (沒主義無實相), were to read this, they would despise the virtue of natural family ethics (天倫) and not hesitate in the evil act of abusing their parents. This, of course, stems from the reader's misunderstanding, without even attempting to study the writer's subtle intentions and profound meaning, narrowly interpreting and blindly following critiques like "just demand filial piety from parents" or phrases like "children are not the property of parents," and considering the destruction of ethics and morality (滅倫悖德) as commonplace (看作例事). This is what is called encouraging self-destructive tendencies (自暴心). Looking at the current state of society, the so-called teachings of Confucius, Mencius, Zengzi, and Zhu Xi (孔孟曾朱之遺訓) have long since disappeared. Although their names and forms are not entirely absent, they fade day by day, month by month, and are about to be swept away entirely. Why should Myohyang Sanin apply his punishing pen and induce our immature compatriots towards drawing swords and self-destruction (按劒自暴)?

To argue back and forth like this is my own confession of being one of the stubborn ones (頑夫), but criticizing and slandering sages revered for thousands of years is not only premature for the current level of the people, but the blame should fall on the ingrained sins of those who, for millennia, formally revered only the fragments of words from past figures from one to ten, modeling only the dregs. I believe it cannot be recognized as an error in the words themselves that the sages intended to warn their contemporary society or individuals. Moreover, the eras of Confucius, Mencius, Zengzi, and Zhu Xi were not this 20th century. How can we blame (追咎) their contemporary words for not fitting this 20th century? And why should we disregard (度外에 附하고) the fact that many of their words are indeed suitable even for this 20th century, and only point out two or three unsuitable statements to criticize them?

The saying "There are no parents in the world who are not [good]" (天下에 無不是底父母) – I believe this so strongly that I consider it an unchanging truth for ten thousand ages (亘萬古不易之言). Regardless of time, ancient or modern, or place, East or West, extending even to encompass the animal kingdom, I believe this is the ultimate maxim (格言). Therefore, the "child-centered principle" (爲子女主義) needs no superfluous talk (贅說).

In my humble opinion, the duty towards parents is solely filial piety (孝道), and the duty towards children is solely education (敎育). To confuse these and, with the single character '爲' (for), advocate "Discard the parent-centered principle (父爲父主義) and adopt the child-centered principle (子爲子主義)" seems unlikely to be a rational argument.

That parents have traditionally lacked a sense of duty towards their children, specifically the concept of education, is truly lamentable (可謂痛哭處). Urging reflection among parents (爲父母者) is an urgent task. However, when discussing this, to first criticize the sage's teachings (聖訓) on filial piety and trumpet the child-centered principle (父爲子主義) raises concerns that the sense of duty among children (爲子者) might become even more lax (解弛)... (Omitted)... Furthermore, recently, writers in newspapers and magazines criticize our fathers and ancestors' (父祖) incompetence and inaction so thoroughly (餘地업시), that we, their descendants, feel our roots exposed (根本이 綻露되어) and truly cannot hold our heads high and conduct ourselves in the world (行世할 수 업서요). Originally, in my foolish ignorance of even my own family lineage, only a sense of decline grew, preventing me from showing my 'true self' (지수군이?) to others. But after reading Paektu Sanin's (白頭山人) "Hong Kyŏng-nae and Chŏn Pong-jun" (洪景來와 全琫準), I was greatly stirred and excited... (Omitted)... As a frog in a well and a rat in a toilet (井蛙厠鼠), born and raised in a remote, secluded countryside (僻陬窮鄕), to dare to shout foolish words (妄說을 狂呌) and impose upon the esteemed reading (崇覽) of the gentlemen in the world of discourse is extremely presumptuous (僣越). However, deeply feeling the affectionate care (眷愛) of Mr. Kaebyŏk, I wish to express my sincerity of admiration and longing (景慕之誠) while exposing my foolishly honest heart (愚直한 衷赤). If I am granted the honor of having my foolish words wisely selected (狂<120>言聖擇), I would be overjoyed. I pray for the health of Mr. Kaebyŏk... (Ch'ŏngju [淸州], Within Ch'ungbuk Provincial Seedling Station [忠北 種苗場內], Pak Chong-nyŏl [朴鍾烈])

Literary Forest (文林)

A, B, C, D [Dialogue between drinking buddies]
To my drinking buddy (酒朋) - Samyonggye Yasŏngsuk (三用界 夜星淑)

The sky is cold, the moon is bright and cool, let's have a drink. I'm bored, let's have a drink. No, I'm busy with work (事務). If you're busy, let's do it tomorrow. No, I quit drinking yesterday. A lifetime (生涯) is but one cup of wine, what's this talk of quitting? No, really, I quit. Alright then, drink today and quit starting tomorrow. Okay, let's drink. I'll make up my mind starting tomorrow. And so, one cup, one cup, and again one cup (一盃一盃復一盃). "When the cup comes to you, don't stop your hand" (盃行到君에 莫手停). Let's cast off all worldly affairs (破除萬事) with wine, they say. They form a drinking party until midnight, swaying left and right, coming and going, and disperse after getting drunk. The next day (翌日), a friend visits, and it's the same. And the day after that, the same again... No. Reflect. Awaken yourself. The work you said was busy? A task planned for one day cannot be decided in ten. And quitting tomorrow? That tomorrow is indefinite. Drinking buddy (酒朋)! Awaken yourself (自覺). Be alert (警醒). When it's cool, cultivate your spirit (精神修養); when bored, read newspapers and magazines aloud. If you must urge someone to drink, add one character and urge them to learn (學術). Having awakened like this, there is no need to urge drinking, nor any need to drink. Decide for yourself (自心自意). Awaken and understand yourself (自覺自解). Break the bad habit (惡習) of drinking. How great and how small is the harm (害毒) of drinking? Small, it affects one individual; large, it affects the nation. One step further, it affects the world. Youth who bear social responsibility! What is the rationale for optimism about the future? Is the purpose to waste (浪費) money, precious even for buying life's necessities, in taverns and brothels (酒肆靑樓)? What hope is there with drinking buddies who remove benefit and cause harm (去益致害)? Is it not that cultivating the spirit with justice and humanitarianism (正義人道主意), advocating education, and encouraging effort will ultimately lead to the path of human perfection? It is natural according to heavenly principles and also clear. In this era of reform (改造), what devastates progressive justice and humanitarianism? What causes the loss of fundamental spirit? I always wonder if it is the creation of Yi Ti (儀狄)? We who bear the grave responsibility of this age should be confident that eradicating (除根) this bad habit (惡習) first is the right thing to do. Do not blame me.

Thoughts on Late Autumn - Wŏljŏng M Saeng (月汀 M 生)

Autumn sounds (秋聲) guide me up the west pavilion,
Seasonal things (時物) complete their pure and solemn forms.
Maples seem to dye the boat-filled forest (舟渥化林?), a world of maples (楓世界),
Golden chrysanthemums fill the garden like family (黃金滿朶菊家庭).
Heart hurt by wind and cold, the virtuous alone toil,
Gratefully watching mothers sewing by the window, are they well?
Whistling, I stand between heaven and earth (嘯立乾坤), observing myriad phenomena,
Why does the tree shade (樹陰) depart from its greenness?

Darkness - Kyŏmip'o (兼二浦), Chang So-ryu (張小流)

Darkness (黑暗), gloomy (우중충한) darkness!
Where did you come from?
Gathering all the dark (暗) from the dense forest (숩) and the black (黑) of ink,
Like the black wrapping cloth (검은 보) of a mischief-maker (질알장이),
Darkness that has turned the whole world upside down! <121>
Your name is pitch-dark night (漆夜).
Pitch-dark night! World of darkness!
Day after day, we see you. Yes,
Without saying sweet or bitter, we trust you will come as usual.
But hating you is my true feeling, so
Even if a sliver of moon shines,
I grasp my lover’s wrist and jump for joy.
Ah - Darkness (暗黑), go away! Yet you still come,
You are my true enemy.
But here are its admirers (讚美者)!
Who are they? Thieves (盜賊놈), conspirators (陰謀者), or bats (박쥐),
All traitors (逆賊) to Way and Virtue.
No, I forgot. I am also an admirer.
The most precious person, I who shout this,
Also came from darkness into this world, "Waaah!"
Ah - how was the powder of mystery (神秘) prepared?
Use it well - even if for evil!
The bright sunlight of life,
When the red sun rises in the East (東域),
Far, far away flees that darkness!

My View - Yi Sun-gi (李舜基)

The current state (現狀) of our society... Attach the lens of breadth (壙大) to the eye of ideals (理想) and view our peninsular Land of Hibiscus (我半島槿域) lengthwise and crosswise (縱橫觀). Among the myriad and diverse things (森羅雜多), there are still places where customs (因習) chewed upon (咀嚼) in the ancient dimness (太古朦朧) still exist (尙存).
While others add experiment (實驗) to research (硏究), append effort to struggle (奮鬪), deeming last year's make as already old style (舊式), turning it into a kind of waste product (廢物), demanding the latest style (最新式), latest make, planning for utmost refinement and newness (惟精惟新) in material development; while spiritually (精神上) and morally (道德上), they display (發揮) humanity's highest ideals, and the screams (絶叫) of the weak (弱者), who lost their natural human rights (天賦人權) under the command (號令) of the strong (强者) and cried that Heaven's way is inconstant (天道無常), finally gain new light (新光明) under justice and public fairness (正義公道); while slaves (奴隷) turn to liberation (解放), despotism (專制) to popular sovereignty (民本), restraint (拘束) to freedom (自由), selfish bestial desire (利己的 獸慾) to altruistic humanity (利他的 人道), fiercely shedding (猛脫) the old shell (舊殼) of incomplete (不徹底) prejudice (偏見), heading towards recovering (恢復) paradise (樂園) on earth with a new spirit of human coexistence (人類共存) – at this very time, are not the Confucian scholars (儒生), who bear the responsibility of so-called leaders, the ones sitting in humble thatched cottages (芽屋草堂), their rigid brains (思庫頭腦) tightly bound (結縛) by dust-filled (塵埃) traditional headbands (網巾), dreaming of Emperor Fuxi (天皇氏?), chanting about the 23rd year of Muin (戊寅 23年?), considering the scriptures saying "Do not wear clothes (服) if they are not those of the former kings (先王); do not perform rites (禮) if they are not those of the former kings" as an inviolable sacred constitution (神聖者不可犯의 憲法), and blindly guiding the promising youth whose future is vast yet vague (渺邈)?

"Money is almighty" (金錢萬能) implies its utilization (活用). Especially money used for public works (公共事業) holds infinite authority (權威). America's (美洲) prosperity today is due to the presence of countless Carnegies (카네기) and Pierpont Morgans (피모듸?). Our surrounding companies, factories, schools, libraries, youth halls (靑年會館), etc., have long been eagerly awaiting the appearance of Korea's Carnegie (朝鮮의 카네기翁). However, it seems that in the money chests (錢机) of misers (守錢奴) and the vulgar rich (拙富豪), money cannot see the bright sun and moon, as if sentenced to indefinite confinement (無期禁錮), merely waiting in darkness (暗黑裡) for the active hand (活手) of capable figures (有爲의 人物 提公?). By utilizing this, our desolate (荒漠) cities could be made into London (런던市) or New York City (뉴욕市), and it could be provided for various facilities (諸般施設) promoting social education. Are not the certain rich people (某某富豪) the ones who possess all these conditions?

A facet of our society's reform (改造)... Today, the entire world chants reform and strives for its realization (實現). However, according to each situation (處地) and circumstance (形便), the methods for implementing reform will have some differences. In any case, reform implies destruction (破壞) passively and construction (建設) actively. Whether materially or spiritually, our society has many things to fundamentally destroy and many things to build. Here, I briefly summarize and present (略括表示) one facet. <122>

Spiritually (精神上으로)
Destroy (破壞): Root out laziness (懶怠心拔去)
Exterminate selfishness (利己心撲滅)
Obliterate arrogance (驕慢抹殺)
Break down superstition (迷信打破)
Construct (建設): Diligently practice (勤勉力行)
Expand public interest mind (公益心擴充)
Realize philanthropy (博愛實現)
Fulfill duties completely (義務盡行)

Materially (物質上으로)
Destroy (破壞): Cut off the Four Colors (四色斷切 - likely political factions)
Abbreviate extravagant spending (濫費省略) on the Four Rites (四禮)
Strictly prohibit early marriage (早婚痛禁)
Construct (建設): Encourage and practice education (敎育勵行)
Develop industry (産業發展)
Reform the system (制度刷新) of clothing, food, and housing (衣食住)

Entering Kanghwa by Boat from Inch'on Harbor - Mongbu (夢夫), Kang Sin-ch'ang (姜信昌)

An autumn wind, a thousand-li traveler (千里客),
Again heading towards Kanghwa Castle/City (江都城).
Family longing (族懷?) is distant as the sky (天共遠),
The inlet sun (浦日) and water compete in brightness (水爭明).
Sails arise with the color of distant islets (帆生遙嶼色),
Gulls roll in the sound of the angry tide (鷗捲怒潮聲).
Willingly carrying the dream of the red dust/world (甘載紅塵夢),
Coming now, hopefully I can awaken (今來庶可惺).

My Beloved Poem - Yanghwasil (養和室), Hong Sŏk-yŏng (洪奭泳)

Beloved (님아), beloved, my beloved (우리님아)
My love, my beloved (내사람는 우리님아)
I want to see you, want to see you (보고지고 보고지고)
I want to see the beloved I longed for (그리던님 보고지고)
Where is my beloved? (님게신 어대메뇨)
I shall go to see my beloved (님뵈오려 가을세라)
Saying the mountain is high (山놉고), the water deep (물깁다고)
I won't make excuses (핑게하고) and not go
Whatever mountain (山이 어던), I'll cross over it (넘어가고)
Whatever water (물이 어던), let's cross it (건너가세)
My beloved is surely (정령) there
I cannot but go (아니 가진 못하리라).

Elm Tree Shade - Kaesŏng (開城), Kye Pong (桂鳳)

On the distant mountain (머ᅟᅵᆫ山), ruby-colored (紅玉빗) haze is dim (朦朧),
In the water palace (水宮), shining green shade (綠陰) hangs like an embroidered curtain (錦繡帳),
Across the river, on the fresh and beautiful (鮮美) green grass,
The shadow (그림자) of a large and lush (茂盛) elm tree
Lies leisurely (閒暇히), head towards the East (東).
A cool (淸凉) gentle breeze drives silver waves (銀波)
Giving a jade baptism (玉洗禮ᅟᅳᆯ?) to the quiet sandy beach (沙濱),
On the bright river, long like a whetstone (숫돌가티),
A cluster of anchored (닷준?) red sunset glow (묽은 놀) from the South
Hides (숨도다), startled by the creaking sound from a pavilion (삐걱어리는 樓聲).

▲ To all friends contributing to the Friendship Column (交情櫊寄稿諸友): It is a regret for this reporter (記者) that we cannot publish all the writings from our limitless friends within the limited page space (有限한 夏數) of each issue. The intention of this reporter is, as much as possible, to publish contributors' writings as they are, publicly revealing their merits and flaws (醜美) before all friends, creating a practice notebook (鍊習帳) to show the path of selection (撰擇) by appreciators (鑑賞하는 이) and progress towards maturity (成熱의 進路). However, most of your writings are long pieces (長篇), causing considerable difficulty in handling them all within the Friendship Column. Not only that, but masterpieces (傑作) among long pieces are quite rare. We hope you will send short pieces (短篇), and even if it's just one refined (洗鍊) phrase you deem inadequate, send good writing. Also, please write neatly (精書) on manuscript paper (原稿用紙). Even if it's just one line, seeing your effort, we have no thought of simply discarding it, but we cannot do anything with messy handwriting (亂書). (Reporter for this column [此欄記者]) <123>

**Summary**

This "Reader's Friendship Column" from Kaebyŏk Issue 7 compiles diverse contributions: letters expressing enthusiasm for the magazine and debating tradition vs. modernity (especially Confucian values like filial piety vs. individual rights and education), an essay denouncing alcoholism, several poems (classical Chinese style and vernacular Korean), and a short essay criticizing conservative Confucian scholars and wealthy misers while advocating for social reform (spiritual and material). An editor's note concludes, requesting shorter, neatly written submissions due to space constraints. From a historical perspective, this column is valuable as it showcases the intellectual ferment and social concerns among Korean readers during the colonial period. It reflects anxieties about cultural identity, the critique of entrenched customs, aspirations for national progress and enlightenment, engagement with new ideas (like social reform and individual rights), and the use of the magazine as a platform for public discourse and literary expression, despite editorial limitations. Historians can observe direct reader responses to published articles, debates on tradition, and calls for social and personal transformation.

滿天下讀者 諸位시어 우리 一般讀者 諸位께옵서는 긔운을 바르게 하고 마음을 한 가지로 하야 祝賀합시다. 祈禱합시다. 이 開闢君을 爲하야 編輯室 諸先生님을 爲하야 생각하고 祈禱합시다. 참으로 나는 이 開闢誌를 請求하여보는 同時 欣喜踊躍의 情을 禁치 못하얏나이다. 俚諺에 渴者에게는 水를 與하고 飢者에게는 飯을 與하여야 되겟다 함과 가티 우리 半島 靑年에게야 말로 大乳母가 誕生된 줄로 밋나이다.
그럼으로 이와 가티 孱拙하고 어리석은 나로 하여곰 更言을 不要하여도 高明하신 여러분 紳士는 十分 斟酌하시겟지마는 될 수 잇는 대로 우리 半島 靑年은 이 거룩하시고 偉大하신 開闢雜誌를 더욱 더욱 購覽하셔서 우리의 學識도 남과 가티 豊富하게 또는 立志를 圓滿하게 高尙하게 堅固하게 涵養하기를 마음을 힘것 다하야 빌고 끄치 나이다.

我觀
李舜基
우리 社會의 現狀…理想의 眼에 壙大의 鏡을 附하고 我半島槿域을 縱橫觀하라. 森羅雜多한 其中에는 아즉까지 太古朦朧한 時代에서 咀嚼하던 因習이 尙存한 處도 잇서
남은 硏究에 實驗을 加하고 奮鬪에 努力을 附하야 去年製는 오히려 舊式이라 하야 一種 廢物로 돌리고 最新式 最新製를 要하야 物質上 發展에 惟精性新을 圖하며 精神上 道德上으로는 人類最高의 理想을 發揮하야 强者의 號令下에서 天賦한 人權을 喪失하고 天道無常을 絶叫하던 弱者의 聲은 마츰내 正義公道下에서 新光明을 어더 奴隷는 解放으로 專制는 民本으로 拘束은 自由로 利己的 獸慾은 利他的 人道로 化하야 不徹底한 偏見의 舊殼을 猛脫하고 人類共存의 新精神으로 장차 地上에 樂園을 恢復하려는 이때에 芽屋草堂 中에서 塵埃가 充積한 網巾으로 思庫頭腦를 잔득 結縛하고 天皇氏를 夢하며 戊寅 23年을 高唱하고 先王의 服이 아니면 服치 아니하며 先王의 禮가 아니면 行치 아니한다는 經句는 神聖者不可犯의 憲法으로 알고 將來가 渺邈한 有望의 靑年을 盲導함은 所謂 指導者의 責任을 가진 儒生이 아닌가?
金錢萬能은 活用을 意味함이라. 더욱이 公共事業에 쓰는 金錢은 無限大의 權威를 가졋도다. 美洲 今日의 繁榮은 無數의 카네기와 피모듸가 잇는 所以라. 우리 周圍의 會社 工場과 學校, 圖書館, 靑年會館 等은 朝鮮의 카네기翁의 出現을 苦待 渴望한지 已久하도다. 그러나 守錢奴 拙富豪의 錢机 中에는 金錢은 光明한 日月을 보지 못하고 無期禁錮의 處分을 바른 듯이 暗黑裡에서 다만 有爲의 人物 提公의 活手를 待하고 잇는 것가티 보인다. 이것을 活用하야 荒漠한 우리 都市로 런던市, 뉴욕市를 맨들 수 잇고 社會敎育을 促進하는 諸般施設에 提供할 수가 잇도다. 이 모든 條件을 가진 者는 某某富豪가 其人이 아닌가?
우리 社會 改造의 一端… 今日은 全世界가 改造를 高唱하여 實現을 努力하는 때이라. 그러나 各其 處地와 形便을 딸아 改造의 實行 方法은 多少 異同의 別이 잇슬 것이다. 何如턴지 改造란 그것은 消極的으로 破壞와 積極的으로 建設을 意味함이라. 物質上으로던지 精神上으로던지 우리 社會에는 根本的 破壞할 것도 만코 建設할 것도 만토다. 茲에 그 一端을 略括 表示하노라.＜122＞
