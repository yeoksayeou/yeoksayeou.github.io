-Attention Regarding Remittances-

One, When sending a combined payment for *Kaebyŏk*, *Sin Yŏsŏng*, *Ŏrini*, and other publications at one time, please itemize each publication in the correspondence section.

One, When sending money, please be sure to pay via postal transfer to Kyŏngsŏng Account No. 8106. However, postage stamps or revenue stamps are absolutely refused.

One, Payments must be made entirely in advance; if the advance payment runs out, sending of the publication will be stopped.

One, Requesting magazines via cash-on-delivery or collection mail is absolutely refused.

One, For the address, be sure to write down the County (郡), Township (面), Village (里), and Street Number (番地) in detail.

One, When changing your address, be sure to clearly state the old address and the new address.

One, Especially limited to readers in the China region, if you do not clearly state your address (down to the small place name, 小地名지) and name when sending money, the publication cannot be sent.<55>

**Summary**

This article is a notice from the publisher of *Kaebyŏk* magazine detailing the required procedures for sending subscription payments in 1925. It specifies acceptable payment methods (postal transfer only), emphasizes prepayment, requires detailed address information, and outlines how to handle combined payments and address changes. It explicitly forbids payment with stamps or through cash-on-delivery/collection mail and includes a special note for subscribers residing in China. From a historical perspective, this notice reveals the practicalities of publishing and distribution networks in colonial Korea, including the reliance on the postal transfer system centered in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul). The rejection of certain payment methods might hint at logistical challenges or attempts to prevent fraud. The mention of readers in China underscores the magazine's circulation beyond the Korean peninsula.
