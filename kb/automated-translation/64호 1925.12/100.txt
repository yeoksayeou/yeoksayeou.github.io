社會日誌
(9월21일부터 11월 15일까지)
September 21: The United States announced the refusal of entry to foreigners affiliated with the Third Communist International.
In Japan's Kansai region, an Agricultural Policy Conference was held.

Sept. 22:
△ In Tokyo, Japan, a clash occurred between over 30,000 people welcoming the delegation of the Soviet Russian Labor-Peasant Union representative "Reps" and about 700 police officers. △ In the Ch'ŏlsan region, heavy wind and rain caused flooding, affecting over 100 households. △ In Guangdong, China, a plot to overthrow the government was discovered, leading to the downfall of the anti-communist faction, and martial law was declared in the city.

Sept. 23:
△ Jilin Province in China issued an order to confiscate weapons from Koreans residing in Manchuria. △ The Keijō (Seoul) Middle School Joint Athletics Meet was held. △ The Bombay (孟嘉坡) textile workers' strike expanded, resulting in the closure of 72 factories and 130,000 workers going on strike.

Sept. 24:
△ The opening ceremony for the Soviet Russian Consulate General was held in Chŏng-dong, Keijō (Consul General is ＜52＞ Mr. "Sharmanov"). △ The Chinese government notified the United States of its non-fulfillment of the wireless contract. △ Yang Sŭng-u, commander of the Pyŏkch'ang Righteous Courage Corps, was again sentenced to death at the P'yŏngyang Review Court (Han Tong-jin received life imprisonment). △ The inauguration ceremony for Prime Minister Yi Sang-nyong was held at the Shanghai Provisional Government. △ A large fire broke out on Mokto Island, Pusan, burning down 25 houses completely.

Sept. 25:
△ A great whirlwind occurred in Yŏnch'ŏn, causing about 10 houses to collapse. △ The League of Nations Council adopted the proposal submitted by France to convene an international economic conference. △ Japanese-Chinese spinning mill workers in Pudong, China, clashed with military police over wage issues, resulting in dozens of casualties. △ Germany accepted the invitation to the Security Conference.

Sept. 26:
△ In Britain, a communist revolutionary plot was discovered, initiating police action. △ In Hungary, a plot to assassinate the regent was discovered, leading to the arrest of over 40 people. △ The 6th General Assembly of the League of Nations closed. △ Germany argued the necessity of concluding a security treaty. △ Russia appointed Mr. "Karakhan" to handle Eastern diplomacy as a substitute for Foreign Minister Chicherin, who is recuperating.

Sept. 27:
△ Russo-Japanese negotiations ＜53＞ ongoing in Moscow stalled due to issues concerning oil and coal mines. △ Mr. Pak Sŭng-bin was inaugurated as the president of Keijō Bosŏng College.

Sept. 28:
△ An international economic conference opened in Paris with representatives from 23 countries participating. △ The Diplomatic Corps in Beijing requested instructions from their home countries regarding the 13 points submitted by China concerning the Shanghai Incident. △ Workers at over 400 telegraph offices in China went on strike demanding wage increases and improved treatment.

Sept. 29:
△ Over 100 workers at the Yongsan Railway Bureau went on a collective strike over wage issues. △ Germany agreed to participate unconditionally in the European Security Conference. △ China decided on a proposal to lift restrictions on tariff autonomy for the Tariff Conference. △ The Chosŏn Farmers' Association was organized in Keijō through the initiative of numerous prominent figures.

Sept. 30:
△ The French Communist Party initiated demonstrations opposing the Morocco campaign. △ The Policy Council of Japan's Kenseikai Party asserted that both Japanese and Koreans are unsuitable for emigration to Manchuria and Mongolia. △ Rumors of an assassination plot against the Emperor caused unrest in Britain. △ The US-French war debt negotiations reached a settlement between the finance ministers of both countries (France to pay $6.12 billion in principal and interest over 67 years). ＜54＞

October 1:
△ The Government-General conducted a nationwide census throughout Chosŏn. △ Floods occurred in Japan's Kantō region. △ A representative of the Japanese Farmers' Union, Administrative Chief Kura(?), entered Keijō carrying 20,000 won in Korean flood relief funds sent from Soviet Russia to the Pukp'unghoe (North Wind Society), visited the Pukp'unghoe the next day, and handed it over to the society's representative, Kim Yak-su. △ Russian Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Mr. "Chicherin" arrived in Berlin and urged various high officials towards Russo-German rapprochement.

Oct. 3:
△ Following the resignation of Chilean President Mr. "Alessandri", Mr. "Luis Barros" took office. △ An armed group attacked an oil ship in Wiwŏn County.

Oct. 4:
△ Japan's Seiyūkai party resolved on an 8-point platform including industrial development and tax system reorganization. △ A battle occurred in Syria between 5,000 Muslims and French police.

Oct. 5:
△ The European Security Conference opened. △ The Japanese government drafted its proposal for the Chinese Tariff Conference. △ The German representative at the Security Conference declared that "German disarmament alone is not peace." △ Chaeryŏng police banned the Hwanghae Youth Federation conference. ＜55＞

Oct. 6:
△ Japan's Suzuki Bunji was re-elected as chairman of the General Federation of Labour. △ A plot to assassinate the royal family was discovered in Romania. △ A battle occurred between an armed group and police in Kusŏng County.

Oct. 7:
△ The judicial investigation committee for the May 30th Nanjing Road Incident in Shanghai convened. △ Kyŏnggi provincial authorities disallowed participation in oratorical contests for private common schools within its jurisdiction. △ The Chinese side opposed the judicial investigation committee for the Shanghai Incident. △ The Fengtian Clique sent telegrams supporting Provisional Chief Executive Duan. △ A major dispute arose between about a thousand tenant farmers and police/bailiffs on Toch'o Island, Muan County. △ A large anti-British demonstration occurred in Constantinople, Turkey.

Oct. 8:
△ On the Pusan-Shimonoseki Ferry, 36 young women being trafficked to Japan were discovered.

Oct. 9:
△ 700 members of the Labor Friendship Association, affiliated with the Taegu Transport Union, went on strike over wage issues. △ A large fire occurred in Niigata, Japan, destroying over 300 houses.

Oct. 10:
△ The tenant dispute on Toch'o Island worsened, and over 100 armed police officers were dispatched. △ The ideological group Chŏnjinhoe (Advancement Society) ＜56＞ was founded in Keijō. △ The Rif Army besieged the city of Tétouan, and the French Communist Party went on strike.

Oct. 11:
△ About 20 tenant farmers were arrested on Toch'o Island. △ China again demanded Japan withdraw its post offices along the South Manchuria Railway line (citing lack of Washington Conference agreements). △ About a thousand tenant farmers from Toch'o clashed fiercely with police forces on behalf of their 20 arrested comrades.

Oct. 12:
△ The problematic Sino-Japanese wireless negotiations were resolved with the Japanese government renouncing its monopoly rights. △ The Russo-German Commercial Treaty was signed.

Oct. 13:
△ Spinning mill workers at Chinese and British-managed factories in Shanghai went on strike opposing the judicial investigation of the Shanghai Incident. △ In Naeja-dong, Keijō, a pistol-carrying youth named Yi Chong-ch'il was arrested. △ Conflict clouds between the Fengtian and Zhejiang cliques resurfaced in China. △ A strike by Paris transportation workers began. The All-Russian Central Executive Committee convened.

Oct. 14:
△ Following the resignation of Keijō Posŏng High Common School Principal Chŏng Tae-hyŏn, Ch'oe Myŏng-hwan was appointed*. △ The United States strongly advocated active support for China's recovery of sovereignty. △ The expert committee at the Security Conference completed a draft arbitration treaty for ******. ＜57＞ △ Turmoil recurred in China.

Oct. 15:
△ The suspension of the Chosun Ilbo and Kaebyŏk was lifted. △ The All-Korea Baseball Tournament, hosted by the Chosŏn Athletics Association, was held in Keijō. △ The newly constructed Keijō Station and Keijō Stadium were completed. △ A Shrine Enshrinement Ceremony was held. △ In Japan, widespread public outcry arose over the issue of the hypothetical enemy country (Koreans and anarchists) during military training exercises at Otaru Higher Commercial School.

Oct. 16:
△ Ch'oe Tu-sŏn and Im Ch'ang-ha returned to Korea after studying in Germany. △ The Five-Province Allied Army of China occupied Shanghai. △ The Security Conference held in Locarno adjourned after completing seven treaties. △ Tenant farmers from Toch'o Island resolved to expose the crimes of wicked landlords.

Oct. 17:
△ The Beijing Government ordered the withdrawal of Sun Chuanfang's army. △ The Fengtian Army's situation became unfavorable. △ In Kyŏmip'o, 20 people perished when an attendant boat sank. △ A large fire occurred in Korangp'o, Changdan County.

Oct. 18:
△ Sun Chuanfang and others from the Southern faction in China declared war on Zhang Zuolin. △ Zhang Zuolin ordered the mobilization of the Jilin Army. △ ＜58＞ The Zhejiang Army occupied Nanjing. A battle occurred between an armed group and police officers in Kanggye.

Oct. 19:
△ In Mokp'o, due to discontent over the detention of Toch'o Island tenant leaders, another clash occurred between tenant farmers and police. △ The eight Locarno Treaties were promulgated.

Oct. 20:
△ Various ideological groups in Keijō dispatched investigators to examine the tragic clash between Mokp'o police and Toch'o tenant farmers. △ Korean ideological groups in Tokyo resolved to denounce Japanese authorities over the Otaru Higher Commercial School's hypothetical enemy issue. △ China's Wu Peifu departed for the war front as the leader of the Five-Province Alliance. △ In Okayama, Japan, a plot by about 20 youths to manufacture bombs was discovered.

Oct. 21:
△ The Korean Sunday School Conference was held in Keijō. △ Bulgaria issued an ultimatum to Greece (following military incursion).

Oct. 23:
△ The Chicago baseball team delegation arrived in Keijō.

Oct. 24:
△ The Fengtian Army advanced ** southward, and martial law was declared in Fengtian and the Three Eastern Provinces. ＜59＞

Oct. 25:
△ Large demonstrations for tariff autonomy, supported by students and merchants, occurred in China. Provisional Chief Executive Duan issued an order to the nation to unite for the Tariff Conference.

Oct. 26:
△ The Tariff Conference opened in Beijing, China, with representatives from 13 countries attending (Chairman: Wang Zhengting; China's first proposal: recovery of autonomous rights). △ The Koryŏ Farmers' Association was founded in Ningguta, Northern Manchuria.

Oct. 27:
△ The trial of Yi Pok-wŏn and 8 others involved in the Black Flag League Incident opened at the Keijō District Court. △ An armed group appeared in Ŭllyul County. △ The Tokyo General Federation of Labour (Japan) dispatched members to investigate the Otaru Incident.

Oct. 28:
△ Military actions between Bulgaria and Greece ceased. △ The movement to overthrow Zhang Zuolin intensified among Beijing students. △ Korean ideological groups in Tokyo held speeches denouncing military education regarding the Otaru hypothetical enemy issue.

Oct. 29:
△ China's Provisional Chief Executive Duan announced the convening of a National Assembly on January 15th.

Oct. 30:
△ Hostilities resumed between Greece and Bulgaria. △ A plot to sabotage the port and harbor railway ＜60＞ was discovered in Saint Petersburg (Leningrad). △ Germany's National Rights Party declared opposition to the Locarno security treaty. △ The Chinese Allied Army Conference decided on a general offensive within nine days. △ The North Chŏlla Youth Federation was established in Chŏnju.

Oct. 31:
△ Soviet Russia's People's Commissar for Army and Navy Affairs, Mr. "Frunze," passed away.

November 1:
△ Pak Ŭn-sik, President of the Shanghai Provisional Government, passed away (Aged 67). △ Martial law was declared in China's Zhili Province. △ Representatives of the three major leaders in China—Zhang Zuolin, Feng Yuxiang, and Yue Weijun—decided on war mediation and separate talks.

Nov. 2:
△ China's Wu Peifu notified the Beijing Diplomatic Corps of the seizure of general taxes in the southern provinces. △ The French Socialist Party held a conference and decided against supporting the current Painlevé Cabinet. △ In Yŏnggwang, a major disturbance occurred during the founding anniversary of the Labor Federation when the Myŏn chief fired a gun.

Nov. 3:
△ The Japanese Farmers' Union declared a nationwide boycott alliance against human night soil fertilizer. △ A food crisis occurred in Vienna due to a strike by bread manufacturers. △ Shanghai spinning mill workers went on strike again demanding improved treatment. ＜61＞

Nov. 4:
△ The Non-War Alliance Association was organized in Shanghai, declaring the eradication of armies. △ Martial law was also declared in Jilin and Heilongjiang Provinces. △ The South Hamgyŏng Labor-Farmer General Federation was also banned by the police. △ In the Philippines, a proposal for a popular referendum on Philippine independence was drafted. △ The funeral ceremony for Pak Ŭn-sik was held in Shanghai.

Nov. 5:
△ The North Kyŏngsang Youth Federation, initiated by Kimch'ŏn youth, was banned by the police.

Nov. 6:
△ Negotiations between China's Zhang Zuolin and Feng Yuxiang broke down. △ Ŭiyŏldan member Yang Kŏn-ho (real name Yi Chong-sun) was arrested by the North Kyŏngsang police.

Nov. 7:
△ Security was tightened in various places for the 8th anniversary of the Russian Revolution.

Nov. 8:
△ The Kantō Association of the Japanese Proletarian Party resolved to hold its formation ceremony on December 1st.

Nov. 9:
△ Regarding the unlawful arrest (in Keijō on the 6th) of Hong Nam-p'yo, regional editor of the Sidae Ilbo, by Hamhŭng police on charges of libel, the Keijō Mumyŏnghoe and other regional journalist groups rose up in protest. △ The United States rejected Italy's proposed war debt settlement.

Nov. 10:
△ Japan's Ministry of Education responded to inquiries from various groups in Tokyo ＜62＞, stating that the person responsible for the Otaru hypothetical enemy issue would be punished. △ Fighting broke out between Zhang's and Feng's armies near Beijing. △ The chief Chinese delegates to the Tariff Conference, Wang Zhengting and two others, submitted their resignations due to grave issues of the current situation.

Nov. 11:
△ Diet members affiliated with Japan's Seiyū Hontō party organized an alliance to overthrow the current cabinet. △ Merchants and residents in Qingdao attacked various tax offices. △ A secret offensive-defensive alliance was concluded between Russia and Feng Yuxiang.

Nov. 13:
△ A battle occurred between an armed group and police in Ch'osan, resulting in the death of one member of the armed group. △ China's Provisional Chief Executive Duan issued an order to the entire nation to withdraw troops for peace. △ An agreement was reached between Zhang and Feng. △ The Polish cabinet resigned en masse.

Nov. 14:
△ The Diplomatic Corps in Beijing protested to the Beijing Government regarding Wu Peifu's non-payment of the salt tax. △ A large demonstration promoting tariff autonomy occurred in Beijing.

Nov. 15:
△ China's Provisional Chief Executive Duan issued an order to Zhang Zuolin to launch a punitive expedition against Wu Peifu and Sun Chuanfang. Simultaneously, a peace treaty was concluded between Zhang and Feng. (Continued in next issue) ＜63＞

**Summary**

This "Social Diary" from the December 1, 1925 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine chronicles significant events globally and domestically (Korea, China, Japan) from September 21 to November 15, 1925. It covers a wide range of topics including international relations (Locarno Treaties, League of Nations, Russo-German relations, China Tariff Conference), political turmoil (Chinese civil war involving Zhang Zuolin, Feng Yuxiang, Sun Chuanfang, Wu Peifu; plots in Hungary and Romania; cabinet issues in France and Poland), social movements (labor strikes in Bombay, Shanghai, Korea; farmer protests in Korea and Japan; communist activities and suppression), colonial issues (Korean arms confiscation in Manchuria, suppression of Korean organizations, the Otaru military training incident, activities of the Korean Provisional Government and independence fighters like the Ŭiyŏldan), and miscellaneous news (natural disasters, arrests, establishment of organizations, visits).

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this diary is particularly revealing. It highlights the constant surveillance and suppression of Korean activities both within Korea (banning youth federations, police clashes with tenant farmers like the Toch'o Island incident, arrest of journalists) and abroad (arms confiscation in Manchuria). The mention of the Shanghai Provisional Government figures (Yi Sang-nyong, Pak Ŭn-sik's death) and armed groups (Pyŏkch'ang Righteous Courage Corps, Ŭiyŏldan, unnamed groups in Wiwŏn, Kusŏng, Kanggye, Ch'osan) underscores the ongoing independence movement. Furthermore, the Otaru incident, where Koreans were designated as a "hypothetical enemy" in Japanese military training, starkly illustrates the discriminatory and hostile environment faced by Koreans under Japanese rule, prompting protests from Korean groups in Tokyo. The diary also reflects Korea's connection to international socialist movements (receiving funds from Soviet Russia via Japanese socialists) and the complex interplay of regional powers (Japan, China, Russia) impacting the peninsula.
