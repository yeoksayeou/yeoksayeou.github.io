기사제목	朝鮮의 一年·世界의 一年 (One Year in Chosŏn, One Year in the World)
필자	起田 (Kitian/Gijeon)
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Argumentative Essay)

朝鮮의 一年·世界의 一年
						起田

This month is December, and the Year of the Wood Ox (乙丑年) is now coming to a close. A year's time (歲月), if looked at one way, is like a flash of lightning (電光石火), but thinking again, it is not such a short duration. If we ask how long we have been living like this, wearing clothes and building houses, it naturally seems limitlessly long and distant (悠長), but this limitlessly long and distant duration, when broken down, ultimately amounts to nothing more than about 5,000 times this one year. Then, this year, equivalent to one five-thousandth of the entire history mankind has built so far, what has it truly accomplished?

Not only this. Even if it is the same time for the same person, the influence that time has on that person differs between working hours and resting hours. Also, even for the exact same time on the exact same day, the change that time brings to that day can differ tremendously depending on the circumstances (形便) in which that time is situated on that day. For example, the few tens of minutes at sunrise or sunset on a given day bring the most change to that day compared to other times. That is, in the way that time acts upon our human society, it possesses the power to brighten a dark world or darken a bright world. Similarly, even if it is the same period of one year (or more than one year), it will naturally differ depending on the season (世季 - likely means era or period here) in which that period is situated. Then, this past year, situated in a great transition period (一大 轉換期) attempting to leap from the civilization of individualism to the civilization of socialism, what has it truly shown us, and what has it made us think?

Looking globally, the most eye-catching events of this past year are (1) the national rights movement (國權運動) of weak and small peoples (弱小民族), (2) the Asianization of Labor-Farmer Russia (勞農露西亞), and (3) the consolidation of several capitalist powers (資本主義 列國), starting with Britain, America, and France (英米佛). Needless to say, although at the beginning of the Paris Peace Conference held in 1918, there was much talk of justice (正義), humanity (人道), and national self-determination (民族自決), leading to widespread discussion of the weak and small peoples issue for a time, ultimately, at the point of concluding the peace treaties, merely to break the postwar power of the defeated nations (戰敗國) and create new puppets for the victorious nations (戰勝國), a few countries like Poland<2>, Czech (첵크), and Yugoslavia (유-고스라비악크) were made independent. As for all the remaining weak and small peoples, they were divided up under various pretexts like partition (分割), mandate rule (委任統治), or protectorates (保護國). Regarding the weak and small races or other oppressed peoples (被壓迫民族) (for example, China (中國)) that had been possessed even before the Great War without relation to it, as domestic and foreign political situations gradually stabilized after the war, the degree of oppression and exploitation (搾取) was only further increased (the method generally involves giving something while threatening). When such things happen, a corresponding reaction (反動) inevitably arises. The pent-up anger of all these weak and small peoples against the tyranny of these capitalist imperialist states began to explode. It truly began to explode this year. The nationwide movement for the restoration of national rights in China, ignited by the student shooting incident on Nanjing Road (南京路) in Shanghai (上海) on May 30th, is one example; the freedom movements of the Rif tribes (리푸族) and Druze tribes (드류스族) arising in French Morocco (佛領 모롯코) and Syria (씨리아) are another. In other words, the explosion first occurred on the Chinese mainland, where the oppression of many had reached its peak, and the revolt first happened within the territory of France (佛蘭西), where internal exhaustion was particularly severe. However, weak and small peoples are not only on the Chinese mainland or within French territory. They exist everywhere. They are over there, and they are also here; are they only in the lands commonly called colonies (植民地), or are they also within what is called the home country (本國內地)? The term "weak and small peoples" today ultimately refers to the masses who have lost political sovereignty (主權) and suffer economic exploitation (搾取). Even if someone holds the flag of their home country, if they cannot actually participate in the country's political power and cannot obtain security of livelihood – in other words, the masses belonging to the proletariat (無産階級) – they can all be called the same weak and small peoples. And in reality, these masses will join hands. Now, this resistance movement of the weak and small peoples began to explode this year and continues as the year ends. 一隨*動萬波波 (One wave follows another, creating ten thousand waves) – the events of the coming days are noteworthy.

One cannot forget that behind this movement of weak and small peoples lies Labor-Farmer Russia (勞農露西亞). As everyone knows, Russia, after carrying out the Communist Revolution (共産革命) early in 1917, sought to undertake a world revolution (世界革命). To achieve this, it first had to overthrow the capitalist powers (列强). To overthrow them, it had no choice but to join hands with the proletarian masses (無産民衆) within those countries and the weak and small peoples within their spheres of influence. It first proceeded with this strategy (方策) towards the European continent. However, due to the bourgeoisification of the German Revolution and the internal political stability of Britain and France (英佛)<3>, the plan did not go as intended. Thus, the Labor-Farmer government somewhat altered its tactics. For the time being, against the well-defended Western European front, it decided to wage a protracted war (持久作戰), while boldly advancing towards the East, where national grievances were most acute. It planned an outflanking maneuver (迂回作戰) against all capitalist powers through the liberation (解放) of all colonies (植民地) in this region. Establishing the two republics of Uzbek (우스베크) and Turkmen (도루구멘) in Turkestan (土耳其斯坦), and fostering the two new republics of Inner Mongolia (內蒙古) and Buryat (푸리야-도) in Siberia (西佰利亞) to consolidate the ranks of national soviets, or establishing relations first with China and then with Japan (日本) to facilitate mutual exchange – all these stem from Russia's Eastern Revolution, guarding the West and advancing East (西守東進). Of course, this effort had been underway for some time, but it feels like it became more active and concrete this year. The fact that the Russo-Japanese treaty normalizing relations was concluded early this year, and that China's movement for restoring national rights became particularly vigorous from this spring, further supports this view. However, what has been shown so far is merely the prologue (序幕). The truly big events lie ahead.

Faced with revolutionary movements targeting all the world's capitalist empires (the resistance movements of various weak and small peoples and the force of Labor-Farmer Russia's world revolution movement emerging or showing signs of emerging), the Powers (列國), having perceived this, devised various countermeasures. First, while seeking agreement among themselves, the capitalist nations, they also felt the need to increase allies joining their side, even if just one country. Thus, they engaged heavily in such activities this year. For instance, in January of this year, they held a finance ministers' meeting of the Allied powers in Paris (巴里), agreed on the so-called Dawes Plan for distributing annual payments, aiming not to damage relations while dividing the reparations money. During the summer, they resolved the war debt issue with the United States (米國), striving to draw the US into the European political sphere. In September, they held a conference concerning the border guarantees of continental European nations, primarily Germany (獨), France (佛), and Belgium (白 - likely Belgium), and concluded a security treaty regarding this (currently undergoing ratification by the relevant countries), thereby planning for the stability of the European political situation, including even Germany. In this way, the relations among the European and American powers, which had been in a state of continuous entanglement (紛紏狀態) since the Great War, achieved a temporary consolidation, and they are once again turning their steps towards the Chinese mainland. The fact that Britain, America, France, etc., at the Customs Conference (關稅會議) currently being held in Beijing (北京), are smoothly acknowledging China's tariff autonomy (關稅自主權) while trying to appease China as much as possible, allows one to guess where their intentions lie.<4>

In short, this past year, seen globally, saw the revolutionary movements arising from all the weak and small peoples or proletarian masses (無産民衆) on one side, and the consolidation movement of the capitalist powers opposing them, arranging their battle lines against each other. Just as Labor-Farmer Russia is the main force in the former revolutionary movement, Britain (英國) has become something like the leader on the side of the latter capitalist countries. One interesting point here is that the Japanese Empire (日本帝國), following in the wake of capitalist imperialism, was early alienated from the European and American powers due to its own conflicts related to capitalist development. Although it considers getting a bit closer to Russia, it cannot get too close because it is fundamentally dangerous. It tries to join hands with the warlord factions in China, but this too is fraught with complications and unusual backgrounds, making it impossible to act uniformly. It is currently engrossed only in winning general favor in China by pretending to take the lead in advocating for the issue of acknowledging autonomy at the recent Customs Conference. How on earth is this whole situation likely to change? World problems are roughly like that, and

what about the situation in Chosŏn (朝鮮) during this year? The first thing to say about this is that although we always say Chosŏn, Chosŏn when we speak, in reality, this region called Chosŏn is not floating somewhere separate between heaven and earth; it is merely one region within this world. Therefore, the situation in Chosŏn cannot exist separately from the world situation. That is to say, just as two major camps confront each other globally, the same is true in Chosŏn. Especially this past year, the most striking aspects of the situation in Chosŏn are that all policies (政策) of the Government-General (總督府) are becoming increasingly reactionary (反動的), and the people's (民衆) movement is step by step becoming more profound (深刻化).

First, looking at the social movement (社會運動) side, there are definite signs of it becoming somewhat more concrete (具體化) and organized (組織化) this year. That is, it has clearly advanced a stage from the abstract to the concrete, from the emotional to the organized. The division of movements into sectors like the youth movement (少年運動) and the young adult movement (靑年運動) (especially demarcated by age), the farmer movement (農民運動) and the worker movement (勞働者運動), and the effort put into policy research (政策硏究) and internal organization (內容組織) within the same endeavors, can also be seen as explaining the news from this side. The fact that particular attention turned to the farmer movement this year (for example, the Youth General Alliance (靑年總同盟) establishing a special Rural Committee, the Cheondogyo Youth Party (天道敎靑年黨) resolving on the revolutionary<5> enlightenment of farmers, the Chosŏn Nongmin Sa (「朝鮮農民」社 - Choson Farmer Company) and others publishing farmer magazines, or the recent decision by the Labor General Committee (勞總委員會) to establish a separate Farmers' Alliance) and that the youth movement and anti-religion movement showed new momentum, are phenomena worthy of attention.

Not only in the realm of social movements, but generally speaking, the people's movement this year achieved considerable development. Above all, various types of assemblies (集會) and associations (結社) flourished significantly. Various meetings such as Farmers' Congresses, City/County People's Congresses, Newspaper and Magazine Reporters' Congresses were almost continuous. General strikes (同盟罷業) demanding improved treatment for various workers like printing, sock-making, rice-milling, and spinning, as well as tenant disputes (小作爭議) by the general landless farmers, notably in places like Amtae (岩泰), Pukryul (北栗 - likely Pungnyul/Pukryul based on historical context), and Toch'o (都草), were also quite fierce. And the emergence of the social science movement (社會科學運動) even in the student world, which had hitherto been without issue, was truly a phenomenon unseen before this year.

Thinking about it, although there are undoubtedly various reasons for witnessing such development in the people's movement in Chosŏn this year, one cannot forget that (1) as the policies of the Government-General authorities became increasingly high-handed (高壓化), especially regarding speech and assembly, and capitalized (資本化) (in all industrial management), the general populace was naturally spurred to action (感奮), and at the same time, (2) the great tide (大潮流) of social transformation occurring globally also contributed to this.

The year of transition – the Year of the Wood Ox – passes like this. Both for Chosŏn and the world, it leaves behind a huge cornerstone (礎石) and a huge task (宿題). To stand firm on this cornerstone and solve this task, isn't this our work from now on? Looking forward only to the New Year of the Fire Tiger (丙寅). (起田 Kitian/Gijeon)<6>

**Summary**

The author, Kitian, reflects on 1925 as a pivotal year globally and within Korea (Chosŏn). Globally, it's characterized by the rise of anti-colonial/national rights movements (China, Morocco, Syria) often implicitly or explicitly supported by Soviet Russia, countered by the consolidation of capitalist powers (UK, US, France, Japan) seeking stability and control, notably through financial agreements and attempts to manage China. Within Chosŏn, this global tension manifests as increasingly repressive colonial policies (by the Government-General) met with a more organized and intensified popular resistance, including distinct farmer, worker, youth, and even student social science movements, alongside numerous strikes and tenant disputes. The year is portrayed as laying the groundwork for future struggles. For historians, this article is valuable as a contemporary Korean intellectual's analysis linking local colonial realities directly to global geopolitical currents (anti-imperialism, rise of socialism vs. capitalist consolidation). It clearly articulates the growing organization and radicalization of Korean resistance movements across different social strata in response to both colonial oppression and international ideological trends.

朝鮮의 一年·世界의 一年
						起田
이 달이 12月, 乙丑年도 이제는 다-져물었다. 1年의 歲月이란 엇지보면 電光石火와도 갓지마는 또 생각하면 그러케 쩔은 동안도 아니다. 우리가 이럿케 옷 입고 집 짓고 살아오는 지가 지금까지 얼마 동안이나 되엿겟느냐 하면 勿論 限업시 悠長해 보이지마는 그 限업는 悠長한 동안이라는 것도 結局 따지고 보면 이 1年 동안을 約 5천倍한 것에 지내지 못한다. 그러면 지금까지의 全人類의 歷史를 지여 온 동안의 5천분지 1에 상당한 今年은 果然 무엇을 하엿느냐.
이 뿐 아니다. 가튼 사람의 가튼 시간이라 할지라도 그 시간이 그 사람에게 對해서 엇던 영향을 주는데 잇서는 일하는 시간과 쉬는 시간이 다르며 또 꼭 가른 그 날의 꼭 가튼 시간이라 할지라도 그 시간이 그 날에 對한 變化를 주는 데에는 그 시간이 그 날에 位한 形便의 如何에 依하야 엄청나게 다를 수가 잇는 것이니 假令 그 날의 해가 뜰 때 또는 질 때의 몃 十分時間이란 것은 그 날 엇던 때의 시간보다는 가장 만흔 變化를 그 날에 주는 것이다. 곳 能히 그 時日이 우리 人類社會에 작용하는 그 점에 잇서는 어둡던 천지를 밝게 하며 밝던 천지를 어둡게 하는 힘을 가진 것이다. 이와 가티 가튼 1年(或은 1年以上)의 時日이라 할지라도 그 時日이 位在한 世季의 如何 依하야 스스로 다름이 잇슬 것이다. 그러면 그 동안의 個人主義의 문명으로브터 일약 사회주의의 文明에 드러서려 하는 一大 轉換期에 位在한 今年 1年은 果然 우리에게 무엇을 뵈여 주엇스며 또 무엇을 생각케 하엿는가.
