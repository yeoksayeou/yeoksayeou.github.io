北京에도 飛行家 (Aviator(s) in Beijing too)

Our aviator, Sŏ Wal-bo (徐曰甫), who experienced extraordinary difficulties for many years in Central China (中原), is said to have honorably graduated from aviation school last winter.

To celebrate his past achievements (過功), around 60 fellow Koreans in Beijing (在北京同胞), including Ch'oe Tong-o (崔東旿), Kim Hong-sŏn (金弘善), Han Hŭng (韓興), Yi Kwang (李光), and Kim Chae-hŭi (金在熙), reportedly held a grand celebration at the Beijing Cheondogyo (天道敎) Mission Office on the 10th.＜20＞

**Summary**

This news brief reports that Korean aviator Sŏ Wal-bo graduated from aviation school in China after enduring hardships. Fellow Koreans residing in Beijing held a celebratory gathering for him at the local Cheondogyo Mission Office. From a historical perspective, this article highlights early Korean involvement in aviation, the presence of a Korean community in Beijing during the colonial period, and the role of organizations like Cheondogyo in providing a meeting place and fostering community among Koreans abroad. The mention of Sŏ Wal-bo, a figure associated with the independence movement, is particularly noteworthy for historians studying Korean activities in China.
