기사형태	논설 (Editorial)

天玄地黃
						弄球生
內外多事한 三月
This month, this day, is called the famous day when the Korean independence movement began, and compatriots abroad hold March 1st commemoration ceremonies and shout "Manse!" Fearing that this influence might reach the interior, the authorities, who are anxious to prevent any incidents, find the mere mention of March 1st tiresome. Unable to bear it, these authorities, even borrowing official power to tear down the shop signs of the weak populace, mobilize guards everywhere each year when this day arrives, tightening the dragnet. Those startled by rumors and cranes (風聲鶴唳) crack down on mountain songs and field laments (山歌野哭), and those who hate red paper (赤紙 - possibly referring to conscription notices or warnings) even keep a wary eye on unusual theater posters. The cavalry, rushing about without regard for where cows or horses go, their thigh muscles worn out; the search parties, dripping sweat from exertion as they meticulously search even the cracks in flea-ridden houses, their fingernails and toenails worn to the point of falling off. It seems that on this day, they barely manage to treat the wounds before their eyes by gouging out the flesh from their hearts. Ah, March, troubled both at home and abroad! How will the world be settled?

自決的 産業運動
Our nation, which seemed to have no destiny no matter how you looked at it from head to toe, seems to have found a tolerable path recently with the industrial movement. Our people, who used to not worry about dinner in the morning, now seem to feel the urgency of the fire at their feet. They quit cigarettes imprinted on their minds, they quit alcohol deeply soaked into their organs—thus shattering their innate rooted habits in a single morning. Alongside this vigorous first step, the rising fervor for using native products under the principle "my livelihood is my own" is, even more than in the material sphere, a most precious development in the realm of thought. Who would not highly praise this! But on the other hand, the police, already viewing this as dangerous, have banned promotional processions. Consequently, there are rumors that the Japanese merchant side, whose economic power is sharp, is preparing to invest large sums and undertake the dyeing and weaving of *sumok* (水木 - likely referring to native trees/plants used for dyes/fibers), one of our native products. Furthermore, the recent sudden rise in the price of raw timber makes one realize the powerless reality of the supplier side. Ah, this movement entering difficult straits! Will it be temporary? Or permanent? On the path ahead for you, gentlemen, upon whom the world's attention is focused, treacherous mountains pile up and winding streams double back! In any case, the rise or fall of the Paedal (배달 - Korean) people will be decided by this alone!!

今年 卒業生의 希望?
This month is the busy period at the end of the academic year for all schools. What is the main hope of the many graduates this time? It is said that one can anticipate the future by examining the past! That seems plausible!! Whether in the homeland or abroad, year after year, among our youth graduating from middle schools or professional schools, nine out of ten, ninety out of a hundred, seem to apply predominantly to political science, law, or literature departments. That's fine, but... in this situation where we struggle along, relying entirely on others' things without a shred of hesitation, wouldn't it be better to learn something entirely new? To make, wear, and use things like others do, wouldn't it be best for each to choose something from the engineering fields? This is the unanimous hope of our people. We implore you, graduating gentlemen, do not forget this earnest request...

속태우는 綿織業者＜89＞

What has become of the cotton textile production organizations, the first step in the native products promotion movement? Within Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) city alone, the established companies are merely the Kyŏngsŏng Chiknyu Stock Company (京城織紐株式會社), Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik Stock Company (京城紡織株式會社), and Tongyang Yŏmjik Stock Company (東洋染織株式會社), amounting to just two or three names. To speak of their general situation: first, the Kyŏngsŏng Chiknyu Stock Company, which seemed to have the foresight to try and reverse the decline of our industrial sector, was established 13 years ago with a capital of 100,000 won. As a result of strenuous efforts while managing a turbulent business, it has merely managed to maintain its current state. Second, regarding the Kyŏngsŏng Pangjik Stock Company, although its history spans only five years since establishment, and it has rarely paid significant dividends, it possesses a large capital of 1 million won. Furthermore, individuals with accumulated practical experience from the Kyŏngsŏng Chiknyu Company have moved back and forth, creating a very amicable cooperative relationship, suggesting it has the most promising future. However, as a lone warrior fighting with its back to the water (背水力戰), it will need robust reinforcements to see its original aspirations through to the end. Third, concerning the Tongyang Yŏmjik Stock Company, established four years ago with the not insignificant capital of 500,000 won, whether due to unfavorable market conditions or the unsound minds of those in charge, it is deeply regrettable that the company's fortunes have not shown resilience to this day. Ah, whose affair is this? Which people are those who gorge themselves to death on overproduction? Whose fathers are the old and weak, stripped bare (纜縷) and sitting on the chopping block?

精神 못차리는 帽子商
The money spent on our heads increases by leaps and bounds daily. Indeed, trying to conform to the general trend, one cannot help but do as others do. Let us first examine the situation of our hat industry. According to a survey from the spring of Taishō 9 (1920), the value of just fedoras worn by Koreans imported from mainland Japan exceeded 3 million won, from China over 1 million won, and from Europe and America over 1 million won, totaling an enormous sum of over 5 million won flowing into others' pockets. Since then, the number has gradually increased. Looking at recent sales, although high-quality items sell less, the sales of medium and low-quality items have sharply increased compared to before (especially last year, the monetary value decreased, but the number of items increased). This clearly proves the rapid rise of the haircutting fever (斷髮熱) among Koreans, and at the same time, is it not as clear as looking at fire (明若觀火) that the day our lifeblood (고혈) runs dry is not far off? Yet, the hat merchants seem mired in makeshift complacency (姑息苟安), showing no sign of any research. Occasionally, one sees signs for hat manufacturers or hat-making companies, but these are merely signboards; at most, they just get others' products and resell them. Ah, do we truly intend to live like others? If so, be it Japanese goods, Western goods, whatever goods, we must make them ourselves and wear them!

朝鮮人 專用의 고무靴
Rubber shoes, absorbing profits of tens of millions of won annually, have completely conquered the footwear world of Koreans with the force of a river breaching its banks (一朝決河). Have the traditional merchants of oil-treated shoes (乾油鞋), wooden clogs (木履), and straw sandals (芒鞋), who suffered utter defeat, simply given up forever, without even considering any improvement measures? Lacking foresight early on, they have no choice but to beg for scraps after giving away the boat. Seeking individual convenience, they disregard the rise and fall of the nation. Currently, the number of rubber shoe stores scattered throughout the entire country reaches several hundred... but is there anyone making them with our hands, properly labeling them as "ours," and putting them out? It seems there are several places that have set up factories with small capital recently, attempting to manage this business, but these are still nothing more than child's play (兒戱的). Among them, the one occupying the most superior position and attracting popularity is the Taeryuk Komu Kongŏp Stock Company (大陸護謨工業株式會社). However, the organization of this company is rather distasteful. Outwardly, its advertisements feature Korean names, but behind the scenes, all real power is wielded by Japanese hands. <90> Ah, rubber shoes unwanted by the rest of the world, do they only taste the money of Koreans? Observe carefully! Know that all rivers flow only eastward to the sea!!

世界化하는 朝鮮文字
Last month, on the 11th, news arrived from Tokyo, Japan, that a new organization called the Taitō Bunka Kyōkai (大東文化協會) was formed to limit Chinese characters and protect Chinese studies. Following this, we received the welcome news that the German dictionary has published the Korean script, receiving enthusiastic acclaim from various Western countries. It is a universal human trait to adopt what is simple for depicting thought and easy for representing spoken language. Westerners, famous for embracing such convenience, who boasted that there was no script in the world as convenient as the Roman alphabet, upon seeing our script published, are already wide-eyed and rushing in, exclaiming, "This is truly mine!" From this, one can see the value of our script... Look... Despite this, isn't it all the more astonishing that those Japanese, perhaps embarrassed because it is the script of their so-called colony, deliberately fuss over others' scripts and studies? If they understood the beginning and end (本末) and still chose the distant over the near (捨近取遠), that would be one thing, but if not, how about they swiftly change (豹變) the clumsy appearance (拙態) of such foolish scholars' hats (巾帽) and embrace the gospel of our script, which holds a plan for ten thousand years (萬年大計)?

天人이 共鳴하는 脫籍運動
Due to the incident last month on the 12th, where Mr. Ch'oe Ch'ang-ho (崔昌浩, 25), a compatriot residing in Lungching (龍井市), Kando (間島), was shot and killed by Chinese authorities without any reason, the 300,000 compatriots residing in that region rose up together. They held a residents' rally, protested to the Chinese government demanding punishment for the perpetrator and compensation for damages, and simultaneously resolved to renounce their nationality en masse to sever ties as subjects of the Japanese Empire. In a chaotic country where a constitution cannot be established, the people cannot find peace and order, and superiors and subordinates alike engage in conflict (上下交征), where there is no distinction between government soldiers and bandits (馬賊)—the foolish wickedness of those northern barbarians (胡奴) is scarcely worth criticizing... but what is most questionable is the attitude of the authorities who drone on about "Japan-Korea non-discrimination" (日鮮無差別) and "co-existence and co-prosperity" (共存共榮) under "impartial benevolence" (一視同仁). How can it be like ice and charcoal? They would raise an international issue over the violation of a single girl. They would inflict torment on villagers even over a single dog. What is the meaning of this? Have they already abandoned the policy of co-existence between the Japanese and Korean peoples and ensuring peace in East Asia, becoming uninvolved bystanders (吳楚不關之事無閑身)? Probably! If not, what is the remedy (善後策) for this? Reveal it! Ah, the souls (魂膽) of twenty million people leap together with the ghost of the deceased!!

細民에게 苦痛주는 米價
Foreigners call Koreans "rice-eating bugs" (米食虫). Translated, this seems like a simple statement of fact: "You who possess nothing else have only rice." There's no need to be angry. It is indeed true... but even the little rice produced by the nation's blood and sweat (血汗), due to what is called price control, or rather, rice price adjustment, has suffered crash after crash. Despite this, there is no strong man who can withstand the urging demands for national taxes. Thus, people had no choice but to sell goods worth 100 *kŭm* for 10 *kŭm* at distress prices (歇買). Furthermore, students who had seized the golden opportunity (千載一遇) of high rice prices to study in Seoul or abroad have, due to the impact of this price collapse, inevitably shed tears and returned home, their numbers unknown, perhaps in the hundreds. After that, the rice price, which had suddenly dropped, has recently begun to soar again. Ah, the common folk (細民) who cannot survive whether it goes down after the mourning clothes are donned (成服 뒤의 藥石 - proverb meaning medicine after death is useless) or whether it goes up! The education of the children of the common folk, impossible whether prices fall or rise!! What is the meaning of this? Should we call it the maladministration of the authorities? Or is it the temporary crime of wicked merchants (奸商輩) exploiting the government's rice purchasing period??<91>

國境은 曲境
Japan, having suffered a major defeat in diplomacy, has turned its attention again, pouring all its efforts into border security. On the other hand, the Korean independence army, skilled in advancing and retreating, collaborating with Red Russia (赤露) with the right hand and connecting with bandits (馬賊) with the left, gradually increases its strength and presses upon the border line.
If they advance to annihilate them in one fell swoop, it raises the major issue of invading Chinese territory. If they retreat to defend against contingencies, the enemy troops will likely exploit vulnerabilities and launch surprise attacks. Trapped in such a dilemma of advancing or retreating (進退兩難), must the national defense strategy inevitably change once more? It is said that under the pretext of Japan-China friendship (日支親善), there are those who employ various desperate measures (百方窮計) to win the favor of Chinese officials and adopt a pincer attack strategy, while simultaneously colluding with bandits to use barbarians to control barbarians (以夷制夷). Setting aside the effectiveness of the plan, in any case, the border (國境) is a troubled border (曲境).

通信敎授의 流行物
Recently, institutions offering correspondence education (通信敎授) as a business are gradually increasing. On the Korean side, the T'ongsin Chunghakhoe (通信中學會) has existed since the year before last, and on the Japanese side, the Tsūshin Kōtō Futsū Gakkai (通信高等普通學會) has also existed since then, as widely advertised. Recently, a Japanese named Hosoi Hajime (細井肇) borrowed the names of many prominent Koreans and is putting considerable effort into advertising under the banner of Tongbang Munhwa Taehak (東方文華大學), publishing lecture compilations (講話窠纂), and sometimes holding regional lecture meetings (地方講話會), placing ads in our newspapers and magazines. Furthermore, Pak Ki-yang (朴箕陽男) and Chŏng Chin-hong (鄭鎭弘), who have been active as old figures in Chinese studies (漢學), are said to be dragging their aged, ailing bodies (稀齡의 病軀) and trying to shed their outdated ways, frantically busy creating some kind of correspondence society (通信學會), their eyes barely having time to blur. On one side, people are busy building stages for their own activities. On the other side, people are struggling to leave some trace behind in the short time they have left. Whether this way or that way, success is the same (成功則一也).

警察界 風紀肅淸?
What do the police do? They are said to be the protectors of the people's lives and property. While one should not excessively blame the one evil among a hundred good deeds or the single mistake of a wise person (百善の一惡, 知者の一失), what was the abuse of office (濫職) like during the era of the Kempeitai police (憲兵警察), which was a specialty of Chosŏn? Then, in Mr. Saitō's (肅藤君 - likely a sarcastic reference to Saitō Makoto) neither-military-nor-civilian-style cultural breeze, their uniforms changed, and they came to wear the skin of frogs. Perhaps because they only changed their clothes, their habits seem not the slightest bit different from their tadpole days or their frog days. What is brighter than the divine eye in a dark room? It is the public eye of the world (天下의 公眼)! It seems even they could not escape this public eye. Recently, the Kyŏnggi Province Police Department announced the establishment of exemplary new systems like a detective division to ensure fairness in criminal investigations and inspectors to correct abuses of power and illegal acts... If it truly becomes like this, setting aside the cleanup of the authorities' own discipline (風紀肅正), the fact that the people might taste an orderly life would indeed be worth celebrating with a cup (一杯). Ah, can even a dog's tail turn yellow after being buried for 10 years?... Hahahaha...!!

작란질이냐? 變態的이냐?

Recently, Westerners visiting the Chosŏn region under the guise of inspecting East Asia are said to be invariably astonished by the ubiquity of tall, wide signboards reading "Such-and-such Religious Headquarters" (敎本部), "Such-and-such Religious General Headquarters" (敎總部), or "Such-and-such Religious Branch" (敎支部) wherever they go. The question, "Should this be called the development of Koreans' religious thought, or the destruction of the sanctity of religion?" troubles their tongues. Indeed. The sight of even three people gathered together handling the head of some nicely named religious leader (敎主道師), or getting obsessed with the price of food and drink as soon Dongaekgeum is earned (백금만 생겨도 밥갑, 술갑에 목을 매는 그 꼴 - meaning easily tempted by small gains/pleasures), would make even wood and stone unable to refrain from spitting curses (唾罵), thinking, "Isn't this the gambling of goblins (독갑이)?" However, Ch'ŏnhyŏnja (天玄子 - likely the author or a persona), sitting in the Hall of Great Patience (百忍堂), though his sharp penpoint may dance wildly, intends not to waste precious time until he can penetrate the mist-shrouded Mount Jiuyi (九疑山)... yet... This sounds like irrelevant chatter, but Ōmotokyō (大本敎), which for a time shook the 50 million Yamato souls (大和魂) as the Tenrikyō (天道敎) of Japan, and which seemed to have no room to shrink due to temporary oppression by political factions (政閥), has recently reared its head again. With activity funds of several million, associating with many prominent figures from government and private sectors, it has changed its name to the Shinrei Kenkyūkai (神靈硏究會 - Society for the Study of Divine Spirits). Organizing various bodies such as a training department, experimental department, research department, overseas dispatch department, publishing department, and library department, it is said to be leaping forward in the shadows (暗中飛躍中) to greatly expand its influence domestically and internationally. Ah, is it mischief (작란질)? Or metamorphosis (變態的)? Let the metamorphosis be as it may... but mischief will surely cause trouble... no matter what...<92>

**Summary**

This editorial from March 1923 covers a wide range of issues facing colonial Korea under Japanese rule, written with a critical and often sarcastic tone. The author, Nonggusaeng, touches upon the suppressed commemoration of the March 1st Movement, the burgeoning native products movement facing Japanese economic pressure, the need for practical (engineering) education over humanities, the struggles of Korean textile and hat industries against imports, the problematic nature of Japanese-controlled "Korean" enterprises like a rubber shoe company, the international recognition of the Korean alphabet contrasted with Japanese attitudes, Korean outrage and a denaturalization movement in Kando following the killing of a Korean by Chinese authorities (and Japanese inaction), the hardship caused by fluctuating rice prices, border tensions involving independence fighters, the rise of correspondence education, and skepticism towards police reforms and the proliferation of new religious groups. The article provides a snapshot of Korean nationalist sentiment, economic anxieties, social commentary, and criticism of Japanese colonial policies during a period of supposed "cultural rule." Historians might find valuable the contemporary perspective on the native products movement's challenges, the specific economic data cited (hat imports, company capital), the account of the Kando incident and the denaturalization movement, and the general mood of disillusionment and critical observation of colonial society.

근래 通信敎授를 업으로 하는 기관이 점차로 늘어간다. 조선인측으로는 通信中學會, 일본인측으로는 通信高等普通學會가 재작년부터 잇서 왓슴은 江湖에 널리 광고된 바인데 요사이 다시 日人 細井肇란 자는 조선인의 명사 다수의 일흠을 빌어가지고 東方文華大學이란 간판 아래서 講話窠纂을 간행하며 時 혹 地方講話會도 개최한다는 광고를 우리들의 신문잡지에 게재하야 어지간이 선전에 힘쓰는 중에 다시 漢學의 老物로 행세에 골몰한 朴箕陽男과 鄭鎭弘씨 등은 稀齡의 病軀를 끄을고 시대에 뒤진 때를 버서 보랴고 무슨 通信學會를 만들기에 眼花가 떠러질 틈이 업다는가. 한편에서는 자기의 활동할 무대를 쌋키에 분주한 모양. 한편에서는 얼마 남지 못한 압길에 무슨 자최를 뵈이고 가보랴고 허덕거리는 모양. 이 모양이나, 뎌 모양이나 成功則一也.

警察界 風紀肅淸?
경찰이란 무엇하는 것인가? 인민의 생명과 재산의 보호자이라 한다. 百善의 一惡과 知者의 一失은 그다지 苛責할 바 아니로되 조선의 명물이든 憲兵警察時代에 그들의 濫職은 엇더하얏던가. 다시 肅藤君의 非文非武的인 얼 文化바람에 그들의 복색은 변하야 깨구리의 탈을 쓰게 되얏다. 變服에 긋친 그들이라 그런지는 모르겟스나 그리하야도 하여간 그네들의 버릇은 올청이 젹이나, 깨구리 쪅이나 족음도 달나 뵈이지 안는다. 암실의 神目 보담도 더 밝은 것은 무엇일가? 천하의 公眼일다! 그네들도 이 천하 公眼 아래서는 엇질 수가 업서 뵈얏든가. 요사이 京畿道 경찰부에서는 범죄수사의 公明을 취하랴고 형사과를 둔다, 濫權非法을 교정해보랴는 監察員 등의 모범적 신제도를 특설한다고...만일 이러케만 되면 당국자체의 風紀肅正은 둘재치고 인민의 질서잇는 생활미를 맛보게 됨에야 그얌말로 一盃를 祝할 만하다. 아아 개꼬리도 10년만 묵고 보면 黃毛될 쩍이 잇든가?...허허허허...!!

작란질이냐? 變態的이냐?

근래 동양 시찰의 이름을 가지고 조선지방을 밟는 구미인들은 가는 곳마다 무슨 敎本部이니, 무슨 敎總部이니, 무슨 敎支部이니 하는 키 크고 상판 넓은 문패가 하도 흔하게 붓터 잇는 것을 보고는 한번 놀내지 안는 자가 업다 한다. 『조선사람의 종교사상이 발달됨이라 할가, 아니 종교의 신성을 파괴함이라고 할가』 하는 말이 그들의 舌頭를 괴롭게 한다. 과연이다. 3인만 모혀 안저도 이름 조흔 敎主道師의 머리를 다루고, 백금만 생겨도 밥갑, 술갑에 목을 매는 그 꼴은 목석이라도 이것이야말로 독갑이의 노름이나 안인가. 唾罵치 안을 수가 업슬 것이다. 그러나 百忍堂中에 안즌 天玄子로서는 날카라운 필봉은 아모리 날뛸 지라도 五里霧에서 써워잇는 九疑山을 鑿通하기까지는 하도 밧분 시간을 허비치 안으랴 한다...아즉...딴말 갓흔 수작이다만은 한참동안 일본의 天道敎라고 오천만의 大和魂을 驚破케 하든 大本敎는 일시 政閥의 압박으로 하야 端縮의 여지가 업든 것이 요사이 다시 머리를 들어 수백만의 활동비를 가지고 朝野名士의 다수를 제휴하야 이름을 고치여 神靈硏究會라 하고 양성부, 실험부, 연구부, 해외파견부, 출판부, 도서부 등의 각 기관를 조직하고 海內海外에 대대적으로 세력을 신장하랴고 暗中飛躍中이라고 한다. 아아 작란질이냐? 변태적이냐? 변태는 그런대로 두어보자마는...작란은 정말 말성일껄...아모리 하야도...＜92＞
