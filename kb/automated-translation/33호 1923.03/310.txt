空想 (Fantasy/Imagination)
						金石松 (Kim Sŏksong)

When I just close my eyes and lie down,
On my head, like a lacquer board,
The hand of fantasy, like white chalk,
Finely draws the divine image of victory,
And to my honest soul,
It beckons, urging us to enjoy it together.

The ice-cold hand of reason,
Which has never even heard the word 'personal feelings,'
Erases the divine image of victory completely,
And my head remains empty as before.

Ah! But, O Fantasy!
I seek to see life within you.

Like a thistle seed, bearing fluff,<17>
Entrusting my body to the wind of fate,
Crossing mountains and waters,
Wandering past the hemp fields,
Upon a stake in the public cemetery,
Listening to the crow's song,
Not even knowing where I am going,
Just going endlessly on the path of fantasy.

Ah! Beautiful Fantasy!
I seek to see life within you.

Your traces like cloud paths,
Your love like the spring breeze,
Your jealousy like a beauty's habit,
Your courage like a suckling baby's steps,
Your wisdom like an old person's calculations,
Your mouth, several times the size of the universe.

Even the so-called culture that humans boast of,
Before you, is but a shadow at noon (正午).<18>
Who is it that speaks of philosophy?
Truth is the object of time and space.
Birth, old age, sickness, death,
From nothingness to existence, from existence to nothingness,
The world where existence and non-existence unite—
Here, my fantasy dances.

Ah—beautiful Fantasy!
I seek to see life within you.
(February 8th)<19>

**Summary**

The poem "空想" (Fantasy/Imagination) by Kim Sŏksong explores the power and allure of imagination in contrast to cold, restrictive reason. The speaker describes fantasy as a creative force that paints images of victory but is easily erased by rationality. Despite its ephemeral nature, the speaker embraces fantasy, seeing it as a source of life and freedom, comparing its journey to a wind-borne seed traveling aimlessly but persistently. The poem elevates fantasy above human constructs like culture and philosophy, positioning it within the fundamental truths of existence, non-existence, and the cycle of life and death. For historians, this poem offers insight into the intellectual and emotional landscape of early colonial Korea. The yearning for escape, the championing of imagination over restrictive reality, and the search for meaning beyond tangible limitations could reflect a subtle resistance or coping mechanism against the constraints of colonial rule, highlighting the inner world where freedom could still be pursued.

空想
						金石松
눈만 감고 누으면
漆板가튼 나의 머리에
白墨 가튼 공상의 손이
勝利의 神像을 곱게 그리고
正直한 나의 靈魂다려
가티 질기자고 손까분다.

私情이란 말은 들은 적도 업는
理智의 차듸찬 손은
勝利의 神像을 싹싹 지우고
나의 머리는 如前히 空일 뿐.

아! 그러나 空想이여!
나는 너의 속에 生命을 보랴 한다.

씸비귀씨와 가티 털을 달고＜17＞
運命의 바람에 몸을 맛겨
山을 넘고 물을 건너
기다가 삼줄을 지나쳐
共同墓地의 말둑 우에
가마귀의 노래를 들으며
가는 곳조차 아지 못하고
가기만 줄곳 가는 空想의 길

아! 아름다운 空想이여!
나는 너의 속에 生命을 보랴 한다

구름길 가튼 너의 자최
봄바람 가튼 너의 사랑
美人의 버릇 가튼 너의 嫉妬
젓먹이 거름 가튼 너의 勇敢
늙은이 셈 속 가튼 너의 智慧
宇宙의 몃곱되는 너의 입

사람이 자랑하는 所謂 文化도
너의 압헤는 한낫(正午)의 그림자＜18＞
누구냐 哲學을 말하는 者가
眞理는 時間과 空間의 對相
生 老 病 死
無에서 有 有에서 無
有無合一의 세계ㅡ
이곳에 나의 空想은 춤을 춘다

아ㅡ아름다운 空想이여
나는 너의 속에 生命을 보랴 한다.
(2월 8일)＜19＞
