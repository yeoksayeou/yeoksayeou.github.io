To the Postal Authorities
The Lack of Common Sense and Sincerity of Clerks
The Blind Inattention of Delivery Men

Due to the incompleteness of the postal service, regardless of whether in the capital or the countryside, the suffering and losses of the general public are not insignificant. The so-called clerks in rural post offices not only do not know what the postal regulations are, but they also cannot distinguish the types of mail they receive daily, treating third-class mail as fourth-class or fourth-class mail as parcels, causing unnecessary losses to the public. Rural delivery men, when mail is destined for a village somewhat far from the post office, do not deliver it faithfully. Instead, they wait until the next market day (市日) and entrust it to a market-goer (場軍) to send, or else they just take it as far as the township office (面所), throw it down, and run off. Consequently, much mail ends up crumpled in the market-goer's pocket, and not a small amount becomes filler for the township office's wastebasket. It's not just the countryside. The insincerity and carelessness of clerks and delivery men in post offices in large cities like Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) and Yongsan are commonplace. To cite one or two examples from incidents that occurred in February:

On February 1st, a registered letter with value declared (containing ten Won - 內入拾圓) addressed to Mr. Min OO at 16 Ch'ŏngsuch'ŏng, within the Yongsan Post Office district, was delivered to the house of OOO at 8 Ch'ŏngsuch'ŏng. As the master of the house was absent, the letter was handed to a woman there, asking her to affix her seal. The woman, being illiterate, did not know it was misdelivered, nor did she know there was money inside. Because the delivery man told her to affix a seal, she stamped a child's seal and placed the letter on a desk, where it was later lost during the children's play. Two days later, through a letter from the person who had sent the money via registered mail, it was learned that the letter was meant for Mr. Min OO at number 16 and contained ten Won. Thus, unavoidably, the household that wrongly received the letter had to compensate the ten Won.

On February 13th, at 3 PM, OO Company submitted fourth-class mail destined for the provinces at the OO-dong post office. The clerk at that post office told the sender to come back the next day to pick up the receipt. The sender agreed and went to get the receipt around noon the next day, the 14th, only to find that the mail received the previous day had still not been processed. Consequently, having no other choice, they took the mail back and submitted it at the Kwanghwamun Post Office. How could such examples be just one or two? There are so many that I cannot mention them all one by one and will stop here, but I hope the postal authorities will repent of their past practices. (Sŏk)<21>

**Summary**

This 1923 article from *Kaebyŏk* magazine is a scathing critique of the Korean postal service under Japanese rule. The author, "Sŏk," details the incompetence, carelessness, and lack of integrity among postal clerks and delivery personnel in both rural and urban areas (specifically mentioning Kyŏngsŏng/Seoul and Yongsan). Examples include misclassifying mail, delaying delivery, entrusting mail to unreliable third parties, and a specific incident where a registered letter containing money was misdelivered and lost due to an illiterate recipient and a careless postman, resulting in the innocent recipient having to pay compensation. The article concludes with a plea for the postal authorities to reform their practices. For historians, the article offers a valuable glimpse into the everyday frustrations and perceived administrative failures experienced by Koreans under colonial rule. It highlights issues of literacy, bureaucratic inefficiency, lack of accountability within a state-controlled service, and the economic impact of such failures on ordinary people, serving as a primary source documenting public discontent with colonial administration infrastructure.

郵便當局者에게
事務員의 沒常識 無誠意
配達夫의 盲目的 不注意
京鄕 물론하고 우편 불완전한 탓으로 일반의 고통과 손해는 약간이 아니다. 싀골 郵便局所에 잇는 사무원 名色들은 우편법규가 무엇인지 아지 못할 뿐 아니라 매일 밧는 우편물의 종류도 분간치 못하야 제3종을 제4종으로도 제4종을 소포로도 취급하야 일반에게 공연한 손해를 끼치며 싀골 배달부들은 郵便局所가 稍遠한 村에 가는 우편물을 信傳치 아니하고 그다음 장날(市日)을 기다려 場軍便에 부탁하여 보내거나 그러치 아니하면 面所에까지만 갓다  내어 던지고 다라난다. 그래서 場軍의 주먼이 속에서 부시깃이 되어버리는 수도 만코 面所의 휴지동 보탬이 되는 수도 적지 아니하다. 시골뿐 아니다. 京城 龍山 가튼 대도시에 잇는 郵便局所의 사무원과 배달부의 무성의, 부주의한 짓이 比比有之하다. 2월 중에 발생된 사건 중 一二例를 擧하면
2월 1일 附龍山局 區內 淸水町 16번지 閔OO의 집으로 가는 가격표기(內入拾圓)書狀一度를 淸水町 8번지 OOO집의 주인 부재중  婦女에게 그 書狀을 전하고 도장을 찍어 달나 하엿다. 그 婦女는 문맹이라 그것이 誤傳인 줄도 모르고 그 속에 돈이 잇는 줄도 모르고서 배달부가 도장을 찍으란다고 애들 도장을 찍어 주고 그 書狀을 책상 우에 두엇다가 애들 작난통에 분실되엇다. 그 후 2일만에 가격표기로 송금한 이의 편지에 의하야 그 書狀이 前記 16번지 閔OO의 집으로 가는 편지요 그 속에 돈 拾圓든 줄을 알앗다. 그래서 할 일 업시 그 書狀 잘못 바든 집에서 그 돈 拾圓을 배상한 일이 잇다.
2월 13일 하오 3시 OO社에서 지방에 보내는 제4종 우편물을 OO洞 우편소에 差出하엿다. 그 우편소의 사무원이 差出人에게 말하기를 영수증은 내일 차자 가라 햇다. 差出人은 그리하라고 翌日 즉 14일 午頃에 영수증을 차지랴 가니까 그때까지도 그 전날 바든 우편물을 처리 아니하고 잇다. 그래서 할 수 업시 그 우편물을 돌우 차저다가 光化門局에 差出한 일이 잇다. 이런 예가 어찌 하나 둘 뿐이랴. 하두 만흔 것을 일일히 다 말할 수 업서서 그만두기는 둔다마는 우편당국자가 前習을 改悛하기를 바란다.(石)＜21＞
