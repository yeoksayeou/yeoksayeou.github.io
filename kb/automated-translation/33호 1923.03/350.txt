The Clearing Mist [Part 2]
						By P'yŏnghŏ

Chapter 1 (Continued)

6.
By the time Hwara reached the ridge of Namsan Park, stopped, and caught her breath for a moment, the red sunset glow was already about to fade into ash-grey. Evening shadows began to creep in sluggishly, spewing dark blue smoke. Wrapped in this smoke, the sky, the earth, and everything in between lost their distinct colors, appearing dim and drowsy. The electric lights dotting the city streets, lit at some unnoticed moment, also lacked brilliance, looking hazy like eyes soaked in sorrow. It was a common sight when the bright light of the setting sun had not yet fully receded, and the dark night had not yet taken its place.
"If he came before me, I wonder where he might be watching me arrive from—"
Hwara, panting shallowly, glanced around, examining here and there. Perhaps because it was suppertime, not even a shadow of a person could be seen, and only the damp, gentle breeze, carrying the scent of grass, coolly brushed Hwara's flushed cheeks and neck.
Hwara was breathing deeply, as if relieved. <39>
"Looks like he hasn't arrived yet. What time could it be now!" she thought, looking at her watch. "It's only just past seven. He said eight o'clock, so there's still half an hour left," she muttered to herself and moved her steps towards the pine grove on this side of the hill. The grove, which seemed dense from afar, turned out to be surprisingly sparse upon reaching it, making her feel exposed. Yet, if she sought a secluded spot deeper inside, it seemed difficult to spot the person arriving. She struggled quite a bit to find a suitable place that was neither here nor there.
"If I stay here, I can see his form. I can see the form of him, searching in vain for the lover who hasn't even come, burning with anxiety..."
Lying down as if sliding onto the softly grass-covered slope, peering through the gaps between the pine trunks lined up like an awning on the abruptly rising hillock, Hwara murmured to herself. A cruel joy flickered in her eyes.
As the darkness deepened, the electric lights began to open their dazzling eyes, twinkling. The houses were blurry, the roads were blurry, and amidst the blurriness of everything, the dotted lights looked like countless stars floating low in the sky. Especially those few scattered far off at the foot of the distant mountain – it was hard even to distinguish whether they were stars or lights.
"Seeing that, he must be stumbling and falling right now, rushing over frantically..." Hwara chuckled, continuously scanning the deserted area where no sign of human presence could be detected. "Like an inanimate doll, he is moving according to my will. Like an ox being led by a person, he is trudging along obediently according to my command." At this thought, Hwara felt a thrilling joy in her chest. But that thrilling sensation was similar to the one felt when uncontrollable sad tears surge forth.
"He must be unable to contain his joy right now, his walking feet must be dancing. Just as he said, his heart must be filled with the breath of spring! Humph! Just let him come! Let's see if there's anyone here to breathe the spring breath into him. I will watch closely, very closely, his crestfallen face... He'll wander this park searching. Let him wander and search the whole wide world. Let him search all night long if he wants. Who's stopping him? Hahaha..." Hwara laughed again, as if convulsing. That laughter <40> echoed eerily like a demonic guffaw through the desolate pine grove on the empty mountain ridge. Hwara shivered as if doused with cold water and flinched. Then, fearfully, she looked up at the sky.
A round moon, risen unnoticed, gently pushed aside wisps of white cloud like dragonfly wings and slid out here and there. The white, clear waves of its light softly soaked this one side. A fresh greenness appeared on the tree leaves. On the white ground lay dark tree shadows horizontally, like black ink paintings on white paper (宣紙). As those waves silently spread yonder, the beautiful city of Seoul, submerged in a blue haze, floated into view like a dream.
Hwara stood mesmerized by this beautiful scenery for a while.

7.
As hazy consciousness returned amidst the haze, she felt it was a pity to view this night scene alone. She thought she should be viewing it paired with a loving sweetheart. She thought she should rest her passion-burning cheek askew on her beloved's shoulder, whispering sweet love, rewarding him...
"But my beloved?..." Hwara's heart felt like it was tearing apart. Thinking that her heart's desire (意中人) desired someone other than herself, she felt the desolation (落寞) of standing alone in a bleak autumn field.
The moonlight shimmered like silver dust on the tree branches.
"He will come. He will come to this place where I am. He and I will face this beautiful scenery together." This consciousness tingled through her entire body and mind. This happiness approaching right before her eyes – yes, the man she loved, regardless of where his heart leaned, being able to appreciate this scene in the same place with him, what else could it be but happiness? – she couldn't bear to pretend not to know it. She couldn't just throw away this happiness, even if it meant sacrificing everything else.
Suddenly, she fantasized about burying her head in his chest and sobbing. ... Yes, but when he comes, I will throw myself into his arms without hesitation. I will throw myself and cry, cry and tell him, tell him through tears how much I <41> have thought of him until today, how much I have secretly tormented myself over him. Then he too will surely feel it. Unless he is wood or stone, being human, he too will surely feel it. Feeling it, he will love me. He will understand that this incident too stemmed from burning passion. And a sacred love will be established. A harmonious family will be formed. ...
Just as her body and mind were melting like spring snow in this stream of fantasy, something brushed past her side with a thud. Hwara jumped in surprise and lifted her head sharply. Two young people were passing by her side.
"Why are you pushing people!" one young man laughed.
"What are you so happy about?" followed the cheerful laughter of another young man.
Hwara startled. People, who had arrived unnoticed, were scattered sparsely here and there.
"He must have already arrived too," the thought flashed like lightning, and her head felt like it was spinning dizzily. In Hwara's whirling gaze, everyone seemed like him, yet everyone also seemed not to be him. No matter how bright the moonlight was, there was no way to clearly identify which person was him. Although she needed to approach closely and look carefully to know for sure, even Hwara, who was usually criticized for being bold, couldn't do such a thing. Like a cat stalking a mouse, she could only scrutinize this person and that person as far as her eyesight allowed. Not a single person was walking alone; all were accompanied by either two or three others.
"He should be coming alone... What's going on? Has he still not arrived?" Hwara decided to watch the path where strollers ascended and descended. She stealthily moved towards an empty area and hid her body behind a pine tree standing on one side of the path.
After a short while, a person came up from below. His clothes, his gait – everything matched him perfectly.
"There he comes!" Hwara was so glad she almost shouted aloud. Still, she forcefully suppressed the impulse, her heart pounding, focusing her vision solely on him. The person approached, one step, two steps. Hwara felt the blood in her entire body throb. He passed in front. It wasn't him! It wasn't the man she was waiting for. <42> It was someone else!
Hwara was utterly disappointed. She had been fooled like this not just once or twice. Her wristwatch already pointed past ten, but the man she waited for hadn't even shown his shadow. The night breeze, rustling the moon-laden leaves, only chilled her lonely heart.

8.
That night, Chŏngae also happened to wake up. The room was bright and the paper sliding door (미다지) looked whitish, so she thought dawn was already breaking, but then realized it was the dim reflection of the moon's shadow.
Chŏngae, who loved the moon, gently pushed aside the edge of her blanket, got up, and opened the sliding door. The night air touched her face coolly, and a faint streak of light silently lay across her blanket.
The moon was currently hidden behind clouds. Clouds floated here and there in clumps; some were white, some were pale grey (軟灰色). Though the centers of these cloud patches were thick, both edges were frayed into threads, seeming to flutter delicately in the wind. And in the gaps between the clouds, the clear sky peeked through bluishly, and one or two tiny stars twinkled as if sleepy.
The moon seemed to be busily weaving its way from this cloud to that cloud, from that cloud to this cloud. Wherever its white body flashed, the clouds momentarily parted, but when the cloud patches were thick, its bright face was sometimes completely obscured. At such times, the whole world seemed to weep as if having lost a lover, and when it escaped the obstacle and revealed its clear white face, the whole world seemed to tremble with joy, as if having found the lost lover. All sounds and movements were quietly asleep, and even the air had no vibrations. It merely turned white and dark following the light and shadow of the moon (月色).
"Oh dear, what's it doing now. The moon is going into that cloud again!"
Whenever the moon went into a cloud, just as the moon was veiled by the cloud, Chŏngae's face also clouded over, and she would cry out anxiously <43> like this. Then, when the moon emerged from the cloud,
"It's out now, it's out now," Chŏngae's facial cloud would also clear, and she would rejoice like a child.
Then, before one could notice, the clouds drifted away like smoke towards the edge of the sky, and in the crystal-clear azure dome (蒼穹), seemingly washed with perfume, only the roundly hung moon scattered its bright light like jade powder.
Chŏngae's heart also suddenly felt brightened. And as if to inhale the moonlight, she breathed in and out with her whole chest. It felt as if moonlight was flowing smoothly into her chest as well. And carried by this bright moonlight, an indescribable happiness seemed to flutter towards her. It even felt as if Chŏngae herself was being carried by the fluttering moonlight, floating gently up into the air.
Suddenly, Chŏngae thought about sacred love.
Sacred love! The very words were fragrant, beautiful, and sweet. Why love was sacred, what constituted sacred love, Chŏngae didn't know. No, it would be more accurate to say she hadn't even thought about it. Isn't sacred love just sacred love? Attaching explanations and picking apart its pros and cons is already misguided talk. It is said that those who practice this simply have joy, laughter, and happiness. Conversely, those who don't know how to do this are always sad, unhappy, and shed tears. It is said that the reason we are in such a sorry state today is also because of the lack of this sacred love. Therefore, people of the new era know they must engage in this sacred love. Of course, a beautiful maiden like herself knows how to engage in this sacred love. However, she simply lacked an object for her love. Who would be the object of her love?
In the hazy distance, the object of love seemed to appear distinctly yet dimly. It was not a distinct male figure. Nor was it female. Something worldly beautiful and worldly pure, with wing-like garments fluttering, seemed to be gracefully walking towards her.
As if embracing this phantasm drawn from her reverie, Chŏngae unconsciously hugged her own chest with both arms. Indeed, something seemed to gently nestle within her embrace, and her mind grew dizzy. <44>
Then, realizing her hands were idly stroking her own chest, a bittersweet sorrow (悲哀), like a spring haze, seemed to enchantingly permeate her heart. Yet, at the same time, she felt a bright, dawning ecstasy.
Eventually, Chŏngae felt her body grow weary, and feeling shivery chills, she closed the sliding door and lay down, pulling the blanket over herself. At this moment, the thought of that letter, which she had completely forgotten as if washed clean, arose.
"Who on earth wrote that letter?" This question made her quite curious. For some reason, thinking about that letter tonight felt very embarrassing. Her face flushed slightly. Like the color of the beautiful love she had been contemplating...
Lying under the blanket, she pondered something for a while longer, then finally fell asleep like a log.

9.
"Child, what kind of sleep is this! Are you sick somewhere?" Chŏngae barely woke up to her mother shaking her. By then, the rays of the red sun were already shooting through the sliding door.
Chŏngae rubbed her sleepy eyes and asked,
"What time is it now?"
Realizing her daughter wasn't sick but had overslept from exhaustion, the mother smiled affectionately,
"What do you mean, what time? It's already ten o'clock."
Chŏngae's eyes widened.
"Already ten o'clock!" She abruptly sat up, pulling her body, which threatened to be dragged back into the land of sleep at any moment, and quickly looked at the table clock placed on the desk. It wasn't ten, but it was past seven-thirty. Chŏngae hastily got dressed and came out to the edge of the wooden floor (maru), waiting for the housemaid to bring washing water. A letter lay on the edge of the maru. Chŏngae picked it up nonchalantly.
Just then, the housemaid, short and stout like a slice of pufferfish (魴魚), brought the washbasin and placed it down, <45>
"Um, this morning when I went to open the main gate, this letter was there," she explained the letter's origin.
Chŏngae's heart fluttered strangely. The letter was exactly like that strange letter Hwara had snatched away. The envelope, the handwriting – it was undoubtedly that letter. Only one part was different.
Chŏngae's flustered expression must have shown on her face. The housemaid also peered curiously at the letter, asking with a puzzled look,
"Why is there no stamp on this letter? Who is it from?"
Chŏngae's face flushed hotly. "Huh? It's for me," she said, and barely washing her face, she abruptly went back into her room.
After pondering something for a moment, Chŏngae, as if resolved, opened the top of the letter. Without even looking at the content, she quickly unfolded it, wanting to know who wrote it. At the very end of the letter, it was written thus:
As the dawn wind flickers the candlelight, Kim Ch'angsŏp respectfully writes.
Kim Ch'angsŏp? Chŏngae, perhaps unable to recall immediately, frowned slightly for a moment, then, after a short while, nodded her head a couple of times.
"Oh! Right! Yŏngsuk's older cousin," she muttered under her breath. And she quickly recalled how she and Ch'angsŏp came to know each other—
It was already about two months ago. One day, when Hwara and Chŏngae went to visit Yŏngsuk's house, they saw a young man coming out of Yŏngsuk's room. He was a slender young man with a very pale face. At that time, Hwara asked Yŏngsuk who he was.
"He's my cousin from the countryside. He was studying abroad in Tokyo (東京留學) but had to return due to family circumstances. He's studying English here now. When I say studying English, don't think he's just learning from some reader like us. He only reads very difficult literature books. If there's something he doesn't know, he just goes and asks Westerners. And he's probably proficient in classical Chinese too. It's truly rare to find a young person so accomplished in both new and old learning. That's not all. He's so affectionate and amiable— <46> —really, I've never seen anyone like him."
As if saying, 'You have such a wonderful cousin,' Yŏngsuk, filled with pride and joy, tirelessly praised her cousin without stopping for breath.
After Yŏngsuk finished speaking, Hwara laughed and said something like this:
"Ah, that's why your English grade was good last semester."
"Hey, my cousin hadn't even come here last semester."
"Even if you didn't learn English separately, your grade was good because you're talented. I wish I had talent like you," Hwara twisted her words.
"Honestly, you always twist people's words at the slightest thing," Yŏngsuk's face turned sullen.
"Why get angry over a joke? Let's drop that. I wish I could learn some English from your cousin too," Hwara forced a smile and turned to Chŏngae, "Let's learn together, huh? And let's beat Yŏngsuk once."
"Why, is Chŏngae's grade worse than mine!"
"If it gets better, that's even better..." Hwara clapped her hands and laughed as if something delighted her.
After that, whenever the two visited Yŏngsuk's house, they met the young man once or twice. The young man always avoided the two girls whenever he saw them. One day, Hwara laughingly said to Yŏngsuk,
"Why should your cousin be so formal (內外) with us... Ask him to come in here for a bit. Let's get friendly and learn some English."
"Really? Shall I?" Yŏngsuk pressed eagerly, seemingly pleased.
"Why would you do that?" Chŏngae blushed deeply for no reason.
"Hey, girls of the New Era need to socialize well with men. What's the use of a schoolgirl being so shy?" Hwara refuted Chŏngae, looking quite indignant.
Yŏngsuk, wanting to boast about both her friend and her cousin, <47>
"Then shall I ask him to come? Shall I?"
"Yes, ask him to come," Hwara urged.
"Oppa!" Yŏngsuk finally called out, looked at her friends, and smiled. Chŏngae lowered her head. Hwara pretended to be perfectly composed, putting on airs.
"Yes. What is it!"
"Please come here for a moment."
There was no reply to those words, but the sound of a sliding door opening in the courtyard was heard with a creak. His footsteps reached the wooden floor, approached the door of the room where the three girls were, and
"Did you call me?" his voice, as he asked, had a slight tremor. The two girls held their breath. Yŏngsuk quickly opened the door and went out. Whispering sounds and laughter were heard, then a smiling Yŏngsuk opened the door and said, "Here, please come in."
The two girls stood up simultaneously. Hwara deliberately put on a charming smile, while Chŏngae's face turned red like a pumpkin.
Ch'angsŏp also entered smiling, his face slightly flushed.
"This child is Yi Chŏngae, this child is Pak Hwara, they are both my friends."
Yŏngsuk, suppressing her laughter, first introduced her friends.
"This is my cousin..." Barely managing to say this much, she burst out laughing and collapsed.
After that, often dragged along by Hwara, Chŏngae visited Yŏngsuk's place. When she went, she often met Ch'angsŏp, and when she met Ch'angsŏp, sometimes they just exchanged pleasant greetings, and sometimes they sat together and chatted. As they gradually became more familiar, the two girls would ask Ch'angsŏp things they didn't know, and Ch'angsŏp would tell the two girls summaries (梗槪) of Western novels.

10. <48>

Chŏngae was thinking about these things, staring intently at the end of the letter as if to pierce it. Meanwhile, her eyes vaguely focused on a spot on the wall. In her eyes, various images from past days flickered.
After a while, Chŏngae began to fold the letter meticulously, section by section, starting from the end. Just as the very first section was about to disappear under the folds of the thick paper, her folding hand stopped involuntarily and trembled slightly. Unconsciously, the characters written there sharply pierced her eyes.

"Miss Chŏngae!
First, I have no words to apologize. I can only beg, only plead for Miss Chŏngae's forgiveness. How much you must have suffered waiting in vain! Thinking of that, this heart feels like it's being cut out with a knife."
Chŏngae couldn't help but be startled again. She had never, not even in her wildest dreams or thoughts, waited for him, so what was this again? She even suspected the letter might not be for her but meant for someone else. Yet, her hand mechanically unfolded the letter, and as the letter unfolded, her eyes followed the lines.

"Miss Chŏngae!
I was not there when that letter arrived. As misfortune loves company (好事多魔), I happened to have some business. Only late at night did I return, and as I was about to unfold the chilly blankets in my desolate, empty room, I saw the letter placed on my desk and hastily opened it. At first, I couldn't understand what it meant. I read it once, twice... only after reading it several times did I barely grasp the meaning. And I doubted if it was a dream. I couldn't believe it was reality. That Miss Chŏngae would grant me her love and even promise to meet was truly a dream. That's right. How many times must I have met Miss Chŏngae's beautiful phantom in my dreams? How many times must I have received Miss Chŏngae's dream-like love?! I am someone who thought I would be forever lonely and forever sad if not for dreams. That such joy and such happiness could be experienced by me, not in a dream but in reality, I truly, truly couldn't believe. Overcome with joy, two streams of tears silently soiled the beautiful letter. <49>

"Miss Chŏngae!
With hurried steps, I stumbled my way up to Namsan Park. But by then, it was already past two in the morning. Not even a shadow of a person could be seen in the park, only the shadows of pine trees swaying in the night breeze looked chaotic in the bright moonlight.

"Miss Chŏngae!
Since the night was already so deep, it's impossible that Miss Chŏngae, with her heart of gold, would still be waiting for me, right? However, I, feverish with love, wandered and wandered the entire park like a sleepwalker (몽유병자), wondering 'Is she here? Is she there?'. My mind being confused, how many times did I mistake the rustling of grass blades brushed by the wind for the sound of Miss Chŏngae's clothes dragging! How many times did I mistake the tree trunks whitened by the moonlight for Miss Chŏngae's anxious standing figure (立像)!

"Miss Chŏngae!
Only when the distant horizon began to brighten faintly did I helplessly cool my boiling heart in the wind and return to my lodgings (숙소) with weary steps. Before even settling down properly, I wrote this letter.

"Miss Chŏngae!
I intend to say no more about other matters. Because spilled water cannot be gathered up again, and past events cannot be reversed. Tonight at eight o'clock, I will go to Namsan Park again. And I will wait for Miss Chŏngae to come.
Finally, rather than troubling a mail carrier (遞傳夫), I will deliver this letter to your house (宅) myself. Because this anxious (燥燥한) heart simply cannot entrust it to the slow (遲遲한) steps of a mail carrier. As I write in haste (怱怱히), there are likely many awkward parts in the message; I deeply beg your forgiveness.

As the dawn wind flickers the candlelight,         Kim Ch'angsŏp respectfully writes.
—(End of Chapter 1)—<50>

**Summary**

This installment follows Hwara as she goes to Namsan Park, intending to stand up a suitor (implied to be Kim Ch'angsŏp) she seemingly lured there with a fake invitation (likely forged in Chŏngae's name). Initially relishing the planned cruelty, the romantic moonlight softens her, and she wishes he were truly her beloved. She waits anxiously, but he never arrives, leaving her disappointed. Later, Chŏngae awakens, muses on "sacred love," and recalls an anonymous love letter Hwara had taken. She then finds an identical letter delivered directly to her house. It's from Kim Ch'angsŏp, apologizing profusely for missing their supposed rendezvous, explaining he received *her* letter late, rushed to the park after 2 AM, searched desperately, and asks to meet again that night. Chŏngae is bewildered, realizing Ch'angsŏp believes *she* invited him. The chapter ends with Ch'angsŏp's earnest plea.

From a historical perspective, the story vividly portrays aspects of modernizing Korean society under colonial rule. The characters, particularly Hwara and Chŏngae, grapple with emerging ideas of romantic love ("sacred love") versus traditional norms, reflecting the "New Woman" archetype debated at the time. Hwara's boldness and manipulation, Chŏngae's introspective nature and naivete, and Ch'angsŏp's passionate yet formal courtship via letter offer insights into changing gender dynamics and courtship rituals. The use of Namsan Park, a modern leisure space in Seoul, as a setting, and the mention of studying abroad (Tokyo) and learning English further ground the narrative in the social context of 1920s Korea.

지새는 안개[第二回]
						憑虛
第一章(續)

6.
花羅가 南山公園 마루턱에 올라 발을 멈추고 잠간 숨을 돌릴 적에는 어느 듯 붉은 놀로 재빗으로 살아지랴할 임물이엇다. 저녁ㅅ그림자는 검풀은 연기를 뿜으면서 어실렁 어실렁 긔어들기 시쟉하얏다. 이 煙氣에 싸히여 한울이나 땅이나 그 가운데 잇는 모든 것이나 낫나치 제 빗갈을 일허버리고 흐리멍덩하게 조으는 듯 하얏다. 어느 결엔지 시가를 점친 전등ㅅ불도 光輝가 빗나지 안하 슯흠에 저진 눈동자 모양으로 어섬푸레하게 보이엇다. 지는 해의 밝은 빗이 아직 다 거치지 아니하고 어두운 밤이 채 그 자리를 차지 못하얏슬 때 흔히 잇는 광경이엇다.
『그이가 나 먼저 왓스면 어대서 나 오는 것을 볼는지 몰라ㅡ』
花羅는 찬숨을 식은거리며 두리번 두리번 이리저리를 삷혀 보앗다. 저녁밥 때가 된 까닭인가, 사람이란 그림자도 볼 수 업고 축축이 저즌 실바람이 풀ㅅ냄새를 날리며 花羅의 훅근거리는 뺨과 목을 산산하게 할틀 뿐이엇다.
花羅는 살 듯이 깁흔 호흡을 하고 잇섯다.＜39＞
『아직 아니 온 모양이야. 시방 몃 점이나 되엇슬가!』 하고 시계를 보며 『일곱 점 밧게 아니 되엇네. 여덜 시라 하얏스니 인제도 半時나 남아구면』 속으로 속살거리고 이 편 언덕 소나무 숩으로 발길을 옴기엿다. 멀리론 조밀한 듯 십든 숩이 다 달아보면 뜻 밧게 나무가 엉성하야 자기가 환하게 들어날 것도 갓고 그러타고 안윽한 자리를 차자 들어가자니 올라오는 사람을 알아보기가 어려울 듯도 하야, 이도 저도 아닌, 適好한 자리를 찻기까지에 애를 먹지 안핫다.
『이리고 잇스면 그의 꼴을 볼 수 잇다. 오지도 안흔 애인을 헛되이 차지면서 애간장을 태우는 그의 꼴을 볼 수 잇다...』
포근포근이 잔듸 깔린 경사면에 미끄러진 듯이 몸을 누이고, 불숙 솟은 언뎍 위 차양 치듯 늘어선 소나무 둥치 틈으로 눈을 내여 노흐며, 花羅는 스스로 중얼거렷다. 그 눈에는 잔인한 깃븜이 번적이고 잇섯다.
어두운 빗이 점점 진해갈스록 전등은 반작반작 부신 눈을 뜨기 시작하얏다. 집도 흐렷고, 길도 흐렷고 모든 것이 흐려진 가운데 점점이 빗나는 전등은 수만흔 별들이 나즌 공중에 떠 잇는 것 가타얏다. 더구나 저 먼 산 미테 두셋 뜨염뜨염 외딸리 떠잇는 것은 별인지 불인지 分揀조차 하기 어려웟다.
『그이가 그것을 보고 시방 업허지며 잡바지며 안달박달 달아오렷다...』 花羅는 호젓하게 사람의 긔척업는 그곳 일판을 연해연방 보삷히면서 빙글 웃엇다. 『인형이나 무엇가티 그이는 내 뜻대로 움즉이고 잇다. 사람에게 끄을리는 소처럼 내 명령대로 꾸벅꾸벅 걸어오는 중이다.』 이런 짐작을 하매 花羅는 가슴이 찌르를 하도록 깃버섯다. 그러나 그 찌르를한 맛은 안슯흔 눈물이 북밧칠 때의 그것과 비슷한 것이엇다.
『저는 시방 깃븜을 것잡다 못하야 것는 발이 춤을 추렷다. 저 말맛다나 가슴에 봄입김이 들어찻슬걸! 흥! 그래도 오기만 와보아! 봄입김을 불어너흘 사람이 잇나업나. 그 失心落膽하는 꼴악선이를 내가 알들이 살들이 구경을 할걸...이 공원을 헤매며 차지렷다. 앗다 삼산구비를 헤매며 차지라지. 밤새도록이라도 차자보라지. 누가 말리나. 하하하...』 花羅는 자질치는 듯이 또 한번 웃엇다. 그 웃음이＜40＞ 惡 哄笑 모양으로 휑덩그러케 비인 산머리 솔밧헤 무싀무싀하게 울리엿다. 花羅는 찬물을 끼언치인 듯이 읏슥하며 몸을 떨엇다. 그리고 두려운 듯이 한울을 처다보앗다.
쇼슨 때 모르는 둥근 달이 츨기 蜻蜒의 날애가튼 흰 구름자락을 고이고이 뒤로 밀치며 이리저리로 미끌어 나온다. 그 광선의 희고 맑은 물결이 슬적 이 편 一幅을 적시엿다. 나무입헤는 푸른 빗이 새로웟다. 허연 땅바닥 우에는 宣紙에 黑工畵를 친 듯이 검은나무 그림자가 가루 누엇다. 그 물결이 스르를 밀리어 저리로 퍼지며 푸른 내에 잠긴 아름다운 서울시가를 꿈속가티 떠보이게 하얏다.
이 아름다운 경치에 花羅는 한동안 망연자실하고 잇섯다.

7.
망연한 가운데 망연한 의식이 돌아오며 이 밤 이 景을 호올로 보기가 앗갑다 하얏다. 정다운 애인과 짝지어 볼 것이라 하얏다. 고은 님의 억개에 정열에 타는 뺨을 엇비슥이 누이고 꿀가튼 사랑을 속살거리며 상 줄 것이라 하얏다...
『그런데 나의 애인은?...』 花羅의 가슴은 뮈여지는 듯 하얏다. 자기의 意中人이 저 아닌 남을 의중에 둔 것을 생각하매 그는 쓸쓸한 가을들에 외로이 서 잇는 듯한 落寞을 늣기엇다.
달빗은 은가루 가티 나무가지에 번적이고 잇섯다.
『그가 오기는 올 것이다. 나 잇는 이곳에 오기는 올 것이다. 그와 나와 한갈가티 아름다운 이 경치를 대하기는 대할 것이다』 이런 의식이 그의 온 몸 온 마음을 자릿자릿하게 지배하고 잇섯다. 제 목전에 닥처오는 이 행복- 그러타, 자기가 사랑하는 이성, 그이야 누구에게로 마음이 쏠렷든, 그 이성과 한 자리에서 이 景을 감상할 수 잇슴은 행복이 아니고 무엇이랴-을 참아 모르는 체 할 수가 업섯다. 모든 것을 희생할 지엉정 이 행복만은 그대로 내여 바릴 수가 업섯다.
문득 그의 가슴에 머리를 파뭇고 훌적어리는 제 자신을 幻想하얏다. ... 올타 그러나 그가 오거든 닷자곳자로 그의 가슴에 이 몸을 던지리라. 던지고는 울리라, 울면서 말하리라, 오늘날까지 내가 얼마나＜41＞ 그를 생각한 것을 남모르게 얼마나 이 속을 태운 것을 눈물석거 하소연하리라. 그러면 그도 응당 늣기리라. 나무가 아니고 돌이 아니고 사람인 다음에야 그도 응당 늣기리라. 늣기여 사랑하리라. 이번 이 일도 뜨거운 정열에서 울어나온 줄 알아주리라. 그리고 신성한 연애가 성립되리라. 和樂한 가정을 이루리라. ...
이런 몽상의 흐름에 봄눈스듯 몸도 녹고 마음도 살아질 지음이엇다. 무엇인지 자기엽흘 툭하며 시처나간다. 花羅는 깜작 놀라 고개를 번적 들엇다. 그의 엽흐로 젊은 사람 둘이 지나간다.
『사람을 웨 떠다밀어!』 청년 하나이 웃는다.
『무얼 조하서』 하고 딴 청년 하나의 쾌활한 웃음소리가 뒤를 니엇다.
花羅는 몸을 소소라치엇다. 올라온 때 모르는 사람들이 여긔저긔 드문드문하얏다.
『발서 그이도 왓겟구나 하는 생각이 번개 가티 번적이며 머리가 횅하고 내여둘리는 듯 하얏다. 핑핑 돌리는 花羅의 시선에는 모두 그이 갓기도 하고 모두 그이 아닌 듯도 하얏다. 어느 사람이 그이인지는 아모리 달빗이 밝다 한들 분명이 알아낼 도리가 업섯다. 압압히 차자가서 자세히 보아야만 분명이 알겟거든 비록 평일에 당돌하다는 비평을 듯는 花羅로도 그럴 수는 업는 일이다. 고양이가 쥐를 엿보듯 시력이 자라는 대로 이 사람 저 사람을 물색할 뿐이엇다. 혼자 다니는 사람은 하나도 업고 모두 둘 아니면 셋을 동반하고 잇섯다.
『그이는 혼자 올 터인데...웬일인가? 아직도 아니 왓나』 하고 花羅는 散步客이 오르나리는 길목을 직히기로 하얏다. 비실비실 사람 업는 대로 돌아서 길목의 한편에 서 잇는 소나무 뒤에 몸을 감추엇다.
얼마 아니 되어 미테서 사람 하나이 올라온다. 옷 입은 것이든지 거름 것는 것이든지 할 일 업는 그이엿다.
『저긔 오는구먼!』 花羅는 하도 반가워 버럭 소리를 질을번 하얏다. 그래도 억지로 그 충동을 참고 가슴을 울렁거리며 시력을 그리로 모으고만 잇섯다. 그 사람은 한 거름 두 거름 다가들엇다. 花羅는 온몸의 피가 파득파득 뛰노는 듯 하얏다. 압흘 지나간다. 아니엿다! 그이가 아니엿다. 花羅의 기다리는 그이가＜42＞ 아니엿다. 딴사람이엿다.!
花羅는 더할 수 업시 실망하얏다. 이 모양으로 그는 한두 번 속지 안핫다. 팔둑시계는 발서 열 점을 가르치건마는 기다리는 그이는 그림자도 나타나지 안핫다. 달 실은 나무닙을 휘날리는 밤바람이 외로운 안가슴을 싸늘하게 헤칠 뿐이엇다.

8.
그날 밤에 晶愛도 어째 잠이 깨이엿다. 방안이 밝고 미다지가 허여스럼하길래 동녁이 발서 트는 줄 녀겻더니 알고본 즉 그것은 달 그림자가 어섬푸레하게 깃드린 것이엇다.
달을 조하하는 晶愛는 이불자락을 고이 밀치고 일어나아, 미다지를 열엇다. 밤공긔가 선득하게 얼굴에 끼치며, 한줄기 희미한 광선이 소리업시 이불 위에 가루 누엇다.
달은 시방 구름 속에 잠겨 잇다. 구름은 이곳 저곳에 멍울멍울 떠잇는데 어떤 것은 희며 어떤 것은 軟灰色이다. 그 구름ㅅ장들의 한복판은 두터워도 량 가장자리는 실실이 풀리어 바람에 하늘하늘 나붓기는 듯 하얏다. 그리고 구름과 구름의 벌어진 틈에 맑은 하늘이 파릇파릇이 엿보이며 한 개 두 개 족으마한 별들이 졸음 오는 듯이 깜박거리고 잇섯다.
달은 이 구름에서 저 구름으로 저 구름에서 이 구름으로 바뿐 듯이 헤우적어리고 잇섯다. 그 흰 몸이 번듸치는 곳마다 구름이 스르를 헤여지기는 하건마는 그래도 구름ㅅ장이 두터울 때는 밝은 얼굴이 온전히 감으러지는 수도 업지 안핫다. 이럴 적엔 왼 천지가 애인을 일흔 듯이 눈물에 어리는 것 갓고 그 장애물을 버서나아 흰 얼굴을 두렷이 들어내면 왼 천지는 일헛든 애인을 차진 듯이 깃븜에 떠는 것 가타얏다. 모든 음향과 모든 동작이 고요하게 잠들고 공기조차 아모련 파동이 업섯다. 다만 月色의 명암을 딸하 희엇다 검엇다 할 뿐이엇다.
『에그 저를 어째. 달이 또 저 구름 속으로 들어가네!』
달이 구릅 속으로 들어갈 적마다, 달이 구름에 가리듯키 晶愛의 얼굴에도 구름이 끼이며 안타가운 소리로＜43＞ 이러케 불으지지다가, 달이 그 구름에서 헤여나오면
『인제 나왓다, 인제 나왓다. 하고, 晶愛의 얼골ㅅ구름도 거치며 어린애 모양으로 깃버하얏다.
그리다 사람 볼 겨를도 업시 구름은 연기가티 저편 한울 가흐로 몰려버리고 향수로 씨서 노흔 듯한 새맑은 蒼穹에 오즉 둥그러케 걸린 달이 옥가루 가튼 밝은 빗을 헛날리고 잇섯다.
晶愛의 가슴도 갑작이 환하야 지는 듯 하얏다. 그리고 달빗을 들어마실 듯이 가슴껏 숨을 드리쉬고 내쉬엿다. 가슴에도 달빗이 수루룩 흘러들어 가는 듯하얏다. 그리고 이 밝은 달빗을 멍에하고 무에라고 형용하기 어러운 행복이 술렁술렁 날아오는 듯하얏다. 晶愛 자신이 그 술렁거리는 달빗에 실리어 공중으로 둥실둥실 떠올라 가는 듯도 하얏다.

신성한 연애! 글자부터 향긔롭고 아름답고 달큼한 것이엇다. 어째서 연애가 신성한지 무엇으로 신성한 연애인지 晶愛는 모른다. 아니, 생각도 안햇다는 것이 맛당할 듯 하다. 신성한 연애는 신성한 연애로 그 뿐이 아닌가. 거긔 설명을 부치고 長短을 캐는 것부터 벌서 틀린 수작이다. 다만 이것을 하는 사람에게는 깃븜이 잇고 웃음이 잇고 행복이 잇슬 뿐이라 한다. 그 반대로 이것을 할 줄 모르는 사람은 언제든지 슯허하고 불행하고 눈물을 흘리는 것이라 한다. 우리가 오늘날 말못되게 된 것도 이 신성한 연애가 업는 까닭이라 한다. 그럼으로 신시대의 사람들은 이 신성한 연애를 하여야 될 줄 안다. 물론 자기와 가튼 꼿다운 처녀는 이 신성한 연애를 할 줄 안다. 그런데 그에게는 다만 연애의 대상이 업슬 뿐이엇다. 연애의 대상은 누가 될고?
어섬푸레한 저 멀리 연애의 대상이 鮮然하면서도 흐리멍덩하게 나타나는 듯 하얏다. 그것은 두렷한 남성도 아니엿다. 그러타고 여성도 아니엿다. 세상에도 아름답고 세상에도 깨끗한 그 무엇이 나뷔날애 가튼 옷자락을 한을거리며 하느적 하느적 자기에게로 걸어오는 듯 하얏다.

이윽고 晶愛는 몸이 노곤해지고 읏슥읏슥 치운 症이 들어서 미다지를 닷고는 이불을 쓰고 누엇다. 이때에 씨슨 듯이 니저버렷든 그 편지ㅅ생각이 일어낫섯다.

『晶愛씨!
먼저 무에라고 사죄할 말슴이 업습니다. 오즉 晶愛씨의 용서를 바랄 뿐임니다, 빌 뿐임니다. 헛되이 기다리시노라고 얼마나 애를 씨섯겟슴니까! 그것을 생각하매 이 가슴은 칼로 에여내는 것 갓슴니다.
