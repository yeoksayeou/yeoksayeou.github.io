Korea and the World in February, From January 23rd to February 20th

▲ The issue of confirming entrance qualifications, which was temporarily tumultuous among middle school students, was finally resolved as demanded (January 23rd).

◎ At the Ruhr coal mine occupied by French troops, all miners staged a general strike in opposition to the occupation (Same day).

◎ As a final measure against the anti-French movement in the Ruhr region, the French government plans to expel German officials from the left bank of the Rhine and the Ruhr region, making the Rhine region a completely independent zone (Same day).

△ Under the pretext of the Chinese government's inability to maintain public order, the US Minister proposed to the Diplomatic Corps that the Chinese Eastern Railway be placed under international management (Same day).

△ Students of Kyŏngsin School submitted a petition to the school authorities, citing the reason that if the Bible is taught, the school cannot be designated as a Higher Ordinary School, and not being a Higher Ordinary School results in various losses (Same day).

▲ In a demonstration opposing the arrival of French and Belgian troops, all railway employees in the Essen region went on a general strike (24th).

▲ The British Attorney General stated that the recent French occupation of the Ruhr already constitutes a violation of the Treaty of Versailles, and various British newspapers criticized it as the most violent and destructive means among the peaceful measures of civilized nations (Same day).

△ The US Congress passed a constitution denying citizenship to Japanese born in the Americas, hindering Japanese advancement (Same day).

△ The people of the Rhine region are fiercely demonstrating against the establishment of a buffer republic.

▲ The US government investigation committee recommended taking legislative measures to prevent foreigners, especially Japanese, from dominating the politics, industry, and commerce of Hawaii (Same day).

△ The Kohŭng County Public Ordinary School's male and female divisions went on a collective school strike against the barbaric behavior of a Japanese teacher (Same day).

△ The Pongsan-dong Public Ordinary School in Taegu Prefecture completely burned down due to a fire (Same day).

▲ French military officers prohibited the transport of all coal from the Ruhr region into the German interior (Same day).

◎ Mr. Sun Wen issued a declaration of peaceful unification to the President, provincial military governors, provincial governors, and various newspaper companies (Same day).

△ For the purpose of disabling transportation in the Ruhr region, German railway employees carried out thorough destruction (Same day).

▲ Kim Kwan-o, living in Tae-myŏn, Pyŏkdong County, was shot dead instantly along with his son by an independence group on suspicion of being an informant (Same day).

△ There is a rumor that Mr. An Ch'ang-ho, head of the National Representatives' Meeting held in Shanghai, has suddenly resigned (Same day).

△ Mr. Yi Tong-sol, in contact with Mr. Sun Wen, is reportedly recruiting and training troops in a certain region with plans to advance swiftly in one move (Same day).

▲ The people of Ch'ŏn-gok and Susan-myŏn in Chungwha County, who relied on the forest land situated between the two townships for their livelihood, are engaged in an ardent movement demanding a lifeline loan as the land has been incorporated into state-owned forest (Date not specified, likely between 24th-27th).

△ Cadres of the independence movement groups North Road Military Administration Office and Koryŏ Revolutionary Army, each leading troops, departed from Nŭngdu-san with plans to enter the interior through the Musan region (Date not specified).

△ Mr. "Yi Ni-kkol-bai" (a Korean) was appointed Far East Bureau Chief and Head of Korean Communization Propaganda by the Labor-Peasant Government (Date not specified).

◎ Murder turmoil in Changgyo-dong: A Japanese man named Fukui, residing at 15 Changgyo-dong, stabbed his wife Nomura and her father with a dagger, claiming his wife was unfaithful. The father-in-law died instantly, and the wife is seriously injured (28th).

△ Students from the Three Eastern Provinces residing in Peking organized a diplomatic support association, demanding the recovery of Dairen and Port Arthur in the spirit of the Qingdao recovery (Same day).

◎ Mr. Joffe departed for Japan aboard the Empress of Asia (Same day).

◎ The Colonial Production Mutual Aid Society held a promotional procession with the theme of engaging in actual labor (March 9th - Note: Date seems out of sequence, possibly a typo or relates to publication prep).

O All students of Taegu Private Kyesŏng School submitted collective withdrawal applications due to the Bible subject issue (Same day - likely Feb 29th or March 1st based on surrounding dates, or another out-of-sequence entry).

◎ The British Minister to China notified the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the intention to continue the Weihaiwei handover negotiation meetings (Same day).

◎ All students of Kyŏngsin School finally went on a collective school strike (30th).

◎ At the Indian Legislative Assembly session, the Governor of Lucknow announced the release of all political prisoners (Same day).

△ Major fire in P'yŏngyang: Around 5 AM, a large fire broke out in Taehwa-dong, P'yŏngyang, completely destroying 3 houses and partially burning 2 (31st).

△ All students of Hwangju Public Agricultural Supplementary School went on a collective school strike, despising the Japanese teacher<99> (31st).

▲ Japanese socialist Ōsugi Sakae appeared in Külön (Urga), Mongolia, together with the blind Russian poet Mr. Eroshenko (Same day).

△ Train collision: The southbound train from Sinŭiju and the northbound train from Pongch'ŏn (Mukden) collided within the Sinanju station premises, resulting in serious injuries to one office worker and one passenger (Same day).

△ 8,000 won for one eye: Pak T'ae-hŭng (7 years old) of Insadong in the city filed a lawsuit demanding 8,000 won in damages against the father of his companion Chŏng Ki-hyŏp, alleging Chŏng injured his eye while they were playing together (2nd - Feb).

△ National self-determination irrelevant: At the Japanese Diet session, when asked about the policy regarding the flourishing national self-determination movement and communization movement in Korea, the Governor-General stated that the vast majority of Koreans are indifferent, and it is merely a movement by some students, thus not worth worrying about (2nd).

◎ In opposition to the Radical Bill, Labor Union Bill, and Tenant Dispute Mediation Bill, various labor organizations in Tokyo Prefecture resolved to hold a large demonstration on the 12th (2nd).

◎ Mr. Joffe's first impression: Mr. Joffe stated that having his luggage searched in his absence was his first impression upon arriving in Japan (Same day).

◎ At the "Haide" Colliery, a gas explosion occurred, and the bodies of 135 miners were discovered (Same day).

◎ A proposal was submitted to the House of Representatives to abolish the entire current military command system, including Circuit Inspectors, Military Governors, Governors-General, Garrison Commanders, etc., which are greatly detrimental to the state and the people (3rd).

▲ Boycott of Japanese Merchants: Due to the issue of shortened transaction deadlines imposed by the exploitative Japanese Import Merchants' Association, about 70 Korean merchants in Anju County united to form the Anju Fabric Merchants' Association and resolutely carried out a non-buying boycott against Japanese merchants (4th).

◎ Mr. Hsieh Fu-ch'eng? (責郛) was appointed as the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Chinese government (Same day).

◎ The Lausanne Near East Conference finally broke down due to the Allied Powers' demand that Turkey recognize all contracts and leased territories signed by the previous sovereign (4th).

◎ Enthusiasm for recovering Lüda (Port Arthur-Dairen): Various public organizations in Shanghai issued telegrams and declarations to the government and associations nationwide, urging efforts to achieve the goal of recovering Lüda (Same day).

▲ Murder in Kwangju: A carpenter named Hōjō? (北條) killed Hsi Fu-mou? (西福), a bill collector for the Taehŭng Electric Company, in broad daylight; the motive was money (Same day).

◎ Even Almighty God in a man-made court: Recently in Moscow, before an audience of over 5,000 radicals, a Communist Party councilor held a mock trial indicting Almighty God as the defendant, refuting and convicting God of sins.

▲ Domestic Product Promotion Women's Association: Realizing that the recent domestic product movement must be thoroughly implemented starting from the home, interested women organized the Domestic Product Promotion Women's Association at the Minu Hall in Sŏdaemun-dong, Seoul (5th).

△ Reason is lack of hope for promotion: High school course students at Masan Private Ch'angsin School collectively withdrew, citing the demand for promotion to Higher Ordinary School status (Same day).

△ Public Ordinary School Students Strike Again: Students of Yongan Public Ordinary School in Iksan County went on a collective school strike, citing the unkindness of the Japanese principal, including insults and beatings (Same day).

△ Hyŏpsŏng School students submitted a petition for the movement to elevate the school to Higher Ordinary School status.

▲ Kyŏngsin students return to school: Students of Kyŏngsin School, who had been on a collective school strike over the promotion issue, returned to school after the issue was deferred until the summer term (Same day).

▲ Students of Pusan Chin Public Commercial School went on a collective school strike due to issues like the extension of the school term and the discrimination created by the character "Chin" (鎭) distinguishing it from Japanese commercial schools (6th).

△ An independence group based in Kuandian County carried out attacks on two houses in Tangho?, Ŭiju County (Same day).

▲ Separate Peace: It is reported that France and Turkey concluded a separate peace treaty (Same day).

▲ 50 won fine for brewing alcohol: The P'yŏngyang District Court ruled according to the original bill in a lawsuit demanding a 50 won fine for violating the Temperance Society regulations, a rare judgment unprecedented in Korea (Same day).

▲ Pan-American Conference: The US government plans to convene the Pan-American Conference in Chile next April.

▲ The US House Immigration Committee passed a bill banning the entry of Japanese immigrants (6th).

▲ Japanese involvement in independence movement: Hashimoto Saikichi (27), residing in Hiki District, Saitama Prefecture, was arrested for secretly selling 1,000 six-shooter pistols to a Korean independence group (Same day).

▲ Turkey issued an ultimatum demanding the withdrawal of all Allied warships over 1,000 tons within 12:00 PM on the 7th (Same day).

▲ US Senator King stated he would submit a Philippine Independence Bill as an additional clause to the Army and Navy Appropriations Bill (Same day).<100>

▲ Source of global disaster: Former British Prime Minister Lloyd George asserted that the current French occupation of the Ruhr not only makes the resolution of the reparations issue impossible but also plunges the world into a great catastrophe (Same day).

▲ About 30 Koreans went from Vladivostok (海蔘威) to Moscow to receive education on communist propaganda (Same day).

▲ Major fire in Ch'ŏngju: A fire broke out at a Japanese store in Ch'ŏngju-ŭp, completely destroying 4 houses, with damages amounting to over 50,000 won (7th).

◎ A major rebellion broke out on the Indian border, resulting in many casualties among Indian and British troops (Same day).

▲ The Sakhalin oil drilling rights, which were a core issue of the Japanese-Russian conference, were suddenly granted to the American Sinclair Company for a period of 36 years (Same day).

▲ It is reported that 350 Japanese soldiers dispatched to annihilate the independence army in West Kando were instead completely wiped out (Same day).

▲ The Allied Powers warned Turkey that they would respond if Turkey took hostile actions (8th).

▲ Student Association: An organization for students of various Korean professional schools, the Student Association, was formed at 7 PM in the Christian Youth Association Hall (9th).

▲ Participation of educators and students in political movements and social movements like communism were banned by presidential decree as violations of the Republic's national policy (Same day - Refers to China).

▲ Students in Peking, joining striking workers, held a joint demonstration under banners such as "Support the Workers," "Overthrow the Warlords" (Same day).

▲ Major fire in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul): Around 2 AM, a fire broke out at the Japanese discount market in Honmachi, completely destroying 13 stores, partially burning 12, with damages around 50,000 won (10th).

▲ Chongno Police Station patrolman Sŏng Nak-hun entered a Chinese person's house under the pretext of controlling opium but was instead charged by the prosecutor's office with illegal confinement, house trespass, attempted extortion, abuse of authority, and blackmail (Same day).

▲ Recently in Vladivostok (海蔘威), laws granting absolute freedom in marriage and divorce for men and women and abolishing prostitution were promulgated (Same day).

▲ The Pusan Chin Commercial School collective strike ended, with students entrusting everything to the committee and returning to classes (10th).

▲ Students of Taegu Public Agricultural School suddenly went on a collective school strike over the school promotion issue (10th).

▲ The US Supreme Court recently ruled that Japanese cannot become US citizens.

▲ 6 or 7 armed members of an independence group entered Sakchu County, causing temporary turmoil (11th).

▲ Turkey granted a 23-day grace period for the withdrawal of Allied warships (Same day).

▲ The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs issued an order to governors in various regions to crack down on foreign merchants violating treaties (11th).

▲ French troops arrested the mayor of Onenjen? (온엔쩬 - likely Essen or another Ruhr city) and confiscated funds for railway security (12th).

▲ Mr. Hickstuhr? (힉쓰튜라), representing about 30 major companies from the central United States, arrived in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) to inspect the situation in the East (Same day).

▲ The British Parliament opened (Same day).

▲ The Russian government notified Japan that mooring Japanese warships in Russian territorial waters could invite international misunderstanding (Same day).

▲ A social gathering of the Commodity Promotion Association was held at 2 PM in the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall, and at 7 PM, promotional lectures were held at the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall and the Christian Youth Association Hall (16th).

▲ The Chief of Staff of the Irish Republican Army declared that the war would continue until independence for Ireland is recognized internally and externally (Same day).

▲ The British government rejected France's request to use the Coden? (코든) region railway (Same day).

▲ The Ruhr deliberation meeting between Britain and France held in London broke down (Same day).

▲ Regarding the Ch'oe Ch'ang-ho assassination incident, a citizens' rally was held, and a telegram arrived at our company stating they resolved they could no longer live under Japan's insincere rule and China's unclear administration, thus deciding to renounce Japanese nationality and submit a protest letter to the Chinese government (17th).

▲ Mr. Yi Kwan-yong, who studied for 10 arduous years in Britain, Switzerland, and Germany and obtained a doctorate in philosophy, arrived in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) (Same day).

▲ Police Affairs Bureau Chief Maruyama suddenly departed for the border region by the 11 PM train (Same day).

△ The US Labor Federation strongly demanded the establishment of conditions for opening the doors to Asian immigrants.

▲ There is a rumor that several Chinese women have secretly entered Japan to propagate Korean independence ideology (18th).

▲ A robbery occurred on Kwanghwamun Street where perpetrators claiming to be detectives stole over 600 won and fled (Same day).

◎ The Oregon State Legislature in the US passed an anti-Japanese land bill (18th - 十入 likely means 18th).

**Summary**

This article from the March 1, 1923 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine provides a digest of news from Korea and around the world, covering roughly January 23rd to February 20th, 1923. Major international events include the escalating French occupation of the Ruhr, diplomatic tensions involving China, Russia, Turkey, and the US (including anti-Japanese measures in America), and unrest in India. Korean news highlights student strikes over educational policies (school designation, curriculum, Japanese teacher behavior), independence movement activities (armed groups, figures like An Ch'ang-ho, arrests, alleged assassinations of collaborators), social issues like fires, crime, accidents, and economic actions like boycotts of Japanese goods and the promotion of domestic products.

For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of the colonial period's atmosphere. It reveals the interconnectedness of global events (Ruhr crisis, US immigration policy) with local Korean concerns. Particularly interesting are the frequent reports of student activism and strikes, indicating education as a key site of resistance and negotiation under colonial rule, and the varied activities of the independence movement both within Korea and abroad (Manchuria, China, liaison with Sun Yat-sen, Soviet connections). The casual mention of Japanese socialist Ōsugi Sakae in Mongolia, US business interests scouting the East, and the Gojong epitaph issue further enrich the picture of this specific moment in time.
