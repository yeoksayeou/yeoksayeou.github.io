**Summary**

This entry lists the titles or descriptions of four cartoons published in the July 1925 issue of Gaebyeok magazine. The cartoons cover international affairs (China's anti-foreign sentiment involving Japan and Britain, Europe's stance on Communism), domestic social issues (exploitation of labor by a wealthy individual in Hwasun), and cultural events (the Chosŏn Art Exhibition). For historians, these cartoon titles offer a glimpse into the social and political commentary prevalent in colonial Korea during the mid-1920s, reflecting concerns about international power dynamics, the rise of communism, capitalist exploitation, and cultural developments under Japanese rule.
