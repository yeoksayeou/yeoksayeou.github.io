The Eight Provinces Representatives' Boasting of the Eight Provinces
						Recorded by Pak Tori
The 5th-anniversary issue was decided to be edited especially as the "Chosŏn Boasting Issue," and while promoting Chosŏn's points of pride from various angles, the editorial office specially hosted an Eight Provinces Boasting Meeting, having representatives from each province boast about their respective province. The time was June 9th, 3 PM. The place was the Kaebyŏk Company editorial office. The host was Soch'un. All eight province representatives were present.

Appended: Eight Provinces Entertainment with Food Items Submitted by the Eight Provinces Representatives
Kangwŏn-do Representative Ch'a Ch'ŏnja - Kyŏnggi-do Representative Min Chachak
Kyŏngsang-do Representative Ch'oe Mundong - Chŏlla-do Representative Kim Kwangdae
Ch'ungch'ŏng-do Representative Yi Ch'ŏngp'ung - P'yŏngan-do Representative Pak Ulttugi
Hamgyŏng-do Representative Cho Yalkae - Hwanghae-do Representative Sin Kyŏngu

Host: Well- the time has come and all the representatives are present, so let's open the meeting. ... But from which province should we start?
Kyŏnggi Rep.: Let's start with Kyŏnggi-do, where Chosŏn's capital is.
Hamgyŏng Rep.: No. That's not right. Let's go straight down from Hamgyŏng-do in the north.
Hwanghae Rep.: There's no need for that either. Let's draw lots.
Kangwŏn Rep.: What lots? How undignified. Let's go in *kanada* (alphabetical) order.
P'yŏngan Rep.: You must be saying that because Kangwŏn-do would be first in *kanada* order. No way.
Host: Hey, is there any discrimination of order here? *Kanada* order is good. Let's do that. Well then- Kangwŏn representative, please come forward.

Kangwŏn Representative Ch'a Ch'ŏnja Takes the Podium
He bounds out boastfully, brimming with confidence as if he's about to achieve some tremendous success, and stands bolt upright. His curly hair and narrow forehead are increasingly comical the more one looks. The audience snickers. <40>
"First, I make a request to the secretary. Please pay attention and record this without omitting a single word. If you omit anything, there will be big trouble. ... Now- the Kangwŏn-do boasting begins. Listen carefully. Now- look here. Isn't Chosŏn's famous mountain, no, the world's famous mountain, the world's famous mountain, the eternally famous mountain, the strange and magnificent Kŭmgangsan in Kangwŏn-do? Aren't the Eight Scenic Views of Kwandong, the divine work of Eastern creation for myriad ages, in Kangwŏn-do? Especially Kyŏngp'odae, which appears in the songs of talented men and beautiful women, is one among them. Never mind the mountains and waters; wasn't the representative eccentric hero of the East, the Ch'anghae Strongman (滄海力士) who struck down Qin Shi Huang, born in Kangwŏn-do? Wasn't the famous scholar and politician Yi Yulgok also a Kangwŏn-do person? There are loyal subjects like Kim Ŭngha, and speaking of recent times, there are patriots like Pak Yongman and Kim Kyusik, and also religious leaders like Kim Yŏnguk in Kangwŏn-do. (Murmurs that this is getting disorganized.) Enough about people; for products, Kangwŏn-do *paekch'ŏng* (white honey), Kangwŏn-do wild ginseng and deer antlers, or Kangwŏn-do tigers (虎) and bears (熊) are famous. Also, Kangwŏn-do *wŏlja* (a type of fish?) is famous, and things like rock tripe (石衣) and oyster mushrooms (늣타리) are also famous, and Kangwŏn-do sea whales are famous throughout all Chosŏn (全鮮). Also, Kangwŏn-do is number one for temples (寺刹), number one for monks (僧), number one for *haengnang sari* (living in servants' quarters?), and also, chastity (貞操) is famous in Kangwŏn-do, so there are no prostitutes (醜業婦). There isn't a single Kangwŏn-do kisaeng (妓生). (Jeers arise that such things aren't points of pride.) Hey, isn't not having something also a boast? What are you talking about... And if there hadn't been the water from Manp'oktong in Kangwŏn-do's Kŭmgangsan, the hundreds of thousands of people in Kyŏngsŏng and Inch'ŏn would have died of thirst, so especially residents of Kyŏngsŏng should bow hundreds of times to Kangwŏn-do, saying 'Thank you for giving us the water of life'. (While jeering that it's idle talk, everyone laughed.) There are so many things to boast about that I can't even sort them by order, and also, if there are too many, it seems the value of the boasts might diminish, so I'll stop."

Kyŏnggi Representative Min Chachak Takes the Podium
Moving his body quite pompously, with an air of utmost triumph, "Look here, with Seoul, the capital of Chosŏn, present, what more is there to say? Even if I just talked about Kyŏngsŏng alone, I couldn't finish even if I spoke for several nights and months. Besides that, there's Kaesŏng, the 500-year royal capital, and Inch'ŏn, Chosŏn's good port, and Suwŏn with its fine scenery, and Manisan with its deep history, and the magnificent Pag'yŏn Falls, and the renowned Samgaksan mountain, and the world-famous Kaesŏng Ginseng, and the Chosŏn famous product Wangsimni water parsley (미나리), and Ansŏng Brassware and the *chach'ae* rice of Yŏju and Ich'ŏn, and Yangju chestnuts, and Kanghwa floral-patterned mats (花紋席), and Changdan soybeans (大豆), and Sŏho crucian carp (鮒魚), are all famous in Chosŏn. The T'aptong Jade Pagoda boasts of ancient art... Is that all? Banks, companies, schools, churches, streetcars, automobiles, rickshaws, bicycles, museums, zoos, botanical gardens, and everything else are most numerous in Kyŏnggi-do! <41> Where else is there better than Kyŏnggi? 'Seoul sightseeing' - who among the people of Chosŏn's 13 provinces doesn't desire it? If I keep listing things, people from other provinces might feel embarrassed, so I'm holding back, but I believe there's none better than Kyŏnggi in terms of people either. I can't list all the successive kings, lords, generals, and ministers, but there are especially famous figures and loyal martyrs like Kang Kamch'an, Chŏng Mongju, Ch'oe Yŏng, Yi Hwal, and in recent times, people like Yu Kilchun, or Chosŏn's richest man like Min Yŏnghwi. (Heckling arises, 'Still can't forget Brother Min, huh?') The great capital Kyŏngsŏng of 300,000, Chosŏn's capital! Everyone, let's shout 'Manse!' for Kyŏnggi-do for the sake of Kyŏngsŏng. Kyŏnggi-do Manse!" (Although the audience acknowledges the facts, they purse their lips.)

Kyŏngsang-do Representative Ch'oe Mundong Takes the Podium
With a stout build and a broad face, acting like the most senior elder,
"Everyone, whatever you say, I believe that in Chosŏn, if you exclude Kyŏngsang-do, there's nothing to boast about. Whether it's geography and climate, products, or people, there's nothing that isn't a point of pride. First, isn't the culture of Silla's thousand years occupying the top position in Chosŏn history? Aren't things like Ch'ŏmsŏngdae, Tabot'ap, Sŏkkuram still boasted about globally? Weren't the ancestors of the Pak clan, Kim clan, Sŏk clan, and Kimhae Kim clan (King Suro) born in Kyŏngsang-do? Weren't famous figures and scholars like Wŏnhyo, Sŏl Ch'ong, Solgŏ, Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn, Pak Chesang, Kyŏn Hwŏn, Kung Ye, Kil Chae, Kwak Chaeu, Pak Po, Pak P'aengnyŏn, Ha Wiji, Yi T'oegye, Mun Ikchŏm, Ch'oe Suun born in Kyŏngsang-do? There's Nongae, a loyal woman among women. There's Pusan, the gateway to Chosŏn. There's Taegu, the major city of Southern Chosŏn. There's the famous Chosŏn market, Kimch'ŏnjang. There's Ch'up'ungnyŏng Pass, which even airplanes cannot cross. There's the thousand-li long Naktonggang River. Andong hemp cloth (安東布) is famous. Ulsan seaweed (海衣) is a famous product of all Chosŏn. Tongnae Hot Springs and Haeundae Hot Springs are famous. T'ongdo[sa], Pŏmŏ[sa], Haein[sa], Chosŏn's three great temples, are all in Kyŏngsang-do, and there's the national treasure, the Tripitaka Koreana (八萬大藏經), and one of the three spirit mountains, Chirisan, is there. Especially, Poju (Chinju) kisaeng are famous, and Kyŏngsang-do colored liquor houses (色酒家) are famous. Though somewhat shameful in some aspects, Kyŏngsang-do is number one for having many *kkalbo* (term for prostitute/promiscuous woman). (The audience burst out laughing, 'Hahaha'.) Never mind everywhere in Chosŏn, but South and North Manchuria, Siberia, even far-off Hokkaido would all be lonely mountains and rivers if not for the prostitutes (賣春婦) produced in Kyŏngsang-do. (The audience agrees, 'True,' and laughs.) Besides that, Choryŏng sandalwood (檀木), Mun'gyŏng azaleas (杜鵑), Poju bullfighting (鬪牛), or white ramie cloth (白木), T'ongyŏng horsehair hats (笠子) or mother-of-pearl (자개), Miryang's cloudy rice wine (濁酒) or sweetfish (銀魚), P'unggi dried persimmons (蹲柿), Ch'angnyŏng ancient tombs (古墳) - these are all famous. And everyone, how good is the disposition (性) of Kyŏngsang-do people? Would others call them 'T'aishan and lofty peaks' (泰山喬嶽) for just any reason? Truly! Magnificent... " <42>

Chŏlla-do Representative Kim Kwangdae Takes the Podium
With a slender build, smiling brightly, clasping and unclasping his hands once,
"Chŏlla-do has so many things to boast about, one representative isn't enough. There should be at least ten people. (Jeers arise that it's idle talk.) Everyone, have you heard the phrase 'the wide fields of Kimje and Man'gyŏng'? Do you know where Chosŏn's granary, Chosŏn's only great plain, is? That is Chŏlla-do. And which province in Chosŏn has the most islands (島)? Also, in which province is Chosŏn's largest island? They are all- in Chŏlla-do. Chŏlla-do bamboo ware (竹器), Chŏlla-do lacquerware (漆器), Chŏlla-do fans (부채), Chŏlla-do bead curtains (珠簾) - these are all famous products of Chosŏn, and also, a famous general like Kim Tŏngnyŏng was born in Chŏlla-do, there are loyal subjects like Chŏng Ch'ungsin, and the great revolutionary Chŏn Pongjun was also a Chŏlla-do gentleman (兩班), and the Great Timely Mandate Son of Heaven Ch'a Kyŏngsŏk also has his headquarters in Chŏngŭp, Chŏlla-do. (Heckling arises, 'This fellow must be one of his gang!') Is that all? Chŏlla-do clowns (광대) are famous. Oops, I forgot. The eternally loyal woman Ch'unhyang was also a product of Chŏlla-do. (Shouts of 'That's not true, you rascal!') Is that all? The *haenyŏ* (海女 - female divers) of Cheju-do are also a famous feature of Chosŏn, and one of the three spirit mountains, Hallasan, is also a famous mountain of Chosŏn; its bamboo forests (竹林) are famous, and Cheju tangerines (橘), Yŏnggwang dried yellow corvina (굴비), Yŏngam fine-toothed combs (찬빗), Chŏnju scorched rice (누른밥), Sunch'ang red pepper paste (고초장), Naju small dining tables (소반), Kŭmsan ginseng (인삼) - those are all- famous products known throughout all Chosŏn (全鮮). And the social skills of Chŏlla-do people are truly famous. And they are bright in reason (理智). Now- how about that? I'll stop."

Ch'ungch'ŏng-do Representative Yi Ch'ŏngp'ung Takes the Podium
With a slender face and composed posture, clearing his throat like a scholar 'Ahem,' once,
"Without needing two words, the world calls Ch'ungch'ŏng-do 'Clear Wind, Bright Moon' (淸風明月). How clean, neat, and refreshing is that? And the world recommends Ch'ungch'ŏng-do as *yangban* (aristocratic). If the praises given by the world are like this, what indeed would be the boasts put forth by Ch'ungch'ŏng-do itself? Now- listen. As the mountains are bright and waters beautiful (山明水麗), the people born are either *yangban* or talented men and beautiful women; as the land and climate are suitable, the products produced are all famous items. Weren't eternally loyal subjects like Kim Yusin, Sŏng Sammun, Yi Sunsin, Im Kyŏngŏp people of Ch'ungch'ŏng-do? And weren't great figures like Kim Okkyun and Son Pyŏnghŭi people of Ch'ungch'ŏng-do? Hansan fine ramie cloth (細모시) is famous as the clothing material for high-collar men and women throughout all Chosŏn, Poŭn large jujubes (大棗) sweeten the mouths of all Chosŏn men and women, Ch'ŏnan walnuts (胡桃) are a famous product known both domestically and abroad, the white cranes (白鶴) of the Tangjin fields represented the pure and noble spirit of Chosŏn, Myŏnch'ŏn's azalea wine (杜鵑酒) and Onyang Hot Springs (溫井) are also famous, the Great Mirŭk Buddha (大彌力佛) of Ŭnjin is a representative work of East and West, Chech'ŏn's Ŭirimji Reservoir is the best in the Samnam (three southern provinces), and Kyerongsan, the future royal capital, is yearned for <43> even by children and common folk (兒童走卒). The ancient culture of Puyŏ, the ancient capital of Paekche, needs no new boasting now, and the grain of Nonsan and Kanggyŏng does not yield to any province or any field. Things like Sŏsan's salted fermented oysters (어리굴젓) and Ch'ungju's yellow tobacco (黃煙草) are also representative famous products of all Chosŏn. Do you all know?"

P'yŏngan-do Representative Pak Ulttugi Takes the Podium
His bumpy face and height of 6 *ch'ŏk* (feet) somehow make him look like a bully. Smiling once,
"I'll dispense with such trifling banter (酬酌). Though brief, I'll present only real points of pride." (Shouts of 'Was anyone else presenting points of shame, not pride?')
"Everyone, leaving other things aside, do you know in which province Tan'gun, the first grandfather ancestor of our Paedal nation, was born? He was born on Myohyangsan (T'aebaeksan), a famous mountain in P'yŏngan-do. (There were also those who denied this fact.) It's no exaggeration to say that you are all descendants of P'yŏngan-do people. (Jeers of 'No!') You must not fail to pay respects to the place where your ancestors were born... And there's P'yŏngyang, which was the national capital for 3000 years. Especially the thousand-year history of Koguryŏ was the representative golden age among Chosŏn's 5000 years. Sui and Tang couldn't raise their heads, and South and North Manchuria were our territory. That was the vital energy (精氣) of P'yŏngan-do's landscape. Great politicians like Kae Sowun (Yeon Gaesomun) and great generals like Ŭlchi Mundŏk were all- P'yŏngan-do gentlemen. And the representative revolutionary of Chosŏn, General Hong Kyŏngnae, was also a P'yŏngan-do person. Even Ondal the fool was a P'yŏngan-do person. There were generals like Kim Ŭngsŏ, scholars like Sŏnu Hyŏp, and calligraphers like Chang Sŭngŏp, and loyal beauties like Kye Wŏrhyang who killed the Japanese general Konishi [Yukinaga], and speaking of modern times, patriots like Yi Kap, An Ch'angho, Yi Chaemyŏng were all- P'yŏngan-do people. And Chosŏn's longest river, the Amnokkang, is also in P'yŏngan-do; the East's longest iron bridge is also in P'yŏngan-do; aren't Moranbong, where three mountains half fall (三山半落), and Nŭngnado, where two waters divide (二水中分), also in the songs of talented men and beautiful women? Is that all? There are the five great rivers: Amnok, Taeryŏng, Ch'ŏngch'ŏn, Taedong, Tongno; there's much of the elixir of immortality, wild ginseng and deer antlers; Anju silk gauze (亢羅), Sŏngch'ŏn tobacco (담배), Hamjong chestnuts (밤), Huch'ang timber (材木), P'yŏngyang smokeless coal (無烟炭), Unsan gold (金), P'yŏngyang cold noodles (冷麵), Anju embroidered pillows (繡枕) are famous products unparalleled in all Chosŏn. Aren't Kanggye beauties (美人) and P'yŏngyang kisaeng praised by everyone while swallowing their saliva? Everyone, how about it? It's true, isn't it? Though brief, just present things worthy of pride like me... Oops, I forgot. Just one more thing... 'Fierce tiger emerging from the forest' (猛虎出林) is a phrase representing the character of P'yŏngan-do people. Scary, isn't it? Fierce tiger emerging from the forest... (Shouts of 'Hey, that has no substance!')

Hamgyŏng-do Representative Cho Yalkae Takes the Podium
With a dark face like a snakehead fish <44> and a stout body like a 4-by-1-*ch'ŏk* board, he looks just like a meddler no matter how you look at him. <44> In Hamgyŏng-do dialect,
"Everyone, do you know where Chosŏn's three thousand *li* started from? Didn't it start from Paektusan in our Hamgyŏng-do? Didn't you all call, memorize, and praise Paektusan as Chosŏn's ancestral mountain (宗山) since your elementary school days? Then, since we possess Paektusan, the main mountain of Chosŏn, what else should we boast about? If we were to boast meticulously like you all, it's not that our Hamgyŏng-do lacks things to boast about. In terms of people, founders of dynasties like Yi Sŏnggye and Zhu Yuanzhang were born here (Opposition arises, 'Not Zhu Yuanzhang'). There were figures like Tong Turan and Yi Siae, and in recent times, there were figures like Yi Chun and Yi Tonghwi. For products, the pollack (明太) that appears on your dining tables morning and evening is a Hamgyŏng-do product. The seaweed (미역) indispensable during childbirth for Chosŏn's mothers is abundant in Hamgyŏng-do, and also the *pukp'o* (北布 - northern hemp cloth) famous as your summer clothing material is a Hamgyŏng-do product. The Kangwŏn representative boasted about tigers, but are they as good as our Paektusan tigers? Is this all? Hamhŭng coal (石炭), Kyŏngsŏng iron (鐵), Sŏgwangsa temple pine mushrooms (松茸), Myŏngch'ŏn pottery (鳥地器), the forests near Paektusan, Changjin sweet potatoes (甘藷), P'ungsan and Kapsan's oats (燕麥), Yŏnghŭng salmon (鰱魚), Chuŭl Hot Springs (溫泉) - these are all famous. Also, in Hamgyŏng-do, there's no great disparity between rich and poor, women's activity is notable, and the patience and diligence (勤儉) of Hamgyŏng-do people are worthy of representing the world, even more so than the Chinese. Do you know? And do you know about the Hamgyŏng-do water carriers who 'live between two waters and enter and exit the gates of generals and ministers' (生居兩水之間 出入將相之門)? If they just held a united strike (同盟罷業), you all would be so thirsty you'd squeeze horse dung houses to drink. (Shouts of 'No! It wouldn't come to that even by our own hands. Anyway, that's grand talk!') Since you're all jealous, I'll stop..."

Hwanghae-do Representative Sin Kyŏngu Takes the Podium
Neither angry nor smiling (不怒不笑), true to his name, with a sturdy demeanor, he speaks slowly.
"If not the first, then the last; I am glad to be doing the Hwanghae-do boasting at the very end. (Sneers of 'What's to be glad about being last?') That's because your memory of Hwanghae-do will be fresher. (Time's running out. Hurry up and speak.) Everyone, did you see the 3 great boasts of Hwanghae-do in the June issue of Kaebyŏk? (We saw the 2 great shames too.) Well, let me tell you. Truly, Hwanghae-do's hot springs (溫泉) are indeed famous. There's hardly a county without hot springs! Even if there are, there are two or three! Among them, the hot springs of Yŏnbaek, Ongjin, Sinch'ŏn, Anak, P'yŏngsan are especially famous. And Hwanghae-do has the best transportation convenience in all Chosŏn, and for products like rice (米穀), cotton (棉花), barley/wheat (兩麥), soybeans (大豆), it doesn't lose to other provinces, and there's the famous Kuwŏlsan where Tan'gun <45> ended his life, and there's Changsu-san, the Kŭmgang of Hwanghae, and didn't Teacher Ch'oe Ch'ung, the Confucius of Haedong (海東孔子), hail from Haeju? Weren't great famous generals like Sin Sunggyŏm and Yu Kŭmp'il all born here? Speaking of modern figures, Yi Sŭngman (Syngman Rhee) is also a Hwanghae-do person. No Paengnin is also a Hwanghae-do person. An Chunggŭn is also a Hwanghae-do person. Yang Kŭnhwan is also a Hwanghae-do person. Yang Kit'ak is also a Hwanghae-do person. Figures known by anyone at home or abroad are all- Hwanghae-do figures. Truly- magnificent, isn't it? And the Myŏngsa Simni (Ten Li of Fine Sand) and Hwagul Cave of Changyŏn are great famous sites unseen throughout all Chosŏn. And Hwangju apples (林檎), Chaeryŏng iron (鐵), Haeju's ink stick (墨) and purple stone (紫), Yŏnp'yŏnghae croaker (石首魚), Ongjin's oysters (石花), Changyŏn's soybean sprouts (大豆黃卷) are famous products praised by all Chosŏn. Now- how does it compare with other provinces? Isn't it magnificent?"

Host: The meeting ends here. I rejoice with you all that it has concluded successfully in great spirits. Let's leave the critique and judgment to the general readers. The meeting is adjourned.

Reporter's Addendum: Among the boasts of the Eight Province representatives, there were statistics regarding products and lineages regarding figures, but they were omitted due to speed writing. Since there are Kaebyŏk issues for each province, the means of reference are also clear.

Eight Province Representative Submitted Food and Eight Province Entertainment
The boasting competition of the Eight Province representatives ended, and in a separate room, an Eight Province Entertainment Meeting was held with special food items submitted by the representatives from their own provinces.
This time, the Kyŏnggi representative comes out first. A large *kyojasang* (square dining table) is loaded heavily with famous Kyŏnggi foods, looking filling just by sight. There's *chach'ae* rice밥, water parsley pickles (메나리 짠지), Yongmunsan thistle rice (취쌀), Seoul *yaksik* (sweet glutinous rice) and colored rice cakes (色餠), Kaesŏng *p'yŏnsu* (dumplings) and pork (猪肉), Yangju chestnuts (밤), and Ch'angŭimun Gate outer apples (林檎) displayed appealingly, accompanied by a pot of medicinal wine (藥酒), a kettle of ginseng tea (인삼차), and eight bowls of *sŏllŏngt'ang* (ox bone soup). And the representative laughs, "Even with food, Kyŏnggi is probably number one, right?"

This time, the Kangwŏn representative comes out. "Our Kangwŏn-do, for food..." he says, seemingly embarrassed and hesitant, putting down a large wooden bowl (*hambak*). Looking inside, what is there? There are buckwheat crepes (모밀전병), radish *songp'yŏn* (rice cakes), acorn jelly (도토리묵), pine nut porridge (잣죽), Kŭmgangsan rock tripe (石茸), Odaesan shiitake mushrooms (표고나물), etc. And the representative says, "For thirsty folks, honey water (蜜水) is the best," and brings out a container of Kangnŭng white honey (白淸). The audience claps, saying "Excellent!"

This time, the Kyŏngsang-do representative comes out. In assorted large and small Japanese-style earthenware bowls (倭砂鉢 되 砂鉢) <46> and earthenware bowls, he presents Taegu *t'angban* (rice soup), Chinju *pibimbap* (mixed rice), Miryang *makgŏlli* (cloudy rice wine), P'unggi dried persimmons (蹲柿), and Yŏngju dog meat soup (개장국). Jeers arise here too, saying dog meat soup is unacceptable.

This time, the Chŏlla-do representative presents refined food on a Naju *soban* (small table): Tamyang bamboo shoots (竹筍), Yŏnggwang dried yellow corvina (굴비), Chŏnju scorched rice (누른밥), Sunch'ang red pepper paste (고초장), and Cheju tangerines (橘). There's a commotion asking why there's so little when Chŏlla-do food is said to be the best.

This time, the Ch'ungch'ŏng-do representative comes out. He puts down a container of barley rice (보리밥), Ch'ŏngju clear soy sauce (淸醬), Yŏngdong green bean jelly (청푸), Myŏnch'ŏn azalea wine (杜鵑酒), a bowl of Sŏsan salted fermented oysters (어리굴젓), followed by Poŭn large jujubes (大棗), Hwanggan persimmons (감), and Ch'ŏnan walnuts (胡桃).

This time, the P'yŏngan-do representative comes out, first presenting eight bowls of P'yŏngyang cold noodles (凉麵), eight bottles of *soju* (素酒 - clear liquor), and one bottle of Kanggye *nŭngju* (능酒 - a type of liquor?). Then he presents common P'yŏngan-do items like corn cakes (강낭떡), bean curd dregs (되비지), Hamjong chestnuts (밤), P'yŏngyang fish platter (*ŏbukchangguk* 어북장국), and about a hundred seagull eggs (갈매기알). Everyone laughed.

This time, the Hamgyŏng-do representative's steamed pollack (北魚찜), seaweed soup (미역국), potato starch noodles (감저국수), oat rice (귀리밥), salmon soup (鰱魚국), and Wŏnsan white chicken soup (白鷄湯) come out. Everyone beams, saying the white chicken soup is great.

This time, the Hwanghae representative's Haeju steamed fresh fish (生鮮찜) and *Pangmunju* (方文酒 - a type of liquor), Yŏnan *injŏlmi* (glutinous rice cakes), Hwangju apples (林檎), Pongsan pears (배), Yŏnp'yŏng croaker soup (조구국), Ongjin fresh oysters (生굴), and Changyŏn vegetables (菜蔬) come out.

Thus, the banquet opened with the rare delicacies and grand feast (珍需盛饌) of the Eight Provinces. After eating their fill according to taste until drunk and full, the Eight Provinces entertainment began. The Kyŏnggi representative sang *Yusanga* (Song of Visiting Mountains), the Kangwŏn-do representative chanted Buddhist prayers (*yŏmbul* 염불), the Kyŏngsang-do representative sang *Yukchabaegi*, the Chŏlla-do representative sang *Tan'ga* (short song), the Ch'ungch'ŏng-do representative sang *Sijo* (classical poem), the Hwanghae-do representative sang *Nanbongga* (Song of the Rakish Fellow), the P'yŏngan-do representative sang *Susimga* (Song of Sorrow), and the Hamgyŏng-do representative sang the *Arirang* ballad (아르랑 타령). After much laughing and leaping about, the gathering dispersed only after 10 PM. <47>

**Summary**

This 1925 article from the magazine *Kaebyŏk* reports on a fictional "Eight Provinces Boasting Meeting" hosted by the magazine's editorial staff for its 5th-anniversary "Chosŏn Boasting Issue." Representatives from each of the eight traditional provinces of Korea take turns extolling the virtues of their home region, citing famous mountains, historical figures, cultural sites, local products, and even the perceived character traits of their people, often leading to humorous interjections and rivalries among the delegates. The event concludes with a feast featuring signature dishes from each province and entertainment consisting of traditional songs and chants performed by the representatives.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects regional identities and stereotypes prevalent in 1920s colonial Korea. The boasting highlights what was considered culturally significant or economically valuable at the time, mixing ancient history (Silla, Koguryŏ, Tan'gun) with contemporary figures (patriots, religious leaders, the wealthy) and products (ginseng, textiles, minerals, food specialties), providing a snapshot of Korean self-perception and regional pride expressed, albeit playfully, within the constraints of the colonial period. Historians might find the specific choices of boasts (e.g., historical figures claimed, products emphasized, even negative stereotypes acknowledged humorously) revealing about regional consciousness and the articulation of Korean identity during that era.

八道代表의 八道자랑
						朴돌이 記
5주년 기념호는 특히 「朝鮮자랑號」로 編輯하기로 되야 각 방면으로 朝鮮의 자랑거리를 내세우는 중인데 편집실 특별 주최로 八道자랑會를 개최하고 각각 그 道 대표로 하여금 그 道를 자랑하기로 되얏다. 일시는 6월 9일 오후 3시. 장소는 開闢社 편집실. 司會는 小春. 八道 대표 총출석.

附八道代表出品飮食으로 八道餘興
江原道代表 車賤者- 京畿道代表 閔自酌
慶尙道代表 崔文童- 全羅道代表 金廣大
忠凊道代表 李凊風- 平安道代表 朴울뚝이
咸鏡道代表 趙얄개- 黃海道代表 申耕牛

사회. 자- 시간도 다 되고 대표들도 다 출석햇스니 개회함시다. ... 그런데 어느 道부터 시작할까요?
京畿대표. 朝鮮의 首府가 잇는 京畿道부터 하지오.
咸鏡대표. 안이오. 그럿치 안소. 北으로 咸鏡道부터 죽-내합시다.
黃海대표. 그럴것도 업소. 제비를 뽑읍시다.
江原대표. 제비가 무슨 제비요. 점잔치 못하게. 가나다 순으로 합시다.
平安대표. 가나다 순으로 하면 江原道가 먼츰 되닛가 그러는가 보오. 안되오.
사회. 여보 여긔도 무슨 先後의 차별이 잇소. 가나다 순이 좃소. 그대로 합시다. 자- 江原대표부터 나오시오.

江原代表 車賤者君 登壇
무슨 끔즉한 성공이나 할 것처럼 氣高萬丈해서 까불까불 나와 딱 제치고 선다. 곱술머리 참새이마는 처다볼수록 우슴이 덜컥 난다. 一同은 킥킥 웃는다.＜40＞
「爲先 書記에게 부탁함니다. 주의해서 한마디라도 빼지 말고 기록해 주시오. 빼면 큰 야단남니다. ... 자- 江原道 자랑 나옴니다. 謹聽하시오. 자- 보십시오. 朝鮮 名山 안이 天下 名山 世界 名山 億萬古名山 奇絶壯絶의 金剛山이 江原道에 잇지오. 萬古東方造化神功 關東八景이 江原道에 잇지오.  특히 才子佳人의 노래에 오르내리는 鏡浦臺가 그 중의 하나이지오. 山水風景은 말도 말고 東洋의 대표적 怪傑 秦始皇을 때려부신 滄海力士가 江原道서 낫지오. 유명한 학자요 정치가인 李栗谷도 江原道 사람이지오. 金應河가튼 忠臣도 잇고 근래로 말하면 朴容萬, 金奎植,가튼 志士도 잇고 또 金演局가튼 敎主도 江原道에 잇소.(너저즐하다고 떠든다.) 인물은 그만하고 산물로 江原道 白淸, 江原道 山蔘 鹿茸, 또는 江原道 범(虎)과 곰(熊)이 유명하지오. 그리고 江原道 月子가 名하고 石衣, 늣타리 가튼 것도 名하고 江原道 바다의 고래는 全鮮에 유명하오. 그리고 寺刹도 江原道가 第一이요 중(僧)도 江原道가 第一이오 行廊사리도 江原道가 第一이요 그리고 江原道에는 貞操가 유명하야 醜業婦가 업소. 江原道 妓生이란 한아도 업소.(그따위는 자랑이 안이라고 야지가 닐어낫다.) 여보, 업는 것도 자랑이지 무슨 말이요... 그리고 江原道 金剛山의 萬瀑洞물이 업섯드면 京城 仁川의 수십만 인구가 목이 말나 죽엇슬 터이니 특히 京城 거주자로서는 江原道에 대하야 「생명수를 주시니 감사함니다」하고 절을 멧 백번식 해야 됨니다.(잡담이라고 야지하면서도 모다 우섯다.) 자랑거리가 하도 만흐니 先後도 가릴 수 업고 또한 너무 만흐면 도리혀 자랑거리의 가치가 損할 듯하야 그만둠니다.」

京畿代表 閔自酌 登壇
몸집을 별로 검방지게 하면서 가장 得意의 勢로써 「여보시오 朝鮮 首府인 서울이 잇는 바에 다시 말할 것이 무엇 잇소. 京城 뿐 드러말한대도 멧 츨 멧 달을 두고 해도 못 다 하겟소. 게다가 500년 王都 開城이 잇고 朝鮮의 良港 仁川이 잇고 풍경조흔 水原이 잇고 역사 깁흔 摩尼山이 잇고 壯快한 朴淵瀑布가 잇고 일홈놉흔 三角山이 잇고 天下名物 開城 人蔘이 잇고 朝鮮名産 往十里 미나리가 잇고 安城 鍮器와 驪州 利川의 자채쌀이며 楊州栗이며 江華 花紋席이며 長湍 大豆며 西湖 鮒魚가튼 것도 朝鮮에 유명함니다. 塔洞 玉塔은 고대 미술을 자랑하고... 이뿐임닛가. 은행, 회사, 학교, 교회당, 전차, 자동차, 인력거, 自行車, 박물관, 동물원, 식물원, 기타 모든 것이 京畿道에 ＜41＞제일 만쿠려! 京畿이상 이 어대 또 잇슴닛가? 「서울구경」- 朝鮮 13道 사람이 누가 원치 안슴닛가. 작구 내세우면 다른 道 사람이 무색할가 봐서 스스로 감추거니와 인물로도 京畿이상이 업슬 줄 아오. 역대의 王侯將相을 다 말할 수는 업고 특히 姜邯贊, 鄭夢周, 崔瑩, 李活가튼 名人烈士가 잇고 근래로는 兪吉濬가튼 이라든지 또는 閔泳徽가튼 朝鮮甲富가 잇소.(그래도 閔哥는 못 닛겟느냐고 야단이 난다) 30萬大京城 朝鮮의 首府! 여러분 京城을 위하야 京畿道 萬歲나 한번 불읍시다. 京畿道 萬歲!」(일동은 사실을 시인하면서도 입은 비죽거린다.)

慶尙道 代表 崔文童 登壇
뚱뚱한 몸집 넙적한 얼굴로 가장 長者인 체 하면서
「여러분, 이러구저러구 해도 朝鮮에 잇서서는 慶尙道를 빼고는 자랑거리가 업슬 줄 아오. 지리와 기후로도 그럿코 산물로도 그럿코 인물로도 그럿코 무엇이나 자랑거리 안인 것이 업소. 爲先 新羅千年의 문물이란 朝鮮역사상에서 제 1위를 점령한 것이 안임닛가. 瞻星臺, 多寶塔, 石窟庵, 가튼 것은 지금도 세계적으로 자랑하지오. 朴氏 金氏 昔氏 金海 金氏(首露王)의 祖上이 慶尙道에서 낫지오. 元曉, 薛聰, 率居, 崔致遠, 朴堤上, 甄萱, 弓裔, 吉再, 郭再祐, 朴普, 朴彭年, 河緯地, 李退溪, 文益漸, 崔水雲가튼 名人名士가 慶尙道 사람이지오. 女中忠烈 論介가 잇지오. 朝鮮의 關門 釜山이 잇지오. 南鮮의 大都會 大邱가 잇지오. 朝鮮名場 金泉場이 잇지오. 비행기도 넘지 못하는 秋風嶺이 잇지오. 千里長流 洛東江이 잇지오. 安東布가 유명하지오. 蔚山海衣는 全鮮의 名物이지오. 東萊溫泉 海雲臺溫泉이 유명하지오. 通度, 梵魚, 海印 등 朝鮮 三大寺가 다 慶尙道에 잇고 國寶 八萬大藏經이 잇고 三神山의 一인 智異山이 잇지오. 특히 普州 妓生이 名하고 慶尙道 色酒家가 名하지오. 엇던 방면으로 좀 수치스러운 듯하나 蝎甫가 만키론 慶尙道가 제일이요.(일동은 하하하 하고 우섯다.) 朝鮮의 到處는 말도 말고 南北滿洲, 西伯利亞, 멀니 北海道까지 慶尙道産의 賣春婦가 안이면 모다가 寂寞江山일테요.(일동은 올소하고 웃는다.) 그밧게 鳥嶺檀木, 聞慶杜鵑, 普州鬪牛 또는 白木, 統營笠子 또는 자개(貝), 密陽의 濁酒 또는 銀魚, 豊基蹲柿, 昌寧 古墳- 이것이 다 유명하지오. 그리고 여러분 慶尙道 人 性이 얼마나 좃슴닛가. 여간해서 泰山喬嶽이라고 남들이 불으겟소. 참! 壯하지오... 」＜42＞

이번은 京畿 대표가 만저 나온다. 크다란 교자상에다가 京畿名飮食을 담북 실엇는데 보기만 해도 배가 부르다. 자채 쌀밥에다가 메나리 짠지에다가 龍門山 취쌀에다가 서울 藥食과 色餠이며 開城편수와 猪肉이며 楊州밤이며 彰義門밧 林檎을 보기좃케 진열한 데다가 藥酒 한 동이와 인삼차 한 주젼자와 설넝탕 여덜그릇을 대밧처 내온다. 그리고 대표는 「음식물로도 아마 京畿가 제일이지오.」 하고 하하 웃는다.
