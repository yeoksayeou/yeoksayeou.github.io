**Body Text Translation:**

The Death of a Young Idealist
						八峰

October 25th, Clear
Mother, sister-in-law, younger sibling, and all the household goods went to P'yŏngt'aek by car this morning. In the afternoon, I took my luggage and came to this boarding house in front of Sangsu's place. If I hadn't insisted so strongly that I was busy with office work and wasn't free, Mother might have dragged me all the way to P'yŏngt'aek.

After dinner, Sangsu and Hyŏngin came over to visit and just left a little while ago. My mind, which had been chaotic and unable to sort things out all day long, gradually cleared, settled, and quieted down after my companions left and I organized my desk. Ah! Thinking about it, I realize a feeling arises that my future path is completely dark, and I cannot know how things will turn out. I realize an ominous premonition, as if some dark shadow looms over my path for some reason, as if I am destined to encounter misfortune, rises like fire. Indeed, what will become of me now? My beloved Yŏngae has banished me. My family – although I never really thought much of them, not even a speck in my heart – anyway, the family has gone to the countryside. I am left alone, needing to somehow survive by clinging to that wretched job as my lifeline. But I myself don't know how long I can continue this kind of deceitful, servile, submissive life. Indeed, what will become of me...? What must I do...? <34>
Ah, the path ahead is just darkness. I must abandon this current occupation as quickly as possible, but it requires immense courage, making it difficult to carry out decisively. Mostly, the work I do at the office involves forcibly writing things that are not in my heart, all for a monthly salary of 40 won. In other words, I am an impure person doing things that go against my conscience. I am a hired employee of a lower government office in a colony! Although I didn't originally desire such a profession, it happened to be the only one I could grasp, so I held onto it for fear of losing even this. It hasn't even been fifty days since I took this job, but in that time, it feels like three years have passed. I've grown sick of this current job beyond measure. Everything involves praising, obeying, and protecting the tools that sustain the fortress of the OOO, the bourgeois, and meticulously recording these duties.

I won't write any further explanation of this profession. It's something everyone knows too clearly. - And so, I, this self-aware young man, have become this type of professional whom others treat with a kind of contempt, writing down things my conscience rejects, regularly, just as the fellow above me commands, just as he instructs. To use an analogy, I am making records identical to reports stating that the people of the OO region admire OOOO. If this is the situation, I am already not a person, but merely like the pen held in their hands when they want to write, dipped in ink and moved as their hand directs, writing characters on white paper. Like a pump drawing water with an electric device, I write the characters they want on paper within the scope of the rules they have established. Therefore, I am a pen. A quill pen tip! Nothing more than a quill pen tip!

Sad. What is this? How can this phenomenon be explained? It goes without saying that people must live by the price of their own efforts. Those who keep their hands and feet idle and live a lifetime of arrogance, leaning back in an easy chair, are unforgivable thieves of effort. Labor must be bestowed upon everyone. That is the <35> law of life for humans. However, I cannot console or comfort my own indignation with this belief. Why? Because I am oppressed by labor that is too forced! Of course, labor is sacred. But when that labor, due to some power, or indirectly as a result of power, becomes forced, and one engages in it unwillingly, work that the conscience does not permit, then labor that should be sacred becomes the most cursed object. And so, I call this phenomenon a peculiarity of modern life. The reality of today's Chosŏn—it is nothing more than this situation tangled and intertwined. And I am at the point of fainting on the boundary line between life and death within it.

What shall I do? Fortunately, there is no one whose livelihood I must guarantee, so surely there's no reason why I, just one person, cannot live in this world. Should I just quit the office job and become a wanderer (浪人)? The family at home surely won't ask me, living and eating in Seoul, to send money every month. Then, if I just have the 15 won for meals here, can't I manage somehow with the rest? Yes, then let's abandon this profession. Until the day my life ends, until the day inspiration and passion disappear, I must live a life true to my ideals. I am decidedly not a lackey of the bourgeois. Especially, I believe I am not *their* lackey. So why do I need to do such work, work fit for a quill pen tip? I think I must simply stand bravely upon reality according to the command of my supreme spirit. Ah, my heart feels generous. I hear a refreshing sound from within my chest, like the sound of sea waves stirred by a spring breeze. I feel brave. I feel energized.

However, I must not suddenly become emotional or lament. Above all, I must gaze upon reality with a solemn attitude. I must not become a sentimentalist.
The night is quiet.

-Written around 12:30 AM
Why is it so painful? Why could it be so agonizing? The night has newly passed 3 o'clock, yet sleep does not come! Ah—it's painful. I've picked up the brush again, but what should I write? I don't know. My chest feels heavy... Still, <36> let me try to seek sleep.

-Written around 3:05 AM
Ah— "Ch'oe Tŏkho"... Why am I like this? Why am I acting this way? My chest feels heavy. It feels like a bonfire is lit inside my chest. Why am I thinking thoughts I resolved not to think? Why? Didn't I promise myself to forget even that Yŏngae was someone I knew? And yet, and yet...

Ah, I know my heart well. This heart. "You still haven't given up. Your heart still hasn't left Yŏngae." I have no words to answer this. So, is it enough to just say I haven't? Does this mean it doesn't matter?

I am just miserable. I was originally born this way, a pathetic, worthless creature. It can't be helped!

Yŏngae? - It's true that I cannot forget the full four months I knew Yŏngae. Just the other day, I even searched through an old diary from four months ago. Whenever I think of Yŏngae, I feel like I'm going mad. Even now, I can't sleep and am struggling like this. Oh, oh—

"Mr. Tŏkho, I love you. It's not that I don't love you. But at the same time, I cannot help but love Mr. Ch'ŏlsu. Yes? Mr. Ch'ŏlsu loves me so much, how could I not love Mr. Ch'ŏlsu? That's why I love Mr. Ch'ŏlsu. And I won't abandon you either. Please forgive me. My heart is big. It is wide. All the people who love me are my lovers. My heart is that wide. I can love you, and I can love Mr. Ch'ŏlsu too. Mr. Tŏkho, Mr. Tŏkho, are you saying that my having a wide heart is bad, a sin? If so, I am disappointed, but you are a gentleman who doesn't know how to love me. You are an elder who lacks the qualification to love me. I don't want to receive love from such a person. Why, you ask? I don't know how to engage in such biased love. I dislike people who restrain my freedom. That's all. Do as you please. From Yŏngae who loves you—" <37>

Yŏngae sent me a letter like this last month. At that time, I was somewhat drawn to her impudent words about her heart being wide and whatnot. I took those words "seriously" (마지메하게) and thought about them. However, when such words didn't just end as words but became reality, my entire consciousness could not forgive it. Can one forgive a single woman loving two or more men, and moreover, can one forgive the woman one loves loving another man besides oneself? I cannot bear it. And the reason Yŏngae sent me this kind of letter was not because she truly loved me, but because she couldn't say anything else, so she vaguely smoothed things over to make me give her up on my own. However, it's difficult to interpret the phrase at the end of the letter, "From Yŏngae who loves you," written out of old habit, as truth. How should I interpret this? If I interpret this as sincere, shouldn't I also interpret the words above it as not false? Yet, to see these words simply as written out of old habit, without any meaning, unintentionally, they seem too natural and sincere. Then does this woman truly love me and Ch'ŏlsu at the same time? Ah—unforgivable. I cannot forgive that.

After receiving that letter, I immediately sent a long reply breaking off relations, and since then, I haven't written to Yŏngae again. At the time I received the letter, I knew it was essentially a letter of separation and was extremely agitated; furthermore, according to the interpretation of my calm reason, it was indeed a letter of separation Yŏngae had sent me. To console my wounded heart even a little, I decided not only to stop writing to Yŏngae but also to drive out all memories of Yŏngae from my head.

Ah, but I cannot forget Yŏngae. It's beyond my power. Why am I suffering like this? It's all because of Yŏngae. Because of Yŏngae! I am a worthless creature. A person like me is useless anywhere in this world. In other words, I am a person created as an extra, a leftover, one more surplus individual beyond the population needed for this world. And yet, even in this state, I must live on. I must live on as long as I can. <38>

Ah! What have I written so lengthily again? I've done something useless again! The rooster crows. The dawn rooster crows. My energy is depleted!

October 26th, Clear
Last night, I couldn't sleep a wink and stayed up all night. I wrote in my diary until five in the morning, and I don't know when I fell asleep, but I opened my eyes today around eleven. My head felt heavy all day, making it unbearable. I inevitably skipped the office. Right now, everything I see, everything I hear, seems only sad. Even the rustling sound of poplar leaves fluttering in the wind in the inn's backyard and the sight of a listless fly flitting before my eyes sadden my heart. Since last night, I have become sentimental.

Until late tonight, I've been alone in my room, sometimes sitting, sometimes lying down, just smoking cigarettes. I find myself pointlessly looking in the mirror. What should I say about the ugly face of this long-haired yellow race...

Sometime ago, Yŏngae told me,
"Your face looks intelligent,"
and tilted her head, looked up at me, and smiled. I thought about that just now. Thinking back, Yŏngae's words at that time seem to have been teasing me.

Thinking about it now, that was June 29th, 116 days ago. And now, 116 days later, Yŏngae and I have become strangers. Yŏngae, whom I believed to be the symbol of my ideals, the "mother of my happiness," and I have become strangers. When an extreme longing that feels like tearing apart and a heart-wrenching sorrow that feels like my bones are chilling surge up like spring water, I honestly feel – though it's embarrassing to say – like cutting my own carotid artery, a mad thought arises. Isn't it a body that will die someday anyway? To forget this unbearable suffering, wouldn't it be easier to just end my life? This uncontrollable, fire-like, crazed feeling attacks my whole body. Ah, but I, I, cannot die yet. As long as a shred of hope remains in my head, as long as there is still some inspiration and passion for life <39> in my heart, I cannot die yet. Although I may be a powerless human being, I am confident that the mission I hold as one person is significant. Yes, how could this body not have a mission as one person, one Chosŏn person?

Then what is my mission? It is the same, significant mission shared among all the newly grown youth of Chosŏn. That mission is to act towards building a world of sublime ideals on Earth, promoting the evolution of humanity guaranteed by evolutionary science. The injustice of modern society exists due to the primitiveness of modern humanity. Therefore, whenever humans eventually become complete beings, the world will become a paradise. That is, the day will come when all humanity reaches the ultimate destination, the point they aim for. Our mission is simply to share the burden and accelerate all humanity towards this goal. I believe this is a truth guaranteed by history.

However, I have received much criticism and contempt that this idea is nothing more than a cheap spiritualist's utopia. But if I were to discard this program of human evolution from my current self, I couldn't live even one more day. My ideal is that important, like my life itself. Some people call me a Tolstoyan. But I believe myself not to be a Tolstoyan. Because I haven't gone mad over God. Others call me a Rollandist. This statement is not very wrong. In fact, I resonate with Rolland on many points. And so, I am uniformly rejected as a spiritualist. But my conviction has not wavered because of that, and I am confident it will not waver in the future either.

Yes, ah, but for some reason, I sense an uncontrollable chaos rising within myself. First, I have forgotten Yŏngae, whom I myself believed to be the symbol of my ideal. When I received the affectionate letter of separation (!) from Yŏngae, I managed to send back a clean, lingering-free reply with good spirits, but thinking about it now, I realize that forgetting Yŏngae was a huge blow to myself. I once called Yŏngae a modern-style courtesan type of woman. Indeed, that wasn't a lie. And I tried to make that Yŏngae into the woman according to my ideals. But <40> I did not succeed with Yŏngae.

Ah—my chest feels heavy again. My head feels like it weighs a thousand catties (斤). Let's stop. Let's stop writing.

October 30th
I haven't written in my diary for three days. Firstly, because whenever I pick up the brush, words about Yŏngae keep trying to come out—.

Today too, Sangsu and Hyŏngin came to visit and left. Those two are the only guests who visit me. Sangsu and I have known each other for a long time, since way back, and he is the friend who resonates with me the most, but Hyŏngin is a friend I got to know recently, since coming to Seoul last year. He likes to argue with me. Today too, Hyŏngin preached his dualistic philosophy before leaving.

Hyŏngin says:
—In ancient times, there was a single gas in the universe. That gaseous body had two types: pure and impure. The pure ascended to become heaven, and the impure descended to become celestial bodies. This was the beginning of the universe. And between heaven and earth, there was yin and yang. The emergence of differences like negative and positive electricity, male and female, all stem from this. Thus, the world was formed from these differences.

That is, the differences in the biological world such as high and low, noble and base, rich and poor, strong and weak. After one lifestyle was established in human society, another lifestyle replaced it and came down through time. As soon as the instinctual life of primitive times ended, people established a life of ideas, and in place of the life of ideas, they now live an intellectual life. In ancient times, the revolt against the tyranny of feudal lords was the revolution of free city dwellers, and the revolt against the oppression of the Pope was the Renaissance revolution. But wasn't that the cause that gave birth to today's capitalism? To take a recent example, the Meiji Restoration in the 19th century was truly thought of as a revolution of the Japanese populace. But wasn't the reality of the Meiji Restoration merely the birth of today's parliamentary politics belonging to the capitalists? And the result, in the end, was just making Japan an international capitalist nation.

You talk about the completion of humanity and whatnot, but that is merely a reaction spun out by the present reality. Humanity can never become a finished product. Why is that? Because all kinds of reforms and innovations that history has repeated <41> have not brought us one step closer to completion.

Some people think communism is the highest ideal. But that is just a reaction arising because today's capitalism wields its cruelty too much. Communism is by no means perfect.

First, communism ignores the free, unrestrained innate nature of human life. If the entire world were made into a communist world, the world would collapse on the spot. The so-called principle of that communism, equality, is impossible. Why? Because humans are animals of desire, and as long as that desire exists, equality cannot be implemented. Even a newborn baby cries if it sees its mother's breast being given to another baby. This is because even children possess things like a sense of ownership or superiority. Furthermore, since there are innate differences in superiority and inferiority in human brains, isn't it justifiable that there are distinctions in the rewards for their efforts? Even if a social movement or revolution occurs, it is ultimately the work of one or two leaders, not the entire populace. Isn't it clear even from the fact that differentiation still exists in communist states that espouse equality as a principle? And you say that all humans are the same, but humans are definitely not the same. You say that anyone, regardless of who they are, feels sad when seeing sad events and happy when seeing happy events, but it's absolutely not like that. How often is it that what Easterners find sad becomes a source of joy for Westerners? Humankind is not the same.

Theory and reality are different. Equality in theory leads to inequality in reality. Generally, the world is not monotonous or simple. Just as the five fingers on one person's hand are all different, there are differences in the world, and among people who seem alike, there are distinctions. Good versus evil, beauty versus ugliness, truth versus falsehood, etc., etc... Just as there is clear weather and clouds in the same sky, this world is established through differences. Your ideal is noble, beautiful, but it is just an ideal."

Hyŏngin laid out a long speech with roughly this meaning and left. I, too, acknowledge this kind of dualistic evolution. However, as long as the lofty understanding of humanity does not rest, as long as our reason is active, the goal of world humanity that we cannot help but seek is not swayed by such sophistry. The crying of a child seeing another child drink its mother's milk is not possessiveness or a sense of superiority. It is a human cry clamoring for equality in distribution. The claim that the very same fact can be felt in two ways,悲喜 (sadness and joy), by different <42> people is also wrong! If such a fact exists, it must be the result of material circumstances. It is not pure perception (純粹智覺). It is natural that leaders are necessary for social movements. But that is not the privilege seen in the capitalist social system! It is a joyous thing that the masses are gradually becoming enlightened. It is a joyous thing that they are becoming wiser. The phenomenon called the distorted development of scholarship is ultimately something only seen within the bourgeois organization. The idea that there is innate superiority or inferiority in human brains is merely a conventional bourgeois viewpoint.

Ah—I've become agitated. This is precious inspiration, too valuable to waste on sleep. The clock in the next house just struck one.

October 31st
Today I went to the office, threw down my letter of resignation, and walked out. Now I am like a cloth wiped clean. My mind feels refreshed. Only now have I escaped being a watchdog. My spirit is cheerful!

At night, I met Sangsu and briefly heard news about Yŏngae. But I wasn't particularly moved. I will try harder to forget cleanly from now on. My body is tired.

December 1st, Cold
The days are gradually getting colder. How am I to live? Worries begin to arise. The month of November somehow passed by, but doing nothing, and with nothing working out, continuing like this makes my heart truly anxious, unbearably so.

—Anyway, I cannot remain idle like this!
Over the past month (actually, this phrase has become almost a habit since the year before last), I have said this to myself hundreds of times. Isn't it true that I cannot remain idle like this? Right before my eyes, right in front of these blankly staring eyes, open murder is committed, the enslavement and mechanization of people occur, "theft is committed openly, regularly, in broad daylight" (白晝에 規則的으로 公公然하게 窃盜가 行하여지며), seeing the white-clad people being murdered, not only seeing it but also experiencing it myself <43> — how can I remain still! Either I die, or when my life ends, I must try to resist. I must try to fight with all my strength. But, ah, but, I cannot do it! I am unable to do it!

I know what violence is. It is not a justifiable means. Truly, OO (violence?) unaccompanied by reason will only smear another error onto the history of mankind, I think. We hate the violence of the present. Therefore, we cannot use another, separate violence to counter violence! But—

—Anyway, I cannot remain idle like this!
Indeed, I cannot just sit idly and die. Then what must I do?
I can't think. My head is just chaotic; no thoughts come. In this state, I am desperate!

December 3rd, Cold, Snow
I want to curse this existence. This feeble existence, like a puppy without the activity of conscience, I want to curse it. Sin does not only reside with the upper echelons of life who receive insults from the world! Sin exists equally on every side. Everyone is committing a grave sin that should not be committed. And it merely weaves a chain of existence that is certainly worth cursing.

Is it really okay for people to be like this? Are people inherently just this kind of object forever? Is the completion of humanity merely an empty fantasy like a child's babble, without any hope? Is this intricately carved work called life, full of ups and downs (波瀾重疊), forever just a relief sculpture (浮彫) that never reaches a separate completion? "Freedom! It's a privilege? Violence! It's an unforgivable error that tramples on humanity! The original free soul of humankind rebels! We are messengers of justice. Champions of equality! Apostles of the god of freedom!" And the great OO (revolution?) that sweeps across the earth like mad waves, like a flood destroying everything—was it truly the desire of the entire nation, the entire populace, and did the OO achieved in such a way completely avoid the beaten path and commit no errors? No! Just as the fame of a few individuals is built upon the corpses of a million soldiers in a militaristic nation, I can only see the fame of the leaders of the OO built upon the bloody battle of the entire populace! The idol was destroyed! But a new, individual idol is erected! Once, they hated the ruling power. But new privileges <44> dominate their bodies. Where is freedom? Where is the equality where all humankind is equal?

Ah, is man an eternal loser? Are people inherently like this from the start?... Before this fact, what authority can my ideal possibly have! Now I am trapped in boundless sorrow...

December 4th, Cold, Snowstorm
It's cold.—
Just as my body craves heat right now, my soul too is in a state of craving heat. But that heat is absent. Once, when I had love, back then, I had heat. But the loss of love robbed the heat from me, from my life, from my soul. Without heat, plants die. Now my soul withers.

Passion for life, inspiration from truth—these too are gone now. It seems I will live on ordinarily like this from now on, until one morning when the frosty wind blows, I will fall away from life, like a reed leaf blown by the wind. Is it sad? Is it painful? Is it heart-wrenching? Is it agonizing?

Ah— Forgive me! I have no strength. I don't even have the strength to answer back. Oh, Yŏngae! If only Yŏngae were here! The only person who can save me is Yŏngae!

December 5th, Cloudy then Clear, Cold
Melancholy has captured me! For the past 2 or 3 days, I have lived while seeing dreams of hell!

Sangsu tells me to either go on a trip or go to my country home. But those words are useless to me right now.

Hyŏngin tells me to start loving again to rekindle passion. He says if I want to, interacting with the opposite sex is good, and if I just agree, he will create such an opportunity. I will never speak with a crazy fellow like Hyŏngin again. There's a limit to treating someone like a fool! <45>

What is absolutely important to me now is conviction, more than love. I don't even wish for something like a woman. If possible, I intend to remain single. Having lost my conviction, I have no choice but to die or go mad! And I am almost dying now!

Ah, it's cold. Cold. I'm trembling from within my bones. My life doesn't seem likely to continue for many more days. My spirit sinks moment by moment. What should I do? Truly, I must do something.

December 10th, Snow
It's a snowy day. For some reason, everything seems sad to me. Everything, even bygone days, I miss and think about. I miss my companions. I miss my older brother who went to Japan and hasn't sent word. I miss him! I miss my younger sibling at home too. Mother! Father!...

Yesterday, Sangsu went hunting in Kangwŏn Province with his father. He urged me endlessly to go with him, but I stubbornly refused, saying I didn't want to. Thinking about it now, it would have been better if I had followed Sangsu; I made a mistake.

Because the room is lonely and the seat is cold, thoughts of Yŏngae come to mind. There is an extremely earnest feeling. Ah— what have I written again!

December 13th, Cold
Today I met Hyŏngin and heard that Yŏngae officially married Ch'ŏlsu! My heart ached.

December 14th, Cold
Even if we don't know why people were born, as long as people live, it is difficult to live without hope, ideals, pleasure, or solace. Truly, why are we living? Is there any reason why people absolutely must live? I have no hope. No ideals, no pleasure, no solace. I feel as though I have no ethnicity, no nation. And it seems there is no humanity, justice, freedom, or equality either. I am in despair! What do I want to do? What do I hope for? What do I intend? What do I know? What kind of thing is a person? Yes, just what kind of <46> thing is a person?
I don't know!

December 15th, Cold
Ah — I am dying! I am dying because I have no hope, dying because I have no ideals, dying because I have no solace. Dying because I have no inspiration and passion. Because I lost Yŏngae — no! What does that Yŏngae matter to me! What does Yŏngae, who basically went off, sold, to a man with money, have to do with anything! — I am merely dying because I have no heat! Dying because this life has no boiling water (熱湯).
Yes! Let's die! To live longer is simultaneously a disgrace and unbearable.

December 20th, Weather looks like snow
For five days, I agonized the agony of a lifetime! Today, I finally resolved and bought 20 chŏn worth of lye (양재물). My life is to be exchanged for this lye.

Ah — thinking about it, indeed, life is lonely (寂寞). I am dying. But to this life, it is merely an ordinary fact. When I think of the countless lives that appear and disappear from morning to evening, I realize my death will be a commonplace death that casts no influence whatsoever on society. But, be ashamed. All humanity! I die scorning your powerlessness and foolishness along with my own. Unable to bear the sinful mass that is the utopia smeared on your lips, I die. To paint blood on your conscience that abandoned me, I die.

Sun, moon, stars! I am dying! Forsaking your paradise, I die. You have heat, but I die because I have no heat! Oh, give me heat, give me boiling water! Give me the sun!

Comrades! I am dying! Forsaking you, I die. I have lost the heat to follow you, lost the heat to boil the steam in the engine of life!... <47>

His diary ends here, only up to December 20th. At the end of this final diary entry, something was written filling the page, then erased with ink so thoroughly that not a single character can be deciphered.

And this idealist wrote one farewell letter each to his friend Sangsu, his older brother, and his father before dying. Tucked within his diary book was a scrap of paper on which was written:
"Finally, to Yŏngae—.
Yŏngae! Yŏngae, wife of Mr. Ch'ŏlsu! I..."
Having written this much, he stopped, and then struck through it with lines from a steel pen (텰필). I surmised that this idealist had intended to write something like a final note to his former lover, but then thought it unnecessary and gave up. He died, so to speak, due to his weak character, or perhaps because he was too steeped in spiritualism, vainly seeking ideals, and ultimately ended his own life due to the conflict between reality and ideals, I thought. However, I cannot forget this companion who died so young and tragically. I still keep his diary very carefully.
(End) <48>

**Summary**

The article presents the final diary entries of a young Korean man in 1925, identified only by his given name Tŏkho in the text (though the preceding narrative mentions the name Ch'oe Tŏkho). He grapples with profound despair stemming from multiple sources: his disillusionment with a bureaucratic job he considers morally compromised and subservient to colonial power ("a hired employee of a lower government office in a colony"), the painful end of his relationship with Yŏngae who chose another man (Ch'ŏlsu), and a deep ideological crisis where his lofty ideals about human progress and his mission seem untenable against the harsh realities he perceives and the criticisms of friends like Hyŏngin. Feeling stripped of hope, passion ("heat"), and purpose, he ultimately decides to commit suicide by ingesting lye.

From a historical perspective, this story is significant as it vividly portrays the intense psychological and ideological struggles faced by young Korean intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period. The protagonist's anguish reflects a common sentiment of hopelessness, alienation, and moral conflict experienced under oppressive colonial rule and within a rapidly changing society. His critique of his job as collaboration, his internal debates about idealism versus perceived reality (including critiques of capitalism and reflections on communism/revolution denoted by 'OO'), and his ultimate despair offer historians insights into the intellectual climate, the personal cost of colonial subjugation, and the complex ways individuals navigated identity, ideology, love, and existential meaning in 1920s Korea. The censorship indicated by "OOO" and "OO" also hints at the political sensitivity surrounding critiques of the colonial system or discussion of radical ideologies.

젊은 理想主義者의 死
						八峰
10月 25日 晴
어머니와 형수와 동생과 집안의 왼갓 세간은 오늘 아츰 車로 平澤으로 갓다. 午後에 나는 짐을 가지고 이곳 相洙의 집압헤 잇는 下宿으로 왓다. 내가 萬一, 事務所의 일이 밧브고, 不自由한 몸이라는 말을 强硬하게 고집하지만 아니 햇드면 어머니는 나를 끌고서 平澤까지 긔여코 갓슬 넌지도 몰낫섯다.
저녁 후에 相洙와 炯仁이가 와서 놀다가 조곰 前에 도라갓다. 왼 종일 부산하야 갈피를 못채리든 정신이 동모들이 도라간 뒤에 冊床을 정돈하야 노홈에 따라 차차 조곰식 맑아지고 가라안고 조용하야젓다. 아아! 생각하고 보니 도모지 나의 압길이 캄캄하야 엇더케 되여갈는지 알 수 업는 듯한 늣김이 이러나는 것을 깨닷겟다. 나의 압길에는 웬일인지 무엇인지 시커만 그림자가 끼여 잇는 것 가튼 조치 못한 運命에 遭遇할 듯 십흔 不吉한 豫感이 불일 듯 이러나는 것을 깨닷겟다. 果然, 나는 이제로부터 엇더케 되여갈꼬? 사랑하든 英愛는 나를 追放하엿다. 家族-家族이라야 나는 本來부터 그들을 대단하게, 그러타 눈꼽만콤도 마음으로 그들을 대단하게 생각하여 오지는 안엇지마는 - 하여간 가족들은 싀골로 갓다. 나는 혼자서 그 알녕한 職業을 生命의 닷줄삼어 붓잡고 잇스면서 그럭저럭 사러가지 아니하면 안되게 되엿다. 그러나 어느때까지 이따위 거짓투성이인 卑屈한 忍從的 生活을 게속하여 갈는 지는 내 自身도 알 수 업다. 果然 나는 엇더케 되여가나...? 엇더케 하여야 하나...? ＜34＞
아아, 압길이 캄캄할 뿐이다. 나는 될 수 잇는 대로 速히 지금의 이 職業을 버리어야만 하겟거늘, 그것이 되지 못하게 無限한 勇氣를 要求하는 것이 되여서 쉬웁게 斷行하지를 못하고 잇다. 대개 내가 事務所에 가서 하는 일은 나의 마음에도 업는 일을, 한달에 月給 40圓으로 말미암아 억지로 써 놋는 일이다. 말하자면 나는 良心에 버서나는 일을 하고 잇는 不純한 사람이라. 나는 植民地의 下級官廳의 雇員이다! 내가 本來에 이와 가튼 職業을 要望하엿슴은 아니지마는 맛참 나에게 붓잡힐 수 잇든 職業이라고는 이것 하나 밧게 업섯든 까닭에 나는 이거나마도 놋칠까 바서 붓들어 노앗다. 내가 이 職業을 붓잡고 지내온지 지금껏 五十日이 다 못되얏지마는 그 동안에 내 생각에는 한 三年 지난 것 갓치 늣기여 진다. 나는 한량도 모르게 지금의 이 職業에 멀미가 낫다. 모든 것은 OOO의, 뿌르좌-의 城廓을 持久케하는 道具를 讚美하고, 그것에 服從하고 그것을 保護하는 所任을 차근차근하게 記錄하여 놋는 것이다.
나는 이 職業에 對한 說明을 더 쓰지 안켓다. 그것은 너무도 明瞭하게 사람 사람들이 알고 잇는 것이닛가. - 그리하야 나는 이 自覺잇는 청년은 일종의 侮辱을 가지고서 對하는 이 職業人이 되여 가지고 내 우에 잇는 놈이 命令하는대로 指示하는대로 規則的으로 내 良心이 排斥하는 글을 써 놋코 잇다. 비유하야 말하면 OO地方의 백성들이 OOOO를 讚仰한다는 報告書를 쓰는 것과 똑 가튼 記錄을 하고 잇다. 이믜 이 모양이면, 나는 벌서 사람은 아니고 저 사람들이 자긔가 쓰고 십흘 때 자긔의 손으로 펜을 쥐고서 그 쓰고자 하든 바를 쓸 때의 그들의 손에 쥐이여 저잇는  다만 잉크를 무텨 가지고 손이 도러가는 대로 놀니여지면서 흰조희에 글자를 써 놋는 그 펜이나 다름이 업다. 電氣裝置로 물을 자아 올니는 품푸와 가티 나는 그들이 지여노흔 規則範圍 안에서 그들이 하고자 하는 글字를 조희 우에 써 놋는 것이다. 그럼으로 나는 펜이다. 털 필촉이다! 털필촉 밧게는 아모 것도 되지 못한다!
슬프다. 이것이 웬일이냐, 이 現象을 무엇으로써 說明하면 조으냐. 사람들은 반다시 각긔 지긔들의 努力의 代價로써 生活하여야 할 것은 勿論이다. 手足을 가만히 두고 一平生을 安樂椅子에 기대여 누어서 高慢한 生活을 하는 사람은 容恕할 수 업는 노력의 窃盜者다. 勞働은 모든 사람에게 賦與되어야만 한다. 그것이 사람으로서의 ＜35＞ 生活法則이다. 그러나 나는 이와 가튼 信念을 가지고 내 自身의 鬱憤을 달내고 위로할 수는 업다. 웨? 나는 너머도 强制當하는 勞働에 壓迫되여 잇스닛가! 勿論 勞働은 神聖하다. 그러나 그 勞働이 엇더한 權力에 依하야 或은 間接으로 權力의 所致로 因하야 强制當함으로 不得已 從事하게 되는 良心이 許諾지 안는 勞働일 때에 神聖하여야만 할 노동은 가장 咀呪할 만한 대상이 되고 만다. 그리하야 나는 이 현상을 가리처 근대생활의 특수성이라고 한다. 오늘날의 朝鮮의 現實-그것은 이 現狀이 서로 얼키고 설키고한 것에 지나지 못한다. 그리하야 나는 그가 온대에서 生死의 境界線에서 悶絶할 지경에 잇다.
나는 무엇을 할까? 다행히 내가 떠 맛흔 생활을 보장하야 주지 아니하면 안 될 사람은 업스니 나 한 몸이야 설마 이 세상에서 살지 못할 리치는 업스니 고만 사무소의 일을 집어 내버리고 浪人이 될까? 집에서는 설마 서울서 밥 사먹고 잇는 나더러 다달이 돈을 보내라고는 하지 안으렷다. 그러면 나는 이곳의 밥감 15圓만 잇스면 그 外의 것이야 엇더케 하든지 지낼 수 잇지 안은가? 그러타, 그러면 나는 이 職業을 버리자. 나늬 生命이 잇는 날까지 感激과 情熱이 업서지는 날까지 나는 나의 理想에 對하야 眞實한 生活을 하여야한다. 나는 決단코 뿌르좌-의 끄나풀이 아니다. 더구나 저것들의 끄나풀은 아니라고 밋는다. 그러커늘 엇지하야 나는 그와 가튼 일을 털필촉이라 할 바의 일을 할 필요가 잇느냐. 나는 다만 나의 至高한 精神의 命令에 依하야 現實 우에 勇敢히 서나갈 뿐이라고 생각한다. 아아, 마음이 너그러워진다. 가슴 속에서 봄바람에 이는 바다의 물결 소래와 가튼 상쾌한 소래가 들닌다. 씩씩하다. 긔운이 난다.
그러나 나는 갑헐하세 感激한다든지 咏嘆한다든지 하여서는 안된다. 나는 무엇보다도 嚴肅한 태도로 現實을 드려다 보고 잇서야한다. 나는 센멘틔탈이스트가 되여서는 못쓴다.
밤이 고요하다.

英愛? - 나는 英愛와 알고 지내든 滿4個月間 일을 니저 버리지 못하고 잇는 것이 事實이다. 엇그적게는 4個月 前의 묵은 日記를 뒤저 보기까지 하엿다. 英愛의 생각만하면 나는 밋칠 듯하다. 지금도 나는 잠을 못자고 이러케 몸부림을 한다. 오오, 오오-
『德浩氏 나는 당신을 사랑함니다. 당신을 사랑하지 안는 것이 아님니다. 그러나 동시에 저는 哲洙氏를 사랑하지 아니 할 수 업슴니다. 녜? 哲洙氏는 저를 이러케 크게 사랑하고 게신데요, 제가 엇더케 哲洙氏를 사랑하지 안케슴닛가? 그런 까닭에 저는 哲洙氏를 사랑함니다. 그리고 당신도 저바리지는 안켓슴니다. 容恕하십시요. 저의 가슴은 큼니다. 넓음니다. 저를 사랑하야 주시는 사람은 모도다 저의 愛人임니다. 그만콤 저의 가슴은 넓음니다. 당신도 사랑할 수 잇스며 哲洙氏도 사랑하여 드릴 수 잇슴니다. 德浩氏, 德浩氏는 제가 가슴이 넓다는 것을 낫브다, 罪다, 라고 말슴하심닛가? 그러하면 섭섭함니다 마는 당신은 저를 사랑하실 줄 몰느는 양반이심니다. 저를 사랑하실 자격이 업스신 어룬임니다. 저는 그런 사람에게서 사랑을 밧고 십지 안슴니다. 왜라니요? 저는 그와 가튼 편벽한 사랑을 할 줄 몰나요. 저의 자유를 구속하는 사람을 저는 실혀합니다. 그 뿐임니다. 아라서 하십시요. 당신을 사랑하는 英愛로부터-』 ＜37＞

언제인가 英愛는 나더러
『당신의 얼골은 영특하게 생기섯서요』
하고서 고개를 갸옷동하게 눈을 위로 떠서 나를 보고 웃든 일이 잇섯다. 나는 지금 그것을 생각하얏다. 생각하면 그때의 이 英愛의 말은 나를 놀니든 말이 엿든 모양이다.
그때가 지금 생각하고 보면 6月 29日이엿스닛가 지금으로부터 116日 前이다. 그리하야 지금 116日 後 지금은 나는 英愛와 딴 남이 되고 마럿다. 나의 理想의 表象이요, 나의 「幸福의 어머니」라고 까지 미더오든 英愛와 나는 딴 남이 되고 마럿다. 끈어지는 듯한 지극한 그리운 생각과 뼈꼴이 저리여지는 듯한 애닯은 마음이 샘물과 갓게도 용소슴할 때에는, 나는 정말이지 붓그러운 말이나, 나의 大頸脈을 끈코십흔 밋칠 듯한  생각도 난다. 언제는 죽지 아니할 몸이랴, 이, 참을 수업는 괴로움을 니저 버리기 爲하야는 목숨을 끈어 버리는 것이 찰하리 편하겟다는 것 잡을 수 업는 불낄과 갓흔, 밋처 날뛰는 마음이 全身을 襲擊하는 것이다. 아아, 그러나 나는, 나는, 아직은 죽기 어려웁다. 나의 머리 속에 한 오락지의 希望이 남어 잇는 以上에는 나의 가슴 속에 아직도 조곰은 人生에 ＜39＞ 對한 感激과 情熱이 잇는 以上에는 나는 아직 죽지 못한다. 나는 비록 아모리 힘이 업는 인간일지라도 내가 한 개의 사람으로서 가지고 잇는 使命은 重大하다고 自信한다. 그러타, 엇지 이 몸에게 한 개의 사람, 한 개의 朝鮮 사람으로서의 使命이 업슬까 보냐.
그러면 나의 使命은 무엇이냐? 그것은 새로 자랴난 朝鮮의 靑年들에게는 全部 나누어 갓게 되여 잇는 同一한, 重大한 使命이다. 그 使命은 進化學으로써 保證바든 人類의 進化를 促成하는 崇高한 理想의 세계를 地上에 建設하도록 活動하는 것이다. 現代社會의 不正은 現代人類의 未開로 말미암아 잇게 된 것이다. 그럼으로 사람이 어느 때에든지 結局 完成品이 된 때에는 世界는 樂園이 될 것이다. 卽 全世界의 人類가 目標삼고 向하야 가는 그 究竟의 到着地인 그 포인트에 到着되는 날은 잇다는 것이다. 우리들의 使命은 이것에 이르기까지 全人類를 分擔하야 促進케 하는 일일 뿐이다. 이것은 歷史가 保證하는 바 眞理라고 밋는다.
그러나 나는 이 생각은 結局 安價한 精神主義者의 한 개의 유토피아에 지나지 안는다는 非難과 輕蔑을 만히 바더왓다. 그러치만 나는 지금의 나에게서 이와 갓흔 人類進化의 프로그람을 업시하여 버리면 더군다나 하로도 더 살 수 업다. 그만콤 나의 理想은 나의 生命이나 맛찬가지 엇던 사람은 나를 톨스토이안이라고 한다. 그러나 혼자는 스사로 톨스토이안은 아닌 줄로 밋고 잇다. 나는 하나님에게 밋치지는 아니하엿스닛가. 또 엇던 사람은 나들 가라처 로-란디스트라고 한다. 그리 이 말은 그다지 틀니지 안은 말이다. 事實, 나는 로-란에게 共鳴하는 점이 만흐닛가. 그리하야 나는 한입으로 정신주의자라는 배척을 밧는다. 그러나 나의 信念은 그것으로 因하야 움즈기지 안엇스며, 또한 압흐로 도움 즈기지 아니 하리라고 自信한하.

그의 日記는 여긔까지, 12月 20日까지 밧게는 업다. 이, 최후의 日記의 끗장에는 무엇인지 하나 가득하게 썻다가 다시 글짜 한 자를 아러보지 못할 만콤 잉크로 지워 노앗다.
그리고 이, 리상주의자는 자긔 동무 相洙와 자긔의 형과 아버지에게 유서를 한 장식 써 놋코 죽엇는데 그의 일긔 책 틈에는
『最後로 英愛에게-.
英愛야! 哲洙氏의 夫人인 英愛야! 나는... 』이 만큼 쓰다가 말고서, 다시 그 위를 텰필로 죽죽 그어버린 한 장의 조희조각이 드러잇섯다. 나는 이것을 이, 리상주의자가 최후로 자긔의 전의 애인에게 유서 비슷한 것을 쓸녀고 하다가 다시, 불필요한 것이라고 생각하고서 고만 둔 것이라고 추칙하얏다. 그는 말하자면, 그의 약한 셩격으로 인하야, 또는 정신주의에 너무도 양염을 바덧든 탓으로 헛되히 리상만 찻다가 현실과 리상과의 배치로 인하야 자긔의 목숨을 끈은 것이라고, 나는 생각하엿다. 그러나, 나는 이 젊어가지고  애닯게도 죽어 버린 동무를 닛치지 못한다. 그의 일긔는 지금도 내가 대단히 보관하고 잇다.
(끗) ＜48＞
