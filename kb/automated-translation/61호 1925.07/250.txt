**Body Text Translation:**
抱石
Badugi Is Without Falsehood

Badugi is without falsehood
When he sees someone he dislikes, he barks that he dislikes them,
When he sees someone he's fond of, he jumps about saying he likes them,
Badugi is truly without falsehood in his heart.

But humans, why are they like this?
When they see someone hateful, they smile and hold hands,
When they see those they cherish, they deliberately act aloof,
Badugi, oh why
Do you not betray these wretched humans?

Badugi is without falsehood, but
However, these wretched humans possess falsehood. <116>

Little Baby

The little baby is a cute baby sent from the land of the sun,
A father's heart, angered as fiercely as frost,
Disappears completely with one smile from that baby.

The little baby is a miraculous baby sent from the land of power,
Even the father's sword of nihilism, meant to destroy the world,
Is thrown down before that baby's cry.

Behold, forever that baby
Mends the heart of the Earth about to burst
With its soft hands,
Supports the wheel of life about to fall
Standing firm with its small arms,
Points the way to a far, far-off land
With its lovely hands.

Then, baby, what should we do? <117>
What should we give you?
Shall we pluck that star from yonder sky?
That's right, let's go pluck the stars, go pluck the stars,
Forever and ever, let's go pluck the stars.

Thus, in this Universe,
Fatherhood pursues Childhood, and Childhood follows Fatherhood,
There is laughter within tears,
And love within hate,
Becoming an eternal Round Dance and "Symphony,"
Painfully, the wheel of life rolls onward.
1924.9.29. <118>
<116-118>

**Summary**

The text consists of two poems by Posŏk (抱石). The first, "Badugi Is Without Falsehood," contrasts the honest emotional expression of a dog (Badugi) with the hypocrisy and deceitfulness of humans. The second poem, "Little Baby," celebrates the profound innocence and power of a baby, depicting it as a divine messenger capable of disarming anger, stopping destruction, mending the world, supporting life, and guiding humanity towards a hopeful future, culminating in an image of an eternal cycle where Fatherhood and Childhood interact in a universal "Symphony." From a historical perspective, these poems, published during the Japanese colonial period, are interesting for their implicit social critique (human falsehood) and their strong emphasis on the purity and redemptive power of children. The latter theme, focusing on the baby as a symbol of hope, guidance, and the continuation of life, could reflect a yearning for innocence, renewal, and a brighter future for the nation amidst the difficulties of colonial rule.

抱石
바둑이는 거짓이 업나니
바둑이는 거짓이 업나니
그는 실흔 이를 볼때 실타고 짓으며
정든 이를 볼때 조타고 가로 뛰나니
바둑이는 이다지도 마음의 거짓이 업나니라.

그러나 인간은 이 어이함인지
미운 이를 볼 때 웃으며 손잡고
귀여운 이들 불 때 짐짓이 빼나니,
바둑아 너는 왜
이 몹슬 인간을 배반치 안느뇨.

바둑이는 거짓이 업나니나
그러나 이 몹슬 인간에게는 거짓이 잇나니.＜116＞

어린아기
어린아기는 해의 나라에서 보낸 귀여운 아기니
서리ㅅ발 카티 무섭게 성낸 아버지의 마음이
그 아기 웃음 한번에 사라지고 마나니.

어린아기는 힘의 나라에서 보낸 신통한 아기니
세상을 뭇지르랴는 아버지의 허무의 칼날도
그 아기 울음 압헤는 그만 던저지고 마나니.

보라 영원히 그 아기는
터지랴는 지구의 심장을
보드러운 손으로 꼬매여주며
너머지랴는 생명의 박휘를
작은 팔로 뻣대고 서셔
머나머-ㄴ 나라의 길을
어엽분 손으로 가르처 주나니.

그러면 아기야 우리는 엇지 하여야 조흐랴＜117＞
네게 무엇을 주어야 조흐랴
저긔 저 한울의 별을 따주랴
올토다 별따러 가자 별따러가
영원히 영원히 별따러가자.

이리하야 이 宇宙에
父性은 子性을 쫏고 子性은 父性을 따라
울음속에 웃음이 잇고
미움속에 사랑이 잇서
영원한 圓舞와 「심포니」가 되여
압푸게도 생명의 박휘는 굴너가나니.
1924.9.29.＜118＞
