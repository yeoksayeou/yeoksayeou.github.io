Article Title: 朝鮮産業과 그 價値 (Chosŏn Industry and Its Value)

Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

Chosŏn Industry and Its Value
						Sŏnu Chŏn

Things to boast about in Chosŏn industry! Could there possibly be anything to boast about in Chosŏn industry? I think this is something often said even by Chosŏn people who do not know Chosŏn well. Today's Chosŏn is not a hidden Chosŏn, and Chosŏn people are not hidden people either. Indeed, that is so. The world has come to know the good and bad of Chosŏn, the handsome and ugly of Chosŏn people. However, for us who are Chosŏn people, the more Chosŏn becomes widely introduced to the world, the more foreigners get to know us well, the more we feel the shame of being Chosŏn people. If anyone were to say, what is there to be so ashamed of for being a Chosŏn person?, I would say that is not a sincere statement but a lie. If it were a sincere statement, why is it that when they encounter foreigners where no other Chosŏn people are present, they do not speak the Chosŏn language and try their best not to reveal any Chosŏn scent? This is not a lie but a fact, something often seen even within Chosŏn, and if you go abroad, there are indeed many Chosŏn people who have become fake Japanese people, and many who have become fake Chinese people. If Chosŏn people had been born as the white race, I think many would have become fake British people or fake American people. If we speak of Chosŏn's points of pride to those who are ashamed of being Chosŏn people, they will laugh, asking how that could possibly be a point of pride for Chosŏn. However, from an industrial perspective, I cannot help but introduce a few things that could be considered points of pride for Chosŏn.

Before listing the points of pride for Chosŏn from an industrial perspective, I want to say this first. Generally, regarding various systems, customs, or all kinds of craftworks, products, and manufactured goods, there are those that were good from the beginning and remained good until the end; there are those that were initially imperfect but became better through gradual improvement and development; and there are those that were initially good but, due to a lack of improvement and refinement in the middle, actually deteriorated rather than progressed.

Similarly, putting aside systems related to political culture <25> or customs initiated in Chosŏn, looking from the economic and industrial side, among various systems, craftworks, products, and manufactured goods, there are many things that were initially good but, due to some reason, failed to be improved midway and instead regressed. Moreover, rather than saying they regressed, quite a few simply collapsed without a trace (表跡업시). This is true for many systems, many crafts, and many products. Wasn't the double-entry bookkeeping (複式簿記法) method, which we are trying to learn today, once developed in Songdo? And isn't the Yuk'ŭijŏn (六矣廛) system, rarely seen in other countries, valuable as data for Chosŏn's unique economic civilization? Besides these, there must be countless other unique Chosŏn industrial and economic systems that have collapsed by today, making them impossible for us to find even if we search. There must also be many such cases among industrial products. If we ask what cause led to this, it wasn't because the systems, industries, or products themselves were imperfect or inferior, but because our ancestors and we failed to develop and improve them. Considering this, we cannot help but feel that the responsibility for the decline (衰殘) of Chosŏn's unique economic culture today lies not elsewhere but with us.

Of course, I am not stubbornly trying to seek out the systems of old, nor am I suggesting we return to the customs of old. As someone who strives to adopt new systems and customs suitable for the new era as much as possible, utilize them as my own, and transform them into my possession, the reason I speak like this is because I deeply lament (悲感) the current situation where, from our ancestors' time down to ours, we believed only our own things were bad and others' things were good, discarding our own and imitating only others', yet failing to grasp the essence and only mimicking the surface, ultimately losing our own things and failing to properly imitate others', ending up in this ambiguous state and eventually facing ruin (亡).

If there is still something that has maintained its value among those things we ourselves deemed bad, deliberately tried to hide, forget, and discard, and never knew how to boast about, then that would truly be not just a treasure (珍寶) of Chosŏn, but a treasure of the world. And indeed, while we failed to recognize their value and did not know how to improve them, there are several famous things whose value foreigners recognized, improved, encouraged, made their own, and now present as their own points of pride. What are these? They originate in Chosŏn and are made by Chosŏn people, but the commercial rights <26> have already left the hands of Chosŏn people and gone to foreigners. They are introduced abroad through foreign hands, and in a "cuckoo takes over the magpie's nest" (鵲巢鳩居) fashion, we receive supplies from foreigners. Foreign trademarks are attached, and they are bought, sold, and consumed. These are Chosŏn rice (朝鮮米), Chosŏn ginseng (朝鮮人蔘), Chosŏn cattle (朝鮮牛), Chosŏn silk cocoons (朝鮮蠶繭), and various processed goods made using by-products of these and other products (其以外諸産物) as raw materials. Are these the only products Chosŏn can be proud of? There must be many others. However, besides the fact that Chosŏn people pay no attention to them, because they are unnecessary for the lives of foreigners and unsuitable for their uses, they too are ignored, eventually collapsing and disappearing, so much so that they cannot be found now, having vanished without a trace (無㾗跡絶種). If they had been necessary for the lifestyle and uses of foreigners—setting aside whether they were needed by the unfortunate Chosŏn people—they would have undergone many times more improvement, achieved many times more progress, and seen many times more increase in production. Consequently, Chosŏn's wealth (富力) would have increased many times over compared to today. However, Chosŏn people clamor to just discard anything called "Chosŏn-made," regardless of form (形), shape (樣), or quality (品質). Even the so-called authorities (當局者) who claim to promote Chosŏn industry pay no attention whatsoever to whether things are necessary for the lives and uses of Chosŏn people, only improving and promoting what is necessary for their own lives and uses. Therefore, all the things that are truly our points of pride have collapsed and gone extinct (絶種).

How can the foundation of life be secure for people who want to live by discarding their own things and relying only on others'? While we cry out about the hardships of life (生活難), we are even more pathetic for being so insensitive to the fundamental reasons why such hardships arise. Isn't the hardship of life worsening because we discard our own things, which our hands made, are cheap, and convenient to use (from the perspective of usage habits), and try to eat, wear (닙고), use, and live (사라가려닛가) with others' things that are expensive and unfamiliar to use? Promote domestic products (국산)! Strive to use self-made products (自作品)! Cherish our own products (自品)! These are words shouted from our side and also shouted from the side of the authorities. However, because the types of promoted products, used products, and cherished products are largely limited <27> to those necessary for other people's lives rather than those directly necessary for our lives, the promotion of domestic products is not for our benefit, and the call to cherish our own products seems not to be for us either.

From an economic perspective, there must be many things that could be our points of pride, but only a few that are useful for foreigners remain. All the things directly necessary for us and truly worthy of being points of pride have collapsed and disappeared because we did not pay attention to them. From now on, let us seek our livelihood ourselves, reveal the miracles of our past industrial development to the world, supplement the needs of others with our unique products (輸出獎勵 - export promotion), and ultimately, to escape our shame, let us find what is hidden of ours, raise up what has collapsed, further improve and develop what exists, and even if something is lacking, acquire it from other countries to make our own things. Let's make all of these our points of pride. <28>

**Summary**

The author, Sŏnu Chŏn, writing in 1925, laments the state of Korean industry and the mindset of Koreans under colonial rule. He criticizes the tendency among Koreans to feel ashamed of their identity and heritage, discarding native products, systems (like Songdo bookkeeping and the Yuk'ŭijŏn), and crafts in favor of foreign ones, often adopted superficially. He points out that valuable Korean resources like rice, ginseng, cattle, and silk cocoons are recognized and exploited by foreigners (implicitly the Japanese), who profit from them while Koreans neglect their potential. The author argues this self-deprecation and neglect of indigenous resources is a root cause of economic hardship and urges Koreans to rediscover, revive, develop, and take pride in their own industries and products for economic self-sufficiency and national dignity.

Historically, this article is significant as it voices Korean economic nationalism and cultural consciousness during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the psychological impact of colonialism (feelings of shame, inferiority complex), the critique of uncritical adoption of foreign ways, and the economic reality of resource exploitation. The specific mention of traditional economic practices like Songdo bookkeeping and the Yuk'ŭijŏn reflects an attempt to construct a narrative of indigenous Korean capability counteracting colonial narratives. Historians might find this piece valuable for understanding intellectual responses to colonialism, internal debates on modernization and tradition, and the call for economic self-strengthening in 1920s Korea.

朝鮮産業과 그 價値
						鮮于全
朝鮮 산업의 자랑꺼리! 朝鮮 산업에도 무슨 자랑꺼리가 잇겟슴니까? 이것은 朝鮮 사람으로도 朝鮮을 잘 아지못하는 사람들이 흔히 하는 말일줄로 생각한다. 오늘날의 朝鮮은 숨어잇는 朝鮮이 안이며 朝鮮 사람들도 수머잇는 朝鮮 사람이 안이다. 과연 그럿타. 朝鮮이 잘난 것 못난 것 朝鮮 사람이 잘생긴 것 못생긴 것 세상에서 다 알게 되엿다. 그러나 朝鮮 사람된 우리로서는 朝鮮이 세상에 널리 소개되여 갈수록 외국인들이 우리를 잘 아라갈수록 우리는 朝鮮 사람된 붓그러움을 더욱더욱 늣기게 된다. 만일 누구가 우리가 朝鮮 사람이 되엇기로서 무엇이 그다지 붓그러울 것이 잇겟느냐고 말한다 하면 그것은 진정의 말이 안이오 거즛말이라고 하겟다. 그것이 진정의 말 갓흐면 엇지 하여서 그네들이 朝鮮 사람 업는데서 외국사람을 대할 때에 말도 朝鮮 말을 쓰지 안이하고 될 수 잇는 대로 朝鮮 내음새를 나타내이지 안이하랴고 애를 쓰는가. 이것은 거즛이 아니고 사실일지니 朝鮮에서도 만히 보는 바이여니와 외국에 가서 보면 참말 朝鮮 사람으로 가짜 日本사람된 사람도 만히 잇고 가짜 中國사람된 사람도 만히 잇다. 만일 朝鮮 사람이 백색인종으로 생겨낫드면 가짜 英國 사람 가짜 米國 사람될 사람도 만히 생겨낫슬 쥴로 생각한다. 朝鮮사람된 것을 붓그러워하는 그네들에게 朝鮮의 자랑꺼리를 말하면 그녜들은 그것이 무슨 朝鮮에 자랑꺼리가 되느냐고 우슬 것이다. 그러나 나는 산업상으로 보아서 朝鮮에 자랑꺼리가 될만한 것 멧 가지를 소개하지 아니 할 수 업다.
산업상으로 보아서 朝鮮에 자랑꺼리를 들기 전에 나는 이러한 것을 몬저 말하려 한다. 대체 엇더한 제도 엇더한 습관 또는 온갓 공예품 산물 제품이 최초부터 조와가지고 끗까지 조흔 것도 잇고 최초에는 불완전하든 것이 점점 개량되고 발달됨을 따라 일층 조와진 것도 잇고 최초에는 조튼 것이 중간에 이서서 개량되지 못하고 정화되지 못한 까닭에 도리여 진보되야 조치 못하게 된 것도 잇다는 그것이다.
그와가티 朝鮮에서 창시된 정치문화에 관한 제도라든지＜25＞ 습관은 둘재로 하고 경제편으로 산업편으로 보아서 여러 가지 제도 공예품 산물 제품 중에는 최초에는 조왓든 것이 중간에 니르러서 엇더한 원인에 의하야 개량되지 못하고 도리여 퇴보된 것이 만히 잇스며 퇴보되엇다느니 보다도 表跡업시 쓰러지고 마는 것도 적지 안이하다. 제도에 이서서 그러한 것이 만히 잇고 공예상에 이서서 그러한 것이 만히 잇고 산물에 이서서 그러한 것이 만히 잇다. 오늘날 우리가 배우러 하는 複式簿記法이 일즉이 松都에서 발달되지를 안이하엿스며 他國에서 見키 難한 六矣廛 制度라든지가 朝鮮 고유의 경제 문명의 자료로 귀중치를 아니한가? 그외에도 朝鮮 고유의 산업제도 경제제도도 오늘날 니르러서는 쓰러지고 말앗기 때문에 우리가 차자 볼내야 차자볼 수 업게된 것이 不知其數일 것이고 산업 산물 중에도 그러한 것이 만을 것이다. 이것이 다 엇더한 원인에서 그러케 되엿는가 하면 그것은 그 제도 그 산업 그 산물 자체가 불완전하고 불미한 까닭으로 그러케 된 것이 아니요 우리의 선조 及 우리가 그것을 발달식히고 개량식히지 못한 까닭이니 그리고 보면 오늘날에 朝鮮에 고유한 경제문화를 衰殘케 한 책임은 他에 잇지를 안이하고 우리에게 잇는 것을 늣기지 안늘 수 업게 된다.
물론 나는 완고스럽게 녯날의 제도를 다시 차즈려하며 녯날의 습관에 다시 도라가자는 것은 안이다. 될 수 잇는대로 新時代에 적합한 새 제도와 새 습관을 취하야 이것을 내의 것으로 이용하며 내의 소유로 化하려고 노력하는 나로서 이러한 말을 하게 된 것은 우리의 선조쩍부터 우리에 니르기까지가 제의 것은 낫브고 남의 것만 조흔 줄 알고 내 것을 버리고 남의 것만 본 밧으되 속은 집십지를 못하고 것만 할다가 필경은 내 것도 일러버리고 남의 것도 본밧지 못하게 되야 이러즁 저러즁 하다가 필경은 亡치게 된 오늘날의 현장을 悲感한 까닭이다.
