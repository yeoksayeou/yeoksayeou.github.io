잡지명	개벽 제61호
발행일	1925년 07월 01일
기사제목	긴 밤이 새여지다 (The Long Night Dawns)
필자	金昌述
기사형태	시 (Poem)

A pitch-dark curtain descended upon this land,
Dimwitted people flounder within it,
Oh Nature, for what reason did you gather these people,
these innocent people, within this nation?＜120＞

Our bodies, becoming afflicted, endure endless mistreatment,
Taste the throbbing of the heart, taste it pitifully.
However, that long night of contradiction,
As the thought "Us too" arose, that long night began to dawn.

"It is trampling, it is crushing,
That gentle heart, thought, the entire soul,
In tenacious hands like an eagle's talons,
The burning breast was bound, was caught."

However, O Newness,
The world, black as ink, is already bright,
The foolish people's eyes are opened.
Oh, the long night dawns, and eyes are opened.

O Newness, if it is a dirty happiness, do not send it,
Rather, within this nation, desire battle (戰鬪),
Ask not of right and wrong,
Just become a people who fight the battle of Justice (正義).＜121＞
<120-121>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Ch'ang-sul, published in 1925, uses the metaphor of a long, dark night ending to depict the awakening consciousness of the Korean people under oppressive conditions. It describes the suffering and helplessness experienced, followed by a dawning realization ("Us too") that breaks the spell of darkness. The poem rejects passive acceptance or compromised "dirty happiness," instead calling for the people to become fighters for justice, regardless of simplistic notions of right and wrong, suggesting a struggle for national liberation or fundamental rights is paramount. Historians would find this poem interesting as an example of cultural resistance and burgeoning nationalist sentiment during the Japanese colonial period. It reflects a shift from despair to active consciousness and a call for struggle, encapsulating the intellectual and emotional climate among some Koreans seeking change. The explicit call for "battle" (戰鬪) and "Justice" (正義) despite potential censorship is notable.

긴 밤이 새여지다
						金昌述
캄캄한 휘장이 이 땅우에 나리엇는데
멍청한 백성들이 그 안에서 허부적으리다,
오 자연이어 너는 무슨 일로 이 사람들을
이 순진한 사람들을 이 나라 안에 모부섯드냐＜120＞

우리의 몸이 붓침이 되어 가이 업는 푸대접에
염통의 북킴을 맛보도다, 애처러이 맛보도다.
그러나, 그 모순의 긴 밤이
「우리도」라는 생각이 들자 그 긴밤이 새여지다.

「짓발핌이로다 짓뭉갬이로다
그 순한 마음이, 생각이, 온령혼이
독수리의 발톱처럼 악착스러운 손에
불타는 가슴을 묵기엇고나 붓잡혓고나」

그러나, 새롭음이어
검향가티 샛캄한 세상이 임의 밝고
우둔한 백성이 눈이 열리다
오 긴 밤이 새여지고, 눈이 열리고.

새롭음이어, 더러운 행복이거든 보내지 말라
차라리 이 나라 안에선 싸홈(戰鬪)을 바래나니
올흠과 글흠은 뭇지도 말고
그저 正義의 쌈싸호는 백성이 되여지라.＜121＞
＜120-121＞
