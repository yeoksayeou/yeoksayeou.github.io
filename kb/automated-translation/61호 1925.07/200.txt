世界의 國家數와 其國軆
(The World's Number of Nations and Their Forms of Government)

Today on Earth, the total number of countries existing as independent nations or possessing elements equivalent to nations is 57. Among these, there are 3 autocratic monarchies, 16 constitutional monarchies, 2 constitutional grand duchies, 1 free city, 34 democratic republics, and 1 socialist communist state.

From this, one can surmise that the forms of state (國軆) or forms of government (政軆) of the various countries and peoples living on this Earth are gradually progressing and renewing themselves, passing through the path from the primitive era (草昧時代) of autocratic monarchy to constitutionalism and republicanism. <86>

**Summary**

This 1925 article counts 57 independent or quasi-independent nations globally, categorizing them by government type: 3 autocratic monarchies, 16 constitutional monarchies, 2 grand duchies, 1 free city, 34 democratic republics, and 1 socialist state (likely the USSR). The author observes a clear historical progression from autocratic rule towards constitutional and republican forms of government. For historians studying Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it reflects a Korean intellectual's awareness of global political trends and implicitly frames political development in terms of stages progressing towards democracy/republicanism, a potentially subtle commentary on Korea's own lack of sovereignty under Japanese rule.
