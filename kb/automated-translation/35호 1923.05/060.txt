Article Title: 開闢運動과 合致되는 朝鮮의 少年運動 (The Korean Youth Movement, Consistent with the Kaebyŏk Movement)
Author: 起瀍 (Ki Ch'an)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

The Korean Youth Movement, Consistent with the Kaebyŏk Movement
						By Ki Ch'an
The Grand Undertaking of the Youth Movement Association = The Ethical Oppression of Korean Youth = Even More Severe Economic Oppression = How Liberation Will Be Achieved = To Those Who Speak of the Youth Problem

The Grand Undertaking of the Youth Movement Association

I hear that officials from various youth organizations in Kyŏngsŏng organized the Youth Movement Association (少年運動協會) on April 17th past, and by contacting numerous youth groups and other social organizations in the provinces, they intend to have the brothers of the 13 provinces of Chosŏn simultaneously raise the banner of the youth movement on the globally significant May 1st.
Regarding the true intention behind the youth movement the association is now advocating, we do not yet know the details (as of April 20th, when this is written). However, since the movement is planned primarily with the youth of Chosŏn as its standard, it is surely beyond doubt, reflecting the special circumstances of today's Korean youth, that it must first aim for "Youth Liberation," and indeed, it must become a reality.
Liberation! Liberation! This word has recently been cried out so much by us Korean people. Not to mention the cries for political liberation and economic liberation, even issues like the "Liberation of Women" have been clamored about until our ears ache. However, for some reason, there has been no significant voice regarding the issue of youth liberation, which is the latent power of today's society and will be the core strength of tomorrow's society. Since two years ago, a few youth organizations have emerged here and there, conveying some news of the youth issue like a voice crying in the wilderness, but it was too faint and too unclear <20> to become a matter of general public opinion and a movement demanding everyone's attention. Seeing such a widespread youth movement arise today is truly the best news among good news. Just hearing the words first fills us with a piece of sincere emotion that leaps up uncontrollably.

The Ethical Oppression of Korean Youth
However, if we speak of liberating youth, we cannot but presuppose the existence of facts that oppress youth. So, how have we Korean people traditionally oppressed youth? The words must inevitably start from here. Thinking deeply, the first thing to count in the traditional oppression of youth is ethical oppression. No matter what religion we believe in or what ideology we speak of today, 99 out of 100 of our actual social lives do not step beyond the ethics of Confucianism. However, Confucian ethics do not observe people as they are but analyze them in various ways, discovering the Three Bonds (三網) of ruler, father, and husband among them, and subjugating the remaining masses to them. Furthermore, by creating something like the mesh of the Five Moral Rules (五倫), they prohibited general escape. The golden saying "Order between elder and younger" (長幼有序), which is the sole moral or ethical basis for youth oppression, is precisely one of these Five Moral Rules.
Quietly considering the circumstances, the Five Moral Rules were made subordinate to the Three Bonds. Among the Three Bonds, the husband was made subordinate to the father, the father to the ruler, the ruler to Heaven (天), and Heaven was considered a kind of perfectly finished product, from which all norms were to be taken. However, since Heaven has neither voice nor smell, the will of the monarch, who claimed to inherit Heaven and establish the standard, came to represent the will of Heaven. Yao, Shun, Yu, Tang, Wen, Wu, and the Duke of Zhou were precisely these figures, and people like Confucius (孔子) became sages, residing almost in the same rank as them, by diligently transmitting and modeling themselves after them. That is, they became great sages endowed by Heaven. Thereafter, emperors, scholars, and the masses who lived centered on the teachings of the Confucian way did nothing more than repeat that formula monotonously. That is, all emperors looked up to Yao and Shun, making it their ultimate ideal to become emperors only slightly inferior to Yao and Shun; all scholars looked up to Confucius, making it their ultimate goal to become sages only slightly inferior to Confucius; and all other masses merely continued their daily, cookie-cutter lives under the guidance and authority of the emperors and gentlemen of their respective times. If anyone took even one step outside of this, they would inevitably become victims under the label of "heretic."<21>
In a word, until now, in the minds of us (steeped in Confucian edification), there was nothing but the past—faith in the past. The words of Laozi (老子), "Grasp the way of antiquity, to manage the affairs of the present" (執古之道, 以御今之有), perfectly symbolize these circumstances. If we ever had a present or future that was not the past, it was a present or future molded from the great model of the past. In other words, it was a present and future that were extensions of the past. In such times—even until today—when transmitting the past was considered the sole purpose of life, the person who was best at flattering the past and closest in connection to the past held the highest social position. However, among humans, the adult, not the child, is the person most closely connected to the past and possesses the most knowledge about the past. Consequently, the adult received the highest position and the most allowances in that society, while the "child," who is not an "adult," was treated as nothing. Fundamentally, their personhood was denied. Their entire existence had meaning only in being a plaything for adults, only in being a messenger for adults. Since the personhood of children was fundamentally denied in this way, even in daily interactions, there might have been affection for them, but there was no respect. That affection was like the affection a master has for his dogs and horses, or the affection dogs and horses have for their offspring. That is, they were loved because they were cute, loved because they were pitiful, and loved because they were recognized as one's own possession. Truly, how shallow and vulgar was this love!
Thinking about how adults ignored children in traditional society is truly suffocating. First, in the language used daily, they were treated as lower beings. Adults invariably treated children condescendingly, and children invariably treated adults respectfully. In all procedures related to movement, dwelling, sitting, reclining, clothing, and food, adults and children were always distinguished, placing adults first and children second. For example, when walking on the road, children were told to stand behind (walking ahead of elders was called impiety - 疾行先長者를 謂之不悌), and when eating food, children were told to eat only after the adults had finished.
Coming-of-age, marriage, funerals, and ancestral rites were the most important ceremonies in traditional social rituals. However, not a single one concerning children was included among these rituals. While coming-of-age and marriage ceremonies might exclude children due to the nature of the rituals themselves, there is certainly room for consideration regarding funerals and ancestral rites. Yet, are children present in funeral rites? <22> Are children present in ancestral rites? When children die, they are simply wrapped in straw mats or oil paper scraps and thrown out. There is no ceremony whatsoever, and no remembrance whatsoever. While adults have mourning clothes worn for several years and ancestral rites performed for several generations, for children, there isn't even a single day of mourning clothes or a single instance of ancestral rites. This is not to say that mourning clothes must be worn or ancestral rites must be performed, but rather that the customary treatment of children in the past was so crudely done.
There are countless examples, but to put it in one word, we Eastern people (東洋사람들) have hidden a great ghostly relic called Heaven behind our backs and, in front of it, began to analyze what we call humanity. Numerous titles such as ruler, subject, husband, wife, elder, younger, old, young, male, female, gentleman, commoner, poor, rich, father, son, ancestor, descendant, uncle, nephew, older brother, younger brother are nothing more than large and small fragments resulting from this analysis. Among them, the ruler became the largest piece, and the child turned out to be the smallest piece. However, the piece called 'child' was so small that it wasn't even considered among the mourning obligations for ordinary people. Truly, until now, children were completely invisible in people's eyes.
Whether things had to be this way due to the circumstances of the time, or whether it was done with the malicious intent of protecting the interests of a few special classes by creating it this way, is still a separate question. Regardless, the fact that the traditional social system analyzed humanity and placed the child class at the very bottom was truly unfortunate (人事不祥). If we maintain such a social system for even one day, we will suffer retribution for that day. Thinking about it makes one's hair stand on end.

Even More Severe Economic Oppression
The second thing to consider is economic oppression (the first being ethical oppression). First, the minds of children are corroded by ethical oppression, and then their bodies are destroyed by economic oppression. To speak truthfully, the economic oppression imposed on children in today's society leads to the complete ruin of their minds and bodies. This is none other than the fact that the hardship of life for propertyless families, stemming from the current social system, directly affects the children in those families, forcing those children, who should be playing joyfully and learning to their fullest capacity, to unfortunately labor and suffer. <23> Thinking about the economic oppression weighing down on the heads of these propertyless children in Chosŏn today reveals a truly stifling situation. Being unable to study because there is no school is an unspeakably monstrous situation, but isn't the situation even more monstrous where a school exists right next door, yet one cannot study because there is nothing to eat? Brothers, do you know how many of our children today cannot study, even though schools exist, because they have nothing to eat? The majority of them are currently ruining their own boundless future prospects due to daily wages of a few pennies (몃 分 錢) and worthless labor (즈즐치 못한 勞役). However, while it is true that this problem cannot be resolved without fundamentally reforming today's social economic system from the ground up, and its locus of resolution is not simple, it goes without saying that we cannot neglect its resolution simply because the problem is not simple.

How Liberation Will Be Achieved
Until now, we have oppressed countless children ethically and economically in this way. Thus, we ourselves have blocked our path for tomorrow, and we ourselves have denied our future light. We have repeatedly made such grave errors until now.
Brothers, in today's bright world (大明天地), should we repeat such errors again? Even today, when Heaven is opening and Earth is cracking (天이 開하고 地가 闢하야), and the world's cultural fortunes are about to be fundamentally overturned, should we repeat such errors again? No. No. They must be fundamentally liberated. First, liberate them ethically, and then liberate them economically. Let us realize that children are not human scraps (屑) or fragments (破片), but, comparing them to grass, they are sprouts, and comparing them to trees, they are buds. Also, let us realize that we humans are not extensions or transmitters of the past, but beings rushing towards the limitless, boundless light of a better tomorrow. And let us realize that this universe we are enveloped in is not a ready-made product (旣成品) or a finished product (完成品) manufactured sometime in antiquity, but a great unfinished work (未成品) that is constantly growing even at this very moment. Furthermore, let us realize that those new sprouts and new buds appearing ceaselessly every year, every day—especially our children—express and sing the praises of the daily growth of this great universe, and that without them, we have no hope or light whatsoever.
Our necks, which have looked back only at the past for thousands of years, may have become pathologically tilted in that direction. But from now on, let us make an effort, even forcibly, to look towards the future. <24> Just as in the past, the symbol of the past was said to be the adult, and all social norms were discussed centering on the adult, from now on, let the symbol of the future be the child, and let all social norms be discussed centering on the child. Look—look at the grass. Look at the trees. Doesn't the entire stem and root system solely support that tiny little new bud? And isn't it arranged so that the dew, the sunlight, and also the sweet rain are first received by that bud? We humans are no different. This is the only way. We must lift the pitiful position of the traditional child, who was crushed at the very bottom of society, and place them in the highest position in society.
So, what are the concrete measures to do this? First, recognize their personhood ethically.
Firstly, let's treat them respectfully in language. Some might say, "What does language matter?" But think about yourself as an adult. How would you feel if you were treated condescendingly by someone you don't even know? Or try using respectful language to a child (as the reporter says from experience); at first, the respectful language simply won't come out. The fact that it doesn't come out is already because there is discrimination against children in one's own mind. Therefore, as the first sign of recognizing the personhood of children, we must first treat them respectfully in language.
Secondly, we must cultivate the habit of treating children exactly the same as adults in clothing, food, housing, and all other aspects of daily life.
Thirdly, in homes, schools, and other general social facilities, we must always keep the existence of children in mind when establishing facilities.
Furthermore, guarantee the peace of their lives economically.
Firstly, provide them with adequate food and clothing so they do not fall into the harm of malnutrition.
Secondly, prohibit child labor and provide opportunities for all to attend school.
However, ethical liberation can be practiced to some extent even under today's social system if there is truly self-awareness, but as mentioned above, economic liberation seems impossible unless the fundamental problem is resolved. This point requires special additional consideration.<25>

To Those Who Speak of the Youth Problem
We now cry out for political liberation as a nation and human liberation from class structures. However, we believe that if we do not first liberate the young boys and girls under our feet, all other liberation movements cannot be thoroughly realized in practice. There is an old saying that the way of the gentleman begins with husband and wife, but we say that the way of liberation culminates in liberating children.
Among those who speak of the youth problem, there might be some who put the liberation issue aside and talk about youth protection issues or youth self-cultivation issues within the current situation as it is. But that is completely wrong thinking. For example, if there is a blade of grass crushed under some boulder, it is utterly unconvincing to talk about saving the grass while leaving the boulder as it is. Today's Korean youth are indeed crushed grass. To speak of other issues without removing the thing that crushes them is nothing more than a temporary stopgap measure or a clever tactic to defend the crushed status quo. At the very outset of discussing the youth problem, one must pay attention to this first. Furthermore, seeing that the discussion of the Korean youth movement today arises mainly against the background of the influence of established religions, this concern becomes even greater. This is a point that those discussing the youth problem today must particularly heed. I wish to deepen the significance of the youth movement that is just now arising by first saying these few words, and also to draw the attention of the many comrades involved in this movement. <26>

**Summary**

The author, Ki Ch'an, writing in the magazine *Kaebyŏk* in 1923, passionately advocates for the "liberation" of Korean children (소년해방). He argues that traditional Korean society, deeply rooted in Confucian ethics (specifically the 'Order between elder and younger' - 長幼有序), fundamentally denies the personhood of children, treating them as inferior possessions rather than respected individuals. This ethical oppression is compounded by severe economic oppression, where poverty forces children into labor instead of education, ruining their future. Ki Ch'an asserts that true societal progress and liberation (political, economic, etc.) cannot be achieved without first liberating children ethically (through respect, equal treatment, respectful language) and economically (ensuring sustenance, education, and prohibiting child labor). He connects this youth liberation directly to the broader "Kaebyŏk" (New Creation/Opening) movement, urging a shift from a past-focused society centered on adults to a future-focused one centered on children, warning against superficial reforms that ignore the need for fundamental change, especially those potentially influenced by established religions defending the status quo.

For historians, this article is significant as it reflects the intellectual ferment and calls for radical social change in 1920s colonial Korea, linking specific social issues (child welfare) to broader anti-traditionalist and potentially anti-colonial sentiments (via economic critique). It shows the influence of Confucianism on daily life and the critique against it, the nascent stage of child rights discourse, the dire economic situation forcing child labor, and the connection progressive intellectuals made between social reform (youth liberation) and national progress, all framed within the context of the influential *Kaebyŏk* journal associated with Ch'ŏndogyo ideals. The explicit warning against reforms backed by "established religions" hints at complex internal debates within the nationalist and social movements.

						起瀍

少年運動協會의 壯擧

듯건대 京城안에 잇는 各 少年團體의 關係者 一同은 지난 4月 17日로써 少年運動協會를 組織하고 地方에 잇는 幾多의 少年團體 其他 社會團體와 聯絡을 取하야써 世界的으로 意義 깁흔 5月의 1日을 期하야 朝鮮 13道 형제로 하야금 一齊히 少年運動의 旗幟를 들도록 하리라 하는도다.
이번에 高調하는 그 協會의 少年運動의 眞意가 那邊에 잇슬가 함에 對하야는 우리가 아직 (이 글을 쓰는 4月 20日까지) 그 仔細한 것을 아러엇지 못하얏스나 그 運動이 爲先 朝鮮의 少年을 標準하야 計劃되는 것이라 한즉 今日 朝鮮少年의 特殊한 處地에 거울하야 몬져 「少年解放」이란 그것을 目標 삼아 나아갈 것은 想必 疑心 업는 事實일지며 또는 그것이 事實이 되지 아니하면 안될 것이다.
解放! 解放! 이 말은 近來의 우리 朝鮮사람에게 퍽도 만히 絶呌되는 말이다. 政治的 解放, 經濟的 解放을 絶呌함은 말도 말고 「女子의 解放」과 가튼 問題도 우리의 귀가 암프리 만큼 喧藉되며 잇다. 그러나 엇던 셈인지 今日 社會의 潛勢力이 되고 明日 社會의 中堅力이 될 少年解放 問題에 對하여는 別로 이러타 하는 소래가 업섯다. 再昨年 以降으로 이곳 져곳에 몃 군대의 少年團體가 생기여 空谷呼聲과 가티 얼마콤이라도 少年問題의 聲息을 傳한 바가 업지 아니 하엿스나 그 問題가 一般의 輿論이 되고 運動이 되여 萬人의 注視를 要하기까지에는＜20＞ 너무나 미미하엿스며 또는 너무나 不鮮明하엿다. 이러한 오늘 이와 가튼 普遍的 少年運動이 니러남을 보게 된 것은 실로 好消息 中의 好消息이라. 우리는 몬져 말만 듯기에도 一片의 衷情이 스사로 躍如함을 禁치 못하겟다.

朝鮮 少年의 倫理的 壓迫
그런대 少年을 해방한다 하면 少年을 壓迫하는 事實의 存在를 前提로 하지 아니치 못할지니 그러면 從來의 우리 朝鮮사람은 果然 엇더케 少年을 壓迫하엿난가. 말은 여긔에서브터 始作될 수 밧게 업는 것이다. 그윽히 생각하면 從來의 少年 壓迫에 잇서 맨 첫재로 헤일 것은 倫理的 壓迫이라. 오늘날의 우리가 무슨 宗敎를 밋고 무슨 主義를 말한다 할지라도 우리의 社會的 生活의 實際는 100의 99가 모다 儒敎의 倫理 밧게 한거름을 나가지 못하는 것이라. 그런대 儒敎의 倫理는 사람을 사람 그대로 觀察하지 아니하고 여러 가지로 사람을 分析하야 그 中에서 君이라 하고 父라 하고 夫라 하는 세베라(三網)를 發見하고 남아지의 群庶를 거긔에 服屬케 하되 別로 5倫이란 그믈코 가튼 것을 지여써 一般의 脫出을 禁制하엿나니 少年 壓迫의 唯一의 道德的 乃至 倫理的 根據가 되는 「長幼有序」라는 金言도 곳이 5倫 中의 하나이다.
가만히 그間의 經緯를 생각하면 五倫은 三網에 屬케 하고 三網 中에도 夫는 父에 屬게 하고

少年問題를 云爲하는 이에게
우리는 지금 民族으로 政治的 해방을 브르짓고 人間的으로 階級的 해방을 부르짓는다. 그런대 우리는 생각하되 우리가 몬져 우리의 발 밋헤 잇는 男女어린이를 해방치 아니하면 其他의 모든 해방運動을 事實로써 徹底하지 못하리라 한다. 君子의 道는 그 끗이 夫婦에서브터 지여진다는 녯말이 잇거니와 우리는 써하되 해방의 道는 그 끗에 어린이를 해방함에서 지여지리라고 한다.
或 少年問題를 말하는 사람 중에 해방問題를 뒤에 두고 今日 이 現狀 그대로의 우에서 少年保護問題를 말하고 少年修養問題를 말할 사람이 잇슬넌지도 모른다. 그러나 그것은 아조 틀닌 생각이다. 假令 여긔에 엇던 盤石 밋헤 눌니운 풀싹이 잇다하면 그 盤을 그대로 두고 그 풀을 救한다는 말은 到底히 首肯할 수 업는 말이다. 오늘 朝鮮의 少年은 果然 눌니운 풀이다. 눌으는 그것을 除祛치 아니하고 다른 問題를 云爲한다 하면 그것은 모다 一時 一時의 姑息策이 아니면 눌니워 잇는 그 現狀을 巧妙하게 擁護하고져 하는 術策에 지내지 아니할 바이다. 少年問題가 論議되는 劈頭에 잇서 몬져 이것을 注意하지 아느면 안된다. 더욱 今日 朝鮮少年運動의 論議가 주로 旣成宗敎의 勢를 背景으로 하여써 니러남을 볼 때에 이러한 念을 더욱 크게 함이 잇다. 이것은 今日 少年問題를 論議하는 사람에계 잇서 特히 注意하지 아느면 안될 點이라 한다. 몬져 두어 마디를 니야기하야써 方히 니러나는 少年運動의 意義를 깁게 하려하며 아울너 이 運動에 關係되는 만흔 同志의 注意를 一煩하려 한다.＜26＞
