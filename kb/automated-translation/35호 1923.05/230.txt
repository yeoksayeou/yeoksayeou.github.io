**Body Text Translation:**
Aviation Officer Mr. Sŏ Ilbo
						Recorded by K Sŏng in Peiching
(I offer this writing to my compatriots in the homeland through Brother Soch'un)

Brother Soch'un, for several years now, airplanes have been flying vertically and horizontally in the skies of Peiching. Among them, taking off from the Nanyüan Airfield, laden with extraordinary plans and hopes, spinning its propeller, rising mile by mile ever higher, gazing afar towards the mountains and rivers of the homeland, was one man shedding tears helplessly. That man is Mr. Sŏ Ilbo. Although he knows the direction of his homeland well, he was greatly saddened that no matter how much he looked, he could not see it. Thus, he tried immensely hard to rise as high as possible to see it. However, unlike his heart's desire, it was not clearly visible to his eyes. He then stopped trying to see it with his eyes. Instead, he painted a picture in his mind of his parents and siblings in the homeland being chased by some powerful entity into treacherous mountains and valleys, falling into fierce flames, and then, hearing their cries of distress and seeing them weep, his entire body's energy surged, crying out "Ah! Let us weep together! Let us die together!" He frantically spun the propeller, intending to rush forth on the swift wind to grasp the hands of his parents and siblings, whose pulse still faintly beat amidst the perilous mountains and fierce fires, and seek a path to new life. However, he unfortunately realized that the airplane he was piloting truly lacked the power to enable him to meet the parents and siblings of his homeland who appeared in his heart. And so, we often saw his muscles, until then firm as iron and stone, suddenly become soft as cotton, returning limply to the airfield, sighing, <84> his face reddening as if unable to bear the shame before his fellow students.

Brother Soch'un, this Mr. Sŏ died twice. Afterward, he came back to life. Once, when the instructor rode behind and Mr. Sŏ was in front, the airplane unfortunately crashed. Consequently, Mr. Sŏ was momentarily dead but revived, while the instructor died forever. At that time, everyone said this: "In an airplane, riding in front is safer." However, after that, when Mr. Sŏ rode behind and the instructor was in front, the airplane unfortunately crashed again. Then too, the instructor became a guest on a journey of no return, unable to revive, while Mr. Sŏ resuscitated two days later. At this time, everyone said again: "Mr. Sŏ is a natural-born aviator." From then on, not only did Mr. Sŏ himself gain firm conviction, but we also came to strongly believe that Mr. Sŏ would truly not die in vain. However, during the month or more Mr. Sŏ spent recuperating at that time, the airplanes flying in the skies of Peiching did not look so dear to our eyes. The sound of their propellers struck our ears as extremely grating. This was because we felt those airplanes had no relation whatsoever to our hopes.

Brother Soch'un, sometime later, Mr. Sŏ went back to the airfield. By this time, his external injuries were almost completely healed, but his spirit had not yet fully recovered to its previous state. Nevertheless, truly courageously, bearing on one hand the shame (though of course, the crash was not due to his own error, but solely due to mechanical failure and the instructor's mistake) and on the other hand a smile (with the thought, "Airplane, you may break, but I do not break"), he requested to fly again. <85> At that time, all his classmates and instructors strongly tried to dissuade him, saying his spirit still seemed lacking and he should fly after more recuperation. However, Mr. Sŏ ultimately did not listen and insisted firmly on flying, so in the end, we once again got to hear with our ears the sound of the propeller spun by Mr. Sŏ's hand. We once again got to see with our eyes the wings of the airplane flown by Mr. Sŏ. And so, we could not help but pray, "Ah, airplane! Ah, Mr. Sŏ! Please do not crash again."

Brother Soch'un, how passionately this Mr. Sŏ has pursued this field of study is not something I intend to elaborate on now. Seeing the few points mentioned above, I believe you can clearly infer it. However, I will briefly tell you the course of events leading up to his engagement in this study. When Mr. Sŏ initially left the homeland, he had an enemy he could not forgive in his lifetime. Thinking that one rifle was enough to eliminate that enemy, he went to a certain place and became a soldier. However, afterward, deeply feeling that this alone was greatly insufficient, he happened to go to the region of Russia (露西亞) and saw a machine gun for the first time. Thus, deciding he must learn this, he immediately studied artillery (砲工). However, lamenting that the facilities there were incomplete and unsatisfactory for learning such skills, he heard about the outbreak of the European War (歐洲 戰亂) and the resounding success of aerial combat. He then resolved that without this, he could not achieve his desire, and finally came to Peiching, sought out this aviation school, and began to research and study this field. And so, after enduring truly countless hardships for several years, he graduated from this school last winter. Among the eighty-odd classmates, over forty graduated together, and among them, his results were the best. <86>

Brother Soch'un, we wholeheartedly congratulate Mr. Sŏ's graduation from the aviation school for various reasons. Last January, a congratulatory meeting was held at the Second Compound of Peking University, organized by our Overseas Students' Association here. At that time, I unfortunately could not attend, so I do not know the details of the situation then, but I heard it was a great success. Subsequently, on February 11th, a congratulatory meeting was held at the Peiching Ch'ŏndogyo Mission Room, organized by various prominent figures residing here. Over 60 people gathered at this time. The meeting opened with Mr. Ch'oe Tong'o presiding, followed by a congratulatory song sung in the lovely voices of the members of the Yuyŏn Korean Youth Club. Then, a silver badge (徽章) was presented. At this moment, receiving it, tears streamed down Mr. Sŏ's face like rain. His large handkerchief was completely soaked. Seeing this, my heart inexplicably trembled, and tears submerged my pupils. For a while, my world descended into a completely hazy realm. Around this time, fervent, solemn, and meaningful congratulatory addresses were delivered sequentially by Messrs. Han Hŭng, Yi Kwang, Kim Chaehŭi, and others. At this moment, the atmosphere of the entire venue was truly solemn and stirring. (Brother, just my thought is that I'd like to relate this exactly as heard and seen, but I don't believe my mere record would even reach your desk, and summarizing even briefly wouldn't be permitted by my heart, disregarding the speakers' intentions, so I feel great regret.) Next came Mr. Sŏ's response. Tearing the badge he had pinned to his lapel, he held it high in his left hand and declared in a powerful voice, "This is the first award I have ever received. Especially for soldiers, the giving of rewards and punishments must be impartial for military discipline to be strict and for courageous valor to be cultivated. However, I do not consider this award given to me now to be impartial at all. <87> Why is that? Because merely graduating from this aviation school makes me feel extremely ashamed to receive such an award. In other words, until I succeed in the resolution I made from the first day I studied at this aviation school - this award is by no means my possession. It is rather like slashing my chest with random knives. Instead, if, after I die fighting in some battle, it were placed even in my coffin (though I'd know nothing after death), I believe that would be considered giving me an award. However, I only have the thought of somehow quickly fighting and dying according to my heart's desire; I lack the preparations to reach the battlefield. This I entrust to you, parents and siblings. That is, those who truly congratulate my graduation, I believe the most valuable congratulation is to grant me the path to death as soon as possible. I do not particularly demand congratulations in various forms. I only wish once again from my parents and siblings for the congratulation that allows me to fly an airplane. If I cannot fly an airplane now, I feel I will die of 'airplane sickness' (飛機病)." Then, after silently contemplating something for a moment, he continued his words, speaking much about various fervent wishes and aspirations. And then, the meeting promptly adjourned.

Brother Soch'un. Seeing the expression of passion welling up from Mr. Sŏ's chest like this, I felt thus: When Mr. Sŏ truly returns to the homeland piloting an airplane stained with the blood of other peoples, it seems a new, joyful chapter will open for our nation (民族). Also, when we hear the sound of the propeller spun by this blood-stained hand of Mr. Sŏ, I think even our departed grandfathers and grandmothers would surely rejoice. However, how can we believe that such success can come from the strength of just one person? Perhaps it is an impossible task! Nevertheless, isn't it precisely our responsibility to help even just one person achieve completeness and <88> fulfillment? In other words, isn't it an obligation we, who seek a new life, must certainly fulfill? Whatever the task, if there is someone capable of doing what most people cannot, the glory belongs not only to that person. It will become the glory of our entire kindred (同族). Externally, then, as a reward for that glory, enabling that person to thoroughly display their skills would be the best thing above all else. If we fail to do so, it will mean losing the true meaning of so-called co-existence and co-prosperity in this society.

Brother. Brother Soch'un. I know that now is not a time when our Korean Nation (朝鮮民族) can be joyful merely by flying an airplane in the sky. I know that merely spinning a propeller (푸로펠라) cannot be a source of pride. Rather, even if one cannot soar to pierce the heavens, there must be the spirit (氣槪) to at least destroy the earth (壞地). Also, whether equipping it with an engine or not, there must be some form of creation. That we now congratulate Mr. Sŏ unceasingly seems fitting because its significance lies more in the former than the latter. Mr. Sŏ also seems to be a person focusing on the former. Thus, Mr. Sŏ spreads out a map (地圖) daily, drawing routes here and there with a red line (朱線). After drawing it all, he often powerfully strikes one end of the final point with his fist. If things turn out according to the meaning of this fist strike, the ultimate intention is to turn it into a desert (沙漠). Brother, please strive together with the many parents and siblings in the homeland (故國) to enable Mr. Sŏ to tread this red line drawn on the map and have one exhilarating flight in the sky. Your opinions on this matter are not what I seek to know; I only introduce and entrust this to you, wise Brother, and to our many compatriots. With this, I respectfully wish you well.
Reporter's Note: "Firstly, due to aspects Mr. Sŏ does not permit, and also due to many circumstances in the mainland (內地 事情) that do not permit it, being unable to introduce this in detail is extremely regrettable, but there is no choice but to look forward to a later date."
Editor's Note: Although this piece is late and should have been published in last April's issue, due to editorial constraints, it is published in this issue. In terms of introducing Mr. Sŏ's sincere heart (赤心) to you, the readers, and conveying your sincere hearts to Mr. Sŏ, we believe the timing, early or late, does not matter. <89>

**Summary**

This article from 1923 introduces Sŏ Ilbo, a Korean graduate of a Chinese aviation school in Beijing. It details his motivations stemming from a desire to fight for Korea, his rigorous training marked by two near-fatal crashes, and his passionate graduation speech where he declared that true congratulations would be enabling him to fly and die in combat for his homeland. The author, "K Sŏng," writing to "Brother Soch'un" and compatriots in Korea, portrays Sŏ as a symbol of national hope and calls for support to help him fulfill his mission, which involves using aviation not just for flight but as a potent force, metaphorically capable of "destroying the earth." From a historical perspective, the article is highly revealing of early Korean nationalist aspirations during the Japanese colonial period. It showcases the diaspora community's role (in Beijing) in supporting the independence movement, the adoption of modern technology (aviation) for national liberation goals, and the fervent, almost sacrificial, patriotism expressed by individuals like Sŏ Ilbo, who viewed martyrdom in struggle as the highest honor. Historians would find the direct link between piloting skills and anti-colonial struggle, the emotional weight attached to graduation, and the raw plea for resources to fight extremely valuable.

飛行將校 徐日甫君
						在北京 K生 記
(이 글을 小春兄을 通하야 故國에 게신 同胞들에게 들임)
小春兄님 數年 前부터 北京 空中에는 飛行機가 縱으로 橫으로 날아 단니엿슴니다. 其中에는 져- 南苑飛行場으로부터 남다른 經綸과 希望을 가득히 싯고 「푸로펠라」를 두르며 한 마일 두 마일 漸漸 높히 떠- 멀리 故國의 江山을 바라보며 시름업시 눈물을 흘리는 한 사람이 잇셧슴니다. 그 사람이 곳 徐日甫君임니다. 君은 故國이 잇는 方向을 자세이도 알지마는 아무리 바라보아도 보이지 안이하는 것을 大端히 설허하얏슴니다. 그리하야 볼 수 잇는 대로 놉히 떠보려고도 無限이 애를 썻슴니다. 그러나 마음과 가티 눈에는 잘 보이지 안이하얏슴니다. 그는 다시 눈으로 볼 생각을 하지 안이하얏슴니다. 다못 마음으로 故國에 게신 父母兄弟들이 엇던 强有力한 者에게 좃기여 險惡한 山谷으로 들어가며 猛烈한 火焰 속에 빠지는 그림을 그려 노코 다시 그들의 哀呼하는 울음소리를 듯고 눈물 흘리는 光景을 보고 全身의 氣運이 總動하야 아- 가티 웁시다! 가티 죽읍시다!를 부르지즈며 「푸로펠라」를 어즐러히 흔들며 단 바람에 모라가서 險山烈火 속에 一脉이 尙存한 父母兄弟들의 손길을 마주 붓들고 新生의 途를 求하려 하얏슴니다. 그러나 不幸이 그 타고 잇는 飛行機는 果然 그의 心中에 나타난 故國의 父母兄弟들을 맛나 보게 할만한 힘이 不足하얏슴을 깨달엇슴니다. 그리하야 지금까지 鐵石가티 튼튼하는 筋肉이 별안간 棉花가티 부들부들 하야저서 힘업시 飛行場으로 돌아 돌아 한숨- 쉬고＜84＞ 同學하는 동모에게 붓그럼을 못 이기는 듯이 얼골이 붉으레하야지는 것을 우리는 보군  하얏슴니다.
小春兄님 이 徐君은 두 번을 죽엇섯슴니다. 其後에 다시 살아낫슴니다. 한번은 敎授가 뒤에 타고 君은 압헤 타슬 때에 飛行機는 不幸히 떠러졋슴니다. 그래서 君은 暫時 죽엇다가 다시 살아나고 敎授는 永遠히 죽고 말엇슴니다. 그때에 모든 사람들은 이러케 말하얏슴니다. 「飛行機에는 압헤 타는 것이 安全한 것이라고」. 그러나 그 뒤에는 君이 뒤에 타고 敎授는 압헤 타슬 때에 飛行機는 또 不幸히 떠러졋슴니다. 그때에도 敎授는 다시 살아나지 못하는 永歸의 客이 되고 君은 2日 後에 다시 蘇生하얏슴니다. 이때에 모든 사람은 또 말하얏슴니다. 「徐君은 天生의 飛行家라고」. 이로부터는 徐君 스스로도 信念이 鞏固하얏다 하지마는 우리들은 참으로 그져 죽지는 안이할 徐君이라고 튼튼히 미더 왓슴니다. 그러나 그때에 徐君이 月餘를 治療하는 동안에 北京 空中에 날아 단이는 飛行機는 우리의 눈에 그리 貴여워 보이지 안이하얏슴니다. 그 「푸로펠라」 소리는 우리의 귀에 대단히 역하게 들리엿슴니다. 그 飛行機는 우리들의 希望하는 何等의 關係가 업는 것을 늣긴 까닭으로 그리한 것임니다.
小春兄님 얼마 後에 君은 또 飛行場으로 차저 들어갓슴니다. 이때까지 外部의 傷處는 거의 完治되얏지오마는 그 精神은 아직도 如前히 回復되지 못하얏슬 때임니다. 그러나 君은 참으로 勇敢스럽게 一面으로는 그 부끄럼과 (勿論 自己의 過失로 떠러진 것은 안이요 다못 機械의 故障과 敎授의 失策으로 因함이지만) 一面으로는 우슴을(飛行機 너는 부서저도 나는 부서지지 안는다 하는 생각으로) 먹음고 다시 飛行機에 타기를 請하얏슴니다.＜85＞ 그때에 모든 同窓들과 敎授들은 만히 留挽하얏슴니다. 아직 精神이 不足한 嬚이 잇스니 좀 더 治療한 後에 타라고. 그러나 君은 終是 듯지 아니하고 其於히 타기를 强請하얏슴으로 結局은 徐君이 손으로 잡아 둘으는 「표로펠라」소리를 우리의 귀로 다시 듯게 되얏슴니다. 徐君이 타고 나는 飛行機 나래를 우리의 눈으로 다시 보게 되얏슴니다. 그리하야 「아- 飛機야- 徐君아! 너는 다시 떠러지지 말어라」라고 祝願함을 마지 아니 하엿슴니다.
