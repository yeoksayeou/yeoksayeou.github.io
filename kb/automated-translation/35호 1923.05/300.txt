Magazine Title: 개벽 제35호
Publication Date: 1923년 05월 01일
Article Title: 思欲絶 (Thoughts Approaching Despair)
Author: 金素月
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

思欲絶
						金素月
I You will remember me so you cannot forget
You will remember me so you cannot forget,
Just let the months and years (歲月) go by like that.

Then perhaps you will forget somewhat,
Let us live like that, though it may be regrettable.

But you will say,
"How could I ever think of leaving you, whom I cannot forget,
Whom I miss so dearly, even if a long time passes?"

II Before, I never knew.

The moon that rises every night, regardless of spring or fall,
"Before, I never knew."

That I would miss it so fervently like this,<139>
"Before, I never knew."

That even if the moon were ever so bright, I would look up at it,
"Before, I never knew."

That now, that moon is sorrow,
"Before, I never knew."

III Even when the sun sets on the mountain ridge
Even when the sun sets on the mountain ridge,
For me, it sets because of you.

"My dear, such thoughts of mine,
My lover (愛人), please don't speak of them again."

Even on a dark night with wind blowing and rain falling,
For me, the morning dawns because of you.

"My dear, such thoughts of mine,
My lover (愛人), please don't speak of them again."<140>

Even if I go to a faraway foreign land (萬里他國),
When the time comes, I will come back to you.
My dear, my lover (愛人)!
After I leave on this road, please do not ask anyone
About my traces (蹤跡) or whereabouts.

"But, but my dear,
You are my lover (愛人)."

IV Tears flow readily.
Tears flow readily,
Because I miss you so much I cannot forget.
Thus, tears flow readily.

That person whom I cannot forget,
Even though I haven't completely abandoned them,
Tears flow readily.

My already sorrowful heart,
Feels like it has departed on a journey it couldn't leave,
When I think of it, tears flow readily.<141>

Ⅴ Awake or asleep, sitting or standing
Awake or asleep, sitting or standing,
There was a friend like a shadow to me.

So how many months and years (歲月) did we spend
Only in useless torment!

Today again, into the very center of your heart
Weeping, I stir it up and leave!

A heart complaining, feelings with no place to rest, a pained chest,
I cannot forget that it was love.<142>
<139-142>

**Summary**

This five-part poem by Kim Sowŏl explores the complex emotions surrounding love, memory, and separation. The speaker grapples with the inability to forget a loved one, acknowledges the sorrow found even in nature (like the moon), and describes how their entire perception of day and night is tied to this person. Despite planning a departure and asking the lover not to seek them, the speaker reaffirms their bond and is overcome by tears and the pain of separation, ultimately recognizing the enduring nature of their love amidst the torment. For historians studying the Korean colonial period, the poem, while intensely personal, resonates with the pervasive themes of loss, longing ("han"), and displacement felt by many Koreans during that era. The imagery of departure to a "faraway foreign land" and the plea not to be sought could subtly reflect the forced or necessary migrations and separations common at the time, channeling national sentiment through the lens of individual heartache.

思欲絶
						金素月
I 못 닛도록 생각이 나겟지요
못 닛도록 생각이 나겟지요,
그런대로 歲月만 가랍시구려.

그러면 더러는 닛치겟지요,
아수운대로 그러케 살읍시구려.

그러나 당신이 니르겟지요,
「그립어 살틀이도 못 닛는 당신을
오래다고 생각인들 떠지 오릿가?」

II 예前엔 밋처 몰랏서요.

봄가을 업시 밤마다 돗는 달을,
「예前엔 밋처 몰랏서요」.

이럿케 사모차게 그립을 줄을,＜139＞
「예前엔 밋처 몰랏서요.」

달이 암만 밝아도 쳐다볼 줄을,
「예前엔 밋처 몰랏서요」.

이제금 저 달이 설음인 줄을,
「예前엔 밋처 몰랏서요」.

III 해가 山마루에 저믈어도
해가 山마루에 저믈어도,
내게는 당신때문에 저믈어집니다.

「여봅셔요, 그러한 내 생각일낭
내 愛人이여, 두番도 마르셔요」.

바람불고 비조차 오는 어둡은 밤이라도,
내게는 당신때문에 아츰이 밝아집니다.

「여봅셔요, 그러한 내 생각일낭,
내 愛人이여 두番도 말으셔요」.＜140＞

萬里他國을 갓다가도
때가 되면 당신한테로 오겟습니다.
여봅셔요, 나의 愛人이여!
이 길로 떠나간 뒤에는 나의 蹤跡가튼 것은,
엇더한 사람에게라도 뭇지 말아 주셔요.

「그러나, 그러나 여봅셔요,
당신은 나의 愛人입니다」.

IIV 눈물이 쉬루르 흘러납니다.
눈물이 수루르 흘레납니다,
당신이 하도 못 닛게 그립어서.
그리 눈물이 수루르 흘레납니다.

닛치지도 안는 그 사람은
아주나 내바린 것이 아닌데도,
눈물이 수루르 흘레납니다.

갓득히나 설흔 맘이
떠나지 못할 運에 떠난 것도 갓타서,
생각하면 눈물이 수루르 흔레납니다.＜141＞

Ⅴ 자나 깨나, 안즈나 서나
자나 깨나 안즈나 서나,
그림자가튼 벗 하나이 내게 잇섯습니다.

그러니 우리는 얼마나 만흔 歲月을
쓸데업는 괴롭음으로만 보내엿습닛가!

오늘은 또다시 당신 가슴의 한복판을
울면서 나는 휘젓고 떠납니다구러!

하소연한 맘, 둘 곳 업는 심사에 쓸아린 가슴은
그것이 사랑이든 줄이 아니도 닛칩니다.＜142＞
