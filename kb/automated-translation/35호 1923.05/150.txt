**No. 6 Correspondence**

**Chinju News**

**Kyŏngnam Public Doctors' Association Meeting Held**
The Sanitation Division of Kyŏngsangnam-do held the Kyŏngsang Public Doctors' Association meeting on the 28th and 29th of last month, researching and deciding on matters concerning the prevention of various infectious diseases that arise according to the season.

**Frequent Harassment of Women**
As the weather gradually warms, it seems the number of "swift-death yangban" (迅死 兩班) going out for amusement to the mountains or suburbs, carrying murderous kisaeng (妓生) and ruinous bottles of liquor by their sides, is considerably increasing. It is strange enough that a certain family in Seoul has two or three adopted sons for property inheritance, but the devotion of Chinju personages to living with kisaeng cannot be overlooked either. Not only this, but it is truly deplorable and pitiful that young men, barely twenty, having nothing better to do day or night, idly suck the blood and sweat of their parents and specialize in visiting kisaeng houses in every alley. Recently, perhaps because their eyes are blinded by mountain sheep (산양 - likely a metaphor for lust or foolishness), they gather in gangs in the alleys at night and frequently assault passing women. It is said that Miss Pae (裵少女) and Miss Ch'oe (崔孃) have both suffered this. Also, general complaints from women are quite high that whenever female night school students, especially unmarried young women, from the school established by the Catholic Church finish their classes and step onto the street to return home, gangs of young men appear from nowhere, follow them, and harass them intensely. This is something parents of families with young daughters must pay special attention to, and it would be good for the young men as well to exercise more caution and control themselves.

**Clash Among Kongjonhoe Members**
This association (共存會) was organized solely by individuals who, with unique thoughts and outstanding spirit, participated in the independence movement and suffered together in prison for several years. The motivation behind this association's founding was likely that since they suffered together for the same cause with the same heart and mind, they should continue to cooperate wholeheartedly in whatever they do in the future without change. When this association was formed, we blessed its eternal future. However, to commemorate this past March 1st, they suspended all business for the day and gathered at a certain tavern, exchanging cups and enjoying themselves. It is said that over a matter that was not even significant, they ended up pounding each other until their fists swelled and hurling insults beyond recovery, creating a scene of utter chaos. Seeing them recklessly exchange punches and curse freely over trivial matters, one cannot trust them and can only find it pathetic. Due to this, the Kongjonhoe is reportedly in a state of self-dissolution. Members of Kongjonhoe! You ought to reflect deeply.

**Students Entering Liquor Stores**
While it's a general trend, so who can blame whom, it is still inappropriate for students, who are still in the midst of learning, to enter liquor stores with the school emblem "Kongbo" (公普 - likely abbreviation for Kongnip Pot'ong Hakkyo, Public Common School) clearly attached to their caps. How much more improper is it, then, after midnight, to demand that the locked main gate be opened, and when told apologetically that there is no more liquor and they should go to another store, and the door is not opened promptly, to threaten to break the liquor crocks? Can this be called the conduct of upright students? If this is true, it particularly requires attention.

**Guarantee by a Man of Influence**
On the 25th of last month, at a certain banquet, several kisaeng and a local notable known as Kim OO (金OO) gathered for drinking and music. The participating police chief saw one kisaeng wearing clothes made entirely of plain native cotton (純土木) and asked why a kisaeng was wearing cotton instead of silk. She replied that selling her singing and dancing alone was insufficient to support her entire family, so although her desire to wear silk was earnest, she had been unable to fulfill it (有意未遂). The chief then said, "However, kisaeng originally entice men with beautiful appearances and splendid clothes, so from now on, do not wear native products again." Turning to the notable Kim OO sitting beside him, he asked him to guarantee that the kisaeng would not wear cotton clothes. Mr. Kim reportedly agreed readily without a shred of hesitation (一毫 躊躇 업시 快諾). Does this mean that if the kisaeng lack money and continue to wear only cotton clothes, Mr. Kim is resolved to provide silk garments even out of his own pocket, and thus agreed readily? If so, fine (己어니와). But if not, what was the true meaning of the guarantee, and what calculation (抑何心算) was behind it, one wonders (不知).

**Village Head Seizes Married Woman**
Among the 19 townships (面) in Chinju County (郡), there is one called Pansŏng-myŏn (班城面), and within that township, in Ch'angch'on-ri (倉村里), there is one small market. This place has long been known for its moral laxity. Among the women there, one known as the Tongnae house wife (東萊집) (Mun-ssi (文氏), 21, wife of Ch'oe Kap-yun (崔甲潤)) <57> had long been involved with the township head, Han U-wŏn (韓禹源). Many township residents who learned of this matter called the township head "Tongnae house township head," and referred to Ms. Mun with the titles of "big husband" and "little husband." As these words gradually spread, the authorities seemed intent on taking action, and rumors arose that the township head's background would be investigated. Showing his resourcefulness, the township head swiftly separated Ms. Mun from her husband, cleverly forcing a divorce on the civil register. For appearance's sake, he could not register her under his own name, and besides, the law did not permit it. Thus, he was forced to attach her to his friend Yu Chang-han's (劉章漢) family register, and they have continued like this until now, so currently there seems to be no particular conflict. If abusing the authority of a township head to steal a resident's wife and make her his concubine does not violate current law, then so be it (巳어니와). But if not, shouldn't the higher authorities, even if it's not the regular spring or autumn personnel review period, carry out a swift clean-up of such individuals as soon as possible to resolve the people's grievances (怨府)?

**Keeping Her Husband, Takes a Japanese Man**
Ms. Kang (姜氏, 35), wife of Hwang Nam-su (黃南守) residing in the same market, while her husband Mr. Hwang is alive and well, formed an illicit relationship with a Japanese man named Fujimoto (藤本) and has already borne three daughters. Now she lives openly with him. Due to personal connections (인기 관계 - likely implying the Japanese man's influence), the husband Mr. Hwang is reportedly receiving unfair treatment from the Japanese man instead. Is it acceptable for a township head to steal a resident's wife, and for a Japanese man to take a Korean's wife? Even if the husbands, Messrs. Ch'oe and Hwang, lack connections and the power to resist, now that the matter is known, should it not be impossible to let it pass? Is the intention to fatten the township head because he is a government official (관속), and the Japanese man because he is a member of the ruling ethnicity (혈족 - lit. blood relative)? If the authorities truly have a heart to love the weak common people, they should deal with such individuals swiftly.

**Western Shoemakers' Business Opening Ceremony**
About 40 Western-style shoemakers belonging to the factory labor department of the Chinju Labor Mutual Aid Society (晉州勞働共濟會) held their inaugural general meeting on April 1st and passed various resolutions. Among these, focusing heavily on the matter of work, they decided on a plan for 500 pairs (1 pair = 10 won), already completed the first installment payment, and held an opening ceremony on the 11th. They began work from the following day, the 12th, and have reportedly been achieving quite good results since then.

**Labor Mutual Aid Society Regular General Meeting**
The Chinju Labor Mutual Aid Society held its second regular general meeting for three days starting April 8th at the Chinju Youth Hall. Representatives from various branches and offices attending numbered over 300. Under the chairmanship of interim chairman Cho U-che (趙佑濟氏), the first day included a report on the progress regarding the tenant farming issue. On the second day, regarding the discussion of revising the bylaws, the proposal to abolish the previous chairperson system and adopt a purely egalitarian committee system reached a final unanimous agreement. An amendment to increase the previous five departments to eight was also passed by a majority, adding Propaganda, Free Labor, and Factory Labor departments to the existing General Affairs, Accounting, Education, Investigation, and Tenant Farming departments. Moving to officer elections, 39 internal executive committee members, including those from branches and offices, were selected by the nomination committee. The budget and settlement passed at 2,100 won. On the third day, suitable persons were assigned to each department according to office allocation: General Affairs: Kang Tal-yŏng (姜達永), Wŏn Yong-sun (元鎔鐏); Accounting: Kim Chae-hong (金在泓), Kim Chŏng-hun (金貞薰); Education: Kang Ch'i-yŏl (姜致烈), Chŏn Ŭi-wŏn (全義源); Investigation: Cho U-che (趙佑濟), Pak Pong-ŭi (朴鳳儀); Propaganda: Kim Kyŏng-dan (金敬端), Yi Nae-ok (李乃玉), Chŏng Chun-kyo (鄭準敎), Pang Chin-hwan (房鎭熸), Kim Chong-myŏng (金鍾鳴); Tenant Farming: Pak T'ae-hong (朴台弘), Sim Tu-hyŏp (沈斗爕); Free Labor: Chang Yŏng-gŏn (張永鍵), Kwŏn Hong-u (權烘宇); Factory Labor: Kim Ha-jin (金河鎭), Kang Tŏk-mun (姜德文).
Executive Committee Members for each Branch Office: Kŭmsan (琴山): Yi To-yŏng (李道榮); Ilbansŏng (一班城): Han Yŏng-jun (韓永俊); Ibansŏng (二班城): Hŏ Man-ch'ŏl (許萬轍); Chinsŏng (晋城): Ku Yŏn-ch'ŏl (具然喆); Sabong (寺奉): Hŏ Kyŏng-gu (許敬九); Munsan (文山): Pak Yong-p'al (朴鏞八); Todong (道洞): Song Myŏng-suk (宋明淑); Taegok (大谷): Ha Tong-no (河東老); Chiphyŏn (集賢): An P'il-ho (安弼鎬); Myŏngsŏk (鳴石): Paek Yŏng-hŭi (白榮熙); P'yŏnggŏ (平居): Kim Hŭi-sun (金熙淳); Chŏngch'on (井村): Chŏn Sŏk-chu (全錫柱); Kimgok (金谷): Kim Chong-wŏn (金鍾元).
Executive Committee Members for each Branch: Pukch'ŏn (北川): Chŏng T'ae-chang (鄭泰璋); Sŏp'o (西浦): Kim Yŏp-kon (金燁坤), Kim Ŭng-bae (金應培).

**Chinju Normal School Entrance Examination**
The newly established Chinju Normal School this year was recruiting 110 students divided into special course and short course programs, but applicants unexpectedly surged, reaching over 580, including 42 women, by the application deadline. They were examined for three days starting April 9th. What was strangely felt among the results was that during the Japanese language exam, when asked to write the phrase 「シンシユノキシヨウ」 (Shinshu no kishō - Spirit of Chinju) in Hanja, the majority reportedly wrote 晋州 妓生 (Chinju kisaeng - Chinju courtesan). Also, when given the topic 「李成桂 崔致遠」 <58> and asked to write what they knew, most submitted blank papers. Looking at the results announced afterwards, 50 men for the special course, 50 men for the short course, and 10 women in total were admitted. Perhaps they wrote that because there are many kisaeng in Chinju (容或無怪), but what is truly pathetic among this is that being members of the "bow-bearing nation" (帶弓民族), being ignorant of such famous figures as Yi Sŏng-gye (李成桂) and Ch'oe Ch'i-wŏn (崔致遠) made me, as an observer, unable to avoid sweating profusely (汗出沾背). If such scholars, after studying just a little for a year or two, take up teaching posts in schools, how can they avoid being parrots (언충이), and how can they properly guide and educate the young children blossoming like flower buds?
To all who passed! Take serious note of this point and reflect deeply.

**Pot'ŏn'gyo Adherents Go to Chŏngŭp**
Regarded as the leader of Pot'ŏn'gyo (普天敎) in Chinju, or rather Kyŏngnam, and exerting much effort for the development of said religion, are Tong-A Ilbo Chinju Bureau Chief Chŏng Chun-kyo (鄭準敎), reporters Yun Pyŏng-ŭn (尹炳殷) and O Kyŏng-p'yo (吳景杓), Chosŏn Ilbo Bureau Chief Sin Hyŏn-su (申鉉壽), Chinyang Trading Company owner Kang Tae-ik (姜大翼), and as individuals, Sim Tu-hyŏp (沈斗爕), Kang Sang-ho (姜相鎬), Chŏng Sŏng-ho (鄭成鎬), Kang Tae-ch'ŏl (姜大喆), Kim Hyŏng-kwŏn (金亨權), Ch'ŏn Sŏk-ku (千錫九), and several others. They left Chinju the day before yesterday, the 15th, heading for Chŏngŭp (井邑) in Chŏllabuk-do, with the plan to have an audience with the religious leader Cha Ch'ŏn-ja (車天子) at the Pot'ŏn'gyo central sanctuary there, pray beneath the Ch'ilsŏngdan (七星壇 - Seven Stars Altar), receive 100,000 wŏn in gold (金 10만원), and construct a Chinjŏngwŏn (眞正院) upon returning to Chinju.

**Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Opera Troupe Preparation**
The Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) Chinju Youth Association (少年會) has been practicing for several days, selecting only the most lovely and interesting pieces, to commemorate and promote the coming May 1st, "Children's Day." Although the date is not yet fixed, it is heard they will perform sometime between late this month and early next month.

**Grand Athletic Meet to be Held**
Although several years have passed since the Chinju Youth Association (晉州靑年會) completed the construction of its hall, the completion ceremony could not be held due to financial reasons. As a result of discussions at the recent board meeting, they plan to hold a completion ceremony combined with a grand track and field meet on the coming 21st (Saturday) with a budget of about 300 wŏn. Currently, they are busy with preparations, having selected committee members for each department.

**P'yŏngyang Random Notes**

In P'yŏngyang too, starting this May, city trams, previously non-existent, will begin operation, and a commemorative exhibition celebrating this will be held. While one cannot say what the impact of holding this exhibition will be, our concern (吾人의 一慮) is whether it might inadvertently promote harmful trends (惡風 폐해), such as stirring up amusement for delinquent types (부랑패類) by featuring performances by so-called old-style and new-style actors day and night, occasionally interspersed with kisaeng performances. Of course, it is unlikely planned to inflict bad customs and negative influences upon the general citizenry, but the precedent of results differing from plans cannot but be another concern (一慮) for the authorities. And one more thing to worry about: P'yŏngyang's trams are set to run right down the middle of Chongno Street (鍾路通). In a place like Seoul, despite running along one side of the wide road, tram accidents causing deaths and injuries occur almost daily (無日不在), earning them the name "murder trams." One cannot help but demand special caution from the authorities and the general public regarding how P'yŏngyang's trams will fare, running through the very center of this narrow P'yŏngyang street for the first time.
P'yŏngyang is called the "Capital of Beauty" (色鄕). It used to be commonly said, mostly among the lower classes, that there was nothing to boast of besides beautiful women. Recently, however, it seems to be fervently pursuing this "Capital of Beauty" identity – whether this will lead to its ruin or its revival is unknown, but it is extremely prevalent. The profligacy of wandering hedonists (浪子) from North and South coveting P'yŏngyang's beauties, and the current trend in P'yŏngyang where young modern intellectuals, young students, and even prominent figures seem intent on becoming obsessed with beautiful women, is an incomprehensible oddity. P'yŏngyang! P'yŏngyang! Isn't this the ancient fortress of Tan'gun and Kija (檀箕의 遺城) spanning 4,000 years? If temperance in alcohol and tobacco (禁酒斷煙) changes to encouraging drinking and smoking (勸酒勸烟), what a rebellious city it becomes against the national economic movement! Is it conceivable that P'yŏngyang, which monopolizes the natural praise as the embroidered rivers and mountains (綿繡江山) and the foremost landscape, should become a traitor to the Peninsula's people? Alas (噫라)! What learned person (식자) in P'yŏngyang would wish to remain? Who among the learned in P'yŏngyang has such intention? If there are two or three concerned individuals who genuinely strive and worry, staying awake at night (不貪不寐 - likely misprint for 不眠不休?) sighing, they are likely just powerless scholars whose spirit is strong but flesh is weak (志强肉弱한 閒士). How could a movement arise to save this city? Do those engaged in learning stay in this city? Do those with purpose, tears, and spiritual strength remain in this city? If you exist, rise up! Disregard face and all hesitation, just rise up in indignation. Why are there no newspapers or magazines in P'yŏngyang? While permanent theaters (常設館) are being built, why are there no factories and companies to employ the poor (貧徒), guiding them towards diligence and finding jobs? Why do schools produce hooligans (망난이) in greater numbers (倍數) instead of gentlemen (紳士)? If we had spirit, why would everything about us degenerate like this? The writer of these random notes expresses a fragment of his feelings here. <59>

**Summary**

This article compiles news reports from Chinju and P'yŏngyang in April 1923. Chinju news covers public health meetings, widespread harassment of women by idle young men, internal conflict dissolving an independence activist group (Kongjonhoe), students misbehaving, a local official's questionable "guarantee" regarding kisaeng attire, corruption involving a village head seizing a married woman, a Korean woman leaving her husband for a Japanese man, the establishment of a shoemakers' cooperative, a large meeting of the Labor Mutual Aid Society detailing its structure and leadership, issues with the Chinju Normal School entrance exam revealing poor knowledge of Korean history among applicants, and activities of Pot'ŏn'gyo and Ch'ŏndogyo followers. The P'yŏngyang section laments the city's focus on superficial entertainment (like a new tram line and exhibition) and its reputation as a "Capital of Beauty," fearing moral decline and criticizing the lack of serious intellectual, journalistic, or industrial development, urging conscientious citizens to act.
Historically, the article offers valuable glimpses into social problems (gender-based harassment, moral decay, corruption), colonial dynamics (Japanese influence, power imbalances), the nascent stages of labor and social organizations (Kongjonhoe, Nodong Kongjehoe), religious activities (Pot'ŏn'gyo, Ch'ŏndogyo), and anxieties about education and cultural identity (criticism of students and P'yŏngyang's perceived decadence) in 1920s Korea. The detailed structure of the Labor Mutual Aid Society and the specific incidents reported provide concrete social data.

慶南公醫會 開催 慶尙南道 衛生課에셔는 客月 28, 9 兩日間 慶尙公醫會를 開하고 節氣에 따라 생기는 諸 傳染病 預防에 關한 件을 硏究 결의.
婦女子의 困辱 頻頻 日氣가 차차 和暢해짐을 따라 山上이나 郊外로 殺人鬼의 妓生과 亡身本의 술甁을 엽헤 끼고 노리가는 迅死 兩班들이 꽤- 늘어가는 모양이다. 서울 某家의 財産相續에 養子가 두셋式이나 됨도 奇怪한 일이거니와 晋州 人士의 妓生살이에 專心盡力함도 等閑視할 수 업는 일이다. 이도 그러하거니와 20이 될낙 말락한 靑年輩들의 할 일 업시 밤이나 낫이나 갈일 것 업시 父母의 피땀만 빠러먹고 골목골목 妓生집 訪問 專攻이야말로 寒心코도 可憐한 일이다. 近日에 至하여는 晝夜 산양에 눈瞳子가 뒤집혓는지 밤이면 때를 지여 골목에 웅거하야 지나가는 안女子들을 侵辱하는 일이 만흔 그것이다. 裵少女도 當하엿고 崔孃도 當하엿다고. 그리고 天主敎堂에셔 開設한 女子夜學生 特히 處女들이 時間을 맛치고 自己들 집으로 도라가노라 길거리에 나서기만 하면 난데 업는 靑年隊가 뒤를 따르며 몹시 困辱한다고 一般 婦人側의 不平辭는 자못 놉하 잇다. 젊은 子女를 두신 家庭의 父母 되시는 이의 特히 注意하여야 할 일이며 靑年들도 一層 操心하야 몸을 가지는 것이 조흘 듯하다.
共存會員 擊鬪 이 會는 남다른 생각과 출중한 기개로 독립운동에 참가하야 수년동안을 철창에서 고초를 한 가지 한 사람으로만 조직된 것이다. 이 會의 일어난 동기로 말하면 가튼 맘과 뜻으로 가튼 일에 고초를 갓치하여슨즉 日后도 변함 업시 무슨 일이던지 同心合力하야 하자는데 잇슬 것이다. 이 會가 일어날 때 우리는 그 會의 영원한 장래를 축복하엿다. 그런데 지난 3월 1일을 기념키 위하야 그들은 그날 만사를 전폐하고 엇던 주점에 모혀 잔을 밧구며 즐기던 바 그대지 대단도 안은 일에 주먹이 세가 나도록 두들기고 욕을 거두지 못할 만큼 퍼부어 일대 수라장을 이루엇다 한다. 些少한 일에 주먹을 함부로 밧구며 무난히 욕질함을 보건대 可信할 수 업고 한심할 뿐외라. 由此로 共存會는 自解되는 형편이라 한다. 共存會員이여! 맛당히 심사함이 잇스라.
學生으로 酒店 出入 일반이 다 그러니 誰怨誰咎하랴만은 아즉 배흐는 중에 잇는 학생으로 모자에 公普란 帽表를 뚜렷이 부친 채로 주점에 출입함도 불가커든 하물머 밤중이 지난 後에 장거둔 대문을 열나 하야 술이 업스니 미안하나 다른 주점으로 가달나고 사례하며 문 안이 연다고 속히 開門치 안으면 술독을 깨트리겟다고 惹端悖言함이야 온당한 학생의 행위라 할가. 果然이면 특히 주의를 요할 바이라 한다.
有力者의 保証 去月 25일 어느 연회석상에 수개 기생과 시내 유지란 金OO가 모히여 飮樂하던 바 동참한 서장이 일개 기생의 純土木 상하의를 보고 기생이 웨 비단옷을 입지 안코 무명옷을 입엇느냐고 무르매 자기 1인이 가무를 팔어 전 가족이 살어가기에도 부족함으로 비단옷을 입고 십흔 마음은 간절하나 有意未遂라 한즉 그러나 기생이란 본래 아름다운 容態와 찬란한 의복으로써 남자를 꾀이는 것인즉 日后는 다시 土産은 입지 말나 하며 엽헤 안즌 유지 金OO를 보고 기생들의 무명옷 입지 안켓다는데 보증하라 하매 金氏는 一毫 躊躇 업시 快諾하엿다 한다. 그러면 기생들이 돈 업서 시종 무명옷만 입게 되면 金氏는 자기 금전이라로 내여 주단으로 입혀 주겟다는 단안이 잇고서 快諾한 것인가. 然하면 己어니와 그러치 안타 하면 보증의 본의가 무엇이며 抑何心算임을 不知.

平壤에도 올해 5월부터는 전에 업든 시가 전차가 개통되며 이를 축하하는 기념전람회가 열닌다. 이 전람회 개최의 영향 여하를 말할 수는 업지마는 그 소위 신구파라는 광대 노름이나 주야 흥행하고 기생 연주나 간간 너허서 이럭저럭 부랑패類의 유흥을 도두어주는 惡風 폐해나 反及지 안을가 함이 吾人의 一慮이다. 무론 그러케 일반 시민에게 악풍과 악영향을 밋치게 예산은 안하겟지만 예산보다 결과가 틀녀지는 전례가 당국자의 또한 一慮- 되지 안을 수 업스리라 한다. 그리고 또 한가지 염려듸는 것은 평양의 전차는 종로通의 한복판을 종행하게 된 바 서울 가튼 곳에는 그 넓은 도로에 一邊을 치우쳐 통행하여도 살인 傷人의 전차 사고가 無日不在하야 살인 전차의 稱이 有한데 이 좁은 平壤街道의 正中을 처음으로 관통하는 平壤의 전차는 其 성적이 여하할는지 당국 又는 일반 시민의 특별한 주의를 요망하야 마지 못한다.
平壤은 色鄕이라. 자랑할 것이 미색 밧게 업다고 대부분 하류급에서 종전에 늘- 말하던 바인데 최근에 와서는 정말 그 色鄕으로 일삼어 그걸로 멸망을 할랴는지 그걸노써 부흥이 될랴는지는 모르나 酷甚히 성행이다. 南北의 浪子가 방만하야 平壤의 미색을 貪好하며 청년 신교자 청년학생, 그 대가들의 미색화하려는 平壤의 이즘 풍조는 알 수 업는 怪事라 아니할 수 업다. 平壤! 平壤! 4천년에 檀箕의 遺城이 이 아닌가? 禁酒斷煙이 변하야 勸酒勸烟으로 되면 그 얼마나 민족적 경제운동에 반역 도시인가? 綿繡江山이오 제일강산의 자연으로의 찬미를 독점한 平壤城으로서 半島 민족의 반역자가 된다 함이 될 말이리오. 噫라 平壤에 식자- 엇지 잇서 더므를 뜻이 잇스리오. 좀더 부끄러운 말이나 平壤의 식자- 뉘 잇는가? 공연히 애쓰고 焦慮하야 不貪不寐로 탄식에 머므는 有志가 兩三의 人이 잇다고 하면 志强肉弱한 閒士에 불과하리니 이 엇지 此城을 구조할 운동이 생기리오. 배움에 잇는 者가- 이 城에 머므느냐. 뜻 잇고 눈물 잇고 정신의 힘이 잇는 이가 이 성에 머믈너 잇느냐. 잇거든 일어나거라. 체면과 모든 주졉음을 생각지 말고 그냥 분기하여라. 平壤에는 왜 신문이 업스며 잡지가 업느냐. 常設館은 생기면서 왜 貧徒를 취직케 하야 근면과 직업을 찻는 길로 인도하는 공장과 회사는 웨 업는가? 학교에서는 웨 신사를 만들지 안코 망난이를 倍數로 만들어내이느냐. 정신이 잇스면 우리의 온것이 이러케 퇴폐케야 왜 하랴. 漫筆子의 늣김의 1절을 여긔에 발표한다.＜59＞
