Remembering Youth Regarding Sex Education
						Kim Suhyŏng

Everyone tastes the sweet dream of youth (靑春). And who wouldn't reminisce about the past traces of youth and feel sorrow? This is also the time when life's feelings are abundant. The sensitivity of the fire of passion (情火) becomes hot, white-hot, burns oneself (燃), and cannot help but burn its object (對象物) as well. If this heat were compared to a melting furnace (熔鑛爐), even a thousand pounds of iron would dissolve in an instant (瞬間). The motive (動機) for this passion (情熱) arising is when a great change (變化) occurs mentally (精神上) or physically (肉體上). First, the voice (聲音) deepens from the vocal cords (聲帶), and other parts like the breasts and genitals (局部) develop to an embarrassing extent, and sometimes, groundless fantasies (空想) drift about. I call this the first line of the period of youth. The young soul (魂) wandering (彷徨) on this first line harbors all sorts of doubts (懷疑), envisions the unseen future (將來) in various ways in the heart, and sometimes smiles to itself. The more I think, it seems like infinite future happiness (幸福) awaits me. The conception (構想) of happiness rising vigorously sometimes unconsciously reaches out a hand to grope (模索) the empty air (空中). The soul of youth, unable to live solely in fantasy, ultimately cannot avoid being drawn into the real world (現實 世界). A feeling like one corner is completely empty! The disposition (片性) feeling solitude (孤獨) cannot restrain itself from contacting something. What indeed is that something? It wasn't taught by anyone, nor recommended by anyone, but what indeed is this desire (要求) that arises unknowingly (不知不識)? Ah—that object I desired was none other than the opposite sex (異性). I don't know why, but when it comes to the opposite sex, one naturally draws near (接近) to their vicinity (身邊), and in the instant (刹那) hands meet, an indescribable pleasure (快感) arises, and those joined hands seem to have some kind of attraction (牽引性), reluctant to separate easily. As souls reflect each other in this way, they become unable to bear the dream (夢) of yearning (相思) for each other. The ancient saying (古人) that "an instant feels like three autumns" (一刻이 三秋와 갓다) when unable to meet, as if going mad or dying, well depicts such a true scene (眞景). I call this the second line of the period of youth. The whole body (滿身) boiling with blood (血潮), pouring out the full measure (全幅) of love (愛), embracing (抱擁) each other, inseparable—what does this image (象) of the two sexes (兩性) signify (意味)? If this is called the absolute pleasure (絶對 快樂) of human life,<45> there must surely be some meaning (意義) within that pleasure. Some scholars (學者) call this the Creator's arrangement (造物主의 配劑) primarily for reproduction and prosperity (生殖 繁榮). Why? Because if humans were not given such pleasure, they would supposedly not enjoy procreation (生殖). Then, the purpose (目的) of human life is not pleasure, and that pleasure cannot but be merely a means (手段) for engaging in reproduction. Of course, the former theory (說) is not without some reason (一理), but knowing that the entirety (全班) of human pleasure does not solely lie in reproduction, attributing the relationship between the two sexes, which is the realm of absolute human pleasure, only to reproduction cannot but be called a mistaken argument (誤論). Reproduction may be called one duty (義務) of human life, but it is by no means the sole (唯一) purpose of human life. Then, I ask again, what indeed do they mutually anticipate (企待) within that pleasure? Shall we investigate (考究) this truth (眞理) philosophically (哲學的)? Or shall we investigate it in reality (現實)? Philosophy still fails to give us a satisfactory answer (滿足한 回答). Then, will reality itself provide a clear answer (明答)? Right. What can be done when it becomes a problem (問題) that cannot be resolved (解決) apart from reality? That is, the pleasure, delight (歡喜), and joy (喜悅) arising from the encounter (對象) of the two sexes, male and female (男女), lies in fulfilling the development (發展) of life (生). In other words, within pleasure lies the essence (要約) of life. The price (代償) for this precious promise (貴重한 約束) can be called the fusion (融合) of soul and soul, and the interaction (交涉) of flesh and flesh; that is, the unity of spirit and body (靈肉一致) is called the highest value (最高價) of life's essence. Reaching the unity of spirit and body cannot be easily realized (實現) through such simple methods (單純한 方法) and short periods (短少한 時日). The first thing that arises is the concept (觀念) of beauty (美). Whether it's attire (衣服) or posture and behavior (擧動), there is nothing that isn't an expression (表現) of beauty. To make the body clean (淸潔) and add luster even to snow (雪), powder (粉) is applied to the face (얼골), rouge (朱) is applied to make the crimson lips (朱脣) redder, fragrant oil (香油) is applied to make black hair (黑髮) more lustrous (潤澤), and in the so-called style of "adding flowers to brocade" (錦上添花格), rings (指環) with brilliant gems (光彩가 陸離한 寶石類) and exquisite hair ornaments (精巧한 머리 裝飾)—which of these would not capture a man's (男子) fancy? This is generally the external appearance (外觀) of the female (女性), but it is a fact (事實) that men also adorn themselves with a corresponding beautiful appearance. I call this the preparatory action (豫備行動) for the approach (接近) between man and woman. The notice (通知) of love enters through the line of sight (視線). First, it evokes that wondrous external appearance, that is, aesthetic feeling (美感). Aesthetic feeling ultimately beautifies (美化) even the soul (靈魂). This beautified soul, following time and seizing opportunity (機), does not hesitate to whisper words of love. Sometimes it appears in the form of love letters (艶書). Before their efforts (努力), there is no right or wrong (是非), blame or praise (虧譽 褒貶), nothing at all. Only, the temple (殿堂) of their hearts is filled with the heart-wrenching fire of passion (戀慕의 情火). When things reach this point, the opportunity is already ripe (成熟). The errand of instinct (本能), without any hesitation (躕躇), leads to hands tightly grasping (緊握) each other, and the pleasure of the moment (瞬間) of giving a kiss on those hot crimson lips—what is this if not a state of ecstasy (無我夢中)? The two souls melt and fuse (融合) into one, a tingling electricity (電氣) passes through the nerves (神經) of the whole body,<46> and the heart (心臟) pounds at a high pitch (高調)—this is called a stage of the aforementioned unity of spirit and body (靈肉 一致). Who among humans could not envy (羡望) a life (生涯) situated in the exquisite state (佳絶境) intoxicated (陶醉) by this warm, mellow flavor (芳醇味)? However, just as a vessel easy to heat is also easy to cool, if the sweet dream of love (戀愛) also amounts to nothing more than a spring dream (一場春夢), one cannot help but feel the cruelty of life. The protagonist (主人公) of a short life (生命) that doesn't even pass a century! How is the unreliability of human life different from the capricious overturning (飜覆 無常) of wind and waves? If this too is the unavoidable fate (運命) of human life, what use is sighing (歎息), what use is shedding tears? However, seeing that from ancient times (녜로부터) until now (至今), numerous talented beauties and gifted scholars (許多한 佳人才子) have ceaselessly strived and wandered to reverse (挽回) this fate, one realizes how strong (强靭) human attachment (愛着心) is, and at the same time (同時), isn't the Creator's (天公) interference (沮戱) also incessant (支離하지 안이한가)? Observing the ceaseless waves (波瀾) arising in this sexual world (性的 世界), which thoughtful (有心) person could overlook (看過) this as an ordinary matter (尋常事)? Though there are people and institutions (機關) in the world researching (硏究) sex (性), this too just gives the feeling (感) of emitting residual light (殘光) like the twinkle of the morning star (晨星)—what light can that possibly bring to human life? Then, shall we say there is no way (道理) to rescue (救出) the promising (多望) young men and women (靑春 男女) wasting away from the anguish (煩憫) of sex? Where could there be a more significant (重大) social problem (社會問題)? From this viewpoint (見地), I advocate (唱導) that sex education (性的 敎育) should be implemented (施). No, in advanced countries (先進國), there are already many people advocating (主張) this. If providing economic education (經濟 敎育) to give people the way and knowledge (知識) to live (生活) is the grand policy (大方針) of the state (國家) or society (社會), then neglecting the problem of sex, which inevitably destroys (破壞) the foundation (基本) of life, what severe contradiction (矛盾) is this? Now, take a glance (一顧) at the system (制度) of modern education (現代敎育). And then give a glimpse (一瞥) at the modern social system (現代社會制度). Education is nothing more than formulaic (科臼的), restrictive (拑制的), and deformed (畸型的) education, so its result (結果) is to cause vibrant life (潑剌한 生) to wither and die (枯死), or even if it lives, it is of the human-shaped (人形的), mechanical (器械的) type—what life (生命), autonomy (自主), originality (獨創), development (發展), or progress (進步) could there be in that? Consequently, among the current social systems devised (考案) by owners (所有者) of such stale brains (陳腐한 頭腦) who received that kind of education, the system concerning sex is not even worth a glance (一顧할 價値도 업다). Before smashing (粉碎) all these obstacles (障碍), relics (遺物) of old morality (舊道德), there is no way to implement sex education. Conceding a hundred steps (百步를 讓), from co-educational middle schools (男女中等學校) onwards, a course (一課) on sex education must be instituted (科치 안이치 못할 것이라). By thus guiding well (善導) the naturalness (自然性) of sex expressed instinctively (本能的으로 發露), their good fortune (幸運) will undoubtedly enter the right path (順路). Even without unreasonable interference (不合理한 干涉) and unnatural restraint (不自然한 拘束), how would they differ from the flowers and grasses (花草) that grow (生長) in rain and dew (雨露) and stand smiling (笑立) in the spring city (春城)? Only then, perhaps, will a spring breeze blow even in the desolate and rough human society (人間社會), and people will live a life worthy of humans (사람다운 生活).<47>

**Summary**

The author, Kim Suhyŏng, reflects on youth as a period of intense emotional and physical change, marked by burgeoning romantic and sexual feelings. He describes the progression from initial awakening and fantasy (First Line) to deep mutual attraction and the desire for union (Second Line). Kim critiques the idea that the sole purpose of this powerful attraction is procreation, arguing it represents a deeper "essence of life" achieved through the unity of spirit and body (靈肉一致). He laments the superficiality often involved in courtship and the potentially fleeting nature of love. Critically, he argues that the profound impact of sexuality on young lives makes sex education essential, condemning the current restrictive, formulaic education system and outdated social morals for failing to address this fundamental aspect of life. He advocates for introducing sex education from middle school onwards to guide natural instincts positively, believing this will lead to healthier development and a more humane society. For historians, this 1923 article is significant as it reveals a progressive discourse on sexuality within colonial Korea, challenging traditional Confucian norms and critiquing the perceived failures of the modern (colonial) education system. It highlights anxieties about navigating modernity and personal development, expressing a desire for open discussion and rational guidance on sex, a topic usually shrouded in silence.
