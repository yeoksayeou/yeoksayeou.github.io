The Rich Who Cruelly Persecute the Poor

My landlord is a very wealthy man, an executive at a certain bank (某 은행), a person of influence, yet he is a heartless and disloyal person who takes the money earned through the blood and sweat of struggling people and rents out a smelly, dirty room that he himself neglects.

This is not a grief or pain that I alone suffer; it is something endured by all those going through similar situations together. Yet, thinking of those who, perhaps pressed down by money and power, have mouths and hands but cannot speak, suffering inwardly, clutching their aching hearts, and enduring unspeakable contempt – no, thinking of my own situation, I have shed hot tears not just once or twice. One month, trying to supplement my meager livelihood, I fell a bit behind on the rent. He called me in, opened his small eyes wide, pursed his lips tightly like a catfish's mouth, and spewed all sorts of curses that a decent person could hardly repeat. Moreover, without even flicking the ash from the cigarette he was smoking, he practically poked me in the eye with it and roared at me to get out immediately.

Ah, this world is filled with money and power! My fists clenched tightly, and with a trembling voice from my constantly pounding heart, I cried out.

Ah, will the moneyless always have to suffer such agony? That's right. In this world, one cannot live without money and power. Money knows no loyalty, no compassion. It probably doesn't recognize kin or even parents. Ah, when will the have-nots escape from this suffering? Ah, it will likely never happen. It will always be like this. No one can live without money. Will a terrifying thunderbolt ever strike their heads? (,ㅂ) <69>

**Summary**

The author, identified only by initials, vents fury and despair about their wealthy landlord, a powerful bank executive. The landlord is portrayed as cruel and heartless, renting out a squalid room while living off the tenant's hard-earned money. The author recounts a humiliating encounter where the landlord verbally abused and threatened them over late rent. The piece concludes with a bitter lament about a world dominated by money and power, where the poor seem destined to suffer perpetually without hope of escape. From a historical perspective, this article vividly illustrates the stark class tensions and economic hardships prevalent in 1920s colonial Korea. It reflects the resentment felt by ordinary Koreans towards the wealthy elite, who were sometimes perceived as collaborators or beneficiaries of the colonial system, and highlights the deep sense of powerlessness and social injustice experienced by the poor during that era.

가난한 사람에게 혹독한 핍박을 주는 부자
나의 든 집 주인은 돈 만흔 부자로 某 은행 중역으로 세력가로서 어려운 사람의 피와 땀을 흘려가며 버는 그 돈을 밧고 자기는 내버려두는 방 냄새나고 드러운 그 방을 빌녀 주는 몰인정하고 의리 업는 사람이올시다.
나만 당하는 悲痛이 아니오 고통이 아이니 다- 가티 겨거 나가는 그들이건만 혹 돈과 세력에 눌리여 입과 손이 잇스나 말하지 못하고 속으로만 알코 압흔 가슴을 부둥켜안고 말 못할 천대를 바다 나가는 그들을 생각할 때 아니 나의 처지를 생각할 때  나는 얼마나 뜨거운 눈물을 흘닌 젹이 한 두 번이 아니올시다. 어느 달인가 업는 살임을 보태느라고 집세가 좀 밀린 것이 잇는데 불너 들이드니 젹은 눈을 크게 뜨며 입을 매기아가리만큼 맨들어서 사람으로서는 참아 입에 옴기지 못할 별별 욕을 다 해가며 하물며 피우든 담배를 떨지도 안코 그대로 눈을 찔느면서 금방 나가라고 불호령이 떠러집듸다.
아- 이 세상은 돈과 세력으로 충만한 세상이다. 나의 주먹은 힘 잇게 쥐여지며 쉴새 업시 뛰는 두 줄기에 떨리는 소리로 부르지졋슴니다.
아- 돈 업는 사람은 언제든지 이런 쓰림을 바들 것인가? 그럿타. 이 세상은 돈과 세력이 업스면 살지 못할 것이다. 돈에는 의리도 업고 인정도 업다. 동족도 업고 부모도 모를 것이다. 아- 업는 사람은 언제나 이 고통속에서 버서 날 것인가? 아- 업슬 것이다. 언제든지 그럴 거이다. 누구나 돈 업시는 살지 못할 것이다. 그들에게는 언제나 무서운 벽력이 그의 머리에 떨어질까. (,ㅂ) ＜69＞
