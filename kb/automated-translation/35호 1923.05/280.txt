Lost Spring
						Kim Anso
(I send this poem, flown on the spring breeze, afar to Yuam)

Lost spring.
When the cry of the first wild goose echoes through the sky,
And the face of the young lady drawing water reflects in the well,
When the coarse song of the farmer driving an ox laden with manure
Is heard together with the boat song winding around the front mountain—
I long for that spring day of my childhood in my hometown.

When the mist gently covers the warm sunlight,
And the clear fragrance of the azalea flowers wanders about,
When the humming voices of children digging for shepherd's purse by the vegetable patch
Mix with the sound of chicks seeking their mother in the yard—
I long for that spring day of my childhood in my hometown.

Untitled
The wind cries forlornly in the crevice by the stream,<132>
When a small, nameless bird in the willow tree
Sings a dreamlike song, pleased with itself, all alone,
To the front sea, to catch fish,
To the back hill, to pick flowers,
The lost faces of old friends who came and went—
Where now is that spring season of my hometown...

True Apricot.
"It's a tasty true apricot seed,"
We ate them sparingly, sharing with each other,
Until in the end, only two were left.
"Let's plant these and make seeds,"
My sister, four years older, persuaded me.

How many years has it been since planting the apricot seed?
Every year after the azaleas wither,
It has long been so that white flowers bloom on that apricot tree.
"It's a delicious true apricot,"
My younger siblings cherish them,
And every year when the late barley ripens,
They, without a word about making seeds,<133>
Make a fuss, taking turns picking and eating them.

How many years has it been since my sister passed away?
Every year after the apricot blossoms fall,
It has long been so that nameless flowers bloom on her grave.

Time.
Let the flow of time
Flow as it flows,
Neither suppressing turmoil,
Nor suppressing resistance,
Just slowly entrusting it to its own will,
Flowing through the valley of sorrow
That is human affairs,
Winding around the mountain slopes of joy,
Into the great sea of虚無 (Nothingness)
Let it flow ever so quietly, without sound.
And so, always
Let time, flowing as it pleases,
Pass before you.
-At Hwangp'o-<134>
<132-134>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by Kim Anso, published in 1923, evokes a deep sense of nostalgia for the lost springs of his childhood hometown. The poet contrasts vivid sensory details of rural spring – the sounds of geese, farmers, children, the scent of azaleas, reflections in wells – with present realities of loss: faded friendships, the death of a sister symbolized by an apricot tree they planted together, and a general feeling of displacement. The final poem reflects philosophically on the relentless, indifferent flow of time, urging acceptance as it carries human experiences towards "Nothingness." From a historical perspective, the poems, while not overtly political, reflect the melancholic atmosphere and sense of loss prevalent among Korean intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period. The longing for a perhaps idealized rural past, the theme of lost connections, and the concluding location "At Hwangp'o" (likely Shanghai, a center for Korean exiles) hint at the displacement and cultural erosion experienced under colonial rule, making it valuable for understanding the psychological landscape of the era.

(이 詩를 봄바람에 날리며 져 멀니 流暗에게 보내노라)

일어진 봄.
첫 기럭기의 울음소리가 한울을 울리며,
물 깃는 따님의 얼골이 움물에 어리울 때
거름 실은 소를 모는 農軍의 싯거리 노래가
압 山을 감도는 배 노래와 함끠 들리는
내 故鄕의 어린 때의 그 봄날이 그립어.

안개가 다사롭은 해볏을 설게도 덥흐며,
진달내꼿의 맑진 香내가 헤메여 돌을 때
菜田가의 냥이 캐는 아희들의 흥어리소리가
뜰 안에서 어미 찻는 병아리 소리에 석기는
내 故鄕의 어린 때의 그 봄날이 그립어.

失題
바람은 개바주 틈에서 설게도 울며,＜132＞
이름 몰을 적은 새가 실버드나무에서
꿈가튼 노래를 혼자 조하 불을 때,
압바다로는 고기를 낙그려,
뒷동산으론 꼿을 꺽그려
오가든 녯 동무의 일허진 얼골의
내 故鄕의 그 봄철은 只今 어대로...

참살구.
고소한 참살구씨라고
서로 앗겨가며 까먹든 것이
나죵에는 두 알 밧게 안 남앗슬 때에
이것은 심엇다가 種子를 하자고,
네살 우 되는 누이님이 나를 勸햇소.

살구씨를 심은 지가 몃해나 되엿는지,
해마다 진달내꼿이 진 뒤에는
그 살구나무에 하얀 꼿이 피게 된지도 오래엿소.
맛잇는 참살구라고
어린 同生들은 貴해하며
해마다 느즌 보리가 닉엇슬 때면
그들은 種子하자는 말도 업시,＜133＞
야단을 하면서 번가라 따 먹소.

누이님이 돌아가신지 멧 해나 되엿는지,
해마다 살구꼿이 진 뒤에는
그 무덤에 이름 몰을 꼿이 피게 된지도 오래엿소.

때.
때의 흐름으로 하야금
흐르는 그대로 흘으게 하여라,
激動도 삭히지 말으며,
抗逆도 삭히지 말고서
그저 느리게 제 맘에 맛기여,
사람의 일 되는
설음의 골작이를 숨여 흘러,
깃븜의 山기슭을 여돌아
큰 바다인 虛無 속으로
고요도 하게 소리 업시 흐르게 하여라.
그리하야 언제나
졔 맘대로 흘러가는 때로 하야금
너의 압흘 지내가게 하여라
-黃浦가에서-＜134＞
