Article Title: 自然, 나, 詩. (Nature, I, Poetry.)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Nature, I, Poetry.
						Kim Sŏksong

When embraced in the bosom of solemn Nature,
I am suddenly startled.
But the true substance (本軆) of Nature,
Is more startling than that.

My perception is extremely dull.
My poetry is less even than perception.

Having realized this fact,
Though I weep in the sorrow of despair,
I also leap in the joy of hope,
Together with the arteries and veins of my heart.

Nature is my eternal companion,
For I am one particle (一分子) of Nature.

(Between August 5th and 6th at Sŏgoksa Temple) <14>

**Summary**

The poem expresses the speaker's profound awe when encountering the vastness and solemnity of nature. This encounter leads to a feeling of personal inadequacy, recognizing the dullness of human perception and the even greater limitations of poetry in capturing nature's essence. This realization brings both despair and hope, ultimately resolving into an acceptance of being an integral part of the very nature that inspires such awe, finding companionship in this connection. For historians, the poem reflects a common theme in colonial Korean literature: turning towards nature as a source of solace, identity, or inspiration, perhaps as a subtle retreat from or contrast to the political realities of the time. The dating at Sŏgoksa Temple anchors the abstract reflection to a specific place and time.

自然, 나, 詩.
						金石松
莊嚴한 自然의 품에 안길 때,
나는 문득 놀내인다.
그러나 自然의 本軆는,
그보다 더 놀나운 것이다.

나의 感覺은 至極히 무듸다.
나의 詩는 感覺만도 못하다.

이 줄은 깨다른 나는,
絶望의 悲哀에 울기도 하나,
希望의 歡喜에 뛰기도 한다,
心臟의 動脉靜脉과 함끠.

自然은 나의 永遠한 길동무,
나는 自然의 一分子임으로.

(8月 5日로 6日 사이 釋玉寺에서)＜14＞
