Competitive Exhibitions Opened in Yŏngnam
						Nonggusaeng (弄球生)
October 10th, in Pusan

This is the first time a Fisheries Competitive Exhibition has been held in Chosŏn. Accordingly, it seems this day also allowed one to see at a glance how closely the sea is related to our industry. The exhibits from within Chosŏn, reaching over 10,000 items, displayed a wealth of 80 million *won*, promising not only the supply of domestic food and regulation of fish prices but also a bright future for foreign trade. Among them, items from prisons, that is, cheaply sold government monopoly goods (賣占廉 賣品), the special features of the Shimonoseki (下關) special section, the carrier pigeons (傳書鳩) from the Fukuoka Prefecture (福岡縣) Fisheries Station's training, various fish species in the aquarium (水族舘), other processed marine products, and various academic reference materials seemed to most attract the visitors' attention. However, looking at the other side, for the Chosŏn people, who have lost their place despite being the same humanity, the same audience, the thousands of displayed items (舖陳千物) are nothing more than a blind man looking at colorful paintings (盲人의 丹靑 구경). With no printed materials for guidance, and no one to explain the item names written entirely in Japanese script with Furigana over difficult Hanja (窮僻한 漢子), the sight of them bustling about is no different from a pasture where herds of cattle and sheep (牛羊의 群) are forcefully driven (疾驅하는). Furthermore, the severe discrimination (酷甚함) in daily wages even for the small number of female attendants, distinguishing between Japanese and Chosŏn people, seems, no matter how one looks at it, like their own confession that their true intention is not for the sake of the exhibition itself.

October 14th, in Taegu

Is it only true for the Specialty Products Exhibition? Imparting knowledge of Japanese products to Chosŏn people to promote the development of Chosŏn industry, and simultaneously introducing Chosŏn specialty products to the Japanese to foster closer economic relations between Japan and Chosŏn, must be Japan's current national policy.
Thus, their fervent organizing power (白熱한 周旋力) finally secured expenses close to 60,000 *won* and exhibits reaching 80,000 items, devising a well-structured display method and achieving results superior to the Subsidiary Business Products Competitive Exhibition; it would not be an exaggeration to call this their great worldly success. Among these, the unique educational items in the Taiwan Pavilion (臺灣舘) and the ancient Chosŏn artifacts (朝鮮 고대의 器物) in the art museum are all worth a glance, and moreover, the activities of the Silla (新羅) Historic Sites Preservation Society and the bustling state of the spot sales hall (卽賣舘) seem to truly fill the place with the exhibition's atmosphere. However, two-thirds of the exhibits are not essential items for the lives of Chosŏn people, and besides this, the exhibits from the various provinces within Chosŏn are so crude (粗率하야) that they provoke unpleasant feelings in the visitors. Seen from this perspective, the powerlessness of the Chosŏn members, dragged along left and right (左提右携), perhaps just looking to profit from the collection of membership fees (會費排歛), is rather pitiable (矜惻할 뿐이다). But where does the fault lie when the majority of those responsible, the authorities in each province, not only show such little sincerity towards this exhibition but also cause the reputation (聲價) of Chosŏn's specialty products to plummet?＜73＞

**Summary**

This article critiques two competitive exhibitions held in the Yŏngnam region (Pusan and Taegu) in October 1923 under Japanese rule. The author, Nonggusaeng, observes that while the Pusan Fisheries Exhibition showcased industrial potential, Koreans felt alienated due to the exclusive use of Japanese language and discriminatory wage practices. Similarly, the Taegu Specialty Products Exhibition, despite its scale, featured goods largely irrelevant to Korean life and poorly presented Korean provincial exhibits. The author suggests Korean participation felt coerced and superficial, highlighting the disparity between the colonial government's stated goals of economic integration and the reality experienced by Koreans. For historians, this piece offers a valuable contemporary Korean perspective on colonial development projects, revealing sentiments of exclusion, critique of superficiality, and veiled criticism of both Japanese organizers and potentially compliant Korean officials involved in these state-sponsored events.

嶺南에 열닌 共進會들
						弄球生
十月十日 釜山에서

朝鮮에 水産共進會가 개최되기가 처음이오. 따라서 海라는 그것이 우리 산업상에 얼마나 밀접한 관계를 一目에 膾灸케 함도 이 날인 듯 하다, 一萬餘点에 달하는 鮮內의 출품은 八千萬圓의 富力을 발휘하야 國內食饌의 공급과 魚價의 조절은 물론 다시 대외무역의 유망한 前途를 囑目케 한다. 개중 刑務所 즉 賣占廉 賣品, 下關特設關의 특색 福岡縣 水産場養成의 傳書鳩, 水族舘의 각종 어류와 기타 水産加工品 及 각종 학술적 參考品 등은 가장 관객의 시선을 牽引하는 것 갓다. 그러나 그 반면을 보면 동일한 인류, 동일한 관객으로 본위를 失한 朝鮮人에게는 舖陳千物이 모다 盲人의 丹靑 구경에 불과하다. 안내에 관한 인쇄물이 업는데다가 窮僻한 漢子에 전부 日本文의 振假名뿐인 품명을 해설하야 주는 자도 업시 奔走하는 꼴은 맛치 牛羊의 群을 疾驅하는 목장과 무엇이 다름이 잇스며 더구나 소수의 女看守의 日給에까지 日鮮人의 차별이 酷甚함은 아모리 보아도 그들로 하야금 大會를 위하는 本意가 안임을 자백하는 것 갓다.

十月十四日 大邱에서

엇지 特産品陳列大會에만 그럴 뿐이랴. 朝鮮人에게 日本산물의 知識을 授하야 朝鮮산업의 발전을 促進케하고 동시에 日本人에게 朝鮮특산물을 소개하야 日鮮經濟의 交涉을 긴밀케함은 日本現下의 當行할 國策일 것이다.
그리하야 그들의 白熱한 周旋力은 맛츰내 六萬圓에 近한 경비와 八萬点에 달하는 출품을 어더가지고 규모잇는 陳列法을 考究하야 副業品共進會 이상의 우월한 성적을 실현하얏다 함은 그야말로 그들의 出世的 大成功이라 하야도 과언이 안일 듯하다. 其 中에도 臺灣舘의 독특한 敎育品과 미술관의 朝鮮 고대의 器物은 모다 一瞥의 가치가 잇서 뵈이고 더구나 新羅古蹟保存會의 활동과 卽賣舘의 성황은 이것이야말로 대회의 기분을 漲溢케 하는 것 갓다. 그러나 출품의 三分의 二는 모다 朝鮮人 생활의 필수품이 못되는 데다가 이외 鮮內 各 道의 출품은 너무나 粗率하야 관객의 不愉快한 감정을 觸生케 한다. 이로써 보면 左提右携에 끄들니어 會費排歛에나 한 목 보는 鮮人會員의 無力은 도리어 矜惻할 뿐이다 만은 책임상 각도의 當局者된 다수가 이러케 同會에 대한 성의가 적음을 표시할 뿐아니라 朝鮮특산의 聲價를 暴落케 함은 그 죄가 어데 잇스랴?＜73＞
