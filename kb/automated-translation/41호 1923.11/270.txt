After the Great Water (and Two Other Chapters)
						Kim Sŏksong

In the morning, alone by the stream,
Standing, facing the rising sunlight,
A pine tree, ancient or skeletal,
Felled in the last great flood, spoke:
The swift current collapses the bank,
And the collapsing bank laid me low,
But without any word,
I just fell as I fell.

The stream water, kicking pebbles as it flees,
Looks up at the sunlight, smiling brightly,
And seeing me standing there넋없이,
Speaks in a lovely voice like silver bells:
Night or day, always the same,
Flowing downward, ever downward,<11>
Wherever my feet reach,
The Revolution spoken of by people comes.

The morning sun, which had been silent until now,
Lifted its face above the pine grove,
And with a countenance both温厚 (mild/gentle) and 嚴肅 (stern/solemn),
Sent a messenger on the wind to say:
Water, flow on! Pine, lie there!
Bank, crumble without rest!
People, there is nothing to fear,
Revolution is a synonym for Growth (生長).<12>
<11-12>

**Summary**

This three-stanza poem by Kim Sŏksong uses personified elements of nature—a fallen pine, a flowing stream, and the morning sun—to reflect on destruction and change after a "great flood." The pine passively accepts its fate, the stream actively brings "Revolution" wherever it flows, and the sun ultimately declares revolution to be a natural and necessary part of growth, urging acceptance and fearlessness. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1923 under Japanese colonial rule, is significant for its metaphorical treatment of "Revolution" (革命). Directly addressing revolution was dangerous, so framing it as a natural force akin to floods and growth, discussed by elements of nature, likely served as a veiled commentary on social and political upheaval, potentially expressing nationalist aspirations or calls for fundamental change in a coded manner.

아츰에 호을로 시내가에서,
떠오는 해빗을 마즈며 섯슬 때,
지난번 큰물에 쓰러저 누은,
골해나 묵은 소나무가 말하되,
急한 물결은 언덕을 문흐고,
문허지는 언덕은 나를 뉘엇소,
그러나 나는 아모 말도 업시,
쓰러지는 대로 쓰러지고 말엇소.

돌몽이를 발로차며 다라나는 내ㅅ물이,
해ㅅ빗을 처다보며 생글생글 우스며,
넉업시 서잇는 나를 보고서,
은방울가튼 고흔 목소리로 말하되,
밤이나 낫이나 언졔나 한결가티,
아래로 아래로 흘러 나려가는,＜11＞
나의 발이 이르는 곳곳마다,
사람의 말하는 革命이 옴니다.

이때까지 아모 말도 업든 아츰해는,
솔밧우에 얼골을 내어들고,
溫厚하고도 嚴肅한 낫빗흐로,
바람의 使者를 보내어 말하되,
물아 흘러가거라 솔아 누어잇거라,
언덕아 쉬지말고 문허지라,
사람아 두려워할 것은 아니다,
