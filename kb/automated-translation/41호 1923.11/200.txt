Seeing the "Letter from North Hamgyŏng" in the Previous Issue, Questioning Kuktŏngsaeng
						Kang Hakpyŏng from Ranam (Contributed)

In *Gaebyeok* No. 40, that is, the October special issue, there was an article under the title "Letter from North Hamgyŏng" written under the name "Kuktŏngsaeng" (國境生), which detailed the situation in Hambuk (North Hamgyŏng Province) point by point. I do not know about people from other provinces, but anyone from Hambuk would have surely seen that article, and having seen it, must have felt gratified. The writer [I] is also one of those who felt gratified by that article.

However, Kuktŏngsaeng, your pen was too sharp, and your critique was too biased. I do not know if that is the usual habit of people like you who take up the pen.

Kuktŏngsaeng, the points in your article are, generally speaking, facts. However, those facts are all facts that ignore the facts of the other side. In other words, they are facts seen from only one perspective. Look, how much your critique of the Sŏsura Youth Association (西輸羅靑年會) and your critique of the Nail Youth Association (羅一靑年會) exposed the severity of your mistaken view!

It was an incident from last summer. Should we call it the Chŏngjin Prison Break Incident involving Chŏn Il (全一)? Or should we call it the Chŏngjin Prison Break Red Oil Righteous Volunteer Corps Incident plotted by Yi Chŏngguk (李政國)? It was truly an unprecedented rare event that chilled the hearts of the Ch'ŏngjin prison officials. At that time, there was no one living near the Ch'ŏngjin Prison who did not shed tears, and it is said that at the Ch'ŏngjin Christian Church (淸津基督敎會), while praying wretchedly for them, two or three hundred church members wailed aloud. The writer [I] also had occasion to visit the scene to investigate that time and event, and Han Chŏngin (韓正仁), the head guard of Ch'ŏngjin Prison (淸津刑務所), struck the surrendering Chŏn Il with a sword, and even at the recent public trial, that clear scar (傷痕) was visible to our eyes. Sir, if you were to take up the pen, you should write fairly about such circumstances as well; what need is there to needlessly scold the Sŏsura Youth Association and the Nail Youth Association?

Listen. Just because it is the Sŏsura Youth Association, is it utterly impossible that all its members are active with pro-Japanese (親日的) sentiments? Whoever a person may be (毋論誰何하고), in their psychology, each person is different; since a youth association is not the possession of any one individual but a kind of corps formed by the gathering of many people, is it not that there is no reason why all their natures must be the same? I hear it is true that one individual among the Sŏsura Youth Association members caught the escaped prisoner at that time. However, isn't it said that he is now avoiding people and moving around? Accordingly,<102> should one not be able to infer what the attitude of Kwanbuk (關北) society towards that person is? You also critiqued the Nail Youth Association, saying, "It makes the promotion of Japan-Korea Harmonization (日鮮融和) its primary mission," etc. Sir, just because it is the Nail Youth Association, how do you know so well that all its 120-odd members are pro-Japanese? I cannot guarantee that there are no pro-Japanese elements (分子) among them. However, this is not limited to Nail; I know they likely exist among members of youth associations in other regions too. Not only do I know, but I have even heard it directly. How could one refuse membership to such elements? Sir, what about the fact that even among the Nail Youth Association members, there are dignified ideologues (주의자) and thinkers (사상가) here and there?

Sir, if you truly intend to wield the pen, do not look at just one end of the matter, but always look at both ends equally, and be sure to distinguish between the group and the individual. Do not needlessly discuss the whole after seeing one part, or disgrace the entire group because of the actions of one person. In saying this, I am not telling you to cease commentary on the affairs of society or individuals; critique what needs to be critiqued as much as you want, but do so after looking more clearly at the inside and outside. I dare to offer a word of advice to Kuktŏngsaeng, hoping that fairer and stricter criticism will discern the rights and wrongs of our Kwanbuk society, leading to constant renewal and reform. <103>

**Summary**

Kang Hakpyŏng from Ranam responds to a previous article by "Kuktŏngsaeng" criticizing North Hamgyŏng youth associations (Sŏsura and Nail) as biased and pro-Japanese. Kang argues Kuktŏngsaeng's view is one-sided, failing to acknowledge complexity and unfairly condemning entire groups for the actions of individuals. He references the Ch'ŏngjin Prison Break Incident as an example of patriotism and criticizes a prison guard's brutality, suggesting Kuktŏngsaeng should focus on such issues rather than blanket condemnations. Kang urges balanced critique that distinguishes individuals from organizations for the betterment of Hamgyŏng society. For historians, this article offers a window into internal debates within Korean society under colonial rule, particularly concerning collaboration accusations ("pro-Japanese"), the activities of local youth groups, and reactions to anti-colonial actions like prison breaks, revealing tensions and differing perspectives within the nationalist movement or broader society in the northern regions.

前號의 「北鮮來信」을 보고, 國境生에게 質正함
						羅南 姜鶴秉(寄)
開闢 40號, 즉 10月 臨時號 中 「北鮮來信」이라는 제목 하에 「國境生」이란 일홈으로써 咸北사정을 일일히 기재한 일이 잇섯다. 他道 인사까지는 모르거니와, 咸北인사는 누구나 업시 그 기사를 보앗슬 것이며, 보앗스면 반듯이 통쾌히 녁여슬 것이다. 記者 亦 그 기사에 통쾌를 늣긴 사람 중의 하나이다.
그러나 國境生이여, 그대의 붓은 너무나 날카왓스며 그대의 평은 너무나 편견적이엿다. 그것이 붓을 잡는 자 당신가튼 사람의 상습인지는 모르나.
國境生이여, 君의 기사가 擧皆 사실은 사실이다. 그러나 그 사실은 모다 다른 한 편의 사실을 무시한 사실이다. 다시 말하면 一片만 본 사실이다. 보아라, 져 西輸羅靑年會를 평한 것과 羅一靑年會를 평할 것은, 얼마나 君의 誤見의 심함을 폭로하엿는가.
지난 녀름의 일이다. 全一의 事인 淸津破獄事件이라 할가. 李政國의 謀事인 淸津破獄赤油義勇團事件이라 할가. 참으로 絶世의 稀有事로서 淸津獄吏의 간담을 서늘케 한 사건이 잇섯다. 그 때 淸津監獄의 近隣에 잇는 사람들은 눈물을 흘리지 안는 이가 업섯스며, 淸津基督敎會에서는 참담하게 그들을 위하야 기도를 하다가, 이삼백명 교도가 목을 노아 울엇다 한다. 記者-역시 그 때와 그 일을 조사키 위하야 현장을 본 일이 잇섯는대, 淸津刑務所의 간수장 되는 韓正仁은 자수하는 全一을 칼로 쳐서 지난 공판 때에도, 그 완연한 傷痕이 우리의 눈에 뵈여섯다. 君이여, 그대가 붓을 잡엇거든, 이런 사정까지를 공공하게 쓸 것이지, 공연히 西輸羅靑年會와 羅一靑年會 꾸지즐 필요가 무엇인가.
드르라. 西輸羅靑年會면 그 회원이 다-親日的 기분을 가지고 활동할 이유는 萬無할 바가 아닌가. 사람이란 毋論誰何하고, 그 심리에 잇서는 각인각색이라 청년회가 엇던 일개인의 소유가 아니요. 다수인의 집합으로 일너진 일종 군단인 이상, 그 심성이 다-가틀 이유는 업슬 것이 아닌가. 드르매 西輸羅靑年會員 중, 엇던 한 개인이 그 때의 탈옥인을 붓잡아 준 것은 사실이다. 그러나, 그 자는 지금 몸을 피하야 단닌다고 하지 안는가. 딸하서＜102＞ 關北사회가 그 자에 대한 태도의 엇더한 것까지를 推知할 바가 안인가. 군은 또 羅一靑年會를 평하되 「日鮮融和의 촉진을 일대 임무로 한다」 云云하엿스니. 君이여, 羅一靑年會면, 그 회원인 120餘名이 다-親日인줄을 엇지 그러케 잘 아는고. 其中에도 親日分子가 업다고는 장담치 못하겟다. 그러나 이것은 羅一뿐 아니요, 타 지방 청년회원 중에도 잇슬 줄 안다. 알뿐 아니라 내가 직접으로 드른 일까지 잇다. 엇지 그런 分子라고 입회를 불허할 수야 잇겟느냐. 군이여 羅一靑年會員 중에도 당당한 주의자와 사상가가 드믄드믄 잇는 것을 엇지 할가.
君이여, 君이 정말로 붓을 잡으려하거든, 일의 한 끗만 보지말고 반듯이 두 끗을 꼭 가티 볼지며, 그리고 단체와 개인을 반듯이 구별해서 보아라. 공연히 한 끗을 보고 전체를 논하거나, 한 사람의 일을 가지고 全團을 욕보이지 말라. 내가 이러케 말함은, 사회나 또는 개인의 행사에 대한 논평을 중지하라 함이 아니오, 평할 것은 얼마라도 평하되, 더 좀 안과 밧글 발키보아서 하라는 말이다. 감히 한 마듸의 말을 하야써, 國境生에게 충고하며, 보다 더 公嚴한 평론이 우리 關北사회의 시비를 가리여 새로새로 혁신됨이 잇게 하기를 바란다. ＜103＞
