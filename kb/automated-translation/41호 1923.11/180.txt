Miscellaneous News from Ch'ŏrwŏn
						A Reporter in Ch'ŏrwŏn

The richest man in Ch'ŏrwŏn, no, the man pointed to as number one in Kangwŏn Province, the so-called Mr. Ko OO, how is it that he has so much compassion and philanthropy? It's said he loves people far away more than those nearby; truly, it's strange sympathy, money-like sympathy. That is, for the Sŏsŏn [West Korea] Flood Relief, after offering 30 chŏn, 50-something [chŏn], 1 wŏn, then 2 wŏn, he finally said he would give just 20 wŏn, but for the Tōkyō Earthquake Disaster, he decided to give 110 wŏn. Truly strange sympathy, for sympathy.

Our Kangwŏn Provincial Office, spending no small amount of expenses, brought the Tōkyō Earthquake Disaster motion pictures to Ch'ŏrwŏn Eup and let people view them for free. It was truly a thankful deed. For us Korean people to see Tōkyō in Japan, we would have to spend at least 70 or 80 wŏn, so how thankful it was that they let us see Tōkyō while sitting down. However, the tone of speech of the provincial office gentleman, Mr. Kim OO, who came on assignment from the Provincial Office with the pictures – well, it was truly like a provincial office gentleman. He invariably said, "It's like that, I tell you," or "It's like this, I tell you." And also, when he came forward to explain the pictures, he had what seemed to be either geishas or women from drinking houses, two of them, standing there, one holding his overcoat and the other holding his hat. Does a provincial office gentleman, while on assignment with such tragic pictures, make it a rule to trail two geishas behind him? Is that the so-called bureaucratic style under the Government-General today? Instead of asking poor village farmers to give sympathy money, how about sending the money given to those geishas to Tōkyō as sympathy money? Ridiculous.

Our town's youth association, several seasons after its founding, had no word of doing anything, nor did we see it doing anything, but it's said that sometime ago, as a result of the association's executives holding a meeting, they decided to hold a training seminar and hired a paid clerical worker. We thought the youth association's executives were all dead, but that they are starting activities again in the falling leaves of the cold wind is, more than anything, a thankful thing. There is just one thing to request: please do not tread the previous path of being famous in name but lacking in substance again.

The 'Government-General Stakes' of Chinju

There is perhaps nothing that gets driven in more relentlessly—not only in the vast wilderness like Mount Tai (泰山曠野) but also in fertile fields thick with myriad grains, and even right on the rooftops where people are currently living—than the Government-General's survey stakes. However, these are merely pieces of wood, used under the authority called the power of the almighty Government-General. But recently in Chinju, Yŏngnam, there are not a few among the youth, whose futures stretch out boundlessly, who strive pitiably to obtain the glorious crown called the 'Government-General Stake'. Because the name was so strange, I listened for the reason, and it was nothing special; it is said to refer to those who constantly frequent the kisaeng houses in Chinju, which has earned the nickname 'the origin place of the human flesh trade' in Kyŏngsangnam Province, no, in southern Korea. That is, because a portion of those youth, day and night, without regard for any alley inside or outside the city walls, leave no 'human flesh trade' house un-entered, the supreme honorary title bestowed upon them is 'Government-General Stake'. Rather than becoming a 'Government-General Stake' of a kisaeng house, wouldn't becoming a 'Stake of the Palace of Yama (閻羅宮 말둑)' beneath Pibongdam (飛鳳潭下) be, contrarily, a step up? Deplorable, deplorable. (Submitted by Chinju Resident)<99>

**Summary**

This article compiles several short news items critical of contemporary society in colonial Korea. The first piece from Ch'ŏrwŏn mocks a wealthy local man for donating significantly more to Japanese earthquake relief than to Korean flood relief. The second criticizes a provincial official for his arrogant manner and for being attended by geishas while showing tragic earthquake footage, questioning the use of funds. The third notes the belated revival of the local youth association, urging it to be effective this time. A separate dispatch from Chinju describes dissolute young men who frequent kisaeng houses so often they are nicknamed 'Government-General Stakes', implying they penetrate everywhere like the ubiquitous survey markers, and suggests they might as well be stakes in hell. For historians, the article offers valuable glimpses into social critique under colonial rule, highlighting perceived hypocrisy in charity, resentment towards official behavior, local community dynamics (youth groups), and moral commentary on social issues like prostitution and the perceived decadence among some Korean youth, using the potent metaphor of the 'Government-General Stake' to link personal failings with colonial power structures.

鐵原雜信
						在鐵原 一記者
鐵原에서 제일 富, 아니 江原道에서 一位로 指하는 부자, 소위 高OO는 엇지도 그리 동정심과 박애심이 만흔지, 가까운대 사람보다 먼대 사람을 더 사랑한다고, 참 동정도 이상한 錢하 동정이지, 즉 西鮮 水害救濟에는 30錢 50며, 1圓 또 2圓하다가, 겨우 20圓을 내겟다고 하고, 東京震災에는 110圓을 내기로 하엿다 한다. 참 동정치고는 이상한 동정.

우리 江原道廳에서는 적지 아니한 경비를 허비하여 가며, 東京震災 活動寫眞을 鐵原邑에 가저다가, 무료로 관람을 식켯다. 참 고마운 일이엇다. 우리 朝鮮사람으로 日本 東京을 구경할래면 젹어도 7,80圓이라는 금전을 허비하여야 할 터인데, 안저서 東京을 구경케 하엿스니 오즉 고마운가. 그런대 道廳에서 사진을 가지고 출장하신 도청양반 金OO의 言套야-말로, 진실로 도청양반 다웟섯지. 言必稱 『그럿탄 말이야』또는 『저럿탄 말이야』 엿것다. 그리고 또 그는, 사진 설명을 하노라고 나섯슬 때에, 그는, 色酒家인지 妓生인지 하는 것이 두 사람이나 잇서서 하나는 「넹고투」을 들고, 또 하나는 모자를 들고 서 잇게 하엿다. 도청양반은 그런 비참한 사진을 가지고 출장을 하면서도, 妓生을 둘 식이나 뒤에 다리고 단이는 법인가. 그것이 오늘 소위 총독부 치하의 官僚式인가. 불상한 村農民에게 同情金을 내라고 하지말고 그 妓生 주는 돈을 同情金으로 東京에 좀 보냇스면 엇덜가. 可笑.

우리 골 청년회는, 생긴지 幾個星霜에 아모 것도 한다는 소리가 업고, 또 하는 일을 보지 못하엿더니, 얼마 젼부터는, 회의 간부 諸氏가 회의한 결과에, 講習會를 하기로 하고, 유급 사무원을 두엇다 한다. 청년회의 간부가 다 죽은 줄 아랏더니, 木落寒風에 다시 활동을 개시한다는 것은 무엇보다도 고마운 일이다. 다못 한가지 주문할 것은 유명무실의 前轍을 다시는 밟지 맙소사 하는 것.

晋州의 『總督府 말뚝』

泰山曠野는 물론이오 百穀이 욱어진 肥田玉畓이나 심지어 방금 사람이 거주하는 지붕 꼭댁이 일지라도 거침업시 드러 박히우는 것은 아마 총독부 말둑 이상이 업슬 것이다. 그러나 이것은 다못 한 토막 나무땍이로서, 萬能의 총독부 세력이란 권력 하에 사용될 뿐이지만은, 요사이 嶺南 晉州에는, 만물의 영장인 사람이요 사람 중에도 前程이 九萬里인 靑年輩가, 苟且히 「총독부 말둑」이란 榮冠을 어드려고 애쓰는 자가 업지 안타나. 일흠이 하두 이상도 하기에 원인을 들어보닛가, 별 것이 안이라 慶南 안이 南鮮에셔 人肉장사 原産地란 別號를 어든 晉州의 기생집에 無常 出入하는 자를 일너 말함이라고. 즉 그들 일부의 청년은 주야로 城안 城밧 어느 골목 할 것 업시 人肉장사 집쳐노코는 안이 들어박히우는 곳이 업슴으로 그를 至尊至待하는 賞稱이 「총독부 말둑」이란 말이다. 기생집의 「총독부 말둑」이 되는 이보다는 飛鳳潭下의 「閻羅宮 말둑」이 되엿스면 그것이 도로혀 進一級이 아닐가. 可嘆可嘆. (晉州生投)＜99＞
