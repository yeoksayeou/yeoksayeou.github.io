Publication Date: 1923년 11월 01일
Article Title: 어두운 밤 숩 숙에서. (In the Dark Night, Deep in the Forest.)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

In the dark night, deep in the forest.
						Kim Sŏksong

In the dark night, deep in the forest,
Kneeling alone, sitting,
Immersed in aimless thought—
The solemn darkness, layer upon layer,
Envelops my frail body and soul,
And the sword of the wind, wandering through space,
Whistles between the tree branches—
Am I the pine tree, or is the pine tree I,<12>
Is the place I stand the earth, or the heavens?
My spirit has lost even its own self.

In the blue sky visible above my head,
Speaking of the bygone affairs of a thousand ages,
Speaking of the future of eternity,
Countless stars merely whisper.

However, I finally ended up crying.
No matter how eloquent the stars may be,
Unable to understand their words,
I, I finally ended up crying.

And so, I knelt down again,
Brought my two hands together against my chest,
...Oh Lord, I pray, I pray,
Please make those stars disappear,
If you will not do so,
Please pluck out these pitiful eyes...
How many times, how many times I prayed,
Asking for the know-it-all stars to be gone.<13>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏksong describes a moment of profound existential crisis experienced by a speaker alone in a dark forest at night. Overwhelmed by the darkness and the incomprehensible whispering of the stars, which seem to hold eternal truths, the speaker loses their sense of self and connection to the world. In deep despair and frustration at being unable to understand the stars' message, the speaker ultimately prays either for the stars to vanish or for their own blindness, seeking escape from the torment of confronting the vast, unknowable universe. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this poem, written in 1923, powerfully conveys feelings of isolation, helplessness, and a desperate longing for relief from an oppressive, overwhelming reality (symbolized by the stars). Historians might find it significant as an expression of individual spiritual and psychological distress during a period of national subjugation, where personal angst could intertwine with the collective experience of colonial oppression and the search for meaning in a seemingly indifferent universe.

어두운 밤 숩 숙에서.
						金石松
어두운 밤 숩 속에서,
호을로 무릅꿀코 안저,
지향업는 생각에 잠착할 때―
嚴肅한 어둠은 겹겹으로,
가날핀 나의 등신과 넉을 싸오고,
空間을 헤매는 바람의 검님은,
나무가지 사이에 휘파람 불제―
소나무가 나인가 내가 소나무인가,＜12＞
나의 선 곳이 땅인가, 한울인가,
나의 얼은 나 自身조차 일허바렷다.

머리 우로 뵈이는 파아란 한울엔,
千古의 往事를 말하는,
永劫의 未來를 말하는,
無數한 별들만 속살거린다.

그러나, 나는 마츰내 울고말앗다.
별이 아모리 雄辯일지라도,
그의 말을 알아듯지 못하는,
나는, 나는 마츰내 울고말앗다.

그리하야 나는 무릅을 다시 꿀코,
두 손을 모아 가슴에 대이고,
...님이어 비압노니 비압노니,
저 별들을 업시하여 주소서,
그러치 아니하시랴거든,
이 可憐한 두 눈을 빼어주소서...
몃 번이나 몃 번이나 빌엇다,
아는 체하는 별들을 업시해 달라고.＜13＞
＜12-13＞
