**Summary**

This article consists of four separate announcements from the magazine *Kaebyŏk*. The first announces the opening of a new outpost in Mokp'o to cover several districts in South Chŏlla Province, listing the assigned reporters. The second and fourth announce changes in leadership and staff at the P'yŏngyang and Kanggye branch offices, respectively, providing new contact information and asking readers for continued support. The third announcement corrects several errors (addresses, phone numbers, names) in advertisements published in the magazine's previous New Year issue. From a historical perspective, the article reveals the operational structure and geographical reach of a major Korean magazine during the colonial period, including its reliance on branch offices and specific personnel. The explicit mention of locations, names, and even business details like postal transfer accounts and corrections to advertisements for various businesses (pharmacy, photo studio, stores) provides a granular view of commercial and media activity in 1926 Korea. The association of the Kanggye branch with the local Ch'ŏndogyo office (天道敎宗理院) is also noteworthy.
