New Publication Introduction

The True State of Russia (露西亞의 眞相)
By Kim Chun-yŏn (金俊淵)
This text, which was serialized for a long time in the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) and received much welcome (歡迎), has now come out to the world again as a booklet (冊子). Its content (內容) is abundant (豊富) and the writing (文章) is plain, making it a supreme good book (無上의 良書) for all gentlemen (諸氏) who wish to know Russia (露西亞).
【Fixed Price One Won】
Kyŏngsŏng-bu, Chongno 2-ch'ŏngmok, No. 42 (京城府 鐘路 二丁目 四二番地)
Publisher: Chungang Sŏrim (中央書林)
Postal Transfer: Kyŏngsŏng 7451 (振替 京城七四五一番)

Ascending Youth (昇天하는 靑春) By P'ain Kim Tong-hwan (巴人 金東煥)
The sorrow (설음) of Chosŏn (朝鮮), over which there has been anguish (苦悶) for a long time, now comes to be expressed in one aspect (一端) through this "Ascending Youth." In this poetry collection (詩集), one can see the unfolding (展開) of life (生命) stirring (跋動) with a small strength in a single stream coming down (나려오는) from snow-covered Paektu Mountain (白頭山).
Western-style binding (洋裝), 177 pages (頁). Fixed Price 70 Chŏn.
Kyŏngsŏng-bu, Yŏn'gŏn-dong, No. 121 (京城府 蓮建洞 一二一番地)
Publisher: Sinmunhaksa (新文學社) <39>

**Summary**

This article announces two new book publications. The first is "The True State of Russia" by Kim Chun-yŏn, previously serialized in the Chosŏn Ilbo, described as an informative and accessible book about Russia. The second is "Ascending Youth," a poetry collection by P'ain Kim Tong-hwan, portrayed as expressing the long-held sorrow of Korea, drawing imagery from Paektu Mountain. For historians, this piece offers insight into the intellectual interests (Russia/Soviet Union) and literary expressions of national sentiment circulating among Koreans during the colonial period in the mid-1920s. It also provides concrete details about publishers (Chungang Sŏrim, Sinmunhaksa) and book prices in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) at the time.
