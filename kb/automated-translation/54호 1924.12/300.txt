기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

痛哭
						基鎭
Amidst the turmoil, this year too draws to a close. Truly, it was so chaotic that one couldn't get one's bearings, and spent in such disarray, this year also confronts us right before its final month. Fallen leaves tumble on the ground, the wind wanders in the void, and as flocks of birds seeking nests distance themselves from human villages shrouded in evening smoke, the day also quietly passes. Truly, it sets quietly. As the sorrowful twilight flickers, human houses fall asleep. Thus, this repeats, and after just a few more days pass like this, we will bid "Adieu!" to 1924... Truly a sorrowful thing. It is quiet. It is desolate. The western sky reveals its forehead, dyed red through the white poplar trees. It is now twilight. I stood on a desolate field cleared of weeds.

I stood on a desolate field. Dried, fallen tree leaves are scattered here and there, and bare trees, left only with switches, huddle and die. Rotten grass lies scattered sparsely like graves, and fallen leaves and pine needles float down the stream. It is an outskirts incomparably desolate. There is no life, no heat, no pungent stimulus that was abundant in summer. Amidst the ordinariness, it has withered and become dilapidated. But is there nothing in the winter wilderness that decays in desolation?

Withered. Everything is withered. Amidst the withered, there is the beauty of decline (凋落). If there is beauty in the spring of construction, there is also beauty in the winter of destruction. If there is music within construction, there is also music within the great catastrophe where the entire earth tilts. Thus, I now lent my ear to the music of this great catastrophe. Crying sounds seep out from all places. From within the stream in the ice-bound ditch, or from the fallen leaves tumbling on the ground, from the naked tree branches,<126> sounds arise from all places. Ah, the music of catastrophe fills the wilderness!

If human worlds had no twilight, if human worlds had no winter of decline (凋落), we would not have known the precious taste of this catastrophe. We would not have heard the wailing of this wilderness. That's right. We would not have heard the wailing of the wilderness.

Ah, the sun sets. The sun sets beyond the horizon. What is precious? What is dignified? What has value? The sun sets. Beyond the rough thicket, the sun sets...

Is there joy in the heart? Is there generosity in the heart? Is there a peaceful feeling? Is there ample love? Ah, it's empty! Utterly empty. Nothing is inside the head. Only the sound of this great wailing cry is heard. Though I am not a poet, though I am not a philosopher...

Like my desolate, cold heart
Everything has withered
Ah, forever!
...
There are faint sounds heard in fragments. There are heart-rending sounds heard in fragments. Ah, what is this? Is it the grumbling of fallen leaves? The sobbing of the stream? The crying of a bell? Is it the cry of blood, seeming to tear the heart that plunges with the soul? Is it the heart-wrenching sigh of the wind coming and going between heaven and earth? I do not know, but there is a sound I hear
......Sombre et glace!
Tout est Fletris
Pour toujours!<127>
Ah, it fills my ears. It does not cease. Is it a song of mourning (弔喪) filling the ruins...? Time passes! It is merely fleeting. Ah, it is futile.

What did I do this year? Truly, what did I do?
- Due to the ceaseless earthquakes, my heart, burned and turned to ash, the ruins of my heart, are now undulating with unknowable feelings like a tide. Tree branches that grew in summer now shoot up starkly skeletal into the empty sky, and the tree leaves that cast thick shadows on the ground fly away weeping in the wind. The haetae, chased away by the bustle of the Exposition (共進會), cries, shouts, sighs, and sobs in every village, everywhere, crossing mountains and passing valleys throughout all of Joseon (朝鮮). The ruins of the heart become even more desolate. Without a life worth living, without true love, carrying the ruins of a heart larger than myself, what am I startled by now, what do I feel, what do I seek, what am I led by, what do I lead, what do I believe, where, ah, where am I fumbling towards? Everywhere people live, where the people of the earth gather, every single person carries the unseen ruins of the heart! Should I laugh or should I cry? It's useless! Since I am weak and lack courage, what use is self-mockery? What is there to be valiant about! Let's abandon the decaying life, cut off the moldy compromise, drive away winter and run. With the "Feeling of Steel" (鋼鐵의 感情), let's dig into the earth and flee. Grasping the earth's heart with both hands, let's gallop towards the "End" (終局)... 』
Having written such words, what am I doing today, a year after truly writing this, and what have I been doing during that time? To ask myself anew now is also a foolish endeavor, but I truly lament that I cannot fathom the fleeting time, the transient human affairs.

What was I born to do, and what need is there for me to be alive? What does it matter if my bean-husk-sized self exists in this world or not? What significant <128> fact would it be if a limitlessly small, tiny existence existed in the morning and disappeared in the evening? Yet I move, I speak, I desire (意欲), I judge right and wrong (是非), and I act.

Now I also have feelings...
I ask. What is "being alive," and what is "a person?"

Anyway, I have lived for 22 years, and especially regarding myself as an individual from the end of last year until today, I have lived through a year full of changes. Anyway, I have lived. To speak most honestly, I have been doing my utmost to achieve what I desired (意欲), falling many times, stumbling many times, getting lost after falling into the Riddle I myself had posed, being deceived by the Trick I myself had laid out, and failing in the Game I myself had started. This is my history of the past year. I have tried sincerely loving others, sincerely advising others, sincerely guiding others, and sincerely introspecting, desiring (意欲), and acting myself. But was there even one thing among them that came to fruition for me? Thus, I was broken, fell down, got up again, and limped. My life – my external life was like that, and my internal life was also like that. The spirit that realized the Dilemma of living took another step forward and realized the Dilemma of life. Trying to die, I couldn't die; trying to stay alive, it became difficult to endure the pain of the limping leg and spirit. Truly, both are difficult things. In this way, I have lived through a year full of changes, a tumultuous year, or going back further, 22 years of jumbled confusion, tumbling head over heels. Truly, I have lived like this.
Ah, I ask again. What is "being alive," and what is "a person?"

- "Oh God, why do you bind me so that I cannot but be alive?"
There is a passage somewhere in Chekhov's (체호프) works with the meaning that a single tree standing on a hill cried out like this. Truly, for what <129> reason does one prepare (?) a single tree and bind it so that it must stand there and live? Is it not truly bothersome for the tree, bound so that it must open its leaves, bloom flowers, and bear fruit in spring, and bound so that it must shed its leaves, huddle, and stand still quietly in winter? Setting aside for a moment that this is seen as the law of nature and the principle of growth?
In the same way, is it not truly bothersome, and moreover, limitlessly painful, that humans too are bound so that they must live?

The brilliant flowers and lush leaves of spring and summer have all disappeared together, and now a landscape of limitless desolation appears before the eyes. For a life that lacks the joy of a spring garden buried in flowers, the generosity of a summer morning smiling in the dew, the delight of the wind passing between blades of grass and tree branches, the beauty of starlight reflected in water, the brilliance (光輝) and honor of the sun and moon, the joy of living – for such a life, indeed, indeed, how much misfortune must being alive be? I ask again. What is life...
- "Truly... what is the reason?... Why are we alive?..."
The voice of Nastya (나스티-야), enveloped in despair and lament, shouting while pounding the table in 'Night's Lodging' is heard. Truly, what is the reason? Why are we alive? I too do not know.

Ah, it is stifling. What is to be done about this situation?
『The East and the West are mine.
The South and the North are mine.』
The person who sang thus was Walt Whitman (왈트·휘트맨). The person who said the East and West, South and North were all his was a poet. He was a person who knew how to eat, drink, and taste the rain and wind, dew and frost, grass and trees, flowing water, sunlight and moonlight, earth and sand, light (빗) and fragrance. He was a person who knew how to love the reflection of stars in the water. He was a person who tasted <130> the generosity of the earth wrapped in the wings of night.
But what must that person's heart have been like when he said the East, West, South, and North were his? I believe he must have said such words while beating his chest. Truly, they are heart-wrenching words. They must be a cry bursting from a stifled heart. Yet, even amidst the stifling feeling, there is generosity...
Do you have the stifling feeling in your heart, the generosity, to say the East, West, South, and North are yours? Do you have the joy of a summer morning smiling in the dew? Do you have an awakening to the pleasure of the coming and going wind? Do you hold love for the winter insects sleeping in the earth? Do you even have a heart that yearns for the fallen leaves fluttering in the wind? Truly, that's right. Do you even have a yearning heart for things that perish?
I don't know!

I don't know. I only know that before us lies the "Long Rest" (長久한 휴식). Like the words of the dear girl Sonya (쏘-냐) in "Uncle Vanya" (와-냐아젓시), let us live until we die, 'having not tasted even a little of what happiness tastes like,' and when the "Long Rest" comes, let us view death as returning home.
That's right! Let us view death as returning home! Let's sing my favorite "Anywhere, anywhere, out of the world!" and return.
Ah, shall I truly return! Shall I return! Cutting off all desires (意欲), abandoning all attachments, let me return gracefully with my conscience! It would be better to wither away like the leaves of a withered tree fluttering in the garden, together with conscience, together with a brush, but ah, what has become of this body, what has become of this mind; it tries to advance on its own but stops its own feet, tries to act decisively (斷行) on its own but also hesitates on its own.
What is to be done with this cursed, worm-like existence, lacking strength, courage, and heat (熱)! Until when will I flail, caught in this precious dilemma (띠렘마), and end my life! <131>

Still, I must live. Truly, I must live a little longer yet. Fine. Even if I receive ridicule, mockery (嘲罵), and contempt (唾棄), let me still remain alive. Until the faint light (微光) of the candle lit in this heart disappears, until the tenacious attachment rooted in this heart vanishes, and until the grass of desire (意欲) stirring in my head dies down, let me stay alive! This is by no means Schopenhauer's (쇼펜하우에르) "Blind Will" (쁠라인드·윌).
That's right! Let me remain alive. Until the "Long Rest" comes, let me sustain this lump of contradiction (矛盾의 肉塊). It cannot be helped. This is what it means to be human! This bas-relief of contradiction (矛盾의 浮彫), this illegitimate child of dilemma (띄렘마), forever an unfinished product (未成品) of eternity!...

What more shall I say now! The only thing I know for sure is that one lives and desires (意欲), and while desiring, disappears into emptiness. To state my feelings most honestly, this is all. Is falling into a dilemma (띄렘마) a way of life? Is it a convoluted tactic (탁크릭크)? Such things are trivial judgments (是非). I avoid the game of logic as much as possible. Only, until just one kind-hearted person (마음이 고흔 사람) among a thousand, or perhaps just one among ten thousand, remains alive in this world, I too shall remain alive. Because the kind-hearted person is my comrade (캄레-드)...
Everything perishes. Just like this rough wilderness facing the catastrophe (카라스트로프) of this winter. Ah, it is desolate. Quiet. Limitlessly lonely. Fallen leaves rustle down onto the stagnant water pooled in the rough field. Bright red pine needles are piled on the ground. The dark red earth falls asleep.
This day is all gone. This year draws to a close. I suddenly realize anew that I have arrived at the threshold of this year.<132>

Alright, let's send off this year. Let's cleanly send off this tumultuous year of inaction (無爲), full of changes. Let's wrap it in the torn pages of an old calendar and send it away. Sending you off, I only weep. Together with the countless multitude who, like me, made a waste of you through inaction (無爲) and incompetence (무능), I send you off with lamentation (통곡). Together with the countless white-clad people (흰 옷 닙은 백성들) who, like me, starved and ate, unable to extend the fists they clenched, floating upon you, the flowing current, I send you off with lamentation (통곡). Together with the countless masses of people who, like me, weep and struggle in dilemma (띄레마), who merely live and desire (意欲) only to vanish like smoke into utter emptiness (空空虛虛), I send you off and merely sniffle. Together with the countless people like me who live hoping for a comrade (캄레-드), hoping for just one kind-hearted person (마음 고흔 사람) who might be one in a thousand or one in ten thousand, I send you off and only lament (통곡).
Then disappear forever. Following you, I too shall go! I shall return. Ah, from the heavens and earth, from tree branches and yellowish-brown grass, from the endless wilderness, from human villages veiled in evening mist, from everywhere, there is a faint sound. Is it mourning (弔喪) you, or mourning oneself? Ah, the sound of weeping fills the universe!
- 24. November -<133>

**Summary**

In this highly melancholic and existential piece from late 1924, the author, Kijin, reflects on the end of a tumultuous year, viewing the desolate winter landscape as a mirror of his inner state and the state of the world. He grapples with feelings of emptiness, failure, and the painful dilemma of existence – the inability to either truly live or die. Referencing Western thinkers and literary characters (Whitman, Chekhov, Gorky, Schopenhauer), he questions the meaning of life and suffering, ultimately resolving to endure until the "Long Rest" of death, clinging to the hope of finding kindred spirits ("comrades") amidst the pervasive sense of decay and collective sorrow, symbolized by the weeping of the entire nation ("white-clad people"). The article is significant for colonial history as it captures the profound despair, disillusionment, and sense of national suffering ("wailing haetae," "white-clad people") experienced by Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule. The author's personal angst is deeply intertwined with a broader sense of national impotence and decay, making it a poignant expression of the era's psychological landscape.
