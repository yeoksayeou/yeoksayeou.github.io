Countless Forms and Appearances
From October 14th to November 15th

(October 14th) At Sŏch'ŏn Public Common School, all Korean instructors and students initiated a strike due to the misconduct of Principal Deguchi Futoshi (出口太) on three occasions. In Yongjŏng Village, Tongjin Township, Kangso County, an anthracite mine exploded, severely injuring Kim Pong-gwan and two others. In Changbai County, China, Ch'oe Sun-myŏng and four others from the Kwangjŏngdan (光正團), previously based in 14-dogu, were arrested by officers from the Sin'galp'a station.

(October 15th) In Guangdong, China, fierce battles erupted between government forces and the Merchant Corps army. Mr. Sun Fo, son of Mr. Sun Yat-sen, passed through Korea from Japan heading towards Fengtian. Spain mobilized a million troops for the conquest of Morocco. Near Wŏnsan, a fishing boat capsized due to strong winds, resulting in the drowning of Kim U-jŏng, Kim Pong-jŏng, and two others. In Tokyo, Japan, a 14-year-old boy, Yamaiki Hikaru (山域光), was arrested for attempting to assassinate the magnate Ōkura Kihachirō.

(October 16th) Members of the Ch'ŏlhyŏldan (Iron Blood Corps) who advocated for Korean independence through force, Yi Kyŏng-ho, Hŏ Yong, and An Se-jin's faction, appealed their preliminary convictions in Ch'ŏngjin and elsewhere to the Kyŏngsŏng Court of Review. French parliamentary members recommended recognition of Russia to the Prime Minister. The Fengtian Army repelled the Zhili Army and entered Shanhai Pass. The continued trial of Ch'oe Yun-dong, Yi Su-yŏng, and Song Tu-hwan's faction regarding the major incident that occurred in Kyŏngbuk in July was reconvened.

(October 17th) The Beijing Diplomatic Corps issued an order to the Shanghai consular corps for the expulsion of Xu Shuzheng. In Tonghŭng, Huch'ang County, two independence fighters were killed by police officers. The Beijing government issued arrest warrants for five Zhejiang leaders, including Lu Yongxiang and He Fenglin, to provincial governors. At Tarang Pass, South Township, Ch'osan County, independence fighters seized weapons from police officers. (Kim Ch'an-dŭk was transporting them on two horses – Kim was killed).

(October 18th) Min Yŏng-dae, who had been arrested at Kyŏngsŏng's Sŏdaemun Police Station, was transferred to the prosecutor's office. About 20 Korean laborers at the Chŏnju Electric Company went on strike demanding wage increases.

(October 19th) The new Swedish cabinet was formed with Mr. Branting (후랑킹코) as Prime Minister. The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs issued a reply to Japan's memorandum concerning rights and interests in Manchuria and Mongolia. A severe storm occurred in the Tanch'ŏn region, causing much damage to crops and collapsing houses.

(October 20th) The German parliament was dissolved. The Allied powers decided to return German properties such as customs houses, forests, and land located in occupied territories to Germany. Chŏng Chae-dal, Sin Paek-u, Yi Sŏng, Yi T'ae-su, and others involved in the communist propaganda incident were transferred to the prosecutor's office. A large mountain fire broke out in Kŭmgangsan and continued to burn for days. The trial of P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl, leader of the Ŭisŏngdan, was opened at the Sinŭiju District Court.

(October 21st) Belgium decided to sign the Allied protocol. Indian troops landed in Guangdong to protect the lives and property of British citizens. Large mountain fires broke out and continued to burn in the Kyŏngsŏng, Puryŏng, and Kilchu regions of Hambuk province. About ten executives of the Wando County Anso Township Labor Association, including Song Nae-ho and Ch'oe Hyŏng-ch'ŏn, were detained at the Sup'o police station in connection with a certain major incident. O Su-yŏng, a 28-year-old youth from Hoengsŏng County, committed suicide out of despair due to the lack of understanding and oppressive coercion from his obstinate father and elder brother.

(October 22nd) Kim Chŏng-p'il, the beauty who poisoned her husband, was sentenced to life imprisonment. At Wŏnsan sea, three barmaids, Yi Sun-ae, Yi Kwang-*, and Yi Ha-ja, threw themselves into the sea in a suicide attempt; one was rescued. In Taejang Village, Iksan County, about 600 farmers caused a major disturbance, attempting to destroy the Iksan Water Control Gate due to a drinking water issue.

(October 23rd) China's Feng Yuxiang turned against the Zhili Clique, led his troops into Beijing, and simultaneously demanded an order from the President to suppress Wu Peifu. A fire broke out at the house of Kim Yŏng-ok in Kahoe-dong, Kyŏngsŏng, and 3-year-old child Kim Han-bok burned to death. <32>

(October 24th) Japan held an emergency cabinet meeting in response to the rapid changes in China's turmoil and announced the decision to dispatch troops to the border area. Feng Yuxiang, having entered Beijing, issued an order for peace talks. President Cao Kun issued orders to dissolve the cabinet and convene a national assembly. Wu Peifu was stripped of his current official positions by presidential decree. The All-Korea Student Oratorical Contest, hosted by the Tonga Ilbo, was held in Kyŏngsŏng. Two Kyŏnggi Province special envoys of the Taehan T'ongŭibu, Kim Kŭng-ch'i and Kim Ch'o-sik, were arrested in Kyŏngsŏng.

(October 25th) China's President Cao Kun and the Yan Cabinet submitted their collective resignation. A large fire broke out in Sinŭiju, completely burning down 8 houses. Mr. Chŏng Kyu-sŏn, who had been arrested at Sŏdaemun Police Station on suspicion of involvement in a major incident, was released without charges.

(October 26th) In Kalchŏn Village, Taesan Township, Ch'angwŏn County, a Japanese hunter named Itō Toraota shot and injured two Korean children, Mun Yŏng-sul and Ch'oe Sŏk-i. Three members of an independence group invaded the house of Ch'oe Yun-bok in North Village, Kogwan Township, Ŭiju County, and took 4,000 won in military funds.

(October 27th) The British, American, and Japanese diplomatic missions residing in Beijing held an important meeting regarding the turmoil in China. An agreement was reached between Sun Yat-sen and Chen Jiongming in Guangdong. Yi Tong-su of Napch'ŏngjŏng, Chŏngju, who received a 15-year sentence in absentia for the attempted assassination of Yi Wan-yong and had been a fugitive for 16 years since, was arrested and transferred to the P'yŏngyang prosecutor's office. A group of Japanese Diet members inspecting China suffered great humiliation from British soldiers on a train between Beijing and Tianjin.

(October 28th) Ch'ŏndogyo held a grand ceremony commemorating the 100th anniversary of the birth of its founder, Suun Taesinsa. The Japanese cabinet resolved to directly manage the South Manchuria Railway (Mantetsu). France recognized Soviet Russia (Worker-Peasant Russia).

(October 29th) The trial of O Kŭn-yŏng, Kim Yong-wŏn, and others from the Taedongdan, who were arrested while fleeing after an unsuccessful attempt to take Yi *-gong (李*公) to Shanghai, was opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court. Lu Yongxiang, the governor of Zhejiang who had sought exile in Japan, entered Fengtian via Korea. Five or six members of an independence group invaded the house of Yun Ch'ang-ŭi in Ch'angwŏn Village, Songjang Township, Ŭiju County, and took 40 won in cash and certificates worth 600 won. △ A disturbance occurred in Ahyŏn, Koyang County due to the cutting of electrical wires.

(October 30th) A large fire broke out near the Shiba Detached Palace in Tokyo, completely burning down over 1,100 houses. The 5th All-Korea Football Tournament, hosted by the Chosŏn Sports Council, was held in Kyŏngsŏng. Japan-Russia negotiations, which had resumed in Beijing, were suspended again. The Fengtian Army occupied Qinhuangdao. Sun Yat-sen entered Guangdong and held a national construction conference with his staff. Wando Labor-Farmer Association executives Pak Ki-suk and Yi Kap-chun were again transferred to the Mokp'o prosecutor's office on suspicion of involvement in a major incident.

(October 31st) China formed a new cabinet: Acting Premier and Minister of Education Huang Fu, Foreign Affairs and Finance Minister Wang Zhengting (C.T. Wang), Interior Minister Wang Yongjiang, etc. In Shimohorikawa Village, Toyama Prefecture, Japan, during the autumn grand army maneuvers and coinciding with the visit of the Prince Regent, a Korean appeared with a bomb amidst heavy security, causing a great commotion; the said Korean threw away the bomb bundle and committed suicide while fleeing. China's Sun Yue Army occupied Baoding, Cao Kun's base, and Luoyang, Wu Peifu's base.

(November 1st) China's President Cao Kun signed the abdication decree, and Premier Huang Fu took over affairs. Mr. Wu Peifu issued a ceasefire order on all fronts. In Yŏngp'ung Village, Ko Township, Ch'osan County, two members of an independence group were killed by police officers.

(November 2nd) Several hundred tenant farmers of the Oriental Development Company (Tōyō Takushoku) branch in Pungnyul Township, Chaeryŏng County, having failed in their petition to the Tōtaku regarding tenancy fees, continued their movement day and night without eating or sleeping. The electricians' strike in Tokyo, Japan, became even more severe, being called "Safety Day." Mrs. Im Su-yŏng, wife of the late independence group leader Mr. Sin P'al-kyun, despaired upon receiving news of Mr. Sin's death and committed suicide together with her daughter. German President Ebert issued an order restricting foreign capital imports. The Samga Labor Friendship Society in Kyŏngnam held a protest movement against malicious landlords but dispersed after shouting "Manse!" three times due to police prohibition.

(November 3rd) Mr. Yi Pong-nyong and Mr. No Ŭng-man of the Wŏnsan Youth Association slip-of-the-tongue incident were acquitted at the Kyŏngsŏng Court of Review. Kim Yong-wŏn and O Kŭn-yŏng of the Taedongdan incident were sentenced to 2 years and 8 months of imprisonment, respectively. Inch'ŏn police arrested three youths who entered the country from China: Yun Ch'ang-man, Kim Ka-jin, and Yang Chae-bo. Executives of the French United Socialist Party resolved to support the current government. The German Democratic Party resolved to establish freedom and peaceful politics. The International Opium Conference opened in Geneva (Chairman: Siamese delegate).

(November 4th) A fire broke out in a grain stack at the house of Kim Kyŏng-ch'un in Iri, Chŏnbuk, causing damage worth over 2,000 won. British Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald submitted his resignation, and simultaneously, Conservative Party leader Stanley Baldwin <33> received the mandate to form a cabinet. The French parliament convened. △ In Cuba, Liberal Party's Gerardo Machado was elected president. Diplomatic relations between France and Russia were restored, and ambassadors were exchanged (Soviet Ambassador to France: Krasin, French Ambassador to Russia: Herbette). Kim Ŭi-jin, Pak T'ae-jun, and others arrested since November 1st in connection with the so-called Chinju major incident were released without incident.

(November 5th) Japanese Army Bomber No. 7, which was supposed to fly non-stop from Tokorozawa to Kyŏngsŏng, made an emergency landing in Taegu due to mechanical failure. Over a hundred tenant farmers from the Tōtaku in Namnyul Township, Chaeryŏng County, and over 200 tenant farmers from the Tōtaku in Sain Township, Nonsan County, also rushed to the Sariwŏn Tōtaku branch with petitions against the Tōtaku's excessive collections. As a result of the US presidential election, Republican Calvin Coolidge was re-elected (Vice President: Charles G. Dawes). Beijing's Feng Yuxiang surrounded the Forbidden City of the Xuantong Emperor and pressed for the Emperor's departure; the Xuantong Emperor fled by car.

(November 6th) Kim Chi-sŏp of the Nijūbashi Bridge bombing incident in Japan was sentenced to life imprisonment by the Tokyo District Court. In Inch'ŏn, a conflict arose between the Stevedore Union and rice mills over wage increases. Around five hundred Tōtaku tenant farmers from Pungnyul Township, Chaeryŏng County, angered by the Tōtaku's excessive collections, held an emergency tenant meeting and entrusted negotiations with the Tōtaku to six committee members. The foreign diplomatic corps in Beijing issued a warning to the Chinese government, stating there was a risk of communist danger following the expulsion of the Xuantong Emperor. Old retainers of the Qing Dynasty, angered by the Xuantong Emperor's expulsion, appealed to the foreign diplomatic corps. The Beijing government issued an order through the president for the restoration of political rights for political offenders (from July 1, 1920, to November 2, 1924).

(November 7th) The trial of Yi Kyŏng-ho and others involved in the Ch'ŏlhyŏldan incident was opened at the Kyŏngsŏng Court of Review. As this day was the 7th anniversary of the revolution in Worker-Peasant Russia, solemn commemorative ceremonies were held in that country. Various ideological groups in Kyŏngsŏng, such as the General Federation of Labor and Farmers, the General Federation of Youth, and the Sinhŭng Youth Association, also planned to hold commemorative lectures but were stopped due to police interference. The British Baldwin cabinet was announced. The Soviet Ambassador stationed in Beijing, Mr. Karakhan, invited Chinese cabinet members and university professors for friendly relations with Bolshevik comrades.

(November 8th) The Austrian Seipel cabinet resigned collectively due to failure in preventing the railway general strike. The Japanese Ministry of Finance decided to provide funds for drought relief: 10 million yen for mainland Japan and 5 million yen for Korea. China's Feng Yuxiang came to Tianjin to meet with Zhang Zuolin and Duan Qirui. Italian General Maffio Carli di Bardi (메피오-칼리바듸) declared a duel against Prime Minister Mussolini.

(November 9th) In Kyŏngsŏng, within the Seoul Youth Association, an ideological group called the Chŏkp'aktan (Red Hail Group) was organized with about 30 members (Platform: 1. We aim for the liberation of Korea. 2. We aim for the establishment of a social order without class confrontation. 3. We aim to organize the currently chaotic front of the Korean liberation movement). The railway strike in Austria became extensive, prompting President Hainisch to convene an emergency parliament.

(November 10th) Kim Chi-sŏp of the Tokyo Nijūbashi incident appealed his life sentence. Another large fire broke out in Onjŏng-ri, Kŭmgangsan. The Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department arrested certain major offenders active in Kyŏngsŏng for independence movement and communist propaganda activities, simultaneously confiscating pistols and bombs.

(November 11th) The trial of Ch'ŏnma unit commander Ch'oe Si-hŭng, who had appealed his death sentence, was opened at the P'yŏngyang Court of Review. As this day was the anniversary of the European Great War armistice, grand commemorative ceremonies were held in various countries in Europe and America. Soviet representative Karakhan rejected all Japanese proposals. Zhang Zuolin, Lu Yongxiang, and Feng Yuxiang came to Tianjin and held important meetings with Duan Qirui.

(November 12th) The new British cabinet proposed the reconstruction of naval ports. The Italian lower house reconvened. Feng Yuxiang in Tianjin concealed his actions, sensing danger from Zhang Zuolin.

(November 13th) Nanba Daisuke (難波大助), the Japanese high treason offender (the 26-year-old son of former Diet member Nanba Sakunoshin, who fired a gun at the Prince Regent proceeding to the opening ceremony of the 48th Imperial Diet at Toranomon on December 27th last year), was sentenced to death by the Tokyo Supreme Court. The Italian Communist Party initiated protest demonstrations against the nationalist government, leading to the arrest of about 60 people.

(November 14th) In Nishikanbara District, Niigata Prefecture, Japan, an area of over 100,000 pyeong collapsed. China's Jiangsu Province declared independence until the Beijing government is fully formed. About 200 famine victims from Sŏhae Haesop'aho Township, P'yŏngwŏn County, relocated to the Pui Farm in Kangwŏn Province.

(November 15th) Japan's treason offender Nanba Daisuke was executed. Trade negotiations between Russia and Germany commenced. China's three giants, Zhang Zuolin, Feng Yuxiang, and Lu Yongxiang, nominated Duan Qirui as president. <34>

**Summary**

This news digest covers events from mid-October to mid-November 1924, chronicling a wide array of incidents within Korea and internationally. Domestically, it reports on student strikes (Sŏch'ŏn), labor actions (Chŏnju Electric, Inch'ŏn dock workers, Tōtaku tenant farmer protests), arrests and trials related to the independence movement (Ch'ŏlhyŏldan, Taehan T'ongŭibu, Taedongdan, Kim Chi-sŏp, Ch'oe Si-hŭng, P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl) and communist activities, Japanese violence against Koreans (Ch'angwŏn shooting), accidents (mine explosion, boat capsizing, fires), and social issues (farmer protests over water, suicides). Internationally, the focus is heavily on the political turmoil in China (Feng Yuxiang's coup, Cao Kun's abdication, warlord conflicts, foreign intervention concerns, Puyi's expulsion), political changes in Europe (Swedish cabinet, German parliament dissolved, UK election, French recognition of USSR, Austrian strike), and Japanese events (assassination attempt, cabinet decisions, Nanba Daisuke trial/execution). The digest paints a picture of a volatile period with significant political instability in East Asia and ongoing resistance against colonial rule in Korea.

For historians, this article offers valuable snapshots of daily life and major events under Japanese rule. It highlights the persistent activities of various independence movement factions (Kwangjŏngdan, Ch'ŏlhyŏldan, Ŭisŏngdan, Taehan T'ongŭibu, Taedongdan, Ch'ŏnma) despite arrests and suppression, indicating the movement's resilience. The reporting on labor strikes, tenant farmer protests against the Oriental Development Company (Tōtaku), and student activism reveals widespread social discontent and organized resistance beyond armed struggle. Furthermore, the close tracking of events in China underscores the interconnectedness of regional politics and how instability there directly impacted Korea and Japanese policy (e.g., troop movements, refuge for Chinese figures). The mention of communist groups and the police crackdown reflects the growing influence of socialist ideologies and the colonial authorities' response.

(동 13일) 일본의 大逆犯 難波大助(작년 12월 27일 일본의 제 48회 의회개원식에 행하는 攝政殿下를 虎之門에서 放銃한 前 代議士 難波 作之進의 子 26세 청년)는 東京 大審院에서 사형 언도를 당하고 伊太利 공산당은 국수정부 반대시위운동을 起하다가 60여명이 체포되다.
(동 14일) 일본 新濱縣 西蒲原郡에는 10만여평의 地가 함락되고 중국 江蘇省은 北京정부가 완성되기까지 독립을 선언하고 平原郡 西海 海蘇罷湖面의 飢民 2백여명은 江原道 不二농장으로 移去하다.
(동 15일) 일본의 逆犯 難波大助는 사형집행이 되고 露獨간 통상교섭이 개시되고 중국의 張作霖 馮玉祥 盧永祥 3巨頭는 段祺瑞를 대통령으로 추대하다.＜34＞
