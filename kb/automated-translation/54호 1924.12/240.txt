기사제목	咸南列邑大觀, 明太王國인 元山府 (A Grand View of Hamnam Towns, Wŏnsan Prefecture, the Kingdom of Pollack)
기사형태	소식 (News/Report)

咸南列邑大觀, 明太王國인 元山府 (A Grand View of Hamnam Towns, Wŏnsan Prefecture, the Kingdom of Pollack)

Looking up, one sees Changdŏk Mountain (長德山); looking down, one sees Yŏnghŭng Bay (永興灣). The long, bent shape of the city lies horizontally like a bent bow (彎弓), dividing the Korean village and the Japanese village to the south and north. Amidst the forest of chimneys (煙突) from foundries, belching out hazy black smoke day and night as they burn away the food of the poor and the spirit of the youth, various figures come and go: female students with unbound pigtails (말총머리); modern gentlemen (時體 新士) wearing only overcoats after pawning their durumagi; bar girls (갈보떼) from drinking houses (色酒家) singing the sorrowful song, "Wŏnsan in Hamgyŏng Province is a good place to live, but I can't survive because of the Japanese rascals' tyranny"; boatmen singing the fisherman's song (漁夫歌), "Listen, pollack! You die, I live. Passing the state exam (進士及第) comes from you, becoming a magistrate or governor (守令方伯) comes from you"; and laborers, staking their lives on 30 or 40 chŏn a day, dressed in rags, leaning on their A-frame carriers (지게 목발), standing silently. This place, where one's ears ache from the sound of church bells, the sound of trains (鐵馬聲), and the clattering sound of Japanese geta (계다소리), is, needless to ask, <87> Wŏnsan Prefecture (元山府).

This Wŏnsan Prefecture occupies a somewhat central part of the east coast of our peninsula, facing Wŏnsan Bay within Yŏnghŭng Bay, which is embraced by the Hodo (虎島) and Kalma (葛麻) peninsulas. Its area (廣袤) is 20 chŏng east-west, 35 chŏng north-south, with a surface area of 0.437 square ri. At the mouth of Yŏnghŭng Bay, islands like Sindo (薪島), Songdo (松島), Hoesado (會沙島), and Modo (茅島) are scattered like Go stones (散棊), naturally protecting against wind and waves. The bay's width is about 16 ri east-west and about 24 ri north-south. The tidal current within the bay is extremely gentle, with the tidal range limited to merely 3 ch'ŏk, and there is no trouble of freezing (凍結之患) even in winter, making navigation and anchoring both convenient. It is truly the only good harbor on the east coast. Wŏnsan Prefecture is surrounded on the north, west, and south by Tŏkwŏn County (德源郡), and faces Wŏnsan Bay to the east, having about 10 ri of coastline. The harbor water is clear, and the depth reaches over 18 ch'ŏk even at low tide. Thus, its quay walls (築港岸壁) can accommodate two pairs of 3,000-ton class ships alongside. Behind the northern part of the city stands a high and steep mountain named Changdŏk (長德), and to the south of the city lies a mountain thick with pine forests, which is Namsan (南山). This port was first opened in the Kyŏngjin year (庚辰年 - 1880) of Emperor Kojong (李太皇), making it the second port after Pusan Harbor, which has a long history as an open port. At that time, it was called Pongsu-dong (烽燧洞) and was merely a desolate fishing village where reeds (蘆荻) rustled (蕭蕭). However, following the progress (進運) of the times, the number of internal and external migrants gradually increased, and commerce and industry also developed, forming the city of today. The city consists of Wŏnsan-ni (元山里) and Wŏnsan Harbor (元山港), separated by the former Akada River (舊赤田川 - Japanese name), with the two areas adjoining each other. Wŏnsan-ni has historically been a leading market in northern Korea, and Wŏnsan Harbor comprises the former Japanese settlement and other urban areas. The entire city is divided into 49 chō (町洞 - districts). According to a recent survey, the total number of households in the city is 6,117, and the total population is 28,506. Among them, Koreans number 4,066 households and 20,390 people; Japanese number 1,910 households and 7,509 people; Chinese number 127 households and 567 people; and other foreigners number 14 households and 32 people. It is the most prosperous city on the east coast of Korea. The city possesses (具有) a Prefectural Office (府廳), Tŏkwŏn County Office (德源郡廳), Yŏnghŭng Bay Fortress Command HQ (永興灣要塞司令部), Army supply depot (陸軍슆�속 창고 - *text corrupt*), Customs House, Chamber of Commerce, and various other public offices, showing no particular difference from other ports. Furthermore, educational institutions include the Japanese Wŏnsan Middle School, Public Higher Girls' School, and two Public Elementary Schools; and for Koreans, one Public Commercial School, <88> two Public Common Schools, plus the private Pogwang (保光), Paesŏng (培誠), and Haesŏng (海星) schools, the Luce Memorial School (樓씨), Chinsŏng Boys' and Girls' School (進誠), and Wŏnsan Kindergarten. Haesŏng is Catholic-affiliated, while the other four schools and one kindergarten are all Protestant-affiliated. As for religion, there are churches/temples for Christianity (Protestant), Catholicism, Ch'ŏndogyo, and Buddhism. Industrial institutions include 4 banks, 1 starch (澱粉) company, 1 electric company, 1 branch of a mail/shipping company (郵般會社支店 - likely NYK Line), 1 branch of the Oriental Development Company (拓植支店), 3 fisheries companies, 3 trading companies, 2 brewing companies, 1 transport company, 1 rubber company, 3 warehouses, 2 financial cooperatives (金融組合), and 1 diving apparatus cooperative (潛水器組合), but most are managed by Japanese.

For transportation, the Kyŏngwŏn Railway (京元鐵道), opened in Taishō 3 (1914), and the Hamgyŏng Railway (咸鏡鐵道), opened in Taishō 9 (1920), pass through the area, facilitating connections with various regions. In addition (加之), since the Japan Sea transversal route (日本海 橫斷線路) began in Taishō 7 (1918), relations with Japan have become closer. Moreover, the harbor construction (築港), completed by the Government-General as a project from Taishō 4 (1915) over 7 years of intermittent work at a cost of 2.64 million won, has further provided convenience (便) for ship docking. Its sea routes are about 290 ri from Pusan (釜山), about 413 ri from Shimonoseki (下關), about 328 ri from Vladivostok (浦鹽), and about 470 ri from Tsuruga (敦賀). Besides this, for land routes, the Kyŏngwŏn Highway (京元街道) reaching Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) via Anbyŏn (安邊) and Kosan (高山), the P'yŏngwŏn Highway (平元街道) reaching P'yŏngyang (平壤) via Tŏkwŏn County (德源郡), the Hoeiryŏng Highway (會寧街道) reaching Hoeiryŏng (會寧) via Yŏnghŭng (永興) and Hamhŭng (咸興) are first-class roads, and other second and third-class roads are all completed, making land and sea transportation extremely convenient.

As mentioned above, Wŏnsan occupies a naturally advantageous location, and thus commerce and industry have also developed. The trade volume for the recent year reached 4,657,308 wŏn in exports (輸移出) and 12,777,864 wŏn in imports (輸移入). Major export items are rice, wheat, soybeans, fish, hemp cloth (麻布), minerals (鑛物), etc., while major import items are millet (粟), foreign timber (洋材), paper goods, fabrics (포목), metal products, foodstuffs, etc. Factories include 1 power plant, 5 ironworks, 5 printing shops, 3 rice mills, 9 breweries, 1 rubber manufactory, and 1 each for glass (硝子), brick (煉瓦), lumber, and canning (鑵詰). There are the Upper Market (上市), Lower Market (下市), and a livestock market, three markets in total, each of which is prosperous (殷盛). The total annual sales volume of these markets reaches over 487,000 wŏn. Besides these, the sales at the fish market and the public market also amount to enormous sums. Moreover (尙又), Wŏnsan Harbor's natural conditions are suitable (適宜) for fishing (漁魚), so the catch is also abundant. The important fish types are pollack (명태), mackerel (鯖), Spanish mackerel (鰆), yellowtail (鰤), sea bream (鯛), flounder/flatfish (鰈), herring? (鯟), salmon (鮭), cod (鱈), agar-agar (石花菜), and laver (海苔). Among these, pollack reaches an annual production of 3 million wŏn (for all of Hamnam - 咸南 一圓). Since ancient times (自來), it ranks alongside sardines (鰯) from Kangwŏn Province and yellow croaker (石首魚) from Chŏlla Province as one of the three major fisheries of Korea. It goes without saying that this will be a particularly promising industry in the future. Besides this, the production value of processed marine products also reaches over 850,000 wŏn annually.

Finally, to speak briefly of its history, this prefecture was originally part of the Pulnae Yeguk (不耐濊國) inhabited by the Maek tribe (貊種). <89> Later, it was conquered by Koguryŏ (高句麗) and called Ch'ŏnjŏnggun (泉井郡) or Ŏŭlmae (於乙買). During the Silla (新羅) period, it was called Chŏngch'ŏnhyŏn (井泉縣) or Tongmogun (東牟郡). In the Koryŏ (高麗) dynasty, it was called Yongju (湧州). In the early Chosŏn (李朝) dynasty, it was called Sŏnch'ŏn (宣川), or Ich'un (宜春), Ch'unsŏng (春城), Tŏkchu (德州), undergoing constant changes until it was renamed Tŏkwŏn County (德源郡) in the 19th year of King Sejong (世宗). After undergoing numerous changes thereafter, it was opened as a port in the Kyŏngjin year (庚辰 - 1880, Meiji 13) of Emperor Kojong (李太皇). Later, in the Kapsin year (甲申 - 1884, Meiji 17), a Trade Supervision Office (監理署) was newly established, making it the center for overseas relations. In October of the Kyŏngsul year (庚戌年 - 1910), it was renamed Wŏnsan Prefecture (元山府), and in April of Taishō 3 (1914), the prefecture system (府制) was implemented, bringing it to the present day. Furthermore, Wŏnsan also has many famous sites and historical relics, but these are deferred to another article and omitted here (玆에 略하다).

大陸的 景趣로 본 元山港의 風光 (The Scenery of Wŏnsan Harbor Viewed with Continental Charm)
Wŏnsan Branch, Kim Ch'un'gang (金春岡)

Wŏnsan Harbor is located at the southern end of South Hamgyŏng Province on the east coast of the Korean peninsula, at latitude 39 degrees 10 minutes 53 seconds North, longitude 127 degrees 26 minutes 8 seconds East. To the southwest, the Changbaek mountain range winds (蜿蜒) and connects (相連); to the east, it faces the vast (洋洋) East Sea (조선해 - Sea of Chosŏn); the Hodo Peninsula surrounds the north like a folding screen; the Kalma Peninsula embraces the southeast like a celestial bridge; and islands like Sindo, Songdo, Sahoedo, Modo guard (扼) the harbor entrance like scattered stones (飛石). The harbor width is about 16 ri east-west and about 24 ri north-south. In terms of location, it is truly one of Korea's rare (稀有) great harbors.

Since old times, when speaking of places with beautiful (佳麗) mountains and waters in Korea, it is said: 1. Wŏnsan 2. Masan 3. Mokp'o. Wŏnsan is indeed hailed as the foremost among the three great scenic spots, and its bright and beautiful (明媚) scenery has a continental charm (大陸的 景趣).

Try climbing Sirubong (詩樓峰) peak in Wŏnsan and gazing towards the bay entrance. The islands dotting the bay like blue conch shells (靑螺) resemble potted stones (盆石) holding azure water. The largest one visible in the distance is Yŏdo (麗島), and the round, white object at its southern tip is the lighthouse. From here, small islands gather on the sea, forming an archipelago (一群島), seeming ready to answer if called. Observe the white sand and green pines of the Kalma peninsula; isolated in the sea, forming a bow-shaped long sandbank (弓形長汀) stretching far to the southeast, is Hwangt'odo (黃土島). Turning your head and looking right, the Hodo peninsula lies obliquely ahead; the high peak at the cape's end soars into the sky; Songjŏn Bay (松田灣), where the sea deeply penetrates, makes one wonder if the distant mist and haze (煙霞杳) are clouds or the sea. Looking down at your feet, the large and small houses where we humans dwell are packed closely together (櫛比), and on the streets where people and horses interweave (相織), one feels the excitement that this is truly a living stage of the struggle for existence. You can see the green of the pine forest at Myŏngsa Simni (明沙十里) reflected on the white sand. Looking southeast from there, in the fields of Anbyŏn and Tŏkwŏn counties (安德 兩郡 - *Likely Anbyŏn and Tŏkwŏn*), where fields and paddies are connected (相連), farmhouses are scattered here and there. The rural scene, where sounds of chickens and dogs are heard among the trees and at the foot of the mountains (樹陰山 麓鷄犬이 相聞), is like a single painting (一幅의 圖畵).

Looking at the sea surface, large and small steamships emit smoke, and their whistle sounds move the mountains and rivers. Sailing ships gliding smoothly with the fair wind gather in threes and fives. The solitary islands like potted stones (盆石) on the blue sea; the lighthouse shining white (白垔) in the sunlight stands in the middle of the harbor—that is Changdŏkdo (長德島 - *likely an error, possibly refers to the lighthouse near Yŏdo or another island, not the mountain*). Further extending the gaze (縱眺), the continuous ranges and overlapping peaks (連巒疊峰) are like greenish-black eyebrows (翠黛語); the closer ones are like a light smile (淡冶笑). Alas, the misty waves (烟波) are vast (洋洋), water and sky are one color. Added to this, water birds (碧浪水禽) fly high, making one feel an overflowing sense of magnificence (壯快).

To the left, in the lush green (靑靑鬱鬱) pine forest, the melody of a zither (琴韻) could be heard; to the right, there is the grand view of the vast and boundless (茫茫浩浩) waves breaking on the shore (岸打浪). Truly, Wŏnsan Harbor should be viewed for its continental charm.

元山花柳界 (Wŏnsan's Flower and Willow World)
Wŏnsan Ilp'ungnyurang (元山 一風流郞 - A Wŏnsan Playboy/Man of Taste)

Regarding the kisaeng of South Hamgyŏng Province, in the past (己往), when the power of governors (監司), county magistrates (郡守), and district magistrates (使道) was omnipotent, it seems there were locally produced (土産) young ladies in several towns. However, in Wŏnsan, they say there have been absolutely no kisaeng who could be called local products since the dawn of time. Even seeing just a few (個나) <90> kisaeng young ladies in recent years, they are all imported goods, either from P'yŏngyang or Taegu. There are two agencies (券番 - kwŏnbŏn). They are called Ch'unsŏng (春城) and Wŏnsin (元信). The number of red skirts (紅裙 - kisaeng) belonging to these two agencies is quite large. However, many of them came here as rejects (落傍筆者) from the Golden Kingdom of Kyŏngsŏng or their fondly remembered hometown mountains and rivers. The young ladies might get angry, but most are second-rate (쓰레기판 - trash heap). Without further critique, let's pick out a few smart and demure young ladies and bring them on stage.

Wŏnsin Kwŏnbŏn's top star (大將), Miss Pak Nokchu (朴綠珠裙)! They say this young lady is number one in two things: number one in Samnam (Southern provinces) songs, and number one in being unattractive. Her singing is truly masterful (名唱). She sings well enough not to fall behind Hwa Chung Sŏn (花中 仙楚香 - *likely a famous kisaeng comparison*). But lately, it's said her complexion is haggard (초췌하다) due to a fragrant dream (香夢) within a surface romance (겉연애) with some businessman.

Ch'unsŏng Kwŏnbŏn's top star, Miss Hwang Kŭmhyang (黃錦香), is from P'yŏngyang, but because she studied under Yi Tongbaek (李東伯 - *famous pansori master*), she sings all sorts of songs, from Samnam to Sŏdo (Western provinces) styles. Although she's considered a Japanese-style beauty and even reputed to have an incomparably kind heart ("can't find a better heart than Hwang Kŭmhyang's"), ever since she ran off (줄다름) to Pongch'ŏn (奉天 - Mukden) with a pockmarked gentleman (郎君) named Chŏng XX, her mind is said to be confused (착란하다) by dreams of loving money (愛金倂夢).

This time, appearing in the second rank (副將), are Kim Okchu (金玉珠) and Kim Myŏngok (金明玉). Myŏngok is in Ch'unsŏng, Okchu in Wŏnsin. Both are kisaeng full of affection and resentment (多情多恨) and skilled in singing and dancing (能歌能舞). However, Okchu plays only for practical gain, constantly lamenting her lack of a lover, while Myŏngok has a good heart and plays with vanity (허영), like a beauty versatile in all aspects (팔방미인격). Here come the beauties: Hwang Kŭmsŏn (黃錦仙), Yi Kŭmhwa (李錦花)! As for Miss Kŭmsŏn, some literary man recently even gave her the name Hwajunghwa (花中花 - Flower Among Flowers). Truly, her every smile (一*一笑 - *text corrupt*) makes all men's hearts ache, and her gait is also so lovely that it's called "fragrance arises with every step" (步步生香). She pretends to compose Hanja poems (漢詩) and paint ink paintings (墨畵). Also, because she serves her parents devotedly, she has a high reputation in Wŏnsan as a filial daughter. Lately, she secretly exchanges glances with the XX literary man. Kŭmhwa is beautiful indeed. But onlookers find her spiteful (얄미웁다고 한다). Behind her stands the young wealthy man Kim XX. Anyway, Kŭmsŏn and Kŭmhwa are like red plums and yellow chrysanthemums among the flowers and willows of Wŏnsan. Here comes the new trendsetters (新流行 牌). The charming Sŏ Hyangp'a (徐香波) and the pouty (?) Pak Kyŏnghŭi (朴瓊姬) receive love from the modern, high-collared (高襟兒) crowd with their new-style short songs (新式短歌) and Japanese short songs (倭短歌). Behind Kyŏnghŭi stands Mr. Han XX, a new figure in Wŏnsan. Go ahead and suck them dry! The pollack (北魚 - northern fish) in Wŏnsan sea are all yours, the salt is all yours too. <90>

**Summary**

This collection of articles from December 1924 provides a multifaceted view of Wŏnsan, a major port city on Korea's east coast during the Japanese colonial period. The first piece details its geography, impressive natural harbor, rapid growth since opening in 1880, demographics (showing significant Japanese presence alongside Koreans and Chinese), infrastructure (port facilities, railways, schools, businesses – many Japanese-owned), trade statistics (large import surplus), and key industries, particularly the vital pollack fishery. The second article offers a lyrical description of Wŏnsan's scenic beauty, emphasizing its "continental charm." The third delves into the city's entertainment world (*kisaeng*), noting the performers are mostly "imports" from P'yŏngyang or Taegu and gossiping about prominent individuals, their talents, patrons, and perceived characters.

From a colonial history perspective, the articles are revealing. They explicitly mention the hardship under Japanese rule ("Japanese rascals' tyranny") and the clear division between Korean and Japanese residential areas. The economic data highlights Japanese dominance in modern businesses and infrastructure development (port, railways) crucial for colonial exploitation and trade links with Japan. The description of social life captures the mix of traditional and modern elements, the presence of various social classes (laborers, modern gentlemen, students), and the cultural dynamics reflected in the *kisaeng* world, including the influence of figures from other regions and the adoption of Japanese songs. Historians would find the detailed statistics, the blend of boosterism and social commentary, and the glimpses into everyday life and colonial power dynamics particularly interesting.

咸南列邑大觀, 明太王國인 元山府
치여다보니 長德山이오, 나려 굽어보니 永興灣이라. 허리 굽은 긴 시가가 彎弓가티 橫在하야 조선인촌과 일본인촌을 남북으로 구분하고 빈민의 양식, 청년의 정신을 주야로 다 썩혀 태워 내너라고 濛濛한 黑烟을 토하는 주조공장의 煙突이 林立한 속에 당긔 업는 말총머리의 여학생, 두루막이 전당 잡히고 외투만 입은 時體 신사, 「咸鏡道 元山이 사람이 살기 조와도 왜놈의 등살에 나 못살겟네」라는 비애의 노래를 부르는 色酒家 갈보떼, 「명태야 말 들어라. 너는 죽고, 나는 살자. 進士及第도 네게서 나고, 守令方伯도 네게서 난다」는 어부가를 하는 船夫, 하루 3, 40錢에 목을 매고 헌털뱅이 옷에 지게 목발을 주고 묵묵히 섯는 노동자 등 온갖 형형색색의 인물이 왓다갓다 하고 예배당 종소리,정차장 鐵馬聲, 日人의 달각거리는 계다소리에 귀가 압흔 곳은 不問可知＜87＞ 元山府엿다.
이 元山府는 우리 반도 동해안에 稍 중앙부를 점하야 虎島, 葛麻 兩 반도로 포옹한 永興灣 내의 서남안에 당한 원산만에 임하얏스니 其 廣袤 동서 20정, 남북 35정, 면적 0,437방리다. 永興灣口에는 薪島, 松島, 會沙島, 茅島 등이 散棊와 가티 나열하야 천연으로 풍파를 防하니 灣의 廣은 동서 약 16리, 남북 약 24리요, 灣內 조류는 극히 완만하야 간만의 차가 僅히 3척이내에 止하고 冬季에도 凍結의 患이 無하야 항해정박이 共히 편리하니 실로 동해안 유일의 良港이다. 元山府는 북서남의 3방이 共히 德源郡에 포위되고 동은 원산만에 임하야 약 10리의 해안선을 有하며 港內는 水가 淸하고 수심이 간조의 時에도 18척 이상에 달함으로 其 築港岸壁에는 능히 3천톤급의 선박 2쌍을 橫着할 만하고 其 시가의 북방 배후에는 高峻한 산이 有하니 名은 長德이오, 又 시가의 남에 松林이 울창한 산이 有하니 그것은 南山이다. 本港은 李太皇 庚辰年에 비로소 개항되얏스니 개항장으로 장구한 역사를 有한 부산항에 次되는 港이다. 당시에는 烽燧洞이라 칭하야 蘆荻이 蕭蕭한 一寒 어촌에 불과하얏스나 시대의 進運을 隨하야 점차 내외인의 이주자가 증가되고 상공업이 또한 발전되야 금일의 시가를 형성하얏다. 其 시가은 元山里 及 元山港으로 成하고 舊赤田川으로 界를 劃하야 兩市가 상접하얏스니 元山里는 고래 北鮮 유수의 시장이오 元山港은 元 일본인 거류지와 기타 시가니 全 시가를 49町洞에 分하얏다. 최근 조사에 의하면 시내 총호수는 6,117호, 총인구는 28,506인인 바 其중 조선인 4,066호 20,390인, 일본인 1,910호 7,509인, 중국인 127호 567인, 기타 외국인 14호 32인이니 조선 동해안의 최번화한 도시다. 시내에 府廳, 덕원군청, 永興灣要塞司令部, 육군슆�속 창고, 세관, 상업회의소 외 각종 관공서가 具有함은 타항과 차이가 別無하고 且 교육기관으로는 일본인의 元山 중학교, 공립고등여학교, 공립소학교 2와 조선인의 공립상업학교 1, ＜88＞ 공립보통학교 2개소 외 사립 保光, 培誠, 海星 3학교와 樓씨, 進誠남여학교, 元山유치원이 有하니 海星은 천주교 소관이요, 其 외 4교 1園은 다 기독교 소관이다. 且 종교로는 기독교, 천주교, 천도교, 불교의 교회당이 有하고 실업기관은 은행 4, 澱粉회사 1, 전기회사 1, 郵般會社支店 1, 拓植支店 1, 수산회사 3, 무역회사 3, 주조회사 2, 운수회사 1, 고무회사 1, 창고 3, 금융조합 2, 잠수기조합 1이 有하나 대개는 일본인의 경영이다.
교통은 大正 3년에 개통된 京元鐵道와 同 9년에 개통된 咸鏡鐵道가 境內를 관통하야 각지의 연락을 편리케 할 뿐 아이라 加之 大正 7년부터 일본해 횡단선로가 개시된 후, 일본과 관계가 밀접하고 且 총독부의 사업으로 大正 4년부터 7개년 斷續工事로 264만원을 投하야 완성한 築港은 일층 船舶寄航의 便을 與하엿스니 其 海路은 釜山을 距하기 약 290리, 下關을 距하기 약 413리, 浦鹽을 距하기 약 328리, 敦賀를 距하기 약 470리다. 此 외 육로로는 安邊高山을 經하야 京城에 至하는 京元街道, 德源郡을 過하야 平壤에 至하는 平元街道, 영흥 함흥을 經하야 境會寧에 至하는 會寧街道의 일등도로, 기타 2, 3등의 도로가 다 완성되야 수륙의 교통운수가 극히 편리하다.
元山은 상술함과 如히 천부의 好 위치를 점한 고로, 상공업이 또한 발달되야 최근 일년간의 무역액은 輸移出 465만 7,308원, 輸移入 1,277만 7,864원에 달하얏는데 輸移出品의 주요한 者는 米, 小麥, 大豆, 魚類, 麻布, 鑛物 등이오, 輸移入品의 주요한 者는 粟, 大材, 紙物, 포목, 금속제품, 식료 등이다. 又 공장은 발전소 1, 철공소 5, 인쇄소 5, 정미소 3, 양조소 9, 고무제조소 1, 硝子煉瓦목재, 鑵詰 등 제조소 각 1개소가 有하고 시장은 上市 下市, 가축 3시장이 有하야 每市가 다 殷盛하니 일개년 시장매매총고는 48만7천여 원에 至하고 其 외 어시장과 공설시장의 매매도 其 액이 亦 막대하다. 尙又 元山港은 其 천연의 勢가 漁魚에 適宜함으로 어획이 亦 풍부하니 其 중요한 어족은 명태, 鯖, 鰆, 鰤, 鯛, 鰈, 鯟, 鮭, 鱈, 石花菜, 海苔 등인 바 기중 명태는 연산 3백만원에 달하야(咸南 一圓) 자래 江原道의 鰯, 全羅道의 石首魚와 相幷하야 조선 3대어업의 1 되는 자로서 장래에도 특히 유망한 業이 될 것은 물론이오 此외 수산제조고도 又 年産 85만여 원에 달한다.
최후에 연혁을 잠간 말하자면 本府는 원래 貊種의 住하던＜89＞ 不耐濊國의 일부로 其 후 高句麗에 정복되야 泉井郡 又는 於乙買라 칭하다가 新羅 時에 井泉縣, 又 東牟郡이라 呼하고 高麗朝에는 湧州, 李朝 초에 宣川, 又는 宜春, 春城, 德州라 칭하야 변천이 무상하다가 世宗 19년에 德源郡이라 改하고 其 후 屢度의 변경을 經하야 李太皇 庚辰(明治 13년)에 개항되고 其 후 甲申에(明治 17년)에 監理署를 신설하야 해외교섭의 府가 되얏다가 庚戌년 10월에 元山府라 개칭하고 大正 3년 4월에 府制를 실시하야 금일에 至하얏다. 且 元山은 명소고적이 亦 多하나 他項 기사에 讓하고 玆에 略하다.

大陸的 景趣로 본 元山港의 風光
元山支社 金春岡
元山港은 朝鮮 반도의 동해안 咸鏡南道의 남단 북위 39도 10분 53초, 동경 127도 26분 8초에 位하야 서남은 長白山脉이 蜿蜒히 相連하고, 동은 洋洋한 조선해에 면하고, 虎島半島는 병풍과 가티 북방을 둘럿고, 葛麻半島는 天橋와 가티 동남을 擁하고, 薪島, 松島, 沙會島 茅島 등은 飛石과 가티 항구를 扼하야 港廣 동서 약 16리 남북 약 24리 위치로는 실로 조선 稀有의 일대 良港이다.
자고로 조선에서 산수 佳麗의 地를 말하랴면 1. 元山 2. 馬山 3. 木浦라고 한다. 元山은 실로 3대풍광의 수위로써 唱하니 그 明媚한 풍광은 대륙적 景趣가 잇다.
試하야 元山 詩樓峰 우에 올라 灣口을 따라면 靑螺가 점점한 島嶼는 남청을 담은 盆石을 보는 것과 가튼데 그 멀리 뵈는 최대한 것은 麗島이오, 그 남단에 원형으로 백색을 呈하는 것은 등대이다. 이로부터 해상에 小島가 相集하야 一羣島를 成하야 此에서 呼하면 彼에셔 답하랴한다. 갈마반도의 白沙靑松, 멀니 동남을 향하야 弓形長汀을 成한 海中에 고립한 것은 黃土島이며 또 頭를 廻하야 右顧하면 호도반도는 그 압헤 빗기여 岬端의 高峰은 한울에 소삿고 松田灣은 해수가 깁히 침입하야 煙霞杳*한 것은 구름인가 바다인가 하는 의심을 생케 한다. 脚底를 瞰視하면 우리네 인생의 주거하는 대소 가옥이 櫛比한 가로에는 人馬가 相織하는 것이 正히 생존경쟁의 活舞臺인 듯한 늣김이 발흥하며 明沙十里의 松林은 白沙에 其 綠이 相映함을 見하겟스며 그로 동남을 望하면 安德 兩郡의 평야에 田圃가 相連한 곳에 田家가 이곳저곳에 산재하야 樹陰山 麓鷄犬이 相聞하는 田舍의 풍경은 1폭의 圖畵이다.
해면을 바라면 대소의 汽船은 烟을 토하고 汽笛聲은 산하를 움직이며 순풍에 밋끌러져 닷는 범선은 삼삼오오 그 碧海에 盆石과 가튼 孤島는 港의 중앙에 白垔을 日光에 빗내는 등대가 立한 곳은 長德島이다. 更히 眼界를 縱眺하면 連巒疊峰 이은 것은 翠黛語와 갓고 갓가운 것은 淡冶笑와 갓다. 티 煙波가 洋洋하야 水天이 일색인데 게다가 碧浪水禽 놉히 날아 壯快의 기- 漲溢함을 感하겟다.
좌로는 靑靑鬱鬱한 松林에 琴韻도 可聞이며 우로는 茫茫浩浩한 岸打浪의 大觀도 有하니 실로 元山港은 대륙적 경취로 볼 것이다.
