Article Title: 咸興과 元山의 人物百態 (A Hundred Aspects of Figures in Hamhŭng and Wŏnsan)
Author: 觀相者 (Physiognomist)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

咸興과 元山의 人物百態 (A Hundred Aspects of Figures in Hamhŭng and Wŏnsan)
						觀相者 (Physiognomist)

It has been a while since there was news from the Physiognomist, so you must have been very curious. Especially after seeing the physiognomy of P'yŏngyang figures, amidst many rumors, where even the sound of a fart is reported as "Bomb Sound of Unknown Origin," and seeing a disheveled, ugly woman caught by Officer Sa while washing clothes in the drinking well is irresponsibly headlined as "Attempted Suicide by Beautiful Woman Throwing Herself into a Well," modern newspapermen sensationally published two-column articles with large, one-ho type headlines like "Denounce Kaepyŏk," "P'yŏngyang Christians Rise Up," or "Mr. So-and-so Files Lawsuit," as if some major incident had occurred. However, both the Kaepyŏk paper and this Physiognomist remained silent like a mute who has eaten honey, so the public's suspicion must not have been absent. Consequently, some might have misunderstood that the Physiognomist was completely intimidated and retreated, never to reappear. But if you knew, this Physiognomist has spent the time further studying physiognomy, becoming knowledgeable not only in facial complexion (色相) but also in bone structure (骨相), mental state (心相), and various other aspects (가지각相), and experimentally traveled far, thousands of *ri* to the northern regions, to read the physiognomies of many people. Furthermore, this Physiognomist fundamentally does not perform readings for profit or to exploit public curiosity. Rather, under a fixed principle, these readings are impartial judgments of each individual's rights and wrongs, good and evil, strengths and weaknesses, gains and losses, according to public opinion. Therefore, even if some misunderstand and raise arguments for denunciation or non-purchase boycotts, we simply submit that misguided judgment to public opinion and observe the natural resolution of the issue. We will not alter or retreat from our original principles in the slightest (小毫). Look! What became of the P'yŏngyang issue? At that time, certain pastors and elders exposed by this Physiognomist in P'yŏngyang tried to stir up trouble by uniting with various churches in P'yŏngyang, but because the content was essentially factual and public opinion was public opinion, they failed due to many dissenters. Then, for a second time, a certain elder, after a worship service, shed chicken-dropping-like tears before the general congregation, speaking words of repentance, excuse, and plea, gaining the support of a few youths to discuss the matter concerning Kaepyŏk. There too, opinions were divided, eventually leading to a vote. The elder who initiated the discussion watched with eyes almost popping out, hoping like waiting for dead parents to return, but alas, it was soundly defeated. Utter defeat upon utter defeat, great embarrassment upon great embarrassment, he tucked his nose into his Bible pouch and ran off with his tail between his legs to his home by the Taedong River bank (大洞江 岸通). And there was no direct communication with our company. Today is already the era of public opinion. No matter how powerful a person is, they cannot argue against public opinion. I don't know what issues might arise after reading the physiognomies of Hamhŭng and Wŏnsan this time, but I am merely informing the public that such events have occurred before. <99> Oops, I spent all my time chatting. Moreover, reading the physiognomies of two regions at once is quite exhausting. If I had dallied, I might not have been able to do the readings. Before the weather gets colder, let's start with Hamhŭng and then move on to Wŏnsan.

Hamhŭng figures! Who should I look at first? No matter what, it's still a world ruled by those with money, so I should look at the rich first. At the very least, I might get a tail piece of salted flounder (鰈鹽) with a glass of Hamhŭng *soju* (咸燒酒). Ecko—here comes old man Han Siŭn (韓時殷), the second son of Hamnam (the first being Cho Changjin) and the wealthiest man in Hamnam. The stench of usury is overwhelming, like my nose will burst. His appearance (相) is like that of a mountain owl; he only knows how to accumulate, not how to spend. Though his assets exceed one million won, far from showing charity to others, he completely disregards even his siblings, to the point that his own younger brother committed suicide by poison years ago. Furthermore, being very suspicious, he fears others knowing his wealth, so he doesn't buy much land, holding onto cash instead. And he doesn't even deposit the cash in a bank, but bundles it tightly and stores it in his warehouse (庫間). This summer, while drying moldy banknotes, a kite (鳶), mistaking them for rotten mice, snatched them away, and over 500 won flew off into the evening wind (飛去夕陽風). Oh, stop it. What hope is there at the age of over 70? It would be better to set aside ample funds for the journey to Pukmangsan (North Mang Mountain, a burial site) and contribute to social welfare projects. Next, Mr. Yi Chaeyŏp (李栽燁) is a socialite filled with passion for keeping concubines (蓄妾熱) and seeking official posts (仕宦熱). His son, Mr. Yi Kyŏngnyŏl (李敬烈), following his father's ways (父風子習), also has considerable passion for official posts. Was the purpose of studying in Japan to become a Chŏnghwarŭng Ch'ambong (定和陵參奉) or a government-appointed provincial council member (官選道許議員)? The sight of father and son bustling east and west, flaunting their money for these goals, still makes me nauseous just thinking about it. And old man Kim Sŭnghwan (金昇煥), perhaps because he is a white-haired old man (白頭翁) over 70, seems to play only with the title "head" (頭字). Thanks to investing a large stake of three thousand, he became the president (頭取) of Pukchŏn Bank (北鮮銀行). To curry favor with officials, he was the first to volunteer when the Hamnam No. 1 Tourist Group (咸南 제일관광단) was recruited. Also, since "long life brings many disgraces" (壽則多辱), his sexual desires are strong, keeping two or three plump mistresses, including a Pukch'ŏng *kisaeng* (北靑妓). He is also a magnate in the rice milling industry. Anyway, he is a major figure in Hamhŭng's financial and business circles (obviously self-serving businesses), a man with a fortunate destiny (好八字) of many sons and grandsons. However, his lack of concern for public good is a flaw. Mr. Ko Yongnyo (高鏞料) made money through his pharmacy, but perhaps due to taking the wrong medicine himself, why did he become paralyzed on one side (반신불수) in middle age? Yet, is there some secret prescription from Hua Tuo (華太) stating that hemiplegia can be instantly cured by watching *kisaeng* (기생) to one's heart's content? He calls *kisaeng* to his sickbed every day. His son, Mr. Chiyŏng (志英), is truly filial. Carrying out his father's wishes (親志), he selects beauties from all directions—today Yŏnok (蓮玉), tomorrow Kyŏngwŏl (璟月). Originally, the way of a filial son (孝子之道) is to nourish both spirit and body (靈肉). Next, Mr. Ko Hyŏngsŏn (高衡璿), what was he thinking donating 3,000 won <100> to the Red Flag Incident (赤旗團事件)? When those involved were arrested, he was scared out of his wits (혼비백산) and cleverly claimed he was robbed, fortunately escaping trouble and recovering the money. However, dealing with the aftermath is difficult, and he is in distress. Next are the moneylenders also known as misers (愛髮, meaning stingy): Han Ch'anghyŏk (韓昌赫), besides Chu Chaebin (朱在賓), Kim Myŏnghŭi (金明熙), Kim T'aegyun (金澤胤), Kim Yŏp (金曄), Kwŏn Pyŏnguk (權炳旭), No Minjik (盧民稷), No Sunjik (盧舜稷), Ch'oe Chaegyŏm (崔在謙), An Kihang (安基恒), and other wealthy individuals exist, but the true colors of capitalists are all the same, nothing special to see. Mr. Kwŏn Pyŏngsŏn's (權炳璇) famine relief efforts in the KimI (1919) and Kyŏngsin (1920) years, and his single-handedly paying the village household tax (洞戶稅獨擔) in the Imin (1902) and Kyemyo (1903) years, might perhaps earn him the title of philanthropist (獨지가) and a wooden cup (木盃) in the future; let's just mention it and move on. Oops, I forgot again. Though not rich himself, one of the rich men's employees, Mr. Yi Myŏngu (李明雨), manager of Pukchŏn Bank, chases after the rich well, so let's give him a look along with the rich— This Mr. Yi has "Myŏng" (明, bright) in his name, but perhaps his eyes (兩眼) are not bright, as he always wears black tortoiseshell glasses (黑玳瑁). His dry lips and exposed teeth (乾唇露齒) invite slander without cause (無事得謗); because his lips are rather dry, no matter how faithfully he works, there's criticism that he is unqualified. However, his earlobes (耳朶) are thick, meaning he has many protectors, and his descendants' palace (子孫宮, facial feature indicating fortune regarding children) is auspicious, so he has nothing to worry about. Just keep helping the capitalists well. His current position as manager is also thanks to President Kim's daughter-in-law being his younger sister (妹氏). The respect he receives from the youth of society also seems largely due to the face of his son, Mr. Tongje (東濟). Next, let's look at the lawyers and doctors. Whether in Seoul (京) or the countryside (鄕), you are the ones who earn money well, and you are the ones who receive many curses. Whether the case is right or wrong, you accept it upon receiving the retainer fee; whether the patient recovers or not, the consultation fee and medicine price (藥價) are collected promptly. Is that all? Among them, the severe ones sometimes seduce the wives or concubines of plaintiffs, defendants, or patients with sweet talk (감언이설) for rape or adultery, and calmly ignore (泰然不顧) the poor even if they have unjust situations or urgent, severe illnesses. How can this be tolerated in humanity (人道)? However, this came out in a fit of anger while speaking; it's not necessarily true that all doctors and lawyers in Hamhŭng are like this. Enough idle talk (閑話休題). Lawyer Hong Sŏngyŏn (洪聖淵) was truly one who got rich by selling words (賣言致富者). He built a large house on the site of Angminnu Pavilion (樂民樓址) and enjoys solitary pleasure (獨樂), changing concubines several times a year. He has much credit with the officials (官邊), holding concurrent positions as a government-appointed provincial council member (官選 道評議員) and township council member (面協議員). He has a habit of harshly rebuking people, backed by official power (官力), financial power (金力), and rhetorical power (辯力). Also typical of lawyers, he is criticized as a greedy wordsmith (욕심쟁이 말장사) for allegedly demanding a 2,000 won fee in the Kim Sehakk father-son lawsuit (金世鶴 父子爭訟) and the Kim Yŏha lawsuit (金麗夏 소송) without even arguing once. And Sin Sŏkchŏng (申錫定), who lived extravagantly in a splendid house on the Sajiktan site (社稷壇奮基), was suspended from practice due to a one-and-a-half-year suspended sentence for some wrongdoing; he recently reopened his practice. <101> People are watching closely to see if such dishonest incidents will occur again. Ch'ae Yongmuk (蔡容黙), with his bent back, tall stature, gloomy complexion, glittering gold teeth (金齒), and wrinkled forehead, possesses various characteristics. Judging by his personality and education, even upon first meeting, one wants to rank him first among lawyers. However, his personality is too blunt, leading to criticism of being unfriendly. Han Ch'angdal (韓昌達), a young lawyer who initially gained some trust upon opening his practice and declared he would defend the rights of the propertyless masses (무산대중), seems to have forgotten his earlier declaration after tasting money. He shows no practical action, only increasing impudence (주저넘은 버릇), which is truly detestable. Doctor Mo Hakpok (毛鶴福) appears calm and meticulous, seeming to have the air of a doctor (의사風). However, perhaps because his surname is Mo (毛, hair), meaning he wouldn't sacrifice even one hair to benefit the world (拔一毛而利天下라도 不爲하랴), there are rumors (세평) that he refuses house calls for critical patients and those without cash. No Imbyŏng (盧琳炳) has a face and style like a wooden Buddha statue (佛木漢) newly brought from Kwijusa Temple (歸州寺). His appearance is ugly (醜陋), his clothes are shabby (醜率), and his examination room is unclean; an ordinary patient visiting might catch an infectious disease rather than get cured. In no aspect does he look like a doctor. Perhaps because of his appearance, he has never experienced romance with a woman in his life (though he has a wife from Hyesan). Maybe he loves kites (紙鳶) as a substitute for women (女); whether a patient or visitor comes, he ignores etiquette (不顧禮節) and only flies kites. However, they say the taste of the bean paste is good despite the earthenware pot (뚝백이 보아서 장맛은 좃타고), meaning he is kind to patients and charges low fees for medicine. Besides them, Messrs. Yu Ch'ilsŏk (劉七石), Ham T'aehong (咸台鴻), Yi Susŏp (李隨燮), Pak Yunha (朴潤夏) also have some reputation, but nothing noteworthy. Mr. Kim Hongje (金鴻濟), famous for traditional Korean medicine (한방의술), focuses on supplementing vitality (補氣) rather than treating diseases (治病), prescribing dozens or hundreds of packs of tonic medicine (보약) for any illness. Thus, he might be a doctor for the rich (부자의 어용의) but cannot be one for the poor (빈민의). Also, his top disciple (高弟), Mr. Yi Kunok (李君玉), follows this hereditary trait, only prescribing *Ikk'it'ang* (益氣湯, Vitality Supplementing Decoction), earning him the nickname "Mr. Ikk'it'ang." From here, let's look at religious figures— First, how about Christianity? (Shh—don't speak carelessly. Another problem like P'yŏngyang might arise.) Elder Han Yŏngho (韓泳鎬) of Chunghari Church (中荷里예배당) weighs over 20 *kwan* (approx. 75 kg) and has feet (足) a *ch'ŏk* (approx. 30 cm) long; he's plump. A devout member of the same church, he participated in the KimI Movement (己未運動, March 1st Movement) and spent several years behind bars. He established Hamsan Kindergarten (咸山幼稚園) and wrestles with children daily, earning him the title "Children's General" (아동대장). He lacks his own principles and decisiveness (과단성), but compensates by readily adopting others' opinions, thus gaining general favor (호감) and still enjoying much trust (신망) in the entire Hamhŭng society as a fine gentleman (好個군자). Elder Hong Kijin (洪基鎭) is also somewhat trusted due to his sincere character (爲人). He eagerly desires to participate in general society and has much vanity (허영심), currently holding the public office of township council member (面協議員) and also being an executive committee member of the Hamhŭng Youth Association (咸興청년회), which holds views opposite to Christian principles. Is Pastor Kim Kwangp'yo (金光票) competing to secure the path to heaven first? His power struggle with Elder Ch'oe Yŏnghak's (崔榮鶴) faction in Sinch'angni (新昌里) <102> is intense (as evidenced by the Yŏngsin School incident, 永信학교 사건). Elder Ch'oe Yŏnghak and Pastor Kim Chungsŏk (金仲錫) of Sinch'angni Church are obsessed with the church land (十地건물) business. Deacon Chŏng Isŏp (鄭利燮) finds great pleasure in usury; perhaps because his church position is deacon (執事), he only knows the character 'chip' (執, to grasp/execute) and is famous for forceful execution. Then, how about the figures in the Ch'ŏndogyo Church (천도교회)? Master (道師) Hong Sŏngun (洪聖運), with blue eyes and a purple beard (碧眼紫髥), is highly trusted in the Hamhŭng Ch'ŏndogyo Church. His principles are completely opposite to those of his younger brother, Hong Sŏngyŏn. His son, Mr. Hong Chinŭi (洪震義) (involved in the Red Flag Incident), is also a respectable young man. The name of Head Teacher (宗理師) Yi Suil (李洙馹) is known by everyone, regardless of gender or age, who knows the *Changhan Monga* (長恨夢歌, Song of Eternal Sorrow). However, in reality, far from strolling with a lover like Sim Sunae (沈順愛) by Pubyŏngnu Pavilion (浮碧樓) on the Taedong River (大洞江邊), he cannot even approach the vicinity of washerwomen ("아마이") by the Sŏngch'ŏn River (城川江邊). He devotes his lifelong love (愛) solely to Ch'ŏndogyo. His face resembles that of a monk (僧相), suggesting he was destined to be a believer since a past life (前生). Also distinctive were Yi Myŏngdo (李明道), resembling a village guardian post (장승) with a flushed, wide face (赤色廣面); Pak Tojung (朴道中), resembling a child Buddha (童子佛) with a yellow face and short stature (黃面短軀); and Ch'oe Kip'il (崔基弼), a Westernized figure (하이카라) with a figure-eight mustache (八字髥), wearing a *sambal* coat (三八衣), and keeping two or three mistresses. And the head monk (住持) of Kwijusa (歸州寺), one of the 30 main Buddhist temples, Yu Poam (柳寶菴), is a monk with dignity (道態), but due to his rigid thinking (思想이 완고), he has become an enemy of those campaigning for the liberation of temple assets (寺有재산 해방운동자), having no time for chanting prayers (염불) because of lawsuits with young monks. Yi P'oun (李包雲), who monopolized the head monk position at Kwanghŭngsa (廣興寺) for over 20 years, seems to have studied Buddhist doctrine (敎理) somewhat, but worldly thoughts (世上 塵念) fill his chest. He is absorbed in commerce (商利) and even keeps wives and concubines to produce novice monks (색기중), being neither monk nor layman (非僧非俗). He lies hidden within the reactionary faction against Yu Poam, intensely maneuvering for the head monk position (住持運動). Ah-ha, constantly reading the physiognomies of men is too tiresome. Let's take a break and briefly look at the women's circle (女子界) before seeing others. However, the women's circle in Hamhŭng is too quiet; who is there to see? Despite everything, Ms. Yun Chongmin (尹鍾珉), who was quite active, recently divorced Mr. Yi Hŭibok (李熙福) completely and, disheartened, went to find her father in Chŏngp'yŏng County Office (定平郡衙) (daughter of Governor Yun Namch'ŏl (尹南喆)). Ms. Im Chŏngdong (林貞東), because her face was pockmarked (깨곰보), couldn't marry until nearly 30, then married Han Ki (韓基) as his first wife at 40. But, as the saying goes, "an unlucky person gets bean powder in their eye even when eating *paegun'gi* (白雲긔, a type of rice cake)" (福力 업는 사람은 白雲긔(餠名)를 먹어도 팟고물이 눈에 떠러진다), she soon divorced again and joined the Ch'ilch'ilhoe (七七會, Hamhŭng Youth Ideology Association), lamenting her fate. Kim Yunja (金允子), once highly praised as the beautiful teacher of Hamhŭng, perhaps trying to practice Japan-Korea harmonization (日鮮融和), was swept away by the wind while transferring to P'ungsan (豊山) and went to find her original lover (情男), Yamamoto Hanjiro (山本判次郞) of the Hwanghaedo Provincial Office's Education Affairs Section (黃海道廳 學務課) (Yamamoto formerly worked in Hamhŭng). It's said the "child of love" (아이노고) is already stirring in her belly. And Miss Chu Sejuk (朱世竹), who briefly studied in Suzhou, China (中國 蘇州), traveled around Seoul and the countryside advocating new ideologies (新主義), becoming a member of either the Women's Friendship Association (女性同友會) or the Ch'ilch'ilhoe. <103> Recently, she tied a new bond (新緣) with Tong-A Ilbo reporter Pak Hŏnyŏng (朴憲永) and is said to be dreaming of happiness. However, having a crater (분화구) on her left wrist, a major taboo in physiognomy (觀相學上 大忌), she cannot avoid early life storms (早年風波). After her, let's just mention Miss Sin T'aesuk (申泰淑), Miss Yi Yusŏng (李裕盛) with acne scars (여드름 딱지) who is a teacher at Hamsan Kindergarten, and Miss Kim Myŏngsŏn (金明善), whose voice is clear and resonant (語音 朗朗), eyes and mouth full of charm (眼과 口補에 가득한 애교), and who imitates chicken crows (鷄鳴聲) well. Now, let's just look at whoever comes to mind. Mr. Kim Hasŏp (金夏涉), director of the Maeil Sinbo branch (每日 申報支局長) and government-appointed provincial council member (官選道評議員), and advisor to the *kisaeng* association (기생조합고문), is a pro-Japanese collaborator among collaborators (親일자 중 又 親일자), so like Sŏnu Sun (鮮于珣) of P'yŏngyang, let's dismiss him without question (不問에 付하고). Mr. Kim Kukhan (金國翰), manager of the Kaepyŏk branch (開闢支社長) and the Chosŏn Ilbo branch (朝鮮日報支局長), has an appearance, physique, and speech that are all Westernized (하이카라적), suitable for a socialite. Mr. Yi Hŭibok (李熙復) of the Sidae Ilbo branch (時代支局) has a habit of tilting his head back when greeting, as if he has a lump on his neck (목에 혹이 돗첫는지). And Mr. Ko Kyŏngp'il (高敬必), who in his prime held positions like director of the Agricultural and Industrial Bank (Nonggong Ŭnhaeng, 殖銀) (殖銀取締役), county councilor (郡參事), chairman of the Chamber of Commerce (商業會議所會長), head of the medical association (醫生회장), head of the restaurant association (음식점조합장), and various other head positions, still unsatisfied, married his daughter to a naval officer, even received congratulations from Japanese (日人의 聘長까지 된 후), changed his name to Kōmoto Keitarō (高本敬太郞), and played the role of a newly affiliated Japanese (新附 日人). After being scared during the KimI Year (己未年, 1919), he fled to Pusan and hid. Is the character "Chang" (長) in Elder (長老) appealing again? He started attending a Christian church. Mr. Kim Hun (金壎), famous for his arrogance due perhaps to his wealth, after obtaining a position as a school councilor (학교평의원) last year, apparently insulted and beat a village gentleman (村兩班), saying "Don't call me *nari* (old term for official), call me Mr. First Exam Pass (*Ch'osi*-nim, 初試님)." Truly, reading the physiognomy of local towns (鄕邑) reveals all sorts of strange things. Besides these, there is the foul-mouthed Mr. Kim Nosŏn (金魯善), and Confucian scholars (유림) Messrs. Han Sŏgyo (韓序敎), Han \*yŏn (韓*淵), Han Changsuk (韓章淑), Mun Ch'angsŏn (文昌善), but their moldy, rotten smell (퀴퀴한 腐臭) makes me unable to look. As for the youths (청년계), I'll see them again at a later opportunity and stop here. Ee-ra, now that I've seen most of them, let's leave Hamhŭng, where I might hear the twelve curses like "Ssang'ganna, chong'ganna, ssŏgŭn'ganna, mottoen'ganna, chapkanna" (various insults ending in '*-ganna*'), and go to the fun-loving Wŏnsan.

Wŏnsan! Ah, the friends in Wŏnsan really drink well. They drink at night and during the day. Just as Zhang Fei (張飛) fights whenever he meets someone, Wŏnsan friends drink whenever they meet. No matter how necessary it is to drink, let's put it aside for a moment and read some physiognomies. However, in this busy time, I can't visit each person individually, so let's gather everyone at the Youth Association Hall (청년회관), which is a nice building in a convenient location, and look at them all at once. Ahem—entering the entrance hall of the Youth Association, it seems all sorts of characters have already gathered. The sharp sound of a violin (빠요링) can be heard, and the clacking sound of billiard balls (彈棊聲) is audible. Without asking questions, I open the office door, and among them are people who look emaciated (瘦瘠) from indigestion caused by eating only dried Korean taffy (朝鮮엿), people drooling while watching others eat Russian bread (露西亞 빵) without getting a taste, new-style gentlemen (新式紳士) half-drunk on Japanese sake (日本酒), <104> and people with dung stains on their overcoats from grabbing the tail of a horned calf (뿔송아지, my creation, nickname for *ppuljoa*). Wait—this isn't the Youth Association Hall, but the Wŏnsan Municipal Hall (元山府民會館)—or perhaps the Wŏnsan Citizens' Club (元山市民俱樂部) or the Wŏnsan Prosperity Association (원산번영회). I must have come to the wrong place. Hesitating again, I step back outside and look closely at the sign. It clearly says Youth Association Hall, but being a newly constructed building, the gray paint (회색 벵기칠) hasn't fully dried yet, and consequently, many of the people frequenting the hall are also painted gray (회색칠한 사람).

Alright, now I understand. Due to unavoidable local circumstances, although still incomplete, it encompasses figures from various fields. Let people criticize somewhat or not; just carry out the work faithfully. Now, let's begin the physiognomy reading. First comes Mr. An Chŏnghyŏp (安廷協), a member of the Youth Association committee, president of the Wŏnsan Trading Company (원산무역회), councilor of the Chamber of Commerce (상업회의소 평의원), and head of the grain merchants' association (곡물상 조합장)—quite impressive titles. **Summary**

This 1924 article by "Kwansangja" (Physiognomist) offers a biting critique of prominent figures in Hamhŭng and Wŏnsan, presented as physiognomic readings. After defending similar past critiques of P'yŏngyang figures against accusations and backlash, the author proceeds to analyze the appearance and character of businessmen, lawyers, doctors, religious leaders (Christian, Ch'ŏndogyo, Buddhist), educators, journalists, and social activists, often employing harsh satire and gossip. The critiques focus on perceived moral failings like greed, hypocrisy, pro-Japanese collaboration, incompetence, vanity, and licentiousness, while occasionally praising individuals deemed sincere, patriotic, or dedicated to public good, especially those involved in nationalist movements or social welfare. The author frames these personal attacks as objective observations reflecting public opinion in the "era of public opinion."

For historians, this article is valuable as a window into the social dynamics and public discourse of 1920s colonial Korea. It reveals contemporary attitudes towards wealth, collaboration, religious institutions, social status, gender roles (through the brief discussion of female figures), and the emerging nationalist and socialist ideologies. The author's judgmental tone and the specific, often personal, criticisms leveled against named individuals provide a vivid, albeit biased, snapshot of societal tensions, local rivalries, and the moral landscape perceived by a critical observer within the colonial context. The defense of the author's method highlights contemporary debates about journalism, public opinion, and freedom of expression under colonial rule.

咸興인물! 누구부터 몬저 볼가? 아모리 무엇무엇 하야도 아즉까지는 돈가진 놈의 세상이닛가 먼저 부자부터 보와주어야 하다못해 咸燒酒 잔에 가잠이젓(鰈鹽) 꽁지라도 생길 것이다. 엣코- 함남의 제2長子(제1은 趙長津)요, 咸南의 首富인 韓時殷노인이 나온다.
고리대금 악취에 코가 터지는 것 갓다. 이는 相이 山부엉이 격으로 생겨서 작구 모을 줄만 알엇지 쓸줄은 모른다. 재산이 백만원 이상에 달하건마는 타인에게 자선을 施함은 고사하고 형제간에도 순연 不顧하야 年前에 그의 親弟가 음독자살한 일까지 잇섯다. 겸하야 의심이 만흔 까닭에 타인이 富力을 알가 염려하야 토지도 잘 사지 안코 현금만 가지고 잇스며 또 현금이나마 은행에 저금도 안이하고 꼭꼭 묵거서 庫間 속에다 藏置한다. 금년 夏期에도 지폐에 곰팽이 난 것을 말리다가서 솔개(鳶)가 썩은 쥐로 알고 툭 차가서 5백여 원이 飛去夕陽風 하얏단다. 아이고 그만두어라 年 만70에 소망이 무엇인가. 北邙山에 갈 노자나 좀 넉넉히 두고 사회사업에 제공함이 조흘 것 갓다. 其次에 李栽燁군은 交際家인 동시에 蓄妾熱, 仕宦熱이 잔뜩 충만한 인물이다. 其子 李敬烈군도 父風子習으로 仕宦熱이 또 어지간하다. 일본에 유학한 목적이 定和陵參奉과 官選道許議員인지 그것을 운동하느라고 父子가 동서로 분주하며 돈냄새를 피우던 꼴은 지금 생각하야도 구역이 난다. 그리고 金昇煥노인은 70여의 白頭翁이 되야 그러한지 頭字로만 놀것다. 삼천의 大株를 출자한 덕분에 北鮮銀行 頭取가 되고 官邊에 아부하기 위하야 咸南 제일관광단 모집을 할 때에 최선두로 지원하고 또 壽則多辱이닛가 색욕도 만하서 北靑妓 외 무엇무엇 2, 3인의 油頭애첩을 두엇고 정미업계에도 거두다. 하여간 그는 咸興財界, 사업계(물론 自利事業)의 大方家로 多子多孫한 好八字의 人이다. 그러나 공익을 모르는 것이 결점이다. 또 高鏞料군은 약국으로 돈을 모왓지마는 자기는 服藥을 잘못하얏던지 중년에 반신불수가 웬말이냐. 그런데 반신불수에 기생읊� 실컨 보면 卽差한다는 무슨 華太의 비방이 잇는지 매일 병석으로 기생만을 불너드린다. 그의 자제 志英군도 참 효성이 지극하것다. 그 親志를 承하야 사방으로 미인을 선택하야 가는데 今日에는 蓮玉이오 明日에는 璟月이다. 원래 孝子之道는 靈肉을 쌍방으로 養하여야 하지. 그 다음에 高衡璿군은 무슨 생각으로 赤旗團事件에 3천원을＜100＞ 내놋코서는 관계자가 검거되는 바람에 혼비백산하야 강탈을 당하얏다고 교묘히 답변하야 僥倖이 몸도 버서나고 돈도 차젓다. 그러나 後事처치가 곤란하야 고통 중에 잇다. 其次 대금업자도 愛髮(吝嗇의 意)의 칭호를 듯는 韓昌赫 외 朱在賓, 金明熙, 金澤胤, 金曄, 權炳旭, 盧民稷, 盧舜稷, 崔在謙, 安基恒군 등의 재산가가 有하나 사회청년에게 다소 대우를 밧는 것도 君의 子 東濟군의 안면으로 인함이 多한 듯하다. 그 다음에는 변호사, 의사를 좀 보자. 京鄕을 물론하고 돈을 잘 벌기도 그대들이오, 욕 만히 먹기도 그대들이다. 사건이야 올턴지 그르던지 착수금만 주면 依例 맛고 병이야 낫던지 안낫던지 진찰료, 藥價는 또박또박 밧는다. 그뿐이냐 그 중에도 심한 자는 原被告나 환자의 처첩을 감언이설로 유혹하야 능욕, 간통을 각금 하고 빈민은 억울한 사정이 잇던지 급독한 병이 잇서도 泰然不顧한다. 이것이 엇지 人道에 참아 할 일이냐. 그러나 말을 하다가 분김에 나온 것이오, 咸興의 의사, 변호사가 꼭 그러하다는 것은 안이다. 閑話休題하고 변호사 洪聖淵군은 참 賣言致富者엿다. 樂民樓址에 高大한 가옥을 신축하고 첩을 매년 멧 번식 연해 갈러 드려놋코 獨樂을 享하는데 官邊에도 신용이 多하야 官選 道評議員, 面協議員을 겸임하얏고 官力, 金力, 辯力, 바람에 사람을 몹시 질책하는 버릇이 잇다. 그리고 변호사의 例事이지마는 金世鶴 父子爭訟 사건과 金麗夏 소송에 한번 변론도 안이하고는 2천원의 보수금을 청구하얏다고 욕심쟁이 말장사라는 평이 잇다. 또 社稷壇奮基에 화려한 가옥을 지여그코 함부로 잘 놀던 동틔로 일년 반 집행유예를 당하야 업무정지 되얏던 申錫定군은 근간에 다시 개업하엿는데 이 후에는 그런 부정사건이 업슬는지 하고＜101＞ 世人이 다 주목하며 굽은 허리, 긴키, 음울한 안색, 반작반작하는 金齒, 줄 만흔 이마 등 여러 가지의 특징을 가진 蔡容黙군은 인격과 학력이 초면에 대하야도 諸변호사 중 首位를 주고 십다. 그러나 그 성격이 너무 무뚝뚝하야 불친절하다는 평이 잇고 연소변호사로 개업초에 다소 신망이 잇고 자기도 무산대중의 이권을 옹호하겟다 선언하던 韓昌達군은 돈맛을 보닛가 전일의 선언도 다 니저버렷는지 아모 실천은 업고 되지 안은 주저넘은 버릇만 느러가는 것은 참 가증하기 짝이 업다. 의사 毛鶴福군은 생기기도 침착하고 자세한 품이 의사風을 가진 듯하다. 그러나 성이 毛씨인 까닭에 拔一毛而利天下라도 不爲하랴는지 급병에도 왕진과 현금 업는 환자에게 불응한다고 세평이 有하며, 盧琳炳군은 얼골과 풍격이 歸州寺에서 새로 잡아온 佛木漢갓다. 인물도 醜陋하고, 의복도 醜率하고, 진찰실도 불결하야 如干患者가 왓다가는 병을 치료하기는 고사하고 전염병을 어더갈 것 갓다. 어느 점으로 보던지 의사행색이 업다. 외모가 그런 까닭에 평생 여자의 연애를
