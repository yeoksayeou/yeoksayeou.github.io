Magazine Title: 개벽 第54號
Publication Date: 1924년 12월 01일
Article Title: 癸亥 甲子의 事 (Events of Gyehae [1923] and Gapja [1924])
Author: [Not specified in the text]
Article Type: 소식 (News/Chronicle)

Gyehae [1923] December
1st: The Chosŏn Railway Company's Kyŏngnam Line between Masan and Kunbuk, and the same company's Kyŏngnam (likely should be a different line, perhaps Chungnam?) Line between Hongsŏng and Kwangch'ŏn were opened. With this, Korea's privately operated railway lines reached 330 ri and 5 pun.
2nd: The United States decided to participate unofficially in the committee of the Allied Reparations Commission investigating Germany's ability to pay.
4th: The inaugural address of Germany's new Chancellor Max [Marx], leader of the Centre Party, organized on the 29th of last month, took place.
6th: U.S. President Coolidge sent a message to Congress stating, "The United States will join the Permanent Court of International Justice under the condition that it does not join the League of Nations." A rebellion against the President broke out in Mexico.
8th: In Tokyo, Japan, the verdict was delivered regarding the Amakasu Incident, the massacre of Ōsugi Sakae and his family during the previous earthquake disaster. The sentence handed down to Amakasu was merely 3 years imprisonment. Centered in Sunch'ŏn, South Chŏlla Province, a tenant rent withholding alliance arose. As it is impossible to live after paying more than 40% of the harvest as rent, the agreement was to withhold the tenant rent at the tenants' homes until this condition is met.<4>
12th: The death sentence was carried out for Ch'ae Kyŏngok, who shot and killed a Japanese informant in North Kando. His final words were reported as, "I merely acted as my conscience dictated."
14th: At the plenary session of the lower house of the specially convened Japanese Diet, a certain Diet member stated, "We cannot remain silent about the massacre of Koreans during the previous earthquake disaster. The government must announce the full details of the incident." In response, Prime Minister Yamamoto merely evaded answering, saying, "I suppose I will answer another day." Thus, this unprecedented incident was forever buried.
16th: As a result of the Greek general election, the Republican Party secured two-thirds of the seats.
17th: Kim Sihyŏn, a key figure in the Ŭiyŏldan (Righteous Brotherhood), who had been determined to fast to death for a week already, started eating again on this day due to the earnest persuasion of his father. British and American expert members were appointed for the Allied Reparations Commission's investigation into Germany's reparation capacity.
19th: The King of Greece was driven out due to the actions of the Republican Party.
21st: As a result of the British general election, the ruling Conservative Party suffered a crushing defeat, while the Labour Party won a landslide victory.<5>
27th: The Han River froze over. While His Highness the Regent of Japan was heading to the opening ceremony of the 48th Diet, at Toranomon, a young man named Namba Daisuke, son of a current Diet member, fired a pistol at the Imperial automobile, creating a first-ever record in Japanese history. The Yamamoto cabinet resigned, taking responsibility.
28th: Chŏng Tuŭn, the elder brother of Chŏng Tukyu, who was involved in the major incident at the notorious Taegu police department, famous for torture cases like the Hŭich'ŏn Incident in North P'yŏngan Province, filed a complaint against certain detectives of the Taegu police department.

Gapja [1924] January
1st: The budget for the Government-General of Chosŏn for the 13th year of Taishō (1924) was announced. Total revenue and expenditure amounted to over 148 million won, a decrease of over 5 million won compared to the previous year. The Code of Criminal Procedure and the Criminal Ordinance were revised and implemented, limiting the pre-trial detention period for defendants at police stations and prosecutor's offices to 10 days.
5th: Kim Chisŏp and his group were arrested after throwing a bomb outside the Nijūbashi Bridge (Imperial Palace entrance).
7th: The Kiyoura Keigo cabinet was formed in Japan, and thereupon, a movement to overthrow the cabinet began across all Japanese political parties.
Ŭiyŏldan member Ku Yŏsun was transferred from Chongno Police Station to the prosecutor's office.<6> In Chinju, a citizens' mass meeting was held regarding the large-scale fraud case involving the Japanese merchant Shimizu-gumi.
8th: Turkish President Kemal Pasha was shot at by opponents.
9th: The motion of confidence in China's Sun Baoqi cabinet passed the House of Representatives.
10th: In Chinju, over sixty organizations from the Samnam (three southern provinces) region gathered and organized the Labor-Farmer Alliance Association (勞農同盟會).
13th: As a result of the Egyptian general election, Zaghloul Pasha's party won.
15th: In P'yŏngwŏn County, South P'yŏngan Province, female independence group member Hŏ Hwakshil and several others were arrested.
16th: In Kwangju, there was a tenant dispute where over five hundred farmers rushed to the police station. In Shiga Prefecture, Japan, there was a clash between about five hundred members each of the Suiheisha (Levelers' Society) and the Kokusui-kai (National Essence Society). Japan's leading political party, the Seiyūkai, split.
21st: Mr. Lenin died.
22nd: The British Labour Party cabinet was formed.
25th: A great storm arose in Yŏngil Bay.
26th: The wedding ceremony of the Japanese Crown Prince took place, and the Indian National Congress passed a resolution to boycott British goods.
29th: Britain recognized Soviet Russia (勞農露西亞 - Labor-Farmer Russia).
30th: The United States recognized the new Republic of Greece.
31st: The 48th Japanese Diet was dissolved due to a vote of no confidence in the Kiyoura Cabinet.

February
3rd: Mr. Rykov was elected Chairman of the Council of People's Commissars of Russia. Former U.S. President Wilson died.
4th: Mr. Gandhi of India was released.
5th: The first anniversary of the Chosŏn Products Promotion Society (조선물산장려회) was celebrated in various locations.
7th: Italy also recognized Soviet Russia.
9th: Kim Tongjun, a special agent of the Kunpŏlchŏngsŏ (Military Administration Office - a Korean independence group), was arrested at Chongno Police Station.
10th: The national conference of the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (Equality Society) was held in Pusan. The Johnson Anti-Japanese Bill passed the U.S. House of Representatives.
11th: The Sinhŭng Youth Alliance (新興靑年同盟會) was organized in Keijō (Seoul).
13th: The diplomatic representative of Soviet Russia stationed in Vladivostok issued a statement denying recognition of the Japanese consul. Norway also recognized Soviet Russia.
14th: The public trial was held for Kim Chŏnghyŏn, Ku Yŏsun, O Sedŏk, and three others related to the Ŭiyŏldan Incident.
15th: U.S. Secretary of the Navy Denby resigned in connection with the oil field bribery issue (Teapot Dome Scandal).<8>
20th: Koreans residing in Tokyo, Japan, held a Korean convention intending to appeal to world public opinion regarding the massacre of Koreans during the Great Kantō Earthquake. The British Labour Party issued a statement condemning the massacre of Koreans and socialists during the Japanese earthquake.
22nd: Austria also recognized Soviet Russia.
24th: Negotiations opened for the first time between Japanese Minister Yoshizawa and Soviet representative Karakhan concerning the issues of consular recognition in Vladivostok, refusal of mail exchange, and expulsion orders for correspondents. This is the problematic Japan-Soviet negotiation that has been dragging on until now.
28th: Martial law in Germany was lifted.
29th: The 3rd congress of the Japanese Farmers' Union (日本 農民之組合) was held in Osaka, showing colors close to socialism. Prior to this, the 13th congress of the Japanese Labor Union Federation was held in Shiba Park, also marking a line in the labor movement.

March
1st: Political prisoners in P'yŏngyang Prison went on a hunger strike,<9> and students of Wŏnsan Pogwang School distributed propaganda leaflets to commemorate the March 1st Movement. Besides these, grand commemorations were held by compatriots in various places such as Vladivostok, Shanghai, and America.
3rd: The 3rd national congress of the Japanese National Suiheisha (水平社) was held in Kyoto. Among its resolutions was an article to establish contact with Korea's Hyŏngp'yŏngsa. At P'yŏngyang Sungsil Middle School, students went on strike (盟休 - maenghyu) over the issue of upgrading the school's status.
4th: A treaty of friendship was concluded between Turkey and Germany. Also, the Turkish National Assembly dismissed the current Caliph and abolished the position of Caliph.
6th: The British Labour cabinet decided to abandon the Singapore Naval Base plan.
7th: Greece recognized Soviet Russia.
8th: Russia ratified the Italo-Soviet trade agreement.
9th: The Namsŏn (Southern Korea) Labor-Farmer General Alliance (南鮮勞農總同盟會) was formed in Taegu.
11th: A large fire broke out in P'yŏngyang.
13th: The German Reichstag was dissolved.
15th: The Swedish-Soviet treaty was signed.
16th: Italy formally annexed Fiume.
17th: Members of the Northern Methodist Youth Association decided to send a letter of inquiry to Bishop Welch regarding his remarks and actions that allegedly insulted Koreans.<10> Sino-Soviet negotiations temporarily broke down.
18th: Tenants of the Hwangju Hŭngŏp Company initiated a round of struggle over the issue of unfair tenant fees. The Greek National Assembly passed a proposal to abolish the monarchy and establish a republic, later proclaiming the abolition of the monarchy effective the 25th.
21st: The Chosŏn Youth Alliance (朝鮮靑年同盟會) was organized in Shanghai.
22nd: In Nagano Prefecture, Japan, 23 members of the Japanese Communist Party were arrested.
24th: Twelve organizations within Sunch'ŏn County held a rally to denounce the local police station.
25th: In Keijō, a so-called pro-Japanese alliance of prominent figures from various factions emerged.
26th: Denmark also recognized Soviet Russia.
28th: Several hundred farmers from Amtae Island, Muan County, attacked the house of the wicked landlord Mun Chae-ch'ŏl.
29th: Fighting began between Sun Yat-sen's army and Chen Jiongming's army in China.
31st: The Sidae Ilbo (Times Daily) newspaper was launched.

April
1st: The Korean Labor Soldiers' General Association (韓國勞兵總會) was held in Shanghai.<11>
2nd: The 30 main temples of Buddhism, which had been divided into the General Affairs Council (總務院) and the Religious Affairs Council (敎務院), were unified once again. Ch'ae Kidu, Kim Myŏngjun, Yi Hŭigwan, Pak Ch'un-gŭm, and others from the alliance of prominent figures from various factions pretended to treat Song Chinu and Kim Sŏngsu at the Sikto-wŏn restaurant, where Pak Ch'un-gŭm aimed a pistol at Song and Kim, threatening them and forcing them to accept something like a letter of apology. This first caused turmoil within the Tonga Ilbo company, eventually leading to changes in its executives.
3rd: The Beijing diplomatic corps demanded 350,000 yuan in compensation for the Lincheng Incident.
6th: In the Italian general election, the Fascist Party (國粹黨 - Kokusui-tō, literally National Essence Party) won a landslide victory, and in the Bavarian general election in Germany, the Monarchist Party won a landslide victory.
7th: An earthquake occurred in the *jin (? possibly Tianjin 天津 or another place name abbreviated) region.
8th: In Harbin, three independence party members, including Yu Kidong, were brutally killed by Japanese police officers.
9th: An Myŏnggŭn, who had been imprisoned for 15 years for the assassination attempt on Itō Hirobumi, was released from prison. A citizens' rally was initiated in Keijō to condemn the so-called Alliance of Prominent Figures from Various Factions and their violence.
10th: Germany's sole industrial magnate, Stinnes, died.
12th: The Allied Reparations Commission decided to adopt the investigation report resolving the reparations issue submitted by the American member, Mr. Dawes.
14th: Japan-Soviet negotiations were stalled. Anglo-Soviet negotiations <12> began.
15th: The All-Korea Labor-Farmer Conference (全朝鮮勞農大會), which had been under preparation since last winter, was held. Representatives from 82 organizations, numbering over 80 people, gathered. The Anti-Japanese Immigration Bill passed the U.S. Senate. With this, the anti-Japanese bill was fully established.
18th: The 1st Far East International Boy Scout Jamboree was held in Beijing.
20th: The provisional conference of the Chosŏn Labor-Farmer General Alliance was ordered to dissolve.
21st: The Chosŏn Youth General Alliance (조선청년총동맹회) meeting opened in Keijō, with 223 participating organizations. Ch'oe Sihŭng (37), leader of the T'ien-ma Brigade (天摩隊長) of the Independence Army, famous for his bravery, who had been previously arrested in Fengtian (Mukden), was transferred to the North P'yŏngan provincial police department on this day.
22nd: The citizens' rally scheduled to denounce the so-called alliance of prominent figures from various factions, or the authorities manipulating them from behind, was banned. Mr. Yi Tongnyŏng became the premier of the Shanghai Provisional Government.
23rd: The Chosŏn Youth General Alliance, which had been meeting daily, was ordered to dissolve.
25th: The 1st-anniversary meeting of the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa was held in Keijō and Chinju. The tragic and heartbreaking drowning deaths of dozens of elementary school students occurred at Yongdangp'o, Haeju.
28th: A large fire broke out in Chŏngdong, Keijō.<13>
30th: Independence groups launched large-scale raids along the border areas. The ordinance for Keijō Imperial University was approved at the plenary session of the Privy Council (樞府 - Ch'ubu).

May
1st: It was May Day and Children's Day. Both in the capital and provinces, labor celebrations and children's festivities were more vibrant than last year. The public trial for Cho Chunnyong and 26 others who attacked the Hŭich'ŏn police station was postponed due to the unbearable display of torture marks.
4th: The Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe (女性同友會 - Women Comrades Association), the first women's socialist organization seen, was formed. "Chosŏn chi Kwang" (朝鮮之光 - Light of Korea), which had been suspended, resumed publication. A general election was held in Germany, favoring the extremist parties, the Nationalists (國粹黨) and the Communists.
5th: The 20th-anniversary celebration of Posŏng College (普成專門學校) was held.
8th: Governor-General Saitō departed for an inspection tour of the border region.
10th: A general election was held in Japan, resulting in a landslide victory for the three constitutional protection factions (護憲三派 - Goken Sanpa) and a crushing defeat for the ruling government party.
12th: A general election was held in France, where the government party suffered a major defeat, and the Left Party gained significant strength.
15th: The Japanese Exclusion Act bill (conference committee version), to be implemented from July 1st, passed both the U.S. Senate and House, thus finalizing the issue. An international emigration conference opened in Italy.
17th: Due to the Governor-General's authorities restricting the passage of laborers to Japan,<14> a Pusan citizens' rally was held.
19th: As Governor-General Saitō's party was passing downstream from Kosan-jin, they received about 40 shots fired from the opposite bank of Wihangma Island (渭原馬嘶對岸) by members of the Taehan T'ongŭibu Army Corps (大韓統義府軍團) under Paek Kwangun.
22nd: The Anglo-American Prohibition Treaty was ratified.
23rd: The 13th Congress of the Russian Communist Party opened in Moscow.
25th: Residents of Sinwŏn-dong, Chaeryŏng County, held a village meeting to devise measures to protest the county head's improper disposal of the model forest.
26th: In Chiktong, Ch'osan County, 40 members of an independence group clashed with a police unit. The U.S. President signed the Japanese Exclusion Act.
27th: The German cabinet resigned en masse.
28th: The Governor-General of Annam, Mr. Merlin, stopped by Keijō.
29th: Mr. Hong Ch'anbo, who had been tortured in relation to the Hŭich'ŏn incident, died in P'yŏngyang prison.
31st: The Sino-Soviet Agreement was signed.

June
1st: The 3rd Art Exhibition hosted by the Government-General opened. The Japanese Police Affairs Bureau (警保局) <15> permitted the free passage of Korean laborers to Japan. The Bulgarian Prime Minister received bullet wounds from an assassination attempt and was seriously injured. Mr. No Paengnin became the Chief of Military Staff of the Provisional Government. The new French Chamber of Deputies convened, the leftist parties decided on a vote of no confidence against President Millerand, and the Poincaré cabinet resigned en masse.
2nd: The International Emigration Conference held in Rome, Italy, closed. The Russian Communist Party Congress closed after resolving to cooperate between the Communist Party and the peasantry.
4th: German Chancellor Marx formally announced in the Reichstag the intention to accept the Dawes Plan.
7th: The Kiyoura cabinet in Japan resigned en masse. About 30 ideological groups and organizations in Keijō formed a committee to impeach the authorities regarding the suppression of speech and assembly, which had been intensifying month by month since September of last year.
8th: The Herriot (malssal - seems like a phonetic transcription error or nickname for Herriot?) new cabinet was formed in France.
10th: French President Millerand resigned, and the same Herriot (malssal) cabinet also resigned. The Shanghai Provisional Government issued a proclamation promoting grand unity.
11th: A great storm arose in Kimch'ŏn.
12th: Independence group movements became active in Kanggye, Chin-ch'ŏn, and other areas.
13th: Doumergue was elected President of France.<16> Students of Hwimun High Common School went on strike (盟休 - maenghyu) expressing no confidence in the acting principal.
14th: The Herriot cabinet was formed in France.
15th: A so-called All-Korea Public Officials Conference (全鮮公職業者大會) was held in Keijō.
16th: The Italian cabinet underwent a partial reshuffle.
17th: The Japanese Cabinet decided to newly establish the parliamentary vice-minister system. The International Labour Conference opened in Geneva, Switzerland.
19th: The national convention of the American Farmer-Labor Progressive Party nominated Mr. La Follette as its presidential candidate. The Governor-General of Annam, Merlin, was bombed by Fan Jintai (范集泰) in Shanghai. Anglo-Egyptian diplomatic relations broke down.
20th: The mass rally of the Committee for Impeaching Suppression of Speech and Assembly was banned.
21st: The French and British Prime Ministers met and discussed holding an Allied conference.
22nd: Six independence army members killed the T'aech'ŏn police chief in T'aech'ŏn County.
23rd: The Government-General dispatched an additional 180 police officers for border defense.
25th: The 49th Japanese Diet was convened.
30th: Chosŏn Government-General Director of Political Affairs Ariyoshi (有吉 - Yugil in Korean reading, Ariyoshi Chūichi) submitted his resignation.<17>

**Summary**

This article chronicles significant events in Korea and internationally from December 1923 to June 1924. It covers political shifts (new cabinets in Germany, Japan, UK, France; Greek monarchy abolished), international relations (reparations, Soviet recognition, US immigration bills targeting Japanese), social movements (tenant disputes, Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, labor unions, women's groups, Suiheisha clashes), independence movement activities (Ŭiyŏldan, border clashes, assassinations, arrests, commemorations, Provisional Government affairs), major incidents (Amakasu verdict, Toranomon Incident, Kantō Massacre aftermath, police brutality cases), and other news like natural disasters and economic updates.

For historians studying Korean colonial history, this timeline is valuable for its detailed, contemporary account of interconnected events. It highlights the persistent Korean independence struggle (both armed and organizational), the harsh realities of colonial rule (censorship, suppression, police brutality like the Hŭich'ŏn/Taegu cases, economic exploitation leading to tenant strikes), the development of social consciousness (Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, labor/farmer alliances, socialist groups), and the impact of international events (like US immigration policy and Soviet recognition) on the Korean situation. The brief mention of the official silence on the Kantō Massacre of Koreans in the Japanese Diet is particularly revealing of the political climate.

잡지명	개벽 제54호

기사형태	소식

癸亥 甲子의 事
癸亥 12월
1일 조선철도회사 慶南線의 馬山, 郡北 간과 仝京南線의 洪城 廣川 간이 개통되니 조선의 사설철도개업선은 이로써 330리 5분에 달하엿다.
2일 연합국 배상위원회의, 독일의 지불능력을 조사하는 위원회에, 미국은, 비공식으로 참가하겟다는 결정을 하엿다.
4일 지난 29일에 조직된, 독일 新재상 막스중앙당 수령의 취임연설이 잇섯다.
6일 미국 쿨리지 대통령은 의회에 교서를 보내여, 『미국은 국제연맹에 가입치 안는 조건 밋헤서 국제사법재판소에 참가하리라』는 말을 하엿다. 墨西哥에 대통령 반대의 난이 닐다.
8일 일본 東京에서는, 젼번 震災 時에 大杉榮 일족을 학살한 甘粨事件에 대한 판결이 잇섯는대, 甘粕한 대한 형의 언도는 다못 징역 3년 全南 順天을 중심으로 하야, 소작료 보류동맹이 닐다. 郞 소작료 4할이상을 내고는 살 수가 업는 바, 이 조건이 실시되기까지, 소작료는 소작인의 집에 보류하쟈는＜4＞ 것이다.
12일 北間島에서 일본 밀정을 쏘아죽인 蔡京鈺에 대한 사형이 집행되엿다. 그는 최후의 유언으로 「나는, 다못, 양심의 명하는 바를 행하엿슬 뿐이라」고 말하엿다.
14일 임시로 열린 일본의회 하원 본의회에서, 엇던 代議士로부터 『우리는 먼저번 震災의 조선인 학살사건에 대하야, 침묵할 수 업다. 정부는 그 사실의 전말을 발표하라』 한즉, 山本수상은 다못 「他日에 대답하게 되겟지오」 하며, 답변을 회피하엿다. 千古未有의 이 사건은 이러케 해서, 영영히 무더버림이 되고 말엇다.
16일 希臘총선거의 결과, 공화당이 3분의 2를 점하다.
17일 벌서 1주간이나 絶食自盡을 期해오던 義烈團 주역 金始顯이, 자기 부친의 간권으로써 이날부터 다시 밥을 먹엇다. 연합국 배상위원회 독일 배상능력조사에 관한 英米전문위원이 결정되다.
19일 공화당의 거사로 希臘황제가 쫏겨나다.
21일 영국 총선거인 결과, 정부당인 보수당이 참패하고, 노동당이 대승하다.＜5＞
27일 漢江이 결빙되다. 일본 攝政宮 전하께서 48의회 개원식을 향하는 도중, 虎之門에서, 현대 議士의 아들 難波大助라는 청년이 御자동차에 육혈포를 노하, 일본 역사상에 처음 잇는 기록을 지엿스며, 山本내각은 인책사직을 하엿다.
28일 平北熙川사건과 가티 고문사건으로 유명한 소위 大邱경찰부의 중대사건에 관계된 鄭斗奎의 實兄 鄭斗殷은 大邱경찰부 형사 모모를 거러 고소를 제출하엿다.

甲子 1월
1일 조선총독부의 大正 13년도 예산이 발포되엇는데, 세입세출 共 1억4천8백여만원으로서, 작년도보다 5백여만원이 주러섯다. 형사소송법 及 刑事令이 개정 실시되며, 경찰서 及 검사국의 피고인 미결 구류일수를 10일로 제한하다.
5일 金祉爕 一團이 二重橋外에 폭탄을 던지고 체포되다.
7일 일본 淸浦奎吾내각이 성립되며, 니여 일본 각 정당을 통한 倒閣운동이 닐다.
의열단원 具汝淳이 鍾路署로 검사국에＜6＞ 넘어가다 晋州에는 일본 상인 淸水組의 大사기사건에 대한 시민 대회의가 잇섯다.
8일 土耳其대통령 케말파사가 반대파의 저격을 밧다.
9일 중국 孫寶琦내각 동의안이 중의원을 통과하다.
10일 晋州에 三南 각지의 육십여 단체가 회합하야, 勞農同盟會를 조직하다.
13일 埃及 총선거의 결과, 짜쿨파사黨이 得勝햇다.
15일 平南 平原郡에서, 여자 독립단원 許確實 외 수명이 체포되다.
16일 光州에서 농민 오백여명이 경찰서로 殺到한 소작쟁의가 잇섯고 일본 滋賀縣에는 水平社員과 國粹會員 각 오백여명의 충돌이 잇섯다. 일본 제1정당 政友會가 분열되다.
21일 레닌씨가 죽엇다.
22일 영국 노동당 내각이 성립되다.
25일 延日灣에 큰 폭풍우가 닐다.
26일 일본 황태자의 御성혼식이 잇섯고, 인도 국민의회는 영국 物貨배척안을 통과하다.
29일 영국이 勞農露西亞를 승인하다.
30일 미국이 希臘 신공화국을 승인하다.
31일 淸浦內閣 불신임으로 일본 제48의회가 해산되다.

2월
3일 露西亞인민위원장에 「뤼콥푸」씨가 당선되다. 미국 전대통령 윌손씨가 죽엇다.
4일 印度 깐듸씨가 석방되다.
5일 조선물산장려회의 1주년 기념이 각지에서 設行되다.
7일 伊太利가 또 露西亞를 승인하다.
9일 軍政署 특파원 金東俊이 鍾路署에 逮囚되다.
10일 釜山에 衡平社 전국대회가 열렷다. 미국의 쫀손 排日案이 하원을 통과하다.
11일 京城에 新興靑年同盟會가 조직되다.
13일 海參威駐在 勞農露西亞 외교대표는 일본영사 否認의 旨를 성명하엿다. 諾威國이 또 勞農露西亞를 승인하엿다.
14일 義烈團事件의 金禎顯, 具汝淳, 吳世悳 외 3명에 대한 공판이 잇섯다.
15일 미국 海軍卿 뎸비-씨가 油田收賄문제에 관련하야＜8＞ 사직하다.
20일 일본 東京在留朝鮮人은 震災 당시의 조선인 학살사건을 들어, 세계의 공론에 訴코져, 먼저 조선인대회를 열엇다. 영국 노동당으로부터, 日本 震災 당시 조선인 학살 及 사회주의자 학살에 대한 반대선언서를 발표하다.
22일 墺凶國에서 또 露西亞를 승인하다.
24일 일본 芳澤公使와 露의 카라한씨 간에, 처음으로 海港의 영사 부인, 우편물교환 거절, 통신원 퇴거처분에 대한 교섭을 열엇스니, 이것이 지금까지 흘정거리는, 문제의 日露交涉이란 것이다.
28일 독일 계엄령이 철회되다.
29일 일본 農民之組合 제3회 대회가 大阪에 열리어, 사회주의에 갓가운 색채를 가졋스며, 이에 先하야, 일본 노동조합 13회 대회가 芝公園에 열리어, 역시 노동운동에 한 선을 그어섯다.

3월
1일 平壤감옥의 정치범인의 食을 斷하고＜9＞, 元山 保光학교 학생은 선전문을 배포하야, 3·1운동을 기념하엿스며 이 밧게 海參威, 上海, 米州 등 각지에 잇는 동포들의 성대한 기념이 잇섯다.
3일 일본 전국 水平社 제3회 대회가 京都에서 열엇는데, 그 결의 중에는 조선의 衡平社와 연락을 취한다는 조항이 잇섯다. 平壤 崇實중학에 학교승격문제로 학생들의 盟休가 잇섯다.
4일 土耳其와 독일 간에 수호조약이 체결되고 또 土耳其 국민의회는 현 회교교주를 파면하고, 교주의 位를 폐지하다.
6일 영국 노동당 내각은 新嘉坡 軍港案을 포기키로 결정하다.
7일 希臘이 露國을 승인하다.
8일 露西亞는 伊露협상조약을 비준하다.
9일 大邱에 南鮮노농총동맹회가 成立되다.
11일 平壤에 大火가 닐다.
13일 독일 의회가 鮮散되다.
15일 瑞典 露西亞 조약이 조인되다.
16일 伊太利가 정식으로 휴메를 병합하다.
17일 北監理敎 청년회원 일동은 웰치 감독의 조선인을 모독햇다는 언행에 대하야＜10＞ 질문서를 보내기로 햇다. 中露교섭이 일시 결렬되다.
18일 黃州 興業會社 소작인 일동이 부정소작료 문제로 한 판의 싸움을 닐으키다. 희랍 國民議會는 왕조폐지, 공화국 건설의 제안을 가결하야, 후 25일로 正政폐지의 뜻을 선포하다.
21일 上海에 朝鮮靑年同盟會가 조직되다.
22일 일본 長野縣에 日 공산당원 23명이 검거되다.
24일 順川 군내 12단체는 同地 경찰서 聲討大會를 열엇다.
25일 京城에는 소위 각파 有志의 친일연맹이란 것이 생겨섯다.
26일 丁抹이 또 露國을 승인하다.
28일 務安郡 岩泰島의 농민 수백이, 악지주 文在哲의 집을 습격하다.
29일 중국의 孫文 軍와 陳炯明 軍이 싸움을 시작하다.
31일 時代日報가 창간되다.

4월
1일 上海에 韓國勞兵總會가 열렷다.＜11＞
