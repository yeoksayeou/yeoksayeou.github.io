Magazine Title: 개벽 제54호
Publication Date: 1924년 12월 01일
Article Title: 甲子文壇縱橫觀 (A Comprehensive View of the Kapcha Literary Scene)
Author: 朴月灘
Article Type: 문예평론 (Literary Criticism)

甲子文壇縱橫觀
						朴月灘
Somehow, a year has gone. While hesitating, another year is about to be sent off. It's not that I only now freshly realized that days and months go by without rest and wear away, but when the north wind blows fiercely (朔風塞日) and white snow flutters down, and people's steps hurry along the streets on the last night of the fast-running twelfth month (駛走의 섯달 除夜), the sound that emerges unwittingly and by chance is a sigh: "This year is now all gone too."
Who could possibly stop the passing year? With whose strength could one possibly bind the flowing time? As I send off this year, which silently departs carrying the history of one short page more, I intend to reflect once again on the events of the literary world that occurred this year.

Give me rice. Give rice to me, who is starving-. Give me clothes. Give clothes to me, who is naked-. Whose voice is this? It was the voice of a person. What kind of person? It was the voice of a Chosŏn (朝鮮) person wearing tattered clothes. Life is about to go bankrupt, and lives are like moths wandering on the verge of death (瀕死의 危地). Receiving pressure two- and threefold, they once again came to receive unspeakable calamities (酷禍) like floods and droughts from heaven.
As there is no rice, their empty intestines rumble; in what spare moment could they possibly seek art? As there are no clothes, they are cold and trembling, unable to cover their red nakedness; in what spare moment could they possibly have time to read texts? In schools,<111> pitiful young friends withdrawing daily were numerous, and in the streets (巷間), the number of those who couldn't even drink a bowl of thin gruel once a day and tragically ended their own living lives increased daily.
In such a tragic world (悲絶한 세상), how could conventional works flourish? In such miserable times (참담한 시절), how could conventional art possibly arise (勃興)?
Starving brethren. Shall I treat you to a warm meal— or have you read a piece of fiction—? If asked this way, they would roar in anger— Give us rice. Hurry and bring out something to fill our stomachs— they would surely shout.
Naked brethren. Shall I give you clothes, or shall I recite a few chapters of poetry for you—? If asked this way, they would glare and shout— You absent-minded fool. Hurry and give me rags to cover my flesh frozen in this cold— they would say.
This year's literary scene (though I use the two characters for 'literary scene' (문단) sometimes, my brush does not stand firm. However, as there are no other suitable characters to substitute, I write it as 'literary scene'.) was filled with a low pressure of lethargy-filled (惰氣滿滿) boredom. It was like gloomy clouds thickly covering the entire sky, low and low, just before a downpour. Yawning, stretching, snoring, drowsiness— this is the state of the literary scene. It's not that it became like this anew this year, but the atmospheric pressure lingered even more strongly, and the gloomy color became even thicker. The literary scene was miles and miles apart from the masses. The literary scene suffered terrible abuse from the masses. Novels and poems became objects of banishment (追放物) by the masses.
Literature abused! Novels abused! By whom are they abused? By whom are they banished? They are abused and chased away by the Chosŏn people—their elder brothers, their younger brothers, their compatriots—who deeply love and respect them. Why is this so? Is it because they are ignorant and cannot understand art? Is it because literature is difficult and lofty, making it impossible to understand? No. The literary scene has betrayed them too much. It has ignored them too much.<112> When they suffered terrible pain, did anyone holding a pen in the literary scene ever console them? When they had terrible sorrow (슯흠), did any author rush to open their heart and coolly sweep away the sorrow? When they cried, did the authors not laugh, and when they laughed, did the works not cry? When the masses' arms were broken and legs snapped, the poet sang humming tunes in an ivory tower. When the masses couldn't smooth their worried brows (愁眉) due to pressing hunger and cold (飢寒), the novelist drank alcohol at a café and wrote novels whispering sweet nothings with a lover at a concert.
Why did literature come into being? For what reason did art come about? Did literature arise as a pastime for a few literary figures? Did art emerge for the sake of art youths resembling mental patients with long, disheveled hair speaking strange words? No. The world of literature is not that narrow. It arose for the sake of many 'lives'. The mission of art is not that light. It came into being for the sake of countless souls.
Art should not be considered the artists' amusement, nor literature the writers' method of diversion (消遣法). Just as the sun and moon belong to the masses, art too must become theirs; just as the clear wind (淸風) and cool springs (冽泉) belong to the masses, literature too must surely become theirs. Thus, just as they love the sun and moon, they should come to love art, and just as they love the clear wind and cool water, they should come to love literature.
So, how was this year's literary scene? Was there an author who took up the pen for them? Was there a poet who sang for them? There was none. Not a single page, not a single phrase. The authors were people of a different world from them, and the poets were people of a different era from them.
For this reason, the literary scene was abused. Works were abused. Abused authors! Abused works— It must be so. It is only natural.
If there had been works loved by the masses, and poets who shouted the masses' cries together with them, they would have sufficiently loved and read those works even while crying from hunger and cold (飢寒). While ending their own living<113> lives because they wanted to live but could not, the masses would have died thinking of those songs. Taking it a step further, if the greatest works concerning them had appeared, perhaps they, gaining new courage from those works, would have endured the cold and hunger, sought some kind of development of their own lives, abandoned the path of death, and courageously tried to live on. Unfortunately, there were no works that could be companions to them. There were no works from which to receive comfort. Therefore, how could they possibly love art? How could they possibly love works? How could they find the leisure to read and love works from a different era, works that do not know them? It is only natural that they should be abused and disregarded.
It is time there should be. It is time for some new literature to arise. Novels that soothe their painful wounds (瘡痍) must emerge, plays that relieve their tormented hearts must emerge, and poems that calm their sorrowful minds and instill new courage must emerge. Works like childish pranks of the past must vanish, and new, vigorous masterpieces must join hands with them and advance, keeping pace together.
However, this year's literary scene showed no new sounds, no new movements. Within the atmosphere full of boredom, several dozen weak, tearless, consumptive-patient-like, feeble, inadequate works, wearing the same old masks, emerged reluctantly. Especially in poetry, it was unspeakably poor. It is no exaggeration to say that drama was entirely absent.
On the other hand, the number of novels and poetry collections published as standalone books seemed to be greater compared to the previous year.
Due to space limitations, I will not write lengthy reviews of the standalone books here. Furthermore, as far as my memory serves, many of the works included in these standalone books have already been read and reviewed once in magazines and newspapers, so there is no need to repeat myself, and I will leave it at that. Recalling from faint memory the creative works that appeared in magazines during this past year, they were as follows:

Besides these, I know there might be several pieces I missed because I couldn't read them. Among these, I intend to critique the remaining works, excluding those I have reviewed once, those I cannot remember in detail because too much time has passed since reading, and works currently being serialized.

I hadn't read Yi Kwang-su's (李光洙) short stories for a long time. Having grown weary (실증) of the mediocrity of works like 『Hŏsaengjŏn (許生傳)』 and 『Kŭm Sipchaga (金十字架)』, which couldn't particularly startle people, I wiped my eyes anew and read his long-awaited short story, 『Hyŏlsŏ (血書)』. However, my expectations when reading the first page and the feeling (늣김) I got after finishing it were vastly different. Of course, his concise literary style (文彩) and more refined brushstrokes (필치) were several levels above those in 『Ŏrin Pŏt (어린벗)』 and 『Yun Kwang-ho (尹光浩)』. Furthermore, rendering the speech of the Japanese<115> old woman (a person from Nagano Prefecture (長野縣)) in a local dialect and constructing the dialogue using Hamgyŏng Province dialect showed considerable effect (功效). However, the personality of the most important character in this novel, the woman named Sinja (信子), is exceedingly unclear. Moreover, the reader cannot figure out why that unknown woman came to love the protagonist so much. To fall ill and eventually die because of an unknown man, furthermore a poor student from Chosŏn (朝鮮), whom she had only seen once in a photograph and a couple of times on the street, unable to forget him waking or sleeping (寤寐), coughing up blood due to lovesickness (相思), and writing a letter in blood (血書) saying, "The wife who goes before my eternal husband"—isn't that excessively contradictory? I doubt it. Especially when the man coldly rejects her, for the woman alone to yearn so intensely for an unknown man, there must be some significant process (經庭) involved. The author paid no attention to this point. If we simply accept the fatalism often seen in old novels, just as it says in this story, "Heoheo, it must all be fate from a past life..." then there would be no problem at all. For a work based on the lives of modern people, writing it so carelessly in the style of *Chŏndŭngsinhwa* (剪燈新話) or *Yŏnjaeji'i* (聯齋誌異) is an extremely severe failure.
In the same author's work 『H Kunŭl Saenggak'ago (H君을 생각하고)』, the most reverent (敬虔) humanism flows throughout the entire piece (流露되얏다). I read it with considerable respect (敬意). However, here too, there was a major flaw (결점). The personality of the woman C, who is H's lover, is incomprehensible. Saying she loves H, receiving tuition fees from him, yet on the other hand whispering sweet nothings with another rich man, and when H's illness becomes critical and he coughs up blood, not coming despite receiving telegrams 3 or 4 times urging her to come quickly, and then after H dies, guarding his grave and playing the role of a virtuous widow (열녀)—isn't this an excessively angular (圭角나는) affair? It cannot be called a successful work either.
Kim Ki-jin's (金基鎭) 『Pulgun Chwi (붉은쥐)』 is like a needle to the vital point (頂門에 一針) for the slovenly literary world, the boredom-filled literary scene. The strong, powerful line that squirms from beginning to end makes the reader's palms sweat and causes the warm blood circulating quietly in the veins to race at the highest speed. The cry of the young soul, the cry of the era, overflowing throughout the piece—. Such hot, powerful art should rightfully emerge. Seen in this sense, Mr. Ki-jin's (基鎭) novel this time was a sharp arrow shot towards the chaotic, devastated, lethargic novel scene, and a small prelude (序曲) that drew a line between eras.<116> However, looking at it as a novel, the lack of thorough description in places and killing off the protagonist like a thunderbolt, abruptly and nonsensically (虛荒孟浪하게), is the biggest failure.

In Pak Yŏng-hŭi's (朴英熙) 『Ijungpyŏngja (二重病者)』, traces of the author's effort to grasp and express some kind of ideology are clearly visible. Betraying comrades due to hollow love and being driven out by them seems to be the crucial focus of this novel. The theme is plausible. However, all the descriptions and techniques are not smooth and fluent. Firstly, the author rushed the pen too quickly when writing the fatalistic dialogue and descriptions intended to express the protagonist's personality and assertions. Furthermore, the dialogue in a novel should always have the charm to draw people in, making the reader unknowingly grasp the gist without getting bored. But this work failed in this aspect. Secondly, the love between the protagonist and the nurse named Un-gyŏng is unnatural. Although the author calls the protagonist a patient with a brain illness (腦病), the protagonist's actions do not seem like those of such a severe mental patient. He appears as an extremely mild patient, no different from a healthy person. For such a person, before gauging the depth of the actions of the nurse who confessed her love (it is not clearly expressed whether the nurse knew the protagonist loved her or not), breaking the promise with comrades, and without even discussing loving the nurse or living together, just putting such thoughts into his own head and already requesting and receiving money from society for living expenses—isn't this shockingly hasty?

Chŏn Yŏng-t'aek's (田榮澤) 『Sajin (寫眞)』 is a concise, refreshing work. It is a flawless, pearl-like work that makes one smile and nod in agreement. Conversely, the same author's 『Hin Tak (흰닭)』 gives an impression of being rambling and disconnected (支離滅裂) in both description and structure.

In Pang Ch'un-hae's (方春海) 『Ŏmŏni (어머니)』, although there are some points where the rhetoric (修辭) lacks fluency (暢達치 못한) at the beginning, the first chapter depicting the son calling his old mother after a long time, the mother shedding tears and welcoming her young son—is filled with the overflowing sentiment of heavenly kinship love (天倫愛) between mother and son.<117> Moreover, the description when dragging the bundle and when receiving the meal table is convincing enough for the reader to affirm. However, conversely, 『Pi Onŭn Nal (비오는 날)』, whether looking at rhetoric, structure, or description, seems forced, clumsy, and full of gaps.

Im No-wŏl's (林蘆月) 『Akma (惡夢)』 is indeed like a nasty bad dream. It is disorderly and confusing, the core issue is unclear, and the events are extremely hazy, making it impossible to understand what is what. And the rhetoric is vulgarly obscene (猥褻) and coarse. For example, in chapter 3 of this novel, phrases like "With a feeling almost close to vengefulness, I recklessly embraced and played with her..." or "Her voluptuous (豊艶) body, belly—her health, all seemed like some inexhaustible resource." Couldn't these have been adequately expressed through other descriptions without using such tasteless, crude (荒), and lewd words? However, what was mentioned above is a peripheral issue (枝葉); the main point is that I don't know why the author wrote this. The attitude and personality of the protagonist O are incomprehensible, and it's impossible to tell whether E killed H or not. Also, one cannot know whether H committed suicide or was murdered, and there is no clear indication whether the woman S loved H, O, or both H and O simultaneously. All that exists is a long story told during the detective's interrogation—whether fabricated or based on knowledge of the actual circumstances is unknown—and the reading of what was written in a notebook to frame O as the culprit. However, O was released due to insufficient evidence. So, what kind of situation is this? Was he possessed by a goblin (독갑이)? It is truly a scene from a hazy nightmare (악몽). Rather, if the author had stated at the beginning of this novel, "This is a story I dreamt," perhaps such a failure would not have been encountered.
Besides these, I read Yi Yŏng-sŏp's (李暎燮) 『Tojŏktchil (도적질)』, Paek Chu's (白洲) 『Kwabu (寡婦)』, and Ch'oe Hak-song's (崔鶴松) 『Koguk (故國)』, which were selected in 『Chosŏn Mundan (朝鮮文壇)』. Of course, all three works have points where the rhetoric and description lack fluency. However, all show great promise (囑望) for the future. I particularly appreciate Yi Yŏng-sŏp's tight structure and Paek Chu's poetic elegance (雅趣). I am endlessly glad for this gain newly acquired this year. Thank you for bearing with my presumptuous review (妄評多謝).<118>

**Summary**

Pak Wŏl-t'an laments the state of the Korean literary scene in 1924 (Kapcha year), describing it as lethargic, boring, and disconnected from the suffering masses plagued by poverty and natural disasters. He argues that literature and art should belong to the people and address their pain, but contemporary writers are instead detached, living in ivory towers or cafes, producing works irrelevant to the public's harsh reality. He critiques several specific short stories published that year, finding fault with characterization, plot logic, and style in works by prominent authors like Yi Kwang-su and newer writers, though he sees promise in some newcomers. The article is a powerful call for a socially relevant literature that engages with the struggles of the Korean people. From a historical perspective, the article vividly portrays the socio-economic hardships under Japanese colonial rule and the intense debate within the Korean intellectual community about the role of art—whether it should pursue pure aesthetics or serve the immediate needs and consciousness of the oppressed populace, reflecting the rise of social realism and proletarian literary concerns during this period.

甲子文壇縱橫觀
						朴月灘
어느듯 1년이 갓다. 머뭇머뭇하는 동안에 또다시 한 해를 보내게 되얏다. 언제 어느 때나 달과 날로 가고 가서 쉬이지 안코 슬어저버리는 날과 때를 새삼스럽게 이제 처음 알어 깨닷게 된 것은 아니나 朔風塞日에 흰 눈이 풀풀 날고 駛走의 섯달 除夜의 거리로 사람의 거름 밧부게 걸을 때 무심코 우연이 나오는 소리는 금년도 인제 다 갓구나 하는 탄식의 소리이다.
가고 가는 해를 누가 능이 막을 수 잇스랴. 누구의 힘으로 능히 가는 세월을 붓드러 맬 수 잇스랴. 한 폐지 짤막한 력사를 더 지고 말업시 가버리는 금년을 보낼 때 나는 올해에 닐어난 문단의 일을 다시 한 번 생각하야 보랴한다.

밤을 다고. 굶주린 나에게 밥을 다고-. 옷을 달라. 헐버슨 나에게 옷을 달라-. 이것이 누구의 소리냐. 사람의 소리엿다. 어떤 사람의 소리냐. 헌 옷 입은 조선 사람의 소리엿다. 생활은 파산되랴하고 목숨은 瀕死의 危地에 헤메는 불나븨 그것과 갓다. 이중 삼중으로 壓搾을 밧는 그네는 또다시 水災, 旱災 말할 수 업는 酷禍를 한울로부터 밧게 되얏다.

同씨의 『H君을 생각하고』 라는 작품에는 가장 敬虔한 휴맨이즘이 전편에 流露되얏다. 나는 적지 안은 敬意를 가지고 읽엇다. 그러나 여긔에도 역시 큰 결점이 잇섯다. H의 애인이라는 C란 여자의 성격을 알 수가 업다. H을 사랑한다 하고 학비 바더다 쓰며 또 한편으론 돈만흔 다른 남자와 사랑을 속살거리면서 H의 병이 위중하야 토혈을 하니 어서 오라는 전보를 3,4차 띄워도 오지 안튼 그로서 H다가 죽은 뒤에 무덤을 직히고 열녀 노릇을 한다는 것은 넘우도 圭角나는 일이 안인가 한다. 역시 성공한 作이라 할 수 업다.
金基鎭씨의 『붉은쥐』는 후줄근한 소설계, 권태 가득한 문단에 頂門에 一針이라 할 것이다. 쳐음으로 끗가지 꿈틀거리는 힘잇는 강한 線은 읽는 이로 하야금 손에 땀을 쥐이게 하고 고요히 혈관 속에 도는 더운 피를 가장 高度로 뛰게 하얏다. 篇을 통하야 넘처 흐르는 젊은 혼의 울음, 시대의 울음-. 맛당이 이러한 뜨거운 힘잇는 예술이 나와야 할 것이다. 이러한 의미로 보와 이번 基鎭씨의 소설은 혼돈, 피폐, 무기력에 가라안진 小說壇을 향하야 한 대의 날카른 화살을 더진 것이며 시대와 시대를 금 그어 논＜116＞ 한 족으마한 序曲이엿다. 그러나 그것을 소설로 볼 때에 군데군데 묘사의 철저치 못한 것과 急轉直下로 虛荒孟浪하게 벼락가티 주인공을 죽여버린 것은 가장 큰 실패이다.

朴英熙의 『二重病者』는 작자가 엇더한 무슨 사상을 붓드러 표현식히랴고 노력한 흔적이 역력히 보인다. 헛겁덱이 사랑으로 인하야 동지를 저버리고 동지에게 쫏김을 당하게 된 것이 이 소설의 중요한 착안점인 것 갓다. 제재는 그럴듯 하다. 그러나 모든 묘사와 수법이 부드러웁고 원활하게 되지 못하얏다. 첫재로 작자는 주인공의 성격과 주장을 표현 식히려한 운명론의 대화와 묘사를 쓸 때 넘우도 급속하게 붓대를 달렷다. 뿐만 안이라 소설의 대화는 항상 사람을 낙구어 들이는 매력이 잇서서 不知不識間에 독자로 하야금 실症이 나지 안코 요령을 알도록 해야 한다. 그러나 이 작품은 이곳에 실패하얏다. 둘재는 주인공과 운경이란 간호부 사이에 그도 사랑이 부자연하다. 작자는 주인공을 腦病 환자라 하얏지만 주인공의 하는 행동은 그다지 중한 정신병자갓지 안햇다. 극히 경미한 病人, 성한 사람이나 다름이 업는 그러한 병자가티 나타낫다. 이러한 사람으로 자긔를 사랑한다 자인한 간호부(간호부가 주인공이 자긔를 사랑하는 것을 알엇는지 몰랏는지 명확하게 표현된 곳이 업다.)의 행동이 얼마나 깁고 야튼 것을 헤아리기 전에 동지의 약조를 깨틀이고 또는 간호부에게도 가티 사랑하자든지 함께 살자든지 하는 의론도 업시 덥허노코 제 머리 속에만 그러한 생각을 집어 늣코 벌서 살림살이 할 돈을 사회로 청구하야 바덧다는 것은 넘우도 긔막히게 速하지 안은가 한다.

田榮澤의 『寫眞』은 간결한 산듯한 작품이다. 사람으로 하야금 微笑하야 수긍할 만한 아무런 티도 업는 明珠가튼 작품이다. 이와 반대로 同씨의 『흰닭』은 묘사나 구상이나 支離滅裂한 생각을 갓게 한다.

方春海씨의 『어머니』는 첫머리에 좀 修辭의 暢達치 못한 점이 잇스나 늙은 어머니를 오래간만에 불으는 아들, 어린 아들을 보고 눈물을 흘려 반기는 어머니, 그 언저리를 그린 1章은 모자의 넘치는 天倫愛의＜117＞ 情이 가득히 흘러 잇섯다. 더욱이 봇다리를 끌을 때의 묘사와 밥상을 바들 때의 묘사는 확실하도록 독자로 하야금 수긍하게 할 만하다. 그러나 이와 반대로 『비오는 날』은 수사나 구상이나 묘사가 무얼로 보든지 억지로 쓴 것가튼 서투르고 틈이 벌게 되엿다.

林蘆月씨의 『惡夢』은 그야말로 한 못된 꿈자리 갓다. 어수선 산란하고 핵심되는 문제를 알 수가 업고 극히 사건이 흐리터분하야 무엇이 무엇인지를 알 수가 업다. 그리고 수사는 저급하게 猥褻하고도 거칠다. 예들 들면 이 소설 3장에 『나는 거의 복수심에 갓가운 맘을 가지고 그를 함부로 껴안으며 놀앗다...』 라든지 『그의 豊艶한 몸, 배-건강이 모도다 무진장한 그 무엇과 가티 생각햇다.』 이러한 멋업시도 荒하고 음란한 말로 쓰지 안코도 넉넉히 다른 묘사로 쓸 수가 잇지 안은가 한다. 그러나 우의 말한 것은 枝葉의 문제어니와 주요한 것은 작자가 무슨 때문으로 이것을 썻는지 몰으겟다. O라는 주인공의 태도도 알 수 업고 성격도 알 수 업스며 E가 H를 죽엿는지 안이 죽엿는지도 알 수 업다. 그리고 또 H가 자살을 햇는지 피살을 당햇는지도 몰으겟고 S라는 여자가 H를 사랑하얏는지 O를 사랑하엿는지 그러치 안으면 H와 O를 한꺼번에 사랑하얏는지 분명하게 드러난 곳이 업다. 다만 잇는 것은 형사가 취조를 할 때 꾸며대엿는지 확실한 사정을 알고 한 것인지 몰을 길다란 이약이. O를 죄인으로 모러 늣느라고 공책에 적힌 것을 읽는 그것 뿐이다. 그러나 O는 증거 불충분으로 나왓다. 그러면 이것이 어지된 세음이냐. 독갑이한테 홀렷느냐. 그야말로 한마당 어렴풋한 악몽이다. 도리여 이 소설 서두에다가 이것은 내가 꿈꾼 이약이다 하얏드면 오히려 이가튼 실패는 당하지 안이 햇슬 줄 안다.
이밧게 『朝鮮文壇』에 입선된 李暎燮씨의 『도적질』, 白洲씨의 『寡婦』, 崔鶴松씨의 『故國』을 읽엇다. 물론 이 세 분 작품이 다 수사와 묘사의 暢達치 못한 점이 잇다. 그러나 다 압흐로 큰 囑望을 둘 만하다. 더욱이 李暎燮씨의 틈 업는 구상과 白洲씨의 詩化한 듯한 雅趣를 取한다. 금년에 새로히 이러한 어든 어듬을 깃버하야 마지 안는다. 妄評多謝＜118＞
