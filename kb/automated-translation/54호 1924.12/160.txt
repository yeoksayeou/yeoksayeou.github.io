**Body Text Translation:**
A Grand View of Hamnam's Towns: Hamhŭng County, the Capital of Hamnam

If one climbs Moranbong Peak and looks out over the entire view of P'yŏngyang, the rivers, mountains, pavilions, and city streets are all bright, beautiful, and splendid, like a lightly made-up beauty holding fully bloomed flower branches and smiling gently in the spring breeze. Unconsciously, affection naturally attaches, love arises, and one feels unable to leave even if intending to. However, if one climbs Pallyongsan Mountain and looks out over the entire view of Hamhŭng, vast fields and imposing mountains spread out indistinctly in all directions, like the unpredictable transformations of wind and clouds from a deep forest where tigers roar and a great marsh where dragons hide. Its vastness and loftiness cannot be captured and described in words. Hamhŭng is truly the magnificent capital of Hamnam. No, in some respects, it is the magnificent capital of Chosŏn. The poem by Master Kim Ch'anghyŏp (金昌協), "Heaven made Hamhŭng magnificent in the north, lush and auspicious energy in old Namyang. Green mountains all embrace the famous pavilion, emerald waters wind through the long market town..." eloquently describes the scenic beauty of Hamhŭng. This Hamhŭng is located in the central, slightly southern part of Hamgyŏngnam-do, bordered by Hongwŏn County to the east, Chŏngp'yŏng County to the southwest, facing the vast blue sea to the southeast, partially bordering Yŏngwŏn County of P'yŏngannam-do to the west, and bordering Changjin and Sinhŭng Counties to the north. It is the location of the Hamnam provincial office. Its breadth is about 15 *ri* 18 *chō* north-south, about 7 *ri* 18 *chō* east-west, with a total area reaching about 116 square *ri*. Geographically, the east, west, and north sides consist of undulating mountains (Nangnim Mountain Range) with little flat land, but the southwest is a vast plain, forming the so-called Hamhŭng Plain, whose area indeed reaches 28 square *ri*. The land is also fertile. Within the county, the Sŏngch'ŏn River (originating from Hwap'a-ryŏng Pass in Kapsan), Yŏwich'ŏn Stream, and Horyŏn River flow through, facilitating irrigation. The coastline extends for 14 *ri*, featuring the anchorages of Sŏhojin and T'oejo Bay. Notable islands include the two islands of Taejin and Sojin. Hamhŭng, possessing such geographical features, has historically been a strategic stronghold of the northern frontier (北關) with a rich history. It is the site where the Okchŏ tribes were active in ancient times, becoming the Hyŏnt'o Commandery <72> during the Han Dynasty, Namokchŏ of Koguryŏ, Nanhai Prefecture of Parhae, Hallanjŏn of the Jurchen, and Hallan Circuit (合懶路) of the Jin Dynasty. During the reign of King Yejong of Koryŏ, it temporarily became the Hamju Grand Protectorate (咸州大都 護府) (after Yun Kwan defeated the Jurchen and acquired this land), then became the Halan Prefecture (哈蘭府) under the Yuan Dynasty before being annexed to Ssangsong (Yŏnghŭng). During King Kongmin's reign, it was recovered and variously administered as a *chijusa* (知州事), *manhobu* (萬戶府), or *mok* (牧) (Yi T'aejo fought the Yuan and recovered the land). The county's old names are Hamju, Hamp'yŏng, and Hamsan. It began to be called Hamhŭng from the 16th year of King T'aejong of the Yi Dynasty, and the governor's main camp (監司의 本營) was also established from that time. Since then, there have been numerous changes. During the county consolidation (郡廢合) in Taishō 3 (1914), the area north of Kap'yŏng-myŏn was ceded to Sinhŭng County, and Sŏt'oeho-myŏn of former Hongwŏn County was merged with Hamhŭng-myŏn and 18 other *myŏn* to form the current Hamhŭng County. It comprises a total of 20 *myŏn*, 395 *tong* and *ri*, with a total household count of 26,069 (1,385 Japanese, 84 other foreigners) and a population of 159,480 (4,558 Japanese, 274 other foreigners).

Transportation is convenient by both land and water. On land, first and second-class roads within the county extend over 320 *ri*, allowing for oxcarts, horse carts, and automobiles. Moreover, the so-called Hamgyŏng Line, the main artery of northern Chosŏn, runs longitudinally north and south, with the station located at the eastern end of the town. To the south, it connects to Wŏnsan (77 *ri*) and Kyŏngsŏng (approx. 217 *ri*), and to the east, it connects to T'oejo (19 *ri*). Furthermore, the line from T'oejo to Yanghwa Market in Pukch'ŏng was opened on October 11th of this year, marking a new era in Hamhŭng's transportation. In addition, a forestry railway managed by Japan's Imperial Coal Company (帝國炭業株式會社), with a capital of 20 million won, runs along the Sŏngch'ŏn River northwest to Oro-ri (9 *ri*). There are plans to lay several hundred *ri* of railway through Changjin County to Huch'ang County in P'yŏnganbuk-do, eventually crossing the Amnok River to reach Fengtian, the center of South Manchuria. This line is crucial not only for the Hamhŭng economy but for the entire economy of northern Chosŏn. Furthermore, by sea, via Sŏhojin (about 30 *ri* by land), the only seaport here, there are frequent steamships traveling north to Vladivostok (浦鹽) and south to Pusan and other ports. Thus, it stands at a vital junction accessible by both land and sea (though the question of in whose hands this advantage lies is a separate matter). The area of cultivated land is 10,385 *chŏng* 1 *tan* of paddy fields (畓) and 25,177 *chŏng* 9 *tan* of dry fields (田). The harvested agricultural products amount to over 105,000 *sŏk* of rice, 15,000 *sŏk* of barley, 35,000 *sŏk* of soybeans, 11,000 *sŏk* of adzuki beans, 67,800 *sŏk* of millet, and over 877,700 *sŏk* of barnyard millet (稗). By-products include over 3,200 *sŏk* of silkworm cocoons, over 3 million *kwan* of vegetables, 40,000 *kwan* of fruit, and over 15,000 head of cattle. Hamhŭng cattle and Hamhŭng pears have long been particularly famous. Next, fishery products are also noteworthy. Among them, pollock (명태) production reaches an annual value of 190,000 won. Mackerel (鯖) and flounder (鰈) are also abundant. Mineral resources are plentiful, including various ores, but currently, they are under the direct management of the Government-General and essentially in a sealed state, so there is nothing worth recording about their production <73> for now. However, coal from Kap'yŏng-myŏn, managed by Japan's Imperial Coal Company, reaches an annual output exceeding 50,000 tons. Among the aforementioned products, exports include about 35,000 *sŏk* of rice, 20,000 *sŏk* of soybeans, 50,000 *kwan* of salted mackerel, and around 300 packs (駄) of pollock. The market transaction of cattle amounts to about 50,000 head annually, with a value reaching the large sum of about 280,000 won. The people's primary occupation is generally agriculture, with commerce and industry as secondary pursuits. However, in Hamhŭng city, 1,637 households engage in commerce, accounting for over 30% of all households. Next are about 660 farming households and about 460 industrial households. This place is the only major market in the province. Market days are held on the 2nd and 7th days of the lunar month (음 27일), with tens of thousands of people attending each market. The diligence and activity of women coming out to the market are truly astonishing. Market transactions are also prosperous. During peak times, sales of rice and soybeans in a single market day reach over 40,000 won. Especially in autumn, the trade of salt and cotton flourishes, with sales reaching 560,000 won, and other miscellaneous goods amount to no less than 40,000 won. However, the people of Hamhŭng are generally diligent and thrifty. Even merchants have little vanity for quick riches and follow a path of steady progress. Therefore, although their shops appear extremely modest externally, their substance is generally solid. They have not yet lost commercial power to foreigners, and even today, amidst severe fluctuations in the financial world, they maintain a stable livelihood. Financial institutions include the Puksŏn Commercial Bank (北鮮상업은행) (capital 500,000 won, President Kim Sŭnghwan (金昇煥), Manager Yi Myŏng'u (李明雨)), managed purely by Koreans, as well as a branch of the Chosŏn Siksan Bank (殖銀), and the Hamhŭng and Hamsŏng Financial Associations (咸興 咸城 兩 금융조합), which dominate the entire Hamhŭng economy. (In Taishō 11 (1922), total loans were 4,067,424 won, total deposits 1,206,166 won). Additionally, the Oriental Development Company (東拓) branch office, the Hamhŭng Trust Company (咸興信託會社), etc., wield their usual high-interest predatory claws, exploiting the lifeblood (膏血) of Koreans. Other companies include 3 in civil engineering, 3 in commerce, 1 in transportation, 1 in electricity, and 1 in construction (築業), but most are managed by Japanese. In terms of education, on the Korean side, there is 1 Higher Common School, 9 Public Common Schools, 1 Private Common School, 1 each of Public Commercial, Agricultural, and Normal Schools, <74> 16 general schools (一般學校), 3 religious schools, 2 private kindergartens, and besides these, 80 *sŏdang* (書堂). On the Japanese side, there are 2 Public Elementary Schools and 1 Public Practical Girls' High School. Among these, Yŏngsaeng School (永生學校) is the only private middle school in northern Chosŏn. It was established by the Canadian Presbyterian Mission (기독교 加奈陀 장로회) in 1906. Its performance has been good since then, but unfortunately, in February 1923, the school building suffered a fire disaster (8인의 災에 罹하야 - likely typo for 火災, 'fire disaster') and was completely burned down. A new school building is under construction at the foot of Unhŭng-ri Mountain and will be completed soon (currently using the Sinch'ang-ri church temporarily). Preparations are underway to change it to the standard designated school (指定學校). Current staff include the principal, a Westerner named Mr. Ye (芮詩 - likely Ross or similar), Academic Dean Mr. Cho Tonghun (趙東薰), and about 10 other teachers. There are over 200 students across four grades, and 147 graduates. Furthermore, students studying abroad number 71 in Japan, 1 in the United States, 2 in China, and 69 in various places within Chosŏn, totaling 143, all attending middle school or higher.

Regarding religion, Christianity has relatively more influence, followed by Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎). For Christianity, there are the Chungha and Sinch'ang churches, with over 200 baptized members and 1,647 other adherents. Affiliated enterprises include the Yŏngsaeng Boys' and Girls' Schools and Chehye Hospital (濟惠病院), run by the mission board, and the Yŏngsin Elementary School (永信小學校), run purely by Koreans. Prominent figures at the Chungha-ri church include Pastor Kim Kwangp'yo (金光票) and Elders Han Yŏngho (韓泳鎬) and Hong Kijin (洪基鎭). At the Sinch'ang-ri church, Elders Ch'oe Yŏnghak (崔榮學) and Mo Hakpok (毛鶴福) are influential. As for Ch'ŏndogyo, about 20 years ago, Messrs. Chŏng Unbong (鄭雲鳳), Yi Kiwan (李岐玩), and Han T'aesam (韓泰三) pioneered its introduction to Hamhŭng. Subsequently, Messrs. Hong Sŏngun (洪聖運), Chu Tohwan (朱道煥), Yi Suil (李洙馹), Kim Hongsu (金弘洙), Mun Ch'ŏlmo (文哲謨), and others spread the faith from Hamhŭng to various parts of Hamnam. As a result, it once reached tens of thousands of households, leading to the Kabo Peasant Revolution (甲午革命亂 - likely refers to Donghak influence), the Kapchin Reform Movement (甲辰改革運動), and the March First Movement (己未民族運動) (naturally involving many sacrifices of life and property), continuing to this day. Currently, there are 3,032 believers and 9 parish centers (宗理院). Key figures include leaders (道師) Yi Kiwan and Hong Sŏngun, as well as Messrs. Mun Ch'ŏlmo, Yi Suil, Chu Tohwan, Hong Inp'yo (洪寅杓), Chu Yŏnggon (朱永坤), Paek Nakryong (白洛龍), and younger members like Yi Myŏngdo (李明道), Pak Tochun (朴道中), Ch'oe Kip'il (崔基弼), Ch'oe Chuŏn (崔周彦), and others. This year, a two-story lecture hall was newly built attached to the Hamhŭng Ch'ŏndogyo Center (咸興宗理院), expanding the existing lecture institute (講習所). Additionally, a lecture institute was established at the Chubuk-myŏn Center (州北面 宗理院), educating several hundred male and female students with very good results.

Civic organizations include the Hamhŭng Youth Association (咸興靑年會), the ideological group Uin Association (愚人會), and the newly emerged Ch'ilch'il Association (七七會) (approx. 20 members), Labor Fraternal Association (勞働동문회), etc., but being in the early stages of revitalization or establishment, they currently lack significant influence. However, Messrs. Yi Suŭl (李秀乙), Kim Yŏng'u (金泳雨) (from Hongwŏn), Kim Hun (金勳) (from Taegu), etc., can be called fighters for their cause (主義의 투사). Besides them, young men like Messrs. Ŏm Sŭnggi (嚴承基), Chŏng Kyuhoe (鄭奎會), To Yongho (都容浩), Ra (羅), etc., exert considerable influence within the Youth Association.

Next, famous sites and historical relics include Pallyongsan Mountain (盤龍山), Pongung Shrine (本宮), Kyŏnghŭngjŏn Hall (慶興殿), Kuju Temple (歸州寺), Toksŏdang Hall (讀書堂), Ch'imadae Terrace (馳馬臺), Kyŏkkujŏng Pavilion (擊毬亭), King Chinhŭng's Monument (眞興王碑), Kugyŏngdae Pavilion (龜景臺), Chŏnghwa Mausoleum (定和陵), Manse Bridge (萬歲橋), etc., but these will be left for a future occasion and are omitted here. <75>

**Summary**

This 1924 article provides a detailed overview of Hamhŭng, the provincial capital of Hamgyŏngnam-do, praising its geographical setting, historical significance, and economic vitality. It describes the city's administration, transportation infrastructure (including railways planned by Japanese companies), agricultural and fishery outputs, thriving markets, financial institutions (both Korean and Japanese), educational facilities (including missionary schools), religious landscape (Christianity and Ch'ŏndogyo), and nascent civic organizations. The author contrasts Hamhŭng's rugged grandeur with P'yŏngyang's delicate beauty and highlights the diligence and thriftiness of its people. From a colonial history perspective, the article is revealing in its descriptions of Japanese economic penetration (Imperial Coal Company, Oriental Development Company, Japanese-run businesses, railway expansion plans), the co-existence of Korean and Japanese administrative and financial structures, the role of foreign missionaries in education (Canadian Presbyterian Mission's Yŏngsaeng School), and the mention of nationalist movements linked to Ch'ŏndogyo (March First Movement). Historians might find the detailed statistics on population (including Japanese residents), economy, education, and the portrayal of social dynamics (like women in markets, contrasting Korean thrift with Japanese capital) valuable for understanding regional life and the complexities of Korean society under Japanese rule.

咸南列邑大觀, 咸南 首府인 咸興郡
牧丹峯에 올라서 평양의 全景을 바라볼 것 가트면 강산, 樓臺, 市街 모든 것이 明媚華麗하야 마치 淡粧한 미인이 滿開의 花枝를 가지고 춘풍에 벙긋 웃는 듯하야 不識不知 간에 자연이 정이 붓고 愛가 생겨 떠나랴도 차마 떠나지 못할 감이 잇다. 그러나 盤龍山에 올라서 咸興의 全景을 바라보면 四圍에 大野壯山이 渺渺茫茫하야 深林嘯虎와 大澤潛龍의 風雲변화를 일시 難測함과 가티 其 광대, 高遠함을 이로 포착하야 형언할 수 업다. 咸興은 참 咸南의 雄都다. 안이 엇던 점으로는 朝鮮의 雄都다. 金昌協선생의 「天作咸興壯北方, 鬱葱佳氣古南陽. 靑山盡拱名樓出, 綠水回通市郭長.」 云云의 시는 咸興의 形勝을 잘 묘사하얏다. 이 咸興은 咸鏡南道의 중앙 稍南方에 位하야 東은 洪原郡, 서남은 定平郡, 동남은 洋洋한 碧海를 면하고 서방 일부는 平南 寧遠郡을 접하고 북은 長津 及 新興 兩郡을 界하얏스니 咸南의 도청소재지다. 廣哀 남북 약 15리 18町, 동서 약 7리 18町, 총면적 약 116方里에 달하고 地勢는 동서북 3方은 산악이 起伏하야(狼林山脈) 평지가 少하나 서남은 광활한 평지로 소위 함흥평야를 成하야 其 면적이 실로 28方里에 달하고 토지가 또한 비옥하며 군내에 城川江(發源于甲山樺坡嶺) 汝渭川, 瑚璉江이 貫流하야 灌漑에 편리하고 해안선은 연장이 14리에 亙하야 西湖津, 退潮灣의 淀泊地가 有하고 島嶼는 저명의 大陳, 小陳 2島가 잇다. 如斯한 지세를 有한 咸興은 古來 北關의 重鎭으로 沿革이 또한 甚多하니 즉 古昔 沃沮 종족의 활약하던 遺地로 漢時代의＜72＞ 玄菟郡, 高句麗의 南沃沮, 渤海의 南海府, 女眞의 曷懶甸, 金의 合懶路가 되고 高麗 睿宗 시에 일시 咸州大都 護府가 되얏다가 (尹瓘 擊 女眞 得 此地) 元읊� 哈蘭府가 된 후 雙城(永興)에 속하얏고 恭愍 시에 다시 회복하야 혹은 知州事, 혹은 萬戶府, 혹은 牧을 置하얏섯다.(李太祖 討元復地) 又 本郡의 古號는 咸州, 咸平, 咸山이니 咸興이라 칭하기는 李祖
