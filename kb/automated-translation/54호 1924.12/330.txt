Article Title: 戱曲 王昭君 (Play: Wang Zhaojun)
Author: 梁白華 譯 (Translated by Yang Paekhwa)
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

戱曲 王昭君
						梁白華 譯
Characters
Emperor Xiaoyuan (孝元帝) (7th Emperor of the Former Han)
Wang Zhaojun (王昭君) (Court Lady)
Mao Yanshou (毛延壽) (Court Painter)
Liu Bai (劉白) (Same as above)
Envoy of Huhanxie Chanyu (呼韓邪單于) (Xiongnu)
Civil and Military Officials, Attendants, Eunuchs, Court Ladies, Young Servant (小奚), etc.
Era
Former Han (竟寧 元年 秋 - First year of the Jingning era, Autumn)

Prologue
Scene 1
Mao Yanshou’s painting studio. On the front wall hang two or three portraits, and a little distance away to the right, a landscape painting. To the right of the studio is a model stand, in front of it a sketching stand, and a short distance away, a table and 4 or 5 chairs. Nearby, painting silk being worked on, painting boards, frames, brushes, ink, etc., are scattered about. There are entrance doors on the left and right. An autumn afternoon.
Yanshou: (Arms crossed tightly, head bowed, pacing back and forth in the studio, muttering to himself) I’ve really seen all sorts of strange women. Everyone else covets wealth and glory, but she says she doesn’t want it even a little bit<149>. Isn't that strange? Every single girl chosen from the provinces and brought into this palace hopes to quickly receive His Majesty's favor, be surrounded by numerous eunuchs and maids, wield the authority of an imperial consort, and indulge in wealth and glory. There's none who doesn't wish for that, and to achieve it, they foolishly spend money, offer endless flattery, and use every trick in the book. Is that all? Moreover, nine times out of ten, they send money and goods to us without thinking it a waste, making requests. Yet, uniquely, that girl doesn't desire such things, doesn't want wealth and glory, and is utterly indifferent. I just can't figure out what a young woman like her is thinking. (Thinks) Perhaps that girl, trusting only in her plain face, thinks that even without asking me, with looks like hers, she can have wealth, glory, or whatever she wants. Maybe that's her pride. No, I can only assume that's the case. If so... I have no choice but to explain that it's a misunderstanding. It's a decent matter for the person involved, and leaving her aside, it's a decent matter for me, so our interests align. If money fails, perhaps use allure. Money and allure, honestly, what else is there in our lives? (Stops abruptly) Should I call that girl and explain it once more so she understands?
(As he quickly goes to the door to call a servant, Liu Bai enters from the front.)
Liu Bai: Teacher!
Yanshou: (Stops talking and looks back) What is it?
Liu Bai: (Eyes showing unspoken meaning) It's utterly hopeless (막無可奈何).
Yanshou: What's utterly hopeless?
Liu Bai: She's so stubborn, no matter what I say, she won't budge an inch.
Yanshou: (Finally understanding) You mean the girl?
Liu Bai: It's about the girl I'm in charge of, Yi Wanhwa (李婉華).
Yanshou: Did you explain the pros and cons clearly?<150>
Liu Bai: I talked until my mouth was dry. I told her, "Your fate hangs on this single brush. Whether you become a consort, attend His Majesty, and spend your life drenched in wealth and glory, or become a court lady, run errands under them, and waste your entire life—it all depends on how this brush moves. If you want things to go well, offer as much as you can. Then I'll make sure you're selected. If you don't offer anything, I'll paint you hideously. Will you still refuse?" I spoke quite bluntly, but she still does this (shakes his head left and right).
Yanshou: There's another one like her, I see.
Liu Bai: She insists stubbornly that she doesn't desire such glory.
Yanshou: You must not have spoken properly.
Liu Bai: Not at all. I spent two hours coaxing and persuading her, talking until my tongue was sore, but in the end, she stopped answering and just stayed silent. If she had replied with something, I could have perhaps skillfully persuaded her accordingly, but since she remains silent, there's nothing I can do. Although I've dealt with all sorts of women for a long time, I've never seen a girl as stubborn as her. For her age, she's quite a bold girl.
Yanshou: Bah, you fool. (Lightly) Who praises someone they couldn't persuade? That's why she's stubborn.
Liu Bai: No, really. I couldn't persuade her, and in the end, I came to admire her stubbornness. Such boldness makes her quite a person of character (人格者).
Yanshou: Even an incompetent person like you would admire her. You must be a person of character too, then.
Liu Bai: Heh heh, there you go again. If you say so, I don't know if I'm a person of character, but let's leave the matter of character aside. Look here, teacher. Won't you try dealing with that girl yourself instead of me? Then you'll know if my words are true or false.
Yanshou: Hey! I've met an even worse troublemaker (惡種) than that, and my head hurts. Do you think I have time for that? Since she was entrusted to you, figure it out yourself. If you can't, that's that. There's no need for me to say anything more. If nothing works, then just paint her<151> hideously.
Liu Bai: Yes... (Whiningly)
Yanshou: Just paint her unattractively, make her unpleasant to look at. As punishment for being stubborn.
Liu Bai: Yes... (Seemingly uninterested)
Yanshou: Hm?
Liu Bai: But, teacher.
Yanshou: (Finding it strange) What is it?
Liu Bai: That girl is truly beautiful. (As if in admiration)
Yanshou: So what if she's beautiful?
Liu Bai: It's not that her face is exquisitely flawless without any imperfections, but she's captivatingly beautiful. Just looking at her makes you feel like your entire soul (三魂七魄) is drawn into her eyes. How should I put it? Shall I say she's like a lone orchid grown deep in the mountains, a maiden possessing a pure fragrance? Holding the brush, I just stood there absentmindedly, entranced.
Yanshou: Hey! (Raising his voice) Are you speaking with a clear mind? You didn't come here to spout such nonsense, did you?
Liu Bai: No. She's not the kind of girl I'd talk nonsense about.
Yanshou: Why are you acting like this? Whenever you see a slightly pretty girl, you go gaga, rambling on with fancy phrases like 'a single orchid stalk' or 'pure fragrance.' You always fail because of this. Remember that time? I entrusted you with the girl from Henan (河南), and you didn't take a penny but painted her properly. I thought you'd surely get a fortune, but you didn't take any money, and on top of that, the painting turned out exceptionally well. You said similar things back then too. Despite having considerable skill, the reason you fail so often is because you fall easily for women. If you lose your head over every woman like this, how will you ever make money? Saying you lose your senses and become entranced just by looking at her—is that<152> appropriate talk? Stop spewing fancy words like some third-rate scholar without restraint. Pull yourself together, you hopeless fool!
Liu Bai: (With a sincere attitude) Hopeless or whatever, this is my honest opinion. When she didn't offer money, I picked up the brush intending to paint her carelessly, but unknowingly, I became intoxicated by her beauty...
Yanshou: Look, look. I told you not to. What are you trying to say? I'm someone who doesn't understand such talk. So, what will you do in the end?
Liu Bai: In the end, I won't be able to get a single penny from her.
Yanshou: So what will you do?
Yanshou: So, I'll paint her absurdly ugly, right? A flat nose, eyes slanted wide, a swollen neck, and protruding teeth.
Yanshou: If you paint her like that, won't that be the end of it?
Liu Bai: But... I can't bring myself to paint her like that.
Yanshou: Why not?
Liu Bai: I suppose it's a painter's conscience (畵工의 양심). To betray my feelings about beauty and paint it repulsively...
Yanshou: So you're saying you can't do it?
Liu Bai: I cannot deceive my true heart. As a painter's conscience dictates.
Yanshou: Hey, hey. Stop with the presumptuous talk. What's gotten into you today? Do you even know why we paint? Needless to say, we don't paint for the sake of painting. We paint for money. A girl who pays a lot gets painted well even if she's ugly, and if she doesn't pay, she gets a flat nose and buck teeth no matter how beautiful she is. That's how we make money. Of course, we're human too, so we see beautiful women as beautiful and ugly women as ugly. But if we were to paint them accurately every time, we wouldn't be able to make a living. By making the beautiful ugly and the ugly beautiful, only then<153> can we make money, drink fine wine together, and recline comfortably on silk cushions. But what's this about a painter's conscience, a single orchid stalk, and what not? If you let yourself be swayed by such emotions every time, you'll always be a poor painter. You'll never know the taste of fine wine and delicious food (美酒佳肴). If you waver like this, you can't make money. Unless you transcend emotion and become as cold as water, you can't join our ranks. If you really can't bring yourself to lift the brush because of this 'painter's conscience' or whatever, then quit. Having even one soft-hearted person like you hinders our group. You won't amount to anything, so leave this palace right now. Even though you're my top disciple, I won't miss you one bit.
Liu Bai: No, no. It's not that I absolutely won't paint her like that. It's just that painting such a beauty hideously is truly agonizing for the true heart of a painter...
Yanshou: That's what I'm saying! If it's agonizing, I'm not forcing you to paint her badly, so get out quickly!
Liu Bai: Ah, teacher. Please don't be so angry. If I leave here, I have nowhere else to go...
Yanshou: Then you must paint as I say.
Liu Bai: Yes, I'll paint her. I will.
Yanshou: Just saying you'll paint isn't enough. How will you paint her?
Liu Bai: I'll paint her as hideously as possible.
Yanshou: You incompetent fool! Pull yourself together. Don't you like money?
Liu Bai: (Makes a face and exits dejectedly, muttering to himself) Damn it. Teacher should have the capacity to think about earning money and art separately... But he'll regret this someday. (Exits below)
Yanshou: (Furious while Liu Bai exits) That useless brat came and wasted half my day. (Towards the adjacent room) Come here! Is anyone there?
(A young servant (小奚) comes out.)<154>
Yanshou: Bring that girl here.
Servant: Which girl might that be?
Yanshou: Don't you know?
Servant: Well, sir, there are so many. Just saying 'that girl'...
Yanshou: You rascal! I mean Wang Zhaojun waiting in the separate room (別室)!
Servant: Ah, you mean that girl? I'll bring her right away. (Exits.)
Yanshou: (Pacing back and forth with arms crossed) People in this world are all just absent-minded fools. They quibble over pointless theories and then amount to nothing. Whether it's Liu Bai, that girl named Yi Wanhwa, or even that young servant just now, they're all like that. What... 'just saying that girl'... what kind of withered talk is that? They twist themselves up like pine branches, look down on the world, and live difficult, impoverished lives. If only they'd curry favor and act cleverly, money would pour in... Don't they know there's nothing more precious in this world than money? I just can't understand people's hearts.
(The young servant brings Wang Zhaojun. Yanshou gestures with his chin for her to sit on the chair on the model stand. After Zhaojun sits, the servant exits. Yanshou continues pacing back and forth a couple of times, thinking, then suddenly stops and looks at Zhaojun. Zhaojun lowers her head. He takes a few steps, then, as if struck by a sudden thought, sits at the sketching desk, picks up a painting board, and begins to sketch Zhaojun. He soon puts down the brush and sets the board aside.)
Yanshou: Zhaojun.
Zhaojun: (Quietly raises her head.)
Yanshou: I'm going to paint your portrait now, but before I start, there's something I need to tell you. As I mentioned earlier, all the women selected from the countryside first have their likenesses painted by us for His Majesty the Emperor to examine (鑑). Then His Majesty chooses the beautiful ones he fancies from among them to make them consorts. Since only about ten are chosen from among roughly a thousand women, it's difficult to be selected unless one is exceptionally outstanding. However, once chosen as a consort, you become a noble person second only to the Empress and are granted a palace. Surrounded by many court ladies, you can enjoy wealth and glory for a lifetime<155>. But if you fail to be selected, you'll be like a serving maid (侍婢), never escaping menial tasks, unable to avoid enduring all sorts of sorrow for 10 or 20 years. Therefore, the painting I'm doing now is the single opportunity that determines your entire life's destiny. If you treat this opportunity, which won't come again, lightly, you'll waste your whole life in vain. But you know, regarding the portrait, we are human too, so we can't always guarantee that we paint exactly true to life without any errors. Depending on our mood at the time of painting, even a beautiful person might be drawn unattractively, and an unattractive person might be drawn beautifully. You might think that if you are beautiful in person, the painting will turn out that way too, but that's a big mistake. It all depends on our state of mind. And our state of mind depends on how you act. We never disregard your goodwill. When the tide comes in, the boat goes. Our interests are perfectly aligned, without any gaps. Hmm, having said this much, you should have understood the meaning behind my words. Ha ha. You must have understood clearly. Still, you won't borrow my power? You don't want to become a consort? If you act rashly based on momentary excitement or emotion, you'll regret it later.
Zhaojun: Yes, please just paint as you wish. I do not desire such wealth and glory.
Yanshou: Don't talk nonsense. If you rely solely on your appearance thinking the painting will reflect it, you'll be in big trouble. His Majesty doesn't see you up close. Trusting entirely in that plain face of yours will lead you into a terrible pit. Still, you say...
Zhaojun: Yes, I understand everything.
Yanshou: Hmm... so you enjoy living through hardship? Why do you dislike things going well?
Zhaojun: It is my nature, please forgive me.
Yanshou: No. I'd like to know the reason.
Zhaojun: I no longer desire anything in this world.
Yanshou: Hmph, desire nothing. Why would you so readily abandon this interesting and good world?<156>
Zhaojun: I don't know either.
Yanshou: At your age, it's a time when emotions are most easily stirred and swayed, so it's common to become pessimistic without reason over small things. Perhaps you too have developed a kind of world-weariness due to those feelings now. Being far from your hometown, leaving your parents' side, coming to an unfamiliar place, and facing a sudden change in lifestyle likely makes everything feel lonely and sorrowful. But such feelings are temporary. As time passes, you'll find this place is also a good world. There's no need to be pessimistic at all. If you speak rashly out of momentary emotional turmoil, saying you hate this world or whatever, you'll just become a subject of others' contempt for your entire life. Still, you dislike it? You don't want wealth and glory?
Zhaojun: No matter how much you ask, my answer won't change.
Yanshou: Hmm, quite stubborn. You want things to go well, but you're stingy with money. Stop that brazen talk. Hmm, if that's your thought, I can't help it. Don't complain later when you face sorrows that bring tears streaming down your face. Will that still be alright?
Zhaojun: I have decided.
Yanshou: (As if to himself) Your stubbornness is quite something. Your face is plain, but why is your heart so contrary? If you insist, then I have an idea too. Don't cry later.
(He thinks for a moment, then immediately puts the painting board back in its original position and starts painting. At first, he paints quickly, with broad strokes, but gradually, drawn by Zhaojun's beautiful appearance, he begins to paint more meticulously. Eventually, he becomes engrossed in depicting Zhaojun's beauty. For a while, he alternates his gaze between Zhaojun and the board, moving the brush unconsciously. The attitude of bending the brush for money completely disappears, replaced by the sincere attitude and passion of an artist filling his entire body. When the general outline is almost complete, Zhaojun lifts her head, her gaze meeting his. Startled as if waking from a dream, he stands up, stares blankly at Zhaojun, then looks back at the board, throws down the brush abruptly, stumbles, and collapses onto a chair. He rests his arms on the table, holds his head in his hands, then quietly looks back at Zhaojun. He slowly stands up, makes a terrifying face, glares at her, and retreats 5 or 6 steps. He mutters "Demon, demon" under his breath. Then his eyes fall on the painting board again. He goes forward again, picks up the board, and looks at it closely. Entranced by the painting, the anger and pain gradually fade from his face, and his expression clears.)<157>
Yanshou: (Entranced) Truly a fragrant work of art. This is the first time in decades I've painted something worthy of being called a painting. Why did I not know until today that I possessed such skill? I am truly a born painter. As others say, the best of our time. Who else but me could paint something like this? Well done. Truly well done. (As he looks at it, his thoughts gradually change.) But if the painting turns out well, can I make money? If I always paint like this, I'll just live the life of a poor man. I don't want that. My parents were desperately poor, I was sold to others as a child, shed tears of blood enduring all kinds of hardship as I grew up. With hands cracked open, I washed vegetables in the stream, ate watery gruel just once a day—due to the misfortune of being someone else's child, I was raised no different from a beggar or a pig. During that time, I naturally loved painting and somehow learned to paint. Since then, the idea that money is the enemy became deeply ingrained in my heart. Having suffered sorrow because of money, I decided I would inflict some pain on others with money! I have consistently adhered to this principle until today. And now, because of this painting... (His eyes go to Zhaojun.) Demon. A demon that shatters my conviction.
(He hesitates for a moment, then fiercely grabs the short sword (短刀) on the table, pulls it out swiftly, and is about to strike the painting board in one breath, but suddenly stops his hand and looks at the painting again. He stares for a moment. He glances at Zhaojun, then, as if having made a decision, puts down the painting board and looks around.)
A demon that disturbed my heart. My conviction of several decades has been utterly ruined by this single painting. Tch, this demon!
(He grips the hilt of the short sword tightly and rushes towards Zhaojun. Zhaojun keeps her head bowed and doesn't flinch. At this moment, Liu Bai appears from below, holding a painting board. Just as Yanshou shouts 'Ha!' and is about to strike Zhaojun, Liu Bai grabs him from behind.)
Yanshou: Who is it? (Quietly but forcefully)
Liu Bai: Teacher! What are you doing?
Yanshou: Is that you, Liu Bai? Let go, let go!
Liu Bai: Teacher, are you out of your mind?
Yanshou: Just let go! There's a reason she must not live. Let go! Let go now!
Liu Bai: No! I won't let go! Whatever the reason, now that I've seen this, I won't let go even if I die. No, if there's a reason she must not be killed,<158> surely you can tell your top disciple? Please, calm down.
(During this, he wrests the short sword away and pushes Yanshou back onto the chair. Hearing the commotion, the young servant runs in from the right and stands there bewildered.)
Liu Bai: Hey!
(He gestures towards Zhaojun with his mouth and signals with his eyes for the servant to take her away. The servant, looking confused, takes Zhaojun's arm and gently leads her out. Yanshou sees her leaving, starts to rise to chase her, but is held back by Liu Bai and sits down again.)
Liu Bai: Teacher, this is truly extraordinary. What on earth happened? For you, the calm teacher, to draw a sword and raise such a commotion is something I never even dreamed of. Were you also rejected, just like me? Or else...
Yanshou: Liu Bai, I must apologize to you. Forgive me.
Liu Bai: For what? Why are you suddenly apologizing?
Yanshou: Liu Bai, for the first time in over ten years, I painted a painting worthy of the name. No, a magnificent painting emerged naturally. Today, for the first time, I painted for art's sake, forgot everything else in the world, and reveled in the realm of art (藝術境). Why it happened, I don't know myself, but anyway, such a strange phenomenon occurred to me just a few minutes ago.
Liu Bai: Yes, I truly never dreamed I would hear such words from you, teacher. So why do you say you must apologize to me?
Yanshou: Be quiet and listen. As you know, for decades I have been in this palace, seen and painted all the beauties under heaven, but never once until now have I lost my composure like this, broken my conviction that I paint for money. But today, I was entranced by the beauty of that girl, Wang Zhaojun, and for a while, I even forgot myself. That girl, just like the one you handled, was stubborn, and no matter how I threatened her, she wouldn't agree to give money. So I completely gave up on the money and picked up the brush intending to paint her carelessly as revenge. But before I knew it, I entered a state of selflessness (無我), forgot all the evil thoughts and ugliness of this world, the distinction between myself and her disappeared, and heaven and earth seemed to fuse harmoniously in her feminine beauty, revealing a world of light filled only with infinite beauty<159>. And so, I was wandering aimlessly, as if through a field on a hazy spring day, enveloped in the piercing fragrance of flowers. I was simply praising beauty, intoxicated with joy, feeling entirely like I was playing in a dream. I don't know how much time passed or how much of the painting I completed during that interval. Suddenly, the girl raised her head, her gaze met mine, and I awoke from the dream, returning from the world of beauty to the miserable world of reality. It's truly strange. In all my long years as a painter, it's something I've never experienced, let alone imagined. Originally, as for me, I wielded my brush based on the amount of tribute placed under my nose, turning beauty into ugliness and ugliness into beauty, freely manipulating my brushwork, never failing until now. No matter what beauty I faced, I could sufficiently suppress my emotions with the amount of money involved. That's how cold I was, yet today, for some reason, my emotions flowed freely like water, and I was nearly swept away on that current. Of course, there was no money there, no wealth or glory, and most importantly, I wasn't even conscious of myself. And the brush moved on its own, resulting in this painting. (Points to the painting board. Liu Bai picks it up and examines it closely.) I, who prided myself on my coldness, am ashamed that the painting turned out better than the real person. It just turned out well somehow.
Liu Bai: (Looking closely at the board) It's a divine piece (神品)! A noble work of art. I have never seen such an artistic creation from you before. Setting aside composition and line work, the feeling received from the entire painting is the fragrance of art itself. Teacher, possessing such superb skill, why did you not use it until today? When faced with such a noble work of art, don't you forget the world, money, everything, and feel the highest, purest joy? It seems my trust in you was well-placed; I am immeasurably happy. Teacher, you are indeed a great artist. The foremost figure of our time. But then, Teacher, why... why did you draw a sword and act violently, and say you must apologize to me?
Yanshou: Liu Bai, you surely haven't forgotten what I said to you earlier? You said that when faced with such pure beauty, your painter's conscience wouldn't allow you to paint beauty as ugliness, no matter what.<160> In response, I rebuked you, saying it was because you were infatuated with the girl, that if you thought of everything as making money, there was no reason you couldn't paint her that way. And right after that, I myself became intoxicated by feminine beauty and absentmindedly produced this painting. I have no face to show you. As your senior, I failed to understand your heart and merely told you to paint seeing only money; I am truly sorry for that. I must apologize for disregarding your conscience and forcing unreasonable demands upon you. It feels like I, the senior, have instead learned from you, my junior.
Liu Bai: I am sincerely happy that you have had such an awakening, Teacher. Teacher, please do not change that heart, and for the sake of your art, I hope you will paint wonderful pictures from now on.
Yanshou: However, Liu Bai, although I apologize to you, I do not intend to paint such fine pictures in the future. Such paintings cannot be painted again like that, and even if they were, there would be no one to appreciate them. Firstly, in terms of earnings, it's extremely detrimental. The world is money. My only enemy is money. As you know, I am someone who grew up enduring unspeakable hardships since childhood. Poverty is the enemy of my entire life. My principle is to paint pictures that make money rather than painting fine pictures. I have lived by this principle for decades. I cannot change this principle now.
Liu Bai: Then, Teacher, are you saying that you cannot become an artist no matter what? That you will forget the feeling of playing in the heaven of art, and that the joy of facing a completed masterpiece is worth less than money?
Yanshou: I am a believer in the almighty power of money. Art too is sold for money. No, if you have money, you can buy art, paint it, and even taste it. I am not an artist. I am a merchant who sells paintings.
Liu Bai: Then, your attempt to kill Zhaojun was because she naturally shattered that principle of yours?
Yanshou: That's right. That girl is the demon who shattered my principle. My resolve was almost broken because of her. I couldn't stand her; she was detestable. When I lost my senses and rushed to strike her, I was stopped by you, and only then did I regain my senses. If you had arrived just one step later at that moment, I would surely have killed<161> that girl.
Liu Bai: If that had happened, perhaps your own life would have been lost too, Teacher.
Yanshou: What? I enjoy His Majesty's deep trust to the extent that I would be found innocent no matter what I did. Besides, even if I were to die, if it's for my principles, it doesn't matter.
Liu Bai: Then my intervention was actually wrong, wasn't it?
Yanshou: No, thinking about it now, it ultimately turned out fine. Because I have a way to effectively kill that girl with the tip of my brush. (Picks up the painting board and adds two moles, one on the cheek and one near the outer corner of the right eye.) Look here, Liu Bai. This should do it, right?
Liu Bai: How does that become the same as killing her?
Yanshou: They say a woman with moles on her forehead (天停) and the corner of her eye (魚尾) has the evil physiognomy (惡相) of one who kills men.
Liu Bai: I see... Truly, Teacher, you know all sorts of things. Then, Teacher, please look at my painting. (Holds up his board and gives it to him.) This is the portrait of that girl from earlier.
Yanshou: (Looking at the painting) Hmm. An utterly hideous woman (絶代의 醜婦). With this, there's no doubt she'll fail selection.
Liu Bai: (Placing Zhaojun's portrait and his side by side) Here is a pair of the most miserly girls you'll ever find.
Yanshou: The two leaders of the rejection pile. Hahaha. Everything is money, money!
(To be concluded in the next issue)<162>

**Summary**

The play depicts Mao Yanshou, a cynical Han Dynasty court painter, whose primary motivation is extorting bribes from court ladies in exchange for flattering portraits for the Emperor. His student, Liu Bai, struggles between his artistic conscience and his teacher's demands when faced with a beautiful but poor candidate. Yanshou then encounters Wang Zhaojun, who calmly refuses to bribe him or seek favour. Despite intending to paint her hideously, Yanshou is overwhelmed by her beauty, enters a state of artistic trance, and creates a masterpiece. Horrified at this betrayal of his money-first principle, he snaps back, calls Zhaojun a "demon" for shaking his convictions, and defaces the portrait by adding unlucky moles, ensuring her rejection. He reaffirms his cynical view that money is the only thing that matters, ruining both Zhaojun's and another non-paying beauty's chances with deliberately ugly portraits.

From a historical perspective, this 1924 translation presents a classic Chinese tale that explores the corruption of art by money and power, and the conflict between artistic integrity and cynical pragmatism. For historians of colonial Korea, the piece might be interesting as a potential allegory reflecting the moral compromises and pressures faced by artists and intellectuals under Japanese rule, where navigating patronage, censorship, and economic hardship was crucial. The stark portrayal of Yanshou's materialism versus Zhaojun's quiet integrity could resonate with contemporary anxieties about collaboration, resistance, and the role of culture in a colonized society.

毛延壽의 畵室. 정면되는 벽에 두 서너 폭의 畵像과, 족음 떨어져서 右便으로 山水의 畵幅이 걸리어 잇다. 室右로 모델臺, 그 압흐로 寫生臺, 족음 떨어져서 탁자와 밋 4,5각의 의자가 잇다. 부근에는 그리든 畵絹이며, 畵板, 액자, 筆墨 등이 흐터져 잇고 좌우로 출입문이 잇다. 가을날의 오후.
延壽. (두 팔을 잔득 끼고 고개를 숙이고 室內로 왓다갓다 하면서 혼자 중얼거린다) 이상한  계집도 참 다 보겟지. 남들이＜149＞ 다 불버하는 부귀영화를 저는 족음도 하기가 실타  니 이상치 안탐. 지방에서 뽑히어서 이 闕內로 들어오는 계집이란 계집은 죄다가 하로라두 속히 폐하의 총애를 바다 多數한 內監과 시녀에게 얼쌔어 皇妃라는 威權을 떨떨  이고 부귀영화에 빠저보랴고 바라지 안는 게 업고 그러케들 되랴고 공연히 섯불리 돈을 쓰며 쓸대업는 첨을 만히 하며 별별 수단을 다 쓰지. 그나 그 뿐인가 더욱이 우리에게는 앗가운 줄 모르며 돈이라, 물건을 턱턱 보내며 청들을 하는게 十常八九인데 유독 저 계집은 그러케 되는 것도 바라지 안코 부귀영화도 하고는 십지가 안타고 아조 냉담하단 말이지. 젊으나 젊은 계집으로 무슨 생각으로 그리는지 도모지 알 수가 업거든. (생각한다) 혹 저 계집이 제 반주구리한 얼굴만 밋고 이애 네게 請 아니 하드래두 이만 용모면 부귀영화든 무에든 맘데로 한다는 자존심에서 그리는겐지두 알 수 업지. 아니 그러타구 밧게 할 수 업서. 만일 그러타구 하면... 그건 오해라구 타이를 수 밧게 업지. 그것이 當者에게두 괜찬흔 일이요, 當者는 둘재 처노코 내게 괜찮흔 일이니까 두 사람의 利害關係는 일치란 말이지. 혹 만일 금전에 실패하거든 色으로 때지. 금전과 色, 실상 말하면 우리들의 인생이 이밧게 어대 잇나. (딱 서서) 그 계집을 불러 한 번 다시 알아듯도록 일러볼가.
