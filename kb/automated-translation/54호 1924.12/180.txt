Article Title: 咸南列邑大觀, 山長水長한 長津郡 (Grand View of Hamnam Towns, Changjin County with Long Mountains and Long Rivers)
Author: Not Specified

**Body Text Translation:**
Among the counties of Hamgyŏngnam Province, Changjin County (長津郡) has the longest rivers and mountains, the largest area (over 331 square *ri*), and the sparsest population (while Wŏnsan has 64,151 people per square *ri*, this county has only 133). This county is located in the high mountainous region of the northwest end of Hamgyŏngnam Province; it is long north-to-south and short east-to-west. Within its borders lie high mountain ranges exceeding 2,000 *ch'ŏk* (feet) in elevation, such as Ch'ongjŏn Pass (葱田嶺, elevation 2,066 *ch'ŏk*), Adŭk Pass (牙得嶺, 2,214), Koam Mountain (姑岩山, 2,049), Yŏnhwa Mountain (蓮花山, 2,355), and Paek Mountain (白山, 2,077), which are branches of the Paektu Mountain range. The Changjin River (長津江), over 660 *ri* long, rises in Sinnam Subcounty (新南面) and flows northwards into the Yalu River (鴨綠江). Everywhere there are treasures of dense, great forests untouched by axes for ages (千古斧鉞이 未入한). To the east, it borders P'ungsan (豊山) and Sinhŭng (新興) counties; to the west, it neighbors Kanggye County (江界郡) of P'yŏnganbuk Province; to the north, it touches Samsu County (三水郡) and Huch'ang County (厚昌郡) of P'yŏnganbuk Province; and to the south, it faces Hamhŭng County (咸興郡) and Nyŏngwŏn County (寧遠郡) of P'yŏnganbuk Province.

Because it is a high mountainous area, the climate is extremely cold, with frost and snow usually falling in July and August, causing much damage to agricultural products. The number of households is 7,582 (91 Japanese, 9 Chinese), and the population is 46,220 (186 Japanese, 9 Chinese), whose occupation is mostly slash-and-burn farming (화전농). The most important products are potatoes (馬鈴薯) at 16.4 million *kwan* and oats (燕麥, 귀리) at 170,000 *sŏk*. <77> Among these, the potatoes are of the P'oyŏm variety (浦鹽種), known throughout the province for being high in starch (15-18%), large, and tasty (味佳). Special agricultural products include opium poppies (罌粟) at over 140 *kwan* and honey (蜂蜜) at over 3,420 *kwan*; such products are hard to find in other counties. Instead, rice (米), barley (麥), and millet (粟) are as precious as jade (옥과 가티 귀하고), and for fruits, they only know the names of wild pears (山梨, 돌배). Furthermore, most of the residents are people who migrated from the Hamhŭng (咸興) and Sinhŭng (新興) regions due to difficulties in livelihood, intending to practice slash-and-burn farming. Therefore, their customs, language, houses, systems, and everything else are like those of Hamhŭng and Sinhŭng counties. Also, since many people migrated from Kanggye (江界), customs of P'yŏnganbuk Province (平北) are occasionally present. However, due to inconvenient transportation and backward culture, many aspects are close to unenlightened (몽매에 近하다).

However, recently, automobiles travel up to the county seat (읍), and a forestry railroad has opened up to Oro-ri (五老里), making transportation relatively convenient. But the power of the Japanese, who exploit the profits of Koreans, has already invaded even these potato mountains, establishing a so-called starch company (전분회사) with the huge sum of 500,000 *wŏn*, manufacturing and exporting 100,000 *kwan* of starch annually. Also, there is commotion about the imminent opening of a forestry railroad with a capital of 20 million *wŏn* to take away the inexhaustible treasures of the forests. Besides these, wild wolfberries and elms (杞楡) are also exported to Gifu Prefecture (岐阜縣) by a Japanese association (일본인조합). How would the brothers in the valleys (峽中형제들), still full from their oat meals (귀리밥), know about this?

As for education, besides the usual elementary school (보통학교), there are only a few village schools (서당) in the mountain villages. Regarding religion, Christianity and Ch'ŏndogyo (천도교) have found their way into various places (*lit. entered some temple*). Among them, Ch'ŏndogyo has spread even to the mountains and has considerable influence. Messrs. Ch'oe Kuk-hwan (崔國煥), Kim Chŏng-ho (金貞鎬), and Chŏn Yang-sin (全양신) are said to be devout followers of that religion, working hard in various fields.

Finally, let's briefly mention the history (沿革) and conclude. This county, about 250 years ago, in the 8th year of King Hyŏnjong (顯宗) of the Yi Dynasty (李朝), established a fort (堡) in Kujil-li (舊津里) of Chungnam Subcounty (中南面), also known as Kujin (舊鎭), calling it Changjinse (長津細) or Ch'ŏngsan Ch'aek (靑山柵). In the 9th year of King Chŏngjo (正宗), it moved to Kuŭp-ri (舊邑里) and became a *chin* (鎭, military camp). Twelve years later, a Tokchin Tohobu (獨鎭都護府, Independent Commandery) was established. In the 15th year of King Sunjo (純祖), a part of Samsu County (三水郡) was added, and it became Changjin Prefecture (長津府), moving to the current county seat (現邑). In the first year of Kŏnyang (建陽 元年) under Emperor Yi T'aehwang (李太皇 - Gojong), it was transferred to P'yŏnganbuk Province (平北) and became a county (郡). The following year, it returned to this province (本道 - Hamgyŏngnam). In the 3rd year of Taishō (大正 3년, 1914), during administrative reorganization, Tongsan Subcounty (東上面) was detached and attached to Sinhŭng (新興), and furthermore, subcounties (*myŏn*) were merged and reorganized from 10 to 7, reaching the present day. The history is roughly like this. Other than that, there are few notable scenic spots or historic sites (명소고적) worth mentioning.

But don't be surprised! In these Changjin mountains where people live only on potatoes and oats, there is Cho Chŏng-yun (趙鼎允), the wealthiest man in Hamnam (咸南), no, one of the prominent rich men in all of Chosŏn (조선)! It is said that he is currently living it up in Kyŏngsŏng (京城, Seoul), residing in a magnificent mansion (고대광실) with numerous beautiful concubines (成川美妾). <78>

**Summary**

This article describes Changjin County in Hamgyŏngnam Province as a vast, mountainous, and sparsely populated region known for its harsh climate, potato and oat cultivation (mainly by slash-and-burn farmers who migrated from other areas), and large untouched forests. Despite recent improvements in transportation like automobiles and a forestry railroad reaching Oro-ri, the article highlights the penetration of Japanese economic interests, specifically a large starch factory exploiting local potatoes and plans for a major forestry railroad to extract timber resources, alongside exports of wild plants. The local population is portrayed as culturally backward with limited education (basic schools and some traditional *sŏdang*), while Ch'ŏndogyo has gained influence. The article briefly traces the county's administrative history and concludes with a striking contrast: the presence of Cho Chŏng-yun, one of Korea's wealthiest men (originally from this impoverished region), living luxuriously in Seoul.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its depiction of colonial economic exploitation extending into remote areas like Changjin, focusing on resource extraction (starch, timber, wild plants) by Japanese capital and organizations. It also sheds light on internal migration patterns within Korea due to economic hardship, the persistence of traditional farming methods (slash-and-burn), the low level of modern infrastructure and education in peripheral regions, the spread of new religious movements like Ch'ŏndogyo, and the stark social contrast between the impoverished local populace and a wealthy Korean elite figure residing in the colonial capital.

咸南列邑大觀, 山長水長한 長津郡
咸鏡南道 중에 제일 水長山長하고 또 면적 大(331여 方里) 인구 稀(元山은 1方里 64151인이나 本郡은 133인에 불과)하기는 長津郡이다. 본군은 咸鏡南道의 서북단 고산지대에 在하니 남북이 長하고 동서가 短하며 境內에는 白頭山의 幹脉인 葱田嶺(해발 2,066척), 牙得嶺(2,214), 姑岩山(2,049), 蓮花山(2,355), 白山(2,077) 등 해발 2천척 이상의 고산준령이 處處에 산재하고 연장 660여 리 되는 長津江이 南出北流하야 鴨綠江으로 주입하며 (출원 新南面) 도처에 千古斧鉞이 未入한 鬱密한 대삼림의 보고가 잇다. 동은 豊山, 新興 양군을 界하고, 서는 平北 江界郡과 隣하고 북은 三水郡과 평북 厚昌郡을 접하고, 남은 咸興郡과 平北 寧遠郡을 對하얏다. 기후는 고산지대인 고로 심히 한랭하야 보통 칠팔월에 霜雪이 降함으로 농산물의 피해가 多하고, 호수는 7,582호(일본인 91호, 중국인 9호), 인구는 46,220인(일본인 186 중국인 9)인데 其 직업은 대개 화전농이다. 중요산물은 馬鈴薯 1640만관, 燕麥(귀리) 17만석이 최다하니＜77＞ 기중 馬鈴薯는 浦鹽種으로서 전분이 다하고 (15-18%) 비대하며 味佳하기로 全道에 유명하고 특수농산물로는 罌粟 140여 관, 蜂蜜 3,420여 관이니 이런 산물은 타군에서 보기 어러운 것이다. 그 대신에 米, 麥, 粟 등은 옥과 가티 귀하고 과실은 山梨(돌배) 밧게는 이름도 모른다. 또 그 주민들은 대개 咸興, 新興地方에서 생활이 곤란하야  화전농을 하랴고 이주한 사람인 고로 풍속, 언어, 가옥, 제도, 모든 것이 咸新 양군과 갓고 또 江界에서 이주한 人이 多한 고로 平北의 풍속도 간간이 잇다. 그러나 교통이 불편하고 문화가 낙후한 고로 여러 가지가 몽매에 近하다. 그런데 근래에는 자동차가 읍까지 통행하고 五老里까지 삼림철도가 개통되 야 교통은 비교적 편리하나 조선인의 이익을 착취하는 일본인의 세력은 벌서 감자산속까지 침입하야 50만원의 거금으로 소위 전분회사를 설립하고 매년 10만관의 전분을 제조 수출한다. 또 자본금 2천만원의 삼림철도도 未久 개통되야 무진장의 삼림보고까지 가저라랴고 야단이다. 其 외에 야생 杞楡도 일본인조합에서 岐阜縣으로 수출한다. 峽中형제들이야 귀리밥에 아즉까지 배가 불으닛가 엇지 이것을 알랴. 교육이라고는 예의 보통학교 외에 산간촌락에 幾個 서당이 잇슬 뿐이오. 종교는 기독교, 천도교가 어느 절에 다 차저 드러갓는데 其 중 천도교는 산간까지 보급되야 자못 세력이 잇고, 崔國煥, 金貞鎬, 全양신 諸씨는 그 교의 독신자로 각 방면에 노력중이라 한다. 최후에 沿革을 略擧하고 구만두자. 본군은 距今 250여 년 전 즉 李朝 顯宗 8년에 中南面 舊津里 즉 舊鎭에 堡를 設하야 長津細 又는 靑山柵이라 칭하다가 正宗 9년에 舊邑里에 移하야 鎭이 되고 其 후 12년에 獨鎭都護府를 置하얏다가 純祖 15년에 三水郡의 일부를 加하야 長津府가 되야 現邑으로 移하고 李太皇 建陽 元年에 平北에 移屬하야 郡이 되얏다가 翌年에 本道로 복귀하고 大正 3년 행정정리 時에 東上面을 割하야 新興에 付하고 且 面을 폐합하야 10면을 7면으로 改하야 금일에 至하얏다. 연혁은 대략 이와 갓다. 其 외 명소고적은 可記할 것이 별로 업다. 그런데 놀라지 마외다. 이 감자, 귀리로만 묵을 매고 사는 長津山 속에 咸南의 首富, 안이 조선에 유수한 부자 趙鼎允 군이 잇는 걸을! 그는 즉금 京城에 가서 고대광실에 成川美妾을 다리고서 떵떵거리고 잘 논단다.＜78＞
