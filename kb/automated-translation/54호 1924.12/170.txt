Grand View of Various Towns in South Hamgyŏng, Sinhŭng County: Renewing and Rising Daily

From Hamhŭng, if you take the light railway train of the Chosŏn Railroad's Forest Line and travel northward for about 9 *ri*, you reach a market town called Oro-ri on the north bank where the Sŏngch'ŏn River and Hŭngnim Stream converge (the railway terminus). A little further north from Oro-ri, amidst the intertwining green of drooping willows and old poplar trees, lies a small town resembling a village with about 350 scattered households and a population of roughly 1,400 people; this is Hŭnggyŏng-ri (Tonggoch'ŏn-myŏn), the seat of the Sinhŭng County Office. This Sinhŭng County, true to its name (Newly Rising), is a new town recently established. That is, when the *Pu* (Prefecture) and *Kun* (County) were merged and abolished in March of Taishō 3 (1914), it was created by patching together pieces ripped off from 8 *myŏn* (townships) of Hamhŭng County, 2 *myŏn* of Hongwŏn County, and 1 *myŏn* of Changjin County. Initially divided into 11 *myŏn*, it was later reorganized into 8 *myŏn* and 131 *ri* (villages), which remains the structure to this day. The total number of households in the county is 10,254, with a population of about 62,600, among whom are 61 Japanese households totaling 140 individuals. The land is fertile and irrigation is convenient, and the climate is also mild, making it suitable for agriculture. In terms of cultivated land area, there are about 1,778 *chō* (町) of paddy fields and 12,028 *chō* of dry fields, yielding an annual harvest worth over 4,414,900 *wŏn*, with rice production reaching 13,000 *sŏk* per year. Sericulture is also quite successful, with cocoon yields reaching about 590 *sŏk*. Additionally, the production of soybeans (about 20,000 *sŏk*), oats (30,000 *sŏk*), potatoes (4 million *kwan*), and cattle (over 8,000 head) compares favorably to other counties. About 2,500 *p'il* (疋) of hemp cloth are also produced, and recently, this has been improved and exported to various regions as material for Western clothing. As for mineral resources, the coal from Changp'ung-ri in Kap'yŏng-myŏn and the gold from Ch'ŏnbul Mountain are famous throughout the province. The gold mine has been monopolized by the Government-General, which built large structures on Ch'ŏnbul Mountain, established a Mining Section Branch Office (鑛業課出張所), and has been conducting test excavations for several years now. The coal mine is similarly controlled by a Japanese company called the Imperial Coal Mining Co., Ltd. (제국탄광주식회사), which employs about 500 workers and annually extracts over 88,360 tons, greedily pocketing the proceeds of over 90,000 *wŏn* without even getting saliva on it. Can Korean people dare even go near? Education here, like in other counties, consists of several Public Common Schools (공립보통학교 - likely intended meaning of '공립늳통학교') and a few private schools. Regarding religion, there are many adherents of Ch'ŏndogyo, whose church building is the largest in the county and provides many conveniences by being open for public gatherings. Besides these, there are also some Christian believers. Next, regarding famous sites and historical relics, <76> on Ch'ŏnbul Mountain stands Kaesim Temple (開心寺), an ancient Silla temple. Not only is the mountain scenery exceptionally beautiful, but the temple buildings are also grand; its Taeung Hall (大雄殿), Kŭngnak Hall (極樂殿), Nahan Hall (羅漢殿), Myŏngbu Hall (冥府殿), Ch'ŏnghŏ Pavilion (淸虛閣), and Uha Pavilion (雨花樓) have long been renowned. Also, about 10 *ri* up the valley from Kaesim Temple, there is the so-called Paegak Falls (白岳瀑布), resembling a ten-thousand-foot white rainbow hanging upside down on a steep cliff, which is also worth seeing. Other sites include the pine forest around Tŏgan Tomb (德安陵) (the tomb of King Mokcho and his queen) in Nŭng-ri, Kap'yŏng-myŏn, along the Sŏngch'ŏn River basin, which is suitable as a temporary summer retreat. Furthermore, the memorial steles for the loyal retainer of the Imjin War, Mr. Yu Ŭng-su (柳應秀), and five others allow one to imagine their heroic spirits enduring through a thousand years. Although details are unclear, it seems one descendant of that loyal retainer, Yu, is Mr. Yu Sŭng-hŭm (柳承欽), who studied in Japan, briefly worked as an educator in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), and then, puffed up with pride after becoming a Councilor (參與官), went to the Hamhŭng Provincial Office. Alas! The ancestor and the descendant – their righteousness differs (噫乃祖乃孫이 其義各異로다).
<76-77>

**Summary**

This 1924 article describes Sinhŭng County in South Hamgyŏng Province, detailing its recent formation through the merging of territories from neighboring counties. It outlines the county's geography, population (including a small Japanese presence), agricultural prosperity (rice, soybeans, oats, potatoes, sericulture, hemp), and significant mineral resources (coal and gold). The article also touches upon education, the strong presence of Ch'ŏndogyo, and notable historical sites like Kaesim Temple, Paegak Falls, and Tŏgan Tomb. A critical tone is adopted when discussing the Japanese monopolization of mining resources and when contrasting a contemporary Korean official, Yu Sŭng-hŭm, with his famously loyal ancestor, Yu Ŭng-su. For historians, the article offers insights into administrative restructuring under Japanese rule, the economic landscape focusing on both agriculture and resource extraction (specifically noting Japanese corporate control and profits from mining), the social fabric including religious affiliations like Ch'ŏndogyo, and contemporary commentary on Korean collaboration within the colonial system through the example of Yu Sŭng-hŭm. The mention of specific production figures and company names provides concrete data points.

咸南列邑大觀, 日新日興의 新興郡
咸興에서 朝鮮鐵道 森林線의 輕便式 기차를 잡아타고 북방으로 9리여를 가면 城川江과 黑林川의 합류되는 北岸에 五老里라는 시장이 잇고 (철도종점) 五老里에서 북방으로 얼마를 또 가면 垂柳老楊이 交翠한 속에 엉셩드뭇한 가옥 약 350여 호 인구 1천4백여 인 되는 일개촌락과 如한 소도시가 잇스니 이것은 新興郡廳 소재지 興京里다(東古川面). 이 新興郡은 이름과 가티 근래에 새로 생긴 新邑이다. 즉 大正 3년 3월 府郡 폐합할 때에 咸興郡의 8면, 洪原郡의 2면 及 長津郡의 1면을 뜨덕이 모양으로 뚝뚝 뜨더서 합해 만든 새고을이다. 최초에는 11面으로 구별하엿다가 其 후 다시 8면 131개리로 改하야 금일까지 至하얏는데 全郡 호수는 10,254호, 인구는 62,600여 인인 중 일본인이 61호 140인이다. 토지는 비옥하고도 관개가 편리하며 기후가 또한 온화하야 농업에 적합하다. 경지면적으로 말하면 沓 1778町여, 田 12028町으로서 일개년 수확고가 4,414,900여 원이고 米의 산출이 年 13,000석이다. 蠶業도 상당이 잘되야 收繭高 590여석에 달하고 또 大豆 2만여석, 燕麥 3만석, 馬鈴薯 4백만貫, 畜牛 8천여 頭도, 타군에 비하야 손색이 업다. 麻布도 2500여 疋이나 산출되는데 근일에는 이것을 개량하야 양복감으로 각지에 수출도 한다. 鑛産으로는 加平面 長豊里의 석탄과 千佛山의 금이 全道에 유명한데 금광은 총독부에서 독차지를 하야 千佛山에 宏大하게 집을 짓고 鑛業課出張所를 내서 몃해재 試掘중이오. 석탄광은 소위 제국탄광주식회사라는 일본인 회사가 또 자치하야 종사원 5백여인으로 매년 88,360여 톤을 채굴하야 그 대가 9만여원을 침도 뭇치지 안코 작고 집어생킨다. 조선 사람이야 감히 근처나 갈가부냐, 교육은 이곳에도 他郡과 일반으로 공립늳�통학교 몃, 사립학교 몃이 잇고 종교는 天道敎 신자가 만히 잇는데 그 敎堂은 군내에 제일 大건물로 일반집회에 공개하야 만흔 편익을 주며 其 외 기독교신자도 다소 잇다. 그 다음에 名所古蹟으로＜76＞ 말하면 千佛山에는 신라 고찰인 開心寺가 잇는데 산수도 奇麗하거니와 건물이 宏大하야 그 大雄殿, 極樂殿, 羅漢殿, 冥府殿, 淸虛閣, 雨花樓는 自來 저명하얏고 또 開心寺로부터 10리 許山谷을 올라가면 懸崖絶壁에 萬丈白虹이 倒掛함과 如한 소위 白岳瀑布가 有하니 亦 一觀의 가치가 잇다. 其외 城川江 유역 加平面 陵里에 在한 德安陵(穆朝 及 其后陵)의 松林도 夏季의 일시 피서지의 適宜하고 또 壬辰忠信 柳應秀씨 외 5씨의 忠魂碑는 千載의 英靈을 可想하겟다. 그런데 자세이 알 수는 업스나 그 柳충신의 자손은 일본유학생으로 일시 京城에서 교육가 노릇을 좀 하다가 參與官 바람에 코가 더 웃둑해서 咸興道廳에 가 잇는 柳承欽씨도 한 사람인 듯하다. 噫乃祖乃孫이 其義各異로다.
＜76-77＞
