Idle Talk

Needless to say, the domestic disastrous situation in Germany recently is worsening, and the value of the Mark, that country's currency, is plummeting daily. Before the Great War, about 4 Marks were equivalent to 1 US Dollar (approximately 2 Won), but on the 20th of last month, it took 7,233,333 Marks to equal one US Dollar. As the situation is roughly like this, the German national bank issues about 7 trillion in banknotes daily, with each banknote having a face value of 100 million Marks. Furthermore, it's said that these banknotes being issued are printed only on one side to save labor and expenses. If things continue like this, Germany's monetary system might completely collapse, and a new nation of barter (a second Russia) might emerge.

The Great Japanese Empire, which demonstrates the practical example of feeding someone while coaxing and deceiving them, decided last year to allocate 2.5 million Yen annually for cultural projects towards China. Out of this, 400,000 Yen was designated for Chinese students studying in Japan. Recently, due to the political turmoil in China, remittances for government-sponsored students in Japan have ceased. Knowing this, the Japanese authorities intended to support them with the aforementioned 400,000 Yen, but the students flew into a rage, declaring they would rather die than accept Japan's help, rejecting it flatly. Japan's situation, where it cannot help but promote Sino-Japanese friendship even while giving money and suffering humiliation, is also pitiful.

Mr. Karakhan, the Russian Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, who is staying in Pongsŏn Castle (Mukden) and conducting, in effect, preliminary Russia-China negotiations with Mr. Chang Tso-lin, is currently receiving extremely lavish treatment from Mr. Chang. Don't be surprised: the money spent just on the bedding for Mr. and Mrs. Karakhan is said to be a sum of 4,000 Yen. Do officials of the Labor-Farmer Republic indulge in such luxury when they become guests in a capitalist country? Haha.

Lieutenant General Yasumitsu Kin'ichi, former Chief of Staff of the Chosŏn Army, who was recently transferred to become the head of the Army Aviation Headquarters, stated in his parting remarks about Chosŏn that transplanting 800,000 Japanese people to Chosŏn and establishing five new army divisions is, above all else, the fundamental good policy for governing Chosŏn. This opinion is indeed something one could only hear from Lieutenant General Yasumitsu, who has the background of having been the Chosŏn Army Chief of Staff. Alright. Not just 800,000, but 8 million; not just 5 divisions, but 50 divisions would be fine. Do as much as you can, if you can. Was it because you lacked the power to do it, not because you lacked the will? Have you ever truly hesitated or abandoned the execution of any plan within your power out of consideration for the obstruction of the true happiness of the Chosŏn people? If there is even one thing you thought of but couldn't do, wasn't it only because you lacked the power to do it, or because the circumstances didn't allow it?

If you ask which region lags behind in the cultural movement, is blind to social movements, and even now is clearly antiquated with its *yangban* posturing and trend of currying favor with officials, the land that steps forward saying "Here I am!" must surely be the North and South Ch'ungch'ŏng Provinces. While a certain young man was traveling in the South Ch'ungch'ŏng region, in the course of conversation, he mentioned something about Yi Wan-yong in Seoul. Upon hearing this, a certain *yangban* flushed with anger and said, "What do you mean by 'Yi Wan-yong'? Wasn't he our country's Prime Minister, anyway? A Prime Minister is like a minor king. How rude it is to call Prime Minister Yi, a minor king, directly by his name!" and immediately picked a fight. Of course, this is just one incidental example, and one cannot judge the whole based on this. In any case, the brethren of Ch'ungch'ŏng Province must exert extraordinary effort for the future complete change of that region.<126>

**Summary**

This article presents four short, unrelated anecdotes reflecting contemporary issues in 1923. The first describes the hyperinflation in Weimar Germany, suggesting a potential collapse into a barter economy like Soviet Russia. The second criticizes Japan's hypocritical "cultural policy" towards China, highlighting Chinese students' refusal of Japanese aid despite hardship. The third mocks the luxurious treatment received by Soviet envoy Karakhan from the Chinese warlord Chang Tso-lin in Mukden. The final, longest piece vehemently critiques the colonial mindset through the reported remarks of a departing Japanese general advocating increased Japanese settlement and military presence in Korea, followed by an anecdote criticizing the perceived backwardness and lingering pro-establishment attitudes (specifically towards Yi Wan-yong) in Korea's Ch'ungch'ŏng provinces. For historians, the article offers insight into Korean perceptions of international events (German inflation, Sino-Soviet relations), critiques of Japanese colonial policy and its paternalistic rhetoric, internal Korean social commentary on regional attitudes and lingering traditional hierarchies (even regarding controversial figures like Yi Wan-yong), and the strong anti-colonial sentiment expressed through sarcastic and defiant language. The piece on Yasumitsu Kin'ichi directly quotes and refutes a specific colonial policy proposal, showcasing contemporary resistance discourse.

헛튼 니약이
근래 獨逸의 국내적 慘狀은 더 말할 것도 업거니와, 그 나라의 통용화폐인 馬克의, 갑은 나날이 폭락하야, 大戰 이전에는 약 4馬克이면 米貨 1弗(約 2圓)을 當하던 그것이, 지난 20日에는, 7,233,333 馬克이라야 米貨 一弗을 當하는 계산이 잇섯다. 형세가 大槪 如許한 지라 獨逸 국립은행에서는 매일 약 7兆의 지폐를 발행하되 지폐 한 장에 1億 馬克의 액면을 附하며, 또 이와 가티 발행하는 지폐는 手工과 經費를 절약키 위하야, 한 면만 인쇄하야 통용한다는 말이다. 이러케만 가면, 獨逸에는 화폐제도가 아조 붕괴되고, 물물교환의 새나라(第二 露西亞)가 現出될 지도 몰나.

얼려가며 무엇을 먹인다는 실례를 演하는 大日本帝國에서는, 작년 중에 對支 문화사업비로 年 250萬圓을 지출키로 하고, 그 중의 40萬圓을 中國사람으로 日本에 와 잇는 유학생에게 지불키로 하엿다. 최근 中國 정국의 紛擾로 日本에 잇는 관비유학생에 대한 송금이 杜絶된다. 이를 아는 日本 당국은, 例의 40萬圓으로 그들을 惠養하려한 즉, 그들은 勃然大怒하야 죽으면 죽엇지 日本의 도움은 밧지 안켓다고, 一言에 거절하엿다. 돈 주어가며 창피 당하도록 日支의 친선을 내세우지 아늘 수 업는 日本의 경우도 딱하다.

奉天城 中에 滯在하야 張作霖씨와 더브러, 사실상으로 露中豫備交涉을 행하고 잇는 露國 외무차관 「카라한」씨는, 지금 張氏로부터 극진한 대접을 밧고 잇는데, 놀나지 마라. 「카」씨 夫妻의 寢具에뿐 소비된 돈이, 一金 4,000圓이라 한다. 勞農共和國의 官員도 자본주의 나라의 손님이 되면 그만한 豪奢는 하는 것인가. 呵呵.

元朝鮮軍 참모장으로서, 先者에 육군항공본부장으로 轉勤된 安滿欽一中將은, 朝鮮을 떠날 때의 感想談에, 朝鮮에는 日本인 80萬名을 移植하고 육군 五個師團을 신설함이, 무엇보다도 朝鮮의 통치를 위한 根本良策이라 하엿다. 이 의견이야말로 曾經 朝鮮軍 참모장의 履歷을 가진 安滿 中將이 아니면 못 드러볼 말이다. 올타. 80萬이 아니라 800萬이라도, 5個 사단이 아니라 50 사단이라도 조타. 할 수만 잇스면 얼마라도 하여라. 너희가 할 힘이 업서서, 못햇지, 할 마음이 업서서 못하엿더냐. 너희가 진정한 朝鮮 사람의 행복의 沮戱를 慮하야, 너희들의 힘으로 할 수 잇는 엇던 계획의 실행을, 진작 躕躇 혹은 抛棄해 본적이 이섯더냐. 만일 생각이 잇고도 못한 것이 하나 이라도 잇다하면, 그것은 할 힘이 업서 못한 것이오, 할 형편이 못되여서 못한 것뿐이 아니냐.

문화운동에 뒤떠러지고, 사회운동에 눈 어둡고, 지금도 지금도 兩班行勢, 官邊趨勢에 古色이 蒼然한 지방이 어느 지방이냐 하면, 나 여긔 잇소 하고 나서는 땅은, 想必 忠淸南北道이겟다. 엇던 청년이 忠南地方을 여행하든 중, 말 말해가다가 서울 잇는 李完用이가 如何하다 하엿더니, 이 말을 들은 엇던 양반은 勃然作色하야 李完用이란 말이 무슨 말이오. 여하간 그가 우리 나라의 總理大臣이 아니엿소, 總理부터는 작은 님금이 이니오. 작은 님금인 李總理의 名啣을 直呼함은 얼마나 無禮하냐고 하야 대번에 싸움을 걸더라는 말이다. 물론 이것은 우연히 나타난 一例로서 이로써 전반을 律할 수는 업는 것이다. 여하간 충청도의 兄弟로서는 該地方의 今後 換判에 대하야 남다른 노력을 취하지 아느면 안될 것이다.＜126＞
