Publication Date: 1923년 09월 01일
Article Title: 內外日誌, 自 7月 21日 至 8月 13日. (Domestic and Foreign Daily Log, From July 21st to August 13th.)
Author: 一記者 (One Reporter)
Article Type: 소식 (News)

內外日誌, 自 7月 21日 至 8月 13日.
						一記者

【July 21st】
It is reported that the full text of the 11 chapters of the constitution of Soviet Russia (勞農露國), ratified (批寶) on the 6th of this month, was announced to all peoples and governments of the world by "Izvestia" on the 14th.

The American Women's University Student Association petitioned President Harding (하딍) to quickly ratify the League of Nations treaty.

In Breslau (푸레스로), Germany, a major disturbance arose due to the cost of living issue, resulting in numerous casualties from clashes between officials and civilians, and over 1,000 people were arrested.

The Chosŏn Boy Scout Troop (朝鮮 少年斥候團) began outdoor mock exercises today, scheduled for about two weeks.

【Same day, 22nd】
US Secretary [of State] Mr. Hughes (휴스) declared to Mr. Gompers (콤파스), president of the National Labor Union Federation, that recognition of Soviet Russia (勞農露國) is impossible unless its leaders abandon their destructive spirit domestically and internationally.

The Kyoto (京都) Veterans' Association (在鄕軍人會) held a preparatory committee meeting at the "Royal Cafe" to give concrete form to the suffrage movement, deciding to distribute 30,000 manifestos nationwide and hold a federation rally on August 1st.

A new commercial treaty between Poland (波蘭) and Turkey (土耳其) was concluded in Lausanne (로-싼누).

【Same day, 23rd】
A conference for youth leaders, hosted by the Saektonghoe (색동會) and Ŏrini-sa (어린이社), opened in the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall (天道敎堂) in Kyŏngun-dong (慶雲洞).

Mr. Ramsay MacDonald (람셰, 마그드날드), leader of the British Labour Party (勞働黨), began a movement to hold a new international conference on arms limitation and sent communications to the leaders of various countries' Labour Parties.

A commercial treaty between Germany and Russia was concluded; Russia is to deliver 212 million poods (布度) of grain by the end of November, and Germany will purchase this with industrial goods.

【Same day, 24th】
The Philippine independence committee, composed entirely of members of the Philippine legislature, seizing the opportunity presented by the recent political change (政變), unanimously resolved to demand absolute independence for the Philippines from the US President and Congress.

At the Lausanne Plenipotentiary Conference, it was decided to sign the Near East Peace Treaty to prevent a Greco-Turkish (希土) war.

【Same day, 25th】
In Frankfurt (푸랭크포드), Germany, 100,000 Communist Party members (共産黨員) and Semi-Nationalist Party members (半國粹黨員) held a rally for a demonstration and beat to death a prosecutor who came for inspection.

Representatives of the US and Turkey began consultations at İsmet Pasha's (이스멧트파사) [location?] to conclude a separate treaty.

【Same day, 26th】
A meeting of the Central Council (中樞院) opened today within the Central Council building in Chŏng-dong (貞洞), scheduled for three days until the 28th.

The Allied Powers lifted the transportation blockade on the Ruhr (루엘) region.

【Same day, 27th】
Kyŏngsŏng City Office (京城府) initiated a night depository service.

The Japanese legation in China made public all details of the Sino-Japanese negotiations regarding the non-recognition issue of Minister Yoshizawa (芳澤公使), citing instances of discourtesy by the Chinese side. Due to this, Sino-Japanese negotiations became noticeably tense.

Mr. Kalinin (카리인), Chairman of the Central Committee of the All-Russian Union Republics (全露國 聯盟共和國), arrived in Chita (智多) today and received a grand welcome from officials, civilians, students, and various organizations, giving the impression just like an imperial procession (帝王의 巡行) of former days.

【Same day, 28th】
The Chinese government had telegraphed its Minister to France (駐佛公使) to negotiate the return of Guangzhouwan (廣州灣) according to the Washington Treaty, but France, awaiting China's northern unification (北統一を侯?), <123> (The following 7 lines are unreadable)

【Same day, 30th】
Chen Jiongming's (陳炯明) army occupied Huizhou (惠州).

France, Italy, and Belgium sent replies regarding the draft of Britain's reparations (賠償公文) memorandum.

The Philippine Party (比律賓黨) residing in Honolulu (호노루루), Hawaii (布哇), held a large rally in support of the movement to oust the Governor-General there, resolving to petition the US President for General Wood's (웃드) transfer. Attendees numbered over 1,000.

【Same day, 31st】
The organizers' meeting for the All-Korea Businessmen's Conference (全鮮 실업가대회) opened within the Kyŏngsŏng Chamber of Commerce (京城 商業會議所).

The British consul stationed in Amoy (*門) was seriously wounded by gunfire from disorderly Chinese troops (亂軍).

The preliminary Japan-Russia negotiations concluded.

【August 1st】
Seven individuals, including Colonel Emorin? (에모린), the ringleader of the White Army conspiracy being tried in the Russian People's Court (人事 재판소), were executed by firing squad today.

A gang of mounted bandits (馬賊團) attacked the Chinese prison in Emu County (額穆縣), Jilin Province (吉林省), shot the head guard dead, and freed over 90 prisoners.

The Baoding Conference (保定會議) in China resolved to elect an extraordinary president (非常總統).

A great downpour came to Kyŏngsŏng (京城), reaching over 2 sõk 1 tu (approx. 380 liters?) in 4 hours, said to be a phenomenon seen for the first time since the Kyŏngsŏng meteorological station was established.

【Same day, 2nd】
US President Harding (하딍) passed away today at 7:30 PM.

The first general meeting of the Chosŏn Fisheries Association (朝鮮水産會) opened today at the Kyŏngsŏng Club (京城 俱樂部), scheduled for six days until the 7th.

【Same day, 3rd】
In Qiqihar (齊齊*爾), Chinese bandits (土匪) and government troops (官兵) clashed; 700 bandits were shot dead, and the government troops also suffered about 200 casualties.

Centering on P'yŏngyang (平壤), the heavy rain (猛雨) that had been pouring since the 1st continued until today, causing the Taedong River (大同江) water level to exceed 35 ch'ŏk (approx. 10.6 meters). The entire city was flooded, with over 7,000 houses washed away or collapsed, and numerous casualties among people and livestock, presenting a disastrous scene unprecedented in 73 years.

US Vice President Calvin Coolidge (칼빈쿨린지) was promoted to the new presidency and held an inauguration ceremony (宣誓式).

【Same day, 4th】
A British torpedo boat sank a German vessel smuggling alcohol off the coast of Kristiania (크리스치나) [Oslo], resulting in numerous casualties.

【Same day, 5th】
The Irish Free State Army (愛蘭自由國軍隊) attacked the Republican Army (共和軍) in the west of the country, capturing several prominent leaders.

【Same day, 6th】
A US-Turkey peace treaty was concluded in Lausanne (로싼누).

The Portuguese (葡萄牙) National Assembly elected Mr. Pinheiro Chagas? (비*제이라고*) as the country's new president. (He has been serving as Minister Plenipotentiary stationed in London since 1918).

【Same day, 7th】
The trial related to the Ŭiyŏldan (義烈團) incident involving Kim Si-hyŏn (金始顯), Hwang Ok (黃鈺), and eleven others opened in Courtroom No. 7 of the Kyŏngsŏng District Court (京城 지방법원) with Presiding Judge Mitsuya? (三矢) and Prosecutor Hirayama? (平山) present.

The Pittsburgh (핏스뻑) steel mills in the US decided to abandon the previous 12-hour workday system and implement an 8-hour system starting from the 16th of this month.

British sailors stationed in Amoy (厦門), China, occupied the railway and coastal areas.

【Same day, 8th】
To reduce the standing army and achieve the goal of universal conscription (國民皆兵), the Soviet government today promulgated decrees regarding the organization of replacement units (交換部隊) and national military education.

Thirty members of an armed independence group (獨立團) raided P'yŏngsŏng (平城), Ŭiju County (義州郡), and Ch'ŏngsŏng (淸城), cutting telephone lines, burning government offices, and engaging in fierce combat with Japanese police.

The British Minister in China received instructions from his home government to sign the Weihaiwei (威海衛) Return Agreement. <124>

【Same day, 9th】
Representatives from 18 organizations in Kyŏngsŏng gathered within the Aguhwe (岳友會) outside the Four Great Gates to launch the West Korea Flood Relief Association (西鮮 水害救濟會) and selected 30 executive committee members.

【Same day, 10th】
Soviet representative Mr. Joffe? (욧폐), who had been staying in Tokyo (東京) for many days charged with the task of preliminary Japan-Russia negotiations, departed Tokyo tonight for Russia.

The Far East International Bar Association (國際 辯護士協會) opened in Manila (馬尼刺), Philippines.

【Same day, 11th】
The second trial of the Ŭiyŏldan (義烈團) opened in Courtroom No. 7 of the Kyŏngsŏng District Court. The prosecutor sought the following sentences: Kim Si-hyŏn (金始顯), Hwang Ok (黃鈺), Yu Sŏk-hyŏn (劉錫賢) - 10 years imprisonment; Nam Nŭng-dŭk (南寧得), Yu Pyŏng-ha (柳秉夏), Yu Si-t'ae (柳時泰), Hong Chong-u (洪鍾祐) - 8 years; Yi Hyŏn-jun (李賢俊) - 7 years; Paek Yŏng-mu (白英武) - 6 years; Cho Hwang (趙晃) - 5 years; Cho Tong-gŭn (趙東根) - 3 years; Yi Kyŏng-hŭi (李慶熙) - 1 year and 6 months.

Celebrations for the anniversary of the promulgation of the Republic's constitution were held in Germany, but the Communist Party (共産黨) and Social Democratic Party (社會黨) caused major disturbances in various places, resulting in over 100 casualties.

The new US President Coolidge (쿨리지) declared that he would follow the policies of the previous president and also assist in realizing world peace.

A ferry capsized on the Han River (漢江), resulting in 11 people falling into the water; 3 were rescued, and 8 drowned in a major tragedy.

【Same day, 12th】
The founding ceremony of Japan's Status Quo Breaking Youth Group (現狀打破 쳥년단) was held upstairs at the Matsumoto-rō in Hibiya (日比谷). Attendees, including lawyer Kopong? (古蜂), numbered about 500.

As German Chancellor Cuno (크노) formally resigned, President Ebert (에벨트) ordered Mr. Stresemann (쓰레쓰맨) to form a new cabinet.

The ministers of Britain, Japan, the US, and France delivered a new note to the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs regarding the protection of foreigners in the future Zhejiang-Jiangsu (浙蘇) turmoil.

【Same day, 13th】
At 8:30 AM, a massive tidal wave (大海溢) occurred in the Yongch'ŏn County (龍川郡) area of North P'yŏngan Province (平北). Over 2,000 farmers are missing, presumed dead, and over 4,000 houses were flooded, creating an unprecedented scene of disaster.

The US Navy Department (海軍省) approved a budget of 110 million dollars for a 20-year continuing project to defend the Pacific coast, resolving to greatly expand the navy.

A storm and massive tidal wave (大海溢) struck Chinnamp'o (鎭南浦), flooding over 1,000 houses, destroying 4-5 ships, and causing many casualties. On the same day, Kwangnyang Bay (廣梁灣) was also entirely flooded due to a tidal wave, with 4-5 laborers missing. Also on the same day, a major storm arose in Inch'ŏn (仁川), capsizing 2 sailboats.

A major disaster occurred on Kuryŏp Island (堀業島), 40 nautical miles (海利) from Inch'ŏn, where a storm caused great damage, destroying over 200 fishing boats and leaving 1,200 fishermen missing.

【Same day, 14th】
Thirty members of an independence group (獨立團) raided Unsi-myŏn (雲時面) in Pyŏktong County (碧潼郡), burning down the police substation (駐在所) and the township office (面所), and shooting one policeman dead.

Soviet representative Zildansky? (질단스키) signed the Straits Convention.

The German parliament passed a vote of confidence in the new cabinet by a large majority of 240 to 76.

Mr. Chŏng Pong-sin (鄭奉信), a socialist (主義者) who was touring Korea as a speaker for the Puksonghoe (北星會), drowned at a beach in Pusan (釜山).

【Same day, 15th】
Royalists (復辟派) rose up in Germany, attacking Communist-affiliated newspaper offices in a major disturbance that resulted in 1 death and dozens detained (拘禁者).

A report came that bandits (土匪) had risen up in the Ancheng (安城) region of China, massacring over 1,000 villagers.

In Hamp'yŏng (咸平), South Chŏlla Province (全南), a major clash occurred between several hundred farmers and police officers (警官), resulting in numerous serious and minor injuries and the arrest of over 30 farmers. The cause lay in an argument between farmers at a farmers' banquet (農宴) and one Kwŏn Ho-il (權昊一), who then fled to the police station for safety.

Four members of an independence group (獨立團) infiltrated Manho-dong (萬戶洞) in Wiwŏn County (渭原郡), shot Kim Sang-sin (金尙信) and three others dead, and also set fire to private houses.

【16th】
Regarding the Yongch'ŏn County flood disaster, 400 bodies of drowning victims have been discovered, over 1,000 are still missing, and it is confirmed (確實되다 -> *실되다 typographical error likely meant 確實되다) that over 1,000 houses were lost.

Anti-Japanese sentiment (排日) in Hankou (漢口) has worsened, and Japanese residents are in great fear.

【17th】
The US and Mexico concluded and signed an agreement aimed at restoring diplomatic relations.

Several dozen members of an independence group (獨立團) raided Wiwŏn town center (渭原邑內) and set fire to the town, causing a major disaster.

【18th】
In Kimhae County (金海郡), a major disturbance arose between several dozen farmers/laborers and the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (衡平社). Farmers attacked, committed arson, and assaulted individuals associated with the Youth Association (청년회) and Education Association (교육회) which had supported the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa. <125>

**Summary**

This news digest from September 1923 covers significant international and domestic events occurring between late July and mid-August 1923. Internationally, it reports on the publication of the Soviet constitution, US policy towards the Soviets, German economic unrest and political instability (including the fall of the Cuno cabinet), the Lausanne Treaty finalizing peace in the Near East, Philippine independence demands, the death of US President Harding and Coolidge's succession, US-Turkey treaty negotiations, ongoing reparations talks, Japanese-Soviet preliminary talks, US naval expansion plans, and unrest in China. Domestically (within Korea), it notes activities of the Boy Scouts, the colonial Central Council meeting, devastating floods in P'yŏngyang and North P'yŏngan province (Yongch'ŏn, Chinnamp'o) leading to relief efforts, the trial and sentencing of Ŭiyŏldan members, continued armed actions by independence groups along the northern border, and social clashes involving farmers and the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa in Kimhae. For historians, this article offers valuable insight into how international events were reported in colonial Korea, the continued activities and suppression of the Korean independence movement (Ŭiyŏldan, border raids), the severe impact of natural disasters exacerbated by colonial conditions, and emerging social tensions like the conflict involving the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (an organization advocating for the rights of the traditionally discriminated Paekchŏng class).

						一記者
【7월21일】
本月 6日에 批寶된 勞農露國의 헌법전문 11章을 14日 「이스베스챠」에서 세계 전국민 全政府에 公表하얏다는 보도가 有하다.
米國 女子大學生協會에서 「하딍」대통령에게 국제연맹조약을 속히 批準케 하라고 訴하다.
獨逸 「푸레스로」에서 생활비 문제로 大騷動이 起하야 官民 충돌의 결과 다수의 사상자가 출하고 人民逮捕된 자 1,000餘 名에 達하다.
朝鮮 少年斥候團은 本日부터 약 2주간 예정으로 야외 假裝練習을 개시하다.
