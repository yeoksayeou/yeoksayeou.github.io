발행일	1923년 09월 01일
기사제목	文士諸君에게 與하는 一文(下), 文化社 一派의 「떼가단」的 傾向과 文人會 一派의 「째나리슴」적 傾向 (A Message to Literary Figures (Part 2), The "Decadent" Tendency of the Munhwasa Faction and the "Cenacle-ism" Tendency of the Muninhoe Faction)
필자	任鼎宰
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Essay)

A Message to Literary Figures
						Im Chŏngjae
No. 3
I wrote in the previous issue that art must be of the masses (民衆的), but that this does not mean appealing to the entire populace (全民衆的). As there seems to be some misunderstanding regarding this, let me explain in a bit more detail.
Whether "of the entire populace" (全民衆的) or "of the masses" (民衆的), although there is a difference in scope (廣狹), the meaning (意義) is the same. The term "masses" refers nationally to one ethnic group (一民族) and internationally to all of humanity (世界人類). Therefore, appealing to the entire populace does not mean basing art on the tastes and trends of one nation (一國家) and the entire society (全 社會), but rather refers to subordinating oneself selflessly (失我的) to the classless (無階級的) mass movement. Examples include the moral art (道德藝術) or religious art (宗敎藝術) movements of ancient times (古代). Thus, that which is induced is without individuality (無個性), and that which is selfless (失我的) becomes lifeless (無生命的); this kind of thing is not class art (階級藝術).

This state of religious art (宗敎藝術), namely church art (敎會藝術), and moral art (道德藝術), namely ethical art (倫理藝術), being a temporizing condition (姑息的 狀態) of the living self (生活自我), arises from the idealization (理想化) of the sense of reality (實在感), that is, the mode of living (生活要式), and the philosophical view of life (哲學的 人生觀) involving transcendence of life (生活超越), in active human life (活動的 人間生活). However, in the modern consciousness of life (現代的 生活意識) (life-centered human life), this is human destruction (人間破滅). Furthermore, in the impulsive life (衝動生活) or rational, intellectual life (理智生活) based on modern consciousness, the dynamic self (動的自我) possesses a fluid life (流動的 生活) as a necessary process of life (必然的 生命의 過程), not a convenient (方便的) one. Therefore, fixed, pre-existing art (固定 既存의 藝術) is, from the perspective of modern consciousness, a record of stagnation (停滯), temporizing (姑息), and self-destruction (自滅). Bourgeois (뿔쪼와) art like this has only been accepted humanly (人間的); besides that, it is also unfaithful to art itself (藝術自體). Why is this? It is because, like escapist religion (逃避的 宗敎), it has been lured into a Platonic ivory tower (象牙塔) as an object of personal enjoyment (個人的 享樂對象). Art is an inner resonance (內心의 響音), one of the laws of the beautification of life (生活의 美化), expansion of life (生의 擴張), and evolution (進化法則). Herein lies the significance (意義) of art's human existence (人間的 存在) and the expansion of those who cultivate the beautification of life and the undeveloped land (未開의 地) by exposing (爆露) life's contradictions (矛盾) and conflicts (撞着). Therefore, <29> in the modern social system (現代社會制度), class art is an expression of humanity (人間性) striving towards perfection (完全) from whatever imperfection (不完全). Here, the expansion of the class self (階級自我擴張) becomes the beautification of life (生의 美化) and social critique (生活批評), and that movement is an expression of the consciousness of life (生活意識) as an act of destroying (打破行爲) the private property system (私有財産制度) within the modern struggle for existence (現代的 生存競爭). Therefore, this trend of the times (時代的 傾向) is a necessary process (必然的 過程) in a dynamic society (動的 社會), and the eternity of bourgeois (뿔쪼아) art is extinguished (復滅). Hence, this trend differs in meaning from the trends in bourgeois art theory (뿔쪼아 藝術學上의 傾向). That is, it is not a pathological movement (病的 運動) expressing a partial aspect of life (局部的 生의 表現), but a movement towards the unity of the cosmic ideal (宇宙理念의 全一) as a pure manifestation of humanity (純然한 人間性의 發露). However, in this class society (階級社會), in a society that perpetually changes (永續的으로 變轉하는 社會), can this art be perfect (完全)? It cannot be perfect. But the movement itself will naturally be faithful (忠實) and will become faithful. True art will likely be seen when these two classes disappear and a completely anarchist society (無政府社會) is formed (形成).

In traditional bourgeois art theory (在來의 뿔쪼와 藝術學上), tendentious art (傾向藝術) was analyzed into categories like ethical (倫理), church (敎會), palace (宮殿) art, etc., and its artistic value (藝術的 價値) was not considered complete. Furthermore, modern bourgeois art scholars (現代의 뿌쪼와 藝術學者) group class art, i.e., socialist art (社會主義藝術), within the traditional tendentious art category (在來傾向藝術部) and claim it lacks artistic value. However, this is a necessary outcome (必然的 過程) of bourgeois scholarship (뿔쪼와적 學究), the product (所産) of hedonistic learning (享樂的 學問). This modern trend (現代的 傾向) is the conclusion of thought (思想의 結論) based on the consciousness of life and the necessary path (必然的 進路) in the process of human social life (人間社會 生活過程). Speaking comprehensively about the trend-driven movements in literature (文藝) and plastic arts (造形美術) within current politics (現今 政治), movements such as Neo-Romanticism (新浪漫主義), Expressionism (表現藝術), Dadaism (다다主義), Metaphysical painting (形而上派), Primitivism (原始派), Cubism (立體派), Impressionism (印象派), Neoclassicism (新古典派), etc., along with some minor currents (少少한 潮流), are internationally divided (國際的으로 分割). However, if one were to insist that there is a current in Korea (朝鮮), one could say there is something mixed (混血) with the liberal consciousness (自由主義的 意識) and thought (思想) of the petit bourgeois (小뿔쪼와). Furthermore, the art movement within class life (階級生活) is currently being carried out by the hands of the nation's proletarians (全國無産者). And this movement is a movement to destroy the culture (文化) of the existing capitalist society (資本家社會) on the path to social revolution (社會革命途上), based on class consciousness (階級意識). In other words (換言하면), the proletariat (無産階級), whose human rights (人權) were trampled (蹂躪) from feudal society (封建社會) to capitalist society (資本家社會), have awakened (覺醒) to their class consciousness through self-awareness (自覺) and education (敎養). That is to say, they have come to know their position (地位). Therefore, it is natural that they should act class-consciously (階級的으로 活動), and this is a part of that activity. However, the writers (文士) of Korea's propertied class (有産階級) and propertyless class (無産階級) are dominated (支配) by the private interests (私益) of the capitalist society, which has formed the modern lifestyle (現代生活要式) through the development (發達) of bourgeois economics (뿔쪼와 經濟學) and the metaphysics (形而上學) of aristocratic life (貴族生活), maturing (成熟) and solidifying (固着) in various aspects (各方面) while trampling (蹂躪) the lives of the propertyless masses (無産民衆). Under the political oppression (政治的 壓迫) and the liberal thought of the middle class (中間階級), mixed up (混亂) with nationalist liberal thought (民族的 自由主義思想), <30> they unconsciously form their consciousness of life (生活意識), lose unity (統一), and are swayed and dominated (動搖, 支配) by the middle class's liberal thought. In this state (狀態), they have inevitably become decadent (墮落的), fin-de-siècle (世紀末), despairing (絶望), and "decadent" (떼가단的). To elaborate, it is an object of passion (熱情對象) that cannot be attained in the process of expressing agitated passion (激動되는 熱情) with infinite energy (無限한 精力). Through unconscious attachment (愛着) and faith (信望) in the whole (全體), and self-conflict (自我葛藤) and contradiction (撞着), they have become thus through self-established (自設的) cooperative actions (協同的 行爲). The "decadent" tendency (떼가단的 傾向) of the Munhwasa Faction (文化社一派) and the "cenacle-ism" tendency (째나리슴的 傾向) of the Muninhoe Faction (文人會一派), along with the middle-class ideological tendency (中間階級的 思想傾向) that tries to transcend ideology (思想的으로 超越), are the naked offspring (赤裸한 産兒) of the state of Korean society (朝鮮社會狀態).
Therefore, literature created from such thoughts (思想) and feelings (感情) has become a useless appendage (無用의 長物) of various deformed shapes (畸形的으로 形形色色). Thus, establishing the self (自我) within the consciousness of the times (時代意識) and class consciousness (階級意識) is an urgent task (急迫한 問題) for Korean writers (朝鮮文士). Furthermore, their literature lacks moral character (德義性), social relevance (社會性), and unified thought (統一한 思想), having become merely a monologue (獨白記) of romantic sorrow (浪漫的 悲哀).
Therefore, there is no beautification of life (生活美化) through art, nor social instruction (社會敎示); what exists is the so-called literary world (文壇) or writing circle (作文界), which is purely human stagnation (停滯) or degeneration (墮落).

No. 4
As mass art (民衆藝術) arises in conformity (順應) with the times (時代) and the bourgeois (와) art of individual enjoyment (個人享樂) follows a path of temporizing (姑息), what does the art of modern people (現代人) signify and where is it heading? In the carnage (修羅의 巷) of modern capitalist society (現代資本家社會) that tramples our human rights (吾人의 人權) and exploits our production (生産을 搾取), what kind of activity (活動) should our humanity (吾人의 人間性) undertake? The art of modern people is not the enjoyment of suffering (苦悶의 享樂) but the expression of suffering (苦悶의 表現). Our humanity deeply penetrates (徹) the environment (環境), and in the earnestness (眞劍한 곳) of human suffering (人間苦) received due to production relations (生産關係), there lies the expression of modern suffering (現代的 苦悶의 表現) and the true meaning (眞正한 意義) of self-development (自我發展). Why is this? Because in modern capitalist society, humans (人間) have become nothing more than machines (機械). This is because, in a life where production relations dominate almost everything about humans, the propertyless class (無産階級) without capital (資本), apart from the privileged class (特權階級), cannot obtain permission (許可) to guarantee their livelihood (自己生活 保障) without becoming wage slaves (賃銀奴隷) to the privileged class under the modern political and economic system (現代政治及 經濟組織下). Therefore, humans have become slaves to machines (機械의 奴隷), slaves to the system (組織의 奴隷), and slaves to power (權力의 奴隷). Consequently, human free will (人間의 自由意志) has been inertially extinguished (慣性的으로 消滅), and humans have come to operate like machines (機械와 가티 活動). This is precisely the proletariat (無産階級). The movement of the proletariat today (現今의 無産階級運動) is based on the class self-awareness (階級的 自覺) of these proletarian humans. Class art (階級藝術), a part of this movement, is the movement and art of these propertyless literary talents (無産文才) living such miserable lives (慘憺한 生活), <31> confronting the bourgeois class (뿔쪼와 階級) and its art (藝術), demolishing (打破) the contradictory social phenomena (矛盾의 社會現象), and striving to bathe in the light (光明) of a new human life (新人生). We can point out how human and necessary (人間的이며 必然的) this movement is, and simultaneously, how false (虛僞), unfaithful (不充實), and humanly degenerate (人間的으로 墮落) bourgeois art is. Furthermore, this proletarian (푸로레타리아) art movement resists the production discipline (生産規律) that mechanizes humans (人間을 機械化). Its ideology and emotion (思想感情), built upon the happiness (幸福) and peace (平和) of all humanity (全人類), aims to destroy the power structure (權力階級) and build a new society (新社會), using the form of art (藝術의 形式) for propaganda (宣傳) in this process. Therefore, the fact that bourgeois art clings to the status quo (現狀姑息), that is, does not realize human liberation (人間解放) from the vortex of contradiction (矛盾의 渦窟), means that humanity is trampled (人間性이 蹂躪) in bourgeois art. Accordingly, a work in which humanity is trampled is not a true work (作品). Therefore, its humanity is not complete humanity (完全한 人間性). Also, for a human who claims to possess complete humanity amidst the phenomena of miserable bloodshed (慘憺한 鮮血) and trampled human rights (人權蹂躪), they should feel the terror (恐怖) of human destruction (人間破滅). Thus, if humanity is said to be trampled in art formed upon environment and life (環境과 生活 上에 形成된 藝術), there can be no expression of art's life (藝術의 生命의 表現). Furthermore, just as there can be no life apart from humanity (人間性을 떠난 生), and art without the expression of life (生命의 表現이 업는 藝術) cannot be created (造成되지 못하는), therefore (然故로), bourgeois art is not art. And as written in the previous issue, this humanity is subject to class domination (階級의 支配), and consequently subject to social domination (社會의 支配); therefore, a life transcending class and society cannot be established even theoretically (理論上으로도 成立되지 못하는 것이다). Therefore, there can be no art transcending class (階級을 超越한 藝術), and no human transcending class (階級을 超越한 人間). Thus, the bourgeois writer's (뿔쪼와 作家) notion of class transcendence (階級超越) is a delusional concept (妄想의 概念). Hence, poets (詩人), novelists (小說家), painters (畵家), playwrights (劇作家), and composers (音樂家) who wish to possess a true self (眞正한 自我) and live a complete life (完全한 生), that is, truly think of the masses (眞正히 民衆을 생각하려는), will participate in the social movement (社會運動에 參與). And those who possess the emerging art (新興藝術) must resist capitalist society (資本家社會) and XX XX. Emerging art, that is, class art, is revolutionary art (革命의 藝術). Therefore, those engaged in the emerging art movement are people doing XXXX. And they are the possessors of modern art (現代藝術). Therefore, those who do not do XX are people of the stagnant capitalist class (停滯된 資本家階級의 人間). If one is of the capitalist class, one is an obstacle (障害者) to this emerging art. Then, these humans, corrupted by temporizing (姑息) and stagnation (停滯), must be swept away (掃除) from our society (吾人社會). Therefore, literary figures (文士諸君), you who are objects to be swept away (掃除物), come out of your ivory tower (象牙塔) and face this great terror (偉大한 公布). Then this terror will give profound life (深刻한 生命) to your consciousness of life (生活意識).
Korean literary figures (朝鮮文士諸君), what do you think of this worldwide human terror (世界的 人間恐怖)? Do you think that a harvest of 100 or 200 sok (石) guarantees your livelihood forever (永遠히 諸君의 生活을 保障), and that your literature, like a maiden's sorrow (處女의 悲哀), contemplates the glorious construction of Korea (榮華스런 朝鮮을 建設) and simultaneously the beautification of life for global humanity (世界的 人類의 生活美化)? **Summary**

This essay by Im Chŏngjae argues for the necessity of proletarian, class-conscious art in 1920s Korea, critiquing bourgeois art as decadent, detached from life, and complicit in the capitalist system that dehumanizes the masses. He dismisses traditional aesthetic values and bourgeois notions of art transcending class as illusions. Im contends that true art must arise from the suffering and revolutionary consciousness of the proletariat (무산계급), serving as a tool for social critique, propaganda, and the eventual overthrow of the capitalist order towards a classless society. He specifically criticizes contemporary Korean literary factions (Munhwasa and Muninhoe) for their perceived decadence and coterie-ism, urging writers to abandon their ivory towers, confront the "terror" of social reality, and engage in the revolutionary struggle through "emerging art" (신흥예술). Historically, this article is significant as a clear example of early Korean proletarian literary theory (KAPF would form later). It reflects the intense ideological debates among intellectuals under Japanese colonial rule, the influence of international socialist and Marxist thought, and the attempt to link artistic practice directly to social revolution and national liberation, framing class struggle as the primary lens through which art and life should be understood. The explicit call for writers to become revolutionaries and the critique of specific literary groups highlight the politicization of culture during this period.

第3
藝術은 民衆的이 아니면 안되겟다는 것과 全民衆的으로 誘引을 말하는 것은 아니라고 前號에 썻는데 그에 대하야 다소의 오해가 잇슬뜻 해서 조곰 더 자세히 써보자.
全全民衆的과 民衆的이라는 것은 全民衆的이나 民衆的은 廣狹의 차이는 잇지만 意義는 동일하다. 그러면 민중이라는 것은 국가적으로는 一民族, 국제적으로는 세계인류를 지시한다. 그럼으로 全民衆的으로 誘引이라는 것은 一國家及 全 社會의 趣味傾向에 立하는 것이 아니고 失我的으로 無階級的 민중운동에 종속하는 것을 말한 것이다. 예를 들면 고대에 잇서 道德藝術 혹은 宗敎藝術運動 등이다. 그럼으로 誘引된 것은 無個性한 것이오, 失我的인 것은 無生命的이 되는 것 가튼 것이 階級藝術이 안이란 말이다.
이 宗敎藝術 즉 敎會藝術, 道德藝術 즉 倫理藝術 가튼 生活自我의 姑息的 狀態는, 활동적 인간생활에서는 實在感 즉 生活要式의 이상화와 生活超越이란 철학적 인생관으로 기인하는 것이며 현대적 생활의식(생활적 인간생활)에선 人間破滅이다. 그러고 현대의식에 立한 衝動生活이나 理, 智生活에 在하야 動的自我는 方便的이 아니고 必然的 생명의 과정으로 流動的 生活을 갓는 것이다. 그럼으로 고정적 기존의 예술은 현대의식상으로부터 停滯와 姑息과 自滅의 기록이다. 여차히 뿔쪼와 예술은 인간적으로 수락되엿슬 뿐 외에 예술자체를 위해서도 불충실한 것이다. 웨그러냐하면 개인적 향락대상으로 도피적 종교와 如히 푸라톤닉흐한 象牙塔에 誘引된 까닭이다. 예술은 내심의 響音으로 생활의 美化와 生의 擴張과 진화법칙에 하나이다. 여긔에 예술이 인간적으로 존재의 意義와 생활의 矛盾과 撞着을 爆露하야 생활의 美化와 未開의 地를 경작하는 者의 확장이 잇는 것이다. 그럼으로＜29＞ 현대사회제도에서 계급예술은 如何한 不完全에서 完全을 향하는 인간성의 표현이다. 여긔에 階級自我擴張은 生의 美化와 生活批評이 되는 것이며 그 운동은 현대적 생존경쟁에 在한 사유재산제도의 打破行爲로 生活意識의 표현이다. 그럼으로 이 시대적 경향은 動的 社會에 在하야 必然的 과정이며 뿔쪼와 예술의 영원성이 復滅되는 것이다. 故로 이 경향은 뿔쪼아 예술학상의 경향과는 의미가 다르다. 즉 局部的 生의 표현인 病的 運動이 아니고 純然한 인간성의 發露로 宇宙理念의 全一에 향하는 운동이다. 그러나 이 계급사회에서 永續的으로 變轉하는 사회에 이 예술이 완전하냐하면 완전하지 못하다. 그러나 그 운동자체는 물론 충실할 것읊�며 충실할 것이다. 진정한 예술은 이 양 계급이 소멸되고 완전한 無政府社會를 형성할 때에 볼 수가 잇슬 것이다.
在來의 뿔쪼와 예술학상으로는 傾向藝術을 倫理, 敎會, 宮殿, 藝術등으로 분석하고 예술적 가치를 완전하게 보지 아니하엿다. 또한 현대의 뿌쪼와 예술학자들은 계급예술 즉 사회주의예술을 在來傾向藝術部 내에 합해가지고 예술적 가치가 업다고 한다. 그러나 이것은 뿔쪼와적 學究의 필연적 과정으로 향락적 학문의 소산이고 이 현대적 경향이라는 것은 생활의식에 在한 사상의 결론과 인간사회 생활과정에서 필연적 진로이다. 現今 정치에 在한 경향운동으로 文藝와 造形美術 방면을 총괄해서 말하면 新浪漫主義 表現藝術, 다다主義, 形而上派, 原始派, 立體派, 印象派, 新古典派, 等外에 少少한 조류가 잇겟스나 대표적 경향으로는 如此한 운동이 국제적으로 분할하고 잇다. 그런데 朝鮮의 조류가 잇다고 앨써 말하면 小뿔쪼와의 자유주의적 의식과 사상이 混血된 것이 잇겟다고 하겟다. 또한 계급생활에 예술운동은 現今 전국무산자의 손으로 운동하고 잇다. 그러고 이 운동이라는 것은 계급의식에 在하야 社會革命途上에서 기존의 자본가사회의 문화를 파괴하는 운동이다. 換言하면 封建社會로부터 자본가사회에 至하기까지 人權蹂躪을 당하든 무산계급이 自覺과 敎養으로 자기들의 계급의식에 覺醒하엿다는 것이다. 즉 자기의 지위를 알게 되엿단 말이다. 그럼으로 당연히 彼等은 계급적으로 활동될 것이며 활동해지는 일부의 운동이다. 그러나 朝鮮의 유산계급의 文士나 무산계급의 文士는 뿔쪼와 경제학의 발달과, 귀족생활의 形以上學 발달로 現代生活要式을 조성하고 각 방면으로 成熟하고 固着하며 무산민중의 생활을 蹂躝하는 자본가사회의 私益으로 지배를 당하며 중간계급의 자유주의적 사상과 정치적 壓迫으로 민족적 슞�유주의사상과 혼란하야＜30＞ 생활의식을 無意識間에 형성하고 통일을 失하는 동시에 중간계급의 자유주의적 사상에 動搖, 支配되는 상태에 在하야 필연적으로 墮落的, 世紀末 絶望의 떼가단的으로 되엿다. 자세히 말하면 激動되는 열정은 무한한 정력으로 표현되는 과정에서 구하지 못하는 熱情對象이다. 무의식적 전턀µ의 愛着과 信望과 自我葛藤과 撞着으로 自設的 協同的 행위에서 如此히 되엿다. 文化社一派의 떼가단的 경향과, 文人會一派의 째나리슴的 경향과, 사상적으로 초월하란¤는 중간계급적 사상경향은 조선사회상태의 赤裸한 産兒이다.
그럼으로 이러한 사상과 감정으로 된 문예라는 것은 畸形的으로 형형색색의 無用의 長物이 되엿다. 함으로 시대의식과 계급의식에 자아를 확립식힐 것은 朝鮮文士의 급박한 문제이다. 또한 그 문예에 德義性도 업스며 社會性도 업스며 통일한 사상도 업고 다만 浪漫的 悲哀의 獨白記가 되엿다.
