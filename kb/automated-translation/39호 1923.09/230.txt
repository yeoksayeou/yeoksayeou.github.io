Magazine Title	개벽 제39호
Publication Date	1923년 09월 01일
Article Title	悲曲 棠棣花 (Tragic Song: Tangdihwa Flower)
Author	郭洙若 (Guo Moruo - Chinese Poet)
Article Type	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Tragic Song: Tangdihwa Flower

Characters
Nyŏp Chŏng 20 years old
Nyŏp Yŏng 22 years old (his older sister)
Background
Looking around, half the fields are desolate, though occasionally there are green shoots of barley. Low mountains encircle the scene from afar. The trailing edge of the mountain range runs down, forming a rather high hill towards the left edge of the field. On it stands a white poplar, and in the poplar tree, black magpies are chattering. Beneath the tree is a single grave mound, and to its right stands a stele inscribed "Grave of Mother Nyŏp." To the right, a narrow side path slopes down from the distance and reaches the gravesite.
(Nyŏp Yŏng carries a large peach blossom branch slung over her shoulder, while Nyŏp Chŏng, dressed for travel with a sword at his side, carries a bamboo basket. They ascend via the side path.)

Nyŏp Chŏng. (Pointing) Sister, look! It's become so desolate here! Right?
Nyŏp Yŏng. (Sighing) Ah—this year we hope for peace next year, and next year we hope for a good harvest the year after. Having done this for nearly 10 years, the scene before our eyes has merely become a world where only magpies chirp and weeds grow rampant. (Pointing) Hey, listen to that. The unpleasant sound of crows cawing from atop that poplar tree seems to mock how our human destiny is like a single reed!
Nyŏp Chŏng. Making human entrails into mere food to fill the bellies of tiny birds, and human blood into mere water for tiny weeds to drink—you weeds! You birds! How long will you revel like this?＜146＞
Nyŏp Yŏng. (Pointing) Look there. Isn't that Mother's stele? It's already been three years since Mother passed away, yet the only thing that dies and comes back to life is this overgrown grass. The one who left forever and does not return is the慈母 (chamo - loving mother) upon whom we mutually relied. How we have thirsted for the spring water of life these past few years!
Nyŏp Chŏng. War doesn't cease, so the spring water of life dries up day by day. Over these past few years, one day they talk of vertical alliances (合縱), the next day of horizontal alliances (連衡); one day they attack the state of Yŏn (燕), the next day they attack the state of Ch'o (楚). Those who fight over cities kill people until they fill the cities, and those who fight over land kill people until they cover the fields. I don't know what they ultimately aim to achieve by doing this. Recently, there are people who propose disarmament (弭兵) or loudly advocate non-aggression (非戰), but the one calling out calls out alone, while those who fight continue to fight as usual, so eventually even those calling out end up fighting amongst themselves.
Nyŏp Yŏng. Since Ha U (夏禹) passed the throne to his son, the world became the ruler's house, and the well-field system (井田制度) was abolished, making land privately owned. From that time, the seeds of eternal conflict and fighting were already sown. The foundation was flawed like that, so just making noise about peripheral matters—what good will it do?
(At this time, the round moon rises over the distant mountains, and the sister and brother have unknowingly entered the graveyard. Nyŏp Chŏng places the bamboo basket before the grave, draws his sword, cuts a branch from the poplar, and sweeps around the grave. Nyŏp Yŏng takes down the peach blossom branch, sticks parts of it to the left and right of the stele, then takes out wine and food from the basket and lays them out. From under the basket, she takes out a single Dongxiao (洞簫 - vertical bamboo flute).)
Nyŏp Yŏng. Goodness, you brought the Dongxiao?
Nyŏp Chŏng. Yes. I haven't been able to play it for three years, so I brought it tonight to play once before Mother's grave.
Nyŏp Yŏng. Good, I too wanted to hear your Dongxiao.
(After laying everything out, Nyŏp Yŏng bows before the grave and kneels. Nyŏp Chŏng also comes, bows, and kneels. After bowing, Nyŏp Yŏng leans against a white poplar tree near the grave, and Nyŏp Chŏng takes the Dongxiao and sits on the grass before the grave.)
Nyŏp Chŏng. Sister, the moon has risen, and the flock of crows is silent. The wide, wide world is so clear and tranquil!＜142＞
Nyŏp Yŏng. Hey, listen. Just like that, a deep and sorrowful sound seems to flow between heaven and earth. Quick, play your Dongxiao to match my voice. My voice comes out together with tears! (Sings. Nyŏp Chŏng accompanies on the Dongxiao.)

Three years already since Mother left us; Mother departed and does not return.
We two, sister and brother, wish only to follow Mother.

Breaking two branches of spring-blooming peach blossoms, we planted them apart beside Mother's grave.
When the flowers on those peach branches wither, where will this sister and brother be?

We do not wish to live long; fervent death is our only utmost desire.
Let us take this one life and try to save all the common people (蒼生)!

The common people have long been in misery (塗炭); not even one in ten households is whole.
They suffer the terrible bitterness of slaughter (屠戮), and again there is the worry of famine (飢饉).

The fault for famine lies not with heaven; the great sin of slaughter lies with humans.
In the houses of the rich, grain and meat remain; in the hands of the strong, private soldiers fight.

I will make the poor and rich equal; I will eliminate the powerful (强權).
Even if I become a plague-spreading demon (施瘟使鬼神), let's eliminate all those harmful groups (害群)!
Nyŏp Chŏng. Sister, sister, your voice carries much of a man's tone. If Mother were alive and heard this, <148> she would surely scold you.
Nyŏp Yŏng. When Mother was alive, she always wished for us to enjoy the true happiness of life. But now I think, all the sisters and brothers in the world are currently trapped in water and fire (水火中). Even if we sacrifice this small body, if we can just save them, that truly is unsurpassed happiness. That is why I am sending you to Pokyang (濮陽) tonight. I know that many sacrifices lie ahead on that path. But I send you off gladly. I believe that without sacrifice, love cannot be seen, and without love, happiness cannot be known!
Nyŏp Chŏng. (Plays the Dongxiao) Sister, sing again!
Nyŏp Yŏng. (Sings)
Why does the bright moon (明月) shine so brilliantly (皎皎)? The sound of the white poplar rustles (蕭蕭).
The parting of us two, sister and brother, occurs tonight.

After parting tonight, a promise to meet again cannot be made.
Look once more into this sister's eyes; listen once more to this sister's song.

Nyŏp Chŏng. (Wiping tears) Sister, why are you so sad?
Nyŏp Yŏng. (Continues singing, doesn't answer)
Vast tears (汪汪) became a lake, reflecting the four-wheeled bright moon.
Where have those traces gone now? The moon's disk never disappears!

Nyŏp Chŏng. (As before) Sister, the night has grown late. Let's go now. <149>
Nyŏp Yŏng. (As before)
Sister wishes to become that moon, shining upon her brother with a gentle light.
Where is sister's home? Where will she go as she journeys forth?
Nyŏp Chŏng. (Stands up) Sister, because you are so sad, my fire-like heroic spirit (雄心) has completely turned cold as ice. I will not go! (Sheds tears)
Nyŏp Yŏng. Hey, brother! That doesn't sound like what I've been saying! My sadness is not because I find it truly hard to part with you, but merely lamenting that this body of mine is not male... Brother, I won't be sad anymore. You too, stop shedding tears! Our tears are definitely not meant to be shed at this time. If tomorrow, on your journey, you happen to meet displaced refugees (流離하는 避亂民) or see ownerless corpses fallen in the fields, shed many tears in my stead! We destitute people lack money and food, so we cannot save our compatriots, but what we do have is just life and tears... Brother, I won't stay long. You must leave quickly! Do not betray your magnanimous spirit (磊落한 心懷). Do not betray this sister's earnest hope (囑望). Do not betray the common people of the world (天下蒼生). Do not betray the patronage (知遇) of Master Ŏm (嚴子). You must leave quickly! I will sing again to brighten your departing path. (Sings)

Go! Hey, brother! I hope that with your red blood,
You will make the flower of freedom bloom, filling all of China (中華)!
Hey, brother! Go!
(At this moment, the moon is suddenly enveloped by a patch of cloud, and the entire stage (舞空 - lit. dance space) becomes pitch black. Only the lingering sound of the song can be heard.)
-Curtain-
Note: Nyŏp Chŏng (聶政) was a man from Chi (軹) during the Warring States period. Ŏm Chungja (嚴仲子) had a grudge against the Han minister Hyŏp Ru (俠累)* and commissioned Nyŏp Chŏng to assassinate Hyŏp Ru. Nyŏp Chŏng declined because his mother was still alive. After his mother died, he assassinated Hyŏp Ru for Chungja and then killed himself.
Original Author: Mr. Kwak Su-yak (郭洙若氏 - Guo Moruo), young Chinese poet. <150>
(*The text has 百, likely a typo for 仇 meaning grudge/enmity: 與韓相俠累有仇)
<146-150>

**Summary**

Set against a backdrop of war-torn fields in ancient China, the play "Tragic Song: Tangdihwa Flower" depicts a poignant farewell between a sister, Nyŏp Yŏng, and her younger brother, Nyŏp Chŏng, at their mother's grave. Nyŏp Yŏng, lamenting the endless conflict and suffering caused by warring factions and social inequality, urges her brother to embark on a dangerous mission (implied assassination, referencing the historical Nyŏp Chŏng) to Pokyang for the greater good, framing it as a necessary sacrifice for love and happiness. Despite her sorrow and his hesitation, she steels his resolve with songs calling for him to use his life to bring freedom to the suffering masses, ending as he departs into the darkness. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this play, written by a prominent Chinese leftist poet (Guo Moruo) and published in a Korean magazine in 1923, is significant. Its themes of suffering under conflict, critique of power struggles, call for heroic sacrifice for the 'common people' (蒼生), and yearning for liberation, though set in ancient China, could readily be interpreted by contemporary Korean readers as reflecting their own subjugated reality under Japanese rule and a veiled call for resistance or revolutionary action.

바라보면 밧이 반은 荒廢하고 간혹 靑靑한 麥秀가 잇다. 멀리로 나즌 산어 둘럿으며 그 산맥의 餘勢가 달아 나려와 왼편 밧 가으로 놉즉하게 언덕을 이루엇고 그 우에 白楊이 잇으며白楊나무 우에는 검은 까치들이 지절거리고 잇다. 나무 미테는 墓가 한 座 잇고 우측으로 향하야 「聶母之墓」라 한 碑가 서잇다. 오른편으로 한갈래 족음아한 지럼길이 멀리로부터 비스듬이 나려와서 묘지에 다앗다.
(聶嫈은 한 큰 桃花가지를 둘러메고 聶政은 旅裝에 칼을 차고 손에 한 개 竹籃을 들고 지럼길로서 올라온다)

어머님 여인지 어느덧 3年에  어머닌 가시고 다시 안오시네
이 우리 두 사람의 누의와 동생은 어머님 딸아서 오기가 願야오

저 복송아가지에 핀 꼿이 질 때에 이 누의와 동생은 어대가 잇슬가?

오래게 살기도 원하지를 안코 열렬히 죽기만 다만 至願이라
원컨대 이 한나의 목숨을 가지고 저 모든 蒼生을 救濟를 해보자!

蒼生은 塗炭에 든지가 오래라 열집에 하낫도 완전치 안흐에
屠戮의 慘苦도 맛나 쓰려니와 飢饉의 걱정도 또다시 잇구나

飢饉의 허물은 한울에는 업고 屠戮의 큰죄는 사람에게 잇네

聶嫈. 어머님께서 살아게실 때에는 每樣 우리들로 인생의 진정한 행복을 누리기를 바라섯지마는 내 이제 생각하니 지금 천하의 자매형제들이 모도다 水火中에 빠저 잇스니 설사 이 조고만 몸 하나를 犧牲을 할지라도 우리들이 그들을 구해만 내고 보면 참으로 이것이 무상한 행복이다. 그러기에 너를 오늘밤에 濮陽에 보낸다. 나도 그 前途에는 만흔 犧牲이 잇슬 줄을 안다. 그러치만 나는 너를 질거히 떠나 보낸다. 내 생각하니 犧牲이 업스면 사랑을 볼 수 업는 것이요 사랑이 업스면 행복을 알지 못하는 것이다!

聶政. (일어선다) 누님, 누님이 그러케 슯허하시니 제의 불 가튼 雄心이 고만 어름 가티 식습니다. 나는 가지 안케습니다! (눈물을 뿌린다)
聶嫈. 이애 동생아 그게 내 하는 말갓지 아니하다! 내가 이러케 슯허하는 것은 무슨 참아 너와 작별하기가 어려워 그러는 것이 아니라 다만 이내 몸이 남자 안님을 한하는 것이다... 동생아 내 인제 슯허 아니하마. 너도 눈물을 흘리지 말어라! 우리들의 눈물은 결코 이 때에 흘릴 것이 아니다. 네가 내일 아츰 도중에서 或 流離하는 避亂民을 맛나거나 들에 넘어진 주인 업는 송장을 맛보거든 나를 대신하야 눈물을 만히 뿌려다우! 우리 구차한 사람은 金錢과 糧食이 업서 동포를 救濟치는 못한다마는 잇는 것은 단지 생명과 눈물뿐이다... 동생아 나는 오래 잇지를 안흐랸다. 너 어서 떠나거라! 네 磊落한 心懷를 辜負하지 말허라. 이 누의의 囑望을 저바리지 말허라. 天下蒼生을 저바리지 말허라. 嚴子의 知遇를 辜負하지 말어라. 너 어서 떠나거라! 내 또 노래불러 너 떠나는 길을 하게하야주마.
