Article Title: 내가 본 平北의 各郡, 龍川-鐵山-宣川-定州-龜城-雲山-寧邊-博川 (The Counties of North P'yŏngan I Saw: Yongch'ŏn-Ch'ŏlsan-Sŏnch'ŏn-Chŏngju-Kusŏng-Unsan-Nyŏngbyŏn-Pakch'ŏn)
Author: 一記者 (One Reporter)

What I Saw in Each County of North P'yŏngan: Yongch'ŏn-Ch'ŏlsan-Sŏnch'ŏn-Chŏngju-Kusŏng-Unsan-Nyŏngbyŏn-Pakch'ŏn
						By One Reporter

Yongch'ŏn
This county is a district located at the southwestern tip of North P'yŏngan Province. To the east, it borders Ch'ŏlsan; to the north, Ŭiju; to the west, it faces China across the Yalu River; and to the south, it borders the West Korea Bay. The area of this county is about 36 *pangni* (square ri); it has few high mountains, and although there are rolling hills, the land is generally flat and fertile. Irrigation benefits and conveniences for water and land transport are many, and marine products are not scarce. The household count is 15,504 Korean, 203 Japanese, 109 Chinese, totaling 15,816 households. The population is 90,769 Koreans, 653 Japanese, 514 Chinese, totaling 91,936 people.
This county has 12 townships (*myŏn*) and 155 villages (*tong*). Educational institutions include 3 general private schools, 5 religious private schools, 4 public common schools (*kongbohakkyo*), 1 private common school, and 1 public elementary school for Japanese, totaling 14 schools. As for famous sites and historical relics in this district, there is nothing particularly noteworthy, except for structures from the Koryŏ period inscribed with Sanskrit characters called Tae Pultchŏng Tarani T'ang (大佛頂陀羅尼幢 - Great Buddha Head Dharani Pillars) to the east and west of the county seat. The county office is located at the port called Yongamp'o. This port was originally named Yongamp'o because Yongam Mountain stands by the coast.
Regarding the origins of this port, it was originally an incomparably quiet fishing harbor. Twenty-three years ago, while the Russians were managing southern and northern Manchuria in China, they were also eyeing Korea. Internally, <77> they harbored intentions of annexation, while formally, they claimed to be managing the logging of the limitless timber upstream on the Yalu River, establishing a Forestry Company (森林會社) in Yongamp'o, at the mouth of the Yalu, in the year Kyemyo (1903). From then on, the Russians, formally managing the Forestry Company, internally engaged in purely military activities. The company buildings they constructed were entirely barracks and armories; the company clerks were all military officers; and on the summit of Yongam Mountain, the highest point in Yongamp'o, they built gun emplacements. Thus, the Russo-Japanese War of Kapchin (1904) and Ŭlsa (1905) ultimately had its fuse lit by these events here.
Even now, about 18 sturdy brick buildings constructed by the Russians remain intact.

The Two Exemplary Projects of Mr. Kim Kyŏnghwe of Yongch'ŏn
First is the Samgyoch'ŏn Water Utilization Cooperative (水利組合) in Yangha-myŏn of this county. Originally, Messrs. Kim Kyŏnghwe and Yi T'aehyŏn initiated the project as a private enterprise, enduring immense hardship. In Taishō 6 (1917), a landowners' cooperative was finally established, taking over the management for joint operation. As a result, now:
Total benefited area is 801 *chŏngbo* (hectares).
Total expenses, before and after, amount to 708,000 won.
Pumping machinery consists of 3 units totaling 155 horsepower.
Annual total harvest yield is approximately 30,000 *sŏk* (bags of grain).
Thus, a highly successful Water Utilization Cooperative was established. Although all 500 landowners contributed, the one who sacrificed the most effort from the beginning was Mr. Kim Kyŏnghwe, followed by Messrs. Yi Ŭnggi and Tōshima Yōsaku (東島要作), who have served as managing directors since its inception. Second is the Cooperative Hospital (組合醫院) in Yangsi. Messrs. Kim Kyŏnghwe and Kim Yonggi, along with several other volunteers, had regretted the lack of a proper hospital in the region for many years. Several years ago, through the initiative of the two Kims, a fund of 30,000 won was raised from about 700 cooperative members. With a total capital of 30,000 won, the Cooperative Hospital began operations:
15,000 won was used for preparing the land and building.
10,000 won was set aside as a reserve fund.
5,000 won was used to prepare various machinery and medicines.
From the examination room to the pharmacy and inpatient rooms, all facilities are sufficient, making it indeed a hospital established by the people, unprecedented in all of Korea. This too was achieved through the great efforts of the cooperative head, Mr. Kim Kyŏnghwe.

Ch'ŏlsan
Ch'ŏlsan is famous not only for producing many clever figures since ancient times <78> but also for its people generally having sharp personalities even today. How large is this area? Its area is about 29 *pangni*; it is 6 *ri* east to west and 12 *ri* north to south. To the east, it borders Sŏnch'ŏn County; to the north, Yongch'ŏn County; and to the southwest, it faces the Yellow Sea.
What is the household count? 10,011 Korean, 65 Japanese, 18 foreign, totaling 10,094 households. The population is 56,936 Koreans, 255 Japanese, 65 foreigners, totaling 57,173 people.
The administrative division is into eight townships (*myŏn*) and one hundred and three villages (*tong*).
What about religion? There are 2,114 Ch'ŏndogyo followers and 1,844 Christians. What about education? There are 2 public common schools, 3 private schools, 1 Japanese public elementary school, and 115 *sŏdang* (traditional village schools), totaling 121 institutions.
What are the famous sites and historical relics in this district? There is nothing particularly noteworthy to record, but regarding historical figures, there are Mr. Chŏng Sawon, who rendered distinguished service during the Imjin War (Japanese invasions of 1592-1598); Mr. Chŏng Kinam, who fought bravely and died in the Imjin War and the Battle of Sarhū (深河, now Tumen River area?) in Musul year (1619? More likely Muo year/1618, leading to Sarhū 1619); Mr. Chŏng Sunnam (younger brother of Kinam), who possessed both loyalty and courage; Duke Yangmu Chŏng Pongsu, who achieved outstanding military merit (fighting Manchu forces five times and winning five times at Yonggol Mountain Fortress) during the Imjin and Chŏngmyo/Pyŏngja Invasions (丁卵役); his younger brother Chŏng Kisu, who had similar merits; and Mr. Kim Kwanggwi, who became a righteous army leader in the caves of Unam Mountain during the Chŏngmyo/Pyŏngja Invasion and achieved repeated military success. These loyal and righteous figures existed.

Miscellaneous Talk about Ch'ŏlsan
The cleverness and economic sense of Ch'ŏlsan people. There's a saying that people from the town (*ŭp*) don't like to associate with Ch'ŏlsan people because they "only look at their own feet." Also, it is said that a Ch'ŏlsan person saw a sparrow carrying away a single grain of millet (栗), and even before breakfast on a winter solstice or mid-winter day, they would run barefoot for 30 *ri* to retrieve that grain before eating their morning meal.

Sŏnch'ŏn

The old names for this county were Anhwa and T'ongju. Located in the southwestern part of North P'yŏngan Province, it borders Chŏngju County to the east across the Tongnae River, Ch'ŏlsan County to the west, faces Sŏnch'ŏn Bay to the south across islands like Sinmi Island, facing the peninsula part of Ch'ŏlsan County, and borders Kusŏng County to the north along the high ridge of T'akki Mountain. It is 9 *ri* east to west, 13 *ri* north to south, with an area of 43 *pangni*. The county seat is located about 7 *chŏng* (approx. 760m) northwest of Sŏnch'ŏn Station, overlooking the town below, offering a rather pleasant scenery.
The administrative divisions consist of 9 townships (*myŏn*) and 61 villages (*tong*).
Education includes 4 public common schools, 1 private school of middle school level, 16 private common schools, 1 public vocational school (簡易機業學校), 1 Japanese public elementary school <79>, and 91 *sŏdang*.
What is the household count? 11,675 Korean, 159 Japanese, 30 foreign, totaling 11,864 households. The population is 66,925 Koreans, 494 Japanese, 88 foreigners, totaling 67,305 people.
What is the religious situation? In Korea, regarding the prevalence of Christianity, Sŏnch'ŏn ranks first, Chaeryŏng second, P'yŏngyang third, and Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) fourth. This is, of course, relative to the population of each place. In the case of Sŏnch'ŏn and P'yŏngyang, Christianity was introduced very early. Thus, mentioning Sŏnch'ŏn naturally brings Christianity to mind. Not only Christianity, but Ch'ŏndogyo is also prosperous, ranking among the top few in all of Korea. This shows the particularly rich religious nature of the people of Sŏnch'ŏn. According to a survey at the end of Taishō 10 (1921), the number of believers was 7,800 Christians, 6,220 Ch'ŏndogyo followers, 510 Buddhists, and 30 Sich'ŏn'gyo followers.
What are the famous sites? The Tongnim Waterfall in Simch'ŏn-myŏn of this county is one of Sŏnch'ŏn's famous sites, with a direct drop of 3 *chang* (about 9 meters), making it an excellent place to escape the summer heat.
As for historical relics, there are the two fortresses of Tongnim Mountain and Kŏm Mountain, the Monument to the Righteous Martyrs of Simha (深河義士殉節碑) near the Hyanggyo (local Confucian school) northwest of the county seat, the Monument commemorating the deeds of Kim Ŭngha, the Earl of Liaodong (遼東伯), the Monument Praising the Relief Efforts and Integrity of Im Kyŏngŏp (林慶業恤兵淸簡碑), General Im's Cave (林將軍窟) on Unjong Mountain on Sinmi Island, and the remains of the place where Mao Wenlong (毛文龍), the Commander of Henan (河南) in the Ming Dynasty, stayed.
The inscription on the Monument to the Righteous Martyrs of Simha reads as follows:
Deep River traces, spirits not forgotten.
Their stern resolve, on a single blue stone.
Past and present, one sorrow. Unattained, their time.
Their heroic forms, eternally departed.
On the back of this monument, the names of the martyred righteous soldiers are: Ch'a Chaejung, O Pongch'un, Kim Ŭngsang, Pak Kunsan, Kim Ŭiryong, Kim Inryong, Kye Yŏgang, Kim Taeryŏng, Ch'a Yŏngjung, Mun Sagil, Ok Kyeram, Kim T'aeson, Kim Taehun, Ch'oe Iryong, Ok T'aeram, Chi T'aegyŏng, <80> Ch'a Kyŏngryang, Kang Illip, O Ŭngdu, Ch'a Imjung, Yu Angyŏng, Ch'a Yejung, Yu Tŏkkyŏng, Yi Ch'ŏlgi, Chŏn Kŏmch'ung, Kim Yŏrim, Yi Kŭkkŭn, Ch'oe Hŭiŭi, Ok Kyŏngbaek, Kim Ch'ŏrhyŏn, Kye Sail, Pak Hoch'ung, Pak Kyŏngrin, Ch'a Ch'ijung, Yi Talbin, Kang Tŭngnyong, totaling 36 individuals.

The Only Afforestation Cooperative in Northern Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River Region (淸北唯一)

Taemok Mountain, which surrounds the southeast part of Ŭmnae-myŏn in Sŏnch'ŏn County, is a long, reclining mountain with an altitude of 1,250 *ch'ŏk* (feet) above sea level. This mountain is originally state-owned land. Through the initiative of four individuals, Yang Sŏngha, Yi Sihang, Kim Hŭich'ok, and Ch'oe Kyŏnggŭk, and the cooperation of 71 others, permission for a loan was obtained on March 8, Yunghŭi 3 (1909), with the purpose of afforesting the mountain. Planting began in Yunghŭi 5 (1911) on an area of 223 *chŏng* 9 *myo* 19 *po*. Now, the results are very good, and it has become a renowned afforestation project in North P'yŏngan.

Sŏnch'ŏn's Nursing Home (養老院)
In our Korea, the term "Nursing Home" (養老院) itself is rarely heard. Mr. Yi Ch'angsŏk, residing in Ŭmnae-myŏn of this district, was well-known in his youth for heavy drinking and gambling. However, even from his youth, there was something commendable in his character. Although his family was impoverished and he made a living through gambling, there were three principles he thought about day and night and never forgot. First, that there is nothing a person cannot achieve through effort. Second, that a person must do what needs to be done and not do what should not be done. Third, that he too would try to live like others someday. Living year after year with these three principles and striving hard, his character, inherently sincere, led him to become one of the wealthiest men in Sŏnch'ŏn. Having believed in Jesus for many years and already attained the position of elder, he felt he had received many blessings from God (한울님) but had nothing with which to give glory back to Him. Thus, in Taishō 7 (1918), he donated all his owned fields and paddies, worth about 300,000 won (at the time), to the church. Starting that year, the income from these lands has been used to operate the Nursing Home. Currently, the elderly residents include 4 men and 15 women, all over 70 years old.

Chŏngju
This county originally had the names Kuju during the Koryŏ Dynasty, and later Chŏngwŏn, Och'ŏn, Chŏngju, and Chŏngwŏn. In Taishō 2 (1913), Kwaksan County was merged into it, making it a district with a total of 19 townships (*myŏn*) and 86 villages (*tong*).
To the east, it borders Pakch'ŏn County and T'aech'ŏn County across the Kama River, a tributary of the Taeryŏng River. To the west, it borders Sŏnch'ŏn County with the Tongse River as the boundary. To the south, it faces the Yellow Sea, and a part of it flanks the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River, facing P'yŏngnam Anju County from afar. To the north, it connects <81> to Kusŏng County via the peaks of Kohyŏn Pass, T'aejo Peak, and the summit of Majŏn Pass.
What is the household count of this district?
19,779 Korean, 373 Japanese, 34 foreign, totaling 20,186 households.
The population is 109,958 Koreans, 1,189 Japanese, 121 foreigners, totaling 111,268 people.
What is the educational situation?
For private schools of middle school level, there is the Osan School, the only one in North P'yŏngan. Next, there are 6 public common schools, 10 general private schools, 5 religious private schools, 3 Japanese public elementary schools, 3 short courses (*kangsŭpso*), and 211 *sŏdang*.
The religious situation is: 2,587 Ch'ŏndogyo believers, 2,485 Christian believers, 46 Sich'ŏn'gyo believers, and 26 Ch'ŏngnimgyo believers.
What historical relics does Chŏngju possess?
The oldest is the site of the Koju Great Wall (古州長成). It is a fortress built during the Koguryŏ period to defend against invasions by the Malgal (Mohe).
Starting from Aip'o-myŏn in Chŏngju County, it extends to Sŏrhan Pass in Kanggye County, with a length of about 1,700 *ri* (Korean *ri*).

Chubipchŏng Pavilion (駐蹕亭) and the Monument of Royal Sojourn (聖蹟碑)
(The building housing the Monument of Royal Sojourn is called Chubipchŏng Pavilion)
Located in Ŭpbu-myŏn. In the 3rd year of King Kongmin (reigned 1351-1374), the 31st king of Koryŏ (about 540 years ago), a person named Ch'oe Mansaeng plotted treason and attempted to overthrow the dynasty by colluding with the Yuan Dynasty. Yi Sŏnggye (later Yi T'aejo) led troops, arrived, and defeated Ch'oe at Nakt'an(? 㺚川 - possibly typo for 薩水 Salsu River or other location). Later, during the reign of King Sŏnjo, a monument was erected to praise this great deed.

Chubipkak Pavilion (駐蹕閣) and the Monument Commemorating Two Royal Sojourns (兩聖紀蹟碑)
(The building housing the Monument Commemorating Two Royal Sojourns is called Chubipkak Pavilion)
Located in Ŭpbu-myŏn. During the Imjin War, King Sŏnjo (reigned 1567-1608), the 15th king of the Yi Dynasty (about 300 years ago), fled and stayed at the house of T'ak Ryong in the county seat before moving on to Ŭiju. Later, a pavilion and monument were erected here.

Chŏngju County and General Hong Kyŏngnae
The Hong Kyŏngnae Rebellion in Kasan and Chŏngju is likely known even to young children among Koreans. However, when considering or recording all aspects of Chŏngju County, one cannot avoid discussing the modern historical event of Hong Kyŏngnae's political revolutionary rebellion.
Hong Kyŏngnae was originally from Yonggang, South P'yŏngan. From childhood, he loved reading, mastering the Four Books and Three Classics, as well as the works of various philosophers. His talent surpassed others, his martial valor was exceptional, his wisdom outstanding, and he excelled in archery and horsemanship, possessing ambitions of appearing and disappearing like a phantom. He even mastered arts like *kimun tun'gap* (奇門遁甲 - esoteric divination/strategy). He often lamented to himself, <82> "When a man is born into the world, he should save the common people drowning in misery and accomplish a great deed. How can one waste a lifetime merely as a 'rice worm' (밥버레)? Looking upon the state of the world, there are too many things to lament and cry over in anguish. To remain silent upon seeing wrongdoing or fail to act upon seeing what is right is truly the act of a dead or rotten person. How could it be the act of a living person? Currently, while the entire politics of Chosŏn is corrupt, what is even more ridiculous is the discrimination between regions within the same land, and discrimination between peoples within the same nation, dividing North and South, noble and common. No matter how great a figure emerges from the Northwest (Sŏbuk), in civil service, they cannot advance beyond *Chipyŏng* or *Changnyŏng*, and in military service, no further than *Ch'ŏmsa* or *Manho*. People from the South, after passing the civil and military examinations, could barely reach the Jade Hall (玉堂 - Hongmungwan) or Sŏnjŏn'gwan (宣傳官 - royal messenger). Not only that, but all other aspects were similarly corrupt." Gritting his teeth to correct all political corruption, he pounded his desk with clenched fists, shook out his sleeves, and left home. First, he traveled through the counties north of the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River (淸北列邑), seeking comrades. He visited *sŏdang* (village schools), reciting texts, or worked as a laborer, gaining many comrades everywhere. Then, for several years, he traveled around various places, selecting a location for the base of the revolution. As a result, he chose Tabok-tong in Kasan (now in Pakch'ŏn region), which had convenient land and water transportation, was centrally located north of the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River, yet remained secluded. He established his strategic headquarters there. On one hand, he sent people out in all directions to attract followers through various means, gather food supplies, and procure all sorts of iron materials for weapon manufacturing. On the other hand, he built a fortress on the mountain, manufactured gunpowder and weapons inside it, and also prepared a training ground on the summit of Wŏnsu Peak (元帥峯), drilling his soldiers. He seized the great famine of Sinmi year (1811) as a unique opportunity, recruiting vast numbers of famine-stricken people, who flocked to him like clouds.
On the night of the 18th day of the 12th month of Sinmi year (Jan 1812), at the third watch, General Hong finally rose up from Tabok-tong, drawing and shouldering his three-foot sword, shouting loudly. He quickly captured the eight towns of Kasan, Kwaksan, Pakch'ŏn, Chŏngju, Sŏnch'ŏn, Ch'ŏlsan, Yongch'ŏn, and T'aech'ŏn. Entering Chŏngju Fortress, he fought against the government forces that surrounded and attacked the fortress from all sides for 3-4 months (34朔 is likely a typo, should be 三四朔 or similar). On April 19th of Imsin year (1812), at dawn (開東), the fortress was breached and destroyed by the government forces. Thus, 119 days after raising the banner, the rebellion was finally crushed. This was the so-called Hong Kyŏngnae Rebellion of Kasan and Chŏngju. (For details, refer to the article titled "North of the Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River and General Hong Kyŏngnae")

The Hong Kyŏngnae Rebellion and the Seven Righteous Men
There were seven righteous men who resisted Hong Kyŏngnae and eventually died as martyrs:
Han Houn, Paek Kyŏnghan, Im Chihwan, Hŏ Hang, Che Kyŏnghok, Kim Taet'aek, Chŏng Si.
Whenever I see or think about the history of such rebellions or the <83> success of heroic generals, this old poem always comes to mind: "Ask not the bystander about matters of war; one general's success is built on ten thousand withered bones (一將成功萬骨枯)." Similarly, whenever there is a war, regardless of victory or defeat on either side, how much effort must countless heroes, that is, nameless heroes, have expended? How many must have died? The seven righteous men listed above are commemorated for their meritorious service in fighting and dying for the country, with the Obong Sŏwŏn (Confucian academy) built at Osan in Chŏngju. However, how many unnamed individuals must have died for the nation at that time? And how many righteous soldiers must there have been who fought fiercely for Hong Kyŏngnae's revolution and died in battle?

Charity Monument of Kim Chaech'o of Chŏngju
This Mr. Kim lived 120 years ago in Yuksŏng-dong, Tŏktal-myŏn, of the same county. From a young age, he possessed abundant charity. Through toil and diligence, he saved money and selected nine locations with the most inconvenient crossings nearby, constructing wooden bridges to provide great convenience for travelers. He even undertook the annual repairs himself. Not only that, but throughout his life, he pondered how he could provide refreshing relief to the many people of the world. As a result of his research, he planted melons (眞苽 - true melons, likely 참외/chamoe) every year and offered them free of charge to passing travelers for exactly ten years, doing so consistently each year. Thus, a stone monument engraved with the words "Ten years planting melons, ten thousand people quenched their thirst (十年種苽萬人解渴)" still stands.

Kusŏng (Land of Three Victories and Little P'yŏngyang)
This county was called Mannyŏn County and Kuju during the Koryŏ Dynasty, and became Kusŏng County in the first year of the Yi Dynasty.
Ch'ŏngnyong Mountain traverses the center of the county, forming a watershed. To the west, the three townships of Sagi, Ch'ŏnma, and Kwansŏ belong to the Taeha River basin. The other seven townships belong to the Taeryŏng River basin. Mountains are numerous and plains are scarce, so the soil is generally barren (磽确).
The administrative divisions consist of 10 townships (*myŏn*) and 91 villages (*tong*).
The area is about 80 *pangni*.
Although this district is nestled in the middle of the southwestern part of North P'yŏngan Province without much to see or boast about, the sole pride of the local people and land is: "Our Kusŏng, the Land of Three Victories (三捷勝地)! Our Kusŏng, the auspicious land of 30,000 years! Our Kusŏng, Little P'yŏngyang (小平壤)!"
"Land of Three Victories" refers to: First, in the 2nd year of King Hyŏnjong of Koryŏ (1011), Lieutenant General Kim Suk'hŭng, along with his subordinate Chŏng Sŏng and Yang Kyu, the garrison commander of Hŭnghwa Garrison (in Sŏnch'ŏn), greatly defeated the large Khitan army (returning west after sacking the capital) on the plains of Aejeon (in Ihyŏn-myŏn).
Second, in the 10th year of King Hyŏnjong of Koryŏ, Kim'i year (1019), the Khitan general Xiao Sunning (蕭遜寧) invaded with 100,000 armored soldiers. Commander-in-Chief Kang Kamch'an and Vice Commander Kang Minch'ŏm led 208,000 troops, intercepted and decisively defeated them at Hŭnghwa Garrison (in Sŏnch'ŏn). <84> Then, in the spring of the following year, when the Khitan army retreated again and reached the eastern outskirts of this county, they were attacked fiercely and defeated once more.
Third, in the 18th year of King Kojong of Koryŏ, Sinmyo year (1231), the Mongols raised a large army and invaded through the South Gate (南門?). Generals Pak Sŏl and Kim Kyŏngson, along with divisional generals from various provinces, defended the fortress staunchly. After 45 fierce clashes, the Mongol army was finally routed and fled. (Defeating the Mongol army three consecutive times is also called the "Three Victories.") "Auspicious land of 30,000 years" refers to the combination of Mannyŏn Pavilion (萬年舘), Mannyŏn Bridge (萬年橋), and Mannyŏn Temple (萬年寺) in this county, totaling "30,000 years" (萬年 means 10,000 years).
"Little P'yŏngyang" is said to be because although Kusŏng fortress is small, its overall topography, orientation, and shape closely resemble P'yŏngyang.
The household count of this county is a total of 9,378 households (9,227 Korean, 45 Japanese, 6 Chinese), and the population is a total of 53,850 people (53,725 Korean, 107 Japanese, 18 Chinese). (Survey at the end of Taishō 10 [1921])
The educational situation includes 3 public common schools, 18 *sŏdang*, and 1 short course (*kangsŭpwon*), with reportedly no general private schools.
The religious situation includes 965 Christians and 3,096 Ch'ŏndogyo followers.
One special product of this district is that many female cattle (牝牛) are raised here. At Kusŏng South Market alone, the number of cattle brought to market annually is 10,837, and the number sold to other places annually is 2,609 head.

T'aech'ŏn (Birthplace of Sages)
What kind of district is this? Although this district has nothing particularly worth seeing, it is perhaps foremost in all of Korea for producing many sages since ancient times. Who are they? There are seven sages.
Tun'am-gong Sŏnu Hyŏp, Ch'unghyo-gong Kim Insang, Toksŏng-gong Kim Sech'ŏl, Haengwŏn-gong Paek Chinŭl, Yongho-gong Paek Yŏngjo, Un'am Sŏnsaeng Pak Munil, and Sŏng'am Sŏnsaeng Pak Muno – these seven individuals. Among them, Masters Tun'am and Un'am, through their lifelong work, established many lecture halls (齋室) in various places, educated disciples, developed and promoted Neo-Confucianism (理學) and literature (文學), and dedicated much effort to writing, leaving many works for posterity, thus their achievements are particularly great.
Master Tun'am's Poem from a Dream:
Ancient descendant of Hyŏnŭl (玄乙孫? Kija?),
Born, but born in an unlucky time.
Metal fades, Zhou's fire rises,
Great traces, sun and moon renew.
Coming here, teaching the fox-like party (狐黨?),
Who is the true man?
In the past, without Shennong,
Who could tame oxen and sheep?
Today, the flock of geese (群鴈行 - scholars/officials?),
How many follow Confucius?
The world declines, people are ignorant,
Yin falls, virtue already burdened.
A foot-high grave mound outside the ruined wall,
A lonely shrine faces the cold window.
After obtaining this poem, he went to P'yŏngyang and found Kija's tomb, it is said.
The location of this county is nestled in the center of the southwestern part of this province, with an area of about 45 *pangni*. <85> Its history includes the name Kwanghwa Prefecture (光化縣) during the Koguryŏ Dynasty.
Regarding its topography, it borders Ch'angsŏng and Sakchu to the north, Kusŏng and Chŏngju to the west, Pakch'ŏn to the south, and Unsan and Nyŏngbyŏn to the east.
The administrative divisions consist of 9 townships (*myŏn*) and 82 villages (*ri*).
What is the household count? Total households: 8,464 (including 8,415 Korean, 46 Japanese, 3 Chinese). Total population: 47,017 (including 46,927 Korean, 83 Japanese, 7 Chinese).
The educational situation is quite lacking. There are 3 public common schools, 1 Japanese public elementary school, 3 private schools, and 77 *sŏdang*.
The religious situation: 300 Christians and 1,540 Ch'ŏndogyo followers.
As for historical relics, the Yanghwa Temple, built over 930 years ago, is famous. Next are the Osan Sŏwŏn in the county seat, and the Three-Headed Monument (三頭碑) and pavilion for Kim Ch'unghyo-gong in Songgok-tong, within the county seat.

A Monument of Unparalleled Tragic Bloodshed (血績碑)
Members of the Ilchinhoe (一進會), having set their purpose on spreading virtue and benefiting the masses (布德廣濟), and advancing without regard for mountains of swords or rivers of knives, gathered from various counties. On September 3rd, Kapchin year (1904), twenty years ago, they held a meeting and proceeded towards T'aech'ŏn town to rectify corrupt politics and the actions of greedy officials.
At that time, County Magistrate Cho (趙郡守) was away, and a man named Village Head Paek (白鄕長) was present. He dispatched troops and gave orders to shoot down, without question, all members crossing the Koch'i River (곳치강?). The completely unarmed, innocent commoners were shot indiscriminately as they disembarked from the boats, while still on the boats crossing, or while tied up. Thirty-six people died instantly. The clear water of the Koch'i River changed color, becoming a river of bright red blood, and the white sandy banks along the river changed color, becoming red sandbanks.
Ah, how tragic! More tragic than the lives of the 36 people who died together at once; perhaps more tragic than the river of blood and the banks of blood at that time; even more tragic, sending shivers down my spine and making my hair stand on end, were the politics of that time and the mentality of those in power. What crime had they committed? Could the dawn possibly break before them after doing this to innocent people?
The names of the martyred believers (殉敎人) were: Pak Yonghak, Kim Pyŏngha, Sin Manok, Yi Ch'anhŭng, Hong Sŏngch'an, O Insŏng, Kim Sinmun, Kim Pyŏngdu, Pang Sakŭn, Yi T'aeyŏng, Kung Sasuk, An Yŏnggak, Kim Nakp'ung, Yŏm Sŏngmin, Kang Ikho, Pak Ch'igil, Kang Ingyong, Pang Munhak, Yang P'ilsu, Kim Chinmun, Kim Sino, Song Ch'anbok, Han Chinok, Pyŏn Yŏngbo, Ch'ŏn Man'guk, Mun Haksun, Kim Yŏngho, Ham Sŏngsin, Cho In'guk, Sin Imnyŏl, Ko Chŏngun, Pang Sahyŏp, and others. <86>

Unsan

This county was called Unjung County during the Koryŏ Dynasty, and was once part of Nyŏngbyŏn Prefecture during the reign of King Sejo of the Yi Dynasty.
The area of this district is about 53 *pangni*, not a particularly large district. Furthermore, the land is extremely barren. Topographically, it borders Ch'osan County to the north along Tongnim Mountain; faces Nyŏngbyŏn to the southeast across the Kuryong River; and borders T'aech'ŏn and Ch'angsŏng to the west along Paekp'i Mountain and its range. Thus, it has many mountains and few plains.
The administrative divisions consist of 5 townships (*myŏn*) and 50 villages (*tong*).
The household count totals 8,199 households (including 7,904 Korean, 74 Japanese, 195 Chinese, 27 other foreigners).
The population totals 39,977 people (including 39,190 Korean, 199 Japanese, 524 Chinese, 64 other foreigners).
Regarding education, based on the number of townships, the "one school per three townships" system has been surpassed.
There are 3 public common schools, 4 general private schools, 1 religious private school, 1 Japanese public elementary school, and 83 *sŏdang*.
The religious situation: 538 Christians and 1,010 Ch'ŏndogyo followers.
Although this district lacks notable facts throughout history that could be presented as remarkable, it does possess unique figures and historical events not seen elsewhere. Who might they be?
On October 20, Imja year (1672?), 250 years ago, in Ipsŏkha-dong, Unsan-myŏn, Unsan County,
A person named Yi Sihang was born. His courtesy name was Hwaŭn (和隱). By nature, he was intelligent, brilliant, and outstanding, with superior academic talent, having read through the Hundred Schools of Thought and various classics (百家詩書) in his youth. Due to his exceptional literary talent, he was known as an extraordinary person. He always maintained a harmonious demeanor, enjoyed helping the poor, promoting others' strengths, and disliked criticizing their shortcomings. He established schools and devoted all his energy to fostering talent.
During the 41st year of King Sukchong of the Yi Dynasty (1715), a person named Yŏ P'irhŭi, acting as a secret royal inspector (暗行繡衣御使), observed the public sentiment, customs, and habits of the Kwansŏ region (Hwanghae, P'yŏngan). Yŏ P'irhŭi submitted a memorial falsely claiming that the people of Kwansŏ lacked propriety and virtuous conduct, resembling barbarians (狄夷蠻人). Because of this slanderous memorial, it was decided in the royal court that people from Kwansŏ could not be appointed to civil or military official positions. Consequently, the people of Kwansŏ not only failed to receive the benevolent grace of "equal regard for all" (一視同仁) but, due to this, the culture (人文) of Kwansŏ fell into the tragic state of daily decline. <87>
At this time, Master Yi Sihang, forgetting sleep and food, greatly worried and could not overcome his public indignation, eventually striving to remedy the situation. He refuted Yŏ P'irhŭi's memorial point by point, proving it false, and submitted memorials titled "Memorial Refuting the False Accusations" (辨誣䟽) a total of 16 times. Only then did the court finally understand that Yŏ P'irhŭi's memorial was indeed false and abolished the discriminatory system.
Intellectuals of Kwansŏ cannot cease commemorating Master Yi's achievement, through which the culture of Kwansŏ was revived. His literary legacy includes a collection of writings called the *Hwaŭn Chip* (和隱集). Next, as a figure from the recent modern era,
There is Master Hakŭn Kim Tusŏng. He was born on May 22, Muo year, the 447th year of the Yi Dynasty (1888?), at Ponggangha, Choyang-dong, Ŭpmyŏn of this county.
His work involved establishing schools to encourage the education of young people. Simultaneously, he studied Neo-Confucian principles (性理) to develop Confucian scholarship. Furthermore, he planted trees in T'aehak-tong, dug a pond to raise lotus and fish, creating a private park where he enjoyed spending time with visiting friends and disciples. His remaining legacy includes a collection of writings called the *Hakŭn Chip* (鶴隱集) and this T'aehak-tong park, which still exists.

The Orient's Foremost Pukchin Gold Mine
This gold mine was originally operated by the Korean government about 30 years ago.
Twenty-eight years ago, the government granted it to Americans for a period of 50 years in exchange for tax payments. The Americans took it over and began operations under the name Oriental Consolidated Mining Company (東洋合同鑛業會社) with a capital of 10 million won. While there is no time to detail everything, a brief overview shows: the total number of miners employed (씨우는?) daily is about 4,000; the depth of the tunnels dug into the ground is 1,800 *ch'ŏk* (English feet); <88> the tonnage of ore crushed (찟는?) per 24 hours is 800 tons; there are about 60 Americans, about 600 Chinese laborers, about 3,000 Korean laborers, and 15-16 Korean clerks. However, it is said to be famous throughout Korea for paying the lowest wages (雇價 젹게). Conversely, it is also said to be a gold mine famous throughout Korea for making large profits. The average annual death rate for miners in the mine shafts has always been at least 13 people.

Nyŏngbyŏn
(Birthplace of the Founder of Chosŏn - likely referring to Dangun, not Yi Seonggye)
This district was originally called Yŏnju during the Koryŏ Dynasty, known as Musan Prefecture since the beginning of the Yi Dynasty. In the 33rd year of King Kojong (Yi T'ae Wang - 1896), the governorship (觀察使) of North P'yŏngan Province was established here, but after the provincial office moved to Ŭiju in Yunghŭi 1 (1907), it became Nyŏngbyŏn County.
This county is located in the southeastern corner of this province, bordering South P'yŏngan Province to the south, Hŭich'ŏn and Unsan to the northeast, and surrounded by T'aech'ŏn and Pakch'ŏn to the west. It is a large district with an area of 150 *pangni*. The administrative divisions consist of 14 townships (*myŏn*) and 114 villages (*tong*). The household count totals 21,000 households (20,904 Korean, 89 Japanese, 7 foreign). The population totals 115,825 people (115,803 Korean, 208 Japanese, 17 foreign). What about the educational situation? There is 1 public agricultural school, 4 public common schools, 10 general private schools, 1 religious private school, 1 Japanese public elementary school, and 228 *sŏdang*.
What is the religious situation? 399 Christians, 2,313 Ch'ŏndogyo followers, 506 Sich'ŏn'gyo followers, and 2-3 Ch'ŏngnimgyo followers.

Famous Places and Old Relics
Myohyang Mountain is a famous mountain in Korea, located in Sanghaeng-dong, Sinhyŏn-myŏn, north of this county. It is the birthplace of Dangun, the founder of the Korean nation. The main temple, Pohyŏn Temple (普賢寺), and 360 other large and small temples and hermitages once existed there. Besides these, there are many historical relics, too numerous to list individually (枚擧키 不遑하다).
Yaksan Mountain is located west of Nyŏngbyŏn town. Its height is 2,000 *ch'ŏk* <89> above sea level. At the foot of the mountain, there is a naturally formed terrace (臺). Naturally sculpted by wind (風飄?), peculiar rocks are piled up, and gnarled pine trees grow thickly (蓊鬱), seeming to float in the blue sky. The nearby mountains and fields spread out below at a glance. It gives climbers an unintentional sense of pleasure; this is the famous landmark of Nyŏngbyŏn known as the East Terrace (東臺) of Yaksan. Around the mountainside are temples like Ch'ŏnju Temple, Sŏun Temple, and Hakkwi Hermitage.
Ch'ŏrong Fortress (鐵甕城 - Iron Jar Fortress) is indeed an Iron Jar Fortress, the strongest fortress in North P'yŏngan. Its circumference is about 3 *ri* (Japanese *ri* - approx. 12km). Built over 500 years ago, the fortress construction is so solid that it truly presents the aspect of "one man defending the pass against ten thousand" (一夫當關 萬夫莫開).
Yuksŭng Pavilion (六勝亭 - Pavilion of Six Victories/Sights) is a pavilion located in the Lotus Pond (蓮塘) west of the county seat. The scenery is beautiful, possessing the so-called Six Sights (六勝): Peonies under the New Moon (牧丹初月), String Music and Recitation at the Hyanggyo (校宮絃誦), Evening Snow on Namsan (南山暮雪), Sunset Glow on Naesan (萊山夕照), Bell Sound from Ch'ŏnju Temple (天柱鍾聲), Watching Troops Drill (訓鍊觀兵).

Nyŏngbyŏn and the Rebellion of Yi Kwal
During the Injo Restoration (仁祖反正 - 1623 coup), the planning and execution were largely due to Yi Kwal's merit. However, when recording the meritorious subjects (靖社功臣), he was placed in the second rank. From this, Yi Kwal became disgruntled. At that time, there was alarm regarding Manchuria, so Chang Man was appointed Commander-in-Chief (都元帥) and stationed in Kisŏng (P'yŏngyang?). Yi Kwal was appointed Vice Commander for Both Western Provinces (兩西副元帥 - Hwanghae & P'yŏngan) and concurrently P'yŏngan Army Commander (平安兵使), stationed in Ch'ŏrong Fortress with 10,000 troops from Chŏlla Province and 170 surrendered Japanese soldiers (降倭). Han Myŏngnyŏn was appointed Inspector (巡撫使) and stationed in Kusŏng. In January of the following year, Kapsa (1624), Yi Kwal colluded with Han Myŏngnyŏn (韓相璉? typo for 韓明璉?) in a conspiracy. Claiming to prepare against Manchuria, he trained his subordinate troops and hunters (獵砲手), marched them to Pyŏkche. The royal carriage fled to Kongju. Yi Kwal, Han Myŏngnyŏn, and others advanced and attacked the capital (京城 - Seoul) but were decisively defeated at Anhyŏn Pass by Anju Defense Commander (安州防禦使) Chŏng Ch'ungsin and Kyŏnggi Governor (京畿監使) Yi Sŏ.

Pakch'ŏn
Although this county is a small district located at the southeastern tip of this province, the railway passes through Yŏngmi Station in Maengjung-ri at its southern end. The large Taeryŏng River flows through the central part of this district, making land and water transportation extremely convenient. Furthermore, since its southern tip borders the West Sea, marine products are also produced in no small quantity.
The administrative divisions consist of 11 townships (*myŏn*) and 91 villages (*tong*).
Regarding education, the "one school per three townships" system has been achieved. There are 4 public common schools, 1 private school, and 2 Japanese public elementary schools, totaling 7 institutions. The religious situation: 438 Christians and 1,974 Ch'ŏndogyo followers.

Pakch'ŏn's Three Major Special Products
For the production center of brassware (鍮器), people living far away might point to Napch'ŏng in Chŏngju County, but in reality, more is produced in the Pakch'ŏn region, which is adjacent to Napch'ŏng. Although more is made in the Pakch'ŏn region, because it is all taken to the Napch'ŏn market to be sold, the Napch'ŏng region naturally became famous.
In Taishō 11 (1922), the value of brassware produced just in the Pakch'ŏn region amounted to 105,000 won. <90>

**Summary**

This 1923 travelogue by "One Reporter" details observations from visiting eight counties in North P'yŏngan Province (P'yŏngan Pukto): Yongch'ŏn, Ch'ŏlsan, Sŏnch'ŏn, Chŏngju, Kusŏng, Unsan, Nyŏngbyŏn, and Pakch'ŏn. For each county, the reporter provides geographical descriptions, population statistics (including Korean, Japanese, and Chinese residents), administrative divisions, educational institutions (public, private, religious, Japanese), religious demographics (primarily Christian and Ch'ŏndogyo), notable historical sites, famous local figures (from ancient heroes to contemporary philanthropists like Kim Kyŏnghwe and Yi Ch'angsŏk), local industries or products (like the Pukchin Gold Mine, Kusŏng cattle, Pakch'ŏn brassware), and significant historical events associated with the area, such as the Russo-Japanese War's origins in Yongamp'o, Hong Kyŏngnae's Rebellion centered in Kasan/Chŏngju, the tragic Ilchinhoe incident in T'aech'ŏn, and Yi Kwal's Rebellion involving Nyŏngbyŏn.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting for its detailed statistics differentiating Korean, Japanese, and Chinese populations, reflecting the colonial administrative reality. It highlights both traditional Korean achievements (historical figures, landmarks like Myohyangsan) and modern developments, some involving Japanese influence or presence (like Japanese schools) and others driven by Koreans (Kim Kyŏnghwe's cooperative projects, Yi Ch'angsŏk's nursing home, Sŏnch'ŏn's afforestation). The mention of the American-run Pukchin Gold Mine, its scale, and alleged low wages and high death rate offers a glimpse into foreign concessions and labor conditions. The recounting of historical injustices like regional discrimination (against Kwansŏ people) and violent suppression (T'aech'ŏn incident) provides context for social tensions. Historians might find the detailed local data, descriptions of modernization efforts (cooperatives, hospitals), notes on religious adherence, and the perspective on historical events valuable for understanding regional conditions and social dynamics in early colonial Korea.

내가 본 平北의 各郡, 龍川-鐵山-宣川-定州-龜城-雲山-寧邊-博川
						一記者
龍川
本郡은 平安北道 西南端에 잇는 골이라. 東예는 鐵山 北에는 義州을 隣하엿고 西는 鴨綠江을 距하야 中國에 對하엿고 南은 西朝鮮灣에 濱하엿다. 本郡 廣O는 약 36방리나 되고 山岳이 少하며 丘陵의 起伏은 잇스나 대개는 토지가 平坦하고 地味가 肥沃하야 灌漑의 利와 水陸의 便宜가 만흐며 海産이 적지 안이하다. 戶口는 朝鮮人 15,504. 日本人 203. 支那人 109. 計 15,816戶오 인구는 朝鮮人이 90,769. 日本人이 653. 中國人이 514. 計 91,936인이다.
本郡의 面數는 12이오 洞數는 155이다. 교육기관으로는 일반 사립학교가 3개소, 宗敎사립학교가 5개소, 公普校가 4개소, 私立普校가 1개소, 日本人의 공립소학교가 1개소 計 14개소이다. 이 골에 名勝古蹟으로 말하면 별로 보잘 것은 업스나 본 郡邑 東西에 大佛頂陀羅尼幢이라는 梵字를 조각한 高麗時代의 건조물이 잇슬 뿐이다. 郡廳所在地는 龍巖浦라는 浦口인데 이 浦口는 본래 龍巖山이란 山이 해안에 起立하야 잇슴으로 仍하야 浦口의 명칭을 龍岩浦라 하게 되엿다.
이 浦口에 由來를 말하면 본래 閑寂하기 비할 데 업는 一個 魚浦러니 距今 23년前에 露西亞인이 支那 南北滿洲를 경영하면서 一邊으로는 朝鮮을 엿보아 內意로는＜77＞ 倂呑의 의사를 품고 형식으로는 鴨綠江 상류 無盡의 목재 採伐을 경영한다 標榜하야 鴨綠江 어구되는 龍岩浦에다 森林會社를 癸卯年에 설립하엿다. 이로부터 露西亞인은 형식으로는 森林會社를 경영한다하고 내용으로 순전이 이 군사적 행위를 취하엿다. 會社집을 짓는다는 것은 전부 兵營과 軍庫이엇고 회사의 사무원이라는 것은 전수 軍人중 將校이엿스며 龍岩浦의 最高處되는 龍巖山 上峯에는 炮臺를 건설하엿다. 그래서 甲辰乙巳의 日露戰役이란 것은 결국 이곳의 이것으로써 導火線이 되엿섯다.
지금까지도 露國사람들이 지엿던 튼튼한 煉瓦制의 건물이 18개쯤은 依然히 남아잇다.

龍川 金敬淮씨의 이대 模範事業
本郡 楊下面 三橋川 水利組合이니 본래 金敬淮
李泰鉉 兩氏가 최초에 개인경영으로 사업에 착수하야 무한 애를 쓰다가 大正 6년 비로소 地主組合이 성립되여 其 經營을 인수하야 共同經營하게 되엿다. 그래서 지금은
蒙利總面積이 801町步
경비의 前後總計가 70만8천원

宣川의 養老院
우리 朝鮮에서 養老院이라 하면 명칭부터 별로히 만이 듯지 못하든 일이다. 이 골 邑內面에 잇는 李昌錫씨는 본래 소년시절에 술 잘하고 잡기 잘 하기로 有名字하던 이다. 그러나 소년쩍 브터라도 그이의 성격가운데 볼 만한 것이 잇섯다. 家勢가 赤貧하야 雜技를 해서 生道를 니을망졍 밤낫으로 닛지안코 생각하는 세 가지의 信條가 잇섯다. 첫재는 사람이란 무슨 일이나 해서 안될 일이 업다는 것, 둘재는 사람이란 반다시 할 일은 하고 안이할 일은 안이 하갯다는 것, 셋재로는 나도 한 번 남과 갓치 살아보겟다는 것이다. 이러한 세 가지의 信條로서 한해 두해 살아가면서 노력한 결과로 인격은 본래브텨 忠實하거니와 家力도 宣川에 유수한 재산가가 되엿다. 氏는 여러 해 전브텨 耶蘇를 밋어 임이 長老의 지위까지 엇고 한울님을 밋음으로서 福을 만이 밧앗는데 무엇으로써 한울님께 영광을 돌닐 일이 업다하야 大正 7년도에 자기의 소유전답 약 30만원(당시의 가격으로) 가치의 것을 교회에 다 밧쳐서 그해브터 그 田畓의 수입으로서 養老院을 경영하게 되니 지금 입원하여 잇는 노인은 70以上의 男人이 4인이오 女人이 15인이다.

嘉嘉가山 定州에 洪景來亂이라 하면 朝鮮사람으로서는 암아 尺童아히라도 다 알 것이다. 그러나 定州 一郡의 모든 방면을 생각하거나 기록함에 당하야는 근대사실로서 유명한 洪景來의 政治革命亂을 한번 니야기 안이할 수 업다.
洪景來는 원래 平南龍岡 사람이라 어려서부터 글 닑기를 조와하야 四書三經과 諸子百家를 無不能通하며 才器過人하고 武勇이 絶倫하며 지혜는 뛰여나고 弓馬之才를 조와하야 神出鬼沒한 抱負가 잇스며 심지어 奇文遁甲之術까지 解得한지라 항상 스사로 탄식하기를＜82＞ 남아가 한번 세상에 남애 맛당이 塗炭에 빠진 蒼生을 건지여 絶大한 사업을 일울 것이라. 엇지 밥버레로 구구히 한 세상을 虛送하리오. 눈을 들어 세사를 살펴보건대 寒心痛哭할 일이 너무도 만토다. 틀닌 일을 보고 가만이 잇거나 올은 일을 두고 행치 안는 것은 진실로 죽거나 썩은 사람의 일이라. 엇지 산사람의 할 일이리오. 지금에 全朝鮮의 정치가 전부가 다 腐敗된 중에도 더욱이 우수은 것은 갓흔 지방으로서 지방올 차별하고 갓흔 민족으로서 민족을 차별하야 남북과 貴賤을 가리여 서북사람은 엇던 위대한 인물이 나더라도 文不過 持平掌令에 武不過 僉使萬戶오. 남방사람은 文武에 登科하야 간신이 玉堂과 宣傳官까지는 하엿다. 그뿐만 아이라 기타의 모든 방면이나 그와 갓치 腐敗하엿다. 정치의 모든 腐敗를 바로잡고져 니를 갈며 부르쥐인 주먹으로 책상을 두다리고 이에 옷소매를 떨치고 집을 떠낫다. 몬저 淸北列邑으로 도라단이면서 同志를 求코저 書堂으로 도라단이며 글도 니르고 혹은 노동하야 각처에 만은 동지를 엇게 되엿다. 그런 뒤에는 여러 해를 두고 각처로 도라단이면서 革命의 根據地 삼을 장소를 선택하엿다. 그 결과로 수륙의 교통이 편리하고 兼하야 淸北의 중심지가 되고도 幽僻한 嘉山 多伏洞(今 博川지방)을 잇엇다. 그리고 그 안에다 叅謀本部를 두고 일방으로는 사방에 사람을 보내여 여러 가지 방법으로 사람을 끌어들이며 食料品을 ꊵ�해들이며 軍器 제조할 모든 鐵物을 구해들이며 일방으로는 山上에다 성을 쌋코 그 안에서 화약과 兵器를 제조하며 又 일방으로는 元帥峯 山上에다 鍊兵場을 닥고 군사를 操鍊하다가 辛未의 大無年을 유일한 期會로 삼아 크게 飢民을 모집하니 응모자가 구름 모이듯 하엿다.

洪景來亂과 七義士
洪景來를 저항하다가 마춤내 殉節한 7의사가 잇스니
韓浩運, 白慶翰, 林之煥, 許沆, 諸景或, 金大宅, 鄭蓍등 7인이다. 나는 이러한 병란의 역사나 英雄將士의＜83＞ 사업성공을 보거나 생각할 때마다 이러한 古詩가 늘 생각난다. 『傍人莫問兵家事, 一將成功萬骨枯』 이 모양으로 한 번 전란이 잇슬 때마다 승리, 실패의 양측을 물론하고 무친 英雄 즉 無名英雄이 노력은 얼마나 만이 하여스며 죽긴덜 얼마나 만이 죽어스랴. 右에 列記한 7의사를 국가를 위하야 싸오다가 殉節한 공이 잇다하야 定州五山에 五峯書院을 짓고 그네들을 紀念하지만은 그 時에 일홈이 들어나지 못하고 나라를 위하야 殉節한 이가 얼마나 만아스며 洪景來측 혁명을 위하야 奮鬪하다가 전사한 義士인덜 얼마나 만흐랴.

定州 金再初씨 慈善碑
氏는 距今 120년전에 仝郡德達面 六星洞에서 사든 사람이라 少時로부터 慈善心이 풍부하야 勞苦와 勤勉으로 돈을 모아서 근처에 제일 교통이 불편한 橋梁 9개소를 골나서 木橋를 架設하야 행인으로 하여곰 지대한 편의를 圖하고 매년 修築하는 것까지도 늘 自擔하엿다. 그것만이 안이라 그는 일생에 생각하기를 엇지하면 이 세상 만은 사람의게 다 시원한 일을 베풀너 줄가 하고 연구하든 결과로 매년 眞苽(참외)를 심거서 去來의 행인의게 무료로 제공하기를 꼭 10년동안을 한 해와 갓치 하엿다. 그래서 『十年種苽萬人解渴』이라고 색인 비석이 여전이 잇다.

龜城(三捷勝地오 또 小平壤인)
本郡은 高麗時에 萬年郡及 龜州의 稱이 有하엿고 李朝元년에 龜城郡이 되엿다.
靑靑청처龍山이 郡의 중앙을 橫하야 分水嶺이 되고 서는 沙器, 天摩, 舘西의 삼면으로서 大蝦江 유역에 속하고 기타 7면은 大寧江 유역에 속하야 山岳이 重疊하고 평야는 乏少하니 地味는 대개가 磽涌하다.
행정구역은 10면91동으로 되고
면적은 약 80여방리이다.
이 골은 별로히 보잘 것 업고 자랑거리 업는 平安北道 西南端 한 복판에 낀기워 잇는 골이지만은 이 골 人土의 유일한 자랑거리는 三捷勝地인 우리 龜城, 3만년 勝地인 우리 龜城, 小平壤인 우리 龜城이라 한다.
三捷勝地라 함은 高麗 顯宗 2년에 別將 金叔興이 부하 鄭成과 興化鎭(在宣川) 僉使楊規와 갓치 契丹의 大兵(京都를 陷하고 西還하는)을 艾田벌판(在梨峴面)에서 大破하야 대승한 것이 一이오.
高麗 顯宗 10년 己未에 契丹 대장 蕭遜寧이 甲士十萬을 率하고 來侵하거늘 都元帥 姜邯贊과 副元帥 姜民瞻이 병 20만8천을 率하고 興化鎭(在宣川)에서 邀擊大破하고＜84＞ 又 翌年春에 다시 契丹兵이 회군하야 本郡東郊에 至하거늘 다시 奮擊大破한 것이 其二오.
高麗 高宗 18년 辛卯에 蒙古가 대병을 擧하야 南門을 來侵하거늘 朴屑, 金慶遜 兩將軍이 諸州 分道將軍과 갓치 城을 固守하다가 격전 45합에 蒙兵이 마츰내 大破走去한 것이 其三이라. (蒙古兵을 三次連破함도 三捷이라 亦云) 3만년의 勝地라 함은 本郡에 잇는 萬年舘과 萬年橋와 萬年寺를 합하야 3만년의 稱이 잇다.
小平壤이라 함은 이 龜城이란 성은 적을망졍 모든 地勢와 방향과 模形이 平壤과 흡사하다하야 小平壤의 稱이 잇다한다.
本郡의 戶口는 합계가 9,378호(朝鮮人 9,227. 日本人 45. 支那人 6)이고 인구는 합계 53,850구(朝鮮人 53,725. 日本人 107. 支那人 18)이다. (大正 10년末 調)
교육상황은 公普校가 3, 書堂이 18, 講習院이 1 뿐이오 일반 사립학교는 한아도 업다한다.
종교상황은 耶蘇敎인이 965, 天道敎人이 3,096이다.
이 골에 한 가지 특산이 잇는 것은 牝牛가 만이 산출된다. 龜城 南시장에만 매년 出市頭數가 10,837이오 매년 他處로 賣出牛數 2,609두이다.

泰川(賢人所産地)
이 골은 엇더한 골인가. 이 골이 별로히 보암즉한 것이 업는 골이나마 고래로 賢人 만이 나기로는 아마 全鮮에 제일이지오. 뉘기뉘기 인가오, 7현이나 됨니다.
遯菴公 鮮于浹, 忠孝公 金麟祥, 篤誠公 金世哲, 杏園公 白志訥, 龍湖公 白永祚, 雲菴先生 朴文一, 誠菴先生 朴文五등 7인임니다. 기중에도 遯菴 雲菴 兩先生은 생전사업으로 齋室을 各處에 만이 설립하고 제자를 교육하야 理學과 文學을 發展進興식히며 著書에 만이 노력하야 후세에 遺傳한 것이 만이 잇슴으로 더욱이 功績이 만슴니다.
遯菴先生의 夢中詩
上古玄乙孫, 生而生不辰. 金消周火起, 巨跡日月新. 來斯敎狐黨, 何人作眞人. 昔靡神農氏, 牛羊誰能馴. 當今群鴈行, 何數仲尼后, 世荒人無識, 殷亡德己負. 尺墳殘城外, 孤祠對寒牖. 이 시를 엇고 平壤으로 나가서 箕子陵을 엇엇다 함니다.
本群의 위치는 本道의 서남부의 중부에 가치워 잇는 골인데 면적은 약 45방리이오＜85＞ 沿革은 高句麗朝의 光化縣의 稱이 잇섯고
地勢로 말하면 북은 昌城, 朔州, 서는 龜城 定州, 남은 博川, 동은 雲山, 寧邊에 境하엿다.
행정구획은 9면 82동리로 되엿다.
戶口는 얼마나 잇나. 총계 호수가 8,464호(중에 朝鮮人 8,415. 日本人 46. 支那人 3호)이오 인구는 총계가 47,017인(內朝鮮人 46,927인. 日本人 83. 支那人 7)이다.
교육상황은 아주 말이 안이다. 公立普校가 3, 日本人 公立小校가 1, 사립학교가 3, 書堂이 77이다.
종교의 형편은 耶蘇校인이 3백명이오 天道敎인이 1540명이다.
古蹟으로는 距今 930여년전에 건설된 陽和寺가 유명하고 다음에는 읍내에 잇는 烏山書院과 읍내 松谷洞에 잇는 金忠孝公의 三頭碑와 碑閣이 잇다.

悽慘無比의 血績碑
한번 뜻을 布德廣濟에다 세우고 釰山과 刀水를 헤아리지 안코 나아가든 一進會員들은 각군에서 회합하야 距今 20년전 甲辰年 9월 3일에 會를 열고 부정한 정치와 탐관오리의 행위를 矯正코저 文學洙외 여러 회원이 泰川邑을 향하고 들어가게 되엿다.
其其기ㄱ時에 趙郡守는 출타하고 白鄕長이란 자가 잇서서 군대를 풀어서 곳치강울 건너서 들어오는 회원덜을 不問曲直하고 모주리 총살하라고 발령을 하엿다. 手無寸鐵한 순전한 양민들이 배를 건너서 下陸하는대로 배를 타고 건너오는 그대로 묵거선 그대로 총을 막 노아서 36인이 즉사되니 곳치江의 맑든 물은 빗치 변하야 발간 피의 강이 되고 강변의 白沙場은 빗츨 변하야 赤沙場이 되엿다.
아- 悽慘하다. 일시에 갓치 죽은 36인의 목숨이 悽慘하기보다 其時에 그 피의 강과 피의 沙場이 悽慘하여스리라기 보다도 그 때의 그 정치와 그 때의 執政者들의 심리가 더욱 悽慘하다고 몸에 소름이 끼치며 머리갈이 움즉인다. 그들이 무슨 죄악이 잇슬가. 무죄한 양민을 이러케 하고도 그 압헤 曙光이 비치일가.
殉敎人의 氏名은 朴龍學, 金炳夏, 申萬玉, 李贊興, 洪成贊, 吳麟成, 金愼文, 金炳斗, 方士根, 李泰英, 弓士淑, 安永覺, 金洛豊, 廉聖敏, 康益浩, 朴致吉, 姜益龍, 方文學, 梁弼洙, 金振文, 金愼五, 宋贊福, 韓鎭玉, 卞永甫, 千萬局, 文學淳, 金永浩, 咸聖信, 曹麟局, 申任烈, 高貞雲, 方士洽. 등 諸氏더라.＜86＞

雲山

本群은 高麗시대에 雲中群이라 하엿스며 李朝 世祖시에는 寧邊府에 속하엿던 일도 잇섯다.
이 골의 면적은 약 53방리나 되는 별로 크지 못한 골이다. 게다가 兼하야서 토지는 극히 쳑박한 곳이다. 地勢로 보면 북에는 東林山으로서 楚山群을 界하고 동남은 九龍江을 狹하야 寧邊과 상대하고 서는 白避山及 其 山脈으로 泰川及 昌城에 界하니 산악은 만코 평야는 적은 곳이다.
행정구획은 5면과 50동으로 되여잇고
戶口는 합계가 8,199호이오(내에 朝鮮人 7,904, 日本人 74, 支那人 195, 기타의 외국인 27)
인구는 합계가 39,977인(내에 朝鮮人 39,190, 日本人 199, 支那人 524, 기타의 외국인 64)이다.
교육은 面數로 보아서는 三面一校制가 지난 셈이다.
公普校가 3, 일반사립학교가 4, 종교사립이 1, 일본인 公小가 1, 書堂이 83이다.
종교의 상황은 耶蘇敎인이 538인이오 天道敎인이 1,010인이다.
이 골에는 고금을 통하야 별로히 이러타 하고 내여노을 만한 사실은 업스나 다른 곳에서 볼 수 업는 특이한 인물과 사적이 잇스니 그 뉘기인가.
距今 250년전 壬子 10월 20일에 雲山群 雲山面 立石下洞에서
李時恒이란 이가 낫다. 雅號는 和隱이라 하엿는데 천성이 聰穎英邁하며 學의 재능이 우수하야 유년시대에 百家詩書를 讀盡하엿다. 其 문예의 재능이 出衆함으로써 異人의 稱이 有하엿고 居常에 和氣靉然하야 인의 貧을 구하기 조와하며 人의 長은 發揚하고 短은 평론하기 실여하엿스며 학교를 設하고 育英에 전력을 다하엿다.
李朝 肅宗 41년에 至하야 呂必禧라는 자가 暗行繡의 御使로서 關西(黃海, 平安)의 민정, 풍속, 습관 등을 관찰하엿다. 呂必禧는 上䟽하기를 關西인민은 예의와 행덕이 업는 狄夷蠻人과 갓다고 誣罔의 상소를 한 따문에 關西인민을 文武百官에 등용할 수 업다는 것이 廟議에 결정되엿다. 이로 인하야 關西인민은 상시 소위 一視同仁의 성은을 못 엇게 되엿슬 뿐이 안이라 이로 인하야 關西의 人文은 날로 퇴보하는 悲境에＜87＞ 빠지게 되엿다.
이 때에 李時恒公은 寢食을 닛고 크게 근심하며 公忿을 이기지 못하야 미츰내 이를 救濟하기에 노력하기 시작하엿다. 呂必禧의 上䟽는 誣䟽됨을 逐條反拍하야 辨誣䟽라는 제목으로 上䟽하기를 凡 16回에 至하엿다. 그 때에야 조정은 비로소 呂必禧의 上䟽가 誣䟽됨을 이해하고 드디여 차별제도를 撤廢하게 되엿다.
關西의 인사는 선생의 공적으로 인하야 關西의 문화가 再興되게 된 것을 지금까지 추모함을 마지 못한다. 그의 문학의 유적으로는 和隱集이라는 문집이 남아잇다. 그 다음으로는 최근 근대의 인물로서
鶴隱 金斗性선생이 잇다. 그는 李朝 개국 447 戊午年 5월 22일에 本群邑面 朝陽洞 鳳岡下에서 낫다.
그가 사업으로는 학교를 세워서 청년자제의 교육을 獎勵하엿고 일방으로 性理를 연구하야 儒學을 발전식엿스며 又 일방으로는  胎鶴洞에다 植木을 하며 못을 파서 蓮과 魚를 養하야 사설공원을 만들고 차자오는 友人과 弟子로 더부러 갓치 질겨하엿다. 지금까지 그의 유적으로는 鶴隱集이란 문집과 이 胎鶴洞 공원이 의연히 남아잇다.

東洋有數의 北鎭金鑛
본래 이 金鑛은 30여년 전에 조선정부에서 경영하던 것이다.
28년전에 정부에서 稅納을 밧기로하고 50년동안 기한하고 美國사람의게 준 것이다, 美國 사람들이 이를 인수하여 가지고 東洋合同鑛業會社라는 명의로서 천만원의 자본금으로서 경영하게 되엿다. 자세한 것은 다 말할 겨를이 업거니와 대략의 상황을 들면 매일 씨우는 인부총수가 약 4천명, 땅으로 들어간 굴의 깁피가 1천8백척(英尺)＜88＞ 一晝夜에 찟는 광석 噸數는 8백톤 美國사람이 약 60명 中國人 인부가 약 6백명 朝鮮人 인부가 약 3천여명 朝鮮人 사무원이 15,6인이다. 그런데 인부의 雇價 젹게 주기로는 全鮮에 유명하다한다. 기대신 이익 만히 보기로는 역시 全鮮에 유명한 금광이라 한다. 매년에 鑛窟에서 인부 사망율이 평균 13인이상은 늘 되엿다.

寧邊
(朝鮮始祖 誕生地인)
이 골은 본래 高麗時에 延州의 稱이 유하엿고 李朝이래로는 撫山縣의 稱이 잇섯고 李太王 33년에 平安北道 觀察使를 두엇다가 隆熙 元年에 道廳을 義州로 옴긴 이후에 寧邊郡으로 되엿다.
本郡은 本道 동남부의 一隅에 잇스니 남방은 平南에 접하고 동북은 熙川, 雲山으로 西方은 泰川 博川으로서 포위되엿고 면적은 150방리나 되는 큰 골이다. 행정구역은 14면 114동으로 되엿스며 戶口는 합계 21,000호(朝鮮人 20,904, 日本人 89, 외국인 7)이오 인구는 합계 115,825구(朝鮮人 115,803. 日本人 208, 외국인 17)이다. 그러면 교육상황은 엇더한가. 공립으로 농업학교 1, 公普校 4, 일반사립학교 10, 종교사립학교 1, 日本人 公立小校가 1, 書堂이 228이다.
종교상황은 엇더한지. 耶蘇敎인이 399인이오 天道敎인이 2,313인이오 侍天敎인이 506인이오 靑林敎人이 二三人이다.

名所舊蹟
妙香山은 本郡 북 薪峴面 上杏洞에 잇는 朝鮮에 저명한 산이다. 朝鮮민족의 시조 檀君의 탄생지이며 大本山 普賢寺와 기타의 大小寺刹이 360庵이 잇섯스며 기외에도 역사상 고적이 만흐나 枚擧키 不遑하다.
藥山은 寧邊邑 서부에 잇스니 高는 해발 2천척이나＜89＞되고 산하에 한 天成의 臺가 잇서 천성으로 風飄하여 奇岩이 重疊하며 矯松이 蓊鬱하야 창공에 뜬 듯하니 近地의 산야는 一眸之下에 전개하여 보인다. 등산자로 하여곰 無意중에 쾌감을 엇게하니 이것이 寧邊명물로 유명한 소위 藥山의 東臺라는 것이다. 山腹의 주위에는 天柱寺, 棲雲寺, 鶴歸庵등이 잇다.
鐵甕城은 果然 鐵甕城이여서 平北에 튼튼하기로 제일 가는 성이다. 주위는 약 3里(日里)나 되고 五百餘年前에 創築한 것인데 축성의 견고함이 실로 一夫當關에 萬夫莫開의 觀이 잇다.
六勝亭은 本邑 서부 蓮塘中에 잇는 亭閣이다. 풍경이 明媚하야 소위 6勝의 景이 잇다. 牧丹初月, 校宮絃誦, 南山暮雪, 萊山夕照, 天柱鍾聲, 訓鍊觀兵

寧邊과 李适의 亂
仁仁祖反正時에 부분과 規劃이 다 李适의 공이라. 그러나 靖社功臣을 錄함에는 제 2등에 入하니 适이 이로부터 불평하다가 時에 滿州의 警이 有하야 張晩으로 都元帥를 任하야 箕城에 鎭하고 李适로 兩西副元帥兼 平安兵使를 任하야 全羅兵 일만과 降倭 170명으로 鐵瓮城에 鎭케하고 韓明璉으로 巡撫使를 任하야 龜城에 鎭케 하엿더니 翌年 甲子正月에 李适이 韓相璉으로 더부러 異謀를 통하야 滿州를 備한다 稱하고 部下軍과 獵砲手를 訓鍊하야 몰아서 碧蹄에 至하니 車駕-公州에 播越하거늘 李适 韓明璉등이 進하야 京城을 犯하엿다가 安州防禦使 鄭忠臣과 京畿監使 李曙등에게 鞍峴에서 대패하엿다.

博川
本郡은 本道의 동남단에 잇는 족으마한 한골이나마 남단에는 孟中里 嶺美驛을 通하야 철도가 잇고 이 골의 중앙지방으로는 大寧江이라는 큰 강이 흘너 수륙의 교통이 극히 편할 뿐만 안이라 남부 一端은 서해에 濱하엿슴으로 해산물도 젹지 안케 난다.
행정구역은 11면 91동으로 되엿다.
교육으로 말하면 三面一校制는 되엿다. 公立普校가 4, 사립학교 1, 日本人 公立小學校 2, 計 7개소이다. 종교상황은 耶蘇敎인이 438인, 天道敎인이 1,974인이다.

博川의 三大 特産品
鍮器의 산지로는 定州郡 納淸을 먼대 사는 사람은 屈指하지마는 사실은 納淸이와 접근하여잇는 博川지방에서 더 만히 난다. 博川지방에서 더 만들지 만은 그 만든 것을 다 納淸시장에 갓다 팔기 때문에 納淸지방이 자연히 이름이 나게 되엿다.
