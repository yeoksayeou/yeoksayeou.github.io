Magazine Title	개벽 제39호
Publication Date	1923년 09월 01일
Article Title	녀름달 (Summer Moon)
Author	요한
Article Type	시 (Poem)

Treasure
Silently by the deep, boundless riverside at night
Endlessly, boundlessly, towards the forest groves, towards the horizon
There is an infinite flow descending.

While people sleep and birds too
Dream in the blue wind of the summer night—
At such times, moonlight flows by the boundless riverside.

Like mother's milk, the moonlight, without stinting,
In fields unseen, on sleeping riverbanks,
Descends, flows, and overflows.
A treasure of unknown value, thus without stinting,
It descends, flows, and overflows.

White Flowers
By the great riverside where the flood tide washes the land's bare breast,
When the moonlight pours sweet dreams onto water and earth,
Countless white flowers bloom on that bank.<144>

Flowers that bloom in the moonlight, their lips are white as snow.
White flowers without fragrance, blooming unseen by people—
Flowers blooming by the riverside where infinite waves play—
Flowers that vanish without a trace when morning comes—
Flowers huddled together, nodding off like children—
Lonely flowers whose lips only the moonlight meets—

By the boundless riverside, moonlit and filled with endless water sounds,
Countless white flowers bloom, hidden in the night.

Visitor
A visitor has come outside the window and seeks me.
In the room, "Fatigue" tries to close my eyes.
I peeked outside through a crack in the window,
But outside the window, there is nothing but wind.

A visitor has come outside the window and seeks me.
But I hesitate to open the window—
Because "Habit" instills a causeless fear.
Should I open the window and greet the visitor when morning comes?

A visitor has come outside the window and seeks me.
My whole body feels about to burst with "Waiting."
I stand up, decide, open the window.
The awaited moonlight washes my bare body.<145>

**Summary**

This work consists of three lyrical poems themed around the "Summer Moon." The first poem, "Treasure," depicts moonlight as a boundless, precious entity flowing generously over the sleeping landscape. The second, "White Flowers," describes ephemeral white flowers blooming secretly under the moonlight by a riverside, symbolizing purity and hidden beauty. The third, "Visitor," uses the metaphor of a visitor (revealed to be moonlight) arriving at night, exploring the speaker's internal conflict between fatigue/fear ("Habit") and longing ("Waiting"), culminating in acceptance and immersion in the moonlight. From a historical perspective, the poems, published in 1923 during the Japanese colonial period, are notable for their focus on nature, lyricism, and introspection rather than overt political commentary. Historians might find the subtle exploration of themes like hidden beauty, longing, and the solace found in nature interesting, potentially reflecting a desire for purity, escape, or quiet resistance amidst the socio-political constraints of the time. The personification of abstract concepts like "Fatigue," "Habit," and "Waiting" adds psychological depth.

녀름달
						요한
보배
밤 깁흔 무한한 강가에 소리업시
끄님업시 한업시 나무 숩으로 수평선으로
흘러나리는 무한한 흐름이 잇슴니다.

사람은 잠자고 새들도
녀름밤의 푸른 바람에 꿈꾸려할 제-
그럴 때에 무한한 강가에 달비치 흘러감니다.

어머니의 젓가튼 달비치 애낌도 업시
보는 사람 업는 들에 잠드른 강언덕에
내리고 흐르며 넘치어 감니다.
갑모르게 귀한 보배이매 애낌도 업시
내리고 흐르며 넘치어갑니다.

흰 꼿
밀물이 땅의 버슨 가슴을 씻는 큰강가에
달비치 물과 흙에 단꿈을 부어 줄적에
그 언덕에 수업슨 흰 꼿이 피어남니다.＜144＞

달비체 피는 꼿이매 그 입슐은 눈가치 흼니다.
사람 몰래 피는 향긔 업는 흰꼿-
무한한 물결 노니는 강가에 피는 꼿-
아츰이 오면 흔적도 업시 사라지는 꼿-
어린아이들가치 머리를 모으고 조으는 꼿-
달빗만 그 입을 마초아주는 적적한 꼿-

달밝고 물소리 끄님업는 무한한 강가에
수업슨 흰꼿이 밤을 숨어 피어남니다.

손님
창밧게 손님이 와서 나를 찻는다.
방에는 「피곤」이 내눈을 감기려한다.
나는 창문 틈으로 밧겻흘 엿보앗스나
바람과 받밧게 아모도 업다.

창밧게 손님이 와서 나를 찻는다.
그러나 나는 창문 열기를 주저한다-
「버릇」이 까닭업는 무서움을 추기때문에.
아츰이 되거던 창을 열고 손님을 마즐까.

창밧게 손님이 와서 나를 찻는다.
나의 온몸이 「긔다림」으로 터질듯하다.
나는 일어선다 결심한다 창을여다.
긔다리던 달비치 나의 버슨 몸을 씻는다.＜145＞
