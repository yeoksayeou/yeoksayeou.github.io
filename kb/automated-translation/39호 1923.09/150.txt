Great City of the East Coast

As a result of the merging of prefectures and counties (府郡廢合) in March of Taishō 3 (1914), the four small counties of Changgi (長䰇), Yŏnil (延日), Hŭnghae (興海), and Ch'ŏngha (淸河) were combined. Behind it lies a land boundary encompassing 27 *ri* and 15 *chō* including Kyŏngju (慶州), Yŏngch'ŏn (永川), Ch'ŏngsong (靑松), and Yŏngdŏk (盈德), and in front, a coastline of 24 *ri* and 21 *chō*. Within this area, the current large county of Yŏngil (迎日) was formed, comprising 18 *myŏn* (townships), 225 *dong-ri* (villages), a total area of 73 square *ri* and 427 units [unit unclear], and a population close to 150,000. The terrain mostly consists of mountains with few plains. However, the connected peaks of the T'aebaek range run south, creating the natural bounty of Hŭnghae, and the great Hyŏngsan River (兄山) divides north and south, opening up the fertile fields of Yŏnil. Furthermore, the long cape of Tongoe-kot (冬外串) and the promontory of Hŭnghae embrace the great bay of Yŏngil, opening the gateway to P'ohang (浦項), the only good harbor in the east, swallowing and spitting out the goods of the eastern peninsula. Additionally, Kuryongp'o (九龍浦), which serves as the collection point for products from the Changgi peninsula, becomes the wings for the development of the Yŏngil region. Seen this way, it seems hardly an exaggeration to say that Yŏngil possesses the qualifications to become a great city of the entire east coast.

Yŏngil Bay of Herring (靑魚)
It would be more appropriate to call Yŏngil Bay the Herring Harbor (靑魚港) rather than Yŏngil Bay. Numbers are something that cannot be deceived in anything. <106> Let us depict how much profit this harbor brings us through this. According to the survey chart as of March this year, the fixed herring pot net (鯟壺網) fishing grounds numbered 58 for Koreans, 65 for Japanese, 19 for the cooperative (組合), and 27 outside the cooperative, totaling 169 locations. There were 65 units of herring lift nets (鯟擧揮罹網), 170 employers (including a few Koreans), and 4,180 employees (including about 50 Japanese). Traditionally, all this herring was consumed solely within Korea, but starting this year, the market has expanded even to mainland Japan, achieving good results. Therefore, they intend to focus more and more efforts in this direction in the future. Here is the record of the catch over the past five years.

Fisheries Association (水産會) and Fisheries Experiment Station (水産試驗場)
Compared to 10 years ago, the fish catch value for the entire 54 *ri* of the East Sea coast two years ago, amounting to over 5.5 million Won – ranking third in all of Korea with an 8.5-fold increase – truly provided a good opportunity to promote the improvement and development of this province's (本道) fisheries industry. Thus, it is no coincidence that the establishment of the Provincial Fisheries Association (道水産會), which produces the Korean Fisheries Association (朝鮮 水産會), and the Provincial Fisheries Experiment Station (道水産試驗場) were all centered around Yŏngil Bay. Listening to the overview of the ongoing work in both areas, the Fisheries Association is said to be a public institution that encourages a spirit of self-governance, mediating between government offices and industry operators. It has 11 council members and about 10 officers, executing tasks related to guidance such as general lectures, fishing village surveys, net-making training, and various facilities accompanying these. Among the association fees, the amount levied for the fishing industry assessment (漁業割) or business assessment (營業割) is based on the members' catch value, production value, or handling value, divided into 29 grades from a maximum of over 100,000 Won to a minimum of under 100 Won, and collected as revenue.
The Fisheries Experiment Station is also an institution for promoting the industry, focusing on mackerel (鯖) and Spanish mackerel (鰆) fishing trials using engine power, surveys of herring habitats (鯟捿息場調査), trials on utilizing mackerel, herring, and herring roe (鯖鯟及 鯟卵利用試驗), and surveys of the supply and demand situation for marine products. This fiscal year, it is said to be demonstrating the status and characteristics of this province's fisheries by allocating an additional budget of 30,745 Won. <107>

Yŏngil Irrigation Association (水利組合) and Fishery Cooperative (漁業組合)

The Yŏngil Irrigation Association (迎日 水利組合) began in the early spring of Taishō 5 (1916) with an initial construction cost of 200,000 Won, centering its beneficiary area on the three major fertile plains of Taesong (大松), Yŏnil (延日), and P'ohang (浦項), covering over 1,400 *chōbo*. Utilizing the Hyŏngsan River (兄山江), a local source of disaster (禍根), it sought ways for drainage (疏水) and initiated irrigation works. Currently, the association members number 1,744 Japanese and 104 Koreans.
Furthermore, the Fishery Cooperative (漁業組合) encompasses fishermen from 656 Korean households and 50 Japanese households along the hundred-*ri* (old *ri* measure) East coastline as its members. It covers six districts: Tonghae (東海), Och'ŏn (烏川), Taesong (大松), Yŏnil (延日), P'ohang (浦項), and Hŭnghae (興海). Since commencing operations in early Taishō 4 (1915), it is said to have achieved considerable results. Regardless, these two cooperatives are said to be capable of becoming vital, living-Buddha-like (活佛) institutions establishing a hundred-year plan (百年大計) for this region. Activities of Hŏnmun Yanghaeng Automobile Company and Taegu Bank Branch
Since the merger of prefectures and counties, the force of development in eastern Kyŏngbuk (慶北) has centered on P'ohang <108>. Numerous main and branch offices of entities like Yŏngil Kŭmjo (迎日金組), P'ohang Kŭmjo (浦項金組), Taehŭng Electric (大興電氣), Ilhwa Industries (日華産業), T'oksan-sa (拓産社), Singer Sewing Machine (씽거미싱), Hŏnmun Yanghaeng Automobile (韓文洋行 自動車), Chigin Bank (殖銀), and Taegin Bank (大銀) have followed one after another, engrossed in expanding their respective interests. However, in this region, the bourgeoisie (뿌르조아) who do not lift a finger (十指不動) and the class who risk their lives for a ten-*chŏn* wage (十錢勞銀) have long felt the threat to their right to exist from the silkworms (蚕兒) greedy for food [metaphor for exploiters]. Generally, human nature is such that the greater the pressure from above, the stronger the pressure applied below—how much more so in that time and place! Currently, the Hŏnmun Yanghaeng Automobile Stock Company (韓文洋行 自動車株式會社), headed by Mr. Mun Myŏnggi (文明琦君), since its opening last July, has made no small contribution by shortening the distance between Taegu (大邱), P'ohang, and Yŏngdŏk (盈德), and between P'ohang and Kuryongp'o (九龍浦), which had caused immense hardship economically and temporally. Simultaneously, the company's own profits are said to be substantial.
Furthermore, the P'ohang branch of the Taegu Bank (大邱銀行 浦項支店), within the short period of merely two years since its establishment, seems to have already gained authority comparable to that of the Chigin Bank branch, thanks to the thorough sincerity and vast creditworthiness of its staff. Thus, despite their meager capital (資力) and lack of backing as weaker parties, the fact that these two companies strive to break through the natural barriers of cliffs and precipices (斷崖絶壁) to pioneer a destiny, at least as Koreans, deserves boundless gratitude... however, the remaining ominous fog (怪霧) in the path of these gentlemen threatens to occasionally form peaks of doubt (九疑峯). Alas! The spirit of these gentlemen who must preserve their rock-solid integrity (江石)!

Wailing Ghosts Beneath Haksan (鶴山下)
The night of April 20th was a moment of extreme tragedy, enacting a catastrophe unseen since the opening of the East Sea. A great southeast storm that struck the entire east coast fiercely hit Yŏngil Bay in particular. The resulting death and injury of nearly 700 fishermen and sailors, who could never be compensated, and damages to vessels and fishing gear amounting to over 500,000 Won, instantly caused a major panic throughout the coastal market. Moreover, it is said that more than two-thirds of those lost at sea were all young Korean men between 20 and 30 years old. Among them, reportedly, were sole sons for three generations and those utterly alone (孑孑隻身). Should this be called fate? Or a sudden calamity (橫厄)? The sight of drifting corpses appearing unexpectedly for a long time made the fish markets (魚物廛) within the harbor desolate. Even viewed this way, the horror of that time is truly difficult to bear, making one shudder even now. Ah, the vast blue sea, the sky nearing dusk (萬頃碧海 欲暮天)—the sound of waves rising in the drizzling rain and cold wind endlessly laments their flawless (瑕玭업는) resentment, merely serving to break the hearts of passersby beneath Haksan (鶴山下).

P'ohang's Fortunes are Improving, But...

P'ohang is the starting point for the Korean Central Railway (朝鮮中央鐵道), which originates from Taegu (大邱), already branches left and right through the fertile fields of Hayang (河陽), Yŏngch'ŏn (永川), Kyŏngju (慶州), and Ulsan (蔚山), and intends to eventually connect to Pusan (釜山) <109>. It is the starting point for the automobile route between Taegu and Yŏngdŏk (盈德), running four times a day; the endpoint of the economic road passing through Taegu and Yŏngil (迎日), one of Kyŏngbuk's foremost; the connection point for the coastal road bringing goods by land and sea via Yŏngdŏk and Ch'ŏngha (淸河) from Kangwŏn Province (江原道); and again, a place where the regular routes of the Chosŏn Mail Steamer (朝鮮郵船) and fishing boats during the peak season converge. That is, sitting clearly on the shore of Yŏngil Bay at the mouth of the Hyŏngsan River (兄山江), it faces distant regions of mainland Japan such as Oki (隠岐) and Izumo (出雲). Its free transportation, abundant land products, and inexhaustible sea resources (海利) make it a naturally endowed commercial center that commands authority, and simultaneously the hidden treasury (天藏寶庫) of the East Sea. No, it is P'ohang that determines the rise and fall (消長) of Yŏngil. Behold, P'ohang of the herring (靑魚)... More than anything, the foundation of P'ohang's prosperity is the 4 million Won annual value of marine products... Among this, 2 million Won, representing half of the total marine product value, is the payment for herring imported from outside each year. (However, *last year, with its poor local catch (地凶漁), was not like that). It seems excessively novel that even the ilk of Song Yang (宋襄) [figure known for misplaced benevolence], who only know how to yield or have things taken by others, should have a day to taste such new beginnings (新生味).
In the past (住昔), this region was merely a humble fishing village. But starting about 30 years ago, influential Japanese land managers and other traders came over, seized the key to opening the port, and gradually brought about prosperity. Now, the number of Japanese residents is already close to over 500 households and more than 2,000 people, ranking second in Kyŏngbuk after Taegu. Furthermore, the average annual commercial transactions in this region exceed 10 million Won. Since the laying of the Central Railway (中鐵), the relationship with Pusan and Wŏnsan has greatly weakened, while the degree of closeness with Taegu has increased. However, as if this were not enough, there is the issue of opening a direct shipping route between P'ohang and Hamada (濱田) in mainland Japan, using the San'in Railway (山陰鐵道) to directly transport goods between Taegu and Kyoto (京都), thereby further expanding the economic sphere of both sides. Under this premise, the movement to establish the P'obin Steamship Company (浦濱汽船會社) with a capital of 500,000 Won, the harbor construction plan (築港設計) costing over 2 million Won, the waterworks installation (水道架設) costing over 60,000 Won, light railway improvements (輕鐵改良), and all sorts of new projects – there is not one among these that the local residents (住氏) do not pursue like a thirsty horse rushing to a spring (渴驥의 奔泉的)... But seeing how those with foresight (先見의 明) have even sharper nerves, wherever they perceive an advantage, they fiercely push their way in, preempt others (機先을 制하야), and concentrate power, one can sense how tense the atmosphere of development in this region is becoming... However, will this development truly be like the spring rain on mulberry and hibiscus (桑槿春雨), where the benefits are equally shared (均霑) without distinction? Is it really so? No matter what, the bourgeoisie (뿌르조아) holding privileges worth tens of thousands (萬本位), and attached beneath them, the ill-fated Paedal laborers (倍達役軍) of over 1,000 households who live by giving their boats and begging for entrails [idiom for exploitation]... Though late, is there or is there not the courage (義勇) from now on to gather even the strength used for suckling, to urgently relieve their hunger and clearly answer whether there are many or few true friends (知己) who will help their efforts? <110>

Changgi's (長鬐) Kuryongp'o (九龍浦)
Kuryongp'o is an indispensable key port (要港) not just for Greater Yŏngil (大迎日) but for the southern Chosŏn region. The current Changgi Port (長鬐港) in Ch'angju-myŏn (滄洲面) was created precisely to open up this key port. Furthermore, because the water within the bay is deep and suitable for sheltering from wind damage from the east, north, and west, during the autumn and winter seasons each year, 600-700 vessels and 3,000-4,000 fishermen gather densely (蝟集). The prosperity of brothels and taverns (靑樓酒肆) reaches its peak, and the scene within the harbor transforms entirely into a world of wealth and spring blossoms (富貴春花). Consequently, government officials and private citizens (官民有志) who realized the need for harbor construction (築港) in this port, which had been left to laissez-faire, finally united and resolved to begin a major construction project costing a total of 350,000 Won – comprising 300,000 Won from national funds (國費) and 100,000 Won from provincial funds (道費) as subsidies, plus 120,000 Won borne by the *myŏn* (面). This project will continue for the next two years, and the groundbreaking ceremony was finally held this past April 16th. When this is completed, how could it merely end as a point of pride for Kyŏngbuk!

Yŏnil's (延日) Historic Sites (舊蹟)

While Yi Sung'in's (李崇仁) *Sin Sŏng Ki* (新城記) states that Yŏnil was "a strategic point for the comings and goings of sea pirates (海寇往來之衝)," Yŏnil's scenic beauty (形勝) and historical remains (遺跡) still have commendable aspects. The Irwŏl Pond (日月池) currently located in Irwŏl-tong (日月洞), Och'ŏn-myŏn (烏川面), is a famous pond with curious historical tales. During the reign of King Adalla (阿達羅王) of Silla, Se'o-nyŏ (細烏女), the wife of Yŏngo-rang (迎烏郞) who was gathering seaweed on the East Sea coast, was accidentally carried away by waves and drifted ashore on an eastern island of Japan (日本 東島). When her husband heard this, he went into that island. After that, both the sun and moon lost their light (日月이 모다 無光함). The king sent an envoy, earnestly requesting their return, but the two ultimately did not comply. Instead, they gave silk cloth they had woven themselves, instructing that annual rites be performed for them (致祭) by the Irwŏl Pond. After the envoy returned, on the day of the closing feast (晦宴), the light of the sun and moon became bright again. Thus, it is said that the name of the pond and the origin of the current county name both stem from this event.
Furthermore, Kujŏng-dong (舊政洞) (formerly Munch'ung-dong 文忠洞) below Unje Mountain (雲梯山下) in the same *myŏn* is famous as the birthplace of the renowned Koryŏ sage Chŏng Mong-ju (鄭夢周) (pen name P'oŭn 圃隱). The foundation stones (廟基) and cornerstone (礎石) of his shrine still exist clearly. Additionally, the Taewang Rock (大王巖) on the peak of this Unje Mountain has a clear, cool jade spring (玉泉) gushing out from crevices in the rock. It is said to instantly cure chronic illnesses (宿病) for those who try drinking it once. Moreover, there is a superstitious story (미신의 이약이거리) that during years of severe drought (旱魃), performing a rain prayer ceremony (기우제) to this rock god (岩神) yields infallible spiritual efficacy (靈感의 効).

Hŭnghae's (興海) Puksong-jŏng (北松亭)
In the past (往昔), Hŭnghae was the great county of Uich'ang (義昌大郡), and both Changgi (長鬐) and Yŏnil (延日) were its subordinate prefectures (領縣). However, the scenic grandeur (形勝) of that time has long since become insignificant. The place of song and dance (歌舞地) on the Kokkang River (曲江), where people competed with flutes and drums (笳鼓) while boating in the spring waters, now only retains the mournful strains of a single song: "Farewell, Mangch'ang Mountain (望昌山)! Let us meet again, Kokkang waters (曲江水)!" The Namjang-dae pavilion (南將臺), from which one could gaze upon the lofty tower (危樓望辰), has become a schoolhouse (글방) for Japanese children (日本 兒孩). <111> Thus, what were called the Seven Scenic Views of Osan (鰲山七景) have all become like this, only serving as sources of grief (傷心거리) for those who hear and see them... However, the 10-*ri* long forest of Puksong-jŏng (北松亭) still stands steadfastly guarding the remaining ramparts (殘廓), seeming to boast of the southern scenery. According to the inscription on the Pukch'ŏn-su Relic Stele (北川藪遺蹟碑) standing by the main road in Puksong-dong (北松洞), Songjŏng indeed has an unusual history. A certain Yi Tŭkkang (李得江), who achieved meritorious service (明治의 績) as the magistrate (倅) of Hŭnghae, cultivated this grove (藪林) and eradicated the flooding from the North Stream (北川), which had been a chronic affliction (固疾) for the entire town each year. Thus, this Songjŏng truly seems to possess a value that cannot be exchanged even for an impregnable fortress (金城湯池). Nevertheless, despite this, recently during the Kabo year [1894], the tax collector (聚歛吏) An Chong-dŏk (安鍾德), seizing the opportunity of a temporary poor harvest (歉農), under the pretext of famine relief (飢饉救濟), felled a portion of this pine forest to fill his personal coffers (私腹). Since then, the beautiful old state of a thousand years (千古美) can no longer be seen. Witnessing this face today makes this reporter's heart ache deeply.

Ch'ŏngha's (淸河) Famous Products and Public Common School
Hong Yŏbang's (洪汝方記) description, "To the east, it presses against the vast blue sea with myriad waves; to the west, it borders layered peaks with thousand forms of clouds and mist (東壓滄溟波濤萬頃, 西竝疊嶽雲霞千態)," depicts the scenic beauty of Ch'ŏngha. Seen this way, the overall charm (韻致) of this region indeed combines the grandeur of mountains and water. Looking separately at Pogyŏng Temple (寶鏡寺) in Naeyŏn Mountain (內延山), Songna-myŏn (松羅面), the surroundings within the mountain are secluded and elegantly tranquil (幽邃閑雅). Along the winding path like a sheep's intestine (羊腸), there are numerous meandering streams (曲折만흔 溪流), sculpted fantastic rocks (조각적인 奇岩, 怪石), and hundred-foot waterfalls (飛流百尺) scattering against rock corners, transforming into rainbows (彩虹). The attractive force (牽引力) of such natural beauty seems to intensify with closer contact. Furthermore, inside the temple, the Chŏkkang Hall (寂光殿) eternally reflects in the mirror-like surface of the stone pond (石潭中鏡面), containing the remaining crystallized mass of Abbot Wŏnjin's (圓眞和尙) devoted energy (結晶된 정력의 덩어리). Also, on the signboard (額面) of the temple's main gate, one saw calligraphy of a *Ch'unmyŏn-gok* (Spring Sleep Song, 春眠曲) verse personally written (親筆) in large characters by King Sukchong (肅宗大王) of the Yi Dynasty. (The inscribed door panel is now secretly stored within the temple grounds.) Seeing this, one can infer that the temple's historical significance is considerable. Ah, how could Ch'ŏngha's famous features be satisfied with just this!
Ch'ŏngha Public Common School (淸河公立普通學校)... The playground of this school was created by filling in the site of the guesthouse (客舘) after Ch'ŏngha ceased to be a county seat (廢邑), transforming it into a large square. Standing on the square with the lingering scenery (殘景) of Haewŏl Pavilion (海月樓) at one's back, gazing eastward at the vast sea (滄溟), the flavor (風味) is beyond description by brush or tongue (筆舌이 俱絶). Densely packed ships seem to run ashore, the clear morning sun seems to rise from one's toes... All such vivid and lively charms (活情趣) feel as if they could not be found anywhere else but here. Moreover, as a public school, this institution has the distinction of being managed solely by Korean staff, setting a precedent (稿矢) in Kyŏngbuk. The vibrant energy filled with affection from the over 300 boys and girls, and the close fellowship (綿密한 交誼) between parents and staff, create a distinct feeling akin to forming an ideal new family (恰然이 이상적 新家庭). Even looking at this one matter, the lesson (敎) from reality (降話) is that Korean children's education should be handled by Koreans <112>, which is true education (眞敎育) reflecting humanity itself. How can those currently in charge (當局한 者) not take deep heed (鑑戒) of this?

Youth Groups and Pernicious Factions (蠹惑派)
The number of youth groups in this region is almost one per two *myŏn*, compared to other counties. Currently, through the seven locations of P'ohang, Hŭnghae, Kokkang, Ch'ŏngha, Songna, Yŏnil, and Ch'angju, 500 able-bodied youths (健兒) are joining hands and actively working. Regardless of the success or failure of their planned endeavors, it is likely true that they wish to quickly improve their own standing in line with the great destiny of the mountains and rivers (山河大運). Despite this, groups singing praises of the Son of Heaven at Kyeryong [Mountain] (鷄龍의 天子), those dreaming of immortals on Changsŏng Mountain (長城山), corrupt elements trying to revive the malpractices of the Sŏwŏn (書院의 末弊), or demonic figures (鬼面) who roam around execution grounds (夭*場) [meaning unclear, possibly places of ill-repute or superstition] with the debauched followers (荒淫輩) of Sobong (小峯)—these are growing rampant daily, spreading poison (酖毒) in the world of new thought. This cannot simply be viewed as a fire across the river (對岸의 火災視). Rise up! Young comrades! Who else will determine the fate of this region!

Yi Pu-ja and Kim Paksa
Recently, it is said there are two eccentric figures (二 奇人) within Yŏngil County. One is an old man named Yi Kyu* (李圭*) (style name Sŏkkok 石谷) living in Sŏk-tong (石洞), Tonghae-myŏn (東海面), who is not only versed in astronomy, geography, medicine, and divination (卜筮), but is particularly said to cure all illnesses with just aconite (附子). The other is a young man around 30 named Kim Yŏngŭi (金永毅) living in Taejin-dongni (大津洞里), Pongsan-myŏn (埄山面), who has not only traveled throughout the Eight Provinces of Kyerim (鷄林八道) but has also dabbled in both old and new literature (新舊文學), and moreover, is said to have special talents in astronomy, geography, mathematics (數理), and music. It is said that the theories of geocentrism (天動地靜說) and fatalism (運命論), recently popular in various southern regions, were all advocated by these two men. (June 15th, at Ch'ŏngha Guesthouse)

**Summary**

This 1923 editorial from *Kaebyŏk* magazine portrays the Yŏngil region, centered around the burgeoning port of P'ohang, as a potential "Great City of the East Coast." It details the administrative reorganization, geography, and economic drivers, particularly the lucrative herring fishery which attracts both Korean and Japanese interests. The article highlights infrastructure developments like irrigation projects, railways, roads, and harbor construction (P'ohang, Kuryongp'o), alongside the establishment of fisheries associations and experiment stations, largely driven by Japanese capital and presence. However, it contrasts this development with the harsh realities faced by Koreans: exploitation of labor, the devastating impact of a storm on Korean fishermen, the growing social stratification between Japanese/bourgeoisie and Korean workers, and anxieties about cultural identity amidst modernization and Japanese influence. The author expresses hope for Korean-led initiatives like the Hŏnmun Automobile Company and Taegu Bank branch, praises a Korean-run school in Ch'ŏngha, laments the loss of historical heritage, and calls on youth groups to counter superstition and work for the region's true progress. For historians, the article offers a valuable glimpse into colonial Korea's regional development dynamics, the complex interplay of Japanese capital and Korean labor/resources, social tensions, environmental disasters, cultural anxieties, and nascent Korean entrepreneurial efforts within the colonial structure. The specific data on fisheries, population, infrastructure projects, and named individuals/organizations provide concrete details about the period.

靑魚의 迎日灣
迎日灣은 迎日灣이라함 보담 차라리 靑魚港이라고 하여야 적절타 하겟다. 무엇에나 속일 수 업는 것은 數字이다. ＜106＞ 이로써 이 항만이 우리에게 얼마나 이윤을 주는가를 그리여 보자. 本年 3月 현재 조사표를 보면 鯟壺網의 定置漁場이 朝鮮人 58, 日本人 65, 조합 19, 組合外 27, 합계 169箇所가 되고 鯟擧揮罹網이 65統, 雇主가 170名(內朝鮮人 약간) 종업인원 4,180인(內日本人 약 50인)이나 된다. 종래 이 靑魚는 전부 朝鮮內 각지에 한하야 소화되얏스나 금년부터는 日本內地에 까지 판로를 擴張하야 良績을 擧하야 옴으로 금후 더욱 더욱 이 방면으로 주력을 집중하랴고 한다. 이제 과거 5年間 어획수를 기록하면 이러하다.

水産會와 水産試驗場
旣往 10年 前에 비하야 8倍 5分으로 全鮮 中 제3位되는 550여 만圓이라는 東海 全沿岸 54里에 대한 재작년의 漁獲高는 실로 本道 수산업의 개량발달을 촉진케 하는 好機會를 주엇다. 그리하야 朝鮮 水産會를 산출하는 道水産會와 道水産試驗場의 설치가 모다 迎日灣을 중심으로 하얏슴이 우연이 아니다. 現下 진행되야오는 양방사업의 槪況을 듯건댄 水産會는 관청과 당업자 간에 介在하야 자치적 정신을 장려하는 공공기관이라 한다. 11人의 의원과 10人內外의 役員을 두고 一般講話, 漁村調査, 編網傳習 등의 지도 及 此에 伴한 각종시설에 관한 사무를 집행하고 회비 중 漁業割 又는 營業割의 부과액은 회원의 漁獲高, 製造高 又는 取扱高에 基하야 최고 10萬圓 이상 최저 100圓미만까지의 29等에 分賦收入케 하얏다.

다시 同面 雲梯山下 舊政洞(舊 文忠洞)은 高麗 名賢 鄭夢周(圃隱)의 탄생지로 유명한 곳이다. 지금 선생의 廟基와 초석의 존재가 完然하고 다시 이 雲梯山頂에 잇는 大王巖은 淸冽한 玉泉이 岩沏 사이로 湧出하야 일차 試飮者로 하야금 宿病을 쾌유케 한다하고 더구나 旱魃이 심한 歉年을 당하야 이 岩神에게 기우제를 행하기만 하면 백발백중 靈感의 効를 엇는다는 일종 미신의 이약이거리도 잇서온다.

興海의 北松亭
往昔의 興海는 義昌大郡으로 長鬐, 延日이 모다 이 郡의 領縣이 되얏섯다. 그러나 당시의 形勝은 어느덧 발서 보잘 것 업시 되얏다. 春水의 배노리로 笳鼓를 다토던 曲江의 歌舞地도 『가노라 望昌山아 다시 보자 曲江水야』 구슬푼 一曲의 노래만 남겨오고 危樓望辰의 南將臺는 日本 兒孩들의＜111＞ 글방이 되고 말엇다. 이러케 鰲山七景이라든 것은 모다 이 모양으로 도리여 듯고 보는 이로 하야금 傷心거리만 될 뿐이다... 만은 그래도 北松亭의 10里長林은 의연히 殘廓을 수호하야 남방의 風光을 자랑하는 듯 하다. 지금 北松洞 대로변에 서잇는 北川藪遺蹟碑의 글을 보면 松亭은 과연 남다른 역사를 가지고 왓다. 일즉이 興海倅로 明治의 績을 일우는 李得江이라는 이가 이 藪林을 양식하야 매년 一邑의 固疾이 되야오던 北川의 水害를 근절하얏다. 그리하야 이 松亭이얌말로 金城湯池로도 밧글 수 업는 가치가 잇서 뵈인다. 그러나 이러함도 불구하고 최근 甲午年間에 聚歛吏 安鍾德은 일시 歉農의 機를 이용하야 飢饉救濟의 美名下에서 이 송림의 일부를 斫伐하야 개인의 私腹을 충족한 이래 다시 千古美의 舊態를 못보게 되얏슴은 이 얼골을 대하는 금일 記者의 가슴이 너무나 쓰리고 앏흐다.

淸河의 명물과 公立普通學校
洪汝方記에 『東壓滄溟波濤萬頃, 西竝疊嶽雲霞千態』가 곳 淸河의 形勝을 그린 것이다. 이로써 보면 이 지방전체의 韻致는 과연 山과 水의 장관을 兼有얏다. 이제 따로이 松羅面 內延山의 寶鏡寺를 보면 山內의 四圍가 幽邃閑雅한 속에 羊腸과 가튼 沿道에 曲折만흔 溪流, 조각적인 奇岩, 怪石, 岩角에 飛散하야 彩虹으로 變幼하는 飛流百尺의 瀑布. 이러한 天然勝의 牽引力은 접촉할사록 강도를 가하는 것 갓다. 다시 寺內의 寂光殿은 영원이 石潭中鏡面에 밝아잇는 圓眞和尙의 結晶된 정력의 덩어리가 남아잇슴과 또 사원 정문의 額面에 李朝 肅宗大王이 春眠曲 一首를 大書한 친필의 글시가 잇슴을 보앗다. (지금은 題字한 門板을 원내에 密藏하얏다) 이것을 보면 이 사원의 유적이 적지 안음을 斟酌하겟다. 아아 淸河의 명물 엇지 이것으로써 만족하랴.
淸河公立普通學校... 이 학교의 운동장은 淸河가 廢邑된 이후 客舘의 基地를 埋築하야 대 광장으로 化하얏다. 등에 海月樓의 殘景을 지고 광장 우에 서서 동으로 滄溟을 眺望하는 풍미는 筆舌이 俱絶이다. 簇立한 선박은 岸上으로 주행, 선명한 朝日은 足尖으로부터 소사오르는 듯... 그럴듯한 모든 活情趣는 이곳이 아니면 다시 어더 볼 수 업슬 것가티 늣기여진다. 더구나 공립으로서의 이 학교는 慶北의 稿矢되는 朝鮮人 직원뿐만의 관리하는 특색이 잇는 학교이다. 3백餘의 남녀아동의 애정에 넘치는 생기와 父兄과 직원사이의 綿密한 交誼는 恰然이 이상적 新家庭을 조직함과 가튼 남다른 늣김이 생긴다. 一事만 보아도 朝鮮人 아동교육은 朝鮮人이라야 할 것이 降話의 敎이오＜112＞ 즉 인간성 그대로의 眞敎育이다. 目下 이 일에 當局한 者 엇지 깁히 鑑戒할 바 아니랴.

청년단체 蠹惑派
이 지방의 청년단체의 수는 타군에 비하야 거의 2面 1箇所가 된다. 現下 浦項, 興海, 曲江,  淸河, 松羅, 延日, 滄洲의 7箇所를 통하야 500의 健兒가 잇서 손을 잡고 활동하는 중이다. 그들의 豫期한 사업의 성패는 엇더턴지 하로밧비 山河大運에 迎合되는 자체의 지위를 향상하야 보랴함은 사실일 것이다. 이리함도 불구하고 鷄龍의 天子를 謳歌하는 무리, 長城山의 신선을 꿈꾸는 者流가 아니면 書院의 末弊를 복흥하랴는 부패물, 小峯의 荒淫輩로 더부러 夭*場으로 도라단니는 鬼面들이 逐日熾盛하야 新 사상계에 酖毒을 줌은 아모리 하야도 그냥 對岸의 火災視할 수 업다. 蹴起하여라. 청년제군아. 이 지방의 운명을 좌우할 이 그 누구이랴!

李附子와 金博士
요사이 迎日郡內에는 二 奇人이 잇다고 한다. 一人은 東海面 石洞에 사는 李圭*(號石谷)이라는 노인으로 천문, 地里, 의약, 卜筮에 全通할 뿐 아이라 특히 附子 한 가지로써 만병을 통치한다 하고 一人은 埄山面 大津洞里에 사는 金永毅라는 30歲 內外의 若年으로 鷄林八道를 踏破치 안은 곳이 업고 또한 新舊文學에 涉獵치 못한 것이 업는 데다가 더구나 천문, 지리 , 수리, 음악 등의 特長이 잇다고 한다. 최근 남방각지에 유행되는 天動地靜說과 운명론이라는 것은 모다 이 두 사람의 提唱한 바라한다.(6월15일 淸河客舍에서)
