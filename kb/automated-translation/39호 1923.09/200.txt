變局
The so-called Naval Treaty and the Four-Power Treaty, products of the Washington Conference (華府會議), completed the exchange of ratification procedures among the related powers (namely Britain, America, France, and Italy) on August 17th, becoming effective immediately on that day. With this, the Anglo-Japanese Alliance is completely abrogated, and Britain and America have stuck firmly together. What is even stranger is that, while on the one hand talking about naval limitations and such, Britain has invested 100 million Yen in one go to establish a new naval base in Singapore, and America has budgeted a new expenditure of 220 million Yen this time to expand naval bases on the Pacific coast, starting with Hawaii (布哇) and others. What kind of calculation for ruin are they making?

Britain and America, especially Britain, harboring ambitions to check Japan's superior position regarding China and seize interests within China as the masters themselves, used the pretext of the recent so-called Lincheng Incident (臨城縣事件) to propose the "China Railway Guard Plan" (中國鐵道警備案), an案 almost amounting to foreign management of Chinese railways, taking a hardline stance, but Japan alone did not agree to this. This is not because Japan has any particular benevolence (恩誼) towards China, but because if it were done that way, and all of China's state-owned railways effectively fell under foreign management, it would truly become a major obstacle, no, a threat, to Japan's actions towards China (對中 行動). How will this ultimately turn out?

It needs no redundant explanation (贅言) that China's anti-Japanese fever (排日熱) is one of China's great specialties, but it reached an even higher pitch (高調) due to the earlier Changsha Incident (長沙事件). However, according to recent news, the Zhili Clique (直隷派), which was reputed to be the instigator (嗾使者) of anti-Japanese sentiment, has decided to employ pro-Japanese principles as a means to develop a political situation centered on themselves, and reportedly first arrested the ringleader (首魁) of the anti-Japanese movement, Lu Yongting (陸榮廷). Being the doings (所爲) of the Chinese Warlord Factions (軍閥派), who do not hesitate to do anything for the expansion of their own power, there is no reason they couldn't resort to even more extreme measures. However, the anti-Japanese fever of the blameless (瑕疵업는) Chinese populace will likely only intensify with such events. What do they intend to do about the aftermath?

The Russian government, which attempted the third round of Russo-Japanese negotiations (日露會商) by sending Mr. Joffe (욧페) to see what could be worked out with Japan, seemed dissatisfied with the Japanese government's dithering negotiations, abruptly recalled Mr. Joffe, breaking off (中折) those talks. This time, they dispatched Mr. Karakhan (카라한), who smartly bypassed Japan and is attempting negotiations with the Chinese government, where it seems they are being received very favorably. If they continue to isolate Japan like this, what do they intend to do in the end? <129>

**Summary**

This article discusses the significant shifts in international relations in East Asia following the Washington Conference. The formal end of the Anglo-Japanese Alliance and the strengthening US-UK relationship, coupled with naval build-ups disguised as limitations (UK in Singapore, US in the Pacific), signal a changing power dynamic. Britain and the US are maneuvering to curtail Japanese influence in China, using incidents like Lincheng to propose increased foreign control over Chinese railways, a move Japan opposes fearing it would hinder its own ambitions. Meanwhile, China's internal politics see factions like the Zhili Clique potentially shifting stances on Japan for political gain, despite persistent popular anti-Japanese sentiment. Russia, frustrated with Japan, bypasses Tokyo to negotiate directly with China, further isolating Japan. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it highlights Japan's increasing diplomatic isolation and the volatile geopolitical environment surrounding the Korean peninsula. The instability in China and the shifting alliances among major powers directly impacted the context within which Japan governed Korea and how Korean independence movements might perceive international opportunities or threats.

變局
華府會議의 산물인 소위 海軍條約 及 4國條約은, 8월 17일로써 關係列國(즉 英, 米, 佛, 伊)이 批準 교환수속을 종료하야, 卽日로써 효력이 발생되게 되엿다. 이로써 日英의 동맹은 완전히 廢棄되고, 英米의 양국은 아조 딱 붓고 마럿다. 더구나 괴상한 것은, 海軍制限이니 무엇이니 하는 한 쪽으로, 英國은 一擧 1億圓을 投하야, 新嘉坡에 해군근거지를 신설하고, 米國은 布哇 기타를 爲始한 태평양연안의 해군근거지를 확장키 위하야, 今次 2억2천만圓의 新支出을 豫算하엿다. 대체 엇더케덜 亡하자는 酬酌이냐.
中國에 대한 日本의 우월권을 制하고 자기네가 主로서 中國 안 이권을 점하려 하는 野心을 有한 英, 米 특히 英國은 先者의 소위 臨城縣事件을 언저리로 하야, 「中國鐵道警備案」이라는, 거의 中國鐵道 外人管理에 갓가š´ 案을 제출하기로 하고, 태도-强硬한데, 日本이 호올로 이에 동의치 아니하엿다. 이것은 日本이 남달리 中國에 대한 恩誼가 잇서 그런 바도 아니오, 그와 가티 하야, 中國의 국유철덀„ 전부가 사실상으로 외국인의 관리에 돌아간다 하면, 그야말로 日本의 對中 行動에 대한 일대 障碍, 아니 威脅이 되는 故이다. 결국 이 엇지 될는가.
中國의 排日熱은 中國의 명물 중 일대 명물인 것은 贅言할 바도 아니나, 그것은 先者의 長沙事件으로 해서 一層 高調되엿다. 그런데 최근의 소식을 접하면 排日의 嗾使者인 稱이 잇던 直隷派에서, 自家中心의 정국을 전개하는 一種 수단으로 親日主義를 쓰기로 하고, 먼저 排日首魁인 陸榮廷을 逮捕하엿다 한다. 自家의 세력확장을 위하야는, 아모 짓이라도 사양치 안는 中國 軍閥派의 所爲이라, 그 보다 더한 수단이라도 못할 理이야 업겟지만, 아모러한 瑕疵업는 中國民衆의 排日熱은, 그러한 일이 잇슬스록 더 심해 갈지라. 그 뒤의 일을 엇지할 양인고.
日本과 엇지 엇지 해 볼가하야, 「욧페」씨를 보내여 第3次의 日露會商을 試하던 露國의 정부는, 日本정부의 흘정글정하는 교섭에 셩이 차지 안턴 모양이던지, 一朝에 욧페씨를 召喚하야, 그 교섭을 中折하고, 이번에는 또 「카라한」씨를 派送하야, 쓱 한번 日本을 에돌나 노코 中國 정부와 교섭을 試하는데 中國에서는 매우 조하들하는 모양이다. 이러케들 日本 하나만 따돌리기로 하면, 나죵은 엇더케들 하쟈는 말인가.＜129＞
