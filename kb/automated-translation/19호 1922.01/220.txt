金잔듸 (小曲) (外)
						素月 (Sowŏl)

金잔듸. (Golden Lawn)

Lawn,
Lawn,
Golden lawn.
In the deep, deep mountains and streams, the faint light is
The golden lawn by the departed lover's grave.
Spring has come, even to the tip of the willow branch
Spring light has come, spring days have come,
Even to the deep, deep mountains and streams, even to the golden lawn.

꿈. (Dream)

They say even chickens, dogs, and beasts
Have dreams, don't they?
That's right, spring days are for dreaming.
Would there be a dream in my body?
Ah, the end of my world,
I long for a dream, I long for a dream.

첫 치마. (First Skirt)

Spring departs on a closing day,
Flowers fade in the closing spring,
Weeping senselessly for the falling flowers,
Feeling senselessly the departing spring.
Clinging to the branch where flowers fell and leaves dropped,
Weeping like mad, on a closing spring day,
The skirt worn for the first time on the body,
Soaked with tears on a closing spring day,
Holding it alone, aimlessly,
The newly married woman (出嫁女) cries sorrowfully.

엄마야 누나야. (Mommy, Sister)

Mommy, sister, let's live by the riverside,
In the yard, the sparkling golden sand light,
Outside the back gate, the song of the reeds,
Mommy, sister, let's live by the riverside.

달마지. (Moon Greeting)

Greeting the moon on the fifteenth day of the first month,
Let's go greet the moon, meet the moon.
Putting on brand new clothes <35>
But the old sorrow remains in the heart.
Let's go greet the moon, neighbors,
When the moonlight shows on the mountain top, on the water's surface.
Let's go back, neighbors,
When the morning star, the three stars, fall.
Let's go greet the moon, meet the moon,
To the grave of the dead old friend,
Let's go greet the moon on the fifteenth day of the first month.

개암이. (Hazelnut Tree / Ant?)
*Contextually, "개암이" likely refers to an ant (개미), given the description of building a house diligently, rather than a hazelnut tree.*

When the azaleas bloom,
And the wind cries in the willow branches,
Even on a day like this, the ant,
The slender-waisted ant,
Absorbedly, diligently, builds its house.

제비. (Swallow)

Even the swallow flying high in the sky
Has a fixed nest it comes and goes from, returning,
How can I not be sorrowful, a body without even a home!

樹芽. (Tree Bud)

It's no ordinary sorrowful spring!
On every tree branch, buds have sprouted,
Spring has come to my heart too,
And is now about to sprout buds.

부헝새. (Owl)

Last night, an owl,
Came and cried outside the back gate,
Today, even over the sea, the clouds are dark,
A day without seeing the sun is already drawing to a close.

黃燭 불. (Yellow Candle Light)

Yellow candle light, so dimly,
Leaning against the fading blue window
In the white night without even sound,
I alone press my face to the mirror,
And gaze into it, without purpose, without thought.
I say, "Our people,
Let the first night be spent in dreams,
And let death come during slumber,
Thus flowing away without a cough." <36>
<35-36>

**Summary**

This collection of ten short poems by Kim Sowŏl, published in the January 1922 issue of *Kaeppyŏk* magazine, explores themes of nature, longing, sorrow, dreams, home, and the passage of time, often intertwining personal feelings with observations of the natural world. The poems evoke a melancholic and lyrical mood, characteristic of Sowŏl's style, using simple language and imagery like golden lawns, spring buds, riversides, swallows, and candlelight. From a historical perspective, these poems, written during the early Japanese colonial period, are interesting for their expression of deep-seated Korean sentiments (han) and attachment to traditional imagery and landscapes (riverside, moon greeting customs, graves). While not overtly political, the pervasive sense of loss, yearning for home ("엄마야 누나야," "제비"), and melancholy ("꿈," "첫 치마," "부헝새") could reflect the underlying national mood of dispossession and sorrow under colonial rule, offering historians insight into cultural expression and emotional landscapes of the time through the lens of one of Korea's most celebrated poets. The use of early modern Korean orthography is also significant for linguistic history.

金잔듸 (小曲) (外)

金잔듸.

잔듸,
잔듸,
금잔듸.
深深山川에 바알한 불빗은
가신 님 무덤 가엣 金잔듸.
봄이 왓네, 버들가지 끗에도
봄빗이 왓네, 봄날이 왓네,
深深山川에도, 金잔듸에도.

꿈.

닭 개 즘생조차도 꿈이 잇다고
니르는 말이야 잇지 안은가.
그러하다, 봄날은 꿈 뀔때.
내 몸에야 꿈이나 잇스랴,
아아 내 世上의 끗이여,
꿈이 그리워, 꿈이 그리워.

첫 치마.

봄은 가나니 저믄 날에,
꼿은 지나니 저믄 봄에,
속 업시 우나니 지는 꼿을,
속 업시 느끼나니 가는 봄을.
꼿 지고 닙 떨닌 가지를 붓안고
미친 듯 울면서, 저믄 봄날에,
몸에도 처음 감은 치마를
눈물로 적시는 저믄 봄날에
혼자서 잡고는 어이업시도
집난이(出嫁女)는 설어 울어라.

엄마야 누나야.

엄마야 누나야 江邊에 살자,
뜰에는 반짝이는 金 모래빗
뒷門 밧게는 갈닙의 노래,
엄마야 누나야 江邊에 살자.

달마지.

正月 대보름 날 달마지,
달마지 달마중을 가자고.
새라 새 옷은 갈아 닙고도＜35＞
가슴엔 묵은 설음 그대로.
달마중 가자 니웃 사람들
山 우에 水面에 달빗 보일 때.
돌아가자, 니웃 사람들,
모작별 三星이 떨어질 때.
달마지 달마중을 가자, 고
몸이 죽은 녯 동무 무덤가에
正月 大보름날 달마중 가자.

개암이.

진달내 꼿이 피고,
바람은 버들가지에서 울 때,
이러한 날 하루도 개암이는
허리 가느른 개암이는
골몰하게도 부즈런히도 집을 지여라.

제비.

한울 놉히 날아 단이는 제비의 몸으로도
一定한 깃을 두고 오가며 돌아오거든,
어찌 설지 안으랴, 집도 업는 몸이야!

樹芽.

웬만한 설은 봄은 아니여!
나무가지 가지마다 눈을 텃서라,
내 가슴에도 봄이 와서
只今 눈을 트랴고 하여라.

부헝새.

간 밤에 부헝새가 한마리,
뒷 門 밧게 와서 울더니,
오늘은 바다 우에도 구름이 캄캄,
해 못본 날 하로도 어느덧 저믈어 가네.

黃燭 불.

黃燭 불, 그리도 깜아케,
슬어저 가는 푸른 窓을 기대고
소리 조차 업는 흰 밤에,
나는 혼자 거울에 얼굴을 뭇고,
뜻 업시도 생각도 업시 드려다 보노라.
나는 니르노니, 「우리 사람들,
첫날 밤은 꿈 속으로 보내고
죽음은 조는 동안에 오아라,
이리하야 고흠도 업시 흘러가나니」＜36＞
