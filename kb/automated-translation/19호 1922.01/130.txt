Review of the Sin'yu Year (1921) (Part 2)
						By One Reporter

You may have seen, as is customary, the "Review of the Sin'yu Year" in the November issue of this magazine, and those who saw it likely commented on its insufficiency. The reporter also feels dissatisfied and cannot help but feel ashamed, but as the time period is one year and the events are complex, it is difficult to enumerate them, and furthermore, it is hard to easily discern which are important and which are minor. Regarding the articles for the latter half of the year in this issue, I have endeavored to exert more effort, so I hope you will look at it once more. Particularly, the "Postscript to the Review" attached at the end of this topic provides a general overview and commentary on the general situation that occurred during the Sin'yu year (1921) to serve as a reference for the public.

July

Great Flood in Kyŏngsŏng: Due to heavy rain from the 5th to the 7th, the vicinity of Kyŏngsŏng turned into a water kingdom, causing much road destruction and loss of houses. Similar heavy rains fell in the three southern provinces (Samnam), causing great damage.

Student Groups and Cultural Movement: Under the grand plan to build a new culture in Chosŏn and give the populace thorough self-awareness, our student groups, both domestic and abroad, which are the central bodies of knowledge and skill, initiated a major cultural movement starting this July. Through various means such as lectures, plays, music, and workshops, they engaged in extensive activities, showing results unseen before. Firstly, among the organizations of students studying abroad, the Lecture Team of the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Association Tokyo Branch, the Lecture Team of the Buddhist Youth Association, the Lecture Team of the Hak'uhoe (Student Friends Association), and the Drama Troupe of the Tong'uhoe (Comrade Association) launched major activities throughout the three thousand *ri* of Chosŏn, north, south, east, and west. Among domestic organizations in Chosŏn, the Student Congress (Haksŏng Taehoe), the Chosŏn Youth Association Federation, the Women's Education Association (Yŏja Kyoyukhoe), and the Kosaeng kaltophoe (Society to Help Struggling Students), among others, also pursued the same activities with the same spirit and purpose. Other local organizations also continued similar activities, busily engaging in the cultural movement.<72>

Execution of Ch'oe Kyŏnghak: Ch'oe Kyŏnghak, an extremist independence activist and the culprit behind the bombing of the Miryang Police Station, was executed at Taegu Prison at 3 PM on the 8th.

Electric Tram Fare Issue and Kyŏngsŏng Citizens' Outrage: Kyŏngsŏng citizens rose up furiously against the Electric Company's unfairness in merging the previous two zones within Kyŏngsŏng city from Yongsan into one zone with a 5 *chŏn* fare, while keeping the two zones for Masijang, Ch'ŏngnyangni, and Wangsimni. They denounced the unfairness to the Electric Company and campaigned for fare uniformity across all lines, but the company stubbornly refused.

World's First Female President: After the end of the European War, under the principle of national self-determination, a woman named "Sarma" was elected president of the newly established nation of "Assyria."

Mr. Sin Hŭngu Departs for Hawaii: To present Chosŏn's education issues at the Pan-Pacific Educational Conference held in Hawaii, USA, Mr. Sin Hŭngu departed Kyŏngsŏng on the 25th.

Mutual Aid Society's Consumer Cooperative: The facilities department of the Labor Mutual Aid Society (Nodong Kongjehoe) organized a consumer cooperative to promote the common interests of laborers.

Chosŏn People's Industrial Congress: The Industrial Congress, formed to conduct field surveys of Chosŏn's industrial conditions and promote the Chosŏn industrial world, held its inaugural general meeting at the Chosŏn Economic Association (Chosŏn Kyŏngjehoe) on the 30th, adopting rules and electing officers.

The Pacific Question and Nations' Bustle: At the word of US President Harding, nations began to stir. Arms limitation, the Far East question, etc., were truly major global issues. Focusing on these problems, nations were very busy deciding whether to participate or not.

Month's End Summary
This is the outline of July's events. Domestically, the cultural movement of student and youth groups was a matter of great value, and externally, public opinion regarding the Pacific question was the major issue.

August

Yang Kŭnhwan Sentenced to Life Imprisonment: Yang Kŭnhwan, who assassinated Min Wŏnsik at Tokyo Station on February 16th, received a life sentence from the Tokyo District Court on the 2nd.<73>

Execution of Pak Sangjin and Kim Hanjong: Pak Sangjin and Kim Hanjong, leaders of the Kwangboktan (Restoration Corps) incident, were executed at Taegu Prison at 1 PM on the 11th.

Great Unrest in India: The people of India, fervent in their independence movement and constantly dissatisfied with British rule, finally staged a major riot, sparked by unfair trials of political prisoners. There was much destruction of buildings and killing/injuring of people (near Aligarh on the 10th).

Min Yanggi Sentenced to Death: Min Yanggi, Ko Tuhwan, et al., who shot and killed Ŭllyul County Magistrate Ch'oe Pyŏnghyŏk, had their verdict finalized at the Haeju District Court on the 19th. Min was sentenced to death, and Ko to life imprisonment.

Northern Chosŏn Region and Troop Deployment: It has long been a fact that the Chosŏn Independence Corps is highly active in the forested areas near Changjin. To suppress this, the authorities finally dispatched troops at the request of Hamgyŏngnam-do Governor Yi Kyuwan.

Pan-Pacific Educational Conference and its Resolutions: The Pan-Pacific Educational Conference unanimously adopted 14 articles on the 18th and closed on the 19th. Its main points included: 1. Hoping that US President Harding succeeds in arms reduction, 2. That Pacific empires make world peace the ideal of education, and that each government promotes research on Pacific issues in its universities and facilitates the exchange of educational researchers, etc.

Naming Ceremony for the House of Yi Royal Family: The royal grandson born at Ch'angdŏk Palace on the 18th was named His Highness Yi Chin, and a naming ceremony was held at Ch'angdŏk Palace on the 24th.

Month's End Summary
Regarding world affairs, the issue of participation in the Pacific Conference continued, and other events included the North-South war in China and the unrest in India.

September

Oil Tycoon Enters Kyŏngsŏng: The world's wealthiest man, American oil tycoon Mr. Rockefeller and his party, entered Kyŏngsŏng on the 2nd.

Bomb Thrown at Government-General Building: At 10:20 AM on the 12th, two bombs were thrown consecutively at the Secretariat and Accounting sections of the Government-General building. One exploded, but the other did not. This caused a major commotion within the Government-General, and police authorities launched extensive activities.

Industrial Survey Committee: From the 15th to the 20th, the Industrial Survey Committee met within the Government-General building to discuss and decide on matters related to agriculture, commerce, industry, forestry, mining, fisheries, fuel, maritime transport, roads, rivers, etc.

Tragic Death of Over 1,000 German Workers: On the 11th, a major explosion occurred at the Aniline Dye Factory in Oppau, Germany, tragically killing over 1,000 workers <75> and injuring over 2,000 more in a great disaster.

Issue of Honorifics Among Children: The Kyemyŏng Club in Insadong, Kyŏngsŏng, passed a resolution to use honorific language instead of the disrespectful language traditionally used among children and submitted its proposal to the educational authorities.

Two Chosŏn Vice Consuls: Under the previously decided ordinance for appointing Chosŏn vice consuls, two were selected: Kim U'yŏng and Yang Chaeha took up their posts.

Bomb Thrown at Kapsan Police Substation: Around 20 members of the Independence Party threw a bomb at the Hamjŏng Police Substation in Tong'in-myŏn, Kapsan, at dawn on the 24th. One servant died instantly, and the substation was destroyed. Simultaneously, they set fire to the Forestry Station Branch Office, burning it down completely.

Major Strike by 5,000 Laborers: Over 5,000 laborers in Pusan attempted to negotiate wage increases with related companies. Ultimately, due to the companies' refusal, they decided on a general strike.

International Press Conference and the Tonga Ilbo: For the first time, the Tonga Ilbo represented the Chosŏn press world at the International Press Conference held in Honolulu, Hawaii, USA, starting October 11th. The Tonga Ilbo dispatched Mr. Kim Tongsong as its representative, who was later elected Vice President of the conference.

Assassination of Japanese Magnate Yasuda: Yasuda Zenjirō, a top Japanese wealthy man, was assassinated by an assailant on the morning of the 28th. The assailant was Asahi Heigo, a former newspaper reporter involved in the labor movement.

Month's End Summary
In September's events, the Government-General bombing incident is noteworthy, as is the assassination of Yasuda. Besides that, international issues primarily concerned the Pacific Conference.

October

Silent Prayer Meeting for Fallen Soldiers: US President Harding declared that on Armistice Day, November 11th, all American citizens should observe two minutes of silent prayer to console the spirits of soldiers who died in the Great European War.

Chosŏn Bar Association: Initiated by Messrs. Pak Sŭngbin, Chang To, Yi Sŭng'u, and others to respect the press and unify the legal profession, the inaugural general meeting of the Bar Association was held on the 2nd.

Great Fire at Namdaemun Market: On the night of the 12th, a large fire broke out at Namdaemun Market in Kyŏngsŏng, completely burning down the market and causing damages exceeding one million Won.

All-Chosŏn Tennis Tournament: Sponsored by the Chosŏn Education Association and supported by the Tonga Ilbo, the All-Chosŏn Tennis Tournament was held over three days, the 15th, 16th, and 17th, at the athletic field of Kyŏngsŏng Posŏng High School.

Major Dispute at Severance Medical College: The entire 3rd-year class of Severance Medical College in Kyŏngsŏng negotiated with school authorities regarding main points such as campus expansion and curriculum changes.<76> Failing to reach a satisfactory resolution, a major dispute arose between faculty and students, resulting in 12 students being expelled.

Release of Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn: Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn, owner of Kwangmunhoe publishing house, who drafted the Chosŏn Declaration of Independence and was imprisoned on March 1st of the year before last, spending three years in prison, was released on parole on the morning of the 19th.

President Sun's Military Expedition and the Three Provinces Alliance: President Sun Yat-sen of the Canton government embarked on a northern expedition. To counter this, the three provinces of Anhui, Hubei, and Jiangsu formed an offensive-defensive alliance (17th).

Society for Respecting the Aged (Kyŏngnohoe): The Kyŏngsŏng Girls' High School organized a Society for Respecting the Aged to cultivate virtue and etiquette as essentials of moral training and promote laudable social customs, showing respect to elders aged 60 and above (21st).

Education Fever and High School Establishment Drive: While Kyŏngsŏng, P'yŏngyang, Taegu, Ŭiju, Hamhŭng, and Chŏnju are places where high schools have already been established, the education fever has newly surged, and places enthusiastic about establishing high schools are the five provinces of Hwanghae, Ch'ungnam, Hambuk, Kyŏngnam, and Chŏnnam. Each has organized establishment committees and is actively campaigning. It can be anticipated that 5 or 6 high schools will appear in Chosŏn next year.

Preservation of Antiquities and Kyŏngju Citizens: Hearing that the antiquities of Kyŏngju, renowned worldwide and valuable for their historical and artistic merit, were to be moved to Kyŏngsŏng at the authorities' insistence, the citizens of Kyŏngju rose up furiously, organized an Antiquities Preservation Society, and resolved to keep these artifacts in Kyŏngju forever.

International Bar Association Conference: Aiming to protect human rights internationally and recognize international public law, the International Bar Association Conference was held in Beijing, China, from the 23rd to the 28th. From the Chosŏn side, there were 16 people, including Chang To, Yu Munhwan, Hŏ Hŏn, Pak Sŭngbin, and Kim Ch'anyŏng.

Calligraphy and Painting Association Bulletin Inaugural Issue: The inaugural issue of the bulletin, the organ of the Calligraphy and Painting Association representing Chosŏn's art world, was published on the 25th.

Death of An Sangik: An Sangik, one of the figures in the Kangso Sach'ŏn Incident of the year before last, who received a heavy sentence of 15 years, died of peritonitis in P'yŏngyang Prison.

All-Chosŏn Baseball Tournament: Hosted by the Chosŏn Sports Association, the All-Chosŏn Baseball Tournament was held starting on the 31st at the Kyŏngsŏng Middle School athletic field. Over 20 teams participated, competing for three consecutive days.

Month's End Summary
The main events of October, both globally and for Chosŏn groups, were the International Press Conference and the International Bar Association Conference. Additionally, as it was the sports season, the holding of various athletic competitions was the next significant aspect.

November<77>

Resolution of 40 Universities: Representatives from over 40 American universities, totaling more than 70 people, gathered at Princeton University on the 26th of last month. They resolved that to maintain future world peace, firstly, the Far East question must be resolved; secondly, current naval construction plans must be halted; and thirdly, future expansion must be prevented. They decided to submit this resolution to the Washington Conference.

19 Signers of the Declaration of Independence Released Upon Completion of Sentence: Messrs. Yi Chonghun, Hong Pyŏnggi, Pak Hŭido, Hong Kijo, Na Inhyŏp, Yu Yŏdae, Sin Sŏkku, Pak Chunsŭng, Na Yonghwan, Im Yerehwan, Kwŏn Pyŏngdŏk, Kim Wan'gyu, Ch'oe Sŏngmo, Kim Wŏnbyŏk, Yi P'ilchu, Pak Tongwan, Sin Hongsik, Yi Myŏngnyong, and Yang Chŏnbaek, who declared Chosŏn's independence on March 1st of the year before last and were involved in that movement, spending three years behind bars since then, were released on the 4th upon completing their sentences.

Prime Minister Hara Assassinated: Japanese Prime Minister Hara Takashi was assassinated by a 19-year-old youth named Nakaoka Kon'ichi at Tokyo Station on the evening of the 4th while en route to Kyoto.

Student Congress Grand Athletic Meet: For the first time in 10 years, over a thousand healthy youths participated vigorously at Changch'ungdan Park on the 6th.

National Holiday: The US Congress officially designated Armistice Day, November 11th, as a national holiday (2nd).

Pacific Conference Formally Opens: The Pacific Conference, upon which the eyes of all people were focused and global public opinion boiled, opened in Washington on the 12th.

Japan's New Prime Minister: It was decided that Takahashi Korekiyo would assume the post of Prime Minister, succeeding Mr. Hara (13th).

Birth Control and World Opinion: Due to the issue that land decreases while population increases, making world peace ultimately unattainable, the idea of restricting births, deeming population reduction necessary, became world opinion. This was discussed during one session of the Pacific Conference, and the Women's Committee of the American Birth Control League also submitted this to the Pacific Conference, campaigning for its success (11th).

Founding of the Mumyŏnghoe (Nameless Society): Figures from the Chosŏn newspaper and magazine worlds (the literary/journalistic sphere - 操觚界) initiated the Mumyŏnghoe to facilitate mutual understanding, promote friendship, spread culture, advance freedom of speech, and respect public opinion. The founding general meeting was held at Kyŏngsŏng Sikto'wŏn on the 27th.

Regency Announced: The Imperial Household Ministry announced on the 25th that His Highness the Crown Prince would assume the regency.

International Press Conference Far East Investigation Committee Enters the Capital: Mr. Glass and his party of 6, dispatched by the International Press Conference to investigate the press conditions in China and Chosŏn, arrived in Kyŏngsŏng on the 29th. They visited newspaper and magazine companies to conduct a field survey of Chosŏn's press world.<78>

Month's End Summary
November's events were entirely focused on the Pacific Conference. The proposals of various countries and the demands of myriad people were numerous and hard to enumerate. Among these, the assassination of Mr. Hara was a major event for Japan, and the birth of the Mumyŏnghoe can be called a new record for Chosŏn.

December

Left-Hand Traffic: The practice of walking on the right side was changed to walking on the left side, implemented starting from the 1st of this month (1st).

Chosŏn Language Research Society (朝鮮語硏究會): Initiated by Messrs. Kwŏn Tŏkkyu, Ch'oe Tuson, Chang Chiyŏng, and Im Kyŏngjae to research the Chosŏn language and script, the inaugural meeting was held at Hwimun High School on the 3rd.

Kyŏngsŏng Library Completed: The Kyŏngsŏng Library, longed for by the 300,000 citizens of Kyŏngsŏng, nay, the 20 million people of the Chosŏn nation, was declared complete in early December through the dedicated efforts and mediation of Mr. Yi Pŏmsŭng.

Irish Free State: The long-watched issue between Britain and Ireland was finally resolved amicably after consecutive meetings between British representative Lloyd George and the leader of the Irish Sinn Féin party. It was decided and announced that Ireland would become a self-governing dominion of the British Empire, enjoying the same freedoms and rights as British citizens.

Abolition of Extraterritoriality in China: As a result of the Washington Conference, the abolition of extraterritoriality concerning China was approved on the 10th.

Four-Power Pact Concluded: Japan, Britain, the US, and France, advocating for peace regarding Pacific and Far East issues, finally concluded the Four-Power Pact.

Freedom Song of Irish Political Prisoners: Irish political prisoners, who had fought hard for freedom and equality, were released en masse under a special amnesty by the British Empire following the Anglo-Irish agreement, loudly singing songs of freedom.

Ch'ŏndogyo Deliberative Council Opens: The Deliberative Council (Ŭijŏnghoe), the central organ of the Ch'ŏndogyo Church, held its first assembly meeting starting on the 10th.

Month's End Summary
Due to editorial constraints, only major events from the first part of December are recorded. Domestically, the switch to left-hand traffic was a major change, while externally, the Four-Power Pact was the focus of great attention. Ireland becoming a free state is also a very valuable event. December seems to be the month with the most events in the year.

Postscript to the Review

Sufficient or insufficient, the review of the Sin'yu year (1921) now comes to an end. There is nothing more to say, but feeling it might be too negligent, I intend to reiterate the main points.

Regarding world events in Sin'yu, anyone would point to the Pacific Conference <79> and the International Press Conference. Following that, there were several significant phenomena like the Pan-Pacific Educational Conference and the International Bar Association Conference. These need no further mention. Concerning individual countries, the death of Japan's Mr. Hara, China's North-South war, Britain's great strike, America's presidential transition, Russia's ideological conflict, and the Four-Power Pact between Japan, Britain, the US, and France were each major events. These also need no further elaboration. Now, I will speak of the actual events in Chosŏn that directly concern us, things done by us and experienced by us. Firstly, speaking from the positive aspect, one would say:
The surge of education fever and the sharp increase in students. The phenomenon during the spring enrollment period remains vivid in our minds. Furthermore, the addition of 3 or 4 educational institutions like Ŭiju High School, Chŏnju High School, and Kyŏngsŏng Second High School; the numerous elementary schools established throughout the Kyŏngsŏng area; and the high school establishment movements in Hwanghae, Ch'ungnam, Chŏnnam, Kyŏngnam, and Hambuk provinces are truly the best phenomena of this year and represent the newest aspect. Next is:
The cultural movement of youth groups. As everyone knows, how high did the cultural movement of youth groups reach throughout the Sin'yu year? Day by day, month by month, season by season, through lectures, music, plays, workshops, newspapers, or magazines, they continuously acted, constantly calling for a new culture. This is truly something for us to celebrate. Next is:
The increase in overseas students. The number of our students studying abroad in Tokyo, Japan, which was said to be 600 to 700 until last winter, reached 1,700 to over 2,000 this year – truly a major phenomenon. Additionally, the increase of 80 to 100 students in China; the studies in Germany by Messrs. Yu Ilchun, Yi Sŏngyong, Yi Sŏksin, Kim Chunyŏn, Pak Sŭngch'ŏl; Mr. Ch'oe Horyŏl's studies in Australia; and the studies in America by Messrs. Ku Chaok, O Ch'ŏnsŏk (others whose names are unknown) – these are also major facts for us to celebrate. Next, a fact that fills us with immense joy is:
The rise of the youth/children issue (少年問題). The youth issue, which we had hitherto treated with such indifference, could no longer be ignored, leading to the formation of many organizations centered around youth in various places. Thus, activities concerning the cultivation of youth character, intellect, and virtue were not few. Examples include the founding and activities of the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Association (소년회); the establishment and activities of the YMCA Youth Department; the activities concerning youth initiated by local youth associations in places like Ulsan, Anju, Chinju, etc.; and the Kyemyŏng Club's issue regarding honorific language for children – these are all new phenomena of this year. Also, many new kindergartens based on early childhood education were established. Kyŏngsŏng Kindergarten, Chung'ang Kindergarten, (last year) Ewha Hakdang Kindergarten Class are current examples. Seen this way, Chosŏn has become a New Chosŏn, that is, a Chosŏn of youth. Next is:
The promotion of the sports world (軆育界) brings us great joy. As everyone knows, the sports world in Sin'yu experienced truly great development. Setting aside the individual physical training of various groups and schools, the fact that the All-Chosŏn Baseball, All-Chosŏn Tennis, and All-Chosŏn Football tournaments were held not just once or twice clearly proves the achievements of our sports world.
Next, in the industrial world, the results are not poor either. The establishment of the Industrial Survey Committee, the emergence of economic organizations like consumer cooperatives and mutual aid societies, and the founding of new companies and factories show that the industrial world has also significantly advanced. However, if I were to speak meticulously, without omitting anything, there would be many things to mention. But keeping it concise to just the main points, let me finally mention one thing that we should write large and celebrate with dancing and joy. That is:
Our Chosŏn's rise in international status. This is truly a great fact for which our nation should raise both hands high and shout "Manse!" (Long Live!). The participation of Mr. Sin Hŭngu, representative of the Chosŏn education world, at the Pan-Pacific Educational Conference; the participation of 16 prominent figures, including Chang To, Hŏ Hŏn, Pak Sŭngbin, representatives of the Chosŏn legal world, at the International Bar Association Conference; and the participation of Mr. Kim Tongsong, representative of the Chosŏn press world, at the International Press Conference, where he attained the position of Vice President – these are truly the first auspicious events for our Chosŏn in 10 years. The Sin'yu year can indeed be called a great commemorative year for us. We must forever remember this Sin'yu year in our minds. However, approaching the end, I must add one more word on the negative side: the passing of three esteemed teachers, Messrs. Yu Kŭn, Chang Chiyŏn, and Yŏ Kyuhyŏng, who were pioneers in our literary, educational, and journalistic worlds and whose contributions were considerable. This is truly a great regret for us regarding Sin'yu.
With this much, we can say that Sin'yu, despite dissatisfactions, was spent well. Let us wait and see what the new Im Sul year (1922) will bring.<81>

**Summary**

This article reviews major events concerning Korea during the latter half of 1921 (the Sin'yu year), covering July through early December. It details natural disasters (floods), significant political events (executions, assassinations, arrests of independence activists), social issues (labor disputes, language reform), cultural and educational developments (student movements, establishment of schools and associations, international conference participation), and economic activities. The concluding "Postscript" summarizes the year's key trends from a Korean nationalist perspective, highlighting the surge in education fever, youth cultural movements, increased international study and participation in global forums (viewed as raising Korea's status), the burgeoning youth movement, and sports development, while lamenting the deaths of prominent intellectuals. The piece reveals a strong sense of national consciousness and aspiration among Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, celebrating progress in education, culture, and international engagement as steps towards national advancement, despite the oppressive political reality. Historians might find the specific events chronicled, the emphasis on cultural and educational activism as forms of resistance/nation-building, and the perspective on international engagement particularly valuable.

汎辛酉의 回顧 (下)
						一記者
여러분께서 依例히 本紙 11月 號의 「汎辛酉의 回顧」를 보섯슬지며, 보신 이는 應當이 不充分함을 말하섯겟습니다. 記者도 스스로 不滿을 感하오며, 또 自愧하기 마지 못하옵거니와, 時日이 一年이오, 事件이 複雜하오매 枚擧키도 難한 中 더욱이 어떤 것이 重하고 어떤 것이 輕한가를 容易히 分揀키 어렵습니다. 本 號의 下半期 記事에 對하야는 一段의 努力을 可하노라 하얏사오니, 다시 한 번 보아주시기 願이오며, 特히 本題의 末尾에 附한 「回顧의 餘」는 辛酉 1年 中에 出來한 一般情形을 槪括的으로 觀評하야써 一般의 參考에 資케 함이외다.

七月

京城에 大洪水 5日 至 7日의 大雨에 京城附近은 水國으로 化하게 되야 道路의 破壞, 家屋의 遺失이 多하얏다. 三南 各地에도 同樣의 大雨가 降하야 大損害를 被하다.
學生團과 文化運動 朝鮮에 새로운 文化를 建設하며, 民衆에게 徹底한 自覺을 與하리라는 큰 企圖하에서 知識과 機能의 中心團 되는 內外의 우리 學生團에서는 이 7月로써 크게 文化運動을 이르켯다. 或은 講演으로 或은 演劇으로, 或은 音樂으로, 或은 講習으로 各 方面으로 크게 活動하야, 前에 보지 못하던 成績을 나타내엿다. 爲先 外國 留學生의 團體로는 天道敎靑年會 東京支會의 講演隊, 佛敎靑年會의 講演隊, 學友會의 講演隊, 同友會의 演劇團, 이 三南西北 三千里 到處에서 大活動을 起하얏스며, 朝鮮 內地의 團體로는 學生大會, 朝鮮靑年會聯合會, 女子敎育會, 苦學生갈돕회 等 各 團體에서 亦是 同一한 精神, 同一한 目的으로 同一한 活動을 하얏스며, 其他 地方에 在한 團體 團體도 亦是 同樣의 活動을 繼續하야 크게 文化運動에 奔走하다.＜72＞
崔敬鶴 死刑執行 極端의 獨立運動者- 密陽警察署의 爆彈 犯人 崔敬鶴은 8日 午後 3時 大邱 監獄에서 그만 死刑을 當하얏다.
電車 賃金 問題와 京城市民의 激憤 龍山 京城市內의 從來의 2區는 1區로 하야 賃金 5錢으로 하고, 麻市와 淸凉里와 往十里는 從來의 2救 그대로 둔다는 電氣會社의 不公平에 京城市民은 크게 奮起 하야 電氣會社에 그-不公을 聲討하며, 一方 각 線 均一을 運動하얏스나 會社側에서는 頑强히 此를 拒絶하얏다.
世界 初有의 女子大統領 歐洲戰爭이 끗나자 民族自決主義 下에서 새로 興한 「앗시리아」國의 大統領은 「사-마」라는 婦人이 當選되엇다.
申興雨氏布哇行 미국 布哇에 開催되는 汎太平洋敎育會에 朝鮮의 敎育問題를 提出키 爲하야 申興雨씨는 25日 京城을 出發하다.
共濟會의 消費組合 勞働共濟會 施設部에서는 勞働者의 共同 利益을 圖謀키 爲하야 消費組合을 組織하다.
朝鮮人 産業大會 朝鮮의 産業現況을 實地調査하며 朝鮮의 産業界를 振興키 爲하야 된 産業大會는 30日 朝鮮經濟會 來에서 創立總會를 開하고 規則 及 任員을 選擧하다.
太平洋問題와 各國의 紛忙 美國 大統領 하딩氏의 一言下에 各國은 그만 웅성거리게 되엇다. 軍備制限, 極東問題 等은 實로 世界的 大問題이엇다. 이 問題를 主로 하야 各國은 參加할가 말가로 大히 奔走하얏다.

달막음
7월의 事는 이것이 그 大綱이다. 內事로는 學生團 靑年團의 文化運動이 一大 價値的 事이고, 外事로는 太平洋問題에 對한 輿論이 그-大事이엇다.

八月
