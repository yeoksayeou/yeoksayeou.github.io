필자	玄哲
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Play Script: "Dog" — A One-Act Comedy — (Unauthorized Performance Prohibited Without Author's Permission)
						Hyŏn Ch'ŏl
This script was specially written by the author for the first issue of the Year of the Dog of our Kaebyŏk Magazine. This script is certainly not a translated play, nor is it an adaptation. While it is my original creation, in terms of its method, it specifically adopts the technique of Anton Chekhov's "The Bear" (태; likely a reference to *Медведь*). In this sense, the author can say this play is simultaneously an original work and an imitation.

Characters
Widower. Paek Namsŏng (白南星) (40s)
Visitor Lady. Kang Sunhŭi (姜順姬) (Around 30)
Old Maidservant. (70s, Hereditary servant of the Paek family)
Additionally, several men and women, lower servants of the neighborhood

Place
Home of a middle-class family. Center stage is the main hall (大廳) connected to a wooden floor (마루). Between the hall and the floor, there's a folding door (分閤門). Of the four panels of the folding door, the two on the sides are closed, and the two middle ones are open. To the left of the main hall is the inner room (안방), with a kitchen (부억) attached, and to the right is the opposite room (건너방).

Time
Present day. Season is early autumn (初秋).

Scene One

(Paek Namsŏng, Old Maidservant - 2 people)
Paek. (Wearing a hemp cloth turumagi, sits at the edge of the wooden floor, blankly staring up at the southern sky, lost in sorrow.)

Old Maidservant. Master, how can you keep grieving like this? If you continue like this, what will you do if you fall ill? Look. The outer gate woman <88> said she was going to do laundry today, took the man servant with her, carrying this and that, talking about lunch, about side dishes, "Let's pack it like this," "Let's pack it like that," "Let's go this way," "Let's go that way," they went off enjoyably and affectionately. Everyone in the world, old or young, finds some kind of enjoyment. Even animals like birds fly and hop when the day breaks, having their own kind of pleasure (樂), but you, Master, day in and day out, just stay inside the house like a monk, without any worldly pleasure... That's right. Counting the months, it's already been over half a year (半年). During that time, you haven't gone out even once....
Paek. What's half a year? I've decided to spend my whole life (平生) like this.... What enjoyable sight would I see if I went out? My life will just pass like this.... The Mistress (아씨) is in the grave (무덤), and I am no different than being buried in this corner of the house.... We are both no different from dead people...
Old Maidservant. You speak utter nonsense. How can that be? That the Mistress passed away is indeed grievous and sorrowful beyond words.... You have cried a great deal, Master. Now, even if you stop crying, it's enough. You've done enough for the deceased Mistress. Crying constantly is not the way for a person. Wearing mourning clothes (服 두루막이) forever isn't what honors the departed. Let me tell you now, this old servant too, 5 or 6 years ago... when Old Man Han (한아범) died, I cried a lot. I spent two whole months crying. Thinking about it now gives me the shivers. One shouldn't always be dreaming of burying one's husband (男便). The more I think, the sicker I feel. He wasn't even a husband worth thinking that much about. (Sighs) Why must you continue just like this, Master?... Forgetting friends (친구), not going out, not seeing guests who come... If I may say so, everyone in this household (宅 食口) has become like a spider (검위), unable to see the sunlight (해ㅅ빗). The clothes that have been made will be eaten by mice (쥐).... No matter how small Changan (長安) is said to be... if you just go out, there are moving pictures (活動 寫眞), <89> theaters (演劇場), restaurants (料理집), Kisaeng (妓生), prostitutes (갈보), secret mistresses (은근자)—there are countless things for passing the time (消日리) and relieving boredom (심심푸리). Like you, Master, still young in age (나히), do you lack wealth (財産)? Is your appearance (風采) inferior to others? What's wrong that you just remain heartbroken (傷心)? If you want to marry (장가), you can marry; if you want to take a concubine (小室), you can take one; if you want to take a secondary wife (양첩 장가), you can do so; shouldn't you do whatever you feel like, whatever comes to mind, whatever you want to do? Try waiting 10 or 20 years. When your hair turns white, your teeth fall out, and wrinkles form on your forehead (이마ㅅ 살), then no matter how much you desire a young lady (시악씨), no one will come to you.

Paek. (Firmly) Listen, Old Han. Don't mention such things again. If I were going to remarry, would I have been like this for nearly a year? Speaking of the departed Mistress, did she lack virtue (淑德)? Was her talent (才質) inferior? Was her appearance (人物) lacking compared to others? When such a mistress left me and died first, how much marital fortune (妻福) could this body possess? If I were to marry again, pursuing something not destined in my share of fortune (分福), what kind of satisfying person would I find? Besides, husband and wife (內外) are strangers who meet and live together through affection (情), whether in life or death. If affection exists only in life and is forgotten in death, can that be called human fidelity (義理)? People in the world, even those in close relationships like sticky rice cake and citron (찰떡 근원 유자 사랑), the moment the wife dies, even before the funeral (초상) is finished, matchmakers (仲媒) come and go, horoscopes (四柱) are exchanged, making a fuss (야단법석) about remarrying quickly because time is urgent. But thinking about it, can that be called human fidelity? Men (男子) and women (女子) are all the same, so why is it that when a man becomes single, he can remarry as he pleases, but when a woman becomes single, she must live her whole life alone as a widow (寡婦)? Is there any reason for such an illogical (理致 업는) thing? Think about it, Old Han. If the Mistress were alive and I had died, wouldn't she have lived her entire life alone as a widow? Just thinking about that, unless one is a dog, how could one do such an unjust (義理不同한) thing? People are all the same, so why is it that when a woman becomes single, she must maintain chastity (守節) and grow old alone, while a man, as soon as his wife dies, remarries without a day's delay? Where is such a thing found? Such ethics (道德) that deviate from human sentiment (人情), I believe, must be <90> corrected, starting with me. Whether the world laughs or insults (辱) me, I am determined to uphold a man's (사나이) fidelity (貞節). For the sake of the departed Mistress...

Old Maidservant. Truly pitiful words you speak. Please don't say such things. Today, after a long time, why don't you go out for some fresh air (行氣) and visit a nearby friend's house?
Paek. (Ignoring her words, gazes at the distant mountains, takes out a handkerchief (手巾), and sobs.) Ohh... ohh...

Old Maidservant. Master... Master, please calm down (鎭靜). Calm down. Don't hurt your heart so much...

Paek. Haaah— If only the Mistress had lived just 2 or 3 more years, it would have been good... Thinking about how she managed the meager household (살림), saving and gathering so frugally (알들살들이), it's unbearable how pitiful it is. Not wearing enough, not eating enough, preparing for departure, working so hard, saving pocket money (용돈) penny by penny, doing everything possible when money was involved—lending money, making and selling soy sauce, making and selling kimchi (김치)... When I told her not to, saying it was hard on her body, she'd say, "A woman must be frugal in managing the household..." deceiving my eyes (내 눈을 긔어 가면서도), unaware of the hardship in her enjoyment of expanding the household... Who knew she would die so soon?
Old Maidservant. That's what I mean. When I think about just that, my bones...
(Sound of the main gate (大門) shaking, and a voice calling, "Open the gate, open the gate," is heard.)
Paek. (Eyes widening) Who has come again? Tell them I'm not seeing anyone and send them away.
Old Maidservant. I will do so. (Exits to the right (右便).)

Scene Two

(Paek Namsŏng alone)
Paek. Who has come again? It must be that matchmaker (중매장이) who came before. Even though I said I won't remarry, they come almost daily and pester me? At this rate, my resolve (決心) will surely break. If people keep coming and bothering (구챤케) me like this, I won't be able to live here long after all (畢竟). Perhaps I have no choice but to sell off all the household goods (세간) and go to a temple (절간) to become a monk (중질). If that happens, perhaps whoever sees me won't tell me to get married or get a wife... (Looks sorrowful) That's the most appropriate (適當한) thing for me. Firstly, it's for the sake of my deceased wife (마누라). <91> Secondly, it's to keep my resolve. Right! There's no better plan (妙策) than that. I must do that, yes...
Old Maidservant. (Enters, highly agitated, from the main gate area.)

Scene Three

(Paek Namsŏng, Old Maidservant)
Old Maidservant. Master— Who... who is it? A lady I haven't seen before, with a Western hairstyle (양머리), wearing black clothes (검정 옷), carrying something like a book bundle (冊褓), is causing a commotion (야단) at the doorstep (門ㅅ 깐).
Paek. Old Han! Did you tell her that since the Mistress of the house (宅 아씨) passed away, I don't see any guests (아모 손님)?
Old Maidservant. I did say that, but she absolutely wouldn't listen (도모지 듯지 아니하서요). "Open the gate! Open the gate!" she yelled, kicking the gate, saying, "Is this house the best just because someone died?" She absolutely wouldn't listen.
Paek. Still, go out and tell her I cannot see guests and send her away.
Old Maidservant. There's no way to send her away. Even for a woman, she's so incredibly stubborn (거벽스러운지), she won't listen. I didn't want to open the main gate, but I was afraid the gate panel would fall off, so I had no choice but to open it. Then, as she came in, I bolted the middle gate (中門). As I bolted it, I saw she was puffed up with anger (성이 통통이 나서), muttering some curse (辱), and dropped the book bundle she was carrying. She's busy picking it up now, so there's no noise. Otherwise, she'd already be at the middle gate causing another ruckus.
Paek. What kind of woman acts like that! Good grief, I've seen all sorts of things. Go out and open the middle gate. A woman like that needs to be taught some manners!
Old Maidservant. (Goes towards the gate area.)
Paek. Why is everyone bothering me like this? I really can't stand it. I must hurry to the temple. Even if I try to stay here a bit longer without going to the temple, others won't leave me alone, so what choice do I have? (As if resolved) Right! To the temple...

Visitor (客). (Enters, followed by the Old Maidservant.)

Scene Four

(Paek, Old Maidservant, Visitor)
Visitor. (To the Old Maidservant) You wicked (妖惡한) old hag! Why did you lock the gate! You dog-like lowly servant (개 가튼 놈의 한미)... (Sees Paek, changes tone) <92> Master (令監)? Pleased to meet you for the first time. I am Kang Sunhŭi (姜順姬), serving as Director (理事), Manager (支配人), Sales Department Supervisor (販賣部員 監督), Accounting Supervisor (會計簿 監督), and External Affairs Supervisor (外交員 監督) at the Asia Women's Trading Company (亞細亞 婦人商會). The reason I have suddenly (空然이) come to visit your residence (宅) is regarding a serious matter (重大한 일) for our company. We sent male clerks (事務員) several times, but they were refused (拒絶) and returned, being told that you, Master, for some reason (事故), would not meet guests. Therefore, today I inevitably (不可不) had to appear (出頭) myself.
Paek. (Without much formal greeting (人事答)) A serious matter, you say? What is it?
Visitor. It's nothing else but that your late wife invested in thirty shares (參拾株) of stock (股本) in our company. The due date (期日) for the first installment payment (第一 拂込金) of 150 Won (圓) passed three months (석달) ago. So, we sent clerks to your house several times (몃 번), but they reported they couldn't see you at all. Thinking it couldn't be true for such a respectable (점쟌흐신) household, although I'm too busy today to spare the time (餘暇), I came myself. No matter how difficult it may be, you must somehow (기여히) arrange the payment...
Paek. She invested stock in the Asia Trading Company?... Why did she invest without telling me?
Visitor. How would I know about your household matters? You would know your household affairs better than I, Master.
Paek. (Sighing, looks at the Old Maidservant) Old Han, take away the noodle soup (국수 장국) from the mourning altar (殯所).
Old Maidservant. (Exits)
Paek. (To the Visitor) In any case (何如큰), since my wife invested stock in your esteemed company (貴 商會), I will pay the money. However, I cannot give you the money right now (當場). It happens that I have no money on hand (手中). As you know, since my wife died, I haven't gone out (出入) for over half a year and have stayed inside the house like this, so I lack such flexibility (變通性). Once the autumn harvest (秋收) comes in, I will prepare the money and send it immediately to the company. Also, today happens to be my deceased wife's birthday, making my heart even more sorrowful (悲悵). Discussing money matters (金錢 말슴) with a guest on top of that is extremely unpleasant (不快) and unbearable.
Visitor. Nevertheless, there's a reason why I absolutely must receive it today. There's a major crisis (狼狽) at the company. Not only that, but it's a matter where I cannot maintain my standing (處地) and reputation (面目). <93>
Paek. As soon as the harvest is in, I will send the money immediately.
Visitor. I am not someone who came to collect your harvest (秋收). I came to collect money.
Paek. It's very regrettable (未安), but I cannot give you the money right now.
Visitor. Nevertheless, I cannot wait until harvest time.
Paek. Nevertheless, what can I do if I don't have it right now (只今 업는 것)?
Visitor. So, you're saying you can't give it right now (時方)? Is that it?
Paek. I'm saying I cannot give it.
Visitor. Hmm... Is that your final answer? Is that it?
Paek. That is my final answer.
Visitor. Indeed (最後)? Is that your final word?
Paek. It is my final word.
Visitor. Aha! Truly, thank you very much for your words. Let's not forget these words until the day we die. (Snorts) Hmm! Even so, people see me and say, "Ms. Sunhŭi, please try to be calm (冷靜히) in whatever you do..." Just now, I met Baroness Pak Ch'unae (男爵 夫人 朴春愛氏), a major shareholder (大株主) in our company, and her first greeting was, "How busy (汨沒) you must be with company affairs these days?" Then she said, "But Ms. Sunhŭi, don't be too arrogant (대) in everything, try to be more meticulous (찬찬이)." It's truly frustrating! How can one bear it without getting arrogant? I came to collect money. If I can't collect this money, I cannot maintain my dignity (體面) at our company. It's been almost a year since the company started (始作), and the share payments (股金) aren't coming in well. On one hand, ordered goods (物件) keep arriving, there are many expenses, but no money is coming in. How can we endure? Today, after much thought, I gathered the clerks, gave them a scolding for not doing their job well, and then boasted, "Watch me, I'll go out for a round, and I'll collect every last penny right away!" I set out at dawn (새벽)? I visited every place likely to pay, leaving none out. Despite all that effort, I barely collected five Hwan (오환) from one person. Pitiful! Not even enough to cover shoe expenses (신발 갑)! Thinking this and that, I came here believing I could collect without worry (念慮), trusting firmly, only to be told, "I'll pay after the harvest." "Today is my dead wife's birthday, so talking about money is unpleasant (滋味가 업다)?" <94> Do you think I came here to show you some pleasantries (滋味)? How can I not be angry?
Paek. Angry or furious, what can I do if there's no money before the harvest? That's why I clearly stated I'd give you the money after the harvest (秋收하면 돈을 드린다고 確實이 말슴을 하엿지요).
Visitor. But I am not someone who came to collect the harvest. I am someone who came to your house to collect money. Every other word is harvest, harvest (秋收 秋收)! Are you boasting about the harvest? What kind of damned beggar's attitude (별 비러 먹을 ) is this?
Paek. What? Beggar's attitude? What offensive (아니꼬운) behavior is this? This is the first time in my life (生後) a woman has spoken like that to another man (남정네). Ugh! (Stands up abruptly, slams the inner room's paper door (장지) shut, and goes inside.)

Scene Five

(Visitor alone)
Visitor. What now? Harvest! Today is the dead person's birthday! So does that mean I shouldn't receive the money? If it's a birthday, does it mean debts don't matter (關係 챤탄)? ... Every time he opens his mouth (뻥끗하면), "Since my wife died, I haven't gone out, so I have no money." "I have to wait for the harvest." "It's the birthday." It's infuriating. If he just pushes that damned harvest (경칠 秋收) or winter storage (冬臧) excuse, does it mean it doesn't matter if someone else's company faces bankruptcy declaration (破産 宣告)? That's secondary, right? At the company, I boasted that if I just went out and swept through (휘돌기만 하면), I'd collect every last penny (한 푼 남아지 업시), right? But after going around (단겨보니), far from anyone paying a penny, they just insult me and attack (뎀비지)? It's maddening. I went to Kim Naksŏng's (金樂成) house, he was out somewhere. Went to Yun Hyŏnbang's (尹顯邦) house, he just avoided me. With that guy named Kang Sangho (姜尙浩), I fought like hell and didn't get a penny. Those damned women! When they stamped their seals (圖章), they did it with their own hands (제 손으로 찍어 노코), but when you go to collect money, why do they push the men forward? Why can't they speak for themselves? Whenever there's a difficult situation, they just push it onto the men (사나이에게 밀고 마니)! And yet they talk about equal rights for men and women (男女 同等權)! Freedom and equality (自由 平等)! Liberate women (女子 解放)! How can such words come out of their mouths? Why blame the men? It's all the women's fault (녀편네들 罪지). If only the women were a bit smarter (똑똑들만). What are those men anyway? No matter how high and mighty they act (제 아무리 날고 기는 놈이라도), is there any man who doesn't melt in a woman's hand (녀편네 손에 아니 녹는 놈이 잇나)? If women just realized this, it would be fine, but they tremble (벌벌 <95> 떨고) like they've seen a tiger (호랭이) whenever they see men and can't speak, so women are oppressed (압박) by men? Ugh! Thinking I'm that kind of woman too? Absolutely not. Thinking I'd get scared (怯) and run thousands of miles (千里 萬里) away? No way! Absolutely not (어림 업지)! Today, truly, let him learn what kind of thing a woman is. Because I didn't get angry, he thought I was just a soft pushover (順한 물컹이)? Today, I really got angry. I'm so angry my arms and legs (다리 팔) are trembling (떨린다). I'm thirsty (목이 말으다). My chest (가슴) feels like it's going to burst (터질 것 갓고), and I have a headache (頭痛).
(Angrily) Where did that damned old servant go!
Old Hag (한머엄)!
Old Maidservant. (Enters.)

Scene Six

(Visitor, Old Maidservant)
Old Maidservant. Did you call for the old servant (불르섯습니까)?
Visitor. Bring me noodle soup or water (물)!
Old Maidservant. (Exits)

Visitor. Unacceptable! Absolutely unacceptable! He says he'll pay, but only after the harvest. What a strange way of talking! Does he think it doesn't matter if a person's authority (權威), position (地位), and reputation (體面) fall into a pit (陷地) where they can't be preserved (保存치 못할)? Does he think I came to collect the harvest? I came for money, money!? He won't give me the money? What reason? What reason (무슨 까닭)! Today is the dead person's birthday. From what logic (論理) does such talk come? That's the sinister (陰凶하고) and gloomy (칙칙한) logic (論法) of men. That's why I try not to talk to other men. Even now, I try to avoid (避해 단이랴고) men whenever I see them. Is it because I'm young and pretty (입부니까)? (Takes out a mirror (거울), looks at her face, strokes her hair) Thinking I'll be dragged around (끄을이여 단일 줄) by a man! Unacceptable. That sort of creature thinks a woman's chastity (貞操) is a plaything (노리개 꺼리) for men? If I were to mix words (입을 석거) with a man like you, it would be safer (安心될) to carry gunpowder (火藥) towards a fire. Oh dear, oh dear, my body is shivering (으쓱으쓱). It's right (올흔) not to associate with such creatures. Just pay the money, just the money!
Old Maidservant. (Enters) <96>

Scene Seven

(Visitor, Old Maidservant)
Old Maidservant. (Brings water) The Master of the house (宅 나으리) says he is unwell (몸이 不平하셔서) and cannot come out to speak.
Visitor. I don't want to hear it (듣기 실어)!
Old Maidservant. (Exits)
Visitor. He's unwell (便챤아서) and can't come out to speak. Who came here to listen to talk? Whether he comes out to talk or not, just pay the money, just the money! If he just pays the money, I'll leave right now (時方). Does he think I'll stay if asked...? If he doesn't pay the money, I won't leave... Let him try staying in his room for a month, see if I don't stay here for a month. Let him try for a year, see if I don't stay here for a year. Thinking I'll just leave meekly (호략 호략이)... Hey, Master (여보 염감)... I came to collect money. Money... Do you think I came for a matchmaking meeting (선)? It's embarrassing (부끄럽소). Why did you go into the room? Do you think I'll leave if you do that? Do you think I'll fall for such tactics (手段)? I may look like this, but I'm the number one person in a 300,000 Won (萬圓) company. Plain cotton jacket (무명 저고리) and black skirt (검정 치마)... What's this dust (몬지냐)? Oh, so you think some beggar (어더 먹는 걸어지) came? Look at these shoes (구두 꼴). Does it matter (相關잇나)? I didn't come to this house as a guest! Pay the money, money? Without getting the money, I can't show my face at the company. No matter what, I just wish you'd consider my circumstances (사페)... Ah, I'm hungry (시장하다). I'm so hungry I can't stand it. I need to eat something... (Shouts) That damned old servant!
Old Maidservant. (Enters)
Visitor. Bring me a bowl (거릇) of noodle soup!
Old Maidservant. (Exits)

Visitor. Generally, I can't figure out the ways (세음) of this damned house. The house is quite large, yet there are only two people (사람 색기)? What's going on? Isn't he getting married? If only he paid the money properly and spoke nicely (말이나 곱은 곱은이 들으면), I might arrange a good match (조흔 仲媒) for him, but is there any use (쓸 대) for such talk? I just need to collect the money I'm owed? Isn't he going to just stay like this? If so, that's it? What's the reason? Is even this damned old servant mocking me?
Old Maidservant. (Enters with a tray of noodle soup)
Very sorry to say this but... <97>
Visitor. (Interrupting sharply) What's wrong?
Old Maidservant. What could I possibly say... What does it matter... Generally I...
Visitor. Who are you talking to... Stop chattering!
Old Maidservant. (Looking aside) There's no way to get a word in. Gosh, I've never seen anything like it in my life (生前). If I met such a ghost (鬼神) at night, it would be terrible. (Exits)
Visitor. Ah, I'm so angry (憤) I can't stand it! I'm so angry I can't see anything. The sky looks dark (캄캄하여), and the whole world seems like a dark land (暗黑 天地). How can a person understand this? Is he going to pay the damned (경칠) money or not? He needs to say something! Does just staying silent (알코만 잇다면) make him the best in the land (第一 江山)? Men are like that, so they lose face (치신을 일치) with women. Are you going to pay the money or not (낼 테야 아니 낼 터야)? Why can't you speak? Ugh! Ah, my chest feels so tight (답답하여) I can't stand it. (Shouts) That damned old servant!

**Summary**

This one-act play, "Dog," published in 1922, portrays a clash between Paek Namsŏng, a widower deeply mourning his wife and refusing to remarry or engage with the world, and Kang Sunhŭi, a persistent, modern businesswoman demanding payment for stocks his late wife purchased. Paek insists on maintaining fidelity to his deceased wife, even arguing for male chastity paralleling female widowhood norms, while Kang, facing pressure from her company, aggressively pursues the debt, dismissing Paek's grief and circumstances as irrelevant excuses. The play ends mid-confrontation, highlighting the tension between traditional mourning/fidelity ideals and modern economic realities/assertive female roles.

For historians, the play offers insights into early modern Korean society navigating changing social norms. Paek's argument for male fidelity challenges traditional patriarchal expectations where widowers quickly remarried. Kang Sunhŭi represents the "New Woman" archetype – educated, working in commerce, assertive, and challenging traditional gender etiquette, though still facing societal judgment and internal company pressures. The mention of specific businesses (Asia Women's Trading Company), entertainment (moving pictures, theaters), and social types (kisaeng, prostitutes, secret mistresses) provides glimpses into the urban landscape and social dynamics of colonial Seoul (Changan). The play's explicit acknowledgment of Chekhov's influence ("The Bear") also shows the reception and adaptation of Western literary forms in Korea during this period.

脚本 「犬」―喜劇 一幕―( 作者의 許諾업시 無端 上演을 禁함 )
						玄哲
이 脚本은 作者가 特히 우리 開闢雜誌의 戌年 第 1湖를 爲하야 지은 것이다. 이 脚本은 勿論 飜譯劇도 아니며, 또 飜案劇도 아니다. 이것이 나의 創作은 創作이나 그 作法에 이르러서는 特히 안톤 체에흡氏의 作인 「態」의 作法을 取한 것이다. 이러한 意味下에서 作者는 이 劇을 創作인 同時에 模作이라고 할 수 잇다.

登場人物
喪妻人. 白南星 ( 40餘歲 )
客夫人. 姜順姬 ( 30歲 內外 )
老 婢. ( 70餘歲, 白氏家 祖傳婢 )
此外, 洞里 下人 男女 數 三人

場所
中等生活者의 家庭, 舞臺 中央은 大廳에 마루가 이어 잇고 廳과 마루 사이에는 分閤門이 가려 잇다. 넷 짝 分閤門에 兩便 두 짝은 다쳐 잇고 가운데 두 짝은 열려 잇다. 그리고 大廳 左便은 안방이고 안방에 달려 부억이 잇스며 右便은 건너방이 달려잇다.

時日
現在, 節候는 初秋.

第一景

(白南星, 老婢2人)
白. (北布服 두루막이를 입고 마루 끄테 나 안자서 하염업시 남쪽 한올을 치어다 보며 愁心으로 精神 업시 안잣다.)

老婢. 나리 마님, 또 이러케 근심을 하시면 어떠케 합니까. 이러케 하시다는 病患이 나시면 어떠케 하시랴고 합니까. 보십시요. 行廊 어멈도 오늘 ＜88＞ 빨내를 간다고 아범을 다리고 지거니 이거니 하며 점심은 어떠니 반찬은 어떠니 이러케 담아 가지고 갑시다. 저러케 담아 가지고 갑시다. 이러케 가세, 저러케 가세 하며 滋味스럽고 情답게 갓습니다. 世上 사람들은 늙으나 젊으나 다 각각 무슨 滋味던지 한 갓 滋味는 다 보는 것이 잇습니다. 새 가튼 짐승도 날이 새면 날거니 뛰거니 하며 相當히 樂이라고 하는 것이 잇는데 나리 마님 한 분이 날이 새나 밤이 드나 집 안에만 계셔서 중 되신 것과 가티 世上에 무슨 樂이라고 하는 것은 하나도 업시 ... 그럿습니다. 달 數를 처 보면 발서 半年이나 넘엇습니다. 그 동안에 한 번도 出入이 업스시고....
白. 半年이 다 무엇인가 平生을 이러케 지낼 作定일세.... 나가면 무슨 滋味잇는 꼴을 보겟나?
나의 平生은 이러케 한 世上을 보내고 말 것일세.... 아씨는 무덤 가운대로, 나는 이 집 구석에서 몸을 무친 것과 다름 업네.... 우리 둘은 발서 죽은 사람과 다름이 업네...
老婢. 천만읫 말슴을 다 하십니다. 그럴 수가 잇습니까. 아씨께서 도라가신 것은 怨痛하고 애닯은 말슴이야 어찌 다 하겟습니까... . 나리 께서도 퍽 만이 우섯습니다. 인제 그만 울어도 넉넉합니다. 고만 하셔도 도라가신 아씨께 對해서는 할 만치 하셧습니다. 사람이라고 하는 것은 울고만 잇는 것이 수가 아닙니다. 어느때까지 服 두루막이만 입고 잇는 것이 도라간 이를 爲하는 것이 아닙니다. 인제 말슴이지만은 이 한멈도 5,6年 前에... 한아범이 죽엇슬 때에, 퍽 만이 울엇습니다. 두 달이나 울면서 歲月을 보냇습니다. 只今 생각하면 진저리 나는 일이 올시다. 언제던지 男便을 뭇는 꿈만 꾸고 잇슬 것은 아닙니다. 생각할스록 멀미가 남니다. 그것은 그처럼 생각할 만한 한아범도 아니엿습니다. (한숨을 쉬다) 어자고 나리께서는 아주 이러케 하고만 계십니까?... 친구도 이즈시고 出入도 아니 하시고 오시는 손님도 아니 보시고 이러케 말슴을 하시면 무얼 합니다만은, 이 宅 食口는 모도 검위 가티 해ㅅ빗도 볼 수가 업게 되엇습니다. 지어 노은 옷은 쥐가 다 써을고 말겟습니다.... 長安이 아모리 좁다고 하더래도... 그래도 나가만 보시면 活動 寫眞도 잇고 ＜89＞ 演劇場도 잇고 料理집도 잇고 妓生 갈보, 은근자 여러 가지로 消日리와 심심푸리 하실 것이 限量업시 만습니다. 나아리 가티 아즉도 나히 기시겟다 財産이 업습니까, 風采가 남만 못하십니까. 무엇이 어떠타고 이러케 傷心만 하시고 계십니까? 장가를 드시랴도 드실 것이고 小室을 어드랴도 어들 것이고 양첩 장가를 가시랴도 가실 것이고 무엇이던지 마음 나시는 대로 생각 드시는 대로, 하고 십흔대로 하실 것이 아닙니까? 그것도 인제 10年이나 20年이 지내 보십시요. 머리털이 호호 이 시시고 이가 빠지시고 이마ㅅ 살이 잡히고 하면 그때는 아모리 마음에 드는 시악씨가 잇더래도 올 사람이 업습니다.

白. (確然하게) 여보게 한멈. 그런 말은 두 번도 말게. 내가 다시 장가를 갈 것 가트면 近一年이나 내가 이러케 하고 잇겟나. 도라간 아씨로 말하면 淑德이 잇고 남만 못한가. 才質이 남만 못한가. 人物이 남에게 빠질 것인가. 그런 아씨가 나를 두고 먼점 죽을 적에야 이 몸의 妻福이 얼마나 잇스면 그러하겟나. 分福에 업는 일을 또 장가를 간다고 하면 무슨 시원한 사람을 엇게 되겟나. 그리고 內外라 하는 것은 남남끼리 맛낫다가 죽으나 사나 서로 情으로 지내는 것인데 살아 잇다고 情이 잇고 죽엇다고 그것을 잇는다 하면 그것이 어대 사람의 義理라고 할 수 잇나. 世上 사람들은 찰떡 근원에 유자 사랑으로 지나다가도 그 안해가 죽기만 하면 미처 초상도 치기 前에 仲媒가 드나든다 四柱가 온다 간다 하여, 時間이 急하다고 다시 장가를 간다 하여 야단법석을 치지만은 그것을 생각하면 사람의 義理라고 할 수 가 잇나, 男子나 女子나 사람은 다 맛챤가진데 어째서 男子는 혼자 되면 마음대로 장가를 가고 女子는 혼자 되면 平生을 寡婦로 혼자 늙는다는 그런 理致 업는 일이 잇슬 까닭이 잇나. 한멈도 생각해 보게. 萬一에 아씨가 사라잇고 내가 죽엇스면 아씨는 平生을 혼자 寡婦로 살앗겟지? 그것만 생각하여도 사람이 개가 아닌 以上에는 그러한 義理不同한 일이야 할 수가 잇나. 사람은 다 마챤가진데 엇째서 女子는 혼자 되면 守節을 하고 平生을 혼자 늙어야 한다면서 男子는 안해만 죽으면 하로가 멀다고 장가를 가니 그런 일이 어데 잇나. 나는 그러한 人情에 버서나는 道德은 나버텀이라도 ＜90＞ 고처 가야할 줄 아네. 世上 사람들이 웃거나 辱을 하거나 나는 사나이 貞節을 직혀 볼 作定일세. 도라간 아씨를 爲해서...

老婢. 참 딱하신 말슴도 다 하십니다. 그런 말슴은 마시고 오래간만에 오늘은 近處 친구님 댁에 行氣나 하시고 좀 단여 오십시요.
白. (그 말은 들은 체도 아니하고 먼 山만 바라보며 手巾을 대이고 훌적거리며 운다) 어 응... 어 응.

老婢. 나아리 마님... 나아리 마님 鎭靜하셔요. 鎭靜하셔요. 그러케 마음 傷치 마시고...

白. 하아- 아씨가 한 2,3年만 더 사섯더래도 조흘 것을... 되쟌흔 살림인지 무엇인지 사신다고 그 알들살들이 애끼고 모으던 것을 생각하면 불상해서 못 견대겟네. 못다 입고 못다 먹고 갈 것을 그러케 애를 써가며 용돈이라는 한 푼 두 푼 모아가며, 돈 날일이면 아니하는 것 업시 돈 노리도 하고 장도 담아 팔고 김치도 맨들어 팔아 가면서... 내가 몸 괴롭다고 그러지 말나고 하면 계집 사람이라고 하는 것은 살림에 알들하여야 하지요.... 하면서 내 눈을 긔어 가면서도 집안 늘이는 맛에 괴로움도 모르더니... 그러케 속히 죽을 줄이야 누가 알앗나.
老婢. 그러게 말슴이지요. 이 한멈도 그것만 생각하면 뼈가...
(大門 흔드는 소리가 나며, 문 열게, 문 열게, 하는 소리가 들인다)
白. (눈이 둥그래지며) 누가 또 왓나. 나는 누구던지 對面을 아니한다고 나가서 보내ㅅ 버리게.
老婢. 그러케 하겟습니다. (右便으로 나간다)

第二景

(白南星 一人)
白. 누가 또 왓나. 아마도 요 前에 왓던 중매장이인 것이지. 장가를 아니 간다고 하여도 하로가 멀다고 와서 사람을 졸르지? 이러다가는 나의 決心도 깨여지고 말 것이다. 이러케들 와서 구챤케 구를면 畢竟 이 곳에서도 오래 살지 못하겟다. 아마도 세간 나부랑이나 잇는 것을 다 팔아 가지고 절간으로나 가서 중질이나 할 밧게 업다. 그러케 되면 아마 어느 사람이던지 나를 보아도 장가를 가거라- 계집을 어더라고 그러한 말은 아니 하겟지... (悲色을 띠며) 그것이 내게 第一 適當한 짓이다. 첫재는 죽은 마누라를 爲하는 ＜91＞ 것이고 둘재는 나의 決心을 지키는 것이다. 을치! 그 밧게는 더 조흔 妙策이 업다. 그러케 하여야지 그러케...
老婢. 大門에서 大端히 興奮되어 드러운다.

第三景

(白南星, 老婢)
老婢. 나리 마님- 누누구이... 신지 못 보던 부인인데 양머리를 하고 검정 옷을 입고 冊褓가튼 것을들고 門ㅅ 깐에 와서 야단을 침니다.
白. 한멈! 宅 아씨가 도라 가신 後로는 아모 손님도 보지 아니한다고 하엿나?
老婢. 그것은 그러케 말슴을 하엿십니다만은 도모지 듯지 아니하서요. 門 열어! 門 열어!하며 門을 발길로 차고 이 놈의 집은 사람이 죽엇다고만 하면 第一인가? 하며 도모지 듯지 아니하여요.
白. 그래도 나가서 손님을 볼 수가 업다고 돌려 보내게.
老婢. 당초에 돌려 보낼 수가 업서요. 婦人네라도 어떠케 거벽스러운지 말을 들어야지요. 大門을 아니 열어 주랴다가 門짝이 떨어지까봐 할 수 업서 門을 열어 주엇습니다. 그리고 들어 오면서 中門을 다다 걸엇지요. 걸면서 보니까 성이 통통이 나서 무슨 辱인지 입으로 중얼거리며 가젓던 冊褓를 떨어 터려서 그것 주어 싼다고 지금 아모 소리도 업습니다. 그러치 아니하면 발서 中門ㅅ간에 와서 또 야단이 낫슬 것이야요.
白. 아니 녀편네가 그래! 원 참 별꼴을 다 보네. 아니 나가서 중문을 열어주게. 그 따위 녀편내는 버릇을 가르처 주어야지!
老婢. (門 깐으로 나간다)
白. 웨 이리 모도 나를 가지고 귀챤케 구으나. 정말 견댈 수가 업네. 암만 해도 速히 절로 가야지. 절로 아니가고 이곳에서 좀 지내려고 하야도, 남들이 그러케 가만이 두지 아니하니까 하는 수가 잇나. (決心한 것 가티) 올치 절로...

客. (들어 오고 老婢가 딸앗다)

第四景

(白, 老婢, 客)
客. (老婢에게) 이 妖惡한 늙은이 가티! 웨 문을 잠거! 개 가튼 놈의 한미... ( 白氏를 보고, 語調를 고쳐서) ＜92＞令監? 처음 뵈옵습니다. 이 사람은 亞細亞 婦人商會의 理事, 兼 支配人, 兼 販賣部員 監督, 兼 會計簿 監督, 兼 外交員 監督으로 잇는 姜順姬라는 사람이올시다. 이러케 空然이 令監 宅을 차자 온 것은 우리 商會의 重大한 일로 여러 번 男子 事務員을 보냇스나, 令監께서 무슨 事故인지 모르지만은 손을 對하지 아니한다고 拒絶을 當하고 왓습니다. 그러기 때문에 오늘은 不可不 本人이 出頭한 모양이올시다.
白. (別로 人事答도 업시) 重大한 일이라니요. 무슨 일인가요.
客. 다른 일이 아니라 우리 商會에 도라가신 婦人께서 股本을 參拾株 드신 일이 잇습니다. 그것의 第一 拂込金 150圓의 期日이 발서 석달이나 지낫지요. 그래서 事務員을 몃 번이나 宅으로 보내도 도모지 뵈올 수가 업다고 그저 오기에 점쟌흐신 宅에서 그얼理가 잇나 하고 오늘은 바빠서 몸 빼칠 餘暇가 업지만은 本人이 나선 길이올시다. 아모리 어려우실 지라도 좀 기여히 만들어 주셔야...
白. 亞細亞商會에 股本을 들엇서요?... 어째 내게 그런 말도 업시 들엇던가요?
客. 그거야 令監宅 일 이니까 내가 알 수 잇나요. 令監宅 일은 令監이 나보다 더 잘 아시겟지요.
白. ( 한숨을 쉬면서 老婢를 보고 ) 한멈 殯所에 국수 장국을 물니게.
