Views of Literary Figures on Marriage

The Scope of Fortune and Misfortune
Marriage expands the scope of our happiness and unhappiness.
British Litterateur Joseph Addison (1672-1719)

Before and After Marriage
Before marriage, do not scrutinize too deeply even if your beloved has faults, and after marriage, do not lapse into excessive leniency. (Ibid.)

The Bond of Friendship
Marriage is incessantly the strictest bond of friendship. Without trust, there is no friendship, and without sincerity, there is no trust.
British Litterateur Samuel Johnson (1709-1784)

A Great Question
Is marriage truly not a prime example where those inside wish to get out, and those outside wish to get in? Is this not a great question?
American Litterateur Raruhŭ \*Tŭŏ Emason (Ralph Waldo Emerson) (1803-1882)

Marriage and Cultivating Virtue
Marriage is the best opportunity to make the coarse person gentle, and for the gentle person, it provides the chance to practice that gentleness.
German Litterateur Ttaede (Goethe) (1749-1832)

**Summary**

This short article presents a compilation of quotes about marriage from prominent Western literary figures: Joseph Addison, Samuel Johnson, Ralph Waldo Emerson, and Goethe. The quotes touch upon marriage's impact on happiness, the importance of balance before and after marrying, the necessity of trust and sincerity, the paradoxical desires of those inside and outside marriage, and its role in personal refinement. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it appeared in *Kaebyŏk*, a leading intellectual magazine in 1920s colonial Korea. It demonstrates the introduction and discussion of Western social thought, specifically concerning the institution of marriage, among Korean intellectuals during a period of significant social and cultural transition. Historians might find it indicative of how Western concepts were being selectively introduced and framed for a Korean audience.

禍福의 範圍 結婚은 우리들의 幸福과 不幸의 範圍를 擴充한다.
英國文學者 쪼세푸·아덴손(1672-1719)
婚前과 婚後 婚姻前에는 나의 사랑하는 사람에게 過失이 잇더래도 넘우 穿鑿하지말고 婚後에는 넘우 寬大한데 흐르지 마라.(同上)
友愛의 끈 結婚은 間斷업시 友愛의 가장 嚴密한 끈이다. 信任이 업고는 友愛가 업고 誠實이 업고는 信任이 업는 것이다.
英國文學者 삼엘·쫀손(1709-1784)
一大疑問 結婚은 果是 안으로 잇는 것은 것으로 나가랴고 하고 것으로 잇는 것은 안으로 들어가랴고 하는 標本이 업스면 이것이 大疑問이 아인가.
米國文學者 라루후·*드어·에마손(1803-1882)
結婚과 修德 結婚은 粗雜한 사람을 溫和하게 하고 溫和한 사람에게는 그 溫和를 實行케 하는 最良의 機會를 주는 것이라.
獨逸文學者 때에데(1749-1832)
