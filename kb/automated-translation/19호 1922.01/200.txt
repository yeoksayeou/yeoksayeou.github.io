元旦 (外)
						金石松

元旦! 이날은
一年의 첫 아츰이다
그러고 生日은
人生의 元旦이다
그러나 宇宙의 첫 아츰-
元旦의 元旦은
果然 언제이던가?

元旦! 너야말로
모든 것의 어머니다
너는 宇宙를 創造하얏고
모든 觀念의 對象을
너- 自身이 産出하엿다

光, 力, 時間, 空間, 生物, 無生物
草木, 禽獸, 人間, 男女
生, 老, 病, 死, 智, 情, 意...
이 따위는 모다 너의 創作이다

永遠한 現實苦를 부르짓는
작은 새의 재재거림!
가엽시 無常함을 象徵하는
가냘핀 菫花의 웃음!
全體가 너- 元旦의
絶對로 偉大한 創作이다

New Year's Day (and others)
						Kim Sŏksong

New Year's Day! This day is
The first morning of the year.
And a birthday is
The New Year's Day of a life.
But the first morning of the universe—
The New Year's Day of New Year's Days—
When indeed was it?

New Year's Day! You truly are
The mother of all things.
You created the universe (宇宙),
And all objects (對象) of conception (觀念)
You—yourself—brought forth (産出).

Light, Force, Time, Space, Living things, Non-living things,
Grass and trees, Birds and beasts (禽獸), Humans, Men and women,
Birth, Old age, Sickness, Death, Intellect, Emotion, Will...
All such things are your creations.

The chirping of small birds,
Crying out eternal real suffering (現實苦)!
The smile of the delicate violet (菫花),
Pitiably symbolizing impermanence (無常)!
All of it is your—New Year's Day's—
Absolutely magnificent creation.

歲暮

壁에 걸린 掛曆은
다만 한 장 밧게 아니 남앗다
이 한장이 마저
업서지는 때에-
이 하루가 過去의 領域으로
마즈막 사라지는 때에-
아! 今年의 一生은 殞命할 것이다

아! 오늘의 하루여!
너는 나의 愛人이다 生命이다
너의 殞命은 마츰내
靑年의 피가 끌는 이 몸을
墓地에! 墓地에...＜22＞
실어감이다. 장사함이니다.

Year's End

The calendar (掛曆) hanging on the wall
Has only one page left.
When this last page
Is gone—
When this one day
Finally disappears into the realm of the past—
Ah! This year's life will meet its end (殞命).

Ah! Today, this one day!
You are my lover, my life.
Your end (殞命) will finally
Carry this body, whose young man's blood boils,
To the graveyard! To the graveyard...<22>
It carries me away. It buries me.

불은 꺼졋다

불은 꺼졋다!
족으마한 화로의
두어조각 숫불은
마츰내 꺼졋다!

불은 꺼졋다!
한 도막 초의
흐릿한 불빗은
마츰내 죽엇다!

불은 꺼졋다!
가냘핀 내 몸의
피를 끌히든 불은
마츰내 식엇다

아! 남은 것은
하아얀 재와
까아만 심지와
싸아늘한 등신뿐

The Fire Has Gone Out

The fire has gone out!
In the small brazier (화로),
The few embers (숫불)
Have finally gone out!

The fire has gone out!
Of the single candle stub,
The dim flame
Has finally died!

The fire has gone out!
In my frail body,
The fire that boiled my blood
Has finally cooled.

Ah! All that remains
Is white ash,
A black wick,
And just a cold corpse.

솔방울 따는 아낙네

그것은 치운 겨을날이다
내가 어느 山路로 지나갈 때에
길가의 솔밧 속에서
애젊은 아낙네 하나가
눈밧 우에 쪼구리고 안저서
어린 아이에게 젓을 줌을
아! 나는 보앗다!
그리고 그는 맛부티를 입고
버얼 벌 떨며 언 손을
호! 호! 불고 안저 잇슴을

未久에 그는 일어나더니
어린 아이를 등에다 업고
자긔 겻에 노혓든 대바구니를
엽헤다 끼고 솔밧 속으로
가만 가만히 몸을 감춘다
(마치 나의 눈을 실혀 하는 듯)
그러나 나의 눈은＜23＞
(公然한 好奇心으로)
그의 뒤를 쪼차갓다.

얼마 아니 가서 그는
솔가지 사이에서
무엇을 따는 듯 하엿다.

그것은 다른 것이 아니라
깜아케 마른 솔방울이다.
將次 불을 때이랴는

아! 솔방울 따는 아낙네여!
나는 너에게서
人生의 참 얼굴을 본다

The Woman Gathering Pinecones

It was a cold winter day.
As I passed along a mountain path (山路),
In the pine grove beside the road,
A young woman
Was crouching on the snowfield,
Nursing a small child—
Ah! I saw it!
And she, wearing rags (맛부티),
Sat shivering violently, blowing
Ho! Ho! on her frozen hands.

Before long (未久), she stood up,
Put the child on her back,
Tucked the bamboo basket (대바구니), which had been placed (노혓든) beside her (겻에), under her arm (엽헤다),
And quietly, quietly disappeared into the pine grove.
(As if wanting to avoid my gaze)
But my eyes <23>
(With open curiosity (公然한 好奇心))
Followed her.

Not long after, she
Seemed to be picking something
Among the pine branches.

It was nothing other than
Black, dried pinecones.
Likely to light a fire later.

Ah! Woman gathering pinecones!
In you, I see
The true face of life (人生).

些少한 突發事件

普通學敎 一年生의 어린 아우가
어대선지 참새 한 머리를 잡아 왓다
우리가 저녁 밥床을 물리자마자

아모 철 모르는 어린 아우는
제가 잡은 새와 가티 輕快한 혀로
「아! 이놈이 쌀 도적놈이다!」
저 호을로 부르지즈며
작은 것을 넓게 펴서
燈불에 비취어 보며
「아! 고흔 비치다!」
또 다시 부르지젓다

蒼白한 얼굴로 病席에 누은 안해는
힘 업는 눈을 깜박 깜박 하더니
별안간 이러케 부르지젓다.
「아! 도련님 노하 주오」
하고 다시 이어서
「두 마리가 終日토록 앨써 일하고,
피곤한 몸으로 돌아와 깃들인 것을
아! 잡아서 어찌하오!」
깔아 안즌 소리로, 중얼대엇다 ＜24＞
＜22-24＞

A Trivial Incident

My younger brother, a first-grader at the common school (普通學敎),
Caught a sparrow somewhere and brought it home.
As soon as we finished the evening meal,

My little brother, knowing nothing of the world,
With a tongue as light (輕快) as the bird he caught,
Cried out by himself,
"Ah! This thing is a rice thief!"
Spreading its small wings,
Holding it up to the lamplight (燈불),
"Ah! Such pretty colors!"
He cried out again.

My wife, lying sick (病席) with a pale (蒼白) face,
Blinked her weak eyes,
And suddenly cried out like this:
"Ah! Young master, please let it go."
And then continued,
"Two birds worked hard all day long (終日토록),
Returning tired to their nest—
Ah! Why catch one!"
She mumbled in a sunken voice. <24>
<22-24>

**Summary**

This collection of five poems by Kim Sŏksong explores themes ranging from the cosmic (the origin of the universe in "New Year's Day") to the deeply personal and existential (the passage of time and mortality in "Year's End" and "The Fire Has Gone Out"). The latter two poems use the end of the year and the extinguishing of fire as metaphors for death. The collection also includes poignant observations of everyday life and hardship ("The Woman Gathering Pinecones," depicting a poor mother foraging in winter) and a moment of empathy ("A Trivial Incident," where a sick wife pleads for a captured sparrow). From a historical perspective, the poems are interesting for their quiet portrayal of poverty ("The Woman Gathering Pinecones") and the contrast between childish thoughtlessness and adult empathy ("A Trivial Incident"), offering glimpses into the social fabric and human concerns of 1920s Korea under colonial rule, expressed through lyrical and sometimes somber tones.
