Magazine Title: 개벽 제19호
Publication Date: 1922년 01월 10일
Article Title: 님생각 (漢詩譯) (Thinking of My Love (Chinese Poem Translation))
Author: N/A
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

My thoughts yearning for my beloved
Are in Seoul, unendingly.

Pushing aside the silk curtain and peeking out,
Late autumn cries at Kŭmjŏng (金井),
In the desolate, fine frost that has fallen,
The autumn light lies cold.

Sitting alone facing only the lamplight,
With earnest thoughts, even slumber does not come,
Pushing aside the curtain to see the shining moon,
Only long sighs flow endlessly.

My beloved is like a flower, ah, my love
Is far, far away at the edge of the clouds.

Above, the distant blue sky
Stretches high, high, and long,
Below, only the meandering
Blue sea waters ripple.

The sky is long, the road too is far, far away,
The further one goes, there is no direction,
The long road of the distant Kwansan (關山), so far and yet farther,
Can even a dream go there? Can even a soul reach it?

My thoughts yearning for my beloved,
This anxious, anxious heart of mine.

長相思(原文)

長相思在長安. 絳幃秋啼金井闌. 微霜凄凄翬色寒. 孤燈不眠思欲絶. 卷帷望月空長歎. 美人如花隔雲端. 上有靑冥之長天. 下有綠水之波瀾. 天長路遠不可涉. 夢魂不到關山難. 長相思催心肝.＜52＞

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean vernacular translation of the famous Chinese poem "Cháng Xiāng Sī" (長相思), attributed to Li Bai (though the Korean text doesn't explicitly name the original poet). The poem expresses intense longing for a beloved person who is far away, located metaphorically in Seoul (originally Chang'an in the Chinese text) and compared to a flower beyond the clouds. The speaker is sleepless, surrounded by desolate autumn scenery, and feels the immense distance—represented by the vast sky, sea, and impassable mountains (Kwansan)—prevents even dreams or the soul from reaching the loved one, causing deep anguish. From a historical perspective, this translation published in *Kaebyŏk*, a prominent Korean intellectual magazine during the Japanese colonial period, is significant. It showcases the effort to render classical Chinese literature into contemporary Korean, reflecting both continued reverence for the Sinitic cultural sphere and a move towards developing the vernacular language and literature. Historians might find the choice of poem (themes of longing, separation, and insurmountable distance possibly resonating with the colonial situation) and the use of early 20th-century Korean orthography and vocabulary interesting for linguistic and cultural studies.

님생각 (漢詩譯)
우리 님을 그리는 생각은
서울잇서, 끈칠 길이 업서라.

비단 발을 헤치고 엿보면
느즌 가을은 金井에서 울며,
쓸쓸하게도 내린 잔자 서리에는
가을 빗이 차게도 싸혓서라.

燈불과 다만 혼자 마조 안즈면
간절한 생각에 조름조차 안 와서,
비최인 달을 보랴고 발을 헤치면
긴 한숨만 하염업시 숨여 흘너라.

우리 님은 꼿과 가타라, 아아 님은
멀게도 저 멀니 구름 끗에 게시어라.

우에는 앗득하게도 푸른 한울이
놉히 놉히 길게도 덥혀 잇스며,
알에에는 구비 구비 흐르는
푸른 바다물만 결을 지어라

한울이 길고 길조차 멀고 멀어
갈스록 方向이 업서라,
멀고도 또 멀은 關山의 긴 길이야
꿈이나 가며, 넉시나 갈 수 잇스랴.

우리 님을 그리는 생각,
조미 조미한 나의 이 맘.

長相思(原文)

長相思在長安. 絳幃秋啼金井闌. 微霜凄凄翬色寒. 孤燈不眠思欲絶. 卷帷望月空長歎. 美人如花隔雲端. 上有靑冥之長天. 下有綠水之波瀾. 天長路遠不可涉. 夢魂不到關山難. 長相思催心肝.＜52＞
