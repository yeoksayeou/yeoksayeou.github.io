Among these, the most unusual thing is that among writers, playwrights occupy the top position among Nobel Prize laureates. Spain's Echegaray and Benavente are both purely from a playwright background. Even if others are not purely playwrights—but rather miscellaneous writers of novels,<50> plays, poetry, philosophy, criticism, etc.—most of them gained their names through their plays. First, France's Prudhomme was purely a man of the stage. Bjørnson is a modern Norwegian playwright. As mentioned before, Spain's Echegaray is a playwright. Although Poland's Sienkiewicz cannot be considered a playwright no matter how you look at it, his representative novel, "Quo Vadis," gained its name on the continental stage through brilliant effort at one time. One might think that Britain's Kipling also did not venture beyond being a poet, but that is not so; there is the play "The Light That Failed," written in collaboration with another author. And the play "Monna Vanna" written by the Belgian Maeterlinck has even been translated in Japan. Next, Germany's Hauptmann is too famous to count as just a playwright. Also, India's Tagore has a play called "Chitra," which was even staged by a foreign amateur theatre group residing in Tokyo, Japan. Furthermore, not only do France's Romain Rolland and Sweden's Heidenstam each have 5 or 6 famous plays remaining, but Spain's Benavente, who received the prize in 1920, i.e., last year, is also famous worldwide as a playwright.

Calculating this way, 10 out of the 20 Nobel Prize in Literature laureates are such writers. Never mind the Nobel Prize laureates; even figures like Shakespeare and Ibsen, who occupy the position of the greatest literary prizes in world history, are renowned purely for their plays.

Although the reasons for this are complex with various contents, to give a simple example, it is because drama, compared to all other literature, inherently involves the fierce development of the protagonist's will in its conception, and also because it gains the assistance of various sister arts in the laboratory called the stage, thereby moving the audience by utilizing the largest portion of their five senses. Consequently, the impression becomes profound, and thus, when fame is achieved, its power increases. In other words, if other literature is chemistry, dramatic literature can be called chemistry with experimental apparatus. That is, it can be said to combine the chemistry textbook and chemistry experiments. If only our country could quickly build good theaters, train good actors, and produce many good playwrights, so that we could participate among these playwrights who win the most Nobel Prizes and receive at least one!?! The reason for repeating this is that there is empty space left on the page, so it's unnecessary talk, but don't take it seriously. It's mostly just saying that these are the laureates since the Nobel Prize was established. —(Recorded during sleep on the night of the 14th)—<51>

**Summary**

The article lists the Nobel Prize in Literature laureates from 1901 to 1921, categorizing them by nationality. The author notes the surprising prevalence of playwrights among the winners (10 out of 20), arguing that drama's power lies in its active portrayal of will and its multi-sensory impact on stage, comparing it to experimental science versus theoretical science. The piece concludes with a strong wish for Korea to develop its theatre scene and produce a Nobel laureate playwright, though the author self-deprecatingly calls this concluding wish space-filler. From a historical perspective, the article reflects the Korean intellectual desire for international cultural recognition and parity during the colonial period. The emphasis on theatre as a path to global acclaim and the lament for Korea's lack of infrastructure (theatres, actors, writers) highlight the cultural aspirations and perceived deficiencies under Japanese rule.

이 中에도 第一 異常한 것은 文學者 中에도 劇文學者가 노오벨 賞金의 受賞者로 第1位를 占領하엿스니 西班牙의 에티카레이, 뻬나벤트 兩人이 둘이 다 純全한 戱曲家 出身이고 기타 純全한 劇作家가 아닐지라도 小說,＜50＞ 戱曲, 詩 哲學, 評論 其他의 雜作家이라도―그 大部가 그네들의 戱曲을 通하야 이름을 어덧스니 第一 佛蘭西의 뿌리유든무는 純全한 舞臺上 사람이고 뾰룬손이 近代 諾威의 戱曲家이며 西班牙의 에티카레이가 劇作家임은 前述함과 如하고 波蘭의 시엔큐이치는 아모리 보아도 劇作家라고 할 수는 업스나 그 小說 中의 代表作인 「코우·바더인」은 一時 燦然한 努力으로 大陸의 舞臺 우에서 그 이름을 어덧스며 英國의 키푸링그가 또한 詩人의 以外에 出足치 못한 사람이라 생각할 듯 하나 그 亦 그러치 아니하니 다른 作家와 協力하여 지은 戱曲 「꺼진 불」이 잇다. 그리고 白義人 메텔잉그의 지은 戱曲 「몬나반나」는 日本서까지 飜譯이 되어 잇다. 그 다음 獨逸의 하퍼만은 劇作家라고 헤아르기가 넘우도 有名하다. 그리고, 印度의 타아골 亦是 일즉이 日本 東京 在住 外國人生手劇團에서 上演까지 한 『치트라』라는 戱曲이 잇다. 그리고 그 다음 佛蘭西의 로만, 로란과 瑞典의 하이덴스탐도 各各 5, 6種의 有名한 作劇이 남아 잇슬 뿐 아니라 1920年 即 昨年에 受賞한 西班牙의 뻬나벤트도 劇作家로써 世界에 이름이 잇다.
이러케 計算해 오면 노오벨 文學賞金 受賞者 20名에 10名이나 이러한 作家가 잇다. 노오벨 賞金 受賞者로 말할 것이 아니라 世界 有史 以後에 第一 큰 文學賞 地位를 占領하고 잇는 쉑스피아나 입센 가튼 이도 純全한 戱曲으로써 그 이름이 赫赫하다.
이러한 그 理由는 여러 가지로 複雜한 內容이 잇지마는 第一 淺近한 例를 들어 말하면 劇曲이라고 하는 것이 다른 모든 文學보다 그 作意上 本軆가 主人公의 猛烈한 意思發展이 잇는 것과 또 舞臺라는 實驗所에서 여러가지 姉妹藝術의 補助를 어더 多數한 民衆의 五感 中 가장 만흔 部分의 힘을 어더 感動시키는 故로 그 印象이 딸아서 深刻하여지고 딸아서 得名을 하면 그 힘이 만하지는 然故이다. 다시 말하면 다른 文學이 假量化學이라고 하면 劇文學은 實驗器를 가진 化學이라고 할 수 잇는 것이다. 即 化學冊과 化學實驗을 兼한 것이라 할 수 잇는 것이다. 아모쪼록 우리 나라에서 어서 急히 조흔 劇場을 짓고 조흔 俳優를 養成시키며 조흔 劇作家가 만히 나서 第一 만이 노오벨 賞金을 타는 이러한 劇作家 中에 한목 叅例하여 하나나마 어더 보앗스면!?! 이러케 重言復言하는 것은 頁數에 餘白이 남기로 쓸 대 업는 말이나 그것은 다 고지 들을 것 업고 大槪 노오벨 賞金이 생긴 後에 受賞者가 이러하다는 말에서 지내지 아니하는 것이다. ―(14夜寢中記)―＜51＞
