Magazine Title: 개벽 제19호
Publication Date: 1922년 01월 10일
Article Title: 오즉 「참」이 잇스소서,―새해의 새 사람이 되기 爲하야――새해의 새 살림을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 일을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 局面을 열기 爲하야― (May There Be Only "Truth," —To Become New People of the New Year—To Make a New Living in the New Year—To Do New Work in the New Year—To Open a New Phase in the New Year—)
Author: 朴達成
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Commentary)

May There Be Only "Truth," —To Become New People of the New Year—To Make a New Living in the New Year—To Do New Work in the New Year—To Open a New Phase in the New Year—
						By Pak Tal-sŏng

Feelings of Sending Off and Welcoming

Everyone sending off the year and greeting the year, Fathers, Mothers, Elder Brothers, Younger Brothers. How are your feelings about sending off and welcoming? Whether you like it or not, the Sin-yu year (1921) has been sent off; whether you are happy or sad, inevitably the Im-sul year (1922) has been welcomed. Even a brief encounter on the road is said to have deep karmic ties, leading to heartfelt greetings wishing safe travels; even staying one night together is said to have great significance, leading to tearful partings over the uncertainty of meeting again. How much more so when sending off the Sin-yu year, having spent 360 long days together, sharing days, months, and seasons, living together, suffering together, and enjoying together, and welcoming the Im-sul year, where we will do so again? How could there not be a word of farewell? How could there not be some kind of emotional tears? I truly feel too depressed and also too rustic to send off the year. I also feel too uneasy and too apprehensive to welcome the year. What did we plead for last year at this time when sending off the Kyeong-sin year (1920), and what did we declare when welcoming the Sin-yu year? We said we would cast off the old selves and become new people; we said we would cast off the old ways of living and live anew; we said we would cast off the old work and do new work; we said we would cast off the old phase and pioneer a new phase. And we said we would move from all falsehoods to all truths; we said we would move from all contradictions to all rationality;<48> we said we would move from all turbidity to all purity; we said we would move from all ideals to all realities. Thus, didn't we even say that last year's failure is this year's success, and that the past's dissatisfaction is the future's satisfaction, setting the Sin-yu year as a great goal and holding many expectations? This was not encouraged by anyone, nor was it ordered by anyone. It was something we decided for ourselves, something we declared ourselves. But then,

Everyone, Fathers, Mothers, Elder Brothers, Younger Brothers. How much of a new person have you become in the past year? How much of a new life have you lived? How much new work have you done? How much of a new phase have you opened up? Also, how much truth has there been? How much reality? How much rationality? How much purity? How much success? How much satisfaction have you gained? If we had satisfaction and success in the past year, if we had truth and reality, then we wouldn't have much to grieve about sending off the year, but if—if we had no satisfaction and success, no truth and reality in the past year, then sending off the year is too regrettable and too shameful for us.

As the New Year arrives, New Year's cards come and go, and Toso liquor (traditional New Year's spiced sake) is passed around. New clothes flutter, and new shoes shine. But everyone, kneel quietly under the cold lamplight by the drafty window and reflect on the past year as your conscience dictates. Was there "truth" or not? Was there "action" (함) or not? Did you live like a human being or not? Did you live a life worth living or not? If—if there is a genuine conscience, not falsehood, your New Year's cards will become letters of denunciation, and your New Year's liquor will become liquor of punishment. Thus, from your lips will come the lament, "Oh, Heavenly Father! Why are you so heartless?" followed by the choked cry, "Oh, human affairs! In the end, they are so transient," and finally, covering your face with your hands, you will collapse under the lamp, saying, "It was false again, there was no truth." And you will pray to Heaven, "May there be only 'truth.' We are all just falsehoods, so may there be 'truth.'" This is true for the writer himself. Thinking about it, who in the world is not in the same situation as me? Without need for tedious explanation, let's not fabricate anything and just frankly lay out the past year as it was. First.

Let's Look at People Themselves.

Let's see. How many truly human people were there in our society? Old or young, man or<49> woman, counting all people, how many truly human people were there? Let the old think as old people, the young as young people, men as men, and women as women. And let the rich think as rich people, the poor as poor people, the strong as strong people, and the weak as weak people. And also, let the religious figures think as religious figures, educators as educators, businessmen as businessmen—let everyone think for themselves. How many truly human "true" people were there in the past year? And did each person themselves become a truly human person? And did they do true work, live a true life, and find true happiness?

All grandfathers, grandmothers, elders, lords, and ladies. How are your feelings—reflecting on the year? Are you truly not ashamed in your conscience? Did you live truly according to your conscience, without any lies, without any deception? If so, what could be more appreciated for our human society, for our elders? However, conscience hesitates to say that among you, there were absolutely no lies and only truth.

Regarding the same human beings, why did you insult, beat, scorn, and oppress them just because they were young or little? Why couldn't you let them sit in the same place, stand in the same place, eat in the same place, or speak in the same place? Why did you deny their personhood because they were young, look down on them like weeds because they were little, jump to call them "rude," label any slight movement as "ill-mannered," praise them with "that kid's quite something" if they did well, and curse them with "that brat is fatherless" if they did poorly? Why did you strip away their human rights like that? And why did you boast so much about being old, saying "My age is seventy," "I've eaten a hundred pots of soy sauce," "My years are many," and why did you disregard the young so much? Is this the proper way and common practice for elders? Is it a natural thing for humans, something that cannot be avoided? I dare say, whether old or young, a person is a person; only respect the personhood itself and do not trample on human rights. Thus, I say, treat them with truth and love them with truth. Anything that is not truth, whether it be a person or old age, I intend to bury it forever. The way of the elders—is indeed difficult. Regarding the past year, how much true heart, true living, and true work our elders accomplished—is uncertain, but conscience is very reluctant to say it was much. <50>

All elder brothers, younger brothers, elder sisters, young ladies, husbands, and young masters. How is your heart—reflecting on the year? Was there truly no falsehood, and was there truth? Does your conscience cheerfully approve of all that has passed? If so, there would be no greater blessing for our society of young people. However, as the keen eye glances, all the realities of the past path are clearly visible.

As fellow human beings, comrades with the same purpose, heading down the same path, why did you fight, hate, envy, and steal from each other so much? Why were your eyes so shifty? Why was your speech so harsh? Why did you slap those pretty cheeks? Why did you pull out that freshly combed hair? Fisticuffs, kicking, clubbing, stabbing—what kind of inhuman acts are these? Why did you call the person selling medicine because they had no money a thief? Why did you spit at the person begging because they had nothing to eat? Why did you peek into others' pockets? Why did you climb over others' walls? Why did you sell your fields and paddies and offer them up at taverns and brothels? Why did you end up behind iron bars after committing fraud and theft? Why did you make your parents swallow bitter tears? Why did you make your siblings shed heart-wrenching tears? "This brat, that brat, this punk, that punk, your mother, my mother, this wench, that wench, this girl, that girl," curses spewing from mouths, curses filling ears—what state of mind led you to act like this? Rejecting the old because they are old, despising the inept because they are inept; among fellow youths, loving more or less based on whether a face is pretty or ugly, hating more or less based on whether clothes are well-worn or not; flattering the children of the rich and disparaging the children of the poor; liking someone more because they attend the same place, mocking country folk, bowing deeply to foreigners, clearing your throat loudly because you are an official, trembling because the law is fearsome, those walking crawling, those standing collapsing—all sorts of strange and nonsensical sights were abundant in our youth society. Was this truly the behavior of decent human beings? And why couldn't you do what you said you would do? Why did you quit the work you were doing midway? Starting a newspaper, starting a magazine, starting a company, starting a school—after making such a fuss, why couldn't you even begin, or why did you quit what you had started? Was this truth, devoid of falsehood? And why did shameless loafers pretend to be well-off? Why did opportunistic hangers-on pretend to be influential figures? Why was selling ancestral tablets called public service or charity? With no success at all, why did you meddle here and there? Who called you heroic? What kind of initiatives were you initiating so <51> much? Was this truth or falsehood? Did you do it intending to do true work? Or did you do it intending to do false work? I hear it's true, but they say it's because it's a transitional period, because there are no capable people, because there is no money. It sounds plausible. But whether this statement itself is true or false, I find it even more untrustworthy. Without needing lengthy words, it is generally falsehood, not sincerity, and even if it were sincerity, it is not ardent, inner sincerity.

Thus, we failed to faithfully implement the truth and reality we each declared last year at this time. I say for the second time, "May there be only truth." And thus, become true people and do true work. It is said that where there is truth, there is life. And,

Whether you have a thousand nyang or ten thousand nyang, all you so-called rich people. How is your heart—reflecting on the year? Are you without shame in your conscience? Money is money, and people are people; why did you persecute people with money and make their lives miserable with money? Why did you grumble when visiting the tenant farmer's threshing ground? Why did you rap your pipe stem after seizing a debtor? Why did you drive away the beggar who entered your gate? Why did you tear off the nameplate from someone else's house? Why did you slyly avoid people? Why did you furtively turn your face away? Saying a rickshaw damages your dignity, you rode a horse-drawn carriage; finding the carriage insufficient, you rode an automobile, yet why did you shout "Get out of the way! Move!" at those struggling pitifully with A-frame loads on the street? Saying cotton clothes are coarse, you wore silk; finding silk tasteless, you wore fur, yet why couldn't you give a single set of clothes to those shivering in thin garments with flesh peeking through? Saying eating rice isn't satisfying, eating rice cakes isn't satisfying, eating meat causes indigestion, and drinking alcohol doesn't suit your taste, you continuously consumed ginseng and deer antlers, yet why didn't you give even a single measure of rice to those wandering about unable even to eat bean dregs? With a full belly, lying in a warm room on a silk cushion, you probably have no thoughts about whether others live or die. But you must have a conscience, so think deeply. How many are wandering the four streets, crying out, without homes, clothes, or food? Do you know why the waters of the Yalu River are so abundant? It is because they hold their tears. Do you know why Paektu Mountain is so worn down? It is because their footprints have run upon it. You rich people, may there be only "truth." Thus, become true people and do much that is true. Money is money, and people are people. Money is definitely not your money; it is the world's money. Also, it is not money that will stay with you forever. <52> It is money that inevitably circulates publicly with the world. You came empty-handed when you arrived, and you will leave empty-handed when you go, so reflect on this yourself. And, to those in the world called

Strong people. That strength is not innate strength, nor is it eternal strength. Yesterday's strength of a thousand catties can become today's strength of ten thousand catties, and today's strength of ten thousand catties can change tomorrow into a weakness unable even to lift a single hair. Today's strength is nothing to boast much about, nothing to make loud noises about. Just be human; whether strong or weak, only respect that human essence, live truthfully, and work truthfully. And all you poor and weak who cry out beneath others, there are absolutely no high or low ranks among people, no noble or base, no strong or weak. All are equal, all are uniform; only respect your own self as a human being and do not subserviently yield beneath others. Why grovel so much to others due to lack of food, clothing, and shelter? Strive to earn a living, and if you still cannot survive, it is better to bite your tongue and go to the northern hills (i.e., die) than to endure the contempt unbecoming of a human being. You must achieve self-determination and carve out your own destiny yourself. Become only true people and be free of falsehood.

All elders, Daoist masters, pastors, preachers, and believers, listen. Your reflections—on the year, how are they? Was there truly no falsehood? Did you really live truthfully? With conscience present, you cannot deceive. While speaking of great mercy and compassion, of abstaining from alcohol, meat, and killing, why do you hide alcohol and meat in hidden rooms and secluded places, and kill chickens and pigs by streamsides and roadsides? Is this falsehood? Or truth? While speaking of serving people like Heaven and broadly saving all sentient beings, why do you distinguish between old and young, senior and junior, those inside and outside the faith? Is this truth? Or falsehood? Why do you have other thoughts while offering prayers? While speaking against adultery and lewdness, why do your eyes wander towards women? While explaining the meaning of universal love, why do you call over latecomers and shout angrily? Why does someone who speaks of turning the left cheek kick others and even file lawsuits? While fearing sin so much, what is the reason for accumulating sin? Is this truth? Or falsehood? How many were true religious figures who did work worthy of true religious figures in the past year? Whether Ch'ŏndogyo believers, Christian believers, Buddhist believers, or believers of any religion—how many were there? Since you have a conscience, you cannot deceive. Reflect for yourselves; the lumps of sin are likely not just one or two. Superstition, vanity, formalism, pretense, seeking fame, monthly salary—these will be among those lumps. <53> Dig them all out, each of you. With conscience present, you will nearly die of self-reproach. And,

Elementary school, middle school, university, orphanage, kindergarten, village school (書堂), short-term school (講習所)—all educators in educational institutions, reflecting on the year, how was it? Was it truth? Was it falsehood? Did you do it to pass the time? Did you do it for the monthly salary? Did you do it for the taste of the title, "Professor So-and-so" or "Instructor So-and-so"? Or did you do it because you wanted to hear the title "Teacher"? Did you truly devote yourself to genuinely informing others? Of course—you likely did. However, among you, there are talks of issues like dissatisfaction with salary, lack of knowledge, points of concern regarding speech and behavior, and even remarks about paying too much attention to appearances or frequenting taverns too often. Is this true? Or false? Was there anyone who agonized over not being able to teach even one word or one character to those genuinely ignorant, and researched how to teach well even for one hour or one minute? Since each of you has a conscience, you won't be able to deceive. And,

Farmers, merchants, industrialists, bankers, moneylenders—all businessmen, how was your year? Thinking about it, falsehood likely constituted more than half of your year too. To water your own paddy, you probably broke down your neighbor's embankment. To deceive the landlord's eyes, you probably secretly harvested unripe grains. When your cow ate your neighbor's crops, you probably considered it normal, but when your neighbor's cow ate your crops, you probably picked up a stone and struck the cow's leg. Is this falsehood? Or truth? Since you have a conscience, you cannot deceive. And merchants, you said, "In business, it's okay even if a son deceives his father," and acted so unreasonably? Why did you inflate the price of a ten-jeon item to one won, and a one-won item to five or even seven won? Because they were country folk, you mocked them with half-measures; because they were children, you palmed off bad goods; because they were women, you slyly cheated them. Is this true or false? Why did something promised in two days take a week or two? Why did something promised for delivery in 30 minutes not arrive even after the whole day passed? Why was your speech so blunt? Why was your posture so haughty? Why did you puff on your pipe so arrogantly? Is this true? Or false? Is this the inherent nature of commerce? Yet, why are seizures of assets so frequent, and bankruptcies so common? Why can't you engage in overseas trade? Why couldn't you manage direct exports and imports? Why only handle leftovers others have sold, things others have eaten and discarded, things that have passed through ten, a hundred hands, huddled under a small signboard <54> thinking only of deception, and trying to fill your own belly by catching your own comrades? Is this true? Or false? Since you have a conscience, you cannot deceive. And industrialists, how much did your head ache, your eyes strain, your arms and legs tremble while creating raw materials, designing forms, and manufacturing actual products? How much greasy sweat did you shed when your back was breaking and your chest felt like splitting? I know you suffered much. But after the product is made, why are your thoughts so base? You called earth iron, and paper leather. Is this true? Or false? And you bankers and moneylenders, frankly, it's disgusting to watch. Whether you earn a few hundred won or a few thousand won monthly salary, what concern is it of mine that you appear so arrogant before my eyes? Whether you wear Western clothes, gold-rimmed glasses, sit in silk chairs, or ride in rickshaws, I am not the least bit envious, so why do you appear so arrogant to my eyes? As soon as one enters the bank door and approaches the cash counter, one invariably encounters unpleasantness. "How much?", "Go over there!", "No small change," etc.—putting aside the half-speech (반말), they don't even lift their eyes to look. It's exactly like a country bumpkin struggling at a government office to file a production report or death notice. Ah—everyone, isn't that also a business? Where is the rule among business operators to act like that? You should be trembling anxiously before the customer, yet the customer ends up trembling anxiously before you. I don't know whose fault it is—but it seems perfectly ripe for bankruptcy. You advertisers, you who advertise well, where did you hear the words 'Kind and Courteous'? Who taught you 'Prompt Response'? What does 'Small Profits, Large Volume' mean? What does 'Low Price Offer' mean? Who told you to do that? Looking at the advertisement and then the advertiser's behavior, it's truly nauseating.

This is all falsehood. This person deceives that person, that person deceives this person, that person deceives another person, and that person deceives this person again, going round and round, deceiving and being deceived. Thus, truth cannot be found in this society. The falsehoods of the past year alone probably cannot be counted even with the sands of the Ganges. And,

All you rickshaw pullers, chimney sweeps, A-frame carriers, servants, dockworkers, fishermen, woodcutters—all laborers. Ask your conscience how much falsehood there was in the past year. After receiving the full fare determined by the distance, you haggled, "just one more cup's worth, just two more cups' worth—" and eventually resorted to insults, "Why did someone like you <55> ride?" "No one would pull the likes of you," cursing freely and grabbing collars freely. Where else could such outrageous behavior be found? Carrying a load of one mal (unit of volume), you whine, "Aigh, it's heavy, must be five mal," demanding more; even when given food, clothes, and wages, you steal behind the master's back; digging one basketful of earth, you act as if digging ten or a hundred baskets—such behaviors were common among you, I know. Since each of you has a conscience, you cannot deceive.

Our past was thus full of falsehood and lacking truth. The words grow too long, so I will stop, and only for the sake of the future, I say, "May there be only truth." Therefore,

Business and the Situation Need No Further Mention

Just as there's no rule that the source can be muddy while the tributaries are clear, since people themselves have become like this, what need is there to speak of the new living, new business, and new phase we anticipated?

Whether false or true, well done or poorly done, the past must simply be burned away as the past. And facing the New Year, we must become new people, live new lives, undertake new ventures, and pioneer a new phase. To do this, with all my heart,
To you, Fathers, Mothers, Elder Brothers, Younger Brothers. I earnestly implore you twice, thrice, a hundred times, a thousand times, "May there be only 'truth.'" And again, "May there be no falsehood."<56>

**Summary**

This passionate editorial by Pak Tal-sŏng, published in the influential magazine *Kaebyŏk* at the start of 1922, is a stark call for "truth" (참) and sincerity among Koreans. The author reflects critically on the past year (1921), lamenting the pervasive falsehood, hypocrisy, and lack of genuine progress across all segments of society—elders, youth, the rich, the poor, religious leaders, educators, businesspeople, and laborers. He accuses them of failing to live up to their own declarations of becoming "new people" and achieving societal renewal. The piece functions as a collective exhortation for deep moral introspection and a commitment to honesty and genuine effort as the foundation for building a better future in the new year.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing of the mood among Korean intellectuals and nationalists in the early 1920s, following the March 1st Movement. It highlights a turn towards internal critique and moral self-strengthening as prerequisites for national progress under colonial rule. Historians might find interest in the detailed social criticism directed at various groups, reflecting contemporary social tensions (generational conflict, class disparities, disillusionment with leadership figures) and the specific forms of perceived moral failings (deception in commerce, hypocrisy in religion, arrogance of the elite, lack of solidarity among youth). The emphasis on "truth" resonates with broader themes of authenticity and national consciousness prevalent in the cultural nationalist movements of the era.

잡지명	개벽 제19호

기사제목	오즉 「참」이 잇스소서,―새해의 새 사람이 되기 爲하야――새해의 새 살림을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 일을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 局面을 열기 爲하야―
필자	朴達成
기사형태	논설

오즉 「참」이 잇스소서,―새해의 새 사람이 되기 爲하야――새해의 새 살림을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 일을 하기 爲하야――새해의 새 局面을 열기 爲하야―
						朴達成
送迎의 感

해를 보내고 해를 맛는 여러분, 아버님 어머님 兄님 아우님이시어. 보내고 맛는 感想이 그― 어떠합니까. 조커나 실커나 어쩔 수 업시 辛酉年은 보내엇스며 깃브거나 슯흐거나 어쩔 수 업시 壬戌年은 맛게 되엇습니다. 路上暫逢도 그것이 因緣이 깁다 하야 安往安來의 情 깁흔 人事가 잇는 것이며 一夜同宿도 그것이 關係가 크다하야 分合無常의 애끗는 눈물을 흘리는 것입니다. 하물며 날을 가티하고 달을 가티하고 節을 가티하야 3百 6十이란 長時日을 同行同宿하고 同苦同樂한 辛酉 1年을 보내며 또 그리할 壬戌 1年을 마즘에 어찌 一言의 送別辭가 업스며 어 一種의 感慨淚가 업겟습니까. 나는 實로 一年을 보내기에 넘우나 抑鬱하고 또 野俗합니다. 一年을 맛기에도 넘우나 未安하고 또 惶恐합니다. 우리가 昨年 이 때 庚申年을 보내며 무어라 陳情하얏스며 辛酉年을 마즈며 무어라 宣言하얏습니까. 녯 사람을 버리고 새 사람이 되겟다 하얏스며 녯 살림을 버리고 새 살림을 하겟다 하얏스며 녯 일을 버리고 새 일을 하겟다 하얏스며 녯 局面을 버리고 새 局面을 開拓하겟노라 하얏습니다. 그리고 모든 虛僞에서 모든 眞實로 나아가겟다 하얏스며 모든 矛盾에서 모든 合理에 나아가겟다＜48＞ 하얏스며 모든 混濁에서 모든 純潔에 나아가겟다 하얏스며 모든 理想에서 모든 實地에 나아가겟다 하얏습니다. 그리하야 去年의 失敗는 今年의 成功이란 말도 하얏섯고 過去의 不滿足은 將來의 滿足이란 말까지 하야 辛酉년으로써 큰 目標를 삼고 만흔 期待를 가지지 안엇습니까. 이것은 누구의 慫慂도 아니엇고 누구의 傳令도 아니엇습니다. 우리 스스로가 作定한 바요 우리 스스로가 宣言한 바 입니다. 그런데
여러분 아버님 어머님 兄님 아우님이시어. 過去 1年에 얼마나한 새 사람이 되엇스며 얼마나한 새 살림을 하얏스며 얼마나한 새 事業을 하얏스며 얼마나한 새 局面을 열어 노앗습니까. 또 眞實은 얼마나 되엇스며 實地는 얼마나 되엇스며 合理는 얼마나 되엇으며 純潔은 얼마나 되며 成功은 얼마나 되며 滿足은 얼마나한 滿足을 어덧습니까. 우리가 過去 1年에 滿足과 成功이 잇다 하고 참과 實地가 잇다 하면 우리는 一年을 보내기에 그다지 슯허 할 것이 업습니다마는 萬若― 過去 1年에 滿足과 成功이 업고 참과 實地가 업섯다 하면 우리는 一年을 보내기에 넘우나 억울하고 넘우나 창피합니다.
新年을 當하얏다 하야 年賀狀이 왓다 갓다 하며 屠蘇酒가 오르락 내리락 합니다. 새 옷이 팔랑거리며 새 구두가 반작거립니다. 그러나 여러분, 風窓寒燈下에 고요히 꿀어 안저 過去 一年을 良心이 命하는 대로 생각해 보시오 「참」이 잇섯든가 업섯든가. 「함」이 잇섯든가 업섯는가. 사람답게 살엇든가 못 살엇든가 살음답게 살엇든가 못 살엇든가. 萬若―虛僞 아닌 眞情한 良心이 잇다 하면 여러분의 年賀狀은 聲討狀이 될 것이며 여러분의 年賀酒는 懲罰酒가 될 것입니다. 그리하야 여러분의 입으로, 「嗚呼天公아 何若是無情」고의 嘆辭가 나올 것이며 뒤미처 「嗚呼人事여 終是無常이로다」의 목메인 소리가 나올 것이며 最後로 「또 거즛이로구나 참이 업섯구나」 하고 얼굴에 손을 가리우고 燈下에 업드릴 것입니다. 그리하야 天께 禱하기를, 「오즉 「참」이 잇스소서. 우리는 모다 거즛 뿐이오니 「참」이 잇스소서」 하리이다. 이것은 筆者 自身이 그러합니다. 생각컨대 世에 누가 나와 가튼 境遇가 아니겟습니까. 煩設을 要할 것 업시 우리는 아무것도 꾸며대지 말고 아조 率直하게 過去 一年을 그대로 내어 노아 봅시다. 爲先.

사람 自體로써

보읍시다. 우리社會에 사람다운 참 사람이 몃 사람이나 되엇습니까. 늙은이거니 젊은이거니 男人이거니＜49＞ 婦人이거니, 統틀어 사람치고 사람다운 참 사람이 몃 사람이엇습니까. 늙은이는 늙은이로서 생각하고 젊은이는 젊은이로서 생각하고 男人은 男人으로서 생각하고 婦人은 婦人으로서 생각해 보시오. 그리고 富者는 富者로서 생각하고 貧者는 貧者로서 생각하고 强者는 强者로서 생각하고 弱者는 弱者로서 생각해 보시오. 또 그리고 宗敎家는 宗敎家로서 생각하고 敎育家는 敎育家로서 實業家는 實業家로서 다― 各各 생각해 보시오. 過去 一年에 사람다운 「참」사람이 몃 사람이 되엇는가를― 그리고 各自 自己부터 사람다운 참 사람이 되엇는가를― 그리하야 참 일을 하고 참 生活을 하고 참 幸福을 어덧는가를―
모든 한아버지 한머니 영감님 대감님 마마님이시어. 一年을 回顧 하는 감상― 어떠하십니까. 果然 良心에 부끄럽지 아니합니까. 아모 거즛업고 아모 속임업시 참으로 良心대로 살앗섯습니까. 그리하섯다 하면 우리 사람 社會를 爲하야 우리 늙은이들을 爲하야 게서 더― 고마운 일이 어대 잇겟습니까. 그러나 여러분 中에 全혀 거즛이 업고 참만 잇섯다 하기는 良心이 躕躇합니다.
