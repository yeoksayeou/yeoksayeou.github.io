Article Type:	잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Love yourself to the very end. And love with compassion those who hate you. The side of justice (正義) will prevail over evil (邪惡). Uphold warm peace (平和) with your strong right hand (右手), and silence that tongue full of suspicion (猜疑). Set your ultimate goal (最終目的) on your compatriots (同胞), all good spirits (諸善神), and truth (眞理).

One who is robbed and smiles has already taken something back from the robber.

Be true to yourself. Then, just as night follows day (夜의 晝에 繼함과 如히), you will be true to others (他人).

Lend your ear (耳) to anyone's words, but do not open your mouth (口) to just anyone.

No matter how much a plan (設計案) seems to fly ahead, if action (實行) does not follow it, it is merely futile. Let it arise from your hand at the same instant (卽時) it arises in your mind. <63>

**Summary**

This short piece offers a series of aphorisms focused on self-love, integrity, ethical conduct towards others (including enemies), the pursuit of justice and peace, and the crucial link between thought and action. It advises readers to be true to themselves, discreet in speech while open to listening, and to ensure plans are followed by execution. From a historical perspective, the article reflects the moralistic and self-strengthening themes prevalent in Korean intellectual discourse during the early colonial period. For historians, it exemplifies the focus on individual character development and ethical principles, perhaps seen as a necessary foundation for national resilience or a subtle form of resistance in a politically constrained environment.

汝의 自身을 最後까지 愛하라. 그리고 汝를 憎하는 者를 慈愛하라. 邪惡보다는 正義의 便이 勝할 것이다. 힘오른 너의 右手로써 따수한 平和를 밧들고 猜疑만흔 그 舌을랑 沈默케하라. 汝의 最終目的을 同胞, 諸善神, 眞理에 두어라.
빼앗기고 웃는 者는 빼앗은 그者로부터 爲先 幾分을 빼앗은 셈이다.
汝自身에 對하야 忠實하라. 그러면 夜의 晝에 繼함과 如히 他人에 對하야도 忠實하게 되리라.
누구의 말에나 耳는 貸하라. 그러나 口는 如何한 人에게도 開하지 말라.
設計案이 如何히 나는 듯 네압장을 선다할지라도 實行이 그에 붓지 못하얏스면 헛꼿뿐이다. 마음으로 낫는 그 卽時에 손으로도 나으라.＜63＞
