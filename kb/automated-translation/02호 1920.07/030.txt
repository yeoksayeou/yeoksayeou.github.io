小兒
						눈메

罪惡의 過去에 苦痛의 現在를
暗黑에 彷徨타 가사탄의 窟에 빠진 慘狀
흐르는 젓을 일코 단꿀을 엇지 못한다.
구진비 사나온 바람 겹지어 몰아
弱한 그 얼골에 私情업시 부듸칠 때

運命을 咀呪하는 惡魔의 소리
無聊하고 凄涼한 그 마음을 찔린다.
絶望의 그 瞬間에 無意識한 부르지즘을
蒼空에 반작이는 새ㅅ별 빗이 고음
重濁한 空氣를 헤치고 빗을 논다.
마음의 轉機에 서잇는 그의 生命
希望의 거름에 새 未來를 맛는다.＜17＞

Little Child
						Nunme

In a past of sin, a present of pain,
Wandering in darkness, a惨状 (miserable state) fallen into Satan's den (가사탄의 窟),
Losing flowing milk, unable to get sweet honey.
When drizzling rain and fierce winds blow together
Striking that weak face without mercy (私情업시),

The devil's voice cursing fate
Pierces that tedious and desolate heart.
In that moment of despair, an unconscious cry (無意識한 부르지즘),
The light of a new star (새ㅅ별) twinkling in the blue sky is beautiful,
Parting the heavy, murky air, it sheds light (빗을 논다).
His life stands at a turning point (轉機) of the heart,
Meeting a new future in the steps of hope. <17>

**Summary**

The poem "Little Child" by Nunme portrays the suffering of a child, symbolizing perhaps a vulnerable entity or even the nation, trapped between a sinful past and a painful present. Lost in darkness and deprived of sustenance, the child faces harsh realities and despair, hearing demonic curses. However, a moment of unconscious outcry coincides with the appearance of a hopeful new star, whose light pierces the gloom, signaling a turning point and the possibility of a new future founded on hope. From a historical perspective, this poem, published in *Gaebyeok* in 1920, likely uses the child as a metaphor for Korea under Japanese rule. The suffering, despair, and eventual glimmer of hope would resonate with the national sentiment of the time, reflecting the hardships of colonial life while expressing a yearning for liberation and a brighter future. Historians might find the poem's allegorical nature and its expression of nascent hope amidst oppression particularly interesting.
