잡지명	개벽 제2호
발행일	1920년 07월 25일
기사제목	山元 갈마半島에서 (From Kalma Peninsula, Sanwon)
필자	잔물
기사형태	시 (Poem)

山元 갈마半島에서
						잔물

사람도 업―는 葛麻半島에
幽寂을 차즈려 배를 다히고
돌길을 더듬어 섬 끝에 서니
물결이 저혼자 소리처 논다.

낫볏을 쪼이며 바위에 안저
바다끗 저便에 머리만 뵈는
머나먼 連山을 바라볼때에
물넘어 北國이 그리워지네.

우리네 同胞가 만히 산다는
間島란 곳에도 이리 가려니
덧업는 생각에 눈물짓는데
밭밑엔 물소리 悽然이 난다.

쓸쓸히 夕陽은 지려하는데
永興灣 어구에 외로뜬 돗은
어대로 가는지 말업시잇고
바다는 寂寂히 저믈어간다. ＜129＞

**Translation**

From Kalma Peninsula, Sanwon
						Chanmul

On Kalma Peninsula (葛麻半島) where there are no people—
Seeking solitude (幽寂), I moored the boat
Fumbled along the stone path and stood at the island's edge
The waves alone cry out and play.

Basking in the afternoon sun, sitting on a rock
At the edge of the sea, on that side ( 저便), only the peaks are visible
When I gaze at the distant mountain range (連山)
I long for the Northern Land (北國) across the water.

Where many of our brethren (同胞) are said to live
Even to the place called Kando (間島), I might go like this
As I shed tears at the transient thought
Beneath my feet, the sound of water rises sorrowfully (悽然).

Lonely, the evening sun (夕陽) is about to set
In the mouth of Yŏnghŭng Bay (永興灣), a lonely sail (돗)
Floats silently, where is it going?
The sea grows dark in silence (寂寂). <129>

**Summary**

This poem by "Chanmul" describes a solitary visit to the uninhabited Kalma Peninsula. Seeking quietude, the speaker observes the natural surroundings – the waves, the distant mountains across the sea, and the setting sun over Yŏnghŭng Bay. This contemplation evokes a deep sense of longing for the "Northern Land" and specifically Kando, a region in Manchuria where many Koreans lived, prompting tears and feelings of melancholy that mirror the desolate beauty of the darkening sea. For historians, the poem is interesting as it captures a sense of displacement and yearning common among Koreans during the colonial period. The specific mention of Kando, a significant destination for Korean migrants and exiles (often linked to the independence movement), alongside the general feeling of sorrow and longing for compatriots ("Tongp'o"), provides insight into the emotional landscape and national consciousness of the time.
