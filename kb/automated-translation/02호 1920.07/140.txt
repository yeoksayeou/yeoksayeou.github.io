풋사랑

처음 올린 부드런 검은 머리의
사과나무 그늘에 나타날 때에
아름다운 그 소리 고흔 그 姿態
아지못할 핏덩이 내게 뛰도다

입 다물고 침묵턴 우리의 누리
孤獨 煩悶 애타던 暗暗한 내 맘
곱고도 달디단 너의 그 소리
길이길이 늘 듣기 내 所願일세＜50＞

외따로이 쓸쓸한 그대의 귀여
불어가는 北風을 琵琶로 듣고
눈물 많은 그대의 쌀쌀한 눈은
色彩 없는 石巖도 꽃으로 보게

앎이 없는 새파란 어린 내 소리
타고 끓는 가슴에 거슬려 듣고
두 손으로 따뜻이 내 손목 쥐소
새로 뜬 별 빛 따라 걸음 같이케＜51＞

***

**English Translation**

Puppy Love

When your soft black hair, first put up,
Appeared in the shade of the apple tree,
That beautiful voice, that elegant posture (姿態),
An unknowable lump of blood leaps within me.

Our world, silent with lips sealed,
My dark (暗暗) heart, anxious with loneliness (孤獨) and anguish (煩悶),
Your voice, lovely and sweet,
To hear it always, forever, is my wish (所願).<50>

Your lonely ear, standing alone,
Hearing the blowing North Wind (北風) as a Pipa (琵琶),
Your tearful, cool eyes
See even colorless stone cliffs (石巖) as flowers (꽃).

My young, naive voice, lacking knowledge,
Heard gratingly in my burning, boiling heart,
Grasp my wrist warmly with both hands,
Let us walk together following the light of a newly risen star.<51>

**Summary**

This poem, "Puppy Love," captures the intense emotions of first love experienced by a young speaker. It describes the beloved's captivating appearance and voice, the speaker's overwhelming internal reaction ("unknowable lump of blood leaps within me"), feelings of loneliness and longing, and a desire for connection and guidance ("grasp my wrist warmly... let us walk together following the light of a newly risen star"). From a historical perspective, published in the influential nationalist magazine *Kaebyŏk* in 1920, the poem's themes of youthful yearning, emotional intensity, and the desire for guidance towards a brighter future ("newly risen star") might subtly reflect the broader anxieties and hopes of Koreans seeking direction and identity during the early Japanese colonial period. The use of Hanja and older Korean orthography is also characteristic of texts from this era.
