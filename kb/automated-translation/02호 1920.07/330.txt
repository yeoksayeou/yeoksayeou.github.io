The Belly and the Limbs

One day, the hands, feet, mouth, and legs held a meeting and said, "We are busy day and night getting food, but the belly does nothing and just eats. What kind of unfairness (경계) is this? From today, let's make a pact: the hands shall not put a spoonful of rice into the mouth, the mouth shall not chew a morsel of food, and the feet and legs shall go nowhere." The belly said nothing and let them proceed with their meeting. However, after not many days had passed, the hungrier the belly became, the less energy the limbs had, the mouth lacked even the strength to speak, and the legs could not move an inch.

Only then did the belly speak, saying, "Getting food is your job; digesting it is my job. Without you, I cannot live, and without me, you cannot live either. We must each do our assigned tasks well and help each other. If we don't do so and each only thinks of themselves, we will be ruined."<116>

**Summary**

This article retells the classic fable of "The Belly and the Members." The limbs (hands, feet, mouth, legs) complain that the belly does nothing but receive food while they do all the work. They decide to stop working, but soon find themselves weak and unable to function as the belly starves. The belly then explains that each part has a vital role and they must cooperate for the survival of the whole body. From a historical perspective, this fable, published in 1920s colonial Korea, could be interpreted by historians as a call for national unity and cooperation among Koreans facing external pressures or internal divisions. It emphasizes interdependence and the dangers of internal conflict or prioritizing individual parts over the collective well-being, a potentially relevant message during a period of occupation and nascent nationalism.

배와 수족
하로는 손과 발과 입과 다리가 회의 하야 말하되 「우리는 음식 어더 들이기에 주야 분주하되 배는 아모 것도 안코 먹고만 잇스니 이런 경계가 어대 잇나. 오늘부터 우리 약조하고 손은 밥 한 술 입에 너치 말고 입은 음식 한 꼬물 씁지 말고 발과 다리는 아모데도 가지마자」하매 배는 아모 말도 안코 회의 하는대로 두엇더니 몃칠 지나지 못하야 배가 곱흘사록 족은 긔운이 업고 입은 말할 힘도 업고 다리는 꼼짝할 수 업는지라. 배가 그제야 말하되 「음식을 어더오기는 너희 일이오.소화하기는 내 일이니 너희가 업서도 나 못살고 나 업서도 너희 못 살 터이니 각기 마튼 일 잘하야 서로 도아 주어야 하지 그러치 안코 각기 제 몸만 알면 결단나느니라」 하더라.＜116＞
