기사제목	人乃天의 硏究 (Study of 'Man is Heaven')
필자	夜雷 (Yarae)
기사형태	논설 (Essay/Editorial)

人乃天의 硏究
						夜雷

一. 宗敎의 改革의 必要 (Need for Religious Reform)

Philosopher Mr. "Hwin'giriman" discussed the causes of religious decline, dividing them into four types.
First is the conflict between religious scripture and societal knowledge and moral progress. That is, the content of religion was initially thought to be based on so-called divine revelation, thus absolutely unshakeable. However, because individual knowledge and morality cannot originally be made to obey the commands of the church, conflict arises here. Second is the conflict between an individual's creed and the church's creed. That is, a church, as an association, cannot exist even for a day without fixed creeds and rituals. Why? Because these are the necessary ties that bind the believers together. Thus, it is a fact that the church needs everyone to fully cooperate with its prescribed creeds and rituals. However, this is impossible to practice in reality. Why? Because A does not accept this doctrine, B does not accept that doctrine, and it is unavoidable in the progress of human culture that each person's subjective beliefs cannot be entirely identical. Third is the conflict between the laity and the clergy. That is, <64> the entity called the church is originally established upon order, and to unify doctrines and rituals and handle various external affairs, it naturally requires special standing personnel. Thus, is it not the natural course of things that those appointed to these positions of standing personnel have great power over the general believers? However, Christian doctrine teaches the equality of all people before God, and furthermore, as human intellect gradually advances, the interpretation of scriptures reaches a point where it no longer requires the trouble of the clergy's hand, leading to an inevitable conflict between the two – this cannot be avoided. Fourth is the conflict between the church and the state, thus predicting the decline of religion from the past. Indeed it is so. The advanced human intellect of today is such that even without the insight of a philosopher, one can sufficiently expose such fallacies.

Generally, the origins of religions mostly lie in the ancient past of 2,000 or 3,000 years ago. Therefore, no matter how perfect their doctrines or clear their creeds may be, they naturally cannot keep pace with the knowledge of an advanced era, let alone when the founders of those religions established doctrines and tenets using allegories and drawing analogies suitable for the minds of the people of their time.

Since the Renaissance movement, the flourishing of scholarship eventually led to a more profound method of studying religion, shaking the concept of faith towards religion from its foundation. Originally, the Renaissance movement was a Greek naturalist ideology that arose in opposition to Christian thought; in other words, it could be called a carnal ideology arising from spiritual resistance. This ideology <65> confronted religion with extraordinary force throughout the 18th and 19th centuries. Consequently, the minds of the people of the time eventually leaned towards materialist thought, and there were even scholars who asserted that the universe and human life were organized solely of matter. The case of Germany's Nietzsche, who advocated extreme carnal instinctualism and went so far as to recklessly assert that Christianity was a kind of fraudulent deceiver, allows us to glimpse a facet of the general thought. However, how can humans live by flesh alone? Those who lost the center of faith due to the flourishing of science ultimately sought the shore of refuge (彼岸) in materialism, but without any unique gain, all merely ended in failure. Consequently, they doubted and agonized. The effect of material worship ultimately settled into agnosticism. Agnosticism consequently bred doubt, and doubt consequently bred agony. Agony is the great enemy of solace. How can humanity, possessing spiritual consciousness, be at peace even for a moment after losing solace? Thus, they came to recognize the value of religion again. What is called the fin-de-siècle skepticism and sorrow refers precisely to this; the intellectual world of an era sunk in doubt and agony wandered for a while in darkness. Heavenly destiny circulates, and history repeats itself. The human mind, weary of agony for some time, came to yearn for the value of faith. However, their so-called faith was not a demand for the reeking, stale old faith, but rather a new faith expressed through new truth. In other words, it was a modern faith made perfect and flawless by extracting only the unchanging truth from among the various old faiths, melting it together, harmonizing scientific thought with it, and adding philosophical ideals. Thus, modern faith would be a faith that, when questioned by religion, does not contradict doctrine; when questioned by science, does not violate science; <66> and when queried by philosophy, is suitable for philosophy. The demand of the modern era is truly such a faith. Therefore, it is certainly modern thought that they seek to achieve the unification of religion through this new faith.

Now, the theory of religious unification is common in both East and West throughout the modern era, but to cite one opinion representative of this thought, let me introduce a passage from the famous Mr. Ukita Kazutami's "Theory of Future Religion" in Japan. He states:
"I think that the religion of the future will likely emerge from such harmonization. First, the current religions like Buddhism, Confucianism, or Christianity will mutually approach and fuse, eventually leading to the natural emergence of a unified and integrated new religion. Thus, this new religion, viewed from the perspective of Buddhism, can be called an evolution of Buddha; viewed from the perspective of Christianity, it can be called the development of Christianity; viewed from the perspective of Confucianism, it can be called the completion of Confucianism. It must be something that never clashes with each other, nor does one overthrow the others to solely dominate the world, but rather becomes a fused entity. Originally, since the three teachings all have their respective characteristics and strengths, it is [a religion] that has absorbed the strengths of each religion and finally fused them. (Omitted) Originally, the principal object of worship (本尊) in religion is an inconceivable reality, so calling it an ideal is possible, but simply calling it reality is not; thus, living based on the influence of this formless ideal was not an easy task. The likes of Shakyamuni, Confucius, and Christ were able to do this and transmit their ideals to later generations, but such acts are truly not something ordinary people can do. Ordinary people necessarily <67> receive the influence of the formless ideal only through tangible things or persons. Thus, religions manifested through things were inferior religions, and those manifested through persons were superior religions. Truly, the principal object of worship in religion is the ideal, hence it came to be called Heaven or God. Accordingly, the person who realizes this has historically been called the Son of Heaven or the Son of God. However, the two are originally one, not two; whether called the ideal or the incarnation of the ideal, the essence is the same, merely one being the abstract ideal and the other being the concrete ideal."

"Next, even regarding the true God of Christianity, if one says, like the transcendentalists, that God exists outside the universe, then the refutation by atheists would not be unreasonable either. Originally, whether the universe has an inside and outside or not, and whether the universe has limits or not, are beyond the capacity of human intellect to investigate. However, is the assertion that God exists outside the universe and governs the universe not an arbitrary judgment and superstition (臆斷迷信) that humans ultimately cannot believe? Therefore, the likes of Buddhism went so far as to declare everything as void."

"Now, the God we know is the God who exists within (and without) the universe. As Christ said, 'Look at the birds of the air,' and also, 'Consider the lilies of the field,' the Father is one who can be seen through the birds of the air and the lilies of the field. However, believing that God exists outside the universe and seeking communication with such a being, or desiring direct manifestation from it, is a faith that cannot be substantiated by reason. The true God exists within all things, exists among us, and thus we live, move, and exist relying on God. <68> Therefore, saying that one sees God in all things is a religious truth, consistent with what Buddhists call 'all things possess Buddha-nature'."

"Therefore, the distinction called monotheism or pantheism (汛神敎) is already a difference not worth discussing. It must be a religion of all phenomena, that is, of reality."

"Generally, religion originally requires a foundation beyond ethics, i.e., supra-ethical, but it certainly did not disregard ethics. Now, looking at the three teachings—Confucianism, Buddhism, and Christianity—this ethical element has already splendidly equipped the ultimate. For example, what Buddhism calls compassion (慈悲), what Confucianism calls 'Do not impose on others what you yourself do not desire' (己所不欲勿施於人), and what Christianity calls universal love (博愛) are already at the pinnacle of ethics, so it would be impossible to create something new separately for self and other, this and that. Although the element of knowledge (知) can be said to be prepared in this way, the method of its application is ultimately incompatible with today's civilization."

"Broadly speaking, Buddhism has potential from the standpoint of 'seeing nature and awakening the mind' (見性覺心), but it has a tendency to drift towards world-weariness. Confucianism has merits in its worldly aspect, but because it excessively focuses only on the present life, it cannot avoid a somewhat regressive tendency. Christianity's tendency to accompany social activity is good, but various doctrinal harms accompany it. To cover it with one belief, each religion, while having its own strengths, also has its own weaknesses, ultimately failing to be called perfect. From here, when they mutually absorb their strengths and assimilate, I believe a perfect new religion will emerge for the first time. (Rest omitted)" <69> he stated. Of course, Mr. Ukita's argument, viewed from a religious standpoint, sought to artificially fuse the strengths of each religion, even if minimally. Whether artificial or natural, we submit to the logic of his argument from the perspective of the ideal. We further think that religious faith is certainly not something that can be dictated by human will; at the very least, the foundation that makes religion religion will be obtained through mystical or divinely revealed suggestion. Under self-aware confidence, the thought of Teacher Ch'oe Suun (崔水雲) can truly be said to have transcendently opened the foundation for religious unification through divinely inspired great awakening. This is sufficiently proven by the Teacher's faith throughout his life and his thoughts and actions.

However, the times create the person, and the person, in turn, creates the times. No matter how divinely inspired a religious figure may be, without the support of the era, they ultimately cannot unfold their thought. For example, the birth of the great practitioner of ethics, Confucius, during the time when the theories of the Hundred Schools of Thought were flourishing in China's Spring and Autumn period; the emergence of the great Shakyamuni, who advocated freedom and equality amidst the ills (弊瘼) of India where the caste ideology of Brahmanism established the doctrines; and the birth of Jesus in Judea, where the whole generation yearned for the Son of God amidst the prophets running rampant—all can be said to be products of their times. Thus, the Great Divine Teacher's (大神師) idea of religious unification must also have received some influence according to the time and place.

Dr. of Literature Takahashi Tōru (高橋亨), under the title "History of the Theory of Three Teachings Harmony Regarding Korea," presented thoughts generally as follows:
"The harmony of the three teachings—Confucianism, Buddhism, and Daoism (儒彿仙 三敎合一)—can be called the highest ideal of the Korean people regarding philosophy and religion, and the culmination of their speculation. <70> Therefore, the harmony of the three teachings was a major banner for Korean religion, nay, East Asian religion. When Teacher Ch'oe Cheu (崔濟愚), the founder of Tonghak (東學), the predecessor of Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎) and Sich'ŏn'gyo (侍天敎), initiated Tonghak, his advocacy (標榜) lay therein. When Ha Sangyŏk (河相易) established Taejonggyo (大宗敎), he also founded its doctrine upon the fusion (渾融) of the three teachings. Speaking of the harmony of the three teachings, it can be said that for Koreans, there is no higher and more profound thought than this. Consequently, the theory of three teachings harmony regarding Korea became the highest point of thought and faith, the ultimate reach of philosophy and religion, possessing extremely vast spiritual power. Therefore, Ch'ŏndogyo and Sich'ŏn'gyo invariably make use of this. And in studying the historical course of this theory, depending on the waxing and waning of Confucianism and Buddhism, those who advocated it differed; that is, when Buddhism flourished, the theory of three teachings harmony was advocated from the Confucian side, and when Confucianism flourished, the theory of three teachings harmony was advocated from the Buddhist monks' side. This is truly interesting data. Currently, this has become the three teachings harmony of Ch'ŏndogyo and Sich'ŏn'gyo, possessing several million believers. Truly, the lineage of the theory of three teachings harmony originated from Buddhist monks."

"What does the theory of three teachings harmony entail? While Confucianism, Buddhism, and Daoism each have their strengths and weaknesses, viewed in terms of the essence (眞髓) of the three teachings, they are mutually permeable and there is nothing to conflict (相鑿) about. The followers of the three teachings disregard the great commonality of their principles and foolishly quarrel, adhering only to minor differences. It is clear that the theory of three teachings harmony arose at the end of the disputes among the three teachings, and also, due to its advocacy, it was sufficient to mitigate the obstinate (固執的) prejudice of the three teachings. The theory of three teachings harmony began in the intellectual world of China's Six Dynasties; <71> its simultaneous flourishing is known to everyone. When Fu Dashi (傅大士) had an audience with Emperor Wu of Liang (梁武帝), he wore a Confucian cap, Daoist shoes, and a Buddhist kasaya. The emperor found it unusual and asked, 'Are you a Confucian?' Dashi pointed to his robe. 'Are you a Daoist (仙)?' Dashi pointed to his shoes. 'Are you a Buddhist?' Dashi pointed to his kasaya. Also, Zhang Rong (張融), on his deathbed, left instructions to hold the Classic of Filial Piety and the Laozi in his left hand, and the Small Perfection of Wisdom Sutra and the Lotus Sutra in his right hand. Simultaneously, in his work 'Tongyuan' (通源), there is a dialogue with Zhou Yong (周顒). Regarding the fundamental substance (本軆) of the Way (道), Confucianism, Buddhism, and Daoism all converge at one pole; what follows is merely its trace, he argued, attempting to harmonize the three teachings. This theory, after passing through the development of Song dynasty Neo-Confucianism, flourished greatly by the Ming (明) dynasty. Setting aside Song Lian (宋濂), Wang Shouren (王守仁), etc., Buddhist monks who followed this view emerged one after another; Lin Zhaoen (林兆恩), Yang Shen (楊愼), Yuan Hongdao (袁宏道), Yuan Zhongdao (袁中道), etc., belong to the former, while Hanshan (憨山) and Zhixu (藉益) belong to the latter. In essence, after the emergence of the learning of Zhou (周), Cheng (程), Zhu (朱), Lu [likely referring to Lu Jiuyuan, contemporary/rival of Zhu Xi, though only 隆 appears], Wang (王) [Wang Yangming], the theory of Confucian-Buddhist-Daoist harmony flourished even more. Simultaneously, those versed in the three teachings came to be recognized as if they had integrated the entire thought of China. Korea, whose thought and faith were originally solely within the domain of China, received the three teachings from China. Accordingly, the first proponent of the theory of three teachings harmony was Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn (崔致遠) of late Silla (新羅). Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn's life story itself manifested the harmony of the three teachings through facts. That is, he crossed to China to study Confucianism, also became close to Buddhism and comprehended Buddhist studies, and in his later years, detached from the world, entered Mount Chiri (智異山), and finally became a Daoist immortal (仙) – such was he. (To be continued)" <72>

**Summary**

This 1920 essay by "Yarae" argues for the necessity of religious reform and unification in the modern era. It outlines reasons for the decline of traditional religions, such as conflicts with modern knowledge, individual beliefs, and societal structures (state, clergy/laity). The author discusses the rise and fall of materialism (citing Nietzsche) and the subsequent yearning for a new, comprehensive faith harmonizing religious truth, scientific understanding, and philosophical ideals. The essay introduces Japanese scholar Ukita Kazutami's vision of a future unified religion synthesizing the strengths of Buddhism, Confucianism, and Christianity. The author then posits that the Tonghak founder Ch'oe Suun's "Innaech'ŏn" (Man is Heaven) philosophy represents a divinely inspired Korean precedent for such religious unification. Finally, it incorporates Japanese historian Takahashi Tōru's research on the historical tradition of "Three Teachings Harmony" (Confucianism, Buddhism, Daoism) in Korea, tracing it from Silla's Ch'oe Ch'iwŏn to its influence on Tonghak (Ch'ŏndogyo), framing it as a core aspect of Korean religious thought. From a colonial history perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects Korean intellectual engagement with global trends (modernism, religious studies, Western philosophy) and Japanese thought (Ukita, Takahashi) while simultaneously asserting a unique Korean spiritual path rooted in Tonghak and the historical "Three Teachings Harmony." It highlights the complex intellectual landscape where Korean thinkers sought to define a modern identity by both drawing from and differentiating themselves from East Asian traditions and Western/Japanese influences during a period of intense cultural and political pressure.

一. 宗敎의 改革의 必要
哲學者 「횐길이만」氏는 由來 宗敎의 衰頹原因을 4種에 分하야 論하엿는대
第1은 宗敎의 經典과 社會의 知識 及 道德進步의 衝突이니 卽 宗敎의 內容은 其 初에 所謂 神의 啓示에 本한 者인 則 絶對的으로 動撓함이 不可하다 思惟하엿더니라. 然한데 個人의 知識과 道德과는 元來-是 敎會의 命令을 服從케 함을 不得하기 때믄에, 玆에 衝突이 起할 것이라 하엿스며 第2는 一個人의 信條와 敎會의 信條과의 衝突이니 卽 敎會는 一個의 結社로 一定의 信條 及 禮儀가 업고는 1日도 存在함을 不得할지라. 何者오. 是 信者를 團結케 하는 須要의 結蔕임으로써라. 然하면 敎會는 人人이 全히 그 規程의 信條 及 禮儀에 協同케 할 事를 要할 것은 事實이라. 然이나 是 實際에는 行키 不能하나니 何故오 하면 甲은 此 敎義를 不取하며 乙은 彼 敎義를 不取하고 各其 主觀的 所信이 全히 同一키 不能함은 人文의 進步上 不得已라 하엿스며 第3은 俗人과 僧侶과의 衝突이니 卽 敎會라＜64＞ 하는 者는 元來 秩序上에 立한 것임으로써 敎義 儀禮를 統一하며 及 諸般의 外面的 事務를 處理키 爲함에는 스스로 特別의 常務員을 要케 되는 것이라. 그리하야 是의 常務員의 任에 當한 者가 一般信者에 對하야 大한 權力이 有함은 盖 自然의 勢가 안일 것이냐. 然한데 基督敎의 敎義는 문득 神의 前에는 萬人의 平等을 訓하고 且 人智 漸進과 共히 經典의 解釋은 又 僧侶의 手를 煩치 아니케 됨에 至하야 2者의 間에 必然的 衝突이 起케 됨은 是-避치 못할 것이라 하엿스며 第4 敎會와 國家와의 衝突이라 하야 由來 宗敎의 衰頹를 預言하엿섯다. 果然하다. 進步한 今日의 人智는 實로 哲人의 識見이 안일지라도 能히 그만한 誤謬를 披露하리만 하다.
盖 由來-宗敎는 그 淵源이 大槪 2, 3千年의 古昔에 在한 것이라. 故로 아모리 그 敎理가 圓滿하고 信條가 뚜렷하다 할지라도 그가 自然히 進步한 時代知識과 伴行치 못할 것이거늘 하물며 그 敎祖된 者-立敎當時에 그 時代의 人心에 適合하도록 敎理와 敎條를 寓言으로 設하며 比喩로 引한 者리오.
彼 文藝復興運動以來-學術의 勃興은 遂-宗敎의 硏究方法도 一層 深刻하야진 까닭에 宗敎에 對한 信仰의 觀念은 根底로부터 動搖하게 되엇다. 元來-文藝復興運動이라 함은 基督敎的 思想의 反抗으로 起한 希臘的 自然主義의 思想이니 更言하면 靈的 反抗으로 起한 肉的 思想이라 할지라. 此 思想은 18, 19兩世紀에 亙하야 非常한 勢力으로＜65＞ 宗敎를 對抗하엿도다. 於是에 一世의 人心은 遂-物質思想에 傾하야 宇宙와 人生을 唯一의 物質로 組織된 者라 斷言한 學者까지 잇섯다. 彼 獨逸의 「니체」와 如함은 極端한 肉的 本能主義를 主唱하야 基督敎로써 一種 虛僞의 詐欺師라 妄斷함에 至한 것으로써 可히 一般思想의 一端을 窺知하겟다. 然이나 人이 엇지 肉뿐으로만 生活할 것이랴. 科學의 勃興으로 信仰의 中心을 失한 그네들은 畢竟 歸依의 彼岸을 物質에 求코저 하얏스나 唯一의 所得이 업시 悉皆失敗에 歸할 뿐이엇섯다. 於是에 그네들은 懷疑하고 煩悶하엿다. 物質崇拜의 效果는 究竟 不可知에 落着하고 말앗다. 不可知는 遂-懷疑를 胚胎하고 懷疑는 遂-煩悶을 胚胎하엿다. 煩悶은 慰安의 大敵이라. 慰安을 失하고 靈識이 잇는 人類로 엇지 一時인들 平安하랴. 그래서 그들은 다시 宗敎의 價値를 認識케 되엇다. 이른바 世紀末 懷疑悲哀라 함은 곳 이를 稱한 것인데 懷疑煩悶에 빠진 一代의 思想界는 한참동안 暗黑의 中에서 헤매엿다. 天運은 巡還하는 것이며 歷史는 繰返되는 것이다. 얼마동안 煩悶에 退屈한 人心은 거의 信仰의 價値를 渴望하야왓다. 그러나 그들의 이른바 信仰은 腐臭陳陳한 舊信仰을 다시 要求하는 것이 아니오 오즉 新眞理로써 表顯된 新信仰이엇다. 다시 말하자면 여러 舊信仰의 中으로 오즉 不變의 眞理를 摘取하야 그를 融化케 하고 又 此에 科學的 思想을 調和하며 哲學的 理想을 加添하야 圓滿無缺케 된 現代的 信仰이엇다. 然하면 現代的 信仰이라 함은 宗敎에 問하야 敎理에 不悖하며 科學에 問하야 科學에 不違하며＜66＞ 哲學에 質하야 哲學에 適合한 信仰이겟다. 現代의 要求는 實로 이러한 信仰이겟다. 그리하야 그들은 新信仰으로써 宗敎의 統一을 圖코저 함은 確實히 現代思想이겟다.

盖 宗敎는 元來-倫理 以上 卽 超倫理의 根底가 必要하나 然하나 決코 倫理를 無視하는 것은 안이엇다. 이제 儒佛基 3敎에 就하야 見하건대 此 倫理的 要素도 旣히 究竟의 者를 훌륭히 具備하고 잇다. 例하면 佛敎에 慈悲라 云하며 儒敎에 己所不欲勿施於人이라 云하며 基督敎에 博愛라 云함과 如함은 어느덧 倫理의 極點에 在한 故로 我他, 彼此에 別로 新한 者를 造成키는 不可能할 것이로다. 如斯히 知의 要素는 備하엿다 할지나 그 應用의 方法이 今日의 文明과 到底 相伴치 못하는 것이라 할지로다.
大軆-佛로 말하면 見性覺心이라 하는 點에서 本能이 有하나 然이나 厭世的으로 流하는 傾向이 잇스며 又 儒敎와 如함은 現世的의 點에서 可取할 바 잇스나 過度히 現生뿐 主하는 故로 多少 退化的 氣味를 不免하며 又 基督敎는 그 社會活動과 共伴하는 傾向은 好하나 敎義上 種種의 弊害가 此에 伴하나니 一信으로 蔽하면 各敎-제각금 長所가 有함과 共히 또 제각금 短處가 有하야 到底 完璧이라 稱치 못할지라. 玆에서 互相 그 長所를 吸收하야 同化케 하는 時에는 처음으로 圓滿한 新宗敎가 되리라 思하노라 (下略)＜69＞ 하엿다. 勿論 氏의 論은 宗敎上으로 見하야 各 宗敎의 長所를 少하야도 人爲的으로 融化코저 하엿도다. 人爲的이나 自然的이나 吾人은 그 理想의 點에서 氏의 論理를 服하는 바도다. 吾人은 更히 思컨대 宗敎의 信仰이란 決코 人爲로 專制할 者-못되는 것이오 少하야도 神秘的 或 天啓的 暗示에 依하야 宗敎의 宗敎되는 根底를 得하리라. 自信 아는 下에서 崔水雲 先生의 思想은 實로 神啓的 大覺으로 超然히 宗敎統一의 基礎를 開한 것이라 할지로다. 그는 先生 一代의 信仰과 밋 그의 思想行爲로써 넉넉히 이를 證明할 者인저.

文學博士 高橋亨氏는 朝鮮에 對한 三敎合一論의 歷史라 云한 題下에 大槪 左와 如한 思想을 發表하엿는대 曰
儒彿仙 三敎合一은 朝鮮人의 哲學 及 宗敎에 對한 最高 理想이며 그 思索의 窮極이라 할지라.＜70＞ 故로 三敎合一은 朝鮮宗敎, 안이 東洋宗敎의 一大看板이엇섯다. 天道敎 侍天敎의 前身이 東學의 開祖 崔濟愚先生이 東學을 일이키자 그 標榜이 이에 잇섯고 河相易이 大宗敎를 開立할새 또한 三敎 渾融上에 敎理를 세웟더라. 三敎合一을 말하건대 朝鮮人에 向하야는 이 以上 高尙하고도 深遠한 思想은 다시 업다 할지로다. 從하야 朝鮮에 對한 三敎合一論은 思想 및 信仰의 最高處요 哲學 及 宗敎의 極致가 되어 精神的 勢力이 至極히 廣大하도다. 그럼으로 天道敎 及 侍天敎는 매양 이를 利用함이 업지 안이하니라. 그리고 本論의 歷史的 經歷을 硏究함에 儒敎와 佛敎의 盛衰를 困하야 이를 唱하는 者의 잇슴이 不同하엿나니 곳 佛敎가 盛할 時에는 儒者側으로부터 三敎合一論을 唱하엿고 儒敎가 盛할 時는 僧侶側에서 三敎合一論을 唱하엿나니 此 實趣味 잇는 바 資料라 하리라. 現在에 이것이 天道侍天의 三敎合一이 되어 數百萬의 信徒를 有케 하엿도다. 實로 三敎合一論이란 그 系統이 僧侶에서부터 생기엇더라.
三敎合一論이란 무엇을 取함인고. 儒彿仙 3敎가 各各 長處가 잇슴과 共히 短處가 잇스나 3敎의 眞髓에 就하야 見하면 서로 融通되어 가히 相鑿할 바 업나니. 3敎의 徒-그 原理의 大同은 내어버리고 相違의 小異에만 固執하야써 爭함이 어리석다 하엿도다. 그 三敎合一論이란 3敎가 爭論한 終末에 생김이 分明하며 또는 그 唱導로 困하야 能히 3敎의 固執的  偏見을 緩和함에 足하리라. 三敎合一論은 支那六朝의 思想界에서 始하엿스며＜71＞同時에 勃興됨은 各人의 知하는 바로다. 傅大士가 梁武帝를 謁할새 儒冠道履袈裟를 披하매 帝-異常히 여기어 「儒이냐」 무른대 大士는 그 衣를 指하고 「仙이냐」 무른대 大士는 그 履를 指하고 「佛이냐」 무른대 大士는 袈裟를 指하엿스며 又 張融은 臨終에 遺言하야 左手에 孝經, 老子를 잡게하고 右手에 小品法華經을 잡게 함과 同時에 그 著書 通源에 周顒과 問答한 바 잇나니. 道의 本軆에 이르러는 儒彿仙 3敎가 共히 一極에 投한 바요. 그 다음은 그 跡일 뿐이라고 論하야 三敎의 調和를 謀하엿스며 此 說이 宋朝와 儒學의 發達을 經한 후-明에 至하야 盛行되엇더라. 宋濂 王守仁 등은 姑置不述하거니와 釋으로써 悅從하는 者-前後에 輩出하엿나니 林兆恩, 楊愼, 袁宏道, 袁中道 등은 前者에 屬하엿고 憨山藉益은 後者에 屬하엿도다. 要컨대 周, 程, 朱, 隆, 王의 學이 出한 以後로부터 儒彿仙合一論이 더욱 勃興케 되엇스며 同時에 三敎에 通한 자는 비롯오 支那 全思想을 統合함과 가티 認定케 되엇더라. 朝鮮은 元來-思想 及 信仰이 오로지 支那의 領域됨으로써 支那로부터 3敎가 傳來하엿고 딸아 三敎合一論을 首唱한 者는 新羅末의 崔致遠이니 崔致遠의 그 行蹟이야말로 三敎合一을 事實로써  表顯하엿도라. 곳 支那에 渡하야 儒學을 學하고 又는 佛敎에 親善하야 佛學을 通曉하엿스며 晩年에는 世와 離하야 智異山에 入하야 마참내 仙이 되엇다 하는 그임으로써라.
