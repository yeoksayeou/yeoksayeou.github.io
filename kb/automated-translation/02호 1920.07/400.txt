발행일	1920년 07월 25일

Play Script: On the Eve
						Hyŏn Ch'ŏl

Syubin: How refreshing must be the walk of that thick-headed old man and the hysterical lady. (A moment of silence)

Pyelsep'ev: (In a serious tone, as if to break the silence) Miss Elena, have you finished reading the manuscript I gave you recently?

Elena: Yes, well, not all of it yet.— It's somehow a bit difficult for me...

Syubin: What? What manuscript is it?

Pyelsep'ev: It's a posthumous manuscript of her late father.

Syubin: Oh. That thing from before, the one with the title about a foot long, written as "The Essence of Kantian Philosophy and Social and Ethical Something Something"?— You told Elena to read that?

Pyelsep'ev: Yes. Still, it was Father's life's work—and my lifelong task is also to inherit and complete it. It’s a major undertaking into which I’ll pour my heart and soul, so I thought I should ask Elena to look it over at least once.— But I feel very sorry for giving you something so lacking in interest (滋味).

Elena: (In an apologetic tone) No, no. It’s not that. It’s embarrassing to say, but I haven't read many books on philosophy... So—did your esteemed father (春府丈) spend his life studying Kant?

Pyelsep'ev: That's right. It was Father's belief that all human thought ultimately returns to Kant...

Syubin: Hey, hey, that's enough now. Are we going to have an open-air lecture on philosophy in this scorching heat? Enough chatter about the Bulgarian revolution this and the essence of Kantian philosophy that. Didn't we come out to have fun today?

Elena: Then what should we talk about?

Syubin: Stories with a bit more feminine charm. Look, everyone. <143> The sun shines brilliantly in the western sky like this, and the sky and water are the same shade of blue. Beautiful midsummer flowers are in full bloom in the mountains and fields. At a time like this, escorting (擁衛하여) our Elena, we should undertake a grand discussion on the beauty of the universe and the beauty of humanity.

Elena: If you want to chatter, chatter by yourself. We prefer stories other than that—

Syubin: You mean you prefer the Bulgarian revolution or Kant's philosophy? Stop lying. You don't even understand it.

Elena: Of course. I don't understand it at all. That's why I listen to the stories. Things like novels, plays, or fashionable clothes, like you talk about—even Mother and Soya know about those.

Syubin: Soya? So you're saying Soya is suitable for me? That sassy, slovenly German girl (獨逸種子의 게집아히) full of crap in her belly is suitable (合當하단) for me?

Elena: Who said such a thing? If you want to decide that yourself, go ahead.

Syubin: Elena. Do you really think of me that way? Ah.
(Overcome with emotion, tears well up)

Elena: Of course. I really dislike people like you who find fault (틔를 뜻는) when someone else is earnestly talking about something.

Pyelsep'ev: Please stop. Miss Elena, that's what they call an artist's stubbornness (固執). Please forgive him.

Elena: No. No matter how much of an artist one is, do they have the right (權利) to interfere (妨害) with another's freedom (自由) out of their own stubbornness? And what's so great about being an artist? Since coming out of his parents' womb (父母의 胎밧게), he hasn't produced a single satisfactory work of art (藝術作品).

Syubin: (In a fierce voice) Elena, there was a time when you too loved me because of (因綠하여) my art. Now my art is being treated with contempt (侮辱), like throwing away worn-out shoes. It has long since become a discarded object that cannot be recovered. Pyelsep'ev, you too, pay attention. It won't be long before your philosophy is utterly trampled and destroyed by Elena.

Elena: What do you mean—

Syubin: Ah, no. I mean there will come a time when the Bulgarian revolution will seem more appreciated than Kant's philosophy. <144> —(As if intentionally cutting off Elena, who is about to speak, in a loud voice) Let the pitiful loser in love, the useless sculptor Syubin, sing a song in celebration, uniting with the sounds of nature in this pitiable forest, for the sake of respected Kantian philosophy or the Bulgarian revolution. "O God, come, descend upon us all together, for the eternal happiness of Maria... Ah, wrong. For the eternal (永久) happiness of Elena—"
(Sounds of people. The maid Soya. Anna and Old Man Uwal enter from behind)

Soya: What on earth are you doing? Shouting so loudly, I wondered who it was—

Syubin: It's Syubin the madman, making a fuss (질알이야) by himself.— Ah, I'm already sick (실여젓다) of this country Russia (露西亞) and Russian people. I want to go abroad as soon as possible (하로 바삐). I want to go to Italy (伊太利).

Soya: Why (웨요)?

Syubin: (Deliberately correcting himself) Because there's sunshine and beautiful women (美人) there. (Looking at Soya) Oh, my mistake, my mistake. I forgot there was a beautiful woman right in front of my eyes (눈압헤). (Tries to kiss her)

Soya: (Dodging aside) What is this behavior! How can you be so rude (失禮) to a lady (貴婦人)?

Syubin: Humph, a lady, Madame. How splendid (훌륭하구려). Ee-reh-eh, hand, Madame. (Grabs Soya's hand and forcibly (無理로) kisses her)

Soya: (Intentionally acting flustered) Why are you doing this! (Saying this, she straightens up and knocks off the straw hat (麥藁帽) Syubin is wearing. As Syubin tries to pick it up, she quickly snatches it and runs away. This scene causes a brief commotion.)

Anna: (Who had been sitting absent-mindedly under the shade of the linden tree (菩堤樹) with Old Man Uwal since earlier. With a pleasant expression) Haha, they're still children. Why are they like that? Youth is the best time.

Uwal noin: (Doesn't answer but mumbles, his fingers twitching convulsively (痙攣的으로))
(At this moment, Soya suddenly lets out a piercing scream (悲痛한 소리). Everyone is startled. Syubin is playfully holding Soya by her small waist, letting go and grabbing her again.)

Pyelsep'ev: Soya seems to quite enjoy playing around too.

Anna: Syubin, what are you doing, like a child. <145>
(At this time, coachmen (馬車軍) and stable boys (馬夫) spread a tablecloth (테이불보) under the linden tree and lay out food (食物) and drinks (酒類).)

Anna: Alright everyone. Stop playing around— please sit down (안지시요).
(Everyone sits in a circle under the linden tree)

Anna: Well now, everyone, please eat plenty (만이들 잡수시요). (Giving a cup (盞) to Old Man Uwal) Here, Uncle (아지버니), have a drink. You worked hard (애만이 쓰셧슴니다) today.— Fortunately, the weather (日氣) is nice today, so it's very pleasant (愉快함니다). (Busily serving everyone (各各 接待하기에 奔走하다))

Pyelsep'ev: The weather really is nice (좃슴니다). (Accepting Anna's cup) Thank you very much (넘어 황송함니다). You look very well (얼골빗이 좆슴니다) today. You look about 20 years younger.

Anna: Do I look that way? It's because I'm still young (아즉 나히 젊으닛가). (Everyone laughs.)

Syubin: (Sitting next to Soya, idly fidgeting (? 집은 집은 하면서)) Auntie (아지머니), is your headache (머리 압흐신 것) a little better (나셧슴니가)?

Anna: Mm-hmm. It's okay (괜챤아).

Syubin: See? What did I tell you (내가 머어라고 합딋가)?

Anna: Well. Still, when I was coming out today, I heard this and that from your uncle and wasn't feeling good (조치 못하더니), but once I came out, it wasn't like that at all, just pleasant thoughts.— Come now everyone, eat plenty. They say you eat more when you eat out in the fields like this (이러케).
(Everyone eats the food (飮食). Here and there (여긔저긔서), people murmur and talk (중얼중얼 이약이를 한다))

Syubin: (Pulling Soya's knee (무릅) close and holding it tight) Ah, I'm getting sleepy (조으름이 온다). Hey (이얘) Soya, move over a bit. Let me dream of going to paradise (극낙).

Soya: (Intentionally moves away (避한다). Syubin tries to grab her, falls onto the grass (풀밧에), and notices the back of Insarov's neck, seemingly startled (깜작 놀라는 듯이))

Syubin: (Surprised) Hey, Insarov, what's that big scar (흠) on the back of your neck?

Insarov: (Blushing, lightly) Oh.

Syubin: (As if interrogating (追窮하는 것 가티)) Were you in a war (戰爭)? Who did you fight with (싸왓나)?

Insarov: No, what. It was over something trivial.

Elena: Isn't that rude (失禮)? Saying such things to someone for no reason—

Syubin: Hmmph. Was it? My apologies (잘못 되엇구려). Yes, indeed. One must respect (尊敬) the hero (英雄) of the revolution. (In a displeased tone (시원치 못한 語調로)) <146>
(At this moment, the setting sun over the lake (湖水) scatters red colors (色彩) in all directions (四面). Everyone applauds (一同이 喝采).)

Anna: (Sitting absent-mindedly (精神업시) with a pleasant feeling, opens her eyes at the sound.) Ah, what beautiful scenery (景致).

Syubin: Eh? Is it nice? After dozing off (조을다가) all this time. Now, it's gradually time to go home. Auntie.

Anna: Mm-hmm. Mm-hmm. Don't worry. (Listening intently) Isn't someone singing across the lake? Quite a good voice (목청이 퍽 조흔 걸). Soya, you too, don't just stand there chattering, since we're leaving, sing us a farewell song (餞別로). Today was truly enjoyable.

Syubin: (Suddenly shouts) That's right! I'll sing one. Can't I sing at least one song (한마듸)? (Forcibly pulls Soya's hand, makes her stand next to him) Come on, everyone, let's sing together (가티 합시다).

Soya: What should we sing (무얼 할가요)? "Lorelei" (『로오레리이』) would be good (조켓지요)?

Syubin: Good, good (조치, 조아).
(Everyone claps (一同拍手))

Syubin, Soya: (Singing together (合唱))

I know not what it means, within my heart
Why sorrow (슯흠) fills me so complete
A tale from times of old, a legend's art
That never fades, its memory sweet.

The evening sun (저녁 볏) is cool, the dusk (저믐(暮色)) descends
The flowing Rhine River (라인河) lies serene and deep
Blue sky, red glow, on peaks the light extends
A brilliant radiance (燦爛이 비친 光彩) the summits (峰) sweep.

Most strange and wondrous, on yonder height
A maiden (處女) fair, unseen on earth before
With jewels bright and graceful hands alight
She combs her flowing golden hair (黃金 머리)...

(Before the song ends, suddenly from the thicket (叢林間), shouts of 『Bravo (뿌라보오)』 『Bravo』 『Well done, well done』 『More, more』 are heard chaotically (亂雜히), followed immediately by the group of laborers (勞働者) seen earlier, appearing disheveled, some in vests, some in shirtsleeves, staggering drunkenly (亂醉하여).) <147>
(Soya sees the group of laborers and frowns, her expression saying, 'They're here again.')

Laborer A: Hey! Looks like fun (滋味나구려)! Hahahahahaha.

Laborer B: Hey! Are you done already? Lasses (시악시들), sing more, sing more! It's fine (괜챤타).

All Laborers: That's a good idea! Sing more, sing more. Sing lots, sing lots. Hahahahahaha.

Laborer A: (Approaching Anna) Bon-chew-well, Madame (? 뽄, 추우웰, 마담). My Lady (貴婦人), the weather (일긔) is very nice (좃슴니다) today. But My Lady. Why don't you make them sing more? Hahahahaha. But My Lady, make them sing just once more, let's all have fun together (우리다 가티 놀아야지). (Anna moves away (避한다).)

All Laborers: Good idea, good idea! You bet (? 네기 부틀것). So you won't make them sing again?

Syubin: (Stepping in front of Anna, stopping Insarov who is about to step forward in anger, in a composed voice) My respected foreign gentlemen (外國 紳士 여러분), listening to your words like this, I don't know if it's impolite (失禮), but you have greatly startled (非常이 놀라게 하엿슴니다) our party (一行). If my judgment (判斷) is not mistaken (틀리지 안이할 것 가트면), I believe you gentlemen (諸君) belong to the Saxon (색손) people among the Caucasian (고가시안) race. Therefore, I trust (밋는 바이 올시다) that you know what social etiquette (社交의 禮儀) entails. However, it is deeply regrettable (甚히 遺憾으로 생각하는 바이 올시다) that you, my respected sirs, wish to exchange impolite words (恭遜치 못한 言語) with a lady (貴婦人) to whom you have never been introduced (한번도 人事가 업는). As for my personal wish (一個人의 希望), I hope for an opportunity (機會) in the future (他日) to share an evening's pleasure (一夕의 歡樂) with you gentlemen. Why (웨그러냐 하면)? Because the muscles (筋肉) of your bodies (肉體), whether biceps (2頭筋) or triceps (3頭筋), are developed in a completely (全혀) ideal (理想的) way, and if I, who hold the honor (名譽) of being a sculptor (彫刻家), were to create a magnificent work (훌륭한 作品) using you gentlemen as models, that would indeed be my lifelong wish (平生 所望). However, unfortunately (不幸이), today is not the time for that, so please be on your way (자아 그만들 가시요).

Laborer A: (Everyone tilts their heads, shoulders slumped, listening to Syubin's speech. As soon as he finishes, shouts loudly) What? What's this? Damn it (? 이 네길 붓지), talking nonsense like a baby magpie seeing a snake (? 구렝이본 까치새끼 모양으로), spitting out difficult words nobody can understand (알아들을 수 업는 말을), you think you're the best (第一인 줄 아늬)? Fine, since we look like this, you probably thought we were old shoemakers (헌 구두 匠인줄). You'll get hurt thinking like that (큰코 다칠라). Hmph, even so, <148> if you look closely, we're government officials (官吏) working in the government office (官廳).

Syubin: Who said you weren't?—

Laborer A: Don't interrupt (떠떠떠들지 말아). Just spouting difficult words from your snout that others can't understand. What is this anyway? You lazy bums who just play and eat, looking down on us workers (職工)! You'll face divine punishment (天罰)! Even if we look like this, with these fists, I feed myself and my parents, wife, and children (父母妻子). If you think about it, isn't that right (그러챤타나)? We're different from parasites (밥벌레) like you who just play and eat. Can't we just get rid of (업새면 못스나) those damn parasites? (Clenches his fist and lunges at Syubin. Syubin dodges (避하다가) and bumps into Old Man Uwal. The old man grabs him with a comical gesture (웃으운 몸짓으로). The laborers see this and all laugh. Pyelsep'ev shields Anna and Soya, and Insarov shields Elena.) What we're saying isn't anything else. We told you to sing more earlier, why didn't you (웨 안해)? Tell us the reason why you didn't. I'm going to settle this (談判) with Madame. Forget about that stuff (그까짓 것은), there's something quicker (速히) than that. Old man... no no, the old man has no business here (일이 업서). That pretty young lady (입뿐 시악시) over there (pointing (가르치며) to Soya) or that beautiful lady (고운 각씨) over there (pointing to Elena), either one is fine (아모라도 조아). In our German language (獨逸말로), 'Kuss' (쿠쓰), here, we wish to have a kiss (입좀 마추기를 바라는 바요). (Pushes out his lips (입슐) and points with his finger (손가락으로 가르치며)) That's nothing (그까짓 것이야). It's not a difficult thing (어렵쟌은 일이야). Anyone can do it (누구던지 될 일이야). Hahahahaha.

Laborer B: That's a good idea! That's it. It's not difficult, not difficult at all (어렵잔치 어렵잔아). Kiss him, kiss him (마춰라 마춰). Hahahaha.

All Laborers: Kiss him, kiss him (마춰라 마춰).

Laborer A: (Spreading his arms and rushing forward) Come here, come here. (Tries to grab Soya. Soya screams and runs away (다라나니). Then he tries to grab Elena. Elena—with a dignified posture (儼然한 態度), unable to contain her anger (憤한 마음), her face turns pale and red (프르락 붉으락 하며), and her body trembles. Insarov hides Elena behind him and steps firmly in front of the laborer.)

Insarov: Get away, get away (저리 가오 저리 가).

Laborer A: Hmph. What now (무엇 엇재)? Get away? You get away. What right do you have to interfere? I come and go on my own feet (내 발로 가고오고 하는데), telling me to go or not go (가거라 말어라). What if I don't go (안가면 엇재)?

Insarov: (With an angry expression (憤怒한 顔色), calmly (沈靜이)) It's not about 'what'. You people are being extremely rude (넘어 無禮한 일) to the ladies (婦人). It's because you're drunk (술이 醉햇스니 그러치). <149>

Laborer A: Hehe, what now (무엇엇재)? Drunk (醉햇다)? So what if I'm drunk (醉햇스니 엇절 터이야)? Alright comrades (同官), supervisor (監督), did you all hear (다들엇소)? So that kid (저이가) says to an official (官吏) 'drunk', 'go away'. Maah, let's forget all that talk (마아 그까짓 말은 다 그만두고), now, we need to get that kiss (자아 입을 마추기 해야지 입을)—(Clenches his fist and lunges)

Insarov: You wicked fellows. Try that one more time (한번만 더 그래보아).

Laborer A: What'll you do if I do it again (더 그러면 엇절 터야 엇재)? (Points his finger accusingly (산때질을 한다))

Insarov: What else? Throw you into the lake (湖水에 집어너치).

Laborer A: Throw me into the lake (湖水에 집어 넛는다)? Hey, this is really getting interesting (滋味난 일이로구나)! Oh ho! Supervisor, did you hear (監督들엇소)? That's all (겨오 고뿐이야)? Just throw me in the lake and that's it (湖水에만 집어너코 말터이야)? Go ahead, try throwing me in (어대 한번 집어 너허보아)! (Lunges aggressively (밧싹 덤빈다))

Insarov: (Grabs the lunging laborer by the collar (멱살) and simply flips (채치니) him, sending him tumbling backward (걱구로) down the bank (언덕미테). A 'plop' sound comes from the water. The women (女子돌) scream 'He fell in!', the laborers frantically cause a commotion (惶忙이 騷動을 하며), yelling from below (? 밋태 서는) 'Save him!' (사람 살니라고), while the laborers shout 'This is terrible!' and rush down to the lakeside (湖水가로 나려갓다).)

Anna: (In a crying voice) He's sinking, sinking (저것 갈안네 갈안져)! Someone has to save him! Save him (건저)...

Insarov: (In a calm tone (冷靜한 語調)) Leave him alone (가만 내버려 두셔요). He'll swim out by himself (제가 저절로 헤여나옴니다). (Forcibly (無理이) leads Anna away)
(The remaining people also look down at the lake, then gather again under the linden tree (菩提樹下), all wearing stunned expressions (精神업는 얼굴). From the lakeside (湖水가), sounds of commotion continue. After a short while, the noise dies down. Suddenly, Old Man Uwal laughs loudly, as if prompted by someone. Following his laughter, Syubin also laughs, Anna and Soya laugh, and Elena laughs too. Finally, even Insarov, who had been standing like a demon (惡魔가티), laughs.)

Uwal: (Holding his stomach, trying hard to control his laughter (制禦하면서)) Hahahaha! Well, I heard a 'plop' and wondered what happened (엇젼일인가), so I looked down, and the way that straw-bundle-like fellow (? 집동가튼 놈이) fell in. Hahahahahahuhuhuhuhuhu.

Soya: When I think about it, it's so funny (엇더케 웃으운지), the way those big guys went into the water with their feet sticking up backwards (두발을 꺽구로)—

Uwal: Exactly! The way he fell in, like a frog jumping into water, as they say (개구리 물에 들 듯 하데나).

Soya: By the way, Mr. Insarov, how did you manage to throw such big men in? You're really strong (참 긔운 셰시지요). <150>

Uwal: That guy? He didn't even need to use much force (손에 부틸 나위도 업섯다). As I watched, as soon as one hand reached his waist (한짝손이 허리에 가자말자), he rolled down like a stone kicked by the tip of a foot (발끗테 채인 돌맹이 가티).

(Before long (於焉間), the sun sets, twilight (黃昏) deepens, and a feeling of moonlight (月光) seems to rise on the stage (舞臺). Martin and others (馬丁 等), carrying bundles (褓) wrapped with equipment (諸具), appear in the background (背景).)

Anna: The moon is up (달이 떳구려). Night has fallen while we were carried away (그럭저럭 밤이 되엇슴니다). Shall we gradually head off now (인제 次次 가볼가요)? We got too absorbed (잠착) in playing (넘어 놀기에).
(Moonlight shines on the lake surface (湖水面).)

Syubin: (Standing up, as if feeling regretful (마음에 섭섭한 것 가티)) Gentlemen (諸君). Though I am unworthy, allow me to offer the closing remarks (閉會말슴). (Everyone claps (一同拍手)) Gentlemen. If you love (慈愛), do not pour your heart's cup (마음 盞) until it is full and finished. If you hold a banquet (宴會), do not dislike (실혀하지) making it long enough to last until the dawn clouds scatter (새벽구름이 허터지도록). So said a poet (詩人) of old. Far from the dawn clouds scattering (허터지기는 커냥), night has only just begun to fall (인제 겨우 밤이 들락말락하엿슴니다). However, as our host (主人公), this auntie (아지머니끠서), seems quite sleepy (大段히 조으름이 오는 것 가트니), though it is regrettable (섭섭은 하나마), we shall hereby conclude (閉會) today's banquet (宴會). In closing (말슴을 맛치는데 이르러써), let us toast (祝賀) the health (健康) of our respected (敬慕) old man, who undertook the difficult task of escorting (? 後陪일) our auntie today (오늘 하로). (Takes a wine bottle (酒甁) and two cups from Martin (馬丁), pours one for the old man and one for himself.)

Anna: (Looking at the old man mumbling (입을 움을움을)) You should wash your beard. Come, let's go (자아 갑시다). I'm exhausted (고단해 죽겟다). (Takes the arm of the old man, whose fingertips (손끗) are moving strangely (이상히), and they exit)
(Soya also forcibly (無理이) takes Insarov's hand and leaves.)

Syubin: (Following Insarov, sees Pyelsep'ev) The Bulgarian (불갈이아) hero (英雄), the revolutionary patriot (革命의 志士), isn't he impressive? How was today's play (演劇)?

Pyelsep'ev: (In a gloomy tone (陰鬱한 語調)) Even for such a thing, what have we accomplished? (Stands thinking)

Syubin: Hmmph. Be careful (注意하게) not to lose Elena. That Bulgarian fellow (불가리아놈) is quite something (? 맹낭챤은 걸)—
(Syubin also exits. Elena follows, then looks back and approaches Pyelsep'ev)

**Summary**

This excerpt from Act 1 of the play "隔夜" (On the Eve) depicts a picnic outing near a lake involving a group of Russian (?) intellectuals and gentry: the sculptor Syubin, the philosopher Pyelsep'ev, the idealistic Elena, the boisterous maid Soya, the older Anna, the quiet Old Man Uwal, and the Bulgarian revolutionary Insarov. Discussions range from Kantian philosophy and art to Bulgarian independence. The mood shifts from intellectual debate and lighthearted banter to tension when drunken German laborers interrupt, behaving rudely towards the women. Insarov confronts them and throws their leader into the lake, an act that initially shocks but then amuses the party. As dusk falls and they prepare to leave, Elena expresses admiration for Insarov's revolutionary zeal and desire to join the Bulgarian cause, contrasting with Pyelsep'ev's more scholarly, detached perspective. The scene ends with Pyelsep'ev alone, contemplating, before Elena calls for him.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, this 1920 play is interesting. Written by Hyŏn Ch'ŏl and published in "Kaebyŏk," a major intellectual journal of the era, its adaptation of a Russian story about foreign oppression (Bulgaria under Ottoman rule, implicitly paralleled by German arrogance in the scene) and revolutionary fervor likely resonated with Korean audiences under Japanese rule. The characters' discussions about national identity, freedom, action versus contemplation (Insarov vs. Pyelsep'ev), and Elena's attraction to the revolutionary cause reflect contemporary Korean intellectual debates and aspirations for independence. Historians might find the specific choice of Turgenev's novel, the portrayal of different character archetypes (artist, philosopher, revolutionary, gentry), and the discussion of foreign ideas (Kant, European nationalism) revealing about the cultural and political climate of early colonial Korea.

슈빈. 대야머리 늙은이와 히스테리 마나님의 것는 거럼이 얼마나 시원할라고. (暫間동안 沈黙)
벨세넵. (沈默을 打破하랴는 沈重한 口調로) 에레나씨 요젼에 드린 原稿는 다 보셧슴니가?
에레나. 예에, 아즉 다 보지는.- 내게는 엇재 좀 어려워서...
슈빈. 무어야. 原稿라는 것이.
벨세넵. 돌아간 아버님의 遺稿.
슈빈. 어엉. 요전에 그것, 칸트 哲學의 神髓와 社會的 倫理的 무엇的 무엇的이라고 쓴 한발  이나 되는 冊이름 적힌 그 冊 말이지.- 그것을 에레나에게 읽으라고 그랫나?
벨세넵. 으응. 그래도 그것이 아벼지의 一生事業이고-나도 亦是 平生 일이 그것을 引繼하여 完成하랴고 하는 것이닛가. 이를터이면 나의 心血을 다할 큰 일이닛가 엇재던지 에레나에게 한번 보와 달나고 생각한 것이지.- 그러나 滋味도 업는 것을 넘어 未安함니다.
에레나. (未安한 語調로) 안이야요. 그런 것이 안이라. 부끄러운 말슴이올시다만은 哲學方面의 冊은 그처럼 얽은 적이 업섯서... 그러면- 春府丈게서는 平生을 칸트를 硏究하여 기셧던가요.
벨세넵. 그래섯슴니다. 一切人生의 思想은 모도 칸트에게 歸入한다고 하는 아버지의 생각으  로...
슈빈. 여보게, 여보게, 인제 그만하게. 이 炎天에 哲學의 野外講演만하고 잇슬 것인가? 불가리아의 革命이 엇더니 칸트哲學의 神髓가 엇더니 고만들 짓거리게. 오날은 놀라오쟌앗나.
에레나. 그러면 무슨 말을 해요.
슈빈. 조금더 여편네 맛이 잇는 이약이. 여러븐 보시오.＜143＞ 이와가티 太陽은 西天에서 燦爛이 비쳐잇고 하늘과 물은 한가지 푸르럿고. 한여름 고흔 꼿은 山이나 들이나 爛漫이 피엇슴니다. 이런 때에 우리 에레나를 擁衛하여 우리들은 한번 크게 天地의 美와 人間의 美를 論斷하랴고 생각하오.
에레나. 떠들고 십거던 혼자 떠들구려. 우리들은 그런 이야기보다도 더-
슈빈. 불가리야의 革命이니 칸트의 哲學이니 하는 것이 조탄 말이지. 그짓말 작작해라. 알지도 못하는 것이.
에레나. 그럼은요. 알지 못하고 말고. 알지 못하길래 이약이를 듯는 것이지요. 당신 가티 小說이 엇더니 演劇이 엇더니 流行하는 옷이 엇더니 그 따위 말은 어머니도 알고 소오야도 안담니다.
슈빈. 소오야, 그러면 내게는 소오야가 相當하단 말이지. 배ㅅ속에 똥만찬. 방정스러운 흐리  터분한 獨逸種子의 게집아히가 내게 合當하단 말이지.
에레나. 누가 그런 말을 햇나요. 당신 혼자 그러커던 그러케 定해 두시구려.
슈빈. 에레나. 정말 나를 그러케 생각하나. 아아.
(感情에 急迫하여 눈물이 돈다)
에레나. 그러먼요. 당신가티, 모처럼 남이 熱心으로 이약이하는데 틔를 뜻는 사람은 정말 실여요.
벨세넵. 그만 두시요. 에레나氏, 그것이 藝術家의 固執이라고 하는 것이 올시다. 고만 容恕하시요.
에레나. 안이야요. 아모리 藝術家라도 自己의 固執으로 남의 自由를 妨害하는 權利가 잇슴니가? 그리고 藝術家가 다 무엇임닛가? 父母의 胎밧게 나서 여태까지 滿足한 藝術作品이라고는 하나도 업슴니다.
슈빈. (激烈한 소리로) 에레나, 그대도 일즉이 나의 藝術을 因綠하여 나를 사랑한 적이 잇섯지. 나의 藝術도 이제는 헌신짝 버리듯이 侮辱을 當코 잇다. 다시 찻지 못할 버린 物件이 된 지가 오래다. 벨세넵 자네도 精神차리게. 자네의 哲學도 얼마 안이가서 에레나에게 餘地업시 밟히어 스러질 때가 잇슬 것이니.
에레나. 무엇이 엇더케요.-
슈빈. 아아 안이야. 칸트의 哲學보다도 불가리야 革命이 고맙게 생각 들때가 잇겟다는 말이  지.＜144＞ -(에레나가 무슨 말하랴는 것을 일부로 막는 것가티 큰 목소리로) 불상한 戀愛의 失敗者, 無用의 彫刻家 슈빈은 尊敬하는 칸트의 哲學이던지 불가리아의 革命을 爲하야 저-可憐한 森林의 自然의 소리와 合하야 祝賀하는 뜻으로 노래나 불르자. 「하느님이여 오소서 모도 한가지 우리들을 爲하여 내립소서, 마리아의 幸福을 永遠하도록... 아아, 틀렷다. 에레나의 幸福이 永久하도록-
(사람의 소리. 侍女 소오야. 後로 안나, 우왈老人이 나온다)
소오야. 그게 무슨 짓이야요. 그처럼 크게 소리를 질르고, 나는 뉘라고-
슈빈. 슈빈의 미치광이지, 혼자 질알이야.- 아아 나는 발서 露西亞란 나라와 露西亞의 사람이 실여젓다. 하로 바삐 外國으로 가고 십다. 伊太利에 가고십다.
소오야. 웨요.
슈빈. (일부러 고쳐서) 거기는 太陽이 비치고 美人이 잇고. (소오야를 보고) 으으 失手失手 눈압헤 美人이 잇는 것을 이졋다. (입을 마추랴고 한다)
소오야. (엽흐로 빠져나가면서) 이게 무슨 짓이야요! 貴婦人에게 對해서 이런 失禮가 어대잇나요.
슈빈. 흥, 貴婦人, 마담 훌륭하구려. 이이레에, 한드, 마담. (소오야의 손을 잡고 無理로 입을  마춘다)
소오야. (일부러 荒唐한 態度를 보이며) 웨이래요! (하면서 허리를 펴서 슈빈이 쓰고 잇는 麥藁帽를 떠어트린다. 슈빈이 주스랴고 할 저음에 발서 주서 가지고 다라난다. 이러한 光景으로 暫間동안 떠들석 한다.)
안나. (아까부터 우왈老人과 가티 菩堤樹 그늘 미테 안자 정신 업시 잇다가. 조흔 氣色으로)  하하 아즉도 어린 아이들이다. 웨들 저래. 젊을 때가 한창이다.
우왈老人. (答은 안이하고 입을 우물우물하며 이 사람은 손가락을 痙攣的으로 움즉이고)
(이때에 소오야는 突然히 悲痛한 소리를 질른다. 여럿이 깜작 놀라고 슈빈은 소오야의 적은 허리를 안고 노으라커니 붓잡거니 작난이 심하다.)
벨세넵. 소오야도 퍽 작난을 조아함니다 그려.
안나. 슈빈도 그것이 무어야, 어린 아이와 가티.＜145＞
(이때에 馬車軍과 馬夫들이 菩提樹미테 테이불보를 펴고 食物과 酒類를 느러 놋는다.)
안나. 자아 여러분. 작난들 그만하고- 다 안지시요.
(모도 菩提樹 미테 둘려 안는다)
안나. 자아 여러분 만이들 잡수시요. (우왈老人에게 盞을 주면서) 자아 아지버니 한잔 잡수시요. 오늘은 애만이 쓰셧슴니다.- 마츰 오늘은 日氣가 조와서 大段이 愉快함니다.(各各 接待하기에 奔走하다)
벨세넵. 정말 참 日氣가 좃슴니다. (안나의 술잔을 바드면서) 넘어 황송함니다. 오늘은 매우 얼골빗이 좆슴니다. 한 20은 젊어 보이는 걸이요.

벨세넵. 참 그럿슴니다. 인사롭은 훌륭한 사나히 올시다. 그 사람의 가는 길은 目標가 잇슴니다. 아모리 해도 심심치 안을 것이올시다.
에레나. 나는 불가리아란 나라에 가보고 십슴니다. 國民들이 自由엇기를 힘쓰고 復讎하기를 기다리는 그런 나라에 가서 그네들과 한가지로 革命의 旗를 두르고 活動을 해보고 십슴니다.
벨셉넵. (沈重한 語調로) 에레나씨 넘어 急히 구을지 마시요. 이 가티 심심하고 單調한 끗업는 平原과 희고도 고요한 밤든 나라에도 멀지 안해서 自由이니 革命이니하고 돌아 단일 사람들이 나을 것이올시다. 우리들도 넘어 오래동안 심심한 것을 참아 왓스닛가요.
