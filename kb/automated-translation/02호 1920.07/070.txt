기사형태	소식 (News/Report)

文明的 農事는 電氣로 한다.

米國의 農村에서는 近來 農事上 電力을 利用함이 盛行하는데 就中 칸사쓰州 等地에서는 多數하더라. 電氣會社가 競爭하야 該村의 耕地上에 電線을 設備하야 打穀 又는 作物의 運搬 害蟲救濟 等 一切를 此 全力을 利用케 하야 農民에게 多大한 便利를 與한다는대 此 電力을 利用하는 時는 蒸氣力을 使用함보다 至極 便利할 뿐 안이라 費用이 低廉함으로 農民에게 大歡迎을 受하는 바인대 그 地方의 統計에 依하건대 21日間 電力을 使用하야 8,814 「뿌ㅅ쇠ㄹ」의 小麥八을 打穀한 經費가 僅히 361弗인대 1「뿌ㅅ쇠ㄹ」에 約 41「셋트」에 該當하고 蒸氣力을 利用할 時는 1「뿌ㅅ쇠ㄹ」에 12乃至 20「세ㅅ트」에 達하는 故로 此에 比하면 費用이 約 4分之 1에 不過하다더라.＜36＞

Civilized Farming is Done with Electricity.

In the rural villages of America (米國), the use of electric power in farming has recently become prevalent, especially in places like Kansas (칸사쓰) State, where it is numerous. Electric companies compete to install power lines over the farmland of these villages, enabling the use of this electric power for everything from threshing (打穀) and crop transportation (作物의 運搬) to pest control (害蟲救濟), providing great convenience to the farmers. When using this electric power, it is not only extremely convenient compared to using steam power (蒸氣力), but also the cost (費用) is low, making it greatly welcomed by farmers. According to statistics from that region, the expense (經費) of threshing 8,814 bushels (「뿌ㅅ쇠ㄹ」) of wheat (小麥) using electric power for 21 days was merely $361, which corresponds to about 4.1 cents (「셋트」) per bushel. When using steam power, it reaches 12 to 20 cents (「세ㅅ트」) per bushel, so compared to this, the cost is merely about one-fourth (4分之 1). <36>

**Summary**

This news report from a 1920 Korean magazine describes the increasing use of electricity in American farming, particularly in Kansas. It highlights how electric companies installed power lines, enabling farmers to use electricity for various tasks like threshing, transporting crops, and pest control. The article emphasizes the advantages of electricity over steam power, noting its greater convenience and significantly lower cost, citing statistics showing electric threshing costs to be roughly a quarter of steam-powered threshing. For historians, this article reveals Korean awareness of and interest in Western technological advancements like rural electrification during the early colonial period. It reflects a potential aspiration for modernization and efficiency, contrasting with the likely conditions in colonial Korea, and shows the type of information about global progress being disseminated to the Korean public at the time.
