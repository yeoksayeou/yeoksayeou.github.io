Article Title: 푸름벗 (Young Friends)
Author: Unknown
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Young Friends

Listen to the dawn bell!
It announces the news of Kaebyŏk (Creation/New Era).
Vigorous is our spirit!
Ah, this morning!

Holding the new life of Kaebyŏk in our bosom,
Our youths, let us advance!

Look at the sun above that mountain!
Kaebyŏk reveals its body.
Fervent is our heart!
Ah, this morning!

Let's awaken the sleeping ones!
They dreamt the dream of Kaebyŏk.
Our skill to make new things!
Ah, this morning!

Look around at those mountains!
They have made a flower garden.
Our song like the oriole's!
Ah, this morning!

Look at that sea!
The monsoon rain has cleared.
Our many and few worries!
Ah, this morning!<40>

**Summary**

This poem, "Young Friends," is a call to Korean youth, invoking the imagery of morning and dawn ("Kaebyŏk") to symbolize a new beginning. It urges them to embrace this new era with energy, awaken others, use their talents to build a new society, and leave behind past sorrows, looking towards a hopeful future represented by nature's beauty. From a historical perspective, appearing shortly after the March First Movement of 1919 in the magazine "Kaebyŏk" (meaning Creation or Genesis), the poem is significant. It reflects the aspirations for national renewal and potentially independence, channeling the energy of the youth towards constructive action and societal transformation under the constraints of colonial rule, using metaphorical language like 'dawn' and 'awakening'.

푸름벗
새벽 쇠북 들어보소
개벽 소식 전하누나
활발하다 우리 긔상
아아 이 아츰이로다

개벽의 새목숨 품에 품고서
우리 청년들아 나아갑시다

저뫼 우에 해를 보소
개벽 몸을 드러내네
뜨겁도다 우리 마음
아아 이 아츰이로다

잠든 님을 깨워보자
개벽 꿈을 꾸엇구나
새것 만들 우리 솜씨
아아 이 아츰이로다

저 뫼들을 둘러보소
꼿동산을 만들엇네
꾀꼴 가튼 우리 노래
아아 이 아츰이로다

저 바다를 바라보소
장마비가 개이엇네
만코 적은 우리 시름
아아 이 아츰이로다＜40＞
