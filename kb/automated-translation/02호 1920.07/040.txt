Two or Three Recent Social Movements in Korea
						By Yi Tonhwa (李敦化)

What are the aims and purposes of the Chosŏn Education Association (朝鮮敎育會), the Chosŏn Student Convention (朝鮮學生大會), and the Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Association (朝鮮勞働共濟會)?

1. Chosŏn Education Association (朝鮮敎育會)
In the previous issue, we already welcomed the recent new phenomena arising in our Chosŏn (朝鮮) with wholehearted sympathy for their present and congratulated their future. Now, as we continue to write about these phenomena, we begin by wholeheartedly endorsing, without reservation, the birth of the Chosŏn Education Association in our Chosŏn. The reason is that the Association's aims and purpose represent the most urgent activity, the most appropriate guidance, and the most necessary enlightenment for Chosŏn. The Association declared at the outset of its aims:
The complete development of society can only be expected after awaiting the full development of each individual organizing that society. Currently, our social condition, regardless of whether it is the material or spiritual realm, has reached an extreme degree of languishing and confusion. High and low alike are dazzled by mammonism, and besides scheming for private gain, the relationship between the individual and society is entirely forgotten from the mind. The spirit of the entire peninsula is swept away solely by presentism (今日主義), and amid the complete collapse of our purely beautiful old culture <18>, the construction of a new culture to replace it still seems distant. Moreover, meeting the great waves of global thought trends that stir the entire globe with sky-surging force, our feeble world of thought has completely lost its course.

From this perspective, the Association's first point of focus is on striving for the parallel development of individuality and society. That is, social development lies in the enlightenment of individuality, and the progress of individuality, in turn, promotes the advancement of society. Thus, the Association laments the current chaotic state of our Chosŏn society and the descent of individuality into materialistic self-interest, and it has vigorously risen with the purpose of fundamentally correcting this. Originally—regardless of the society—to rescue its phenomena from a diseased state and strive for healthy development, it is first indispensable to require fundamental and foundational reform under some organization most urgently needed by that society. This is why the Association, with foresight, first cried out for the cultivation of human talent, the most urgent task for our Chosŏn society.

Recently, the cries for 'reform' and 'improvement' echoing from various quarters are indeed a delightful phenomenon. Perhaps stemming from this, the emergence of banks and companies, lubricating society's poor economic sphere, is certainly not unimportant. However, if we intend to attempt a fundamental reform of our society, it cannot possibly be achieved merely through the temporary reform of formal, external systems. First, without improving the cultural level of the general populace and cultivating human talent, it is no different from climbing a tree to seek fish (緣木求魚).

Indeed. In the current situation of our Chosŏn, there is nothing that is not necessary, nothing that is not appropriate. Agriculture is necessary, commerce is necessary, industry is necessary; religion, scholarship, literature, arts—not a single thing is unnecessary. However, regardless of which necessity or which appropriateness, if there is no place to build that necessity and create that appropriateness, and if there are no people capable of responding to that construction and creation, how is it different from trying to build a castle in the air by whipping the void? From this perspective, the Association's focus on the enlightenment of Chosŏn beginning with the cultivation of human talent is truly insightful foresight. However, the cultivation of talent is certainly not something based on accidental feelings or temporary excitement. It absolutely requires appropriate guidance based on tenacious activity and diligent research, thoroughly understanding the great trends of the world, appreciating the culture of the times, interpreting the level of the nation, and providing edification suitable for the era and opportunities fitting the level. This is why the Association has particularly focused on this point.

Now, it is a <19> particularly clear fact that the result of the great world turmoil has been to provoke a movement for new culture. Thus, the sole measure to rescue our society today from its sunken state lies entirely in encouraging and promoting education adapted to this era. Alas! The education problem in Chosŏn today is not material for idle talk by leisurely people; it is a crossroads concerning the future rise and fall of our nation, a matter of life and death. Among the scattered educational institutions currently dispersed within Chosŏn, how many are managed purely by the financial power and effort of our Chosŏn people? (Omitted hereafter—main text) That they aim to supplement in any way the cultural development of our peninsula, urging all fathers and elder brothers of the entire peninsula to awaken to the urgency of education, while simultaneously researching and resolving all sorts of problems related to education, is certainly not a matter of chance. (Omitted below—main text)

The principle of the evolution scholar, Mr. Darwin (따윈氏), known as 'survival of the fittest, death of the unfit' (適者生, 不適者死), can also be appropriately interpreted as meaning that humans live if they adapt to the times and die if they do not. When the Association advocates for the enlightenment of Chosŏn, it states, "[It] lies entirely in encouraging and promoting education adapted to this era," and thus, "It is a matter of life and death for the future of Chosŏn." Furthermore, when it points out sharply, "Few Chosŏn people manage and strive for such a momentous issue by their own hands (朝鮮人 自手)," those who are Chosŏn people must engage in deep self-reflection, inevitably resonate with the Association's purpose, and cannot help but be impressed by the Association's aims. Thus,

Finally, concluding decisively, "It aims to research and resolve all sorts of problems related to education," we thoroughly understand where the Association's purpose lies. That is, the Association, after appropriately interpreting world culture and the current and future course of Chosŏn, will research what symbol to use as a model for Chosŏn's educational ideal, and accordingly, what policy to use as a guide for its implementation. Furthermore, its practical policy aims to promote educational public discourse externally towards society, and internally, based on that public opinion, directly choose the good and bad with the educational authorities, compromise on truth and falsehood, and step-by-step, head towards the other shore (彼岸) to promote the advancement of education.

Generally, talking is easy, but acting is difficult—this is a human weakness. Having perceived the ideals and purpose of this Education Association as described above, we earnestly hope that the Association's practice and execution will strive to conform to those ideals.

2. Chosŏn Student Convention (朝鮮學生大會)
As stated above, the reason why we welcome the present and celebrate the future of the Chosŏn Education Association, citing its purpose and aims, <20> is it not implicitly for the sake of the present Chosŏn students and also for the future of Chosŏn youth? Think about it. For what purpose are all our present efforts and activities? To put it in one word, it is solely to firmly establish a grand plan for the next hundred years (百年大計) for the sake of the student youth's future. From this perspective, students must regard society as their second home, second hometown, and second set of parents and elders, and must strive in their studies to meet those expectations and demands. Thus, in their contemporary youthful cultivation, based on self-awareness and self-reflection, they must firmly establish their will.

Originally, our society still wanders in the early dawn, so it is a fact that youth have not heard appropriate instruction at home, nor have they received systematic influence from society. The only thing that could be called cultivation was just the few hours of daily instruction from teachers, known as school education. Is it not sufficiently proven by Mencius' (孟子) saying, "Expose it to the sun for one day, chill it for ten" (一日曝之十日寒之), that an individual's character cannot be perfected solely through a few hours of oral instruction from a teacher each day? Therefore, for those who are currently students in our Chosŏn, it is absolutely indispensable to require self-reliant effort (自力的 工夫)—that is, self-awareness (自覺) and self-cultivation (自修). However, self-awareness and self-cultivation are certainly not easy tasks; even adults with fully formed personalities often fail, let alone youth whose wills are yet undecided, to whom it could be practiced and left alone? Therefore, the self-awareness and self-cultivation of youth should not be left merely to individual will, but if practiced and fulfilled under some organization or guidance, a corresponding effect will surely appear. From this point, we are confident that the Chosŏn Student Convention is the most indispensable and excellent organization for requiring students' self-awareness and self-cultivation. The Convention stated in its aims: "Following the recent drastic changes in the currents of the times, those carrying their book satchels (笈) from various regions and flocking towards the capital gate (都門) are following closely on each other's heels (踵を接하게됨), which is truly a delightful phenomenon. However, while Kyŏngsŏng (京城) is the center of our Chosŏn culture, it is also the center of temptation for youth and the source of youth's corruption. Just as it is equipped with all kinds of civilized facilities, on the reverse side, the expansion of all kinds of institutions for temptation and corruption is an undeniable fact. Consequently, pure youth and students from the provinces gradually become infected by the city, fail to carry through their initial resolution for studying abroad (留學), and instead sink into the abyss (淵) of corruption, not only ruining their own precious lives but also extending immense loss to their families and society—this is what we often witness (往往目睹). Is this not a great source of regret for our academic world?" Thus, it keenly analyzed <21> the mid-course corruption of students. Furthermore, it asserted that the future path of life is like a perilous sea route (險難한 航路), and to successfully complete the planning of that route depends entirely on one's self-cultivation (自己修養). And that cultivation requires self-awareness. At this point, while expressing our wholehearted sympathy for the Convention's aims, it feels as if the future of Chosŏn is enveloped in a kind of sacred light. And the Convention expressed its purpose, saying: In the leisure time (餘暇) remaining after concentrating on their specialized studies, comrades will gather together (團會) to discuss interests (趣味), exchange knowledge (知識交換), seek mutual help in times of trouble (患亂相求), communicate feelings of fellowship (情誼相通), cultivate character (品性陶治), and combine utmost sincerity (至誠) to establish a sound public discipline (健實한 風紀) in our student world. This is truly a sign (兆朕) of the progress and advancement of Chosŏn students. However, we must unavoidably send a word to the student gentlemen (諸君): As you have already declared verbally, "On our sea route, there will be countless hidden reefs (暗礁); we will fight against raging waves (狂瀾) and angry billows (怒濤); there will be pain (苦痛); we will taste sorrow (悲哀); there will be times when we lament setbacks (跌蹉); there will be times when we weep in disappointment (失望)." Even when you actually experience these things and fight against them, do not depart from the ideals already anticipated in your hearts, even in moments of haste (造次), nor abandon them in times of hardship (顚沛). Only strive wholeheartedly with soundness, patience, effort, and perfection, so as not to fall into rashness (輕躁) and forgetfulness (忘失), to build the grand plan for the next hundred years for the sake of society.

3. Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Association (朝鮮勞働共濟會)
Labor is a great issue throughout the world. Therefore, when one speaks of the labor problem, everyone has come to know of it, and consequently, concerning its true meaning and true activities, almost anyone with common sense (常識) has come to be able to explain its general meaning (略義). Regarding the global labor problem, we have repeatedly discussed it (喋喋) in this journal. Accordingly, that the labor problem in Chosŏn naturally differs in degree from the labor problem in Europe and America (歐米) is a matter of course, as was also briefly mentioned in this journal (Refer to "The Ripple Effects of the World's Three Great Problems and the Awakening Required of the Chosŏn People"). Therefore, we will not add superfluous explanations (贅說) here. We truly believe that the labor problem in Chosŏn will find an appropriate solution through this Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Association.

Of course, there is naturally a distance (距離) between ideals and reality. Whether the Association will thoroughly realize (實現) the aims and purpose declared by the Association itself belongs to a separate question. However, from the perspective that the Association's aims and purpose are suited (適合) to the current labor situation in Chosŏn, we raise both hands (雙手) and sincerely congratulate the Association's future. The Association, at the very beginning (劈頭) of its aims, struck down the class-based scoundrels (階級的 不汗黨) with a ten-thousand-catty (萬斤) iron hammer (鐵槌), saying: <22> Those who sell their names (賣名) and abuse power (弄權), eating the strength of others (他力食) and wearing the labor of others (他勞衣), monopolizing (擅行) a lifetime of ease and comfort (安樂) with high towers and grand pavilions (高樓臣閣 - *possibly 高樓峻閣*), brocade clothes and jade food (錦衣玉食)—these historical relics (歷史的遺物) are the immorality (悖德) of the modern age. To beat the drum (鳴鼓) and purge (廓淸) them is the justice (正義) of the Supreme Being (上帝). To work sincerely and diligently (誠勤), eating by one's own strength (自力食) and clothing oneself by one's own labor (自勞衣), is the proper duty (正職) of human life. To treat this with respect (敬待) and support (扶持) it is the essence (本體) of the way of humanity (人道). Therefore, that labor is sacred (神聖) and the laborer is noble (尊貴)—is this not the holy living voice (活聲) of God? (Omitted—original text) Honor (名譽) to the laborer, gold (黃金) to the laborer, ease and comfort (安樂) to the laborer—so the Supreme Being eagerly awaits (苦待) to give (與).

Our past, appealing to reason (理性), has been lived amidst severe contradiction (矛盾). On one side, there were those who died from satiety (飽), while at the same time, on the other side, there were those who died from hunger (飢). And the circumstances (境遇) of the satiated involved no activity, no effort, no assistance (補助). No, not only that, they were elevated from being metaphorical scoundrels (不汗黨) to actual 'scoundrels'. Think about it. In Chosŏn, what did the so-called scholar class (士) among the 'scholars, farmers, artisans, and merchants' (士農工商) generally do? Their occupation of honor was, through and through (徹頭徹尾), idle eating and clothing (遊衣遊食). After learning Han laws and Tang poetry (漢律唐詩), the Four Books and Three Classics (四書三經), and about regional factions (地閥) like Southerners and Northerners (南人北人), Noron and Soron (老論少論), their subsequent activity, their sole legitimate and grand activity, was serving as an official (仕宦). Of course, what could be wrong with the passion for official service, and what could be improper about the duties (職務) of official service? However, their purpose in seeking office, more than the service itself, lay inside (裡面): using the two machines (機械) called fame (名聲) and power (權力), they strove (力圖) to plan their livelihood (生活計) for old age and the future (老後將來). Their ideal was to defraud (騙取) the hard-earned grains (粒粒皆辛苦) of the pitiful farmers while in office, thereby establishing a hundred-year plan for their descendants. Look! Among the Western-style houses (洋屋) rising in the mists of Namsan and Pukch'on (南村北村), how many of the so-called Chosŏn yangban (兩班) wealthy (富豪) acquired (圖得) their wealth through their own legitimate efforts? In the manner of 'big fish eat middle fish, middle fish eat small fish, small fish eat even smaller fish' (大魚食中魚 中魚食小魚 小魚食乂小魚 - *乂 likely typo for 小*), in the world of the late Yi dynasty (李末), the so-called scholar class (士者), from those in important government posts (要路) above to the lowest ranks (末流) of drifting yangban who couldn't even pass the initial state exam (初試) below—their lives were entirely sustained by receiving predatory provisions (强盜的 提供) under oppressive conditions (壓迫的 條件下) from the diligent farmers, artisans, and merchants. As a result of such evil customs (惡風) prevailing, even legitimate workers lost the original strength (原力) to pursue their livelihoods, and consequently, the general populace came to regard idle eating and clothing as the supreme comfort (無上安樂) and the supreme means (無上手段). The result—labor was absolutely abhorred (壓忌), and laborers were seen as a kind of lowly service (賤役), something done by 'unfortunate' (못생긴) people, material for exploitation (魚肉). Ultimately, society became corrupt (腐敗), weakened (衰弱), and [character missing/illegible - perhaps 衰頹 decayed?], and unconsciously (不知不識間) <23> arrived at today's phenomenon. Now, to restore the weakened vitality (原氣) in this Chosŏn and return it to a somewhat healthy state, the supreme strategy (無上之上策) is truly, before anything else, to strike down (打滅) the gangs (徒輩) of idle eaters and clothed, and to support (扶助) the yangban who exert mind and effort (努心努力). From this perspective, the emergence of the Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Association is truly like a heaven-sent gospel (天賜福音) descending upon the peninsula. Furthermore, the Association's purpose, as its name suggests, is to provide mutual aid (共濟) to laborers, which is even more fitting (適合) for the current situation in Chosŏn. The Association states:
The interests (利害) of laborers are mutual, and their status (地位) is also mutual. Unable to educate their children (子女), unable to secure their occupations (職業), unable to find relief (救濟) from illness (疾病) and calamity (災難), they have only endured menial service (使喚) and contemptuous treatment (賤待), raising silkworms (養蠶) for others, and doing stonemasonry (石工) for others. If they stroke their chests (膺を撫하고) and reflect deeply (三思), they cannot help but reproach themselves (自責) rather than blaming others (他を惡함).

Look! How oppressive, how sorrowful. Can we truly refrain from pouring out ten thousand measures (萬斛) of tears (淚) and sympathizing (同情) with this? To liberate (解放) from that oppression and sorrow, the Association declared its purposes:
1. Knowledge Development (知識啓發)       2. Character Improvement (品性向上)
3. Relief from Hardship and Calamity (患難救濟)       4. Job Introduction (職業紹介)
5. Encouragement of Savings (貯蓄奬勵)       6. Encouragement of Hygiene (衛生奬勵)
7. Other Investigation and Research on General Labor Conditions (其他 一般 勞動狀況의 調査硏究)

From this, we have thoroughly understood the Association's purpose, and thus we know that its purpose is suited to the current labor situation in our Chosŏn. Thus, we deeply believe (깁히 밋노라) that while the Association strives and works with utmost sincerity (誠心誠意), it will factually reform (事實的으로 改造) the phenomenon of Chosŏn, annihilate (剿盡) in one fell swoop (一擧) the gangs of scoundrels living idly (遊衣遊食의 不汗黨의 輩), and the praise (贊美) of sacred labor will be heard in all fields (四野). (Incomplete 未完) <24>

Prepare early to die, then both life and death will be pleasant.
Ascend! Ascend quickly, O your soul! Your seat is in Heaven. Even though the body sinks into death in the earth.
Young people should acquire things entirely by their own strength, rather than receiving favors or borrowing from others.
Stealing one's own life when there is absolutely no way to help oneself, no matter how considered, is an unforgivable crime of theft (窃盗罪).
Cast away (族棄) that base and sordid (너절하고 칙칙한) ambition! For that sin, even an angel would become a dog fly (개파리). <24>

**Summary**

This editorial by Yi Tonhwa discusses three emerging social organizations in 1920 Korea: the Chosŏn Education Association, the Chosŏn Student Convention, and the Chosŏn Labor Mutual Aid Association. Yi strongly endorses all three, seeing them as crucial for Korea's revitalization. He praises the Education Association's focus on cultivating talent as the fundamental basis for societal reform, lamenting the current moral and cultural decay. He views the Student Convention as vital for guiding youth towards self-cultivation and preventing moral corruption in the cities, seeing students as the nation's future. Finally, he passionately supports the Labor Mutual Aid Association for championing the dignity of labor, aiming to uplift workers from exploitation and poverty, and condemning the traditional parasitic elite (yangban) who lived off others' toil. The article reflects a strong nationalist sentiment focused on self-strengthening through education, youth mobilization, and recognizing the value of labor as paths to overcome Korea's perceived stagnation and weakness. For historians, the article offers insight into early Korean nationalist thought under colonial rule, highlighting the emphasis on internal reform, the critique of traditional social structures (especially the yangban class), the adoption of modern concepts like social movements and labor rights, and the urgent desire for cultural and educational development as a means of national survival and progress. The strong anti-elite (specifically anti-yangban) rhetoric and the embrace of labor's 'sacredness' are particularly notable.

最近 朝鮮社會運動의 二三
						李敦化
朝鮮敎育會, 朝鮮學生大會, 朝鮮勞働共濟會의 趣旨及 目的은 如何한가.
1. 朝鮮敎育會 吾人은 前號에서 이미 우리 朝鮮에 起하는 最近 新懸象에 對하야 滿腔의 同*으로 그들의 現在를 歡迎하고 그들의 將來를 祝賀하엿거니와 이제 그 現象을 繼續的으로 쓰고저 함에 當하야 吾人은 劈頭로 우리 朝鮮에 朝鮮敎育會가 出生함을 衷心으로써 贊成하되 마지 안이 하노니 其故는 同會의 趣旨와 目的이 朝鮮에 가장 急務인 活動임으로써요, 가장 適當한 指導임으로써요, 가장 必要한 啓發임으로써라. 同會는 그 趣旨 劈頭에 宣言하얏스되 가튼
社會의 完全한 發達은 其社會를 組織한 各個人의 圓滿한 發達은 待한 然後에야 可히 期望하나니 現下 우리의 社會狀態는 物質界 精神界됨을 勿論하고 萎靡混同함이 極度에 達하야 上下가 一樣으로 金錢萬能主義에 眩惑하야 私利를 是圖하는 外에 個人과 社會의 關界는 全然히 염두에 忘却하고 全半島의 精神은 다만 今日主義로 風靡함으로 純美한 舊文化는 一墮無餘한 中에 此에＜18＞ 代할만한 新文化의 建設은 아즉도 前途가 杳然할 뿐만 안이라 더욱이 世界的 思潮의 大 波瀾이 滔天의 勢로 全球를 掀動함에 際會하야 薄弱한 吾人의 査想界는 完然히 針路를 失하엿도다.
이로써 보면 同會着眼의 第一點은 個性과 社會의 並行 發達을 圖함에 잇다. 卽 社會의 發達은 個性의 啓發에 잇고 個性의 進步는 更히 社會의 向上을 促하는 點에서 同會는 吾朝鮮社會의 現在 混沌狀態에 入함과 個性의 唯物的 私慾에 陷함을 慨嘆하야 此를 根本으로부터 匡正할 目的으로 蹶然히 奮起한 所以라 하겟다. 元來 - 엇더한 社會를 勿論하고 그 社會의 現像을 病的 狀態로부터 拯濟하야 健全한 發達을 圖코저 함에는 먼저 그 社會에 最先急務로 要할만한 或 機關下에서 根本的 且 基礎的 改造를 要치 안이함이 不可하나니 是 - 同會가 先見의 明으로써 吾 朝鮮社會의 最先急務인 人材養成을 劈頭로 絶叫한 所以이엇다. 가튼
挽近吾人의 改造라 改善이라 하는 呌聲이 各方面에 喧聒함은 可喜할 現狀이라. 此에 基因함인지 銀行이나 社會가 簇出하야 社會의 貧弱한 經濟界를 潤滑케 함도 勿論 急치 안임은 안이나 그러나 우리 社會의 根柢的 改造를 企圖코저 할진대 如干 形式的 外部的 制度의 一時的 改造로 到底히 能할 바 안이오, 먼저 一般 國民의 文化程度를 向上케 하며 人材를 養成함이 無하면 此는 木에 緣하야 魚를 구함과 異할 것이 업도다.
果然하다. 吾 朝鮮의 今日 現象에 잇서는 무엇이나 必要치 안임이 업스며 무엇이나 適切치 안임이 업스리라. 農도 必要하고 商도 必要하고 工도 必要하고, 宗敎, 學術, 文藝, 어느것이던지 必要치 안인 바 一도 無하다. 그러나 何者의 必要 何者의 適切을 勿論하고 그 必要를 建設하고 그 適切을 創造할만한 곳 그 建設과 創造에 需應할만한 人物이 無하면 그는 空中을 策하야 樓閣을 建設코저 함과 무엇이 다르랴. 이 點에서 同會가 朝鮮의 啓發을 먼저 人材養成으로 主觀한 所以는 實로 洞察한 先見이라 할진저. 然이나 人材의 養成은 決코 偶然한 感情과 一時的 興奮에 잇는 者―안이오. 반듯이 根氣잇는 活動과 努力잇는 硏究下에서 世界의 大運을 通察하며 時代의 文化를 鑑賞하며 民族의 程度를 解釋하야 適時代의 敎化와 合程度의 契機로써 適當한 引導가 잇지 안이하면 不可하니 是同會가 此點에 특히 着眼한 所以언저. 가튼
이제 世界的 大戰亂의 結果―新文化의 運動을 惹起케 됨은 ＜19＞ 特히 著明한 事實인즉 今日 우리의 社會를 沈溣中에서 挽回할 唯一의 方策은 다만 이 時代에 適應한 敎育을 獎勵振興함에 專在하도다. 噫라 今日 朝鮮의 敎育問題는 閒人의 閒談 材料가 안이라 우리 民族의 將來 消長에 관한 岐點이며 死活에 關한 問題라. 現下 朝鮮內에 散在한 零星한 敎育機關中에서 純然한 우리 朝鮮人의 財力과 努力으로 經營하는 者 - 幾個나 有하뇨. (이하 中略―본문) 우리 半島文化 發展의 萬一을 補導키 爲하야 全半島의 父兄으로 하야곰 敎育의 急務임을 促醒하는 同時에 敎育에 關한 온갓 問題를 硏究解決코저 함이 決코 偶然의 事가 안이라. (下略―본문)
進化學者 따윈氏의 이른바 適者生, 不適者死의 原理는 卽 人은 時代에 適하면 生하고 不適하면 死할 뿐이라 함으로 解釋하야도 또한 適切하니라. 同會가 朝鮮啓發을 主張함에 曰 「다만 이 時代의 適應한 敎育을 獎勵振興함에 專在하다.」하고 그리하야 其는 「將來 朝鮮의 死活問題라.」하엿고 進하야 「朝鮮人은 이러한 重大問題를 朝鮮人 自手로 經營하며 努力하는 者 - 幾希하다.」喝破함에 至하야는 朝鮮人된 者 - 一番猛省하야 同會의 目的에 共鳴치 안이함이 不可하며 同會의 趣旨에 感服치 안이치 못하리라. 그리하야
終에 決言하되 「敎育에 關한 온갓 問題를 硏究解決코저 함이라 斷定함에서 吾人은 처음으로 同會의 目的이 那邊에 在한 것을 徹底히 理會하엿다. 卽 同會는 世界文化와 朝鮮의 現下及將來의 過程如何를 的當히 解釋한 下에서 朝鮮의 敎育理想을 엇더한 象徵으로 標本을 삼으며 따라서 그의 施措는 엇더한 方針으로 指南을 삼을가 硏究할 것이며 그리하야 그의 實行 方針은 外로 社會에 對한 敎育적 餘論을 振興케 하고 內로 그 輿論에 依하야 直接 敎育當局으로 더불어 그 善否를 選擇하며 그 眞僞를 妥協한 上―步一步―彼岸에 向하야 敎育의 向上을 促進코저 함에 잇다하겟다.
대개 言키는 易하되 行키는 難함은 人世의 弱點이라. 吾人은 此敎育會의 理想과 目的을 以上과 如히 主觀한 上―同會의 實行實踐이 그 理想에 符合하도록 勵行하기를 切望하노라.

2. 朝鮮學生大會 以上에 述한 바와 如히 吾人이 朝鮮敎育會의 目的과 趣旨를 들어 그의 現在를 歡迎하며 또 그 將來를 祝하는 所以는＜20＞ 慇懃이 그 裏面에 朝鮮學生의 現在를 爲함이며 또 그 朝鮮靑年의 將來를 爲함이 안일 것이냐. 思하라. 現在 吾人의 모든 努力과 모든 活動은 다가티 무엇을 爲함일 것이냐. 一言으로 此를 蔽하면 오로지 學生靑年의 將來를 爲하야 百年의 大計를 튼튼히 세워주고저 함에 잇다하리라. 이 點에서 學生된 者 - 社會를 第2의 家庭으로 알며 第2의 鄕里로 알며 第2의 父兄으로 認定하야 그 期待와 그 要望에 應할만한 學業에 努力치 안이함이 不可하며 그리하야 그들 現代 靑年的 修養上 - 自覺自省의 上에 그 意志를 튼튼히 세워야 할 것이다.
元來 우리의 社會는 아즉도 黎明의 初期에 彷徨하는 故로 靑年된 者―家庭에서 適當한 敎訓을 들어보지 못하얏고 또 그 社會上으로도 系統잇는 感化를 밧지 못할 것은 事實이다. 오즉 修養이라 할 것은 다만 學校敎育이라 하는 一日 幾 時間의 師長의 訓育 그것 뿐 이엇다. 一日 幾 時間의 師長의 口頭訓鍊 뿐으로 一個人의 人格이 完全되지 못할 것은 孟子의 이른바「一日曝之十日寒之」라한 格言에 依하야 넉넉히 證明할 것이 안이냐. 그럼으로 現下 우리 朝鮮의 學生되는 자는 自己 스스로 自覺하고 自己 스스로 自修하는 卽 自力的 工夫를 要치 안이함이 到底不可하도다. 그러나 自覺自修라 함은 決코 容易한 事業이 안이니 그는 完全한 人格을 具備한 大人으로도 오히려 能치 못하거던 하물며 意志未定한 靑年에게 그를 實行과 放任함이리오. 그럼으로 靑年의 自覺自修라 함을 다못 一個人의 意志에 放任치 勿하고 엇더한 機關 엇더한 指導下에서 그를 實踐履行하면 반듯이 相當한 效果가 나타날 것이니 吾人은 이點에서 朝鮮學生大會는 學生의 自覺自修를 要하는 점―가장 업지 못할 好個機關으로 自信하엿노라. 同會는 그 趣旨에 일넛스되 「近來 時代思潮의 激變을 從하야 各地方에서 笈을 負하고 都門을 向하야 雲集하는 者 - 踵을 接하게됨은 참으로 可喜할 現象이다. 그러나 京城은 우리 朝鮮文化의 中心地인 同時에 靑年誘惑의 中心地요 靑年墮落의 發源地라. 온갓 文明의 施設에 備하야짐과 가티 그 反面에는 온갓 誘惑墮落의 機關이 擴張되어감은 掩할 수 업는 事實이라. 그리하야 地方의 純潔한 靑年學生이 漸次 都會의 感染됨을 따라 留學의 初志를 貫徹치 못하고 도리혀 墮落의 淵에서 沈淪하야 貴中한 自己의 一生을 誤할 뿐 안이라 延하야 一家와 社會에 莫大한 損失을 끼치게 됨은 우리의 往往目睹하는 바―니 이것이 우리 學界의 一大恨事안이리요.」하야 學生의 中途墮落을＜21＞ 切切히 解剖하엿고 그리하야 人生의 前途를 險難한 航路와 如하다 斷定하고 그 航路의 豫定을 完全히 成功코저 하면 專히 自己修養 如何에 在하다 하엿스며 그리하야 그 修養은 自己의 自覺을 할 것이라 한 點에서 吾人은 同會의 趣旨에 滿腔의 同情을 表함과 同時에 朝鮮의 前途가 一種 神聖한 光明의 中에 무쳐잇는 듯하다. 그리고 同會는 그 目的을 表明하되 曰 各自 專門의 學業을 專心攻究하는 餘暇에 同志가 서로 團會하야 趣味를 談論하며 知識을 交換하며 患亂을 相求하며 情誼를 相通하며 品性을 陶治하며 至誠을 合하야 우리 學生界의 健實한 風紀를 確立하리라 宣言하엿나니 이 實로 朝鮮學生의 向上進步하는 兆朕이라 할지로다. 然이나 吾人으로 不得已 一言을 送하야 學生諸君에게 寄할 所以는 諸君이 이미 口頭로 宣言함과 가티 「우리의 航路에는 무수한 暗礁도 잇슬것이오. 狂瀾과 怒濤와도 싸을 것이오. 苦痛도 잇슬 것이오. 悲哀도 맛볼 것이오. 跌蹉에 嘆할때도 잇슬 것이오. 失望에 泣할때도 잇스리라. 」하엿스니 諸君이 事實로 그를 맛보고 그와 싸울 때에도 諸君의 胸中에 이미 豫期한 理想과 가티 造次의 間에도 離치 말며 顚沛의 下에도 *치 勿하야 오즉 健全과 忍耐와 努力과 完全으로써 輕躁忘失에 빠지지 안토록 社會를 爲하야 百年의 大計를 建設하도록 盡心할지어다.

3. 朝鮮勞働共濟會 勞動은 世界를 通한 大問題라. 故로 勞動問題라 하면 누구나 그를 알게 되엇고 따라셔 그의 眞意義와 眞活動에 至하야도 常識이 잇는 者 - 거의 다 그의 略義를 說明하게 되엇다. 世界的 勞動問題라 하면 吾人이 本誌上에 再三喋喋하엿고 그리하야 朝鮮의 勞動問題는 程度上 스스로 彼歐米의 勞動問題와 差異가 生할 것은 勿論의 事임은 또한 本誌上 (世界3大問題의 波及과 朝鮮人의 覺悟여하 參照)에 略言하얏슴으로 玆에 贅說치 안이하거니와 吾人은 참으로 朝鮮의 勞動問題는 此朝鮮勞動共濟會에 依하야 適當한 解決을 어들줄르 確信하노라.
