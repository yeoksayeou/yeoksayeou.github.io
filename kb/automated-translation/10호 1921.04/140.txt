**Summary**

This article from the April 1, 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine lists the names of graduates from various specialized schools (colleges) in Korea that spring. It covers institutions like Kyŏngsŏng Chŏnsu School, Kyŏngsŏng Medical Specialized School, Sungsil University, Posŏng College (Law and Commerce departments), Kyŏngsŏng Engineering Specialized School, Suwŏn Agriculture and Forestry School, Yŏnhŭi Specialized School, and Ewha Haktang. The names are presented without any specific order within each school's list. For historians, this list provides valuable data on the early cohorts of professionally trained Koreans (and some Japanese students like those at Suwŏn) during the colonial period. It documents the individuals graduating from key educational institutions, offering insights into the composition of the emerging Korean intellectual and professional class, including the presence of women graduates from Ewha and the mix of Korean and Japanese names in certain schools, reflecting the structure of colonial education.
