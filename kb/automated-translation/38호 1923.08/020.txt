Let Us Not Despair

Comrade, let us not despair today.

Even if the prospect of us Korean people achieving our national movement politically, or the prospect of achieving our social movement economically, does not appear right before our eyes on the ground (現場), let us not despair. Let us absolutely not despair.

Even if the Creationists (刱造派) and Reconstructionists (改造派) glare at each other abroad, and even if the national movement and social movement follow different paths domestically, let us not despair. Let us absolutely not despair.

When we see the lives of Korean people becoming increasingly difficult day by day (in cities and rural villages - 都市 農村) due to the relentless push of the money-making activities of Japanese people backed by political power (政治的 勢力), how can our hearts remain calm? And when we hear of or witness even more concrete tragic situations (具體的 慘狀) than this, how could our emotions possibly remain natural? However, let us not despair. Let us absolutely not despair.

People might say that human affairs, nay, the affairs of the world, naturally proceed in the order of 1, 2, 3, 4. But what can be done? Were there not ways in which things sometimes leaped forward by hundreds, thousands, tens of thousands, hundreds of millions?

The ancients (넷 사람) already said that in the heavens, there are unpredictable storms (不測의 風雨), and for people, fortune and misfortune (禍福) alternate between morning and evening. How much more so in these recent times, when dark clouds (黑雲) gather hazily from all four directions in the heavens, and when humanity (人間) wavers amidst the drizzle of anxiety (不安) in these recent times? There is nothing to despair about. Even if one despairs, there is no use for it; it's not that there's nothing because it's useless, but rather that there is no need (必要) to despair.

How could our saying this be dragging out the old-style fatalism (運命說) to seek temporary self-comfort (自慰)? We say this because this is the coming order (順序), and we see it this way only because it is the truth that ought to be (當有한 眞理).

There is one single task for us, one that will shine for hundreds of millions of generations (億千萬代) and cannot be changed or abandoned. What is it? It is to doubt all the phenomena (一切의 現像) we possess today and to think until our heads break, and to act until our bodies crumble, in order to establish a new world. That is all. In such thinking (思索), in such acting (擔行), there is nothing else whatsoever. There is only the solemn fact (嚴肅한 事實) of utmost devotion and unified effort (鞠躬盡瘁 同歸一體). Comrade, what is there now to despair about? <1>

**Summary**

This editorial exhorts Koreans not to succumb to pessimism despite the lack of visible progress in achieving national independence or social/economic justice under Japanese rule. The author acknowledges internal divisions within the movement (Creationists vs. Reconstructionists abroad, Nationalists vs. Socialists domestically) and the dire economic conditions caused by Japanese exploitation. However, despair is rejected; progress is portrayed as potentially sudden and unpredictable, not merely linear. The core message emphasizes the essential duty of Koreans: to critically question their current reality and dedicate themselves wholeheartedly, through intense thought (思索) and decisive action (擔行), to creating a new world, united in purpose (鞠躬盡瘁 同歸一體).
From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into the mood of Korean activists and intellectuals in the early 1920s, facing disillusionment after the March First Movement while confronting increased colonial oppression and internal ideological splits. Historians might find the explicit mention of the Creationist/Reconstructionist debate and the National/Social movement division significant, alongside the strong emphasis on intellectual critique and unwavering, self-sacrificial action as the path forward, reflecting the determined spirit found in publications like *Kaebyŏk*.

悲觀하지는 말자
동무여 오늘날 悲觀하지는 말자.
우리 朝鮮 사람이 政治的으로 民族運動을 成就할 可望이, 現場에 또는 經濟的으로 社會運動을 成就할 可望이 常場 눈 압헤 뵈지 안는다 할지라도 悲觀하지는 말자. 決코 悲觀하지는 말자.
國外에서 刱造派와 改造派가 눈을 흘기고 國內에서 民族運動과 社會運動이 서로 길을 달니한다 할지라도 悲觀하지는 말자. 決코 悲觀하지는 말자.
政治的 勢力을 背景으로 한 日本 사람의 돈모리 하는 運動이 막 드리밀님으로 因해서 朝鮮 사람의 生活이 날로 글너가는 것(都市 農村)을 볼 때에 우리의 마음이 엇지 平然할 수 잇스며 이 보다도 더 한 가지의 具體的 慘狀을 耳聞코 또 目擊할 때에 우리의 감히가 엇지 天然할 수야 잇스랴. 그러나 우리는 悲觀하지는 말자. 決코 悲觀하지는 말자.
사람의 일 아니 世上의 일은 勿論 1234의 순으로 써 나아가는 것이라고들 하겟지 그러나 엇지하리요 때로 百, 千, 萬, 億의 境遇로 엉큼엉큼 뛰여 나아가는 方文이 업지 안하엿는 것을.
한울에는 不測의 風雨가 잇고 사람에게는 朝夕의 禍福이 잇는 法이라고 넷 사람은 벌서부터 말해 두엇다. 面況 近來의 한울에는 黑雲이 4處에서 어물어물하고 近來의 人間에는 不安의 순우에 오락가락 하는 이때임이리요. 悲觀할 것은 업다. 悲觀한 대도 쓸 대가 업스니까 업는 것이 아니라 悲觀할 必要가 업스니까 업다는 말이다.
우리가 이러케 말함이 엇지 녯투의 運命說을 끄리 一時의 自慰를 엇쟈 함이리오. 이것이 當來할 順序이니까 이러케 말함이며 當有한 眞理이니까 이러케 보는 것 뿐이다.
우리에게는 億千萬代에 삐치여 변치 못하고 할, 單 한 가지의 일이 잇다. 무엇이냐. 우리가 가진 오늘 一切의 現像에 懷疑하고 새로운 世上을 내세우기 爲하야 머리가 깨여지도록 思索하는 그것이며 몸덩이가 부스러지도록 擔行하는 그것이다. 그것 뿐이다. 그러케 思索하는 거긔에는, 그러케 擔行하는 거긔에는 다른 아모러한 것도 업는 것이다. 오즉 鞠躬盡瘁 同歸一體의 嚴肅한 事實이 잇슬 뿐이다. 동무여 이제 또 무엇을 悲觀하겟느냐.＜1＞
