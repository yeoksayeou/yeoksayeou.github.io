Wriggling Aspects of the World
						One Reporter
From May 24th to July 20th

May 24th: The Jita Revolutionary Committee (智多革命委員會) promulgated an eleven-article Order for Religious Control.
△ The Pan-Pacific Korean Association resolved to expand the scope of its conference by encompassing all peoples of the Pacific Rim nations.

May 25th: The Shanghai National Representatives' Conference resolved four major points concerning the livelihood issues of the entire Korean people.
△ Prominent newspaper correspondents and influential Diet members in Japan initiated and held a rally in Tokyo to maintain the break from the political status quo.
△ Britain's Bonar Law cabinet was fully formed, and all cabinet members had an audience with the Emperor on the same day.
△ A report arrived stating that the Chinese government resolved to subjugate bandits (土匪) at a provisional cabinet meeting on the 23rd.

May 26th: The trial of Kim Han, Yun Ikchung, etc., part of Kim Sangok's faction, opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court. Kim Han received a sentence of 7 years imprisonment, Yun Ikchung 3 years, Sŏ Pyŏngdu 2 years, An Honghan 1 year, Chŏng Sŏlgyo 1 year 6 months, and Sin Toksu was acquitted.
△ In Chinju, representatives of various farmers resolved to form a Beef Boycott League (牛肉非買同盟) to oppose the Hyŏngp'yŏng Movement.
△ Due to the reparations issue, the situation between Greece and Turkey worsened again, and the Greek Foreign Minister sent an ultimatum to the Turkish Committee.

May 27th: A Women's Study Tour Group, hosted by the Riwŏn Youth Association, arrived in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul).
△ The Veterans' Association of Kanagawa Prefecture, Japan, discussed and decided on three major proposals to demand suffrage rights.
△ Due to the non-payment of salaries, the Chinese courts and judicial officials commenced a general strike, bringing the judicial system to a state of annihilation.
△ An agreement was reached between Greece and Turkey, with the conditions being that Greece cedes Karagach and Turkey renounces its claim for reparations.

May 28th: A major dispute arose between the Buddhist General Affairs Institute (불교총무원) and the Religious Affairs Institute (교무원) over the issue of meat consumption and alcohol drinking, leading to the unfortunate incident of physical fighting.
△ Fengtian representative Sun Lieh-ch'en and Zhili clique representative Wang Ch'eng-pin met at Shanhaiguan, discussed important issues, and mutually promised the withdrawal of both armies.

May 29th: A meeting of Customs Chiefs was held in the Government-General's second conference room.
△ A major clash occurred between the Communist Party and police forces in Dresden, Germany, resulting in eleven deaths and sixty injuries.

May 30th: A report arrived stating that Han Kyŏnggwŏn, negotiating representative of the Koryŏ Communist Party, arrived in Vladivostok (海港) to submit a consultation proposal to the Moscow government.
△ A major earthquake occurred in Eastern Persia, causing the collapse of 6 villages and reaching 40 deaths.
△ A large general strike occurred in the Ruhr, with the number reaching five hundred thousand.
△ A new cabinet was formed in Poland. The Prime Minister is the leader of the Peasant Party, Vincent Witos, and the Foreign Minister is Mr. Marian Seyda.

May 31st: A telegram reached somewhere in Fukuoka stating that a faction of the Ŭiyŏldan (Righteous Brotherhood) had infiltrated Japan to assassinate high-ranking Japanese officials, causing Japanese police circles to exercise extreme caution.

June 1st: An Independence Group (獨立團) based along the Amnok River began large-scale activities, raiding the area near Ch'angsŏng, <119> troubling the police forces, and collecting a large amount of military funds.
△ A revolutionary movement arose in France, and three leaders of the Socialist Party were executed by firing squad.
△ A report came that the Chinese government decided at the cabinet meeting on the 31st to recognize the two republics of Estonia and Latvia.
△ A conference of principals of middle schools from all over Japan, hosted by the Ministry of Education, opened today.

June 2nd: A major reshuffle occurred among the leaders of the Japanese Ministry of the Navy.
△ The Taiwan Governor-General's Office decided to change the name of Taiwanese indigenous peoples (臺灣生蕃) to Takasago-zoku (高砂族).
△ In Changsha, Chinese merchants and sailors clashed with the Japanese Navy landing party after throwing stones at the Japanese steamship Muling Maru (武陵丸). Two Japanese soldiers suffered minor injuries, and among the Chinese, three people of rank equivalent to Captain died, and six others were injured.

June 3rd: The southern and northern armies of China began a major battle, with Mr. Sun Wen personally leading the southern troops.
△ A large clash occurred between a religious group and police forces in the Philippines, resulting in over fifty Moro casualties.
△ Anti-Japanese Chinese people in Hunan, greatly angered by the Changsha incident, started an anti-Japanese demonstration march carrying the bodies of those killed by Japanese soldiers; participants numbered an astonishing hundred thousand.
△ New York State Governor Smith approved and signed the bill repealing the enforcement of Prohibition.

June 4th: Regarding the Changsha incident, the Military Governor of Hunan submitted a protest with five conditions to the Japanese consul.
△ The Japanese Society for Breaking the Status Quo (現状打破會) held a preparatory meeting to discuss the declaration and resolution to be submitted to the grand meeting on the 10th.
△ The weekly magazine Tongmyŏng (東明), managed by Mr. Ch'oe Namsŏn, is suspending publication.

June 5th: The Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department conducted a large-scale roundup of conspirators of the All-Japan Communist Party; Mr. Sakai Toshihiko and many other prominent figures were apprehended.
△ At the Lausanne Conference, Turkey conceded on extraterritoriality for seven years and consular jurisdiction for five years.

June 6th: The Sanitation Division of the Government-General revised and promulgated regulations concerning the control of morphine, cocaine, and related substances, effective today.
△ A large fire occurred at Pungmi-ch'ang (北米倉 - North Rice Warehouse?), completely burning down one building with three Japanese households and partially burning one building with two Korean households; damages amounted to about ten thousand won.
△ The Zhang Cabinet in Beijing resigned en masse at today's cabinet meeting.
△ Bandit atrocities occurred again in Jinan, resulting in the tragic massacre of over 50 people.
△ The U.S. government, after consulting with various countries, formally notified the Chinese committee to postpone indefinitely the dispatch of envoys to China via the Minister stationed in China.
△ The Japanese Imperial Military Reservists Association held a council meeting to begin efforts for the suffrage movement, electing officers and resolving other important matters.

June 7th: French Prime Minister Poincaré met with Belgian Prime Minister Theunis in Brussels to discuss the German reparations issue. The content was reportedly to further increase pressure on Germany to expedite the fulfillment of its obligations.
△ France is maneuvering in Moscow to reopen Franco-Russian trade relations.

June 8th: A report arrived stating that Mr. Ch'oe Chinbyŏng, leader of an Independence Party (獨立黨) based in Russian territory and leading three thousand troops, is negotiating with Chinese authorities to permit the free passage of Korean troops through Chinese territory (中領).
△ Students at Peking University from Hunan held a special meeting regarding the Changsha incident, resolved seven anti-Japanese articles, and then urged Military Governor Zhao Hengti (趙督軍) to declare war on Japan.
△ Gotō Shinpei, commissioned by the Japanese government, visited Mr. Adolph Joffe, conveyed the intention to commence preliminary negotiations, and asked for his agreement. Mr. Joffe generally accepted this but replied that he would give a formal response after receiving instructions from his home government.

June 9th: A youth baseball tournament hosted by the P'irun Sports Club opened at the P'irun-dong sports field. <120>
△ Due to a conspiracy by the Cao Kun (曹錕) faction, several hundred people surrounded President Li's (黎總統) residence, creating a temporarily dangerous situation, but they dispersed immediately.
△ The Allied Powers delivered a note to Germany regarding the resumption of allied control.

June 10th: The Hamhŭng-Oryori forest railway (9 miles and 3 fractions) began operation today.
△ The first general meeting of the Korean Journalists Association was held at the Kyŏngsŏng Hotel, with over 60 attendees on that day.
△ The Alliance for Breaking the Status Quo (現状打破同盟會) meeting was held at Ueno Seiyōken in Japan, with over a thousand participants.
△ A report arrived stating that the Bulgarian military clique had overthrown the Stamboliyski government.
△ Mr. Yan Huiqing (顔惠慶), concerned about numerous financial and other difficulties, visited President Li today and declined the premiership.
△ French novelist Pierre Loti passed away at the old age of 73.

June 11th: The Foreign Affairs Committee of the French Chamber of Deputies approved the report on the Washington Naval Treaty and submitted it to the Chamber.
△ Three hundred soldiers belonging to the Cao Kun faction and a crowd of over a thousand calling themselves the Citizens' Group (公民團) surrounded President Li's residence again, making the situation extremely dangerous.
△ French military forces executed five German civilians by firing squad for allegedly violating martial law.
△ About 70 residents of Yesan submitted a petition to the Government-General regarding the improper leasing of forest land.

June 12th: The Kyŏnggi Province Sanitation Division manufactured fifteen thousand promotional flags to encourage the catching of flies (捕繩 - lit. catching rope, likely referring to flypaper/traps) and distributed them to students of each common school.
△ The preliminary examination for the Ŭiyŏldan incident concluded.
△ Former Bulgarian Prime Minister Stamboliyski was arrested by his own guards.
△ Anti-Japanese sentiment in Hunan intensified further, with various organizations resolving to expel Japanese people.

June 13th: It was reported that a major clash occurred between over two hundred Korean workers and Japanese workers at a certain factory in Osaka, resulting in four serious injuries.
△ President Li resigned and went to Tianjin, attempting suicide on the way but failing.
△ The Bulgarian revolutionary party proclaimed the establishment of a new government both domestically and internationally, and declared that it would faithfully observe the Treaty of Neuilly and reject all military actions.

June 14th: A conference of principals of government-run middle schools from all of Korea was held in the Government-General's second conference room.
△ The Belgian cabinet resigned.

June 15th: A committee composed of 25 representatives from All-Worker-Peasant Russia (全勞農露國) planned to establish a united states formed by grouping all the republics of All-Russia.
△ Student representatives from 72 schools in Beijing held a joint meeting and resolved five points, including opposing a military president and the national assembly.
△ The arrested former Bulgarian Prime Minister Stamboliyski attempted to escape en route but was shot dead by armed peasants.

June 16th: The Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs formally notified the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs that Mr. Joffe had been selected as the Russian preliminary negotiation representative.
△ The Vladivostok (부市) revolutionary committee forced foreigners (excluding Koreans and Chinese) residing in the Aul Oblast (아울州) to leave within one week.
△ The Seipel cabinet of Austria resigned en masse.

June 17th: Mr. Foehren, a Communist Party member of the German Reichstag, was expelled from France.
△ 150 Japanese residents in Hankou evacuated due to threats in the surroundings making safe living impossible.

June 18th: 53 members of the Women's Study Tour Group hosted by Kaebyŏk went to Kaesŏng and returned to Kyŏngsŏng that night.
△ It is reported that heavy rain fell in Osaka, Japan, flooding over ten thousand houses.
△ The first public hearing of the International Court of Justice was held at The Hague; the presiding judge is Mr. Loder from the Netherlands.
△ The diplomatic corps argued for withdrawing recognition of the irresponsible Beijing government regarding the bandit incident. <121>
△ The Executive Committee of the Communist International elected Mr. Lenin as honorary chairman by lot.

June 19th: It was reported that the Koryŏ Communist Party is training aviators to attack Korea around August of this year.
△ The new Bulgarian government announced that over 600 people died in the recent revolutionary war.
△ Mount Etna erupted, with lava expanding in all directions at a speed of 250 meters per hour; 5 villages were annihilated, and thirty thousand residents lost their homes.
△ Chinese authorities in Hunan demanded the withdrawal of the Japanese warship Fushimi.

June 20th: About 30 members of an armed Independence Group (武裝獨立團) attacked and set fire to the Konyŏngsak-myŏn police substation in Sinŭiju; the whereabouts of 6 policemen are unknown.
△ Regarding the Lincheng Incident, the Diplomatic Corps' Reparations Committee, after days of deliberation, submitted three major demands to the Chinese government.
△ U.S. President Harding and his entourage of about 70 people traveled to Alaska.

June 21st: About a thousand residents from T'oeho-myŏn, Hamhŭng-gun, and Namch'ŏng-myŏn, Hongwŏn-gun, resolved to attack the T'oeho-myŏn police substation. The reason is that on Tano Day, the policemen of that substation arrested and caused the death of residents.
△ The Canada Asia Exclusion League submitted a petition for Japanese exclusion with seventeen hundred signatures to the parliament.
△ Before departing for his Alaska trip, the U.S. President granted amnesty to 74 prisoners serving time, reportedly the first such event in U.S. history.
△ The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs again demanded the withdrawal of the Japanese warship anchored in Changsha from the Japanese government.

June 22nd: It is said that Mr. Tang Shaoyi, with the support of Lu Yongxiang, is planning to establish a new government in Hangzhou.

June 23rd: Regarding the improper lease of Osŏk-san and Sindŏk-san mountains in Yonggang-gun, representatives Kim Sŏngok and Kim Ŭisu, representing over 500 residents of the county, submitted a petition to the Directorate of Industry (殖産局) of the Government-General.
△ The National Federation of Labour in Japan (日本全国労働総同盟会), led by Suzuki Bunji and Matsumoto Komakichi, divided into seven groups, issued resolutions on the Russia issue and labor problems, and conducted large-scale demonstrations.
△ The Worker-Peasant Government (勞農政府 - Soviet Russia) declared a boycott of Swiss people and all Swiss goods. This was because Switzerland refused compensation for the assassination of the Lausanne representative (Vorovsky).
△ The King of Belgium, Albert I (白耳義 황제 락켄陞下), was injured in a fall from a horse.

June 24th: The headquarters of the Korean Boy Scouts (朝鮮少年軍) organized a special Boy Scout Red Cross unit to propagate hygiene ideas among general youth.
△ Mr. Cao Kun notified the leaders of the various northern provincial military cliques and important figures of his own faction to gather in Baoding at the end of this month.
△ The Worker-Peasant Russia is planning to open an international exposition in Moscow; Germany reportedly has over 50 types of exhibits applied for by today.
△ The All-Russian Central Executive Committee and the Council of People's Commissars resolved to sever diplomatic relations with Switzerland.

June 25th: A meeting of Provincial Finance Department Chiefs opened in the Government-General's second conference room.
△ It is said that Chang Tso-lin, Lu Yongxiang, and Tuan Ch'i-jui are colluding to establish a central government in Shanghai and are plotting to elect Mr. Li Yuanhong, currently in Tianjin, as president.
△ The Shanghai Chinese Labour Union Federation sent telegrams to various places denouncing Mr. Cao Kun and Wu Peifu for coercing the head of state and brutally murdering workers with soldiers.

June 26th: It is said that various groups like the Korean Independence Army Blood Pledge Corps (大韓獨立軍 血誠團) and Red Flag (赤旗) have formed an offensive-defensive alliance and are planning large-scale assassinations, with weapons and funds supplied by the Red Army (赤軍).
△ The Kyŏngsŏng Municipal Council meeting was held within the School Association building; an important item for discussion was the construction of the Municipal Office building.
△ A report arrived stating that the Indian millionaire Mr. Motilal Nehru? (모데칼) donated 600 million won for Indian independence.
△ The Seventh International Aeronautical Congress opened in London, attended by representatives from 16 countries.
△ The Anglo-American arbitration treaty was extended for another 5 years.

June 27th: A major fire broke out in the Tianfu Palace (天福宮) of China's Xuantong Emperor (宣統帝 - Puyi), spreading to the Yanqing Pavilion (艶淸閣); damages are estimated at about ten million won. <122>
△ An agreement was reached between Poland and Romania; this was aimed at checking Russo-German cooperation.

June 28th: The first session of the unofficial Russo-Japanese consultations began today at 2 PM.
△ At a meeting of the leaders of the Chen Jiongming (陳炯明) faction, the pro-war argument was strong, rejecting compromise with the Sun (Sun Wen) faction and deciding to contest Guangdong by force.

June 29th: Political movements in India surged, and seven hundred volunteers were arrested by authorities.
△ A French court-martial sentenced 7 German individuals to death for destruction and violence.

June 30th: The trial of Chŏn Ujin, Yi Hyesu, etc., related to the Kim Sangok incident, who were being tried separately due to illness, opened. Chŏn Ujin was recommended for a 2 year 6 month sentence, and Yi Hyesu's trial was further postponed due to severe illness.
△ The first All-Korea Women's Tennis Tournament, hosted by the Dong-A Ilbo, opened at the Cheil Girls' High School sports ground in Chŏng-dong.
△ The Worker-Peasant government resolved to erect a statue of Marx in Moscow.

July 1st: The revised Family Register Law (戶籍令) came into effect today.
△ The Kwanghwamun telephone branch office opened.
△ Railway schedules were revised.
△ U.S. Secretary of the Treasury Mr. Mellon met with British Prime Minister Mr. Baldwin regarding the future economy of Europe.
△ Four hundred copper and iron workers in the Keflavik? (케푸부레든) region went on a general strike, resulting in major violence including factory destruction and assaults on officials.

July 2nd: Over one hundred female workers from four rubber shoe factories (Haedong, Seguchi, Tongyang, Kyŏnghae) within the Namsan Company went on a general strike.
△ A group of Korean students residing in Hawaii (希哇) arrived in Kyŏngsŏng; besides 20 students, Messrs. Min Ch'anho, Kim Yŏngu, and Kim Nodŭi accompanied them.
△ A telegram arrived from Berlin stating that French troops had occupied Krupp's factories.
△ A train in Kesshō? (케스빽) exploded, killing 18 Belgian soldiers.

July 3rd: Over 150 members of an armed Independence Group (武裝獨立團) attacked the Samjang substation in Musan-gun, killing 2 Japanese policemen and injuring 2 others.
△ The Turkish government proclaimed that approximately one hundred thousand foreigners residing in the capital region (郡府) would be fined if they did not obtain residence permits within one month.

July 4th: The first performance by T'owŏrhoe (土月會), a drama group formed by students in Tokyo, was held at the Chosŏn Theater in Seoul.

△ Japanese consular police in Kando (間島) apprehended Pang Hanmin and 24 other Korean radical communists centered around the Dongyang Institute (東洋學院) and confiscated one bomb.

△ Kim Ujin, related to the Kim Sangok incident, received a sentence of 2 years and 6 months at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court.

**Summary**

This article chronicles global and local news events from late May to late July 1923. It covers a wide range of topics including political turmoil in China (warlord conflicts, presidential ousting), international diplomacy (Lausanne Conference, Greco-Turkish relations, Russo-Japanese talks, Washington Naval Treaty ratification), social movements (Hyŏngp'yŏng movement opposition in Korea, suffrage demands in Japan, labor strikes in Korea, Japan, Germany, and US), Korean independence activities (border clashes, assassination plots, communist organizing, overseas negotiations), natural disasters (earthquake, volcanic eruption, floods), and colonial administration updates (new regulations, infrastructure projects). The sheer breadth of events reported reflects the interconnected and volatile nature of the world in the early 1920s. For historians, the article is particularly valuable for its detailed, dated accounts of Korean resistance efforts (Ŭiyŏldan, Independence Army activities, communist links), social tensions like the Hyŏngp'yŏng movement backlash, and labor activism under Japanese rule, providing specific names, dates, and locations often missing in broader narratives. It also contextualizes Korea's situation within wider regional (China, Japan, Russia) and global developments.
