Sorrowful Love, Yearning Thoughts
						Kim Ki-jin
Every night, every night I cannot sleep
Every night, every night I cannot sleep
I take out your photograph and look at it.

Every night, every night I cannot sleep
The stars look down from the heavens-

Every night, every night I cannot sleep
This body’s heart feels and weeps.

Every night, every night I cannot sleep
Inside Changgyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑), the cuckoo cries-

Every night, every night I cannot sleep
Filled entirely with your face<147>
In this body’s eyes, the monsoon rains fall.

Every night, every night I cannot sleep
Without rest, this body’s heart
Searches for you and flies away!

Let’s bury it,
Let’s bury it!
Biting my lips-

Forgetting the pain and suffering entirely
Let’s bury this pretty love.

Taking up a pickaxe
Let’s dig out the heart-

Forgetting the pain and suffering entirely
Let’s bury this pretty love.

Let’s bury it deep within the heart
So it cannot come out before this body grows old<148>

Forgetting the pain and suffering entirely
Let’s bury this pretty love.

Let’s bury it and turn away
Having buried this pretty love!

The broken mirror
Too sorrowful-
Holding the clear glass of a broken half
Reflecting in it, my heart’s
Broken half, where has it gone?

My heart's broken half
Shatters and flies away on a winter night
A bottomless clear mirror
A broken mirror!

A bottomless clear mirror
A broken mirror
Holding it and reflecting, the broken mirror
Who knows the sorrow of the mirror-<149>

The weeping heard when the mirror shattered
The tears shed when the mirror cracked
Upon my quietly withering heart
Fall drop by drop, spreading.

The mirror that reflected morning and evening
The mirror that showed my heart first
The face grown sick with thoughts of him
The mirror of love that reflected the face

The broken piece of the mirror of love
The sorrowful sigh that flowed when it shattered
Is never, never forgotten
Will never, never be forgotten
The broken half of the heart’s mirror

The heart’s broken mirror’s half
That broken half, where can I find it-
My heart, separated from you,
Is broken, shattered, trembling.<150>
<147-150>

**Summary**

This three-part poem by Kim Ki-jin expresses intense emotions of longing, heartbreak, and the desperate attempt to overcome sorrow. The first part depicts sleepless nights filled with yearning for a loved one, symbolized by looking at a photograph while nature (stars, cuckoos in Changgyŏngwŏn) reflects the speaker's pain. The second part portrays a violent effort to bury the "pretty love" and its associated pain, using the imagery of digging into one's own heart. The final part uses the metaphor of a broken mirror to represent the fragmented self and the enduring pain of lost love, emphasizing the inability to forget and the lingering sorrow. From a historical perspective, the mention of Changgyŏngwŏn, a former royal palace demoted to a park and zoo by the Japanese colonial government, subtly grounds the personal grief within a context of national humiliation and loss, potentially allowing personal sorrow to resonate with or reflect the collective experience of the era. The raw expression of pain and the attempt to suppress it might also echo the constrained atmosphere under colonial rule.
