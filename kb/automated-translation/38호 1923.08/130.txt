						雪嶽山人

India's Summer
						Sŏraksanin

Because India's territory is vast and its topography varied, its climate is also inconsistent. In terms of temperature, the Himalayan region is cool, while the rest belongs to the tropics or subtropics. The seasons are broadly divided into three periods: the dry hot season, the wet hot season, and the cool season. The dry hot season is from March to June; there is little rainfall, the air is dry, and the temperature reaches its peak, making it intensely hot, as if the whole world is inside a red furnace. The wet hot season is from June to September; it is hot with high rainfall. In particular, regions like Cherrapunji in Bengal (벵걀의 치에라분지) are said to be the rainiest areas in the world. The cool season is the period from the autumnal equinox to the vernal equinox of the following year; the northeast wind brings dry weather without rain, but as the temperature is not high, this period is called the best season in India.

In India, the year is not divided into four seasons like in Korea (朝鮮), but into the three periods as described above. The dry hot season and the wet hot season constitute India's summer. Although phrases like "long summer" (長夏) or "long, long summer days" (長長夏日) are often used in Korea or China (中國), India's summer is truly a long summer, lasting for a long period of seven months from March to September. Since it is tropical, excluding special cases, it is naturally hot, but the summer is exceptionally hotter and unbearable. Many people die from sunstroke, and animals hibernate underground or in tree hollows. Just as temperate animals hibernate in winter (冬眠), tropical animals aestivate in summer (夏眠).

The reason Buddhism speaks extensively of the Lotus Throne (蓮花臺) is that Indian people, unable to endure the suffering from heat (苦熱), constantly thought of tree shade as the supreme place of pleasure, and this idea was utilized in their teachings. The Government of India (印度政廳) conducts its affairs in Delhi (데레) during the five months of the cool season, but upon entering the early summer period, it relocates to Simla (시무라), a high-altitude location at 6,000 feet in the mid-slopes of the Himalayan mountain range, and conducts its duties there for seven months. In temperate regions, schools close for summer vacation <52> and government and public offices sometimes take leave, but in India, even the government seat relocates. Just seeing this allows one to guess how hot the Indian summer is. The southern regions, other coastal areas, and the inland plains are as hot as Korea's mid-summer (伏中) throughout spring, summer, autumn, and winter. The temperature difference between winter and summer is merely within about 5 degrees. In this zone, during the so-called rainy season, the temperature drops, offering some slight coolness, but from early summer onwards, the steaming heat becomes severe, making it difficult for humans and animals to endure. Also, in the northwestern region, near Punjab (빤짬), the temperature is generally around 90 degrees [Fahrenheit likely intended] except during winter, but the great Himalayan range to the north remains brilliantly white with snow even in summer. What is called the Snow Mountain (雪山) in Sanskrit texts refers to this mountain. The accumulated snow of this Himalaya mountain melts partially only upon reaching the peak of summer, flowing south into various rivers. The main rivers are the Ganges (恒河), the Indus (印度河), and the Brahmaputra (뿌라마푸트라). These three great rivers and the high Himalayan mountains are scenic spots in India, attracting many visitors throughout the four seasons, but summer visitors, seeking to escape the heat, are especially numerous. Indians have a bad habit of laziness (惰怠). However, it is not actually the Indian people themselves who are lazy, but rather, due to the climate, their vitality is naturally suppressed and diminished. India's summer is a major obstacle to India's development.

That seven months of the year belong to a summer period difficult for activity is a great misfortune for the Indian people. <53>

**Summary**

This article describes the climate of India, dividing it into three seasons: dry hot, wet hot, and cool. It emphasizes the extreme length (7 months) and severity of the Indian summer (combining the dry hot and wet hot seasons), detailing its unbearable heat, impact on humans (sunstroke), animals (aestivation), and even governance (the seasonal relocation of the capital from Delhi to Simla). The author contrasts India's climate with Korea's four seasons and attributes the perceived "laziness" (惰怠) of Indians not to inherent character but to the debilitating effects of the prolonged heat, calling the summer a major obstacle to India's development. From a historical perspective, the article offers insight into how Koreans viewed other parts of the world, particularly another region under colonial influence (British India), during the 1920s. The commentary linking climate directly to national character or development reflects environmental determinist ideas prevalent at the time, which historians might find interesting in the context of colonial-era discourse.

印度의 夏
						雪嶽山人
印度는 土地가 廣大하고 地勢의 變化가 多한 故로 氣候가 또한 不一하다. 溫度로 말하면 히마라야山地는 淸凉하고 其他는 熱帶 或은 亞熱帶에 屬하얏다. 季節을 大別하면 3期가 有하니 卽 乾熱季, 濕熱季, 冷凉季다. 乾熱季는 3月로부터 6月까지니 우량이 少하고 空氣가 乾燥하며 氣溫은 高度에 達하야 熱氣가 甚함으로 擧天地가 紅爐中에 在한 것과 갓다. 濕熟季는 6月로부터 9月까지니 高溫多雨하다. 特히 「벵걀」의 「치에라분지」와 如한 地方은 世界 第一의 多雨域이라 한다. 冷凉季는 秋分으로부터 翌年 春分에 至하는 期間이니 北東風이 來하야 無雨乾燥하나 氣溫이 高치 안이함으로 印度에셔는 此 時期를 第一 好季節이라 稱한다.
印度에셔는 朝鮮처럼 1年을 4季로 分치 안이하고 以上과 갓치 3期로 分한다. 乾熱季와 濕熱季는 卽 印度의 夏이다. 朝鮮이나 中國에셔 長夏이니 長長夏日이니 하는 文句를 만이 쓰지만은 참 印度의 녀름이야말로 長夏다. 卽 3月로부터 9月까지 7個朔의 長期다. 熱帶이니까 特別한 境遇를 除한 以外는 勿論 더읍갯지만은 녀름은 特別이 더 더워서 견딀 수 업다. 사람은 日射病에 걸녀 죽는 일이 만이 잇고 動物들은 地中이나 樹穴에 蟄伏하야 休眠을 한다. 溫帶의 動物이 冬眠을 하는 것 가치 熱帶의 動物은 夏眠을 한다.
佛敎에서 蓮花臺를 盛說하는 것은 印度 사람이 苦熱을 익이지 못하야 恒常 樹陰을 最上의 樂地로 思하는 故로 이것을 利用하야 말하는 것이다. 印度政廳은 冷凉季 5個月間은 「데레」에서 政務를 執하다가 初夏의 期에 入하면 海拔 6千尺 되는 高地 히마라야山脉 中腹에 잇는 「시무라」로 移轉하야 7個月間을 此處에서 執務한다. 溫度地方에서 夏季에 學校는 放學＜52＞하고 官公署에서는 或 休務를 하지만은 印度에서는 政廳까지 移轉을 한다. 이것만 보와도 印度의 녀름이 얼마나 더운지 可히 推測할 것이다. 南部 其他 海岸地方 及 國內 平原地方은 春夏秋冬이 다 朝鮮의 伏中과 가티 덥다. 冬과 夏의 溫度 差異는 5度 內外에 不過한다. 此 地帶는 所謂 雨季에는 氣溫이 下하야 多少 微凉을 感하지만은 初夏부터는 蒸炎이 甚하야 人畜이 堪耐키 難하다. 又 西北地方 卽 「빤짬」 附近은 冬季 以外에는 溫度가 大槪 90度 內外가 되나 北方 히마라야大山脉은 夏季에도 白雪이 皚皚하다. 梵書에 雪山이라 云한 것은 卽 此山을 指한 것이다. 此 히마라야山의 積雪은 盛夏에 至하여야 비로소 一部分이 消解하야 南으로 諸河에 流入한다. 川의 主要한 者는 恒河, 印度河, 뿌라마푸트라 等이니 이 3大河와 히마라야高山은 印度의 勝地로 4時 登臨客이 多하나 夏期에는 避暑客이 特히 多하다. 印度人은 惰怠의 惡習이 잇다. 하지만은 實은 印度人 그 사람이 惰怠한 것이 안이라 氣候의 關係로 自然 活動力이 滅殺되고 委縮되는 것이다. 印度의 녀름은 印度 發展의 大障害다.
1年 中 7個朔이 活動키 難한 夏季에 屬한 것은 印度人의 大不幸이다.＜53＞
