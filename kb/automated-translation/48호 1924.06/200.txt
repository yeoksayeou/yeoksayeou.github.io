Kaebyŏk Pukch'ŏng Branch Office

We have recently established a branch office as described above in Pukch'ŏng town for the convenience of our esteemed readers in Pukch'ŏng, Hongwŏn, Iwŏn, Tanch'ŏn, P'ungsan, Samsu, Kapsan, and surrounding areas. This office will handle "Kaebyŏk," "Sin Yŏsŏng," "Ŏrini," and other publications from Kaebyŏksa (Kaebyŏk Publishing Company). We hope that esteemed readers in the aforementioned regions will make requests directly to the said branch office.
Branch Manager: Yi Hyŏnjae
General Affairs Manager & Reporter: Yi Kujun
General Affairs Manager: Kang Sinkyo
Reporter & Advertisement Handler: Song Ch'anyong

Sŏnch'ŏn Branch Office Reporter Change
Reporter Mr. Wŏn Sangnim has resigned due to personal circumstances, and Mr. Kye Haengno has been newly appointed as liaison and reporter. <100>

**Summary**

This announcement informs readers of the Kaebyŏk magazine about the establishment of a new branch office in Pukch'ŏng (North Hamgyŏng Province) to serve readers in several northern counties. It lists the appointed staff for the new branch, including the manager, general affairs personnel, reporters, and an advertisement handler. It also announces a change in personnel at the Sŏnch'ŏn branch office, where one reporter has resigned and a new person has been appointed as both reporter and liaison. From a historical perspective, this notice highlights the organizational expansion of a major Korean cultural and nationalist magazine, Kaebyŏk, during the Japanese colonial period. It demonstrates the publisher's efforts to reach a wider audience in provincial areas, suggesting a growing readership and the importance placed on distributing potentially nationalist content despite colonial oversight. The specific mention of staff roles provides insight into the operational structure of such publishing ventures.
