**Body Text Translation:**
Kyŏngsŏng Seen from the Perspective of Organizations
						One Reporter

As newspapers and magazines often report, Kyŏngsŏng (京城), being the capital of Chosŏn (朝鮮) after all, has various individual organizational movements corresponding to each field, whether substantial or nominal. Labor movements, women's movements, youth movements, industrial movements, educational movements, religious movements, and even pro-Japanese movements (親日運動) – although they may be famous in name only, signboards exist in every field.

Originally, these are not historical organizations that have persisted steadfastly and systematically for hundreds or tens of years under the self-awareness of individuals with a set ideology and purpose. Rather, they are organizations that arose within just 2 or 3 years after the Ki-mi year [1919], transitional and experimental organizations, and moreover, organizations facing all sorts of political and economic persecution. Therefore, it can be said that there are still no organizations with significant strength and proper organization. <84>

Originally, a people without political freedom and economic freedom cannot form organizations or conduct organizational movements. Even if they do, it might be things like friendship societies (親睦會), self-cultivation societies (修養會), or alumni associations (同窓會) under the dictates of politics and economics, but it is extremely difficult to initiate a specific movement based on some ideology.

This is proven by the brief history of the past 3 or 4 years. We vividly remember how many ephemeral gatherings (作出今沒 今起明伏, 朝露的 會合) – appearing today and gone tomorrow, rising now and falling later like morning dew – have occurred just in Kyŏngsŏng. The current ambiguous state (有耶無耶的 現况) of existing organizations also stems from this cause.

Let me say it once more. For a people without political freedom and economic freedom, the organization and operation (運行) of groups are extremely difficult. Therefore, aside from secret societies, nothing more than the current state can be maintained.

From this perspective, we can neither strongly approve nor strongly criticize (攻迫) the substance or lack thereof (有實無實) of the various organizations in Kyŏngsŏng. That being said, I will now introduce these organizations, even if only by name.

First, looking at cultural and social movements, what is fresh in our memory are the two organizations formed in Kyŏngsŏng this spring: the All Korea Labor-Farmer General Alliance (全鮮勞農總同盟) and the All Korea Youth General Alliance (全鮮靑年總同盟) (although nationwide, their headquarters are in Kyŏngsŏng). In addition, there is the Seoul Youth Association (서울靑年會), which for years has stood on the side of ideology and caused trouble (말성) in both the capital and the countryside with its propaganda of new thought; the New Rising Youth Alliance Association (新興靑年同盟會), newly established this spring with the aim of building a new society for the proletariat (無産級); and the recently established Women Comrades Association (女性同友會). Besides these, in the purely labor field, there are the Labor Mutual Aid Society (勞働共濟會) (formed in January of Kyŏngsin [1920] chiefly by Mr. Pak Chung-hwa (朴重華) and Mr. Pak Il-gyu (朴珥圭)) and the Labor Convention (노동대회) (in February of Kyŏngsin [1920] chiefly by Mr. Kim Kwang-je (金光濟) and Mr. Yi Sŏl-jong (李說鍾)), which continue activities in the same field.

Next, in the industrial field, besides the Korean Products Promotion Society (朝鮮物産獎勵會) and the Native Products Loving Use Women's Association (土産愛用婦人會), initiated mainly by certain gentlemen (紳士級) of Kyŏngsŏng at the end of the Im-sul year [1922], there are only small signboards in each district aimed at honor or profit, such as the Taishō Industrial Friendship Society (大正實業親睦會) or the Worker Friends Club (工友俱樂部). In the education field, besides the Korean Education Society (朝鮮敎育會) and the People's University Headquarters (民立大學本部), there is the Women's Education Association (女子敎育協會) advocated by Ms. Kim Mirisa (金美理史). In the religious field, there is the YMCA (基督敎靑年會), as well as Christian Epworth Youth Associations (基督敎엡웟靑年會) and Diligence Youth Associations (勉勵靑年會) scattered in various neighborhoods (洞町). Additionally, there are the Cheondogyo Youth Party (天道敎靑年黨), Buddhist Youth Association (佛敎靑年會), Daejonggyo Youth Association (大倧敎靑年會), and Daehwagyo Youth Association (大華敎靑年會). For the youth movement, there are the Youth Movement Association (少年運動協會), Central Scout Troop (中央斥候團), Cheondogyo Youth Association (天道敎少年會), and Christian Youth Department (基督敎少年部). For the women's movement, there are the Korean Women's Youth Association (朝鮮女子靑年會), the aforementioned Women Comrades Association (女性同友會), and the YWCA (基督敎女子靑年會). In the student field, there are the Korean Students' Association (朝鮮學生會), <85> the Struggling Students Mutual Aid Society (苦學生갈돕會), and the Women Struggling Students Mutual Aid Society (女子苦學生相助會). Other miscellaneous associations include the Enlightenment Club (啓明俱樂部) of Min Tae-sik (閔大植) and Pak Sŭng-bin (朴勝彬); the Korean Economy Society (朝鮮經濟會) (Taishō 8 [1919]) of Pak Yŏng-hyo (朴泳孝) and Ch'oe Chin (崔鎭); the Confucian Way Promotion Society (儒道振興會) of Yun Yong-gu (尹用求) and Chŏng Pong-si (鄭鳳時); the Great Eastern Literati Society (大東斯文會) (Taishō 9 [1920]) of Chŏng Man-jo (鄭萬朝) and Ŏ Yun-jŏk (魚允迪); the People Preservation Society (維民會) (Taishō 8 [1919]) of Ko Hŭi-jun (高羲駿) and Kim Sang-muk (金尙黙); the National Association (國民協會) (Taishō 9 [1920]) of Kim Myŏng-jun (金明濬) and Yi Tong-u (李東雨), aimed at Japanese-Korean assimilation (日鮮融化); the Tenant Farmers Mutual Aid Society (小作人相助會) of Song Pyŏng-jun (宋秉駿) and Ch'ae Ki-du (蔡基斗); and the recent so-called Alliance of Representatives from Various Factions (各派 有志聯盟會), Fellow People Association (同民會), Mutual Love Society (相愛會), and other such countless (枚擧키 難한) various organizations. Besides these, under the banner of religion, there are Chesegyo (濟世敊�), Cheugyo (濟愚敎), Ch'ŏngnimgyo (靑林敎), Paekpaekkyo (白白敎), T'aegŭkkyo (太極敎), Tan'gun'gyo (檀君敎), Sich'ŏn'gyo (侍天敎), Chehwagyo (濟化敎), T'ongch'ŏn'gyo (統天敎), Samsŏng Mu'gŭkkyo (三聖旡極敎), Poch'ŏn'gyo (普天敎), Taehwagyo (大華敎), Kisŏnggyo (箕聖敎), Suun'gyo (水雲敎), or Sungsingyo (崇神敎), Sungsinin Chohap (崇神人組合), and countless other various organizations, but it is difficult to enumerate them all one by one. Furthermore, there are various types of organizations among Japanese and Westerners, but they are omitted.

Kyŏngsŏng is indeed a Kyŏngsŏng of signboards (看板), a Kyŏngsŏng of mere names (名色), a Kyŏngsŏng of formality (形式). One wonders how it would be if the content were more substantial than the name, but for now, there is little that can be done about the situation (處地). Let us wait and see what the future (後期) holds. <86>

**Summary**

The article surveys the landscape of social, political, economic, educational, and religious organizations in Kyŏngsŏng (colonial Seoul) in 1924. The author notes the proliferation of groups formed after the 1919 March 1st Movement, covering diverse fields from labor and women's rights to pro-Japanese assimilation efforts and various new religions. However, the author emphasizes that due to the lack of political and economic freedom under Japanese rule, these organizations are mostly superficial, lacking deep roots, substantial power, or effective organization, often existing merely as "signboards." The author expresses a reserved judgment, acknowledging the difficult circumstances while hoping for future substance. For historians, this article offers a valuable snapshot of Korean civil society organizing under colonial constraints. It lists numerous specific organizations (including leftist, nationalist, cultural, religious, and pro-Japanese groups) and key figures, providing primary source data on the diverse but fragile nature of associational life and the socio-political climate in Seoul just five years after the March 1st Movement. The mention of specific dates (Ki-mi, Kyŏngsin, Im-sul, Taishō years) and the explicit linkage between political/economic freedom and effective organization are particularly revealing.

발행일	1924년 06월 01일
기사제목	團軆方面으로 본 京城
필자	一記者
기사형태	소식

團軆方面으로 본 京城
						一記者
신문 잡지가 자조 보도함과 가티 京城은 그래도 朝鮮의 首府인지라 有實無實間에 각 방면에 亙하야 그 방면을 따라 各個의 단체적 운동이 잇다. 노동운동 부인운동 소년운동 산업운동 교육운동 종교운동 또는 親日運動까지 비록 有名無實이나마 간판은 각 방면에 다 잇다.
본래-멧 백년 멧 십년을 두고 각자의 자각 하에서 일정한 主義 일정한 목적을 두고 조직잇게 根氣잇게 내려 온 역사적 단체가 안이오 己未 이후 불과 2,3年 內에 이러난 단체인지라 또는 과도기의 시련적 단체인지라 더구나 정치상 경제상 모든 迫害를 당하는 단체인지라 상당한 실력과 상당한 조직이 잇는 단체는 아직 업다 해도 可하다.＜84＞
본래-정치의 자유 경제의 자유가 업는 민족에게는 단체의 조직 또는 단체적 운동을 못하는 것이다. 한다야 정치 경제가 식키는 下에서 親睦會니 修養會니 同窓會니 그 따윗것이나 할 지 모르나 제법 엇더한 사상을 根底로 하야 일정한 운동을 喚起하기는 극히 難한 事이다.
이는 短하나마 과거 3,4年來의 역사가 証明하는 것이다. 作出今沒 今起明伏의 朝露的 會合이 京城에만 그 數가 幾許나 되얏는가를 吾人은 歷歷히 기억한다. 현재 단체의 有耶無耶的 現况도 亦 此에서 原因함이다.
다시 한번 더 말해둔다. 정치의 자유 경제의 자유가 업는 민족에게는 단체의 조직 또 運行이 極難한 것이다. 故로써 비밀결사 외에는 今日의 현상밧게 더 유지 할 수 업는 것이다.
이러한 점에서 吾人은 京城 각 단체에 대한 有實無實을 그닥 찬성치도 못하고 그닥 攻迫치도 못한다. 그럿타 하고 이제부터 그! 단체 이 名義만이라도 소개하기로 한다.
