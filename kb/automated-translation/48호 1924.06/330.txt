필자	懷月
기사형태	소설 (Short Story)

愛의 挽歌
						懷月
一
In Chŏngp'al's room, the light was not turned on even late into the night. That didn't mean Chŏngp'al was asleep. He lay covered with a blanket, but his heart trembled like someone caught in the cold. Nothing could be seen in the pitch-black night. However, twinkling stars peeked through the torn gaps in the window.
Chŏngp'al was not a lazy person. But the window, like an ancient temple housing an old Buddhist statue, like an abandoned ruin, was pierced with holes here and there. And that wasn't all. As the light was never turned on in Chŏngp'al's room at night, the bedding was never folded. Sometime or other, he had spread out the mattress and blanket once, and never folded or spread them again. To others, he might seem lazy, but for Chŏngp'al, infinite peace resided there.
If anyone were to look at Chŏngp'al's room from the outside, it would seem as though no existence whatsoever was within.
But within that room like a ruin, there was the history of Chŏngp'al's blood, like ancient remains, and within Chŏngp'al's room, somber as a shrine, there was Chŏngp'al's sacred heart, like a Buddhist statue.
Sometimes, the cold wind entering through the torn room and window gaps strangely became warm once inside the room.
Within Chŏngp'al's room, where no hope seemed visible to others, there were overflowing tears.
Chŏngp'al belonged to no religion, but had a religion he served alone. Accordingly, he was a believer who knew nothing but himself. When sleeping at night, when working during the day, when waking in the morning—<151>

Ah! It is love. What is your wish?
Ah! It is love.
The love unseen, yet always embraced within the heart, Ah! It is the wish.

People's dreams are in the night, and their reality is in the day.
But my reality is in the night, and my dream is in the day.
It is because people's love is in the day, and my love is in the night.
I, who see that love exists, call the night my reality.
Ah! What is your wish?
Ah! My wish is the love seen at night.
Ah! Please quickly take away this painful dream of daylight, and give me the night with love! Ah, it is my wish—.

Prayers like this were heard daily from Chŏngp'al's lips. Anyone hearing that prayer sound might think,
—Oh! I see. A lovely young lady must go into that dreadful fellow's room every night and melt the bachelor Chŏngp'al away. But anyone suspicious should watch outside Chŏngp'al's door to see if any young lady enters. Night after night may pass, but not even a fly enters Chŏngp'al's room. Yet, listen to Chŏngp'al's song at night:

Lovely, the front appearance
Turn around, the back appearance
When smiling, the lip appearance<152>
Let's kiss, my appearance

Don't go, my love
When dancing the love dance
Did you not know it was a dream fragment?
Let this dream always be

Such song could be heard from Chŏngp'al's room deep into the night. Anyone hearing it couldn't help but think there must be a maiden in Chŏngp'al's room. And so, if someone were to open Chŏngp'al's door, they would know. But Chŏngp'al's door was locked from the inside and bolted from the outside. Thus, those looking from outside became even more suspicious.
And so, some people would say,
—That dreadful fellow must put the maiden in a chest and take her out at night—and laugh.
Especially, watch Chŏngp'al's room on a windy, uncanny, moonlit night.
It is said that a young widow is startled by the sound of a spring flute, but more than being playful, any kind-hearted and amiable girl, old widow, or woman quick to laugh who might peek into Chŏngp'al's room would find no one who wouldn't shed tears, no woman who wouldn't feel sympathy. When the trembling paper screen shook, the pale moonlight alone could peek into Chŏngp'al's room.

In the sky, the moon sea
Tear sea, my heart
Eyes closed, blood sea
Thinking, fire sea.<153>

In the sky, the moon sea
In my heart, the blood sea
You who cross the sea coming
Why are you late?

If the road is long as you come,
Ride a boat on the moon sea
If you are sick as you come,
Rest in my heart

Whenever the moon was bright, the song Chŏngp'al cried out was always like this. Then, when Chŏngp'al fell deep asleep, a joyful expression finally appeared on his face. And then,
Lovely, the front appearance
Turn around, the back appearance...
he would murmur indistinctly in his sleep.
Who could hear this sound and be without tears?
But there was no one to see Chŏngp'al, nor anyone who came looking for him.
Only his old mother and young sibling were there, so there was also no one who could well understand Chŏngp'al's heart's circumstances.
Sometimes, when the old mother asked,
Hey, Chŏngp'al! Isn't the room cold?
Chŏngp'al would give no answer, but instead,<154>

If fire is lit in the cold room,
Will my frozen heart melt?
If my heart becomes warm,
Traces of dreams will vanish

But the old mother did not know what these words meant. Although it was not unknown externally, Chŏngp'al's mother always wept alone inwardly, thinking, "How can I cure this madness?"
She had given him much medicine, but she did not know what Chŏngp'al's illness was.

二
My love who comes in dreams,
If you only come at night,
Today is also night,
It's strange you do not come.

Chŏngp'al's parched lips sang this song as he lay down in a corner of his heart. Chŏngp'al's body, usually intoxicated by sweet dreams day after day, had been unable to sleep even for a moment since yesterday, tossing and turning in the stained blankets, only nurturing a heart of suffering. Accordingly, the dream that used to carry Chŏngp'al's love also did not come since yesterday.
—Dream—
Within that dream, there was only Chŏngp'al's brightly leaping life. Within it were Chŏngp'al's laughter, joy, hope, and love. And so, Chŏngp'al's dream came 찾아 every night to soothe his tormented chest. But that dream did not return last night or tonight. Chŏngp'al's heart was incomparably tormented and weary. Even saliva dried up in his mouth,<155> and sticky cold sweat appeared on his forehead. When he occasionally sighed, "Hoo—," a fiery heat emerged that startled even Chŏngp'al. The daily torment of reality that Chŏngp'al received was barely escaped only in dreams. But a night without dreams sought to cut Chŏngp'al's life into pieces.

Whenever the dream came, it unfailingly carried the beautiful figure of the woman named Ŭnsun.
But as the dream did not come, Ŭnsun's lovely figure could not be seen either.
—Ah! Miss Ŭnsun— Calling out Ŭnsun's name during the day, whom he embraced in dreams, always disappointed Chŏngp'al. There was no chance she would come, nor could he see her face. Yet, before falling asleep—Ah! Miss Ŭnsun—the times he cried out loudly were not just once or twice. At least ten times... twenty times... a hundred times... he called out countlessly like this. When he called her name, he did not do so carelessly. He called as if someone were listening nearby, with a reverent heart, a respectful tone, and a voice overflowing with love. This is because he believed an invisible phantom of Ŭnsun was always beside him.
—Ah! Today I will follow Miss Ŭnsun. I will follow Miss Ŭnsun— Chŏngp'al pushed aside the stained blanket in the dark room and neatly fixed his clothes and ties. Then he re-laid his sleeping place just enough for him to lie down comfortably.
—Ah!— Sighing, Chŏngp'al touched the glistening dagger placed beside him. At that moment, the ice-cold dagger sharply startled Chŏngp'al. As if caressing a beloved creature (좀생) he was about to part with, he stroked the dagger this way and that. He touched it to his face, and to his chest. He touched it to his belly, and held it close, thinking for a while. During that time, the ice-cold dagger became almost as warm as Chŏngp'al's body temperature. He closed his eyes again and thought. Then he heard a song rising from within his heart.<156>

—If the trace of the person who came in dreams
Does not return in dreams,
And the trace of the dream that came by night
Does not return by night,
I, who weave dreams and night, will go eternally.—

When thoughts of such a song faintly arose in his mind, goosebumps ran all over Chŏngp'al's body. And he began to tremble. In Chŏngp'al's heart, from which neither tears nor words came, there was a resentful cry like the mournful song of a maiden drowned in the desolate night sea.
—Ah! Then does that mean she died?— His voice trembled uncontrollably.
—Ah! Love! Love! Are you dead or alive? If alive, come in a dream; if dead, show me your body—
But what use was there! The cold night wind blew in—ssoo—through the torn door gap and brushed over the dagger in Chŏngp'al's hand, disappearing.
Chŏngp'al's chest felt cool. But the blood of his boiling heart did not cool even a bit, nor did it yield even a bit. His mind being cool was because he possessed cool reason, and his cold blade becoming hot was because he possessed a passion hot as fire. However, soon, even the cold reason would surely heat up.
When Chŏngp'al closed his eyes and his mind was momentarily faint, he saw a past dream again.
= A field on a splendid spring day, flowers blooming everywhere. The stream water is cold, but watching it flow down gently has a warm quality that pulls at the heart. Drooping willows by the water, the cries of many birds on the mountain, the sound of a grass flute on the lawn—if you place a hand on your chest, you will hear the riotous song of dancing blood.<157> Chŏngp'al waited for a long time by the stream. But Ŭnsun did not come. He waited until his body and mind were exhausted. But she did not come. He waited with all his sincerity. But she was not seen. The saying, "Meeting the great person amidst the hundred orchids," once again hinted at its truth to Chŏngp'al.
Unable to bear it any longer, Chŏngp'al looked again with doubtful eyes at the letter from Ŭnsun he held in his hand.
『Please wait for sure! Okay? April 20th, 1 PM, okay? -Omitted- Without your love, I cannot know the meaning of living in this world even a little.』
Chŏngp'al nodded his head and couldn't suppress a laugh, letting out a "Pfft—." "The date and time are definitely today," he thought, looking out at the wide field of Changch'undan, when suddenly a "Bang—" sound occurred behind Chŏngp'al.
It was just noon now. Chŏngp'al's surprise was not small. He would have to wait for another hour. Waiting for a loved one should be pleasant enough, but his heart's demand for the loved one was extremely urgent.
He bravely endured the unbearable fatigue and intolerable weariness. Finally, when Ŭnsun took Chŏngp'al's hand and said,
『You waited quite a long time, didn't you?』 Chŏngp'al's withering, tired nerves gained the strong power of resurrection.
『No. It hasn't been long since I arrived.』
He smiled, looking at Ŭnsun. Just then, a gust of wind threw a cluster of cherry blossoms onto their faces.
『The flowers are already falling.』
『Time passes so quickly.』
Without any shyness, the two held hands at the same moment. And then they couldn't find any more words that would harmonize.<158>
How Chŏngp'al's heart must have leaped with joy and agony! Then, without even a moment to speak again, half a year suddenly fled.

To relieve his melancholic feelings, Chŏngp'al was taking a walk late at night on the Han River Railway Bridge. His body, which had been walking alone, found itself holding Ŭnsun's hand and strolling before he knew it.
『Miss Ŭnsun! You understand all my feelings well, don't you?』
『..』
『Then why don't you give me a clear answer?』
『..』
Chŏngp'al rushed towards Ŭnsun. And he roughly embraced Ŭnsun. But his heart was indescribably compassionate.
『Let go,』 Ŭnsun cried out in a quiet voice when her chest was buried deep within Chŏngp'al's strong chest.
『Hmm?』
When Chŏngp'al's body trembled violently, Ŭnsun suddenly wept. She sobbed, choking back tears. How great must Chŏngp'al's surprise have been?
『Why are you crying?』
『..』
『Huh?』
『I am a body that must leave!』
『Where to?』<159>
『Eternally—』
『When will you come back, then—?』
『To eternity—』
『Are you abandoning me?』
『..』
『Then you mean you're leaving me?』
『Yes?』
At that moment, from Ŭnsun's lovely crying lips, a song like this flowed out.

If the trace of the person who comes in dreams
Does not return in dreams,
And the trace of the dream that came by night
Does not return by night,
I, who wove dreams and night, will go eternally.—

『Ah! So you really mean to go?』
Suddenly, along with that song, Ŭnsun's form vanished.
At that moment, Chŏngp'al stared blankly down at the endlessly flowing river where dark waves surged. From the distant fog, only a single fishing boat (漁船) drifted by, its light twinkling. The gloomy night wind chilled Chŏngp'al's chest.
He heard a sorrowful song from afar.<160>

Oh, fisherman drifting by,
Having cast your line in the dark night,
In the dark waves of the river,
Don't rejoice that there are fish.

The girl from the house upstream last night
Died at this water's edge,
Her spirit became a fish
And came out to lure you.

The fish caught at night,
Carefully enshrined and placed,
Pray three times a day
And release them to the Dragon Palace.

Listening to this song, Chŏngp'al was walking, shedding tears on the deserted bridge, when suddenly from behind, someone called, 『Mr. Chŏngp'al!』 As Chŏngp'al turned back to look, he woke from this dream.—

For Chŏngp'al, this was not just a dream but a beautiful, precious, treasure-like reality. More than anything else in this world, Chŏngp'al's dream was an unforgettable reality for Chŏngp'al's soul. If there hadn't been dreams every night for Chŏngp'al's feverish body, burning hot from thinking of Ŭnsun nightly, Chŏngp'al would have burned to death.<161> Not a single drop of blood would have remained; it all would have dried up. But only through the cool Water of Life poured by the dream did he continue his precious life.
Since yesterday, the dream that came at night did not come to Chŏngp'al. Chŏngp'al could not see Ŭnsun, who wove dreams and love for him.
—Then Ŭnsun must have died—
Thinking this, he recalled the song of Ŭnsun heard long ago in a dream.
『If the dream that comes at night, and the love that comes in the dream, do not come, know that I have gone eternally.』
—Oh! That's right. Ŭnsun is dead—.
At that moment, Chŏngp'al had no will to live.
Chŏngp'al, who believed dreams were reality, hated living without seeing the Ŭnsun he saw in dreams.
And again, he caressed the dagger that had grown warm in his hand. And he nodded his head a couple of times. Like a dying person's final gesture, Chŏngp'al glanced around the room. In the dim room lay scattered:
—One letter. One photograph. A shriveled apple in a basket. The dagger in his hand.—
These four things lay haphazardly in Chŏngp'al's room. Chŏngp'al looked at them one last time. These four things were a great fortune that money could not buy. This was Chŏngp'al's soul, and Chŏngp'al's soul was precisely these four objects. At this time, Chŏngp'al slowly put down the dagger from his hand and reminisced about the flower-like old thoughts concerning his entire fortune.

三
『One Letter』
First, Chŏngp'al unfolded this letter again and laid it out. As he held the letter, his heart began to tremble. His hand began to tremble. But from his heart, cold blood danced hotly, like leaves greeting summer. Chŏngp'al pressed his lips to the letter and held the kiss for a long time. Then, in a trembling voice, he read it.<162>
— Why do you annoy me like this? With that ugly face, with that ugly face— Do you still think you're handsome? —I cannot love someone like you. You said you would love me no matter what, even dedicating your life, right? But I cannot love you dedicating my life. Yes! Accordingly, I already have someone I love. Therefore, I cannot love you too. Of course, you can love me as much as you want. But I cannot.
Yi Ŭnsun
It was like this. Nevertheless, Chŏngp'al had been so delighted to receive the flower-like handwriting of Ŭnsun. Whatever the content inside the letter, when Ŭnsun wrote the letter to Chŏngp'al, her heart and brush held the smallest element capable of pleasing Chŏngp'al.
Whether the words were 『You are lovely』 or 『You are hateful』, either way, coming from those lovely, cute lips, it was the most valuable judgment. Just for that, Chŏngp'al's heart had a cry overflowing with joy.

Once, about a month or two ago, Chŏngp'al went to visit a friend's house and happened to see a female student. That girl was the niece of Chŏngp'al's friend, Sunbok. He saw her once when he went the first time, twice the second time, three times the third time; doing this, Chŏngp'al received an unbearable shock to his heart. He couldn't endure the cry emerging despite attempts at composure, nor the fierce hoofbeats of blood galloping uncontrollably despite attempts at silence. From then on, whenever Chŏngp'al went to see Sunbok, he always faced inwards and strained his ears. One day,
when he heard the sound —Ŭnsun-ah—
the reply —Yes— was undoubtedly that girl's voice. From then on, he knew well that the girl was Ŭnsun. However, Chŏngp'al shed endless tears of agony in his heart for about a week. Having done all he could,<163> Chŏngp'al gathered all his shame, all scorn (치소), and all courage, and wrote a letter to Ŭnsun. But the reason he didn't inform Sunbok beforehand of his intention to write the letter was none other than that he had anticipated receiving useless criticism even if he mentioned it. If Chŏngp'al's face were handsome like others, it might have been different, but with Chŏngp'al's face that received scorn even among men, he lacked the courage to tell Sunbok. Of course, although they didn't speak, Ŭnsun and Chŏngp'al knew each other's faces well enough from Chŏngp'al's frequent visits. Chŏngp'al's heart was exceptionally beautiful, compensating for his ugly face. But what was the result after Chŏngp'al sent the letter? A cry of painful and bitter despair emerged from that reply from Ŭnsun.
The letter Chŏngp'al just reread was that very one. But instead of being disappointed, Chŏngp'al was so delighted upon receiving the letter that he could have danced. Of course, Chŏngp'al knew there would be harsh criticism about his face from Ŭnsun. But that she would reply with a letter at all was completely unexpected. Moreover,
『I cannot love you, but you can love me as much as you want』 was a beautiful statement.
However, after that, he didn't meet Sunbok for about five or six days.
Chŏngp'al folded this letter again. And kissed it again. Then he tucked it into his warm bosom.
He picked up the photograph again. At that moment, indeed, Chŏngp'al's life seemed to be drawn out through this photograph. This was truly, truly Ŭnsun's photograph.

—That half-smiling face
Flower bud, your face
When smiling, moon face
When blinking, star face<164>
Seeing me, getting angry
Crying, looking at me
Always smiling
Your face looking at me—

Whenever Chŏngp'al held the photograph and looked at it, he always mumbled a song like this. Only this photograph was the Angel that always generously soothed Chŏngp'al's soul. Even now, Chŏngp'al held this photograph, smiled at it, and also shed tears. But Ŭnsun's smiling face alone overflowed with joy. It was a smile like that of someone beside their lover, a smile that couldn't be more joyful than this.

四
『Ŭnsun's Photograph』
One moonlit night, about a week after Chŏngp'al received the letter from Ŭnsun, the thought suddenly flashed in his mind that he had seen Ŭnsun's photograph in Sunbok's desk drawer.
—Yes, a marvelous invention for an unbearable night— he thought, pondering how he could retrieve that photograph. But there was no way. Then Chŏngp'al thought of a truth.
—To succeed in the greatest things, there must be the greatest courage and the greatest sacrifice— he realized. If Chŏngp'al's love was his entire life, he thought he must have the courage and passion to offer his life.
Chŏngp'al nodded his head as if having decided something and let out a determined sound "Ung—" under his breath. And he slipped outside. Lost in thought, Chŏngp'al reached Sunbok's house. It was long past eleven at night. But fortunately, the main gate was not locked, perhaps someone from the house had gone out.<165> Chŏngp'al neatly tucked up his overcoat (두루막이). And like a thief entering to steal a great treasure, he carefully lifted and opened the door panel. Then he sharply ducked his body forward and slipped through. Fortunately, he entered the gate without making any sound. And he quickly found the small alley on the right leading to Sunbok's room, which he knew well.
The electric light in Sunbok's room was still on. Chŏngp'al, who knew Sunbok's habits well—thinking of Sunbok who always turned off the light when sleeping at night—thought Sunbok was still awake. But not knowing for sure, he first stood outside Sunbok's room and listened for a while. But there was no sound of people talking. So, Chŏngp'al first pierced a small hole in the window paper and peered inside. Only then did he discover that Sunbok was sleeping.
Sunbok was asleep, and fortunately, the desk was placed near the room door.
Chŏngp'al quietly opened the door. The sharp rays of the waning moon, three days past full, like an assassin, slipped in ahead of Chŏngp'al. Chŏngp'al's heart felt cool. At this moment, Chŏngp'al's mind departed from all worldly thoughts and weariness; a small boat waited on a sea of overflowing blood, with the hammer of a red heart in new life creation, ready to carry Chŏngp'al's trembling soul.
Chŏngp'al gently tried to lift the desk. But it was surprisingly light. The desk that had seemed heavy when he saw it before was so light. At this moment, he instinctively realized the great power possessed by the focused mind. He lifted it out safely. Then he tried to open the drawer. But it was locked. He hesitated and panicked for a moment, but Chŏngp'al remembered again that the key to Sunbok's desk drawer was in the right-hand drawer. So he carefully searched every corner of the unlocked right-hand drawer. And he found the key. Finally, Chŏngp'al took out the lovely and beloved photograph of Ŭnsun. He quickly put Ŭnsun's photograph into his bosom. Then he lifted the desk as before and safely placed it back in its original spot. Just as he was about to close the room door, Chŏngp'al was startled tremendously by the sound of the main gate. Whether someone was leaving or entering this late at night, he didn't know, but regardless, the gate of happiness Chŏngp'al was about to exit through was now locked.<166>
For a moment, Chŏngp'al didn't know what to do and was afraid. Although the crime he committed was most pure, clean, and sacred, he had no time for such thoughts; the object he possessed felt as frightening as embracing a sleeping giant. Having no other choice, Chŏngp'al discovered a low wall exposed above the outhouse. And so, Chŏngp'al climbed over the wall and reached the main street. He had created a splendid paradise out of the most terrifying hell of fear. When he looked at the photograph under the blue moonlight, how his heart pounded! When he saw Ŭnsun's smiling face, what unbearable joy he tasted! Even if Chŏngp'al could freely embrace Ŭnsun, kiss her, and laugh, there wouldn't have been such a profound and vast joy as now. Like the river seen through mist, it was dim, yet full of hope.

In Chŏngp'al's hand now, holding this photograph with such a history, he gazes at it blankly. Although Chŏngp'al tasted overflowing joy from this photograph, he couldn't help but shed sorrowful tears for Ŭnsun, who hadn't appeared in his dreams for two days already. Just as Chŏngp'al's existence gained value only after possessing Ŭnsun's photograph, Ŭnsun's photograph gained more value only when the Ŭnsun who came in dreams existed.
With blood-tinged eyes, he looked once more at the smiling Ŭnsun. Then he folded it carefully and also tucked it into his bosom. Then he looked at the apple in the basket, still emitting a good scent. Although it was thoroughly dried up, it still held a beautiful fragrance. With Chŏngp'al's own parched hand, he lifted the basket and placed it before him. And he thinks of the old days again.

五
『The Shriveled Apple』
One day, less than ten days after Chŏngp'al had taken Ŭnsun's photograph, he met a friend named Yongch'ŏl on the street. Yongch'ŏl<167> was not someone he met often. But he was a friend who often knew all of Chŏngp'al's circumstances. Though they met infrequently, his thoughts for Chŏngp'al were significant. Yongch'ŏl was greatly surprised seeing Chŏngp'al's gaunt face.
—How did your face get like that?— My face?— Chŏngp'al laughed. Chŏngp'al delighted in his face becoming paler. It was because he wanted to quickly eliminate this painful reality. Chŏngp'al quickly counter-questioned.
—Where are you going?
—I'm on my way back from visiting Sunbok's house. — Chŏngp'al was startled by these words.
—Sunbok?
—Yeah.
—Why?
—Sunbok's niece is being admitted to the hospital, so he asked me to introduce some doctors I know—.
—Sunbok's niece? Then that—
Before he could finish, Yongch'ŏl said,
—Ah, you mean Ŭnsun!
And he looked meaningfully at Chŏngp'al's face.
—What illness?
After saying this, Chŏngp'al felt embarrassed. It was as delightful as talking about the person he loved. But hearing that Ŭnsun was ill, his heart sank and his strength drained away. And with an anxious heart,
—What illness?
—I don't know yet.<168>
—To which hospital?
—It's decided she'll be in room number 2 of the inpatient ward at XXXX Hospital.
Chŏngp'al was extremely grateful that Yongch'ŏl had even told him the inpatient room number.
—Hey, do you know why your face has gotten so bad lately?
—Why? he asked, anticipating some marvelous words.
—Thinking of Ŭnsun... Ah, but really, you're a man without courage. If you sent a letter to Ŭnsun, whether you were rejected or not, you should have seen it through to the end. Why haven't you even come to Sunbok's house?
—There's no way I'd just quit.
Saying this, Chŏngp'al looked blankly at Yongch'ŏl. Yongch'ŏl also looked up at Chŏngp'al. And laughed. At this moment, Chŏngp'al felt as if he was being ridiculed. The words, "How shameless, trying to initiate love recklessly with a face like that—" came to mind within that laughter. Yongch'ŏl seemed to have urgent business— Well then, let's meet again.
And he parted ways with Chŏngp'al.

In front of room number 2 in the inpatient ward of XXXX Hospital, Chŏngp'al hesitated. But he couldn't dare to open the door. Fortunately, there was no one on the long corridor. Yet, if he just stayed outside the door like this, not only would no one ask him to come in, but he might even face embarrassment if things went wrong—this most urgent thought arose. So, just as he was about to leave, having no other choice, suddenly from the east side, a nurse holding a bowl of milk came towards where Chŏngp'al was standing. At this moment, Chŏngp'al, perhaps having thought of something, re-tied his coat strings and prepared to strike up a conversation with the nurse. The nurse, having come halfway down the path, saw someone and went through a door that opened to the west side midway. After a little while, she chattered noisily along with the laughter of some man.<169> At this time, Chŏngp'al, having perhaps thought of something bold and brave, headed towards where the nurse was talking with some man.
—Excuse me.
Chŏngp'al called the nurse.
—Yes? she replied, turning around as if embarrassed.
—I am a visitor for room two, the patient is urging me to bring the milk quickly, please give it here.
—Yes. she said, giving the milk bowl to Chŏngp'al. The nurse, who had expected a reprimand, was perhaps grateful for the unexpected offer to take it herself, and quickly handed it over. Furthermore, though it was unknown who the man was, it seemed very difficult for them to part, and it also seemed like their meeting like this was very secret. Chŏngp'al, holding the milk, was delighted. The thought, "Now I can finally see Ŭnsun!" overflowed uncontrollably. Without thinking about consequences, he opened the door to room two. But was it disappointment or success? Ŭnsun was asleep. Her eyes looked tired, her lovely lips closed together. Chŏngp'al stood blankly for a long while, looking at Ŭnsun's lovely sleeping face. The more he looked, the more affection grew for Ŭnsun's face; the more he looked, the more heart-wrenching was the faint contour of Ŭnsun's bosom. But fearing someone might enter, he had no choice but to gently place the milk on the floor outside and leave. If Ŭnsun hadn't been asleep, what kind of scorn might Chŏngp'al have received!
Returning home, Chŏngp'al was somehow joyful. An unspeakable joy filled his chest.
Three days passed while arranging for money. Chŏngp'al bought ten of the best and tastiest apples, put them in a basket, and went again to find the hospital room.
—Excuse me, may I ask you something?
Chŏngp'al asked like this as a nurse passed by.
—Yes?<170>
She turned around.
—I know it's troublesome, but could you please take this to the patient in room number two?
he asked, about to hand over the basket.
—What is the name?
—It's Yi Ŭnsun.
—Miss Yi Ŭnsun? She was discharged.
—When?
Chŏngp'al's voice trembled with disappointment.
—Yesterday.
Having no other choice, Chŏngp'al came out of the hospital gate. —Ah, how can I ever meet her now!
From this day on, Chŏngp'al's mind went awry. Though not mad, anyone who saw Chŏngp'al couldn't help but view him with suspicion.
After that, Chŏngp'al did not go outside. He didn't try to clean his room, nor did he try to fold his bedding. Heavy meditation in the dark room, glorious melancholy in the somber room—this alone was Chŏngp'al's true life.

Chŏngp'al took out the withering apples one by one and lined them up behind his pillow, which he had re-laid. Then Chŏngp'al dressed himself properly and sat in the middle of the mattress. And he looked again at the dagger held in his hand.

Ŭnsun had gone. She had gone somewhere Chŏngp'al could never see her again. Whether she had gone eternally was unknown, but even the figure that appeared in dreams had vanished. Had she, after leaving the hospital, gone to the land of death from which she could never return, or had she gone into the arms of some man in a splendid room? She never appeared to Chŏngp'al again.<171>
Nevertheless, the Ŭnsun in Chŏngp'al's heart was a pure Ŭnsun, an eternally smiling Ŭnsun. Even if Ŭnsun were to fall into depravity or become unhappy, the Ŭnsun in Chŏngp'al's heart was a noble Ŭnsun, a pure Ŭnsun, an Ŭnsun that could not be bought with money and gold. Even if Ŭnsun were to grow old, in Chŏngp'al's heart, she would be eternally flower-like. Even if Ŭnsun's blood were impure, the Ŭnsun in Chŏngp'al's heart would be chaste and pure. Whenever Chŏngp'al's boiling blood surged from his heart, Ŭnsun smiled within it.
Whatever storms Ŭnsun might encounter, there was still a resting place (安터) of peace for her in the paradise within Chŏngp'al's heart.

Chŏngp'al lay down on the mattress. And closed his eyes. Then he deeply raised his hand and fumbled for Ŭnsun's photograph inside his chest. At that moment, he remembered this song again.

「It has gone, spring has gone over the mountain.
Though youth was not unaware it would depart,
Spring has gone, but if flowers bloom alone and it be called spring, though you may be dead, let there be only love, I shall call it youth...」

This song was one sung long ago by someone cutting grass on a mountain.

Chŏngp'al lies still, motionless. Outside, only darkness deepens its cover. The early stars begin to twinkle, one by one.

**Summary**

This short story, "Elegy of Love," portrays the intense, obsessive love of a young man named Chŏngp'al for a woman named Ŭnsun. Chŏngp'al, self-conscious about his perceived ugliness, lives in a dilapidated room and finds solace only in his dreams, where Ŭnsun appears and reciprocates his affection. His reality is bleak, marked by rejection (a harsh letter from Ŭnsun) and near-madness, but his dream world provides a vital escape. He treasures meager mementos—the letter, a stolen photograph, and apples he failed to deliver—as embodiments of his idealized love. When his dreams of Ŭnsun cease after she falls ill and disappears from the hospital, he interprets this as her death or eternal departure, contemplates suicide with a dagger, and finally resigns himself to preserving her idealized image within his heart, finding a kind of tragic peace in his internal world.

From a historical perspective, the story offers insight into the romantic sensibilities and psychological landscape potentially shaped by the early colonial period (1924). Chŏngp'al's extreme inwardness, his retreat into dreams as a superior reality, and his obsession fueled by perceived social inadequacy (his appearance) could reflect broader feelings of alienation, powerlessness, or a desire for escape from a harsh external world. The intense, almost morbid romanticism and the blending of love, dream, and death might be seen as characteristic expressions of the era's literary trends, perhaps influenced by Wertherian sentiments or local traditions, providing historians with a glimpse into personal despair and coping mechanisms during that time.

일즉이 한 두어달 전에 正八이가 엇던 동무의 집에 놀너 갓다가 우연이 한 녀학생을 보앗다. 그 녀자는 正八의 동무 순복이의 족하되는 계집애엿다. 한 번 갓을 때에 한 번 보고 두 번 갓슬 때에 두 번, 세번채에 세 번, 이러케 하다가 正八이는 참을 수 업는 마음의 충격을 밧엇다. 고요하려 하나 억제하고 나오는 부르지짐과 잠잠하려 하나 참을 수 업는 피의 다름박질의 험악한 굽소리를 참을 수 업섯다. 그 후부터는 正八이는 순복이를 보려 가면 꼭 안쪽을 향하고 귀를 기우리엿다. 하로는

지금 正八이가 다시 읽은 편지가 즉 그것이다. 그러나 正八이는 실망하는 대신에 오히려 그 편지를 밧어들고 춤을 출 만치 즐겨하엿다. 물론 正八이가 은순이에게로부터 자기 얼굴에 대한 악평이 잇슬 줄은 알엇다. 그러나 편지까지 답장을 해줄 줄은 천만 뜻밧기엿다. 더욱이

그러나 그 후로는 한 오륙일 순복이를 만나지 안이하엿다.
正八이는 이 편지를 다시 접엇다. 그리고 또 입을 맛첫다. 그리고는 자기 따듯한 품에다 품엇다.

四
『은순이의 사진』
은순이에게 正八이가 편지를 밧든 후 한 쥬일이나 되엿든 어느 날 달 밝은 밤에 문득 正八이는 순복이 책상 설합 속에서 은순이의 사진을 보앗든 생각이 번적 낫다.
-오냐, 참을 수 업는 밤에 신기로운 發明이다-하고 엇더케 하면DS 그 사진을 집어 올 것을 생각해서 보앗다. 그러나 방책이 업섯다. 그리다가 正八이는 한 진리를 생각해 보앗다.
-가장 큰 것을 성공하는 데는 가장 큰 담녁과 가장 큰 희생이 잇다-는 것을 깨다럿다. 正八이의 사랑이 全 生命이라면 그의 목숨을 밧치는 담녁이 잇고 열정이 잇서야겟다는 것을 생각하엿다.
正八이는 무엇을 결심한 듯이 머리를 끄덕이며 입 속으로 「응-」하는 결심의 소래를 질넛다. 그리고 밧그로 니이깃다. 생각에 싸엿든 正八이는 순복이의 집까지 다다럿다. 밤은 열 한 시가 지낸 지 오래엿다. 그러나 집사람 속에서 누가 나아간는지 대문은 다행이 걸니지 안엇다. 正八이는 자기의＜165＞ 두루막이를 호매하게 거드처매엿다. 그리고 위대한 보물을 훔치러 드러가는 도적 모양으로 가만히 문짝을 들어 열엇다. 그리고 날카롭게 몸을 숙-압흐로 잡어 빼엇다. 다행이 아모 소리 업시 문 안에를 드러섯다. 그리고 얼는 오른편으로 순복이의 방을 드러가는 적은 골목이 잇는 것을 잘 알엇다.
순복이 방은 아즉것 뎐등불이 켜서 잇섯다. 늘 순복이의 성질을 잘 아는 正八이는, 밤에 잘 때면 불을 끄고 자든 순복이를 생각할 때에 아즉도 순복이는 아니 자는 줄로 생각하엿다. 그러나 자세히는 모르는 까닭에 위선 순복이 방 밧게 서서 얼마동안 엿들어 보앗다. 그러나 사람의 떠드는 소리는 업섯다. 그러하야 正八이는 먼저 창문을 조곰 뚤코 드려다 보앗다. 그 때에 비로소 순복이가 자고 잇는 것을 발견하엿다.
순복이는 자고 잇고 다행이 책상은 방문 엽헤 노엿다.
正八이는 문을 가만히 열엇다. 보름 지난 지 사흘 후에 이즈러진 달의 날카로운 光線은 刺客과 가티 正八이보담 먼저 숙-드러갓다. 正八이의 마음은 서늘하엿다. 이 순간에 正八이의 마음은 모든 세상의 잡념과 권태를 떠나, 새로운 생명창조에 붉은 염통의 맛치와 넘처 흘으는 피바다에 적은 배가 正八의 떠는 혼을 다려가려고 기다리고 잇다.
正八이는 책상을 가만히 들어 보앗다. 그러나 놀라울 만치 가비야웟다. 전에 볼 때에는 무거워 보이든 그 책상이 그러케도 가비야웟다. 이 때에 그는 본능덕으로 정신이 가주고 잇는 큰 힘을 깨다럿다. 무사히 들어 내엿다. 그리고 서랍을 열어보려고 하엿다. 그러나 잠기고 말엇다. 얼마동안은 주저하고 황망하엿스나 正八이는 순복이의 책상 서랍 열쇠가 올은편 서랍에 잇는 것을 다시 생각하엿다. 그리해서 잠기지 안이한 오른편 서랍을 낫낫치 잘 조사하엿다. 그리해서 그는 열쇠를 차젓다. 기여히 正八이는 귀엽고 사랑스러운 은순이의 사진을 끄내여 들엇다. 은순이의 사진은 얼는 자긔 품속에 느엇다. 그리고 책상은 전과 가티 들어서 전에 잇든 자리에 무사히 노앗다. 그리고 방문을 막 닷들여고 할 때이에 正八이는 대문 소리에 삼척 소스라지게 놀낫다. 이 깁흔 밤에 누가 나아가는지 누가 들어오는지는 몰으겟으나 엇지 하엿든 正八이가 장차 나아갈 행복의 문은 잠거지고 말엇다.＜166＞
잠간 동안 正八이는 엇지할 줄 모르고 두러웟다. 자기가 지은 죄는 가장 순결하고, 깨끗하고 신성하엿지만 그러한 생각을 할 새 업시 자기가 가진 물건이 잠자는 巨人을 안는 것과 가티 두려워섯다. 할 수 업시 正八은 뒤깐 우흐로 터진 얏트막한 담을 발견하엿다. 그리해서 正八이는 담을 넘어 나와 큰길에까지 일으럿다. 그는 가장 두러운 공포의 지옥에서 화려한 낙원을 창조하엿다. 그가 사진을 푸른 달빗 아래서 볼 때 얼마나 마음이 뛰며, 그가 웃는 은순의 얼굴을 볼 때 얼마나 견댈 수 업는 즐거움을 맛보앗으랴! 正八이가 은순이를 마음대로 안고서 우스며 입마촌다 할지라도 지금과 가티 침울하고 遠大한 즐거움은 업섯슬 것이다. 안개를 통해서 보는 강물과 가티 흐미하나 희망이 만헛섯다.

正八이의 손에는 지금 이러한 력사를 가진 사진을 들고서 물그럼이 바라다보고 잇다. 正八이는 마음에 넘치는 깁붐을 이 사진으로부터 맛보게 되엿으나 벌서 잇틀동안이나 꿈에 보이지 안는 은순이에게 대해서 슬푼 눈물을 안이흘릴 수 업섯다. 正八의 존재가 은순이의 사진이 잇슨 後라야 가치가 잇는 것과 가티, 은순이의 사진은 꿈에 오는 은순이가 잇서야 더 가치가 잇는 것이엿다.
그는 피빗 눈으로 다시 한 번 웃고 잇는 은순이를 보앗다. 그리고는 고이고이 접어서 또한 품속에 넛다. 그리고는 아즉도 조흔 냄사를 뿜는 빠스켓트 속에 사과를 보앗다. 비록 밧삭 마르기는 하엿을지라도, 오히려 아름다운 냄새를 가주고 잇다. 밧삭 마른 正八이의 손으로 그 빠스켓트를 들어서 자기 압헤 노앗다. 그리고 다시 옛일을 생각한다.
