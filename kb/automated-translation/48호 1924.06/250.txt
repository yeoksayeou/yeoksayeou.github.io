Kyŏngsŏng's Characters of a Hundred Kinds
						Physiognomist

As Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) has a large population, its appearances are also diverse and colorful. It's difficult to mention every single one in a short time, but let me speak roughly about what I know. First, speaking of the medical field, the abundance of "pockmarked" individuals was a distinctive feature. Messrs. An Sangho, Chi Sŏngyun, Kang Wŏnyŏng, Pak Kye'yang, and Hong Sŏkhyo of Severance Hospital are truly pockmarked among the pockmarked. Mr. Wŏn Tŏksang is also quasi-pockmarked, and although he's gone to North Kando (Jiandao) these days, Mr. Kim Ik-nam would likely be sad if he were left out; Mr. Yu Pyŏngp'il is also noteworthy. Truly, it seems one isn't qualified to be a doctor in Kyŏngsŏng unless they are pockmarked. Then what about the new doctors Kim Yong*, Yu Hongjong, and Yi Yunjae? Ah, instead of that, Mr. Kim is tall and hears the nickname "telephone pole," Mr. Yu is short and plump, earning the nickname "rattler" (a small, active dog), and Mr. Yi, with his stout body, wears dark glasses day and night, said to resemble an owl. Right, I forgot again. Though news of him has been scarce lately, Mr. Pak Chonghwan's dwarf-like height is also quite a spectacle. Let's leave the medical field and look at the educational field. The long face of Mr. Im Kyŏngjae, principal of Whimoon High School, and his height, like a straw stalk standing tall after all the grains have fallen off during the June-July rainy season, are worth seeing. Comparing a school teacher to a *kisaeng* (courtesan) might be impolite, but since both 'Saeng' (先生 teacher) and 'Saeng' (妓生 kisaeng) share the character 'Saeng' (生 life/person), it seems irrelevant to compare them. Therefore, if he were to gather in one place with *kisaeng* like Pae Chugyŏp, Kim Kyewŏl, Kim Ch'wihong (who became the mistress of the Changnyŏng millionaire Ha Chaegu), and Yun Ogyŏp (who is said to be bustling about trying to debut as a *kisaeng* again these days) and perform a dance, their sleeves would likely brush against the pine branches of Namsan and Pukak mountains, and their shadows would stretch as far as the Han River Railway Bridge. This sounds a bit like boasting. And the appearance of Mr. Hŏ Hŏn, principal of Posŏng College, is quite fine. However, I wish he would act naturally without trying to put on affected airs and graces. When he conducts some school ceremony, his slight smile as he says in awkward Japanese, "Tadaima shiki o hajimarimasu" (The ceremony will now begin), sounds just like the voice of a cross-dressing woman in an amateur play. It is very ill-suited to his physique. Also, eating with his left hand was a characteristic. Since the topic came up, let me mention it. The attempt by Mr. Ko Wŏnhun, the new councilor of the Central Council of the current Government-General of Chōsen and former principal, to be *haikara* (high-collar, fashionable) was also a characteristic. Seeing him walking back and forth in Ch'ŏngjin-dong, wearing silk clothes and clogs with upturned toes (*twibalmak*), his hair parted neatly left and right like his Kaiser mustache, and wearing glittering gold rings on his fingers, no one <110> could see him as a simple, honest educator. However, no matter how hard he tries to be *haikara*, what can be done about the clear trace of the head-band (*mang'gŏn*) on his head, looking like the ruins of a fortress wall? These days, he has shaved off his "boastful" mustache and become purely Tokyo-style. Also, the tilted head and feminine way of speaking of Mr. Chŏng Taehyŏn, principal of Posŏng High School, are endearing, and Mr. Cho Namhŭi, the school manager who hears the nickname "Daruma," is also a famous stout man in Kyŏngsŏng. When Hong T'aeyun was alive, he was merely a disciple, but now he can be called unrivaled. It's said that when he went on a school trip to Nonsan and saw the Ŭnjin Mireuk (Maitreya statue), he thought of himself and chuckled alone. And the scholarly, old-fashioned yellowish face of the research-minded Mr. Hyŏn Sang'yun, principal of Chungang School, the smooth talk of Mr. Ch'oe Kyudong, principal of Chungdong School, and the well-dressed Western suit style of Mr. Ŏm Chunik, principal of Yangjŏng High School, are also characteristics. The lack of a lower jaw and protruding nose of Mr. Kim Yebin, principal of Kyŏngsin School, is also a peculiar sight. The stooped posture of Mr. Chŏng Tojun, principal of Posŏng Elementary School, relative to his age, is a physical shortcoming. Mr. Cho Tongsik, principal of Tongdŏk Girls' School, seems to have aged before growing large, his face looks haggard, and his eyes always seem listless. However, his voice is relatively loud. Speaking of schools brings another thought to mind. Mr. Pak Chunghwa, who earned the nickname "Japanese policeman" during his time as principal of Posŏng Middle School—what he is doing now, news is hard to hear. And Mr. Yun Ik-sŏn, the former principal of Posŏng College, known for his stubbornness like pig iron, quit his position as director of Baron Song Pyŏngjun's Tenant Farmer Mutual Aid Association and, like a bird flying high and far, went to North Kando to become the principal of Tonghŭng Middle School. Next, let's look at figures in the press. Of Mr. Yi Sŭnghun, the new president of the Tong-A Ilbo, there is nothing heard or seen yet. But the former president, Mr. Song Chin'u, with his pale face and lack of even a single strand of beard, resembles a palace chamberlain of old. Mr. Yi Sanghyŏp's face turning red at the slightest thing seems overly nervous. Mr. Namgung Hun, the acting president of the Chōsen Ilbo (acting for Song Pyŏngjun), is said to be quite the nag. Although he is an old man, he is very diligent, bustling in and out of the editorial room and the printing plant from around 8 AM until evening with his stiff white beard and sharp, blinking bean-like eyes, uttering useless nagging remarks, which gives all the employees and workers a headache. That's why they reportedly nicknamed him the "old cat." And recently, he has become a promoter of the Tongminhoe (Association for Fellow countrymen)? It's not strange for a cat to like raw fish (회), but if he eats too much, there's a risk of getting a bone stuck. Mr. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, president of the Sidae Ilbo, has recently changed his ways, tossing aside his characteristic straw sandals (*mit'uri*) and often wearing leather shoes or rubber shoes, and also discarding his cow-dung-shaped hat for a fedora. Since he's a newspaper president, his head and feet will naturally be a bit different from his student days. No matter how docile he pretends to be, it's Mr. Hong Mokch'un of the Maeil Shinbo whose eyes <111> look mischievous. The handsome men are said to be Mr. Han Ki'ak of the Tong-A Ilbo, Mr. Cho Kanghŭi, a former reporter for the Maeil Shinbo, and Mr. Hyŏn Chin'gŏn of the Sidae Ilbo. However, Mr. Cho's face is a bit long, resembling an Edo-style beauty, and Mr. Hyŏn's eyes are somewhat sharp, resembling a femme fatale from motion pictures. I don't know how much material for novels he's gathering now that he's gone to the Sidae Ilbo. During his time at the Chōsen Ilbo, after his first debut, he had one affair with a certain *kisaeng* in Tadong, got drunk at someone's banquet at the Myŏngwŏlgwan branch despite not being able to hold his liquor, and then, like chewing on a horse bone, wrote 2 or 3 short stories using the same material, calling himself a fallen man, lamenting the society that encourages drinking, writing about the dawning mist, etc. For a pure handsome man with little experience of society, perhaps this is not strange. Also, Mr. Na Pin (pen name Tohyang) of that company, with his pinched waist and face like a *mŏktong* melon, was also a peculiar sight. Years ago, on his way to become a teacher at some private school in Andong, he stopped by Taegu. A local friend guided him to a certain *kisaeng* house. The head *kisaeng*, hearing he was a visitor from the capital (Kyŏngsŏng), expected some *haikara* gentleman. But when she actually met him, Mr. Tohyang's face didn't particularly appeal to her eyes. She whispered to a fellow *kisaeng*, "Why would someone like that come all the way to Taegu to visit a *kisaeng*?" But after hearing him perform his unique specialty, the *Sach'esugok* song, and learning he was the author of such-and-such novels, the *kisaeng*'s gloomy face gradually turned peach-colored. This is also a funny story. At Kaebyŏk Publishing Company, Mr. Kim Kijin's frequent nose-picking, Mr. Pang Sop'a's plumpness, and Mr. Yi Tonhwa's way of tying his Western-style necktie askew (*sŭt'urŭge*) are also worth seeing. Also, Mr. Yi Chongnin, president of the Ch'ŏndogyo Monthly Report, meddling here and there is a characteristic. I wish he would be a bit more prudent. Mr. Paek Taejin, who was the chief editor of *Sinch'ŏnji*, reportedly went to Inch'ŏn to manage a magazine called *Sin Kajŏng* (New Family) or something similar with Mr. Pak Ch'anghan, but that didn't work out well either, and he is said to have returned to Kyŏngsŏng. Although his surname is Paek (白, white), his face is covered in freckles (*chugŭnkkae*), so black that when you meet him, if his eyes aren't gleaming, it's hard to tell his hair from his face. And Mr. Sŏnu Il, who looked like a Japanese person, went off to the *Pukman Sinbo* (North Manchuria Report) or somewhere, and there's been no news since. Mr. Yi Kwangsu, whose entry into and exit from the Tong-A Ilbo were both less than stellar, with his blue eyes and purple face (*pyŏgan chaan* - likely figurative for complexion), and his gait like a beanpole (*anjongnaengi kŏrŭm*), waddling along, is easy for anyone to recognize. Since I've talked a lot about skinny people in the press, let's now look at some well-fed, greasy figures in the business world. Mr. Min Taesik, president of Hanil Bank, suffers from severe bad breath (*akch'wi*), so much that even his mistress sometimes frowns, and in the *kisaeng* world, he has earned the nickname "Stinky" (*kusai*) Min. It's said that no matter how much money he offers, everyone dislikes getting close <112> to him. His younger brother, Mr. Min Kyusik, looks somewhat like a pockmarked fool (*ŏlbyŏng'alli*), but thanks to having lots of money and having been to America, he enjoys great fame in the world of flowers and willows (i.e., entertainment districts) as "Imported" (*Pangnae*) Min. Mr. Han Sangnyong, executive director of Hansŏng Bank, is plump and dark-complexioned, and likely quite pockmarked too. Due to his relationship as the nephew of Baron Yi Wanyong, he wields autocratic power in the aristocrats' bank, has achieved a considerable position and gained substantial credibility, thus acting like a dragon (龍, *yong*), as his name (相龍, Sangnyong) suggests, in the banking world. If he lived in poverty with that face, he might have become Han Sangdon (韓相豚, Han Sang-pig). Mr. Ko Kyeha, president of Haedong Bank, already earned the nickname "pig" in Ch'ŏrwŏn, so there's no need to say more. Mr. Chang Tuhyŏn, director of Sangŏp Bank, is quite talkative and gossipy (*kwaesa*). He's a good-natured person, round like his head shape. However, as the proverb goes, "If a person is too nice, they'll have twelve fathers-in-law in the village." Persuaded by others, he recently became a promoter of the Tongminhoe without really knowing what it was. While talking about bankers, let me say a bit about a few wealthy individuals. Though it was in the past, Mr. Han Kyusŏl, perhaps admiring the actions of Emperor Shun too much, just as Mencius always cited Yao and Shun, took sisters from the Yŏngbyŏn house as concubines. Mr. Yi Chongsŏk makes it a daily routine to have his toes sucked by his mistress. According to Kimhong So-and-so, who was his mistress, even if his money were piled up like mountains, the disgusting task of sucking his smelly toes made life unbearable. What kind of perversion (*oipchangi*) is this? Also, Mr. Yi Yongmun is said to change mistresses every three months, totaling several dozen so far. And after the death of the Yi Kŭnsang brothers, who competed in taking concubines, the Chang T'aeksang brothers are said to have succeeded them. Let's stop talking about such sordid individuals and look at the lawyers. Mr. Ch'oe Chin's premature white hair (*sonyon paekpal*) and Mr. Pak Sŭngbin wearing only his underclothes at home with just an outer robe (*chuŭi*) are topics of conversation. Mr. Yu Munhwan reportedly got punched by Pak Ch'un'gŭm over the issue of advancing or retreating from the so-called Alliance of Like-minded Individuals from Various Factions (*Kakp'a Yuji Yŏnmaenghoe*), and his once prominent nose became completely flattened. Mr. Sin U'yŏng's frequent farting (he earned the nickname "Farting Chambong" - *panggu ch'ambong* - since his military academy days) is a source of ridicule among friends. Mr. Kim Pyŏngno sweating profusely and drooling foam while pleading a case shows his passionate dedication. Mr. Hwang Ch'ŏlsu, with his Chinese-style face, who boasted the grand signboard of "Top Graduate of Posŏng College, Top Scorer in the Bar Exam, Former Judge, Current Lawyer," has been unheard of lately. Mr. Chang To, with his booming voice, and Mr. Yi Chongha, tall and lanky in his baggy trousers (*ŏn'gupaji*), are also not often seen. I'll postpone mentioning the newly emerged lawyers to the next opportunity and speak generally as things come to mind. Mr. Yu Sŏngjun, less than 4 *ch'ŏk* (feet) tall, carrying a walking stick (*haengjang*) almost 1 *ch'ŏk* long, looks exceedingly strange. Mr. Min Kyŏng's hair parted North-South is even more remarkable. Mr. Yŏ Unhong, despite studying in America, seems to have assimilated with the Chinese after staying long in Shanghai, as his teeth look truly unbrushed. When I saw him once, they had accumulated plenty of tartar and green onion bits. To exaggerate, <113> one could say the food scraps from the welcome party upon his return to Korea are still preserved there. However, as they say, "Affection ignores flaws," he is reportedly having a love affair with some *haikara* woman in Sadong. Also, Mr. Ch'oe Rin covering his mouth with his hand when chatting is a characteristic. Mr. O Sech'ang's silence is truly difficult. In this era without freedom of speech, perhaps that is a way of navigating the world. Mr. Kwŏn Tongjin's posture is also truly fine, Mr. Kim Sŏngsu's physique is just like a Tokyo native's, and Mr. Sin Hŭng'u's reputation as a handsome man seems to diminish year by year due to age. Speaking of the women's circle, Ms. Kim Mirisa, president of the Chosŏn Women's Education Association, was originally always absent-minded (*ttanch'ŏngi*). She is said to yawn upon hearing a donkey bray and scold students for farting upon hearing the noon gun. If you think about it, she seems to want to avoid hearing all the rights and wrongs of the world, so God's hand seems to have plugged both her eardrums. Ms. Son Chŏnggyu of Kobo Girls' School has many "wisdom holes" (*sŭlgigumŏk*, i.e., resourceful) and guides the students well. When she is on the playground, surrounded by many students, she looks like a strange rock standing in a flower bed, creating a unique spectacle. Also, Ms. Sin Albert, president of the Chosŏn Women's Youth Association, has experienced many storms of society and family, so her hair has become far too white for her age. When she attends some meeting, her stout body with her white hair, like a cotton gourd (*mok'wa pakaji*), twisted askew (*sŭt'urŭge*) on her head, comes onto the stage acting childishly, making her look like an old woman in costume for an amateur play, even if she is genuinely old. Also, carrying her purse loosely (*no kajigo*) is her characteristic. Ms. Chŏng Chongmyŏng, president of the Women's Destitute Students Mutual Aid Association, forgive the impoliteness, still runs around actively like a donkey with a bell (*pang'ul tangnagwi*). She seems very busy with the Mutual Aid Association work, the recently initiated Women's Friendship Association (*Yŏsŏng Tonguhoe*) work, and also, being newly married, with household chores. Here too, a purple *chŏgori* (jacket) flashes, and there too, a purple *chŏgori* flashes. It's truly appreciated that she doesn't shy away from work despite her delicate frame. However, I wish she would take off the purple *chŏgori* and keep it, perhaps to give to her second-generation daughter when she becomes a coffee shop waitress (*tabang assi*). Ms. Pak Wŏnhŭi's principles and rhetoric are considerable, though it's unknown if her actions truly match her words. However, perhaps because she has too many complaints, her tone and complexion always seem like she is crying. Also, Ms. Kim Ch'an, a committee member of the Labor Federation (*Nodong Ch'ongdongmaeng*), has relatively great courage for her short stature (*tanku*). When she argues with someone after having a bit to drink, <114> she doesn't shy away from fire or water. Being stabbed by Pak Ch'un'gŭm's faction in Tokyo and recently suffering insults from Japanese people in Inch'ŏn can be attributed to her personality. Mr. Yi Hŏn's beard (*suyŏm*) is also quite thick for a young man. If he goes to Russia to study Labor-Farmer ideology, he wouldn't need to prepare a muffler even in winter. Finally, let me mention a few aristocrats and conclude. Baron Min Yŏnggi's Taehak Eyedrops (*Taehak Mokyak*) are well-advertised, so there's nothing more to say. Mr. Yun Tŏgyŏng's "general's head" (*changgunmŏri*, large, round head) is truly magnificent. If he stands in the yard of his house at the top of Nugak-kol, nicknamed the Afang Palace (*Abanggung*), on a moonlit night, it's hard to tell whether his shadow or the In'gyŏng Rock (*In'gyŏngbau*) on Inwangsan mountain is bigger. Also, Marquis Yi Wanyong's habit of biting his fingernails (*sont'op*) is strange. Selling out the country doesn't seem to have been that easy. It's said that during the annexation period, he bit his nails so hard while lost in thought that they bled. His son, the new Baron Hangg'u's mouth twitching (*ip silluktae*) is also worth seeing. Baron Song Pyŏngjun speaking while making fists shows the spirit of an ambitious man (*yasimga*). Mr. Min Pyŏngsŏk frowning his forehead (*ima chinggŭrinŭn*) is a flaw in his otherwise fine appearance of rosy cheeks and white hair (*hong'an paekpal*). Also, Mr. Min Yŏnghwi's smooth, affable manner (*nŭnggŭlnŭnggŭlhan kŏt*) remains unchanged even in old age.
I apologize for saying many impolite things about everyone. However, considering that even ancient physiognomy texts (*sangsŏ*) compared people to Buddhas, dog bodies, or whatnot, perhaps it's not excessively rude. Also, what else can you expect from a physiognomy reading (*kwansang*) done without receiving money? Please forgive me. <115>

**Summary**

This 1924 article from *Kaebyŏk* magazine, penned by an anonymous "Physiognomist," offers a satirical and often critical look at prominent figures in colonial Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul). Spanning various sectors like medicine, education, journalism, business, law, women's activism, and the aristocracy, the author focuses on physical appearances, mannerisms, perceived flaws, and gossip surrounding these individuals. The tone is highly personal, judgmental, and uses vivid, sometimes mocking, descriptions, comparing people to animals, objects, or highlighting perceived hypocrisies and eccentricities.

For historians, this piece provides a fascinating, albeit biased, snapshot of the social elite and public figures in 1920s colonial Korea. It reveals contemporary perceptions, social commentary (often critical of affectation, perceived moral failings, or pro-Japanese collaboration), prevalent nicknames, and the kinds of gossip circulating at the time. The candid, sometimes harsh, judgments on appearance and behavior, including critiques of figures associated with the colonial regime (like Ko Wŏnhun or Yi Wanyong) and observations on changing styles (*haikara*), offer insights into social dynamics, cultural shifts, and underlying tensions within the colonial capital's society. The inclusion of women activists and their portrayal also adds a dimension to understanding the women's movement of the era.

京城의 人物百態
						觀相者
京城은 인구가 만흔이만콤 그 모양도 形形色色이다. 짜른 시간에 일일히 말하기는 어럽지만은 안는대로 대강 말해보자. 첫재에 醫師界로 말하면 「곰보」 만흔 것이 특색이엿다. 安商浩, 池成允, 姜元永, 朴啓陽, 세부란스병원의 洪錫孝 諸氏는 곰보 중에도 찰곰보오. 元悳常 씨도 準곰보는 되고 요새는 北間島에 가서 잇지만은 金益南씨도 한목 빠지면 스려할 것시요 劉秉泌씨도 行世할만하다.
참 京城의 의사는 곰보가 안이면 자격이 업다고 할 만하다. 그러면 新醫師 金容*, 劉洪鍾, 李潤載 諸氏는 엇지하나, 압다 그 대신에 金君은 키가 커서 電線柱의 칭호를 듯고 劉君은 키가 짤고도 통통함으로 발발이 別名을 듯고 李君은 뚱뚱한 몸에 黑眼鏡만 밤낫 써서 올뱀이 갓다 한다. 올치 또 이젓다. 近日에 소식이 渺然하지만 朴宗桓씨의 난장이 키도 한 奇觀이다. 醫師界는 고만 두고 敎育界로 보자. 徽文高普校長 任璟宰씨의 긴 얼골과 6,7월 장마에 알강이 다 떠러지고 웃둑하게 선 밀집대 가튼 키도 볼만하다. 학교 선생님을 妓生에 比하는 것은 미안하지만은 先生이나 妓生이나 生은 一般인즉 比해 말하야도 무관할 듯하다. 그로 만일 기생의 裵竹葉, 金桂月, 昌寧 富豪 河在鳩의 愛妾된 金翠紅, 요새이 또 기생 나오랴고 들먹들먹한다는 尹玉葉 등과 一席에 會合하야 춤을 한번 춘다 하면 그들의 兩袖은 南山, 北岳의 소나무 가지를 툭툭 치고 그림자는 漢江鐵橋에까지 비칠 것 갓다. 이것은 좀 풍장이말 갓다. 그리고 普專校長 許憲씨의 허울은 꽤 좃타. 그러나 그로 하야금 作態의 愛嬌를 부리지 말고 천연하게 행동을 하얏스면 조켓다. 그가 학교의 무슨 禮式을 할 때에 유창치 못한 日本말로 態를 지여 「다다이마식기오하지마리마스」하고 살작 웃는 것은 맛치 素人劇 할 때에 變裝 여자의 목소리 갓다. 그것은 그의 체격과 넘우 不合하는 일이다. 또 식사를 왼손으로 하는 것도 특징이엿다. 말이 낫스니까 말이다. 現 朝鮮總督府 中樞院 新叅議 前 校長 高元勳 씨의 하리카라 하랴고 하는 것도 특성이엿다. 그가 비단옷에 뒤발 막을 딱 신고. 카이젤 수염과 同方向으로 頭髮를 할튼 것티 左右에 짝 갈너부치고 손에 반짝반짝하는 금반지를 끼고 淸進洞으로 왓닥갓닥하는 것을 보면 아모라도＜110＞ 純實한 교육자로는 볼 수 업다. 그러나 아모리 하이카라를 하랴 하야도 머리에 망건자국이 흔 城터 모양으로 잔득 난 데야 엇지 하리오. 요새이는 그의 「이바루」하던 수염을 깍고 純東京이가 되얏다. 또 普成高普 校長 鄭大鉉씨의 머리 빼드름하고 여성적으로 말하는 것도 귀염성스럽거니와 다루마의 別名을 듯는 幹事 趙男熙씨도 뚱뚱하기로는 京城에 名物男이다. 그가 洪泰潤 生前에는 一個 徒弟에 불과하얏지만 지금에는 可謂 獨步다. 論山 修學旅行을 갓슬 때에 恩津彌勒을 보고는 그도 자기 생각이 낫던지 혼자 픽 우섯단다. 그러고 硏究性 만흔 中央學校長 玄相允씨의 舊式 學究장이 가튼 노랑 얼골과 中東學校長 崔奎東씨의 살살거리는 것과 養正高普 校長 嚴柱益씨의 양복자세 조흔 것도 특색이어니와 儆新學校長 君芮彬씨의 안레턱 업고 코만 삐죽한 것도 一奇觀이오. 普成初等校長 鄭道俊씨의 년령에 비하야 허리 굽은 것도 체격상 欠節이다. 同德女學校長 趙東植씨는 크기도 젼에 늙기부터 하야 얼골이 憔悴해 뵈고 눈이 항상 땍국하다. 그러나 목소리는 비교적 크다. 학교 이약이를 하니 또 한가지 생각이 난다. 前日 普成中學校長 시대에 日巡査의 별명을 듯던 朴重華씨는 무엇을 하는지 소식도 잘 드를 수 업고 쇠고집의 칭호가 잇던 舊 普成專門學校長 尹益善씨는 宋秉畯伯의 小作人相助會 理事도 구만 두고 高飛遠鳥의 격으로 北 間島에 가서 東興中學校長이 되얏단다. 其次에 언론계 인물을 보자. 東亞의 新任社長 李昇薰씨는 아즉 無所聞 無所觀이어니와 前 社長 宋鎭禹씨는 허여멀숙한 얼골에 수염도 한아 업는 것은 맛치 前日 宮中常侍郞과 갓고 李相協 君의 조곰하면 顔色이 赤變하는 것은 너무도 신경질인 것 가트며 朝鮮日報 代理社長(宋秉畯 代理) 南宮薰씨의 잔소리도 어지간하단다. 그가 老人이지만 퍽 부지런하야 오전 8시경부터 저녁때까지 빳빳한 힌수염과 핼긋 핼긋하는 메물눈이 모진 눈으로 편집실과 공장으로 들낙날낙하면서 無用의 잔소리를 하는 것은 一般社員이나 職工이 다 골머리를 흔든단다. 그래서 그를 늙은 고양이라고 별명까지 지엿다 한다. 그런데 그는 근래에 또 同民會 發起人이 되얏다나. 고양이가 회를 조와하기도 無怪하지만은 넘우 먹으면 뼈가 걸릴 염려도 업지 안다. 時代日報 社長 崔南善씨는 근래에 變節을 하야 그의 특색인
