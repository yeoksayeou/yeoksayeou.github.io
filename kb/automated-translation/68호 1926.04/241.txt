Company Announcement

⊙ Hamnam Branch Expansion
Previously, the Hamnam Branch was under the management of Mr. Yi Ŭng-bong, but due to circumstances, he has been dismissed. Now, under the management of Mr. Pak Hwa-sŏp, we have opened with a new organization as described below and are further expanding various operations concerning reader recruitment, article handling, and advertisement processing. We ask our esteemed readers for double the support as before.

**Summary**

This company announcement from the magazine *Kaebyŏk* details the expansion and restructuring of its branch network in April 1926. It reports the change in management for the Hamnam branch, the establishment of new branches in Yŏngdŏk and Pyŏngyŏng (Ulsan), and new sub-branches in Chasŏng and Changsŏng. It also announces staff additions to the Hoengsŏng sub-branch, listing the names and positions of the personnel involved in each location, including branch managers, reporters, general affairs staff, advisors, and field staff. Historians might find this interesting as it documents the growing organizational structure and reach of a significant Korean cultural/intellectual magazine during the colonial period, showcasing its efforts to establish a nationwide presence. The list of specific individuals involved in local branches provides valuable data for social network analysis and understanding regional intellectual activities. The use of Japanese administrative district names like "Taehwa-chō" in Hamhŭng reflects the colonial reality.
