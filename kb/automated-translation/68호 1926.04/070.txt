中國은 惡乎定?
						一記者

China, which has no peaceful days both internally and externally, sees major and difficult problems overlapping as time goes by, truly causing observers to lament, "China, where will it settle?" Not to mention the continuous internal strife like the Fèngtian-Zhílì War (奉直戰) and the Fèngtian-Guómínjūn War (奉國戰), just last month, conflict arose with Russia (露國) over the Chinese Eastern Railway (中東鐵道) issue, drawing the world's attention with talk of going to war, and now, centering on the Taku (太沽) incident, it is staging a farce similar to the second Boxer incident with Japan (日本) and the Allied Powers including Britain (英), America (米), France (佛), and Italy (伊).

The incident is said to have occurred near T'anggū (塘沽) at the mouth of the Ch'ŏnjin (天津) River around 3 PM on March 12th. That is, the Sino-Japanese problem arose when China's Guómínjūn (國民軍) shelled a Japanese destroyer, causing four serious and minor injuries, and problems also arose with the various allied nations because the Chinese side laid naval mines and land mines in Taku harbor.

Whether the fault lies with China or Japan, the claims of both sides are inconsistent, so we cannot easily judge. That is, the Chinese side claimed, "The Japanese warship did not observe the promised port entry time. Or it sped up in a slow-speed zone. Therefore, the Guómínjūn fired blank shots as a warning. However, the Japanese military responded with live ammunition, causing injuries on the Chinese side. Therefore, the Chinese side also responded with live ammunition, leading to this unfortunate incident," insisting the fault lies with Japan. However, Japan, using the pretext of insult to the national flag, the Guómínjūn firing first, and serious and minor injuries to Japanese soldiers, immediately (at 7 PM the same day) lodged a strong protest and, separately, on the 16th, the Japanese government issued an ultimatum with four points: 1. Severe punishment of the perpetrators; 2. The Chinese government and the responsible officials shall apologize to the Japanese government; 3. Payment of 50,000 won in damages to the injured Japanese soldiers; 4. Clear guarantee that such actions will not occur again in the future. It threatened that if China did not comply, the Japanese government would take self-defense measures using its own power. Regarding this incident, the related allied nations also, on the 16th, stated, "To protect the rights under general treaties concerning international commerce and the special rights concerning navigation between the capital and the coast stipulated in the Protocol, the related nations demand as follows:" <66> issuing an ultimatum with five points (1. Cease all combat activities from Taku to Ch'ŏnjin; 2. Remove all naval mines, land mines, and other obstacles; 3. Restore all navigation markers and ensure no interference in the future; 4. All warships shall anchor outside Taku and not interfere in any way with foreign vessels; 5. Cease all inspections of foreign vessels except when conducted by customs officials). It stated that if a satisfactory guarantee was not obtained by noon on March 16th, the navies of the related nations would take necessary measures to remove and suppress all obstacles to free or safe navigation near Ch'ŏnjin (This is based on the Boxer Protocol of 1901).

Thus, Japan concentrated 15 warships in the Ch'ŏnjin area, and the related nations readied their navies, truly applying the pressure of an imminent major event.

China, with its weak government and national power, unable to withstand the strong pressure from the powers and without even the leisure to question the rights and wrongs of the incident, ended up satisfying the ultimatums of Japan and the allied nations.

Outraged by this, the left wing of the Kuomintang (國民黨) and students, etc., rose up in large numbers, attacking the government's weakness while simultaneously crying out against the tyranny of Japan and the allied powers in large-scale demonstrations. Eventually, they clashed with military police, resulting in over 40 student casualties and the arrest of many left-wing leaders, enacting a great tragedy both externally and internally throughout China. At the same time, the Jia cabinet (賈內閣), only recently formed, took responsibility for the shooting of students and resigned en masse.

Whether China complies with the demands of the powers or not, since it has agreed to comply, the problem may reach a temporary conclusion, but the anti-foreign and anti-government fervor of the 400 million Chinese people is rising day by day like a prairie fire. Alas, what will China's future truly be? <67>
<66-67>

**Summary**

The article reports on the 1926 Taku Incident, where Chinese Guominjun forces clashed with a Japanese destroyer near Taku harbor, leading to international tension. Japan and allied powers (UK, US, France, Italy), citing treaty rights and the Boxer Protocol, issued separate ultimatums demanding apologies, reparations, removal of harbor obstructions, and guarantees for future passage. Facing overwhelming pressure, the weak Chinese government capitulated. This sparked widespread domestic outrage, leading to student protests violently suppressed by authorities (resulting in casualties and arrests) and the resignation of the Jia cabinet. The author concludes by lamenting the uncertain future of China amidst internal turmoil and foreign pressure. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is revealing as it showcases Japanese military assertiveness and diplomatic strong-arming in China, mirroring tactics potentially used or feared in Korea. It also highlights the vulnerability of China to foreign powers and internal fragmentation, a regional dynamic keenly observed from colonized Korea.

기사형태	소식

中國은 惡乎定?
						一記者
內政外事 두루 寧日이 업는 中國은 갈사록 大事難事가 重疊되야 觀者로 하야금 실로 「中國惡乎定?」의 嘆을 發케 한다. 奉直戰 奉國戰 등 內亂連出은 말도 말고 月前은 中東鐵道問題로 露國과 葛藤이 나서 開戰을 하느니 엇저느니 한참 世人의 이목을 끄을더니 今에는 太沽사건을 중심하야 日本 及 英米佛伊 諸列强과 제2團匪사건과 비슷한 滑劇을 演出하고 잇다.
事端은 3월 12일 오후 3시 天津河口 塘沽부근에서 발생하얏다 한다. 즉 中國의 國民軍이 日本 驅逐艦을 砲擊하야 4명의 重輕傷을 出한데서 日中문제가 起하고 又는 太沽港에 中國측이 水雷 及 地雷를 敷設한데서 諸列國과도 문제가 起하얏다.
사건의 曲直은 中國에 잇는지 日本에 잇는지 兩便의 주장이 不一하닛가 吾人은 容易히 判知할 수 업다. 즉 中國측은 「日本군함이 약속한 入港 시간을 지키지 안엇다. 또는 徐行구역에 속력을 가하얏다. 그래서 國民軍은 空砲로 위협하얏다. 그런데 日本軍은 實彈으로 대하야 中國측에 傷害를 가하얏다. 그래서 中國측도 實彈으로 대하야 今次의 不祥事를 出케 되얏다」하야 曲在於日本을 주장하얏다. 그러나 日本은 國旗侮辱, 國民側先發砲, 日軍重輕傷 등을 구실로 하야 사건발생 즉시(동일 오후 7시)로써 嚴重抗議를 提하는 동시 日本정부로서는 16일로써 별로히 4개條 즉 1. 加害者 嚴重處罰 2. 中國政府 及 當該責任者는 日本政府에 대하야 陳謝할 것 3. 日軍人 부상자에게 5만원 손해배상 지불할 일 4. 장래에 如斯한 행동이 다시 업슬 것을 명확키 保障할 일 등으로써 最後通牒을 發하얏다. 此에 不應하면 日本政府는 自國의 실력으로 自衛手段을 取하겟다고 强迫하얏다. 그리고 此사건에 대하야 關係列國도 16일로써 國際通商에 관한 一般條約 상의 권리 及 議定書에 규정된 首都海濱간 航行에 관한 특수권리를 擁護하고자 關係各國은 此와 如히 要함」이라 하야＜66＞ 5개條(1. 太沽로부터 天津에 至하기까지 모든 전투행위를 廢할 일 2. 水雷地雷 기타 障碍物을 除去할 일 3. 모든 航路標識을 復舊하고 또 장래에 何等 妨害가 업게 할 일 4. 一切 兵船은 太沽외에 泊하고 또 외국선박에 대하야 何等 간섭을 아니 할 일 五, 稅關官憲에 의하야 하게 될 경우 외에는 외국선박에 대하야 一切 臨檢을 廢할 일)의 最後通牒을 發하얏다. 此를 3월 16일 정오까지에 만족한 保障을 得치 못할 시는 關係各國 해군은 天津 부근의 자유 또는 안전한 항해에 대 한 모든 장애를 제거하고 또는 禁壓함에 필요한 수단을 取하겟다 하얏다(此는 1901년의 義和團議定書에 基한 것이다).
이리하야 日本은 15隻의 軍艦을 天津 방면으로 집중하며 關係各國은 해군을 整頓하며 실로 大事臨迫의 위협을 가하얏다.
정부 及 국가의 力이 微弱한 中國은 事件曲直은 무를 餘暇도 업시 列强의 强壓에 不堪하야 日本 及 列國의 通牒에 滿足을 與하게 되얏다.
此에 憤慨한 國民黨 左派 及 학생 등은 大擧하야 정부의 軟弱을 攻하는 동시 日本 及 列强의 强暴을 絶呌하는 大示威運動을 起하다가 결국 軍警과 衝突되야 학생 40여명이 死傷되고 左派領首들이 多數히 체포되야 中國 上下에 외적 내적의 일대 慘劇을 演하는 동시 組閣未幾의 賈內閣은 學生射殺의 책임상 總辭職을 하고 마럿다.
中國측이 列强의 요구에 不應하면 己어니와 應한다 하닛가 문제는 잠간 一段落을 告할넌지 모르나 4萬萬 중국민의 排外 及 反政府熱은 日復日燎原의 火와 가티 니러나는 중이니 嗚呼 中國의 將來는 果如何?＜67＞
＜66-67＞
