기사형태: 잡저 (Miscellaneous essay)

Be glad. That indeed is the light of Virtue (德).
This is what Diogenes (데오케네쓰) said to console a youth who was blushing deeply (赧色) with shame. Yes, knowing shame (知耻) is Virtue (是 德).

If I were to die right now (只今), you would discover the letters 'Calais' (『키레』) engraved (深刻되얏슴) upon my (妾) heart (心臟).
These are the words spoken to her retainers (臣下) by Queen Mary (메리 女皇) of England (born 1517, died 1558), lamenting (슯히 여겨) the loss of the city of Calais (『카레』市) in France (佛蘭西) from England's hands. If one's regret (所恨) is that profound (切*하면), what could one not achieve (何를 取하야 不得하리오)?

Assassination (暗殺) has not yet ever (아즉까지 嘗히) changed the history of the world.
This was the desperate cry (絶叫한 말) of Disraeli (저스레리) in the House of Commons (下院) in May 1865, when reporting the sad news (悲報) of the assassination of President Lincoln (린컨 大統領) of the United States of North America (北米合衆國).<96>

**Summary**

This untitled piece presents three historical anecdotes from Western figures: Diogenes on shame as virtue, Queen Mary I on her deep regret over losing Calais, and Disraeli on the ineffectiveness of assassination in changing history, referencing Lincoln's death. From the perspective of Korean colonial history in 1920, shortly after the March 1st Movement, these quotes could be interpreted allegorically. Historians might find interest in how the article uses Western examples to subtly discuss themes relevant to the Korean situation: the importance of national shame or conscience (知耻), the depth of resolve needed (like Mary's regret potentially symbolizing the desire for lost sovereignty), and a possible cautionary note against violent acts like assassination as a means for political change.

기뻐할 지어다. 그것이야말로 德의 빗이다.
『데오케네쓰』가 一靑年의 甚히 부끄려하는 赧色을 보고 慰勞하여준 말이다. 그러타 知耻가 是 德이다.
余가 只今 死한다하면 汝 等은 『키레』라는 文字가 妾의 心臟에 深刻되얏슴을 發見하리라.
英國 『메리』 女皇(1517年生 同 58年 死)이 佛蘭西에 在한 『카레』市가 英國의 手로서 離奪된 것을 슯히 여겨 臣下들에게 한 말이다. 所恨이 그만큼 切*하면 何를 取하야 不得하리오.
暗殺은 아즉까지 嘗히 世界의 歷史를 變更케한 일업다. 1865年 5月 下院에서 北米合衆國 大統領 『린컨』의 暗殺된 悲報를 告할 時 『저스레리』의 絶叫한 말이다.＜96＞
