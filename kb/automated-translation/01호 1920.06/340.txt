Two Great Poisons Seen Through the Eye of Hygiene
						By Yu Hongjong, Director of Hongje Hospital

It is a fact that all myriad things and affairs in the world cannot depart from the two aspects of benefit (利) and harm (害), nor can they escape the connection to gain and loss. What reason is there for this, and what truth lies herein? Whether the judgment regarding this is good or bad has a paramount connection to human life, and one knows that within the two aspects of benefit and harm, infinite truth (眞理) wanders, and supreme teachings and edification exist. This is indeed what the Creator (造物主) intends as a test, a whip (鞭鞬), and also a forging for humanity. Of course, benefit and harm are not discussed merely materially; the spiritual aspect is also thus. Then, from what does the connection of benefit and harm arise? If one discusses this benefit and harm from the perspective of universal truth, it is not only living beings that cannot depart from the connection of benefit and harm, but non-living things are also the same. For living beings, it arises from desire (慾), and for non-living things, benefit and harm arise from change. Thus, the benefit and harm of non-living things are those given by nature, while for living beings, besides those given by nature, those they create themselves are also many. Of course, the benefit and harm of non-living things include not only the natural but also the artificial. The benefit and harm of non-living things are not consciously perceived by the non-living things themselves, but are known through the impartial indication of cosmic truth. Here, I will not discuss the benefit and harm of non-living things but intend to enter the main discussion starting from the benefit-harm relationship of living beings.

Now, the desires of living beings are manifold, namely the desire for food (食慾), sexual desire (性慾), the desire for knowledge (知慾), and so forth. The problems arising from these desires are beyond words. In this sense, human life requires self-awareness (自覺). This self-awareness awakens from the Creator's test for humanity, from the whipping (鞭撻). Self-awareness is precisely the mastery of reason that transcends benefit and harm, the judgment of justice that is liberated from the connection of gain and loss. Our reason constantly communes with cosmic truth; the path taught by reason is indeed the true path (眞路) of human life. Therefore, if we commonly call the happiness of those who walk the true path 'benefit' (利) and the punishment received by those who do not walk the true path 'harm' (害), then the aforementioned benefit and harm reside within the self (自我). The benefit and harm of the self arise from the presence or absence of self-awareness, and the benefit of the self lies in walking the true path. Is this not the time when, through this self-awareness, we should prevent harmful poisons (害毒) beforehand, completely annihilate suffering, strive to create paradise (樂園) in the world, summon courage, and call out for the right path (正路)? If peace is benefit, then war is a harmful poison. If justice is benefit, then injustice is a harmful poison. In a human world with self-awareness, harmful poisons cannot be left unchecked<108> and must inevitably be eradicated (撲滅). Ah—the method of discerning benefit and harm is too overwhelming (張皇). From this standpoint, we organize an eradication squad for harmful poisons and investigate the greatest harmful poisons that require urgent attention first. What are the greatest harmful poisons? How could my humble self (不肖) possess the ability to observe all harmful poisons? I merely intend to investigate what is seen through the eye of hygiene (衛生眼) and offer it for the reference of the esteemed readers. Among the harmful poisons seen through the eye of hygiene, what are the greatest? Some might say the harm of infectious diseases is great; others might say the harm of war is great; still others might say the harm received from irresistible natural disasters is immense. However, my humble self does not side with (左袒) nor nod in agreement (首肯) with these. Infectious diseases are due to the toxic action of microbes (徵菌). War arises from national borders... if one were to say that conflicts between masses of people are great, even greater harmful poisons have been investigated, which are somewhat urgent to mention. Namely, not other things but the self (自我), not the masses but the individual (個體), not great nature but small nature, indeed the fundamental self harming the self—there is no greater and more fundamental harmful poison than this, an absolutely greatest harmful poison. Among these harmful poisons, if one were to say the two greatest seen through the eye of hygiene are alcohol (酒) and sex (色), none would deny it.

Alas! Since human history began, how many great men, heroes, men of will, and talented scholars have been buried by these two great harmful poisons? How many countless humans have failed in their intended goals and established endeavors due to these harmful poisons, sighing in regret amidst miserable winds and sorrowful rains? And the benefit and harm affecting society, the nation, and the people due to this are also not insignificant. Is this the sin of alcohol and illicit sexual desire, or the fault of the self? Even if people did not drink alcohol,<109> it is indispensable for disinfection, medicine, and industrial use. And sexual desire, even if not illicit, is a beneficial tool (利器) given by nature for the purpose of procreation, population increase, and according to the truth of the life cycle, something that cannot be dispensed with. Yet, through these beneficial things and tools, conversely, the greatest harmful poison is received, burying precious youth. It is clear that this is the sin of human life, of the self. Of course, it is a harmful poison received due to a lack of thorough self-awareness, and at the same time, a self-inflicted harmful poison. When I, in my daily clinical examinations, feel the pulse, the stream proving human life; when I hear the truthful, falsehood-free news taught by truth within the microscope (미크로스코푸 - stethoscope); when I see at the tip of the hammer (함메루 - percussion hammer) all the reflex actions that cannot be resisted by human effort—I cannot help but be astonished at the number of patients who have become defeated soldiers due to this harmful poison. The above concerns the harmful poison to the individual. If we classify the harmful poisons, besides the harm to the individual, when viewed from the perspective of social medicine, they should be distinguished as harm to society and harm affecting the nation and people. From the perspective of individual health, it is the constant assertion of us clinicians that neurological weakness (神經衰弱), mental illness (精神病), arteriosclerosis (動脈硬化症), cerebral hemorrhage (腦出血), liver cirrhosis (肝硬化症), etc., are often caused by alcohol. The harmful poisons seen from illicit sexual desire are syphilis (梅毒), gonorrhea (淋疾), chancroid bubo (橫痃), and soft chancre balanitis (軟性下疳龜頭炎), which are the most numerous. To cure these, physicians invent famous medicines and provide special effects, but [patients] only trust them more and worsen, so even good doctors are of no use; the only thing to cure is self-awareness. What kind of harm will a society formed by the gathering of individuals possessing such harmful poisons receive? From the standpoint of social medicine, it is natural that a society organized with unhealthy members cannot avoid being unhealthy itself. The societal harm received due to this is manifold; whether viewed in terms of health rate, activity level, or economically, inferiority cannot be avoided. Observing reality, [people] are drunk daily (每日 泥醉), having no discipline regarding time for sleep, meals, and work. Alcohol has a close relationship with sexual desire. Therefore, sexual hygiene and sexual ethics have departed from their own conscious world. If one abuses illicit sexual desire due to the demon of alcohol (酒魔), one becomes a defeated soldier of venereal disease (花柳病). What strength can be seen in such undisciplined life? Scholars since ancient times have said that among the descendants of drunkards (酒夫), there are no great men, and mental illness frequently appears—this is an actual fact. How could a child with a underdeveloped brain (低腦兒) possess a clear mind? In the psychology of these people, the world of alcohol and sex is probably known as paradise and heaven. How could a brain intoxicated by this plan for the development of society, perceive the general trend, and have the perspective, purpose, and foresight to make my position secure, firm, and superior? Furthermore, speaking of syphilis, there is no place in the entire body it does not invade, and even mental illness arises due to syphilis,<110> so one can know how virulent its poison is. Against this venereal disease, Mr. Ehrlich (에루리히) and Mr. Hata (하다) were eradication squad leaders possessing medicine, but they have not yet achieved success. And Mr. Ellen Key (에렝케), advocating the principle of monogamy, is a vanguard leader possessing sexual ethics, but has not yet fully accomplished [her goal]. That is, even with the syphilis eradication agent No. 606, complete eradication is hopeless, and ethically, complete success has not yet been achieved. What will eradicate it in the future? Everyone knows this is a harmful poison, but is it not the instinct of human life—that is, the desire for life (生活慾)—to awaken and act to eradicate this harmful poison and enjoy a life of happiness? Therefore, my humble self calls this a test question. The Creator places such harmful substances before humanity and tests them. The answer to this question will be the self-awareness of truth (眞理) and the true path (眞路). In other words, the Creator's first test for humanity is the problem of benefit and harm. Do they choose harm, or do they choose benefit? People are sharp regarding the benefit and harm related to wealth, but many fail regarding this fundamental benefit and harm, this greatest benefit and harm, the benefit and harm requiring self-awareness. I conclude that among the harmful poisons seen through the eye of hygiene, the two greater than any others are alcohol and sex. How can one not lament burying a meaningful life meaninglessly due to these two great harmful poisons? The countermeasures against this are: 1. Self-awareness. If each person had thorough self-awareness, there would be no need to discuss other methods. However, at this time when that is not the case, social measures such as temperance societies (禁酒會), alcohol taxes (酒稅), proposals to abolish public prostitution (公娼廢止案), and advocating for sexual hygiene and sexual ethics show how exceedingly great the influence of this harmful poison is. I wish that, regardless of the method and means, the day these two great harmful poisons are removed from society will be the day that person and that society receive the gospel. We must certainly strive [for this]. Especially our youth, who are the flowers of human life and the life and hope of society and the nation, I earnestly hope that they, with thorough resolve against these unparalleled evils of alcohol and sex, will quickly remove them, even if just for one day.<111>

**Summary**

The author, Yu Hongjong, director of Hongje Hospital, argues from a "hygienic perspective" that the two greatest "poisons" afflicting individuals and society are alcohol (酒) and illicit sex (色). He contends that while natural desires and substances like alcohol exist, their misuse stems from a lack of individual "self-awareness" (自覺), which he describes as a failure of a moral test set by the Creator. He details the severe individual health consequences (neurological, mental, physical diseases, STDs) and broader societal harms (unhealthy population, reduced productivity, moral decay, potentially inferior offspring). While acknowledging medical treatments (like Salvarsan 606) and social reforms (temperance, abolition of prostitution, sexual ethics), he insists the ultimate solution lies in individual moral awakening and self-control, especially urging young people to overcome these vices.

From a colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects the Korean enlightenment (계몽) discourse emphasizing self-strengthening and moral reform during a period of national weakness and subjugation. Framing social problems primarily as individual moral failings (lack of "自覺") related to alcohol and sex, rather than focusing on structural issues like colonialism, could be seen as both a genuine public health concern and perhaps a politically safer avenue for advocating social improvement under Japanese rule. It highlights the intersection of Western medicine/hygiene concepts, traditional morality, and nationalist aspirations for a stronger populace.
