小說 流帆 (Novel: Flowing Sail)
						牧星 (Mok Sŏng)
Part 1
It is a warm spring Saturday afternoon.
The spring sun, growing warmer day by day, shone warmly and peacefully on this day too. The village at the foot of the mountain, like a poem with flowers about to bloom in reds and blues, lay quietly like a dreamland. Its appearance, shining in the spring sun, seemed as if playing a melody of springtime (陽春) with a delicate sound. - Everything is spring! -.
Everything is spring. The mountains are spring, the water is spring, the people are spring, and even the air is spring air. Mixed with that soft and gentle spring breeze, the most graceful and peaceful sound of singing surrounds the entire Independence Gate (獨立門) area. It was
the sound of a comforting song, sung loudly for the brethren suffering in stifling confinement, unaware even of the arrival of spring, by three young men and women standing like angels (天使) atop the end of the fortress wall (the curved fortress) on the peak of Inwangsan (仁旺山), to the left of Muakchae (母嶽재) pass.
Having finished the Song of Nostalgia for Home (望鄕歌), and now the sorrowful Prison Song (獄中歌) called "Nightmare" (惡夢), Hye-suk (惠淑) wiped her tears with a handkerchief (手巾).
As Chung-sik (重植) folded the notebook (手帖) he had been holding and put it into his Western suit (洋服) pocket, urging, "Let's just go down today..." Hye-suk, folding her handkerchief, linked arms and faced Tong-ho (東昊), who stood looking powerlessly at the red wall below the mountain, saying,
"Shall we go down?" and she herself started down ahead of Chung-sik. Tong-ho, who had stood silently with linked arms, gazing into the walled area below the mountain, lost in some thought (生覺), wordlessly turned his feet and followed behind the two.
Hye-suk appeared to be under twenty, a girl with a round, fair face and clear eyes, currently a student (學生) attending a certain High School (高等學校). Chung-sik was her older brother, a young man attending the Law Department (法科) of OO Speciality School (專門學校). And Tong-ho was Chung-sik's closest trusted friend (信友), a young man attending a certain Law School (法學校).
Descending along the fortress wall <139>, winding this way and that through pine trees grown about as tall as they were, the three were all silent.
Hye-suk, in front, used both hands to part the pine branches brushing against her face, and Chung-sik followed exactly behind her, while Tong-ho followed as if trying to step precisely in Chung-sik's footprints.
After descending silently for a while, Tong-ho, who had been wordless until then, spoke,
"That person waving the handkerchief was probably Sin U-sŏk (申禹錫)..." to which Chung-sik replied,
"How can you tell among so many people?"
"Well, there are many people, but the room (房) is that room."
"Is it exactly that room?"
"Yes, it's that room..." and after a moment (暫間),
"There are probably three or two in that room right now (只今)..."
Silence fell again. The three had already passed well beyond Sŏnbawijae pass.
Hye-suk, who had been walking silently, said,
"Oh my, people are already coming with evening food deliveries (差入)," and looking, they saw two or three women (婦人) climbing up, carrying food containers (食器), having crossed the hill path outside the fortress wall, passed under the ginkgo trees, and stepped onto the broken fortress path.
"Has it gotten that late already...?" Chung-sik said, parting the front of his Western suit and taking out his watch (時計).
"Oh my, it's already five minutes to four..." he said.
Soon, today too would end. The black smoke (烟氣) that had billowed from the prison (監獄) chimney now trickled out little by little.
The three reached the fortress path at the branching point (分歧點) between Sajikkol (社稷골) and Independence Gate (獨立門), where the fortress wall was breached. This was the place where Chung-sik and Hye-suk, heading for Haengch'on-dong (杏村洞) outside the wall, and Tong-ho, heading for Toryŏm-dong (都染洞) inside the wall, would part ways.
The three lingered, feeling as if something remained unsaid, though they had nothing specific to say, and then stood tall on the fortress stones by the path. All facing outwards from the fortress...
The setting sun (夕陽), nearing the top of Kŭmhwasan (金華山), cast the long, slanted shadows (影子) of the three onto the soft grassy field.
The spring day fades peacefully too.
From somewhere, the sound of a willow flute <140>, as if singing of the warm South, drifted slowly, fitting for a spring sound. On the sloping path of Muakchae pass, woodcutters wearing conical hats strolled leisurely past with their cows.
The three stood on the fortress wall, gazing at the spring scenery near and far.
"What's that white thing over there?" Hye-suk asked her brother, pointing towards the Namdaemun Station (南大門 停車塲) area.
"Where?" Chung-sik asked, focusing on where her finger pointed. Beyond the Namdaemun Station yard, next to the large chimney of the Tongmo (東慕) brick factory, there was some kind of building, perhaps a Western (西洋) iron (鐵) roofed house, and behind it were green trees (樹木), and behind those green trees, something white was visible.
"Isn't that some kind of flag (旗)...?"
"Why would there be such a big flag there?"
"Well, it's still a flag..."
While they debated, Tong-ho, who had been standing silently beside them, said,
"That's not a flag, it's a sail. You can't see the water there, but it must be the Han River (漢江). It's a sail on a boat. Look there. Hasn't it already gone down this far in the meantime? While we were talking, the boat has already gone that far."
Indeed, it was clearly a sail. If it weren't a sail, there would be no reason (理) for its position (位置) to change (變) without them noticing.
"And Oppa called it a flag..."
"Then why didn't you know?"
All three laughed together. But Chung-sik's laugh was a bitter one, not very cheerful. Tong-ho continued,
"The flow of time (歲月) is that swift (速). Just as that boat's position changed without us knowing, time passes without us knowing. Accordingly, the opportunity (機會) to accomplish our work (事業) also passes by unknowingly like that. Thinking about it, I feel we shouldn't be spending even this brief moment so meaninglessly (無意味). While we stand here like this, who knows what opportunities, what time is passing by unknowingly..."
"Yes - it's truly heartless (無情) how time passes," Hye-suk said, turning her head slightly and lowering it.
"Well, of course time passes quickly. But that's true for the entire (全體) world. It's not like it's uniquely fast for just one person among them. For people surviving (生存) on this world, this Earth (地球), is there any use (效用) in lamenting (慨歎) something that cannot be helped by human power (人力)? <141> If one were to lament only the swift passing of time, wouldn't they be unable to sleep or rest? If one were to run parallel (併行) with the flow of time (歲流) without resting or sleeping, in how many days would their physical body (肉身) be destroyed (破壞)?"
At Chung-sik's words, Tong-ho stood silently, not attempting to answer (對答), gazing towards Kwanaksan (冠嶽山) beyond the river, while Hye-suk, for some reason, kept her head lowered, her face flushed red. The three stood silently again. The sun touched the mountain skyline. The sky gradually turned reddish.
Evening smoke rose from the areas of Haengch'on-dong, Songwŏl-dong (松月洞), Okch'ŏn-dong (玉天洞), and Kwan-dong (館洞). Under the ginkgo trees, two old men (老人) began (始作) gathering the oiled paper (油紙) that had been spread out on the grass all day long.
Presently, Tong-ho stepped down onto the fortress path first.
"Well - let's go down now," he prompted. Chung-sik followed, saying,
"Let's go," while Hye-suk, her head still slightly bowed, not raising it fully, followed Chung-sik.
"Aren't you stopping by the house?"
"Going straight home! It's quite late... You'll bring that to school the day after tomorrow, right? Be careful (注意)..."
"Don't worry (念慮). Our school is fine."
"Miss Hye-suk! I trust you will be extremely (萬萬) careful."
"Yes - thank you (感謝)," she bowed slightly in greeting.
"Well then... Will you be out again tomorrow (來日)? Shall I come by...?"
"I'll come out. In the afternoon (午後)."
And so, Chung-sik and Hye-suk headed outside the fortress wall, while Tong-ho went inside, parting ways.

Part 2
Chung-sik ate dinner and left the house.
Though he held Longfellow's poetry collection (詩集) in his hand, he had no thought of opening the poetry book (詩冊), walking blankly until he reached the spot under the ginkgo trees. He hadn't come out seeking anything, nor for a stroll (散步). He had simply come out because his head felt heavy and stuffy, without any particular thought. But even outside, his head remained foggy and aching just the same.
He walked again up onto the fortress wall and sat on a flat fortress stone. <142>
The brilliant electric lights (電燈) of the Seoul (京城) city streets (市街) appeared before his eyes like a grand (宏大) illumination. Ah - this city, the hundreds of thousands of figures (人物) moving within it, amidst that crowd, I, together with Tong-ho, begin a great, unknown activity (活動). Yes. It is indeed an exhilarating (壯快), brave (勇敢), and valuable (價値) activity.
Thinking this way, even for a moment, he could forget all his anguish (煩悶), his spirit (精神) and energy (元氣) renewed, feeling as if the vast city spread before his eyes was his own, awaiting a fate (運命) to be controlled by his own hands.
Ah - his collaborator (協力者), Tong-ho, is now there in Toryŏm-dong. In this world full of people who obstruct (妨害) one's work and seek to violate (侵害) oneself, barely countable as human, the only trusted friend (信友) to believe in, discuss (議論) with, grasp hands with, and work with to the end is Tong-ho...
But ah - but if my feelings for Hye-suk are indeed romantic love (戀)! If it is love (愛)! Then my only trusted friend, Tong-ho, is a rival (敵) in love.
Ah ah! This base (卑劣) heart that sees a trusted friend, with whom I would share life and death (生死) for the great cause (大義), as a rival in love! With such a heart, what could I possibly achieve...
No. No!! Hye-suk is my younger sister (同生)!!
He cried out like this several times, but ever since seeing Tong-ho's letter (便紙) addressed to Hye-suk, he couldn't maintain a good feeling (心氣) whenever Hye-suk agreed (贊同) with Tong-ho's words, or when Tong-ho listened attentively to Hye-suk's words. He couldn't suppress a kind of jealousy (嫉妬), no matter how hard he tried to forbid (禁) it.
If I were to have someone like Tong-ho as a brother-in-law (妹夫), how happy (幸福) Hye-suk would be, and how relieved (安心) and happy I would be too. Entrusting the Hye-suk I love most to the friend I trust most, how good that would be. But
Ah - but looking back now, my own feeling of attachment (戀着) and affection (念) for Hye-suk was second to none. Hye-suk, Hye-suk... how could I... with Hye-suk...
As Chung-sik wandered like this, the image of Hye-suk, likely reading a book under the electric light in the inner room of his house, floated in his mind. And following that, memories of childhood came back like a dream.
Exactly thirteen years ago from now, when Chung-sik was seven and Hye-suk was six, on a day in early summer when the flowers had fallen and the cherries were ripening. <143>
Chung-sik, raised preciously by the hand of his widowed mother, the only person in the house, was eating a late breakfast before his mother's table. He then went up to the back hill and was playing alone when a little girl, who was picking blades of grass one by one in the garden of the neighboring house that had recently moved in, held onto the wire mesh fence and looked over, asking,
"Hey, what are you doing...?"
"This? I'm making 'kkamp'aengi' money. Breaking it into round pieces..." he replied, and thus the conversation began.
"What do you make that for?"
"Make lots and play money games..."
"Hey, stop doing that and come over here, let's play 'bisach'igi' (a tag-like game). Huh? We can make horses here... Don't you want to?"
"Then you come over here. Our garden has lots of cherries and is wider..."
"I don't want to. Is it good just because it has lousy cherries? It doesn't even have flowers... You come over here. I'll lift the wire mesh for you-"
"If you don't want to, then forget it. Will I die if I don't play lousy 'bisajapki'? I'll just play with my money..." As Chung-sik turned away, the girl seemed disappointed and said,
"Hey. I want to go, but I'm afraid my mother will scold me..."
"Scold you for what- it's okay if you play for a little while. We're not playing all day. I'll make a horse for you. Come over here. Huh? Hurry up."
Saying this, Chung-sik stepped on the bottom wire with his foot and lifted the upper wire with his hand, "There - hurry and come over." The girl hesitated but eventually climbed over.
So the two children played amicably, unaware that lunchtime had arrived. Chung-sik's mother, who had come to the soy sauce crock platform in the backyard to scoop red pepper paste, called, "Hey. Time for lunch," and seeing the girl, asked whose child she was. When told she was the neighbor's child, perhaps finding her cute, she invited her to come eat lunch and play together. The girl looked down at her dirty palms and said,
"My mother will scold me."
"No, it's okay. I'll talk to your mother, so eat with Chung-sik and play together. Okay?"
"I don't want to. I'll get spanked."
"No, you won't. I'll talk to your mother later, and you won't <144> get spanked. Come on."
Thus, the mother brought the girl, fed her lettuce wraps (saengch'issam) for lunch, watching with a happy smile.
A little later, a loud voice calling for the girl came from the backyard. Hearing the voice, the girl put down the lettuce wrap she was holding and sat still, not getting up immediately. Then Chung-sik's mother went around to the backyard, and a woman looked over from the other side of the fence, asking,
"Hasn't our girl gone over there?"
"Yes - she came. She was playing, so I called her and am feeding her some lunch now. How can she be so cute..."
Thus, the mothers also became acquainted. And so they learned that the newly moved-in girl's family was also a widow's household. A woman raising one precious girl alone. So, about ten days after becoming acquainted, the two widows living separated by a single fence pledged sisterly bonds (義) and their households became almost like one. Consequently, the girl and Chung-sik also became siblings, with the six-year-old girl calling the seven-year-old Chung-sik "Oppa" (older brother). That girl was Hye-suk.
The two families, in similar circumstances (境遇), became like one household, even tearing down the fence between them. Hye-suk's mother trusted Chung-sik like her own son (親아들), and Chung-sik's mother also knew Hye-suk as her own daughter (親딸) and raised her preciously.
While the two families lived in utmost harmony (和睦), unfortunately (不幸), later that year, Hye-suk's mother passed away from a stroke (風病), and it was decided that Hye-suk would stay permanently at Chung-sik's house.
Believing him to be her real older brother and knowing her as his real younger sister, raised under one roof, by one mother for over ten years, they grew up (長成) until now without any change (變態). But
last spring (昨年), after Tong-ho, who had entered prison (獄) and suffered hardship (苦生) for a long time, came out the previous February, they began discussing matters. As Tong-ho and Hye-suk's relationship (交際) gradually (漸漸) grew closer, I developed a new anguish. Thinking about it, no matter how I tried, the feelings I had for Hye-suk up until then were not just those of an older brother towards a younger sister.
Ah - the fearsome feeling of attachment (戀着)! Base (卑劣) worldly passion (俗情)!
Because of that, my views began to clash (背馳) with Tong-ho's opinions (意見)... Ah ah. No, no. Hye-suk is my sister. Tong-ho <145> is my trusted friend (信友).
Oh, even having these thoughts is a sin (罪) towards both of them. I am a sinner (罪人).
Lately, whenever the three of us gather, I frequently express opposition (反意) to Tong-ho's opinions, and Hye-suk seems to have realized it's triggered (發動) by jealousy. So, she seems to try to keep her mouth shut in front of Tong-ho as much as possible, and sometimes even seems to avoid (避) him.
Ah ah. Hye-suk goes to such lengths, worried that the friendship (友誼) between Tong-ho and me might break... Ah - but I! I have come to see the trusted friend with whom I intended to share life and death as a rival in love. Ah ah. Chung-sik lifted his heavy head. A cool breeze brushed under his chin. The night was already deepening.
The city of no night spread before his eyes, still brilliantly (燦爛) shining. Chung-sik looked up at the sky.
As if asking why he was suffering such anguish, the small stars twinkled naturally (天然). He sat blankly, gazing at the blue sky (蒼天).
Unconsciously, a verse of the Song of Wandering (漂泊歌) escaped his lips.
Shall I go forth, shall I return, beneath the Northern Lights (北極光)?
Russia's northern land is boundless.
The sun sets in the West (西), the day breaks in the East (東)
The noon bell (午鐘) rings, there - in mid-heaven (中天).

Too bright to weep, too dark to go
In the distant village (村), the lamp (燈盞) flickers.

With what heart he sang, the sorrowful (哀然) song pierced the soft night darkness (夜暗) of the spring night, transmitting its sound waves (音波) far away.
The sky was still, the world (世上) quiet, as the night deepened little by little.

Part 3
The flowers that seemed about to bloom, about to bloom, were now in full bloom. White were the apricot blossoms, yellow the forsythia, red the azaleas.
People carrying branches budding with forsythia and peach blossoms were now commonly seen. <146>
Tong-ho came out of school, threw down his book bundle (冊褓) as it was, left the house, and strolled through the quiet, wide alley near Tojŏnggung Palace (都正宮) in Sajikkol, enjoying the flowers. He reached the edge of the old fortress wall and sat down on the grass in a secluded corner that marked the boundary (境界) of Hŭnghwamun Gate (興化門) Grand Palace (大闕).
Being a secluded spot, passersby (行人) walking over the fortress wall were not visible, and no one came to the vicinity (近處), making it perfectly quiet. However, being on the fortress wall, even sitting there, he could see both outside and inside the fortress. The sight of beautifully blooming flowers and green willows in poetic spots pleased Tong-ho's heart.
Tong-ho, originally of a taciturn nature (沈默性), had not opened his mouth once since leaving home and sat silently on the grass.
He looked all around, inside and outside the fortress wall. The sight of one or two trees standing within the walls of ordinary houses, blooming quietly and solitarily, held even greater charm (興趣). After watching for a long while, he looked down at the city streets again. Everything was spring. The city streets too were enveloped in spring.
Ah ah. From somewhere, the sound of a reed pipe, slowly playing that spring has come, could be heard. That sound enveloped Tong-ho's whole being in a spring-like feeling.
Ah - the world clamors that it's spring, but... Tomorrow, at the Chongno (鍾路) corner, hundreds of bodies will be entangled again...
Muttering this, Tong-ho quietly lowered his head again. The figures of Chung-sik and Hye-suk appeared together in his mind.
Yes. It's clear. The reason Chung-sik's attitude (態度) seems to change from time to time is because of Hye-suk.
Hye-suk and Chung-sik grew up knowing each other as real siblings since childhood. Until now, past the age of twenty! But in Chung-sik's feelings towards Hye-suk, a separate love (愛) that Chung-sik himself didn't recognize (認識) was hidden. He himself was unaware of it, but from the time I, a third party (第三者), started visiting frequently, no, from the time I began to admire (思慕) Hye-suk, that hidden love finally began to activate (發動).
So, recently, Chung-sik seems to be undergoing infinite (無限) anguish (苦悶), trying endlessly to suppress (抑制) those feelings.
Ah ah! Chung-sik, full of spirit (意氣), who disregards (不顧) his life (生命) for the great cause (大義), is tormented so much because of this thing called love... It must be so. Being an extremely passionate person (熱情家), it's customary (依例) for him to be like this. Between us, who are planning (計劃) a great undertaking (大事), we must not <147> stir up other turmoil (波瀾), yet the feeling towards Hye-suk cannot be controlled (制) no matter how hard he tries, so he must be agonizing as much as possible.
Ah ah. We, with a great undertaking before us, torment our hearts over such matters. Truly, this world is laughable. For what reason did the Creator (造物主) put this thing called feeling/passion (情) into humans when creating them? Passion, the essence (本質) of love! Because of it, at such a time, over such matters, we suffer anguish.
The saying that being born into the world is already a sin (罪) is indeed true.
Ah - Chung-sik and Miss Hye-suk, Miss Hye-suk and I!
Miss Hye-suk, having noticed, also seems to be suffering greatly lately. She is agonizing, trying not to damage (傷) the friendship (友誼) between me and Chung-sik-. Secret anguish, each (各己) burning inside! Because of this, it is also a fact (事實) that the distance between the three of us is gradually widening.
Then I, this Tong-ho, am the one who has driven a wedge between the siblings Chung-sik. If the life they lived more amicably than real siblings is destroyed because of me, ah - I am a sinner (罪人).
Ah ah. I shall go. For the sake of the untroubled life of the Chung-sik siblings, and also to preserve (保存) the eternal (永遠) friendship between Chung-sik and myself, I will remove myself from the tangled trio.
I will sacrifice (犧牲) this puppy love, the memento of my life, to save the true (眞正) love of those siblings. And I will not lose the trusted friend who cannot be exchanged for anything.
He cried out. But how could he suppress the feeling of longing while being near his beloved (愛人)?
Ah - the world is sin/evil (罪惡). Passion is sin/evil. Yet here I am, wandering within it...
He lifted his head, unbuttoned his Western suit jacket, and stood up powerlessly.
The noisy (騷然) sounds of the city streets still drifted from afar, and the western glass window (琉璃窓) of the pointed house on the slope of Namsan (南山) reflected (反射) the setting sun, glinting dazzlingly.
Tong-ho turned again, looked down at Haengch'on-dong below the fortress wall, thought of Chung-sik and Hye-suk, sighed, and turned back once more.
Tomorrow, tomorrow there will be loud cries again, and several more will be entangled. And then, prison life again... Oh, yes. From tomorrow, let my body be beyond my control. Oh, yes. That way, I won't <148> meet them again... Ah ah, tomorrow I must take the lead. Yes. Let's do that. Then I can save the love of Chung-sik's siblings and never lose my trusted friend eternally.
Having made this resolution (決心), Tong-ho plopped down on the grass again.
The sun had already hidden behind the mountain, the reddish afterglow gradually faded, and things grew dimmer and dimmer bit by bit.
When Tong-ho lifted his head again to look at the fading sky, an early star was twinkling brightly.

That night, at nine o'clock, in the room across from Chung-sik's house, seven or eight students gathered around a single lamp, discussing tomorrow's events.
"Whatever happens, tomorrow afternoon at two, there must be someone to step out first and shout. Of course (勿論), fully prepared (覺悟)..." At these words, unexpectedly (意外) - Tong-ho, who usually stayed behind to handle subsequent matters - said,
"Don't worry about that. I will do it."
"No, Mr. Tong-ho must stay behind for the aftermath..."
"No. Since you all are here for the aftermath, I can go in tomorrow with peace of mind."
"N-no, Mr. Tong-ho absolutely must stay..."
"No. I have resolved (決心) to do it tomorrow, so let's not discuss it further. Is there anything else to discuss?"
"There isn't much else to discuss now, but..."
"Then let's disperse." Having roughly concluded the discussion, they left the main gate one by one, two by two, sporadically, wary of attention, disappearing into the dark alley... and went their ways.

About twenty days later.
The bustling spring season now began to age.
Among the flowers, peach blossoms were at their peak, while forsythia and apricot blossoms had long since withered.
It was Saturday afternoon. Chung-sik and Hye-suk were on their way down after singing songs on the curved fortress wall where the three used to go every Saturday.
"We need to know if Tong-ho is still in Chongno or if he's been transferred here." <149>
"He must have been transferred already, would he still be there?"
"I went to the reception (受付) and asked if someone like him had been transferred, but they said they didn't know. If you ask at Chongno, they say they know nothing at all..."
"Tsk, those scoundrel-like guys..."
Thus, Chung-sik and Hye-suk, wondering about the unknown fate of Tong-ho, soon reached the breached part of the fortress wall. Previously, this was always the place where they parted ways with Tong-ho, often lingering on the fortress wall here for quite a while, but today, since it was already getting dark, they decided to descend slowly without stopping.
Stepping onto the path outside the fortress wall, the two walked along the narrow grassy path towards their home in Haengch'on-dong, talking about Tong-ho, whose unknown fate worried them.
As they slowly reached the spot under the ginkgo trees, the sun hung on the mountain ridge, creating quite a magnificent sunset scene (落日의 景), and Chung-sik and Hye-suk stopped walking and stood there.
At this moment, standing tall on the fortress wall the two had just descended, a young man watched the pair standing side-by-side in the shade of the ginkgo trees and smiled faintly.
It was Tong-ho, whom the two were just discussing. He had been released after admonishment (說諭) twenty days after entering. However, Tong-ho could not tell the two the happy news (消息) of his safe (無事) release. If they knew he was out, they would meet frequently again, and if they met frequently, anguish would arise again in the hearts of the three...
Thus, Tong-ho, who had saved love with love, pretended not to know and stood on the fortress wall, watching them standing quite harmoniously side-by-side on the green grass in the shade of the ginkgo trees, smiling wordlessly.
The hanging sun completely disappeared, and the sky, reddish with the afterglow, turned violet.
Peaceful late spring (晩春)!! Evening smoke rose gently from every village, and the world grew darker little by little from the far edges... (End)
(June 1, 6, 18th night) <150>

**Summary**

Set in Seoul in 1920, the story "Flowing Sail" follows three young students, Chung-sik, his adopted sister Hye-suk, and his close friend Tong-ho. They are involved in clandestine activities related to the Korean independence movement, hinted at through references to prison, planned demonstrations ("entangled at Chongno corner"), and the need for secrecy. A love triangle develops: Chung-sik realizes he has romantic feelings for Hye-suk, who grew up as his sister, while Tong-ho and Hye-suk also develop mutual affection. This creates internal conflict for Chung-sik, torn between his love, his friendship with Tong-ho (a key collaborator in their "cause"), and his sense of propriety. Tong-ho, sensing the tension and Chung-sik's anguish, and fearing the disruption to their friendship and political work, decides to sacrifice his own feelings. He deliberately gets himself arrested during a planned event, removing himself from the situation. The story ends weeks later with Chung-sik and Hye-suk unaware that Tong-ho has been released, while Tong-ho observes them from afar, content that his sacrifice preserved their bond and potentially their relationship.

From a historical perspective, the story is interesting for its depiction of the atmosphere among young Korean intellectuals in Seoul shortly after the 1919 March 1st Movement. It subtly portrays the risks involved in nationalist activities (prison, needing to be "prepared" for arrest), the camaraderie among activists ("trusted friend," "collaborator"), and the tension between personal lives/emotions and dedication to the "great cause" (大義). The mention of specific locations like Independence Gate, Chongno, and the prison adds realism to the colonial setting. Historians might find the blend of personal romantic drama with the underlying political tension illustrative of the complex lives individuals navigated under Japanese colonial rule.

小說  流帆
						牧星
上
따뜻한 봄 어느 土曜日 午後이다.
나날이 폭은 하야지는 봄볏은 이날도 따뜻이 平和롭게 비치여 붉으락푸르락 꼿 픠려는 詩가튼 山밋 洞里가 꿈속 나라가티 고요히 잇서 봄볏에 빗나는 樣이 마치 가늘한 소리로 陽春의 曲을 奏하고 잇는 것 갓다. -모도가 봄이다!-.
모도가 봄이다. 산도 봄 물도 봄이고 사람도 봄이고 공기까지도 봄공기이다. 그 부둘업고 다사한 봄바람에 섯기어 가장 유찬하고 가장 평화로운 노래소리가 독립문 전체를 싸고 돈다 그것은
母嶽재 고개 左便 仁旺山 꼭다기 城壁 끗(곱은 城)우에 세 사람 靑年南女가 天使가티 서서 陽春이 온 것도 아지 못하고 갑갑한 속에서 지내는 兄弟를 爲하야 놉히 부르는 慰安의 노래소리얏다.
望鄕歌도 마치고 이제 惡夢이라는 哀然한 獄中歌를 마치자 惠淑이는 手巾으로 눈물을 씻엇다.
들고섯던 手帖을 접어 洋服주머니에 너흐면서 重植이가 「오날은 그만 나려가지...」하고 돌아가기를 催促하닛가 惠淑이가 手巾을 접으면서 팔ㅅ장을 끼고 서서 山밋 붉은 담을 힘업시 보는 東昊를 向하야
「나려가시지요」하고 自己는 重植의 압헤 서서 나려간다. 무슨 生覺을 하는지 팔ㅅ장을 끼고 山밋 담안을 드려다 보며 잠잠히 섯던 東昊는 말업시 발을 돌려 두 사람의 뒤를 딸아 나려간다.
惠淑이는 스므살이 못되어 보이는 흰 얼골이 둥글고 눈시원하게 생긴 女子로 現今 어느 高等學校에 通學하는 學生이고 重植이는 그 오라비로 OO專門學校 法科에 通學하는 靑年이다. 그리고 東昊는 重植의 가장 親한 信友이니 어느 法學校에 通學하는 靑年이다.
키만큼씩 자란 소나무 사이로 이리 꿉을 저리 꿉을 城을＜139＞ 끼고 나려오는 세 사람은 모다 잠잠하다.
압헤 선 惠淑이가 두 손을 들어 낫을 시치는 솔가지를 이리저리 헤치어 가면 똑 그대로 重植이는 뒤를 딸으고 東昊는 重植이의 발자국을 고대로 드디려는 듯이 딸을 뿐이다.
한참이나 잠잠하고 나려가다가 이때껏 말업시 잇던 東昊가
「그 手巾을 흔들던 사람이 아마 申禹錫이지...」하닛가 重植이가
「그만흔 사람中에 엇더케 알 수가 잇나?」한다.
「글세 사람은 만치만은 房이 그 房이닛가」
「바로 그 房인가?」
「응, 그 房이지...」하고는 暫間 後에
「그 房에 只今 셋이 잇나 둘이 잇나 그러치...」
다시 잠잠하야젓다. 세사람은 벌서 선바위ㅅ재를 훨신 지나왓다.
잠잠히 오던 惠淑이가
「에그 벌서 저녁 差入들을 하고 와요」하는 소리에 본즉 城밧갓 언덕길을 넘어 銀杏나무밋을 지나 터진 城ㅅ길을 밟아 婦人네가 二三人씩 差入食器를 들고 올라온다.
「발서 그러케 되엇나...?」하면서 重植이가 洋服압자락을 헤치고 時計를 꺼내 보더니
「에그 벌서 네時 五分前인걸...」한다.
이제 얼마 아니잇서 오날도 저물엇다. 監獄굴ㅅ둑에 무럭무럭 쏫아지던 검은 烟氣도 只今은 조곰씩 조곰씩 흘러나온다.
세사람은 城터진 곳 社稷골과 獨立門의 分歧點인 城길에 이르럿다. 重植이와 惠淑이는 城밧 杏村洞으로 東昊는 城안 都染洞으로 세사람이 헤여지려면 이곳에서 헤여질 곳이다.
세사람은 할말은 업스면서 무엇인지 할이약이가 남은 것가태서 머믓머믓하다가 길엽 城돌 우에 웃둑이 섯다. 모다 城밧글 向하야...
金華山우에 갓가와온 夕陽이 세사람의 影子를 보들어운 풀밧우에 비스듬이 길게 던젓다.
봄날은 저믈기도 平和롭게 저믄다.
어대서인지 따뜻한 南國을 노래하는 듯한 버들피리＜140＞ 소리가 봄소리답게 느리게 들려오고 母嶽재 빗탈길에 삿갓쓴 나무ㅅ군들이 소와 한께 閑暇히 넘어간다.
세사람은 城壁우에 서서 遠近의 春景을 바라보고 잇다.
「저기 저 허연 것이 무얼가요」옵바하고 惠淑이가 손을 들어 南大門 停車塲便을 가르친다.
「어대」하고 重植이가 그 손끗의 向하는 곳을 注視한다. 南大門 停車塲 그넘어 東慕벽돌 맨드는 큰 굴둑엽헤 무슨 집인지 西洋 鐵집웅이 잇고 그 뒤는 푸른 樹木이 잇고 그 푸른 樹木 뒤로 무엇인지 허연 것이 보인다.
「그게 무슨 旗 아니게...?」
「거긔 무슨 그러케 큰 旗가 잇겟서요」
「글세 그래도 旗는 旗야...」
이러는 판에 엽헤 默默이 섯던 東昊가
「그게 旗가 아니라 돗임니다. 거긔가 물이 아니 보여 그러치 漢江일것임니다. 배에 돗달린 것이야요. 저것 보시요. 그동안에 벌서 이 아래까지 가지 안앗슴닛가? 그러케 이약이 하는 동안에 배는 벌서 저만큼 갓슴니다」
딴은 돗인 것이 分明하다. 돗이 아니면 저러케 모르는 동안에 位置가 變할 理가 업는 것이다.
「그런대 오빠는 旗라고...」
「그럼 왜 自己는 몰랏던가?」
셋이 다-가티 웃어다. 그러나 重植의 웃음만은 그리 快活치 아니한 쓴웃음이엇다. 東昊는 다시
「歲月의 흐름이 저러케 速한 것임니다. 저 배가 모르는 동안에 位置가 變해진 그만콤 歲月이 모르는 동안에 지나간 것임니다. 딸아서 事業을 일울 機會라는 것도 저러케 모르는 동안에 지나가고 마는 것임니다. 生覺하면 只今 우리의 이 暫時 동안도 이러케 無意味하게 보낼 것이 안인가 해요. 이러고 잇는 동안에 엇더한 機會 엇던 歲月이 모르게 지나가는지 모름니다...」
「네-정말 너무나 歲月 가는게 無情해요」惠淑이가 이러케 말하고 고개를 조곰 돌리면서 숙인다.
「그야사 歲月가는 것이야 速하지. 그러나 그것은 世上 全體의 일이지. 그中에 어느 누구에만 限해서 그러케 速한 것이 아니닛가. 이 世上 이 地球에 生存하는 사람으로서 人力으로 엇지하지 못할 그런 것을 慨歎해서야＜141＞ 效用잇나? 歲月가는 것이 速한 것만 慨歎하랴서야 잠도 못자고 쉴새도 업슬 것 아닌가? 그러면 그러케 쉬지도 안코 자지도 안코 歲流와 한께 併行한다 하면 몃칠가서 그 肉身이 破壞될 것이냐 말이지...」
重植의 이말에 東昊는 잠자코 서서 對答을 하려도 안흐며 그는 江넘어 冠嶽山을 바라보고 잇고 惠淑이는 엇진 일인지 숙인채로 잇는 얼골이 붉으레-해졋다. 세사람은 다시 잠잠히 섯다. 해는 山웃線과 다핫다. 하늘이 점점 붉으레-해온다.
杏村洞 松月洞 玉天洞 館洞 一帶에 저녁 烟氣가 오르고 銀杏나무밋 잔듸에 왼終日 널엇던 油紙를 老人 두분이 거두기 始作한다.
이윽고 東昊가 먼저 城ㅅ길로 나려서면서
「자-이제 그만 나려갑시다」하닛가 重植이가
「가지」하면서 딸아나려서매 惠淑이는 고개를 숙인채로 시원히 들지도 아니하고 重植의 뒤를 쫏는다.
「집에 안이단여 가려나?」
「바로 가지! 꽤 느졋는대...모레 學校에 그것 가지고 갈터이지? 注意해 하게...」
「念慮말게. 우리 學校는 念慮업네」
「惠淑氏! 萬萬 注意해 하실줄 밋습니다」
「네-感謝함니다」허리를 暫間 굽혀 인사하엿다.
「야-그러면...來日 또 나올터이지? 내가 들어갈가...?」
「내가 나오지. 午后에」
이러케하야 重植이와 惠淑이는 城밧그로 東昊는 城안으로 헤여졋다.

中
重植이는 저녁을 먹고 집을 나섯다.
롱펠로의 詩集은 손에 들엇지마는 그 詩冊은 펼 生覺도 업시 멀건이 걸어서 銀杏나무밋에 이르럿다. 무엇을 求하려는 것도 아니고 散步하려 나온 것도 아니다. 다만 아모生覺업시 머리가 무겁고 갑갑함으로 나온 것이다. 그러나 밧게로 나와도 머리가 흐리고 압흐기는 마챤가지엇다.
그는 다시 걸어서 城위로 올라가서 반반한 城ㅅ돌우에 안젓다.＜142＞
京城市街의 燦爛한 電燈이 宏大한 일미네순과 가티 눈압헤 보인다. 아-이 市街 이속에 움즉이는 몃十萬의 人物 그속 그틈에 석기어 남모르는 큰 活動을 나는 東昊와 함께 始作한다. 그러타. 그것은 果然 壯快하고 勇敢하고 價値잇는 活動이다.
이러케 生覺하야 오면 暫時나마 모든 煩悶을 이져버리고 精神과 元氣가 새로와지고 마치 눈압헤 보이는 宏大한 市街가 自己것 가티 自己의 손에 左右될 運命을 기다리고 잇는 것 가티 느껴진다.

來日 來日은 또 큰소리가 나고 또 몃이 얽키겟고나. 그래서 또 철창 生活을... 오오, 올타. 내일부터 내 몸을 내 맘대로 엇져지 못하게. 오오, 그러타. 다시 그네와＜148＞ 맛나지 못하게... 아아, 來日은 내가 압장을 서야겟다. 그러타. 그러케 하자. 그리면 重植의 男妹의 愛도 살리고 信友를 永遠히 일치 아니하겟다.
이럿케 決心한 東昊는 다시 잔듸에 털석 주저 안젓다.
해는 이미 山 뒤에 숨고 붉으레하던 餘紅이 차츰차츰 사라지며 조곰조곰式 희미희미해 온다.
東昊가 다시 고개를 들어 저믈어 가는 하늘을 쳐다볼 때에는 일즉 뜬 별하나가 깜박깜박 빗나고 잇섯다.

그날 밤 아홉時 重植의 집 건넌 房에서 람포불 하나를 에워싸고 七八人의 學生이 來日 일을 議論하는 中이다.
『엇지햇던가. 來日 午后 두時에 맨 먼저 나서서 소리칠 사람이 잇서야겟소. 勿論 미리 覺悟하고...』 이 소리에 意外에- 依例 남아 잇서서 뒤ㅅ일할- 東昊가
『그것은 念慮마시요. 내가 하리다.』
『아니 東昊氏는 남아 잇서야 뒤ㅅ일을...』
『아니요. 뒤ㅅ일은 여러분이 계시닛가 나는 安心하고 來日 들어갈 터이을시다.』
『아-니 東昊氏는 반듯이 남아 잇서야...』
『아니요. 내가 來日은 하기로 決心하얏스닛가 이제 그것은 다시 議論맙시다. 다른 것 또 議論할 것 업나요?』
『인제 다른 것은 別로 議論할 게 업지마는...』
『그러면 헤여지십시다.』 이러케 대강 議論을 마치고 一時에 헤여지기도 注意가 되어 한 사람式 두 사람式 드문드문이 大門을 나서서 컴컴한 골목속으로 몸을 감추고... 하야갓다.

한 二十日 後이다.
거드러 거리던 봄철도 이제는 늙기를 始作한다.
꼿으로는 복사꼿이 한창이고 개나리와 살구꼿은 시들은 지 오래엿다.
土曜日 午后이다. 세 사람이 土曜日마다 가던 곱은 城우에 가서 重植이와 惠淑이가 노래를 부르고 나려오는 길이다.
『東昊가 그저 鍾路에 잇는지 이리 넘어왓는지 알 수가 잇서야지.』＜149＞
『벌서 넘어왓지 이때껏 잇겟셔요?』
『受付에 가서 이런 사람 넘어왓느냐 하닛가 모른다던걸. 鍾路에가 무러 보면 일절 모른다고...』
『애이 깍정이 가튼 놈들...』
이럿케 東昊의 安否를 몰라하는 重植이와 惠淑이는 어느 덧 城터진 곳에 니르럿다. 前 가튼면 늘- 이곳에서 東昊와 作別하던 곳이라 이 곳 城우에서 한참이나 머믈다가 갓지마는 오날은 벌서 해질 때가 되닛가 그대로 徐徐히 나려가기로 하얏다.
城 밧길로 나려서서 두 사람은 좁다른 풀밧길로 杏村洞 自己네 집을 向하야 가며 安否 몰라 궁금한 東昊의 이약이를 하는대
그네가 천천한 거름으로 銀杏나무 밋에 이르를 제 해는 山머리에 이 걸려 자못 落日의 景이 壯觀이라 重植이와 惠淑이는 발을 멈추고 섯다.
이 때 只今 두 사람이 지나내려온 城壁우에 웃둑이 서서 銀杏나무 그늘에 두 사람의 나란히 섯는 양을 보고 빙그레- 웃난 南子가 잇섯다.
그는 只今도 두 사람이 이약이하던 東昊이다. 들어간지 二十日 만에 說諭밧고 나온 것이다. 그러나 東昊는 自己가 無事히 나온 깃븐 消息을 두 사람에게 告할 수가 업섯다. 나온 줄 알면 또 자조 맛나게 되겟고 자조 맛나면 또 세 사람 가슴속에 煩悶이 생기겟스닛가...
이러케 하야 사랑으로 사랑을 救한 東昊는 모른체하고 城우에 서서 銀杏나무 그늘 푸른 잔듸우에 자못 和睦하게 나란이 섯는 것을 보고 말업시 빙그레 웃고 섯는대.
걸쳣던 해는 아조 숨고 하늘에는 餘紅이 붉으레 하야 보라빗을 이루엇다.
