**Body Text Translation:**
A Confession to Everyone

Holding the name Kaebyŏk (開闢 - Creation/New Era) without a creation-like event, we who bear the responsibility for Kaebyŏk feel not only regretful, but also suspect that the readers who anticipated Kaebyŏk might be greatly disappointed. However, there were various unavoidable circumstances surrounding this inaugural issue. Since January of this year, we have tried endlessly to make Kaebyŏk become Kaebyŏk [live up to its name]. But due to certain circumstances (엇더한 事情), we postponed day after day, and thus, after five months have passed, it barely manages to come out today. Consequently, all the manuscripts prepared in advance naturally became outdated, and under the mere desire "we must do it," we hastily threw things together as best we could, so how could the content possibly be substantial? Please just understand our humble sincerity (微誠) that "we just had to do it, even so." Starting from the next issue, we will add attention upon attention as much as our strength allows, striving to console our readers again for the disappointment of the first issue. We earnestly hope only that you will generously forgive (海恕) us, love this Kaebyŏk even more, help us, and guide us.

From the Kaebyŏk Editorial Office <130>

**Summary**

The editorial office of *Kaebyŏk* magazine apologizes to its readers for the significant delay in publishing the first issue (originally planned for January, released in June 1920) and for the potentially inadequate quality of its contents. They explain that "unavoidable circumstances" forced the delay, making prepared materials outdated and requiring a hasty compilation. They plead for understanding of their determination to publish despite difficulties and promise significant improvements in future issues, asking for continued support and guidance. From a historical perspective, the article is revealing due to its coded language ("unavoidable circumstances," "certain circumstances"). Published shortly after the 1919 March 1st Movement under Japanese colonial rule, these phrases likely allude to the pressures, censorship, or logistical difficulties faced by Korean nationalist or cultural publications during this sensitive period. The editors' emphasis on their "humble sincerity" and desperate need to publish "even so" highlights the challenging environment for Korean cultural expression.

開闢이란 名義를 가지고 開闢的 事實이 업사오니 開闢의 責任을 當한 者-未安하기는 勿論이어니와 開闢을 企待하시던 讀者 여러분께서 넘어 失望하실 듯 하외다. 그러나 이번 創刊號에는 여러 가지 할 수 업는 事情이 個中에 잇섯슴니다. 本年 一月부터 開闢으로 하여금 開闢이 되게 하랴고 애는 限업시 썻삼니다. 그러나 엇더한 事情으로 因하야 此日彼日하고 이러케 미루어 어느덧 五個月 뒤 오날에야 겨오 나오게 되엇습니다. 그러닛가 豫備하얏던 모든 原稿는 自然時代에 뒤지게 되엇사은中 다만 「하야겟다」는 慾望下에서 불치 듯 되는대로 막 덤비엇사오니 그 內容이 엇지 充實하오릿가. 다만 本社의 「그나마 하야만 되겟다」는 微誠만 諒解하옵소서. 다음 號부터는 힘잇는데까지는 注意에 注意를 加하야 讀者 여러분의 初號의 失望을 다시 위로코저 하나이다. 海恕하옵시고 本 開闢을 一層 더 사랑하시며 도으시며 指導하옵기만 애써 바라는 바올시다.
