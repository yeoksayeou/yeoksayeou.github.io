Regarding Studying Abroad in Europe and Russia
						From Harbin, Yi Pi-n-son

Although, sitting here in just one corner overseas, it is impossible to know the trends in thought among our compatriots in mainland Chosŏn, based on recent reports in newspapers and magazines, one can generally surmise that things are becoming socialistic. Consequently, whether it is out of admiration for Russia, the motherland of the global realization of socialism, leading to a desire to go to that country and learn firsthand; or whether drawn by the rumor that "if you just go to Russia, you can study without money"; or perhaps because, due to the recent Tokyo Earthquake, the path to study has become non-existent, I do not know. In any case, it is a fact that recently many student comrades intending to go to Russia have been coming and going, which we witness here (in Harbin), and it is also true that many inquiries about the feasibility of going to Russia ("루시아行能不能") arrive via correspondence through friends.

What I wish to say now through "Kaebyŏk" to our student comrades is by no means intended as a critique judging the rightness or wrongness of going to Russia. Rather, it is intended to serve as a piece of reference material for those who have set their minds on this or those who are wandering en route to Russia.

According to what has been recently reported in the newspapers, for studies above the vocational school level, one can study fully at government expense or half at private expense (approximately 20 won), and for those studying ideology, full government funding is said to be available. Regarding the procedure for entering Russia, it has recently become a difficult problem, almost to the point of being impossible. Generally, it is possible to obtain a travel certificate from the Representative Office of Workers and Peasants Stationed in Harbin (駐哈爾賓勞農代表舘), but whether one can find someone to provide an introduction is also an issue. Furthermore, while there are sometimes ways to go through arrangements by the Korean Communist Party (高麗共産黨) side in Russian territories such as the Maritime Province (沿海州等 俄領), it is not easy due to matters related to language ability, ideology, etc. First of all, crossing the Sino-Russian border (中俄國境) is also an obstacle.

In any case, while there are generally ways to enter Russia, the difficulty of the procedures is far beyond comparison to obtaining an overseas travel passport from the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs (日本外務省). Therefore, brothers who harbor intentions of studying abroad in Europe or Russia should think deeply before deciding their path, and furthermore, those fixated (*念) on the rumor that "if you just go to Moscow (莫斯料), you can study" should think even more carefully. (There is also a rumor that if you just go, you can study freely by working just a few hours a day).

I will convey more detailed news as opportunities arise in the future, but for now, this is all. <69>

**Summary**

This article from 1924, written by "Yi Pi-n-son" in Harbin, addresses Korean students considering studying in Soviet Russia. The author notes a growing interest possibly due to socialist leanings, rumors of free education, or the lack of opportunities in Japan after the 1923 Great Kanto Earthquake. However, the author strongly cautions that entering Russia is extremely difficult, requiring hard-to-obtain travel documents from the Soviet office in Harbin (often needing connections) or risky arrangements potentially via the Korean Communist Party in the Russian Far East. Crossing the Sino-Russian border is presented as a major hurdle, far more complicated than getting a Japanese passport. The author urges students to reconsider relying on rumors about easily accessible, free education in Moscow, potentially linked to mandatory labor. For historians, this piece is valuable as it reflects the early allure of the Soviet Union for colonized Koreans seeking alternatives to Japanese rule and education, highlights the practical dangers and bureaucratic obstacles faced by those attempting to reach Russia via Manchuria, mentions the role of early Korean communist networks, and shows the tangible impact of the Great Kanto Earthquake on Korean student mobility and choices.

歐俄留學에 對하야
						哈爾賓에셔 李비-ㄴ손
지금에 朝鮮內地同胞의 사상의 경향은 한갓 海外一隅에 안저 가히 알수 업스나 近者에 신문 잡지의 보도하난 바에 의하야 대략 社會主義化하난 것을 추측할 수 잇난 바이외다. 그래서 세계적으로 社會主義실현의 母國인 「루시아」를 羡望함에 따라 그 나라에 가서 실제적으로 배오고저 함이 온지 혹은 『루시아만 가면 돈업시 공부한다』하는 일편의 傳說에 끌니어 그 나라에 가고저 함인지 혹은 近者에 이르러 東京震災로 인하야 就學의 道가 無하야 그르난지는 모르겟스나 여하간 近間에 루시아行(가고저 하난 者)의 학생동모가 만이 來往함은 吾人이 이곳(哈爾賓)에 잇서 目睹하바이며 그 他朋友便으로 오난 通信에 『루시아行能不能』을 만이 問議함도 사실이외다.
나가 이제 「開闢」을 통하야 우리 학생동모에게 一言코저 함은 루시아行에 대하야 是非的 비평을 論코저 함이 결코 안이라 이에 뜻을 두는 者 혹은 루시아 途上에서 방황하는 者에게 한 조각 참고꺼리를 삼게 할가 함이외다.
近來 報紙上으로 전하 바에 의한즉 전문학교 이상은 全官費半私費(대략 20원)로 就學한다하오며 혹 主義學工夫者에는 全官費가 有하다 함이외다. 그리고 入露手續에 가서는 近者에 이르러는 殆히 不能이라 할 만하게 難問題인대 대개 駐哈爾賓勞農代表舘에서 旅行證明書를 내난 수 잇사오나 이를 소개하 者 求不求가 亦 문제이외다. 그리고 沿海州等 俄領에서 問或 高麗共産黨側의 周施으로 가는 수 有하오나 語學 主義 等 관계로 용이할 바 안이오며 爲先 中俄國境넘난 것이 또한 난관임니다.
여하간 대체로 入露방법이 절대로 업 바는 아니로되 그 수속의 곤란한 것은 여간 日本外務省에서 洋行旅券내는 것에 비할 바가 안이외다. 그럼으로 歐俄留學에 뜻을 품고 잇난 형제들은 깁히 생각한 후 行路를 정할 거시며 더욱이 『莫斯料만 가면 공부한다』는 傳說에 *念하난 이난 一層생각하난바 이스라 함이외다. (가기만 하야 一日에 幾時間 노동만 하면 공부하는 자유롭게 한다는 소문도 잇슴니다만)
이후라도 기회 잇난 로 상세한 소식을 전하겟슴니다만은 爲先 이만 함니다.＜69＞
