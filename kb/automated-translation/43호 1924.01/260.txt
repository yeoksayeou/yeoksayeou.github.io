기사제목	눈물의 巡禮 (Pilgrimage of Tears)
필자	金基鎭
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

눈물의 巡禮 (Pilgrimage of Tears)
						金基鎭 (Kim Kijin)
She could not offer anything finer than your tears of sorrow, silver tears,
For the torment of this rough heart.
-SAROJINI NAIDU-

Wherever eyes go, there are tears. Wherever I go, tears go. Since the children of man came into being, I have not seen a life without tears, nor have I seen a heart without tears. Like "speech," our tears, which emerged together, are seen wherever we go, seen wherever our feet take us. Tears, the treasure of the children of man, which seem perhaps to have arisen even before "speech," are our flowers. Tears are flowers.

Are the flowers of tears red, or blue? I do not know, I do not know. Only, in our hearts, in our lives, in our homeland, in our world, they have become flowers of tears, the flowers of tears have formed a sea. The sea of tears merely surges.

Flowers have fruit, fruit has seeds, seeds have sprouts, sprouts have growth.—The flowers of tears also have fruit, and growth.

In the morning, a chimney swift flew in through the open field window. The bird was caught and tied to a reel for winding thread. Its thin legs, bound by a weak thread, cannot leave. It tries to fly, but cannot fly further<225> than the length of the thread. The bird has been completely bound, abandoned. Though it flies, it flies within the room; though it leaps, it cannot exceed the length of the thread. The bird has been completely conquered by me.

A single, tiny chimney swift. It seems not many months have passed since it came into the world; the agony and impatience of the chimney swift, likely in its youth, are extreme. The body of the chimney swift, crafted with such exquisite skill that not even a feather on its wing could be made by ordinary hands, is, though it may look like this, a treasure of the heavens and earth. In its blinking eyes, in the movement of its tail bobbing up and down, in its thin, red, weak legs treading the room floor, there is an indescribable, subtle harmony of color. This tiny bird's body, containing life, is enveloped in an amalgamation of limitless fury, desperate cries, and struggle. Beneath the soft feathers of its breast and belly, it must harbor fearsome thoughts and plots to break the thread, kill me who captured it, shatter this room, and return to the great atmosphere of the sky and earth, its homeland, or into its nest, its sleeping place. —The bird is trembling. Unable to control its small body out of anger, it fidgets.

The small bird is caught in my clumsy grasp. Even onto my dull palm, the bird's life is deeply conveyed. The bird's chest flutters. The bird's body holds warmth. Its delicate feathers tremble slightly. My palm holds the bird's life.

I offered the bird grains of rice. I forced the bird's beak into the rice grains. Still, the bird refuses to eat and tries to fly, flapping its small wings. Helplessly, I pried open the bird's beak and put in a grain of rice. A moment later, the grain fell to the floor. The bird absolutely refuses to eat. It flaps its small wings. The bird's chest pounds extremely fast; trembling all over, it carefully scans its surroundings, seemingly looking for an escape route. The bird's body holds warmth, and its chest flutters. The small bird looks as if it is about to cry. —The bird must live, truly, the bird must live.

My clumsy palm, which held the tiny bird's great life, deeply felt the warmth of the bird's life, received the heat of the bird's life. Seeking the spring of the blue sky, yearning for the warmth of its homeland atmosphere, wanting to see its cozy home nest,<226> the baby bird, seeking the freedom to fly away swiftly, had tears; the baby bird had tears.

My palm, holding the bird's life—a small prank by a large hand—was conquered by the grand pulsation of that tiny life. My hand also had blood vessels; my hand also has life. How different is the blood pulsing in the bird's heart from my blood circulating to the very tips of my fingers? Why should the warm sensation be different? They are the same life, how are they different? Through a sixth sense, hearing the same faint whispers deeply felt, I had my tears, tears were in my heart. —How are the bird and I different? The bird and I share the same fate. Within the lives of the bird and me, within our hearts, there is a broken place. There is a warped place.—
Wherever eyes go, there are tears; wherever the heart reaches, tears reach...

Broken Wing
The great dawn approaches, and the night of sorrow has flown away.
At last, from the deep slumber of long, long centuries, I awaken!
The sprout, coiled within the tedious and long sleep,
Opens its fresh leaves to the returning wind of hope.
Our firmly hoping hearts are renewed in the brightness flying towards
The glory of the resurrection's light.
Our lives, and our homeland await a spring full of happiness...
Singing bird, why do you have a broken wing?<227>
Answer
Will spring truly awaken my former lands within the suffering of my heart, so rough and wrapped in emptiness?
Or will fate's blind arrow calm the weak, unconquered,
Craving battle of my throat?

The tears of the broken wing are here. These are the tears of Sarojini Naidu's broken wing. The tears of the Indian revolutionary woman poet are poured onto the broken wing. Can spring truly summon their former nation within such a tormented heart, and can it erase the record of the battle of their craving throats? Ms. Sarojini Naidu lamented, "Singing bird, why do you have a broken wing!" India's tears lie upon our desk. —Tears, let's go. Let's push aside the book pages, leave the desk, and go outside.

I began this piece by quoting Sarojini Naidu's poem, and now I introduce another of her poems. I did this with intention: to introduce to the women of Chosŏn—to you—an Indian woman poet worthy of global pride. Pausing for a moment the steps of tears heading outside from the desk, I have translated a few more of her poems below.
If You Call Me
If you call me, oh love,
Quickly, urgently I will come
Faster than the deer in the thick woods,
Than the panting, flying dove,
To the slave who raises them<228>
Faster, more urgently than the flying serpent...
If you call me, I will come
Without fear, whatever may happen.

If you call me, I will come,
Faster, more urgently than the seeking heart,
Than the feet of flowing light
Clad in fiery feathers,
Through the black foam of life rolling,
Or death's deep valley—
If you call me, I will come
Without fear, whatever may happen.
Caprice
You hold a wildflower in your fingertips
Carelessly you touch the flower to your lips,
Carelessly you tear the red petals and scatter them...
Alas! That was my heart.

You hold a wine cup in your fingertips
Lightly you bring the cup to your lips,
Lightly you drink it down and toss the cup away...<229>
Alas! That was my soul.
Silver Tears
Countless strange "Sarŭm" (People/Times?) brought gentle things and
Also thrilling, radiant emotions to this body...

Among the many various gifts and presents,
She could not offer anything finer than
Your tears of sorrow, silver tears
For the torment of this rough heart.

Countless wicked "Taim" (Times?) moved me,
Causing health and happiness to shatter...
Among all joys or memorable sorrows
He could not offer anything finer than
Your tears of sorrow, silver tears
For the torment of this rough heart.
-All from the poetry collection “Broken Wing”-

The people gathered on the earth, the people living in the ruins of the heart, they have tears. The flower petals held carelessly, plucked one by one, two by two, and scattered while looking away—those petals were their hearts. Lightly lifting a wine cup, gulping it down, then tossing the cup away, without realizing in the slightest that the cup was their soul. And so, there are tears.<230> There are Sarojini Naidu's "Silver Tears." In everyone holding a broken love, holding the ruins of a broken heart, holding the tiny sprout of a bent hope, I saw the same tears.

From the next room, the faint moans of a middle-aged woman can be heard. Occasionally, a sound like "Aha..." is heard, followed at the end by "Ahi-, Aigo-." It seems a part of her body, somewhere, aches persistently. According to what is said, she fell ill midway while traveling with her husband towards the Kando (間島) region and had no choice but to check into this inn. Why she is heading to Kando in this cold winter is not known in detail, but it seems she carries a heavy wound in her heart. The eerie moaning sound of the middle-aged woman, who seems to have endured much hardship of the heart, strangely makes the stuffy air of the quiet inn feel even heavier. After "Aha...", the moaning sound "Ahi, Aigo-," seeming to draw in even her breath—behind that sound, another kind of tear I had not seen is hidden. —Dark red tears are there...

"You wench, do you know where you are, making such a noise!"
Suddenly, such a sound abruptly enters my ears. It's coming from the servants' quarters (haengnangbang) attached to the corner, three or four rooms away. I now realize it's the sound of a man and woman fighting, staying in the inn's servants' quarters.
"You wench, do I have to shout louder because you can't hear that sound?" It's the gruff voice of the man.
"Well, if I can't hear it, I say I can't hear it, what then!"
"You wench, still making noise. So, you can't hear that sound, you wench!"
The man bellows like this from inside the room. The woman, tending the fire in the fireplace (agungji) just outside the room door, poking at a few pieces of damp firewood, has eyes brimming with tears.
"Well, what can I do if I can't hear it. Why did you bring me here just the other day? Didn't you know I can't understand quiet, quiet words, why realize it<231> now?... Always 'you wench this', 'you wench that'..."
"You wench, do you know where you are, sitting outside and making noise, huh!"
"Why, what's wrong with being a little noisy..."
"Alright, you wench, just try making a loud noise one more time. See what comes your way, I'll give you something good... You wench, still shouting! Do you want a taste of my fist?"
"...Ai, why won't this cursed fire catch. It's like rain-soaked wood..."
Realizing she lacked the strength to resist again the voice of her tough-looking husband, several times stronger than her, who sat inside the room bellowing and threatening with a taste of his fist, the woman changed the subject, escaping to the topic of the wood not burning well. —There is water in the woman's eyes.

The woman working in the inn's servants' quarters, her neck bent day and night by her husband's commands, she has tears in her life. That woman, subjected to double, triple, quadruple layers of abuse, must have tears frozen solid in her heart. Realizing that if she resisted just once more, a fist would land on her head, she tried to calm her resentful heart by saying, "It's like rain-soaked wood..." Unable to endure without talking back, but knowing she would be hurt physically if she did, she directs her words elsewhere; there are tears in her eyes. Wherever eyes go, there are tears; wherever I go, tears go...
The women of Chosŏn, how many unspeakable tears do they have? Chosŏn appears before my eyes, the life of Chosŏn appears before my eyes. The society of men appears before my eyes. Beneath the feet of our lives, standing on the verge of suicide, are entangled the countless dark red tears of women vomiting blood.
Leaving aside interests, let's think for the liberation of humanity's spirituality. The lives of our aunts, our older sisters, are like lamps without oil placed out in the wind. Why, for what reason, forgetting the larger picture and entering the small confines of the household, distinguishing between trivial interests and hierarchies, do they oppress pitiful women like this?<232>
What if the sacred spirituality is trampled under the pretext of physiological differences? For them, the mothers of humanity, when they have to take leave from activity due to physiological functions, we should treat them with even greater respect. Outside of those times, what difference could there be between men and women? —In Chosŏn, women's liberation must come first. But where is the person to lead it?

Wherever eyes go, there are tears; wherever I go, tears go...
I go out to Chongno (鍾路) and look at the faces of the people coming and going. Even on the faces of those passing by pretending to smile, on the friends in white clothes walking listlessly with faces wrapped in thought, on the red scarf and black skirt full of the color of pride, they all carry the same seed of sorrow. Wherever one goes, there are tears; what can be done about the fact that wherever eyes go, there are tears!
The door of a certain shop is closed, and a crowd of people has gathered outside. Through the gap in the shop's front door, which is half-open, the scene of goods being auctioned is visible. After a while, on the glass window of that shop's front entrance, a different name is written than the name of the shop owner until yesterday. It seems one person has gone bankrupt, and another person is entering as the owner. The bankrupt person must have tears in their eyes, but however, the day is not far when the new owner of this shop will also have tears. Let's lift our feet and direct our gaze towards Namsan (南山).

A body carrying a hollowly empty head staggers up into the park.
The moon crosses a sea-like cloud in the middle of the sky. The flickering lights of the marketplace below
Are more numerous than the scattered stars in the sky.

Stuffing something full into the hollowly empty head,
Staggering down,<233>
Night, in Seoul's Chin'gogae (진고개), the wind of Taishō 12 (大正 12年, 1923) slaps this damned cheek with a crack and runs away!

I got slapped! Slapped by the "wind of Taishō 12." Slapped by the commercialism surrounding Namsan. Wherever I go, the overwhelming commercialism slaps my cheek. The tears that were walking thoughtlessly turn around the corner of Chin'gogae after being slapped on the cheek, emerging weakly. The thoughtless tears that started walking from the Chongno corner go to Namsan, get slapped, and return. Tears are everywhere. Wherever eyes go, there are tears.
Brilliant electric lights flicker from every house, snatching away wide-open eyes. Elegantly decorated show windows make weary feet stop. Piercing commercialism surrounds me and attacks. I trembled, my breath caught...

"Year-End Grand Sale" (歲暮大賣出), "Year-End Gifts" (歲末贈答品)—various words catch my eye. "Ah, the year must be ending," I thought. For the first time, I had the thought that this year is ending. And then, I clearly realized that 1923 truly ends with this month. Yes, right now, on the peak of 1923, I am trembling, shedding tears...
Flyers are posted here and there. The words "Year-End" (歲末) catch the eye here and there. Even on the clothes of a child running errands busily, on the bicycle rushing past, in the bundle held by a dazed person in white clothes, the words "Year-End" seem to be written. Ah, 1923 is truly passing away.

December has all passed. An eventful year leaves me alone and passes by. In just a few days, the night will come when 1923 will be wrapped tightly in scraps of newspaper and sent back to the unknowable eternity (永刦). Soon, the time will come to bid "Adieu!" and part with this year called 'this year'. Bury this year, which held so much hope, amidst the cries of the chased-away haetae (해태)! Let's bury it!
It's the year-end! 1924 civil calendars and daily calendars hang in every shop window. Last year-end too, I was alone, thinking, "Ah, the year must be ending."<234> And this time too, unchangingly, in the same tone, all alone, I thought, "Ah, the year must be ending." I am foolish, I am worthless. With arms hanging listlessly, back stooped, eyes flickering weakly, carrying the "light" called modernity on my back, I seek out shaded places and spew fishy words! Seeking out only shadowy places, shedding thoughtless tears! I am foolish. I am worthless.
However, I cannot bear to leave the shadowy Chosŏn, our shaded homeland. Is there anyone holding me back? Not so. This worthless and foolish person called 'me', though wanting to move on their own, does not move. Looking out at Chekhov's "long, long rest," I merely lift my weary feet...

Dawn of Chosŏn, dawn of Chosŏn, dawn of Chosŏn—
Are you coming into the darkness, walking with open eyes?

Arise, wash away the pain and sorrow,
Pushing through the boiling waves of the Eastern sea,
Welcome your dawn that now slowly rises!
Over the decaying roofs, into your dust-covered room, the vanguard of the sacred dawn walks in.
Rubbing your sleep-crusted eyes, kicking off the moldy blanket, rise and greet it, embrace it!

Is our grandmother still sleeping? By the pillow's head,
Strip off the clothes of the straw-made effigy (제옹),
Bathe it in the first light of the sacred dawn, banish it!
The sky now, washing away the darkness, bearing the fading morning star on its forehead,<235>
Gathering the clouds and pushing them beyond,
Kisses the brow of the sun rising, spreading its seven-colored wings,
Kisses the brow of the sun rising, spreading its seven-colored wings.

Ah, you are coming into the darkness, walking with open eyes,
Oh, dawn of Chosŏn! Dawn of Chosŏn, dawn of Chosŏn!

Is Chekhov's "long, long rest" all that lies before us?
If there truly will be a day of humanity's completion, any day, centuries from now, then we must not engage in futile pranks today. And especially since we know that human evolution will someday reach completion, how could we entrust ourselves to useless idleness? Our duty, that is bound here, and our hearts are bound here. —Humanity evolves. Someday, humanity, with its long history of defeat, will gather its voices and cry out—"Now, it is 'completion'!"
There will be such a day. Even if the "long, long rest" lies before the "white hand" (흰손), completion awaits our descendants (後生). Since I believe this, and we believe this, what need is there to look elsewhere? There is none.—

The single goal towards which all humanity is heading, that is "completion." For long centuries, the children of man have failed. Footsteps taken towards completion have slipped. But light appears before us. There is thrill for the truth. All endeavors of all humanity, all actions of all humanity, must keep pace with us. We must align our pace with it.<236>
But tears flow ceaselessly. People's hearts are all different. Those who quarrel only over interests, who insist on their own circumstances, who commit bizarre violence at will, who turn their backs on truth and wield power, who discriminate by hierarchy and oppress, who trust in financial power (金力) and act tyrannically, who are captured by departed spirits (亡靈) and act stubbornly—such people are countless. Far too many, far too many. —We must bring about a revolution of the senses. We must transform human nature.
Money, made for convenience, binds life everywhere, in every place. Laws, created for convenience, persecute the children of man everywhere, in every place! Laws created for convenience, currency created for convenience—they instead lead us to disaster, commit the tyranny of slapping our cheeks at will. Conceptual life, created to eliminate the disorder of instinctual life, binds our limbs, and intellectual life, which started to eliminate the constraints of conceptual life, presses down on our necks. Heredity creates heredity, and tradition newly creates tradition. This is the complexity of human life.
But this is not something for us to boast about. Leave the complexity of human life and try to mend it into something simple. Reclaim original human nature. Towards the only goal before humanity, "completion," let us advance firmly with all our strength. Though tears may flow ceaselessly, though tears may not know how to stop, let us go, let us walk forward together with tears.—

Moreover, life is short; before us lies the "long, long rest." The reason to exert all our strength becomes even more urgent here. Devote your entire life, burn it up for this cause. Strive for the completion that will surely dawn upon our posterity. This is the only means for us, while living, to taste eternity (永劫). Whether one does this work in a short time, or does it throughout a lifetime, it is the same; there is no difference. Welcoming 1924, welcoming the great dawn of Chosŏn, I undertook a pilgrimage of tears. The small bird struggling to find life, the middle-aged woman moaning on a journey, the woman tending fire in the servants' quarters, the young merchant retreating after bankruptcy, the overwhelming commercialism below Namsan—turning away from these, my tears have returned once again to my desk.
However, unseasonal rain patters outside the window. At this time, as the year ends, for what reason does such unseasonal rain fall like this?<237> The dripping from the eaves beats out a heavy rhythm, high, low, sounding mournful. What is the reason? There are many things that cannot be understood.—
When this year goes, the next year comes; when the next year goes, the year after comes. Before the long rest arrives, white hair will come to carry us away; with a white carriage, it will come to carry away our hair. The rain falling outside, the calendar on the wall with few pages left—they make my firmly held heart feel, inescapably, a sense of the "transience of life" (人生의 無常). Truly, life seems short. Feeling this way while sending off an eventful year must also be a matter of chance. A French folk song comes to my tongue...
La vie est brève Life is short,
Un peu de rêve A little dream
Un peu d'amour A little love
Et puis, Bonjour And then, Good day

La vie est brève, Life is short,
Un peu d'haine A little hatred
Un peu d'espoir A little hope
Et puis, Bon soir. And then, Good evening.
"Man has a little dream, gradually grows up and experiences love, becomes mature and busies himself with socializing, and then grows apart even from the one he loved, harboring a little hatred. As youth passes like that, there's a small hope of maybe gathering some money, but white hair doesn't forgive even that and leads man into the grave. So, what remains? Only the cross on the grave. With the greeting 'See you again' (또 봅시다), only the cross-shaped stake stands on the grave"—that is the meaning of this folk song.
Meanwhile, the sound of dripping from the eaves now sounds intermittent. Perhaps the day is breaking. From tomorrow, the new year will come even faster. Standing on the peak of 1923 and looking back at the footprints left behind, traces of tears are here and there.—The transience of life is the transience of life, and the effort of life is the effort of life. Welcoming the new year, let us put down the brush of this pilgrimage of tears.
-December, '23-<238>

**Summary**

This article by Kim Kijin, published in *Kaebyŏk* magazine on January 1, 1924, is a deeply personal and symbolic meditation on suffering, represented by "tears." Kim sees tears everywhere – in nature (a captured bird), in the suffering of individuals (a sick woman, an abused wife, a bankrupt merchant), and pervading Korean society under the weight of commercialism and the end of another year. He draws parallels with the suffering expressed by the Indian poet Sarojini Naidu, using her work to frame the shared pain of oppressed peoples and particularly highlighting the plight of Korean women. Despite the overwhelming sense of sorrow and the apparent transience of life, Kim ends with a call to strive towards human "completion" and welcomes the "dawn of Chosŏn," urging Koreans to embrace the new year (1924) with renewed effort and hope, even amidst the tears.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for reflecting the intellectual and emotional climate of colonial Korea in the mid-1920s. It captures the pervasive sense of sorrow (han) and oppression felt under Japanese rule, explicitly linking personal and societal suffering. Kim's reference to Sarojini Naidu indicates an awareness of and solidarity with other anti-colonial movements, positioning Korean struggles within a global context. The specific focus on the subjugation of Korean women ("women's liberation must come first") highlights the intersection of national and gender oppression. Finally, the concluding call for a "dawn of Chosŏn," despite the bleakness, embodies the persistent nationalist hope for liberation and progress that characterized the era.

눈물의 巡禮
						金基鎭
그이는 당신의 슬픔의 눈물, 銀눈물보다, 더 훌융한 것을 내놋치 못햇다.
거치런 이마음의 괴로움을 위해서.
-SAROJINI NAIDU-
눈가는 곳마다 눈물이 잇다, 내가 가는 곳마다, 눈물이 간다, 사람의 자식이 생겨난 이후로, 눈물업는 살림을 보지 못햇스며, 눈물업는 마음을 보지 못하엿다. 「말」과 함가지로 생기여 나온 우리의 눈물을 가는 곳마다 본다, 발가는 곳마다 본다. 혹은 「말」보다도 먼저 생긴 듯 십흔 사람의 자식의 보배인 눈물은, 우리의 꼿이다. 눈물은 꼿이다.
눈물의 꼿은 붉으냐, 푸르냐, 나는 몰으겟다, 나는 몰으겟다. 다만, 우리의 마음에, 우리의 살림에, 우리의 鄕土에, 우리의 세계에, 눈물의 꼿이 되여잇다, 눈물의 꼿이 바다를 일우엇다. 눈물의 바다가 츌넝거릴 뿐이다.
꼿에는 열매가 잇다, 열매에는 씨가 잇다, 씨에는 싹이 잇다, 싹에는 生長이 잇다.-눈물의 꼿에도 열매가 잇스며, 生長이 잇다.

아츰에 열어논 들챵문으로 굴둑섀 한 마리가 날러들어왓다. 새는 붓잡혀서 실감는 실패에 붓잡혀 매엿다. 가느른 다리가 약한 실파람에 묵기여 가지고 떠나지 못한다. 날흐려하나 실의 기럭지보다는, 더 멀리는, 날르지＜225＞ 못한다. 새는 실에게 완전히 구속되여, 버렷다. 날른다하나 방안에서 날르는 것이요, 飛躍을 한다하나 실의 기럭지를 지나치게하지 못한다, 새는 나에게 완전히 정복되고 마랏다.
한 마리의 조고만 굴둑새, 세상에 나온지 몃 달이 못된 듯 십흔, 少年期에 잇는 굴둑새의 煩悶焦燥가 極度에 잇다. 날개쭉지의 털억하나도, 凡常한 손으로 만들지 못하게, 精巧를 다하야 다듬어 노흔 굴둑새의 몸동아리 하나는, 이래보혀도, 천지의 보배다. 떳다가 감는 눈동자 하나에도, 아래우으로 움죽이고 잇는 꽁지의 한모슴에도, 방바닥을 듸듸고 잇는 가느른, 붉은, 약한 다리에도, 말할수 업는, 精妙한 색채가 조화를 가젓다. 생명을 담은 이 조고마한 새의 몸동아리는, 限量도 몰으는 激憤과, 絶叫와, 爭鬪의 혼합체로 싸히어잇다. 가슴과 배의 보드러운 털억밋해는, 실을 끈코, 저를 잡은 나를 죽이고, 이 방을 깨트리고서, 저희의 고향인 한울과 땅의 大氣속으로, 혹은 저희의 잠ㅅ자리인 보금자리속으로 드러가고자 하는 무서운 생각과 음모를 품고 잇슬 것이다. -새는 떨고 잇다. 분노에 못익여 조고만 몸을 엇잘줄 몰으게 움죽이고 잇다.
무듸게 생긴 나의 손아구에 조고만 새가 붓잡혀 잇다. 감각이 둔한 나의 손바닥에도, 새의 생명이 그윽히 전하여 온다. 새의 가슴은 발닥어린다. 새의 몸에는 온기가 잇다. 纖細한 털억이 微動을 한다. 나의 손바닥은 새의 생명을 붓잡고 잇다.
나는, 새에게 쌀애를 주엇다. 새의 주둥이를 쌀애속에 집어 늣토록 강제하엿다. 그래도 새는 먹지를 안코서 날르려 한다, 조고만 날개를 푸더덕거린다. 나는 하는 수 업시 새의 입을 벌니고서 쌀애하나를 집어 느어주엇다. 조곰 잇다가 쌀애기는 방바닥으로 떠러저버렷다. 새는 그여코 먹지를 안는다. 조고만 날개를 푸더덕 거린다. 새의 가슴은 극도로 뛰논다, 전신을 떨면서 사방을 자셰히 본다, 도망갈 구녕을 찻는 모양이다. 새의 몸에는 온기가 잇고, 새의 가슴은 발닥어린다. 조고만 새는 울려고 하는 듯 하다. -새는 살아야겟다, 참말로 새는 살아야겟다.

조고만 새의 크나큰 생명을 붓잡고 잇든, 무듸게 생긴 나의 손바닥은, 새의 생명의 온기를 그윽히 늣겻다, 새의 생명의 熱을 바닷다. 푸르른 한울의 봄을 찻고저, 따스한 大氣의 고향을 그리여, 포근한 내집의 보금자리를 보고저,＜226＞ 후르르 날으는 자유를 구하는, 새의 색기에게 눈물이 잇섯다, 새의 색기에게 눈물이 잇섯다.
크나큰 손의 조고만 작란인, 새의 생명을 붓잡고 잇든 나의 손바닥은, 조고만 생명의 위대한 躍動에 정복되엿다. 나의 손에도 血脈이 잇섯다, 나의 손에도 생명이 잇다. 새의 심장에 뛰는 혈액과, 나의 손고락 그 끗헤까지, 돌아단이는 나의 혈액과 무엇이 다르랴, 따뜻한 맛이 엇지하여서 다르랴. 똑가튼 생명이 무엇이 다르냐. 第6感을 통해서, 그윽히 늣기는 똑가튼 속살거림을 내가드를 때, 나의 눈물이 잇섯다, 나의 마음에 눈물이 잇섯다, -새와 나와 무엇이 다르냐, 나와 새와는 똑가튼 신세다. 나와 새와의 살림속에는, 마음속에는, 부러진 곳이 하나가 잇다. 이그러진 곳이 하나가 잇다.-
눈가는 곳마다 눈물이 잇다, 마음닷는 곳마다 눈물이 닷는다...

더구나 생명은 짧다, 우리의 압헤는 『長久한 휴식』이 잇다. 전력을 다할 필요가 더욱히 緊切해지는 이유가 여기에 잇다. 전생명을 들어서 이것 때문에 燃燒식혀라. 우리의 후세에 반다시 닥치여올 완성을 위해서 노력하여라. 이것이 우리가 살어잇스면서 永劫을 맛보는 유일한 수단이다. 짤막한 시간에 이 일을 하나, 평생을 두고 이 일을 하나 맛찬가지다, 다름이 업다. 1924년을 마지면서, 위대한 朝鮮의 黎明을 마지면서, 나는 눈물의 巡禮를 하여보앗다. 생명을 찻고자 발닥어리든 조고만 새, 나그네 길에서 신음하는 중년의 여자, 행랑방에서 불때는 녀편네 破産을 하고서 물너나가는 젊은 상인, 南山아래에 氣焰이 萬丈한 상업주의, -이것들을 돌아서 나의 눈물은 또다시 나의 책상으로 도러와 버렷다.
그러나, 때안인 비가 창문밧게셔 훌적어린다. 이 해가 다가는 오늘이때, 무슨 까닭으로 철안인 비가 이다지＜237＞오느냐. 처마의 落水가 놉게, 얏게, 무거운 쟝단을 치며 늣기여 운다. 무슨 까닭이냐, 알 수 업는 일도 만타.―
올해가 가면 내년이 온다, 내년이 가면 내후년이 온다. 長久한 휴식이 오기전에 白髮이 우리를 실으러 올터이지 흰수레를 가지고서 우리의 머리털을 실으러 올터이지. 밧갓헤 오는 비가 몃장이 남지 안은 벽우의 「카렌다-」가 굿세게 가진 나의 마음에게, 오히려, 엇잘수 업게, 『인생의 無常』이라는 늣김을 가지게 한다. 참말로 인생은 짧은 듯 십다. 多端한 1년을 보내면서 이와가튼 늣김을 갓는 것도 우연한 일일 것이다. 佛蘭西의 俗謠하나가 혀ㅅ바닥우으로 떠올러 온다...
La vie est breve 삶은 짧다,
Un peu de rave 조고만 꿈
Un peu d'amour 적은 사랑
Et puis, Bon jour 그러구는, 엇더시요

La vie est breve, 삶은 짧다,
Un peu d'haine 적은 미움
Un peu d'espoir 적은 바람
Ef puis, Bon soir. 그러구는, 또 봅시다.
『사람은 조고만 꿈을 갓게 되고, 차차 자라면 사랑도 해보고, 老成하게 되면 교제도 해보느라고 분주하며, 그러구는 사랑하든 사람과의 사이도 벌어지게 되여서 조고만 미움도 갓게되고, 그럭저럭 청춘이 지나면, 돈이나 모하볼가 하는 적은 바람도 잇지만은, 白髮은 그거나마도 용서하지 안코서 사람을 잇글고서 무덤속으로 들어가버린다. 그러면, 남는 것은 무엇이냐? 다만 墓우에서 잇는 십자가뿐이다. 「또 봅시다」하는 인사로, 墓우에는 열십자의 말뚝만 서잇슬 뿐이다』-하는 것이 이 俗謠의 뜻이다.
그동안, 추녀의 落水소리도 지금은 떼염떼염 들니게 되엿다. 아마 날은 드는 모양이다. 내일 부터는 새해가 더 빨으게 올터이지. 1923년의 고개 우에 서서 거러온 발자욱을 돌아보면 눈물의 흔적이 여기저기에 잇다.-인생의 無常은 인생의 無常이고, 인생의 노력은 인생의 노력이다. 새해를 마즈며, 눈물의 巡禮의 붓대를 거두자.
