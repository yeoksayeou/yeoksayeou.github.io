Author: 一記者 (One Reporter)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

The Heart of the Problem is Systemic Reform, The Inside Story of Whimun High Common School During the Student Strike, Their Wicked Schemes that Ignore the Independence of Education
						One Reporter

The student strike incident at Whimun High Common School (徽文高等普通學校) was already briefly reported in this journal's issue 42 (December, Year of Kyhae [1923]). I had earnestly hoped that the incident would be resolved amicably as soon as possible. However, contrary to this hope, the incident has gradually escalated, resulting in the suspension of all students from the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th years, and the unfortunate expulsion (馘首) of the 13 so-called ringleaders.

Of course, the school authorities must have handled this with considerable deliberation and prudence. Also, whatever measures are taken, I believe some resolution will be reached and the problem will be settled by the beginning of the new semester (reportedly January 7th). However, unless Whimun High Common School fundamentally reforms its current system, even if a superficial resolution is reached this time, it is unknown whether similar unfortunate incidents will repeatedly occur in the near future, resulting in numerous sacrifices among innocent students and staff (here, sacrifice of course does not mean the sacrifice of life). Saying this might lead some to misunderstand me. However, I truly speak out of necessity, ardently wishing for such unfortunate incidents to be permanently eradicated for the sake of Whimun High Common School, its staff, and the educational world of Chosŏn.

This recent student strike at Whimun High Common School appears on the surface to be simply a movement to expel the principal, but looking closely at the inside story reveals it to be a movement for the reform of the current system. That is, Principal Im's incident involving the illicit registration of athletes was merely a fuse, the cause being the students' long-held grievances and dissatisfaction with the school's system. Originally, Whimun High Common School was solely managed by Mr. Min Yŏnghwi (閔泳徽). It is true that Mr. Min has spent a considerable amount of money and some effort on the school over many years. However, he is inherently a man of influence and also a man of property. He is also a cunning figure among the old-fashioned conservatives (頑固派). His establishment of the school was more for his own honor than for the public good <81> and more a strategy to protect himself than for the education of youth. He does not consider the school a sacred public instrument (公器) of society that educates, but rather thinks of it as some farm or company aiming for profit. Consequently, he views the school staff as if they were his retainers (舍音) or clients (門客), and the students as mere playthings.

First of all, he couldn't even give the school a sacred name; like the recent ugly practice of commending so-called filial sons and virtuous women with awards like "Yi Yŏl-hwi Award" or "Kim Hyo-hwi Prize," he named it Whimun (徽文) to promote his own name (though named by imperial decree (勅命), it was actually lobbied for at the time). Even when donating the basic assets, he couldn't do it straightforwardly but did so under the pretext of commemorating his 79th birthday (望八壽年) (In June, Imsul year [1922], commemorating his 79th birthday, he donated 150,000 won in cash, 50,000 won in Hanil Bank (韓一銀行) stock certificates, and over 1.4 million pyeong of land in P'yŏngwŏn (平原) and Anju (安州)). When expanding the school building, to commemorate himself, he had "Huijungdang" (稀重堂) carved onto the nameplate above the office door (meaning Mr. Min rebuilt it at age 70). Furthermore, reading his so-called admonition to students reveals many nauseating passages. He first tells students that to become truly great men, they must practice true virtue (實德), that is, logic and morality, and also claims that in educating the younger generation for over 20 years, he has exhausted his efforts (誠力), so hearing of a student's single good deed makes him unable to contain his joy (喜不成眼), and hearing of a single bad deed makes his food taste bad (食不甘味).

Of course, ethics and morality are necessary not only for students but for everyone. However, for Mr. Min to advocate ethics and morality (even the traditional moral code [倫綱]) is truly bizarre, like a drunkard giving a lecture on temperance or a prostitute discussing chastity. Did Mr. Min truly practice loyalty between ruler and subject (君臣有義), the most important aspect of traditional East Asian ethics? What public justice and virtue (公道公德) has he shown towards the common people of society? Did hearing of a student's good or bad deed truly make him lose his appetite (食不甘味) and unable to rest comfortably in his seat (寢不安席)? No, the morality and ethics he advocates simply mean obedience and deference to himself. It means that students, staff, and even the general propertyless class should all submit. To speak more frankly, his morality is not true morality (道德) but thievery-morality (盜德), and his ethics are not true ethics (倫理) but profit-ethics (綸利). "Food tasting bad" (食不甘味) probably means his stomach is too full from a sumptuous feast (食前方丈) to eat more, and "unable to rest comfortably in his seat" (寢不安席) likely means he can't sleep well because he's tormented by too many attending maids and beautiful concubines (侍女美妾).

How could Whimun High Common School, formed from such a mass of various unholy and虚伪 (false/empty) things, possibly have a perfect system? That is, Mr. Min holds the title of Chancellor (總長) of the students and interferes not only in finances but in all general affairs, even arbitrarily appointing and dismissing (黜陟) teachers. Therefore, although Principal Im (任校長) has the title of principal, the real power lies with the Chancellor. If Principal Im were rich in decisiveness and possessed a distinct sense of independence, even if it meant <82> resigning from the principal's position, he would never directly accept interference from the Chancellor or the entire Min clan, but would naturally exercise his authority and strictly separate school affairs from Mr. Min's family matters (閔氏家事). However, Principal Im is originally a lenient, kind, sincere, and diligent person, but extremely weak-willed, hence he has endured this situation until today. Of course, Principal Im must have suffered great anguish and agony during this time. People criticize him as spineless (無骨虫) or a puppet (木偶人), but he has certainly not remained until today out of greed for the principal's position and honor. Even if there were some acts of submission, he thought that merely enduring and educating would yield results. It's like a farmer believing that even if he smells manure, just applying fertilizer will result in a good harvest.

And the school has a so-called executive committee (幹部) system. That is, there is a system resembling a triumvirate (三頭政治) composed of Principal Im Kyŏngjae (任璟宰), Dean Chang Ŭngjin (張膺震), and Vice-Principal Min Kwangshik (閔廣植). All general school affairs are decided solely by these three magnates, and other staff (24 full-time teachers, 6 part-time teachers) have no rights regarding school administration. Therefore, school affairs constantly lack unity, and student disciplinary methods are also inconsistent, causing great harm to education. As Whimun High Common School has thus transformed into a power group (勢力團體), those without influence naturally end up performing ugly acts of submission, flattery, and adherence (附隨) that their conscience does not permit. That is, the executives (幹部) flatter, submit, and adhere to the Chancellor, the Chancellor's family, and even his clients (門客), and the teachers have become unable to avoid adopting a doubly compliant attitude towards both the school executives and the Chancellor's household (總長家). (Not everyone is like this).

Because of this, when Chancellor Min or his family visits the school, precious teaching time is entirely wasted, and all staff, not to mention students, are too busy (奔走不暇) with welcoming and entertaining them. Even during excursions or trips, if they happen to see the ancestral tombs of Mr. Min along the way, they are made to pay respects. When they win a championship flag in baseball or soccer competitions, they lead the students to Mr. Min's house and shout "Manse!" (万歳) so loudly that the whole neighborhood of Kyŏngundong (慶雲洞) seems to shake, thereby buying the curiosity and favor of the entire Min family. Sometimes, they even went all the way to Yŏngdo Temple (永道寺), where Mr. Min's wife was staying for Buddhist prayers, just to shout "Manse!".

Moreover, the school has recently been obsessed with winning championship flags, paying little attention to academics and focusing solely on sports. Of course, sports are indispensable for physical education (體育). However, if they are not promoted appropriately but the main focus is placed solely on winning championship flags <83> out of temporary vanity or curiosity, this is not physical education for educational purposes but a gambling-like fighting technique (賭博的 鬪技術). The school authorities, without considering this point, gave special treatment to any student who was a good athlete, regardless of their behavior (there was a case where a student facing expulsion was forgiven due to being an athlete). Also, the Min household treated athletes as if they were imperial envoys (勅使) or mighty warriors (力士). During competitions, they provided lodging and meals at their own house, feeding them nourishing foods (滋養品) like steamed ribs, eggs, and milk until their stomachs were about to burst. When a championship flag was won, they recklessly packed students into automobiles unsuitable for their status and drove them around to places like Myŏngwŏlgwan (明月舘) and Kugilgwan (國一舘). Superficially, this looks like special treatment for athletes, but in reality, it is like a gambler (俠兒) who likes bullfighting or cockfighting feeding his bulls and roosters well to ensure his own victory. Furthermore, this treatment was limited only to the athletes; other students couldn't even study properly and only cheered until their throats were sore and their arms broken, merely filling the bellies of the athletes (actually, those who nurture the athletes).

Furthermore, the school's facilities are inadequate. That is, a large amount of expenses are spent only on sports, while the purchase of books, experimental equipment, etc., necessary for academics is neglected (等閒視). (Last year's sports expenses were over 8,000 won, while books and equipment were merely 3,000 won). Superficially, the grandeur (宏大) of the school buildings and the vastness of the sports field are said to be the best among private schools in Kyŏngsŏng (京城). However, the internal facilities are incomplete and inadequate (不完不備). It's like a starving beggar (嘶腸한 乞人) wearing a fox-fur coat (狐白裘) or a naked soldier carrying a long sword. Because of this, students' academic performance is said to be relatively poorer than that of other schools. All these points constitute the general students' long-held grievances and dissatisfaction.

Throughout history, nations that revered literature (文) weakened through literature, and nations that崇尚 (admired) martial arts (武) perished through martial arts; if this is true even for nations, how much more so for educating immature students? That is, Whimun High Common School tried to seize hegemony in the academic world through sports, but conversely, it met failure because of sports. The baseball bats ("뺏도방맹이") practiced with for 2-3 years could not be used effectively and became tools for threatening 5th-year students during exams (there was an incident where expelled or suspended students threatened to assault 5th-year students with clubs at the school gate to disrupt their exams). The cheering squad organized to compete against other schools consequently fostered the formation of a group that systematically conducted the student strike. During the recent Soccer Tournament (蹴球大會), if the school authorities had not been blinded (眩惑) solely by winning the championship flag, they would not have <84> committed the ugly act of including ineligible players. Also, even if the general students had distrusted Principal Im for some time, the expulsion movement would likely not have erupted so suddenly. This is truly regrettable for the school.

So, should the blame for this incident be placed on sports, or on the students' rash actions (妄動), or the principal's misconduct (失態)? Of course, the principal's misconduct and the students' rash actions are blameworthy, and the harm caused by promoting only sports is also harm. However, the fault lies entirely in the incompleteness of the system described above. The expelled principal and the suspended/expelled students were merely sacrificed as a result of the initial flaws in that system. I hope that the school's founder will reflect deeply (猛省), stop thinking of the school as his private property and regard it as an absolutely sacred public instrument (公器), entrusting all matters entirely (專任) to the school authorities without interference. Furthermore, I hope the school authorities will not merely act according to temporary expedients or social considerations just to suit the founder's intentions, but will stand independently (儼然히 자립하야), maintain the dignity of education, show more sincerity and devotion (誠心誠意), respect the opinions of the general staff, and even if students misunderstand or act rashly, not merely consider expulsion or suspension according to school regulations as their prerogative, but engage in self-reflection (自求自省) so as not to ruin the future of students, whose potential is like ten thousand li (萬里), for the sake of the face of one or a few individuals. <85>

**Summary**

The article critiques the situation at Whimun High Common School following a student strike that led to mass suspensions and expulsions. The author argues the root cause isn't just the principal's recent misconduct (related to ineligible athletes) but the fundamental flaws in the school's system, dominated by its founder, Min Yŏnghwi. Min is portrayed as treating the school like a private enterprise for personal glory rather than a public educational institution, interfering excessively, fostering a culture of sycophancy among staff, and prioritizing athletic victories over academics and proper facilities. The principal is depicted as well-meaning but weak, unable to assert educational independence against the founder's influence. The author calls for fundamental reform, urging the founder to step back and the administration to prioritize genuine education and student welfare over superficial achievements and personal connections. For historians, the article offers a critical contemporary perspective on the challenges faced by private Korean educational institutions under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting the tensions between founders' interests, administrative realities, educational ideals, and student activism. It vividly illustrates the critique of an elite figure (Min Yŏnghwi) using education for personal status and the detrimental effects of an autocratic, personality-driven management style on educational quality and student morale.

問題의 中心은 制度의 改革에, 同盟休學中에 잇는 徽文高普의 內幕, 敎育의 獨立性을 無視하는 저들의 陰邪
						一記者
徽文高等普通學校의 동맹휴학사건은 本紙 제42호(癸亥12월호)에 이미 약간 보도하엿다. 나는 該事件이 1日이라도 조속히 원만해결되기를 哀心으로 갈망하얏섯다. 그러나 사건은 此와 반대로 漸益擴大되야, 1,2,3,4학년은 전부 停學을 당하고 소위 주모자인 13인은 馘首의 비운을 당하게 되얏다.
물론 학교당국자는 此에 대하야 十分 苦心하고 十分 신중히 처리하얏슬 것이다, 또한 何等의 방법을 강구하던지, 今학기 개학 초(1월 7일云)에는 일단락이 生하야 문제가 해결될 줄로 思한다. 然而 徽文高等普通學校는 근본적으로 현재의 제도를 개혁치 안이하면, 今回에 비록 표면상 해결이 된다 할지라도 未久한 장래에 또 如斯한 불상사가 踵出하야 無辜한 학생 及 직원의 다수 희생이 生할지도 不知하겟다. (여긔에 희생이라함은 물론 생명의 희생은 안임) 이럿케 말하먼 혹은 나를 오해할는지도 모른다. 그러나 나는 실로 徽文高等普通學校를 위하고 同校職員을 위하고 또는 朝鮮의 교육계를 위하야 此等 불상사가 永久히 絶滅하기를 切望함으로 부득이 言코자 함이다. 今般 徽文高等普通學校 학생의 동맹휴학은 표면상으로 보면 단순한 校長排斥運動이나 裏面을 자서히 보면 現制度의 改革運動이다. 즉 任校長의 不正選手加入事件은 一個 導火線에 불과하고, 평소에 학생이 同校制度에 대하야 불평불만이 多한 所以다. 원래 徽文高等普通學校는 閔泳徽씨의 단독경영이다. 閔씨가 同校에 대하야 다년간 막대의 金錢과 幾分의 誠力을 用한 것은 사실이다. 그러나 그는 본래 세력자인 동시에 有産者이다. 또는 구식 頑固派중에도 老獪人物이다. 그가 학교를 설립한 것은 일반의 公益을 위함보다도 자기의 명예를 위함이＜81＞ 多하고, 청년의 교육을 위함보다도 자기를 옹호하는 책략이 多하다. 학교를 신성한 교육하는 사회의 公器로 思치 안이하고, 營利를 목적하는 무슨 농장이나 회사로 思한다. 따러서 학교의 직원을 자기의 舍音이나 門客과 가티 보고, 학생을 一玩弄物로 思한다. 爲先 그 학교의 명칭부터 신성하게 짓지 못하ꊳ� 近日 소위 孝子烈女를 褒彰하는데 李烈徽贈이니 金孝徽賞이니 하는 醜態와 가티, 자기의 名을 標榜하기 위하야 徽文이라 하얏다.(勅命으로 命名한 것이나 시실은 其時運動한 것) 기본 재산을 내놋는데도 당당히 내놋치 못하고 자기의 望八壽年紀念名義下에 내놋코 (壬戌 6월에 자기 望八壽年紀念으로 현금 15만원, 韓一銀行株券 5만원, 平原安州兩地土地 140여만평을 기부함) 校舍를 증축하는데도 자기를 기념하기 위하야 사무실 門額에다 稀重堂이라 刻하얏다 (閔씨가 70에 重建하얏다는 의미). 또는 그의 소위 學生諭告文를 보면 嘔逆날 句節이 多하다. 그는 第一 학생에게 진정한 偉人이 되자면 實德 즉 論理道德을 行함에 잇다하고, 또 자기가 後生을 교육함에 20여년간 誠力을 殫竭하야 학생의 一善을 聞하면 喜不成眼하고 一惡을 聞하면 食不甘味라 云云하얏다. 물론 윤리와 도덕은 학생뿐 안이라, 누구에게던지 필요한 것이다. 그러나 閔씨로서 윤리도덕(如何히 재래의 倫綱이나마)을 主唱한다는 것은 얼마나 해괴한일일가, 마치 醉漢이 금주연설을 하고 매춘부가 정조를 논함과 갓다. 氏는 과연 재래 동양윤리중 제일 중요한 君臣有義를 실행하얏스며 사회민중에 대하야 如何한 公道公德을 行하얏는가. 학생의 一善一惡을 聞하면 참으로 食不甘味하고 寢不安席하얏는가. 올치 그의 주장하는 도덕윤리는 즉 자기에게 복종을 잘하고 節制를 잘 바드란 말이다. 학생이던지 직원이던지 또는 일반 無産者가 다 屈伏하란 뜻이다. 좀더 솔직히 말하면 그의 도덕은 진정한 도덕이 안이라 盜德이오, 윤리는 진정한 윤리가 안이라 綸利다. 食不甘味라 하는 것도 食前方丈이 넘우 배가 불너 못먹는단 말이오, 寢不安席이라는 것도 侍女美妾이 넘슚� 괴로이 굴어서 잘 못잔다는 말인 듯 하다.
이러한 여러 가지의 不神聖하고 허휘의 덩어리로 생긴 徽文高等普通學校가 엇지 제도인들 완전하랴, 즉 閔씨는 학생의 總長名義를 帶하고 재정은 물론이어니와, 일반 사무를 다 간섭하며, 심지어 교원 黜陟까지도 임의로 한다. 그럼으로 任校長은 비록 명의는 교장이라도 實權은 總長에게 잇다. 그나마 任校長이 果斷性이 풍부하고 자립심이 特有한 이 가트면, 비록 校長의 職을 辭하는境에＜82＞ 至할 지라도, 결코 직접으로 총장 又는 全閔族의 간섭을 受치 안이하고, 당연히 職權을 행사하야 학교행사와 閔씨 家事를 儼然區別하게 할 것이다. 그러나 任校長은 원래 寬厚仁慈하고 충실 근면할 뿐, 극히 柔弱한 人인 故로 此를 감수하고 今日까지 至하얏다. 물론 任校長도 其間에 고뇌와 번민이 多大하얏슬 것이다. 그를 世人은 비평하기를 無骨虫이니 木偶人이니하지마는 그는 결코 교장의 지위와 영예를 탐하야 今日까지 잇는 것은 안이다. 조곰 굴복하는 일이 잇슬지라도 다만 인내하고 교육만 하면 효과가 有할 줄로만 思한 것이다. 맛치 농부가 똥내를 맛더라도 施肥만 하면 농사가 잘될 줄로 밋는 것과 갓텃다. 그리고 학교에는 소위 幹部制度가 잇다. 즉 교장 任璟宰씨, 學監 張膺震씨, 校監 閔廣植씨로 조직한 三頭政治비슷한 제도가 잇서, 校內 一般事를 다 이 三巨頭뿐으로 처결하고, 기타 직원은 (專任敎員 24, 囑托敎員6人) 교무에 대하야 何等의 권리가 업다. 그럼으로 교무가 항상 통일이 되지 못하고 학생의 訓練方法이 또한 일치치 못하야 교육상 弊害가 多大하다. 如斯히 徽文高等普通學校는 一個 세력단체로 化한 故로 무세력자는 자연 양심에 不許하는 굴복, 아첨, 附隨의 醜態를 연출하게 되얏다. 즉  幹部는 總長또는 總長家族, 門客에 까지 아첨, 굴복, 附隨를 하고, 교원은 학교 간부와 總長家에 2重의 迎合的 태도를 持치 안이치 못하게 되얏다.
