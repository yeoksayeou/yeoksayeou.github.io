**Body Text Translation:**
Regarding the Publication of the Deluding-the-World-and-Deceiving-the-People *Chŏng Kamnok* (鄭堪錄)

They will do anything if it means making money. They will do anything as long as the Police Affairs Bureau (警務廳) doesn't forbid it. What need is there to know the interests of the entire Chosŏn people? As long as I get fat first, that's all that matters. If people with this kind of mentality are openly carrying out such activities in today's society, what attitude must we take towards them?

Recently, the so-called *Chŏng Kamnok*, a text of empty absurdity, has been openly published in the world, and the Japanese and Korean moneygrubbers (日鮮의 돈버리꾼들) who published it are making a fuss, advertising it day after day. In general, documents such as the *Chŏng Kamnok* are nothing more than things spread by those belonging to special classes in ancient times as a scheme to maintain and expand their own power through such fatalistic observations. Considering that while Japan underwent the Restoration (維新) and China had its revolution (革命), the masses of Chosŏn alone became prisoners (拘囚者) of such absurd legends and failed to advance and stand on their own, it is truly dumbfounding even to think about in one's dreams. And now those scoundrels bent on profit (謀利의 徒輩) are publishing and selling it off?<43>

**Summary**

The article vehemently criticizes the recent publication and promotion of the *Chŏng Kamnok*, a traditional Korean book of prophecy, dismissing it as superstitious nonsense spread by greedy publishers ("Japanese and Korean moneygrubbers") solely for profit. The author laments that while Japan and China modernized, Koreans remained trapped by such "absurd legends," hindering national progress (self-advancement and self-reliance). This critique highlights the frustration among some Korean intellectuals during the colonial period with what they saw as backward-looking superstitions preventing national self-strengthening and enlightenment, especially in contrast to neighboring countries' modernization efforts. Historians might find this article interesting for its insight into the internal cultural debates within colonial Korea, the clash between tradition/superstition and modernity/nationalism, and the specific targeting of the *Chŏng Kamnok* as a symbol of stagnation.

惑世誣民의 鄭堪錄 發行에 對하야
돈만 생길 일이면 무엇이라도 한다. 警務廳에서 금하지만 아느면 무엇이라도 한다. 朝鮮人族 전체의 利害를 알 것이 무엇이냐. 爲先 나만 살젓스면 그만이지. 이러한 심리를 가진 사람이 이러한 행사를 금일의 現 사회에서 공공연히 하고 잇다 하면 우리는 그에 대하야 반듯이 엇더한 태도를 취하여야 可할가.
근래에 소위 鄭堪錄이라는 虛無荒誕의 글이 공연히 世에 발포되며 그를 발행한 日鮮의 돈버리꾼들은 연일 광고를 해가면셔 야단법석을 하고 잇도다. 盖 鄭堪錄 등의 書類는 古昔의 특수계급에 속한 그 者들이 自家의 세력을 부지 확장하자는 술책으로써 그러한 운명적 관찰을 유포한 者에 불과한 바라. 일본이 維新하고 중국이 혁명하는 그간에 잇서 유독 조선의 대중이 그 따위의 허황한 전설의 拘囚者가 되야 自進自立하지 못한 그것을 생각하면 꿈에 생각하야도 기가 맥히는 일이어늘 저 謀利의 徒輩가 이제 또 그것을 出版競賣한단 말인가.＜43＞
