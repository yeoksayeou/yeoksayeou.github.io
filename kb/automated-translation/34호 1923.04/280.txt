There are legends circulating that in communist Russia (露西亞), a system of 'wife sharing' (婦人共有) is practiced, which can tempt people's minds. However, the reality is quite different. Now, if we excerpt the modern (1919) marriage laws of Russia (露國):

(1) The age of marriage is 16 for girls and 18 for men,
(2) Each person marries only one person; that is, bigamy (重婚) is not permitted,
(3) Marriage is not hindered by relatives or religion, etc.; that is, things like parental authority (親權) are not recognized,
(4) A second marriage cannot occur unless the first marriage has been dissolved,
(5) Divorce can be initiated by the wish of one spouse, but the other party can file a lawsuit,
(6) Becoming a Russian citizen cannot be achieved through marriage. For example, even if a non-Russian woman marries a Russian man, she cannot become a Russian citizen solely through that marriage,
(7) For marriage, it is not necessary for one party to move their residence to the other's, nor is it necessary to pool property,
(8) If one spouse is unable to support themselves and the other spouse is able to provide support, they must do so,
(9) An unmarried mother (a mother with an illegitimate child - 私生者) can receive living support from the child's father; if there are multiple fathers, the burden on the woman is shared by each man involved,
(10) Children do not inherit their parents' property.

Reportedly, women in Russia (露西亞) today are extremely free in the spousal relationship. This is because women there have the right and duty (權義) to engage in social labor from age 16 to 50, thus eliminating the social and economic basis for male dominance. Therefore, marriage there amounts to nothing more than a kind of private contract concluded between two citizens (公民).<74>

**Summary**

This article aims to dispel rumors about "wife sharing" in Soviet Russia by outlining the actual marriage laws enacted in 1919. It details regulations concerning marriage age, monogamy, freedom from familial/religious constraints, divorce procedures, citizenship, property independence, spousal support, rights of unmarried mothers, and the abolition of inheritance. The text concludes that Russian women possess significant freedom within marriage due primarily to their right and duty to participate in social labor, which undermines traditional male dominance and reduces marriage to a private contract between citizens. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects the engagement of Korean intellectuals with radical new ideologies like communism and their specific interest in the Soviet model's approach to social structures like marriage and women's rights, which starkly contrasted with traditional Korean Confucian norms. It shows an attempt to present factual information about the Soviet Union, possibly to counter negative propaganda or simply to inform readers about alternative social systems during a period of national crisis and searching for new paths.

공산주의의 露西亞에서는 「婦人共有」의 制를 취한다는 등의 전설이 行하야 사람의 마음을 유혹케 함도 잇다. 그러나 사실은 만히 그러치 아니함이 잇스니 이제 露國現代(1919년)의 결혼법을 摘示하면 (1)결혼연령은 소녀는 16 남자는 18세, (2)各人은 다못 한사람과 결혼한다, 즉 重婚을 許치 아니한다, (3)친척 또는 종교 등을 인하야 지장을 밧지 아니한다, 즉 親權 가튼 것은 인정치 아는다, (4)제2의 결혼은 제1의 결혼이 폐지한 후가 아니면 못된다, (5)배우자되는 한편의 희망으로써 이혼할 수 잇다, 그러나 다른 한편에서 소송을 제기할 수가 잇다, (6)露國民이 되는 것은 결혼에 의해서는 되지 못한다, 例하면 露國人이 아닌 다른 여자가 露國人과 결혼한다 할지라도 결혼 그것으로써 露國人이 될 수는 업다, (7)결혼을 위하야 한편이 다른 한편에 주거를 옴길 필요가 업스며 또 재산을 協同할 필요가 업다, (8)만일 배우자의 한편이 생활키 不能하고 다른 한편이 그 생활을 보조할 수 잇스면 그리 보조하여야 한다, (9)결혼치 아니한 母(私生者를 가진 母)는 그 子의 父로부터 생활의 보호를 바들 수 잇스며 其父가 多數되는 때는 그 女의 負担은 각 관계남자가 分担한다, (10)兒女는 부모의 재산을 상속하지 못한다. 듯건대 今日 露西亞의 婦人은 부부의 관계에 잇서 심히 자유이다. 그것은 그곳의 婦人은 16세로부터 50세까지 사회적 노동에 就하는 權義가 잇슴으로 인하야 남성지배의 사회적 내지 경제적의 기초가 업서진 故이다. 따라서 그곳의 결혼은 두 사람의 公民 간에 체결되는 일종의 사적 계약의 성질을 가짐에 지내지 못한 것이다.＜74＞
