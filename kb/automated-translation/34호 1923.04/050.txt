Magazine Title	開闢 제34호
Publication Date	1923년 04월 01일
Article Title	海內海外의 同志에게 (To Comrades at Home and Abroad)
Author	北旅東谷
Article Type	논설 (Editorial)

To Comrades at Home and Abroad
						Pungnyŏ Tonggok

Comrades! We refer to those brothers standing on the same path for our cause, the regeneration movement of the Korean people, as comrades. Therefore, all who are Korean must strive for the cause of Koreans, that is, the cause of the self. Thus, I am not addressing these words to just a few comrades, but this is a letter from one comrade, a Korean, to all comrades, all Koreans.

Comrades! I truly wanted to offer much. I was prepared not to hesitate even in contributing this one life in the end. Thus, I wished to speedily achieve the work that all our Korean comrades commonly desire and present it before you, Comrades. This must be what all our comrades mutually feel and yearn for. Therefore, the greatest thing we can joyfully give and receive from each other is to confess how things stand regarding our cause. Thus, with the greatest meaning and the greatest emotion, I deeply considered offering these words. As we cannot but lament the swift passage of years, one year, two years, during the not inconsiderable past time we have fought fiercely and struggled bitterly for our cause, furthermore, amidst the rapid turn of events and the stirring of conscience, I have come to present these words before all our Korean comrades. I believe that upon reaching the year Ŭi-sin-jŏng-wŏn Kyehae [癸亥 corresponds to 1923], I have certainly not missed the opportune moment (正機). Last year, while lying low in the northern region (北陸), keeping company with the lone lamp of my lodging at year's end, I wrote this text as great preparation to greet the new year of Kyehae, intending to publish it in the New Year's issue of Kaebyŏk magazine. Truly, as they say, "ten things, eight or nine go contrary to one's wishes" (不如意者ㅣ 十常八九), even this task, which was not the most difficult, <14> could not be accomplished as intended, and being pushed back and pushed back, it is finally published in this journal issued on April 1st of this year.

Comrades! It goes without saying now what suffering we have already received. However, the suffering, agony, and doubts (疑難) experienced while directly engaging in the work truly seem to increase as we go. If we just put forth the single phrase, "What should we do about our cause?", it seems to express the entirety of our anguish. Here, we must overcome the difficulties that are inevitably necessary to pass through for the sake of the cause. While it is not untrue that external hindrances have constituted the entirety of these difficulties during that time, we cannot know how much the degree of difficulty has been increased by our own mistakes and self-inflicted troubles (自作된 孼). Moreover, although the current disintegration (支離滅裂) and strife and jealousy (鬪爭嫉妬) of society are unavoidable, they are the great evils of a society trying to be reborn (新生) and great obstacles to progress. How much more bitter and painful is our anguish because of this? Although one might say this is all the unavoidable pain of difficult childbirth for rebirth, who can fail to be greatly concerned about this? Besides this, the lack of firmness in one's own spirit and the deficiency (消乏) of courage to risk everything on one throw (乾坤一擲的 용기) increase the degree of hesitation and indecision (猶豫), leading, in severe cases, to abandoning the righteous path (正路) of the cause to seek narrow paths of temporary ease (苟安), and finally, circumstances arise where one only awaits the bitter plight of awaiting death (待死). Can we not but call this our point of affliction (傷氣處)? Truly, if we were to pour out our unspeakable discontent and pent-up resentment (憤鬱), would it not be boundless?

But what can be done? We can only say they are the inevitable difficulties of a transitional period (과도기). If we keenly realize (痛覺) that all this is sin and hindrance against the cause, is there any other way than to keenly repent (痛切히 悔悟), ceaselessly strive with unremitting effort, and advance straight ahead (一直히 驀進)? We must not just leave our cause as it is; we must strive to the end and achieve it. Therefore, we must devise new methods, realize new decisions (斷案), quickly stand on the right track (正軌) of the cause, and focus solely on the execution of the cause. I, too, am one who possesses the many sentiments (感念) mentioned above and sometimes suffers such anguish. Not only that, but I have also not been without moments of harboring much pessimism. <15> Therefore, it is natural that we all share such concerns, but it is only by establishing countermeasures that we can prepare for all possibilities.

Does not all this give us trials and experiences? Based on these trials and experiences, we must make resolutions, grasp the true face (眞面) of the work directly, become workers truly worthy of the name, and carry out the work as it should be done. We can say that we have already become workers tested to a certain degree and have become capable enough to handle the work. From here, we must sum up the past and present (過現을 總打算) and truly engage in the work, beginning the direct movement. Therefore, I speak of

**The振作 (Chinjag - rousing/invigoration) of New Spirit (新精神)**

Our spirit is already firm and always new, so it may seem unnecessary to call for the invigoration of a new spirit. However, during our past work, we often saw that the spirit was not consistent, and the resulting hindrance to the cause was indeed great. There is no need to cite examples, but instead of focusing our spirit on the right track of the cause we intended, we were bound by policies of temporary ease (一時的 苟安). While cultural movements, economic movements, and ideological movements (主義運動) within the regeneration movement (更生運動) cannot but be called parts of the regeneration movement, it cannot be said that complete main force (主力) has been put into the regeneration movement itself. Therefore, placing the entirety of our spirit directly there and advancing together—this is what I mean by the invigoration of new spirit. From now on, let all our Korean comrades place the invigoration of the new spirit right there and make the final leap (躍動) at that critical juncture (機際).

The matter of our spirit is truly great. One might think it commonplace that any Korean would naturally focus their spirit on the regeneration of Koreans, <16> but as experienced over the years, there were many pseudo-regeneration movements (사이비 更生運動) and many plausible but uncertain (似而不確) regeneration movements. While these did provide some support to the direct regeneration movement itself, on the other hand, they also caused much harm. Even without speaking more vividly, you all likely know that even among those claiming to conduct the direct regeneration movement, quite a few instances (實로 不尠) of thoroughly wicked deeds (大不徹底한 惡跡) have already been exposed to the world. Truly, if we leave our spirit in a muddled state, my heart sometimes trembles with the fear (疑懼) that its ultimate outcome (究竟) might be to suffer again the legendary disgrace of the past Koryŏ and Yi dynasties (麗李朝). Therefore, deeply, deeply reflecting (明鑑) on this, we must place our spirit on the right track of regeneration, and anything deviating even slightly (一毫라도 差가 잇는 것) must absolutely not be permitted for any reason. In this way, we must quickly advance and commence actions invigorating the new spirit and reawaken the spirit that is decaying. Next is,

**Build a National Central Body (民族的 中心團軆) through National Grand Unity (民族的 大同一致)**

Truly, what we feel most acutely when it comes to doing the work, more than anything else, is the power (力) of organization (단체). It is not that we were unaware of the consciousness of purely organizational life in the past, but even looking through the lens of historical legend, it is no exaggeration to say that we not only neglected organizational life but also did not know its urgent necessity. It is certain, of course, that the work can only be done through the power of organization. Have we not deeply felt this traditionally? We must now concentrate our entire main force (全主力) here. Because we still lack the organizational power of a national center, all other efforts end up in vain (수포로 돌아가고 맙니다). And so, we suffer from a great malady. First, there is the fervor of regional cliques (地方黨的 熱) and other minor factions (小小의 偏派)! This sounds simple, but is it not what pains and angers our comrades on the front lines the most? Also, jealousy, exclusionism (排他), struggle (鬪爭), and slander (陰害)—are these not the evil phenomena (惡現象) flowing as an undercurrent (暗流) in our society today? <17> One might say these are the unavoidable sins of a transitional period, the residual progeny (餘孶) of Yi Dynasty factional strife (李朝黨爭). However, they are all evil fruits (惡果) born from the absence of a central organization. Furthermore, at a time when the desire to build a national central organization, deeply yearning day by day for rebirth (新生), pervades the hearts of the entire populace, the state of disintegration (지리멸렬의 상태) appearing due to deviation into superficial new ideologies (皮相的 新思潮), crying "individual rebellion" (個人反逆) or "exaltation of the self" (自我의 高調), is truly cause for deep concern (深憂). Moreover, mutual respect (互相의 崇仰) and mutual trust (互相의 신임) are extremely weak, and because everyone sets themselves as the standard for all matters, all endeavors in society are fragmented (片片히 분리) and cannot be concentrated. Because each person has the mentality of being the great generalissimo (爲大將軍) and isolates themselves, trustworthy leaders cannot emerge in society. Thus, the concept of "organization" (단체) feels entirely absent from our society. If, then, our cause is necessarily possible only through the power of a national central organization, is there not a need to quickly escape the current evil boundary line and first cultivate the organizational power that is the essential element of that cause? Thus, we must absolutely obey the commands and guidance of that central organization, stand on the identical boundary line of the work, and perform the tasks assigned under the guidance of the commonly trusted leader. At this time, when new ideologies (新思潮) are rushing forward so much, saying such things might be dismissed as the drunken ramblings (醉語) of someone lagging behind the times. However, the work does not lie in the empty exaltation of rhetoric (虛文의 高調); I believe the truth of the work is manifested precisely in doing things that way. From the aforementioned invigoration of the new spirit, we must firmly build the national central organization.

And the final point I wish to make is, **Let us stand on the Right Track (正軌) of the Direct Movement (直接運動) through Unified Execution (一致의 實行)** <18>

Now, based on what we have already experienced in the field of work, I believe we have gained many feelings and trials. Not just now, but always, until our work reaches its end, our spirit must be thorough, and it must be renewed. Under that spirit, we must cultivate the power of a central organization and carry out the work. Does this not deeply resonate (共鳴) with all of our comrades? If this is indeed the experience we have deeply felt and gained from the front lines of work thus far, and if it is what must necessarily be done for the sake of the cause, then now, all that remains is unified execution. This is by no means said lightly. Whenever we face the work, it is true that our ideals or plans are often insufficient, but more than anything, the common flaw (결함) in our society is the power of execution (實行의 力). Moreover, not only do we fail to always focus on and strive towards the work to be executed, persevering to the very end, but we sometimes stumble into narrow paths (狹路), become bound by minor views (小觀), and fail to achieve unity (一致). One might say this is because what is seen is originally uncertain, but it could also be said that we cannot endure (堪能) the labor of braving difficulties (冒難). It might also be because we have withered too much under the hindrances of the surroundings and the pressure of the environment, losing much courage. However, if one tries to realize a subjective goal even once, such difficulties will surely exist, but isn't it true that the work cannot be done without being based on reality (實地) and executed? Therefore, having already set the objective and discovered the path forward, there should be no further deliberation or hesitation; all that remains is to execute. If, at this point, there is even the slightest deviation, sidestepping, or weakening (軟化), the execution of the targeted work will entirely lose its true meaning (眞義). All that remains is to execute straight ahead (一直히) on the right track of that work, without looking left or right, back or forth. Although we have always declared that we will not forget the goal already proclaimed, haven't some segments tried various methods in search of an easier alternative path? Now, truly, as the saying goes, "losing one's step while imitating the Handan walk" (邯鄲失步), I am saying this emphatically at a point where there is a not insignificant feeling that all those attempts have resulted in failure. Even if there is an easy alternative path in a narrow passage <19> away from the right track, one must definitely stand on the right track to bring about any kind of fruition (結實) in the end (究竟).

From now on, we have no choice but to get on the right track of the direct movement. This is certainly necessary for the sake of the cause. Moreover, as a measure for correcting the various ills (諸般救弊) of our current society, I wish to state this even more urgently. I will not deliberately expose the various evil phenomena flowing as an undercurrent within our society itself. Doesn't everyone think of them first when worrying about the cause? Such things must surely be voluntarily rooted out (낏틀이며) and thoroughly cleansed (廓淸). Furthermore, the general threat (脅威) and fear (공포) to life lately are almost like the final moments of a dying life being hastened moment by moment (刻刻으로 催促). This dangerous state, approaching the very end, seems like it will wither and wither again, like it will be trampled and trampled again—only fear remains. What shall we do here? I believe there is no choice but to stand on the right track of the direct movement. By doing so, the various evil phenomena within society itself that worry us so much will be cleansed from their roots, and then, whatever the outcome regarding the fear before our eyes, it will emerge. Without this, we will only sit idly in the current situation. I believe this moment is definitely one where we should rise up (奮起). Therefore, I thought that by realizing this starting this year, the budding sprout (엄) would not wither again.

Comrades! I cannot say all that I wish to convey. Moreover, I cannot reveal everything (暴白). If only to give a thorough cleansing to the stagnating society, if only to give stability to the state of life withering under threat, please just clearly understand (明諒) that we must promote the execution of the direct movement from this very moment. This must be the common yearning (渴求) arising from the emotions crying out in suffering and the resolution springing from conscience.

Comrades! This is a confession from my heart (衷心). May there be resonance (共鳴) with this. <20>

**Summary**

Writing in April 1923 for *Kaebyŏk* magazine, the author "Pungnyŏ Tonggok" addresses fellow Korean "comrades" at home and abroad, lamenting the slow progress and internal problems plaguing the Korean "regeneration movement" (likely the independence or nationalist movement). The author identifies key issues: lack of consistent spirit, distraction by partial measures (like purely cultural or economic movements), pervasive factionalism, jealousy, mistrust reminiscent of Yi Dynasty strife, and a crippling lack of unified action and leadership. The author passionately argues for three urgent necessities: 1) Invigorating a "New Spirit" solely focused on the primary goal of national regeneration, 2) Achieving "National Grand Unity" to build a powerful "National Central Body" (organization) demanding absolute loyalty, and 3) Embarking on "Direct Action" along the movement's "Right Track," abandoning hesitation and alternative paths. The author sees this unified, direct action as the only way to overcome internal decay, counter external threats, and achieve the movement's ultimate objective. The piece reflects the frustrations and strategic debates within the Korean nationalist movement in the early 1920s, particularly the tension between gradualist/cultural approaches and calls for more direct, unified political action. Historians might find the explicit critique of internal division (regionalism, factionalism, mistrust), the call for a single unifying organization with strong leadership demanding obedience, and the dismissal of solely cultural/economic efforts as potentially diluting the core "regeneration" goal particularly revealing about the state and mindset of activists during this period.

海內海外의 同志에게
						北旅東谷
동지여! 우리는 우리의 일 곳 조선인의 갱생운동을 위하야 한 진로에 서잇는 형제를 가르처 동지라 하나이다. 그러면 조선인된 자는 다 가티 조선인의 일 곳 자아의 일을 위하야 반듯이 노력할 것이외다. 그럼으로 저는 幾個의 동지에게 이 글월을 들이는 것이 아니라 동지인 한 조선인으로부터 동지인 전조선인에게 들이는 글이외다.
동지여! 저는 참으로 무엇을 만히 들이려고 햇습니다. 최후에 이 한 생명을 공헌함에도 사양치 안흐려 햇습니다. 그리하야 우리의 온 조선인 동지의 공통히 뜻하는 바 일을 속히 이루게 하야 諸同志의 압헤 내노핫스면 햇습니다. 이것은 우리의 온 동지의 피차의 동감되는 배요 갈구하는 바일 것이외다. 그러면 서로히 깃겁게 들이고 밧는 것이 오즉 우리 일에 대한 如何를 서로 고백함이 가장 큰 것이라 하겟습니다. 그럼으로 저는 가장 큰 의미를 가지고 가장 큰 감동을 가지고 이 글 들일 생각을 깁히 하얏섯습니다. 우리가 일을 위하야 惡戰苦鬪해 온 지 적지 안은 과거의 시간에 한 해 두 해의 세월이 빠름을 한탄치 안흘 수 업는 중에 더욱히 時勢의 急轉과 양심의 奮動에는 이 글월을 동지인 온 조선인의 압헤 내놋케 되얏슴니다. 저는 밋건댄 意在新元 癸亥의 년을 다달케 되는 때에 확실히 그 正機를 失치 아니하얏다고 함니다. 제가 去年에 北陸에 蟄伏하야 歲暮旅邸의 孤燈을 伴하야 癸亥의 신년을 마즐 큰 준비로 이 글을 써 신년호 開闢誌上에 발표하려 하얏나이다. 참으로 세상만사가 不如意者ㅣ 十常八九라고 가장 힘드지 안는 이것이나마도＜14＞ 그 뜻대로 되지 못하고 밀우고 밀우다가 마지막 今歲의 4월 1일에 발행되는 同誌上에 비롯오 登載케 되나이다.
