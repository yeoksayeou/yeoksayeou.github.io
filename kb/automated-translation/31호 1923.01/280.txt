Article Title: 未知의 나라에 (To the Unknown Land)
Author: 春城
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

To the Unknown Land
						Ch'unsŏng

At the shore of the evening sky's sea
White sand is spread
Like the cheek of a maiden flushed by a "kiss"
The sweet ripples (漣) of tangerines (甘柑) dance.

Stretching my two feet out on the sand
I sit powerlessly and close my eyes
In my sea of the heart (心海) overflowing with sentiment
Scarlet waves of fire leap up.

At the far end of the horizon
Where the fire-waves boil up
Crimson (紫紅色) sunset glows are layered thickly
Through that glow, shining its sail/covering,<106>
Flowing silently
A single small boat (一葉扁舟)!
Ah! Where might it be going!

Let us flow without end!
To the unknown land (未知)
To seek that place filled with pink light
The hateful hypocrisy (虛僞) in this coarse world!
And, the place where wolves (狼) in sheep's clothing live.
Where discontent and ugliness squirm
Upon the dark land of "Sodom"
After setting a fire of curse.

Leaving the horizon of hypocrisy (虛僞) behind
Floating on the fire-water
The boat of longing (憧憬)
Until it plucks the flower buds of "Eden"
In the unknown land (未知) seen in dreams
It shall flow.
Ah! It shall flow without end.
(Upon seeing the sunset glow (落照) at the Sea of Inch'ŏn) <107>
<106-107>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unsŏng expresses a deep yearning for escape from a perceived corrupt and hypocritical reality ("Sodom") towards an idealized, unknown land ("Eden"). The speaker, observing a sunset over the sea at Inch'ŏn, watches a lone boat sailing into the fiery horizon and imagines it as a vessel of longing heading towards a pure, unknown destination. The poem uses vivid imagery of the sunset and sea to contrast the ugliness of the present world with the beauty of the desired future. For historians, the poem reflects a common sentiment of disillusionment and escapism potentially prevalent among Korean intellectuals during the colonial period. The strong critique of "hypocrisy," "wolves in sheep's clothing," and the desire to "set a fire of curse" on the current "dark land" could be interpreted as veiled criticism of the colonial situation or societal decay, expressing a desire for radical change or liberation represented by the journey to the "Unknown Land."

未知의 나라에
						春城
저녁 한울의 바다가에
흰 모래는 깔렷는데
「키쓰」에 붉어진 처녀의 뺨 가튼
甘柑의 잔물(漣)은 춤을 춘다.

모래 우에 두 발을 뻐치고
힘업시 안저 눈을 감으니
感傷에 넘친 내 마음 바다(心海)에는
새빨간 불물결 뛰어오른다.

불물결 끌허오르는
수평선 저 끗에는
紫紅色 노을이 겹겹히 둘렷다
그 노을 가으로, 면장을 빗내며＜106＞
말업시 흘러가는
一葉扁舟여!
아! 어대로 가랴나!

끗업시 흐르자!
未知의 나라로
분홍빗 가득한 그곳을 차지려
거츠른 세상에 얄미운 虛僞!
그리고, 羊의 옷 입은 일희(狼)가 사는 곳.
불평과 추악이 꿈틀거리는
시컴한 「소돔」의 따 우에
저주의 불을 살라 노코서.

虛僞의 수평선 뒤로 두고
불물에 떠가는
憧憬의 배는
꿈으로 바라본 未知의 나라에
「에덴」의 꼿봉오리 꺽글 때까지
흐르리라.
아! 끗업시 흐르리라.
(仁川海에서 落照를 보고)＜107＞
＜106-107＞
