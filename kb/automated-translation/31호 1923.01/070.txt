Article Title:	新年의 新意見 (New Opinions for the New Year)
Author:	趙喆鎬 (Cho Ch'ŏl-ho), 曹晩植 (Cho Man-sik), 在中國 太空 (T'ae Kong in China), 金永耈 (Kim Yŏng-gu), 魯基禎 (No Ki-jŏng), 廉台振 (Yŏm T'ae-jin), 李範昇 (Yi Pŏm-sŭng), 李昇薰 (Yi Sŭng-hun)
Article Type:	설문 (Survey/Questionnaire)

New Opinions for the New Year
In the truest sense, we established this column to seek the New Year opinions of various friends working silently in different fields. However, it did not necessarily turn out as intended. If you read these words not as mere customary hopes but with the same sincere heart as those who expressed these opinions, we hope our small effort will not have been in vain.

Youth Corps! Chosŏn Boy Scouts – Starting from Human Reform First
Cho Ch'ŏl-ho

This civilization of today, where form is almighty,
Is a triangular tower of the Mammonist influence.
The masquerade of jealousy and envy
Is the conduct of today’s great men and gentlemen.

Pitiful, OOO our brethren!
Though with mouths they cry for freedom,
And with brushes they well depict humanity,
Empty theories, empty writings – all are mere formality.

To save this chaotic world,
The global servant, the Boy Scout,
The day it first began in Kyerim (鷄林 - Silla/Korea)
Was October 5th, 1922. <83>

This song is a verse sung during the marches of the Chosŏn Boy Scouts. I have no other thoughts. My only wish is that the Chosŏn Boy Scout (Boy Army) movement, started just three months ago, will arise throughout all of Chosŏn during the year Kyhae (癸亥 - 1923). If only that happens, I would have no regrets even in death. The reason I so fervently pray with all my heart and strength for the rise of this movement is none other than this: to start first with the reform of the human being itself, and thereby declare war and charge against all the empty formalities and evil customs of this society. To achieve this, we must first begin with the reform of boys, who are the beginning of people, and have them acquire the most appropriate self-awareness and trials for the sake of society and themselves.
Chosŏn Boy Scouts! The statement of purpose explaining its general outline and the Corps Regulations (團則) have all been printed. To those who request them, I will bow and offer them.

We must defend our Hometowns to the death. – Each must perform the Foundation Ceremony for a New Chosŏn
Cho Man-sik

Among us who claim to be doing something, such things happen. That is, even if one makes this or that fuss in the place where one was born and raised, or even if not born and raised there, in a place somewhat familiar, if things do not go as planned, one immediately abandons that place and flees to another region. This is definitely wrong. If a person connected to a region abandons it, who else will work for that region? And even from the perspective of the person working, if they abandon their own region where the ground (地井) is relatively prepared, which region would be more convenient for them to work in? Brethren, the regions where you were born and grew up (生斯長斯) are becoming desolate day by day. Is the place you are now Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul)? Do not just gather there and murmur. Or is it Taegu (大邱) or perhaps P'yŏngyang (平壤)? Do not wander about aimlessly. Let each of us resolve to protect our own hometowns. Let us try protecting them to the death. Thus, let us, the people called Chosŏn, build the foundations (根基) upon which each of us can rely and stand.
Return to your regions, and thereby let each perform the Foundation Ceremony (定礎式) for a New Chosŏn.
I wish to make this one request once more. <84>

New Hopes for the New Year
T'ae Kong (太空), in China

The years flow like water, the days gone seem many! For a man of ambition (有爲男兒), what are the thoughts? Furthermore, in this past year, how much creativity was there? How much construction? And how much human evolution? Reflecting on these categories, one cannot help but feel dejected as if bereaved (嗒然若喪), lost as if something is missing (*然若失). From this perspective, there is no time even for mourning, so what heart is there for celebration? Moreover, since the end of the European War, ways of thinking have greatly changed (思潮丕變), and the voice of national self-determination broke through the void, resounding through the heavens (響徹雲衢). (Following 2 lines deleted - National Institute of Korean History) Then, raising glasses and drinking toasts today (稱觴擧酒) should truly be joyful (洵可樂也). Yet, since the March First Movement (『三一』), though those before have fallen, those after do not follow; the white bones of our brothers within and without are already cold, the trees on their graves have grown tall (墓木已拱), yet our dear and beloved compatriots sink deeper into water, burn hotter in fire, writhing and wailing pitifully (宛轉哀號) under ― (Following 1 line deleted - National Institute of Korean History), recalling the past, how painful is it?
The sun and moon have risen, yet the torchlight (爝火) is not extinguished; whose fault (*) is this? (Following 3 lines deleted - National Institute of Korean History) to fulfill our long-held aspirations, this is truly the new hope in the New Year, and also our one and only heavenly duty (天職)!
O, ambitious men (有爲之男兒)? The fleetingness of time (駒光之駛), the years do not wait for us (歲不我與), will you strive anxiously (岌岌皇皇) to fulfill the heavenly duty of humankind? Or will you drift along with the mundane, enduring unbearable suffering? I know that ambitious men must surely be those who slap their thighs in lament (撫髀生歎) and rise to dance upon hearing the rooster crow (聞雞起舞).
Mountains multiply, rivers twist, suspecting no path ahead (山重水複疑無路), yet willow shade, flower brightness, another village appears (抑*花明文一村). Do not say the current situation cannot be overturned! Do not say the fences cannot be broken through! Strive forward! Unyielding through a hundred setbacks (百折不屈), there will finally be a day to escape the fence and the sea of suffering.

There must arise sincere researchers, that is, Scholars
Kim Yŏng-gu

Firstly, I earnestly desire (懇願) that sincere researchers – that is, scholars (學者) – emerge from among our brethren. It may not be entirely true for everyone, but among the discussions (運위하는 거긔) of our brethren, there often lacks a firm, unwavering basis (確乎不勤할 근거) rooted in one's unique philosophy. Mostly, it is driven by the circumstances of the moment and emotional impulses, leading to doing this and that. Consequently, what is done in the morning is abolished by evening (朝行하고 暮廢도 하며), diligence at the start turns into negligence at the end (始勤하고 終怠도 하며), and what starts like a dragon's head ends like a snake's tail (龍頭이다가 蛇尾도 됩니다). What is the reason for all this? It is because the person initiating (運위하는) <85> all these actions lacks a philosophical foundation for their own principles and assertions, deciding easily and trying to implement easily (쉽게 작정하고 쉽게 실시하려는 탓).
Let people who study like scholars (學究) emerge in every field of society, even if just a few. Let them first instill a conviction weightier than the Nine Tripods (重於九鼎) regarding all the things that numerous brethren are doing this way and that, enable them to possess a life consciousness clear as observing fire (明若觀火), and let them exude inspiration overflowing the four limbs (溢於四體의 인스피레손).

Earnestly Hope for the Rise of the Rural Enlightenment Movement
No Ki-jŏng

No matter how precious or important things are said to be in this world, if the two characters 'survival' (生存) are erased, what else could be precious or important? Therefore, in seeking the path of survival, regardless of the highness or lowness of the means or the goodness or badness of the policy, I believe it is sufficient just to achieve the goal.
However, the fate of our twenty million people now truly faces a perilous and desperate situation (地頭). Setting aside all other matters, we must wipe our eyes and clear our minds to see which direction our path of survival lies.
From ancient times, we have considered agriculture as the Great Foundation (大本), and spring plowing and autumn harvest (*) were our Way of Life (生道), and plowing by day and reading by night (朝畊暮讀) was our life (生涯). But now, our farming villages decline day by day, the state of agriculture (農形) withers day by day (萎靡하야), and our single path of survival becomes extremely dim. Furthermore, to quench the temporary thirst for finance, limited land is recklessly mortgaged or sold (典執賣却함으로) to foreigners (外人). Thus, today, a fraction (幾分) of the total land out of ten parts is lost, and tomorrow another fraction of the total land out of ten parts is lost, gradually increasing the numerator's (分子) share. If this phenomenon continues, eventually the denominator (分母) of ten will disappear, and our 4.3 million-plus chŏngbo (정보 - unit of area) of cultivated land (耕作地) will become entirely (全一의 數) the possession of others. I do not know about the future, but for us currently, lacking development in crafts (工藝) like others and lacking commercial skills, our only weapon for survival in this world is agriculture. If farming villages decline like this and land shrinks like this, how will the lives of our twenty million people be preserved in the future? Jesus (예수) said, "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God (한우님)." However, now we must live by bread alone.
Politics, law, literature, philosophy, art, religion – which of these is not necessary <86> for our society? But for us, who lack clothes to wear and food to eat, whose lives are in peril (幾危), cultivation, irrigation, sericulture, and animal husbandry are even more necessary.
In my heart, I wish that various institutions like newspapers, magazines, schools, and companies would increase daily and monthly, and that great figures and accomplished scholars (偉人達士) in various fields like scholars (學士), doctors (博士), politicians, and writers would become as common as cartloads and bushels (車載斗量). However, with such difficulties in livelihood, how can newspapers, magazines, or schools be managed? And even if they were managed, who would subscribe to newspapers and magazines, and who would attend schools to learn? For the past couple of years, it seems there has been quite a commotion in schools both in the capital and the provinces, unable to accommodate all students. But if things continue like this for a few more years, it is clear as observing fire (明若觀火) that the number of students studying in Kyŏngsŏng, where annual expenses do not exceed 500-600 won, will also greatly decrease.
With circumstances being such, our upper class and intellectual class still wander only in idealistic and spiritual realms, generally neglecting practical matters like the rural problems.
However, as mentioned above, agriculture is not only our Way of Life (生途), but the numerous farming brethren also constitute the majority of our society. Therefore, if our society is to become enlightened (開明), those brethren must become enlightened; if it is to prosper (繁榮), those brethren must prosper. But what is the condition of those brethren today? Anyone can see that they are still buried in a dark world (暗黑乾坤).
Therefore, I say here: from now on, let us, the intellectual class, the gentlemanly clique (紳士閥), not be intoxicated only by the smoke and dust (煙塵) of the cities under that status and reputation, but let us each spread out to the farming villages. It is our most urgent task to, on the one hand, promote our primary occupation, and on the other hand, vigorously support (力援) and enlighten (啓導) our farming brethren.
Now, facing the New Year, I earnestly wish and pray: starting from the New Year, let this movement arise among us. Let it arise individually and collectively, let it arise in public opinion and in practical action.
October 22, 1922.

Let's Find a Way to Exchange New Year's Greetings Again Next Year
Yŏm T'ae-jin

If we do things to survive, the New Year is a joyful New Year; if we cannot, the New Year is a New Year for wailing (慟哭). Compatriots! <87> For 59 years since the last Kapcha (甲子) year, we have faced New Years for wailing. The ttŏkkuk (떡국 - rice cake soup) we ate was ttŏkkuk of ill omen, and the New Year's bows (歲拜) we made were bows of condolence (吊喪). I wish that in this Kyhae (癸亥) year, which is the last year of the past Kapcha cycle, we eat ttŏkkuk of repentance (悔改) and make bows of determination (決心), so that in the coming new Kapcha year, we may eat ttŏkkuk of good omen (吉兆) and make bows of celebration (慶賀).
Compatriots! When you eat Kyhae year ttŏkkuk, let's greet each other like this:
"Compatriot! We ate this year's ttŏkkuk, but what about next year?"
"That's right. We ate this year's ttŏkkuk, but it will be easy not to eat next year's ttŏkkuk. Do we farm because we have fields? Do we trade because we have money?"
And when making the Kyhae year New Year's bows, let's greet each other like this:
"Compatriot! This year, let's risk death and find a way (道理) to live."
"Right! This year, let's risk death and find a way to live. Let's find a way not to starve to death. If we find a way to live within this year, let's meet next year; if not, let's never exchange New Year's greetings again, forever."
Ah, compatriots! What is the way to live? There is only one path, only one. The same.
"Eat what we make with our own hands,
"Wear what we make with our own hands,
"Use what we make with our own hands,
"Let's hold onto the land tightly and not let go."
This is all there is.
Then, what is my New Year's hope?
It is for the 20 million Chosŏn compatriots to become members of a Self-Producers' Association (自作會員), "unitedly use only Chosŏn products and not use imported goods," and "form a large-scale industrial cooperative (産業組合) similar to the British guilds (영국의 낄드식) capable of producing and supplying Chosŏn goods for the food, clothing, and use of Chosŏn people, making it an organ for production and consumption throughout Chosŏn."

Let's Not Seek Glory and Fame
Yi Pŏm-sŭng

As the one yang (一陽 - positive principle) arrives, the three springs (三春 - springtime) are not far. Just imagining the splendid scenery (韶光) when myriad flowers bloom brilliantly (萬花爛漫) naturally evokes uncontrollable excitement.
However, lifting my eyes to behold the utterly desolate (滿目蕭條) flowers and plants (花卉), the leaves <88> have fallen, the twigs are almost dry, and the splendid appearance (華容) of former days has faded to faint traces, nowhere to be found. Yet, the north wind (朔風) flaunts its stern authority (嚴威), and the piled snow (積雪) exercises tyranny (專橫).
Ah, when will the flowers bloom and the leaves sprout!?
However, making clothes from fallen leaves and shelter from twigs, enduring as the storm rages, bearing as the piled snow presses down, they silently concentrate their own nutrients more and more into their roots (根蔕), preparing for the coming spring without neglecting it (懈怠) for even a moment. They do not resent the harshness (凜烈함) of the climate, nor lament the cold dampness (陰冷함) of the ground. They do not envy the greenness (靑靑함) of the pine and cypress (松栢), nor are they angered that other trees do not adopt the same strategy. They simply understand clearly what they must do and faithfully follow what they understand, each completing its responsibility. They do not compete to be first or seek glory and fame (功名). Following their composition and innate nature, whether early or late, whether blue or red, they color the three springs – are these not the flowers and plants?
Ah! Flowers and plants, what if they had not prepared silently amidst the harsh winter?

This Is All
Yi Sŭng-hun

Many things could be said. However, I earnestly request (懇求) that just the two conditions written below, which are my lifelong wishes, be deeply remembered in the hearts of my brethren.
1.  That there emerge genuine, substantial individuals (質實한 眞人) who work with all their heart and body, thereby reaching the point of receiving the faith (信仰) of the people in each society.
2.  That if there are people who work sincerely, not masked, even the vigorous youth (血氣方强의 청년들) should respect (敬愛) and revere (숭앙) them considerably, creating a trend towards a social center.
In my thought, if these two new realities are not created (作出) in our Chosŏn today, the day when we achieve new construction under a new order will be postponed (延退) to a very distant tomorrow. My words, in short, mean to discard all empty formalities (虛式) and focus only on facts (事實). In the past, we were not sincere in building the new and were deceitful (詐僞) in handling the existing, leading (招致) to today's situation. From now on, it is good that we must be sincere. If sincere, achievements (功績) appear before reputation, and faith (信仰) arises following achievements. When achievements precede reputation and faith follows achievements, and individuals connect and societies form through such real power (實勢), the underlying strength (底力) of that people (人族) and that society cannot be overcome by anyone. I say that without this, there is nothing. This is all. <89>

**Summary**

This 1923 New Year's survey in *Kaebyŏk* magazine collects opinions from eight Korean intellectuals and activists on their hopes and prescriptions for the nation. Common themes include the need for practical action over empty rhetoric, character reform (starting with youth via Boy Scouts), returning to and developing rural areas (especially agriculture to combat land loss and ensure survival), fostering genuine scholarship, economic self-sufficiency (promoting Korean products and cooperatives), sincerity, and abandoning the pursuit of superficial fame. The writers express deep concern about the present state and future prospects of Chosŏn under Japanese rule (though often alluded to rather than stated directly), urging unity, determination, and practical efforts for national revitalization and survival.

Historically, this article is significant as it reflects the diverse strategies and anxieties of Korean intellectuals in the early 1920s, a period following the suppression of the March First Movement. It showcases the shift towards cultural nationalism, self-strengthening movements (자강 운동), emphasis on economic development (particularly rural issues and consumer cooperatives like the one proposed by Yŏm T'ae-jin, anticipating later movements), and moral/educational reform (Boy Scouts, scholarship, sincerity) as ways to cope with and resist colonial pressures. The mix of practical proposals (rural development, Boy Scouts) and moral exhortations (sincerity, rejecting formalism) provides insight into the multifaceted approaches considered for national survival and progress. The contribution from "T'ae Kong in China," with its classical Chinese and references to national self-determination and sacrifice, hints at the ongoing connections with independence activists abroad.

新年의 新意見
우리는 가장 진실한 의미에서 이 난을 設하야써 각 방면에서 말업시 일하는 여러 친구들의 신년에 대한 新意見을 구하려 하엿다. 그러나 그것은 반듯이 마음과 가티 되지는 못하엿다. 예사의 희망담으로 녀기지말고 이 의견을 발표한 그들과 가튼 진실한 마음으로써 이 글을 읽어본다 하면 우리의 적은 노력이나마 虛地에 몰아가지는 아니하랴 한다.
