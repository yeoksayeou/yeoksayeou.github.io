발행일	1923년 01월 01일
기사제목	배 (Boat/Ship)
필자	金岸曙
기사형태	시 (Poem)

배
						金岸曙
끗도 업는 한 바다 우를<103>
미듬성도 적은 사랑의 배는
흔들리우며, 나아가나니.

애닯게도 단 혼자서,
그러나마 미소를 띄우고
거츨게 춤추는
푸르고도 깁흔 한 바다의 먼 길을
사랑의 배는 나아가나니,
아아 머나먼 그 끗은 어대랴.

희미한 달에 비추어 빗나며, 어둡은
끗모를 한 바다를 배는 나아가나니.<104>
<103-104>

Boat
						Kim An-sŏ
Over the endless sea<103>
The boat of love, with little trustworthiness,
Goes forward, swaying.

Sadly, all alone,
Yet bearing a smile,
Along the distant path of the deep blue sea
That dances roughly,
The boat of love goes forward,
Ah, where could that faraway end be?

Shining dimly, lit by the faint moon, dark,
Over the unknowable sea, the boat goes forward.<104>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim An-sŏ uses the metaphor of a small, lone boat representing love, navigating a vast, endless, and rough sea. Despite its lack of certainty ("little trustworthiness") and isolation ("all alone"), the boat persists on its journey towards an unknown destination, illuminated only by the dim moonlight. The poem evokes feelings of loneliness, uncertainty, endurance, and a faint, persistent hope. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's imagery of a precarious journey on an endless sea could symbolize the uncertain and difficult situation of Korea or individual Koreans under Japanese rule. The lone boat navigating rough waters towards an unknown end might reflect the national or personal struggle for survival and an uncertain future during that period.
