Stream
						Kim An-sŏ
My hope is like the stream,
My despair is like the sea,
Yesterday and today it flows down,
Yesterday and today it is lost.

Therefore, I say—
O my small, flowing stream,<104>
In the quiet depths of the wide, deep sea
Find the comfort of the darkening twilight.<105>
<104-105>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim An-sŏ uses the metaphor of a stream flowing into the sea to contrast hope and despair. Hope is represented by the continuously flowing stream, while despair is the vast, deep sea where the stream ultimately gets lost. The speaker advises the stream (representing hope or perhaps the self) to find solace not in overcoming despair, but within the quiet darkness ("twilight") of the immense sea. For historians, the poem offers insight into the emotional landscape of the colonial period, potentially reflecting feelings of individual insignificance, the overwhelming nature of despair or difficult circumstances, and a search for quiet resignation or internal peace rather than outward resistance or triumph. The imagery of finding comfort in twilight within the sea of despair could be interpreted as a subtle commentary on coping mechanisms under oppressive conditions.
