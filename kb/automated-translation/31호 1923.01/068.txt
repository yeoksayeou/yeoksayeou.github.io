天玄地黃/이스라엘神話를 自己의 囊中有所로 아는 牧師
						猪巖
This is one amusing story from my impressions during my recent inspection tour of the Northwest (西北). It happened in Yongch'ŏn town (龍川邑內). I cannot remember the exact date, but it was late last month. At the request of the influential people of Yongch'ŏn, a lecture was arranged to be held in the local Christian church. Among my lecture phrases, there was this statement:
"What is called mythology (神話)? In our Chosŏn (朝鮮), there is the Tan'gun (檀君) myth; in China (中國), the P'an Ku (盤古氏) myth; in Judea (猶太), the Israelite myth (이스라엘 神話), and such."
Just after this one phrase, a person from the audience raised his voice, saying, "You cannot say such things in this church," and tried to stop the lecture. At first, I thought it was perhaps an ignorant police officer misunderstanding the phrase and censoring the lecture, but don't be surprised – the person who said this was the so-called Pastor Ch'a (車牧師), a famous gentleman there. The reason he took issue with my words was apparently this:
"The Israelite myth is a sacred and inviolable word found within the Old Testament of the Christian Bible. How dare you call the words in the Old Testament 'mythology' in a Christian church? This is improper."
See how ignorant he is! How can the phrase "Israelite myth" be offensive to religion? Thinking about it, it seems that pastor knew the Israelite myth as his own possession in his pocket (囊中所有物) and acted as if I were snatching away his property.

The Sophistry of an Ignoramus Who Thinks 'Inspection' Means an Imperial Envoy's Tour
This story comes from Hwangju (黃州). During this Northwest inspection tour, I had sent a postcard like this to a certain church in Hwangju:
"Mr. XX will arrive in your county on such-and-such date as part of his Northwest inspection tour (西北視察次). How about your church taking advantage of this opportunity to host a lecture?"
This was the meaning of the postcard. Upon receiving it, a certain young gentleman, who holds the most sway within that church, flew into a rage and said: "Inspection (視察)? What an <81> arrogant word for a private individual to use! Do they dare to 'inspect' our Hwangju?"
Consequently, I later heard in detail that he immediately sent a telegram refusing. How ignorant! Doesn't he think 'inspection' (視察) is like the dispatch of an imperial secret inspector (御使出道) in the old days? And yet, this is the man who struts about proudly as some kind of pioneer of equality and freedom! Alas, the saying "knowing is a disease" refers to such types.

New Stubborn Fools Obsessed with Names
The biggest gain from this inspection tour was discovering many of the world-famous "new stubborn fools" (新頑固). These are frustrating people who will say fire is water if it just has the name "new" (新), and will say it's wrong even to cook rice from grain if it's called "old" (舊). I discovered many stubbornly "newly enlightened" (新開化) people who would call even a completely decaying village an "enlightened village" (開明한 村) if its name was "New Village" (新里), and assume that only utterly useless people live in even the most open-minded street if its name is merely "Old Street" (舊巨里). There were many instances where people judged things as new or old based solely on the name, without thinking about the essence (本質) of the new or old thing, thus becoming stubborn fools themselves. This is evidence that stubbornness has merely shifted from the old conservatives (舊頑固) to the new conservatives (新頑固).

**Summary**

The author, Chŏam, recounts three frustrating anecdotes from his tour of northwestern Korea in late 1922. In Yongch'ŏn, a pastor took offense at the academic term "Israelite mythology" being used in his church, viewing it as an attack on the Bible. In Hwangju, a local figure misinterpreted the author's announcement of an "inspection" (sich'al, meaning observation tour) as an arrogant claim to official authority, like an old imperial inspector. Finally, the author generally observed a rise of "new stubborn fools" who blindly embraced anything labeled "new" and rejected anything "old," regardless of substance, merely shifting the locus of rigid thinking. For historians, this article offers glimpses into the cultural and intellectual landscape of 1920s colonial Korea: the tensions between modern academic language and conservative religious interpretations (Pastor Ch'a), sensitivities surrounding terminology related to authority (the sich'al incident), and the social phenomenon of reactive 'modernism' where the label 'new' could trump actual value or progress in the eyes of some Koreans navigating rapid societal change.

天玄地黃/이스라엘神話를 自己의 囊中有所로 아는 牧師
						猪巖
이번 西北視察하든 感想中한 웃음거립니다 龍川邑內에서 當한 일입니다 날자는 仔細히 記憶할 수 업스나 먼저 달금음입니다 龍川有志의 請求에 依하야 當地耶蘇敎堂內에서 講演을 開催하게 되엇는데 나의 講演句節가운대 이러한 말이 잇섯습니다.
「神話라 하는 것은 무엇이냐 하면 우리 朝鮮에 檀君神話, 中國에 盤古氏神話, 猶太에 이스라엘 神話가튼 것이다」
하는 말 한마듸가 잇섯는데 聽衆가운대서 한 사람이 목소리를 놉혀「이 敎堂에서 그러한 말은 하지 못하오」하고 講演을 禁止하기에 나는 처음에 節不知한 警官이말 句節을 誤解하고 講演을 取締하나부다 하엿더니 놀래지마시오 그 말을 한 작자는 所謂當地에 잇는 車牧師라는 有名한 兩班인데 그 分이내 말을 責을 잡는 原因은 이러하다합니다.
「이스라엘 神話는 耶蘇聖經舊約中에 잇는 神聖不可侵의 말이어늘 耶蘇敎會堂에 敢히 舊約中에 잇는 말을 神話라 하니 이것이 不穩當하다」
하는 입디다 얼마나 無識한가 보시오 이스라엘 神話라 하는 말이 宗敎에 무슨 抵觸이 되는 말입니 생각건대 그 牧師는 이스라엘 神話를 自己의 囊中所有物로 알아가지고 自己의 所有物을 내가 奪取하는 듯이 아는 貌樣입디다.
視察을 御史出道로 아는文盲의 詭辯
이것은 黃州에서 난 말입니다 이번 西北視察中에 黃州某敎會에다 이러한 葉書를 發하야둔 일이 잇습니다
「〇〇氏가 西北視察次로 貴郡에 某日到着하겟사오니 貴敎會에서는 此時機를 利用하야 講演을 主催함이 어하올는지」
의 意味로 葉書를 하엿더니 그 敎會안에 가장 勞力을 가진 靑年紳士한 분이 葉書를 바다보고 火를 벌컥내여 하는 말이「視察이라니 個人으로 視察이란 말이 무슨 無＜81＞嚴한 말이야 저이들이 敢히 우리 黃州를 視察하랴하고 」
因而拒絶電報를 單行으로 하엿단 말을 後에 仔細히 들엇습니다 얼마나 無識합니 視察을 以往時代에 御使出道모양으로 아는 것이 아닙니 게다가 무슨 平等自由의 先驅者로 하야 揚揚自得하는 그 分이! 마시요 안다는 것이 病이라 하는 말은 그런 무리를 두고 하는 말입니다
이름에 객낀 新頑固들
이번 視察中에 가장 큰 所得은 世界에 有名한 新頑固들을 만히 發見하엿습니다 무엇이든지「新」이란 이름만 가지면 불을 물이라고 하야도 올타하고 무엇이든지 舊라 하면 쌀로 밥을 짓는다 하야도 글타하는 沓沓한 사람들이 만습디다 다 썩어저가는 洞里라도 이름이 新里라 하엿스면 開明한 村이라 말하고 아모리 열린거리라도 이름만 舊巨里라 하면 아조못쓸사람만 잇는 것으로 아는 新開化의 頑固가만히 잇는 것을 發見하엿습니다 新과 舊일의 本質로 부를터 생각하지 아니하고 다만 이름만 가지고 新舊를 論하야 스스로 頑固가 되고 마는 일이 만히 잇습디다 이것은 舊頑固로부터 新頑固에 음긴 證據입니다.
