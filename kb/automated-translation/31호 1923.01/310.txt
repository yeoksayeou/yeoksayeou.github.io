**Summary**

This document is the table of contents for the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) covering all issues published in the year 1922 (January Special Issue No. 19 to December Issue No. 30). It lists the titles of articles, essays, literary works (novels, poems, scripts like a translation of *Hamlet*), and reports published each month, sometimes indicating genre. The contents reveal a strong focus on Korean nationalism, social critique (poverty, rural decline, labor issues), cultural movements, international affairs (China, Germany, Russia, Turkey), historical figures, art, literature, and the promotion of new ideas like Esperanto and the Ch'ŏndogyo religion. Notably, it records that three issues (January Special, July Special, September Special) were confiscated ("本號押收") by the Japanese colonial authorities. For historians, this table of contents provides a valuable snapshot of the intellectual currents, cultural production, and political concerns preoccupying Korean thinkers and writers during a critical period of colonial rule. The frequent confiscations underscore the strict censorship environment and the magazine's engagement with sensitive topics challenging colonial policy and promoting Korean self-awareness and reform.
