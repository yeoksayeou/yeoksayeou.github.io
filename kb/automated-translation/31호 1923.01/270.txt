갈매기
						金岸曙

어둡어가는 바다 우에,
봄철의 어린 꿈과도 가티,
희게도 떠도는 갈매기.

이즐어지는 저녁해는
고요히 남은 빗을 거둘 때,
소리도 업시 잠자코 슬어저가는
어린 날의 황금의 어린 꿈은
느저가는 바다의 갈매기와도 가티...
-(1922년 12월 18일 夜)-
黃浦 가에서 ＜105＞

Seagull
						Kim Ansŏ

Above the darkening sea,
Like a young dream of springtime,
A seagull, wandering whitely.

The fading evening sun
When it quietly gathers its remaining light,
Silently, without a sound, fading away
The golden, young dream of younger days
Is like the seagull on the darkening sea...
-(Night of December 18, 1922)-
At the edge of Hwangp'o <105>

**Summary**

The poem "Seagull" by Kim Ansŏ uses the image of a lone seagull wandering over a darkening sea at sunset to symbolize a fading youthful dream. It contrasts the bright, hopeful image of a "young dream of springtime" with the reality of the fading light and the disappearing dream, much like the seagull against the dimming backdrop. The poem evokes a sense of melancholy and the passage of time. From a historical perspective, the poem's themes of fading light, disappearing dreams, and quiet melancholy, written during the Japanese colonial period (1922), could be interpreted as reflecting the subdued hopes or the sense of loss experienced by Koreans under colonial rule. The gentle, almost resigned tone might be of interest to historians studying the cultural and emotional atmosphere of the era.
