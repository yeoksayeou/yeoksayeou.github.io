Article Title: 祈願 (Prayer/Wish)
Author: 懷月
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Prayer/Wish
						Hoe Wŏl (懷月)
Lover, to my withered breast from your many false words,
bring bitter tears and a sigh as if tearing apart,
and with your hair, dark like the last night of the moon, yet shining,
long and wide, wrap me up.

Then in the embrace of your hair, shining like black marble (黑 代理石),
my soul, enveloped in the dimly flowing fragrance of your hair,
when I am held in your bosom as if dead,
from your breasts in the darkness flows<101>
a thin, weak black line of sound (音響의 黑線), riding upon which,
I myself, alone, endlessly, shall be intoxicated.

Lover, to my eye withered by your many false smiles,
bring the light like a skull and the gleam of a ghost's flowing blood
to my eye withered by your brilliant smile,
and painting your face most frighteningly,
startle my spiritlessly weakening eye.

Then I shall gaze upon your frightening face
from morning till evening with a true heart,
and my heart, filled with sincerity (誠意) and terror (恐怖),
my withering spirit (靈), trusting you,
shall fall fearlessly into the pitfall-like bosom of your love.
—(From the poetry collection 「鶯粟花」)—<102>

**Summary**

This poem is an intense plea from a speaker to their lover. Feeling withered and empty due to the lover's falsehoods, the speaker begs for extreme sensations—bitter tears, frightening appearances, terror—to revive their numbed spirit. They desire to be enveloped and intoxicated by the lover's dark beauty and even frightening aspects, ultimately wishing to surrender completely, even recklessly ("fall fearlessly into the pitfall-like bosom"), finding a strange solace or revival in intense, potentially destructive, emotion. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the influence of Western decadent or symbolist aesthetics on early modern Korean literature. The focus on intense, dark, personal emotions and sensations, seeking oblivion or extreme feeling, might be interpreted by historians as an individual response to the broader societal constraints, anxieties, or sense of powerlessness experienced during the early colonial period, turning inward toward intense subjectivity.

祈願
						懷月
애인이어, 너의 거즛 만흔 단말에 시든 내 가슴에
쓴 눈물과 찌어지는 듯한, 한숨을 가저서 오라,
그리고 너의 그믐밤 가티, 검고도 빗나는
길고도 넓은, 네 머리털로 휩싸서 다오.

그러면 黑 代理石 가티 빗난, 네 머리털 품에
내 혼은 어둡게 흐르는, 네 머리 향기에 싸혀
네 가슴에 나는 죽은 듯이 안킬 때
어둠 속에 네 유방에서 흐르는＜101＞
가늘고 약한 音響의 黑線을 나는 타고서
스스로, 호을로 끗업시 심취하리라.

애인이어, 너의 거즛 만흔 웃음에 시든 내 눈에
해골가튼 빗과 유령의 피 흐르는 비츨
네 화려한 웃음에 시든 내 눈에 가저서 오라,
그리고 너의 얼굴을 가장 두렵게 칠하고
긔운 업시 衰해 가는 내 눈을 놀래여 다오.

그러면 나는 두려운 네 얼굴을
아츰에서 저녁까지 참된 마음으로 바라다보고
나의 마음은 誠意와 恐怖로 차서
시들어가는 내 靈은 너를 밋고서
함정가튼 네 사랑의 가슴에 겁업시 빠지리로다.
ㅡ(시집 「鶯粟花」에서)ㅡ＜102＞
