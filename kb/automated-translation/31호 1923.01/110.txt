**Summary**

This article from the January 1, 1923 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine announces the winners of a traditional Korean folktale contest. It notes the unexpectedly high number of submissions (over 150) and lists the names and locations of the 3rd place winners and numerous honorable mentions, who received a magazine subscription. Due to space constraints, the winning stories and critiques will be published later. The announcement also mentions sending consolation prizes (books) to those who submitted identical stories to the winners and small token prizes to authors whose unselected works might serve as research material. Finally, it acknowledges good submissions received after the deadline. From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it showcases a vibrant cultural activity focused on preserving Korean folklore during the Japanese colonial period. The wide geographic distribution of participants, including locations within Korea, Japan, China (Nanjing, Beijing, Harbin, Zhili Province), indicates widespread literacy and a network connecting Koreans across borders, possibly driven by diaspora communities or students abroad. The sponsorship by a magazine like *Kaebyŏk*, known for its nationalist and cultural leanings, underscores the effort to maintain Korean cultural identity.
