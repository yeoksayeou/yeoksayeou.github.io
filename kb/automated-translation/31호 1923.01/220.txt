僧女 (Nun)
						懷月 (Hwaewŏl)

Deep is the night, the candle lonely in the wide shrine hall (神堂),
A young nun (女僧), draped in a black long robe (黑長衫),
Before the solemn Buddha statue, deeply, quietly,
Like a colorless (빗업는) flower (꼿), withering and falling,
She bows her head and prays.

Like a nightingale (夜鶯) crying late (늣기어) in the night valley (골짜구니),
Her chanting voice trembles sorrowfully.
In the heavens (한울), the stars whisper,
But the song of her young love rots in her breast,
Only resentment-filled (恨만흔) sighs swirl around the Buddha statue and flow (흐르도다).

The night wind of the solemn shrine hall, whenever it creeps
Into the breast within the black long robe of the young nun sitting (안즌) on the cold wooden floor (찬마루),
Her eyes are hidden by tears, unseen (보이지 안코) <100>
The solemn Buddha statue too, like her lover (애인과 가티),
She embraces the Buddha statue and collapses (업더지도다).

Lying down, embracing the stone-made Buddha statue to her heart's content (마음것),
Like a lover who knows no pain (압흠도), forgetting (잇는) suffering,
She falls into a splendid dream of paradise (낙원),
Her weak, young breast is deeply (깁히 업시) intoxicated (醉하엿스나),
But her cold body lies (누엇도다) on the wooden floor, as if dead.
—(From the poetry collection 「鶯粟花」 (Aengsokkhoa / Poppy Flower))—<101>

**Summary**

This poem by Hwaewŏl portrays the intense inner turmoil of a young Buddhist nun praying alone at night. While outwardly performing religious duties in a solemn shrine hall, she is consumed by suppressed desires and sorrow, her chants sounding like a mournful nightingale. Her longing for love is so strong that she ultimately embraces the cold, stone Buddha statue as a substitute for a lover, finding momentary escape in a dream of paradise, even as her physical reality remains stark and lifeless. For historians, the poem vividly illustrates the personal cost of societal and religious constraints, particularly for women, during Korea's colonial period. It highlights the tension between tradition and individual longing, offering a glimpse into the hidden emotional landscapes of individuals within rigid social structures.

僧女
						懷月
밤은 기픈, 초불 외로운 넓은 神堂 안에는
젊은 女僧이 黑長衫을, 몸에 두르고
엄숙한 불상 압헤 그윽히, 고요히,
시들어 떨어지는 빗업는 꼿처럼
그는 머리를 굽으리고 기도하도다.

밤, 골짜구니에서, 夜鶯의 늣기어 우는 것 가티
그의 염불소리는, 구슬프게도 떨려 오도다.
한울엔 별들이, 소근거리는데
그의 젊은 사랑의 노래는 가슴에서 썩어
恨만흔 한숨만 불상을 에워싸고 흐르도다.

엄숙한 神堂의 밤바람은, 찬마루 우에 안즌
젊은 女僧의 黑長衫 속, 가슴으로 기어들 때마다
그의 눈은 눈물에 가려, 보이지 안코＜100＞
엄숙한 불상도, 그의 애인과 가티
그는 불상을 안고 업더지도다.

돌로 만든 불상을, 그는 마음것 안고 누어
압흠도 모르고, 괴로움을 잇는 나이 애인과 가티
그는 낙원의 화려한 꿈속에 빠져
약한 젊음의 가슴은, 깁히 업시 醉하엿스나
찬 몸은 마루 우에, 죽은 듯이 누엇도다.
