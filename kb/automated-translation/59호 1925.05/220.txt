기사제목	舊稿 二章 (Two Old Drafts)
필자	李相和
기사형태	시 (Poem)

舊稿 二章
						李相和

My flapping spirit, struggling,
How many times yesterday and today has it thrashed about?
Unceasing "Time," behind my weeping,
Flows, flows, flows to kill me.

Through the space where starlight races,
When the wind brushes the tips of tree branches,
Why does the cricket cry, looking at the wordless heaven?
Thus the world remains in deep night.

Last year, past days, in that dream,
Unknowingly, I passed through them like that.
The ground, the ground I trod, how many times did it roll over,
Ah, throwing me into this valley of tears. <37>

I did not know that a comfortable world lies in self-satisfaction.
I did not know that a happy life lies in submission.
But seeking a new path, and walking that path,
My spirit, willing to die even in the street, is too lonely.

Rather than finding my way in self-satisfaction and submission,
Rather than binding my interests to another's life,
Oh, rather death—death is my path!
That death which enters into another country, a new life!

But until I tread this path,
Ah, that day, that time is most agonizing.
Surely, there is still lingering sorrow,
The time when I think of it is sore and painful.

With this life that goes and cannot return,
If I am to live always only within empty laughter,
Oh sword! Make my spirit into a stone,
Make it into a stone rotting at the bottom of a stream.
-Extreme- <38>

To others, I am said to be a madman,
But as I know myself, I am a true person.

In this world that regards me with displeasure,
There are so very many who wish to live.

Oh, it is fearful, it is shameful,
I can see their flower-like reasoning.

Lest perhaps because my life exists,
They cannot live their lives—ah, it is burdensome.

Do I know little, or do I not know much?
Am I too foolish, or too wise?

No matter what, my desire is only for mad deeds;
I might, without knowing, tear down the house where others sip honey.

People! Just follow behind my mad self,
Carried away by mad excitement, I will even show you death.
-Song of the Pioneer- <39>
<37-39>

**Summary**

This two-part poem by Yi Sang-hwa expresses profound existential angst, disillusionment with conventional life, and a yearning for a radical, perhaps self-destructive, path. The speaker rejects the comfort found in "self-satisfaction" (自足) and "submission" (屈從), seeing death as a potential escape or transformation ("another country, a new life"). The second part embraces the identity of a 'madman' or 'pioneer,' scornful of conventional desires and urging others to follow his extreme path, even unto death. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the despair, alienation, and desperate search for meaning among Korean intellectuals during the oppressive colonial period. The rejection of "submission" and the embrace of an extreme, potentially self-sacrificial path, even framed as madness, could be interpreted by historians as a veiled expression of resistance against the colonial reality or the suffocating societal norms it imposed. The figure of the 'pioneer' seeking a 'new path' resonates with nationalist or revolutionary aspirations, albeit expressed through intensely personal and nihilistic imagery.

舊稿 二章
						李相和
펄덕이는 내 신령이 몸부림치며
어제 오늘 몃 번이나 발버둥질하다
쉬지안는 『타임』은 내 울음 뒤로
흐르도다 흐르도다 날 죽이려 흐르도다

별 비치 다름질하는 그 사이로
나무가지끄틀 바람이 뭇지를 때
귓두람이 웨 우는가 말 업는 한울을 보고?
이러케도 세상은 야밤에 잇서라.

지난해 지난날은 그 꿈 속에서
나도 몰래 그러케 지나왓도다
땅은 내가 드듼 땅은 몃 번 궁구려
아 이런 눈물 꼴짝에 날 던젓도다. ＜37＞

나는 몰럿노라 安逸한 세상이 自足에 잇슴을
나는 몰럿노라 幸福된 목숨이 屈從에 잇슴을
그러나 새 길을 찻고 그 길을 가다가
거리에서도 죽으려는 내 신령은 너머도 외로워라.

自足 屈從에서 내 길을 찻기보담
남의 목숨에서 내 사리를 얽매기보담
오 차라로 죽음-죽음이 내 길이노라
다른 나라 새 사리로 드러갈 그 죽음이!

그러나 이 길을 밟기까지는
아 그날 그때가 가장 괴롭도다
아즉도 남은 애닯음이 잇스려니
그를 생각는 그때가 쓰리고 압흐다.

가서는 오지못할 이 목숨으로
언제든지 헛웃음 속에만 살려거든
검아 나의 신령을 돍맹이로 만드러다고
개쳔 바닥에 석고 잇는 돍맹이로 만드러다고.
-極端- ＜38＞

나는 남 보기에 미친 사람이란다
마는 내 알기엔 참된 사람이노라.

나를 아니꼽게 녀길 이 세상에는
살려는 사람이 만키도 하여라.

오 두려워라 붓그러워라
그들의 꼿답은 사리가 눈에 보인다.

행여나 내 목숨이 잇기 때문에
그 살림을 못살까-아 죄롭다.

내가 알음이 적은가 모름이 만흔가
내가 너머나 어리석은가 슬기롭은가.

아모래도 내 하고저움은 미친 짓 뿐이라
남의 꿀 듯는 집을 문훌 지 나도 모른다.

사람아 미친 내 뒤를 따라만 오느라
나는 미친 흥에 겨워 죽음도 뵈줄테다.
-先驅者의 노래- ＜39＞
