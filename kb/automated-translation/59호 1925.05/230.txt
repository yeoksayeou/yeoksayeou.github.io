街相 (Street Scenes)

구루마꾼 (Rickshaw Puller)

“This shameful work I do every day,
Do you all look upon it as ordinary?”
The bare-chested rickshaw puller,
Sweat dripping from his bloodshot-eyed face,
Ignoring even the woman’s lapel,
Makes cow noises at the crossroads.

엿장사 (Candy Seller)

What is it that you give?
To the child and to the old man,
To humans, said to be more divine than beasts,
It’s not just candy that offers a sweet taste.
The heart that knows the taste beyond sweetness,
Don’t forget anyone might have it,
You, striking the wooden clapper with large scissors, <40>
How could what you give be just candy!

거러지 (Beggar)

Oh beggar, seen only in the morning and evening!
Why come out to make this already hardened world
Seem even more pitiful?

Oh beggar, the Record of Sins (罪錄) of God’s (한우님) sons!
They, deserving to be struck by lightning, pity themselves,
Not knowing your heart that hides in a dugout even in broad daylight. Come out! <41>
<40-41>

**Summary**

This poem, titled "Street Scenes," captures vignettes of marginalized figures in early 20th-century Korean urban life: a sweating, dehumanized rickshaw puller, a candy seller whose wares offer more than just sweetness, and a beggar portrayed as both a pitiable sight and a living symbol of societal sin ("Record of Sins of God's sons"). The poem uses stark imagery to comment on hardship, social hierarchy, and the underlying humanity or spiritual significance often overlooked in these figures. For historians, the piece offers a poignant glimpse into the social landscape of colonial Korea, reflecting the struggles of the lower classes (rickshaw puller, beggar), common street professions (candy seller), and a critical perspective on societal failings, potentially hinting at the perceived moral decay or hardship under colonial rule. The use of older Korean spellings and the specific term "Hanŭnim" are also of linguistic and cultural interest.

街相
구루마꾼

「날마다 하는 남 붓그런 이 짓을
너의들은 예사롭게 보느냐?」고
웃통도 버슨 구루마꾼이
눈 붉혀 뜬 얼골에 땀을 흘리며
안악네의 압흠도 가리지안코
네거리 우에서 소흉내를 낸다.

엿장사

네가 주는 것이 무엇인가?
어린애게도 늙은이게도
즘생보담은 신령하단 사람에게
단맛뵈는 엿만이 아니다
단맛 넘어 그 맛을 아는 맘
아모라도 가젓느니 잇지 말라고
큰 가새로 목닥치는 네가 ＜40＞
주는 것이란 엇재 엿 뿐이랴!

거러지

아츰과 저녁에만 보이는 거러지야!
이러케도 완악하게 된 세상을
다시 더 가엽게 녀여 무엇하랴 나오느라.

한우님 아들들의 罪錄인 거러지야!
그들은 벼락마질 제들을 가엽게 겨
한나제도 움 속에 숨어주는 네 맘을 모른다 나오느라. ＜41＞
＜40-41＞
