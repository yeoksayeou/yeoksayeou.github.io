開會前에 禁止된 民衆運動者大會
己未以後로 처음 본 京城의 萬歲騷動

The People's Activists Conference, initiated to forge a grand unity encompassing over 420 organizations (with a membership exceeding 100,000) including labor, farmer, thought, youth, Hyŏngp'yŏng (equality movement), and women's groups scattered throughout all of Chosŏn, had been preparing for its opening for two months. With preparations largely complete, it was scheduled to hold its inaugural ceremony as the first voice on April 20th at the Hasegawa-chō Public Hall (長谷川町 公會堂). However, on the night of the 19th, two members of the conference's preparatory committee, Mr. Kwŏn O-sŏl and Mr. Cho Pong-am, were suddenly summoned by the Ponjŏng Police Station (本町署). By order of the head of the station's Higher Affairs Section (高等係 主任), the gathering was banned under the pretext that there was a risk of disturbing public order. The conference again dispatched committee members and repeatedly negotiated with the authorities, but permission was absolutely denied. The following day, even the assembly of the conference delegates was ordered to disperse. Angered by this, over 300 delegates planned a large-scale demonstration to resist the unreasonable oppression by the police. Around 9 PM on April 20th, centered around the Tansŏngsa and Umigwan theaters, several hundred people proceeded through the night market, led by red flags and shouting "Long live the People's Activists Conference! Long live the Proletariat!" The general crowd joined in, causing a great commotion. The Chongno area instantly turned into a scene of chaos (修羅場), with even the mounted police unit being dispatched. Seventeen of the leaders were arrested that night. Several were released, but Messrs. Kim Sang-ju (Masan), Sin Ch'ŏl-su (Taegu), Chŏng Yong-sŏk (Kyŏngsŏng), Kim Yŏng-gon (Kyŏngsŏng), Kim Ch'ang-jun (Kyŏngsŏng), An Chong-gyu (Kyŏngsŏng), Kwŏn Kil-to (Kyŏngsŏng), and Chŏn Hae (Kyŏngsŏng) remain under investigation. (Details will be published at a later opportunity.)<62>

言論壓迫의 奇怪한 實例

As reported separately, the unreasonable police suppression of the People's Activists Conference gathering led to a "Long Live the Ideologists" commotion, the first seen in Kyŏngsŏng's Chongno area since the March First Movement (己未), on the night of April 20th. The police officers controlling the movement that night, in the midst of their frenzied rampage, beat a photographer from the Sidae Ilbo (時代日報) who was documenting the scene and arrested a photographer from the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報), acting with tyrannical impunity as if in an uninhabited land (無人之境), thus showing a bizarre example of press suppression.
(Added note, night of the 20th) <62>

**Summary**

This news report from May 1925 details the banning of a major "People's Activists Conference" just before its scheduled opening in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul). The conference aimed to unite over 420 diverse groups (labor, farmer, youth, women, etc.) across Korea. After the ban by Japanese colonial police under the pretext of maintaining public order, the delegates staged a large protest on Chongno, shouting slogans and carrying red flags, leading to a significant commotion and arrests. The article also reports police violence against newspaper photographers covering the event, citing it as an example of press suppression. For historians, the article is significant as it documents a large-scale, cross-sectoral attempt at organizing popular resistance beyond the initial March First Movement, the specific grievances (police suppression), tactics (demonstration, slogans, red flags), and the harsh response of the colonial state, including overt media censorship and violence against journalists. The mention of diverse groups like Hyŏngp'yŏng also highlights the broadening scope of social movements in 1920s Korea.

開會前에 禁止된 民衆運動者大會
己未以後로 처음 본 京城의 萬歲騷動

전 조선에 散在한 勞農, 思想, 靑年 衡平, 女性 등 420여 단체(회원수 10만여)를 網羅하야 일대 단결을 지으랴고 발기된 민중운동자대회는 2개월전부터 개회준비에 착수하야 會務가 대개 완료되고 4월 20일에 長谷川町 公會堂내에서 제일 聲으로 發會式을 행하랴다가 19일 夜에 그 대회의 준비위원 權五卨
曺奉岩 兩氏를 本町署에서 突然 呼出하야 同署 高等係 主任의 명령으로 치안을 방해할 우려가 잇다는 구실하에 집회를 금지하얏다. 同會에서는 다시 위원을 파견하야 누차 당국에 交涉하얏스나 절대 불허하고 그 翌日에는 會의 대의원 그들의 집회까지도 해산을 명하얏다. 이에 분개한 300여의 대의원은 경찰의 무리한 압박을 반항하랴고 일대 시위운동을 계획하야 20일 오후 9시경에 團成社 及 優美舘 부근을 중심으로 하야 수백의 군중이 적기를 선두로 하고 「민중운동자대회 만세, 無産者 만세」를 高唱하며 夜市中으로 진행하매 일반의 군중은 附和合勢하야 큰 소동을 닐으키니 종로 일대는 불시에 修羅場으로 化하야 騎馬警察隊까지 출동하야 其 先導者는 當夜 17명이 檢束되얏다가 몃명은 放釋되고 金尙珠(馬山) 申哲洙(大邱) 鄭溶錫(京城) 金永坤(京城) 金昌俊(京城) 安鍾奎(京城) 權吉道(京城) 全海(京城) 等 諸氏는 繼續取調중이다. (詳細事는 후 기회에 발표)＜62＞

言論壓迫의 奇怪한 實例

민중운동자집회에 대한 경찰의 무리한 압박으로 4월 20일 夜, 京城의 종로통 일대에서 己未 이후로 처음 보는 主義者萬歲소동을 닐으킨 것은 別報와 갓거니와 이 날밤의 그 운동을 取締하던 경관은 열광적으로 橫行하던 끄테 그 광경을 촬영하는 시대일보의 사진반 기자를 구타하며 조선일보 사진반 기자를 檢束하는 등 無人之境으로 횡포를 감행하야 언론압박의 기괴한 실례를 보혓다.
(20일 夜 補記) ＜62＞
