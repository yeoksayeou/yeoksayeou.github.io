Magazine Title: 개벽 제59호
Publication Date: 1925년 05월 01일
Article Title: 촛불 (Candlelight)
Author: 金昌述
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

촛불
						金昌述

엇더한 숨김을 가슴에 두고
소리 업시 바르르 뜨는
아지못할 촛불이여!
말하렴으나,

어엽브게, 아름답게,
그대의 뜨는 봄을 안고도는
가느란 바람이
침침한 밤한울을 휩쓸며
나려안질 때
소리도 업시 바르르 뜨는
아지못할 촛불이여!
말하렴으나, ＜42＞

까막질하는 별바다
햇득이는 달소리
고요히 잠드른 한울에도
싯그러운데
그대는 무엇을 그리느냐,
홀로 잠잠이 섯으니,

붉다란 불꼿이 심지를 믈고
슬금슬금 타 기어오르니
그 뜻이 무엇이뇨?
아지못할 촛불이여?
말하렴으나,

그러나, 인제 다 타버리면
한 말도 하지못할 애처러운 몸
애처러운 몸,
소리도 업시 바르르 뜨는
아지못할 촛불이여!
말하렴으나, ＜43＞
＜42-43＞

Candlelight
						Kim Ch'angsul

With what kind of secret held in your heart,
Soundlessly trembling,
O unknowable candlelight!
Please speak,

Lovelily, beautifully,
When the slender wind,
Embracing your trembling bloom,
Sweeps through the dim night sky
And descends to settle,
Soundlessly trembling,
O unknowable candlelight!
Please speak, <42>

The winking sea of stars,
The rising moon's sound,
Even in the quietly sleeping sky,
Are tumultuous,
What do you long for,
Standing alone in silence?

The reddish flame bites the wick
And stealthily climbs, burning—
What is its meaning?
O unknowable candlelight!
Please speak,

But now, when you have burned away completely,
A pitiful body unable to utter a single word,
Pitiful body,
Soundlessly trembling,
O unknowable candlelight!
Please speak, <43>
<42-43>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Ch'angsul personifies a candlelight, repeatedly asking the silent, trembling flame what secrets or longings it holds within. The candle's quiet existence is contrasted with the vastness and implied noise of the night sky (stars, moon). It burns itself out, unable to speak, embodying a sense of pathos and mystery. For historians, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted as a metaphor for the Korean condition – enduring suffering silently, holding unspoken grief or resistance, and being consumed without being able to voice its plight. The imagery of quiet trembling and eventual self-immolation might reflect the suppressed atmosphere and hidden struggles of the time.
