For the convenience of our esteemed readers in the towns of Yŏngdong, Kangwŏn Province, and the areas of North Manchuria and Inner Mongolia, whose numbers have recently been increasing monthly, we have now expanded the operational area of our branch offices and appointed additional branch staff as follows, who will diligently handle correspondence and advertising.

Staff

Branch Head: Yi Tongil
General Affairs & Accounting: Pak Ch'un

**Summary**

This company announcement from the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* informs readers of the expansion of its branch network in May 1925. Due to increasing readership, new staff have been appointed to handle correspondence and advertising at the Kangwŏn branch (located in Kangwŏn-ŭp) and an expanded Harbin branch in Manchuria (located at No. 8, Sixth Road Street, Sinanp'u). The announcement lists the names and positions of the newly appointed staff for both locations. For historians, the article clearly documents the reach and organizational efforts of a major Korean intellectual magazine during the colonial period, particularly its engagement with the growing Korean population in Manchuria, establishing a physical presence in Harbin. The specific addresses and names provide valuable data points for mapping Korean networks and activities outside the peninsula.
