◇Company Notice◇
◎Kaebyŏk Kowŏn Branch Office Specially Established◎
Recently, for the convenience of the numerous local readers in the four counties of Yŏnghŭng (永), Kowŏn (高), Munch’ŏn (文), and Chŏngp’yŏng (定) to the north, Naju (羅州) and Hwasun (和順) to the south, and Anak (安岳) to the west, we have newly established branch and sub-branch offices as follows, commencing operations for handling news correspondence and advertising matters from May 1st.

Branch Office Name: Kaebyŏk Kowŏn Branch Office
Location: Tojŏng-ni, Habal-myŏn, Kowŏn County (高原郡 下鉢面 都井里)
Territory: The entire areas of Kowŏn·Munch’ŏn·Yŏnghŭng·Chŏngp’yŏng Counties (高原·文川·永興 定平 各郡 一圓)

**Summary**

This company notice announces the establishment of new branch and sub-branch offices for the *Kaebyŏk* magazine in Kowŏn (Hamgyŏng Province), Naju (South Chŏlla Province), Hwasun (South Chŏlla Province), and Anak (Hwanghae Province) as of May 1, 1925. The notice details the locations, territorial coverage, and key staff members (including managers, reporters, advisors, finance, and general affairs personnel) for each new office. For historians, this notice provides valuable insight into the organizational expansion and growing reach of *Kaebyŏk*, a significant cultural and intellectual magazine, during the Japanese colonial period. It demonstrates the magazine's efforts to build a nationwide network to serve its readership and manage local operations, including news gathering and advertising, reflecting its importance and influence across different regions of Korea.
