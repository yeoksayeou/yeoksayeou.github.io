**Body Text Translation:**
Artificial Laborer
						Čapek (Author), Pak Yŏnghŭi (Translator)
Final Act

A research lab inside the factory of the Rossum Universal Robot Manufacturing Company. When the back door opens, the factory building is visible. On the left side, a window; on the right, a door leading to the dissection room. On the left wall is a large bench, and on top of it are test tubes, flasks, and other chemicals. Also placed there are a small incubator and a microscope covered with a glass case. On the right side is a desk with many books and an electric lamp stand. In the left corner is a washbasin and a mirror. In the right corner is a long chair (sofa).
Alquist is facing the desk on the right. He sits flipping through book pages with a look of despair.

ALQUIST: Ah, so I cannot discover it no matter what? Is it truly impossible? Mr. Kol! Mr. Kol! Why did you start making Robots? Mr. Haremi'el! Mr. P'aburi! Why did you devise so many plans in your heads? Why didn't you leave behind any trace of that secret formula? Oh, God—please hear my prayer—if you do not intend to leave humans, at least leave the Robots—somehow, leave at least the shadow of humanity! (Turning pages again) I just want to sleep.

(He gets up and goes to the window) It's still night! The stars are still twinkling over there! What use are those stars when not a single human lives in this world anymore? (Turns from the window towards the long chair on the right) Let's just sleep! Shall I sleep before life is remade? (Examines the test tubes on the bench) There's nothing I can do! It's useless. Everything is useless (Breaks a test tube <18> smashing it. The sound of machinery reaches his ears). Machines! Machines again! (Opens the window) Robots, stop the machines! Do you think you can create life from machines? (Closes the window and slowly comes towards the desk) If only there was a little time—if only there was a little more time—(Sees his reflection in the mirror hanging on the left wall) Red eyes—a trembling chin—yes, this is the last human! Ah, I've eaten too much interest—no. No. I must discover it. I must find it. I cannot give up (Turns to the desk again and eagerly flips through pages) Discovery! Search! (A knocking sound is heard at the door. He calls out sharply) Who is it? (A Robot servant enters and stands at the door) What is it?

SERVANT: Sir, Mr. Radius and his party are waiting outside, saying they wish to see you.

ALQUIST: I don't want to meet anyone!

SERVANT: But sir, he is the commissioner from the East. He has just arrived here from Naples.

ALQUIST: (Hurriedly) Yes, yes, let them come in!

(The servant leaves, and Alquist continues flipping through the book pages as before)

ALQUIST: There must be time, but there's absolutely no time—(The servant returns with Radius and three other commissioners, leaving only the commissioners behind before exiting. The commissioners gather together and wait quietly. Alquist looks up at them.)

ALQUIST: What is it?

(The commissioners quickly approach Alquist's desk)

ALQUIST: Speak quickly! There's no time!

RADIUS: Sir! The machines are useless now. We have become unable to make Robots.

(Alquist murmurs and looks at his book)

RADIUS: We have tried with all our might. We dug 500 million tons of coal from the earth. We ran 9 million machines without rest. Not only that, but we did everything possible. We wasted a year doing such things. <19>

ALQUIST: (Looking up over the book) For whom did you do such things?

RADIUS: For the next era—that was the thought. But we cannot make the Robots who will be our descendants. The machines only create formless lumps of clay. The skin doesn't adhere to the flesh, and the flesh doesn't adhere to the bones. This year, 8 million Robots were killed. In 20 years, not a single one will remain. Please teach us the secret of life! While you remain silent like this, sir, we will perish.

ALQUIST: I cannot teach you.

RADIUS: If you cannot teach us, you will die. I will order everyone to kill you.

ALQUIST: (Standing up) Kill me! Then kill me.

RADIUS: You must follow our orders.

ALQUIST: Me? Who is there to order me?

RADIUS: The Robot government.

ALQUIST: What do you intend to do here? Get out! (Sits down, facing the desk)

RADIUS: The Robot government of the entire world orders you to hand over the method for manufacturing Robots.

ALQUIST: Don't waste my time! (Buries his head in his hands)

RADIUS: Then state the price you demand. (Silence) State any conditions regarding yourself!

ALQUIST: Didn't I ask you to find people?

RADIUS: There are no humans left.

ALQUIST: I asked you to go to the wide fields (野) and mountains to find people. Go find them! (Starts looking at the book)

RADIUS: We have sent countless ships (船) and search parties until now. Thus, there is no place in the world we haven't gone. They all returned, but there were no humans.

ALQUIST: Not a single one? Not even one? <20>

RADIUS: There is no one but you.

ALQUIST: Then I have no power. Ah! Ah! Why did you kill all the people?

RADIUS: Even for you, if you want to live like a human, aren't slaughter and sovereignty necessary? Look at history. Read the books of humans. If you too want to live like a human, you must become a sovereign and a slaughterer. We had strength. Our numbers multiplied. We created a new world. A perfect world. We also created a world that doesn't exist, canals from the South Pole to the North Pole, and also a new Mars. We can also read books. Thus, we studied science and art. The Robots have perfected human culture.

ALQUIST: To humans, nothing is as strange as the form of the human itself (自軆). Now, go! Withdraw! If you cannot live, then bear young like animals!

RADIUS: Humans did not teach us such things. We do not know how to bear children. We have not a single child among us.

ALQUIST: Ah, what do you intend to do? What are you telling me to do? Are you telling me to produce children from inside my sleeve?

RADIUS: Teach us the method for making Robots. Why do you not teach us the secret of our increase?

ALQUIST: I forgot it.

RADIUS: You must have written down the secret formula.

ALQUIST: That—I burned it.

(The Robot commissioners all step back, startled.)

ALQUIST: I am the last human. Therefore, I cannot know what others knew.
(Pause)

RADIUS: Then experiment! And research the method anew!

ALQUIST: No matter how much I try, I cannot do it. I am merely an architect.—I only work with my hands. I am not a scholar. Therefore, I cannot <21> create life.

RADIUS: But please experiment. Yes, please do!

ALQUIST: I don't know how many times I have experimented.

RADIUS: Then please tell us what we should do. Robots can do anything.

ALQUIST: I have nothing to tell you. I could not create life in this test tube!

RADIUS: Then experiment on us.

ALQUIST: To experiment, I must kill you.

RADIUS: Do as you please regarding that. Even if you kill a hundred of us! Even a thousand!

ALQUIST: No, no, let's stop talking about that. Stop it!

RADIUS: Take the people you need and dissect them!

ALQUIST: I don't know how to do it. I am not a scientist. Something is written about the human body (人軆) in these books, but I cannot understand it.

RADIUS: Take a living one, I say. And please discover how we are made.

ALQUIST: Then you're telling me to kill someone? Look how my fingers tremble! I cannot even hold a scalpel. No, no, I don't even want to hold it—

RADIUS: Then life will disappear from this Earth. Take a living body, a living body! Do not lose this chance.

ALQUIST: Have pity on me, gentlemen! Truly, I do not know what would be best to do.

RADIUS: A living body—a living body—

ALQUIST: Then will you offer yourself? Then come with me!

(Radius steps back.)

ALQUIST: Humph! You fear death!

RADIUS: Me? Why did you choose me?

ALQUIST: If you don't want to, then fine. I am not forcing you. <22>

RADIUS: No, I will go to the dissection room. (Exits to the right)

ALQUIST: Oh God, give me strength. May this murder not be in vain. (To the three Robot commissioners) Take off his clothes! And lay him on the table!

(The three Robot commissioners start to exit right)

ALQUIST: Wait! No, no, I cannot do it. It's a useless sacrifice. Stay out—Experiment on your own bodies if you wish, or don't. Just leave me out of it. In any case, I cannot do it tonight. Everyone leave for tonight. Go away. (The three Robot commissioners exit. Alquist opens the window)

ALQUIST: The night is brightening! A new day is coming! But I haven't progressed at all. I haven't moved forward even one step. All research has come to nothing (水泡). So why has this dawn come again? What good is a new day when I am in the grave of life? Ah! It's truly quiet! If only I could just give up everything and sleep!

(Alquist turns off the electric lamp, lies down on the long chair, and covers himself with a black coat. After some time, the Robot woman Helena and the Robot Primus enter from the right.)

PRIMUS: (Whispering at the door) Helena! We shouldn't be here. That person is sleeping.

HELENA: Come on.

PRIMUS: No one is allowed to enter here. (Looking at the items on the desk) Look at this, Helena!

HELENA: (Goes over to Primus) There are many small test tubes. What are these used for?

PRIMUS: For experiments. Don't touch them.

HELENA: (Looking at the microscope) I saw Mr. Alquist looking into this once. What could he have seen?

PRIMUS: This is a microscope. Let me see!

HELENA: Be careful, okay. (Knocks over a test tube) Oh dear, I knocked it over.

PRIMUS: Why did you do that? <23>

HELENA: It's okay if we just clean it up.

PRIMUS: You just invalidated that person's experiment.

HELENA: It's your fault. If you hadn't stood next to me, nothing would have happened—

PRIMUS: Then why did you call me?

HELENA: Even if I called, you wouldn't come—. (Goes towards Alquist's desk) Hey, what are all these pictures?

PRIMUS: (Looks through an anatomy book) This is the book that Sir always reads.

HELENA: I don't understand things like this at all. (Goes to the window) Primus! Come here!

PRIMUS: Why?

HELENA: Look, the sun is rising.

PRIMUS: (Still looking at the book) Yes, this is the most precious thing in the world. This is the secret of life.

HELENA: I said come here.

PRIMUS: I'll be right there.

HELENA: Look, Mr. Primus, the secret of life or whatever, it doesn't suit you. It has nothing to do with you. Come on! Hurry up and look at that!

PRIMUS: (Goes to the window) What is it?

HELENA: Ah! Isn't it truly beautiful? The sun is rising. Can you hear that sound? The birds are chirping. Yes, Mr. Primus, I want to be a bird.

PRIMUS: Why?

HELENA: I don't know. But today my heart feels strangely different. It's like dreaming. For some reason, my body, my heart, everything feels like it hurts. Mr. Primus! I think I might be dying!

PRIMUS: Have you ever thought that dying might be good? Hmm? Maybe even now we are sleeping. Yesterday too, in a dream, I spoke with Helena.

HELENA: In your dream?

PRIMUS: Yes. In the dream, we spoke in strange words we didn't understand. But now I can't remember a single word.

HELENA: What was the conversation about?

PRIMUS: I can't remember what it was about, but it was a beautiful conversation, the kind I'd never had before. <24> So I thought I could die happily if I could just touch Helena. In the dream, that place was different from this world.

HELENA: I saw a nice place too, Mr. Primus. It was truly a strange place. People lived there before, but now only grass grows there. No one can go there. Only me—

PRIMUS: What is there?

HELENA: Just a small house, a garden, and two dogs. And those dogs licked my hand, Mr. Primus. And there were several puppies. Truly, Mr. Primus. If you put those puppies on your lap and played, you would spend the whole day without thinking of other things, forgetting all worries. But when the sun sets, you would be hundreds of times more tired than from working in the world. Everyone says I don't want to work, but if I were just in that garden, I would want to do anything.—What should I do? Primus!

PRIMUS: I don't know. But Helena is lovely.

HELENA: What is it? Primus!

PRIMUS: You are lovely, Helena! And I am the strongest among the Robots.

HELENA: (Looking at her reflection in the mirror) Am I lovely? What's lovely about me is the rose flower (薔薇꼿). My head—it just presses down on me from above. My eyes—I only see things with these eyes. My lips—they just help me speak. What is beautiful about this? (Looking at Primus reflected in the mirror) That person in there is Mr. Primus, right? Come here and be with me. Your head is a bit different from mine. And your shoulders are different—and your lips too—
(Primus moves away from Helena's side)
Huh? Why aren't you staying beside me? Why do you make me follow you all day long?

PRIMUS: You are the one who avoids me! Helena! <25>

HELENA: Your hair is messy. Let me fix it. There's no time I feel better than when I touch you. Mr. Primus! I should make you look lovely too.

PRIMUS: (Helena's hand is caught.) Helena, has your heart never beaten rapidly? Helena! How about it? See, something is starting again, isn't it?

HELENA: What will happen to us? Mr. Primus! (She places a rose (薔薇花) in Primus's hair. Primus and Helena look in the mirror. Then they laugh loudly.)

(Alquist gets up)

ALQUIST: What's this! Who is laughing? Is it a human?

HELENA: (Dropping the comb (櫛)) Mr. Primus! We're in trouble!

ALQUIST: (Stumbling towards the two) Are you human? Where did you come from? (Helena cries out and steps back) Are you human? Where from? (Touches Primus) Who are you?

PRIMUS: I am a Robot.

ALQUIST: What? A Robot? (To Helena) Who are you?

HELENA: I am the Robot woman, Helena.

ALQUIST: Let me see you turn around. You're a coward. (Grabs Helena's arm) Let me see your face! (Helena runs away)

PRIMUS: Sir, please don't startle this woman.

ALQUIST: What? Are you protecting this woman? When was this woman made?

PRIMUS: She was made two years ago.

ALQUIST: By Dr. Kol?

PRIMUS: Yes. Same as me.

ALQUIST: Laughing—blushing—protecting—I need to study you two more. You are the latest model of the Kol-type Robot. Take this girl to the dissection room!

PRIMUS: Why are you doing this?

ALQUIST: I'm going to experiment on this woman.

PRIMUS: What! On Helena?

ALQUIST: Of course. Won't you obey? Shall I call other Robots to take this woman away? <26>

PRIMUS: If you insist on doing that, I will kill you.

ALQUIST: Kill me! Then kill me! Then what will become of the Robots? What will become of your future?

PRIMUS: Sir, take me instead. I am a Robot made just like that woman. We were also made on the same day. Please kill me, Sir!

HELENA: (Stepping forward) No, no, please stop, just stop! Yes, please stop!

ALQUIST: Be quiet, be quiet! (To Primus) So, you don't want to live?

PRIMUS: I don't want to live without this woman. Without this woman, I don't want to live.

ALQUIST: I see! So you prefer to take this woman's place!

HELENA: Primus! Primus! (Cries)

ALQUIST: Helena! Helena! You know how to cry? Why are you crying? What need do you have for Primus, huh? What relation does one Primus in the world have to you?

HELENA: Then I will go.

ALQUIST: Where?

HELENA: To the place where they kill. (Primus holds her back as she tries to go to the dissection room) Please experiment on me. Primus! Let me go.

PRIMUS: Helena! Helena cannot go there!

HELENA: If you go there, I won't go. I will kill myself.

PRIMUS: (Restraining Helena) I won't let you die. (To Alquist) Sir, please don't kill us, okay?

ALQUIST: Why?

PRIMUS: We... we... must live together.

ALQUIST: (Opening the back door) Then go! Go forth! Adam, Eve! The world is yours.

(Helena and Primus exit, embracing each other)

ALQUIST: (Closing the door) Ah! Blessed day! Oh God! Mine eyes have seen thy salvation. According to thy holy will, let thy servant depart this world in peace.
(Alquist stretches out his hands)
-Curtain-
-Artificial Laborer Complete- <27>

**Summary**

This text is the final act of Pak Yŏnghŭi's 1925 Korean translation of Karel Čapek's play "R.U.R." Alquist, the last human, is desperately trying and failing to rediscover the lost formula for creating Robots. The Robots, led by Radius, confront him, demanding the formula as they face extinction due to their inability to reproduce. Alquist cannot help, having destroyed the notes. When pressed to experiment, he challenges Radius to be dissected, but Radius falters. Hope unexpectedly arrives with two newer Robots, Primus and Helena, who exhibit spontaneous human emotions like laughter, shame, fear, and ultimately, self-sacrificing love for each other. Seeing this, Alquist recognizes that life, through love, has found a way. He sends them out as a new Adam and Eve to inherit the world. From a historical perspective, the translation and publication of this play in colonial Korea are significant. It reflects the engagement of Korean intellectuals like Pak Yŏnghŭi (a key figure in proletarian literature) with contemporary world literature and thought. The themes of artificial creation, dehumanization, revolution, extinction, and the unexpected emergence of 'humanity' (love, self-sacrifice) in the non-human resonated deeply during a period of national identity crisis and oppression under Japanese rule, offering complex allegories about survival, legacy, and the definition of life itself.

(문을 두드리는 소리가 들린다. 그는 급히 부르지진다) 누구야? (人造 勞働者의 下人이 드러와서 문 압헤 섯다) 무슨 일야?
