Special Products of South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province

Ch'ŏnan Kwangdŏk-myŏn Walnuts (胡桃), Sŏnghwan Oriental Melons (眞苽), Yesan Oga-myŏn Writing Brushes (毛筆), Yŏt (엿(飴)), Sinyang-myŏn Lacquered Trays (漆盤), Tangjin White Cranes (白鶴), Sŏsan Kanwŏl Island Eorijeot (어리젓), Haemi Sŏkp'o-ri Saljogae Clams (살조개) (It is said that outside this place, they are absent in the whole country 此地外 全國에 無하다 云), Kongju Sŏlla (蟬羅) (Ramie (苧) and Silk (繭絲) Blend Fabric), Ch'unp'o (Spring Cloth 春布), Willow Craft Items/Utensils (杞柳制器具), Walnuts (胡桃), Sŏch'ŏn County Old Hansan Ramie Fabric (舊韓山苧), Namp'o Inkstones (藍浦硯石), Taejŏn Nonsan Paper Products (紙物).<153>

**Summary**

This short article lists the well-known specialty products from various towns and regions within South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (Chungnam) as of 1924. It catalogues agricultural products like walnuts and melons, crafts such as writing brushes, lacquered trays, willow items, inkstones, and paper goods, textiles including ramie-silk blends and famous Hansan ramie, and unique local foods like fermented oysters (eorijeot) and a specific type of clam claimed to be exclusive to the Haemi area. For historians, this list provides valuable insight into the regional economies, local craftsmanship, and agricultural landscape of colonial Korea. It highlights specific local identities tied to particular products and indicates specialized production zones, like Hansan for ramie and Namp'o for inkstones, reflecting both traditional crafts and the economic conditions of the period.
