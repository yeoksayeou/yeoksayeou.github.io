**Body Text Translation**

Beyond the Chosŏn of the Disillusionment Period
						Kim Kijin

From the bottom up
-From the bottom up
Flames, arise!
Revive and clear away!

-From the bottom up
Spring water, well up!
Flow on down!

-From the bottom up
New buds, burst forth!
Branches, spread out!

Oh, from the bottom up
Revive and clear away!
Flow on down!<10>

New buds, burst forth!
Branches, spread...
On our heads
Place a wreath of flowers!

Spring has come! Spring has indeed finally come. While people were unaware, or perhaps while people were aware, spring has finally come to us. Do you not see the faint shadow of spring everywhere you go? Do you not feel the stirring of the earth wriggling beneath our feet? ... A gentle wind caresses the dead grass and passes by.
Water flows below the eroded hill. If you place your ear quietly upon the ground, you can faintly hear the sound of small streams flowing from all directions. It is the water flowing from melted snow and thawed ice. It is astringent yet savory water.
That's right. Water flows now. From between the dead blades of grass, fragrant water, savory water, flows down with all its meager strength. Water with oil floating, oil congealing, oil melted, flows down from every nook and cranny, gathers together, becomes one, and covers our land. I realize that murky, unclear water stirs beneath my feet wherever I go.—What is good about murky, unclear water? It is because there is a reason for its murkiness, and a reason for its astringency. It is all the sleeping creatures washing the sleep from their eyes, all the things that were huddled washing themselves, all the things that were hidden washing away their grime. That is why it is oily, has an astringent taste, and a savory fragrance.
Ah, it has become spring. The muddy waters gather together to form a grand symphony... .

Truly, spring has indeed come! Ah, that's right! Truly, spring has indeed come, but in human society, in people's lives, spring has not come. The spring of rebirth has not yet come.<11>
Let's lift our eyes and look at the streets. Among the people visible, few are not disabled. It is a world of the disabled. Every person you meet limps. It seems to be a world of cripples. Every person you encounter crawls. It is a world of hunchbacks. Truly, it is so. The entire globe is tilted, limping... It is a world of cripples.

The cripple comes. The cripple goes. People limping on one leg come and go, busily passing by. Spring has come, but why are there so many cripples in the human world?
A person who fixed one leg found the other leg needed fixing too. A person whose legs were both disabled sits and moves. Crawls along.
Chosŏn is handicapped. When has it ever walked without limping? When will it ever escape the environment of the cripple? It is a strange environment. Is it impossible to straighten the legs and stand up in one breath...?

Countless people have become cripples trying to straighten their legs and stand up in one breath. Just as when a person who has fallen into a mire flounders and tries to get out, pulling out one leg only to have the other sink back into the mud, there are untold numbers of people who walk on, continuously limping, unable to fix it.
I am one of them. ...

My life was that of a cripple. My life, ceaselessly limping, did not know where the hand of repair should be applied. There were times when I thought of throwing this life, so full of disabilities, onto the ground and shattering it in one breath. Last winter was such a time. When I stood on the ground, carrying a ruin of the heart larger than my own, dragging two unhealthy legs, there was a fuse (火繩) in my chest. There was fire in my chest. Terrorism venturing onto the streets dominated my mind then. It was a time when the history of the cripple nearly became stained with fresh blood. It was also the time when I resolved<12> to bury myself for the sake of a single conviction. When I keenly felt the 『Ultima Ratio of Life』, the more intense it became, the tighter I held onto the 『Nameless Hero』. The lofty logic extracted from my head rose higher and higher, while the soles of my feet sank lower and lower. I failed to skillfully manage the ascending spirit and the descending flesh, losing balance. The mind striving to achieve everything at once vainly quickened its pace. But I could not carry it out. ...

To mend the life of a cripple, I sought a livelihood with my own arms. It is the process of the consciousness of life.
My body that grew up in ease, my brain soaked in crooked education, my hazy consciousness of life—to reform these, I tried to fix my way of living. The most urgent issue for me was the change in life consciousness. It was a life reform aimed at changing life consciousness. But at that moment, I realized that the body I had mended to escape being unable to stand had once again become a cripple. It is a sad thing.

But the world visible to the eyes is a World of Ghosts (亡靈의 世界). A world where gray ghosts dance. There is a distinction between sharp points and blunt points, but there is no doubt it is a ballroom for the Dance of Death (死의 亂舞). There was no room for discernment. It is a sorrowful thing. But to professionalize the consciousness of life, there was no other way than to place my body under harsh trials. A body raised in a well-off family, a consciousness of life that had lived comfortably, a hazy consciousness that couldn't find its bearings—only while being pressed down and shaved away under oppression could a complete consciousness be obtained. Each time pressed, each time shaved, a bright red spirit emerges. Uncovered flesh is revealed. Our spirit is forged (鍜鍊) here.
This is what I believed then, and what I also know now.

『You are a bundle of contradictions. You are shallow. Even someone who just repeats others' words wouldn't be as irresponsible as you. And yet, what have you been blabbering about all this time? Examine yourself. You should rightly advertise in the newspaper and apologize. ...』<13>
The grateful person who spoke to me like this... .
『From the start, I didn't intend to say anything to you. But as I write this letter to you, I cannot help my brush slipping this far, so I write thus.
Mostly, I trust you. Why is that? Because the words you spoke when we often met and talked while I was in Seoul with you still cling to my ears and do not leave.—That is, at that time, you said this to me. ...
... 「Hey, Y Kun. I'm telling you, I at least have principles (프린스풀). Whatever others say, I'll charge ahead, charge ahead indeed!」
... 「I have a program (프로그람). Whether it will be executed or not, I don't know...」
I still trust these words of yours. Because I trust them, I don't want to say much more. Just, let's say a person named A needs to go quickly to village E for urgent business. But his breath is short (갑부고) and his legs hurt (압하서), so he reaches a point where he must rest (수이고) for a moment. Let's say A sits down on a sharp-pointed rock named D by the roadside to rest his legs. People will point at A and ridicule him, asking why he sat on the conspicuous (有標한) D instead of sitting on the stone named C lying next to D. If he couldn't sit on C either, my dear comrade, wouldn't it be better to cross the stream and find the flickering light on the other bank to spend a night? It would be good for A's body, and also good for his work. What do you think?...』
The grateful comrade who gave me such a letter... .

But I think. What about the person who has neither the light across the stream nor the flat stone C, but only possesses D?—I think this.
It is like a person who has at least something to eat for the day telling another person, ‘It’s better to eat *injŏlmi* (인절미), why<14> are you eating *siruttŏk* (시루떡)?’ A person who likewise has no *injŏlmi* but only *siruttŏk* cannot say that. There must be some room for ‘leisure’ (餘裕) somewhere to be able to say this or that. Therefore, the one who can speak that much is perhaps more fortunate than the one receiving the words. ...
When A lives by begging from his friend A, the world does not blame A. But when A makes his own rice with his own hands, the world blames A. It is this kind of world.

What the reality of Chosŏn taught (가리처) me until last autumn was the single thing called “Cooperation” (協同). And thus, what it taught and made me think about until last winter was the single thing called “Tactics” (戰術).
And so, what I came to know was the reform of life consciousness; what I grasped (붓잡은) was winding tactics. It is abandoning logic and proceeding to practical reality.
Then, what kind of tactics should one adopt?
The attitude of artistic creation must possess conscience and be truthful to the end.
The tactics used as a means for a movement should proceed with as much “falsehood” (거즛) as possible. To engage in actual movement, one must deceive the opposing side as much as possible. One must deploy the strategy of fighting with one's back to the water (背永의 陣 - likely 背水의 陣). Especially here. The efforts of Japanese labor activists for immediate universal suffrage (普選卽施) are not solely because their goal is universal suffrage. Rather (찰하리), universal suffrage is merely their means. Practical actions in all fields become complete only by proceeding with falsehoods. Therefore, compromise (妥協) also becomes a tactic. If one does not adopt winding tactics, especially within an environment like Chosŏn, one can achieve nothing. There will only be disillusionment.

That's right. There will only be disillusionment. Look anywhere. Is there (업스나) any place without disillusionment? Everything is engrossed in the Dance of Death. Black-clothed monsters, gray ghosts, all figures great and small from top to bottom are engrossed in the Dance of Death. Poke any corner, it just twitches; there is no other movement. ... There will only be disillusionment.<15>
But, should one leave because of that? Abandon the Chosŏn of the disillusionment period and leave for another place where there is even a little movement? ... Ah, it is a difficult thing. An impossible thing.

What to do? Become a terrorist and take to the streets? Cling to idealism and commit suicide? Or, because of that, run away into decadence?
There is no precedent for success as a terrorist. Perhaps some might say, 『To enlighten the vast majority who don't know letters (글ㅅ자) and are slow to understand speech (말ㅅ귀), wouldn't showing them a brief, intense scene, like telling children fables (寓話) or showing them plays with meaning, have considerable effect?』 That's right, of course showing an intense scene (劇熱한 一場) provides much stimulation. Terrorism is not without effect either.
Becoming decadent is not even an issue. To cling to ideals and commit a love suicide (情死) with a hungry ghost (아귀) is also something a human cannot do. Then what is it?
Compromise (妥協)? Tactics (戰術)? Seeking fame (賣名)? Practical reality (實際)?

That's right, the problem lies here.
Compromise? Tactics?
Seeking fame? Practical reality?
-But let's avoid the play of logic as much as possible. Let's avoid the game of Logic as much as possible. Right now, these words have come to my mind.
-『*Ware ni genki ari, saiwai ni shite rikutsu nashi.* (われに元氣あり, 幸にして理窟なし.)』 [I have energy; fortunately, I have no logic/theory.]

Logic doesn't matter anyway. Let's just faithfully carry out our tasks. Beyond the Chosŏn of the disillusionment period, let's plant our soles firmly on the ground,<16> extinguish the fire before us, and proceed slowly. We must carefully observe which step we are standing on.
That's right, we must observe the steps. We cannot proceed emotionally. A revolution merely for the sake of revolution is no good. It must be a revolution that thinks about and prepares for the world after the revolution. Terrorism is fine too. But what about the affairs after the revolution?

Let's sow seeds. Let's plow the field (밧). The movement of a few stands on a single step. It is a minority (미노리티-) movement. It is the first step of the primary activity project. It is a Minority movement. This is Chosŏn, with 14 or 15 million peasants. This is Chosŏn, where over 14 or 15 million people cannot comprehend the national script (國文). A single scene of a terrorist's play will hold no authority either. Let's walk steadily.—
Then stand firm. Let's dig in. 20 years? 30 years? That, we don't know. Let's proceed just like that. Like the rich spring water flowing through the dead grassy field (밧), let's walk on, holding our peace (담을고). Let's walk on, maintaining secrecy (은밀을 직히고), such that the great symphony cannot be heard unless one strains one's ears. ...
That's right, let's be secretive like the spring water flowing beneath my feet now. We must be quiet like the gentle spring breeze passing by. We must be full of smiles like the flower buds that will soon open. That's right, knowing joyfully, we must do this work with laughter. Compromise? Tactics? Seeking fame? Practical reality? Let's cast aside logic and proceed steadily. Listen closely to the sound of the stream. Let's hear the signal. Dig a trap beneath the feet of the ghosts. From the bottom up, revive and clear away, flow and cover, embrace new buds, open flower buds, extend (뼈더라) the branches. The gate of spring has opened (열녓다). Let's walk on (거러나가자), beyond the Chosŏn of the disillusionment period.

**Summary**

Kim Kijin expresses profound disillusionment with the state of colonial Korea (Chosŏn), describing it as a crippled, stagnant society dominated by death and ghosts, a stark contrast to the arriving spring. He recounts his own past despair, contemplation of radical terrorism, and subsequent turn away from abstract ideals and grand gestures. Criticized by peers for perceived compromise, he defends the necessity of "winding tactics," pragmatism ("實際"), and even strategic "falsehood" and "compromise" (妥協) in the harsh colonial reality, arguing that abstract logic and idealism are ineffective. He advocates for patient, persistent, almost secretive groundwork ("sowing seeds," "plowing fields") among the largely illiterate populace, working "from the bottom up" like the subtle but powerful forces of spring to eventually overcome the "period of disillusionment."

For historians, this article offers a valuable glimpse into the intellectual ferment and ideological struggles among Korean nationalists/intellectuals in the mid-1920s. It highlights the deep sense of impotence and "disillusionment" (幻滅期) following the 1919 movement's suppression, the internal debate between radical action (terrorism) and gradualist, pragmatic approaches (what Kim calls "tactics" and "practical reality"), and the personal anguish involved in navigating collaboration/compromise versus principled resistance under colonial rule. Kim's justification for potentially deceptive tactics ("거즛," "굴곡된 전술") and compromise ("妥協") as necessary tools for survival and eventual progress within the constraints of colonial Chosŏn is particularly revealing.

봄이 왓다! 그여코 봄은 오고야 마랏다. 사람 사람들이 모르는 동안에 혹은 사람들이 아는 동안에 봄은 드듸여 우리에게 왓다. 가는 곳마다 낫하나 잇는 봄의 그림자를 보지 못하느냐. 우리의 발 아래에 꿈틀거리는 대지의 움즈김을 늣기지 못하느냐. ... 가만한 바람이 죽은 잔듸를 어루만지고서 지나가 버린다.
골탕진 언덕 밋흐로 물이 흘른다. 땅바닥 우에 가만히 귀를 기우리고 드르면 사방에서 흐르는 조고만 물소리가 그윽히 들닌다. 눈이 녹고 어름이 풀녀서 흘으는 물이다. 틉틉하고도 구수한 물이다.
그럿타. 지금 물이 흘른다. 죽은 잔듸풀 틈틈이에서 향내나는 물이 구수한 물이 저그나마 저희의 힘을 다하야 흘러 나려간다. 기름이 뜨고 기름이 엉키고 기름이 녹은 물이 방방곡곡이에서 흘러 나려와 가티 모히고 함끼 되여서 우리의 땅뎅이를 뒤덥허 버린다. 흐리고 맑지 못한 믈이 가는 곳마다 발 아래에서 움즉거리는 것을 나는 깨닷는다.-흐리고 맑지 못한 물은 무엇이 조흐냐. 그것은 흐린데에도 그 까닭이 잇고 틉틉한 데에도 그 까닭이 잇는 것으로 인함이다. 잠자고 잇든 모든 생물이 눈꼽을 씻고 움츠렷든 모든 것들이 목욕을 하고 숨어 잇든 모든 것들이 때를 씨서 내려 보내는 것이다. 그럼으로 기름이 끼이고 틉틉한 맛이 잇고 구수한 향내가 나는 것이다.
아아 봄이 되엿다. 흙탕물이 함끼 모혀서 커드란 씸포니-를 이루어 놋는다... .

참말로 봄은 그여ㅅ코 왓고나! 아아 그럿타! 참말로 봄은 그여ㅅ코 왓스나, 인류의 사회에는 사람의 생활에는 봄이 안 왓다. 소생의 봄이 아직것 안 왓다.＜11＞
눈을 들어서 행길을 보자. 눈에 보이는 사람들 중에서 병신 안인 사람이 얼마가 못 된다. 병신의 세상이다. 대면하는 사람마다 다리를 전다. 절늠바리의 세상인가 보다. 맛나는 사람마다 긔여 단인다. 꼽사의 세상이다. 참말로 그럿타. 지구전체가 기웃둥 하여가지고 절늠거린다...절늠바리의 세상이다.
