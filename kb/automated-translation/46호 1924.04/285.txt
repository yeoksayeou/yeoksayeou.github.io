△Urgent Company Notice▽

Our company, which has hitherto strived diligently for the readership, has frequently and erratically published enlarged issues, expanded issues, or otherwise temporary or special issues whenever we captured something spatially or temporally significant. As a result, errors in the magazine fees and shipping costs sent daily by readers in various regions have become extremely numerous, causing mutual vexation layer upon layer. Considering this a truly enormous obstacle to the improvement of the company's fortunes, and simultaneously recognizing the urgent necessity of rectifying this situation without fail, we hereby resolutely implement the revision of the magazine's fixed price, a long-pending issue, as shown below. Accordingly, we add notes regarding payment remittance. We earnestly hope that our esteemed readers will pay special attention to this company notice henceforth.

(Kaebyŏk Fixed Price Revision Table)
[Note: The table itself is mentioned but not provided in the text excerpt.]

However, the publication dates for enlarged issues will be four times a year: January, April, July, and October. Beyond this, for any increase in page count exceeding this or for temporary special publications, the price will not be increased.

Payment Notes

One: Magazine fees and postage must all be prepaid. If the prepayment runs out, dispatch will be stopped.
However, if a shortfall in prepayment occurs due to an enlarged issue or other change in fixed price, dispatch will await further payment.

One: Please be sure to use the Postal Transfer Savings Account number.

One: Substitution with postage stamps is refused.
However, limited to remote areas with inconvenient transportation, substitution is specially permitted with a 10% surcharge.

One: Cash on Delivery and Parcel Post handling are entirely refused.
However, when requesting a sample copy, the fixed price plus shipping cost must be prepaid.

One: When wishing for delivery via Collection Mail (集金郵便), please notify us in advance of the total amount, which includes the collection consignment fee and shipping cost listed below, in addition to the payment amount (Limited to Chosŏn, Japan, and China).
Seven *chŏn* for amounts from one *wŏn* up to five *wŏn*; eight *chŏn* up to ten *wŏn*.
However, if only a request for Collection Mail is made without full cash payment, it will not be dispatched.

One: When remitting a combined payment for *Kaebyŏk*, *Sin Yŏsŏng* (新女性), and *Ŏrini* (어린이), or other publications, please itemize each type and write down the amount.

One: For requests for receipts, return postage is required; for requests for refunds, return shipping fees are absolutely required.<155>

**Summary**

The publisher of *Kaebyŏk* magazine announces revised pricing and stricter payment rules for subscriptions. This change is prompted by confusion and payment errors resulting from the frequent, irregular publication of special and enlarged issues. The notice standardizes the release of enlarged issues to quarterly and mandates prepayment via postal transfer, refusing payment by stamps (except for remote areas with a surcharge), COD, or parcel post, while detailing procedures for collection mail and combined payments for its sister publications (*Sin Yŏsŏng*, *Ŏrini*). This document provides insight into the operational and financial challenges faced by Korean publishers during the colonial era, including managing reader payments, distribution logistics across Chosŏn, Japan, and China, and the economic necessity of standardizing erratic publishing schedules that likely arose from both editorial opportunities and production constraints. Historians may find the specific details on payment methods (postal transfers, collection mail refusal of stamps), distribution limitations, and the explicit mention of financial difficulties revealing about the business side of cultural production under colonial rule.
