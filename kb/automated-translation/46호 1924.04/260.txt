**Body Text Translation:**

A Glimpse into the Customs of the Hoseo Region Before the Kap'o Year [1894]
						By Yi Chong-nin

It seems to be common sentiment that whenever anyone talks about their family or hometown, they try as much as possible to hide the bad and speak of the good and beautiful. Therefore, it is also customary to trust an English or Japanese view of American history more than an American's American history, and to trust a Korean or Russian view of China more than a Chinese person's view of China. Now, no matter how much the author transcends the common sentiments and customs of this world in writing about this issue, because the author's hometown is in the Hoseo region (湖中), it will be viewed like an American's American history or a Chinese person's view of China. However, the author's hometown is Korea, not the Hoseo region. No, the world is my hometown. Therefore, I have nowhere to borrow good and beautiful things from, even if I wanted to fabricate them, and nowhere to push away the inferior things, even if I wanted to discard them. Ah—like a tiger larger than the mountain (虎大於山), the introduction has already taken up half of this narrow space.

The 500-year history of the Yi Dynasty is a history of power struggles among the *yangban*. Though it may seem insignificant if singled out, when discussing Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do (忠南), there is nothing else to talk about without mentioning the *yangban*. If the *yangban* in all of Korea before the Kap'o year (甲午) were ten parts, Ch'ungch'ŏng-do (忠淸道) occupied six or seven parts, and if the *yangban* of Ch'ungch'ŏng-do were ten parts, Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do clearly occupied two-thirds. With the exception of T'aean County (泰安郡), the *yangban* were so dominant that even those holding positions like Provincial Magistrate (牧府使) or Military and Naval Commander (兵水使) could not act like *yangban*. Anyway, the fact that Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do held the top position among the Eight Provinces before the Kap'o year was due to the virtue of the *yangban*, and the fact that Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do became an insignificant province among the Thirteen Provinces after the Ŭlmi year (乙未) [1895] is the fault of the *yangban*. The 300-year life of the people of Ch'ungch'ŏng-do was lived for the sake of propriety, righteousness, integrity, shame, morality, learning, status, and honor; there was truly nothing done for the sake of actually living. The one who died of starvation holding books (抱書餓死) in a windy-windowed, snowy house (風窓雪屋) was considered the purest and noblest person of character. The one who ruined his family (傾家破産) for the complicated formalities and empty rituals (煩文虛式) of coming-of-age, marriage, funerals, and ancestral rites (冠婚喪祭), and then lived well-clothed and well-fed (好衣好食) through the favor of others, while not paying national taxes (국세) for over ten years, was considered the most outstanding hero (호걸). Consequently, the administration of a Provincial Governor (監司) or County Magistrate (郡守) was merely running errands for the *yangban* of that district; even criminals guilty of murder or robbery could not be dealt with uniformly according to the law. This much should show how rampant (跋扈) those called *yangban* were before the Kap'o year. The common people suffered so much under the *yangban* that they could not even mount a single popular uprising (民擾) for 300 years. For that reason, the great revolution of the Kap'o Tonghak (甲午 東學) exploded here. Is this the author's pride, or shame? <149>

**Summary**

The author, Yi Chong-nin, critiques the dominance of the *yangban* class in the Ch'ungch'ŏng region (specifically Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do) before the Kabo Reforms of 1894 (甲午). He argues that while the *yangban* were responsible for the region's prominence in the past Yi Dynasty, their excessive focus on status, empty rituals (like elaborate ceremonies leading to bankruptcy), and suppression of the populace ultimately led to the region's decline and hindered real progress. The author attributes the outbreak of the Tonghak Revolution in this region directly to the extreme suffering the common people endured under *yangban* rule for centuries. From a historical perspective, the article provides a contemporary (1924) critical view of the pre-modern *yangban* system's negative impacts, linking its rigid social structure and exploitation directly to the peasant uprisings like Tonghak. Historians might find the author's strong regional focus and his assigning of both past glory and subsequent decline solely to the *yangban* class particularly interesting, as well as his ambivalent closing question about whether this legacy is a source of pride or shame.

甲午 以前의 湖俗一班
						李鍾麟
누구던지 자기 가정이나 고향 이약이를 하라면 될 수 잇는 대로는 劣惡한 것은 숨기고 善美한 것을 말하는 것이 常情일 듯하다. 그럼으로 米人의 米史보다 英人이나 日人의 米史觀을 信하며 支那人의 支那史보다 朝鮮人이나 露西亞人의 支那觀을 信하는 것이 또한 통례이다. 이졔 필자가 아모리 이 세상의 常情과 통례를 초월하야 이 문제를 쓴다 하야도 필자의 家鄕이 湖中인 까닭에 米人의 米史 支那人의 支那史로 觀하리라. 그러나 필자의 家鄕은 朝鮮이고 湖中은 안니다. 아니 세계가 곳 나의 家鄕이다. 그럼으로 나는 선미한 것을 꾸어올내야 꾸어올 대가 업고 劣惡한 것을 떠밀내야 떠밀 곳이 업다. 아- 虎大於山이라고 죱은 지면에 緖言이 벌셔 절반을 잡앗다.
李朝 500년사는 兩班의 勢力爭奪史다. 뽑으면 보잘 것 업는 것 가티 忠南 이약이에 兩班을 빼고는 다시 말할 거리가 업다. 甲午 이전 朝鮮 全道 兩班이 10이라 하면 여섯 일곱 즘은 忠淸道가 차지하엿고 忠淸道 兩班이 10이라면 忠南이 3분지 2는 녁녁히 차지하엿다. 泰安 1郡을 제한 외에는 兩班이 엇지나 드세던지 牧府使 兵水使가튼 집은 兩班인 체도 목하엿다. 엇젓던 甲午 이전 忠南이 8道에 수위를 점령한 것도 兩班의 德이고 乙未 이후 13도 중 忠南이 보잘 것 업는 忠南된 것도 兩班의 罪이다. 忠淸道 사람의 300년 생활은 禮義 廉恥 道德 學問 地位 名譽 등 그것을 하기 위하야 살앗고 졍말 살랴고 한 일은 하나도 업다. 風窓雪屋에셔 抱書餓死한 이가 가장 淸高한 인격자요. 冠婚喪祭의 煩文虛式을 위하야 傾家破産하고 나서는 남의 덕으로 好衣好食하며 국세를 10년 이상이나 안니 무는 이가 가장 출중한 호걸이엿다. 그리하야 監司나 郡守의 행정이라 하는 것은 그 골 兩班의 심부렴 뿐 살인 강도의 죄인까지라도 법대로 한가지 處決치 못하엿다. 이만하면 甲午 이전 兩班이라는 그네들의 그 얼마나 跋扈하엿던 것을 알 것이다. 민중이 엇덧캐나 兩班위 서러운 마젓던지 300년 간 民擾 한 번 못 일으켯다. 그 까닭으로 甲午 東學의 大革命이 여긔서 폭발되엿다. 이것이 필자의 자랑니냐. 붓그러움니냐.＜149＞
