Article Title: 내 못 견데여 하노라 (I Cannot Endure)
Author: 趙明熙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

I Cannot Endure
						Cho Myŏnghŭi

You whom I welcomed grow distant,
And though I long for you who are distant,
That I grieve anew over this,
Even I do not understand my own heart.
Let the flowers fall, let the water flow,
But having no place to rest this heart, I cannot endure. -3,9-<26>

**Summary**

This short poem by Cho Myŏnghŭi expresses deep sorrow and longing for someone who has departed. The speaker is confused by their own persistent grief and feels an unbearable sense of displacement, contrasting their inner turmoil with the indifferent passage of nature (falling flowers, flowing water). From a historical perspective, the poem reflects personal anguish during the colonial period. While not explicitly political, the expression of profound sadness and a feeling of being unable to endure might resonate with the broader sense of loss or helplessness experienced under colonial rule, making it of interest to historians studying the emotional landscape of the era.

반기든 그대 머러지고
머러진 그대 그리옵거날,
