**Body Text Translation:**

Company Announcement

From Chaos to Order, from Order to Expansion—thus proceeds the Kaebyŏk Movement (開闢運動), advancing horizontally day by day. Adding a golden whip (金鞭) to the indomitable (百折不屈) swift horse capable of a thousand *li* (千里駿驄), we are renewing the P'yŏngyang Branch Office in the west, reviving the Kwangju Branch Office and newly establishing the Masan Branch Office in the south. Through this, we intend to fulfill our active, heaven-sent duty (積極的 天職) of renewing the mountains and rivers (山河 一新) across cities and countryside (都鄙縱橫). We earnestly hope (顒望) that our esteemed readers (愛讀者 諸賢) will exert further effort (一層 奮發) and offer even more support (更 一層 聲援) to help complete this practical enterprise (實事業) of the Rebirth Movement (更生運動).
Kaebyŏksa (開闢社)

**Summary**

This announcement from Kaebyŏksa, the publisher of *Kaebyŏk* magazine, details the company's expansion efforts in 1924. It highlights the reorganization of the P'yŏngyang branch, the reopening of the Kwangju branch, and the establishment of a new branch in Masan. The announcement frames this expansion as part of the broader "Kaebyŏk Movement" and "Rebirth Movement," emphasizing progress and a mission to renew the nation, calling on readers for continued support. For historians, this text reveals the organizational structure and geographical reach of a major Korean cultural and nationalist publication during the colonial period. It showcases the active expansion strategy despite the challenging environment, lists key personnel involved in regional operations, and uses nationalist rhetoric ("renewing the mountains and rivers") common in cultural movements of the era, linking business expansion directly to a larger national project.
