Article Title: 이 생, 저 생, 이 몸. (This Life, That Life, This Body.)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

This Life, That Life, This Body.
						Kim Sŏksong

That this life was such a place,
Truly, I did not know.

That this life was such a place,
Now, I have come to know.

That this life was such a place,
Whether I knew or did not know,
For this body born into this life,
There is utterly no way to fix it anymore.

That the afterlife is peaceful,
Death tempts me.<27>

That the afterlife is splendid,
Faith tempts me.

However peaceful the afterlife may be,
However splendid the afterlife may be,
For this body born into this life,
It is just like stepping on a shadow.

This body, suffering even in this life,
How could it be peaceful in the afterlife?
This body, burdensome even in this life,
How could it be precious in the afterlife?

Both this life and the afterlife are all in vain,
What then shall I do with this body?
Since it is this body born into this life,
Still, I intend to fight with this life.(2,7) <28>
<27-28>

**Summary**

The poem expresses a deep disillusionment with life ("이생") and a rejection of the comforting illusions of an afterlife ("저생"). The speaker realizes the harsh reality of existence and feels trapped within their current life, acknowledging that there's no way to change their fundamental condition. Despite temptations of peace or splendor offered by death or faith concerning the afterlife, the speaker dismisses them as irrelevant illusions ("like stepping on a shadow") for someone bound to this life. Concluding that both this life and the next seem futile, the speaker resolves, not to escape, but to confront and struggle within the confines of their present existence. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, reflects a profound existential angst possibly mirroring the suffering and lack of agency felt by Koreans. The rejection of otherworldly solace in favor of confronting present hardship ("fight with this life") could be interpreted as a subtle call for engagement with immediate reality, however bleak, which might resonate with historians studying the psychological and philosophical responses to colonial rule.

이 생, 저 생, 이 몸.
						金石松
이생이 이러한 곳인 줄을,
내 정말 몰낫소이다.

이생이 이러한 곳인 줄을,
내 이제 알앗소이다.

이생이 이러한 곳인 줄을,
몰낫거니 알앗거니,
이생을 타고난 이 몸으로는,
다시는 곳칠 길 바이 업소이다.

저생이 편안하다고,
죽음이 나를 꾀입니다.＜27＞

저생이 화려하다고,
미듬이 나를 꾀입니다.

저생이 아모리 편안하대도,
저생이 아모리 화려하대도,
이생을 타고난 이 몸으로는,
그림자 밟기나 일반이외다.

이생에서도 괴로운 이 몸이,
저생에선들 무엇이 편하리오.
이생에서도 궈찬은 이 몸이,
저생에선들 무엇이 귀하리오.

이생 저생이 모도다 헛되오니,
이 몸을 장차 엇지나 하오릿가.
이생을 타고난 이 몸이오니,
그래도 이생과 싹호랴 합니다.(2,7) ＜28＞
＜27-28＞
