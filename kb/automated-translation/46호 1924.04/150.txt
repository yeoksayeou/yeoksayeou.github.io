**Body Text Translation:**
Is It a Final Scream or a Novel Begging? Regarding the So-called Pact of Various Faction Leaders

The eleven organizations that make a profession or pretense of being pro-Japanese as usual – Kungmin Hyŏphoe (國民協會), Sojakin Sangjohoe (小作人相助會), Ch'ŏngnimgyo (靑林敎), Taishō Ch'inmokhoe (大正親睦會 - Taishō Friendship Society), Yuminhoe (維民會), Tonggwanghoe (同光會), Yudo Chinhŭnghoe (儒道振興會 - Society for the Promotion of Confucianism), Kungminhoe (國民會), Chosŏn Kyŏngjehoe (朝鮮經濟會 - Korean Economic Association), Sangaehoe (相愛會), Kyop'unghoe (矯風會) (most of which exist in name only) – under the guise of a so-called "Alliance of Leaders from Various Factions," have loudly proclaimed anti-independence and anti-revolution (this is a definite new invention, not seen in their pronouncements a few years ago). They have pledged to greatly exert their dog-and-horse loyalty (犬馬의 忠), advocating the three articles of government-people unity (官民一致), grand unity (大同團結), and labor-capital harmony (勞資協調).

Thinking about it, such performances by these sorts of fellows have been repeated since who knows when, and it's hardly worth making a fuss about anew. But for what necessity do they concoct such a format again? Perhaps it is because they have fallen into such dire straits that they cannot maintain even their meager existence without making such a pact, or else they have reached a situation where they cannot move that large bundle placed over there without putting on at least such a show. To put it briefly, it is either a final scream lamenting their own downfall, or else it is a kind of novel begging, trying out a change in style like this.

If it is a final scream, then there should be some final desperate act; if it is novel begging, then they should prepare some materials worthy of registration. That is, since they say "government-people unity," they should perhaps perform their dog-and-horse service (犬馬의 役) to the authorities more faithfully; since they say "grand unity," they should perhaps submit a petition for political participation under the joint names of the various factions to the upcoming provisional assembly; since they say "labor-capital harmony," should they not perhaps draw upon even a share of the money hunted by the Japanese exploiting class (日本搾取級)? Thus, while further dedicating themselves to their natural duty as a special action unit (別働隊) (for the current authorities), they will likely not neglect to act as an opposing body either. In any case, our general populace needs to pay deep attention to their actions, while also recognizing the need to prepare measures for the people to protect themselves for the true benefit of the masses.

Alas, is there any other reason for all this? It is solely for the sake of eating and putting on airs, and for the sake of eating and putting on airs, they have reached the point of daring to adopt even the form of being Koreans who oppose Korean independence, and proletarians who oppose the revolution of the propertyless. Every time we encounter such examples, we feel the need to achieve a fundamental resolution to this economic problem and power problem even one day sooner, as quickly as possible, and we feel once again that for this resolution, there is only the uprising (蹶起) of each individual among the masses. (Reporter's note: Due to the editing deadline, this is all for now - 25th night) <105>

**Summary**

This article from the magazine *Gaebyeok* critiques a newly announced alliance of eleven pro-Japanese organizations in colonial Korea. The author dismisses the alliance as either a desperate last gasp of irrelevant groups facing decline ("final scream") or a calculated maneuver to gain favor or resources from the Japanese authorities ("novel begging"). The article mocks their stated goals of "government-people unity," "grand unity," and "labor-capital harmony," predicting they will merely intensify their role as collaborators ("special action unit") while potentially feigning opposition elsewhere. It urges the Korean public to be vigilant and emphasizes the need for the masses to rise up and address the fundamental economic and power imbalances underlying such collaboration, driven ultimately by self-interest. For historians, this piece offers insight into the contemporary nationalist critique of pro-Japanese collaborators, highlighting the perceived motives (survival, gain) and tactics of these groups. It also showcases the explicit linking of collaboration to economic issues and the call for popular mobilization against both colonial rule and its facilitators in 1924.

기사형태	소식

最後의 悲鳴이냐 新案의 求乞이냐 所謂 各派有志의 縱約에 對하야
國民協會, 小作人相助會, 靑林敎, 大正親睦會, 維民會, 同光會, 儒道振興會, 國民會, 朝鮮經濟會, 相愛會, 矯風會 등 例의 親日을 직업 혹은 행세로 하는 11개의 단체(대개는 有名無實)는, 소위 各派有志의 聯盟이란 형식 밋헤서, 反獨立, 反革命(이것은, 몃해 전의 그들의 표방에서는 보지 못하던, 확실한 新發明)을 揚言하고, 官民一致, 大同團結, 勞資協調의 3개 條를 표방하야, 크게 犬馬의 忠을 盡할 事를 誓約하얏다.
생각컨대, 이러한 輩流의 이러한 興演은 언제보터 反覆되든 바로서, 새삼스럽게 떠들어 말할 거리도 못되는 일이거니와, 대체 그들은 무슨 필요로, 또 그와 가튼 형식을 지여내는 것일가. 모르거니와, 그것은, 그들이 그만한 縱約이라도 하지 아느면, 그만한 존재이나마 유지하지 못할 窮境에 빠젓거나 그러치 안으면 져 한편에 노힌 크다라한 무슨 뭉치를, 젹어도 그만한 형세를 꾸미지 안코는 옴겨올 수 업는 경우에 니르럿슴으로써 일갓이다. 주려 말하면 自家의 沒落을 自弔하는 最後의 悲鳴이거나, 그러치 아느면, 이러케 한 번 式樣을 변해본다는 일종의 新案의 求乞일 것이다.
最後의 悲鳴이냐, 그러면 最後의 發惡이라도 잇슬 것이오, 新案의 求乞이냐, 그러면 등록하기에 족할 무슨 자료라도 준비할 것이다. 즉 官民一致라 하엿스니, 당국에 대한 犬馬의 役이나마 좀 더 착실히 할 것이오, 大同團結이라 하엿스니, 오는 臨時議會에 各派聯名의 叅政願書라도 제출할 것이오, 勞資協調라 하엿스니, 日本搾取級의 獵錢分이라도 끌어 쓸 것이 아닐가. 그래서 그들은 자기의 천직인 別働隊(現當局의)의 임무에 일층 忍勉하는 동시에, 별로 反對團軆의 작용을 하기에도 등한하지 안을 것이다. 여하간, 우리의 일반 민중은 그들의 所爲에 대해서, 심심한 주의를 拂하는 동시에, 민중의 진정한 福益을 위하야는, 민중 스사로가 警護하는 策에 나아가지 아느면 안될 것을 각오할 필요가 잇는 것이다.
슬프다, 이것 져것이 都是 다른 까닭이 잇스리요, 오즉 먹기 위함이오 행세키 위함이며, 먹고 행세하기 위하야는 朝鮮 사람으로 朝鮮의 獨立에도 반대하는, 無産級 사람으로 無産의 革命에도 반대하는 형식까지를 敢作하기에 니른 것 뿐이다. 우리는 이러한 실례를 어들 떄마다, 이 經濟問題 權力問題에 대한 근본적 해결을 하루라도, 가급히 할 필요를 늣기는 바이며, 이것의 해결을 위하야는 오즉 민중 각자의, 蹶起가 잇슬 뿐임을 다시금 늣기는 바이다. (記者曰 編輯期의 關係로 爲先 이만 한다-25夜) ＜105＞
