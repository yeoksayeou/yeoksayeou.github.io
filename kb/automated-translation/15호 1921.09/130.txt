Propagator of Baha'i Faith Enters Seoul

Regarding the religion called the Baha'i Faith (빠하-敎), not only is there not a single believer in our Chosŏn (朝鮮), but even its name was known only to a limited segment of人士 (people of standing/intellectuals). However, in late August, an American woman, Miss Alexander (알렉산다-女史), entered Seoul as a propagator of the Baha'i Faith and carried out some activities.

This religion originated in Persia (波斯) in the Western year 1844, a religion started by a great personality called "Habu" (하부) [likely referring to the Báb] who aimed for the reformation of society and the spiritual world. At that time, it faced much public criticism, but afterwards, it gradually became known to the world, and recently, it is said to be gaining strength anew in America (美國). It is said that the spirit of the religion has 12 articles.

1.  The unity and equality of all humankind in the world, 2. The independent investigation of truth, 3. That the fundamental basis of all religions is the same, 4. Religion should be the cause of harmony among humankind, 5. The agreement of religion with science and reason, 6. The equality of both sexes, male and female, 7. The abandonment of all prejudice, 8. Peace throughout the entire world, 9. Universal education common to all nations, 10. The solution of the economic problem, 11. The adoption of a universal auxiliary language, 12. The establishment of an international tribunal, etc.

This is recorded for everyone's reference.<111>

**Summary**

This news report from the September 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine announces the arrival of an American woman, Miss Alexander (likely Agnes Alexander), in Seoul to promote the Baha'i Faith. The article notes the faith's obscurity in Korea at the time, mentions its Persian origins in 1844 with a figure named "Habu" (the Báb), its recent growth in America, and lists its twelve core principles, including unity, equality, harmony between religion and science, world peace, and universal education. For historians, this article is interesting as it documents the very early introduction of the Baha'i Faith into colonial Korea, reflecting the inflow of diverse global ideas despite Japanese rule. It also highlights the progressive principles (gender equality, racial unity, world peace, international language/tribunal) promoted by the faith, which may have held a particular appeal or interest in the context of a colonized nation seeking new frameworks for social and spiritual progress.

빠하-敎의 宣傳者 入京
빠하-敎라는 敎는 우리 朝鮮에 一人의 信者가 업는 것은 勿論이오 그 名稱조차 겨우 一部의 人士에 限하야 알던 바이다. 그런데 去 8月 下旬에 빠하-敎의 宣傳者로서 美國人 알렉산다-女史가 入京하야 多少間의 活動을 行하얏다.
그 敎는 西曆 1844年 其源을 波斯에 發한 것인데 「하부」라 하는 大人格者가 社會와 精神界의 改造를 期하고 起한 宗敎이다. 그 當時는 만히 世人의 指目을 바닷스나 其後 漸次 世間에 알린 바 되어 近日 美國에서 새삼스럽게 其勢를 張하는 중이라 한다. 그 敎의 精神은 12個條가 잇다 하나니.
1. 世界 人類의 統一 平等, 2. 眞理의 獨立硏究, 3. 모든 宗敎의 根本基礎는 同一하다는 것, 4. 宗敎는 人類和合의 原因이 될 것, 5. 宗敎는 科學과 理論의 一致, 6. 男女兩性의 平等, 7. 모든 偏見의 忘却, 8. 全世界의 平和, 9. 萬國共通의 敎育, 10. 經濟問題의 解決, 11. 世界共通語의 採用, 12. 國制裁判의 建設 等.
이라 하는 바 記하야써 여러분의 參考에 供한다.＜111＞
