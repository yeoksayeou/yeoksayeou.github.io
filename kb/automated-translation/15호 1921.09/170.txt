小說 標本室의 靑개고리
						廉想涉
四
The afterglow of the evening sun setting in the Yellow Sea (黃海) pierced through the white clouds and flowed far away, glittering like silver on the roof of the two-story house visible over there.
Our party, having left Y's house, walked about one *chō* (町) north along Ch'uktong (築洞) Street, then turned east at the crossroads and entered a new street. The traffic became a bit more leisurely. I followed along, suffering my usual over-sensitivity, wondering if Y's words were indeed true, or if they were just a kind of deliberate joke aimed at me, as Y was prone to pointless satire and mockery.
"What kind of work did that gentleman originally do?" I asked Y.
"I don't know the details exactly, but... they say he was a teacher (訓導) at a common school!... Didn't A-kun learn from him too?"
"Yeah!... He speaks Japanese quite well... He used to be quite handsome back then. Hahaha."
At the end of A's words, Y also laughed.
"Handsome or ugly, whatever the case, last spring, after spending about three or four months in prison, he supposedly became strange... I don't know the detailed reason..."
"Does he have a wife and children?"
"Yes—they say the wife went to her parents' home, or that they separated (놀아낫다), but again, I don't know the details." A answered.<141>
"Y-kun, what if that woman was lured away by some guy, wandered around selling herself, and ended up rolling into that red-light district (遊廓)?" I interjected, having been walking silently.
"Hmph!... And then he goes looking for her every day, pretending to be crazy..." Y made a matching retort.
Leaving the new street, we came out onto the middle of a field covered in weeds turning yellow. Taking the road running north-south, we turned north towards Yu-chō (柳町). From the two-story building of the red-light district, visible about ten *kan* away, electric lights were already twinkling and spilling out.
"Ah! There it is..." A pointed towards something like a lone melon lookout hut (참외원두막) standing tall on the slope of a low mountain opposite us. Seeing something whitish (힛금으레한) twitching (움즐움즐하는) up there, we quickened our pace.
After walking about ten steps further, I pointed to the left, saying, "This is the red-light district." Between the eaves lanterns (軒燈) densely installed in a line, where the electricity had just come on, ghostly figures with only their faces stark white, returning from the public bath (沐浴湯), carrying basins (大也) filled with cosmetics, wandered here and there through the desolate valley. It looked more bleak (凄凉히) than gaudy (浮華하다).
"The teacher gets quite a bit of benefit here too... This is still the only place that gives him a bottle of liquor for every lecture (講演)..." A explained, smiling.
The master of the house, who had been sitting sprawled on the very top of the three-story structure, idly gazing at the mountain ridge where the faint sunlight gradually receded, glanced back as we noisily approached. In an instant, he turned around again and was tapping (똑딱똑딱) away at something. We hesitated in front of the fence, loosely woven with bush clover, looking for an entrance, then simply stepped over it and strode in.
A, who entered first, reached up, put his hand on the third story where the master sat with his back turned, peered in, and greeted him,
"Teacher! We're here again."
"Teacher! How are you?" A laughed out loud and greeted him again. But the man remained seated, hammering a nail into the door panel of a chest (籠欌門짝). A and Y simultaneously looked back at H and me, winked, and laughed silently.<142>
"...Are you feeling unwell?... We came today specifically to hear your lecture..." This time, Y started the banter (酬酌). Only then did the man, as if startled, whip around his shaggy head covered in dust.
"Yes? You've come?" he replied simply, and turning back again, picked up his hammer. The three men burst into laughter. However, when I glimpsed his fist-sized white face (相), covered densely with black, brush-like whiskers (鬚髥) from the ears down, I gasped, "Ah!" and was startled. As if struck by electricity (感電), a chill ran through my chest, and a violent trembling (戰慄) overwhelmed my entire body. Then, in the next moment, an unusual suspicion and fierce curiosity surged like a tide in my somewhat relieved chest. I had thought the natural history teacher from the middle school laboratory had followed us. But without any reason, unconsciously, a feeling of reverence or solemnity (崇嚴한 感) seemed to push at the back of my head, and I inadvertently (無心中間에) took off my hat and bowed. Seeing the others chuckling, I felt embarrassed, as if I were doing something greatly disrespectful (不敬한), and conversely, it seemed impudent. Or perhaps I worried that this man, getting upset, might suddenly jump down and assault us.
"Teacher! You really seem to be feeling unwell!" A, growing impatient, spoke again. "A guest has come all the way from Sŏul (徐鬱), please give us a lecture, ha..."
The master, who had been pushing down and stuffing back in grimy clothes and laundry bundles that kept spilling out, opening and closing the repaired door panel, seemed to have caught the words "guest from Sŏul." He turned towards us and opened his mouth.
"Yes—I've caught a bit of a cold," he said, looking down directly at me with dull, lusterless (映彩업는) eyes, then,
"...As you can see, I've started this construction work (役事),..." he glanced around the room, then fixed his gaze on me again,
"These days, I don't even have time to blink.... Moreover, with lectures day after day (連日), my throat is completely hoarse..." He finished speaking and looked towards H.
However, his throat didn't sound particularly hoarse. When Y introduced H and me,
"Ah—Is that so? From Sŏul... You've come a long way." As if pleased, "I am Kim Ch'angok (金昌億) from Nam-p'o (南浦)." A slight<143> smile even appeared on his face.
"Yes—I am XXX," I replied courteously, taking off my hat. H also finished his greetings.
"Teacher! You have a good memory... You didn't forget your name... Hahaha," A teased.
Ch'angok didn't respond to that but turned to me.
"Please come up a bit. The work isn't finished yet, so there's no reception room (應接室), but..." he urged me repeatedly to come up, adding,
"I'll eventually put a stove (스토-프) in here, and when guests come, they should be able to sit down and share a cup of wine, but..." He pointed to the makeshift shelf-like structure, the so-called second floor.
The three men laughed loudly. But he, thinking there was not the slightest unnatural exaggeration in his words, looked down blankly at the laughing people as if their laughter was strange, snorted "Hmph," and looked away. I couldn't tell whether it would be better to call this man crazy or a great philosopher who had achieved great enlightenment (大悟) and liberation (解脫) from everything.
"It would be too presumptuous (惶悚하야) for us to go up, but could you perhaps give us a lecture?" This time, H teased him.
"Well, you've come all this way, but I'm sorry I don't even have a cup of wine to offer." He changed the subject (딴전을 부텻다). It was strange that he kept bringing up wine talk (술 이악이) upon first meeting someone.
"All the guests who come here are sons of God, so they say they don't drink alcohol," Y, who had been standing listening to the conversation with a constant smile, chimed in.
"Eh? Do you also believe in Jesus, sir?" He looked across at me as if surprised, then chuckled, "hee hee hee."
"It seems the Jesus army (예수軍) is also full of ignorant fools.... When the Jesus folks pray, don't they say, 'God the Father! Forgive my sins, Amen (아-맹)'?... But what is 'Amen'? Calling Mencius (孟子), an orator for the ages, a stutterer by saying 'Ah-maeng' (啞孟 - Mute Meng)? Ah, where could such ignorant words be? If you're asking to be forgiven for your sins,<144> shouldn't you say 'Ah-myŏn' (我免 - I am exempted)?" Having launched into the introduction of his lecture, he beamed with satisfaction and chuckled "hee hee." The surrounding people also laughed. But apart from intuitively sensing that he was an extraordinary fantasist, I couldn't tell if I had laughed or not.
Seeing several people laughing along, he became even more inspired and continued his lecture.—
"But God is truly utterly impartial and selfless (至公無私). That I... was able to build this three-story house in just one month and thirteen days (1個月 13日) for only thirty-five *nyang*—it's all God's grace (恩澤).
No matter how much Westerners boast about civilization or the development of machinery, where is there a guy who built a three-story house for just thirty-five *nyang*?... God comes to see it every day and praises it."
"Since he praises it, it seems impartial and selfless, right?" H interjected.
"Absolutely not! This was all done because there was God's command (分付)... Now that the judgment (審判) by fire is over, and the time has come for the world to form one great family, He ordered the organization of the East-West Friendship Association (東西親睦會). Therefore, I first built this office and became the Chairman, but it's a big problem because there's no Supervisor (監督官) to inspect (巡察) the disputes of various countries."
Everyone burst out laughing with a "Waaah—".
"How about Mr. X here?" Y slapped my shoulder and quickly nominated me.
"Well, if he'd do it, I'd be grateful..."
The three men shouted, "Yay—, Your Excellency, Supervisor of the East-West Friendship Association!" and laughed even louder.
Sure enough, dangling among the straw mat fragments and tangerine crate pieces hanging haphazardly under the eaves (簷下), was a piece of cardboard (馬糞紙) like a cigarette box lid, on which was thickly written "East-West Friendship Association Headquarters" (東西親睦會本部), and beside it, "Chairman Kim Ch'angok" (會長 金昌億), attached with a tack. I, still holding my hat, stood with folded arms, turned my eyes from staring at the cardboard to the Chairman of the East-West Friendship Association and asked,
"What is the purpose (趣旨) of the association?"
"As I mentioned earlier, hasn't the judgment by fire taught in the Bible ended? The horrific smoke of cannons and rain of bullets (砲煙彈雨) of the Great European War was precisely the judgment by fire. But why did this war break out?... This world is an era of materialism (物質萬能), where money is almighty (金錢萬能),<145> there is no benevolence, righteousness, propriety, or wisdom (仁義禮智), no five moral rules (五倫), and no love. Isn't it because people's hearts have been tainted by greed due to this materialism?... Father and son, brothers look at each other with hostility and jealousy (反目嫉視), husband and wife are discordant, neighbor fights neighbor, village fights village... and thus, nation fights nation. Isn't it ultimately because people's hearts are obscured by material desire? Thus, according to the great principle of the survival of the fittest (弱肉强食), all nations of the world came to confront each other with weapons (干戈), which was precisely the Great European War. However, now the judgment by fire is all over. The era for East and West to be friendly has arrived, I believe, according to God's word. Therefore, according to God's revelation (啓示), I must travel around the world policing it... I won't be here long either. After studying a bit more... I must travel around England, America, France, Germany, see the famous sights of the world, and preach (說敎)."
Finishing his words, he knelt down, rustled around on the shelf, found a cigarette butt he had put out earlier, put it in his mouth, and sat down again.
"Teacher! Then, when will you enter Kŭmgang-san (金剛山)?" A teased.
"After I've traveled everywhere once, I'll go in—"
"Then, what happens to me? How can I wait until then?"
"Hmm?..." He opened his eyes wide and stared at A.
"Ah, Teacher, you seem to have become senile (妄佞)... You said you'd make me a county governor or something when you go into Kŭmgang-san..."
Everyone clapped their hands and roared with laughter (拍掌大笑).
"Hmm— I'll appoint you before I go—"
There was not a trace of joking in his words. When he passionately delivered his fluent, oratorical speech, he seemed to possess some indubitable conviction.
"But is the jade throne (玉座) in Kŭmgang-san ready yet?" Y asked, smiling.<141-146>
"Yes—, they said it was prepared on the day this house was completed," he said, waiting for everyone's laughter to subside.
"Among surnames, Kim (金) is the best surname. Jade (玉) comes from K'un-kang (崐崗 - Kunlun Mountains), but doesn't gold (金) come from Yŏsu (麗水 - Beautiful Waters/a place associated with gold)? Therefore, God says, since you are Kim-ka (金哥 - Brother Kim), go into Kŭmgang-san, which is mountainous and has beautiful waters (山高水麗), sit upon the jade throne, and let the world enjoy peace..." he trailed off, looking at me, who was standing silently still, seeking my sympathy with a faint smile.
"That's truly excellent... But what thought led you to build this three-story house?" I asked thus.
"During the summer vacation the year before last, when I went up to Sŏul to attend a Japanese language course (日語講習) at Chungdong School (中東學校), I saw Westerners' houses. They seemed hygienic and fit for human living, so I thought, why shouldn't we Koreans also live comfortably? If I'm going to build, why not build it three stories high (놉즉이)...
What makes us inferior to those guys? I've attended church a bit too, but I don't think there are any bastards as ignorant and fond of flattery as those guys... Heh, especially those called pastors or whatever, dressed up (띌처 입고) in leftover pieces of Western clothes as if attending the Great Regent (大院君) in his prime, bowing and scraping under those filthy bastards. When I see them wandering around, I want to smash their heads with this fist..." He raised his dark, rough fist. At that moment, an unusual gleam shone in his eyes, and his face twitched convulsively (痙攣的), trembling and contorted. I looked up absently and was startled.
"But the weather is gradually getting colder... What do you intend to do?" I changed the subject thus.
"Cold? Inside God's embrace, it's spring all four seasons.... But I'll put a stove (스토-프) here," he said, pointing to the second floor.
"So, where did you order (註文) the stove from?" Someone beside him interjected.
"Order? What order!..." he snapped, sounding highly displeased. "Do you think only Westerners know how to make (맨들줄) stoves and I can't?... If it's work they do in a day, I can make it in half a day. Do you know how many days this house took?... Just one month and thirteen days! Westerners say the number thirteen is unlucky, but even while building a Western-style house, I finished it in exactly one month and thirteen days."<147>
"If you were told to go east, but just went west, that's enough, isn't it?" H retorted.
"Exactly. The people of the world are nothing but perverse (빙통그러진) fools who run west when told to go east.... Isn't this a wretched world where, even though there's a Korean language and Korean script, people only get respect if they use Chinese characters or the tongue-twisting (혀 꼽으러진) words of Westerners?"
As if seeking my agreement with each phrase, he looked straight down at me, pausing momentarily, then finally poured out words like a passionate orator without rest.—
"Yes! Isn't that so, yes!... And if only they could read it correctly, that would be good, but... For example, when reading '天地玄黃' (Ch'ŏnjihyŏnhwang - Heaven, Earth, Black, Yellow), they read it as 'ha-nal ch'ŏn' (하날 텬 - sky ch'ŏn). Why don't they just say 'sky' for '一大' (il-tae - one big)? Isn't it because the blue sky (蒼穹) is the sole greatest thing in the universe that a great figure like Cangjie (蒼頡) wrote it as '一大'? Also, saying 'tta-di' (따-디) for what should be 'hŭk ya' (흙 야 - earth ya) is also wrong. What is 'tta-'? Isn't it earth (흙)? That's why the character '也' (ya), the very last character of the Thousand Character Classic (千字文), meaning 'also/particle', is written next to the earth radical (土邊). In other words, 'tta-' is earth, and because it occupies the lowest position (最末位) in the universe, the earth radical (土) is combined with the character '也' (ya), the final character of the Thousand Character Classic."
We stood listening, amazed.
"Then, how is the character for gold, '金' (Kim/kŭm), formed, that God loves Brother Kim (金哥) so much?" Y asked.
"That's the right question. Yes—, you've asked very well..." Beaming with satisfaction, as if grateful and pleased to be reminded of something he had almost forgotten, he began his lecture with his usual torrential eloquence.
"Inside the character for person '人' (in), isn't there a jade '玉' (ok), and a dot '.' on one side?... Therefore, it shouldn't be read 'soe kŭm' (쇠 금 - metal gold) but 'saram kusŭl kŭm' (사람구슬 금 - person-jewel gold). "
Everyone snickered "kick kick kick".
"No. It's not something to laugh about... To fulfill the role of a person (사람구슬을 할랴면), mustn't one first be benevolent (仁), as the sages and virtuous men (聖賢) taught?<148> That's why it's 'saram in' (사람 인 - person in). Next, a person must have two 'jewels' (구슬); if what should be right there between the spread legs like this (人 - he mimed writing the character with his finger) is missing, one absolutely cannot pass for a person. You might say a eunuch is still a person even without them, but that's why they can't fulfill the person-jewel role (사람구슬을 못하지), isn't it? Hee hee hee... Whatever the case, aren't those two things the treasure (寶貝) that allows a person to pass for a person? That's why a dot was added to the character for jade '玉', the foremost among treasures!... "
A, who had been bending over laughing at every phrase, interjected,
"So that's why you made the jade throne (玉座) in Kŭmgang-san... hahaha," and laughed again.
"Then women don't have the 'Kim-ka' (金哥), do they?" This time, H teased. The man sat with his eyes just blinking blankly, as if thinking about something, then suddenly,
"Right! Right! That's why my wife (宅內) is An-ka (安哥),... hmm!... hee hee hee. A woman wearing a crown (冠)... a woman wearing a crown... hee hee hee hee." Like someone talking in his sleep, he looked from one person to another, nodding his head, and then chuckled "hee hee hee" two or three times.
"Right, where did the lady go?" Hearing him say "my wife is An-ka," my curiosity about his wife's whereabouts suddenly arose, and I asked this.
"Eh? You didn't see her?... Hey, hey, Yŏnghŭi's mother! Yŏnghŭi's mother!..." He twisted his body, crouched down, looked below, and called out. Then, as if talking to himself, "Did she go out again!" he straightened up.
"She probably went over there," he said, pointing towards the red-light district (遊廓).
"She's causing trouble again... hahaha, this is serious. If you don't tie her up..." A cut in, teasing.
"Hee hee hee, that's originally her home over there."<149>
"Isn't that the red-light district?" H also asked, laughing.
"Because a woman wears a crown (冠)... hahaha." This time, Y chimed in.
The man seemed to have a thought, tilted his head, and sat there, then said,
"Yes—, she's in there... in there. Even during the 5 years she lived with me, she was still in there. Hee hee hee, hee hee hee hee."
"...In there,... in there!" I thought of A's earlier mention of a rumor that she had run away, and repeated the words inwardly.
"Call her out for a bit." - A
"She'll come at night now. When it's time to sleep..."
"That's right... She's only useful at bedtime, right? Hahaha," H added jokingly, which I thought was inappropriate.
"Hee hee hee. But she says it's too hot, she's dying... Yesterday, she got beaten badly (鍛鍊을 밧고) by the gatekeeper and came crying, so I lit a fire and let her sleep on the bed (寢臺) before sending her off... hee hee hee."
As if chasing some phantom (幻像), he gazed at the distant mountains, baring his yellowed teeth and chuckling "hee hee hee." His face looked as base (卑劣하게) as a monkey's (猿숭い).
The sunlight, which had been gradually receding from the mountain ridge, disappeared silently without notice, and only the electric lights directly opposite on the second floor of the red-light district shone warmly.
Feeling acutely fatigued (疲勞) amidst the roar of laughter (哄笑), clamor (喧談), and mockery (嘲弄), he huddled, gripping his fuzzy head that resembled a rotten chestnut, atop the three-story structure. The chilly autumn evening wind, wandering the gloomy mountain base that was growing dusky, and the sinister (陰凶하고) and desolate (寂寥한) darkness, gnashing black teeth, rushed in with sighs, biting and shaking. He sat leaning against a pillar, seemingly oblivious to anyone nearby.
"Shall we go now?" The words came out weakly from someone's mouth.<150>
We exchanged farewell greetings with the Chairman of the East-West Friendship Association—the advocate for world peace—the martyr (殉難者) of a strange fate—the favored retainer (寵臣) of the Holy Spirit, drunk on the sweet wine of imagination and illusion in the world of dream and reality (夢現)—the holy disciple (聖徒) of Buddha who had attained liberation from the five desires and six defilements, the seven misfortunes and eight sufferings, and forsaken all ties (諸綠) of the fleeting world (浮世). With lips tainted by mockery, we stepped outside the fence.
Having reached the base of the fence, I turned back again towards him. As I met him jumping down from the second floor, he saw the whiskey bottle in my hand and chuckled "hee hee." I pressed the liquor bottle, leftover from Y's house, into his hand, then took two red apples from my pocket and gave them to him.
"This is, really, I feel bad..." He accepted them with a satisfied smile, lined them up beside a folding screen leaned horizontally against the second-floor wall, and followed us out to say goodbye.
Leaving the pitiable fellow behind, I sank into a heavy and gloomy (鬱積한) mood, kept my mouth shut, looked down at the tips of my shoes, and walked absently. Y, also walking silently ahead, paused briefly, looked back, and said,
"X-kun! How was it?"
"Well..."
"...But the sight of you standing there listening to the lecture respectfully with your hat off—even thinking about it now makes me split my sides laughing (腰絶을 하겟서)... hahaha."
"Hmph..." I laughed weakly.
The evening autumn wind briskly licked at the inside of my collar where it touched my neck and fled. By the time our party reached the three-way intersection and parted with A, the face of a person two or three *kan* away appeared only as a hazy white blur. In the wilderness enveloped by the dusk settling ever more softly, the sound of the shamisen (三味線), thin and long, flowing from the red-light district, and the sound of the *changgo* drum, muddy and spreading wide, sometimes fast, sometimes slow, chased our heels urging us towards the train station until some point.
Blown by the cold wind at the foot of the bleak and lonely Northern Mang (北邙 - graveyard hill), the master of the three-story house lay curled up, limbs drawn in. Did he hear that drum sound as a waltz from Heaven, or perceive it as the agonizing cries from Hell? Until I entered the station gate, I kept glancing back, but could only see the electric lights of the red-light district, like ghostly fires (妖火) in a secluded valley (幽谷), twinkling in the darkness. (Unfinished)<151>

**Summary**

The narrator and his friends (Y, A, H) visit Kim Ch'angok, an eccentric man living in a self-built three-story shack near a red-light district in Nam-p'o. Formerly a schoolteacher, Kim became peculiar after a stint in prison. He claims to be the Chairman of the "East-West Friendship Association," founded on God's command after the "judgment by fire" (WWI), and delivers bizarre lectures interpreting the Bible, Chinese characters (claiming 'Amen' should be 'Ah-myŏn' and explaining '金' character based on human anatomy), and discussing his plans to build stoves and travel the world. His wife, he claims, lives in the nearby brothel but visits him at night. The friends mostly mock him, though the narrator feels a mixture of pity and unease. They leave him whiskey and apples as they depart into the darkening evening, haunted by the sounds from the brothel and the image of the lonely man.

From a historical perspective, the story offers glimpses into colonial Korean society: the presence of Japanese place names (Yu-chō) and language use, the mention of common schools (普通學校 訓導), the social marginalization suggested by Kim's imprisonment and descent into apparent madness, and his complex reaction to Western influence (building a Western-style house, criticizing Western missionaries and Korean pastors imitating them, yet seeing WWI as divine judgment). Kim's eccentric interpretations and grand delusions, particularly his anti-establishment sentiments cloaked in religious/philosophical rambling, could reflect the psychological pressures and disorientations experienced under colonial rule.

小說 標本室의 靑개고리
						廉想涉
四
黃海에 잠긴 夕陽의 後光은 白雲을 뚤코 흘러 멀리 바라보이는 저 便 二層집 집웅에, 銀빗으로 반짝어리엇다.
Y의 집에서 나온 우리 一行은, 築洞巨里를, 1町쯤 北으로 가다가, 十字路에서 東으로 꼽쳐, 새 巨里로 들어섯다. 往來가 좀 從容하게 되엇다. 나는 Y의 말이, 果然 事實인가. 實업슨 諷刺나 嘲弄을 잘하는 Y의 말이라 或은 나에게 對한 一種의 偶意를 품은 弄談이 아닌가 하는 例의 神經過敏的 解釋을 하며 딸아 오다가
「先生은, 元來 무엇을 하던 사람인구?」-Y에게 무럿다.
「별로 仔細히는 모르지만,... 普通學校 訓導라던가!... A君도 아마 배왓다지?」
「응!... 日本말도 제법하는데... 以前에는 그래도 美男子이엇섯는데. 하하하」
A의 말끗에, Y도 웃으며,
「美男子이엇던 醜男子이엇던, 何如間, 昨年 봄에, 한 서너 달, 監獄에 들어갓다가, 나온 뒤에, 異常하야젓다는데,... 仔細한 理由는 몰라...」
「妻子는 잇나?」
「예-계집은 親庭에 가서 잇다기도 하고, 놀아낫다기도 하나, 그 亦是 仔細한 것은 몰라요.」A가 對答을＜141＞ 하얏다.
「Y君, 그 계집이 어느 놈의 誘惑으로, 팔리어서 돌아단이다가, 그 遊廓에 굴러 들어와 잇다면 어떨가.」 나는 잠자코, 것다가 말을 걸엇다.
「흥!... 그리고 每日 차자가서 미친 체를 부리면...」 Y는 對句를 하얏다.
새 巨里를 빠져 黃葉이 되어가는 雜草에 싸인 벌판 中턱에 나와서, 南北으로 通한 길을, 北으로 꼽드러, 柳町을 바라볼 때는, 10餘間이나 떨어저 보이는 遊廓 二層에서는, 벌서 電燈 불빗이 반짝어리며 흘러나왓다.
「응! 저긔 보이는군...」A는 마조 보이는 나즉한 山麓에 외따로이 우뚝선, 참외원두막 가튼 것을 가르첫다. 힛금으레한 것이, 그 우에서 움즐움즐하는 것을, 바라보며, 우리는 발길을 催促하얏다.
10餘步쯤 가다가, 나는, 「이것이, 遊廓이야.」하며, 左便을 가르첫다. 方今 電氣가 들어온 軒燈이, 一字로 총총들이 박인, 사이로, 沐浴湯에서 돌아오는 얼굴만 하-얀 怪物들이, 化粧品을 담은 大也를 들고, 쓸쓸한 골작이를 이리 저리로 돌아단이는 것이, 浮華하다 함보다도 돌이어 凄凉히 보엿다.
「先生이 여긔 德도 꾀보지... 講演 한 番에 술 한 甁式 주는 곳은, 그래도 여긔밧게 업서... 」 A는 웃으면서 說明하얏다.
三層집 꼭댁이에 퍼더버리고 안저서, 熹微한 해발이 漸漸 멀어가는 山등성이를, 일업시 바라보고 잇던 主人은, 우리들이 우중우중 올라오는 것을 힐금 돌아보더니 瞥眼間에 돌아 안저서 무엇인지 똑딱똑딱 두드리고 잇다. 우리는 싸리로 듬은듬은 얼거매인 울타리 압헤서, 들어갈 곳을 찻느랴고, 이리저리 躊躇햐다가, 그대로 넘어서서 성큼성큼 들어갓다.
압서 들어간 A는 主人이 돌아안즌 三層 우에다 손을 걸어 잡고 드려다 보며,
「先生님! 또 왓습니다.」라고 人事를 하얏다.
「先生님! 安寧하심니까.」 A는 소리를 내어 웃으며, 잼처 人事를 하얏다. 그러나 彼는 如前히 籠欌門짝에, 못을 박고 안젓다. A와 Y는 同時에, H와 나를, 돌려다 보고 눈짓을 하며 소리업시 웃엇다.＜142＞
「... 神氣가, 그저 不便하신가요?... 오늘은 꼭 講演을 들으러 왓는데요...」 이番에는 Y가 酬酌을 건넷다. 그제야 彼는 깜짝 놀란 듯이, 먼지가 뿌여케 안즌, 더벙머리를 휙 돌이키며,
「예? 왓소?」 簡單히 對答을 하고, 如前히 돌아안저서 장도리를 들엇다. 세 사람은 一時에 깔깔깔 웃엇다. 그러나 귀밋부터 귀얄 가튼 鬚髥이 깜아케 덥흔 주먹만한 하얀 相을, 힐끗 볼 際, 나는 「앗!」하며 깜짝 놀랏다. 感電한 것 가티, 가슴이 선듯하며, 甚한 戰慄이 全身을 壓倒하얏다. 그리고 그 다음 瞬間에는 多少 安心된 가슴에 異常한 疑惑과 猛烈한 好奇心이, 一時에 물밀 듯 하엿다. 中學校 實驗室의 博物先生이 딸아온 줄로만 안 것이엇다. 그러나 아모 理由업시 無意識的으로, 敬虔한 或은 崇嚴한 感이, 머리 뒤를 떠미는 것 가타야서, 無心中間에 帽子를 벗고, 人事를 하얏다. 여러 사람들이, 흥흥흥 하며 웃는 것을 볼 때, 나는 未安하기도 하고, 무슨 큰 不敬한 일이나 하는 것 가타야서, 돌이어 괘씸한 듯이도 보이고, 或은 이 사람이 心事가 나서 곳 뛰어나려와 暴行이나 하지 안흘가 하는 念慮도 생기엇다.
「先生님! 正말 神氣가 不便하신 모양이외다 그려!」 A는 갑갑症이 나서, 또 말을 부텻다. 「徐鬱서 일부러 손님이 오셧는데, 한번 하시구려, 하...」
때무든 옷가지며, 빨래보통이 가튼 것이, 꾸역꾸역 나오는 것을, 꾹꾹 눌러 데밀면서, 고친 門짝을 열엇다, 다덧다 하고 안젓던 主人은, 서울 손님이란 말에, 귀가 뜨엿던지, 우리를 向하야, 돌아안즈며 입을 벌엿다.
「예-感氣도 좀 들엇쇠다.」하고, 映彩업는 뿌연 눈으로, 나를 有心히 똑바로 나려다 보다가,
「... 보시듯이 이러케 役事를 벌려노코,...」한번 房안을 휘익 돌아다 본 後, 또다시 나에게로 視線을 주며,
「요사이 가타서는 눈코 뜰짬도 업쇠다.... 더군다나 連日 講演에, 목이 꽉 쉐서...」말을 맷고, H를 돌려보앗다.
그러나 別로, 목이 쉰 것 갓지는 안핫다. Y가 H와 나를 紹介하니까,
「네-그러신가요? 徐鬱서... 멀리 오셧쇠다 그려.」 반가운 듯이, 「나는 南浦사는 金昌億이외다.」 彼의＜143＞ 얼굴에는 若干 微笑까지 나타낫다.
「네-나는 XXX올시다.」 나는 帽子를 벗으면서, 鄭重히 答禮를 하얏다. H도 人事를 마첫다.
「先生님! 그 용하시외다 그려... 이름도 아니 이즈시고... 하하하」 A가 놀렷다.
昌億은 거긔에는 對句도 아니하고, 나를 向하야,
「좀 올라오시소 그려. 아즉 役事가 끗이 안나서, 應接室도 업쇠다마는...」하며, 올라오라고 再三 勸하다가,
「제다가 次次 스토-프도 드려노코, 손님이 오시면, 좀 들어안저서, 술盞이나 난호도록 하여야 하겟지마는,...」 어긋매인 선반 가튼 所謂 2層間을 가르첫다.
세 사람은 깔깔 소리를 내어 웃엇다. 그러나 自己의 말에 족음도 不自然한 誇張이 업다고, 생각한 彼는, 웃는 것이 돌이어 異常하다는 듯이, 힘업는 視線으로 물그럼히 웃는 사람들을 내려다 보다가, 「힝」하고 코웃음을 치고 外面을 하얏다. 나는 이 사람이 밋첫다고 하여야 조흘지, 모든 것을 大悟하고 모든 것에서 解脫한 大哲人이라고 하여야 조흘지 몰랏다.
「넘어 惶悚하야 올라가진 못하겟습니다마는, 어떠케 講演이나 좀 하시구려.」 이번에는 H가 놀렷다.
「글세 모처럼 오셧는데, 술도 한 盞업서서 未安하외다.」 彼는 딴전을 부텻다. 처음 맛나는 사람을 보고 술 이악이만 꺼내는 것이 異常하엿다.
「여긔 온 손님들은 모다 하나님 아들이기 때문에, 술은 아니 먹는답니다.」 늘 웃으면서 對話를 듯고 섯던 Y가 입을 벌엿다.
「예? 兄公도 예수 밋습니까.」彼는 놀랜 듯이, 나를 마조 건너다 보다가, 히히히 웃으며
「예수軍도 無識한 놈만 모엿나 봅디다.... 예수軍들, 祈禱할 때에, 하나님 아바지시어! 나의 罪를 赦하소서, 아-맹,-하지 안소?... 그러나 아-맹이란 무엇이요. 孟子 가튼 萬古의 雄辯家다려 버버리라고 啞孟이라 하니 그런 無識한 말이, 아, 어대 잇단 말이요? 나를-나의 罪를 謝하야 달라고 할 지경이면,＜144＞ 我免이라고 해야 올치 안습니까.」 講演의 緖論을 끄어낸 彼는 得意滿面하야 히히 웃엇다. 둘러섯던 사람들도 웃엇다. 그러나 나는 彼가 非常한 空想家라는 것을 直覺한 以外에, 웃은지 어쪈지를 알 수가 업섯다.
여럿이, 딸아서 웃는 것을 보고, 彼는 더욱 신이 나서 講演을 繼續하얏다.-
「그러나 하나님은 참 至公無私하시외다. 나를,... 이 三層집을, 單 서른 닷兩으로, 꼭 한달 열사흘(1個月 13日)만에 짓게 하신 것이 다-하나님의 恩澤이외다.
西洋놈들이, 아모리 文明을 햇느니, 機械가 發達이 되엇느니 하지만, 그래 單 서른 닷양에 三層집을 진 놈이 어대 잇습니까... 날마다 하나님이 와보시고 稱讚을 하십니다.」
「稱讚을 하시니까, 至公無私한 것 갓지요.」 H가 한마디 새치기를 하엿다.
「千萬에 이것이 모다 하나님의 分付가 잇서서 된 것이외다... 인제는 불의 審判이 끗나고, 世界가 一大家庭을 이루을 時機가 되엇스니, 東西親睦會를 組織하라고 하신 故로, 爲先 이 事務所를 짓고, 내가 會長이 되엇스나, 各國의 紛爭을 巡察할 監督官이 업서서 큰 일이외다.」
일동은 와-ㅅ 웃엇다.
「여긔 X君이 어떨까요?」 Y는 나의 어깨를 탁 치며, 얼른 推薦을 하얏다.
「글세, 해주신다면, 고맙지만...」
세 사람은 「야-, 東西親睦會監督官 閣下!」하며, 더욱이 소리를 놉혀 웃엇다.

컴컴하고 쓸쓸한 北邙 밋 찬 바람에, 불리우며, 四肢를 오그리고 들어누운 三層 집 主人翁은, 저 長鼓소리를, 天堂의 왈쓰로 듯는지, 地獄의 阿鼻叫喚으로 깨닷는지, 나는 停車場 門에 들어설 때까지, 흘금흘금 돌아다 보아야, 오즉 幽谷의 妖火 가튼 遊廓의 電燈불이 暗黑 가운대에 반짝어릴 뿐이엇다. (未完)＜151＞
