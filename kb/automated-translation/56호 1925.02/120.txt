Magazine Title: 개벽 제56호 (開闢 第56號)
Publication Date: 1925년 02월 01일
Article Title: 安定을 일흔 朝鮮 基督敎宣敎會 (The Chosŏn Christian Mission Society That Lost Stability)
Author: None specified
Article Type: 소식 (News)

Whether it will prosper or perish is still difficult to predict, but regardless, the Chosŏn Christian church, which has begun to be shaken, seems to have progressed from minor tremors to strong earthquakes. Chosŏn Christians, who for decades blindly bowed down to Western missionaries, now seem to have discovered the path of independence and self-reliance, as movements to reject Western missionaries arise everywhere.

The self-governing faction and the elder faction of the Namsŏng-ch'on church in Taegu, who fought and fought again for two years with verbal battles, fistfights, and legal battles—saying this is right, that is wrong—finally split completely at the beginning of this year and declared independence. Their purpose was simply expressed as the rejection of Westerners. Around the same time, the Kyŏngju Christian church also declared independence. As one wave just stirs, ten thousand waves rise; the issue of independence for the Chosŏn Christian church is now very interesting. Let's look to the next issue to discuss this concretely. Since the topic of the Christian church came up, let me add this: the finance committee of the American Methodist Church has decided to reduce subsidies to Chosŏn by 40% starting this January.

Consequently, the salaries of Korean church workers will be reduced by 20% from January to June, and by 40% from July to December. Furthermore, regarding educational expenses, subsidies for elementary schools will be completely stopped, and for middle schools, Nyŏngbyŏn Sungdŏk School will be discontinued. Charitable expenses will be cut by 50%. Due to this, the approximately 1,600 church workers across all of Chosŏn and the 978,000 students across the entire country are left wide-eyed, gazing only toward the other shore (彼岸 - P'ian, i.e., America/the West). In any case, this is not a small problem. It is said that church workers are bustling about, east and west; let's also look to the next issue for this.—<54>

**Summary**

The article reports on growing instability within the Korean Christian church in 1925, marked by movements advocating for independence from Western missionary control. Schisms occurred in churches like Taegu Namsŏng-ch'on and Kyŏngju, where factions declared autonomy after prolonged conflict. This trend is amplified by a significant 40% reduction in subsidies from the American Methodist Church, leading to drastic cuts in salaries for Korean clergy and funding for church-run schools (like Nyŏngbyŏn Sungdŏk School) and charities, affecting nearly a million students and thousands of workers. From a colonial history perspective, this article is interesting as it highlights the intersection of nascent Korean nationalism and religious self-determination challenging Western dominance, even within the church. It also reveals the tangible impact of foreign mission policies and funding decisions on the livelihoods and institutions within colonial Korea, potentially fueling further discontent or forcing structural changes within the Korean church.

安定을 일흔 朝鮮 基督敎宣敎會
흥할넌지 패할넌지 그는 아직 期必키 어러우나 엇잿든 動撓되기 시작한 조선의 기독교회는 微震으로부티 强震에까지 니른 모양이다. 수십년 두고 서양인 선교수라면 덥허놋코 굽실거리든 조선의 기독교도들은 이제 비로소 독립자존의 도를 발견한 듯이 도처에서 서양선교수 배척운동이 닐어난다.
2개년이나 두고 言戰 拳戰 法戰-으로 曰是曰否 싸우고 또 싸우든 大邱 南城町 교회의 자치파 장로파는 금년 年始로써 아주 갈리어 독립을 선언하얏다. 그 취지는 간단히 서양인 배척으로 表하얏다. 전후하야 慶州 기독교회도 독립을 선언하얏다. 一波纔動 萬波起로 조선 기독교회 독립문제는 이제부터 매우 흥미가 잇다. 하회를 보아 한 번 구체적으로 논하자. 기독교회말이 낫스니 말이지. 米감리교회 재정위원회는 금년 1월로써 조선에 대한 보조금을 4할을 감하기로 하얏다한다.

그래서 조선인 교역자의 봉급은 1월로 6월까지는 2할감 7월로 12월까지는 4할을 감하게 되고 그리고 교육비는 소학교는 보조를 전부 중지하고 중학교는 寧邊 崇德學校를 중지하고 慈善費는 5할을 중지한다고 한다. 이리하야 全조선 교역자 1,600여명 及 全鮮 97만8,000여명 학생은 눈이 멀거케 彼岸만 바라보게 되얏다. 엇잿든 적지 안은 문제다. 교역자들이 동서분주한다니 亦 하회를 보자.-＜54＞
