Ruins of the Heart, Standing in Winter
						Kim Kijin
Where there is no one, where there is nothing, a certain dark shadow stumbles and runs away. I, too, chased after it without rest. It runs, stumbling without knowing where it's going. Hey! Thinking this is strange, I stopped walking. Standing still, I watched where that thing was going. That thing seems like a blind person. Like a drunk person. It stumbles. I shouted.
-What is that going there?
There is no answer from the shadow. I shouted a second time.
-What is that?
The shadow looks back. This is strange. It's a 벌어지 (beorŏji - insect/worm, possibly used metaphorically or as a typo for '벌거숭이' - naked person?). It has no face. I asked again.
-What are you?
-I am you. You.

From an old manuscript dated January 19, 1911, I recently found the diary entry above. Thinking about it, <116> it seems like the halted footprints of my stumbling soul from that time. When foolish, ignorant, ugly me was far away alone, unable to endure within that ‘taste of desolation,’ it was the footprints of my stumbling soul that I had recorded. Now, where is my tiny soul wandering off to again…
It became autumn, and now it is the time when it gradually begins to move its feet toward winter. Taking away the tree leaves, flowers, and grains it gave in summer, where is nature going now? Step by step, it slides into the pit of winter. At night, the wild geese cry. From this clothing store, the sound of fulling blocks cries. In the empty sky, the fallen leaves cry. Everything slides into the hollow of winter. The sound of weeping fills the universe.-
Ah. Where is my soul now? What is it drawn by, where is it going? What does it hope for, where does it wander?- Autumn has deepened. Silently, the sun sets.

What am I to do with these decaying ruins in my heart? An unceasing earthquake occurs in the heart. I don't know where the epicenter is. I don't know if it arises from the east or the west. Only, shaken without rest like toys gathered together, the beautifully built nooks and crannies just crumble. They crumble and catch fire. They catch fire and become ashes. Across the way, the beloved Tōkyō I see in my eyes turned to ashes two months ago. Tōkyō, which had long supported the soles of my feet, has become ruins. Everything there was shaken. Before our eyes, the people of Tōkyō appear more vigorous than before, more full of life than before. A great internal or external change occurred for the children of men who clung tightly to the earth's crust, refusing to fall. Various 'thoughts' began to well up in their minds. Various 'words' began to fall from their lips. All thoughts, all words - everything, all kinds of light culminated in the 'Song of Power'. Those people, who were floundering at the edge of boredom, discovered power within their own lives. The Song of Power surges from the East. In burnt Tōkyō, how could there <117> be no vitality? How could there be no 'Song of Power'?
Tōkyō burned. Ah, what a [Censored] thing! Because fire is the incarnation of power. Fire, the incarnation of power, had occupied all of Tōkyō. All the magnificent buildings, which were a stain on human history, were reduced to elements. And so, Tōkyō has now become ruins. But is there nothing within those ruins? Ah, how could there be nothing! Because they are ruins, there is a buzzing (부르지즘) within them. Within the ruins, complete power is being born. Because they are ruins, the resounding buzzing of power arises. The 'Song of Power' has conquered all of Tōkyō. The great music of creation rings in our ears.-

Tōkyō, rise up. Like a phoenix.
From within the pile of ashes left after burning, spread your wings.
Tōkyō, rise up. From within the Suffering (苦患),
Forget the pain in the soles of your feet. Stop your feet!
The heavens now withdraw the flames,
All things, every object is buried in the ash heap.
Ah, Tōkyō of darkness and silence, on the lumps of earth burnt and festering, what is stirring now?
Between corpses and corpses, between broken bricks and lumps of earth,
Listen to the sound of the pickaxe being heard.
Over the debris lying fallen on your ground,
Look at the firm strength of the people digging in.
...<118>
Let there be happiness for the working people. Life is upon it!
The Triumphal Hymn that will soon be heard, indeed,
Upon the new City, upon the shining pavement, ah,
Tōkyō, rise up. Like a phoenix!
-By Kawaji Ryūkō-
The Song of Power emerges like this. The buzzing of creation arising from within the ruins is truly great. Truly, Tōkyō will survive. It must survive.-

There are those who say, "Rather than decaying luxury—". That's right. Of course, today's Tōkyō is probably many times better than decaying luxury. The casual friends who were buried in that decaying luxury—creating trends by going to Mitsukoshi today and the Imperial Theater (帝劇) tomorrow, raising prices, manufacturing perfumes—must have disappeared. Although it's the remnants of ashes, how many times better is the current Tōkyō than that decaying luxury?
It is said that some person danced during the earthquake. Drank alcohol. Shouted "Magnificent!" (壯快). That person is a valiant general (驍將) of the Japanese proletarian literary world.
It is said that some person clenched their fist and shouted, "For Tōkyō, from now on, we must work!" It is said that their comrades, moved by that passion, were about to return to their hometowns but stopped their feet in Tōkyō again. That person is said to be a favorite child (寵兒) of the Japanese bourgeois literary world. There are also those who write "Saber Eulogy". There are also those who write poems, shouting things like "Draw the saber!". I will read a passage I wrote in my diary during the earthquake.
-I now imagine the completely burnt Imperial Capital (帝都!). People shouting and wandering around appear before my eyes. This girl passing before me, alone, with burn marks on her body, evokes a strangely familiar memory from somewhere. <119> Yes. She resembles the face of a woman I saw at some lecture meeting. The children of men crossing barefoot over the piles of corpses deeply pain my heart. Ah, wherever I look, there are only monstrous figures shuffling about, who can only be thought of as people in hell.-
If things become like this, whether it's the established literary circle or the newly emerging one, it doesn't matter at all. It becomes even clearer that literature is a reflection of the real life—instinctual life—of that era. Both the bourgeois literary circle and the proletarian literary circle, how much of their shadow truly remained when they entered the fire...? - We receive lessons only from this. Human intellectual life, the superstructure—philosophy—politics—literature—art—music—departing from instinctual life, how much evidence does it possess to claim it is not an 'illusion'? - Reality teaches us this.
-From the September 2nd diary entry-

We cannot believe the statement that literature is not a reflection of the economic structure of its era. In the flourishing Elizabethan era, there was Shakespeare; under the oppression of the 'Tsar', there was Dostoyevsky. Who is the person who points to the current Japanese literary scene and says it is not a reflection of today's Japanese economic structure?-
Tōkyō burned. In 10 or 15 years, we cannot know how splendid a Tōkyō will be constructed compared to the Tōkyō before the earthquake, but if they continue using today's 'policies' as they are, how worn out will the future Tōkyō become? Today's Superstructure (上部構造), the intellectual life, will tarnish the Tōkyō of 15 years later.
Thinking of Tōkyō, there is one more thing I cannot forget. I cannot forget the white-clad comrades who returned [Censored]. Although there are many rumors about the reason why the long-oppressed white-clad people were massacred, so I don't know exactly, it seems clear that our white-clad comrades were caught between two classes and used. How many comrades passed away from this world, floundering while caught in the middle? ([Deleted Text]) Whose is two-thirds of the <120> peninsula of 14,300 square ri (方里)? Do the 17 million Lives (生靈) play in the hands of 380,000 people, or do they not?-
Chosŏn appears before my eyes. My heart is so full that words cannot come out.-

October fifth, the haetae cries.
The haetae cries, the haetae cries! <121>

An earthquake occurred in Japan. Goods in Seoul's Chin'gogae did not sell well.- The Subsidiary Products Fair (副業品共進會) ended up driving out the haetae. Ah, where has the haetae gone? The haetae is crying. Secretly, in some corner unseen by the sun, in the dim shade, it must be crying its heart out. As autumn deepens, the cry of the haetae is heard from all directions. It is heard from Namsan. It is heard from Pugaksan too. All of Chosŏn is filled with the cry of the haetae!
Ah, why does the haetae cry? It cries out of sadness. It cries out of injustice. It cries out of anger. - Even if the two haetae that guarded against fire facing Kwanaksan for nearly 50 years in front of Kwanghwamun are hidden where we cannot see them, the cries of countless large and small haetae invisible to our eyes grew louder daily following the dates of the Fair. Formless, voiceless old and young haetae cried along with me throughout Chosŏn. They cried until their throats burst. Their throats burst, and they collapsed from exhaustion. Ah, the haetae is crying. It cries unceasingly.
Even if they hold a Fair, what's wrong with the haetae being there? Was it chased out because the haetae guarded the Kyŏngbokkung of Old Chosŏn, and now that the owner is moving into the Kyŏngbokkung site, it's considered inappropriate (不緊) as a servant who guarded the previous owner? Even so, if it's the servant of the previous owner. For the master whose威名 (wimyeong - authority/prestige) now overwhelms the East like Xiang Yu, moving in now, it wouldn't have been difficult to make that haetae loyal by any means. By whatever method, even if just by giving one of those common Wooden Cups (木盃) from the Government-General, they could have made it loyal. It was possible. It was easy. Why did they discard such a thing? Our close friend of nearly 300,000(?), our haetae, where are you now!-

[Censored], [Censored] It has been ten days since the Chosŏn Subsidiary Products Fair, an external expression of impulse, ended. Although it was announced before the earthquake in Japan's Kantō region, the fact that this Fair was held allows for various interpretations, but the destination of all those interpretations is the spirit of [Censored]. Ultimately, look into whose crow's pouch the 300,000 won squeezed from the barley husk moneybag went.- It only fanned the flames of the merchants' feelings in Kyŏngsŏng's Honmachi, who were hit <122> by the earthquake disaster in Japan. How could the haetae remain silent without crying?
The haetae cried. So the haetae cried loudly.

Fortunately or unfortunately (!), this fellow too—one of the white-clad haetae—unable to resist my old mother's inescapable pleading, went inside Kwanghwamun, perhaps the Fairground, on the afternoon of the 24th as it was ending. It was the first time in my life I went inside Kwanghwamun. Like a creature that had lost the lump of bean paste in its head, the heart inside its Diaphragm (胸隔膜), I wandered listlessly and came out through Kŏnch'unmun, ah, it was then. I saw a Japanese-made haetae, flimsy like Japanese wooden clogs.- Ah, plump haetae, whose whereabouts are unknown! How could you not have cried!...
You, who for 50 years, fooled by the foolish talk of the monk who pointlessly built the Kuksadang (國師堂) on Mongmyŏksan (Namsan) making it look up, stared only at Kwanaksan day and night, now caught in the claws of a strange fate, abandoning your beloved home, where are you? Have you at least not lost your life? Haetae, are you well? Is there no danger to your spirit?-
Ah, the haetae cries. To live again, to be reborn, that's why it cries. It cries loudly.-

The haetae must live again. Our haetae must live again. But is the haetae in a state to live again? Or is it not?- The haetae's neck is pressed down by a black hand. Pressed so hard it cannot breathe. The land the haetae lives on, it is not the haetae's. The haetae, driven to desperation by losing its home and becoming a servant, is now unable to move, its neck tightly pressed. This state of the haetae began after the iron bridge was built over the Han River. After Pusan and Ŭiju faced each other connected by railway, both the superstructure and substructure of the haetae's society were overturned.
The superstructure, it moved over to Waesŏngdae (Japanese residential area), and the substructure fades day by day, replaced by the formation of the Japanese friend's substructure. The haetae's life cannot be sustained without the guarantee of the black-clad one. White has <123> been completely conquered by Black. The documents for the land producing 15 million sŏk (섬 - unit of grain) of rice, in whose hands are they now? Even the so-called large landowners, few probably haven't Mortgaged (典執) their land documents. The situation of the poor peasants (빈농) goes without saying, but the property of the Large Farmers (大農) too, due to debts to the Agricultural and Industrial Bank (農工銀行) or the Oriental Development Company (東拓會社), will soon be transferred in place of currency. Moreover, that time is not far off. It is a day visible before our eyes. For the past 20 years, our land has been exchanged for currency, and the money received in exchange for land has been squandered buying their industrial goods. The people of the agricultural nation of 17 million, due to their cunning machine industrial products, have had their houses, fields, rice paddies, everything, shaken clean and are forced to leave Chosŏn. Their capitalism—their surplus production of machine industrial goods—has thrown our homeland, a simple, peaceful agricultural nation that practiced Self-Sufficiency (自作自給), into turmoil. Because of machine industrial goods, our 15 million sŏk of rice has come to be produced as a commodity. Rice, which used to be produced for self-sufficiency, is now produced to become a commodity, and the result is that it has become the object of today's speculative business. Consequently, useless Hoarding (貯積) has arisen among the Large Farmers. Accumulation for speculation. What will become of the poor peasants' livelihoods?
The poor peasants—tenant farmers (소작인)—are suffering double exploitation. The plight of the 15 million (?) poor peasants, exploited doubly by the ruling class, those people, and the propertied class, the Large Farmers, is beyond words! - If there is anyone who says that not everything originates from those people's capitalism, let them speak! If you still have something like a lump of bean paste left in your head, examine the piece of land you are standing on. How could there not be the 'haetae's cry'!......
Ceaselessly, threats descend upon the lives of the ruled class, the propertyless class (무산계급) haetae. Pressure on our livelihood never ceases. Threats ceaselessly descend upon the livelihood of the 188,000-plus haetae living in Seoul where I currently reside. Lately, it is said that nearly 30 merchants in Chongno are on the verge of bankruptcy. Money has dried up completely, like fields drying up in a 7-year Great Drought (大旱). Today, when it's impossible to tell if currency is life or life is currency, money has dried up in our livelihood. Money has dried up. The haetae, driven by Starvation (餓鬼), stands on the street shedding tears! <124>

One of my friends went bankrupt and went to the countryside. Unable to live in Seoul, he went to the countryside. But I don't know how long a person's life can be sustained in the countryside. Since the countryside is also Chosŏn, what difference could there be? That person with two enemies, I cannot guarantee their life.—That person ran a business in Seoul. Ran a stationery and general goods store. A person who used to earn 200 won a month. But unable to survive, he went to the countryside. The countryside will certainly not be a land that guarantees that person's happiness. The faint silhouette of that person's back swaying in the desolate, windy countryside appears before my eyes. Ah, when will the spring breeze ever blow upon the pitiful haetae's livelihood? The haetae sobs. Weeps softly...
I met someone who went to Ch'ŏngjin to sell salted shrimp. He said he took someone else's money, received salted shrimp, took it to Ch'ŏngjin, and made a profit. He bought them for 5 won and some tens of chŏn each and sold them for about 10 won each. But after deducting expenses for fifteen days, transportation costs, and interest on the capital, the remaining money was less than a few chŏn. My friend, who said he became a salted shrimp seller just to taste some money, is also someone chased by livelihood struggles. One of the pitiful haetae.
Among my friends, there is one who works as an employee for a Firewood and Charcoal Merchant (薪炭商). He has a family of four. He says it costs at least 50 won a month. This person, who goes out at six in the morning and returns at seven in the evening with 80 chŏn, is also one of the pitiful haetae. One of the chased-out haetae.
-It is said that factories don't pay more than 80 chŏn per day. Printing workers, after 2-3 months of unpaid apprenticeship, earn only 18 won a month. An assistant locomotive engineer earns no more than 38 won at the lowest grade. With what can we guide our daily chased livelihood?- Our life is the life of a mayfly. How long will the shadow of a candle flickering in the wind truly continue...

My family and I have endured through terribly painful compromises. I continue to get free meals even now. <125>
"Our dear thing—." This is the phrase that has been hammered into me in the past. Now, I have failed to become their "—dear thing". I am no more than a dependent (居侯) in this house. Occasionally, I light the fire. I split firewood. I run errands. This is merely the flimsy reason that fails to establish the fact that I am playing and eating. I was foolish. I was weak. But now, we are in the era of Turgenev's "Fathers and Sons"!—

My friend, the salted shrimp seller, my friend who went down to the countryside, the pitiful comrade pulling a cart,— I cannot escape being among them. Not only does the Threat of life (生活の威嚇) come upon those people alone, but threats of life also relentlessly rush towards me. Whenever they rush in, goosebumps rise all over my body.— Ah, I too am a pitiful person...
Our lives, who is the person to guide them? More than the Individual 'I', who is the person to guide the lives of the masses, 'us'?
If there is a duty for the artist, it is this. The livelihood of the teeming people on this bean-pod-sized peninsula, a corner piece of the world, is a problem to be resurrected today. It is definitely not a problem for tomorrow. Our homeland—I dislike using the word 'nation' here—is not ours, and our livelihood is not in our organization—hands. Our livelihood is in the grasp of those people, the guests. Just as Namsan is not ours, and the Han River is not ours, our livelihood is also not ours, and just as the front field and back field belong to those people, the threshing in our yard will not be ours but will go into the grain stack of Yi P'ansŏ or Kang Ch'amp'an's house over there. "Double oppression" creates "Two enemies". Ah, why is the Moaning (吟呻) sound of the propertyless class so loud!
This past spring, the labor of summer cannot feed us through this winter, and this year's 15 million sŏk of rice will not belong to us, the white-clad people! Beloved comrades, while you sit idly by, gaping and just watching, our necks will part from our Torsos (胴體)! Our necks will fall off like reed leaves falling. Even so, even so, ah, even so, don't shout, "I am an artist!" and run off to Germany holding a violin!... <126>
Ah, what is happening? Before our eyes, we see frost-like blades. On the nape of our necks, there is a huge hand pressing down. Trampling faces, caught by established concepts, if you cannot produce even a lukewarm resolution, what will become of you? Ah, what will become of you?!

Nakanishi Inosuke's "From Behind Your Backs" (汝等の背後より) suddenly comes to mind. I wrote it down as I thought of it.
Love—. The fellow who says 'Love is best' must be an idle person, but to say that love is a kind of play of emotions or a product of petit-bourgeois sentiment is too much of a prejudice against life. Although such words might be used frequently depending on the situation, it surely cannot all be like that. Among the pitiful majority of humanity living in the ruins of the heart, how many people are thirsty for love? Love—. Love established after circumstances are the same, ideals are the same, such love could be said to be close to 'perfection'. Therefore, in the end, 'love' too is merely a means of living. The person who says love is best must be a madman.
There is utopian love. There is ideal love with much potential for realization. The distance between the ideal and the utopian there is far.
Revolutionaries have religious vision. Barbusse's Simon Paulin has an ideal love with Marie. Nakanishi Inosuke's Kwŏn Chu Yŏng has Ch'unyong's love. Turgenev's <132> Solomin has the establishment of an ideal Love with Marianna. When two souls intoxicated by ideals charge towards a single goal, the two souls join Hands (掘手 - likely means 'join hands' or 'work together'). There lies the symphony of souls. It is more than decoration.
Two different souls whose circumstances (positions) differ, environments differ, ideals differ, and views on life differ cannot shake hands. If love were to form there, it would be the product of idleness. It amounts to no more than the play of romance. If perfect love does not exist in one's life, that person's life becomes crippled.
The words of Benjamin De Casseres, 'Without Idealism, there is no Drama, no Literature, no History,' come to mind. There is no human life apart from ideals.
However, in the desolate Chosŏn across the way, there is no love. There is only play resembling love. The minds of men and women are different. The consciousness of life differs by 4 or 5 levels. They are merely playing out of inability to overcome boredom. Unconsciously constructing play. Romance, the by-product of boredom, a decorative item, clings entangled in our lives. It is a cheap flower.
That's right. True love has become unable to bloom in our lives. As winter approaches, and the tree branches stand starkly naked, when the fallen leaves lament in the empty sky, the desolate ruins in our hearts are revealed. The chased-out haetae stands in winter and cries its heart out. The fallen leaves cry along. The cold evening wind hangs itself on the tree branches and wails. Entering winter, the sound of weeping fills all of Chosŏn!

**Summary**

Kim Kijin's essay expresses profound despair and anger regarding the state of colonized Korea (Chosŏn) in 1923, metaphorically described as the "ruins of the heart." He contrasts the recent destruction of Tōkyō by the Kantō Earthquake, seeing potential for renewal ('Song of Power') in its ruins, with the seemingly stagnant decay of Chosŏn under colonial oppression. The 'haetae', a traditional Korean symbol, represents the displaced and suffering Korean people, particularly lamenting its removal for a Japanese-sponsored exhibition. Kim critiques the devastating economic exploitation, the loss of land and livelihood, and the resulting 'double oppression' of peasants by both Japanese colonizers and Korean landowners. He wrestles with the role of intellectuals and artists, questioning the relevance of literature ("smoke") and love ("play") in the face of harsh reality, referencing various literary figures and works. Ultimately, he calls for abandoning despair and compromise, urging Koreans (symbolized as 'haetae') to adopt "steel emotions," become "nameless heroes," engage with the masses ("go to the people"), and strive for spiritual liberation and national rebirth from the "ruins."

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for its raw portrayal of Korean intellectual anguish and nascent nationalist/socialist sentiment in the early 1920s. It vividly captures the economic distress caused by colonial policies (land alienation, shift to commodity production, impact of Japanese capitalism) and the cultural displacement symbolized by the haetae's removal. The references to the Kanto Earthquake and the subsequent massacre of Koreans (alluded to cryptically) provide context for the heightened sense of crisis and injustice. The author's internal conflict about the role of art versus direct action, and his call for a connection with the masses, reflects broader debates among Korean intellectuals during this period, grappling with how to respond to colonial rule and envision a future for Chosŏn. The blending of personal despair with national grievance, and the invocation of both Western and Japanese literary/philosophical ideas alongside Korean symbolism, makes it a rich text for understanding the complex intellectual landscape of colonial Korea.

中西伊之助의 『汝等の背後より』가 문득 생각난다. 생각나는대로 적어보앗다.
사량-. ‘Love is best'라고 하는 놈도 한가한 녀석이겟지만은, 그럿타고 연애라는 것을 일종 감정의 유희라든가 혹은 프티·뿌르즈와의 감정의 소산물이라는 말은 넘어도 인생에 대한 편견일 것이다. 경우에 의해서 이와 가튼 말이 사용될 때는 만켓지만, 그럿타고 전부가 다 그럴리는 업겟다. 마음의 폐허에 사는, 불상한 다대수의 인류에는, 사랑의 기갈이 든 사람이 얼마나 만흐냐. 사랑-. 그것도 역시 처지가 갓고, 이상이 갓고, 그러한 뒤에 성립되는 사랑은 「완전」에 갓가웁다고 일을 것이다. 그럼으로 결국은 「사랑」이라는 것도 생활수단에 지나지 못한다. 러브·이스·뻬스트·라는 사람이야말로 밋친 사람일 것이다.
사랑에도 공상적 사랑이 잇다. 실현성이 만흔 이상적 사랑이 잇다. 거기의 이상적과 공상적과의 사이는 거리가 멀다.
혁명가에게는 신앙적 삐지온이 잇다. 빠르쀼-스의 시몬·포-란에게는 마리-와의 이상적 사랑이 잇다. 中西伊之助의 權朱英이게는 春容이의 사랑이 잇다. 투르게네-프의 ＜132＞ 소로민에게는 마리안나와의 이상적 愛의 성립이 잇다. 이상에 도취한 두 개의 영혼이, 한 개의 목표를 향하야 돌진할 때에, 두 개의 영혼은 掘手를 한다. 거기에는 영혼의 교향악이 잇다. 그것은 떼코레-숀 이상이다.
처지(입장)가 달고, 환경이 달고, 이상이 달고, 인생관이 달는 두 개의 틀닌 영혼은 악수하지 못한다. 그곳에 사랑이라는 것이 성립된다면 그것은 한가한 것의 소산물일 것이다. 연애의 유희에 지나지 못한다. 완전한 사랑이 살임 속에 업다 하면 그 사람의 생활은 불구자가 되고 마는 것이다.
