The Evil State of Affairs

In general, what kind of world is this becoming? Among us at present, such phenomena are not absent.
The young point to the old, calling them a decrepit and narrow-minded lot (老朽, 固陋의 輩), and dislike associating with them. The old disparage the young, calling them a frivolous and unprincipled crowd (浮薄, 無範의 徙), and refuse to discuss matters with them. Women point to men, calling them those of generous words but mean actions (厚言, 薄行의 者), and resent their untrustworthiness. Men disparage women, calling them showy and reckless individuals (浮華, 無謀의 者), and reproach them as not worth dealing with. The propertyless intellectuals point to the propertied ignorant, calling them "usurpers of wealth" (富의 橫領者), and curse their future. The propertied ignorant disparage the propertyless intellectuals, calling them a crude and vulgar lot (粗暴, 野卑의 輩), and sneer at their actions. Those who have not yet secured a social footing point to those who established their footing early, saying they are nothing more than a kind of puppet (傀類) produced by a transitional era, and try to deny everything they say and do. Those who established their footing early refer to those who have not, calling them addicts of the era's ideology (時代思潮의 中毒者) or possessors of jealousy (猜忌心의 所有者), and try to forbid their interference. Not only this, but similar phenomena exist between employers and employees, between students and society, and even severely within single organizations and single families. The result is mutual suspicion and mutual hatred, showing a momentum that seems unlikely to stop unless they peel off their own skin. If you think our words are mere abstract observation, try paying close attention to page three of the various newspapers. We call this the "Evil State of Affairs."

Postscript: To say this is not to encompass everyone and claim it is universally so, but rather that such a situation is visible, and that situation emerges centered on the emotion of hatred existing mutually between parties.

Restrained destruction and organized construction! This must be the earnest desire of the vast majority of us. On the stage where this desire is realized, of course, some disputes and conflicts cannot be guaranteed not to occur, but those disputes and conflicts must inevitably arise from the best goodwill, based on the single matter of "Korea's Revival!" (朝鮮의 中興!). If one steps beyond this and allows even the slightest private motive <2> therein, that is, if under the fine names of destruction for construction, or defense of preservation, there is a tendency to reenact historical factionalism (歷史的 黨爭心) or animalistic selfishness (動物性 利己心), or perhaps to vent usual grudges or temporary hostility (一時的 敵愾心), this truly is an evil phenomenon deciding our great work's future. Even the thought makes one shudder.
There is a proverb, "Taking out anger over the mother-in-law on the dog's belly" (시어미역정에게 腹臟), which likely means that a weak daughter-in-law, unable to confront her strong mother-in-law's injustice, vents her anger by wildly kicking the belly of a weak dog lying outside the kitchen. Are we (of course, a part of us) recently learning the behavior of that weak wife? In other words, is it perhaps that there is no other way to vent the hatred and hostility accumulated in our hearts year after year, so we turn its sharp point towards our own society (which has no army, no police, nor stern public opinion)?

To put it in one word, in our society at present, there is much discord (扞格) between old and young, men and women, rich and poor, new and old. This discord is nothing more than the disputes and differences arising amid escaping from everything conventional and trying to build something new, which indeed is fittingly described as unavoidable given the circumstances. However, regarding this, what we want to urge upon the general attention with a single word is that even if disputes and conflicts are unavoidable by circumstance, their motives should be utterly pure and their course utterly restrained. In other words, even with the same disputes and conflicts, do not act out of hostile or hateful feelings, but solely out of concern and sincerity (憂慮와 赤心) that for the salvation of our people (民族의 救濟), things must be done this way or that way, and yet, in the means employed, there should be particular restraint and organization.

When flowers are about to bloom, wind and rain are especially plentiful; when the sun is strong, the shadow is correspondingly deep. Where is success that is not accompanied by hardship (危難)? Where is light (光明) that does not consort with darkness (暗黑)? If we proceed with knowledge, and reflect as we proceed, there will likely be almost no great error (大過).<3>

**Summary**

The editorial laments the deep divisions fragmenting Korean society in 1922 – generational, gender, class, ideological – characterizing it as an "Evil State of Affairs" fueled by mutual hatred and suspicion. While acknowledging conflict as potentially unavoidable during a period of transition aimed at national renewal ("朝鮮의 中興"), the author strongly advocates for unity of purpose, purity of motive (focused on national salvation), and crucially, "restrained destruction and organized construction." It warns against allowing personal grudges, factionalism, or misdirected anger (likened to a frustrated daughter-in-law kicking a dog) to derail the greater cause. From a historical perspective, this article is revealing of the internal tensions and anxieties within the Korean nationalist movement during the colonial period. It highlights the struggle to maintain cohesion and discipline amidst diverse social groups and competing ideas on how to achieve national liberation, suggesting that internal conflict was a significant concern alongside the fight against external oppression. Historians might find the explicit articulation of these specific social cleavages and the call for disciplined action particularly interesting.

惡現狀
大體 어떠케 되려는 세음인가, 目下의 우리에게는 이러한 現像이 업지 안하잇도다.
젊은이는 늙은이를 指目하되 老朽, 固陋의 輩라하야 더부러 對하기를 실혀하고, 늙은이는 젊은이를 貶論하되, 浮薄, 無範의 徙라하야 가티 論議하기를 不肯하며. 女子는 男子를 指目하되 厚言, 薄行의 者라하야 그의 不足信賴를 叱怨하고, 男子는 女子를 貶論하되 浮華, 無謀의 者라하야 그의 無足與爲을 責하며. 無産有識의 人은 有産無識의 人을 指目하되 「富의 橫領者」라 하야 그의 前途를 咀呪하고, 有産無識의 人은 無産有識의 人을 貶論하되 粗暴, 野卑의 輩라하야 그의 行動을 冷笑하며. 아즉 社會的 地盤을 엇지 못한 사람은 일즉이 地盤을 싸하 어든 사람을 指目하되 過渡의 時代가 나하 노흔 一種의 傀類에 지내지 못한다 하야 그의 云爲하는 全部를 否認하고저 하며, 일즉이 地盤을 싸하 어든 사람은 그러치 못한 사람을 指稱하되 時代思潮의 中毒者, 猜忌心의 所有者라 하야 그의 容喙를 不許하려 한다. 뿐 아니라 이와 類似한 現像은 雇主와 雇人, 學生과 社會의 間에도 잇고, 甚하야는 한 團軆의 間과 한 家庭의 內에도 잇스며. 그 結果는 서로 猜疑하며 서로 憎惡하야 自皮를 自剝치 아니하면 말지 아니 할 氣勢를 보이며 잇나니. 우리의 말로써 한갓 抽像의 觀察이라 생각하거든 試하야 各 新聞紙의 第三面을 注視하라. 우리는 이를 稱하야써 「惡現像」이라 한다.
後解 이와 가티 말함은 一般을 網羅하야 皆然타 함이 아니오 그러한 形便이 보인다 함이며 그 形便은 互相間에 在한 憎惡의 感情을 中心삼아 出現된다 함이다.

節制잇는 破壞와 組織잇는 建設! 이것은 우리들 多大數의 企願이리라, 이 企願을 實現하는 마당에서는 勿論 多少의 是非 衝突이 업기를 不保할지나 그 是非와 衝突은 어대까지 「朝鮮의 中興!」이라는 一事를 本位로 하는 最善의 好誼에서 나옴이 되지 아니치 못할 지니, 만일 여긔에 一步를 脫出하야 一毫의 私議를＜2＞ 其間에 容함이 잇다하면 卽 破壞의 建設, 或은 保持의 擁護의 美名下에서 從來의 歷史的 黨爭心, 或은 動物性 利己心을 復演코저 하거나 또 或은 平素의 憾情이나 一時的 敵愾心을 洩盡코저 하는 傾向이 잇다하면 이야말르 우리의 큰일을 決斷 來日 惡現像이라. 생각만 하여도 치가 떨리도다.
俗談에 「시어미역정에게 腹臟」이라는 말이 잇나니 大盖弱한 며느리가 强한 시어머니의 不義를 對抗치 못하야 그 忿푸리로써 廚房밧게 누어 잇는 弱犬의 腹臟을 亂蹴한다는 말이라. 近來의 우리(勿論 一部)가 저 弱婦의 行爲를 배움은 아닌지?. 다시 말하면 年復年來로 우리의 胸中에 集積된 憎惡, 敵愾의 心情을 달리 洩盡할 道理가 업서 그 精鋒을 自己社會(軍隊도 警察도 또 嚴正한 輿論도 업는)에 돌려 대이는 세음은 아닌지?.

一言으로 蔽하면 目下의 우리社會에는 老少, 男女, 貧富, 新舊의 間에 幾多의 扞格이 잇스며 그 扞格은 從來의 一切로 부터 脫出하야 새로운 무엇을 지으려 하는 中에서 일어나는 是非와 相違에 지내지 못하는 것인 바 實로 勢에 免치 못할 者이라 함이 맛당하나, 이에 對하야 다 못 우리가 一言으로써 一般의 注意를 促코저 하는 것은 비록 勢에 免치 못할 是非와 衝突이라 할지라도 그 動機에서는 至極히 純潔하고 그 行方에서는 至極히 節制가 잇스라 할 뿐이다. 다시 말하면 가튼 是非와 가튼 衝突이라 할지라도 敵對 或은 憎惡의 感念으로써 말고, 오즉 우리民族의 救濟를 爲하야는 이리이리 하지 아니 할 수 업다하는 憂慮와 赤心으로써 하되 그 手段에 잇서는 別로 節制와 組織이 잇스라 할 뿐이다.

꼿이 피려하면 風雨가 別로 만흐며 볏이 도타우면 그림자도 그만큼 두터운 法이라. 危難을 짝하지 아니 한 成功이 어대 잇스며 暗黑을 벗하지 아니 한 光明이 어대 잇스리요. 알고 나아가며, 나아가며 反省하면 거의 大過가 업슬진저.＜3＞
