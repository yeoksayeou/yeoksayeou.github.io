Total Population of Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture

Counting the Kyŏngsŏng population at the end of Taishō 10 (1921), Koreans numbered 188,640 in 39,121 households, and Japanese numbered 69,273 in 17,120 households; the total population including foreigners was 261,698 people. Comparing this to the 9th year (1920), households increased by 2,419 and the population increased by 11,490. <30>

**Summary**

This news item reports the population statistics for Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (colonial Seoul) at the end of 1921 (Taishō 10). It breaks down the population by Koreans (188,640) and Japanese (69,273), noting the number of households for each group, and gives the total population as 261,698. It also notes an increase of 2,419 households and 11,490 people compared to the previous year (1920). For historians, this provides specific demographic data illustrating the colonial composition of Seoul, showing a significant Japanese population alongside the Korean majority and indicating urban growth during this period. The figures quantify the Japanese presence and overall population change early in the colonial era.
