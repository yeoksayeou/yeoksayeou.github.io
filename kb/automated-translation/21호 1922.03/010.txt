Cultural Movements of Past and Present

If considered solely through the lens of the single fact called human development, the history of us humans is nothing but a record of a kind of cultural movement. At the same time, if the history of the Korean (朝鮮) people is 4,255 years long, then there was likely never a time during these 4,255 years when cultural movements did not erupt or continue. However, the cultural movement this article intends to discuss does not refer to cultural movements in such a broad sense. Rather, it refers to the recent cultural movement in Korea, which arose with overwhelming force (沛然之勢), being related externally to the trends of the world situation and internally unable to restrain the impulses of humanity's inherent life force.

Thinking quietly, for us recently—standing at the crossroads of change—cultural movements have indeed been frequent. The Kapsin (甲申) Political Incident, the Ŭlmi (乙未) Reforms, the Restoration Movement of the Yunghŭi (隆熙) era, and the Reconstruction Movement of recent years—while their forms and methods are certainly not uniform, they cannot but be considered fundamentally the same (基揆一) in the sense that they were a type of cultural movement arising with the goal of the sound development of those people gathered under the name Korea (朝鮮) as one people or as human beings.

However, despite their very laudable objectives, the Kapsin (甲申) and Ŭlmi (乙未) movements lacked popular support and thus their effects were unsatisfactory. The Restoration Movement of the Yunghŭi (隆熙) era had momentum that impressively reached even the streets and alleys, offering the prospect of achieving real results, but it ended up being frustrated by the subsequent rapid political changes. Only the Reconstruction Movement that arose most recently, spanning several years, sees its steps grow sounder day by day (步武-날로 健實하야), and entering this spring of the Imsul (壬戍) year, it is unfolding yet another new phase.

We previously wrote in this column of last year's September issue, under the title "Unfolding of a New Phase":

> We have, for the first time in recent years, gained an immense self-awakening (尨大한 自覺) regarding life. However, that self-awakening, as vast as its scope was, was also extremely abstract in its content. Later, with the goal of constructing a new culture, there was a sense of it becoming somewhat more concrete, but since that goal of 'new culture' was also similarly abstract, many of our brethren still felt difficulty in grasping its meaning. Amidst this, coming to the spring of Sinyu (辛酉), the general populace uniformly keenly recognized the urgent task of education, and by that summer, <2> they were again crying out for the necessity of industry. Thus, the immense self-awakening of recent years has finally, one by one, found its paths forward. At the same time, our activities appearing henceforth in various fields will become more organized and practical, and there will be a particularly straightforward or poignant flavor to them.

This memory might still remain in the minds of readers who do not read our words merely as empty phrases (套語). Now, merely half a year after writing that piece, we are greatly pleased that the conjectures (推察) of that time are already appearing as facts, and we declare this unfolding, alongside these facts, the newest phase and one of the greatest current phenomena.

What, then, do we mean by this newly unfolding phase? In short, as we have stated, it is the fact that the thoughts, words, and actions of the general populace are becoming much more organized and practical than some time ago. Expounded, it means: (1) our fervor for education and industry is becoming more universal and profound with each passing moment; and thus (2) all twenty million individuals are gaining the self-awareness (in the most thorough sense) to be reborn through the reconstruction (改造) of their own minds and hearts and resurrected through the transformation of their own environments; and (3) that self-awareness is currently creating new phenomena in our society through diverse forms.

The fine season of bright spring returns again. In every corner of the old garden, rich rain and dew fall as before, and on every branch of the hibiscus (槿花), the joyous songs of birds sound anew. Compatriots, do you see? Even on that withered tree (枯木) in your courtyard, new buds (새엄) are sprouting. Brothers, do you ponder? Within your own heart (方寸), new life is leaping. The spirit of Paektu (白頭) and the souls of our ancestors teach us, saying, "Carefully nurture (兒養) that life."

The time is now—today. Titled "Cultural Movements of Past and Present," emotions (感慨) surge even before taking up the brush. I reverently write these few short words in place of everything else. <3>

**Summary**

This 1922 editorial from *Kaebyŏk* magazine reflects on the history of "cultural movements" in Korea, distinguishing earlier, less successful political attempts like the Kapsin Coup and Ŭlmi Reforms from the contemporary movement. The author argues that previous movements failed due to lack of popular support or political setbacks, but the current "Reconstruction Movement" is gaining strength. This new phase, emerging strongly in 1921-1922 (Sinyu-Imsul years), is characterized by a shift from abstract ideals to concrete, practical action, specifically focusing on the urgent needs for widespread education and industrial development. The movement emphasizes individual self-reform ("reconstruction of mind and heart") and environmental change, manifesting in organized, practical activities across society, symbolizing a national awakening and revitalization likened to spring's renewal.

For historians, this article is significant as it captures the evolving nature of Korean nationalism under Japanese rule. It highlights a crucial shift in strategy from primarily political agitation to a focus on self-strengthening through education and economic development – themes common in cultural nationalist movements facing colonial constraints. The emphasis on "organization," "practice," and mass participation ("twenty million individuals," "general populace") reflects a conscious effort to build a broader, more resilient foundation for national progress, moving beyond elite-led initiatives. The optimistic, almost spiritual tone invoking ancestral spirits and natural renewal (Paektu, spring) underscores the deep emotional investment in this cultural project as a path to national rebirth.

文化 運動의 昔今
人文發達 이라는 한 가지의 事實뿐으로써 考察하면 우리 人間의 歷史는 一種 文化運動의 記錄에 밧하지 아니한 것인 同時에 朝鮮 民族의 歷史가 4255年이라 하면 이 4255年 間의 日日時時 어느 때가 文化運動의 勃發 또는 繼續을 일삼지 아니한 적이 업섯슬 것이다. 그러나 本題에서 말하고저 하는 文化運動이라 함은 이와 가튼 汎博한 意味에서 文化運動을 指稱함이 아니오 적어도 밧그로는 世界 大勢의 趨向과 서로 關係함이 잇고 안으로는 人間 固有의 生命力의 衝動을 스스로 牽制치 못함이 잇서 沛然의 勢로써 일어난 近來 朝鮮의 文化運動을 指稱함이라.
가만히 생각하면 近來의 우리- 轉變의 街頭에 立한― 에게는 文化運動이 實로 頻煩하얏다. 甲申의 政變, 乙未의 更張이며 隆熙年間의 維新運動, 數年來의 改造運動 等은 그 形式과 方法에 잇서 서로 一樣이 아닌 것은 勿論이나 朝鮮이라는 稱號下에 모인 그 사람들이 한 民族 或은 한 人間으로의 健全한 發展을 目的하야써 일어난 一種의 文化運動되는 點에서는 先者 後者가 基揆-一이라 할 수 밧게 업도다.
그러나 甲申, 乙未의 運動은 그 目的이 매우 훌륭하엿슴에도 關係업시 民衆의 後援이 그에 딸으지 못하야 그 效果가 시원치 못하엿스며 隆熙年間의 維新運動은 그 氣運이 蔚然히 巷間에까지 미처 그 實效를 거둘 可望이 잇서스나 그 後 政局의 急轉과 가티 挫折되고 말앗스며 오즉 最近에 일어난 數年來의 改造 運動뿐이 그 步武-날로 健實하야 歲在 壬戍의 今春에 들어는 또 한번 새로운 局面을 展開하며 잇도다.

하얏나니 우리의 말을 一種의 套語로써 뿐 읽지 아니하는 讀者의 머리에는 그 記憶이 오히려 남아잇슬지라. 그런데 우리는 그 글을 쓴지 겨우 半年을 나지 못한 今日에 잇서 當時의 推察이 어느덧 事實로써 나타나는 것을 甚히 깃거워하며 이 事實과 짝하야 展開되는 가장 새로운 局面을 무엇보다도 큰 現狀의 하나라 하는 바이다.
그러면 우리가 가르켜 새로이 展開되는 새 局面이라 함은 그 무엇을 이름인가 졸이어 말하면 우리가 말하야 둔 바와 가티 一般 民衆의 思言行이 얼마 前 보다도 매우 組織的 實踐的이 되어가는 그것이며 풀어 말하면 (1) 우리의 敎育熱 産業熱이 刻 一刻으로 普遍化 深刻化하는 그것이며 그리하야 (2) 2千萬 各自가  自己心性의 改造로써 重生하고 自己 環境의 轉換으로써 復活할 自覺(가장 徹底한 意味에서)을 가지는 그것이며 (3) 그 自覺이 方今多樣의 形式으로써 우리 社會의 새 現象을 지으며 잇다는 그것이다.
