필자	步月
기사형태	시 (Poem)

가을늣김
						步月

가을이 왓다 가을이 왓다.
해마다 오고가는 가을이 또 왓다.
나무입 떨어지고 풀줄기 골아지니,
죽음이냐 쉼이냐 내너더러뭇노라.

달밝고 서리찬 바람밤 기럭이 울음은 놉핫는데,
임보내고 向方모르는 이가슴의 秘密을,
너에게 부티노니 나를 대신하여,
행여나 임 맛나거던 이런事情을.

하염업는 마음, 둘대업는 가슴을,
차라리 草木과가티,
가을이 오거던, 떨어지거나, 쉬엿스면,
나도 남과 다름업시 한때나마 安逸을 어덧것을.＜135＞

Autumn Feeling
						Powŏl

Autumn has come, autumn has come.
The autumn that comes and goes each year has come again.
Tree leaves fall and grass stems wither,
Is it death or rest? I ask you this.

On a night with a bright moon, frosty cold wind, and the high cries of wild geese,
The secret of this heart, having sent away my beloved and knowing not where to turn,
I entrust to you, on my behalf,
Should you happen to meet my beloved, tell them of these circumstances (事情).

This aimless heart, this unsettled breast,
If only, like the plants and trees (草木),
When autumn came, I could fall or rest,
Then I too, like others, might have gained some peace (安逸) for a time.<135>

**Summary**

This poem by Powŏl expresses deep autumnal melancholy and longing. The speaker observes the signs of autumn – falling leaves, withering grass, migrating geese – and equates them with feelings of loss and uncertainty, possibly death or mere rest. The speaker laments sending away a beloved ('im') and feels lost, wishing to entrust their feelings to the autumn elements. They yearn for the oblivion or peace that plants seem to find in autumn, desiring respite from their restless heart. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1921 during the Japanese colonial period, resonates with the common themes of loss, separation, and yearning found in literature of that era. The 'im' could be interpreted not just as a personal beloved but potentially as a metaphor for the lost nation or sovereignty, and the speaker's desire for rest could reflect a weariness under oppressive circumstances.
