Publication Date: 1921년 11월 01일
Article Title: 春 (Spring)
Author: 玄星
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Spring
						Hyŏn Sŏng

In the Old Country's
Mountains,
Deeply
The Sun (해) has already set,
Only
The Clouds (구름)
Are Red (붉다).


How lovely,
Drunk on Love (사랑),
A young girl's
Pupils,
Flickering with Joy (깃븜) and
Sorrow (슬품),
A moist Star (별).


Pitiable
Crescent Moon (눈섭달),
This heart
Of mine,
In the red (붉은)
Cloud (구름) rift,
Presses the Chest (가슴). <135>

**Summary**

The poem "Spring" by Hyŏn Sŏng paints a melancholic picture set at sunset in the "Old Country". It contrasts images of natural beauty—red clouds after sunset, a young girl's eyes likened to stars flickering with mixed emotions, a crescent moon—with feelings of sadness and constraint ("pitiable," "presses the chest"). The overall mood is one of quiet sorrow and longing beneath a beautiful surface. For historians, the poem's reference to "故國 (Old Country)" and its somber tone, published in 1921 under Japanese rule, are significant. It likely reflects the suppressed national consciousness and the complex emotions of living under occupation, expressing a poignant mix of beauty, sorrow, and constraint felt during the colonial period.
