Tears on the Hill
						Hwang Sŏgu

It is tears. It is tears.
Now, upon some hill,
Looking into a nameless grave,
I am shedding endless tears. It is tears. It is tears.

Ah, truly, there my pitiable young past,
like a nursing infant (젓먹이),
With a resentful, pale white face,
Just as it was at the time of death, that unbearable sight,
Just so, it lies horizontally, looking up at the drizzling evening sky.
Now, looking at it, I am weeping profusely.
It is tears. It is tears.
Thinking that what is buried here is my own past, I cannot help but weep.
No, my feeling (氣持) now is just like looking upon such a thing—pitiful and sad.
I am now certainly
Weeping on such a hill while waiting for the dawning morning sun.
It is tears. It is tears.
The more I look, the more tears, endless tears, flow. <134>

**Summary**

This poem by Hwang Sŏgu expresses profound sorrow and grief. The speaker stands on a hill, weeping over a metaphorical grave containing their "young past," depicted as a deceased infant with a pale, resentful face. This intense mourning for a lost past occurs while waiting for the dawn, symbolizing a faint hope for the future amidst despair. For historians, the poem offers insight into the collective mood of loss and suppressed national identity during the early Japanese colonial period (1921). The metaphorical burial of the past could represent the perceived death of national sovereignty or cultural vitality, while the act of weeping on a hill awaiting dawn might symbolize resilience and the enduring hope for liberation or a better future.

눈물일다. 눈물일다.
只今 나는 어느 언덕 우의
이름도 업는 한 무덤속을 바라다 보면서
하욤업는 눈물을 흘리고 잇다. 눈물일다. 눈물일다.

아아 實은 그곳에는 나의 乳飮兒(젓먹이)와 가튼
可憐한 젊은 過去가
恨스럽은 쓴 蒼白의 얼굴로
臨終의 때의 그 참아 볼 수 업던 모습대로,
그대로, 저녁 비의 부슬부슬 오는 한울을 쳐다보고 가루누어잇다.
나는 지금 그것을 바라다보면서 盛大히 울고 잇다.
눈물일다. 눈물일다.
이곳에 무틴 것이 自己의 過去냐고 생각하면, 나는 다못 울지 안코는 잇슬 수 업다.
아니, 나의 지금의 마음(氣持)이 마치 이런 것을 바라보고 잇슴과 가튼 애처럽고 슬픈 그것일다.
나는 지금 확실히
밝는 새벽의 太陽을 기둘르면서 그런 언덕 우에서 울고 잇다.
눈물일다. 눈물일다.
