기사제목	社會諷剌 銀파리 (Social Satire: Silver Fly)
필자	목성 記 (Recorded by Moksŏng)
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)

社會諷剌 銀파리
						목성 記
Because I spent the summer busily traveling around various regions, I had very little spare time and there were many things I had intended to do but couldn't. Seeing the big, round eyes of the big-eyed teacher at Kaebyŏk Publishing House widen as much as they could was both frightening and apologetic, but what could I do when I truly had no time? Instead, I saw and learned many things.
What's that? Summer is over, so the season of flies has passed!? Oh, just go on feeling relieved. This is none other than the "Silver" Fly, born precisely on New Year's Day. Even if frost comes and snow pours down, the Silver Fly's vigor does not wane.

I went to visit the esteemed Mr. Ko—famous not only as a young orator and thinker but also as a devout religious figure—at his residence, just as Mr. Ko was stepping out of the main gate.
White shoes, white trousers, a sleek black jacket over them, a bright blue necktie, horn-rimmed glasses, a tilted straw hat. If one didn't know his prestigious name, anyone would see him as the premier dandy, but reputation is a fine thing; to those in the know, he is a splendid model youth, a young patriot. With extraordinary eloquence, lofty thoughts, and devout faith, the renowned Mr. Ko, famous through mouth, pen, and action! Where is he heading now as he steps out of his own gate? —No matter how much humans live by lies and how well they lie, surely this Mr. Ko, a thinker and religious figure, must have some slight difference... Anyway, today I decided to follow this teacher's tail. —However, even though it's tailing, it's different from the tailing of a detective following a familiar person like a puppy. It's a comfortable and cool<70> tailing, perched upon the shoulder of Mr. Ko's perfume-scented Western suit.
Whether aware or not that the tailing of an insolent fly had begun, the Teacher walked with his not-so-long legs stretched out, mimicking a Westerner's gait, until he reached the entrance of Kyedong. He entered a large, tile-roofed house with a grand gate, and gained one companion. He was a student at a certain professional school located outside Seoul. As the two walked together in step, whispering about the hot weather, Changch'ungdan New Park, church matters, and so on, they reached the midway point of Kyodong. Mr. Ch'oe, the professional school student, hesitated and then ducked into an unusual sundry goods store with a signboard by the roadside (I found out it was a Women's Store). I waited on the street, still perched atop Mr. Ko who remained standing there. After a short while, Mr. Ch'oe came out holding a pure white handkerchief, showed it to Ko, and said while walking,
"Ah, this was 40 *chŏn*...?! Couldn't see a shadow of the actual owner, she must have gone somewhere, and some old crone came out... Eeeng. But how could I just turn back without buying it...? Just wasted money paying too much..." he said.

Even if the price is high, if a young woman sells it, one buys it... In a way, it seems plausible. For the owner of a perverted sexuality who feels a kind of satisfaction just from having a young woman wearing face powder hand it over with her pretty hand, if he cherishes that handkerchief bought from a woman like a lifelong amulet, it will neither wear out nor have any worry of getting dirty, and consequently, there will be no need to buy one often. Humans are cunning creatures—especially a professional school student, his trick for saving precious money seems quite plausible.

As these two gentlemen cut through T'apkol Park (Pagoda Park) and exited through the main gate, a certain gentleman rushed towards them with a pleased expression, grabbing Mr. Ko's hand and shaking it. Mr. Ko also rushed forward as if meeting someone unexpectedly delightful, and they shook hands vigorously.
"Are you well? How busy you must be. You work so hard for society. Not at all. By no means."—After exchanging these set greetings, repeated countless times whenever they meet, the gentleman said,
"That manuscript you mentioned—you must be terribly busy, but..."
He seemed to be an editor for some magazine. Mr. Ko replied,
"Yes—I'm truly sorry. I've been busy... It should be ready around the day after tomorrow," and the editor, grateful just for the promise that it would be written, said,
"Yes! You must be very busy. Then, I will come by the afternoon after next. This is terribly inconvenient for you. Ah—by the way, <71> you're giving a lecture at OO Church tonight, aren't you? I saw it in the newspaper. It starts at half-past seven, right? I will go too. You really do work hard..." It seemed they had already run out of things to say to each other, but they stood awkwardly, each seemingly hoping the other would leave first. After dithering for a while, the editor spoke first,
"I must have taken much of your time when you're so busy. I'll see you again later." They exchanged seemingly relieved farewells, and Mr. Ko and Mr. Ch'oe stood waiting for the streetcar. After a moment, Mr. Ch'oe asked,
"Who was that? Wasn't it U something?"
"I don't know, was that fellow U-ga?" Mr. Ch'oe exclaimed in surprise,
"You talked with him without even knowing who he was?"
"I've seen him somewhere before."
"Then you should have said you didn't know him from the start."
"My dear fellow, where is such a thing permissible in social interaction? Where is such rudeness allowed? In any case, isn't it enough to just pretend to know him like that? The manuscript? That's something to talk about when we meet again then..."

I didn't know Mr. Ko was like this either. What is social interaction, and what is etiquette? The affairs of humans are truly unknowable. It doesn't matter at all. Just spew lies as they come out of your mouth. That's social interaction, that's etiquette, they say, putting on a straight face. Mr. Ko, the thinker, the religious figure, is also of that ilk. Since he too is a human being, how could he possibly be different?
Even if I tailed him day and night, there probably wouldn't be anything particularly refreshing to see, but where on earth are these fellows going? I'll keep following for now. Presently, the two boarded an East Gate (Tongdaemun)-bound streetcar coming from Chongno. To escape the hot afternoon sun around 3 PM, the car heading towards Ch'ŏngnyangni was packed with passengers.
Below the East Gate, everyone poured out, leaving only Ko and Ch'oe, plus one Japanese person. The car with just these three remaining headed along the castle wall towards Kwanghŭimun and reached the corner of Hullyŏnwŏn (Training Ground). Ko and Ch'oe got off, changed to a Wangsimni-bound streetcar, and after passing the Wangsimni school, got off at the lower corner behind the Wangsimni village. They turned onto the south field path along the newly made road and walked. After walking for a while along the hot, sun-drenched field path, they entered a pine grove resembling a cremation ground and slipped into a neat thatched house in front of OO Temple without any greeting. It seemed an advance party had already arrived; three early arrivals, who were getting up noisily from the wooden floor, exclaimed, "Hey, hey, you're finally here, finally here!" Among them was a young man with tousled long hair, looking like a philosopher or a poet. More noteworthy here <72> was the fact that there were two women present, saying, "Mr. Ch'oe, please come quickly. Mr. Ko, you've just arrived?"
Wearing black skirts and with Western-style hair, I wondered if they were schoolgirls from somewhere, but one was named Puyong and the other Sohong, or something like that. According to the explanation of the long-haired philosopher sitting there, they were goddesses of love, incarnations of affection, the most precious and lovely flowers in life. Whether they were lovely flowers or pits of money, I had no reason to know, but Mr. Ko, famous as a devout believer, Mr. Ko, currently the general secretary of the OOO Youth Association, didn't seem likely to attend such a drinking party... But what's this, what's this? He drinks strong *soju* (燒酒) like water. Eating, drinking, chatting loudly, taking off his upper garments, embracing the *kisaeng* (妓生), jumping here and there, vomiting, then eating again, eating and jumping again—the more alcohol went in, the more the fellows' masks and disguises began to peel off. Etiquette, dignity, social grace, whatever—the silk clothes they wore, wrapped layer upon layer, labeling them thinkers, religious figures, socialites, were skillfully stripped away by the demon called alcohol. The masks of the religious figure and the thinker were already shed. Dignity, social grace, etiquette were all shed. The fellows completely forgot their customary phrases like "I'm sorry," "Thank you," "Excuse me." By now, nothing remained. Only the naked human body. Appetite, sexual desire, whatever desires, ran rampant.
Today, I saw the original form of the naked human being for the first time. There was no "lord of all creation." Humans had no distinct characteristics. Only by hiding this way and wrapping that way with falsehood and pretense did they pretend to have morality, pretend to have etiquette, and were called lords or whatever. The difference in how wildly and grotesquely they leap and frolic might vary from person to person. But in the end, it's fifty steps versus a hundred steps. Look there. Look at the sight of the one who cast off the clothes of a religious figure, a thinker, jumping about. Look at the sight of five males surrounding two females, leaping wildly. Where, in what aspect, is a human different from a pack of puppies? Does it seem likely that etiquette and dignity exist in that state? Look at that. They're already calling each other "this punk" and "that punk" (*inom*, *chŏnom*). Calling someone "this punk" without feeling sorry, hearing oneself called "that punk" without getting angry. They accept that term as natural. This is what humans originally are. And these fellows, having lived so much by deception in a false world, playing while wearing all sorts of disguises, feel exhilarated after running wild like that.
There's no way to know which is the real thing.
They jumped and jumped until exhausted, then embraced and collapsed. Mr. Ko, the general secretary of the OOO Youth Association, whose face showed no flush despite drinking so much alcohol, used the liquor table as a makeshift lectern, slapped the overturned food dishes and cups with his palm, and began to ramble in his characteristic <73> lecture style. A man lying with his head on a *kisaeng*'s lap began to sing "Taedong Riverbank" (大同江邊). Another man sang "A Pair of Pigeons by Nodol Riverside" (노-돌 江邊비닭이 한 쌍).
Mr. Ko delivered some speech for quite a while, but only sporadically could one hear him shout loudly, "The sweet wine and women God has given us! We who are human should rightfully pluck and drink!!" then burst into laughter. "Right, right!" they cheered, pounding the floor. Mr. Ko, brimming with spirit, suddenly seemed to think of something.
"Ah, right, I have something to introduce to you gentlemen. Hey you punk, get up. Huh? Get up, I have something to introduce," he said, earnestly trying to raise the reclining man. A chorus of "What is it? What?" erupted. Mr. Ko slammed the liquor table and announced,
"Gentlemen, we had a secret society."
"What? What? What kind of society—"
"It has been an absolute secret until now, but today, in commemoration, no, as a great privilege of us comrades gathering like this and having fun, I will reveal it only here today..."
One man grabbed a *kisaeng*'s hand, raised it, and shouted "Manse!" (Long live!).
"That secret society is—I will reveal this society, so you all pour me a cup of liquor."
The remaining four fellows poured a cup each. After drinking one cup, he continued,
"That secret society is called the 'Chae San Chu Sik Hoe Sa' (재 산 주 식 회 사)."
Several fellows asked what kind of company it was.
"It is precisely the *Chae San Chu Sik Hoe Sa* (財 散 酒 食 會 社 - Wealth-Scattering Drink-Eat Company)!"
As he pronounced each character, they jumped up and down shouting, "Agreed! Agreed!" Ko continued,
"And I am the secretary of that company. Therefore, gentlemen, if you agree, I hope you will join. There are currently four members. First, the initiation procedure is to treat all existing members, and then, afterwards, we all treat the new member as a welcome greeting... Gentlemen, but this is absolutely secret. Yes. The world's fellows still don't understand such things, so we have no choice but to keep it secret..."

Indeed, the fellow is a thinker. He came up with something ingenious. The Wealth-Scattering Drink-Eat Company—a society for playing like beasts to one's heart's content, I suppose.

The sun set. Among them, Mr. Ko stood up, saying he had to go give his lecture. "Hey, man. Let's skip it today. How can you do it like this?" someone said. "No. There was an incident like this early last month too, but I did it fine without any trouble. Well—I'm leaving first," he said and started to leave. Still lying sprawled, <74> the long-haired fellow asked, "What's the lecture title?" Ko replied, "Title? Uh—The True Life of Man," and left. "Hmph, The True Life of Man—fine! Go tell them drinking and loving is the true life. Tell them living like us here is the true life. Then you'll get applause," the man mumbled to himself, lying sprawled.
Utter chaos!! And yet, Mr. Ko, who left this place first to maintain his act as a religious figure, his act as a thinker, will soon be on the pulpit of OO Church, crying out about the sanctity of faith. The unfortunate thousands in the audience will clap at every phrase and verse, deeply moved.

Ah, what a miserable, strange phenomenon this is. What a state the world is in. Amidst this, these fellows live on.

Centered around Puyong and Sohong, they sang at the top of their lungs, then that too subsided, and they lay down. This time, the long-haired philosopher-type began to chatter. "Puyong, won't you commit a love suicide with me?" "Why?" "Haha. If you ask 'why,' can it happen? The Italian youth born of love, living for love, dying for love are truly happy, I tell you. Where there is earth, there must be grass, and where there are people, there must be love, but because that's missing, isn't life just lonely? If we were to eliminate women from this world, what would become of that world? It would become a cold, cold, ice-like world. Should we, born as humans, live in a world of love warm like spring? Or should we live in a cold and desolate ice world without this or that? Isn't that right? Puyong, huh, isn't that right?" he asked, and while still lying down, pulled the seated Puyong's face and pressed it against his own cheek. The others, interrupting their humming, exclaimed, "Right, right! That's absolutely right!" The long-haired man, energized, said, "That's why, for the Training Association (修養會) we're initiating this time, let's permit women to join too." "No, not permit, we must drag them in," shouted one fellow. "Alright, let's decide that on the day of the initiation meeting."

How about that? Fellows like these, with such thoughts, chattering like this, eventually mimic others by forming a Youth Training Association or whatnot. In other words, vagrants gather, create a headquarters for debauchery, and hang a sign, imitating others, largely inscribed: "So-and-so Youth Training Association."
What can fellows like you achieve by invoking something called a Training Association? Wouldn't it be more honest to follow Mr. Ko's Wealth-Scattering Drink-Eat style and write "Inviting-Love Following-Girls Association" (請戀隨孃會)? <75> And standing with that sign behind you, dig the hole of destruction larger and deeper. That will be your only path.

Since I started writing this way, I'll finish it. In the second-floor rooms of Kyŏngsŏng restaurants like Kugilgwan and Myŏngwŏlgwan, between the paper sliding doors separating the rooms, someone wrote:
"Love, money—, money, love—
I ended up crying. Because there was nothing I could do."
It seems to be a lament sighed out by some long-haired poet or philosopher, peeking through a crack in the paper door at the *kisaeng* party in the next room, crossing his legs.

Ah, you pitiful beings. Isn't this too much!
Who will stand on the desolate ruins and build a sacred new Chosŏn (Korea)? From amidst the awakening masses, do you not cry out with your own mouths?
"Our new culture must be built with our own hands!"
Indeed. Without paying the price with precious young blood, it cannot be obtained.
Be a little more truthful. Be a little more meaningful.
Tagore (타골-) says,
"Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high; Where knowledge is free..."
Young people! Can you not do things that you are not ashamed of in your hearts?... <76>

**Summary**

This satirical piece, "Silver Fly," by Moksŏng, follows and critiques the hypocritical behavior of a prominent young intellectual and religious leader, identified as Mr. Ko (高先生). The narrator secretly observes Mr. Ko engage in superficial social interactions, blatant dishonesty, and ultimately, a drunken, debauched gathering with friends and *kisaeng* (female entertainers) at a secluded house outside Seoul. The article contrasts Mr. Ko's public persona as a moral guide and speaker with his private indulgence, highlighting the facade of respectability and the perceived moral decay among some self-proclaimed leaders of the youth. The author extends the critique to similar youth groups, suggesting their "Training Associations" are merely fronts for pleasure-seeking, and ends with an appeal for greater sincerity and meaningful action from the younger generation, invoking Tagore. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its sharp social critique during the colonial period, reflecting disillusionment with figures of authority and intellectual elites. It provides a glimpse into the tensions between traditional morality, Western influences (clothing, social manners, intellectual concepts), and the perceived hypocrisy within nascent nationalist or social movements, as well as commentary on youth culture, drinking, and gender dynamics in 1921 Seoul (Kyŏngsŏng).

무엇 어쨰? 녀름이 다가서 파리의 時節도 지나갓다고!? 오오 그리고 安心만 하고 잇거라. 더도 덜도 업시 꼭 正月 初하루 날 誕生한 『銀』파리시다. 서리가 오고 눈이 쏘다져도 銀파리의 元氣는 衰하지 안는다.

靑年雄辯家 靑年思想家를 兼한 外에 篤實한 宗敎家로까지 高名한 高先生을 本宅으로 차저 가니까 고先生이 마악 大門을 나선다.
힌 구두 힌 바지 우에 검은 웃옷을 날씬히 입고 새파란 넥타이에 角테 眼鏡 비스듬한 麥藁帽, 高名한 名號를 모르고 보면 누가 보던지 八란봉의 大首席이지만 名譽란, 조흔 것, 알고 보는 이에게는 훌륭한 模範靑年 靑年有志이다. 口辯이 非凡하고 思想이 高尙하고 信仰이 篤實하고 입으로 붓으로 行動으로 닷는대로 高名한 高先生! 그가 只今 어대를 向하는지 自己집 大門을 나선다.-사람이란 놈들이 아모리 거짓말로 살아가고 아모리 거짓말을 잘한대도 思想家요, 宗敎家인 이 高先生이야 족음쯤은 다른 점이 잇겟지…아모려나 오늘은 이 先生의 尾行을 해보자고 딸아 나섯다.-그러나 尾行이라도 낫닉은 사람 뒤에 개새끼 따르듯하는 형사의 尾行과는 다르다. 便安하고 시원하게＜70＞ 고先生의 香水내가 나는 洋服 억깨우에 올라 안저서의 尾行일다.
