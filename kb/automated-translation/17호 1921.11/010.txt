기사형태	논설 (Editorial)

Place Your Feet on the Very Bottom Layer (맨 밋層에 발을 부티라)

Grains of sand roll along the surface of the riverbed (河床). However, gold nuggets sink into its bottom layer (밋層), and duckweed (浮萍) floats upon the water's surface. Yet fish and dragons (魚龍) submerge themselves beneath it; therefore, comrade, place your feet only on the very bottom layer. Then, besides sand, you shall gather (採取) gold, and besides duckweed, you shall obtain (蓾獲) fish and dragons.

Although addressing wise brothers (賢明한 兄弟), if you would permit (容許) a few words of suggestion (提言), we wish to appeal (呼訴) with enthusiasm (熱心): Brothers! In whatever actions (云爲) you undertake, strive only to place your feet on the very bottom layer. Do not merely lick the surface and declare it bitter or sweet; chew (咀嚼) it down to its core (核心) to assess (勘定) its true taste. If you start (始作) something, see it to its end; if it is words, limit them to what you have thought; if it is thought, let it conform (合一) to your principles (主義).

Do not feel pleased merely by cultivating appearances and skillfully handling daily social interactions (日常의 社交); do not consider it grand (壯) simply to speak loftily and clench your fists well. Consider only that frankness (率直), sincerity (誠實), and passion (熱烈) alone will shape the righteous future (義로운 將來) for us as individuals (個人) or as a nation (民族).

Do not senselessly pull in the words or norms (規範) left by ancient sages (聖哲) and act self-satisfied (足); rather, brothers, directly penetrate (叅入) the very source (本源頭) of things (事物) yourselves, scrutinize (覈辨) what must be scrutinized, and acknowledge (是認) what must be acknowledged. Accordingly, bear clearly in mind (銘記) that the various forms (形式) and traditions (傳說) remaining in the present (現在) will offer no particular contribution (別般의 寄與) to the true life (生活) of tomorrow (明日) that we all anticipate (期約).

Know that your body is like a naked infant just sprung from the great bosom of the universe (宇宙); though it may seem small, though it may seem weak, day and night, upon that body of yours, brothers, the blazing (焰焰) flame (火炎) of life (生命) is always burning. Thus, wherever that flame turns, surely all ugliness (醜傀) shall be burned away (燒盡), and in that incinerated site, a newly recognized symbol (記號)! Consider that alone to be the most precious (貴) among the treasures we can possess.

Before we know it, the coolness passes, and the cold arrives. For a while, we shall be perplexed (困惑) by the show of authority (呈威) of the White Emperor (白帝; god of autumn/winter). However, the winter of bleak landscapes (滿目*然), wind and snow (風雪), and falling leaves (搖落) in a sense conversely promotes (促) our substance (質實) and stirs (動) our fortitude (强氣). May it be most fortunate (幸甚) if, by encountering this winter, all the steps forward of our people gain an added layer (一層) of thoroughness (徹底).<1>

**Summary**

This editorial passionately urges readers ("brothers") to reject superficiality and engage deeply with the core substance of life, thought, and action ("place your feet on the very bottom layer"). It contrasts fleeting surface elements (sand, duckweed) with valuable underlying realities (gold, fish/dragons). The author advocates for sincerity, critical thinking, seeing things through, and aligning actions with principles, rather than focusing on appearances, empty rhetoric, or blindly following past traditions. The piece calls for embracing one's innate potential ("flame of life") to burn away ugliness and forge a new, authentic future, even amidst hardship (symbolized by winter), which can strengthen resolve and thoroughness. From a Korean colonial history perspective, this text is significant. Published in *Gaebyeok* (Creation/Dawn), a major nationalist cultural magazine, in 1921, shortly after the March 1st Movement, its call for depth, self-reliance, critical examination of tradition, and forging an authentic future strongly resonates with the era's nationalist aspirations. The emphasis on inner strength ("substance," "fortitude") in the face of hardship ("winter") can be interpreted as a coded message encouraging Koreans to cultivate resilience and prepare for a genuine national future despite the oppressive colonial reality. Historians might find the blend of philosophical urging and veiled nationalist sentiment, typical of cultural resistance under censorship, particularly interesting.

맨 밋層에 발을 부티라
모래알은 河床거트로 굴은다. 그러나 金ㅅ조각은 그 밋層에 들어박히며 浮萍은 물낫우으로 떠오른다. 그러나 魚龍은 그미테 잠기나니 동무여 오즉 맨 밋層에 발을 부티라. 그러면 모래알 以外에 黃金을 採取할 것이며 浮萍 以外에 魚龍을 蓾獲하리라.
賢明한 兄弟의 案前임에 不拘하고 여러분이 만일 두어 마디 提言을 容許한다 하면 우리는 熱心으로써 呼訴코저 함이 잇노니 兄弟여 兄弟의 云爲하는 그 무엇에서나 오즉 맨 밋層에 발을 부틸 工夫를 하라. 다못 것만 할고 쓰다 맵다 하지 말며 그 核心까지를 咀嚼하야써 그 맛의 如何를 勘定케 하라. 始作이어던 그 끗을 보게 하며 말이 어던 그 생각한 바에 限케 하며 생각이어던 그 主義에 合一케 하라.
다못 것모양을 꾸미고 日常의 社交를 巧妙허 함으로써 快하다 하지 말며 한갓 말을 놉히고 줌억을 잘 쥠으로써 壯하다 하지 말라. 오즉 率直, 誠實, 熱烈뿐이 다시 우리 個人이나 또는 民族의 義로운 將來를 꾸밀 것을 생각하라.
턱업시 옛 聖哲의 남긴 말이나 或은 規範을 끌어 당기어써 스스로 足한 체 하지 말며 오즉 兄弟 스스로가 直接으로 事物의 本源頭에 叅入하야써 覈辨할 것을 覈辨하며 是認할 것을 是認하라. 딸아서 現在에 남아 잇는 여러 가지 形式이나 傳說이 우리 一般이 期約하는 明日의 참 生活에 別般의 寄與가 업슬 것을 銘記하라.
宇宙의 큰 가슴속으로부터 지금 막 뛰어나온 빨가숭이가 그 몸이라 하며 비록 작은 듯하나 弱한 듯하나 밤으로 낫으로 兄弟의 그 몸 우에는 焰焰한 生命의 火炎이 늘-타며 잇는 것을 알라. 그래서 그 火炎이 向하는 곳에는 반듯이 모든  醜傀은 燒盡할지며 그 燒盡된 불터에서 새로히 認하는 或 記號! 그것뿐이 우리가 가질 보배 中 가장 貴한 것이라 하라.
어느덧 서느러움이 가고 치위가 오도다. 한참동안 우리는 白帝의 呈威에 困惑하리라. 그러나 滿目*然, 風雪搖落의 겨울은 어느 意味에서 돌이어 우리의 質實을 促하며 强氣를 動하나니 이 겨울을 마즘으로 因하야 우리 一般의 나아가는 모든 거름이 一層 徹底함이 잇스면 幸甚이라.＜1＞
