Magazine Title: 개벽 제17호
Publication Date: 1921년 11월 01일
Article Title: [내가 이른바 어질다함은…] ([What I Call Goodness Is...])
Author: 壯子
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous writing)

壯子
What I call goodness (어질다) is not referring to Benevolence and Righteousness (仁義), but only to entrusting oneself as is to one's Virtue (其德), that is, the true state (情) of one's Nature and Destiny (其性命).

What I call Hearing and Sight (聰·明) does not refer to hearing that (彼) well or seeing that well, but only refers to hearing oneself and seeing oneself.

Generally, one who cannot see oneself but sees others, who cannot obtain for oneself but obtains from others, is one who obtains what others obtain but does not obtain for oneself what one should obtain (得人之得而 不自得其得者也); one who conforms only to others and cannot conform to oneself (適人適而不自適其適者也). (Original Text = 是, 得人之得而 不自得其得者也 適人適而不自適其適者也) To only conform to others (남의 것에만 맛추고) and not know how to conform to oneself (自己의 것에 맛게 할 줄을 몰랏다 하면) is to merely become a licentious and perverse (淫僻) person, like Tao Chih (盜跖) or Po I (伯夷). <39>

**Summary**

This short piece, attributed to Chuang Tzu (壯子), critiques conventional morality (Benevolence and Righteousness - 仁義) and perception. It argues that true goodness and clarity come from aligning with one's inner nature (其德, 其性命之情) and self-perception, rather than conforming to external standards or focusing on others. Those who only follow others without understanding or connecting with their own selves are compared unfavorably to figures like Tao Chih (盜跖) and Po I (伯夷), deemed "licentious and perverse" in this context for their extreme adherence to external principles (or lack thereof) without self-attunement. For historians of colonial Korea, this article published in *Kaebyŏk* is interesting as it invokes classical Chinese philosophy (specifically Taoism via Chuang Tzu) to discuss themes of authenticity, self-reliance, and the dangers of conforming to external pressures ('others'). In the context of Japanese assimilation policies and the influx of Western ideas, such a discussion implicitly encourages introspection and adherence to one's own 'nature' (potentially interpretable on a personal or even national level), contrasting with externally imposed values.

내가 이른바 어질다 함은 仁義를 이름이 아니라 其德, 即 其性命의 情에 그대로 맷기는 그것뿐이며 내가 이른바 聰·明이라 함은 그 저(彼)를 잘 듯고 或은 저를 잘 보는 그것을 이름이 아니라 스스로 듯고 스스로 보는 그것을 말함 뿐이니 대개 스스로 보지 못하고 저를 보며 스스로 엇지 못하고 저를 엇는 者는 이 남의 엇을 것을 엇고 스스로 엇을 것을 엇지 못하는 者이며 남의 것에만 마추(適)고 自己의 것에는 마추지 못하는 者라.(原文=是, 得人之得而 不自得其得者也 適人適而不自適其適者也) 남의 것에만 맛추고 自己의 것에 맛게 할 줄을 몰랏다 하면 盜跖이나 伯夷나다-가티 淫僻한 者가 될 뿐이니라.＜39＞
