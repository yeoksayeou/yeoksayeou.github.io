Article Title: 擲筆之餘 (After Laying Down the Pen / Editorial Afterthoughts)
Author: Not specified
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Editorial Note/Postscript)

There must be space on the page, but truly, the space is so limited, there's nothing that can be done. This month, because articles related to Kyŏngbuk province were included, we especially felt difficulties in editing. Regarding this point, we particularly beg the understanding of the various gentlemen who submitted contributions.

The upcoming issue is the July issue (No. 37), the very issue marking the full 3rd anniversary since this magazine's first publication (刱判後). It has been decided to put out a commemorative enlarged issue. Although it might be called an enlarged issue according to precedent, it is said that its content will strive for unprecedented novelty (蘄新).

The party of survey members for the "Chosŏn Culture Basic Survey," heading for P'yŏngbuk province, departed Kyŏngsŏng in mid-May. According to correspondence received, among the four members of the party, Mr. Pak Talch'eng (朴達成) and Mr. Han Chungjŏn (韓重銓) headed towards the border region, while Mr. Cho Kigam (趙基栞) and Mr. Yi Sŏngsam (李成三) headed towards the area along the railway line. By the way, the stalwart (장부다운) activities of Mr. Han and the saint-like (聖者다운) sincerity of Mr. Cho seem to be particularly drawing the attention of our brethren.

To Mr. Mun Kyŏnghŏn (文敬憲君), who is at the Kyōto Municipal Udanoryōyōjo (京都市 立宇多野療養所) in Japan: Sir, we truly read the factual account (實記) you sent of sanatorium life while holding back tears. If that record were to be published now, how bitter and painful would the feelings of the readers be? Mr. Mun! Please, stay healthy. Though this world ignores your existence, how could you possibly submit to that peacefully (晏然히)? However, Sir, you must not become agitated. Calmly convalesce and aim for gradual recovery (漸快).<61>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the June 1923 issue of *Kaebyŏk* apologizes for space constraints, particularly due to articles about Kyŏngbuk, forcing rejection of some submissions. It announces an enlarged 3rd-anniversary issue for July, promising unique content. The editors report on the progress of the magazine's cultural survey team in P'yŏngbuk, noting the division of the team and praising the efforts of two members. Finally, it includes a heartfelt message of encouragement to a reader, Mun Kyŏnghŏn, recuperating in a Japanese sanatorium, urging him to recover despite feeling ignored by the world. For historians, the mention of a "Chosŏn Culture Basic Survey" conducted by the magazine itself in P'yŏngbuk offers insight into private cultural research initiatives during the colonial period. The personal message to Mun Kyŏnghŏn highlights social issues like illness (likely tuberculosis, common in sanatoriums) and the sense of alienation potentially felt by Koreans, even those receiving treatment in Japan.

擲筆之餘
紙面이 잇서야 하지 진실로 紙面이 적어서 엇절 수가 업다. 이 달은 慶北에 관한 기사가 들기 때문에 더욱이나 編輯上 곤난을 느꼇다. 이 점에 대하야 특히 投稿하신 諸兄이 諒恕를 빈다.
來號는 7月號(第37號) 本誌 刱判後 만 3주년되는 號이다. 紀念增大號를 내이기로 되엿다. 例에 의한 增大號이라 할지나 그 내용에 잇서는 유례업는 蘄新을 極할 것이라 한다.
「朝鮮文化基本調査」를 위한 平北踏査員 일행은 5月 중순에 京城을 떠낫다. 來信에 의하면 일행 4인 중 朴達成, 韓重銓 兩君은 국경방면으로 趙基栞
李成三 兩君은 鐵道沿線 方面으로 向하게 되엿다. 그런대 韓君의 장부다운 활동과 趙君의 聖者다운 진실은 別로히 형제의 注意를 이끄는 樣이다.
日本 京都市 立宇多野療養所에 잇는 文敬憲君, 君의 보내온 사나토리움생활의 實記는 졍말 눈물을 먹음고 닑엇다. 그 기록이 이제 發布된다 하면 그것을 닑는 독자의 심정은 얼마나 쓰리로 압프랴. 文君이여 부대 건강하라, 이 世間이 君의 존재를 무시하나니 君이 엇지 晏然히 그에 服할 수 잇스랴. 그러나 君이여 흥분되여서는 안 된다. 고요히 療養하야써 漸快를 期約하라.＜61＞
