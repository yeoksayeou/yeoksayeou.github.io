The Returned Heretic
						O Yŏnghwi

A is a young man of 21 this year.
However, he suffers from melancholia (憂鬱症). The illness began 2 or 3 years ago. Consequently, he quit school and now spends his days idly, doing nothing. For him, passing the 24 hours of a day is so tedious, more bitter and agonizing than chewing on gallnuts. Ah, the day of anguish (懊惱) has passed. But tomorrow will surely come again. Thus, one day, then two, human affairs are linked in a chain (連鎖). Yet, "This too is emptiness (空虛), that too is disillusionment (幻滅)" is the sound he constantly cries out.

Ah, shall I go out for a walk after such a long time? He took down the soft hat (소프트帽), faded from black to grey, which hung on the wall, also wearing a desolate (寂寥한) expression. He pressed it onto his dishevelled, lustreless (潤氣업는) hair, unparted by a comb, grabbed a thin walking stick made of bamboo root (대뿌리), and stepped out of his house gate.

His aimless (定處도 업시) steps somehow led him to the field where he always used to play. Crossing the field, there is a small hill. A slowly climbed the hill. The hill, tinged with the golden frost-color (凍色) of early winter leaves, sloped gently, forming a deep valley with the hill on the opposite side (건너 便). In the valley bottom (谷底), covered as if by a black velvet curtain (羽緞帳幕), a single stream of clear water, like a strip of laundry spread on a sandbank, flowed with a murmuring sound.

A stood staring down at the flowing water, his eyes fixed as if he had fainted.
How refreshing (淸爽할가) it would be, he thought, if I could shatter this heavy head in that clear water, like blue glass (靑琉璃), and wash away the moss clinging inside my heart.

He lay down in the grass, spreading his arms and legs wide like the character 大. The young grass of early winter gave him a softer tactile sensation (觸感) than sheep's wool, softer than the clouds rising in summer. Lying still, breathless like a corpse (屍體), he felt the scent of grass <67> and sunlight piercing into his flesh.

On the other side of the sky, clouds like flocks of white sheep rose billowingly. They clung together, then separated, separated, then clung together again. It looked just like young village wives gathering their heads together, deeply engrossed (滋味잇게) in playing house (솟곱질).

Those clouds too seemed to chatter amongst themselves, jostling, and finally fighting, then reconciling. After a while, a swarm of dark, unsightly (밉살스러운), disgusting clouds, like ragged scraps, rose from below. Like a drunkard (酒酊軍) with his chest bared, it headed towards where the young wives were playing absorbedly and delightfully, and then recklessly beat and trampled the lovely, beautiful cloud maidens. The maidens, terrified (驚㥘), not knowing what to do, seemed to weep and wail as they fled. The large, dark cloud swarm collided (衝突) with the young clouds, shattering them into pieces (粉碎), like those wandering (彷徨) in unprepared sorrow, unable to shed a single tear. A clenched his fists tightly and shook them at the clouds as if to restrain (制肘) the hateful (憎惡) and cruel (慘虐) cloud tyrant (타이렌트). In the end, finding it so repulsive, he spat at the clouds.

"I thought hateful tyrants existed only on this Earth, persecuting the weak. How could I have ever dreamed that there would be demons setting fire to paradise (파라따이스) in the heavens? Seeing this, there seems to be no great difference (懸隔) between Earth and Heaven. It is the unspoken law (定律) everywhere that the weak face destruction.
Why was I born into this world?"

This is the solitary utterance A makes when lonely (孤寂), or when his weak nature reveals his shrunken (萎縮된) soul, observing the various phenomena of this world, when all things in the universe (森羅萬象) appear like messengers (使者) of the devil, feeling the threat (脅威) constantly pressing down on his head, cried out from the depths (巷底) of despair (絶望).

When each person is born, there must be a divine oracle (托宣) to do something upon entering human society. Whether to become a thief (盜賊), a devil (惡魔), a saint (聖者), a hero (英雄), a farmer (農夫), or a poet (詩人), each must have received their respective letter of appointment (辭令狀) <68> upon birth (誕生) into this world. But why is it that I couldn't become a great (偉大한) person, nor an extremely wicked (極惡한) one, not a farmer, nor a poet! Probably not even a poet. Just an ordinary (凡庸한) lump of flesh. It seems my commission (所任狀) was likely revoked (褫奪) at the customs gate (關門) of human life. However, besides me, it is a fact that ordinary people (凡庸者) secretly exist among the populace of this world. That the multitude of the ordinary, buried within the eternal sphinx (스핑쓰), has been gilded (金箔) with a riddle (謎) unsolvable for all ages (萬古), is the mischief (作戱) of a spiteful god, yet on the other hand, I am grateful. Compared to the harm (害毒) spread in this world by geniuses (天才) and idiots (白痴), the ordinary person is the luckiest child (幸運兒) in this world. Those who have received God's grace (恩寵) are solely the multitude of the ordinary, us.

The avaricious (貪欲) rich (富者), eyes bulging, shedding blood and sweat to scrape together gold (黃金), staring at it and drooling; the powerful (權力者), satisfying themselves that their power is absolute by subjugating all the weak under their sharp sword (利劍); the femme fatale (傾國의 麗人), delighting (狂喜) in the skill (技巧) of her beauty as thousands of fools (痴人) kneel (跪拜) before her; the moralists (道學者) and the pious (敬神者), claiming to save (救濟) the people of this world, calling the words from their mouths the gospel (福音) of heaven and the writings from their hands scriptures (經典), extracting the souls (魂) of the fools of this world as much as possible, feeding them the knockout medicine (녹크아웃메듸씰?) of Heaven and Nirvana (涅槃), paralyzing (痲痺) infinitely precious human nature, making them rely solely on the pillar (樞柱) of empty (虛荒한) faith (信仰) – this is the embodiment (權化) of their purpose.

O rich, powerful, beautiful, and saviors of humanity in this world! You are truly idiots. Do you think you received the power (權能) you wield from God without cost, forever? No. No. God lent you that power only for the duration of your lives to test (試練) you. Seeing you lose your souls and run madly (狂奔), God will surely laugh heartily, mouth splitting to the ears. God is not such a nice old man as you imagine. He is not like the grandfather (祖父) of your childhood, whom you pestered for roasted chestnuts (굼밤) when passing a chestnut stall, or for taffy when meeting a taffy seller on the street; God is not such an indulgent grandfather.

O followers of the Cult of Gold (黃金宗), Cult of Power (權力宗), Cult of Beauty (美貌宗), Cult of Piety (敬神宗)! Do you have the courage, even after entering the tomb (墳墓) and becoming skulls (髑髏), to still try to embrace gold, draw your sharp swords, boast (자량하려) of your beauty, and propagate (傳波) the gospel?
Time is short. Isn't the ticking sound of the clock's second hand (세콘드) the footsteps of the hell messenger (地獄使者) capturing (捕捉) you for the grave (墓所)? <69> This world is a single panorama (파노라마) linked from billions of years ago to billions of years hence. All is nothingness (無). The power you covet is merely a beautiful mirage (蜃氣樓) arising from nothingness. Have you never seen what remains after a mirage dissipates? What you do is one scene (씨-ㄴ) in a grand comedy-drama (코메띄-뜨라마). You are comic actors (喜劇俳優). Those who desire the blessings of your empty boasts (헛풍성이) are the spectators (觀察들) intoxicated (醉한) by your performance (演伎). Both the performing actors (演出) and the audience adopt such solemnity (嚴肅性) and attitudes of pressing reality (眞實이 切迫한 態度) while performing and watching, that truly, observing from the side (傍觀) could not be more awkward (未安).

Oh, God! If you brewed absinthe (아뿌산?) laced with chloroform (클로포름) and fed it to these foolish ones, please also prepare a potion from which they can never awaken (永刦히). To make them feel the sorrow (哀愁) of disillusionment (幻滅) upon waking (醒後) is too cruel (過酷). Hahahaha.

When A's melancholia (메란코리아) flared up (發作), his reason became deranged (錯亂) with extreme excitement (興奮). His pale (蒼白한) cheeks showed no trace of blood (血氣), his eyelids were rolled up, and like someone who had fainted (失神), he babbled incoherently, gesturing with hands and feet. When the fit finally subsided (鎭定), he sat up in the grass as if awakened from a nightmare (惡夢), hugging his knees with his arms.

The chilly early winter wind washed over his thin cheeks and passed by, striking the young grass on the opposite hill impartially, making it tremble violently. For a moment, A realized the abnormality (異常) arising from the extreme fatigue (疲勞) of his soul recently. He felt so sorry (未安한) for having abused (虐待) and overworked (酷使) his soul and body like whipping a skinny horse, that he hugged his own chest tightly with his thin arms as if embracing (抱擁) a lover, and pressed his hot lips to his arm. He felt as if his head, arms, and legs were each complaining (怨罔). How should I apologize (謝罪) to my soul and body? For me, in my unfortunate (不遇) circumstances (境), is this the natural course of reason (理의 當然)?

An indescribable, tragic (悲壯한) feeling further stirred (攪亂) his sentimental (感傷的) disposition (性情). Hot tears flowed endlessly from his eyes. I will end my life with this illness. But I have no regrets. This world treats me too coldly (冷待). So how could any affection (愛着心) arise for this world? That's right. Beginning (創始) is immediately the end (終了). A small wave rising on the horizon (水平線上) eventually flutters in the wind of time (타임?) and disappears without leaving a trace (痕跡).

Whether my shadow hides from this worthless, ever-unsettling Earth (地球), falling into the abyss (奈落) thousands, tens of thousands of feet deep within this stratum (地層), or floats (浮遊) in the vast (浩蕩*茫한) blue expanse (蒼穹) where nebulae (星雲) scatter and a hundred million fixed stars (恒星) hang across the great cosmos (大千世界), or goes to the Hell of Scorching Heat (焦熱地獄) <70> to receive the baptism (洗禮) of iron and fire, or enters the magnificent Heaven depicted in the Book of Revelation (黙示錄) to wear a tasseled crown (冕旒冠) and attain eternal life (永生不滅), I do not know. However, the agony of the Hell of Scorching Heat and the happiness of Heaven cause me not a speck of astonishment (驚愕), nor a hair's breadth of joy (喜悅). It's no more than feeling lukewarmness (微溫) from bathing in warm water versus feeling the chill from bathing in cold water. Just as doubt and curiosity about the afterlife do not arise, I possess senses (官能) too dull (痴鈍한) to savor hope and joy regarding life.

Death is zero (零). The eternal return (歸還). Therefore, no consciousness (意識) can be found within death.
The journey (途程) from life to death is like crossing a national border (國境) while on the infinitely long path from birth towards the goal (目標) of nothingness, traversing the grey road called life, then traversing the black road called death.
I am now a pilgrim (巡禮) of nothingness, walking ceaselessly, without looking back, seeking nothingness from nothingness. Will my goal, the holy land (聖地) of nothingness, be purer than the Holy Land of Jerusalem (에루살넴), the blessed land of Canaan (가나안), the Mount of Olives (橄欖山), or under the Bodhi tree on Dante's Mountain (檀特山?)? Or will it be as hideous (醜惡) as Purgatory (煉獄), the valley of Golgotha (골고다의 谿谷), or the caves (土窟) of the Brahmins (波羅門徒)? However, if possible, the holy land of nothingness I desire is not a peaceful (平和), splendid (華麗), serene (安穩) paradise (樂園), but one whose entrance is strewn with thorns (荊蘇) and thistles (蒺蔾), whose vast expanse (廣濶無邊) is crisscrossed (交叉) by spears and swords (槍劒) with flashing blades (刃光), whose ground is scattered (散在) with skeletons (骸骨) grinning fiercely, where poisonous snakes (毒蛇) tens of fathoms long slither, spewing venom (毒氣), a thrilling (壯快한) place with swift winds and thunder (疾風迅雷) that could shatter (擊裂) the pillars of heaven (天柱) and raging torrents (奔流怒濤) that could break (破碎) the earth's axis (地軸)...
And there, my only friend (親舊), Judas (유대), would be sitting yawning, waiting impatiently for me...

Ah, Judas, whom I saw in my dream last night, for some reason, sticks to my mind like a brand, refusing to leave. He, having sold his idol (偶像) Jesus (耶蘇), was instead brutally killed (虐殺), his whole body and face covered in fresh blood (鮮血). Wiping the blood flowing from his body with the feather of a white bird (*鳥), he smeared it thoroughly over my entire body, face, and head, saying, "A, you are still weak. Become stronger and stronger. And believe in me. Let us, you and I, with our soles touching across the horizon, you on Earth, I underground, communicate spiritually until our scales fall off, be utterly faithful to Nothingness and die as martyrs for Nothingness." The echo (餘韻) of Judas's low, serene (景初한? serene/clear?), powerful voice <71> still remains in my ears.

Oh, Judas! Judas!! The blood you smeared all over me last night pierced my skin (皮膚), stained my flesh, ground my bones, and now flows endlessly in my veins (血管), a boiling blood of ochre-colored (赭岱色) rebellion (反逆). Judas is truly the prophet (先智者) of Nothingness. Ah, I want to go see him soon. To the holy land of Nothingness, to the holy land of Nothingness.

A pictured the holy land of Nothingness and the image of Judas in his mind. Oh, how foolish I am. Where is the holy land of Nothingness, and how far must I go to reach it? Nothingness is the nothingness of all things. There are no hypotheses (假說), no definitions (定義), and certainly no interpretations (解釋). Nothingness is absolute (絶對). If I were forced to give a painful explanation, I could say nothing more than "Nothingness is Nothingness." If I say Nothingness is Nothingness, people will call me crazy. But how could a non-crazy person possibly understand the psychology of a crazy person? Humph. Anyway, obtaining the answer that Nothingness is Nothingness is quite refreshing (爽快하다).

Various complex illusions (幻想) spin like machine wheels (機械 박휘) in his head. Aha— A let out a long sigh (嘆息) and exhaled a whitish (힛그스름한) breath.

Ah, solitude (孤獨), solitude, truly desolate (寂寞). Why does spring never visit me? At times like this, if there were a woman (女性) who loved me with all her heart, breathing warm and fragrant breath into this withered chest, how joyful and grateful I would be! A, having reached 21, had never known the opposite sex. When he was healthy, with his pure, Puritan-like heart, he considered never having known the opposite sex a great pride. Since melancholia began to erode his mind, when fear and cold solitude襲來襲來襲來襲來襲來袭来 would襲來袭来来, he would let out the words, "Ah, if only I had a lover..." Especially after the illness flared up, there was a tendency for his sexual desire (愛欲) to intensify (亢進) unbearably.

In the middle of the perfectly clear sky, a pale, weak sliver of the waning moon casts its light onto the opposite hill. Amidst the desolation, a single, nameless wild flower (野生의 꼿) trembles violently, unable to bear the cold. A finds the flower infinitely lovely. You too are a lonely soul blooming alone in a desolate ruin (荒凉한 廢墟). How similar (彷彿한) your situation is to mine, O flower. You are my friend and my lover. If only I could go to where you are, I would cherish you gently, take you into my warm embrace, and thaw you... <72> Then, A remembered the story of the forget-me-not (勿望草) he heard in childhood. A young man, the lover of a girl, tried to pick the beautiful little flower blooming on the banks of the Rhine River (라인江) for his beloved. He slipped, fell into the river, and died. As he floated downstream clutching the flower, the words coming from his mouth were said to be "forget me not" (포아 껫 미 낫). If I too, in this place, had a lover who desired that flower, I would have the heroic (히-로익한) courage to cross this stream to pick that flower, even if it meant drowning in this river. A thought of a lovely young woman he often encountered on his walks, going to and from school. Her slender waist, her short black skirt, and beneath it, her thin, straight legs wrapped in black stockings (스토킹), her high-heeled enamel (에나멜) short boots (短靴) stepping out rhythmically (리씀칼로?) – seeing her feet, he widened his eyes in amazement (驚異) the first time. When A walked along the street, he usually kept his head bowed low, looking as if he were searching for something to pick up. That had become his habit (習慣). He truly disliked seeing the faces of people passing by on the street – faces like corpses (屍身), lifeless expressions (生氣업는), and on top of that, crying faces worn down (찌드른) by human suffering (苦痛). So, he kept his head bowed as much as possible to avoid seeing people's faces. But one day, he wanted to see the face of the unknown maiden he always encountered. Mustering all his courage (勇氣), he lifted his head. Ah! Their eyes met (視線은 모조첫다).

Instantly, her face turned the color of a red radish (紅唐蕪빗). The maiden lowered her head and passed by like a young pigeon (비닭기) passing before a hunter (獵人), more like flying than running. Though it was a brief moment, A, with a gaze like a searchlight (사치라이트?), clearly captured her features in the lens (렌쓰?) of his pupils. He had never felt such joy (歡喜) in his life before. At once, a passion (情熱) that could melt iron and fire coursed through his body. After seeing the maiden, he used the highest superlatives (最上級 比喩) to praise her beauty. Ah, that face – so lovely, not even found among blonde dresses (뿔온드레-쓰?), her white skin, her nose like that of the Greek goddess Venus (비-너스?), those fresh, moist eyes that looked as if dew drops would fall, those eyebrows like a peacock's (孔雀) tail, that plump neck with a curve (카-브?) like a white pigeon's neck, those ears seemingly molded from flour dough, those lips like a dahlia (따리아?) half-opened by a gentle rain, and between them, those teeth gleaming like pearls (眞珠) polished by the waves of the Adriatic Sea (아트리아틱海?). Those two breasts concealing a mystery (神秘) unsolvable for eternity... People of the world, do not judge (評價) beauty before seeing that maiden! I lament (恨) that I cannot yet find <73> appropriate words in our language to praise the maiden.

Ah, if only I could possess (專有) that face, that nose, that eye, that eyebrow, that neck, that ear, that lip, those teeth, those two breasts! No, possession is not right. For me alone to possess that unique, great work of art (藝術品) would be an unforgivable crime (罪惡). Just, if she would strangle me with her hair, blacker than lacquer (漆), I would meet a joyful death in the midst of extreme ecstasy...

A's soul gradually fled to the land of splendid dreams, the realm of subtle and profound beauty (幽婉微妙의 領域). Just then, ding... after a pause... ding, ding, ding—the sound of the bell (鍾소리) from the church (敎會) across the field, blessing the Third Sunday (第3曜日?), pierced (透傳) the chilly winter air, shattering A's beautiful dream. Ugh, annoying! Smacking his lips, A stood up from the grass. He picked up his hat and short stick (短杖) lying in the grass. Young frost glittered like pearls (光彩) on the dilapidated (頹癈한) hat. With a faint smile (微笑) on his desolate lips, he brushed off the hat, placed it on his head, descended the hill, and headed towards the field. The field, frozen in the cold, echoed thinly and sharply with each footstep. Head bowed low, he walked some distance, then lifted his face. There, the Gothic-style (꼬씨크타이푸?) spire (尖塔) of the church tower stood looking down at A with a solemn (儼然한) attitude. He felt as if the knife of passion (팟숀의 메스?) was carving into his chest. He dragged his weak feet to the church door. From inside the hall, the singing voices of the saints (聖徒), filled with holy joy (聖悅) praising the Heavenly Emperor (天帝), formed a magnificent (?) (邯然한?) symphony (交響樂), seeming to pierce the church roof (屋盖) like a melody (旋律), ascend to the celestial realm (天界), and go out to welcome angels descending (降臨) to the lower world. A hesitated (躊躇) several times whether to enter or not, then mustered all his courage (滿身의 勇氣) and went inside the church. By then, the hymn had ended, and the pastor stood on the altar (聖壇), the solemn sound of his prayer (祈願) transmitting the edict (令旨) of the Holy Emperor (聖帝) to the chosen people (擇함을 바든 百姓) of God's kingdom kneeling (跪拜) before him.

A sat leaning against the back wall among them. For some reason, he felt unable to sit alone with his head held high. So, he bowed his head. As the pastor's prayer ended, he instinctively lifted his head along with the congregation's (會衆) "Amen" (아멘). The sounds of coughing (햄 칵 콜록콜록), sniffling – it sounded more heroic than an orchestra (오- 케스트라) performing the "Heaven and Hell" overture. When the assembly finally quieted down, the pastor spread open a long Bible (聖書) on the ancient-looking (古色이 蒼然한) holy table (聖卓) and delivered a sermon (說敎) with a solemn (嚴肅한), even <74> sorrowful (沈痛한) expression. Did his impassioned words, phrase by phrase, receive spiritual inspiration (靈感) as if stamping an indelible seal (不滅의 印) on the hearts of the congregation, God's so-called chosen people (選民)? Only occasional sounds of groaning (呻吟) from within mouths could be heard. A felt both amused and sad. Should he cry or laugh? Like a dazed person (얼빠진 사람), mouth agape, he listened to the pastor's sermon (講道). The pastor's sermon ended. The congregation all stood and sang hymn number 3 (3章讚美歌) together (合唱). Immediately after, the pastor stretched out both arms as if to embrace something and offered the final (最終) prayer. The congregation left, one by one, two by two. Soon, the spacious hall was empty, a cold atmosphere swirling around. A stood stock-still like a rubber doll. He didn't think of leaving, nor did he think of sitting down, just stared fixedly at the altar, not blinking once. After everyone had left, the pastor remained alone, bowing over the altar chair (倚子), offering some solitary prayer. After praying, the pastor came down from the altar and approached A. He extended his hand, requesting a handshake (握手). A stared at the pastor's outstretched hand for a moment, then reluctantly offered his own hand, placing it on the pastor's. The pastor's warm hand grasped A's hand, cold as ice (어름갓치), and shook it firmly two or three times. Instantly, all the warm blood (血) in A's body rushed to his head. The pastor spoke in a composed tone, "Mr. A, I knew you would come here. I prayed to God every single day for you to come here. Indeed, even if Mr. A abandoned God, the great and loving God did not abandon Mr. A and guided you here this evening. This too is just one aspect of God's grace (恩惠)." A lifted his head sharply and stared intently into the pastor's face. His eyes turned bloodshot, changing into a tragic figure like a wounded lion. The crafty (老狡한) pastor, experienced from years of appearing on such battlefields (戰場) amidst cannon fire and smoke (砲彈烟雨), instantly changed (一變) his attitude (態度) and used gentle (柔順한) words to hypnotize (催眠) him. "Mr. A, I am not forcing you to believe in Jesus (耶蘇). To be frank, Mr. A, although your age (年齒) is not yet great, there is no one who does not admire (嘆服) your learning (學識) and character (品格). I know that now, due to physical suffering (肉身의 苦患), many things in this world seem tasteless to you. That is because you have not attained the fullness of the soul (靈魂). Therefore, if you occasionally come visit the church to calm your spirit and cultivate yourself (修養), I believe it will be very beneficial (有益) for your body, Mr. A. Consequently, you will eventually come to know well how great God's grace is."

A's pale face flushed in an instant (瞥眼間), and words tumbled out so quickly his voice stammered, "God's grace? <75> In my twenty-one years of life, I have never received even a speck (亳末) of God's grace, nor do I even know if grace truly exists."
The pastor chuckled "Heh heh," forcing laughter that wasn't funny, repeatedly, "Well, you have to believe in Jesus to know God's grace and understand how great it is. So, anyway, believers are truly fortunate (幸福)."
A suddenly shouted loudly, "Yes! Great fortune! Having moths (좀?) put into their souls, eating them away until they rot, yet remaining ignorant – that's fortune! If they knew, what greater suffering could there be?"

The pastor looked at A's face for a while. His face showed a complex expression, mixed with pity (憐憫) for A on one hand, and contempt (蔑視) on the other. After a moment, he opened his mouth, "Mr. A, your illness (病患) is quite severe (甚하시오), isn't it?" and placed his hand on A's shoulder (억게).

A felt the weight of that hand pressing down (壓迫) on him, heavier than a ten-geun iron hammer (鐵槌). He lifted his head and looked at the pastor's face. The face, with its yellowish, thick (肥厚한) skin puffy with fat (脂肪), looked just like a leftover breadcrumb (麵麭쪽아리). The pastor, his already unpleasant face contorted with wrinkles on both cheeks (兩頰) and between his eyebrows (眉間), opened his wide mouth limitlessly and produced a hollow laugh, "Heh heh heh." Each time he laughed, the yellowish breath from his mouth shot unhesitatingly (躊躇업시) onto A's face, standing right in front. The unpleasant breath suffocated his head. Like someone drunk on bad liquor (惡酒), he dragged his numb (痲痺) legs to the church door and leaned his heavy head and body against a sturdy pillar (圓柱). The pastor followed behind like a debt collector (債鬼) and stood behind A, still breathing his lukewarm breath onto A's thin, white neck. Inside the quiet church hall, the faint, decaying light (衰殘한 빗) of the lamp (람프) radiated (幅射) onto the two silently standing figures.

"Look here, Mr. A. Truly, thinking of Mr. A, or those in situations like Mr. A's, I cannot help but shed tears and pray that you receive God's grace quickly."
A, with a trembling voice, "The tears you shed for me, first shed one drop for yourself. Receiving grace is receiving equivalent misfortune (禍); do you not know that charity (慈善) might be a mountain of accumulated evil (積惡)? He who is faithful to himself rather than giving grace to others <76> is the cleverest (怜悧한) person. Not giving others so-called grace is the way (세음?) to not inflict harm (害毒). In that respect, I believe in Judas. Judas, whom you call a heretic (異端者), Satan (사단?), a devil (惡魔), a lump of sin (罪惡덩어리), was truly one who possessed perfect humanity. Since one who possesses perfect humanity is God, Judas is my sole object of praise (禮讚의 標的)!" he cried out, forcing his voice, nearing a scream (悲鳴).

Hearing the words worshipping (祟拜) Judas, the pastor stood stock-still, as if struck by an unexpected blow (打擊), like an electric current (電流) passing through a conductor (導軆), stunned, his tongue seemingly hardened (硬化), unable to speak.

A felt his limbs (四肢) go numb as if injected with cocaine (코카인 注射). A fire-like rage (癪) boiling up from his chest reached his throat (목줄기), and the sound of saliva, turned acidic (엑쓰?), gurgling in his pharynx (咽喉) could be heard. He felt unbearably suffocated (窒息). Gathering all the strength in his body into his left arm (左便팔), he pounded hard on the classroom door as if to break it. The door opened slightly with a dull (魯鈍한) echo. A mass of cold wind rushed in violently, striking A's face, hot as if melting in fire. Ah, the refreshing taste at that moment was like an opium addict, suffering terribly from withdrawal, finally getting a dose; only then did his stagnant blood seem to circulate again.

A stepped out of the church door. In the cobalt blue (코빨트빗) sky, countless gold and silver stars were scattered. Among them, three red stars in a seemingly straight line constellation (星座) looked down at him, blinking as if mocking (嘲弄) him – "You foolish weakling! Why are you making such a fuss about living in this world?" – then gleaming again, hiding and reappearing. A found even those lovely stars hateful. "You lightning-struck (霹靂) stars! You too are my enemies (怨讎)!" he shouted. Do stars get struck by lightning too?

He clenched both hands tightly, his limbs trembling violently. Closing his eyes, he ran towards the wide field. I will live. I will not die. I will live for a thousand years, ten thousand years, billions of years, grinding all phenomena of this world to dust (粉碎), tearing down even the iron fences (鐵銷) of Hell's enclosure, scattering even the dust under Heaven's threshold, and only then will I die. Until then, I absolutely will not die. Even if the devil comes and stabs me with a spear, making ink-black blood spurt, tearing my flesh, grinding my bones, I have the shield (防牌) of the soul, impervious to spears and cannonballs. He cursed (呪咀) this world, Heaven, and Hell to the extreme, <77> running on like a sleepwalker (夢遊病者), ceaselessly. Nothing appeared in his eyes. All phenomena seemed merely like plain water (淡淡한 물). This world is quite clever too. Seeing that I intend to annihilate them, it quickly changes form (變形) out of fear. Hah! Weaklings! Hahahaha! he laughed, running continuously. After running some distance, he tripped over something and fell flat. He had hit a small rock in the middle of the grassy field. In that instant (刹那), a small bird, shivering pitifully without a nest on the rock, barely asleep, startled by the sudden attack (不意襲擊), thinking itself persecuted (迫害), flew away in terror (驚㥘). A felt such a pang in his heart, regretting deeply having done something he shouldn't have. He reached out and embraced the rock. On the cold rock, there was a spot of white bird droppings (새똥). He pressed his cheek to that spot. When the faint warmth (溫氣), seemingly risen from bird feathers, touched his cheek, A felt an indescribably subtle (微妙한) sensation. Oh, rock! You are truly a saint. Standing aloof (超然히) without interfering (干涉치) in this world, dirty as a sewer, even if all creatures (김생?) defecate on your head or peck sharply with pointed beaks, you show no sign of displeasure, remaining sublimely (숭고히) fixed – truly the demeanor of a saint. These worldly fools kneel (跪拜) before stinking blood and flesh, but are ignorant even of the existence of something as pure (淸淨) and sublime (崇嚴한) as you.

A used the rock to support himself and barely got his weak legs up, staggering as he walked. His head felt empty of all consciousness (意識), just filled with a hazy (矇矓히), indescribable smoke (烟氣), causing a splitting headache (頭痛). Concepts (觀念) vanished, memory (記憶) disappeared, images (映像) registered by the eyes ceased, only the heart's beat (心臟의 鼓動) continued mechanically (機械的). He mustered all his remaining strength (젓먹은 힘) and shouted. But it didn't reach his ears. Was no sound coming out? Or were his ears unable to hear? His legs felt numb like withered radishes, and before his eyes, it became pitch black as if inside a dark cave (暗窟). A collapsed on the spot (잡바젓다).

After lying still for some time, he suddenly let out a scream like thunder (霹靂) and tried moving his hands and feet. But now his limbs were free, and his eyes opened. He looked up at the sky. The sky he saw then was not the star-studded sky he had seen before, but appeared as an infinitely vast ocean. Upon that ocean, the large character '無' (Nothingness) was written sprawled across it. A moment later, large waves (波濤) arose and washed away the character '無'. A's facial muscles (筋肉) twitched as he let out a dry, cackling laugh like that of a damned soul in hell (地獄亡者), "Hee hee hee hee." The withered (枯渴한) laughter vibrated (振動) the nearby air. Ahaha! The summons (催促) has come from the holy land of Nothingness. Where shall I go? <78> Saying this, he sprang up like a rubber ball (고무뽈), staggered (비틀 거름), singing like a song, "Let's go, let's go, quickly, quickly, to the land of Nothingness, to the holy land of Nothingness?" He ran towards the hill where he had lain three hours earlier.

The deep valley (谿谷) between the hills was dark (暗黑) as if ink had been spilled, yet to his eyes, it seemed bright as if lit by ten thousand lamps (萬燭燈). Looking down into its depths, "Oh, bright! Clear! Beautiful! Is that the Dragon Palace (龍宮)? The Moon Palace (月宮殿)? They're calling me from there. What will I do if I go? What? Their king wants to have a dialogue about Nothingness with me? Okay, then let's go. Hahahaha!" he babbled incoherently. "Oh, have you brought the jade palanquin (玉轎)? Then I'll ride it." Extending his hands, he swayed his body back and forth towards the depths below, intending to jump, but then suddenly fell backward flat on his back. Foaming at the mouth like crab froth, he muttered to himself for some time. Finally, his energy exhausted, he lay still.

On the wide plain (廣原), open front and back, fierce whirlwinds (旋風) rushed about, whistling (휫바람), coalescing here and there, then striking A's face as they passed.

At that moment, regaining some of his senses (本精神), he looked up at the sky and saw the stars twinkling, however faintly (熹微하나마).
The premonition (豫感) of death (死) dominated (支配) his mind. He let out a long sigh (長歎息). Death, Death, Death! I am dying. Ah, from now on, I won't be able to see the sun setting or the moon rising. Life bids me farewell (作別)! It is relieving yet regrettable. If I must die anyway in this world, I will die with the valiant attitude of an iron knight (鐵騎士) going into battle. Death! Death itself is the expression (表現) of Nothingness. And death is the crystallization (結晶) of the art (藝術) I have cherished in my heart for twenty-one years. I will substitute my corpse (屍軆) for an immortal masterpiece (不杇의 傑作) in this world.

He stood up and looked down into the deep valley. That gloomy (陰幽한) valley looked like a triumphal arch (凱旋門). As if he were a victorious general (凱旋將軍) looking up at the triumphal arch erected for him before entering, feeling the joy (歡喜) of victory (勝利), A felt the sorrow (悲哀) of victory as he stood looking down at the triumphal arch of Death.
Even if I die, there will be no one to rejoice, nor anyone to grieve. Such is a human life. Rather than dying unconsciously when my illness flares up, I will consciously embrace and praise Death, and depart on the path of Death. <79>

He closed his eyes (冥目), raised both hands high towards the sky, his lips trembling faintly, murmuring "Death, Death, Death." But on his face appeared a genuine purity (眞純한) unseen before, an inviolable, solemn (嚴肅한) expression.

The next morning, A's corpse (屍軆) was retrieved by the villagers' hands, brought up onto the hill, and laid out. The furrowed brow (찡그린 주름) on the water-bloated corpse seemed to bear the eternal (久遠) imprint (刻印) of life's suffering.
The love (愛) of Christ (基督), the compassion (慈悲) of Shakyamuni (釋迦), said to be stronger than the summer sun's rays and to shine everywhere, failed to illuminate even A's small body, being a non-conductor (不導軆).
A's sorrowful (哀처러운) soul, which ended its life in this world like a flickering lamp (燈火), harboring great hope and unending resentment (恨) – where is it wandering (彷徨) now?
His soul will pierce through the water where he drowned, dig through the earth, break through the Earth's crust (地殼), and run forward, forward, ceaselessly.

At the edge of the field, a grave mound, fresh with the scent of earth, stands alone. The sun casts a yellowish amber (琥珀色) light upon the wide plain (廣原). Sparrows perched on tree branches sing the sad song (悲歌) of sunset (日沒) and fly off fluttering towards their respective nests (보금자리). Before the grave (墓), a handful of white daffodils (白水仙花), not yet wilted, is placed. Unable to withstand the north wind sweeping the field, they bow their heads towards the grave, seemingly mourning (哀吊) the lonely soul of A lying underground.
Did A know now... that the owner who placed those flowers was the lovely maiden he encountered on the street that day... the beloved daughter of the pastor from the church across the field, where he suffered on the night he abandoned this world? (End)

**Summary**

This bleak story follows A, a 21-year-old suffering from severe melancholia and existential despair. Feeling alienated from society and disillusioned with life, religion, and human pursuits like wealth, power, and even conventional morality, A wanders through nature, contemplating emptiness and death. He finds a strange kinship with Judas Iscariot, viewing him as a prophet of "Nothingness" and perfect humanity, contrasting sharply with the hypocrisy he perceives in Christianity, represented by a condescending pastor. His mental state deteriorates, marked by hallucinations and intense emotional swings, culminating in his suicide by drowning in a valley stream. The story ends on a desolate note, questioning where his tormented soul wanders and mentioning that the pastor's daughter, a girl A admired, later places flowers on his lonely grave.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this story is deeply revealing. A's profound sense of alienation, nihilism, and critique of established structures (including Western religion) can be interpreted as reflecting the despair and powerlessness felt by some Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule. His embrace of the "heretic" Judas and the concept of "Nothingness" signifies a radical rejection of both traditional values and imposed modern ones, searching for meaning in extreme negation. Historians might find the intense psychological portrayal of suffering, the critique of religious institutions (potentially seen as collaborators or ineffective), and the overall tone of hopelessness significant indicators of the intellectual and emotional climate among certain segments of Korean society during the 1920s.

돌아간 異端者
						吳英輝
A는 今年 21歲의 靑年이다.
