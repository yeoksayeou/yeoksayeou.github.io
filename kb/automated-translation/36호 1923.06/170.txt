**Body Text Translation:**
If I Cross That Water
						O Ch'ŏnwŏn

Ah, upon the rippling water, upon the water,
Far away, the fading evening fire pours down,
From the fisherman's boat on the rapids, the thin evening smoke rises,
In the forest of Wŏlmi Island, the pale gray evening wanders.

Ah, whenever I see that water, whenever I see it,
This burning heart,
If I cross that water, flowing south,
There lies an unseen land of different culture and customs.

There, my beloved, for me,
Would kneel in the Holy Church (聖堂) offering prayers,
Waiting with an anxious heart,
Ah, but where is the land I tread?
Only the heedless seagulls fly over the West Sea (西海).

Ah, whenever I see that water, whenever I see it <97>
The memory of vomiting black blood surges up,
It must have been the early summer of July last year,
In P'yŏngyang (平壤域), where green shade (綠蔭) sprouts on Ŭlmil Terrace (乙密臺) and Moran Peak (牧丹峰).

Deep in the night, under the still stars,
Burying my head in your warm breast,
Though I wept and pleaded I could not go,
With eyes like stones baked all day in the sun (볏),
You only gazed into my heart,
Ah, how many times did I want to bite you!

Ah, the night draws near, the darkness deepens,
The drizzling wind sighs in the tree leaves,
Stars are in the sky, lighthouses are on the water,
From the train station, occasionally, *choo-choo, pa-pa*,
The train, suffering the toil (苦役) of civilization (文明), cries.

Ah, until when will you torment me, my love?
Until when must I dream of future happiness and be content?
Ah, I weep, I weep, looking at that water,
The night has deepened—. (1921. 7. 6) <98>

Raging waves (狂濤) crash,
Ceaselessly, upon the rapids of the small, isolated island (孤島),
The rocks (도래 - likely 돌은) break apart, the cliffs wear down,
Still, the island silently sheds only tears.

Ah, day after day, the morning sun rises,
The evening sun sets, without rest,
Thus, a person's youth (靑春) fades away.
One by one, hairs turn white.
Still, people laugh and play. (1921. 6. 14)

Tears
As the evening sun sets into dusk,
A young maiden by the well,
Gently, gently, fills her jar with water, yet
Struggles, unable to manage it alone.
Quietly helping her lift it upright,
As I watch the retreating figure
Of the maiden, staggering precariously,
In my eyes, unknowable tears well up. (1921. 6. 14) <99>

In Room 7 of C Hospital (C病院), on a night filled
Only with melancholy (憂鬱), solitude (孤寂), and groans (呻吟),
Hearing the footsteps of innocent "Yŏngsun" (英順), who talked of the joy
Of the excursion (遠足會) picnic planned for the dawning day,
Echoing down the hall as she leaves late at night,
Together with the cry of the cuckoo (杜鵑) from the back mountain,
Fading away faintly (알는 알는 스러저감),
Tears trace down my emaciated cheek—. (1921. 6. 15) <100>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by O Ch'ŏnwŏn expresses deep sorrow, longing, and despair. The speaker reflects on separation from a loved one, possibly across the water to the "South," contrasting the present suffering with memories of P'yŏngyang and imagining the loved one praying. Themes include the relentless passage of time eroding youth, the pain of memory (vomiting blood), feelings of helplessness observing others' struggles (the maiden at the well), and personal suffering juxtaposed with others' innocence (in the hospital). For historians, the poems offer a glimpse into personal anguish during the colonial period (1923). The specific locations (Wŏlmi Island, P'yŏngyang, West Sea), the yearning for a different place ("South," "Unseen Land"), the sense of confinement and despair, and the imagery of suffering could reflect the broader socio-political atmosphere and personal hardships faced by Koreans at the time, even if not explicitly political.

저물을 건느면
						吳天園
아아 출넝거리는 물 우에 물 우에,
저 멀리 저므는 저녁 불은 붓는다,
물여울 漁夫의 배에서 가늘게 오르는 저녁 煙氣,
月尾島 숩 속에 열븐 灰色 저녁은 떠돈다.

아아 저 물을 볼 때마다 볼 때마다,
애 타는 이 가슴,
저 물을 활 활 南으로 건느면
거긔는 人文과 風土 다른 未見의 땅.

거긔서 님은 나를 위해,
聖堂에 무릅 꿀코 긔도 올리며,
조리는 마음으로 기다리련만,
아아 그러나 나의 밟은 땅은 어대런고
속절 모르는 갈매기만은 西海에 날너라.

아아 저 물을 볼 때마다 볼 때마다＜97＞
소사 오르는 검은 吐血의 追億,
지나간 해의 7月이란 첫녀름이리라
乙密臺 牧丹峰에 綠蔭돗는 平壤域.

밤 깁허 고진악한 별 밋헤서
님의 다사한 가슴에 머리를 팟 뭇고,
울면서 못 가리라 哀願하여도
終日 볏에 쬐인 돌괴가튼 눈으로
오직 나의 가슴 바라보기만 할 때에,
아아 나는 몃 번이나 님을 물어틋고 십헛슬고!

아아 밤은 갓가워 온다 어두움은 진해온다
보슬 바람은 나무입에 한솜 쉬인다
별은 한울에 燈臺는 물 우에,
停車場에서는 때때로 푸파푸파,
文明에 苦役하는 汽車가 운다.

아아 님은 언제까지나 나를 속태이랴는가,
나는 언제까지나 未來의 幸福을 그리며 滿足하여야 할까,
아아 나는 운다 나는 운다 저 물을 바라보고
밤은 깁헛는데―. (1921. 7. 6) ＜98＞

狂濤는 부드친다,
조고만 孤島여읅에 쉬임도 업시,
도래는 헤여진다, 바위는 달는다,
그래도 섬은 말 업시 눈물만 흘린다.

아아 날마다 날마다 아츰해는 뜬다
저녁해는 저믄다 쉬임도 업시,
이리하야 사람의 靑春은 꺼진다.
하나 둘 머리털은 희여저 간다.
그래도 사람은 우슴 우스며 노리한다. (1921. 6. 14)

눈물

뒷모양 그리워서 보고 잇는 나의 눈에
