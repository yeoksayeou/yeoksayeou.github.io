잡지명	개벽 제50호
발행일	1924년 08월 01일
기사제목	끌리는 농부의 무리여 (Oh, the Dragged Throngs of Farmers)
필자	乭伊
기사형태	시 (Poem)

끌리는 농부의 무리여
						乭伊
아아 끌리는 농부의 무리어
너희는 양복 닙은 자에게 끌리어
서울 長安을 이리로 저리로 끌리도다
무엇에 놀란듯한 얼빠진 듯한 눈으로
비틀비틀 끌려 다님을 볼때에
아아 나는 너희를 위해 눈물 지노라

Ah, the dragged throngs of farmers,
You are dragged by those wearing Western suits,
Dragged here and there through Seoul Changan (長安).
With eyes as if startled, as if bewildered,
Seeing you stumble along as you are dragged,
Ah, I shed tears for you.

아아 끌리는 농부의 무리어
너희의 왕국인 벌판에서
광이를 힘껏 들엇다 노흐며
西山이 문허져라고 소리치던 豪氣,
그 自由, 그 主翁的 태도는 어이 하엿나.
서울 악동들의 비웃는 소리에
눈이 둥글어 끌려를 다니는고
아아 나는 너희를 위해 눈물 지노라

Ah, the dragged throngs of farmers,
In the fields that are your kingdom,
Where you lifted and dropped the hoe with all your might,
Your heroic spirit (豪氣) that shouted loud enough to make the West Mountain (西山) crumble,
What has become of that freedom, that master-like (主翁的) bearing?
Do you now wander dragged about, wide-eyed,
At the mocking sounds of Seoul's wicked youths?
Ah, I shed tears for you.

아아 끌리는 농부의 무리어＜7＞
너희는 무엇을 보러 서울에 왓던고
너희의 膏血을 빠는 무리의 安樂이 기름이 흐르는 살이더냐
너희의 땀으로 된 양식과
너희 안해들의 피로 된 필육이
어듸로 간데를 차자옴일러냐
무엇을 찻는듯한 너희 눈이어
나는 너희를 위해 눈물지노라

Ah, the dragged throngs of farmers,<7>
What did you come to Seoul to see?
Was it the comfort (安樂) and the fat flesh dripping with oil of the throng that sucks your lifeblood (膏血)?
Was it to seek where the food (糧食) made from your sweat (汗)
And the cloth (匹肉) made from your wives' blood have gone?
Oh, your eyes that seem to be searching for something,
I shed tears for you.

아아 양복쟁이에게 끌리는 농부의 무리어
제발 그의 뒤를 따르지 말라
네의 순박한 정직한 천성으로
저 손흰 무리의 감언에 속지도 말고
「너는 나를 따르라」하고 그가 호령할 때에
「도적놈아 물러가라!」하고 웨치라
갓을 빗두루 쓴 농부들이어
天下는 너희의 것이니라-

Ah, throngs of farmers dragged by the suit-wearers,
Please do not follow him.
With your simple, honest nature,
Do not be deceived by the sweet words (感言) of that white-handed throng.
When he commands (號令), saying, "You follow me!"
Shout, "Thief (盜賊놈), get back!"
Oh, farmers wearing your gat tilted,
The world (天下) is yours!

아아 서울의 큰 거리로 끌리는 농부여
어서 집을 가라, 벌로 돌아가라
南大門을 나설 때에 발에서도 서울의 먼지를 떨어 버리라
형제여! 그것은 怨讎의 것이니라. 죄악의 것이니라,
형제여 넓은 들애 모혀 그 크다란 발들로 힘껏 땅을 굴르라
땅이 큰 소리를 찌르고 떨어 세계의 不義를 흔들어 버리게 하라
아아 세계의 창조자요 양육자요 유지자요 주인인 농부여＜8＞

Ah, farmers dragged through the grand streets of Seoul,
Hurry, go home, return to the fields.
When you pass through Namdaemun (南大門), shake even the dust of Seoul from your feet.
Brothers (兄弟)! It belongs to the enemy (怨讎); it belongs to sin (罪惡).
Brothers, gather in the wide fields and stomp the earth hard with your huge feet.
Let the earth cry out with a great sound and shake off the world's injustice (不義).
Ah, farmers, creators (創造者), nurturers (養育者), sustainers (維持者), and masters (主人) of the world!<8>

**Summary**

This poem by "Tol-i" laments the plight of farmers who are depicted as being lured or "dragged" into Seoul by figures in Western suits, losing their dignity and strength in the bewildering urban environment. The poet contrasts their former "master-like" freedom in the fields with their current exploited state, observing that the city's comforts are built on their suffering ("lifeblood," "sweat," "wives' blood"). The poem urges the farmers to reject the deceptive "white-handed throng," return to their rural roots, and recognize their own power as the true creators and masters of the world, calling on them to metaphorically stomp the ground and shake off global injustice. From a colonial history perspective, the poem is significant as it captures the social dislocation and exploitation faced by the rural population during a period of modernization and colonial rule. It highlights the emerging class consciousness and critiques the urban elite (potentially collaborators or beneficiaries of the colonial system, symbolized by "Western suits" and "white hands"), framing the farmers not just as victims but as the rightful owners of the nation ("The world is yours!") and agents of potential revolutionary change, reflecting the nationalist and potentially socialist sentiments prevalent in *Gaebyeok* magazine.
