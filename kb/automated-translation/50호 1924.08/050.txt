Article Title:	問題의 時代日報, 紛料의 顚末과 社會輿論 (The Problematic Sidae Ilbo, the Full Story of the Dispute and Public Opinion)
Author:	一記者
Article Type:	논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

The Problematic Sidae Ilbo, the Full Story of the Dispute and Public Opinion
						By One Reporter

The problematic Sidae Ilbo, launched on March 31st and suspended on July 10th after publishing a total of 102 issues—what twists and turns, what tempests occurred during this interval? People have frowned endlessly trying to understand this, and people have worried more intensely about this than about a sick only child (*獨子*). Now that the affair (*事端*) seems to have reached a resolution, one way or another, I intend to lay out the full story (*顚末*) to inform public opinion (*社會輿論*) and, at the same time, fire an arrow (*一矢*) at the gentlemen responsible for the dispute (*紛料*) at the said company.

The Founding of the Sidae Ilbo and Our Expectations

If, like some stubborn old bumpkin (*頑固老氓*), one were to say, "Whether I sell or tear apart the child I bore, what business is it of yours?", we would have nothing more to say to such an inhuman old ghost (*老鬼*). Likewise, if Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun were to say, "Whether we sell or kill off the Sidae Ilbo we bore, what business is it of yours?", we would also feel no need to utter a single word, this way or that, to such base fellows (*鄙夫鄙夫*). However, as they initially promised society, the Sidae Ilbo was not the private property of any individual but a public instrument (*公器*) and a clear mirror (*明鏡*) for society. For this, we were grateful for their sincerity, and society as a whole recognized the Sidae Ilbo as a public instrument. Needless verbosity aside, how much effort went into conceiving (*胚胎*) this media organ called the Sidae Ilbo in the desolate Korean society, and through how many procedures (*手續*) was its birth managed? What glory and pride its birth was for Korea (*朝鮮*)! Although it was the effort of a few individuals, looking at their background<30>, wasn't it the collective achievement (*攻勞*) of all Korea? Moreover, wasn't the person directly responsible for its birth none other than Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, who was trusted not just as a Korean pioneer but even as a national patriot (*國士*) of Korea? In this respect, while expressing our gratitude to Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, the direct laborer in the birth of the Sidae Ilbo, all 20 million people together clasped hands and warmly received the Sidae Ilbo, thinking, "Ah! Our society has gained another such valuable public instrument!"

Thus, while quietly wishing for the health (more spiritual than physical) of the mother, Mr. Ch'oe, and secretly praying for the growth of the newborn, the Sidae Ilbo, we felt increasing joy as its healthy form (*健姿*) roamed freely day by day, month by month.

Just as things were going like this, just as the entire society held such expectations and hopes, before even half a year had passed, rumors began to spread—this corner was amiss, that corner was askew. Now, after 102 issues and less than four months, amidst clouds of suspicion (*疑雲萬丈*) thick with right and wrong (*시시비비*), talk of Poch'ŏn'gyo (*普天敎*) and Sin Sŏk-u (*申錫雨*), selling and transferring, ultimately led to the declaration of a temporary suspension by a group of thieves (*훔치의 무리*) we never dreamed of.

"Heaven's time (*天時*) is hard to predict; human affairs (*人事*) are uncertain (*無常*)," they say. Where else could such uncertainty exist? Is it really possible that the voice of 20 million people, the public instrument and clear mirror of the entire society, could vanish completely into such dark clouds (*黑雲萬丈*)? Must we once again leave behind the sneer (*鼻笑*), "That's just how things done by Koreans (*朝鮮놈*) turn out"? There's a limit to being dumbfounded. This is likely the first time experiencing such a complete blockage from below to above.

There's no need to repeat talk like touching the ear of a dead baby or praising the conduct of a cow (*牛*) already sold off. (Whether Poch'ŏn'gyo manages it or not is a separate issue. There's no law saying Poch'ŏn'gyo cannot run a newspaper.) For the sake of warning about our society's future (*來頭*), taking the overturned front cart (*前車*) as a lesson (*戒*) for the rear cart (*後車*), let us at least know the full story of how things came to this. Also, since the path to revival is not entirely non-existent, let's submit it to public opinion.

How the Sidae Ilbo's Publishing Rights Passed to Poch'ŏn'gyo

As the world commonly knows (*共知*), the Sidae Ilbo was applied for to the authorities during the time of the previous weekly, Tongmyŏng (*東明*), by Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun, who were executives (*幹部*) managing Tongmyŏng. Whether due to the efforts of the two gentlemen or the temporary goodwill of the authorities, fortunately, the Sidae Ilbo was permitted last year under Chin Hak-mun's name. Once permission was granted, publication needed to start immediately, but since our society was fundamentally poor (*貧弱*), the issue of operating funds was a bigger problem than anything else.<31> Running east and west (*東奔西走*), managing and planning (*經之營之*), they tried tirelessly, but ultimately failed to secure the flow of capital (*金力*). Thus, under the name "Sidae Ilbo Co., Ltd.," they organized a promoters' meeting, decided to raise 400,000 won in capital with 20,000 total shares (20 won per share), and while soliciting share payments (*株金*) on one hand, they obtained some founding expenses (*創立費*) on the other, managing to publish the inaugural issue at the end of February this year.

Though they somehow managed to launch the paper by scraping things together, the capital income (*資金收入*) did not meet expectations (*所期*). Consequently, due to financial difficulties, various opinions reportedly emerged among the executives.

1. The Relationship between the So-called 4-Person Contract and the Promoters' Meeting
Putting aside the operating funds issue for a moment, early in the founding phase, there was a so-called 4-person contract regarding the ownership of publishing rights. Though the exact meaning is unknown, it is said there was a written contract among four individuals—Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, Chin Hak-mun, Sŏ Sang-hyŏn (*徐相灦*), and Min Pyŏng-dŏk (*閔丙德*)—stating that they would jointly preserve the publishing rights of the Sidae Ilbo. Also (it's unclear if this was before or after the 4-person contract), Ch'oe Nam-sŏn is said to have declared at a promoters' meeting, addressing all the promoters, that the publishing rights of the Sidae Ilbo were bestowed upon the promoters (*發起人 諸氏*). At this time, the promoters, as a sign of gratitude (*意*) to Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, reportedly agreed to cover (*報債*) 10,000 won of debt from Tongmyŏngsa (*東明社*), which Ch'oe Nam-sŏn had previously managed.

In this way, the publishing rights already began to be treated like a commodity (*商品*), put up for sale (*出品*) here and there according to financial power (*金力*). However, that thing called money still did not come in easily (*容易*). Management became increasingly difficult.

2. The Full Story of the Connection with Poch'ŏn'gyo
The faction of Sŏ Sang-hyŏn and Yi Tŭng-nyŏn (*李得年*), who were promoters and directors (*理事*), had initially become directors by pledging to acquire 3,000 shares, but they had not yet paid for them. As expenses became difficult, the company pressed Sŏ and Yi for payment, but Sŏ and Yi procrastinated (*차일피일*) and evaded (*延避*), while secretly plotting something. Unbeknownst to the entire staff, they connected with Poch'ŏn'gyo, ultimately creating the mess we see today.

It seems Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn originally had close ties (*親分*) with Poch'ŏn'gyo members Ko Yong-hwan (*高龍煥*), Yi Chong-ik (*李鍾翊*), and Im Kyŏng-ho (*林敬鎬*). From the time the Sidae Ilbo was born, Yi Tŭng-nyŏn reportedly introduced the prospect of Poch'ŏn'gyo investment (*出資*) to Ch'oe Nam-sŏn. However, Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, not yet cornered (*窮境*), is said to have refused, deeming a connection with the controversial Poch'ŏn'gyo inauspicious (*祥瑞*롭지 못하다). Nevertheless, the two, Sŏ and Yi, secretly welcomed Poch'ŏn'gyo's investment and continued their maneuvering internally. Eventually, as the company's management grew desperate and Ch'oe Nam-sŏn faced difficulties, <32> Sŏ and Yi seized the opportunity. Using the sweet talk (*甘言*) of a 100,000 won investment for 5,000 shares, they brought Poch'ŏn'gyo and Ch'oe Nam-sŏn/Chin Hak-mun together.

Specifically, as a result of a meeting between Sidae Ilbo director Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Yi Chong-ik, the head of Poch'ŏn'gyo's Kyŏngsŏng (*京城*) Chinjŏngwŏn (*眞正院*), Yi Chong-ik conveyed this intention (*此意*) to Yi Sang-ho (*李祥昊*), one of the executives at the Poch'ŏn'gyo headquarters (*本部*) in Chŏngŭp (*井邑*). Yi Sang-ho responded to Yi Chong-ik expressing approval (*此意*를 찬동한다). Consequently, both sides held preliminary negotiations (*豫備交涉*) at Taejŏn Station (*太田*驛) (May 22nd). Representing the Sidae side were Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn, and representing the Poch'ŏn'gyo side were Yi Sang-ho, Yi Tal-ho (*李達濠*), and Im Kyŏng-ho (*林敬鎬*). They agreed that Poch'ŏn'gyo would acquire 5,000 shares (*株券*) of Sidae Ilbo.

Following these preliminary negotiations, Yi and Sŏ secretly reported this intention (*此意*) to Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and tempted him with the 100,000 won. Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, originally financially strained both publicly and privately (*公私間*), hesitated (*躊躇*) but could not resist.

Finally, on May 30th, both parties gathered at Yŏnghŭnggwan (*永興館*) in Kyŏngsŏng and, under the noble pretext (*美名*) of social work (*社會事業*), made a pact (*言約*) for the transfer of 5,000 shares.

While the Poch'ŏn'gyo side claimed to contribute to social work in this manner, and the Ch'oe Nam-sŏn side agreed to accept this goodwill (*好意*), Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn hatched a separate scheme (*別計*). On the 31st, at Yi Tŭng-nyŏn's house, Sŏ Sang-hyŏn, Yi Tŭng-nyŏn, Yi Sang-ho, Yi Tal-ho, and Im Kyŏng-ho held a secret meeting (*祕會*) and drafted a so-called secret contract (*秘密契約*). On June 2nd, three representatives from the Sidae side—Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, Yi Tŭng-nyŏn, Sŏ Sang-hyŏn—and three from the Poch'ŏn'gyo side—Yi Sang-ho, Yi Tal-ho, Im Kyŏng-ho—met and formally signed the contract. This was the crucial point (*核点*) that decided the fate of the Sidae Ilbo. (At this time, the Poch'ŏn'gyo side agreed to pay 20,000 won to Party B (*乙*) first.) The full text of the contract is as follows:

Contract

Kim Hong-gyu (*金洪圭*), Sŏ Sang-hyŏn (*徐相灦*), Mun Chŏng-sam (*文正三*), Yi Tŭng-nyŏn (*李得年*), Yi Sang-ho (*李祥昊*), Yi Tal-ho (*李達濠*), Yi Wŏn-yŏng (*李源永*) – these 7 persons shall be referred to as Party A (*甲*).
Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (*崔南善*), Chin Hak-mun (*秦學文*) – these 2 persons shall be referred to as Party B (*乙*).
The following contract terms are concluded:
1. Party B transfers the rights of editor and publisher of the Sidae Ilbo, authorized by the Government-General of Korea (*朝鮮總督府*), to Party A, and Party A accepts (*讓受*) this transfer.
1. Simultaneously with accepting (*讓受*) the aforementioned rights, Party A shall pay Party B the sum of Ten Thousand Won (*金壹萬圓*) as compensation (*補償*) for the debts of Tongmyŏngsa (*東明社*), the predecessor (*前身*) of the said Sidae Ilbo, managed by Party B in the previous year.
1. Regarding the transfer of the editor and publisher title registration, Party B shall, upon execution of this contract, prepare the title transfer documents (*名義移轉書類*) and provide them, along with the original authorization certificate (*認可證*), to Party A.
1. Party A may (*得*) submit the title transfer documents to the authorities (*當局*) when deemed necessary (*必要*로 認하는 時).
1. Party B shall not (*不得*) abandon (*抛棄*) Party B's current position (*地位*) without incident (*事故*) nor resign arbitrarily (*任意*로).
1. From the date of execution of this contract, all expenses (*經費*) related to the management (*經營*) of Sidae Ilbo shall be borne (*負擔*) by Party A<33>, and Party B agrees (*承諾*) that it has no right (*權利*) to allow other financial entities (*財團*) to invest (*入社*).
1. Two copies (*貳通*) of this contract shall be prepared (*作製*), with Party A and Party B each retaining one copy (*壹通*).
Taishō 13 (1924), June 2nd
Party A: Kim Hong-gyu Seal (*印*)
Sŏ Sang-hyŏn Seal (*印*)
Mun Chŏng-sam Seal (*印*)
Yi Tŭng-nyŏn Seal (*印*)
Yi Sang-ho Seal (*印*)
Yi Tal-ho Seal (*印*)
Yi Wŏn-yŏng Seal (*印*)
Party B: Ch'oe Nam-sŏn Seal (*印*)
Chin Hak-mun Seal (*印*)

The contract is as stated above. It is so clear that even a three-foot child (*三尺童子*) could understand it. All issues were decided here. From this point, the Sidae Ilbo was no longer the Sidae Ilbo of Sidae Ilbo Co., but the Sidae Ilbo of Poch'ŏn'gyo. Publishing rights, management rights, everything from head to tail (*대가리부터 꼬리끗까지*) was swallowed whole (*홈박 집어 생켯다*). Even the personal rights (*身權*) of those called Party B were swallowed. From Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun's perspective, they were sold off, and sold off dirtily, foully (*쿠린내 나게*). It could even be said they sold their own bodies, blinded by money. However, Poch'ŏn'gyo got an unexpected windfall (*橫財*), like settling into a rice cake chest (*떡함지*). They acquired, without difficulty, a precious media organ, hard to obtain and treated as a public instrument of society, for just a few ten thousand won.

3. Three Questions Regarding This
So, were Ch'oe and Chin truly blinded by money and acted intentionally? Or were they deceived (*見欺*) by Sŏ and Yi? Or did they look down on (*壓視*) the Poch'ŏn'gyo topknot-wearers (*상투쟁이들*) and not treat them as humans? Or did they originally intend to make the Sidae Ilbo a commodity (*商品*) to sell to the highest bidder (*高價者*)? Or, truly unable to manage it themselves, did they hand it over to Poch'ŏn'gyo as a last resort (*最後의 거름*), thinking it better than letting it die completely? There are various questions (*疑問*) regarding this. Why did Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, who is not a simpleton (*天痴*) nor insane (*發狂者*), do such a thing? This is one question. What conspiracy (*陰謀*) did Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn have? As members of the Sidae Ilbo side (holding the important position (*重役*) of director (*理事*)), how could they join (*加擔*) Party A, the Poch'ŏn'gyo side? In other words, how could the seller (*賣渡者*) also become the buyer (*買受者*)? What kind of fox spirit's (*九尾狐*) game is this? This is the second question.
And the Poch'ŏn'gyo side, claiming to contribute generously to social work, why did they, when it came to the contract, arrange to swallow it whole, head and tail, more completely than if it were their own property (*專有物*)? This is also a question. Regarding this, each party likely has its own face-reddening, shabby (*苟苟*)<34> excuses (*辨明*). First, listening to Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, he claims he thoroughly trusted (*信用*) the introduction by Yi and Sŏ, believed the sincerity (*誠意*) of the Poch'ŏn'gyo members' stated dedication to society (*社會奉貢*), and trusted the introducers' and Poch'ŏn'gyo's word that this contract was merely a temporary measure (*臨時方便*), to be kept strictly secret (*極祕密*) until the stock company was established, at which point it would be offered (*提供*) to the company. He claims it was entirely based on moral trust (*德義上 信望*). It sounds plausible, but what nonsense (*웬말이냐*). Didn't the contract clearly state that the publishing rights could be changed arbitrarily (*任意*) when necessary (*必要의 時*)? Didn't it state that they would take full responsibility (*專擔*) for all management? Didn't it state that no other financial entity could invest (*入社*)? In other words, once it became the exclusive property (*專有物*) of Poch'ŏn'gyo, what kind of rotten stock company could there be? Even if there were a possibility of establishing a stock company, under the complete control (*專權*) of Poch'ŏn'gyo, would there be another fool like Ch'oe Nam-sŏn willing to stick his neck out (*대가리를 드리 밀겟느냐*)? No matter how you look at it, this was Ch'oe Nam-sŏn's foolish act (*못난 行爲*), essentially burying himself (*自ら 埋葬*). (Chin Hak-mun, who denies his individual existence (*個體 存在*) before Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, is not worth mentioning. Thus, at the time of the contract, he reportedly entrusted even his seal (*圖章*) to Ch'oe, saying, "You'll handle it properly, won't you? I'll just go along.")
As for Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn, although we don't know what kind of people (*爲人*) they inherently are, just looking at the full story of the affair makes it clear what kind they are. Carrying the Sidae Ilbo in, then quietly turning around and sitting down, smiling "hehe" as they receive it—what more needs to be said about that posture (*꼴*)? Their own explanation is that they feared Poch'ŏn'gyo might monopolize (*專橫*) the Sidae Ilbo if they invested heavily and held the publishing rights, so they, as executives from the Sidae side, joined both sides to balance the power (*勢力*). However, the world attests (*公證*) that they are the main culprits (*元兇*) who created (*做出*) the Sidae Ilbo dispute (*紛擾*).
Poch'ŏn'gyo, for its part, offers this childish excuse (*어린애 수작*). They claim the contract signing itself had no bearing on the contracting parties. Rather, because Poch'ŏn'gyo had often been deceived (*見欺*) by outsiders (*世人*) and suffered much financial extortion (*橫奪*), there was distrust among the followers (*教徒*). This time, as they were putting out a large sum (*巨額*) of 100,000 won, they feared the followers would be suspicious again. Therefore, to show trustworthiness among the followers, they employed it as a temporary measure (*一時的 手段方便*). However, one can see they are the Poch'ŏn'gyo followers, the world's bunglers (*뭉구럭이*), who don't inspect the cow (*牛*) they receive but just take it (*밧는 소는 씩안이하고 밧는다고*).
Enough of this talk. Then why was this contract, meant to be kept strictly secret (*極祕密*), exposed (*暴露*) to the world, and why were the publishing rights changed so suddenly?

3. Sin Sŏk-u's Joining and the Exposure of the Dispute (*紛糾*)
While the so-called top executives (*最高幹部*)<35> Ch'oe, Chin, Yi, and Sŏ were engaged in the shady dealings (*야바위*) with Poch'ŏn'gyo as described above, the staff members (*社員*), completely unaware (*全然히 모르는*) whether their hearts were tightening (*염통이 공기는지*) or their guts twisting (*창자가 뒤집히는지*), just kept working diligently (*꾸준이*), merely assuming that since the executives were present, bundles of money (*돈뭉치*) would somehow drop from somewhere. However, the money bundles never dropped, and continuing work became difficult. Thus, a separate action squad (*別動隊*) concerned for the company (*社*) emerged and sought ways to obtain funds (*資金*).
Prior to this, such rumors (*風說*) were circulating in the world: that is, during the major upheaval (*大變動*) at the Tonga Ilbo (*東亞日報社*) months ago. A person named Sin Sŏk-u (*申錫雨*), joining hands (*携手*) with Yi Sang-hyŏp's (*李相協*) faction that had left the Tonga Ilbo, was going to enter the Sidae Ilbo, with Sin becoming president (*社長*) and Yi becoming managing editor (*編輯局長*)... such talk existed. "Frequent farts reveal the buttocks (*똥끗치*)," as they say. Whether it was just a rumor or fact is unknown, but in any case, Sin Sŏk-u's faction did indeed join (*入社*) Sidae Ilbo Co. (*時代社*). Bringing a lump sum (*金一塊*) of 5,000 won, he, along with Paek Kwan-su (*白寬洙*) and Cho Sŏl-hyŏn (*曹偰鉉*), entered the company and occupied the director's (*理事*) chairs (*椅子*). And he let out a great boast (*號氣*). "Well, since I've put in 5,000 won, being a director is natural (*毋論*), give me a share (*한목*) of the publishing rights you hold too," he demanded co-ownership (*共有*) of the publishing rights. Powerless (*項羽*) under money, Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun replied, "Sir (*에 올리다*), would that be all?" and gave Sin Sŏk-u another contract regarding the ownership of publishing rights (the fourth time (*네번재*) already).
Thus, the Sidae Ilbo company, like pissing on frozen feet (*凍足放溺格*), temporarily showed superficial vitality (*活氣*). On one hand, they announced (*置*) this fact anew, held receptions (*披露宴*) for the newly appointed directors, creating a bustling scene (*욱적북적*). This occurred over five days starting from June 1st.
The dark secrets (*黑幕*) of both sides were bound to be exposed. The newcomer Sin Sŏk-u, puffed up (*氣*) like it was his personal property (*專有物*), was startled when the Poch'ŏn'gyo side reacted, "Hey! Isn't this another deception (*見欺*)? This is serious! We agreed just yesterday (*昨日*) that we would handle all management and absolutely no other financial entity could invest. Who the hell (*웬 놈*) is Sin Sŏk-u? What's this 5,000 won? What directorship? What reception? Ch'oe Nam-sŏn is betraying (*背信*) us!" Thus, Poch'ŏn'gyo, on one hand dealing with Sin Sŏk-u, on another harboring resentment (*感情*) towards Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, and on another consolidating (*鞏固策*) their own position, submitted the publisher title change application (*發行人 名義變更願*) to the authorities and, simultaneously, copied (*謄寫*) the said contract document (*契約文*) and distributed it (*布告*) to various newspaper companies.
With this, everything was exposed. Sin Sŏk-u, smacking his lips (*임맛을 다시며*), turned away saying, "Give me back my money (*내 돈 돌우 내게*). I'm not dealing with you (*임자 네하고는 안이함네*)." Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, thinking, "I tried to do it secretly (*비밀히 해먹을 냇드니*), but it got exposed (*탄로*)," fidgeted (*꽁문이가 옴질거리고*). The general staff members (*一般社員*), realizing, "It's dark under the lamp (*燈下不明*), indeed we were fools (*바보*)," slapped the back of their heads (*뒷통수*) and rose up. The affair exploded (*왁자자*<36> 터젓다).

4. The Staff Association (*社友會*) and Financial Adjustment Measures (*財政調定策*)
Thus, the general staff, greatly astonished and bewildered (*大驚失色*), amidst extreme suspicion (*疑訝千萬*), first, as a fact-finding measure (*事實調査策*), held an editorial staff meeting (*編輯局員 會議*) on June 12th. After discussion, they questioned (*問*) Ch'oe and Chin about the facts (at this time, Chin Hak-mun reportedly denied it, while Ch'oe Nam-sŏn equivocated (*어물거렷다*)) and, while initiating serious negotiations (*交涉*) on another front, continued working diligently.
However, facing a grave situation determining the company's fate (*運命*), could they just overlook it? Under the great realization (*大覺下*) "A grave mistake has occurred (*大事誤矣*)," they held a general staff meeting (*社員總會*) on June 24th and discussed and resolved (*討議決議*) measures regarding Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun, measures regarding Poch'ŏn'gyo, and measures regarding Sin Sŏk-u. (Resolution text omitted - original text (*決議文略-原文*))
On one hand, they dispatched Ch'oe Nam-sŏn to the Poch'ŏn'gyo side to get the contract cancelled (*契約解際*). On the other hand, under the assumption of a neutral financial entity (*中立財團*) centered around Min Pyŏng-dŏk, they attempted (*運動*) to mediate (*調整*) between the two financial entities, Sin Sŏk-u and Poch'ŏn'gyo (all failed (*皆失敗*)). After spending one or two decades (*旬*) in this unsettled (*浮散*) state, ultimately, on July 9th, as the publishing rights passed (legally, completely (*아조*)) to Poch'ŏn'gyo, under the sole order (*一令下*) of the new publisher Yi Sŏng-yŏng (*李成英*), the newspaper was temporarily suspended.
This is roughly the full story. There might be some inversions of sequence (*先後顚倒*), but the general meaning seems unlikely to be significantly different. There's no need to seek further details (*詳細*).

The Sidae Ilbo Incident and the Rising Up (*奮起*) of Concerned Citizens (*社會有志*)
How could there not be public opinion (*社會輿論*) when the Sidae Ilbo, a public instrument of society, became the private possession (*專有*) of a single religious sect (*宗門*) due to the conspiracy (*陰謀*) and tyranny (*專橫*) of a few individuals? Even children and street vendors (*兒童走卒*) are bristling with righteous indignation (*義憤*). While the opinions of various sectors and individuals cannot all be recounted, introducing the resolution (*決議文*) of the "Sidae Ilbo Incident Discussion Meeting" (*時代日報 事件 討議會*), which faced the brunt (*衝*) of public opinion as a collective body, will convey the gist (*大意*) of that opinion.

Resolution

1. Recognizing that a newspaper, a public instrument (*公器*) of society, becoming the exclusive organ (*專有機關*) of a religious sect (*宗門*) or individual causes great harm (*害毒*) to society, we absolutely oppose (*反對*) the recent passing (*歸*) of the Sidae Ilbo into the hands (*手中*) of the Poch'ŏn'gyo followers (*普天敎徒*).
1. Kapcha Year (1924), July 14th, Sidae Ilbo Incident Discussion Meeting (*時代日報事件 討議會*)

**Summary**

This editorial laments the demise of the Sidae Ilbo newspaper, founded less than four months prior with great public expectation as a vital "public instrument" for Korean society. The author, writing under the pseudonym "One Reporter," harshly criticizes the original founders, Ch'oe Nam-sŏn and Chin Hak-mun, for effectively selling the paper's publishing and management rights to the controversial religious group Poch'ŏn'gyo through a secret contract, seemingly motivated by financial desperation. The piece details the complex sequence of events, including alleged betrayals by directors Yi Tŭng-nyŏn and Sŏ Sang-hyŏn, the failed intervention of another investor (Sin Sŏk-u), and the eventual exposure of the deal, leading to the paper's suspension. The author condemns the actions of the key individuals involved, expresses society's outrage through a cited resolution from a public meeting, and questions the viability of Poch'ŏn'gyo running the paper, while also lightly chiding the newspaper staff for their earlier passivity.

From a historical perspective, this article is significant for revealing the precarious financial realities and internal politics surrounding early Korean nationalist media ventures under Japanese colonial rule. It highlights the vulnerability of such enterprises to financial pressures and controversial takeovers (here, by Poch'ŏn'gyo, a group viewed with suspicion by many intellectuals). The harsh criticism directed at Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, a prominent intellectual figure, and the mention of figures like Yi Wan-yong offer insights into contemporary perceptions of collaboration, betrayal, and national integrity within the colonial context. The detailed account of the contractual agreements and negotiations provides valuable primary source material on business practices and power dynamics involving Korean elites and organizations during this period.

問題의 時代日報, 紛料의 顚末과 社會輿論
						一記者
3월 31일 창간되야 통계 102호로써 7월 10일에 停刊된 문제의 시대일보- 這間에는 엇더한 *節이 잇스며 엇더한 파란이 잇섯는다. 世人은 이를 알기에 미간을 한업시 찝프럿스며 世人은 이것을 위하야 獨子의 병보다 더 심하게 *心하얏섯다. 이제 그-事端은 좌우간 一界를 劃한 듯 하니 그 전말을 擧하야써 천하의 여론에 고함과 동시에 딸하 同社紛料의 책임자 諸君에게 一矢를 放하려 한다.

時代日報의 創刊과 吾人의 期待
엇던 頑固老氓의 말과 가티 내가 나은 내 자식을 내가 팔아먹든지 뜨더 먹던지 그대 상관할 배 무엇이냐 하면 吾人은 그 까짓 비인간적인 老鬼에 대하야 다시 말할 것도 업겟다. 맛찬가지로 崔南善
秦學文이가 나은 時代日報를 崔南善
秦學文이가 팔아먹든지 죽여 업새든지 그대들이 상관할 배 무엇이냐고 하면 吾人은 역시 그까짓 鄙夫鄙夫에게 一言半辭 이러구 저러구 할 필요가 업다. 그러나 최초 그들이 사회와 약속함과 가티 時代日報는 엇던 개인의 전유물이 안이요 사회의 公器며 또 明鏡이라 한데서 吾人은 그 성의에 감사하얏스며 또한 時代日報 그것을 사회의 公器로써 사회 전반이 공인하얏다. 煩說을 요할 것 업시 荒凉한 조선 사회에 이 시대일보란 言論기관이 그 얼마나 노력으로써 胚胎 되얏스며 또 얼마나 한 手續으로써 그를 출생직켓느냐. 그가 출생함이 朝鮮에 대한 얼마나한 光榮이며 또 자랑이엿느냐 비록 멧 개인의 노력이엿다 하나 그들의 배경을＜30＞ 보면 朝鮮 전체의 攻勞가 안이엿느냐. 더군다나 그의 출생에 직접 책임자는 그나마 조선적 선진 안이 朝鮮의 國士라고까지 신임을 맛든 崔南善이 안이엿느냐. 이 점에서 吾人은 時代日報 탄생에 대한 직접 노력자인 崔南善에게 감사를 표함과 동시에 「아! 우리 사회에 또 이러한 고마운 公器가 생겻구나!」하고 2,000만이 손을 가티하야 時代日報 그것을 곱게 바더 안엇다.
그리하야 산모인 崔군의 건강(육적보다 영적이엿다.)을 은근히 빌며 産兒인 時代日報의 장성을 암암히 祈禱하면서 日로 月에 그의 健姿가 縱橫自苦함에 더욱 환희를 늣겻섯다.
이리하든 것이 이러케 전 사회로서 기대하며 희망하든 것이 반년이 못하야 이 귀퉁이가 엇더니 저 귀퉁이가 엇더니 醜聞이 들니기 시작하더니 이제 號로 102호 月로 4個月 末滿에 그만 시시비비 疑雲萬丈중에서 普天敎니 申錫雨니 하야 매도니 양도니 하다가 결국 꿈도 생각지 못하든 훔치의 무리에게 임시휴간을 선포하고 마니
天時는 難測이요. 人事는 無常이라드니. 요려케도 無常한 일이 어대 또 잇스랴. 2,000만 민중의 표현기관 사회 전반의 公器요. 明鏡이라든 것이 요러케도 黑雲萬丈에 싹 드러가고 마드란 말가. 「朝鮮놈의 하는 일이란 그럿타닛가.」라는 鼻笑를 또 남기고야 마드란 말이냐, 기가 막켜도 분수가 잇지. 요러케도 알에 웃구멍이 꼭 막켜 보기느 처음이겟다.
죽은 애기 귀 만지기로 안이 파라먹은 소(牛)행실 조타는 말도 2번 할 필요는 업다. (普天敎의 경영 여부는 별 문제로 한다. 普天敎라고 新聞하지 말라는 법은 업스니까.) 前車의 覆은 後車의 戒로 우리 사회의 來頭을 경계키 위하야 그러케 된 전말이나 알어두자. 또는 회생의 道도 업는 바 안이니 사회의 여론에나 付하야 보자.

時代日報 發行權이 普天敎로 넘어가기까지
세상이 共知함과 가티 時代日報는 前週報 東明 經營의 幹部이든 崔南善
秦學文 兩人이 벌서 東明時代에 日刊 時代日報를 당국에 출원해두엇는 것이다. 兩군의 노력이엿든지 당국의 일시 호의엿든지 다행히 時代日報 그것이 작년에 秦學文 명의로써 허가가 되얏섯다. 허가가 되고 보니 곳 발행은 해야겟스나 원체 빈약한 우리 사회인지라 경영비 문제가 무엇보다 큰 문제엿다.＜31＞ 東奔西走 經之營之에 한업시 노력을 하얏스나 종시 金力의 유통을 엇지 못하얏다. 그리하야 주식회사 時代日報라는 명목 하에서 발기인회를 조직하고 총주식 20,000주(1주 20원) 40만원 임금을 모집하기로 하고 일방으로 株金을 모집하며 일방으로 약간의 창립비를 求得하야 금년 2월 말일로써 창간호를 내노앗섯다.
이러케 저러케 두루 비비대가지고 창간은 하얏스나 아직 資金收入은 所期대로 안되얏다. 그래서 금전 고통으로 간부 사이에 의론이 백출되든 모양이엿다.

1. 소위 4人契約과 發起人會의 關係
경영비 문제는 잠간 두고 창간 초에 발행권 보유에 대한 소위 4人契約이라는 것이 잇섯다. 엇더한 의미엿는지는 모르나 時代日報의 방행권은 崔南善
秦學文
徐相灦

이러케 저러케 발서부터 발행권이란 일개 상품 모양으로 금력을 딸하 이리저리 출품이 되기 시작하얏다. 그러나 아직까지도 금전 그 물건은 容易히 드러오지 안엇다. 경영은 갈수록 곤란하게 되얏다.

3. 申錫雨 入社와 紛糾의 暴露 소위 최고 간부라는＜35＞ 崔, 秦, 李, 徐 4인이 普天敎와 이상과 가튼 야바위를 꾸미는 일면에 염통이 공기는지 창자가 뒤집히는지 전연히 모르는 사원들은 그저 꾸준이 일만 해가면서 간부들이 게시니 돈뭉치가 어대로서든지 떨어지겟거니 할 뿐이엿다. 그러나 종시 돈뭉치는 안이 떨어지고 일해 가기는 곤란하니까 社를 위하는 別動隊가 나서서 자금 엇기에 꾀하엿섯다.
先是하야 세상에는 이러한 풍설이 떠돌앗다. 즉 月前 東亞日報社가 대변동이 될 때이다. 申錫雨라는 사람이 東亞日報社를 나온 李相協 일파와 携手하야 時代日報로 들어가는데 申은 사장이 되고 李는 편집국장이 되고- 이런 말이 잇섯다. 방귀가 자즈면 똥끗치 보인다고 사실 풍설이엿는지는 모르나 엇잿든 申錫雨 일파가 사실로 時代社에 입사케 되얏다. 5,000원이란 金一塊를 가지고 白寬洙
曹偰鉉등과 입사하야 理事의 椅子를 占하고 안젓다. 그리고 일대 호기를 토하게 되얏다. 「자 5,000원이나 내엿스니 理事는 毋論이고 그대들이 가진 발행권을 나에게도 한목 다고」하야 발행권 其有를 또 요구하얏다. 금전의 下에 項羽업다고 「에 올리다 뿐이겟슴니까」하고 崔南善
秦學文은 申錫雨에게 발행권 소유에 대한 계약을 또 해주엇다.(발서 네번재)
이리하야 時代日報사는 凍足放溺격으로 일시 표면적 활기를 帶하야 일편 이 사실을 새로 置하며 新任理事 피로연을 하며 하야 욱적북적 하얏다. 이것이 6월 1일로 5일간의 일이다.
양편의 흑막은 폭로되게 되얏다. 신입인 申錫雨는 자기 전유물가티 만장으로 기가 놉핫는데 普天敎측은 깜작 놀내이며 「야-또 見欺가 안이냐. 큰일낫다. 일체의 경영은 우리가 하며 타방의 재단은 절대 입사치 못하기로 한 계약이 昨日 가튼데 申錫雨는 웬 놈이며 5,000원은 무엇이며 理事는 무엇이며 피로연은 무엇이냐. 崔南善이 배신하는구나」하야 이에 普天敎는 일편 申錫雨의 대책 일편 崔南善에 대한 감정 일편 자기네의 지위 鞏固策으로 일편 발행인 명의변경원을 당국에 제출하며 일편 該 契約文을 謄寫하야 각 신문사에 포고하얏다.
이에서 일은 다 폭로되얏다. 申錫雨는 임맛을 다시며 「내 돈 돌우 내게. 임자 네하고는 안이함네」하고 돌아서고 崔南善은 「비밀히 해먹을 냇드니. 그만 탄로로군」하야 꽁문이가 옴질거리고 일반 사원들은 「燈下不明이라드니 과연 우리들이야 말로 바보엿구나」하고 뒷통수를 치며 닐어서게 되얏다. 일은 왁자자＜36＞ 터젓다.

4. 社友會와 財政調定策 이리하야 大驚失色의 일반사원은 疑訝千萬의 중에서 爲先 사실조사책으로 6월 12일 편집국원 회의를 開하고 토의한 결과-崔秦 양인에게 사실유무를 問하며 (이때까지도 秦學文은 부인하고 崔南善은 어물거렷다 한다) 타일방으로 존중하게 교섭을 始하면서 일은 꾸준이 하야 왓다.

時代日報 事件과 社會有志의 奮起
사회의 公器인 時代日報가 멧 개인의 음모 전횡의 독을 입어 일개 종문의 전유가 됨에 엇지 사회의 여론이 업스랴. 兒童走卒이 모다 義擧를 불둑거리는 판이다. 이에 각방면 각개인의 여론은 다- 말할 수 업스나 단결적으로 여론의 衝에 당한 時代日報 事件 討議會의 決議文을 소개하면 그 여론의 大意를 알 것이다.

徐相灦 兩分은 李完用을 學하얏든가요. 구미호에게 홀렷는가요. 당신들의 위인이 걀죽한지 몽롱한지는 모르나 여하간 수단은 조흔듯 하구려. 普天敎에 대한 세상의 여론이 만흐니 또 질머지고 다른 데로 꿍꿍이를 대여 보시요. 늘 그 모양대로만 지내십시오.
普天敎여러분 당신들 참- 감사합니다. 不少한 금전을 제공하야 사회사업 안이 신문을 매수하야 경영하신다 하니 참- 예상외의 仰天할 一大奇事임니다. 아무러나 잘해 보십시오. 普天敎라고 新聞하지 말나는 법-잇슴니까. 그러기에 出願즉각에 허가가 되섯지요. 그 가운데 무슨 꿍꿍이 짓이 업섯는가요. 참말 예외의 특례는 당신들이 먼츰 밧는구려. 그러나 신문이란 돈만 가지고는 잘 안되는 것임니다. 사회의 여론에 빗치여 한번 더 생각해 보심이 엇덜가요.
時代日報 社友여러분 妖將鬼卒이 다 나간 뒤 일편 孤城을 굿게 직키고 新君을 위하야 최후까지 싸우는 성의는 진실로 감사함니다. 그러나 여러분 한가지는 뉘웃처 두십시오. 발행권이 3,4차에 오고 가고 한 것을 왜 모른 체하고 지내섯소. 왜 어물어물하고 지내섯소. 알고도 그리햇소. 모르고 그리햇소? 이뿐.＜38＞
