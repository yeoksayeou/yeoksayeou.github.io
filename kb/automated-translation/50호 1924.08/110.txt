Incheon, What Kind of City Are You?
A Devil's Den Sucking Blood

This refers to the Incheon Rice and Bean Exchange (仁川 米豆 取引所). Let's set aside the blatant sophistry presented upfront, claiming it standardizes the quality and price of rice and beans to provide convenience for domestic and foreign trade. There is some praise, suggesting that Incheon residents earn their livelihood from it and that it contributes to the prosperity of the entire port, and perhaps there were times when they danced, even if only to others' tunes of fortune. Regardless, why should we hesitate at the reckless croaking of frogs in a well, unable even to discern what benefits or harms Koreans as fellow Koreans?

Look! The performance of the Exchange, which once showed figures like a transaction volume of 76,633,600 *sŏk* and shareholder dividends of 60% to 75% per year—indeed, for the 28 years since the 14th year of Incheon's port opening, how many Koreans have benefited from its profits, and how much harm have they suffered?

Initially starting its reach around the Kŭn'gi (近畿) region, it gradually swept up rice from the three southern provinces (三南). If, by some stroke of luck, like a fish caught on a blind hook (無鱗釣), one tasted even a grain as small as a speck in the Ganges (恒河沙), ah! thinking this was a once-in-a-thousand-years (千載一時) chance to make a fortune overnight (一攫千金), farmers threw down their hoes, merchants abandoned their abacuses (壽板), fishermen broke their fishing rods (釣竿), and everyone rushed in. The result was losing even their principal (本錢), leading to pawning, pawning turning into permanent foreclosure (放賣), and finally, losing even the ancestral tablets (神主) without even the ghosts knowing. Families broke apart, brothers and wives scattered, yet not a single soul uttered a word of sympathy—this is the gamble of the rice and bean speculator (米豆쟁이). Having thus drained the blood of the regions south of Kyŏnggi, these poisonous fangs (毒牙) moved again to the northwest (西北), devouring everything in their path, big or small. This net is neither a snare from heaven (天羅) nor a trap from the earth (地網), yet no matter how stubborn a bull-headed (壁倉 牛) person might be, once caught, whether they profit or lose, they are dragged in to fulfill their final destiny, like a water ghost <56> in the proverb. When one fails, two or three follow, and if one person profits, a hundred rush in. The hardship of Korean life arises from this, and the day is not far when the destruction of both public and private Korean economy will inevitably follow. Thus, there is none who does not clearly state that this is a training ground for the children of ruin (滅亡童) and a hell of vanity. Yet, those obstinate, barefoot brats (딸각발이 兒孩) claim, "Koreans only see the Exchange as a gambling den, and acting contrary to the authorities' true intentions is utterly foolish and wicked."

Stop it. It's said finding fault with others is easier than eating cold porridge. Firstly, who established such a market? And if it was publicly established, why are only 4 out of 18 brokerages (中媒店) permitted to Koreans? What evasion of responsibility is this? While claiming equal rights, the unfairness of the commission fees (手數料) between brokerages and customers, making it a gambling den exclusively for the companies and brokerages, what omen of ruin (亡兆) does this signify? Furthermore, ignoring the clear provisions of the transaction regulations (取引定欸) and publicly recognizing some 80 so-called Rice Clubs (米俱樂部), similar to brokerage branches or offices, scattering them densely (林林葱葱) within Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture (京城府), caring only about attracting the interest of counterparties left and right (於左於右) to rake in money—this sight of them racking their brains (머리 惡을 쓰는) is truly bizarre. If they continue like this, oblivious to the pinnacle of enlightenment (毗盧絶頂), never mind a second Araki's crime, what else should they fear?

Furthermore, what is the relationship of interest between Incheon and this Exchange? Recently, rice spot markets (米穀現物取引場) in Pusan, Taegu, and Kunsan stand opposed like a three-legged cauldron (鼎足), engaging in fierce competition for customers (白熟). Where is the first place to suffer the negative effects of the aforementioned proliferation of so-called Rice Clubs? It is the Incheon port, whose residents' livelihood and the city's prosperity are affected. Because of this, Incheon's innkeepers (旅館業者) can no longer retain even a single guest who gathers in the morning and disperses by evening (朝聚暮散). The Yongha geishas (龍河藝妓), who used to enjoy a constant rain of money whether they gained or lost a table, have become cheap commodities. In the Fushimichō licensed quarter (敷島町 遊廓), they lament that staying up all thirty nights a month, they cannot even meet one respectable gentleman from the rice and bean army (米豆軍). How then, no matter how good the rice and bean market conditions are, can it help the livelihood of Incheon residents or the prosperity of Incheon city? What connection does it have? Seeing this, the Exchange has now become an estranged 'office of grievances' (寃府), abandoned even by a part of Incheon. Thus, the company itself long foresaw the future of Koreans, but alas, how many Koreans have awakened <57> to this realization (恍然覺破)?

The Labor World That Lost Its Center
28 years ago, in the year Chŏnghae (丁亥), after Incheon Port (仁港) opened, Kim Hongsin (金宏臣), serving as the Hwado (花島) Garrison Commander and Incheon Police Chief, observed the daily increase in trade at the port and the gathering swarm (蝟集) of dockworkers (船場募軍). He assigned Sŏ Ŭngdo (徐應道) and Kim Tŏkhong (金德弘) the duty of issuing identification tags (等牌), gathered the *mogun* (募軍 - recruited laborers) from various Japanese groups (*kumi*), and established the Kyŏnggi Office (京畿廳) and the Yŏngnam Office (嶺南廳). The Kyŏnggi Office handled unloading (下陸業), and the Yŏngnam Office handled measuring (斗量業). They operated under government supervision until 23 years ago, in the autumn of Im'in (壬寅), when the two offices merged and were renamed the Ch'ŏngsin Office (廳信廳). Mr. Kim Chŏnggon (金貞坤), the current head of the Yŏngsin Group (永信組), took charge then. Seizing the sudden opportunity presented by the Russo-Japanese conflict, he directed thousands of laborers (役夫) to diligently assist the Japanese military actions, earning the distinction (殊功) of a 6th rank decoration (榮動). For today's Mr. Kim, who received such exceptional recognition, this kind of résumé (肩書) might not seem overly embarrassing. However, the situation within the four walls of Yŏngsin, bloated with complacency for a long time in a 'den without tigers' (無虎洞), became generally unacceptable. Calls arose to lower the soaring wages (高跳) that demanded hundreds of *kŭm* (金) at a single call and consequently break their stubborn (頑强) spirit. The result was that 15 years ago, in the autumn of Kyŏngsul (庚戌), this group finally split into three factions. The original Kim Chŏnggon faction transformed into the Yŏngsin Group (永信組). Kim Pongŭi (金鳳儀) and Pang Yŏngsam (方永三) led the Yŏngnam faction to establish the Insin Group (仁信組). Yi Insŏn (李寅善) and others outside the original offices, due to a temporary emotional dispute over security deposits (保弊金), allied with laborers who had withdrawn (脫退) from Yŏngsin and put up the signboard of the Ilsin Group (日信組). Besides these, there are 5 or 6 temporary gathering places called *punbang* (分房 - branch rooms) near the coast for drifters (支械軍) wandering east and west, where hundreds, sometimes thousands, attach themselves to Japanese employers for loading work (擔負役) whenever there is work.

Furthermore, since the establishment of 'Ttamsoni's (人名 - a person's name/nickname?) mill' 20 years ago, the number of Korean factory workers (職工) has dramatically increased in recent years with the prosperity of Japanese commerce and industry. Currently, there are about 2,000 workers in factories for printing, ironworking, candles (洋燭), soap (飛陋 - likely 비누), salt production (製鹽), brewing (釀造), matches (燐寸), dyeing (染色), rice hulling (籾摺/매갈이), and rice polishing (精米) alone. Moreover, I saw for the first time here over a thousand female workers (女工) idling around at various rice mills. The 'white-clothed army' (白衣軍) entering and exiting the earthen huts (土屋) of places like Hwap'yŏng (花平), Sinhwasuri (新花水里), Songnim (松林), and Songhyŏn (松峴) every morning and evening are all labor troops (勞作隊) active in various factories.

Currently, the *mogun* organizations, across the three groups, have entirely ceased the measuring system (斗量統). They only handle the unloading and transport of grains belonging to various commission agents (客主) within the port and the loading and transport of merchant goods belonging to various Japanese groups. All such work is <58> monopolized by the Yŏngsin and Insin groups. The Ilsin group's dockworkers, detached from the commission agent business, reportedly only work at the wharf (船艙) and railway station.

Comparing the strength of each group, Ilsin, leading 40-50 dockworkers, has been managing a meager existence (살님사리) in rented quarters, only recently securing a small loan to build a new group office (組房). Insin currently has over 100 members, but the signboard has been removed from their old office, leaving only the personal nameplate of group leader Pang Yŏngsam clearly displayed, which raises considerable suspicion whenever seen. Despite being called ambitious, wicked, or deserving death, among them, Yŏngsin, amidst repeated turmoil, stands indomitable (百折不屈), firmly guarding the old office building (舊廳舍). At the command of Group Leader Kim, over 300 members cannot straighten their backs, their entrances and exits showcasing the history of the 'big house'... but how can one be satisfied or content with just this?

The world has already moved beyond seeking only localized profit, finding it necessary to cultivate the sanctity of individuality. Thus, influenced by the global clamor for 'Labor, Labor' (勞働勞働), the proletarian class (無産級) of Incheon, their eyes wide open, attempted to participate. In April of Kyehae (癸亥), the Soch'ŏng Labor Association (邵城勞働會) was founded. Like others, it ostensibly raised labor-capital issues (勞資問題), aiming on one hand to punish (膺懲) the tyranny of the capitalist class and on the other hand to prevent (防渴) the encroachment of foreign labor and agriculture, striving for self-sufficiency. Later, conforming to the general trend, it transformed (豹變) into the Labor Federation (노동 총동맹). Currently, 1,000 comrades are said to be joining hands, striving to create a stage for action (活舞臺), further clarifying the banner (旗幟) of grand unity. Regardless of the actual situation, just hearing these words fills everyone, without exception, with uncontrollable joy.

However, who is taking the lives of the blue-panted coolies (고력) crossing over from the opposite shore of Shandong (山東) every month, 2,000 by 2,000, like swarms of clouds? Far from devising countermeasures for this, perhaps the two or three profit-seeking groups mentioned earlier have yet to break free from their innate customs, or perhaps they curse the frivolous and fickle actions of contemporary society, or maybe this is just the way of moderate elements (穩健分子)—who knows? Regardless, the friction (軋轢) among a few savage children (蠻性兒) still bowing their heads in submission (卬頭屈從) under the chin of capital (資閥) not only undermines the unity of the various groups, thereby encouraging the勢勢 of rampant capital, but worse, it repeatedly places hidden reefs (暗礁) in the path of the multitude of the proletariat seeking a new life. Although some protest this and cry out for individual freedom, on the other hand, there are not a few timid souls worried that their lifeline (밥줄 - lit. rice line) will be cut off instantly if they make a wrong move. Consequently, the Federation (총동맹), burdened with the heavy responsibility of embracing the magnanimity of 'universal harmony under heaven' (萬里同風, 四海一室) and shaking the foundations of each group—what can be done when their activism is so feeble? Alas, the 10,000 proletarians of Incheon, wandering without finding their center—where <59> will they turn for refuge (歸依)?

The Consumer Cooperative, Left an Empty Shell (空殼)
The cries of 'Manse!' during the March First Movement (己未運動), proclaiming "We must find what is ours," were considerable, but the shouts urging people to live "My life with my things"—promoting Korean products (朝鮮物産獎勵)—were certainly not inferior. Due to this, like someone startled by a turtle shell after being frightened by a terrapin, wondering if this was the "boycott" (뽀이코드) or "non-violent non-resistance" (비폭력 무저항주의) seen in China or India, it temporarily made one side overly sensitive (過敏). Then, somehow, it fizzled out (흐지부지) unnoticed by anyone. The hearts that were burning like fire yesterday suddenly turned to ice, and nowadays, one cannot even hear a breath of it. Thus, those who watched this child's play (아해 작난) now mockingly retort, "If you guys quit smoking for three months, we'll stop eating rice for three years!" Those whose eyes were wide with surprise initially are now the ones laughing loudly.

However, one thought, "Surely Incheon wouldn't be like that..." but perhaps "surely" deceives people. In March of last year, 722 so-called leading figures (維持) of Incheon, the vanguard advocating for the use of local products, initiated the Korean Products Consumer Cooperative (朝鮮物産 消費組合) with a total of 3,000 shares and a capital of 30,000 won, commencing business. Don't be surprised. Despite months and years passing, 617 members have already forfeited their shares. The paid-in capital of 3,640 won amounted to less than half of the operating expenses. Ultimately, the result for approximately one year was a net profit of 1,507 won 53 jeon, and a total loss of 4,830 won 81 jeon for the period, leaving a net loss of 3,323 won 28 jeon for the period.

And there is something truly heart-wrenching and absurd. Among the 105 shareholders with voting rights, not a few are women who endured the hardships of poverty and contributed their sincere devotion (赤誠) solely out of righteous indignation (義憤). The miserable state of this cooperative, carrying only its long, unloved sign, wandering from house to house with nowhere welcome, is made worse by the assault of this women's army (娘子軍). The cooperative executives are now on the verge of collapsing twice a day (꽁문이가 빠질 지경이다), but far from receiving even the slightest sympathy, many reportedly view them with hostility (仇讎視) as if it were someone else's problem across the water.

Is this what Incheon's leading figures should be doing? Is this the best way to seek survival? Whose possession will the ancestral lands (乃祖乃父의 遺傳庄土), dwindling day by day, become? Do you know, or do you not know, that the gasping fish fry (涸轍의 魚子) in the dried-up rut, appealing to heaven, are your own children? If you are not wooden stones without tears or blood and sweat, surely the day will come with the autumn wind when repentance (悔心) arises!!!<60>

Six Cooperatives Active Within One Gate (門內)
Product Commission Agents Cooperative (物産客主組合)
In the center of Hang-chō (港町 - Port Town), atop a towering, magnificent (宏傑) building, a signboard with brilliant gold lettering (金光) is adjacent.
One among them, this cooperative is the sole institution guiding and supervising product sales and consignment businesses within Incheon Port. For 14 years, from Pyŏngsul (丙戌) 29 years ago to Kyŏngsul (庚戌), under the name Sinsang Association (紳商協會), it functioned like a chamber of commerce, responding to inquiries not only from commission agents (客主業) but also general merchants and industrialists, and reportedly served as the central body influencing the public opinion of the entire city. However, changing times prompted the reform of this institution, transforming its structure into the Kaekchu Association Office (客主團合所), a purely mutual benefit organization for commission agents. Then, starting 7 years ago and continuing to this day, under the current structure, its tasks include manufacturing surveys and statistics, mediating disputes (紛議) among members regarding transactions, and arranging labor-management cooperation (勞資協同), reportedly involving considerable work.

Rice Merchants Cooperative (米商組合)
This cooperative is an association (紏合) of Korean rice hulling (籾摺) businesses in Incheon Port that turn unhulled rice (正租) into brown rice (玄米). For 10 years since its founding, it has strived for friendship among fellow traders internally and stood against opposing organizations externally, working towards the business improvement and development of each member. As a result, the annual rice production (造米) performance reportedly reached hundreds of thousands of *sŏk*. Two years ago it was 224,680 *kamas*. Last year, 353,99*kamas* (*sŏk* was likely intended for the first figure, *kamas* (叺) for the second, or there's a typo). Witnessing such progressive prosperity is indeed praiseworthy as the enhanced collective welfare of this cooperative.

Consumer Cooperative (消費組合)
This cooperative is distinct from the Korean Products Consumer Cooperative, aiming its business at the manufacturing and sale of earthenware mortars for rice hulling (籾摺土臼). Previously, all rice hulling businesses in Incheon Port exclusively used earthenware mortars and related equipment manufactured by Japanese. Whether the quality was poor or the price high, there was absolutely no room for objection (容喙); simply buying and using them was considered the only way, acting like blind men with open eyes. With the establishment of this cooperative, it seized the exclusive right (專權) of single-handedly manufacturing and selling these items. Now, having passed the stage of self-production for self-consumption (自作自給), it has shifted course (一轉) to promote the new trend of reverse exports (역수출). Currently, the Yuchō (柳町) factory has 11 workers producing over 5,000 mortars annually, with sales reaching at least 20,000 won. This confirms the revival of Incheon, no, the prelude to the revival of all Korea.

Kŭnŏpso (勤業所 - Diligence Office)
This institution is an association (團合所) primarily acting as an intermediary, facilitating supply and demand through the Grain Cooperative and the Commission Agents Cooperative. With its main office in Yulmok-ri (栗木里) and a branch in Hang-chō (港町), it expands its operations, encourages cooperation among all members, and provides sincere social service (社會奉仕) for job seekers gathering from various regions, which everyone <61> should appreciate (鳴謝).

Grain Association (穀物協會)
This association is a Japanese organization founded 16 years ago. The initial motive for its creation was that while the Korean side had long had the Sinsang Association (紳商協會) serving as the center (中樞) of Incheon's grain trade, the Japanese side suffered from inconsistent market prices due to individual transactions, as well as numerous disputes (紛議) in deliveries (受渡), leading them to realize their constant disadvantage against the power of Korean merchants. Since then, 37 influential figures joined hands. They primarily deal with general Korean grain merchants, always positioning themselves as buyers, while also exporting rice arriving like mountains (산떰이 가티) from various parts of Korea to places like Osaka, Japan. Their active momentum is quite fierce.

Rice Milling Cooperative (精米業組合)
This cooperative is an association of Japanese polished rice (精白米) manufacturers, currently comprising 12 influential members. Besides striving for fellowship and profit among members, their cooperative principle (組合是) is reportedly to unite and confront the Osaka Rice Association (大阪米穀會) should the latter impose disadvantages. The activity of this cooperative, capable of producing 5,500 *sŏk* of polished rice per day, is truly hard to disregard (侮視키 어렵다). Last year, out of the total Korean rice harvest (實收高) of 15,213,000 *sŏk*, the amount of polished rice exported (輸移出) through their hands was 663,668 *sŏk*.

Sam Sŏng Shoes (三成靴), Welcomed by the World
Before even understanding how the world was changing, intoxicated only by material civilization, carelessly shedding traditional attire (冠帶 벳김) saying, "Bring it on, let's eat, let's use," Western shoes (洋靴) drove out oil-treated leather shoes (乾油鞋), and rubber shoes drove out wooden clogs (木履) and straw sandals (草鞋). Thus, the first victim of this rapid economic destruction was Korean footwear. Mr. Yi Sŏngwŏn (李盛園), who keenly felt this pain (痛切) early on, diligently sought countermeasures through painstaking effort. As a result, 19 years ago, he finally invented the 'economic shoe' (經濟靴). From the economic shoe to the 'durable shoe' (耐久靴), and from the durable shoe to the Sam Sŏng Shoe (三成靴), he truly repeated orderly and energetic improvements, receiving enthusiastic welcomes everywhere.
Compared to previous types of footwear, the special features of these Sam Sŏng Shoes are: first, the use of sturdy U-shaped metal nails (釘鐵) preventing the seams (縫目) of the sole from breaking (破綻); second, attaching light and convenient rubber pads (심판), combining hardness and softness, to the front and back of the sole and shoe, eliminating worries about wear and tear (躡挫) or wetness (兩濕) no matter how roughly used. Given such reliable quality, the unprecedentedly low price (廉價) further surprises the buyers.
Thus, customers who have seen these Sam Sŏng Shoes once, without exception, argue among themselves, "How many renowned figures like Mr. Yi do we have in the footwear world?" and offer congratulations (敬賀).

Miscellaneous Scattered Shapes and Forms <62>

Incheon is Akch'ŏn (惡川 - Evil River)?
A gentleman is supposed to choose a benevolent village (仁里) to reside in, but Incheon is the opposite. There isn't a single bookstore (冊肆) worth buying even a volume of romance novels or popular songs. There are misers (守錢奴) who would risk their lives for money, making even their own children, precious as jade or gold, urinate in Japanese oil drums (倭油桶). Seeing the rampancy (跋扈) of loan shark demons (고리대금의 아귀쟁이) crawling around every alley in broad daylight, ready to gouge out the eyes of living people if possible, one truly feels the urgent need for 'university eye drops' (大學目藥 - metaphor for enlightenment/clear sight).

Today Chungcheong, Tomorrow Gyeongsang
It is said that 8 or 9 out of 10 people who come to Incheon Port often lament, "Just hope sores don't break out on your heels, because money earned just runs away." Perhaps that's why even old-timers who decided they should protect their base with a reformed life since the port opening don't last long. Moreover, the OO Inn, Incheon's number one inn, shamelessly advertises its business by having the head of the innkeepers' association build a public toilet right in front of its main entrance, making one feel like asking, "Damn it, is this the kind of business one does leaning on a cane in a place like Incheon?"

The Strong Man Loses Face (壯士無顔色)
While being bled on one side and having their marrow boiled (骨湯) on the other, young proletarians, with a blood-boiling determination to strive until their dying day, try to support the crumbling educational institutions and other social projects, thinking, "If not us, who will do it?" They seek out the 'ppoljoa' (뽈조아 - likely slang for wealthy/influential people, perhaps 'bourgeois') hoping for some help. However, if approached at the front door, they slip out the back, employing the '36 stratagems' (三十六計). Though not suffering from malaria (학질쟁이), their appearance, wrapped in cotton blankets even in the sweltering heat of midsummer (三伏炎蒸) and breathing through their anuses (똥구먹으로 숨을 쉬는 - extreme cowardice/hiding), would truly make even an idiot (白痴) clutch their stomach and collapse with laughter (抱腹絶倒). Yet, in front of them, the young proletarians have no choice but to bow their heads (머리를 숙으리고), paying respects once, twice, catering to their unpleasant moods, striving until the very end. Whenever encountering them, one cannot help shedding tears of warm sympathy (同情淚). And some wooden-headed (木枕 가튼) 'ppoljoa' begrudged even a 5-pun coin (五分銅), telling a beggar (乞兒) at the gate, "Hey, I'm in a worse state than you."

The Siksa Society that Ate Seaweed Soup (멕국먹은 殖産社 - i.e., failed/disbanded)
The Incheon Siksa Society (仁川 殖産社), an institution that played a significant role in the loan sharking business, abruptly declared dissolution on May 28th last. Whether others engage in usury or not, the entity itself, whose purpose was inherently to make money by any means, needs no further comment... However, this group of over 200 prominent Incheon figures, due to a temporary clash of emotions (感情의 충돌) among a few individuals, ignited a prairie fire (燎原의 火), burying without a trace (속점업시) even this savings institution with a history weathered through half the storms of its existence (半風霜). Is this something we, as Koreans, should be doing? Alas! Cursing the humans who only know the pain of a hangnail (손톱 곰긴 즐) in this day and age! <63>

**Summary**

This article paints a scathing picture of Incheon in 1924, focusing on economic exploitation and social fragmentation. It harshly criticizes the Incheon Rice and Bean Exchange as a predatory institution harming Koreans, details the divisions and weaknesses within the Korean labor movement (dockworkers, factory workers) struggling against Japanese capital and internal strife, and laments the failure of the nationalist Korean Products Consumer Cooperative due to apathy and lack of commitment from its members. The piece contrasts these failures with the organized strength of Japanese business cooperatives and highlights individual successes like the Sam Sŏng Shoe company while decrying the general moral decay and greed perceived among Incheon's elite. For historians, the article offers a valuable ground-level perspective on the socio-economic realities of colonial Korea, showing the complex interplay of colonial economic structures, Korean responses (both organized and individual), internal class and factional divisions within Korean society, nascent labor movements, nationalist economic initiatives, and the tangible impact of Japanese economic dominance on Korean livelihoods and social fabric in a major port city. The specific details on wages, cooperative structures (both Korean and Japanese), labor groups, and the critique of the Korean elite provide rich material for analysis.

仁川아 너는 엇더한 都市?
피 빠라들이는 惡魔窟
이것은 仁川 米豆 取引所를 두고 하는 말이다. 米豆의 품질과 가격의 표준을 일정하야 내외무역의 편리를 준다고 떠드는 당면의 强辯은 그만두고라도 이것으로 하야 仁川 주민이 생계를 엇고 따라서 全港의 번영거리가 된다는 일부 賞讚도 잇다만은 그것도 어느 시기까지는 남의 풍락에나마 춤추어 본 적도 잇섯슬 것이다. 엇잿든지 조선인으로서 朝鮮人에게 이해되는 것을 경계치도 못하고 한갓 우물속에서 함부루 짓거리는 깨고리의 소리야 憚해서 무엇하랴.
보아라. 一時는 賣買高 七千六十六萬三千六百石, 주주이익 배당금 년 6할 至 7할 5분이라는 수자를 뵈이든 ´ 取引所의 성적은 과연 仁川개항 14년 되든 해로부터 28개년이나 되는 동안에 朝鮮人으로서 그 이윤을 입은 자는 멧멧이며 그 해독을 밧은 정도는 얼마나 되는가.
처음 近畿 좌우에 손을 대이기 시작하야 점차 3南 각지의 쌀을 글거들이는 판에 엇지다가 한번 천행으로 장님낙시(無鱗釣)에 물니는 고기와 가티 恒河沙의 一粒만치라도 먹엇다 하면 아아 잇때가 一攫千金할 千載一時라 하야 농부는 꽹이를 던지고 商買는 壽板을 버리고 어부는 낙대(釣竿)를 꺽고 나도나도 들어덤비다가 밋천(本錢)까지 잘니게 된 결과는 典當질로 典當질은 영원 放賣로 맛츰내 귀신도 모르게 선조의 신주까지 들어먹고 형제처자가 이산하야도 어느 뉘가 말 한마듸 불상타고 안는 것이 이 米豆쟁이의 노름이다. 이러케 京畿以南의 피를 흡진한 毒牙는 다시 西北으로 옴기어 적다 만타 할 것 업시 닥치면 닥치는 대로 막 먹어 들어간다. 이 그몰이 天羅가 아니요. 地網도 아니언만은 제 아모리 벽창 牛가튼 자도 한번 걸니어 들기만 하면 利를 보거니 해를 보거나 최후의 사명을 내기하야 속담에 물귀신처럼＜56＞ 끌고 들어가서 하나가 망하면 둘셋이 뒤를 대서고 한 명이 利를 먹는다면 100명이나 쏠니어저서 朝鮮人의 생활난이 여기에서 일어나고 장차 朝鮮人 公私경제의 파멸이 이로부터 다 닥칠 날이 멀지 안타. 그리하야 남들은 이것을 가르처 滅亡童의 양성소이니 허영의 지옥이라고 명언치 안는 이가 업다. 그리면서도 저 모질은 딸각발이 兒孩들은 言必稱 朝鮮사람들은 取引所를 일개 도박장으로만 알고 당국자의 본의에 버서나는 행동을 하는 것은 너무나 愚惡하다고 한다.
아서라. 남의 시비는 식은죽 먹기보담도 쉽다고 하얏다. 첫째 이따위 시장을 공설한 자는 누구이며 旣爲공설한 바이거든 중매점 18개소에서 朝鮮人에게는 겨우 4명에 한하야 허가함은 무슨 塞責이며 동일한 권리를 정유한다면서 회사와 중매점뿐만의 전횡적 노름판이 되는 중매점 대 고객간 수여하는 手數科의 불공평은 그것이 무슨 亡兆이며 다시 9할 이상이나 넘치는 朝鮮人 손님에 대하야 시설에나 대우로나 무엇에나 할 것 업시 모다 주객전도의 차별은 뉘의 행사라 하겟스며 명명백백한 取引定欸의 조문을 무시하고 중매점 지점 又는 출장소와 유사한 소위 80여개소의 米俱樂部를 공인하야 京城府내에다가 林林葱葱이 떠버려 노코 於左於右對方의 관심만 사서 돈만 휘모라 들이면 그만으로 알고 머리 惡을 쓰는 꼴은 볼사록 기괴천만이다. 만일 이처럼 毗盧絶頂을 모르고 나아간다면 第2荒木의 범행은 그만두고 그 이상 무엇무엇은 두려울 것이 잇스랴.
다시 仁川과 이 取引所와의 이해관계는 엇더한가. 挽近以來 釜山, 大邱 群山각지에 米穀現物取引場이 鼎足과 가티 對峙하야 고객의 쟁탈전이 白熟한 데다가 이상 소위 米俱樂部 濫設의 악영향을 먼저 밧는 곳은 어듸인가. 곳 住民의 생계와 전시의 繁榮이 엇더라는 인천항이 다 이것으로 하야 현하 仁川의 旅館업자는 朝聚暮散하는 손님 한분도 挽留해 볼 수가 업느니 생겨도 한 卓, 일코도 한 卓 주야장천 돈벼락 맛든 龍河藝妓는 싸구려 물건이 되어 버렷느니 敷島町 遊廓에서는 한달 설흔날 밤을 새여 가면서도 점잔은 米豆軍 양반 한 분을 못되서 보는 것이 恨事하고 한다. 그러고야 米豆市勢가 제 아모리 조타한들 仁川주민의 생계에나 仁川府의 번영에 무엇이 도움이 되며 무슨 관계가 잇스랴 한다. 이러고 보니 目下이 取引所는 적어도 仁川일부에서까지 정 떠러진 寃府가 되고 말엇다. 이러케 회사자체로서는 발서 朝鮮人의 장래를 豫判한지 오랫다만은 아아 이것을 恍然覺破한＜57＞ 朝鮮人된 자-幾何?

中心을 일어버린 노동계

이 협회는 距今 16년전에 창설된 日本人의 단체이다. 최초 이 단체가 생겨난 동기는 朝鮮人側은 오래전부터 紳商협회가 잇서 仁川 穀物取引界의 中樞가 되얏스나 日本人측으로는 각개의 取引으로 하야 시세의 불통일은 물론 그 受渡上에도 서로 紛議가 백출하야 朝鮮商人의 세력 대 항상 불리함을 깨달은 결과라 한다. 爾來 37명의 유력자가 손을 잡아 일반 朝鮮人 穀商을 상대로 하야 항상 買方의 지위에 처하는 일방으론 鮮內각지로부터 산떰이 가티 廻着하는 米穀을 日本 大阪등지에 수출하는 등 그들의 활동기세는 매우 맹렬하다.

이 조합은 日本人의 정백미 제조정업자들의 조합된 단체로 목하 12명의 유력한 조합원이 잇다. 조합원 사이의 친목과 이익을 力圖함은 물론 日本의 大阪미곡회를 대치하야 일단 彼方으로부터 不利를 가할 時는 일치단결하야 대항한다 하는 것이 그들의 組合是라 한다. 정백미 1日 5,500石의 제조능력을 발휘하는 이 조합의 활동이야말로 侮視키 어렵다. 昨年中全鮮産米의 實收高15,213,000石內에서 이들의 손을 것치어 輸移出된 정백미의 수량은 663,668石이나 된다.

天下가 환영하는 三成靴
세계가 엇더케 되야 가는 셈판도 알기 전에 물질문명에만 중독되야 오냐 드려라 먹자, 쓰자, 함부루 冠帶 벳낌을 하는 바람에 洋靴가 乾油鞋를 모라내고 고무화가 木履草鞋를 파멸하야 이러케 急潮로 가장 먼저 경제의 파괴를 당하는 것은 朝鮮履物이 다 일즉이 이것을 痛切이 늣긴 李盛園군은 勤勤孜孜 이것의 대책을 강구하기에 고심한 결과 距今 19년전에 비로소 경제화를 발명한 이래 경제화로부터 耐久靴 耐久靴로부터 三成靴 참말로 着着 질서잇는 정력적 개량을 거듭하야 도처의 열광적 환영을 밧아 왓다.
종래 각종 화에 비하야 이 三成靴의 특수한 점은 첫재 靴底縫目의 破綻을 방지하는 堅緻한 ㄷ字形의 釘鐵을 사용하고, 둘재는 靴底와 靴의 전후에 强柔竝用의 輕便한 고무 심판을 접착하야 아모리 濫用할지라도 躡挫의 弊와 兩濕의 苦를 밧을 기우가 업다. 이가티 품질이 견실한 이상 더구나 破天荒의 廉價임은 도리여 買方의 경이를 산다.
그리햐야 한번 이 三成靴를 구경한 고객은 너나할 것 업시 「우리는 靴界에 李군가튼 명성이 몃분이나 되는가」하야 서로 닷토아 敬賀치 안는 이가 업다.

한번 仁港으로 차자 들어오는 사람이면 십상 8,9나 걸풋하면 「제발 발꿈치에 헌듸만 나지 마라든만 벌면 달아난다」고 한다. 그래서 그러지 개항이래 개정한 생활로 본사를 守護하야 제법 남과 가티 살아보아야 하겟다는 고물도 얼마가 못되야 보임은 물론 소위 여관업자 조합장의 경영으로 정문압헤 其同便所를 지노코틴 염치업시 영업광고를 대행하는 仁川 1의 OO여관은 그야말로 「앗다. 이것이 이 따위 仁川서 집팡막대집고 해먹을 노름이냐」하는 것처럼 늣기게 한다.
