Magazine Title: 개벽 제50호
Publication Date: 1924년 08월 01일
Article Title: 入學試驗 (Entrance Examination)
Author: 申必熙
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Short Story)

Entrance Examination
						Sin Pil-hŭi

The date for the entrance examination for the Girls' Higher Common School that Soae intends to enter this year is now barely a month away. The hearts of the family members, old and young alike, are becoming needlessly tense. Born as the only girl among four siblings, Soae has received the most affection among the brothers and sister. To think that Soae, who until just the other day used to have a runny nose, is now facing something like the Girls' Higher Common School examination... thinking this, the family members found it amusing, wondrous, and delightful. Therefore, the family members were naturally filled with the desire to get her admitted one way or another. Soae herself, of course, was also putting her whole heart and soul into it.

"If it's not that school, I won't go anywhere else..." Soae says, blinking her black crystal-like eyes as if acting spoiled.

"That's right! You need that kind of determination."

Even her father, who usually is quite indifferent to the children, has recently been calling her over with affection every day, earnestly explaining and asking about things like Classical Chinese texts. When he hears Soae's bright and clever answers, he nods approvingly, saying, "That's right, that's right," breaking into a smile.

The thought of Soae, wearing a short skirt high up on her chest, a white jeogori, carrying a book bundle and an embroidery frame, gracefully entering and exiting the main gate of the girls' school morning and evening—especially since she was his precious only daughter—made him, as her father, feel even more delighted. <12>

"What will you do if you fail there?"

Her older brother, Illyŏng, asks with a smile, as if teasing. Illyŏng is a first-year student at the Higher Industrial School. Having passed through the difficult gates of examinations several times—Middle School, Middle School Qualification Examination, Speciality School—and somehow made it this far, he thinks about how his younger sister, who is 6 or 7 years younger than him and still seems somewhat childish, has encountered what he experienced as the most painful thing. He nods his head as if amused, smiling. However, compared to his parents, who are behind the times and have never understood the taste of this thing called an examination, he best understands the suffering of his younger sister, feeling that she, at her still tender age, is enduring hardship beyond her capacity. Therefore, behind his teasing words, "What will you do if you fail there?" and his seemingly amused smile, lies the warmest sympathy.

"If I fail, I'll hang myself and die..." Soae says without the slightest hesitation, in a voice like splitting bamboo.

Hearing this, the father bursts out laughing. To him, as a parent who always thinks his own child is superior to others', Soae's bold and confident answer sounded incredibly dependable and strong-willed. That's good enough... the father nods in satisfaction. However, Illyŏng, even though it was a thoughtless remark from a child who didn't know any better, found the comparison too extreme and different from what he expected. His heart felt a chill, and his face frowned. At the same time, what spontaneously arose in his chest was the memory of when he took the Middle School Qualification Examination. He had thought he would pass nine times out of ten, but he failed. He remembered crying all day without eating due to anger, resentment, disappointment, and discouragement. Indeed, the extreme agitation at that time made him want to die. Thus, his psychology was the exact opposite of his father's, who laughed it off nonchalantly; a gloomy feeling <13> overshadowed his heart. He regretted his own careless words that had given his younger sister such a needlessly dark suggestion. But seeing his father's face beaming with a bright smile, and especially his sister's clear, bright face unexpectedly shining with hope, his own gloomy feelings melted away like snow in spring.

"Hmph! Hang yourself... Let's see. Drooling..."

As Illyŏng's gloomy mood vanished in an instant, he instead mocked her further with teasing words like these.

"Ah- I don't want to hear it..."
"Stop saying such ominous things!"

Soae and her mother, who heard Illyŏng's words, spoke in unison. As if scolding him for being unlucky, the mother's sharp gaze focused on Illyŏng's face.

"Even jokes have their limits!" the mother's harsh voice struck Illyŏng again.

"Yes, I was wrong," Illyŏng just bowed his head and remained silent.

"Soae! Hurry up and study. If you just get accepted, won't I give you an emerald pendant! That's really talking big..."

The mother ignores Illyŏng and speaks to her daughter.

Soae suddenly droops like a blade of grass scorched by the sudden sunlight of a summer day, becoming heartbroken. Those clear, round eyes, the small lips like red lotus blossoms soaked in water, the even, white teeth like ivory, and the soft earlobes hanging down, tinged with a faint, transparent blood color—all these features possessing that pure innocence are suddenly overshadowed by a dark cloud, showing a profound anxiety. Her eyes blink rapidly three or four times, and the elastic flesh of her cheeks puffs out, then she adopts a composed posture as if deciding something. Hearing the two words "pass the exam" (合格) was more frightening to Soae than anything <14> else. The first entrance exam she ever faced, the countless competitors she had never seen or heard of, the imposing school building standing solemnly—and all the events encroaching upon her young body with relentless, heavy, ponderous force, like the earth rumbling—drawing these in her mind, Soae's heart pounds, and her face flushes. Last year, her friends Hyeok and Oksun, and especially Kyŏngsuk, who had even received the top excellence award, had failed. But quite unexpectedly, Yŏngae and Chŏnghŭi, when she meets them on the street these days, act self-important, carrying their book bundles and attending school. Thinking about this, Soae felt that there were indeed many strange things. —Right now, in Soae's heart, the worry "What if I fail?" resonates more strongly than the joy "It would be great if I pass." Therefore, naturally, even while concentrating on her studies at her desk, her heart suddenly starts pounding wildly, as if hearing some kind of musical performance inside her chest. —She draws triangles and circles with her pencil, lost in thought. The pencil lead breaks. She sharpens the pencil. —Time keeps passing while she is stuck on one arithmetic problem. —This is terrible! What should I do! What should I do! she feels anxious. Truly, in this way, Soae's innocent and charming demeanor gradually fades, and her cheerful and bright personality diminishes, clearly visible to anyone's eyes.

"This is really something terrible for children like her. Pitiful! Heartbreaking!"

Illyŏng, who well knew the dreadful, agonizing taste of exams, felt sympathy for his sister's plight whenever he saw her face, thinking thoughts like these.

"Soae! Come inside quickly! Hurry!"

Seeing Soae hesitating, looking down at the wood grain of the veranda floor bathed in warm sunlight, the mother urges her with a sharp, piercing voice.

Soae gets up and enters the room, glancing at her mother's expression with eyes that seem to say she finds it bothersome.

"How can a child be so timid! Just watching from the side is frustrating enough," the mother <15> nags at herself. Yet, when Soae sits down facing her desk, she wears a faint smile on both cheeks, as if feeling some sort of satisfaction.

Even within the family, the mother is the most intensely focused on Soae's entrance exam. Therefore, with the slightest thing, she nags incessantly, "Study! Study!" The homeroom teacher of Soae's class holds extracurricular lectures for the students preparing for the exam. But the mother, not reassured even by this, has arranged for Soae to go and learn from a teacher at some school for two hours a week. Truly, the mother's ignorant fervor, yearning for Soae's success, was extraordinary. In her mind, there was a strong vanity, common among parents of ordinary children, envisioning putting her daughter into a government school so that later people would say, "So-and-so became a teacher and earns several thousand nyang a month," or "So-and-so married someone, and the couple earns several thousand nyang a month—." However, what stimulated her excessive enthusiasm even more deeply was the fact that her husband's younger brother's daughter, Chuka, who was the same age as Soae, was taking the exam for the same school.

"It would be nice if both girls passed, but what if only one of them gets in..." she would say from time to time. But in reality, she hoped that Soae would beat Chuka soundly... or that even if Chuka failed, Soae alone would pass.

"I went there the day before yesterday, and Chuka was studying without playing for even a moment. You need to pull yourself together too, what will you do if you lose..." the mother prods Soae.

"Mother! If Chuka passes and I pass too, I'll walk hand in hand with her..." Soae says, her eyes sparkling with no ulterior motive. But hearing these words, the mother's expression turns grim. And she gives her daughter a hint, as if telling her to push aside Chuka, who is always praised as being more talented and prettier than her daughter and is a strong competitor, and become the sole object of others' envy. Thus, she makes Soae fall again into the anxiety of competition. <16>

Illyŏng always watches his mother's attitude like this, feeling as bitter as chewing wormwood, his mind furrowing.

One day, the mother hurriedly enters Illyŏng's room and speaks in a lowered voice.

"Hey! Go and make a request (請)! Make a request! I just went to Chŏnghŭi's house, and Chŏnghŭi's mother said so. If there's a close teacher, make a request... They say the younger uncle's family is also campaigning right now."

"Oh! Mother, you say such pitiful things... Next, you'll say all sorts of things..."

Illyŏng looks at his mother, feeling displeasure.

"All sorts of things! All sorts of things! What do you mean, all sorts of things? It seems their daughter probably got in that way too?"

"Please, stop saying such pitiful things."

"But everyone else is making requests, what should we do if we just sit still?"

"So, you're saying our family should make a request too?"

"Well..." the mother trails off evasively.

"Don't even say such absurd things. What is this 'request'? Exams are about real ability (實力). And there's also luck (運數)," Illyŏng cuts her off sharply.

"What do you know..." the mother retorts, sneering at Illyŏng's firm words.

"When it comes to taking exams—I know better than you, Mother... What need is there to go so far as to make a request just to get Soae into that school?"

The mother was rendered speechless by her son's reasonable words.

"Why are you making such a fuss! Shouting like that..."

The mother, feeling embarrassed, pouts and walks towards the inner room (anpang).

Illyŏng watches his mother's retreating figure, feeling the pain of having spoken a bit too harshly.—<17>

As his mother's shadow completely disappeared into the inner room, he had, in a momentary fit of temper, regarded his mother's psychology—her anxiety driving her to even consider making a request out of the sole desire to get her beloved daughter accepted—as utterly base. But the moment he saw her walking away powerlessly, he felt pity instead. Right! It's not unreasonable at all! It's natural to do such things! It's the heart of a parent. Illyŏng thought this way.

Although he had stubbornly insisted, with the righteous emotion of a student completely untouched by worldly affairs, that "Exams are about real ability. And luck," in this real society, things definitely do not proceed solely according to "fairness" (公). He also thought that for that school's exam, no, not just that school but all school exams, making a "request" (請), though a base method, could certainly yield results to some extent. It's not limited just to exams; in everything in this world, "requests" are necessary, Illyŏng thinks, grimacing as if chewing an unripe persimmon (生柿). At the same time, he thinks of the sort of social atmosphere surrounding his somewhat wealthy father.

"That's right! It's certainly not unreasonable for Mother to think that way. It seems entirely plausible, just as Mother said, that the younger uncle's family is doing something.—Yes! It's quite possible!" Thinking this, Illyŏng couldn't suppress the part of his own psychology that wanted to sympathize with his mother's feelings. Simultaneously, an image appears of his aunt: her powerful-seeming arrogance, her pretense of being a woman of new-era knowledge, her affectation of being clever in social interactions, her plump figure that looks like she wouldn't blink her left eye even if lightning struck. A woman of 34 or 35, with slightly darkish skin for a female student type, sagging cheeks, and within that face, two sly, narrow eyes that seem determined to catch anyone's gaze, blinking rapidly. With these eyes, she puts on a feigned smile she doesn't mean in her heart, speaking skillfully with a flattering tone—seeing this image of his aunt, Illyŏng couldn't suppress his displeasure. The arrogance and vanity of a life born from excessive indulgence, seemingly unable to overcome laziness; an attitude as if battered and bruised by all sorts of schemes—Illyŏng speculated how well all these conditions fit the phrase "is campaigning." And while feeling the same emotion as his mother, wishing that the annoyingly precocious Chuka (who, of course, was raised that way by his aunt), lacking any of the charming innocence of young children, would fail, he simultaneously conceded to his mother's base attitude. However, leaving aside all these twisted emotions, Illyŏng, purely for the sake of his little sister, sincerely wished and prayed for her beautiful success.

"Indeed, everything about the current examination system is fundamentally flawed.—How can a small number of schools accommodate the multitude of children increasing every year?—The parents' hope to place their children in good schools, by any means necessary, is the same for my mother and my aunt, without any difference. But the necessity of resorting to unreasonable schemes or forcing unsuitable studies upon innocent, rapidly growing children exists fundamentally because of this thing called the examination system. That's right! The only thing unacceptable is this. There is no need to hate or argue about the rightness or wrongness of everything else," Illyŏng finally concluded, channeling all his resentment into this thought.

"The sea is calm like a mirror, carrying the morning and evening sun, the shadows of white sails hiding behind islands are also peaceful. The night scene where the moonlight shatters on the ripples and fishing lights appear and disappear among the waves is also..." (海の靜かなることは鏡の如く 朝日夕日を負ひて 島かくれ行く白帆の影ものどかなり。月影 のさざなみにくだけ 漁火の波間に出沒する夜景もまた...)

Soae's voice, sounding like she is reading a textbook aloud in such clear tones, rings in Illyŏng's eardrums. He unconsciously perks up his ears. The sound of reading and rereading is heard, then suddenly stops. After a short pause, she starts reading again. He remembers when he was preparing for the Speciality School Qualification Examination, meticulously writing down difficult characters and idioms from the textbook one by one in a notebook.

"Ah! She's giving her all..." Illyŏng smiles faintly and looks outside. —The clear sky, typical of spring after the New Year holidays have passed, shines brightly. It's a quiet Saturday afternoon. Through this bright air and quiet vibration, the sound of a violin being played flows lightly from the neighboring house. This lingering sound intersects with Soae's voice reading the textbook, <19> sometimes leading, sometimes following. Illyŏng couldn't help but think about the different worlds shaped by these two kinds of sound and voice.

"On such a nice day, I wish I could let her play freely..."

Illyŏng turns away from the violin sound and pities Soae's diligent reading.

The tranquil spring day itself seems to sympathize with Soae, gently embracing the window where she reads.

Suddenly, it was February. Dazzlingly bright and warm weather continued for several days.

One morning during breakfast. The father, who, as usual, had spread the newspaper on his lap and was reading it, said,
"Huh, they say the azaleas have already bloomed... Seems the season is very early this year..."
"The weather has been extremely warm for the last 4 or 5 days, so the flowers have already bloomed," the mother replies.

"Last winter, there was little snow..."
"Last year, you could hardly call it winter... if winter days were always like last year, I could manage,"

Through the glass window, the soil in the backyard that had been frosted seemed dry already, and the buds on the maple tree by the wall looked suddenly plump. The morning sunlight shone warmly and quietly on the branches of the evergreen tree. —As if the biting cold of winter days that seemed to chill the flesh was forgotten, the peaceful blessing of the spring day appeared on the faces of the family members, unable to be hidden.

"Father—take me to see the zoo..." says Illyŏng's youngest sibling.
"Well..." the father nods his head.
"Let's all go together on a holiday..." says the mother.
"Oh, great!" says the youngest sibling, bouncing in place, raising both hands still holding a spoon. <20>

Illyŏng had been carefully observing Soae's attitude, who had been silently listening to this conversation from the beginning with a resentful look. In her eyes, there was clearly a flicker of wanting to join the conversation, then hesitating, a look of impatience. Illyŏng had noticed this from the start. And he resented the mentality of the others who didn't even think of Soae.

"Where are you going alone, leaving your sister behind!" he says, as if scolding his younger sibling, casting a pressing gaze towards his parents.

"Oh right, what about Soae..." the mother says, as if just remembering, avoiding her son's intense gaze.

"Where would she go? What about her exam studies..." the father says, closing his mouth firmly.

The group, as if encountering an unexpected intruder, is dumbfounded and unable to continue the conversation, each staring blankly at random points.

Soae, who had been looking down at her lap with her head bowed slightly, suddenly starts sniffling. Illyŏng turns his head towards her and watches her slender, delicate shoulders trembling faintly. —For some reason, a 'sentimental' (센듸멘달) psychology, making him want to cry along with her, creeps over his entire body. He raises his right hand, places it on her shoulder, and consoles her in a voice thick with emotion.

"Don't cry. Let's study together at home. If you pass, where couldn't you go—You'll definitely pass this time, once you pass, once you pass..."

As Illyŏng consoles his younger sister like this, he is struck by the words "once you pass, once you pass..." which he repeated. He thought about how, from his long experience, these two words "pass the exam" (及第) had filled his own heart with fear every time he took an exam. And he realized that he had used these terrifying words to disturb and fill with anxiety the heart of a young child whose nerves were already frayed, even though she didn't need it. When he considered how he had spoken these words out of his own agitated state, <21> causing the young heart to tremble violently like a bellows, his tongue (舌) suddenly stopped. He looks blankly at Soae's posture, wondering what he should do. —A warm, faint trembling is felt through the palm of his right hand.

"Don't cry! What's the use of crying? Come on, pull yourself together and let's study. That's the most important thing!"

"Right! Studying is the most important thing for you. If you just get accepted, I'll not only take you on that outing... What shall I buy you? A watch? An umbrella? A reed organ? Ah! A reed organ would be nice. A reed organ..." the father says.

"Father! Then I won't go sightseeing either, buy me a reed organ," the youngest sibling whines.

"Stop it! Be quiet!!"

Illyŏng snaps irritably. Then, looking at Soae, this time in a very gentle voice,
"Soae! I know you don't want to study, but what can you do? If you just grit your teeth and endure it for a few days, you can play all you want later."

Soae is still sniffling. Illyŏng, trying hard to comfort her, couldn't help feeling unsatisfied that he couldn't express all his emotions. He clasps his hands and watches his sister sniffling. Meanwhile, his own heart begins to be drawn into his sister's sorrow. His breathing starts to become labored. He finally felt an impulse rising like flames in his chest, wanting to hold his pitiful sister tightly. But at that moment, seeing his parents and siblings around him, he hesitated.

"Soae. It's already eight o'clock. You need to get ready for school quickly. What if you're late..."

Illyŏng says these words as if in pain, then quickly returns to his study. He throws open the window and stands there limply. Looking at the lush branches of the evergreen tree, <22> which seemed to be breathing in the dazzlingly beautiful sunlight with its whole being, he endlessly dreamed phantom images of sympathy for his sister.

There were only two weeks left until Soae's entrance exam, scheduled for early March. It was one Sunday morning. Illyŏng, lying in his study reading various books, heard the sounds of joyful laughter from Soae and his youngest brother playing in the front yard. It seemed the middle sibling was also mixed in, as his voice, often trailing off unintelligibly, could be heard occasionally. Hearing the unrestrained, carefree playing sounds of these three siblings, Illyŏng couldn't help but feel grateful for the blessing of the spring day, where truly "all things take pleasure in themselves" (萬物이 皆有以自樂).

"Soae! Soae! Are you playing again? Not studying..."

Suddenly, the mother's voice, sharp as a laurel branch, is heard. By the time it vibrated Illyŏng's eardrums, the playing sounds of the three children had already abruptly stopped.

"If you just play like that, will rice cakes fall from the sky? What will you do if you fail..."

When the mother's voice once again harshly propagated through Illyŏng's auditory nerves, he clicked his tongue, "Tsk." And frowned. He felt displeasure less at his mother's attitude towards Soae, and more infinitely displeased by his mother's murderous tone which had utterly destroyed the peaceful taste of the quiet spring morning—the joyful laughter of the three carefree siblings, the peaceful sunlight of the bright morning, his own free enjoyment... all of it.

"When children play, they should play. Do you think just studying is good..." he muttered his displeasure to himself.

"You should have some sense too! With only a few days left, you just play..." the mother nags again. Imagining his pitiful younger sister, deprived of joyful play and enveloped in anxiety and impatience, Illyŏng couldn't bear it anymore and abruptly stood up.

"Mother! Please leave her alone. When it's time to play, she should play." <23>

Illyŏng faces his mother, frowning, feeling sorry for the weary appearance of his sister standing blankly beside her, exhausted from excessive studying.

"It's because she only plays..."
"When does she only play? What more can she do! Besides attending extracurricular lectures at school every day, she also goes to learn from Mr. Kim... But Mother, you only nag her relentlessly, how can a young child endure it? Even if she passes, what's the use if her health weakens!?"

"That may be true, but look at other children. First, look at the younger uncle's child..." the mother casts a meaningful glance at her daughter.

"Soae! Go into the room quickly," she says, then turns to her mother with a sharp voice,
"The younger uncle's house is the younger uncle's house, and our house is our house."

"So you're saying it's okay if she fails?"
"It's okay if she fails! I want Soae to pass more than ten times as much as you do, Mother... That's enough, how can she do more?"

"They say to ask even for roads you know, you should strive to do even better on top of doing well!"

The mother's face flushes with agitation. And there is clearly an expression of cold mockery contained within it.

Illyŏng felt a kind of nervous anguish, knowing he couldn't possibly make his mother understand his true intentions through words... He turned away, showing an expression as if to say, "Do as you please. I don't care," and shed tears watching his mother turn away. He too felt a self-abandoning feeling that the result of the exam didn't matter anyway. Thinking about how this thing called an examination, flawed in all its lawlessness and fundamental principles, mercilessly harms the psyche of innocent and straightforward young children, ruthlessly cuts down their budding bodies like sprouts, and moreover, creates discord arising from it—like the psychological gap currently forming between his mother and himself—he couldn't help but hate the existence of the examination system for Soae's sake, <24> no, for the sake of all children wanting to learn, and for the peace of the family.

"Percentage (步合) is calculated by dividing the rate amount (步合高) by the principal amount (元高). The rate amount is obtained by multiplying the principal amount by the percentage..." (步合は步合高を元高で割る。步合高は元高に步合を乘ける...)

Hearing Soae's voice studying with single-minded concentration, he frowned even more.

It was afternoon that day. Illyŏng left his house with his classmate K, who had come to visit him, and went out for a walk towards Pagoda Park. The warm sunlight shone on their backs. Seeing the faces of the busily coming and going people, each filled with the color of spring and overflowing with vitality, Illyŏng thought of his younger sister at home, using her frail brain alone for studying, and felt inexplicably unsettled. Pitiful! Pitiful! On a day like this... While thinking like this, they reached the park gate before he knew it.

"The most annoying thing is probably exams, right?" Illyŏng asked K.
"Why? Did something suddenly occur to you?" K asked back.
"No, it's just... My younger sister is going to take an entrance exam this year..."
"I see," K says matter-of-factly, without much sign of sympathy, as he doesn't have a younger sister.

"Young children, now just thirteen or fourteen, are already facing exam competition and whatnot... Isn't it really too pitiful?"
Illyŏng speaks in a sentimental tone, as if seeking sympathy.
"Well, if you say it's pitiful, it's pitiful."
"Honestly, I wish they would abolish the exam system for young children. Firstly, think how much harm it does to their innocent character and health..."
"Well, that might seem plausible, but isn't this 20th-century active society a battlefield of adversarial competition stemming from the demand for self-preservation? No matter how young the children are, isn't it an era where they inevitably have to compete! Even if it's painful and inconvenient, as long as there are many competitors, isn't the exam system necessary <25> after all? Think about our elementary school days. Back then, didn't they say 'Come! Come!' while giving out books and even money? But now, the times are different from then, aren't they? Of course, this is directly proportional to cultural development, so it can't be helped by human means..." K pauses for a moment, then continues, "Also, thinking about it another way, in order to cultivate personalities capable of enduring the struggle for existence on this active stage in the future, isn't it right for children, who will become the second generation of citizens, to undergo such trials from a young age? A child whose body weakens or whose nerves become frail just from studying a bit for an exam is already useless in this 20th century. They will likely become losers in the future anyway..."
"Well, the future is the future, and now is now. Children, as far as possible, should be allowed to grow with their innocent hearts, free from greed and malice, without any hindrance. But middle school or girls' school exams destroy all of this."
"At the age to enter middle school or girls' school, can they remain like three-year-olds as you say?"
K insists on his own theory without yielding an inch. Illyŏng lost interest in talking further about this issue. Thus, his heart, seeking sympathy, instead felt lonelier. At the same time, he judged that K's cold mentality was limited to those who did not possess the shared blood of a sister... The bright afternoon sunlight reflects beautifully on the water... He stares blankly at it, once again starting to imagine Soae's figure sitting solitarily at her desk.

However, when the exam date was only 2 or 3 days away, Illyŏng could no longer remain a bystander. The hope of how to overcome the many competitors and crown her with the beautiful laurel wreath diluted his pity and sympathy for Soae. Thus, not only on holidays but as soon as he returned from school, he would call her over, sit her down, and make her practice arithmetic, Japanese dictation, and interpretation. Soae was also earnest. But due to fatigue from studying every day and lack of sleep at night, <26> and also due to anxiety and worry about the exam, her eyes were red and feverish, and her complexion was pale with a yellowish tint... The liveliness and cheerfulness from before were nowhere to be seen. However, he had no time (暇) to pity her for this. Consequently, if the answer to an arithmetic problem was wrong or the interpretation of an idiom was incorrect, he would become irritable without realizing it himself.

"You don't know this! Something like this!" he shouts.
"You're definitely going to fail! Fail! Fail!" he clicks his tongue.

Soae's face, reacting to such words, shows terror. Seeing her hesitation, lacking confidence and unsure what to do, he quickly realizes he has spoken too harshly and snaps back to his senses. But when such situations recurred, he still couldn't help becoming agitated.

"It can't be helped! It can't be helped!" Illyŏng repeatedly sighs to himself. Each time, Soae shivers and just wets the top of the desk with her tears.

The first day of the entrance exam: Arithmetic. The family members encourage Soae as if sending off a warrior going to the battlefield for the first time.

"Soae! Don't rush. Read the problems carefully, slowly, and write the answers deliberately. And don't hand it in until the time is up; check it again and again before handing it in. Don't worry about others... Not rushing is the most important thing in an exam," the father instructs. His words carry the strong and powerful devotion of a parent for their child.

"Do what you know first, and think about what you don't know later. It's okay to change the order... And no matter how many unfamiliar kids there are, firmly believe that they are all less capable than you," Illyŏng also earnestly advises.

Soae nods at each point. But her eyes are still filled with anxiety. <27>

"Do well!" the mother says, unable to finish her words, her lips trembling faintly.

As Soae says her final "Yes" and walks towards the gate, all eyes follow her, as if silently praying for her success...

"Just 10 more minutes!" (もうあと 10分!) the proctor's deep voice struck Soae's overly sensitive ears.

Soae, feeling as if doused with cold water, sharply lifted her head and looked at the teacher's calm face.

Out of eight problems, she had solved six without any trouble. But problems 3 and 7—Soae had agonized over these two from the beginning. Already, more than half of the 60 examinees had left.—In the quiet classroom, receiving the warm morning sunlight through the south-facing glass windows, about twenty remaining examinees were scattered irregularly here and there. The sound of pencils scratching and throats clearing echoed strangely off the ceiling, stimulating the tense atmosphere.—Soae's chest heaved, and the hand holding the brush trembled.

"The wages for 4 carpenters for 7 days are..." (大工四人の七日間の賃金は...) reading the 3rd problem, Soae's eyes suddenly jumped to the 7th problem, "inner length 20 cm, width 15 cm..." (內法長さ二十糎 幅十五糎...). Like this, holding onto the two problems, she thought about one, and if it didn't work out, jumped to the other to think about it. But her mind only grew anxious, her face flushed as if drunk, and her thoughts wouldn't unify at all. Her face turned bright red, and she began to panic. Meanwhile, the sound of footsteps indicated a female student leaving the room, catching her eye. Her heart grew even more anxious. One second... ten seconds... fifty seconds... Silence dominates.

Unable to think further, Soae impulsively casts her gaze towards the person in front of her. Instantly, a corner of the answer sheet beneath the girl student's indigo-edged (남빗끗동) sleeve flashes into view. Feeling the pangs of conscience, she quickly changes the direction of her gaze. But the urge to look once more became an irresistible impulse. Soae almost unconsciously steals a glance at the teacher's face. Then, focusing her gaze back on the answer sheet, she looks greedily. Ant-like <28> numbers strike her retina until her eyes sting. It was indeed the answer to the 7th problem.—During several tens of seconds of continued silence, the sound of the teacher's shoes approaching her is heard. By the time this sound brought her to her senses, it was already too late. She flinches as if struck by something and lowers her head deeply.

The teacher stood in front of Soae's desk and carefully looked at the number on her examination ticket. The tips of Soae's fingers trembled violently, and her breathing stopped as if suffocating, making no sound.

A little later, the bell signaling the time rings.—About 20 remaining examinees, their faces colored with expressions of success and failure, joy and sorrow, hope and despair—all stand up at once.

Soae entered her house, her heart enveloped in anxiety and fear. Her face clearly showed an expression of wanting to avoid people.

"How did it go?"
Illyŏng, who couldn't wait any longer, runs out in one leap and asks. Then, seeing the paper with the problems Soae was holding, he snatches it like lightning. On it were the answers Soae had written in pencil. After checking it, he smiles with satisfaction and looks at his sister. But Soae just remains silent.

"You got two problems wrong. There were eight problems in total, right? Two wrong out of eight..."
Illyŏng shows a pleased expression, as if she did well. But Soae still says nothing.

"This should be enough to pass," Illyŏng says, looking at his father as if seeking his approval.
"Of course! Two wrong out of eight problems should be fine, right?"
The father smiles as if giving his approval. The mother joins in, pleased.
"Alright, now just one more day tomorrow. Tomorrow is Japanese, right? No need to worry about Japanese. Since you did well in Arithmetic..." <29>

Hearing her brother's words like this, Soae, enveloped in regret and anguish, remains silent. But inwardly, the worry "Brother is happy like that, not knowing anything. What will happen tomorrow?" made her heart even more anxious.

The next day, Illyŏng, who had gone early to check the list (榜) of those who passed Arithmetic, returned around nine o'clock in a panic. The family members, who had been anxiously wondering about the result, heard his hurried footsteps and rushed out to the edge of the veranda all at once, as if it were advance notice of good news.—Illyŏng's face showed the color of disappointment, and his lips trembled.

"What happened?" the father asks first.
"Her number isn't there."
"What! Not there? You must have seen it wrong!" the father asks back frantically.
"I didn't see it wrong. I looked three times. And I even went to the office and asked."
"So, did the office say she failed?"
"Yes—but..." Illyŏng stops mid-sentence.
"But... what happened?" the father's eyes gleam.
"They said she did well on the exam, but there was a reason for failing her. So I asked for the reason, but they said it was difficult to say."

Hearing this, the family members all remain silent.
"Soae! Soae!" the father calls out in an anxious voice.
"Waaah," a sound of violent crying comes from the room across.
"What's going on!?" the mother stands up, her face pale with shock from disappointment and discouragement. —The father and Illyŏng also stand up. The indoor air of the quiet morning suddenly seems turbid, and a kind of indescribable discomfort and sorrow dominates their hearts.

Soae, leaning against the wall, collapses forward, sobbing uncontrollably. No matter what anyone says, <30> whether comforting or trying to stop her, she weeps hysterically. Her hair and shoulders tremble with each sob. Tears flow endlessly. The family members could only watch her, helpless.

Illyŏng suddenly envisioned the time he failed the Middle School Qualification Examination. Deeply sympathizing with his younger sister's current despair and sorrow, he shed tears.

Dinnertime arrived. The family members received their dinner table while dominated by gloomy feelings.
"Maid—go call the young miss..." Illyŏng, unable to wait any longer, orders the servant.

"It's terrible, sir. The young miss isn't here," the maid says frantically.
"What!" the father turns pale. —The mother and Illyŏng too—Instantly, like a flash of lightning, a premonition of some ominous dread strikes Illyŏng's chest.

They searched the room, the front yard, the backyard, and every other nook and cranny. But her shadow was nowhere to be seen. The mother runs around frantically, crying like mad.

On the quiet spring evening, only an air of anxiety envelops the entire house.
The servants and family members, their eyes bloodshot with intense emotion and excitement, searched here and there, parting the dim evening curtain, quickening their steps.—As the refreshing breeze of the soft spring night dampens people's bodies, the electric lights from houses shine brilliantly.

Around nine o'clock that night, Soae was brought back by the house servant (아범), found near Yongsan.

She didn't cry anymore. She didn't make a sound. She just lay quietly, looking blank, as if heartbroken.

The dreadful premonition that flickered through Illyŏng's brain turned out to be merely a premonition, of course. Soae had simply, mindlessly, almost unconsciously, taken one step, then another, out of the house and wandered aimlessly. —The End—<31>

**Summary**

This 1924 short story depicts the intense pressure surrounding a young girl, Soae, as she prepares for the entrance examination to a prestigious Girls' Higher Common School in colonial Korea. The narrative focuses on the anxiety experienced by Soae and her family, particularly her mother who pins high hopes and vanity on her success, even considering improper means ("請" - using connections). Soae's older brother, Illyŏng, sympathizes with her suffering, recalling his own painful exam experiences, and critiques the harshness of the system. Despite performing well on the arithmetic portion, Soae is inexplicably failed for an unstated reason (hinting at external factors or perhaps a consequence of cheating), leading to immense distress and a brief, desperate wandering episode.

From a historical perspective, the story vividly illustrates the extreme competition for limited educational opportunities in colonial Korea and the immense social pressure placed on children and families, where exam success was tied to status and future prospects. It highlights the psychological toll of the examination system, parental anxieties driven by vanity and comparison (especially rivalry with relatives), and hints at the potential for corruption or unfairness ("請," unexplained failure), reflecting the societal stresses and inequalities of the time. Historians might find the depiction of family dynamics, the explicit critique of the exam system's impact on children's well-being, and the reference to potentially using connections particularly revealing about early 20th-century Korean society under Japanese rule.

땃뜻한 日光에 싸여 마루바닥의 나무결을 나려다 보면서 문칫문칫하고 잇는 素媖이를 볼 때 母親은 빽빽 질으는 소리로 이와 가티 재촉한다.
「素媖이는 닐어나 굿챤타하는 듯한 視線으로 母親의 顔色을 보살피면서 房으로 들어온다.
「어린애가 엇지면 고러케 배포가 유하냐! 엽헤서 보고만 잇서도 답답해 숙겟구나」하면서 母親은＜15＞ 自己를 向하야 잔소리한다. 그래도 素媖이가 冊床압흘 向하야 안즐 때는 그 무슨 滿足함을 늣기는 듯이 가는(細)우슴을 兩頰에 띄운다.
집안中에서라도 母親은 누구보담도 素媖이 入學시험에 第一 熱中되여 잇다. 그럼으로 조곰만 하면 「工夫해라 工夫해라!」하면서 살이 나리도록 잔소리를 한다. 素媖이 班의 擔任先生이 시험바드러 갈 生徒들을 위하야 課外講義를 한다. 그러나 母親은 이에도 安心되지 못하야 어느 學校에 단이는 先生에게 請하야 素媖이를 1週日에 두시간식 가서 배우도록 하얏다. 실로 母親은 素媖이의 及第를 憧憬하는 無智한 熱心은 尋常이 아니엿섯다. 그에는 自己딸을 官立學校에 집어너 나종에라도 「아모개는 교사로 나가 한달에 月給을 멧 千兩식 타온대지」또는 「아모개는 누구한테 시집을 갓는데 두 兩主가 한달에 月給이 멧 千兩식 된대-」하는 이와 갓흔 普通子女를 둔 父母들과 가티 虛榮心이 가장 굿세우게 움즉엿섯다. 그러나 그 보담도 그의 度를 넘치도록 熱心을 깁게 剌戟하고 잇는 것은 自己 男便의 親동생 딸 竹媖이가 素媖이와 갓흔 동갑으로 갓흔 學校 시험을 밧게 되여 잇슴이엿다.
「두집 아해가 다 잘 뽑폇스면 조흐런만 어느 편이든지 한집에만 뽑피게 되면 엇지하나...」하면서 그는 때때로 말한다. 그러나 실상으로는 竹媖이를 素媖이가 보기조케 이겻스면...또는 竹媖이는 落第를 하야도 素媖이만은 及第가 되엿스면 하고 생각하는 것이엿다.
「그적께도 가보닛가 竹媖이는 한시를 놀지안코 工夫하고 잇드라. 너도 精神 밧짝 차려야지 지면 엇지하랴니...」하면서 母親은 素媖이를 츙동이 한다.
「어머니! 竹媖이도 뽑피고 나도 뽑피면 난 둘이 손목 붓잡고 단일테야...」하면서 素媖이는 아모 野心업는 눈을 반짝인다. 그러나 이와 갓흔 말을 듯는 母親의 顔色은 험악하야 진다. 그리고 自己 딸보담도 才質이 잇느니 어엽부니 하면서 기림(譽)이 만흔 恒常 굿세운 競爭者가 되는 竹媖이를 박차바리고 너 홀로 남의 羨仰의 目標가 되여라 하는 듯한 暗示를 준다. 그리하야 素媖이로 하야금 또다시 競爭이라 하는 不安가운데 빠저바리게 한다.＜16＞
一英이는 이와 가튼 母親의 태도를 어느 때든지 씀바구나 씸는 듯이 맘에 주름쌀을 잡피면서 바라본다.
엇던날 母親은 허둥허둥 一英이 房에 들어와 소리를 낫춰가면서 말한다.
「얘-야! 저- 請 좀 해봐라 請! 只今 貞姬집을 갓더니 貞姬어머니가 그리드라. 親한 先生이 잇거든 請을 하라고... 적은 집에서도 只今 운동하고 잇대드라」
「온! 어머님도 딱한 말삼도 하슈... 나종에는 別소리를 다...」
一英이는 불쾌한 심사를 늣기면서 자긔 母親을 바라본다.
「別소리! 別소리! 別소리가 다 무엇이냐. 그 집 애도 아마 그래서 들어갓나 보든데?」
「글세 딱한 말삼 좀 고만 하십시요」
「그래도 남들은 다 請을 한다는데 우리만 가만이 잇스면 엇지잔 말이냐?」
「그럼 집에서도 請을 하잔 말이예요?」
「글세...」母親은 우물쭈물 해바린다.
「그런 어림업는 말삼은 하시지도 마슈. 請이란 다 무엇이예요? 試驗은 實力이지요. 그리고 運數도 잇지요」하면서 一英이는 것침업시 잘너 말한다.
「네가 알기는 뭘 알겟니...」하면서 母親은 철저한 一英이 말을 도리여 冷笑한다.
「試驗보는 일은- 어머님보담 제가 더 잘 안담니다... 대관절 請까지 해서라도 素媖이를 그 學校에 꼭 늣코자 할 必要가 무엇이야요?」
母親은 理致에 당연한 自己 아들말에 고만 입을 움찔하야 바렷다.
「웨 그리 야단이냐! 소리를 빽빽 질너가면서...」
母親은 自己 무안에 취하야 고만 실쭉하야 가지고 안ㅅ방으로 행하야 거름(步)한다.
一英이는 좀 너무 不快하게 말하엿구나 하는 괴로움을 늣기면서 自己 어머니의 뒤를 바라본다.-＜17＞
자긔 어머니의 그림자가 아조 안ㅅ방으로 살어질 때 그는 자긔 사랑하는 딸을 及第식히랴 하는 그 一心으로 저와 가티 焦慮하야 請까지라도 하고자 하는 자긔 母親의 心理를 일시 욱한 성미로 限업시 비루하게 녁이엿섯스나 자긔 어머니가 힘업시 걸어가는 그 뒤ㅅ태도를 바라보는 그 瞬間에 그는 도리여 惻忣함을 늣기엿다. 올타! 조금도 無理치 안타! 저와 가티 하는 것이 當然하다! 어버이 된 사람의 맘이다. 이와 가티 一英이는 생각하엿다.
어대까지든지 學生다웁게 全히 世情에 부댁기지 못한 赤子갓흔 正義的 感情으로 「試驗은 實力이다. 그리고 運이다」하고 고집不通의 말을 하엿스나 그러나 이 實社會에는 決코 「公」그것대로만 進行되는 것은 아니다. 또한 그 學校에 아니 그 學校뿐만 아니라 모든 學校試驗에 「請」이라 하는 것이 비루한 手段이나마 어느 程度까지는 확실히 効果를 볼 수 잇다고 생각하엿다. 다만 試驗에만 限한 것이 아니라 이 世上가운데 모든 것에 「請」이 必要하다 하면서 一英이는 날감(生柿)이나 씹는 듯이 얼골을 찝푸려가며 생각한다. 동시에 돈이 좀 잇는 자긔 父親의 周圍를 싸고 잇는 一種 社會的 空氣까지도 생각난다.
「그러타! 어머님이 그와 가티 생각을 잡숫는 것도 決코 無理는 아니다. 全히 어머님 말삼과 가티 적은 집에서는 그 무엇이고 하고 잇는 듯이 생각된다.- 그럿타! 잇슬 만한 일이다!」하면서 一英이는 母親心理에 同感코자 하는 自己 心理의 一部分을 억제치 못하고 이와 가티 생각하야 보앗다. 同時에 自己 叔母의 權勢잇는 듯한 豪慢 그리고 新時代智識잇는 女子인체 하며 社交에 怜悧한 체하는 오동통한 벼락이 나려도 왼편 눈 하나 깜작이지 아니할 만한 자태가 나타나 보인다. 女學生틀에 머리로 조금 검은 듯한 살빗에 두 뺌 늘어진 34,5歲의 얼골 그 가운대로 깜작깜작하는 누구의 視線이라도 끌지안코는 마지 안는다 하는 듯한 두 낫 愛嬌잇는 가는(細) 눈, 이 눈으로 맘에서는 꿈도 아니꾸는 눈초리 우슴을 띄우며 追從하는 듯한 語調로 말솜씨잇게 하는 自己 叔母의 자태- 一英이는 이를 볼 때마다 不快한 심사를 억제치 못하엿다. 飽滿해 빠진 生活에서 일어나는 게으름에 이기지 못하는 듯한 生活의 倨慢과 虛榮 모든 詐謀에 부댁끼고 부댁낀 듯한 態度- 이 모든 조건이 「운동을 하는 中이다」하는 句節에 얼마나＜18＞ 一致되는가를 一英이는 推側하야 보앗다. 그리고 딸의 얄밉도록 조금도 어린 아해들의 잇는 란만한 귀염성이 업는 竹媖이 (勿論 자기叔母가 그와가티 敎養하지만)에게 落第하야 바렷스면 하는 自己 母親과 갓흔 感情을 일으키는 同時에 自己 母親의 비루한 태도에까지 그는 首肯하엿다. 그러나 이와 갓흔 모든 뒤틀린 感情을 떠나 一英니는, 다만 한낫의 女동생을 위하야 眞心으로 그의 꼿다운 及第를 바라며 암츅하엿다.
「역시 現今 試驗制度의 모든 것이 根本的으로 틀려 잇다.- 少數의 學校로 엇지 저 每年 늘어가는 多數의 子女들을 收容할 수 잇스리요.- 自己 子女들을 아모조록 조흔 學校에 집어 너 주고자 하는 어버이의 希望心은 자긔 어머니나 叔母나 조금도 變함업시 일반일 것이다. 그러나 無理하게 詐謀를 手段한다든지 또는 天眞란만하게 무럭무럭 잘 아는 아해들에게 不適當한 工夫를 强制치 아니하면 아니됨은 本來 저 試驗制度라 하는 것이 잇기 때문이다. 그러타! 首肯치 못할 것은 오즉 이것이다. 그외 모든 것을 憎惡한다든지 또는 是야 非야 할 必要는 업는 것이다」하면서 一英이는 겨우 이와 갓흔 怨妄으로 모든 것을 밧구엇다.
「海の靜かなることは鏡の如く 朝日夕日を負ひて 島かくれ行く白帆の影ものどかなり。月影 のさざなみにくだけ 漁火の波間に出沒する夜景もまた... 」
이와 가티 맑은 소리도 讀本을 읽고 잇는 듯한 素媖이의 音聲이 一英이 鼓膜을 울린다. 그는 무의식간에 두귀를 기울렷다. 되읽고 되읽고 하는 소리가 들리다가 고만 뚝 끗처 바린다. 그리다가는 잠간 잇다가 또 읽기 시작한다. 그는 自己가 專門學校檢定受驗準備를 할 때 讀本가운데에서 어려운 글ㅅ자와 熟語갓흔 것을 하나식 하나식 노트에 써보든 記憶이 난다.
「아! 全心全力이로구나...」하면서 一英이는 빙그러히 우스면서 밧글 내다본다. -正月이 다 지낸 봄철다운 晴朗한 한울이 和麗하게 빗난다. 맛참 土曜日 午后의 고요한 때이다. 이 和麗한 空氣와 고요한 波動을 痛하야 이웃집에서 빠이오링 뜻는 소리 輕快하게 흘러온다. 이 餘韻이 讀本을 읽는 素媖이 목소리와＜19＞ 압서거니 뒤서거니 하면서 서로 交錯된다. 一英이는 이 두낫의 音과 聲이 각각 形造하는 世界의 區別을 생각지 아니할 수 업섯다.
「이러케 日氣가 조흔 날에는 맘대로 좀 놀려 주엇스면...」
一英이는 빠이오링소리를 떠나 素媖이의 一心으로 讀書하는 것을 가엽시 녁엿다.
長閑한 봄날까지도 素媖이를 同情하는 듯이 그의 讀書하는 窓을 고이고이 抱擁한다.

어느듯 2月이 되엿다. 눈이 부시도록 和麗하고 땃뜻한 日氣가 멧츨이고 게속하엿다.
엇더한 날 아침 밥 먹을 때이다. 어느 때나 다름업시 무릅우에 新聞을 펄처노코 보든 父親은
「허 벌서 진달내꼿이 픠엿다고 허엿는데... 올(今年)엔 時節이 매우 일은 모양이야...」「요새 날이 한 4,5일 몹시 땃듯하더니 벌서 꼿이 다 픠엿구려」하면서 母親은 對句한다.
昨年 겨울엔 눈(雪)도 적더니...」
「昨年이야 말이 겨울이지 뭘 추웟섯소... 겨울날이 모다 昨年만 갓햇스면 난 살겟습듸다」
류리窓을 통하야 보이는 뒤ㅅ뜰에 서리 마젓든 흙들도 어느듯 말넛스며 담밋헤 선 丹楓나무의 싹들도 별안간 볼눅하야진 것갓다. 사철나무가지에는 아침해ㅅ발이 땃뜻이 고요이 쪼여잇다. - 겨울날에 살을 어이는 듯이 쌀쌀하고 찬맛은 이즌듯이 집안사람들의 얼골에도 숨기랴 숨길 수 업는 봄날의 平和로운 惠澤이 낫타나 보인다.
「아버지- 나 動物園 求景식혀 줘...」하면서 一英이 끗헤 同生이 말한다.
「글세...」하면서 父親은 고개를 끄떡인다.
空日날 나하고 모다 가티 가자...」하며 母親이 말한다.
「아이구 조와!」하며 끗헤 同生은 엉딍이방아를 찌면서 슛가락 든 채 두 손을 번쩍 든다.＜20＞
처음부터 여러사람들의 이와가튼 會話를 원망스러운 듯이 黙黙히 듯고 잇든 素媖이 태도를 一英이는 자세히 보살폇다. 그 눈에는 確實히 자긔도 말참견을 하고자 하다가는 고만 움찔하고 움찔하는 焦燥한 빗이 낫타나 보인다. 一英이는 이를 벌서부터 눈치채 왓다. 그리하야 여러 사람들의 素媖이를 생각지도 아니하는 그들의 心理를 可憎히 녁엿다.
「누의는 엇지하고 너 혼자가기는 어듸를 가!」하며 그는 自己 아오를 꾸짓는 듯이 말하면서 자긔 兩親에게 핍박하는 視線을 던진다.
「참 素媖이는 엇지나...」하면서 母親은 그제야 생각나는 듯이 말하며 자긔 아들의 굿세인 視線을 피한다.
「가기는 어듸를 가. 시험工夫는 엇지고...」하면서 父親은 입을 담으러 바린다.
一座는 不意의 侵入者를 만난 듯이 어안이 벙벙하야 다시 말끗을 이으지 못하고 제각기 亂視의 視点만 바라보고 잇다.
다숙우니 고개를 숙이고 무릅우만 바라보고 잇든 素媖이는 별안간 훌쩍훌쩍 늣기기 시작한다. 一英이는 고개를 向하야 가늘게 떨리는 연약한 그의 두 억개를 바라본다. -웬셈인지 自己까지도 가티 울고 십흔 「센듸멘달」한 心理가 全身을 음습하야 온다. 그는 오른편 손을 들어 그의 억개에 언지며 語音이 흘인 목소리로 慰勞한다.
「우지말아. 넌 나하고 집에서 工夫하자. 及第하면 어듸는 못가겟는니- 뭘 이번에는 꼭 及第할 터인데 及第만 하면 及第만...」

入學試驗의 第1日 算術이다. 집안사람들은 맛치 처음으로 戰場에 나가는 戰士를 보내는 듯함으로 素媖이를 激勵식힌다.
「소영아! 허둥대지 말라. 착은착은이 問題를 자세히 읽어보고 천천히 答案을 써라. 그리고 時間이 다 되기까지는 내놋치 말고 보고보고 또 보고 한 후에 내노아라. 남들은 상관말고... 試驗은 허둥대지 안는 것이 第一이다」하면서 父親은 일너준다. 그 말속에는 子息에 대한 어버이의 굿세고 힘잇는 熱誠이 잇다.
「아는 것부터 먼저하고 모르는 것은 나죵에 생각해라. 방고는 박궈해도 괜찬으니... 그리고 아모리 낫선 애들이 만트라도 저애들은 다 나만 못하겟거니하고 맘을 단단히 먹어라」하면서 一英이도 熱心으로 일너준다.
素媖이는 一一히 点頭한다. 그러나 그 눈에는 오히려 不安의 빗이 가득하다.＜27＞
「잘해라!」하며 母親은 다시 말을 못이루고 그 입살을 간을게 痙攣된다.
素媖이가 「네」하는 最後의 대답을 하면서 門間을 向하야 나갈 때 모든 사람의 視線은 一齊히 그뒤에 모듸엿다. 맛치 及第되기를 暗祝하는 듯이...

「もうあと 10分!」하는 監督先生의 굴근 목소리가 素媖이 귀를 過敏하게 울리엿다.
素媖이는 全身에 冷水를 맛는 듯이 고개를 번쩍 들어 冷靜한 敎師의 얼골을 바라보앗다.
여들 問題에서 여섯까지는 아모 수고업시 해바렷다. 그러나 3番과 7番- 이 두 問題에 素媖이는 처음부터 勞心焦思하엿다. 벌서 60人이나 되는 受驗生들은 過半이나 나갓다.- 南便 류리窓을 通하야 땃뜻한 아침 日光을 밧는 교요한 敎室안에는 이곳저곳 不規則하게 이십여명의 남저지 受驗生이 잇다. 鉛筆 문질으는 소리와 지침하는 소리가 異常스럽게도 天井에 反響되여 緊張한 空氣를 剌戟한다.- 素媖의 가슴은 울넝거리며 붓을 잡은 손은 떨린다.
「大工四人の七日間の賃金は...」하면서 3番問題를 읽어보는 素媖이 눈에는 「內法長さ二十糎 幅十五糎...」하고 별안간 7番問題로 뛰여갓다. 이와 가티 두 問題를 가지고 이것도 생각하다가 그것이 잘 아니되면 저리로 뛰여 생각하야 본다. 그러나 맘은 焦燥하야지며 얼골은 醉하야만 질뿐이요 머리속이 모도지 統一되지 안는다. 그는 얼골이 새빨개가지고 허둥대기 시작한다. 그 사이 구쓰소리가 나면서 한 女學生이 退室하는 모양이 눈에 빗추인다. 그의 맘은 더욱 안탑가워진다. 1秒... 10秒... 50秒 沉黙이 支配한다.
생각다 생각다 못한 素媖이는 얼ㅅ낌에 視線을 압사람에게 던졋다. 순간에 그 女學生의 남빗끗동단 소매 알에로 答案紙의 一片이 눈에 선뜻 보인다. 그는 良心의 苛責을 바드면서 곳 視線의 方向을 밧구엇다. 그러나 또 한번 보고십흔 맘을 억지하랴 억지할 수 업슬만치 츙동되엿다. 素媖이는 거위 無意識하게 敎師의 얼골을 도적해 보앗다. 그리고 다시 視線을 答案紙에 모듸면서 貪하는 듯이 보앗다. 개미가 기여가는＜28＞ 것갓흔 數字가 눈이 시도록 그의 網膜에 부듸치엿다. 이야말로 7番問題의 解答이엿다.- 沉黙이 게속되는 數十秒間 自己에게 갓가히 오는 敎師의 구쓰소리가 들리운다. 그가 이 소리에 精神차리게 될 때는 벌서 때가 느졋다. 그는 무엇에 츙동을 바든 듯이 몸을 움찔하면서 고개를 푹 숙으려 바렷다.
敎師는 素媖이 冊床압헤 서서 受驗票의 番號를 有心히 보앗다. 素媖이의 손끗은 몹시나 痙攣되며 그의 呼吸作用은 窒息되듯이 아모 소리도 업섯다.
조곰 잇다가 時刻을 報하는 鍾소리가 울린다.- 得意와 失意와 歡喜와 悲痛과 希望과 絶望과- 이갓흔 表情에 물든 남저지 受驗生의 20名가령은 일제히 起立하얏다.

素媖이는 不安과 恐怖에 싸인 가슴을 안꼬 自己집 문안을 들어섯다. 그의 얼골에는 確實히 사람을 끄리는 表情이 낫타낫다.
「엇지되엿니?」
기대리다 기다리다 못하든 一英이는 한달음에 뛰여나와 뭇는다. 그리다가 問題박은 조희를 素媖이가 들고 잇는 것을 보고 그는 번개가티 빼섯다. 거긔에는 素媖이가 鉛筆로 쓴 答案이 잇섯다. 이를 검사하야 보든 그는 滿足한 우슴을 띄우고 自己 누의를 바라본다. 그러나 素媖이는 그저 잠잠하고 잇슬뿐이다.
「두 問題틀렷구나. 모다 여들 問題지? 여들 問題서 두 問題쯤...」
一英이는 잘하엿다 하는 듯이 것분 빗을 낫타내인다. 그러나 素媖이는 역시 두말 업다.
「이만하면 及第에는 파스되엿다」하면서 一英이는 그 承認을 要求하는 듯이 父親을 바라본다.
「그럼! 여들 問題에서 두 問題쯤이야 괜찬켓지?」
부친은 承認한다 하는 듯한 우슴을 띄운다. 母親은 덩달어 조와한다.
「자아 인제 來日 한루면 고만이다. 來日은 日語지? 日語야 뭘 걱졍할 것 업다. 算術을 잘하엿스니...」＜29＞
自己 兄의 이와 갓흔 말을 듯는 素媖이는 後悔와 煩悶과에 싸이여 아모말도 업다. 그러나 內心으로는 兄님은 아모것도 몰으시고 저가티 깃버하시네. 來日이면 엇지 할까 하는 근심이 그의 맘을 더욱 안탑가이 하엿다.
그 잇튼날 일즉이 算術合格者의 榜을 보러간 一英이는 아홉시나 되여 황황히 돌아왓다. 그 結果의 엇지되엿슴을 궁금히 녁이고 잇든 집안사람들은 그의 急한 신발소리를 들을 때 맛치 吉報의 미리 알림이나 다름업시 一齊히 마루끗흐로 뛰여 나왓다.- 一英이의 顔色은 落望의 빗이 낫타나며 그의 입살은 떨린다.
「엇지됏니?」하며 父親은 爲先 뭇는다.
「그애 방고는 업세요」
「뭐! 업서? 잘못 본 게구나!」父親은 황황히 反問한다.
「잘못 본 것이 안야요. 세번이나 보앗는데요. 그리고 事務室에까지 가서 물어보앗는데요」
「그래 事務室에서 落第라드냐?」
「네- 그런데...」하다가 一英이는 말끗을 담을어 바린다.
「그런데... 엇잿단 말이냐?」부친의 眼光은 빗난다.
「試驗은 잘 봣스나 落第식힌 理由가 잇다고요. 그래 그 理由를 물어 보앗더니 그것은 말하기 어렵다고 해요」
이 말을 듯은 집안사람들은 모다 잠잡코 잇다.
「소영아! 소영아!」하면서 父親은 焦燥한 목소리로 불은다.
「으악」하고 激烈히 우는 소리가 건넛방에서 들린다.
「원셈이냐!?」하면서 母親은 失望과 落膽에 놀난 빗으로 얼골이 햇쓱하야저 가지고 닐어난다. -父親도 一英이도 딸어 닐어난다. 淸閑한 아침날 室內空氣가 별안간에 混濁하여진 듯이 一種 말할 수 업는 不快와 悲哀가 그들의 맘을 支配한다.
素媖이는 壁에 몸을 의지하엿다가 고만 업푸러지면서 흙흙 늣기여 운다. 누가 무엇이라 하든지 또한＜30＞ 慰勞하든지 말리든지 그는 狂的으로 痛哭한다. 머리털과 두 억개는 흑흙 늣기는 대로 떨린다. 눈물은 끗칠 줄을 모르고 흘은다. 여러 집안사람들은 오즉 그를 바라만 보고 잇슬 外 다른 道理가 업섯다.
一英이는 선뜻 自己가 中學校檢定試驗을 보다가 落第한 때의 생각을 幻影하엿다. 그리하야 只今 자긔 누의동생의 絶望과 悲痛에 싸임을 깁히 同情하야 눈물지엇다.
저녁밥 때가 되엇다. 家族들은 鬱鬱한 심사에 支配되여 가면서 밥상을 바덧다.
「어멈- 아씨 가 볼너 오게...」하면서 기대리다 기대리다 못한 一英이는 하인에게 命令한다.

「큰일낫슴니다. 아씨께서 안게세요」하며 어멈은 황황히 말한다.
응! 하면서 父親은 새파라진다. -母親도 一英이도- 순간에 一英이 가슴에는 번개불가티 그 무슨 상서롭지 못한 두려움의 豫感을 바덧다.
室內 압뜰 뒷뜰 그 外 구석구석이 차저 보앗다. 그러나 그의 그림자는 永永 볼 수 업섯다. 母親은 밋친듯이 날뛰면서 운다.
고요한 봄날 저녁에 오즉 不安의 空氣가 온 집안을 둘너싸고 잇다.
下人들과 집안 食口가 극렬한 感情과 興奮에 눈이 새빨개 가지고 이곳 저곳 어둠침침한 저녁 장막을 혓처 가면서 거름을 빨리하야 차저 단엿다.- 부드러운 봄날밤의 산뜻산뜻한 바람이 사람의 몸을 음습할 때 집집의 電燈불들은 휘한찬란하다.

그날 밤 아홉시나 되여 素媖이는 龍山附近에서 아범에게 붓들려 왓다.
그는 다시 울지도 아니하엿다. 소리도 내지 안엇다. 그저 오즉 傷心한 듯이 고요히 누어 멀거니 잇슬뿐이다.
一英이 腦속을 번듸기는 두려운 豫感이 단순한 豫感에 지내지 못하엿섯슴은 勿論이다. 素媖이는 다만 아모 精神업시 自身도 意識지 못하게 한거름 두거름 집문을 나서 彷徨하엿든 것이엿다. -끗-＜31＞
