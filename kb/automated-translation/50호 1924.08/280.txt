잡지명	개벽 제50호
발행일	1924년 08월 01일
기사제목	戱曲 「犧牲者」 一幕 (Play: "The Victim" One Act)
필자	金泰秀
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Play: "The Victim" One Act
						Kim T'aesu
-Characters-
Pak Yŏngjun (Young man of letters) 24 years old
Kim Myŏngja (Yŏngjun's lover) 22 years old
Yi Wŏnsang (Elementary school teacher) 40 years old
Boy (Servant)

-Time-
Present day, Early Spring, around 9 PM

-Place-
Kyŏngsŏng, a room in a certain boarding house

-Stage-
Setting - On the left is a Japanese-style sliding door (미다지문), and outside the door, a wooden floor leads downstairs. On the back wall hang an oil painting in a frame and a clock, pleasingly arranged. Against the right wall stands a large wardrobe. In front of it is a desk with books, stationery, cosmetics, etc., neatly arranged, and beneath the desk rests a violin (빠요링) in its case. A 10-candlepower electric light illuminates the splendid room.
Opening - The stage is quiet, with only the tick-tock sound of the clock audible. Myŏngja, wearing a jacket (짜켓트) above and a black skirt below, sits at the desk, looking engrossed in a book. (Pause)
Myŏngja: (Lifting her head, seemingly impressed) Ah—truly well-written... Verlaine's "L'heure du berger"... Shall I try reciting it? (Recites as if singing)
"Upon the hazy horizon, the red moon shines<39>
In a moment, the pasture fills with mist.
When all is silent in a mysterious dream,
In the reed field where the bittern cries, a tremor runs.
Water plants cover the flower petals (화편) and achieve sleep,
And the poplar trees standing far over there,
Faint, appear both orderly and dense.
In the thicket, wandering moonlight shines.
The owl, awakened, stealthily beats its silent wings
And descends into the black sky.
Look up! In the sky, shining like lightning,
Is the white-clad goddess of Venice (베네쓰의 여신). Thus is the night."
(Silence, Pause)
Myŏngja: (As if excited) Truly well-written... Perhaps I should start studying poetry (詩) from now on... (Tilts her head, thinking) Yes... And then I'll become good at writing poetry... Then publishers will flock to me, pestering me to buy my manuscripts (原稿). And if I become even better, people from foreign countries will come, wanting to translate my work, all sorts of things will happen... So I'll become a renowned female poet (녀류시인), writing poetry, while Yŏngjun writes novels, (smiles gently) forming an ideal (리상뎍) family, living an artistic life... (Shivers slightly) Oh my, if it's that enjoyable, how could I live... (As if startled) But why... hasn't Yŏngjun come even once today... Is he writing something again... (Looks at the clock) It's almost half-past nine... Should I go over there... But if I go, I hate seeing that darn lodging owner fellow... Eh, but I'll wait just five more minutes...
(Closes the book, puts it away, and sits thinking. Pause) <40>
Myŏngja: (Sighs deeply) But Mr. Wŏnsang is rather pitiable... An old man... (As if collecting herself) Why am I thinking this again... No matter how pitiful and unfortunate he is, can I just throw away my beautiful youth and future stretching like ten thousand li, and sacrifice myself for him... Besides, I already have my ideal lover, Yŏngjun... (As if frustrated) Why hasn't he come yet? (Looks at the clock) Five minutes have passed already...
(Yŏngjun enters)
Yŏngjun: (Clears his throat and opens the door) What are you doing?
Myŏngja: (Runs up and takes Yŏngjun's hand) Why are you only coming now?
Yŏngjun: Why?
Myŏngja: (Happily) I haven't seen you all day, I was dying to see you... Please sit down. (Takes Yŏngjun's hand and they sit)
Yŏngjun: You were dying from not seeing me? (Laughs)
Myŏngja: (As if resentful) Of course. Didn't you miss me, Yŏngjun? (Her face flushes [얼골에 江潮를 띈다])
Yŏngjun: I came because I missed you. But when was there time to come? I planned to come in the evening, but just then, a friend came by, so I couldn't... That's why I'm only here now.
Myŏngja: (As if satisfied) Who came?
Yŏngjun: Yes... Uh—a friend from my hometown came up and visited.
Myŏngja: I see...
(Yŏngjun lights a cigarette and thinks about something. -Pause)
Myŏngja: Talk to me.
Yŏngjun: ...
Myŏngja: (As if feeling sorry) What are you thinking about?
Yŏngjun: (As if worried) Oh, nothing really... <41>
Myŏngja: (Places her hand on Yŏngjun's leg) Then...
Yŏngjun: (As if getting angry, just puffing hard on the cigarette) Myŏngja, no matter how I think about it, I just keep feeling sorry towards Mr. Yi. (His voice trembles)
Myŏngja: Is that why you're worried?
Yŏngjun: Yes, no matter how I think about it, only feelings of apology remain. As you know, Mr. Yi loved me very much. And the fact that I was able to attend S University (S대핵) was also due to Mr. Yi's arrangement, wasn't it? Yes, but today, I heard from that friend that he recently resigned even from the school. (Sits worriedly)
Myŏngja: (As if hurt) Why do you bring that up again... And even if he resigned from school, just think about how the situation has become such that nothing more can be done...
Yŏngjun: (As if worried) Yes. I tried not to think about it again, but hearing that he even resigned from school, my heart naturally...
Myŏngja: (As if ashamed) But can I really sacrifice my entire life, burying it in tears, for his sake? And how can I bear to sever the love that binds me to you, Yŏngjun? (As if about to cry) Yŏngjun, perhaps not everything in this world goes according to one's heart... It would be so good if Mr. Yi could be happy and we could be happy too, but what can we do about something that happened because we sought happiness? And if you always worry uselessly, how can we taste joyful (질거운) love?
Yŏngjun: Joyful love? Unlike what you think, Myŏngja, I cannot think of love as joyful. Love is unspeakably bitter.—How can the heart that stole the love of the teacher who taught and loved me be joyful and pleasant? Love, in the end, is tears...
Myŏngja: (Voice trembling, expression near tears) Then what are you saying you'll do? I, too, certainly don't think of love as only joyful and pleasant. However, I also know that love is indispensable for people, especially for young men and women in their youth. Yŏngjun! Please understand this... Yes. <42>
Yŏngjun: (As if frustrated) No, it's definitely not that I don't understand love. Love is love, but my heart can't help but feel troubled...
Myŏngja: (As if crying) But can I really sacrifice my entire life for that incomprehensible man who's over forty... How can the cold blood of that old man, like a father, satisfy the passionate love of youth? Yes... (Sobs) Yŏngjun, people all live for their own individuality, so even Mr. Yi probably won't be excessively angry...
Yŏngjun: Just... (As if apologetic) Yes, Myŏngja, I love you more dearly than my own life... That's why I have this unavoidable, painful (압흔) suffering in my heart...
Myŏngja: (As if grateful) Yes, I know. That you love me incomparably, Yŏngjun. But when your heart hurts so much, Yŏngjun, what must my heart be like? If you say it hurts, my heart probably hurts twice as much. Yes, Yŏngjun— And love must transcend everything. Before love, there is no teacher, no friend, nothing at all. If anything exists, it would only be the red bodies of a man and a woman bound by the thread of love. Yŏngjun! I love you like this. If I didn't have you, Yŏngjun, I know there would be no society, no sun and moon, nothing at all for me.
Yŏngjun: (Apologetic and as if moved by something) No. It's absolutely not that I don't understand love. It's just that my friend from home came and talked about Teacher, so my heart felt a bit uneasy...
Myŏngja: (As if relieved) Yŏngjun! This is all fate that cannot be helped. That is already in the past, so let's leave the past as it is, and for the sake of our future path yet to come, let us cry if we must cry, and worry if we must worry. Yes, forever... (Bows her head and places her hand on Yŏngjun's knee)
Yŏngjun: (With a pleased expression) Yes, thank you.
Myŏngja: (Looks up at Yŏngjun and smiles) Are you thankful?
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Yes, I'm thankful. <43>
Myŏngja: (Moves closer as if to nestle in Yŏngjun's chest) Are you really thankful? (Smiles sweetly)
Yŏngjun: (Lovingly embraces Myŏngja tightly and kisses her) Yes. I'm thankful.
Myŏngja: (As if satisfied) Why are you doing this? (Pulls away slightly)
Yŏngjun: (As if just remembering) Oh, right. By the way, aren't you going to the concert tomorrow evening?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Yŏngjun, if you go, I'll go too.
Yŏngjun: What if I don't go?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Then I won't go either.
Yŏngjun: (As if satisfied) Thank you. But why aren't you performing, Myŏngja? It's hosted by the Young Women's Association...
Myŏngja: (Smiling) I don't know how to do anything.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Why don't you know? You, the self-proclaimed genius of the arts...
Myŏngja: (Presses Yŏngjun's knee firmly) Who ever said I was a genius of the arts? And why, isn't music considered part of the arts?
Yŏngjun: If it weren't, who would trust the term "artist"?
Myŏngja: (Shrinks back and moves closer) Who ever said I was an artist, why are you saying that? And why, last time, did you tease me in front of friends, calling me an artist and whatnot?
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Are you bringing up what happened last time now?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) And besides, "artist" is enough, why did you attach "momentary" on top of art and call me a "momentary artist," speaking so pointedly?
Yŏngjun (Feigning surprise) Who spoke pointedly to our Myŏngja? If I find out who it was, I'll beat them to death.
Myŏngja: (Glaring playfully) Who did it? Only you would do that, Yŏngjun. (Laughs)
Yŏngjun: (Laughing cheerfully) Well then, do you think music has the same artistic value as literature? <44>
Myŏngja: Why, is it because music is only for that moment?
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Of course, that's part of it. Since it's only "momentary." That art probably can't last long. Then consequently, doesn't it lack great power?
Myŏngja: (As if frustrated) No. Really... Because it's "momentary," isn't it direct? So you can feel it more intensely...
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) That's why, isn't it handicapped? Once you turn away, it's gone. How long will it last?
Myŏngja: (Looking even more frustrated) Oh my, really, what about the power received during that time... If you said such things in front of our school's piano teacher, Yŏngjun, you wouldn't even get a word in.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Well, we'd have to talk to find out. And why can't you speak like that, Myŏngja?
Myŏngja: (Pouting coquettishly) Fine. Are you going to be the only artist then...
Yŏngjun: (Laughing harder) Oh my, are you angry because I didn't call you an artist... I don't even know exactly what "art" is.
Myŏngja: (Pouting coquettishly) Stop it... I'm going to study "poetry" now too.
Yŏngjun: (Laughing heartily) Oh my, now Korea will have female poets too...
Myŏngja: (Smiling, frustrated) Is it wrong if we do?
Yŏngjun: No. It's not wrong. I mean I'm glad. Especially if my love becomes a poet, becomes a poet, how wonderful... (Hugs Myŏngja)
Myŏngja: (Turns away) Stop it. You're just teasing...
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Are you angry? I apologize... But have you tried writing a poem?
Myŏngja: (As if remembering something, smiles brightly and sits up straight) Ah—right, there's one I wrote to give to you, Yŏngjun.
Yŏngjun: (As if pleased) Yes. Thank you. Then please recite it for me.
Myŏngja: (Smiling) I can't recite it properly. I'll just tell it to you in plain words. <45>
Yŏngjun: Why do that? Recite it like you're taking an exam at school. Especially as a musician, not being able to memorize that...
Myŏngja: (Smiling) If you mock me like that, I won't recite it.
Yŏngjun: (As if coaxing) Who ever mocked you? Please recite it quickly. Yes?
Myŏngja: (Covers her eyes tightly with both hands) Oh my, it's embarrassing...
Yŏngjun: (As if curious) It's okay. I'm listening... (Shakes her gently, urging her on)
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Well then, if you just stare at me, it's more embarrassing, so please turn around.
Yŏngjun: No. No need to turn around, I'll just shut my eyes tightly. (Smiles and closes his eyes)
Myŏngja: (About to recite, but seems truly embarrassed) Oh really... (Leans back against Yŏngjun's chest) Okay—I'm reciting...
1. Upon the hazy horizon, the red moon shines
In a moment, the pasture fills with mist.
When all is silent in a mysterious dream,
In the reed field where the bittern cries, a tremor runs.

2. Water plants cover the flower petals and achieve sleep,
And the poplar trees standing far over there,
Faint, appear both orderly and dense.
In the thicket, wandering moonlight shines.

3. The owl, awakened, stealthily beats its silent wings
And flies into the black sky.
Look up! In the sky, shining like lightning,
Is the white-clad goddess of Venice (베네스의 여신). Thus is the night.
Myŏngja: (Smiles brightly and sits up straight) How was it? Well written, wasn't it? <46>
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) You wrote it well. Did you write it, Myŏngja?
Myŏngja: Of course.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Really?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Well, can't I write like that?
Yŏngjun: (Laughing heartily) No. It's not that you can't write. It's just because you wrote it so well... (Chuckles) Now, the genius of Verlaine (베르렌) has been born in Korea... Leaving behind Korea where a living Verlaine exists, all this time I've only longed for France (불란서), where the dead Verlaine lived... And especially that this Verlaine would be embraced in my arms...
Myŏngja: (Smiling) I know.
Yŏngjun: (Pretending not to know) Know what?
Myŏngja: (Smiling gently) That the poem is Verlaine's.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling gently) Is that poem Verlaine's?
Myŏngja: (Presses Yŏngjun's knee firmly) You knew, but you were just teasing me... (Glances up and smiles)
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Teasing? Who's teasing? Ah—didn't you say you wrote it, Myŏngja?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Stop it. (Turns away)
Yŏngjun: (Turns Myŏngja back to face him) Then you should have said you didn't write it... By the way, did you buy Verlaine's poetry collection?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Yes... I bought it on my way from school.
Yŏngjun: (Lovingly) Why, do you find poetry interesting?
Myŏngja: Yes. It's interesting.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) Now you're entering the world of "literature and arts" (문예), I see.
Myŏngja: (Pouting coquettishly) By the way, haven't you written anything recently?
Yŏngjun: Yes... Nothing much written. <47>
Myŏngja: Oh, don't hide it, if you have something, please show me.
Yŏngjun: (Takes Myŏngja's hand, lovingly) Do you want to see it?
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Of course, don't I want to see it? The work of art woven from my lover's blood!
Yŏngjun: (Lovingly holds Myŏngja and kisses her. Pause) There's nothing much written... Only something I wrote at the request of a magazine company...
Myŏngja: (Happily) Then, please show it to me. Did you bring it?
Yŏngjun: No.
Myŏngja: What is it?
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) A short story...
Myŏngja: Then please go get it and show me.
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) I'll bring it tomorrow. Look at it later. (Takes Myŏngja's hand)
Myŏngja: (As if intensely curious) Oh, really! Is it long?
Yŏngjun: Yes, it's a short story, but quite long.
Myŏngja: (Moves closer in front of Yŏngjun) Then, let's go together and bring it.
Yŏngjun: What... It's not even written very well...
Myŏngja: (As if unable to bear the curiosity) Oh, please show it to me. Really... Yŏngjun, you're so heartless... (Glances sideways at Yŏngjun and smiles) I even recited a poem for you...
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) If you want to see it that much. I'll go get it and show you!
Myŏngja: (Happily gestures) Shall I go too?
Yŏngjun: (Takes Myŏngja's hand and stands up) No, I'll go alone and come back. Women, when they say they're walking, walk their hardest but always end up standing in the same place...
Myŏngja: (Smiling) Do I walk like that? <48>
Yŏngjun: (Smiling) No, Myŏngja, you fly around.
(Myŏngja follows Yŏngjun out onto the wooden floor)
Myŏngja: (Holding Yŏngjun's hand) Bring it right back.
Yŏngjun: (Putting on his shoes) Yes. I'll be right back.
Myŏngja: How many minutes will you be?
Yŏngjun: (Thinking) Yes... I'll be back in fifteen minutes.
Myŏngja: (As if unable to let go, shakes her body) Then make sure you come back within that time.
Yŏngjun: Yes...
(Yŏngjun exits. Myŏngja enters the room and sits down. Pause)
Myŏngja: (Looking at her wristwatch) Only one minute now. Pause) Eh... two minutes. (As if curious) How can I wait for thirteen minutes? I should have gone along with him... Really. I hate being away from Yŏngjun even for a moment... Now three minutes...
(Boy enters, guiding Yi Wŏnsang)
Boy: (Comes to the wooden floor and points to Myŏngja's room) She's in this room.
Wŏnsang: (Nods to the Boy)...
Myŏngja: (Looks attentively at the sliding door) Who is it? Yŏngjun? (Stands up)
(Boy exits)
Wŏnsang: (Opens the sliding door and steps in)
Myŏngja: (Her face turns pale)... When did you arrive...
Wŏnsang: (Panting as if out of breath) What... (Suppressing indignation) Ung... Myŏngja... I saw the letter Myŏngja sent... Is that truly a letter sent by Myŏngja?
Myŏngja: (As if ashamed) Yes... Please forgive me... (Bows her head and collapses) (Pause)
Wŏnsang: (A look of disappointment and anger appears) Ah—you dirty woman... Do you really mean you've abandoned me, Myŏngja? Ung, Myŏngja! Do you know how I have suffered <49> for you all this time?
Myŏngja: (Shedding tears) Yes—I know—everything. But—but I unfortunately had no choice but to abandon you. Yes, please forgive me.
Wŏnsang: (As if dumbfounded) Ah—you heartless woman, do you know how much pain and anguish I have endured for you all this time...? Saying you were undertaking the noble pursuit of studying, I, out of love for you, enabled your studies, and now you turn around and... (As if crying) abandon me? Do you know how I've treated even your parents all this time, you heartless woman... (Tears flowing, as if pleading) Myŏngja... Myŏngja. I am an endlessly pitiful and lonely person... Ung, Myŏngja.
Myŏngja: (Sobbing emotionally) Yes... Wŏnsang. For three long years, I have been indebted to you, and that my parents too caused you much trouble (폐), I cannot bear to forget even if I close my eyes. But... but can I really bear to sacrifice my life, which stretches before me like ten thousand li? Yes, that's why, knowing you, who have shown me deep kindness, are pitiful, I had no choice... Wŏnsang, please forgive me. Yes...
Wŏnsang: Forgive? What is forgiveness...
Myŏngja: (Sobbing sadly) Wŏnsang, I will now speak of everything that has passed. I was nineteen when I, so to speak, married you. Coerced (강권) by my parents and persuaded by your kind words, I, knowing nothing, ended up calling you "husband." But, Wŏnsang, how can one endure a life without love? That's why, there wasn't a single day when I didn't shed tears or sigh. However, I only cursed my unfortunate fate, weeping alone and suffering alone, but as the days and months passed, the sorrow ingrained (뻬헤 맷친) in my bones only grew deeper. Then I thought, perhaps if I learned my beloved music well, I could find solace for the choking (목매치는) sorrow of my life through it... And so I ended up causing (낏처) you so much pain... But... (Speaks emphatically) Wŏnsang, now I have realized for certain that I too can live enjoyably if I choose to. And... having realized that, how can a person not seize the happiness right before their eyes? <50> Yes... Wŏnsang! This is all a fate that humans cannot control. Please forgive me.
Wŏnsang: (A look of disappointment and sorrow appears) Myŏngja... Now I understand clearly... But Yŏngjun is the young man I love most. For the sake of my beloved Yŏngjun, and for Myŏngja, caught (붓잡힌) in an inescapable fate, for the future stretching like ten thousand li for both of them, this aging body, like the sun setting behind the western mountains, should become a victim (犧牲者) and offer blessings (측복), but Myŏngja (as if pleading) Myŏngja... I am a pitiful soul who truly cannot live without Myŏngja... Ung, Myŏngja...
Myŏngja: (Crying) Yes, Wŏnsang, I know that you are endlessly pitiful and unfortunate, but really... Please forgive me. Yes.
Wŏnsang: (Feeling extreme disappointment and sorrow) Then, Myŏngja, you mean you cannot love me again... (Looks at Myŏngja and sobs)
Myŏngja: ... (Sobs)
Wŏnsang: (Tears flowing) Myŏngja... In this world, I have nothing more to hope for and nothing more to believe in. In this desolate, vast universe, I am an aging, rejected, pitiful, and lonely soul with no one to shed even a single drop of tear for me! There's nothing more to say, but finally, I have one request for Myŏngja. If Myŏngja feels sorry for me, will you grant this one request, even for the sake of old affection, ung?
Myŏngja: (Collecting herself) Yes, please tell me.
Wŏnsang: Then, Myŏngja, look at me... Ung (gestures). Give me that warm (땃듯하고) and fragrant (향긔로운) kiss one last time. And since our situation is mutually unavoidable, for Myŏngja's sake and for my sake in this painful world (Takes out a knife hidden in his bosom and holds it), my final wish is that we die together with this knife. (Looks intently at Myŏngja, as if resolved)
Myŏngja: (Startled) Since you say it's a final request, I will listen to whatever you say, but... I truly cannot bear to die, leaving behind my beautiful youth and my beloved lover. Yes, please forgive me.
Wŏnsang: (Suddenly enraged) What? Cannot die... Then never mind, I will kill you for you. (Takes the knife and lunges towards Myŏngja)
Myŏngja: (Hastily dodges) Ah—
Wŏnsang: (Extreme anger and sorrow) What, demon... <51>
(As Wŏnsang raises the knife and lunges to stab Myŏngja, the door silently slides open, and Yŏngjun enters)
Myŏngja: (Dodges and falls at Yŏngjun's feet) Ah—Yŏngjun! (Sobs emotionally)
(Yŏngjun, startled by the utterly unexpected scene, panics, and the manuscript (原稿) he was holding falls and scatters over Myŏngja's head. Wŏnsang, further enraged, lunges towards Yŏngjun with the knife)
Yŏngjun: (Panicked, face pale, throws his hands out) Ah—Teacher!
(Wŏnsang stops in his tracks, the knife he was holding silently drops to the ground)
Wŏnsang: (A look of extreme disappointment and sorrow appears) Ah—my beloved Yŏngjun! I can't bear to...
(Wŏnsang, shedding tears, quietly and lovingly embraces Yŏngjun)
(Quietly, the Curtain falls)
-Written March 1924-<52>

**Summary**

The one-act play "The Victim" depicts a tense love triangle in colonial Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul). Kim Myŏngja eagerly awaits her lover, the young writer Pak Yŏngjun. Their reunion is filled with affection but also shadowed by guilt over Yi Wŏnsang, Myŏngja's older former benefactor (and implied husband figure) and Yŏngjun's respected former teacher, whom they have abandoned for their modern love. After Yŏngjun briefly leaves to fetch a manuscript, Wŏnsang arrives. He confronts Myŏngja with heartbreak and anger, recounting his sacrifices and her betrayal. Feeling utterly desolate, he proposes a final kiss followed by a double suicide with a knife. When Myŏngja refuses to die, he tries to kill her, but Yŏngjun returns at the critical moment. Seeing Yŏngjun, Wŏnsang's rage dissolves into sorrow, he drops the knife, and embraces his former student.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the play vividly portrays the clash between traditional obligations/filial piety (represented by Wŏnsang's sacrifices and expectations) and the burgeoning ideals of modern individualism and romantic love embraced by the younger generation (Myŏngja and Yŏngjun). Historians might find the depiction of the "new woman" (Myŏngja pursuing education, personal happiness, and Western culture like Verlaine's poetry) and the "new man" (Yŏngjun as an aspiring modern writer) negotiating complex emotional and ethical terrain particularly interesting. The play reflects the anxieties and social transformations occurring in urban colonial Korea, including the adoption of Western cultural elements (music, literature, clothing like the '짜켓트') and the painful consequences of breaking from established social norms, framing the older generation as a potential "victim" of this shift.

元相.  (눈물 흘리며) 명자... 나는 이 세상에 다시 바랠 것도 업고 미들 것도 업는, 쓸쓸한 넓은 천디에, 누가 나를 위하야, 눈물 한 방울이라도, 흘려 줄 사람이, 업는 늙어가고, 배척바든 불상하고 외로운 몸이여! 다시 더 말할 것도 업지만 최후로, 명자에게 한 가지 원할 것이 잇스니, 만일 명자가 나를 불상히 안다면 옛날의 정으로라도, 이 말 한 마듸를 들어 주겟나, 응?
明子.  (정신을 차리며) 네, 말삼해 주서요.
元相. 그러면, 명자, 나를 봐... 응(손짓을 하며) 내에게 최후로 그 땃듯하고 향긔로운 키쓰를 한번 해주어. 그리고 피차 서로 엇더케 할 수 업는 사정이니까 괴로운 이 세상에 명자는 명자를 위하고, 나는 나를 위해서라도(가삼에서 품엇든 칼을 끄내여 쥐며) 이 칼로 가티 죽어주기를 최후로 바라는 것이여.(결심한 듯이 명자를 주의해 처다본다)
明子.  (놀내며) 최후 부탁이라고 하시니까 무슨 말이든지 듯겟슴니다만은... 저는 참아. 꼿다운 청춘과 사랑하는 애인을 남겨두고는, 죽지를 못하겟서요. 네, 용서해 주서요.
元相.  (별안간 분노하며) 무엇? 못죽는다... 그러면 그만두어 내가 죽여줄 테니까.(칼을 가저 명자에게 달녀들냐고 한다)
明子.  (황급하야 몸을 피한다) 아-
元相.  (극도의 분노와 비애) 무엇, 악마...＜51＞
(元相은 칼을 들고 명자에게 달녀들어, 찔을냐고 하는 刹那, 문이 시르르 열니며 永俊 登場)
明子.  (몸을 빼여. 영준의 발밋헤 업더지며) 아- 영준씨!(늣겨 운다)
(永俊. 넘어나, 意外임에 놀내며, 황겁하야 손에 가젓든 原稿가, 떠러저 명자의 머리우에 헛터진다. 원상이 더욱 분노하야 칼을 가지고 영준에게로 달녀든다)
永俊.  (황겁하야 얼골이 하얘지며, 두손을 짝- 버리고) 아- 선생님!
(元相. 발길을 멈추며, 쥐엿든 칼이 시르르 땅에 떠러진다)
元相.  (극도의 실망과 비애의 빗이 나터나며) 아- 사랑하는 영준아! 참아...
(元相. 눈물을 흘리며. 고요히 영준을. 사랑스럽게 끼여안는다)
(고요히 幕)
