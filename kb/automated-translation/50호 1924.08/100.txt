**Body Text Translation:**
World Affairs in July, from June 21st to July 10th

June 21st
In Kyŏngsŏng, the All-Chosŏn Women's Tennis Tournament hosted by the Tong-a Ilbo Sa was held. Japan's Seiyū Hontō party acclaimed Mr. Tokonami Takejirō as its president. Japan's Minister to China, Mr. Yoshizawa, returned to Japan to hear the government's opinion on Japan-Russia negotiations. In Ssangbu-dong, Kokha-myŏn, Kanggye-gun, an Independence Group shot dead the two sons of the pro-Japanese Korean Paek Pong-gi, then set fire to the family house. In Ch'osan-myŏn, Ch'osan-gun, an Independence Group appeared, collected military funds, and cut telephone lines.

June 22nd
Mr. Yasuhiro Tomoichirō was newly appointed as president of Japan's South Manchuria [Railway], replacing Mr. Kawamura Takeji who resigned. At Ant'aeryŏng, Kangsŏ-myŏn, Chinch'ŏn-gun, there was a battle between 6 armed independence soldiers and police; T'aech'ŏn station chief Honda Chika[?] and one other policeman were shot dead. In Chinyŏng, Kimhae-gun, a church building collapsed, causing 1 immediate death and injuring over 10 seriously or lightly. Communist Party member Yu Si-hyŏn, arrested by Japanese police in Changchun, was escorted to Sinŭiju.

June 23rd
The British and French Prime Ministers reached an agreement on the implementation of the reparations plan for Germany. Simultaneously, both prime ministers issued a recommendation to the German chancellor, pointing to Germany's internal affairs, to permit arms inspections by the Allied Powers. In Kyŏngsŏng, the entire student body of Paehwa Girls' School started a movement for the school's elevation in status. The Government-General of Chosŏn dispatched an additional 180 police officers for border defense.

June 24th
Over 700 students of Hwimun Higher Common School in Kyŏngsŏng, having no choice due to the school authorities' stubbornness, submitted a petition for collective withdrawal from the school. Japan proposed to China the retention of its rights to the Chinese Eastern Railway, but China refused. The Japan Federation of Christian Churches held a rally to stop the US Anti-Japanese Bill.

June 25th
At Ip'yŏng, Chasŏng-gun, there was a fierce battle between an armed independence group and police, resulting in injuries to 2 policemen. Various agencies of Russia's Far Eastern Revolutionary Committee and the Communist Party's Far East headquarters moved from Chita to Khabarovsk. France decided to recognize Russia. In Kongbung-myŏn, Kanggye-gun, 6 members of an independence group attacked a graphite mine (黑鉛鑛) managed by the Japanese Kimoto Munesumi [?].

June 26th
The Italian Senate passed a vote of no confidence in the government by 225 votes to 21. Students at the Agricultural School in Ch'ŏngnyangni, Kyŏngsŏng, were all suspended indefinitely due to disturbances arising from the issue of revising English class hours.

June 27th
In Ōsaka, Japan, about 1,300 employees of the Hanshin Electric Railway began a strike over the issue of improving working conditions. The Press and Assembly Suppression Impeachment Association rose up again, resolving to reform its committee and issue a declaration (declaration text publication prohibited). A commerce treaty was signed between Japan and Belgium. At the US Democratic Party convention, it was decided that if the Democratic Party president or a majority of Democratic Party members were elected, Philippine independence would be granted within 4 years.

June 28th
The principals of 12 secondary schools in Kyŏngsŏng held a meeting and issued a recommendation to the principal of Hwimun School not to resign. In Japan, the 49th extraordinary session of the Diet convened. The Korean Proletarian Social Alliance Association in Ōsaka, Japan, also held a speech rally impeaching the suppression of press and assembly. In Shanghai, a secret society, the KKK Tri-Party [?] (KKK 3당), which had existed, was shut down by Chinese authorities.

June 29th
In Naehadong, Sŏ-myŏn, T'aech'ŏn-gun, Self-Defense Group (自衛團) member Kim Chŏng-dae and 9 others were shot dead by an independence group. In Samsandong, Pukchin-myŏn, Unsan-gun, 6 members of an independence group were collecting military funds and abducted Yi Ŭng-nyong, a student studying in Tōkyō, mistaking him for the son of a wealthy man.

June 30th
Chosŏn's Vice Governor-General Ariyoshi submitted his resignation. Germany sent a notice to the Allied Powers agreeing to manage armaments thoroughly. In Tŏkhŭngdong, Tongha-myŏn, Yongch'ŏn-gun, there was a battle between 6 members of an independence group and 70 police officers; 3 independence group members died instantly, and <54> 3 police officers were injured. On the same day, near Yongamp'o, 2 policemen were shot dead by an independence group.

July 1st
The problematic US Anti-Japanese Immigration Act was implemented today. In Tōkyō, Japan, because it was the implementation day of the US Anti-Japanese Act, a national rally was held in protest against Japan, with demonstration processions and the resolution and propagation of 6 articles: (1. Do not forget the nation's eternal shame for the sake of profit. 2. Sternly discipline those who imitate the US. 3. Do not employ Americans. 4. Use domestic products as much as possible. 5. Work hard, even for a moment. 6. Avoid luxury and frivolity.)
At midnight on the same day, an incident occurred where an unidentified person entered the US Embassy in Tōkyō, lowered the US flag, and fled, causing a major issue between Japan and the US. The government held an emergency cabinet meeting to discuss countermeasures. On the same day, the Far Eastern Peoples' Congress was held in Ōsaka. China's Sun [Bao]qi cabinet resigned en masse. The Chinese diplomatic corps refused Russia's demand to hand over the former Russian legation. Japan's House of Representatives unanimously resolved to oppose the Anti-Japanese Immigration Act.

July 2nd
Shimooka Chūji, formerly the secretary-general of the Kenseikai party, was appointed as the new Vice Governor-General of Chosŏn. In Illyangdong, Samp'ung-myŏn, Chasŏng-gun, 2 members of an independence group died in battle. The culprit who lowered the US flag in Tōkyō on the 1st was arrested in Ōsaka (Red Purge Prevention Corps member Tamada Yoshiteru [?] 1 person, Okada Rihei, 21 years old). In Kŭksŏngdong, Sŏ-myŏn, Hŭich'ŏn-gun, 7 members of an independence group appeared and cut security telephone lines. Japan's elder statesman Matsukata Masayoshi passed away (age 90). China's Gu Weijun (V.K. Wellington Koo) was appointed acting premier.

July 3rd
A commerce treaty was concluded between Russia and Persia. Several hundred farmers in Tongsan-ni, Iksan-gun, protesting their inability to cultivate due to drought, caused a major disturbance by destroying the embankment of the Oksu-ri Irrigation Association (水利組合). An agreement was reached between China and Germany, the contents of which are: 1. China will return private property of Germans confiscated during the Great European War to the respective owners. 1. Germany will transfer railway bonds to China to compensate for damages suffered by China due to the war. 1. Formal reopening of the Deutsch-Asiatische Bank (獨亞銀行) in Beijing and Hankou, etc.

July 4th
In Oakland, USA, an American threw a bomb at the store of Hayashi Hana [林花 - Japanese name?] / Im Hwa [Korean name?]. 95 members of both houses of the Chinese Parliament jointly notified the French Prime Minister to abolish aggressive policies.

July 5th
At Ch'ŏnwi-san, Choyang-myŏn, Hamhŭng-gun, there was a battle between 6 members of an independence group and 4 police officers. Parents of 400 students from Kwangju Higher Common School, involved in a school suspension (學停) issue, held a parents' meeting and negotiated with school authorities but failed; the parents' association resolved to hold a provincial citizens' rally. China's Luo Huiqing was tasked with forming a cabinet. 600,000 construction workers (建築職工) in Britain began a general strike.

July 6th
At Namch'angha, Kogwan-myŏn, Ŭiju-gun, 1 member of an independence group died in battle. The Chinese government demanded the abolition of extraterritoriality from the Japanese government.

July 7th
Japan's three factions for protecting the constitution (護憲 3파) unanimously passed a reform plan for the House of Peers (貴族院). In Guangdong, China, the Nationalist Party (國民黨) of the Sun Yat-sen faction was established. Pak Che-ho, who had been imprisoned for the *Sinch'ŏnji* writing incident (筆禍件), had been released on bail for medical treatment due to illness, but medicine proving ineffective, passed away (永眠) at the age of 26. At the Sakakibara Farm in North Lingshen [? - 陵榊原], South Manchuria, Chinese soldiers invaded, causing a riot that left 30 Korean farmers seriously or lightly injured.

July 8th
British Prime Minister MacDonald went to Paris to meet with the French Prime Minister. At the Taegu District Court, the trial began for 9 individuals including Ch'oe Yun-dong and Yi Su-yŏng regarding the Kyŏngbuk Incident. Japan, Britain, and France protested to China regarding the Guangdong government's seizure of salt tax (鹽稅押收). The Chinese government submitted a request to Japan, Britain, the US, and France to elevate their legations to embassies. A mutual protection alliance was concluded between Italy and Czechoslovakia.

July 9th
A major revolution broke out in Brazil, South America; over 400 revolutionaries suffered casualties at the hands of government troops. In Amt'aedo, Muan-gun, 600 farmers gathered before the Mokp'o District Court Branch Office for 13 people imprisoned due to a tenancy dispute (소작쟁의), starting a starvation pact (餓死同盟).

July 10th
The problematic *Sidae Ilbo* newspaper was temporarily suspended as its publishing rights were transferred to the Poch'ŏn'gyo religion. <55>

**Summary**

This article provides a day-by-day account of significant events from June 21st to July 10th, 1924, covering Korea, Japan, China, and international affairs. Key themes include armed activities by Korean independence groups against Japanese authorities and collaborators, political developments in Japan (new party leadership, Diet session, response to US immigration law) and China (cabinet resignation, negotiations with foreign powers), international tensions (US-Japan relations strained by the Exclusion Act, Anglo-French talks on German reparations, Sino-German agreement), and social unrest such as student protests and school status demands in Korea, labor strikes in Japan and Britain, and farmer protests in Korea. For historians, this chronicle offers valuable details on the persistent Korean resistance movement, the colonial administration's responses (increased policing), the interconnectedness of regional politics (e.g., impact of US policy on Japan and subsequent reactions), and the social ferment occurring across East Asia and beyond during this period. The specific incidents of independence fighter actions and the reprisals offer a ground-level view of the conflict.

6월 21일
京城에는 東亞日報社 주최의 全鮮女子庭球대회가 개최되고 日本의 政友본당은 床次竹二郞씨를 總裁도 추대하고 日 駐中公使 芳澤씨는 日露交涉上 정부의 의견을 聽取키 위하야 귀국하고 江界郡 曲河面 雙富洞에서는 獨立團이 친일선인 白鳳基의 二子를 총살한 후 仝人家에 放火하고 楚山郡 楚山面에는 獨立團이 출현하야 군자금을 취하고 電線을 단절하다.

同 22일
日本의 滿錢 사장 川村竹治氏 사임한 代에 安廣伴一郞씨가 신임되고 秦川郡 江西面 安泰嶺에서 무장독립군 6명 對 경관의 교전이 有하야 泰川署長 本田親측 외 巡査 1명이 총살되고 金海郡 進永에서는 예배당이 붕괴되야 1명 즉사 10여명 중경상을 出하고 長春에서 日警官에게 체포된 공산당원 柳時彦은 新義州로 押來되다.

同 23일
英佛爾首相은 회견의 결과-獨逸에 대한 배상안실시에 일치되고 동시에 兩수상은 獨수상에게 獨逸의 내정을 指하야 군비검사를 연합국에 許하라는 권고를 발하고 京城의 培花女學校학생 일동은 학교승격운동을 起하고 朝鮮總督府는 국경방비로 경관 180명을 증파하다.

同 24일
京城의 徽文高普校 학생 700여명은 학교당국의 頑强에 부득이하야 일제 퇴학청원을 提하고 日本은 東支鐵道 이권보류를 中國에 제의한 바 中國은 거절하고 日本 기독교연합회는 米排日案 沮止대회를 開하다.

同 25일
慈城郡 梨坪에서 무장독립단 對 경관의 맹렬한 교전이 有하야 巡査 2명이 부상하고 露國의 極東혁명위원회의 諸機關 及 공산당 極東본부는 知多로부터 「하바로스크」에 이전하고 佛國은 露國을 승인하기로 결정되고 江界郡 公北面에서는 독립단 6명이 日本人 機本宗澄이 경영하는 黑鉛鑛을 습격하다.

同 26일
伊國上院은 21표 대 225표로 정부 불신임안을 가결하고 京城 淸凉里의 農業學校 학생은 영어시간 개정문제로 동요가 起하야 학생전부가 무기정학을 당하다.

同 27일
日本 大阪에는 阪神電鐵 종업원 1,300여명이 대우개선문제로 파업이 시작되고 언론집회압박  탄핵회는 재차 분기하야 위원개선 及 선언을 결의하고 (선언문 게재금지) 日本 及 白耳義간에 통상조약이 조인되고 米國 민주당대회에서는 민주당의 대통령 又는 민주당의원이 다수로 되는 시에는 4개년 내에 此律賓 독립을 許하기로 가결되다.

同 28일
京城의 12중등학교장은 회의를 開하야 徽文학교장에게 사직말나는 권고를 발하고 日本에는 第49회 임시의회가 개정되고 日本 大阪의 朝鮮無産者社會聯盟會에서도 언론집회압박에 대한 탄핵연설대회를 開하고 上海에는 비밀결사 KKK 3당이 有하든 바 中國관헌에게 봉쇄되다.

同 29일

同 30일
朝鮮 정무총감 有吉씨는 사표를 제출하고 獨逸은 연합국 측에 軍備관리를 周到히 해달나는 승낙통지를 발하고 龍川郡 東下面 德興洞에서는 독립단 6명 對 경관 70명의 교전이 有하야 독립단 3명은 즉사하고 경관＜54＞ 3명은 부상하고 仝日 龍岩浦 부근에서는 巡査 2명이 독립단에게 총살되다.

7월 1일

仝一 零時에 東京주재 米國대사관에 괴한 1명이 입하야 米國旗를 인하 도주한 사건이 발하야 日米간의 대문제가 起하야 정부는 臨時閣議를 開하야 선후책을 講하고 仝日 大阪에 極東민족대회가 有하고 中國의 孫*琦 내각은 總辭職하고 中國외교단은 露國의 舊공사관 인도 요구를 거절하고 日本의 衆議院은 만장일치로 排日法案 반대를 결의하다.

同 2일
