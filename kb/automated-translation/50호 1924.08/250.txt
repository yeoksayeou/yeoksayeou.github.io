The Fallen Curtain
						Chu Yohan
The Fallen Curtain
Malleville Haller

Constantly, without cease, yellow leaves, red leaves, brown
Tree leaves fall in the clear autumn sun.
Swirling away, yellow leaves, red leaves, brown tree leaves.
They fall upon the curtain that covered his sleeping place.

Days came and went, and autumn passed completely.
Even in winter, the curtain did not fall over in the wind.
Onto the wooden planks where his bare feet had been
Snow, swirling white, rushed in.

Burned by sunlight, rotted by rain,
The ropes, frozen and thawed fifty times,
Tightening when it rained, loosening when the sun shone,
The ropes that held the curtain broke one by one.

One harsh winter night, with a loud noise,
The wind came and tore off the roof.
The entire curtain fell over onto the snow.
When its remaining ropes and rags appeared in March
From the snowdrift, mixed with dirt and crumpled—

Is even the shadow of that curtain remaining?<9>
Who would know that this grey heap
Was the safe home of a rosy-cheeked boy during the summer?

GHOST
Byrno Morconnier

Bremton! Bremton! Of olden days,
Bremton's old house, white, white snow.
The water was blue, the sky too was blue—
That Bremton, I can never forget.

Bremton, the small port, is a narrow land
Where green trees and green groves built "Tess." (?) *Translator's note: '「테쓰」를 지은' unclear, could refer to building terraces or perhaps a specific structure/name "Tess".*
From east and west in Bremton, where even birds fly low,
Tired ships come in to rest in Bremton.

Clover grown at the gate, a side path off the main road,
In the grass, the low voice of a spirit is heard.
No shadow of firelight in the windows—no laughter through the trees—
In the flowerless garden, only one black persimmon tree stands.

Bremton, Bremton, of olden days,
Bremton's old house, white, white snow.
The grass was green, the sky too was blue—
That Bremton, I will never forget.<10>

The Young Nun (女僧)
Elith Looinis

The young nun dressed in a white robe
Looks up at the clear daytime sky and says,
"The only blue color this body possesses
Is nothing but my pupils."

The young nun dressed in a grey robe,
Facing a beautiful flower, says,
"The beauty this body possesses
Is nothing but my face."

The young nun in a pitch-black robe
Looks at the red setting sun, sighs, and says,
"When I die and go to the afterlife,
I shall possess clouds like flames."
(Translated May 1924)

This is a translation of a few lines from contemporary American poets.
Amy Lowell, who was famous for the "Imagist" movement, has already entered the ranks of the masters. The authors presented here still belong to the next era. I hope this becomes a refreshment for our reading circle, breathless from the mood of "Decadence." (Chu Yohan)<11>
<9-11>

**Summary**

This article presents Korean translations by Chu Yohan of three short poems: "The Fallen Curtain" by Malleville Haller, "Ghost" by Byrno Morconnier, and "The Young Nun" by Elith Looinis. The poems evoke themes of decay, memory, and constrained longing. Chu Yohan's concluding note explains these are works by contemporary American poets associated with or following the Imagist movement, positioning them as newer talents compared to the established Amy Lowell. He hopes these translations will serve as a "refreshment" (청량제) to the Korean literary scene, which he characterizes as being suffused with "Decadence" (데가단스). From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as it shows a prominent Korean intellectual, Chu Yohan, actively introducing modern Western (specifically American Imagist) poetry to a Korean audience during the Japanese colonial period. His framing of this introduction as an antidote to "Decadence" offers insight into contemporary literary debates and cultural aspirations in Korea at the time.

쓰러진 장막
Malleville Haller

잇다콤 끄니지 안코 누른 닙 붉은 닙 갈색의
나무닙이 맑은 가을해에 떠러진다.
휘돌아서 떠나가는 누른닙, 붉은 닙, 갈색의 나무닙.
그의 잠자리 가리우던 막우에 떠러진다.

날은 오고 또 가서 가을이 다 지나갓다.
일은 겨을에도, 장막은 바람에 너머 가지 안엇다.
그의 버슨발이 잇던 널조각 우으로
눈이 희러치며 몰녀 드러왓다.

해빗에 타고 비에 썩고
쉰번 얼엇다가 녹은 줄이,
비가 오면 조이고 해나면 늘어서,
막을 뺏던 줄이 하나식 다 끈어젓다.

사나은 겨을 하로밤, 요란한 소리와 함께
바람이 와서 집웅을 뜨덧다.
온쟝막이 눈우에 너머젓다.
그 묵고 남은 줄과 현겁이 삼월달
흙믓고 죽으러진 눈덤이에서 나타날 때

그 장막의 그림자이나 나마 잇나?＜9＞
이 회색의 한덤이가 녀름동안
홍안의 소년의 안젼한 집이 잇던 줄 누가 알겟나?

GHOST
Byrno Morconnier

브렘톤! 브렘톤! 오랜 녯날의,
브렘톤의 낡은 집, 희고흰 눈.
물도 푸르럿고, 하늘도 푸르럿튼-
그 브렘톤을 영 닛지 못하리.

브렘톤 적은 항구는 푸른나무
푸른 수풀이 「테쓰」를 지은 좁은따.
새들도 나추 뜨는 브렘톤 동에서 서에서
곤한 배들이 쉬러 드러 오는 브렘톤.

문어구에 자란 「클로버」, 큰 길가에 샛길,
풀속에는 요녀의 나즌 소리가 들린다.
창에는 불그림자도 업고-나무새로 우슴도 업다-
꼿도 업는 뜰에는 검언 게자나무 하나뿐.

브렘톤 브렘톤, 오랜 녯날의
브렘톤의 낡은 집, 희고 흰 눈,
풀도 푸르럿코, 하늘도 푸르럿튼-
그 브램톤을 영원히 못니즈리.＜10＞

어린 녀승(女僧)
Elith Looinis

희나흰 도복 닙은 어린 녀승
대낫에 맑은 하늘을 처다 보고 하는 말
「이몸이 가진 푸른 빗치라고는
내 눈동자 밧게 업서요」

회색 도복 닙은 어린 녀승,
아름다운 꼿을 대하야 하는 말,
「이 몸이 가닞 아릿다움은
내 얼골 밧게 업서요」

색감안 도복의 어린 녀승
빩안 지는 해 보고 한숨 쉬는 말,
「죽어 저승에 가면
볼꼬가튼 구룸을 가질 터이야요」
(1924, 5 譯)

이것은 美國 현시 시인의 멧절을 번역해 본 것이다.
「이마지스트」 운동으로 유명하던 「아미, 로웰」은 이미 대가의 녈에 드럿다. 여긔 올닌 작가들은 아직도 다음 시대의 속한 이들이다. 「데가단스」의 졍됴에 숨찬 우리 독서계에 한 청량제가 되기를 바란다.(쥬요한)＜11＞
