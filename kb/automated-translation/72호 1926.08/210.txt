**Body Text Translation:**
From the Poem of Action
						Yi Ho

Like the empty, ill-natured minds of new tin cans rolling on the road burning under the scorching sun (炎天)

Like those who, while unaware, had the Buddha stolen away, yet still face that direction and only bow
Like those who know they will be chased away to another country in the new year... yet still live with their necks propped up on completely worn-out patches of field

On the scorching sunny ground of midsummer—on a white sandy field (白沙地) where not even a blade of grass can breathe
Like these people, like these people
Trying to build a house, trying to build a wall...

Ah—this kind of boundless Wish (希願)

Like these people in dark and cold hibernation (冬眠)
Ah! Trying to seek fortune, this boundless Wish<13>

This fire
Lighting it with body, with mind
Guarding the 『darkness』 of which one knows not when it will end.

This flickering candlelight
Protecting it with hands, with body, with mind
Straining to keep it alive.

Before this candle runs out

Setting fire to the sorrow of the age-old rotten bird
Setting fire [to] this Seoul, fallen down hatefully—asleep—on the sediment of melancholy

Melting a frozen heart in this—lit fire
Letting the wind breathe in the steaming heat
I intended to—
Why is the wind so harsh on this fire?
Why is the wind so harsh on this fire?<14>

A dream of escape (逃避)
The village edge of the world—that far-off 『Siberia』—
In a solitary house
Bearing the twilight
With the 『world』 spread out beneath my feet
I stood looking down

Grasping the tail of the century (世紀)

Everything that humans possess
Counting them out on the palm

Roaring laughter (哄笑), roaring laughter, roaring laughter

This sound, as if bursting into laughter enough to tear the heavens
Permeates every corner of the world

This dream revolves even in sleep
Even in the smoke of a lit cigarette<15>
This dream is carried along
Nirvana (寂滅)! This dream of escaping to XX

Poem of Action
Towards the earth, towards the earth, let's get close
Let's not grope over the Earth's crust (地殼)
Let's dig through the layered earth beneath
May the age-old geothermal heat (地熱) let the wind breathe

To the masses (민중)
So that even while being trampled, starved, and chased away
From beneath the horizon of fate (宿命) and tradition (傳統)
Hibernating (冬眠) 「Life」 and 「Rebellion」 (叛逆) may grow out

To the masses
Let's strip off the rags soaked in resentment, long-cherished desires (宿願), and despair (絕望)
Of those trampled, starved, and chased away
Let's strip off the veil of ignorance (無知)<16>

May the sleeping炼焰 (煉焰 - refining flames)—spewing blood vessels
Breathe the sunlight of the streets (街頭), breathe the freedom of the new century

To the masses
Naturalism is makeup
Objectivity (客觀) is escape (逃避), a stopgap measure (彌縫)
From observation (觀望) to gazing intently (凝視)
From recognition (認識) to struggle (鬪爭)
From all shallow superficiality (皮상) to the vertical world (縱의 세계)

To the masses
Connect his arteries (動脈) to the stem of the poem—and
Grasping the pen in his fist with veins standing out
Let's write a living poem
Let's sing the poem of action

Let's discover ourselves within it

To the streets (街頭)
To the masses (민중)
To action, to struggle (鬪爭)
(1925)<17>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Ho, published in 1926, is a passionate cry against the passivity and despair perceived in Korean society under colonial rule. It contrasts imagery of stagnation, helplessness, and empty rituals with a fervent call for "action." The poet urges a turn towards the "masses" (minjung), advocating for digging beneath superficialities, shedding ignorance and despair, and engaging in active struggle. The poem envisions a "living poetry" forged in the streets and connected to the people's suffering and potential for rebellion, aiming to ignite change and self-discovery through direct engagement. For historians, the poem vividly reflects the intellectual and artistic ferment of the 1920s in Korea, showcasing the rise of socially engaged literature (often associated with socialist or nationalist movements like KAPF, though Yi Ho's affiliation might be complex) reacting to colonial oppression. It highlights the desire to awaken the populace, the critique of detached intellectualism ("objectivity is escape"), and the desperate yearning for revolutionary change, using powerful, often violent, imagery ("setting fire," "spewing blood vessels").
