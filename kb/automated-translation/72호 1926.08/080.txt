Recent World Situation, From June 18th to July 15th

June 18th
The trial of the Kim Ik-hwan incident, involving those arrested for planning a certain movement using the State Funeral day as an opportunity, opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court under a *ban on observation (傍聽禁止); the prosecutor demanded an 8-month prison sentence.

△ A plot by a faction led by former Turkish parliament member "Jival Rusidbeyber" to assassinate the country's president, Kemal Pasha, was discovered, and 200 participants were arrested. △
France and Spain decided to exile Abd el-Krim, the leader of the Moroccan Rif tribes, to an isolated island (絶島).

△ Minister *Yoshizawa (芳澤) formally notified the Peking Government of the approval of the trademark law; the countries that have approved it so far are Britain, France, America, Italy, Netherlands, Switzerland, Denmark, and Japan, totaling 8 countries. △
The German National Assembly rejected the bill to confiscate the former German Emperor's property, drawing strong protests from the Social Democratic Party.

June 19th
64 students arrested in the June 10th Manse Incident were transferred, along with related documents, to the Kyŏngsŏng District Court Prosecutor's Office.

△ The Pongch'ŏn Army Military Council <29> resolved to commence a general subjugation of the Red Army within one week. △ 80,000 British coal miners refused strike pay due to a lack of funds in their union.

June 20th
The International Eucharistic Congress, America's first large-scale religious gathering, opened today in Chicago, with over 1 million participants. △ A citizens' rally was held in Kurye, South Chŏlla Province, regarding the issue of misuse of the site for the new post office construction.

June 21st
Mounted bandits (馬賊) raided P'adogu in Imgang County across the border, burning down over 90 houses and engaging in a fierce battle with over 600 government troops, resulting in over 50 casualties on both sides. △ Kim Ik-hwan's second trial opened, and he was sentenced to 8 months in prison. △ Students from P'yŏngyang Kwangsŏng High School caused an unseemly incident by committing violence at the All-Korea Football Tournament venue, indiscriminately beating referees and spectators.

June 22nd
First and second-year students at Yesan Public Agricultural School went on a collective school strike citing insufficient teaching. △ The Akyang Township head and 17 others, arrested over the grass field dispute (草戰事件) in Akyang and Ch'ŏngam townships of Hadong County, South Kyŏngsang Province, were transferred to the Prosecutor's Office on charges of disturbance and assault.

June 23rd
The third trial for Pak Sang-man and Kim Pyŏng-hyŏn, accused of murdering pro-Japanese collaborators along the South Manchuria Railway line, opened at the *Ryojun (旅東廳?) District Court <30>; the prosecutor demanded the death penalty. △ The Migration Conference hosted by the International Labour Organisation opened in London, with 23 participating countries and 153 representatives. △ A new cabinet was formed in France, a coalition of the moderate faction of the Republican Socialist Party, with Briand as Prime Minister and Caillaux as Deputy Prime Minister. △ A Polish woman named "Konkoja" was discovered and arrested for planning the assassination of Marshal Piłsudski, a hero of the Polish revolution. △ Students at Suwŏn Agricultural College went on a collective school strike demanding improvements in the school's substance. △ Over 850 members of the dispute group from the Japan *Hamamatsu (浜松) Instrument Company held a large demonstration at the company's *shareholder meeting (主總會), resulting in about 60 arrests.

June 24th
The U.S. House Committee on Foreign Affairs passed a resolution proposing to various nations the convening of the Third Hague Conference and confirming U.S. participation. △ The trial for Hong ** related to the June 10th Manse Incident opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, with the prosecutor demanding a 10-month prison sentence. △ The appeal trial for Yun U-yŏl in the Anarchist Party Incident opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, where Mr. Yun withdrew his appeal (控訴). △ During road construction in Pongch'ŏn, the remains of over 20,000 soldiers killed during the Russo-Japanese War were unearthed.

June 25th
Second-year students at Cheju Public Agricultural School went on a collective school strike due to lack of confidence in the acting principal. △ A revolutionary turmoil led by former Minister of War General Aguilera erupted in Spain. △ The Far Eastern Communist Party Congress <31> opened in Vladivostok (海蔘威), with Korean representatives also in attendance.

June 26th
The case involving numerous arrests during the Mokp'o Oil Company labor dispute, which had been under preliminary examination for four months, concluded today. Five members of the Mokp'o Vanguard Alliance, including Cho Kŭk-hwan and Kim Yŏng-sik, were dismissed (免訴), while Pak Che-min, O To-gŭn, and 10 others were indicted and sent to Kwangju Prison.

The Korean Revolutionary Military Association in Canton resolved to recruit 1,000 soldiers to plan a certain OO movement and simultaneously participate in the Canton Northern Expedition Army.

June 27th
Over 20,000 male and female workers from Shanghai Tailai *and 25 other silk factories (上海泰來*外 25製絲工場) initiated a strike, destroying machinery and causing numerous injuries.

June 28th
The 10th-anniversary ceremony of the Battle of Verdun during the World War was held in Verdun.

June 29th
The French Chamber of Deputies passed a vote of confidence in the Briand cabinet by 272 votes to 130. △ The west coast of Anju County was hit by a tidal wave (海溢), causing the Cheongcheon River (淸川江 - likely intended meaning for 淸州江) to rise by 35 ch'ŏk (척, approx. feet), resulting in significant crop damage in the Yŏnho-myŏn and Sinanju-myŏn areas. △ Ratifications of the Russo-German Neutrality Treaty were exchanged. △ The trial for Ch'oe Hyŏn, arrested on suspicion of being a secret envoy for Karahan, opened at the Haeju District Court, where the prosecutor requested a 1-year prison sentence. <32>

June 30th
During military exercises by the Polish Army in the Kovel region, an artillery shell exploded, killing 40 soldiers instantly and injuring over 50 others. △ About 30 ringleaders of the Chŏnju High School collective strike were arrested by the police. △ Mr. Chu * Kŏn, imprisoned in Sinŭiju Prison for the Communist Party Incident, was released on bail due to illness.

July 1st
Around 150 third-year students from Chŏnju High School, who were on a collective strike, became enraged by the breakdown of negotiations and dragged the principal, still in his chair, outside the school gate, causing a *major disturbance (一場*風波). △ At Paejae High School, eight students, including Mun Ch'ang-mo, Kim * Yŏng, and Son Sŏng, whose indictments were suspended in the Piŏsŏn Academy Incident (皮魚善學院事件), were suddenly given indefinite suspensions. △ The allied forces of Pongch'ŏn, Zhili, and Shandong launched a general offensive against the Guominjun (Nationalist Army) at Nankou.

July 2nd
A clash occurred between Pongch'ŏn and Guominjun forces in Changping, China, resulting in 200 casualties and over 100 prisoners for the Guominjun, and 36 prisoners for the Pongch'ŏn forces. △ The *Canadian (*奈陀) Parliament was dissolved to maintain the current cabinet, as the Conservative Party supporting the Prime Minister remained in the Lower House.

July 3rd
The Plenipotentiary Conference of Nations for the China *Tariff (*關税 - likely intended meaning for *關*) Conference, scheduled to open at the Dutch Legation, was suspended.

July 4th
The 150th anniversary ceremony of American independence was grandly held in the United States. △ There are reports that Worker-Peasant Russia (Soviet Union) provided 750,000 yuan in military funds to the Canton Army in China and 10 million yuan <33> to the Guominjun, and dispatched *** cavalry (騎兵) as assistance. △ The Mexican Government confiscated the properties (寺領) of general temples, banned other religious rituals, dissolved monasteries,* and further strictly ordered the prohibition of religious education.

July 5th
A strong earthquake occurred near "Füt-te-kok" (퓌ㅡ드·뎌·콕), resulting in over 400 deaths.

July 6th
A large whirlwind (typhoon) struck Nishimuro District in Wakayama Prefecture, Japan, causing a training airplane to crash; roof tiles and rocks flew through the air. In Moji, a landslide caused 6 instant deaths, and in Shimonoseki, heavy rain led to over 2,000 flooded houses. △ A major military conference of the Fengtian Army, centered around Zhang Zuolin, opened in Tianjin.

July 7th
An armed group and police engaged in a fierce battle for about 3 hours in Sangkwang, Kwangp'yŏng-myŏn, Ŭiju County. △ Five members of an armed group intruded into the house of Wŏn Sŏk-ch'an in Nambuksong Village, Sŏ-myŏn, Kusŏng County, demanding military funds. When refused, they shot Mr. Wŏn dead, set fire to the house,* and there are reports that a large loan agreement of 7 million * (unit unclear) has been established between Feng Yuxiang (憑玉祥 - typo for 馮玉祥) and Russia.

July 8th
The Chongno Police Station confiscated 5 pistols and 300 bullets from a house in Pongik-dong, downtown. △ The inaugural meeting of the Korean People's Promotion Association (朝鮮民興會), encompassing various fields and centered around individuals such as Myŏng Ch'ŏng-se, Kim Chong-hyŏp, Yŏm Ki-dŏk, Cho **, Kim Kyo-yŏng, Kim Ku *, <34> was held at the Chōsen Industrial Promotion Association building in Hwang Kŭm-jung (Koganechō). △ Three suspicious youths on Samsak-tong (Misaka-dōri) street downtown drew policeman An Sŭng-mo's military sword, stabbed the said policeman indiscriminately, and fled.

July 9th
Mr. "Carlton Thompson" (칼니톱·손 - interpretation), a member of the Philippine Fact-Finding Committee, arrived in the Philippines. This is to investigate the islands' qualifications for independence, thus drawing general attention.

July 10th
A major earthquake occurred in the Dutch East Indies, resulting in over 20 deaths. △ Bulgarian soldiers and Romanian (毒馬利亞 - likely 羅馬尼亞) troops clashed, resulting in 10 deaths. There are reports that the *Romanian army (毒馬利亞軍 is implied by context) invaded the Bulgarian border and massacred over 120 Bulgarian civilians. △ Around 10 PM, a youth with a pistol attacked the Tongsomun police substation, seriously injuring policeman Tokunaga Katsuji (德永勝次). △ The U.S.'s largest gunpowder magazine at the Lake Denmark Naval Ammunition Depot caught fire due to a lightning strike, tragically killing over 100 naval personnel and completely burning down about 30 nearby houses. △ The Executive Committee meeting of the Asian Peoples' Conference opened in Tokyo, Japan.

July 11th
The National Branch Heads' Meeting of the Japan Farmers' Union opened in Osaka, resolving to set a date for a large-scale demonstration against landlords involving 400,000 union members. △ In Shanghai, China, a major clash occurred between over 4,000 striking workers from the Naigai Wata Cotton Mill (內外綿) and police, resulting in numerous serious and minor injuries, and many arrests. <35> Syrian revolutionary forces attacked French and Senegalese troops in "Faruj" (파루지), inflicting *significant damage (*하고). △ White Army leader General Wrangel disbanded the White Army.

July 12th
The Russo-Japanese Forestry Conference held in Moscow broke down due to issues over forestry zones (林區). △ Regarding the June 6th Incident and the Second Communist Party Incident, Kang Yŏn-ch'ŏn, Son Chae-gi, and Pak Ŭi-yang, who were under arrest, were released today with suspension of indictment; Yi Pong-su and Yi * Wŏn were released without indictment. Twelve individuals including Kwŏn O-sŏl, Yŏm Ch'ang-yŏl, Pak Nae-t'aek, Hong Tŏk-yu, Kim Hang-chun, Yi Chi *, Min Ch'ang-sik, Yi Yong-chae, Pak Min-yŏng, Yang Chae-sik, Paek Myŏng-ch'ŏn, and Kim Kyŏng-chae were *indicted (*되야 - likely 起訴되야) and referred to preliminary examination.

July 13th
Mr. Hŏ Si-mo (許時模), an American who committed the barbaric act of carving a *character (*字) on the face of the boy Kim Myŏng-hyŏp, stirring public opinion, was indicted on charges of bodily injury at the P'yŏngyang Prosecutor's Office.

July 14th
Thirteen revolutionary leaders who attempted to assassinate Turkish President Kemal Pasha and overthrow the government were executed by hanging in the city of Smyrna (스일라).

July 15th
The trial for Song Hak-sŏn related to the Kim Ho-mun Incident (金虎門事件) opened at the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, where the prosecutor demanded the death penalty. <36>

**Summary**

This article chronicles global and domestic (Korean) events from June 18 to July 15, 1926. It covers political turmoil (Turkey, France, Spain, China, Mexico, Poland, Bulgaria/Romania), international relations (trademark law, Hague conference proposal, Russo-German treaty, Russo-Japanese talks), social issues (strikes in Britain, Japan, China; US Eucharistic Congress; US arsenal explosion), natural disasters (earthquakes, typhoon in Japan, flood in Korea), and numerous incidents within colonial Korea. Korean events include trials related to independence movements (Kim Ik-hwan, June 10th Manse Incident, Anarchist Party, Communist Party, Kim Ho-mun Incident), student strikes and protests (P'yŏngyang, Yesan, Suwŏn, Cheju, Chŏnju, Paejae), labor disputes (Mokp'o), activities of armed groups (border raid, Kusŏng incident), arrests, police actions, and the formation of a socio-political group (Korean People's Promotion Association).

From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is highly revealing. It juxtaposes major world events with the granular details of resistance and suppression within Korea. The frequent mentions of trials for independence activists (Kim Ik-hwan, Manse Incident participants, Communist suspects), student strikes across various schools demanding better conditions or protesting actions, armed resistance activities, and the formation of nationalist groups like the Minheunghoe (民興會) highlight the persistent anti-colonial struggle despite Japanese rule. The brief mention of the Korean Revolutionary Military Association in Canton aiming to join the Northern Expedition also points to the transnational nature of the Korean independence movement. Historians would find the specific details of arrests, trials (including sentences demanded or given), locations of incidents, and names (even partial ones) valuable for reconstructing the timeline and scope of resistance activities and Japanese counteractions during this period.
