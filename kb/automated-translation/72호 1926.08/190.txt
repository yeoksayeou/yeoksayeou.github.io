社告 (Company Announcement)

東京支社新設 (Tokyo Branch Newly Established)
Recently, for the benefit of our compatriots residing in Tokyo, Japan, under the management of Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-gŭn (徐廷根), a Tokyo branch has been newly established. As detailed below, we have opened with a new structure and have begun handling news correspondence, recruiting readers, and soliciting advertisements. We earnestly request the understanding and support of all our readers.
Branch Location: Tokyo City, Shitaya Ward, Nishimachi 11 [東京市 下谷區 西町 11번지]
Area Covered: The entire Tokyo area [東京 一圓]
Staff: Branch Manager and General Affairs: Sŏ Chŏng-gŭn (徐廷根)

Reporter and Advertising Manager: Chu T'ae-do (朱泰道)

Reporter: Kim Wan-jin (金完鎭)

永川支社新設 (Yŏngch'ŏn Branch Newly Established)
Regretting that in the Kyŏngdong area (慶東 一帶 - Eastern Kyŏngsang Province), where reader enthusiasm has recently been rising, an institution capable of representing public opinion in various fields had not been fully established, among those concerned, especially the gentlemen listed below, have established the Yŏngch'ŏn Branch. They will be engaged in local correspondence, reader recruitment, and handling advertisements. We earnestly hope for the further support of the respected individuals within the district.
Branch Location: Kyŏngbuk (North Kyŏngsang Province), Yŏngch'ŏn County, Town Interior [慶北 永川郡 邑內]
Area Covered: The entire Yŏngch'ŏn County [永川郡 一圓]
Staff: Branch Manager: Kim Yŏng-gap (金英甲)

General Affairs and Reporter: Kim Yŏng-hak (金英學)

Reporter: Pak Hŭi-yang (朴曦陽)

江陵支社復設 (Kangnŭng Branch Re-established)
Location: Kangnŭng Town Interior [江陵邑內]
Area: The entire Kangnŭng County [江陵郡 一圓]
Staff: Branch Manager: Ch'oe Pong-gyu (崔鳳圭)

General Affairs and Reporter: Chu Il-lyong (朱一龍)

Reporter: Ch'oe Uk (崔 郁)

**Summary**

This announcement from the August 1, 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine details the establishment or restructuring of several branch offices. A new branch was opened in Tokyo to serve the Korean community there, managed by Sŏ Chŏng-gŭn. New branches were also established in Yŏngch'ŏn (North Kyŏngsang Province) and Kangnŭng (Kangwŏn Province), indicating growing readership and the magazine's desire for broader news coverage and subscriber base in these regions. Additionally, staff were added or newly appointed in the existing Sŏnch'ŏn (North P'yŏngan Province) and Sach'ŏn (South Kyŏngsang Province) offices. For historians, this article reveals the expanding operational network of a major Korean intellectual magazine during the colonial period, showing its ambition to connect with readers across different regions of Korea and even within the Korean diaspora in Japan. The specific locations and personnel listed provide concrete data on the magazine's organizational structure and regional engagement efforts under Japanese rule.
