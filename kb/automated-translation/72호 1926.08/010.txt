Thinking of This Time Last Year

July 18th, turning it over in my mind, it feels like just the other day, but it has already been exactly one year. That unprecedented great flood disaster (千古未曾有의 大水難) experienced along the Hangang River (漢江) banks and the Naktonggang River (洛東江) area has already passed its first anniversary and is entering its second year.

Facing July 18th and quietly thinking of that day last year, the tragic sights of that time vividly appear before my eyes like a motion picture. As the ominous floodwaters swept down, engulfing the land, mountains, and rivers, the sounds of embankments (防築) collapsing, houses collapsing, people crying for help, the cries of cattle, horses, chickens, and pigs (牛馬鷄豚)—all those tragic sounds of destruction and annihilation (破壞殄滅) clearly startle my eardrums (耳膜) once again.

How many of our lives did this unexpected great malicious play (大惡戱) of nature snatch away? How much of our property did it take? How much fear and trembling did it make us feel? Not to mention the human losses, how much damage was done to the fertile fields (沃土良田), forests, fields, and roads (林野道路)—those natural losses? Nature's malicious play and resistance are cruel, but the more I think about it, the colder my heart and liver (심담) grow.

Since I could not witness (當觀) the Naktonggang River (洛東江) area or the vicinity of the Imjingang River (臨津江) at that time, it is difficult to fully grasp (周知) the extent of the damage. Perhaps because I couldn't witness it, I don't feel such deep emotions (深曲한 감정), but the tragic scene (慘景) along the Hangang River (漢江) banks seems like it will never disappear.

Who would not shed tears of reminiscence (追懷) when passing through the completely destroyed (全滅된) Ich'ondong? Who would not weep thinking of the hundreds of lonely souls lost in the water (水中孤魂)? — Who could that be? Reaching the head of the Hangang River (漢江頭) alone (獨步) on an evening when wind and rain seem imminent under the setting sun (風雨欲來夕陽天), recalling last year fills me with no small fear for this year.

Will nature always torment us like this? This year, was it already the Fire God (火神) or the Drought Demon (旱鬼)? That tormented us. What other ghosts are there in the future intending to torment us? It would be the greatest fortune if, luckily, that unpredictable flood disaster does not occur this year.

Worldly folk hold memorials or ancestral rites for one, three, up to three or five generations when their own grandfathers or fathers (祖與父) die. Or, when some name-selling fellows (賣名漢)＜2＞ die, they gather for memorial services (추도) or mourning ceremonies (吊喪) and make a fuss. However,

Oh, all people of the world, how about holding a memorial service once for the hundreds, thousands of lonely souls sacrificed in last year's great flood disaster, and offering condolences (吊慰) once for the nature destroyed at that time? No matter how I think about it, it doesn't seem like a reckless act (무모한 짓).

Thinking of August 1st, this too is undoubtedly exactly one year ago, also entering its second year. It means exactly one year has passed since our *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) was confiscated (압수) and subsequently fell into the cruel calamity (酷禍) of suspension (停刊). Thinking of that time, the emotions regarding our situation are immeasurable, but there is also no lack of curses for them. What were we doing at that time to receive such a tragic disaster (慘禍)?

We briefly introduced Mr. Yi (李某), Mr. Pak (朴某), and all the seniors residing overseas, making a fuss calling them patriots (國士) or men of noble purpose (志士). (Those whom they call bandits (馬賊), that is.) And so, in the end—we received an indefinite suspension. (The ban was lifted last October).

While editing the August issue, thinking back to last August, various emotions rise, and the tip of my brush (붓끗) naturally trembles. Being in a situation where we lack confidence, we cannot ease our minds (심기) due to groundless fear. Will we fortunately be safe this year? What if—something happens? However, we have no choice but to do what we must do.

Not just last August, but looking back issue by issue over the 72 issues published in the seven years since our founding, fear and anxiety constituted nearly more than half (過半), and censorship deletions (삭제) and confiscations (압수) were almost evenly matched (相半). While there is no lack of joy from having fought bravely (勇戰) ourselves, haven't the authorities (당국자), no matter how much they speak of the authority (威) of holding power (執柄), been too severe?

Let's not speak of who is right and who is wrong (孰是孰非), as there is no one to judge (可否). Let us each steadfastly proceed according to our own mission (사명). As this year passes, and next year passes, and ten years, a hundred years pass, and a thousand, ten thousand years pass, *Kaebyŏk* intends to steadfastly and bravely be active throughout the great earth and all nations (大地萬邦). (Ch'unp'a 春坡) <3>

**Summary**

This editorial from the August 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* reflects on two somber anniversaries: the devastating Great Flood of July 1925 and the magazine's own suspension in August 1925. The author, writing under the pen name Ch'unp'a, contrasts the public commemoration of certain individuals with the lack of remembrance for the flood victims and laments the unpredictable cruelty of nature. He then recounts *Kaebyŏk*'s suspension, linking it to their coverage of overseas figures deemed "bandits" (likely independence activists) by the authorities, expressing both anxiety about future censorship and a resolve to continue publishing despite constant pressure, deletions, and confiscations experienced over its seven-year history. The article reveals the dual anxieties faced by Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule: natural disasters compounding the hardships of life, and the constant threat of political repression stifling expression. Historians might find the explicit mention of introducing figures called "bandits" (馬賊) by authorities as the cause for suspension particularly interesting, alongside the quantitative description of censorship ("deletions and confiscations were almost evenly matched") over the magazine's publication history, offering a glimpse into the specific pressures faced by the nationalist press.

작년 이때를 생각하면서
7월 18일 돌쳐 생각하니 엇그제 가튼데 발서 꼭 1년이 되얏다. 漢江沿岸 洛東江一帶에서 당한 그- 千古未曾有의 大水難은 발서 1년이 지나고 이태채 드러가려 한다.
7월 18일을 당하야 가만히 작년 그날을 생각하니 그때의 그 처참한 광경이 활동사진 모양으로 눈에 훤하게 빗치인다. 흉흉한 홍수가 大地山河를 휩쓰러 내려가는데 防築 문어지는 소리 집 문어지는 소리 사람 살리라는 소리 牛馬鷄豚의 소리 그- 모든 破壞殄滅의 처참한 소리가 분명히도 耳膜을 다시금 놀내인다.
생각하지 못햇든 이 자연의 大惡戱는 吾人의 생명을 얼마나 빼아섯스며 吾人의 재산을 얼마나 가져갓스며 吾人으로 하여금 얼마나 공포와 전율을 늣기게 하얏는가. 인간으로의 손실은 말할 것도 업고 沃土良田 林野道路- 그 자연의 손실은 얼마나 바닷는가, 자연의 惡戱와 저항이 無道하지만 생각사록 심담이 서늘해진다.
洛東江一帶나 臨津江附近은 그때에 當觀을 못한지라. 피해의 범위를 周知하기가 어렵다. 當觀 못한 탓인지 그닥 深曲한 감정도 업다마는 漢江沿岸의 그 慘景은 영원히 사라지지 안을 것갓다.
全滅된 二村洞을 지날 때 누가 追懷의 눈물을 안이 뿌리며 수백 명 水中孤魂을 생각하고 우지 안을 者- 그 누구이냐. 風雨欲來夕陽天에 獨步로 漢江頭에 이르러 작년을 회상하매 금년의 공포가 업지 못하다.
자연은 언제까지든지 吾人을 이러케 괴롭게 할텐가. 금년은 발서 火神인가 旱鬼인가. 그것이 吾人을 괴롭게 하얏다. 압흐로 또 무슨 귀신이 잇서 吾人을 괴롭게 하려는가. 금년은 다행히 그 不測의 水難이 나오지 안으면 게서 더한 幸이 업겟다.
세상놈들은 자기의 祖與父가 죽으면 1회 3회 내지 3대 5대 두고 기념이니 제사이니 하것다. 또는 누구＜2＞누구 賣名漢들 죽으면 모야드러 추도니 吊喪이니 하고 떠들어 내겟다. 그러나
모든 세상 사람들아 작년 중의 大水難에 희생된 수백 수천의 孤魂을 위하야 한번 추도하고 그때에 파괴된 그 자연을 위하야 한번 吊慰함이 엇더할가. 아모리 생각해도 무모한 짓은 안일 것 갓다.

8월 1일 생각하니 이것도 틀님업는 꼭 1년이오 또 이태재 드러가려 한다. 우리 「開闢」이 압수를 당하고 뒤밋처 停刊의 酷禍에 빠지든 때가 꼭 1년이 되얏다는 말이다. 그때를 생각하니 우리의 처지에 대한 감회도 무량하지마는 저들에게 대한 저주도 업지 안타. 그때에 우리들은 무슨 일을 하고 그 慘禍를 바닷는지?
李某 朴某 모든 해외에 게신 선배들을 간단간단히 소개하고 國士니 志士니 떠드러 대엿것다.(저들이 이른바 馬賊과 가튼 자를) 그래서 결국- 무기정간을 밧고 말앗다.(작년 10월 解禁)
8월호를 편집하면서 작년 8월을 생각하니 여러 가지 감회 끄테 붓끗이 자연 떨리인다. 자신 못하는 우리의 처지인지라 공연한 공포에 심기를 펼 수 업다. 금년은 幸히 무사하려는지? 만약 -有事하면 엇지 할고? 그러나 우리의 할 일은 우리가 할 수 밧게 업다.
작년 8월 뿐만이 안이라 창간 7개년에 72호를 逐號해서 생각해보니 공포와 불안이 거의 過半하얏고 삭제와 압수가 거의 相半하얏다. 스스로 勇戰한 희열도 업는 바 안이지만 당국자야말로 아무리 執柄의 威를 말하지만 너무나 심하지 안엇는가 말이다.
孰是孰非는 可否할 者가 업스니 말하지 말자. 각각 자기의 사명을 딸하 꾸준히 나아가 보기로 하자. 금년이 지나고 明年이 지나고 십년 백년이 지나고 천년만년이 지나도록 開闢은 꾸준히 용감히 大地萬邦에서 활약하려 한다.(春坡) ＜3＞
