The Nature of the New Political Movement, The Fields of Total Movement and Partial Movement
						By Pae Sŏngnyong

Bourgeois society signifies that this society is dominated by the bourgeoisie. Before the social transformation brought about by the historical development of bourgeois society, the one force of a different quality that arises, grows, and develops within it is this burgeoning power of the proletariat. The social movement that currently shakes the entire world with the greatest roar is another name for the activation of that proletarian power.

The social movement is the newly emerged effort of the proletariat within bourgeois society to swiftly build a beautiful future society. Since it means that all institutions and organizations of bourgeois society cannot be left as they are, its sole purpose is to change them into something different in quality and form; in other words, it is a movement to [CENSORED] bourgeois society. Therefore, the target of the social movement is that single mass, the unified force that is capitalist society. Naturally, none of the facts concerning all the politics, economics, morality, and laws contained, enveloped, bound, created, and attached within it can fail to be the object of that movement. As the scope of the movement is that broad and its goal<4> is that immense, the time required in the course of historical transformation is also considerably long. Its journey can certainly not be traversed overnight. Here, as the content of the social movement becomes complex, it differentiates into various movement sectors, and as its scope expands, there come to be distinct stages of growth through qualitative change. Thus, the movement continuously changes both qualitatively and quantitatively, developing and transitioning from a partial movement in one sector to a total, comprehensive movement, and from a partial movement in one region to a nationwide popular movement. Ultimately, it concentrates countless small movement capacities into one large movement capacity, which can be called the natural path that organizational movements inevitably take.

The course of qualitative change in the emerging class movement begins with the conscious movement of the proletariat, progresses to direct economic movement, and from economic movement further advances to political movement. This political movement constitutes the final stage of the emerging class movement; that is, the social movement achieves its intended goal with the complete victory of this political movement. This emerging political movement aims to [CENSORED] the ruling power held by the bourgeoisie in bourgeois society; that is, it is the inevitable, great struggle movement of the proletariat, situated in the position of the ruled, against the established capitalist political forces. When the proletariat engages in partial economic movements, such as united demands to improve labor conditions by demanding wage increases, shortening working hours, or lowering tenant rents, the bourgeoisie mobilizes or utilizes its established political power to obstruct those demands. They create various harsh laws capable of oppressing the proletariat, ruthlessly hindering the proletarian movement. Here, partial economic movements alone have no way to confront their political power. In this sense, a social movement that is uninterested<5> in or neglects political struggle inevitably becomes a movement that negates [CENSORED] all actions. Even if partial movements flourish, they are necessary only for the sake of the total movement. If they cannot be formed into a single opposing force, even if partial movements thrive and achieve sectoral gains, the capitalist political power of the bourgeoisie will still continue its activities in the traditional form. In the social movement, this established political force must be called the headquarters of that [CENSORED].

However, on the other hand, some might say that the politics and economy of this capitalist society are two sides of the same coin, two aspects of one entity. Whether viewed qualitatively or quantitatively, the sum total of economic power and the sum total of political power are ultimately identical. Thus, they might question the distinction between these two movements and speak of their inseparability. Furthermore, if specific tenant farmers in various regions demand the establishment of permanent tenancy rights from specific landlords in those regions, it would be called a movement to improve the economic conditions of the proletariat. But if tenant farmers nationwide demand a nationwide system of permanent tenancy rights through legislation, although this is also a movement to improve economic conditions, no different from the former, because it is simultaneously a demand to reform or abolish a part of the capitalist economic system's organization and a demand to reform or abolish a part of the political system's organization, it must be counted not only as an economic movement but also as a political movement. This is because economic and political movements arise under the same objective, differing only in form, and also because, in the organization of capitalist society, politics and economy are intertwined and cannot be viewed separately. However, as long as we distinguish these two movements, whether by stage or sector, the economic movement must inevitably be interpreted as the precursor, the partial movement, to the political movement.<6> Once its form and scope reach the point of reforming or abolishing capitalist institutions and organizations, not only does it naturally become a political movement, but in reality, such reform or abolition is impossible without resorting to political action. Disregarding this interpretation, one would have no choice but to say that there is no need to maintain a political movement organ for the proletariat.

Thus, the political movement is the representative movement of the proletarian struggle, aiming to comprehensively fulfill all the objectives of the economic movement and other movements. It is a large-scale movement that follows sectoral and partial movements, an inevitable transformation dictated by the growth process of the actual movement. However, just because the course of a social movement in a particular society has entered the political movement phase does not mean that movements for the partial interests of the economic movement disappear. That is, even when the political movement is flourishing, small-scale economic struggles like tenancy disputes, labor disputes, boycotts, and strikes simultaneously prevail, which is practical evidence of the coexistence of these two class movements. However, even this fact cannot erase the clear distinction manifested in the forms and methods of economic and political movements.

Just as the ultimate goal of the economic movement is achieved by resorting to the means of this political movement, the ultimate goals of all other sectors—youth movements, women's movements, Hyŏngp'yŏng movements (levelers' movement for Paekchŏng rights), student movements—are also achieved by resorting to the means of this political movement. The various [CENSORED] parties in each country, standing alongside all the capitalist constitutional parties and preparing for the realization of proletarian politics, are the movement organs for achieving that goal.

In our Chosŏn (Korea), there has not yet been a proletarian political movement. However, the thought movement (사상운동) in Chosŏn bears the alias of that political movement, and thought organizations (사상단체) are thus institutions possessing the function of a proletarian political party aiming for that political movement. Due to Chosŏn's special political circumstances, the thought movement has not yet been able to openly display its functions.<7> However, in its objectives and nature, organizational form, and mode of action, it is clearly a political movement organ. Henceforth, the political color of that movement will surely become deeper and clearer. Therefore, the constituent elements of these thought organizations are the active elements of general sectoral movement groups and the relatively conscious radical intellectual elements among the general propertyless masses. This shows that the thought movement is not an entity independent of the general sectoral movements. Furthermore, thought movement organizations do not have objectives aimed at partial interests like other sectoral movement groups; their raison d'être is non-existent apart from the political movement. Therefore, such groups aiming for political movement are inevitably related to acting for the comprehensive goals of the groups aiming for various sectoral movements. If they cannot become the organ that expresses and executes the opinions and assertions of the various sectoral movement groups, their own reason for existence will naturally be obliterated.

The fact that Chosŏn's political situation is special does not mean that there cannot be a [CENSORED] new political movement in Chosŏn. However, the absence of a new political movement in Chosŏn signifies that the emerging class movement in Chosŏn cannot develop popularly. It is also a movement that cannot be absent in Chosŏn. What means of progress will Chosŏn's thought movement indeed adopt henceforth?
I intend to discuss concrete opinions on this matter in a future opportunity.<8>

**Summary**

Pae Sŏngnyong argues that the ultimate goal of the proletariat's struggle against the bourgeoisie in capitalist society is achieved through political movement, not just economic ('partial') movements. He contends that while economic struggles (for wages, rent reduction, etc.) are necessary steps, the bourgeoisie uses its control of the state and laws to suppress them. Therefore, a comprehensive ('total') political movement aiming to seize power is essential. Applying this to colonial Korea, Pae identifies the contemporary "thought movement" (사상운동) as the nascent form of this necessary proletarian political movement, functioning like a proto-political party despite colonial restrictions. He stresses its role in unifying various sectoral movements (youth, women, Hyŏngp'yŏng, students) and promises future discussion on its specific path forward in Korea's "special political circumstances." For historians, the article offers a valuable glimpse into early Korean socialist/communist thought, particularly the strategic thinking about navigating colonial repression. The distinction between 'partial' economic struggles and the 'total' political goal, and the framing of the 'thought movement' as a substitute political entity under censorship, reveal key aspects of the nascent left-wing movement's self-perception and tactical considerations in 1920s Korea. The censored words highlight the dangers faced by such movements.

사회운동은 뿔으조아 사회의 내부에서 미래의 아름다운 사회를 신속히 건설하랴는 푸로레타리아의 새로 대두한 노력이다. 뿔으조아 사회의 모든 제도와 조직을 그대로 둘 수 업다는 의미에서 그 질과 형식의 다른 무엇으로 고치라는 것이 그 유일한 목적이니 말하자면 뿔으조아 사회를 XXXXXX하랴는 운동이다. 그럼으로 사회운동의 목표하는 대상은 그 자본주의사회라는 한 덩어리가 되야 잇는 한 개의 渾一的 勢力이니 그 내부에 넛코 잇고 싸고 잇스며 얼거매고 만드러 두고 붓처노흔 모든 정치와 경제사실 도덕과 법률에 관한 사실의 그 어느 것도 그 운동의 대상이 아니 됨이 업슬 것도 물론이다. 그 운동의 범위가 그만치 광범하고 그 운동의＜4＞ 목적이 그만치 浩大함을 따라서 그 역사적 변혁의 途程에서 요하는 時問도 상당히 긴 것이라. 그 行程은 결코 一朝一夕에 통과할 수 업는 것이다. 여긔에서 사회운동은 그 내용이 복잡하야짐을 따라서 각개의 운동부문으로 분화하게 되고 그 범위가 확대됨을 따라서 질적 변동에 의한 각개의 생장계단이 잇게 되는 것이니 그 운동이 질적 양적의 양 방면으로 間斷업시 변동되야 一部門的의 부분 운동에서 총체가 전적 운동으로 一地方的의 부분 운동에서 전 지역적의 국민운동으로 發展推移하는 것이다, 결국 각개의 무수한 小運動能力을 일개의 大運動能力으로 집중하는 것이니, 그 조직운동의 당연히 취하게 되는 진로이라 할 것이다.

신흥계급운동의 질적으로 변천하는 行程은 푸로레타리아의 意識的 運動에서 기점하야 경제적 직접운동으로 진전되고 경제적 운동에서 다시 정치적 운동으로 진전되야 이 정치적 운동으로 신흥계급운동의 最終段을 作하는 것이니 즉 사회운동은 이 정치적 운동의 완전한 승리에서 그 소기의 목적이 달성되는 것이다.
이 신흥정치적 운동은 뿔으조아 사회의 뿔으조아지-들의 把持하고 잇는 지배력을 XXXXXXXXX것이니 즉 피지배자의 지위에 잇는 푸로레타리아로서의 필연적으로 잇게 되는 자본주의적 기성정치세력에 대한 큰 항쟁운동이다. 저 푸로레타리아가 부분적 경제운동으로 노동임금의 値上을 요구하며 노동시간의 단축을 요구하며 소작료의 인하를 요구하는 등의 노동조건을 개선하랴는 단결적 요구가 잇는 때에는 뿔으조아지- 들은 그 요구를 妨碍하기 위하야, 그 旣成한 정치적 권력을 활동식히고 또는 이용하게 된다. 여러 가지로 푸로레타리아의 압박할 수 잇는 가혹한 법률을 창설하야 푸로레타리아의 운동을 여지 업시 방해하나니 여긔에서 부분적 경제운동만으로는 그들의 정치적 권력을 대항할 길이 업다. 이러한 의미에서 정치운동에＜5＞ 관심치 아니하고 또는 등한한 사회운동은 XXXXXX XXX 모든 행동을 부정하는 운동이 되고 마는 것이다. 그 부분운동이 熾盛하는 것도 그 전적 운동을 위하야서만 필요한 것이라 그를 일개의 대항세력으로 결성하지 못한다 하면 그 부분운동이 熾盛하야 부문적 이익을 획득한다 할지라도 뿔으조아지- 들의 자본주의적 정치적 권력은 의연히 재래의 형식 그대로 활동을 지속하고 잇슬 것이니 사회운동에 잇서 이 旣成된 정치세력이야말로 그 XXXX의 총본부이라 아니 할 수 업슬 것이다.

그러나 혹 他便으로 이 자본주의 사회의 정치와 경제는 동일한 물건의 表裡이오 일체의 양면이라 경제적 세력의 總和軆와 정치적 세력의 總和軆와는 그 질로 보던지 그 양으로 보던지 필경 동일한 것이니 이 兩個運動의 구별점이 무엇인가 하고 그 兩個運動의 不可分離할 것을 말하는 이도 업지 아니할 것이다. 뿐만 아니라 각 지방의 특정지주에 대하야 각 지방의 특정 소작인들이 永小作權設定要求가 잇다 하면 그는 푸로레타리아의 경제조건을 개선하랴는 운동이라 하겟지마는 전국의 소작인들이 전국적 永小作權制를 입법적으로 요구한다 하면 이도 그 경제조건을 개선하랴는 운동임에는 전자와 다를 바가 업지마는 그가 자본주의경제제도조직의 일부를 改廢하랴는 요구인 동시에 정치적 제도조직의 일부를 改廢하랴는요구인 관계상 그는 경제운동인 동시에 또한 정치운동으로 數하지 아니할 수 업슬 것이다. 이는 경제운동과 정치운동은 그 동일한 목적하에 發作되는 형식이 相違할 뿐인 까닭이오 또 他便으로 자본주의사회조직에서도 또한 정치와 경제가 공통되야 잇서 서로 분리하야 볼 수 업는 까닭이다. 그러나 우리가 계단으로던지 부문으로던지 이 兩個運動을 구분하는 이상 경제운동은 정치운동의 전기운동, 부분운동으로 해석하지 아니할 수가 업슬 것이라.＜6＞ 그 형식과 범위가 자본주의의 제도와 조직의 改廢에까지 밋치게 되면 스스로 정치적 운동이 되지 아니할 뿐 아니라 그 실제에 잇서 정치적 운동에 依치 안코는, 그 改廢가 불가능한 것이니 이러한 해석을 捨하고는 푸로레타리아의 정치운동기관은 存置할 필요도 업는 것이라 할 수 밧게 업슬 것이다.
