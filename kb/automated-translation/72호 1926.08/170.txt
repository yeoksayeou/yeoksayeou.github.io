不老長生의 秘訣
The Secret of Eternal Youth and Long Life

At the end of the famous book "The Secret of Eternal Youth and Long Life" (不老長生의 秘訣) by the renowned British [英國] doctor, Mr. P'uk'aa [푸카아], several conditions as noted below [左記] are recorded. Though the words are simple, their meaning [意味] is very profound [甚深 - inferred from 심장하다].

1.  Do not keep in your head any thought that your age is gradually increasing.

2.  Strictly [嚴格] control [節制] the amount of food [食物] you eat.

3.  Always [恒常] be optimistic [樂觀] about the future [前途].

4.  Do not be discouraged [挫折] by difficulties [困難]; know that difficulties serve as good [好個] lessons [敎訓] and rouse yourself [奮起] anew [更].

5.  Do not become agitated [激昂] or enraged [憤怒]. It only harms [損] your energy [精力].

6.  Do not be anxious [煩惱] thinking about tomorrow's [明日] affairs.

7.  Work and play, play and work again.

8.  Do not enter [入] into the field of competition [競爭場裏] where money is the objective. Such things will definitely [決] make it impossible [不能] to become a hundred-year-old elder [百歲의 長者].

9.  Consideration [思慮] requires meticulousness [周密], and food [食物] should be bland [淡].

10. Clean [淸潔] food and clean [淸潔] thoughts [思想].

11. Absorb [吸收] fresh [新鮮] air [空氣] and fresh [新鮮] ideals.

12. Simplify [簡單] life and discard [廢] complexity [複雜].

13. Longevity [長壽] is found in laughter [笑中].

14. Always [恒常] possess the spirit [精神] of youth [靑年] and the constitution [體質] of youth [靑年].<99>

**Summary**

The article presents fourteen points for achieving longevity, purportedly from a book by a British doctor named P'uk'aa. The advice emphasizes maintaining a youthful mindset, dietary moderation, optimism, resilience against hardship, emotional control, avoiding worry and monetary competition, simple living, cleanliness in food and thought, fresh air, and laughter. From a historical perspective, this article reflects the influx of Western self-improvement and health ideas into colonial Korea during the 1920s. For historians, it showcases the adoption of modern, individualistic approaches to health and well-being, contrasting potentially with more traditional Korean views, and highlights the themes of self-discipline and mental fortitude circulating at the time.
