실실이 드린 버들가지에
비는 촉촉히 나려라.

울어라 오스름한 달밤.

Solitude, Prize Submission Announcement: New Poetry, 3rd Place
						Han Sabae

On the gently hanging willow branches
The rain softly falls.
Under the lotus leaves (蓮닙), the narrow-mouthed frogs
Cry out—oh, dim moonlit night.

Critique: As a murmur of "Solitude," it may be insufficient, but setting the theme aside, it is a splendid landscape poem. <22>

**Summary**

The poem "Solitude" by Han Sabae, which won 3rd place in *Gaebyeok* magazine's New Poetry contest, paints a brief, evocative scene of a gentle rainy night with weeping willows, croaking frogs under lotus leaves, and a dim moon. The accompanying critique praises it as excellent landscape poetry but suggests it might not fully embody the theme of solitude. From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as an example of early "New Poetry" (신시) published in a prominent nationalist cultural magazine during the Japanese colonial period. It showcases the nascent development of modern Korean poetic forms and the role of literary contests in fostering new writers, even as the critique hints at the subjective interpretation of thematic requirements.
