Creeds, Assertions, and Rejections of Various Notables

Let us call it good. Let us call it evil. In any case, the atmosphere of this society is shaped by the thoughts of those with knowledge and real power. If this statement is true, then it is certainly not useless for us today to inquire into the thoughts of the various notables residing in the classes of knowledge and real power. Feeling this keenly, our company posed three questions:
1. What are your lifelong creeds?
2. What do you wish to assert above all?
3. What do you wish to reject above all?
We troubled numerous notables for their answers, and present the general responses below. Although it may be impolite, we hope that by doing this, (1) the words of those who responded gain further weight, and (2) the words of our general brethren gain new significance, making the society we inhabit a bit purer, giving our endeavors a bit more poignancy, and a bit more certainty. If so, our wish is fulfilled.

**Summary**

This article from the July 1921 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine presents the results of a survey asking prominent Korean figures about their personal creeds, core assertions, and things they most wished to reject. The editors state their hope that publishing these views will lend weight to the respondents' actions and inspire others, leading to a purer society and more meaningful endeavors. The responses cover a wide range of philosophical, religious, social, and political stances, reflecting the diverse intellectual currents among Korean leaders during a period of Japanese colonial rule. For historians, this survey offers a valuable snapshot of the ideologies and priorities of the Korean elite in the early 1920s, revealing concerns about national identity, education, social justice, morality, modernization, and resistance strategies, ranging from advocating unity, self-strengthening, and education to rejecting classism, falsehood, foreign dependence (toadyism), and injustice. The inclusion of figures from various fields—education, journalism, religion, business, activism—provides a broad perspective.

諸名士의 信條와 主張과 排斥
善이라 하자. 비록 惡이라 하자. 如何間 이 社會의 空氣는 有知識, 有實權者의 心思 如何에 依하야 써지어지는 것이다. 이 말이 참이라면 오늘날에 잇서 우리가 그 有知識, 有實權의 階級에 居在한 諸名士의 心思 如何를 叩見함과 如함은 決코 無益한 일이 아니다. 本社는 이에 늑김이 切하야
1. 平生의 信條 如何
2. 어대까지 主張하고 십흔 것
3. 어대까지 排斥하고 십흔 것
은 무엇이냐? 하는 세 가지 물음을 發하야 幾多名士의 對答을 煩한 바 그 一般을 左에 보이노니 비록 缺禮의 말이나 本社가 이것을 함으로 因하야 (1) 對答한 그네의 所云爲에 一層 氏가 들며 (2) 一般 兄弟의 所云爲에 다시 새로운 意義가부터 우리들의 깃들이는 社會가 더 좀 純摯한 社會가 되며 우리의 하는 모든 일이 더 좀 辛酸한 맛이 잇스며 더 좀 確實性이 잇게 되엇스면 우리의 所望은 達이라.
