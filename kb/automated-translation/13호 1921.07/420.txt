저자	玄相允 (Hyŏn Sangyun), 張膺震 (Chang Ŭngjin), 金石松 (Kim Sŏksong), 玄僖運 (Hyŏn Hŭiun)

考選餘感
論文選者 玄相允 (Essay Selector Hyŏn Sangyun)

Firstly, when I looked at everyone's writings this time, I was very pleased. This is generally because through your writings, I saw how much our society has progressed. To mention just one or two examples first, the soundness of thought and the fluency of literary style are those things. Compared to four or five years ago, it can be said that there has been significant improvement.

However, looking at the topics you chose or the ideas you constructed, I felt that perhaps they were still somewhat lacking. For essays concerning social issues, at least, I couldn't help but feel they were still insufficient. Therefore, inevitably, this time I could not set the standard for selection on social issues, but rather on ordinary literary essays. Of course, among them were quite a few excellent essays concerning social issues, but some had topics with too little connection to the actual circumstances of present-day Chosŏn (朝鮮), and even if such connections existed, the structure or formulation of those essays seemed somewhat inadequate. Hence, this time, I made the bold decision to shift the standard to simple literary art, that is, writing style.

Next, what comes to mind when looking at your writings is the excessive imitative use of foreign words just as they are. This is not necessarily limited to your writings alone – recently, almost all writing in Chosŏn has this defect. People frequently use foreign words directly. If these were Western words, perhaps using them directly – no, rather, using them directly – might be better. However, because these are written using Chinese characters (漢字), they often become entirely meaningless – no, quite different – from the perspective of a Chosŏn person or a pure Chinese person. Therefore, I think this is something you must pay attention to from now on.

Next, another point I want to bring to your attention is that you fail to distinguish between literary language (文語) and colloquial language (口語). This is something you must pay attention to more than anything else when you intend to write. Why is that? Because when anyone reads a text, if it goes on for a while saying, "Do it this way (이리하외다). Please do this (이러케 하시오)," and then suddenly switches to, "It is so (그러하도다). It is like this (이러하니라)," one can sense that the narrative, judging by its attitude first, is improper.<57>

Besides these, there are many other things I would like to mention, but as I am extremely busy, I will cut it short here for now and only ask for your forbearance and pardon for the rest.

小品文選者 張膺震 (Short Piece Selector Chang Ŭngjin)

Regarding the prize competition for short pieces (小品文) this time, while we initially did not have great expectations for our still immature new literary scene (新文壇), the results of the collection were so meager in content that there was practically nothing worth noting. This is greatly regrettable for our new literary scene, and I earnestly hope for the strenuous efforts of all authors.

新詩選者 金石松 (New Poetry Selector Kim Sŏksong)

Being tasked with the selection of New Poetry (新詩) among the prize literary works for the special supplement of the Kaebyŏk 1st Anniversary Commemorative Issue is an honor beyond my station, and at the same time, it caused me extreme hesitation (躊躇). However, as they say, "even a chicken will do when there's no pheasant," and since it was a strict order from the various gentlemen on the editorial staff, I had no choice but to try it this time.

"A poet may call composing poetry a pleasure, but discussing poetry is agony," said a certain poet, and I would say, "A poet is one who composes poetry, not one who critiques poetry." Therefore (not that I consider myself a poet), doing such a task is, no matter how I look at it, not only meaningless for myself but also seems to be of no benefit to my respected poet friends.

Let's leave ideals as ideals. In any case, I had believed that the results of the submissions this time would be very good. This was based on the literary fever of the past few years, and especially on the proliferation (簇生) of "New Poets" (新詩家). However, to state the facts plainly, I ultimately could not help but be disappointed. The number of submissions: 30 pieces! Even considering our inadequate literary scene, this was evidence demonstrating astonishing poverty (貧弱).

What use is it if there are many things like an empty sack? Among the not-so-many 30 poetry manuscripts, if only there were poems that are truly poem-like – the unfeigned outcry (絶叫) of a human – that would be enough,<58> and not only would I be satisfied, but it would also align with the original intention of this journal in undertaking this event. However, what could I do when I was made to be astonished for a second time? What could I do!

However, my so-called astonishment does not lie solely in the point that the submitted poems were not essays, nor prose, nor a section of a novel, and therefore not poetry, but merely passed as the vague impressions of each individual author. Absolutely not. Among the poems of my less-than-adept friends submitted this time, a great majority possessed truly great poetic ideas (詩想) but failed to express them as poetry. Saying this might give the impression of distinguishing between thought and expression – content (內容) and form (形式) – but the failure of the submitting friends this time truly lies not in the thought-content, but in the expression-form aspect.

Just as it is unclear when and by whose hand the term "New Poetry" (新詩) came into use, New Poetry currently has a sense akin to a kind of "sphinx" (스퓡쓰), like the two characters for "love" (戀愛), among our young men and women. Everyone writes without knowing what they are writing, and even for those who do write, there is still a kind of magical, indescribably strange atmosphere surrounding it. Consequently, many write and yet have no idea what they wrote or how.

Furthermore, the symbolist poetry (象徵詩) written by a segment of poets under the banner of some "-ism" or "school" (these poets, of course, write with considerable confidence; this is not in the least a criticism of their artistic attitude) seems to have, to some extent, openly caused beginners, who might become the future protagonists of our poetry scene, to wander lost on the path of "expression" (表現) - "form" (形式), thereby even disturbing (攪亂) their pure and sincere poetic thoughts. For example, the abuse (濫用) of unnecessary adjectives (形容詞), the uncritical imitative use (襲用) of symbolic terminology, and the obscuration (朦朧化) of content can only be called conspicuous trends.

Thus, among the 30 poems submitted this time, I could not find, no matter how hard I looked, any work that expressed the author's own intention directly and frankly (率直). Being in a busy situation, I specially took a day off work today and read the 30 poems roughly three times from morning till evening. It is something I cannot bear to misjudge, through my own carelessness, the works into which the submitting friends poured their full effort. But, but, no matter how I searched, there were no works that caught my eye. Many poems were like <59> a beginner in a foreign language groaning over an original text – indeed, poems that my intellect could not comprehend.

Thinking about how much sweat my 30 respected poet friends must have shed writing such difficult poems, beads of sweat on my own forehead instantly turned into salt stains, and it felt as if my back was being planed with ice. They are ones who want to learn, ones who want to know, ones who want to do, yet there has never been anyone to teach them, inform them, or enable them. Even if there were attempts, it is a fact that it was impossible for such people to exist.

When they intended to write poetry, they lacked too much naive sincerity (天眞性) to simply cry out like little birds, just as thoughts came, as they were pricked, as they felt. Thus, traces are unmistakably visible where they deliberately lengthened what could have been written simply, and arbitrarily shortened what should have been long. Many wrote relatively long poems, but this is taboo for beginners in poetry. Even for great poets with mature thoughts, long poems are easy to fail at, and considering the essence of poetry, one should definitely not write unreasonably long poems. Yet, the submitting friends mostly put much effort into writing long poems. This is my special advice to my friends: "The life of poetry lies, above all, in expressing our emotions most frankly, most simply, most directly. Please do not struggle with vain embellishments."

What I have written above, rather than calling it post-selection reflections, I would like to call my own meager repentance (懺悔). Lastly, I express my gratitude (謝禮) for the great efforts of the submitting friends and hope they will strive even more for our New Poetry. And I wish to add one more word: I hope for the rousing (奮起) of poets who have at least passed the beginner stage. Currently, our New Poetry is often used meaninglessly as filler (비인터 채임) for magazines or newspapers, or as decoration, or for flavor, while at the same time, many beginners who want to try writing poetry just write without any awareness, simply because others are writing. Gentlemen, I earnestly desire that you make the kingdom of poetry, your artistic domain (領域), become that of the modern era. Let us make it clearly known that if the sound of poetry is not heard where people live, that place, whatever kind of place it may be, cannot escape becoming a place of barbarism. -(Written on the night of the 16th day of the 6th month, Sinyu year [1921]) - <60>

小說選者 玄僖運 (Novel Selector Hyŏn Hŭiun)

Looking at the number of submissions received after this journal announced the call for prize literary works (懸賞文), the results seem quite favorable. Among them, concerning novels, one cannot help but be quite satisfied in terms of both number and volume. However, regarding the selection of novels, there were more than one or two difficult issues in determining the standard. The reason is that the very questions of how many writers who create (創作) novels exist in our so-called literary scene, and how many creative novels they publish, are problems to consider first. Indeed, when we compare the novel sector of our literary scene to those of other countries, we cannot help but feel despair. If the currently manifested literary scene is like this, then seeking hidden writers' creations from it, and trying to find something excellent therein, seems like a fantasy or an ideal. Although our creative ability (創作力) is thus meager, I believe there are many who have read with appreciation, or currently read with appreciation, works from the Western literary scene or the Japanese literary scene. Therefore, even though creative power may be weak due to current circumstances, appreciative ability (鑑賞力) does not necessarily run parallel (竝行) with creative power, and I believe there can be a significant difference. In other words, even if one is not proficient at writing, one may have considerable ability in reading. Therefore, when selecting these prize novels, it was very difficult to decide whether to set the standard purely by the criteria of the short story itself, or to standardize based on the current creative capacity of our literary scene. However, the selector has evaluated these various novels by compromising (折衷) between these two. Consequently, viewed strictly by the criteria of the short story itself, there might be a suspicion of excessive leniency, and viewed only from the perspective of our creative world, it might easily seem that the judgment is overly harsh (苛酷). I sincerely hope that esteemed readers will take into consideration beforehand the selection attitude taken by the selector.

However, even within such standards, it is quite regrettable (遺憾) that there is no first-prize winner. Among them, the second-prize winner, the novel "Moon" (달) by KS Saeng of Pojŏn School (普專校), has its plot gathered not from purely novelistic material but from sources of a kind of sad tale (哀話) or sad history (哀史), which is somewhat regrettable for a novel. Nevertheless, the coherence of its writing and the progression of its plot possess naturalness and profound charm (深曲味), holding the power to draw in the reader. The selector thinks that one-third of this novel's composition (成分) has novelistic flavor, and two-thirds possesses romance (로맨쓰) flavor. However, obtaining even this much in this prize novel competition is considered quite delightful, and at the same time, I wish for the future of KS Saeng's life as a writer.

Next, the third-prize winner, "Contradiction" (矛盾) by Mr. Hŏ Yŏngho (許永鎬君), <61> has a rather immature plot collection, making it questionable as a novel. However, the effort of laborious work (勞作) is visible, and since it cannot be said that there are absolutely no points worth taking, it was selected. But perhaps some readers might say that this is too generous.

And next, "Teacher H's Three Days" (H敎師의 三日間) by Mr. Hyangwŏl (香月君) could not be selected, nor can it be called an honorable mention (佳作). However, compared to the various other submitted novels, it was too regrettable to disregard (沒却) it entirely, considering the effort, the writing style, or the form. Thus, it was decided to publish it merely as an "out-of-selection" piece.

Looking at the total number of submissions, there is a sense of satisfaction, but looking at the content, one cannot help but say it is too meager (貧弱). Among them are works that seem almost ignorant of what a novel even is. The composition (構成) of a novel was discussed in the "Outline of the Novel" (小說槪要), published continuously from the first issue of this journal, so there is no need to repeat (飜覆) it now. However, if one intends to create a novel or appreciate a novel, I believe it would be necessary to consult it carefully as a reference. <62>

**Summary**

The article presents the reflections of four judges (Hyŏn Sangyun, Chang Ŭngjin, Kim Sŏksong, Hyŏn Hŭiun) for a literary contest sponsored by *Kaebyŏk* magazine in 1921, covering essays, short pieces, "new poetry," and novels. The judges express general disappointment with the quality and quantity of submissions, despite acknowledging some progress in writing skills compared to previous years. Key criticisms include: essays lacking relevance to contemporary social issues in Korea, overuse of unassimilated foreign terms (especially Sino-Japanese coinages) and poor distinction between literary and colloquial styles, poetry submissions being few, formally weak, overly influenced by symbolism without understanding, and favoring unnecessarily long forms, and novels lacking structural soundness and originality, often resembling sad anecdotes rather than true fiction. While some potential was noted (especially a second-place novel), the overall assessment highlights the immaturity of the Korean literary scene at the time.

Historians might find this article particularly interesting for its candid snapshot of the Korean literary world during the early colonial period (1921). It reveals the struggles of writers grappling with language modernization (Hanja vs. Hangul, foreign loanwords, literary vs. colloquial), the nascent development of modern genres like "new poetry" and the novel, the influence of foreign literary trends (Western, Japanese symbolism), and the explicit criteria (social relevance, originality, formal technique) applied by contemporary critics. The judges' frustrations underscore the challenges faced by intellectuals trying to forge a modern national literature under colonial constraints and rapid cultural change.

첫재 나는 이번 여러분의 글을 볼 때에 매우 깃버하얏습니다. 이는 대개 여러분의 글을 通하야 우리 社會의 얼마큼 進步된 것을 보앗습이외다. 爲先 그 中의 하나 둘을 들어 말하자면 思想의 健全한 것과 文辭의 暢達한 것이 그것이외다. 이것을 지금부터 4, 5年前에 比하면 훨신 나아갓다 할 수 잇습니다.
그러나 여러분의 選擇하신 題目이라던가 結搆하신 思想을 보건대 아즉도 좀 어떠하지 안흔가 하는 생각이 잇섯습니다. 적어도 社會問題에 關한 論文으로 하여서는 아즉도 不足하다고 아니할 수 업는가 하얏나이다. 그럼으로 不得已하야 이번에는 選拔의 標準을 社會問題에 두지 못하고 普通 文藝的 論文에 두엇나이다. 勿論 그 中에는 훌륭하게 社會問題에 關한 論文이 不少하얏스나 그러나 그것은 問題가 넘우 朝鮮現下의 實際하는 因緣이 적은 것도 잇고 또는 그런 因緣은 잇다할지라도 그런 論文으로 하야서는 족음 結搆라던가 立法이 未洽한 듯한 故로 차라리 이번에는 勇斷하야 보는 標準을 單純한 文藝 卽 文章으로 옴겻나이다.
그 다음 여러분의 글을 볼 때에 생각나는 것은 여러분의 쓰시는 用語가 넘우 外語를 그대로 襲用한 것이라 하는 일이외다. 이것은 何必 여러분의 글뿐만이 아니외다. - 近來 朝鮮안에서 쓰는 文字가 모도 다 이런 缺點이 잇습니다. 툭하면 外語를 그대로 씁니다. 이것도 西洋말이나 가트면 或 그대로 쓰는 것이-아니 오히려 그대로 쓰는 것이 - 나흘는지 모르겟습니다. 그러나 이것은 漢字로 쓰이는 때문에 朝鮮사람의 생각으로나 純中國사람의 생각으로는 全然히 意味가 無한 것이-아니 자뭇 달라지는 것이-例事외다. 그럼으로 여러분이 이것은 이 압흐로라도 注意치 안흐시면 안될 것인가 합니다.
그 다음 또 한가지 여러분에게 注意할 것은 여러분이 文語와 口語를 分別치 못하시는 일이외다. 이것은 여러분이 글을 쓰시랴할 때에 무엇보다도 먼저 注意하실 것이외다. 웨 그러랴 하면 누구던지 글을 보아갈 때에 얼마동안은 『이리하외다. 이러케 하시오』하다가 또 瞥眼間에 『그러하도다. 이러하니라』하고 나오면 그 陳述이 爲先 態度를 보아 穩當치 못한＜57＞것을 感할 수 잇는 까닭이외다.
그밧게도 여러가지를 말슴들일 것이 만히 잇사오나 그러나 몸이 甚히 바빠서 아즉 이만 사르고 남아는 오즉 여러분의 寬恕와 容許를 비나이다.

新詩選者 金石松
내가 이번에 開闢 1周年記念號의 特別附錄인 懸賞文藝品 中에 新詩의 考選을 맛게된 것은 分에 過한 榮光으로 생각하는 同時에 度에 極한 躊躇를 한 것이다. 그러나 꿩쓸대 닭이라도 쓰는 세음으로 編輯하는 여러 先生의 嚴命임으로 할 일업시 이번에는 그대로 하여 보게 되엇다.

⌈詩人이 詩를 作함을 快樂이라 하려니와 詩를 論함은 苦痛⌋이라 함은 謨 詩人의 말이어니와 ⌈詩人은 詩를 作하는 者요 詩를 評하는 者는 아니라⌋고. 나는 말하랴 한다. 딸아서 (내가 詩人으로 自處함이 아니라) 내가 이러한 일을 함은 아모리 하야도 나 自身에 對하야 無意味한 일일뿐 아니라 나의 敬愛하는 詩友들에게도 아모 有益이 업슬 듯하다.

理想은 理想대로 내바려두자. 어찌하얏던지 나는 이번에 應募하는 成績이 매우 조흘 줄로 미덧다. 그것은 數年來의 文藝熱?에 依하야. 더욱히 ⌈新詩⌋家?의 簇生에 依하야. 그러나 事實은 事實을 直言하야 나는 마츰내 失望치 아니치 못하얏다. 應募點數 30篇!. 이야말로 아모리 우리의 갓갓지 못한 文壇이라 할지라도 놀랄만한 貧弱을 表明하는 證據이엇다.
