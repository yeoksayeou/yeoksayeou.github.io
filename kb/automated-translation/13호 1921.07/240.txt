Article Title: 오! 棺짜는 木手야! (Oh! Coffin-making Carpenter!)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Oh! Coffin-making Carpenter!
						Kim Sŏksong

Oh! Coffin-making Carpenter!
Whose hair is flecked with white,
Kind-hearted old Carpenter!
Inside the coffin you are making,
A person's corpse (屍體) will go!
(You know this clearly)

Oh—Coffin-making Carpenter!
But you too,
In the near future (將來), certainly (叮嚀히),
Will need (必要) such a coffin.
The coffin you now earnestly (熱心) craft,
And the coffin you will enter—
Whose hands, indeed, will build it!
-(Excerpted from (Beneath the Tomb of my Great-Grandmother (曾祖母의墓下)))- <121>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏksong contemplates mortality through a direct address to an elderly carpenter making a coffin. The speaker acknowledges the carpenter's craft and awareness of death but reminds him that he too will eventually need a coffin, pondering who will then perform this final task for the carpenter himself. The poem reflects on the universal nature of death and the cycle of life and craftsmanship. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into personal, existential reflections published in *Kaebyŏk*, a significant nationalist and intellectual magazine during the Japanese colonial period. Its simple, direct style and focus on an ordinary craftsman and the theme of death provide insight into the cultural and emotional landscape of early colonial Korea. The final note situating the poem's origin adds a layer of personal grief.

오! 棺짜는 木手야!

오!棺짜는 木手야!

마음씨고흔 늙은木手야!

그러나 너도
가까운將來에 叮嚀히
그따위棺이 必要하리라
네가 이제熱心으로 맨드는
그리고 네가들어갈 棺은
果然누구의 손을빌을지!
