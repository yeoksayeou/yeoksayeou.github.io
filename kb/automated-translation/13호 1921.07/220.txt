Magazine Title: 개벽 제13호
Publication Date: 1921년 07월 01일
Article Title: 傷者의 呻吟 (Groan of the Wounded)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Groan of the Wounded
						Kim Sŏk-song

Groan of the Wounded!
That is Humanity's
Eternal Agony!

That—the spiritually wounded
Even with 20th Century Medical Arts
Even with the Embrace of Pure Love
Utterly, utterly
Cannot be treated!
No, Love—that is what
Only makes his wound fester

Ah! Groan of the Wounded!
That is Humanity's
Eternal Agony! <120>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏk-song laments the incurable suffering of the spiritually wounded, portraying it as humanity's eternal agony. The poet emphasizes that neither modern medicine nor even pure love can heal this deep-seated pain; in fact, love might exacerbate it. From a historical perspective, this poem, published in 1921 during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted as reflecting the profound despair and psychological wounds inflicted upon individuals or the Korean nation under colonial rule. Historians might find it interesting as an expression of existential suffering and disillusionment, possibly veiled commentary on the inadequacy of external solutions (like modernization or superficial appeasement) for deep national or personal trauma.
