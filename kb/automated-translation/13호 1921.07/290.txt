발행일	1921년 07월 01일
기사제목	하믈레트 (Hamlet)
필자	쉑스피아(原作), 玄哲(譯述)
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Hamlet
						Shakespeare (Original), Hyŏn Ch'ŏl (Translation/Adaptation)
Act 1
Scene 2
The same. Elsinore Castle. A large hall within the castle.
Trumpets sound. King Claudius enters first, followed by Queen Gertrude, then Prince Hamlet, Lord Chamberlain Polonius, his son Laertes, courtiers Voltemand and Cornelius, and many other nobles and attendants.

King. Though the passing of King Hamlet (國王「하믈레트」王), my brother bound by flesh and blood (骨肉), occurred not yet a full month ago, it is deeply fitting for us to be immersed in profound sorrow (悲歎), and for the entire nation (擧國) to wear brows furrowed with grief (愁眉). This is naturally right according to reason (事理). However, since the great change (大變) has already occurred, merely spending time in empty sorrow (空然히 悲哀로만 歲月을 보내는 것) cannot be called such virtuous conduct. Therefore, suppressing our feelings with reason, battling against human sentiment, while deeply mourning (슯허하면서도) the late King (故王), I also cannot forget my duty (本分) as the sovereign (國主). In other words, enduring unbearable sorrow (悲歎), sharing joys and sorrows (悲喜哀歡), with one eye weeping and the other holding back a smile, we have respectfully completed the funeral rites (因山) and, clearing away tears, performed the wedding ceremony (新婚의 式). As you lords (卿等) know, establishing the former King's Queen, my sister-in-law (兄嫂), Gertrude (「카아쭐우드」), now again as Queen, and sharing the sovereignty (主權) of this Denmark (「덴마아크」), was done after consulting with wise lords like yourselves and fully following the counsel of the court (朝議), which I consider deeply satisfying... However, there is one more thing to say, which you lords likely already know. Young Fortinbras (年少輕薄者 「포오틴풀라쓰」), perhaps thinking me an inept ruler (庸主), or perhaps hearing of my brother King's (兄皇) death and speculating (臆測) that the country<149> is in turmoil, believing in some dreamlike fancy, troublesomely sends messengers (使者), babbling about recovering the territories (領土) his father, Fortinbras (其父 「포오틴풀라쓰」), ceded years ago (年前) to our valiant brother King based on the clear terms (明文) of a contract (契約). Be that as it may... the reason I have gathered you all here today is this: Fortinbras's uncle (叔父), the King of Norway (「놀웨이」王), is severely afflicted (沈重) by old age and illness (老病) in recent years and confined to his bed. Not only is he unaware of such schemes (陰謀), but also, since those mobilized (徵募) and levied (賦役) for this current effort are all his own subjects (部下), he likely lacks the power to stop them. The reasons for this are recorded (記錄) here... Lords Cornelius (「고오네리야쓰」) and Voltemand (「볼올티만드」), you shall serve as messengers (使者) on this matter to old Norway (老「놀웨이」). The authority (權利) entrusted (委任) to you extends only to the details (細目) recorded in this letter; no other power is granted. Please return swiftly (速히 復命) and show your loyal hearts (忠勤의 直心).
Cor. Whatever the stern command (嚴命), I shall strive to fulfill it with loyalty (忠勤).
Vol.
King. I trust in that as well. May your journey be safe (無事).
Cornelius and Voltemand exit.
Now, Laertes (「레에야아티이쓰」), what is it you wish to say? What request (所願) do you have? If it is a request that accords with reason (道理에 合當한 所願), is there any reason (理致) why the King of Denmark (「덴마아크」人君) would not hear it? Laertes, if it is something you ask (請), I shall grant your wish even before you ask. The bond (情分) between your father and this Danish throne (「덴마아크」王座) is closer than head (頭) and heart (心), more intimate (密接) than hand (手) and mouth (口). Laertes, what is your wish?
Laer. Your Majesty (惶悚하오나), I humbly request your royal permission (勅許) to return again to France (佛蘭西國). I returned joyfully to celebrate (奉祝) Your Majesty's coronation (卽位大典), but now that the grand ceremony (盛典) has passed smoothly and my official duties (公務) as a humble servant (小臣) are completed, I wish to go again to France. I humbly beg (伏乞) that you perceive (洞察) my sincere feelings (衷情).
King. Have you then received your father's permission (父命)? What are Lord Polonius's (「뽀로오니냐쓰」卿) thoughts (意向)?
Pol. Your Majesty (惶恐하오나), after much discussion (問答) regarding my son's earnest desire (懇切한 願望), I inevitably<150> consented (承諾) to his return. I humbly hope (伏望) Your Majesty will grant the royal command (勅敎) according to his wish.
King. Laertes (「레야아티이쓰」), then choose an auspicious day (吉日) and depart. Time (時日) is yours (卿의 物件이다). May you spend your days fruitfully (滋味잇는 歲月). ...But now, Hamlet (「하믈레트」), 'nephew-prince' (姪王) was what I called you before. Now, my son...
Ham. (Aside) A little more than kin (親戚 以上) outwardly (外面), but less than kind (親戚 以下) inwardly (內心).
King. Is there discontent (不平) in your heart? Why do the clouds of worry never lift from your brow (眉間)?
Ham. Far from clouds of worry, Your Majesty, I am too much i' the sun (넘우 볏이 들어 견디기 어려울 地境이옵니다).
Queen. Nay, Hamlet (「하믈레트」), cast off (거더버리고) the clouds of worry hidden in your brow and serve His present Majesty (今上陛下), who is so affectionate (多情), with sincerity (精誠). What use is there in forever mourning (思慕) your father (父君) who rests in the underworld (冥府)? Is it not so that where there is life, there is death? People pass through this world to the eternal future (永劫의 未來). Isn't this the common way (常事) of this fleeting world (浮世)?
Ham. Ay, madam (어머니), it is common (常事).
Queen. If it be, why seems it so particular with thee?
Ham. Seems, madam! Nay, it *is* (『그런것』이올시다). I know not 'seems' (『보인다』고 하시는 것은 알 수 업지요). Truly, what I hold within my heart (마음 가운대 먹음고 잇는 것을) cannot be expressed (發表) by this inky cloak (잉크빗의 外套), nor this customary suit of solemn black (定例의 喪服), nor windy suspiration of forced breath (일부러 품어내는 한숨), no, nor the fruitful river in the eye (샘(泉)가티 흘러내리는 눈물), nor the dejected 'havior of the visage (이마에 줄음을 짓고 눈귀를 첫더리며 입을 비쭉어리는 것)—nay, all the forms (儀式), modes (禮節), shows (外容) of grief in the whole world (온 世上). These indeed seem, for they are actions that a man might play (보이는 것이요 또 누구라도 흉내낼 수 잇는 것이지마는). But I have that within which passeth show (눈에 보이지 아니하는 것이 잇습니다); these but the trappings (形式) and the suits (衣服) of woe.
King. Hamlet (「하믈레트야」), it is sweet and commendable in your nature to give these mourning duties to your deceased father (父王). It shows a son's utmost filial piety (孝子의 至極한 情理). But you must consider (생각해볼 것은) that your father lost (失) his father, that father lost<151> his. It is a natural duty (當然한 情義) for a son (子息) to show sorrow (哀悼) with due rites (禮節). But to persevere in obstinate condolence (넘우 지나서 哀傷으로만 歲月을 보낸다면) is, first, impious stubbornness (神明에게 不敬한 것); 'tis unmanly grief (男子답지못한 愁嘆); it shows a will most incorrect to heaven (한울에 對한 非禮), a heart unfortified (마음에 信心업는 証據), a mind impatient (短慮), an understanding simple and unschooled (無智, 愚味한 表示). For what we know must be (죽음은 이 必然으로 돌아오는 일이요), and is as common as any the most vulgar thing to sense (五官으로서 깨달을만한 一般의 常事인줄 알면서도), why should we in our peevish opposition take it to heart (마음을 거슬리고 가슴에 새겨서 이를 嘆息만 하는가)? Fie! 'tis a fault to heaven (가장 穩當치 못한 일이다. 이와가튼 일은 한울을 거스르는 것이고), fault against the dead (죽은 사람을 배반하는 것이며), fault to nature (自然을 어기며), to reason most absurd (道理를 어그러지게 하는 것이다), whose common theme is death of fathers (어버이가 먼저 죽는 것은 道理에 가장 普通의 일이요), and who still hath cried, from the first corse till he that died to-day, 'This must be so' (또한 『이것이 必然의 事』이라고 부르짓는 것이다). Hamlet (「하믈레트」), I pray thee, throw to earth this unprevailing woe (無益의 悲嘆), and think of me as of a father (나를 참으로 나흔 아비로 알아라). For let the world take note, you are the most immediate to our throne (나의 가장 直接되는 王座의 繼續者이요), and with no less nobility of love than that which dearest father bears his son do I impart toward you (내가 慈父와 다르지 아니하는 恩愛로써 너를 撫育하는 줄 알아라). For your intent in going back to school in Wittenberg (「빅덴빠아크」의 大學에 다시 가랴고 하는 것은), it is most retrograde to our desire (나의 가장 깃거하지안는 바이다). And we beseech you, bend you to remain here in the cheer and comfort of our eye, our chiefest courtier (近親), cousin (重臣), and our son (나의 사랑으로써 나의 面前에 머물기를 바란다).
Queen. Let not thy mother lose her prayers, Hamlet (「하믈레트」야 어미의 바라는 마음을 저버리지 말아라). I pray thee, stay with us (으응, 여긔 잇고); go not to Wittenberg (「빅덴빠아크」에는 가지를 말아라).
Ham. I shall in all my best obey you, madam (抑制하여 말슴대로 하지요).
King. Why, 'tis a loving and a fair reply (그것은 참 奇特한 對答이다). Be as ourself in Denmark (이 「던마아크」에 머물러 우리들과 한가지 歲月을 보내자). ...Madam, come; this gentle and unforced accord of Hamlet (「하믈레트」의 柔順한 對答은) sits smiling to my heart (나의 마음으로 울어나오는 깃붐을 참지 못하겟다). In grace whereof, no jocund health that Denmark drinks to-day (只今으로부터 祝賀하는 잔치를 열고), but the great cannon to the clouds shall tell (同時에 祝砲를 구룸에 向하면), and the king's rouse the heaven shall bruit again, re-speaking earthly thunder (한울도 또한 王家의 萬歲를 깃버하여 地上의 霹靂과 서로 應하겟지). Come away (자아 저리로)...
All exit except Hamlet. (Trumpets sound again at this time)
Ham. O, that this too too solid flesh (여물고 단단한 이 고기덩이) would melt, thaw and resolve itself into<152> a dew (이슬과 가티 되엇스면)! Or that the Everlasting (한우님) had not fix'd His canon 'gainst self-slaughter (自殺을 큰 罪라고 하지나 아니하엿스면)! O God (한우님)! God! How weary, stale, flat and unprofitable (無聊하고도 空虛하며 平凡과 無益으로만) seem to me all the uses of this world (이 世上의 萬가지의 營造)! Fie on't! ah fie! 'tis an unweeded garden (열매를 매지랴고 하는 毒한 풀이 茂盛한 거츤 뜰(荒庭)) that grows to seed; things rank and gross in nature (내음새 나고 더러운 물건) possess it merely. That it should come to this! But two months dead (돌아가신지 오즉 두달)...nay, not so much, not two (아니 두달도 다 못되어서). So excellent a king (類에 드믄 國君)! that was, to this, Hyperion to a satyr (太陽의 神明과 獸羊의 怪物); so loving to my mother (母后) that he might not beteem the winds of heaven visit her face (面上) too roughly (거츤 바람 한번 쏘이지 아니하랴고 하시던 父皇). Heaven and earth (天地)! Must I remember (생각지 아니하면 아니될 것가)? Why, she would hang on him, as if increase of appetite had grown by what it fed on (사랑하시면 사랑하실스록 사랑과 貞操로써 縋從하랴고 하던 母后가). And yet, within a month (한달이 겨우 지날지 말지 하여)... Let me not think on't (아아, 그런 일은 생각을 마라야지 생각을)...Frailty (弱한 물건아), thy name is woman (계집이라고 하는 것이 너의 별명이다)! A little month (겨우 한달), or ere those shoes were old with which she follow'd my poor father's body, like Niobe (「니오베」와 가티), all tears (눈물을 흘리고 靈樞를 딸튼 그 신의 몬지도 털기 前에)...she, even she (母后가, 母后까지도)... O God! a beast (五倫을 모르는 禽獸), that wants discourse of reason, would have mourn'd longer (아즉동안은 슯헐 할 것인데)...married with my uncle (叔父되는 사람과 또 婚姻을 하다니), my father's brother (父皇의 親同氣하고), but no more like my father than I to Hercules (이 「하믈레트」가 「하아기유리이쯔」에 가튼 것만치도 갓지 아니한 아우와). Within a month (한달도 지나지 아니한 사이에)? Ere yet the salt of most unrighteous tears (거짓말 눈물이라도) had left the flushing in her galled eyes (눈시울에 시치어 붉은 자리가 업서지기도 前). O, most wicked speed (無慚! 非道!), to post with such dexterity to incestuous sheets (姦邪하고 淫蕩한 자리에서 기다리고 잇섯던 것가티 그처럼 急히)! It is not nor it cannot come to good (이것이 반듯이 조흔 兆朕은 아니다. 必然코 凶惡한 일의 前兆이다). But break, my heart (으응, 이 가슴이 터질대로); for I must hold my tongue (누가 오는 것갓다)!
Horatio (「호레에시오」), Marcellus (「마아세라쓰」), and Bernardo (「빠아나아드어」) enter.
Hor. Hail to your lordship's well-being (殿下의 康寧을 비옵나이다).
Ham. I am glad to see you well (그대도 無事한가. 맛나니 반가웨). Horatio (「호레에시오」)—or I do forget myself (아마 「호레에시오」가튼데 或 내가 잘못보앗나).
Hor. The same, my lord (틀님업시 臣 「호레에시오」이옵니다), and your poor servant ever.
Ham. Sir, my good friend (아아 나의 兄아); I'll change that name with you (나는 그대의 불으는 이름을 바꾼다). And what make you from Wittenberg (「빅덴빠아크」), Horatio? ...Marcellus (「마아세라쓰」)!
Mar. My good lord (殿下께옵서는)...<153>
Ham. I am very glad to see you. (To Bernardo) Good even, sir (아아 너도 또한...). But what, in faith, make you from Wittenberg (「빅덴빠아크」)?
Hor. A truant disposition (懶惰한 性質), good my lord.
Ham. I would not hear your enemy (怨誰) say so (그러한 險談은 그대의 怨誰가 하더래도 고지 들리지 아니한다), nor shall you do mine ear that violence, to make it truster of your own report against yourself (하물며 自身이 말하는 그것을 고지 들을 듯 한가). I know you are no truant (그대가 어찌 계을느다고 하나). But what is your affair in Elsinore (「엘시노오아」)? We'll teach you to drink deep ere you depart (잘못한다는 逗留할 동안에 暴飮을 배우기 쉬울걸).
Hor. My lord, I came to see your father's funeral (實相은 先皇陛下의 因山에 參拜하랴고 왓읍니다).
Ham. I pray thee, do not mock me, fellow-student (거짓말이지); I think it was to see my mother's wedding (아마도 母后의 婚禮式을 보러온 것이지).
Hor. Indeed, my lord, it follow'd hard upon (그러타고 하시면 이어서 큰 慶事).
Ham. Thrift (經濟)! Thrift (節險), Horatio! the funeral baked meats (靈前에 쓴던 구은 고기) did coldly furnish forth the marriage tables (婚姻床에다 벌여놋는 冷淡한 饗應이다). Would I had met my dearest foe (不共戴天之讎) in heaven (天國) ere I had ever seen that day (그러한 슯흔 날을 當하는 것 가트면 차라리 不共戴天之讎와 天國에서 맛나는 것이 낫겟다)!...Horatio (「호레에시오」)? Methinks I see my father (先皇陛下가...아버지의 얼굴이 보이는 것갓다).
Hor. Where, my lord (에에, 어대 계신가요)?
Ham. In my mind's eye (나의 마음 눈에).
Hor. I saw him once (臣도 일즉이 謁見한 일이 잇삽거니와); he was a goodly king (참으로 莊嚴하옵신 그 英姿).
Ham. He was a man, take him for all in all (어떠한 곳에서 아모리 調査를 하여도 다시 볼 수 업는 그 氣質), I shall not look upon his like again.
Hor. My lord (殿下)? I think I saw him yesternight (어제밤에 先皇陛下를 謁見한 듯 합니다).
Ham. Saw? who (謁見을 하다니 누구에게)?
Hor. My lord, the king your father (先父皇陛下께).
Ham. The king my father (父皇陛下께)!
Hor. Season your admiration for a while with an attent ear (놀라지 마시고 마음을 姑定하셔서 들으기를 바랍니다), till I may deliver, upon the witness of these gentlemen (그 證人은 여긔 잇는 이 두 사람 이올시다), this marvel to you (近來에 업는 不可思議의 한 條件).
Ham. For God's love, let me hear (어대 좀 仔細히 들어보자).
Hor. Two nights together (이틀밤이나 連이어서) had these gentlemen (여긔 잇는 이 두 士官), Marcellus (「마아세라쓰」) and Bernardo (「빠아나아드어가」), on their watch (守直을 하고 잇슬 그때), in the dead vast and middle of the night (草木도 한가지 잠든 正밤中), been thus encounter'd. A figure like your father (世上에도 異狀한 容姿를 보앗습니다), armed at point exactly, cap-a-pe (그 形軆가 머리끗으로부터 발톱끗까지 甲冑투구를 한 것이 족음도 틀림업시 先皇陛下의 玉軆로 나타나서), appears before them, and with solemn march (凜然한<154>步調로) goes slow and stately by them (徐徐이 지나가십디다): thrice he walk'd by their oppress'd and fear-surprised eyes (두 사람의 압으로 두서너자나 사이를 隔하여), within his truncheon's length (戰戰兢兢하는); whilst they, distilled almost to jelly with the act of fear (놀란 남아지에 肝膽이 떨어저서), stand dumb and speak not to him (입을 다물고 선 채로 한마디 말도 못햇다고 벌벌 떨면서 그런 말을 합디다). This to me in dreadful secrecy impart they did (臣이 이 두 士官으로 더불어); and I with them the third night kept the watch (第3日 밤에 守直을 해본즉): where, as they had deliver'd, both in time, form of the thing (들은 바와 틀림업시 時刻과 形軆가), each word made true and good, the apparition comes (털끗마치도 어김이 업시 나타나는 怪異한 幻影). I knew your father (先皇陛下의 玉軆와 갓기는 姑捨하고); these hands are not more like (두 손까지 갓지안흠이 업습디다).
Ham. But where was this (그래서 그곳은)?
Mar. My lord, upon the platform where we watch (저이들 守直하고 잇는 高臺이옵니다).
Ham. Did you not speak to it (무슨 말을 못해보앗나)?
Hor. My lord, I did (말은 해 보앗지마는); but answer made it none (답은 하지 안흐시고): yet once methought it lifted up its head and did address itself to motion, like as it would speak (그래도 한번은 머리를 드시고 무슨 말슴을 하시랴고 하실 지음에); but even then the morning cock crew loud (때마츰 울어나는 닭(鷄)의 소리에), and at the sound it shrunk in haste away, and vanish'd from our sight (소스러처 사라저 업습디다).

**Summary**

This text is a Korean translation of William Shakespeare's *Hamlet*, Act 1, Scene 2, published in the magazine *Kaebyŏk* (개벽) in July 1921. The scene depicts King Claudius addressing his court at Elsinore, justifying his recent marriage to Queen Gertrude, his brother's widow, and addressing the political threat from young Fortinbras of Norway. Laertes requests and receives permission to return to France. Claudius and Gertrude urge Hamlet to cease his prolonged mourning for his father. Left alone, Hamlet delivers his famous "O, that this too too solid flesh would melt" soliloquy, expressing despair and disgust at his mother's hasty remarriage. Finally, Horatio, Marcellus, and Bernardo arrive and tell Hamlet about the ghost of his father they have seen on the battlements, prompting Hamlet to resolve to see it himself.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, this translation is significant. The publication of a major Western literary work like *Hamlet* in Korean, complete with Korean transliterations for names and places, demonstrates an effort to engage with world culture and maintain Korean linguistic and cultural identity during a period of intense Japanese assimilation pressure. *Kaebyŏk* magazine itself was a prominent intellectual and cultural journal associated with the Ch'ŏndogyo religion, often pushing nationalist and enlightenment themes within the constraints of colonial censorship. Translating and disseminating such a complex work indicates a sophisticated readership and a desire among Korean intellectuals like the translator, Hyŏn Ch'ŏl (玄哲), to modernize Korean literature and thought by introducing foreign classics.
