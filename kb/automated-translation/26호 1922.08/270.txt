Magazine Title: 開闢 제26호
Publication Date: 1922년 08월 01일
Article Title: 깁히 밋든 心誠 (Deeply Trusted Sincerity)
Author: 素月
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

깁히 밋든 心誠이 荒凉한 내 가슴 속을<25>
오고 가는 두서너舊友를 보면서 말이,
「이제는 당신네들도 다 쓸데업구려!」<26>

Deeply trusted sincerity, in my desolate heart<25>
Watching two or three old friends (舊友) come and go, the thought comes,
"Now, even you all are quite useless!"<26>

**Summary**

This short poem by Sowŏl expresses a profound sense of disillusionment and emptiness. The speaker reflects on their once deeply held beliefs or sincerity ("心誠") within their now "desolate heart." Seeing old friends come and go only reinforces this feeling, leading to the stark conclusion that even these relationships are now "useless." From a historical perspective, this poem, published in 1922 during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted by historians as reflecting not just personal despair but potentially a broader societal disillusionment or loss of faith among intellectuals or the populace facing the harsh realities of occupation, where old bonds and beliefs seemed inadequate or futile.
