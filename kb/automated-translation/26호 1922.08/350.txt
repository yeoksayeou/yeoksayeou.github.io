발행일	1922년 08월 01일
기사제목	小說 滬上의 눈물 (Novel: Tears on the Shanghai Bund)
필자	獨山
기사형태	소설 (Novel)

小說 滬上의 눈물
						獨山

One
Twelve miles west of the bustling Shanghai cityscape, where the struggle for existence boils, lies a university run by Germans at Wusong Bay (吳淞砲灣), the intersection of the Yangtze River (楊子江) and the Yellow Sea (黃海). As it is managed by Germans, whose scientific development has reached its peak, it emphasizes practical sciences (形而下學) over metaphysical studies (形而上學問). Among these, the engineering department (工科) is particularly renowned. Though China has various specialized schools and universities, none, whether run by Chinese or foreigners, can match this school in engineering. Located away from the bustling, complex "London of the East" (東洋倫敦) with its myriad worldly distractions, adjacent to the elegant and clean German district, where the sea breeze gently blows, this school is a very suitable place for student cultivation. There are 7 or 8 Korean (高麗) students at this school, among whom Kim Ilsŏn is currently studying diligently in the Mechanical Engineering department. Not only does he possess natural genius, but he is also famous throughout the school for his enthusiasm. When he fails to grasp something in the laboratory, he forgets to eat. Thus, his experiments have never ended in failure. He sleeps only four hours a day. Consequently, even though he has such a sanguine temperament, his face has recently become quite pale. Friends who know him are very worried and sometimes even advise him to take a break from his studies. However, he says that since he considers this his calling (天職), he would not hesitate even to sacrifice himself for it. He plans to establish at least 13 factories and at least 3 engineering colleges with his own hands after graduation. He also intends to extend the railway network within 15 years to be five times longer than what is currently <37> laid, crisscrossing our Korean land like a spiderweb. He is full of great promise. The German teachers call him *die wunder gabe* (prodigy), and his peers, especially the German students, call him the "Korean Boy Engineer" (高麗少年工學者). His family is also well-off. Therefore, he has never experienced financial hardship regarding tuition fees. He has parents and even grandparents at home. Thus, among his friends, he receives much praise and envy, with comments like, "You are truly a lucky child. If I were you..." Ah, but for some reason, he always feels dissatisfaction and loneliness. He constantly lives in anguish and pain. He always feels like he is suffering from something, like something is missing. He wants to discard something he possesses and strives to grasp something else. While his concentration on studies is one reason, it seems that lately, due to some pain?, his body has become very thin, his face worn, and it even seems like his mental state has become abnormal. If he had any pain, it was this: if only one person like me experiences joy, at least a certain society should experience it together; if I feel pain, this entire 'world' must be in pain, so how can I eliminate pain for every person in this world and let them enjoy happiness? However, he definitely seems to feel something. It appears he has some deficiency (欠處). It seems he is determined to fill this deficiency. In any case, it seems factual that he has received a severe blow to his spirit. The phenomenon of him sometimes looking up from his book, clasping his hands, staring blankly outside, or resting his head on his arms on the desk, seemingly lost in thought, is something not seen before. There were times he went into the wooded park after dinner and returned after midnight, and he increasingly took early morning walks along the banks of the Yangtze River. Those who didn't know might call it strolling, but for him, it was definitely not strolling. Yet, he doesn't speak of this to anyone and tries not to reveal it in any way, not even slightly. The only person who knows him and understands him is Yi Kwangil, who lives with him in Room OO of the T University dormitory. Ilsŏn and Kwangil have been close friends for a long time. They studied in the same grade since middle school 7 or 8 years ago and ate and slept in the same dormitory. They hated being separated even for a short while. During school vacations, when other students all went home, Ilsŏn and Kwangil <38> disliked parting and customarily stayed together a few more days before leaving. Even after returning home, they couldn't stay long without seeing each other and would travel the long 40-li road back and forth to play together. If they couldn't meet for just a few days, they corresponded by letter. Then, when the school term began, they would take the same train together. Then, in the spring a year before graduation, that is, during the turmoil (風雲) three years ago, Ilsŏn and Kwangil became prisoners behind the same iron bars (鐵窓) for the same incident, spending a long year together in the same cell. On the day they were released from prison life, they made a decision and clasped hands, resolving to cross the Yellow Sea (黃海). Now, Ilsŏn and Kwangil study in the same department at the same school and live together in the same dormitory. This should suffice to gauge the depth of their friendship. Therefore, Ilsŏn considered Kwangil his one and only true friend in the world. Ilsŏn hid nothing from Kwangil. One day, Ilsŏn said to Kwangil, "Look here, Mr. Yi! I truly cannot endure it anymore. An unknown charm presses down on me, and something irresistible pulls my hand! It seems there isn't a single point of light (光明) before me, only darkness (暗黑) approaching! This morning too, I went to the bank of the Yangtze River and was about to take one more step, but again something pulled my back, and I ended up returning! You probably don't know, but the other evening too, I was about to throw myself from the upper floor of the Wing On Department Store (永安公司) but stopped because I hated the sight of the people milling below!" Kwangil had been very worried seeing the mental blow Ilsŏn had suffered recently. But hearing these words now, he had not expected such a drastic change. Hearing this, Kwangil couldn't help but be startled. Kwangil himself had been away from home for several years and, due to circumstances, couldn't often hear news from home. How could he have imagined that his only friend, with whom he had set out hand in hand, would fall into such a perilous state? A kind of indescribable sorrow arose in his heart, and he quietly shed tears. Then he said, "Look here, Ilsŏn! Solving this problem is perhaps not such an urgent matter! Wouldn't it be not too late even after we achieve all our goals? Shouldn't we just be faithful to the present (現在)?" However, Ilsŏn didn't offer much of a reply to this. It seemed there was some firm resignation (斷念) in his heart. Moreover, the pain that had arisen recently could not be relieved by anyone's words, <39> and his wound (傷處) had become utterly untreatable by any tangible (有形) means. Therefore, even Kwangil's words, which usually sounded so reliable, were too weak to console his loneliness. Kwangil sighed deeply. From the Wusong Fort (吳淞砲臺), the noon gun (午砲) sounded with a bang.

Two
Ilsŏn is a 21-year-old youth, full of vigorous spirit (英氣潑潑). Although pimples now sprout on his face and dark stubble appears below his upper lip, until he was 15 or 16, he was a beautiful boy (美少年) with a good complexion and a face that smiled readily. Among his peers, he was famous for his talent and liveliness. People meeting him for the first time called him handsome (美男子), and the village elders predicted a promising future for him. His family lives in a small town in Ŭiju, but as an established family (世家), they hold a respectable position and possess considerable assets (財産), lacking little. His father entered business early and, although a village gentleman (村냥반), frequently traveled to places like P'yŏngyang and Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul), and had also traversed various regions of China. He was not only proficient in business but also had much understanding of worldly affairs (世事) and was well-acquainted with new trends (新風潮). Therefore, Mr. Kim Taesŏng was known by almost everyone in the neighboring towns. He realized early on that children's education (子女敎育) was the top priority. Thus, he sent his six children to study abroad in Tokyo, China, Kyŏngsŏng, and other places. Although the family fortune had declined somewhat compared to before, he did not cease his enthusiastic efforts in the slightest. Some nearby elders (令監) who didn't understand his intentions even tried to dissuade him, asking what he would do in the future if he spent all the family assets educating his children like this now. However, he held fast to the conviction (信念) that if he just provided them with good cultivation (修養), they would each manage their own lives without worry. Ilsŏn is the eldest son among four brothers. From childhood, he received immense love from his parents. Being bright-faced, he was specially favored by every teacher since his elementary school days. When he attended S Middle School, an upperclassman named P eventually fell into ruin because of Ilsŏn. Thus, when he turned 16 or 17, many marriage proposals (婚議) arose from all directions. Among them were some suitable prospects. Until then, Ilsŏn lacked thorough self-awareness (自覺), but he had occasionally read love novels (戀愛小說), and he had also observed (觀察) how many modern youths suffered <40> due to their parents' unreasonable marriages (不合理한 結婚) and the unspeakable tragi-comedies that frequently resulted. Therefore, he intended not to concern himself with the marriage issue until his own discernment (眼目) reached a certain level. He wanted to do things by his own free will (自由意思) after reaching a sufficient understanding with his partner (相對者). Not only that, but his teachers and friends, knowing his great potential, also advised him never to act rashly in any matter. His father, also having early enlightenment, had rejected all the more than 30 marriage proposals, saying he was still young and there was no need for it yet. However, there was some difference in perspective (見地) between Ilsŏn's reluctance and his father's rejection. His father simply thought he should at least finish middle school since he was still studying. Ilsŏn, of course, also intended not to do anything before finishing his current school. On this point, he agreed with his father. However, regarding schooling, Ilsŏn did not plan to stop at just graduating from middle school. He intended to go at least as far as a specialized university (專門大學). Regarding marriage, he aimed to take a step further and decide things based on his own intentions even after graduation.
It was the spring when Ilsŏn turned eighteen. Graduation exams were imminent, and in the dormitory rooms, long after the lights-out bell had rung, students still huddled over their books, diligently reviewing. In some rooms, they even covered the doors with blankets to avoid being caught by the dorm supervisor. That night, Ilsŏn received a letter. It was from his father. Ilsŏn respectfully opened it. The tone (辭意) was very stern (峻嚴). As he read on, his heart grew apprehensive (惶恐). His face suddenly changed color, and his hands trembled. Finally, tears welled up in his eyes. The content of the letter was as follows: (Omission) "You are aware that marriage proposals for you have been arising from various places for several years. However, I blocked them because you were young and studying at the time. But now, your age has reached the marriageable age (婚齡), and since you will graduate this spring, there should be no hindrance. Marriage has been arranged with the daughter (女息) of Mr. Chang Chinsa (張進士) of OO town, and the matter is already settled. Be informed of this. That family is a renowned family for generations (累代名門) and also wealthy (資産家). The person concerned (當者) is said to be currently studying at PH Hakdang in Kyŏngsŏng." Ilsŏn crumpled the letter in his hand and sighed deeply. Ah, in this world, even the closest relationship, that between father and son, even the parent who gave birth to me <41> does not understand me! They say she is from a wealthy family. Ah, do I demand wealth? Perhaps my parents desired that wealth. And they said the person concerned is currently studying. But I do not value that little bit of knowledge. Isn't it enough if there can be understanding, no matter how little knowledge there is?, he thought. But they said the matter was already settled. Then, is everything lost (萬事休矣)? What should I do then? Must I inevitably obey the command (命令)?! Ah, no. Should I push aside all difficulties (萬難) and proceed according to my heart? His heart ached severely. From then on, he lost interest in everything. He became neglectful in his review studies. Having always achieved the top position (首席) in exams, due to this matter, he ended up slipping to third place in this graduation. Ah, a husband and wife should have mutual understanding. But considering language and perhaps personality, one must find some point of understanding to be truly complete, yet she and I have never even met, so where could love arise, let alone any understanding? It's utterly impossible, he thought.
That spring, when the peach blossoms were in full bloom, a grand wedding ceremony was held at the OO town church (禮拜堂). Ilsŏn, his spirit crushed by his father's strict order (嚴令), reluctantly went to the ceremony like an ox being led to the local government office (鄕廳). Not only the relatives of the groom and bride, but also many spectators, perhaps envious or perhaps showing happy faces because it was the wedding of students, were present. However, Ilsŏn showed no sign of happiness at all. The pastor asked various questions. - In the future, will you support each other whether strong or weak, help each other through life whether noble or lowly...? Ilsŏn could only answer affirmatively out of obligation. Finally, the pastor urged the two to shake hands (握手). Ilsŏn reluctantly extended his hand. It was at this moment (瞬間) that he saw the bride's veiled face. Ah, is my heart so weak?, he thought.

Three
Fifteen days after this event, Ilsŏn, embracing many aspirations and hopes, set foot on the long road of study abroad. He kicked away various confusing worries and thoughts and crossed the Yalu River Railway Bridge (鴨綠江鐵橋). Ilsŏn arrived far away in Peking (北京) and began studying at YK University. As he originally possessed <42> outstanding talent, his academic progress advanced daily, attracting much envy from his peers. It was autumn of that year. About two *chō* (町 - approx. 220 meters) away from YK University was M Women's University, where 2 or 3 of our female students were studying. Among them, a woman named S, studying in the medical department (醫科), was nineteen years old. She had previously graduated from a certain Girls' High School in Tokyo, Japan, and came here the following year. She had been very lonely since childhood. She had lived her life as a tearful and sorrowful figure. When she was eight, she lost her father early, and as an only daughter with no brothers, she was raised solely under her widowed mother's care (偏母侍下). Then, in the spring of the year she turned eleven, just three years after her father passed away, her beloved mother also departed on the road of no return (永遠). After that, she had no parents to rely on (怙恃) and no support (支接). Finally, she had no choice but to depend (依托) on her maternal grandmother's house in Chinjup town (晋州邑). Her maternal grandmother was also widowed early and had no son to support her. So, she treated this granddaughter (外孫女) like her own grandchild (親孫子), cherishing her dearly (愛之重之). Therefore, her grandmother had her graduate from the town's Ordinary School (普通學校). Afterwards, she crossed the Genkai Sea (玄海) and spent four or five years (星霜) residing in Edo (江戶 - Tokyo). Then, intending to devote herself to medicine (醫學), she came to China to prepare her German language (獨逸語) skills and handle travel procedures (旅行手續). Arriving in Peking, she enrolled in the German Language Department at M University. Recalling her past life (半生), she could not suppress a wave of sorrow (悲哀). However, thinking about how she, originally born to an ill-fated (薄命) destiny and fated to drift like duckweed (浮萍), had somehow received good fortune (幸運) to become who she was today, she smiled faintly. She originally possessed extraordinary (非凡) talent, and her reputation as a Korean talented woman (高麗才媛) shone brilliantly (焃焃) within the school. To her advancing studies (學業) and her beautiful face blooming daily like a flower, more than a few butterflies (蝴蝶 - suitors) gathered. However, she merely harbored ambitious (遠大) hopes and focused intensely on her studies, having never awakened to the opposite sex (異性) nor seriously considered (詮衡) anyone. Therefore, she had never associated with any man (男子). But, solely because her lodging (宿所) was close to Ilsŏn's, they met frequently. Ilsŏn and S also met on their way to school. They would meet, greet each other, walk the same path, and return the same way. However, until then, there had been no particular interaction (交通) between them. S simply saw Ilsŏn as a gentle man, and Ilsŏn saw S as an affectionate woman. They spent three months like this <43>. It was autumn. As the tree leaves began to fall, the Korean Students' Autumn Sports Meet (高麗學生秋期運動會) was held in the spacious C Park inside Peking's Zhengyangmen Gate (正陽門). While all the students jumped about, striving to win honor (榮譽), Ilsŏn's liveliness particularly stood out, adding a special color to the entire event. S also participated (參禮 - likely typo for 參列, participate) in various competitions (競技), and Ilsŏn's spirit (氣像) that day was subtly etched in S's memory (記憶). From then on, S's heart naturally fluttered, and it felt as if something was caught in her chest. Then S, clutching her chest, thought, "Why do I feel like there's something unusual about my mind and body these days!? Ah, it's truly strange. Huh, am I now becoming aware of something? I can't understand my own feelings," and she strove infinitely to discover the source of her yearning. Then she thought, "Ah! Perhaps spring is coming to my 19-year winter, receiving some positive energy (陽氣) and causing some bud to sprout," and she smiled to herself. Yet, when she met Ilsŏn on the way to school, her face would naturally seem to blush, and her heart felt shy. Naturally, Ilsŏn seemed closer and dearer to her. So, even when walking ahead, she would glance back unintentionally, and sometimes she wanted to say something. But when she actually tried to speak, she didn't even know what to say. Thus, Ilsŏn also noticed that S's expression (表情) towards him had changed somewhat recently. One day, S, on her way to school, saw Ilsŏn coming about 10 steps behind. She deliberately slowed her pace, waited for Ilsŏn to catch up, turned around, greeted him, and said, "The other day was truly enjoyable (愉快)." Ilsŏn replied abruptly, "You mean the sports meet? Not at all." Then, not to be outdone, Ilsŏn asked, "How are your studies these days?" S replied, "Yes, nothing special!" and said no more. After that, Ilsŏn's heart was also not calm (安穩), and turmoil (風浪) occasionally arose. As things progressed, Ilsŏn and S found more opportunities (機會) to meet frequently. Or rather, they tried to create opportunities. On Sundays (日曜日), they routinely went to church together. In this way, accidental meetings (偶然한 對面) led eventually to visits (訪問) made with excuses, sometimes feeling the cold autumn waters (冷冷한 秋水) of Longevity Hill (萬壽동산), sometimes lamenting the fluttering fallen leaves (飄颻하는 落葉) of the Summer Palace (頤和園). Thus, an inseparable fusion (融合) developed between them. When they met, they whispered about the future (將來). They sketched out dream-like designs (圖案) for their future. One day, S <44> told Ilsŏn, "I will go to Germany to study medicine. After graduating, I plan to return and establish a Women's Medical College." To this, Ilsŏn replied, "I plan to go and research engineering. Then, upon returning, I intend to establish an Engineering College," each stating their aspirations. Then Ilsŏn said to S, "Then let me propose a design. Let's make the layout (配置) of our ideal village (理想村) we plan to manage in a right-angled (直角) or perhaps acute-angled (銳角形) form. For instance, let's call the angle AOB. At the vertex O, let's build our residence (住宅). Then, along the line AO, let's build the Engineering College, and along the side OB, let's build the Women's Medical College. Thus, coming out from our residence O, you will enter the OB line, the Medical School, and I will enter the other side, the Engineering School. And let both lines extend infinitely (無限大), building factories along the Engineering School line and hospitals (病院) along this other line. Thus, hospital, factory, hospital... let's extend it indefinitely!" S thought this design was very well-conceived and agreed. And it seemed as if the design was already realized, faintly visible before her eyes, and she smiled gently.

Four
The following spring, Ilsŏn moved down to Shanghai and enrolled in the Engineering Department at L University. S, unable to acclimatize (水土不服), fell ill and needed treatment from a German doctor. She too came down to Shanghai and was admitted to the T University Affiliated Hospital (附屬病院). Ilsŏn took time off from his studies to nurse (看護) her day and night. Just as S recovered (快差) and was about to be discharged (退院), the German travel permit (獨逸旅行券) she had applied for six months earlier arrived. They were overjoyed. Now, it seemed nothing stood in their way – travel expenses (旅費) were arranged, and S's illness was cured. However, one remaining obstacle (拘碍) was the issue of Ilsŏn's wife (夫人), C. This needed to be resolved (解決). S considered this problem very difficult. But Ilsŏn believed it wouldn't be too difficult. One day, S asked Ilsŏn, "What should we do about this issue?" Ilsŏn replied, "Don't worry (念慮 마시오), that can be easily resolved." Ilsŏn's solution plan (解決策) was this: = "Regarding C's matter, I have no choice but to go and bring her here," he said. Hearing the words "bring her here," before understanding Ilsŏn's true intention (眞意), S felt displeasure (不平) and doubt (疑心) stirring in her heart. So she asked, "And then what do you intend to do?" <45> Ilsŏn replied, "Look here, when it's about both herself and others doing well, who would refuse?" S still didn't grasp the content of these words. Therefore, she asked, "By what measure (方策)?" "It's nothing special. Isn't it enough to let her study or do whatever she wants, help her develop into a useful person (人格), and enable her to live a happy (幸福) life? Besides, even if I separate from her now, it doesn't mean forgetting her forever (永遠)! I do not intend to forget her ever, applying some term (名詞) to our relationship," he said. S nodded assent (首肯) to these words. However, she couldn't help but have slight doubts about its possibility (可能性). And the phrase "will not forget forever" made her worry if some disadvantage (不利) might arise for her. But when he mentioned applying "some term," her heart eased slightly. Still, she wanted to ask right down to the final point (究竟處) what that "some term" might be. However, S ultimately trusted Ilsŏn's liveliness and capability (能力). So she deemed Ilsŏn's plan (策案) a sound scheme (圖謀).

At the small B Station in Ŭiju, the express train bound for Fengtian (奉天 - Mukden/Shenyang) stood panting, out of breath. The waiting room entrance and the platform (푸라트폼) were crowded (雜踏) with people in white clothes who had come to see off (餞送) Ilsŏn and his wife (內外). Ilsŏn's grandfather, over 80, leaning on his cane with a bent back, requested, "You two, please study harmoniously (誼조히) and come back quickly (쉬히)," while his mother, entrusting the health (健康) of her two children being sent to a distant foreign land (萬里異域) to God, bowed her head and offered earnest prayers (祈禱), finally saying, "You two, come visit during the summer vacation." Ilsŏn's eight-year-old younger sister, Irwŏn, carried on her mother's back, stretched out her hand and asked innocently (天眞), "Big brother, sister-in-law! Have a good trip?" Ilsŏn replied, "Okay," and took his little sister's hand, shaking it a couple of times. Hearing that this Ilsŏn couple was going abroad to study, the village elders scoffed, "Huh, taking a young wife to a distant foreign land (萬里他國)?" while Ilsŏn's young friends expressed envy, "Going abroad to study with his wife (同夫人)!" However, both these approvals and disapprovals (讚否) were misplaced regarding Ilsŏn. Therefore, Ilsŏn felt a pang of sorrow (悲哀), thinking, "Ah, they all don't know my inner situation." In reality, <46> nobody knew the inner situation. Even his mother, supposedly closest to her children, didn't know. As the train began to move, Ilsŏn and his wife bowed to bid farewell (告別) to the crowd. Until the train disappeared around the mountain bend, his mother stood staring blankly. When the train completely turned the corner and even his mother was out of sight, Ilsŏn thought about this unusual journey and tears welled up. Ah, the stubborn elders (頑固하신 父兄) in the village will oppose it, regardless of the good or bad of the situation (內容의 善惡 如何를 莫論하고). Instead (代身), it's plausible that young friends might envy it. But what can be done if they all don't know the content! Anyway, now that I am leaving, when will I ever step on this land again! Indeed, is it a path where I can come back for the summer vacation as Mother said!? Grandfather told me to return quickly. Isn't that understandable! But now that I am going, I will likely be gone for at least 10 years. Ah, they say mountains and rivers change in 10 years, how can I fathom the changes in human affairs during that time! This unreliable life. Ah, moreover, leaving my grandfather who is like the setting sun in the western mountains (西山落日), might this long journey be the last time I see him? Ah, in this unreliable world. Who knows that I am heading to the ends of the earth (天涯萬里), and would even this person traveling with me know? he thought, and with tear-filled eyes, he listlessly returned to his seat. His father accompanied them as far as Andong County (安東縣 - Dandong). Ilsŏn and his wife spent four days on the train and arrived at Shanghai North Station at 10:05 PM on February OO. At the station head, braving the pouring rain, Kwangil and S came out gladly to meet them. Getting off at the station, Ilsŏn introduced S to his wife, C.

Five
Ilsŏn enrolled C in the Western Painting Department (洋畵科) at the Shanghai Women's Art School (滬海女子美術學校). C indeed possessed a genius (天才) for art (美術). Since childhood, whenever she saw drawings, she would grab them and not let go. As a child, she often drew flowers and butterflies indiscriminately on walls (壁) and windows (窓) with a pencil (鉛筆), frequently earning reprimands (책망) from her father, asking if she wanted to become a lunatic painter. Her disposition (性質) was gentle (溫和) and her heart simple (單純), truly possessing artistic talent. Her future was also full of promise. However, her personality (性格) was too feminine (女性的). For a woman to possess femininity is indeed her proper characteristic and a virtue (美德). But this excessive femininity, conversely, became a flaw (欠點) for Ilsŏn. This was what gave Ilsŏn dissatisfaction and caused him <47> pain. Ilsŏn's requirement, conversely, was closer to the masculine (男性的). He sought talkativeness (多辯) and liveliness (活潑) over gentleness and quietness. This personality matched Ilsŏn's. But C was too taciturn (寡言) and reserved (沈潛). This was where her personality did not match Ilsŏn's. Therefore, understanding became absent. Ilsŏn knew this well. Thus, he believed he could never obtain understanding from C. Ilsŏn had clearly discovered (確實히 發見) who possessed a personality that matched his and, consequently, had common ground (共通點) with him. And so, he intended to sacrifice (屠) everything to carry through (貫徹) this discovered ideal (理想) of his. C also knew this – that Ilsŏn sometimes suffered anguish because of her, and consequently tried to distance himself from her, and even sensed the faint exclusion (排斥) arising. However, she trusted him, thinking, "Since he brought me this far, surely he wouldn't...?!" But Ilsŏn's firm determination (決心) could not be swayed. It had reached its absolute peak (絶頂). It felt as if someone was urgently calling him, as if something was pulling him with a rope. Someone stood at the doorway (門間), pressing (催促) him intensely to come out quickly. At this, Ilsŏn's heart grew very impatient (燥急).
It was the night of April OO. Ilsŏn, with firm preparation (準備) and all his courage (勇氣), sat facing C to begin the final discussion (最後의 談判). = "Look here, C! Three years have passed before we knew it (於焉間) since you and I met through our parents' contractual act (契約的 行爲)! During that time, because we were separated, we never had a chance to discuss detailed inner circumstances (仔細한 內情). But now, having spent some time living together in one room, we have likely been able to grasp each other's personalities. During this time, I have tested (證驗) things in various aspects (方面) – the result (結果), unfortunately (不幸), is that I have discovered (發見) there are somewhat clearly different points (判異한 點) in our natures. What did you think?" he asked. C's answer was simple. "I understand. Your thoughts." And then she retorted (反問), "So what then?" "Because it was arranged by our parents, it doesn't mean it absolutely disagrees with our opinions (意見)! Not at all! Even if it were arranged by a stranger on the street, if it suits us, that's all that matters, isn't it!? But the regrettable (遺憾) thing is that the cause (原因) is our incompatibility in personality! If friends (朋友) with mismatching intentions cannot live together (同居) for even 10 days, then for this union (合) of opposite sexes (異性) to last a lifetime (一生), isn't this truly an unbearable thing <48>?" he said. To this, C asked, "Then what do you intend to do!" "Therefore, you and I must inevitably...!" Before he could finish his sentence, C asked sharply, "Then why did you bring me here?" "Yes, there are two reasons (理由) for that. First, as I said before, it was to spend at least a month together to examine (審査) our personalities further and try to find some point of understanding. By now, you too should have grasped my temperament. Second, even if you and I part ways now, it doesn't mean forgetting forever! Nor is it about only myself doing well. When I try to do well, it was with the intention of helping you do well too that I brought you here!" he said. C, her eyes brimming with tears, looked up at Ilsŏn and asked, "By what method (方法)?" "Yes, firstly, from now on, I intend to change the term (名詞) used between you and me. I will call you a beloved friend and never intend to forget you. Secondly, I have resolved (決心) to help you too become a useful person (有用한 人格). Therefore, please pursue your cultivation (修養) to your heart's content. I intend to look after your future affairs. Getting you into the Art School now was also done under this reasoning," he said. C, wiping her tears with a handkerchief (手巾), said, "Ah, then thank you. For saying you will look after me for my sake. Anyway, if we don't match, what can be done? I am not in a position to force (强要) anything. In any case, as long as you say you will exert effort (用力) for my sake, I think it would be best not to let the world (世上) know about this until my character reaches a certain level (程度)." To this, Ilsŏn said, "That is indeed something I have had in mind for a while now. I intend not to announce (發布) it to the world for at least 10 years." C, conversely, smiled faintly and said, "Ah, an old-style (舊式) marriage has transformed into a new-style (新式) divorce (離婚). Anyway, I believe in your success (成功)." Ilsŏn, wiping sweat from his face, said, "Yes, this too is pioneering (開拓), isn't it? This seems to be a so-called ideal (理想的) divorce," and he firmly grasped C's hand in the same manner (格式) as they had at the wedding ceremony three years prior and shook it. Then he bowed his head and offered a final prayer (祈禱) to God. Outside the door, a newspaper vendor called out desolately (凄凉), "Shen Bao (申報), Latest News (最新報)!" The next day, Ilsŏn told Kwangil and another friend, Pak Kyuhwan, who managed Ilsŏn's living arrangements, the whole story (前後顚末) of what had happened the previous night. <49> The two friends' eyes widened, and they could only say, "What is this all about!?" Ilsŏn said, "Anyway, now all my pain is gone, and only happiness remains. Until yesterday, the teeming 2 million population walking the streets all looked cold (冷冷), and the twinkling stars in the blue sky (蒼穹) seemed merely frigid. But from this morning, all things (萬物) look alive and affectionate, seeming to dance joyfully in the warm embrace of the Creator (造物). Before me, only a line of light (光明) seems to follow," he said. Kwangil and Kyuhwan, seeing how their once-sharp friend had undergone such a radical change (激變), and worrying (念慮) whether some other abnormality (變態) might arise in his future, felt sorrow mixed with deep concern. And thinking of C's situation (身勢), they felt great pity (可憐) and shed tears of sympathy (同情) secretly (隱然).

On April 25th, the descending sunlight reflected (反射) on the Yangtze River water, creating silver and jade waves (銀波玉波) of blue (藍色) and yellow (黃色). The SS Frederick (프레데릭號), a ship of 5,700 tons floating buoyantly at the Shanghai wharf (碼頭), was about to set sail (發航) for Berlin (伯林). On its deck (甲板) stood a young man and woman, holding ribbons (리본) thrown from the shore – they were Kim Ilsŏn and the woman named S. On both sides of the shore, a crowd (群衆) of men and women who had come to bid farewell (送別) to the Europe-bound (洋行) new Ilsŏn couple threw ribbons or waved hats (帽子). Among them were Ilsŏn's friends and S's companions. Kwangil was there, of course, and C was also there. C threw ribbons to Ilsŏn and S, holding both strings in one hand, and said, "I pray for the success of you both." Ilsŏn and S also took off their hats and responded (答禮), "Thank you." As the ship began to weigh anchor (發錨) and depart, the ribbons held in people's hands either limply (脉業시) unraveled onto the water or snapped and fell onto the shore. Only after the ship had moved about three *majŏng* (마장 - approx. 300m) did Kyuhwan arrive, panting and thinking, "I'm late!" He rushed up, calling out, "Hey, Ilsŏn! Are you leaving already?" and took off his hat to wave. Then, as the ship rounded a bend in the water, everyone called out "Goodbye" (꿋바이) and turned to leave. However, C, Kwangil, and Kyuhwan remained standing, waving their hands, until the faces of the two people on the deck were no longer visible. Then, when the ship descended below the horizon (水平線) and disappeared completely, the three turned around. Tears welled up in C's eyes. Tears also flowed from the eyes of Kwangil and Kyuhwan. (End)

**Summary**

Set in Shanghai and Peking around 1920-1922, "Tears on the Shanghai Bund" tells the story of Kim Ilsŏn, a talented and ambitious Korean engineering student studying abroad. Despite having a privileged background and academic success, Ilsŏn feels profound anguish stemming from an arranged marriage to C, a gentle art student, as he finds himself deeply in love with S, a fellow student with shared ideals and aspirations for modernizing Korea. The narrative follows Ilsŏn's internal struggle, his deepening relationship with S, their plans for future contributions to Korea (building colleges and factories), and his eventual decision to seek an "ideal divorce" from C, proposing they remain lifelong friends while he supports her artistic pursuits. The story culminates with Ilsŏn and S departing for Germany, seen off by friends and a tearful but composed C, highlighting the complex emotional and social negotiations faced by modernizing Korean youth during the colonial era. From a historical perspective, the story offers insights into the lives of Korean intellectuals studying abroad in China, their nationalist aspirations intertwined with personal desires for self-determination and modern relationships (free love, divorce), contrasting sharply with traditional Confucian values (arranged marriage, filial piety). The mention of imprisonment likely related to the 1919 March 1st Movement underscores the political backdrop influencing these young lives.

小說 滬上의 눈물
						獨山
一
生存競爭이 물끌틋 하는 上海市街를 12哩이나 西으로 두고 楊子江, 黃海의 交叉點에 잇는 吳淞砲灣에는 獨逸人 經營의 大學이 잇다. 元來 科學 發達이 極度에 達한 獨逸 사람의 經營이라, 形而上學問 보다도 形而下學을 主要로 한다. 그 中에도 工科는 매우 이름이 놉다. 中國에 某某 專門學校와 大學이 잇다 하되 工科로는 內外國人의 經營을 勿論하고 이 學校를 따를 곳이 업다. 繁華 複雜한 紅塵萬丈인 東洋倫敦을 떠나 雅淡淸潔한 獨逸 사람의 市街를 녑헤 끼고, 海風이 슬슬 불어 오는대 노힌 이 學校는 學生의 修養 터로는 매우 適切한 地點이라 할 수 잇다. 高麗 學生으로는 7,8人이 이 學校에 잇는데 그 中에 金一善은 方今 機械工科에서 熱心으로 배우고 잇다. 그는 본래 天才를 가젓슬 뿐 아니라, 熱心잇기로 또한 校內에 이름이 놉다. 그는 實驗室에서 무엇이나 깨닷지 못하게 될 때에는 밥먹기를 니저 버린다. 그럼으로 그의 實驗은 한번도 失敗에 돌아간 적이 업섯다. 그는 하루에 네時 밧게 자지 아니한다. 그럼으로 그러케 多血質인 그도 이즘에는 얼굴이 매우 해쓱하여 간다. 그를 아는 친구들은 매우 걱정으로 생각하여 잇다금 學課를 좀 쉬이라고 勸하기까지 한다. 그러나 그는 自己가 이것을 天職으로 삼은 以上, 자긔는 이를 爲하야 犧牲하고 말 것까지 사양치 안노라 한다. 그는 적어도 畢業하고 나서는 자긔 손으로 13個 以上의 工場을 세우고 3個 以上의 工科大學을 세우리라 한다. 그는 다시 15年 內에 鐵道를 지금＜37＞ 敷設한 것보다 5倍 以上을 延長하야 우리 조선땅을 거미줄 걸어 노틋 하리라 한다. 그는 매우 囑望이 富하다. 獨逸人 先生들은 그를 die wunder gabe(奇才)라 일컷고 同僚 中 特히 獨逸 學生들은 그를 高麗少年工學者 라 부른다. 그는 집도 넉넉하다. 그럼으로 學費의 窘塞도 그는 일즉 맛보지 안핫다. 그는 집에 父母와 祖父母까지 계신다. 그럼으로 동무 중에는 「너는 참 幸運兒이다. 너가트면 무엇이」하는 칭찬과 羨望도 만히 밧는다. 아- 그러나 그는 웬일인지 늘 不滿과 孤獨을 느낀다. 그는 항상 煩悶과 苦痛으로 지낸다. 그는 늘 무엇을 알흔 것 갓고 무엇이 업는 것 가태한다. 그는 잇는 무엇을 버리고 십허하고, 또 다른 무엇을 붓잡으려 힘쓴다. 그가 學課에 執中한 것도 한 원인이지마는 그는 近來에 어떤 苦痛? 때문에 몸이 매우 瘦瘠하고 얼굴이 傷하고 甚至於 精神에 異狀까지 생긴 것 갓다. 그가 만일 苦痛이 잇다하면 오즉 나 한사람이 快樂을 맛본다 하면 적어도 어떤 한 社會 사람은 가티 맛보아야 할터이오, 내가 만일 苦痛이 잇다하면 이 全「누리」가 苦痛 속에 잇스리니 어찌하면 이 세상 사람 사람에게 苦痛이 업게하고 快樂을 누리게 할고, 하는 그것이엇다. 그러나 그는 단정 무엇을 느끼는 것갓다. 아모래도 그는 무슨 欠處가 잇서 보인다. 이 欠處를 塡充하고야 말려하는 것 갓다. 아모러나, 그는 精神에 단단한 打擊을 바든 것은 事實인 것 갓다. 그가 잇다금 冊을 보다가도 손을 마조 잡고 머-ㅇ허니 밧겻만 내여다 보기도 하고 或은 팔구비로 책상을 집고 업대여 무엇을 생각하는 것가튼 現狀은 前에 보지 못하든 일이다. 그는 저녁을 먹고 나서 樹林 만흔 公園 속에 들어갓다가 열두시가 지내어 돌아오는 때도 잇섯고, 새벽 일즉 일어나 長江 어구에 나아가 그닐은 적도 만하진다. 속모르는 사람들은 이것을 散步라 하겟지만 그는 決코 散步가 아니엇다. 그러난 그는 이것을 누구에게나 말하지 아니하고 어떤 모양으로나 족음도 나타내지 안흐려 한다. 오즉 그를 알고 그를 理解해 주는 사람은 T大學 寄宿舍 OO號에 가티 잇는 李光一 한 사람뿐이다. 一善과 光一은 오래 前부터 親交가 잇섯다. 7,8年 前 中學校時節부터 가튼 年級에서 工夫하고 가튼 寄宿舍에서 먹고 자고 하엿다. 그들은 얼마동안이라도 서로 떠나기를 설허하엿다. 或 放學 때가 되어 다른 學生들은 다 집에 돌아갓스되 오즉 一善과 光一은＜38＞ 서로 떨어지기를 실혀하야 依例히 몃츨式 더 떨어저 잇다 가기도 하엿다. 집에 돌아가서도 歸省하는 날은 몃날이 못되고 40里나 되는 머-ㄴ 길을 서로 왓다갓다 하며 놀앗다. 다만 몃츨이 막혀도 서로 편지로 通하엿다. 그러다가 開學 때가 되면 가티 한車를 타고 나가기로 하엿다. 그러다가 一善과 光一은 畢業을 1年이나 압헤 둔 봄 即 3昨年 風雲에 가튼 일로 가튼 鐵窓에 縷絏의 몸이 되어 1年이나 오랜 세월을 가튼 房에서 보내엇다. 그들은 鐵창 生活을 벗어나는 날에 뜻을 決斷하고 손목을 마조 잡고 黃海를 건느기로 하엿다. 지금에 一善과 光一은 가튼 學校 가튼 科에서 工夫를 하게 되엇고, 한 寄宿舍에서 가티 먹고 자게 되엇다. 이만하면 그들 두 새의 交分을 넉넉히 짐작할 것이다. 그럼으로 一善은 光一을 세상에는 둘도 업는 친구로 알앗다. 一善은 아모 일이나 光一에게 對해서는 숨김이 업섯다. 하로는 一善이가 光一을 보고, 여보게 李君! 나는 참 인제는 견딜 수가 업시 되엇구려, 알 수 업는 魅力이 나려 누르고 이길 수 업는 무엇이 손을 끌구려! 아모래도 내 압헤는 光明이란 한 點도 아니 보이고, 오즉 暗黑만 다다라 오는구려! 오늘 아츰도 長江 언덕에 나아가서 한 발만 더 내듸듸려 하다가 또 무엇이 내 등을 이끌어 그만 돌아오고 말앗구려! 아마 君은 모르 리다 마는 저번 저녁에도 永安公司 웃層에서 몸을 소스러하다가 알에 사람들이 널려 잇는 것을 미워하야 停止하엿구려! 하엿다. 光一은 그 동안 一善이가 精神上 打擊이 生긴 것을 보고 매우 근심하엿다. 그러다가 지금에 이전 말을 들으니 이러케까지 激變이 된 줄은 뜻하지 안핫다. 光一은 이 말을 듯고 한번 놀나지 안흘 수 업섯다. 光一도 집을 떠난 지 數年이나 되엇스되 무슨 形便으로 집에 消息을 자조 듯지 못하는데, 오즉 둘도 업는, 손목을 마조 잡고 나가는 벗까지 이러케 險惡한 狀態에 빠질 줄이야 어찌 뜻하엿스랴 하고 一種 形言할 수 업는 悲哀가 가슴 가운대 일어나 潛然이 눈물을 흘렷다. 그리고 여보게, 一善君! 이 問題일랑 解決하기가 그리 바뿐 일이 아닐세 그려! 우리가 目的한 것을 다 達하고 난 뒤에라도 그리 느즐 것이 아니겟구려! 우리는 오즉 現在에 忠實할 것이 아닌가? 하엿다. 그러나 一善은 이 말에는 別르 對答도 아니한다. 그의 마음에는 무슨 단단한 斷念이 잇는 것 갓다. 더구나 이즘에 생긴 苦痛은 누구의 말로마는 풀 수가 업고＜39＞ 그의 傷處는 다른 有形한 무엇으로는 到底히 治療할 수가 업시 되엇다. 그럼으로 平常에 그러케 밋부게 들리는 光一의 말도 그의 孤獨을 위로하기에는 넘우 弱하엿다. 光一은 후하고 한숨을 내어 쉬엇다. 吳淞砲臺에서는 쾅 하고 午砲 소리가 난다.

二
一善은 21歲의 英氣潑潑한 靑年이다. 지금은 얼굴에 여드림이가 生기고 알에 웃턱에 깜한 수염빨이 잡혓스되 15,6歲까지도 血色 조코 쌍긋쌍긋 웃는 얼굴을 가진 美少年이엇다. 그는 동무 중에 才操 잇기와 活潑하기로 有名하엿다. 처음보는 사람들은 그를 美男子라 불럿고, 동리 어른들은 그의 압길에 바람이 豊富하엿다. 그의 집은 義州 어떤 족으마한 市街에 잇지마는 世家로 圓滿한 地位도 차지하엿고, 相當한 財産도 거느려 別로 不足한 것이 업섯다. 그의 父親은 일즉 商路에 出脚하여 비록 시골 村냥반이나 平壤 京城 等地로 자조 往來하엿고, 中國地方도 여러 곳 踏破하엿다. 그는 商路에 精通할 뿐 아니라 世事에 깨다름이 만핫고, 新風潮에 또한 씻기움이 만핫다. 그럼으로 金大成씨라 하면 隣邑 사람으로는 別로 모를 사람이 업시 되엇다. 그는 먼저 子女敎育의 急先務임을 깨다랏다. 그럼으로 自己 子女 6男妹를 東京 中國 京城 기타 시굴로 留學을 보내엿다. 家産은 前보다 좀 零落하엿스되 그는 족음이라도 그 熱心을 쉬지 아니한다. 그의 속을 아지 못하는 近處 令監들은 만일 지금 이러케 子女 工夫시키는데 家産을 다 업새이면 후일에는 어찌 하는고, 하고 좀 挽留하는 일까지 잇섯다. 그러나 그는 오즉 修養만 잘 시켜 노흐면 各各 自營 生活은 걱정 업스리라는 信念을 固執한다. 그런대 一善은 4兄弟 中 맛아들이다. 어려서부터 父母의 사랑을 극진히 바닷다. 그는 얼굴도 똑똑함으로 小學校 단일때부터 어느 先生에게나 特別한 사랑을 아니 바다본 적이 업섯다. 그가 S中學校에 나갓슬 때에는 上級學生 中 P라는 사람은 一善의 때문에 필경 墮落까지 되고 말앗다. 이리하여 그가 16,7歲 되엇슬 때에는 四方에서 婚議가 만히 일어 낫 섯다. 그 중에는 相當한 곳도 업지 안핫다. 그때까지 一善은 아즉 徹底한 自覺은 업섯지만 그 동안 或 間間이 戀愛小說 가튼 것도 읽어 보앗고, 또는 父母의 不合理한 結婚으로 말미암아 現代 靑年이 만흔 苦痛을＜40＞ 느끼는 것과 그로 因하야 말못할 喜悲劇이 자로 이러나는 것까지 그는 觀察하엿다. 그럼으로 그는 自己 眼目이 어느 精度에 이르기까지는 婚姻 문뎨에 눈을 걸지 안흐려 하엿다. 그는 적어도 相對者와의 充分한 理解를 어든 후 自由意思로 무엇을 해보려 하엿다. 그뿐 아니라, 그의 압길에 바람이 만흠을 아는 그의 師友들까지도 決코 무슨 일이나 輕輕히 말라고 勸하엿다. 그의 父親도 일즉이 깨다름이 잇서 30餘處나 되는 그러케 만흔 婚處에도 나이 어린 것을 벌서 그럴 必要 업다고, 모조리 拒絶하고 말앗다. 그러나 一善의 실혀함과 그의 父親의 拒絶함과는 그 見地가 어느 點으로 보아 좀 差異가 잇섯다. 그의 父親은 다못 아즉 工夫하는 때이니 그 中學이나 마추고야 보리라 함이엇다. 一善도 勿論 지금 잇는 學校를 마추기 前에는 무엇이나 하지 안코저 한다. 이點에 對해서는 그의 父親과 一致한다. 그러나 一善은 學校로 말하면 中學校만 畢業할 作定이 아니다. 적어도 어느 專門大學까지는 하고야 말 作定이다. 그는 婚姻에 對하여는 한 거름 더 나아가 學校 畢業 後라도 自己 意思에 맛겨 무엇을 해보려 함이엇다.
一善이가 열여덜살 되는 봄이다. 방금 畢業試驗이 迫頭하야 寄宿舍 各房에서는 消燈鍾이 친지 이미 오랫스되 오히려 불을 끄지 아니하고 머리를 붓동이고 熱心으로 복습들을 한다. 어떤 방에서는 舍監에게 들킬가 하여 이불로 門을 가리우기도 하엿다. 이밤에 一善은 1封 글월을 바닷다. 그는 父親의 下書엿다. 一善은 삼가 떼엿다. 그 辭意는 매우 峻嚴하엿다. 나려 볼스록 마음이 惶恐하여진다. 그의 얼굴은 감작히 變해지고, 그의 손은 떨리엇다. 마츰내 그의 눈에는 눈물이 핑 돌앗다. 그 親書의 內容은 이러하다. 三

四

五
一善은 C를 滬海女子美術學校 洋畵科에 入學을 시켯다. C는 과연 美術에 天才를 가젓다. 어려서부터 그림을 보면 붓잡고 노치 아니하엿다. 그는 어려서 鉛筆을 가지고, 壁과 窓에 함부루 꼿입새와 나뷔를 그리다가 부친에게 너는 환장이가 되겟느냐 하고 책망을 바든 적이 만핫다. 그는 性質이 溫和하고 마음이 單純하야 참으로 美術的 才質을 具備하엿다. 그도 압길에 囑望이 만핫다. 그러나 그의 性格은 넘우 女性的이다. 女子가 女性을 갓춤이야 말로 제格이오 美德이다. 그러나 이 넘우 女性的임이 돌이어 一善에게는 欠點이 된다. 이것이 一善에게 不滿을 주는 것이오 一善으로 하야곰＜47＞ 苦痛을 느끼게 하는 점이다. 이 一善의 要求는 돌이어 男性的에 가까운 것이다. 溫和하고 잠잠한 것보다도 多辯하고 活潑한 것을 求한다. 이 性格이 一善과 맛는 바이다. 그런데 C는 넘우 寡言하고 沈潛하다. 이것이 一善과 性格이 맛지 안는 것이다. 그럼으로 딸하 理解가 업시 되엇다. 一善은 이것을 잘 알앗다. 그래서 O에게서는 아모래도 理解를 엇지 못할 줄을 밋는다. 一善은 性格이 自己와 맛고 딸하 自己와의 共通點을 가진 사람은 누구인 것을 確實히 發見하엿다. 그리하야 온갓 모든 것을 屠하야 가지고라도 이 發見한 自己의 理想을 貫徹하고야 말려한다. O도 이런 줄을 알앗다.- 一善이가 自己 때문에 잇다금 煩悶을 當하는 것과 또는 그로 말미어 自己를 멀리 하려는 것과 憙微한 排斥이 생기는 것까지 짐작하엿다. 그러나 나를 여긔까지 데리고 나온 바에야 설마!? 하고 미덧다. 그러나 一善의 단단한 決心은 풀릴 수가 업섯다. 아조 絶頂에 達하고 말앗다. 누가 急히 오라고 부르는 것갓고 무엇이 줄을 매여 당기는 것 가탓다. 누가, 門間에 서서 빨리 나오라고 재축이 甚하다. 이에 一善의 마음은 매우 燥急하여젓다.
4月 OO 夜이다. 一善은 단단한 準備와 온갓 勇氣를 내어 가지고. C와 마조 안저 最後의 談判을 始作하게 되엇다. = 「여보 C氏! 당신과 나와가 父母의 契約的 行爲로 맛난지가 於焉間 3年이 지냇구려! 그러니 그 동안은 서로 떠나잇기 때문에 자셰한 內情도 이약이 해본 적이 업섯소이다. 그러나 인제는 얼마동안을 두고 한방에 군일엇스니 대개는 彼此의 性格을 짐작할 수가 잇게 되엇습니다. 그동안 나는 여러 方面으로 證驗하여 보앗소이다- 그 結果는 不幸히 당신과 나와는 性質上 좀 判異한 點이 잇슴을 發見하엿소이다. 당신은 어떠타고 생각햇습니까」 하엿다.
