잡지명	개벽 제26호
발행일	1922년 08월 01일
기사제목	먼 後日 (Distant Future Day)
필자	素月
기사형태	시 (Poem)

먼 後日
						素月

먼 훗날에 당신이 차즈시면
그때에 내말이 「니젓노라」

맘으로 당신이 나무려하시면
그때에 내말이 「무척 그리다가 니젓노라」
당신이 그래도 나무러하시면
그때에 이말이 「밋기지안하서 니젓노라」

오늘도 어제도 못닛는 당신을
먼 훗날 그때에는 니젓노라.＜24＞

***

**English Translation**

Distant Future Day
						Sowŏl

If you should seek me in the distant future,
Then my words will be, "I have forgotten."

If you should reproach me in your heart,
Then my words will be, "I missed you dearly, then forgot."
If you still reproach me even then,
Then these words, "Because I couldn't believe it, I forgot."

You, whom I cannot forget today or yesterday,
In the distant future, then, I will have forgotten. <24>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl expresses a speaker's painful resolution to forget a beloved person ("you") in the distant future, despite being unable to forget them in the present. The speaker anticipates being sought out and even reproached by this person, offering defiant yet sorrowful justifications for having forgotten – explanations tinged with the memory of deep longing and disbelief. The poem captures a complex mix of enduring attachment, anticipated resentment, and the forced passage of time. For historians studying Korean colonial history, this poem, like much of Sowŏl's work, can be seen as reflecting the deep sense of sorrow (han) and loss prevalent among Koreans during that era. While ostensibly a personal lyric, the theme of forced forgetting and lingering pain could resonate with the collective experience of cultural suppression and the struggle to maintain identity under colonial rule.
