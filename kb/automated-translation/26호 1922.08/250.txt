발행일	1922년 08월 01일
기사제목	그 山우에 (On That Mountain)
필자	素月
기사형태	시 (Poem)

그 山우에
						素月

그 山 우에 올라서서 바라보면은
바다를 건너, 가로막힌 바다를 건너,
님 게시는 마을이 눈압흐로
꿈 한울가티도 떠오릅니다.

흰모래가 빗긴 船倉가에는
다만 뱃노래, 한가롭은 벳노래,
날은 점을고 안개는 덥히는데,
물꼿이 흐터저 아득입니다.

프른물결에 물새가 울고
하나둘 돗단배는 떠나서
한바다로 저 멀리 한바다로
가랑닙가티 흘러갑니다.

그山우에서 밤을 새우고
아츰 해 붉은 볏에 몸을 씻으며
귀기울이고 안젓느라면
님의窓알에 물결소리 납니다.

꿈을 깨우는 물결노래에
님이 놀래어 차즈실 때엔
그 山 우에서 그 山 우에서
어느덧 내잠이 깃헛습니다.＜25＞

On That Mountain
						Sowŏl

When I climb that mountain and gaze out,
Across the sea, across the hindering sea,
The village where my beloved dwells appears before my eyes,
Floating like a dream sky.

By the shore (船倉가) slanted with white sand,
Only boat songs, leisurely boat songs,
The day darkens and fog descends,
Water flowers scatter into the distance.

Seagulls cry on the blue waves,
One by one, sailboats depart,
Out to the open sea, far out to the open sea,
Flowing away like fallen leaves.

Staying awake all night on that mountain,
Washing my body in the red morning sunlight,
If I sit and listen intently,
I hear the sound of waves below my beloved's window.

When the beloved, surprised, awakens
To the wave-song that rouses dreams,
On that mountain, on that mountain,
My sleep has already settled in. <25>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sowŏl expresses deep longing and separation. The speaker stands on a mountain, gazing across the sea towards the place where their beloved ("님") resides, which feels distant and dreamlike. The poem uses imagery of the sea, boats, fog, and sounds (boat songs, waves, seagulls) to create a melancholic atmosphere of yearning. The speaker spends the night on the mountain, imagining hearing the waves near the beloved's window, and finally falls asleep there, suggesting a spiritual or dreamlike connection despite physical distance. For historians, the poem reflects the pervasive themes of separation, loss, and longing prevalent in Korean literature during the colonial period. While not overtly political, the figure of the "님" (beloved) could be interpreted not just as a person but also as a lost homeland or ideal, a common motif in Sowŏl's work, making it resonate with the national sentiment of the era.
