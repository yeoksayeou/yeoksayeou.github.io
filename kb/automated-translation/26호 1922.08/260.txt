Publication Date: 1922년 08월 01일
Article Title: 바다 (The Sea)
Author: 素月
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The Sea
						Sowŏl

Far, far away, where the white waves play,
The sea where red grasses softly grow is far away.

Where the fishermen sit on the boat's edge
And sing love songs, the sea is far away.

In the deep blue sky, softly dyed,
Where bright clouds come and go, the sea is far away.

Where large water birds, wandering without a fixed place (定處업시),
Return in flocks, the sea is far away.

Cross over, and the far side (便) is another land;
The longed-for, yearned-for sea is far away.<25>

**Summary**

This poem by Sowŏl expresses a deep sense of longing and distance towards the sea. Each stanza describes an idyllic or evocative image of the sea – its waves, vegetation, fishermen's songs, sky, clouds, and birds – emphasizing that this desired place is "far away." The final stanza links the sea to "another land," reinforcing the theme of separation and yearning for a place beyond reach. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, resonates with themes of longing for freedom, escape, or a connection to a national space (the sea surrounding the peninsula) that feels distant or inaccessible under occupation. The persistent refrain "멉니다" (is far away) could reflect the psychological distance and yearning experienced by Koreans during this era.

바다
						素月
멀리 저멀리 흰물결의 넘노는
붉은 풀이 고히 자라난 바다는 멉니다.

고기잡이 사람들이 배우에 안저서
사랑노래를 부는 바다는 멉니다.

새파라케 고히 물든 남빗 한울에
훤구름이 오고가는 바다는 멉니다.

定處업시 떠단이는 크다란 물새가
떼를 지어 돌아가는 바다는 멉니다.

건너서면 먼 저便은 딴나라이요.
가고십흔 그립은 바다는 멉니다.＜25＞
