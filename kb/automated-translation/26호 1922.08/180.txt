발행일: 1922년 08월 01일
기사제목: 囚人의 生活 (Life of a Prisoner)
필자: 金石松
기사형태: 시 (Poem)

囚人의 生活
						金石松
一

When I passed by a certain Prison (監獄),
Outside the brick wall, much higher than five fathoms,
Wearing reddish-clay (赤土) colored trousers and jackets, barefoot,
Several tens of Convict Laborers (懲役軍) stood in lines,
Two by two, linked by the chains (사실) they rattled,
I saw them digging the earth with listless (힘업는) spades.

Their faces, pickled by the scorching sun (볏에끌어),
Their faces, lacking oil due to malnutrition,
Their faces, envious as they looked at me passing on the street,
(To their eyes, I was the God (神) of Freedom (自由))
Their faces, revealing the laziness (게으름) of hating work,
Their faces, busier looking at the distant mountains than digging.

Ah! What calculation (세음) are they engaged in now? <8>
To prevent the escape (다라남) of the Convict Laborers,
They dig yet another ditch outside the brick wall,
Who indeed are they?
"Hey you! What are you looking at!" bellows the order,
The shouting voice of the Prison Guard (獄卒) is heard.

二

Oh! Prisoner (囚人)! People who have lost Freedom!
Your laziness is a natural (當然) thing.
Your envy watching me stride freely (濶步) on the street,
Your waiting only for the sun to set on the distant mountains,
For you in prison (囹圄)—you who have lost freedom—
It is something you must do, and something you cannot help but do.

Life (人生)! Digging its own grave in order to live!
The Masses (大衆)! Volunteering for work they hate!
Have you seen your own faces?
People of all Occupations (職業), people of all Job Types (職種),
All things tangible and intangible (有像無像) that have set foot (발부틴) upon the earth (따우에),
Oh! What is your Wish (所願), your Ultimate (究極) Purpose (目的)?

Oh! I am a Prisoner, having lost Freedom, <9>
I hate (실타) everything, I hate everything.
Quickly, quickly, may the sun set on the West Mountain,
So that even in a Prison Cell (監房), Rest (安息) may come to me,
Absolutely (絶對) independent (獨立) Rest, I wish for it,
And that Tomorrow's (來日) morning (아츰) may never come again, that the sun's fire may be extinguished Forever (永永). <10>

**Summary**

The poem "Life of a Prisoner" by Kim Sŏksong depicts the grim reality of convicts engaged in forced labor outside a prison wall. The speaker observes their listless movements, sunburnt faces, and longing gazes, interpreting their laziness and envy as natural reactions to their loss of freedom. The poem then broadens its scope, equating the prisoners' plight to the universal human condition, suggesting that all people are trapped in lives of unwanted toil, ultimately questioning the purpose of existence. The speaker concludes by identifying with the prisoners, expressing a profound weariness and a desire for oblivion. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem is significant for its portrayal of forced labor and the palpable sense of confinement and loss of freedom, likely reflecting the oppressive conditions under Japanese rule in 1922. The metaphorical extension of imprisonment to the general populace could be interpreted as a subtle critique of the lack of agency and the constrained lives of Koreans during that era.

囚人의 生活
						金石松
一

내가 어느 監獄압을 지날때에,
다섯길은 훨신넘는 벽돌담밧게서,
赤土빗 바지저고리에 맨발벗은,
懲役軍數十名이 늘어서서,
두사람식 역거노흔 사실을 덜그럭대며,
힘업는 가래로 땅을 팜을 보앗다.

볏에끌어 장아찌가 된 그들의 얼굴,
營養不足으로 기름기업는 그들의 얼굴,
거리로 지나가는 나를보고 불어워하는 그들의 얼굴,
(그들이 보기에 나는 自由의 神이엇다)
일하기 실혀 게으름이 돌어나는 그들의 얼굴,
가래질 보다 먼山보기가 바뿐 그들의 얼굴.

아! 저들은 이제무엇을 하는 세음인가.＜8＞
懲役軍의 다라남을 막기爲하야,
벽돌남밧게 또다시 웅덩이를 파는,
저들은 果然 누구인가.
「이놈아! 무얼보아!」하며 불號令하는,
獄卒의 메어부티는 소리가들린다.

二

오!囚人아! 自由를 일흔 사람들아!
너의의 게으름은 當然한 일이다.
거리에 濶步하는 나를 보고 부러워함도,
먼山에 해가지기만 기다림도,
囹圄의너-自由일흔 너로는,
그리하여야 할일이오, 안해도 안될일이다.

살기爲하야 무덤을 파는人生!,
하기실흔일을 自願하는 大衆!,
너의는 너의의 얼굴을 보앗느냐.
모든職業의 사람, 모든職種의 사람,
따우에 발부틴 모든 有像無像,
오! 너의는 무엇이 所願이냐, 究極의目的은.

오!나는囚人이다, 自由를 일허버린,＜9＞
나는 아모것도실타, 아모것도실타.
어서어서 빨리 西山에 해가저서,
비록 監房이라도, 나에게 安息이,
絶對로 獨立한 安息이 잇기를 나는 바란다,
그리고 來日아츰이 다시업기를, 해의 불이 永永꺼지기를.＜10＞
＜8-10＞
