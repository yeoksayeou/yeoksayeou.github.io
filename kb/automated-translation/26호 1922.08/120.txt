Satirical Cartoon

Night Market (夜市)? Or Women's Market (女市)?

Changes in thought (思想變化) hammer from below, making the old ways (舊食) be vomited out.
What is vomited out puts on an overturned donation box (寄附한 箱子) and acts important (行勢).

Direct action (直接行動)! Truly a powerful word.
However, if there's a separate fellow holding a spring in the shadow of the forearm, wouldn't it rather be shameful (昌皮)?

The vibrant youth (潑剌한 少年)! How could he be one who only stays secluded (蟄居) under the feet of adults?
Look! How well the flag (旗ㅅ발) they hold flutters!

<90-93>

**Summary**

This series of satirical cartoon captions from the August 1922 issue of *Kaebyŏk* comments on societal changes and activism in Korea. The first critiques the forceful, perhaps superficial, nature of ideological shifts, where new ideas displace old ones, but those embracing the new might be hypocritical. The second expresses skepticism about "direct action," suggesting it could be manipulated by hidden actors, thus rendering it shameful. The third caption, however, offers a hopeful view of the youth, portraying them as vibrant and breaking free from constraints, symbolized by their proudly fluttering flag. For historians, these captions reveal contemporary anxieties about the pace and nature of modernization, skepticism towards certain forms of activism possibly influenced by colonial conditions, and the rising prominence and hope invested in youth movements during the 1920s cultural nationalist period.
