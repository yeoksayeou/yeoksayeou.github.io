발행일	1924년 09월 01일
기사제목	開城漫筆 (Random Jottings from Kaesŏng)
필자	松都閑人
기사형태	소식 (News/Dispatch)

開城漫筆
						松都閑人

△ The Koryŏ Youth Association (高麗靑年會), which had withered and sunk, has found new strength, and a stage for great activity has unfolded. Thus, they first collected 4,000 won in donations from members to construct an association hall, and general supporters even organized an Association Hall Construction Preparatory Committee (會館建築期成會) and are active. We rejoice that this time, a youth association hall will surely come into being. However, looking at the budget for the hall construction, it is said that the total estimated cost for building a two-story stone structure on a 50-pyeong plot of land is 14,000 to 15,000 won. Isn't this an excessively small hall? If the Youth Association Hall is this cramped, even to hold just one lecture meeting, won't they have to go crawling to the *gedajjak* (a derogatory term, possibly referring to Japanese clogs/footwear, implying bowing to Japanese interests or establishments) and borrow a pigsty-like theater at a high price, or else go to that big-nosed fellow (코주부 - possibly referring to Western missionaries or foreigners) and borrow a church while complying with all sorts of conditions? Isn't this upsetting? In this situation, don't we keenly feel the need for some kind of assembly place made by our own hands?

Therefore, the youth association hall to be newly built must be a structure large enough to accommodate these great expectations. Thus, our public gatherings should be able to convene here without regret. Esteemed executives of the Youth Association Hall Construction Preparatory Committee! Since this is a task undertaken by a gathering of Kaesŏng's top businessmen like yourselves, how about thinking a bit further ahead, examining deeply, and truly setting aside a corner of your abundant coffers?

△ It has been a long time since it was said that a library would be established on the upper floor of Namdaemun Gate using the assets of the Kaesŏng Hyanggyo (開城鄕校財産 - Kaesŏng Local Confucian Academy Assets). However, it seems there has recently been some trouble behind the scenes. From our perspective, we point out that establishing a library on the upper floor of Namdaemun Gate, the most crowded part of the city, is inappropriate from the start, and express our opposition to it. Furthermore, it goes without saying that a library should originally be equipped with books capable of infusing the general public with new thought and new culture. If, as rumored, it is stocked with government-sponsored books (御用圖書) introducing government-sponsored learning (御用學問)—meaning newspapers like the *Maeil Sinbo* or magazines carrying articles on Japanese-Korean harmony (日鮮融和論), and general books limited to political works praising contemporary Japanese politics founded on despotism and oppression, or economic books praising capitalist economic organization founded on capitalism, all according to the government's liking—then we oppose the very intention. It must stand on the side of the public, view things justly, always keep the majority in mind, and fulfill the role of a true institution that pours into them something beneficial, something that will become their lifeblood. Truly, it is doubtful whether such sincerity, ambition, and ceaseless effort exist.

△ It is widely known that the third and fourth-year students of Songdo High School (松高校) collectively went on strike (盟休 - *maenghyu*, alliance strike), and because unrest also arose among the first, second, and fifth-year students, the school temporarily closed until August 31st. And it is already known that 38 students from the third and fourth years were sacrificed (i.e., expelled or disciplined). The reason given for sacrificing them is reportedly that they instigated the collective strike incident by inciting many students, thus being undesirable elements and troublemakers (不穩輩 - *pulonbae*), and that if such students were left alone, the teachers' authority would disappear, consequently endangering the teachers' positions. Indeed, it seems there are quite a few teachers at this school who deserve the students' distrust!

The school authorities think the recent collective strike occurred due to the incitement of a few undesirable students, but the students resorted to a collective strike, sacrificing precious time, because their demands were the same and their feelings were the same! It is absolutely impossible that the majority of students would have foolishly joined in due to the incitement of just a few.
Even if there were instigators and those instigated, they must have reached the point of collectively striking (53) together because they sympathized with the demands. If the strike had arisen from incitement, conflicts would easily have arisen, leading to fractures, yet why have they marched forward firmly in the same stride, like the beginning and end are one, without a single instance of internal discord from the day the strike began until now? Judging from this alone, it is clear that it was not the incitement of a few but was spontaneous and based on free agreement. Despite this, for the school authorities to single out a few and place the entire responsibility for the strike on them is absolutely unjust. And isn't citing the future position and authority of the teachers a self-contradiction that exposes their own weakness without reservation? Asking for good teachers to be brought in and for facilities to be fully equipped! How is this undesirable, and how is this a subversive act? If they are expelled, labeled as undesirable elements and troublemakers for this, won't all those who are somewhat discerning and truly want to learn end up being driven out? Then, under the tyranny of unqualified teachers, those who simply submit with "Yes! Yes!" without any objection will receive top marks in conduct and achieve high grades. Ah! Looking at it this way, this is not a school but an idiot training center (白痴養成所) and a slave training center (奴隷養成所). It is not true education, and it certainly isn't a real school. Wherein lies the true meaning of education?

Since the topic came up, Principal Yun's (尹校長) slip of the tongue has surely offended the students and parents. The remark was too shameful to publish, but it is something an educator could not bear to utter. How could such words come from someone displaying the signboard of a sacred educator? It goes beyond a slip of the tongue; it is reckless talk (妄言 - *mang'ŏn*) and a reckless scheme (妄計 - *mankye*). Who wouldn't be shocked upon hearing this reckless talk? No matter how I think about it, can this be the same Mr. Yun who once tried to set Chosŏn straight? A person's heart changes easily, but can it change that much? Principal Yun! Close your eyes and reflect quietly! Think about the future of the 600-700 youths entrusted to you! And carefully reconsider the motives and causes of this collective strike! A stream of tears should flow from this. A precious stream of tears should flow. Without saying more, let's keep an eye on the next issue!

△ Under the auspices of a bizarre organization called Chin Sŏn Sa (進善社 - Society for Promoting Goodness), whose facade is fine but whose insides are empty, they are making a fuss about renovating the Kwanwangmyo (關王廟 - Shrine to Guan Yu), collecting donations on one hand, visiting every household, and demanding obligatory labor service (義務赴役). Consequently, foolish people followed and responded, donating money and turning out for obligatory labor service in large numbers. Thus, the second round of labor service by 300-400 people from Kokyŏng-jŏng (高慶町) and the obligatory labor service by several hundred ignorant folks from Chi-jŏng (池町), Kyŏng-jŏng (京町), and Namsan-jŏng (南山町) were truly a great spectacle. What sin do the ignorant masses have? We merely point out that the Chin Sŏn Sa fellows who exploit this ignorance to make them perform labor service are detestable and wicked. Let's denounce the Chin Sŏn Sa members who deceive the general public! Let's bury the group that not only ignores the citizens but tries to manipulate them all! Let's get rid of the phantoms who cleverly sell Guan Yu to promote superstition among foolish men and women! Now! Stand up! Youth with blood and spirit! Youth! Koryŏ Youth Association! You pride yourself on being the organ of public opinion in Kaesŏng. Then what are you doing? Having pledged yourselves as an organ of public opinion, arouse the public opinion of the general citizenry! Koryŏ Youth Association! Didn't you prohibit the grand shaman ritual (大굿) at Manwŏldae (滿月臺) years ago? Why are you watching idly now?

When launching flood relief committees or other public projects and soliciting donations, the esteemed gentlemen (有志兩班 - *yuji yangban*) are reluctant to contribute even 2 or 3 won, yet for this meaningless renovation of the Kwanwangmyo, they readily fork over 10, 20, 30-40, 50-60 won without hesitation. What kind of sign is this supposed to be? You ruined and ever-ruining influential gentlemen! You fame-seekers (釣名輩)! Are you influential gentlemen (有志紳士 - *yuji sinsa*)? Or maintainers of swift death (維持迅死 - *yuji sinsa*, a pun)?
The Kwanwangmyo is state property (國有). What need is there for the general public to perform unpaid labor service and donate money to renovate a state-owned building?
These people are truly indispensable meritorious subjects for the Japanese government. Won't they even bestow a wooden cup (木盃)? Wake up! Members of Chin Sŏn Sa! Awaken! General populace!

△ Among those who lost all their property in the great flood of July 20th, many had nothing to wear, nothing to eat, and no place to shelter their bodies. They wandered the streets, not knowing where to go, clutching their empty stomachs and weeping. Yet, the household of Kaesŏng's top millionaire, Chin Poch'ŏn (秦抱川), did not distribute the flood-damaged rice (沈水米) to those disaster victims but sold it for money. There was much cursing, and it is said some gentlemen reproached him, asking if this befitted the dignity of a rich man. Isn't he a Chin person (秦人 - perhaps a pun on his name or implying stinginess associated with Qin dynasty?) with a nasty heart? No matter how sweet the taste of money is, isn't this going too far? In these times when the world is turning upside down, how long do you think your household alone can enjoy fine clothes and good food? The death knell for the bourgeoisie (뿔조아 - likely a transliteration/adaptation of 'bourgeoisie') has tolled. Do you know or not? (54)
<54>

**Summary**

This article, penned by "Songdo Hanin" (a Kaesŏng local), offers sharp critiques of various happenings in Kaesŏng in 1924. The author laments the small scale planned for the new Koryŏ Youth Association hall, criticizes the proposal for a potentially government-controlled library at Namdaemun, defends student strikers at Songdo High School against unreasonable school authorities (including Principal Yun), condemns the Chin Sŏn Sa organization for exploiting public ignorance to renovate the state-owned Kwanwangmyo shrine while elites readily donate, and castigates a local wealthy man (Chin Poch'ŏn) for profiting from selling flood-damaged rice to suffering victims.

For historians, the article provides a vivid snapshot of social tensions and nationalist sentiment in colonial Kaesŏng. It reveals grassroots activism (youth association, student strikes), critiques of perceived collaboration or apathy among elites (businessmen, "influential gentlemen"), skepticism towards official institutions (library plans, school authorities), anger at social injustice (exploitation of flood victims), and the underlying friction between Korean aspirations and colonial/traditional structures (Japanese influence, Confucian academy assets, Guan Yu shrine). The explicit mention of "Japanese-Korean harmony" discourse, "government-sponsored learning," and the warning about the "bourgeoisie's death knell" are particularly indicative of the period's ideological currents.

그러면 신축할 청년회관은 적어도 이 큰 期待를 容納할 만한 큼지막한 건축물이라아 되겟다. 그리하야 우리네의 公共한 集會는 이곳에서 유감업시 會合되도록 하여야 할 것이다.  靑年會館 建築期成會 幹部 諸君이여! 諸君가튼 開城一流 實業家들이 모혀서 하는 일이니 좀더 멀니 생각하고 깁히 살피여 참으로 諸君들의 푸근한 금고의 한 모퉁이를 덜어 놋는 것이 엇찌할가?

△ 開城鄕校財産을 가지고 南大門樓上에 圖書館을 設置한다고 한지가 벌서 오래다. 그런데 둿등에져께 말성이 잇는 모양이다. 우리의 보는 바로는 市街의 가장 複雜한 南大門樓上에 圖書館을 設置한다 함은 場所부터 適宜치 안은 것을 指摘하야 이에 反意를 가젓슴을 말한다. 또 圖書館은 元來 一般의게 新思潮와 新文化를 부어줄만한 圖書를 具備하게 設置하여야 할 것은 多言이 不要할 것이다. 萬若 所聞으로는 每日申報나 雜誌로는 日鮮融和論을 실은 것과 一般圖書라도 專制와 壓迫을 터삼은 現代日本政治를 讚揚한 政治書가 안이면 資本主義를 터삼은 資本主義的 經濟組織을 讚하는 經濟書 等! 이와 맛찬가지로 官의 조흔대로 卽 御用學問을 紹介하는 御用圖書를 備置한다 하면 그 立意부터 反對한다. 어데까지든지 公에 立하고 正에 觀하며 大多數를 念頭에 늘 두어 가지고, 저들에게 有益하고 저들의게서 生命이 될만한 그 무엇을 부어 줄 참된 機關노릇을 하여야만 될 것이다. 진실로 이마마한 誠意와 抱負와 끈임업는 努力이 잇슬가 疑問이다.

△ 松高校는 3, 4學年生 一同이 盟休하고 1, 2, 5年生도 動搖가 생김으로 8月 31日까지 臨時休學을 하얏다 함은 世上이 다- 안다. 그리고 3, 4學年에 38名의 犧牲者를 낸 것도 벌서 아는 일이다. 犧牲者를 낸 理由로는 多數 學生을 煽動하야 盟休事件을 니르켯슴으로 不良分子이며 不穩輩라 하고 또 이러한 學生을 그대로 두면 先生의 權威가 업서지며 따라서 先生의 地位가 危殆해진다고 하엿다 한다. 果然 이 學校에는 學生에게 不信任을 밧을 만한 先生님들이 相當히 만흔 모양이야!
