Article Title: 滿洲粟에 목을 매는 細民의 生活苦, 쓸 것은 만하 가고 벌이는 줄고 人心은 薄해 가고 (The Hardships of the Poor Who Depend on Manchurian Millet, Expenses Grow While Income Shrinks, and Human Feelings Grow Cold)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

**Body Text Translation:**
The Hardships of the Poor Who Depend on Manchurian Millet, Expenses Grow While Income Shrinks, and Human Feelings Grow Cold

In P'yŏngan Namdo (平南), apart from the plains counties like P'yŏngwŏn (平原), Anju (安州), Kangsŏ (江西), Yonggang (龍岡), Taedong (大同), Chunghwa (中和), and Sunch'ŏn (順川), most are mountainous counties (山邑). Among these, places like Yŏngwŏn (寧遠), Yangdŏk (陽德), Maengsan (孟山), and Tŏkch'ŏn (德川) are unspeakably mountainous counties. The people living there primarily make a living through slash-and-burn farming (火田), with chestnuts (栗) and corn (玉蜀黍) as staple foods, supplemented by barley (大麥), sweet potatoes (甘藷), and grass roots (草根). Furthermore, even in the counties located along the border, there isn't a single region where paddy fields and dry fields are balanced; dry fields are predominant everywhere. Therefore, there isn't a single county where the entire population eats rice, and in the mountainous counties, you cannot find even one household where everyone eats rice. Even in the wealthiest household of such a county, perhaps only the head of the family might eat rice, while the rest of the family all eat millet porridge. In other words, the overall situation of the province's residents has traditionally been like this.

However, since experiencing the terrible drought and famine (旱凶) of Taishō 8 (1919), this region has consecutively suffered poor harvests (歲事). The people who barely scraped by farming others' land (마직) have become completely unable to survive. Among them, those living along the Taedong River (大同江) who suffered particular damage began to wander (유리하기) last year, begging in cities like P'yŏngyang (平壤) and other areas. Within P'yŏngyang city, beggars crowd in morning and evening (朝夕); at inns (여관집), an average of 20 begging women are seen coming and going (送迎) daily these days. Moreover, the majority of P'yŏngan Namdo people now live on Manchurian millet (滿洲粟). Even looking at the rice stores (米廛) in a city like P'yŏngyang, Manchurian millet is the main item, while white rice presents the appearance of being displayed merely as a sample product (標品). If P'yŏngyang is like this, other places need not be mentioned.

For instance, looking at the arrivals and dispatches of Manchurian millet centered on P'yŏngyang Station (平壤驛) since Taishō 8: <123>
Year	Taishō 8	Taishō 9	Taishō 10	Taishō 11	Taishō 12	Total
Arrivals (tons)	2,571	9,028	1,852	1,545	9,895	24,891
Dispatches (tons)	1,238	4,248	572	754	1,372	8,184

According to the above, in the five years since Taishō 8, over 24,000 tons of Manchurian millet arrived at P'yŏngyang Station alone in P'yŏngan Namdo. Although over 8,000 tons were dispatched elsewhere, looking at the details (內譯), except for about 1,500 tons sent to places like Kyŏngsŏng (京城), Kaesŏng (開城), and Sariwŏn (沙里院), all the rest was sent to various stations within P'yŏngan Namdo. Ultimately, about 23,000 tons were distributed (配布) within the province, centered around P'yŏngyang. Furthermore, this data is only up to the end of last year; it is said to be even more severe this year.

Also, in Taishō 8, claiming to aid the drought victims (罹旱民), mutual aid associations (공제조합) were organized in each county. Under the joint liability (連帶) of these association members, money was loaned (貸與) from the Chiksan Bank (殖産銀行). However, due to consecutive poor harvests each year, there was no way to repay it. The bank is even carrying out execution (執行) and pressing for payment (督俸). The propertyless people (無産 人民), who went into debt when money was cheap (賤한) and now have to repay the debt when money is extremely valuable (極貴한), are instead resenting and lamenting (怨嗟) the improper arrangement (周旋) made at that time.

Stated this way, it seems the residents' hardship is solely due to the poor harvests, but in reality, there are other causes. Occasionally entering the farming villages and listening to the tear-filled words of the residents, there is nothing but talk of "we're going to die." Why are they going to die? They say it is because of (1) the aforementioned annual famines, (2) the various taxes (賦稅) that increase every year, (3) the compulsory labor service (賦役) requisitioned (徵發) at any time (無時로), (4) the human sentiment (人心) that grows harsher (각박해지는) year by year for this reason or that (이틋 저틋해서), and (5) for some unknown reason (엇재 그런지 모르지만), the money going out increases every year while earnings decrease (주러가는) every year.

This year is different from last year, and today is different from yesterday. Regardless of city or countryside, land is sold but cannot be bought, debts increase but cannot be repaid. Thus, households leave one by one, and people become poorer one by one – this is the state of the region today. Ah, who has spread this great net (그믈) over them? And who guards (守護) and uses (使用) this net? Friends who still say one can survive if only one does well – do you know or not that you too are already caught (쌔워든) in this net? <124>

**Summary**

The article describes the severe economic hardship faced by the residents of P'yŏngan Namdo province in 1924. Consecutive crop failures, particularly since 1919, have impoverished farmers, forcing many to rely on imported Manchurian millet as their staple food, displacing rice even in urban markets like P'yŏngyang. This reliance is evidenced by the large volume of millet imports recorded at P'yŏngyang Station. Widespread poverty has led to increased begging and displacement. Beyond poor harvests, the populace suffers from escalating taxes, frequent demands for unpaid labor (corvée), increasing debt burdens (exacerbated by loans from the colonial Chiksan Bank taken out during inflation and repaid during deflation), and a general decline in income coupled with rising expenses, creating a sense of pervasive despair and societal breakdown. For historians, this article offers a stark ground-level view of the economic distress in rural colonial Korea, highlighting the impact of agricultural failures, colonial economic policies (taxation, banking practices), forced labor, and the resulting social consequences like food dependency, migration, and a breakdown of community spirit, all framed within a critique of an exploitative system ("the great net").

기사형태	소식

滿洲粟에 목을 매는 細民의 生活苦, 쓸 것은 만하 가고 벌이는 줄고 人心은 薄해 가고
平南은 平原, 安州, 江西, 龍岡, 大同, 中和, 順川, 등 군을 제한 외에는 모다 산읍에 속하며, 그 중에도 寧遠, 陽德, 孟山, 德川 가튼 곳은 말할 수 업는 산읍으로서, 거긔에 사는 사람은 주로 火田을 耕耘하야, 栗, 玉蜀黍를 주식으로, 大麥, 甘藷, 草根 가튼 것을 助食으로 하야 생활하며, 또 여하히 沿邊에 잇는 군읍이라 할지라도 田畓 상반되는 지방이 업고, 어대나 田이 주가 되는 고로, 전 군이 쌀밥을 먹는 곳은 한 곳도 업스며, 산간 군에는, 전 군치고도 쌀밥 먹는 집, 한 집을 구경할 수 업다. 즉 그 군 一富이라 할지라도 그 집의 家長쯤이 쌀밥을 먹거나 말거나 하고, 그 남은 가족은 모다 조밥을 먹는 것이다. 말하면 본도민의 諸 상태는 自來로 이러하다.
그런대, 이 지방에는 大正 8년의 지독한 旱凶을 격근 후로 그間 連해서 歲事가 됴치 못하야, 남의 땅 마직이나 갈아서, 간신 간신히 살아가든 백성은 아조 살 수가 업시된 중, 그 중에도 大同江 연안에 살며 특별한 손해를 당한 사람은 작년 이래로 유리하기를 시작하야, 平壤과 가튼 도회지 기타 지방으로 걸식하는 바 平壤市 내에는 걸식인이 조석으로 듸려 밀녀, 여관집 쯤은 매일 평균 20명의 걸식 여인을 送迎하는 금일이며, 그리고 平南 사람의 大部는 지금 滿洲粟으로 사러가는데 平壤 가튼 도회지의 米廛을 볼지라도 滿洲粟이 주가 되고, 白米는 약간 標品으로 진열된 觀을 呈하는 바, 平壤이 이러하니, 다른 곳은 더 말할 것도 업슬 것이다.
試하야, 平壤驛을 중심 잡고 大正 8년 以降 滿洲粟의 着發을 보면, ＜123＞
대정8년  대정9년  대정10년  대정11년  대정12년    계
도착   2,571     9,028     1,852     1,545     9,895      24,891
발송   1,238     4,248      572      754      1,372       8,184

右로써 보면, 大正 8년 以降 5년 問에 平安南道 중 平壤驛에만 도착된 滿洲粟이 2만 4,000여噸이 도착되엿스며, 그 중에서 8,000여噸이 외지로 발송되엿다 하나, 그 내역을 보면, 京城, 開城, 沙里院 등지로 발송된 약 1,500噸을 제한 외에는, 모다 平南의 각 역으로 발송된 것인바, 결국 2만 3,000噸 가량이 平壤을 중심 잡아, 본도 내에 배포되엿스며, 또 이것은 작년 말까지의 일이거니와 금년 중에는 도로혀 심함이 잇다 한다.
그리고 大正 8년에 罹旱民을 구조한다 하고, 각 군에서 공제조합을 조직케 하야, 이 조합원의 연대로 殖産銀行의 돈을 貸與하엿는대, 해마다의 흉년으로 그것을 갑플 것은 업고, 銀行에서는 집행까지 하야 그를 督俸하는 바 돈이 賤한 떄에 빗을 지고, 돈이 極貴한 오늘에 빗을 갑게 되는 무산 인민은, 도로혀 그때의 맛당티 아는 周旋을 怨嗟하는 중에 잇다.
이러케 말하면, 그 지방민의 생활이 곤란한 것은 흉년 탓에만 잇는 듯하나, 기실은 원인이 또 잇다. 각금 농촌에 들어, 그 주민의 눈물 석긴 말을 드르면, 죽겟다는 소리 밧게는 다른 말이 업다. 웨, 죽겟느냐 하면 (1)前記의 年凶이오 (2)해마다 느러가는 각양의 賦稅오 (3)無時로 징발을 밧는 부역이오 (4)이틋 저틋해서 해마다 각박해지는 인심 때문이오 (5)엇재 그런지 모르지만 나가는 돈은 해마다 만하가고 버러드리는 것은 해마다 주러가는 故이라 한다.
금년이 작년과 다릇코, 오늘이 어제와 달나, 도시나 향촌을 물론하고, 땅은 팔지연뎡 사지 못하고, 빗은 더 질지연뎡 갑지 못하고, 그래서, 한 戶 두 戶 떠나가고, 한 사람 두 사람 더 못살게만 되는 것이, 오늘의 지방 형편이다. 아아, 이들의 우에 큰 그물을 벌인 자는 누구이냐, 그리고 이 그물을 수호 사용하는 자는 누구이냐, 그래도 자기만 잘하면 살 수 잇다는 친구들아, 너도 벌서 이 그믈 속에 쌔워든 줄을 아느냐 모르느냐. ＜124＞
