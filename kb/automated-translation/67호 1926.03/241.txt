**Summary**

This article is a company announcement from the magazine *Kaebyŏk*, detailing the establishment of new branch (支社) and sub-branch (分社) offices, as well as staff changes in existing ones, during July and August (presumably of 1925, given the publication date). It lists new offices in Ōsaka (Japan), Ch'ŏngjin, Changnyŏn (Haesŏ region), Chech'ŏn, and Kwaksan, along with staff appointments for each. It also notes staff changes (resignations and a reappointment) at the Kangso sub-branch. For historians, this provides valuable insight into the organizational structure and expanding reach of a major Korean nationalist magazine during the colonial period. The establishment of a branch in Japan with Korean and Japanese staff, and the detailed listing of local branches across Korea, illustrates the magazine's efforts to build a wide distribution and reporting network despite colonial constraints.
