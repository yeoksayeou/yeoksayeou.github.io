발행일	1926년 03월 01일
기사제목	宣言 (Declaration)
필자	金宙遠
기사형태	시 (Poem)

宣言
						金宙遠
Comrade of the soul, sick with a Silence that is neither death nor life!
Now is not the time to let your one precious life be eaten away
within the chamber of Hell, filled only with tears of repentance and sorrow.

To welcome the eternal comrade who will embrace your body and mind,
To find the suffering of today that strives to birth a new Century swift as a comb's teeth,
Oh, beloved comrade of the House of Fire!
With steps more precious than death, aided by boundless help and strength...
Let us go receive the sacred Baptism of the Sun that will pierce through the night streets of this land, like a drunken sea.
Until the spring light of vivid blue hope beats its wings of laughter and sings,
Until all tears of hatred and longing turn into the laughter of intimate love,
Unable to freely love even a single pitiful flower blossom—
Ah! To vent the unbearable humiliation of my spirit today,<118>
I shall now begin to dig within my breast
a sea of fiery blood that will melt away all the humiliation of weariness and despair.

Trying to dry up the tears of the heart that the pitiful night leaves behind,
The immortal Heart, dyed with red blood, leaps up far above the horizon of the East Sea (東海).
To knock on the gate of happiness in your country where not even a star can be seen—

Though soon my body's fate may end, guarding a lonely grave
on a rugged hill,
Only the unchanging lamp of my soul, woven with Passion and Reason, until the Kingdom of Hope comes before our eyes,
Will come to your breast, and sharing the tears of worry and sorrow in my heart, I believe it will live on.

Oh, comrade of the soul, starved for peace and freedom!
Behold! The bare mountainside of the valley facing the sun and the reddish-brown fields (垈赭色의 밧들) wet with dew,
Shining ever brighter in the early morning sunlight and the dreamlike evening glow,
Are they not the symbol (表象) of that Passion which burst forth from the immortal youthful heart, proving it lives on, unceasing?

To save me, starved for a Triumphal Song (凱旋歌) like a storm,
To make the history of humankind bloom once more with eternal freedom (久遠의 자유) and the liberation (解放) of rebirth (新生),
Oh—let us bless in our hearts our honorable death today as pioneers (先驅者)!<119>
<118-119>

**Summary**

This poem, "Declaration," by Kim Chuwŏn is an impassioned call to awaken from passive suffering ("Silence") and actively strive for a new era of freedom and life. The poet urges comrades to reject despair, embrace struggle ("the suffering of today"), and seek transformation ("Baptism of the Sun"). Despite the potential for sacrifice and death ("our honorable death today as pioneers"), the poet expresses faith in the enduring power of the spirit ("unchanging lamp of my soul") and the eventual arrival of a "Kingdom of Hope." The poem uses strong natural imagery (sun, sea, spring, fields) to symbolize resilience and the promise of rebirth. For historians, this piece, published on March 1st, 1926 (anniversary of the 1919 Movement), is a significant example of Korean nationalist sentiment and resistance literature during the Japanese colonial period. It reflects the deep yearning for liberation, the willingness to sacrifice, and the persistent hope for a national awakening, articulated through powerful, evocative poetic language.

오- 그리운 火屋의 동무여!
죽음보담도 더 갑진 도음과 힘을 다한 끈침업는 거름으로...
술취한 바다 가튼 이 땅의 밤거리를 뚤코 나갈 神聖한 太陽의 洗禮를 바드러 가자
새ㅅ파란 희망의 봄빗이 웃음의 나래를 치며 노래부를 때까지
다시는 모든 미움과 그리움의 눈물이 살가운 사랑의 웃음으로 옴길 때까지
나는 가엽시도 한 폭이의 꼿송이나마 자유롭게 사랑할 수 업는

나는 지금브텀 내 가슴속에다
倦怠와 絶望의 모든 屈辱이란 다 녹여 바릴 火血의 바다를 파기 시작하련다.

불상한 밤이 주고 가는 마음의 눈물을 말녀 바리려는
붉은 피로 물드린 不死의 心臟이 멀니 東海의 水平線 우흐로 뛰여 올은다

멀지 아니하야 나의 肉身의 운명은 다하야 거츨은 언덕 우에서
외로운 무덤 속을 직히고 잇슬지나
오직 情熱과 理智로 짠 내 령혼의 변치 안는 등불만은 희망의 王國이 눈압헤 올 때까지
네 가슴에 와 내 가슴속의 근심과 걱정의 눈물을 서로 난호며 살아 갈 줄 나는 밋노라.

오-평화와 자유에 주린 령혼의 동무여!
보라! 해마즌편 골작의 헐버슨 산비탈과 이슬에 저즌 垈赭色의 밧들은
