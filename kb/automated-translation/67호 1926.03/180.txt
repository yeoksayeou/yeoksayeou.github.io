잡지명	개벽 第六十七號
발행일	1926년 03월 01일
기사제목	이해를 보내는 노래 - 一九二四 - (Song Sending Off This Year - 1924 -)
필자	李相和
기사형태	시 (Poem)

『It was a year of drought, great floods, fires, and many deaths. Oh, people of Chosŏn (朝鮮)! The single phrase "Fire broke out on Kŭmgangsan (金剛山)" – what a profound revelation (黙示) it is! Isn't it a spine-chilling phrase? Oh Hanunim (한우님) – not a goblin (독갑이) created by the weak hearts of humans, but the singular wisdom (睿智) of humankind, the spirit of nature (自然의 靈精) that gives strength to the world – I call upon you and speak: Do not misjudge, but see clearly. Before this year completely passes –. Oh, all the Hanunim dwelling hidden in the hearts of the people of Chosŏn!』

Hanunim! I send it back to you.<114>
Wrapped in rotten sighs and blood-soaked tears, this year,
I send back to you, unsure whether to receive it laughing or crying.

This year you sent, tried to drown me, who was thirsty, in water,
Tried also to burn this naked body barely covered in rags (누덕이),
And starved and starved, until people resented each other, then starved to death – this year I return.

Hanunim! I implore you.
Lying prostrate on the ground, looking up at the heavens with a gut-wrenching sound,
I implore you, unsure if you will hear it hatefully or sorrowfully.

This year you sent brought us "Noah's Flood,"
And showed that the "Sulfur fire" of that day could also be made by humans, burning us,
But even starving and starving, we did not awaken. Did you starve us to death? I implore.<115>

Ah, Hanunim!
Receive this year, and starting from the morning of the coming new year, send down lightning bolts.
Either we shall know that evil can sometimes be kinder than good, or, if we do not know, we shall die.
- From 『Wailing (慟哭)』-<116>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Sang-hwa, titled "Song Sending Off This Year - 1924 -," is a powerful lament directed at Hanunim (a Korean term for God). The speaker recounts the devastating events of 1924 in Korea – drought, floods, fires (specifically mentioning one on Kŭmgangsan), and widespread death from starvation. Filled with anguish and resentment, the speaker "returns" the disastrous year to God, questioning whether these calamities were punishments or trials. The poem culminates in a desperate plea for a radical change in the new year, even invoking destructive lightning, suggesting that even harsh divine intervention is preferable to the current suffering, and expressing a willingness to die if unable to grasp a deeper, perhaps paradoxical, understanding of good and evil. From a historical perspective, the poem vividly reflects the hardships faced by Koreans under colonial rule, exacerbated by natural disasters in 1924. The reference to the Kŭmgangsan fire and the general despair, framed as a confrontation with a divine figure, could be interpreted as expressing deep national suffering and a yearning for drastic change or liberation, making it a significant piece for understanding the socio-political and emotional climate of the era.

이해를 보내는 노래  - 一九二四 -
						李相和
『감음이 들고 큰물이 지고 불이 나고 목숨이 만히 죽은 올해이다. 朝鮮사람아 金剛山에 불이 낫단 이 한 말이 얼마나 깁흔 黙示인가. 몸서리 치이는 말이 아니냐. 오 한우님-사람의 弱한 마음이 만든 독갑이가 아니라 누리에게 힘을 주는 自然의 靈精인 한아 뿐인 사람의 睿智-를 불러 말하노니 잘못 짐작을 갓지 말고 바로 보아라. 이 해가 다 가기 전에-.朝鮮사람의 가슴 마다에 숨어사는 모든 한우님들아!』
한우님! 나는 당신께 돌려 보냄니다.＜114＞
속석은 한숨과 피저즌 눈물로 이해를 쌋서
웃고 바들지 울고 바들지 모르는 당신께 돌려 보냄니다.
당신이 보낸 이해는 목마르든 나를 물에 빠저 죽이려다가
누덕이로 겨오 가린 헐버슨 몸을 태우려도 하엿고
주리고 주려서 사람끼리 원망타가 굶어 죽고만 이 해를 들려 보냄니다.
한우님! 나는 당신께 뭇조려 함니다.
땅에 업대여 한을을 우럴어 창자비-ㄴ소리로
밉게 드를지 섧게 드를지 모르는 당신께 뭇조려 함니다.
당신보낸 이 해는 우리에게 『노아의 洪水』를 갓고 왓다가
그 날의 『硫黃불』은 사람도 만들 수 잇다 태워보엿스나
주리고 주려도 우리들이 못깨첫다. 굶어 죽엿든가 뭇조려 함니다.＜115＞
아 한우님!
이해를 바 드시고 오는 새해 아츰부턴 벼락을 내려 줍쇼.
惡도 善보담 더 착할 때 잇슴을 아옵든지 모르면 죽으리다.
-『慟哭』에서-＜116＞
