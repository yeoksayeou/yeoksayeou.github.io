Article Title: 餘言 (Postscript/Closing Remarks)
Author: Not specified (likely editorial staff)
Article Type: 사고·편집후기 (Notice/Editorial Postscript)

△ March! I don't know if people in other countries think the same way, but it's a fact that we Chosŏn people, regardless of the year, think of March with a distinctly different significance. Whether this is because we contemplate (想) the March 1st Movement, or because all things in the universe take on colors of renewal (갱신) in March, in any case, March in Chosŏn is considered the most meaningful and joyful month among the twelve months of the year.

△ This meaningful March! How many times has our [Kaebyŏk] met it? Shall we say it's been long? By year count, it's the seventh year, and by month, this March is the sixth one we meet this year [sic, likely means 'this is the sixth March issue we've published']. During the period of meeting it these six times, whether "Kaebyŏk" has provided benefit or harm to society and the populace is left to the judgment of third parties. However, it is a fact that we, for our part, have done our utmost, and we resolve once again to do so going forward.

△ We did indeed edit the March issue with a different significance, aiming to meet the expectations of the Chosŏn populace. However, leaving that to the reader's judgment, we merely recommend a few items here: the "Historical Consideration of Chosŏn's Social Movements" and the "Overview of the Whole and Roster of Figures" (全體와 全體及人物一覽) are expected to be of great reference. And one point for which we feel apologetic: Mr. Kim Yangsu's "World of Last Year" (작년의 世界), kindly sent from distant America, could not be published entirely in one installment and had to be divided into two, for which we apologize to the author and readers.

△ Our original thought was whether it isn't our duty to provide a concrete view (具體視) of both directions – Chosŏn's social movements and national movements – in the March issue. We postpone this to a future opportunity (機會).

△ Due to page count constraints, what was pushed back from the previous issue could not be included this time either. Above all, the continuation of Mr. Yi Sanghwa's "Proletarian Writers and Proletarian Works" (無産作家와 無産作品) could also not be published. We wonder how to apologize (謝할는지) for the futile efforts (徒勞) of our writers; we can only ask for your kind understanding (高諒).

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the March 1926 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine reflects on the significance of March for Koreans, particularly due to the March 1st Movement (1919) and the sense of renewal associated with spring. The editors acknowledge the magazine's seventh year and sixth March issue, recommitting to serving society while leaving judgment of their past contributions to others. They highlight specific articles in the current issue focusing on the history of Korean social movements and apologize for splitting an article by Kim Yangsu from America and delaying the continuation of Yi Sanghwa's piece on proletarian literature due to space limitations. The most interesting aspect for historians is the direct reference to the ongoing significance of the March 1st Movement seven years later, the mention of specific articles analyzing social and national movements, and the inclusion of contemporary intellectual debates like proletarian literature. It also reveals practical publishing constraints and editorial priorities during the colonial period.
