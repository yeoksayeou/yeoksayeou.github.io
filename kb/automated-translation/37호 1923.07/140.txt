Important Domestic and International Diary
From April 20th to May 23rd

April 20th
The Kyŏngsŏng Chamber of Commerce holds an Advertising Materials Exhibition for 5 days starting today at the Hasegawa-chō Public Hall.
A foreign debt consolidation loan of 400 million yuan is concluded between the Chinese government and the New Loan Consortium.

Same day [April] 21st
Mr. Yi Tong-hwi, leader of the Koryŏ Communist Party, executes 8 Koreans who were Japanese secret agents.
Ch'en Chiung-ming (진형명) convenes important figures in Hong Kong and secretly discusses attacking Sun Wen's (손문) headquarters.

Same day [April] 22nd
The monthly tuition fee for each public common school in Kyŏngsŏng City, previously 50 *chŏn*, is revised and approved today to 80 *chŏn*.
A lecture session on remodeled *ondol* construction methods is held in the Kyŏnggi Provincial Council meeting room.
The German government arrests 70 Communist Party members in Mülheim to suppress communists and nationalists.

Same day [April] 23rd
Hosted by the Kyŏngsŏng Chamber of Commerce, over 40 businessmen gather to hold the Chosŏn Industry Discussion Meeting.
Representatives of the Irish Free State deliver an application for the country's admission to the League of Nations to the League's Secretary-General.

Same day [April] 24th
The Chosŏn Lawyers Association holds its regular general meeting and re-elects officers: President Yun T'ae-yŏng, Vice President Mun T'aek-kyu.

Same day [April] 25th
The Maritime Labor Union, with 126,000 union members, declares a general strike at noon today.

Same day [April] 26th
A triangular alliance between Chang Tso-lin (장작림), Feng Yü-hsiang (풍옥상 - Korean reading: P'ung Ok-sang, though the Hanja 虛永祥 in the text seems incorrect for Feng, likely a typo for 馮玉祥), and Sun Wen (손문) is realized, isolating Shen Hung-ying (심홍영) and Sun Ch'uan-fang (손전방), and plans are devised to attack the southwestern provinces.

Same day [April] 27th
The Labor Federation holds its 2nd Regular General Meeting and discusses matters concerning labor unions, tenant farmer unions, unemployment prevention, etc.

Same day [April] 29th
The All-Chosŏn Baseball Tournament is held at the Chungang Higher Common School grounds in Kye-dong.
Yi Yŏl-gyun's (이열균) army, having obtained a loan of 1 million won from some quarter, dispatches troops to Liuheung (유향) to invade Guangdong, while Liu Hsi-min (유희민), a former subordinate of Ch'en Chiung-ming, declares independence from the Guangdong government in Jingzhou (형주).
The Irish Republican Army proposes to cease hostilities if a general referendum on the new constitution is held.

Same day [April] 30th
The 1st Regular General Meeting of the Chosŏn Products Promotion Association opens in the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall.
Landlords from the three counties of Posŏng, Kohŭng, and Sunch'ŏn hold a United Landlords Convention in Pŏlgyo to oppress tenant farmers, decide on 7 conditions, and then negotiate with provincial authorities for their support.

May 1st
The Juvenile Movement Association declares three basic articles concerning the juvenile movement, designates today as "Children's Day" ("어린이날"), distributes 120,000 declaration leaflets throughout the capital and countryside, and holds commemorative lectures and entertainment performances.
The International Labor Day commemorative procession hosted by the Chosŏn Labor Federation is halted due to police authorities' crackdown.
The Chosŏn Journalists Association is founded by reporters from newspapers and news agencies based in Kyŏngsŏng.
In Tokyo, over 10,000 workers from about 30 organizations hold a large May Day demonstration procession and many are arrested.

Same day [May] 2nd
A cooperative organization for the medical world, encompassing all physicians in Chosŏn, is initiated.
The Yunnan allied forces launch a general offensive, inflicting a major defeat on the Guangdong army,<84> with 800 prisoners and 800 casualties on both sides, staging the largest fierce battle in Guangdong's history.
The second-anniversary celebration of Sun Wen's inauguration is grandly held in Guangdong.
The British Columbia [likely California, given context/common issues] Lower House passes an anti-Japanese land/harvest law [likely related to alien land laws].

Same day [May] 3rd
The Government-General announces in the official gazette that graduates of special courses and specialized departments established under the old education ordinance will also be recognized as graduates of specialized schools under the new education ordinance.
The Chinese government, following the insistence from the Luoyang faction, discusses the matter of issuing an order to subjugate Sun Wen at today's cabinet meeting.

Same day [May] 4th
About 200 female workers at the rice mill run by Katō Heitarō in Asahi-machi, Chinnamp'o, go on a collective strike over wage reduction issues.
Over 70 tenant farmers of Sŏ U-sun in Kach'ang-myŏn, Talsŏng-gun, appeal to the county office regarding the landlord's illegal actions.
The opening ceremony for the 14-mile new line between Ch'ŏngju and Ch'ŏngan on the Chungang Railway is held, and operations begin.
An emergency cabinet meeting is held at the Japanese Prime Minister's residence to discuss policy towards Russia.
The envoys of Britain, America, France, and Japan meet with Chinese Premier Chang [likely Zhang Shaozeng] and advise preventing the Fengtian-Zhili war.

Same day [May] 5th
The names of prisons in Chosŏn are changed to 'Hyŏngmuso' (Penitentiary), and branch prisons to 'Hyŏngmuso Chiso' (Penitentiary Branch Office), effective today.
The 3rd All-Chosŏn Soccer Tournament, hosted by the P'yŏngyang Christian Youth Association, opens today for two days; the championship flag goes to Sungsil School.
The All-Russian Church Council declares the adoption of the new calendar in Russia and that general Christians should henceforth observe church holidays.
France and Italy notify the Beijing government that they will not participate in the tariff conference unless China resolves the Austrian loan and Gold Franc issues.
The Japanese National Language Research Council decides and announces the limitation of commonly used Kanji to 1,961 characters.

Same day [May] 6th
A bicycle race meet opens at Changch'ungdan; the crowd becomes enraged and fights erupt, claiming that Eom Pok-tong's 3rd place finish was due to interference by Japanese individuals.
Around 1:30 AM, a fire breaks out at the house of Chŏn Ch'i-do in Posu-dong, Pusan, completely burning 17 houses and partially burning 3.
The Chosŏn Fisheries Association Ordinance is promulgated as a Government-General decree and implemented immediately.
The Guangdong government grants special amnesty to 200 prisoners, excluding political offenders, on the occasion of the National Humiliation Day.

Same day [May] 7th
As today corresponds to China's National Humiliation Day, various places in China display the full text of the May 7, 1915 [referring to the Twenty-One Demands] ultimatum with black borders. 5,000 students hold a National Opinion Rally, propagandize opposition to the Twenty-One Demands, recovery of Liaodong, and boycott of Japanese goods while marching through the city. Also, one division under General Feng Yü-hsiang (빙옥상) waves a half-mast flag reading "Never Forget National Humiliation," and the artillery unit fires blank shots.
The British cabinet deliberates on the recent situation regarding German reparations.

Same day [May] 8th
The Red Army executes 5 pro-Japanese Koreans in Vladivostok.
His Majesty King George V of England meets with Italian Prime Minister Mussolini.
A riot occurs in Amritsar, India, resulting in 50 casualties among Sikh devotees [Original uses 印度 敎徒, 'Indian believers', Amritsar context suggests Sikh].

Same day [May] 9th
The trial of a faction of the East Asian Communist Party is postponed [likely means 'convened' or 'resumed']; prosecutor demands 8 years imprisonment for Hwang Ton and 6 years for Kim Tae-sŏn.
Numerous documents related to socialism sent from the Tokyo area are seized by the Kyŏngsŏng Post Office.
Due to the Jinpu Railway bandit incident, Britain and America issue mobilization orders for their troops stationed in Beijing and Tianjin.

Same day [May] 10th
The British and American associations in Beijing and Tianjin hold an emergency joint meeting in Tianjin regarding the Jinpu line bandit incident. After discussion, they decide to place foreign guards on the railway.
Three Russian delegates staying in Lausanne are shot dead by a Swiss nationalist member; this is said to be personal revenge as the youth's father and uncle were killed by extremists. <85>

Same day [May] 11th
A joint conference composed of representatives from the British Trades Union Congress, the Independent Labour Party, and the Labour Party parliamentary group is held in the House of Commons.
The judging results for submissions to the 2nd Chosŏn Art Exhibition are announced and simultaneously opened to the public.
A conference of provincial judicial officers is held in the Government-General's No. 2 Conference Room.
Li Yuan-hung (여원홍) declares to Ts'ao K'un (조*) [曹锟, Cao Kun - asterisk likely indicates missing Hanja or illegibility] that he will resign on the Republic's commemorative day, October 10th.

Same day [May] 12th
The trial for the Kim Sang-ok incident opens in Courtroom No. 7 of the Kyŏngsŏng District Court, and the facts are examined.
A delegation of 150 Japanese newspaper reporters arrives in Kyŏngsŏng to attend the 11th General Meeting of the Newspaper Association.

Same day [May] 13th
The 11th General Meeting of the Newspaper Association and the National Newspaper Reporters Convention are held at the Chosŏn Hotel.
An agreement is reached between the Chinese government and the bandits through the mediation of Anderson [likely Roy Chapman Andrews or another prominent foreigner involved in negotiations] et al.
The British and Italian official notes regarding the German reparations memorandum are handed over to the Berlin government tonight.

Same day [May] 14th
A Belgian business delegation of 11 members arrives in Kyŏngsŏng from Kobe, Japan, as part of a tour to inspect various places in the East.
A clash occurs between a Japanese warship and an anti-Japanese group in Changsha, resulting in injuries to numerous Chinese people.

Same day [May] 15th
It is announced that the preparatory course for Chosŏn University, scheduled to open in Taishō 13 (1924), will be based on the medical college, with a budget of 6 million yen.
The Provincial Governors' Conference is held from today until the 19th in the Government-General's No. 2 Conference Room.
The Kyŏngsŏng Urban Research Society meets at the Chamber of Commerce to discuss preparations for the general meeting and other matters.
The Japanese Women's Peace Association organizes an International Affairs Study Group.

Same day [May] 16th
The inaugural ceremony of the Hyŏngp'yŏngsa (Equality Society) is held in Chinju, Kyŏngnam; about 30,000 *paekchŏng* (members of the traditionally outcast butcher/leatherworker class) from the entire province attend.
The 6th General Convention of the All-Chosŏn Chambers of Commerce Federation is held within the Kyŏngsŏng Chamber of Commerce.
A large lecture meeting denouncing the illegal confinement of Koreans, organized by lawyer Fuse Tatsuji, is held within the Korean Christian Youth Association in Nishi Ogawa-machi, Tokyo; 8 people are arrested.
The National Federated Education Association of Japan decides to extend compulsory education starting from Taishō 13 (1924).

Same day [May] 17th
The 4th All-Chosŏn Baseball Tournament is held for 3 consecutive days starting today at the Paejae School grounds in Chŏng-dong.
Labor Russia arrests 3,265 corrupt officials.

Same day [May] 18th
In response to the hostage-taking incident by bandits on the Jinpu line, the Beijing government places 40 armed soldiers and one machine gun on the Beijing-Hankou line train departing from Beijing.

Same day [May] 19th
The first anniversary memorial service (*ki\**sik) [祈禱式 - Prayer Service, likely] after the passing ('return to origin') of the 3rd Patriarch of Ch'ŏndogyo, Uiam Sŏngsa Sŏn Pyŏng-hŭi, is grandly held inside the Ch'ŏndogyo Hall in ****-dong [Illegible].
The 1st
Award Ceremony for Persons of Merit in Education is held in the Government-General's No. 2 Conference Room.
The Diplomatic Corps makes a stern demand to the Chinese government for the release of detained foreigners.
French authorities expel 700 railway and customs officials from the occupied zone and arrest 5 prominent figures from the industrial world.

Same day [May] 20th
The Kyŏnggi Provincial Police Department announces the arrest of Yun Eung-nyŏm, a member of the Shanghai Provisional Government, in Inch'ŏn.
It is reported that Mr. Hwang Ki-hwan, secretary-general to Mr. Kim Kyu-sik of the same government, passed away from heart disease in America on April 17th.
British Prime Minister Bonar Law announces his resignation due to ill health.

Same day [May] 21st
Mr. Hŏ Sŏng, who specialized in physical education in America for many years, returns home.
The 1st General Meeting of the Chosŏn Education Association is held at the Kyŏngsŏng Public Hall, with about 700 participants.
A conference of Provincial Police Chiefs is held in the Government-General's No. 2 Conference Room.
As negotiations between the Chinese government and the bandits have broken down, the Diplomatic Corps again engages in serious negotiations with the government.

Same day [May] 22nd
The Enforcement Rules for the Chosŏn Vaccination Ordinance are promulgated (Effective date is September 1st).
A commemorative exhibition for the opening of the municipally operated tramway is held in P'yŏngyang.
The British King summons Chancellor of the Exchequer Stanley Baldwin to the palace and commands him to form a new cabinet.

Same day [May] 23rd
A group of 31 Korean sightseers residing in Kando arrives in Kyŏngsŏng for a visit to the motherland. <86>

**Summary**

This article chronicles significant events, both domestic (within Korea) and international, from April 20th to May 23rd, 1923. Domestically, it covers colonial administrative actions like tuition hikes, changes in prison nomenclature, and education reforms; social and political movements including communist activities, labor strikes (shipping, rice mill workers), the founding of Children's Day, the establishment of various associations (lawyers, journalists, doctors, Hyŏngp'yŏngsa for *paekchŏng* rights), tenant farmer disputes, and cultural events like sports tournaments and art exhibitions. Internationally, it reports on Chinese political turmoil (warlord conflicts, Sun Wen's struggles, anti-Japanese sentiment, banditry affecting foreigners), European affairs (German reparations, Irish independence efforts, Mussolini's rise), Russian developments (calendar change, execution of Koreans, assassination of diplomats), US/Canada-Japan relations (anti-Japanese legislation), and British politics (change of Prime Minister). The diary highlights the interconnectedness of local Korean issues with broader East Asian and global events during the colonial period. Historians would find valuable the specific dates and details of Korean social movements (labor, tenant, *paekchŏng*, youth), the formation of civil society groups, colonial government responses, the reporting on Korean independence figures abroad (Yi Tong-hwi, Shanghai Provisional Government members), and the local perspective on international crises like the Chinese civil war and anti-Japanese sentiment.
