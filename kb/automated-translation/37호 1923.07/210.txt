쓰린 웃음
						金岸曙

탈춤 (Mask Dance)

Everyone, if you wish to taste the joy of living,
Politely put on the formal attire of "Morality" (道德) and the hat of "Law" (法律),
And enter here, this is
Life's "Self-interest" (利己) mask dance hall.
You must dance well; if you stumble awkwardly,
You will fall into the trap of that fellow called Fate (運命),
Then your name will be lost from the "Register of Happiness" (幸福의 名簿),
And you will never again get the admission ticket, the right to life (人生權).
Life is short, and the time for dancing is long.
If you lose one minute, one minute's worth of the dance of happiness
Will disappear. Good deeds (善) must be done quickly.<80>
Well then, let us dance quickly! Good, good, Eolsigu!...

友情 (Friendship)

Love is more boundless than the flowers of a late spring day,
And Friendship wanders from wine cup to wine cup,
Shedding false laughter and cheap tears, then
Disappears more easily than the lingering smell of tobacco smoke,
Will there be any limit to the sorrow left behind?

Oh people, we who tread the path of Life vigorously,
Our hearts and minds have never once been united,
So, on this evening when even the late spring flowers are falling,
I bring the wandering wine cup to my lips,
And watching your face full of friendship, I laugh alone. <81>
<80-81>

**Summary**

This two-part poem by Kim An-sŏ presents a deeply cynical view of life and relationships. The first part, "Mask Dance," portrays society as a hypocritical performance where individuals must wear masks of morality and law while pursuing self-interest to achieve happiness, lest they fall into fate's trap. The second part, "Friendship," laments the superficiality and transience of human bonds, comparing friendship to something even more fleeting than love or tobacco smoke, ending on a note of solitary, ironic observation. For historians, the poem offers insight into the disillusionment and social critique present among some Korean intellectuals during the early colonial period. It reflects a weariness with societal expectations and a questioning of conventional values like morality and friendship, possibly influenced by the pressures and perceived insincerities of the time.

탈춤

「道德」의 禮服과 「法律」의 갓을 얌전히 쓰고,
다 이곳으로 들어옵시요, 이 곳은
人生의 「利己」의 탈춤 會場입니다.
춤을 잘 추어야 합니다, 서툴어 넘어지면
運命이라는 놈의 陷井에 들어갑니다,
하면 「幸福의 名簿」에서는 이름을 어이게 되며,
入場券인 人生權을 다시는 엇지 못합니다.
人生은 짧고, 춤추는 時間은 깁니다.
한 分을 일허버리면 한 分만큼 한 幸福의 춤이

友情
사랑은 저믄 봄날의 꼿보다도 가이 업고
友情은 술잔에서 술잔으로 떠돌아가며
거짓의 웃음과 갑 업는 울음을 흘리다가는
어리운 담뱃내보다도 더 쉽게 슬어지나니,
다음에 남은 설음이야 限이나 잇스랴.

사람아, 氣運잇게 人生의 길을 밟는 우리의
맘과 맘과는 한 番좃차 合한 적이 업서라,
그러면, 느즌 봄날의 꼿도 지는 이 저녁에
나는 떠돌아 가는 술잔을 입에 대이고,
友情 가득한 그대의 얼골을 혼자 보며 웃노라. ＜81＞
