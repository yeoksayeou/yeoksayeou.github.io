Magazine Title: 개벽 제37호
Publication Date: 1923년 07월 01일
Article Title: 春星 (Spring Star)
Author: 稻香
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Short Story)

春星
						稻香

"Ŭnju-ya! Hey, Ŭnju-ya!"
Ch'unsŏng called for the housemaid the moment he entered his house. Ŭnju, who was washing dishes with a cloth in the kitchen, glanced at Ch'unsŏng coming in through the kitchen window bars, then pretended not to see him again.
"Yes," she answered, but said nothing more.
Ch'unsŏng's dark face was flushed red like an overcooked liver from the alcohol he had drunk. The irritation from his daily shave, done to tidy his hairy face, was starkly blue.
He went up onto the *maru* (wooden floor hall) again and threw open the doors of each room across the way.
"Ŭnju-ya!" he shouted, then tore off his suffocating collar and threw it down.
"Is this girl deaf? Ugh, what is this? What is this room! Is this a pigsty or a place where people live? Hey Ŭnju-ya! Ŭnju-ya! Hey, my throat hurts! Ŭnju-ya!"
Ŭnju, who had been deliberately not answering, seemed to feel sorry for him making such a fuss.
"What is it!" she burst out of the kitchen as if stamping her feet.
"What do you mean, 'What is it!' What have you been doing all day? Why didn't you clean the room when I told you to? Is the broom just for lighting the fire! And what do you do all day long? Humph, you're in big trouble these days, big trouble!"
Ŭnju pouted her lips and narrowed her eyes hatefully.<15>
"I went on an errand to the little master's house (작은댁) today," she said, putting her hands into her apron pockets and then taking them out, mumbling something under her breath so others couldn't hear.
"An errand? What errand took you all day?"
Ŭnju said nothing. Ch'unsŏng's mother, who had been in the back, appeared at the back door of the *maru*.
"Fighting again. Where did you go all day today? Drunk again? You just don't listen no matter how I tell you. Hey, you, hurry up and set the table! Hmm?" she said, then glared at Ŭnju.
Ch'unsŏng, still feeling unsatisfied, grumbled,
"Once she goes out, she doesn't know how to come back in! It's chaos! Chaos!" His cheeks puffed out as he was about to go into the room, when Ŭnju retorted,
"What's chaos? What chaos is there?" looking up at Ch'unsŏng with sharp, defiant eyes as if ready to quarrel.
The mother, perhaps thinking of something, looked behind her and stood blankly for a moment. Hearing Ŭnju's voice, she made a fist as if to strike her.
"You brat, shut your mouth!"
Then, to her son again,
"Really, child! Please just go in and sleep or something. Why can't you be more dignified! Eh?"
She clicked her tongue, but Ch'unsŏng, whether he heard or not, said,
"Bring me a bowl of cold water! Hurry!"
He went into the room, gathered the scattered paper scraps and dust on the floor, then collapsed onto the warmer part of the *ondol* floor near the fireplace. He lay there, eyes open, staring at the ceiling.
His mind spun dizzily, as if winding one end of a rainbow around his finger, round and round, faster and faster,<16> and his body felt like it was being pulled into it. Sometimes, like being on a boat, his body seemed to sink into the ground, or sometimes, like falling from a height, it felt dizzying, yet his limbs and body felt stuck fast to the floor, comfortable like a tired person collapsing onto damp earth.
He sighed, "Hoo-" Inside the room, things appeared only as hazy outlines in a fog, and an inexplicable, overflowing joy made his shoulders want to dance. He chuckled to himself,
"That's right, that! Right!"
'For some reason, drinking always feels good! How wonderful it would be if Yŏngsuk were here at a time like this. When I come home drunk like this, she'd be overjoyed, help me take off my clothes, and spread out the bedding. Then she'd say, "Where did you drink so much *yakchu* (rice wine)? I hate it when you drink *yakchu*. Ugh, I hate seeing it!" And when she turns away slightly, haha, that's right, I'd gently try to coax her, "I won't drink anymore, okay? Hmm? Come here!" And if I pull her, she'd say, "I hate it!" Her sharp face would try to hide an irresistible smile, turning her head away even more. Then I, pretending to surrender, would say, "Really, I said I won't drink, I won't, I won't!" Then Yŏngsuk, deliberately waiting a moment, would say, "Won't drink what? A leopard can't change its spots, can you fix a habit once it's formed?" And as she sits quietly, I'd gently tickle her side. Then she'd jump in surprise, turn around, and say, "What are you doing?" and the laughter she'd been holding back would burst out. Then I'd grab both her arms tightly and say, "Come here, I won't drink anymore!" At first, she'd try to pull her arms away, but then she'd rush into my chest like a swallow, pleading, "Don't drink *yakchu* anymore, please, please!" Ugh, that girl. What can I do! She's just so unbearably lovely!'
He smiled foolishly to himself. Then he took off his outer jacket again.
"That's right. Right," he muttered, putting the clothes on the hanger, and as he passed the desk again, he saw a letter on it.<17>
"A letter!"
He looked at it quizzically for a while, then picked it up with one hand, saw it was opened, and exclaimed,
"Yŏngsuk sent a letter!"
He turned it over and over, examining the handwriting, then tilted his head.
"Well, this is unexpected. She's picked up a new habit. She's started writing letters, which she never did before?"
He turned on the electric light and lay down again on the bedding in the warm part of the room.
"Let's see!"
He opened it with the happy and joyful eyes of a groom receiving a marriage proposal letter. Then he stopped.
"No, no. I should read it slowly! To rush through something so delightful would be a waste, a waste!"
He placed it back in a corner of the desk. After a while,
"What could she have written?"
He picked up the letter from the desk again.
"Now, let's just read it! She wrote it so demurely."
He praised it once as if satisfied, then tore open the envelope.
Then,
"Right, Mr. Ch'unsŏng! That's right,"
He read a few more lines, his eyes moving up and down. Then his eyes became blank like those of someone shocked to death.
"What? 'I have no tears. Since I have no tears, how could I have the warm affection worthy of your love...?' Moreover, 'Please have no tears...'"
Ch'unsŏng crumpled the letter in his hand.<18>
"What? Because I have no tears, I don't have warm affection worthy of love?"
He stood blankly for a moment.
"Anyway, women are foxes, foxes! If you don't like me, just say you don't like me. What's this about not being able to love because I have no tears?"
Anger flared up anew from somewhere.
"Aeng!"
He spitefully tore the letter in his hand into pieces.
"Forget it! Do you think there are no other women besides you? You worthless thing!"
He turned his head as if Yŏngsuk were beside him and shut his eyes tightly.
However, inside his chest, a tangled knot of resentment, lingering sadness, and attachment remained. Yŏngsuk appeared before his eyes again. Then Yŏngsuk came closer and closer to him. So, in his heart, he thought, 'Surely she couldn't have sent me away? Maybe she just wanted to see my tears because I have no tears, so she did it on purpose! That's right.' He smiled, watching the approaching Yŏngsuk, intending to appease her and reconcile. But Yŏngsuk came very close, bent over as if mocking him, raised one hand as if to point, and then passed by. If she had just passed by, it would have been better, but from a distance, she looked back over her shoulder and smiled faintly, as if sneering. At that, he slammed his fist on the floor and opened his eyes wide. The electric light shone brightly, as if shattering the darkness in one go.
"Eh, heartless woman!"
He muttered to himself, "Because I have no tears?" then stared blankly at the ceiling again.

It was about eight o'clock. Ŭnju brought the dinner table into the room across.<19>
"Please have your dinner."
"..."
Ch'unsŏng didn't answer, and the room was quiet.
"I brought the table."
But Ch'unsŏng had fallen asleep lying in the warm part of the room. Ŭnju, seeming somewhat frightened of Ch'unsŏng, gently shook him.
"Please wake up."
He shifted, turning over with a groan.
"Ung, ung,"
He covered his eyes with his arm, lay face down, and began to sob quietly, sniffling repeatedly. Ŭnju's eyes widened.
"Oh my, why are you crying? Madam! The master is crying!"
She dashed into the main room (anpang). Then, looking back as if with pity and sympathy, she hesitated, standing there looking pitiful.
"Madam, please go see."
"What is it?"
The mother crossed over to the other room and saw Ch'unsŏng lying face down on the bedding, sniffling and crying.
"Child! Why are you crying right after waking up! Did you have a bad dream? You crazy child! Hey, hurry up and get up and eat."
Ch'unsŏng paid no attention, just continuing to cry like a small child. Ŭnju watched this strange scene from behind the mother.
"Get up! Hurry, hurry!"<20>
Tears streamed down Ch'unsŏng's face like rain as he turned his head.
"Dinner?"
He looked up at his mother.
"What dinner? Eat dinner at a time like this?"
"What do you mean, 'a time like this'? It's evening dinner!"
"I don't want it. It's bothersome. Please go back across."
He turned over again and started crying once more.
"But why are you crying? Really, all sorts of strange things happen in the end!"
After a while, Ch'unsŏng suddenly sat up.
"No. I'll go see right now,"
He rummaged around, pulling out clothes and putting them on.
"Right, how can a person be so heartless?"
As he tried to go out the door, his mother stood up and held onto Ch'unsŏng like a child.
"Really, are you not fully awake? Are you possessed by a ghost? I can't understand you at all. Where are you planning to go?"
"No. I'll go see right away."

Ch'unsŏng left his house. He went to Yŏngsuk's house, but Yŏngsuk wasn't there.
He went for two days but couldn't see her.
Previously, she would always come out happily and smile when he arrived, but now that he couldn't meet her even after three visits, for some reason, he wanted to pound on her gate and wanted to writhe in agony in front of her gate. The naturally <21> weak-hearted Ch'unsŏng, experiencing such disappointment for the first time, desperately wanted to meet Yŏngsuk, hold onto her, pour out his heart, and cry.
Then he thought,
"That's right, whenever I meet Yŏngsuk, I'll try crying just like I did in my dream. Then, seeing my tears, she surely won't be able to say she doesn't love me anymore. Why am I a man without tears? It's not that I don't have tears, but when I see her, everything is so joyful and pleasant, how can I not laugh?"
On the fourth day, Ch'unsŏng came to Yŏngsuk's house and waited at the gate for Yŏngsuk to come out. Standing at the gate, waiting for Yŏngsuk, pacing back and forth, felt exactly like what he had seen in his dream. Even a small stone rolling in front of him seemed exactly the same as he remembered.
After a little while, a servant came out.
"She says to come in."
Ch'unsŏng was incredibly happy inside. "Come in? Then that letter must have been just a joke?" he thought to himself as he went inside. In the room across, Yŏngsuk sat listlessly, turned away at the piano. Her white fingers, laid out on her skirt front, occasionally twitched, showing that she knew he was there but was pretending not to.
Ch'unsŏng, feeling awkward and inexplicably nervous, took off his shoes at the edge of the *maru* and entered the room. The room was exceedingly quiet. It was as silent as if a patient near death lay there; Yŏngsuk, dressed in a white ramie *chŏgori* (jacket) and white skirt, sat there without sorrow.
Ch'unsŏng stood behind Yŏngsuk.
"Ehem,"
He cleared his throat softly, but she didn't even pretend to look back.<22>
"Miss Yŏngsuk!"
His voice, struggling to come out, was quieter than he intended and trembled slightly. Only then, like someone woken from sleep, Yŏngsuk turned only her head slightly to look at Ch'unsŏng.
"Oh my, when did you arrive?"
Ch'unsŏng was dumbfounded. And he thought inwardly, "What do you mean, 'Oh my, when did you arrive!' Then who was it that told me to come in?"
Until Ch'unsŏng came near the chair where she sat, Yŏngsuk's face was full of sorrow, but then, as if realizing something, she lifted her head to look at Ch'unsŏng.
"Mr. Ch'unsŏng!"
she said. Ch'unsŏng replied,
"Yes,"
watching Yŏngsuk's white, smooth arm as her white fingers tapped the piano beside her a couple of times. Yŏngsuk turned her head again to gaze at the distant mountains, then the hand holding a handkerchief went up to her face. Jewel-like tears welled up between her dark, dusky eyelashes.
Ch'unsŏng was inwardly shocked to see this. And for some reason, as if infected by Yŏngsuk's sorrow, he felt tears welling up in his own eyes. Yŏngsuk, after a long pause, said again,
"Mr. Ch'unsŏng!"
When she looked up at Ch'unsŏng, tears streamed down her cheeks like a child's. And her voice was thick with a pleading emotion.
"Yes,"
Ch'unsŏng too felt an inexplicable sadness, like tasting salty tears on his lips, threatening to make him cry.<23>
"I am truly a lonely person,"
she said, then collapsed onto the keyboard, sobbing with emotion.
A feeling of sympathy and pity arose unconditionally in Ch'unsŏng, seeming to pull him into its midst.
"I have no one to trust, and no one to rely on."
Ch'unsŏng wanted to hug Yŏngsuk's heaving back and cry with her.
"Why do you say such things!"
Yŏngsuk, as if sulking, said,
"There isn't a single person in this world. There's no one to cry with me in my sorrow, and no one to sympathize even a little with my pain."
Ch'unsŏng felt resentful that she didn't understand him on one hand, and on the other hand, he wanted to explain and plead that he loved Yŏngsuk enough to sacrifice his entire life.
Yŏngsuk repeated,
"Not a single person. Not a single person. There isn't a single person in this world."
Ch'unsŏng said,
"Miss Yŏngsuk!"
moving closer as if to lift Yŏngsuk up.
"Why do you say such things? Miss Yŏngsuk, do you truly not think of me?"
At that, Yŏngsuk jerked her head up, looked at Ch'unsŏng, who was gazing at her with tear-filled eyes, for a long moment, then turned her head away. An unbearable smile appeared on her tear-stained face, then she stopped crying and, with a cold expression, asked,
"Mr. Ch'unsŏng, why are you crying?"<24>
Ch'unsŏng, trying to stop his trembling lips as tears came, bit his lower lip between his white teeth for a moment, then said,
"Miss Yŏngsuk, you told me I have no tears, didn't you?"
"     "
"It wasn't that I didn't know I had no tears. But today, for the first time, I realized I do have tears. Only today."
Seeing his dark, thick lips and hairy face contort, Yŏngsuk suddenly felt disgusted. His crying face seemed cowardly and weak like a mountain spirit's. And Ch'unsŏng's thick, black hands, like a snake's skin, poised as if to grab her wrist, felt creepy and unpleasant. So she quickly moved her body away.
"Even if something sad happens, I consider it my misfortune that there isn't a single person I'd wish to cry together with. But why were you crying, Mr. Ch'unsŏng? Yes?"
Ch'unsŏng hesitated for a long time, seemingly at a loss for words.
"I don't know that either."
Yŏngsuk, smiling shamelessly with tear-filled eyes as if looking at a fool, said,
"Yes? You don't know? What does that mean? How can you not know why you cried?"
Ch'unsŏng stood blankly, seemingly having nothing to say.
"Then, Miss Yŏngsuk, why did you cry?"
Yŏngsuk's tears had already dried. Adjusting her appearance, she said,
"Me crying?"
Smoothing down the ties of her *chŏgori* (저고리 끈),<25>
"I cried because I had a reason to cry."
"What reason was there?"
After a moment, Yŏngsuk took out a telegram from the desk drawer (冊床 舌盒).
"This is it,"
she said, handing it to Ch'unsŏng. Ch'unsŏng took it. When he looked at it, it was a telegram (通訃電報) announcing that Yŏngsuk's father had passed away in Shanghai (上海).
Ch'unsŏng threw the telegram back towards Yŏngsuk as if to strike her with it.
And inwardly, he felt foolish for having cried all this time, and Yŏngsuk's actions seemed hateful and infuriating. Thinking about how he had been deceived, he wanted to tear out her eyes and mouth right then and there, or maybe slap her.
And he felt foolishly ashamed that he had cried in response to the sorrow of a weak-hearted woman like Yŏngsuk, who, suddenly facing grief she had never experienced, was heartbroken and overflowing with sadness, finding no place to vent it, burning up inside alone, and upon meeting him—even if she disliked him—had expressed her sorrow, if not through words, then through tears, making his face flush red.
Ch'unsŏng laughed inwardly, choked with emotion, but the laughter was bitter like swallowing bile.
"Then the letter you sent me, was it sent in earnest?"
Yŏngsuk nodded her head slightly, staring blankly at Ch'unsŏng.
"I don't know how to mock others,"
she said, pulling open a clothing chest drawer (의거리 설합).
"Here, take them. Take them all,"
she threw out all of Ch'unsŏng's photos and letters.<26>

Ch'unsŏng left Yŏngsuk's house as if he had been kicked out. Walking alone, his face flushed red, then blue, then yellow, and he clenched and unclenched his fists.
Feeling ashamed, he couldn't lift his head properly and just stared at the ground as he walked.
"Ah well, let's just drink!"
He put his hand in his pocket.
"Let's see. How much money do I have."
As his hand came out of his pocket, a wad of paper money (지젼 뭉치) came out with it.
"This should be enough for the whole night!"
He entered a certain restaurant (料理집). Then he called a friend. Let's call this friend A. He was short with a somewhat round face. And when he occasionally smiled, only his mouth would curve slightly.
Ch'unsŏng sat leaning against a cushion (보료).
"Hey, have you ever felt so indignant!"
He looked across at A. A flicked his cigarette ash into the ashtray.
"About what?"
He lay down too.
"Really, all women are like that."
"How so?"
"Didn't I get a letter from Yŏngsuk?"
"Yeah."
"Guess what the letter said."<27>
"That's a good story."
"That I have no tears."
"So you mean she can't love you?"
Again, A smiled, his mouth twitching.
"Right. Then you should have gone to her and cried your eyes out."
"Just listen to me. It's embarrassing and shameful to talk about."
"What is? Is there anything you can't say in front of me?"
"So, three days later, today, didn't I go to her place?"
"And what did she say when you went?"
"It's heartbreaking to tell."
"What's there to be heartbroken about?"
"When I went, she looked at me and started crying."
A, his eyes full of curiosity, listened to Ch'unsŏng's words, then adjusted his pillow.
"Crying?"
"Yeah, and seeing her cry, somehow tears started welling up in my eyes too."
"So, did you cry?"
"I cried."
"Then Yŏngsuk will love you now, right?"
"Don't talk about it. It's too embarrassing."
"What's embarrassing again?"
Just then, a *gisaeng* named Ch'aok and another named Sŏrhyang entered. After greetings were finished, A claimed Sŏrhyang, who was young,<28> had a roundish face, and whose eyes formed double eyelids whenever she smiled.
However, Ch'aok, known for being good at singing, dancing, calligraphy, conversation, and having a kind heart, somehow didn't appeal to Ch'unsŏng.
So he sat a little distance away.
"It's been a while."
Although he greeted her, he inwardly resented A for calling that *gisaeng* and was jealous that A had taken Sŏrhyang. He also disliked that A, who seemed fond of Sŏrhyang, had called Ch'aok, whom Sŏrhyang seemed fond of. But there was nothing he could do but swallow his feelings.
A, although meeting Sŏrhyang daily, acted as if he hadn't had enough, whispering secrets in her ear, laughing together, and pinching each other playfully.
Ch'aok, perhaps because she was getting older and had endured all sorts of hardships, showed signs of age on her face and an undisguisable sorrow.
Ch'unsŏng found it unpleasant to watch A, who should have realized the weariness and utter dryness of the *gisaeng* world (花柳界) by now, acting so undignifiedly familiar with Sŏrhyang. So he deliberately called Sŏrhyang, who was sitting close to A, and pulled her hand.
"Come here!"
At that moment, Ch'aok's eyes showed a flash of surprise and jealousy before it disappeared.
"Why are you doing this?"
Sŏrhyang, also reluctant to go to Ch'unsŏng, struggled and tried to pull her hand away.
"I said come here!"
Ch'unsŏng forcefully pulled Sŏrhyang, glancing sideways at A, who, though unwilling to let Sŏrhyang go from his grasp,<29> could only sit there clicking his tongue.
"Why only sit next to Mr. A? Am I unpleasant to look at?"
"Why would seeing you be unpleasant? I'm practically sick from not seeing you every day. Look, I even got this rash because of it," she said, smiling sweetly and showing a small boil-like rash on her wrist, then, as if it hurt terribly,
"Sss-"
She grimaced. To Ch'unsŏng's eyes, it seemed incredibly deceitful and hateful.
Ch'aok hesitated, then went to sit alone in a corner. Still, she kept a close watch on Ch'unsŏng and Sŏrhyang, not letting them out of her sight.
A, seeming very bored after Sŏrhyang was taken, just smoked his innocent cigarette, then said,
"Alright, let's finish the story we were telling."
He was thinking only of how to separate Ch'unsŏng and Sŏrhyang, even just a little.
"Right, let's finish the story! But it's embarrassing! *Gisaengs*, cover your ears. Don't listen!"
Sŏrhyang laughed freshly.
"What story is it that we need to cover our ears?"
She rushed towards him as if lamenting she didn't have four ears instead of two. Ch'aok, who seemed to understand the gravity of the words, covered her ears even if just for show, smiling faintly.
"Haha. You really covered them. Crazy girl,"
Sŏrhyang teased Ch'aok. But Ch'aok, as if it wasn't something she didn't know herself, took her hands away from her ears.
"You told me to cover them, so I did,"
she said, gathering her skirt and sitting down again.<30>
Ch'unsŏng continued the story he was telling earlier.
"So, wasn't Yŏngsuk crying! Seeing her cry..."
Ch'aok, looking pitiful, asked,
"Who cried? Why?"
looking up at Ch'unsŏng. But Ch'unsŏng, finding Ch'aok's lips unpleasant to look at, turned his head away and didn't answer her, saying instead,
"Somehow, I couldn't stop my own tears."
Sŏrhyang glanced sideways at the snubbed Ch'aok as if sympathizing.
A pulled Sŏrhyang's hand again.
"Right,"
He was only half-listening, his mind preoccupied with figuring out how to pull Sŏrhyang back to his side.
"So I cried for a while. Then, after a bit, Yŏngsuk lifted her head, looked at me, and asked why I was crying."
"And then?"
"So I said I didn't know either. And then when I asked Yŏngsuk the reason for her crying, oh, it's unbelievable! Look at this! Didn't she hand me a telegram announcing her father in Shanghai had died..."
A laughed louder than before. Just then, the table was brought in.
"Hey, the table's too early! Anyway, let's eat first."
He picked up a cup. Ch'aok poured Ch'unsŏng's drink, looking up at him as if offering affection and smiling faintly. Ch'unsŏng felt disgusted enough to throw the cup down.
Sŏrhyang poured A's drink.<31>
When they were half-drunk, the *gisaengs* brought out a drum (*janggo*) to sing. Ch'aok stuck close to Ch'unsŏng. And frequently, as if unable to bear not touching him, her hand often brushed against Ch'unsŏng's body, and she even offered her lap as a pillow without being asked.
However, as Ch'unsŏng drank more, Sŏrhyang faded from his sight, and Ch'aok became not just invisible but hideously repulsive, provoking strong aversion. The humiliation he suffered earlier from Yŏngsuk felt unbearably infuriating.
Ch'unsŏng drank more, threw off his outer jacket, and declared,
"Anyway, women are deceitful creatures..."
Sŏrhyang, straining her cute voice, asked,
"How so?"
darting forward as if to challenge him. Ch'aok, still composed, said,
"That can't be true. Not all are like that."
Drunk Ch'unsŏng, in his drunken state, put his arm around Ch'aok's waist.
"Really? Is that so?"
He looked at her affectionately. Ch'aok initially tried to pull away but then remained still.
"There's no reason for Mr. Ch'unsŏng to think like that."
"Why not?"
"Because there's a woman who loves Mr. Ch'unsŏng."
A heart-pounding curiosity filled Ch'unsŏng, offering him some kind of luck and expectation.
"Who is it..."
In Ch'unsŏng's mind, a beautiful and demure woman appeared—in other words, his ideal beauty.<32>
"It could be anyone."
"Who is it? Tell me. Don't just tease me."
"There is such a person."
"Damn it. Who is it, I say!"
Ch'aok took Ch'unsŏng's hand, stood up, and said,
"Come this way."
She led him to a deserted spot, embraced Ch'unsŏng's chest, and said,
"It's this Ch'aok I'm talking about."
She lowered her head shyly, tightly gripping Ch'unsŏng's hand.
Ch'unsŏng didn't want to reject her. He accepted it with much gratitude. And Ch'aok, blurred in his drunken eyes, didn't seem as hateful as before.
"Really!"
"Of course. It's true."
Ch'unsŏng laughed as if choked with emotion.
"Am I the most suitable and satisfying person for Ch'aok's heart?"
Ch'aok paused for a long time, then said,
"Is anyone ever truly satisfied in this world?"
"Then!"
"Until now, I have looked for many people I found satisfying, and I have tried to receive the love of such a person, but for some reason, such people do not give me their love."
"So you're saying I'm not a satisfying person?"
Ch'aok said,
"Yes, yes, even if not completely satisfying, you seem the best fit as my lover."
"The best..."
Ch'unsŏng stood blankly, speechless, his mouth still open from the unfinished sentence.<33>

**Summary**

This story follows Ch'unsŏng, a man reeling from a confusing rejection letter from his beloved Yŏngsuk, who claims she cannot love him because he lacks tears. Drunk and upset, he confronts his servant and mother. Later, he attempts reconciliation with Yŏngsuk, finding her crying. Misinterpreting her tears as empathy for his own (newly discovered) capacity for sorrow, he is shocked and humiliated to learn she is grieving her father's death, not moved by him. Feeling mocked, he seeks solace in a gisaeng house with a friend. There, disillusioned and drunk, he interacts with two gisaeng, Sŏrhyang and Ch'aok. Ch'aok eventually confesses her affection for him, calling him the "best fit" though perhaps not perfectly satisfying, leaving Ch'unsŏng speechless.

The story offers a glimpse into modernizing Korean society in the 1920s, juxtaposing elements like letter writing, electricity, and seemingly more independent female characters (Yŏngsuk meeting Ch'unsŏng alone) with traditional settings like the gisaeng house and the presence of servants. For historians, it illuminates male emotional vulnerability and confusion regarding changing relationship dynamics, the persistence of traditional entertainment venues like *yōrijip* (fancy restaurants often with gisaeng), and perhaps anxieties about sincerity and communication in relationships during a period of social transition under colonial rule. The portrayal of Yŏngsuk's complex behaviour and Ch'aok's pragmatic view of affection could also be points of interest regarding female agency or coping mechanisms within the constraints of the time.

기사형태	소설

春星
						稻香
『銀珠야! 얘 銀珠야!』
春星은 自己 집에 들어스며 댑바람 게집종을 불은다. 부억에서 행주로 그릇을 씻든 銀珠는 부억 창살 틈으로 드러오는 春星을 바라 보더니 다시 본 체 만 체하고
『녜』 대답을 하고 아모 말이 업다.
春星의 식검은 얼골에는 醉한 술기운이 올나 와서 익히다 남긴 肝뗑이 가티 겁불근데다. 털만흔 얼골을 맵시 내느라고 날마다 하는 면도 독이 십푸르 등등하게 들엇다.
그는 다시 마루로 올너가서 건는 방마다 지를 열어 젓치드니
『은주야!』하고 목청 질너 한번 부르고서 답답한 칼라를 뜨더 던지고서는
『이 게집애가 귀가 먹엇나? 에그 이게 무엇이냐? 방이 이게 무엇이냐! 이게 돼지우리간이지 어대 사람 사는 방이냐? 얘 은주야! 은주야! 얘 목 압허! 은주야!』
일부러 대답을 안 하든 銀珠도 넘어 떠드는 바람에 송구한 생각이 낫든지
『왜 그러세요!』 하고서 발을 동동 굴을 듯이 부억에서 튀여 나온다.
『왜 그러세요가 무엇야! 너 오늘 종일 한 것이 무엇이냐? 왜 방 좀 치여 노라니까 안 치엇서? 비짜루는 두엇다가 군불이나 때련! 그러고 너 하로종일 하는 것이 무엇이냐? 흥 너 요새 큰 일 낫더라 큰 일 낫서!』
銀珠는 입을 쫑긋 쫑긋하면서 눈쌀을 얄미웁게 찝흐리고＜15＞
『오늘 자근댁에 심부름 갓셧서요』 하고서는 행자치마 속에다 두 손을 느엇다 끄내여 입 속으로 남에게 들리지 안을 만치 무엇이라 종알종알 한다.
『심부름? 무슨 심부름을 가서 하로 종일 잇서?』
銀珠는 아모 말이 업다. 뒤꼇헤 잇든 春星의 어미가 마루 뒤문에 나타나며
『또 쌈한다. 오늘은 종일 어대를 갓섯니? 또 술 먹엇고나? 그저 그러케 일너도 듯지를 안어.  얘 어서 너는 상이나 보아라! 응?』 하며 다시 銀珠를 흘겨 본다.
春星은 그래도 무엇이 未盡한 듯이
『똑 나가면 들어올 줄을 몰나! 야단낫서! 야단!』 하며 두 볼이 퉁퉁 부어서 방으로 들어가랴 할 때 銀珠는
『무엇이 야단 낫서요. 야단 난 것이 무엇예요?』 하면서 포달을 부릴 듯이 독살 마진 눈으로 春星을 처어다 본다.
어머니는 무슨 생각을 하는지 뒤를 도라도 보고 멀거니 섯다가 銀珠의 목소리를 듯고서 쥐여박을 듯이 주먹질을 하면서
『요 배라먹을 것 주둥이를 좀 담으러!』
하더니 다시 자긔 아들에게는
『글세 이 애야! 제발 좀 들어가서 자든지 해라. 왜 그럿케 점잔치가 못하냐! 엥』
하며 입맛을 다시나 春星은 들엇는지 말엇는지
『냉수나 한 그릇 가저 오너라! 어서!』
하고서는 방으로 들어가서 방바닥에 헛트러진 조희 조각 몬지 북덱이를 주섬주섬 치어 노터니 그대로 아래목에 가 쓸어젓다. 그러고서는 눈을 뜬 체 천정을 치어다 보앗다.
공중에서는 무지개 한 끗을 자긔 손가락에 홰홰 감감 삥삥 내둘느는 모양으로 정신이 팽팽 내둘려 제＜16＞ 몸둥아리가 그리로 몰려 들어가는 것 갓다. 그리고는 때때로 배탄 것 모양이 몸뚱이가 땅 속으로 쑥 들어가는 것 갓고 또는 놉흔데서 떨어지는 것 가티 앗질 하기도 한데 四肢와 몸뚱이가 척 구들장 우에 치밧처 잇는 듯하고 피곤한 사람이 축축한 따 우에 그대로 잡바진 것 가치 편안하다.
그는 후- 하고 한숨을 수엿다. 방 안에 잇는 것이 輪廓만 朦濃한 안개 속으로 보이는 듯하고 부질 업시 읏슥 옷슥 우줄 우줄 넘치는 깃븜이 억개 츔을 추게 한다. 그는 혼자
「그러치 그! 래!』 하며 껄껄 우섯다.
『술 먹으면 웬일인지 일상 조터라! 이런 때 英淑이나 잇섯스면 오즉 조흘나구. 떡 술이 취해서 들어오면 반가워 마지면서 옷도 벗겨 주고 자리도 깔어 줄터이지. 그러고는 「어대서 약주을 이러케 자셋서요? 저는 약주 잡수시면 슬여요. 에그 보기 슬혀!」 하고서 살작 돌아 안지면 허허 그러치 나는 슬그머니 타일느느라고 「인제는 안 먹을게 응! 이리와!」 하고서 잡어 다니면 슬혀요!」 하고 톡 쏘는 얼골에는 참지 못하는 우슴을 가리느라고 더욱 고개를 돌니렷다. 그러면 나는 거짓 항복을 하여 가면서 「글세 안 먹는 다닛가 안 먹요 안 먹어!」 그러면 英淑은 일부러 한참이나 잇다가 「무얼 안 먹어? 제 버릇 개도 안 준다고 한 번 밴 버릇을 고칠 수 잇나!」 하고 가만히 안젓스면 나는 슬그머니 그의 겨드랑이를 간지려 보자 그러면 깜작 놀내여 돌아 안지며 「왜 이래요」하면서 참엇든 우슴이 툭 터지렷다. 그러면 나는 그의 두 팔을 꽉 붓잡고서 「이리 와도 이제는 술 안 먹을 터이니!」 그러면 처음에는 팔를 빼랴다가 제비 가티 나의 가슴으로 달려들며 「인제는 약주 잡숫지 마세요 녜녜」하면서 간청을 할 터이지! 에그 그저 고것을. 엇더케 하나! 고만 진저리가 치도록 어엽부거든!』
하고서는 혼자 벙싯벙싯 한다. 그러다가는 다시 웃옷을 벗고서
『그러치. 그래』 하면서 의거리에다 옷을 느코서 다시 책상 압흘 지내랴다가 冊床 우에 잇는 편지를 보앗다.＜17＞
『편지!』
의아한 듯이 한참이나 들여다 보다가 한 손으로 집어 파봉을 보고서는
『英淑이가 편지를 햇다』
하고 이리 뒤치고 저리 뒤집어 그 필적을 상고한 후에 고개를 갸웃하고서
『참 이것은 의왼걸. 이제는 버릇 한 가지가 늘엇다. 당초에 하지 안튼 편지하는 버릇이 느럿서?』
하고서는 전기불을 켜고서 다시 아래목 보려 우에 가 누면서
『어듸 보자!』
하고서 求婚狀을 바든 新郞처럼 반갑고 깃거운 눈으로 그것을 뜨덧다. 그러다가는
『안이지 안야. 천천히 볼 일야! 넘어 반가운 것을 그리케 얼핏 지내 처 버려서는 濫用이야 濫用!』
하면서 그것을 冊床 한 구퉁이에 다가 다시 노코 한참이나 잇다가
『무엇이라고 썻슬고?』
하고서 다시 冊床 우에 잇는 편지를 들더니
『인제는 고만 보자! 얌전하게도 썻다』
하고서 흡죡히 역이는 듯이 칭찬을 한번 하더니 편지 피봉을 뜨덧다.
그러고는
『그럿치 春星氏! 그러렷다』
하고서는 또 무엇이라 두어줄 읽더니 두 눈이 우아래로 올너갓다 나려갓다 한다. 그러다가는 두 눈이 놀라 죽은 눈처럼 멀건해 지면서
『무엇야? 눈물이 업슴니다. 눈물이 업스닛가 사랑하여 주실만치 따거운 정이 업겟서요...? 더구나 눈물아 업스세요...』
春星은 편지를 한 손에다 웅켜 쥐며＜18＞
『무엇야? 눈물이 업스닛가 사랑해 줄 만치 따거운 정이 업다고?』
하면서 멀거니 잇다가
『엇더튼 게집애들이란 여호야 여호! 실커든 이냥 실소할 것이지 눈물이 업스닛가 사랑할 수가 업소란 무엇야?』
하고서 화가 어대서 새삼스러웁게 낫든지
『앵!』
하고서 손에 쥐엿든 편지를 심술스럽게 북북 찌즈면서
『고만 두어라! 너 안이면 녀자가 업다더냐? 되지 안은 것!』
하고서는 英淑이가 엽헤 잇는 듯이 고개를 돌리며 눈을 딱 감엇다.
그러나 그의 가슴 속에는 분하고도 그래도 섭섭하고 戀戀한 뭉클한 情이 얼키어 잇섯다. 그의 눈 압헤는 다시 英淑이가 보인다. 그리고는 英淑이가 자긔 압흐로 갓가히 갓가히 왓다. 그래서 속마음으로 설마 제가 나를 떼여 보냇슬나고? 아마 내가 눈물이 업스닛가 나의 눈물을 한 번 보고 십허서 일부러 그런게지! 그러치 하고서 갓가히 오는 英淑을 타일으로 妥協을 하랴는 듯이 빙그레 하면서 바라보고 잇스랴닛가 英淑은 자긔에로 아조 갓가히 와서 놀려 먹는 듯이 허리를 굽실하고 한 손으로 사때질을 할 뜻이 손을 들더니 지내간다. 그러고는 그대로 지내 갓스면 오히려 조흐련마는 저만침 가다가 억개 넘어로 도라다 보며 비옷는 듯이 쌩긋 우슬 때 그는 주먹으로 방바닥을 치며 눈을 번쩍뜨닛가 暗黑을 한 번에 깨틀인 듯이 電氣불이 환하다.
『에 무정한 년!』
그는 혼자 『눈물이 업스닛가?』 하다가 멀거니 천정을 다시 치어다 보고 잇섯다.

여덜시나 되엿다. 銀珠는 저녁상을 가지고 것는 방에를 들어왓다.＜19＞
『진지 잡수세요.』
『OOO』
春星은 대답이 업고 방안은 조용하다.
『상 가저 왓세요.』
그러나 春星은 아래믁에 누어서 잠이 들엇다.
