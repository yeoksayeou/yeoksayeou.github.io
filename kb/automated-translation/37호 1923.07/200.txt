In the Land at Sunset
						露雀
In search of that person,
In the land where the sun has set,
On the great street, I went forth,
But by the deceit of a passing traveler,
Common, cheaply sold, worn-out sorrow,
I foolishly bought like this.

Stirring up old feelings,
A sigh that dries the heart,
What use is it to blame all things?
The hem of a stained skirt listlessly trails,
A faded shadow, a collapsed spring dream,
Mixed with the song of a crazed woman,
That day, heart-aching May eleventh.<74>

Spring, oh wordless spring,
The departing spring, without notice,
That spring when flowers bloomed, without notice,
They said to come when the azaleas bloom, but
Whether spring, or love, or the heart,
Are different from people, together,
Before this body could go with love,
That returning spring, without notice...

Truth and tears,
Whose words were they?
Even now, I am this sorrowful,
Back then, that person's heart-wrenching tears,
How... red must they have been?
The hill endlessly turned back upon,
The distant field where long sighs were breathed,
Soaked in tears,
Only weeds have sprouted and grown thick,
The mountain bird coming and going from this peak to that,
Heartbreakingly cries "뻐뻑국" (Cuckoo).
That person cleanly wiped and left <75>
The mirror (어루쇠) of my heart is rusted,
Whether joyful or sad, the face it reflected,
Can no longer be seen, not even a shadow,
Ah- that day,
My sickly life,
Heart-aching May eleventh.
I am still searching for that person,
Wandering in this dark land.

To Mother
Mother!
How is it that
I have become so reserved?
Unable to cling again to mother's nipple,
How have I become so reserved like this?
That is what I lament,
This child.

Mother!
How is it that
I cannot become the child of ten years ago? <76>
Hearing mother's scolding, I cannot become the child of ten years ago again.
And why do you not scold me anymore now?
That makes me sad,
This child.

Mother!
How is it that
You nurtured the young one, only wishing for growth?
Did you not know that as the slender bones grew thicker, greed and deceit grew too?
Did you not know that holy love would be cheaply forsaken?
That is felt now,
By this child.

Mother!
How is it that
To me asking for rice cake, you gave white *muritteok*?
Thinking only that I would grow without a speck of dust, did you give only white *muritteok* on my birthday?
Now I have become too lazy even to shed my stained clothes.
That is painfully regretted.
This child.

When Drawing That Person’s Portrait <77>

That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
Crying eyes, smiling lips,
Red cheeks, blue eyebrows,
Gathering all the beauty of this world,
I intend to draw it lovely, lovely.
That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
This body and mind were weary,
Like that person's beautiful face,
I tried to boast of this heart's purity too.

That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
That person's picture and this soul of mine,
Scatter powerlessly on the dark room floor,
Rolling here and there without an owner.
That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
Drawing with affliction, that person's face,
Was drawn most beautifully. <78>
The more this body struggles and suffers,
The lovelier the face in the picture became.
That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
The more beautifully that person's face appears,
One thing I absolutely cannot draw,
Hidden away so as not to be seen,
Is that person's breast and red heart.

That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
Peeking at the unknowable person to draw,
But what appears before my eyes,
Was always just the lovely face.
That person's face, unliving in this heart,
Unable to forget, when drawing it as a picture,
Yet only this body became pitiful,
What cannot be drawn is the girl's inner thoughts,
What cannot be drawn is that person's heart.
Clutching the undrawable picture,
I can only weep like this. <79>
<74-79>

**Summary**

The text consists of three poems by Nojak (露雀). "In the Land at Sunset" expresses a deep sense of loss and sorrow, depicting a search for a beloved ("that person") in a metaphorical "dark land," haunted by memories of a specific painful day (May 11th) and feelings of disillusionment. "To Mother" is a lament about the loss of childhood innocence and the burdens of adulthood, expressing regret for having grown up and perhaps disappointing the mother's pure love. "When Drawing That Person’s Portrait" describes the painful attempt to capture the image of the unforgettable beloved, finding that while the external beauty can be rendered, even enhanced by the artist's suffering, the inner heart and mind remain inaccessible and undrawable. From a historical perspective, the poems, particularly "In the Land at Sunset," offer potential allegorical readings relevant to colonial Korea. The "land where the sun has set," the pervasive sorrow, the tarnished mirror, and the futile search could symbolize the loss of national sovereignty and the bleak atmosphere of the era. The themes of lost innocence and disillusionment in "To Mother" might resonate with the experiences of a generation growing up under colonial rule.
