Promeneade Sentinental
						Kim Kijin (金基鎭)
The longed-for spring of Seoul has vanished without a trace. The spring that deepened step by step over the pasqueflowers, forsythias, and pussy willows seems to have passed quietly, trampling flower petals onto the earth beneath the fence.
When this body was far away, how much Seoul was the target of yearning, and moreover, how much I missed the mountains and rivers of my hometown these days. The desire to see the spring of Seoul grasped this body and made me tread the soil of Seoul.
But where has Seoul's spring gone? Seoul's spring has vanished without a trace. On the hillside slope, violets remain alone, heads bowed as if withered. Azaleas and apricot blossoms have fallen, and peonies and rhododendrons bloom profusely in laughter...

Now is the time when Seoul is quickening its steps into the scorching summer. The burning sun will likely pull Seoul closer to the position of the equator. It will seize this body, which rushed here to see Seoul's spring, and throw it into a "pot" (독안이) of steaming heat. Seoul, a city of chaos, arrogance, and deep sleep, is being dyed day by day with the fragrance of summer.

Chaos, arrogance, and deep sleep of Chosŏn - Seoul, Seoul of the same meaning! - I missed it. I wanted to see Chosŏn. I wanted to see the piece of land that bore me, the piece of land that raised me.
Ah, where has Seoul gone-. <82>
Seoul is this place. This place, pressed down at the nape by Pukak Mountain and supported at the chin by the Han River, is Seoul. Beneath the 4- or 5-story buildings lined up one by one, this is the place where people begging, "A penny, a penny," are wringing their necks—this is Seoul.
They say this is Seoul, where people, clutching tightly in grimy fists, twisting their necks and having their faces trampled, shriek in the crowing voice of a hen, "It's art!" "It's literature!" "We cannot live without art!" This is Seoul, where the middle class, promoting cooperation and whatnot, raising the poisoned, sickly face of established culture while plotting for their own class interests, is active. They say this is Seoul, where groups of Japanese radishes (日本무[赤大根]), who have made their life's policy selling and idling while holding the lanterns of Soviet Russia, gather and wander.
But I wanted to see Seoul. I longed for Seoul. Where is Seoul? Which place is Seoul?
Seoul is here. They say this place is Seoul.

The Seoul spring, where the dream of chaos, arrogance, and deep sleep was cozy, has passed. Seoul's spring has passed.
Where has the spring of chaos, arrogance, and deep sleep gone? Where has spring gone, leaving thick leaves full on the branches? The burning rays of the sun pierce down onto the cozy dream-spot.
Where the sun's rays reach, the goblins (독가비) disappear. Ghosts inevitably vanish in the hot sunlight.
Let what will disappear, disappear. Let what will vanish, vanish. Let all of Chosŏn become a ball of fire, scorched hot. Let's chase the goblins into their graves.

How many goblins come and go. How many goblins squirm. How many goblins mumble.
Goblins occupy even the hovels. Goblins occupy the schools too. Goblins occupy the large brick houses, and goblins occupy the main streets too. <83>
Everything, everything plays in the dance of the goblins. The lower body is an unseen, shabby goblin. Goblins romp even on the printed type, goblins romp even on the paper.
Seoul is the world of goblins. Chosŏn is the world of goblins.
But I wanted to see Seoul. I longed for Chosŏn.
Which place is Chosŏn? Which place is Seoul?
Chosŏn is this place. They say Seoul is here.

No-. Still, my dear and precious compatriots are dozing in a terrifying sleep!

Whether in the dead of winter or mid-summer under the dog days of the fifth or sixth month, they stand dozing,

The people sleeping like corpses are digging the earth while dozing,

Everything is dozing! The one hitting dozes, and the one being hit dozes too!

Holding fast to the tavern's cup,
Pillowing its head on the North Pole and stretching its feet to the Caucasus (코-카사쓰),

What was Russia like in the 1860s (1867年代)? Turgenev's novel tells this.

Even one person is fine. If only one person among us who is thinking about "truth" (졍말) would emerge, just one person is fine.
What are people searching for? Do you know what Mr. Kim next door or Mr. Yi next door is searching for? Everyone wants a "conclusion" (結論). <85>
Everyone is thinking about the conclusion.
In the desolate virgin land, isn't clearing the land itself the very first task to undertake, rather than sowing seeds? But what you seek and desire is only the "conclusion."

What is necessary for the greatest number (最大多數)? Is it art? Is it literature?
No. That is not necessary. What is necessary for them is to reform the roots of art and literature from the foundation up. The urgent task is to fundamentally reform the structure of life (生活組織) from the lowest layer. Things like art and literature will emerge on their own later, even if told not to. They will spring forth like a fountain.
If literature arises from the consciousness of life, it cannot happen without relying on the reform of the social structure that determines the consciousness of life. What becomes of just mouthing words like revolution in literature, or the 'whatever-ization' of life consciousness? Nothing. It doesn't work. We are not seeing just one or two examples of people who cried out only with words, only with spirit. How much reform did those people bring about upon their own spirits, and how much effect did they manifest in their own creations?
It's a lie. That is a lie. Discard the shallow spiritualism. Our life consciousness is not determined by what is in our spirit. The state of life (生活狀態) determines our life consciousness for us.
How much have writers and artists of all ages clamored about transcending the times (時代)? More than one or two have struggled to shed their national character (民族性). But now, what are we seeing? What can be done about them floundering within a few pages of literary history, unable to break free?

Transcending reality is a lie. Forgetting national character is a lie. English literature is studied down through the ages as English literature, and Russian literature as Russian literature.
Everything is contained within it. <86>
19th-century literature is 19th-century literature, 18th-century literature is 18th-century literature. Medieval literature could not escape the Middle Ages, and 19th-century literature could not leave or escape the background of the 19th century. Everything squirms within a small background. Baudelaire (뽀드레-르) according to Baudelaire's thought, Maupassant (모파싼) or Flaubert (프로베르) also according to their thoughts, revolve and orbit within the background of the 19th century.
What draws our interest (興味) to Baudelaire is the question of what the state of life was like then, and what Baudelaire's environment (環境) was like, that such thoughts originated—that is the focus (焦點) of the issue. The social structure of that time, in other words, his environment, determined such a life consciousness.

Contemporary literature stands on the materialist conception of history (唯物史觀).
Even if there are some misdiagnoses (誤診) in the materialist conception of history, we cannot deny (否認) the truth (眞理) of the materialist conception of history.
How far apart, really, could the work of Marx (맑쓰) or Engels (엥겔쓰) be from that of Turgenev (투루게네-푸) or Dostoevsky (또스토이예푸스키-)?

Now is the time when things are flowing down. The spring already burst forth long ago, and now is the time when that water flows gently but irresistibly downstream. Do you think this water cannot go where it intends to flow down, no matter how many layers of dikes (防川) are built? Everything can only be clearly understood after it has passed.

The saying that literature is the symbol (象徵) of anguish (苦悶) is something known even without needing to borrow the mouth of Kuchikawa Hakuson (廚川白村).
If so, if it is the symbol of anguish, is there anything that symbolizes our anguish in our writing, whether well done or poorly done? If we were to say there isn't, then what, indeed, do the various phenomena (現象) visible before our eyes right now symbolize?
No. Everything is a lie. Everything is a lie.
It is a clear fact (明白한 事實) that artists, regardless of the era, have been the pioneers (先驅) of their time. Even if there are a few dissenters (少數의 反對者), <87> what can be done about the fact that artists are the pioneers of the age?
But is there anything in Chosŏn that could be a pioneer, anything that hints at the era ahead?
It's a lie. It's a lie! Our companions have been telling lies all along. Those called artists have deceived (欺瞞) their own emotions (感情). And then they created the iron chain (쇠사실) saying, "This much art is enough (足하다)."
Who narrowed the world to be possessed by art, who tightened the intension (인텐손) of the proposition (命題) "Art"? Is it not you writers who clamor about literature and sigh about art? Is it not you who lock art up in a faded tower (塔) and sing only songs of escape (逃避)?

That it has come to this is not unreasonable (無理). In a place like present-day Chosŏn, where the ruling class (支配階級) and the ruled class (被支配階級) are clearly divided (區分), hoping for "art of life's intrinsic demand" (生의 本然한 要求) in its proper meaning (正當한 意味) is hopeless (可望도 업는 일). Why is that? Because the life structure (生活組織) of present-day Koreans determines our life consciousness in this way.
Under such a class system (階級制度), there is no literature. There is no art. If there were, it would be only letters (文字), only a shell (껍데기). Where can one find letters that can edify the spirit (靈性)? Where can one obtain the "power" (힘) called "life" (生命)? All that is visible is a bare tower on a desolate plain, listless laughter (無氣力한 哄笑), and skeletons (骸骨).

If artists are more sensitive (敏感), have more receptivity (感受性), and more public indignation (公憤) than ordinary people (普通사람들), then you should rise up holding both a brush (붓) and a hoe (괭이). There should be a single connecting flow (一脈의 通流) between the sentiments (心情) of the greatest number and the sentiments of you artists, and there should be a blended symphony (混然한 교향악) between the spirit of the greatest number and your spirit.
However, overestimating (過信) the greatest number leads to failure (失敗). We do not need to follow exactly the historical (歷史) footsteps that Russia trod. <88>

Then what do we demand (要求)? Ease (安逸)? Solitary pleasure (獨樂)? What is it?
Can we live believing in an unseen, dream-like world, a fragrance-like world, the world called the future (未來)? Until when can we live with longing (憧憬) for an unseen world? What was Oscar Wilde (오스카 와일드), who pointed to the beautification (美化) of formal life (形式的 生活), thinking after coming out of prison (獄中) until he died as a foreign resident (客死) in Paris (巴里)? We gain great lessons (多大한 敎訓) from the end (末路) of the nihilistic aesthete (虛無한 唯美主義者).
How long can things like the future or the world of the soul (靈魂) truly endure in a person's head? It is written in his biography (傳記) that Wilde, in his later years, said he would rely on practical means (實際的 手段) and told his friend (親舊) that life itself was now more important (重大) than art itself.
Then again, what do we demand? Ease? Solitary pleasure? What is it?
It is truth (眞理). It is light (光明). It is "Clarté" (클라르테).

When the revolution occurred in Russia, how many of their poets rushed towards the revolution?
Valery Bryusov (빨레르뿌류-쏘푸), Andrei Bely (안드례,삐엘리-), Alexander Blok (아렉씬드르, 뽈록크), Alexei Tolstoy (아롁쎄이, 톨수토이) - how much thought must these and many other poets have put in before rushing off?
A poet is human. Nothing more than human, nothing less than human. A poet is a complete human. A poet can breathe together with the greatest number, and can merge (融化) the soul of the greatest number with their own soul.
Let's deeply consider the fact that Russia's symbolist poets (象徵詩人) rushed towards the revolution.

There are too many writers (文學家) in Seoul. Too many writers walk around with their hearts (염통) removed. Too many writers walk around with their faces lifted, as if demanding recognition (認證) of their special privileges (特權).
What special privileges do artists have?
There are none. There are none. Artists must share life with the greatest number. Otherwise, what meaning does being a pioneer of the age (時代의 先驅) hold?
Look closely at what the immediate, primary issue (當面의 先着한 問題) is. <93>
The enlightenment (敎化) of the greatest number - this is what is necessary. Literature is by no means a machine (器械), but when urgent, it cannot be helped. What about the responsibility (責任) of the intelligentsia (인테리겐튜어)-? I will extract a few passages from Alexander Blok's (아렉싼드르, 뿔록크) "The Revolution and the Intelligentsia" (革命과 智識階級).

It is not uncommon for it to maim precious things (貴重品) within its net's mesh (그물구비). It may also safely land worthless things onto the riverbank (江언덕). Indeed, we have cut off the tree branch (나무 가지) we were sitting on. A sad state (슬픈 狀態). - Upon the heavy pile of wood soaked by snow or rain, with all the fanaticism (狂熱) towards cruelty (殘忍) - dry wood, wood chips, and rubbish (허접쓰레기) were piled up. And then suddenly, when the flames (불꼿) flare up (like banners and feet) and rise to the sky, they shrink back from the vicinity and cry out - Ah, we are burning!"
- "Arrogant politics (傲慢한 政治) is - a great sin (罪惡). The more arrogant and cruel the intelligentsia becomes, the more terrifying and gloomy (陰散) the surroundings (周圍) will become. This reflective (反射的), dry (乾燥), tasteless (無味), 'dogmatic doctrine' (獨斷的 敎理), seasoned with the spice (양념) of humble spiritualism (謙遜한 精神主義), is <96> terrifying and dangerous (危險). Behind spiritualism, there is blood (피). Why must this path of the spirit (靈性) be obstructed (妨害) by spiritualism? The beautiful is difficult (困難) enough as it is.
The soul is music (音樂). Long ago, the daemon ordered Socrates (소크라테스) to listen to the soul of music.
With all your body, with all your heart, with all your consciousness (意識) - listen to the revolution."
When the red flag (붉은 긔ㅅ발) flew in Russia, Russia's poets ran beneath that flag. A poet's job is not just writing poetry. Nezhdanov (네즈다노푸) in "Virgin Soil" (處女地) even committed suicide because he was a poet. Within the one Nezhdanov, there were two Nezhdanovs. This was because there was Nezhdanov the ideologue (主義者) and Nezhdanov the poet.
What Turgenev teaches is the sorrow (悲哀) of the intelligentsia. Delving deeper, it is Chekhov's (쳭호-푸) creations that realistically depicted the idle life (無爲徒生) of the Russian intelligentsia. "Uncle Vanya" (와-냐 아젓시) is one such work.
However, Alexander Blok, in his short essay "The Revolution and the Intelligentsia," speaks as if he holds great hope (信望) for the intelligentsia.
But ultimately, the final task cannot be accomplished by the intelligentsia.
It cannot be done without someone like Solomin (소로민) from "Virgin Soil."

Then what should the intelligentsia of today do? What should the writers who write do? Can literature of "life's intrinsic demand" (生의 本然한 要求) truly be born from our current state of life? What else could there be besides literature of servility (卑屈), endurance (忍從), compromise (妥協), deception (欺瞞), escape (逃避), and despair (切望)? Such writing is not needed.
Literature of life's intrinsic demand - this is necessary. Literature with life (生命잇는 文學) is necessary.
Then, comrade who takes it upon yourself (自任) to be the pioneer of the age! The desolate "Virgin Soil" must be tilled and prepared by your hands. <97> You must "till," those who come after you must "sow," and those who come after them must "reap." You must not be hasty in seeking the conclusion (結論).

However, Seoul is full of people seeking conclusions. Among them, many don't even seek the conclusion but just try to lick the surface (表面) of the conclusion.
Otherwise, middle-class socialists (中産階級的 社會主義者) are gathered (羣集). If the appearance (出沒) of the yangban class members (兩班階級者) becomes rampant (非常하면), a reactionary mood (反動的 氣運) inevitably arises. Especially when they themselves believe it is not a reactionary movement (反動運動)!...
The final outcome (歸結) of all movements (運動) ultimately returns to the hands of the proletariat (푸로레타리아). All conclusions of all movements.

No matter how grand (壯烈) the force (氣勢) of the reactionary movement, it is useless (所用업는 것이다). What is destined to fall (沒落) will fall and disappear.
"Truth" (졍말) hides its shadow (그림자) wherever it goes.
Wherever one goes, "truth" cannot be found. Everything is a "lie" (거짓말). Everything is a lie.
If only one person, just one person thinking about "truth," would emerge. If only one person, comrade, would reach out a hand-.

The rain has cleared, and the sky is bright. Stars gaze down intently. A crescent moon hangs in mid-sky (中天).
How clear the night is after the rain. How vigorous. But Seoul is dozing (조을고 잇다). Seoul, of chaos, arrogance, and falsehood (虛僞), is falling into deep sleep (熟睡). From Tongdaemun (東大門) to Sŏdaemun (西大門), piercing through Chongno (鐘路), up to the foot (밋) of Pukak Mountain (北岳山), all that is visible are goblins! Goblins wearing the skirts of old morals (舊道德), old customs (舊習慣), endurance, and compromise, their lower bodies unseen, overflow (橫溢) the entire city (全市街). The hem of the old concept (舊槪念) of "value" (價値) flutters in the early summer night breeze (첫녀름 밤바람). <98>
"Goblins" of every meaning occupy (占領) all of Chosŏn (全朝鮮).
Ah- But I missed Seoul. I wanted to see faces in turumagi (두루마기). I wanted to see red faces (붉은 얼골) in white jeogori and black skirts (감장 치마). I wanted to hear the voices saying "Yes" (녜), "No" (안예요). -
I wanted to see the azaleas (진달네) on the slopes (비탈) of Namsan (南山). I wanted to see the nameless flowers (일홈 모르는 꼿) and grasses (풀) blooming everywhere on our land (땅뎅이). I wanted to see the blue sky looking down upon our land-
I missed the wind coming and going over our land from South (南) to North (北), from East (東) to West (西).
I missed the bare mountains (벌거버슨 山), the grandfather (한아버지) with a long pipe (長竹) walking with hands clasped behind his back (뒤찜), and the elder sisters (누나들) in white jeogori and work aprons (행조 치마) drawing water-. Ah, but!
Where is Chosŏn? Where is there life (生命)!
Everything is hidden by the goblin's shadow. Everything is playing in the goblin's dance!
A black skirt lined with green (초록으로 안을 밧친 감장 치마) ducked into the street corner to avoid the goblin...

What are we to do about our sisters (누의들)?-
I think of Chuyŏng (朱英). I think of Elena (에레-나).
I think of Elena, the Russian maiden who followed the Bulgarian patriot Insarov (인싸로-푸). I think of Chuyŏng, who shot a policeman at the border and died with Ch'unyong (春容) on the banks of the Taedong River (大同江 언덕 우)-.
Sisters' work is not just Japanese language (日本 말) and arithmetic (算術), sisters' pride is not just "All right, that's true!", sisters' task is not changing clothes (옷 한 벌) every three days, sisters' study (工夫) is not just the "Serenade" (쎄레나-드), sisters' assignment (工課) is not taking walks (散步) after school (下學 後), nor is it just writing letters in flower-patterned envelopes (꼿봉투) and kissing (입 맛추는 것) them.
Before dreaming of flower palanquins (꼿가마) and pianos (피아노), sister (누나야)! Look down at the piece of land (땅뎅이) you are standing on. Isn't everything, everything decided after that? Before dreaming of attending banquets (會席) hand-in-hand after dinner (저녁 먹고 손목 잡고), and guest rooms (客室) with pianos and landscape paintings (風景畵), <99> think of the cold wind (찬바람) and the gravel fields (자갈 밧).

Ah- Everything is a lie. I've only seen "lies" (거짓말) and haven't seen "truth" (졍말). Perhaps this is this fellow's fate (運命).
Mana te matama! Mana te matama! (?)
But we must live. We must live. Sisters, stand firmly on the ground (땅뎅이를 밟고 서 잇섯다고). Until the Earth cools, until life disappears (生命이 업서지기까지)-.
Ah, I long for Russian women (露西亞의 女性). I long for Marianna (마리안나) from "Virgin Soil." I long for Elena (에레-나) from "On the Eve" (그 前날 밤).
It is said that Nietzsche once loved and wept for Russian women. In Chosŏn, which treads the history (歷史) of Russia, how many Elenas are there really, and how many Mariannas are there really?
It's unknowable. How could one know-
I miss Chuyŏng again.

Is Chuyŏng in the South? No. She is in the North.
Chuyŏng died, but Chuyŏng is alive. One Chuyŏng died, but how many Chuyŏngs are alive, one cannot know.
Ah- Chuyŏng is in the North. The wind-swept North becomes longed for once again...

The moon is hidden by clouds. The stars twinkle faintly.
In Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑), cranes (鶴두루미) cry - cuckoos (杜鵑) cry, and lions (獅子) roar.
A cool wind sweeps past. The feet that set out searching for the longed-for spring of Seoul return empty-handed (븬손), and from the goblin's lair (독가비의 구혈), the sounds of mumbling and squirming (꿈즈거리는 소리) never cease.-
Ah- Seoul! Let me stop here the footprints of this 'Promenade Sentimentale' (프로므나-드 산티만탈) scattered haphazardly (順序 업시 헛허노흔) upon your face. <100>

**Summary**

Kim Kijin's "Promenade Sentimentale" is a passionate and deeply critical essay lamenting the state of Seoul and Korea (Chosŏn) in 1923. The author contrasts his nostalgic longing for his homeland with the harsh reality he finds: a society steeped in "chaos, arrogance, and deep sleep," symbolized by pervasive "goblins." He criticizes the superficiality of the art and literary scene, the stagnation of the intelligentsia, the hypocrisy of the middle class, and the lack of genuine progress or revolutionary spirit. Referencing Russian literature (Turgenev, Blok) and Western thought (Marx, Barbusse), Kim calls for an awakening based on "truth" and "power," urging intellectuals and artists to engage with reality, reform society from its foundations, and connect with the "greatest number," even drawing parallels between Korea's situation and pre-revolutionary Russia. The essay ends on a melancholic yet resolute note, acknowledging the grim reality but reaffirming the need to live and strive, particularly invoking the image of strong, revolutionary women like the fictional "Chuyŏng."

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing for several reasons. It vividly portrays the disillusionment and critical consciousness among some Korean intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period, showing their engagement with international socialist and nationalist ideas (Marxism, Russian Revolution, figures like Barbusse). The critique of the established cultural scene and the call for art rooted in social reality reflect the burgeoning proletarian literature movement (of which Kim Kijin was a key figure, though later controversial). Furthermore, the essay's focus on the perceived passivity and "sleep" of Korean society, contrasted with the urgent need for radical change and the specific invocation of revolutionary female figures, offers insight into the complex dynamics of cultural nationalism, social critique, and gender ideals within the colonial context. Historians might find the blend of nationalist longing, sharp social criticism, international intellectual influences, and the call for a fundamental societal transformation particularly interesting.

Promeneade Sentinental
						金基鎭
그립던 서울의 봄도 자최 업시 되고 마랏다. 할미꼿, 개나리, 버들강아지 우흐로 한거름 한거름 깁허저 드러가든 봄이 울 아래 흙 우에다 꼿입을 짓밟어 놋코서 고요히 지나갓나 보다.
이몸이 멀리 잇슬 때에 서울이 얼마나 憧憬의 관역이 되엿섯스며, 더구나 요지음은 얼마나 故鄕의 山川이 그리웟섯더냐. 서울의 봄을 보고 십흔 마음은 내 몸을 붓드러 서울의 땅을 밟게 만든 것이다.
그러나 서울의 봄은 어대로 갓나? 서울의 봄은 자최도 업시 되고야 마랏다. 언덕 비탈에는 오랑캐꼿이 시들픈 듯이 고개를 숙이고 호을로 남어 잇다. 진달네 살구꼿이 떠러지고 芍藥과 躑躅이 爛漫히 웃는다...

지금은 바야흐로 서울이 熾熱한 녀름으로 거름을 빨리하고 잇는 때이다. 灼熱된 太陽이 서울을 이끌어 赤道의 位置에 갓가히 갓다 노흘 터이지. 서울의 봄을 보려고 달아온 이 몸을 蒸熱의 「독안이」 속에다 잡아 너흘 것이다. 混沌과 傲慢과 熟眠의 서울이 芳香의 녀름으로 나날이 물드러간다.

混沌과 傲慢과 熟眠의 朝鮮 - 똑갓흔 意味의 서울! 서울! - 이 그리웟섯다. 朝鮮이 보고 십헛다. 나를 나어준 땅뎅이, 나를 길너낸 땅뎅이가 보고 십헛다.
