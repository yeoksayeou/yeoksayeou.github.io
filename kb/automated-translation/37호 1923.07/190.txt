Preface Above 『Tatami』
						Kim Sŏksong

Impure blood!
Beware of false prophets (先知者), who come to you in sheep's clothing but inwardly are ravenous wolves (狼). By their **** you will know them. Do people pick grapes from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? = Matthew 7:15, 16

Impure blood!,
Impure blood, return to the veins (靜脉).

Now is midday, high summer,
The peak of life, the season of the artery (動脉).
From bacteria to humankind,
In every corner of the world,
The sound of the marching trumpet is heard.

Oh, impure blood!,
It is time for you to return (to your fate (宿命)).

I am in agony.<70>
From head to toenail,
From your impure trampling (蹂躪).
Living the brightness of midday as the darkness of midnight,
Oh, my sorrow!

Return, to the land of night,
Oh, my impure blood! (6. 15)

Over the fence, over the fence
Alone in the morning,
Lost in thought, I was,
Suddenly, by a dreamlike sound,
Startled, wondering if it was an illusion (錯覺).

In the neighbor's cage (籠),
The song of a small bird living confined,
When it sharply pierced my ear,
The sound of a child's chatter was also heard.
Though the small bird's body is confined in the cage,
The small bird's song flows over the fence.
My soul, confined by tradition (傳統), too,<71>
Will fly over the fence, over the fence. (6. 15)

Monsoon Rain
The tedious, tedious monsoon rain, today too,
Poured down from morning till night.

I, suffering from a stomach ache,
Lord, grant light swiftly,
Prayed as I lay in bed.

The low pressure, the dregs of the old ways of thinking (舊思潮),
Still hinders (阻害) our growth.

I, suffering in my heart,
Lord, grant light swiftly,
Prayed as I lay in bed. (6. 16)

Let's pull the weeds (지심)
This morning too,
A welcome letter from my wife,
Brought me the sad news (悲報) about her illness,<72>
To the bedside of a poor traveler.

Poverty (貧寒), Disease (疾病), Death (死).
Do you know that the meanings (意味)
Of these three nouns are entirely the same?
With continuous, systematic action (作用),
Sowing the seeds of ruin in the field of humankind,
Three nouns! Poverty, Disease, Death!
And do you know that all this,
Is your own self-inflicted suffering (自繩自縛),
Oh, pitiful (可憐) brothers!

This is not the time to hesitate,
The weeds (지심) of ruin grow steadily.

Have you picked up the hoe and spade?
Let's pull them! The weeds of ruin.
Singing "Eolneolneol sangsadŭya,"
Let's pull, let's pull, let's pull the weeds.

(Revolution (革命)) is the exercise of truth (眞理).
There is no obstacle before us. (6. 18)<73>

**Summary**

This collection of four poems by Kim Sŏksong expresses a powerful yearning for renewal and revolution. The first poem uses the metaphor of "impure blood" to represent stagnation and the past, urging its return to the veins as the vibrant "artery" of life and progress takes over. The second contrasts the physical confinement of a caged bird with the freedom of its song, symbolizing the soul's desire to transcend restrictive traditions. The third poem links personal suffering (illness) and societal gloom (monsoon rain) to the oppressive weight of "old ways of thinking," praying for light. The final poem directly addresses the interconnected suffering caused by poverty, disease, and death, calling them self-inflicted wounds and urging collective action—symbolized by weeding—culminating in the declaration that "Revolution is the exercise of truth." For historians, these poems offer insight into the intellectual climate of 1920s colonial Korea, reflecting a potent mix of personal anguish, sharp critique of tradition and societal ills, and an explicit call for revolutionary change against perceived stagnation and oppression. The identification of poverty, disease, and death as systemic issues requiring radical solutions is particularly noteworthy.

不純한 피!
거짓 先知者들을 삼갈지어다. 양의 옷을 입고 너의에게, 나아오나 속에는 노략질하는 일희(狼)라. 그 ****로 저의를 *지니, 가시나무에서 엇지 포도를 따며, 엉겅퀴에서 엇지 무화과를 따겟느냐. = 馬太 7章 15, 16

不純한 피!,
不純한 피는 靜脉으로 돌아가라.

只今은 正午이다, 盛夏이다,
生命의 絶頂이다, 動脉의 철이다.
細菌으로부터 人類에까지,
누리의 구석구석에,
行進의 喇叭 소리가 들린다.

오, 不純한 피!,
너는 돌아갈 때이다(너의 宿命으로).

나는 괴로웁다.＜70＞
머리에서 발톱까지,
不純한 너의 蹂躪으로.
光明의 한낫을 闇黑의 한밤으로,
밧구어 사는, 오, 나의 슬픔!.

돌아가거라, 밤의 나라로,
오, 不純한 나의 피!. (6. 15)

울 넘어, 울 넘어로
아츰에 호을로,
생각에 잠착한 나는,
忽然히 꿈결가튼 소리에,
錯覺인가 하야 놀내엇다.

이웃집 어리(籠) 속에,
갓쳐 사는 작은 새의 노래가,
나의 귀를 날캅게 찌를 때,
어린애의 떠드는 소리도 들럿다.
작은 새의 몸은 어리 안에 갓쳐도,
작은 새의 노래는 울 넘어로 흐른다.
傳統에 갓치은 나의 靈魂도,＜71＞
울 넘어 울 넘어로 날아갈거나. (6. 15)

장마비
지긋지긋한 장마비는, 오늘도,
아츰부터 밤까지 퍼부엇다.

胃를 알는 나는,
主여, 速히 光明을 주소서,
자리에 누으며 빌엇다.

舊思潮의 餘沫인 低氣壓은,
아즉도 우리의 生長을 阻害한다.

마음을 알는 나는,
主여, 速히 光明은 주소서,
자리에 누으며 빌엇다. (6. 16)

지심을 매이자
오늘 아츰에도,
안해의 반가운 편지가,
그의 病에 對한 悲報를,
나에게 傳해 주엇다,＜72＞
가난한 나그내의 머리 맛헤.

貧寒, 疾病, 死.
그대들은 이 세 가지 名詞의,
意味가 全等한 줄을 아는가,
連續的, 組織的 作用으로,
人類의 밧헤 滅亡의 씨를 뿌리는,
세 가지 名詞!, 貧寒, 疾病, 死!
그리고 이는 모다,
그대들의 自繩自縛인 줄을,
아는가, 오, 可憐한 兄弟야!.

머믓머믓할 때는 아니다,
滅亡의 지심은 점점 자란다.

호미와 가래를 들엇는가,
매이자! 滅亡의 지심을.
「얼널널 상사듸아」 부르며,
매이자 매이자 지심을 매이자.

(革命)은 眞理의 行使이다.
우리의 압헤는 것침이 업다.(6. 18)＜73＞
