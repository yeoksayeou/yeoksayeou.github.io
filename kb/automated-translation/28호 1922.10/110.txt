Magazine Title: 개벽 제28호(1부)
Publication Date: 1922년 10월 01일
Article Title: 레닌 胸像 (Lenin Bust)
Author: Unknown
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing/Essay)

◆ Lenin's medical condition is said to now truly be at a stage requiring absolute rest. Whether his illness will recover or not, no one seems able to declare with certainty; even if he does recover, everyone also agrees with the observation that he cannot resume his political duties. The world is now finding interest in imagining a second Lenin.

◆ Since early summer this year - since Lenin's illness - the person acting as Lenin's deputy - Chairman of the Council of Ministers - is Rykov (Rik'o). He is neither a scholar nor an orator like Lenin. He is merely a worker of former days who deeply believes in the ideal society of Communism, just as he deeply believes in Lenin.

Lenin was born on April 10, 1870, making him a middle-aged man of fifty-four this year. The photograph published here was sculpted by the English sculptress Mrs. Clara Sheridan (K'urara Seriṭan) when she specially visited Moscow (Mosŭk'o) last July. "Upon ascending three flights of stairs, inside a room draped with velvet (天鵞䋐) curtains, sits a rather curiously made wooden desk. In this small room, the bald-headed Lenin sits quietly. He wears a woolen shirt and clothes that seem to have been bought quite some time ago and then re-pressed. His room is extremely quiet. Just as he scolded drunkenness on revolutionary phrases, he seems to reject even revolutionary excitement. He glances at various documents placed on his desk, interrupting this to grant audiences to visitors for ten minutes, five minutes, one minute intervals. Mostly, he speaks while standing. He speaks in a low voice, as if thinking it unnecessary to raise his voice with these various people." (Robins [Robinssŭ])

"His height is stockily short, and a round, completely bald head is set upon his shoulders. His eyes are small, his nose is like a lion's, his mouth is wide and large, and his cheeks seem slightly thick, making him too unimpressive for an idol who receives love and respect almost unparalleled in history. An inconceivable leader of the masses - purely a leader relying only on intellect - he has no radiance, no warmth, is uncompromising, and lacks any brilliant quality that could be called otherworldly. There is only the ability to express profound thoughts in simple words, the power to analyze practical situations, and the combination of intellectual boldness and acuity." (Ttosimimit')<96>

**Summary**

This 1922 article from the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* discusses the declining health of Vladimir Lenin, noting widespread doubt about his ability to return to power and mentioning Aleksey Rykov as his acting deputy. It describes Lenin as a 54-year-old man, referencing a bust created by British sculptress Clara Sheridan. The piece includes quoted descriptions of Lenin's simple appearance, quiet demeanor, efficient work habits (from "Robins"), and his physically unimpressive yet intellectually powerful presence as a leader (from "Ttosimimit'"). For historians, this article is interesting as it shows contemporary Korean intellectual interest in the leader of the Russian Revolution during the Japanese colonial period, reflecting a search for alternative political models and information about world events, particularly those concerning anti-imperialist or revolutionary figures. The detailed physical and personal descriptions sourced from Western observers also highlight the channels through which information about figures like Lenin reached Korea.

레닌 胸像

◆「레닌」의 病狀은 이제야말로 絶對休養의 時期에 잇다한다 그의 病이 回復될지말지는 누구나 斷言할수는 업는 樣이니 回復된다할지라도 다시 政務를 引繼할수업다는 觀察에는 한 누구나 一致한다 世上에서는 只今 第二의 「레닌」을 想像하기에 興味를 느며 잇다
◆今年初夏以來-레닌의 病患以來-로「레닌」의 代理-內閣議長-를 보는 사람은 「리코-」이다 그는 「레닌」과 가튼 學者도 아니며 辯舌家도 아니다 다못「레닌」을 深信함과 가티 共産主義의 理想社會를 深信하는 한넷날의 勞働者이다
「레닌」은 一八七○年四月十日生의 今年五十四歲의 中老이며 여긔에 揭載한 寫眞은 英國의 婦人彫像家『쿠라라·세리』이 昨年七月에 「모스코」지 委訪하야 描造한 것이다『三層의 階段을 上하면 天鵞䋐의 帷를 張한 房안에는 자못 妙하게된 木机 한 個가 노혀 잇다 이 작은 一室에는 秃頭의 「레닌」이 고요히 걸어안저잇다 그는 毛織의 사쓰와 퍽 以前에 사두엇다가 다시 주름을 편 듯한 衣類를 입엇다 그의 房은 極히 고요하다『革命的文句에 醉함을 咜罵한 그 만큼 그는 革命的興舊에 醉함지도 排斥하는 듯십다 그는 机上에 노힌 여러가지의 書類에 눈을 보내는 그 中에서도 十分間, 五分間, 一分間으로 時間을 허서 訪問者를 引見한다 그것도 大槪는 선 그대로 말하는 것이다 그는 이러한 여러 사람과 소리를 가다듬어 말할 必要도 업다 생각하는 듯한 작은 소리로써 말한다』(로빈스)
『키는 암팡스럽게 작으며 훌덕 버서진 둥글어운 대머리라 억개우에 언치어잇다. 눈은 작고 코는 獅子코이며 입은 넓고 크며 은 조곰 두터운듯하야 거의 歷史에 比類가 업는 愛와 尊敬을 밧는 者의 偶像으로는 넘우 非印像的이다. 思議할수업는 民衆의 指導者-純粹히 智力에 에 依하는 指導者의 그는 光彩도 업고 和氣도 업스며 非妥協的이오 出世間的으로 아모이것이라고할 光輝잇는 特質도 업다, 다못 深遠한 思想을 單純한 말로 表示하며 實際的의 形勢를 分析하는 力과 는 智力上의 大膽과 明敏을 兼備한 그것이다』(시미미)＜96＞
