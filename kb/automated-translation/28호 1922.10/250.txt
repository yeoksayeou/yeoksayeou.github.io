Notice
※ Readers of this magazine, if you change your address, please be sure to notify our company of both your new and old addresses. In addition, please clearly write your address and full name in other correspondence.
※ Those wishing to subscribe to this magazine, please be sure to send payment in advance. If it is not prepaid, the magazine will not be dispatched.
※ For the method of sending money, please use the transfer account Keijō 8106, Kaebyŏk-sa, as much as possible.

**Summary**

This article is a notice to the readers of the magazine "Kaebyŏk" published in October 1922. It provides essential administrative instructions: subscribers must inform the publisher of address changes, prepayment is required for subscriptions, and the preferred method for payment is postal transfer to the Kaebyŏk-sa account in Keijō. It concludes with the magazine's publication details, including printing and issue dates, price, copyright notice, and the names and addresses of the editor, publisher, printer, printing house, and publishing house, along with contact information. For historians, this notice offers valuable insights into the operational aspects of magazine publishing in colonial Korea, including subscription management, payment methods (postal transfer), printing logistics, and the specific locations of publishing-related businesses in Keijō (colonial Seoul). The use of the Japanese Taishō era name and Japanese place names like Ch'ŏngsu-ch'ŏng and Hwanggŭm-ch'ŏng alongside Korean names underscores the colonial context.
