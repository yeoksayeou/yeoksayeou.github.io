The Stench of Decaying Life
						Kim Sŏk-song

The night is already deep
The wind seeping through the window crack
Brings the gentle autumn
And busies itself cooling the sweat
Beaded on my forehead.

Whenever the candlelight flickers,
*Jjiririn jjirin*... the sound
Of insects outside the window
Raises *jjiririn jjirin*... sharp
Waves within my heart.

Ah! Summer is on its way out.

Autumn is already stepping close behind.
From the flow of life trapped
In a puddle all summer long, a stench of decay
Pierces my nose ever more sharply.

The Thorn of Autumn

As the cool breeze begins to blow morning and evening,
My heart, ever more... it's not that common affliction...
Of being hurt by the sweet wine of love,
Nor is it being struck by the arrow of worldly gain.

Even the sound of the cicada <47>
That so stirred others' hearts is now unheard,
And even the sound of the stream before the gate is quiet,
But the tree leaves falling endlessly
Make my heart ache all the more.

In the world, certainly,
For everything there is a "For what reason... For what cause,"
A motive and a reason,
But I, without such fine conditions,
Just ache, just ache in my heart.

Ah—my heart aches.
The thorn of autumn, sharp as a needle,
Pierces my heart, bare as ribs,
At its own whim, recklessly.
Ah—thorn of autumn! Ache of my heart!

My Funeral Banner Inscription

To put it plainly in vulgar terms,
As a person lives and goes through this world,
All the things done, this and that,
Are merely preparations for the inscription on one's funeral banner after death.

Then what deeds shall I do
To make the inscription before my funeral bier?
This is the greatest question for me,
And the resolution of this question is my first duty.

As an individual, shall I become a scholar?
As a family member, shall I become a filial son?
For the nation, shall I become a patriot?
For society, shall I become a leader? <48>

Such things are good, it's true,
But these are inscriptions for far more heroic figures.
Ah! Then what shall I do?
What indeed shall be my funeral banner inscription?

Since I like flowers (), shall it be 'flower'?
Since I like the sun (해), shall it be 'sun'?
"Kim Sŏk-song, who loves the sun and flowers!"
Ah! Is this my funeral banner inscription?

My Wish

An old man appeared in my dream
And asked me, "What is your wish?"
Without hesitation, I answered,
"I have no wish whatsoever."

The old man asked again,
"Where is the person with intellect, emotion, and will
Who has no wish?
Speak whatever it is without reservation."

Reluctantly, I answered.
"Just to live as an ordinary person.
As I am, as I please, by my own means,
I will live just as I am and then go."
〓From Sŏngdong Sanga (城東山家)〓 <49>
<47-49>

**Summary**

This collection of four poems by Kim Sŏk-song expresses a deep sense of seasonal melancholy, existential questioning, and a rejection of grand ambitions. The poet feels the oppressive decay of life as summer ends and the sharp pain of autumn's arrival. He contemplates the meaning of life, symbolized by the inscription on one's funeral banner, questioning conventional paths like becoming a scholar, patriot, or leader. Ultimately, he finds solace not in heroic pursuits but in simple things like the sun and flowers, and expresses a desire to live an ordinary life, true to himself, without grand aspirations. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in *Kaebyŏk* in 1922, are interesting as they reflect an individual's introspective mood and perhaps a subtle disillusionment during the early Japanese colonial period. While not overtly political, the poet's turning away from societal roles like 'patriot' or 'leader' towards personal authenticity and a simple life could be interpreted as a quiet form of resistance or withdrawal in a time of constrained national expression. Historians might find the focus on individual feeling and the specific articulation of desired ordinariness noteworthy for understanding personal responses to the era.

生命의썩은내
						金石松
밤은 이미 깁헛다
窓틈으로 새어드는 바람이
산들산들한 가을을 가저다가
나의 이마에 보실보실 난
을 식히기에 奔走하다

燭불이 흐릴적 마다
리린 린………들려오는
窓밧게 버레소리는
나의가슴에 리린 린……
날카로운 波動을 일으킨다

아! 여름은 가는中이다

가을은 이미 그뒤를 밟아온다
온 여름동안 웅덩이에 가쳐잇든
生命의흐름에서는 썩은내가
더욱더욱 나의 코를 른다

가을의가시
아츰저녁으로 선들바람이 불기시작하더니
나의가슴은 더욱더욱 흔히말하는………
사랑의甘酒에 傷한것도 아니요
功利의 화살에 마즌것도 아니외다

그가티 남의마음을 설레게하든＜47＞
매암이 소리도 이제는 안들리고
門前의 시내ㅅ물소리조차 고요한데
하염업시 어저나리는 나무입히
나의가슴을 더욱압흐게 합니다

世上에는 반듯이
「무엇문에……무슨理由로」라하야
每事에 動機와緣由가 잇지마는
나는 그러한 훌륭한 條件도업시
그저그저 가슴만 알코잇습니다

아- 나의가슴은 압흐외다
바늘가티 날카로운 가을의가시가
肋骨만앙상한 나의가슴을
제마음대로 함부로 침질합니다
아- 가을의 가시여! 내가슴의압흠이어!

나의銘旌거리
누구나 알기쉽게 육담으로말하면
사람이 한세상 사고가는데
이러니 저러니 하는일이 모다
죽은뒤에 명정거리 작만이다

그러면 나는 무슨일을하야
나의喪轝압헤 명정거리를 삼을가
이것이 나에게는 가장큰問題요
이問題의解決은 나의 첫義務이다

個人으로는 學者가될가
家庭으로는 孝子가될가
國家로는 愛國者가될가
社會로는 指導者가될가＜48＞

그러한것들도 조키는조흐나
이는 훨신 英雄的人物의 명정거리다
아! 그러면 나는 무엇을할가
나의 명정거리는 果然무엇일가

을 조하하니 으로할가
해를 조하하니 해로할가
「해와을사랑하는金石松!」
아!이것이 나의 명정거리인가!

나의所願
어한 老人이 에 나다려
「너의所願은 무엇이냐」물엇다

그老人이 다시 뭇기를
「智情意를 가진 사람치고

「그저 凡人으로살기외다
