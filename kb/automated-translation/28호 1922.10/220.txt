情熱 (Passion)
						朱榮邦 (Chu Yŏngbang)

Breathing out red sighs
Panting, crossing the Western Mountain
Is the summer evening glow
On a lonely roadside
He wanders alone in fear

An unending sound
Flows from the other side
Heard from a humble hut
Could it be calling him?
He strains his small ears

Walking through an endless desert
A small and young heart
Eyes burning like fire
Ah! Who, for his sake,
Has held the iron key of Silence!

He sits on the cliff edge of Life
Beyond the moments of Pleasure
Meditating on Death and the Afterlife
Ah! Who, for his sake,
Will sing the song of Passion!

First Autumn

Like a lover's whisper
It hollows out my chest
Between the sparse branches of the pale paulownia
The roadside wind rises high again

Like a lover's weeping
It melts my insides
When frogs cry in the overgrown lotus pond
Nature's rhythm is endlessly sorrowful

Like the blooming face of a lover too
In the dark blue sky of breaking dawn
Coming toward a distant land like a traveler
I heard the cry of the cold wild geese

Like the falling teardrops of a lover too
Pearls gathered on every blade of grass<55>
When they soak the poet's knees
I felt the mood of autumn

First Autumn, Thinking of My Love

You and I
In a flower-blooming dream garden
Treading upon the grass of Joy

With your sweet lips
You whispered in my ear
When the fishing boat lights (漁火) flowed on the river ahead at dusk
When the fragrance of rice (稻香) drifted in the back garden in the fledgling morning

In the land of mother
Let's meet again

I cannot forget
The sorrowful memories of bygone days
That do not fade like a dream
All alone
Unable to overcome my sorrow
Unable to bear my joy
I took off my shoes and wept
I clapped my hands and laughed
Ah, sorrow and joy, all alone

Using my own arm as a pillow
I lay down quietly
Resting my legs on my own
I stand tall without a care
Ah, asleep or awake, all alone

Walking my own steps
Going freely down the main road
Carried away by my own excitement
I turn back the way I came
Ah, coming or going, all alone

Thirsty, I went to the well
No water, so I swallowed hard
Hungry, I looked for rice
No rice, so I drank gruel
Ah, starving or eating, all alone

With the pure heart
Born from mother
On the clean sand
I will build a pagoda, and then—
Ah, living or dying, all alone<56>

**Summary**

This article presents four poems by Chu Yŏngbang. The first, "Passion," depicts a solitary figure grappling with fear, longing, existential thoughts of life and death, and a yearning for passionate expression. The subsequent poems shift to the theme of autumn, evoking melancholy through imagery of fading nature, lovers' whispers and tears, and the lonely cries of wild geese. The final two poems delve deeper into solitude and memory, expressing intense loneliness, conflicting emotions of sorrow and joy experienced alone, and a resolve to maintain purity despite isolation. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in *Gaebyeok* in 1922, offer insight into the personal sentiments and artistic expressions prevalent among Korean intellectuals during the early colonial period. The themes of alienation, introspection, longing, and a complex mix of sorrow and resilience might reflect the broader socio-political atmosphere and the individual's struggle within it, making them potentially valuable for historians studying the emotional landscape of colonial Korea.
