Wind Drunk on Spring Light
						Kim Ansŏ

Joyful Night

Is there anything finer than the night?
Soft rest, a smile upon the lips,
Gives fine solace, sleep, and sweet dreams
To life weary from the day's hard toil.

Is there anything finer than the night?
The love of this world, trapped in the prison of bright daylight,
Joyfully casts off its bonds under the starlight,
Whispering fine secrets with ecstatic kisses.

Drinking Song

I shall stay up the short midsummer night,
Where the scent of ripening apricots drifts
On the cool breeze,
And drink, cup after cup.<107>

Friends arise from wine, are missed through wine,
Drunkenness is in wine, exists not outside wine;
Oh people, let us cast off the sorrow of sobriety,
Hold full cups, offer them to each other,
And sing songs of untrue sincerity and falsehood.

Leaning on the Mansae Draft (1923.12.4)

When untimely drowsiness descends on the waning day,
The melody coming and going in my quiet mind
Makes me think of old dreams like a gust of wind;
Is this memory, is this illusion?

Looking back, the past path is dark,
The Sŏngch'ŏn River (成川江) is filled only with ice,
The cold wind circles the mountain, stirring things up;
My path is futile, is there any use?

Is there no melody of past days here?
Not even a dream drifting with the wind?
In the deep, jet-black night without dreams,
Lies quietly asleep, Hamhŭng (咸興).<108>

Sigh (1923.2.2)

When your faint shadow shone, shimmering
Upon my quiet night sea with no waves,
The drunken wind, whirling as if mad with spring light,
Clung to the slender branches of the weeping willow,
Singing songs, dancing freely.

Now, when your shimmering shadow is broken
Upon my night sea stirred by waves,
Only the fading rugosa roses on the shore are blown by the wind,
Their scent drifting toward the endless sky (한울),
Wandering directionless in the wind.

White Moon

When the golden evening sun
Dies, spilling blood upon the sea,
And the lonely wind
Weeps forlornly in the village tree branches,

Oh, my person, too busy even to cry,
Look up at such a time;
The white moon sits atop the mountain peak,
Watching over my darkening world.<109>

**Summary**

This collection of five poems by Kim Ansŏ, published in 1924, explores themes of night, drinking, memory, disillusionment, and nature's beauty juxtaposed with sorrow. The poems often contrast the solace found in darkness, dreams, or intoxication with the harsh realities or sorrows of the waking world ("Joyful Night," "Drinking Song"). A sense of melancholy and futility permeates "Leaning on the Mansae Draft" and "Sigh," using imagery of dark paths, icy rivers, broken reflections, and wandering scents. "White Moon" offers a moment of quiet observation and perhaps distant comfort amidst sadness. From a colonial history perspective, the poems are interesting for their potential indirect expressions of the era's atmosphere. "Leaning on the Mansae Draft," dated shortly after the March 1st Movement (referred to as the Manse Movement), along with the general mood of disillusionment, escapism (drinking), and reflections on dark paths and sleeping cities (Hamhŭng), could suggest the poet's grappling with the political realities and suppressed national aspirations of Korea under Japanese rule, though expressed through personal lament and natural imagery rather than overt political statements.

봄빗에 醉한 바람
						金岸曙
즐겁은 밤
밤보다도 곱은 것이야 오히려 잇스랴,
입살에 미소를 띄운 보드랍은 안식은
하로동안의 고된 노역에 시달닌 인생에게
위안의 곱은 잠과 달금한 꿈을 주나니.

밤보다도 곱은 것이야 오히려 잇스랴,
밝은 白日의 감옥에 가친 이 세상의 사랑은
별빗아레서야 즐겁게 결박을 버서바리고
恍惚의 키쓰와 함끠 곱은 비밀을 속삭이나니.

술노래
나는, 닉어가는 참살구의
향내가 떠도는 서늘바람의
짤븐 한녀름의 밤을 새와가며,
잔에 잔을 거듭하야 마시랴노라.＜107＞

벗은 술에서 생겨 술에서 그립고
취함은 술에 잇서 술밧게 업나니,
사람아, 우리는 깨는 설음을 바리고
가득한 잔을 서로 잡아, 마조 권하며
맘에도 업는 眞情과 거즛의 노래를 불으리.

萬歲稿를 기대고(1923.12.4)
저므는 날의 때아닌 졸음이 내릴 때,
고요한 내머리속을 가고오는 노래가락은
바람결가튼 오랜 꿈을 생각게 하나니,
이는 기억이랄가, 이는 환상이랄가

와서보면은 지내온 녯길은 어둡고
成川江에는 어름만 가득히 덥히어,
찬바람은 山을 감돌아 뒤설네나니,
나의 길은 속졀업서라, 쓸데나 잇스랴.

이곳에는 지내간 날의 노래가락도 업고
바람결과 함끠 도는 꿈좃차 업는가,
꿈도 업는 깁고도 새캄한 밤속에
눕어서 고요히도 잠들은 咸興이어.＜108＞

歎息 (1923,2,2)
저의 고요한 잔 물살도 업는 밤바다우에
그대의 엿튼 그림자가 빗최어 이리울 때에는
봄빗에 밋친 듯이 휘나는 취한 바람이
실버드나무의 가늘은 가지에 매달녀
노래를 불으며, 맘것 춤을 추엇슴니다.

지금 저의 물살젓는 밤바다우에
그대의 어리운 그림자가 깨여진 이때엔
해안의 시들어 가는 해당화만 바람에 불녀,
끗업는 한울로 향내를 떠돌니며
바람에 *서는 방향도 업시 헤맵니다.

흰달
金빗의 저녁해는
바다우에 피를 뿌리고 죽으며,
외롭은 바람은
村가의 나무가지에서 어이업시 울을 때,

울기에도 배밧븐 내사람이어,
이러할 때에 눈을 들어 보라,
흰달은 산꼭대기우에 안자서
어둡어가는 내 세상을 직혀주나니.＜109＞
