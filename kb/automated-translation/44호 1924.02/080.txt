Free Communication/Exchange of Feelings
From Vladivostok (海參威)
Sosu Saeng

(Omitted beginning) I am translating and presenting (譯呈) a poem written by the famous Russian poet Merezhkovsky (멜슈꼽스키) after being moved by seeing a Korean Buddha statue (this is the translation of my comrade Han Pinggwan [同志韓氷觀]), so please publish it in the February issue of Kaebyŏk to provide a reference for understanding how Western poets feel about Eastern philosophy. Around March, I will pass through China (中國), touch upon India (印度), and take the path to tour Italy (伊), France (佛), Germany (獨), and various Southern European countries (南歐諸國). It probably won't be a very meaningful observation, but if it can be even a small help to your esteemed magazine (貴誌), I will not shy away from the labor of contributing from each place I pass through. All circumstances here are quite good. The situation of Ch'ŏndogyo (천도교) is also unexpectedly prosperous (料外에 대성황). The only regret (한) is that there are few people qualified to be missionaries (宣敎師) or middle school teachers. Please introduce many thoughtful young people [to this opportunity]. For this land, a vibrant new world, a new landscape (活天地新山河). (Omitted end)

Editor's Note: The poem by Merezhkovsky (멜수꼽스키), sent separately (別紙로 胎送), cannot be published in this issue due to page limitations. And Elder Brother, wherever you may go, please do not forget "Kaebyŏk" (開闢).

From Tokyo (東京)
B Saeng

Ah, what words can I use to tell you about the compatriots murdered in this land since September 2nd of last year. Recently, a memorial service (추도會) was held here, hosted by various organizations, and I am sending (胎呈) the mourning song (追悼歌) from that time; please read it with the feeling of facing their resentful spirits (원흔).

Mourning Song (追悼歌) (In the style of a moving song [感動歌曲調])
Alas, our Compatriots
What tragedy is this in a foreign city (異城) half a myriad *li* away?
A lifetime's ideals scattered in a single morning
Ten years' labor reduced to mere bones (骸骨)

(Refrain)
With what words can we console
The恨 (han/grudge) as deep as the sea, as high as the mountains (OO)?
On the day our ideals are realized
Flowers will bloom on the desolate graves

Where the late moonlight shines on Musashino (武藏野)
Where could your graves be?
Are the stars flowing in the autumn night's azure sky (碧空)
Your resentful souls (冤魂) wandering (彷徨)?

From Shanghai (上海)
Yohan

(Omitted beginning) In "Kaebyŏk" (開闢), I always enjoy reading the different sections. In the literary section, I always admire Tohyang's (도향) works, and occasionally I enjoy reading the children's songs (동요). I felt that Ch'unwŏn's (춘원) "Sacred Death" (거룩한 죽엄) was lacking in artistic meaning. From Ch'unwŏn's short story collection, I enjoyed reading the two pieces "Kasil" (가실) and "Hanmŏm" (한멈). I have also occasionally obtained and read magazines like "New Woman" (신녀성) and "Children" (어린이). Anyway, seeing vibrant people always gives me hope. I am happy (깃버함니다) to see improvements month by month, both in content and appearance. (Omitted middle) There are about a hundred students in Shanghai (상해) and Nanjing (남경). The goal of the majority is soon to cross the Pacific (태평양). Among them, about ten seem to want to learn something in China (즁국). However, there isn't much to learn, and it's very difficult to seize opportunities (긔회). This Saturday (Editor's note: Already the end of last year), the Shanghai Student Association (샹해학생회) is said to be holding an oratory contest. That might serve as a touchstone (시금석) for the intellectual capacity (지력) of Shanghai students. However, from what I've seen so far, I cannot expect (예긔치 못하겟슴니다) very much. It seems they are still far from reaching the same level as the Tokyo exchange students.—even though Shanghai (샹해), since Tokyo burned, cannot be said not to be the foremost city in the East. <38>

**Summary**

This article compiles three letters sent to *Kaebyŏk* magazine from Koreans living abroad in Vladivostok, Tokyo, and Shanghai in late 1923/early 1924. The letters discuss cultural exchange (a Russian poem about Korean art), travel plans, the state of the Korean diaspora community (Ch'ŏndogyo in Vladivostok, students in Shanghai), commentary on contemporary Korean literature and magazines, and the traumatic aftermath of the Great Kanto Earthquake massacre in Tokyo, including a mourning song for the victims. For historians, the article provides valuable firsthand accounts of the Korean diaspora's experiences, concerns, and intellectual life during the colonial period. It highlights the impact of the Kanto massacre, the transnational connections maintained through publications like *Kaebyŏk*, the differing circumstances and aspirations of Koreans in Russia, Japan, and China, and contemporary critiques of prominent writers like Yi Kwangsu (Ch'unwŏn).

自由通情
海參威에서
소수生

(上略) 露國 名詩人 「멜슈꼽스키」의 朝鮮 佛像을 보고 늣기여서 지은 詩 한편을 譯呈(同志韓氷觀의 譯)하오니 개벽 2月호에 登載하야 西洋 詩人의 東洋哲學에 대한 소감의 如何를 알게 하는 叅考을 지여주시오. 나는 3월경에 中國을 지나 印度를 것치여 伊, 佛, 獨, 南歐諸國을 구경하는 길을 갓겟슴니다. 무슨 의미잇는 관찰은 되지 못할 것이오나 貴誌를 위햐야 조곰이라도 도움이 된다하오면, 所經 各地에서의 投稿의 勞를 사양치 안켓나이다. 이곳의 모든 形便은 썩 좃슴니다. 천도교의 상황도 料外에 대성황 임니다. 단 宣敎師의 資格 또는 중학교의 교사될 資格을 가진 사람이 젹어서 한임니다. 생각잇는  청년들에게 만히 소개해 주시오. 活天地新山河인 이땅을 위하야.(下略)
編者曰 別紙로 胎送한 「멜수꼽스키」의 詩는 頁數의 관계로 이번 호에 揭載치 못함니다. 그리고 형님 어느 땅에 가시던지 「開闢」을 닛지 마러주시오.

上海에서 요한

(先畧) 「開闢」에서는 각도로 사성젹을 늘 재미잇게 봅니다. 문예란으로는 도향씨의 작품을 늘 감탄하오며 잇다끔 나는 동요를 지미잇게 봅니다. 춘원의 「거룩한 죽엄」은 예술뎍 의미로 보아 부족함을 늣기엇슴니다. 춘원의 단편집에서 「가실」 「한멈」 두 편을 재미잇게 보앗슴니다. 「신녀성」 「어린이」들 잡지도 각금 어더보앗슴니다. 하여간 생기잇는 사람을 보고 늘 희망을 부침니다. 내용이나 외모나 다달히 나아가는 것을 보고 깃버함니다. (中略)상해 남경의 학생이 한 백명됨니다. 다수는 미구에 태평양을 건너감이 목뎍이오. 그 즁십여는 즁국서 무엇을 좀 배워보려고 하는 모양이외다. 그러나 배울 것도 업고 긔회를 잡기도 매우 힘듭니다. 이번 토요(編者曰 벌서 昨年末)에 샹해학생회에서 웅변회를 개최한다함니다. 그것이 아마 샹해학생지력의 시금석이 될 수 잇겟슴니다. 그런데 제가 보기까지에는 별로 만흔 것을 예긔치 못하겟슴니다. 동경류학생과 비등한 수평선에 오르러 하여도 아직 멀엇나 봅니다.-샹해가 동경 불탄이후로 동양에 첫재가는 도회가 아니라 하지 못할 곳이 것마는.＜38＞
