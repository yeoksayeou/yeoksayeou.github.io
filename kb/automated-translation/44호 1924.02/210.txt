Magazine Title: 개벽 제44호
Publication Date: 1924년 02월 01일
Article Title: 同志여 (Comrade!)
Author: 長白山人
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

同志여
						長白山人

同志여!
우리의 數爻가 적다고 말라
우리의 사업이 넘어나 망망하다고 말라
녀름 한울 한 구석에 두너서 구름ㅅ장을 동지여 젹다하나뇨
눈감앗다 뜨는 동안에 왼 한울은 어두어지고
우레 울다, 번개 치다
천하에는 큰 비가 나리다!

아아 동지여!
우리가 젹다고 말라!
동지여 오직
「우리가 잇다!」할 지어다.
=1923,10,14夜= <113>

Comrade!
						Changbaeksanin

Comrade!
Do not say our numbers (數爻) are few
Do not say our task stretches boundlessly before us
In a corner of the summer sky, do you say the few (두너서) cloud sheets (구름ㅅ장) are too small, comrade?
In the blink of an eye, the whole sky (한울) darkens
Thunder roars, lightning flashes
And great rain falls upon the world!

Ah, comrade!
Do not say we are few (젹다)!
Comrade, only
Say, "We exist (잇다)!"
=1923, 10, 14 Night= <113>

**Summary**

This poem, titled "Comrade!", is an exhortation by "Changbaeksanin" (Person of Changbaek Mountain) addressed to fellow activists or sympathizers. The author urges them not to be discouraged by their seemingly small numbers or the daunting scale of their task. Using the metaphor of a small cloud quickly growing into a powerful thunderstorm that covers the sky and brings heavy rain, the poem insists that even a small group can bring about immense change. It concludes with a call for self-affirmation: "Comrade, only say, 'We exist!'" From a historical perspective, this poem, published in *Kaebyŏk* magazine in 1924 during the Japanese colonial period, reflects the spirit of resistance and the need for solidarity and perseverance among Korean nationalists or socialists. The pen name "Changbaeksanin," referencing the sacred Paektu Mountain, adds a layer of nationalist sentiment, and the message of hope against overwhelming odds would have resonated strongly with Koreans struggling under colonial rule.
