**Body Text Translation:**
Korea's Social Movements at the Start of the New Year, Labor-Farmer Rally in Kyŏngsŏng, Labor-Farmer Alliance in Chinju
						By A Reporter in Chinju

It seems the power of the bourgeoisie, which once overflowed the entire world, can now hardly avoid the misfortune of being at the top of a hundred-foot pole. In the days ahead, divided in all directions, with nowhere to rely on and nowhere to go, hundred out of a hundred each find their path leads to a death trap (死穴), and what they receive is abuse. However, the masses of the proletariat, who have lived unable to die, suppressing the fire-like sorrow and tears in their chests through restraint, solely engaging in slave life, now realize that their means are cooperation (提携) and unity (團結). Through cooperation and unity, under the banner of justice and humanity, they awaken to the need to advance together on the same battlefront. Feeling that there is no need to make meaningless sacrifices from now on, fierce resistance movements are rising up everywhere. In the spirit of supporting these masses, I sincerely congratulate the health of the organizations already established in our society for the masses, and at the same time, I hope that such mass movements will arise through constant cooperation and unity in every village (面面村村). Simultaneously, I wish to urge the self-awareness of the capitalist class, that is, the propertied class (有産者) and the class that defends them (擁護階級). Now, introducing the mass meeting, the first of its kind in all of Korea (全鮮的 初有), held in Chinju at the start of the New Year (setting aside the Kyŏngsŏng matter for now) and its resolutions: The Chinju Labor Mutual Aid Society (晋州 勞働共濟會), Masan Labor-Farmer Friendship Society (馬山勞農同友會), and Pusan Labor Alliance Society (釜山勞働同盟會), three organizations that had been striving in preparation since the end of last year to hold such a meeting, hosted the Kyŏngnam Labor-Farmer Activists' Fellowship Meeting (慶南 勞農運動者 親睦會) at the Kyŏngnam Hall (慶南舘) upper floor in Chinju on the 10th at 7 PM. Many organizations and individuals from Chŏnnam, Kyŏngbuk, Kyŏngsŏng, etc., also attended, totaling over 90 people. As the time came, there was an opening address by Mr. Cho U-che (趙佑濟) from the hosting side, and congratulatory responses from Mr. Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi (徐廷禧, Chairman of the Kwangju Labor Mutual Aid Society Executive Committee) and several other guests. The following day, the 11th, the Kyŏngnam Labor-Farmer Activists' Fellowship Meeting was held at the Chinju Youth Hall (晋州靑年會舘) and continued for three days, passing resolutions as follows (The temporary chairman at this time was Kim Chong-bŏm (金鍾範); Secretaries Kang Ch'i-yŏl (姜致烈),
Pak T'ae-hong (朴台弘),
Pak Pong-ŭi (朴鳳儀)).

Labor Issues
1.  Matter of Investigating Laborer Conditions in Each Region
    Investigate the conditions of laborers in each region.
2.  Matter of Organizing Labor Groups
    Each organization shall assist in organizing groups in nearby regions, and after establishment, strive for mutual connection and federation.
3.  Matter of Different-Stream Labor Groups
    Regarding existing groups that conflict with the fundamental spirit of the labor movement, their content shall be improved or they shall be destroyed.
4.  Matter of Laborer Education
    (a). Hold labor night schools, short courses, lectures, forums, etc.
    (b). Strive for the dissemination of pamphlets (「판프렛트」), leaflets (「리프렛트」), etc., that can promote class consciousness.
    (c). Compile labor textbooks.
    (d). Train lecturers.

Tenant Farming Issues
1.  Matter of Investigating Tenant Farming Conditions in Each Region
    (a). Number of households, population, and cultivated land area of landlords, owner-farmers, and tenant farmers.
    (b). Existing tenant conditions and customs (慣例).
    (c). Details of tenant farmer organizations in the relevant region.
    (d). Annual farming expenses (upper, middle, lower) per tu (斗) fallow of paddy land (畓).
    (e). Obstructors of the tenant farming movement and countermeasures (對策) against them.
2.  Matter of Organizing Tenant Farmer Groups
    (a). Same as labor groups.
    (b). The method shall be to organize tenant groups at the myŏn (面, township) level and establish federated bodies at the kun (郡, county) level.
3.  Matter of Different-Stream Tenant Farmer Groups
    Groups conflicting with the fundamental spirit of the tenant farming movement shall have their content improved or be destroyed.
4.  Tenant Conditions
    (a). Strive to guarantee tenancy rights.
    (b). Tenant fees shall be within 40% (limited to the main crop harvested once a year).
    (c). Prohibit the abuse of unfairly collecting land taxes and public levies (地稅公課金) from tenant farmers.
    (d). For measuring volume (斗量), use a level measure (斗槪).
    (e). Transport of tenant fees shall be within 1 ri (里); for distances beyond 1 ri, appropriate transport fees (運賃) shall be received (受할 일).
    (f). Absolutely refuse unreasonable tenancy contracts demanded by landlords.
    (g). Abolish all miscellaneous abuses (一切 雜弊) related to tenancy (straw (藁草), provisions (供給), measure tax (斗稅), gifts (贈物), resident servant system (舍音制度), etc.).
5.  Matter of Tenant Farmer Education
    Same as the methods for laborer education, but especially strive to break down superstitions and fatalistic views (迷信宿命觀).
6.  Matter of Agricultural Improvement
    Encourage (勵行할 일) improvement of cultivated land, farming tools (農具), threshing methods (打租法), and seed selection.
7.  Matter of Encouraging Side Businesses (副業獎勵)
8.  Matter Concerning Production and Consumer Cooperatives
    Tenant farmer groups shall organize production and consumer cooperatives to strive for the overall improvement of the rural economy, including joint sales of products and joint purchasing of consumer goods.
9.  Matter Concerning Rural Hired Laborers
    Plan (圖하고) the unity of farmhands ("머음") and day laborers (日雇), improve their wages (賃金) and treatment, and ensure smooth relations with tenant farmers.
10. Matter Concerning County Agricultural Associations (郡農會)
    (a). Ensure County Agricultural Associations focus on the interests of tenant farmers.
    (b). Absolutely reject (排斥할 일) County Agricultural Associations that defend the interests of landlords.
11. Matter Concerning the Mutual Relationship between Laborers and Farmers (勞農民)
    Laborers and tenant farmers shall cooperate (互相提携) to align their steps in the liberation movement of their own class.
12. Matter Concerning the Oriental Development Company (東洋拓殖會社)
    (a). Work towards the abolition of the emigration system (移民制).
    (b). Work towards the abolition of farm supervisors (農監).

Youth Issues
1.  Matter of Organizing Youth Groups
    Organize youth groups aiming for mass liberation.
2.  Matter of Youth Education
    (a). Encourage the reading (講讀) of books, newspapers, magazines, etc., necessary for promoting class consciousness.
    (b). Hold lectures, study groups (硏究), courses, etc.
3.  Matter of Different-Stream Youth Groups
    Regarding youth groups that conflict with the fundamental spirit of the liberation movement, their content shall be improved (改選) or they shall be destroyed.

Women's Issues
1.  Matter of Organizing Women's Groups
    Organize women's groups aiming for women's liberation.
2.  Matter of Women's Education
    (a). Hold night schools, short courses, study groups, lectures, etc.
    (b). Make women aware of their current standing regarding human rights and the misery of their economic life, elevate the ideology of women's XX (censored?), and break down religious and superstitious notions.

Issue of Organizing a Federated Body for Labor-Farmer Groups
1.  Matter of Organizing the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance (南鮮勞農同盟)
    Decided to be discussed at the formal meeting of representatives of South Chosŏn labor groups.

Special Issues
1.  Matter Concerning the Relationship between the Labor-Farmer Movement and the Hyŏngp'yŏng Movement (衡平運動)
    Strive to make laborers and farmers understand the fundamental spirit of the Hyŏngp'yŏng movement.
2.  Matter Concerning the Killing of Korean Laborers during the Tokyo Earthquake
    -Redacted- (削)
3.  Countermeasures Regarding Reports on Special Local Issues
    Regarding the issue of 부정 衡器 (improper scales/measuring devices) usage by Shimizu Satarō (淸水佐太郞), the designated exclusive buyer (買占人) for the Kyŏngnam Cotton Joint Sale (慶南 棉花 共同販賣指定), send a letter of encouragement (激勵文) to the Chinju citizens' rally to strive for the achievement of its goal. If a satisfactory resolution cannot be obtained (不得할 時) at said rally, the federated body of labor-farmer groups shall devise appropriate countermeasures.
4.  Matter of Organizing a Traveling Lecture Troupe (巡廻講演團)
    Postpone until the federated body of labor-farmer groups is established.
5.  Matter Concerning the Dong-A Ilbo (東亞日報)
    (a). Censure (問責할 일) its biased reporting that ignores the mass movement.
    (b). Demand it retract its declaration of being the "expression organ of the 20 million populace."
    (c). After questioning (質問) the improper handling of comfort funds for overseas compatriots, decide on countermeasures.
    (These issues shall be delegated to the federated body of labor-farmer groups for execution.)
6.  Matter Concerning the Situation of the All-Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Movement
    Each group participating in the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Federated Body shall unite and sever all relations (일절 관계를 단절할 일) with existing (旣成), preparing-to-organize, or organization-halted labor-farmer groups and thought groups (思想團體) located in Kyŏngsŏng.

South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Group Representatives' Meeting
On January 14th, the Kyŏngnam Labor-Farmer Group Representatives' Meeting was held at the Chinju Youth Hall, and it was resolved to organize the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance Preparatory Inaugural Committee (南鮮勞農同盟發起準備會). This preparatory committee further decided to hold the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance Inaugural General Meeting (南鮮勞農同盟發起總會). On the spot, it was resolved that:
1.  The General Declaration (總領宣言) and rules of the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance shall be entrusted (일임케 할 일) to a drafting committee of 5 members.
2.  The inaugural general meeting shall be held in Taegu starting from the 25th day of the first lunar month of the Kapcha (甲子) year.
3.  Messrs. Sŏ Chŏng-hŭi (徐廷禧), Chŏng Un-hae (鄭雲海), Cho U-che (趙佑濟), Kim Ki-su (金基洙), Kim Chong-bŏm (金鍾範), and Ch'oe Wŏn-t'aek (崔元澤) were elected as preparatory committee members, entrusted with the preparatory affairs (事務), and the office was decided to be located within the Taegu Labor Mutual Aid Society (大邱 勞働共濟會) at Nisŏng Town 2-chome, Taegu Prefecture (大邱府 西城町 2丁目). The initiating organizations this time encompassed labor-farmer groups from various prefectures and counties (諸府郡) including Samga, Kimhae, Chuch'ŏn, Ch'angwŏn, Masan, Sŏngan, Kwangju, Myŏngch'ŏn, Kwangyang, Kurye, Yŏsu, Taegu, Pusan, T'ongyŏng, Kosŏng, Chinju, Hapch'ŏn, Talsŏng, Hadong, reaching a large number of 93 groups.

**Summary**

This 1924 article reports on the burgeoning labor and farmer (勞農) social movements in Korea, contrasting the perceived decline of the bourgeoisie with the rise of proletarian class consciousness and organization. It focuses on meetings held in Chinju, initiated by labor groups from Chinju, Masan, and Pusan, which led to the Kyŏngnam Labor-Farmer Activists' Fellowship Meeting. This meeting, attended by over 90 representatives from various southern regions, passed detailed resolutions addressing labor conditions, tenant farming rights (rent reduction, abolition of abuses, land rights), agricultural improvement, education for workers and peasants (including combating superstition), organizing youth and women for liberation, and establishing consumer/producer cooperatives. Key decisions included forming a preparatory committee for the South Chosŏn Labor-Farmer Alliance, severing ties with certain Seoul-based groups, criticizing the Dong-A Ilbo newspaper, demanding action regarding the killing of Koreans in the Kantō earthquake aftermath, and supporting local struggles like the protest against unfair cotton purchasing practices. The article highlights the formation of a significant regional alliance aiming to unite 93 groups across southern Korea. For historians, this article provides valuable insight into the early stages of organized socialist-influenced labor and peasant movements in colonial Korea, detailing their specific grievances, demands, organizational strategies (regional federation, exclusion of competing groups), ideological leanings (class struggle, anti-capitalism, liberation), engagement with other social issues (Hyŏngp'yŏng Movement, women's rights), and confrontation with colonial structures (Oriental Development Company) and media (Dong-A Ilbo). The detailed resolutions offer a clear picture of the socio-economic conditions and aspirations of workers and tenant farmers during this period.

전세계에 넘치우든 뿌르즈와의 세력도 이제는 百尺竿頭의 厄運을 難免일 것 갓다. 압날에는 4面8方으로 분리되야 의지할 곳도 업고 갈곳도 업서 백이면 백이 제각금 가는 곳이 死穴이오 밧는 것이 虐待이엿다. 그러나마 죽지 못하야 살량으로 火災갓흔 胸中의 슬음과 눈물을 抑制로 참아가며 오즉 노예생활을 하여오든 무산계급의 대중은, 자기네의 취할 방편이 提携와 단결이오 提携와 단결로써 正義人道의 旗발 알에 일제히 동일한 戰線에 나아가려는 자각에 의하야, 지금부터는 무의미한 희생을 하여갈 필요는 無하다 感하는 동시에 處處에서 맹렬한 반항운동이 일게된다. 나는 이러한 대중을 위하는 의미에 잇서 우리 사회에서 임의 일어난 대중을 위하는 단체의 건강을 誠祝하고 따라 面面村村에서 작구 提携團結하야 이러한 대중운동이 일어나기를 기대하는 동시에 저-자본계급 즉 유산자의 擁護階級과 밋 유산계급의 자각을 促코저 한다. 이제 新年劈頭의 晋州에서(京城事는 且置하고)일어난 全鮮的 初有의 대중회합과 결의사항을 소개하면 작년말부터 이러한 회합을 짓고저 準備에 노력하든 晋州 勞働共濟會, 馬山勞農同友會
