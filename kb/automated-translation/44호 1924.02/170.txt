Magazine Title: 개벽 제44호
Publication Date: 1924년 02월 01일
Article Title: 농부 (Farmer)
Author: 요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

After the rain cleared, the farmer went out to the rice paddy.
The wind came down from the mountain peak
And danced through the steaming field furrows
And the ridges of the paddy.

A black water bird flew from paddy to paddy,
Calling out as if teasing.
That the intense summer sun shines down silently
Is to try and fathom the farmer's heart.

But our farmer, who knows how to wait,
Took a long breath as if boasting,
And now, in the frighteningly green, wide field,
Clearly saw the autumn when golden waves would ripple. <105>

**Summary**

This poem depicts a farmer returning to the fields after rain. Despite the teasing calls of birds and the silent, intense scrutiny of the summer sun, the farmer remains patient and resilient. Taking a deep breath, the farmer confidently envisions the future autumn harvest, seeing golden waves rippling across the currently green fields. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's emphasis on the farmer's endurance, patience, and unwavering hope for a future bounty (autumn) despite present hardships (summer sun, vast green fields) could symbolize the Korean people's resilience and aspirations for a better future under challenging colonial circumstances. Historians might find the simple yet profound portrayal of hope and connection to the land significant during this period.
