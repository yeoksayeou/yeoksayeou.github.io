Gradually, Gradually, Gradually Becoming Strange: Chosŏn's Cultural Movement

The issue has rolled around and around. From the independence movement to the cultural movement, from the cultural movement to the social movement, the issue has thus rolled around and around.

However, to say that one movement has rolled into another like this does not mean that the previous movement has ceased simply because the other new movement arose. Therefore, among the Chosŏn people today (speaking of both within and outside the borders), one can see that three forms of effort—the XX movement, the cultural movement, and the social movement—are being carried out.

Just as the proverb says there's a distinction between long and short fingers born on the same day at the same time, when many people gather over many days to carry out any task, it's not necessarily the case that people's minds or their surrounding environment will be exactly the same. Unable to escape this principle, among us people carrying out these various movements, factions have naturally formed.

Shall we call them the Hard-line Faction (硬派) and the Soft-line Faction (軟派), or the Radical Faction (急進派) and the Moderate Faction (穩健派)? In any case, factions that seem similar yet are not exactly the same have come into being. This exists within the independence movement and also within the cultural movement, extending to the social movement. Because of this—the factions—those movements have gradually, gradually, gradually changed their form (and perhaps even their content) as time has passed. It wasn't necessarily meant to be this way, but anyway, it turned out like this.

Look at the facts rather than theory, first from the side of the independence movement. Among them, the Hard-line Faction turned towards the path of social XX or armed XX, while the Soft-line Faction turned towards nurturing practical strength (실력양성), in other words, towards the cultural movement. Next, what about the cultural movement? Over the past two or three years, this too—the Hard-line Faction has turned towards the social movement (the "social movement" mentioned here includes the recent labor movement or even movements one step further) or reverted to XXXXism. As for the social movement, it's still <2> impossible to say anything definite, but this too shows signs of forming some factions in the not-too-distant tomorrow.

However, among these, what becomes the biggest issue and what we think we must pay special attention to are the cultural movement proponents within Chosŏn proper today—in other words, those who have not yet turned to the social movement, nor reverted to armed XX-ism, but are solely emphasizing the doctrine of nurturing practical strength (實力養成主義).

They (the Soft-line Faction or Old Faction within the cultural movement) invariably advocate for "national unity" (민족일치) and "grand unity" (대동단결) for us Chosŏn people. Entering this year, 1924, their assertions have become gradually, gradually, gradually clearer, bringing up things like India's national assembly (in other words, a Chosŏn assembly) or the Philippines' petition issue for independence. That Provincial Citizen Assemblies (道民大會) were held in several local areas last year, that the elections for Municipal/Township Councilors (府面協議員) in various places became more vigorous, and that articles about the Provincial Councils (各道 道評議會) in each province were reported with particular prominence—are these not all precursors to the evolution of this doctrine of nurturing practical strength? Is this all? They say they promote domestic products (토산) and thus join hands with the propertied class (有産階級); they say they disseminate education and thus hasten connections with liberals (自由主義者) (spoken in contrast to socialists); they say they expand human rights and thus frequently visit government offices (관청출입)—their actions closely resemble the deeds of a privileged class in some country. In other words, they are engaging in actions that seem like a kind of check, no, a reactionary movement, against the social movement just arising over there. Whether the individuals at its core all act with such consciousness and purpose is unclear, but anyway, it is a fact that it appears so, and it seems doubtless that it will eventually become so.

Anyway, the recent state of the cultural movement (in other words, the practical strength movement) is becoming somewhat peculiar. Such sprouts caught the eye starting early last year, and coming into this new year, they seem to have become quite conspicuous. Whether this turn of events is truly a good thing or a wrong thing is a separate issue; for now, I record this much to urge general attention. <3>

**Summary**

The article critiques the evolution of Korean nationalist movements, tracing a path from independence activism to cultural movements, and then to social movements. The author focuses critically on a faction within the cultural movement—the moderates or "Soft-line Faction"—who emphasize gradual "nurturing of practical strength" (실력양성). The author observes this group advocating for unity and referencing examples like Indian and Philippine autonomy efforts, while simultaneously aligning with the propertied class, liberals (vs. socialists), and engaging with the colonial authorities. The author views these actions with suspicion, suggesting they are becoming conservative, resembling the actions of a privileged class, and potentially acting as a reactionary force against the emerging socialist/labor movement. For historians, the article offers valuable insight into the ideological fragmentation within the Korean colonial-era movements, particularly the tension between gradualist cultural nationalism and more radical social/socialist activism in the early 1920s, and the critique of the former by proponents (or observers) of the latter.

漸漸漸 異常해가는 朝鮮의 文化運動
문제는 디굴디굴 굴엇다. 獨立運動으로부터 문화운동에, 文化運動으로부터 社會運動에, 문제는 이러케 디굴디굴 굴엇다.
그러나 이와 가티, 한 운동이 다른 한 운동으로 구을넛다 함은, 그 다른 한 운동이 새로 생겻슴으로 해서, 그전의 엇던 운동이 쉬여버렷다는 것은 아니다. 따라서 오늘의 朝鮮사람 새에는 (境內境外를 通해서 말이다) XX운동, 文化運動, 社會運動의 세 모양의 노력이 行해 잇슴을 알 것이다.
한날 한시에 난 손까락에도 길고 짤은 구별이 잇다는 속담도 잇거니와, 무슨 일이나 여러 사람이 모여서 여러 날을 두고 해 나아가는 거긔에는 사람들의 마음이나 또는 그 周圍의 환경이라던가, 그러케 꼭 가트라는 법은 업는 것이다. 이 이치에 버서나지 못하야 이 各樣의 운동을 하는 우리 사람들 중에도 스스로, 派가 잇게 되엿다.
硬派, 軟派라 할가, 急進派, 穩健派라 할가, 엇더튼, 가튼 듯 하면서도 꼭 갓지는 아니한 派流가 잇게 되엿다. 이것은 獨立運動의 속에도 잇고 文化運動 내지 社會運動의 속에도 잇다. 이것-派流-이 잇슴으로 해서 그 운동들은, 時日의 흘너감과 가티, 漸漸漸 형태(혹은 내용까지도)를 달리하게 되엿다. 반듯이 그래야 쓴다는 것도 아니나, 엇잿던 그러케 되고 보앗다.
論보다도 사실, 爲先 獨立運動˜ 편에서 보아라, 그중의 硬派는 사회XX이나 武力XX의 길노 돌아지고, 軟派는 실력양성, 다시 말하면 문화운동의 편으로 돌아섯다. 다음, 文化運動은 엇던가, 이 亦 兩3년을 지내오는 동안— 硬派는 社會運動(여긔에서 말하는 「社會運動」이라함은, 근래의 勞働運動, 또는 여긔에서 一步를 進한 운동까지를 포함함)으로 돌아지거나, 또는 XXXX主義로 還元햇스며, 社會運動에 잇서는, 아직＜2＞ 무엇이라 말할 수가 업스나, 이 亦 멀지아는 明日에, 엇던 派流가 생길 형세를 보이며 잇다.
그런대, 이중에서 가장 문제가 되는 동시에, 우리로서는 특별히 注視치 안하서는 안되겟다고 생각하는 것은, 오늘 朝鮮內地에 잇는 文化運動者, 다시말하면 아직 社會運動으로 돌아서지 안코, 또는 武力XX主義로 환원치 안코, 오즉 實力養成主義를 高調하고 잇는 그네들이다.
그네들(文化運動者중의 軟派 혹은 舊派)은 言必稱, 우리 朝鮮사람으로서의 「민족일치」, 「대동단결」을 주창한다. 1924년인 금년에 드러와서는, 그 主張이 漸漸漸 분명해저서, 印度의 국민의회(밧구아 말하면 朝鮮議會), 菲律賓의 獨立請願問題가튼 것을 꺼들어 낸다. 작년내로 지방의 몃군대에 道民大會가 열리고, 각지의 府面協議員의 선거가 一層活潑히 되고, 各道 道評議會의 기사가 별로 유력하게 보도됨과 가튼 것은, 모다 이 實力養成主義의 진화의 豫示가 아닌지, 이다뿐이냐, 그네는 토산을 장려한다 하야 有産階級과 더블어 손을 잡고, 교육을 보급한다 하야 自由主義者(社會主義者에 대비해서 하는 말)와의 연결을 급히 하고, 인권을 擴張한다하야 관청출입을 頻煩히 하는 等, 그 행동은 恰然히, 엇던 나라의 특권계급의 所爲에 彷彿하다. 다시말하면, 뎌한 편에서 금방 일어나는 社會運動에 대한, 一種의 견제, 아니 反動運動과 가튼 행동을 하고 잇다. 그 축의 개개인이, 다-가티 그러한 의식을 가지고 그러한 일을 하는 것인지, 그것까지는 분명치 못한 일이나, 엇잿던, 그러케 보여지는 것은 사실이며, 또 결국 그러케 될 것도 의심업슬 일일 것 갓다.

엇잿던, 文化運動 (달리 말하면 實力運動)의 최근 형세란, 좀 別하게 된다. 작년초부터 그러한 싹이 눈에 씌우더니, 이 새해에 와서는, 퍽 現著해 지는 것 갓다. 이러케 되는 것이, 정말로 조흔 일이냐 또는 그른 일이냐 하는 것은 별문제이요, 爲先 이만큼 기록하야써, 一般의 주시를 촉한다.＜3＞
