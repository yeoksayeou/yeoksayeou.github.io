Magazine Title: 개벽 제44호
Publication Date: 1924년 02월 01일
Article Title: 그게 누구인가. (Who is it.)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Who is it.
						Kim Sŏk-song

Who is it, the one calling me from there?
Are you a typesetter (植字人), a type picker (探字人), a compositor (整版人)?
Ah! Are you a newspaper company worker (職工), an editor-in-chief?
With type sizes Six, Five, Four, Two, One, Initial, Special,
To represent all the conflicts of Good and Evil on earth,
Are you the person busily wielding a brush from morning till night?
Are you the person handling lumps of lead, the person turning the wheel?
Ah! Who is it? What are you doing now?

Who is it, the one calling me from there?
Is it the last train's steam whistle (汽笛)? A locomotive fireman (火夫)? Someone about to be run over by a train?
Is it a passenger departing far away, leaving a beloved one?
Is it a passenger arriving in a coffin, having died in a foreign land?
Ah! Who is it? What are you doing now? <114>

Are you a beggar (乞人)? A self-supporting student? A porter with an A-frame? A carter (車夫)?
Are you a prostitute (娼妓)? A prostitute (賣淫女)? A female student? A housewife?
Are you propertyless (무산자)? Properited (유산자)? Independence faction (독립파)? Revolutionary faction (혁명파)?
Are you learned (유식자)? Unlearned (무식자)? Disabled (병신)? Healthy (건강자)?
Ah! Who on earth are you, the one calling me?

Are you the sky (하눌)? The earth? A bird? A butterfly (나뷔)? A star? The sea?
Ah! Why are you calling me, until I dream?

All races, all creeds, lives, and concepts of the past, present, future…
Ah! Why are you calling me, until I dream?

Who is it, coming before my window (창압헤) and calling me?
Why (웨) do you not answer? Are you a ghost? The wind?

No one is calling. Yet, still, it calls.
From beneath (밋헤서) your ribs (갈비ㅅ대), it persistently calls in a loud voice, a soft voice.
Everything is good, everything is evil.
However, there is (잇다) only (오즉) one special Good, that is—
Life’s purpose (生의 旨定). The praise of growth (生長의 讚美). <115>
<114-115>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏk-song repeatedly asks "Who is it?" calling the speaker, listing a vast array of people from different occupations (printers, train workers, beggars, prostitutes), social classes (propertied/propertyless), political affiliations (independence/revolutionary), physical states (disabled/healthy), and even natural elements. The speaker feels called by all aspects of existence, past, present, and future. Ultimately, the poem concludes that the call originates from within, from beneath the speaker's own ribs, asserting a fundamental truth beyond good and evil: the inherent purpose and value found in life itself and its growth. For historians, the poem vividly reflects the complex social fabric of 1920s colonial Korea, mentioning specific modern occupations alongside traditional ones, acknowledging stark social divisions and political currents (independence vs. revolution). It captures a sense of existential questioning amidst this rapidly changing and stratified society, suggesting a search for deeper meaning beyond external identities.

그게 누구인가.
						金石松
그게 누구인가, 거기서 나를 부르는 이가,
그대는 植字人인가, 探字人인가, 整版人인가,
아! 신문사의 職工인가, 편집장인가,
六號로부터 五號 四號 二號 一號 初號 特號의 활자로,
모든 지상의 善과 惡의 갈등을 재현하기 위하야
아츰부터 밤까지, 분주히 붓을 놀니는 사람인가,
납덩이를 만지는 사람인가, 박휘를 돌니는 사람인가,
아! 그게 누구인가, 그대들은 지금 무엇을 하는가.

그게 누구인가, 거기서 나를 부르는 이가,
막차의 汽笛인가, 기관차의 火夫인가, 차에 치어 죽게된 사람인가,
정든 님을 이별하고 멀니가는 승객인가,
타향에서 죽은 몸이 관속에 누어오는 승객인가,
아! 그게 누구인가, 그대들은 지금 무엇을 하는가.＜114＞

그대는 乞人인가, 고학생인가, 지게군인가, 車夫인가,
그대는 娼妓인가 賣淫女인가, 여학생인가, 주부인가,
그대는 무산자인가, 유산자인가, 독립파인가, 혁명파인가,
그대는 유식자인가, 무식자인가, 병신인가, 건강자인가,
아! 나를 부르는 그대는 도모지 누구인가,

그대는 하눌인가, 땅인가, 새인가, 나뷔인가, 별인가, 바다인가,
아! 그대들은 엇지하야 나를 부르는가, 내가 꿈꿀때까지.

과거, 현재, 미래의 모든 종족, 모든 信條와 생활과 개념…
아! 그대들은 엇지하야 나를 부르는가, 내가 꿈꿀때까지.

그게 누구인가, 내 창압헤와서 나를 부르는 이가,
웨 대답을 하지 아니하는가, 그대는 유령인가, 바람인가.

아모도 부르는 이는 업다, 그러나 지금도 부른다,
너의 갈비ㅅ대 밋헤서 큰소리 적은 소리로 꾸준히 부른다,
모든 것은 선이다, 모든 것은 악이다,
그러나, 오즉 한가지 특별한 善이잇다, 그것은-
生의 旨定이다, 生長의 讚美이다.＜115＞
