Special Company Announcement

Our company (本社), which publishes many publications (出版物) each month, has intended for some time to unify the orthography according to the "Hangŭl Unification Proposal" (한글 統一案) regarding characters (文字), but as this was not promptly achieved, we felt regret towards our esteemed readers (讀者 諸氏). Now, since publishing the *Kaebyŏk* (開闢) magazine, preparations have been made to a certain extent, and we have already begun implementation starting with this month's issue (今月號). Henceforth, we will make special efforts regarding the spelling system (綴字法), and we especially hope that esteemed contributors and submitters (寄稿 及 投稿하시는 諸位) will particularly understand this point and write according to the Hangŭl unification.

Kaebyŏksa Editorial Department<13>

**Summary**

This brief announcement from the Kaebyŏksa Editorial Department informs readers that, starting with the December 1934 issue of *Kaebyŏk* magazine, the publisher will adopt the "Hangŭl Unification Proposal" (likely the 1933 standard) for its publications. They apologize for the delay in implementation and request contributors to also adhere to this new standard. From a historical perspective, this notice is significant as it reflects the active efforts towards standardizing the Korean language orthography during the colonial period, amidst Japanese assimilation policies. It shows a private publishing house participating in the nationalist linguistic movement spearheaded by organizations like the Korean Language Society.
