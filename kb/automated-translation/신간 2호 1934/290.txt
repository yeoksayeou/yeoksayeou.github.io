**Body Text Translation:**
Shanghai Zion Association's Jewish Nation-Building Movement
From the Russia-Manchuria Border Area

Known from early on as God's chosen people, the Jewish people, having left their own country, wander the world, scattered everywhere. Despite producing great men, sick people [Note: context suggests this might be a typo or archaic term, possibly intended as 'scholars' or 'sages', especially given surrounding asterisks indicating missing text], wealthy people, and ****, attracting the world's attention***, wherever they went, they were persecuted as a different people (異民族). This persecution intensified after Germany (獨逸) became the land of the Nazis, and the Jews there began to be expelled and abused, to the point where even a world-renowned scholar like Einstein reached a state of miserable wandering.

Consequently, their nation-building movement (建國運動) has become fierce. The Shanghai Zion (시온) Association, the headquarters for Far Eastern Jewry, has become the main force behind the movement to build a Jewish state in the Far East and has begun active efforts.

Thus, plans and movements have arisen to establish their ideal state in the plains of Siberia and Manchuria. Opportunely, there is news that an autonomous state (自治國) centered on Jews is being established in the Birobidzhan region, located in the Russia-Manchuria border (露滿國境) area, centered around the Jews in Soviet Russia. However, because the Jews believe in Judaism (猶太敎), and Soviet Russia does not recognize (是認) their religion, the issue is said to not be simple. Indeed, it is a great question as to what the fate of their nation-building movement will be. <89>

**Summary**

This 1934 article reports on the intensified Jewish nation-building movement (Zionism) spurred by increased persecution, particularly Nazi actions in Germany which affected even figures like Einstein. It highlights the Shanghai Zion Association as the center of this movement in the Far East and discusses plans to establish a Jewish state, specifically mentioning the news of a Soviet-backed Jewish Autonomous Oblast forming in Birobidzhan near the Manchurian border. However, it raises doubts about the project's success due to the conflict between Jewish religious beliefs and Soviet atheism. For historians of Korean colonial history, the article is interesting as it shows Korean awareness of international affairs, particularly the plight of diasporic peoples and nationalist movements, potentially resonating with Korea's own situation under Japanese rule. The focus on Birobidzhan, near Manchuria where many Koreans also lived, highlights the complex geopolitical landscape of Northeast Asia at the time.

上海시온會의 猶太人建國運動
						露滿國境地帶에서
일즉붙어 하누님의 택한 민족으로, 자기 나라를 떠나 세계로 유랑에 길을 떠나 돌아 다니는 유태민족은 각각 산재한 그대로 위인과 환자와 부호와 **** 생겨 세계의 이목을 ***
하엿으나 가는 곳마다 이민족(異民族)이라 하야 구박을 받어 오든 중 더욱이 독일(獨逸)이 「나치스」의 천하가 된 후로 그 곳에 잇는 유대인들은 모다 쫓기여나며 학대하기 시작해서 세계적 학자인 「아인스타인」같은 사람도 유랑의 참담한 지에 이르렷든 것이다.
그러므로 그들의 근국운동(建國運動)은 맹열하여졋다. 극동유태인의 총본부인 상해** 「시온」회는 극동유대국 건설운동의 주력이 되여 적극적 운동을 개시하고 있다.
그리하야 시베리아 만주 뜰에서 그들의 이상국을 세워볼가 하는 게획과 운동이 생기엿다. 따마츰 「쏘베트」로시아에 잇는 유대인을 중심으로 로만국경(露滿國境) 지대인 「비로비찬」지방에 유대인 중심으로 자치국(自治國)을 건설한다는 소식이 잇다. 그러나 유대인들은 유대교(敎)를 밋는 관계상 「쏘베트러시아」가 그들의 종교를 시인치 안음으로 문제는 간단하지 안타고. 아니 이 또한 그들의 건국운동의 운명이 어찌될는지 큰 의문이다. ＜89＞
