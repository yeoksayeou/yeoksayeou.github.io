Bait for catching a bonus

Hey, this is too much for me.

Stop it. If you bite, it'll be big trouble. <87>

**Summary**

This cartoon by Kim Kyut'aek depicts a scene where bait labeled "Bonus" is offered, but characters express hesitation and warning, suggesting it's excessive or dangerous to accept. The message implies that bonuses offered during this period might have hidden costs or be traps, possibly reflecting skepticism towards employer incentives or commenting on exploitative labor conditions under colonial rule. For historians, this piece offers insight into social commentary on economic practices and potential worker distrust or precarity in 1930s Korea, conveyed through the accessible medium of a cartoon.
