Overview of the Current Political Situation in Extraordinary Times, Achievements of the Okada Cabinet in Extraordinary Times, The Current State of the Chinese Stage like the Three Kingdoms
						Hong Seung-gi (洪承耆)

1. The Fire Brigade Amidst the Great Fire: The May 15 Incident and the Saitō Cabinet
Everyone was talking about it; it was truly an extraordinary time for the nation, fraught with internal and external troubles. Cannon fire continued in Manchuria (滿洲) and Shanghai (上海), the gold embargo was reimposed, finances were in the red, unemployment increased, the masses faced hardships, the Diet was dissolved, there was a general election for the House of Representatives, the League of Nations investigation team came and went – Tōkyō's political sea truly presented a turbulent and spectacular scene. Former Finance Minister Inoue Junnosuke (井上準元助) was shot down by a young man's bullet while rushing to support a Minseitō (民政黨) candidate's political speech meeting. Baron Dan Takuma (圍琢磨), a pillar of the Mitsui Kingdom and a leader in the financial world, was assassinated in broad daylight. Following this, on May 15, Shōwa 7 (1932), the great Prime Minister Inukai Tsuyoshi (犬養毅) shed blue blood at the official residence. Simultaneously, the Metropolitan Police Department, party headquarters, and major banks were attacked with bombs. Many elder statesmen, senior officials, and prominent figures such as Prince Saionji (西園寺公爵), Makino Nobuaki (牧野仲顯), Wakatsuki Reijirō (若棙禮次郞), Tokonami Takejirō (床次竹二郞), and Shidehara Kijūrō (幣原喜金郞) were marked on the assassin's death list (閻魔帳) and narrowly escaped with their lives. The frantic suppression by military police, the shocked and trembling political and financial circles – it was literally a state of chaos and fear. Soon, the elder statesman Saionji, tasked with recommending the succeeding cabinet to handle the situation, arrived in the capital from Okitsu. The comings and goings of senior statesmen became more frequent, creating a strange tension for a week. It is fresh in our memory how Admiral Saitō Makoto (齊藤實), a retired naval officer and former Governor-General of Chosŏn (朝鮮總督), known from the "Namiko" (『川島良子』) character in Tokutomi Roka's (德富蘆花) *Hototogisu* (不如歸), who was leisurely spending his quiet days on the Zushi coast, emerged at the age of 75, his white hair flying, pulling out his old friends Takahashi Korekiyo (高橋是淸) and Yamamoto Tatsuo (山本達雄), and登場ed in a three-legged race style to exert efforts in firefighting. Over the following two-plus years, this cabinet managed four Diet sessions (special, extraordinary, and ordinary), passing enormous budgets exceeding 2 billion yen each time. While replacing five cabinet members, it internally implemented measures such as securing the status of officials, consolidating the iron manufacturing industry, strengthening the Army and Navy, and reforming the election law. Externally, it undertook major actions like recognizing Manchukuo (滿洲國) and concluding a mutual defense agreement <14>, and withdrawing from the League of Nations (聯盟). Looking at each individual action, critical evaluation of gains and losses must still be reserved, but in any case, for an old cabinet whose backs (허러 - 腰) were bent like bows (弓子形), its vigor was quite impressive. Unfortunately, due to the exposure of two or three cabinet ministers implicated in disciplinary scandals and a group of reprobates lurking within the Ministry of Finance (大藏省), even the old Prime Minister Saitō, exceptionally skilled at enduring shame and bearing burdens, could not save face and made a regrettable retreat. Otherwise, this fortunate and thick-skinned old man would likely not have enjoyed stepping down from the Prime Minister's chair.

2. A Third-Rate Extension Cabinet: Mr. Okada, Unsuited for Extraordinary Times
When the Saitō Cabinet resigned en masse in early June, the political world tensed again with numerous self-nominated and recommended candidates for Prime Minister emerging. However, the outgoing Prime Minister had already recommended his chosen successor and obtained the elder statesman's consent. That person was, needless to say, the current Prime Minister, Admiral Okada Keisuke (岡田啓介). Admiral Okada, like the venerable Saitō, is an old naval veteran, but in terms of age and experience, he is far junior, having once served under Saitō. It is said they share many similarities in speech, action, and personality, having understood each other for a long time. Furthermore, Mr. Okada received the recommendation from his great senior Saitō, having acknowledged Saitō's faults, and shook hands with Gotō [Fumio] (後藤), the Chief Cabinet Secretary left behind by Saitō, forming what is essentially an extension cabinet, different in name but similar in substance, following a "stick to the established rules" (一遵何約束) approach. Despite this, what has the current cabinet been doing for the five-plus months since its formation? Apart from the issue of reorganizing the administrative structure in Manchuria (在滿行政機構改組問題), which caused enough conflict and friction to threaten a political crisis for a time, and sending representatives to the preliminary talks for the Japan-UK-US naval disarmament conference currently underway in London (倫郭 - 倫敦), there is little else worth noting. While handling state affairs cautiously during extraordinary times might be a valid reason, somehow, despite bringing many relatively young actors onto the stage, the script remains conventional, and their actions are clumsy and lack vitality, causing the audience to feel slight boredom. When people say that even the Saitō cabinet, criticized as an "old bones cabinet," wasn't like this, slight yawns and drowsiness occur. Diplomatic issues involve counterparts, so unilateral efforts naturally won't lead to easy and swift conclusions. However, with an extraordinary Diet session imminent and the ordinary session following closely, we have already entered the political season, yet not only are the bills to be submitted unprepared, but political maneuvering towards the parties seems not even to have begun. What about the so-called National Policy Council (國策審議會)? What about measures for government-public cooperation? What about rural revitalization and the rice issue? Although the conflict over the administrative structure seems to be calming down with the change of heart of local officials and the appointment of a dedicated Minister of Colonial Affairs (拓相 - 拓務大臣), offering a glimmer of hope for resolution, what about the responsibility for the decline in official discipline (官規弛漫) accompanying the turmoil? What about prestige? Furthermore, the preliminary disarmament talks have already hit snags right from the opening, casting doubt on the future. What are the countermeasures and follow-up plans for this? Counting these issues, not only is the nation of Japan truly in extraordinary times, but the Okada cabinet itself is facing an unprecedented emergency. Yet, it seems to be a commonly held view that it is too ordinary a cabinet for extraordinary <15> times. Even the previous cabinet, despite being called an "old bones cabinet," had Foreign Minister Hirota [Kōki] (康哉外相), a gluttonous old man prone to yawning like a donkey braying, who could bravely shout about "scorched earth diplomacy" (焦土外交). It also had General Araki [Sadao] (荒木大將軍), who circulated lengthy statements about controlling those who sow discord between the military and civilians, and thundered with glaring eyes that anyone opposing the building of cannons and airplanes should be sent to Eihei-ji (永平寺, head temple of the Sōtō Zen sect) for meditation, thus somewhat strengthening public resolve. Now, even such people are absent, making both the appearance and substance pitifully weakened and diminished, suppressing interest. To sum it up in one phrase: it is a third-rate cabinet unsuitable for extraordinary times.

3. Increasingly Clumsy Political Party Maneuvering: Cover-ups and Patch-ups are a Disgrace for a Military Man
Although the Okada cabinet is nominally a national unity cabinet (擧國一致), it is in reality a collaborative cabinet where bureaucrats and party politicians coexist. Key posts such as Foreign Affairs, Home Affairs, Finance, Justice, Army, and Navy are all filled by representative officials from those fields. However, regarding political parties, Adachi [Kenzō]'s (安達老) Kokumin Dōmei (國民同盟) is completely distanced. Only two elders, Machida [Chūji] (町田) and Matsuda [Genji] (松田), were snatched from the powerless Minseitō (民政黨), which has taken a liking to dwelling in the corridors. From the large Seiyūkai (政友會) party with over 280 members, the cabinet suffered the humiliation of having its offer to join rejected, barely managing to recruit three unaffiliated individuals – Tokonami [Takejirō] (床次), Yamazaki [Tatsunosuke] (山崎), and Uchida [Nobuya] (內田) – and providing them with five sinecure positions (伴食椅子). The party members squatting in these seats are one thing, but the Okada cabinet, which feels compelled to offer even sinecures to party members falling and scattering like paulownia leaves in the autumn wind, is another. Even before the imperial appointment ceremony (親任式), the cabinet's authority had already plummeted irrecoverably. Since the Okada cabinet's mission is to overcome the national crisis, not to engage in relief work for political parties, why be so preoccupied with picking up the skeletons of these currently despised and powerless party hacks? Rather than courting the Seiyūkai, which, even at this juncture, doesn't know how to settle its past misdeeds and dreams of undeserved political power while quibbling over trivialities, it would be a service to the political world – injecting a sense of refreshing clarity and guiding party members towards regeneration – to resolutely bring down the hammer of dissolution. Expelling President Suzuki [Kisaburō] (鈴木總裁), a model of shamelessness and lack of conviction, and his faction from politics would be a meritorious act. As someone with no particular favor or grudge towards the Seiyūkai, especially President Suzuki, I nonetheless despised the Seiyūkai for its history, stretching back to Hoshi Tōru (星亨) and Hara Takashi (原敬) and more recently Tanaka Giichi (田中) and Tokonami Takejirō. Despite individuals worthy of respect, the party shamelessly engaged in unprincipled alliances and breakups, scrambles for concessions, and factional infighting driven by the pursuit of power, exhibiting behavior akin to Bandit Zhi (盜跖). This became even more blatant and tenacious under Suzuki Kisaburō, whose presumptuous and reckless behavior was both hateful and comical. Furthermore, whether it's the intelligent but incompetent Wakatsuki [Reijirō] (若槻), the sinister and cunning Adachi (安達), or the frivolous and talkative so-called "fighters" of the proletarian parties, not a single party politician seems worthwhile. Be that as it may, after the deaths of Hara Takashi and Katō Takaaki (加藤高明), and the subsequent passing of Hamaguchi Osachi (演口雄幸 - should be 濱口雄幸, Hamaguchi Osachi), political parties have become mere gatherings of foxes and <16> jackals. Relying on this crowd to follow the so-called "normal course of constitutional government" and ensure a smooth succession of power is something that even Japan's emerging bureaucrats would dismiss with a snort. Nevertheless, to be concerned about the allegiance of these individuals, luring them with the bait of Parliamentary Vice-Minister (政務官) posts – described as British-style cookies – and enticing them with seats on the National Policy Council (國策審議會), thereby covering up and patching things over, further corrupting their misguided spirits, is unbecoming of a great prime minister, a military man entrusted with handling extraordinary times.

4. The Issue of Reorganizing the Administrative Structure in Manchuria: Turmoil Subsided, but What About Responsibility?
Prime Minister Okada not only left traces of failure in his political maneuvering with parties but also retained one of the unpleasant legacies of the previous cabinet: the ironclad guarantee of official positions (地位鐵障). This system ensures that once someone becomes an official, unless they develop a tumor (壓症) themselves, no one can touch them. If the wise are valued at a thousand gold pieces and the unworthy son is also valued at a thousand, who would willingly choose diligence over idleness? Since the fear of sudden dismissal (馘首) disappeared, administrative bureaucrats have become full of inertia, shaking their heads and turning their eyes, leading to a pervasive bad trend of arrogant self-importance. No matter how strictly they are instructed on disciplinary rectification and loyal service, how can authority be enforced and orders followed? Consequently, the very first instruction issued by Prime Minister Okada merely troubled the printing bureau's typesetters. The temporary turmoil arising from the issue of the administrative structure in Manchuria (在滿機構問題) led to an unprecedented unstable situation where over ten thousand officials, large and small, under the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務本省) and the Kantō Agency (關東廳管下), abandoned their duties. They issued resolutions, statements, made desperate petitions, and resolutely rejected the government's original plan, stirring up an ominous trend of "subordinates overthrowing superiors" (下剋上) within the bureaucracy. Although the stern stance of the military and the cabinet's decision to adhere firmly to the original plan fortunately prevented a major incident, the fact that the situation calmed down does not erase the reality of disciplinary breakdown or absolve the cabinet of responsibility. Furthermore, Prime Minister Okada initially concurrently held the position of Minister of Colonial Affairs (拓相), dismissing the Ministry's proposal in a cabinet meeting via default judgment (闕席判決), while only seeking compromise between the Foreign Ministry and the military. Military Commander Hishikari [Takashi] (菱刈軍司令官), while holding the important position of Governor of the Kantō Leased Territory (關東長官), never once listened to the appeals of the civilian officials, treating them purely as stepchildren. The military viewed the Kantō Agency staff as if they were rebels or bandits, displaying an attitude that suggested martial law might even be imposed if necessary. None of these actions were praiseworthy. Originally, matters like the organization and structuring of administrative bodies should be handled by the government leadership according to the specific circumstances of the time, with lower officials simply following suit. They are not matters where subordinates should be allowed to argue for or against legislation and regulations, staging such resistance. Whether civilian control takes precedence over military (文主武從) or vice versa, or even if the title of police officer is abolished and replaced with military police (憲兵), those who dislike it have the option to resign. However, for officials large and small to form factions in the very place where maintaining order is paramount, running wild day after day and threatening mass resignation to pressure superiors, is certainly a bizarre phenomenon of official unrest (吏擾) analogous to popular unrest (民擾). When popular unrest occurs, even if the demands are justified, it is suppressed with imprisonment and apologies if not dispersed <17>. Yet, official unrest is treated as commonplace (視若尋常). Not only that, but involved superiors busily seek so-called "understanding" (諒解) in a subservient manner. Even those who implicitly directed and manipulated the situation are excused, claiming their actions stemmed not from selfish motives but from sincere devotion to the state (爲國至誠). They are not only treated leniently but even receive praise akin to commendation, leaving observers dumbfounded. If one insists this is not a breakdown of official discipline (官規紊亂), then Admiral Okada must possess a rare shamelessness for a military man. The lifeblood of governance and law lies in strictness and fairness (嚴正과 공평). Once governance and law lose their lifeblood, authority and public sentiment become impossible to manage. Having lost the strictness and fairness of governance, how will the Okada cabinet maintain public morals and sentiment going forward?

5. The So-Called National Policy Council: Why is this Useless Appendage Necessary?
The Okada cabinet, in a state of severed relations with the Seiyūkai due to the refusal of its offer to join the cabinet, initially tried to have the three ministers led by Tokonami incite their protégés and allies within the party to rebel against the Seiyūkai and form a new party. However, their numbers turned out to be unexpectedly small, comparable to Adachi's faction that broke the Minseitō years ago. Even attempting to use them as internal collaborators to disrupt the Seiyūkai proved difficult, as the alliance of major figures like Suzuki, Mochizuki [Keisuke] (望月), Kuhara [Fusanosuke] (久原), and Yamamoto [Jōtarō] (山本) was surprisingly solid. Apart from those lured away by appointments as parliamentary vice-ministers, there were few positive responses. Consequently, cabinet ministers from party backgrounds, such as Tokonami and Machida, again advised the Prime Minister to establish a National Policy Council (國策審議會). This council would encompass not only the presidents of both the Seiyūkai and Minseitō and one or two of their leading figures, but also elders from the previous cabinet like Saitō, Takahashi, and Yamamoto, as well as other influential figures from government and private sectors, thereby reinforcing the cabinet under the umbrella of national unity. The main objective, of course, was to draw Suzuki and the Seiyūkai into the cabinet's support base. However, Mr. Okada had already washed his hands (손(手)을 듸엿슬뿐더러) of negotiations with the Seiyūkai. Moreover, Home Minister Gotō (後藤內相), acting as a de facto deputy prime minister, had bitterly experienced during the previous cabinet how Seiyūkai President Suzuki, despite sending five party members and affiliates – including elders like Takahashi, and trusted members like Mitsuchi [Chūzō] (三土) and Hatoyama [Ichirō] (鳩山), plus Nakajima [Chikuhei] (中島) and Minami [Hiroshi] (南) as a separate contingent – failed to support the cabinet. Instead, Suzuki had engaged in insidious and overt cabinet-wrecking activities using petty and despicable methods and schemes. Therefore, Gotō believed that bringing Mr. Suzuki back in would offer no particular advantage and would instead lead to constant obstruction (掣盯), causing trouble and inconvenience, so he did not agree. However, recently, voices advocating for the downfall of the Okada cabinet have emerged even within the Minseitō. Simultaneously, the movement for Seiyūkai-Minseitō cooperation (政民提携運動) has revived, with a growing trend for parties to return to their proper roles and abandon their peripheral existence (行廊사리). Given this, facing opposition from over 400 members out of the 460-plus seats in the House of Representatives makes the situation and outcome all too clear. Consequently, Ministers Okada and Gotō have reportedly changed their minds and suddenly decided to give serious consideration (深甚한 고려를 費하기로) to establishing the Council. Whether this succeeds remains to be seen. Even if it does succeed, while it might patch up the appearance of national unity (擧國一致) and conceal the compromise (苟合), gathering these tried-and-tested figures is unlikely to miraculously generate any new national policies <18>. Creating a cabinet outside the cabinet will only make the already sluggish current cabinet unnaturally bloated and even more incapable of maneuvering (運身不能). Unless Suzuki undergoes a complete transformation (滫心洗面) and becomes an entirely different person, this will likely become a source of trouble (禍胎) for the cabinet rather than being beneficial. Generally, political party types are like trained hawks (養鷹者): keep them hungry, and they won't refuse to be deployed; but show even the slightest hint of leniency (和한 顔色) or fill their bellies (膓을 재워주면), and they will either fly away (颺去) or bite their master (友噬). Therefore, creatures like these should be kept in zoos surrounded by iron fences (鐵柵), not let loose in the land of freedom. Is this not an extraordinary times cabinet headed by a courageous admiral of yesteryear? If the power-hungry party members unite in opposition, fire the giant cannon of dissolution a second and third time until their faction submits! Did not Yamagata Aritomo (山縣有朋), a Meiji Restoration hero, faced with resistance from the combined forces of Ōkuma [Shigenobu] (大隈) and Itagaki [Taisuke] (板垣), not even propose the "temporary suspension of the constitution" (憲法の一時中止設)? If such measures were considered even in times of peace, how much more so in extraordinary times? However weakened Admiral Okada may be, how can he hesitate and dither, causing the great elder Yamagata in the afterlife to click his tongue (舌)를 차게 헐 것이냐)? Instead of using conciliatory tactics with a cat-coaxing (猫) voice, adopting a gallant, military-like attitude and fighting back squarely and honorably would surely garner much more applause from the audience. Furthermore, if the Seiyūkai and Minseitō had the courage to properly oppose the extraordinary times budget, nearly half of which is allocated to military spending, they would not have degenerated into their current sorry state.

6. Gloomy Preliminary Disarmament Talks: What are the Proposed Three Major Principles?
The final remaining issues are how the notification of abrogation of the Washington Naval Treaty (華府軍縮條約) and the preliminary negotiations for concluding a new treaty will be settled, and whether the diplomatic efforts and corresponding military buildup in preparation for the so-called 1935-36 crisis will succeed or fail. The success or failure of this disarmament treaty truly marks a turning point for world peace. Whether the preliminary talks result in compromise or breakdown will directly have a significant impact on next year's main conference, which is why the eyes of the entire world are now focused on London (倫敦). Upon the opening of the talks, the two representatives, Matsudaira [Tsuneo] (松平) and Yamamoto [Isoroku] (山本), respectively presented Japan's three major principles for disarmament to the British and American representatives. According to what has been heard, these principles are:
1. Establish the principle of national defense equality, ensuring that the people of each nation do not feel threatened regarding their national defense. Consequently, the current Washington and London treaties, which are based on the ratio system contrary to this principle, should be abrogated at an appropriate time.
2. Regarding the above, the new naval arms limitation treaty to be concluded next year should implement overall tonnage limits (緫噸數主義) for national defense, sufficient for each country to defend itself but insufficient to attack others.
3. Consequently, offensive armaments such as capital ships (主力艦), heavy cruisers (大巡洋艦), and aircraft carriers (航空母艦) should be completely abolished or significantly reduced. However, submarines (潛水艦), being defensive armaments, should be excluded from this.
To put it more simply, Japan argues that the Washington and London treaties, established according to the wishes of the UK and the US, cause anxiety among the Japanese people and are thus unacceptable. A new treaty must impose limitations to a degree where the Japanese people do not feel insecure about their national defense <19>. If each nation is satisfied with a naval strength that allows defense but not offense (能守, 不能攻), that itself will maintain world peace. In response, the British and American sides, determined to maintain the existing ratio system (比率主義) and preserve their own security and sense of superiority, turned their heads away. They described Japan's proposed principles as throwing a bomb at world disarmament and implicitly showed their disapproval of the new principles by asking why Japan had expressed satisfaction with the Washington and London treaties in the past. Amidst this, Britain, compared to the US, shows some inclination to approach Japan more closely, seemingly willing to mediate depending on the circumstances, maintaining silence and avoiding criticism as much as possible, which is a noteworthy fact.

7. Origin and Content of the Washington Treaty: Japan's National Situation at the Time of Acceptance
To explain the issue of arms reduction somewhat colloquially, it can aptly be described as a "reduced eating alliance conference" among the wealthy. Weak nations, the poor, have nothing to limit even if they wanted to, and following the wealthy in reducing intake would only lead to starvation with no benefit. Therefore, the world's minor powers and destitute nations, both tangible and intangible, raise their hands (手) in applause and support for the arms reduction among the three major naval powers: Japan, Britain, and the US. Second-tier naval powers like France (佛) and Italy (伊), belonging to the middle class between rich and poor, have been vacillating between the two sides. Looking at the relationships among the three major naval powers, before the US, a newly emerging wealthy nation, Germany (獨逸), entered the world stage at the end of the 19th century with the rising sun's sharp momentum. However, it found that Britain, the old naval emporium nicknamed the "Great Pirate King" (海賊大王), had already set up "No Trespassing" signs and erected fences and barbed wire in every corner of the world, leaving no room for entry. Only the Chinese market remained, where Britain had already begun, but not fully secured its grip. However, Germany was not the only one eyeing China; Russia (露國) came before, and the US came after. These three nations competed with the established powers, Britain and France (佛), in the East. When Japan finally rose with bold ambitions, Britain was already cornered and besieged by these four powers. Britain, always calm, cunning, practical, calculating, thinking ponderously but acting nimbly like an old snake (老蛇), made profound observations and judgments about the future world political situation with its sharp diplomatic insight. As a result, it first planned to unite Japan and the US to defeat Russia and Germany, and then sought to befriend the US to restrain Japan. This diplomatic strategy designed by Britain succeeded step by step as anticipated. International relations over the past 30-plus years have largely involved the actors of the US, Japan, and France (芋 - should be 佛?) performing the script written by Britain. Until the recent World War, the cooperation among the three nations proceeded relatively smoothly. Moreover, Japan, the world's latecomer, served as Britain's loyal watchdog (番犬) in Asia through the Anglo-Japanese Alliance, while also upholding the principles of the Open Door Policy and equal opportunity in China advocated by the US. However, once the war ended and Japan's power suddenly surged, establishing itself as a <20> new wealthy power in its own right, and its 8-8 Fleet (8.8艦隊) naval expansion plan gradually neared completion, the US and Britain could no longer rest easy. Consequently, the US persuaded Britain to destroy Japan's 8-8 Fleet before its completion, which resulted in the so-called Washington Naval Treaty (華府軍縮條約), the wealthy nations' "reduced eating alliance." This proposed limiting the capital ships of the three countries to a ratio of 5:5:3. This proposal faced strong opposition not only from the Japanese military but also from political parties. The Kenseikai (憲政會), the predecessor of the Minseitō, then led by Katō Takaaki (加藤高明), vehemently opposed it, crying out that the 8-8 Fleet represented Japan's minimum defense requirement and reducing it meant national suicide. However, at that time, Japan was influenced by the drastic changes in global thought after the war. The rapidly growing power of the masses advocated for "laying down arms and cultivating learning" (偃武修文) as the national policy, and the cry for arms reduction swept the nation. Even without this, Hara Takashi (原敬), president of the Seiyūkai, aimed to contain the power of the military cliques (瀋閥 - should be 藩閥, hanbatsu) and bureaucracy through the strength of party rule. Seizing this opportunity, he conspired with Navy Minister Katō Tomosaburō (加藤友三郞) to accept the 60% ratio relative to the US in this "reduced eating alliance." He persuaded Army Minister Yamanashi Hanzō (山梨半造) to cut 60,000 troops from the standing army, thereby completely expelling the residual influence of the Satsuma and Chōshū cliques (蕯長兩閥) from the fortresses of the Army and Navy. Simultaneously, he had Foreign Minister Uchida Kōsai (內田康哉) declare abroad the abandonment of Shandong (山東) and a policy of non-interference towards China (對支不干涉主義), establishing a new diplomatic policy of international cooperation (國制協調主義) and laying the foundation for following Britain and America's lead in diplomacy. Only the eastward advance of Red Russia (赤露) remained a feared object, so the troop deployment in Siberia (西伯利 出兵) initiated during the Terauchi [Masatake] (寺內) cabinet was continued, maintaining troops in the so-called adjacent regions (接壤地方). Internally, to suppress the rise of the proletariat (無産者), the universal suffrage system (普通選擧制) was not granted. Since public opinion generally aligned with this, opponents had no recourse. Figures like Yamagata Aritomo, Yamamoto Gonnohyōe (山本權兵衛), and Katō Takaaki collectively fell silent and submitted. Katō Takaaki, conversely, emerged at the forefront of public opinion, advocating for withdrawal from Siberia (西伯利 撤兵) and the swift implementation of universal suffrage (普選速施). Following this, Japan entered a decade-long era of party omnipotence (政黨萬能). Even military figures and bureaucrats with ambitions for political advancement had to shed their uniforms and join the parties if they wanted to stand out. Financially astute conglomerates (財閥) manipulated party members with the power of gold and silver (黃白), thereby seizing control over life and death in the political world.

8. The 8-8 Fleet and Japan's Ideals: Mr. Katō Takaaki's Power Diplomacy Theory
Judged fairly, if the Washington and London Treaties, the "reduced eating alliance," deserve credit for maintaining world peace in the past, they also bear the blame for potentially shattering world peace in the future. Why? Because things are inherently unequal if they lack balance, and inequality inevitably leads to complaints (嗚). Britain, intoxicated by the glory of a full stomach (滿腹), fails to understand the mindset of the US, which is not yet satiated. The US, with five (五) bowls laid out before it, fails to understand the mindset of Japan, which is restricted to only three (三) bowls. Furthermore, the US and Japan fail to understand Britain's mindset – glancing right and left (右顧左眄), feigning support while subtly suppressing (陽扶陰抑), and provoking conflict between the two – all to protect its own five bowls. No, <21> they do understand each other's relationships, but the more they understand, the more envy and anger arise between those who have much and those who have little. Therefore, now that Japan has voiced the dissatisfaction it suppressed for a decade and proposed breaking the status quo, unless Britain and the US grant Japan the same five bowls, or unless they themselves become dissatisfied with their five bowls, the future of this issue holds little prospect for harmony and is more likely to end in breakdown. Consequently, the situation will revert to the pre-Washington Conference era, leading to a naval construction race between Japan and the Anglo-American powers. However, the strength of the Japanese Navy lies in its focus on self-defense (自衛), not attacking others (攻他). Unlike the US, which needs transoceanic operational capabilities, Japan has no need for the large battleships and heavy artillery (大艦, 巨砲) of the 8-8 Fleet ideal. Unlike Britain, which has colonies scattered worldwide, large cruisers equipped with eight-inch gun turrets (八吋砲塔) are not crucial either. Besides the 8-8 Fleet concept, simply building more submarines (潛水艦), light cruisers (**洋艦 - likely 輕巡洋艦, light cruisers?), and destroyers (驅逐艦), along with sufficient air defense (防空) facilities, would be perfectly adequate for asserting dominance in the Far East and competing for hegemony over China. Therefore, it is the US, which initiated the naval construction race and even now boasts it wouldn't hesitate to compete, that will eventually back down and propose, "Let's be satisfied with 5:5:3." Regarding Japan's naval construction competition and the building of the 8-8 Fleet, I have heard the following story. When the Russo-Japanese War began, Japan relied on the Anglo-Japanese Alliance, but in reality, American assistance exceeded Britain's. Here too, the contrast between self-interested Britain and the agile and chivalrous (輕快好俠) America reveals their respective characteristics. Among Americans, President [Theodore] Roosevelt (『루스벨트』대통령) at the time strongly supported Japan, guaranteeing its position in the East while intending to use it as an agent for the Open Door Policy in China, serving as an auxiliary force in the competition against Britain for hegemony. Thus, he sent a man named Harriman (『하리만』) to propose a joint venture between the American Transcontinental Railroad and Pacific Mail Steamship companies and the South Manchuria Railway (南滿洲鐵道). The plan was to connect this to the Siberian Railway and further traverse Europe to the Atlantic, creating a round-the-world transportation route (世界一周 循環運路) to demonstrate American power globally. This happened in the summer of Meiji 41 (1908). At that time, Japan's Prime Minister Katsura Tarō (桂太郞) and the elder statesmen (元老) Itō [Hirobumi] (伊藤), Yamagata [Aritomo] (山縣), Matsukata [Masayoshi] (松方), and Inoue [Kaoru] (井上) were lamenting the lack of funds for managing the South Manchuria Railway. They readily accepted this proposal conveying the US President's intentions, and the American envoy crossed the Pacific again in high spirits. However, the day after his departure, Komura Jutarō (小村壽太郞), Japan's renowned modern diplomat who had returned from his post as Ambassador to Britain to become the new Foreign Minister, vehemently opposed the plan. Before the envoy's ship arrived back in San Francisco (桑港), a telegram notifying the cancellation of the agreement by the Japanese government arrived first. The President and others were greatly angered, declaring:
"Japan, remember this! There is something we will show an impudent fellow like you."
From this point on, anti-Japanese movements surged within the US. Furthermore, the "World's Invincible Fleet" (世界無敵艦隊), composed of over 100 ships led by 16 capital ships, visited Japan under the guise of a round-the-world voyage, flexing its muscles (耀威揚武) in Yokohama Bay for several days before departing. The Japanese government mobilized its entire naval <22> fleet to extend the warmest possible welcome, but inwardly they felt like they were swallowing boiling water (熱湯), enduring the humiliation with no other choice.
"Establish a pro-Russian policy (親露策)! Redirect the efforts spent on the army towards the navy. Build the 8-8 Fleet! Our national defense must counter the invasion of America's invincible fleet."
This became the traditional policy of the Japanese government from the end of the Meiji era to the beginning of the Taishō era. Although the cabinet changed hands several times among Katsura, Saionji (四園寺 - should be 西園寺, Saionji), Yamamoto, Ōkuma, and Terauchi, the goal of "Navy: 500,000 tons (capital ships), Army: 25 standing divisions" became common knowledge for Japanese national defense. Plans evolved from 8-4 to 8-6 and 8-8-8 fleets, finally culminating in the "Twelve-Year Plan Budget for the 8-8 Fleet" (8.8艦隊十二年計劃의 예산) established in the special Diet session of July, Taishō 9 (1920). At that time, Japanese officials and civilians, except for a few so-called pacifists singing praises of the League of Nations (國際聯盟), all applauded and cheered. I witnessed an example of this from the Diet floor. Katō Takaaki, leader of the opposition Kenseikai, which had been reduced to a minority party and pushed out of power after successive dissolutions by the Terauchi and Hara cabinets, watched the passage of the naval budget from the gallery of the House of Representatives. Descending to the waiting room, he gathered the party leaders – Kōno [Hironaka] (河野), Wakatsuki [Reijirō] (若槻), Hamaguchi [Osachi] (濱口), Minoura [Katsundo] (箕浦), Adachi [Kenzō] (安達), Shimo-oka [Chūji] (下岡) – and patted the back (등(背)) of Hayami Seiji (早速整爾), who had just returned after delivering a supporting speech that day, saying:
"Excellent work (大殷 勞苦)! With this, Japan's national defense is established, and we can engage in diplomacy backed by real power (實力). People call the Kenseikai the Retrenchment Party (消梀黨) or the Austerity Party (緊縮黨), but that's a misunderstanding of our criticism of lax financial policies and opposition to wasteful government budgets. On matters like this, we should not just approve but actively push (鞭撻) for their establishment. You expressed my intentions perfectly in your speech, so I am truly grateful."
He called an attendant and instructed the Secretary-General, Koizumi Matajirō (小泉 又 次郞), to go to the dining hall and break open some beer for a celebratory toast. The leader of the opposition raising a toast to the passage of the government's budget before even the current cabinet ministers did – given the relationship between Hara and Katō at the time, everyone found it strange (奇怪視). Some malicious newspaper reporter remarked with a smug smile, "Well, once the naval budget is passed and shipbuilding begins, won't Mitsubishi Shipbuilding (三菱造船所) profit? If Mitsubishi makes money, Katō's pockets will have ample funds to send to the Kenseikai. So, Katō, adept at syllogisms, is celebrating because: Shipbuilding -> Mitsubishi -> Military Funds. That's why he's celebrating." A month later, I met Mr. Katō at his villa in Karuizawa (輕井澤) and had the opportunity to listen to his views on world affairs, starting with the Chosŏn problem (祖鮮問題). As someone who had long held pivotal positions of responsibility in diplomacy, he spoke in conceptual terms but avoided touching upon any sensitive details (機微). Also, known as a practical politician in the British style (英國流), he never uttered a single sentence about hypothetical situations or predictions beyond practical matters. However, being naturally serious, taciturn, resolute, and upright (沉重寡黙하고 剛毅固直한), once he started speaking, though simple, he spoke truthfully and taught honestly.
"Japan must survive. I am Japanese. I will shake hands with <23> anyone if it benefits Japan, and I will reject anyone who obstructs Japan for the sake of our survival. However, it is deeply regrettable that the current government, instead of using a pro-Russian policy combined with the naval construction plan to check Britain and America, who are troubling our Japan, is only now establishing a slow 12-year plan for the naval scheme designed since the Ōkuma cabinet. Furthermore, they are still maintaining large forces in Siberia, depleting military strength and national effort. If the diplomatic course (針路) were steady, China (支那) would naturally abandon Britain and America and follow Japan, and Japan's rights and interests in Manchuria (滿洲權益) would be protected by China first. Then, Japan too, for China's sake, would not only take the lead in repealing unequal treaties concerning tariff autonomy (稅權) and extraterritoriality (法權) but also has the readiness and confidence to make other powers follow suit. Until Asia is completely made into an Asia for Asians through Japan's strength, enduring everything is the way for both us and them to survive. This is my ideal, if you can call it that. Perhaps the ideal of the Japanese Empire is also like this. But don't write things like this in the newspaper. I don't know how much trouble (『매이와꾸』 - 迷惑, meiwaku) I've gotten from newspapers."
Mr. Katō's words proved true later. His speech in the House of Peers advocating withdrawal from Siberia, his confrontation with Prime Minister Katō Tomosaburō opposing the Washington Treaty, and his expression of conviction and spirit regarding diplomacy towards China, especially the defense of rights and interests in Manchuria and Mongolia (滿蒙權益擁護論), all demonstrated this. The conclusion of the Soviet-Japanese Basic Convention (日露北京條約) by Yoshizawa [Kenkichi] (芳澤) and Karakhan [Lev Mikhailovich] (카라한), and sending Hioki Eki (日置益), a party involved in the Twenty-One Demands (21개 條約 - should be 二十一箇条要求), to China to make proposals that startled Britain and America at the Beijing Tariff Conference (北京關稅會議), also occurred during his time as Prime Minister. If his spirit exists, he would point to Wakatsuki and Shidehara, who concluded the London Treaty, calling them "unworthy disciples who failed to understand my intentions." He would also grumble, "This is all the fault of Hara and Katō (Tomosaburō) who abandoned the 8-8 Fleet plan to appease popular opinion (俗論), and even more so, the fault of Terauchi who dispatched troops to Siberia." Hearing Mr. Uchida's scorched earth speech (焦土演說), he would likely say, "You shameless rubber doll (內田氏綽名 - Uchida's nickname)! Who started the diplomacy of following Britain and America? What did I teach you when you fled headlong from the Russian capital (露都)? To whom are you complaining about 42 against 1 now, and how can you resent the Soviet Union's attitude? Before shouting about scorched earth diplomacy, quickly complete the coastal defenses (海防)! Until you make Britain and America bow their heads, don't even bring up the China and Soviet issues (中, 蘇問題)." It is clear he would say this. In short, the current opinions of the three ministers – Hirota [Kōki] (廣田), Ōsumi [Mineo] (大角), and Hayashi [Senjūrō] (林) – represent Katō's thinking, which the Minseitō failed to advocate, now preempted by another faction.

9. Conclusion - Whither Goes the World? Beware the Ambition of Sima Shi (司馬氏)!
From what has been said above, though perhaps inadequate and incomplete, the basis and outline of Japan's position on the disarmament issue should be understandable. However, the key to resolving this conference lies not with Japan, nor the US, but with Britain. Britain once used Japan as a loyal watchdog at the gate of the East. Therefore, in past diplomacy, Japan suffered hardships over the China and Soviet issues, while Britain reaped the benefits <24>. However, since abandoning Japan and approaching the US, the US, unlike Japan, does not faithfully look after Britain's interests. Furthermore, possessing equal naval power, the US frequently attempts to surpass its great senior and seize command over the world, while also incurring Japan's wrath, causing Britain to feel significant unease about its position in the East. Pro-American or pro-Japanese (親米乎 親日乎)? This is the conundrum currently vexing British politicians. If it were only about the China-Soviet issue, they might have already abandoned the US and shaken hands with Japan again. However, Britain, burdened with numerous new and old adversaries besides China and the Soviets, such as France (佛), Italy (伊), and Germany (獨), finds itself unable to completely cut ties (手) with the US. Therefore, until it decides definitively between being pro-American or pro-Japanese, it will likely display goodwill equally to both, urging compromise. Comparing this to the Three Kingdoms period in China (中國) – Wei (魏), Wu (吳), and Shu (蜀) – the US is Cao Cao's (曹) Wei, Britain is Sun Quan's (孫) Wu, and Japan is Liu Bei's (劉) Shu. If Sun and Liu ally, they can resist Northern Wei, but conversely, if Sun and Cao unite, Shu is endangered. This is because Liu and Cao are fundamentally incompatible. Looking at these three-way relations again through the lens of modern China, the white powers like Britain, America, the Soviet Union, Germany, France, and Italy are Wei; Japan is Wu; China is Shu; and Manchuria (滿洲) is Jingzhou (荊州), the province contested by the three kingdoms. China's anti-Japanese faction (排日派) is like those urging Liu Bei's eastern campaign (東征), enraged that Sun Quan killed Guan Yu (關羽) and took Jingzhou. Others argue, similar to saying "The national traitor is Cao Cao, not Sun Quan" (國賊曹操換非孫權), but the prevailing sentiment seems to favor the argument of uniting with Cao Pi (曹丕) out of hatred and dispatching troops for a joint campaign. Furthermore, regarding the aforementioned Japan-Britain-US relationship, America's policy of befriending Russia (親露), drawing in Britain (引英), and supporting China (扶中) resembles Cao Pi summoning the Eastern Wu (東吳), Western Rong (西戎), and Southern Man (南蠻) barbarians for a five-pronged invasion of Shu (五路進兵). In that case, the fate of Japan and China, now in the position of Shu, will depend solely on the presence or absence of a Zhuge Liang (諸葛亮). The actors are different, but the stage is China, the old land of the Three Kingdoms. The confrontation of the three kingdoms is one thing, but more importantly, one must not ignore the existence of a certain country positioning itself as the Jin dynasty (晋) of Sima Shi (司馬氏).
(Manuscript dated October 29th) <25>

**Summary**

This 1934 article by Korean author Hong Seung-gi provides a critical analysis of contemporary Japanese politics and international relations. It reviews the turbulent transition from the Saitō cabinet, formed after the May 15 Incident, to the Okada cabinet, which the author deems weak and "third-rate," particularly criticizing its handling of political parties and the Manchurian administrative reorganization crisis. The latter half analyzes the looming naval disarmament negotiations, tracing the history of the Washington Naval Treaty, Japan's past acceptance due to domestic pressures, and its current demand for parity ("national defense equality") based on the legacy of the unfulfilled "8-8 Fleet" plan and Katō Takaaki's realpolitik views. The author compares the complex geopolitical triangle of Japan, Britain, and the US to China's Three Kingdoms period, casting Japan as the vulnerable Shu kingdom and warning against a hidden power (like Sima Yi) waiting to usurp control. For historians, this piece offers a valuable Korean intellectual perspective on the internal weaknesses and external ambitions of the Japanese Empire in the mid-1930s. It reveals a keen awareness of Japanese political infighting, military-civilian tensions, diplomatic strategies surrounding the naval treaties, and the perceived parallels between contemporary power struggles and historical Chinese conflicts, all viewed through the lens of a colonized subject analyzing the colonizer's vulnerabilities and strategies on the world stage.

						洪承耆
1. 大火中의 消防隊 5·15事件과 齊藤內閣
萬口一談으로 대성질호하든 내외 다사 國家非常之秋이엿다. 滿洲와 上海에서는 砲火가 接續되고 金輪出再禁止, 赤子財政, 失業者 增加, 大衆의 生活難, 議會解散, 衆議員議員總選擧, 聯盟調査團의 來往 等等으로 東京의 政海는 실로 波瀾萬丈의 淒壯한 광경을 연출하얏섯다. 前藏相井上準元助가 民政黨 議員候補의 政見披露演說會場에 응원차로 馳至하다가 一靑年의 兇彈에 斃倒하고 三井王國의 棟樑으로 財界의 總帥格이든 男爵 圍琢磨가 白晝에 狙擊卽死 된 뒤를 니여 昭和7년 5월 15일에는 大宰相 犬養毅가 官邸에서 碧血을 뿌리엿다. 그와 동시에 警視廳과 정당본부와 대은행 등이 폭탄의 습격을 밧엇스며 西園寺公爵, 牧野仲顯 若棙禮次郞, 床次竹二郞, 幣原喜金郞 등의 원로중신과 大官名土로서 자객의 閻魔帳에 기명이 되여 요행으로 一命을 支保하게 된 이가 不少하엿다. 혈안이 된 軍警의 彈壓隊, 錯愕戰憟하는 政界와 재벌의 有象無象 문자 그대로 혼란공포의 상태가 示現되엿섯다. 俄而오 時局 수습할 後繼內閣 奏薦의 命을 받은 西園寺 元老가 興津으로부터 入京하며 중신들의 來왕往은 더욱 빈번하야 이상한 緊張禮에 일주간을 보냇다. 德富蘆花의 不如歸에 씨여잇는 『川島良子』로 유명한 逗子海岸에서 悠然히 閑歲月을 보내든 退役海軍大將 原任朝鮮總督 齊藤實 老人이 75세의 白髮을 날니며 老友高橋是淸과 山本達雄을 끄러내여 三人四脚式으로 등장하야 消防에 盡力하든 것은 우리의 기억이 當新한 일이다. 伊來 2년 餘에 이 내각은 특별, 임시, 통상으로 4回의 의회를 치러 20有餘億의 厖大한 豫算을 그 때마다 통과식히고 5人의 閣僚를 교역하면서 안으로는 관리의 신분 보장, 製鐵事業合同, 陸海軍의 충실, 선거법개젊� 등을 行하고 外로는 滿洲國 승인과 밋 攻守 동맹의 議定,＜14＞ 연맹탈되 등의 大擧描를 단행하얏다. 그 行한 바의 사건 個個를 보면 利害得失의 비평은 아즉 보류하는 수 박게 업스나 하여튼지 허러(腰)가 弓子形으로 되여 가는 老骨內閣으로는 氣勢가 자못 비장하얏섯다. 불행히 綱紀紊亂의 죄명 指疑잇는 2,3 閣僚와 大藏省內에 蟠居하얏든 醜類의 一群이 白日下에 폭로되엿슴으로 아무리 忍耻負重하기에 出類拔萃한 齊藤老相으로도 체면을 보지 안을 수가 업서 遺憶의 退陣을 하얏거니와 그럿치 안엇스면 宦福조코 배ㅅ심튼튼한 이 老人이 總理椅子에서 내려오기를 즐기지 안엇슬 것이다.

2. 第3流의 延長內閣 非常時에 不相應한 岡田氏
齊藤內閣이 6월 상순에 總辭職을 할 때에 政界는 다시 긴장하야 自薦他薦의 總理후보자가 濟濟林立하얏슴에 불구하고 首相은 임의 意中의 人을 推薦하야 元老의 동의를 엇고 잇섯스니 그가 즉 오늘의 총리대신인 岡田啓介大將인 것은 多言할 것도 아니다. 岡田大將은 齊藤翁과 함께 해군출신의 노장이지만은 그 연령과 踐歷에 잇서서는 遙히 후배에 속하야 일즉이 그의 幕下에 從軍하든 사람이오 言行性格의 유사한 점도 만허 兩心相印한 지가 임의 오래이라 한다. 그리고 岡田氏는 齊藤大先輩의 知過를 밧어 그의 추천으로 또 그가 남기고 간 後藤叅參謀와 악수하야 『一遵何約束』式으로 일홈은 다르지만은 실체에는 연장 內閣과 다름 업는 형태를 쓰고 나온 것이다. 이러함에 불구하고 現內閣은 성립 이래 임의 5개월 餘에 무엇을 하고 잇섯는지 소위 在滿行政機構改組問題로 일시 정국의 위기를 전할만치 粉紏와 葛藤을 보히든 것과 目下 倫郭에서 열니고 잇는 日英米海軍軍縮豫備會商에 대표자를 보낸 이외에 별로 可記할 것이 업다. 비상시일수록 신중히 국무를 처리한다면 이유가 상당치 안은 것도 아니로되 엇지된 까닭인지 비교적 少壯俳優를 만히 등장 식혀놋코도 각본은 舊套依然한  위에 그 動作擧止가 서투르고 활발치 못하야 관객에게 가벼운 권태를 늣기게 한다. 10년의 長이 되는 齊藤老人 內閣도 이럿치는 안헛다 할 때에 약간의 하픔과 조름도 생긴다. 외교문제는 상대방이 잇스니까 이편에서 혼자 애를 쓴대야 그대로 容易迅速하게 귀결이 나지 못할 것은 물론이지만은 임시 의회의 소집이 순식간에 잇고 계속하야 통상의회가 迫至하며 때는 임의 정치계절에 드러왓는데 제출될 의안이 정비치 못할 뿐 아니라 정당에 대 한 정치공작조차 착수가 되지 못한 모양이다. 소위 國策審議會는 여하 政民提携의 대책은 여하 농촌진흥과 미곡문제는 여하 기구문제의 粉紏는 비록 현지 吏員의 翻意와 함께 續靜되는 모양이오 專任拓相도 任命이 되여 해결의 好曙光을 보히지만은 그 粉擾에 伴한 官規弛漫의 책임은 여하 威信은 여하 또 軍縮準備會談도 開會壁頭부터 벌서 난관에 걸녀 前途를 危視하게 되엿스니 此에 대한 대책과 밋 善後는 여하 如斯히 數來하면 다만 日本이라는 국가가 실로 비상시에 잇슬 뿐 아니라 岡田內閣 자신도 絶無僅有한 비상시에 처하야 잇다. 그런데 비상시＜15＞ 내각으로는 넘우나 범상한 내각인 것이 또한 일단의 定論인 것 갓다. 老骨內閣의 評이 잇든 前內閣이면서도 오히려 나귀우름을 하품(欠伸)한다 할마한 먹보 令監의 주제에 능히 『焦土外交』를 용감하게 절규하든 康哉外相이 잇섯고 軍民離間者 取締에 대한 長文聲明書를 돌니며 무릇 대포와 비행기를 만드는 일에 不贊成하는 놈이 잇스면 永平寺(禪宗의 本山)로 보내여 참선이나 식히겟다고 瞋目大喝하든 荒木大將軍이 잇서 저윽이 人意를 강케 하드니 지금은 그런 사람조차 업는 것이 얼마나 外觀內容이 共히 貧弱低下하야 흥미를 肅素케 하는지 一言以籍之曰 비상시에 不相應한 3流內閣이다.

3. 去益拙劣한 政黨工作 紛飾糊塗는 武人의 羞恥
岡田內閣은 일홈은 비록 擧國一致 내각이라 하지만은 실질은 관료와 정당의 雜居合作內閣이다. 그 중에도 外務, 內務, 大藏, 司法, 陸海軍 등의 요지에는 모다 그 방면의 대표적 屬僚들을 배치하얏스나 정당방면에는 安達老의 國民同盟은 전연 멀니 하고 廊下生活에 着味한 무력의 民政黨으로부터 町田, 松田의 2老를 납치할 뿐이오 280여 명이나 되는 大政黨 政友會로 부터서는 入閣拒絶이라는 昌皮를 滿喫하고 겨우 床次, 山崎, 內田이라는 無籍者 3名을 데려다가 5개의 伴食椅子를 제공하얏다. 이 자리에 蹲坐하고 잇는 黨人도 黨人이려니와 추풍맛난 梧葉처럼 轉落顠零하는 당인을 마저다가 伴食椅子일지라도 제공치 아니하면 못되는 岡田內閣도 岡田內閣이다. 親任氏도 擧行하기 전에 내각의 威重은 임의 여지 업시 떠러젓다. 岡田內閣의 사명이 國難打開에 잇는 것이오 政黨에 대한 구제事業을 하기에 잇지 아니한 이상에 요지음 가티 천대 밧고 무력한 政黨者流의 해골을 줍기에 그다지 급급할 것이 무엇이냐. 차라히 비상시국을 인식퓀˜고 協戮을 자원하는 자는 모르되 이 때에 안저서도 舊日過惡을 청산할 줄을 모르고 콩이야 팟이야 하면서 非分의 政權慾을 꿈꾸고 잇는 政友會를 같은 당파에 대하야는 遮二無二解散의 痛棒을 내려 喪廉耻 無定見의 標本같은 鈴木總裁와 그 一味를 政界로부터 放逐하는 것이 政界를 위하야 淸爽味를 주입하고 당인에게 갱생의 길을 인도하는 공덕이 될 것이다. 필자는 政友會와 특히 鈴木總裁와 아무 恩怨利害가 업는 처지로되 政友會가 멀니는 星亨과 原敬이래로 갓가히는 田中, 床次에 니르기까지 개인으로 尊敬할 만한 점이 만흔 신사임에 불구하고 정권의 압헤늊� 節操 업는 離合聚散과 이권양탈과 私黨斗護에만 열중하야 盜跖의 행위를 부끄러워 하지 안튼 것을 惡斥하엿섯다. 그것이 鈴木喜三郞에 니르러서는 더욱 노골하고 악착화하야 분수 모르고 날뛰는 꼴이 밉고도 滑稽하얏다. 그리고 두뇌는 조흐나 無能한 若槻라든지 陰險老獪한 안달이라든지 輕薄饒舌한 無産黨의 소위 鬪士라든지 당인 각색치고 한 사람도 그럿타할 물건이 업시 되엿다. 이러니 저러니 하야도 原敬과 加藤高明이 죽고 演口雄幸이 마저 他界로 간 뒤로는 政黨이란 것은 狐狸와＜16＞ 豺狼의 집합체가 되고 말엇다. 이 무리에게 의하야 소위 憲政의 常軌를 밟고 정권이 순순히 후원되기는 日本의 신흥관료가 안일지라도 鼻笑에 附之할 외에 他法이 업슬 것이다. 그럼에 불구하고 이 자들의 嚮背에 관심하야 英國製의 과자라 하는 政務官의 香餌로 꼬히고 國策審議會委員으로 낙가드려 粉飾糊塗하야 그들의 망령을 더욱 타락케 하는 것은 비상시를 담임한 무인출신의 대재상으로서 할 것이 안일 것이다.

4. 在滿機構의 改組問題 紛擾는 鎭定이나 책임은 如何
岡田首相은 다만 政黨工作에서 실패의 흔적을 남길 뿐 아니라 전 내각의 탐탁하지 못한 遺物의 하나이든 관리의 地位鐵障을 그대로 남겨두어 관리된 자로 일차 지위만 얻으면 제-가 壓症이 나기 전에는 누구나 奈何치 못하는 그것을 精하게 遵守하고 잇다. 賢者도 千金이오 不肖子도 千金이라 하면 누가 즐겨서 안일을 버리고 근로를 택할 것이냐 不意馘首의 우려가 업서진 행정관료들이 一自以後로 惰氣滿滿한 중에도 搖頭轉目하야 妄自尊大하는 악풍이 滔滔히 생기게 되엿다. 이 사람들에게 網紀肅正과 忠勤奉公을 아무리 엄하게 훈시한들 어듸로부터 威行令施가 될 것이냐. 그럼으로 岡田首相의 發한바 新內閣 最初의 훈령은 공연히 인쇄국의 활자만을 수고럽게 할 뿐이오 在滿機構問題로 생겨난 一時의 紛擾는 拓務本省과 關東廳管下大小萬餘의 吏員으로 하야금 직무를 廢하고 決議이니 聲明이니 결사적 陳情이니 政府原案을 斷乎排擊이니 하는 前古未聞의 不穩한 사태를 釀出하야 『下剋上』의 官界不祥之弊風을 니르켯다. 비록 軍部의 峻烈한 기세와 閣議의 原案固執을 一決한 것이 다행히 奏功하야 大事에는 니르지 아니하얏스나 그것이 寧穩하얏다 하야 官紀紊亂의 사실과 內閣의 책임이 消滅解除된  것은 아니다. 더욱이 岡田首相이 처음부터 拓相을 兼攝하야 閣議 席上에서 拓務省案은 闕席判決로 一蹴하야 버리고 외무와 군부에만 互讓妥協을 식힌 것이라든지 菱刈軍司令官이 一方으로는 關東長官의 重職에 잇스면서 문관측의 訴願에는 한번도 傾耳치 안코 순전히 의부ㅅ자식으로 취급하든 것이라든지 군부에서 關東廳職員을 보기를 무삼 叛徒이나 匪類처럼하야 심하면 계엄령이라도 시행하랴는 기세를 보히든 것이든지 , 무엇 한 가지 賞讚할 것이 업섯다. 원래 행정기구의 조직편성과 같은 것은 그 때 그 때의 專情形便에 따러 政府의 수뇌부가 편익한 대로 시설하면 下僚들은 이에 따를 뿐이오 그 입법과 제정에 曰是曰否하야 저와 같은 반항을 許할 성질이 되지 못한다. 더욱이 文主武從이 되든지 武主文從이 되든지 내지 경찰관의 명칭을 폐하고 헌병으로 개편한다 할지라도 제-가 실흐면 버리고 갈 법은 잇서도 질서유지를 생명삼는 그 본원지에서 大小官吏가 成群作黨하야 가지고 連日狂奔하야 總辭職으로써 上司를 위협하는 것은 확실히 民擾에 대한 吏擾의 怪現象이다. 民擾가 나면 비록 그 주장이 정당한 것이라도 해산을 식히지 안으면 투옥과 사죄로써 다스리고＜17＞  官擾에 대하야는 視若尋常할 뿐 아니라 상관된 자가 소위 諒解를 구한다는 일홈으로 屈身迎合하기에 분주하며 은연히 지휘조종하든 자까지도 사심이 아니오 爲國至誠에서 나온 것이라 하야 寬縱은 고사하고 反히 褒揚 비슷한 찬사를 주는데 니르러서는 論者가 오히려 啞然하게 된다. 이것이 官規紊亂이 아니라고 강변한다 하면 岡田大將은 무인으로 희귀한 뻔뻔한 面皮의 소유주일 것이다. 政法의 생명은 嚴正과 공평에 잇다. 정법이 한번 그 생명을 일흐면 세도와 인심은 수습할 수가 업시 되는 것이다. 정도의 엄정 失墜한 岡田內閣이 장차 무엇으로써 世道人心을 유지하야 가랴는가.

5. 所謂 國策審議會 無用長物이 何故로 必要乎
入閣交涉의 거절로 政友會와 絶緣狀態에 잇는 岡田內閣은 처음에는 床次 이하의 三閣僚를 식혀 당내에 잇는 그의 乾兒와 一味들을 紏合하야 政友會에 반기를 들게 하고 新黨을 조직하랴 하얏다. 그러나 그 數爻는 의외로 寡少하야 왕년의 民政黨을 깨트린 안달의 일당과 대차가 업는 정도이엿고 그들을 남겨두워 內應을 삼어 가지고 政友會의 교란책을 쓰자하야도 鈴木 望月, 久原, 山本 등 巨頭의 결합이 또한 의외로 공고하야 정무관에 끌녀 背黨한 자 외에는 별로 快應者가 업섯다. 於是에 床次와 町田 等 黨人 출신 각료는 다시 수상에게 進言하되 國策審議會를 설치하야 政民兩黨의 총재와 그 領袖級 1,2人은 물론 齊藤, 高橋, 山本 등 前內閣의 長老와 기타 조야의 有力者를 網羅하야 擧國一致의 傘下에 內閣의 보강공작을 행하자 하얏다. 그러나 주안점은 鈴木과 政友會를 내각지지파로 引入하자는 것임은 물론이다. 그러나 岡田氏는 임의 정우회와의 교섭에는 손(手)을 듸엿슬뿐더러 副總理格으로 잇는 後藤內相이 前內閣時代에 鈴木政友總裁가 高橋長老를 비롯하야 三土, 鳩山 等 心腹黨員과 다시 別働隊로 中島, 南의 都合五名이나 당원 준당원을 보내 놋코도 내각을 지지하야 주지 안코 陰으로 陽으로 內閣破壞를 일삼어 稚稚비열한 수단과 술책을 부리든 쓰린 경험을 備嘗하얏섯슴으로 이제 다시 鈴木氏를 마저 드린대야 신통할 것이 업고 도리혀 事事에 掣盯를 밧어 괴롭고 거북하다는 견지에서 찬동치 안엇다. 그리하든 것이 昨今에 니르러 民政黨內에서까지 岡田內閣의 倒壞를 주장하는 자가 생기는 一方에서 政民提携運動이 재연하야 政黨은 정당으로 복귀하야 行廊사리를 그만두자는 機運이 자못 늆�후하야 간다. 그리고 보면 衆議員 460餘 席에서 400명 이상의 의원을 반대파로 삼어 마조스게 되면 형세와 결과 여하는 넘우도 분명함에야 엇지 하리오. 이에 岡田, 後藤의 2大臣도 마음을 도리켜 갑작이 審議會 設置에 深甚한 고려를 費하기로 약속하얏다한다. 그 成否 如何는 이제부터 逆覩見치 못하거니와 假使 그것이 성공한다 하기로 擧國一致의 형식은 糊塗하야 苟合을 掩飾한다할지언정 試驗旣濟의 그 인물들을 모화 놋키로니 새삼스러운 무슨 국책이 湧出될 理致도 업슬 것이오＜18＞ 內閣外에 內閣을 더 작만하야 갓득이나 활발치 못한 現內閣이 不自然한 비만증으로 더욱이 運身不能하게 될 것이다. 이것도 鈴木이가 滫心洗面하야 아조 別人으로 化하지 안는 한에 內閣의 禍胎는 될지언정 무엇이 유익하랴. 무릇 政黨者流는 그 취급법이 養鷹者와 같이 굼겨두면 驅使되기를 辭치 안치만은 少毫야도 和한 顔色을 보히거나 膓을 재워주면 颺去가 아니면 友噬가 잇슬 뿐이다. 그럼으로 이런 생물들은 鐵柵으로 圓繞한 동물원에서나 기를 것이오 자유의 천지로는 불너낼 것이 되지 안는다. 당년의 勇提督을 首班삼은 비상시 內閣이 아니냐. 政權餓鬼의 당인들이 결속반항하거든 그 패가 굴복할 때까지 2次 3次로 解散의 巨彈을 발사할 것이다. 왕년에 明治元勳이든 山縣有朋老人은 大隈와 板垣의 연합군에게 저항하다 못하야 『憲法의 一時中止設』까지 提議치 안엇는가. 太平無事之日에도 猶然하거든 허물며 비상시일까부냐. 岡田大將이 아무리 노쇠하얏다한들 엇지 逡巡遲疑하야 지하의 山縣大先輩로 혜(舌)를 차게 헐 것이냐 고양이(猫) 달내는 0聲으로 회유책을 쓰는 것보다도 무장다운 씩씩한 태도로 정정당당하게 응전하는 것이 얼 마나 관객의 갈채를 받으리오. 그 뿐 아니라 군사비로써 거의 반액을 占한 비상시 예산에 제법 반대할 용기가 잇다면 政友會와 民政黨이 지금 모양으로 醜亡하지 안엇슬 것이다.

6. 暗澹한 軍縮豫備會商 提案한 三大原則은 如何한 것
최후로 남어 잇는 것은 華府軍縮條約의 廢棄通告와 新約訂結의 豫備會商이 여하히 落着될 것이며 소위 1935,6년 위기에 備하는 외교공작과 아울너 군비충실이 잘 되고 못 되는 것이다. 이 군축조약의 成否야말노 세계평화의 분기점이 달녀잇는 것이오 예비회담에서 타협이 될는지 결렬이 될는지 이것은 直히 明年 본회의에 중대한 영향을 가즌 까닭에 全天下의 이목은 이제 倫敦에 집중되여 잇는 것이다. 그런데 松平, 山本 2代表는 開議劈頭에 各히 英, 米代表者에 대하야 軍縮에 대한 日本政府의 3大原則을 제출하얏다. 드른 바에 의하면 그 원칙은
1 국방평등주의를 확립하야 각자의 국민으로 하야금 국방에 대한 위협을 感치 안케 할 것. 따러서 此主義에 반한 비율제한의 現行華府, 倫敦 兩條約은 적당한 시기에 폐기할 일.
2 右에 대하야 新訂할 明年 海軍軍備制限條約은 각국이 他를 침공함에 부족하고 자기를 守禦함에 족한 정도로 緫噸數主義의 국방제한을 행할 것.

이것이 명치 末로 대정 초에 니르는 일본 정부의 전통방침이오 桂四園寺, 山本, 大隈, 寺內 등으로 내각 更 여러 차레 잇섯지만은 『海軍 50만돈(主力艦), 陸軍 상비 25개 사단이 建設』은 日本國民의 國防上 상식으로 인하야 八四로부터 八, 六과 八, 八, 八에 니르는 계획을 세우고 최후에 이것을 완성한 것이 大正9년 7월 특별의회에서 성립된 『8.8艦隊十二年計劃의 예산』 이엿섯다. 當詩의 日本官民은 國際聯盟謳歌의 平和論者라는 소위 幾個의 親人以外에는 누구나 拍手歡呼한 것이다. 그 실례를 필자는 의장에서 목격한 것이 잇다. 그 때 在野黨으로 寺內와 原內閣에게 연속解散을 맛나 소수당이 되고 政權線外에 퇴각된 憲政會總裁 加藤高明이가 중의원 방청석에서 해군예산 통과의 광경을 趣下하더니 控室로 내려와 河野, 若槻, 濱口, 箕浦, 安達, 下岡 등 領袖를 모화노코 그 날 찬성연설을 하고 도라온 早速整爾의 등(背)을 어루만즈면서
大殷 勞苦하엿네. 이것으로 日本國防은 확립되엿고 실력 잇는 外交戰을 하게 되엿네. 憲政會를 가르처 世人은 消梀黨이니 緊縮黨이니 하지만은 그것은 방만한 재정책을 비평하야 政府의 낭비예산에 반대하는 것을 오해하는 때문이다. 이런 일에야 찬성보다도 寧히 鞭撻하여서 성립식힐 것일세. 그런데 君이 我의 의사를 그대로 잘 표시하야 연설하얏스니 참으로 고맙네.
給仕를 부르더니 幹事長 小泉 又 次郞을 식혀 식당에 보내여 맥주라도 깨트려서 축배를 준비하라 하얏다. 政府의 에산통과에 敵黨首領이 現內閣大臣보다도 먼저 축배를 드다니 그 때의 原, 加藤의 관계로 보아 누구든지 奇怪視하얏고 엇든 惡口의 신문기자는 『무얼 해군예산이 성립하야 建艦이 시작되면 이익을 밧을 것이 三菱造船所가 아니냐. 三菱이가 돈을 벌면 加藤의 懷中에서 憲政會로 보낼 자금이 넉넉할 터이니까 三段論法 조화하는 加藤이가 建艦-三菱-軍資金-이 때문으로 조화하는 것이다』 得意然하게 一笑하는 것을 보앗다. 그런지 一朔 後에 筆者는 輕井澤 別邸에서 加藤氏를 맛나보고 祖鮮問題를 爲始하야 그의 세계정론을 一場拜聽한 일이 잇는데 그는 多年 외교의 樞機에서 책임의 要衡에 당한이 만티 개념론을 말할지언정 一點도 機微에 저촉하는 말은 하지 안는다. 또 소위 英國流의 실제적 정치가의 名이 잇는니만큼 실제문제 이외에는 일절의 假想과 豫斷 비슷한 이상은 一句도 吐치 안는다. 그러나 그는 천성이 沉重寡黙하고 剛毅固直한 까닭에 말을 안으면 몰나도 말문이 터지면 단순 하나마 진실을 말하고 정직하게 가르친다.
『日本이 사러야 한다.』나는 日本人이다. 日本에 유리한 일이면＜23＞ 누구와도 악수할 것이오 日本에 가루 거치는 놈은 우리의 살기 위하야 배격할 뿐이다. 그런데 現政府가 우리 日本을 괴롭게구는 英米에 備하기 위하야 建艦計劃과 함께 親露策을 써서 이들을 견제할 줄을 모르고 大隈內閣때부터 설계한 해군계획을 이제야 와서도 완만한 12년 계획을 세울분 아니라 지금도 西伯利에 大兵을 두고 군력과 國努을 소모하는 것이 매우 딱한 일이다. 외교의 針路만 일정하며 支那은 자연히 英米를 버리고 日本에 따를 것이오 日本의 滿洲權益은 支那가 먼저 보호하야 줄 것이다. 그러면 日本도 支那을 위하야 稅權과 法權의 不平等 諸條約을 率先 撤回할 뿐 아니라 列國으로 하야금 이에 隨從식힐 用意와 自信이 잇다. 日本의 힘으로 亞洲를 완전하게 亞洲人의 亞洲를 만들기 전에는 모든 것을 堪耐하는 것이 우리도 살고 그들도 살게 되는 것이다. 이것이 내의 理想이라면 理想이다. 아마 日本帝國의 理想도 이럴 것이다. 그러나 이런 것을 신문에는 쓰지 말어라. 신문으로 해서 내가 받은 『매이와꾸』가 얼마인지 모른다.
加藤氏의 이 말은 후일에 진실하든 것을 알게 되엿다. 貴族院에서 연설하든 西伯利撤兵論이 그것이오 加藤友三郞首相에게 육박하든 華府條約反對論과 對支外交 특히 滿蒙權益擁護論에서 신념과 기백을 吐하든 것이 그것이다. 芳澤, 카라한의 日露北京條約을 체결한 것과 21개 條約 當事者 『日置益』을 中國에 보내여 北京關稅會議에서 英米를 놀내든 제의를 식힌 것도 그가 首相으로 집권하고 잇든 시대의 일이다. 그로 하야금 靈이 잇다하면 倫敦條約을 체결한 若槻, 弊原을 가르처 『내의 意思를 理解치 못하는 不肖子弟』라 할 것이오 또 『이것이 모다 俗論에 영합하야 8.8艦隊計劃을 放棄한 原, 加藤(友)의 罪오 그보다도 西伯利에 출병한 寺內의 罪』라고 중얼걸일 것이며 內田氏의 焦土演說을 드르면 『이 뻔뻔한 고무人形(內田氏綽名)아 英米追從外交는 누가 시작한 것이냐 네-가 露都에서 倉黃히 逃脫하야 오는 때에 내가 무엇이라고 너를 가르치드냐. 지금와서 42 對 1을 누구에게 稱寃하며 蘇聯의 態度를 엇지 恨하느냐. 焦土外交를 부르짓기 전에 어서 海防을 완성하여라. 英米의 머리를 숙이게 하기 전에는 中, 蘇問題는 話頭에도 올니지 마러라』할 것이 명백하다. 얼는 말하면 지금 廣田, 大角, 林 등의 三相意見이란 것이 加藤의 이 생각을 民政黨이 주장치 못하고 딴쪽에서 선점한 것이다.

9. 結論-天下는 鳥乎定고 司馬氏의 野心을 警戒하라
이상의 말한 것으로써 부적절 불철저할는지 모르나 군축문제에 대한 日本의 주장하는 根柢와 윤곽은 알게 될 것이다. 그런데 이번 회의에 대하야 해결의 關鍵을 가즌 나라는 日本이 아니오 米國도 아니오 英國이 잇다. 英國은 일즉이 日本을 부려 東洋門戶에 충실한 番犬을 삼엇섯다. 그럼으로 과거 외교에 잇서 中, 蘇 문제로 害困은 日本이 밧고 이익은 英國이＜24＞ 누렷섯다. 그러든 것이 일차 日本을 버리고 米國과 접근한 이후로는 米國은 日本과 가티 자기네 일을 충실히 보와주지 안을뿐더러 均率의 海軍力을 가즈고 動輒大先輩를 凌駕하야 세계에 號令權을 가저갈 뿐 아니라 日本의 憤怒를 사서 東洋에 잇는 지반이 큰 협위를 늣기고 잇다. 親米乎 親日乎 이것이 지금 英國政治家들의 머리를 썩이는 숙제이다. 그런데 中蘇문제 뿐이면 그들은 벌서 米國을 버리고 다시 日本과 악수하얏슬 것이지만은 中, 蘇 이외에 佛, 伊 獨 등의 新敵 舊敵을 만히 가지고 잇는 英國은 어듸까지든지 米國에게 은근한 손(手)을 떼일 수 업는 형편이다. 그러므로 친미, 친일의 태도를 확정하기까지는 二者에게 一樣으로 호의를 표시하야 타협을 권할 모양이다. 이것을 中國의 魏, 吳, 蜀 삼국시대에 비하면 米는 曹의 魏요 英은 孫의 吳요 日本은 劉의 蜀이다. 孫, 劉가 연합하면 北魏를 능히 항거할지나 그 반대로 孫, 曹가 합하면 蜀이 위태하다. 그것은 劉와 曹는 도저히 相合할 수 업는 까닭이다. 그리고 이 三國의 관계를 다시 現代中國으로 본다면 英米蘇獨佛伊 등의 백인은 魏요 日本은 吳요 中國은 蜀이요 滿洲는 三國相爭하는 荊州이다. 中國의 排日派는 마치 孫權이가 關羽를 죽이고 荊州를 取한데 분개하야 劉備의 東征을 勸하는 것이오 그럿치 안은 사람은 『國賊曹操換非孫權』을 말하는 것과 유사한 형편인데 대세는 증오의 일념으로 曹丕와 합하야 합병에 출병식히는 論者가 우세인 것 갓다. 그리고 前記 日, 英, 米의 관계로 보면 米國의 親露, 引英, 扶中의 정책이 맟이 曹丕가 東吳, 西戎, 南蠻으르 모다 불너대여 蜀에 五路進兵하든 것과 방불하다. 그러면 蜀이 되여잇는 日本과 中國은 오즉 諸葛亮의 有無로써 그것이 결정될 것이다. 배우는 다르지만은 무대는 三國舊土의 中國이다. 三國의 對抗전도 조흐려니와 그보다도 司馬氏의 晋으로 自處하는 某國의 存在를 無視하면 아니된다.
