Records
The record world lately is truly quiet beyond compare. These days, it's so quiet you might wonder if record companies even exist anymore. Therefore, there isn't much news to report, but let's gather a few words!

Kang Hongsik (姜弘植), who filled Columbia's pockets with the *changt'aryŏng* tune "Spring has come, spring has come, even to the virgin maiden's heart," left the theater world and wandered into the record industry, finally landing at Columbia. After suddenly rising to fame with "Ch'ŏnyŏ Ch'onggak" (Maiden Bachelor), record number 40,489 became something of his nickname (綽名즘), his popularity rose within the company, and it's said he recently signed an exclusive singer contract (契). However, will a goby jump at every market? We ask for Mr. Kang's efforts.

This is also about Columbia. We thought the company's signboard (?), Ch'ae Kyuyŏp (蔡奎燁), was in Tokyo (東京) concentrating on music, but it's said he was recently sued for violation of chastity. The accuser was a young girl of just 16. This is something everyone already knows. However, rumor has it the case was later overturned, claimed to be a case of mistaken identity involving someone with the same name (同名異人), and the aforementioned Mr. Ch'ae has become safe. This whole thing feels like a dream. Instead (도리혀), this incident might even lead to his records selling better.

At Victor (빅타-), there's been no particular news since Mr. Hong Nanp'a (洪蘭坡) joined the company; it remains quiet as ever. According to reports, he says he still lacks the confidence to compose popular songs and will release them after more research. When Mr. Nanp'a's compositions finally appear before the public (世間), it seems poised to cause a major 'sensation' in the Chosŏn (朝鮮) record world, so it's greatly anticipated. However, Arts Department Head Mr. Yi Kise's (李基世君) policy is reportedly not to release popular songs on Chosŏn records, so perhaps Mr. Hong's research isn't into popular songs but into Yi Kise-ism.

The review of newcomer Kim Hae (金海君) finished successfully at the Public Hall (公會堂). Expectations were very high. We found out it's already been decided he will record for Victor (빅타-).

It's said that Victor's (빅타-) Pak Wŏljŏng (朴月庭) and Columbia's (콜림비아) Sŏk Kŭmsŏng (石金星) will exchange recordings. Pak Wŏljŏng, faded at Victor, goes to Columbia, and Sŏk Kŭmsŏng, faded at Columbia, goes to Victor! Though the recording opportunities and the labels (盤) are different, the listening ears are the same. We don't know what results this will yield, but perhaps this is indeed Mr. Kise's brilliant plan. Or should we call it Columbia Mr. An's (安君) brilliant plan!

The company that has picked out (?) the elite (?) of popular singers is Polydor (포리돌). It decidedly leads in recent popular songs.

Though they gathered good popular singers with the aim of selling Chosŏn records of popular songs, without much research, they face some difficulty competing with the same '1 Wŏn 50 Chŏn records'. Consequently, the company is undergoing all sorts of deep thought and research, but nothing has materialized yet. We hope they achieve a surprising accomplishment that causes a big 'sensation' in this field (斯界). Following Columbia's tour last time, Polydor also left around the end of last month (去月 말경) for the Northern Korea (北鮮) region. With such a lineup, they should achieve results, benefiting from Columbia's previous poor performance. <95>

**Summary**

This 1934 article discusses the quiet state of the Korean record industry, focusing on major labels like Columbia, Victor, and the rising Polydor. It covers artist news, including Kang Hongsik's success at Columbia, a scandal involving Ch'ae Kyuyŏp, Hong Nanp'a's anticipated debut at Victor under Yi Kise's potentially restrictive policy, newcomer Kim Hae joining Victor, and an artist exchange between Victor (Pak Wŏljŏng) and Columbia (Sŏk Kŭmsŏng). Polydor is noted for its popular song focus and recent tour of Northern Korea. The article highlights the competition, artist movements, and strategies within the nascent Korean popular music market under Japanese rule.

For historians, the article offers insights into the early Korean recording industry's structure, key figures (artists, producers like Yi Kise), popular taste (mention of *changt'aryŏng*, popular songs), marketing strategies (artist contracts, tours, pricing), and the influence of Japanese companies (Victor, Columbia, Polydor) operating in colonial Korea. The mention of specific record numbers, artist scandals, company policies regarding "Chosŏn records" vs. popular songs, and regional tours provide valuable details about cultural production and consumption during this period.

레코-드
레고-드界의 최근이야말노 참 잔잔하기 짝 업다. 요새가태서는 레코-드회사가 잇는지 언는지? 할만콤 잔잔하다. 그럼으로 별로 이약이 거리도 업스나 몃 마듸 주서 드러보자!

『봄이 욋네 봄이 와 숫처녀의 가슴에도』하는 장타령조로 콜럼비아 주머니를 불눅하게 해준 姜弘植군은 극단을 등지고 레코-드계로 배회하다가 최후에 『콜럼비아」로 와서 처녀총각으로 일약 명성을 날나게 되자 레코-드 番*40,489가 그의 綽名즘 되게 同社內에서는 人氣가 올나 얼마 전에 전속 가수로 契을 햇다 한다. 그러나 장마다 망둥이가 날는지 姜君의 노력을 부탁한다.

이것도 『콜럼비아』이약이다. 同社의 看板(?) 蔡奎燁군은 東京에 잇서 음악에 專心을 하고 잇는 줄 알앗드니 얼마 전에 정조유린으로 고소를 당햇다 한다. 고소한 색시는 불과 16세의 소녀. 이것은 누가나 벌서 다 아는 일이다. 그러나 그 후에 사건이 번복되여 同名異人이라 하야 例의 蔡군은 무사해졋다는 소문이 들인다. 이게 도모지 꿈 속 가튼 일이다. 도리혀 이것으로 그의 소리판이 더 잘 팔닐는지도 모르는 일.
「빅타-」에는 洪蘭坡君이 입사 이후에 별소식이 업시 여전히 잠잠하다 전하는 말을 드르면 아즉 유행곡을 지을 자신이 업스니 좀 더 연구를 한 다음 내놋겟다니 蘭坡군의 곡이 한 번 세간에 나오는 때는 **朝鮮레코-드界에는 일대 『센세이슌』을 이르킬 모양이니 매우 기대되는 일이다. 그러나 문예부장 李基世君의 주의는 朝鮮盤에는 유행가를 안낼 주의라니 洪君의 연구도 유행곡의 연구가 아니라 李基世主義의 연구인지도 모르겟다.

新進金海君의 레뷰-가 公會堂에서 성황리에 마첫다. 매우 촉망을 가젓섯다. 알고 보니 벌서 「빅타-」에 취입하기로 결정이 되엿다고.

「빅타-」의 朴月庭 「콜림비아」의 石金星이가 교환취입케 되엿다 한다. 『빅타-』에서 시드른 朴月庭이가 콜럼비아로 『콜럼비아』에서 시드른 石金星이가 빅타-로! 취입기는 다르고 盤은 다르다 하야도 듯는 귀는 갓다. 과연 엇더한 성과를 내일지는 모르나 과연 이것이 基世君의 묘책이다. 그러타고 이것을 콜럼비아 安君의 묘책이라 할는지!

유행가수의 精銳(?)를 뽀바*은 회사가 「포리돌」이다. 최근의 유행가로는 단연 리-드한다.

朝鮮소리판 유행가로 팔어먹으랴는 주의로 유행가수만은 잘 모앗스나 별반 연구가 업서가지고는 가튼 「1圓50錢盤으로는 좀 어려운 감이 업지 안타. 이에서 同社에서는 별별 고심과 연구를 다 하는 중이나 아즉 실현되지는 안엇스니 斯界에 큰 『센세슌』을 이르킬 만치 놀날만한 업적을 내기 바란다. 전번 『콜럼비아』에서 순회공연을 하고 온 北鮮지방을 『포리돌』서도 뒤미처서 去月 말경에 떠난다. 진용이 그만하면 전번『콜럼비아』의 不成績의 덕으로 효과를 내일 것이다. ＜95＞
