Review of the Korean Farmland Ordinance
						Chŏng In'gwan

Foreword: After much debate over whether to call it the "Korean Tenancy Ordinance" or the "Farmland Ordinance," it came into the world bearing the official name "Korean Farmland Ordinance." There was also indecision about whether to promulgate it in Shōwa 8 (1933) or Shōwa 9 (1934), showing reluctance regarding the timing, but it was finally issued as Ordinance No. 5 on April 1, Shōwa 9 (1934). During drafting, there was debate over setting the tenancy period at 5 years or 3 years, ultimately deciding on the latter. There was also discussion about setting the rent ratio at 3:7 or 4:6, but it emerged shapelessly after a difficult birth. Subsequently, there was further debate about whether the effective date should be November 1st, or before or after the harvest season, until it was finally promulgated along with the Enforcement Regulations of the said Ordinance and the Regulations for the Prefectural, County, and Island Tenant Farmer Committees on October 20th, a date indistinguishable as either before or after the harvest.

Whether the Farmland Ordinance emerged through such numerous difficulties because it favored landlords or because it favored tenant farmers is a separate issue. However, it is said to be a fact that traces of efforts to appease the frantic outcry of the landlord faction, which launched a frontal attack, rather than considering the side of the tenant farmers who were required to remain silent, are clearly evident (昭然).

Once, a certain prominent Korean figure remarked to a certain official at the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務省):
"It is regrettable that a Tenancy Ordinance has not yet been established in an agricultural country like Korea."
The official reportedly smiled and sighed, saying, "In Korea... Japanese landlords... have connections in the House of Peers (貴族院) or the House of Representatives (衆議院) and elsewhere in Japan, raising opposition, so it doesn't proceed as intended."
Indeed, when a substantive law concerning tenancy was about to emerge, the fact that some landlords, as if waiting for the opportunity, attempted certain political maneuvers seems to endorse the aforementioned Ministry official's words as fact.

This is a brief digression, but while not unaware of the authorities' painstaking efforts, the general consensus seems to be that, in the end, only a deformed child was produced.
It is fresh in our memory that the Tenancy Bill was presented at the 59th Diet session, gained the support <40> of the House of Representatives, yet failed to be enacted due to opposition from the House of Peers. It is indeed noteworthy that such a difficult law to enact anywhere has now reached the stage of implementation in Korea, and its future course deserves attention.
As soon as this Farmland Ordinance appeared, newspapers and magazines have been wielding their pens at every opportunity. While rehashing (反齧) this issue may seem somewhat redundant, I will attempt a comparative critique below to the best of my ability.

"Outline of Tenancy Law" and the Farmland Ordinance
In May of Taishō 15 (1926), the Wakatsuki cabinet government consulted the Tenancy Investigation Committee (小作調査會) regarding measures for the tenancy problem. In October of the same year, the committee submitted a response titled "Outline Regarding Matters to be Stipulated in the Enactment of Tenancy Law." This is what is commonly known as the "Outline of Tenancy Law" (小作法要綱), which became the basis for the draft Tenancy Law announced by the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry (農林省) in March of Shōwa 2 (1927). It is believed that the Korean Farmland Ordinance, now being implemented, has also adopted much of its content and form from this Outline and Draft. Therefore, comparing this Outline and the Farmland Ordinance is considered not without meaning. However, listing and comparing the full text would take up much space, so only the key points will be noted.

II. Transfer of Tenancy Leasehold Rights = Allows exceptions for transfers according to custom even without the landlord's consent - This provision is absent in the Farmland Ordinance but present in the Draft Tenancy Law (hereinafter simply referred to as the "Draft") (Article 5).

V. Continuation and Termination of Tenancy Contract
1. Fixed-Term Tenancy Contract Period - (Perpetual tenancy is regulated separately, set at 20 to 70 years) Usually not less than 5 years, and terms less than 5 years are considered 5 years - The Farmland Ordinance sets it at 3 years for ordinary crops and 7 years for perennial crops (Article 7), and terms shorter than these are deemed (看做) or presumed (推定) to be 3 or 7 years respectively depending on the crop. The "Draft" (Article 11, Article 38) follows the "Outline."
2. Renewal of Fixed-Term Contract = If notice of non-renewal is not given 6 months before the expiration date, the contract is considered renewed under the previous conditions - The Farmland Ordinance regulates this in Article 18, setting the notice period at 3 months to 1 year. The "Draft" (Article 12) sets it at 6 months <41> to 1 year.
3. Termination of Indefinite-Term Tenancy Contract - Requires at least 1 year's notice with the condition of paying a certain compensation amount during the period before starting the next cultivation. - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance. The "Draft" (Articles 8, 9) requires 1 year to pass after notice of termination, but without the condition of compensation payment, and stipulates that the lease continues until the end of harvest even if terminated.
Also, if the term's fixedness is unclear, it is presumed to be an indefinite-term tenancy contract - The Farmland Ordinance (Article 8) presumes even unclear terms as 3 or 7 years depending on the crop.
4. Termination due to Debt Default = When 1 year's rent is overdue for 1 year, when partial rent is overdue for each year over 3 years, or when arrears span 2 or more years and the total reaches 1 year's rent, the landlord must demand (催告) payment within 2 months from the tenant farmer - The Farmland Ordinance (Article 19) allows termination if the lessee (tenant farmer) commits an act of bad faith. The "Draft" (Article 16) allows termination for arrears equivalent to one year's rent or other similar defaults specified by order.

1. Purchase of Standing Crops (毛上買收) - (Explanation omitted)
2. Reimbursement for Beneficial Expenses = The remaining value (殘存價額) of soil improvement (客土), irrigation, drainage works, etc., carried out by the tenant with the landlord's consent is reimbursed to the tenant - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance, but stipulated in the "Draft" (Article 19).
3. Other Compensation = Ordinary damages, i.e., compensation for losses incurred by the tenant leaving the land - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance. The Draft (Article 23) requires payment of a departure fee (作離料) of at least one-twelfth of the annual rent amount.
4. Compensation for Cultivation Rights (作株) = Applied to land where the practice of buying and selling cultivation rights exists, compensating the customary local price of the cultivation right - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance, but present in the "Draft" (Article 75).

As mentioned above, the Draft Tenancy Law was based on the "Outline," <42> and the Farmland Ordinance appears to have largely borrowed from the "Outline" and the "Draft." So, what are the differences between them?
In terms of the number of articles, the "Draft," which codified the "Outline," has 76 articles in total, whereas the Farmland Ordinance has only 40. Regarding content, the "Outline" includes:
Transfer of leasehold rights (exception side) "Outline Section II"
Notice of sale of tenancy land (recognizing tenant's right of first refusal) "Outline Section IV"
Indefinite-term tenancy "Outline Section V-3"
Reimbursement for beneficial expenses "Outline Section VI-2"
Departure fee (作離料) "Outline Section VI-3"
Compensation for cultivation rights (作株) "Outline Section VI-4"
Compensation in case of land expropriation "Outline Section VI-5"
Rent deposit (小作料供託) "Outline Section VIII"
etc., which are absent in the Farmland Ordinance. Conversely, items not in the "Outline" but present in the Farmland Ordinance include:
Regulations on Saŭm (舍音) (Farmland Ordinance Articles 3, 4, 5)
Inheritance of Lease (Farmland Ordinance Article 11)
etc. Besides these, there are significant differences in content despite having the same objective, such as concerning the "Tenancy Contract Period," as detailed above.
Otherwise, the general content and spirit can mostly be traced back to the "Outline," and the phrasing of articles can often be found in the "Draft." Despite this parent-child legal relationship, they can be said to be similar yet different, trying to resemble each other but ultimately failing to do so.

"Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices" and the Farmland Ordinance
In Shōwa 3 (1928), a directive titled "Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices" (小作慣行改善案) was issued under the name of the Administrative Superintendent (政務總監) to each Provincial Governor. Since this can be seen as having served as the placenta (胎盤) in the gestation (胚孕) of the Farmland Ordinance, excerpts are presented below.

Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices
I. Formal Requirements for Tenancy Contracts (Explanation omitted) = Should be in writing as much as possible.
II. Changes in Tenancy Rights or Rent Increases Accompanying Transfer of Ownership of Tenancy Land (Explanations omitted for content similar to the Farmland Ordinance, applies hereafter)
III. Sublease of Tenancy Land (Explanation omitted)
IV. Duration of Tenancy Rights = 3 years for ordinary cultivation (compare Farmland Ordinance Art. 7, Para. 1), and 10 years for mulberry fields, etc. - Farmland Ordinance Art. 7, Para. 2 sets it at 7 years - Ensure tenants are not changed arbitrarily.
V. Inheritance of Tenancy Rights (Explanation omitted)
VI. Termination of Tenancy Contract = Allow contract termination if the full amount of one year's rent is overdue, or if part of the rent for each year is overdue for two consecutive years, and payment is not made despite the landlord's demand (催告) within a period not less than 2 months - The Farmland Ordinance allows termination for the tenant's act of bad faith (背位行爲), <43> as mentioned earlier.
VII. Fixed Rent (定租) = For fields (田) and paddies (畓) with convenient irrigation, tenancy should be based on fixed rent as much as possible - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance.
VIII. Fairness of Rent Amount = Gradually reduce to an appropriate amount based on local conditions.
IX. Measurement (量正) of Rent Amount = Follow regulations concerning weights and measures - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance.
X. Transport Costs (渾撽賃) for Rent = Tenant bears costs within 2 ri (里), landlord bears costs for the excess distance beyond 2 ri - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance.
XI. Tenant Burdens Other Than Rent = Public charges (公課) borne by the landlord; labor service, other than minor repairs to the tenancy land, should not be arbitrarily demanded from the tenant - Absent in the Farmland Ordinance.
XII. Purchase of Crops on Tenancy Land upon Return (Explanation omitted)
XIII. Landlord's Encouragement to Tenants
XIV. Correction of Abuses by Saŭm (舍音) - The Farmland Ordinance regulates Saŭm in Articles 3, 4, 5 -
1. Landlords should manage tenancy land themselves in principle; Saŭm should only be appointed in unavoidable cases like the landlord residing far away, illness, old age/infancy, etc.
2. Pay attention to the selection of Saŭm; contracts should be in writing - i.e., Tenancy Land Management Regulations (小作地管理規程一)
3. Notify tenants of the Saŭm's name and authority.
4. Do not appoint too many Saŭm - i.e., have as few Saŭm as possible manage a wide area of land -
5. Limit Saŭm's authority to land management, rent collection, inspection attendance (檢見立會), and tenant guidance; involvement in concluding, changing, or terminating tenancy contracts only in unavoidable circumstances.
6. Diligently pursue legal action (勵行) against Saŭm's misconduct.
XV. Resolution of Tenancy Disputes = Avoid resorting to court as much as possible; have administrative offices mediate (居中調停). In provinces with many disputes, have officials funded by national or provincial/local expenses (國費 又는 道地方費支辦吏員) study tenancy issues, liaise with police authorities, and cooperate with county magistrates, agricultural association heads, etc., for resolution (this will likely be handled mostly by Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committees henceforth).
Since this Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices was not a legal text, it wasn't expected to have penetrating effect, nor were great expectations placed upon it originally. However, it was indeed a kind of trial item (試驗頭目格) among matters for establishing tenancy regulations. Yet, this too can be said to have been primarily devised by referencing the aforementioned Outline of Tenancy Law and Draft Tenancy Law, although the intention to thoroughly correct (匡正) the abuses of Saŭm is clearly visible.
However, among these 15 items, excluding those not needing codification, most have entered into the Farmland Ordinance. The only differences are that this improvement plan set the duration for perennial crops at 10 years, <44> while the Farmland Ordinance set it at 7 years; this Plan specifically defined regulations for tenancy contract termination, whereas the Farmland Ordinance did so abstractly. Items present in this Plan but not included in the Farmland Ordinance are:
"Fields (田) and paddies (畓) with convenient irrigation should be based on fixed rent"
"Rent amount measurement (量定) follows weights and measures regulations"
"Unreasonable burdens cannot be imposed on tenants"

Tenant Farmer's Status under Civil Law and the Farmland Ordinance
(A) Tenant Farmer's Rights
The tenant farmer has the right to use and profit from the tenancy land. This is essentially the leasehold right to the land.
If damage occurs to the tenancy land or its inherent accessory structures, the tenant can demand repairs from the landlord. If there is any hindrance to the use of the tenancy land, the tenant can demand its removal from the landlord. If there is a defect in the tenancy land contrary to the contract, the tenant can demand compensation for damages from the landlord. When the harvest yield is less than the contracted rent due to force majeure, the tenant can request a rent reduction equivalent to the actual harvest amount (Civil Code Art. 609).
If a part of the tenancy land is lost through no fault of the tenant, the tenant can demand a rent reduction proportional to the lost part (Civil Code Art. 611, Para. 1). And if the partially lost tenancy land becomes unsuitable for the purpose of the lease, the tenant can terminate the tenancy contract (Civil Code Art. 611).
If, due to force majeure, the harvest yields less than the rent for two consecutive years or more, the tenant can terminate the tenancy contract (Civil Code Art. 610).
If the landlord intends to perform preservation actions on the tenancy land against the tenant's will, and this prevents the tenant from achieving the objectives as per the contract, the tenant can terminate the tenancy contract with the landlord (Civil Code Art. 607).
If the tenant incurs necessary expenses (必要費) for the tenancy land that the landlord should bear, the tenant can immediately demand reimbursement from the landlord (Civil Code Art. 608). Necessary expenses refer to those required to maintain the land in a usable and profitable state.
Furthermore, if the tenant demands reimbursement for these necessary expenses from the landlord, and the landlord does not pay, the tenant can refuse to return the land until the full amount is reimbursed (Civil Code Art. 295, Art. 296). The right exercised by the tenant in such cases is called the right of retention (留置權).
If the tenant incurs beneficial expenses (有益費) (soil improvement, irrigation, drainage works, etc.), they can demand reimbursement for these beneficial expenses when returning the land later (Civil Code Art. 608, Para. 2; Art. 196, Para. 2).
If the tenant demands reimbursement for beneficial expenses from the landlord, and the landlord does not pay, the tenant can refuse to return the land until payment is made.
Next are the tenant farmer's obligations.

(B) Tenant Farmer's Obligations <45>
First, land obtained for cultivation cannot be used arbitrarily. That is, one cannot create mulberry fields, orchards, or construct buildings (Civil Code Art. 616).
Second, the tenant farmer cannot transfer the leasehold right or tenancy right of the tenancy land to another person without the landlord's consent (Civil Code Art. 612, Para. 1).
Third, the tenant farmer cannot sublease the tenancy land to another person without the landlord's consent (Civil Code Art. 612, Para. 1). That is, intermediate tenancy is not recognized.
Fourth, it is common sense that the tenant farmer must pay rent to the landlord. Regarding the payment date, if there is a special agreement (特約), it follows the agreement; if there is a custom followed by the parties, it follows the custom; if there is neither a special agreement nor a custom followed by the parties, the payment time is set immediately after the end of the harvest season according to the provisions of Civil Code Art. 614.
Also, if the landlord refuses to accept the rent payment, the tenant can deposit (供託) the rent (Civil Code Art. 492 & Art. 494).
Besides these, there are various other obligations such as the obligation to return the land upon contract termination, but further explanation is omitted.
Summarizing the tenant farmer's status under civil law based on the above:
Rights (?) include:
Right to use and profit from tenancy land (leasehold right)
Right to demand repairs
Various rights to claim based on contract
Right to demand reduction due to force majeure
Right to terminate contract
Right to demand reimbursement for necessary and beneficial expenses
Right of retention
However, there is no right to assert against third parties, possessing only freedom in the specific terms of the contract.
And obligations include:
Obligation not to change the purpose of land use
Obligation not to transfer leasehold rights
Obligation not to sublease leasehold rights
Obligation to pay rent
Obligation to return land
Bearing these lopsided obligations, they lose practical freedom. Thus, for a long time, they have demanded tenancy rights that match in name and reality (名實相副), struggled to eliminate various unilateral (片務的) obligations, and wept under unjust extortion (苛斂誅求).
What effect will the Korean Farmland Ordinance truly bring about?
As can be understood from the comparative examination above, the Farmland Ordinance has quite a few deficiencies (踈漏) compared to the Outline of Tenancy Law and the Draft Tenancy Law, is not more substantial in content than the Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices, and can only be said to have codified a few items not stipulated in the Civil Code. <46>

Overall Picture of the Farmland Ordinance as Special Law
Having grasped the outline (槪略) of the Farmland Ordinance in the preceding sections, let us now examine what the Farmland Ordinance, as a special law, stipulates for ease of understanding.
Scope of Application = Applies to land leases (賃契借) for the purpose of cultivation (Art. 1) and contracts for undertaking (請負) land cultivation or other agreements (Art. 2, Para. 1). Does not apply to forest tenancy, salt field tenancy, sericulture tenancy, etc.
Saŭm (舍音) Regulation = The landlord must report (届出) to the Prefectural Governor (府尹), County Magistrate (郡守), or Island Administrator (島司): 1. Saŭm's address, name, occupation; 2. Location, land category, area of managed tenancy land; 3. Date of management contract; 4. Scope of management duties and period; 5. Remuneration and payment method (Art. 2 & Enforcement Rule Art. 1). The Prefectural Governor, County Magistrate, or Island Administrator can order changes if deemed inappropriate (Art. 4).
Tenancy Conditions = If parties cannot agree, the court decides (Art. 2, Para. 2). Appeals against the court's decision can be made within 2 weeks (Art. 2, Para. 3).
Invalidity Clause = Special agreements disadvantageous to the tenant that contradict provisions such as partial rent payment (15), rent reduction/exemption due to force majeure (16-1), notice of contract renewal (19), matters to observe during mediation (21), purchase of standing crops (22), etc., are deemed invalid (Art. 6).
Tenancy Period = 3 years for ordinary crops, 7 years for perennial crops (Art. 7, Para. 1). Terms shorter than these are considered 3 or 7 years (Art. 7, Para. 2). Contracts without a fixed term are also considered 3 or 7 years depending on the crop (Art. 7, Para. 3). Perennial crops are defined as mulberry fields, orchards, ramie (苧麻), and paper mulberry (楮) (Enforcement Rule Art. 6). Unclear terms are also presumed to be 3 or 7 years (Art. 8). This applies mutatis mutandis to contract renewals (Art. 9, Para. 1), but the 7-year term becomes 3 years unless renewing for the purpose of replanting perennial crops (Art. 9, Proviso). This does not apply when temporarily leasing land due to injury, illness, other unavoidable circumstances, or change in land use purpose, or other special reasons (Art. 10).
Inheritance = Heirs of either the landlord or tenant inherit the rights and obligations of the tenancy contract (Art. 11).
Real Right Effect (物權效力) = The tenancy contract has the effect of a real right even without registration (登記) (unlike the Civil Code, which requires registration) (Art. 12, Para. 1). Civil Code Art. 566, Paras. 1 & 3, and Art. 533 are applied mutatis mutandis (Art. 12, Paras. 2 & 3). (Content of Civil Code Art. 566, Para. 1: Buyer can terminate the contract or claim damages if unaware [of defects]. Content of Para. 3: Buyer can terminate or claim damages within 1 year of knowing the fact. Content of Civil Code Art. 533: In a bilateral contract, one party can refuse their own performance until the other party performs their obligation, unless the other party's obligation is not yet due.) <47>
Sublease (轉貸) = Subleasing (intermediate tenancy) is prohibited even with the landlord's consent (Art. 13, Para. 1). However, temporary subleasing is allowed if the tenant is unable to cultivate due to injury, illness, or other unavoidable circumstances (Art. 13, Para. 1, Proviso). In this case, the landlord cannot refuse the sublease (Art. 13, Para. 2). If subleasing occurs due to unavoidable circumstances, the sublease terminates when the circumstances cease. However, if crops planted according to the original contract can be harvested within 1 year from the time the reason for sublease ceased, the sublease continues until the harvest is completed, unless the sublessee planted in bad faith. The sublease also terminates if the sublessee does not wish to continue. Upon cessation of the reason for sublease, the sublessor must immediately notify the landlord and sublessee (Enforcement Rule Art. 7). The sublessee cannot further sublease or transfer the rights (Art. 13, Para. 4).
However, this does not apply when industrial cooperatives or other non-profit corporations or associations lease land from other landlords and sublet it to their members, or when Prefectures (府), Towns (邑), or Townships (面) lease land and sublet it to residents (Art. 14, Paras. 1, 2). But said members or residents cannot allow others to use or profit from the land (Art. 14, Para. 3).
Partial Rent Payment = The landlord cannot refuse acceptance of partial rent payment. Receiving partial payment is not presumed to mean the landlord has consented to a reduction or other requests from the tenant (Art. 15).
Rent Reduction/Exemption = If the harvest is significantly reduced due to force majeure, the tenant can request reduction or exemption from the landlord (Art. 16, Para. 1). This request must be made within 15 days from the start of harvest, unless a special agreement dictates otherwise (Art. 16, Para. 2 & Proviso). If there are grounds for postponement (宥恕), the request can be made at an appropriate time (Art. 16, Para. 3).
Inspection (檢見) = There are inspections to determine the rent amount and inspections regarding rent reduction/exemption (Art. 17). For the latter, an inspection must be requested within 8 months from the date the reduction/exemption was requested (Enforcement Rule Art. 8, Para. 1). If there are grounds for leniency (宥恕), it can be done at an appropriate time (Art. 8, Para. 1, Proviso). Requests for inspection cannot be refused (Art. 8, Para. 2). Inspections are conducted on a date agreed upon by the parties, in the presence (立令下) of the parties or their representatives (Enforcement Rule Art. 9).
Contract Renewal = If notice of the renewal date or notice of non-renewal without changing conditions is not given to the other party between 3 months and 1 year before the contract period ends, the contract is considered renewed under the previous conditions (Art. 18). The landlord cannot refuse renewal unless the tenant commits an act of bad faith (Art. 19), but can refuse if the landlord has reasonable grounds (Art. 19, Proviso).
Contract Termination = The landlord can terminate the contract if the tenant subleases the land without reason, if a sublessee (under unavoidable tenant circumstances) further subleases or transfers rights, or if members of non-profit entities or residents subleasing from Prefectures/Towns/Townships allow others to use or profit from the land (Art. 20). If a mediation request (調停申請) is filed by either party regarding rent reduction/exemption due to force majeure, the landlord cannot arbitrarily terminate the contract due to the tenant's rent arrears until the mediation <48> concludes (Art. 21).
Purchase of Standing Crops (毛上買取) = When returning the land, if there are crops cultivated according to the original contract, the tenant can demand the landlord purchase them at a reasonable price, except for crops planted by the tenant in bad faith (Art. 22).
Judgment Adjudication = Parties can jointly request adjudication (制定) from the Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committee regarding rent or other tenancy matters (Art. 24). Until the adjudication is finalized, if the matter is pending in court or mediation, the court can suspend the trial or mediation (Art. 25). If the Committee's adjudication is deemed significantly unfair, the court can cancel it (Art. 26, Para. 1).
Penalties = Anyone attending a Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committee meeting who leaks (漏泄) the proceedings (顚末) or opinions of the committee members faces a fine of up to 1,000 won (Art. 30). A landlord who fails to submit the Saŭm report (届出), submits a false (處僞) report, or violates an order to change Saŭm faces a fine of up to 300 won (Art. 31). The supplementary provisions mainly concern transitional rules, so a detailed explanation is deemed unnecessary and omitted.
Summarizing the above, the newly stipulated items as special law are:
Saŭm reporting system (届出制度)
Tenancy period of 3 years for ordinary crops, 7 for perennial crops
Real right effect (物權的 效力) of tenancy rights
Inheritance of tenancy rights
Partial rent payment
Landlord purchase of crops upon land return
Functions of the Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committee (府郡島小作委員會)

Pros and Cons of the Farmland Ordinance
1. Reducing the Authorities' Anxiety = Bumper crop panic (豊年恐慌), famine during bad harvests (凶年饑饉), intensifying spring poverty (春窮麥嶺), unbalanced fluctuations in rice prices (米價變動), collapsing silkworm cocoon prices (蠶繭價) causing only losses, survival on roots and bark (草根木皮延命), wandering displacement (流離轉顚), starvation (飢餓) - these tragic terms are unforgettable because of the reality of the countryside today.
Deeply rooted feudal legacies (盤根錯節) and the overwhelming waves (激浪滔天) of modern management pushing from front and back (前倒後押) have caused the cries for survival from the tenant masses – who constitute 80% of the 17-18 million farmers, themselves 80% of the 20 million population – to rise up one after another (接踵蜂起) everywhere, expressed in the modern term "tenancy disputes." To alleviate this, the ruling authorities (爲政當局) raised the slogan of self-reliant rehabilitation and attempted rural development. They tried implementing relief projects for the poor. They aim to stem the tide of tenant farmers, increasing by hundreds of thousands each year, by creating 2,000 owner-farmer households annually for 10 years, totaling 20,000 (自作農創定). They reform the tax system and encourage side businesses. However, these are all like a drop in the ocean (紅爐點雪), a drop of blood from a bird's foot (鳥足之血).
They lamented, "The tenancy rights of rehabilitated farm households (更生農家) remain unstable, their displacement occurs frequently across various regions, and disputes arising from this cause actually show an increasing trend (漸增)." <49>
Thus (於是乎), they eyed the Saŭm with suspicion. Let's correct the abuses (弊害) of the Saŭm. In Shōwa 3 (1928), they wielded a club – the aforementioned Administrative Superintendent's directive, the "Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices." Let's also stabilize tenancy rights somewhat. For these reasons, needing a law, they drafted the Tenancy Ordinance proposal. During this process, shrewd (?) landlords gathered and began protesting, saying, "Korean agriculture developed because we landlords came from the early Meiji period and guided the tenant farmers. Now, treating landlords coldly with a Tenancy Ordinance ignores our past contributions to Korea's development," or
"In Japan proper, the Tenancy Law passed the House of Representatives and even the Privy Council, but was crushed by the House of Peers. Trying to implement a Tenancy Ordinance in Korea one step ahead of its implementation in Japan proper... stems from the ambition (功名心) of officials wanting to try it first in Korea, where there are no obstacles."
But was it truly born out of ambition, as they claimed? Rather, it seems likely that rural instability was, in fact, the authorities' instability. Couldn't the Tenancy Ordinance be seen precisely as something created to alleviate that anxiety?
This can be inferred from the clear intentions within the Farmland Ordinance: preventing minor disputes (小爭) by thoroughly regulating Saŭm, saving face for the law by setting (at least nominally) a tenancy period, and intending for the Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committees and courts to prevent major conflicts.
2. Advantages for Landlords - Then, as the landlords themselves claimed, did the Farmland Ordinance treat them coldly? Even without citing the authorities' statement, "We are not trying to protect only the interests of tenant farmers. The legitimate interests of landlords will be defended to the end," the advantages for landlords can be scattered throughout (散見) the Farmland Ordinance.
First, while the Saŭm reporting system (舍音届出制) might be a hassle for landlords who prefer ease (安閑を是事とする) due to procedural complexities (煩弊), how reassuring must this regulation be for absentee landlords? Originally, Saŭm were not just demons (惡鬼) to tenant farmers but also sprites (妖鬼) before landlords, a fact landlords themselves likely know better.
Second, how grateful must landlords be that the court intervenes to handle disputes over tenancy conditions before they escalate?
Third, since the law prohibits subleasing (轉貸) of tenancy land, anxieties about land preservation will be swept away (一掃).
Fourth, although tenancy rights are supposedly fixed for 3 years, the clause about the tenant's "act of bad faith" (배신행위) is not clearly defined, raising fears of landlords' malicious interpretations (惡意解釋).
Fifth, the provision stating that "the lessor can terminate the tenancy contract if they have reasonable grounds" not only fails to treat landlords coldly but actually raises concerns about tenant insecurity.
Sixth, being able to seek adjudication from the Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committee on any tenancy-related matter, including rent, allows them to sleep soundly with a high pillow (高枕甘睡).
3. Tenant Farmer's Interests - First, granting tenancy rights, previously unprotected <50> by general law, the effect of a real right to assert against third parties is somewhat fortunate. However, if this right lasted 50 or 70 years, it might be meaningful, but a mere 3 or 7 years feels like scratching an itch through a boot (隔靴掃癢).
Second, the partial mitigation of the abuses of the Saŭm, a feudal remnant that toyed with tenants' lives and violated human rights, is something that would gradually be resolved by tenant awareness anyway, but this can be seen as accelerating the process of eliminating its chaotic shadow (亂影消滅).
Third, landlords in the Korean countryside, whether native (土着地主) or immigrant (舶來地主), are generally insatiably greedy (貪不己), often making unreasonable demands on tenants (作人). The provision invalidating private agreements disadvantageous to tenants beyond legally specified conditions could offer some help to tenants capable of utilizing it.
Fourth, tenant farm households often face displacement (流離) after the head of the household dies, largely due to the loss of labor power when the family pillar falls. Losing the source of livelihood, the cultivated land (作地), on top of that is adding frost to snow (雪上加霜). The Farmland Ordinance stipulates inheritance, providing, though not substantial, some aid for bereaved family relief (遺族救助).
Fifth, landlords refusing partial rent payment, demanding tenants return to fill the measure even if short by a single hop (升) per søk (石), is a common tactic (常套). Prohibiting this refusal, while a matter of course, might suffice to remedy the immediate problem (眼前\*을 醫하기에는 足할 것이다). (*Note: 眼前 seems misused here, perhaps intended to mean 燃眉之急 'singed eyebrows emergency' or similar).
Sixth, restraining shameless landlords who demand the full rent regardless of flood, drought, wind damage, or insect infestation, by allowing tenants to claim rent reduction or exemption due to force majeure, might also be a blessing (萬石?) for pitiful tenants unable to even secure such relief voluntarily.
Comparing this to the Outline of Tenancy Law, the Draft Tenancy Law, or the Plan for Improving Tenancy Practices, necessary elements are missing, and things that should be complete are unsatisfactory. The differences between this special ordinance and the Civil Code can be understood from the discussions in the respective sections. Now, summarizing simply again, looking at the articles of this ordinance and related regulations: the Farmland Ordinance has 40 articles, its Enforcement Rules have 13, the Prefectural, County, Island Tenant Farmer Committee Organization Decree has 10, and the Committee Regulations have 30, totaling 93 articles. However, throughout the whole, the main points can be divided into three. First is the granting of real right status (物權附與) to tenancy rights, albeit meagerly ending the previous simple use/profit right, and setting a tenancy period. Next is clarifying the Saŭm regulations, subjecting them to the interference of Prefectural Governors, County Magistrates, or Island Administrators. Another is organizing the Tenant Farmer Committees, mobilizing the Prefectural Governor, County Magistrate, or Island Administrator, Tenancy Officials, and other potential committee members – the so-called influential figures – to handle adjudication (判 定) and conciliation (勸解) regarding tenancy issues. Among these three, the first merely maintains the facade of tenancy legislation, while the fundamental spirit of the legislation can be perceived (看取) in the latter two. Consequently, the movement of one side is extremely restricted, while the linkage (連環) of the other side becomes extensive and incomparably strong (廣汎且堅牽無比). <51>

**Summary**

This 1934 editorial by Chŏng In'gwan critically examines the newly enacted Korean Farmland Ordinance under Japanese rule. Chŏng argues that while ostensibly addressing tenant farmer issues, the Ordinance primarily serves the interests of landlords and the colonial authorities by controlling disputes rather than granting substantial rights. He compares it unfavorably to earlier Japanese proposals (like the "Outline of Tenancy Law"), noting omissions like the tenant's right of first refusal or compensation for improvements, and the short tenancy periods (3-7 years). While acknowledging minor improvements like regulating intermediaries (Saŭm) and allowing inheritance, he concludes the law strengthens landlord control and state oversight (via Tenant Farmer Committees) more than it empowers tenants, representing a superficial attempt to manage rural unrest rather than enact genuine reform.

For historians, this article offers a contemporary Korean intellectual's critical perspective on Japanese colonial agricultural policy. It highlights the political maneuvering behind the legislation, the perceived inadequacy of the reforms from the tenants' viewpoint, the explicit comparison with Japanese domestic proposals (revealing colonial disparities), and the detailed breakdown of the law's provisions and their likely limited impact on the deeply unequal landlord-tenant relationship, offering insights into the socio-economic tensions and legal frameworks of colonial Korea.

(甲) 소작인의 권리
소작인은 소작지에 대한 사용 及 수익의 권리가 잇다. 그것은 즉 토지의 임차권이다.
소작지 혹은 소작지의 固有附屬工作物에 파손이 생긴 경우에는 그 修繕을 지주에게 청구할 수 잇고 소작지사용에 대해서 어떤 방해가 잇다면 그 배제를 지주에게 청구할 수 잇으며 계약과 상반되는 소작지의 하자가 잇다면 지주에게 손해배상을 청구할 수 잇다. 소작인은 불가항력으로 말미암아 수확이 계약한 소작료보다 減收될 때에는 그 收穫高만치 소작료감액을 求할 수가 잇다.(민법 제609조)
소작인은 자기과실이 아닌데 소작지 일부가 滅失된 때는 그 멸실된 부분만치 소작료 감액을 청구할 수 잇다. (민법 제611조 제1항) 그리고 일부 滅失된 소작지가 借地目的에 相副치 못하는 경우에는 소작인은 소작계약해제를 할 수가 잇다.(민법 제611조)
소작인은 불가항력에 인하야 引續 2년 이상 소작료보다 적은 수확밖에 안날 때에는 소작계약해제를 할 수가 잇다.(민법제610조)
지주가 소작인 의사에 반하야 소작지 보존행위를 하고저 하는 경우에 그 때문에 소작계약대로 목적을 達할 수 없을 때는 소작인은 지주에게 대하야 소작계약해제를 할 수 잇다.(민법 제 607조)
소작인이 소작지에 대하야 지주가 부담할 必要費를 지출한 때는 지주에게 즉시 그 비용상환을 청구할 수가 잇다.(민법 제608조) 必要費라는 것은 該小作地를 使用收益할 수 잇는 상태의 必要費이다.
그리고 소작인이 右必要費의 상환을 지주에게 청구하엿는데 지주가 지불치 않는 때는 그 전부의 상환을 받기까지 그것을 이유로해서 토지반환을 거절할 수 잇다.(민법 제295조 제296조) 이런 경우에 소작인이 행사하는 권리를 留置權이라고 한다.
소작인이 有益費(客土, 灌漑, 排水工事 등)의 지출을 하엿는데 그 후 토지를 반환할 때에 有益費의 상환을 청구할 수가 잇다. (민법 제608조 제2항 同 제196조 제2항)
소작인이 有益費상환을 지주에게 청구하엿는데 지주가 지불치 않으면 지불할 때까지 토지반환을 거절할 수 잇다.

(乙)소작인의 의무＜45＞
첫재로 경작하기 위해서 얻은 토지를 마음대로 사용하지 못한다. 즉 桑園 과수원을 만든다든지 건축같은 것을 못한다.(민법 제616조)
둘재로 소작인은 지주의 승낙이 없으면 소작지의 임차권 소작권을 다른 사람에게 양도하지 못한다. (민법 제612조 제1항)
셋재로 소작인은 지주의 승낙이 없이는 소작지를 다른 사람에게 轉貸할 수가 없다.(민법 612조 1항) 즉 중간소작을 인정치 않는다.
넷재로 소작인은 지주에게 소작료를 지불치 않으면 안되는 것은 보통상식이다. 그 지불기일에 대하야는 特約이 잇으면 特約대로 당사자가 좇는 관습이 잇으면 그 관습대로 만일 특약도 없고 당사자가 관습도 좇지 않는 때는 민법 제614조의 규정에 의하야 수확계절 종료 직후로써 지불시기하도룩 되엿다.
또 소작인이 작료를 지불하는데도 지주가 그 領收를 거절하는 경우에는 그 소작료를 供託할 수가 잇다. (민법 제492조 及 同 제494조)

(민법 제566조 제1항의 내용은 買方이 모르고 삿을 때에 계약해제할 수 잇고 또는 손해배상을 청구할 수 잇다는 것이오 同條 제3항의 내용은 買主가 그 사실을 안때로부터 1년 내에 계약해제 又는 손해배상을 청구할 수 잇다는 것이오 민법 제533조의 내용은 雙契約者 당사자의 한쪽은 상대방이 債務履行을 하기까지 自己債務履行을 거절할 수 잇는데 상대방의 債務가 辨濟期가 아닌 때는 此限에 不在라는 것이다)＜47＞
轉貸=토지의 승낙이 잇어도 轉貸는(중간소작 못하고(제13조 제1항) 소작인이 傷痍疾 病 기타 부득이한 사정으로 경작불능한 때는 일시 轉貸할 수 잇는데(同條 제1항 但出)이 경우에 지주는 轉貸를 거절 못하며(제13조 제2항)부득이한 사정으로 轉貸하는 경우에 그 사정이 소멸되는 때는 轉貸借도 종료되는데 그때에 처음 계약대로 作付한 작물로서 轉貸事由消滅의 時로부터 1년 내에 수확할 것이 잇다면 轉借人이 악의로 作付하지 않은 이상 수확종료하기까지 轉貸借는 존속하고 轉借人이 존속을 원하지 않으면 물론 종료되는데 轉貸事由가 소멸되자 轉貸人은 곧 지주와 轉借人에게 통지해야 한다고(同令시행규칙 제7조)하엿으며 轉借人은 다시 轉貸를 하거나 또는 권리를 양도할 수가 없다.(제13조 제4항)
그러나 산업조합 기타 영리를 목적하지 않는 法人 又는 단체가 다른 지주에게서 賃借한 소작지를 그 단체원에 소작시키때 府邑面이 賃借한 소작지를 주민에게 소작식히는 것은 관계치 않은데 (제14조 1, 2항)前記 단체원 又는 주민이 다시 그 소작지의 사용수익을 타인에게 시키면 안된다.(제14조 제3항)
소작료일부지불=지주는 소작료의 일부 지불에 대한 受領을 거절할 수 없고 일부를 受領하엿다고 해서 곧 소작인에게 감액 기타 所請을 승낙한 것으로 추정치 않는다.(제15조)
소작료감액면제=불가항력 때문에 수확이 현저히 감소햇으면 소작인은 지주에게 감액 又는 면제를 청구할 수 잇는데(제16조 제1항) 수확 착수일로부터 15일 이전에 신청해야 하고 別段의 特約이 잇으면 그 시기에 할 것이며(同條 제2항 及 但出) 유예할 사유가 잇는 때는 상당한 시기에 신청할 수 잇다.(同條제3항)
檢見=소작료액을 정하는 檢見과 소작료감액면제에 대한 檢見이 잇는데(제17조) 후자의 경우에는 감액면제를 신청한 날로부터 8월 이내에 檢見을 신청해야 하고 (同令 시행규정 제8조 제1항) 宥恕할 사유가 잇으면 상당한 시기에 할 것이며(同條 제1항 但出)檢見申出은 거절치 못하게 되엿다. (同條 제2항)그리고 檢見은 당사자 합의로 정한 기일에 당사자 又는 대리인 立令下에 한다.(同令 시행규칙 제9조)
계약갱신=계약기간종료전 3월 내지 1년 이내에 상대방에게 갱신 시일이 언제라는 통지 또는 조건을 고치지 않고는 갱신치 않겟다는 통지를 하지 않으면 계약이 이전 조건대로 갱신된 것으로 간주한다고(제18조)하며 지주는 소작인에게 배신행위가 없는 한 갱신거부를 못하나(제19조) 지주에게 상당한 사정이 잇으면 할 수 잇다(同條但出)고 하엿다.
계약해제=소작인이 소작지를 無故히 轉貸햇다든지 轉貸人의 부득이한 사정으로 인한 轉借人인 다시 轉貸 又는 권리양도를 햇다든지 또는 비영리법인 又는 단체 及 府邑面에서 轉借한 단체원 又는 府邑面住民이 다른 사람으로 하야금 그 소작지의 사용수익을 하게 햇을 때는 지주가 계약을 해제할 수 잇다.(제20조) 불가항력에 인한 소작료 경감면제에 대한 문제로 어느 쪽에서든지 調停申請이 잇을 때는 調停이 끝날＜48＞ 때까지 소작인이 소작료를 체납한다고 지주가 마음대로 계약해제를 못하기로 되어 잇다.(제21조)
毛上買取=토지반환할 때에 처음 계약대로 경작한 농작물이 잇으면 소작인은 지주에게 상당한 가격으로 買取하기를 청구할 수 잇는대 소작인이 악의로 경작한 것은 안된다고 하엿다.(제22조)
판정재판=소작료 기타 소작관계에 대해서 당사자 합의로 府郡島小作委員會에 제정을 구할 수 잇는데(제24조) 그 판정이 끝날 때까지 그 동안에 재판 혹은 調停 중의 것이면 재판소는 그 재판 혹은 調停을 중지할 수 잇으며(제25조) 該小作委員會의 판정이 현저히 부당하다고 인정될 때는 재판소에서 그 판정을 취소할 수 잇게 되엿다.(제26조 제1항)
벌칙=府郡島小作委員會에 출석햇든 자가 회의의 顚末小作委員의 의견을 漏泄하면 1,000원 이하의 벌금이 잇고(제30조) 지주가 舍音에 대한 届出을 하지 않는다든지 處僞의 届出을 한다든지 舍音變更命令에 위반하면 300원 이하의 벌금이 잇다.(제31조) 부칙은 주로 경과규정이므로 긴 설명이 필요치 않을 줄로 생각하고 그만두기로 하다.

農地令의 得失二三 一. 당국의 불안을 減한 점=豊年恐慌, 凶年饑饉 심각화해가는 春窮麥嶺 군형을 잃은 米價變動, 결손만 보는 蠶繭價폭락 草根木皮延命, 流離轉顚, 飢餓 등 그 비참한 文字는 오늘날 농촌이 잇기 때문에 우리들의 기억에 살아진지 않는 文字들이다.
盤根錯節한 봉건유산과 激浪滔天의 근대적 경영이 前倒後押하야 2,000만 인구의 8할을 차지한 농민, 1천 7,8백萬 농민의 8할을 점령한 소작대중으로 하여금 도처에서 생을 부르짓는 叫號가 接踵蜂起하야 소작쟁의라는 현대어로 표현되고 잇다. 이를 완화하기 위하야 爲政當局은 자력갱생의 표어를 들고 나와 농촌진흥을 꾀하엿다. 窮民救濟工事를 시행해 보앗다. 1년에 2,000戶式 10년에 20,000戶의 自作農創定으로 매년 수십만호가 늘어가는 소작농을 막으려고 한다. 稅制를 정리하고 부업을 장려한다. 그러나 모도다 紅爐點雪이요 鳥足之血에 불과하다.
「更生農家의 小作權은 依然히 안정되지 않어 其 移動은 빈번히 각지에 행하고 잇어 그것을 원인으로 하는 쟁의가 도로혀 漸增의 경향을 呈한다」고 자탄하기에 이르럿다. ＜49＞
於是乎 눈흘겨 본 것이 舍音이엿다. 舍音의 弊害를 矯正해 보자. 昭和3년에 방망이 하나를 내리워 보앗스니 前記 政務總監通牒의 「小作慣行改善案」이 그것이다. 또 소작권을 幾分間 안정시켜주자 이러한 이유하에 법을 필요로 하여서 小作令案을 꾸미는 중에 怜悧(?)한 지주들이 모혀 「明治 初年부터 우리들 지주가 와서 소작인을 지도한데서 朝鮮農業이 발전되엿다. 그런데 이제 小作令으로 지주를 냉대함은 우리들 지주의 과거 朝鮮開發의 공로를 무시함이라」 또는
「日本內地에서 小作法이 衆議院에서 통과되고 樞密院도 지나왓으나 貴族院에서 물크러트렷다. 日本內地에서 시행안된 小作令을 朝鮮에서 한거름 앞서 시행하려는 것은... 아무 장애가 없는 朝鮮에다 먼저 시행해 보겟다는 관리의 功名心에서 나온 것이다」하고 들고 이러나기 시지 하엿으나
과연 그말과 같이 공명심에서 나온 것이냐 하면 그보다도 농촌의 불안은 즉 당국의 불안인 것은 사실이엿을 것이다. 小作令은 正히 그 불안을 덜기 위하야 나어은놓것 이라고도 볼 수 잇지 아니할까?
舍音규정을 철저하게 함으로써 小爭을 防遏하고 소작기간을(명분으로나마) 정함으로서 법의 면목을 세우고 府郡島小作委員會와 재판소로 하야금 큰 싸홈을 막으려는 의도가 農地令 속에 歷歷히 보히고 잇는 것으로도 알 수가 잇다.
二. 지주의 利點-그러면 지주 자신들의 말한 바와 같이 農地令은 지주를 냉대한 것인가? 당국의 「다만 소작인만의 이익을 보호하랴는 것이 아니다. 지주의 정당한 이익은 어대까지 옹호하야」라는 말을 援用하지 않드라도 農地令의 到處에서 지주의 이점을 散見할 수 잇다.
첫재로 舍音届出制가 手續의 煩弊가 잇어서 불편하다면 安閑을 是事로 하는 지주들의 고통일지 모로겟으나 이 규정이 잇음으로해서 不在 지주가 얼마나 안심될 것인가? 원래 舍音이란 것은 소작인에게만 惡鬼노릇을 한 것이 아니라 지주앞에 妖鬼노릇을 한 줄은 지주들 자신이 더 잘 알 일이다.
둘재 소작조건의 결정에 대해서 분쟁이 이러나기 전에 재판소가 가로 맡아서 처리해주니 지주들에게 얼마나 감사한 일인가?
셋재 소작지를 轉貸못하게 법으로 제재해주니 토지보전에 대한 불안이 一掃될 것이오.
넷재 소작권이 2년 간 鉦着하게 되엿다 하나 소작인의 배신행위라는 것은 조건부가 아닌 만큼 지주들의 惡意解釋을 두려워 하는 바이다.
다섯재 「賃貸人에게 상당한 사정이 잇을 때는 소작계약을 해제한다는 것이 잇으니 지주를 냉대하기는 고사하고 도로혀 소작인의 불안이 우려된다.
여섯재 소작에 관한 것이면 소작료든지 무엇이든지 府郡島小作委員會의 판정을 구할 수 잇으니 더욱 더 高枕甘睡할 수 잇을 것다.
三. 소작인의 利害-첫재 종래의 일반법으로 전연 보장되지＜50＞ 못하든 소작권을 위선 物權의 효력을 인정해서 제삼자에게 대항하게 한 것은 어느 정도로 다행한 일이나 그 권리를 50년이나 70년쯤 지속한다면 모르거니와 겨우 3년 7년이라는 것은 隔靴掃惡의 感이 不無하다.
둘재 소작인의 생명을 농락하고 인권을 유린하든 봉건적 잔재인 舍音의 惡弊를 幾分間 緩和하게 된 것은 소작인의 자각에 의해서도 점차 청산될 것이려니와 亂影消滅의 과정에 박차를 가한 것이라고 할 수 잇다.
셋재 원래 朝鮮農村의 지주들이란 土着地主거나 舶來地主거나를 불문하고 람貪不己하는지라 作人에게 무리한 강요가 많은데 법의 지정한 조건 이외의 소작인에게 불리한 私約을 무효로 한다는 것은 應用할 능력이 잇는 한 소작인에게 약간의 도음이 안되는 바 아니다.
넷재 戶主가 사망한 후의 소작농가는 자칫하면 流離하게 되는 것은 일가의 支柱가 넘어짐으로 해서 노동력을 喪失하는 관계가 많으나 그 우에 生命扶持의 원천인 作地까지 奪還되는 것은 雪上加霜이 아닐 수 없다. 農作令은 상속을 규정하엿으니 그리 厚하지는 못하나마 遺族救助의 한 도움이 되는 것은 사실이다.
다섯재 一石에 一升이 못차도 다시 채워가지고 오라고 지주가 소작료의 일부 지불을 거절하는 것은 常套 수단이다. 그런대 그것을 거절치 못하게 한 것은 당연한 일이면서도 眼前*을 醫하기에는 足할 것이다.
여섯재 수재가 나든지 旱災가 들든지 風災가 지나든지 虫災가 침범햇든지 정액만 가저 오라는 몰염치한 지주를 견재한 것으로 불가항력에 인한 소작료 감액 又는 면제청구를 할 수 잇게 된 것도 그런 것 조차 자발적으로 못 免하는 불상한 소작인의 萬石이 될는지?
小作法要綱 及 小作法草案이나 또는 小作慣行改善案에 此하야 잇어야 할 것이 업는 것이며 완전해야 할 것이 불만족케 된 점과 특별법으로서의 본법령이 민법과 상이한 점은 當該 각항의 논술을 보아 알 수 잇거니와 이제 다시 단순히 거두어 말하면 본령 及 그 관계법규의 조문으로 볼 때에 農地令이 全文 40조요 동령 시행규칙이 전문 13조요 府郡島小作委員會官制가 全文10조요 同委員會規程이 全文 30조로 도합 93조에 亙하나 전체를 통하야 그 중요점은 세가지로 구분할 수 잇으니 그 하나는 빈약하나마 종래의 단순한 사용수익에 끄치든 소작권의 物權附與 及 小作期間을 정한 것이요 다음은 舍音規定을 분명히 해서 府尹, 郡守 又는 島司의 간섭을 받게 한 것이요 또 하나는 小作委員會를 조직하야 府尹, 郡守, 又는 島司 及 小作官 기타 委員될 소위 유력자를 총동원하야 소작문제에 관한 한 판정, 勸解를 하게 한 것이다. 이 세가지 중에서 첫재 것은 소작법규의 명분을 유지한대 끄치고 입법의 근본정신이 後二者에 잇음을 看取할 수가 잇는데 이로 말미암아 한 편의 동향이 극도로 제한되고 다른 한 편의 連環이 廣汎且堅牽無比하게 된 것이다.＜51＞
