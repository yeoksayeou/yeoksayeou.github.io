**Body Text Translation:**
Editor's Musings

There was much anticipation from the general public for Kaebyŏk, and its popularity was extraordinary, such that the first issue sold out just a few days after publication, and there were many places where we could not send books to those who ordered later from the provinces. As editors, while we are personally glad, we apologize to our readers in the provinces.

Though it sounds like self-praise, the content of this issue is even more substantial than the first. To briefly mention just the overlapping articles, the essays such as Pak Yŏnghŭi's "Re-recognition of Chosŏn Culture," Chŏng In’gwan's "Review of the Chosŏn Farmland Decree," and Sin T’aeik's "Outlook on Chosŏn Rice Prices" are pertinent to the reality of Chosŏn and worth reading for anyone. Hong Sŭnghyo's "Grand View of the Current Political Situation in the Emergency" and Sin Ir’yong's "The Danger of the European Political Situation," along with Kim Tongsu's poetry review, are insightful analyses of the domestic and international political situation during this emergency period and are also worth seeing. The literary section, beginning with the full-length novels by Yŏm Sangsŏp and Hyŏn Min, features creative fiction by Yi Hyosŏk and Ŏm Hŭngbyŏn, a play by Kim Unjŏng, works by Pak Wŏlt’an, and others, amounting to a grand total of over 100 pages. For intermediate reading, there is our Shanghai correspondent Kang Sŏnggu's "Blood Sacrifice of an International Love Triangle," Sin Sŏgu's "Confessions of a Newspaper President," Yi Sŏgu's continuation of the Anak secrets, the historical tale of Hong Mokch’un's 500-Year History of Factional Strife, and the Account of the Kapsin Coup, among others—all are intensely interesting and will surely be good companions under the lamp on a long night. We recommend everyone give it a read.

Finally, another piece of good news to deliver is that Hwoewŏl (懹月) Pak Yŏnghŭi, who took responsibility for the literary section and put in much effort during the earlier Kaebyŏk era, has rejoined the company this month and taken charge of editing this magazine. We hope all our readers will rejoice together and anticipate great activity on these pages.

This is the second issue of the newly launched "Kaebyŏk". It is a joyous occasion that the Kaebyŏk Company (開闢社), after struggling ceaselessly for over 10 years, is able to publish a new Kaebyŏk today, but it is also not an easy thing for me personally to have this second connection with the company.
During the long passage of time, personnel matters have become complex and the ways of the world have changed greatly, so meeting old acquaintances again is also not an easy thing.

While walking a difficult path, the Kaebyŏk Company sent two workers to the next world and placed one friend in a sanatorium <92> while only Ch’ŏng’o and Miso are engaged in a lonely struggle. Although both are undoubtedly the same people as before, the many wrinkles that have increased significantly on their faces perhaps speak of the traces of their lives? It is fortunate, I think, that there is still nothing wrong with Ch’ŏng’o hyŏng's eloquence. In these desolate circumstances, my presence should be of great help, but I lament that I too am an unskilled (拙劣) figure, so there is no reason for anything great to emerge. However, my plan is to try to make it solid, even if small; long-lasting, even if weak; tasty, even if not flashy; interesting, even if not splendid. Secondly, I aim to create a magazine where one can gain even a little common sense about reality and knowledge about academics. I hope for much guidance from the readers. (*Wŏl)

The reappearance of Kaebyŏk magazine! This is truly like an "oasis" in the desert, an unprecedented spectacle in the magazine world, and it is extremely gratifying that we achieved results better than expected amidst everyone's absolute praise and support. However, more than the sincerity of us few, this makes us feel anew the gravity of our responsibility, realizing just how great the public anticipation for this book was.

Thus, with redoubled strength and effort, we finished this issue and have already long since turned our hands to editing the New Year issue. Furthermore, joining hands again with Hwoewŏl Pak Yŏnghŭi, one of the figures connected to the old Kaebyŏk magazine, we declare that the majestic form of the special New Year's issue, molded with our utmost energy, will be an epoch-making editorial feat that draws a major line in the history of Chosŏn magazines.

And there is one word of frank advice for the devoted readers of our company's publications "New Woman" (新女性), "Pyŏlgŏn’gon" (別乾坤), and "Children" (어린이). As already announced through correspondence or in newspapers, due to the continuation of Kaebyŏk and the consequent increase in various company tasks, the previously published "New Woman," "Pyŏlgŏn’gon," and "Children" have all been delayed and could not be published on time. However, "Pyŏlgŏn’gon"'s next issue will come out in December, "Children" will also be published then, and "New Woman" will resume publication from next spring. We ask for your particular understanding in this matter. (Miso) <93>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the second issue of the relaunched *Kaebyŏk* magazine (December 1934) celebrates the unexpected success of the first issue, outlines the diverse contents of the current issue (covering culture, politics, economics, literature, and history pertinent to contemporary Chosŏn), and announces the return of former literary editor Pak Yŏnghŭi (Hwoewŏl). The editors acknowledge the challenges faced by the publishing house, including the loss of colleagues and the struggles of the remaining staff (Ch’ŏng’o, Miso). They express their renewed commitment to producing a high-quality, informative magazine and preview an ambitious New Year's issue. Finally, they apologize for delays in publishing sister magazines *New Woman*, *Pyŏlgŏn’gon*, and *Children* due to the demands of relaunching *Kaebyŏk*.

For historians, this piece offers valuable insights into the Korean publishing world under Japanese colonial rule. It highlights the perceived intellectual and cultural vacuum (*Kaebyŏk* as an "oasis"), the specific socio-political concerns addressed (Chosŏn culture, land decrees, rice prices, the "emergency situation"), the personal struggles and resilience of Korean intellectuals and publishers, and the operational difficulties (staffing, financial pressures suggested by delays) faced by cultural enterprises during this period. The return of Pak Yŏnghŭi, a significant literary figure, also underscores the magazine's ambition to reclaim its former prominence.

編輯餘墨
開闢은 일반의 企待가 많엇더니만치 인기가 비상하야 第 1號가 발행된지 불과 몃칠에 품절되야 지방에서 追後注文하시는 분에게는 冊을 보내 드리지 못한 곳이 많다. 編輯者로서 스사로 깃버하는 동시 地方讀者 여러분께 미안을 謝한다.

自畵自贊과 가트나 이번 號는 第 1號보다 내용이 더욱 충실하다. 記事의 중첩한 것만 대강 말하자면 朴英熙氏의 『朝鮮文化의 再認識』 鄭寅寬氏의 『朝鮮農地令檢討』 申泰翊氏의 『朝鮮米價의 展望』등 論文은 朝鮮現實에 적절한 논문으로 누구나 읽을 만하고 洪承孝氏의 『非常時現政局大觀』 辛日鎔氏의 『歐洲 政國의 危險』 金東秀君의 詩評은 비상시 內外政局을 洞察明觀한 논문으로 또한 볼만하며 文藝篇은 廉尙燮, 玄民氏의 長篇을 爲始하야 李孝石, 嚴興變氏의 창작소설, 金雲汀氏의 희곡 朴月灘氏 등 堂堂 100頁이상에 달하고 中間讀物로 本社上海特派員 姜聖九氏의 國際三角愛의 血祭, 申錫雨氏의 신문사장 참회록, 李瑞求氏의 안악의 비밀 계속 史話로 洪木春의 500년 黨爭史 外甲申政變記 등은 모다 興味津津하야 長夜靑燈에 단연 好伴侶일 것이다. 누구나 一讀하기를 勸한다.

끗흐로 또 한가지 깃분 소식을 전해 들일 것은 전날 開闢時代에 文藝篇책임을 마터서 만흔 노력을 하면 懹月 朴英熙氏가 이 달부터 다시 入社하야 本誌의 編輯責任을 지게 된 것이다. 讀者 여러분도 다 가티 깃버하며 본지면의 大活躍이 잇을 것을 기대하기 바란다.

새로 나온 「開闢」은 이것이 第 2號다. 開闢社는 10여년간 不絶히 분투하야 오늘날 다시 새로운 開벽을 발행하게 됨도 깁분 일이지만 내 자신이 두 번재 社와 因緣이 잇게 된 것도 그리 쉬운 일은 아니다.
오랜 歲月이 흐르는 동안 人事는 복잡하고 세태는 變遷이 만엇으니 옛사람을 또 다시 맛남은 이것도 쉬운 일이 아니다.

開闢社는 어려운 길을 거러오는 동안에 두사람의 일군을 저 世上으로 보내고 한사람의 친구를 療養院에 ＜92＞ 너어 두고 오즉 靑吾, 微笑 두분이 孤軍奮鬪를 하는 중이다. 두분이 다 예전의 그이에 틀림이 없스나, 그들의 얼굴에는 많은 주름쌀이 헐신 느른 것이 그 인생의 자최를 말함인가? 靑吾兄의 才辯에는 아즉도 이상이 없으니 萬幸이라고 생각한다. 이러한 쓸쓸한 살림에 나의 존재는 많은 助力이 되어야겟으나, 나 亦 拙劣한 인물이라 무슨 거물이 나올 까닭이 없는 것을 탄식하나, 적드라도 단단하게, 弱하드라도 장수하게, 번치는 없드라도 맛잇게, 화려하지는 못하드라도 자미잇게 만들어 볼까 하는 것이 나의 腹案이며 둘재로는 현실에 대한 상식, 학문에 대한 지식을 조금이라도 어들 수 잇는 잡지를 만들어 볼가 하는  바이다. 독자들의 많은 편달이 잇기 바란다.(*月)

開闢誌의 再한 出現! 이것은 맛치 사막의 『오아시쓰』와 갓치 雜誌界空前의 盛觀으로 여러분의 절대한 賞讚과 성원 속에서 예상이상으로 조흔 성과를 어든 것은 대단히 깃분 일이다. 그러나 이것은 우리 멧 사람의 정성보다도 이 冊에 대한 一般의 企待가 얼마나 컷든 것을 헤아려 알 수 잇는 점에서 우리의 책임이 더 한층 중해짐을 거듭 늣기게 된다.

그리하야 우리는 倍前의 힘과 노력으로 이번 호의 끗을 내고 다시 新年號編輯에로 손을 옴긴지도 임의 오래이다. 더욱히 예전 開闢誌 緣故者의 한분이든 懹月 朴英熙氏와 다시 손을 맛잡고 우리들의 최대 精力을 다- 하야 비저질 新年特大號의 偉容이야 말로 朝鮮雜誌史上에 一大劃線을 그을 경이적 편집일 것을 斷言하는 바이다.

그리고 本社發行 『新女性』 『別乾坤』 『어린이』 愛讀者 여러분께 한가지 苦言을 드릴 것이 잇다. 임의 통신으로 또는 신문지상으로 발표한 것이 잇스닛가 아시는 분은 아시겟지만 今般 開闢續判과 아울너 사내의 여러 가지 일이 늘물 따라 從來 發行의 新女性 特乾坤 『어린이』 등이 죄다 제때 발행을 못하고 밀려나온 것이다. 그러나 別乾坤은 12월 중에 그 續刊號가 나올 것이고 어린이도 그 때에 出刊되겟스며 新女性도 明春부터 다시 續刊될 것이니 이 점을 특히 양해해 주기 바라는 것이다. (미소) ＜93＞
