"Grandfather, when you go to Seoul, you must walk on the left side. Because in Seoul, everyone observes left-side passage (左側通行)."

"Hey, stop talking such nonsense. If everyone walks on the left, who walks on the right..." <93>

**Summary**

This very short piece presents a brief dialogue between a younger person and their grandfather about traffic rules in Seoul. The younger person explains the mandatory left-side passage rule, while the grandfather dismisses it as nonsensical, questioning who would use the right side if everyone must use the left. From a historical perspective, this dialogue highlights the imposition and potential public confusion surrounding modern, standardized traffic regulations (specifically left-side passage, reflecting Japanese influence) in colonial Korea during the 1930s. It offers a glimpse into the everyday adjustments and misunderstandings faced by ordinary people encountering new urban systems under colonial rule.
