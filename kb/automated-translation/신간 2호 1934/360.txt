Idle Talk about Newspapers (新聞漫談)

While foreign newspapers focus primarily on reporting and rarely write editorials unless there's a significant issue, newspapers in Chosŏn (朝鮮), for some reason, seem to think they absolutely must write an editorial every day, just like a language course in an ordinary school. Given this, and since there are hardly any incidents in Chosŏn, what material could even the most renowned commentators possibly find? The topics are often like elementary school writing prompts or the songs of idle people (閒臥人). Recently, a certain paper (某報) even wrote an editorial about the attempted suicide of a café waitress. If it's come to that, wouldn't it be a more elevated argument to write about abolishing café waitresses altogether? It would be refreshing if someone wrote an "editorial abolition theory" for all companies starting next year.

At the Chungang Ilbo (中央日報), it's said that the company executives resolved to issue an order to the general reporters: "Reporters of the Chungang Ilbo who write for other newspapers or magazines will face appropriate punishment (處罰), so absolutely refrain from writing (집필) from now on." While this might be understandable for the sake of ensuring faithful work for the company, isn't it excessively restricting individual freedom (個人自由) for a newspaper that supposedly champions freedom of the press (言論自由)? Incidentally, what are the feelings (所感) of the reporters at that company?

At the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), after hiring modern female reporters and virgin reporters, the result was that only scandals spread. Therefore, they say they are now greatly relieved after hiring Pak Sŭngho (朴承浩氏), the most senior and virtuous among all past female reporters. However, when someone asks the reporters there about the efficiency (能率) of the newly joined female reporter, they say things like, "Her efficiency is certainly adequate, but because she is too experienced (老宿), the editorial staff (編輯局員) don't feel like they are working alongside a female reporter, but rather like working under the watch of an elderly mother (老母侍下), so every matter (每事 - assumed based on context) feels cautious." That seems plausible too—

Since there isn't much news material, shall we conclude with a report of major events at each company this year?

The Tonga Ilbo, perhaps because its foundation (地盤) is solid and it does everything others do, hasn't had much new business apart from things akin to judging topography (地形), such as the exclusive women's tennis tournament (女子庭球大會), student work exhibition (學生作品展覽會), and embroidery and knitting workshops (刺繡編物講習所). Oh, publishing the 5,000th issue commemorative edition (記念號) and initiating the construction of a bronze statue (銅像建設發起) for Mr. Kim * [Sŏngsu - 金性洙 assumed] are major projects. Besides that, like suffering the double blow of losing a battle in Qi and having the eldest son die (東敗於齊長子死焉格), losing key figures such as Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), Kim Tongjin (金東進), Sŏ Ch'un (徐椿), Ham Sanghun (咸尙勳), and Sin T'aeik (申泰翊) to the Chosŏn Ilbo (朝鮮日報) in the spring is also something notable (特記) this year.

Chosŏn Ilbo = Moved from east to west like a bookstore - Relocated from East to West and constructed a new building (社屋建築) (But still not completed - 但尙未洛成). Thanks to wind and flood disasters (風災水災), flew in an airplane (飛行機) twice, President Pang (方社長) purchased a car (自動車購入), purchased 10,000 rolls of newsprint (新聞卷紙一萬軸購入). (But it's regrettable that photos couldn't be printed larger and editorials weren't written - 但)

Chungang Ilbo = Established a 300,000 won company (三十萬圓大會社成立), published 8 pages (8頁發行), increased newspaper price (新聞價引上), declared martial law on reporters' writing (記者 집필 戒嚴令 發布), President Yŏ (呂社長) and Director Hong (洪局長) constructed company buildings (社屋建築), initiated a public funeral (社會葬發起) for Pak Hŭido (朴熙道), etc. (等等). ＜96＞

**Summary**

This article from the December 1934 issue of *Kaebyŏk Sin'gan* critiques the state of Korean newspapers (Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Chungang Ilbo). The author laments the lack of substantial news, leading to trivial editorials, compares this unfavorably with foreign press practices, and mockingly suggests abolishing editorials. It details specific events: Chungang Ilbo restricting its reporters from writing elsewhere, Tonga Ilbo hiring an older female reporter to avoid scandals but finding her presence somewhat stifling, and summarizes the year's major activities for each paper, including relocations, commemorative issues, staff movements (notably key figures moving from Tonga to Chosŏn Ilbo), infrastructure investments, and responses to natural disasters.

For historians, this piece offers a contemporary, critical perspective on the colonial Korean press landscape. It reveals internal newspaper politics (staff restrictions, hiring strategies), the perceived lack of significant domestic news ('incidents') under colonial rule forcing papers towards 'soft' content or trivial editorials, competition between major newspapers (staff poaching), and their engagement in cultural activities (exhibitions, workshops, commemorations) alongside business developments. The mention of specific events like disaster relief efforts (airplane usage), statue initiatives, and public funerals provides concrete details about newspaper operations and their public roles during this period. The casual, somewhat cynical tone also reflects a particular intellectual sentiment of the time regarding the media's function and limitations under colonialism.

新聞漫談
외국의 신문들은 보도를 주로 하고 사설가튼 것은 중대한 문제가 안이면 별로 쓰지 안치만은 朝鮮의 신문들을 웬셈인지 맛치 보통학교의 어학과정 모양으로 날마다 사설을 쓰지 안으면 안될 일가치 생각하는 모양이다. 그러고 보니 아모 사건도 업는 朝鮮에서 아모리 名論卓論家인들 무슨 재료가 잇스랴. 문제도 소학교의 작문문제가튼 것이 안이면 **閒臥人의 노래가락 가튼 것이 만타. 근일 某報가튼 데는 심지어 카페 여급의 자살미수사건을 보고 사설을 쓴 일까지 잇스니 그럴진대 차라리 카페 여급폐지론가튼 것을 쓰는 것이 더 高論이 안일가. 明年부터는 各社 사설폐지론을 누가 썻스면 통쾌하겟다.

中央日報에서는 그 社 중역의 결의로 일반기자에게 명령曰 中央日報기자로 다른 신문이나 잡지에 집필하는 사람은 상당한 처벌을 달 터이니 금후 절대 집필을 하지 말나고 하엿다 한다. 그 社의 일을 충실하게 하기 위하야는 혹 그럴 수도 잇슬는지 알 수 업스나 소위 언론자유를 주창하는 신문으로서 너머 정도 넘치게 개인 자유를 구속하는 것이 안일지 借間 그 社 기자들의 소감은 如何.

東亞日報에서는 모뎐 여기자와 처녀기자를 채용하야 본 결과 모도가 염문만 퍼지는 까닭에 이번에는 역대 여기자 중 第一年 高德高한 朴承浩氏를 채용하야서 아주 안심이 된다고 한다. 그런데 어떤 사람이 그 社 기자들에게 신입 여기자의 능률이 어떠하냐고 물으면 능률이야 물론 상당하지만은 너머 노숙한 까닭에 편집국원들이 여기자와 가티 일을 하는 감이 업고 老母侍下에서 일하는 것 가터서 每*가 조심스럽다 운운. 그것도 그럴듯한 일-

신문의 문제거리가 별로 업스니 끗흐로 各社의 금년 중 중요사건보고나 할가.

東亞日報는 지반이 튼튼하고 남 할 일을 다 하야 그러한지 지형을 재판하는 격으로 전매특허의 여자정구대회, 학생작품전람회, 자수편물강습소 등 외에 별로 새 사업이 업다. 올치 5,000號 記念號 낸 것과 金*中氏의 동상건설발기도 사업은 큰 사업이요 그 외 東敗於齊에 長子死焉格으로 春間에 李光洙, 金東進, 徐椿, 咸尙勳, 申泰翊 등 중요 인물을 朝鮮日報에 빼앗긴 것도 금년에 特記할 일.
