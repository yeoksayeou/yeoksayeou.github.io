**Brief Comments on the Financial World**
						Wangch'onsaeng

**The Economic War of Armed Peace**

▲ The world's economic war is armed while pretending peace. ▲ America, having failed in its N. R. A. movement, has dealt a fatal blow to its industrial sector, and Roosevelt's test of strength has beautifully met defeat. ▲ Due to this, the American prosperity (緊相) from before 1929, which Roosevelt dreamed of, is gone, and only confusion (混亂) and dark shadows (暗影) drift through the economic society. ▲ It is said that it ended up merely increasing the instability of currency value (貨幣價値). ▲ So, what new dream is Mr. Roosevelt dreaming now to recover from this failure? ▲ Having started the N. R. A. movement staking the President's neck and boasting that there would be a 100% effect, ▲ since it resulted in a 100% failure, Mr. Roosevelt eventually dreamed the dream of Nanke (南柯의 夢 - an illusory dream) of establishing a special agency (特別機關) to harmonize the aims (精神) of the N. R. A. and the Sherman Anti-Trust Law. ▲ However, the overproduction panic (生産過剩恐慌) intensified, only fostering labor disputes (勞働爭議), causing large-scale strikes (大罷業) to erupt everywhere. ▲ And on the other hand, it invited fluctuations in currency value, bringing upon itself financial instability. ▲ Stimulated by this, the Gold Bloc (金 『뿔럭』) of various European countries has fallen into crisis, ▲ and signs of its collapse are also a pressing issue (時■■題). ▲ Looking at it this way, the major powers, having received America's untimely declaration of economic warfare (■■戰) under armed peace (武裝平和), are each pointing their gun barrels at America ▲ and continuing a fierce war without bloodshed, ▲ refusing war debt payments (戰債支拂) and delaying interest payments. ▲ Indeed, when will this state of war with silent cannons (無聲■■砲) be resolved, ▲ and what will be the spoils of war? It is one of those things one wishes to hear about and see.

**Finance Minister's Caprice Costs Two Billion**

▲ They say people get capricious when they age, but it seems there's youthful caprice too. ▲ However, since the cost of this caprice amounts to a whopping two billion yen, ▲ such caprice is quite something (?) ▲ Mr. Fujii succeeded Old Man Takahashi and received the chair of the Minister of Finance (大藏大臣), like a young court noble (少年 堂上 格). ▲ With favorable winds blowing, he declared that the tax increase (增稅), which burdened the people frighteningly, would absolutely not be implemented (斷不實行). ▲ But lo and behold, when he started compiling the budget (豫算編成), the revenue (歲入) and expenditure (歲出) were enormously different. ▲ Demands from various ministries were like children in Nolbu's house asking for food, ▲ but his pockets only contained red tags (붉은 닦지) (deficit bonds - 赤字公債), so after sweating profusely, ▲ he finally showed reckless courage (蠻勇), reversing his words (食言飜覆), saying a tax increase was unavoidable (無可奈何). ▲ He declared that businesses making large special profits (特別利得) would be taxed more. ▲ Because of that, businessmen who had been making unearned income (不勞所得) were thrown into great turmoil. ▲ And the business companies subject to that tax increase fell into utter disappointment and despondency. ▲ Consequently, their stock certificates (株券) plummeted and collapsed (瓦落), falling rapidly by five to over ten yen, and dangerous signals of a major abnormality in the stock market emerged. ▲ Thus, it's said the total decline of all stocks within Japan amounted to two billion yen. ▲ That a sacrifice of two billion yen was made to implement a tax increase of thirty to forty million yen is undeniably large. Anyway, the price of Mr. Fujii's single caprice—don't be surprised—two billion yen...

**◇High Rice Prices and the Lower Classes**

▲ Last year's rice harvest is said to be unprecedentedly large (無前大量). ▲ The actual yield (實收) of Chosŏn rice was over 18 million sŏk (섬, unit of grain volume), and the actual yield within the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry (農林省) was over 67 million sŏk. ▲ So, from last November to December, the rice price dropped as if they were giving it away. ▲ Farmers sold the grain they cultivated by sweating for a whole year for a pittance (우거지갑). ▲ Then, policies called the Rice Price Policy (米價政策) or the Rice Control Law (米穀統制法) came out, ▲ and after buying up 15-16 million <90> sŏk when it was cheap, ▲ they finally set an official price (公定價格) ▲ and insisted that the price must not go above it, holding firm. ▲ As a result, rice prices soared and soared due to a famine amidst plenty (豊作饑饉), ▲ reaching a high price close to 30 yen per sŏk during the ■■ period. ▲ Farmers sold cheap and bought dear, only accumulating debt (負債). ▲ Is this policy truly intended for the lower classes (細民層)? Or does it target landlords and big merchants (地主大商)? ▲ However, they bravely claim it is for the farmers and the lower classes. ▲ While the words sound grateful, isn't there a need to be more careful with such policies? ▲ This year's rice crop, due to natural disasters (天災地變), the second forecast for Chosŏn rice harvest is estimated at 16.55 million sŏk. ▲ That is, compared to the first forecast, it is a decrease of over 150,000 sŏk. ▲ The harvest forecast within the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry's jurisdiction is said to be below 49 million sŏk, the worst harvest in 30 years. ▲ Since they bought unlimitedly during a bountiful year, ▲ do they lack the courage to sell unlimitedly during a lean year? ▲ Due to this, the position of Chosŏn rice is said to have become advantageous, ▲ but while it may be advantageous for the import merchants (移入商) handling Chosŏn rice, ▲ one cannot fathom that there is no profit for the Chosŏn ■people who actually produced the rice.

**Yellow Goods and the Common Front**

▲ Currently, in the world capitalist society, a monster (怪物) is徘徊ing, and that is the monster called Yellow Goods (黃色商品). Therefore, all the powers of the world's capitalist countries ▲ are waging a common front (共同戰線) and frantically rushing to exterminate this monster. ▲ Africa, England, British India, British Australia, the Netherlands Colonies, the Dutch East Indies (蘭印), etc., have participated. ▲ Thus, where their crossfire (十字砲火) intersects, it explodes in the form of tariff increases (關稅引上), import quotas (輸入配定), exchange tariffs (爲替關稅), exchange compensation surtaxes (爲替補償加稅), unfair dumping laws (不當廉賣法), and exchange dumping taxes (爲替 「핑」稅). ▲ However, even amidst such crossfire, Yellow Goods are acting invincibly (不死身) ▲ and, though in honorable isolation (名譽의 孤立), are riding the tide of victory (乘勝長驅) to threaten the world market. ▲ Japanese goods are showing leap after leap of progress in the world market, no matter how much rejection they receive. ▲ Can the world powers indeed effectively check (防遏) this? ▲ Monster! The true identity of Yellow Goods has thus drawn the world's attention (視聽), ▲ and through this, it has faced honorable isolation.

**◇Land Consolidation and Rural Impoverishment**

▲ Observing the recent trend (推移) of land consolidation (土地兼倂), over twenty years, landlords (地主) have increased by 17 per thousand, reaching approximately double the number. ▲ Owner-farmers (自作農) decreased from 220 to 159 per thousand, a reduction of 61 per thousand. ▲ And owner-tenant farmers (自小作農) are 162 per thousand; this signifies a rapid downfall (沒落). ▲ The principle of the rich getting richer and the poor getting poorer (富益富 貧益貧) is a characteristic feature of capitalist economic society, so it is not surprising. ▲ However, considering the rural villages declining and becoming impoverished (疲廢) at such a rapid speed, who could suppress a tear? Therefore, the situation of Chosŏn's rural villages is in a truly dire (逼迫) state. ▲ The population is excessive (過剩), the number of unemployed farmers (失業農民) is enormous, ▲ and the area of farmland is very small (狹小) compared to the population. ▲ Following this, land is incessantly consolidated (兼倂), which signifies the downfall of small and medium landlords. ▲ When that land is released, large landlords and capitalists take possession of it, and thus class differentiation (階級의 分化) occurs. ▲ That the number of large landlords increases and the number of tenant farmers grows symbolizes the intensification (激化) of rural villagers' poverty (窮迫), signifying the downfall of this middle stratum. ▲ The more this happens, the more the social situation (社會情勢) of the rural villages enters an unstable state. ▲ Although this is not a phenomenon unique to Chosŏn but common (共通) to the capitalist agricultural world, ▲ that of Chosŏn's rural villages, where poverty is more severe and economic power (經濟力) is weaker (脆弱) than in any other country, progresses even more rapidly. ▲ This will intensify further in the future, so what indeed does the future hold? ▲ Truly, with what strength can the impoverishment and downfall of Chosŏn's rural villages be stopped? ▲ The rural problem can rather be seen as belonging to the future.
(November 6, 1934 Record) <91>

**Summary**

This 1934 article by Wangch'onsaeng provides sharp commentary on several pressing economic issues. It critiques the failure of Roosevelt's NRA in the US, highlighting the resulting global economic instability and tensions, including debt disputes. It lambasts the Japanese Finance Minister Fujii's policy reversal on taxes, noting its devastating multi-billion yen impact on the stock market. The author decries the paradoxical situation in Korea where government rice control policies, despite a good harvest, led to soaring prices that benefited merchants and landlords while plunging farmers further into debt. The piece also discusses the global backlash against surging Japanese exports ("Yellow Goods") through protectionist measures, yet notes Japan's continued market penetration. Finally, it presents stark statistics on land consolidation in Korea, showing a dramatic increase in landlordism and decrease in owner-farmers, leading to rapid rural impoverishment and social instability, a situation depicted as particularly severe in colonial Korea. For historians, this article offers a valuable contemporary Korean perspective on the global depression's impact, criticism of Japanese economic policies (both domestic and colonial), the rise of global trade protectionism targeting Japan, and a bleak assessment of the accelerating socio-economic decline in rural colonial Korea under Japanese rule, highlighting class differentiation and farmer dispossession.

黃色商品과 共同戰線
▲現下 世界資本主義社會에는, 一個의 怪物이 徘徊하고 잇스니 그것은 黃色商品이란 怪物이다 그래서 世界資本主義國家의 온갓 權力은 ▲이 怪物을 退治하기 爲하야 共同戰을 펴고 極度로 狂奔하고 잇다 ▲亞不利可, 英吉利, 英領 印度, 英領 濠洲, 和蘭 植民地, 蘭印 等々이 參加하엿다 ▲그래서 그의 十字砲火가 相交하는 곳에는 關稅引上 輸入配定 爲替關稅, 爲替補償加稅, 不當廉賣法, 爲替 「핑」稅로 化하야 爆發하고 잇다 ▲그러나 이러한 十字砲 가운데서도 黃色商品은 不死身의 活躍을 하고 잇스며 ▲名譽의 孤立일망정 乘勝長驅하야 世界市場을 威脅하고 잇나니 ▲日本商品은 世界市場에서 아모리 排斥을 바들지라도 躍進의 躍進을 示하고 잇다 ▲果然 世界列强은 이것을 能히 防遏할 수 잇다 하느뇨 ▲妖怪! 黃色商品의 正體는 이러케 하야써 世界의 視聽을 끄럿스며 ▲또 이로써 名譽의 孤立을 當한 것이다
◇土地兼倂과 農村疲廢
▲最近 土地兼倂의 推■ 考察하야보건대 二十年間에 地主는 千分之十七이 增加되여 約 倍數에 達하고 ▲自作農은 二二○에서 一五九로 卽 千分之六一이 減少하엿다 ▲그리고 自小作農은 千分之一六二이니 이것은 急速度의 沒落이다 ▲富益富 貧益貧의 原理는 資本主義 經濟社會에 잇서서 한 特徵이라 그리 놀날것은 업스나 ▲이가티 急速度로 沒落疲廢하는 農村을 생각하면 누구나 한줄기 눈물을 禁하랴? 그럼으로 朝鮮農村의 事情은 ■히 逼迫 處地에 잇다 ▲人口는 過剩하야 失業農民의 數가 膨大하고 ▲農■地 面積은 人口 比較하면 매우 狹小하다 ▲이에 따러 土地는 임이 업시 兼倂되고 마니 이는 中小地主의 沒落을 意味하는 것이다 ▲그 土地를 내어노흐면, 그것을 大地主 資本家가 차지하게 되는 것이니 이리하야 階級의 分化가 되는 것이다 ▲이가티 大地主가 늘고, 小作農 數爻가 는다는 것은 農村民의 窮迫이 激化된다는 것을 象徵하는 것이니, 이 中間層의 沒落을 意味하는 것이 된다 ▲그럴수록 農村의 社會情勢는 더욱 不安한 狀態로 들어가게 된다 ▲이것이 비록 朝鮮만의 現象이 아니오 資本主義 農業界에 共通한 것이라고 하지만 ▲어느 나라보다 窮迫이 甚하고 經濟力이 ■弱한 朝鮮農村의 그것은 더욱 急速度로 進行하는 것이다 ▲이것이 압프로 더욱 激化할 것이니 그 將來는 果然 어떠한 것인가? ▲實로 朝鮮農村의 疲廢와 沒落을 무슨 힘으로 막어내랴는지 ▲農村問題는 차라리 今後에 잇다고 볼 수 잇다.
