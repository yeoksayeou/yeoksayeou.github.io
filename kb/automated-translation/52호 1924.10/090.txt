A Thousand Trends and Ten Thousand Aspects, From August 11th to September 12th

August 11th: In Sinhŭng-dong within Hwach'ang-myŏn, Wiwŏn-gun, North P'yŏngan Province, an incident of suspicious origin, whose perpetrators are unknown, suddenly occurred at the village station (洞站). Seven households in the village were completely burned down, and over 30 family members of Ch'oe Ŭng-gyu, Yi Ch'ang-sŏp, Kim Ch'ang-sŏng, Kim Myŏng-gil, Kim Ŭng-ch'ae, Song Chi-hang, and Ch'oe Hŭng-ju were massacred.
A conference of consuls from nations related to the Pacific Rim opened in San Francisco (桑港).

August 12th: The Labor-Farmer Association of Soan-myŏn, Wando-gun, South Chŏlla Province, burned the ancestral tablets and genealogies of member households and destroyed the ritual hall (祭廳) to break down traditional superstitions and customs and eliminate concepts of clan and lineage.
The Beijing Diplomatic Corps, reluctant to have Mr. Karakhan (카과한氏) as its doyen, resolved that "henceforth, the doyen of the diplomatic corps shall be the most senior member by age, regardless of whether they are a minister or ambassador." This is the first such resolution in diplomatic history.
Soviet envoy Mr. Karakhan (카라한氏) visited China's Gu Weijun (顧維釣氏 - V.K. Wellington Koo) and discussed the details regarding the Sino-Soviet conference to open on the 21st.

August 13th: A preliminary meeting for the restoration of diplomatic relations was held between the Labor-Farmer representative stationed in Berlin (伯林) and the representative of Mexico (墨西哥).
The Guangdong (廣東) government confiscated German-made weapons purchased by the Merchant Corps Army.

August 14th: Riots broke out in the Sudan region of Egypt (埃及), and Britain dispatched three warships. Bandits (土匪) in China's Anhui Province (安徽省) occupied Yingshan (英山).
A large fire broke out in Taehwa-chō, Kaesŏng, destroying 7 Korean houses and half of a Japanese house.
In Tamarai-machi, Naoiri-gun, Ōita Prefecture (大分縣 直入郡 玉來町), Japan, a conflict of opinions arose among laborers (centered around Koreans) regarding a wage increase, leading to mutual fighting, resulting in 50-60 casualties.

August 15th: The Hyŏngp'yŏngsa Unification Meeting opened in Taejŏn (太田).
Near Yongsang-dong, Ch'osan-gun, North P'yŏngan Province, four independence army soldiers exchanged fire with Japanese policemen and then retreated to the opposite bank, but their boat capsized, and one person drowned.

August 16th: The Allied Conference concluded, and the signing of its protocol was completed.

August 17th: A major flood occurred in Tianjin (天津), China, resulting in a tragedy with 15,000 drowning victims.
The Allied Conference, which had opened in London, concluded today.
About 10 members of an armed group set fire to the Nam Sa-yŏng Forestry (*) Worksite in Huch'ang-gun, North P'yŏngan Province, causing damages exceeding ten thousand won. Also, at Anch'an Pass (安贊嶺) in Anch'an-dong, Sŏng-myŏn, Ch'osan-gun, North P'yŏngan Province, an independence army led by Kim Tŏk-sam fought with a police unit, killing one Japanese policeman.
With Chŏn Il, Kim Yŏn-ŭi, and seven others as promoters, the Chosŏn Labor Party (조선노동당) was organized.

August 18th: An early frost descended upon North Ch'ŏng and P'ungsan counties in South Hamgyŏng Province; this is said to be the first such natural disaster in 60 years. British Prime Minister MacDonald (맥도날드氏) sent a letter to French Prime Minister Herriot (에리오氏), threatening that the occupation of the Ruhr (루얼) was illegal and demanding a swift withdrawal.
France withdrew its occupation forces from Offenburg (오펜뿌르크) and Appenweier (압펜와이엘) on the right bank of the Rhine (라인). The Beijing Diplomatic Corps decided to hand over the Russian Legation building and delivered two documents to Mr. Karakhan (카라한氏) signed by Acting Doyen of the Diplomatic Corps, Minister Yoshizawa (芳澤公使).
The case regarding the violation of the Peace Preservation Police Law related to the Movement for the Establishment of a Taiwan Parliament, involving Jiang Weishui (蔣渭水) and 17 other Taiwanese, resulted in acquittal for all at the Taipei (臺北) District Court.
A mass meeting of Chinju citizens was held to oppose the relocation of the South Kyŏngsang Provincial Office.

August 19th: Russian Minister Karakhan (카라한氏) appointed Mr. Zvol'skiy(?) (수왈사론氏) as the committee member for receiving the old legation building, completing the handover procedures with the Dutch Minister, the handover committee member from the diplomatic corps.
A large fire broke out in Honmachi (本町), Kyŏngsŏng, completely burning 7 houses and partially burning 8 houses.

August 20th: The Labor-Farmer government ratified a 36-year lease right for various mining areas to the German-Russian joint venture "Prayna"(?) (뿌라이나) company and also ratified the granting of gold mining rights in the Haruga(?) (하루가) River to the American Far East Development Company (米國 極東開拓會社).

August 21st: The German Federal Council passed the entire implementation bill for the Dawes (<78> 또스) Plan Reparations Expert Committee report.
The London Chamber of Commerce stated its disapproval of the Anglo-Soviet Treaty, opposing the British government's guarantee of Russian public bonds.

August 22nd: Ŭiyŏldan leader P'yŏn Kang-nyŏl (片康烈氏) was arrested by a policeman from the Andong Prefecture (安東縣) Police Affairs Office and transferred to the Sinŭiju Police Station. The British Prince of Wales departed for America.

August 23rd: The trial of Messrs. Sŏ Kwang-ho and Yun Tu-sŏk, involved in the Amt'ae Tenant Farming Dispute in South Chŏlla Province, opened at the Mokp'o Branch of the Kwangju District Court, where the prosecutor demanded a sentence of 6 months imprisonment for each.
The Danish (丁抹國) cabinet decided to abolish the Army and Navy, preserving and expanding only the navigation corps (항행대), and to submit the bill to the Parliament this autumn. The Japanese-Soviet conference, which had been suspended for 4-5 days, resumed at the Russian Embassy at Japan's request.
The Labor-Farmer Russia examined the academic results of university students nationwide and took expulsion measures against approximately 20,000 students with poor records.
The P'yŏngyang Police Station conducted a large-scale roundup, arresting about 40 people, including Han Hae, An Yŏng-gi, and Yi Ik-chu, on suspicion of being socialists.

August 24th: A large-scale White Army restoration conspiracy was uncovered in Russia, leading to the arrest of 39 ringleaders. Also, Duke Cyril (커틸太公) of that country submitted a protest against the recently concluded Anglo-Soviet treaty.
In Kanggyŏng City (江景市), South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, after a Japanese policeman named Ōi (大井) stabbed stonefish merchant (石魚商) Cho Hyŏn-sik with a military sword, 400-500 local citizens became enraged and were about to assault the policeman but were subdued by other police officers.
Mars (火星) appeared at 2 a.m.

August 25th: Citizens of Guangdong (廣東), China, staged a city-wide general strike to protest the forced circulation of Central Bank banknotes by the same government.

August 26th: The foreign diplomatic corps in China, based on a proposal from the Shanghai consular body, inquired with the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs, stating that starting hostilities within a 30-li radius around Shanghai would not be permitted.

August 27th: A new trade treaty was concluded between the Netherlands (和蘭) and Canada (加奈陀).

August 28th: Lu Yongxiang (盧永祥), Military Governor (督軍) of Zhejiang (浙江), China, issued a declaration to suppress President Cao Kun (曺錕氏). Hwimun High Common School (徽文高普) took disciplinary action, expelling over 600 students who had participated in a collective strike (盟休).

August 29th: As today marks the 15th anniversary of the Japan-Korea annexation (日韓合倂), police stations throughout the capital city (府內) were on high alert.
The 5th Council meeting of the League of Nations opened in Geneva (제네바) under the chairmanship of Belgian Foreign Minister Hymans (이만氏). The German Parliament passed various laws related to the implementation of the Dawes Plan.
The Zhejiang-Jiangsu armies in China began fighting; martial law was declared in Shanghai outside the concessions, and the Guangdong Army began arson and burning in the city streets.

August 30th: Italian Prime Minister Mussolini (모소리니氏) was shot by an opposition party member while en route to Rome and is in critical condition.
Japan's new luxury goods tax law was promulgated and is set to be enforced in Chosŏn and Taiwan as well.

September 1st: Wada Kyūtarō (和田久太郞), a disciple of Ōsugi Sakae (大杉榮) who was murdered during last year's Tokyo disaster (東京罹災), shot Army General Fukuda Masatarō (福田雅太郞), the martial law commander at the time, with a pistol on the street and was arrested at the scene.
The League of Nations opened in Geneva (제네바) with 47 participating countries; the chairman is Swiss representative Motta(?) (모라氏).

September 2nd: Encephalitis lethargica (啫眠病 - sleeping sickness) occurred in Japan, reportedly resulting in over 1,600 deaths and 3,000 patients.
As today is the 5th anniversary of Kang U-gyu's (姜宇奎) bombing attack on Governor-General Saitō (齋藤) at Nammun Station (南門驛), police stations throughout the city were on special alert.

September 3rd: Sun Wen (孫文氏 - Sun Yat-sen) decided at a military council meeting tonight to launch a Northern Expedition and telegraphed an order to *Kongch'ok(?) (*恭綽) in Shanghai to procure one million yuan for military funds.
Another bombing incident occurred at the Motofuji(?) (元富土) Police Station in Hongō, Tokyo, Japan.
The Consuls General of Britain, America, Italy, and France (英米伊佛) in Shanghai, China, issued a joint note to Qi Xieyuan (齊燮元), Lu Yongxiang (盧永祥), and He Fenglin (何豊林氏), the central figures in the Chinese turmoil, regarding the guarantee of the concessions and foreign nationals.

September 4th: The Labor government sentenced 24 leaders of an anti-Labor government rebel group that arose in the Cossack region to death. Fighting between the Jiangsu-Zhejiang armies began in the Huangdu (黃渡), Jiading (嘉定), and Longhua (龍華) areas of China.
In Oe Chung-ni, Sŏnggan-myŏn, Kanggye-gun, North P'yŏngan Province, dozens of independence army soldiers and a police unit engaged in battle for <79> about 20 minutes, with no losses on either side.

September 5th: Principal Im Kyŏng-jae and Dean Chang Chae-jin of Hwimun High School (徽高), who had maintained their positions after expelling over 600 students, resigned.
He Fenglin (何豊林氏), commander of the 1st Army of the Zhejiang Army, ordered the commencement of a general offensive on the front lines. The first fierce battle took place in the Liuhe (유하) area, resulting in over 1,000 deaths for the Zhejiang Army. The Trades Union Congress held in Hull (할), Britain, passed a resolution to exclude East Asian laborers (東洋勞働者排斥案).
The Anti-Imperialism Group (反帝國主義團), headquartered in Shanghai, decided to conduct a large-scale propaganda campaign regarding its principles on September 7th. A bomb exploded in Yotsuyasugachō(?) (四谷監町), Tokyo, Japan, causing some injuries; the Metropolitan Police Department launched a major investigation.

September 6th: A bomb parcel incident occurred at the residence of General Fukuda (福田大將邸) in Japan, causing a brief major commotion.
Hong Chin-ŭi, Pak Yong-ha, and Yi Chŏng-ho, arrested in connection with the Red Flag Group (赤旗團) incident, were indicted today; Yi Kyu-hwan was released without indictment.
The Fengtian Army (奉天軍) began to mobilize, and rumors circulated that President Cao Kun (曹錕氏) was preparing to flee.

September 7th: As today is International Youth Day (국제청년데), police stations throughout the capital city conducted special surveillance of various ideological groups.
Chinese President Cao Kun (曹錕氏) issued an order to suppress Zhejiang Military Governor Lu Yongxiang (盧永祥). Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) of Fengtian (奉天) also telegraphed orders to the deputy commanders-in-chief of Heilongjiang (黑龍) and Jilin (吉林) to dispatch troops immediately.
A typhoon struck Taipei (臺北), completely destroying over 340 houses and causing much other damage.

September 8th: The trial related to the Amt'ae incident in South Chŏlla Province opened at the Kwangju District Court. The prosecutor requested sentences of 3 years for Sŏ Uk-chol (徐郁晢); 1 year each for Sŏ Ch'ang-sŏk and 3 others; 8 months each for Pak Yong-san and 7 others; for the landowner side, 11 months for Mun Myŏng-ho, 10 months for Min Sun (珉順), and a fine of 50 won for Mun Chae-bong.
The Jiangsu Army fought fiercely at Liuhe (劉河), China, suffering over 500 deaths before retreating.

September 9th: The trial of Kim Chi-sŏp, Yun Cha-yŏng, Hidetō Kōichi(?) (秀島廣一), Kobayashi Kan(?) (小林關), and Kuroda Rikyō(?) (黑島利鄕), who caused a public sensation by throwing a bomb at the Nijūbashi Bridge (2重橋) in Tokyo on January 5th of this year, opened at the Tokyo District Court. The Japan-Russia negotiations, which were scheduled to resume on the 10th, suddenly commenced today.
Regarding the turmoil in China, landing parties from Japan, Britain, America, and Italy (日英米伊) landed to guard the concessions. Japan decided in a cabinet meeting to maintain strict neutrality in the Chinese turmoil. The volunteer corps of various countries decided to mobilize and deploy. The Zhejiang Army occupied Yixing (宜興).

September 10th: The trial of Yang Sŭng-u and Han Tong-jin, commanders of the 6th Brigade of the Korean Liberation Army (大韓光復軍), opened at the P'yŏngyang Court of Review (覆審法院).
The Shanghai Municipal Council (上海租界 政廳) declared that Shanghai had fallen into a state of emergency. Internal conflict arose among the leadership of the Poch'ŏn'gyo (普天敎) regarding the Sidae Ilbo newspaper issue, resulting in the dismissal of the church's T'ongnyŏng Wŏnjang (統領院長 - Head of the Governing Council?) Yi Sang-ho and Yi Sŏng-yŏng.
The Metropolitan Police Department in Tokyo, Japan, arrested all the true culprits of the frequent bombing incidents that had occurred since July early this morning. (Names not yet released). The Russian government declared non-intervention in the Chinese civil war and initiated efforts to oppose intervention by other countries.

September 11th: The inaugural meeting of the Chosŏn Student General Federation (朝鮮學生總聯合會) was held at the YMCA building in Chongno.
Due to poor harvests in Kŭmsan, Chinan, Iksan, and 5 other counties in North Chŏlla Province, it is reported that the number of people eating only one meal a day reached 450,000, with 15,000 starving.
The German government paid the second installment of reparations, 20 million gold Marks (馬克), according to the Dawes (또스) Plan schedule. A French committee officially announced it was drafting a proposal regarding the recognition of Russia.
Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) established the Zhenxian(?) General Headquarters (鎭憲總可令部) as the main camp for the anti-Zhili army (討直軍), divided the mobilization into 4 [phases/years?], and announced a declaration against the Zhili clique.
The lower house of the Philippines (比島下院) submitted a bill to prohibit foreign fishing, reportedly aimed at excluding the monopolistic fishing practices of the Japanese.

September 12th: The Chinese gentry and merchants residing in Shanghai advised President Cao Kun (曹錕氏) to step down. The Beijing-Fengtian (京奉線) railway line was attacked by Zhili forces near Shanhaiguan (山海關), and service was frequently interrupted.
The Japanese cabinet decided on the organizational structure for the special police related to thought control (思想取締), which had passed the 49th Diet session. The United States announced that the number of registered volunteers for national defense had reached over 1.4 million as of today. <80>

**Summary**

This article chronicles various events from August 11 to September 12, 1924, covering Korea, Japan, China, and international affairs. Key Korean events include a massacre in North P'yŏngan Province, the destruction of traditional symbols by a labor-farmer association in South Chŏlla, skirmishes involving independence fighters, arrests of socialists in P'yŏngyang, the formation of the Chosŏn Labor Party, and trials related to tenant disputes and independence activities. It also reports on the burgeoning civil war in China (Jiangsu-Zhejiang conflict, Fengtian clique mobilization), international diplomacy (Sino-Soviet talks, Allied conferences, Dawes Plan implementation, Anglo-Soviet treaty issues), events in Japan (aftermath of Ōsugi Sakae's murder, bombing incidents, political decisions like the luxury tax and thought police), and other global news like riots in Sudan and political changes in Denmark. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is significant for its documentation of both violent oppression (the Wiwŏn massacre) and continued resistance (independence fighter actions, socialist organizing, student federation). It also highlights social changes (Hyŏngp'yŏngsa, the Soan-myŏn incident) and the extension of Japanese control mechanisms (luxury tax, planned thought police) into Korea, alongside the difficulties faced by Korean laborers in Japan.

千態萬相, 自 8月 11日 至 9月 12日
8月11日 平北 渭原郡 和昌面 新興洞內 洞站에서 何者의 소위인지 不知하는 疑向의 사건이 돌발하야 一洞 七戶가 전소되고 崔應奎
李昌涉, 金昌盛, 金明吉, 金應採, 宋芝恒, 崔興周 등의 가족 30여 명이 학살되다.
太平洋 沿岸 關係國 領事會議가 桑港에 開하다.

同 12日 全南 莞島郡 所安面 勞農會에서는 종래 미신, 인습을 타파하고 종족과 문벌 관념을 소멸게 하기 위하야 회원 가정의 신주 족보을 소각하고 祭廳을 破壞하다.
北京外交團에서는 「카과한」氏로 수석 삼기를  嫌忌하야 「장래 외교단 수석은 其公使 혹 大使를 불문하고 최연장자를 수석으로 한다」결의하얏는데 此는 외교사상 初有의 결의.
露使「카라한」씨가 中國 顧維釣씨를 방문하고 21일에 開할 中露會議에 관한 細目을 협의하다.

同 13日 伯林駐在 勞農代表와 墨西哥대표 간에 국교 회복의 預備會議가 有하다.
廣東정부에서 商團軍의 구입한 獨製무기를 몰수하다.

同 14日 埃及 스탄 지방에 폭동이 起하야 英國에서 軍艦 3隻을 파견하다. 中國 安徽省 土匪가 英山을 점령하다.
開城 大和町에 大火가 起하야 朝鮮人 7戶와 日本人家 半戶가 소실되다.
日本 大分縣 直八郡 玉來町에서 노동 임금 인상 문제로 노동자 간에(朝鮮人 중심) 意思가 충돌되야 互相 格鬪한 바 사상자 5, 60명에 달하다.

同 15日
衡平社 統一會가 太田에 開하다.
平北 楚山郡 龍上洞 부근에서 독립군 4명이 日순사와 교전 후 對岸으로 退歸하다가 覆船되야 1명이 익사하다.

同 16日 聯合國會議가 종료되고 그 議定書에 조인까지 필하다.

同 17日 中國 天津에 대홍수가 出하야 15,000인의 익사자가 生한 참변이 有하다.
倫敦에 開하엿던 연합국회의가 本日 종료.
무장단 10여 명이 平北 厚昌郡 南社營 林*作業所에 방화하야 손해가 만원 이상에 달하고 又 平北 楚山郡 城面 安贊洞 安贊嶺에서는 金德三을 수령으로 한 독립군이 경찰대와 교전하야 日순사 1명을 사살하다.
全一
金演義 兩氏외 7인의 발기로 조선노동당이 조직되다.

同 18日 咸南 北靑 及 豊山 兩郡에 早霜이 降하얏는데 此는 60년來 初有의 天災라 하고 英國 수상「맥도날드」씨는 서면으로 佛수상「에리오」씨에게 「루얼」점령은 위법이니 속히 철퇴하라고 위협하다.
佛國은 「라인」右岸의 「오펜뿌르크」 及 「압펜와이엘」에서 점령군을 철퇴하다. 北京外交團에서는 露國公使舘을 引渡하기로 결의하고 공사단 수석대리 芳澤공사의 서명으로 「카라한」씨에게 2통의 문서를 교부하다.
臺灣人 蔣渭水 이하 18명의 臺灣議會設治運動에 관한 治安警察法違反事件은 臺北 地方法院에서 전부 무죄되다.
慶南 道廳 이전을 반대하기 위하야 晉州 시민대회가 開하다.

同 19日 露公使 카라한씨는 「수왈사론」씨를 舊公使舘 引受委員으로 임명하야 외교단의 引渡委員 和蘭公使와 인수사무를 완료하다.
京城 本町에 大火가 起하야 全燒 7戶 半燒 8戶에 달하다.

同 20日 勞農政府는 獨露合辦의 「뿌라이나」회사에 대한 각 광산지의 36개년의 租借權을 批準하고, 且 「하루가」河의 금광 채굴권을 米國 極東開拓會¤¾에 讓與하기로 批準하다.

同 21日 獨逸聯邦會議는 「또스」＜78＞ 賠償專門委員會 報告實行案 전부를 가결하고
倫敦商業會議所는 英國政府의 露國 公債保證을 반대하야 英露條約을 불찬성한다 言明하다.

同 22日 의열단장 片康烈씨가 安東縣 警務署 순사에게 체포되야 新義州 경찰서로 압송되다. 英國 황태자가 渡米하다.

同 23日 全南 岩泰 小作爭議 관계자인 徐光浩, 尹斗石 등 양씨의 공판이 光州 지방법원 木浦지청에 開하야 검사로부터 각 6개월 징역의 논고가 有하다.
丁抹國 내각회의에서는 陸海軍을 폐지하고 항행대만 보존 확장하기로 결의하고 仝法案을 今秋議會에 제출하기로 하다. 4, 5일 중지되얏던 日露會議는 日本의 요구로 露國大使舘에 재개되다.
勞農 露國은 전국대학생성적을 심사하야 劣績生 약 2만명에 追放 처분을 行하다.
平壤 경찰서에서 사회주의자 혐의로 韓海, 安永基, 李益柱 등 40여 인을 대검거하다.

同 24日 露國에서 대규모의 白軍復辟음모가 발각되야 수모자 39명이 체포되고 且 仝國의  커틸太公은 今般 성립된 英露 조약에 반대항의를 제출하다.
忠南 江景市에서 일순사 大井이가 石魚商 趙鉉植을 軍刀로 刺傷함으로 당지 시민 4, 5백명이 분기하야 순사를 肉迫하랴다가 警官에게 鎭撫되다.
오전 2시에 火星이 출현하다.

同 25日 中國 廣東市民은 同정부에서 中央銀行지폐의 강제 통용을 반항하기 위하야 全市 대파업을 행하다.

同 26日 中國에 在한 외국 公使團은 上海 영사단 제의에 의하야 上海의 주위 30리 지점에서 開戰을 불허한다고 中國 외교부에 조회하다.

同 27日 和蘭, 加奈陀 간의 新통상조약이 체결되다.

同 28日 中國 浙江 督軍盧永祥은 대총통 曺錕氏 토벌선언을 발하고 徽文高普에서는 盟休학생 600여 명에게 제명처분을 하다.

同 29日 本日은 日韓合倂 제15회 期日인 고로 府內 각 경찰서에서 엄중경계하다.
제5回국제연맹이사회는 白國外相 이만氏 司會 하에 제네바에 開하고 獨逸議會는 또스안시행에 관한 각종 법률을 통과하다.
中國의 浙江 양군이 전투를 개시한 바 上海는 租界 이외에 계엄령이 발포되고 廣東軍은 시가에 放火 燒却을 개시하다.

同 30日 伊太利 수상 모소리니氏는 羅馬 途中에서 반대당의 狙擊을 당하야 생명이 危篤할 境에 至하다.
日本의 사치품 稅法이 新히 공포된 바 朝鮮 及 대만에도 시행하게 되다.

9月 1日 작년 東京罹災 시에 학살된 大杉榮의 제자 和田久太郞이 당시 계엄사령관이던 육군대장 福田雅太郞을 도중에서 拳銃으로 狙擊하다가 현장에서 체포되다.

同 4日 勞働政府는 코사크 지방에서 발생된 反勞農政府 폭동단령 袖 24인에게 사형선고를 하다. 中國의 黃渡, 嘉定, 龍華方面에서 江浙軍의 전투가 개시되다.
