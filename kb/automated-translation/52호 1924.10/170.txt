Inside the Shaking Government-General: The Decline of the Newcomers and the Rise of the Old-timers

Since Governor-General Saitō's arrival, for the past five years, Waesŏngdae [the Government-General building] has been the exclusive stage for the so-called newcomer Japanese (新來種 日本人). Starting with Political Affairs Superintendent Mizuno, most important seats—from department chiefs, bureau chiefs, section chiefs down to local officials—were occupied by newcomers. The so-called old-timer Japanese (在來種 日本人), who had much merit and experience since the Itō and Terauchi eras, were like children who had lost their mothers, crouching in a corner, merely tasting leftover scraps and weeping secretly in the dark. (It goes without saying that there was jealousy, antagonism, and cursing between the two groups. Especially Mizuno's two proud protégés, Police Affairs Bureau Chief Maruyama and General Affairs Department Chief Moriya, were representative figures of the newcomers. Wielding the full power of the Government-General, they monopolized all authority. In their eyes, the populace (especially Koreans) did not exist, the Governor-General did not exist, and at extreme times, even their own country did not exist. In other words, the politics of Korea for the past five years, under the banner of so-called culture, was not the politics of Baron Saitō alone; it was a duumvirate politics of Maruyama, who wielded tyrannical and unrestrained police power, and Moriya, who manipulated with a cunning tongue and pen. Maruyama focused on suppressing Koreans through the police, while Moriya advocated for Japan-Korea harmony through lectures, speeches, and articles, and also organized the so-called Self-Cultivation Group (修養團) to promote the co-existence and co-prosperity of Japanese and Koreans.) Their solid and unshakeable power was maintained even during the Ariyoshi era after Mizuno's departure, becoming Governor-General Saitō's wings and the bulwark of the newcomers. However, the formation of the Katō cabinet by the coalition of the three constitutionalist factions (護憲三派) and the placement of Mr. Shimooka, a relic of the Kenseikai party, in the Political Affairs Superintendent's chair was a major whirlwind for the newcomers. That is, shortly after Mr. Shimooka's arrival, the simultaneous departure of the two giants, Maruyama and Moriya, along with the autumn wind, speaks of the signs that the newcomers are declining (凋落) and the old-timers are regaining strength and putting forth new shoots (舊芽). In other words, from now on within the Government-General, the newcomers will vanish, and the old-timers will raise their heads again (再擡). Among them, the influence of Ōtsuka of Internal Affairs, publicly recognized as a representative of the old-timers, will become central. Although this Mr. Ōtsuka is merely an office worker without much skill or political vision, he has been in Korea for 17 years, has much experience, and is also a direct subordinate of Mr. Kiuchi Jūshirō, a heavyweight behind the scenes in the current cabinet. Since Kiuchi is said to have already made arrangements [for Ōtsuka] when Political Affairs Superintendent Shimooka came to Korea, his influence will naturally be particularly great. (He has marital ties with Iwasaki, and Kiuchi is also Iwasaki's son-in-law.) When he spoke to a certain visiting reporter with a self-satisfied air, saying, "Although I have been in the Government-General officialdom for 17 years<120>, this is the first time I have thought of taking someone's head myself," one can glimpse a part of it. Also, Governor Ikuta of North P'yŏngan Province is one of the old-timers and also a direct subordinate of Kiuchi, so he should have considerable influence (there is currently talk of him becoming Industry Bureau Chief). Current Inspector Tanaka Usaburō should also have some influence. (There is also talk of an honorable transfer.) Consequently, it is predictable that Governors Iio of Hwanghae, Wada of Kyŏngsang, Nakano of Hamgyŏng North, Yoneda of P'yŏngan South, Pak Chungyang of Ch'ungch'ŏng North, and Yi Kyuwan of Hamgyŏng South, who belong to the newcomer faction, will find it difficult to preserve their positions. There are also rumors of resignation for Dr. Shiga, Shimomura of Industry, and Nagano of Education. Governor Yi Kapyŏng of Kangwŏn Province has Count Song Pyŏngjun, who has deep ties to the Kenseikai, behind him, so his position will conversely be solidified. There is talk of honorable transfers for Yuge of Railways and Mizuguchi of Finance, who have worked diligently in Korean educational administration for 10 years. Also, the police department chiefs in each province, who are direct subordinates of Mr. Maruyama, are expected to undergo a major reshuffle; first, there is talk of the dismissal of Chiefs Fujiwara of Kyŏnggi, Takeuchi of North P'yŏngan, Andō of South P'yŏngan, and Noda of North Hamgyŏng. Then, what kind of figures are the newest newcomers, the newly appointed Police Affairs Bureau Chief Mitsuya Miyamatasu and Finance Bureau Chief Kusama Hideo, and what will their influence be on the Government-General going forward? Mr. Mitsuya, originally from Yamagata Prefecture, graduated from Tokyo Imperial University's German Law Department and was first employed as an attaché (屬) in the Ministry of Education. After serving as an administrative official (事務官) in Gifu and Fukui prefectures, he also served as police department chief in Nara, Mie, Miyagi, and Kyōto prefectures. In the 6th year of Taishō [1917], while serving as Internal Affairs Department Chief of Fukui Prefecture, he was implicated in a corruption case involving Kyōto assembly members and faced temporary suspension from duty. When the matter ended without incident, he was re-employed by the Home Ministry, becoming a professor at the Police Training Institute and concurrently a councilor (參事官) at the Home Ministry. Subsequently, he served as head of the Ports and Harbors Section and concurrently the General Affairs Section of the Home Ministry's Public Works Bureau. He spent about two years on an overseas inspection tour. After returning, during the Kantō Earthquake, he served as the director of the temporary Earthquake Relief Bureau's Yokohama branch. Last October, during the Fujiwara cabinet era, he succeeded Mr. Tago Kazutami as the chief of the first department of the Social Affairs Bureau, where he remained until today. He is a vigorous man of 45, said to have studied social policy, labor issues, and ideological matters extensively, and is also famous as a sword researcher (刀釰研究家). (His family collection includes over 200 swords.) During his time as Police Department Chief...

...there are comments that it feels somewhat like sending Governor-General Saitō after the March First Movement<121> to appease the Korean people's hearts. When he boasted to a certain visiting reporter, "I have considerable research and much experience regarding social policy and ideological issues, so I will pay deep attention to the ideological education in complex and chaotic Korea. On the other hand, I will strive to disseminate social work facilities, focusing on removing anxieties in real life and spirit, and fulfill the duty of maintaining order and security," it seems he has some self-confidence. Anyway, the general speculation is that his methods and policies will differ somewhat from Mr. Maruyama's. Also, since he had a very close relationship with Kiuchi when Kiuchi was Governor of Kyōto Prefecture and Mitsuya was Police Department Chief, his influence will certainly be great as long as the current cabinet remains in power. Next is Finance Bureau Chief Kusama. Like the former bureau chief, Dr. Wada (currently head of the Gōdō Bank), he has a reputation as a heavy drinker and womanizer (好色男). (Mr. Wada has a second wife younger than his own daughter, and Mr. Kusama's wife is also a beauty.) Originally the head of the Osaka Mint Bureau, he has now been brought to Korea. Although he comes from a tax official background, he was unfamiliar with Korean affairs, leading to much criticism within the Government-General even at the time of his appointment. Shortly after arriving, he told a reporter, "Korea is still undeveloped territory, so there is much work to do, and since the finances amount to 140 or 150 million won, there is potential for utilization." Judging by this, it seems he has some ambition. However, seeing that his first act upon arrival was to circulate an official notice within the Finance Bureau absolutely forbidding the use of the Korean language, receiving unnecessary attacks from the general public, one can know his ignorance of Korean affairs. Consequently, it is doubtful whether he can truly develop Korea and effectively utilize the huge sum of 140 or 150 million won as he stated. Judging by the prohibition of the Korean language, it seems unlikely he will make even the slightest effort for the sake of Koreans.) However, he has ties with Police Affairs Bureau Chief Mitsuya from their middle school days, and since there has traditionally been bad blood between the Government-General and the Ministry of Finance and the Home Ministry, Political Affairs Superintendent Shimooka is said to have specifically selected Mr. Kusama, who belongs to the Ministry of Finance faction, to alleviate this. Therefore, regardless of his character, skill, or insight, there is no doubt he will have some influence. To sum up, the power within the Korean Government-General henceforth will center on the old-timer Ōtsuka of the Kiuchi faction, followed by the influence of Ikuta from the same faction, and also the considerable influence of the newest newcomer, Mitsuya. In other words, Waesŏngdae, which was previously a stage for the rivalry between the two giants Maruyama and Moriya, will now become a stage for the rivalry of the three giants: Ōtsuka, Ikuta, and Mitsuya. Consequently, the atmosphere of secret struggles, factional disputes, and jealousy, just like in the Maruyama-Moriya era, will once again fill the building. Furthermore, Political Affairs Superintendent Shimooka, formerly a leader of the Kenseikai, has the backing of the Kenseikai, so his policies and facilities may clash with Governor-General Saitō's. From a certain perspective, the Government-General might become even more discordant than during the Maruyama-Moriya era. (Due to this point, rumors of the Governor-General's resignation frequently circulate.) However, Superintendent Shimooka, seasoned in factional struggles for many years, known as "Chi-san Sōkan" [治山總監 - likely a nickname referring to his political skill or perhaps forestry background, context unclear],<122> specifically renounced his party affiliation upon appointment and also declared after arriving, "I will take utmost care to avoid partisan favoritism (黨同伐異)." Thus, perhaps the disgraceful behavior of the Maruyama-Moriya era, as generally speculated, will not be repeated. This is a point to watch closely in the future. However, if the consolidation and abolition of bureaus and departments, said to be announced soon, is implemented, personnel changes will naturally be numerous. Consequently, old-timers will occupy various key positions, and the newest newcomers (those newly arriving from Japan) will also likely occupy some key positions. Thus, the current period is a time of greatest anxiety for the Government-General officialdom, and it goes without saying that behind the scenes, there will be much disgraceful behavior in the maneuvering for positions.
(According to rumors, the General Affairs Department (庶務部) and Documents Section (文書課) will be abolished and merged into the Secretariat (官房); the Investigation Section (調査課) will be abolished, with its duties delegated to various bureaus and departments as before; the Education Bureau (學務局) will also be abolished and merged into the Internal Affairs Bureau (內務局), consolidating the Religion, Education, and Compilation sections; Railways and Public Works will also be merged; the positions of Civil Affairs Inspector (民情視察事務官) and Central Council Advisor (中樞院 參議) will be reduced; there was initial talk of abolishing the Councillor (參與官) position, but due to circumstances, it will remain for now; and the retention or abolition of the Forestry Station (營林廠) is still undecided.) (觀相者 - Kwansangja) <123>

**Summary**

This 1924 article from the Korean magazine *Kaebyŏk* details a significant power shift within the Japanese Government-General of Korea. It describes the decline of the "newcomer" faction (新來種, Shinrae-jong), associated with officials like Maruyama and Moriya who rose under Governor-General Saitō's initial "cultural policy," and the resurgence of the "old-timer" faction (在來種, Chaerae-jong), officials with longer experience in Korea, now backed by connections to the new Katō cabinet in Japan, particularly through figures like Ōtsuka and Kiuchi Jūshirō. The article anticipates continued internal conflict and maneuvering for positions, highlighting the appointment of new figures like Police Chief Mitsuya and Finance Chief Kusama, noting Kusama's controversial ban on Korean language use within his bureau. For historians, this piece offers a fascinating contemporary Korean perspective on the internal politics, factionalism, and personalities within the highest levels of the Japanese colonial administration. It reveals the instability beneath the surface of colonial rule, the direct influence of metropolitan Japanese politics on colonial appointments, and Korean observations on the perceived attitudes and policies (like language suppression) of incoming Japanese officials.

흔들리는 總督府속, 新來種의 凋落과 在來種의 擡頭
齊藤總督 到任후 과거 5개년의 倭城臺는 소위 新來種 日本人의 독점적 무대이엿다. 水野政務總監을 위시하야 각부, 국, 과장 至於 지방 관리까지 중요의 의자는 대개 新來種이 점거하고 伊藤, 寺內 시대부터 공로가 多하고 경험이 多하다는 소위 在來種 日本人은 失母의 兒와 가티 一隅에 雌伏하야 但히 殘飯餘 滓을 得嘗하고 암중예 飮泣할 뿐이엿다. (兩種間 질시, 反目, 저주 등이 有함은 물론, 특히 水野 문하의 兩驕兒인 丸山警務局長과 守屋庶務部長은 新來種의 대표적 인물로 總督府의 전세력을 持하야 萬權을 擅弄하얏다. 彼等의 안중에는 민중도 無하고 (특히 朝鮮人) 總督도 無하고  심한 때에는 자기의 국가까지도 無하얏다. 다시 말하면 과거 5년 간 朝鮮의 정치는 소위 문화를 표방한 齊藤男 一頭의 정치가 안이오. 橫暴縱逸한 경찰권을 握한 丸山군과 교활한 口와 필을 弄하는 守屋군의 兩頭政治엿다. 丸山은 警察로 朝鮮人 억압을 위주하나 守屋은 강연, 연설, 논문으로 日鮮融和를 제창하고 又 소위 修養團을 조직하야 鮮日人의 共存共榮을 주창함) 堅固難拔한 彼等의 세력은 水野去後 有吉시대에도 의연 保持되야 齊藤總督의 雙翼이 되고 新來種의 보장이 되얏다. 然而 護憲3派 聯立의 加藤內閣이 성립되고 憲政會의 유물인 下岡군이 政務總監의 의자를 據하게 됨은 新來種에 일대 颴風이다. 즉 下岡군이 내임한 지 未久에 丸山 守屋 양 거두가 일시 퇴거함은 秋風과 共히 新來種이 凋落되고 在來種이 기운을 回하야 舊芽를 更發하는 兆懲을 語함이다. 밧구어 말하면 금후의 총독부 내에는 新來種이 其影을 絶하고 在來種이 頭를 再擡할 것이다. 其中에도 세인이 공인하는 바 在來種의 대표인 大塜內務의 세력이 중싊�이 될 것이다. 이 大塜군은 비룩 수완이 無하고 정견이 無한 일개 사무가에 불과하나 17년 간이나 朝鮮에 在하야 경험이 多하고 且 現 內閣 이면의 重鎭인 木內重四郞군의 직계로서 下岡政務總監이 渡鮮할 시에 木內로부터 旣히 내탁이 有하다 云한즉 其 세력이 特大할 것은 물론이다.
