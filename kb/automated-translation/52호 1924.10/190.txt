自由通情 (Free Communication/Open Forum)
Japan After the Earthquake
Ma Saeng

Because of the earthquake, post-earthquake Japan has discovered a clear green color (緣色) and a crimson color like the garden balsam flower. When first entering Tokyo, the first thing that caught my eye were the signboards, clear and beautiful in green and crimson, reaching far. The signboards were even larger than the shops. Japan has certainly discovered crimson and green. All decorative arts were incomplete without them.

Similarly, the hearts of the Japanese people—the Japanese people themselves—have found green and crimson.—From their own hearts—Even after the disaster, they work tirelessly, expending all their energy without being exhausted. There is a crimson passion. People strive to help each other. They have come to know each other as fellow humans. The green of peace and hope shines. And the green and crimson were exceedingly beautiful and clear. Clear and beautiful. The gorgeous clothing and makeup were exceedingly beautiful. Every woman seen was captivatingly—lovely.

Two Meals a Day
Pang Hwankwi, Tokyo Kōgyō Senshū Gakkō (Tokyo Industrial Training School)

This day last autumn marks when my life, amidst the great perils of the Great Earthquake and Great Fire, crossed the line of death from惨殺 (massacre) again, gaining survival in this world today. Recalling and commemorating that time, the more I think deeply, the more it truly feels like a dream and not reality. At the same time, myriad emotions surge, pressing against my chest, but it is difficult to write them all down one by one. (Middle omitted) Having thus regained my life, which was not by chance, I vow to experience the world's myriad hardships even more, gain greater self-awareness, and make new discoveries. During September, I commemorate the bitter past events for myself and my compatriots by eating two meals a day. (End omitted)

To the Editor
Chang Chinsik

Regarding my article "Questioning Mr. Yi Tonhwa's View on Love" published in the "Free Communication" section of the July "Kaebyŏk" 4th-anniversary issue, as a result of several discussions in the meantime, although I fully understand Kaebyŏk's intention in establishing the "Free Communication" section and the nature of that section, in any case, that article of mine was not sent as a manuscript for "Free Communication," so I am just stating that intention now.

To the Readers
Editor

Please look closely at this section and send many articles suitable for "Free Communication." Communication between reader and reader, reader and writer, reader and editor—anything is fine. Also, when you see any country or any region, and you don't have time to write at length, but cannot remain silent, it would be good if you could briefly jot down and send such real feelings and real observations. <124>

**Summary**

This "Free Communication" section features four short pieces: Ma Saeng reflects on the aesthetic changes and renewed spirit observed in Tokyo after the Great Kanto Earthquake, focusing on the prominence of green and crimson colors symbolizing peace, hope, and passion. Pang Hwankwi recounts his survival of the earthquake and the subsequent massacre of Koreans, vowing to gain greater awareness and commemorating the tragic event by eating only two meals a day in September. Chang Chinsik clarifies that a previously published piece was not intended for this specific section. Finally, the editor encourages readers to submit short observations, feelings, or communications for the open forum. From a historical perspective, Pang Hwankwi's piece is particularly significant, offering a direct personal testimony to the trauma of the Kanto Massacre, a often-overlooked aspect of the earthquake's aftermath where Korean residents were targeted. Ma Saeng's piece, while focusing on aesthetics, provides a contemporary Korean perspective on Japanese society's response to the disaster. The editor's note highlights the magazine's role as a platform for diverse Korean voices and experiences during the colonial period.

自由通情
震災 後의 日本
馬生

일본은 震災로 하야, 震災 후의 日本은 맑은 緣色과 봉선화 가튼 빩안 빗을, 차자 냇다. 東京을 처음 들어설 때, 제일 먼저 눈에 뜨인 것이 緣色 빩안 빗의 어대까지 맑고 고흔 간판이엇다. 상점보다도 큰 간판이엇다. 日本은 확실히 빩안 빗과 緣色을 발견하얏다. 모든 장식 미술이 이것을 빼놋코는 업섯다.
그와 맛찬가지 日本, 사람의 마음도-日本 사람도-緣色과 빩안 빗을 차저 냇다.-自己 마음으로부터-災 後에도 오히려 기진하지 안코 정력을 다하야 일한다. 새빩안 열이 잇다 사람 사람은 서로서로 도와 주려고 힘쓴다. 서로 사람을 알게 되앗다. 緣色의 평화와 희망이 빗난다. 그리고 緣色과 빩안 빗은 몹시 곱고, 맑앗다. 맑고 고왓다. 화사한 의복과 화장이 몹시 고왓다. 뵈는 여자마다, 반게-어엽브다.

日二食
東京工業專修學校 方煥龜

昨秋 금일은 나의 생명이 大震, 大火의 大災危 중에서, 다시 慘殺의 死線을 越하야 금일, 이 세상에서 생존을 엇고 其時를 추억, 기념하게 되는 것은 진실로 깁히 생각할사록 꿈갓고 생시갓지는 안습니다. 동시에 만감은 가슴을 밀고 소사오르나이다만은 일일이 집필키 어렵슴니다. (中畧) 이가티 우연치 안은 생기를 다시 어든 나는 더욱더욱이 세상의 천신만고를 체험하야 일층 큰 자각과, 새 발명을 엇기를 맹세하오며, 9월 중은 日二食으로써 나와 밋 동포이 쓸알인 녯 일을 기념함니다. (下畧)

編輯兄에게
張鎭植
