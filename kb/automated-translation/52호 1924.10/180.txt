Rumors

Perhaps because the Police Affairs Bureau (警務局) is originally a place that esteems military rule (武斷), it seems the surname of the person who becomes its Director must also resemble a weapon. The former Director had the character 丸 (Maru - round/bullet) for 'bullet' (탄환), and the new Director again has the character 矢 (Ya - arrow) for 'bow and arrow' (弓矢). However, since it's the rule that the power of three arrows (三矢 - Mitsuya) is inherently less than that of a mountain-like bullet (如山의 丸 - Maruyama), the pressure of the military rule police (武斷警察) on Chōsen (朝鮮) will likely decrease somewhat compared to the Maruyama era.

Recently, when Mr. Maruyama was coming to bid his final farewell to Chōsen, he showed his pass identifying him as the Director of the Chōsen Government-General Police Affairs Bureau (朝鮮總督府 警務局長) to the Pusan (釜山) stationmaster. When asked if it wasn't improper for a dismissed person to travel for free, the stationmaster reportedly said it was fine as he was still handling residual duties (殘務 廢理), so he boarded with peace of mind. Judging by his habit of arresting people well, he seems somewhat clean when it comes to money. Whether this is a case of "When a person is about to die, their words are good" (人之將死 其言也善格) is unknown, but how about the railway bureau gives Mr. Maruyama a wooden cup (木盃) as a final commendation for being admirable?

It is said that in the current administrative and financial reorganization, about 3,000 officials in the Government-General are expected to be eliminated. However, a certain janitor (掃除夫) at the Government-General is worried and curious, wondering whether the feces in the high officials' (高等官) toilets or the junior officials' (判任官) toilets will decrease.

There's a Kyŏngsŏng (京城) slang expression: "You don't know if the Namsan (南山) pinecone (솔방울) will fall upwards or downwards." Indeed, Mr. Saitō Makoto (齊藤實) is the Namsan pinecone (南山 松實). The autumn wind (秋風) keeps blowing—will he fall or not? <123>

**Summary**

This short piece uses sarcasm and wordplay to comment on changes in the Japanese colonial administration in Korea in 1924. It notes the replacement of Police Affairs Bureau Director Maruyama (丸山) with Mitsuya (三矢), speculating playfully that Mitsuya ("three arrows") might be less oppressive than Maruyama ("bullet mountain"). It includes an anecdote about Maruyama's departure, touches upon rumored large-scale layoffs of officials within the Government-General, and ends by likening the uncertain position of Governor-General Saitō Makoto (齊藤實) to a pinecone waiting to fall in the autumn wind.

Historians might find the article's sarcastic tone revealing of Korean sentiment towards colonial officials and policies like "military rule" (武斷). The specific references to Maruyama, Mitsuya, Saitō, the rumored layoffs, and the use of contemporary slang offer insights into the political atmosphere and public discourse in colonized Korea during the mid-1920s, a period often characterized by the shift towards the so-called "Cultural Policy" yet still marked by underlying tensions and critiques of Japanese authority. The wordplay on names and the janitor's perspective add a layer of social commentary.

流言蜚語
警務局은 원래 武斷을 尙하는 곳이닛가 그러한지 局長되는 사람의 姓까지도 무기를 像한 姓이 안이면 안되는 모양이다. 舊局長은 탄환이라는 丸字를 가젓더니 新局長은 弓矢라는 矢字를 또 가젓다. 그러나 3개의 矢(三矢)는 본시 其力이 如山의 丸(丸山)만 못한 법이닛가 朝鮮에 대한 武斷警察의 압력도 丸山시대보다는 좀 감소되겟지.
근간 丸山군이 朝鮮을 최후 고별하랴고 오는 도중에 釜山역장에게 朝鮮總督府 警務局長이라는 명의의 파스를 示하고 遞任된 자로 무료승차가 부당하지 안으냐고 言한즉 역장은 아즉 殘務 廢理 중이닛가 무방하다고 해서 안심하고 승차하엿단다. 사람의 잘 잡던 버릇을 보와서는 금전에는 좀 청결한 듯 하다. 이것이 人之將死에 其言也善格인지 不知하거니와 철도국에서도 최후로 丸山군에게 기특하다고 木盃나 한 개 상여함이 何如.
금번 행정 재정의 정리를 하는 판에 총독부 관리의 도태될 자가 약 3,000명이나 된다 전한다. 그런데 高等官便所의 똥이 감소될지 判任官 변소의 똥이 감소될지 알 수 업다고 총독부의 某 掃除夫는 궁금해서 걱정.
京城 속어에 南山의 솔방울이 우으로 떠려질지 알에로 떠려질지 모른다더니 齊藤實군이야말로 南山 松實이다. 秋風은 작구 부는데 떠러질지 안 떠러질지.＜123＞
