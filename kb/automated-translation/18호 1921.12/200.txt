Magazine Title: 개벽 제18호
Publication Date: 1921년 12월 01일
Article Title: 氷流 (Ice Flow)
Author: 金石松
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Ice flows on the River (江).
House-like cold crystal bodies (結晶軆),
Like rocks and like blades,
Frost flowers (성에ㅅ장) flow.
Brimming as if the riverbank (江ㅅ둑) will burst.

Swish! Swish! Swish!
Crackle! Crackle!
The sound of collapsing
And the sound of crashing
Give endless terror (恐怖).

The grey curtain (灰色幕) of dusk (黃昏)
Has already enveloped the great earth (大地).
From somewhere, with a thin voice,
Crying "Save me..."
A ghastly (凄慘) cry (부르지즘) is heard.

(However, that cry
Is also heard within my heart.
Ice flows in my heart too.)
In the long river (가람) of life (生命),
I see the frost flowers (성에) remaining.<119>

**Summary**

The poem vividly depicts the powerful and terrifying scene of ice floes moving down a river at dusk, creating sounds of destruction and evoking fear. Amidst this, a faint cry for help is heard, which the speaker internalizes, realizing that a similar coldness and cry exists within their own heart and the "river of life." From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this poem, published in 1921 in the nationalist magazine *Kaebyŏk*, can be interpreted as a metaphor for the harsh and oppressive reality of Japanese rule. The destructive ice flow likely symbolizes the colonial power or the bleak situation of the nation, while the cry for help represents the suffering of the Korean people. The speaker's internal ice and cry reflect the shared national despair and the individual's anguish under oppression, making it a poignant piece for historians studying the cultural and emotional responses to colonialism.

氷流
						金石松
江에 얼음이 흐른다
집채가튼 치운 結晶軆가
바위갓고 칼날가튼
성에ㅅ장이 흘러간다
江ㅅ둑이 터질듯이 갓득

쇄! 쇄! 쇄!
빠지직! 빠지직!
문허지는 소리와
부다치는 소리가
限업시 恐怖를 준다

黃昏의 灰色幕은
어느덧 大地를 에워쌋다
어대선지 가녈핀 소리로
「사람 살리오..」하는
凄慘한 부르지즘이 들린다

(그러나 그 부르지즘은
나의 가슴속에서도 들린다
나의 가슴에도 얼음이 흐른다)
生命의 기인 가람에
성에가 남을 나는 본다＜119＞
