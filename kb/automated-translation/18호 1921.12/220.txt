Article Title: 겨을 아츰 (Winter Morning)

A Winter Morning
						Kim Sŏksong

Early in the morning
When taking a walk

The telephone wires fiercely
Seemed to make a humming sound

In the sky, frozen clear
With a cobalt blue light

My heart-strings also resonated
My spirit also trembled

Above my head
A single kite
Pee-oh - Pee-oh...

-(Excerpted from Below Great-grandmother's Grave)- <120>

**Summary**

This short poem by Kim Sŏksong depicts a solitary walk on a clear, cold winter morning. The poet observes the stark beauty of the scene – the intensely humming telephone wires against a cobalt blue sky – which evokes a strong internal response, making the poet's "heart-strings" resonate and "spirit" tremble. The poem concludes with the cry of a kite overhead, adding a touch of wildness. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in *Kaebyŏk*, reflects the continuation of lyrical expression in Korean during the early colonial period. Historians might find the poem's evocative imagery, the blend of modern elements (telephone wires) with nature, and the use of early 20th-century Korean orthography (e.g., '아츰', '한울', '울엇다') of interest.
