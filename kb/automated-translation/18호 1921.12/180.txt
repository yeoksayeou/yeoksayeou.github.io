잡지명	개벽 제18호
발행일	1921년 12월 01일
기사제목	藝術界의 回顧 一年間 (Retrospective of the Art World for One Year)
필자	曉鍾 (Hyojong)
기사형태	논설 (Editorial/Essay)

藝術界의 回顧 一年間
						曉鍾

Sitting down on New Year's Day (元月元旦) and thinking of the year, it seems long; looking at the 12 months, it seems distant; counting the 365 days, one cannot help but wonder how they will pass. However, as one day passes, then two, one month passes, then two, suddenly all 12 months have gone by. Looking back once more, the feeling of length one had sitting on New Year's Day has vanished, and only the illusion (幻影) remains in one's head that the entire year has passed like a dream. Who was it that called life (人生) a spring dream (一場春夢)? Perhaps this was not said when a person is born into this world, but rather a lament uttered in the moment (刹那間) of death, looking back (回顧) on one's past life (過去一生).

During this past year, what have we accomplished for ourselves personally, for society (社會), or nationally (民族的)? What distinct memories (記憶) have become eternally embedded in our minds (腦裡)? Ultimately (畢竟), when we look back on the past year, how much did it differ from what we initially thought and hoped (希望) for? How many things went awry? How much were we deceived, and how much did we deceive others? No matter how much we think about it, it seems we spend one year, ten years, and indeed our entire lives deceiving ourselves, deceiving others, deceiving society, while simultaneously being deceived by ourselves, deceived by others, and deceived by society. All people interpret this deceiving and being deceived with good intentions (善意), calling its cause (原因) hope (希望), and try to get through this world using things like compromise (妥協) or saving face (體面) to hide this deceiving and being deceived; this is our current human society (人類社會). How can one not feel disheartened (寒心)?

Now, as we face the end of the year, how has our art world (藝術界) deceived us over the past year, and how have we been deceived? At the very least, the reporter earnestly hoped and believed there would definitely be great progress (一大進步) <111> this year compared to last year, and that a higher level of improvement would be visible, but ultimately (畢竟), that too turned out to be mere deception. Of course, in our society where cultural fortunes (文運) are not ample, perhaps this current state (現狀) is the norm (常態), but it is exceedingly disappointing compared to what was hoped for. Looking at all aspects of literature and art (文藝方面), gathering the memorable events that are worth mentioning, even in passing: in the art world (美術界), there was the Western painting exhibition (洋畫展覽會) by Madam Na Hyesŏk (羅蕙錫 女史) held early this year on the upper floor of the Kyŏngsŏng Ilbo building (京城日報社樓上), followed in the spring by the Calligraphy and Painting Art Association's (書畫美術協會) exhibition (書畫展覽會) held at the Chungang School (中央學校) building. Although these two exhibitions may not have received the desired enthusiastic response, considering the unpreparedness (未備) of our current society, they must be considered quite good news (好消息). Besides these, I don't know if there was any other good news in art, but there seems to have been nothing major worth remembering. Turning to the music world (音樂界), in terms of numbers, there were quite many events, and in terms of frequency, one cannot say there wasn't an excess of sponsorship (主催). Setting aside the concert held by Mrs. Yu (柳氏夫人) for our side as a separate issue (別問題), apart from the visit to the homeland (祖國) by the Russian Territory Korean Student Music Troupe (露領朝鮮人學生音樂團), it seems no exaggeration (過言) to say there wasn't a single gathering worthy of being called a concert. In the reporter's personal (個人) opinion, no matter how amateur the performers, regardless of their objective (目的) or inclination (趣向), as long as they attach the name (名稱) 'concert,' they should not merely aim for 50 or 30 jeon in money (金錢), but rather make it a bit more enjoyable (滋味) for the listeners, helping them understand the appreciation (趣味) of music a little better. Wouldn't this be beneficial (有益) for the organizers (主催者), the audience (觀覽者), and for the development of our art (藝術發展上)? Go to any of the monthly concerts, regardless of the type or organizer, and it's invariably the same thing, the same people. Because of this, our public (一般) has come to distrust (信用) concerts. Consequently, organizers can no longer find satisfaction (滋味) in the income (收入) from 50 or 30 jeon admission fees (入場料), bringing our society's music scene to a慘酷 (miserable) state (地境). Thinking about it overall, this can only be called the thoughtlessness (無分別) of those who organize concerts. Thus, looking at the music world over this past year, one cannot help but call it a disheartening situation. In the reporter's personal opinion, if one is to organize a concert, it should be done with material (資料) that can foster goodwill (好意) towards music among the general public, or else it would be better to stop altogether, so as not to create a negative perception (不信한 觀念) of music itself (自軆). Shouldn't it be right for us, regardless of the group (團軆) or project (事業), rather than seeking immediate small profits (目前의 小利) by borrowing some fine name (美名) out of momentary curiosity (一時의 好奇心) to gather some money (돈냥), <112> to avoid damaging the honor (名譽損害) of music itself when calling something a concert? Let us turn our pen to the theater world (演劇界). The existing troupes calling themselves such-and-such Troupe (團) or such-and-such Theater (座), self-proclaimed new school (新派) or old school (舊派), are not even worth discussing. Beyond them, the staging of plays (演劇을 上演) by various social organizations (社會의 各團軆) this year is probably a first in recent times (近者). Did this theater fever (演劇熱) truly arise from their love (愛護) for dramatic art (劇藝術)? Absolutely not. The majority (大部分), after losing the authority (權威) of the name 'concert' among the general public (一般民衆) for collecting admission fees, have merely switched to the new signboard (新看板) of amateur theater troupes (生手劇團)—they call it amateur drama (素人劇)—out of necessity (不得己) for income (收入上). In this way, the name 'theater' is also losing credibility (信望) with the general public at the hands of these youth groups. The recent emergence of the Art Association Theater Troupe (藝術協會劇壇), finally managing to establish the name 'drama,' albeit immaturely (未成), can be called a great harvest (收穫) for our theater scene. However, if they become satisfied (滿足) with just that or fail to make even small progress (進就), we cannot help but be disappointed (落望) here too. We cannot help but feel regret about our theater world over the past year. Next, let's look at our literary scene (文壇). Of course, in our current situation (現今의 形便), the lack of freedom of publication (出版의 自由) and freedom of speech (言論의 解放) are undeniably significant obstacles (障碍), but there is far too much desolation (寂寥). Thankfully, creations (創作) are seen monthly through our Kaebyŏk Magazine (開闢雜誌), but beyond that, although works are occasionally published (發表) in the world like beans sprouting in a drought, it is no small regret (遺憾) that in some magazines, one can see things titled 'novel' (小說) where it's impossible to distinguish (分揀) whether it's a novel or just rambling (大說), labeled only as a 'short story' (短篇). Of course, it is a fact (事實) that in our current circumstances (目今 形便), earning a living with the sharp tip of a pen cannot match scooping food with a large spoon (匙), and we know that one cannot abandon real life (實生活), large or small, to devote oneself wholeheartedly (專心專力) to creative work. However, even so, there is no lack of utterly shameless (沒廉恥) behavior. Therefore, while we cannot say that the quantity (量) or quality (品位上) produced by our literary scene this past year was absolutely nothing (皆無), considering our circumstances and situation (形便), one cannot deny that it falls far short of expectations.

Now, looking specifically at the arts (藝術方面) over the past year—in art (美術), music (音樂), theater (演劇), and literature (文學)—putting aside the quantity (量) or quality (質), the absence of any stable dedication (安定된 執着力) is something the reporter, along with discerning readers (讀者諸位), laments <113> and deeply deplores (痛嘆). Whenever the author says such things, some among you who do not hold favorable feelings (好感) towards the author may call it arrogance (高慢) or narrow-mindedness (偏狹), but this is definitely not self-justification (自我辨明). Thinking about it, it is not out of arrogance or narrow-mindedness. It is because the author expects too much from the general public, and because of an impatient heart that wishes for us to quickly (急히) advance onto the path of civilization (文明) like others. This hoping heart and impatient nature (性情) should not be dismissed merely as narrow-mindedness. Deep down, it is because the author, like all of you, is a Korean (朝鮮人); because I grew up on this land, matured on this land, will live on this land, and will die on this land. Unfortunately, because this era is different from others, where world (世界) civilization crosses boundaries like neighbors visiting, and seeing others is easier than meeting village (洞里) folk, when we see others and then look at ourselves, naturally (自然) feelings of envy arise, followed by anxiety (心事), resentment (憤心), and sorrow. Therefore, when I take up the pen, there are inevitably some radical words (過激한 言辭), but if our readers cannot accept (容認) this, then I have no choice but to throw down even this foolish, dull (鈍), and clumsy pen. If I were not Korean, why would I meddle and repeat myself (重言復言) about what you all do? If I had not grown up on this land, matured on this land, and were not someone who will die on this land, for what reason (理由) would I pronounce judgment (曰可曰否) on your actions? Ultimately (畢竟), this stems from mutual affection (愛情) and is nothing more than the intention to help each other live. If you were not Korean, and I were not Korean, would I say such things? If you and I had no connection whatsoever (何等의 關連) to the Korean people (朝鮮民族), why would I say such things? But regardless, that doesn't matter. If on one hand, there are people who dislike (실혀하고) and insult (辱) the author, then on the other hand, there will be people who like (조하하고) and praise (稱讚) the author. Whether insulted or praised, that too is irrelevant. The author – just as I do not like being insulted by a certain group of people, I also do not particularly like hearing praise. Only by measuring against the standard (標準) of whether the words I cry out (부르짓는) come from a sincere heart (赤心)? Whether they are free from shame before my conscience (良心)? – even if there are errors in the method (方式) or mistakes in the means (手段), if the words I intend to speak come from a sincere heart for our compatriots (同胞), I will not refrain (辭讓) from opening my mouth even if thousands dislike it. Even if thousands insult this pen tip, if I am shameless before my conscience, <114> I will not hesitate (躕躇) to wield even this worn-out brush (禿筆). It is in this sense (意味下) that because I expect much from you all, I am consequently often discouraged (落膽) when I see what you do. Compared to advanced (先進) foreign countries, we should consider our one year as ten or twenty years. If they lament having achieved nothing in one year, we should lament having achieved nothing in ten or twenty years. Lifting our eyes and looking around our society, there is no shortage of unpreparedness. Why only the art world? Everything equally lacks creation (創設), progress (進步) is slow, and there is no reform (改造). However, the reason I repeatedly harp on the people in the art world is that I expect more from the art world than anything else, and because I know that among all the things necessary for us in various fields, art is especially necessary. We need to promote industry (實業을 獎勵) and gather some money (金錢). That is certainly necessary. We need to know things. We need to educate (敎育) our children (子姪) and guide (鞭撻) society with what we know. That is an extremely good thing. But that alone is not enough to feel secure (安心). No matter how much money one has, we do not desire businessmen (實業家) or wealthy people (財産家) who view their compatriots like snakes and scorpions (蛇蝎視) and disregard society (社會를 度外視). Even if one's knowledge (知識) is abundant (豐富), if they make no effort (努力) for the nation (民族) and take actions against their compatriots (反同胞의 行動), we definitely do not require (要求) such intellectuals (知識家) or scholars (學者). Therefore, we must fundamentally seek human nature (人類의 本性) and desire intellectuals and the wealthy who are grounded in the virtues (美德) of warmth (溫情), philanthropy (博愛), and mutual aid (互相扶助). To find these virtues, we must seek Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (眞, 善, 美) above all else. This is why the author has constantly, through our Kaebyŏk magazine, spoken of the elements (要素) of literature (文學) since 'Hyŏndang Tokp'ae' (玄堂獨吠), advocated (提唱) for popular drama (民衆劇) as an urgent task (急務) for enlightenment work (主化事業), introduced (紹介) Germany's (獨逸) art movement (藝術運動) and Expressionism (表現主義), argued that everything starts from beauty (美), and serialized plays (戱曲) from the inaugural issue until now. We too must create (創造), reform (改造), and import (輸入) like others. Among these, we desire creation, wish for reform, and seek importation based on Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (眞, 善, 美를 根底로 한). It is in this sense that, whether small or large, right or wrong, the author strives to chatter away. Therefore, we must enrich the internal sentiments (內部的 情操) of human life (人間生活). The fact that the Great European War (歐洲大戰) leads to the search for world reform (世界의 改造) today and the advocacy of culturalism (文化主義) may differ in form (形式), means (方便), and methods (手段), but if we expose (露骨的) the underlying demands (裡面의 要求), they stem from the realization that the current social system (現社會의 이 制度) and methods (方式) cannot possibly perfect (完全) human life <115> or maintain world peace (世界의 平和). However, in our past human society, who made (맨든 것이며) those systems and methods? What kind of people created them? They were also made (맨든 것이요) and created by us humans. Therefore, proposing to reform those systems and methods is merely fixing the surface (끗), not fixing things fundamentally (根本的). If we truly seek fundamental (基礎的), root-level reform and call for culture, then above all, we must reform the humans who create everything in society. Reforming humans, of course, does not mean instantly changing their physical form (形軆), making two eyes into one or one nose into two. Is it not about enriching their spirit (心靈), the sentiments of their inner lives (內部生活의 情操)? In other words, is it not about seeking human nature (人間의 本性) based on Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (眞, 善, 美)? Therefore, in seeking and calling for reform or culture, we cannot treat art lightly (等閒視). Not only can we not treat it lightly, but we absolutely must seek and find art. Whether this thought of mine is right or wrong is a separate issue, but for me, holding such thoughts, as I reach the end (끗달) of the year and look back at our art world, experiencing that sense of desolation (寂寥), how can I not feel disheartened and possess feelings of grief (悲痛의 情)? Let's say the words emerging from this are somewhat radical (過激). That can only be called the colored glasses (色眼鏡) of the viewer and listener, and furthermore, the author cannot refrain from saying what he wants to say or writing what he wants to write just because such people exist.

Of course, I am not asking you to interpret the meaning of reform (改造) or culturalism (文化主義) solely through the artistic advocacy (藝術主張) or the focus on Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (眞, 善, 美) that I have mentioned, nor am I demanding such an interpretation. Interpretations will vary depending on the advocate or the opinion. To take the easiest example, for the past 4 or 5 years, it is said that Korea's (朝鮮) military rule (武斷政治) has changed (變) into cultural rule (文化政治). It is said that power politics (威力政治) has been reformed (改造) into benevolent politics (溫情政治), politics of equal treatment (一視同仁政治). As the governed (被治者), merely hearing these names fills us with such gladness and joy that we cannot contain our feelings of gratitude (感謝). However, looking at the methods of implementation (實行方法) of that reform and culture, the Cemetery Ordinance (墓地令) was reformed to provide convenience (便利) for the burial of the white bones (白骨安葬) of the propertied class (有産者), the yellow uniforms of the military police (憲兵) were removed and replaced with black police (巡査) uniforms, and their gold-rimmed hats were swapped for fedoras (中折帽). Beyond these, no particularly significant reasons for gratitude seem apparent. Perhaps this is because the author expected too much from cultural politics and reform politics, <116> but I wish that here too, a little more Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (眞, 善, 美) could be added (加味). If, among our readers, some are intoxicated (醉) by the word 'culture' and deluded (惑) by the sound of 'reform,' thinking that merely shedding Korean clothes (朝鮮옷) and wearing Western clothes (洋服) constitutes reform, or loosening pinned-up hair (쪽진머리) and adopting Western hairstyles (洋머리) is culture, then shouldn't we desire neither reform nor culture in that sense or manner? Therefore, shouldn't we somehow organize (整理) and settle (安頓) the internal feelings (內部的 感情) of human life, make it a bit more meaningful (意味), think a bit more, adopt a stance of cool observation and passionate critique (冷觀熱評的 態度), and seek reform and culture that have some substance (實地) and true taste (眞味)? Whenever I think about this, culturalism seems to be artism (藝術主義), and reform feels like a synonym (代名詞) for the promotion of art (藝術振興). Consequently, seeing our art world so stagnant and inactive (萎微不振) over the past year, no matter how much other reforms (他方面으르 아모리 다른 改造) are claimed to have occurred or culture (文化) is said to have been propagated (宣傳), I cannot feel at ease (安心) or trust (미들 수가 업는 것이다) it just like that. However, this year too has already passed. Past facts (事實) only remain in our memory (記憶) to enlighten us about the future; there is no way to pursue (追及) them again and make them otherwise. How can we ensure that next year brings true progress with hope? Or will we again feel regret when pursuing past events as we did this year? That is the future, so we cannot sit here and predict (豫測) it. But if only there could be even one step forward, a taste of solidity (着實한 맛), strengthening the exterior while hardening the interior, so that inside and out (內外) are substantial (充實) and appearance and reality (表裡) are consistent, hurrying day by day to live like others and behave like decent human beings like others, how shameless would we then be before our descendants (後世子孫)? If you ask this author whether I spent this year meaningfully, I am truly too ashamed to answer. Therefore, to forcibly justify (辯明) this, I can only say that this year too passed in deception. Time (歲月) passes. Life (人生) grows old. How much longer does the path of deception stretch ahead? This year of Sinyu (辛酉 - 1921) that we saw this year will likely be hard to see again. Farewell, year of Sinyu. Year of Sinyu, whom I never met before and will likely never see again. As I bid you farewell, along with my regretful heart, the fact that I couldn't leave behind even one special accomplishment (事業) with you will likely be a lifelong regret (遺憾).
Farewell, year of Sinyu!! <117>

**Summary**

The author, Hyojong, writing at the end of 1921, expresses deep disappointment and frustration with the state of the Korean arts scene (visual arts, music, theater, literature). He feels the year was marked by deception – artists and organizers deceiving the public (often for money) and the public being deceived, leading to a loss of faith, particularly in concerts and amateur theater productions presented under false pretenses. He laments the lack of genuine progress, stable dedication, and artistic substance compared to his hopes and the achievements of other nations. He argues passionately that true societal progress and "cultural reform" (a term he critiques as superficial in its contemporary political usage) require a fundamental reform of the human spirit, nurtured through art grounded in Truth, Goodness, and Beauty, rather than just superficial changes or materialistic pursuits. This piece is interesting for historians as it provides a critical insider's perspective on the nascent Korean modern arts scene during the early colonial period. It reveals the struggles artists faced, the tension between artistic ideals and economic realities, the pervasive sense of lagging behind the 'civilized' world, and a critical engagement with the Japanese colonial government's rhetoric of "Cultural Rule" (文化政治), dismissed by the author as superficial changes lacking genuine substance or artistic depth.

元月元旦에 안저 1年을 생각하면 긴 것도 갓고 12個月을 두고보면 까마키도 하며 365日을 꼽아보면 어떠케 보내나하는 마음도 업지 아니하지마는 하로가며 이틀가고 한달가며 두달가서 어느덧 12個月을 다 보내고 다시 한번 뒤로 돌아보면 元月元旦에 안저 길다고 생각하던 그 마음은 다 어대로 가고 한갓 1年동안을 꿈가티 지낫다는 幻影만 머리속에 남아잇슬 뿐이다. 누가 人生을 一場春夢이라고 하엿는지? 아마 이것은 사람이 이 世上에 날 때에 한 말이 아니고 죽을 그 刹那間에 過去一生을 回顧하고 嘆怨한 말갓기도 하다.
우리는 今年 이 1年間에 自己의 個人으로나, 또 社會로나, 民族的으로 무슨 일을 하엿스며 또 무슨 두드러진 記憶을 永遠이 腦裡에 잠겨 두게 되엇는가? 우리는 畢竟 過去의 1年을 回顧할 때에 처음부터 自己의 생각하고 希望하던 그것에 比하면 얼마나 틀림이 만흐며 얼마나 어그러진 것이 맛핫고 얼마나 속아낫스며 또 얼마나 남을 속엿는지? 아모리 생각하여도 우리는 自己가 自己를 속이고 自己가 남을 속이며 社會를 속이어가는 同時에 自己가 自己에게 속임을 밧고 남에게 속키며 社會에 속음을 입는 것으로 1年을 보내며 10年을 보내고 또 一生을 마치는 것 갓다. 이러케 속이고 속임을 밧는 그것을 모던 사람들이 善意로 解釋하여 그 原因을 希望이라고 하며 이 속고 속이는 것을 감추어가기 爲하야 妥協이니 體面이니 하는 것으로 이 世上을 지내가랴고 하는 것이 現在의 우리 人類社會이다. 어찌 寒心한 생각이 업스리요.
이제 1年을 다 보내는 이때를 當하야 過去1年間에 우리 藝術界는 어떠케 우리를 속엿스며 또 어떠케 우리가 속혀왓는가. 적어도 記者는 懇切히 바라는 그 마음으로 作年보다 今年은 반듯이 一大進步가 잇슬 줄＜111＞ 알앗고 놉흔 向上이 보일 줄 알앗스나 畢竟 그것도 한갓 속음에 지나고 말앗다. 勿論 文運이 넉넉치 못한 우리 이 社會에 잇서서는 이러한 現狀이 常態일는지는 모르겟스나 바라는 바에 넘우도 섭섭한 일이 만타. 모든 文藝方面이나 藝術方面으로 보아 記者의 腦裡에 記憶되어 잇는 것으로 지나는 말이나마 말할 거리에 드는 것을 거두씀´ 보면 美術界로는 羅蕙錫 女史의 洋畫展覽會가 今年初?에 京城日報社樓上에 開催되엇고 그 뒤를 이어 봄에 書畫美術協會의 書畫展覽會가 中央學校校舍에 開催된 것이 잇섯다. 이 두 展覽會에 바라는 바 두터운 響應은 업섯슬지라도 우리 이 現社會의 未備한 거긔에 對하여서는 자못 조흔 消息이라고 하지 안흘 수가 업섯다. 그 外에 또 무슨 美術에 好消息이 잇섯는지는 모르겟스나 別로 記憶할 무슨 큰 것이 업섯는 것갓다. 그리고 音樂界에 이르러서는 그 數爻로는 퍽도 만핫고 回數로도 過分의 主催가 업섯다고 하지는 못하겟스나 柳氏夫人이 우리側을 爲하야 開催한 것은 別問題로 하고 그 外 露領朝鮮人學生音樂團이 祖國을 訪問한 外에는 하나도 音樂會라고 할만한 무슨 會合이 업섯다고 하여도 過言이 아닌 것 갓다. 記者의 個人의 생각으로는 아모리 生手들일지라도 그 目的이 如何하던지 그 趣向이 어대잇던지 音樂會라는 名稱을 부틴 以上에는 다만 거트로 50錢이나 30錢의 金錢만 바랄 것이 아니라 듯는 사람으로 하여곰 족음 더 滋味가 나게 족음 더 音樂에 對한 趣味를 알게하도록 하는 것이 主催者의 便으로나 觀覽者의 便으로나 우리 藝術發展上에 有益할 것이 아닌가? 다달이 여는 그 音樂會를 가보면 어떠한 音樂會이던지 어떠한 主催者를 勿論하고 千篇一律로 그것이 그것이고 그 사람이 그 사람이다. 여긔에서 우리 一般은 音樂會라는 것을 信用하지 못하게 되엇다. 따라서 主催者로는 50錢이나 30錢의 入場料의 收入에 滋味보지 못하게까지 우리 社會의 音樂界가 慘酷한 地境에 돌엇다. 이것이 도통으로 생각하면 音樂會라는 것을 主催하는 그 사람들의 無分別이라고 할 수밧게는 업다. 그러면 올 1年間의 音樂界를 보아도 寒心한 일이라고 하지 안흘 수가 업다. 記者의 個人의 생각으로는 音樂會를 主催하랴거던 좀더 우리 一般이 音樂에 好意를 품을 만한 資料를 가지고 하던지 그러치 아니하면 차라리 그만두어 音樂이라는 自軆에 對하여 不信한 觀念이나 업도록 하는 것이 조흘 것갓다. 우리는 어떠한 團軆나 어떠한 事業을 勿論하고 金錢의 必要로 어떠한 美名을 빌어서 一時의 好奇心으로 돈냥이나 모아서 目前의 小利를 보는 것보다도＜112＞ 音樂會라고 하면 音樂 그 自軆의 名譽損害은 시키지 아니하는 것이 올흔 일이 아닌가? 吾人은 붓을 돌려 演劇界를 보자. 在來의 무슨團 무슨座라고 하고 自稱新派이니 舊派이니 하는 그 團軆는 하나도 可否를 말할 거리도 업거니와 其外에 社會의 各團軆에서 今年과 가티 演劇을 上演한 일은 아마 近者에 처음되는 일이다. 이러한 演劇熱?이 참으로 그네들의 劇藝術의 愛護에서 나온 것이냐 하면 決코 그러한 것이 아니다. 그 大部分은 音樂會라는 美名下에서 入場料를 모아들여다가 一般民衆에게 音樂이라는 權威를 일흔 뒤로 收入上不得己하여 新看板의 生手劇團(그네들은 素人劇이라고 한다)이라는 것을 돌라 박은대서 지나지 아니한다. 이러케 演劇이라는 名稱도 靑年團軆의 그네들의 손에서 一般社會에 對하야 信望을 일허버려간다. 最近에 藝術協會劇壇이 생기어 겨우 未成이나 劇이라는 名稱을 엇게된 것은 우리 劇壇의 큰 收穫이라고 할 만한 것이다. 그러나 그네들도 그것만으로 滿足한다던지 족음식이라도 進就가 업다고 하면 또한 여긔에서 落望치 안흘 수가 업다. 우리는 1年間의 우리 劇界에 對하여서도 섭섭한 생각이 업지 아니하다. 그 다음 우리 文壇을 보자. 勿論 現今과 가튼 우리의 形便으로 出版의 自由가 업고 言論의 解放이 업는 것도 큰 障碍가 아니라고 할 수 업지마는 넘우도 寂寥한 것이 만타. 겨우 우리 開闢雜誌를 通하야 조흐나 그러나 다달이 創作이 보일 뿐이요 그 以外에는 감을에 콩낫가티 或時 世上에 發表되는 것이 업지는 아니하나 어떠한 雜誌에는 題目만 小說이지 무엇이 小說인지 大說인지 하는 것도 分揀하지 못하는 사람의 名稱만 短篇이라는 것을 볼 수도 잇는 것은 적지 아니한 遺憾이라고 아니할 수가 업다. 勿論 우리의 目今 形便이 뾰죽한 붓끗으로 벌어가지고 넘적한 숫갈(匙)로 퍼넛는 것을 當치 못하는 것은 事實이라 작으나 크나 實生活을 저버리고 專心專力으로 創作에 힘을 쓰지 못할 것은 모르는 것이 아니나 아모리 그러타 하더래도 넘우나 沒廉恥의 짓이 업지 아니하다. 그러면 우리 文壇이 이 1年間에 産出한 그 量이나 品位上으로 決코 皆無라고 하지는 못할지나 우리의 지내는 바와 우리의 形便을 叅酌하여도 넘우나 바라는 바에 어그러짐이 만치 아니하다고 아니할 수가 업다.
이제 過去 1年間으로 두고 特히 藝術方面으로 보아 美術, 音樂, 演劇, 文學便으로 생각하면 그 量이나 質의 多少는 말할 것도 업고 하나도 安定된 執着力이 업는 것은 記者도 뜻잇는 讀者諸位와 한가지 설어하는＜113＞ 바요 痛嘆히 여기는 것이다. 筆者가 매양 이러한 말을 할 때에 箇中에도 筆者에게 好感을 가지지 못한 사람들은 高慢이니 偏狹이니 하지마는 決코 自我辨明이 아니라 생각하여 보면 高慢하여 그러한 것이 아이요 偏狹하여 그러한 것이 아니다. 筆者가 넘우 一般에게 바라는 것이 만흔 까닭에 그러한 것이고 하루라도 急히 남과 가티 文明의 길로 나아가자는 조비 비는 마음이 만흔 까닭이다. 이러한 바라는 마음과 조비 비는 性情의 偏狹한 것으로만 그러타고 할 것이 아니라. 그윽히 생각하면 筆者도 한가지 여러분과 가티 朝鮮사람인 까닭에 그러한 것이요 이 땅에서 컷고 이 땅에서 굵엇스며 살아도 이 땅에서 살 사람이고 죽어도 이 땅에서 죽을 사람이기 때문이다. 不幸히 이 때가 다른 때와 달라 世界의 文明이 이웃집가티 넘나들고 남을 구경하기가 洞里사람 맛나기보다 쉬운 까닭에 남을 보고 우리를 보면 自然이 부러운 생각도 나고 따라서 心事가 나며 憤心이 생기고 애닯은 생각이 나기 때문에 붓을 들면 스스로 多少의 過激한 言辭가 업지 아니하나 우리 讀者가 이것을 容認치 못한다하면 하는 수 업시 이 어리석고 鈍하고 미련한 붓이나마 집어더지고 말수 밧게업는 것이다. 萬一에 筆者가 朝鮮사람이 아니엇섯든들 여러분이 하는 일에 무엇으로 주저넘게 重言復言하며 萬一筆者가 이 땅에서 자라지 아니하고 이 땅에서 굵지 아니하며 이 땅에서 죽을 사람이 아니면 무슨 理由로 여러분의 하는 일에 曰可曰否를 일커르겟는가? 이 畢竟은 서로 사랑하는 마음으로부터 나옴이요 서로 도아서 살아가랴고 하는 뜻에 지나지 아니하는 것이다. 萬一에 여러분이 朝鮮사람이 아니고 筆者도 朝鮮사람이 아니면 이러한 말을 할 리가 잇스며 여러분과 내가 朝鮮民族과 何等의 關連이 업다고 하면 어찌 이러한 말을 하겟는가? 그러나저러나 그것은 關係할 것이 업다. 一面으로 筆者를 실혀하고 辱하는 사람이 잇다하면 그 反面으로 筆者를 조하하고 稱讚하는 사람이 잇슬 것이다. 辱을 하거나 稱讚을 하거나 그것 亦相關할 것이 업다. 筆者 - 반듯이 一部사람들에게 辱먹기를 조하하지 아니하는 同時에 딸아서 稱讚듯기를 조하하지도 아니한다. 오즉 筆者의 부르짓는 말이 그 赤心에서 나온 것인가?
