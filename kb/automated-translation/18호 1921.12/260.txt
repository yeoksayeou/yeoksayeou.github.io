발행일	1921년 12월 01일
기사제목	때 모르는 가마귀 (Crows Ignorant of the Times)
필자	辛槿圃
기사형태	시 (Poem)

때 모르는 가마귀
						辛槿圃

With much Hope and deep feeling
Settling down in the Fairyland I had longed for all my life
Meaninglessly thinking of the Vague Past and the Uncertain Future
I realized a sorrowful sound sadly echoing
In the Fluctuation of the Atmosphere, shaken so abruptly

Quietly, quietly
Following the sound coming from afar
I advanced one step, two steps. And so I saw.
It was the Scene where black crows, having placed down a dead mouse,
Were crying out and clamoring, wanting to eat more
And afraid of having it stolen by others.
I picked up a large stone to strike them
And drew closer and closer.
But they, engrossed in eating,
Still cried out, ignorant of the urgent time.

"Having received bones (뻬(骨)) from Emptiness (虛) and obtained flesh (살(肉)) from Nothingness (無)
To advance as Animals victorious through Spiritual Life (靈生活)
The Skill (才操) learned from the first day was the Art of Chewing (咀嚼術)
And the work done day and night was thievery.
Having caught and eliminated the intolerable mouse pup,
The great Merit (功) of the crows is to be Praised (稱揚).

However, forgetting the work obtained
By Cooperating (協助) with the same strength in one place,
These wicked crows only try to fill their own bellies.
It is Justifiable (當然하다) that they be hit by the fearsome stone."

Only when the forcefully thrown stone almost reached them
Did they finally fly and scatter
-Leaving the dead mouse there-
And still crying, they wander around the Vicinity (近傍).
Truly, pitiful! The crying crows!!
(October 25th, at Anjŏngsa Temple (安定寺))<137>

**Summary**

The poem describes the speaker observing a group of crows fighting selfishly over a dead mouse, oblivious to the danger of a stone the speaker intends to throw. The speaker reflects on the crows' potential ("eliminating the harmful mouse") contrasted with their current state of greedy infighting, lamenting their shortsightedness and lack of cooperation. They only scatter when the danger is imminent, leaving the prize behind but continuing their lament nearby. From a historical perspective, written in 1921 during Japanese colonial rule, the poem is likely an allegory criticizing the internal conflicts and self-interest within Korean society or among certain groups, hindering collective progress or resistance against external threats (represented by the speaker and the stone). Historians might find the poem's critique of infighting, the contrast between "spiritual life" and base survival instincts ("art of chewing," "thievery"), and the overall sense of disillusionment and pity valuable for understanding Korean intellectual sentiments during this period.

필자	辛槿圃

때 모르는 가마귀
						辛槿圃
만흔 希望과 깁흔 느낌으로
平生에 憧憬하던 仙境에 주저안저
漠然한 過去와 渺然한 將來를 뜻업시 생각다가
急激히 흔들리는 大氣의 波動에
처량한 소리가 哀然히 돌림을 깨달앗다

나는 가만가만히
멀리서 울려오는 소리에 딸아
한거름 두거름 나아갓다 그래서 보앗다
그는 검은 가마귀들이 죽은 쥐를 노코
서로 만히 먹으랴고 또한 남에게 빼앗길가봐
울면서 부르짓는 現場이엇다.
나는 큰 돌을 들어 그를 치랴고
더욱더욱 距離를 가깝게 하얏다.
그러나 그들은 먹을데 精神이 나서
如前히 울면서 때 急함을 모른다

「虛에서 뻬(骨)를 밧고 無에서 살(肉)을 어더
靈生活로 勝利하는 動物로 나가지고
첫날부터 배운 才操 咀嚼術이오
밤낫으로 하는 일이 도적질이던
두지 못할 쥐새끼를 잡아 업시 한
가마귀의 만흔 功을 稱揚할 바다

그러나 한곳에서 가튼 힘으로
協助하야 어든 일은 이저버리고
다만 내 배만 불리랴는 고약한 가마귀
무서운 돌망이에 마즘도 當然하다」

힘세게 던진 돌이 거의 이른 때야
그들은 비롯오 날아흐텃다
-죽은 쥐는 그곳에 두고-
그리고 如前히 울면서 近傍에 돌아뜬다.
참, 딱하다! 우는 가마귀!!
(10月25日 安定寺에서)＜137＞
