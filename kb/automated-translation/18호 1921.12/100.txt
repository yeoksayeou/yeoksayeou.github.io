Article Title: 李白沙의 詼謔 (Yi Paeksa's Wit)
Author: None stated (Sourced from 奇齋雜記)
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

Yi Paeksa (Yi Hang-bok)'s wit (詼謔) is famously well-regarded by general consensus (一般의 定評), as is self-evident (自有하거니와). One day, a certain person remarked, "The East-West conflict (東西의 戰) [the factional strife between the Tongin (東人) and Sŏin (西人) parties (黨爭)] finally brought about (致하얏다) the Japanese invasions (倭寇)." Paeksa immediately retorted (應口輒對하되), "If that is so (그러탓). If the Easterners and Westerners fought so well (善戰하얏스면), why did the Royal Court (朝廷) not use those fellows (그 輩) to defend (禦하지 아니 하얏는가) against the enemy (賊)?" (From Kijae Chapki (奇齋雜記))<83>

**Summary**

This short piece recounts a witty anecdote attributed to the Joseon Dynasty official Yi Paeksa (Yi Hang-bok). When someone blamed the factional strife between the Easterners (Tongin) and Westerners (Sŏin) for causing the Japanese invasions (Imjin War), Yi Paeksa cleverly turned the statement around. He questioned why, if these factions were so adept at "fighting" each other, the Royal Court didn't employ their supposed fighting skills to repel the actual Japanese invaders. The article highlights Yi Paeksa's renowned wit and subtly touches upon the theme of internal division weakening the nation. For historians, this piece shows the continued relevance of Joseon-era anecdotes and historical figures in early colonial Korean periodicals, reflecting ongoing discourse about historical causes of national weakness (like factionalism) even under Japanese rule.
