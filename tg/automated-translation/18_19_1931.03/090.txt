기사형태	시 (Poem)

Yŏnggwang School Song

I.
Backed by the great mountain, embracing the sea,
That house rising beneath the dark green forest,
Is where beautiful, lovely, and strong children
Are nurtured and raised, our Yŏnggwang School.

II.
The profound Truth (眞理), distant beyond the sky,
And all the treasures hidden within the earth,
Are found and dug out from this mountain spring of Wisdom (智慧).
It springs forth and springs forth endlessly.

III.
Growing children, be persistent.
Think high thoughts and have firm wills.
Though the paths we take may differ,
What we look up to is all the same heaven.

IV.
Wherever my advancing steps may reach,
May the light received here shine far and wide.
The song sung with voices in harmony,
Flows on and on, to the ends of the sky.<39>

**Summary**

This is the school song for Yŏnggwang School, likely located in a place with a mountain behind it and the sea in front. The lyrics celebrate the school as a place for nurturing children, pursuing deep truth and wisdom, and instilling high aspirations and strong will in its students. It encourages students to spread the "light" (knowledge, values) they received at the school wherever they go. From a historical perspective, the song reflects the importance placed on education and the fostering of Korean identity and aspirations during the colonial period. The emphasis on "truth," "wisdom," and looking towards "heaven" (potentially hinting at Christian influence common in early modern Korean education) suggests a desire for enlightenment and upliftment despite the constraints of colonial rule. Historians might find the blend of local pride, educational ambition, and possibly subtle religious or philosophical undertones significant for understanding cultural life in 1930s Korea.

우러러 바라기는 다 같이 하늘.

四. 내치는 나의 발길 닷는 곳마다
멀리멀리 빛외라 예서 받은 빛.
소리를 가즈런히 부르는 노래
흘르고 또 흘러서 하늘 끝까지.＜39＞
