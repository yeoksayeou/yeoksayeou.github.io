A Cooled Heart
						Chŏn Pong-je

My heart, which drank alcohol by setting it alight!
My heart, which struck a match and threw it onto a gunpowder sack!
And my heart, which sought to divert the river's course,
And sought to create wind on the mountain!
-But-
On this day when my artery, which circulated like an arrow,
Has cooled,
Chewing on dry blades of grass beneath a crumbling, collapsed castle wall,
Reciting a cooled song,
This heart is bitter, ah, painful!

Stopping the faintly sung song, I tilt my ear to days gone by,
Searching through this heart, like cooled, black ash!
Closing my eyes, I listen silently!
Alas! This heart, where even the flame of anguish has cooled, looks on, sees clearly—already
The smoke that has risen for many days, from the gunpowder spot, from the alcohol trace,
Billowing blue *in the air, cooled black smoke (黑煙) rises.
Oh, my heart hiding in the blue sky—
Footprints of silence! <53>

**Summary**

The poem contrasts the speaker's past fiery passion and ambition—likened to burning alcohol, exploding gunpowder, and reshaping nature—with their present state of cold disillusionment and apathy. The once vibrant "heart" is now like cooled ash, capable only of reciting a "cooled song" amidst ruins. This shift likely reflects the stifled atmosphere and loss of hope experienced by many Korean intellectuals and artists under Japanese colonial rule, where youthful energy and nationalistic aspirations were suppressed. Historians might find the poem's vivid imagery of extinguished fire and rising black smoke a poignant expression of the psychological impact of colonialism on individual spirit and ambition during that era.

이 마음은 쓰리고나 아프고나!

희밋히 불든 노래를 멈추고 귀를 기울려 옛때에

눈을 감고 묵묵히 듣나니!
오호라! 침통의 불길도 식어진 이 마음은 바라보나니 분명히 보나니 벌서

무룩무룩 푸른 *공에 식어진 黑煙이 오르나니
창공에 숨는 오 - 내 마음이여
無言의 발자취어! ＜53＞
