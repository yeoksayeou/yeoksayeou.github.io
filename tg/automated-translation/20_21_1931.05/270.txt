Wilderness Youth Corps, A Play
						Ch'ae Man-sik
Characters
Pokman   14 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Sangsu   14 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Obok   16 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Kwangsik   13 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Tolswoe   13 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Muswoe   10 years old, Wilderness Youth Corps

Wakdori           Fruit seller
Kim Sŏbang  Servant at Kwangsik's uncle's house

Kkweswoe    Servant at Kwangsik's uncle's house

Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka
Time   Modern Day, Summer, Moonlit Night
Place   Vicinity of a Rural Market
(Among the Wilderness Youth Corps, only Kwangsik is dressed like the son of a wealthy family; the rest are barefoot and their clothes are shabby.)

First Scene
A main street. Straight roads are visible left and right, and facing forward, a shop with its plank door closed is visible. As the curtain opens, the stage is briefly empty, then Sangsu enters from the left, carrying something in a sack. Having just eaten dinner, he smacks his lips, faces the audience, and looks around.
Sangsu. (Putting the sack down beside him) Not a single one has come yet.
Pokman. (Enters from the right)
Sangsu. (Sees Pokman first and whistles sharply)
Pokman. (Whistles back and approaches Sangsu) Haven't the others come?
Sang.   Looks like they haven't come yet.
Pok.   Did you bring it?
Sang.   Yeah.
Pok.   (Looking down at the sack beside Sangsu) So this is it. What is it?
Sang.   Barley.
Pok.   Barley? Did you pester your mother?
Sang.   Pfft. It’s what I gathered gleaning ears of grain.
Pok.   Oh? Really.
Obok. (Enters from the right. He sees his two friends and tries to whistle, but it doesn't come out well, so he just makes a 'hoo hoo' sound.)
Pok. Sang. (Whistle simultaneously)
Pok.   (A bit loudly) Did you bring it?
Obok.   (Being hard of hearing, looks around blankly) Huh?
Pok.   (Louder) Did you bring it?
Obok.   (Finally understanding, smiles foolishly) Yeah, did I bring it? (Looks around blankly again) Wait, what?
Pok. Sang. (Laugh, exasperated) <78>
Obok.   (Suddenly getting angry) Why are you laughing? Just because I'm a little deaf?
Pok.   (Aside) Look at that frustrating guy. I just shouted my lungs out talking to him, and he still didn't get it and was looking elsewhere! (Grabs Obok's ear, puts his mouth to it, and shouts loudly) Yongnam's mother—
Obok.   (Relaxing, smiles brightly) Yeah yeah, Yongnam's mother.
Pok.   —is having a baby—
Obok.   Huh? (Looks unexpectedly happy) A baby? A baby? (Makes gestures, pretending to soothe a baby)
Pok.   Yes, she's having a baby—
Obok.   Yeah yeah, baby, baby.
Pok.   (Pulls the ear he's holding tightly) Just be quiet for a second. (Loudly again) But—she has nothing to eat—
Obok.   (Startled) Huh? She ate? She’s deaf like me?
Pok.   (Annoyed, lets go of the ear and pushes him hard)
Obok.   (Touching the sore spot, stands there dumbfounded) Why'd you hit me? For no reason!
Sang.   (Frustrated, grabs Obok's ear, puts his mouth to it, and shouts very loudly) Nothing to eat—food—
Obok.   (Finally understanding, nods) Yeah—nothing to eat? Doesn't she eat rice?
Sang.   (Pinches him hard) Aaargh, you nuisance!
Obok.   (Stands blankly) What's wrong? Hitting me for no reason!
Pok.   (Puts his mouth to Obok's ear again) She has no rice—no barley, no vegetables either—understand?
Obok.   Yeah—no rice. (Thinks for a moment, then suddenly) I have some leftovers from dinner, should I bring them? (Is about to run off immediately)
Pok.   (Grabs Obok's ear tightly and pulls) Just wait a minute.
Obok.   Ouch, ouch.
Pok.   So, we—rice, barley, cooked rice is fine too, or vegetables, or money, anything—
Obok.   Yeah, yeah.
Pok.   Decided to gather a little bit each and give it to her—
Obok.   Yeah yeah.
Pok.   So you should bring something too, anything— (Lets go of his ear.)
Obok.   (Finally understanding everything) Yeah—, sure, we should do that. (Aside) Wait a minute. (Thinks) That's right, that's right. (Looks back and forth between Pokman and Sangsu) We have lots of barley stalks at home. Is it okay if I bring those?
Pok. Sang.  (Nodding) Good, good.
Obok.   (Walking towards the right) Then I'll bring them right away. (Exits running)
Kwangsik. (Enters from the right. Whistles)
Pok. Sang.  (Whistle)
Pok.   Did you bring it?
Kwang.   (Takes out a wallet from his vest pocket, pulls out a 1-won bill, and gives it to Pokman)
Pok.   (Takes it without a word)
Sang.   (Grumbling) Kwangsik only brought one won?
Kwang.   (Embarrassed, hesitates) Well—that's after paying 70 jeon for the monthly school fee, the rest was for buying paper...
Pok.   (Patting Kwangsik's back reassuringly) Yeah yeah, it's okay. This is too much already. (Turns to Sangsu) Why are you like that! If someone's rich, it's his father who's rich, is he rich himself?
Sang. Kwang.  (Relax, look at each other, smile, and whistle)
Pok.   What's up with Muswoe and Tolswoe?
Tolswoe. (Enters from the right holding about ten stalks of seaweed. Whistles)
All. (Whistle)
Pok.   (Takes the seaweed Tolswoe gives him and looks at it) Oh, it's seaweed?
Tol.   Yeah.
Pok.   Did you buy it? <79>
Tol.   No, Mother gave it to me.
Sang.   Why isn't your younger brother coming?
Tolswoe. He's eating right now.
Sang.   He'll come soon then.
Tol.   Yeah.
Sang.   What's Muswoe bringing?
Tol.   Money.
Obok.   (Enters from the right, carrying a large bundle of barley stalks tied together on his shoulder, grunting)
All. (Whistle)
Obok.   (Puts it down in front of everyone) Is this okay?
All. Good, good.
Pok.   (Clears his throat and speaks commandingly to everyone) Now, we're going to deliver these. What we've gathered is one won in money, and... (unties a 20-jeon silver coin tied to his belt and holds it up) twenty jeon, making it one won and twenty jeon. Barley is... (looks at Sangsu) how many *toe*?
Sang.   Five *toe*.
Pok.   Five *toe* of barley, six stalks of seaweed, one bundle of barley stalks. The ones to take it are Obok and Kwangsik.
All. (Whistle)
Kwang.   (Takes the barley sack, money, and seaweed and steps forward.)
Pok.   (Puts his mouth to Obok's ear) You and Kwangsik take all this to Yongnam's house and come back.
Obok.   Yeah. (Picks up the barley stalks and stands up) This is me?
Pok.   (Nodding) Yeah. (Looks at Kwangsik) Tell Yongnam it's okay if he doesn't come for about ten more days. And as for what to say when you give them this, you figure it out. Also, tell them we'll come visit after the *samch'ilil* (三七日 - 21st day after birth).
Kwang. Obok.   (Whistle. Exit right)
All. (Whistle. Brief silence)
Muswoe. (Enters from the right, crying loudly) That damn brat, waaah.
All. (Whistle, startled, gather around Muswoe)
Pok.   Why are you crying, huh?
Mus.   (Stops crying abruptly) That—that darn—
Pok.   (Wiping his tears) Go on.
Mus.   That—that Wakdori brat.
Pok.   And then?
Mus.   I was bringing five jeon.
Pok.   Yeah.
Mus.   Well—I asked him to change it into copper coins, but he hit me saying I wasn't buying apricots and snatched the money too.
All. (Anger rises on their faces)
Pok.   So, it was five jeon, and you couldn't get it back?
Mus.   Yeah. Well—Mother said to give three jeon to Yongnam's family and buy lye with the remaining two jeon to do laundry.
Tol.   (Steps forward abruptly towards Muswoe) You idiot, you useless thing! (Glares)
Pok.   (Restraining Tolswoe) Stop, stop, hold on. (Looks around at everyone) So, what should we do?
Sang.   We have to catch him and spank his bottom, according to our rules.
Pok.   But we need to be able to catch him first, right?
Sang.   Why can't we catch him?
Pok.   How?
Kwang. Obok. (Enter from the right, whistling)
All. (Whistle)
Pok.   Quick, quick.
Kwang. Obok.  (Gather together) What is it?
Pok.   (Puts his mouth to Obok's ear and shouts) That Wakdori brat—
Obok.   Huh? Wakdori? That guy's a real lowlife (깍정이). They say he makes thirty-eight jeon a day from his business, but he just buys alcohol and cigarettes, then gets drunk and rowdy, acting like a complete bum. He hits kids randomly <80> too.
Pok.   Right, but listen—
Obok.   And that guy, just because he thinks he's strong, he picks on country folk. He's a real lowlife. He sucks up to kids with money.
Pok.   Okay, just be quiet and listen to me—
Obok.   Yeah yeah.
Pok.   Our Muswoe—was bringing five jeon—
Obok.   Huh? Fifty won? (Eyes go wide, looks at Muswoe) That much? Where? Let me see it. Where did you get so much money? (Looks at Pokman) Fifty won must be like ten thousand *nyang*, right? It would probably take me one trip to carry it all, no, maybe two trips.
All. (Laugh)
Pok.   (Annoyed, grabs Obok's ear tightly) Not fifty won—
Obok.   Huh? Then more?
Pok.   Five jeon! Five jeon.
Obok.   Oh, five jeon. (Makes a small circle with his fingers) This much? Five jeon, one *p'un*?
Pok.   Right. But that Wakdori brat snatched it—
Obok.   Huh? That bastard? (Gets angry, rolls up his sleeves) That bastard, just, just, I'll beat that bastard to death.
Pok.   We can't say kill him, but we'll catch him and punish him according to our rules.
Obok.   Yeah yeah (Smiling brightly) Tease him, beat him up, oh boy, this is great. Hehe, that guy, hehe, the executioner is me, me!
Pok.   (Lets go of Obok's ear and looks at Sangsu) So, how do we catch him?
Sang.   (Spreads his arms, gathers everyone together, arms around each other's shoulders in a circle, whispering. Joy gradually appears on everyone's faces)
Sang.   (Finishes explaining) Got it?
All. Good. Got it. (Whistle)
Obok.   (Looking blankly) I don't know what's going on.
Pok.   (To Obok) Just be quiet and do as you're told. (To everyone) Okay—let's start now. (Looks at Tolswoe) Don't mess up, do it well. Got it?
Tol.   (Whistles. Exits right)
All. (Whistle. Exit left) (Stage revolves)

Second Scene
A path runs left and right, and a narrow alley is visible center front. Fences line the left and right of the alley. To the right of the alley entrance, Obok stands, then Pokman, then Sangsu holding a rope and stick, Kwangsik, and Muswoe, lined up in that order, tense and listening intently. Opposite them is a long bench. After about a minute, loud footsteps are heard, and Tolswoe, holding an apricot, runs in from the right and enters the alley. The group becomes silently more tense. Immediately after, Wakdori, panting heavily, follows Tolswoe, running into the alley. With a sharp whistle, Obok darts out first, sticks out his leg, tripping Wakdori. The others rush in, tie up Wakdori (whom Obok is sitting on), and make him sit up. Wakdori, caught in the surprise attack, finally collects himself and looks around at the group. The group drags Wakdori onto the main path, makes him sit facing left, and lines up facing him, with Pokman in the center.
Pok.   (Very sternly) Hey you, you're Wakdori, right?
Wak.   (As if dumbfounded) Hah, what kind of dog-like situation is this? (Getting angry) You rotten lowlife (깍정이) brats, let me go right now!
Pok.   (Nonchalantly) Hmph, you haven't learned your lesson yet. Then we'll give you a taste. Just wait. (Looks left and right, commandingly) Grab that punk and flip him over!
(The group immediately rushes over, brings the bench, and flips Wakdori over onto it, facing the audience. Wakdori resists, struggles, and curses endlessly. The group wordlessly moves deftly, seating Wakdori on the bench, tying his hands and feet tightly, and exposing his buttocks. Finishing the task, they step back and whistle)
Pok.   (Slowly) Hey punk, still don't get it?
Wak.   Get what? Get your mother?
Pok.   (Looks at Obok, gestures with his hand) Hit that punk.
Obok.   (Happened to be standing there holding the stick, smiles broadly and rushes forward)
Pok.   (Authoritatively) Hit him! Ten times.
Obok.   (Looks at Sangsu as if asking how many) <81>
Sang.   (Holds up ten fingers then makes a fist)
(Obok begins the caning. One, two, three... As he's hit, Wakdori gradually loses his nerve, crying "Ouch, ouch," and eventually weeps. Obok finishes all ten hits and steps back. The group is secretly pleased.)
Pok.   Hey punk, now do you understand?
Wak.   (In a crying voice) I understand.
Pok.   You understand? Hey punk, is your tongue cut in half?
Wak.   Yes, I just... understand, sir.
Pok.   Good. That's how our law works. Now it's time for the questioning. Listen carefully.
Wak.   Yes, yes.
Pok.   You punk, you take what you earn from your business, and even though you're young, you spend it daily on alcohol and tobacco. Even if we say that's your own business, after drinking, you get rowdy, hit young children, and pick on easygoing country folk. Was that the right thing to do?
Wak.   I was just wrong, sir.
Pok.   And then tonight, you hit our Muswoe for no reason and took his money. So, you're basically a thief... not even a big thief, but a dirty thief.
Wak.   Yes, I was just wrong. I'll add one jeon to the five jeon and give you six jeon.
Pok.   Enough with the excuses. The beating you got earlier was for being disrespectful to us. Now, the real punishment remains. (Looks towards Obok) Hit him again. Just ten times.
(Obok, still smiling, steps forward. Sangsu signals ten to Obok. The beating starts. Wakdori cries loudly. After ten hits, Obok steps back.)
Pok.   Now you understand everything, right?
Wak.   Yes, I understand everything, sir.
Pok.   You won't act like that again?
Wak.   Yes, I won't do it again.
Pok.   You'll go now and return the five jeon you took earlier?
Wak.   Yes, I'll add one jeon and give six jeon.
Pok.   No, just give five jeon.
Wak.   Then just five jeon.
Pok.   (Looks around at the group) Untie him.
(The group rushes over and quickly unties him. Wakdori stands up and adjusts his waistband.)
Mus.   (Steps in front of Wakdori, holding out his hand) Give me my money.
Wak.   (Stands blankly) Let's go, I'll give it to you.
Pok.   Muswoe, follow him, get the five jeon, and then go home and sleep.
Mus.   What if he doesn't give it?
Pok.   That won't happen, but if he doesn't, come and tell us.
Mus.   Okay.
Pok.   Tolswoe, what happened to that one apricot you picked up earlier?
Tol.   (Shows the apricot in his hand) It's here.
Pok.   Give it to Wakdori.
Tol.   (Goes to Wakdori) Here!
Wakdori takes the apricot and exits right with Muswoe. Brief silence. When Wakdori is likely far away, the group claps, laughs loudly, and whistles.
Pok.   (Signals the group to quiet down) Okay—let's have some fun.
All. Sure, let's do it.
Pok.   Ready—!
The group gathers around Sangsu. Sangsu takes a handkerchief from his waist and holds it. Kwangsik takes out a harmonica, puts it to his mouth, ready to play.
Pok.   (Looks around the group, then) Okay—Start!
The group sings the "Taeho March" (大湖行進曲) to Kwangsik's harmonica, clapping their hands. Sangsu does a hopping dance (꼽박). They play merrily for 3-4 minutes. Afterwards, applause and whistling.
Sang.   Hey, we've got a lucky break tonight. <82>
All. (Look at Sangsu)
Pok.   A lucky break?
Sang.   Food luck.
Pok.   Food luck?
Tol.   Food sounds good. I didn't even eat dinner.
Pok.   You didn't eat?
Tol.   Only Muswoe ate. Mother and I didn't eat.
Sang.   I ate a spoonful of leftover barley, so I'm hungry, but I'm just farting a lot.
Kwang.   (Just looks at everyone's faces)
Pok.   What exactly is this food luck?
Sang.   It's nothing else but, apparently, the rich folks are gathering on the back mountain today for drinks. They've called geishas, clowns, and everything. Trays of alcohol are going back and forth. When I was coming here, I saw Kwangsik's uncle's house in a total uproar, preparing the dinner tray to send up.
Pok.   (Unimpressed) What, you think they'll let us eat too?
Sang.   (Hesitantly) We'll snatch it.
Pok.   How?
Sang.   (Looking at Kwangsik) It absolutely requires Kwangsik's help.
Pok & others. (All look at Kwangsik)
Kwang.   I'll do whatever you tell me to.
Sang.   Perfect.
Pok.   So how exactly do we do it?
Sang.   Hold on. (To Kwangsik) Are they definitely preparing it at your uncle's house?
Kwang.   Yeah.
Sang.   The dinner tray hasn't gone up yet, right?
Kwang.   It's noodles. It hasn't gone yet.
Sang.   Kim Sŏbang or someone like that will probably take it?
Kwang.   Yeah.
Sang.   Then it's settled. (Gathers everyone and whispers)
All. Wow, that's perfect. (Whistle)
Pok.   But Kwangsik might feel a bit left out.
Kwang.   It's okay. I ate a lot at home. (Although he says this, a slight look of disappointment shows)
Pok.   No, I don't mean because you can't eat, but because you can't play together, even for a little while.
Kwang.   It's okay, it's okay. (Forces himself to appear cheerful)
Pok.   (Turns to the group) Alright then, to the base of the back mountain!
All. (Whistle. Exit left) (Stage revolves)

Third Scene
At the foot of the mountain, a narrow path runs left and right. The front shows a steep cut in the hillside. As the curtain opens, Kim Sŏbang enters from the left, carrying a large meal tray on his head. Behind him, Kkweswoe appears, carrying a large kettle. Just as the two reach the center of the stage, Kwangsik's urgent cries of "Kim Sŏbang! Kim Sŏbang!" mixed with sobbing are heard from afar.
Kim Sŏbang. (Stops, startled, looks awkwardly back at Kkweswoe) Isn't that the young master from the main house?
Kkweswoe. (Listening intently) Yes, yes. Maybe he got hurt playing on the mountain—
Kwang.   (Voice only, from afar) Kim Sŏbang! Kim Sŏbang! Ouch, ouch!
Kim Sŏbang. Oh no, this is serious. I have to go see. I'll put the tray down. You wait here.
The two work together to set the tray down, and Kim Sŏbang exits quickly to the left. A moment later, the Wilderness Youth Corps, led by Pokman, enters from the right. Seeing Kkweswoe unexpectedly there, they stop, disappointed.
Pok.   (Goes up to Kkweswoe) Oh—hey, Kkweswoe, thanks for your hard work. You really struggled to bring this all the way here. (Turns to the group) Come on, everyone—we have to eat it, if only out of consideration for Kkweswoe who brought it.
Kkwe.   (Eyes widening) What are you kids doing? (Puts down the kettle he's holding and stands protectively in front of the tray)
Pok.   What do you mean, 'what are we doing'? <83>
Kkwe.   Just try touching the tray. I'll scream my head off.
Pok.   Hmph, go ahead and scream if you want. Scream your heart out. But do you think you'll only meet us today and never again? You know who we are, right?
Kkwe.   (Losing his nerve) But this is for the adults to eat.
Pok.   Hey Kkweswoe, listen to me. Adults or whatever (어룬인지 망덕인지), I don't know, but those people aren't eating this because they're hungry. They pretend to eat just for show, fiddling around with their chopsticks before putting it down, and then they still burp. Later, when it comes down, it'll be all soggy for the dogs or pigs to eat. But we're eating because we're really hungry. Food is nectar when you're hungry. Food is meant for hungry people to fill their stomachs, why the hell (옘병헌다고) would full people be picking at it (께죽께죽 허고)? Those people can get more food later if they get really hungry. (Pause) So, what's it going to be? Are you going to shout to stop us from eating and face the consequences from us tomorrow? Or—
Kkwe.   I won't shout.
Pok.   Of course! Our Kkweswoe, who is he? Aren't you and I the same? Okay—then Kkweswoe, here's a good idea. You should follow Kim Sŏbang right now. If they ask why you came, say you were worried because the young master from the main house was shouting like that—
Kkwe.   (Startled) How do you know the young master shouted?—
Pok.   Hmph, it's all part of the plan.
Kkwe.   (Dumbfounded) Oh my.
Pok.   See? You can't win against us, right? (Pause) So you should hurry up and go too. If you stayed here and stopped us from eating, you'd get punished severely (팟다발같이).
Kkwe.   (Tempted) Well, that's true.
Pok.   See? Your name is Kkweswoe (꾀쇠 - Clever Iron), but how come you lack cleverness (꾀)? Just go and pretend you don't know anything. If you unnecessarily reveal anything, you'll get punished by your master's house, and next by us—
Kkwe.   (Backing away hesitantly) Then I'll go.
Pok.   Okay, hurry up and go. (Urgently) Keep your mouth shut tight.
Kkwe.   Yeah. (Exits right)
Pok.   (Playfully) God, Earth, Sun, Moon—thank you! Okay, let's eat!
The group whistles and rushes forward, each grabbing food and eating. When they seem almost finished, the sound of a very drunk person singing with slurred words is heard from afar. The sound gradually gets closer to the stage. The group stops eating and listens intently.
Pok.   That sounds like Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka.
Sang.   That's right, that's right.
Tol.   He's dead drunk (곤냐꾸가 되엇어).
Obok.   (Eating greedily) What are you doing instead of eating quickly?
Pok.   Is he coming this way?
Sang.   Yeah.
Pok.   Isn't he the guy we wanted to tease?
Tol.   They say he's a police informant (순사 끄나풀이래요).
Pok.   Perfect.
Sang.   How?
Pok.   (Gathers the group and whispers quietly)
All. (Whistle) Perfect. (All exit right)
The stage is empty for about a minute. During this time, the drunkard's song gets progressively closer until Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka enters from the right. His clothes and gait show he's completely out of it. Behind him, Pokman and Sangsu follow stealthily. When Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka gets close to the tray, the two boys suddenly push him from behind and run off again. Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka falls, propping his hands on the tray.
Pak.   (Getting up with difficulty, in a slurred voice) Ho ho ho ho, fortune has struck me today. Ho ho ho ho. (Grabs various things with his hands and eats) Eat. Just eat. Oh, tastes good. Yum yum.
Kim Sŏbang. (Enters quickly from the left, followed by Kkweswoe. Seeing what Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka is doing, he is utterly dumbfounded) Oh dear, oh dear.
Kkweswoe stands behind Kim Sŏbang, smiling meaningfully. From behind the stage, the cheerful laughter and whistling of the Wilderness Youth Corps are heard as the curtain slowly falls.
(1928, 9, 5, at Imp'a (臨坡)) <84>

**Summary**

The play "Wilderness Youth Corps" by Ch'ae Man-sik depicts a group of poor rural boys who form their own 'corps' to enact justice and provide mutual aid. They collect food and money for the family of a newborn, confront and punish a local bully (Wakdori) who stole money from one of them, and cleverly intercept a lavish meal intended for wealthy elites, sharing it amongst themselves. The play concludes with them setting up another local troublemaker, the drunken Kkakchŏngi Pak Ka, to take the blame for the stolen food. From a colonial history perspective, the play highlights the stark poverty and social stratification in rural Korea, contrasting the struggles of the common folk with the indifference of the wealthy. The boys' 'Wilderness Youth Corps' represents a form of grassroots resistance and self-governance, creating their own rules and justice system in the absence of effective or fair authority, which could be interpreted as a subtle commentary on the colonial administration itself or the breakdown of traditional social structures.

第二場
좌우로 통한 길이 잇고 정면 중앙에 좁은 골목이 보인다. 골목 左右로는 울타리. 골목 우편에 五福, 그댐에 福萬, 새끼와 매를 들은 相洙, 光植, 무쇠의 순서로 늘어서서 긴장한 빛으로 연해 귀를 기울이고 잇다. 그 마즌 편에는 기다란 걸상이 놓여 잇다. 一分쯤 된 뒤에 발자국 소리가 요란히 나며 돌쇠가 살구 한 개를 손에 들고 右手에서 다름질을 처 골목안으로 들어선다. 一同은 無言중에 더욱 긴장된다. 뒤미처 왁돌이가 숨을 허덕거리며 돌쇠의 뒤를 따러 골목안으로 뛰어든다. 휙 하는 휘파람소리와 아울러 위선 五福이가 내다러서 다리를 내어 밀어 왁돌이를 너머 트리고 一同은 달러들어 五福이가 타고 앉은 왁돌이를 결박을 지어 일으켜 앉친다. 不意의 습격을 받은 왁돌이는 겨우 정신을 수습하여 가지고 일동을 들러본다. 一同은 왁돌이를 끌어다가 큰길에다 左向하여 앉처 놓고 그 앞에 福萬을 중심으로 왁돌이를 향하여 늘어선다.
