In the Year 1950

The question of how the world will change several decades from now holds much interest for us. A magazine published in New York called "Tomorrow's Business/Industry" (明日의 實業) carried 20 clear predictions agreed upon by the National Education Association (國民敎育協會). Introducing a few of them, they are as follows:

Material Aspects in 1950.
- Preventable infectious diseases will be eradicated.
- Telephone charges throughout the entire country will be unified and become cheap.
- Global air transport will become cheaper, allowing people to fly freely through the air.
- Working hours will be shortened, and there will be no one who does not work.

Social Aspects
- Anyone who needs it will be able to use hospitals.
- Free public libraries will enhance everyone's knowledge (智識).
- Crime will disappear through schools and educational training. Police, courts, prisons, etc., will become unnecessary.
- Everyone will have financial leeway and lead creative artistic lives.
- Ethical standards (倫理的 標準) will become elevated in step with the rise of commerce and industry.
-The Literary Digest- <77>

**Summary**

This 1931 article relays predictions for the year 1950, sourced from the American "National Education Association" via a New York publication and "The Literary Digest". It paints a utopian future characterized by technological advancement (eradication of disease, cheap unified telephone rates, affordable air travel), improved labor conditions (shorter hours, universal employment), and social progress (universal healthcare access, ubiquitous libraries enhancing knowledge, elimination of crime through education, financial security for all, and elevated ethical standards). From a historical perspective, the article is fascinating as it reflects the optimistic technological and social idealism prevalent in some Western circles during the interwar period, presented to a Korean audience under Japanese colonial rule. For historians, it starkly contrasts the hopeful predictions with the devastating reality of Korea in 1950, ravaged by the Korean War, highlighting the gulf between imported futuristic visions and colonial/post-colonial realities.

1950년에는
몇10년 후에, 세상이 어떠케 변할가 하는 문제는 우리에게 많은 흥미를 준다. 「明日의 實業」이라는 紐育에서 發行하는 잡지에는 國民敎育協會에서 意見이 일치된 20개조의 명확한 豫言을 실엇는데 그 중 몇 가지를 소개하면 이러하다.
1950년의 物質的 方面.
- 예방할 수 잇는 傳染病은 전멸하리라.
- 全國을 통하야 전화요금은 통일되고 싸게 되리라.
- 世界 空中輸送은 값이 싸저서 마음대로 공중을 날게 되리라.
- 勞働時間은 단축되고 누구나 勞働안하는 사람이 없게 되리라.
社會的 方面
- 필요한 사람은 누구나 병원을 사용할 수 잇게 되리라.
- 無料公衆圖書舘이 모든 사람의 智識을 향상시키게 되리라.
- 學校와 敎育的 訓練에 의하야 범죄란 없어지리라. 警察, 裁判所, 刑務所등은 소용없게 된다.
- 모든 사람이 다 財政에 여유를 갖게 되고 創造的 藝術生活을 하게 되리라.
- 倫理的 標準은 商工業의 新興과 步調를 같이 하야 고상하게 된다.
-The Literary Digest- ＜77＞
