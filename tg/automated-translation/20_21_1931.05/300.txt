Publication Date:	1931년 05월 01일
Article Title:	義妹, 乙男이에게 주는 (To my sworn sister, Ŭlman)
Author:	安必承
Article Type:	시 (Poem)

To my sworn sister, Ŭlman
						An P'ilsŭng

My deeply beloved younger sister,
When I think of how the chilly winds of morning and night
Make your kingly cheeks rough,
And how your red lips turn blue,
Ah, it's cold, it's cold!
Even lying in a warm room under a thick blanket,
This older brother shivers terribly cold.

My deeply beloved younger sister,
When I think of how I skip breakfast, open a cold lunch box (변또) only around noon,
And late in the evening too, trudge through the dark streets,
Returning home clutching a hungry stomach,
Ah, I'm hungry, I'm hungry!
Even now, holding my spoon and closing my eyes,
This older brother is terribly hungry.

My deeply beloved younger sister,
When I think of how you, so young, wring oil from your slender limbs (四肢),
Shed cold sweat like blood upon your brow,
And clash against the hellishly harsh waves of the world (世波),
Ah, it hurts, it hurts!
No matter how I gasp for breath or strike the ground with my fist,
This older brother’s chest aches fit to burst.

My deeply beloved younger sister,
Your face—pretty, clever (怜悧), kind-hearted, and upright—
And your young, pitiful (可憐) form,
Like the sacred image (映像) of a goddess (女神) or heroine (女傑) I revere (崇尙),
Ah, they cover this older brother’s heart, cover his heart!
Whether I lie groaning (呻吟) or wander (彷徨) the streets,
Your form (姿態) always covers this older brother’s heart.
(1931, 3, 3, at night) <90>

**Summary**

This poem by An P'ilsŭng is an emotional outpouring from an older brother figure ("옵바") expressing deep empathy and sorrow for the hardships faced by his younger "sworn sister" (or possibly sister-in-law), Ŭlman. He mirrors her suffering – feeling cold when thinking of her exposure, hungry when considering her potential hunger (or his own toil preventing him from providing better), and heartbroken imagining her struggles against the harsh realities of life ("hellishly harsh waves of the world"). Despite her suffering, he elevates her image to that of a revered goddess or heroine, whose memory sustains him. From a historical perspective, the poem offers insight into the expression of familial bonds and personal suffering during the colonial period. Historians might find the intense empathy, the depiction of hardship (hunger, toil, "harsh waves of the world"), and the blend of pity and admiration valuable for understanding individual emotional responses within the broader socio-economic context of 1930s Korea.

義妹, 乙男이에게 주는
						安必承
지극히 사랑하는 내 뉘동생아
아츰과 밤의 쌀쌀한 바람에
너의 림금같은 두뺨이 거칠어지고
너의 빨간 입술이 푸르러지는 것 생각을 하면
아아 치웁다 치웁다
두껍은 이불 따수은 방에 누어 잇어도
이 옵바는 진저리치게 치웁다

지극히 사랑하는 내 뉘동생아
내가 아츰을 굼고 午正때나 되어야 차디찬 변또를 풀으고
저녁에도 늦게야 어두운 거리를 터벅터벅
허기진 배를 안고 돌아오는 것 생각을 하면
아아 배고프다 배고프다
지금에 밥수깔을 들고 눈을 감어도
이 옵바는 치떨리게 배고프다

지극히 사랑하는 내 뉘동생아
어린 네가 네 간얄핀 四肢에 기름을 짜고
이마 우에 식은땀을 피같이 흘리며
地獄같이 險惡한 世波에 부다끼는 것 생각을 하면
아아 아프다 아프다
아무리 숨을 몰아쉬고 주먹으로 땅을 처도
이 옵바의 가즘은 미어지게 아프다

지극히 사랑하는 내 뉘동생아
어여뿌고 怜悧하고 마음이 착하며 곧은
너의 얼골과 어리고 可憐한 姿態가
나의 崇尙하는 女神과 혹은 女傑의 거룩한 映像과 같이
아아 이 옵바의 마음을 덮는다 마음을 덮는다
내 누어서 呻吟할 때나 거리로 彷徨할 때나
늘 너의 姿態는 이 옵바의 마음을 덮는다
(1931, 3, 3, 밤에) ＜90＞
