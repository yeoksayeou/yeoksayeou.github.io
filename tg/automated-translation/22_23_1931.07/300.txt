Young Idealist, Poem and Sijo
						Pak Sŭng-gŏl

Embracing the sun setting in the Western Hills in your heart,
Oh, young traveler walking the crimson-dyed desert!
I know your heart;
You are the young idealist of this country (이 나라),
Truly our vanguard.

As you walk this darkening wilderness (荒原),
With head bowed low,
Each time you move a footstep forward,
Blood boils within your heart.
The shining footprints you leave behind as you tread
Are the path that the youth of this land
Must walk to find the joy of Tomorrow.

Oh—you are the young idealist of this country,
The pioneer. <83>

**Summary**

This poem by Pak Sŭng-gŏl portrays a "young idealist" as a solitary figure walking through a desolate landscape at sunset. The speaker expresses deep understanding and admiration for this figure, calling them the vanguard and pioneer for the youth of "this country." The idealist's difficult journey, marked by inner turmoil ("blood boils"), is presented as blazing a trail toward a hopeful future ("Tomorrow's joy"). From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1931 under Japanese rule, uses metaphorical language (desolate wilderness, setting sun, pioneer) likely to evoke nationalist sentiment and inspire hope among Korean youth facing colonial oppression, without overtly challenging the authorities. Historians might find the poem's indirect expression of national aspirations and the idealization of youth leadership significant for understanding cultural resistance during this period.

젊은 理想者, 詩와 時調
						朴承杰
西山에 떠러지는 해를 가슴에 안고
벌거케 물든 사막을 것는 젊은 나그네여!
나는 그대의 마음을 아노니
그대는 이 나라의 젊은 理想者로다
참따운 우리의 앞잡이로다.

저무는 이 荒原을,
고개를 느리우고
한 발자곡 옴겨 놀 때마다
그대의 가슴속에서는 피가 끊는구나,
그대가 밟고 가는 그 빛나는 발자최는
이 땅의 젊은이가
明日의 기쁨을 찾기 위하여 거러야 할 그 길이다.

오―그대는 이 나라의 젊은 理想者로다,
선구자로다. ＜83＞
