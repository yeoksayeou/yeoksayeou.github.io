Magazine Title: 동광 제23호
Publication Date: 1931년 07월 05일
Article Title: 젊은의 魂! 젊은의 피! 피에 설인 旗빨! (Youth's Soul! Youth's Blood! Blood-stained Flag!)
Author: 全鳳濟
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

1
In my hollowed skull (頭骨),
Lightning-like cracks (龜裂) have formed.
And the pale-looking,
Cooled blood flows like murky water
Through branching blood vessels,
Growing chillingly cold in my heart.

Within my cracked skull, a trembling soul
Tries to mend the scattering skull,
Clutching a bent needle on an invisible thread,
Groping at the gaunt bone fragments.
—But! Always my soul and blood whisper.
—Youth's soul! —Youth's blood!
—Blood-stained flag!

2
The once-sharp lines of nerves
Hang limply like a rain-beaten spider web,
And the sound of wailing, "Wŏng! Wŏng!"
From the piercing, violent storm (風雨), seeps into the very gaps between my bones.

An arrow-like gaze (視線), dulled
By tear-misted eyes,
Like the eyes of a soulless tiger,
Rolls powerlessly its deeply sunken eyeballs,
Silently looking up at the blue sky (蒼空) and sighing.
—But always my soul and blood whisper.
—Youth's soul! —Youth's blood!
—Blood-stained flag!
(1931. 4. From Ryugyŏng) <15>

**Summary**

The poem "Youth's Soul! Youth's Blood! Blood-stained Flag!" by Chŏn Pong-je portrays a state of profound physical and mental decay, using visceral imagery like a cracked skull, cooled blood, limp nerves, and a soulless gaze. This overwhelming sense of desolation, possibly reflecting the harsh realities of life under colonial rule, is repeatedly contrasted by an internal whisper affirming the "Soul of Youth," the "Blood of Youth," and a "Blood-stained Flag." This recurring refrain acts as a counterpoint, suggesting an inextinguishable spirit, resilience, or perhaps suppressed revolutionary ideals persisting amidst suffering. For historians, the poem offers insight into the psychological state of Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, illustrating the tension between despair caused by oppression and the enduring, potentially nationalistic, spirit symbolized by youth, blood, and the evocative image of a blood-stained flag. The reference to Ryugyŏng (Seoul) grounds the poem in its specific time and place.

젊은의 魂! 젊은의 피! 피에 설인 旗빨!
						全鳳濟
1
골아든 나의 頭骨에는
번개 결 같은 龜裂이 젓다.
그리고 두골을 싸고도는 히멀한등
식은 혈액이 호린 물 흐르듯
가지 뻗힌 혈관을 흘러
나의 심장에 써늘히 식어버린다.

龜裂진 나의 두골 속에는 발발 떠는 혼이
허터지는 두골을 껴매여 보이지 않는
실오리에 휘어진 바눌을 껴잡고
앙상한 뼈 조각을 더듬어 만진다.
―그러나! 항상 나의 혼과 피는 속삭인다.
―젊은의 혼! ―젊은의 피!
―피에 설인 기빨!

2
날카로웁든 신경의 줄이
비 마즌 거미줄같이 맥없이 느리우고
사무치는 격렬한 風雨에 웡! 웡!
우는 우럼 소리는 뼈 짬까지 배어든다.

살 같은 視線은 서리운 눈물에
무지여
魂빠진 호랑의 눈같이
음퍽 든 눈알을 힘없이 굴리며
묵묵히 蒼空을  우러러 한숨을 쉰다.
―그러나 항상 나의 혼과 피는 속삭인다.
―젊은의 혼! ―젊은의 피!
―피에 설인 기빨!
(1931. 4. 柳京에서) ＜15＞
