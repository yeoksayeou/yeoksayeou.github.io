**Anecdotes**
A young Japanese woman came to Kyŏngsŏng (京城) for the first time. She graduated from a women's professional school and came over to Chosŏn (朝鮮) to do "social work." Her impressions from her first day in Chosŏn are as follows:
"Coming to Chosŏn, why are there so many Chosŏn people?"

**Separate Eyebrows**
It's said that American women nowadays shape their eyebrows separately to give "charm" to the opposite sex. For example, the right eyebrow goes down, and the left eyebrow goes up.

**Wedding Gift**
In a village called Masakage(?) in Shimane Prefecture (島根縣), a pretty bride named Hanako (花子) got married. Right in the middle of the wedding ceremony, she developed stomach pains and gave birth to a healthy baby boy. It was said to be an unparalleled gift at the joyous ceremony, and the groom decided to receive and raise the child.

**1 Million Won**
Mr. Min Chŏngsik (閔庭植), son of Mr. Min Yŏngik (閔泳翊) who was famous as a politician during the late Korean Empire (韓末政局), discovered a Shanghai Bank (上海銀行) deposit passbook among old household items. Dreaming of becoming suddenly rich, he went to Shanghai (上海) to withdraw the money, only to find the deposit had all been withdrawn with nothing left, so he returned. A certain Mr. X, who had been backing him financially with the expectation of getting a share if this money came through, is reportedly planning to sue Mr. Min for fraud (사긔죄).

**Unfortunate Empress**
Queen Helen (헬렌 陵下) of Romania (루마니아國), despite striving to restore her family life since King Carol (카롤) was reinstated, ultimately failed and received an imperial order for expulsion from the country on May 10th. The cause of this discord is, of course, the existence of King Carol's lover, Madame Lupescu (루페스크 부인).
The only leniency granted to the Queen is that she can return to the country twice a year to meet with her own son, Crown Prince Mihai (미하이 皇子).

**In Chinese Homes Too**
According to statistics from the Great Shanghai Social Bureau (大上海社會局), the number of divorces among Chinese people surged last year. The total number of divorces in 1930 was 852, an increase of 208 cases compared to the previous year. The total number of divorce petitions filed was 952, of which 177 were submitted by men, and the rest were submitted by women.

**Urgent Letter**
A letter from a provincial retailer to a wholesaler in Seoul.
"Dear Sir, if you're going to send the pump (뽑푸), you must send the handle (손잽이) too. Without the handle, what am I supposed to do? Sincerely, Mosanghŭi(?), respectfully submitting this letter (모상희 재배상서). P.S. I just searched the box again, and the darn handle came out."

**The Master's Touch**
"Honey, this morning the Master (나리) touched the flowerpot (꽃밤 - likely 꽃화분) a bit."
"Yes, that's fine. I'll fix it back properly myself (제가 도로 잘 해 놓습지오)."

**Until Death**
A wife committed suicide using gas. Her husband comes home and checks the gas meter (까스 게량기) first.
The gas usage jumped by 10 meters all at once. "She couldn't drop her habit of spending money freely even when dying."

**Things Worth Seeing**
A visitor from Seoul (at Kŭmgangsan): "What is there worth seeing in this area?"
Local person: "For visitors from Seoul, the mountains are worth seeing, and for country folk (시굴 사람), visitors from Seoul are worth seeing." <67>

**Summary**

This article, "Modern March," is a collection of short anecdotes, news snippets, and jokes typical of popular magazines in 1931 colonial Korea. It covers a diverse range of topics including a naive comment by a Japanese newcomer to Seoul, American fashion trends, an unusual birth during a wedding in Japan, the failed attempt of a descendant of a Korean politician to claim an old bank deposit, European royal gossip involving the Romanian Queen, rising divorce rates in China, and several humorous exchanges or observations reflecting everyday life and attitudes (an urgent business letter mix-up, a servant dealing with a master's clumsy touch, a dark joke about a gas suicide, and a witty exchange between a tourist and a local at Kŭmgangsan).

From a historical perspective, the piece offers glimpses into the social and cultural milieu of the time. The opening anecdote about the Japanese woman arriving for "social work" and her surprise at the number of Koreans subtly reflects the colonial power dynamic and perhaps a degree of obliviousness or detachment among some Japanese newcomers. The mention of Min Yŏngik connects to the late Joseon/Korean Empire period history. The inclusion of international news (Romania, China, USA) alongside local Korean matters and jokes shows the flow of information and the mix of interests catered to by the magazine's readership. The language itself, with its older spellings and grammar, is also of linguistic interest.

모던 行進曲
名談
젊은 일본 여자가 京城을 처음 왓다. 그는 여자전문학교를 졸업하고 朝鮮에서 「사회사업」을 하기 위하야 건너 온 사람이다. 그가 조선 온 첫날의 感想談이 如左하다.
『朝鮮에 오니까 웬 朝鮮人이 그러케 많으냐』고.

각각 눈섭
요새 미국 녀자는 이성에게 「챠암」을 주기 위하야 눈섭을 각각으로 짓는다 한다. 가령 바른 눈섭은 아레로 나려가고 왼 눈섭은 위로 올라가게.

結婚 선물
島根縣 正下地라는 촌에서는 花子라는 이쁜 색시가 시집을 갓는데 결혼식을 막 거행하는 중간에 복통이 일어나더니 옥동자를 탄생햇슴으로 기쁜 예식석상에 둘도 없는 선물이라 하야 신랑이 받아서 기르기로 되엇다고.

100만원
韓末政局에 정객으로 이름 잇는 閔泳翊씨의 아들 閔庭植씨가 묵은 세간 속에서 上海銀行 예금통장을 발견하고 졸부가 될 꿈을 꾸고 상해로 돈을 찾으러간 즉 예금은 다 찾아 쓰고 남은 것이 없다 하야 도라왓는데 이 돈이 나오면 한 몫 먹기로 하고 그의 뒤를 대주든 某氏는 閔氏를 사긔죄로 고소를 하리라고 한다.

不遇의 皇后
루마니아國의 황후 헬렌 陵下는 카롤 황제가 復位한 이래로 가정의 복구를 애썻지마는 마츰내 不成功되어 5월 10일 國外退去의 勅命을 받게 되엇다. 이 불화의 원인은 물론 카롤 황제의 애인인 루페스크 부인의 존재한 그것이다.
황후에게 유일한 寬大處分은 년2회 귀국해서 그 친자인 미하이 皇子와 對而할 수 잇다는 것이라 한다.

中國家庭에도
大上海社會局의 통계에 의하면 작년도의 중국인의 이혼 수가 격증햇다. 1930년도 총 이혼 수가 852, 즉 前年에 비하야 208건이 늘엇다. 離婚請願件數가 도합 952건인데 그 중 177은 남자가 재출한 것이요 남저지는 여자 측에서 제출한 것이라고.

급한 편지
지방의 소매상이 서울 잇는 도매상에게 하는 편지.
『경게자 여보 뽑푸를 보내랴거든 손잽이까지 보내야지 손잽이가 없으니 어떠케 하란 말이요. 모상희 재배상서 재, 지금 상자 속을 다시 뒤저보니까 망할 놈의 손잽이가 나왓소』

나리의 솜씨
『아범 오늘 아츰에 나리께서 꽃밤을 좀 만지섯다네』
『네 괜찬습니다. 제가 도로 잘 해 놓습지오』

죽을 때까지
안해가 까쓰 자살을 햇는데 남편이 도라 와 보고 까스 게량기부터 본다.
까스가 단번에 10미터가 다라 낫다. 『돈 잘 쓰는 버릇은 죽을 때도 못 놓앗군』

볼만한 것
