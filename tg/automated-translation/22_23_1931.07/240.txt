Magazine Title: 동광 제23호
Publication Date: 1931년 07월 05일
Article Title: 鐵人形의 노래, 詩와 時調 (Song of the Iron Doll, Poem and Sijo)
Author: 稻原 鄭日秀
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

(Though imperfect, I respectfully offer this poem to inquire after Ch'unwŏn's recent well-being)

Iron, iron, iron—
Shining when worked with fire, growing stronger
the more it is hammered, iron, the cornerstone of civilization, iron, iron...

The cutting knife and the shearing scissors, the folding tongs,
From a single tiny awl
To be used in countless machines, decorations, instruments.
The pot (솣) for cooking rice when hungry,
The brazier or stove for holding fire when cold,
The lock (잠울통) guarding the safe in a money-filled bank,
The key that can undo the sorrowful prisoner's handcuffs (손사슬).
To become the iron bell (쇠북) of a monastery, to plead for
The unfortunate people of the whole (왼) world and pray to the gods and Buddhas (신불), to sing praises.

Combining in one body the talents and duties
Of the orator, the singer, the clown—all these various (왼갓) kinds,
To play the role of the star in the world's (세게) performance stage (예단) and communication sphere (통신게),
Becoming a part of the "radio" (라듸오) would also be delightful,
The airplane sliding through the sky,
The "ski" flying over the ice (어름)—that exhilarating wish—
Or becoming a plowshare (살납) or the tip of a chisel
To break up the old earth,
Or becoming a warning bell (인경) like the sound of thunder <80>
To announce the time, the urgent time, the dawn
To the foolish people fallen asleep in the dark world (세게),
Or becoming a saw, chisel, plane, adze (짜구니)
To build a palace, grandest on earth,
Or, to introduce and promote
Passionate poetry and profound philosophy,
Even if not becoming type itself, to at least join the chorus in a printing press.
To promote peace and friendship between nations,
On the vast waters of the five oceans, across the six continents,
To be used in a corner (구퉁이) of the steamships and trains that travel (단니는) and dominate!

Oh, but when my talent is displayed once,
When my original duty (본무) as a doll is shown (보어질),
Even the most fault-finding child
Cannot help but stop crying.
My responsibility as a parent to humankind, and more than anyone in the world,
My duty to look after the affectionate, great children!
The easily startled old folks (로인네) merely
Praise me, saying, "How marvelous!"
And the laughter-loving young women (시악시), though finding me cute, even tease me!

Worked with fire and hammered, I become stronger—I am iron (쇠미라).
If this body should be shattered,
More than anything else, I would first roll onto the battlefield
To crush and destroy
Those who disrupt (어그리는) the peace of the earth,
Becoming a cannon or its shell...
—(From an old manuscript)—<81>
<80-81>

**Summary**

This poem, "Song of the Iron Doll," personifies iron, celebrating its essential role in civilization. It details iron's versatility, from basic tools and household items (knives, pots, locks) to modern technologies like radios, airplanes, skis, printing presses, ships, and trains. Iron is depicted as strong, transformative, and vital for progress, communication, construction, and even art and philosophy dissemination. The poem also includes a section where the "iron doll" speaks of its duty to entertain children and elicit reactions from adults. It concludes with a powerful image: if shattered, the iron wishes to become a weapon—a cannon or shell—to destroy those who disrupt world peace. From a historical perspective, this 1931 poem from colonial Korea is interesting for its strong emphasis on modernization, industrial strength (symbolized by iron), and progress through technology. The dedication to Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu) links it to a major intellectual figure of the era. The final violent imagery, desiring to become weaponry for peace, could reflect the complex socio-political climate, perhaps hinting at desires for strength against oppression or reflecting the broader militarism of the period. Historians might study the poem's blend of modern aspirations and its concluding martial tone within the context of colonial discourse and national identity formation.

鐵人形의 노래, 詩와 時調
						稻原 鄭日秀
(덜되나마 이 시를 삼가 올려 春園의 近侯를 問하옴)

쇠 쇠 쇠―
불로 다루면 빛나고, 두다리면 두다릴사록
굳세지는 쇠, 문명의 지추돌인 쇄 쇠...

끊는 칼과 베는 가위, 접는 집게나,
조고만 한 개의 송곳으로부터
허구 많은 기계, 장식, 기명에 쓰일 것을.
배 고프면 밥짓는 솣이나,
치울 때 불담기는 화로나, 스토브나,
돈 흔한 은행소 금고를 맏는 잠울통,
애 닯은 수인의 손사슬을 풀 수 잇는 열쇠.
수도원 쇠북이 되어서, 왼 세상의
불행한 겨레를 위하고 신불에 기도드릴 것을 올 것을 노래할 것을.

연설장이, 노래꾼, 광대 이 왼갓
재질과 직분을 한 몸에 겸하야
세게 예단에 통신게에 총아 노릇을 하는
『라듸오』한 부분이 되어봄도 유쾌한 것을,
하늘을 미끄럼타는 비행기,
어름 우를 날는 『스키』 그 통쾌한 소원-
혹은 고광이나, 살납 끝이 되어서
묵은 띠를 파이루든지,
우뢰소리 같은 인경이 되어서 ＜80＞
어둠 세게에 한잠 드른 바보백성에게
때를, 화급한 때를, 새벽을 알리든지
톱, 끌, 대패, 짜구니가 되어서
장엄하기 지상에 없는 왕궁을 지어보든지
열렬한 시와, 그윽한 철학을,
소개 또 선전하기 위하야
활자까진 못될 망정 인쇄기에나 합주되든지.
나라와 나라의 평화와 친목을 도모하기 위하야,
오대양 넓은 바다에, 여섯 대지를
주름잡고 단니는 기선, 기차의 한 구퉁이에 쓰일 것을!

오오, 그러나 한번 내 재조를 구경할 때,
인형인 나의 본무를 보어질 때,
아모리 트집 잘 부리는 아이라도
울음을 안그치는 법이 없단다.
인생의 어버이인, 그리고 세상에 누구보다도
정다운, 위대한 아이 보기를 맡은 내 책임이어!
놀람증 많은 로인네는, 다만
『히한하다!』고 칭찬을 할 뿐이고,
웃기 좋아하는 시악시들은 귀여우나마 야유까지 하나니!

불로 다루고 두다리면 더 강해지는 나는 쇠미라.
만약 이 몸이 부수어 질 땐
무엇보다도 먼저 전장에 굴러나가
지상의 평화를 어그리는 자들을
노어 깨트리고 망칠 수 잇는
대포나 그 탄환에나...
―(舊稿에서)―＜81＞
