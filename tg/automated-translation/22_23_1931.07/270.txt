My Dream, Poetry and Sijo
						Hwang Sunwŏn

Dream! Last night my dream—
I dreamt a strange dream
After trampling and crushing the world
I planted it full of flowers of life
And sang to my heart's content within it

This dream I can never forget
Even in this head of mine, awakened and scattered
Is firmly nailed
Though all other things are brushed by the waves of the world (世波), only my dream of longing exists—<82>

**Summary**

The poem describes a powerful dream where the speaker obliterates the existing world and replaces it with a vibrant one filled with "flowers of life," where they can sing freely. This intense dream remains vividly imprinted on the speaker's mind even after waking, representing an unforgettable longing that persists despite worldly hardships ("世波"). From a colonial history perspective, the poem, written in 1931 Korea, could be interpreted as a veiled expression of desire for radical change and liberation from the oppressive reality. Historians might find the imagery of destruction and rebirth, coupled with the enduring "dream of longing," significant as a reflection of underlying national sentiment or personal escapism during that period, articulated through poetic metaphor rather than direct political statement.

나의 꿈, 詩와 時調
						黃順元
꿈! 어제 밤 나의 꿈―
이상한 꿈을 꾸엿노라
세계를 짓밟아 문질은 후
생명의 꽃을 가득이 심으고
그 속에서 마음껏 노래를 불럿노라

언제든지 잊지 못할 이 꿈은
깨여 흐터진 이 내 머리에도
굳게 못박혓노라
다른 모든 것은 世波에 스치어도 나의 동경의 꿈만이 존재하나니―＜82＞
＜82＞
