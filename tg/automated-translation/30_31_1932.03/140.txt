Chosŏn Journal, Chosŏn in January
Let's know Chosŏn—with this as our motto, we intend to gather materials as they are. Though insufficient, we try to gather a few materials that have appeared on the surface.
Chosŏn in January—The Kyŏngsŏng Electric Company municipal management issue (京城電氣府營問題), which has been contentious since last year, fizzled out due to an order from the Government-General. The statement of the pro-municipal management faction is just a statement, reality is reality; a statement unaware of the power of the plutocrats (재벌). Was the Prefectural Council member (府議員) who claimed to know from the beginning it wouldn't work clever (령리)? It seems my predictions are often correct.

Around the end of the lunar year, there were many thefts (窃盜) and robberies (强盜). On the 21st, a robber appeared in Koksan; on the night of the 25th, five robbers in Iri; on the 27th, a robber in Yŏngju; a suspicious man (怪漢) appeared in Iksan; a trio of money-snatching robbers in Samnye; a knife-wielding criminal in Haenam; a Suun-gyo believer (水雲敎徒) robbery incident in Taejŏn, etc. Amidst the many thefts and robberies, the theft of 780,000 *wŏn* from the P’yŏngyang Bank of Chosŏn stands out; details are in a separate section of this journal. Thieves, thieves, big thieves, small thieves.

The people of Chosŏn starve. Those who starve are the have-nots, sustaining life (連命) with grass roots and tree bark (草根木皮). Oh, those who love the nation, what are the concrete relief measures (救濟策)? In Hamgyŏng Pukto and parts of Kangwŏn-do alone, there are 66,000 people needing relief (要救濟者).

Farmer movements (農民運動) have broken out (勃發) everywhere. Among them, the disputes (爭議) at the Tamok Farm in Kimje <43> and the Hakkan Farm in Chinyŏng are the largest. The trend is moving towards crop non-payment alliances (作科不納同盟) rather than petitions (陳情). Thus, the Koryŏng tenant dispute achieved victory, and the women divers (海女) of Cheju Island even snatched XX's hat. The petitions of the Water Irrigation Associations (水利組合) are braver than the landlords'. If that's courage, it seems courage comes more from practical necessity than from ethics class (修身時間).

The livelihood of laborers (勞働者) has been dire (생활급박) since the beginning of the new year. Pay the wages for work done (일한 싹)! Don't lower wages further! Unggi laborers held a demonstration parade (示威行列). The Masan Labor Union lost its leaders and control, thus failing, but what will become of the strikes (罷業) at the Samsŏng Rice Mill in Chinnamp’o and the Kwangyang Gold Mine?

Social movements continue without rest; what could be the reason? One can know by looking at the January journal of Chosŏn. The times being what they are, many were arrested (被檢) in relation to the Anti-Imperialist Manifesto Incident (反帝檄文事件). The world is indeed full of difficulties and affairs (多難多事). (Recorded by Yi Chong-su)

1. Incidents
Electric Company Municipal Management Issue
6th: The Preparatory Committee for Municipal Management (府營期成會) resolved to send committee members to Tōkyō and to recommend the resignation of Prefectural Council members who had not yet submitted their resignations.
13th: Thirteen Prefectural Council members who had submitted resignations withdrew them, stating it was natural for the Prefectural Governor (府尹) to submit a proposal for an opinion paper on the Electric and Gas Municipal Management Plan to the Prefectural Assembly (府會) for discussion (附議).
15th: The Vice Governor-General for Political Affairs (政務總監) declared (言明) to the press corps that municipal management was impossible, but instead, the company headquarters would be moved to Kyŏngsŏng, and it would donate 1 million *wŏn* <44> to the prefecture. 云云 (Etc., etc.)
21st: At the Prefectural Council Informal Meeting (府議懇談會), an alternative plan (代案) to municipal management was passed. The pro-public management faction walked out.
25th: Fifteen Prefectural Council members issued a statement opposing the alternative plan for electric company municipal management, declaring it merely made a mockery of the prefectural citizens.

Hamgyŏng Pukto Sardine Oil Agreed Price Issue (咸北鰮油協定價額問題)
6th: A rally was held opposing the agreed price (協定價額) decided between Hamgyŏng Pukto Province and the Consolidated Fats Company (合同油脂會社). 7th: At the Hamgyŏng Pukto Sardine Fertilizer Business Association meeting (咸北鰮肥業者大會), resolutions were passed rejecting the sardine oil price agreement and opposing the monopoly sales system (一手販賣制). 9th, 10th: At the Ch’ŏngjin Sardine Oil Manufacturers Association meeting (淸津鰮油製造業者大會), a resolution rejecting the fish oil price agreement, signed by 340 attendees, was passed, and six points were submitted to the fisheries section chief.

780,000 *Wŏn* Theft Incident
18th: Discovery of the major theft of 780,000 *wŏn* from the P’yŏngyang branch of the Bank of Chosŏn (朝鮮銀行). 20th: At 4:30 AM, the seven Japanese culprits were arrested, and 770,000 *wŏn* in cash was seized (押收) and returned to the bank.
9th: Mr. Im Ch’i-jŏng (林蚩正氏) passed away from a stroke at age 53. John R. Griffith (죤·알·구리휫) from Princeton, USA, and Alice McGillan (아리스, 마킬이란) from Washington state sent job applications (就職願) to the Bureau of Education.
16th: A condolence ceremony (吊慰式) for compatriots victimized in Manchuria was held at the Central YMCA hall (중앙기독교 청년회관).
20th: The Chosŏn Leprosy Eradication Research Association (朝鮮癩病病根絶硏究會) decided on a project to transfer all leprosy (癩病) patients near Kyŏngsŏng to Yŏsu.
22nd: Six people involved in the Public Rally Incident were provisionally released (假出獄).

2. Hardship of Life
The miserable sub-standard (線下) living conditions of Chosŏn laborers and farmers are indescribable (不可形容). Gathering only what caught the eye from newspaper reports during January: <45>
(10th) Itō Matsuji (伊藤松次) of Sŏkjŏng-ri, Ch’ŏnan-gun, ordered 27 households in Sadang-ri, Chinch’ŏn, and 40 households in Naesan-ri, Ŭmsŏng, to tear down their houses and leave.
(13th) The Haknam-myŏn office in Sŏngjin-gun seized even the cooking pots (가마) for unpaid land use fees.
(17th) Over 160 households in Kimje-gun, unable to bear their debts (債務), left without a destination (정처없이 떠나).
(17th) An example from Pangok-tong, Yŏngil: Production 60 *tu*, farm rent (作料) 59 *tu*. The landlord takes it all (몽탕).
(21st) In Tŏksan-ri, Changsu-myŏn, Changsu-gun, over 20 out of 60 households formed groups of men and women to search for food.
(22nd) The future livelihood (生途漠然) of 600 slash-and-burn farmer (火田民) households in T’aet’an-myŏn, Ich’ŏn-gun, is uncertain.
(23rd) Residents of Injé, Kangwŏn-do, cannot even sow (播種) due to lack of seed grain (種穀)?
70-80% of farmers in the Injé-gun area suffer reduced harvests, surviving on buckwheat bran (?) (모밀겉가루) and grass roots and tree bark (草根木皮). Those needing relief (要救助者) in the county exceed 21,000 (County office survey).
(26th) A certain An of Ch’ŏnghang-ni, Yangju-gun, pressed by debt (債務), fled with his family of five in the middle of the night.
(29th) In Hawŏnch’ŏn-myŏn, Sinhŭng-gun, over 200 households have already become displaced persons since the harvest. The remaining 1,200-odd households lack even the strength to leave.
(31st) In Chunam and Chubuk myŏns, Kyŏngsŏng-gun, over 200 slash-and-burn farmer households cry from hunger, and men, women, old, and young alike suffer from swelling (부증) [likely edema from malnutrition].
(31st) Major crop failure in Kangwŏn and Hamgyŏng Pukto. Disaster victims (罹災民) number over 800,000; those needing relief 66,000. The Government-General (總督府) requested an 80,000 *wŏn* budget from the Ministry of Finance (大藏省 - Japan). At 10 *chŏn* per day, it would run out in five or six days. The tragic plight of Maengsan slash-and-burn farmers: 120 households displaced in January, the future increasingly bleak.
(Note) ( ) indicates the date appeared in the newspaper. Same below. <46>

3. Farmer Movement
Tenant Disputes (小作爭議)
5th: The Tamok Farm dispute in Kimje resolved through mutual compromise.
6th: The Koryŏng tenant dispute (Land Tax Non-Payment Alliance (地稅不納同盟), Tenant Fee Reduction (小作料減下), Establishment of Tenancy Rights (小作權確立)) resolved by the landlord side's concession.
10th: The Hakkan Farm in Chinyŏng (進永), Kyŏngsang Namdo, issued notices of tenancy transfer (小作權移動通知) to leaders and 11 others. The tenant farmers (作人) prepare to resist.
12th: Regarding the Hakkan Farm issue, about 20 people set off on foot to petition (陳情) the county (郡).
14th: Centered on the Hakkan Farm issue, seizure notices were issued to 11 leaders concerning fertilizer payment problems. Tenant farmers who went to petition about the tenancy transfer issue returned (還歸) without success.
19th: In Yŏngo-myŏn, Kosŏng-gun, Kyŏngsang Namdo, tenants of Pang Pyŏng-guk (方炳國) united, returned the notices (告知書), and formed a Rent Non-Payment Alliance (作料不納同盟).
17th: The Tamok Farm dispute in Kimje escalated. Over 800 tenant farmers surrounded the police substation (駐在所) where the landlord was hiding and demanded a meeting.
24th: Over 200 tenant farmers from the Chinyŏng Hakkan Farm departed by boat late at night to petition the Pusan head office and the Provincial Office with demands including fertilizer cost sharing (肥料半分) and several other conditions.
25th: The Hakkan Farm dispute group of over 200 rushed (殺到) the Hakkan head office.
26th: The Hakkan tenant farmers returned home without success.
30th: In the Tamok Farm dispute, 11 people were indicted for disturbance (騷擾), house trespassing, and obstruction of official duties (公務執行妨害).

Jeju Haenyo Incident
Triggered by the arrest of about 20 *haenyo* (women divers) due to suppression of the Haenyo Association (海女組合), on the morning of the 24th, a group of over 500 *haenyo* staged a large demonstration, attacked the police substation (駐在所), resulting in injuries on both sides. <47> On the 26th, during the predawn transfer of about 30 arrested individuals, a group of 800 *haenyo* surrounded the police squad (警官隊), making the situation dangerous (事態險惡). Partial suppression was achieved by firing blanks (空砲發射). Seeking to rescue 40 members of the Public Consultation Committee (民衆協議會) arrested on suspicion of instigation from behind, they rushed (殺到) the police substation around 7 PM on the 27th.

Water Irrigation Association (水利組合) Issues
8th: Police prohibited landlords of the P’yŏngan Namdo Water Irrigation Association (平南水利組合) in Sunan from going en masse to petition (陳情).
17th: Landlords complain about the Kowŏn Sangok-myŏn Water Irrigation Association, saying the entire production is insufficient even for the water tax (水稅). They say no one would farm it even if given for free.
23rd: Dispute over profits (去益紛紏?) at the P’yŏngan Water Irrigation Association. At a landlord meeting, a resolution was passed opposing the investigation centered around Puch'on.
23rd: At the Chungnam Water Irrigation Association (中南水組) in Ulsan, land auctioned due to unpaid water tax (水稅) had absolutely no buyers (求買者 全無) even at 8 *chŏn* per *p'yŏng*.

4. Labor Movement
1st: Someone from Posŏng Yangjŏng Company (良井會社) fired a gun (放銃) at laborers demanding wages (勞賃) instead of paying them.
2nd: The owner of the sardine oil factory in Ch’aho, Iwŏn, fled/hid (피신) without paying wages (不給).
3rd: Laborers rushed (殺到) the homes of sardine factory owners in Ch’ŏngjin, Ch’ŏngp’a, and Ch’ŏngam-dong, protesting wage cuts.
At the Kŭmje Cotton Mill (久製棉工場) in Sŏ-myŏn, Tongnae, about 40 male and female workers went on strike (盟罷), submitting 12 demands including wage increases of over 50% and implementation of an 8-hour workday system.
Kowŏn police arrested 40 youths. Reason secret.
4th: All employees of the Tong-A Printing Company (동아인쇄소) in Ŭiryŏng, Kyŏngsang Namdo, <48> went on strike (盟罷), demanding opposition to dismissal (解雇), a 20% wage increase, and 5 other points.
5th: 50 miners at the Ch’ŏngyang Taebong Gold Mine (靑陽大鳳 金鑛) went on strike.
36 transport workers for Hamhŭng Nitrogen Fertilizer (咸興室素肥料) went on strike.
Due to the Armed Communist Party Incident (武裝共産黨事件), Kando consular police (領警) arrested and interrogated 130 students of Taesŏng Middle School (大成中學).
6th: 50 miners at the Sunch’ŏn Soft Silica Mine (順川 軟硅石鑛) stopped work.
80 temporary employees (臨時傭) of P’yŏngyang Electric Company stopped work, demanding allowance payments (手當金).
7th: 200 construction workers for the T’aesang-myŏn Water Irrigation Association in Yangsan-gun went on collective rest (盟休).
Rice mill workers at Samsŏng Rice Mill (三成精米面) in Hup’o-ri, Chinnamp’o, went on strike demanding wage increases.
7th: Due to the Anti-Imperialist War Manifesto Incident (反帝戰檄文事件), Pusan police became active. Eight leaders of the Pusan Labor Union (釜山勞組) and the Tongnae Youth Alliance (동래 少年同盟) were arrested.
10th: 59 workers at the grain processing (?) plant of the Yŏngsanp’o Grain Cooperative (榮山浦 穀物組合團 量工場職工) went on strike (盟罷) demanding wage increases.
11th: Workers on the Unggi River construction project demanded wage increases and opposed advance payments (立替支拂).
12th: Unggi laborers held a demonstration parade (示威行列).
554 workers on the Nonsan river control project (治水工夫) went on strike.
13th: 900 miners at the Kwangyang Gold Mine (光陽 金鑛) in Chŏlla Namdo went on strike (盟罷) opposing wage cuts.
Due to the Anti-Imperialist Manifesto Incident, Tongnae police became active, arresting about 10 people.
14th: 25 workers at the Sŏngch'ŏn Monopoly Bureau Branch Office (成川專賣局 出張所) went on strike (盟罷).
The strike (工盟罷) at Chinnamp’o Samsŏng Rice Mill shows no sign of resolution. The factory side continues operations with 50 new workers and 30 who returned unconditionally (無條件 復業者).
14th, 15th: At the Hoeryŏng Chosŏn Consolidated Coal Mine (會寧 朝鮮合同炭鑛所), 300 workers were suddenly dismissed. Decreased demand was the reason.
15th: The strike group at Chinnamp’o Samsŏng Rice Mill occupied the factory at dawn. Police arrested about 20 people. <49>
16th: Over 200 Unggi laborers, shouting demands for price reductions (物價減下), rushed (殺到) the police station. They attacked the warehouse of the Pak某 trading company. About 10 were arrested (被檢).
Anti-Imperialist War Manifestos (反帝戰檄文) posted in Samnye 4-ga-ri.
(18th) The mastermind of the Pusan Anti-Imperialist War Manifesto Incident was arrested (被檢) in Mokto.
(18th) 24 laborers at the Kim Ironworks (金鐵工所) in Songjŏng-ri, Kwangju-gun, went on strike.
19th: All laborers belonging to the Maruil Transport Union (丸一連送組) and the Iwata Group (岩田組) on Haean-t'ong, Masan-bu, went on strike opposing wage cuts.
19th: Near the Susŏngch'ŏn repair site in Hamgyŏng Pukto, manifestos (檄文) were scattered, and arson occurred at the construction site regarding the wage increase issue.
20th: Due to a shutdown at the Pusan Katō Rice Mill (釜山 加藤精米所), an indefinite suspension of work was declared for over 600 male and female employees.
20th: 69 workers at the Tongsŏng Silk Mill (東信製糸場) outside Tongdaemun, Kyŏngsŏng, went on strike (盟罷).
20th: 21 youths from Yongsŏng, Iwŏn, were arrested (被檢). Reason unknown.
22nd: In the Masan Labor Union (馬山勞組) strike, about 40 members of the Wŏn'gu Group (圓口組) and the Warehouse Company Transport Department (倉庫會社運搬部) joined in a sympathy strike.
24th: 21 workers at the Tongsŏng Western Goods Company(?) (東星洋頀工) in P'yŏngyang succeeded through a work slowdown (怠業).
(28th) The Masan Labor Union strike incident (馬山 勞組盟罷事件) was resolved with victory for the owners' side due to the arrest of leaders and lack of control.
29th: Due to the Taegu Monopoly Bureau Branch Factory (大邱 專賣支局工場) Worker Communization Plan Incident (赤化計劃事件), 14 workers, students, and women were arrested (被檢).
30th: 40 male and female workers at the Ch’ŏngju-gun Silk Mill (淸州郡 製糸工) went on strike.
Sinhŭng police, on alert, arrested 14 youths; details secret.
Three people involved in the Sinŭiju Factory Labor Union Incident (新義州工場勞 組事件) were referred to public trial. <50>

**Summary**

This article from the March 1932 issue of *Tonggwang* magazine chronicles events in colonial Korea during January 1932. It highlights severe economic hardship, widespread starvation (especially in northern and eastern regions), and resulting social unrest. Key issues reported include the failed attempt at municipalizing Kyŏngsŏng's electric company, a major bank robbery in P'yŏngyang, numerous tenant farmer disputes (Kimje, Chinyŏng, Koryŏng), significant protests by Jeju *haenyŏ* (women divers) against exploitation, and widespread labor strikes across various industries (mining, transport, textiles, construction) demanding better wages and opposing dismissals. The article also notes arrests related to anti-imperialist activities, painting a picture of a society struggling under colonial rule, economic depression, and increasing social tension.

For historians, this article offers a valuable contemporary snapshot of the multifaceted crises facing colonial Korea in early 1932. It details specific instances of economic distress, farmer and labor activism (including methods like non-payment alliances and strikes), state suppression (arrests, police actions), and the impact of colonial economic policies (e.g., irrigation association fees, company monopolies). The specific data on famine relief needs, the details of farmer/labor demands, the Jeju *haenyŏ* uprising account, and the mention of anti-imperialist activities provide concrete evidence of the social conditions and resistance movements during this period.

잡지명	동광 제31호
발행일	1932년 03월 05일
기사제목	朝鮮日誌, 1月의 朝鮮
기사형태	소식

朝鮮日誌, 1月의 朝鮮
朝鮮을 알자-이것을 標語로 잇는 그대로의 재료를 모으려고 한다. 불충분하나마 표면에 나타난 2, 3의 재료를 모으려고 한다.
1月의 朝鮮-작년부터 말성이 되어 오는 京城電氣府營問題는 總督府의 명령으로 흐지부지. 府營派의 聲明書는 聲明書, 실제는 實際, 재벌의 권력을 모르는 성명서. 처음부터 안될 줄 알고 잇엇다는 府議員이 령리? 내는 잘 맞는 모양.
음녁세말에는 窃盜, 强盜가 많앗다. 21일에 谷山에 强盜出現, 25일 밤에 裡里에 5인 强盜, 27일에 榮州 강도, 益山에 怪漢 출현, 參禮에 奪金强盜 3인조, 海南 食刀犯, 大田에 水雲敎徒 强盜事件 등등 절도, 강도가 많은 중에 平壤 朝鮮銀行에 70萬圓 절도 사건이 우뚬, 자세는 본지별지, 도적 도적 큰 도적 적은 도적.
朝鮮 사람은 굼는다. 굼는 사람은 없는 사람 草根木皮로 連命. 民族을 사랑하는 이들이어 구체적 救濟策 여하. 咸北, 江原 일부에만 要救濟者 6萬6千.
農民運動은 도처에서 勃發. 金提 多木農場과＜43＞ 進永 迫間農場의 爭議가 그 중 크다. 陳情보다도 作科不納同盟으로 나가는 것이 추세다. 그리하야 高靈小作爭議는 승리, 濟州島 海女는 XX의 모자까지 베껴. 水利組合의 陳情地主들 보다 勇敢, 그것이 용기라 하면 용기는 修身時間에서 보다 실제의 필요에서 나오는 모양.
勞働者의 생활급박은 신년벽두로부터, 일한 싹을 내라. 일싹을 더 내리지 말라. 雄基人夫는 示威行列, 馬山勞組는 간부를 빼앗기고 통제를 잃어 실패햇거니와 鎭南浦 三成精米所, 光陽 金鑛罷業은 어떠케 될른지.
社會運動은 쉬일새 없으니 그 무슨 이유인고. 일월의 朝鮮의 日誌를 보면 알 일. 시기가 시기라 反帝檄文事件으로서 被檢이 다수. 世上은 바야흐로 多難 多事.(李鍾洙記)

1. 事件
電氣府營問題
6일 府營期成會는 동경에 전정의원을 보낼 것과 아직 사표를 내지 않은 府議員의 사직을 권고하기로 결의.
13일 사표하엿든 府會議員 13명은 府尹이 電氣瓦斯府營案을 제출하라는 意見書案을 府會에 附議할 것을 당연하다고 聲明하엿다고 하야 辭表撤回.
15일 政務總監은 府營은 不可, 그대신 本社를 京城에 이전하야 100만원을 府에 기부케＜44＞ 한다. 云云을 기자단에 言明.
21일 府議懇談會에서 府營代案可決. 公營派退席.
25일 府議員 15명 名義로 電氣府營代案은 府民을 우롱함에 불과하다고 反對聲明書發表.

咸北鰮油協定價額問題
6일 咸北道와 合同油脂會社새의 결정된 協定價額에 대하야 反對大會開催, 7일 咸北鰮肥業者大會에서 鰮油協定價否認과 一手販賣制 反對決議可決. 9일, 10일 淸津鰮油製造業者大會에서 會衆 340명의 연명으로 魚油價格協定否認決議, 6개조 수산과장에게 제출.

78만원 盜難事件
18일-朝鮮銀行平壤支店에서 78만원 大盜難事實 發見. 20일-새벽 네시반에 진범 日人 7명 검거하고 현금 77만원만 押收하야 銀行에 返還.
9일 林蚩正氏 노일혈로 별세 享年 53, 米國 부린스톤 죤·알·구리휫 와싱톤주 아리스, 마킬이란 사람이 학무국에 就職願 寄送.
16일 중앙기독교 청년회관에서 滿洲被害同胞吊慰式 거행.
20일 朝鮮癩病病根絶硏究會에서 京城부근의 癩病환자를 전부 麗水로 移送하는 사업 결정.
22일 民衆大會事件 6명 假出獄.

2. 生活苦
朝鮮 勞働者農民의 생활 線下의 비참한 살림은 不可形容. 1월중 신문지상에만 나타난 것으로 눈에 띠우는 것만을 모아 보면＜45＞
(10일) 天安郡 石井里 伊藤松次는 鎭川砂堂里 27戶와 陰城內山里 40戶에 집을 헐어 가지고 나가라고 철거를 언명.
(13일) 城津郡 鶴南面事務所는 토지사용료에 가마까지 差押.
(17일) 金提郡 160여 戶 債務에 못견디어 정처없이 떠나.
(17일) 迎日盤谷洞의 一例 生産 60斗에 作料 59斗. 지주는 몽탕 가저가.
(21일) 長水郡 長水面 德山里 60戶 중 20여 戶 먹을 것을 찾아 남녀부대.
(22일) 伊川郡 態灘面 600戶 火田民 生途漠然.
(23일) 江原道 麟蹄住民 種穀이 없어 播種도 不能?
麟蹄郡 일대의 농민 7, 8割 減收로 모밀겉가루와 草根木皮로 연명. 郡下要救助者 2萬1천명 돌파.(郡廳의 조사)
(26일) 楊州郡 靑鶴里 安某는 債務에 쪼끼어 가족 다섯명이 밤중에 도망.
(29일) 新興郡 下元川面 流離者 추수이후 이미 200여戶. 남어지 1,200여戶는 流離할 힘도 없어.
(31일) 鏡城郡 朱南 朱北 兩面 火田民 200여 戶 배고파 울고 남녀노소가 모다 부증이 낫다.
(31일) 江原 咸北의 大凶作. 罹災民 80여 만명 要救濟者 66,000. 總督府가 8만원 예산을 大藏省에 요구, 하루 10전치면 닷새엿새에 다 없어질껄. 孟山火田民의 참경 1월중 流離 120戶 앞으로는 점점 암담.
(註) ( )는 신문에 난 日字 以下同＜46＞

3., 農民運動
小作爭議
5일 金堤 多木農場爭議 쌍방양보로 해결.
6일 高靈小作爭議(地稅不納同盟, 小作料減下, 小作權確立)는 지주측 양보로 해결.
10일 慶南 進永 迫間農場은 간부 及 11명에게 小作權移動通知. 作人들은 抗爭을 준비.
12일 迫間農場 문제로 20여 명은 郡에 陳情次 徒步로 출발.
14일 迫間農場 문제를 중심하야 비료대금 문제로 간부 11명에게 차압통지. 小作權移動問題로 陳情갓든 小作人 소득없이 還歸.
