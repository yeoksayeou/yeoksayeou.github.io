**Literary Reader, Volume 1 (Revised Edition)**
Edited by Yi Yunjae (李允宰)

When the first edition of Literary Reader, Volume 1 came out, this reviewer pointed out its characteristic features: 1. The inclusion of a table summarizing the Hangeul spelling system. 2. The entire text being written according to the Hangeul table. 3. The detailed inclusion in the margins of the author's pen name, date of birth, address, written works, etc., providing much reference material. I mentioned that regarding its selection of materials and arrangement, it was suitable for the editor's stated purpose: "This book is edited for use as supplementary material for the Korean language course and as a model for composition in all schools of middle school level and above."

The assessment made at the time of the first edition of the Literary Reader can also be said for the revised second edition. And the statement that this book is worth recommending not only to middle school students but also to anyone who intends to study Korean language and writing, however little, remains unchanged from the first edition.

Why is that? It's not because the content exhaustively gathers material spanning all fields of literature, from children's stories and songs to poetry, essays, novels, and plays, but because the quality of the content is comparatively high in many aspects.

The editor's intention to select materials with an established reputation, such as Ch'unwŏn's "Ode to Bovine Virtue" (牛德頌) and P'ain's "The Water Carrier from Pukch'ŏng" (북청물장사), is even more evident in the second edition.

Most of the second edition consists of material from the first, but there are quite a few additions and deletions of titles, and looking at the whole, corrections to spelling and other aspects are not insignificant. Especially, the appendix, the Hangeul spelling system table, has undergone major revision, making it even easier to understand.

What we should note about the Literary Reader is that it is written in Hangeul and contains several fine pieces of writing that could be called "classics." That its entire content gives the impression of belonging to the Naturalism era remains unchanged from the first edition. One cannot escape the impression that it only collects writings from the old days. (Price 60 chŏn, Published by Chinkwangdang (震光堂)) (Yi Chongsu)

**Peace and Freedom**
Edited by Kim Tonghwan (金東煥)

Seeing only the title of this book, one might think it's a collection of essays on peace and freedom, but that is not the case. It mainly collects around 50 pieces—essays, travelogues, impressions, etc.—by some 30 to 40 individuals, mostly previously published in the magazine *Samch'ŏlli* (三千里). "Currently, the skies over Asia (亞細亞) are filled with war clouds, and the cries for peace and freedom are shaking the entire world. At such a time, how can voicing our true thoughts be considered meaningless?" (From the Foreword). That is why it is titled "Peace and Freedom."

A friend, commenting on this book, called it "Kim Tonghwan style." Looking at the contents now, from "The Future of Chosŏn," "Yi Tonghwi's Youth," "Ireland (愛蘭) Resurrecting," "Red Russia (赤色露西亞)," "The Current State of Compatriots in America (在米同胞)," "Hŭngsadan (興土團) and the Hawaii (布哇) Comrades Association," etc., to softer articles (軟記事) like "My View on Remarriage," "My View on Love and Marriage," "The New Woman and Birth Control," "Debate on a Wife's Fidelity While Husband is Imprisoned," there isn't a single title that doesn't pique one's interest at least once. "An Analysis of the Kim Sŏngsu Chaebol," "New Male-Female Relations in Russia (露西亞)," etc.—all 50 or so titles merely serve to arouse curiosity, making one want to read them at least once. Combine those titles appropriately with the names of 30-40 famous figures, renowned scholars, patriots, and writers from both overseas, such as Sŏ Chaep'il, An Ch'angho, Yi Sŭngman, etc., and domestically—or rather, attach those titles to those figures, listing brilliant titles—and regardless of the content, one cannot help but feel the urge to flip through it at least once. This is the so-called "Kim Tonghwan style." However, this by no means implies that the content is poor. Many of the words from those specific people are worth listening to. It is just regrettable that some pieces were not directly written by the individuals themselves. But since this is a collection of good essays previously published in *Samch'ŏlli* magazine, and since it's not uncommon in any magazine for a reporter to write and publish based on an interview with the person, it's unavoidable. "Peace and Freedom," as my friend said, is undoubtedly a "Kim Tonghwan style" essay collection in the good sense that it makes you want to read it at least once, regardless. (Price 1 wŏn. Samch'ŏllisa (三千里社) Publisher, Postal Transfer Kyŏngsŏng, No. 4282) (李鍾洙) <120>

**Summary**

This article presents two book reviews by Yi Chongsu. The first reviews the revised edition of Yi Yunjae's "Literary Reader, Volume 1," praising its use of Hangeul, inclusion of classic Korean literary works like those by Ch'unwŏn and P'ain, and its utility for students learning Korean, though noting its content feels dated, belonging to the Naturalism era. The second review covers Kim Tonghwan's edited collection "Peace and Freedom," compiled mostly from *Samch'ŏlli* magazine articles. The reviewer highlights the editor's knack for choosing eye-catching titles paired with famous contributors (like Sŏ Chaep'il, An Ch'angho, Yi Sŭngman) covering diverse topics from politics to social issues, terming this the appealing "Kim Tonghwan style," while acknowledging the value of the content despite some pieces not being personally written by the named authors. From a historical perspective, the reviews shed light on Korean literary and intellectual life under Japanese rule. The first review underscores efforts to standardize Hangeul and promote Korean literature through educational readers, even while reflecting a sense of nostalgia or limitation ("old days," "Naturalism era"). The second review reveals the vibrant (and perhaps sensationalist) nature of popular magazines like *Samch'ŏlli* and the public interest in writings by prominent nationalist figures, both domestic and overseas, touching on sensitive topics like Korea's future, international affairs, and social change, demonstrating a continued engagement with national identity and contemporary issues despite colonial constraints.

讀書室
文藝讀本 上卷(修正版)
李允宰編

文藝讀本 上卷 初版이 낫을 때에 評者는 그 特徵으로 1, 한글 綴字法 一覽表가 잇는 것 2, 全文을 한글 일람표에 의하야 쓴 것. 3, 欄上에 作者의 號, 生年月日 주소 著書 등을 일일히 써서 많은 참고가 되는 것 등을 지적하고 그 取材, 배열에 잇어서 編者가 「이 책은 中等程度 이상 모든 學校에서 朝鮮語科의 補習과 작문의 文範으로 쓰기 위하야 편집한 것」이라고 한 編者의 목적에 적합하다는 것을 말하엿다.
文藝讀本 초판시에 한 평은 수정재판에 잇어서도 같이 말할 수 잇다. 그러고 이 책은 中等學生에게 뿐 아니라 다소라도 조선말과 글을 공부할려고 하는 사람에게는 한번 보기를 권하고 싶다는 말도 초판때에서와 다름이 없다.
왜 그런고 하면 그 내용이 童話童謠로부터 詩, 論, 小說, 戱曲에 이르기까지 文藝 各方部門에 亙하야 빼놓지 않고 뫃아 놓앗다는 의미에서가 아니라 그 내용이 우수한 것이 비교적 많다는 意味에서다.
春園의 牛德頌, 巴人의 북청물장사 등등 이미 정평이 잇는 재료를 취할려고 한 編者의 用意는 재판에서 더욱 현저하다.
재판의 대부분은 초판에 잇는 것이지마는 제목의 增削이 적지 않으며 전체로 보아서 철자 기타를 수정한 것이 직지 않다. 특히 附錄인 한글 철자법 일람표는 大修正을 加하야 일층 더 알기 쉽게 하엿다.
우리가 文藝讀本에서 볼 것은 한글로 썻다는 것과 그 중에 「클라씩」이라고 할 만한 좋은 글이 멫 편 잇는 것이다. 그 내용은 전부가 自然主義時代에 속하는 감을 주는 것은 초판에서나 다름이 없다. 옛날 글만을 뫃아 놓앗다는 감을 금할 수 없다. (정가 60전 震光堂 발행) (리종수)

平和와 自由
金東煥編

이 책의 제목만 보고는 平和와 自由에 대한 論文集같이 생각되나 사실은 그러치 않다. 주로 三千里誌에 揭載되엇든 무려 3,40여명의 論文, 紀行, 感想文 등 50여편을 뫃아 놓은 것이다. 「지금 亞細亞의 하늘에는 戰雲이 가득하야 平和와 自由를 부르짓는 소리가 全世界를 뒤흔들고 잇습니다. 이때에 우리의 참다운 소리를 發함이 어찌 뜻 없는 일이라 하오리까」(發刊辭) 그런고로 「平和와 自由」라고 한 것이다.
어떤 친구가 이 책을 평하야 하는 말이  金東煥式이라고. 이제 그 내용을 볼 것 같으면 「朝鮮의 將來」 「 李東輝의 靑年時代」 「復活하는 愛蘭」 「赤色露西亞」 「在米同胞의 現狀」 「興土團과 布哇同志會」 등등으로부터 「나의 再婚觀」 「나의 戀愛와 結婚觀」 「新女性과 産兒制限」 「在獄中妻의 守節論」 등 軟記事에 이르기까지 한번 보고 싶은 마음이 으쓱안하는 제목이 없다. 「金性洙財閥解剖」 「露西亞의 新男女關係」 등등 50여 제목이 다 한번 읽어보고 싶은 호기심을 끄는 것 뿐이다. 그 제목에다가  徐載弼,  安昌浩,  李承晩 등등 해외에 잇는 名士로부터 海內 名士 志士 文士 3,40名士의 이름난 사람들을 적당히 결합시켯으니, 라고 하는 것보다 그 人物에 그 제목을 부치어 찬란한 제목을 羅列하엿으니 그 내용은 여하간 한번 들처보고 싶은 생각이 안날 수 없다. 이것이 소위  金東煥式이다. 그러나 이것은 결코 내용이 빈약하다는 것을 의미하는 것이 아니다. 그 사람의 그 말 一聽할 가치가 잇는 것이 많다. 다만 그 중에는 직접 본인이 집필하지 않은 것이 잇는 것이 유감이다. 그러나 이것은 三千里誌에 揭載하엿든 좋은 論文을 뫃은 것이요. 또한 어떤 잡지에나 본인의 談을 듣고 一記者가 써서 내는 것이 없지 않은 이상 할 수 없는 일이다. 「平和와 自由」는 그 친구의 말과 같이 여하간 한번 보고 싶게 하는 좋은 의미에서  金東煥式 論文集임에 틀림없다. (정가 1원. 三千里社 行振替京城, 4282番) ( 李鍾洙) ＜120＞
