**Tonggwang Round Table Conference**
Aftermath of the Doctor Critique - Editorial Reporter

Regarding the "Doctor Critique" (醫師評判記) published in the previous and preceding issues of this magazine, we have heard some comments. As it is a critique of individuals, it is not unreasonable that it becomes a subject of comment. There is no need to newly bring up whether it was written well or poorly, nor do we intend to. We only hope for an evaluation based solely on the value the article itself possesses.

Of course, as it is a critique of individuals, it is unavoidable that there might be sharp phrases involving satire, humor, or advice. Whether these indeed hit the mark (正鵠) is not something to be determined here either. That is left solely to the ability of the writer and the free judgment of the reader. However, one thing we wish to state clearly is that as the sharp point of the brush spun around, it might have slipped too far and resulted in phrasing that could be misunderstood. Regarding this point, for the honor of the gentlemen placed on the chopping block (俎上), we cannot but add words of apology for any reckless critique.

Among these, particularly concerning the passages related to our respected friend (畏友) Mr. O Han-yŏng (吳漢泳君), we add, by way of explanation, that the original writer's intent was nothing more than well-meaning advice, and at the same time, should there be those who misunderstand the phrasing, we wish to enlighten their ignorance (蒙を啓する). Furthermore, although this was not the original intention, regarding the fact that potentially misleading phrasing was used, we retract the entire text related to Mr. O.

Finally, we wish to add a word regarding one matter that cannot be overlooked. That is, there are those who, taking the appearance of the aforementioned critique as a good opportunity, as if they have caught a tiger by its skin, attempt to make it fodder for petty factional strife and slander. Whether they do so unconsciously or consciously, we cannot know, but when it comes to blindly following (蹈襲) the harmful customs of the quarrelsome and narrow-minded old Chosŏn society and fabricating (做出) things that did and did not happen, one is truly dumbfounded (啞然), pitiful (憫然), and indeed, words of criticism do not come to mind.

Both the writer who takes up the brush of criticism and the editor who publishes it strive only from a fair standpoint, with a fair brush, to publish fair and original writing; this is the lifeblood of a magazine enterprise. Discourse otherwise is merely plotting suicide. That the discourse of "Tonggwang" (東光), in the past, present, and future, proceeds solely according to this policy – in this, our conscience is clear by not a single point. Being human, there will surely be errors. We are not ones lazy in correcting errors. Only deliberately bending the brush is beyond imagination. This is consistently proven by the past history of "Tonggwang."

We take this opportunity to issue a warning to those sufferers of the colored-glasses disease. Stop the harmful and useless counter-propaganda immediately. If they do not cease this unnecessary broadcasting, we declare that we will not hesitate to once again lift the brush of literary chastisement (筆誅).

From a Cold Window in Early Spring - Anonymous Layman (無名居士)

"We played the flute for you, and you did not dance; we sang a dirge, and you did not mourn." This truly is the lament of a prophet (豫言者), and at the same time, a song that clearly expresses how the reward for the lives of all great figures throughout human history was too meager at times. However, that is a fate they could not avoid receiving as representative figures of each era.

I think of St. Paul (聖바울), who died hanging upside down before the Ostia Gate of Rome, yet thanked the Lord and prayed for all those remaining. I also think of Teacher P'oŭn Chŏng (圃隱鄭先生), who died struck down at Sŏnjukkyo Bridge (善竹橋) for his loyalty to the country, and Ch'oe Su-un (崔水雲), who did not yield his beliefs even as his head was severed on the execution block (斷首臺). Did not the great Greek philosopher Socrates (쏘크라테쓰), saying to his weeping disciples before the prison gate <118> "Well then, do you think I deserve to die for a crime I committed?", encourage them for the sake of the teacher dying without any crime, and sweetly receive the cup of poison?

O Christ (그리스도)! O Dante (딴떼)! If you were to appear again in this era and in this country, the regrettable things would be multiplied. The bitter cup you would have to receive would overflow the bowl.

I think of the life of Pestalozzi, the mother and true father of orphans and proletarian (無産) children, that educational saint (聖者) whose tragic and desperate life cannot even be pitied. One can only give thanks and praise.

Great seers (先知者)! Prophets (豫言者)! Your names are solely sacrifice. Your entire lives were only for the future of humanity and for children. Christ and Pestalozzi, who had no self-interest, ended their lives with the cross and freezing to death (凍死), respectively. Are truly superhuman people of God like them all born to be hated, mocked, and persecuted by the ignorant masses (衆愚)? They truly dedicated their hearts, minds, and loyalty for the masses, yet ignorant worldly people ended up killing them.

People of God! True fathers and mothers of humanity! You sweetly received even the bitter cup of death for the ignorant people, and you loved beggars, men and women, old and young alike, embracing them in the bosom of invincible, complete love. Unable to sleep at night, worrying if they were cold or hot, choosing dry spots over wet ones for them to lie down, blessing them to grow well without illness and become true human beings – with hearts superior even to a mother's heart, you showed the path for the sons and daughters of ignorant, propertyless (無産) people to tread, cleared the way for them, and taught them. Truly, Pestalozzi followed the footsteps of Christ on the path of education. Thus, while living a life of hardship beyond words, the only thing that encouraged and comforted his heart was solely complete love. What is my work compared to God's work... What is my love compared to God's complete love... he must have prayed many times while baking dried, hardened pieces of bread, his desired work crumbling, sometimes suffering contempt and ridicule even from comrades. The true mother's heart of our educational saint Pestalozzi, emerging alive even amidst such sorrow and disappointment... that heart is the wise, true mother's heart that fosters comfort from sorrow, new hope from disappointment, and harmony even amidst anxiety and panic, a heart that cannot be broken or defiled by anything in the world. While gazing at the red sunset glow of the evening as the bright day darkens, when the desolate landscape (萬目肅條) is submerged in disappointment and poverty, and the dark curtain already descends (掩襲) beneath one's feet, how many times must Pestalozzi, the educational saint of humanity, have wept with a bursting, bitter heart, enduring the painful waves crashing in his chest, head bowed in the fatigue of hunger and cold (飢寒), yet pressing onward – that is the true mother's heart that even future generations, posterity after posterity (後生 後生 來後生), cannot fully repay. Amidst disappointment, amidst sorrow, and amidst sighs preventing peaceful eye-closing, how many times must Pestalozzi, the educational saint of humanity, have cried with that bursting, bitter heart?

Friend! Compatriot! People... Just as Christ, on the cross, blessed the ignorant humans (衆愚) killing him, saying "It is finished," and "I will now rest peacefully in eternal paradise," Pestalozzi too... must have calmly met his end in a cold place, receiving the bitter cup of freezing to death (凍死). When we see all things being born, growing, and developing (成生化育) in the harmonious spring breeze, each pursuing its place and enjoying its life, we can understand the great, broad, deep, and impartial motherly love of great nature. Another thing to consider is General Garibaldi (까리발디장군), who picked up a small wildflower on a roadside mountain path, trampled and broken by unthinking words and feet, and was lost in thought for a while. Even for a general who cut down human necks like rotten grass when facing thousands upon thousands in enemy lines, it was not that he felt some special sympathy for a wildflower pointlessly trampled and broken. There too, within the general's heart, one can glimpse a great and deep sentiment akin to the motherly love of great nature.

Father Pestalozzi saw, more keenly than others, the indwelling activity of the Holy Spirit of God, who is love and righteousness – truth – in the depths of all things in the universe and human affairs, which appeared to his eyes, sounded in his ears, and both tormented and delighted him. More than anything, what tormented, delighted, intoxicated, made foolish, and drove Pestalozzi, the father of our humanity and educational saint with a true mother's heart, to yearning, was seeing the form of God潜潜 – the activity of life – latent deep within the hearts of the sons of men. He woke, slept, and worked like the Holy Spirit of God; he was a person who could not live even a single day without living like Him, obeying and revering Him. Like Christ...

My few friends scattered on the perilous paths of Chosŏn (朝鮮)! If you friends truly love Chosŏn and wish to work for Chosŏn, let us share, even if just a little, the motherly love of Great Nature! Let us embrace the hearts of those who sweetly received the bitter cup of death from the ignorant masses (衆愚)! If we cannot do so, let us abandon all worldly tactics (處世術) of empty fame and rather be faithful to ourselves, striving for the completion of life called self-individuality.

With awareness, determination, effort, patience... <119>

**Summary**

This article consists of two parts. The first, by an editorial reporter, defends a previously published controversial article ("Doctor Critique"), apologizes specifically to O Han-yŏng for any misunderstandings caused by potentially harsh phrasing, and strongly warns against using the critique to fuel factionalism and slander, reaffirming the magazine *Tonggwang*'s commitment to fair and honest journalism. The second part, by "Anonymous Layman," is a moral essay reflecting on the historical suffering and misunderstanding faced by great figures like St. Paul, Chŏng Mong-ju, Socrates, Jesus, and Pestalozzi. It praises their selfless sacrifice for humanity, particularly highlighting Pestalozzi's dedication to education despite immense hardship, and urges Korean readers ("friends") to either emulate this spirit of selfless, motherly love or focus sincerely on personal self-cultivation, abandoning quests for empty fame. From a historical perspective, the article reveals internal debates and sensitivities within the Korean intellectual community under colonial rule, touching upon issues of journalistic responsibility, personal reputation (specifically mentioning O Han-yŏng), the dangers of factionalism rooted in "old Chosŏn society," and the use of historical and religious exemplars (both Western and Korean) to inspire moral fortitude and national consciousness among Koreans. The strong defense of the magazine's integrity and the call for selfless dedication or authentic self-improvement reflect the complex pressures and aspirations of Korean intellectuals during this period.

東光 圓卓會議
醫師評判記餘沫     編輯記者

本誌 前號 及 前前號에 揭載된 「醫師評判記」에 대하야 약간의 評判을 들엇다. 人物 評判이니만큼 평판거리가 되는 것은 무리가 아니다. 새삼스러이 잘 쓰고 못쓴 것을 들추어 낼 필요도 없거니와 또 그러랴고도 아니 한다. 오직 그 글 자신의 가진 價値 그대로의 평가를 바랄 뿐이다.
물론 人物 評判記이니 만큼 혹은 諷刺, 혹은 유모어, 혹은 충고 등의 날센 구절이 없으랄 수 없다. 여기 대하야 과연 正鵠을 얻엇는가 못 얻엇는가도 여기서 斷할 것도 아니다. 그것은 筆者의 능력과 독자의 自由判決에 맡길 뿐이다. 그러나 한 가지 明言하고 싶은 것은 뾰족한 붓끝이 휘돌아 갈 때에 혹은 과히 미끄러저 달아나서 오해받을 만한 필치가 된 점이 잇을는지도 모른다. 이 점에 대해서는 俎上에 오른 諸氏의 名譽를 위해서 妄評謝罪의 辭를 첨부 아니할 수 없는 것이다.
그 중에도 특히 畏友 吳漢泳君에게 관계가 되는 文句에 대하야는 原筆者의 의사가 好意的의 충고에 지나지 안는 것이라는 것을 변명삼아 添言하는 동시에 혹은 그 字句를 오해하는 이가 잇다하면 그의 蒙을 啓하고저 하는 바다. 그리고 이미 本意는 그러치 아니 하것마는 오해할 수 잇는 字句가 사용되엇슴에 대하야 同氏에게 관계되는 全文을 취소한다.
최후로 看過치 못할 一件에 대하야 一言을 加하고저 한다. 그것은 전기의 評文의 출현을 好機로 범의 가죽이나 잡아온 듯이 이를 조고만 派爭과 中傷의 거리를 삼으려는 자들이 잇는 것이다. 그들이 無意識的으로 그러는지 의식적으로 그러는지 알 수는 없거니와 말성 많고 偏狹한 舊朝鮮社會의 弊風을 그대로 蹈襲하야 씩뚝걱뚝 잇는 일 없는 일을 做出하는데 이르러 서는 啞然, 憫然, 실로 무어라고 비평할 文字가 생각나지 안는다.
批評의 붓을 잡는 筆者나 이를 揭載하는 編者나 다 오직 공정한 입장에서 공정한 붓으로 공정하고도 獨創的인 글을 실리려고 애쓰는 것이 雜誌業者로서의 生命이다. 그러치 아니한 言論은 자살을 圖하는 것 뿐이다. 「東光」의 言論이 과거에나 현재에나 장래에나 오직 이 方針에 의하야 나간다는 데 잇어서 우리는 일점 良心을 소기는 바가 없다. 사람인지라 물론 過誤는 잇으리라. 과오는 청산하기에 게으른 者가 아니다. 오직 고의적으로 붓대를 휜다는 것은 상상밖에 일이다. 이것은 「東光」의 과거의 歷史가 始終一貫하게 證하는 것이다.
우리는 이 기회에 그들 색안경병자에게 경고를 發한다. 그 有害無益한 逆宣傳을 단연 중지하라고. 만일 그들이 이 쓸데없는 방송을 정지하지 안는다 하면 우리는 다시 筆誅의 붓을 들기를 주저 안는다는 것을 선언한다.

新春寒窓에서      無名居士

「너이가 피리를 불어도 춤추지 아니하며 슬픈 노래를 발하여도 가슴을 치지 안는구나」 이것이야말로 참으로 豫言者의 歎聲이요 아울러 인류의 史的 歷代의 모든 偉人으로의 한 때 생활에 대한 보수가 너무 엷엇든 것을 여실히 言表한 노래다. 그러나 그것은 모다 시대 시대의 代表的 人物로서 받지 아니치 못할 운명인 것이다.
로마의 오쓰데야 門前에 까꾸루 달려 죽으면서도 주께 감사하며 남아잇는 모든 사람들을 위하야 기도한 聖바울을 생각한다. 또 나라에 대한 忠節로 善竹橋에서 맞아 죽은 圃隱鄭先生과 斷首臺에 목이 다라나면서도 자기의 소신을 굴치 아니한 崔水雲을 생각한다. 希臘의 大哲人 쏘크라테쓰는 「그럼 너이들의 생각에 내가 罪가 잇어 그 죄값으로 죽어야 마땅하겟느냐」고 獄門 앞에 ＜118＞ 와서 아무 죄없이 죽는 선생을 위하야 우리 제자를 激勵시키고 죽을 藥사발을 달게 받지 않엇는가!
그리스도여 딴떼여 당신들이 다시 이 시대에 또 이 나라에 나타나신다면 통탄하실 일이 倍前하실 것이외다. 받으실 쓴 잔은 사발에 넘칠 것이외다.
고아와 無産兒童의 어머니요 참 아버지인 페스탈로치의 동정할 수도 없는 그 悲絶慘絶한 敎育的 聖者의 生活을 생각한다. 오직 감사와 찬미할 뿐이다.
위대한 先知者여 豫言者여! 당신들의 이름은 오직 희생 그것이외다. 당신들의 全生活은 인류의 장래를 위햇고 아동을 위햇을 뿐이외다. 自我라는 자신을 위함이 없는 그리스도와 페스탈로치는 그 최후를 십자가와 凍死 그것으로 마추섯다. 그들과 같이 참으로 超人間的 神의 사람들은 모다 衆愚에게 미움받고 嘲弄받고 迫害를 받기 위하야 낫는가? 그들이야말로 대중을 위하야 마음과 뜻과 忠誠을 다 햇건만 無知한 世人은 그들을 죽이고야 말앗다.
