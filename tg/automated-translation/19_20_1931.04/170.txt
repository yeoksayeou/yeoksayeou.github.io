**Humor**

**Present**
"Mother, for your birthday, I'll buy you this pretty vase."
"What? A vase? I already have a good one."
"Mother, that one is gone. I just broke it."

**Welcome**
"Since you go around collecting money, you must be disliked everywhere you go."
"Not at all. Wherever I go, they always tell me to come again next time."

**Poor Person**
"The family that moved in next door this time is so poor."
"How did you know already?"
"Of course, I know. Everything I don't have and go to borrow, they don't have either."

**Dog Excellency**
The American upper class these days tends to put on airs in strange ways. Previously, they used to call dogs Blackie, Spot, Yellow, Mutt, but now they reportedly use honorifics like Salute! Victoria III, Minister Min, His Excellency Yi, Noblewoman Song.

**Suitor**
"You say you didn't propose to my daughter because of her wealth, but I don't know about that."
"But who knows if your house will go bankrupt today?"

**Men Are Affectionate**
Woman: "Look at this. There's a man who gave his entire inheritance to a woman he rejected years ago."
Man: "See? Honey, don't say men are heartless."

**Admonition**
Father: "You shouldn't just go out and play; you need to study. Old Man Edison didn't even glance at things unnecessary for his research goals. Don't waste your energy."
Son: "Father, Mah-jong..."

**To the Very End**
Writer's Wife: "Honey, I'm leaving. We don't even have rice to cook dinner in our house, how can we live like this?"
Writer: "Wait, let me go with you."

**Different Professions**
Woman: "Look here, Mr. Yŏngho, wouldn't you jump into fire for me without hesitation?"
Man: "Is that so necessary, Yŏngae?"
Woman: "Yes, Mr. Yŏngho."
Man: "Then you should marry a firefighter."

**The Car's Fault**
Traffic Policeman: "Hey! Why are you stopped in the middle of the road instead of going?"
Driver: "Look here! Does the car move?"

**How Handsome**
"Hey, look. This morning on Chongno, a female student saw me and laughed. It felt so good."
"Man, even I can't help but laugh when I see you."

**Economy**
Son: "Father, congratulations. In this current recession."
Father: "Congratulations?"
Son: "Yes, I saved money on textbooks. Because I didn't get promoted to the next grade."

**Lie**
Father, who had been waiting to scold his son for lying: "Where did you go last night?"
Son, regretting his lies and determined to become an honest person: "Last night, I went to a restaurant."
Father: "That's a lie, isn't it?"<80>

**Summary**

This article is a collection of short, unrelated jokes and humorous anecdotes reflecting everyday life and social observations in 1931 Korea. The jokes cover topics like family interactions, poverty, courtship, perceived differences between men and women, social status (humorously applied to dogs in America), and coping with economic hardship. From a historical perspective, the jokes offer glimpses into social attitudes, common conversational topics, and the type of humor prevalent during the colonial period. The mention of economic hardship ("recession," "saving" textbook money by failing a grade) and the reference to American social trends (naming dogs with titles) are particularly interesting reflections of the time.

유모어
선물
『어머니, 어머니 생일에는 내 이쁜 꽃병하나 사다드릿게.』
『머? 꽃병? 그건 좋은거 하나 잇는데.』
『어머니 그건 없어요. 내가 이재 깨처뻐렷서요.』

환영
『자네는 돈 받으러 단니니까 가는데 마다 미움만 사겟네 그려.』
『천만에 내가 가면 꼭 이 담에 또 오라고 하는데.』

가난뱅이
『이번 우리 집 옆에 온 집이 어떠케 가난뱅인지.』
『어떠케 발서 알앗나?』
『알고 말고. 내게 없어서 꾸러 가는 것은 죄다 없어.』

개대감
미국 양반게급은 요좀 별한데다 점잔을 빼는 경향이 잇다. 이전에는 개보고 깜둥이 바둑이 노랭이 발바리 하든 것을 지금은 경닙! 빅도리아 삼세 민대감 리각하 송귀부인이라는 경어를 쓰신다고 한다.

구혼자
『자네는 재산을 보고 나의 딸에게 청혼한 것은 아니라고 허지만 원 난 모르겟네.』
『그러치만 오늘 당신집이 파산될는지 누가 압니까.』

남자는 다정
여자 『이것 보세요. 수년전에 배척한 여자에게 유산 전부를 준 남자가 잇대요.』
남자 『자 보시우. 애야 남자는 박정하단 말 마시우.』

훈게
아버지 『나가 놀지만 말고 공부해야 된다. 에디슨옹은 자긔가 연구하는 목적에 필요치 안은데는 눈도 떠 보지 안엇다. 정녁을 남비허지 말어라.』
아들 『아버지 마-쟝은... .』

따끝까지
문사의 처 『아이 나는 가요. 우리 집에 저녁질 쌀도 없으니 어떠케 살아요.』
문사 『가만 나도 같이 갑시다.』

직업이 달나
여자 『이것 보세요. 영호씨, 저를 위해서는 불이라도 헤기지 안코 뛰어 드러가 주시지오?』
남자 『그것이 그러케 필요합니까. 영애.』
여자 『네 영호씨.』
남자 『그러면 당신은 소방부하고 결혼하야 되겟습니다.』

자동차의 잘못
교통순사 『여보 왜 가지 안코 길가운데 섯소.』
운전수 『자 이것보시우. 자동차가 갑니까.』

어찌 잘 낫는지
『이거 보게 오늘 아츰 종로에서 여학생이 나를 보고 웃겟지. 어찌 좋은지.』
『이 사람아. 나도 자네를 보고는 안 웃을 수 없네.』

경제
아들 『아부님 수낫습니다. 지금 이 불경기에.』
아버지 『수가 낫서?』
아들 『네 교과서 살 돈은 경제 경제되어어요. 진급을 못하니까.』

거즛
거즞 말하는 아들을 책망할려고 벼르든 아버지 『너 어디 갓다 왓니. 어제 밤에.』
거즞말 하든 것을 후회하고 졍직한 사람되기를 작정한 아들 『어제밤에는 요리집에 갓대서요.』
아버지 『거즞말이지?』＜80＞
