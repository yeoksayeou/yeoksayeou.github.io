Whither the Farming Villages, What are the Minimum Demands for Rural Emergency Relief, What is the Most Urgent Task for the Rural Movement?

An unprecedented rural crisis (農村恐慌) is dragging Chosŏn's farming villages into extreme poverty. As Chosŏn lives by agriculture, the rural crisis has become a crisis for all of Chosŏn. What is the world coming to? How can we survive? This cannot but be our great, urgent problem of the moment. In this sense, we decided to publish a special issue on the rural problem and listen to opinions from various quarters. We regret that under various constraints, opinions could not be fully expressed, but we would be most fortunate if even just this could serve as some reference.
(Replies listed in order of arrival)

Questions (Abbreviated, optional selection)
One. Personal examples of rural destitution (農村窮況) witnessed by the teacher (先生).
Two. What are the minimum demands for Chosŏn's rural emergency relief (農村救急) at present?
Three. Among the items listed below (左開 各項), which is the most urgent task (最緊急務) at present? And the reason?
(a) Production Increase (기술개량 - technology improvement, 부업장려 - sideline encouragement, 농업다각화 - agricultural diversification, etc.)
(b) Lifestyle Reform (소비절약 - consumption saving, 금주단연 - alcohol/tobacco prohibition, 색의단발 - colorful clothes/bobbed hair, 조혼방지 - early marriage prevention, etc.)
(c) Cooperative Movement (협동조합운동)
(d) Literacy Movement (식자운동)
(e) Tenant Farming Movement (소작운동) (대지주항쟁 - struggle against landlords, 소작조합운동 - tenant union movement, etc.)
Four. Other thoughts on the rural problem.

The Inevitability of the Tenant Farming Movement
Singanhoe Headquarters, Yi Hangbal (李恒發)

One. As over 80% of Chosŏn's population are farmers, it would not be an exaggeration to say that the rural crisis is the crisis of the Chosŏn masses. However, the third-stage world economic crisis (世界 經濟恐慌) has caused the prices of agricultural products in Chosŏn<46>, a backward agricultural nation, to fall endlessly, so it is a decisive fact that the farmers' destitution has reached its peak.

Two. Minimum Demands for Rural Emergency Relief
a. Establishment of tenant rights (小作權).
b. Reduction or exemption of tenant fees (小作料).
c. Acquisition of the right to organize (團結權).
d. Abolition of water taxes (水稅), or landlord burden.
e. Abolition of舎音 (Saŭm - likely a form of corvée labor or levy).
f. Establishment of free clinics (無料治療所).
g. Free cultivation for fire-field farmers (火田民).
h. Abolition of free labor from tenants to landlords (小作人의 무료 勞働).
i. Landlord burden for fertilizer (肥料) and seeds (種穀).
j. Increase in land productivity.

This cannot but rely on the improvement of agricultural technology or the intensive increase of farmer labor power. However, in Chosŏn, it inevitably becomes an intensive increase in farmer labor power, which can only become an increase in the means of farmer exploitation, thus it must be rejected.

b. Lifestyle Improvement (生活改善)
This is a problem for after life stabilizes; clamoring for it now is ultimately nothing more than a kind of lamentation (悲鳴曲) saying not to work.

c. Cooperative Movement (協同組合運動)
Originally, within capitalist countries, this movement is a reformist movement that obliterates XX consciousness, so it is not an issue.

d. Tenant Farming Movement (小作運動)
This movement can achieve final victory only through the farmers' organizational power, under the leadership of the working class, by XX-ing. Since farmers cannot constitute a single class in capitalist society, they are inevitably bound to follow this process.

Cooperatives are Most Crucial
Chosŏn Ilbo, An Chaehong (安在鴻)

One. Although I grew up in a farming village and still have my permanent domicile (原籍) there, I am always away from the village, so I have witnessed little destitution (窮況). However, just from what I have occasionally seen:
(a) I saw people who, right after threshing in the fall, paid off all their debts to others, turned around holding only a broom (箒) in the yard, yet still had remaining debts. If they carried those over, the old debts just grew with interest, becoming new debts.
(b) The village attached to where I live has over 50 households. Even in an average year, four or five households run out of food before the Lunar New Year. However, this village is said to be quite well-off.
(c) Spring poverty (春窮) makes the spring season very difficult. Years ago, I went to my country home and saw a young daughter-in-law from the village who, after starving, had gathered and boiled Scilla bulbs (무릇), then drank the lye (간수) and committed suicide. It was a suicide out of despair (悲觀自殺).
(d) I often see sturdy young men walking around slumped over with pale faces and freckles (시루주근). They say it's from starvation.
(e) Not a few people spend the three winter months (三冬) without bedding. They just light a fire in the ondol floor (구둘) and sleep huddled together.<47>
(f) Lack of access to medicine (醫藥) must be a great worry. The morbidity rate (有病率) in Chosŏn's farming villages is likely very high. I often see certain diseases constantly circulating within a specific area without ever leaving.

Two. As for the minimum for emergency relief in Chosŏn's farming villages right now, I have no viable good plan. In any case, the price of rice (米價) must be high for Chosŏn landlords above the small-medium level, as well as owner-farmers (自作農) and tenant farmers (小作農), to have a chance, but the rice price remains慘落 (miserably low), and measures to raise it are not easy.

Three. Regarding the items listed below (左開 諸項):
(a) Production Increase: Technology improvement, sideline encouragement, agricultural diversification, etc., would all be good, in any era.
(b) Lifestyle Reform would also be necessary. Let's do everything that is necessary and possible.
(c) However, I believe the Cooperative Movement centered on consumer cooperatives (消費組合) is most crucial. It would be good to even create training institutions for cooperative leaders, including graduates from high schools (高普) and professional schools (專門校), and actively promote this.
(d) The Literacy Movement is also very good. The Chosŏn Ilbo Company (朝鮮日報社), where I work, is also putting great effort into this project.
(e) The Tenant Farming Movement will be necessary, both for its own organization and for all kinds of self-defense measures. It is a point for intellectuals (識者) to note that tenant disputes (小作爭議) in Chosŏn mostly arise defensively out of excessive tenant fees (小作料 暴徵) or unreasonable changes in tenancy rights (作權移動). Therefore, it will generally be necessary.

Lifestyle Reform and Consumption Saving
Pyongyang Christian Youth Association (YMCA)
Cho Mansik (曹晩植)

Among the items in the third question, I consider the second item, Lifestyle Reform, specifically Consumption Saving and Alcohol Prohibition (禁酒), to be the most essential and urgent issues in our farming villages currently. And regarding this Consumption Saving, I particularly want to address saving consumption related to coming-of-age/weddings (冠婚) and funerals/ancestral rites (喪祭).

1. Consumption Saving for Weddings (冠婚)
Regarding coming-of-age/weddings, specifically the sending of wedding gifts (送釆) and wedding banquets (婚宴), there has long been a habit of excessive spending (濫費) in our farming villages. Some farming households with means and some余裕 (surplus) prepare for sending gifts and banquets by diligently accumulating money, cloth, grain, etc., for several years and manage the event with this. However, such households are extremely few, and it is common to sell or mortgage fields (田土) and forests (山林) to cover the costs of sending gifts and banquets. Indeed, selling or mortgaging land to hold weddings seems to be considered a customary (依例) and natural thing.
It is said that 30 to 40 percent of land transfers among middle-class farmers (中農) and below (小農) are caused by these weddings. How can this be considered a small problem for the approximately 2.8 million farming households?
Although this question specifically targets farming villages, so I speak only of them, how can one fully describe the extravagance regarding weddings, which is many times greater among city dwellers than farmers?
No matter how much sending gifts and banquets are considered great family events (大事) and joyous occasions (喜慶), <48> it is utterly unacceptable to suddenly alter the source and foundation of life to be passed down through generations (代代 子孫計), the land, for this event, despite being able to save on consumption. Moreover, weddings are events that almost every family will likely have, and they don't necessarily stop at one or two instances but can be repeated several times.
Therefore, let us reform the habit of extravagant spending on weddings. Let us eradicate (根除) that notion. Looking at the state of our collapsing families and impoverished farming villages, the way to reform the habit of extravagance for weddings seems feasible for religious groups and organizations, for instance, Christian and Cheondoist (天道敎) groups, to regulate extravagance through rules (規定) or resolutions (決議). In other farming villages, it seems feasible to organize groups like Morals Improvement Societies (矯風會), Temperance Societies (節制會), Rural Promotion Societies (農村振興會), Village Associations (洞會), etc., and regulate this through the power of these organizations.

2. Consumption Saving for Funerals and Ancestral Rites (喪祭)
These funeral matters (喪事) and ancestral rites (祭禮) generally concern the final affairs related to one's parents (親上), so discussing consumption saving regarding them feels very daunting (惶悚). However, in reality, saving more than the expenses for weddings would be desirable. In fact, wedding expenses involve purchasing clothes, chests/desks (籠机), utensils (器皿), and other furniture (家具), so to some extent, they are necessary for the children's family life. But regarding funerals and rites, customs aside, it is known that much extravagance occurs unwillingly (不得已) due to being swayed by appearances (體面) and etiquette (禮貌).
Here, let us be wary of multi-day funerals (多日葬) and abolish the major and minor anniversary rites (大小祥). I have personally witnessed (目覩) several people who sold their farm cattle (農牛) and fields (庄土) for these events and moved to Northwest Jiandao (西北間島).
Let's not be constrained by conventional, conceptual rituals (因襲的 觀念的 儀式) and focus on the actual life we face. All our circumstances today do not permit (許) extravagance in such matters.
The method for reforming the habit of extravagance related to funerals and rites would be the same as for weddings.

3. The Alcohol Prohibition Problem (禁酒問題)
Nothing impoverishes farmers (貧窮) and devastates farming villages (疲廢) more than alcohol (술). How can one list all the farmers who ruin their families (敗家) and become displaced (流離) due to alcohol, or the villages that become deserted (廢洞) due to alcohol?
It would not be an exaggeration to say that the prosperity or decline (榮枯休戚) of a farming village is directly proportional to the number of liquor stores (酒店). That is, in any farming village, if the number of liquor stores is small, it declines (衰敗) a little less, and if it's large, it declines much more.
Therefore, in the rural movement, that is, rural relief (農村救濟), I keenly feel that the Temperance Movement (禁酒運動) is the most urgent task above all else.
In short, although these issues of consumption saving and alcohol prohibition are certainly passive (消極的) and awkward to discuss, the amount spent on such things in our farming villages is steadily increasing, and alcohol consumption (飮酒量) is gradually rising, seeming like a plan to use up all the remaining assets before stopping. That is why preventing this, though passive, is considered urgent (緊切) and a pressing task (急務).

First, the Tenant Farming Movement
Cheondogyo Youth Friends Party (天道敎靑友黨)
Kim Kijŏn (金起田)

One. Last spring, while staying for about two weeks in a farming village in Cheongju County (淸州郡), North Chungcheong Province (忠北), I saw an example of the actual rural destitution.
First, there was nothing to eat. It was spring then, <49> and the women (婦女) of each household were fully mobilized, gathering wild greens (나물) in the mountains. They were making those mountain greens (山菜) their regular food. The able-bodied men (壯丁), the farm army (農軍) out plowing the fields, couldn't eat properly and, due to fatigue (疲勞), often stopped their cows (牛) to rest. The faces of all the men, women, old, and young coming and going showed signs of hunger (주린 빛). Whenever even two people gathered, the talk was about being unable to survive. It was truly unbearable destitution. High tenant fees (小作料), high-interest loans (高利 貸借) of money or grain, the burden of corvée labor (夫役) and various other duties (諸負擔), changes in tenancy rights (作權), purchasing foreign goods (外來商品), etc., were utterly destroying today's Chosŏn farming villages.

Two. Shall we say: enactment of a Tenant Law (小作法) with content such as a maximum tenant fee of 45%, absolute guarantee of tenant rights (小作權), legal recognition of tenant unions (小作組合), etc.; implementation of effective rice price adjustment policies (米價調節政策); circulation of low-interest financing (低利金融); legal suppression of high-interest loans (高利貸金); implementation of the Cooperative Movement, etc.?

Three. I believe the following three are particularly urgent (最緊).
First. Tenant Farming Movement (may differ by region).
Second. Production Increase. Especially, agricultural diversification is needed.
Third. Cooperative Association.

Four. Land to the tiller (耕者有其田).
Everything is power (힘).

Extremely Low-Interest Fund Circulation
Tonga Ilbo, Song Chinu (宋鎭禹)

One. Although I haven't seen it myself recently, there's no room for seeing or not seeing, is there? The other day, I heard the following from a friend (親故). He harvests 5,000 sŏk (石), which at 6 wŏn per sŏk, amounts to 30,000 wŏn. From this, after deducting 9,000 wŏn in public levies (公課金) and all other miscellaneous expenses required for income, his actual income (實收入) was only 10,000 wŏn. Then, someone harvesting 1,000 sŏk would have an actual income of 2,000 wŏn, and someone harvesting 100 sŏk would have an actual income of merely 200 wŏn. Monthly income is 20 wŏn. How can a whole family eat, dress, and live on this? If someone harvesting 100 sŏk is like this, needless to say, the lives of owner-farmers and tenant farmers are beyond words. If they still practiced self-sufficiency (自作自給) like before, money wouldn't flow out, but looking at the consumption side, they now live on non-Chosŏn products, and looking at the production side, the income of owner-farmers and tenant farmers is far less than their expenditures. So, Chosŏn's farming villages are not just in destitution (窮狀) but in a tragic state (慘狀) that surpasses destitution.

Two. To provide emergency relief for Chosŏn's farming villages:
1. Circulation of several million wŏn in extremely low-interest funds (極低利資金) - rather than just low-interest funds, the circulation of funds not necessarily intended for recovery is absolutely necessary.
2. Reduction of public levies (公課金), deferment (猶豫) of payments for water taxes (水稅), farm debts (農債), and all other debts are needed for farmers to survive.

Three. All are necessary, but among them, Production Increase is more necessary.

Landlords Also Bankrupt
Tonga Ilbo, Kim U-p'yŏng (金佑枰)

One. As a person from a farming village and also one interested <51> in economic issues, I do not simply listen and think casually whenever I go to the countryside. Thus, although I cannot say it is complete, let me tell you about the rural destitution I have witnessed.
It is no exaggeration to say that landlords (地主), owner-farmers (自作農), and tenant farmers (小作人) are all on the verge of bankruptcy (破産). Let me explain the reasons and extent.
One might think landlords have余裕 (leeway) in life, but it's different from imagination. Let me explain this using an abacus (算盤). Generally, a farming household harvesting 1,000 sŏk is called rich (부자). If we calculate the public levies (公課金) they pay annually for harvesting 1,000 sŏk, it amounts to 2 wŏn 50 chŏn to 3 wŏn 50 chŏn per sŏk. Then, based on this year's market price, valuing 1 kŭn at 3 chŏn to 3 chŏn 8 ri, the price per sŏk (1石價) is only 6 wŏn to 6 wŏn 50 chŏn. Subtracting various expenses (諸費用) from this, the actual income (實收入) is merely 3,000-4,000 wŏn. How could those who used to live on over 10,000 wŏn a year when unhulled rice (籾) was 14-15 wŏn per sŏk live on 3,000-4,000 wŏn? Their only way of life, other than debt (부채), is no other path (他途). At this rate, one cannot but say they are on the verge of bankruptcy rather than just struggling.
Owner-farmers harvesting 30-40 sŏk are said to face a somewhat different level of threat compared to landlords. The reason is said to be that they, unlike the so-called rich, can adjust their living standards somewhat arbitrarily and have adaptability (順應性) based on their income. However, the rice price has plummeted by more than half compared to before, and the harvest volume (石數) has not increased in direct proportion (正比例). One might say other commodity prices (他物價) have also fallen (低落), but it is a fact that they have not fallen as much as the rice price. Accordingly, various public levies remain the same, so it is a fact that they naturally cannot escape hardship (窮境). (Whether they have debt or not is a personal matter, so it was not included in the lifestyle of landlords or owner-farmers.)
Tenant farmers seem to be slightly better off compared to landlords and owner-farmers. Their living standard has more abundant freedom (自由) for adjustment (伸縮) than landlords and owner-farmers, and various public levies amount to only an extremely small sum (極히 少額). Since the amount of grain (石數) they acquire has increased compared to before, their hardship (窮形) will naturally be less compared to landlords and owner-farmers. Therefore, it is said that tenant farmers without debt are somewhat better off than before.
(Although what I've said above may seem like a theory rather than a real example, it is a factual account (事實談) that simply omits the names of the unfortunate.)

Two. The minimum demands for Chosŏn farmers' emergency relief at present are as follows:
1. Secure a policy to raise the rice price (米價引上策) by whatever means.
2. Postponement (延期) of payment for various public levies (諸公課金).
3. Deferment (猶豫) of payment for farm household debts (農家負債).
4. Reduction (減下) of farm household borrowing taxes/fees (農家借用稅 - unclear term, possibly interest or fees on loans).
5. Increase (增加) in the low-interest financing amount per sŏk (石當低資融通額).
6. Relief (救濟) for the destitute poor (窮貧者) by initiating public works projects (共公事業起工).

Three. The most urgent task (最緊 急務) for farming villages at present should be increasing the productivity (生産力增進) of agriculturalists, that is, diversifying farmers' income (農民의 收入 多角化). Production increase can be divided into items such as (1) technology improvement, sideline encouragement, or agricultural diversification. Not only that, but increasing productivity also implies the rationalization of labor, meaning the economization of labor. We think that only by resolving the current agricultural population surplus can we realize the rationalization of farmer labor. In this sense, <52> I believe that even increasing the productivity of agriculturalists, if not accompanied by the rationalization of labor, might come to naught (水泡에 歸하다).
Next, the Cooperative Movement will be necessary. On one hand, increase productivity, and on the other hand, strengthen the farmers' bargaining power (買賣力 - 빠게인 - bargain) to acquire the main force for market control (市場統制). Thus, though it may sound inadequate (구차하나마), through these two means—actively planning production increase and passively conducting the Cooperative Movement as a self-defense measure (自衛策)—we should proceed.

Cooperative Movement is First
Chosŏn Nongmin Sa (Korea Farmer Company), Kim Ildae (金一大)

One. During the tour of various county Farmer Associations (各郡 農民社) in the Western Region (關西地方) in December last year, there were only examples of rural destitution witnessed, so I will omit details as it was long ago, but at that time, tenant farmers had already used up every last grain of food.

Two. The minimum demand for Chosŏn's rural emergency relief at present is to provide loans of farm rations (農糧) to eat until the barley/wheat (牟麥) harvest season.

Three. The Most Urgent Task at present is:
First. Cooperative Movement
Reason: a. Production Increase, without sales channels (販路), will be disadvantageous, similar to how the increased rice harvest (米穀增收) last year turned out unfavorable.
b. Lifestyle Reform is an indirect movement done while maintaining the current state of life (生活現狀).
c. The Literacy Movement also requires some surplus after eating.
d. Tenant Farming Movement: Considering the reality of Chosŏn, where even landlords are mostly collapsing, unless targeting large foreign-owned farms (外人의 大農場), the tenant movement conversely hastens the collapse of small landlords (小地主).
Therefore, to secure the immediate interests (當面利益) of the farmer masses and provide even a modicum of relief for the current tragic state of farming villages, the Cooperative Movement is necessary. There is an order here too: First, the Consumer Cooperative Movement (消費組合運動); Second, the Producer Cooperative Movement (生産組合運動).

Four. As I have submitted a separate manuscript (追稿), I will omit details here, but since Chosŏn is an agricultural country (農業國), all the strength of Chosŏn society originates from the farming villages. Therefore, I believe it is the best strategy (第一策) for all social activists (社會活動家 諸氏) to focus their efforts on the rural problem.

All Five Conditions Necessary
Chosŏn Ilbo, Yŏm Sangsŏp (렴상섭)

One. (a) I remember being quite shocked, as someone raised in the city (都會 生長), when I saw village women (村婦), especially young wives (小婦), wandering around with pale, swollen faces (누렇게 부황이 떠서) during the spring poverty (春窮期) in the North Pyeongan (平北) region about 10 years ago. Needless to say, it was because they were sustaining themselves (延命) not with grass roots and tree bark (草根木皮), but with something similar – dried radish green porridge (시레기 죽).
(b) Another thing, this past New Year's Eve, the so-called sŏttal taemok (섯달대몫 - end-of-year settlement time), I didn't witness it myself but heard directly that someone from a poor farmer's (貧農) house in the suburbs (近郊) about 10 ri (里) from here came into the city to borrow (꾸으랴) 2 sŭng (升) of white rice (白米) from an acquaintance (知人). Perhaps they had already borrowed from everyone possible in their vicinity and had nowhere else to ask (開口). But it's an unbelievably absurd fact that this happened in a farming village, unlike in the city, and even before the spring poverty arrived, in a year called a great harvest (大豊). <53>

Two. I grew up in a poor urban family (都會의 貧家) and am too ignorant of the actual situation in farming villages. I only recently became interested in the rural problem, so I am ashamed (부끄럽습니다) to offer my opinion.

Three. It seems right for all five items (5개條項) to proceed together, and doing so wouldn't cause any conflict among them. However, regarding the item "Early Marriage Prevention" (早婚防止), I wonder if birth control (産兒制限) might be the issue instead. But again, without being familiar with the actual circumstances (實際 情況) and statistics (統計), it's difficult to assert this definitively.

"Rural Cooperatives" and Joint Responsibility
Yonhi College (延禧專門), Yi Sundŏk (李順鐸)

One. Among my close relatives (친척), one person used to farm and live in a village called A years ago. In that region, land prices were relatively high, and land yield (土地 所出) was relatively low. After paying land taxes (地稅), other miscellaneous taxes (雜稅), and fertilizer costs (肥料代) with the fruits of a year's bloody sweat (汗血), it wasn't enough to cover a year's expenses (計量거리). As the years went by, debt (빗) accumulated, gradually eroding the principal (元本) of the land. Thinking that at this rate, he would inevitably be left with empty fists (빈주먹) in a few years, he decided to change his thinking and look for a place where land prices were relatively cheap and yields were relatively high. As a result, he found a place called T and decided to move (移居) there. He sold his land and house in region A, even sold all his movable property (動産), and left. Since he was my relative and could pass through Kyŏngsŏng (京城 - Seoul) on his way to region T, he visited me and we had various conversations (談話), so I knew well. It has been five years since then. How much capital (全 자금) did he take at that time? After selling everything in his previous residence, he had 2,800 wŏn. After spending money on travel expenses (路費), about 2,400 wŏn remained. He didn't waste (浪費) a single penny elsewhere and took it all, bought a house, 44 turak (斗落 - unit of land area) of paddy field (논), and some dry field (田), becoming a respectable owner-farmer (自作 中農). He was extremely happy, and even I was happy for him. What was the result? About a month ago, he stopped by Kyŏngsŏng, carrying his belongings with his wife (男負女帶), on his way back to his hometown (故鄕). Thinking it unexpected, I asked about the whole story (始終). He said that after farming in region T for the first year, the yield was substantial, and the whole family rejoiced, ate well, and lived well. But from the next year, things like a Water Utilization Association (水利組合) were established there, and water taxes (水稅) were levied at the 4th grade level. After paying land tax and water tax from the year's yield, only enough remained to live on for 6 months. He borrowed money (빗) to cover the remaining 6 months. The following year, after paying land tax, water tax, and interest on the previous year's debt from the yield, only enough remained to live on for barely 3 months. What happened the year after that? After paying land tax, water tax, debt interest, and fertilizer costs, absolutely nothing was left. So, eventually, he mortgaged his land and house, obtained some money and goods (金品), and farmed last year. But last year, due to the general recession (不景氣) and the rural crisis, he barely managed to pay the land tax but couldn't pay the water tax. The Water Utilization Association confiscated (入庫) the entire yield. He couldn't repay the debt. Calculating his losses (打算), he realized that even if he sold the land this year, there would be no buyers, and due to the low price (低價), there would likely be no surplus (剩餘) after paying off the debt. So, he gave all the land to the creditor (債務者), sold the house to cover travel expenses, and is now returning <54> shamefully (브끄럼을 무릅쓰고) to his hometown A. He said there's probably no way forward in his hometown, but he plans to rely on relatives and old acquaintances (親戚 故舊), maybe get some tenant land (小作地) to pass the time (소일). I think such cases are not isolated incidents (非一非再); the situation in our Chosŏn farming villages largely involves similar circumstances (大同小異한 상태).

Two. For owner-farmers, on one hand, abolish or reduce various imposed levies (諸賦課金); on the other hand, open paths for low-interest financing (低利金融). And for tenant farmers, lower tenant fees (小作料) to the minimum possible level.

Three. It is difficult to single out one item from this question as the urgent task. Why? Because the various items cannot individually become the urgent task. Therefore, I want to say this:
A. In terms of Production (生産上), sideline encouragement (副業獎勵) is the most urgent task.
B. In terms of Consumption (消費上), complete abolition of alcohol and tobacco (酒草 全廢), encouragement of colorful clothing (色衣 獎勵 - likely meaning modern/Western dress, contrasting with traditional white), and prevention of early marriage (早婚防止) are the most urgent tasks.
C. In terms of Social Movement (社會運動上), the Cooperative Movement is urgent. But saying it like this is too vague (막연함). So, among cooperatives, I particularly want to promote Agricultural Cooperatives (農業組合) and pursue various movements based on joint responsibility (共同連帶責任) with the village (촌락) as the unit. Therefore, I think it is urgent to carry out a kind of Rural Cooperative Movement (農村組合運動) involving joint financing, joint acquisition of farmland (耕作地), joint purchase of farm tools (農具) and fertilizer (肥料), joint sale of crops (作物), joint farming (耕作), and joint payment of tenant fees.

Yonhi College (延禧專門), No Tonggyu (盧東奎)

Two. Reduction, exemption (減免), deferred payment (延納), or payment based on the price of staple grains (正租) or other major agricultural products for rural burdens (農村負擔) (租稅 - taxes, 普通學校授業料 - elementary school tuition, 農會 기타 組合費 - agricultural association and other cooperative fees, 各種 寄附金 - various donations, 農債 기타 각종 債務 元利金 - farm debt and other debt principal/interest, etc.).

However, correcting the unfairness of distribution (分配) to increase the income of middle (中農) and poor farmers (貧農), who constitute the vast majority (多大數) of the rural population, is the most urgent task. Four. It is true that we always abstractly cry out about the rural crisis or rural collapse (農村破滅). It is also true that we have already lost sensitivity (感受性) to some extent to this abstract buzzword. Furthermore, it's true that abstract arguments (抽象論) are not only useless but actually obstacles (妨害物) in recognizing the essence (本質) of the rural problem and discussing its countermeasures (對策). Therefore, we first need to investigate the essence of the peasant economy (農民經濟), a non-capitalistic management entity (非資本主義的 經營軆) within capitalist society, and the essence of the current rural crisis. Second, we need to discard the incomplete or fragmented knowledge (斷片的 知識) about farming villages obtained through government statistics (官廳統計) and newspaper pages (新聞紙面) and feel the urgent need to actually <55> investigate the true state (眞相) of farming villages and the reality (實相) of peasant life.

If there is a major problem in Chosŏn at present, it must be the rural problem. To this extent, the farming village is a crucial issue for us and directly holds the fate of life and death (死活).

To scientifically increase production and reform lifestyle, everything depends on self-awakening (스사로 깨달음). One acts only after knowing something. Quickly Towards Commerce and Industry
Yonhi College (延禧專門), Ch'oe Sunju (崔淳周)

The rural problem is so broad and important that it could be called the economic life problem of all Chosŏn people. This is because 80% of Chosŏn people are farmers, and over 90% of them live in farming villages with populations under 2,500. One. An example of rural destitution can be seen in a fact reported in the newspapers (新聞紙上) at the end of last year. "The number of seizures (差押件數) is 315,000." Of course, these seizures didn't all occur only in farming villages, but they are largely related. And the situation is progressively worsening.

Looking at the income estimate (收入豫算) of a landlord owning 10 chŏngbo, their fields and paddies produce 200 to 300 sŏk of unhulled rice (籾). Income

800 wŏn to 1,200 wŏn
Expenditure

Therefore, I believe Chosŏn's rural problem will be resolved by taking these various conditions (諸條件) into consideration. <59>

**Summary**

This 1931 survey from *Donggwang* magazine gathers opinions from various Korean intellectuals and activists on the severe agricultural crisis devastating rural Chosŏn. Respondents detail extreme poverty, starvation, debt cycles, and exploitative tenant conditions. Proposed solutions range widely: radical calls for tenant movements challenging the socio-economic structure (Yi Hangbal, Yi Chuyŏn), emphasis on cooperative movements for economic self-defense (An Chaehong, Kim Ildae, Ham Sanghun), advocacy for lifestyle reforms like temperance and frugality (Cho Mansik), the need for increased production and diversification (Song Chinu, Kim U-p'yŏng), the fundamental importance of literacy (Cho Tuso, Im Insik), and even a suggestion to shift towards commerce and industry (Ch'oe Sunju). The responses reflect diverse ideological leanings, from moderate reformism to more radical, potentially class-based analyses, highlighting the depth and complexity of the crisis and the search for solutions under colonial rule.

For historians, this article provides a valuable snapshot of elite discourse on the rural crisis during a critical period of global depression impacting colonial Korea. The diverse viewpoints, ranging from practical economic measures (cooperatives, finance) to social reforms (lifestyle, literacy) and radical political action (tenant struggles), illustrate the spectrum of nationalist and social thought. The frank descriptions of poverty, the specific grievances against landlords and the state (taxes, water fees), and the references to broader economic forces (world depression, capitalism) offer rich material for understanding the lived reality and ideological debates within Korean society in the early 1930s. The censored words in some responses hint at the political sensitivities surrounding class struggle and anti-capitalist critique under Japanese surveillance.

生活改新과 消費節約
平壤基督靑年會
曹晩植

第3 設問의 몇 項目 중 나는 第二項의 生活改新 거긔서도 消費節約과 禁酒를 目下 우리 농촌에서 가장 切要하고 긴급한 문제라고 생각합니다. 그러고 이 消費節約에 대하야는 특히 冠婚, 喪祭에 대한 消費節約을 말하려 합니다.
1. 冠婚에 대한 消費節約
冠婚 즉 送釆와 婚宴에 잇어서 종래로 우리 농촌에서 너무 濫費하는 습관이 잇어 왓다. 규모가 잇고 다소의 여유가 잇는 農家에서는 送釆와 婚宴에 대한 준비로 수년간 금전 필육, 穀物 등을 잘 축적하엿다가 이것으로 그 일을 츠려서 나가는 집이 혹 잇다. 그러나 이러한 집은 其 수가 극히 적고 흔이는 田土와 山林을 팔거나 저당하여 가지고 送釆와 婚宴을 츠리는 것이 보통이다. 과연 送釆나 婚宴하기 위하야 토지를 放賣 또는 저당하는 일은 依例외 件이요 당연한 일로 아는 모양이다.
中農 이하 小農들의 토지의 이동을 生케 하는 원인은 30퍼센트 내지 40퍼센트가 이 冠婚으로 인하여 된다 한다. 280여 만戶의 農家를 위하야 이 어찌 적은 문제라 할 것인가.
이 設問은 특히 농촌을 대상으로 하엿기 때문에 농촌만을 말하지마는 이 冠婚에 대한 濫費야말로 도시사람은 농민보다 몇배나 더 濫비함을 어찌 다 말하랴.
아무리 送釆와 婚宴을 가정의 大事요 喜慶이라 할지라도 此에＜48＞ 대한 소비를 능히 절약할 수 잇슴에 불구하고 代代 子孫計할 생활의 원천이요 기본인 토지를 이 일로 인하야 一朝에 변동시킴은 도저히 못 할 일이다. 더구나 이 冠婚의 일은 어느 가정이나 거의 다 잇을만 한 일이요 잇서도 반드시 한 두번에 멎지도 안코 몇번을 거듭할 수도 잇는 일이다.
그러기 때문에 우리는 冠婚에 대한 濫費의 습관을 개혁하자. 그 관념을 根除하자. 쓰러저가는 우리 가정, 피폐한 우리 농촌의 현상을 보아 이 冠婚에 대한 濫費의 습관을 개혁하는 방책은 종교, 단체, 예컨댄 기독교와 天道敎같은 단체에서는 此에 대한 規定 又는 決議로 濫費를 制裁함이 可하고, 不然한 농촌에서는 각기 矯風會, 節制會, 農村振興會, 洞會 등의 단체를 조직하야 어 단체의 힘으로 此를 制裁함이 可할 듯하다.
2. 喪祭에 대한 消費節約
이 喪事와 祭禮는 대체가 親上의 最終에 관한 일인지라 此에 대한 消費節約을 云云함은 매우 惶悚된 일이다. 그러나 실제에 잇어서는 冠婚에 관한 비용 이상으로 절약함이 可할 것이다. 사실 冠婚 비용은 衣服, 籠机, 器皿, 기타 家具 등의 구입임으로 어떤 정도까지는 자녀의 가정생활상 필요함도 잇는 것이다. 그러나 喪祭에 관하야는 습관도 습관이려니와 대개 체면과 禮貌에 끌리워서 不得已 濫費함이 많은 줄 안다.
여긔에 잇어서 우리는 多日葬을 戒하며 大小祥을 廢하자. 이 일로 인하야 農牛를 팔며 庄土를 팔아서 소비하고 西北間島로 가는 사람을 여러 사람 나는 目覩하엿다.
因襲의 觀念的 儀式에 拘碍되지 말고 직면한 실제생활에 착안하자. 오늘 우리의 모든 형편은 우리로 하여금 이러한 일에 대한 濫費를 許치 아니한다.
이 喪祭에 관한 濫費의 습관을 개혁하는 방책은 冠婚의 그것과 같을 것이다.
3. 禁酒問題
농민을 貧窮케 하며 농촌을 피폐케 함에는 술 이상이 없다. 술로 말미여 敗家하고 流離하는 농민, 술로 말미여 廢洞되는 촌락, 그것을 어찌 다 들어 말할 수 잇으랴.
농촌의 榮枯休戚은 酒店수에 정비례에 의한다고 하여도 과언이 안일 것이다. 즉 어느 농촌이나 酒店수가 적으면 좀 적게 녹(衰敗)고 많으면 푹 많이 녹아난나.
그러기 때문에 농촌운동 즉 농촌구제에 잇어서는 무엇보다도 禁酒運動이 가장 급무인 것을 절실히 느낀다.
요컨댄 이 소비절약과 禁酒問題는 물론 소극적이요 말하기도 구차하지마는 우리 농촌에서 이런데 쓰는 消費高는 점점 더 많아 가고 飮酒量은 차차 더 늘어가면서 아직 좀 남아잇는 재산을 다 쓰고 마서서 없이 하구야 말랴는 작정 간애 뵌다. 그래서 이것을 방지함이 소극적이지마는 緊切하고 急務라고 하는 바이다.

地主도 破産
東亞日報 金佑枰

一. 本人은 농촌사람이며 겸하야 경제문제에 흥미를 가지는＜51＞ 한 사람으로 농촌에 갈 때마다 평범하게 둣고 생각하지를 아니합니다. 그리하야 완전하다고는 하지 못하겟지마는 本人이 目睹한 農村窮況을 말하자면 이러합니다.
地主, 自作農 及 小作人을 물론하고 전부가 파산 지경에 잇다고 하야도 과언이 아닙니다. 그 이유 그 정도를 말하겟습니다.
지주라고 하면 생활의 여유가 잇을 듯하나 상상과는 다릅니다. 算盤을 가지고 이것을 설명하면 이러합니다. 대개 1천石 추수하는 農家라 하면 부자라고 합니다. 그러면 그네가 추수 1천石을 함으로 매년 納入하는 公課金을 계산하야 1石당 얼마나 되냐하면 2원 50전 내지 3원 50전이나 됩니다. 그러면 금년가튼 시세로 每斤 3전 내지 3전 8厘의 가격으로 1石價를 評定하면 6원 내지 6원 50전밖에 되지 안습니다. 이에서 諸費用을 減하면 實收入이 겨우 3,4천원에 불과하게 됩니다. 在來 籾 1石 14,5원하든 시절 1년 만여 원으로 생활하든 그네가 3,4천원으로 어찌 생활하겟습니까. 그네의 유일한 생활방도는 부채 이외에는 他途가 엇읍니다. 이러고야 궁하다는 것보다 파산지경이라 아니 할 수 없읍니다.
自作으로서 三四十石 秋收한다는 農家는 지주에 비하야 다소 그 위협을 받는 정도는 다르다고 합니다. 그 이유로는 그네는 그 소위 부자와 같지 안어 생활정도의 신축을 다소임의로 하야 그네의 수입에 의한 順應性이 잇는 까닭이라 하겟으나 米價는 전에 비하야 반이상에 暴落되엇으며 추수 石數는 此에 正比例하야 증가되지 못하엿습니다. 그러나 他物價도 低落이 되엇으니까 하겟지마는 米價와 같이 低落되지 아니 한 것이 사실이며 따라서 諸公課金은 여전하니 自然窮境을 면치 못할 것이 사실이외다.(負債有無는 개인적관계인 고로 지주나 自作農의 생활에 가입하지 안엇다.)
小作人으로 말하면 지주와 自作農에 비하야 좀더 낳은 것 같습니다. 그네의 생활정도는 地主와 自作農보다도 伸縮에 대한 자유가 풍부하며 諸公課金은 극히 少額에 불과한데 획득 石數는 전보다 증가되엇음으로 자연히 지주와 自作農에게 비하야 그 窮形이 적게 될 것입니다. 그럼으로 小作人으로 채무나 없는 사람은 전에 비하야 좀 생활이 나아젓다고들 합니다.
(이상 말한 것이 實例처럼 생각되지 안코 한 理論같이 생각되나 이것이 不運者의 성명만을 쓰지 아니한 事實談입니다)
二. 目下 朝鮮農民救急의 최소한도의 요구는 이러합니다.
1. 어떠한 政策으로든지 米價引上策을 求得할 事.
2. 諸公課金納入 延期.
3. 農家負債支拂 猶豫.
4. 農家借用稅 減下.
5. 石當低資融通額 增加.
6. 共公事業起工에 의하야 窮貧者 救濟.
三. 目下 農村의 最緊 急務는 農業者의 생산력 증진 즉 농민의 수입 다각화일 것입니다. 생산증진이라 하면 (1) 기술개량, 副業獎勵라든지 농업다각화 등이라는 項目으로 分할 수 잇겟지요. 뿐만 아니라 생산력증진에는 노동의 합리화, 즉 노동의 經濟化를 의미하게도 되는 것입니다. 우리는 現下 농업인구 과잉을 해결함으로 비로소 농민노동의 합리화를 실현할 수 잇으리라고 생각합니다. 이러한 의미에＜52＞ 잇어서 農業者의 생산력증진도 어찌 생각하면 노동의 합리화가 아니되면 水泡에 歸하리라고 믿습니다.
이 다음으로 協同組合運動이 필요할 것입니다. 일편으로 생산력을 증진하며 일편으로 농민의 買賣力(빠게인)을 堅固하게 하야 市場統制의 주력을 取得하여야 할 것입니다. 이리하야 좀 구차하나마 이 두 가지에 의하야 적극적으로 생산력증진을 謀하며 소극적으로는 協同組合運動을 行하야써 自衛策을 삼어야 할 것입니다.

三. 小作運動
단 분배의 불공평을 匡正하야 농촌인구의 多大數를 점하는 中農 及 貧農의 소득을 증가함이 最緊急務이다. 그런데 其 방책중 最重要한 자로 小作運動을 통하야 농촌의 기생적 존재인 지주계급 특히 不在大地主의 소득에 歸하는 小作料의 輕減을 圖할 것이다. 其外에도 농촌(특히 貧農)을 압박하는 稅制의 改正, 農村의 吸血鬼的 존재인 高利貸金制度의 驅逐, 합리적 庶民金融制度의 수립등이 절대적으로 필요하다.

三. 小作運動과 協同組合運動이 가장 긴급한 줄 압니다. 朝鮮의 농촌은 다른 나라 농촌에 비하야 훨신 피폐정도가 심하다고 봄니다. 원래 인구에 비하야 경지면적이 좁고 게다가 自作地가 적어 小作制度는 극히 가혹합니다. 그곳에다가 外來資本이 朝鮮天地를 휩싸돌매 自作農은 날로 몰락하야 小作農으로 됨으로 더욱 더욱 地主의 小作人에 대한 誅求는 酷甚하야 小作權의 이동, 小作料의 인상, 제반 公課의 小作人에의 轉嫁는 年年 심해갑니다. 이러한 趨移를 자연에 맡기면 小作人의 前途는 暗澹함으로 이것을 방비코저 小作運動을＜59＞ 하지 안으면 안됩니다.
그러나 小作運動만 가지고는 어쩔 수 없는 것이 기껏 생산을 해놓앗드래도 그 생산물을 저렴하게 팔거나 생산비를 많이 먹여서는 농사지은 효과가 없읍니다. 그럼으로 생산물을 되도록 비싸게 팔기 위하야 共同販賣組合-즉 生産組合을 組織하여 肥料, 農具 기타 生産資料를 사드릴때 되도록 싸게 사기 위하야 共同購入組合-즉 消費組合으로 조직하는 것은 小作運動과 不可分한 농촌운동이겟습니다.
四. 세계를 자본주의체계에 잡아 넣은 현대 경제조직에 잇어서는 朝鮮만이 홀로 이 자본주의제도에서 벗어날 수가 없엇습니다. 반듯이 朝鮮도 과거에 이미 朝鮮과 같은 사정에 떠러진 나라의 覆轍을 밟는 것이 사실이니 우리는 그 나라의 경험을 배울 필요가 잇습니다. 물론 지역상으로 人格上으로 시대상으로 꼭 朝鮮과 같을 도리야 없겟지만 일반적 유사점은 많을지니 꼭 배울 필요가 잇습니다. 朝鮮엔 이미 경지면적에 비한 인구밀도가 과다한 것, 朝鮮의 지주는 不在地主가 많은 것, 朝鮮의 산업은 농업 이외로 발전할 가능성이 적은 것-이런 것들은 오즉 朝鮮 뿐이 아니엇을 줄 압니다. 그리고 朝鮮은 母國의 邊方에 잇어서 군사상 그다지 중요하지 안치 안은 것, 현대는 상업자본주의시대가 아닌 것-등은 다른 곳과 구별되지 안을 수 없읍니다. 그럼으로 朝鮮의 농촌문제는 이러한 諸條件을 고려에 넣어 해결될 것으로 믿읍니다.＜59＞
