Publication Date: 1931년 04월 01일
Article Title: 기름이 말은 젖인 것을 외 1편 (That the Oil is Dried Milk, and 1 Other Piece)
Author: 李揆元
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

기름이 말은 젖인 것을 (That the Oil is Dried Milk)
The baby frets,
Clutching its mother's nipple,
Its bright red lips tremble.

Don't ask, that is
A sorrow known only to the pale mother.
Suck and suck, no matter how much it sucks,
What can be done? It's milk where the oil doesn't flow.

The hungry baby, upon its mother's face,
Licked the hot tears with the tip of its tongue.
And the gaunt outline of bones on the blue face,
It touched with its fernbrake-like hand.

Hey you! Who said the flower has withered on the young nipple,
And the oil has dried up on the milk-selling street?
The fragrance of plump breasts and the drifting smell of milk,
Who said this baby must not suck them?

The baby frets.
Lacking the strength to fret, now it has fallen asleep.
Perhaps listening to the sound of its mother's falling tears,
It seems to sleep, still holding the withered breast in its mouth.<87>

어머니 부르면서 잠이 들겟네 (Calling Mother, It Will Fall Asleep)
When the north wind blows and cold snow falls,
What will that little sparrow do?
Hiding its tiny beak beneath its feet,
It will huddle under the eaves and tremble.

Who will send warm sunlight to the little sparrow?
Who will bring food and toss it to the pitiful sparrow?
Shivering and shivering from the cold, after shivering for a while,
Calling its mother, it will fall asleep.

But another thing pains the heart,
What will that lonely child do?
Blowing on its hands, red with cold,
Leaning against the street corner, it will weep sorrowfully.

Who will feed the lonely child a warm lump of rice?<87>
Who will dress the shivering child in a warm set of clothes?
Crying and crying from the cold until its voice is hoarse,
Calling its mother, it will fall asleep.<88>
<87-88>

**Summary**

This article presents two poems by Yi Kyu-wŏn depicting scenes of extreme hardship and poverty. The first poem focuses on a starving infant unable to get milk from its malnourished mother, highlighting the mother's silent suffering and questioning the societal disparity where abundance exists elsewhere but is inaccessible. The second poem draws parallels between a freezing sparrow and a destitute child, both abandoned to the elements and ultimately succumbing to cold and hunger while crying out for their mothers. For historians, these poems offer a poignant glimpse into the social conditions of colonial Korea in the early 1930s, reflecting the widespread poverty, hunger, and despair experienced by marginalized populations under Japanese rule. The raw emotion and stark imagery serve as powerful testimony to the human cost of the era.

기름이 말은 젖인 것을 외 1편
						李揆元
기름이 말은 젖인 것을
아기가 보챔니다,
어머니의 젖꼭지를 물고서,
새빨간 입술을 떰니다.

뭇지마세요, 그것은
파리한 어머니만이 알고 잇는, 슬픔이에요.
빨고 빨고 암만 빨아보아도,
어떠케 합니까, 기름이 흐르지 안는 젖인 것을.

배고푼 아기는 어머니 얼골우에 흐르는
뜨거운 눈물을, 헤끝으로 핥앗습니다.
그리고 푸른 얼골의 앙상한 뼈의 윤곽을,
고사리같은 손으로 만저 보앗습니다.

여보시오 누가 젊은 젖꼭지에 꽃이 시들고,
우유파는 거리에 기름이 말랏다고 합디까.
살진 유방의 향기와 떠도는 우유의 냄새를,
이 아기가 빨아서는 안된다고, 누가 그랫습니까.

아기가 보챕니다요.
보채든 힘이 모자라서, 인저는 잠이 들엇습니다.
아마도 어머니의 눈물 떨어지는 소리를 드르면서,
시들은 젖을 문 채 잠자는가 봐요.＜87＞

어머니 부르면서 잠이 들겟네
북풍이 불고 찬 눈이 나리면,
저 어린 참새는 어찌할건가.
조고마한 주둥이를 발아래에 감추고,
첨하끝에 업드려서 발발 떨겟네.

누가 어린 참새에게 따뜻한 해빛을 보내주겟나.
누가 불상한 참새에게 먹을것 갖다가 던저 주겟나.
추워서 떨고 떨고 한참 떨다가,
어머니 부르면서 잠이 들겟네.

그러나 또 한가지 마음 아픈건,
저 외로운 아이는 어찌할건가.
빨가케 얼은 손을 입으로 불며,
담모통에 기대어서 슬피 울겟네.

누가 외로운 아이에게 따뜻한 밥한덩이 먹여주겟나.＜87＞
누가 떨고 잇는  아해에게 포근한 옷 한벌을 입혀주겟나.
추워서 울고 울고 목쉬게 울다가,
어머니 부르면서 잠이 들겟네.＜88＞
