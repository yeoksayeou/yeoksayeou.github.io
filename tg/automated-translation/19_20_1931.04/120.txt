Magazine Title: 동광 제20호
Publication Date: 1931년 04월 01일
Article Title: 非暴力論 (On Non-Violence)
Author: 李光洙
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Essay)

I
Violence is the darling of the modern age. Not only imperialists are admirers and apostles of violence, but Leninists have also been so. Today, the term Communism necessarily includes the meaning of violent revolution.
It goes without saying that the state today is maintained by violence. Therefore, it would be natural that all ideologues who intend to oppose this state dream of seizing violence. (deleted - original text)

III
The tactic of mass movement called Non-Violence (非暴力), which Gandhi introduced into human history, suggests the destiny to revolutionize the front line of the worldwide popular revolutionary movement.
The benefits of the tactic called Non-Violence are:
(1) Fewer sacrifices of life.
(2) Since it does not cause harm to the people other than those on the movement's front line, there is less exhaustion of the people's strength (民力), and consequently, it does not incur popular resentment (民怨).
(3) Because it is non-violent, the prison terms of the sacrificed are short, allowing them to quickly return to the movement line.
(4) As it does not provoke severe hostility (敵愾心) in the ruling class or nation, which is the opponent of the movement, communication (䟽通) of intentions and feelings can always be attempted.
(5) Because the severity of punishment (刑의 量) is low, many people (多數人) can participate fearlessly with their bodies or property.
(6) It can be enduring (持久的) and permeating (滲透的)—this is what the ruling class, which possesses military force as a means of suppression, finds most distressing.
(7) Finally, because a non-violent (非暴力的) resolution does not inflict resentment (怨恨) upon the opponent, it does not harbor hostility and thus the crisis of war after the resolution.
etc.

IV
Unity is not unnecessary even when using violence as a means, but since violence can forcibly drive (驅使) the masses, it can be said that unity is not that necessary. However, in a Non-Violence Movement, because there is no coercion whatsoever, it relies solely on firm unity as its strength.
If there is firm unity among tens of thousands of the masses, and that unity initiates a disobedience and boycott movement through non-violence, it is imagined that its effect will be greater than the same number of people rising up in revolt with weapons and violence. Firstly, suppressing such a mass movement with violence would be almost impossible, because completely calming it down would only lie in the congruence (合致) of rational conditions.
It seems that the weapon of Non-Violence (非暴力), when used by a firm unity with a just purpose, might produce an effect greater than violence.<60>

**Summary**

Yi Kwangsu argues in favor of non-violence as a superior tactic for mass movements compared to violence, which he identifies with both imperialism and Leninism. Drawing heavily on Gandhi's example, he outlines the strategic advantages of non-violence: fewer casualties, less popular exhaustion and resentment, shorter prison terms allowing activists to return, potential for communication with opponents, broader participation due to lower risk, endurance, and a lack of lingering hostility after resolution. He posits that a firmly united, non-violent movement engaging in disobedience and boycotts could be more effective and harder to suppress than an armed uprising, relying on rational agreement rather than force for resolution. From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it shows a prominent Korean intellectual advocating for Gandhian non-violent resistance during the Japanese colonial period. At a time when various strategies for independence were debated, Yi Kwangsu's explicit promotion of non-violence over revolutionary violence (associated with communism) provides insight into the ideological currents within the nationalist movement, though his later actions complicate interpretations of his motives.

非暴力論
						李光洙
一
폭력은 현대의 寵兒다. 帝國主義者만이 폭력의 찬미자요 使徒가 아니라 레닌주의자도 그러하엿다. 오늘날의 共産主義라는 말에는 폭력혁명이란 의미가 필연적으로 포함되어잇다.
금일의 국가가 폭력으로 유지되는 것을 말할 것도 없다. 그럼으로 이 국가에 대항하랴는 모든 主義者들이 폭력의 장악을 夢想하는 것도 자연한 일이라 하겟다.(삭제-원문)

三
간디가 인류의 역사에 끌어들인 非暴力이라는 민중운동의 전술은 全世界民衆革命運動의 戰線을 혁명할 운명을 암시한다.
非暴力이라는 전술의 이익은
(1) 생명의 희생이 적은 것.
(2) 運動戰線에 선 자이외의 인민에게 해를 및이지 아니함으로 民力의 피폐가 적고 따라서 民怨을 아니 받는 것.
(3) 非暴力인 고로 희생자의 刑期가 짧아서 곳 運動線에 복귀할 수가 잇는 것.
(4) 운동의 對手인 지배계급, 또는 민족에게 심각한 적개심을 아니 이르키어 언제 意思와 感情의 䟽通을 圖할 수가 잇는 것,
(5) 刑의 量이 적기 때문에 多數人이 겁없이 몸으로나 재산으로나 참가할 수 잇는 것,
(6) 持久的, 滲透的일 수가 잇는 것-이것은 무력을 진압수단으로 가진 지배계급이 가장 고통으로 하는 바이다.
(7) 최후에 非暴力的 해결 對手에게 원한을 끼치지 아니함으로 해결후의 적개심과 따라서 전쟁의 위기를 포장하지 아니하는 것,
등이다.

四
폭력을 수단으로 할 때에도 단결이 필요치 아니 함이 아니나 폭력은 민중을 강제로 驅使할 수가 잇음으로 단결이 그다지 필요하다고도 할 수 없다. 그러나 非暴力運動에 잇어서는 강제라는 것이 전혀 없기 때문에 오직 굳은 단결만을 힘으로 하는 것이다.
만일 수만의 다수 민중의 굳은 단결이 잇고 그 단결이 非暴力으로 불복종, 보이콭운동을 개시한다고 하면 그것은 同數의 민중이 무기를 가지고 폭동을 가지고 폭력을 이르키는 것 보다 효과가 클 것이 상상된다. 제일딴 이러한 민중운동은 폭력으로 진압하기가 거의 불가능에 속할 것이니 이것을 완전히 진정할 것은 오직 합리적 조건의 合致에 잇을 것이기 때문이다.
非暴力이란 무기는 정당한 목적을 가진 굳은 단결에게 사용될 때에는 폭력 이상의 효과를 내일 것 같이도 생각된다.＜60＞
