**Student Literary Scene**

**The Waves of Namch'ŏn**
Muju Kim Ch'angsik

The uncooled blood of this land,
The unending thing of this village,
The water of the South stream flows and flows.

Embracing the village, flowing gently,
The movement of the Namch'ŏn waves—
Now I see the movement of strong power.
- Circling mountains and piercing the wilderness,
- Tearing its breast before protruding capes,
Even while striking its head on bulging reefs,
Without rest, the Namch'ŏn's waves flow,
Like the will and blood (意志血) of youth.<46>

From eternity to eternity,
Teaching the unity of this village and our strength,
As if symbolizing the history of this land—
- Uniting when broken,
- Spreading out as it flows,
With the spirit pointing towards the other side of the wilderness,
The small Namch'ŏn waves, while gentle,
Flow powerfully, powerfully.

When the flowing waves strike the stones,
And the scattered droplets hide in the sand,
In my anxious heart too, dew-like droplets have scattered.
But the movement of firmly forged will—
- Like molten iron (鐵물) melted and forged in a bellows,
- Like the rolling blue waves of the deep sea (深海),
Uniting when separated, the united water flowing again,
When I see the movement of tenacious power,
Only then did I feel the power of power.

Embracing the village, flowing gently,
The water of the South stream.
As the times become troubled,<47>
We who lived drinking the milk of Namch'ŏn
Cross the stream and vanish afar,
Standing houses crumble into stone walls,
And even amidst the village becoming desolate,
The waves flow;
In this unsightly place, silently, only the Namch'ŏn water
Alone... .

The uncooled blood of this land,
The unending milk of the village,
The water of the South stream flows and flows. (End)

**The Bitterness of Mr. Kim’s First Harvest**
Chaeryŏng Myŏngsin Min Pyŏnggyun

Waking early at six in the morning to go hiking, I found Mr. Kim and his wife already threshing rice in our yard, having hired 4 or 5 neighbors for wages. Mr. Kim lives right next door to us; still young, or rather a boy farmer, this is the first year he started farming his own share, having left his parents’ home with empty hands (뷘 손) and meager belongings with his wife this past spring.<48>
“Hey, Mr. Kim! Harvesting today! How happy you must be.”
Rubbing my eyes, I opened the gate and smiled like this, looking at Mr. Kim diligently stripping rice ears fed into the machine one bundle at a time by his wife. Mr. Kim smiled back, saying, “Ah, you’re up early! Anyway, today is the harvest day, gathering the crystallization of blood and sweat steadily shed over the past year, so I’m filled with deep emotion.”
And he continued busily threshing the rice his wife handed him, standing there with a bright smile.

That night – indeed, I was truly dragged along by Mr. Kim to a Chinese restaurant called XX Wŏn in town. But strangely, Mr. Kim, who hadn't known alcohol before, kept drinking alone, became extremely sorrowful, and just kept crying.

“Hey, Mr. Kim! What’s wrong, tell me, huh?”
No matter how much I tried to dissuade him, he just sobbed without any answer, crying his heart out bitterly for a long while before finally composing himself and speaking these words mixed with sighs. No, they were words of bitter indignation.
“Mr. Min! Excuse me. Today I just keep feeling senselessly sad... But Mr. Min! As you might know, wouldn’t you say I’m quite a sound person? Look, I don’t smoke and I haven’t drunk alcohol! Even when going to the market, since I set up what I call my own household, I’ve passed by without knowing a bowl of noodles! Truly, it’s not to boast, but a frugal farmer like me wouldn’t have been common! And also, as you’ve always seen, among young peasant wives, mine was quite diligent too! However, after threshing six *sŏm* of rice today and settling accounts for everything—the loaned grain (農糧), fertilizer costs (金肥), water tax (水稅), this and that—taken in advance from the landlord’s (地主) house, this first year of farming actually ended up short by a good *sŏm* of rice! Good heavens, this is absurd! So, even if we farm again next year, it will only fill their bellies, while for us, only debt (負債) will increase! It seems that for us, there’s no other way but that—I’ve keenly realized this anew today!”<49>

**Summary**

The first piece is a poem titled "The Waves of Namch'ŏn," which uses the image of a constantly flowing stream, Namch'ŏn, as a metaphor for the enduring spirit, resilience, and historical continuity of the Korean people despite hardships and the decay of traditional village life. The second piece, "The Bitterness of Mr. Kim’s First Harvest," is a short narrative describing the author's encounter with a young, hardworking tenant farmer who, after his first harvest, falls into despair upon realizing that after paying exorbitant dues (loaned grain, fertilizer costs, water tax) to his landlord, he is left with debt rather than profit, highlighting the exploitative nature of the colonial agricultural system. For historians, the poem reflects nationalist sentiment and cultural perseverance under occupation through natural metaphors. The prose piece offers a stark, personal account of the economic realities and systemic exploitation faced by Korean tenant farmers under Japanese colonial rule, illustrating the mechanisms (high rents, input costs) that kept peasants impoverished despite their labor.

處女秋收한 金君의 悲憤
載寧明新 閔丙均

아침 여섯 시 등산을 하려고 일즉 일어나니 우리 집 마당에서는 벌서 金君네 내외가 이웃에 사는 4,5人을 품삯을 얻어 벼마당질을 하고 잇다. 김군은 우리 집 바로 옆 집에 살고 잇는 아직도 젊다느니보다도 소년농군으로 그가 자기몫으로 농사를 짓기 시작한 것은 그의 妻와 같이 금년 봄에 부모슬하에서 뷘 손으로 구차한 세간이랍시고 나선 첫해다.＜48＞
「야 김군! 오늘은 추슬세 그려! 얼마나 기쁜가.
나는 눈껍을 부비며 대문을 열고나서 안해의 한 목쿰식 들어주는 벼이삭을 기게에다 열심히 흘치고 잇는 김군을 바라보며 이러케 웃엇다. 김군도 따라 웃으며 「어 일즉 일어낫구만! 하여간 오늘이 일년 동안 꾸준히 흘려 둿든 피와 땀의 結晶을 거두는 추수날이 되고 보니깐 감개가 무량하네.」
하고는 연다라 안해의 생글생글 웃고 서서 쳉겨주는 벼를 바쁘게 흘치고 잇는 것이다.

그날 밤 - 아닌게 아니라 나는 정말로 김군에게 끌리어 읍내 XX원이라는 중국인 요리집을 찾게 되엇다. 그런데 이상한 것은 김군이 오늘은 전에 모르든 술을 혼자서 자꾸 마시드니 몹시도 설어워하며 그저 자꾸 울기만 하는 것이엇다.

「여보게 김군! 왜 그래 글세 응?」
아무리 만류를 해도 그는 그저 아무 대답이 없이 흙흙 느끼어 가면서 비통한 울음을 한참 동안이나 실컨 울고 나서야 비로소 정신을 차리며 이러케 한숨 섞인 말을 하는 것이엇다. 아니 悲憤한 말을 하는 것이엇다.
「민군! 실례햇네. 오늘은 실없이 자꾸 슬퍼만 지는구만... 그런데 민군! 자네도 아다 싶이 나로 말할 것 같으면 퍽이나 건실한 편이 아닌가? 자 담배도 안먹고 술도 안먹엇고! 장에를 가도 여지까지 내가 살림살이랍시고 버려놓은 다음부터는 국수그릇을 모르고 지나온 판일세 그려! 사실 내 자랑이 아니라 나만치 근검한 농민도 쉽지는 않앗을게야! 그리고 또 자네도 늘 보앗으니 알겟네마는 농가의 젊은 안악네 치고도 나의 안해도 꽤 부지런한 편이엇으리! 그러나 오늘 타작벼 엿섬을 논아놓고 거기서 地主 집에서 장례로 갖다 먹은 農糧값 金肥값 水稅 무엇무엇 다 치르고 나니 도리어 금년 첫해 농사가 볏섬이나 조히 모자란단 말이지! 허어 참 이런 기가 막혀서! 그러니 오는 해에 또 농사를 짓는대야 그들의 뱃집이나 불려 줫지 우리에겐 負債 밖에 늘 것이 없단 말이지! 아무래도 우리에겐 그것 밖에는 별한 도리가 없다는 것을 오늘 새삼스럽게 다시 깨닫는 바일세!」＜49＞
