Article Title: 平和를 사랑하는 者는 (One Who Loves Peace)
Author: 李大容
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

**Body Text Translation:**
One Who Loves Peace
					Yi Tae-yong

One who loves peace fights the world
Fights the world's hatred (憎惡), lust (淫慾), and suspicion (猜疑)
Overcoming the temptation of a life of flattery (阿附) and easy living (苟安)
Sacrificing one's body for eternal happiness

Was it not Jesus, who wore a crown of thorns and shed blood there on Golgotha,
Who took upon himself the sins of all living beings (蒼生)?
Casting aside fame and success like worn-out shoes
He suffered persecution for the sake of earthly justice

O multitude, unable to escape from the abyss (深淵) of sin (罪孽)
Plotting the extension of a fleeting life's moment (찰나)
The smile worn upon your wrinkled faces (얼골)
Is surely weary, but how can you forget me?
The beloved whom you cannot greet even when the morning sun rises
You are deceived into staying awake until the frequent cock crows
-(Old manuscript from Yangjŏng (於洋亭舊稿))- <56>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Tae-yong equates the love of peace with a struggle against the world's evils like hatred, lust, suspicion, flattery, and complacency. It presents Jesus Christ's sacrifice on Golgotha as the ultimate example of selflessness for justice, contrasting this with those mired in sin who cling to fleeting life, their weary smiles hiding a forgotten truth or a long-awaited dawn they cannot yet greet. From a historical perspective, the poem's intense focus on struggle, sacrifice, and the denouncement of worldly corruption (flattery, easy living), framed with Christian imagery, could be interpreted allegorically. In the context of 1930s colonial Korea, the "peace" sought might subtly represent national liberation, and the fight against the "world's evils" could symbolize resistance against oppressive forces and collaborators, making the religious theme a potential vehicle for veiled nationalist sentiment.

平和를 사랑하는 者는
					李大容
평화를 사랑하는 자는 세상과 싸운다
세상의 憎惡와 淫慾과 猜疑와 싸운다
阿附와 苟安의 생활의 誘惑을 이기고
영원한 행복을 위해 한 몸을 희생하냐니

저 골곳다에 가시관을 쓰고 피 뿜은 이가
蒼生의 죄악을 대신한 예수 아닌가
名利와 榮達을 헌신처럼 내어버리고
지상의 정의를 위해 핍박을 당하엿나니

罪孽의 深淵에서 인해 벗어날 줄 모르고
찰나의 생명의 연장을 꾀하는 무리여
그대들 주름잡힌 얼골에 띠운 웃음이
고단도 하시지만 어찌 날잊노
아침해가 솟아도 못 맞날 임을
잦은 닭이 울도록 속아 밝히오
-(於洋亭舊稿)- ＜56＞
