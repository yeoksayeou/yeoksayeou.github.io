Article Title: 정처없이 떠다니고 싶지는 않나? (외 1편) (Don't You Wish to Drift Aimlessly? (and 1 other))
Author: 張萬榮
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Don't You Wish to Drift Aimlessly?
					Chang Man-yŏng (張萬榮)

Don't you wish to drift aimlessly?
Above the mountain peaks, a white cloud drifts away,
It drifts away, its destination unknown.

Hey you! Like that white cloud,
Don't you wish to drift aimlessly?

On a quiet autumn day, lying on the grass,
Endlessly longing for the path of wandering...

Gazing at the blue sky, if I whistle,
Even the wind enters my embrace, pleading.

You come,
The sky without clouds is blue,
The sunlit place without wind is warm.

Grass soft as lamb's wool,
Won't you come and roll around?
Do you know how worthless and deceitful things are.

Though they harbor sharp knives in their hearts,
Oh, crowd of Hypocrisy (僞善) calling for peace outwardly!
Within the love you speak of so affectionately,
The rotten stench of Greed (貧婪) is completely full.

He who loves peace fights with the world,
Without fighting, one cannot have peace.
The heart of the one who sang while burning Rome
Is needed also for the peace of the world. <56>

**Summary**

This poem by Chang Man-yŏng expresses a deep yearning for freedom and aimless wandering, contrasting the serene beauty of nature (white clouds, blue skies, soft grass) with the perceived hypocrisy and conflict of the human world. The latter part shifts tone, decrying false peace and hidden malice ("sharp knives," "rotten stench of Greed"), ultimately asserting that true peace requires struggle, even invoking the controversial image of Nero. For historians, the poem offers insight into the escapist desires perhaps felt under colonial rule, alongside a potentially coded or ambivalent message about the necessity of conflict (even destructive acts like Nero's) to achieve genuine peace, possibly reflecting disillusionment with passive approaches or critiquing superficial calls for harmony in a constrained society.

정처없이 떠다니고 싶지는 않나?
					張萬榮
정처없이 떠다니고 싶지는 않나?
산머리 우로 힌구름 떠가네
가는 곳 정치 않고 떠가네.

이 사람아! 자네는 저 힌 구름같이
정처없이 떠다니고 싶지는 않나?

고요한 가을 날 잔디에 누엇노라면
끝없이 방랑의 길 그리워...

푸른 하늘 바라보며 추파람 불면
바람도 품에 들며 애원하나니.

자네는 와서
구름 없는 하늘 푸르고
바람 없는 양지 따뜻하네.

양털 같이 풀한 잔디풀
자네는 와서 딩굴지 않나?
얼마나 값없고 간사한 것을 아는가.

가슴엔 날카론 칼을 품고도 그러나
겉으로 평화를 부르는 僞善의 무리여
그대들 다정하게 말하는 사랑의 속엔
貧婪의 썩은 냄새가 가득이 찻고나

평화를 사랑하는 자는 세상과 싸운다
싸우지 아니하고는 평화를 갖지 못한다
로마를 불지르고 노래한 이의 마음이
세상의 평화를 위하야도 필요하고나 ＜56＞
