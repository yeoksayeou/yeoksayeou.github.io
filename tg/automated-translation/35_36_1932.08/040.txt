Ch'ae P'il-gŭn
...If you stick your tongue out at a mirror, the person in the mirror will also stick their tongue out; if you make a fist at the mirror, the person in the mirror will also make a fist. (Farmer's Life) <4>

Kim Ch'ang-je
...Isn't the current state of society mostly afflicted with indigestion and constipation? Take education, for example. In trying to satisfy the desire for all sorts of knowledge, doesn't it eventually happen that this desire for knowledge cannot be digested and is simply excreted, or else, isn't it like being a constipation patient who only learns but cannot pass it? (Youth) <5>

Kim Hwal-lan
...Today, we will establish a view of fidelity that both men and women practice, where one man and one woman voluntarily form a sexual relationship encompassing both body and spirit, and are mutually faithful to it. (Women's Discourse) <7>

<Unidentified Author>
...It is extremely regrettable and shameful that the Korean people, who have a history of cultural life spanning 4,000 to 5,000 years, do not possess a single dictionary for learning their own language and script. (Hangul) <9>

Ko Chae-uk
...For inflation to stimulate general purchasing power and bring about a genuine economic boom, one must expect improvements in corporate finance and international borrowing and lending. However, now that the dream of [recent?] economic recovery is shattered and the economic situation in various countries worsens daily, even if the volume of currency circulation increases due to inflation, it is clear that it will soon flow back to the Bank of Japan (日銀) in the form of corporate debt repayment or deposits. (The Front Line) <11>

Pak In-dŏk
- If even a single individual or a nation possesses the spirit of hope, they will ultimately rise again. (Danish Folk High School) <50>

<Unidentified Author>
- It is unconditionally necessary for Koreans to clearly understand the real situation of Korea. Any trivial reasons or explanations for this are just superfluous words. (Study of Korea through Numbers) <53>

**Summary**

This article compiles short opinion pieces from various authors in 1932 on diverse topics. Ch'ae P'il-gŭn uses a mirror analogy for social interaction. Kim Ch'ang-je critiques contemporary education as causing intellectual 'indigestion' or 'constipation'. Kim Hwal-lan advocates for a modern, mutual concept of fidelity between sexes. An unnamed author laments the lack of a Korean dictionary. Ko Chae-uk analyzes the dim prospects of inflation stimulating the economy under current global conditions, predicting money would flow back to the Bank of Japan. Pak In-dŏk emphasizes the power of hope for national resurgence, citing the Danish Folk High School model. Another unnamed author stresses the absolute necessity for Koreans to understand their own reality. From a colonial history perspective, the pieces reflect anxieties about cultural identity (lack of a dictionary), the limitations of the colonial economy (inflation discussion mentioning the Bank of Japan), critiques of modern systems like education, evolving social mores (fidelity), and a persistent undercurrent of national consciousness and hope for the future (Pak In-dŏk, final snippet).

蔡弼近

金昶濟
...현 사회상은 거이 다 소화불량과 변비증에 걸린 상태가 아닌가 한다. 爲先 교육으로 말하드래도 각양각색의 지식욕을 채우랴다가 필경은 그 지식욕이 소화되지 못하고 그대로 배설해 버리거나 不然이면 배우기만 하고 누지 못하는 변비증 환자와 같지 아니하냐.(靑年)＜5＞

金活蘭
...오늘날의 우리는 자발적으로 일남일녀의 육체와 정신을 아울러 성적 관계를 맺고 피차에 거기 충성하므로 남녀가 다 실행하는 정조관을 세울 것이다.(女論)＜7＞

＜OOO＞

...4, 5천년 문화생활의 역사를 가진 조선민족에게 제 말과 글을 배우는 사전 한 권이 없다는 것은 나무나 섭섭하고 부끄러운 일이다.(한글)＜9＞

高在旭
...인플레이숀이 일반 구매력을 자극하야 진정한 호경기를 출현하려면 기업 재정의 호전을 비롯하야 국제 貸借의 개선을 기대하지 않을 수 없거늘 재금지 경기의 꿈이 깨트러지고 각국의 경제정세가 나날이 악화하는 금일에 설령 인플레이숀에 의하야 통화유통량이 증가한다 할지라도 곳 기업채무의 返濟 또한 예금의 형식으로 日銀에 還流하고 말 것은 명백하다.(제1線)＜11＞
