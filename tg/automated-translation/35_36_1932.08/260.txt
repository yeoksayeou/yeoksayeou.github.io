**Summary**

This text provides the answers to a "Mental Test" quiz published in the August 1932 issue of the magazine *Donggwang*. The answers cover topics including specific dates, names of individuals, Korean and Manchurian geography, economic terms, government monopolies under Japanese rule (including opium), estimated figures (likely related to budgets or economy), specific laws and ordinances from the colonial period, and the official name for the Soviet Union. For historians, the article offers insight into the general knowledge expected of the readership in colonial Korea during the early 1930s, touching upon contemporary figures, key locations within Korea and occupied Manchuria, the structure of the colonial economy (monopolies), and the legal framework imposed by Japan (Security Law, ordinances). The inclusion of opium as a state monopoly alongside ginseng, tobacco, and salt is particularly noteworthy.
