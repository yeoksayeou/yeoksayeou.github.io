Two Little Songs
						Tokyo Kim Yŏnghyang

Ah, is it autumn?
Is it autumn?
Is it autumn?
Ah—
It must be autumn.
Seeing the willow leaf
fallen in the puddle,
the young lady fetching water
bows her head.

Thinking of Him
I miss him today,
him who went far away.
Ah-ah, uh-uh,
Oh-oh, oo-oo—.
The willow branches flutter,
even more so today.
Ah-ah, uh-uh,
Oh-oh, oo-oo—.
-From the Musashino Plain-<88>

**Summary**

These two short poems by Kim Yŏnghyang, written from Tokyo, evoke a sense of autumnal melancholy and longing. The first poem uses the image of a falling willow leaf in a puddle to mark the arrival of autumn and prompt a moment of quiet contemplation in a young woman. The second poem explicitly expresses yearning for a loved one who is far away, intensified by the sight of fluttering willow branches, accompanied by sounds expressing deep sighs or longing. From a historical perspective, the poems offer insight into the personal sentiments of Koreans living in Japan during the colonial period. The gentle melancholy and longing for someone distant, written from Tokyo, could reflect broader feelings of displacement, homesickness, or separation experienced by Koreans under Japanese rule.

小曲 둘
						東京 金永鄕
아 가을인가
가을인가,
가을인가,
아―
가을인가봐
물똥에 떠러진
버들닢 보고
물깃는 아가씨
고개 숙이지.

그이 생각
멀리 가신 그이가
오늘따라 그립지
아아, 어어,
오오, 우우―.
버들가지 날니는
오늘따라 더하지
아아, 어어,
오오, 우우―.
-武藏野벌에서-＜88＞
